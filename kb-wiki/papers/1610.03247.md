# Strongly interacting matter from holographic QCD model

> We introduce the 5-dimension dynamical holographic QCD model, which is constructed in the graviton-dilaton-scalar framework with the dilaton background field $Φ$ and the scalar field $X$ responsible for the gluodynamics and chiral dynamics, respectively. We review our results on the hadron spectra including the glueball and light meson spectra, QCD phase transitions and transport properties in the framework of the dynamical holographic QCD model.

## Key claim

The dynamical holographic QCD (DhQCD) model, constructed in a graviton-dilaton-scalar framework, can simultaneously describe hadron spectra, QCD phase transitions, and transport properties, but the model's glueball spectra for three-trigluon states (0−−, 0+−, 2+−) are 1.5 GeV lighter than lattice results, indicating a specific limitation in the odd-parity sector.

## Method

The authors construct a 5D graviton-dilaton-scalar action where the dilaton background field Φ(z) and scalar field X(z) are dual to the gluon operator and quark operator at the UV boundary, respectively. The metric is automatically deformed by nonperturbative gluon condensation and chiral condensation in the vacuum, and at finite temperature a black hole is introduced to study thermodynamics. The glueball spectra are obtained by solving Schroedinger-like equations derived from 5D actions for scalar, vector, and tensor glueballs, while meson spectra are computed by adding light flavor fields on the gluodynamical background. Transport properties (jet quenching parameter, shear viscosity, bulk viscosity) are extracted via Kubo formulas and AdS/CFT dictionary, and chiral phase transitions are studied by solving the equation of motion for the scalar field vacuum expectation value χ(z).

## Key numbers

- Glueball spectra parameter μ_G = 1 GeV, fixed by the Regge slope of the scalar glueball spectra.
- Three-trigluon glueball states (0−−, 0+−, 2+−) have masses 1.5 GeV lighter than lattice results.
- Jet quenching parameter ratio hat{q}/T^3 shows a peak with height around 40 at approximately T = 1.1 T_c.
- Shear viscosity over entropy density ratio η/s shows a valley at approximately T = 1.1 T_c for parameters β = 0.01, γ = -√(8/3).
- The 5D mass of the complex scalar field X is fixed as M_5^2 = -3 from the AdS/CFT dictionary.

## Context

This work extends the soft-wall holographic QCD model of Karch (2006) by introducing a dynamical graviton-dilaton-scalar framework that self-consistently deforms the metric via gluon and chiral condensates. The results for glueball spectra are compared with lattice data from the glueball-lattice collaboration, and the equation of state for hot gluonic matter is validated against pure SU(3) lattice data from the LAT-EOS-G collaboration. The model enables future studies of the interplay between confinement/deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration, and provides a holographic framework for computing transport properties that reflect phase transition features, such as the peak in hat{q}/T^3 and bulk viscosity near T_c.
