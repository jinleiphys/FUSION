# Schwinger displacement of the quark-gluon vertex

> The action of the Schwinger mechanism in pure Yang-Mills theories endows gluons with an effective mass, and, at the same time, induces a measurable displacement to the Ward identity satisfied by the three-gluon vertex. In the present work we turn to Quantum Chromodynamics with two light quark flavors, and explore the appearance of this characteristic displacement at the level of the quark-gluon vertex. When the Schwinger mechanism is activated, this vertex acquires massless poles, whose momentum-dependent residues are determined by a set of coupled integral equations. The main effect of these residues is to displace the Ward identity obeyed by the pole-free part of the vertex, causing modifications to its form factors, and especially the one associated with the tree-level tensor. The comparison between the available lattice data for this form factor and the Ward identity prediction reveals a marked deviation, which is completely compatible with the theoretical expectation for the attendant residue. This analysis corroborates further the self-consistency of this mass-generating scenario in the general context of real-world strong interactions.

## Key claim

The Schwinger mechanism, when activated in QCD with two light quark flavors, endows the quark-gluon vertex with massless poles whose residues displace the Ward identity satisfied by the pole-free part of the vertex; the resulting deviation between lattice data and the Ward identity prediction for the tree-level form factor is compatible with the theoretical expectation for the residue, providing evidence for the self-consistency of the mass-generating scenario.

## Method

The authors solve a coupled system of Bethe-Salpeter equations derived from the Schwinger-Dyson equations for the three-gluon and quark-gluon vertices within the 3PI formalism, using a one-particle-exchange approximation for the four-particle kernels. They compute the momentum-dependent residue functions associated with the poles of the quark-gluon vertex in the soft-gluon limit (q→0), and compare the resulting displacement of the form factor λ₁(p²) against lattice data from two asymmetric lattices (denoted L08 and L07) from Kizilersu (2021).

## Key numbers

- The comparison between λ₁(p²) and λ₁⋆(p²) on the L08 lattice produces a signal of 8σ significance.
- The comparison on the L07 lattice produces a signal of 6σ significance.
- The gluon mass m² is determined by integrals of the form m² ∼ ∫ d⁴k k² Δ²(k²) C(r²), where C(r²) is the residue/displacement function.
- The analysis uses QCD with N_f = 2 light quark flavors.

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger mechanism from pure Yang-Mills theory (quarkless QCD) to QCD with two light quark flavors, generalizing the displacement effect previously established for the three-gluon vertex by Aguilar (2021, 2022) and Papavassiliou (2022). The displacement functions for the quark-gluon vertex are the direct analogue of the C(r²) function that produces the displacement L_s(r²) = L_s⋆(r²) + C(r²) in the three-gluon vertex. The results are compatible with lattice studies by Ayala (2012) and Binosi (2016) indicating that the unquenched gluon mass is larger than the quenched one, and enable future tests of the Schwinger mechanism through comparison with lattice data for the quark-gluon vertex form factors.
