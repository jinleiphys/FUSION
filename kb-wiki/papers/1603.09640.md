# Mesonic Decay of Charm Hypernuclei $Λ^+_c$

> $Λ^+_c$ hypernuclei are expected to have binding energies and other properties similar to those of strange hypernuclei in view of the similarity between the quark structures of the strange and charmed hyperons, namely $Λ(uds)$ and $Λ^+_c (udc)$. One striking difference however occurs in their mesonic decays, as there is almost no Pauli blocking in the nucleonic decay of a charm hypernucleus because the final-state nucleons leave the nucleus at high energies. The nuclear medium nevertheless affects the mesonic decays of charm hypernucleus because the nuclear mean fields modify the masses of the charm hyperon. In the present communication we present results of a first investigation of the effects of finite baryon density on different weak mesonic decay channels of the $Λ^+_c$ baryon. We found a non-negligible reduction of the decay widths as compared to their vacuum values.

## Key claim

The authors find a non-negligible reduction of the weak mesonic decay widths of the Λ⁺_c baryon in nuclear matter compared to their vacuum values, but note that several issues (particularly SU(4) flavor symmetry breaking in meson-baryon couplings) need careful examination before definite conclusions can be drawn.

## Method

The authors evaluate the in-medium weak mesonic decay rate of Λ⁺_c in infinite nuclear matter via the imaginary part of a one-loop baryon-meson self-energy diagram, using an effective weak-decay Lagrangian with coupling constants determined by the Fermi constant and CKM matrix elements. They first examine Pauli blocking effects in the Λ⁺_c → pK̄⁰ channel separately, then incorporate the density-dependent mass shift of Λ⁺_c from the quark meson coupling (QMC) model to compute the in-medium decay widths for all three channels (Λπ⁺, Σ⁺π⁰, pK̄⁰). Finite nuclei are treated using the local density approximation.

## Key numbers

- At nuclear matter saturation density (ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³), the in-medium Λ⁺_c mass is m*_Λc = 2.146 GeV.
- At normal nuclear matter density, the reductions in decay width are 14.5% for Λ⁺_c → Λπ⁺, 12% for Λ⁺_c → Σ⁺π⁰, and 6.5% for Λ⁺_c → pK̄⁰.
- The QMC model parametrization of the Λ⁺_c mass shift uses coefficients a₀ = 0.121, a₁ = 0.565, and a₂ = 0.878.
- The Fermi constant used is G_F = 1.16 × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻², with CKM matrix elements V_ud = 0.974 and V_cs = 0.973.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of charm hypernuclei binding energies using the quark meson coupling model by Tsushima and Khanna, and the meson exchange model of Dover and Kahana. The authors note that previous binding energy predictions for Λ⁺_c, Σ_c, and Ξ_c hypernuclei range from O to Pb and average about 7.5 MeV. The results enable future investigations of non-mesonic weak decays of charmed hypernuclei and are relevant to understanding the DN interaction in connection with D-mesic nuclei, as well as to planned experiments at FAIR and J-PARC.
