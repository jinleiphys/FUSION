# Hadron Structure and Form Factors

> We review recent results on hadron form factors and nucleon generalized parton distibutions obtained with dynamical lattice QCD simulations. We discuss lattice artifacts and open questions, and present the connection of lattice results to hadron structure and to the corresponding quantities measured in experiment.

## Key claim

This review paper summarizes lattice QCD results on hadron form factors and nucleon generalized parton distributions, showing that while lattice calculations with dynamical quarks at pion masses down to about 300 MeV agree qualitatively with experiment, the chiral extrapolation to the physical pion mass remains the dominant source of uncertainty, particularly for the nucleon axial charge and moments of parton distributions.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD calculations of two-point and three-point correlation functions using dynamical fermion simulations with pion masses below 500 MeV and spatial lattice lengths above 2 fm, employing sequential inversion through the sink and smearing techniques for ground state dominance. Non-perturbative renormalization in the RI'-MOM scheme is applied, with perturbative subtraction of O(a^2) terms for twisted mass fermions, and the generalized eigenvalue method is used to improve identification of the ground state and extend the accessible Q^2 range.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon axial charge g_A = 1.12(8) at the physical point from N_f=2 twisted mass fermions after continuum extrapolation and chiral extrapolation using one-loop heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, compared to the experimental value
- Pion masses used in simulations range from approximately 300 MeV to 500 MeV for form factor calculations
- Lattice spacings used for continuum extrapolation: a = 0.089 fm, 0.070 fm, and 0.056 fm for N_f=2 twisted mass fermions
- Spatial lattice lengths L such that Lm_pi is greater than approximately 3.3 for volume control, with Lm_pi = 3.3 and 4.3 compared at m_pi approximately 300 MeV
- Rho meson width extracted at m_pi = 308 MeV on a lattice of L = 2.8 fm using N_f=2 twisted mass fermions
- Dirac radius r_1^2 and Pauli radius r_2^2 times kappa_v shown as functions of (a/r_0)^2 for cut-off effect assessment

## Context

This review extends the lattice QCD program that successfully reproduced the low-lying hadron spectrum (Durr 2008, Alexandrou 2008) to the more challenging domain of form factors and generalized parton distributions that probe hadron structure. The results enable comparison with experimental programs on nucleon form factors that began in the 1950s and now use polarized beams and targets for high precision data, while also providing input for chiral perturbation theory expansions through determination of N-Delta coupling constants. The paper identifies the need for larger volumes with Lm_pi greater than approximately 4 for the axial charge at pion masses below 300 MeV, and highlights that the chiral extrapolation uncertainty remains the largest obstacle to precision comparisons with experiment.
