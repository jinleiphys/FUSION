# Structure of $^{38}$Cl and the quest for a comprehensive shell model interaction

> The higher-spin structure of $^{38}$Cl ($N = 21$) was investigated following the $^{26}$Mg($^{14}$C, $pn$) reaction at 30 and 37 MeV beam energies. The outgoing protons were detected in an $E- ΔE$ Si telescope placed at 0$^\circ$ close to the target with a Ta beam stopper between the target and telescope. Multiple $γ$ rays were detected in time coincidence with the protons using an enhanced version of the FSU $γ$ detection array. The level scheme was extended up to 8420 keV with a likely spin of 10 $\hbar$. A new multishell interaction was developed guided by the experimental information. This FSU interaction was built by fitting to the energies of 270 experimental states from $^{13}$C to $^{51}$Ti. Calculations using the FSU interaction reproduce observed properties of $^{38}$Cl rather well, including the spectroscopic factors. The interaction has been successfully used to interpret the $1p1h$ and $2p2h$ configurations in some nearby nuclei as well.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a new comprehensive shell-model interaction (FSU) fitted to 270 experimental states from ¹³C to ⁵¹Ti can reproduce the observed properties of ³⁸Cl, including spectroscopic factors, but the agreement for the highest-spin states (above 6 MeV) requires considering both 1p1h and 2p2h configurations, and the spin-parity assignments for some states remain tentative due to limited statistics.

## Method

The experiment used the ²⁶Mg(¹⁴C, pn)³⁸Cl fusion-evaporation reaction at 30 and 37 MeV beam energies, detecting protons in an E-ΔE Si telescope at 0° and γ rays in an enhanced FSU γ-detector array with clover and single-crystal Ge detectors. A new shell-model interaction (FSU) was developed by fitting 70 parameters to 270 experimental energy levels from ¹³C to ⁵¹Ti, using a valence space spanning the spsdpf model space with 1p1h excitations across oscillator shells. The fitting procedure iteratively solved eigenvalue problems, linearized energies in terms of fit parameters, and minimized χ², reaching convergence after 6 iterations with an overall rms deviation of 190 keV.

## Key numbers

- Level scheme extended up to 8420 keV with a likely spin of 10 ħ.
- Six new states observed in ³⁸Cl, with eleven new γ transitions added.
- The FSU interaction was fitted to 270 experimental states, achieving an overall rms deviation of 190 keV.
- 224 of the 270 fitted states are sensitive to the N = 8 and N = 20 shell gaps.
- The 5⁻ state at 671 keV has a half-life of 715 ps (isomeric transition not observed due to 100 ns coincidence gate).
- DCO ratio for the 1029-keV transition (dipole gate at 755 keV) is 1.05(4), indicating dipole multipolarity.
- DCO ratios for the 292- and 171-keV transitions in the 638-keV dipole gate are 0.88(3) and 0.83(4), respectively, indicating dipole type; both are magnetic (M1) from polarization asymmetry.
- The 2331-keV transition from the 6145-keV state is quadrupole type; in the 2331-keV gate, DCO ratios for the 292- and 171-keV transitions are 0.59(5) and 0.57(5).
- The 3142-keV transition from the 3814-keV state is quadrupole and magnetic (M2), supporting the 7⁺ assignment to the 3814-keV state.
- The 0p0h 7⁻ state is predicted at 8357 keV, showing a 4.6 MeV jump from the 6⁻ state.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of ³⁸Cl from β-decay, transfer reactions, and neutron capture (e.g., transfer reaction studies by the authors of Ref. [transfeReac2] and the binary grazing reaction study by O'Donnell et al. (Ref. [heavyIon1]) that suggested 7⁺, 6⁺, and 5⁺ states around 3.5 MeV). The new FSU interaction addresses limitations of prior interactions such as SDPF-M (which failed for odd-N isotopes), WBP and its variants (which required ad hoc adjustments of single-particle energies for different nuclei), and SDPF-U (which could not predict intruder states in ³⁸Cl). The interaction enables systematic description of both normal and intruder states across the sd shell and beyond, and can be applied to interpret 1p1h and 2p2h configurations in nearby nuclei.
