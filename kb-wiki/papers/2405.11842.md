# High energy nucleus-nucleus collisions with accounting for Coulomb interaction

> The differential elastic cross sections of $^{12}$C - $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O - $^{16}$O and $^{20}$Ne - $^{20}$Ne nuclei scattering are calculated in the complete Glauber theory with the account of the modification due to Coulomb interaction and form factor effects. The role of the Coulomb interaction is shown to be significant mainly in the diffractive minima. The results of the complete Coulomb calculations are noticeably different from those obtained in the Born approximation.

# High energy nucleus-nucleus collisions with accounting for Coulomb interaction

## Key claim

The differential elastic cross sections of light nucleus-nucleus scattering calculated in complete Glauber theory with a full eikonal Coulomb phase factor differ noticeably from those obtained with the Born approximation for the Coulomb contribution, with the difference being most significant in the diffractive minima and increasing with the product of nuclear charges.

## Method

The authors compute the elastic scattering amplitude for symmetric collisions of light nuclei using the Glauber multiple-scattering formalism, where the strong interaction amplitude is evaluated via a generating function method (Shabelski and Shuvaev, 2021) with harmonic-oscillator nuclear densities. The Coulomb interaction is incorporated as an eikonal phase factor added to the strong-interaction profile function, with the infrared divergence regulated by separating the divergent logarithmic term and working at non-zero momentum transfer where the cross section remains finite.

## Key numbers

- For $^{12}$C - $^{12}$C scattering at 1 GeV per nucleon, the product of charges is $Z_A Z_B = 36$; for $^{16}$O - $^{16}$O it is 64; for $^{20}$Ne - $^{20}$Ne it is 100.
- The harmonic-oscillator density parameter $\lambda$ is chosen to fit mean square radii of 2.49 fm ($^{12}$C), 2.69 fm ($^{16}$O), and 2.99 fm ($^{20}$Ne).
- The nucleon-nucleon amplitude uses total cross section $\sigma_{NN}^{tot}$ and slope parameter $\beta$ with $a^2 = 2\pi\beta$ as the effective interaction radius.
- The differential cross section is given by $d\sigma/dt = (\pi/p^2) |f'_{AB}(q)|^2$ for $t = -q^2 \neq 0$.

## Context

This work extends the method of Glauber (1970, 2019) for including Coulomb effects in proton-nucleus scattering to the more complex case of nucleus-nucleus scattering, where the Glauber theory goes beyond the optical approximation. The results show that the full eikonal Coulomb treatment produces significantly larger effects near diffractive minima than the first-order Born approximation (which adds a Coulomb amplitude linearly to the strong amplitude), and that these effects grow with the nuclear charge product. The approach enables more accurate analysis of elastic scattering data for light nuclei, though it cannot describe Coulomb excitation processes such as giant resonances or nuclear photo-effect.
