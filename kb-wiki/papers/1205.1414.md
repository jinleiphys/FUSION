# Near-BPS Skyrmions: Non-shell configurations and Coulomb effects

> The relatively small binding energy in nuclei suggests that they may be well represented by near-BPS Skyrmions since their mass is roughly proportional to the baryon number $A.$ For that purpose, we propose a generalization of the Skyrme model with terms up to order six in derivatives of the pion fields and treat the nonlinear $σ$ and Skyrme terms as small perturbations. For our special choice of mass term (or potential) $V$, we obtain well-behaved analytical BPS-type solutions with non-shell configurations for the baryon density, as opposed to the more complex shell-like configurations found in most extensions of the Skyrme model . Along with static and (iso)rotational energies, we add to the mass of the nuclei the often neglected Coulomb energy and isospin breaking term. Fitting the four model parameters, we find a remarkable agreement for the binding energy per nucleon $B/A$ with respect to experimental data. These results support the idea that nuclei could be near-BPS Skyrmions.

## Key claim

The authors propose a generalized Skyrme model with terms up to order six in derivatives, treating the nonlinear sigma and Skyrme terms as small perturbations, and claim that with a specific choice of mass term, they obtain analytical BPS-type solutions with non-shell baryon density configurations that yield remarkable agreement for binding energy per nucleon with experimental data, but only after including the often-neglected Coulomb energy and isospin breaking term.

## Method

The authors construct a Lagrangian density containing terms of order zero, two, four, and six in derivatives of the pion fields, with the quadratic and quartic terms treated as small perturbations. They find analytical axial solutions for the static solitons by minimizing the energy for the case where the quadratic and quartic terms vanish, then compute the static energy, rotational energy via semiclassical quantization, and add the Coulomb energy and an isospin breaking term to obtain nuclear masses. The four free parameters of the model are fitted to reproduce experimental binding energies per nucleon.

## Key numbers

- The static energy for the nonshell solution yields \(E_0 = \frac{4}{3}\sqrt{2}(-3+2\sqrt{3}) n \pi^{3/2} \lambda \mu\) and \(E_6 = \frac{4}{3}\sqrt{2}(-3+2\sqrt{3}) n \pi^{3/2} \lambda \mu\), both proportional to baryon number \(A=n\)
- The root mean square radius of the baryon density is \(\langle r^2 \rangle^{1/2} = \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{18A\lambda}{\mu} \right)^{1/3} \sqrt{-1+\ln 16}\), consistent with the experimental form \(\langle r^2 \rangle^{1/2} = r_0 A^{1/3}\)
- The rotational energy for the nucleon (\(A=1\)) is \(E_{\text{rot}}^N = \frac{3}{8U_{11}}\)
- The rotational energy for the deuteron (\(A=2, i=0, j=1, \kappa=0\)) is \(E_{\text{rot}}^D = \frac{1}{V_{11}}\)
- The potential coefficients for the nonshell solution are \(C_1 = -0.129631\mu^2\), \(C_2 = -0.100632\mu^2\), \(C_3 = -0.045532\mu^2\), \(C_4 = -0.0105061\mu^2\), satisfying \(\sum_{k=1}^\infty k^2 C_k = -1.12798\mu^2 = -\frac{m_\pi^2 F_\pi^2}{16}\)

## Context

This work extends the near-BPS Skyrme model approach of Marleau (2011) and the BPS Skyrme model of Adam, Sanchez-Guillen, and Wereszczynski (2010) by introducing a new potential that yields non-shell baryon density configurations, unlike the shell-like configurations found in those earlier models. The authors also incorporate Coulomb energy and isospin breaking terms that were previously studied for \(A=1\) by Durgut (1985), Kaelbermann (1986), Ebrahim (1987), Jain (1989), Weigel (1989), Rathske (1988), and Meissner (2009) but were usually neglected for higher baryon numbers. This enables more precise calculation of nuclear masses across all baryon numbers and supports the viability of near-BPS Skyrmions as a description of nuclear matter.
