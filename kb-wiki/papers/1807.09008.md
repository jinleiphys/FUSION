# Effects of Finite Size of Constituent Quarks on Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction

> We have investigated the effect of the finite size of the constituent quarks on singlet and triplet nucleon-nucleon potentials, obtained in the framework of the $SU(2)$ nonrelativistic quark model using the resonating group method in the Born-Oppenheimer approximation. The full Hamiltonian used in the investigation includes the kinetic energy, two-body confinement potential, one gluon exchange potential (OGEP), and instanton induced interaction (III). The effects of the smearing of the contact interactions and the variation of the constituent mass of the quarks are discussed.

## Key claim

The finite size of constituent quarks, modeled by smearing the delta-function contact interactions with a Gaussian of width \(r_0\), significantly reduces the magnitude and range of the short-range repulsion in the nucleon-nucleon (NN) adiabatic potential; the qualitative features of the NN potential are preserved only for \(r_0 < 0.4\ \text{fm}\), and the attractive well is deepest when \(r_0\) is close to the oscillator size parameter \(b\).

## Method

The authors employed the SU(2) nonrelativistic quark model (NRQM) with a Hamiltonian containing kinetic energy, a harmonic confinement potential, the one-gluon exchange potential (OGEP), and the instanton-induced interaction (III). They replaced the Dirac delta functions in the color-magnetic term of OGEP and in the III with a Gaussian \(\frac{1}{\pi^{3/2} r_0^3} e^{-r_{ij}^2/r_0^2}\) to account for the finite size of constituent quarks. The resonating group method (RGM) in the Born-Oppenheimer approximation was used to compute the adiabatic NN potential for the singlet \(^1S_0\) and triplet \(^3S_1\) channels, with the quark size parameter \(r_0\) varied from \(0.1\ \text{fm}\) to \(0.6\ \text{fm}\) and the constituent quark mass \(m_q\) varied from \(150\ \text{MeV}\) to \(350\ \text{MeV}\).

## Key numbers

* The short-range repulsion (measured at internucleon separation \(S_I = 0.1\ \text{fm}\)) decreases as \(r_0\) increases and becomes feeble when \(r_0 \sim b\) (the oscillator size parameter).
* The NN potential loses intermediate-range attraction and becomes completely attractive for \(r_0 > 0.4\ \text{fm}\); qualitative features are preserved for \(r_0 < 0.4\ \text{fm}\).
* The depth of the attractive well is maximal when \(r_0 \sim b\) (the oscillator size parameter); the attraction saturates as \(r_0\) approaches zero.
* The internucleon separation at the potential minimum (\(S_I^{\text{min}}\)) increases as \(r_0\) decreases.
* In the absence of III, the intermediate-range attraction becomes feeble (\(< 0.25\ \text{MeV}\)) and the short-range repulsion more than doubles in magnitude.
* The strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s\) and the III strength \(W\) depend on \(r_0\) and \(b\) via expressions such as \(\alpha_s = C_1 m_q^2 (r_0^2 + b^2)^{3/2} (M_\Delta - M_N)_{\text{OGEP}}\); \(\alpha_s\) exceeds 1 for large \(r_0\).
* The constituent quark mass \(m_q\) was varied from \(150\ \text{MeV}\) to \(350\ \text{MeV}\); as \(m_q\) decreases, short-range repulsion increases and intermediate-range attraction decreases.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier NRQM study (Shastry and Kumar, 2016) that examined the roles of OGEP, III, and OPEP in the NN adiabatic potential, by now incorporating the finite size of constituent quarks via smeared contact interactions. It builds on the suggestion by Stanley and Robson (1980) that the rms radius of constituent quarks must be about \(0.15\ \text{fm}\) to reproduce the pion rms radius, and on the argument by Lutz and Weise (1990) that constituent quarks are not point-like due to quark-antiquark polarization. The results enable future calculations of NN scattering phase shifts and cross sections to quantitatively evaluate the impact of quark finite size, and they indicate that the III cannot be replaced by simply introducing a quark size parameter.
