# Evidence for a narrow $D_{03}$ state in $K^- p \rightarrow ηΛ$ near threshold

> Recently, we reported a theoretical study on the $K^- p \to ηΛ$ reaction near threshold by using an effective Lagrangian approach. It was found that the description of angular distribution data measured by the Crystal Ball Collaboration needs a $D_{03}$ resonance with mass $M=1668.5\pm 0.5$ MeV and total decay width $Γ=1.5\pm 0.5$ MeV, which is not the conventional $Λ$(1690) or other $Λ$ state listed in the Particle Data Group book. In the present work, we study the $Λ$ polarization in the $K^- p \to ηΛ$ reaction within the same framework. The results show that the existence of this narrow $D_{03}$ state is also compatible with current $Λ$ polarization data and that the more accurate $Λ$ polarization data at $P_{K^-}=735$MeV can offer further evidence for the existence of this resonance. Furthermore, the role of the $Λ(1690)$ resonance in this reaction is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the angular distribution data for the reaction \(K^- p \to \eta \Lambda\) near threshold require a narrow \(D_{03}\) resonance with mass \(M=1668.5\pm 0.5\) MeV and width \(\Gamma=1.5\pm 0.5\) MeV, which is distinct from the conventional \(\Lambda(1690)\) listed by the Particle Data Group. The authors show that this narrow state is also compatible with existing \(\Lambda\) polarization data, but they hedge that the polarization data have large error bars and that the model without the narrow state can also reproduce those data within uncertainties.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach to model the \(K^- p \to \eta \Lambda\) reaction, including t-channel \(K^*\) meson exchange, u-channel proton exchange, and s-channel \(\Lambda\) resonance exchange. They performed a \(\chi^2\) fit to experimental total and differential cross section data from the Crystal Ball Collaboration with eleven free parameters, including the mass, width, and coupling constants of the proposed \(D_{03}\) state and the \(\Lambda(1670)\) resonance. They then calculated the \(\Lambda\) polarization in the same framework using the fitted parameters, without additional free parameters, and compared the predictions to existing polarization data.

## Key numbers

- Mass of the narrow \(D_{03}\) resonance: \(M = 1668.5 \pm 0.5\) MeV.
- Total decay width of the narrow \(D_{03}\) resonance: \(\Gamma = 1.5 \pm 0.5\) MeV.
- Mass of the \(\Lambda(1670)\) resonance from the fit: \(M = 1672.5 \pm 1.0\) MeV.
- Total decay width of the \(\Lambda(1670)\) from the fit: \(\Gamma = 24.5 \pm 2.7\) MeV.
- Resultant \(\chi^2/\text{dof}\) for the best fit: \(0.9\).
- At \(P_{K^-} = 734\) MeV, the experimental total cross section is \(\sigma = 1.15 \pm 0.10\) mb; the model including the narrow \(D_{03}\) state gives \(1.14 \pm 0.05\) mb, while the model without it gives \(0.99 \pm 0.03\) mb.
- In Fit III (omitting angular distribution data at \(P_K = 734\) MeV), the best fit still favors a narrow resonance with \(M = 1670.2 \pm 1.6\) MeV and \(\Gamma = 4.5 \pm 1.6\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous theoretical study (Liu, 2012) of the \(K^- p \to \eta \Lambda\) reaction, which first identified the need for a narrow \(D_{03}\) state to describe angular distribution data from the Crystal Ball Collaboration (Klempt, 2010). The present paper tests that claim against \(\Lambda\) polarization data and explicitly rules out the conventional \(\Lambda(1690)\) resonance (as listed in the PDG) as the source of the observed bowl structure in the angular distributions. The results suggest that more accurate polarization data around \(P_{K^-} = 734\) MeV could provide decisive evidence for this narrow state, and the authors recommend remeasurement of both differential cross sections and \(\Lambda\) polarization.
