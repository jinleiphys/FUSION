# Semiempirical Shell Model Tabulated Masses for Translead Elements with Magic Proton Number Z = 126

> We present two tables of calculated masses of translead nuclei, for 351 nuclei with N from 94 to 126 and Z from 82 to 100, and for 1969 nuclei with N from 126 to 184 and Z from 82 to 126. The tables are calculated from a semiempirical shell-model mass equation based on Z = 126 as a proton magic number which seems to be highly extrapolatable inside shell regions. Useful separation and decay energies are given as well. Some properties of the calculated masses and applications to superheavy elements are indicated in the introduction.

## Key claim

The paper presents calculated masses for translead nuclei based on a semiempirical shell-model mass equation (SSME) that adopts Z = 126 as the proton magic number beyond lead, rather than Z = 114 used in earlier work; the authors note that the agreement with data obtained with Z = 126 is suggestive but not a proof of superior magicity compared to Z = 120 or 124, because no comparative mass studies of this kind were made.

## Method

The authors constructed a semiempirical shell-model mass equation (SSME) where the total nuclear ground-state energy is written as a sum of pairing, deformation, and Coulomb energies, with separate parameter sets for two shell regions: region A (82 ≤ N, Z ≤ 126) and region B (126 ≤ N ≤ 184, 82 ≤ Z ≤ 126). The coefficients were determined by least-squares adjustment to experimental masses and Q-values, with region A requiring a partial readjustment of four parameters (α, ε, Θ, κ) while the other seven were held fixed at their 1973 values. The resulting atomic mass excesses and separation/decay energies are tabulated for 351 nuclei in region A and 1969 nuclei in region B.

## Key numbers

- Region B: 15 adjustable parameters; overall average deviation of 13 keV and rms deviation of 156 keV from 267 known masses; corresponding -5 keV and 178 keV from 231 known Qα values
- Region A: 11 adjustable parameters; overall average deviation of 2 keV and rms deviation of 246 keV from 150 known masses; 2 keV and 99 keV from 109 known Qα values
- For the 56 new masses in region B measured after 1973: average deviation 53 keV, rms deviation 236 keV for ΔM; excluding N=126-128 nuclei gives -1 keV and 155 keV
- For the 121 new masses in region A after 1973: average deviation 48 keV, rms deviation 216 keV for ΔM (after readjustment of four coefficients)
- Discontinuity along N=126 boundary reaches 1.3 MeV in 227Md, the heaviest N=126 isotone included
- For the 293118 decay chain: predicted Qα values show δav = -197 keV and δrms = 308 keV from observed values; largest deviation of -735 keV for 293118 itself

## Context

This work extends the earlier SSME table of Liran and Zeldes (1976), which used Z=114 as the proton magic number, to Z=126 based on recent experimental results on superheavy elements (Ninov et al., 1999) where the 293118 decay chain showed smooth α-decay energies that seemed to preclude Z=114 as a major proton magic number. The authors note that the SSME with Z=114 became unsuitable for extrapolation beyond Hs (Z=108), while the Z=126 version agreed rather well with the new data. The tables are intended as a substitute for the earlier SSME in the interior of both shell regions, particularly for the extrapolatable-proven Qα values, and are applied to four recent superheavy element experiments including those of Ninov et al. (1999), Oganessian et al. (1999), Wilk et al. (2000), and Oganessian et al. (2001).
