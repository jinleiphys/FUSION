# The Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule and the single-pion photoproduction multipole E0+ close to threshold

> The long-standing discrepancy between the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule and the analysis of pion photoproduction multipoles is greatly diminished by use of s-wave multipoles that are in accord with the predictions of chiral perturbation theory and describe the experimental data in the threshold region. The remaining difference may be due to contributions of channels with more pions and/or heavier mesons whose contributions to the sum rule remain to be investigated by a direct measurement of the photoabsorption cross sections.

## Key claim

The long-standing discrepancy between the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule and the analysis of pion photoproduction multipoles is greatly diminished (from 38% to 28%) by using s-wave multipoles that agree with chiral perturbation theory and describe experimental data in the threshold region, but the remaining difference may be due to contributions from channels with more pions or heavier mesons that remain to be investigated.

## Method

The authors compared two analyses of single-pion photoproduction multipoles: the VPI-SAID program (version SP97K) and the Hanstein-Drechsel-Tiator (HDT) approach, which imposes constraints from fixed-t dispersion relations and unitarity with ten free parameters fitted to photoproduction data for photon energies of 160-420 MeV. They evaluated the integrand of the GDH sum rule for the proton up to 500 MeV using both sets of multipoles, focusing on the E0+ and M1+ contributions, and computed the resulting integral values including estimates for two-pion contributions from Karliner (1993).

## Key numbers

- The HDT threshold value of the amplitude E0+(n pi+) is 28.4 x 10^{-3}/m_pi+, compared to 24.9 x 10^{-3}/m_pi+ in the SAID analysis (SP97K), and 28.3 +/- 0.2 x 10^{-3}/m_pi+ from an older experiment
- Using SAID multipoles for single-pion production gives I_p(1 pi) = -216 microbarn; with Karliner's two-pion estimate of -65 microbarn, the total I_p = -281 microbarn, matching the GDH value of -281 microbarn
- Using HDT multipoles gives I_p(1 pi) = -196 microbarn, and with the two-pion contribution the total I_p = -261 microbarn, reducing the discrepancy from 38% to 28%
- For the neutron, the change in multipoles leads to a change of 17 microbarn

## Context

This work extends the analyses of Karliner (1993), Workman and Arndt (1992), Burkert and Li (1993), Sandorfi, Whisnant and Khandaker (1994), and Arndt, Strakovsky and Workman (1996), which found an apparent discrepancy for the isovector-isoscalar component of the GDH sum rule. The authors show that using the HDT multipoles, which agree with chiral perturbation theory predictions, substantially reduces this discrepancy. The remaining difference motivates direct measurements of photoabsorption cross sections at MAMI and ELSA to investigate contributions from two-pion and heavier meson channels.
