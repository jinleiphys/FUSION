# Strangeness in the Nucleon on the Light-Cone

> Strange matrix elements of the nucleon are calculated within the light-cone formulation of the meson cloud model. The $Q^2$ dependence of the strange vector and axial vector form factors is computed, and the strangeness radius and magnetic moment extracted, both of which are found to be very small and slightly negative. Within the same framework one finds a small but non-zero excess of the antistrange distribution over the strange at large $x$. Kaon loops are unlikely, however, to be the source of a large polarized strange quark distribution.

# Strangeness in the Nucleon on the Light-Cone

## Key claim

Strange matrix elements of the nucleon calculated within the light-cone formulation of the meson cloud model yield very small and slightly negative strangeness radius and magnetic moment, but the magnitude depends strongly on the cut-off mass parameter of the kaon-hyperon vertex function; kaon loops alone cannot explain a large polarized strange quark distribution.

## Method

The authors expand the physical nucleon state on the light-cone as a superposition of a bare nucleon and two-particle kaon-hyperon (K Lambda and K Sigma) Fock states, using a monopole-type vertex function dependent on the invariant mass squared of the kaon-hyperon system. They compute the strange vector and axial vector form factors from the kaon loop diagrams in the Breit frame, and extract the strangeness radius, strange magnetic moment, and strange axial charge. The same framework is used to calculate the deep-inelastic strange and antistrange quark distributions via convolution of the light-cone splitting functions with intrinsic kaon and hyperon structure functions.

## Key numbers

- Strangeness (Sachs) radius: r_s^2 ranges from approximately -0.004 fm^2 for Lambda_{K Lambda} = 0.7 GeV to approximately -0.008 fm^2 for Lambda_{K Lambda} = 1.3 GeV
- Strange Dirac radius: r_{s, Dirac}^2 ranges from approximately -0.0007 fm^2 to -0.0012 fm^2 for the same cut-off range
- Strange magnetic moment: mu_s ranges from approximately -0.04 to -0.1 for cut-offs between 0.7 and 1.3 GeV
- Strange axial charge ratio: eta_s = G_A^s(0)/g_A ranges between approximately 0.0017 and 0.002 for cut-offs between 0.7 and 1.3 GeV, with positive sign
- Average kaon number in the nucleon: <n>_{K Lambda} approximately 3-7% for cut-off masses between 0.7 and 1.3 GeV
- Coupling constants used: g_{KN Lambda} = -13.98, g_{KN Sigma} = 2.69
- Masses used: M = 939 MeV, M_Lambda = 1116 MeV, M_Sigma = 1190 MeV, m_K = 494 MeV

## Context

This work extends the meson cloud model approach of Signal and Thomas (1990s) and earlier instant-form calculations by Holzwarth (1990s), Musolf and Burkardt (1990s), and others, by providing the first light-cone analysis of strange nucleon form factors within the same framework used for structure functions. The authors address the proposal by Tang and Ji (1990s) that the strangeness radius and deep-inelastic strange quark distributions might be correlated, finding that the kaon cloud model predicts a very small excess of antistrange over strange at large x, consistent within experimental errors with the CCFR Collaboration data. The results enable future comparison with parity-violating electron scattering experiments at MIT-Bates and Jefferson Lab, and demonstrate that consistent treatment of vertex functions on the light-cone is essential for meaningful comparison of form factors and parton distributions.
