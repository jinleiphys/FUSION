# Properties of the Resonance Lambda(1520) as seen in the Forward Electroproduction at JLab Hall A

> High-resolution spectrometer measurements of the reaction H(e,e' K+)X at small Q2 are used to extract the mass and width of the Lambda(1520). We investigate dependence of the resonance parameters on different parametrizations of the background and the resonance peak itself. Our final values for the Breit-Wigner parameters are M=1520.4+-0.6(stat)+-1.5(syst) MeV and Gamma=18.6+-1.9(stat)+-1(syst) MeV. The width appears to be more sensitive to the assumptions than the mass. We also estimate, for the first time, the pole position for this resonance and find that both the pole mass and width seem to be smaller than their Breit-Wigner values.

## Key claim

The Breit-Wigner mass and width of the Lambda(1520) extracted from forward electroproduction at JLab Hall A are M=1520.4±0.6(stat)±1.5(syst) MeV and Gamma=18.6±1.9(stat)±1(syst) MeV, but the width is more sensitive than the mass to the choice of background parametrization and fitting procedure; the pole mass and width are estimated for the first time and appear smaller than the Breit-Wigner values.

## Method

The reaction H(e,e' K+)X was measured at small Q2 using the High Resolution Spectrometers in JLab Hall A with a 5.09 GeV electron beam on a liquid hydrogen target, covering missing-mass spectra from 1.45 to 1.65 GeV with 4 MeV binning. The missing-mass distribution was fitted with a Breit-Wigner resonance term for the Lambda(1520) plus a background that included a linear term and a tail from the Lambda(1405), using both least-squares (min-chi2) and log-likelihood methods, with relativistic and non-relativistic forms and with energy-dependent or energy-independent widths. The pole position was extracted by solving the denominator equation of the relativistic Breit-Wigner form.

## Key numbers

- Breit-Wigner mass: M = 1520.4 ± 0.6 (stat) ± 1.5 (syst) MeV
- Breit-Wigner width: Gamma = 18.6 ± 1.9 (stat) ± 1 (syst) MeV
- Pole mass: M_p = 1518.8 MeV
- Pole width: Gamma_p = 17.2 MeV
- Average momentum transfer: <Q2> ≈ 0.1 (GeV/c)^2
- Average center-of-mass photon energy: <E_cm_gamma*> = 1.1 GeV, corresponding to <W> = 2.53 GeV
- Missing-mass resolution: sigma = 1.5 MeV
- Number of events in the fitted mass interval (1.45–1.65 GeV): 13,070

## Context

This work updates the mass and width of the Lambda(1520), for which all experimental inputs in the Review of Particle Physics (PDG) prior to this paper dated from before 1980, and it is the first to extract the pole position for this hyperon resonance. The results are consistent with previous bubble-chamber and partial-wave analysis values but show that systematic uncertainties from fitting assumptions (especially background treatment) are comparable to statistical uncertainties, enabling future studies of the resonance's nature (e.g., possible molecular structure) and improved hyperon spectroscopy.
