# Anatomy of octupole correlations in $^{96}$Zr with a symmetry-restored multidimensionally-constrained covariant density functional theory

> A recent analysis based on the STAR measurement in relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments provides evidence of octupole correlation in the ground state of $^{96}$Zr, the description of which presents a challenge to nuclear structure models. In this work, we perform projection-after-variation calculations for $^{96}$Zr based on a multidimensionally-constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model. Our results show that an octupole deformed shape is favored in energy after symmetry restoration, and this phenomenon cannot be reproduced in the pure mean-field calculations. These complex structures originate from the competition among various shell structures in this mass region. Our results suggest that the allowance of symmetry breaking at the mean-field level and the restoration of broken symmetries are essential elements for understanding the structure of $^{96}$Zr in density functional theories.

## Key claim

The paper claims that an octupole-deformed shape is energetically favored in the ground state of $^{96}$Zr only after angular momentum and parity projection are applied to the mean-field wave function; pure mean-field calculations without symmetry restoration fail to predict octupole deformation, reconciling a long-standing discrepancy between theory and experiment.

## Method

The authors performed projection-after-variation (PAV) calculations using the multidimensionally-constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (MDCRHB) model with the PC-PK1 energy density functional and a separable pairing force of finite range. They computed one-dimensional and two-dimensional potential energy surfaces (PESs) for $^{96}$Zr by constraining various combinations of quadrupole ($\beta_{20}$, $\beta_{22}$) and octupole ($\beta_{30}$, $\beta_{32}$) deformation parameters, then projected the mean-field states onto good angular momentum $J$ and parity $\pi$ to obtain the projected PESs for $0^+$, $2^+$, $4^+$, $1^-$, and $3^-$ states.

## Key numbers

* The projected $0^+$ energy minimum is located at $(\beta_{20},\beta_{30}) = (0.01, 0.23)$ in the $K\slashed{\pi}$ (axial but reflection-asymmetric) calculation, with a projection energy gain of $E_{\rm proj} = 8.53$ MeV relative to the mean-field minimum.
* The calculated $B(E3;3^- \rightarrow 0^+)$ is $20.68 \times 10^3~{\rm e}^2\,{\rm fm}^6$, comparable to the experimental value of $23(2) \times 10^3~{\rm e}^2\,{\rm fm}^6$ from Iskra (2019).
* The excitation energies from the $\slashed{K}\slashed{\pi}$ (non-axial and reflection-asymmetric) calculation are $E_x(2^+) = 3.06$ MeV, $E_x(4^+) = 4.59$ MeV, $E_x(3^-) = 4.98$ MeV, and $E_x(1^-) = 6.13$ MeV, compared to experimental values of 1.75, 2.75, 1.90, and 4.07 MeV, respectively.
* The ground-state binding energy from the $\slashed{K}\slashed{\pi}$ calculation is $-834.546$ MeV, while the experimental binding energy is $-828.99$ MeV.

## Context

This work addresses a long-standing discrepancy where systematic mean-field calculations using the macroscopic-microscopic model of Moeller (2016), the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory of Agbemava (2016), and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theories with Gogny and Skyrme interactions (Robledo 2011, Cao 2020) all failed to find octupole deformation in $^{96}$Zr, despite experimental evidence from spectroscopy and recent STAR heavy-ion collision data (Zhang 2022). The results demonstrate that beyond-mean-field effects, specifically symmetry restoration via projection, are essential for describing octupole correlations in this nucleus. The work enables future generator-coordinate-method calculations that include shape mixing to provide a more complete description of the low-lying collective states and octupole vibrational bands.
