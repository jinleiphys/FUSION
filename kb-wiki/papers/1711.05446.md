# Perturbative accelerating solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics

> In ultra-relativistic collisions of heavy ions, the strongly interacting Quark Gluon Plasma (sQGP) is created. The fluid nature of the sQGP was one of the important discoveries of high energy heavy ion physics in the last decades. Henceforth the explosion of this matter may be described by hydrodynamical models. Besides numerical simulations, it is important to study the analytic solutions of the equations of hydrodynamics, as these enable us to understand the connection of the final and initial states better. In this paper we present a perturbative, accelerating solution of relativistic hydrodynamics, on top of a known class of solutions describing Hubble-expansion. We describe the properties of this class of perturbative solutions, and investigate a few selected solutions in detail.

## Key claim

The paper presents a perturbative method to construct accelerating solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics on top of a known class of Hubble-expanding solutions, but the method breaks down when perturbations become larger than the original fields at very small radial distances and large perturbation parameter values, limiting applicability.

## Method

The authors linearize the relativistic perfect-fluid hydrodynamics equations (energy-momentum conservation, continuity, and Euler equation) around a known Hubble-flow solution with flow field \(u^\mu = x^\mu / \tau\) and pressure \(p = p_0 (\tau_0 / \tau)^{3 + 3/\kappa}\). They introduce small perturbations \(\delta u^\mu\), \(\delta p\), and \(\delta n\) and derive first-order equations that yield a class of perturbative solutions expressed in terms of scale functions \(\chi(S)\), \(\pi(S)\), \(\nu(S)\) and profile functions \(F(\tau)\), \(g(x^\mu)\), \(h(x^\mu)\), subject to condition equations ensuring consistency. A specific sub-class is solved explicitly for the scale variable \(S = r^m / t^m\) with \(m = -1\) and a Gaussian profile \(\mathcal{N}(S) = e^{-b S^{-2}}\).

## Key numbers

- Initial thermalization time is \(\mathcal{O}(1\ {\rm fm}/c)\) and evolution lasts \(\mathcal{O}(10\ {\rm fm}/c)\) for the sQGP created in heavy ion collisions.
- The Hubble-flow pressure scales as \(p = p_0 (\tau_0 / \tau)^{3 + 3/\kappa}\) and density as \(n = n_0 (\tau_0 / \tau)^3 \mathcal{N}(S)\).
- For visualization, parameters are chosen as \(\tau_0 = 7.7\ {\rm fm}/c\), \(\kappa = 10\), and \(b = -0.1\).
- The perturbation parameter \(\delta = 0.001\) and \(c = -3\) are used in the example figures.
- The speed of sound is \(c_s = 1 / \sqrt{\kappa}\) from the wave equation \(\partial_t^2 \delta p - (1/\kappa) \Delta \delta p = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the ellipsoidal Hubble-flow solution of Csorgo et al. (2003) by adding acceleration via perturbations, addressing the limitation that the original solution lacks acceleration despite describing final-state observables well. It contrasts with the approach of Shi et al. (2014), who studied ripples propagating on Hubble-flow, by instead constructing global perturbations in \(\delta u^\mu\), \(\delta p\), and \(\delta n\). The results enable future calculation of observables such as transverse momentum distributions, flow, and Bose-Einstein correlation radii from the perturbed fields, with planned comparison to measurements from RHIC and LHC.
