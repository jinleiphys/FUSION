# Cluster Separability in Relativistic Few Body Problems

> A convenient framework for dealing with hadron structure and hadronic physics in the few-GeV energy range is relativistic quantum mechanics. Unlike relativistic quantum field theory, one deals with a fixed, or at least restricted number of degrees of freedom while maintaining relativistic invariance. For systems of interacting particles this is achieved by means of the, so called, "Bakamjian-Thomas construction", which is a systematic procedure for implementing interaction terms in the generators of the Poincare group such that their algebra is preserved. Doing relativistic quantum mechanics in this way one, however, faces a problem connected with the physical requirement of cluster separability as soon as one has more than two interacting particles. Cluster separability, or sometimes also termed "macroscopic causality", is the property that if a system is subdivided into subsystems which are then separated by a sufficiently large spacelike distance, these subsystems should behave independently. In the present contribution we discuss the problem of cluster separability and sketch the procedure to resolve it.

## Key claim

The Bakamjian-Thomas (BT) construction, which is the only known systematic procedure for implementing interactions in Poincaré generators while preserving the algebra, violates cluster separability for systems with more than two interacting particles; the paper sketches a formal resolution via "packing operators" but notes that practical applicability depends on the ability to calculate these operators for a particular system.

## Method

The authors review the BT construction in the point form of relativistic dynamics, where the four-momentum operator is factorized into a free four-velocity operator and a mass operator that contains interactions. They then demonstrate that for a three-particle system with pairwise interactions, the BT-type mass operator fails to satisfy cluster separability because it conserves the overall three-particle velocity rather than the velocity of the interacting two-particle subsystem. The resolution follows Sokolov (1978) by introducing unitary "packing operators" that transform the four-velocity operators of each 2+1-body subsystem to share a common free three-particle velocity, thereby restoring both Poincaré invariance and cluster separability in a generalized BT construction.

## Key numbers

- The spurious form factor \(g\) for pseudoscalar mesons (e.g., B mesons) vanishes for large Mandelstam-\(s\), and the physical form factor \(f\) becomes negligibly \(s\)-dependent in this limit, motivating extraction of the physical form factor as \(F(Q^2) = \lim_{s\to\infty} f(Q^2,s)\).
- The harmonic-oscillator wave function used in the B meson calculation had parameters: oscillator strength \(a = 0.55\) GeV, bottom quark mass \(m_b = 4.8\) GeV, light quark mass \(m_{u,d} = 0.25\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the foundational BT construction (Bakamjian and Thomas, 1953) and addresses the cluster separability problem identified by Mutze (1978) and Keister and Polyzou (1991). The authors connect the formal issue to observable unphysical features in electromagnetic currents of bound states, as previously seen in point-form calculations of meson form factors (Biernat et al., 2009; Gomez Rocha and Schweiger, 2012; Biernat et al., 2014) and in front-form calculations (Keister, 2011). The packing-operator method of Sokolov (1978) is presented as the formal solution, and the authors plan to apply it to cure the unphysical current contributions in future work.
