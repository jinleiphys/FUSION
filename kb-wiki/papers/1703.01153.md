# Nucleon structure functions from lattice operator product expansion

> Deep-inelastic scattering, in the laboratory and on the lattice, is most instructive for understanding how the nucleon is built from quarks and gluons. The long-term goal is to compute the associated structure functions from first principles. So far this has been limited to model calculations. In this Letter we propose a new method to compute the structure functions directly from the virtual, all-encompassing Compton amplitude, utilizing the operator product expansion. This overcomes issues of renormalization and operator mixing, which so far have hindered lattice calculations of power corrections and higher moments.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new lattice QCD method to compute nucleon deep-inelastic structure functions directly from the forward Compton amplitude using the operator product expansion (OPE), claiming this approach overcomes the renormalization and operator mixing issues that have previously limited lattice calculations of power corrections and higher moments.

## Method

The method computes the forward Compton amplitude \(T_{33}(p,q)\) from the second derivative of the nucleon energy with respect to a background field coupling, using a Feynman-Hellmann technique on the lattice. The Compton amplitude is then inverted via either a Vandermonde matrix fit to extract the moments of the structure function, or via singular value decomposition (SVD) to reconstruct the full \(x\)-dependence of \(F_1(x,q^2)\). The approach is demonstrated using a phenomenological parameterization of the nonsinglet structure function \(F_1^{u-d}(x)\) as a test case, with a proof-of-principle lattice calculation on a \(32^3\times 64\) lattice at \(a\approx 0.074\,\text{fm}\).

## Key numbers

* The test calculation uses the LO parton distributions at scale \(q^2=1\,\text{GeV}^2\) from Martin et al. (2009) to generate synthetic data.
* The proof-of-principle lattice calculation uses \(O(900)\) configurations at the SU(3) symmetric point on a \(32^3\times 64\) lattice with lattice spacing \(a\approx 0.074\,\text{fm}\).
* The Compton amplitude \(T_{33}\) is computed for \(\omega\) values from 0.1 to 0.9 in steps of 0.1 for the test case.
* The SVD reconstruction of \(F_1^{u-d}(x)\) uses \(M=19\) discrete \(x\) points and 9 \(\omega\) values, with 3 of 9 eigenvalues found to be zero.
* The projected overall error on the Compton amplitude is estimated at \(10\%\), based on the lattice results shown in Fig. 6.
* The first three moments \(\mu_1, \mu_3, \mu_5\) are accurately reproduced from five data points using a fifth-order polynomial fit.

## Context

This work extends the "OPE without OPE" approach previously discussed by Martinelli et al. (1998) and builds on earlier lattice calculations of nucleon structure at the parton level (dislat). The method enables direct computation of structure functions including higher-twist power corrections, which were previously inaccessible due to operator mixing issues identified by Martinelli et al. (1996). The approach can be generalized to nonforward Compton scattering for generalized parton distribution functions (GPDs) and to odd moments via axial vector currents.
