# Zeroing in on the initial state -- tomography using bulk, jets and photons

> One of the unsolved problems in the current 'standard model' of heavy ion physics is the apparent rapid thermalization of QCD matter in the pre-equilibrium stage. While it is challenging to probe this mechanism directly, there are now several observables available which allow tomographic imaging of the initial state geometry, which is expected to carry remnant information of the equilibration mechanism. On the fluid dynamics side, scaled fluctuations in the momentum space anisotropy parameters v_n image the initial eccentricity fluctuations epsilon_n almost directly with only a weak dependence on the details of the fluid dynamical evolution. From a different direction, due to the strong non-linear dependence of their emission rates on temperature, thermal photons and their v_n are very sensitive to the initial state graininess. Finally, the v_2 and v_3 of high P_T hadrons coming from hard processes reflect the attenuation pattern of partons propagating through the inhomogeneous matter density after some fluid dynamical evolution. Combining information from all these channels does not yet lead to a fully consistent picture, however intriguing trends pointing towards non-trivial initial state dynamics emerge.

## Key claim

Combining multiple tomographic probes (bulk flow harmonics, high-\(p_T\) hadron \(v_n\), and thermal photon \(v_n\)) does not yet yield a fully consistent picture of the initial state of heavy-ion collisions, but the observed trends point toward non-trivial initial-state dynamics such as primordial flow and sub-nucleon degrees of freedom.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic scan of a large parameter space of initial-state models (varying colliding degrees of freedom, entropy production mechanisms, and fluctuation sources) using approximate scaling relations that reduce the computational cost of event-by-event fluid dynamics from hours to seconds. They compared the resulting predictions for bulk \(v_n\) fluctuations, high-\(p_T\) hadron \(v_2\) from jet quenching (using the YaJEM-DE, ASW, and AdS models), and thermal photon \(v_n\) against experimental data from 200 AGeV Au-Au collisions at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- For 30-40% central 200 AGeV Au-Au collisions, the relative enhancement of high-\(p_T\) charged hadron \(v_2\) due to a near-\(T_C\) enhancement of jet quenching is about 20% (1.17 for ASW, 1.22 for YaJEM-DE 3d, 1.20 for YaJEM-DE 2d), independent of the parton-medium interaction model.
- For the same system, the relative enhancement of high-\(p_T\) \(v_2\) due to viscous entropy production over an ideal inviscid baseline is about 50% (1.51 for ASW, 1.44 for AdS, 1.55 for YaJEM-DE), again independent of the jet quenching model.
- The geometric size of the jet-medium interaction region is the single most important factor determining the magnitude of high-\(p_T\) \(v_2\); other factors include formation time of a thermalized medium (50% relative importance), viscous entropy production (35%), and differences in the density profile (15%).
- The scaling relation \(\langle v_n \rangle \approx C_n \epsilon_n\) holds for individual events, and constructing \(\delta v_2 = (v_2 - \langle v_2 \rangle)/\langle v_2 \rangle\) allows direct comparison of final-state \(P(v_2)\) to initial-state \(P(\epsilon_2)\).

## Context

This work extends the event-by-event scaling approach of the authors' earlier study (Renk et al., 2014) and the systematic analysis of bulk \(v_n\) fluctuations (Renk et al., 2014). It contrasts with the large-scale statistical analysis of Novak et al. (2014) by using approximate scaling relations to rapidly scan parameter space. The photon \(v_2\) puzzle, where standard hydrodynamics underestimates the data, is connected to the possibility of primordial flow before thermalization (Fries et al., 2008). The results enable future work to constrain the equilibration time \(\tau_0\), shear viscosity \(\eta/s\), and near-\(T_C\) enhancement of jet quenching simultaneously, though no unambiguous determination is yet possible.
