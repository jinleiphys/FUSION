# Truncation Method for the Shell Model Calculation

> A method of truncating the large shell model basis is outlined. It relies on the order given by the unperturbed energies of the basis states and on the constancy of their spreading widths. Both quantities can be calculated by a simple averaging procedure. The method is tested in the sd shell where the JT dimensions are of the order of a few thousand. It proves to be very effective in the middle of the fp shell where JT dimensions of the order of a few million are truncated to a few thousand.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a truncation method for shell model calculations that selects basis states based on their unperturbed energies and the near-constancy of their spreading widths, claiming that with less than 30% of the full basis dimension one can obtain more than 90% overlap with the exact ground state wave function in the sd shell, and that the method should allow energy calculations to within less than one MeV accuracy for ground states and low-lying excited states in even larger model spaces.

## Method

The method orders basis states by their unperturbed energies (diagonal Hamiltonian matrix elements) and uses the empirical observation that the spreading widths σk are nearly constant across all basis states. The authors retain only those partitions whose average centroids (calculated via m-scheme averaging formulas from French and Ratcliff) are lower than a cutoff value Hcutoff = (Ēk)min + 3σ̄, where σ̄ is the average spreading width. This selection is performed before constructing the full Hamiltonian matrix, avoiding the most time-consuming part of shell model calculations.

## Key numbers

- In the sd shell with 10 particles (0+1 states), the full JT dimension is 1132; truncating to dimension 310 (about 27% of full space) yields eigenvalues approaching exact values
- For the sd shell test, σ̄ = 9.7 MeV and Hcutoff = -66.5 MeV
- With less than 30% of the full dimension, overlaps of more than 90% with the exact ground state wave function are obtained
- For 54Fe (14 particles in the fp shell, 0+1 states), the full JT dimension is 2,229,178 and the m-scheme dimension is 345,400,274; σ̄ = 8 MeV and Hcutoff = -143.6 MeV
- For 14N with (0+2+4)ħω excitations, the full JT dimension is 19,498; truncation to one-fifth of this space reproduces the 0+1 yrast state energy within 400 keV

## Context

This work extends the truncation approach of Hasp (1993) by providing a quantitative criterion based on spreading widths rather than a fixed percentage of configurations, and by allowing selection of relevant partitions before basis state construction rather than after. The method is tested against exact results in the sd shell using the Wildenthal interaction and against Monte Carlo calculations for 54Fe using the Brown-Richter interaction (Koonin and collaborators, 1993). The approach enables shell model calculations in the middle of the fp shell where JT dimensions of millions can be truncated to thousands, and may also be applicable to Monte Carlo methods such as stochastic diagonalization.
