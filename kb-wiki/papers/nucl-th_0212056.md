# The influence of asymmetry on a magnetized proto-neutron star

> Using the Relativistic Mean Field Theory (RMF) it is shown that different proton fraction which is directly connected with the neutron excess and with the asymmetry of the system affects proto-neutron stars parameters and changes their composition. The obtained form of the equation of state allows to construct the mass-radius relations and shows that the increasing asymmetry creates more compact stars. The inclusion of delta meson together with nonlinear vector meson interaction terms and magnetic field make this effect even stronger.

## Key claim

The paper shows that increasing proton-neutron asymmetry (lower proton fraction) in proto-neutron star matter creates more compact stars, and that this effect is strengthened by the inclusion of the delta meson, nonlinear vector meson interaction terms, and a magnetic field of moderate strength (B ~ 10^3 B_c^e), but only within the limits of a spherical symmetric approximation that neglects deformation.

## Method

The authors use Relativistic Mean Field Theory (RMF) with a Lagrangian including nucleons, leptons (electrons, muons, neutrinos), and scalar, vector, and isovector mesons (sigma, omega, rho, delta) plus nonlinear vector meson interactions. They construct the equation of state for hot (T = 20 MeV), magnetized (B ~ 10^3 B_c^e) proto-neutron star matter under conditions of beta equilibrium, charge neutrality, and lepton number conservation with trapped neutrinos, using three parameter sets (TM1, TM1+nonl., GM3). The equation of state is then used as input to the Oppenheimer-Volkoff-Tolman equations to obtain mass-radius relations for different proton fractions.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strength: B ~ 10^3 B_c^e, where B_c^e = 4.414 × 10^13 G (the critical magnetic field for an electron)
- Temperature fixed at T = 20 MeV
- Proton fractions studied: Y_p = 0.34, 0.22, and 0.11
- Nuclear saturation density n_S = 0.1455 fm^-3 for TM1 and TM1+nonl. parameter sets; n_S = 0.153 fm^-3 for GM3
- Binding energy at saturation: E_b = -16.3 MeV for all three parameter sets
- Symmetry energy a_sym = 36.82 MeV (TM1), 32.22 MeV (TM1+nonl.), 32.5 MeV (GM3)
- Incompressibility K_0 = 281.99 MeV (TM1 and TM1+nonl.), 240 MeV (GM3)
- Effective nucleon mass ratio m_eff/M = 0.658 (TM1), 0.664 (TM1+nonl.), 0.78 (GM3)
- Theoretical prediction for total lepton number at trapping: Y_L ~ 0.35-0.4
- Proto-neutron star matter proton fraction range: Y_p ~ 0.3-0.36 (with trapped neutrinos); neutron star matter: Y_p ~ 0.1

## Context

This work extends prior studies of dense matter in strong magnetic fields for white dwarfs, proto-neutron stars, and neutron stars (Strobel et al. 1999, Bombaci et al. 1995, Takatsuka 1995, Manka et al. 2001) by specifically examining the role of proton fraction (asymmetry) in magnetized proto-neutron stars. The inclusion of the delta meson and nonlinear vector meson interactions builds on the TM1 and GM3 parameterizations from Osa (2000) and Lattimer (2000). The results enable more realistic modeling of proto-neutron star interiors by showing how asymmetry, magnetic fields, and nonlinear meson couplings together determine compactness, and they suggest that the spherical symmetric approximation used here limits the density range where the model is valid, pointing toward the need for fully anisotropic calculations as done by Konno et al. for strongly magnetized stars.
