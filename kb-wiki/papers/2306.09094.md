# Universality of energy-momentum response in kinetic theories

> We study the response of the energy-momentum tensor in several kinetic theories, from the simple relaxation time approximation (RTA) to Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). Irrespective of the differences in microscopic properties, we find a remarkable degree of universality in the response functions from conformal theories. We find that the response to scalar perturbations in kinetic theory can be effectively described by a pair of one hydrodynamic sound mode and one non-hydrodynamic mode. We find that even beyond the range of validity of hydrodynamics, the energy-momentum response in position space can be effectively described by one single mode with non-trivial dispersion relation and residue.

## Key claim

The energy-momentum response functions in conformal kinetic theories exhibit a remarkable degree of universality when expressed in terms of scaling variables based on the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, and this universality extends well beyond the hydrodynamic regime into transient early-time and large-gradient regions where hydrodynamics is invalid.

## Method

The authors solved the linearized Boltzmann equation for scalar perturbations in four conformal kinetic theories: relaxation time approximation (RTA), scalar phi^4 theory (SCL), SU(3) Yang-Mills gauge theory (YM), and QCD with N_f=3 flavors. They computed the energy response function G_s^s(k,t) from the time evolution of linearized temperature perturbations at various wave-numbers k, then extracted effective hydrodynamic sound modes and non-hydrodynamic modes by fitting the late-time and early-time behavior of the response functions respectively.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratios used: RTA with tau_R T = 5 gives eta/s = 1.00; SCL with scalar coupling lambda = 83.25 gives eta/s = 1.00; YM with 't Hooft coupling lambda = 10 gives eta/s = 0.62; QCD with 't Hooft coupling lambda = 10 gives eta/s = 1.00
- The hydrodynamic sound mode dispersion relation at first order is Re(omega_s(k))/k = c_s = 1/sqrt(3) and -Im(omega_s(k)) = Gamma k^2 = 2k^2/3 in scaled units
- The second-order BRSSS hydrodynamic dispersion relation uses tau_pi = 5.1-5.2 in scaled units
- The extraction window for sound modes used t_s in [2pi, 6pi] and for non-hydrodynamic modes used t_n in [0, 0.8pi]
- The position space Fourier transform used Gaussian smearing with sigma = 0.01

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hydrodynamic emergence in kinetic theories by Jeon (1995), Romatschke (2015), Heller (2016), Kurkela (2017), and Jaiswal (2021), as well as holographic studies by Policastro (2002), Baier (2007), and Heller (2007). The universal scaling behavior demonstrated here enables construction of effective hydrodynamic descriptions that remain valid beyond the gradient expansion, and the extracted sound mode parameters provide guidance for developing new hydrodynamic effective theories that interpolate between the hydrodynamic and free-streaming limits.
