# Optimized delta expansion for relativistic nuclear models

> The optimized $δ$-expansion is a nonperturbative approach for field theoretic models which combines the techniques of perturbation theory and the variational principle. This technique is discussed in the $λϕ^4$ model and then implemented in the Walecka model for the equation of state of nuclear matter. The results obtained with the $δ$ expansion are compared with those obtained with the traditional mean field, relativistic Hartree and Hartree-Fock approximations.

## Key claim

The optimized delta expansion, when applied to the Walecka model at zeroth order in delta, reproduces the relativistic Hartree-Fock results for nuclear matter binding energy without requiring self-consistent equations, provided exchange diagrams are included and the principle of minimal sensitivity is applied to the energy density.

## Method

The authors implement the linear delta expansion by introducing an artificial parameter delta into the Walecka model Lagrangian, interpolating between a soluble free theory (with an arbitrary nucleon mass parameter M0 = M + mu) and the full interacting theory. They compute the nuclear matter energy density using full propagators expressed in terms of the zeroth-order nucleon propagator, then apply the principle of minimal sensitivity (PMS) by minimizing the energy density with respect to the arbitrary mass parameter mu. The calculation is performed at zeroth order in delta (using only the free propagator) and at second order in delta (including self-energy corrections), with vacuum contributions neglected throughout.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constants used: g_s^2 = 91.64 and g_v^2 = 136.2
- Masses used: M = 939 MeV (nucleon), m_v = 783 MeV (vector meson), m_sigma = 550 MeV (scalar meson)
- The self-consistent Hartree-Fock equations do not converge for Fermi momenta larger than P_F ~ 1.7 fm^{-1}
- The PMS solution for the mass parameter at P_F = 1.19 fm^{-1} is mu/M = -0.275 (without exchange terms) and mu/M = -0.35 (with exchange terms at second order)

## Context

This work extends the optimized delta expansion method, previously applied to quantum mechanics and the lambda phi^4 model, to the Walecka model (Walecka, 1974) for nuclear matter. The results are compared with traditional mean-field (Hartree), relativistic Hartree, and Hartree-Fock approximations (Horowitz and Serot, 1981). The method provides a simpler numerical alternative to self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculations, enabling future inclusion of vertex corrections and vacuum effects that are difficult to handle in traditional approaches.
