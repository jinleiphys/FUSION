# Open charm meson in nuclear matter at finite temperature beyond the zero range approximation

> The properties of open charm mesons, $D$, $\bar D$, $D_s$ and $\bar D_s$ in nuclear matter at finite temperature are studied within a self-consistent coupled-channel approach. The interaction of the low lying pseudoscalar mesons with the ground state baryons in the charm sector is derived from a $t$-channel vector-exchange model. The in-medium scattering amplitudes are obtained by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation at finite temperature including Pauli blocking effects, as well as $D$, $\bar D$, $D_s$ and $\bar D_s$ self-energies taking their mutual influence into account. We find that the in-medium properties of the $D$ meson are affected by the $D_s$-meson self-energy through the intermediate $D_s Y$ loops coupled to $DN$ states. Similarly, dressing the $\bar{D}$ meson in the $\bar{D}Y$ loops has an influence over the properties of the $\bar{D}_s$ meson.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the simultaneous self-consistent dressing of both the $D$ and $D_s$ mesons (or both the $\bar{D}$ and $\bar{D}_s$ mesons) in nuclear matter produces non-negligible changes to their in-medium properties compared to dressing only one meson, with the $\Lambda_c(2595)$ resonance appearing approximately 35 MeV higher in energy when the $D_s$ meson is also dressed.

## Method

The authors solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation at finite temperature for meson-baryon scattering in the charm sector, using a $t$-channel vector-exchange model as the driving force with the full $t$-dependence (beyond the zero-range approximation). Medium effects are incorporated through Pauli blocking via Fermi-Dirac distributions and by self-consistently dressing the $D$, $\bar{D}$, $D_s$, and $\bar{D}_s$ meson propagators with their in-medium self-energies, which are obtained by integrating the in-medium scattering amplitudes over the nucleon Fermi sea.

## Key numbers

- The cut-off parameter $\Lambda$ is set to 903 MeV/c to reproduce the position of the $\Lambda_c(2595)$ resonance.
- The $\Lambda_c(2595)$ resonance appears at 2595 MeV with a width of 0.5 MeV in free space.
- When only Pauli blocking is included, the $\Lambda_c(2595)$ and $\Sigma_c(2804)$ states are displaced to higher energies by about 60 MeV and 50 MeV, respectively.
- The simultaneous dressing of $D$ and $D_s$ mesons makes the $\Lambda_c(2595)$ appear 35 MeV higher in energy compared to dressing only the $D$ meson.
- The $D$-meson quasiparticle peak is located around 35 MeV below the free $D$-meson mass at normal nuclear matter saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-1}$ and zero temperature.

## Context

This work extends previous coupled-channel studies of open-charm mesons in nuclear matter by Tolos et al. (2005, 2007, 2009) and Mizutani and Rapp (2006), which employed the zero-range approximation ($t \rightarrow 0$), by using the full $t$-dependent vector-exchange interaction kernel developed in Jimenez-Tejero et al. (2009). The results differ from those of Lutz and Korpa (2005), where the $\Sigma_c$ resonance was found at a much lower energy (around 2620 MeV) and the $D$-meson quasiparticle peak experienced a repulsive shift of 32 MeV. The simultaneous dressing of $D$ and $D_s$ mesons is addressed for the first time in this work, enabling more accurate predictions for charmonium production in hot dense matter and the formation of $D$-mesic nuclei.
