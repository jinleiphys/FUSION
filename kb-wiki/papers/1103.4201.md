# Probing pretzelosity $h^{\perp}_{1T}$ via the polarized proton-antiproton Drell-Yan process

> We show that the polarized proton-antiproton Drell-Yan process is ideal to probe the pretzelosity distribution ($h_{1T}^\perp$), which is one of the new transverse-momentum-dependent parton distributions of the nucleon. We present predictions of the $\cos(2ϕ+ ϕ_a - ϕ_b)$ asymmetry in the transversely polarized proton-antiproton Drell-Yan process at PAX kinematics and find that the results are significantly larger compared with those of the $\sin(3ϕ_h - ϕ_S)$ asymmetry in the semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering process at HERMES, COMPASS, and JLab kinematics. We conclude that the $\cos(2ϕ+ ϕ_a - ϕ_b)$ asymmetry in the PAX experiment can probe the new physical quantity of the pretzelosity distribution.

# Probing pretzelosity $h^{\perp}_{1T}$ via the polarized proton-antiproton Drell-Yan process

## Key claim
The $\cos(2\phi + \phi_a - \phi_b)$ asymmetry in transversely polarized proton-antiproton Drell-Yan scattering at PAX kinematics provides a feasible probe of the pretzelosity distribution $h_{1T}^\perp$, with predicted asymmetries significantly larger than those of the $\sin(3\phi_h - \phi_S)$ asymmetry in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering at HERMES, COMPASS, and JLab kinematics, though the numerical predictions depend on the choice of model (light-cone quark-diquark model versus CTEQ6L parametrization with Gaussian transverse momentum factor).

## Method
The authors compute the double-spin-dependent differential cross section for the Drell-Yan process with both beams transversely polarized, isolating the structure function $F_{TT}^{\cos(2\phi + \phi_a - \phi_b)}$ that involves a convolution of the pretzelosity distribution $h_{1T}^\perp$ with the transversity distribution $h_1$. They evaluate the weighted and unweighted $\cos(2\phi + \phi_a - \phi_b)$ asymmetries at PAX kinematics ($s = 45~\mathrm{GeV}^2$, $Q^2 = 12~\mathrm{GeV}^2$) using two approaches: a light-cone quark-diquark model with explicit wave functions, and a hybrid approach using CTEQ6L unpolarized parton distribution functions with a Gaussian transverse momentum dependence and model relations connecting pretzelosity to unpolarized distributions.

## Key numbers
- The $\cos(2\phi - \phi_a - \phi_b)$ asymmetry (transversity probe) at $s = 45~\mathrm{GeV}^2$ and $Q^2 = 12~\mathrm{GeV}^2$ is comparable in magnitude to results from Efremov (2004) for transversity measurement.
- The weighted $\cos(2\phi + \phi_a - \phi_b)$ asymmetry at $s = 45~\mathrm{GeV}^2$ and $Q^2 = 12~\mathrm{GeV}^2$ is shown as a function of $x_F$ for two approaches (dashed and solid curves in Fig. 2).
- The unweighted $\cos(2\phi + \phi_a - \phi_b)$ asymmetry at $s = 45~\mathrm{GeV}^2$ and $Q^2 = 12~\mathrm{GeV}^2$ is shown as a function of $x_F$ for two approaches (dashed and solid curves in Fig. 3).
- The Gaussian transverse momentum width used in approach 2 is $k_{av}^2 = 0.25~\mathrm{GeV}^2$.
- The light-cone quark-diquark model parameters are: $\alpha_D = 0.33~\mathrm{GeV}$, $m_q = 0.33~\mathrm{GeV}$, $m_S = 0.60~\mathrm{GeV}$, $m_V = 0.80~\mathrm{GeV}$.
- The $\sin(3\phi_h - \phi_S)$ asymmetry in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering at HERMES, COMPASS, and JLab kinematics is up to $1\%$, or up to $4\%$ with a transverse momentum cut (Ma 2009, Boffi 2009).

## Context
This work extends the study of pretzelosity distributions from semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (where asymmetries are small, up to $1\%$ as predicted by Ma (2009) and Boffi (2009)) to the polarized proton-antiproton Drell-Yan process proposed by the PAX Collaboration (2005, 2010). The paper builds on model relations connecting pretzelosity to helicity and transversity distributions (Avakian 2008, Pasquini 2008, Ma 2009, Efremov 2009) and to quark orbital angular momentum (Ma 2009, Avakian 2010). The results suggest that the PAX experiment, originally designed for transversity measurements, can simultaneously probe pretzelosity distributions with larger asymmetries, enabling discrimination between different model predictions through the different $k_T^2$ dependence of the weighted versus unweighted asymmetries.
