# Photon production from gluon splitting and fusion induced by a magnetic field in heavy-ion collisions

> In heavy-ion collisions, an excess in photon production, together with a larger than expected positive elliptic flow, has been observed, a phenomenon commonly referred to as the direct photon puzzle. In this work we study the mechanism of photon production arising from gluon splitting and fusion during the pre-equilibrium stage in the presence of magnetic fields in peripheral heavy-ion collisions. We begin by analyzing the general tensor structure of the two-gluon one-photon vertex, computing it at the one-loop level for magnetic fields of arbitrary strength without resorting to additional approximations. Using these expressions, we calculate the contribution of gluon fusion and splitting to the photon yield, revealing that splitting dominates over fusion at low photon energies. Our results are compared with experimental data from the PHENIX collaboration. Finally, we incorporate a longitudinal anisotropy into the initial gluon distribution and find that it does not significantly alter the photon yield compared to an isotropic distribution.

# Photon production from gluon splitting and fusion induced by a magnetic field in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
Gluon splitting and fusion in the pre-equilibrium stage of peripheral heavy-ion collisions, computed with a full one-loop two-gluon one-photon vertex for magnetic fields of arbitrary strength, can account for part of the excess photon yield observed by PHENIX; however, the calculation shows that splitting dominates over fusion only at low photon energies, and the inclusion of a longitudinal anisotropy in the initial gluon distribution does not significantly alter the yield compared to an isotropic distribution.

## Method
The authors computed the two-gluon one-photon vertex at one-loop order in the presence of a constant magnetic field of arbitrary strength, using Schwinger's proper-time representation for fermion propagators and the Ritus basis for polarization vectors. They then used this vertex to calculate the invariant momentum distribution of photons from gluon fusion (gg → γ) and gluon splitting (g → gγ) processes, employing an isotropic Bose-Einstein gluon distribution from the shattered glasma and comparing the results to PHENIX data after subtracting state-of-the-art hydrodynamical calculations from Gale (2021). They also tested two anisotropic gluon distributions, one with a longitudinal anisotropy coefficient and one from a color-glass condensate-inspired model used by Kurkela (2015) and Garcia-Montero (2023).

## Key numbers
- Magnetic field strengths considered: B = 3 m_π^2 and B = 10 m_π^2, where m_π is the pion mass.
- Peak magnetic field intensities at RHIC energies are as high as B ∼ 10^19 G, citing Skokov (2009) and STAR (2019).
- The gluon distribution uses a saturation momentum scale Λ_s and a high occupation factor η; the saturation scale controls the curvature of the yield.
- The anisotropic distribution of Kurkela (2015) uses a normalization constant A = 5.34 and a mean transverse momentum ⟨p_T⟩.
- The comparison is made for the [20–30]% centrality class from PHENIX data (PHENIX, 2022).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of gluon-induced photon production in magnetic fields (Ayala 2017, Ayala 2019, Ayala 2022, Ayala 2024) by computing the two-gluon one-photon vertex for arbitrary magnetic field strength without the strong-field approximation used earlier, and by actually computing the photon yield rather than just the vertex structure. It addresses the direct photon puzzle, the excess yield and large elliptic flow of direct photons observed by PHENIX (2011), by providing a pre-equilibrium mechanism that does not rely on thermal flow properties. The results are compared to hydrodynamical calculations by Gale (2021) and to earlier strong-field approximations by Ayala (2017). The work enables future computation of the elliptic flow v_2 from these processes, which the authors state is ongoing.
