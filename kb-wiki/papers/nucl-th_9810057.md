# The triple-alpha process and its anthropic significance

> Through the triple-alpha process practically all of the carbon in our universe is synthesized as the ash of helium burning in red giants. The triple-alpha process proceeds trough the ground state of 8Be and though the 02+-state in 12C. We investigate the dependence of 02+-state and the production of carbon as a function of the strength of the underlying nucleon-nucleon interaction. This is performed by using the complex scaling method in a microscopic cluster model.

# The triple-alpha process and its anthropic significance

## Key claim

The production of carbon via the triple-alpha process is exponentially sensitive to small variations (on the order of 0.1%) in the strength of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, with changes of about 4 orders of magnitude in the reaction rate at 10^8 K for a 0.2% change in interaction strength, though the exact enhancement or reduction factor depends on which effective NN-interaction model is used.

## Method

The authors used a microscopic three-cluster (alpha + alpha + alpha) resonating group model to solve the 12-nucleon Schrodinger equation, employing the complex scaling method to determine the three-body resonance energies of the 0^+_2 state in 12C. They varied the strength of all attractive and repulsive terms of three different effective nucleon-nucleon interactions (Minnesota, Volkov 1, and Volkov 2) by a multiplicative factor p, and calculated the resulting change in the triple-alpha reaction rate at a temperature of 10^8 K.

## Key numbers

- The modern experimental parameters of the 0^+_2 resonance in 12C are epsilon = 0.3796 MeV and Gamma = 8.5 x 10^-6 MeV.
- For a 0.2% strengthening of the NN-interaction (p = 1.002), the reaction rate enhancement factor f_p ranges from 64.4 (V2 force) to 422 (MN force) at 10^8 K.
- For a 0.2% weakening of the NN-interaction (p = 0.998), the reaction rate reduction factor f_p ranges from 0.003 (MN force) to 0.02 (V2 force) at 10^8 K.
- A lowering of the 0^+_2 resonance energy by about 60 keV, corresponding to a 0.2-0.4% strengthening of the NN-interaction, would lead to about a fourfold increase of carbon production in a 20-solar-mass star.
- An increase of the 0^+_2 state by about 60 keV, corresponding to a 0.2-0.4% weakening of the NN-interaction, would decrease the 12C abundance in a 20-solar-mass star by roughly a factor of two to three.
- If the 0^+_2 level is increased by about 650 keV, corresponding to about a 2-4% weakening of the NN-interaction, then practically no more carbon could be produced in core and shell helium burning.

## Context

This work extends the earlier investigation by Livio, Hollowell, Weiss, and Truran (1989), who studied hypothetical ad hoc shifts of the 0^+_2 resonance energy in stars of 20 solar masses and 5 solar masses, by instead starting from variations of the underlying nucleon-nucleon interaction. The results enable quantitative constraints on how precisely the strong nuclear force must be tuned for sufficient carbon production to occur in stellar nucleosynthesis, providing a physically grounded basis for anthropic arguments about the fine-tuning of fundamental constants.
