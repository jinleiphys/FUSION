# Effective field theory for the two-nucleon system

> I discuss the dynamics of the two-nucleon system as obtained from a chiral nucleon-nucleon potential. This potential is based on a modified Weinberg power counting and contains chiral one- and two-pion exchange as well as four-nucleon contact interactions. The description of the S-waves is very precise. Higher partial waves are also well reproduced. We also find a good description of most of the deuteron properties.

## Key claim

A modified Weinberg power-counting scheme for the chiral nucleon-nucleon potential, when carried to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), can reproduce two-nucleon observables with precision comparable to modern phenomenological potentials, contrary to earlier claims that the approach could only give qualitative insight.

## Method

The irreducible chiral NN potential was constructed to third order in small momenta (NNLO) including one-pion exchange (OPE), two-pion exchange (TPE) with insertions from the dimension-one and dimension-two pion-nucleon Lagrangian, and four-nucleon contact interactions with zero and two derivatives. The potential was regularized with a sharp or exponential cutoff and iterated in a Lippmann-Schwinger equation to obtain bound and scattering states. The nine low-energy constants associated with the four-nucleon contact terms were fixed by fitting the S-waves, P-waves, and the mixing parameter epsilon_1 for nucleon laboratory energies below 100 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: 2.1650 MeV at NLO and 2.2239 MeV at NNLO (empirical value 2.224575(9) MeV), reproduced within 2 percent at NLO and 1 permille at NNLO without fine tuning.
- For the triplet S-wave (3S1) at NNLO: scattering length a = 5.424 fm, effective range r = 1.741 fm, shape parameters v2 = 0.046 fm^3, v3 = 0.67 fm^5.
- For the singlet S-wave (1S0) at NNLO: scattering length a = -23.72 fm, effective range r = 2.68 fm, shape parameters v2 = -0.61 fm^3, v3 = 5.1 fm^5.
- Deuteron quadrupole moment: 0.266 fm^2 at NLO, 0.261 fm^2 at NNLO (empirical 0.2859(3) fm^2).
- Deuteron asymptotic D/S ratio eta: 0.025 at both NLO and NNLO (empirical 0.0256(4)).
- Deuteron D-state probability: 3.8 percent at NLO, 6.5 percent at NNLO.
- Cutoff values used: Lambda = 500 MeV at NLO and 875 MeV at NNLO for global fits; for deuteron properties, exponential regulator with Lambda = 600 MeV (NLO) and 1.05 GeV (NNLO).

## Context

This work extends the Weinberg (1990) power-counting approach for the NN interaction, which earlier numerical studies at NNLO by the author and collaborators had found insufficiently precise to compete with modern potentials or phase shift analysis. The results improve upon that earlier work and demonstrate that a suitably modified Weinberg scheme can become a precision tool. The approach contrasts with the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) scheme, which treats pion exchange perturbatively and is shown here to work well only at low momenta below about 100 MeV. The results enable systematic extension to few-nucleon systems and to coupling with external fields.
