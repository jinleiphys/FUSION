# Consistent Treatment of Propagator Modifications in Elastic Nucleon-Nucleus Scattering within the Spectator Expansion

> The theory of the elastic scattering of a nucleon from a nucleus is presented in the form of a Spectator Expansion of the optical potential. Particular attention is paid to the treatment of the free projectile$\,-\,$nucleus propagator when the coupling of the struck target nucleon to the residual target must be taken into consideration. First order calculations within this framework are shown for neutron total cross-sections and for proton scattering for a number of target nuclides at a variety of energies. The calculated values of these observables are in very good agreement with measurement.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including the coupling of the struck target nucleon to the residual nucleus (via a mean-field potential in the propagator) significantly improves agreement with experimental data for elastic nucleon-nucleus scattering, but this correction becomes negligible above 300 MeV projectile energy.

## Method

The authors implement a first-order Spectator Expansion of the optical potential, where the free projectile-nucleus propagator is modified by replacing the target Hamiltonian with a mean-field potential representing the coupling of the struck nucleon to the residual nucleus. The first-order optical potential is constructed in momentum space using the free NN t-matrix from the full Bonn potential, with the effective NN t-matrix folded with nuclear densities in the "optimum factorized" form, and spin degrees of freedom are included. Two different mean-field potentials are used for the propagator modification: one from a Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculation using the Gogny D1S interaction (HFB) and one from a Dirac-Hartree calculation based on the sigma-omega model (DH).

## Key numbers

- Neutron total cross sections calculated for targets 12C, 16O, 28Si, 40Ca, 90Zr, and 208Pb at energies from 65 to 400 MeV
- The "local free tau-rho" approximation overestimates total cross sections by 25-30% for heavier elements compared to the full calculation
- Propagator modification effects are most prevalent between 100 and 200 MeV projectile energy and become "almost negligible" at 300 and 400 MeV
- Statistical error bars on neutron total cross section data are approximately 1%
- Calculations are performed for incident projectile energies ranging from 65 to 400 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Spectator Expansion approach developed by Watson, Kerman-McManus-Thaler (KMT), and earlier work by the authors (Chinn, Elster, Thaler, Weppner) to include a consistent treatment of the many-body propagator. The paper contrasts its full first-order calculations with the simpler "local free tau-rho" approximation that was previously standard, showing that approximation fails significantly. The results enable future work on second-order corrections (three-body type equations) and provide a foundation for microscopic scattering theory needed for high-energy coincidence experiments at CEBAF and reactions at radioactive beam facilities.
