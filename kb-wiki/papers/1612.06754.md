# Rapidity-dependent jet energy loss in small systems with finite-size effects and running coupling

> Longitudinal dynamics of particle production and rapidity-dependent jet energy loss are investigated in small and asymmetric colliding systems. We utilize an improved version of \textsc{martini} in which two improvements are implemented to calculate the effect of the strongly coupled QGP droplet on jet energy loss. We show that those realistic prescriptions improve the results of nuclear modification factor calculations. We also observe visible energy loss of jets in a thermal background of high-multiplicity p-Pb collisions, and a clear correlation between the energy loss and elliptic flow coefficients for energetic particles. We conclude that systematic measurements of jet quenching in central collisions of small systems can support the formation of the QGP droplet.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including finite formation time effects and a running coupling constant in the MARTINI Monte-Carlo event generator produces visible jet energy loss in high-multiplicity p-Pb collisions, with the caveat that the nuclear modification factor R_pPb is predicted to be around 0.8-0.9, not a dramatic suppression.

## Method

The authors implemented two improvements to the MARTINI framework: a finite formation time for inelastic radiation using a light-cone path integral formalism reformulated in momentum space, and a running coupling constant with the renormalization scale set to the average momentum transfer between mother and daughter partons. They coupled this improved MARTINI with full 3+1 dimensional hydrodynamic backgrounds from MUSIC using Monte-Carlo Glauber initial conditions, and simulated jet evolution in p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV using CTEQ6L parton distribution functions and EPS09LP nuclear PDFs.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear modification factor R_pPb predicted to be around 0.8-0.9 in 0-5% p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV
- Running coupling shows a 20% reduction in interaction strength at parton momentum p = 100 GeV
- Elliptic flow coefficient v2{SP} for energetic charged hadrons (10 < pT < 50 GeV) shows values of 1-3% depending on rapidity interval in 0-5% p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV
- The paper shows R_pPb as a function of rapidity at 10 GeV and 50 GeV in 0-5% p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV

## Context

This work extends the MARTINI framework (Schenke, 2009) by incorporating finite-size effects and running coupling that were identified as essential for small systems by Shen (2016). The results contradict the expectation that jet quenching would be negligible in small collision systems, and instead predict sizeable energy loss that correlates with elliptic flow coefficients. The findings enable experimental searches for QGP droplets in high-multiplicity p-Pb collisions at LHC energies, with the authors noting that more in-depth analyses on medium structure and jet energy distribution are in progress.
