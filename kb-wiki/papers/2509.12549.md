# A weak entanglement approximation for nuclear structure: review and recent developments

> The nuclear shell model is a useful and widely used tool for nuclear structure, but it can be hampered by the exponential growth of the basis. Drawing inspiration from quantum information theory, one can show that the proton and neutron components are typically weakly entangled. This has led to the Proton And Neutron Approximate Shell-model (PANASh). I review the underlying ideas and present recent developments. In particular I show how PANASh can accelerate beyond-mean-field methods such as the generator coordinate method.

## Key claim

The proton and neutron components of nuclear shell-model wave functions are typically weakly entangled, which justifies truncating the basis to only low-lying eigenstates of the separate proton and neutron Hamiltonians, yielding a practical approximation (PANASh) that can also accelerate beyond-mean-field methods such as the generator coordinate method.

## Method

The author partitions the nuclear shell-model Hamiltonian into separate proton, neutron, and proton-neutron parts, then solves for eigenstates of the proton and neutron Hamiltonians independently. These eigenstates are used to construct a coupled J-scheme basis, truncated by excitation energy in each component space, and the full Hamiltonian matrix (including the proton-neutron coupling via one-body transition density matrices) is diagonalized in this reduced space. The approach is also adapted to the generator coordinate method by separately projecting and diagonalizing proton and neutron Slater determinants from reference states, then recoupling them using the PANASh code.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁸Cr in the pf valence space with GXPF1A interaction: entanglement entropy of 2.84 out of a maximum of 8.48
- For ⁶⁰Cr in the pf valence space with GXPF1A interaction: entanglement entropy of 1.84 out of a maximum of 8.48
- For ¹³⁰Ba in the 50-82 valence space with GCN5082 interaction: PANASh ground state energy of -346.8 MeV versus truncated shell-model ground state energy of -344.3 MeV (2.6 MeV lower)
- For ¹³⁰Ba PANASh calculation: 500 proton component states and 1000 neutron component states (approximately 6 MeV excitation in each space), yielding a 0⁺ J-scheme dimension of 24,793
- For ¹³⁰Ba truncated shell-model calculation: M-scheme dimension of 760 million, equivalent to J-scheme dimension for 0⁺ states of about 5.4 million
- For ⁶⁰Zn in the pf valence space with GXPF1A interaction: full configuration interaction M-scheme dimension of slightly more than two billion

## Context

This work extends the weak entanglement approximation introduced by Johnson (2023) and formalized in Physical Review C (2024), which showed that proton-neutron partitioning yields the lowest entanglement entropy compared to other basis partitions (Perez 2023). The PANASh approach contrasts with earlier iterative basis optimization methods by Papenbrock (2003, 2004, 2005) by using a fixed "good enough" basis rather than an optimized one. The method enables shell-model calculations for systems with dimensions far beyond the current tractable limit of approximately 10¹⁰-10¹¹ M-scheme states (McCoy 2024, Dao 2025), and shows promise for improving generator coordinate method calculations (Ring 2004) with minimal additional computational cost.
