# Electric dipole strength in $sd$-shell nuclei from small-angle proton scattering

> The present work reports new total photoabsorption cross sections for the $N=Z$ nuclei in the $sd$-shell $^{20}$Ne, $^{24}$Mg, $^{28}$Si, $^{32}$S, $^{36}$Ar, and for $^{26}$Mg. The results are compared to predictions of a data-driven artificial neural network application and to configuration-interaction shell-model calculations covering the excitation energy region of the isovector giant dipole resonance. Double-differential cross sections of the $(p,p^\prime)$ reaction at 295 MeV have been measured between $0^\circ$ and $14^\circ$. The angular distributions of the $E1$ parts due to Coulomb excitation have been extracted with a multipole decomposition analysis for excitation energies 12 to 24 MeV and converted to equivalent photoabsorption cross sections with the virtual photon method. Reasonable agreement of the photoabsorption cross sections with previous experiments is found for $^{24}$Mg and $^{28}$Si, while the present results diverge for $^{32}$S. For the first time, data are presented for $^{20}$Ne, $^{26}$Mg and $^{36}$Ar. Configuration-interaction shell-model calculations provide an overall satisfactory description of the fragmented $E1$ strength distributions. The same holds for absolute cross sections except for $^{26}$Mg and $^{36}$Ar, where the experimental results significantly exceed the expected exhaustion of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn energy-weighted sum rule. Fot light nuclei, there is a larger model dependence compared to previous analyses in heavy nuclei, in particular for excitation energies above 20 MeV, due to the need to constrain the continuum background with additional assumptions. The overall success of the shell-model approach to describe the features of the experimental photoabsorption cross sections motivates its application in large-scale reaction network calculations aiming at an understanding of the mass composition of ultrahigh-energy cosmic rays.

## Key claim

The paper reports new total photoabsorption cross sections for sd-shell nuclei derived from forward-angle (p,p') scattering at 295 MeV, but notes that results above 20 MeV have larger model dependence than in heavy nuclei due to the need to constrain the continuum background with additional assumptions, and that for 26Mg and 36Ar the experimental cross sections significantly exceed the expected exhaustion of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn energy-weighted sum rule.

## Method

Double-differential cross sections of the (p,p') reaction at 295 MeV were measured at the RCNP cyclotron facility using the Grand Raiden magnetic spectrometer at scattering angles between 0.40 degrees and 14.0 degrees for targets of 20Ne, 24Mg, 26Mg, 28Si, 32S, 36Ar, and 40Ca. The E1 parts due to Coulomb excitation were extracted with a multipole decomposition analysis using collective form factors calculated with the distorted-wave Born approximation code DWUCK4 for excitation energies 12 to 24 MeV, and converted to equivalent photoabsorption cross sections using the virtual photon method in the eikonal approach.

## Key numbers

- Incident proton energy: 295 MeV
- Scattering angle range: 0.40 degrees to 14.0 degrees
- Excitation energy range for analysis: 12 to 24 MeV
- Energy resolution: typically 20-30 keV full width at half maximum
- Data rebinned to 250 keV for multipole decomposition analysis
- For 26Mg and 36Ar, reproducing the experimental cross sections would require an enhancement factor of about 100% relative to the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule expectation
- Root-mean-square deviations between shell-model and experimental centroids: 0.53 MeV; widths: 0.28 MeV (calculated between 12 and 20 MeV)
- Shell-model centroid for 20Ne: 18.40 MeV (experimental: 17.50 MeV); for 24Mg: 18.06 MeV (experimental: 18.15 MeV); for 28Si: 17.95 MeV (experimental: 18.41 MeV); for 32S: 17.23 MeV (experimental: 17.82 MeV); for 36Ar: 17.49 MeV (experimental: 18.01 MeV); for 26Mg: 16.49 MeV (experimental: 16.65 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the forward-angle (p,p') technique previously applied to 40Ca by Fearick (2023) and to heavier nuclei such as 208Pb by Tamii (2011) and others, now targeting lighter sd-shell nuclei where Coulomb cross sections scale with target charge while nuclear background is approximately mass-independent. The results provide new photoabsorption data for 20Ne, 26Mg, and 36Ar where no prior measurements existed, and enable tests of configuration-interaction shell-model calculations by Sieja (2023) and Le Noan (2025) that can be applied to reaction network calculations for understanding ultrahigh-energy cosmic ray composition within the PANDORA project.
