# Pair creation and plasma oscillations

> We describe aspects of particle creation in strong fields using a quantum kinetic equation with a relaxation-time approximation to the collision term. The strong electric background field is determined by solving Maxwell's equation in tandem with the Vlasov equation. Plasma oscillations appear as a result of feedback between the background field and the field generated by the particles produced. The plasma frequency depends on the strength of the initial background field and the collision frequency, and is sensitive to the necessary momentum-dependence of dressed-parton masses.

## Key claim

Plasma oscillations in strong-field particle creation are a necessary consequence of back-reaction between the background field and the field generated by produced particles, and their frequency depends on the initial field strength, collision frequency, and the momentum-dependence of dressed-parton masses; the authors use a relaxation-time approximation to the collision term in a quantum kinetic equation, which they argue is valid because the system evolves to a quasi-equilibrium state shortly after the initial collision impulse.

## Method

The authors solve a quantum Vlasov equation for the single-particle distribution function, coupled self-consistently with Maxwell's equation for the electric field, using a relaxation-time approximation for the collision term. The external field is modeled as an impulse profile \(E_{ex} = -(A_0/b)\,\text{sech}^2(t/b)\) to mimic a relativistic heavy-ion collision, and the system is solved for fermion pair production. The quasi-equilibrium distribution is defined by matching energy and momentum densities at each time step, and the dressed-quark mass is computed from a simple Dyson-Schwinger equation model to incorporate momentum-dependent masses.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density in RHIC collisions: \(\varepsilon \sim 10\text{--}100\) GeV/fm\(^3\), stated as sufficient for QGP formation.
- String tension from lattice QCD: \(\sigma \sim 4 \Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^2 \sim 1\) GeV/fm.
- Dressed-quark mass at zero momentum at the critical temperature: \(m(0,T_c) \approx 0.35\) GeV, obtained from a DSE model with current-quark mass \(m_0 = 5\) MeV and mass scale \(\eta = 1.33\) GeV.
- Critical temperature for deconfinement in the DSE model: \(T_c = 0.17\) GeV.
- Relaxation times used in calculations: \(\tau_r = 5.0\) (arbitrary units) for the momentum-dependent mass comparison; three values (not specified numerically) are compared in Fig. 3 to show damping of plasma oscillations.

## Context

This work extends the flux-tube picture of particle production via the Schwinger mechanism (Schwinger, 1951) and the back-reaction formalism (Back, 1990s) to include collisions via a relaxation-time approximation, building on prior quantum kinetic equation derivations (Rau, 1994; Bastianelli, 1995; GSI, 1996; Schmidt et al., 1998). It enables future studies of non-equilibrium QGP evolution with momentum-dependent dressed-parton masses, and the authors note that an extension to include dissipative inelastic scattering is nearly complete.
