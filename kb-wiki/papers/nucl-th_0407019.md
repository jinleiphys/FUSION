# Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov Sum-Rule for the Deuteron in Nuclear Effective Field Theory

> The Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov sum rule for the deuteron is studied in nuclear effective field theory. The low-energy theorem for the spin-dependent Compton amplitude $f_1(ω)$ is derived to the next-to-leading order in low-energy expansion. The spin-dependent photodisintegration cross section $σ^P-σ^A$ is calculated to the same order, and its contribution to the dispersive integral is evaluated.

# Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov Sum-Rule for the Deuteron in Nuclear Effective Field Theory

## Key claim

The Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov sum rule for the deuteron is verified to next-to-leading order in nuclear effective field theory, but the photodisintegration contribution to the sum rule integral is evaluated only as a crude estimate by extending the integration to infinity, since the EFT is not applicable at energies where the Delta resonance becomes important.

## Method

The authors use a pionless effective field theory for the two-nucleon system, introducing nucleon fields and a dibaryon field for the $^3S_1$ channel. They compute the spin-dependent forward Compton amplitude $f_1(\omega)$ to next-to-leading order, including relativistic spin-orbit interactions and electromagnetic counter-terms, and verify the low-energy theorem. Using the optical theorem, they then calculate the spin-dependent photodisintegration cross section $\sigma^P-\sigma^A$ for $\gamma+d\rightarrow n+p$ to the same order, with the counter-term coefficients $L_1$ and $L_2$ fixed by the $n+p$ radiative capture rate and the deuteron magnetic moment respectively.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron anomalous magnetic moment: $\kappa_D = -2 \times 0.143$, giving a left-hand side of the DHG sum rule of 0.65 $\mu$b
- Deuteron binding energy: $B = 2.225$ MeV, yielding $\gamma = \sqrt{M_N B} = 45.703$ MeV
- Counter-term $L_2$ fitted to the deuteron magnetic moment: $L_2 = -0.03$ fm
- Counter-term $L_1$ fixed by the $n+p\rightarrow d+\gamma$ cross section of $334.2 \pm 0.5$ mb at incident neutron speed 2200 m/s: $L_1 = -4.42$ fm
- EFT calculation of the photodisintegration contribution to the DHG integral (extended to infinity): $-385$ $\mu$b

## Context

This work extends the application of nuclear effective field theory to spin-dependent observables in the two-nucleon system, following the pionless EFT formulations of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) and the dibaryon field approach of Savage (1999). The results are compared to the potential model calculations of Arenhövel (2001, 2003), which classify the deuteron photodisintegration, single-pion production, and multi-pion production contributions to the DHG integral. The EFT calculation enables a systematic, model-independent treatment of the low-energy photodisintegration contribution, which can be tested by future measurements at the HIGS facility at Duke University.
