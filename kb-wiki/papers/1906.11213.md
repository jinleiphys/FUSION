# Gluon Field Digitization for Quantum Computers

> Simulations of gauge theories on quantum computers require the digitization of continuous field variables. Digitization schemes that uses the minimum amount of qubits are desirable. We present a practical scheme for digitizing $SU(3)$ gauge theories via its discrete subgroup $S(1080)$. The $S(1080)$ standard Wilson action cannot be used since a phase transition occurs as the coupling is decreased, well before the scaling regime. We proposed a modified action that allows simulations in the scaling window and carry out classical Monte Carlo calculations down to lattice spacings of order $a\approx 0.08$ fm. We compute a set of observables with sub-percent precision at multiple lattice spacings and show that the continuum extrapolated value agrees with the full $SU(3)$ results. This suggests that this digitization scheme provides sufficient precision for NISQ-era QCD simulations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the discrete subgroup S(1080) of SU(3) can reproduce continuum SU(3) gauge theory results with sub-percent precision, but only when using a modified action with an additional adjoint trace term; the standard Wilson action fails because the theory freezes at a coupling too weak to reach the scaling regime.

## Method

The authors performed classical Monte Carlo simulations of S(1080) gauge theory on lattices up to 24^4 using a modified action S = -sum_p (beta_0/3 Re Tr U_p + beta_1 Re Tr U_p^2). They mapped the phase diagram in the (beta_0, beta_1) plane using parallel tempering to overcome metastability, then chose a trajectory beta_1 = -0.1267 beta_0 + 0.253 that avoids the freezing transition. On this trajectory they computed the deconfinement temperature T_c from the Polyakov loop distribution and the Wilson flow scales t_0 and t_{0.2} for N_t = 4, 6, 8.

## Key numbers

- Freezing transition for S(1080) with Wilson action (beta_1=0) occurs at beta_c = 3.935(5) on a 2^4 lattice, larger than the previously reported value of 3.58(2)
- Along the chosen trajectory, the critical couplings for deconfinement are beta_0^c = 9.154(2) for N_t=4, 12.795(9) for N_t=6, and 19.61(4) for N_t=8
- Wilson flow scales on the trajectory: sqrt(t_0)/a = 1.016(3)(3) for N_t=4, 1.508(3)(5) for N_t=6, 2.000(4)(8) for N_t=8
- Continuum extrapolated value T_c sqrt(t_0) = 0.2489(11), agreeing with SU(3) results of 0.2489(14) from Francis (2015) and 0.2473(7) from Kitazawa (2016)
- Continuum extrapolated ratio sqrt(t_0)/sqrt(t_{0.2}) = 0.1269(6), agreeing with the SU(3) value 0.1264(4) from Asakawa (2015)
- The 24^4 lattice corresponds to lattice spacing a approx 0.08 fm (2.5 GeV^{-1})

## Context

This work extends early attempts to replace continuous SU(3) gauge fields with discrete subgroups, which were studied by Bhanot (1981), Petcher (1980), and others but found inadequate because the freezing transition occurred before the scaling regime. The authors overcome this limitation by introducing a modified action with an adjoint trace term, similar in spirit to the approach attempted with S(648) by Bhanot (1981) but now successful with the larger S(1080) group. This enables quantum simulations of QCD on NISQ-era devices, where representing each SU(3) link would require 1152 qubits versus only 11 qubits for S(1080), and opens the door to future studies of hadronic spectra, fermion inclusion, and gate-efficient actions.
