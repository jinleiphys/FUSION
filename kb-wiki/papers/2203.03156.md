# Multi-physics constraints at different densities to probe nuclear symmetry energy in hyperonic neutron stars

> The appearance of strangeness in the form of hyperons within the inner core of neutron stars is expected to affect its detectable properties such as its global structure or gravitational wave emission. In this work, we explore the parameter space of hyperonic stars within the framework of the Relativistic Mean Field model allowed by present uncertainties in state-of-the-art nuclear and hypernuclear experimental data. We impose multi-physics constraints at different density regimes to restrict the parameter space: Chiral effective field theory, heavy-ion collision data as well as multi-messenger astrophysical observations of neutron stars. We investigate possible correlations between empirical nuclear and hypernuclear parameters, particularly the symmetry energy and its slope, with observable properties of neutron stars. We do not find a correlation for the hyperon parameters and the astrophysical data. However, the inclusion of hyperons generates a tension between the astrophysical and heavy ion data constraining considerable the available parameter space.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether hyperonic neutron star properties correlate with nuclear symmetry energy parameters, finding that while hyperon parameters show no correlation with astrophysical observables, the inclusion of hyperons creates a tension between astrophysical and heavy-ion collision data that significantly constrains the available parameter space, a nuance not fully captured in the abstract's general statement about parameter space restriction.

## Method

The authors use a Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model with the full baryon octet (p, n, Λ, Σ⁻, Σ⁰, Σ⁺, Ξ⁻, Ξ⁰) plus leptons, varying nuclear saturation parameters (n₀, E_sat, K_sat, E_sym, L_sym, m*/m) and hyperon potentials (U_Λ fixed at -30 MeV, U_Σ varied 0 to +30 MeV, U_Ξ varied -30 to 0 MeV) within experimental uncertainties. They apply a "cut-off filter" scheme imposing constraints from chiral effective field theory (χEFT) at low densities (0.5-1.4 n₀), heavy-ion collision data (KaoS, FOPI, ASY-EOS) at intermediate densities (1-3 n₀), and multi-messenger astrophysical observations (PSR J0740+6620 mass, GW170817 tidal deformability) at high densities, then compute Pearson correlation coefficients among parameters and neutron star observables.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density n₀ varied in range 0.14-0.17 fm⁻³
- Symmetry energy E_sym varied in range 28-34 MeV
- Slope of symmetry energy L_sym varied in range 40-70 MeV
- Effective mass m*/m varied in range 0.55-0.75
- U_Λ fixed at -30 MeV; U_Σ varied 0 to +30 MeV; U_Ξ varied -30 to 0 MeV
- Maximum neutron star mass constraint: ≥ 2.08⁺⁰·⁰⁷₋₀·₀₇ M_⊙ from PSR J0740+6620
- Effective tidal deformability constraint: Λ̃ < 720 from GW170817
- χEFT constraints applied in density range n_b/n₀ ~ 0.5-1.4
- Filtered hyperonic EoSs yield neutron star radii spanning 11-14 km
- Correlation coefficient between n₀ and m*/m: 0.71
- Correlation coefficient between E_sym and L_sym: 0.79
- Correlation coefficient between L_sym and R_1.4M_⊙: approximately 0.4
- Correlation coefficient between n₀ and 1.4 M_⊙ observables: 0.54

## Context

This work extends the purely nucleonic investigation of Ghosh et al. (2022) by consistently including hyperons within a multi-physics constraint scheme, addressing a gap where previous Bayesian analyses (such as those by Pang et al. 2021, Biswas et al. 2021, or Dietrich et al. 2020) did not incorporate strangeness degrees of freedom. The finding that hyperon parameters show no correlation with astrophysical observables, combined with the tension between astrophysical and heavy-ion data, enables future studies to focus on resolving this tension through improved hypernuclear experimental data or refined theoretical treatments of hyperon-nucleon interactions.
