# Collision term dependence of the hadronic shear viscosity and diffusion coefficients

> In this work the shear viscosity $η$ and the diffusion coefficients of conserved charges $κ_{ij}$ with $i,j\in\{B,Q,S\}$ of hadronic matter are investigated within the hadronic transport approach SMASH. We systematically study the effect of multi-particle reactions, angular distributions and additional elastic cross sections via the additive quark model description, the numbers of degrees of freedom and the baryon density on the transport coefficients using the Green-Kubo formalism. We find that multi-particle reactions decrease the shear viscosity in a simplified hadron gas whereas the electric charge diffusion coefficient is not modified. Furthermore, additional elastic cross sections have a strong impact on both $η$ and $κ_{ij}$ whereas anisotropic scatterings enhance the shear viscosity in the full hadron gas. When increasing the number of degrees of freedom the shear viscosity is only slightly modified in comparison to the diffusion coefficients. Finally, the calculation within a finite baryon chemical potential reveals that the shear viscosity itself does not depend on $μ_B$ but on the ratio $η/ s$. The diffusion coefficients show a strong dependency which we compare to Chapman-Enskog calculations.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity and diffusion coefficients of hadronic matter depend strongly on the specific treatment of the collision term, with multi-particle reactions decreasing the shear viscosity in a simplified hadron gas while not modifying the electric charge diffusion coefficient, and with additional elastic cross sections from the additive quark model having a strong impact on both transport coefficients.

## Method

The shear viscosity η and diffusion coefficients κ_{ij} (with i,j ∈ {B,Q,S}) were extracted from infinite matter simulations of the hadronic transport approach SMASH using the Green-Kubo formalism, where transport coefficients are obtained by numerically integrating auto-correlation functions of the energy-momentum tensor and charge currents. The collision term dependence was systematically studied by switching individual interaction types on and off, including multi-particle reactions (3↔1 processes), angular distributions in binary scatterings, and additional elastic cross sections via the additive quark model (AQM) description. The results were compared to Chapman-Enskog calculations and previous SMASH results.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity η increases by approximately 30% at T = 175 MeV when using binary reaction chains instead of direct multi-particle (3↔1) reactions in a π, ρ, ω gas.
- The electric charge diffusion coefficient κ_{QQ} shows no modification from multi-particle reactions in the π, ρ, ω gas across the temperature range studied.
- AQM cross sections drastically decrease the shear viscosity over entropy density ratio η/s compared to the case without AQM cross sections in the full SMASH hadron gas.
- All diffusion coefficients except κ_{QQ} show a monotonic increase with temperature, with AQM cross sections significantly decreasing the diffusion coefficients at higher temperatures where baryonic degrees of freedom dominate.
- The integral for transport coefficients is evaluated until a relative error of 5% is reached, with a time step size of Δt = 0.05 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of shear viscosity in SMASH (Rose 2017) and electric charge diffusion coefficients (Rose 2020) by systematically studying the collision term dependence, including the first investigation of multi-particle reactions on transport coefficients in the hadronic medium. The results complement Chapman-Enskog calculations of the full diffusion coefficient matrix (Fotakis 2019, Greif 2017) and provide input for hydrodynamical simulations of heavy-ion collisions, where the shear viscosity over entropy density ratio has been found to be close to the Kovtun-Son-Starinets (KSS) bound of 1/4π. The findings enable better understanding of how microscopic interaction treatments affect transport coefficients when comparing model results to first-principle lattice QCD calculations of electric conductivity and diffusion coefficients.
