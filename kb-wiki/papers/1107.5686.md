# Transverse in-plane flow: a new probe of symmetry energy in Fermi energy region

> We study the sensitivity of transverse flow towards the different density dependence of symmetry energy in Fermi energy region. Our results show that transverse flow shows sensitivity to different density dependence of symmetry energy. The mechanism for sensitivity towards different density dependence of symmetry energy is also discussed.

# Transverse in-plane flow: a new probe of symmetry energy in Fermi energy region

## Key claim

The transverse in-plane flow of nucleons in the Fermi energy region is sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy, but only for certain functional forms: the flow becomes positive for symmetry energies proportional to ρ or ρ^0.4, while it remains negative for a ρ^2 dependence or when no symmetry energy is included, indicating that the probe distinguishes stiff from soft density dependencies only under specific conditions.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin-dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate thousands of events for the neutron-rich system ^48Ca + ^48Ca at beam energies of 100, 400, and 800 MeV/nucleon at impact parameters b/b_max = 0.2–0.4. They calculated the time evolution of the directed transverse momentum <p_x^dir> for nucleons separated into two density bins: those with ρ/ρ_0 < 1 (bin 1, low density) and those with ρ/ρ_0 ≥ 1 (bin 2, high density), using four different forms of the symmetry energy: proportional to ρ, ρ^0.4, ρ^2, and a case without symmetry energy.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy at normal nuclear matter density is E_sym(ρ_0) = 32 MeV.
- Simulations were performed for ^48Ca + ^48Ca at beam energies of 100, 400, and 800 MeV/nucleon.
- The impact parameter range used was b/b_max = 0.2–0.4.
- For symmetry energies proportional to ρ and ρ^0.4, the total <p_x^dir> becomes positive during the reaction; for the ρ^2 dependence and the case without symmetry energy, it remains negative throughout.
- For the ρ and ρ^0.4 cases, particles in bin 1 (ρ/ρ_0 < 1) reach a peak positive <p_x^dir> at about 15 fm/c, then decrease to zero at approximately 20–25 fm/c.
- For the ρ^2 case and the no-symmetry-energy case, particles in bin 1 are accelerated toward the overlap zone for a longer time interval of about 20 fm/c (between 10–30 fm/c), compared to only about 5 fm/c for the ρ and ρ^0.4 cases.

## Context

This work extends studies of the nuclear symmetry energy by proposing transverse in-plane flow as a new observable in the Fermi energy region, complementing prior work on collective flow and subthreshold kaon production that constrained the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter (Zhang, 2005). The results show that the density dependence of the symmetry energy can be distinguished through the sign and magnitude of the directed transverse momentum, which could be tested at existing and upcoming radioactive ion beam facilities. The study enables future experimental searches for the density dependence of the symmetry energy using flow observables in neutron-rich collisions at intermediate energies.
