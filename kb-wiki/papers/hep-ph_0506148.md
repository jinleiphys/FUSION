# Study of the Fundamental Structure of Matter with an Electron-Ion Collider

> We present an overview of the scientific opportunities that would be offered by a high-energy electron-ion collider. We discuss the relevant physics of polarized and unpolarized electron-proton collisions and of electron-nucleus collisions. We also describe the current accelerator and detector plans for a future electron-ion collider.

## Key claim

A high-energy electron-ion collider (EIC) would enable a transformative program in QCD by providing a lepton probe with center-of-mass energies up to ~100 GeV and luminosities up to ~10³³ cm⁻² s⁻¹, but the specific design parameters and achievable luminosities depend on the accelerator configuration (ring-ring vs. linac-ring) and on the availability of upgrades such as electron cooling, with the highest luminosities requiring significant technical development beyond present capabilities.

## Method

The paper reviews the scientific case and technical design for an electron-ion collider, focusing on the eRHIC proposal at Brookhaven National Laboratory which would use the existing RHIC facility. Two main accelerator designs are described: a ring-ring design using a 5-10 GeV electron storage ring intersecting the RHIC ion beams, and a linac-ring design using an energy recovery linac to provide fresh electron bunches per collision. The detector concepts are based on lessons from the HERA H1 and ZEUS detectors, with enhanced forward and backward coverage to access low-x and high-x kinematics.

## Key numbers

- e-p collision luminosity of 4.4 × 10³² cm⁻² s⁻¹ for the high-energy ring-ring design (10 GeV electrons on 250 GeV protons)
- e-p collision luminosity of 1.5 × 10³² cm⁻² s⁻¹ for the low-energy ring-ring design (5 GeV electrons on 50 GeV protons)
- e-Au collision luminosity of 4.4 × 10³² cm⁻² s⁻¹ for high-energy tune (10 GeV electrons on 100 GeV/u gold)
- e-Au collision luminosity of 2.0 × 10³² cm⁻² s⁻¹ for low-energy tune (5 GeV electrons on 100 GeV/u gold)
- Linac-ring design could achieve luminosities up to 10³⁴ cm⁻² s⁻¹, and the ELIC design at Jefferson Lab aims for up to 10³⁵ cm⁻² s⁻¹
- 70% polarization goal for both lepton and proton beams
- Beam-beam parameter limit of 0.007 per interaction point for protons, and 0.08 for electrons at 10 GeV (reduced to 0.04 at 5 GeV)
- Collision frequency of 28.15 MHz (360 bunches in ion ring, 120 in electron ring)
- HERA detectors achieved electromagnetic energy resolution σ/E ~ 15%/√E(GeV) + 1% and hadronic energy resolution σ/E ~ 35-50%/√E(GeV) + 2%

## Context

This paper extends the scientific case for an electron-ion collider that was developed in the earlier EIC whitepaper (BNL-68933-02/07-REV) and builds on the experience from the HERA program at DESY, where the H1 and ZEUS detectors demonstrated the capabilities of ep collider physics. The proposed eRHIC design would enable measurements of gluon distributions in nuclei, nucleon spin structure, and tests of QCD at low x that are beyond the reach of fixed-target experiments such as those at SLAC or CERN, and would complement the heavy-ion program at RHIC and the LHC.
