# Electromagnetic Mean Squared Radii of Lambda(1405) in Meson-baryon Dynamics with Chiral Symmetry

> Electromagnetic mean squared radii of Lambda(1405) are evaluated in chiral unitary approach. In this approach we regard Lambda(1405) as dynamically generated resonances in the octet meson and octet baryon scattering, and also as KbarN bound state. Especially for the Lambda(1405) as KbarN bound state we obtain negative electric mean squared radius. With the small binding energy of Lambda(1405) in the chiral unitary approach, our results imply that Lambda(1405) has structure that K^- is widely spread around p.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Lambda(1405) resonance, when treated as a KbarN bound state in the chiral unitary approach, has a negative electric mean squared radius, implying that the K- meson is widely spread around the proton core. This result is conditional on the small binding energy (about a few MeV) of the higher Lambda(1405) state in this approach.

## Method

The authors evaluate electromagnetic form factors and mean squared radii of the Lambda(1405) using a chiral unitary approach, where the scattering amplitude is obtained from an algebraic equation with an s-wave interaction kernel from the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction and a meson-baryon loop function. The form factors are extracted from the residue of the double pole of the meson-baryon-photon amplitude, calculated from three gauge-invariant diagrams where the photon couples to the constituent mesons and baryons. The calculation is performed both in a fully coupled-channel approach and in a simplified KbarN bound state approach where couplings to other channels are neglected.

## Key numbers

* The two poles of the Lambda(1405) in the complex energy plane are at z1 = 1390 - 66i MeV and z2 = 1426 - 17i MeV.
* The absolute value of the second moment for the higher Lambda(1405) state z2 is 0.32 fm^2, which is larger than that of the neutron (approximately -0.12 fm^2).
* For the KbarN bound state with mass 1429 MeV, the electric mean squared radius is -2.19 fm^2 and the magnetic mean squared radius is 1.97 fm^2.
* The electric root mean squared radius sqrt(|EMSR|) is approximately 1.48 fm, compared to the proton electromagnetic size of roughly 0.9 fm.
* For deep binding energy (more than about 40 MeV), the electric mean squared radius is as small as that of the neutron (less than about -0.1 fm^2).
* For small binding energy (about a few MeV), the absolute value of the electric mean squared radius is larger than 1 fm^2.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to meson-baryon scattering by Kaiser (1995), Oset (1997), Oller (2000), Lutz (2001), and Jido (2003), which successfully reproduced K-p scattering cross sections and the Lambda(1405) mass spectrum. The results are relevant for understanding kaonic nuclei, as suggested by Akaishi (2002), Yamazaki (2007), Hyodo (2007), and Dote (2008), where the binding energy of the KbarN system in the Lambda(1405) plays an important role. The finding that the Lambda(1405) has a widely spread K- cloud around a proton core enables further study of the structure of kaonic nuclei and the nature of dynamically generated resonances in meson-baryon dynamics.
