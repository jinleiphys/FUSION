# Modeling baryonic interactions with the Clausius-type equation of state

> The quantum statistical Clausius-based equation of state is used to describe the system of interacting nucleons. The interaction parameters $a$, $b$, and $c$ of the model are fixed by the empirically known nuclear ground state properties and nuclear incompressibility modulus. The model is generalized to describe the baryon-baryon interactions in the hadron resonance gas (HRG). The predictions of such a Clausius-HRG model are confronted with the lattice QCD data at zero and at small chemical potentials, and are also contrasted with the standard van der Waals approach. It is found that the behavior of the lattice QCD observables in a high-temperature hadron gas is sensitive to the nuclear matter properties. An improved description of the nuclear incompressibility factor correlates with an improved description of the lattice QCD data in the crossover transition region.

## Key claim

The Clausius equation of state, with its three free parameters, can reproduce the empirical nuclear incompressibility modulus (250-315 MeV), unlike the two-parameter van der Waals model which greatly overestimates it (763 MeV); this improved description of nuclear matter stiffness correlates with an improved description of lattice QCD data in the crossover transition region.

## Method

The authors generalize the classical Clausius equation of state to include quantum statistical effects for fermions, obtaining a grand canonical ensemble formulation. The three interaction parameters (a, b, c) are fixed by the nuclear ground state properties (saturation density n0 = 0.16 fm^-3 and binding energy -16 MeV per nucleon) and by the empirical nuclear incompressibility modulus K0. The model is then extended to a multi-component hadron resonance gas (HRG) where all baryons and antibaryons interact via the same Clausius parameters, while mesons remain non-interacting; the resulting Clausius-HRG predictions for pressure, baryon number susceptibilities, and net baryon density are compared to lattice QCD data at zero and small chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear incompressibility modulus K0 = 763 MeV for the quantum van der Waals (QvdW) model, compared to K0 = 315 MeV (Clausius-I) and K0 = 250 MeV (Clausius-II), which match the empirical range of 250-315 MeV.
- Clausius-I parameters: a = 437 MeV fm^3, b = 2.14 fm^3, c = 3.51 fm^3; Clausius-II parameters: a = 472 MeV fm^3, b = 1.73 fm^3, c = 4.74 fm^3.
- Critical point predictions: Clausius-I gives Tc = 16.8 MeV, nc = 0.054 fm^-3, pc = 0.28 MeV/fm^3; Clausius-II gives Tc = 16.3 MeV, nc = 0.050 fm^-3, pc = 0.24 MeV/fm^3; experimental estimates are Tc = 17.9 ± 0.4 MeV, nc = 0.06 ± 0.01 fm^-3, pc = 0.31 ± 0.07 MeV/fm^3.
- Nuclear ground state saturation density n0 = 0.16 fm^-3 and binding energy per nucleon E/A = -16 MeV are used as constraints.

## Context

This work extends the quantum statistical real gas formalism of Vovchenko (2017) by applying it to the three-parameter Clausius equation, improving upon the two-parameter quantum van der Waals (QvdW) model of Vovchenko (2015) which overestimated nuclear incompressibility. The Clausius-HRG model provides a better description of lattice QCD data from the Wuppertal-Budapest and HotQCD collaborations in the crossover region (T = 140-190 MeV) than either the ideal HRG or the QvdW-HRG model. This enables future studies where interaction parameters for strange baryons might differ from non-strange ones, as explored by Vovchenko (2017) for multi-component QvdW-HRG, or where only ground-state baryons interact, as considered by Huovinen (2017).
