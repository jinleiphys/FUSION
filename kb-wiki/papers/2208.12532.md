# Kinematical higher-twist corrections in $γ^* γ\to ππ$

> We apply the Braun-Manashov technique to improve the description of $γ^*(q_1) γ(q_2) \to M(p_1) M(p_2)$ amplitudes at large $Q^2=-q_1^2$ and small $s=(q_1+q_2)^2$. We derive the kinematical higher-twist contributions of order $s/Q^2$ and $m^2/Q^2$ to the helicity amplitudes and estimate their sizes in the kinematics accessible at Belle and Belle II.Since pion GPDs cannot be directly measured by experiment, $ππ$ GDAs are the best way to investigate the energy-momentum tensor form factors for pions.

## Key claim

The paper derives kinematical higher-twist corrections of order \(s/Q^2\) and \(m^2/Q^2\) to the helicity amplitudes for \(\gamma^* \gamma \to \pi\pi\), and estimates that these corrections cannot be neglected when \(\sqrt{s} > 1\) GeV, though the extracted GDA from Belle measurements may not be accurate enough due to large experimental uncertainties.

## Method

The authors apply the Braun-Manashov technique, previously used for deeply-virtual Compton scattering (DVCS), to separate kinematical and dynamical contributions in the product of two electromagnetic currents for the process \(\gamma^*(q_1) \gamma(q_2) \to \pi(p_1) \pi(p_2)\). They derive expressions for the three independent helicity amplitudes \(A^{(0)}\), \(A^{(1)}\), and \(A^{(2)}\) in terms of generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs), including kinematical higher-twist contributions. The numerical estimates use the pion GDA extracted from Belle measurements to compute the cross section ratio \(d\sigma_{t2+t3+t4}/d\sigma_{t2}\) for kinematics accessible at Belle and Belle II.

## Key numbers

* The kinematical higher-twist corrections are of order \(s/Q^2\) and \(m^2/Q^2\) relative to the leading-twist amplitude.
* Numerical estimates use \(Q^2 = 9, 16, 25\) GeV\(^2\), \(s \in (0.25, 4)\) GeV\(^2\), and \(s_{e\gamma} = 30\) GeV\(^2\) (typical value at Belle).
* Around \(\sqrt{s} \sim 1.5\) GeV, the kinematical corrections become dominant in the cross section for \(\cos\theta = 0.8\), though the twist-2 cross section is tiny with the extracted GDA.
* As \(Q^2\) increases, the kinematical contributions become less important, consistent with suppression by \(1/Q\) or \(1/Q^2\).

## Context

This work extends the Braun-Manashov technique, previously applied to DVCS by Braun (2012), to the \(\gamma^* \gamma \to \pi\pi\) process, which is accessible in \(e^+ e^-\) collisions at Belle and Belle II. The kinematical corrections generalize the target mass corrections in deep inelastic scattering introduced by Nachtmann (1973). The results enable more accurate extraction of pion GDAs from future Belle II data, which is essential for investigating the energy-momentum tensor form factors of pions, as pion GPDs cannot be directly measured.
