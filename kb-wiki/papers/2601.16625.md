# Non-Equilibrium Trace Anomaly And Bulk Viscosity in Heavy Ion Collisions From Kinetic Theory

> We investigate the far-from-equilibrium dynamics and transport properties of a relativistic massive gas obeying Maxwell-Boltzmann (MB), Bose-Einstein (BE), and Fermi-Dirac (FD) statistics undergoing a boost-invariant Bjorken expansion. We solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation (RTA) using the method of moments. We focus on the time evolution of the trace of the energy-momentum tensor $Θ^μ{}_μ$ and the bulk viscous pressure $Π$, which are key diagnostics of conformal-symmetry breaking in the rapidly evolving fireball created in heavy-ion collisions. We find that the non-equilibrium quantity $Θ^μ{}_μ/T^{4}$ exhibits a non-monotonic time dependence, with a local maximum at early times and a pronounced dip around the characteristic relaxation time scale $τ_{R}$. We further show that the scaled bulk pressure $Π/P_{0}$, where $P_{0}$ denotes the isotropic equilibrium pressure, depends sensitively on the particle statistics. In addition, increasing the initial chemical potential enhances the magnitudes of both $Π$ and $Θ^μ{}_μ/T^{4}$. Finally, by initializing the system with random non-equilibrium configurations, we demonstrate that the evolution of the scaled bulk pressure and the pressure anisotropy converges to a common late-time solution.

## Key claim

The non-equilibrium trace anomaly \(\Theta^{\mu}_{\;\mu}/T^{4}\) in a massive relativistic gas undergoing Bjorken expansion exhibits a non-monotonic time dependence with a local maximum at early times and a distinct dip around the characteristic relaxation time scale \(\tau_R\), contrary to the monotonic behavior implied by equilibrium lattice QCD calculations.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation (RTA) using the method of moments for a massive gas undergoing boost-invariant Bjorken expansion. They considered three types of particle statistics: Maxwell-Boltzmann (MB), Bose-Einstein (BE), and Fermi-Dirac (FD). The system was initialized with an equilibrium configuration at temperature \(T_0 = 1\) GeV and rest mass \(m = 0.1\) GeV, and the evolution of the moment hierarchy was truncated and solved numerically to obtain the time-dependent bulk viscous pressure \(\Pi\) and trace anomaly.

## Key numbers

* The initial temperature was set to \(T_0 = 1\) GeV and the particle rest mass to \(m = 0.1\) GeV, giving an initial mass parameter \(\chi_0 = m/T_0 = 0.1\).
* The initial scaled chemical potential was set to \(\alpha_0 = 0\) for the baseline calculations.
* The non-equilibrium quantity \(\Theta^{\mu}_{\;\mu}/T^{4}\) shows a local maximum at early times and a pronounced dip around the characteristic relaxation time scale \(\tau_R\).
* The scaled bulk pressure \(\Pi/P_0\) depends significantly on whether the particles obey MB, BE, or FD statistics.
* Increasing the initial chemical potential enhances the magnitudes of both \(\Pi\) and \(\Theta^{\mu}_{\;\mu}/T^{4}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of non-equilibrium dynamics and hydrodynamic attractors in heavy-ion collisions, following the seminal work by Spalinsky and Heller (2020) and subsequent developments by Denicol et al. (2012) and Blaizot and Yan (2021). While lattice QCD calculations by Meyer (2008) and Bazavov et al. (2014) provide equilibrium results for the trace anomaly with a peak near the crossover temperature \(T_c \sim 155\) MeV, this paper addresses the far-from-equilibrium regime relevant for the rapidly expanding fireball created in heavy-ion collisions. The results enable the self-consistent determination of bulk viscosity from non-equilibrium attractor solutions without assuming equilibrium values, which is particularly relevant for understanding the approach to hydrodynamic behavior in the early stages of heavy-ion collisions.
