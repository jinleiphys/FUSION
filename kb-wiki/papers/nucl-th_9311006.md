# Is Schrödinger's Conjecture for the Hydrogen Atom Coherent States Attainable

> We construct the most general SO(4,2) hydrogen atom coherent states which are the counterpart of Schrödinger's harmonic oscillator coherent states. We show that these states cannot be localized and cannot follow the classical orbits. Thus, Schrödinger's conjecture for the hydrogen atom coherent states is unattainable.

## Key claim
The most general SO(4,2) coherent states for the hydrogen atom, constructed as the direct counterpart of Schrödinger’s harmonic oscillator coherent states, cannot be localized wavepackets and cannot follow Kepler orbits; therefore Schrödinger’s 1926 conjecture that such states exist for the hydrogen atom is unattainable. The authors show that these states fail to satisfy the general minimum uncertainty relation and that the quenching index for the SO(4,2) coherent states is fixed at 1/2, precluding a classical limit.

## Method
The authors constructed SO(4,2) coherent states for the hydrogen atom by exponentiating the generators of the SO(4,2) dynamical group (M, Γ, S, T) acting on the hydrogen ground state |n=1,l=0,m=0⟩, following the general group-theoretical algorithm for coherent states. They expressed these states in a boson representation using two sets of harmonic-oscillator creation and annihilation operators (a_i, b_i) and expanded them as superpositions of hydrogen eigenstates |nlm⟩. They then tested whether these states satisfy the general minimum uncertainty relation for the radial coordinate and momentum, reducing the condition to a set of matrix equations that they proved cannot be fulfilled.

## Key numbers
- The quenching index Ξ for the SO(4,2) hydrogen atom coherent states is fixed at Ξ = 1/2, meaning no classical limit exists for these states.
- For comparison, the SU(1,1) coherent states of the hydrogen atom have a quenching index Ξ = 2(l+1), which can become large for high angular momentum l.
- The coherent states are defined on the coset space SO(4,2)/SO(4)⊗SO(2), a 4-dimensional complex manifold.

## Context
This work directly addresses Schrödinger’s 1926 conjecture that wavepackets analogous to his harmonic oscillator coherent states could be constructed for the hydrogen atom and would travel along Kepler elliptic orbits. The authors show that the most general SO(4,2) coherent states, which span the full irreducible representation space of the hydrogen atom, cannot realize this conjecture. Earlier attempts by Mostowski (1977) claimed such states followed Kepler motion, but that claim was questioned by Stroud (1990). The paper also notes that other wavepacket constructions (Brown 1973, Stroud 1991, Nauenberg 1989) do not satisfy the resolution of identity. The authors point toward SU(1,1) coherent states, which are superpositions of a subset of hydrogen eigenstates with fixed angular momentum, as a possible alternative route to classical-like wavepackets.
