# Dark Matter-Induced Nuclear De-Excitation at SBND with Ab Initio Nuclear Theory

> We explore the sensitivity of the Short-Baseline Near Detector (SBND) experiment to light dark matter using MeV-scale electromagnetic activity. Inelastic scattering of dark matter with argon nuclei can excite nuclear states that subsequently de-excite via the emission of MeV-scale photons, producing localized low-energy "blip" signatures in a liquid argon time projection chamber. We perform state-of-the-art ab initio nuclear calculations, including all relevant argon excited states with energies up to 18 MeV, to provide reliable predictions for these signals. After accounting for relevant backgrounds, we find that SBND can probe previously unexplored regions of parameter space for light dark matter.

# Dark Matter-Induced Nuclear De-Excitation at SBND with Ab Initio Nuclear Theory

## Key claim
SBND can probe previously unexplored regions of light dark matter parameter space through inelastic scattering on argon nuclei followed by MeV-scale de-excitation photon emission, but only if the detector's ability to reconstruct isolated MeV-scale "blip" signatures is validated and backgrounds from neutrino-induced nuclear excitation (approximately 235 events in target mode) and beam-related neutrons can be sufficiently suppressed through timing, fiducialization, and topology-based selections.

## Method
The authors performed ab initio valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) calculations using three chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions (1.8/2.0(EM), ΔNNLOGO(394), N3LOTexas) to compute inelastic dark matter-nucleus scattering cross sections for all relevant argon excited states up to 18 MeV. They simulated dark matter production via neutral meson decay and proton bremsstrahlung at both the beryllium target and iron dump of the Booster Neutrino Beam, then estimated signal event rates for SBND in both target-mode and proposed dump-mode configurations.

## Key numbers
- SBND is located 110 m downstream of the BNB target, which receives 8 GeV protons
- Target mode: 8.2×10^20 POT at target and 1.8×10^20 POT at dump over three years
- Dump mode (proposed): 6.0×10^20 POT at dump, with neutrino background reduced by a factor of 50 relative to target mode
- Approximately 18% of protons on target pass through without interacting and strike the iron dump 50 m downstream
- Dominant contributions to the signal come from 1+ states between 4.5 and 10 MeV and the first 2+ state at approximately 1.5 MeV excitation energy
- Estimated 235 neutrino-induced inelastic events in SBND for target mode exposure
- Exclusion bounds shown for 2.3 events (dump mode, 90% CL, background-free assumption) and 10 and 30 events (target mode, with estimated neutrino background)
- Dark matter mass ratio fixed at m_A'/m_χ = 3 with α_D = g_D^2/(4π) = 0.5

## Context
This work extends the inelastic dark matter scattering approach previously applied to the CCM experiment by Dutta et al. (2023, 2022) to the SBND liquid argon time projection chamber at Fermilab. It improves upon prior phenomenological large-scale shell model calculations by employing ab initio nuclear theory (VS-IMSRG) that does not require operator quenching, and systematically includes all argon excited states up to 18 MeV for the first time. The results enable SBND to probe dark photon and leptophobic dark matter models in parameter space regions not accessible to elastic scattering searches from COHERENT (2021), LSND (2001), CCM (2021), MiniBooNE (2018), NA64 (2021), and E137 (1988).
