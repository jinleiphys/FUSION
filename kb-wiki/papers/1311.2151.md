# Photon emission in neutral current interactions at intermediate energies

> Neutral current photon emission reactions with nucleons and nuclei are studied. These processes are important backgrounds for nu_mu to nu_e (bar(nu)_mu to bar(nu)_e) appearance oscillation experiments where electromagnetic showers instigated by electrons (positrons) and photons are not distinguishable. At intermediate energies, these reactions are dominated by the weak excitation of the Delta(1232) resonance and its subsequent decay into Ngamma There are also non-resonant contributions that, close to threshold, are fully determined by the effective chiral Lagrangian of strong interactions. In addition, we have also included mechanisms mediated by nucleon excitations (N*) from the second resonance region above the Delta(1232). From these states, the contribution of the D13 N*(1520) turns out to be sizable for (anti)neutrino energies above 1.5 GeV. We have extended the model to nuclear targets taking, into account Pauli blocking, Fermi motion and the in-medium Delta resonance broadening. We present our predictions for both the incoherent and coherent channels, showing the relevance of the nuclear corrections. We also discuss the target mass dependence of the cross sections. This study is important in order to reduce systematic effects in neutrino oscillation experiments.

## Key claim

Neutral current photon emission (NCγ) is an irreducible background to νμ → νe and ν̅μ → ν̅e appearance oscillation experiments because electromagnetic showers from electrons (positrons) and photons are indistinguishable; the authors present a model that extends previous descriptions to intermediate energies (Eν ∼ 1 GeV) by including, for the first time, contributions from second-resonance-region nucleon excitations (N*) beyond the dominant Δ(1232) resonance, finding that the D13 N*(1520) contribution becomes sizable for (anti)neutrino energies above 1.5 GeV.

## Method

The authors construct a model for NCγ reactions on free nucleons using Lorentz-covariant amplitudes that include the dominant Δ(1232) resonance excitation and its decay into Nγ, non-resonant nucleon-pole terms constrained by chiral symmetry, and an anomalous t-channel pion-exchange term. They extend the model to higher energies by adding direct and crossed pole contributions from three second-resonance-region states (P11(1440), D13(1520), S11(1535)), with vector form factors obtained from helicity amplitudes (MAID parametrization) and axial couplings fixed by off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relations. For nuclear targets, the model is extended to both incoherent (1p1h excitations) and coherent channels, incorporating Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, and in-medium Δ resonance broadening.

## Key numbers

- The Δ(1232) resonance has mass 1232 MeV, width 117 MeV, and axial coupling C⁵_A(0) = 1.00 ± 0.11.
- The D13 N*(1520) resonance has mass 1520 MeV, width 115 MeV, and axial coupling C⁵_A(0) = −2.14.
- The P11 N(1440) resonance has mass 1440 MeV, width 300 MeV, and axial coupling F_A(0) = −0.47.
- The S11 N(1535) resonance has mass 1535 MeV, width 150 MeV, and axial coupling F_A(0) = −0.21.
- The axial mass for the nucleon dipole form factor is M_A = 1.0 GeV; for the Δ(1232) axial form factor, M_A = 0.93 GeV.
- The Fermi constant is G = 1.1664 × 10⁻¹¹ MeV⁻²; the weak mixing angle is sin²θ_W = 0.231.
- The D13 N*(1520) contribution becomes sizable for (anti)neutrino energies above 1.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends and improves upon earlier models for NCγ reactions on nucleons by Hill (2009) and Serot (2012), which were limited to lower energies or lacked nuclear corrections, and by Zhang (2012), which extended a chiral effective theory phenomenologically to higher energies but with poorly understood contact-term form factors. The present model builds on the authors' previous work on weak pion production on nucleons (Hernandez 2007) and nuclei (Hernandez 2013, Alvarez-Ruso 2007, Amaro 2008), and applies standard medium corrections previously tested in pion, photon, and electron scattering on nuclei (Oset 1981, Nieves 1991, Carrasco 1989, Gil 1997). The results are important for reducing systematic uncertainties in neutrino oscillation experiments such as MiniBooNE and T2K, where NCγ events contribute to the background for ν_e appearance measurements.
