# Kaon to Pion ratio in Heavy Ion Collisions

> The momentum integrated Boltzmann equation has been used to study the evolution of strangeness of the strongly interacting system formed after the heavy ion collisions at relativistic energies.We argue that the experimentally observed non-monotonic, horn-like structure in the variation of the $K^+/π^+$ with colliding energy appears due to the release of large number of colour degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the experimentally observed non-monotonic, horn-like structure in the variation of the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio with colliding energy arises due to the sudden increase of entropy from the release of a large number of colour degrees of freedom at the deconfinement transition point, rather than from other proposed mechanisms.

## Method

The authors used the momentum-integrated Boltzmann equation to study the evolution of strangeness in the strongly interacting system formed after heavy ion collisions. They solved coupled differential equations for the time evolution of strange quarks and hadrons (including \(K^+\), \(\Lambda\), and \(\Sigma\)) through the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase, mixed phase, and hadronic phase, incorporating both QGP reactions (\(q\bar{q} \rightarrow s\bar{s}\), \(gg \rightarrow s\bar{s}\)) and an exhaustive set of hadronic reactions. For each centre-of-mass energy, they used experimental particle multiplicities to estimate initial temperatures and then computed the anti-strangeness to entropy ratio (\(S_{theory}\)), which is proportional to \(K^+/\pi^+\).

## Key numbers

- The QGP to hadronic phase transition temperature is taken as \(T_c \sim 175\) MeV, based on lattice QCD calculations.
- For initial temperatures \(T_i = T_c\) or \(T_i > T_c\), the ratio of non-equilibrium to equilibrium kaon density at the end of the mixed phase is \(r_{K^+} \sim 1.2 - 1.4\).
- For initial temperatures \(T_i < T_c\) (system formed in hadronic phase), \(r_{K^+} < 1\) for \(\delta = 0.5\).
- The parameter \(\delta\), representing the fraction of \(\bar{s}\) quarks hadronizing to \(K^+\), is set to 0.5 when considering only \(K^+\) and \(K^0\) formation in the mixed phase.
- The quantity \(R_{lattice} \equiv [g^{(2+1)F} - g^{2F}] / g^{(2+1)F}\) derived from lattice QCD shows a peak near \(T_c \sim 175\) MeV, indicating the deconfinement of quarks and gluons.

## Context

This work extends theoretical efforts to explain the non-monotonic behaviour of \(K^+/\pi^+\) with colliding energy observed experimentally by Gazdzicki and collaborators, which has led to intense theoretical activities by authors such as CORS, GG, BT, and LR. The study uses lattice QCD results indicating a phase transition from hadronic matter to QGP, and builds on the Bjorken scaling solution for the cooling of the heat bath. The findings enable a connection between the horn-like structure in the kaon-to-pion ratio and the deconfinement transition, suggesting that the ratio can serve as a signature for QGP formation in heavy ion collisions.
