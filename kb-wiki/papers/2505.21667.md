# Prospect of Constraining the EoS of Neutron Stars Using Post-Merger Signals

> Post-merger gravitational-wave emission from a binary neutron star merger carries crucial information about the equation of state (EoS) of matter at high temperatures. Current gravitational wave detectors have limited sensitivities at post-merger frequencies in the range [1.5, 4] kHz. Therefore, valuable inferences can only be made after combining information from multiple (BNS) events. Criswell et al. [Phys. Rev. D 107, 043021 (2023)] carries out an injection study to infer the radius posterior for a $1.6 M_{\odot}$ NS by combining the information from injected BNS events via the hierarchical Bayesian inference (HBI) formulation. This formulation utilizes empirical relations that connect the peak frequency of the post-merger remnant with the chirp mass of the system, and the EoS proxy parameter $R_{1.6}$. In this work, we extend the HBI formulation to other EoS proxy parameters (i.e., the radius of (NS), $R_{1.X}$, with masses 1.2, 1.4, and 1.8 $M_{\odot}$) and combine the four $R_{1.X}$ posteriors through the piecewise polytropic EoS model to obtain measurable constraints on the EoS of the NS. We show that the NS radii can be constrained to within $\sim$1 km ( $\sim$0.55 km) assuming uniform (astrophysical) prior on $R_{1.X}$ for injections in the A+ noise. We also study systematic biases in the analysis coming from the limitations of empirical relations.

# Prospect of Constraining the EoS of Neutron Stars Using Post-Merger Signals

## Key claim

The authors extend the hierarchical Bayesian inference (HBI) formulation from constraining a single EoS proxy parameter (R1.6) to constraining four radii (R1.2, R1.4, R1.6, R1.8) and combine them through a piecewise polytropic EoS model, showing that neutron star radii can be constrained to within approximately 1 km (or approximately 0.55 km with astrophysical priors) using post-merger signals in the A+ era. However, these constraints are subject to systematic biases from the limitations of empirical relations and waveform interpolation, particularly for higher-mass systems.

## Method

The authors used the BAYESTACK package to compute HBI posteriors for four neutron star radii (R1.2, R1.4, R1.6, R1.8) from simulated binary neutron star post-merger gravitational-wave signals injected into A+ noise, using empirical relations connecting peak frequency f_peak with chirp mass and radius. They then combined these four radius posteriors through a piecewise polytropic EoS model (with parameters Gamma_5, Gamma_6, Gamma_7, and log P_5) by assuming all neutron stars follow the same EoS, marginalizing over the radii to obtain posteriors on the EoS parameters and subsequently on mass-radius and mass-tidal deformability curves.

## Key numbers

- Radii constrained to within approximately 1 km (90% credible interval) assuming uniform prior on R1.X for injections in A+ noise
- Radii constrained to within approximately 0.55 km (90% credible interval) assuming astrophysical (MM Riley) prior on R1.X for injections in A+ noise
- 357 expected events in simulated A+ noise with four-year duration assuming BNS merger rate density of 320 (+490, -240) Gpc^-3 yr^-1 (Set C)
- 109 simulated events in Set B (Set A plus 83 events in simulated A+ noise of one-year duration)
- 200 events in Set D from Set C with chirp mass less than 1.254 solar masses
- 90% credible interval reduction of approximately 34% when using informed prior on R1.X compared to uniform prior with BAYESTACK alone
- Additional 90% credible interval reduction of approximately 38% when imposing common EoS model across four radii
- 90% credible interval reduction of approximately 50-60% for Set B when imposing common EoS constraint
- SFHx EoS radii distributions have approximately 27% smaller 90% credible intervals than SLy4 EoS distributions

## Context

This work extends the HBI formulation of Criswell et al. (2023), who inferred the radius posterior for a 1.6 solar mass neutron star by combining information from injected BNS events. The authors build on that framework by constraining four radii simultaneously and combining them through a piecewise polytropic EoS model, enabling tighter constraints on the EoS. This approach enables future studies to probe the hot merger phase of neutron star evolution and the inner core of neutron stars, particularly with third-generation gravitational-wave detectors, though the authors note that improved empirical relations and updated merger rate densities consistent with observed BNS systems are needed.
