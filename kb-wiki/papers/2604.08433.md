# Nuclear forward scattering of Bessel beams in $^{229}$Th:CaF$_2$

> The coherent pulse propagation of a Bessel beam resonant to the 8.4 eV nuclear clock transition in $^{229}$Th-doped crystals is investigated theoretically. Due to the magnetic dipole character of the clock transition, Bessel beams which present non-uniform transverse profiles and carry orbital angular momentum might enhance excitation channels or offer new control degrees of freedom compared to standard plane waves. We model the nuclear forward scattering of a resonant Bessel beam pulse propagating through the crystal, extending an formalism based on the iterative wave equation for plane waves. Thereby we take into account the nuclear quadrupole splitting in the crystal, considering the possibility of multiple quantization axes and present results for scenarios involving a single nuclear transition and multiple simultaneously driven transitions, analyzing temporal and spatial intensity patterns. Our findings show that the propagation of Bessel beams can be used to determine the relative distribution of different directions of quantization axes inside the crystal.

## Key claim
The paper claims that coherent propagation of Bessel beam pulses through a $^{229}$Th:CaF$_2$ crystal can be used to determine the relative distribution of different quantization axis orientations inside the crystal, but only under specific conditions where the quantization axis is not parallel to the beam propagation direction, leading to spatially varying time spectra.

## Method
The authors extended the iterative wave equation (IWE) formalism, originally developed for plane wave nuclear forward scattering in Mössbauer nuclei by Shvyd'ko (1993, 1994, 1999), to handle Bessel beams by treating each plane wave component on the Bessel beam's cone separately. They applied this formalism to the $^{229}$Th:CaF$_2$ system, accounting for nuclear quadrupole splitting with three possible mutually orthogonal electric field gradient orientations, and analyzed both single two-level transitions and multiple simultaneously driven transitions under paraxial and non-paraxial conditions.

## Key numbers
- The $^{229}$Th nuclear clock transition energy is approximately 8.4 eV, with a lifetime on the order of $10^3$ s
- The crystal parameters used: nuclear number density $N = 10^{18}$ cm$^{-3}$, resonant absorption cross section $\sigma \approx 2.4 \times 10^{-10}$ cm$^2$, crystal length $L = 1$ cm, radiative decay rate $\Gamma \approx 10^{-3}$ s$^{-1}$, yielding effective thickness $\xi \approx 6 \times 10^7$
- The quadrupole moment of the ground state is $Q_g = 3.11$ b and of the isomer state $Q_e = 1.8$ b, where b = $10^{-24}$ cm$^2$
- The electric field gradient component $V_{zz} = -296.7$ V/Å$^2$ for the 180$^\circ$-doping configuration, with asymmetry parameter $\eta = 0$
- The Bessel beam opening angle used in paraxial calculations is $\theta_k = 5^\circ$

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical studies by Kirschbaum et al. (2024) on photoexcitation of $^{229}$Th with twisted light, which found no enhancement of excitation probability compared to plane waves for either single-ion or solid-state cases. The paper builds on the IWE formalism for plane waves developed by Shvyd'ko (1993, 1994, 1999) and applies it to Bessel beams for the first time in nuclear forward scattering. The results enable future experimental determination of the relative distribution of different doping orientations in $^{229}$Th-doped crystals, addressing a key uncertainty identified in recent experimental work by Zhang et al. (2024) where more energy levels were observed than predicted by density functional theory calculations by Dessovic et al. (2014).
