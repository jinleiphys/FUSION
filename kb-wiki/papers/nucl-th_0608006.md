# Impact parameter dependence of heavy ion e+ e- pair production to all orders in Z alpha

> The heavy ion probability for continuum e+ e- pair production has been calculated to all orders in Z alpha as a function of impact parameter. The formula resulting from an exact solution of the semiclassical Dirac equation in the ultrarelativistic limit is evaluated numerically. In a calculation of gamma = 100 colliding Au ions the probability of e+ e- pair production is reduced from the perturbation theory result throughout the impact parameter range.

## Key claim

The exact solution of the semiclassical Dirac equation for continuum e+ e- pair production in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions yields a probability that is reduced from the perturbation theory result throughout the entire impact parameter range, not just at small impact parameters where Coulomb corrections were previously expected.

## Method

The author derived an exact solution of the semiclassical Dirac equation in the ultrarelativistic limit using two delta-function potentials for the countermoving heavy ions, then evaluated the number operator for total pair production as a function of impact parameter by numerically integrating the squared amplitude over electron and positron momenta. The calculation used a physically motivated spatial cutoff on the transverse potential via a modified Bessel function K0, replacing the ad hoc momentum cutoff of earlier eikonal approaches. For non-zero impact parameters, a piecewise analytical Fourier integration technique was employed to handle the oscillatory factor exp[i kx b] in the momentum integrals.

## Key numbers

- For Au + Au at gamma = 100, the exact integrated probability at b = 0 is P(0) = 0.94, which is 0.57 times the perturbation theory result P^0(0) = 1.64
- The eikonal calculation at b = 0 gives P^E(0) = 1.03 = 0.63 P^0(0)
- The integrated perturbation theory total cross section is 34.6 kbn (kilobarns)
- The integrated exact total cross section is 29.4 kbn, in fair agreement with the author's previous calculation of 28.6 kbn
- The integrated eikonal total cross section is 35.5 kbn
- 80% of the contribution to the total probability at b = 0 comes from k > 100 omega / gamma
- Less than 0.1% of the contribution at b = 0 comes from k < 10 omega / gamma

## Context

This work extends the author's previous total cross section calculations (Baltz 2006) to impact parameter dependence, and provides the exact all-orders-in-Z-alpha result needed for comparison with RHIC and LHC experiments using zero degree calorimeters. The results contradict the earlier eikonal approximation (which showed a crossover where eikonal exceeded perturbation theory at large impact parameters) and improve upon the approximate Coulomb-corrected impact parameter formulas of Bertulani and Baur (1988) and Lee, Milstein, and Serbo (2004). The impact parameter dependent probabilities are designed to be combined with mutual Coulomb dissociation calculations to model triggered events at RHIC's STAR detector.
