# Effective field theory as the bridge between lattice QCD and nuclear physics

> A confluence of theoretical and technological developments are beginning to make possible contributions to nuclear physics from lattice QCD. Effective field theory plays a critical role in these advances. I give several examples.

## Key claim

Effective field theory (EFT) will allow extraction of useful nuclear physics information from lattice QCD simulations performed at unrealistic lattice parameters (heavy quark masses, finite volume, finite lattice spacing), but this requires an intense theoretical effort because a brute-force approach on a Pflops machine will not provide useful information about even the alpha particle at realistic quark masses.

## Method

The paper reviews how EFT, particularly chiral perturbation theory and nuclear EFT, can be used to extrapolate lattice QCD results to physical quark masses, correct for finite lattice spacing errors using the Symanzik action matched onto a generalized chiral Lagrangian, parametrize finite volume effects, and extract S-matrix elements from Euclidean simulations via the volume dependence of energy eigenvalues (Luscher's method). The author also discusses partially quenched chiral perturbation theory, where valence and sea quark masses are varied independently, and mixed-action approaches combining staggered sea quarks with domain wall valence quarks.

## Key numbers

- Cost estimate for generating gauge field configurations with dynamical Wilson fermions: Cost ~ 0.15 Tflops-yrs × [# configs/1000] × [m_q/20 MeV]^{-1} × [V/32 fm^4]^{5/4} × [a/0.08 fm]^{-6}
- Light quark masses: m_u ~ 2.5 MeV, m_d ~ 5 MeV (not the 20 MeV used in cost formula)
- Pion Compton wavelength: ~1.4 fm; deuteron scattering length: ~5 fm
- Lattice QCD studies of a helium nucleus require 6!^2 = 518,400 quark propagator contractions
- Chiral expansion parameter for mesonic processes: m_π^2/Λ^2 with Λ ~ m_ρ ~ 1 GeV; a light quark mass of 20 MeV corresponds to m_π ~ 325 MeV
- Mixed action calculations achieved g_A at ~10% level, f_π/f_K to ~1%, and π-π, K-π, and two-nucleon properties
- Box size of 5-15 fm needed to study deuteron properties using Luscher's method
- For p ≳ m_π/2, the pion must be included explicitly in nuclear EFT

## Context

This talk extends the program of applying chiral perturbation theory to lattice QCD, as reviewed by Sharpe (2006), and builds on the nuclear effective field theory pioneered by Weinberg (1990) and developed by many subsequent authors. The paper identifies four frontier areas where lattice QCD can make useful predictions: masses and couplings of QCD resonances and hybrids; strangeness physics (anti-kaon-nucleon, hyperon-hyperon, and hyperon-nucleon interactions); three-body interactions such as in the I=3/2 channel; and quark mass dependence of nuclear properties. These results could inform the JLab experimental program, answer questions about dense matter in neutron stars, improve ab initio nuclear structure calculations, and test cosmological theories where quark masses are dynamically determined.
