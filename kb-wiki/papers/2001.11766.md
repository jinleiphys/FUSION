# Pre-critical soft photons emission from quark matter

> We compute the soft real photon emission rate from the QCD matter in the vicinity of the critical line at moderate density and the temperature approaching the critical one from above. The obtained production rate exhibits a steep rise close to $T_c$ due to the formation of the slow fluctuation mode.

## Key claim

The soft real photon emission rate from QCD matter rises steeply as the temperature approaches the critical temperature from above, scaling as \(\varepsilon^{-1/2}\) where \(\varepsilon = (T-T_c)/T_c\), due to the formation of a slow fluctuation mode (the fluctuation pair propagator). This enhancement is conditional on the system being in the pre-critical fluctuation region at moderate density (\(\mu \simeq 300\)–\(400\) MeV) and is derived under the assumption that the Gaussian (non-interacting) fluctuation approximation holds, which may break down for \(\varepsilon \lesssim 10^{-4}\).

## Method

The author computes the retarded photon self-energy using the Aslamazov-Larkin diagram, which consists of two quark loops connected by two fluctuation pair propagators (FP). The FP is derived from a time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau functional with Langevin forces, yielding a propagator of the form \(L(\mathbf{q}, \omega) = N / [ (T-T_c)/T_c - i\beta \omega + \xi^2 q^2 ]\). The self-energy is evaluated via contour integration (the Eliashberg trick) and expanded to linear order in the photon energy \(\omega\), leading to an expression for the imaginary part of the polarization operator. The soft photon emission rate is then obtained from the standard relation between the thermal emission rate and the imaginary part of the retarded self-energy.

## Key numbers

* The Ginzburg-Levanyuk parameter \(\operatorname{Gi} = \delta T / T_c\) is estimated to lie in the range \(10^{-4}\) to \(10^{-2}\) for \(T_c \simeq 40\)–\(50\) MeV, \(\mu \simeq 300\)–\(400\) MeV, and pair radius \(\rho \simeq 1\)–\(2\) fm.
* The photon emission rate scales as \(\varepsilon^{-1/2}\) with \(\varepsilon = (T-T_c)/T_c\), and is plotted for \(T_c = 40\) MeV and \(T_c = 50\) MeV with relaxation times \(\tau = 0.1\) fm and \(\tau = 0.3\) fm.
* The electrical conductivity derived from the same formalism at \(T = 0.05\) GeV, \(\tau = 0.2\) fm, and \(\varepsilon^{-1/2} = 20\) is \(\sigma = 0.09\) fm\(^{-1}\).
* The characteristic soft photon emission rate in this work is \(\sim 10^{-3}\) fm\(^{-4}\) GeV\(^{-2}\), compared to \(\sim 10^{-4}\) fm\(^{-4}\) GeV\(^{-2}\) in the CFL phase calculation of Ref. [16].

## Context

This work extends the study of photon emission from quark matter to the finite-density, pre-critical fluctuation region above the critical temperature, where perturbative methods (e.g., hard thermal loop) are inadequate. It builds on the earlier identification of the importance of the collective fluctuation mode for dilepton production by the same author (Kerbikov, 2015) and contrasts with the CFL-phase calculation of Ref. [16], where the emission rate is an order of magnitude lower. The results are directly relevant to the experimental programs at NICA and FAIR, which aim to explore the QCD phase diagram at moderate density, and the derived conductivity matches the author's previous calculation (Kerbikov, 2019).
