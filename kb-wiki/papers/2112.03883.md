# On transverse momentum broadening in real-time lattice simulations of the glasma and in the weak-field limit

> In these proceedings, we report on our numerical lattice simulations of partons traversing the boost-invariant, non-perturbative glasma as created at the early stages of collisions at RHIC and LHC. Since these highly energetic partons are produced from hard scatterings during heavy-ion collisions, they are already affected by the first stage of the medium's time evolution, the glasma, which is the pre-equilibrium precursor state of the quark-gluon plasma. We find that partons quickly accumulate transverse momentum up to the saturation momentum during the glasma stage. Moreover, we observe an interesting anisotropy in transverse momentum broadening of partons with larger broadening in the rapidity than in the azimuthal direction. Its origin can be related to correlations among the longitudinal color-electric and color-magnetic flux tubes in the initial state of the glasma. We compare these observations to the semi-analytic results obtained by a weak-field approximation, where we also find such an anisotropy in a parton's transverse momentum broadening.

## Key claim
Partons traversing the glasma accumulate transverse momentum up to the saturation momentum during the pre-equilibrium stage, with a larger broadening in the rapidity direction than in the azimuthal direction. This anisotropy originates from correlations among longitudinal color-electric and color-magnetic flux tubes in the glasma initial state, and it persists in both the full real-time lattice simulations and the semi-analytic weak-field approximation.

## Method
The authors performed real-time lattice gauge theory simulations of a boost-invariant glasma background, treating an ultra-relativistic test parton as a classical colored particle that experiences a non-Abelian Lorentz force without back-reaction. They computed the transverse momentum broadening via lightlike Wilson lines and the color-rotated Lorentz force, discretizing the Yang-Mills equations on a square transverse lattice with periodic boundary conditions and solving them with a leapfrog scheme. Separately, they derived a semi-analytic weak-field approximation at fourth order in the charge density, where the time evolution becomes Abelian and the momentum broadening can be expressed in terms of initial color-electric and color-magnetic correlators.

## Key numbers
- Transverse momentum broadening in the weak-field limit shows anisotropy: \(\langle p^2_z \rangle / \langle p^2_y \rangle \neq 1\), linked to the difference between color-electric and color-magnetic correlators.
- In the weak-field limit, the electric correlator at zero momentum is \(\lim_{k \rightarrow 0} c_E(k, m) = 1/(12 \pi^2 m^2) > 0\), while the magnetic correlator vanishes: \(\lim_{k \rightarrow 0} c_B(k, m) = 0\).
- In the dense glasma (strong fields, \(m/(g^2 \mu) \ll 1\)), the \(z\)-component of momentum broadening begins to decrease below \(Q_s \tau = 10\), and the anisotropy vanishes at very late times \(Q_s \tau \gtrsim 10\).
- For dense glasmas with \(m/(g^2 \mu) \lesssim 0.1\), the color-electric correlator in coordinate space exhibits a negative region, in contrast to the dilute glasma.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies (Ipp 2020a, Ipp 2020b) on parton momentum broadening in the glasma, comparing full real-time lattice simulations to the weak-field approximation of Kovner (1995). The results connect the observed anisotropy in transverse momentum broadening to the difference between color-electric and color-magnetic flux tube correlations, which was not accessible in earlier dilute-limit calculations. The findings enable future studies with more realistic initial conditions, such as finite nuclear thickness (Ipp 2021) and finite transverse extent for off-central collisions, as well as extensions to parton energy loss and back-reaction effects.
