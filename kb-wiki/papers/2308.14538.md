# Production of leptons from decay of heavy-flavor hadrons in high-energy nuclear collisions

> This paper presents a theoretical study on the production of the heavy-flavour decay lepton (HFL) in high-energy nuclear collisions at the LHC. The pp-baseline is calculated by the FONLL program, which matches the next-to-leading order pQCD calculation with the next-to-leading-log large-$p_T$ resummation. The in-medium propagation of heavy quarks is driven by the modified Langevin equations, which consider both the elastic and inelastic partonic interactions. We propose a method to separate the respective influence of the six factors, such as pp-spectra, the cold nuclear matter (CNM) effects, in-medium energy loss (E-loss), fragmentation functions (FFs), coalescence (Coal), and decay channels, which may contribute to the larger $R_{AA}$ of HFL $\leftarrow b$ compared to that of HFL $\leftarrow c$ in nucleus-nucleus collisions. Based on quantitative analysis, we demonstrate that both coalescence hadronization, decay channels and the mass-dependent E-loss play an essential role at $p_T<5$ GeV, while the latter dominates the higher $p_T$ region. It is also found that the influences of the CNM effects and FFs are insignificant. At the same time, different initial pp-spectra of charm and bottom quarks have a considerable impact at $p_T>5$ GeV. Furthermore, we explore the path-length dependence of jet quenching by comparing the HFL $R_{AA}$ in two different collision systems. Our investigations show smaller HFL $R_{AA}$ in Pb+Pb than in Xe+Xe within the same centrality bin, consistent with the ALICE data. The longer propagation time and more effective energy loss of heavy quarks in Pb+Pb collisions play critical roles in the stronger yield suppression of the HFL compared to that in Xe+Xe. In addition, we observe a scaling behavior of the HFL $R_{AA}$ in Xe+Xe and Pb+Pb collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the larger nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) of heavy-flavour decay leptons from bottom hadrons compared to those from charm hadrons in nucleus-nucleus collisions arises from a combination of mass-dependent energy loss, coalescence hadronization, and decay channels at low transverse momentum ($p_T<5$ GeV), while mass-dependent energy loss dominates at higher $p_T$, with cold nuclear matter effects and fragmentation functions playing insignificant roles.

## Method

The authors used the FONLL program to calculate the proton-proton baseline for heavy quark production, matching next-to-leading order pQCD with next-to-leading-log large-$p_T$ resummation. The in-medium propagation of heavy quarks was modeled using modified Langevin equations incorporating both elastic (collisional) and inelastic (radiative) partonic interactions, with the medium evolution described by the 2+1D CLVisc hydrodynamic model. Heavy quarks hadronized via a combination of fragmentation and coalescence mechanisms, and the semileptonic decays of heavy-flavor hadrons into leptons were implemented using the ParticleDecays module of PYTHIA8. A test particle method was developed to isolate the individual contributions of six factors (pp-spectra, cold nuclear matter effects, in-medium energy loss, fragmentation functions, coalescence, and decay channels) to the observed differences in $R_{AA}$ between charm- and bottom-derived leptons.

## Key numbers

* The jet transport parameter was fixed at $q_0=1.2$ GeV$^2$/fm at the LHC, extracted from fitting identified hadron production in A+A collisions.
* The diffusion coefficient $\kappa$ was obtained by $\chi^2$ fitting to D meson $R_{AA}$ data, yielding $\kappa\sim \pi T^3$, consistent with Lattice estimations of $\kappa=1.8\sim3.4\, T^3$ in pure gluon plasma.
* The hydrodynamic evolution used starting time $\tau_0=0.6$ fm, shear viscosity to entropy ratio $\eta_v/s=0.08$, and freeze-out temperature $T_f=137$ MeV.
* The Woods-Saxon parameters for $^{129}$Xe were $a=0.590$ fm, $R_0=5.40$ fm, $\beta_2=0.18$; for $^{208}$Pb were $a=0.546$ fm, $R_0=6.62$ fm, $\beta_2=0$.
* The model showed that coalescence hadronization, decay channels, and mass-dependent energy loss play essential roles at $p_T<5$ GeV, while mass-dependent energy loss dominates at higher $p_T$.
* Different initial pp-spectra of charm and bottom quarks have considerable impact at $p_T>5$ GeV.
* The model predicted smaller HFL $R_{AA}$ in Pb+Pb than in Xe+Xe within the same centrality bin, consistent with ALICE data.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of heavy quark energy loss and jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions, building on the Langevin transport approach developed by Cao (2013, 2014, 2015) and the FONLL framework by Cacciari (1998, 2001). It addresses the mass dependence of jet quenching by comparing charm and bottom quarks, a topic previously explored experimentally by ALICE (2015, 2022), CMS (2016, 2017, 2018), and ATLAS (2018, 2021), and theoretically by Cao (2013, 2016), Xing (2019, 2023), and Li (2020). The system size dependence study comparing Xe+Xe and Pb+Pb collisions follows work by Zigic (2018), Shi (2018), and Huss (2020). The results enable future experimental efforts to disentangle the various contributions to heavy-flavor decay lepton suppression and test the scaling behavior of $R_{AA}$ across different collision systems.
