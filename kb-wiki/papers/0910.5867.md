# Discovery potential for charmonium-like state Y(3940) by the meson photoproduction

> In this work, we investigate the discovery potential for Y(3940) by the photoproduction process $γp\to Y(3940)p$. The numerical result shows that the upper (lower) limit of the total cross section for $γp\to Y(3940)p$ is up to the order of 1 $nb$ (0.1 $μb$). Additionally, the background analysis and the Dalitz plot relevant to the production of Y(3940) are studied. The Dalitz plot analysis of Y(3940) production indicates that Y(3940) signal can be distinguished from the background clearly. The lower limit of the number of events of Y(3940) reaches up to 10/0.02GeV$^2$ for one billion collisions of $γp$ by studying the invariant mass spectrum of $J/ψω$. Experimental search for Y(3940) by the meson photoproduction is suggested.

## Key claim

The photoproduction process $\gamma p \to Y(3940)p$ offers a viable discovery channel for the charmonium-like state $Y(3940)$, with the total cross section's upper and lower limits depending on the undetermined decay width $\Gamma[Y(3940)\to J/\psi\omega]$, which is taken as 1 MeV (lower) and 34 MeV (upper) from Babar and Belle results.

## Method

The authors calculate the total and differential cross sections for $\gamma p \to Y(3940)p$ via $t$-channel $\omega$ exchange using vector meson dominance, with $J/\psi$ as the intermediate vector meson coupling to the photon. They consider both scalar ($0^{++}$) and tensor ($2^{++}$) quantum number assignments for $Y(3940)$, using effective Lagrangians for the $Y J/\psi\omega$ vertex and the $\omega NN$ vertex with monopole form factors. Background analysis is performed for the $\gamma p \to J/\psi\omega p$ process via pomeron exchange, and Dalitz plots of the $J/\psi\omega$ invariant mass spectrum are used to assess signal distinguishability.

## Key numbers

- Upper limit of total cross section for $\gamma p\to Y(3940)p$: on the order of 0.1 $\mu b$ (comparable to $J/\psi$ photoproduction at HERA).
- Lower limit of total cross section for $\gamma p\to Y(3940)p$: on the order of 1 $nb$ (comparable to $\psi(2S)$ or $\Upsilon$ photoproduction at HERA).
- Coupling constant $g_{Y J/\psi\omega}$ for scalar $Y(3940)$: lower limit 0.264 GeV$^{-1}$ (from $\Gamma=1$ MeV), upper limit 1.54 GeV$^{-1}$ (from $\Gamma=34$ MeV).
- Coupling constant $g_{Y J/\psi\omega}$ for tensor $Y(3940)$: lower limit 0.461 GeV$^{-1}$, upper limit 2.69 GeV$^{-1}$.
- Lower limit of number of events of $Y(3940)$: 10/0.02 GeV$^2$ for $1\times 10^9$ collisions of $\gamma p$ (from invariant mass spectrum of $J/\psi\omega$).
- Upper limit of number of events of $Y(3940)$: 450/0.02 GeV$^2$ for $50\times 10^6$ collisions of $\gamma p$.
- Decay width of $J/\psi\to e^+e^-$ used for parameter extraction: $5.55\pm0.14\pm0.02$ keV.

## Context

This work extends the search for charmonium-like states beyond $B$ meson decays, following prior studies of $Z^\pm(4430)$ photoproduction by Liu, Zhao, and Close (2008) and nucleon-antinucleon scattering proposals by Ke et al. (2008). The results suggest that $Y(3940)$ could be observed at HERA via photoproduction, with cross sections comparable to known $J/\psi$, $\psi(2S)$, and $\Upsilon$ photoproduction, and the Dalitz plot analysis shows the signal can be distinguished from pomeron-exchange background. The work enables experimental searches for $Y(3940)$ and other $X$, $Y$, $Z$ states in photoproduction, and highlights the need for precise measurement of the $\Gamma[Y(3940)\to J/\psi\omega]$ decay width.
