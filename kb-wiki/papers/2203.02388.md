# Contribution of the non-resonant mechanism to the double and single differential distributions over the invariant variables in the reaction $e^+ + e^- \to N + \bar N +ŽĆ^0$

> The general analysis of the differential cross section and various polarization observables is performed for the process $e^+ + e^- \to N + \bar N +ŽĆ^0 $ assuming that the annihilation occurs through the exchange of one virtual photon. The dependence of the differential distributions over invariant variables is derived for the reaction $e^+ + e^- \to N + \bar N +ŽĆ^0 $ in the so-called non-resonant mechanism, applying the conservation of the hadron electromagnetic currents and the P-invariance of the hadron electromagnetic interaction. The detection in an exclusive experimental set up where the nucleon (or antinucleon) and pion are detected in coincidence is considered. A number of single and double differential distributions have been calculated analytically and numerical estimates are given for the $p\bar pŽĆ^0$ and $n\bar nŽĆ^0$ channels, in the Born (non-resonant) approximation, in the energy range from threshold up to $s=16$ GeV$^2$.

## Key claim

The paper derives the general structure of the differential cross section and polarization observables for the reaction \(e^+ + e^- \to N + \bar N + \pi^0\) under the one-photon-annihilation approximation, but the numerical results are restricted to the non-resonant (continuum) mechanism only, using the Born approximation and neglecting any contributions from intermediate vector mesons or excited baryon resonances.

## Method

The authors constructed the hadronic tensor for the process using six invariant amplitudes, imposing gauge invariance and P-invariance of the hadron electromagnetic interaction. They calculated the non-resonant contribution from the two Feynman diagrams where the pion is emitted from the nucleon or antinucleon line, using the Dirac and Pauli nucleon electromagnetic form factors and the \(\pi^0 N N\) coupling constant. Analytical expressions for the double differential distributions over the invariant variables \((s_1, s_2)\), \((s_1, s_{12})\), \((t_1, t_2)\), and \((t_1, s_2)\) were derived, and numerical estimates were obtained for both the \(p\bar p\pi^0\) and \(n\bar n\pi^0\) channels using two different vector dominance model parametrizations of the nucleon form factors.

## Key numbers

- The energy range considered is from threshold up to \(s = 16\) GeV\(^2\).
- The kinematical region plots are shown at \(s = 10\) GeV\(^2\) for the double invariant variables.
- The invariant mass limits for the \(N\pi^0\) system are \((m + M)^2 \leq s_1 \leq (\sqrt{s} - M)^2\), where \(m\) is the pion mass and \(M\) is the nucleon mass.
- The limits for the nucleon-antinucleon invariant mass squared are \(4M^2 \leq s_{12} \leq (\sqrt{s} - m)^2\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for studying nucleon electromagnetic form factors in the time-like region, complementing experimental measurements from BESIII (2014, 2017) on \(e^+e^- \to p\bar p\pi^0\) near the \(\psi(3770)\) and \(Y(4260)\) resonances. The formalism provides the necessary basis for future inclusion of resonant contributions from vector mesons and excited baryons, and enables comparison with data from the PANDA experiment at FAIR and the HADES collaboration. The paper uses the vector dominance parametrizations of Iachello (1972) and Bijker (2004) for the nucleon form factors, and references the general approach of Adler (1968) for the invariant amplitude structure.
