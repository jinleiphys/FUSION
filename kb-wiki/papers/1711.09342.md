# Novel Soft-Pion Theorem for Long-Range Nuclear Parity Violation

> The parity-odd effect in the Standard Model weak neutral current reveals itself in the long-range parity-violating nuclear potential generated by the pion exchanges in the $ΔI=1$ channel with the parity-odd pion-nucleon coupling constant $h_π^1$. Despite decades of experimental and theoretical efforts, the size of this coupling constant is still not well-understood. In this Letter we derive a soft-pion theorem relating $h_π^1$ and the neutron-proton mass-splitting induced by an artificial parity-even counterpart of the $ΔI=1$ weak Lagrangian, and demonstrate that the theorem still holds exact at the next-to-leading order in chiral perturbation theory. A considerable amount of simplification is expected in the study of $h_π^1$ by using either lattice or other QCD models following its reduction from a parity-odd proton-neutron-pion matrix element to a simpler spectroscopic quantity. The theorem paves the way to much more precise calculations of $h_π^1$, and thus a quantitative test of the strangeness-conserving neutral current interaction of the Standard Model is foreseen.

# Novel Soft-Pion Theorem for Long-Range Nuclear Parity Violation

## Key claim

The authors derive a soft-pion theorem that relates the parity-odd pion-nucleon coupling constant \(h_\pi^1\) to the neutron-proton mass splitting induced by an artificial parity-even counterpart of the \(\Delta I=1\) weak Lagrangian, and demonstrate that this matching relation holds exactly at next-to-leading order in chiral perturbation theory, meaning its accuracy is expected to be better than one percent.

## Method

The authors construct a parity-even chiral partner of the \(\Delta I=1\) parity-violating weak Lagrangian by replacing each parity-odd four-quark operator with a corresponding parity-even operator while keeping the same Wilson coefficients. Using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory with a spurion method, they expand the leading-order weak chiral Lagrangian to first power of the pion field, revealing that the same low-energy constant \(\alpha\) governs both the neutron-proton mass splitting term and the parity-odd pion-nucleon coupling term. They then verify that the resulting matching relation \(F_\pi h_\pi^1 = -(\delta m_N)_{4q}/\sqrt{2}\) is preserved by all one-loop corrections and next-to-leading-order low-energy constant contributions.

## Key numbers

- The naive dimensional analysis estimate gives \(h_\pi^1 \sim \mathcal{O}(G_F F_\pi \Lambda_\chi) \sim 10^{-6}\)
- The DDH "reasonable range" for \(h_\pi^1\) is \((0-11) \times 10^{-7}\) with a "best value" around \(4.6 \times 10^{-7}\)
- The experimental upper bound from \({}^{18}\)F gamma emission gives \(h_\pi^1 \leq 1.3 \times 10^{-7}\)
- Extraction from proton-proton scattering yields \(h_\pi^1 = (1.1 \pm 2.0) \times 10^{-6}\)
- The accuracy of the soft-pion theorem is expected to be better than \((M_\pi/\Lambda_\chi)^2 \sim 1\%\) at physical quark masses, where \(M_\pi\) is the pion mass and \(\Lambda_\chi \approx 1\) GeV is the chiral symmetry breaking scale
- The only existing lattice QCD calculation at \(M_\pi \simeq 389\) MeV yielded a result with approximately 50% statistical uncertainty (Wasem, 2011)

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing problem of determining the parity-odd hadronic coupling constant \(h_\pi^1\), which has been studied using quark models, Skyrme models, QCD sum rules, and lattice QCD (Wasem, 2011) with limited precision. The theorem parallels techniques used for the parity-and-time-reversal-odd pion-nucleon coupling \(\bar{g}_\pi^i\), where relations to nucleon mass shifts were first derived using current algebra (Crewther, 1979; Pospelov, 2005) and later refined in chiral perturbation theory (de Vries, 2015; Mereghetti, 2015; Seng, 2016; Cirigliano, 2016). The reduction from a nucleon-pion matrix element to a simpler spectroscopic quantity eliminates three major complexities in lattice QCD calculations: power-law finite-volume effects from rescattering states, complications from energy insertion at the weak vertex, and suppressed overlap amplitudes for three-quark interpolating operators. This enables much more precise calculations of \(h_\pi^1\) using lattice QCD or other nonperturbative approaches, which can be directly compared to upcoming experimental results such as the NPDGamma measurement of gamma-ray asymmetry in neutron capture on the proton.
