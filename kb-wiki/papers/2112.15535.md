# Analytic solutions of relativistic dissipative spin hydrodynamics with radial expansion in Gubser flow

> We have derived the analytic solutions of dissipative relativistic spin hydrodynamics with Gubser expansion. Following the standard strategy of deriving the solutions in a Gubser flow, we take the Weyl rescaling and obtain the energy-momentum and angular momentum conversation equations in the $dS_{3}\times\mathbb{R}$ space-time. We then derive the analytic solutions of spin density, spin potential and other thermodynamic in $dS_{3}\times\mathbb{R}$ space-time and transform them back into Minkowski space-time $\mathbb{R}^{3,1}$. In the Minkowski space-time, the spin density and spin potential including the information of radial expansion decay as $\sim L^{-2}τ^{-1}$ and $\sim L^{-2}τ^{-1/3}$ in large $L$ limit, with $τ$ being proper time and $L$ being the characteristic length of the system, respectively. Moreover, we observe the non-vanishing spin corrections to the energy density and other dissipative terms in the Belinfante form of dissipative spin hydrodynamics. Our results can also be used as test beds for future simulations of relativistic dissipative spin hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The authors derive analytic solutions for relativistic dissipative spin hydrodynamics under Gubser flow, which includes radial expansion, showing that spin density and spin potential decay with proper time and system size in the large-\(L\) limit. The hedge is that these solutions require setting the bulk viscosity to zero, the heat flux to zero, and assuming a high-temperature equation of state with a linear relation between spin density and spin potential.

## Method

The authors start from the canonical form of relativistic dissipative spin hydrodynamics in Minkowski space-time, then perform a Weyl rescaling to transform the conservation equations of energy-momentum and angular momentum into the \(dS_{3}\times\mathbb{R}\) space-time. They impose Gubser symmetry, which fixes the fluid velocity to be static in the de Sitter coordinates, and assume the high-temperature equations of state \(\hat{e}=3\hat{p}\) and \(\hat{S}^{\mu\nu}=a\hat{T}^{2}\hat{\omega}^{\mu\nu}\). The resulting differential equations for energy density and spin density are solved analytically in the \(dS_{3}\times\mathbb{R}\) space-time, and the solutions are transformed back to Minkowski space-time via the inverse Weyl rescaling.

## Key numbers

* Spin density in Minkowski space-time decays as \(\sim L^{-2}\tau^{-1}\) in the large \(L\) limit, where \(\tau\) is proper time and \(L\) is the characteristic length of the system.
* Spin potential in Minkowski space-time decays as \(\sim L^{-2}\tau^{-1/3}\) in the large \(L\) limit.
* The equation of state used is \(\hat{e}=3\hat{p}\), corresponding to a speed of sound \(c_{s}^{2}=1/3\) in the ultra-relativistic limit.
* The transport coefficients \(\hat{\eta}_{s}/\hat{s}\) and \(\hat{\gamma}/\hat{s}\) are set to constants, with \(\hat{\eta}_{s}=\tau^{3}\eta_{s}\) and \(\hat{\gamma}=\tau^{3}\gamma\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analytic solutions for relativistic spin hydrodynamics in Bjorken flow (Wang, 2021) to include radial expansion via Gubser flow, which is more realistic for heavy-ion collisions. The solutions provide test beds for future numerical simulations of relativistic dissipative spin hydrodynamics and can be used to study the decay behavior of spin potential in modified Cooper-Frye formulas, as discussed in recent works by Buzzegoli (2021) and Liu (2021). The approach follows the strategy used for relativistic magnetohydrodynamics in Gubser flow by Pu (2016) and Roy (2015).
