# Polynomial Solutions of Shcrodinger Equation with the Generalized Woods Saxon Potential

> The bound state energy eigenvalues and the corresponding eigenfunctions of the generalized Woods Saxon potential are obtained in terms of the Jacobi polynomials. Nikiforov Uvarov method is used in the calculations. It is shown that the results are in a good agreement with the ones obtained before.

# Polynomial Solutions of Schrödinger Equation with the Generalized Woods-Saxon Potential

## Key claim
The bound-state energy eigenvalues of the generalized Woods-Saxon potential are obtained exactly in closed form using the Nikiforov-Uvarov method, but only for the s-wave (zero angular momentum) case, and the solution requires that the deformation parameter q be set to 1 to express eigenfunctions in terms of Jacobi polynomials.

## Method
The radial Schrödinger equation for the generalized Woods-Saxon potential is transformed using the variable s = -e^(2αr), where α = 1/(2a) and a is the surface thickness. The resulting differential equation is cast into the hypergeometric-type form required by the Nikiforov-Uvarov method, and the polynomial π(s) is determined by requiring the discriminant of the expression under the square root to vanish. The energy eigenvalues are obtained by equating the parameter λ from the method with the quantization condition λ = λ_n, and the wavefunctions are constructed from the Rodrigues relation yielding Jacobi polynomials.

## Key numbers
- The generalized Woods-Saxon potential parameters used for numerical examples: V₀ ≈ 40.5 + 13A MeV (with A = 56 as the target mass number), a = 0.65 fm, r₀ = 1.285 fm, R₀ = r₀A^(1/3), and C = 10 MeV.
- The energy eigenvalue formula (Eq. 25): E_{nq} = -(ħ²/(2ma²)) × { (1/16)[√(1 + 8mCa²/(ħ²q)) + (1+2n)]² + 4(mV₀a²/ħ²)² / [√(1 + 8mCa²/(ħ²q)) + (1+2n)]² + mV₀a²/ħ² }.
- When C = 0, the solution reduces to the standard Woods-Saxon potential result.

## Context
This work extends prior analytical solutions for exponential-type potentials (such as the Eckart potential and Rosen-Morse potential) to the generalized Woods-Saxon potential, which includes an additional surface term proportional to C. The Nikiforov-Uvarov method, previously applied to Coulomb-type potentials by other authors, is here applied to a nuclear shell-model potential. The results are compared with empirical optical-model parameters from Perey et al. and are stated to agree with earlier calculations. This enables future studies of neutron-nucleus scattering and metallic cluster descriptions using the generalized Woods-Saxon form.
