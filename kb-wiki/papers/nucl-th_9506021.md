# Colour-singlet strangelets at finite temperature

> Considering massless $u$ and $d$ quarks, and massive (150 MeV) $s$ quarks in a bag with the bag pressure constant $B^{1/4} = 145$ MeV, a colour-singlet grand canonical partition function is constructed for temperatures $T = 1-30$ MeV. Then the stability of finite size strangelets is studied minimizing the free energy as a function of the radius of the bag. The colour-singlet restriction has several profound effects when compared to colour unprojected case: (1) Now bulk energy per baryon is increased by about $250$ MeV making the strange quark matter unbound. (2) The shell structures are more pronounced (deeper). (3) Positions of the shell closure are shifted to lower $A$-values, the first deepest one occuring at $A=2$, famous $H$-particle ! (4) The shell structure at $A=2$ vanishes only at $T\sim 30$ MeV, though for higher $A$-values it happens so at $T\sim 20$ MeV.

## Key claim

The colour-singlet restriction on the grand canonical partition function increases the bulk energy per baryon by about 250 MeV compared to the colour-unprojected case, making strange quark matter unbound for the bag constant used (B^{1/4} = 145 MeV). The shell structures become more pronounced and shift to lower baryon numbers, with the first deepest shell closure occurring at A=2 (the H-particle) rather than at A=6.

## Method

The authors construct a colour-singlet grand canonical partition function for a system of massless u and d quarks and massive s quarks (150 MeV) confined in a MIT bag with bag constant B^{1/4} = 145 MeV, using the standard SU(3) projection technique. They compute the free energy as a function of bag radius for temperatures T = 1-30 MeV, and determine the equilibrium radius by minimizing the free energy at fixed baryon number A. The baryon number is fixed by adjusting the quark chemical potential so that the excess number of quarks over antiquarks equals 3A, and the stability condition balances the gas pressure against the bag pressure constant B.

## Key numbers

- Bulk energy per baryon for colour-singlet case: approximately 1.1 GeV (compared to 850 MeV for colour-unprojected case), making strange quark matter unbound for B^{1/4} = 145 MeV
- First deepest shell closure occurs at A=2 (H-particle) with mass approximately 2.5 GeV
- Shell closures for higher baryon numbers occur at A = 8, 14, 18, 28
- Shell structure at A=2 vanishes only at T approximately 30 MeV
- Shell structures for higher A-values vanish at T approximately 20 MeV (compared to about 10 MeV for colour-unprojected case)
- Temperature range studied: T = 1-30 MeV

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature study of strangelets by Mustafa and Ansari (1995, nucl-th/9505044) and the zero-temperature shell model approach of Gilson and Jaffe (1993) by imposing the colour-singlet constraint that is natural for a confined system. The results contradict the earlier expectation that strange quark matter might be absolutely stable as conjectured by Witten (1984), at least for the bag parameters used here. The prediction of a metastable H-particle at A=2 and the shifted shell closures at A=8, 14, 18, 28 provide specific targets for experimental searches in relativistic heavy ion collisions, such as those by Rusek et al. (1995).
