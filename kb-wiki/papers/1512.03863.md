# Gluonic Structure of the Constituent Quark

> Based on both the constituent quark picture and the instanton model for QCD vacuum, we calculate the unpolarized and polarized gluon distributions in the constituent quark and in the nucleon. Our approach consists of the two main steps. At the first step, we calculate the gluon distributions inside the constituent quark generated by the perturbative quark-gluon interaction, the non-perturbative quark-gluon interaction, and the non-perturbative quark-gluon-pion anomalous chromomagnetic interaction. The non-perturbative interactions are related to the existence of the instantons, strong topological fluctuations of gluon fields, in the QCD vacuum. At the second step, the convolution model is applied to derive the gluon distributions in the nucleon. A very important role of the pion field in producing the unpolarized and the polarized gluon distributions in the hadrons is discovered. We discuss a possible solution of the proton spin problem.

## Key claim

The authors calculate gluon distributions in the constituent quark and nucleon using the instanton model, finding that the non-perturbative quark-gluon-pion anomalous chromomagnetic interaction dominates both unpolarized and polarized gluon distributions at small momentum fractions, while perturbative QCD dominates only at large momentum fractions; this provides a possible resolution of the proton spin problem without requiring the large gluon polarization needed by the axial anomaly mechanism.

## Method

The authors first compute the unpolarized and polarized gluon distributions inside a constituent quark using the Altarelli-Parisi approach, considering three contributions: perturbative quark-gluon interaction, non-perturbative quark-gluon interaction from instanton-induced anomalous chromomagnetic coupling, and non-perturbative quark-gluon-pion interaction from the chiral-symmetry-preserving extension of that coupling. They then apply a convolution model with valence quark distributions in the nucleon to obtain the nucleon-level gluon distributions, using the instanton form factor and the anomalous chromomagnetic moment derived from the instanton model.

## Key numbers

- Instanton average size: $\rho_c \approx 1/3$ fm
- Confinement size: $R_c \approx 1$ fm
- Constituent quark mass in instanton vacuum: $M_q = 86$ MeV
- Expansion parameter: $\delta = (M_q \rho_c)^2 \approx 0.02$
- Strong coupling at instanton scale: $\alpha_s(\rho_c) \approx 0.5$
- Pion decay constant: $F_\pi = 93$ MeV
- Pion mass: $m_\pi = 140$ MeV
- Perturbative cutoff scale: $1/\rho_c^2 = 0.35$ GeV$^2$
- At $Q^2 = 10$ GeV$^2$, the integrated gluon polarization in the nucleon is $\int_0^1 dx \Delta g(x) = 0.19$
- The unpolarized gluon distribution at $Q^2 = 2$ GeV$^2$ matches the GJR parametrization $xg(x) = 1.37 x^{-0.1} (1-x)^{3.33}$
- Sphaleron energy: $E_{sph} = 3\pi / (4\alpha_s(\rho_c) \rho_c) \approx 2.7$ GeV
- Instanton packing fraction: $f \approx 1.5 \times 10^{-2}$

## Context

This work extends the instanton-based approach to gluon structure pioneered by Diakonov (1996) and Kochelev (1997), correcting an earlier kinematic error in the non-perturbative gluon distribution calculation. It provides the first calculation of the quark-gluon-pion anomalous interaction contribution to gluon distributions, which was proposed by Balla (1997) and Diakonov (1996) to preserve PCAC. The results enable a natural explanation of both hard and soft Pomeron behavior seen in DIS and high-energy hadron cross sections, as discussed by Landshoff (2009), and offer an alternative to the axial anomaly solution of the proton spin problem proposed by Altarelli (1988).
