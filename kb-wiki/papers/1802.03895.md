# Isospin Conservation in Neutron Rich Systems of Heavy Nuclei

> It is generally believed that isospin would diminish in its importance as we go towards heavy mass region due to isospin mixing caused by the growing Coulomb forces. However, it was realized quite early that isospin could become an important and useful quantum number for all nuclei including heavy nuclei due to neutron richness of the systems~\cite{robson}. Lane and Soper~\cite{lane} also showed in a theoretical calculation that isospin indeed remains quite good in heavy mass neutron rich systems. In this paper, we present isospin based calculations~\cite{jain,swati} for the fission fragment distributions obtained from heavy-ion fusion fission reactions. We discuss in detail the procedure adopted to assign the isospin values and the role of neutron multiplicity data in obtaining the total fission fragment distributions. We show that the observed fragment distributions can be reproduced rather reasonably well by the calculations based on the idea of conservation of isospin. This is probably the very first direct experimental evidence of the validity of isospin in heavy nuclei, which arises largely due to the neutron-rich nature of heavy nuclei and their fragments. This result may eventually become useful for the theories of nuclear fission and also in other practical applications.

## Key claim

The paper claims that isospin conservation can reproduce observed fission fragment distributions in heavy-ion fusion-fission reactions, providing "direct experimental evidence" for the validity of isospin as a good quantum number in heavy neutron-rich nuclei, with the caveat that the agreement is "reasonably well" and shows deviations at shell closures.

## Method

The authors analyzed the heavy-ion fusion-fission reaction 208Pb(18O, f) forming 226Th as a compound nucleus. They assigned isospin values to fission fragments using Kelson's conjectures, which state that neutron emission leads to highly excited states with T > T3 and that fragments are preferentially emitted in isobaric analog states. Relative yields were calculated from the square of isospin Clebsch-Gordan coefficients multiplied by weight factors obtained from experimental neutron multiplicity data, then compared with experimental fission fragment distributions from Bogachev et al.

## Key numbers

- The compound nucleus 226Th has isospin T_CN = 23, determined from T_X(208Pb) = 22 and T_Y(18O) = 1
- For the lead isotope chain, isospin values range from near zero in neutron-deficient isotopes to nearly 50 in neutron-rich isotopes
- 208Pb has ground state isospin T = 22, as T3 = 22
- The experimental mass resolution in fission fragment data is about 3-4 units
- Experimental data from Bogachev et al. have an estimated error of 10-30%
- For A = 112, three isobars were considered: 112Ru with T3 = 12, 112Pd with T3 = 10, and 112Cd with T3 = 8; the assigned isospin is T = 12

## Context

This work extends the theoretical predictions of Lane and Soper (1962) who showed isospin remains good in heavy neutron-rich systems, and the calculations of Sliv and Kharitonov (1965) who found similar isospin admixture in 16O and 208Pb. The results provide experimental support for Kelson's conjectures about isobaric analog state formation in fission, and may inform theories of nuclear fission and practical applications involving neutron-rich heavy nuclei.
