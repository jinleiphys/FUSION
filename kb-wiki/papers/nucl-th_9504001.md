# Three-body halos. III. Effects of finite core-spin

> We investigate effects of finite core-spin in two-neutron halos in the three-body model consisting of two neutrons and a core. The states in the neutron-core subsystem can then have an additional splitting due to the coupling of the neutron and core spins. The connection between the structure of the total system and the states of the neutron-core subsystem depends strongly on this spin splitting. However, the spatial structure is essentially unchanged. The neutron momentum distribution in a nuclear dissociation reaction will consist of a broad and a narrow distribution arising from the two spin split neutron-core virtual states. The large distance behavior is investigated and the conditions for the Efimov effect turn out to be more restrictive. The magnetic moment is equal to the core-value independent of spin splitting and energies of the neutron-core states. The excited states of opposite parity depend strongly on the spin splitting. The parameters in our numerical examples are chosen to be appropriate for $^{11}$Li and $^{19}$B.

## Key claim

Finite core-spin introduces a splitting of the neutron-core virtual s-states into two components (with total spin \(s_c \pm 1/2\)), which strongly affects the connection between the structure of the total three-body system and the states of the neutron-core subsystem, but the spatial structure of the halo remains essentially unchanged.

## Method

The three-body system (two neutrons plus a core) is described using Faddeev equations solved via an expansion in hyperspherical coordinates, where the angular functions for each hyperradius \(\rho\) are eigenfunctions of the angular part of the Faddeev equations. The neutron-neutron interaction is parametrized with a gaussian potential including spin-spin, spin-orbit, and tensor terms, adjusted to reproduce low-energy scattering data; the neutron-core potential is also gaussian, with a spin-splitting term \(\gamma_s \mathbf{s}_c \cdot \mathbf{s}_n\) introduced in the s-wave. The resulting coupled radial differential equations are solved to obtain binding energies, angular eigenvalues, and wavefunction components.

## Key numbers

- The neutron-neutron singlet s-wave scattering length is \(a(^1S_0) = 18.8\ \text{fm}\) and effective range \(r_e(^1S_0) = 2.76\ \text{fm}\); triplet p-wave scattering lengths are \(a(^3P_0) = 3.6\ \text{fm}\), \(a(^3P_1) = -2.0\ \text{fm}\), \(a(^3P_2) = 0.30\ \text{fm}\).
- The neutron-core gaussian range is \(b_{nc} = 2.55\ \text{fm}\); the neutron-neutron gaussian range is \(b_{nn} = 1.8\ \text{fm}\).
- For parameters appropriate to \(^{11}\text{Li}\), the total three-body binding energy is set to \(0.3\ \text{MeV}\).
- For \(^{19}\text{B}\), the two-neutron separation energy is estimated as \(500 \pm 450\ \text{keV}\).
- For the case of both neutron-core scattering lengths infinitely large, the lowest \(\tilde{\lambda}\) eigenvalue for \(^{11}\text{Li}\) is \(\tilde{\lambda} = -0.00545\), giving an effective radial potential \(-0.25545/\rho^2\).
- For the case \(a_- \ll \rho \ll a_+\) in \(^{11}\text{Li}\), the lowest positive \(\tilde{\lambda}\) is \(0.702627\); for \(a_+ \ll \rho \ll a_-\) it is \(1.34930\).

## Context

This paper is the third in a series on three-body halos (Fedorov, Jensen, 1994; Fedorov, Garrido, Jensen, 1994) and extends previous work that assumed a spin-zero core by explicitly including finite core-spin effects. It shows that the Efimov effect, previously discussed for halos (Fedorov, Jensen, 1993), becomes more restrictive: both spin-split neutron-core scattering lengths must be extremely large for infinitely many bound states to appear, whereas a single large scattering length is insufficient. The results are applied to \(^{11}\text{Li}\) and \(^{19}\text{B}\), and the formalism enables future calculations of momentum distributions, magnetic moments, and excited states in halo nuclei with nonzero core spin.
