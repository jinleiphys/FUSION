# Ab initio coupled-cluster study of 16-O

> We report converged results for the ground and excited states and matter density of 16-O using realistic two-body nucleon-nucleon interactions and coupled-cluster methods and formalism developed in quantum chemistry. Most of the binding is obtained with the coupled-cluster singles and doubles approach. Additional binding due to three-body clusters (triples) is minimal. The coupled-cluster method with singles and doubles provides a good description of the matter density, charge radius, charge form factor, and excited states of a 1-particle-1-hole nature, but it cannot describe the first excited 0+ state. Incorporation of triples has no effect on the latter finding.

## Key claim

The paper reports converged coupled-cluster calculations for 16-O using realistic two-body nucleon-nucleon interactions, finding that the CCSD approach captures essentially all correlations from two-body forces (with triples contributing less than 1% to binding), but the Hamiltonian underbinds 16-O by approximately 1 MeV per particle, pointing to the need for three-body forces.

## Method

The authors used realistic two-body nucleon-nucleon interactions (Idaho-A and N3LO, derived from effective-field theory) renormalized via a no-core G-matrix approach, and solved the A-body problem using coupled-cluster methods from quantum chemistry. Ground-state calculations employed CCSD (Coupled-Cluster Singles and Doubles) in up to 8 major oscillator shells (480 single-particle basis states), with triples corrections via CR-CCSD(T); excited states and properties used equation-of-motion CCSD (EOMCCSD) and CR-EOMCCSD(T) in up to 7 oscillator shells (336 single-particle states). The one-body density matrix was computed from the left and right eigenstates of the similarity-transformed Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- Extrapolated CCSD ground-state energy for Idaho-A: -119.4 MeV; extrapolated CR-CCSD(T) energy: -120.5 MeV; difference of 1.1 MeV (triples contribute less than 1% to binding)
- Estimated Idaho-A ground-state energy including Coulomb (11.2 MeV): -109.3 MeV (experimental value: -128 MeV), corresponding to underbinding of ~1 MeV per particle
- N3LO CCSD ground-state energies (including Coulomb): -112.4 MeV (N=7) and -111.2 MeV (N=8); N3LO CR-CCSD(T) at N=7: -112.8 MeV
- CR-EOMCCSD(T) excitation energy for the first 3- state: 12.0 MeV (experimental: 6.12 MeV)
- EOMCCSD excitation energies for the negative-parity quartet (5 oscillator shells, Idaho-A): 13.57 MeV (3-), 15.37 MeV (1-), 17.07 MeV (2-), 17.15 MeV (0-)
- Charge radii for Idaho-A: 2.45 fm (5 shells), 2.50 fm (6 shells), 2.51 fm (7 shells); experimental: 2.73 ± 0.025 fm
- Occupation probabilities (Idaho-A, N=7): 3.2% for 0d5/2 and 2.3% for 1s1/2; experimental: 2.17 ± 0.12% and 1.78 ± 0.36%, respectively

## Context

This work extends earlier coupled-cluster calculations by Mihaila and Heisenberg (using bare interactions) and the authors' own previous work (Kowalski et al., 2004) in a small 4-shell model space, now achieving converged results in up to 8 oscillator shells for 16-O. The results agree with no-core shell-model calculations using similar two-body Hamiltonians (Navratil et al., 2004, private communication) in finding that two-body interactions alone cannot reproduce the experimental 3- excitation energy. The paper demonstrates that low-cost coupled-cluster methods are a promising alternative to traditional shell-model diagonalization for medium-mass nuclei, and establishes that three-body forces are needed to resolve the ~1 MeV per particle binding energy discrepancy.
