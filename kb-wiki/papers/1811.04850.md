# Description of bulk observables in Au+Au collisions at top RHIC energy in the integrated HydroKinetic Model

> The results on the main bulk observables obtained in the simulations within the integrated hydrokinetic model (iHKM) of Au+Au collisions at the RHIC energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV are presented along with the corresponding experimental data from the STAR and the PHENIX collaborations. The simulations include all the stages of the collision process: formation of the initial state, its gradual thermalization and hydrodynamization, viscous relativistic hydro-evolution, system's hadronization and particlization, and, finally, an expansion of the interacting hadron-resonance gas. The model gives a satisfactory description of charged-particle multiplicities, particle number ratios, transverse momentum spectra for pions, kaons, protons and antiprotons, charged-particle $v_2$ coefficients, and femtoscopy radii at all collision centralities. It is demonstrated how one can estimate the times of the pion and kaon maximal emission from the femto-scales.

## Key claim

The integrated HydroKinetic Model (iHKM) provides a satisfactory simultaneous description of multiple bulk observables in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, but the description of pion femtoscopy radii shows systematic deviations (overestimated R_out and underestimated R_side) that are not present in the kaon femtoscopy results, and the model requires a centrality-dependent strangeness suppression factor gamma_S to achieve good agreement for kaon and (anti)proton spectra.

## Method

The iHKM simulates all stages of heavy-ion collisions: initial state formation using a GLISSANDO Glauber Monte Carlo with a linear combination of wounded nucleon and binary collision contributions, pre-thermal evolution in the relaxation time approximation with a Color-Glass-Condensate-like initial momentum distribution (anisotropy Lambda=100), viscous relativistic hydrodynamics in the Israel-Stewart formalism with a Laine-Schroeder equation of state modified for small baryon (mu_B=21 MeV) and strange (mu_S=5 MeV) chemical potentials, particlization at T=165 MeV, and final hadronic cascade using the UrQMD model. A strangeness suppression factor gamma_S, parameterized as gamma_S(tau_p) = A exp(-b/tau_p) with A=1.1 and b=0.8 fm/c, is applied multiplicatively to hadron yields based on the particlization time tau_p for each centrality.

## Key numbers

- Maximal initial energy density at tau_0=0.1 fm/c: epsilon_0 = 235 GeV/fm^3 for Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV (compared to 679 GeV/fm^3 at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV and 1067 GeV/fm^3 at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV)
- Binary collision contribution parameter: alpha = 0.18 at RHIC (compared to 0.24 at LHC)
- Chemical potentials at hadronization (T=165 MeV): mu_B = 21 MeV, mu_S = 5 MeV
- Mean charged particle density for 0-5% centrality: iHKM gives 688, STAR data gives 691 +/- 49 (at |eta|<0.5)
- Mean charged particle density for 70-80% centrality: iHKM gives 19, STAR data gives 22 +/- 2
- Strangeness suppression factor gamma_S ranges from 0.989 (0-10% centrality, tau_p=7.55 fm/c) to 0.777 (70-80% centrality, tau_p=2.30 fm/c)
- Combined fit to p_T spectra (0.45<p_T<1.0 GeV/c) yields effective temperature T=141 MeV, alpha_pi=7.86+/-2.11, alpha_K=5.54+/-2.61
- Maximal emission time for pions: tau_pi = 7.12 +/- 0.01 fm/c
- Maximal emission time for kaons: tau_K = 9.71 +/- 0.02 fm/c (with kaon alpha=0.12+/-0.02)

## Context

This work extends the iHKM framework, previously successful at LHC energies (Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV), to top RHIC energy Au+Au collisions, adjusting parameters for the lower collision energy and non-zero chemical potential. The model builds on the earlier hydrokinetic model (HKM) that had been applied to RHIC data, and the results enable comparison of emission time scales between RHIC and LHC energies (finding maximal emission times about 2 fm/c smaller at RHIC). The finding that kaon maximal emission time exceeds pion maximal emission time, attributed to K* meson decays and recombinations, confirms a similar observation in ALICE experimental analysis at LHC.
