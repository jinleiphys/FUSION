# Neutron star properties in a chiral SU(3) model

> We investigate various properties of neutron star matter within an effective chiral $SU(3)_L \times SU(3)_R$ model. The predictions of this model are compared with a Walecka-type model. It is demonstrated that the importance of hyperon degrees are strongly depending on the interaction used, even if the equation of state near saturation density is nearly the same in both models. While the Walecka-type model predicts a strange star core with strangeness fraction $f_S \approx 4/3$, the chiral model allows only for $f_S \approx 1/3$ and predicts that $Σ^0$, $Σ^+$ and $Ξ^0$ will not exist in star, in contrast to the Walecka-type model.

## Key claim

The chiral SU(3) model predicts a maximum neutron star mass of 1.84 solar masses for stars without hyperons, significantly lower than the 2.16 solar masses predicted by the Walecka-type model (TM1), despite both models having nearly identical nuclear matter properties at saturation density. When hyperons are included, both models yield similar maximum masses (1.52 vs 1.55 solar masses), but the chiral model predicts that Σ⁰, Σ⁺, and Ξ⁰ hyperons will not exist in the star core, resulting in a strangeness fraction of only about 1/3 compared to about 4/3 in the Walecka-type model.

## Method

The authors constructed a QCD-motivated chiral SU(3)_L × SU(3)_R model in the mean-field approximation, incorporating baryon octet states (p, n, Λ, Σ⁻, Σ⁰, Σ⁺, Ξ⁻, Ξ⁰), scalar mesons (σ, ζ), vector mesons (ω, φ, ρ), a dilaton field χ representing the gluon condensate, and leptons (e, μ). They solved the field equations self-consistently under charge neutrality and β-equilibrium conditions at zero temperature, using parameter set C1 with additional explicit symmetry breaking terms to adjust hyperon potentials to U_Λ^N = -28 MeV. The results were compared with the Walecka-type model using parameter set TM1, which includes cubic and quartic σ self-interactions and quartic ω self-interactions.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³ (chiral model) vs 0.145 fm⁻³ (TM1 model)
- Binding energy at saturation: B/A = -16 MeV (chiral) vs -16.3 MeV (TM1)
- Effective nucleon mass ratio: m*_N/m_N = 0.61 (chiral) vs 0.63 (TM1)
- Incompressibility: K = 276 MeV (chiral) vs 281 MeV (TM1)
- Symmetry energy: a_sym = 40.4 MeV (chiral) vs 36.9 MeV (TM1)
- Maximum mass for npe stars: M_max = 1.84 M_⊙ (chiral) vs 2.16 M_⊙ (TM1)
- Radius at maximum mass for npe stars: R = 11.10 km (chiral) vs 11.98 km (TM1)
- Central density at maximum mass for npe stars: ρ_c = 6.98ρ₀ (chiral) vs 6.08ρ₀ (TM1)
- Maximum mass with hyperons: M_max = 1.52 M_⊙ (chiral) vs 1.55 M_⊙ (TM1)
- Radius at maximum mass with hyperons: R = 11.64 km (chiral) vs 12.14 km (TM1)
- Central density at maximum mass with hyperons: ρ_c = 5.92ρ₀ (chiral) vs 5.97ρ₀ (TM1)
- Strangeness fraction at center of maximum-mass star with hyperons: f_S = 0.33 (chiral) vs 0.75 (TM1)
- Hyperon potential depths (with a=1 parametrization): U_Λ^N = -28 MeV, U_Σ^N = +3.2 MeV, U_Ξ^N = -42 MeV
- Direct URCA process threshold: ρ_B ≥ 2.2ρ₀ (both models)

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral SU(3) model studies (Paper2, Paper3) to neutron star matter and directly compares predictions with the Walecka-type model (TM1 parameter set from Sugahara and Toki). The results contrast with earlier Walecka-type calculations by Glendenning and Weber that predicted hyperon-rich cores with strangeness fractions near 4/3. The finding that maximum masses alone cannot constrain nuclear matter incompressibility or effective nucleon mass has implications for interpreting future neutron star mass and radius measurements from pulsar observations such as PSR 1913+16.
