# Studying the parameters of the extended $σ$-$ω$ model for neutron star matter

> In this work we study the parameters of the extended $σ$-$ω$ model for neutron star matter by a Bayesian analysis on state-of-the-art multi-messenger astronomy observations, namely mass, radius and tidal deformabilities. We have considered three parameters of the model, the Landau mass $m_L$, the nuclear compressibility $K_0$, and the value of the symmetry energy $S_0$, all at saturation density $n_0$. As a result, we are able to estimate the values of the Landau mass of f $m_L = 739\pm17$ MeV, whereas the values of $K_0$ and $S_0$ fall within already known empirical values. Furthermore, for neutron stars we find the most probable value of 13 km $<R_{1.4}<$ 13.5 km and the upper mass limit of $M_{max} \approx 2.2$ M$_{\odot}$.

## Key claim

The authors use a Bayesian analysis of multi-messenger astronomy observations to estimate three parameters of the extended sigma-omega model for neutron star matter, finding that the Landau mass is constrained to m_L = 739 +/- 17 MeV while the nuclear compressibility K_0 and symmetry energy S_0 fall within previously known empirical ranges, with the caveat that the Landau mass has a dominant effect on the maximum neutron star mass that is roughly ten times larger than the effect of K_0 and one hundred times larger than that of S_0.

## Method

The authors solved the extended sigma-omega relativistic mean-field model to generate 1000 equations of state by varying the Landau mass m_L, nuclear compressibility K_0, and symmetry energy S_0 at saturation density within ranges of 80% to 110% of reference values. They computed neutron star mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations, then applied Bayesian inference using observational constraints from the pulsars PSR J0740+6620 and PSR J0348+0432, the gravitational wave event GW170817, and the NICER measurement of PSR J0030+0451.

## Key numbers

* Landau mass at saturation: m_L = 739 +/- 17 MeV from the Bayesian analysis
* Most probable radius for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: 13 km less than R_1.4 less than 13.5 km
* Upper mass limit: M_max approximately 2.2 solar masses
* Reference saturation density: n_0 = 0.156 fm^{-3}
* Reference compressibility: K_0 = 240 MeV
* Reference symmetry energy: S_0 = 32.5 MeV
* Range of Landau mass values sampled: 0.623 GeV to 0.857 GeV
* Range of compressibility values sampled: 0.192 GeV to 0.264 GeV
* Range of symmetry energy values sampled: 0.026 GeV to 0.036 GeV
* Maximum mass for the stiffest equation of state: approximately 2.7 solar masses

## Context

This work extends a previous single-parameter study by Alvarez-Castillo (2020) that estimated the Landau mass as 750 +/- 30 MeV while keeping other parameters fixed, now generalizing to a three-parameter Bayesian analysis. The results are consistent with laboratory measurements of nuclear compressibility around 240 +/- 20 MeV from Shlomo (2016) and symmetry energy of 31.7 +/- 3.2 MeV from Li (2013) and Oertel (2016), and the methodology follows earlier Bayesian approaches by Steiner (2010) and Raithel (2017) that used X-ray bursters and multi-polytrope parameterizations. The findings enable tighter constraints on the high-density equation of state for neutron stars and can be compared with the accompanying study by Blaschke (2020) that focuses on equations of state producing a third branch in the mass-radius diagram.
