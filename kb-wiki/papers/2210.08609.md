# Probing gluon TMDs with reconstructed and tagged heavy flavor hadron pairs at EIC

> Study of the transverse structure of the proton is one of the major physics goals of the upcoming Electron Ion Collider (EIC). The gluon transverse momentum dependent distributions (TMD) form an essential focus of this effort and are important towards understanding the angular momentum contribution to proton spin as well as QCD factorization. However, very limited experimental constraints on the gluon TMD exist currently. As the heavy quark production in lepton-nucleon DIS gets a dominant contribution from the photon-gluon-fusion process, heavy quark production makes an attractive tool to probe gluon distributions in nucleons. In this paper we present a study of heavy flavor hadron pair reconstruction at a future EIC detector with MAPS based inner tracking and vertexing subsystems to constrain gluon TMD. We utilize the excellent track pointing resolution provided by the detector to exclusively reconstruct heavy flavor hadron pairs via their hadronic decay channels and also to develop a heavy flavor hadron tagging algorithm. Statistical uncertainty projections on azimuthal asymmetries corresponding to gluon TMD at the EIC is evaluated. The heavy flavor tagging is found to substantially enhance the purity of heavy flavor hadron pair selection, and the statistical precision of the measurement compared to that from exclusive reconstruction. The correlation between the azimuthal angle of the transverse momentum of the gluon initiating the process and that of the corresponding heavy flavor hadron pair was also studied and found to be well correlated. This study opens up heavy flavor hadron pair measurements as an attractive channel to access gluon TMD at the EIC.

## Key claim

Heavy flavor hadron pair measurements at the EIC can provide access to gluon TMDs, but the statistical precision depends critically on the reconstruction method: a heavy flavor tagging algorithm using displaced decay topology yields about an order of magnitude improvement in statistical uncertainty projections compared to exclusive reconstruction of heavy flavor hadrons via their hadronic decay channels.

## Method

The authors performed a fast simulation study of electron-proton collisions at 18 GeV electron and 275 GeV proton beam energies using PYTHIA 6.4 event generation, with detector response modeled via parameterized single-track momentum and pointing resolutions from GEANT4 simulations of an all-silicon MAPS-based tracking and vertexing detector. Heavy flavor hadron pairs were reconstructed either explicitly through the D0 → K−π+ channel with topological selection cuts, or via a heavy flavor tagging algorithm that uses a Boosted Decision Tree trained on pseudojet variables (sum of track DCA values, number of displaced tracks) to identify heavy flavor decays without requiring explicit hadron identification. The correlation between the gluon transverse momentum azimuthal angle and the reconstructed heavy flavor hadron pair azimuthal angle was evaluated, and statistical uncertainty projections for the transverse single spin asymmetry AUT and the cos(2φT) observable were computed.

## Key numbers

- D0 meson reconstruction uses topological cuts: decaylength > 40 μm, pair DCA < 150 μm, cosθ > 0.98, achieving improved signal-to-background ratio at higher D0 transverse momentum.
- Heavy flavor tagging without topological selection yields signal purity of 2%; with topological selection, purity of about 50% is achieved at signal efficiency of approximately 10%, and purity of about 60% with tighter cuts.
- Tagged heavy flavor pair purity improves from 2% to approximately 70% with topological selection at reasonable signal efficiency.
- The input gluon-level asymmetry of 6% is reduced by about 30% at hadronization and to about 50% of the parton level signal after tagging, indicating retained correlation.
- Events are generated with Q2 > 1 GeV2 and 0.005 < y < 0.95, using electron beam energy 18 GeV and proton beam energy 275 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous simulation studies of heavy flavor hadron pair measurements at the EIC by Zheng et al. (2018), which did not include detector effects and were limited to explicit reconstruction. The study demonstrates that a heavy flavor tagging algorithm utilizing displaced decay topology can substantially improve statistical precision over exclusive reconstruction, providing comparable uncertainties to inclusive di-hadron and di-jet measurements from prior studies. This enables heavy flavor hadron pair measurements as a complementary channel to access gluon TMDs at the EIC, particularly at intermediate and large Bjorken x where inclusive measurements are dominated by quark contributions.
