# Nuclear Data Adjustment for Nonlinear Applications in the OECD/NEA WPNCS SG14 Benchmark -- A Bayesian Inverse UQ-based Approach for Data Assimilation

> The Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD) Working Party on Nuclear Criticality Safety (WPNCS) proposed a benchmark exercise to assess the performance of current nuclear data adjustment techniques applied to nonlinear applications and experiments with low correlation to applications. This work introduces Bayesian Inverse Uncertainty Quantification (IUQ) as a method for nuclear data adjustments in this benchmark, and compares IUQ to the more traditional methods of Generalized Linear Least Squares (GLLS) and Monte Carlo Bayes (MOCABA). Posterior predictions from IUQ showed agreement with GLLS and MOCABA for linear applications. When comparing GLLS, MOCABA, and IUQ posterior predictions to computed model responses using adjusted parameters, we observe that GLLS predictions fail to replicate computed response distributions for nonlinear applications, while MOCABA shows near agreement, and IUQ uses computed model responses directly. We also discuss observations on why experiments with low correlation to applications can be informative to nuclear data adjustments and identify some properties useful in selecting experiments for inclusion in nuclear data adjustment. Performance in this benchmark indicates potential for Bayesian IUQ in nuclear data adjustments.

## Key claim

Bayesian Inverse Uncertainty Quantification (IUQ) can perform nuclear data adjustments for nonlinear applications where Generalized Linear Least Squares (GLLS) fails, though the method's scalability to higher-dimensional parameter spaces and computational requirements remain challenges that must be addressed.

## Method

The authors applied Bayesian IUQ, originally developed for nuclear thermal-hydraulics and fuel performance problems, to the OECD/NEA WPNCS SG14 benchmark exercise consisting of 4 experiments (Albert, Bohr, Chadwick, Dyson) and 3 applications (Bravo, Castle, Trinity) with 5 input parameters representing nuclear cross-sections. They compared IUQ results against GLLS and Monte Carlo Bayes (MOCABA) across 5 cases using different subsets of experiments, where IUQ uses machine learning surrogate models to reduce the computational burden of Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling from posterior parameter distributions given measured data.

## Context

This work extends Bayesian IUQ methods previously demonstrated in nuclear thermal-hydraulics (Wu 2018) and nuclear fuel performance (Wu 2018) to nuclear data adjustment problems, comparing against the established GLLS method used in SCALE (Rearden 2011) and MCNP's Whisper (Kiedrowski 2015) as well as MOCABA (Hoefer 2015, 2019, 2021). The results indicate that while GLLS remains effective for linear criticality safety applications, IUQ and MOCABA show promise for expanding data adjustment to nonlinear applications, with IUQ providing complete empirical posterior parameter distributions without linearity or normality assumptions.
