# Reactor Fuel Fraction Information on the Antineutrino Anomaly

> We analyzed the evolution data of the Daya Bay reactor neutrino experiment in terms of short-baseline active-sterile neutrino oscillations taking into account the theoretical uncertainties of the reactor antineutrino fluxes. We found that oscillations are disfavored at $2.6σ$ with respect to a suppression of the $^{235}\text{U}$ reactor antineutrino flux and at $2.5σ$ with respect to variations of the $^{235}\text{U}$ and $^{239}\text{Pu}$ fluxes. On the other hand, the analysis of the rates of the short-baseline reactor neutrino experiments favor active-sterile neutrino oscillations and disfavor the suppression of the $^{235}\text{U}$ flux at $3.1σ$ and variations of the $^{235}\text{U}$ and $^{239}\text{Pu}$ fluxes at $2.8σ$. We also found that both the Daya Bay evolution data and the global rate data are well-fitted with composite hypotheses including variations of the $^{235}\text{U}$ or $^{239}\text{Pu}$ fluxes in addition to active-sterile neutrino oscillations. A combined analysis of the Daya Bay evolution data and the global rate data shows a slight preference for oscillations with respect to variations of the $^{235}\text{U}$ and $^{239}\text{Pu}$ fluxes. However, the best fits of the combined data are given by the composite models, with a preference for the model with an enhancement of the $^{239}\text{Pu}$ flux and relatively large oscillations.

## Key claim

The Daya Bay evolution data disfavor pure active-sterile neutrino oscillations at 2.6 sigma compared to a model with only a suppression of the 235U flux, and at 2.5 sigma compared to a model with independent variations of the 235U and 239Pu fluxes; however, the global rate data from short-baseline reactor experiments favor oscillations and disfavor the pure flux-variation models at 3.1 sigma and 2.8 sigma respectively. The best fits to the combined data come from composite models that include both flux variations and oscillations, with a preference for an enhancement of the 239Pu flux together with relatively large oscillations.

## Method

The authors performed least-squares fits of the Daya Bay evolution data (eight values of the cross section per fission as a function of the effective 239Pu fission fraction) and of the global reactor antineutrino rate data from 15 experiments, using five different models: variation of the 235U flux only (model 235); independent variations of the 235U and 239Pu fluxes (model 235+239); active-sterile neutrino oscillations (model OSC); and composite models combining oscillations with either a 235U flux variation (model 235+OSC) or a 239Pu flux variation (model 239+OSC). The theoretical uncertainties of the Saclay-Huber reactor antineutrino fluxes were incorporated via a covariance matrix for the fractional uncertainties of the four fissionable isotopes. Non-nested model comparisons were performed using Monte Carlo simulations to compute p-values of the chi-squared differences.

## Key numbers

* Daya Bay evolution data: model 235 gives r235 = 0.927 +/- 0.022, implying sigma_f,235 = 6.20 +/- 0.15 (units not specified, but cross section per fission in 10^-43 cm^2/fission implied by context)
* Daya Bay evolution data: model 235+239 gives r235 = 0.922 +/- 0.025 and r239 = 0.974 +/- 0.046, implying sigma_f,235 = 6.17 +/- 0.16 and sigma_f,239 = 4.29 +/- 0.20
* Daya Bay evolution data: model OSC gives sin^2(2theta_ee) = 0.12 +/- 0.06, with oscillations favored over no-oscillation at only 1.9 sigma
* Global rate data: model 235 gives r235 = 0.939 +/- 0.012, implying sigma_f,235 = 6.28 +/- 0.08
* Global rate data: model 235+239 gives r235 = 0.950 +/- 0.013 and r239 = 0.873 +/- 0.064, implying sigma_f,235 = 6.36 +/- 0.09 and sigma_f,239 = 3.84 +/- 0.28
* Global rate data: model OSC has best-fit Delta m^2_41 = 0.48 eV^2 and sin^2(2theta_ee) = 0.13, with oscillations favored over model 235 at 3.1 sigma
* Combined analysis: model 235 gives r235 = 0.934 +/- 0.010, implying sigma_f,235 = 6.25 +/- 0.07
* Combined analysis: model 235+239 gives r235 = 0.934 +/- 0.009 and r239 = 0.970 +/- 0.032, implying sigma_f,235 = 6.25 +/- 0.06 and sigma_f,239 = 4.27 +/- 0.14
* Combined analysis: model 239+OSC gives the best fit, with oscillations favored over no-oscillation at 3.0 sigma; this model is preferred over model 235 at 4.2 sigma

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Daya Bay collaboration (An 2017) by explicitly incorporating theoretical uncertainties of the Saclay-Huber reactor antineutrino fluxes (Mueller 2011, Mention 2011, Huber 2011) and by considering composite models that allow both flux variations and active-sterile neutrino oscillations. The results contradict the pure oscillation interpretation of the reactor antineutrino anomaly, showing that the Daya Bay evolution data can be explained by a suppression of the 235U flux alone, while the global rate data favor oscillations. The analysis enables future work to distinguish between flux normalization errors and new physics by combining fuel-evolution data from multiple reactor experiments with improved theoretical flux calculations.
