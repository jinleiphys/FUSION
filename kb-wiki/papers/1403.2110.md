# Data analysis techniques, differential cross sections, and spin density matrix elements for the reaction $γp \rightarrow ϕp$

> High-statistics measurements of differential cross sections and spin density matrix elements for the reaction $γp \to ϕp$ have been made using the CLAS detector at Jefferson Lab. We cover center-of-mass energies ($\sqrt{s}$) from 1.97 to 2.84 GeV, with an extensive coverage in the $ϕ$ production angle. The high statistics of the data sample made it necessary to carefully account for the interplay between the $ϕ$ natural lineshape and effects of the detector resolution, that are found to be comparable in magnitude. We study both the charged- ($ϕ\to K^+ K^-$) and neutral- ($ϕ\to K^0_S K^0_L$) $K\overline{K}$ decay modes of the $ϕ$. Further, for the charged mode, we differentiate between the cases where the final $K^-$ track is directly detected or its momentum reconstructed as the total missing momentum in the event. The two charged-mode topologies and the neutral-mode have different resolutions and are calibrated against each other. Extensive usage is made of kinematic fitting to improve the reconstructed $ϕ$ mass resolution. Our final results are reported in 10- and mostly 30-MeV-wide $\sqrt{s}$ bins for the charged- and the neutral-mode, respectively. Possible effects from $K^+ Λ^\ast$ channels with $p K\overline{K}$ final-states are discussed. These present results constitute the most precise and extensive $ϕ$ photoproduction measurements to date and in conjunction with the $ω$ photoproduction results recently published by CLAS, will greatly improve our understanding of low energy vector meson photoproduction.

## Key claim

The paper presents the most precise and extensive measurements of differential cross sections and spin density matrix elements for the reaction γp → φp to date, covering center-of-mass energies from 1.97 to 2.84 GeV, but the results are conditional on careful accounting for the interplay between the φ natural lineshape and detector resolution effects, which are comparable in magnitude and require calibration across three different decay topologies.

## Method

The CLAS detector at Jefferson Lab was used with a 4.023-GeV electron beam producing bremsstrahlung photons incident on a liquid-hydrogen target. Three event topologies were analyzed: charged-two-track (K⁺ detected, K⁻ reconstructed via missing momentum), charged-three-track (all final-state charged particles detected), and neutral-mode (φ → K⁰_S K⁰_L with K⁰_S → π⁺π⁻). Kinematic fitting was applied to improve reconstructed φ mass resolution, and the natural φ lineshape was modeled as a mass-dependent relativistic Breit-Wigner with a P-wave barrier factor convolved with a Gaussian detector resolution function.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy range covered: 1.97 to 2.84 GeV
- Final results reported in 10-MeV-wide √s bins for the charged-mode and 30-MeV-wide √s bins for the neutral-mode
- φ natural width Γ₀ ≈ 4.26 MeV, comparable to CLAS detector resolution of 1-2 MeV
- Photon energy resolution was about 0.1% of the incident beam energy (4.023 GeV)
- Timing resolution of the photon tagger was 120 ps
- CLAS momentum resolution was approximately 0.5%
- The data sample contained roughly 20 billion triggers recorded during the g11a run period
- The K⁰_S → π⁺π⁻ branching fraction used was 60.2%
- The K⁰_S → π⁰π⁰ branching fraction (not used) was 39.7%

## Context

This work extends the earlier CLAS measurements at Eγ = 3.6 GeV (Anciant 2000, McCormick 2004) and the low-energy near-threshold measurements from Bonn (Besch 1978), SAPHIR (Barth 2003), and LEPS (Mibe 2005). The results are intended to be used in conjunction with the recently published CLAS ω photoproduction results to improve understanding of low-energy vector meson photoproduction, and will enable coupled-channel analyses of the φ, Λ(1520), and ω channels to resolve the nature of the non-monotonic behavior observed near threshold.
