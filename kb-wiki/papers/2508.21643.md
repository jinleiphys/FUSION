# Small $x$ behavior in QCD from maximal entanglement and conformal invariance

> Recent evidence suggests that, at small Bjorken $x$, QCD evolution drives the proton into a state of maximal entanglement. If the evolution kernel is assumed to be conformally invariant -- as is the case for the Balitsky-Fadin-Kuraev-Lipatov (BFKL) equation -- we can describe it by a conformal field theory. Moreover, the central charge $c$ of the corresponding conformal field theory emerges as the key parameter governing the $x$-dependence of both the entanglement entropy and the structure function. Here we apply the exact Bethe Ansatz methods to the quantum spin chain dual to Lipatov's high energy effective action to extract the central charge of the theory, and find that $c=1$. This implies the $\sim x^{-1/3}$ small $x$ behavior for the structure function -- the prediction that can be tested at the forthcoming Electron-Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The authors use exact Bethe Ansatz methods to extract the central charge of the conformal field theory (CFT) dual to the Balitsky-Fadin-Kuraev-Lipatov (BFKL) equation, finding \(c=1\); this result depends on the assumptions that QCD evolution at small Bjorken \(x\) drives the proton into a state of maximal entanglement and that the evolution kernel is conformally invariant.

## Method

The authors map the BFKL equation for reggeized gluons in multicolor QCD to an integrable quantum spin chain (Lipatov's spin chain), specifically the XXX Heisenberg model with spin \(s=-1\), which is equivalent to the quantum nonlinear Schrodinger equation. They apply the quantum inverse scattering method and Bethe Ansatz to compute finite-size corrections to the ground state energy of this spin chain in the thermodynamic limit. By comparing the finite-size energy expression with the universal CFT prediction \(E_{\text{CFT}} = E_{\text{bulk}} - c \pi v_F / (6L)\), they extract the central charge \(c\) from the coefficient of the \(1/L\) correction.

## Key numbers

- The central charge extracted from finite-size corrections is \(c = 1\).
- The predicted small-\(x\) behavior of the gluon structure function is \(xG(x) \sim x^{-1/3}\), corresponding to a BFKL intercept \(\lambda = c/3 = 1/3\).
- The entanglement entropy in deep inelastic scattering grows as \(S(t) = (1/3) \ln(t/\tau)\) after a local quench in Lipatov's spin chain, as found in prior work by Hao (2019) and Zhang (2021).

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Kharzeev (2017) that at small Bjorken \(x\) the proton reaches a maximally entangled state, linking entanglement entropy to the gluon distribution via \(S(x) = \ln[xG(x)]\). It provides a rigorous derivation of the central charge \(c=1\) for the BFKL effective theory, confirming the earlier implicit assumption in Hao (2019) and Zhang (2021) that the spin chain corresponds to a CFT with \(c=1\). The result places BFKL dynamics in the universality class of free fermions and compactified bosons, and the predicted \(x^{-1/3}\) scaling can be tested at the forthcoming Electron-Ion Collider.
