# Nuclear matrix element for two neutrino double beta decay from 136Xe

> The nuclear matrix element for the two neutrino double beta decay (DBD) of 136Xe was evaluated by FSQP (Fermi Surface Quasi Particle model), where experimental GT strengths measured by the charge exchange reaction and those by the beta decay rates were used. The 2 neutrino DBD matrix element is given by the sum of products of the single beta matrix elements via low-lying (Fermi Surface) quasi-particle states in the intermediate nucleus. 136Xe is the semi-magic nucleus with the closed neutron-shell, and the beta + transitions are almost blocked. Thus the 2 neutrino DBD is much suppressed. The evaluated 2 neutrino DBD matrix element is consistent with the observed value.

# Nuclear Matrix Element for Two Neutrino Double Beta Decay from 136Xe

## Key claim

The Fermi Surface Quasi Particle (FSQP) model, using experimentally measured single beta matrix elements without artificially adjusting nuclear parameters, reproduces the observed extremely small two-neutrino double beta decay matrix element for 136Xe, which is suppressed by a factor of about 5 compared to other nuclei due to the closed neutron-shell structure.

## Method

The author evaluated the 2νββ matrix element for 136Xe → 136Ba using the FSQP model, where the matrix element is expressed as the sum of products of successive single β− and β+ matrix elements via low-lying quasi-particle states in the intermediate nucleus 136Cs. Effective coupling constants g^{eff}_− = 0.13 and g^{eff}_+ = 0.15 (in units of g_A = 1.256) were derived from experimental Gamow-Teller strengths measured by charge exchange reactions at RCNP Osaka and from neighboring β+ decays of 138Nd and 138Ce, respectively. The occupation and vacancy amplitudes (V and U factors) were obtained from BCS equations for the closed neutron-shell nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Observed 2νββ matrix element for 136Xe: M^{2ν} = 0.010 m_e^{-1} (measured by EXO collaboration)
- Evaluated FSQP running sum of M^{2ν}: approximately 0.012 m_e^{-1}, consistent with the observed value within 20% (ΔM^{2ν} ≈ 0.002 m_e^{-1})
- Effective coupling constant for β− transitions: g^{eff}_− = 0.13 (in units of g_A = 1.256), derived from observed matrix element M^−_{11} = 0.39 for the lowest intermediate state at E_{11} = 0.59 MeV
- Effective coupling constant for β+ transitions: g^{eff}_+ = 0.15 (in units of g_A = 1.256), derived from neighboring β+ decays of 138Nd and 138Ce
- Summed GT− strength from FSQP: 0.77, close to the observed value of 0.71 from charge exchange reaction
- Product of effective coupling constants: g^{eff}_− × g^{eff}_+ ≈ 0.02, representing universal core effects
- UV factor product for β+ transitions in 136Xe: approximately 0.05, compared to approximately 0.25 for half-filled shell nuclei

## Context

This work extends the FSQP model (Ejiri 1996, 2009) to the key double beta decay nucleus 136Xe, using recent experimental GT− strength measurements from charge exchange reactions at RCNP (Puppe 2011) and the EXO measurement of the 2νββ rate (Ackerman 2011). The results contrast with QRPA models (Vogel 1986, Suhonen 1998, Rodin 2007, Simkovic 2001, Civitarese 2005) which explain small M^{2ν} values by cancellation of two terms at a certain g_{pp} parameter; the FSQP model instead attributes the suppression to the small UV factors for β+ transitions in the semi-magic nucleus. This work enables improved evaluation of 0νββ nuclear matrix elements for 136Xe, which is being used by two experimental groups (EXO and KamLAND-Zen) for neutrino mass searches, and suggests that 0νββ matrix elements may also be reduced by similar nuclear core and surface effects.
