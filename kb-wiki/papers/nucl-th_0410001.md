# Deuteron Compton Scattering: A Random Walk

> In this sketch, some recent developments in Compton scattering off the deuteron are reviewed. The strong energy-dependence of the scalar magnetic dipole polarisability β_M1 turns out to be crucial to understand the data from Saskatoon at 94 MeV. Chiral Effective Field Theory is used to extract the static iso-scalar dipole polarisabilities as \barα^s=12.6\pm1.4_{stat}\pm1.0_{wavefu} and \barβ^s=2.3\pm1.7_{stat}\pm0.8_{wavefu}, in units of 10^{-4} fm^3. Therefore, proton and neutron polarisabilities are identical within error bars. For details and a better list of references, consult the given references.

## Key claim

The strong energy-dependence of the scalar magnetic dipole polarisability β_M1, driven by the Δ(1232) resonance, resolves the apparent discrepancy between extractions of static iso-scalar polarisabilities from deuteron Compton scattering data at 94 MeV (Saskatoon) and those from lower-energy data; the static approximation "static-plus-small-slope" for β_M1(ω) is inadequate at energies near 90 MeV.

## Method

Chiral Effective Field Theory (χEFT) with explicit Δ(1232) degrees of freedom was used to compute deuteron Compton scattering to next-to-leading order (N³LO in the counting used). The two short-distance, energy-independent parameters δα and δβ were fitted to deuteron Compton scattering data above 60 MeV from Urbana (49 and 69 MeV), Lund (55 and 66 MeV), and Saskatoon (94 MeV), using the full energy-dependent (dynamical) polarisabilities rather than static approximations.

## Key numbers

- Static iso-scalar electric dipole polarisability (unconstrained fit): ᾱˢ = 12.6 ± 1.4_stat ± 1.0_wavefu (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³)
- Static iso-scalar magnetic dipole polarisability (unconstrained fit): β̄ˢ = 2.3 ± 1.7_stat ± 0.8_wavefu (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³)
- With Baldin sum rule (ᾱˢ + β̄ˢ = 14.5 ± 0.6): ᾱˢ = 12.4 ± 0.8_stat ± 0.8_wavefu, β̄ˢ = 2.1 ∓ 0.8_stat ± 0.7_wavefu (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³)
- At ω ≈ 90 MeV, β_M1ˢ is about 3 units larger than its static value
- Proton static polarisabilities from same method: ᾱᵖ = 11.0 ± 1.4_stat ± 0.4_sys, β̄ᵖ = 2.8 ∓ 1.4_stat ± 0.4_sys (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³)

## Context

This work extends the χEFT approach to nucleon polarisabilities developed by Griesshammer, Hemmert, and collaborators (2003-2004) and applies it to deuteron Compton scattering, resolving the "SAL-puzzle" where previous analyses by Levchuk and L'vov (2000), Beane et al. (2004), and the original Saskatoon publication (Horn et al., 2000) gave conflicting static polarisability values from the same 94 MeV data. The key advance is the explicit inclusion of the Δ(1232) resonance's energy-dependent contribution to β_M1, which was subsumed into weakly-dispersive short-distance operators in the analysis of Beane et al. (2004). This enables consistent extraction of neutron polarisabilities from deuteron data and shows that proton and neutron polarisabilities are identical within error bars. Future work includes extending the analysis to higher energies, incorporating the Δ width, and using polarised experiments to extract spin-polarisabilities.
