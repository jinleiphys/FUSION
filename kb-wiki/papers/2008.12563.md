# Quark matter in light neutron stars

> Higher-order repulsive interactions are included in the three-flavor NJL model in order to describe the quark phase of an hybrid star. The effect of 4-quark and 8-quark vector-isoscalar interactions in the stability of hybrid star configurations is analyzed. The presence of a 8-quark vector-isoscalar channel is seen to be crucial in generating large quark branches in the $M(R)$ diagram. This is due to its stiffening effect on the quark matter equation of state which arises from the non-linear density dependence of the speed of sound. This additional interaction channel allows for the appearance of a quark core at moderately low NS masses, $\sim 1M_{\odot}$, and provides the required repulsion to preserve the star stability up to $\sim2.1M_{\odot}$. Furthermore, we show that both the heaviest NS mass generated, $M_{\text{max}}$, and its radii, $R_{\text{max}}$, are quite sensitive to the strength of 8-quark vector-isoscalar channel, leading to a considerable decrease of $R_{\text{max}}$ as the coupling increases. This behavior imprints a considerable deviation from the purely hadronic matter equation of state in the $Λ(M)$ diagram, which might be a possible signature of the quark matter existence, even for moderately low NS masses, $\sim 1.4\, M_\odot$. The resulting $M(R)$ and $Λ(R)$ relations are in accordance with the latest astrophysical constraints from NICER and Ligo/VIRGO observations, respectively.

## Key claim

The inclusion of an 8-quark vector-isoscalar interaction in the three-flavor NJL model allows hybrid neutron stars to develop quark cores at masses as low as approximately 1 solar mass while simultaneously reaching maximum masses up to approximately 2.1 solar masses, provided the 4-quark vector coupling is kept small; the abstract's claim of compatibility with observations depends on this specific tuning of the two vector coupling parameters.

## Method

The authors constructed hybrid equations of state using a Maxwell construction for a first-order phase transition between hadronic matter (described by the DDME2 relativistic mean-field model) and quark matter (described by the SU(3)_f NJL model with four-quark and eight-quark vector-isoscalar interactions). They fixed the NJL model parameters to reproduce vacuum meson properties, treated the vector coupling ratios xi_omega = G_omega/G_S and xi_omegaomega = G_omegaomega/G_S^4 as free parameters, and fixed the bag constant at B = 10 MeV/fm^3. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities for charge-neutral matter in beta-equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass reaches approximately 2.1 solar masses for the hybrid EoS with (xi_omega = 0, xi_omegaomega = 20)
- Quark cores appear at masses as low as approximately 0.9 solar masses for small xi_omega and large xi_omegaomega values
- The heaviest quark core mass reaches approximately 1.8 solar masses for (xi_omega = 0, xi_omegaomega = 20), corresponding to 85% of the star having quark degrees of freedom
- The lightest quark core mass is approximately 0.8 solar masses for (xi_omega = 0.2, xi_omegaomega = 0)
- Speed of sound squared at central density of maximum mass stars reaches approximately 0.93 for the stiffest quark matter configurations
- The onset density of strangeness occurs at approximately 0.5 fm^{-3} independent of vector couplings
- The bag constant is fixed at B = 10 MeV/fm^3
- The saturation density used is n_0 = 0.155 fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hybrid stars using the NJL model (Pereira 2016, Bonanno 2011) by incorporating eight-quark vector interactions, which were previously explored only in two-flavor NJL models (Benic 2014). The results contrast with earlier findings using local and nonlocal NJL models with vector interactions (Ranea-Sandoval 2015) that typically produced no hybrid stars or only small quark branches. The paper enables future searches for observational signatures of quark matter in neutron stars, particularly through deviations in the tidal deformability-mass relation for stars around 1.4 solar masses, which could be tested by combining NICER and LIGO/Virgo observations.
