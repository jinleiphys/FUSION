# Heated nuclear matter, condensation phenomena and the hadronic equation of state

> The thermodynamic properties of heated nuclear matter are explored using an exactly solvable canonical ensemble model. This model reduces to the results of an ideal Fermi gas at low temperatures. At higher temperatures, the fragmentation of the nuclear matter into clusters of nucleons leads to features that resemble a Bose gas. Some parallels of this model with the phenomena of Bose condensation and with percolation phenomena are discussed. A simple expression for the hadronic equation of state is obtained from the model.

## Key claim

The paper presents an exactly solvable canonical ensemble model for heated nuclear matter that, at intermediate temperatures, exhibits features resembling a Bose gas and a phase transition analogous to percolation, but the model's low-temperature behavior reduces to that of an ideal Fermi gas, and the "cusp" in specific heat is smoothed by finite-size effects.

## Method

The authors construct a canonical partition function for a system of A nucleons that can fragment into clusters, with the weight of each fragmentation outcome proportional to \(x^{m} y^{A-m}\), where \(m\) is the number of clusters. The parameters \(x\) and \(y\) are functions of volume, temperature, and density, incorporating translational phase space, binding energy, and internal excitations via a Skyrme density-dependent approach. Thermodynamic quantities such as specific heat and pressure are expressed as analytic functions of the cumulant moments of the cluster multiplicity, which are computed recursively.

## Key numbers

- The binding energy per nucleon at saturation density \(\rho_0\) is set to 8.0 MeV.
- The critical exponent \(\tau\) is chosen as \(\tau = 5/2\) to match a three-dimensional Bose gas, but data from Gilkes et al. suggest \(\tau \approx 2.2\).
- The critical point condition is \((x/y)_c / A = 1/\zeta(\tau-1)\), where \(\zeta\) is the Riemann zeta function, and this requires \(\tau > 2\) for the infinite cluster to exist.
- The order parameter \(\sqrt{\langle n_{\infty} \rangle}\) scales as \((T - T_C)^{1/2}\), giving the second critical exponent \(\beta = 1/2\).

## Context

This work extends earlier models by Mekjian, Chase, and others (Mekjian 1991, Chase 1993) that provided simple expressions for cluster yields, by making the thermodynamic functions explicit and analytic. The model draws parallels to Feynman's approach to the \(\lambda\) transition in liquid helium (Feynman 1953) and to percolation theory (Stauffer 1979, Campi 1986), and it enables the study of phase transitions in finite nuclear systems via finite-size scaling, as discussed by Gilkes (1994) and Campi (1988).
