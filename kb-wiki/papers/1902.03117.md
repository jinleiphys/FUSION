# Direct probing of the cluster structure in ${}^{12}$Be via $α$-knockout reaction

> Background: Recent theoretical and experimental researches using proton-induced $α$-knockout reactions provide direct manifestation of $α$-cluster formation in nuclei. In recent and future experiments, $α$-knockout data are available for neutron-rich beryllium isotopes. In $^{12}$Be , rich phenomena are induced by the formation of $α$-clusters surrounded by neutrons, for instance, breaking of the neutron magic number $N=8$. Purpose: Our objective is to provide direct probing of the $α$-cluster formation in the $^{12}$Be target through associating the structure information obtained by a microscopic theory with the experimental observables of $α$-knockout reactions. Method: We formulate a new wave function of the Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-R{ö}pke (THSR) type for the structure calculation of ${}^{12}$Be nucleus and integrate it with the distorted wave impulse approximation framework for the $α$-knockout reaction calculation of $^{12}$Be$(p,pα)^{8}$He. Results: We reproduce the low-lying spectrum of the $^{12}$Be nucleus using the THSR wave function and discuss the cluster structure of the ground state. Based on the microscopic wave function, the optical potentials and $α$-cluster wave function are determined and utilized in the calculation of ${}^{12}$Be($p,pα$)${}^{8}$He reaction at 250 MeV. The possibility of probing the clustering state of $^{12}$Be through this reaction is demonstrated by analysis of the triple differential cross sections that are sensitively dependent on the $α$-cluster amplitude at the nuclear surface. Conclusions: This study provides a feasible approach to validate directly the theoretical predictions of clustering features in the $^{12}$Be nucleus through the $α$-knockout reaction.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the triple differential cross section (TDX) of the $^{12}$Be$(p,p\alpha)^{8}$He reaction at 250 MeV is sensitively dependent on the $\alpha$-cluster amplitude at the nuclear surface, providing a feasible approach to validate theoretical predictions of clustering features in $^{12}$Be, though the mixing ratio between cluster configurations depends on model-dependent parameters in the nucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Method

The authors formulated a new Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Ropke (THSR) type wave function for $^{12}$Be that superposes three cluster configurations (binary $\alpha+^{8}$He, $\pi$-orbit molecular orbit, and $\sigma$-orbit molecular orbit) and integrated it with the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) framework for the $\alpha$-knockout reaction. The optical potentials and $\alpha$-cluster wave function were determined from the microscopic THSR wave function using the Melbourne $G$-matrix interaction, and the reduced width amplitude (RWA) was approximated to extract the $\alpha$-cluster amplitude for the reaction calculation.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy of $^{12}$Be calculated as $-$59.5 MeV (experimental value is $-$68.6 MeV)
- Excitation energy of the $2_1^+$ state in $^{12}$Be: 2.2 MeV (consistent with experimental value 2.1 MeV)
- Excitation energy of the $0_2^+$ state in $^{12}$Be: 4.1 MeV (AMD result: 3.7 MeV, experimental: 2.3 MeV)
- Incident proton kinetic energy: 250 MeV; emitted proton kinetic energy: 180 MeV
- Emission angle of outgoing proton: $(\theta_1^{\rm L},\phi_1^{\rm L}) = (60^\circ,0^\circ)$; emitted $\alpha$ angle $\theta_2^{\rm L}$ around $51^\circ$, $\phi_2^{\rm L}=180^\circ$
- With default spin-orbit coupling strength (3700 MeV), the ground state contains 90% $\alpha+^{8}$He component; with weakened strength (3000 MeV), the $\alpha+^{8}$He component reduces to about 60%
- Ratio of about 2 observed for TDXs between default and weakened spin-orbit cases at zero momentum

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation of the $^{10}$Be$(p,p\alpha)^{6}$He reaction (Lyu et al., 2018) to the more complex $^{12}$Be nucleus, where coexistence of binary cluster and molecular orbit configurations leads to breaking of the neutron magic number $N=8$. The study builds on theoretical predictions from the generalized two-center cluster model (Ito et al., 2004, 2008, 2012) and antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (Kanada-En'yo et al., 2003; Kobayashi et al., 2012; Kanada-En'yo et al., 2016) regarding cluster configurations in neutron-rich beryllium isotopes. The results enable experimental validation of clustering predictions through comparison with emerging ($p,p\alpha$) reaction data in inverse kinematics at the Radioactive Isotope Beam Factory (Yang et al., 2017).
