# Components of an algebraic solution of the multichannel problem of low-energy n-${}^{12}$C scattering plus sub-threshold (${}^{13}$C) states

> The effects of components in an assumed model interaction potential, as well as of the order to which its deformation is taken, upon resonances in the low-energy cross sections and upon sub-threshold bound states of the compound nucleus (${}^{13}$C) are discussed.

## Key claim

The multichannel algebraic scattering (MCAS) method, when applied to low-energy n-${}^{12}$C scattering and sub-threshold ${}^{13}$C bound states, requires not only central and spin-orbit potentials but also orbit-orbit ($\ell \cdot \ell$) and spin-spin ($I \cdot s$) operator terms to achieve agreement with data, with the $\ell \cdot s$ and $\ell \cdot \ell$ components having large but offsetting effects that are crucial for the final spectrum.

## Method

The authors used the MCAS formalism with a collective-model interaction potential matrix coupling the ground $0^+_1$, $2^+_1$ (4.44 MeV), and $0^+_2$ (7.65 MeV) states of ${}^{12}$C, with nuclear surface deformation taken to second order. They systematically removed individual potential components ($\ell \cdot s$, $\ell \cdot \ell$, $I \cdot s$) and reduced the deformation order (0th, 1st, 2nd) to isolate the effects on calculated elastic scattering cross sections and sub-threshold bound state spectra, while also varying the deformation parameter $\beta_2$ from -0.52 down to -0.0001.

## Key numbers

- Base potential geometry: $R_0 = 3.09$ fm, $a = 0.65$ fm, $\beta_2 = -0.52$
- Central potential strengths: odd parity -49.144 MeV, even parity -47.563 MeV
- Spin-orbit ($V_{\ell s}$) strengths: odd parity 7.384 MeV, even parity 9.176 MeV
- Orbit-orbit ($V_{\ell \ell}$) strengths: odd parity 4.559 MeV, even parity 0.610 MeV
- Spin-spin ($V_{Is}$) strengths: odd parity -4.770 MeV, even parity -0.052 MeV
- Excising the $\ell \cdot s$ component increases binding of negative parity states by approximately 2.5 MeV
- Excising the $\ell \cdot \ell$ component increases binding of negative parity states by approximately 5.5 MeV
- The $\frac{5}{2}^-$ state in the Base calculation has an extremely small width ($\le 10^{-11}$ MeV)
- With $\beta_2$ decreasing, the $\frac{5}{2}^-$ state moves into positive energy at energies $\le 0.3$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the MCAS formalism previously detailed by Amos et al. (2003) for nucleon-nucleus scattering, which used sturmian expansions of a model interaction matrix. The paper systematically tests the sensitivity of results to each component of the phenomenological coupled-channel interaction, demonstrating that the $\ell \cdot \ell$ and $I \cdot s$ operator terms are necessary additions to the usual central and spin-orbit potentials. The findings enable future applications of MCAS to other nucleon-nucleus systems by clarifying which potential components are essential and how the deformation order affects convergence of both bound state spectra and resonance structures.
