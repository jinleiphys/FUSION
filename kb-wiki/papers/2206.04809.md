# Renormalization group evolution of optical potentials: explorations using a toy model

> To take full advantage of experimental facilities such as FRIB for applications to nuclear astrophysics, nuclear structure, and explorations of neutrinos and fundamental symmetries, we need a better understanding of the interplay of reaction and structure theory. The renormalization group (RG) is the natural tool for maintaining a consistent treatment of reaction and structure. Here we make a first study of RG for optical potentials, which are important ingredients for direct reactions. To simplify the analysis, we use a pedagogical one-dimensional model and evolve toward low RG resolution using the similarity RG (or SRG). We show how SRG decoupling at low resolution carries over to the optical potential and enhances perturbative approximations, and how induced SRG nonlocality compares to the nonlocality of the optical potential. We discuss the results in the larger context of consistent SRG evolution of operators and wave functions in the analysis of direct reactions.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that SRG evolution of a toy-model optical potential preserves decoupling of high- and low-momentum modes and enhances perturbative approximations, but only when the full reaction problem is solved at each SRG scale and the optical potential is then constructed from the evolved Hamiltonian, not by evolving the optical potential itself.

## Method
The authors extend a one-dimensional two-level scattering model from Lipkin (1973) to construct an optical potential via the Feshbach formalism, using both delta-function and finite-range two-Gaussian potentials. They apply similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolution to the full Hamiltonian at each resolution scale λ, then numerically construct the optical potential from the evolved components using the Feshbach projection formalism, solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for phase shifts at each scale.

## Key numbers
- Ground state energy E_g = 0 and excited state energy E_e = 4 (in units with ℏ²/2m = 1) for the two-level system
- Delta-function potential strengths: V_0 = 12, V_1 = 7 for the analytic demonstration case
- Two-Gaussian potential parameters: Off-Diagonal I uses V_1 = 7.5, V_2 = -7.5, σ_1 = 0.2, σ_2 = 0.8; Off-Diagonal II uses V_1 = 2.0, V_2 = -2.0, σ_1 = 0.2, σ_2 = 0.8
- Regulator cutoff applied at k = √25 (corresponding to E_k = 25) for decoupling tests
- Phase shifts converge to unevolved values when SRG resolution λ is decreased below the regulator cutoff

## Context
This work extends the SRG methodology previously applied to nucleon-nucleon potentials (Bogner 2006, Jurgenson 2008) to the domain of optical potentials, which are essential for direct reaction theory. The one-dimensional toy model follows the strategy used by Jurgenson (2008) for developing three-body force evolution techniques. The results enable future studies of consistent SRG evolution of operators and wave functions in direct reaction analysis, and address the open question of how to implement structure-reaction consistency in phenomenological optical potential approaches.
