# Production of heavy neutron-rich nuclei in transfer reactions within the dinuclear system model

> The dynamics of nucleon transfer processes in heavy-ion collisions is investigated within the dinuclear system model. The production cross sections of nuclei in the reactions $^{136}$Xe+$^{208}$Pb and $^{238}$U+$^{248}$Cm are calculated and the calculations are in good agreement with the experimental data. The transfer cross sections for the $^{58}$Ni+$^{208}$Pb reaction are calculated and compared with the experimental data. We predict the production cross sections of neutron-rich nuclei $^{165-168}$Eu, $^{169-173}$Tb, $^{173-178}$Ho, and $^{181-185}$Yb based on the reaction $^{176}$Yb+$^{238}$U. It can be seen that the production cross sections of the neutron-rich nuclei $^{165}$Eu, $^{169}$Tb, $^{173}$Ho, and $^{181}$Yb are 2.84 $μ$b, 6.90 $μ$b, 46.24 $μ$b, and 53.61 $μ$b, respectively, which could be synthesized in experiment.

# Production of heavy neutron-rich nuclei in transfer reactions within the dinuclear system model

## Key claim
The dinuclear system (DNS) model can reproduce measured transfer cross sections for several heavy-ion reactions and predicts that the neutron-rich nuclei 165Eu, 169Tb, 173Ho, and 181Yb can be synthesized via the 176Yb+238U reaction with cross sections of a few to tens of microbarns, provided the incident energy is optimized near 600 MeV center-of-mass energy.

## Method
The authors solve a set of master equations for the probability distribution of fragment proton and neutron numbers, evolving along the potential energy surface of the dinuclear system. Nucleon transfer is treated as a one-nucleon diffusion process with quasifission accounted for via a Kramers rate. The primary fragment yields are then fed into the statistical evaporation code GEMINI to model de-excitation by neutron, proton, alpha, and gamma emission competing with fission, yielding final mass distributions.

## Key numbers
- Production cross sections for 165Eu, 169Tb, 173Ho, and 181Yb from 176Yb+238U at E_c.m. = 600 MeV: 2.84 microbarn, 6.90 microbarn, 46.24 microbarn, and 53.61 microbarn, respectively.
- For the 238U+248Cm reaction at E_c.m. = 800 MeV, predicted cross sections for 261Md and 262Md are 1.52 nb and 0.17 nb, respectively.
- The height of the interaction potential at touching configuration for 176Yb+238U is about 565 MeV.
- For 136Xe+208Pb at E_c.m. = 514 MeV, the interaction potential height at touching for tip-tip collisions is 421 MeV.
- The quasifission parameters used: Gamma = 2.8 MeV, hbar*omega^Bqf = 2.0 MeV, hbar*omega = 3.0 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the dinuclear system model, previously applied to fusion reactions for superheavy nuclei synthesis (Adamian 2002, 2004, 2005, 2006; Zhu 2014), to multinucleon transfer reactions as an alternative route to neutron-rich heavy nuclei. It builds on discussions by Zagrebaev and Greiner (2011, 2012) regarding production of neutron-rich superheavy nuclei via transfer, and on time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations by Sekizawa (2013) and Golabek (2009). The predictions for 176Yb+238U provide specific candidate reactions for experimental synthesis of neutron-rich lanthanide and rare-earth isotopes that are difficult to reach via fusion or fragmentation.
