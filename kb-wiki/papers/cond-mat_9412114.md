# Hard-core Final State Interaction Effects in Deep Inelastic Scattering

> Hard-core final state interaction effects in the response function are investigated in the asymptotic limit of momentum transfer q->Infinity. A new treatment of the response is presented, which displays the relevant scattering contribution. A modification of the otherwise expected free response is obtained for Fermi and Bose systems and smearing of the condensate peak is shown in this limit. A comparison with other treatments of final state interactions is made.

## Key claim

The impulse approximation (IA) is not exact in the asymptotic limit of infinite momentum transfer when the inter-particle potential contains a hard-core repulsive component; the hard-core final state interactions (FSI) survive in this limit and modify the response, smearing the delta-function condensate peak for Bose systems and altering the Fermi gas response, even though for regular potentials the IA would be exact.

## Method

The authors treat the asymptotic response using a multiple scattering series (MSS) for the final state interactions, which they sum into closed analytical expressions using an eikonal approximation. They derive the exact asymptotic response function from a geometrical optics solution for hard-core scattering, expressed as an integral over the A-body density matrix modified by shadowing factors. They then apply this formalism to model systems with constant density matrices (ideal Bose condensate) and to more realistic correlated density matrices, including a product-form wave function, and finally to a Fermi gas with a hard-core interaction.

## Key numbers

- For the ideal Bose condensate model in the limit \(A\rightarrow\infty\), the asymptotic response is a Lorentzian: \(\tilde\phi_c^{(1)}({\mbox y}) = \frac{n_0}{\pi} e^{-4\pi\rho a^3/3} \frac{\sigma\rho}{(\sigma\rho)^2 + {\mbox y}^2}\), where \(\sigma = \pi a^2\) and \(a\) is the hard-core radius.
- For the same system with a hard-core constraint imposed on the density matrix, the asymptotic response becomes \(\tilde\phi_c^{(2)}({\mbox y}) \simeq \frac{n_0}{\pi} e^{4\pi\rho a^3/3} \frac{\sigma\rho}{(\sigma\rho)^2 + {\mbox y}^2}\) at low densities or low momenta \(|{\mbox y} a| \leq 1\).
- For a Fermi gas with nuclear matter parameters (\(k_F = 1.33\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(\rho = 2k_F^3/3\pi^2\)), the response at the quasi-elastic peak is reduced by about 10% for a hard-core radius \(a = 0.5\) fm; the ratio of hard-core volume to \(1/\rho\) is 0.084 for \(a = 0.5\) fm and 0.34 for \(a = 0.8\) fm.
- The asymptotic sum rules are satisfied for the correlated model: \(\int d{\mbox y} \tilde\phi^{(2)}({\mbox y}) = n_0\), \(\int d{\mbox y} \tilde\phi^{(2)}({\mbox y}) {\mbox y} = 0\), and \(\int d{\mbox y} \tilde\phi^{(2)}({\mbox y}) {\mbox y}^2 = \frac{2m}{3} \langle K \rangle\).

## Context

This work extends the study of final state interactions in deep inelastic scattering, specifically addressing the hard-core component which had been previously suggested to cause smearing of the condensate peak by Hohenberg and Platzman (1970s). The authors show that the Gersch and Rodriguez (1973) formula reproduces the exact asymptotic limit for hard-core interactions, while the hard-core perturbation theory of Silver does not. The results enable analytical calculations of the asymptotic response for both Bose and Fermi systems with hard-core interactions, and clarify that an infinite number of collisions is required to produce the smearing, contrary to claims in earlier work by Rinat and collaborators.
