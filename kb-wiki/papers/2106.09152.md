# Semi-microscopic construction of multi-$α$ cluster spaces

> An approximate but straight forward projection method to molecular many alpha-particle states is proposed and the overlap to the shell model space is determined. The resulting space is in accordance with the shell model, but still contains states which are not completely symmetric under permutations of the alpha-particles, which is one reason to call the construction semi-microscopic. A new contribution is the construction of the 6- and 7-$α$-particle spaces. The errors of the method propagate toward larger number of alpha-particles and larger shell excitations. In order to show the effectiveness of the construction proposed, the so obtained spaces are applied, within an algebraic cluster model, to $^{20}$Ne, $^{24}$Mg and $^{28}$Si, each treated as a many-alpha-particle system. Former results on $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O are resumed.

# Semi-microscopic construction of multi-α cluster spaces

## Key claim
The authors propose an approximate iterative projection method to construct multi-α-particle Hilbert spaces that avoids the involved integral kernels of exact microscopic methods, at the cost of including some states that are not fully symmetric under permutation of α-particles; the approximation is justified for low-lying states and low shell excitations, but errors propagate toward larger numbers of α-particles and higher shell excitations.

## Method
The method constructs molecular α-particle states iteratively using Jacobi coordinates with an ordering restriction on the oscillator quanta (Nλ1 ≥ Nλ2 ≥ ... ≥ Nλn−1) to partially enforce permutation symmetry, then compares the resulting SU(3) irreps to the explicitly constructed shell model space. The authors apply this procedure to systems of 3 through 7 α-particles (corresponding to 12C through 28Si) and test the resulting spaces within the Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model (SACM) using a phenomenological Hamiltonian with quadrupole-quadrupole, angular momentum, and Casimir operator terms.

## Key numbers
- For 12C (3 α-particles): the model space is identical to Horiuchi's list for up to 6ħω excitations, with irreps including (0,4) at 0ħω, (3,3) at 1ħω, and (2,4), (4,3), (6,2) at 2ħω.
- For 16O (4 α-particles): at 2ħω the irrep (2,0) appears with multiplicity 2 in the present compilation versus multiplicity 1 in Kato's list.
- For 20Ne (5 α-particles): at 0ħω the list is identical to Kato's; at 1ħω the present compilation shows additional irreps such as (1,1) with multiplicity 2 and (6,0) not present in Kato's list.
- The harmonic oscillator energy scale is set as ħω = 45 A^(−1/3) − 25 A^(−2/3) MeV, giving 13.19 MeV for 20Ne, 12.60 MeV for 24Mg, and 12.11 MeV for 28Si.
- B(E2) transition values: for 20Ne the 2₁⁺ → 0₁⁺ transition is 21.78 WU (theoretical) versus 20.3 ± 1 WU (experimental); for 24Mg it is 25.97 WU versus 21.5 ± 1.0 WU; for 28Si it is 15.54 WU versus 13.2 ± 0.5 WU.
- Spectroscopic factors (divided by e^Ã) for 20Ne: 0.2248 for the 0₁⁺ state, 0.2241 for the 2₁⁺ state, and 0.1365 for the 1₁⁻ state.

## Context
This work extends the microscopic projection method of Kato (1995) for up to 5 α-particles to 6- and 7-α-particle systems (24Mg and 28Si), using a simpler algebraic procedure that avoids the numerical errors in overlap matrix elements that the authors argue affect Kato's results. The method is compared to the exact SU(3) treatment of Kramer and Moshinsky (1968) and provides Hilbert spaces applicable to any cluster model requiring a microscopic basis, including the SACM previously applied to two-cluster systems by Cseh (1992) and to 12C and 16O by the same group. The results enable systematic studies of multi-α cluster states in medium-mass nuclei and highlight the importance of the Pauli Exclusion Principle in obtaining correct low-energy spectra.
