# Dihadron and gamma-hadron correlations from jet-induced medium excitation in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> Jet propagation is shown to produce Mach-cone-like medium excitation inside a quark-gluon plasma. However, only deflection of such medium excitation and jet shower partons by radial flow leads to double-peaked dihadron correlation in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. Dihadron correlations from harmonic flow, hot spots and dijets are studied separately within the AMPT Monte Carlo model and all lead to double-peaked dihadron azimuthal correlation. The $γ$-hadron correlation has similar double-peak feature but is free of the contributions from harmonic flow and hot spots. Dihadron and $γ$-hadron correlations are compared to shed light on jet-induced medium excitation and hot spots in an expanding medium.

## Key claim

Jet propagation in a quark-gluon plasma produces Mach-cone-like medium excitation, but only deflection of this excitation and jet shower partons by radial flow in an expanding medium leads to the observed double-peaked dihadron azimuthal correlation; the abstract's claim that dihadron correlations from harmonic flow, hot spots, and dijets "all lead to double-peaked" structure is qualified by the finding that the double-peak from hot spots alone has a reduced magnitude compared to the full simulation.

## Method

The authors used a linearized Monte Carlo simulation of the Boltzmann transport equation with elastic 1+2→3+4 scattering processes and a screened gluon propagator to study jet shower propagation and medium excitation in a uniform medium. They then embedded this into a (3+1)D ideal hydrodynamical calculation of an expanding medium to study deflection by radial flow. Separately, they used the AMPT Monte Carlo model to simulate dihadron and γ-hadron correlations in central Au+Au collisions at √s=200 GeV, isolating contributions from harmonic flow, hot spots, and dijets by successively randomizing the azimuthal angle of transverse momenta and transverse coordinates of initial jet shower partons.

## Key numbers

- Dihadron correlations were studied for trigger hadrons with pT^trig > 2.5 GeV/c and associated hadrons with 1 < pT^asso < 2 GeV/c.
- The initial parton density distribution in a typical AMPT central Au+Au event (b=0) at √s=200 GeV had ellipticity ε2=0.02 and triangularity ε3=0.02.
- Contributions from harmonic flows up to n=5 were significant for the dihadron correlation.
- The double-peaked away-side correlation from hot spots alone had roughly the same opening angle Δφ ~ 1 rad as the full simulation, but its magnitude was reduced by about 40% compared to the full simulation.
- γ-hadron correlations were studied for trigger photons with pT^trig(γ) ≥ 15 GeV/c and trigger hadrons with pT^trig(h) > 4 GeV/c, with associated hadrons in the range 1 < pT^asso < 2 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends studies of Mach-cone-like medium excitation from jet propagation in a quark-gluon plasma, which had previously been modeled in hydrodynamics by Casalderrey-Solana (2004) and in string calculations by Gubser (2009), but those approaches failed to reproduce the observed conic azimuthal correlations. The paper builds on the AMPT model by Zhang (1999) and addresses recent findings by Takahashi (2009) on hot spots and Alver (2010) on triangular flow, which also contribute to double-peaked dihadron correlations. The key advance is the proposal to use comparative γ-hadron and dihadron correlations to disentangle contributions from expanding hot spots and jet-induced medium excitation, enabling experimental separation of these effects in heavy-ion collisions.
