# Two-flavored heavy-light mesons' nuclear bound states

> We calculate the $B^-$-, $\overline{B^0}$-, $D^+$-, $D^0$-, $K^-$-, and $\overline{K^0}$-$^{12}$C bound state energies by solving the Klein-Gordon (K;G.) equation in momentum space, and also obtain the corresponding coordinate space radial wave functions. The strong Lorentz scalar and vector potentials in $^{12}$C are calculated using a local density approximation, where the scalar potentials are obtained based on the mass shifts of the respective mesons in nuclear matter. The mesons' mass shifts, the $^{12}$C nuclear density distributions, the strong potentials as well as the Coulomb potentials, are calculated by the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model.

# Two-flavored heavy-light mesons' nuclear bound states

## Key claim
The authors predict that $B^-$, $\overline{B^0}$, $D^+$, $D^0$, $K^-$, and $\overline{K^0}$ mesons should form bound states with the $^{12}$C nucleus, but this result is obtained under the restriction that the imaginary parts of the meson-nucleus potentials (meson widths) are neglected.

## Method
The authors solve the Klein-Gordon equation in momentum space for each orbital angular momentum $\ell$ to calculate bound-state energies of meson-$^{12}$C systems. The strong Lorentz scalar and vector potentials in $^{12}$C are computed using a local density approximation based on the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which also provides the nuclear density distributions and Coulomb potentials. The partial wave decomposition of the potentials in momentum space is obtained via double spherical Bessel transforms of the coordinate-space potentials.

## Context
This work extends a previous study of D-meson nuclear bound states by Tsushima (1998) to include $B$ and $K$ mesons as well, using the same QMC model framework developed by Guichon (1987) and Tsushima (2020). The results enable future investigations that incorporate the imaginary parts of meson-nucleus potentials (meson widths) and extend the calculations to other nuclear targets beyond $^{12}$C.
