# Chemical equilibration due to heavy Hagedorn states

> A scenario of heavy resonances, called massive Hagedorn states, is proposed which exhibits a fast ($t\approx 1$ fm/c) chemical equilibration of (strange) baryons and anti-baryons at the QCD critical temperature $T_c$. For relativistic heavy ion collisions this scenario predicts that hadronization is followed by a brief expansion phase during which the equilibration rate is higher than the expansion rate, so that baryons and antibaryons reach chemical equilibrium before chemical freeze-out occurs.

# Chemical equilibration due to heavy Hagedorn states

## Key claim

The paper proposes that heavy, short-lived Hagedorn states (HS) with masses around 3–6 GeV, present near the QCD critical temperature, can catalyze fast chemical equilibration of (strange) baryons and antibaryons on timescales of about 1 fm/c, provided that the additional energy density from these states is Δε_c ≈ 0.3–0.5 GeV/fm³ and that their total decay widths exceed 0.5–1 GeV. This mechanism avoids the oversaturation problem of an earlier scenario by BSW, which predicted too many baryons and antibaryons.

## Method

The authors estimate the number density of Hagedorn states from the additional energy density needed to match lattice QCD results near T_c, then compute baryon-antibaryon production rates from HS decays using a microcanonical statistical model. They evaluate branching ratios for decays such as HS → B + B̅ + X, where X represents additional mesons, and derive chemical equilibration times from the production rates divided by equilibrium densities. The analysis is extended to strange baryons (Λ, Ω) and to baryonic Hagedorn states for finite baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Additional energy density from Hagedorn states: Δε_c ≈ 0.3–0.5 GeV/fm³
- Typical Hagedorn state mass: M_HS ≈ 3–6 GeV
- Number density of Hagedorn states: n_HS ≈ 0.05–0.15 fm⁻³
- Total decay width of Hagedorn states: Γ_tot_HS ≳ 0.5–1 GeV
- Average baryon number per HS decay: ⟨B⟩ ≈ 0.2–0.4
- Partial decay width for B B̅ X: Γ_B B̅ X ≈ 100–300 MeV
- Baryon-antibaryon production rate: dN_B B̅ / d⁴x ≈ 0.05 fm⁻⁴
- Equilibrium baryon/antibaryon density at RHIC: n_B_eq = n_B̅_eq ≈ 0.04 fm⁻³
- Chemical equilibration time for baryons/antibaryons: τ_chem_B B̅ ≈ 0.8 fm/c
- For Ω from nonstrange HS: τ_Ω ≈ 4–8 fm/c
- For Ω from strange HS (sss q̅ q̅ q̅): τ_Ω ≈ 2–4 fm/c
- For Ω from Ω-like baryonic HS: τ_Ω ≈ 2–4 fm/c
- Branching probability for Ω from nonstrange 4 GeV HS: ⟨Ω⟩ ≈ 2×10⁻⁴
- Branching probability for Ω from strange 4 GeV HS: ⟨Ω⟩ ≈ 0.05
- Equilibrium density of Ω at T=170 MeV: n_Ω_eq ≈ 4×10⁻⁴ fm⁻³
- Maximum relative overpopulation from HS decays: δn_B B̅ / n_B_eq ≲ 0.2–0.4

## Context

This work extends the Hagedorn model of an exponentially increasing hadron mass spectrum (Hagedorn, original idea) to address chemical equilibration in heavy-ion collisions. It directly addresses shortcomings of the BSW scenario (BSW, 2004), which proposed overpopulated pion and kaon densities to drive baryon-antibaryon production but predicted excessive baryon yields not seen experimentally. The paper builds on earlier work by Greiner et al. (CAR1, CAR2) on multi-meson fusion reactions for antibaryon production at SPS energies, and on lattice QCD results showing that the PDG hadron resonance gas model underestimates the pressure near T_c (RGM, BB). The mechanism enables a natural explanation for chemical freeze-out occurring very close to T_c, as observed at RHIC, and suggests that Hagedorn states represent strongly interacting plasma correlations near the deconfinement transition.
