# The superdeformed excited band of Ca-40

> The superdeformed band, recently discovered in Ca-40 is analysed in an spherical shell model context. Two major oscillator shells, sd and pf are necessary to describe it. The yrast band of the fixed 8p-8h configuration fits extremely well with the experimental energies and transition rates of the superdeformed band. The 4p-4h configuration generates a normally deformed band plus a gamma-band pattern, both are also present in the experimental data.

## Key claim

The superdeformed band in Ca-40 is well described by the yrast band of a fixed 8p-8h configuration in a spherical shell model calculation using the sd and pf major oscillator shells, with the caveat that the calculation assumes a fixed particle-hole number and excludes the 1d5/2 orbital to manage basis size and center-of-mass effects.

## Method

The authors performed spherical shell model calculations in a valence space consisting of the 2s1/2, 1d3/2, 1f7/2, 2p3/2, 1f5/2, and 2p1/2 orbits, using a closed Si-28 core and the effective interaction sdpf.sm. They first computed the 4p-4h and 8p-8h configurations separately, then attempted a mixed calculation in a reduced valence space (zbm2) that excluded the upper pf-shell orbits and the 1d5/2 orbital, with an increased quadrupole-quadrupole interaction to compensate for the truncation.

## Key numbers

* The 4p-4h configuration's yrast band (Band 2) shows gamma-ray energies within 200 keV of experiment, with the J=16+ state coming out too low at 3374 keV compared to the experimental 4050 keV.
* The 8p-8h configuration yields an intrinsic quadrupole moment Q0 = 172 efm2, in agreement with the experimental value Q0(t) = 1.80+0.39-0.29 eb (corresponding to beta ~ 0.6, a superdeformed shape).
* B(E2) values for the 8p-8h yrast band range from 589 e2fm4 (J=2 to 0) to 7 e2fm4 (J=24 to 22), with the band termination predicted at J=22,24.
* The 4p-4h configuration's yrast band has B(E2) values from 266 e2fm4 (J=2 to 0) to 46 e2fm4 (J=16 to 14), with an intrinsic quadrupole moment Q0(t) = 0.74 +/- 0.14 eb from experiment, corresponding to beta = 0.27 (normal deformation).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous shell model studies of deformed bands in Ni-56 and Ar-36 (Caurier, Nowacki, Poves, Zuker, 2001) to the superdeformed band recently discovered in Ca-40 by Gammasphere experiments. The calculation demonstrates that the spherical shell model can describe a superdeformed rotational band with near-saturation of the SU(3) limit (Q0 = 190 efm2) and quasi-SU(3) limit (Q0 = 180 efm2) in two major shells, enabling future studies of mixing between np-nh configurations and the role of the 1d5/2 orbital in achieving full quadrupole coherence.
