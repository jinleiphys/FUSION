# Number of Collisions in the Glauber Model and Beyond

> The so called number of hadron-nucleus collisions n_coll(b) at impact parameter b, and its integral value N_coll, which are used to normalize the measured fractional cross section of a hard process, are calculated within the Glauber-Gribov theory including the effects of nucleon short-range correlations. The Gribov inelastic shadowing corrections are summed to all orders by employing the dipole representation. Numerical calculations are performed at the energies of the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC). We found that whereas the Gribov corrections generally increase the value of N_coll, the inclusion of nucleon correlations, acting in the opposite directions, decreases it by a comparable amount. The interplay of the two effects varies with the value of the impact parameter.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the number of hadron-nucleus collisions \(n_{coll}(b)\) and its integral \(N_{coll}\) within the Glauber-Gribov theory including nucleon short-range correlations (SRC), finding that Gribov inelastic shadowing corrections generally increase \(N_{coll}\) while SRC decrease it by a comparable amount, with the net result often similar to the plain Glauber value except when the detector misses large rapidity gap (LRG) processes, in which case \(N_{coll}\) is appreciably lower.

## Method

The authors computed \(n_{coll}(b)\) and \(N_{coll}\) for proton-\(^{208}\)Pb scattering using four approaches: (i) the plain Glauber model with the single-density approximation; (ii) the Glauber model including SRC via a modified hadron-nucleus thickness function \(\widetilde T_A^h(b)\); (iii) the Glauber model plus SRC and Gribov inelastic shadowing summed to all orders using the light-cone dipole representation; and (iv) the same as (iii) but with diffractive LRG cross sections subtracted to mimic detectors insensitive to LRG events. The dipole-nucleon cross section and proton light-cone wave function were taken from earlier work by the same group (Kopeliovich et al., 2007-2008), and realistic one- and two-body nuclear densities and correlation functions from Ciofi degli Atti et al. (1991) were used.

## Key numbers

* For \(p-^{208}\)Pb at RHIC energy (\(\sigma_{in}^{pN}=42.1\) mb): \(N_{coll}=4.70\) (Glauber), 4.65 (Glauber+SRC), 4.75 (Glauber+SRC+Gribov IS with LRG detected), and 3.42 (Glauber+SRC+Gribov IS with LRG missed).
* For \(p-^{208}\)Pb at LHC energy (\(\sigma_{in}^{pN}=68.3\) mb): \(N_{coll}=6.88\) (Glauber), 6.82 (Glauber+SRC), 6.88 (Glauber+SRC+Gribov IS with LRG detected), and 5.67 (Glauber+SRC+Gribov IS with LRG missed).
* The inelastic proton-nucleus cross section \(\sigma_{in}^{pA}\) for \(p-^{208}\)Pb at RHIC: 1863.2 mb (Glauber), 1881.0 mb (Glauber+SRC), 1842.1 mb (Glauber+SRC+Gribov IS with LRG detected), and 1823.0 mb (Glauber+SRC+Gribov IS with LRG missed).
* The inelastic proton-nucleus cross section \(\sigma_{in}^{pA}\) for \(p-^{208}\)Pb at LHC: 2064.8 mb (Glauber), 2082.7 mb (Glauber+SRC), 2064.2 mb (Glauber+SRC+Gribov IS with LRG detected), and 2064.0 mb (Glauber+SRC+Gribov IS with LRG missed).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of SRC and Gribov inelastic shadowing effects on total, elastic, quasi-elastic, and diffractive cross sections (Ciofi degli Atti et al., 2010) to the specific quantities \(n_{coll}(b)\) and \(N_{coll}\) used for normalizing hard-process cross sections in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions. The results confirm the opposite roles of SRC (which increase nuclear opacity) and Gribov IS (which increase nuclear transparency), as previously noted for total cross sections by Ciofi degli Atti et al. (2009) and for deuteron-gold scattering by Kopeliovich et al. (2007). The findings enable more precise normalization of hard-process data from RHIC experiments (STAR, PHENIX, PHOBOS) and LHC experiments, particularly highlighting the importance of accounting for both effects when detectors have different sensitivities to LRG events.
