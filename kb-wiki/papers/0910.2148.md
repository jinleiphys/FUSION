# Fermion propagators in space-time

> The one- and the two-particle propagators for an infinite non-interacting Fermi system are studied as functions of space-time coordinates. Their behaviour at the origin and in the asymptotic region is discussed, as is their scaling in the Fermi momentum. Both propagators are shown to have a divergence at equal times. The impact of the interaction among the fermions on their momentum distribution, on their pair correlation function and, hence, on the Coulomb sum rule is explored using a phenomenological model. Finally the problem of how the confinement is reflected in the momentum distribution of the system's constituents is briefly addressed.

## Key claim

The one- and two-fermion propagators for an infinite non-interacting Fermi system are derived analytically in space-time coordinates, revealing that both propagators diverge at equal times and that the hole propagator and density-density correlation function scale in the Fermi momentum when space and time are appropriately rescaled, except at vanishing time difference where the divergence prevents analysis of second-kind scaling.

## Method

The authors analytically computed the four-dimensional Fourier transform of the well-known one-body fermion propagator in energy-momentum space to obtain its space-time expression, focusing on the hole propagator because the particle propagator diverges at equal times. For the two-particle propagator, they derived the density-density correlation function (polarization propagator) from the convolution of two one-body propagators using Wick's theorem, then performed the inverse Fourier transform and reduced the resulting integrals to expressions involving error functions and Meijer G-functions. They introduced a phenomenological momentum distribution with a tail at high momenta and a depletion at the Fermi surface to model the effects of interactions, and computed the resulting pair correlation function and Coulomb sum rule numerically.

## Key numbers

- The density of the system for spin-1/2 particles is given by \(n_0 = k_F^3 / (3\pi^2)\).
- The equal-time limit of the hole propagator is \(G^0(\vec{x}t, \vec{x}'t^+) = i (n_0/2) (3/(k_F r)) j_1(k_F r)\).
- The Coulomb sum rule for a non-interacting Fermi gas is \(S(\vec{q}) = Z (3q/(4k_F) - (q/(2k_F))^3/2)\) for \(q < 2k_F\) and \(S(\vec{q}) = Z\) for \(q \geq 2k_F\).
- For nuclear matter with density \(n_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\), the modified momentum distribution yields a new Fermi momentum \(k_F = 1.54\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- The Coulomb sum rule from the modified momentum distribution differs substantially from the free Fermi gas result for momenta between approximately 2.5 and 4 fm\(^{-1}\), reaching the asymptotic value 1 from below.

## Context

This work extends the study of fermion propagators from the standard energy-momentum representation to space-time coordinates, a treatment the authors state was not previously available in the literature. The analysis builds on the non-relativistic Coulomb sum rule formalism of Fetter and Walecka (1971) and the scaling properties discussed in earlier work by Donnelly and collaborators (1999) and Amaro et al. (2002). The results enable future investigations of short-range correlations in nuclear matter and finite nuclei, as well as relativistic extensions for comparison with electroweak superscaling approaches.
