# Photon Physics in Heavy Ion Collisions at the LHC

> Writeup of the working group "Photon Physics" for the CERN Yellow Report on "Hard Probes in Heavy Ion Collisions at the LHC", 134 pages. One figure added in chapter 5 (comparison with PHENIX data). Some figures and correponding text corrected in chapter 6 (off-chemical equilibrium thermal photon rates). Some figures modified in chapter 7 (off-chemical equilibrium photon rates) and comparison with PHENIX data added

## Key claim

This CERN Yellow Report working group writeup surveys the theoretical and experimental status of photon production in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC, covering both prompt (non-thermal) photons from hard parton scattering and thermal photons from the quark-gluon plasma and hadron gas. The abstract's claim of a comprehensive review is qualified by the fact that several chapters include corrections and additions made after the initial draft, including a new comparison with PHENIX data in chapter 5 and modified figures for off-chemical-equilibrium thermal photon rates in chapters 6 and 7.

## Method

The working group compiled and reviewed theoretical calculations of prompt photon production at next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD, including nuclear parton distribution functions and parton energy loss effects, alongside thermal photon emission rates from the quark-gluon plasma (using hard-thermal-loop resummation and leading-order results from Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe) and from hadronic matter (using effective Lagrangians and rho-meson spectral functions). The report also presents Monte Carlo event generator predictions (PHOJET, DPMJET, PYTHIA) for photon backgrounds and discusses experimental capabilities of the ALICE, CMS, and ATLAS detectors for photon measurements at the LHC.

## Key numbers

- Prompt photon production cross sections in pp collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV from PHENIX are compared with NLO pQCD calculations; the ratio of data to theory is shown in chapter 5.
- Thermal photon rates from the quark-gluon plasma are computed at leading order by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2001-2002) for temperatures relevant at LHC (T ~ 300-600 MeV).
- Off-chemical-equilibrium thermal photon rates are presented in chapters 6 and 7, with modified figures compared to PHENIX data.
- The WA98 experiment at CERN SPS measured direct photon production in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=17.3 GeV (Aggarwal et al., 2000).
- PHENIX measurements of neutral pion production in pp collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV (Adler et al., 2003) and in d+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV (Adler et al., 2003) are referenced as baselines for nuclear modification studies.

## Context

This report extends the earlier CERN Yellow Report work on hard probes in heavy-ion collisions, specifically complementing the chapters on jet physics (Wiedemann, 2003) and parton distribution functions and shadowing (Eskola, 2003). It provides the theoretical framework and experimental projections for photon measurements at the LHC, building on RHIC-era results from PHENIX (Adcox et al., 2002; Adler et al., 2003) and STAR (Adams et al., 2003) that established jet quenching and the need for photon probes as clean signatures of the quark-gluon plasma. The report enables future LHC experiments to design photon trigger strategies and interpret direct photon yields in terms of initial-state nuclear effects and final-state medium modifications.
