# Approximation method for the nuclear structure functions at small $x$

> We have shown that the relationship between the DIS structure functions is stable for a nuclear target with mass number A at $x{\leq}10^{-3}$. Numerical results are provided for the specific nuclei 12C and 208Pb using the nuclear PDF parameterizations implemented in the HIJING2.0 model. These results fall whithin the EIC kinematic acceptance for heavy ion running. The ratio $R^{A}_{F_{L}}$ is calculated as the ratio $R^{A}_{F_{2}}$ in the HIJING2.0 model.

## Key claim

The relationship between the longitudinal structure function \(F_L\) and the derivative of \(F_2\) with respect to \(\ln Q^2\), previously established for the proton at small \(x\), is shown to be stable for nuclear targets with mass number \(A\) at \(x \leq 10^{-3}\), with uncertainties less than 5%.

## Method

The author derives nuclear structure function relations by expanding the gluon distribution in the pQCD expressions for \(\partial F_2^A / \partial \ln Q^2\) and \(F_L^A\) around arbitrary points \(z = \alpha'\) and \(\alpha\), incorporating the nuclear gluon shadowing ratio \(R_G^A\) from the HIJING2.0 model. The ratio of coefficients \(\upsilon/\tau\), \(\zeta/\eta\), and \(\tau/\eta\) are evaluated numerically for the proton, carbon-12, and lead-208 to verify that they approach the same constants (0.5, 0.5, and 1.0 respectively) at \(x \leq 10^{-3}\), allowing the proton relation to be extended to nuclei. The nuclear effect ratio \(R_{F_L}^A\) is then expressed in terms of \(R_{F_2}^A\) using the HIJING2.0 parametrizations for light and heavy nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For the proton, the ratios \(\upsilon/\tau = 0.5 \pm 0.003\), \(\zeta/\eta = 0.5\), and \(\tau/\eta = 1 \pm 0.003\) hold in the range \(10^{-5} \leq x \leq 10^{-2}\) for \(x \leq 10^{-3}\).
- For carbon-12, the ratios are \(\upsilon/\tau = 0.5 \pm 0.02\), \(\zeta/\eta = 0.5 \pm 0.01\), and \(\tau/\eta = 1 \pm 0.02\) for \(x \leq 10^{-3}\).
- For lead-208, the same ratios \(\upsilon/\tau = 0.5 \pm 0.02\), \(\zeta/\eta = 0.5 \pm 0.01\), and \(\tau/\eta = 1 \pm 0.02\) hold for \(x \leq 10^{-3}\).
- The scaling factors relating the structure functions are \(\xi_2 = 0.5\) and \(\xi_L = 0.4\).
- The \(\Phi\) function, relating \(R_{F_L}^A\) to \(R_{F_2}^A\), has value \(\Phi = 1.0 \pm 0.2\) over a wide range of \(x\) and \(Q^2\).
- The maximum center-of-mass energy for electron-heavy ion runs at the EIC is \(\sqrt{s} = 89\) GeV (18 GeV electrons with 110 GeV ions).

## Context

This work extends the approximation methods of Cooper-Sarkar (1988) and Prytz (1993) for proton structure functions, and the generalized forms of Gay Ducati and Goncalves (1997) and Boroun and Rezaei (2012), to nuclear targets using the HIJING2.0 model of Li and Wang (2002) and Deng, Wang, and Xu (2011). The results are relevant for the kinematic acceptance of the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) for heavy ion running, and enable extraction of nuclear gluon densities and shadowing ratios from measurements of nuclear structure functions at small \(x\).
