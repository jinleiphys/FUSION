# Quark-Meson Coupling Model for a Nucleon

> The quark-meson coupling model for a nucleon is considered. The model describes a nucleon as an MIT bag, in which quarks are coupled to scalar and vector mesons. A set of coupled equations for the quark and the meson fields are obtained and are solved in a self-consistent way. It is shown that the mass of a nucleon as a dressed MIT bag interacting with sigma- and omega-meson fields significantly differs from the mass of a free MIT bag. A few sets of model parameters are obtained so that the mass of a dressed MIT bag becomes the nucleon mass. The results of our calculations imply that the self-energy of the bag in the quark-meson coupling model is significant and needs to be considered in doing the nuclear matter calculations.

# Quark-Meson Coupling Model for a Nucleon

## Key claim
The mass of a single dressed MIT bag in free space, when calculated with the bag constant and zero-point parameter fixed from the free MIT bag, deviates significantly from the nucleon mass of 939 MeV due to quark-meson coupling; this self-energy effect has been neglected in previous QMC calculations and must be accounted for when choosing parameters for nuclear matter calculations.

## Method
The authors solve a set of coupled differential equations for the quark wavefunctions (upper and lower radial components) and the sigma and omega meson fields inside a static spherical MIT bag, using the Lagrangian of the quark-meson coupling model. The nucleon mass is obtained from the bag energy with center-of-mass corrections, minimized with respect to the bag radius. For nuclear matter, the mean-field approximation is applied to the meson fields, and the total energy per nucleon is computed including Fermi motion.

## Key numbers
- Free MIT bag mass: 939 MeV at bag radius 1.0 fm, using B^(1/4) = 136.3 MeV and Z = 1.153
- With only sigma coupling (g_sigma^q = 4, g_omega^q = 0): nucleon mass reduced to 903 MeV, bag radius 0.979 fm
- With only omega coupling (g_sigma^q = 0, g_omega^q = 4): nucleon mass increased to 994 MeV, bag radius 1.03 fm
- With equal couplings (g_sigma^q = g_omega^q = 2): nucleon mass 946 MeV at R = 1.01 fm (7 MeV above free mass)
- With equal couplings (g_sigma^q = g_omega^q = 4): nucleon mass 964 MeV at R = 1.02 fm (15 MeV above free mass)
- Meson field ranges extend to approximately 1.5 fm for both sigma and omega mesons

## Context
This work extends the quark-meson coupling model originally proposed by Guichon (1988) and refined by Fleck and collaborators, which treats nuclear matter as non-overlapping MIT bags interacting through scalar and vector meson exchange. The authors show that previous QMC calculations by Saito and Thomas (1994) used coupling constants (g_sigma^q = 5.605, g_omega^q = 1.152) that produce no physical solution for a single nucleon, and that no set of parameters within the present framework can simultaneously reproduce the free nucleon mass and nuclear matter binding energy. This finding provides an additional constraint for refining the QMC model.
