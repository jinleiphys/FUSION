# Violation of Wiedemann-Franz Law for Hot Hadronic Matter created at NICA, FAIR and RHIC Energies using Non-extensive Statistics

> We present here the computation of electrical and thermal conductivity by solving the Boltzmann transport equation in relaxation time approximation. We use the $q$-generalized Boltzmann distribution function to incorporate the effects of non-extensivity. The behaviour of these quantities with changing temperature and baryochemical potential has been studied as the system slowly moves towards thermodynamic equilibrium. We have estimated the Lorenz number at NICA, FAIR and the top RHIC energies and studied as a function of temperature, baryochemical potential and the non-extensive parameter, $q$. We have observed that Wiedemann-Franz law is violated for a non-extensive hadronic phase as well as for an equilibrated hadron gas at high temperatures.

## Key claim

The Wiedemann-Franz law is violated for a hot hadronic gas both when the system is out of equilibrium (described by non-extensive Tsallis statistics with parameter q > 1) and for an equilibrated hadron gas at high temperatures, contrary to the law's validity in ordinary metals.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation in relaxation time approximation using a q-generalized Tsallis distribution function to incorporate non-extensive effects. Electrical and thermal conductivities were computed for a hadron resonance gas with mass cutoff 2.0 GeV, using a constant cross-section of 200 mb and averaging the relaxation time over the distribution function. The calculations were performed for temperatures from 90 to 160 MeV and five baryochemical potentials corresponding to different collision energies at RHIC, FAIR, and NICA.

## Key numbers

* Electrical conductivity σ_el/T decreases with temperature from approximately 0.005 at T=90 MeV to near zero at T=160 MeV for μ_B=25 MeV at q≈1.
* Thermal conductivity κ/T² decreases from about 0.0004 GeV at T=90 MeV to near zero at T=160 MeV for μ_B=25 MeV at q≈1.
* The Lorenz number κ/(σ_el T) varies widely with temperature, showing a minimum around T_c ~ 160 MeV for low μ_B (25 MeV) but increasing monotonically for high μ_B (630 MeV).
* Five baryochemical potentials were used: μ_B = 25, 45, 200, 436, and 630 MeV, corresponding to collision energies √s_NN = 200, 130, 19.6, 7.7, and 3 GeV respectively.
* The non-extensive parameter q was limited to q < 1.15, beyond which no phase transition is expected.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of electrical and thermal conductivity in hadron gas using equilibrium Boltzmann statistics, such as those by Kadam (2017) and Mitra (2014), by incorporating non-extensive effects via Tsallis statistics. The results are compared with lattice QCD calculations by Gupta (2003), kinetic theory by Greif (2016), and the PHSD model by Cassing (2013). The violation of the Wiedemann-Franz law found here parallels recent findings for 2-flavor quark matter by Harutyunyan (2017) and for hot QGP by Mitra (2017). This work enables future studies of transport properties in the hadronic phase at finite baryon density relevant for NICA, FAIR, and low-energy RHIC runs.
