# Signal of Theta^+ in quenched lattice QCD with exact chiral symmetry

> We investigate the mass spectrum of the pentaquark baryon ($ udud \bar s $) in quenched lattice QCD with exact chiral symmetry. Using 3 different interpolating operators, we measure their $ 3 \times 3 $ correlation matrix and obtain the eigenvalues $ A^{\pm} (t) $ with $ \pm $ parity, for 100 gauge configurations generated with Wilson gauge action at $ β= 6.1 $ on the $ 20^3 \times 40 $ lattice. For the lowest-lying $ J^P = 1/2^- $ state, its effective mass is almost identical to that of the KN s-wave, while for the lowest-lying $ J^P = 1/2^+ $ state, its effective mass is smaller than that of the KN p-wave, especially for the regime $ m_u < m_s $. By chiral extrapolation (linear in $m_π^2$) to $ m_π= 135 $ MeV, we obatin the masses of the lowest-lying states: $ m(1/2^-) = 1424(57) $ MeV and $ m(1/2^+) = 1562(121) $ MeV, in agreement with the masses of $ m_K + m_N \simeq 1430 $ MeV and $ Θ^+(1540) $ respectively.

## Key claim

In quenched lattice QCD with exact chiral symmetry, the lowest-lying \(J^P = 1/2^+\) state of the pentaquark system \(udud\bar{s}\) has a mass of \(1562(121)\) MeV after chiral extrapolation to the physical pion mass, which is smaller than the corresponding KN p-wave scattering state for light quark masses below the strange quark mass; the authors interpret this as evidence that the state is a bound pentaquark rather than a scattering state, and identify it with the \(\Theta^+(1540)\).

## Method

The authors generated 100 gauge configurations using the Wilson gauge action at \(\beta = 6.1\) on a \(20^3 \times 40\) lattice. They computed point-to-point quark propagators for 30 bare quark masses using the optimal domain-wall fermion formalism with \(N_s = 128\) sites in the fifth dimension, achieving chiral symmetry breaking below \(10^{-14}\). Three different interpolating operators for the \(udud\bar{s}\) system were used to construct a \(3 \times 3\) correlation matrix, which was diagonalized via the variational method to extract the eigenvalues \(A^{\pm}(t)\) for positive and negative parity states; masses were obtained from single exponential fits to the plateau regions of the effective masses.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: \(a^{-1} = 2.237(76)\) GeV, determined from the pion decay constant with experimental input \(f_\pi = 132\) MeV.
- Strange quark bare mass: \(m_s a = 0.08\), giving a vector meson mass \(M_V = 1029(10)\) MeV, consistent with the \(\phi(1020)\).
- Chiral extrapolation (linear in \(m_\pi^2\)) to \(m_\pi = 135\) MeV yields: \(m(1/2^-) = 1424(57)\) MeV and \(m(1/2^+) = 1562(121)\) MeV.
- The KN s-wave scattering state mass from chiral extrapolation: \(m_{KN}(1/2^-) = 1433(72)\) MeV, consistent with \(m_K + m_N \simeq 1430\) MeV.
- The lowest \(J^P = 1/2^-\) state mass is nearly identical to the KN s-wave for the entire range of \(m_u\) studied.

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of the pentaquark \(\Theta^+\) using exact chiral symmetry, following experimental observations by the LEPS collaboration (Nakano, 2003) and predictions from the chiral-soliton model (Diakonov, 1997). The variational method with multiple interpolating operators, as developed by Michael (1985) and Luscher (1990), is employed to disentangle pentaquark states from KN scattering states. The results support the Jaffe-Wilczek diquark model (Jaffe, 2003) and the Karliner-Lipkin model (Karliner, 2003) in predicting positive parity for the \(\Theta^+(1540)\), while contradicting interpretations that the signal arises solely from KN scattering. The authors note that confirmation requires larger lattices, smaller lattice spacing, and volume-dependence studies of spectral weights as suggested by Mathur (2004).
