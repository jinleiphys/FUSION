# Neutron-skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb, and symmetry-energy constraints from the study of the anti-analog giant dipole resonance

> The $^{208}$Pb($p$,$nγ\bar p$) $^{207}$Pb reaction at a beam energy of 30 MeV has been used to excite the anti-analog of the giant dipole resonance (AGDR) and to measure its $γ$-decay to the isobaric analog state in coincidence with proton decay of IAS. The energy of the transition has also been calculated with the self-consistent relativistic random-phase approximation (RRPA), and found to be linearly correlated to the predicted value of the neutron-skin thickness ($ΔR_{pn}$). By comparing the theoretical results with the measured transition energy, the value of 0.190 $\pm$ 0.028 fm has been determined for $ΔR_{pn}$ of $^{208}$Pb, in agreement with previous experimental results. The AGDR excitation energy has also been used to calculate the symmetry energy at saturation ($J=32.7 \pm 0.6$ MeV) and the slope of the symmetry energy ($L=49.7 \pm 4.4$ MeV), resulting in more stringent constraints than most of the previous studies.

# Neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb, and symmetry-energy constraints from the study of the anti-analog giant dipole resonance

## Key claim

The energy difference between the anti-analog giant dipole resonance (AGDR) and the isobaric analog state (IAS) in 208Pb, measured via gamma decay, is linearly correlated with the neutron-skin thickness; comparing the measured transition energy to self-consistent relativistic random-phase approximation (RRPA) calculations yields a neutron-skin thickness of 0.190 ± 0.028 fm, under the condition that the theoretical uncertainty is estimated at 10%.

## Method

The 208Pb(p,nγp)207Pb reaction was performed at a beam energy of 30 MeV at the Oslo Cyclotron Laboratory. Proton decay of the IAS was used as a coincidence tag, and gamma rays from the AGDR decay to the IAS were measured using the SiRi particle telescope and CACTUS NaI detector array. The experimental transition energy was compared to calculations using the self-consistent relativistic proton-neutron random-phase approximation (pn-RRPA) with a family of density-dependent meson-exchange interactions having systematically varied symmetry energy at saturation (J = 30, 32, 34, 36, and 38 MeV) plus the DD-ME2 interaction.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb: ΔRpn = 0.190 ± 0.028 fm (including 10% theoretical uncertainty)
- Measured gamma-ray transition energy from AGDR to IAS: Eγ = 8.90 ± 0.09 MeV (statistical error ±0.02 MeV, systematic error from calibration 1.0%)
- Symmetry energy at saturation: J = 32.7 ± 0.6 MeV
- Slope of the symmetry energy: L = 49.7 ± 4.4 MeV
- Dipole polarizability of 208Pb calculated with DD-ME2: αD = 20.8 fm3, compared to experimental value αD = 20.1 ± 0.6 fm3 from Tamii (2011)

## Context

This work extends the correlation between AGDR energy and neutron-skin thickness previously established by Krasznahorkay (2013) and Krasznahorkay (2013a), and provides more precise constraints than most prior studies. The results agree with the neutron-skin thickness from coherent pion photo-production by Tarbert (2003) (0.19 ± 0.03 fm) and with the dipole polarizability analysis of Tamii (2011). The AGDR method yields J-L constraints that are more stringent than those from heavy-ion collisions, quantum Monte Carlo and neutron star studies, nuclear binding energies, isobaric analog states, proton elastic scattering, and pygmy dipole resonances, while showing excellent agreement with the dipole polarizability constraints when reanalyzed with the same set of effective interactions.
