# Density dependence of the symmetry energy and the nuclear equation of state: A Dynamical and Statistical model perspective

> The density dependence of the symmetry energy in the equation of state of isospin asymmetric nuclear matter is of significant importance for studying the structure of systems as diverse as the neutron-rich nuclei and the neutron stars. A number of reactions using the dynamical and the statistical models of multifragmentation, and the experimental isoscaling observable, is studied to extract information on the density dependence of the symmetry energy. It is observed that the dynamical and the statistical model calculations give consistent results assuming the sequential decay effect in dynamical model to be small. A comparison with several other independent studies is also made to obtain important constraint on the form of the density dependence of the symmetry energy. The comparison rules out an extremely " stiff " and " soft " form of the density dependence of the symmetry energy with important implications for astrophysical and nuclear physics studies.

# Density dependence of the symmetry energy and the nuclear equation of state: A Dynamical and Statistical model perspective

## Key claim

The dynamical (AMD) and statistical (SMM) model calculations give consistent results for the density dependence of the symmetry energy when the sequential decay effect in the dynamical model is assumed to be small (10-15% correction), and the comparison with experimental isoscaling data rules out both extremely "stiff" and "soft" forms, favoring a parametrization with exponent γ = 0.69.

## Method

The authors analyzed experimental fragment isotopic yield distributions from multifragmentation reactions of 40Ar, 40Ca, 58Fe, and 58Ni beams on 58Fe and 58Ni targets at beam energies from 25 to 53 MeV/nucleon, using the isoscaling observable R21(N,Z) = Y2/Y1 = C exp(αN + βZ). They compared the experimentally extracted isoscaling parameter α to predictions from the Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) model with Gogny and Gogny-AS interactions and from the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM), using the relation α = (4Csym/T)[(Z1/A1)² - (Z2/A2)²] to connect α to the symmetry energy.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy at saturation density is Csym⁰ ≈ 31.6 MeV, with the density dependence parametrized as Csym(ρ) = 31.6 (ρ/ρ₀)^γ MeV, where γ = 0.69 from the dynamical model analysis of the isoscaling data.
- The experimental isoscaling parameter α decreases with beam energy from 25 MeV/nucleon to 53 MeV/nucleon, and decreases with decreasing N/Z of the system.
- The neutron detection efficiency of the TAMU 4π neutron ball was about 83%, as measured with a ²⁵²Cf source.
- The ratio of events with neutron multiplicity ≥6 at b = 0 fm versus b = 5 fm was 19.0 at 44° and 11.1 at 152° for 33 MeV/nucleon, and 1.3 at 44° and 2.2 at 152° for 45 MeV/nucleon.
- The fragment asymmetry (Z/A)² values were interpolated from AMD calculations at t = 300 fm/c for the Gogny and Gogny-AS interactions at 35 MeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the isoscaling studies of Tsang (2001) and Xu (2000) by comparing dynamical and statistical model interpretations of multifragmentation data to constrain the symmetry energy. The results are compared with the isospin diffusion measurements of Chen (2005) at NSCL-MSU, which used momentum-dependent Gogny interactions with γ values of 1.6, 1.05, and 0.69. The finding that γ = 0.69 is consistent with a moderately stiff density dependence enables improved predictions for neutron skin thickness (Horowitz 2001, Oyamatsu 1998) and neutron star properties (Lattimer 1991, 2000, 2004; Steiner 2005). The work also highlights the unresolved discrepancy between Ono (2006), who found sequential decay effects of up to 50% in AMD, and Tian (2006), who found no significant change using the IQMD model.
