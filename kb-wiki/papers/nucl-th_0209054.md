# Mixed-Mode Shell-Model Calculations

> A one-dimensional harmonic oscillator in a box is used to introduce the oblique-basis concept. The method is extended to the nuclear shell model by combining traditional spherical states, which yield a diagonal representation of the usual single-particle interaction, with collective configurations that track deformation. An application to 24Mg, using the realistic two-body interaction of Wildenthal, is used to explore the validity of this mixed-mode shell-model scheme. Specifically, the correct binding energy (within 2% of the full-space result) as well as low-energy configurations that have greater than 90% overlap with full-space results are obtained in a space that spans less than 10% of the full-space. The theory is also applied to lower pf-shell nuclei, 44Ti-48Ti and 48Cr, using the Kuo-Brown-3 interaction. These nuclei show strong SU(3) symmetry breaking due mainly to the single-particle spin-orbit splitting. Nevertheless, the results also show that yrast band B(E2) values are insensitive to fragmentation of the SU(3) symmetry. Specifically, the quadrupole collectivity as measured by B(E2) strengths remains high even though the SU(3) symmetry is rather badly broken. The IBM and broken-pair models are considered as alternative basis sets for future oblique-basis shell-model calculations.

# Mixed-Mode Shell-Model Calculations

## Key claim
The oblique-basis (mixed-mode) shell-model approach can reproduce full-space binding energies within 2% and low-energy states with over 90% overlap using less than 10% of the full model space, but only when the Hamiltonian contains two competing modes (spherical single-particle and collective quadrupole-quadrupole) that are each diagonal in one of the chosen basis sets.

## Method
The authors introduce the oblique-basis concept using a one-dimensional harmonic oscillator in a box as a pedagogical analog. They then extend the method to nuclear shell-model calculations by combining traditional spherical harmonic-oscillator states (which diagonalize the single-particle interaction) with collective SU(3) configurations (which diagonalize the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction). The mixed-mode scheme is applied to 24Mg using the Wildenthal two-body interaction and to lower pf-shell nuclei (44-48Ti and 48Cr) using the Kuo-Brown-3 (KB3) interaction, comparing results against full-space calculations.

## Key numbers
- For 24Mg: binding energy reproduced within 2% of the full-space result using a space spanning less than 10% of the full model space.
- For 24Mg: low-energy configurations have greater than 90% overlap with full-space results.
- For 24Mg: a conventional m-scheme spherical shell-model calculation requires about 60% of the full space to obtain results comparable to the oblique-basis results.
- For lower pf-shell nuclei (44-48Ti and 48Cr): yrast band B(E2) values remain high despite strong SU(3) symmetry breaking from the single-particle spin-orbit splitting.

## Context
This work extends the Elliott SU(3) model and the spherical shell model by combining both into a single oblique-basis framework, addressing the long-standing competition between single-particle and collective degrees of freedom in nuclear structure. The results for pf-shell nuclei demonstrate that quadrupole collectivity persists even when SU(3) symmetry is badly broken, consistent with a quasi-SU(3) symmetry. The authors suggest future extensions using eigenvectors of the pairing interaction within the Sp(4) algebraic approach, as well as Hamiltonian-driven basis sets that would mimic the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) and broken-pair theory, enabling calculations in spaces too large for exact diagonalization.
