# Phase structure of three flavor QCD in external magnetic fields using HISQ fermions

> We study the phase structure of QCD with three degenerate flavors in the external magnetic fields using highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ). The simulations are performed on $16^3\times 6$ lattice. In order to investigate the quark mass dependence of the chiral transition we choose the values of the bare quark masses 0.015 and 0.0009375 in the lattice unit, corresponding to $m_π=320$ MeV and 80 MeV in the continuum limit. We found no indication of a first order phase transition in the current window of quark masses and external magnetic fields. Unlike to the case with standard staggered fermions inverse magnetic catalysis is always observed at about the critical temperature. The microscopic origin of this phenomena are further discussed by looking into the Dirac eigenvalue spectrum.

## Key claim

The authors find no indication of a first order phase transition in three-flavor QCD with external magnetic fields for the quark masses and field strengths they simulated, contrary to expectations from earlier work with standard staggered fermions where the magnetic field tended to strengthen the transition. Inverse magnetic catalysis is always observed near the critical temperature, unlike the case with standard staggered fermions.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations of three degenerate quark flavors using the Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) action on a \(16^3 \times 6\) lattice with a tree-level Symanzik gauge action. They generated configurations using the Rational Hybrid Monte-Carlo algorithm at two bare quark masses corresponding to pion masses of 320 MeV and 80 MeV in the continuum limit, with magnetic field strengths up to \(\sqrt{eB} \lesssim 870\) MeV. They measured the chiral condensate, its susceptibility, the Binder cumulant, and the Dirac eigenvalue spectrum using a stochastic estimator with Chebyshev expansion.

## Key numbers

*   Lattice size: \(16^3 \times 6\), with inverse lattice spacing \(a^{-1} \sim 740\) MeV.
*   Pion masses studied: \(m_\pi = 320\) MeV and \(m_\pi = 80\) MeV in the continuum limit.
*   Magnetic field range: \(0 \leq N_b \leq 56\), corresponding to \(0 \leq \sqrt{eB} \lesssim 870\) MeV.
*   For the Dirac spectrum calculation, the step size used was \(\delta = 0.0005\).
*   The Binder cumulant value for a first order phase transition is 1, for a second order transition with the Ising \(\mathbb{Z}_2\) universality class it is \(\sim 1.6\), and for a crossover it is 3.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of QCD in external magnetic fields, particularly those using stout smeared staggered actions which found inverse magnetic catalysis at physical pion mass (Bruckmann 2013) and studies of the pseudo-critical temperature with heavy pions (D'Elia 2018). It directly contrasts with earlier work by Tomiya (2017) using standard staggered fermions, where the magnetic field tended to make the phase transition stronger. The results enable further investigation of the chiral limit and the search for a possible critical endpoint in the magnetic field-temperature plane, though the authors note that the non-monotonic behavior seen for the 80 MeV pion mass may be a finite volume effect requiring larger volumes.
