# Deformations of the AdS-Schwarzschild black brane and the shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma

> Deformations of the AdS$_5$-Schwarzschild black brane, implemented in the AdS/CFT membrane paradigm, are scrutinized in the dual viscous hydrodynamic infrared limit. The latest experimental data analyses, regarding the shear viscosity-to-entropy density ratio of the quark-gluon plasma produced by heavy-ion collisions at the LHC and RHIC, are shown to constrain these deformations severely. Although corroborating with the robustness of the standard AdS$_5$-Schwarzschild black brane against deformations, there is still a margin for mild deformations which may carry 2-loop quantum corrections to gravity, whose implications to the strongly-coupled dual field theory are addressed and discussed.

## Key claim

Deformations of the AdS5-Schwarzschild black brane are severely constrained by experimental data on the shear viscosity-to-entropy density ratio of the quark-gluon plasma from LHC and RHIC, but a narrow margin for mild deformations remains that may encode 2-loop quantum corrections to gravity.

## Method

The author constructs a family of deformed AdS5-Schwarzschild black brane solutions using an embedding algorithm with Hamiltonian and momentum constraints from the Arnowitt-Deser-Misner (ADM) formalism. The shear viscosity-to-entropy density ratio and black brane temperature are computed from the deformed metric and compared to experimental constraints from heavy-ion collision data at LHC and RHIC, using results from the JETSCAPE Bayesian model, the Duke group, the Jyvaskyla-Helsinki-Munich group, and the MIT-Utrecht-Geneve group.

## Key numbers

* The standard KSS viscosity-bound saturation value eta/s = 1/4pi is calculated for the N = 4 SU(N_c) super-Yang-Mills plasma in the planar limit and for infinitely large 't Hooft coupling.
* The QGP is investigated in the temperature range T ~ 150-350 MeV, where QCD matter acts as a strongly-interacting, nearly perfect fluid.
* The inverse temperature of the AdS5-Schwarzschild black brane is beta = pi L^2/r_0, where r_0 is the horizon radius and L is the AdS radius.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of AdS-Schwarzschild black brane deformations in the context of phase transitions in AdS/QCD and holographic entanglement entropy (da Rocha, 2021). It also builds on the fluid/gravity duality framework (Bhattacharyya, 2008; Haack, 2008) and the membrane paradigm (Kovtun, 2003; Parikh, 1997). The results enable tighter experimental bounds on possible quantum corrections to gravity encoded in black brane deformations, and provide a framework for analyzing higher-curvature and higher-derivative corrections to thermal and hydrodynamic properties of dual gauge theories.
