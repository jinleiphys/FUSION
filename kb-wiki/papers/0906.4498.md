# Mass dispersion in transfer reactions with a stochastic mean-field theory

> Nucleon transfer in symmetric heavy-ion reactions at energies below the Coulomb barrier is investigated in the framework of a microscopic stochastic mean-field theory. While mean-field alone is known to significantly underpredict the dispersion of the fragment mass distribution, a considerable enhancement of the dispersion is obtained in the stochastic mean-field theory. The variance of the fragment mass distribution deduced from the stochastic theory scales with the number of exchanged nucleon. Therefore, the new approach provides the first fully microscopic theory consistent with the phenomenological analysis of the experimental data.

## Key claim

The stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach provides the first fully microscopic theory that yields a variance of the fragment mass distribution consistent with the phenomenological relation \(\sigma_{AA}^2 \simeq N_{\rm exc}\) (variance equals number of exchanged nucleons), whereas standard mean-field (TDHF) alone significantly underpredicts this dispersion.

## Method

The authors apply the stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach to head-on collisions of symmetric heavy-ion systems at energies just below the Coulomb barrier. They project the mean-field evolution onto the mass-asymmetry degree of freedom, extract the nucleon diffusion coefficient from the SMF equations in a semi-classical approximation, and compute the variance of the fragment mass distribution by integrating the diffusion coefficient over time. The results are compared with the number of exchanged nucleons and with standard TDHF calculations using the SLy4d Skyrme effective force.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{40}\)Ca+\(^{40}\)Ca at \(E_{\rm c.m.}=53.0\) MeV: SMF variance \(\sigma^2_{AA}(+\infty)=3.790\), TDHF variance \(\sigma^2_{\rm TDHF}(+\infty)=0.008\), number of exchanged nucleons \(N_{\rm exc}(+\infty)=3.634\).
- For \(^{56}\)Ni+\(^{56}\)Ni at \(E_{\rm c.m.}=100\) MeV: SMF variance \(\sigma^2_{AA}(+\infty)=5.606\), TDHF variance \(\sigma^2_{\rm TDHF}(+\infty)=0.142\), \(N_{\rm exc}(+\infty)=5.567\).
- For \(^{90}\)Zr+\(^{90}\)Zr at \(E_{\rm c.m.}=180\) MeV: SMF variance \(\sigma^2_{AA}(+\infty)=40.40\), TDHF variance \(\sigma^2_{\rm TDHF}(+\infty)=1.085\), \(N_{\rm exc}(+\infty)=41.56\).
- Coulomb barrier energies (frozen density approximation): 54.7 MeV for \(^{40}\)Ca, 103 MeV for \(^{56}\)Ni, 184 MeV for \(^{90}\)Zr.

## Context

This work extends the stochastic mean-field approach proposed by Ayik (2008) to nucleon transfer in heavy-ion reactions, addressing the long-standing failure of standard TDHF (Koonin 1980, Negele 1982) to describe fluctuations of one-body observables. The SMF results are consistent with the phenomenological nucleon exchange model of Feldmeier and with the semi-empirical relation \(\sigma_{AA}^2 \simeq N_{\rm exc}\) used to analyze experimental data (Freiesleben 1984, Adamides 1994). The approach provides a practical microscopic framework for describing dissipation and fluctuation dynamics at low energies, applicable to fission, fusion near the barrier, and deep-inelastic collisions.
