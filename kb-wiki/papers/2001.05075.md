# First glimpse of the $N=82$ shell closure below $Z=50$ from masses of neutron-rich cadmium isotopes and isomers

> We probe the $N=82$ nuclear shell closure by mass measurements of neutron-rich cadmium isotopes with the ISOLTRAP spectrometer at ISOLDE-CERN. The new mass of $^{132}$Cd offers the first value of the $N=82$, two-neutron shell gap below $Z=50$ and confirms the phenomenon of mutually enhanced magicity at $^{132}$Sn. Using the recently implemented phase-imaging ion-cyclotron-resonance method, the ordering of the low-lying isomers in $^{129}$Cd and their energies are determined. The new experimental findings are used to test large-scale shell-model, mean-field and beyond-mean-field calculations, as well as the ab initio valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group.

## Key claim
The mass of $^{132}$Cd provides the first experimental value of the $N=82$ two-neutron shell gap for proton numbers below $Z=50$, confirming the phenomenon of mutually enhanced magicity at $^{132}$Sn, but the size of the drop in the shell gap for $Z<50$ is not reproduced by any of the theoretical calculations tested.

## Method
The ISOLTRAP spectrometer at ISOLDE-CERN was used to measure masses of neutron-rich cadmium isotopes ($^{124}$Cd to $^{132}$Cd) and isomers. The multi-reflection time-of-flight mass spectrometer (MR-ToF MS) determined masses of $^{131,132}$Cd, while the time-of-flight ion-cyclotron-resonance (ToF-ICR) method in a Penning trap measured $^{124,126,128,131}$Cd. The phase-imaging ion-cyclotron-resonance (PI-ICR) method separated and determined the masses of the $3/2^+$ and $11/2^-$ states in $^{127,129}$Cd with a resolving power of about $2\times10^6$ in 106 ms.

## Key numbers
- Mass excess of $^{132}$Cd: $-50499(72)$ keV (using $^{132}$Ba$^+$ reference) and $-50386(110)$ keV (using $^{132}$Cs$^+$ reference)
- Excitation energy of the $3/2^+$ state in $^{129}$Cd: 343(8) keV (new value)
- Excitation energy of the $11/2^-$ state in $^{127}$Cd: 283(12) keV (agrees with TITAN result)
- Mass excess of $^{131}$Cd: $-55167(31)$ keV (ToF-ICR) and $-55238(24)$ keV (MR-ToF MS)
- Mass excess of $^{129}$Cd $11/2^-$ state: $-63122.1(5.4)$ keV
- Mass excess of $^{129}$Cd $3/2^+$ state: $-62779.1(5.6)$ keV

## Context
This work extends mass measurements of neutron-rich cadmium isotopes beyond previous studies by Atanasov (2015) and Lascar (2017), providing the first direct determination of the $N=82$ shell gap below $Z=50$. The results test large-scale shell-model calculations (jj45pn, NA-14, EPQQM), beyond-mean-field calculations using the SLy4 Skyrme interaction, and ab initio valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) calculations. The data enable improved predictions for nuclear structure near the $r$-process path and benchmark microscopic calculations of valence-space Hamiltonians for heavy nuclei.
