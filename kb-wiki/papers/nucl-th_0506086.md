# Influence of Chaos on the fusion enhancement by electron screening

> We study the effect of screening by bound electrons in low energy nuclear reactions. We use molecular dynamics to simulate the reactions involving many electrons: D+$d$, D+D, $^3$He+$d$, $^3$He+D, $^6$Li+$d$, $^6$Li+D, $^7$Li+$p$, $^7$Li+H. Quantum effects corresponding to the Pauli and Heisenberg principles are enforced by constraints in terms of the phase space occupancy. In addition to the well known adiabatic and sudden limits, we propose a new "dissipative limit" which is expected to be important not only at high energies but in the extremely low energy region. The dissipative limit is associated with the chaotic behavior of the electronic motion. It affects also the magnitude of the enhancement factor. We discuss also numerical experiments using polarized targets. The derived enhancement factors in our simulation are in agreement with those extracted within the $R$-matrix approach.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a new "dissipative limit" for the electron screening enhancement in low-energy nuclear fusion, associated with chaotic behavior of electronic motion, which can reduce the enhancement factor below the adiabatic limit and even cause hindrance of tunneling in some cases.

## Method
The authors used constrained molecular dynamics (CoMD) with Lagrange multipliers to enforce the Pauli exclusion principle and Heisenberg uncertainty principle, simulating head-on collisions for reactions involving up to many electrons. They treated tunneling by switching to imaginary-time evolution when the collective momentum of the projectile-target system becomes zero, computing the penetrability from the action integral. For each reaction system, they generated ensembles of events with different initial phase-space configurations and averaged the enhancement factors.

## Key numbers
- For D+d (1 electron): screening potential U_e = 15.9 ± 2.0 eV from fit to simulated enhancement factors
- For D+D (2 electrons): screening potential U_e = 21.6 ± 0.3 eV from fit
- For 3He+d: screening potential U_e = 82.4 ± 1.9 eV from fit
- For 3He+D: screening potential U_e = 102.8 ± 3.0 eV from fit
- For 6Li+d: screening potential U_e = 152.0 ± 9.9 eV from fit
- For 6Li+D: screening potential U_e = 214.4 ± 18.5 eV from fit
- Adiabatic limit screening potentials: 40.7 eV (D+d gerade state), 51.7 eV (D+D ground state), 119 eV (3He+d), 110 eV (3He+D), ~174 eV (Li isotopes with LiF target approximation)
- For D+d at E_cm = 0.15 keV, individual event enhancement factors ranged from 6.5 to 170.8

## Context
This work extends prior time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) studies of electron screening (Kimura et al., 2004; Koonin et al., 1987) to systems with many electrons and to very low energies where fluctuations become important. The CoMD approach captures correlations and fluctuations that mean-field TDHF calculations miss due to their cylindrical symmetry constraint. The results are compared with screening potentials extracted from R-matrix analyses (Barker, 2002) and Trojan Horse Method measurements (Spitaleri et al., 2004), and the paper suggests that experiments with polarized targets could control the enhancement factor.
