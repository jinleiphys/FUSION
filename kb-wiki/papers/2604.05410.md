# Impact of neutron-proton pairing on the nucleon high-momentum distribution in symmetric nuclear matter

> The effect of neutron-proton ($np$) pairing on the high-momentum tail (HMT) of nucleon momentum distributions in symmetric nuclear matter is investigated within a combined framework of the extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach with off-shell BCS theory. The HMT ratio, quantifying the high-momentum fraction in the BCS state relative to the normal state, reaches about $1.06$ around the density of $0.052\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$, indicating that the maximal contribution of the $np$ pairing, amounts to approximately 6\% that from short-range correlations (SRCs). This contribution exhibits a density dependence that closely follows the squared relative pairing gap $\widetildeΔ_F=Z_FΔ(k_F)$ with respect to the kinetic energy $E_{k_F}^*$ evaluated using the effective mass, suggesting that $\widetildeΔ_F^2/E_{k_F}^{*2}$ provides a qualitative measure of the $np$ pairing effect on the HMT. These findings highlight the significant role of $np$ pairing and its interplay with SRCs in shaping nucleon momentum distributions in nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The neutron-proton (np) pairing contribution to the high-momentum tail (HMT) of nucleon momentum distributions in symmetric nuclear matter is modest, reaching a maximum of about 6% of the contribution from short-range correlations (SRCs) at a density near 0.052 fm^{-3}, and this effect is qualitatively captured by the squared relative pairing gap \(\widetilde{\Delta}_F^2/E_{k_F}^{*2}\).

## Method

The authors combined the extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (EBHF) approach, which accounts for SRCs through ladder-diagram resummations, with off-shell BCS theory to incorporate np pairing effects. The nucleon self-energy was approximated up to third order in the hole-line expansion, including the renormalization term \(M_3\) that accounts for partial occupation of hole states due to NN correlations. The gap equation was solved self-consistently with the density constraint using the Argonne \(V_{18}\) two-body interaction, and the momentum distributions were obtained by integrating the spectral functions over energy.

## Key numbers

* The HMT ratio \(N_{BCS}/N_{normal}\) reaches a maximum of about **1.06** at a density of **0.052 fm^{-3}** when the third-order self-energy \(M_3\) is included, indicating that np pairing contributes approximately **6%** of the HMT arising from SRCs.
* The calculations used a maximum momentum cutoff of **\(k_\Lambda = 5\) fm^{-1}** to ensure convergence, with lower integration limits of **\(k_a = 2\) fm^{-1}** and **\(k_a = 3\) fm^{-1}** to isolate the SRC-dominated regions.
* The pairing gap obtained with the self-energy including \(M_3\) is significantly larger than that obtained with only \(M_1+M_2\), with the density dependence of the effective pairing gap \(\widetilde{\Delta}_F = Z_F \Delta(k_F)\) qualitatively following the same trend as the HMT ratio.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nucleon momentum distributions in nuclear matter by quantifying the interplay between long-range tensor-force-driven np pairing and short-range correlations (SRCs). Prior work by Shang (2013) and Duan (2025) suggested that np pairing could induce a high-momentum tail, and the present results provide a quantitative estimate of this effect relative to SRCs. The findings enable future investigations using chiral interactions (where the tensor component is weaker) and including polarization corrections to the pairing interaction, which were omitted here due to complexity.
