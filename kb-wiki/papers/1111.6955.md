# New physics in B_s^0 -> J/ψϕdecays?

> After a brief review of B_s^0 - \bar B_s^0 oscillations, we discuss the weak decays B_s^0 -> J/ψϕand B_s^0 -> J/ψf_0(980) and the ratio R_{f_0/ϕ} of their decay rates in the light of recent measurements by the LHCb, D0 and CDF Collaborations. We point out that the experimental values for R_{f_0/ϕ} impose tight limits on new physics contributions to both decay channels.

## Key claim
The experimental values of the ratio R_{f0/phi} of decay rates for B_s^0 -> J/psi f_0(980) and B_s^0 -> J/psi phi impose tight limits on new physics contributions to both decay channels; the measured ratio favors Standard Model predictions with a form factor F_1^{B_s^0 -> f_0}(m_{J/psi}^2) < 0.4, while new physics contributions would require unrealistic form factor values or different phases to match observations.

## Method
The authors calculate the ratio R_{f0/phi} using QCD factorization (QCDF) decay amplitudes for B_s^0 -> J/psi f_0(980) and B_s^0 -> J/psi phi, combined with a relativistic dispersion-relation model for the non-perturbative B_s -> f_0(980) transition form factor. They introduce phenomenological short-distance amplitudes zeta^{(h)} to mock up possible new physics contributions, adjusting them to fit experimental data on B_s^0 -> J/psi phi branching ratio, polarization fractions, and relative phases, then inserting them into the B_s^0 -> J/psi f_0(980) amplitude to predict R_{f0/phi}.

## Key numbers
- Standard Model prediction for the CP-violating phase: -2 beta_s = phi_s = -0.038 +/- 0.002
- D0 measurement of Delta Gamma^s: 0.163^{+0.065}_{-0.064} ps^{-1}
- Form factor prediction from the authors' model: F_1^{B_s^0 -> f_0}(m_{J/psi}^2) ~ 0.4
- Form factor estimate from QCD sum rules: F_1^{B_s^0 -> f_0}(m_{J/psi}^2) ~ 0.3
- Predicted ratio R_{f0/phi} with Standard Model only (zeta=0): 0.35 +/- 0.05 for form factor range 0.3-0.4
- Predicted ratio R_{f0/phi} with new physics contributions (zeta nonzero): ~0.6 +/- 0.05 for form factor range 0.3-0.4
- D0 measurement: R_{f0/phi} = 0.275 +/- 0.041 +/- 0.061 (statistical and systematic)
- CDF measurement: R_{f0/phi} = 0.257 +/- 0.020 +/- 0.014
- LHCb measurement: R_{f0/phi} = 0.252^{+0.046+0.027}_{-0.032-0.033}
- f_0(980) mass: m_{f0} = 980 +/- 10 MeV
- f_0(980) width range: 40-100 MeV

## Context
This work extends the analysis of CP violation in B_s^0 -> J/psi phi decays, which has been studied by CDF (Aaltonen 2007), D0 (Abazov 2008, 2011), and LHCb collaborations, where initial hints of large deviations from the Standard Model have been refined. The paper builds on earlier estimates of the ratio R_{f0/phi} by Stone (2008) based on charm-sector decays, and on QCD sum rule calculations of B_s -> f_0 form factors by Colangelo (2010) and Ghahramany (2009). The results enable tighter constraints on new physics contributions to b -> s c cbar transitions and inform ongoing experimental analyses of the CP-violating phase beta_s at LHCb.
