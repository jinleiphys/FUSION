# Wobbling Motion in Atomic Nuclei with Positive-Gamma Shapes

> The three moments of inertia associated with the wobbling mode built on the superdeformed states in 163Lu are investigated by means of the cranked shell model plus random phase approximation to the configuration with an aligned quasiparticle. The result indicates that it is crucial to take into account the direct contribution to the moments of inertia from the aligned quasiparticle so as to realize J_x > J_y in positive-gamma shapes. Quenching of the pairing gap cooperates with the alignment effect. The peculiarity of the recently observed 163Lu data is discussed by calculating not only the electromagnetic properties but also the excitation spectra.

## Key claim

The wobbling mode in positive-gamma nuclei requires the direct contribution to the moments of inertia from an aligned quasiparticle to realize \(\mathcal{J}_x > \mathcal{J}_y\), and quenching of the pairing gap cooperates with this alignment effect; the paper presents the first RPA calculation for a rotating odd-\(A\) configuration to demonstrate this.

## Method

The authors used the cranked shell model plus random phase approximation (RPA) in the rotating frame, applied to configurations with aligned quasiparticles (QPs) in \(^{163}\)Lu and in Gd isotopes. The QP states were obtained by diagonalizing the cranked triaxial Nilsson plus BCS Hamiltonian at each rotational frequency \(\omega_\mathrm{rot}\), and the RPA was performed with a pairing plus doubly-stretched \(Q\cdot Q\) interaction, using a dispersion equation that decouples the Nambu-Goldstone mode analytically.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{163}\)Lu at \(\epsilon_2 = 0.43\), \(\gamma = 20^\circ\), and \(\Delta_\mathrm{n} = \Delta_\mathrm{p} = 0.3\) MeV, the calculated transition quadrupole moments are \(Q_\mathrm{t} = 10.9\)–\(11.3\) \(eb\) for \(\hbar\omega_\mathrm{rot} = 0.20\)–\(0.57\) MeV, consistent with the measured \(Q_\mathrm{t} = 10.7 \pm 0.7\) \(eb\).
- For \(^{163}\)Lu at \(\hbar\omega_\mathrm{rot} = 0.3\) MeV, the ratios of effective moments of inertia are \(\mathcal{J}_y^\mathrm{(eff)}/\mathcal{J}_x = 0.90\) and \(\mathcal{J}_z^\mathrm{(eff)}/\mathcal{J}_x = 0.19\), with \(\vert Q_1^{(-)}/ Q_2^{(-)}\vert = 0.78\).
- The wobbling angle for \(^{163}\)Lu ranges from \(19^\circ\) to \(13^\circ\) over the calculated frequency range.
- The interband \(B(E2)\) strength in \(^{163}\)Lu is measured at 170 Weisskopf units; the RPA calculation accounts for 1/2 to 1/3 of this value.
- For the \(^{147}\)Gd calculation, \(\epsilon_2 = 0.19\) and \(\Delta_\mathrm{n} = \Delta_\mathrm{p} = 0.6\) MeV were used at \(\hbar\omega_\mathrm{rot} = 0.3\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical description of wobbling motion from the particle-rotor model (PRM) used by the Lund group (2001) to a fully microscopic cranked shell model plus RPA framework, addressing the inability of the PRM with irrotational moments of inertia to describe positive-gamma rotation. It builds on earlier RPA formulations by Marshalek (1979) and by Matsuzaki, Shimizu, and Matsuyanagi (1998) for even-even nuclei, and it enables future microscopic studies of three-dimensional nuclear rotations in triaxial superdeformed nuclei, including the interpretation of the TSD2 band in \(^{163}\)Lu as a wobbling excitation.
