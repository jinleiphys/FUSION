# Quasi-Elastic Scattering in the Inclusive ($^3$He, t) Reaction

> The triton energy spectra of the charge-exchange $^{12}$C($^3$He,t) reaction at 2 GeV beam energy are analyzed in the quasi-elastic nucleon knock-out region. Considering that this region is mainly populated by the charge-exchange of a proton in $^3$He with a neutron in the target nucleus and the final proton going in the continuum, the cross-sections are written in the distorted-wave impulse approximation. The t-matrix for the elementary exchange process is constructed in the DWBA, using one pion- plus rho-exchange potential for the spin-isospin nucleon- nucleon potential. This t-matrix reproduces the experimental data on the elementary pn $\rightarrow $np process. The calculated cross-sections for the $^{12}$C($^3$He,t) reaction at $2^o$ to $7^o$ triton emission angle are compared with the corresponding experimental data, and are found in reasonable overall accord.

## Key claim

The quasi-elastic peak region in the inclusive $^{12}$C($^3$He,t) reaction at 2 GeV beam energy can be accounted for by an independent-particle charge-exchange knock-out mechanism alone, without invoking strong residual particle-hole correlations, and the calculated cross-sections are in "reasonable overall accord" with experimental data, though discrepancies remain at some angles (e.g., overestimation by about a factor of 3/2 at 5 degrees and underestimation at 7 degrees).

## Method

The authors model the quasi-elastic region as populated by the $^{12}$C($^3$He,tp) reaction, where a proton in $^3$He charge-exchanges with a neutron in the $^{12}$C target and the final proton goes into the continuum. The cross-section is calculated in the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) using eikonal distorted waves for $^3$He and triton, and plane waves for the unobserved proton. The elementary t-matrix for the pn $\rightarrow$ np process is constructed in the DWBA using a one-pion plus rho-exchange potential for the spin-isospin nucleon-nucleon interaction, with parameters constrained by other experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 2 GeV for $^3$He on $^{12}$C target.
- Triton emission angles studied: $2^\circ$ to $7^\circ$.
- Neutron binding energies in $^{12}$C: 34 MeV for $1s_{1/2}$ orbital, 16 MeV for $1p_{3/2}$ orbital.
- Momentum transfer (q) in the quasi-elastic range: approximately 250 MeV/c at 2-3 degrees, up to about 500 MeV/c at larger angles.
- Parameters for the $^3$He $\rightarrow$ t transition form factor: $F_o = 0.96$, $\gamma = 11.15$ GeV$^{-2}$, $\eta = 14$ GeV$^{-4}$.
- Pion-nucleon coupling constant $f_\pi = 1.008$, rho-nucleon coupling constant $f_\rho = 7.815$; cutoff parameters $\Lambda_\pi = 1.2$ GeV/c, $\Lambda_\rho = 2$ GeV/c.
- Elastic pn scattering parameters at the relevant energy: total cross-section $\sigma_T \approx 34$ mb, slope parameter $\alpha \approx 6$ (GeV/c)$^{-2}$, ratio of real to imaginary part $\beta = -0.45$.
- The isospin-dependent term ($t_\tau$) contributes less than 10% to the cross-section.
- The calculated cross-section at the peak can change by a factor of 2 depending on the value of $\beta$ within its experimental uncertainty (range 0.05 to -0.7).
- At 7 degrees, the cross-section magnitude is approximately 7 $\mu$b.

## Context

This work extends the study of quasi-free charge-exchange reactions, such as (p,n) and ($^3$He,t), into the quasi-elastic region, following experimental work by Bergqvist et al. (1995) at Saturne and theoretical efforts by Alberico et al. (RPA surface response) and Ichimura et al. (continuum RPA). The authors respond to the observation by Bertsch et al. (critical review) that residual particle-hole correlations in the quasi-free region appear much weaker than expected, by testing whether an independent-particle framework can account for the data. The approach enables future exploration of single-particle spin-isospin response in nuclei and provides a baseline against which correlation effects can be measured.
