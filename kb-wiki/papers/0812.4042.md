# Electromagnetic form factor of the pion from twisted-mass lattice QCD at Nf=2

> We present a lattice calculation of the electromagnetic form factor of the pion obtained using the tree-level Symanzik improved gauge action with two flavors of dynamical twisted Wilson quarks. The simulated pion masses range approximately from 260 to 580 MeV and the lattice box sizes are chosen in order to guarantee that M L > 4. Accurate results for the form factor are obtained using all-to-all quark propagators evaluated by a stochastic procedure. The momentum dependence of the pion form factor is investigated up to values of the squared four-momentum transfer Q**2 ~ 0.8 GeV**2 and, thanks to the use of twisted boundary conditions, down to Q**2 ~ 0.05 GeV**2. Volume and discretization effects on the form factor appear to be within the statistical errors. Our results for the pion mass, decay constant and form factor are analyzed using (continuum) Chiral Perturbation Theory at next-to-next-to-leading order. The extrapolated value of the pion charge radius is <r**2>{phys} = 0.456 +/- 0.030(stat.) +/- 0.024(syst.) in nice agreement with the experimental result. The extrapolated values of the pion form factor agree very well with the experimental data up to Q**2 ~ 0.8 GeV**2 within uncertainties which become competitive with the experimental errors for Q**2 > 0.3 GeV**2. The relevant low-energy constants appearing in the chiral expansion of the pion form factor are extracted from our lattice data, which come essentially from a single lattice spacing, adding the experimental value of the pion scalar radius in the fitting procedure. Our findings are in nice agreement with the available results of ChPT analyses of pion-pion scattering data as well as with other analyses of our collaboration.

## Key claim

The authors present a lattice QCD calculation of the pion electromagnetic form factor that, after extrapolation using next-to-next-to-leading order Chiral Perturbation Theory, yields a pion charge radius and form factor in agreement with experiment, but the analysis is limited by coming essentially from a single lattice spacing, requiring the addition of the experimental value of the pion scalar radius in the fitting procedure to constrain the low-energy constants.

## Method

The calculation uses the tree-level Symanzik improved gauge action with two flavors of dynamical twisted Wilson quarks tuned to maximal twist for automatic O(a) improvement. Pion masses from approximately 260 to 580 MeV are simulated on lattices with sizes ensuring M_pi L > 4, and all-to-all quark propagators are evaluated using a stochastic "one-end-trick" procedure. The momentum dependence of the form factor is investigated up to Q^2 ~ 0.8 GeV^2 and down to Q^2 ~ 0.05 GeV^2 using twisted boundary conditions in the Breit reference frame, and the results are extrapolated to the physical point using continuum Chiral Perturbation Theory at next-to-next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers

* Pion charge radius: <r^2>^{phys} = 0.456 +/- 0.030(stat.) +/- 0.024(syst.) fm^2, in agreement with the experimental value 0.452 +/- 0.011 fm^2
* Simulated pion masses range from approximately 260 to 580 MeV
* Momentum transfer investigated up to Q^2 ~ 0.8 GeV^2 and down to Q^2 ~ 0.05 GeV^2
* Low-energy constants extracted: bar-l_1 = -0.4 +/- 1.3 +/- 0.6, bar-l_2 = 4.3 +/- 1.1 +/- 0.4, bar-l_3 = 3.2 +/- 0.8 +/- 0.2, bar-l_4 = 4.4 +/- 0.2 +/- 0.1, bar-l_6 = 14.9 +/- 1.2 +/- 0.7 (statistical and systematic errors respectively)
* Lattice spacing approximately 0.09 fm for six quark mass values and approximately 0.07 fm for two quark mass values

## Context

This work extends earlier quenched lattice studies of the pion form factor (Draper, 1989; LHP Collaboration, 2004) and unquenched studies with two flavors (JLQCD, 2005; QCDSF/UKQCD, 2007) by using twisted-mass fermions with all-to-all propagators for improved statistical precision. The results enable reliable extraction of the low-energy constants of Chiral Perturbation Theory from lattice data and provide a benchmark for future calculations with three dynamical flavors and multiple lattice spacings to control discretization effects.
