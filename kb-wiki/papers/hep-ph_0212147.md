# Solar Neutrinos Before and After KamLAND

> We use the recently reported KamLAND measurements on oscillations of reactor anti-neutrinos, together with the data of previously reported solar neutrino experiments, to show that: (1) the total 8B neutrino flux emitted by the Sun is 1.00(1.0 \pm 0.06) of the standard solar model (BP00) predicted flux, (2) the KamLAND measurements reduce the area of the globally allowed oscillation regions that must be explored in model fitting by six orders of magnitude in the Delta m^2-tan^2 theta plane, (3) LMA is now the unique oscillation solution to a CL of 4.7sigma, (4) maximal mixing is disfavored at 3.1 sigma, (5) active-sterile admixtures are constrained to sin^2 eta<0.13 at 1 sigma, (6) the observed ^8B flux that is in the form of sterile neutrinos is 0.00^{+0.09}_{-0.00} (1 sigma), of the standard solar model (BP00) predicted flux, and (7) non-standard solar models that were invented to completely avoid solar neutrino oscillations are excluded by KamLAND plus solar at 7.9 sigma . We also refine quantitative predictions for future 7Be and p-p solar neutrino experiments.

## Key claim

The KamLAND reactor anti-neutrino measurements, combined with all available solar neutrino data, show that the Large Mixing Angle (LMA) solution is the unique oscillation solution at 4.7σ confidence level, and that the total ⁸B solar neutrino flux is 1.00(1.0 ± 0.06) times the standard solar model (BP00) prediction, under the assumption that CPT is satisfied and that the anti-neutrino measurements apply directly to neutrinos.

## Method

The authors performed a global χ² analysis combining the KamLAND reactor anti-neutrino total rate and energy spectrum data with solar neutrino data from the chlorine (Homestake), gallium (SAGE, GALLEX, GNO), Super-Kamiokande, and SNO (CC and NC) experiments. They treated the ⁸B neutrino flux as a free parameter while using BP00 standard solar model predictions for other fluxes, and included Earth matter effects for the KamLAND analysis at the level of a constant density approximation.

## Key numbers

- Total ⁸B solar neutrino flux: 1.00(1.0 ± 0.06) times the BP00 standard solar model predicted flux (1σ)
- KamLAND measured rate relative to no-oscillation expectation: R_KamLAND = 0.611 ± 0.094
- LMA is the unique oscillation solution at 4.7σ confidence level
- Maximal mixing (tan²θ = 1) is disfavored at 3.1σ
- Active-sterile admixture constrained to sin²η ≤ 0.13 at 1σ
- Observed ⁸B flux in sterile neutrinos: 0.00^{+0.09}_{-0.00} times BP00 predicted flux (1σ)
- Non-standard solar models excluded by KamLAND plus solar data at 7.9σ
- Best-fit KamLAND oscillation parameters: Δm² = 7.1 × 10⁻⁵ eV², tan²θ = 0.52
- Allowed 1σ range for mixing angle: 0.36 < tan²θ < 0.58
- Predicted ⁷Be ν-e scattering rate: 0.64 ± 0.02 (1σ) relative to BP00 prediction
- Predicted SNO CC day-night asymmetry: 3.3^{+0.7}_{-0.6}% (1σ)
- LOW solution excluded at 4.8σ, vacuum solutions at 4.9σ, SMA solution at 6.1σ

## Context

This paper extends the pre-KamLAND solar neutrino analyses by Bahcall, Gonzalez-Garcia, and Pena-Garay (2002) and the SNO collaboration results (2002) by incorporating the first KamLAND reactor anti-neutrino measurements (KamLAND collaboration, 2002). The work definitively excludes the SMA, LOW, and vacuum oscillation solutions that were previously allowed, and rules out non-standard solar model explanations (such as spin flavor precession or flavor-changing interactions) at high significance. It enables precise predictions for future experiments including SNO day-night asymmetry measurements, BOREXINO ⁷Be observations, and p-p neutrino detectors, and provides a foundation for precision tests of the LMA solution and the solar neutrino flux.
