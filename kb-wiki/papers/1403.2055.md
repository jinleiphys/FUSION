# Density-dependent symmetry energy at subsaturation densities from nuclear mass differences

> We extract the mass-dependent symmetry energy coefficients $a_{\text{sym}}({A})$ with the nuclear mass differences reducing the uncertainties as far as possible. The estimated $a_{\text{sym}}({A})$ of $^{208}\text{Pb}$ is $22.4\pm 0.3 $ MeV, which is further used to analyze the density-dependent nuclear matter symmetry energy at subsaturation densities. The slope parameter of the symmetry energy at the saturation density $ρ_{0}$ is $L=50.0\pm15.5$ MeV. Furthermore, it is found that, at the density of $ρ=0.69ρ_{0}=0.11$fm$^{-3}$, the symmetry energy $S(ρ=0.11\text{fm}^{-3})=25.98\pm0.01$ MeV and the correspondingly slope parameter is $L=49.6\pm6.2$ MeV, which are consistent with other independent analysis.

## Key claim

The symmetry energy coefficient of finite nuclei, extracted from nuclear mass differences, can be used to determine the density-dependent symmetry energy of nuclear matter at subsaturation densities, yielding a slope parameter \(L=50.0\pm15.5\) MeV at saturation density that is consistent with several independent analyses, but the result depends on the choice of reference density for the finite nucleus.

## Method

The authors extracted mass-dependent symmetry energy coefficients \(a_{\text{sym}}(A)\) for translead nuclei by taking partial derivatives of the liquid-drop binding energy formula with respect to isospin asymmetry, using experimental mass differences between \((Z-1,A)\) and \((Z+1,A)\) nuclei to cancel pairing and reduce shell effects. The resulting \(a_{\text{sym}}(A)\) for \({}^{208}\)Pb was then related to the nuclear matter symmetry energy \(S(\rho)\) at a reference density \(\rho_A \approx 0.55\rho_0\) using a DDM3Y-type parametrization, with the saturation symmetry energy \(S_0 = 32.3 \pm 1.3\) MeV taken as input from prior work.

## Key numbers

* Symmetry energy coefficient of \({}^{208}\)Pb: \(a_{\text{sym}}(^{208}\text{Pb}) = 22.4 \pm 0.3\) MeV.
* Slope parameter of the symmetry energy at saturation density \(\rho_0\): \(L = 50.0 \pm 15.5\) MeV.
* At density \(\rho = 0.69\rho_0 = 0.11\) fm\(^{-3}\): symmetry energy \(S(\rho=0.11\text{ fm}^{-3}) = 25.98 \pm 0.01\) MeV.
* At density \(\rho = 0.69\rho_0 = 0.11\) fm\(^{-3}\): slope parameter \(L = 49.6 \pm 6.2\) MeV.
* Ratio of surface to volume symmetry coefficient: \(\kappa = 2.62^{+0.48}_{-0.46}\).
* Parameters in the DDM3Y symmetry energy formula: \(C_1 = 36.3 \mp 4.5\) MeV and \(C_2 = -17.0 \pm 5.8\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of using nuclear binding energies to constrain the symmetry energy, improving upon prior studies that used \(\beta^{-}\)-decay energies of odd-A heavy nuclei (Dong et al., 2013) by using mass differences that better cancel pairing and shell effects. The extracted \(L\) value overlaps with results from charge radius differences of mirror nuclei (Wang and Li, 2013), astrophysical neutron star observations (Steiner et al.), and empirical density functional analyses (Agrawal et al.). The precise determination of the symmetry energy at \(\rho = 0.11\) fm\(^{-3}\) is particularly relevant for constraining neutron skin thickness and neutron star cooling, and the authors note that reducing the uncertainty in \(S_0\) is needed to further narrow the \(L\) value.
