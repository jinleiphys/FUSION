# Coulomb-nuclear interference in elastic scattering: eikonal calculation to all orders of $α$

> The Coulomb-nuclear interference (CNI) has recently been used by the TOTEM Collaboration to analyse proton-proton elastic-scattering data from the LHC and to draw physics conclusions. This paper will present an eikonal calculation of the CNI effects performed to all orders of the fine structure constant, $α$. This calculation will be used as a reference to benchmark several widely-used CNI formulae and to verify several recent claims by other authors.

# Coulomb-nuclear interference in elastic scattering: eikonal calculation to all orders of α

## Key claim

The paper presents a numerical eikonal calculation of Coulomb-nuclear interference (CNI) effects performed to all orders of the fine structure constant α, and uses it as a reference to benchmark several widely-used CNI formulae. The calculation shows that the Cahn and Kudr´at-Lokaj´ıcek (KL) formulae reproduce the numerical reference with relative deviations of O(10⁻⁴), while Petrov’s formula (missing one term) deviates by almost 5%, and a plain sum of Coulomb and nuclear amplitudes deviates by up to 3.5%.

## Method

The author starts from the fundamental eikonal assumption of additivity of eikonals: the total eikonal δ^(C+N)(b) = δ^C(b) + δ^N(b) is obtained by summing the Coulomb eikonal (from Fourier-Bessel transform of the Born-level Coulomb amplitude) and the nuclear eikonal (from the nuclear amplitude via Fourier-Bessel transform). The total amplitude F^(C+N)(t) is then computed by an inverse Fourier-Bessel transform of the full eikonal expression, evaluated numerically using adaptive integration from the GSL library. The numerical calculation is performed for two nuclear amplitude models (central and peripheral) based on TOTEM measurements at √s = 8 TeV, with a realistic proton form factor from Puckett et al., and the results are compared to analytic CNI formulae by Cahn, KL, Petrov, SWY, and the trivial sum.

## Key numbers

- For point-like protons, the complete Coulomb cross-section from the numerical calculation agrees perfectly with the Born-level expression; for a realistic proton form factor, relative deviations are O(10⁻⁴).
- The phase of the complete Coulomb amplitude for point-like protons agrees perfectly with Cahn’s η(t) calculation; for a realistic form factor, deviations are O(10⁻³).
- The Cahn and KL formulae reproduce the numerical eikonal calculation with relative deviations of O(10⁻⁴) for both central and peripheral nuclear amplitudes.
- Petrov’s formula deviates from the numerical reference by almost 5% for the peripheral nuclear amplitude.
- The trivial sum of Coulomb and nuclear amplitudes deviates by up to 3.5% from the numerical reference.
- The SWY formula shows relative deviations of O(10⁻³) for the central nuclear amplitude and up to about 1% for the peripheral case.

## Context

This work extends the eikonal treatment of CNI originally developed by Cahn (1982) and Kudr´at and Lokaj´ıcek (1994), and directly addresses recent claims by Petrov (2018, 2019, 2020) that Cahn’s approximation of the complete Coulomb amplitude is inexact and that the expansion in orders of α was insufficiently truncated. The paper also tests proposals by Godizov (2019) and Donnachie and Landshoff (2019) that CNI effects may be negligible on amplitude level. The numerical approach provides a reference calculation to all orders of α for the first time, enabling future studies of Petrov’s all-orders formula and evaluations of inelastic intermediate state effects as estimated by Khoze et al. (2019).
