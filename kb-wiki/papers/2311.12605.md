# Double spin correlations in the reaction dd->pnpn and in the pn-elastic scattering

> An unexpectedly large double spin correlation with a sharp dependence on energy, found in elastic proton-proton scattering at large angles in the c.m.s. energy range $\sqrt{s_{pp}}= 3 ÷5.5$ GeV, may be associated with the formation of exotic eight-quark resonances at the thresholds of the strangeness and charm production. For a deeper understanding of the dynamics of this process it is important to measure double spin correlation in elastic $pn$ scattering in the same kinematics. The possibility of obtaining this data from the reaction $d d\to pnp$ with two polarized deuterons on NICA SPD is considered in this paper.

## Key claim

The double spin correlation measured in the reaction \(dd\to pnpn\) with both deuteron beams polarized is directly proportional to the double spin correlation in elastic \(pn\) scattering, specifically \(C_{y,y} = \frac{4}{9}A_{YY}^{NN}\), provided the S-wave component of the deuteron wave function dominates and the double pole mechanism is valid.

## Method

The authors derive the transition amplitude for the reaction \(dd\to pnpn\) assuming a double pole mechanism where each deuteron virtually decays into a nucleon pair, and one nucleon from each deuteron undergoes elastic \(NN\) scattering. Using the S-wave component of the deuteron wave function and rotational invariance, they express the double spin asymmetry \(A_{YY}^{dd}\) for polarized deuteron beams in terms of the double spin correlation \(A_{YY}^{NN}\) of the elastic \(NN\) subprocess. The relation is shown to be independent of the orientation of the quantization axis when the S-wave dominates.

## Key numbers

- The double spin correlation in the \(dd\to pnpn\) reaction is related to that in elastic \(NN\) scattering by \(A_{YY}^{dd} = \frac{4}{9}A_{YY}^{NN}\).
- For polarized deuteron beams, the vector polarization is \(P_y = +\frac{2}{3}\) for spin-up and \(P_y = -\frac{2}{3}\) for spin-down, with tensor polarization \(P_{YY}=0\).
- The differential cross section of elastic \(pp\) scattering at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5\) GeV and \(\theta_{cm}=90^\circ\) is \(\sim 10^{-2}\ \mu b/sr\).
- At luminosity \(\sim 10^{29}\ cm^{-2}sec^{-1}\) in \(pp\)-collision, the counting rate is \(N\sim 10^{-3}/sec\) at \(\theta_{cm}=90^\circ\), increasing by two orders of magnitude at \(\theta_{cm}=50^\circ\).

## Context

This work extends the study of unexpectedly large double spin correlations observed in elastic \(pp\) scattering at large angles (Crabb 1978, Bhatia 1982, Lin 1978, Crosbie 1980, Court 1986) to the \(pn\) channel, where data are scarce (only two points at 6 GeV/c from Crabb 1979). The proposed measurement at NICA SPD could test the octoquark resonance hypothesis of Brodsky (1987) and distinguish it from alternative explanations such as the nuclear filter mechanism of Ralston (1988). The relation derived enables extraction of \(pn\) spin correlations from the experimentally more accessible \(dd\to pnpn\) reaction.
