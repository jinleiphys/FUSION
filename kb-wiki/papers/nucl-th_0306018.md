# Quadratic momentum dependence in the nucleon-nucleon interaction

> We investigate different choices for the quadratic momentum dependence required in nucleon-nucleon potentials to fit phase shifts in high partial-waves. In the Argonne v18 potential L**2 and (L.S)**2 operators are used to represent this dependence. The v18 potential is simple to use in many-body calculations since it has no quadratic momentum-dependent terms in S-waves. However, p**2 rather than L**2 dependence occurs naturally in meson-exchange models of nuclear forces. We construct an alternate version of the Argonne potential, designated Argonne v18pq, in which the L**2 and (L.S)**2 operators are replaced by p**2 and Qij operators, respectively. The quadratic momentum-dependent terms are smaller in the v18pq than in the v18 interaction. Results for the ground state binding energies of 3H, 3He, and 4He, obtained with the variational Monte Carlo method, are presented for both the models with and without three-nucleon interactions. We find that the nuclear wave functions obtained with the v18pq are slightly larger than those with v18 at interparticle distances < 1 fm. The two models provide essentially the same binding in the light nuclei, although the v18pq gains less attraction when a fixed three-nucleon potential is added.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether replacing the ${\bf L}^2$ and $({\bf L\cdot S})^2$ operators in the Argonne $v_{18}$ potential with ${\bf p}^2$ and $Q_{ij}$ operators (producing the Argonne $v_{18pq}$ model) yields a phase-equivalent interaction, and finds that while the two models give essentially the same binding in light nuclei, the $v_{18pq}$ gains less attraction when a fixed three-nucleon potential is added, implying that the choice of quadratic momentum dependence matters for many-body calculations.

## Method

The authors constructed a variant of the Argonne $v_{18}$ potential, designated Argonne $v_{18pq}$, by replacing the ${\bf L}^2$ operator with ${\bf p}^2$ and the $({\bf L\cdot S})^2$ operator with $Q_{ij}$, then refitting the strength parameters to reproduce the $v_{18}$ phase shifts as closely as possible. Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) calculations were performed for the ground states of $^3$H, $^3$He, and $^4$He using both the $v_{18}$ and $v_{18pq}$ potentials, with and without the addition of three-nucleon interactions (Urbana IX and TM$^\prime$).

## Key numbers

- The ${\bf p}^2$ dependent terms in AV18pq are smaller than the corresponding ${\bf L}^2$ terms in AV18q in all cases; for example, the net contribution of quadratic momentum-dependent terms in the deuteron is $\langle v^{p2} + v^{q} \rangle = 0.086$ MeV for AV18pq versus $\langle v^{l2} + v^{q} \rangle = -0.331$ MeV for AV18q.
- In the absence of three-nucleon interactions, the quadratic momentum-dependent terms reduce binding in $A=3$ and $4$ nuclei by approximately 2.5% and 4% respectively, with nearly the same reduction for AV18 and AV18pq.
- When a three-nucleon interaction is added, the effect of quadratic momentum dependence grows to approximately 3% and 5% for $A=3$ and $4$ with AV18, and approximately 4% and 6% with AV18pq.
- The rms deviations of AV18pq phases from AV18 phases for $pp$ scattering are generally smaller than the experimental uncertainty from PWA93, except in $^3$P$_2$, $^3$F$_4$, and higher $T=1$ phases, and in $^3$S$_1$, $^3$D$_3$, and $^3$G$_4$ $T=0$ phases, where deviations are concentrated at higher energies.
- The deuteron binding energy and other observables are virtually identical between AV18q and AV18pq; the total kinetic and potential energies obtained with AV18pq are smaller by about 1%.

## Context

This work extends the line of realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials that fit scattering data, specifically the Argonne $v_{18}$ potential of Wiringa, Stoks, and Schiavilla (1995), by exploring an alternative operator representation motivated by meson-exchange models such as the Paris potential (1980) and the Nijmegen potentials (1994). The study enables future comparisons of deuteron form factors and current operators between the two representations, and highlights that the choice of quadratic momentum dependence affects the strength of three-nucleon interactions needed to reproduce experimental binding energies, as previously seen in the Bochum Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations for the Nijmegen potentials (Nogga, Kamada, and Gloeckle, 2000).
