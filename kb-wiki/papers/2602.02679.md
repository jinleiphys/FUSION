# Exploring Thermalization and Multi-Freeze-Out Effects in Pb-Pb collisions Based on Tsallis pT Distributions

> This study investigates transverse-momentum (pT) distributions of pi-, pi+, K-, K+, p, pbar, K0s, and Lambda in several centrality classes of Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(sNN) = 2.76 TeV. The measured spectra are analyzed with the Tsallis non-extensive distribution, from which the effective temperature T, non-extensive parameter q, and the mean transverse momentum mean_pT are extracted for each particle species and centrality interval. To disentangle thermal and collective effects, the mean kinetic freeze-out temperature T0 is obtained from the intercept of the T-versus-mass relation, while the average transverse flow velocity betaT is extracted from the slope of mean_pT versus the mean moving mass for pions, kaons, and protons. The results show that T increases and q decreases with increasing centrality, indicating a hotter and more equilibrated system in central collisions. A clear mass dependence of T supports a multi-freeze-out scenario, with heavier particles decoupling earlier. Both T0 and betaT rise from peripheral to mid-central collisions before saturating toward central events, which may suggest the onset of collective behavior or changes in freeze-out dynamics. These observations provide new insights into the thermal and dynamical properties of the medium created in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim
The Tsallis non-extensive distribution provides an excellent description of transverse-momentum spectra for pions, kaons, protons, and strange hadrons across all centrality classes in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, but the effective temperature T extracted from these fits shows a clear mass dependence that supports a multi-freeze-out scenario where heavier particles decouple earlier, rather than a single common freeze-out temperature for all species.

## Method
The authors fitted published ALICE mid-rapidity pT spectra of pi+, pi-, K+, K-, p, pbar, K0s, and Lambda across ten centrality intervals (0-5% to 80-90%) using the thermodynamically consistent Tsallis distribution (Eq. 2). From these fits, they extracted the effective temperature T, non-extensivity parameter q, and mean transverse momentum <pT> for each particle species and centrality bin. To separate thermal and collective effects, they performed two independent linear fits: T versus particle rest mass m0 to extract the kinetic freeze-out temperature T0 from the intercept, and <pT> versus mean moving mass to extract the average transverse flow velocity <betaT> from the slope.

## Key numbers
- For central 0-5% collisions: T ≈ 0.12 GeV for pions, T ≈ 0.42 GeV for protons and Lambda hyperons; q ≈ 1.05-1.08 across particle species
- For peripheral 80-90% collisions: T ≈ 0.07 GeV for pions, T ≈ 0.14-0.15 GeV for protons; q ≈ 1.16-1.20 for pions
- For K+ in central 0-5%: T = 0.2459 ± 0.0024 GeV, q = 1.0412 ± 0.0020, <pT> = 0.4738 ± 0.0201 GeV
- For Lambda in central 0-5%: T = 0.4229 ± 0.0074 GeV, q = 1.0012 ± 0.0029, <pT> = 0.8111 ± 0.0405 GeV
- For pi+ in central 0-5%: T = 0.1173 ± 0.0044 GeV, q = 1.1180 ± 0.0047, <pT> = 0.2513 ± 0.0133 GeV
- For pi+ in peripheral 80-90%: T = 0.0723 ± 0.0019 GeV, q = 1.1607 ± 0.0027, <pT> = 0.1634 ± 0.0125 GeV

## Context
This work extends Tsallis distribution analyses previously applied at RHIC and LHC energies (Cleymans 2012, Yang 2021) to a comprehensive multi-species study in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(sNN) = 2.76 TeV. The mass-dependent effective temperatures support the "differential freeze-out" concept discussed by Badshah (2023) and Ajaz, where each hadron species can have its own kinetic freeze-out temperature. The results enable more detailed comparisons with hydrodynamic models (hydroModels) and provide empirical constraints for understanding the approach to thermal equilibrium in different collision centralities, particularly the saturation of T0 and betaT toward central events that may indicate the onset of collective behavior.
