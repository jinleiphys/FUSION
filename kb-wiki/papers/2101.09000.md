# Transport properties of superfluid phonons in neutron stars

> We review the effective field theory associated with the superfluid phonons that we use for the study of transport properties in the core of superfluid neutrons stars in their low temperature regime. We then discuss the shear and bulk viscosities together with the thermal conductivity coming from the collisions of superfluid phonons in neutron stars. With regard to shear, bulk, and thermal transport coefficients, the phonon collisional processes are obtained in terms of the equation of state and the superfluid gap. We compare the shear coefficient due to the interaction among superfluid phonons with other dominant processes in neutron stars, such as electron collisions. We also analyze the possible consequences for the r-mode instability in neutron stars. As for the bulk viscosities, we determine that phonon collisions contribute decisively to the bulk viscosities inside neutron stars. For the thermal conductivity resulting from phonon collisions, we find that it is temperature independent well below the transition temperature. We also obtain that the thermal conductivity due to superfluid phonons dominates over the one resulting from electron-muon interactions once phonons are in the hydrodynamic regime. As the phonons couple to the $Z$ electroweak gauge boson, we estimate the associated neutrino emissivity. We also briefly comment on how the superfluid phonon interactions are modified in the presence of a gravitational field or in a moving background.

## Key claim
This review argues that superfluid phonon collisions provide the dominant contribution to shear and bulk viscosities and thermal conductivity in the low-temperature core of neutron stars, but only under the condition that the phonons are in the hydrodynamic regime; the shear viscosity from phonons is compared with electron collisions and found to be relevant for the r-mode instability.

## Method
The authors employ an effective field theory (EFT) for superfluid phonons, deriving the phonon interaction vertices from the equation of state and the superfluid gap. They then compute the shear viscosity, bulk viscosities, and thermal conductivity by solving the Boltzmann equation for phonon-phonon collisions in the low-temperature limit. The neutrino emissivity from phonon coupling to the Z electroweak gauge boson is also estimated.

## Key numbers
- The thermal conductivity from superfluid phonon collisions is found to be temperature independent well below the transition temperature.
- The shear viscosity coefficient due to phonon interactions is compared with that from electron collisions, with the phonon contribution becoming dominant in the hydrodynamic regime.
- The neutrino emissivity from phonon-Z boson coupling is estimated, though no explicit numerical value is given in the abstract or truncated text.

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory approach to superfluid phonons previously applied to neutron star transport (e.g., by Manuel and Tolos in earlier studies) and provides a systematic comparison with electron-muon scattering processes. The results enable more precise modeling of the r-mode instability in rotating neutron stars and can be used to refine cooling simulations by incorporating phonon-mediated neutrino emission. The paper also notes that phonon interactions are modified in a gravitational field or moving background, which opens the door for future studies of transport in rapidly rotating or accreting neutron stars.
