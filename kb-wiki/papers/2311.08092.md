# Light Nuclei Production in Au+Au Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 3 GeV within Thermodynamical Approach: Bulk Properties and Collective Flow

> We present results of simulations of light-nuclei production in Au+Au collisions at collision energy of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 3 GeV within updated Three-fluid Hydrodynamics-based Event Simulator Extended by UrQMD (Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) final State interactions (THESEUS). The results are compared with recent STAR data. The light-nuclei production is treated within the thermodynamical approach on equal basis with hadrons. The only additional parameter related to the light nuclei is the energy density of late freeze-out that imitates afterburner stage of the collision because the light nuclei do not participate in the UrQMD evolution. It is found that the late freeze-out is preferable for deuterons, tritons, and $^3$He. Remarkably, the $^4$He observables are better reproduced with the standard freeze-out. This suggests that the $^4$He nuclei better survive in the afterburner stage because they are more spatially compact and tightly bound objects. This is an argument in favor of dynamical treatment of light nuclei. The simulations indicate that the collision dynamics is determined by the hadronic phase. The calculated results reveal not perfect but a good reproduction of the data on bulk observables and directed flow. The elliptic flow turns out to be more intricate.

## Key claim

The thermodynamical approach within the updated THESEUS event generator reproduces STAR data on bulk observables and directed flow of light nuclei in Au+Au collisions at 3 GeV, but the elliptic flow is more intricate; the key condition is that a late freeze-out (imitating the afterburner stage) is required for deuterons, tritons, and 3He, while 4He observables are better reproduced with the standard freeze-out, suggesting that 4He nuclei better survive the afterburner due to being more compact and tightly bound.

## Method

The authors used the Three-fluid Hydrodynamics-based Event Simulator Extended by UrQMD final State interactions (THESEUS), which combines three-fluid dynamics (3FD) with the UrQMD afterburner. Light nuclei were treated thermodynamically on equal basis with hadrons, using temperature and chemical potential fields from the 3FD output, with the only additional parameter being the energy density of late freeze-out for light nuclei (which do not participate in the UrQMD evolution). The baryon chemical potentials were recalculated to conserve local baryon number in the system extended by light nuclei species including deuterons, tritons, 3He, 4He, and low-lying 4He resonances.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 3 GeV were simulated and compared with recent STAR data
- The light-nuclei list includes stable nuclei (deuterons, tritons, 3He, 4He) and 4He resonances with excitation energies from 20.21 MeV to 29.89 MeV, with branching ratios for decay into protons, neutrons, and deuterons
- The calculated results show "not perfect but a good reproduction" of data on bulk observables and directed flow, while elliptic flow "turns out to be more intricate"

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous thermodynamic treatment of light nuclei in THESEUS (Kozhevnikova and Ivanov, 2023) from the collision energy range of 6.4–19.6 GeV down to 3 GeV, where light nuclei yield plays a noticeable role in the total baryon charge balance. The results are compared with recent STAR data (STAR, 2023) and with coalescence-based simulations from JAM, SMASH, UrQMD, PHQMD, and the hybrid dynamical-statistical approach (Buyukcizmeci et al., 2023). The finding that 4He behaves differently from lighter nuclei provides an argument for dynamical treatment of light nuclei and enables further refinement of afterburner modeling.
