# The loop corrections to the parity-violating electron-proton elastic scattering

> We calculate the two-boson-exchange (TBE) corrections to the parity asymmetry of the elastic electron-proton scattering in a model using the formalism of generalized parton distributions (GPDs).

## Key claim
The two-boson-exchange (TBE) corrections to the parity-violating asymmetry in elastic electron-proton scattering are calculated using a parton model with generalized parton distributions (GPDs), finding that these corrections can reach several percent in certain kinematics and are sensitive to \(Q^2\), with the \(\gamma\)-\(Z\) exchange effect showing very mild \(\epsilon\) dependence.

## Method
The authors compute the two-photon-exchange (TPE) and \(\gamma\)-\(Z\) exchange corrections at the quark level using the handbag approximation, then embed the quarks in the proton via GPDs for \(u\) and \(d\) quarks (excluding strange quarks due to small contributions). The hard part of the \(\gamma\)-\(Z\) exchange amplitude is isolated from infrared-divergent soft parts, and the parity-violating \(T\)-matrix is expressed as a convolution of the electron-quark hard scattering with nucleon GPDs, following the unfactorized model of Chen (2004). The corrections are evaluated for \(Q^2\) values of 1.0, 2.0, 5.0, and 9.0 GeV\(^2\) as functions of the kinematic variable \(\epsilon\).

## Key numbers
- The parity-violating asymmetry \(A_{PV}\) at tree level ranges from 0.1 to 100 ppm, as measured by the SAMPLE, HAPPEX, A4, and G0 experiments.
- The TPE correction to \(A_{PV}\) can reach several percent in certain kinematics, comparable in size to existing measurements of strange-quark effects.
- The full TBE corrections (TPE + \(\gamma\)-\(Z\) exchange) are shown for \(Q^2 = 1.0\), 2.0, 5.0, and 9.0 GeV\(^2\) in Fig. 2, with the \(\gamma\)-\(Z\) exchange effect having very mild \(\epsilon\) dependence.
- The sum of two-\(Z\)-boson-exchange (TZE) and two-\(W\)-boson-exchange (TWE) effects is about the same magnitude as TPE and \(\gamma\)-\(Z\) exchange effects, and increases when \(Q^2\) decreases.

## Context
This work extends the parton-model calculation of TPE corrections to parity-violating electron-proton scattering by Afanasev (2005) and the hadronic model calculation by Zhou (2007), which neglected excited intermediate states and used on-shell nucleon form factors. The authors find that the \(\gamma\)-\(Z\) exchange effect is not overestimated in the zero-momentum-transfer approximation, contrary to the claim by Zhou (2007), and that two-\(Z/W\)-boson-exchange effects are non-negligible in the partonic calculation. These TBE corrections are important for extracting the strange-quark content of the nucleon from parity-violating asymmetry measurements, though the impact on strangeness extraction requires further study.
