# On the Threshold Resummation in Forward pA Collisions

> In this paper, using the Higgs production in forward rapidity region in proton-nucleus collisions as an example, we demonstrate that we can construct a systematic formalism for the threshold resummation for forward rapidity particle productions in the saturation formalism. The forward threshold jet function, which satisfies the corresponding renormalization group equation, is introduced into the new factorization formula. This calculation can be easily generalized to other processes, such as single forward hadron productions at forward rapidity region, and have important phenomenological implications.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a systematic formalism for threshold resummation can be constructed for forward rapidity particle productions in the saturation formalism, using Higgs production in proton-nucleus collisions as an example. The key hedge is that the formalism is derived in the limit where the Higgs mass \(M\) is much greater than the measured transverse momentum \(k_\perp\), and the leading-power contributions come from a subset of diagrams while others are suppressed by factors of \(k_\perp/M \ll 1\).

## Method

The authors work in the dilute-dense factorization framework, using collinear parton distribution functions (PDFs) for the proton projectile and small-\(x\) Weizsäcker-Williams (WW) gluon distributions for the nucleus target. They compute the one-loop correction to the Higgs production cross section in the forward rapidity region, identify the threshold logarithms proportional to \(\alpha_s \ln(1-x_p)\) that arise from plus-distributions in the gluon splitting function, and perform the resummation in Mellin space. The resummed result is expressed via a new "forward threshold jet function" \(\Delta(\mu^2, \mu_b^2, \ln(x/\tau))\) that satisfies a renormalization group equation similar to that for jet functions in soft-collinear effective theory (SCET).

## Key numbers

- The one-loop correction contains terms \(\frac{\alpha_s N_c}{\pi} \left[-\frac{1}{2}\ln^2 \frac{M^2}{\mu_b^2} + \beta_0 \ln \frac{M^2}{\mu_b^2} + \frac{\pi^2}{2}\right]\) (Sudakov-type logarithms) plus a term \(\frac{\alpha_s N_c}{\pi} \ln \frac{\mu_b^2}{\mu^2} \int_{\tau}^1 \frac{d\xi}{\xi} \mathcal{P}_{gg}(\xi) g(\tau/\xi, \mu)\) that generates threshold logarithms.
- The anomalous dimension parameter \(\gamma_{\mu, b_\perp} \equiv N_c \int_{\mu^2}^{\mu_b^2} \frac{d \mu^{\prime 2}}{\mu^{\prime 2}} \frac{\alpha_s(\mu^{\prime 2})}{\pi}\) controls the threshold resummation; when \(\gamma_{\mu, b_\perp} > 0\), the forward threshold jet function takes the form \(\Delta = \frac{e^{(\beta_0-\gamma_E) \gamma_{\mu, b_\perp}}}{\Gamma[\gamma_{\mu, b_\perp}]} z^{\gamma_{\mu, b_\perp} - 1}\) with \(z = \ln(x/\tau)\).
- Numerical results using MSTW gluon PDFs show that the ratio \(g_{\text{t}}(\tau, \mu_b)/g(\tau, \mu_b)\) deviates by less than \(10\%\) for \(\tau = 0.5, 0.7, 0.85\) over the range \(\mu_b^2 = 1\ \text{GeV}^2\) to \(1000\ \text{GeV}^2\), with \(\mu^2\) varied from \(1\ \text{GeV}^2\) to \(25\ \text{GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work extends the threshold resummation formalism originally developed by Sterman (1986) and Catani and Trentadue (1989) for Drell-Yan processes to the forward rapidity region in proton-nucleus collisions within the saturation formalism. It addresses the negativity problem in NLO single hadron production cross sections observed by Stasto et al. (2013), where a term proportional to \(\alpha_s \ln(1-x_p)\) was identified as a key source. The formalism enables joint resummation of small-\(x\) logarithms (via the WW gluon distribution) and threshold logarithms, which is particularly relevant for ultra-forward rapidity measurements by LHCf (Adriani et al., 2015) and for improving phenomenological predictions in the large-\(x\) regime where PDFs have large uncertainties.
