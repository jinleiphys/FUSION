# The quark orbital angular momentum from Wigner distributions and light-cone wave functions

> We investigate the quark orbital angular momentum of the nucleon in the absence of gauge-field degrees of freedom, by using the concept of the Wigner distribution and the light-cone wave functions of the Fock state expansion of the nucleon. The quark orbital angular momentum is obtained from the phase-space average of the orbital angular momentum operator weighted with the Wigner distribution of unpolarized quarks in a longitudinally polarized nucleon. We also derive the light-cone wave function representation of the orbital angular momentum. In particular, we perform an expansion in the nucleon Fock state space and decompose the orbital angular momentum into the $N$-parton state contributions. Explicit expressions are presented in terms of the light-cone wave functions of the three-quark Fock state. Numerical results for the up and down quark orbital angular momenta of the proton are shown in the light-cone constituent quark model and the light-cone chiral quark-soliton model.

## Key claim

The quark orbital angular momentum (OAM) of the nucleon can be obtained from the phase-space average of the orbital angular momentum operator weighted with the Wigner distribution of unpolarized quarks in a longitudinally polarized nucleon, but only in the absence of gauge-field degrees of freedom; the extension to include gauge fields is left for a further study.

## Method

The authors derived the quark OAM from Wigner distributions and expressed it equivalently in terms of generalized transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (GTMDs). They then obtained an overlap representation of the OAM using light-cone wave functions (LCWFs) of the nucleon Fock state expansion, performing a partial-wave decomposition of the three-quark Fock state into components with total OAM eigenvalues \(l_z = 0, \pm 1, 2\). Numerical results were computed in two light-cone quark models: the light-cone constituent quark model (LCCQM) and the light-cone chiral quark-soliton model (LC\(\chi\)QSM), restricted to the three-quark sector.

## Key numbers

*   In the LCCQM, the total quark OAM of the proton is \(\sum_q \ell_z^q = 0.126\), with up-quark contribution \(\ell_z^u = 0.131\) and down-quark contribution \(\ell_z^d = -0.005\).
*   In the LC\(\chi\)QSM, the total quark OAM of the proton is \(\sum_q \ell_z^q = 0.069\), with up-quark contribution \(\ell_z^u = 0.073\) and down-quark contribution \(\ell_z^d = -0.004\).
*   The total contribution from the \(l_z = 0\) partial wave to the OAM is zero: \(\ell_z^{l_z=0} = 0\).
*   The \(l_z = 1\) partial wave contributes \(\ell_z^{l_z=1} = \rho_{l_z=1}\) and the \(l_z = -1\) partial wave contributes \(\ell_z^{l_z=-1} = -\rho_{l_z=-1}\), where \(\rho_{l_z}\) is the probability to find the nucleon in that partial wave.
*   The \(l_z = 2\) partial wave contributes \(\ell_z^{l_z=2} = 2\,\rho_{l_z=2}\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Wigner distributions developed by Ji (2003), Belitsky (2003), and Lorce (2011) to the specific case of quark orbital angular momentum, providing a connection to the light-cone wave function representation used by Diehl (2000) and Brodsky (2000). The numerical results from the LCCQM and LC\(\chi\)QSM, which had previously been applied to parton distribution functions with typical accuracy of about 30% in the valence region, show that the net quark OAM is positive and larger in the LCCQM than in the LC\(\chi\)QSM. This work enables future studies that include gauge-field degrees of freedom to obtain a complete picture of the nucleon spin decomposition.
