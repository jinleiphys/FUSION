# Thermal width of the Higgs boson in hot QCD matter

> Following Caron-Huot and combining results for the thermal dependence of spectral functions at large time-like momenta, we write an explicit expression for the thermal width of the Higgs boson to $\mathcal{O}(α_\mathrm{s})$ for $T \ll M_H$. It is an $\mathcal{O}( α_\mathrm{s} (T/M_H)^4 )$ correction for $H\to gg$ and $H\to q\bar{q}$. We also compile corresponding results for the thermal width of the $Z$-boson, and we recall which generic structures of the field theory, accessible via the operator product expansion, fix the $T/M$-dependence of the decay of heavy particles.

## Key claim

The thermal correction to the Higgs boson width in a QCD plasma is an O(α_s (T/M_H)^4) correction to the vacuum branching ratios for H→gg and H→q\bar{q}, valid only for temperatures T ≪ M_H and for Higgs bosons at rest or with moderate three-momentum such that the light-cone momentum k^- ≫ T.

## Method

The authors use the operator product expansion (OPE) approach of Caron-Huot (2009) to determine thermal corrections to spectral functions at large time-like momenta, exploiting the hierarchy M_H ≫ T to separate short-distance and long-distance physics. They express the Higgs decay widths in terms of spectral functions of the scalar operator S = m_q ψ̄_q ψ_q for H→q\bar{q} and of the gluonic operator O_Hg = -1/4 F^a_{μν} F^{a μν} for H→gg, then insert known thermal corrections to these spectral functions derived from the OPE of the bulk viscous channel and the scalar channel. The temperature dependence enters via thermal expectation values of the quark and gluon energy-momentum tensor components, evaluated in the Stefan-Boltzmann limit.

## Key numbers

- δΓ_{H→gg} = - Γ_{H→gg}^vac × α_s × (T^4/M_H^4) × (112 π^3/45) × (8 - n_f^T), for Higgs decay in the plasma rest frame, where n_f^T is the effective number of approximately massless flavors (3 < n_f^T < 4 for temperatures above the strange quark mass and below the bottom charm mass)
- δΓ_{H→b\bar{b}} = - Γ_{H→b\bar{b}}^vac × α_s × (T^4/M_H^4) × (128 π^3/135), for Higgs decay in the plasma rest frame and T ≪ m_b
- The vacuum width Γ_{H→gg}^vac = α_s^2 M_H^3 / (72 π^3 v^2) + O(α_s^3), where v ≅ 246 GeV
- The vacuum width Γ_{H→q\bar{q}}^vac = d_F m_q^2 M_H / (8π v^2) for each mass state
- The Stefan-Boltzmann limits: ⟨T^{00}_g⟩ = π^2 T^4 d_A / 15 and ⟨T^{00}_{fq}⟩|_{m_q=0} = 7 π^2 T^4 d_F / 60, where d_A = N_c^2 - 1 = 8 and d_F = N_c = 3

## Context

This work extends the OPE approach of Caron-Huot (2009) for thermal corrections to spectral functions at large time-like momenta, applying it to the Higgs boson for the first time. It provides explicit expressions that were missing from the literature when discussing thermal corrections to Higgs branching ratios at future multi-TeV heavy ion collider experiments, as discussed by Berger (2018), Citron (2018), and d'Enterria (2018). The results enable quantitative estimates of whether thermal modifications to Higgs decays could be observable in heavy ion collisions, and the methodology can be applied to other heavy particles such as the Z-boson, for which the authors compile corresponding results.
