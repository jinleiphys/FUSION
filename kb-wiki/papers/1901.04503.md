# Thermal width of the Higgs boson in hot QCD matter

> Following Caron-Huot and combining results for the thermal dependence of spectral functions at large time-like momenta, we write an explicit expression for the thermal width of the Higgs boson to $\mathcal{O}(╬▒_\mathrm{s})$ for $T \ll M_H$. It is an $\mathcal{O}( ╬▒_\mathrm{s} (T/M_H)^4 )$ correction for $H\to gg$ and $H\to q\bar{q}$. We also compile corresponding results for the thermal width of the $Z$-boson, and we recall which generic structures of the field theory, accessible via the operator product expansion, fix the $T/M$-dependence of the decay of heavy particles.

## Context

This work extends the OPE approach of Caron-Huot (2009) for thermal corrections to spectral functions at large time-like momenta, applying it to the Higgs boson for the first time. It provides explicit expressions that were missing from the literature when discussing thermal corrections to Higgs branching ratios at future multi-TeV heavy ion collider experiments, as discussed by Berger (2018), Citron (2018), and d'Enterria (2018). The results enable quantitative estimates of whether thermal modifications to Higgs decays could be observable in heavy ion collisions, and the methodology can be applied to other heavy particles such as the Z-boson, for which the authors compile corresponding results.
