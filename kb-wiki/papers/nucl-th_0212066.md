# Tagging the p n -> d phi reaction by backward protons in p d -> d phi p_{sp} processes

> The reaction p d -> d phi p_{sp} is studied within the Bethe-Salpeter formalism. Under special kinematical conditions (slow backward spectator proton p_{sp} and fast forward deuteron) relevant for forthcoming experiments at COSY, the cross section and a set of polarization observables factorize in the contribution of the pure subprocess p n -> d phi and a contribution stemming from deuteron quantities and kinematical factors. This provides a theoretical basis for studying threshold-near processes at quasi-free neutrons.

# Tagging the p n → d φ reaction by backward protons in p d → d φ p_sp processes

## Key claim

Under special kinematical conditions (slow backward spectator proton with momentum below 100 MeV and fast forward deuteron), the cross section and polarization observables of the complete reaction p d → d φ p_sp approximately factorize into the pure subprocess p n → d φ multiplied by a factor depending on deuteron quantities and kinematics, with deviations less than 0.5% from exact calculations.

## Method

The authors employ a covariant Bethe-Salpeter formalism to compute the fivefold differential cross section and polarization observables for p d → d φ p_sp. The amplitude is expressed in terms of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude of the deuteron and the truncated amplitude for the subprocess p n → d φ, with the latter calculated using an effective one-boson exchange model with parameter set "B" from Titov (2002). Numerical evaluations use Bethe-Salpeter deuteron wave functions, with Paris and Bonn wave functions also considered for comparison, under kinematical conditions corresponding to COSY Proposal #75.

## Key numbers

- Kinetic energy of the initial proton: T_p = 2.69 GeV (maximum achievable at COSY is T_p ≤ 2.7 GeV)
- Maximum differential cross section occurs at deuteron momentum p_d' ≈ 2.35 GeV/c, corresponding to spectator proton momentum p_p' ≈ 0.035 GeV/c
- Excess energy in the p n → d φ subprocess at the cross section maximum: Δs^{1/2} ≈ 4 MeV (very near threshold)
- Spectator proton detected with momentum range p_p' ≈ 0 – 0.04 GeV/c
- Spectator proton detected in backward hemisphere with θ_p' ≈ 120° – 140°
- Forward deuteron detected with momentum range p_d' ≈ 1.9 – 3 GeV/c
- Asymmetry A approaches -1 at the cross section maximum
- Tensor analyzing power T_20 predicted to be 1/√2 from selection rules
- Difference between exact and factorized calculations less than 0.5% in the whole kinematical region of interest

## Context

This work extends the spectator technique developed by Johansson (2002) and Anke (2002) for isolating quasi-free reactions at neutrons in deuteron targets. It provides a theoretical foundation for studying threshold-near p n → d φ reactions at COSY, building on earlier model calculations by Titov (2002) for φ meson production and Nakayama (2002) for p n → d M reactions with M = η, η', ω, φ, a_0⁺. The factorization derived enables direct extraction of the elementary p n → d φ cross section from the more experimentally accessible p d → d φ p_sp reaction, and the authors suggest extension to the twice-tagged reaction d d → n n M p_sp1 p_sp2 for studying quasi-free n n reactions.
