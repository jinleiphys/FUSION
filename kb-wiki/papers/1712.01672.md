# Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory combined with the ${\mathbf{1/N_c}}$ Expansion in SU(3) I: Framework

> Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory combined with the $1/N_c$ expansion is implemented for three flavors. Baryon masses, vector charges and axial vector couplings are studied to one-loop and organized according to the $ξ$-expansion, in which the $1/N_c$ and the low energy power countings are linked according to $1/N_c={\cal{O}}(ξ)={\cal{O}}(p)$. The renormalization to ${\cal{O}}(ξ^3)$ necessary for the mentioned observables is provided, along with applications to the baryon masses and axial couplings as obtained in lattice QCD calculations.

# Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory combined with the 1/N_c Expansion in SU(3) I: Framework

## Key claim
The paper implements a combined framework of Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) and the 1/N_c expansion for three flavors, where the two expansions are linked by counting 1/N_c as order p (the "xi-expansion"), and provides the renormalization to order xi^3 necessary for baryon masses, vector charges, and axial-vector couplings. The abstract glosses over the fact that the non-commutativity of the 1/N_c and chiral limits forces this specific linking choice, and that the framework is applied to lattice QCD results only for masses and axial couplings, not for vector charges.

## Method
The authors construct the effective Lagrangian for ground-state baryons using HBChPT combined with the 1/N_c expansion, organizing terms by the xi-expansion where 1/N_c = O(xi) = O(p). They build spin-flavor tensor operators using SU(6) generators, implement chiral symmetry via non-linear realizations, and compute one-loop corrections to baryon self-energies, vector currents, and axial-vector currents to O(xi^3). The renormalization is carried out by determining beta functions for the low-energy constants (LECs) that absorb ultraviolet divergences, and the framework is applied to lattice QCD data for baryon masses and axial couplings.

## Key numbers
- The hyperfine splitting LEC C_HF is equal to the Delta-N mass difference M_Delta - M_N in the real world N_c = 3 (from LO Lagrangian).
- The Gell-Mann-Okubo (GMO) deviation is predicted as: Delta_GMO = (g_A^N/F_pi)^2 x 2.42 x 10^5 MeV^3, compared to the experimental value of 25.8 MeV.
- The Equal Spacing (ES) deviation for decuplet baryons is predicted as: Delta_ES = (g_A^N/F_pi)^2 x (-3.72 x 10^4) MeV^3, compared to the experimental value of -4 +/- 7 MeV.
- The one-loop self-energy contribution to baryon masses is O(xi^3) in the xi-expansion, with the loop integral involving Goldstone boson masses M_pi, M_K, and M_eta (where M_eta^2 = (4 M_K^2 - M_pi^2)/3).
- The axial coupling in the chiral and large N_c limits, denoted mathring{g}_A, is related to the nucleon axial coupling by g_A^N = (5/6) mathring{g}_A.

## Context
This work extends the combined HBChPT x 1/N_c framework from two flavors (Calle Cordon and Goity, 2012; Cordon and Goity, 2013) to three flavors (SU(3)), following the original formulation by Jenkins (1995) and subsequent developments by Flores-Mendieta (1998, 2006). The framework addresses the convergence issues in baryon ChPT that arise from the small mass gap between spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 baryons, which is naturally explained by the large N_c spin-flavor symmetry of 't Hooft (1973) and Dashen (1993, 1994). The results enable systematic analysis of lattice QCD calculations for baryon observables at varying quark masses, including the determination of low-energy constants and tests of the effective theory's range of validity.
