# Feasibility Study of Observing eta' Mesic Nuclei with (p,d) Reaction

> A novel method is proposed to measure eta'(958) meson bound states in 11C nuclei by missing mass spectroscopy of the 12C(p,d) reaction near the eta' production threshold. It is shown that peak structures will be observed experimentally in an inclusive measurement in case that the in-medium eta' mass reduction is sufficiently large and that the decay width of eta' mesic states is narrow enough. Such a measurement will be feasible with the intense proton beam supplied by the SIS synchrotron at GSI combined with the good energy resolution of the fragment separator FRS.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that peak structures from eta' mesic nuclei formed in the 12C(p,d) reaction can be observed experimentally in an inclusive measurement, but only if the in-medium eta' mass reduction is sufficiently large and the decay width of eta' mesic states is narrow enough; the feasibility depends critically on these conditions being met.

## Method

The authors performed a feasibility study using the Green's function method within the impulse approximation to calculate formation spectra of eta' mesic nuclei via the (p,d) reaction on a 12C target at an incident proton kinetic energy of 2.50 GeV. They simulated the expected missing-mass spectrum including both the signal from eta' bound states and background contributions from quasi-free processes such as pp to dX and pn to dX, using the fragment separator FRS at GSI-SIS as a spectrometer with an estimated overall resolution of 1.6 MeV (sigma).

## Key numbers

* The incident proton kinetic energy is 2.50 GeV, slightly above the threshold for the elementary pn to d eta' process (around 2.40 GeV).
* The estimated differential cross section for the elementary pn to d eta' reaction at zero degrees in the laboratory frame is approximately 30 microbarn/sr.
* The overall spectral resolution is estimated to be 1.6 MeV (sigma), with contributions from beam momentum spread (0.40 MeV), energy loss in target (1.3 MeV), and spectrometer (0.77 MeV).
* The signal-to-noise ratio is estimated to be of the order of 1/100 when the experimental resolution is negligibly small compared to the decay width.
* The simulation corresponds to 3.24 x 10^14 protons on a 4 g/cm^2-thick 12C target.
* The background level from quasi-free processes is approximately 1 microbarn/(sr MeV), about two orders of magnitude larger than the peak from eta' mesic nuclei.
* The cross section for the elementary pn to d eta' reaction is estimated to be around 3 microbarn, based on the pp to pp eta' cross section of about 0.2 microbarn from COSY-11 and the ratio from CELSIUS/WASA data for eta production.

## Context

This work extends theoretical predictions of eta' mesic nuclei formation by Nagahiro and Hirenzaki (2005) and Jido, Nagahiro, and Hirenzaki (2012), proposing a specific experimental method using the (p,d) reaction at GSI-SIS. The study is motivated by the need to understand the UA(1) anomaly and eta' mass generation mechanism, where in-medium mass reduction of the eta' meson is expected from partial restoration of chiral symmetry. The proposed measurement would complement existing constraints from the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration on eta' absorption widths (15-25 MeV at saturation density) and from COSY-11 on the eta'-proton scattering length, and could test predictions from Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model calculations suggesting 150-250 MeV mass reduction at saturation density.
