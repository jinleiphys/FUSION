# Mass number and excitation energy dependence of the $Θ_{eff}/Θ_{rig}$ parameter of the spin cut-off factor in the formation of an isomeric pair

> The $Θ_{eff}/Θ_{rig}$ parameter of the spin distribution of the level density was determined for 61 nuclei covering the mass range of 44 to 200. The experimental isomeric cross-section ratios for 25 isomeric pairs were compared with the model calculation to find the best fit to the experimental data. The model calculations were carried out with the TALYS code using the BSFG model for the level density. The reduced $χ^2$ values were calculated to describe the deviation of the experimental data from the model calculation. An $η_d $ was introduced calculating the $Θ_{eff}/Θ_{rig}$ value from the low energy discrete levels of the nucleus. The $η/η_d $ values seem to be independent of the mass number, their average value near to one. The $η/η_d $ values also seem to be independent of the average excitation energy in the continuum, indicating that $η$ is independent of the excitation energy. The mass number and (N-Z) dependence of the $η$ values were studied. The $η$ values for the nuclei with odd mass number show an exponential decrease as a function of A or (N-Z). The $η$ values for the odd-odd type of nuclei appear to be constant, near one, up to the mass number 110 or (N-Z) of 13. Above these values, the $η$ values show exponential decrease. The $η$ values for even-even type nuclei decrease exponentially up to A equal to 142 or (N-Z) equal to 20. The values are lower than those for the nearby odd-odd type nuclei. The $η$ values increase for nuclei with A between 142 and 156 and (N-Z) between 20 and 28, and they finally reach the value of odd-odd type systematics.

## Key claim

The ratio η = Θ_eff/Θ_rig, which parameterizes the spin distribution of nuclear level density, shows systematic dependence on mass number and odd-even character of the nucleus, but appears independent of excitation energy in the continuum; the abstract's claim of "near one" average for η/η_d values is qualified by a "slight dependence on the odd-even characteristic of the proton and neutron number."

## Method

The authors determined η = Θ_eff/Θ_rig for 61 nuclei (mass range 44-200) by fitting experimental isomeric cross-section ratios for 25 isomeric pairs using the TALYS code (version 1.6) with the back-shifted Fermi gas (BSFG) model for level density. Reduced χ² values were minimized as a function of both η and the number of discrete levels used (N_d), treating N_d as a free parameter between N_isomer and N_dmax. Additionally, η_d values were calculated from low-energy discrete levels using Eq. (6) with 0.5 MeV energy bins and a three-bin moving average to study energy dependence.

## Key numbers

- 61 nuclei analyzed, covering mass range A = 44 to 200
- 25 isomeric pairs used for experimental isomeric cross-section ratio comparison
- For ⁴⁴Sc (A=44, Z=21): η = 0.85 ± 0.27 from continuum, η_d = 0.93 ± 0.07 from discrete levels (1.5-3 MeV)
- For ⁴⁶Ti (A=46, Z=22): η = 1.03 ± 0.07 from continuum, η_d = 0.79 ± 0.36 from discrete levels (0-7 MeV)
- For ⁵¹V (A=51, Z=23): η = 0.75 ± 0.06 (reported by Avrig02), η_d = 0.66 ± 0.12 from discrete levels (4-7.5 MeV)
- For ⁵²Mn (A=52, Z=25): η = 1.05 ± 0.07 from continuum, η_d = 1.14 ± 0.35 from discrete levels (0-5.5 MeV)
- For ⁵⁴Fe (A=54, Z=26): η = 0.58 ± 0.06 from (³He,xn) reaction, η_d = 0.51 ± 0.17 from discrete levels (3-6.5 MeV)
- Weighted average of η/η_d ratio across the Sc-V-Cr-Fe-Co region: 0.91 ± 0.12
- Odd-odd nuclei: η ≈ 1.0 constant up to A = 110 or (N-Z) = 13, then exponential decrease
- Even-even nuclei: η decreases exponentially up to A = 142 or (N-Z) = 20, then increases for A between 142 and 156 and (N-Z) between 20 and 28
- Odd-mass nuclei: η shows exponential decrease as function of A or (N-Z)

## Context

This work extends earlier determinations of η for specific nuclei such as ¹⁹⁷Hg and ¹⁹⁵Hg (Sudar 2006), ¹³⁹Nd and ¹⁴¹Nd (Hilgers 2007), and ¹⁹⁴Ir (Uddin 2011) to a systematic study across 61 nuclei. The analysis uses the TALYS code rather than the STAPRE code used in earlier work, and introduces a method to extract η from discrete levels (η_d) for comparison with continuum values. The finding that η is independent of excitation energy in the continuum contradicts some theoretical expectations of energy dependence (Agrawal), while the observed odd-even effects and mass-dependent systematics provide empirical constraints for improving global level density parametrizations in nuclear reaction calculations.
