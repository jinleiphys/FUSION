# Particle-hole ring diagrams for fermions in two dimensions

> The set of particle-hole ring diagrams for a many-fermion system in two dimensions is studied. The complex-valued polarization function is derived in detail and shown to be expressible in terms of square-root functions. For a contact-interaction the perturbative contributions to the energy per particle $\bar E(k_f)$ are calculated in closed analytical form from third up to twelfth order. The resummation of the particle-hole ring diagrams to all orders is studied and a pronounced dependence on the dimensionless coupling parameter $α$ is found. There is a substantial difference between the complete ring-sum with all exchange-type diagrams included and the standard resummation of the leading $n$-ring diagrams only. The spin factor $S_n(g)$ associated to the $n$-th order ring diagrams is derived for arbitrary spin-degeneracy $g$.

## Key claim

The paper derives closed-form analytical expressions for the energy per particle from particle-hole ring diagrams in two-dimensional fermion systems up to twelfth order, but the resummation to all orders reveals that the complete ring-sum including all exchange-type diagrams differs substantially from the standard resummation of only the leading n-ring diagrams, with a pronounced dependence on the dimensionless coupling parameter α.

## Method

The complex-valued polarization function Π(q₀,|q|) for non-relativistic fermions in two dimensions is derived and expressed in terms of square-root functions of two dimensionless variables (x, ν). Using a contact-interaction with coupling constant C₀ = 2πα/M, the perturbative contributions to the energy per particle Ē(k_f) from n-th order ring diagrams are calculated by evaluating double-integrals over the polarization function raised to the n-th power, with spin-factors S_n(g) derived for arbitrary spin-degeneracy g via multiple spin-traces.

## Context

This work extends the study of particle-hole ring diagrams from three-dimensional many-fermion systems (previously studied by Hammer and Schaefer) to two spatial dimensions, where the polarization function simplifies to square-root functions rather than the logarithmic functions appearing in three dimensions. The analytical results from third to twelfth order and the resummation formulas enable applications to two-dimensional condensed matter systems such as graphene and strongly interacting two-dimensional Fermi gases, as studied by Froehlich and Randeria.
