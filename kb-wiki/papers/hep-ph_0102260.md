# Vector and axial-vector correlators in a chirally symmetric model

> We present a chirally symmetric hadronic model for the vector and axial-vector correlators in vacuum. The dominant contributions to these correlators come from intermediate pions, rho and a_1 mesons which are calculated in one-loop approximation. The resulting spectral functions are compared with the data obtained by the ALEPH collaboration from tau decay. In the vector channel we find good agreement up to q^2 = 1 GeV^2, in the transverse axial-vector channel up to q^2 = 2 GeV^2, corresponding to the regimes dominated by two-pion or three-pion decay channels, respectively. The longitudinal axial correlator is in almost perfect agreement with the PCAC result.

## Key claim

The authors present a chirally symmetric hadronic model for vector and axial-vector correlators that abandons the local gauge symmetry principle (and thus the current-field identity) in favor of only global chiral symmetry, which allows them to adjust coupling constants independently and avoid problematic momentum-dependent vertices that plague the gauged linear sigma model.

## Method

The authors construct a Lagrangian with global SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R chiral symmetry containing pions, sigma, rho, and a_1 mesons, with the vector meson self-interactions (3-vector and 4-vector terms) set to zero. They calculate the meson propagators and correlation functions in a one-loop approximation, using dimensional regularization with a Stueckelberg term for the vector meson kinetic part to control ultraviolet behavior. The resulting spectral functions are compared with ALEPH collaboration data from tau decay.

## Key numbers

- Vector channel shows good agreement with ALEPH data up to q^2 = 1 GeV^2
- Transverse axial-vector channel shows good agreement up to q^2 = 2 GeV^2
- With rho mass = 770 MeV, a_1 mass ≈ √2 × 770 = 1090 MeV, and pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV, the coupling constant g is found to be 5.9
- The effective rho-pi-pi coupling constant g_{ρππ}^{eff} is given by an expression involving g, h_1, sigma_0, m_a, m_rho, xi, and m_pi

## Context

This work extends the gauged linear sigma model approach of Lee and Nieh (1960s) and the vector meson dominance model of Sakurai, while addressing the phenomenological failures of those models in describing rho and a_1 meson properties. The authors explicitly show that the gauged linear sigma model produces a zero in the a_1 → ρπ amplitude at q^2 = m_a^2 + m_ρ^2 that contradicts experimental data from ARGUS and ALEPH. By relaxing local gauge symmetry constraints, following a strategy similar to Ko and Rudaz but more radical, the authors achieve a description that reproduces experimental spectral functions without higher-dimension terms. This work enables future application to finite temperatures and studies of chiral symmetry restoration in hot or dense matter.
