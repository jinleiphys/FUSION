# The polarization of thermal dileptons emitted in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> This work presents calculations of thermal dilepton emission and polarization observables. It features a comprehensive framework which comprises virtual photon spectral functions complete at next-to-leading-order in the strong coupling and iEBE-MUSIC hydrodynamic simulations. The polarization of thermal lepton pairs is shown to be sensitive to in-medium properties of the quark-gluon plasma. We consider Pb+Pb collisions performed in conditions specific to the LHC and examine the magnitude and behaviour of the polarization as measured in different frames, the effects of the pre-equilibrium gluon abundance, and we derive a one-to-one mapping between dielectron and dimuon polarization.

## Key claim

The polarization of thermal dileptons emitted from the quark-gluon plasma is shown to be sensitive to in-medium properties, but this sensitivity is conditional on the specific reference frame used for measurement and requires accounting for pre-equilibrium gluon abundance effects; the paper derives a one-to-one mapping between dielectron and dimuon polarization that holds under the framework's assumptions.

## Method

The authors combine next-to-leading-order (NLO) virtual photon spectral functions with iEBE-MUSIC hydrodynamic simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies. The initial state is modeled with IP-Glasma, followed by pre-equilibrium evolution using the KøMPøST model from 0.1 to 0.8 fm, then matched to MUSIC viscous hydrodynamics with shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s = 0.12. The polarization coefficients λθ, λφ, λθφ, λφ⊥, and λθφ⊥ are computed by integrating over all fluid cells and the full space-time history of the collision, with the rotationally-invariant combination λ̃ defined as (λθ + 3λφ)/(1 - λφ).

## Key numbers

- Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV at the LHC are simulated
- The pre-equilibrium stage is simulated for a proper-time window between 0.1 and 0.8 fm
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s is set to a constant value of 0.12
- The sum of squared quark charges for a 3-flavour QCD plasma is Cem = 2/3
- The kinematic phase-space factor B(ξ) = (1+2ξ)√(1-4ξ) where ξ = mℓ²/K²

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of thermal dilepton yields at NLO (Churchill 2023) by adding polarization observables, and builds on theoretical frameworks for dilepton polarization from Hoyer (1986), Baym (2017), and Speranza (2018). The study enables experimental comparisons with LHC measurements of dilepton angular distributions, and provides a method to relate dielectron and dimuon polarization measurements through the derived one-to-one mapping. The rotationally-invariant λ̃ is shown to be preserved after averaging over the fireball, which is important for comparing results between different experimental reference frames.
