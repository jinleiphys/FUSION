# A study of the relativistic corrections to polarized tritium $β$-decay

> Forthcoming experiments like Project8 and Ptolemy aim at investigating with high precision the end-point of the tritium $β$-decay spectrum sensitive to the neutrino mass. In light of this, using the standard parametrization in terms of nuclear polar form factors, we analyze the complete relativistic expression for the spectrum of the $β$-electron emitted by a tritium nucleus. Given the small parameters in the problem, we systematically discuss the approximations that can be made, and present the first two corrections to the standard lowest order formula. We particularly discuss the case of an initially polarized target, and the consequences on the spectrum as a function of the neutrino mass. We show that, while it induces an angular anisotropy that can be measured by future experiments, such anisotropy cannot be used as an additional handle to constrain the neutrino mass.

## Key claim

The authors show that while initial polarization of tritium nuclei induces an angular anisotropy in the beta-electron spectrum that will be visible in the Ptolemy experiment, this anisotropy cannot serve as an additional handle to constrain the neutrino mass because its relative variation with neutrino mass is comparable to the expected statistical error.

## Method

The authors start from the standard parametrization of the nuclear matrix element in terms of polar form factors (vector, axial-vector, magnetic, and pseudoscalar) and compute the fully relativistic differential decay rate for tritium beta-decay, including the case of an initially polarized target. They systematically expand the rate in the small parameters of the problem (such as Q/m_e, m_e/M_i, and m_nu/M_i) and retain all corrections up to O(10^{-6}), which are relevant for the Ptolemy experiment at maximum coverage. The theoretical rate is then convolved with a Gaussian experimental resolution of width sigma = 100 meV to simulate the expected event distribution.

## Key numbers

- The tritium Q-value is Q = 18.59201(7) x 10^3 eV, the tritium mass is M_i = 2.80943249663(19) x 10^9 eV, and the electron mass is m_e = 0.5109989461(31) x 10^6 eV
- The axial coupling is g_A = 1.27641(55) and the magnetic coupling is g_M = -6.106
- For a 100 g tritium target, the expected event rate in the energy range Delta in [-2,0] eV is N ~ 10^12 events per year, corresponding to a relative statistical uncertainty of order 10^{-6}
- The relative directional asymmetry is of order 5 percent, with more events expected in the direction anti-parallel to the initial polarization
- The asymmetry ratio r shows a relative variation of order 10^{-5} with varying neutrino mass, and the expected relative error sigma_r/r ~ 10^{-5} for Ptolemy at maximum coverage

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of relativistic corrections to tritium beta-decay by Simkovic (2007) and Masood (2007), correcting some sign errors and overestimates in the expansion scheme of Simkovic (2007). The authors note that a fully relativistic expression without specific assumptions was previously presented only by Ludl (2016). The work is motivated by the upcoming Ptolemy and Project8 experiments, which aim to measure the neutrino mass with high precision at the tritium beta-decay end-point. The authors identify that for neutrino masses m_nu greater than 100 meV, the leading and next-to-leading order formulae have different shapes potentially distinguishable by Ptolemy, making these corrections necessary to avoid theoretical biases. The paper also discusses that initial polarization of tritium, as considered by Lisanti (2014), likely cannot be used by forthcoming experiments for neutrino mass extraction.
