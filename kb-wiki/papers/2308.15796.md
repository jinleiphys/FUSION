# A Neural Network Approach for Orienting Heavy-Ion Collision Events

> A convolutional neural network-based classifier is elaborated to retrace the initial orientation of deformed nucleus-nucleus collisions by integrating multiple typical experimental observables. The isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model is employed to generate data for random orientations of ultra-central uranium-uranium collisions at $E_\text{beam} = 1\, \text{GeV/nucleon}$. Statistically, the data-driven polarization scheme is essentially accomplished via the classifier, whose distinct categories filter out specific orientation-biased collision events. This will advance the deformed nucleus-based studies on nuclear symmetry energy, neutron skin, etc.

## Key claim

A convolutional neural network-based classifier can statistically retrace the initial orientation of deformed uranium-uranium collisions by integrating multiple experimental observables, but the approach is limited to a classification scheme (six discrete orientation categories) rather than continuous angle prediction, and the azimuthal angle φ shows limited sensitivity to the chosen inputs.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model to simulate ultra-central (impact parameter ≤ 1 fm) uranium-uranium collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon beam energy, with nucleon densities from a relativistic mean-field model plus Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer pairing. They generated 10,000 collision events with random orientations (defined by Euler angles θ₁, φ₁ for the target and θ₂, φ₂ for the projectile), varied the test particle number N from 1 to 100 to control fluctuations, and trained a multi-input convolutional orientation filter (COF) network on observables including hadron counting distributions, anisotropic flows v₁-v₄, charged particle multiplicities, and angular distributions of high-momentum nucleons. The network outputs six discrete orientation classes based on the polar angles θ₁ and θ₂, using one-hot encoding and a cross-entropy loss function with LReLU activation.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 1 GeV/nucleon for uranium-uranium collisions
- Impact parameter: b ≤ 1 fm (ultra-central collisions)
- Quadrupole deformation of ²³⁸U: β = 0.287
- Training/validation split: 7:3 ratio from 10,000 simulated orientations
- Validation set accuracy: 22.0% (N=1), 65.1% (N=20), 72.2% (N=50), 74.1% (N=100)
- Training set accuracy: 22.8% (N=1), 67.5% (N=20), 72.4% (N=50), 77.1% (N=100)
- For N=50, tip-tip and body-body collision accuracy exceeds 80%
- Grid intervals: Δpₜ = 0.15 GeV/c for transverse momentum, Δy₀ = 0.1 for central rapidity bins
- Angular intervals for high-momentum nucleon counting: Δθ⊥(Δφ) = 30°
- Skyrme parameters: A = -232 MeV (attractive), B = 179 MeV (repulsive), σ = 1.3, incompressibility K = 230 MeV

## Context

This work extends the line of research on deformed nuclei in heavy-ion collisions initiated by Li (2000) for uranium-uranium systems, and builds on recent discoveries of linear correlations between anisotropic flows and multipole deformations at ultra-relativistic energies by Zhang (2022) and Jia (2022). It also follows prior machine learning approaches for impact parameter determination using quantum molecular dynamics models by Bass (1994, 1996) and others, and the use of 2D proton/neutron distributions to find symmetry energy signatures by Wang (2021). The COF network enables future experimental studies at facilities such as HIAF/IMP, J-PARC, FAIR, NICA, and HIMAC, and could be applied to reanalyze existing RHIC data on Au+Au at 200 GeV and U+U at 193 GeV from Adamczyk (2015).
