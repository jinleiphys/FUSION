# Early time dynamics and constraints on medium evolution

> In relativistic heavy-ion collisions, a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is created whose pre-equilibrium evolution includes a rich variety of exciting phenomena of Quantum Chromodynamics. In these Proceedings, we provide a short overview of our current understanding of the pre-equilibrium dynamics of the QGP from a weak-coupling perspective and highlight recent developments. Focusing on new studies, we then discuss how initial stages of the QGP medium may impact hard probes including direct photons, dileptons, jets, and heavy quarks. This introduces promising opportunities to obtain experimental signatures of the pre-equilibrium QGP.

## Key claim

The pre-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma (QGP) exhibits large, anisotropic transport coefficients that smoothly connect the Glasma phase to the hydrodynamic regime, and these pre-equilibrium effects should not be neglected in phenomenological models of jet quenching, heavy-quark diffusion, and quarkonium evolution.

## Method

The paper reviews weak-coupling approaches to pre-equilibrium QGP dynamics, combining classical-statistical real-time simulations (for the Glasma phase where occupancies are large, f ≫ 1) with QCD effective kinetic theory (EKT) simulations that solve the Boltzmann equation including elastic 2↔2 and inelastic 2↔1 scattering processes under Bjorken expansion. Transport coefficients (the heavy-quark diffusion coefficient κ and the jet quenching parameter q̂) are computed during both the Glasma phase using force-force correlations and during the kinetic bottom-up thermalization stages using QCD kinetic theory simulations.

## Key numbers

- In the Glasma phase, the jet quenching parameter q̂ exceeds typical thermal values during hydrodynamic evolution by an order of magnitude
- For the jet quenching parameter q̂ during the kinetic regime, the anisotropic broadening between q̂_z and q̂_y reaches up to 20% for the physical parameters used
- The ratio κ_z/κ_T of beauty quarks and the ratio κ_T/κ_z of heavy quarks are computed as functions of time during the Glasma and kinetic stages respectively
- Dilepton spectra with invariant mass within 1 < M < 5 GeV have potential to provide signals of pre-equilibrium stages

## Context

This conference proceedings extends the weak-coupling picture of pre-equilibrium QGP dynamics established by the bottom-up thermalization scenario of Baier (2000) and the Glasma framework of Gelis (2012). It connects recent calculations of transport coefficients during the Glasma phase by Ipp (2020) and Avramescu (2023) with new kinetic theory results by Boguslavski (2023) that bridge to the hydrodynamic regime. The work enables experimental tests of pre-equilibrium dynamics through hard probes, including direct photon polarization studies by Hauksson (2023) and dilepton production calculations by Coquet (2021), and suggests that medium-sized systems like O+O collisions may be particularly sensitive to pre-equilibrium effects.
