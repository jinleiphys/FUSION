# Hypernuclear single particle spectra based on in-medium chiral SU(3) dynamics

> A previously derived relativistic energy density functional for nuclei, based on low-energy in-medium chiral dynamics, is generalized to implement constraints from chiral SU(3) effective field theory and applied to $Λ$ hypernuclei. Density-dependent central and spin-orbit mean fields are calculated for a $Λ$ hyperon using the SU(3) extension of in-medium chiral perturbation theory to two-loop order. Long range $ΛN$ interactions arise from kaon-exchange and from two-pion-exchange with a $Σ$ hyperon in the intermediate state. Short-distance dynamics is encoded in contact interactions. They include scalar and vector mean fields reflecting in-medium changes of quark condensates, constrained by QCD sum rules. The $Λ$ single particle orbitals are computed for a series of hypernuclei from $^{13}_Λ$C to $^{208}_Λ$Pb. The role of a surface (derivative) term is studied. Its strength is found to be compatible with a corresponding estimate from in-medium chiral perturbation theory. Very good agreement with hypernuclear spectroscopic data is achieved. The smallness of the $Λ$-nuclear spin-orbit interaction finds a natural explanation in terms of an almost complete cancellation between short-range scalar/vector contributions and longer range terms generated by two-pion exchange.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the smallness of the Λ-nuclear spin-orbit interaction arises from an almost complete cancellation between short-range scalar/vector mean-field contributions and longer-range terms generated by two-pion exchange with an intermediate Σ hyperon, with the three-body Fujita-Miyazawa mechanism absent for the Λ since it exists only as a single valence particle.

## Method
The authors generalized a previously derived relativistic energy density functional for nuclei (Finelli et al., 2006-2007) to include Λ hyperons by implementing constraints from chiral SU(3) effective field theory. Density-dependent central and spin-orbit mean fields for the Λ were calculated using in-medium chiral perturbation theory to two-loop order, with long-range ΛN interactions from kaon-exchange and two-pion-exchange with a Σ hyperon intermediate state, and short-distance dynamics encoded in contact interactions constrained by QCD sum rules. The Λ single-particle orbitals were computed self-consistently via Kohn-Sham-type Dirac equations for a series of hypernuclei from ¹³_ΛC to ²⁰⁸_ΛPb, with the surface (gradient) term strength treated as an adjustable parameter and compared to chiral perturbation theory estimates.

## Key numbers
- The contact term ΔG^Λ from chiral 2π exchange is approximately -0.5 fm², much smaller than the individual scalar and vector contact couplings G_{S,V}^{Λ(0)} which are about 10 fm² for nucleons
- Best-fit values of ΔG^Λ range from -0.55 to -0.58 fm² for ζ values 0.4 to 2/3, producing a central Λ single-particle potential U^Λ_central = 2ΔG^Λρ(r) ≈ -(35-37) MeV·(ρ(r)/ρ₀)
- The cutoff scale Λ̄ from fits is approximately 0.7 GeV (718.6-726.1 MeV), consistent with KWhyp (2006)
- The surface term strength D^Λ_S from best fits is -(0.3-0.4) fm⁴, compared to the chiral perturbation theory estimate of -0.56 fm⁴
- For ¹³_ΛC, the BNL 929 experiment reported Δε^Λ(p) = 152 ± 54 ± 36 keV between p₁/₂ and p₃/₂ Λ levels
- For ⁸⁹_ΛY, updated analysis gives Δε^Λ(f) ≈ 200 keV, Δε^Λ(d) ≈ 150 keV, and Δε^Λ(p) ≈ 90 keV

## Context
This work extends the relativistic energy density functional approach of Finelli et al. (2006-2007) from ordinary nuclei to hypernuclei, building on a preliminary study of ¹⁶_ΛO (Finelli et al., 2007). It provides a natural explanation for the weak Λ-nuclear spin-orbit coupling observed experimentally (Kohri et al., 2001; Ajimura et al., 2001; Akikawa et al., 2002) and confirmed in heavy hypernuclei (Motoba et al., 2008), contrasting with earlier relativistic mean-field models (Brockmann and Weise, 1977; Keil et al., 1999) that required ad-hoc suppression mechanisms. The framework enables systematic calculations across the hypernuclear chart and tests the cancellation mechanism proposed by Kaiser and Weise (2006) between short-range scalar/vector contributions and longer-range two-pion exchange terms.
