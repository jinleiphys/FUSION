# Three-dimensional imaging in nuclei

> We perform the first simultaneous global QCD extraction of the transverse momentum dependent (TMD) parton distribution functions and the TMD fragmentation functions in nuclei. We have considered the world set of data from semi-inclusive electron-nucleus deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan di-lepton production. In total, this data set consists of 126 data points from HERMES, Fermilab, RHIC and LHC. Working at next-to-leading order and next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy, we achieve a $χ^2/dof = 1.045$. In this analysis, we quantify the broadening of TMDs in nuclei comparing with those in free nucleons for the first time. We also make predictions for the ongoing JLab 12 GeV program and future EIC measurements.

## Key claim
The authors perform the first simultaneous global QCD extraction of nuclear transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (nTMDPDFs) and nuclear transverse momentum dependent fragmentation functions (nTMDFFs), quantifying the broadening of TMDs in nuclei compared to free nucleons under the assumption that TMD factorization and perturbative evolution remain unchanged from the vacuum case.

## Method
The analysis uses TMD factorization for semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) and Drell-Yan (DY) processes, replacing vacuum TMDs with nuclear TMDs while keeping perturbative Sudakov factors unchanged. The nuclear broadening is parameterized through two fit parameters, \(a_N\) and \(b_N\), which modify the intrinsic transverse momentum width as \(g_q^A = g_q + a_N (A^{1/3} - 1)\) and \(g_h^A = g_h + b_N (A^{1/3} - 1)\). The fit is performed at next-to-leading order (NLO) and next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) accuracy using 126 data points from HERMES, Fermilab, RHIC, and LHC, with collinear nuclear PDFs from EPPS16 and nuclear FFs from LIKEn.

## Key numbers
- Global fit achieves \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 1.045\) for 126 data points.
- Fit parameter for nTMDPDF broadening: \(a_N = 0.0171 \pm 0.003\) GeV\(^2\).
- Fit parameter for nTMDFF broadening: \(b_N = 0.0144 \pm 0.001\) GeV\(^2\).
- HERMES SIDIS data contributes 63 points with \(\chi^2 = 40.2\); E866 DY contributes 28 points with \(\chi^2 = 42.2\); E772 DY contributes 16 points with \(\chi^2 = 22.2\); CMS \(\gamma^*/Z\) contributes 8 points with \(\chi^2 = 9.7\); ATLAS \(\gamma^*/Z\) contributes 7 points with \(\chi^2 = 13.1\); RHIC DY contributes 4 points with \(\chi^2 = 2.1\).
- Kinematic cuts applied: SIDIS \(P_{h\perp}^2 < 0.3\) GeV\(^2\) and \(z < 0.7\); DY \(q_\perp/Q < 0.3\).

## Context
This work extends the collinear nuclear PDF analyses such as EPPS16 (Eskola 2016) and nCTEQ (Kovarik 2016) into the transverse momentum dependent domain, and is the first global extraction of nuclear TMDs. It builds on vacuum TMD extractions by groups such as Echevarria (2020) and Bacchetta (2019), and provides a framework for interpreting future measurements from the JLab 12 GeV program and the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), enabling three-dimensional imaging of nuclear partonic structure.
