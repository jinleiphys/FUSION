# Nucleon matrix elements of the antisymmetric quark tensor

> If physics beyond the Standard Model enters well above the electroweak scale, its low-energy effects are described by Standard Model Effective Field Theory. Already at dimension six many operators involve the antisymmetric quark tensor $\bar q σ^{μν} q$, whose matrix elements are difficult to constrain from experiment, Ward identities, or low-energy theorems, in contrast to the corresponding vector and axial-vector or even scalar and pseudoscalar currents. However, with normalizations determined from lattice QCD, analyticity and unitarity often allow one to predict the momentum dependence in a large kinematic range. Starting from recent results in the meson sector, we extend this method to the nucleon case and, in combination with pole dominance, provide a comprehensive assessment of the current status of the nucleon form factors of the quark tensor.

# Nucleon matrix elements of the antisymmetric quark tensor

## Key claim
The nucleon form factors of the antisymmetric quark tensor can be predicted in a large kinematic range below sqrt(|t|) less than about 1 GeV by combining lattice QCD normalizations with analyticity, unitarity, and pole dominance, but the normalizations of the induced nucleon form factors carry large uncertainties because the sum rules for the normalization exhibit slow convergence.

## Method
The authors derive the unitarity relation for nucleon tensor form factors from pi-pi intermediate states, analogous to the electromagnetic form factor case, and show that the imaginary parts of the tensor form factors depend only on P-wave pi-pi to N-anti-N amplitudes and the pion tensor form factor. They combine this dispersive framework with narrow-resonance descriptions for the omega, h1(1170), and b1(1235) mesons to cover isoscalar and axial-vector contributions, using lattice QCD results for the tensor charges as normalizations.

## Key numbers
- Pion tensor form factor normalization: B_T^{pi,u}(0) = -B_T^{pi,d}(0) = 0.195(10) at MS scale mu = 2 GeV
- Pion tensor charge radius: <(r_T^{pi,u})^2> = 0.43(1) fm^2
- Kaon tensor form factor normalization: B_T^{pi K}(0) = 0.686(25)
- Kaon tensor charge radius: <(r_T^{pi K})^2> = 0.30(2) fm^2
- Nucleon tensor charges: F_{1,T}^{u,p}(0) = 0.784(28), F_{1,T}^{d,p}(0) = -0.204(11)
- Isovector tensor anomalous magnetic moment sum rule: kappa_T^{u,p} - kappa_T^{u,n} = 1.31(7) from the dispersive relation, compared to lattice QCD values kappa_T^{u,p} approx 3.0 and kappa_T^{d,p} approx 1.9
- Isoscalar tensor anomalous magnetic moment sum rule: kappa_T^{u,p} + kappa_T^{u,n} = 3.94(20) from the dispersive relation, compared to lattice QCD value 4.9
- F_{2,T}^{u,p} normalization: -1.5(1.0), slope: -7.0(8) GeV^{-2}
- F_{2,T}^{d,p} normalization: 0.5(3), slope: 2.5(3) GeV^{-2}
- F_{3,T}^{u,p} normalization: 0.1(2), slope: 1.8(2) GeV^{-2}
- F_{3,T}^{d,p} normalization: -0.6(3), slope: -2.1(2) GeV^{-2}
- Strangeness tensor form factor F_{1,T}^{s,N}(0) = -0.0027(16)

## Context
This work extends the method previously applied to meson tensor form factors (Cata 2007, Baum 2011) to the nucleon case, using the same analyticity and unitarity techniques that have been successful for electromagnetic nucleon form factors (Frazer 1960, Hoferichter 2016). The results provide the first comprehensive dispersive determination of nucleon tensor form factors, enabling improved calculations for dark matter direct detection (Goodman 2010, Hoferichter 2016), muon-to-electron conversion in nuclei (Cirigliano 2009, Crivellin 2017), and searches for non-standard hyperon decays (Chang 2014), where previously only hadronic model estimates were available (Pasquini 2005, Ledwig 2010).
