# Role of quantum fluctuations in a system with strong fields: Onset of hydrodynamical flow

> Quantum fluctuations are believed to play an important role in the thermalization of classical fields in inflationary cosmology but their relevance for isotropization/thermalization of the classical fields produced in heavy ion collisions is not completely understood. We consider a scalar $ϕ^4$ toy model coupled to a strong external source, like in the Color Glass Condensate description of the early time dynamics of ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. The leading order classical evolution of the scalar fields is significantly modified by the rapid growth of time-dependent quantum fluctuations, necessitating an all order resummation of such "secular" terms. We show that the resummed expressions cause the system to evolve in accordance with ideal hydrodynamics. We comment briefly on the thermalization of our quantum system and the extension of our results to a gauge theory.

## Key claim

Quantum fluctuations around classical fields in a scalar φ⁴ toy model with Color Glass Condensate-like initial conditions grow rapidly via parametric resonance and linear instabilities, and their all-order resummation causes the system to evolve according to ideal hydrodynamics; however, the authors caution that there is no a priori guarantee that lessons from this scalar theory translate automatically to gauge theories like QCD.

## Method

The authors consider a scalar φ⁴ field theory coupled to a strong external source J ~ Q³/g that is nonzero only for x⁰ < 0, mimicking the Color Glass Condensate description of heavy ion collisions. They compute the energy-momentum tensor at leading order (classical fields) and next-to-leading order (one-loop quantum fluctuations), identify secular divergences from exponentially growing parametric resonance modes and linearly growing low-lying modes, and develop a resummation that exponentiates the shift operator acting on the leading-order energy-momentum tensor, effectively replacing the linearized fluctuation treatment with an ensemble average over Gaussian-distributed initial field configurations.

## Key numbers

- The NLO correction to the energy density is approximately 1.4% of the LO result for coupling g = 1.
- The envelope of the integral I(x⁰) = ∫ d³k/(2π)³2k a_{-k}(x)a_{+k}(x) grows exponentially with a rate λ ≈ 2 × μ_max, where μ_max is the maximal Lyapunov exponent in the resonance band.
- The thermalization time estimates from hydrodynamic models range from τ_relax ~ 0.6–1 fm, which the authors note is difficult to reconcile with quasi-particle scattering pictures.
- The conjectured universal lower bound for η/s is 1/4π, with RHIC data suggesting η/s ≲ 5/4π.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of quantum fluctuations in the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory (Gelis et al., 2009; Gelis, Lappi, Venugopalan, 2008–2009) and draws strong analogies to preheating dynamics in inflationary cosmology (Greene et al., 1997; Micha and Tkachev, 2003). The resummation technique builds on earlier observations in inflationary contexts (Son, 1996; Polarski and Starobinsky, 1992; Khlebnikov and Tkachev, 1996). The results suggest that decoherence of quantum fluctuations can produce an equation of state and ideal hydrodynamic flow, and the formalism is intended to be extendable to gauge theories for first-principles studies of thermalization in heavy ion collisions.
