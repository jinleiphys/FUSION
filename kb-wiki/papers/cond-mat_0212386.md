# On A Possible Scheme of q-Deformation of Many-Boson Systems in Time-Dependent Variational Method

> 11 pages, talk given by M.Y. at XXVI International Workshop on Condensed Matter Theories, Luso, Portugal, 2-7 September 2002. To appear in CMT26 (Luso) Workshop Proceedings Vol.18, Condensed Matter Theories, Nova Science Publishers

# On A Possible Scheme of q-Deformation of Many-Boson Systems in Time-Dependent Variational Method

## Key claim

The authors review a scheme they recently developed in which q-deformed boson operators arise naturally from generalizing the boson coherent state used as a trial state in the time-dependent variational method, by introducing a function of the boson number operator into the exponential of the trial state. The scheme is presented as a possible framework rather than a unique or proven approach, and the authors stress that the starting idea is the focus, not a complete theory.

## Method

The time-dependent variational method is formulated with a trial state parametrized by canonical variables (p,q), leading to Hamilton's equations of motion. The boson coherent state is generalized by introducing three forms of trial states (labeled c0, cP, cQ) that incorporate a function f_R(hat N) of the boson number operator in the exponential. The corresponding annihilation-like operators hat gamma_R are constructed, and their eigenstates define the generalized coherent states; the commutation relations of these operators yield the q-deformed boson algebra characterized by the function f_0(n). Three concrete examples of f_0(n) are given, and the classical counterpart is derived via expectation values in the generalized coherent state.

## Key numbers

- For the most popular form of q-deformation: f_0(n) = sqrt( (n+1)(q - q^{-1}) / (q^{n+1} - q^{-(n+1)}) ), leading to the commutation relation hat gamma_0 hat gamma_0^* - q^{-1} hat gamma_0^* hat gamma_0 = q^{hat N}
- For the Penson-Solomon form (1999): f_0(n) = q^{n/2}, leading to hat gamma_0 hat gamma_0^* - q^{-1} hat gamma_0^* hat gamma_0 = q^{-hat N}
- For a modification by the present authors: f_0(n) = sqrt( (n+1)(1 - q^{-2}) / (1 - q^{-2(n+1)}) ), leading to hat gamma_0 hat gamma_0^* - q^{-2} hat gamma_0^* hat gamma_0 = 1
- For the Holstein-Primakoff representation of su(1,1): f_0(n) = ( sqrt(1 + n/n^0) )^{-1} with n^0 positive, giving hat gamma_0 = (sqrt(n^0))^{-1} hat T_- where hat T_- = sqrt(n^0 + hat N) hat c
- For the Holstein-Primakoff representation of su(2): f_P(n) = ( sqrt(1 - n/n^0) )^{-1} with n^0 positive, giving hat gamma_P = (sqrt(n^0))^{-1} hat P_{n^0} cdot hat S_- cdot hat P_{n^0} where hat S_- = sqrt(n^0 - hat N) hat c

## Context

This work extends the authors' own recent series of papers (Kuriyama et al., 2001-2002) on q-deformation of many-boson systems, and builds on the time-dependent variational method formalism of Marumori, Maskawa, Sakata and Kuriyama (1980). The paper also draws on the Coimbra group's work (Providencia, Brito and da Providencia, 1993) and the independent work of Penson and Solomon (1999) on generalized coherent states. The scheme enables the description of damped and amplified oscillations through q-deformed models, and the authors note that the generalized Schwinger boson representation for su(M+1) and su(N,1) algebras may allow description of thermal effects on pairing rotation, pairing vibration, and intrinsic structure.
