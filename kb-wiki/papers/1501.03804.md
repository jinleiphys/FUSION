# Two particle correlation effects and Differential HBT for rotation in heavy ion collisions

> Peripheral heavy ion reactions at ultra relativistic energies have large angular momentum that can be studied via two particle correlations using the Differential Hanbury Brown and Twiss method. We analyze the possibilities and sensitivity of the method in a rotating system. We also study an expanding solution of the fluid dynamical model of heavy ion reactions.

# Two particle correlation effects and Differential HBT for rotation in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
Peripheral heavy ion collisions at ultra-relativistic energies produce large angular momentum that can be studied via two-particle correlations using the Differential Hanbury Brown and Twiss (DHBT) method, but the sensitivity of the standard correlation function to rotation becomes negligibly weak when a large number of densely placed fluid cells all contribute equally, so the emission probability asymmetry from different space-time regions of the system is essential for the evaluation.

## Method
The authors used a fluid dynamical model with high cell resolution and low numerical viscosity at LHC energies, with impact parameter b = 0.7 b_max, where both rotation and the Kelvin-Helmholtz Instability (KHI) occur. They computed the two-pion correlation function using the Jüttner (relativistic Boltzmann) distribution for the phase space distribution, and evaluated the Differential Correlation Function (DCF) defined as the difference between correlation functions measured at two symmetric angles forward and backward shifted in the reaction plane. They compared configurations with and without the rotation component of the flow, and also analyzed the DCF in reference frames deflected by an angle alpha to isolate the rotation effect.

## Key numbers
- Angular momentum of the system: L ≈ 10^6 ℏ at LHC energies
- Impact parameter: b = 0.7 b_max for the chosen configuration
- Average pion wavenumber used in DCF analysis: k = 5/fm
- Fluid dynamical evolution time: t = 3.56 fm/c
- Deflection angle of the symmetry axis for Pb+Pb at √s_NN = 2.36 TeV: α = -11 degrees
- Deflection angle of the symmetry axis for Au+Au at √s_NN = 0.2 TeV: α = -8 degrees

## Context
This work extends the Differential HBT method introduced in DCF (2014) and applies it to a high-resolution Particle in Cell Relativistic (PICR) fluid dynamical model from CVW2014. The study builds on fluid dynamical estimates of rotation from hydro1 (2013) and on numerical estimates of the Kelvin-Helmholtz Instability from hydro2 and KHI-Wang. The results enable quantitative measurement of rotation in the reaction plane of heavy ion collisions, and the authors note that the direction of the symmetry axes should be found with other methods such as global flow analysis or azimuthal HBT analysis as described by M. Lisa.
