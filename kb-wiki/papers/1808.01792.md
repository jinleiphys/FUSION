# Measurement of the analyzing powers in pd elastic and pn quasi-elastic scattering at small angles

> The analyzing powers in proton-deuteron elastic and proton-neutron quasi-elastic scattering have been measured at small angles using a polarized proton beam at the COSY storage ring incident on an unpolarized deuterium target. The data were taken at 796MeV and five higher energies from 1600MeV to 2400MeV. The analyzing power in pd elastic scattering was studied by detecting the low energy recoil deuteron in telescopes placed symmetrically in the COSY plane to the left and right of the beam whereas for pn quasi-elastic scattering a low energy proton was registered in one of the telescopes in coincidence with a fast scattered proton measured in the ANKE magnetic spectrometer. Though the experiment explores new domains, the results are consistent with the limited published information.

## Key claim

The analyzing powers in proton-deuteron elastic and proton-neutron quasi-elastic scattering were measured at small angles using a polarized proton beam on an unpolarized deuterium target at 796 MeV and five energies from 1600 to 2400 MeV, with the results being consistent with limited published information despite exploring new kinematic domains.

## Method

The experiment used the ANKE magnetic spectrometer at the COSY storage ring with a vertically polarized proton beam incident on an unpolarized deuterium cluster-jet target. For pd elastic scattering, low-energy recoil deuterons were detected in silicon tracking telescopes (STT) placed symmetrically left and right of the beam, and the analyzing power was extracted using the cross-ratio method. For pn quasi-elastic scattering, a fast scattered proton was measured in the forward detector in coincidence with a slow proton in one STT, and quasi-free events were isolated by applying a cut on the ratio of spectator proton momentum to momentum transfer (pspec/pT < 0.5).

## Key numbers

* Beam polarizations at the six energies: 0.511 ± 0.001 (796 MeV), 0.378 ± 0.001 (1600 MeV), -0.476 ± 0.003 (1800 MeV), -0.508 ± 0.005 (1965 MeV), -0.513 ± 0.005 (2157 MeV), 0.501 ± 0.004 (2368 MeV), each with an additional 3% systematic uncertainty.
* For pd elastic scattering at 796 MeV, the analyzing power A_y^p(pd) ranges from 0.257 ± 0.012 at Θ_cm = 7.5° to 0.406 ± 0.090 at Θ_cm = 28.5°.
* For pd elastic scattering at 2368 MeV, the analyzing power A_y^p(pd) ranges from 0.082 ± 0.005 at Θ_cm = 4.5° to 0.150 ± 0.011 at Θ_cm = 16.5°.
* The missing-mass peak for pd → dX at 796 MeV had a width of 15.6 MeV/c² (FWHM) averaged over the deuteron kinetic energy range.
* The background under the neutron peak in the missing-mass distribution for pd → ppX at 1800 MeV was about 6%.
* The overall systematic uncertainty in the pn analyzing power measurement was estimated as about 5.5% at 2157 MeV and below 4% at other beam energies, with typical statistical errors of about 10%.

## Context

This work extends the sparse data base for neutron-proton elastic scattering above 1500 MeV, where previous phase-shift analyses (SAID SP07 solution by Arndt et al., 2007) were limited to at most 1300 MeV for the isoscalar channel. The pd elastic results at 796 MeV are consistent with earlier LAMPF data (Irom et al., 1983) to within 2%, while no previous data exist at 1600 MeV and above for comparison. The pn quasi-elastic results at 796 MeV agree with the SAID SP07 predictions and with data from Bardsley et al. (1983), and the measurements at higher energies provide new constraints for future phase-shift analyses in the isoscalar channel.
