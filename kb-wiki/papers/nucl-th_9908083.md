# Skyrme mean-field studies of nuclei far from the stability line

> Two applications of mean-field calculations based on 3D coordinate-space techniques are presented. The first concerns the structure of odd-N superheavy elements that have been recently observed experimentally and shows the ability of the method to describe, in a self-consistent way, very heavy odd-mass nuclei. Our results are consistent with the experimental data. The second application concerns the introduction of correlations beyond a mean-field approach by means of projection techniques and configuration mixing. Results for Mg isotopes demonstrate that the restoration of rotational symmetry plays a crucial role in the description of 32Mg.

# Skyrme mean-field studies of nuclei far from the stability line

## Key claim

Self-consistent Skyrme-HFB calculations in 3D coordinate space can describe odd-N superheavy elements and, when augmented with angular-momentum and particle-number projection plus configuration mixing, can capture correlations beyond mean field; however, the method fails to reproduce the pronounced collectivity of 32Mg, indicating that the delicate balance between spherical and deformed configurations is sensitive to the effective interaction.

## Method

The authors solved the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations on a 3D coordinate-space mesh using the SLy4 Skyrme interaction in the particle-hole channel and a zero-range delta force in the pairing channel, with pairing strengths adjusted to reproduce average gaps in even-even nuclei around 254Fm. For the Mg isotopes, they performed particle-number and angular-momentum projection of HF+BCS wave functions generated with an axial quadrupole constraint, followed by configuration mixing via the generator coordinate method (GCM) to solve the discretized Hill-Wheeler equation for each angular momentum.

## Key numbers

- For the 289114 alpha-decay chain, the ground-state-to-ground-state Q_alpha is 9.64 MeV; the allowed [611]1/2+ to [611]1/2+ transition gives Q_alpha = 10.2 MeV, compared to the experimental Dubna/Livermore value of 9.9 MeV.
- For the 293118 alpha-decay chain, the ground-state-to-ground-state Q_alpha is 11.59 MeV; the allowed [611]1/2+ to [611]1/2+ transition gives Q_alpha = 11.8 MeV, compared to the experimental Berkeley/Oregon value of 12.6 MeV.
- For 285112, the calculated Q_alpha for the allowed [611]1/2+ to [611]1/2+ decay is 8.76 MeV, very close to the experimental second alpha transition energy of 8.84 MeV reported by the Dubna/Livermore group.
- For 289116, the calculated Q_alpha for the allowed [611]1/2+ to [611]1/2+ decay is 10.14 MeV, underestimating the experimental second alpha energy of 11.8 MeV from the Berkeley/Oregon experiment.
- For 281110, the predicted Q_alpha for the allowed [611]1/2+ to [611]1/2+ decay is 9.4 MeV (Dubna/Livermore: 9.0 MeV); for 285114, the prediction is 10.6 MeV (Berkeley/Oregon: 11.5 MeV).
- In 24Mg, the configuration-mixing energy gain is largest for the 0+ state at approximately 800 keV, decreasing at higher spins.
- In 32Mg, the HF+BCS ground state is spherical; after projection, the 0+ ground state corresponds to a deformed intrinsic state with a quadrupole moment of 100 fm^2.

## Context

This work extends the 3D coordinate-space mean-field techniques developed by Bonche, Flocard, Heenen, and collaborators (1985) for Zr isotopes, and by Terasaki et al. (1996) for HFB with zero-range interactions. The superheavy-element calculations are compared directly to the experimental alpha-decay chains reported by the Dubna/Livermore collaboration (Oganessian, 1999) and the Berkeley/Oregon team (Ninov, 1999). The Mg isotope study builds on the particle-number projected HF+BCS method of Heenen et al. (1993) and generalizes it to include rotational symmetry restoration; the results for 32Mg are compared to the work of Reinhard et al. (1999) showing sensitivity to the effective interaction. The method enables future studies of medium-mass nuclei far from stability where restoration of broken symmetries is essential.
