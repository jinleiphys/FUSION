# Quarkonium Binding and Entropic Force

> A Q-Qbar bound state represents a balance between repulsive kinetic and attractive potential energy. In a hot quark-gluon plasma, the interaction potential experiences medium effects. Color screening modifies the attractive binding force between the quarks, while the increase of entropy with Q-Qbar separation gives rise to a growing repulsion. We study the role of these phenomena for in-medium Q-Qbar binding and dissociation. It is found that the relevant potential for Q-Qbar binding is the free energy F; with increasing Q-Qbar separation, further binding through the internal energy U is compensated by repulsive entropic effects.

# Quarkonium Binding and Entropic Force

## Key claim

If quarkonium binding and dissociation in a hot quark-gluon plasma is studied using a potential approach, the relevant potential is the free energy F(T,r), not the internal energy U(T,r); the stronger binding suggested by U is compensated by repulsive entropic forces, so that the overall binding is governed by F.

## Method

The paper derives the thermodynamic potentials (free energy F, entropy S, internal energy U) for a heavy quark-antiquark pair in a deconfined medium, separately for the strong coupling regime (string tension model with screening mass mu(T) proportional to T) and the weak coupling regime (screened Coulomb potential). The pressure P = -dF/dr is decomposed into an energetic force K_u = -dU/dr and an entropic force K_s = T dS/dr, and their competition is analyzed. Lattice QCD results at T = 190 MeV and T = 270 MeV are used to illustrate the behavior near the deconfinement temperature.

## Key numbers

- For charmonium in vacuum: ground state separation r_0 = 0.43 fm, mass M_0 = 3.2 GeV, using string tension sigma = 0.2 GeV^2, charm quark mass m_c = 1.3 GeV, and parameter c = 1.3.
- For bottomonium in vacuum: ground state separation r_0 = 0.27 fm, mass M_0 = 9.6 GeV, using bottom quark mass m_b = 4.6 GeV and parameter c = 1.1.
- Lattice results shown at T = 190 MeV (close to deconfinement temperature of about 160 MeV) and T = 270 MeV, with forces given in units of fm^{-2}.
- The screening mass mu(T) is assumed proportional to T in the strong and weak coupling regimes, with critical behavior modifying this near T_c.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing debate over whether the free energy F or the internal energy U is the correct in-medium potential for quarkonium binding, previously discussed by authors such as those cited as p1, p3, p4, p5 in the text. The conclusion that F is the appropriate potential agrees with earlier work comparing real and imaginary time Q-Qbar correlators (cited as "weak" in the text) and with recent real-time Wilson loop studies (cited as r10). The paper enables a clearer framework for calculating quarkonium dissociation temperatures, but notes that near T_c the entropic force becomes so large that a two-body potential approach may break down entirely, potentially requiring a more collective treatment of dissociation as suggested by Kharzeev (2015).
