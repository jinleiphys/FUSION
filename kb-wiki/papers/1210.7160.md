# On the description of two-particle transfer in superfluid systems

> Exact results of pair transfer probabilities for the Richardson model with equidistant or random level spacing are presented. The results are then compared either to particle-particle random phase approximation (ppRPA) in the normal phase or quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) in the superfluid phase. We show that both ppRPA and QRPA are globally well reproducing the exact case although some differences are seen in the superfluid case. In particular the QRPA overestimates the pair transfer probabilities to excited states in the vicinity of the normal-superfluid phase transition, which might explain the difficult in detecting collective pairing phenomena as for example the Giant Pairing Vibration. The shortcoming of QRPA can be traced back to the breaking of particle number that is used to incorporate pairing. A method, based on direct diagonalization of the Hamiltonian in the space of two quasi-particle projected onto good particle number is shown to improve the description of pair transfer probabilities in superfluid systems.

## Key claim

The paper shows that both particle-particle RPA (ppRPA) in the normal phase and quasiparticle RPA (QRPA) in the superfluid phase globally reproduce exact pair transfer probabilities for the Richardson model, but QRPA systematically overestimates probabilities to excited states near the normal-superfluid phase transition; this shortcoming is traced to the breaking of particle number symmetry in QRPA, and a projected two-quasiparticle Tamm-Dancoff method (P-QTDA) is shown to improve the description.

## Method

Exact pair transfer probabilities were computed by direct diagonalization of the Richardson pairing Hamiltonian for a system of N=8 particles in Omega=10 doubly degenerate single-particle levels, using both equidistant and random level spacings. These exact results were compared to ppRPA (in the normal phase) and QRPA (in the superfluid phase) calculations. A projected two-quasiparticle Tamm-Dancoff approach (P-QTDA) was then implemented, where the Hamiltonian is diagonalized in a space consisting of the projected ground state plus two-quasiparticle states projected onto good particle number, using the Fomenko method with 199 gauge-angle integration points for the number projection.

## Key numbers

- For the equidistant level case, the BCS threshold (G_cr) is 0.33 MeV for the 8-particle system; for the random level case, G_cr is 0.44 MeV.
- The ppRPA collapse point occurs at G = 0.48 Delta-epsilon for the equidistant case (with Delta-epsilon = 1 MeV).
- At G = 0.5 MeV with equidistant spacing, the P-QTDA(GS) ground state energy is 300 keV lower than the original projected mean-field ground state; at G = 0.9 MeV the reduction is only 30 keV.
- The random single-particle energies used (in MeV) are: 0.551, 2.176, 4.033, 5.142, 6.444, 7.029, 7.827, 8.343, 9.226, and 9.571.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of pairing vibrations and pair transfer in superfluid nuclei, particularly the concepts of Giant Pairing Vibration introduced by Broglia (1973) and Bes (1966), and the QRPA-based predictions by Khan (2004, 2009) and others. The exact Richardson model solutions provide a benchmark for approximate methods used in realistic nuclear transfer calculations, such as those by Matsuo (2010), Shimizu (2011), and Grasso (2012). The paper enables improved treatment of two-particle transfer in superfluid systems, especially near the normal-superfluid transition where QRPA overestimates excited-state probabilities, and suggests that previous QRPA predictions by Fortier (2002) may significantly overestimate Giant Pairing Vibration cross sections.
