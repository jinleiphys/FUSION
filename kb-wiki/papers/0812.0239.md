# Collective modes of trapped Fermi gases with in-medium interaction

> Due to Pauli blocking of intermediate states, the scattering matrix (or $T$ matrix) of two fermionic atoms in a Fermi gas becomes different from that of two atoms in free space. This effect becomes particularly important near a Feshbach resonance, where the interaction in free space is very strong but becomes effectively suppressed in the medium. We calculate the in-medium $T$ matrix in ladder approximation and study its effects on the properties of collective modes of a trapped gas in the normal-fluid phase. We introduce the in-medium interaction on both sides of the Boltzmann equation, namely in the calculation of the mean field and in the calculation of the collision rate. This allows us to explain the observed upward shift of the frequency of the quadrupole mode in the collisionless regime. By including the mean field, we also improve considerably the agreement with the measured temperature dependence of frequency and damping rate of the scissors mode, whereas the use of the in-medium cross section deteriorates the description, in agreement with previous work.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including the in-medium interaction (via the $T$ matrix in ladder approximation) on *both* sides of the Boltzmann equation, the mean field and the collision rate, can explain the observed upward shift of the quadrupole mode frequency in the collisionless regime, but using the in-medium cross section actually *deteriorates* the description of the scissors mode, in agreement with previous work.

## Method

The authors calculate the in-medium $T$ matrix in ladder approximation for a two-component Fermi gas with attractive interaction, then use it to compute both the single-particle self-energy (giving a mean field $U$) and the in-medium differential cross section. They incorporate these into a linearized Boltzmann equation within the local-density approximation (LDA) for a harmonic trap, and solve for collective mode frequencies and damping rates by taking moments of the Boltzmann equation with trial functions.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature $T_C$ is obtained from the Thouless criterion: $\Re\tilde{J}(\omega=0,\vek{k}=0;T=T_C) = m/(4\pi a)$ (Eq. \ref{Thouless}).
- For a uniform system at zero temperature, the Hartree approximation predicts a maximum density $\rho_{\mathit{max}} = \pi/(48|a|^3)$ and a critical $k_F|a| > \pi/2$ for instability, which is an artefact.
- The free-space cross section is $d\sigma_0/d\Omega = a^2/[1+(qa)^2]$, where $q$ is the incoming relative momentum in the center-of-mass frame (Eq. \ref{freexsection}).
- For a trapped gas, the Fermi energy is defined as $\epsilon_F = (3N)^{1/3} \bar{\omega}$ and $k_F = \sqrt{2m\epsilon_F}$, where $\bar{\omega}$ is the geometric mean trap frequency (Eq. \ref{epsilonFkFtrap}).
- Density profiles are shown for $N=400000$ atoms at $1/k_F a = -0.45$ for temperatures $T/T_F = 0.5$ and $T/T_F = 0.21$ (the critical temperature), comparing NSR and quasiparticle approximations (Fig. \ref{fig:density}).

## Context

This work extends earlier Boltzmann-equation treatments of collective modes in trapped Fermi gases that used the Hartree approximation (Menotti, Pedri, Guery-Odelin, Toschi, Massignan, Bruun) or neglected mean-field effects entirely (Riedl, Bruun, Smith). It builds on the in-medium $T$-matrix approach of Nozieres and Schmitt-Rink (1985) and the Thouless criterion for the critical temperature. The results enable a more consistent description of the normal-fluid phase above $T_C$ across the BCS-BEC crossover, including the unitary limit, and provide a framework for comparing with experiments at Duke University (Kinast, Hemmer, Turlapov) and Innsbruck (Bartenstein, Altmeyer, Wright, Riedl).
