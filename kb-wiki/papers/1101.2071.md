# The X(3872) and other X,Y,Z Resonances as Hidden Charm Meson-Meson Molecules

> We report on some ideas concerning the nature of the X(3872) resonance and the need for approximately equal charged and neutral components of $D \bar{D}^* +cc$. Then we discuss how some hidden charm states are obtained from the interaction between vector mesons with charm and can be associated to some of the charmonium-like X,Y,Z states. Finally we discuss how the nature of these states could be investigated through different types of radiative decay.

## Key claim
The X(3872) resonance, while often considered a molecular state dominated by neutral $D^0 \bar{D}^{*0}$ components, actually requires approximately equal charged and neutral components to reproduce experimental decay ratios; the isospin-zero nature of the state is determined by the wave functions at the origin rather than by the absolute probabilities of each component.

## Method
The authors model the X(3872) as a dynamically generated resonance in isospin I=0 using a coupled-channel approach with a separable potential in momentum space, solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation to obtain scattering amplitudes and pole residues. For the X,Y,Z states, they study the $D^* \bar{D}^*$ system and search for poles in the second Riemann sheet of the scattering amplitudes, extracting couplings to various channels including light vector mesons and charmonium states.

## Key numbers
- Ratio $\mathcal{B}(X\rightarrow J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-) / \mathcal{B}(X\rightarrow J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0) = 1.0 \pm 0.4 \pm 0.3$ (experimental value)
- Pole at $\sqrt{s}=3943 + i 7.4$ MeV with $I^G[J^{PC}]=0^+[0^{++}]$, associated with Y(3940); coupling to $D^*\bar{D}^*$ is $18810 - i 682$ MeV
- Pole at $\sqrt{s}=3945 + i 0$ MeV with $I^G[J^{PC}]=0^-[1^{+-}]$, a predicted state with no experimental counterpart; coupling to $D^*\bar{D}^*$ is $18489 - i 0.78$ MeV
- Pole at $\sqrt{s}=3922 + i 26$ MeV with $I^G[J^{PC}]=0^+[2^{++}]$, associated with Z(3930); coupling to $D^*\bar{D}^*$ is $21100 - i 1802$ MeV
- Pole at $\sqrt{s}=4169 + i 66$ MeV with $I^G[J^{PC}]=0^+[2^{++}]$, associated with X(4160); coupling to $D^*_s\bar{D}_s^*$ is $18927 - i 5524$ MeV
- Pole at $\sqrt{s}=3919 + i 74$ MeV with $I^G[J^{PC}]=1^-[2^{++}]$, a predicted state with no experimental counterpart; coupling to $D^*\bar{D}^*$ is $20267 - i 4975$ MeV

## Context
This work extends the molecular interpretation of the X(3872) first proposed by Swanson (2003), Voloshin (2004), and Braaten (2005), and builds on the dynamical generation approach of Gamermann and Oset (2007, 2009). The vector-vector interaction model for the X,Y,Z states generalizes earlier studies of light vector-light vector interactions by Molina et al. (2008) and Geng et al. (2008). The results enable predictions for radiative decays into $D^* \bar{D} \gamma$ and $\gamma \gamma$ final states, which can be tested against future experimental data from B-factories and LHCb.
