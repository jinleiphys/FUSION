# Dichotomy of Baryons as Quantum Hall Droplets and Skyrmions In Compact-Star Matter

> We review the recent exploration of a possible "domain-wall structure" of compressed baryonic matter in massive compact stars in terms of fractional quantum Hall droplets and skyrmions for baryons in medium. The theoretical framework is anchored on an effective nuclear effective field theory that incorporates two hidden symmetries, flavor local symmetry and scale symmetry conjectured to be dual to the gluons and quarks of QCD. It hints at a basically different, hitherto undiscovered structure of nuclear matter at low as well as high densities. Hidden "genuine dilaton (GD)" symmetry and hidden local symmetry (HLS) gauge-equivalent at low density to nonlinear sigma model capturing chiral symmetry, put together in nuclear effective field theory, are seen to play an increasingly important role in providing hadron-quark duality in baryonic matter. This strongly motivates incorporating both symmetries in formulating "first-principles" approaches to nuclear dynamics encompassing from the nuclear matter density to the highest density stable in the Universe.

## Key claim

The paper argues that baryons in dense matter can be understood through a "dichotomy" where flavor-singlet baryons (associated with the $\eta^\prime$ meson) behave as fractional quantum Hall droplets while flavor-nonet baryons (with $N_f \geq 2$) behave as skyrmions, and that this topological duality may unify under increasing density in compact-star matter, though the authors acknowledge their arguments are "inevitably speculative or conjectural" given the exploratory nature of the work.

## Method

The authors construct a scale-invariant hidden local symmetric (sHLS) Lagrangian that incorporates pions, vector mesons ($\rho$ and $\omega$), the dilaton field $\chi$ (representing $f_0(500)$ as a "genuine dilaton"), and the $\eta^\prime$ meson, then analyze baryonic matter by mapping topological properties of skyrmions to a density-functional-type theory (G$n$EFT) with explicit baryon fields. They study the behavior of this system in the mean-field approximation approaching the "dilaton limit fixed point" (DLFP), where both chiral and scale symmetries are realized in the Nambu-Goldstone mode, and examine the interplay between the $\omega$-nucleon coupling and the dilaton condensate as density increases.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: $n_0 \approx 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$
- The half-skyrmion transition density $n_{1/2}$ occurs at approximately $3 n_0$ in compact-star phenomenology
- The spin of the fractional quantum Hall droplet baryon $B^{(0)}$ is $J = N_c/2 = 3/2$ for $N_c = 3$, corresponding to the $\Delta(3/2,3/2)$ resonance
- At the dilaton limit fixed point, the constraints give $g_A \to g_{V\rho} \to 1$ and $f_\pi \to f_\chi \neq 0$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of G$n$EFT (Ma and Rho, 2020; Paeng et al., 2017) which incorporates hidden local symmetry and scale symmetry into nuclear effective field theory, and builds on Komargodski's (2018) suggestion that the $\eta^\prime$ baryon forms a fractional quantum Hall droplet rather than a skyrmion. The paper addresses the "dichotomy problem" identified by Karasik (2020) regarding how $N_f=2$ quarks choose between forming skyrmions or nonabelian fractional quantum Hall droplets, and proposes that dialing baryon density may reveal hadron-quark duality relevant to the equation of state of massive compact stars. The approach enables future investigation of how topological structures from condensed matter physics (quantum Hall droplets) might manifest in dense nuclear matter.
