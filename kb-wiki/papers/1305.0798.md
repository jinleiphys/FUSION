# Viscosity, wave damping and shock wave formation in cold hadronic matter

> We study linear and nonlinear wave propagation in a dense and cold hadron gas and also in a cold quark gluon plasma, taking viscosity into account and using the Navier-Stokes equation. The equation of state of the hadronic phase is derived from the nonlinear Walecka model in the mean field approximation. The quark gluon plasma phase is described by the MIT equation of state. We show that in a hadron gas viscosity strongly damps wave propagation and also hinders shock wave formation. This marked difference between the two phases may have phenomenological consequences and lead to new QGP signatures.

## Key claim

The paper shows that viscosity strongly damps wave propagation and hinders shock wave formation in a cold hadron gas, whereas in a cold quark gluon plasma waves propagate nearly undisturbed; this marked difference arises essentially from the very different viscosity coefficients in the two phases, which can differ by two orders of magnitude or more.

## Method

The authors used the non-relativistic Navier-Stokes equation to study linear and nonlinear wave propagation in cold hadronic matter and cold quark gluon plasma. The equation of state for the hadronic phase was derived from the nonlinear Walecka model in the mean field approximation, while the quark gluon plasma phase was described by the MIT bag model equation of state. For linear waves, the authors performed a linearization formalism to obtain a viscous wave equation and dispersion relation; for nonlinear waves, they employed the reductive perturbation method (RPM) to derive wave equations that include nonlinearities, dissipative and dispersive terms.

## Key numbers

- The viscosity coefficients (both bulk and shear) in the two phases can differ by two orders of magnitude or more, according to estimates cited from prior work.
- The nuclear baryon density is given as ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³, and the density range considered is ρ₀ ≤ ρ_B ≤ 2ρ₀.
- The speed of sound squared is taken as c_s² = 1/3 for both phases.
- The phase velocity in a viscous medium is given by v_p² = c_s² − (νk/2)², where ν = (1/(Mρ₀))(ζ + (4/3)η).
- The damping factor in the linear wave solution is e^(−νk²t/2), and the condition for real phase velocity is k < 2c_s/ν.
- The nucleon mass is M = 939 MeV, the vector meson mass is m_V = 783 MeV, and the scalar meson mass is m_S = 550 MeV.
- The coupling constants used are g_V = 9.197, g_S = 8.81, with b = 13.47 fm⁻¹ and c = 43.127.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group on wave propagation in perfect fluids (Fogaça and Navarra 2006, 2007; Ferreira Filho et al. 2009, 2010, 2011) by introducing viscosity for the first time in a systematic comparison between hadron gas and quark gluon plasma. It builds on findings that the QGP is an almost perfect fluid with very small viscosity, while the hadron gas has very large viscosity (as estimated in works by various authors cited in the text). The results suggest that the strong damping of waves in hadronic matter could serve as a new QGP signature, and the work has similarities to studies by Randrup (2009) and Shokov (2011) on the role of viscosity in phase separation and nucleation in heavy ion collisions.
