# Equivalence between first-order causal and stable hydrodynamics and Israel-Stewart theory for boost-invariant systems with a constant relaxation time

> We show that the recently formulated causal and stable first-order hydrodynamics has the same dynamics as Israel-Stewart theory for boost-invariant, Bjorken expanding systems with a conformal equation of state and a regulating sector determined by a constant relaxation time. In this case, the general solution of the new first-order formulation can be determined analytically.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the recently formulated first-order causal and stable (FOCS) hydrodynamics is exactly equivalent to Israel-Stewart (IS) theory for boost-invariant, Bjorken-expanding systems with a conformal equation of state, but only when the regulating sector of FOCS is expressed in terms of a constant relaxation time rather than being determined by conformal invariance. In the strictly conformal case where regulators also obey conformal scaling, the two approaches cannot be exactly matched.

## Method
The authors compared the evolution equations of IS theory and FOCS hydrodynamics for a boost-invariant, baryon-free system with a conformal equation of state (p = epsilon/3). They rewrote both formulations as coupled first-order differential equations for temperature T and its proper-time derivative y = dT/dtau, then equated coefficients term-by-term to determine when the two sets of equations become identical. The mapping required expressing the FOCS regulator coefficients (epsilon_1, epsilon_2, pi_1, pi_2) in terms of the IS relaxation time tau_R and the parameter lambda, with the additional constraint of vanishing bulk viscosity.

## Key numbers
- The mapping requires the FOCS regulator coefficients to scale as T^4 (n=4), giving epsilon_1^0 = 4 a tau_R, where a is the constant in the conformal equation of state epsilon = a T^4.
- The vanishing bulk viscosity condition yields epsilon_2^0 = (4/3) a tau_R, pi_1^0 = (4/3) a tau_R (4 + 3 lambda), and pi_2^0 = (4/9) a tau_R (4 + 3 lambda).
- Causality in the FOCS approach with the matched coefficients requires lambda to be negative; the standard 14-moment approximation value lambda = 10/21 leads to causality violations.
- The general analytical solution for the energy density is expressed in terms of Whittaker functions M_{k,mu}(x) and W_{k,mu}(x) with the scaled proper time hat_tau = tau/tau_R.

## Context
This work extends the comparison initiated by Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha, and Kovtun (2017-2019) between their new first-order causal hydrodynamics and traditional Israel-Stewart theory. It builds on the analytical solution of IS equations for Bjorken flow found by Denicol (2017) and uses the causality conditions derived by Bemfica (2019). The results enable the first analytical solution of the FOCS equations for an expanding system and clarify that the IS parameter lambda must take values distinct from the standard 14-moment result to maintain causality in the full nonlinear regime.
