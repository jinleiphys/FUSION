# Quantum phase transitions and collective enhancement of level density in odd-A and odd-odd nuclei

> The nuclear shell model assumes an effective mean-field plus interaction Hamiltonian in a specific configuration space. We want to understand how various interaction matrix elements affect the observables, the collectivity in nuclei and the nuclear level density for odd-A and odd-odd nuclei. Using the sd and pf shells, we vary specific groups of matrix elements and study the evolution of energy levels, transition rates and the level density. In all cases studied, a transition between a "normal" and a collective phase is induced, accompanied by an enhancement of the level density in the collective phase. In distinction to neighboring even-even nuclei, the enhancement of the level density is observed already at the transition point. The collective phase is reached when the single-particle transfer matrix elements are dominant in the shell model Hamiltonian, providing a sign of their fundamental role.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in odd-A and odd-odd nuclei, a quantum phase transition between a "normal" and a collective phase is induced by varying the relative strength of the single-particle transfer (δ=1) matrix elements in the shell-model Hamiltonian, and that, unlike in even-even nuclei, the enhancement of level density is observed already at the transition point itself, not only in the fully collective phase.

## Method

The authors used the nuclear shell model in the sd and pf configuration spaces, constructing a Hamiltonian H = h + (1−λ)V₁ + λV₂, where V₁ contains the δ=1 (single-particle transfer) matrix elements and V₂ contains the remaining (δ=0 and δ=2) matrix elements, with λ as a control parameter varied from 0 to 1 in steps of 0.1. They performed full diagonalizations for odd-A (²⁷Al) and odd-odd (²⁶Al, ²⁸Al, ³⁰P, ⁵⁰Mn) nuclei, studying the evolution of yrast energies, transition probabilities, wave function decompositions, and level densities computed via the moments method.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁶Al, the minimum of yrast energies occurs at λ ≈ 0.3–0.4; for ³⁰P, the minimum is at λ ≈ 0.1.
- The cumulative number of levels (J=0–10, up to 10 MeV) for ²⁶Al increases from 1122 at λ=0.0 to a maximum of 1311 at λ=0.3, then decreases to 250 at λ=1.0.
- For ²⁷Al (J=1/2–21/2, up to 10 MeV), the number of levels rises from 1069 at λ=0.0 to 1199 at λ=0.2, then falls to 137 at λ=1.0.
- For ⁵⁰Mn (J=0–10, up to 60 MeV), the number of levels is 1.449×10⁷ at λ=0.0, peaks at 1.453×10⁷ at λ=0.2, and drops to 1.334×10⁷ at λ=1.0.
- The second derivative of the ground state energy shows a steep minimum at λ=0.3 for ²⁷Al, at λ=0.12 for ³⁰P, and at λ=0.2 for ⁵⁰Mn, identifying the phase transition locations.
- For ²⁶Al, the B(E2; 2⁺₁ → 0⁺₁) transition probability shows a maximum near the transitional λ region.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of even-even nuclei (Karampagia et al., 2016, PRC94) to odd-A and odd-odd systems, where the presence of unpaired fermions makes the phase transition more pronounced and extends its signatures to high excitation energies. The approach builds on earlier work by Horoi (2015) identifying δ=1 matrix elements as crucial for prolate deformation, and on the random interaction ensemble studies of Robledo and Zelevinsky (2004). The results provide the first evidence of an excited-state quantum phase transition within the shell-model framework, opening the door to understanding how specific interaction components shape nuclear observables beyond ground-state properties.
