# Microscopic aspects of gamma softness in atomic nuclei

## Key claim
The Triaxial Projected Shell Model (TPSM) reproduces the energies and transition probabilities of both the gamma-soft nucleus 104Ru and the rigid triaxial nucleus 112Ru, but only when quasiparticle admixtures beyond the vacuum configuration are included; truncation to the vacuum alone yields the opposite staggering pattern (even-I-up) regardless of the nucleus.

## Method
The authors diagonalize a shell model Hamiltonian (containing monopole pairing, quadrupole pairing, and quadrupole-quadrupole interactions) in a basis of angular-momentum projected multi-quasiparticle configurations generated from a triaxial Nilsson Hamiltonian. The basis includes the 0-quasiparticle vacuum, two-quasiproton, two-quasineutron, and four-quasiparticle configurations. The triaxiality parameter gamma is adjusted to reproduce the gamma band head energy E(2+_2), while the deformation epsilon is taken from B(E2) systematics and pairing gaps from odd-even mass differences.

## Key numbers
- For 104Ru: the TPSM ground-band shape invariant <cos 3 delta> is approximately 0.5, corresponding to gamma approximately 20 degrees, with dispersion sigma<cos 3 delta> approximately 0.3 indicating 70% of the distribution within 9 degrees < gamma < 24 degrees
- For 112Ru: the shape invariants show narrow distributions of 7 degrees width centered at gamma approximately 26 degrees for the ground band and gamma approximately 20 degrees for the gamma band
- The experimental staggering parameter for 104Ru is bar S(6) = -0.15 (even-I-down pattern, gamma soft)
- The experimental staggering parameter for 112Ru is bar S(6) = 0.31 (even-I-up pattern, rigid triaxial)
- The TPSM reproduces the experimental B(E2) matrix element for 2+_2 -> 0+_1 in 104Ru as -0.141 e.b (experimental: -0.156 e.b with error 0.02)
- The TPSM reproduces the experimental B(E2) matrix element for 2+_1 -> 0+_1 in 104Ru as 0.973 e.b (experimental: 0.917 e.b with error 0.025)

## Context
This work extends the Triaxial Projected Shell Model studies of Jehangir, Ruoofa, Ruoofb, and Ruoofc to provide a microscopic interpretation of gamma softness versus gamma rigidity, contrasting with the traditional phenomenological Bohr Hamiltonian approach. The TPSM interpretation explains the rapid change of the staggering parameter S(I) with neutron and proton number as arising from the competition between mixing of the K=2 vacuum band with the K=0 ground band (which pushes even-I states up) and mixing with two-quasiparticle states (which pushes even-I states down). This enables a microscopic understanding of shape fluctuations across 35 studied nuclei, where the quasiparticle structure near the Fermi level plays a central role.
