# The impact of future $D$- and $B$-meson measurements with the SMOG2 program at LHCb on the determination of nuclear parton distribution functions

> We perform an analysis of the potential impact of future $D$- and $B$-meson measurement within the SMOG2 fixed-target program at the LHCb experiment on nuclear parton distribution functions. Following~\cite{Bursche:2018orf}, we assume that SMOG2 will collect data for five nuclear targets: He, Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe and hydrogen which will provide a baseline for constructing nuclear ratios. The analysis is performed by using the PDF reweighting method. We demonstrate that such a measurement will allow to considerably reduce the current uncertainties of the nuclear gluon distribution and, to some extent, even the uncertainties of the light sea-quark distributions. Furthermore, it will provide the first possibility to systematically study the current assumptions on the $A$-dependence of the nuclear parton distributions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that future D- and B-meson measurements from the SMOG2 fixed-target program at LHCb will considerably reduce current uncertainties of nuclear gluon distributions and, to some extent, light sea-quark distributions, but this conclusion depends on the assumed luminosities and systematic uncertainties (4-5% for D mesons, 8% for B mesons) and on the PDF reweighting method applied to pseudo-data.

## Method

The authors generated pseudo-data for D0 and B+ meson production in proton-nucleus collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 113 GeV using MadGraph5_aMC@NLO, assuming the SMOG2 detector acceptance (2 < y_lab < 4.6) and efficiencies (1% integrated for D mesons, similar for B mesons). They applied the PDF reweighting method to estimate the impact of these future measurements on nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs), using five nuclear targets (He, Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe) plus hydrogen as a baseline, with luminosities ranging from 22 pb^-1 (Xe) to 150 pb^-1 (H2) for D mesons and up to 310 pb^-1 (Xe) for B mesons.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy for pA collisions: sqrt(s_NN) = 113 GeV with a proton beam energy of 6.8 TeV, and 72 GeV for heavy-ion beams at 2.76 TeV per nucleon
- Rapidity coverage in center-of-mass frame: -2.79 < y_cms < -0.19, corresponding to Delta y = 4.79
- x_2 range probed: 0.02 to 0.27 for D mesons, 0.06 to 0.76 for B mesons
- Integrated efficiency for D mesons: 1%, varying from 0.04-0.3% at lowest rapidities to 2.1% at y_lab = 3.7 and 0.7% at y_lab = 4.6
- Assumed systematic uncertainties: 4-5% for D mesons, 8% for B mesons
- Annual luminosity with proton beam on H2 target: up to 100 pb^-1
- Luminosities for D meson pseudo-data: 150 pb^-1 (H2), 40 pb^-1 (He), 40 pb^-1 (Ne), 45 pb^-1 (Ar), 30 pb^-1 (Kr), 22 pb^-1 (Xe)
- Luminosities for B meson pseudo-data: 10^4 pb^-1 (H2), 310 pb^-1 (Xe)

## Context

This work extends the SMOG fixed-target program at LHCb, which previously collected data with He, Ne, and Ar targets during LHC Run 2 (LHCb 2018-2024), by analyzing the potential of the upgraded SMOG2 system with a gas storage cell that increases luminosity by up to two orders of magnitude. The study addresses the poorly constrained gluon nPDFs in the antishadowing and EMC regions (0.02 less than x less than 0.8), building on prior nPDF determination efforts by groups such as those reviewed by Ethier (2020), Gao (2017), Kovarik (2019), and Klasen (2023). The results will enable improved pQCD calculations for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, as well as for astrophysical cross-section computations.
