# Flow anisotropies due to momentum deposition from hard partons

> In nuclear collisions at the LHC large number of hard partons are created in initial partonic interactions, so that it is reasonable to suppose that they do not thermalise immediately but deposit their energy and momentum later into the evolving hot quark-gluon fluid. We show that this mechanism leads to contribution to flow anisotropies at all orders which are non-negligible and should be taken into account in realistic simulations.

# Flow anisotropies due to momentum deposition from hard partons

## Key claim
Hard partons created in initial partonic interactions at the LHC do not thermalise immediately but deposit their energy and momentum later into the evolving quark-gluon fluid, and this mechanism contributes non-negligibly to flow anisotropies at all orders. The effect is significant enough that it should be taken into account in realistic hydrodynamic simulations aiming to extract transport properties, though the authors note that changing the rate of energy loss had no influence on the generated elliptic flow, which remains an open question.

## Method
The authors set up a three-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic model with an initial energy density profile from an optical Glauber model (wounded-nucleon and binary-collision density) and added source terms J^nu representing forces from hard partons into the energy-momentum conservation equation. Hard partons were produced according to binary collision density in the transverse plane with azimuthally symmetric directions and a p_t spectrum from Levai (2002), and their energy loss scaled with entropy density as dE/dx = (dE/dx)_0 * (s/s_0) with s_0 = 78.2/fm^3. The model was tested for central collisions (b = 0 fm) and non-central collisions (b = 6 fm, corresponding to 30-40% centrality).

## Key numbers
- Initial time tau_0 = 0.5 fm/c with central cell energy density set to 60 GeV/fm^3
- Gaussian width sigma = 0.3 fm for distributing energy and momentum deposition around the parton trajectory
- Reference entropy density s_0 = 78.2/fm^3 corresponding to epsilon_0 = 20 GeV/fm^3
- Parameters for the p_t spectrum: B = 14.7 mb/GeV^2, p_0 = 6 GeV, n = 9.5
- In non-central collisions at b = 6 fm, hard parton contribution increased the observed elliptic flow v_2 by about 50%
- The initial rapidity profile was flat over 10 units with Gaussian tails at the edges

## Context
This work extends the standard picture where flow anisotropies arise solely from initial-state spatial anisotropies (geometric or fluctuating) that evolve hydrodynamically, as studied by Luzum (2011), Niemi (2013), Schenke (2012), and others. The authors note that linear relations between initial spatial anisotropy and final flow anisotropy hold for v_2 and v_3 but break for higher orders (Niemi 2013). The mechanism of momentum deposition from hard partons adds a new source of flow anisotropies during the hydrodynamic evolution, complementing earlier work on dynamical fluctuations by Herold (2013) and on jet-induced fluid streams by Betz (2011). The results indicate that realistic simulations must include this contribution, and future work should incorporate viscosity effects and fluctuating initial conditions using a newly developed hydrodynamic model.
