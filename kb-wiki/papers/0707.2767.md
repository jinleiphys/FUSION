# Strange quark contributions to neutrino and antineutrino nucleus scattering via neutral current in quasi-elastic region

> Strange quark contributions to the neutral current reaction in the neutrino scattering are investigated on the nucleon level and extended to the $^{12}$C target nucleus through the neutrino-induced knocked-out nucleon process in the quasi-elastic region within the framework of a relativistic single particle model. The incident energy range between 500 MeV and 1.0 GeV is used for the neutrino(antineutrino) scattering. Effects of the final state interaction for the knocked-out nucleon are included by a relativistic optical potential. We found that the sensitivity of the strange quark contents could be salient on the asymmetry between neutrino and antineutrino scattering cross sections. In specific, $A (ν({\bar ν}), ν^{'} ({\bar ν}^{'}) N)$ reaction is shown to be very sensitive test in the searches of the strangeness.

# Strange quark contributions to neutrino and antineutrino nucleus scattering via neutral current in quasi-elastic region

## Key claim
The strange axial form factor $g_A^s$ effects in neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering on $^{12}$C are largely cancelled between proton and neutron knockout contributions in inclusive reactions, making the inclusive cross section insensitive to strangeness; however, the asymmetry between neutrino and antineutrino scattering in exclusive knocked-out nucleon reactions ($A(\nu(\bar{\nu}), \nu'(\bar{\nu}')N)$) provides a more sensitive test, particularly in the $T_p = 200-250$ MeV region.

## Method
The authors calculate neutral-current neutrino and antineutrino scattering on nucleons and on a $^{12}$C target in the quasi-elastic region using a relativistic single-particle model with bound-state wave functions from the $\sigma$-$\omega$ model. Final-state interactions for the knocked-out nucleon are included via a relativistic optical potential. The strange quark contributions enter through the vector form factors $F_i^s$ (with $F_1^s = 0.53$ GeV$^{-2}$ and $\mu_s = -0.4$) and the axial form factor with $g_A^s = -0.19$, and the asymmetry $A_{NC} = (\sigma_{NC}^\nu - \sigma_{NC}^{\bar{\nu}})/(\sigma_{NC}^\nu + \sigma_{NC}^{\bar{\nu}})$ is computed for incident energies of 500 MeV and 1.0 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Strange axial form factor value used: $g_A^s = -0.19$, with $g_A = 1.262$ and $M_A = 1.032$ GeV
- Strange vector form factor parameters: $F_1^s = 0.53$ GeV$^{-2}$, $\mu_s = -0.4$, $M_V = 0.843$ GeV
- Weinberg angle: $\sin^2 \theta_W = 0.2224$
- On the nucleon level, the asymmetry $A_{NC}$ on the proton is maximally decreased by about 15% at $Q^2 \sim 0.6$ GeV$^2$ due to $g_A^s$
- For inclusive $^{12}$C($\nu, \nu'$) at 500 MeV: $g_A^s$ increases proton contribution by 30% and decreases neutron contribution by 30%, but net cross section increase is only about 3%
- For inclusive $^{12}$C($\nu, \nu'$) at 1.0 GeV: $g_A^s$ increases proton contribution by 31% and decreases neutron contribution by 29%, net increase about 2%
- For inclusive $^{12}$C($\bar{\nu}, \bar{\nu}'$) at 500 MeV: $g_A^s$ enhances protons by 21% and reduces neutrons by 20%, net reduction of total cross section by 1%
- For inclusive $^{12}$C($\bar{\nu}, \bar{\nu}'$) at 1.0 GeV: $g_A^s$ enhances protons by 19% and reduces neutrons by 18%, net reduction by 2%
- Flux-averaged asymmetry on nucleon level: $\langle A_{NC} \rangle \approx 0.5$, consistent with BNL experimental ratio $R_{\bar{\nu}/\nu}^{BNL} \approx 0.32$

## Context
This work extends previous studies of strange quark contributions in neutrino-nucleus scattering, particularly the relativistic Fermi gas models of Horowitz et al. (1993) and Umino et al. (1995), the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation of Alberico et al. (1997), and the work of Meucci, Giusti, and Pacati (2004-2006) using a relativistic optical potential. The authors note that Nieves et al. (2005) showed the ratio of proton and neutron cross sections in the quasi-elastic region is sensitive to the strange axial form factor using Monte Carlo rescattering. The key finding that inclusive reactions on $^{12}$C suffer from cancellation between proton and neutron contributions motivates the proposal that exclusive knocked-out nucleon reactions provide a cleaner probe of strangeness, enabling future experimental searches at facilities such as those using the BNL neutrino flux.
