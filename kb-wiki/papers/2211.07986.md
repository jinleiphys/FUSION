# Perturbed nuclear matter studied within Density Functional Theory with a finite number of particles

> Nuclear matter is studied within the Density Functional Theory (DFT) framework. Our method employs a finite number of nucleons in a box subject to periodic boundary conditions, in order to simulate infinite matter and study its response to an external static potential. We detail both the theoretical formalism and its computational implementation for pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter with Skyrme-like Energy Density Functionals (EDFs). The implementation of spin-orbit, in particular, is carefully discussed. Our method is applied to the problem of the static response of nuclear matter and the impact of the perturbation on the energies, densities and level structure of the system is investigated. Our work is a crucial step in our program of ab initio-based nuclear EDFs [Phys. Rev. C 104, 024315 (2021)] as it paves the way towards the goal of constraining the EDF surface terms on ab initio calculations.

## Key claim

The paper presents a finite-nucleon DFT method for perturbed nuclear matter that is a crucial step toward constraining EDF surface terms on *ab initio* calculations, but the method is currently limited to spin-saturated pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter with Skyrme-like functionals, and the static response results are demonstrated only for a sinusoidal external potential.

## Method

The authors simulate infinite nuclear matter by placing a finite number of nucleons (A) in a cubic box of side L with periodic boundary conditions, solving the Kohn-Sham equations for Skyrme-like energy density functionals. The single-particle wave functions are factorized into plane waves in the transverse (xy) directions and one-dimensional orbitals in the z-direction, with the spin-orbit term handled by introducing helicity eigenstates. The one-dimensional Schrödinger equations are solved self-consistently using a plane-wave basis expansion, with the many-particle ground state constructed by occupying the lowest A energy levels.

## Key numbers

- The method achieves convergence thresholds of 0.1–1 keV per nucleon, typically within a few tens of self-consistent iterations.
- The minimum wave number for the external potential is q_min = 2π/L, where L is the box side length.
- The first few momentum-space "magic numbers" for the free gas are A/g = 1, 7, 19, 27, 33, etc., where g = 2 for spin-saturated pure neutron matter and g = 4 for spin-saturated symmetric nuclear matter.
- The mixing parameter α for the self-consistent loop is chosen in the range 0.8–0.9 at the beginning and gradually decreased.

## Context

This work extends the finite-A DFT approach previously applied to uniform matter (Marino 2021) to perturbed (inhomogeneous) nuclear matter, building on techniques used for the electron gas (Moroni 1995, Senatore Book) and cold atoms (Gandolfi 2014). It complements *ab initio* studies of neutron matter static response using Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (Gezerlis 2016, 2017, 2021) and provides a DFT framework that can be matched to *ab initio* calculations with the same number of particles, enabling future constraints on EDF surface terms. The method also relates to analytical treatments of the static response for Skyrme EDFs in the thermodynamic limit (Pastore 2015, Pastore 2021).
