# Octupole deformations in actinides at high spins within the cranking Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach

> The cranked Skyrme III effective Hamiltonian is applied for the analysis of the rotational dependence of the quadrupole and octupole moments in Ra, Th, and U isotopes. A comparison of the intrinsic electric dipole moments calculated in the model with available experimental and theoretical values is presented. It is found that the non-axial octupole deformation $Y_{32}$ becomes favorable at high spins for the actinide nuclei.

# Octupole deformations in actinides at high spins within the cranking Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach

## Key claim
The non-axial octupole deformation \(Y_{32}\) becomes energetically favorable at high rotational frequencies in actinide nuclei, but this result is obtained only within the reflection-asymmetric minima of the total energy, and the range of rotational frequencies where such minima exist is nucleus-dependent and may be modified by residual interactions not included in the calculation.

## Method
The authors performed self-consistent cranking Hartree-Fock calculations using the Skyrme III effective interaction, implemented via the HFODD code (version 1.75). For each nucleus, they obtained two minima of the total energy: one with conserved reflection symmetry (option i, with three symmetry planes) and one allowing nonzero octupole moments (option ii, with only one symmetry plane). The reflection-asymmetric minima were obtained using a constraint on the axial octupole moment \(\bar{Q}_{30} = 10^2 \, \mathrm{fm}^3\) with stiffness parameter \(C_{30} \approx 0.01\), and calculations were performed for rotational frequencies up to \(\Omega = 0.6 \, \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Key numbers
- For \(^{226}\mathrm{Ra}\), the octupole-deformed minimum lies lower than the reflection-symmetric minimum by 1 MeV.
- The axial octupole moment \(Q_{30}\) reaches a maximum at \(\Omega \sim 0.3 \, \mathrm{MeV}\) for the lightest Ra and Th isotopes (\(^{218-220}\mathrm{Ra}\), \(^{218-220}\mathrm{Th}\)) and then decreases.
- The intrinsic electric dipole moment \(D_0\) changes sign at \(\Omega = 0 \, \mathrm{MeV}\) at \(A \sim 224\) for Ra, \(A \sim 227\) for Th, and \(A \sim 232\) for U, and this sign change persists over the full rotational frequency range \(0.0 \leq \Omega \leq 0.6 \, \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The non-axial octupole moment \(Q_{32}\) increases with rotational frequency in \(^{221,225,228}\mathrm{Ra}\), \(^{223,229,231}\mathrm{Th}\), and \(^{226,231,233-238}\mathrm{U}\) isotopes.

## Context
This work extends earlier self-consistent calculations by Bonche et al. (1986) for ground states of the Ra-Th region using Skyrme III, and by Robledo and Egido (1990) using the Gogny interaction, which found axial octupole deformation in ground states. It also contrasts with macroscopic-microscopic calculations by Möller and Nilsson (1970) and by Butler and Nazarewicz (1996) that considered only axial octupole shapes. The results enable experimental tests via measurements of \(E1\) transition probabilities in rotating uranium isotopes, where non-axial octupole deformation is predicted to become dominant at high spins.
