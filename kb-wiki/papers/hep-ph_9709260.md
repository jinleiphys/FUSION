# Thermal Fluctuations of Domains of Disoriented Chiral Condensates

> We argue that disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) domains are not well defined for temperatures above the Ginzburg temperature $T_G$ ($\simeq 0.7 T_c$). Above $T_G$, the dynamics of DCC domains is dominated by thermal fluctuations leading to fluctuating orientation of the chiral field in a given domain. It implies that DCC domains may form even in relatively lower energy collisions where the temperature only reaches $T_G$, and never rises to $T_c$. It also means that detection of DCC can not be taken as a signal for an intermediate chirally symmetric phase of matter. Using these considerations, we estimate the probability distribution for DCC domains as a function of the chiral angle.

## Key claim

Disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) domains are not well defined for temperatures above the Ginzburg temperature \(T_G\) (\(\simeq 0.7 T_c\)), meaning that DCC formation can occur even in collisions where the temperature only reaches \(T_G\) and never rises to \(T_c\), but consequently detection of DCC cannot be taken as a signal for an intermediate chirally symmetric phase of matter.

## Method

The authors estimate the Ginzburg temperature \(T_G\) by equating the thermal energy \(T\) to the energy \(E_d\) needed to fluctuate a correlation domain of size \(m_\pi^{-1}\) to the chirally symmetric phase, using the temperature-dependent effective potential of the linear sigma model at one-loop order in the high-temperature approximation. They then model the time evolution of the number of DCC domains with different chiral angles \(\theta\) using a rate equation with thermal fluctuation rates, assuming a Bjorken longitudinal expansion model for the cooling plasma with initial temperature \(T_c \simeq 134\) MeV at proper time \(\tau_0 = 3\) fm.

## Key numbers

* \(T_G \simeq 0.7 T_c\) for the chiral limit, or about \(0.8 T_c\) for non-zero pion mass, where \(T_c = \sqrt{2} f_{\pi} \simeq 134\) MeV for parameters \(m_{\pi} = 140\) MeV, \(f_{\pi} = 94.5\) MeV, and \(m_{\sigma} = 1\) GeV.
* For \(\theta = \pi\), \(T_G \simeq 0.6 T_c\); in a Bjorken expansion model with \(T = T_c\) at \(\tau \simeq 3-10\) fm, the temperature drops to \(0.6 T_c\) at \(\tau \simeq 14-46\) fm.
* At \(\tau \simeq 16\) fm (corresponding to \(T \simeq 0.6 T_c\)), the ratio between the number of DCC domains with \(\theta \simeq \pi\) and \(\theta \simeq 0\) is about 0.3.
* If the correlation length is taken as \(m_\sigma^{-1}\) instead of \(m_\pi^{-1}\), \(T_G\) comes out to be extremely small, \(\simeq 2-4\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of DCC formation in the linear sigma model, previously explored in quench scenarios by Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993) and in annealing scenarios by Gavin and Muller (1994), by incorporating thermal fluctuations of the order parameter known from condensed matter phase transitions (Ginzburg, 1960; Zurek, 1985). The results imply that DCC formation does not require an intermediate chirally symmetric phase, contradicting the common assumption that DCC signals would indicate quark-gluon plasma formation, and instead suggests that DCC may be accessible in lower-energy heavy-ion collisions. The probability distribution for DCC domains as a function of chiral angle provides an initial condition for future studies of domain growth and field evolution.
