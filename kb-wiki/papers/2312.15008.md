# Toward Initial Conditions of Conserved Charges

> At top collider energies where baryon stopping is negligible, the initial state of heavy ion collisions is overall charge neutral and predominantly composed of gluons. Nevertheless, there can also be significant local fluctuations of the baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge densities about zero, perturbatively corresponding to the production of quark/antiquark pairs. These previously ignored local charge fluctuations can permit the study of charge diffusion in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), even at top collider energies. In this paper we present a new model denoted \code{iccing} (Initial Conserved Charges in Nuclear Geometry) which can reconstruct the initial conditions of conserved charges in the QGP by sampling a ($g \rightarrow q\bar{q}$) splitting probability over the initial energy density. We find that the new charge distributions generally differ from the bulk energy density; in particular, the strangeness distribution is significantly more eccentric than standard bulk observables and appears to be associated with the geometry of hot spots in the initial state. The new information provided by these conserved charges opens the door to studying a wealth of new charge- and flavor-dependent correlations in the initial state and ultimately the charge transport parameters of the QGP.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new model called iccing (Initial Conserved Charges in Nuclear Geometry) that can reconstruct initial conditions of baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge densities in the quark-gluon plasma by sampling a gluon-to-quark-antiquark splitting probability over the initial energy density, even at top collider energies where the overall system is charge neutral and dominated by gluons.

## Method

The iccing model samples a perturbative g → qq̄ splitting probability over the initial energy density profile from a TRENTo initial state model, generating event-by-event distributions of baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge densities. The conserved charges are assigned to quark flavors (up, down, strange) according to their quantum numbers, with the splitting probability determined by the local energy density and the quark masses. The resulting charge distributions are then compared to the bulk energy density geometry through eccentricity calculations and spatial correlations.

## Key numbers

- Strangeness distribution eccentricity is significantly larger than standard bulk observables, with the strangeness distribution associated with the geometry of hot spots in the initial state
- The light quark masses used are: up quark 2.16 MeV, down quark 4.67 MeV, strange quark 93.4 MeV (current quark masses from Particle Data Group)
- The crossover phase transition temperature from lattice QCD is T = (154 ± 9) MeV
- The field has converged on a shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of η/s ≈ 0.1 ± 0.1 and a maximum bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio of ζ/s ≈ 0.15 ± 0.15

## Context

This work extends the standard initial state modeling of heavy-ion collisions, which previously focused only on energy or entropy density profiles from models like TRENTo, by adding conserved charge fluctuations that were previously ignored at top collider energies. The model enables future studies of charge diffusion in the quark-gluon plasma and charge- and flavor-dependent correlations, building on the established framework of event-by-event viscous relativistic hydrodynamics that has been successful in describing experimental data from LHC and RHIC (Gardim 2012, Heinz 2013, Shen 2015, Niemi 2015, Bernhard 2016).
