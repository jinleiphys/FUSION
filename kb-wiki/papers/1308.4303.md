# Dyson-Schwinger study of chiral density waves in QCD

> The formation of inhomogeneous chiral condensates in QCD matter at nonzero density and temperature is investigated for the first time with Dyson-Schwinger equations. We consider two massless quark flavors in a so-called chiral density wave, where scalar and pseudoscalar quark condensates vary sinusoidally along one spatial dimension. We find that the inhomogeneous region covers the major part of the spinodal region of the first-order phase transition which is present when the analysis is restricted to homogeneous phases. The triple point where the inhomogeneous phase meets the homogeneous phases with broken and restored chiral symmetry, respectively, coincides, within numerical accuracy, with the critical point of the homogeneous calculation. At zero temperature, the inhomogeneous phase seems to extend to arbitrarily high chemical potentials, as long as pairing effects are not taken into account.

## Key claim

Using Dyson-Schwinger equations for the first time to study inhomogeneous chiral condensates in QCD, the authors find that the inhomogeneous phase (a chiral density wave) covers the major part of the spinodal region of the first-order phase transition found in homogeneous calculations; the triple point where the inhomogeneous phase meets the homogeneous phases coincides, within numerical accuracy, with the critical point of the homogeneous calculation, and at zero temperature the inhomogeneous phase appears to extend to arbitrarily high chemical potentials as long as pairing effects are not taken into account.

## Method

The authors solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the dressed quark propagator in QCD with two massless quark flavors, using a truncation scheme where the dressed quark-gluon vertex has the same structure as the bare vertex with a phenomenological dressing function, and the gluon propagator is based on a parametrization of lattice Yang-Mills data corrected for quark effects via hard-thermal-loop/hard-dense-loop approximation. They make an ansatz for the inverse quark propagator that includes a chiral density wave (CDW) where scalar and pseudoscalar condensates vary sinusoidally along one spatial dimension with wave number Q, containing 10 dressing functions, and invert the propagator analytically due to the block structure of the CDW. The wave number Q is fixed by requiring stationarity of the effective action with respect to Q, which yields a gap equation involving the dressing function e5(p).

## Key numbers

* At T=0, the inhomogeneous solution exists above mu = 350 MeV and becomes favored above a critical chemical potential of about 365 MeV, where the wave number jumps from zero to a finite value and the mass amplitude drops discontinuously but remains nonzero.
* At mu = 370 MeV, the mass amplitude becomes zero at about T = 29 MeV, where a second-order phase transition to the restored phase takes place.
* At T=0, the critical chemical potential for the inhomogeneous phase is about 10 MeV lower than in the homogeneous case.
* The homogeneous first-order phase boundary at low temperatures (green dotted line) is completely covered by the inhomogeneous phase within numerical resolution.

## Context

This work extends the study of inhomogeneous chiral condensates from effective models like the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model (Nickel 2009) to strong-coupling QCD using Dyson-Schwinger equations, which had previously been applied only to homogeneous phases (Fischer 2009, 2010, 2011, 2012). The phase diagram shows great similarities with the NJL model, but a qualitative difference is that in the NJL model the inhomogeneous phase ends at some upper critical chemical potential, whereas here it seems to extend to arbitrarily high mu at T=0. The authors note that at high densities, inhomogeneous chiral symmetry breaking should become disfavored against color superconductivity (Shuster 1999), which they have recently studied in a similar framework (Muller 2013) and plan to extend to include pairing effects.
