# Reducible chiral four-body interactions in nuclear matter

> The method of unitary transformations generates five classes of leading-order reducible chiral four-nucleon interactions which involve pion-exchanges and a spin-spin contact-term. Their first-order contributions to the energy per particle of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter are evaluated in detail. For most of the closed four-loop diagrams the occurring integrals over four Fermi-spheres can be reduced to easily manageable one- or two-parameter integrals. One observes substantial cancelations among the different contributions arising from 2-ring and 1-ring diagrams. Altogether, the net attraction generated by the chiral four-nucleon interaction does not exceed values of $-1.3$\,MeV for densities $ρ<2ρ_0$.

# Reducible chiral four-body interactions in nuclear matter

## Key claim

The net attraction generated by the leading-order reducible chiral four-nucleon interaction in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter does not exceed values of -1.3 MeV for densities below twice nuclear saturation density, but this bound holds only for the whole range of the spin-spin contact-coupling constant |C_T| < 0.5 fm^2; for the specific value C_T = -0.45 fm^2 the net attraction reaches -1.7 MeV at ρ = 0.36 fm^{-3}.

## Method

The authors use the method of unitary transformations to construct five classes (VII, V, IV, II, I) of leading-order reducible chiral four-nucleon interactions involving pion-exchanges and a spin-spin contact-term. They evaluate the first-order Hartree-Fock contributions to the energy per particle of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter by computing closed 2-ring and 1-ring four-loop diagrams, reducing most integrals over four Fermi spheres to one- or two-parameter integrals depending on the dimensionless ratio k_f/m_π.

## Key numbers

- Net attraction from reducible chiral four-nucleon interaction in symmetric nuclear matter: does not exceed -1.3 MeV for densities ρ < 2ρ_0 = 0.32 fm^{-3} (for |C_T| < 0.5 fm^2)
- For C_T = -0.45 fm^2, net attraction reaches -1.7 MeV at ρ = 0.36 fm^{-3}
- For pure neutron matter, attractive energy per particle reaches about -2.1 MeV at neutron density ρ_n = 0.4 fm^{-3}
- Spin-spin contact-coupling values used: C_T = 0.22 fm^2 and C_T = -0.45 fm^2 (taken from Darmstadt group, ref. [darmstadt])
- Physical parameters: g_A = 1.29 (axial-vector coupling), f_π = 92.4 MeV (pion decay constant), m_π = 135 MeV (neutral pion mass)
- For comparison, Δ(1232)-isobar induced four-nucleon interactions give repulsive contribution of 2.35 MeV at saturation density ρ_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}, about 10 times larger at ρ = 2ρ_0

## Context

This work extends the analytical calculation of chiral four-body interactions in nuclear matter performed by Kaiser (2012) for irreducible four-nucleon interactions, which gave contributions below 0.6 MeV for densities ρ < 0.4 fm^{-3}. It also complements the Δ(1232)-isobar mediated four-nucleon interactions studied by Kaiser (2012) that produced a moderately repulsive contribution of 2.35 MeV at saturation density. The results agree perfectly with those of the Darmstadt group (Krueger et al., 2013) when the regulator function is set to unity. The chiral four-nucleon correlations studied here are at least one order of magnitude smaller than those from the strongly coupled πNΔ-system with its small mass-gap of 293 MeV.
