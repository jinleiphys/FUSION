# The $p(γ,K^+)Λ$ reaction: consistent high-spin interactions and Bayesian inference of its resonance content

> A Bayesian analysis of the world's $p(γ,K^+)Λ$ data is presented. We adopt a Regge-plus-resonance framework featuring consistent interactions for nucleon resonances up to spin $J = 5/2$. The power of the momentum dependence of the consistent interaction structure rises with the spin of the resonance. This leads to unphysical structures in the energy dependence of the computed cross sections when the short-distance physics is cut off with standard hadronic form factors. A plausible, spin-dependent modification of the hadronic form factor is proposed which suppresses the unphysical artifacts. Next, we evaluate all possible combinations of 11 candidate resonances. The best model is selected from the 2048 model variants by calculating the Bayesian evidence values against the world's $p(γ,K^+)Λ$ data. From the proposed selection of 11 resonances, we find that the following nucleon resonances have the highest probability of contributing to the reaction: $S_{11}(1535)$, $S_{11}(1650)$, $F_{15}(1680)$, $P_{13}(1720)$, $D_{13}(1900)$, $P_{13}(1900)$, $P_{11}(1900)$, and $F_{15}(2000)$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) framework with consistent (gauge-invariant) interactions for nucleon resonances up to spin J = 5/2, combined with a novel spin-dependent hadronic form factor, can eliminate unphysical structures in the computed cross sections that plagued earlier models; Bayesian model selection then identifies the most probable set of contributing resonances from 2048 possible combinations of 11 candidates.

## Method

The authors adopt a Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) framework where the background is described by Reggeized K+(494) and K*+(892) trajectories in the t-channel, and the s-channel exchanges are modeled using consistent (unconstrained Rarita-Schwinger gauge-invariant) interaction Lagrangians for nucleon resonances up to spin 5/2. A novel spin-dependent "multidipole-Gauss" hadronic form factor is introduced to regularize the amplitudes without introducing unphysical artifacts. All 2048 possible combinations of 11 candidate resonances are optimized against the world's p(γ,K+)Λ data (6148 data points), and the Bayesian evidence (corrected for systematic errors) is calculated for each variant to select the most probable model.

## Key numbers

- 11 candidate resonances were considered, including established resonances S11(1535), S11(1650), D15(1675), F15(1680), D13(1700), P11(1710), and P13(1720); less-established resonances P13(1900) and F15(2000); and missing resonances D13(1900) and P11(1900).
- 2048 model variants (2^11) were evaluated against 6148 data points.
- The model with the highest Bayesian evidence (RPR-2011) excludes D15(1675), D13(1700), and P11(1710).
- The 8 resonances with positive probability ratios (most probable to contribute) are: S11(1535), S11(1650), F15(1680), P13(1720), D13(1900), P13(1900), P11(1900), and F15(2000).
- The RPR-2011 predictions are shown at photon lab energies of 1275, 1575, 1875, and 2175 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier RPR-2007 model of Corthals (2007), which used contact-invariant Lagrangians for spin-3/2 resonances with off-shell parameters and a common Gaussian hadronic form factor, and which produced unphysical bumps at backward kaon angles. The new consistent interaction formalism was developed by Vrancx (2011) and eliminates the need for off-shell parameters. The Bayesian analysis resolves disagreements between previous analyses (summarized in Table I of DeCruz (2011)) about which resonances contribute to the p(γ,K+)Λ reaction. The results enable more reliable extraction of nucleon resonance properties from kaon photoproduction data and can be applied to other strangeness-production reactions.
