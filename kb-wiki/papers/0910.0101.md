# $η/s$ at finite coupling

> We compute the ratio of the coefficient of shear viscosity to entropy density at finite coupling and at zero chemical potential using holographic duality up to ten derivative terms in the low energy effective 5-dimensional action, of a specific kind, which may or may not be connected to the supersymmetric completion of Type IIB theory. The result suggests that this ratio can be positive only for the 8th derivative term even with the form of that term in the action as ${{\cal C}^{ij}}_{kl}{{\cal C}^{kl}}_{mn}{{\cal C}^{mn}}_{rs}{{\cal C}^{rs}}_{ij}$, where ${\cal C}$ is the Weyl tensor.

## Key claim

The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density, η/s, is computed at finite coupling using holographic duality up to ten-derivative terms in a specific 5-dimensional effective action built only from contractions of the Weyl tensor. The result suggests that the ratio can remain above the KSS bound of 1/(4π) only for the eighth-derivative term (fourth power of the Weyl tensor) with a specific index contraction, provided the coupling λ₄ is positive; all other terms up to tenth order reduce the ratio below the bound for positive couplings.

## Method

The author uses the holographic approach of Iqbal and Liu (2009) to compute shear viscosity from the zero-frequency limit of the metric fluctuation in the scalar channel, evaluating the momentum at the horizon. The entropy density is computed using Wald's formula for the entropy of a black hole horizon. Both quantities are calculated to leading linear order in each coupling λᵢ (i = 2,...,5) for an action containing the Einstein-Hilbert term, a cosmological constant, and successive powers of the Weyl tensor of the form 𝒞ⁱʲ_kl 𝒞ᵏˡ_mn ... 𝒞ᵘᵛ_ij, without needing the λᵢ-corrected metric solution because the spatial metric component receives no correction at linear order.

## Key numbers

- For the Weyl-squared term (λ₂): η/s = (1/(4π)) [1 − 8 λ₂/L²]
- For the cubic Weyl term (λ₃): η/s = (1/(4π)) [1 − 384 λ₃/L⁴]
- For the quartic Weyl term (λ₄): η/s = (1/(4π)) [1 + 960 λ₄/L⁶]; this is the only term that increases the ratio above 1/(4π) for positive coupling
- For the quintic Weyl term (λ₅): η/s = (1/(4π)) [1 − (35840/9) λ₅/L⁸]
- The combined result to leading order: η/s = (1/(4π)) [1 − 8 λ₂/L² − 384 λ₃/L⁴ + 960 λ₄/L⁶ − (35840/9) λ₅/L⁸]
- For the Gauss-Bonnet term (a different structure at the λ₂ order): η = (r₀³/(16π G L³)) [1 − 8 λ₂/L²], s = (r₀³/(4 G L³)), giving the same ratio η/s = (1/(4π)) [1 − 8 λ₂/L²]

## Context

This work extends calculations of η/s in holographic theories beyond the leading large-N, infinite-coupling limit where the ratio equals 1/(4π) (Kovtun, Son, and Starinets 2005). It builds on prior computations for 𝒩=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills at finite temperature (Policastro, Son, and Starinets 2001) and on studies showing that η/s can fall below 1/(4π) at finite 't Hooft coupling (Buchel, Liu, and Starinets 2005; Kats and Petrov 2009). The results for λ₂ and λ₃ match those of Buchel, Myers, and Sinha (2009), while the λ₄ and λ₅ coefficients are new. The work enables further investigation of whether micro-causality constraints or convergence of the derivative expansion can restrict the couplings λᵢ, and whether the KSS bound can be violated by higher-derivative corrections.
