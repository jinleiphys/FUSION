# Energy and momentum deposited into a QCD medium by a jet shower

> Hard partons moving through a dense QCD medium lose energy by radiative emissions and elastic scatterings. Deposition of the radiative contribution into the medium requires rescattering of the radiated gluons. We compute the total energy loss and its deposition into the medium self-consistently within the same formalism, assuming perturbative interaction between probe and medium. The same transport coefficients that control energy loss of the hard parton determine how the energy is deposited into the medium; this allows a parameter free calculation of the latter once the former have been computed or extracted from experimental energy loss data. We compute them for a perturbative medium in hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation. Assuming that the deposited energy-momentum is equilibrated after a short relaxation time, we compute the medium's hydrodynamical response and obtain a conical pattern that is strongly enhanced by showering.

## Key claim

The paper presents a first-principles perturbative QCD calculation of the energy and transverse momentum deposited into a QCD medium by a jet shower, where the radiative and elastic energy loss, virtuality evolution, gluon showering, and energy deposition are all computed consistently within the same formalism, assuming perturbative interaction between probe and medium and a short thermalization time for the deposited energy.

## Method

The authors derive medium-modified evolution equations for the energy deposition and transverse momentum deposition by a hard parton, analogous to DGLAP evolution equations for fragmentation functions, using in-medium splitting functions that account for interference between gluon emission at different points along the parton's trajectory. They solve these coupled evolution equations for quark and gluon jets, starting from an initial condition at virtuality μ₀ ≈ 4T where the deposited energy and transverse momentum are obtained from hard thermal loop (HTL) expressions for the transport coefficients ê and q̂, and then compute the linear hydrodynamic response of a homogeneous static medium to the deposited energy-momentum source.

## Key numbers

- Temperature of the partonic plasma: T = 300 MeV, with 3 quark flavors
- Minimum virtuality scale for initial condition: μ₀ = 4T
- Sound attenuation length: Γ_s = η/(sT) with specific shear viscosity η/s = 1/(2π)
- Thermalization time delay: τ_rel = 1/m_D (where m_D is the Debye screening mass)
- Hydrodynamic response evaluated at t = 5 fm/c after parton creation
- Showering leads to an enhancement by a factor of 3 in the overall magnitude of the hydrodynamic response compared to a single non-radiating parton
- Partons with energy E < 4T are considered to become part of the thermal medium

## Context

This work extends prior pQCD energy loss calculations (Majumder 2007) by computing the energy deposition profile on the same footing as the energy loss, and improves upon the semi-phenomenological approach of Neufeld and Müller (2009) which used the differential single gluon emission spectrum but lacked information about virtuality evolution and gluon multiplication by showering. The results enable more realistic calculations of the medium's hydrodynamic response to jet energy deposition, showing that showering strongly enhances the Mach cone structure formed in the medium.
