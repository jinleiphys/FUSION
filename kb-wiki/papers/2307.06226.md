# In-medium gluon radiation spectrum with all-order resummation of multiple scatterings in longitudinally evolving media

> Over the past years, there has been a sustained effort to systematically enhance our understanding of medium-induced emissions occurring in the quark-gluon plasma, driven by the ultimate goal of advancing our comprehension of jet quenching phenomena. To ensure meaningful comparisons between these new calculations and experimental data, it becomes crucial to model the interplay between the radiation process and the evolution of the medium parameters, typically described by a hydrodynamical simulation. This step presents particular challenges when dealing with calculations involving the resummation of multiple scatterings, which have been shown to be necessary for achieving an accurate description of the in-medium emission process. In this paper, we extend our numerical calculations of the fully-resummed gluon spectrum to account for longitudinally expanding media. This new implementation allows us to quantitatively assess the accuracy of previously proposed scaling laws that establish a correspondence between an expanding medium and a ``static equivalent''. Additionally, we show that such scaling laws yield significantly improved results when the static reference case is replaced by an expanding medium with the temperature following a simple power-law decay. Such correspondence will enable the application of numerical calculations of medium-induced energy loss in realistic evolving media for a broader range of phenomenological studies.

## Key claim

The paper extends numerical calculations of the fully-resummed gluon radiation spectrum (beyond the harmonic oscillator approximation) to longitudinally expanding media, and shows that previously proposed scaling laws mapping expanding media to a "static equivalent" yield sizeable discrepancies at low gluon energies, but that replacing the static reference with a power-law temperature decay profile significantly improves the matching accuracy.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the differential equations for the transverse momentum broadening kernel and emission kernel (eqs. 4 and 5) using a Yukawa parton-medium interaction model (Gyulassy-Wang model), extending their previous brick-medium framework to accommodate time-dependent linear density \(n(t)\) and screening mass \(\mu^2(t)\). They extract temperature profiles \(T_{\rm hydro}(\xi(t))\) along straight-line trajectories from a 2+1 viscous hydrodynamic simulation of Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02\) TeV, and compare the fully-resummed spectra computed along these trajectories to spectra obtained via scaling laws that map the evolving medium to either a static brick or a power-law profile.

## Key numbers

* For a central trajectory in 0-10% centrality Pb-Pb collisions, using average values of medium parameters with \(\chi=5\) (left panel) or \(\chi=10\) (right panel), the static spectrum overestimates the full trajectory result, especially at large \(\omega/\bar{\omega}_c\).
* The high-energy tail of the spectrum follows \(\omega \, dI^{\rm med}/d\omega \approx (\alpha_s C_R/2) (\chi \bar{\omega}_c)/\omega\) for the static case, and \(\omega \, dI^{\rm med}/d\omega \approx (\alpha_s C_R)/(2\omega) \int_0^L ds \, s \, n(s) \, \mu^2(s)\) for evolving media.
* The scaling law matching the high-energy tail requires \(\chi \bar{\omega}_c = \int_{\tau_{\rm hydro}}^{L+\tau_{\rm hydro}} dt \, t \, n_{\rm hydro}(t) \, \mu^2_{\rm hydro}(t)\).
* For \(\chi=5\) and \(\bar{R} \rightarrow \infty\) (left panel) or \(\bar{R}=1000\) (right panel), the static spectrum matched via the scaling laws accurately reproduces the high-\(\omega\) tails but shows significant discrepancies at lower gluon energies.
* The hydrodynamic simulation uses an initial proper time \(\tau_{\rm hydro}=1\) fm/c, freeze-out temperature \(T_f=140\) MeV, and shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s=0.08\).

## Context

This work extends the numerical framework of Andres et al. (2020) for fully-resummed medium-induced gluon spectra from static bricks to longitudinally expanding media, enabling tests of the scaling laws originally proposed by Salgado and Wiedemann (2002, 2003) for the harmonic oscillator approximation and first-order opacity. The paper demonstrates that these scaling laws fail at low gluon energies when applied to realistic hydrodynamic trajectories, and proposes an improved matching to power-law temperature profiles, which will allow pre-computation of spectra for efficient use in phenomenological studies of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions.
