# Further Development of the Improved QMD Model and its Applications to Fusion Reaction near Barrier

> The Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics model is further developed by introducing new parameters in interaction potential energy functional based on Skyrme interaction of SkM$^{*}$ and SLy series. The properties of ground states of selected nuclei can be reproduced very well. The Coulomb barriers for a series of reaction systems are studied and compared with the results of the proximity potential. The fusion excitation functions for a series of fusion reactions are calculated and the results are in good agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim

The Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics (ImQMD) model is further developed into a version called ImQMD-II by introducing new parameters in the interaction potential energy functional based on the Skyrme interactions SkM* and SLy series, with the specific aim of increasing the time over which an individual nucleus remains stable without nucleon emission to about several thousand fm/c, which is necessary for studying fusion reactions of heavy nuclei.

## Method

The authors modified the parameters of the potential energy density functional in the ImQMD model, which includes terms for volume, surface, symmetry, and a new small correction (tau) term, based on the Skyrme forces SkM* and SLy series. A new parameter set (IQ1) was determined by fitting the ground state properties of selected nuclei (208Pb, 90Zr, 40Ca, 16O) and fusion cross sections for 40Ca+48Ca and 40Ca+90,96Zr. The model then uses Hamiltonian equations of motion to propagate nucleons under the self-consistently generated mean field, with a phase space occupation constraint to approximate antisymmetrization, to simulate fusion reactions and calculate Coulomb barriers and fusion excitation functions.

## Key numbers

- The new parameter set IQ1 includes: α = -310.0 MeV, β = 258.0 MeV, γ = 7/6, g0 = 19.8 MeV fm², gτ = 9.5 MeV, η = 2/3, Cs = 32.0 MeV, κs = 0.08 fm², and ρ0 = 0.165 fm⁻³.
- The binding energy of 208Pb calculated with IQ1 is 7.77 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 7.87 MeV; the RMS charge radius is 5.51 fm, compared to the experimental 5.50 fm.
- The binding energy of 90Zr calculated with IQ1 is 8.71 MeV, matching the experimental value of 8.71 MeV; the RMS charge radius is 4.25 fm, compared to the experimental 4.27 fm.
- The binding energy of 40Ca calculated with IQ1 is 8.65 MeV, compared to the experimental 8.55 MeV; the RMS charge radius is 3.44 fm, compared to the experimental 3.49 fm.
- The binding energy of 16O calculated with IQ1 is 8.23 MeV, compared to the experimental 7.98 MeV; the RMS charge radius is 2.72 fm, compared to the experimental 2.73 fm.
- The bound nuclei evolve stably without spurious emission for a period of time of about 3000 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous ImQMD model (Wang 2002, 2003) by adopting parameters based on modern Skyrme forces (SkM* and SLy series) to improve the stability of individual nuclei for the long timescales (thousands of fm/c) required for heavy-ion fusion simulations. The model successfully reproduces Coulomb barriers in agreement with the proximity potential of Myers (2000) and fusion excitation functions for systems including 40Ca+48Ca, 40Ca+90,96Zr, and the neutron-rich system 132Sn+64Ni, where coupled-channel calculations (Liang 2003) significantly under-predicted sub-barrier fusion cross sections. This enables future microscopic studies of the capture process in the synthesis of superheavy elements.
