# Spin polarization and alignment in heavy-ion collisions

> We report the measurements of global spin polarization of hyperons ($P_Λ$) from FAIR, RHIC and LHC energies. The $P_Λ$ continue to follow an increasing trend at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm NN}}$ < 7.7 GeV indicating that enormous vorticity prevail even in hadronic dominant region. New measurements of local spin polarization ($P_{z,2}$) from isobar collisions follow the same pattern observed in previous RHIC and LHC energies. An observable motivated by predicted baryonic Spin Hall Effect, the net local polarization ($P_{z,2}^{\mathrm{net}}$), shows negative value in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm NN}}$ = 19.6 and 27 GeV. The first $P_{z}$ measurement with respect to third order harmonic in 200 GeV RHIC isobar collisions show a non-zero sine modulation, indicating the presence of complex vortical structure in the medium. Surprising new patterns of spin alignment ($ρ_{00}$) for various vector mesons ($K^{*0}$, $K^{*\pm}$, $ϕ$ and $J/ψ$) emerged at both RHIC and LHC energies. The large deviation in $ρ_{00}$ of several species poses challenge to the theory.

## Key claim

The global spin polarization of Λ hyperons continues to increase monotonically at center-of-mass energies below 7.7 GeV, indicating that enormous vorticity persists even in the hadron-dominated region, which is surprising because it is an open question how hadron gas can support such vorticity.

## Method

The global spin polarization of Λ and anti-Λ hyperons was measured via their parity-violating weak decays into daughter baryons, using the angular distribution of the decay products in the parent hyperon's rest frame. Local spin polarization with respect to second and third order flow harmonics was extracted from isobar (Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr) collisions at 200 GeV and Au+Au collisions at 19.6 and 27 GeV. Spin alignment of vector mesons (K*0, K*±, φ, and J/ψ) was quantified by measuring the ρ00 element of the spin density matrix from the angular distribution of decay daughters relative to the polarization axis.

## Key numbers

- Global Λ polarization reaches about 5% at the lowest beam energies (2.4 GeV), following a monotonically increasing trend from 5.02 TeV down to 2.4 GeV
- The vorticity extracted from polarization data averaged over all RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies is (9 ± 1) × 10^21 /s
- In 2007, the STAR experiment set an upper limit on PΛ of less than 2% in 200 GeV Au+Au collisions
- Net local polarization Pz,2^net in mid-central Au+Au collisions at 19.6 and 27 GeV shows a negative value, consistent with absence of baryonic Spin Hall Effect
- First measurement of Pz with respect to third order harmonic in 200 GeV isobar collisions shows a non-zero sine modulation
- K*0 ρ00 in mid-central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies is significantly smaller than 1/3 in the low pT region, while φ meson ρ00 is consistent with 1/3 within uncertainty
- φ meson ρ00 from RHIC Beam Energy Scan is significantly larger than 1/3 in mid-central collisions, while K*0 ρ00 is consistent with 1/3
- K*± ρ00 is observed to be larger than K*0 ρ00 in isobar collisions, opposite to naive expectation from magnetic moment differences
- J/ψ ρ00 at forward rapidity in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV is larger than 1/3

## Context

This work extends the measurements of global hyperon polarization from the STAR experiment at RHIC (2007, Nature 2017) and ALICE at LHC (2018) down to fixed-target energies from STAR and HADES, and presents new local polarization and spin alignment results from isobar collisions. The measurements challenge existing hydrodynamic and transport models that successfully describe global polarization but fail to explain the sign of local polarization (the "sign-puzzle"), and they motivate theoretical developments in shear-induced polarization, baryonic Spin Hall Effect, spin hydrodynamics, and spin kinetic theories. The surprising patterns in vector meson spin alignment across species and energies require new theoretical inputs beyond conventional sources such as vorticity and electromagnetic fields.
