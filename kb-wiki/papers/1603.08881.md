# Light-front representation of chiral dynamics with Delta isobar and large-N_c relations

> Transverse densities describe the spatial distribution of electromagnetic current in the nucleon at fixed light-front time. At peripheral distances b = O(M_pi^{-1}) the densities are governed by chiral dynamics and can be calculated model-independently using chiral effective field theory (EFT). Recent work has shown that the EFT results can be represented in first-quantized form, as overlap integrals of chiral light-front wave functions describing the transition of the nucleon to soft-pion-nucleon intermediate states, resulting in a quantum-mechanical picture of the peripheral transverse densities. We now extend this representation to include intermediate states with Delta isobars and implement relations based on the large-N_c limit of QCD. We derive the wave function overlap formulas for the Delta contributions to the peripheral transverse densities by way of a three-dimensional reduction of relativistic chiral EFT expressions. Our procedure effectively maintains rotational invariance and avoids the ambiguities with higher-spin particles in the light-front time-ordered approach. We study the interplay of pi-N and pi-Delta intermediate states in the quantum-mechanical picture of the densities in a transversely polarized nucleon. We show that the correct N_c-scaling of the charge and magnetization densities emerges as the result of the particular combination of currents generated by intermediate states with degenerate N and Delta. The off-shell behavior of the chiral EFT is summarized in contact terms and can be studied easily. The methods developed here can be applied to other peripheral densities and to moments of the nucleon's generalized parton distributions.

## Key claim

The paper extends the light-front representation of chiral dynamics to include \(\Delta\) isobar intermediate states in the peripheral transverse densities of the nucleon, showing that the correct large-\(N_c\) scaling of charge and magnetization densities emerges from the combination of currents generated by intermediate states with degenerate \(N\) and \(\Delta\), without requiring explicit off-shell contact terms.

## Method

The authors perform a three-dimensional reduction of relativistically invariant chiral effective field theory (EFT) expressions for the electromagnetic current matrix element, rewriting them as overlap integrals of chiral light-front wave functions for \(N \rightarrow \pi N\) and \(N \rightarrow \pi \Delta\) transitions. They use the Rarita-Schwinger formulation for the spin-3/2 \(\Delta\) propagator with a specific off-shell extension, and implement large-\(N_c\) relations by assuming standard scaling \(M_\Delta - M_N = O(N_c^{-1})\) and \(M_\pi = O(N_c^0)\), then studying the interplay of \(\pi N\) and \(\pi \Delta\) intermediate states in the quantum-mechanical picture of transversely polarized nucleon densities.

## Key numbers

- The \(\pi N\Delta\) coupling constant is taken as \(g_{\pi N \Delta} = 20.22\) (empirical value).
- The axial coupling is \(g_A = 1.26\) and the pion decay constant is \(F_\pi = 93\ \text{MeV}\) (physical values used in LO chiral EFT).
- The peripheral densities decay as \(\exp(-2 M_\pi b)\) at large distances \(b = O(M_\pi^{-1})\), with \(M_\pi\) the pion mass.
- The \(N\)-\(\Delta\) mass splitting scales as \(M_\Delta - M_N = O(N_c^{-1})\) in the large-\(N_c\) limit.

## Context

This work extends the light-front representation of peripheral transverse densities previously developed for chiral EFT with nucleons only (Granados and Weiss, 2015) to include \(\Delta\) isobar intermediate states. It builds on dispersive studies of the \(\Delta\) contribution to peripheral densities (Granados et al., 2013) and provides a mechanical explanation for the large-\(N_c\) scaling relations that were observed but not explained in that earlier work. The methods enable future applications to other peripheral densities and to moments of the nucleon's generalized parton distributions, including spin structure.
