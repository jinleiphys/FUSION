# Nuclear Matter Equation of State in the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock Approach and Standard Skyrme Energy-Density Functionals

> The equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter as well as the neutron and proton effective masses and their partial-wave and spin-isospin decomposition are analyzed within the Brueckner--Hartree--Fock approach. Theoretical uncertainties for all these quantities are estimated by using several phase-shift-equivalent nucleon-nucleon forces together with two types of three-nucleon forces, phenomenological and microscopic. It is shown that the choice of the three-nucleon force plays an important role above saturation density, leading to different density dependencies of the energy per particle. These results are compared to the standard form of the Skyrme energy-density functional and we find that it is not possible to reproduce the BHF predictions in the $(S,T)$ channels in symmetric and neutron matter above saturation density, already at the level of the two-body interaction, and even more including the three-body interaction.

## Key claim

The standard Skyrme energy-density functional cannot reproduce the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock predictions for the (S,T) channels in symmetric and neutron matter above saturation density, even at the two-body interaction level, and the discrepancy grows when three-body interactions are included. The choice of three-nucleon force (phenomenological versus microscopic) significantly affects the density dependence of the energy per particle above saturation density.

## Method

The authors performed Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations for asymmetric nuclear matter using several phase-shift-equivalent nucleon-nucleon (NN) forces combined with two types of three-nucleon forces (3NF): phenomenological and microscopic. They computed the equation of state, neutron and proton effective masses, and their partial-wave and spin-isospin decompositions. Theoretical uncertainties were estimated by comparing results across the different force combinations. The BHF results were then compared to the standard form of the Skyrme energy-density functional.

## Key numbers

- Saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter were computed using multiple NN forces plus 3NF combinations, with the energy per particle evaluated as a function of density up to several times saturation density.
- Neutron and proton effective masses were extracted from the BHF self-energy at the Fermi surface for various densities and asymmetries.
- The partial-wave and spin-isospin (S,T) decomposition of the BHF energy per particle was compared channel-by-channel to the Skyrme functional predictions.
- The density dependence of the energy per particle above saturation density (approximately 0.16 fm^-3) was found to be strongly dependent on the choice of three-nucleon force.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing program of microscopic nuclear matter calculations using the BHF approach, which has been developed by Baldo (1999) and others, and compares directly to the phenomenological Skyrme energy-density functional framework widely used in nuclear structure calculations (e.g., the work of Chabanat et al., 1998). The study enables improved constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry energy and effective masses for neutron star modeling, while also highlighting the limitations of standard Skyrme functionals in reproducing microscopic BHF results above saturation density. The findings point to the need for extended or modified Skyrme parametrizations that can better capture the spin-isospin channel structure from microscopic theory.
