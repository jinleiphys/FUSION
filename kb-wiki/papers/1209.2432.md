# Neutrality of a magnetized two-flavor quark superconductor

> We investigate the effect of electric and color charge neutrality on the two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase of cold and dense quark matter in presence of constant external magnetic fields and at moderate baryon densities. Within the framework of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, we study the inter-dependent evolution of the quark's BCS gap and constituent mass with increasing density and magnetic field. While confirming previous results derived for the highly magnetized 2SC phase with color neutrality alone, we obtain new results as a consequence of imposing charge neutrality. In the charge neutral gapless 2SC phase (g2SC), a large magnetic field drives the color superconducting phase transition to a crossover, while the chiral phase transition is first order. At larger diquark-to-scalar coupling ratio $G_D/G_S$, where the 2SC phase is preferred, we see hints of the Clogston-Chandrasekhar limit at a very large value of the magnetic field ($B\sim 10^{19}$G), but this limit is strongly affected by Shubnikov de Haas-van Alphen oscillations of the gap, indicating the transition to a domain-like state.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how imposing both electric and color charge neutrality, rather than color neutrality alone, modifies the behavior of the two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase in cold dense quark matter under strong external magnetic fields, finding that in the charge-neutral gapless 2SC (g2SC) phase a large magnetic field changes the color superconducting phase transition to a crossover while the chiral transition remains first order, and that the Clogston-Chandrasekhar limit appears only at very high fields (B ~ 10^19 G) but is disrupted by Shubnikov-de Haas-van Alphen oscillations.

## Method

The authors employ a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for two quark flavors with scalar and diquark interactions, introducing auxiliary bosonic fields via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation and working in the mean-field approximation. They impose electric and color charge neutrality by introducing chemical potentials μ_e and μ_8c, minimize the thermodynamic potential with respect to the chiral condensate (σ), diquark gap (Δ), and the two chemical potentials, and solve the resulting coupled gap equations and neutrality conditions at zero temperature using a smooth cutoff regularization. The magnetic field is incorporated through a rotated U(1) field that couples to quarks via their rotated charges, with Landau level quantization included in the momentum integrals.

## Key numbers

- NJL model parameters: cutoff Λ = 0.6533 GeV, scalar coupling G_S = 5.0163 GeV^{-2}, with diquark coupling G_D = ρ G_S where ρ takes values 0.75, 1.15, and 1.4
- The Clogston-Chandrasekhar limit is hinted at a magnetic field value B ∼ 10^19 G for the g2SC phase at larger diquark-to-scalar coupling ratio G_D/G_S
- The smooth cutoff parameter α is set to 0.01Λ throughout the numerical analysis
- For ρ ≳ 0.8 the 2SC phase (Δ > δμ) is found; for 0.7 ≲ ρ ≲ 0.8 the gapless 2SC (g2SC) phase (Δ < δμ) appears; for ρ ≲ 0.7 normal quark matter (Δ = 0) results

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Mandal 2009) of magnetized chiral and diquark condensates without neutrality conditions, and the color-neutrality-only analysis of Fayazbakhsh (2010) for magnetized 2SC matter. It builds on earlier unmagnetized studies of charge-neutral 2SC phases by Huang (2002) and the discovery of gapless phases by Shovkovy (2003) and Huang (2003). The results enable future comparison of different phases in neutron star cores, where magnetic fields up to 10^18 G and charge neutrality are both relevant, and suggest the possibility of strongly magnetized domains of neutral superconducting quark matter.
