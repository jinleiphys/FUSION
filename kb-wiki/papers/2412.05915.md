# Effective theory for strongly attractive one-dimensional fermions

> We study a one-dimensional system of two-component fermions in the limit of strong attractive particle-particle interactions. First, we analyze scattering in the corresponding few-body problem, which is analytically solvable via Bethe ansatz. This allows us to engineer effective interactions between the system's effective degrees of freedom: fermions and bosonic dimers (tightly bound pairs of fermions). We argue that, although these interactions are strong, the resulting effective problem can be mapped onto a weakly interacting one, paving the way for the use of perturbation theory. This finding simplifies studies of many-fermion systems under confinement that are beyond reach of state-of-the-art numerical methods. We illustrate this statement by considering an impurity atom in a Fermi gas.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a one-dimensional system of two-component fermions with strong attractive interactions can be mapped onto a weakly interacting mixture of fermions and bosonic dimers, enabling the use of perturbation theory; this mapping is valid when the dimensionless interaction strength satisfies $n/|\tilde g| \ll 1$, where the difference between exact energies and effective theory results is less than one percent when $n/|\tilde g| < 0.04$.

## Method

The authors first analyze the few-body scattering problem for three and four fermions on a line using the Bethe-ansatz solvable Gaudin-Yang model, extracting effective-range parameters for fermion-dimer and dimer-dimer scattering. They then construct effective interactions, a delta-function for even-channel scattering and a boundary condition derived from a square-well potential for odd-channel scattering, that reproduce these parameters. Finally, they demonstrate that the effective Hamiltonian can be mapped onto a non-interacting mixture of two mass-imbalanced Fermi gases in the limit $g \to -\infty$, allowing first-order perturbation theory to be applied.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of a dimer: $B_\text{D} \simeq \hbar^2 \tilde{g}^2 / (4m)$, where $\tilde{g} := m g / \hbar^2$
- Dimer size: $r_\text{D} = -1/\tilde{g}$, defined such that $\int_{-r_\text{D}}^{r_\text{D}} \Phi_\text{D}(z)^2 dz = 1 - e^{-1} \approx 0.63$
- Fermion-dimer scattering parameters: $a^\text{FD}_\text{e} = r^\text{FD}_\text{o}/2 = 3 r_\text{D}$; $r^\text{FD}_\text{e} = 0$; $a^\text{FD}_\text{o} = 0$
- Dimer-dimer scattering parameters: $a_\text{e}^\text{DD} = r_\text{D}$; $r_\text{e}^\text{DD} = 0$; odd-channel scattering amplitude $f_\text{o} = 0$
- For the impurity problem in a harmonic trap with $M \gtrsim 5$ unpaired fermions: $\lim_{|\gamma_{\mathrm{scaled}}|\to\infty} |\gamma_\text{scaled}| \Delta E_{M}/E_\text{F} \simeq 2.6$
- In the thermodynamic limit ($M \to \infty$): $\lim_{|\gamma_{\mathrm{scaled}}|\to\infty} |\gamma_\text{scaled}| \Delta E_{M}/E_\text{F} = 8/3$

## Context

This work extends the Bose-Fermi mapping of Girardeau (1960), which maps strongly repulsive one-dimensional fermions onto non-interacting fermions, to the opposite limit of strong attractions. It builds on Bethe-ansatz solutions for the Gaudin-Yang model (Yang, 1967; Gaudin, 1967) and on prior studies of dimer-dimer scattering (Mora, 2005). The effective theory enables studies of trapped spin-imbalanced systems that are beyond reach of state-of-the-art numerical methods, and can be extended to $SU(N)$ fermionic mixtures such as those realized in cold-atom experiments (Pagano, 2014).
