# Semiclassics for the QCD vacuum structure through $T^2$-compactification with the baryon-'t Hooft flux

> We study QCD vacuum structure with the topological $θ$ angle using a recently proposed semiclassical approach on $\mathbb{R}^2 \times T^2$ with the 't Hooft and baryon magnetic fluxes. Under the assumption of adiabatic continuity in this setup, the confining vacuum can be described by the dilute gas of center vortices. With this semiclassical approach, we derive the 2d effective description at small $T^2$ and successfully explain the reasonable theta dependence of the QCD vacuum: In the one-flavor QCD at $θ= π$, the $CP$ symmetry is spontaneously broken for quark mass above a critical value and restored for a subcritical mass, while the $CP$ symmetry is always spontaneously broken in the multi-flavor QCD at $θ= π$. From our semiclassical description, we discuss implications to the $4$d chiral Lagrangian and propose how the $η'$ meson should be incorporated in consistent with known global structures: The periodicity of the $η'$ should be extended from the naive one $2π$ to $2πN$. Additionally, we revisit the phase diagram of $N_f = 1+1$ and $N_f = 1+1+1$ QCD on the up and down quark mass plane, confirming and refining the existence of the $CP$-broken Dashen phase.

## Key claim

Under the assumption of adiabatic continuity for QCD compactified on \(\mathbb{R}^2 \times T^2\) with 't Hooft and baryon magnetic fluxes, the confining vacuum can be described by a dilute gas of center vortices, and this semiclassical approach successfully reproduces the expected theta dependence of the QCD vacuum, including spontaneous \(CP\) breaking at \(\theta = \pi\), but the validity of the entire analysis depends on the nontrivial working assumption that no phase transition occurs as the torus size changes.

## Method

The authors compactify \(SU(N)\) QCD with \(N_f\) fundamental quarks on \(\mathbb{R}^2 \times T^2\) at small torus size, imposing both a minimal 't Hooft flux for the gauge group and a \(U(1)_B\) magnetic flux to maintain consistency with the fundamental fermions. They identify the low-energy modes on the small torus: gluons acquire a mass gap of order \(O(1/NL)\) with no zero modes, while each flavor yields one 2d Dirac fermion zero mode. These fermions are bosonized into compact scalars, and center vortices (fractional instantons with topological charge \(\pm 1/N\) and action \(S_I/N\)) are incorporated via a dilute gas approximation, yielding a 2d effective potential for the \(\eta'\) field and the flavor scalars.

## Key numbers

* The gluon Kaluza-Klein mass gap is of order \(O(1/NL)\).
* The fractional instanton (center vortex) action is \(S_I/N\), where \(S_I = 8\pi^2/g^2\) is the instanton action.
* The topological charge of each center vortex is \(\pm 1/N\).
* The \(\eta'\) field has an extended periodicity of \(2\pi N\), not the naive \(2\pi\).
* The 2d effective potential for one-flavor QCD contains terms \(- m \mu \cos(\eta')\) and \(-2 K e^{-S_I/N} \cos((\eta' - \theta)/N)\), where \(m\) is the quark mass, \(\mu\) is a bosonization scale, and \(K e^{-S_I/N}\) is the fractional instanton weight.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical approach of Tanizaki (2022) for \(SU(N)\) Yang-Mills theory and QCD-like theories to a detailed analysis of the QCD vacuum structure with a \(\theta\) angle, including the phase diagram for one-flavor and multi-flavor QCD. It builds on the center-vortex picture of confinement (Greensite, 2011) and the 't Hooft anomaly matching approach (Gaiotto, 2017; Tanizaki, 2017), and it aims to resolve ambiguities in the chiral Lagrangian treatment of the \(\eta'\) meson (Witten, 1980; Di Vecchia, 1980) by deriving the correct \(2\pi N\) periodicity from microscopic QCD. The results enable a refined analysis of the Dashen phase in \(N_f = 1+1\) and \(N_f = 1+1+1\) QCD on the \((m_u, m_d)\) plane.
