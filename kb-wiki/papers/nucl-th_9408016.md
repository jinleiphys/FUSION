# An accurate nucleon-nucleon potential with charge-independence breaking

> We present a new high-quality nucleon-nucleon potential with explicit charge dependence and charge asymmetry, which we designate Argonne $v_{18}$. The model has a charge-independent part with fourteen operator components that is an updated version of the Argonne $v_{14}$ potential. Three additional charge-dependent and one charge-asymmetric operators are added, along with a complete electromagnetic interaction. The potential has been fit directly to the Nijmegen $pp$ and $np$ scattering data base, low-energy $nn$ scattering parameters, and deuteron binding energy. With 40 adjustable parameters it gives a $χ^{2}$ per datum of 1.09 for 4301 $pp$ and $np$ data in the range 0--350 MeV.

## Key claim

The Argonne $v_{18}$ potential achieves a $\chi^{2}$ per datum of 1.09 for 4301 $pp$ and $np$ data by including explicit charge dependence and charge asymmetry through three charge-dependent and one charge-asymmetric operator operators, plus a complete electromagnetic interaction, but the authors note this is a "compromise" between the nearly perfect $\chi^{2}=1$ of the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis and the simpler local operator structure needed for many-body calculations.

## Method

The potential was constructed by updating the Argonne $v_{14}$ charge-independent operator structure with fourteen operator components, then adding four additional operators for charge dependence ($T$, $\sigma T$, $tT$) and charge asymmetry ($\tau z$), plus a complete electromagnetic potential including Coulomb, Darwin-Foldy, vacuum polarization, and magnetic moment terms with finite-size effects. The 40 adjustable parameters were fit directly to the Nijmegen $pp$ and $np$ scattering data base (1787 $pp$ and 2514 $np$ data from 0-350 MeV), low-energy $nn$ scattering parameters from $d(\pi^{-},\gamma)nn$ experiments, and the deuteron binding energy, using a Woods-Saxon short-range core with parameters $r_{0}=0.5$ fm and $a=0.2$ fm.

## Key numbers

- Total $\chi^{2}$ of 4675 for 4301 data points (1787 $pp$ and 2514 $np$ data in 0-350 MeV range), giving $\chi^{2}$ per datum of 1.09
- $\chi^{2}(pp)=1962$ and $\chi^{2}(np)=2713$ for the full potential
- OPE coupling constant fixed at $f^{2}=0.075$, the recommended value from Nijmegen analysis
- Electromagnetic terms give 18 keV repulsion in the deuteron
- Cutoff parameter in OPE functions set at $c=2.1$ fm$^{-2}$ (slightly different from 2.0 fm$^{-2}$ in Argonne $v_{14}$)
- When the $np$ version of Argonne $v_{18}$ is confronted with $pp$ data, $\chi^{2}$ per datum rises to 4.4; when $T=1$ $pp$ part replaces $np$ part and confronted with $np$ data, $\chi^{2}$ per datum rises to 1.8
- Neutron electric form factor slope $\beta_{n}=0.0189$ fm$^{2}$ used for $np$ Coulomb term
- Dipole form factor parameter $b=4.27$ fm$^{-1}$ used for nucleon form factors

## Context

This work extends the Argonne $v_{14}$ potential (Wiringa, 1984) and Urbana $v_{14}$ potential (Lagaris, 1981) by adding explicit charge-independence breaking, addressing the problem that potentials fit only to $np$ data give poor descriptions of $pp$ data and vice versa, as shown by Stoks (1993). The potential enables more reliable construction of three-nucleon potentials for calculations of trinucleon binding energy, charge-symmetry breaking studies such as the ${^{3}}$H-${^{3}}$He mass difference (Wu, 1990) and the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly (Nolen, 1969), and many-body calculations of finite nuclei, nuclear matter, and neutron stars.
