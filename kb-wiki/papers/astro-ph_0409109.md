# Chiral Symmetry Breaking in Presence of Strong Quantizing Magnetic Fields- A Nambu-Jona-Lasino Model with Semi-Classical Approximation

> The breaking of chiral symmetry of light quarks at zero temperature in presence of strong quantizing magnetic field is studied using Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with Thomas-Fermi type semi-classical formalism. It is found that the dynamically generated light quark mass can never become zero if the Landau levels are populated and increases with the increase of magnetic field strength.

## Key claim

Using a semi-classical Thomas-Fermi type mean-field approach within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, the authors find that the dynamically generated light quark mass can never become zero if Landau levels are populated in a strong quantizing magnetic field, and that the trivial solution \(m=0\) (the Wigner phase) does not exist in this relativistic Landau diamagnetic system.

## Method

The authors construct a density matrix from negative-energy Dirac spinors in a constant magnetic field along the \(z\)-axis, then compute the vacuum energy density using the NJL chiral Hamiltonian with a contact interaction. They evaluate the kinetic term, direct interaction term, and exchange interaction term (including contributions from \(\beta\) and \(\beta\gamma_5\) traces) using orthonormality relations for Hermite polynomials and \(\gamma\)-matrix trace identities. The dynamically generated quark mass \(m\) (assumed equal for \(u\) and \(d\) quarks) is obtained by minimizing the total vacuum energy density with respect to \(m\), yielding a non-linear gap equation that has no trivial \(m=0\) solution when Landau levels are populated.

## Key numbers

- Dynamical quark mass diverges beyond magnetic field strength \(B_m \approx 10^{17}\) G (see Fig. 1).
- The critical magnetic field scale used is \(B_m^{(c)(e)} = 4.4 \times 10^{13}\) G (the electron critical field).
- Input parameters: current quark mass \(m_0 = 10\) MeV, pion mass \(m_\pi = 140\) MeV, pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV, coupling constant \(g = 10\) GeV\(^{-2}\), electron mass \(m_e = 0.5\) MeV.
- The maximum Landau quantum number for flavor \(f\) is given by \(\nu_{\rm{max}}^{(f)} = \left[ (\mu_f^2 - m^2) / (2 q_f B_m) \right]\), where \(\mu_f\) is a chemical-potential-like quantity.

## Context

This work extends the NJL model calculations of Bhaduri et al. (reference R20) and Providencia et al. (reference R21) to include strong quantizing magnetic fields, using a semi-classical formalism recently developed for dense fermionic matter in astrophysical contexts (reference R22). It contradicts the conventional non-magnetic NJL result where a trivial \(m=0\) (Wigner) solution exists, and instead finds that chiral symmetry remains spontaneously broken whenever Landau levels are populated. The results question the applicability of the bag model in the presence of strong quantizing magnetic fields, with implications for the equation of state and phase transitions in magnetar cores.
