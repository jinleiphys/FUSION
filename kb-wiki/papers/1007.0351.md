# Progress in the Neural Network Determination of Polarized Parton Distributions

> We review recent progress towards a determination of a set of polarized parton distributions from a global set of deep-inelastic scattering data based on the NNPDF methodology, in analogy with the unpolarized case. This method is designed to provide a faithful and statistically sound representation of parton distributions and their uncertainties. We show how the FastKernel method provides a fast and accurate method for solving the polarized DGLAP equations. We discuss the polarized PDF parametrizations and the physical constraints which can be imposed. Preliminary results suggest that the uncertainty on polarized PDFs, most notably the gluon, has been underestimated in previous studies.

## Key claim
The NNPDF methodology, when applied to polarized parton distributions, removes bias from fixed functional forms and suggests that the uncertainty on polarized PDFs, especially the gluon, has been underestimated in previous studies, though the results are preliminary and do not yet support quantitative conclusions.

## Method
The NNPDFpol1.0 analysis uses artificial neural networks as unbiased interpolants to parametrize four polarized PDFs (singlet, non-singlet triplet, non-singlet octet, and gluon) at an initial evolution scale of Q0^2 = 1 GeV^2. The polarized DGLAP evolution equations are solved using the FastKernel method, benchmarked against the Les Houches tables using HOPPET and PEGASUS. The fit is performed on approximately 250 inclusive polarized deep-inelastic scattering data points from CERN, SLAC, DESY, and JLAB, with kinematical cuts Q^2 >= 1 GeV^2 and W^2 >= 6.25 GeV^2 to remain in the perturbative region.

## Key numbers
- Number of data points after kinematical cuts: N_dat ~ 250, about an order of magnitude smaller than in the unpolarized case.
- Kinematical cuts applied: Q^2 >= 1 GeV^2 and W^2 >= 6.25 GeV^2.
- Each polarized PDF has 37 free parameters (2-5-3-1 neural network architecture).
- Initial evolution scale: Q0^2 = 1 GeV^2.
- Axial sum rule constraints: a_3 = 1.2670 +/- 0.0035 (triplet axial charge from weak baryon decays); a_8 = 0.585 +/- 0.025 (octet axial charge assuming exact SU(3) symmetry).
- Benchmark accuracy for polarized PDF evolution at NLO in the ZM-VFNS: relative errors range from 5.4 x 10^-6 (Delta Sigma at x=0.1) to 1.1 x 10^-2 (Delta d_V at x=0.9).

## Context
This work extends the NNPDF methodology, previously developed for unpolarized PDFs (Del Debbio 2007, Ball 2008-2010), to the polarized sector, addressing the "spin crisis" first highlighted by the EMC experiment in 1988. It contrasts with earlier polarized PDF determinations by de Florian (2009) and Leader (2006), which used fixed functional forms and likely underestimated uncertainties, especially for the gluon. The NNPDFpol1.0 set will be the first polarized PDF set determined consistently alongside its unpolarized counterpart, and future work aims to include exclusive DIS data and polarized hadronic data from RHIC without K-factor approximations.
