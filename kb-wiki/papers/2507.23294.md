# Poincaré covariant quantum molecular dynamics: a covariant description of a system of interacting wave packets

> We present a new formulation for the mean-field propagation part of the relativistic quantum molecular dynamics, simulating an $N$-body system of interacting Gaussian wave packets via Lorentz scalar and vector potentials. Covariant equations of motion are derived based on the principle of least action. These covariant equations of motion can be solved with a computational cost comparable to that of conventional noncovariant quantum molecular dynamics. Furthermore, the new equations of motion accurately estimate the density-dependent potential, as demonstrated through comparison of the forces with the numerical integration. We apply them to $N$-body systems interacting via the Skyrme-type potentials or the relativistic mean field to simulate heavy-ion collisions. Our results show that the derived equations of motion provide a robust approximation to the dynamics of the full numerical integrations.

## Key claim

The authors derive Lorentz-covariant equations of motion for relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (RQMD) that can be solved with computational cost comparable to noncovariant QMD, while providing a more accurate estimate of density-dependent potentials than conventional QMD approaches, particularly for nonlinear potentials with large exponent gamma.

## Method

The authors formulate covariant equations of motion based on the Weiss action principle (modified Hamilton's principle) for an N-body system of Gaussian wave packets interacting via Lorentz scalar and vector potentials. They derive "BUU-like" covariant equations of motion and then transform them into "QMD-like" covariant equations within the local density approximation, where the density-dependent potential is evaluated using a Monte-Carlo method with relativistic Gaussian weight. The new equations are applied to systems interacting via Skyrme-type potentials and relativistic mean-field (sigma-omega) theory, and are implemented into the JAM2 event generator for heavy-ion collision simulations.

## Key numbers

- For a two-particle system with potential U(n)=n^gamma and Gaussian width L=2.0 fm^2, the original QMD underestimates the force magnitude significantly for large gamma, while the new method (QMD2) provides good agreement with numerical integration (Fig. 1)
- For a 400-nucleon system sampled inside a sphere, the original QMD underestimates the force by about 20% for larger gamma values (gamma=1.7, 2.0), while the new method provides good approximation over a wide range of densities (Fig. 2)
- For gamma=1.2 (typical for a soft equation of state), the original QMD agrees well with the exact solution in the 400-nucleon system

## Context

This work extends the relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (RQMD) framework by Sorge (1989), Maruyama (1991), and others, addressing the known issue that traditional QMD calculations introduce an approximation in evaluating density-dependent potentials that results in a weaker gradient of the repulsive mean-field potential, as documented by the TMEP collaboration (2016-2023). The new formulation improves upon the harmonic approximation used in the relativistic Landau-Vlasov method by Fuchs (1995) and the standard QMD approximation that replaces <n^gamma> with <n>^gamma. The covariant equations of motion enable fully covariant event-by-event simulations of heavy-ion collisions when combined with the covariant cascade method developed by Nara (2023).
