# How Deep is the Antinucleon Optical Potential at FAIR energies

> The key question in the interaction of antinucleons in the nuclear medium concerns the deepness of the antinucleon-nucleus optical potential. In this work we study this task in the framework of the non-linear derivative (NLD) model which describes consistently bulk properties of nuclear matter and Dirac phenomenology of nucleon-nucleus interactions. We apply the NLD model to antinucleon interactions in nuclear matter and find a strong decrease of the vector and scalar self-energies in energy and density and thus a strong suppression of the optical potential at zero momentum and, in particular, at FAIR energies. This is in agreement with available empirical information and, therefore, resolves the issue concerning the incompatibility of G-parity arguments in relativistic mean-field (RMF) models. We conclude the relevance of our results for the future activities at FAIR.

## Key claim

The non-linear derivative (NLD) model, which incorporates explicit energy and density dependence of the mean field, predicts a strong suppression of the antinucleon-nucleus optical potential at FAIR energies, resolving the incompatibility of G-parity arguments in conventional relativistic mean-field (RMF) models without requiring ad-hoc rescaling of coupling constants.

## Method

The authors applied the non-linear derivative (NLD) model to infinite nuclear matter, deriving the Dirac equations for nucleons and antinucleons with Lorentz-vector and Lorentz-scalar self-energies that depend exponentially on the single-particle energy through a cut-off parameter Λ = 770 MeV. They computed the Schrödinger-equivalent optical potential for both protons and antiprotons at various baryon densities and kinetic energies, and estimated the imaginary part of the antiproton optical potential using a dispersion relation applied to the NLD real part.

## Key numbers

- At saturation density (ρ_sat = 0.16 fm⁻³) and zero momentum, the linear Walecka model gives an antinucleon mean-field potential of approximately −700 MeV, while the NLD model reduces this depth by almost a factor of two.
- The NLD model predicts the real part of the antiproton optical potential at zero kinetic energy and saturation density to be much softer than the Walecka model, consistent with extrapolated empirical data (filled box at zero kinetic energy in Fig. 3).
- At low densities ρ ≈ (0.005–0.02) ρ_sat, the NLD model gives Re U_opt ≈ −(6–50) MeV for antiprotons.
- The imaginary part of the antiproton optical potential at saturation density and zero kinetic energy is approximately −200 MeV, consistent with empirical data from antiprotonic atoms.
- The cut-off parameter Λ = 770 MeV is used, of typical hadronic mass scale.

## Context

This work extends the NLD model (Gaitanos et al., 2009) from nucleon-nucleus interactions to antinucleon interactions in nuclear matter. It addresses the long-standing problem that conventional RMF models (Walecka, 1974) fail to describe antiproton-nucleus data when G-parity is applied, requiring ad-hoc rescaling of antinucleon-meson couplings by a factor ξ ≈ 0.2–0.3 (Larionov et al., 2009). The results are consistent with dispersion-relation analyses (Cassing et al., 1998) and transport calculations (Larionov et al., 2009; Zhang et al., 2009), and are directly relevant for the PANDA experiment at FAIR, where the energy dependence of the antinucleon optical potential will affect antiproton-induced reactions, nuclear compression, and strangeness production.
