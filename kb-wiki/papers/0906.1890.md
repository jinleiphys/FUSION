# Thermal Transport and Drag Force in Improved Holographic QCD

> We calculate the bulk viscosity, drag force and jet quenching parameter in Improved Holographic QCD. We find that the bulk viscosity rises near the phase transition but does not exceed the shear viscosity. The drag force shows the effects of asymptotic freedom both as a function of velocity and temperature. It indicates diffusion times of heavy quarks in rough agreement with data. The jet quenching parameter values computed via the light-like Wilson loop are in the lower range suggested by data.

## Key claim

The bulk viscosity in Improved Holographic QCD rises near the deconfinement phase transition but remains slightly below the shear viscosity, contrary to earlier expectations from sum-rule and lattice-based approaches that suggested a more dramatic rise. The drag force on heavy quarks shows effects of asymptotic freedom as a function of both velocity and temperature, and the jet quenching parameter computed via the light-like Wilson loop falls in the lower range suggested by experimental data.

## Method

The authors use a five-dimensional Einstein-dilaton gravity model (Improved Holographic QCD, IHQCD) with a dilaton potential fixed to reproduce the perturbative Yang-Mills beta-function up to two loops and to match lattice thermodynamics. The bulk viscosity is extracted from the retarded Green's function of the stress-energy tensor via Kubo's formula, computed by solving the fluctuation equation for the scalar metric perturbation in the gauge where the radial coordinate equals the dilaton field. The drag force on a heavy quark is obtained from the equilibrium configuration of a trailing string worldsheet in the black hole background, and the jet quenching parameter is calculated from the light-like Wilson loop.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature of the deconfinement phase transition is found to be \(T_c = 247\) MeV, close to the lattice determination for pure Yang-Mills.
- The latent heat and pressure at \(T=2T_c\) are matched to lattice results by fixing the potential parameters \(V_1 = 14\) and \(V_3 = 170\).
- The 5D Planck scale is fixed so that in the \(T \to \infty\) limit the equation of state matches a free relativistic gas of \(N_c^2\) photons: \(M_p^3 = \ell^{-3} / (45\pi^2)\).
- The conformal jet quenching parameter in \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM scales as \(\hat q_{\text{conformal}} = \frac{\Gamma[3/4]}{\Gamma[5/4]} \sqrt{2\lambda} \, \pi^{3/2} T^3\).
- The conformal drag force is \(F_{\text{conformal}} = \frac{\pi}{2} \sqrt{\lambda} \, T^2 \frac{v}{\sqrt{1-v^2}}\).

## Context

This work extends the Improved Holographic QCD model previously developed by the same authors (Gursoy et al., 2008-2009), which accurately reproduces the static properties and equilibrium thermodynamics of large-\(N_c\) pure Yang-Mills theory as computed on the lattice. It provides the first holographic computation of bulk viscosity, drag force, and jet quenching in a realistic QCD-like model, as opposed to the conformal \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM plasma used in earlier holographic studies by Herzog et al. (2006), Gubser (2006), and Liu et al. (2006-2007). The results enable comparison with heavy-ion collision data from RHIC and LHC, and can be used as input for Langevin simulations of heavy-quark energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma.
