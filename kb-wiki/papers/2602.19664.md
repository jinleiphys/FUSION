# 2025 EIC-France Workshop: Physics Highlights and Perspectives

> This document presents a synthesis of the theory contributions and discussions from the 2nd EIC-France Workshop, held at IJCLab (Orsay) on 1-3 December 2025. The workshop brought together members of the French hadron-physics community to review recent theoretical developments relevant to the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and to coordinate national efforts in preparation for its early physics program. The report first summarizes the collider's initial running conditions and luminosity performance, as outlined in the EIC Early Science Matrix. It then provides concise overviews of the theoretical presentations on inclusive, semi-inclusive, exclusive, heavy-flavor, and small-x physics. Based on these discussions, two measurements emerged as especially well suited for early EIC operation and strongly aligned with areas of established French expertise: inclusive diffraction and inclusive quarkonium production. These channels offer clean signatures, robust theoretical interpretability, and direct sensitivity to fundamental QCD phenomena such as gluon saturation, heavy-quark dynamics, and the small-x structure of hadrons and nuclei. In addition, the workshop identified longer-term physics opportunities that will benefit from the full capabilities of the EIC after its ramp-up phase. These include accessing the three-dimensional structure of the pion through the Sullivan process and a broader program of exclusive three-body final states, both of which represent high-impact avenues for exploring hadronic structure and non-perturbative QCD. Together, the elements summarized in this report provide a coherent overview of the strategic priorities and scientific ambitions shaping the French community's contribution to the EIC physics program.

## Key claim

The workshop identified inclusive diffraction and inclusive quarkonium production as the two measurements best suited for early EIC operation, based on their clean signatures, robust theoretical interpretability, and alignment with established French expertise in small-x physics, gluon saturation, and heavy-flavor phenomenology. The authors present these as priority targets while noting that the precise evolution of the running plan will depend on machine performance and operational experience.

## Method

The 2nd EIC-France Workshop (1-3 December 2025 at IJCLab, Orsay) brought together theorists, phenomenologists, and instrumentation specialists from the French hadron-physics community to review theoretical developments relevant to the future Electron-Ion Collider and coordinate national efforts for its early physics program. The report synthesizes presentations on inclusive, semi-inclusive, exclusive, heavy-flavor, and small-x physics, and summarizes the EIC Early Science Matrix outlining beam species, energy configurations, polarization modes, and expected yearly luminosities for the first five years of data taking.

## Key numbers

- Year 1 commissioning: e+medium-mass nuclei (Ag, Ru, Cu) at 10 x 115 GeV with 0.9 fb^-1/year, no electron polarization
- Year 2: e+D at 10 x 130 GeV with 13.1 fb^-1/year, and e+p at 10 x 130 GeV with 5.6-6.1 fb^-1/year, with longitudinal electron polarization
- Year 4: e+Au at 10 x 100 GeV with 0.95 fb^-1/year, and e+p at 10 x 250 GeV with 7.1-10.6 fb^-1/year
- Year 5: e+Au at 10 x 100 GeV with 0.95 fb^-1/year, and e+^3He at 10 x 166 GeV with 9.8 fb^-1/year
- With 10 fb^-1 of data, inclusive prompt J/psi photoproduction reach will exceed 10 GeV, surpassing HERA
- For associated J/psi+charm production at sqrt(s_ep)=140 GeV, the PT-differential cross section should be measurable up to around 8 GeV with 10 fb^-1
- For exclusive three-body final states in years 2-3: expected ~10^4 events for gamma pi+, gamma rho0 and gamma rho+ production, and up to ~10^6 events for rho0 rho0, pi+ rho0 and pi- rho0 production

## Context

This workshop report extends the planning framework established by the EIC Yellow Report (AbdulKhalek et al., 2021) and the EIC Early Science Matrix, focusing on how French community expertise can contribute to early EIC measurements. The identified priorities of inclusive diffraction and inclusive quarkonium production build on theoretical foundations including the Color Glass Condensate effective theory (Gelis, 2010), geometric scaling (Stasto, 2000), and quarkonium production via photon-gluon fusion (Flore, 2020). The longer-term opportunities for pion structure via the Sullivan process (Sullivan, 1971) and exclusive three-body final states (Qiu, 2022) represent high-impact avenues that will become accessible after the EIC ramp-up phase, enabling tests of non-linear QCD dynamics and three-dimensional hadron structure that were impossible at HERA.
