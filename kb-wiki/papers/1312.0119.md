# Chiral phase transition of $N_f$=2+1 QCD with the HISQ action

> We present studies of universal properties of the chiral phase transition in $N_f$=2+1 QCD based on the simulations using Highly Improved Staggered fermions on lattices with temporal extent $N_τ$=6. We analyze the quark mass and volume dependence of the chiral condensates and chiral susceptibilities in QCD with two degenerate light quarks and a strange quark. The strange quark mass is chosen to be fixed to its physical value ($m^{phy}_s$) and five values of light quark masses ($m_l$) that are varied in the interval 1/20$\gtrsim m_l/m^{phy}_s \gtrsim$1/80. Here various quark masses correspond to pseudo Goldstone pion masses ranging from about 160 MeV to about 80 MeV. The O(N) scaling of chiral observables and the influence of universal scaling on physical observables in the region of physical quark mass values are also discussed.

## Key claim

The chiral phase transition in 2+1 flavor QCD with the HISQ action on \(N_\tau=6\) lattices is consistent with second-order O(N) scaling, but the scaling window is narrower than previously observed with the p4fat3 action; the authors find no evidence of a first-order transition in the quark mass range studied, though the analysis relies on the assumption that the two lightest quark masses lie within the scaling regime.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations of \(N_f=2+1\) QCD using the Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) action on lattices with temporal extent \(N_\tau=6\) and spatial volumes ranging from \(12^3\) to \(48^3\). The strange quark mass was fixed to its physical value, while five light quark masses were varied in the interval \(1/20 \gtrsim m_l/m_s^{\mathrm{phy}} \gtrsim 1/80\), corresponding to pion masses from about 160 MeV down to 80 MeV. They analyzed the volume and quark mass dependence of the chiral condensate and disconnected chiral susceptibility, and performed O(2) scaling fits to the order parameter using the two lightest quark masses to extract non-universal parameters and test universality.

## Key numbers

- Pion masses studied: approximately 160, 140, 110, 90, and 80 MeV, corresponding to light quark mass ratios \(m_l/m_s^{\mathrm{phy}} = 1/20, 1/27, 1/40, 1/60, 1/80\).
- Lattice temporal extent: \(N_\tau = 6\) for all simulations.
- Spatial lattice sizes: \(N_s = 12, 16, 20, 24, 32\) at \(m_l/m_s^{\mathrm{phy}} = 1/27\) (physical quark mass); \(N_s = 32\) and 48 at \(m_l/m_s^{\mathrm{phy}} = 1/80\) (lowest quark mass).
- Number of trajectories per parameter set: 3000 to 10000, with the lowest statistics (3000) at \(m_l/m_s^{\mathrm{phy}} = 1/80\) on \(32^3\times 6\).
- The condition \(m_\pi L \gtrsim 4\) was ensured for all volumes: e.g., \(N_s=24\) at \(m_\pi\approx 160\) MeV, \(N_s=32\) at \(m_\pi\approx 110\) MeV, and \(N_s=40\) at \(m_\pi\approx 90\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of the chiral phase transition in 2+1 flavor QCD using an improved fermion action (HISQ) that reduces taste-symmetry violations by more than an order of magnitude compared to earlier studies with the p4fat3 action on coarser \(N_\tau=4\) lattices by Ejiri et al. (2009). The results show a narrower scaling window than those earlier calculations, but the data can be described by O(2) scaling functions with a simple regular term, enabling quantitative control over scaling violations at non-zero quark masses. This analysis is a step toward establishing the chiral critical behavior needed to define the pseudo-critical temperature at physical quark masses and to constrain the QCD phase diagram at non-zero baryon chemical potential.
