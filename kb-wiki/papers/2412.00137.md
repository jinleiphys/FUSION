# Induced three-neutron interactions with low cutoffs for dilute neutron matter

> The properties of dilute neutron matter are mostly determined by the s-wave two-body (2N) interaction, while three-body (3N) interactions are suppressed by the Pauli principle. In a previous work, we showed that it can be advantageous to use renormalization group based effective interactions with cutoffs scaled with the Fermi momentum, especially at low densities. In that case, induced 3N interactions may become important. In this work, we compute the 3N interaction induced by the similarity renormalization group flow of the s-wave 2N interaction. We work in the momentum-space hyperspherical partial wave basis and investigate its convergence properties. Then we study the effect of the induced 3N interaction on the equation of state of dilute neutron matter. We observe that the cutoff dependence of the equation of state is strongly reduced when the effect of induced 3N interaction is included.

## Key claim
The cutoff dependence of the dilute neutron matter equation of state is strongly reduced when the induced three-neutron interaction from the similarity renormalization group flow of the s-wave two-body interaction is included, but the induced 3N interaction is treated only in first-order perturbation theory and the residual cutoff dependence weakens as Fermi momentum increases from 0.2 to 0.8 inverse femtometers.

## Method
The authors compute the three-neutron (3N) interaction induced by the similarity renormalization group (SRG) flow of the s-wave two-body (2N) interaction, using the momentum-space hyperspherical partial wave (HPW) basis. They solve the coupled SRG flow equations for the 2N and induced 3N matrix elements, starting from the N4LO chiral interaction, and then evaluate the induced 3N contribution to the neutron matter equation of state in first-order perturbation theory using both Hartree-Fock (HF) and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) ground states, with a density-dependent cutoff scaled by the Fermi momentum.

## Key numbers
- The s-wave scattering length used is a = -18.95 fm (from Entem 2017).
- Convergence of the induced 3N matrix elements requires hyperspherical partial waves up to L_max = 13 in the intermediate L'' summation.
- Convergence of the induced 3N contribution to the energy requires hyperspherical partial waves up to L_max = 13 and l_max = 5.
- The 2N calculations include partial waves with j ≤ 6 for Fermi momenta up to k_F = 1.4 inverse femtometers.
- The induced 3N contribution is computed for scale factors f = λ/k_F in the range approximately 1.3 to 2.5, for Fermi momenta from 0.2 to 0.8 inverse femtometers.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Palaniappan 2023) which used V_lowk interactions with density-dependent cutoffs to compute the neutron matter equation of state via Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov plus Bogoliubov many-body perturbation theory, but found residual cutoff dependence indicating missing induced three-body forces. The present paper computes those induced 3N interactions using the SRG approach, complementing earlier work by Hebeler (2012, 2013, 2021) on SRG-evolved 3N interactions in Jacobi partial-wave basis and by Wendt (2013) who introduced the HPW basis for the triton. The results enable more reliable microscopic calculations of dilute neutron matter properties relevant for neutron star crust physics, such as the equation of state and pairing gap, and can help constrain energy density functionals used in inner crust calculations.
