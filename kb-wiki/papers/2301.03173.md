# Popcorn transitions and approach to conformality in homogeneous holographic nuclear matter

> We study cold and dense nuclear matter by using the gauge/gravity duality. To this end we use the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model and the V-QCD models with an approach where the nuclear matter is taken to be spatially homogeneous. We focus on the ''popcorn'' transitions, which are phase transitions in the nuclear matter phases induced by changes in the layer structure of the configuration on the gravity side. We demonstrate that the equation of state for the homogeneous nuclear matter becomes approximately conformal at high densities, and compare our results to other approaches.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that homogeneous holographic nuclear matter, studied via the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto (WSS) model and the V-QCD model, exhibits "popcorn" transitions (phase transitions induced by changes in the layer structure of the gravity-side configuration) and that the equation of state becomes approximately conformal at high densities, with the caveat that the double-layer solution in V-QCD is structurally different from that in WSS and that the approach treats nuclear matter as spatially homogeneous rather than crystalline.

## Method
The authors used the gauge/gravity duality to study cold, dense nuclear matter by applying a homogeneous Ansatz for non-Abelian gauge fields in two holographic models: the top-down Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model and the bottom-up V-QCD model. They expanded the Dirac-Born-Infeld action to leading order in non-Abelian field strengths while keeping Abelian terms unexpanded, then solved for the gauge field profiles and bulk charge densities for both single-layer and double-layer configurations, performing Legendre transforms to work in the canonical ensemble at fixed baryon charge density.

## Key numbers
- The critical chemical potential for the vacuum-to-nuclear transition in the WSS model is μ_c ≈ 0.205 (in model units), corresponding to approximately 195 MeV when the Kaluza-Klein scale M_KK = 949 MeV is set by the rho meson mass.
- The popcorn transition from single-layer to double-layer phase in the WSS model occurs at μ_l ≈ 0.342 (model units), approximately 325 MeV, with μ_l/μ_c ≈ 1.67.
- The density at the popcorn transition ρ_l relative to the density at the vacuum-to-nuclear transition ρ_c satisfies ρ_l/ρ_c ≈ 3.4 in the WSS model.
- The critical density for the popcorn transition in the WSS model is ρ_l ≈ 2.52 × 10^{-4} (in model units).

## Context
This work extends prior holographic studies of dense nuclear matter using homogeneous Ansätze in the WSS model (Rozali 2007, Elliot-Ripley 2016) and V-QCD model (Ishii 2019), and connects to the effective field theory approach for topology-changing transitions in Skyrmion crystals (Paeng 2015, Paeng 2017). The results enable comparisons between holographic predictions and neutron star observations, particularly regarding the speed of sound approaching the conformal value c_s^2 = 1/3 at high densities, and support the possibility of quark-hadron continuity.
