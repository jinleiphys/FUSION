# Phonon-particle coupling effects in single-particle energies of semi-magic nuclei

> A method is presented to evaluate the particle-phonon coupling (PC) corrections to the single-particle energies (SPEs) in semi-magic nuclei. In such nuclei always there is a collective low-lying $2^+$ phonon, and a strong mixture of single-particle and particle-phonon states often occurs. As in magic nuclei, the so-called $g^2_L$ approximation, where $g_L$ is the vertex of the $L$-phonon creation, can be used for finding the PC correction $δΣ^{\rm PC}(\varepsilon)$ to the initial mass operator $Σ_0$. In addition to the usual pole diagram, the phonon "tadpole" diagram is also taken into account. In semi-magic nuclei, the perturbation theory in $δΣ^{\rm PC}(\varepsilon)$ with respect to $Σ_0$ is often invalid for finding the PC corrected SPEs. Instead, the Dyson equation with the mass operator $Σ(\varepsilon){=}Σ_0{+}δΣ^{\rm PC}(\varepsilon)$ is solved directly, without any use of the perturbation theory. Results for a chain of semi-magic Pb isotopes are presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method to evaluate particle-phonon coupling (PC) corrections to single-particle energies (SPEs) in semi-magic nuclei that goes beyond perturbation theory, solving the Dyson equation directly because the perturbation theory in the PC correction to the mass operator is often invalid in such nuclei due to small denominators from low-lying collective states.

## Method

The authors solve the Dyson equation with the mass operator Σ(ε) = Σ₀ + δΣ^PC(ε) directly, without perturbation theory, using the g²_L approximation for the PC correction to the mass operator. They include both the pole diagram and the phonon "tadpole" diagram, and use the self-consistent basis generated by the DF3-a version of the Fayans energy density functional. For the chain of even Pb isotopes (²⁰⁰Pb, ²⁰²Pb, ²⁰⁴Pb, ²⁰⁶Pb), they consider two low-lying phonons (2⁺₁ and 3⁻₁) plus the ghost 1⁻ phonon, and solve for the SPEs within a model space of two shells near the Fermi surface.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energies of the 2⁺₁ phonon in Pb isotopes: 0.789 MeV (²⁰⁰Pb, theoretical), 0.823 MeV (²⁰²Pb), 0.882 MeV (²⁰⁴Pb), 0.945 MeV (²⁰⁶Pb); experimental values range from 0.803 MeV (²⁰⁶Pb) to 1.026 MeV (²⁰⁰Pb)
- Excitation energies of the 3⁻₁ phonon: 2.620 MeV (²⁰⁰Pb, theoretical), 2.704 MeV (²⁰²Pb), 2.785 MeV (²⁰⁴Pb), 2.839 MeV (²⁰⁶Pb); experimental values are 2.517 MeV (²⁰²Pb), 2.621 MeV (²⁰⁴Pb), 2.648 MeV (²⁰⁶Pb)
- For ²⁰⁴Pb, the 1h₁₁/₂ state has a spectroscopic factor S = 0.814 for the dominant solution at ε = -7.509 MeV
- The 3s₁/₂ state in ²⁰⁴Pb has a dominant solution with S = 0.839 at ε = -6.493 MeV
- The 1h₉/₂ state in ²⁰⁴Pb has a dominant solution with S = 0.760 at ε = -3.447 MeV
- PC corrected SPEs for ²⁰⁴Pb range from -10.80 MeV (1g₇/₂) to -1.07 MeV (1i₁₃/₂)
- Tadpole corrections in ²⁰⁴Pb range from 0.17 MeV (3s₁/₂, 2d₃/₂, 2d₅/₂) to 0.32 MeV (1i₁₃/₂)
- The recoil correction from the ghost 1⁻ phonon is at most 0.04 MeV in the lead region

## Context

This work extends previous studies of PC corrections in double-magic nuclei (Litvinova and Ring, 2006; Bortignon et al., 2010; Dobaczewski et al., 2014; Baldo et al., 2015; Saperstein et al., 2015) to semi-magic nuclei, where the presence of low-lying 2⁺ states makes perturbation theory invalid. The method enables consistent treatment of PC corrections to double odd-even mass differences in semi-magic nuclei, building on the approach developed for magic nuclei by Saperstein et al. (2015) and Baldo et al. (2015). The results show that the tadpole correction is of primary importance in semi-magic nuclei, often dominating over the pole correction and sometimes determining the sign of the total SPE correction.
