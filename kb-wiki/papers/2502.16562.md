# Limiting fragmentation in the dilute Glasma

> We discuss the local longitudinal scaling behavior of the dilute Glasma. We gain insight into how the fragmentation region is dominated by the longitudinal structure of one of the two colliding nuclei in heavy-ion collisions and study the effect of the correlation scales of the nuclear color charge distributions. We compare with results from the full dilute Glasma framework and analyze the rapidity limits of the fragmentation region. From our findings, it follows that all scalar observables that can be constructed out of the dilute Glasma field strength tensor show limiting fragmentation behavior and the field strength tensor itself transforms locally via Lorentz boosts when changing the collision energy.

## Key claim
The dilute Glasma field strength tensor exhibits local longitudinal scaling behavior in the fragmentation region (spacetime rapidity eta_s >> 1), meaning that scalar observables constructed from it show limiting fragmentation where a change in collision energy can be compensated by a shift in beam rapidity. This property holds analytically under an approximation where the longitudinal argument of one nucleus's field is integrated out, leaving the fragmentation region dominated by the longitudinal structure of only one of the two colliding nuclei.

## Method
The authors solve the classical Yang-Mills equations in the dilute Glasma framework, where the full gauge field is decomposed into single-nucleus fields plus a Glasma field. They derive analytic expressions for the Glasma field strength tensor components in terms of integrals over the backward light cone, then take the large spacetime rapidity limit (eta_s >> 1) to obtain simplified expressions where the effective field of one nucleus becomes a transverse-only quantity. Numerical comparisons are performed using a generalized McLerran-Venugopalan model with Woods-Saxon profiles and tunable longitudinal correlation length xi, discretized on a lattice with ultraviolet regulator Lambda_UV = 10 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Local rest frame energy density epsilon_LRF and transverse energy dE_perp/deta_s are compared for Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (RHIC) and Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 2700 GeV (LHC) at proper time tau = 1.0 fm/c
- The ultraviolet regulator Lambda_UV is set to 10 GeV for the lattice calculations
- The longitudinal correlation length xi is varied, with the approximation breaking down at smaller rapidities for small xi values
- Beam rapidity Y_beam = arcosh(gamma_beam) is used to shift rapidity profiles for comparison between different collision energies

## Context
This work extends the Color Glass Condensate framework's treatment of limiting fragmentation, first described in the CGC by Jalilian-Marian and Nara (2002) and Gelis et al. (2006), to the three-dimensional dilute Glasma with non-trivial longitudinal structure. The authors build on their own previous work (Ipp et al., 2021, 2024; Leuthner et al., 2025) that developed the dilute Glasma framework. The results enable future studies of whether local longitudinal scaling holds for the non-perturbative Glasma and how later-stage descriptions like relativistic hydrodynamics modify the longitudinal scaling behavior predicted by the dilute Glasma.
