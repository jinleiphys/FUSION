# Symmetry-energy dependence of the dynamical dipole mode in the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck model

> Using an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model, we have studied the connection between the symmetry energy and features of the dynamical dipole mode in fusion reactions with charge-asymmetric entrance channel. The yield and angular distribution of the prompt photon emission are extracted by a bremsstrahlung approach. The experimental data of $^{36}$Ar+$^{96}$Zr at 16 MeV/nucleon and $^{32}$S + $^{100}$Mo at 9.3 MeV/nucleon are compared with IBUU model calculations, and the soft symmetry energy is found to describe the data reasonably well.

## Key claim

The centroid energy of the dynamical dipole mode has a strong dependence on the symmetry energy at subsaturation densities, and the soft symmetry energy (γ = 0.5) describes the experimental data reasonably well; however, this conclusion might be modified if momentum-dependent effective interactions (absent in the current IBUU model) are taken into account.

## Method

The authors used an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model with test particles and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections to simulate fusion reactions at impact parameters b = 0, 2, and 4 fm. The dipole moment time evolution was computed from the coordinate-space separation of proton and neutron centers of mass, and the prompt photon yield and angular distribution were extracted via a bremsstrahlung approach using the Fourier transform of the dipole acceleration. The symmetry energy was parametrized as a power law with exponent γ, where γ = 0.5 (soft) and γ = 2 (stiff) were compared.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction $^{36}$Ar + $^{96}$Zr at 16 MeV/nucleon, the experimental data from Pierroutsakou (2009) were compared with IBUU calculations; a mean impact parameter b$_{mean}$ = 2.3-2.5 fm was deduced from the data.
- For the reaction $^{32}$S + $^{100}$Mo at 9.3 MeV/nucleon, the experimental data from Pierroutsakou (2017) were compared with IBUU calculations.
- The soft symmetry energy (γ = 0.5) gave a larger centroid energy and stronger dynamical dipole emission than the stiff symmetry energy (γ = 2).
- The centroid energy E$_c$ showed a monotonic decreasing behavior with increasing γ; a constant symmetry energy (γ = 0) gave the largest value of E$_c$.
- For the $^{36}$Ar + $^{96}$Zr system at 16 MeV/nucleon, the normalization factor M$_0$ ≈ 0.6 × 10$^{-4}$ was taken from Pierroutsakou (2009).
- The collective oscillation was defined to damp over t$_{max}$ - t$_0$ = 300 fm/c.
- The soft EOS with compressibility K = 200 MeV was used (a = -356 MeV, b = 303 MeV, σ = 7/6), with symmetry energy coefficient C$_{sym}$ = 35.19 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the dynamical dipole mode using transport models such as time-dependent Hartree-Fock (Simenel, 2001), Boltzmann-Nordheim-Vlasov (Baran, 2009), constrained molecular dynamics (Papa, 2005), and isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (Wu, 2010; Tao, 2013). The results are consistent with the BNV model analysis of Pierroutsakou (2009), where integrated pre-equilibrium gamma-ray yields were fitted with different symmetry energies and nucleon-nucleon scattering cross sections. The study enables future experiments with $^{132}$Sn beams (as suggested by Baran, 2009) to provide more accurate angular distribution data for further constraints on the symmetry energy.
