# Zooming in on Multiquark Hadrons within QCD Sum-Rule Approaches

> Aiming at self-consistent descriptions of multiquark hadrons (such as tetraquarks, pentaquarks, hexaquarks) by means of QCD sum rules, we note that the totality of contributions to two-point or three-point correlation functions that involve, respectively, either two or just a single operator capable of interpolating the particular multiquark under study can be straightforwardly disentangled into two disjoint classes defined by unambiguously identifiable members. The first is formed by so-called multiquark-phile contributions which indeed might support multiquarks. In the case of flavour-exotic tetraquarks, by definition composed of four (anti-) quarks of mutually different flavours, a tetraquark-phile contribution has to exhibit two or more gluon exchanges of appropriate topology. The second consists of contributions evidently not bearing any relation to multiquarks; these must be discarded when studying multiquarks by QCD sum rules. The first class only should enter the "multiquark-adequate" QCD sum rules for exotic hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for QCD sum-rule studies of multiquark hadrons (tetraquarks, pentaquarks, hexaquarks), only a specific subset of QCD-level contributions to correlation functions, those that exhibit a nonpolynomial dependence on the Mandelstam variable \(s\) and develop a branch cut starting at the four-quark threshold \(\hat s = (m_a+m_b+m_c+m_d)^2\), should be retained; all other contributions must be discarded as irrelevant to multiquark physics.

## Method

The authors analyze two-point and three-point correlation functions built from tetraquark interpolating operators, which are themselves products of two conventional meson interpolating currents (quark–antiquark bilinears). They classify all QCD-level contributions to these correlation functions using the Landau equations to identify which diagrams have the correct analytic structure (branch cut at the four-quark threshold) to potentially support a tetraquark pole. For flavour-exotic tetraquarks (four quarks of mutually different flavours), a tetraquark-phile contribution must involve two or more gluon exchanges of appropriate topology. The surviving contributions are then assembled into "multiquark-adequate" QCD sum rules, which equate a Borel-transformed integral of the tetraquark-phile spectral density (plus power corrections from condensates) to hadron-level expressions involving the tetraquark mass, decay constants, and transition amplitudes.

## Key numbers

* The branch point for a tetraquark-phile contribution is \(\hat s \equiv (m_a+m_b+m_c+m_d)^2\), where \(m_a,m_b,m_c,m_d\) are the quark masses (basic QCD parameters).
* The effective threshold in the dispersion integral is a function of the Borel parameter: \(s_{\rm eff} = s_{\rm eff}(\tau)\).
* The tetraquark-adequate sum rules involve the tetraquark mass \(M\), decay constants \(f_{\overline ab\overline cd}\) and \(f_{\overline ad\overline cb}\), and transition amplitudes \(A(T\to j_{\overline ab}\,j_{\overline cd})\) and \(A(T\to j_{\overline ad}\,j_{\overline cb})\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier program (Lucha, Melikhov, Sazdjian, 2010–2022) to construct QCD sum rules that are adequate for multiquark states, building on the observation that standard sum-rule treatments inadvertently include contributions from ordinary-meson intermediate states. The criterion for tetraquark-phile contributions was previously formulated for tetraquarks (Lucha et al., 2017–2018) and is here systematized for all multiquark types. The resulting multiquark-adequate sum rules enable future extractions of tetraquark masses and couplings from QCD without contamination by two-meson reducible diagrams, and can be extended to pentaquarks and hexaquarks by analogous reasoning.
