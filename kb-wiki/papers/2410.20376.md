# Medium recoil mode of $Δ$ production in single isobaric charge-exchange reactions

> The dynamic mechanisms underlying single charge-exchange reactions have been investigated using a theoretical framework that combines the Isospin-dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model with the statistical decay model GEMINI++. Two distinct channels contribute to the single isobaric charge-exchange reaction: quasi-elastic channel, where neutron-proton scattering drives the charge-exchange, and inelastic channel, where the $Δ$ particle is produced during the process. In a referenced study [Phys.RevC 106.014618(2022)], experimental data have revealed that the inelastic channel accounts for approximately 50 percent of the single isobaric charge-exchange reaction. However, our current model fails in reproducing the significant contribution of inelastic channel unless the novel medium recoil mode associated with $Δ$ production is considered in the calculations. Notably, this in-medium effect arising from inelastic nucleon-nucleon collisions is not yet incorporated into mainstream microscopic transport models. The dynamical properties of protons and pions emitting in the single isobaric charge-exchange reactions are predicted. This exploration of in-medium effects adds a valuable dimension to our understanding of the intricate dynamics involved in single charge-exchange reactions.

## Key claim
The inelastic channel (involving Delta particle production) contributes significantly to single isobaric charge-exchange reactions, but standard transport models using only the cascade scattering mode for Delta dynamics fail to reproduce this contribution; the authors show that introducing a "medium recoil mode" for Delta production, which accounts for in-medium effects in inelastic nucleon-nucleon collisions, is necessary to bring calculations into closer agreement with experimental data.

## Method
The authors combined the Isospin-dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model with the statistical decay model GEMINI++ to simulate single charge-exchange reactions in 112Sn + p and 136Xe + p collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon. They replaced the standard cascade scattering mode for Delta particle dynamics with a novel medium recoil mode, where the inelastic nucleon-nucleon collision generating the Delta occurs within the nuclear medium and the remaining nucleons in the projectile recoil as a three-body effect, keeping the Delta at its original energy level.

## Key numbers
- Experimental production cross sections for 112In and 112Sb in 112Sn + p at 1 GeV/nucleon are 0.29 +/- 0.01 mb and 0.60 +/- 0.02 mb, respectively; the model with medium recoil mode yields 0.70 +/- 0.04 mb and 2.10 +/- 0.07 mb, reducing discrepancies compared to the cascade scattering mode
- For 112Sb production in 112Sn + p at 1 GeV/nucleon, the experimental inelastic cross section is 369 +/- 15 microbarn and quasi-elastic cross section is 235 +/- 10 microbarn, giving an inelastic-to-quasi-elastic ratio of approximately 1.57; the model yields a ratio of 1.17
- The calculated cross section for 112In production via the inelastic channel in 112Sn + p is 700 +/- 40 microbarn, approximately 2.5 times larger than the experimental value of 291 +/- 9 microbarn
- The most probable kinetic energy of pions associated with SICE reactions is 2.5 MeV, with most events below 15 MeV; slope temperatures extracted from Maxwellian fits are 1.58 MeV (112In) and 1.70 MeV (112Sb) in 112Sn + p, and 1.54 MeV (136I) and 1.27 MeV (136Cs) in 136Xe + p

## Context
This work extends experimental studies by Rodriguez (2022) which showed that the inelastic channel accounts for approximately 50 percent of single isobaric charge-exchange reactions in 112Sn isotopes at 1 GeV/nucleon, and earlier investigations by Kelic (2004) on 208Pb + 1,2H collisions that distinguished quasi-elastic and inelastic contributions. The medium recoil mode introduced here addresses a gap in mainstream microscopic transport models, which currently lack this in-medium effect from inelastic nucleon-nucleon collisions; the improved framework enables predictions of proton and pion dynamical properties in single charge-exchange reactions and may inform studies of Delta resonances in nuclear matter relevant to neutron star properties.
