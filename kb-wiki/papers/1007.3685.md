# Coherent pion production off nuclei at T2K and MiniBooNE energies revisited

> As a result of a new improved fit to old bubble chamber data of the dominant axial C_5^A nucleon-to-Delta form factor, and due to the relevance of this form factor for neutrino induced coherent pion production, we re-evaluate our model predictions in Phys. Rev. D79, 013002 (2009) for different observables of the latter reaction. Central values for the total cross sections increase by 20% to 30%, while differential cross sections do not change their shape appreciably. Furthermore, we also compute the uncertainties on total, differential and flux averaged cross sections induced by the errors in the determination of C_5^A. Our new results turn out to be compatible within about 1 sigma with the former ones. Finally, we stress the existing tension between the recent experimental determination of the sigma(CC coh π^+)/sigma(NC coh π^0) ratio by the SciBooNE Collaboration and the theoretical predictions.

# Coherent pion production off nuclei at T2K and MiniBooNE energies revisited

## Key claim

The authors' updated model, using a new fit to the dominant axial C5^A nucleon-to-Delta form factor, predicts total coherent pion production cross sections that are 20% to 30% larger than their previous results (Phys. Rev. D79, 013002, 2009), but the new and old results remain compatible within about 1 sigma due to the 21% uncertainties from the form factor determination.

## Method

The authors re-evaluate their microscopic model for neutrino-induced coherent pion production on carbon and oxygen nuclei, which includes the dominant Delta-pole mechanism plus background terms required by chiral symmetry, using a new parameterization of the C5^A axial form factor obtained from a combined fit to ANL and BNL bubble chamber data for nu_mu p -> mu- p pi+. The fit includes deuteron effects, treats neutrino flux normalization uncertainties (20% for ANL, 10% for BNL) as fully correlated systematic errors, and uses a pure dipole form for C5^A(q^2) with Adler's model constraints (C4^A = -C5^A/4, C3^A = 0). The new form factor parameters are C5^A(0) = 1.00 +/- 0.11 and M_{A Delta} = 0.93 +/- 0.07 GeV with a correlation coefficient of -0.06.

## Key numbers

- New fitted form factor parameters: C5^A(0) = 1.00 +/- 0.11, M_{A Delta} = 0.93 +/- 0.07 GeV, with chi^2/dof = 0.32
- Total cross sections increase by 20% to 30% compared to the authors' 2009 predictions
- Flux-averaged CC coherent pi+ cross section on carbon for MiniBooNE: (3.8 +/- 0.8) x 10^{-40} cm^2
- Flux-averaged NC coherent pi0 cross section on carbon for MiniBooNE: (2.6 +/- 0.5) x 10^{-40} cm^2
- Flux-averaged CC coherent pi+ cross section on carbon for T2K: (3.2 +/- 0.6) x 10^{-40} cm^2
- Flux-averaged NC coherent pi0 cross section on carbon for T2K: (2.3 +/- 0.5) x 10^{-40} cm^2
- Flux-averaged CC coherent pi+ cross section on oxygen for T2K: (3.8 +/- 0.8) x 10^{-40} cm^2
- Flux-averaged NC coherent pi0 cross section on oxygen for T2K: (2.9 +/- 0.6) x 10^{-40} cm^2
- Ratio sigma(CC coh pi+)/sigma(NC coh pi0) on carbon at 0.8 GeV neutrino energy: 1.45 +/- 0.03, compared to the SciBooNE experimental value of 0.14^{+0.30}_{-0.28}
- Errors from C5^A uncertainties are approximately 21%

## Context

This work extends the authors' microscopic model from Amaro et al. (2009) by incorporating an improved determination of the C5^A axial form factor from Hernandez et al. (2010), which includes both ANL and BNL bubble chamber data with deuteron effects and flux normalization uncertainties. The results highlight a major tension with the SciBooNE Collaboration's measurement of the CC-to-NC coherent pion production ratio, which is about ten times smaller than the theoretical prediction from this model and from other approaches including the Rein-Sehgal model and microscopic calculations by Martini et al. (2009) and Nakamura et al. (2009). The authors argue that the SciBooNE result likely stems from inappropriate use of the Rein-Sehgal model at low energies, and their work enables more reliable predictions for the T2K experiment, where cross sections on carbon are predicted to be 2.4 to 3.2 x 10^{-40} cm^2 and on oxygen 2.9 to 3.8 x 10^{-40} cm^2.
