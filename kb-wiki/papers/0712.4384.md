# Quasielastic axial-vector mass from experiments on neutrino-nucleus scattering

> We analyze available experimental data on the total and differential charged-current cross sections for quasielastic neutrino and antineutrino scattering off nucleons, measured with a variety of nuclear targets in the accelerator experiments at ANL, BNL, FNAL, CERN, and IHEP, dating from the end of sixties to the present day. The data are used to adjust the poorly known value of the axial-vector mass of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper extracts the axial-vector mass \(M_A\) from a global statistical analysis of all available quasielastic neutrino and antineutrino scattering data on nuclear targets, finding a value that is systematically lower than the recent high-statistics results from K2K and MiniBooNE, but consistent with the world average from Bodek et al. (2007) when using updated vector form factors.

## Method

The authors performed a global likelihood analysis of total and differential charged-current quasielastic cross sections for \(\nu_\mu\) and \(\overline{\nu}_\mu\) scattering on various nuclear targets (hydrogen, deuterium, carbon, aluminium, argon, iron, propane, freon, and neon-hydrogen mixtures) from experiments at ANL, BNL, FNAL, CERN, and IHEP, covering energies from about 150 MeV to 350 GeV. They used the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model by Smith and Moniz (1972) to account for nuclear effects, and employed two sophisticated parametrizations for the vector form factors: BBBA(07) from Bodek et al. (2007) and GKex(05) from Lomon (2006). The axial-vector mass \(M_A\) was treated as a free parameter in the conventional dipole approximation for the axial form factor, and fits were performed separately for neutrino and antineutrino data, as well as for the combined dataset.

## Key numbers

- The global fit to all neutrino and antineutrino data using the BBBA(07) vector form factors yields \(M_A = 1.014 \pm 0.014\) GeV (world average from Bodek et al. 2007).
- The K2K experiment (oxygen target) reports \(M_A = 1.20 \pm 0.12\) GeV.
- The MiniBooNE experiment (carbon target) reports \(M_A = 1.23 \pm 0.20\) GeV.
- A fit of the MiniBooNE \(Q^2\) shape above 0.25 GeV\(^2\) leads to \(M_A = 1.25 \pm 0.12\) GeV.
- The formal weighted average from \(\nu_\mu,\overline{\nu}_\mu\) experiments is \(M_A = 1.026 \pm 0.021\) GeV.
- The formal weighted average from charged pion electroproduction is \(M_A = 1.069 \pm 0.016\) GeV.
- The differences between \(M_A\) values extracted with BBBA(07) and GKex(05) vector form factors vary between 0.3% and 1.3%, less than or of order one standard deviation.

## Context

This work extends the reanalysis by Bodek et al. (2007) by including a broader set of experimental data from ANL, BNL, FNAL, CERN, and IHEP, and by using two modern vector form factor parametrizations (BBBA(07) and GKex(05)) instead of the simple dipole approximation used in earlier extractions. The results highlight a tension between the lower world average \(M_A\) (around 1.01–1.03 GeV) and the larger values reported by K2K (Gran 2006) and MiniBooNE (Aguilar-Arevalo 2007), suggesting that nuclear effects or systematic uncertainties in the energy spectra may need further investigation. The analysis enables more precise predictions for neutrino oscillation experiments and provides a benchmark for future measurements of the axial-vector form factor.
