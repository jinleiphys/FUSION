# Angular mode expansion of the Boltzmann equation in the small-angle approximation

> We use an expansion in angular mode functions in order to solve the Boltzmann equation for a gluon plasma undergoing longitudinal expansion. By comparing with the exact solution obtained numerically by other means we show that the expansion in mode functions converges rapidly for all cases of practical interest, and represents a substantial gain in numerical effort as compared to more standard methods. We contrast the cases of a non expanding plasma and of longitudinally expanding plasmas, and follow in both cases the evolutions towards thermalization. In the latter case, we observe that, although the spherical mode function appears to be well reproduced after some time by a local equilibrium distribution function depending on slowly varying temperature and chemical potential, thereby suggesting thermalization of the system, the longitudinal and transverse pressures take more time to equilibrate. This is because the expansion hinders the relaxation of the first angular mode function. This feature was also observed in a simpler context where the Boltzmann equation is solved in terms of special moments within the relaxation time approximation, and attributed there to the particular coupling between the first two moments of the distribution function. The present analysis confirms this observation in a more realistic setting.

## Key claim

The angular mode expansion of the Boltzmann equation converges rapidly for both non-expanding and longitudinally expanding gluon plasmas, but in the expanding case the longitudinal and transverse pressures take more time to equilibrate than the spherical mode function suggests, because the expansion hinders the relaxation of the first angular mode function.

## Method

The authors expand the gluon distribution function in Legendre polynomials of the angular variable cos θ = p_z/p, truncating the infinite hierarchy of coupled kinetic equations for the mode functions f_n(τ,p) at low order (N ≤ 4). They solve these one-dimensional equations numerically using an implicit method with 200–400 nonuniform momentum grid points, and compare the results to a full two-dimensional solution of the Boltzmann equation obtained by the method of Tanji (2017). The collision kernel uses the small-angle approximation for elastic gluon-gluon scattering with a constant Coulomb logarithm.

## Key numbers

- Initial occupation number f_ini = 0.1 and anisotropy parameter ξ_0 = 1.5 are used throughout the numerical calculations.
- For the non-expanding case, the final equilibrium temperature is T = 0.23 Q_s and chemical potential μ = −0.075 Q_s, calculated from the initial condition.
- The relaxation time for the expanding case is estimated as τ_R = (3π ξ_0)/(2 N_c^2 α_s^2 l_Cb f_ini) τ_0, where τ_0 is the initial proper time.
- For typical values α_s ≈ 0.3, ξ_0 ≈ 1, l_Cb ≈ 1, one finds τ_0/τ_R ≈ 0.2 f_ini as a crude order of magnitude.
- The pressure ratio P_L/P_T from truncations N ≥ 1 agrees with the full result for t/τ_R ≳ 10^−2 in the non-expanding case.
- At t/τ_R = 0.3 in the non-expanding case, the zero mode function agrees with the thermal Bose-Einstein distribution, while the transverse pressure still exceeds the longitudinal pressure by a few percent.

## Context

This work extends the moment method developed by Blaizot (2017) and Blaizot (2019), which used L-moments within the relaxation time approximation, to a more realistic collision kernel for elastic QCD interactions in the small-angle approximation as formulated by Mueller (1999) and Blaizot (2013). The angular mode expansion provides a numerically efficient alternative to direct solutions of the full Boltzmann equation (e.g., Tanji (2017), Kurkela (2015)) by reducing a two-dimensional kinetic equation to a small set of one-dimensional equations. The analysis confirms that the slow relaxation of the first angular mode in expanding systems, previously observed in the relaxation time approximation, persists with a more realistic collision kernel.
