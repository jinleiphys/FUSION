# Thermal photons from heavy ion collisions: A spectral function approach

> We analyze the photon rates from a hadronic gas in equilibrium using chiral reduction formulas and a density expansion. The chiral reduction is carried to second order in the pion density which in principal includes all kinetic processes of the type $X\to πγ$ and $X\to ππγ$. The resulting rates are encoded in the form of vacuum correlation functions which are amenable to experiment. The hadronic rates computed in this work along with the known perturbative QGP rates are integrated over the space-time evolution of a hydrodynamic model tuned to hadronic observables. The resulting yields are compared to the recent photon and low mass dilepton measurements at the SPS and RHIC. Predictions for the LHC are made.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the inclusion of two-pion final states (Wππ) in the chiral reduction expansion provides a substantial enhancement to thermal photon rates from hadronic gas, particularly for soft photons, and that this enhancement is necessary to explain the low-momentum photon excess observed at SPS and RHIC, but the authors caution that the virial expansion parameter becomes of order one at the highest temperatures (κ ≈ 0.84 at 190 MeV), limiting the reliability of the expansion near the transition region.

## Method

The authors compute photon emission rates from a thermal hadronic gas using chiral reduction formulas expanded to second order in pion density, encoding the rates in terms of vacuum correlation functions (VV and AA spectral functions) constrained by e⁺e⁻ annihilation and τ-decay data. These hadronic rates are combined with perturbative QGP rates (including LPM resummation) and integrated over the space-time evolution of a 1+1D relativistic hydrodynamic model tuned to reproduce hadronic observables at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless expansion parameter κ ≈ nπ/(2mπfπ²) takes values κ ≈ 0.18, 0.30, and 0.84 at temperatures of 120, 140, and 190 MeV respectively
- At T = 400 MeV (typical initial temperature in hydrodynamic evolution), αs(πT) = 0.38 versus αs(2πT) = 0.26
- At the transition temperature T = 190 MeV, αs(πT) = 0.75 versus αs(2πT) = 0.40
- The S factor (process-dependent factor for photon extrapolation from dileptons) changes by as much as 30% at M = 300 MeV when q = 1 GeV when including Wππ contributions, leading to a ≈15% discrepancy in the PHENIX extrapolation procedure
- QGP yield enhancement from scale choice: ≈60% at SPS, ≈30% at LHC when decreasing scale from 2πT to πT; ≈40% systematic uncertainty at RHIC
- Initial temperatures in hydrodynamic models: SPS 245 MeV, RHIC 1 336 MeV, RHIC 2 398 MeV, LHC 501 MeV
- Freezeout temperatures: SPS 120 MeV, RHIC 1 140 MeV, RHIC 2 160 MeV, LHC 140 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral reduction approach of Steele (1996) for dileptons and photons to include two-pion final states (Wππ), which had not been previously computed. The results are compared to kinetic theory calculations by Turbide (2003) for πρ→πγ and ππ→ργ processes, and to pion bremsstrahlung calculations by Liu (2007). The hadronic rates are contrasted with perturbative QGP rates from Arnold (2001, 2002) including LPM effects. The paper enables improved comparisons with WA98 photon data at SPS and PHENIX photon and dilepton data at RHIC, and provides predictions for LHC measurements. The authors note that baryon contributions, which could increase rates by a factor of about two at low momentum based on Steele (1997, 1999), were not included in this analysis.
