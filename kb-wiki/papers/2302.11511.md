# Recombination of $B_c$ mesons in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> High-energy heavy-ion collisions have been suggested as a favorable environment for the production of $B_c$ mesons, due to a much larger abundance of charm and bottom quarks compared to elementary reactions. Motivated by recent CMS data for $B_c^+$ production in Pb-Pb($5.02\,$TeV) collisions at the LHC, we deploy a previously developed transport approach for charmonia and bottomonia to evaluate the kinetics of $B_c$ mesons throughout the fireball formed in these reactions. The main inputs to our approach are two transport parameters: the $B_c$'s reaction rate and equilibrium limit. Both quantities are determined by previous calculations via a combination of charm and bottom sectors. In-medium binding energies of $B_c$ mesons are calculated from a thermodynamic $T$-matrix with a lattice-QCD constrained potential, and figure in their inelastic reaction rates. Temperature-dependent equilibrium limits include charm- and bottom-quark fugacities based on their initial production. We compute the centrality dependence of inclusive $B_c$ production and transverse-momentum ($p_T$) spectra using two different recombination models, instantaneous coalescence and resonance recombination. The main uncertainty in the resulting nuclear modification factors, $R_{\rm AA}$, is currently associated with the $B_c$ cross section in elementary $pp$ collisions, caused by the uncertainty in the branching ratio for the $B_c^-\to J/ψμ^-\bar ν$ decay. Our results indicate a large enhancement of the $R_{\rm AA}$ at low $p_T$, with significant regeneration contributions up to $p_T\simeq\,20\,$GeV. Comparisons to CMS data are carried out but firm conclusions will require a more accurate value of the branching ratio, or alternative channels to measure the $B_c$ production in $pp$ collisions.

## Key claim

The authors find that $B_c$ meson production in Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV is significantly enhanced by recombination of charm and bottom quarks in the quark-gluon plasma, but firm conclusions compared to CMS data require a more accurate value of the $B_c^- \to J/\psi \mu^- \bar{\nu}$ branching ratio, which currently introduces the main uncertainty in the $pp$ reference cross section.

## Method

The authors deploy a previously developed transport approach (kinetic rate equation) for charmonia and bottomonia to evaluate $B_c$ meson kinetics throughout the fireball formed in Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV. In-medium binding energies of $B_c$ states are calculated from a thermodynamic $T$-matrix with a lattice-QCD constrained potential, and these binding energies figure in the inelastic reaction rates computed via the quasifree approximation. The centrality dependence of inclusive $B_c$ production and transverse-momentum spectra are computed using two different recombination models: instantaneous coalescence and resonance recombination, with state-of-the-art transported heavy-quark spectra.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum masses of $B_c(1S)$ (6.324 GeV) and $B_c(2S)$ (6.850 GeV) are calculated, compared to experimental values of 6.274 GeV and 6.871 GeV respectively
- Two predicted $P$-wave $B_c$ bound states have masses 6.711 GeV ($B_c(1P)$) and 7.100 GeV ($B_c(2P)$)
- The $S$-wave ground state survives to temperatures of approximately 400 MeV, while the $P$-wave ground state ceases to exist for temperatures above approximately 250 MeV
- The $B_c$ production cross section in $pp$ collisions is estimated as $d\sigma_{B_c(1S)}/dy = 57.8-110.8$ nb, derived from the measured ratio $\sigma_{B_c^-}/\sigma_{b\bar{b}} \cdot \mathcal{B}(B_c^- \to J/\psi \mu^- \bar{\nu}) = (5.04 \pm 0.11 \pm 0.17 \pm 0.18) \times 10^{-5}$ and a branching ratio estimate of $\mathcal{B} = 1.9^{+0.7}_{-0.4}\%$
- Shadowing suppresses the total $c\bar{c}$ ($b\bar{b}$) cross-section by up to 10(0)-30(10)% in most central collisions
- The $B_c$ ground-state meson observed is most likely the pseudoscalar one, with 100% feeddown from the slightly heavier vector state

## Context

This work extends previous transport calculations for charmonia and bottomonia (Zhao 2010, Du 2017) to the $B_c$ sector, motivated by recent CMS data (CMS 2022) that hint at enhanced $B_c^+$ production in Pb-Pb collisions. The study builds on earlier theoretical predictions by Schroedter (2000) and Liu (2012) that found large increases in $B_c$ abundance relative to $pp$ collisions, and more recent instantaneous coalescence model calculations by Chen (2021). The results enable comparisons to CMS data with a $p_T > 6$ GeV cut, but the authors note that alternative decay channels or improved branching ratio measurements are needed to draw firm conclusions about $B_c$ production in $pp$ collisions.
