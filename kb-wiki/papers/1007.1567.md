# A BPS Skyrme model and baryons at large Nc

> Within the class of field theories with the field contents of the Skyrme model, one submodel can be found which consists of the square of the baryon current and a potential term only. For this submodel, a Bogomolny bound exists and the static soliton solutions saturate this bound. Further, already on the classical level, this BPS Skyrme model reproduces some features of the liquid drop model of nuclei. Here, we investigate the model in more detail and, besides, we perform the rigid rotor quantization of the simplest Skyrmion (the nucleon). In addition, we discuss indications that the viability of the model as a low energy effective field theory for QCD is further improved in the limit of a large number of colors N_c.

## Key claim

The BPS Skyrme model, a submodel of the Skyrme model containing only a sextic derivative term (the square of the baryon current) and a potential term, reproduces several features of the liquid drop model of nuclei at the classical level and may serve as a viable low-energy effective field theory for QCD, especially in the large \(N_c\) limit, although there is no obvious large \(N_c\) limit based directly on QCD that would produce this model as its leading order.

## Method

The authors study a Lagrangian consisting of the square of the baryon current (a sextic term in derivatives) plus a potential term, using an SU(2) matrix field parametrized by a real scalar \(\xi\) and a unit vector \(\vec{n}\). They derive the Bogomolny bound for static solutions, find exact hedgehog solutions for the standard Skyrme potential \(V = 1 - \cos \xi\), and perform rigid rotor quantization of the \(B=1\) soliton (the nucleon). The model's symmetries, including volume-preserving diffeomorphisms on base space, are analyzed.

## Key numbers

- Energy of the \(B=1\) soliton (classical): \(E_0 = 64\sqrt{2}\pi \mu \lambda / 15\), set to 931.75 MeV by fixing the \(B=4\) solution mass to the mass of He\(^4\) (3727 MeV).
- Energies for nuclei: \(B=2\): 1863.5 MeV (experiment: 1876 MeV); \(B=3\): 2795.25 MeV (experiment: 2809 MeV); \(B=6\): 5590.5 MeV (experiment: 5601 MeV); \(B=8\): 7454 MeV (experiment: 7455 MeV); \(B=10\): 9317.5 MeV (experiment: 9327 MeV).
- Compacton radius: \(R_B = R_0 \sqrt[3]{B}\) with \(R_0 \approx 1.25\) fm.
- For the standard Skyrme potential, the Bogomolny bound constant \(C[V] = 32\sqrt{2} / (15\pi)\).
- Energy density and topological charge density are zero outside the compacton radius \(\tilde r = |n|^{1/3}\); densities at the center are independent of baryon number.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier proposal of the BPS Skyrme model (Adam et al., 2010) and addresses known problems of the standard Skyrme model: large binding energies (e.g., 23% for \(B=2\) in the standard model vs. zero in the BPS model), crystal-like rather than liquid-like nuclear matter, and strong forces at intermediate distances. The model contrasts with the standard Skyrme model (Skyrme, 1961) and its massive versions (e.g., Adkins et al., 1983), as well as with another BPS generalization by Sutcliffe (2010) based on dimensional reduction of Yang-Mills theory. The BPS model enables a description of nuclei as incompressible liquid droplets with zero binding energy, contact interactions, and a linear energy-charge relation, providing a starting point for adding small perturbations to generate realistic binding energies.
