# Multiphoton Exchange Processes in Ultraperipheral Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> The very strong electromagnetic fields present in ultraperipheral relativistic heavy ion collisions lead to important higher order effects of the electromagnetic interaction. These multiphoton exchange processes are studied using perturbation theory and the sudden or Glauber approximation. In many important cases, the multi-photon amplitudes factorize into independent single-photon amplitudes. These amplitudes have a common impact parameter vector, which induces correlations between the amplitudes. Impact-parameter dependent equivalent-photon spectra for simultaneous excitation are calculated, as well as, impact-parameter dependent gammagamma-luminosities. Excitations, like the multiphonon giant dipole resonances, vector meson production and multiple e+e- pair production can be treated analytically in a bosonic model, analogous to the emission of soft photons in QED.

## Key claim

Multiphoton exchange processes in ultraperipheral relativistic heavy ion collisions can be treated analytically using perturbation theory and the sudden (Glauber) approximation, with the key result that for independent modes the multi-photon amplitudes factorize into products of independent single-photon amplitudes, provided the different processes share a common impact parameter vector which induces correlations between them.

## Method

The authors use a semiclassical approach where one nucleus moves on a straight-line trajectory with impact parameter \(b > R_1+R_2\) while the other is at rest, treating the electromagnetic field as a classical external field. They solve the time-dependent coupled equations for excitation amplitudes using perturbation theory and the sudden approximation, where the collision time \(\tau_{coll}=b/\gamma\) is much smaller than the excitation time \(1/\omega\). For bosonic modes such as giant dipole resonances, vector mesons, and electron-positron pairs, the problem reduces to the excitation of a harmonic oscillator by an external force, yielding analytical solutions in terms of coherent states.

## Key numbers

- Coulomb parameter \(\eta = Z_1 Z_2 e^2/(\hbar v) \approx Z_1 Z_2 \alpha\); for Au-Au (RHIC) or Pb-Pb (LHC) collisions \(\eta \gg 1\), with \(\eta \approx 50\) for these heavy systems
- Probability of \(\rho^0\)-production in grazing collisions: about 0.01 for RHIC and 0.03 for LHC
- Probability of double electromagnetic \(\rho^0\) production: of order \(10^{-3}\) to \(10^{-4}\)
- Grazing angles: about \(5 \times 10^{-5}\) radians for Au-Au at RHIC and \(2 \times 10^{-6}\) radians for Pb-Pb at LHC (collider frame)
- GDR excitation probability parameterization: \(P(b) = S/b^2\) with \(S = 5.45 \times 10^{-5} Z_1^2 N_2 Z_2 A_2^{-2/3}\) fm\(^2\)
- GDR excitation energy approximately \(80\) MeV \(A^{-1/3}\), with oscillation time about 10 fm/\(c\) in the nuclear rest frame, dilated to about 1000 fm/\(c\) at RHIC
- For multiple \(e^+e^-\) pair production, the dimensionless parameter \(\Xi = (Z_1 Z_2 \alpha^2)^2/(m_e b)^2\) is approximately 1 for heavy systems when \(b \gtrsim 1/m_e\)
- Muon pair production total cross sections are smaller than electron pair production by a factor of \((m_e/m_\mu)^2 \approx 2 \times 10^{-5}\)
- The quantity \(x = \omega/E\) is less than \(4 \times 10^{-3}\), \(3 \times 10^{-4}\), and \(1.4 \times 10^{-4}\) for oxygen, tin, and lead ions respectively

## Context

This work provides a unified theoretical framework for multiphoton processes in ultraperipheral collisions, extending earlier treatments of single-photon exchange such as the equivalent photon approximation of Fermi (1924) and the classical Coulomb scattering formalism of Jackson. The paper builds on prior studies of GDR excitation by Baur and Bertulani (1986), multiple \(e^+e^-\) pair production calculations by Hencken et al. (1995), and diffractive vector meson production studies by Klein et al. (1999). The factorization result enables experimental analysis of simultaneous processes such as \(\rho^0\) production accompanied by GDR excitation, which has been studied at STAR (Adler et al., 2002) and is relevant for luminosity determination and triggering at RHIC and LHC. The bosonic model for multiple pair production, analogous to soft photon emission in QED, provides a foundation for understanding correlations in the momenta of outgoing particles that share a common impact parameter.
