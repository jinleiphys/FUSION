# Strong Decays of Light Vector Mesons

> The vector meson strong decays rho-->pi pi, phi-->KK, and K^star-->pi K are studied within a covariant approach based on the ladder-rainbow truncation of the QCD Dyson--Schwinger equation for the quark propagator and the Bethe--Salpeter equation for the mesons. The model preserves the one-loop behavior of QCD in the ultraviolet, has two infrared parameters, and implements quark confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. The 3-point decay amplitudes are described in impulse approximation. The Bethe--Salpeter study motivates a method for estimating the masses for heavier mesons within this model without continuing the propagators into the complex plane. We test the accuracy via the rho, phi and K^{star} masses and then produce estimates of the model results for the a_1 and b_1 masses as well as the mass of the proposed exotic vector pi_1(1400).

## Key claim

The ladder-rainbow truncation of QCD Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations, combined with a Taylor expansion method to avoid analytic continuation of quark propagators into the complex plane, can reproduce vector meson masses and strong decay widths to within about 10% of experimental data, but the Taylor expansion has not fully converged even at third order for the heavier mesons.

## Method

The authors solve the rainbow-truncated Dyson-Schwinger equation for dressed quark propagators and the ladder-truncated Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states in Euclidean metric, using a model effective gluon interaction with two infrared parameters and one-loop QCD ultraviolet behavior. To avoid the numerical difficulty of analytically continuing quark propagators into the complex plane for meson masses above about 1 GeV, they employ a Taylor expansion of the propagator amplitudes around the real spacelike axis up to third order. The strong decay amplitudes for rho to pi pi, phi to K Kbar, and K* to pi K are then computed in impulse approximation using the triangle diagram.

## Key numbers

- rho meson mass: 0.74 GeV from second-order Taylor expansion, compared to model-exact 0.74 GeV and experimental 0.77 GeV
- phi meson mass: 1.03 GeV from second-order Taylor expansion, compared to model-exact 1.02 GeV and experimental 1.02 GeV
- K* meson mass: 0.93 GeV from second-order Taylor expansion, compared to model-exact 0.94 GeV and experimental 0.89 GeV
- rho decay width to pi pi: 149 MeV from second-order Taylor expansion, compared to model-exact 149 MeV and experimental 150 MeV
- phi decay width to K Kbar: 3.6 MeV from second-order Taylor expansion, compared to model-exact 3.7 MeV and experimental 4.4 MeV
- K* decay width to K pi: 51 MeV from second-order Taylor expansion, compared to model-exact 52 MeV and experimental 50 MeV
- Pion mass: 0.138 GeV from second-order Taylor expansion, matching model-exact 0.138 GeV and experimental 0.1385 GeV
- Kaon mass: 0.498 GeV from second-order Taylor expansion, compared to model-exact 0.497 GeV and experimental 0.496 GeV
- Pion decay constant: 0.131 GeV from second-order Taylor expansion, matching model-exact 0.131 GeV and experimental 0.131 GeV
- Kaon decay constant: 0.157 GeV from second-order Taylor expansion, compared to model-exact 0.155 GeV and experimental 0.160 GeV

## Context

This work extends the ladder-rainbow Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter model developed by Maris and Tandy (1997, 1999) for light pseudoscalar and vector mesons, which had previously been applied successfully to meson masses, decay constants, and electromagnetic form factors. The Taylor expansion method for avoiding complex-plane analytic continuation is similar in spirit to an approximation employed by Jain and collaborators (1993). The results enable estimates of masses for heavier mesons such as the a1, b1, and the exotic pi1(1400) within the same model framework without requiring numerically difficult analytic continuation of quark propagators.
