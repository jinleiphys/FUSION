# Time reversal violating Magnetic Quadrupole Moment in heavy deformed nuclei

> The existence of permanent electric dipole moments (EDMs) and magnetic quadrupole moments (MQMs) violate both time reversal invariance ($T$) and parity ($P$). Following the $CPT$ theorem they also violate combined $CP$ symmetry. Nuclear EDM are completely screened in atoms and molecules while interaction between electrons and MQM creates atomic and molecular EDM which can be measured and used to test CP-violation theories. Nuclear MQM are produced by the nucleon-nucleon $T,P$-odd interaction and by nucleon EDM. In this work we study the effect of enhancement of the nuclear MQM due to the nuclear deformation. Using the Nilsson model we calculate the nuclear MQMs for deformed nuclei of experimental interest and the resultant MQM energy shift in diatomic molecules of experimental interest $^{173}$YbF , $^{177,179}$HfF$^+$, $^{181}$TaN, $^{181}$TaO$^+$, $^{229}$ThO and $^{229}$ThF$^+$.

# Time reversal violating Magnetic Quadrupole Moment in heavy deformed nuclei

## Key claim

Nuclear magnetic quadrupole moments (MQMs) are enhanced by an order of magnitude in quadrupole-deformed nuclei compared to spherical nuclei, and the use of the Nilsson model with deformed orbitals rather than spherical wave functions leads to a significant further increase, for example, a factor of 3 for the neutron contribution in hafnium isotopes, making these deformed nuclei promising for detecting T,P-violating effects in the hadronic sector.

## Method

The authors used the Nilsson model, which accounts for nuclear quadrupole deformation via an anisotropic oscillator potential, to calculate the MQM for each nucleon in open and closed shells of heavy deformed nuclei. The MQM arises from a perturbed "spin hedgehog" wavefunction generated by the T,P-odd nucleon-nucleon interaction and from nucleon EDMs, and the total nuclear MQM in the laboratory frame was obtained by summing contributions from all nucleons and applying the rotational transformation between intrinsic and laboratory frames. For each odd-A isotope, the correct deformation factor was determined by filling Nilsson energy levels to reproduce the known nuclear spin and parity.

## Key numbers

- For $^{177}$Hf and $^{179}$Hf, the neutron contribution to the MQM is enhanced by a factor of 3 compared to calculations using spherical orbitals (Flambaum 2014).
- For $^{173}$Yb, the neutron contribution to the MQM has doubled compared to the spherical basis calculation.
- The MQM energy shift in $^{229}$ThO due to the proton EDM is $|W_M M S| = 35 \times 10^{25} d_p$ μHz per $e\cdot$cm, corresponding to $<300$ μHz using current limits $|d_p| < 8.6 \times 10^{-25}$ $e\cdot$cm.
- The MQM energy shift in $^{229}$ThO due to the strong CP parameter is $|W_M M S| = 102 \times 10^{10} \bar{\theta}$ μHz, corresponding to $<250$ μHz using $\bar{\theta} < 2.4 \times 10^{10}$.
- The MQM energy shift in $^{229}$ThO due to quark chromo-EDMs is $|W_M M S| = 56 \times 10^{27} (\tilde{d}_u - \tilde{d}_d)$ μHz per cm, corresponding to $340$ μHz using $\tilde{d}_u - \tilde{d}_d < 6\times 10^{-27}$ cm.
- The minimal Standard Model CKM prediction for the MQM energy shift in $^{229}$ThO is approximately $1$ nHz, four orders of magnitude below current experimental accuracy.
- The proton and neutron MQM contributions from the strong CP parameter are $M_p^0(\bar{\theta}) = 1.9 \times 10^{-29} \bar{\theta}$ $e\cdot$cm$^2$ and $M_n^0(\bar{\theta}) = 2.5 \times 10^{-29} \bar{\theta}$ $e\cdot$cm$^2$.

## Context

This work extends earlier estimates of collective MQM enhancement in deformed nuclei by Flambaum (1994, 2014) which used a spherical wave function basis, replacing that approach with the empirically successful Nilsson model. The calculated MQM energy shifts for molecules such as TaN, TaO$^+$, and ThO are larger by a factor of 2 than those obtained using the spherical basis method by Skripnikov (2015, 2017, 2014) and Fleig (2018). The results enable experimental searches for T,P-violation in diatomic molecules already used for electron EDM measurements, such as $^{229}$ThO, where the ACME collaboration (2014, 2018) achieved 80 μHz accuracy, potentially allowing improved constraints on the strong CP parameter $\bar{\theta}$, nucleon EDMs, and quark chromo-EDMs.
