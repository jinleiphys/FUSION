# Precision measurements of the pp\to π^+pn and pp\to π^+d reactions: importance of long-range and tensor force effects

> Inclusive measurements of pion production in proton--proton collisions in the forward direction were undertaken at 400 and 600 MeV at COSY using the Big Karl spectrograph. The high resolution in the $π^+$ momentum ensured that there was an unambiguous separation of the $pp\to π^+d/π^+pn$ channels. Using these and earlier data, the ratio of the production cross sections could be followed through the $Δ$ region and compared with the predictions of final state interaction theory. Deviations are strongly influenced by long-range terms in the production operator and the tensor force in the final $pn$ system. These have been investigated in a realistic $pp\toπ^+d/π^+pn$ calculation that includes $S \rightleftharpoons D$ channel coupling between the final nucleons. A semi-quantitative understanding of the observed effects is achieved.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the energy dependence of the normalization factor N in the Faldt-Wilkin final state interaction theorem for the ratio of pp to pi+pn and pp to pi+d cross sections is not primarily due to tensor-force-induced S-D interference in the final pn system, but instead arises from long-range components in the pion production operator, particularly those associated with on-shell intermediate pions.

## Method

Inclusive pion production in proton-proton collisions was measured at beam energies of 401 MeV and 601 MeV at COSY using the Big Karl magnetic spectrograph, with the proton beam electron-cooled and stochastically extracted to achieve high momentum resolution. The high resolution (sigma_X = 97 keV/c^2 at the deuteron peak) allowed unambiguous separation of the pp to pi+d two-body final state from the pp to pi+pn continuum, and the cross section ratio R_pn/d was extracted. Model calculations including S-D channel coupling via the tensor force (using the Reid soft core potential) and s-wave pion rescattering were performed for both final states and compared with the data and with calculations where the tensor force was switched off.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies: 401 MeV and 601 MeV (proton kinetic energy T_p)
- Mass resolution at the deuteron peak: sigma_X = 97 keV/c^2
- Normalization factor N at 401 MeV: 0.51 +/- 0.06 (this work)
- Normalization factor N at 601 MeV: 1.06 +/- 0.04 (this work)
- Normalization factor N at 951 MeV: 2.2 +/- 0.1 (from previous work by Abdel-Bary et al., 2005)
- Normalization factor N at 420 MeV: 0.94 +/- 0.07 (from Pleydon et al., 1999)
- Normalization factor N at 500 MeV: 1.11 +/- 0.07 (from Pleydon et al., 1999)
- Constant background subtracted from continuum: 1.5, 4.8, and 0.66 microbarns/(sr MeV) at 401, 601, and 951 MeV respectively
- Upper limit on pp to {pp}_s pi^0 cross section: less than 0.1 microbarn/(sr MeV) (from Betigeri et al., 2002)

## Context

This work extends earlier measurements by Abdel-Bary et al. (2005) at 951 MeV and by Pleydon et al. (1999) at TRIUMF (420 and 500 MeV), providing data below and at the Delta resonance peak. The results enable tests of the Faldt-Wilkin final state interaction theorem (Faldt and Wilkin, 1996) and show that long-range terms in the production operator, rather than tensor force effects, are the dominant cause of deviations from the theorem. The calculations build on earlier two-body work by Niskanen (1978, 1994) and extend them to the full three-body final state, providing a semi-quantitative understanding of the energy dependence of the normalization factor N.
