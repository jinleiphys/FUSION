# Complex valence-space effective operators for observables: the Gamow-Teller transition

> Nuclei in the vicinity of driplines have been receiving a lot of attention in nuclear structure studies. In the nuclei, the continuum coupling is crucial in reproducing weakly-bound and unbound phenomena. To calculate observables of the nuclei as open quantum systems, we have developed valence-space effective operators in the complex-energy Berggren basis using many-body perturbation theory. We focus on the Gamow-Teller $β$ decay in the {\it sd} shell. The two- plus three-nucleon force from the chiral effective field theory (EFT), named EM1.8/2.0, has been used. The Gamow shell model which takes the continuum coupling into account can properly reproduce experimental observations of weakly-bound and unbound states. The $β$-decay isospin asymmetry between the dripline nucleus $^{22}\rm Si$ and its mirror partner $^{22}\rm O$ is reproduced, in which the $s_{1/2}$ continuum plays a key role. Significant Thomas-Ehrman shift is seen through mirror energy differences between the mirror daughters $^{22}\rm Al$ and $^{22}\rm F$, in which the continuum effect plays an important role.

## Key claim

The paper develops valence-space effective operators in the complex-energy Berggren basis using many-body perturbation theory to calculate Gamow-Teller beta decay observables, with the hedge that the continuum coupling and three-nucleon forces are both necessary to reproduce experimental isospin asymmetry in mirror nuclei, and that the quenching factor of 0.78 derived from chiral effective field theory two-body currents is used rather than a purely phenomenological value.

## Method

The authors start from chiral effective field theory two- and three-nucleon forces (EM1.8/2.0) and use the Gamow Hartree-Fock method to generate a Berggren basis in the complex-momentum plane, which includes bound, resonance, and continuum states. They then construct valence-space effective Hamiltonians and effective Gamow-Teller operators via many-body perturbation theory with the extended Kuo-Krenciglowa method for the Hamiltonian and a so-called Theta-box technique for the transition operator, truncating the perturbation series at third order. The effective operators are diagonalized in the sd-shell valence space with O-16 as the core, allowing at most two valence particles in the continuum, and the resulting wave functions are used to compute reduced Gamow-Teller matrix elements.

## Key numbers

* Quenching factor q = 0.78, derived from chiral EFT two-body currents at nuclear density rho = 0.10 fm^-3, used in all Gamow-Teller calculations.
* For the decay Si-22 to Al-22 (1_1+ state), |M_GT| = 0.257 (GSM with EM1.8/2.0), compared to experimental value 0.176(16).
* For the decay Si-22 to Al-22 (1_2+ state), |M_GT| = 1.012 (GSM), compared to experimental value 0.750(41).
* For the decay O-22 to F-22 (1_1+ state), |M_GT| = 0.497 (GSM), compared to experimental value 0.310(32).
* For the decay O-22 to F-22 (1_2+ state), |M_GT| = 1.068 (GSM), compared to experimental value 0.775(77).
* The mirror energy difference for the 1_1+ state between Al-22 and F-22 is reproduced by GSM with EM1.8/2.0, while standard SM with USDC gives smaller values than data.
* The single-neutron separation energy in F-22 is 5.230 MeV, while the single-proton separation energy in Al-22 is close to zero.

## Context

This work extends the Gamow shell model framework developed by Michel et al. (2021) and Zhang et al. (2022) by adding valence-space effective operators for observables beyond energies, specifically Gamow-Teller transitions. It directly addresses the isospin asymmetry observed in the mirror pair Si-22 and O-22 reported by the experiment of PhysRevLett.125.192503 (2020), where standard shell model calculations required adjusting isospin-nonconserving interactions. The method enables ab initio calculations of beta-decay observables for weakly bound and unbound nuclei near driplines, and can be extended to electromagnetic transitions and multipole moments in future work.
