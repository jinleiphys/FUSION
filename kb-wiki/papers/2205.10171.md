# First results from the INDRA-FAZIA apparatus on isospin diffusion in $^{58,64}$Ni+$^{58,64}$Ni systems at Fermi energies

> An investigation of the isospin equilibration process in the reactions $^{58,64}$Ni+$^{58,64}$Ni at two bombarding energies in the Fermi regime ($32\,$MeV/nucleon and $52\,$MeV/nucleon) is presented. Data have been acquired during the first experimental campaign of the coupled INDRA-FAZIA apparatus in GANIL. Selecting from peripheral to semi-central collisions, both the neutron content of the quasiprojectile residue and that of the light ejectiles coming from the quasiprojectile evaporation have been used as probes of the dynamical process of isospin diffusion between projectile and target for the asymmetric systems. The isospin transport ratio technique has been employed. The relaxation of the initial isospin imbalance with increasing centrality has been clearly evidenced. The isospin equilibration appears stronger for the reactions at $32\,$MeV/nucleon, as expected due to the longer projectile-target interaction time than at $52\,$MeV/nucleon. Coherent indications of isospin equilibration come from the quasiprojectile residue characteristics and from particles ascribed to the quasiprojectile decay.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first experimental results from the coupled INDRA-FAZIA apparatus, demonstrating that isospin equilibration between projectile and target in asymmetric Ni+Ni reactions at Fermi energies is clearly observable and stronger at 32 MeV/nucleon than at 52 MeV/nucleon, consistent with the longer projectile-target interaction time at the lower energy.

## Method

The four reactions 58,64Ni+58,64Ni were measured at 32 MeV/nucleon and 52 MeV/nucleon using the coupled INDRA-FAZIA apparatus at GANIL. Events from peripheral to semi-central collisions were selected by requiring a single heavy fragment (Z>14) identified as the quasiprojectile (QP) remnant, accompanied only by light charged particles and intermediate mass fragments (Z=3,4). The isospin transport ratio technique was applied using two probes: the neutron content of the QP remnant and the chemical composition of light ejectiles from QP evaporation, with the reduced QP momentum (p_red = p_z^QP / p_beam in the center-of-mass frame) serving as the centrality order parameter.

## Key numbers

- Bombarding energies: 32 MeV/nucleon and 52 MeV/nucleon for all four Ni+Ni combinations
- QP remnant selection required Z > 14, corresponding to a maximum projectile charge loss of Delta Z = 13 (about 46% of the original projectile charge)
- About 46% (57%) of total statistics survived preliminary conditions for reactions at 32 MeV/nucleon (52 MeV/nucleon)
- Around 40% (30%) of events surviving preliminary conditions were assigned to the QP evaporation channel for reactions at 32 MeV/nucleon (52 MeV/nucleon)
- Total of about 30 x 10^6 events acquired for each measured reaction
- FAZIA blocks covered forward polar angles from 1.4 degrees to 12.6 degrees
- INDRA covered polar angles between 14 degrees and 176 degrees (about 80% of 4pi solid angle)
- Target thickness: 0.3 mg/cm^2 for 58Ni, 0.4 mg/cm^2 for 64Ni
- AMD simulations used impact parameters up to b = 11.6 fm with a triangular distribution
- AMD calculations stopped at 500 fm/c before applying GEMINI++ afterburner

## Context

This work extends previous studies of isospin diffusion in heavy-ion collisions at Fermi energies, such as those by Tsang (2004), Galichet (2009), Lombardo (2010), Barlini (2013), and Camaiani (2021), by simultaneously probing both the QP remnant and its evaporation products using the combined capabilities of INDRA and FAZIA. The results enable future comparisons with transport model predictions to constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy in the nuclear equation of state, building on the isospin transport ratio methodology established by Rami (2000).
