# Light Hadron Masses from Lattice QCD

> This article reviews lattice QCD results for the light hadron spectrum. We give an overview of different formulations of lattice QCD, with discussions on the fermion doubling problem and improvement programs. We summarize recent developments in algorithms and analysis techniques, that render calculations with light, dynamical quarks feasible on present day computer resources. Finally, we summarize spectrum results for ground state hadrons and resonances using various actions.

## Key claim

This review summarizes the current state of lattice QCD calculations for the light hadron spectrum, emphasizing that physically light quark masses have only recently become accessible through advances in efficient lattice regularizations and algorithms, but the results remain dependent on the specific discretization action used and require controlled extrapolations to the physical point, infinite volume, and continuum limit.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD as a nonperturbative regularization of QCD, where spacetime is discretized on a finite lattice with spacing \(a\) and the path integral is evaluated via stochastic Monte Carlo methods using importance sampling. The fermion determinant is handled via pseudofermion fields and the hybrid Monte Carlo (HMC) algorithm, with various fermion discretizations (Wilson, staggered, domain-wall, overlap) discussed to address the fermion doubling problem and improve chiral properties. The review covers techniques for extracting ground-state and excited-state hadron masses from correlation functions, and methods for extrapolating results to the physical quark masses, infinite volume, and continuum limit.

## Key numbers

- The typical QCD scale is \(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}} \sim 250\text{ MeV}\).
- The naive fermion propagator has 15 additional poles at the edges of the Brillouin zone, located at momenta \(\Pi\) where each component \(p_\mu \in \{0, \pi/a\}\).
- The Wilson gauge action (plaquette action) has discretization errors of \(O(a^2)\).
- The nonperturbative running coupling constant for \(N_f=4\) QCD was computed by Sommer (2010) and compared to perturbative calculations.
- The fermion matrix \(M(U)\) for a lattice with \(N = \prod_\mu N_\mu\) sites is typically a \((12 \times N)^2\) matrix (reduced to \((3 \times N)^2\) for staggered fermions).
- The HMC algorithm typically aims for an acceptance rate of \(\sim 80-90\%\).

## Context

This review extends earlier lattice QCD reviews by Montvay (1994), Gupta (1997), Smit (2002), and Gattringer (2010), and summarizes the progress that enabled calculations at physically light quark masses, achieved by reweighting (Aoki, 2009) or directly at large volumes and multiple cutoff values (Durr, 2010). It enables future first-principles predictions of the full light hadron spectrum with controlled systematic uncertainties, including resonances and excited states, and provides the methodological foundation for extending calculations to heavier quark flavors and nuclear physics.
