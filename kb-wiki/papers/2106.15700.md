# Degeneracy patterns of chiral companions at finite temperature

> Chiral symmetry represents a fundamental concept lying at the core of particle and nuclear physics. Its spontaneous breaking in vacuum can be exploited to distinguish chiral hadronic partners, whose masses differ. In fact, the features of this breaking serve as guiding principles for the construction of effective approaches of QCD at low energies, e.g., the chiral perturbation theory, the linear sigma model, the (Polyakov)--Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model, etc. At high temperatures/densities chiral symmetry can be restored bringing the chiral partners to be nearly degenerated in mass. At vanishing baryochemical potential, such restoration follows a smooth transition, and the chiral companions reach this degeneration above the transition temperature. In this work I review how different realizations of chiral partner degeneracy arise in different effective theories/models of QCD. I distinguish the cases where the chiral states are either fundamental degrees of freedom or (dynamically-generated) composed states. In particular, I discuss the intriguing case in which chiral symmetry restoration involves more than two chiral partners, recently addressed in the literature.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that different effective theories and models of QCD predict qualitatively different patterns of mass degeneracy among chiral hadronic partners at finite temperature, and that the pattern depends on whether the states are treated as fundamental degrees of freedom or as dynamically-generated composite states, with the recently studied case of more-than-two chiral partners presenting a particularly intriguing scenario.

## Method

The author reviews how chiral symmetry restoration manifests in several effective approaches to low-energy QCD, including chiral perturbation theory, the linear sigma model, and the (Polyakov-)Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. The review distinguishes between two classes of chiral partners: those that appear as fundamental fields in the Lagrangian and those that emerge as dynamically-generated composite states (e.g., from scattering amplitudes or Bethe-Salpeter equations). The discussion focuses on the behavior of hadronic masses as functions of temperature at vanishing baryochemical potential, where the transition is smooth.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing program of using chiral symmetry restoration to understand hadronic spectra at finite temperature, which underpins effective approaches such as chiral perturbation theory, the linear sigma model, and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (pioneered by Nambu and Jona-Lasinio, 1961). It contrasts the standard two-partner degeneracy pattern with the more complex multi-partner case recently addressed in the literature. The review enables future work to discriminate between competing effective theories by comparing their predicted degeneracy patterns against lattice QCD results and experimental data from heavy-ion collisions.
