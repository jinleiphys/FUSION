# Charge-symmetry breaking forces and isospin mixing in 8Be

> We report Green's function Monte Carlo calculations of isospin-mixing (IM) matrix elements for the 2+, 1+, and 3+ T=0,1 pairs of states at 16--19 MeV excitation in 8Be. The realistic Argonne v18 (AV18) two-nucleon and Illinois-7 three-nucleon potentials are used to generate the nuclear wave functions. Contributions from the full electromagnetic interaction and strong class III charge-symmetry-breaking (CSB) components of the AV18 potential are evaluated. We also examine two theoretically more complete CSB potentials based on rho-omega mixing, tuned to give the same neutron-neutron scattering length as AV18. The contribution of these different CSB potentials to the 3H-3He, 7Li-7Be, and 8Li-8B isovector energy differences is evaluated and reasonable agreement with experiment is obtained. Finally, for the 8Be IM calculation we add the small class IV CSB terms coming from one-photon, one-pion, and one-rho exchange, as well as rho-omega mixing. The expectation values of the three CSB models vary by up to 20% in the isovector energy differences, but only by 10% or less in the IM matrix element. The total matrix element gives 85--90% of the experimental IM value of -145 keV for the 2+ doublet, with about two thirds coming from the Coulomb interaction. We also report the IM matrix element to the first 2+ state at 3 MeV excitation, which is the final state for various tests of the Standard Model for beta-decay.

## Key claim

Green's function Monte Carlo calculations of isospin-mixing matrix elements for the 2+, 1+, and 3+ T=0,1 state pairs in 8Be at 16-19 MeV excitation produce 85-90% of the experimental isospin-mixing value of -145 keV for the 2+ doublet, with about two thirds coming from the Coulomb interaction, but the three charge-symmetry-breaking models tested vary by up to 20% in isovector energy differences while varying by only 10% or less in the isospin-mixing matrix element.

## Method

The authors performed Green's function Monte Carlo calculations using the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Illinois-7 three-nucleon potentials to generate nuclear wave functions for pure isospin states of 8Be. They evaluated contributions from the full electromagnetic interaction and strong class III charge-symmetry-breaking components of AV18, then examined two alternative charge-symmetry-breaking potentials based on rho-omega mixing tuned to reproduce the same neutron-neutron scattering length as AV18. For the 8Be isospin-mixing calculation, they added small class IV charge-symmetry-breaking terms from one-photon, one-pion, and one-rho exchange as well as rho-omega mixing.

## Key numbers

* The experimental isospin-mixing matrix element for the 2+ doublet is -145(3) keV, with mixing parameters alpha_2 = 0.7705(15) and beta_2 = 0.6375(19)
* The total calculated isospin-mixing matrix element for the 2+ states ranges from -127 to -131 keV across the three charge-symmetry-breaking models, giving 85-90% of the experimental value
* The Coulomb contribution to the isospin-mixing matrix element is -89 keV, about 30% larger than Barker's original estimate of -67 keV
* The strong class III charge-symmetry-breaking term v^CSB contributes -24 to -28 keV to the isospin-mixing matrix element
* For the 1+ doublet, the empirical isospin-mixing matrix element is -103(14) keV with mixing parameter alpha_1 = 0.21(3)
* For the 3+ doublet, the empirical isospin-mixing matrix element ranges from -47 to -71 keV
* The isovector energy difference E(3He)-E(3H) is 764 keV experimentally, with calculated values of 755(1), 761(1), and 769(1) keV for AV18, AV18(s), and AV18(L) respectively
* The isovector energy difference E(7Be)-E(7Li) is 1645 keV experimentally, with calculated values of 1611(10), 1618(10), and 1633(10) keV
* The isovector energy difference [E(8B)-E(8Li)]/2 is 1770 keV experimentally, with calculated values of 1813(11), 1811(11), and 1820(11) keV
* The neutron-neutron scattering length used is -18.5(4) fm from pi- d -> nn gamma experiments, with a current best value of -18.6(4) fm

## Context

This work extends earlier variational Monte Carlo evaluations of isospin mixing in 8Be by Wiringa et al. (2000) using more accurate Green's function Monte Carlo methods, and builds on the original analysis by Barker (1966) who found the Coulomb contribution to the isospin-mixing matrix element was only about half the required magnitude. The calculations enable better constraints on charge-symmetry-breaking forces in nuclear interactions, with the 8Be isospin-mixing system being one of the best for constraining such forces since the strong charge-symmetry-breaking terms are relatively more important here than in isovector energy differences between mirror nuclei. The isospin-mixing matrix element to the first 2+ state at 3 MeV excitation is also reported, which is the final state for beta-decay tests of the Standard Model.
