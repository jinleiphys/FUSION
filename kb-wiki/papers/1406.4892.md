# Large Nc gauge theory with quarks in high representations

> This paper explores a novel tractable regime for ultraviolet-complete quantum field theories -- the large Nc limit of non-abelian gauge theories with quarks in high dimensional representations (scaling with Nc faster than Nc^2 ), such as quarks with 'a' fundamental indices with a greater than or equal to 3. A smooth and nontrivial Nc limit can be obtained if g^2 Nc^(a-1) is held fixed instead of the standard t Hooft coupling g^2 Nc as Nc goes to infinity where g is the gauge coupling. SU(Nc) gauge theories in 3+1 dimensions are not asymptotically free at large Nc when they contain quarks in representations whose dimensions scale faster than Nc^2 and hence are not ultraviolet complete. However, in lower space-time dimensions (2+1, 1+1), for any Nc, renormalization group flow for such theories always has a stable ultraviolet fixed point at g = 0; the theory is thus ultraviolet complete. For the case of massless quarks, the theory has an infrared fixed point. For massive quarks, the theory is confining. The confining scale is parametrically of the order Nc^((2-a)/(4-d)) and is driven to zero at large Nc for theories with a > 2 and d < 4 where d is the space time dimension.

# Large Nc gauge theory with quarks in high representations

## Key claim

The paper claims that a smooth and nontrivial large Nc limit exists for non-abelian gauge theories with quarks in representations whose dimensions scale faster than Nc^2 (a ≥ 3), provided that g^2 Nc^(a-1) is held fixed instead of the standard 't Hooft coupling g^2 Nc, and that the theory is considered in lower space-time dimensions (2+1 or 1+1) where it remains ultraviolet complete.

## Method

The authors analyze SU(Nc) gauge theories in 2+1 and 1+1 space-time dimensions with quarks in high-dimensional representations (scaling as Nc^a with a ≥ 3), deriving the appropriate scaling of the coupling constant from one-loop contributions to the gluon polarization tensor. They compute the beta function at large Nc, showing it is dominated by quark loops, and solve the renormalization group equation to obtain the running coupling. Correlation functions for meson-meson and glueball-glueball sources are computed at leading order in the 1/Nc expansion using resummed gluon propagators.

## Key numbers

- The dimension of the quark representation scales as R ∼ Nc^a with a ≥ 3
- The confining scale scales as Λ_conf/Λ ∼ Nc^((2-a)/(4-d)), giving for example 1/Nc for a three-index representation in 2+1 dimensions
- The one-loop beta function coefficient in 2+1 dimensions is c_d = 1/32, and in 1+1 dimensions is c_d = 1/(2π)
- The infrared fixed point occurs at g̃^2 Nc^(a-1)/(a-1)! = (4-d)/(2 c_d N_F)
- The leading-order meson-meson correlation function for three-index anti-symmetric representation in 2+1 dimensions scales as Nc^3/6π times the loop integral
- The leading-order glueball-glueball correlation function scales as Nc^2

## Context

This work extends the large Nc analysis of gauge theories beyond the standard 't Hooft limit (1973) with fundamental quarks and the two-index representations studied by Corrigan and Ramond (1979) and later by Armoni, Bolognesi, and others. The authors note that theories with quarks in representations with three or more indices have not been systematically studied at large Nc, partly because they lack asymptotic freedom in 3+1 dimensions. The paper shows that in lower dimensions these theories are well-defined and exhibit qualitatively similar behavior to gauge theories with fixed Nc and many flavors of quarks in any representation. The results enable exact calculation of correlation functions and beta functions in a regime where quark loops dominate over gluon dynamics.
