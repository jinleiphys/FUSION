# Phenomenological view on baryon-baryon potentials from lattice QCD simulations

> A qualitative discussion on the range of the potentials as they result from the phenomenological meson-exchange picture and from lattice simulations by the HAL QCD Collaboration is presented. For the former pion- and/or $η$-meson exchange are considered together with the scalar-isoscalar component of correlated $ππ/K \bar K$ exchange. It is observed that the intuitive expectation for the behavior of the baryon-baryon potentials for large separations, associated with the exchange of one and/or two pions, does not always match with the potentials extracted from the lattice simulations. Only in cases where pion exchange provides the longest ranged contribution, like in the $ΞN$ system, a reasonable qualitative agreement between the phenomenological and the lattice QCD potentials is found for baryon-baryon separations of $r \gtrsim 1$ fm. For the $ΩN$ and $ΩΩ$ interactions where isospin conservation rules out one-pion exchange a large mismatch is observed, with the potentials by the HAL QCD Collaboration being much longer ranged and much stronger at large distances as compared to the phenomenological expectation. This casts some doubts on the applicability of using these potentials in few- or many-body systems.

## Key claim

The intuitive expectation that baryon-baryon potentials at large separations (r ≳ 1 fm) should be governed by one- and two-pion exchange does not always match the potentials extracted from lattice QCD simulations by the HAL QCD Collaboration; only for systems where pion exchange provides the longest-ranged contribution (like ΞN) is there reasonable qualitative agreement, while for ΩN and ΩΩ, where isospin conservation forbids one-pion exchange, the lattice potentials are much longer-ranged and stronger than the phenomenological meson-exchange picture predicts.

## Method

The authors constructed phenomenological baryon-baryon potentials using one-pion and/or η-meson exchange combined with scalar-isoscalar correlated ππ/K̅K exchange (σ or f₀(980) exchange), with coupling strengths taken from SU(3) flavor symmetry relations and from the microscopic dispersion-theoretic model of Reuber (1996). They then compared these phenomenological potentials, evaluated for S-wave states at baryon-baryon separations r ≳ 1 fm, directly with the local potentials published by the HAL QCD Collaboration for the NN (¹S₀), ΞN (³S₁, I=0), ΩN (⁵S₂), and ΩΩ (¹S₀) channels, all from lattice simulations at nearly physical pion mass (mπ = 146 MeV).

## Key numbers

* For NN scattering in the ¹S₀ partial wave, the sum of one-pion exchange and correlated ππ exchange (effective σ coupling g²_s/4π = 7.77 at mσ = 550 MeV) reproduces the shape and magnitude of the empirical Reid and Argonne V18 potentials in the range r ≈ 1–2 fm.
* For ΞN (³S₁, I=0), the phenomenological potential (pion exchange at ~20% of the NN strength plus σ exchange with g²_s/4π = 1.52) agrees qualitatively with the HAL QCD lattice potential at r ≳ 1 fm; the ranges are compatible, and adjusting the σ strength by a factor of ~2 makes the two potentials practically coincide in the region r ≈ 1–2 fm.
* For ΩΩ (¹S₀), the η-exchange contribution (using the lattice-QCD-based coupling fΔΔπ ≈ fNNπ/20) plus effective f₀(980) exchange (g²_s/4π = 1.77) yields a potential that is much shorter-ranged than the HAL QCD lattice potential; the lattice potential at r ≈ 1 fm is roughly reproduced only if the f₀ coupling is enhanced by up to a factor of (9/2)² ≈ 20, but the r-dependence remains mismatched.
* The η–π⁰ mixing strength is estimated as fΩΩπ ≈ 0.0106 fΩΩη, providing a quantitative measure of the isospin-violating contribution that would allow pion coupling to the Ω.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological meson-exchange framework of Reuber (1996) and Machleidt (1987) to confront the lattice-QCD potentials of the HAL QCD Collaboration (Sasaki 2018 for ΞN, Iritani 2018 for ΩN, Gongyo 2017 for ΩΩ). It highlights a striking discrepancy: for ΩN and ΩΩ, where one-pion exchange is forbidden by isospin conservation, the lattice potentials are much longer-ranged and stronger than expected from η and correlated ππ/K̅K exchange. The authors suggest that non-local effects, which become important when pion exchange is absent, may produce artifacts when represented as local potentials, echoing concerns raised by Yamazaki (2017), Aoki (2017), and Yamazaki (2018) about the HAL QCD method. This casts doubt on the applicability of these lattice-derived potentials in few- or many-body calculations.
