# Possible evidence of extended objects inside the proton

> Recent data on the Nachtmann moments of the unpolarized proton structure function F2p, obtained at low momentum transfer with the CLAS detector at Jefferson Lab, are interpreted in terms of the dominance of the elastic coupling of the virtual photon with extended substructures inside the proton. The CLAS data exhibit a new type of scaling behavior and the resulting scaling function can be interpreted as a constituent form factor consistent with the elastic nucleon data. A constituent size of ~ 0.2 - 0.3 fm is obtained.

# Possible evidence of extended objects inside the proton

## Key claim

The CLAS data on Nachtmann moments of the unpolarized proton structure function F2p at low Q2 exhibit a new scaling behavior consistent with the dominance of elastic coupling of the virtual photon with extended constituent quarks, but this interpretation holds only in a local duality sense and only for moment orders n = 4, 6, 8 (excluding n = 2), and the extracted constituent quark size of approximately 0.2–0.3 fm depends on the specific model parameters used for the internal constituent quark motion.

## Method

The authors analyzed Nachtmann transverse moments M_n^(T)(Q^2) of order n = 2, 4, 6, 8 extracted from CLAS data on the proton structure function F2p measured at Jefferson Lab in the resonance region (W < 2.5 GeV) for Q^2 below approximately 4.5 (GeV/c)^2. They applied a generalized two-stage model where hadrons are composed of constituent quarks with partonic substructure, assuming that at low Q^2 (between Lambda_QCD^2 and Lambda_chi^2, i.e., 0.1–0.2 to 1–2 (GeV/c)^2) the elastic coupling of the virtual photon with constituent quarks dominates over inelastic channels. The ratio R_n^(T)(Q^2) of experimental moments to theoretical moments (calculated from a model of constituent quark momentum distributions) was constructed and tested for scaling behavior independent of the moment order n.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark size r_Q = 0.21 fm obtained from a monopole form factor fit to the scaling function
- Constituent quark size range: approximately 0.2 to 0.3 fm, depending on model parameters
- Q^2 range of applicability of the generalized two-stage model: 0.1–0.2 to 1–2 (GeV/c)^2
- Maximum Q^2 of CLAS measurements: approximately 4.5 (GeV/c)^2
- Resonance region cutoff: W < 2.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the two-stage model of Altarelli (1974), originally developed for deep inelastic scattering, to lower Q^2 values around and below the chiral symmetry breaking scale. The analysis uses CLAS data from Jefferson Lab (2003) and builds on the theoretical framework of Petronzio, Simula, and Ricco (2003). The extracted constituent quark form factor is consistent with elastic nucleon data when calculated using the covariant light-front approach of the nucleon model (2003). The results suggest constituent quarks as intermediate substructures between hadrons and current quarks/gluons of QCD, and the authors note that forthcoming data on longitudinal and polarized structure functions will provide further tests.
