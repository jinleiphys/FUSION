# Neutrino nuclear response and photo nuclear reaction

> Photo nuclear reactions are shown to be used for studying neutrino/weak nuclear responses involved in astro-neutrino nuclear interactions and double beta decays. Charged current weak responses for ground and excited states are studied by using photo nuclear reactions through isobaric analog states of those states, while neutral current weak responses for excited states are studied by using photo nuclear reactions through the excited states. The weak interaction strengths are studied by measuring the cross sections of the photo nuclear reactions, and the spin and parity of the state are studied by measuring angular correlations of particles emitted from the photo nuclear reactions. Medium-energy polarized photons obtained from laser photons scattered off GeV electrons are very useful. Nuclear responses studied by photo nuclear reactions are used to evaluate neutrino/weak nuclear responses, i.e. nuclear beta and double beta matrix elements and neutrino nuclear interactions, and to verify theoretical calculations for them.

## Key claim

Photo nuclear reactions can be used to study neutrino/weak nuclear responses for charged-current (CC) beta-plus and neutral-current (NC) transitions, but the isovector weak matrix element is not exactly the same as the corresponding electromagnetic matrix element because the electromagnetic coupling includes both isovector and isoscalar components that are renormalized differently by nuclear spin-isospin correlations and nuclear medium effects.

## Method

The authors theoretically derive the relationship between weak and electromagnetic transition matrix elements for isobaric analog state (IAS) gamma transitions, showing that the beta-plus matrix element for a transition between final and initial states can be obtained by measuring the analogous gamma absorption to the IAS. They calculate photo nuclear cross sections for E1 and M1 excitations on even-even nuclei and compute angular distributions of emitted neutrons from photo nuclear reactions on 76Se and 100Mo using linearly polarized photons, considering natural parity (1-, 2+) and unnatural parity (1+, 2-) excitations with various orbital configurations of the outgoing neutron.

## Key numbers

- Integrated photo nuclear cross section for E1 excitation via IAS: 2.9 x 10^-3 MeV fm^2
- Integrated photo nuclear cross section for M1 excitation via IAS: 2.7 x 10^-3 MeV fm^2
- Counting rate for E1 excitation with a 10 g/cm^2 target: Y(E1) = 1.7 x 10^-6 x epsilon x N_gamma per second
- Counting rate for M1 excitation with a 10 g/cm^2 target: Y(M1) = 1.6 x 10^-6 x epsilon x N_gamma per second
- Typical photon intensity needed for realistic experiments: N_gamma approximately 10^8-10^9 per MeV per second
- Gamma width formula for E1 transitions: Gamma_gamma(E1) = 1.59 x 10^15 x E^3 x M(E1)^2 per second
- Gamma width formula for M1 transitions: Gamma_gamma(M1) = 1.76 x 10^13 x E^3 x M(M1)^2 per second
- Typical isovector matrix elements for medium-heavy nuclei (A=100, T=8, K=4): M1(E1) approximately 0.125, M1(M1) approximately 1.15
- Resulting matrix elements for photon absorption into IAS: M(E1) approximately 0.03, M(M1) approximately 0.28
- IAR excitation energy around 8 MeV in the A=100 mass region

## Context

This work extends the use of isobaric analog state gamma transitions, originally developed by Fujita (1967) and Ejiri (1968), to study weak nuclear responses for double beta decay and astro-neutrino interactions. It complements charge-exchange reactions such as the (3He,t) reaction at RCNP used for CC beta-minus responses (Thies 2012) and (t,3He) reactions requiring radioactive triton beams (Guillaume 2011). The proposed photo nuclear method enables studies of CC beta-plus and NC responses using well-understood electromagnetic interactions with polarized photons from facilities like the High Intensity Gamma-ray Source (HIGS) at Duke (Weller 2009) and the NewSUBARU laser-backscattered source (Miyamoto 2012), providing experimental verification for theoretical calculations of nuclear matrix elements needed for neutrino mass extraction from neutrinoless double beta decay.
