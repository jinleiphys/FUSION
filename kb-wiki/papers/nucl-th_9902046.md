# The Reaction $pp\to p ΛK^+$ Near Threshold

> We analyze the recent total cross section data for $pp \to pΛK^+$ near threshold measured at COSY. Using an effective range approximation for the on-shell $pΛ$ S-wave final state interaction we extract from these data the combination ${\cal K} = \sqrt{2|K_s|^2+|K_t|^2} = 0.38$ fm$^4$ of the singlet ($K_s$) and triplet ($K_t$) threshold transition amplitudes. We present an exploratory calculation of various (tree-level) vector and pseudoscalar meson exchange diagrams. Pointlike $ω$-exchange alone and the combined ($ρ^0,ω,K^{*+} $)-exchange can explain the experimental value of $\cal K$. The pseudoscalar meson exchanges based on a SU(3) chiral Lagrangian turn out to be too large. However, when adding $π^0$-exchange in combination with the resonant $πN \to S_{11}(1650)\to K Λ$ transition and introducing monopole form factors with a cut-off $Λ_c= 1.5$ GeV one is again able to reproduce the experimental value of $\cal K$. More exclusive measurements are necessary to reveal the details of the $pp\to pΛK^+$ production mechanism.

## Key claim

The combination of singlet and triplet threshold transition amplitudes extracted from COSY data, \( \mathcal{K} = \sqrt{2|K_s|^2+|K_t|^2} = 0.38 \) fm\(^4\), can be reproduced by several distinct subprocesses (pointlike \(\omega\)-exchange alone, combined vector meson exchange, \(K^+\)-exchange alone, or \(S_{11}(1650)\)-resonance excitation), meaning the unpolarized total cross section data alone cannot distinguish between production mechanisms.

## Method

The author parametrizes the threshold T-matrix for \(pp \to p\Lambda K^+\) in terms of a singlet amplitude \(K_s\) and a triplet amplitude \(K_t\). The energy dependence of the total cross section near threshold is modeled using three-body phase space and an effective range approximation for the on-shell \(p\Lambda\) S-wave final state interaction, with scattering length \(\bar a = 2.0\) fm and effective range \(\bar r = 1.0\) fm. A best fit to seven COSY data points yields the experimental value of \(\mathcal{K}\). Relativistic tree-level Feynman diagram calculations are then performed for vector meson (\(\rho^0, \omega, K^{*+}\)) and pseudoscalar meson (\(\pi^0, \eta, K^+\)) exchange diagrams using SU(3) symmetric Lagrangians, and the results are compared to the extracted \(\mathcal{K}\).

## Key numbers

*   Extracted combination of threshold amplitudes: \(\mathcal{K} = \sqrt{2|K_s|^2+|K_t|^2} = 0.38\) fm\(^4\) from a fit to COSY data.
*   Pointlike \(\omega\)-exchange alone yields \(\mathcal{K}^{(\omega)} = 0.39\) fm\(^4\).
*   Total vector meson (\(\rho^0, \omega, K^{*+}\)) exchange yields \(\mathcal{K}^{(V)} = 0.45\) fm\(^4\).
*   Pointlike \(K^+\)-exchange alone yields \(\mathcal{K}^{(K)} = 0.41\) fm\(^4\).
*   Pseudoscalar meson exchange contributions from the SU(3) chiral Lagrangian give \(K_t^{(ps)} = -1.16\) fm\(^4\) and \(K_s^{(ps)} = -0.25\) fm\(^4\), leading to \(\mathcal{K}^{(V+ps)} = 1.28\) fm\(^4\) when combined with vector exchange.
*   With monopole form factors (cut-off \(\Lambda_c = 1.5\) GeV) and including the \(S_{11}(1650)\)-resonance contribution, the total sum gives \(\mathcal{K}^{(\rm tot)} = 0.40\) fm\(^4\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kaiser (1999) for pion and eta production (\(pp\to pp\pi^0, pn\pi^+, pp\eta\)) to the strangeness production channel \(pp\to p\Lambda K^+\), using the same phenomenological separation of on-shell final state interaction from the production process. It contradicts the notion that a single mechanism dominates, showing that pointlike \(\omega\)-exchange, \(K^+\)-exchange, and the \(S_{11}(1650)\)-resonance excitation (emphasized by Faeldt and Sibirtsev) can each individually reproduce the data. The analysis enables future work by demonstrating that more exclusive measurements of angular distributions and polarization observables are necessary to distinguish between production mechanisms, and it provides a benchmark value \(\mathcal{K}=0.38\) fm\(^4\) for testing models.
