# Tests of the random phase approximation for transition strengths

> We investigate the reliability of transition strengths computed in the random-phase approximation (RPA), comparing with exact results from diagonalization in full $0\hbarω$ shell-model spaces. The RPA and shell-model results are in reasonable agreement for most transitions; however some very low-lying collective transitions, such as isoscalar quadrupole, are in serious disagreement. We suggest the failure lies with incomplete restoration of broken symmetries in the RPA. Furthermore we prove, analytically and numerically, that standard statements regarding the energy-weighted sum rule in the RPA do not hold if an exact symmetry is broken.

## Key claim

The random-phase approximation (RPA) yields transition strengths in reasonable agreement with exact shell-model results for most transitions, but fails seriously for very low-lying collective transitions such as isoscalar quadrupole; the authors suggest this failure arises from incomplete restoration of broken symmetries in the RPA, and prove that standard energy-weighted sum rule statements in the RPA do not hold when an exact symmetry is broken.

## Method

The authors compared transition strengths computed in the random-phase approximation (RPA) against exact results from full \(0\hbar\omega\) shell-model diagonalization for several nuclei in the \(sd\) and \(pf\) shells, using the Wildenthal USD interaction in the \(sd\) shell and the monopole-modified Kuo-Brown KB3 interaction in the \(pf\) shell. They computed isoscalar and isovector electric quadrupole (E2), spin-flip (SF), and Gamow-Teller (GT) transition strength distributions, using bare charges (\(e_p=1\), \(e_n=0\)) for E2 operators. The RPA calculations started from self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) mean-field solutions that break rotational symmetry, and the authors compared total strengths, centroids, and widths of the strength distributions between the two methods.

## Key numbers

- For isovector E2 in \(^{20}\)Ne: total strength \(S_0\) = 0.98 (SM) vs 1.15 (RPA); centroid \(\bar S\) = 14.53 MeV (SM) vs 11.92 MeV (RPA); width \(\Delta S\) = 3.47 MeV (SM) vs 2.44 MeV (RPA)
- For isovector E2 in \(^{44}\)Ti: total strength \(S_0\) = 2.15 (SM) vs 1.90 (RPA); centroid \(\bar S\) = 8.23 MeV (SM) vs 6.68 MeV (RPA); width \(\Delta S\) = 2.86 MeV (SM) vs 1.98 MeV (RPA)
- For isoscalar E2 in \(^{20}\)Ne: total strength \(S_0\) = 7.86 (SM) vs 0.19 (RPA); centroid \(\bar S\) = 2.12 MeV (SM) vs 9.81 MeV (RPA); width \(\Delta S\) = 1.92 MeV (SM) vs 2.30 MeV (RPA)
- For isoscalar E2 in \(^{24}\)Mg: total strength \(S_0\) = 12.57 (SM) vs 0.51 (RPA); centroid \(\bar S\) = 2.13 MeV (SM) vs 7.99 MeV (RPA); width \(\Delta S\) = 2.09 MeV (SM) vs 2.75 MeV (RPA)
- For isoscalar E2 in \(^{22}\)Na: total strength \(S_0\) = 9.53 (SM) vs 7.49 (RPA); centroid \(\bar S\) = 1.47 MeV (SM) vs 1.27 MeV (RPA); width \(\Delta S\) = 2.63 MeV (SM) vs 1.82 MeV (RPA)
- For spin-flip in \(^{28}\)Si: total strength \(S_0\) = 5.82 (SM) vs 5.20 (RPA); centroid \(\bar S\) = 12.75 MeV (SM) vs 11.62 MeV (RPA); width \(\Delta S\) = 4.34 MeV (SM) vs 1.89 MeV (RPA)

## Context

This work extends previous tests of RPA against exact models for binding energies by Stetcu and Johnson (2002) to transition strengths, filling a gap where broader tests of RPA transition strengths against exact shell-model calculations had not been done. The results contradict the common assumption that the RPA energy-weighted sum rule holds when symmetries are broken, which has implications for ambitious RPA calculations that allow broken symmetries in the mean-field, such as those by Bertsch and collaborators (2000). The findings enable future investigations into whether the quasiparticle RPA (QRPA), which breaks particle number conservation, suffers from similar issues with missing low-lying collective strength.
