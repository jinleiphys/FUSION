# Constraining twin stars with cold neutron star cooling data

> We investigate the influence of a phase transition from hadronic matter to a deconfined quark phase inside a neutron star on its cooling behaviour including the appearance of twin star solutions in the mass-radius diagram. We find that while the inferred neutrino luminosity of cold transiently-accreting star in MXB $1659\text{-}29$ is reproduced in all of the constructed twin star models, the luminosity of a colder source, the neutron star in SAX J$1808.4\text{-}3658$, cannot be described by equations of state with quark-hadron transition densities below $1.7$ saturation density, suggesting that twin stars with such low density transitions to the quark phase are not realized in nature. We also discuss how constraints to the quark-hadron phase transition density are strongly dependent on the cooling effectiveness of neutrino reactions in the quark phase.

## Key claim

The paper finds that twin star models (hybrid stars with a quark-hadron phase transition that produce two stars of the same mass but different radii) can reproduce the neutrino luminosity of the warmer transiently-accreting neutron star in MXB 1659-29, but cannot describe the luminosity of the colder source in SAX J1808.4-3658 when the quark-hadron transition density is below 1.7 times nuclear saturation density, suggesting such low-density transitions are not realized in nature.

## Method

The authors constructed four hadronic equations of state (EOS I-IV) using relativistic mean field theory with varying symmetry energy slope (L=50 or 90 MeV), Dirac effective mass (m*=0.55 or 0.60), and omega-meson self-interaction parameter (zeta=0 or 0.02). They built hybrid star EOSs by replacing the hadronic EOS with a constant-speed-of-sound quark phase at a chosen transition pressure using a Maxwell construction, selecting parameters that generate twin star configurations. They then calculated neutrino luminosities from nucleonic and quark direct Urca cooling processes, including superfluid gap models, and compared these to the inferred luminosities of two transiently-accreting neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- The neutron star in MXB 1659-29 has an inferred surface temperature at infinity of 55 eV, taken from Cumming (2017) and Brown (2018)
- The quark-hadron transition density below which twin star models cannot reproduce the SAX J1808.4-3658 luminosity is 1.7 times nuclear saturation density
- The dUrca threshold density for purely hadronic EOS I is 3.51 times saturation density, for EOS II is 3.55, for EOS III is 1.77, and for EOS IV is 1.78
- The maximum masses for the purely hadronic EOSs range from 2.09 solar masses (EOS II) to 2.70 solar masses (EOS I)
- The smallest radius at two solar masses among the hadronic EOSs is 11.85 km, and the largest is 13.7 km
- The tidal deformability at 1.4 solar masses for EOS II hybrid stars with phase transitions above 4 times saturation density is Lambda = 596, marginally above the GW170817 upper limit of Lambda = 580

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hybrid star cooling by de Carvalho (2015) and Lyra (2023), and builds on estimates of quark direct Urca cooling effectiveness in hybrid stars by Mendes (2022). It is the first study focusing specifically on twin stars without strangeness in the hadronic phase, using cold transiently-accreting neutron stars as constraints. The results enable future work to set upper limits on the quark-hadron phase transition density and to potentially identify twin star candidates through combined mass-radius and cooling observations.
