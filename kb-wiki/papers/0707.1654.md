# Strong pickup-channel coupling effects in proton scattering: the case of p + Be-10

> The dynamic polarization potential (DPP) contribution to the effective proton-nucleus interaction, that is due to the coupling of deuteron channels, is evaluated by applying $S_{lj} \to V(r)$ inversion to the elastic channel $S$-matrix from coupled reaction channel calculations of proton elastic scattering. This was done for protons scattering from $^{10}$Be at 12, 13, 14, 15, and 16 MeV; non-orthogonality corrections were included. We find a consistent pattern of a repulsive real and an absorptive imaginary DPP, with the absorption shifted to a larger radius. This is consistent with what has been found for proton scattering from the neutron skin nucleus $^8$He. The DPP is not of a form that can be represented by a renormalization of the bare potential, and has properties suggesting an underlying non-local process. We conclude that deuteron channels cannot be omitted from a full theoretical description of the proton-nucleus interaction (optical potential).

# Strong pickup-channel coupling effects in proton scattering: the case of p + Be-10

## Key claim

The dynamic polarization potential (DPP) arising from deuteron pickup channel coupling in proton scattering on ¹⁰Be is consistently repulsive in its real part and absorptive in its imaginary part, with absorption shifted outward in radius, but the magnitude of the effect is smaller than for the previously studied case of p + ⁸He, likely due to the more negative Q-value (−4.6 MeV versus −0.36 MeV) and smaller spectroscopic factor (1.58 versus 2.9).

## Method

Coupled reaction channel (CRC) calculations including non-orthogonality corrections and complex remnant terms were performed for p + ¹⁰Be elastic scattering at five incident proton energies (12, 13, 14, 15, and 16 MeV), using the code FRESCO. The deuteron exit channel was treated with continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) to account for breakup effects. The elastic-channel S-matrix from the CRC calculations was then subjected to S_lj → V(r) inversion via the iterative-perturbative (IP) method to extract a local, L-independent representation of the DPP, defined as the difference between the inverted potential and the bare (no-coupling) potential.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factor C²S for ¹⁰Be/⁹Be: empirical value 1.58 ± 0.15 (taken from systematic DWBA analysis by Tsagari et al., 2005), compared to shell-model value of 2.36 (Cohen and Kurath, 1967)
- Q-value for ¹⁰Be(p,d)⁹Be pickup reaction: −4.6 MeV
- At 15 and 16 MeV, bare potential volume integrals: J_R = 576.1 MeV fm³, J_I = 108.8 MeV fm³
- Change in real volume integral ΔJ_R at 16 MeV: −66.9 MeV fm³ (repulsive, ~10% effect)
- Change in imaginary volume integral ΔJ_I at 16 MeV: +57.3 MeV fm³ (~50% effect)
- Change in imaginary rms radius ΔR_I at 16 MeV: +0.623 fm
- Largest ΔJ_I observed: +77.2 MeV fm³ at 14 MeV
- Largest ΔR_I observed: +0.699 fm at 13 MeV
- For comparison with p + ⁸He at 15.7 MeV: ΔJ_R = −51.2 MeV fm³, ΔJ_I = +261.41 MeV fm³, ΔR_I = +0.827 fm
- Model calculation for p + ¹⁰Be at 13 MeV with spectroscopic factor increased to 2.9 (the ⁸He value): ΔJ_R = −73.0 MeV fm³, ΔJ_I = +100.2 MeV fm³, ΔR_I = +1.004 fm
- χ²/N values for full CRC fits: 3.46 (16 MeV), 2.38 (15 MeV), 1.93 (14 MeV), 1.00 (13 MeV), 8.52 (12 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the earlier study of p + ⁸He by Skaza et al. (2005), which found very strong pickup coupling effects for that neutron-skin nucleus, to a more typical nucleus (¹⁰Be) with the same neutron number but two additional protons. The results confirm that the repulsive real DPP and outward-shifted absorptive DPP are general features, not peculiar to ⁸He. The DPP cannot be represented by simple renormalization of a folding model potential, contradicting the common practice of using real and imaginary normalization factors. The repulsive nature of the real DPP is at variance with dispersive optical models (Johnson et al., Tandy et al., and others) in which the energy-dependent part added to the Hartree-Fock component is attractive. This work enables systematic studies of how the pickup DPP evolves with incident energy, Q-value, and spectroscopic factor, and raises the challenge of relating specific pickup channels to irregularities in the N and Z dependence of elastic scattering.
