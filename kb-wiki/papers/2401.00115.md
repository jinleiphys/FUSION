# Doubly heavy tetraquark states in a mass splitting model

> Treating the $X(4140)$ as a compact $J^{PC}=1^{++}$ $cs\bar{c}\bar{s}$ state and using its mass as a reference scale, we systematically estimate the masses of doubly heavy tetraquark states $QQ\bar{q}\bar{q}$ where $Q=c,b$ and $q=u,d,s$. Their decay properties are studied with a simple rearrangement scheme. Based on our results, the lowest $I(J^P)=0(1^+)$ $bb\bar{n}\bar{n}$ state is a stable tetraquark about 20 MeV below the $\bar{B}^*\bar{B}$ threshold. The mass and width of the low-mass $0(1^+)$ $cc\bar{n}\bar{n}$ ($n=u,d$) tetraquark are compatible with the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ observed by the LHCb Collaboration. The location of the lowest $0(0^+)$ and $0(1^+)$ $bc\bar{n}\bar{n}$ states are found to be close to the $\bar{B}D$ and $\bar{B}^*D$ thresholds, respectively. We hope that the predicted ratios between partial widths of different channels may be helpful to identify compact tetraquark states from future measurements.

## Key claim

Treating the X(4140) as a compact \(J^{PC}=1^{++}\) \(cs\bar{c}\bar{s}\) tetraquark and using its mass as a reference scale, the authors estimate masses of doubly heavy tetraquark states \(QQ\bar{q}\bar{q}\) (with \(Q=c,b\) and \(q=u,d,s\)) in a chromomagnetic interaction (CMI) model; the results are presented as more reasonable than those from either the threshold scheme or the direct CMI formula, but the authors note that the model’s systematic uncertainty is not quantified and that the obtained masses should be regarded as intermediate between upper and lower limits.

## Method

The authors adopt a mass splitting scheme based on the CMI Hamiltonian, using the X(4140) as a reference tetraquark state to fix the overall scale. They extract effective coupling constants \(C_{ij}\) and quark masses from conventional hadron masses (e.g., \(N\), \(\Delta\), \(J/\psi\), \(\Upsilon\), \(B\), \(D\)) and then compute the CMI eigenvalues for all \(QQ\bar{q}\bar{q}\) color-spin wave functions in the diquark-antidiquark basis. Decay widths are estimated via a simple rearrangement model with a constant decay Hamiltonian \(\mathcal{C}=7282.15\) MeV, taken from the X(4140) width, and applied to all systems.

## Key numbers

* The lowest \(I(J^P)=0(1^+)\) \(bb\bar{n}\bar{n}\) state is predicted to be about 20 MeV below the \(\bar{B}^*\bar{B}\) threshold, indicating a stable tetraquark.
* The mass and width of the low-mass \(0(1^+)\) \(cc\bar{n}\bar{n}\) (\(n=u,d\)) tetraquark are compatible with the \(T_{cc}(3875)^+\) observed by LHCb (width \(\Gamma=410\pm165\pm43^{+18}_{-38}\) keV, mass \(273\pm61\pm5^{+11}_{-14}\) keV below \(D^{*+}D^0\) threshold).
* The lowest \(0(0^+)\) and \(0(1^+)\) \(bc\bar{n}\bar{n}\) states are found to be close to the \(\bar{B}D\) and \(\bar{B}^*D\) thresholds, respectively.
* The extracted coupling constants include \(C_{nn}=18.3\) MeV, \(C_{c\bar{c}}=5.3\) MeV, \(C_{b\bar{b}}=2.9\) MeV, \(C_{cc}=3.6\) MeV, \(C_{bb}=1.9\) MeV, and \(C_{bc}=2.2\) MeV.
* The effective mass gaps are \(\Delta_{bc}=3340.2\) MeV and \(\Delta_{sn}=90.6\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous CMI-model studies of doubly heavy tetraquarks by Luo et al. (2017) and Cheng et al. (2020), which used either a threshold scheme or heavy diquark-antiquark symmetry. The present paper improves on those by using a compact tetraquark (X(4140)) as a reference, aiming to give more reliable mass estimates than the earlier upper/lower limit approaches. The predicted masses and decay patterns are intended to guide experimental searches for stable \(bb\bar{n}\bar{n}\) states and to help distinguish compact tetraquarks from molecular interpretations of states like \(T_{cc}(3875)^+\).
