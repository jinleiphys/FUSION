# Microscopic description of quadrupole-octupole coupling in actinides with the Gogny-D1M energy density functional

> The interplay between quadrupole and octupole degrees of freedom is discussed in a series of U, Pu, Cm and Cf isotopes both at the mean-field level and beyond. In addition to the static Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach, dynamical beyond-mean-field correlations are taken into account via both parity restoration and symmetry-conserving Generator Coordinate Method calculations based on the parametrization D1M of the Gogny energy density functional. Physical properties such as correlation energies, negative-parity excitation energies as well as reduced transition probabilities $B(E1)$ and $B(E3)$ are discussed in detail and compared with the available experimental data. It is shown that, for the studied nuclei, the quadrupole-octupole coupling is weak and to a large extent the properties of negative parity states can be reasonably well described in terms of the octupole degree of freedom alone.

# Microscopic description of quadrupole-octupole coupling in actinides with the Gogny-D1M energy density functional

## Key claim
For the studied U, Pu, Cm, and Cf isotopes, the coupling between quadrupole and octupole degrees of freedom is weak, and the properties of negative-parity states can be reasonably well described using only the octupole degree of freedom, though this conclusion is qualified by the observation that the mean-field potential energy surfaces are rather soft along the octupole direction, indicating that dynamical beyond-mean-field correlations are important.

## Method
The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny-D1M energy density functional with simultaneous constraints on the axially symmetric quadrupole (Q20) and octupole (Q30) moments. Beyond-mean-field correlations were incorporated through parity projection (restoring reflection symmetry) and two-dimensional Generator Coordinate Method (2D-GCM) calculations that included both parity restoration and configuration mixing over the quadrupole and octupole collective coordinates. The HFB quasiparticle operators were expanded in a deformed harmonic oscillator basis containing 16 major shells.

## Key numbers
- Mean-field octupole correlation energies for the N=134 isotones: 1.25 MeV (226U), 1.04 MeV (228Pu), 0.81 MeV (230Cm), and 0.59 MeV (232Cf)
- Static HFB quadrupole deformations in the range 0 b ≤ Q20,GS ≤ 14 b across all studied isotopes
- Static HFB octupole moments in the range 2 b^(3/2) ≤ Q30,GS ≤ 5 b^(3/2) for isotopes with octupole-deformed ground states (222-230U, 224-232Pu, 226-234Cm, 228-232Cf)
- Dynamical octupole deformations from 2D-GCM: 0.53 b^(3/2) ≤ (Q30)σ=1^(π=+1) ≤ 2.15 b^(3/2) for ground states, with largest values at N=132-138
- Dynamical octupole moments for lowest negative-parity states: 1.87 b^(3/2) ≤ (Q30)σ^(π=-1) ≤ 3.75 b^(3/2)
- 2D-GCM correlation energies range: 1.76 MeV ≤ ΔE_CORR,2D-GCM ≤ 2.46 MeV
- For 226U, the global π=+1 minimum at Q30=4.0 b^(3/2) is only 500 keV deeper than the minimum at Q30=1.0 b^(3/2)

## Context
This work extends previous studies of octupole correlations in actinides using Gogny functionals, particularly the 1D-GCM calculations with only octupole constraints by Robledo and Bertsch (2015) and the quadrupole-octupole coupling study in Sm and Gd nuclei by Rodriguez-Guzman and Robledo (2012). It also builds on earlier octupole-constrained HFB calculations using the D1S, D1N, and D1M parametrizations (Rodriguez-Guzman and Robledo, 2012). The results enable systematic comparison with experimental data for 1- excitation energies, B(E1), and B(E3) transition probabilities in heavy actinides, and suggest that the correlation energies should be incorporated in improved versions of the Gogny energy density functional.
