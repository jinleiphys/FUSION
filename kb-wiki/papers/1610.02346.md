# Analysis of hadron yield data within hadron resonance gas model with multi-component eigenvolume corrections

> We analyze the sensitivity of thermal fits to heavy-ion hadron yield data of ALICE and NA49 collaborations to the systematic uncertainties in the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model related to the modeling of the eigenvolume interactions. We find a surprisingly large sensitivity in extraction of chemical freeze-out parameters to the assumptions regarding eigenvolumes of different hadrons. We additionally study the effect of including yields of light nuclei into the thermal fits to LHC data and find even larger sensitivity to the modeling of their eigenvolumes. The inclusion of light nuclei yields, thus, may lead to further destabilization of thermal fits. Our results show that modeling of eigenvolume interactions plays a crucial role in thermodynamics of HRG and that conclusions based on a non-interacting HRG are not unique.

# Analysis of hadron yield data within hadron resonance gas model with multi-component eigenvolume corrections

## Key claim
Thermal fits to heavy-ion hadron yield data are surprisingly sensitive to the specific modeling of eigenvolume (short-range repulsive) interactions between hadrons, meaning that conclusions drawn from a non-interacting hadron resonance gas (HRG) model are not unique; the inclusion of light nuclei yields further destabilizes the fits due to additional assumptions required about their eigenvolumes.

## Method
The authors performed thermal fits to hadron yield data from the NA49 collaboration (Pb+Pb collisions at five beam energies from 6.3 to 17.3 GeV) and from the ALICE collaboration (0-10% central Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV) using two different multi-component eigenvolume formulations: the "Diagonal" model and the "Crossterms" model, which is consistent with the second-order virial expansion of hard spheres. They compared fit quality and extracted chemical freeze-out parameters (temperature T and baryochemical potential μ_B) between the eigenvolume HRG models and the point-particle (non-interacting) HRG, and studied the effect of adding light nuclei (deuterons, ³He, ³ΛH) with different assumptions about their hard-core radii.

## Key numbers
- At all five NA49 energies (√s_NN = 6.3, 7.6, 8.8, 12.3, and 17.3 GeV), wide regions of improved χ² values are observed at high temperatures and chemical potentials when using the "Crossterms" eigenvolume model with bag-like constant ε₀ fixed to reproduce a hard-core proton radius of r_p = 0.5 fm.
- In the two-component model with point-like mesons and baryons of fixed hard-core radius r_p = 0.3 fm, fitting ALICE data without light nuclei yields an irregular χ² profile with a wide double minimum structure in the 155-210 MeV temperature range.
- When deuterons are added with the same eigenvolume as protons (r_d = r_p = 0.3 fm), the χ² profile shows a regular structure with a minimum at T ≃ 161 MeV; changing the deuteron eigenvolume to r_d ≃ 0.38 fm (twice the proton volume) produces a two-minimum structure in the 155-210 MeV range with improved fit quality at the global minimum.
- Including yields of ³He and ³ΛH with N_dof = 17 does not stabilize the fit, regardless of the eigenvolume parameterization used.

## Context
This work extends the long-standing use of the HRG model for describing hadron multiplicities in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions (Cleymans, 1992; Andronic, 2005) by systematically studying the sensitivity to eigenvolume corrections of the van der Waals type (Hagedorn, 1980; Kapusta, 1982). It builds on earlier multi-component eigenvolume formulations by Yen (1997) and Gorenstein (1999), and on the observation by Vovchenko (2015) that a two-component model with point-like mesons and baryons of fixed radius yields an irregular χ² profile for ALICE data. The results show that entropy per baryon S/A is a robust observable nearly independent of eigenvolume modeling, while the extracted T-μ_B pairs are not unique. This work enables future studies to treat eigenvolume interactions as a systematic uncertainty in thermal fits rather than assuming a non-interacting HRG is sufficient.
