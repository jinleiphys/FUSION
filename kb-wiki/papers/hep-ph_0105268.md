# How Protons Shatter Colored Glass

> We consider the implications of the Color Glass Condensate for the central region of p+A collisions. We compute the k_t distribution of radiated gluons and their rapidity distribution dN/dy analytically, both in the perturbative regime and in the region between the two saturation momenta. We find an analytic expression for the number of produced gluons which is valid when the saturation momentum of the proton is much less than that of the nucleus. We discuss the scaling of the produced multiplicity with A. We show that the slope of the rapidity density dN/dy provides an experimental measure for the renormalization-group evolution of the color charge density of the Color Glass Condensate (CGC). We also argue that these results are easily generalized to collisions of nuclei of different A at central rapidity, or with the same A but at a rapidity far from the central region.

## Key claim

The paper derives an analytic expression for the gluon production in p+A collisions valid when the saturation momentum of the proton is much less than that of the nucleus, showing that the transverse momentum distribution changes from a 1/k_perp^4 behavior at high k_perp to a 1/k_perp^2 behavior in the region between the two saturation momenta.

## Method

The authors solve the classical Yang-Mills equations for the collision of two color-charge sheets with different saturation scales, linearizing the equations in the weaker field. They compute the gluon radiation spectrum by matching boundary conditions at the light-cone and averaging over Gaussian color-charge distributions using the McLerran-Venugopalan model, including renormalization-group evolution of the color charge density. The calculation yields analytic expressions for the transverse momentum and rapidity distributions of produced gluons valid when one source is much weaker than the other.

## Key numbers

- The perturbative regime (high k_perp) gives dN/d^2k_perp dy proportional to 1/k_perp^4 with a logarithmic correction log(k_perp^2 / (g^4 N_c chi_2/8pi))
- In the saturation regime (between the two saturation momenta), dN/d^2k_perp dy is proportional to 1/k_perp^2 times log(k_perp^2 / (g^4 N_c chi_1/8pi))
- The average transverse momentum in the perturbative regime is <k_perp> = 4 Q_s^(2), where Q_s^(2) = sqrt(g^4 N_c chi_2(y)/8pi)
- The rapidity density from the perturbative contribution scales as dN/d^2b_perp dy = g^2 (N_c^2 - 1)/pi^2 chi_1(y)
- The multiplicity in the saturation regime scales as dN/d^2b_perp dy = (1/4) g^2 chi_1(y) (N_c^2 - 1)/(2pi)^2 log^2(chi_2(y)/chi_1(y))

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate framework from equal-size nuclear collisions (previously treated numerically by Kovchegov and collaborators) to asymmetric collisions where one saturation scale is much smaller than the other. The analytic results complement the numerical approach of Kovchegov and collaborators for equal-size nuclei and generalize the diagrammatic approach of Kovchegov (1998) for deep inelastic scattering to the central rapidity region of p+A collisions. The paper enables experimental measurement of the renormalization-group evolution of the CGC color charge density through the slope of dN/dy, and provides a framework for understanding particle production in asymmetric nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.
