# Transient dynamics of quasinormal mode sums

> Quasinormal modes of spacetimes with event horizons are typically governed by a non-normal operator. This gives rise to spectral instabilities, a topic of recent interest in the black hole pseudospectrum programme. In this work we show that non-normality leads to the existence of arbitrarily long-lived sums of short-lived quasinormal modes, corresponding to localising packets of energy near the future horizon. There exist sums of $M$ quasinormal modes whose lifetimes scale as $\log{M}$. This transient behaviour results from large cancellations between non-orthogonal quasinormal modes. We provide simple closed-form examples for a massive scalar field in the static patch of dS$_{d+1}$ and the BTZ black hole. We also provide numerical examples for scalar perturbations of Schwarzschild-AdS$_{d+1}$, and gravitational perturbations of Schwarzschild in asymptotically flat spacetime, using hyperboloidal foliations. The existence of these perturbations is linked to certain properties of black hole pseudospectra. We comment on implications for thermalisation times in holographic plasmas.

## Key claim
Non-normality of the Hamiltonian governing quasinormal modes of spacetimes with event horizons leads to the existence of sums of short-lived quasinormal modes whose lifetimes scale as log M, where M is the number of modes in the sum, contradicting the naive expectation that the slowest decay is set by the longest-lived individual mode.

## Method
The authors study linear perturbations (scalar, electromagnetic, and gravitational) on fixed background spacetimes using hyperboloidal foliations that pierce future horizons. They compute the energy of perturbations via the stress-energy tensor and energy inner product, then construct finite-dimensional subspaces W spanned by M quasinormal modes. Using Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization with respect to the energy inner product and singular value decomposition of the projected time evolution operator, they compute the energy growth curve G_W(τ) and identify optimal initial data that maximizes energy at a given time τ.

## Key numbers
- For the de Sitter static patch (d=3, l=0, m²=2), an analytic example shows energy E(τ) = 1 - (1-e^{-τ})^{2M-1}(1 + (2M-1)e^{-τ}), with energy flux peaked at τ = log M.
- The energy growth curve G_W(τ) for de Sitter shows that increasing M from 10 to 1000 extends the transient plateau where G_W(τ) remains near unity to τ values exceeding 10 in Hubble units.
- For the BTZ black hole (d=2), the energy growth curve G_W(τ) for M=10, 20, 50, 100, 200, 500, 1000 shows transient lifetimes extending to τ ~ 6 in units of the inverse surface gravity.
- For Schwarzschild-AdS₅ scalar perturbations (l=0), G_W(τ) for M=10, 20, 50, 100, 200, 500 shows transients extending to τ ~ 2 in units of the inverse surface gravity.
- For Schwarzschild gravitational perturbations (l=2), G_W(τ) for M=10, 20, 50, 100, 200, 500 shows transients extending to τ ~ 40 in units of mass M.

## Context
This work extends the black hole pseudospectrum programme initiated by Jaramillo (2020) and subsequent studies by Arean (2023), Cownden (2023), and Boyanov (2023) by moving beyond spectral stability questions to directly study transient dynamics in the energy of perturbations. The approach parallels techniques from hydrodynamic stability theory, particularly the Orr-Sommerfeld equation studied by Orr (1907) and Sommerfeld (1909), where non-normality leads to transient growth. The results enable new investigations into observable phenomena in gravitational wave physics, analogue gravity, and holographic thermalization times in strongly-coupled plasmas.
