# Relativistic nucleon-nucleon potentials in a spin-dependent three-dimensional approach

> The matrix elements of relativistic nucleon-nucleon $(NN)$ potentials are calculated directly from the nonrelativistic potentials as a function of relative $NN$ momentum vectors, without using a partial wave decomposition. To this aim, the quadratic operator relation between the relativistic and nonrelativistic $NN$ potentials is formulated in momentum-helicity basis states. It leads to a single integral equation for the two-nucleon $(2N)$ spin-singlet state and four coupled integral equations for two-nucleon spin-triplet states, which are solved by an iterative method. Our numerical analysis indicates that the relativistic $NN$ potential obtained using CD-Bonn potential reproduces the deuteron binding energy and neutron-proton elastic scattering differential and total cross-sections with high accuracy.

## Key claim

The relativistic nucleon-nucleon potential constructed from the nonrelativistic CD-Bonn potential via a quadratic operator relation in momentum-helicity basis states reproduces the deuteron binding energy and neutron-proton elastic scattering observables with high accuracy, but the paper's hedge is that this agreement is demonstrated specifically for the CD-Bonn potential and the method's convergence requires careful tuning of weighted averaging parameters (alpha and beta) that differ across spin-isospin channels.

## Method

The quadratic operator relation between relativistic and nonrelativistic NN potentials was formulated in momentum-helicity basis states, yielding a single integral equation for the spin-singlet state and four coupled integral equations for spin-triplet states. These equations were solved iteratively using the CD-Bonn potential as input, with the matrix elements of the relativistic potential obtained as functions of the magnitude of two-nucleon relative momenta, the angle between them, and helicity eigenvalues. The resulting relativistic potential was then tested by solving the homogeneous Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the deuteron and the inhomogeneous Lippmann-Schwinger equation for neutron-proton elastic scattering.

- Total cross-section of np elastic scattering at E_lab = 0.001 MeV: relativistic 20337.1 mb, nonrelativistic 20336.6 mb; relative difference 0.00246%
- Total cross-section of np elastic scattering at E_lab = 750 MeV: relativistic 24.4850 mb, nonrelativistic 24.4872 mb; relative difference 0.00898%

- Number of iterations needed for convergence in (s=0, t=0) channel with alpha=1, beta=1: 76 iterations
- Number of iterations needed for convergence in (s=1, t=1) channel with alpha=3, beta=1: 10 iterations
- Nucleon mass used: m = 938.91852 MeV; conversion factor hbar c = 197.327053 MeV fm

## Context

This work extends the three-dimensional (3D) scheme previously applied to spin-independent Malfliet-Tjon (MT) potentials by Hadizadeh (2017, 2020) to realistic spin- and isospin-dependent potentials, specifically the CD-Bonn potential of Machleidt (2001). The approach follows the iterative method of Kamada and Gloeckle (2007), who demonstrated the quadratic relation in a partial-wave decomposition for AV18, CD-Bonn, and Nijmegen potentials. The 3D formulation avoids partial-wave decomposition and enables future fully relativistic calculations of triton and Helium-3 bound states, as well as scattering problems at few-GeV energy scales.
