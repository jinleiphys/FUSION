# Recent observation of short range nucleon correlations in nuclei and their implications for the structure of nuclei and neutron stars

> Novel processes probing the decay of nucleus after removal of a nucleon with momentum larger than Fermi momentum by hard probes finally proved unambiguously the evidence for long sought presence of short-range correlations (SRCs) in nuclei. In combination with the analysis of large $Q^2$, A(e,e')X processes at $x>1$ they allow us to conclude that (i) practically all nucleons with momenta $\ge$ 300 MeV/c belong to SRCs, consisting mostly of two nucleons, ii) probability of such SRCs in medium and heavy nuclei is $\sim 25%$, iii) a fast removal of such nucleon practically always leads to emission of correlated nucleon with approximately opposite momentum, iv) proton removal from two-nucleon SRCs in 90% of cases is accompanied by a removal of a neutron and only in 10% by a removal of another proton. We explain that observed absolute probabilities and the isospin structure of two nucleon SRCs confirm the important role that tensor forces play in internucleon interactions. We find also that the presence of SRCs requires modifications of the Landau Fermi liquid approach to highly asymmetric nuclear matter and leads to a significantly faster cooling of cold neutron stars with neutrino cooling operational even for $N_p/N_n \le 0.1$. The effect is even stronger for the hyperon stars. Theoretical challenges raised by the discovered dominance of nucleon degrees of freedom in SRCs and important role of the spontaneously broken chiral symmetry in quantum chromodynamics (QCD) in resolving them are considered. We also outline directions for future theoretical and experimental studies of the physics relevant for SRCs.

## Key claim

The paper argues that short-range correlations (SRCs) in nuclei have been unambiguously observed through hard probes that remove a nucleon with momentum above the Fermi momentum, and that these SRCs consist predominantly of proton-neutron pairs, with the isospin structure and absolute probabilities confirming the dominant role of tensor forces in internucleon interactions.

## Method

The authors review combined analyses of high-energy A(e,e')X processes at x > 1 and Q² ≥ 1.5 GeV², together with exclusive measurements of nuclear decay functions from double-coincidence experiments at BNL and Jefferson Lab, where a fast nucleon is removed from a nucleus and a correlated spectator nucleon is detected. They apply closure approximation and light-cone nuclear wave function formalism to extract the momentum distributions, spectral functions, and decay functions that isolate SRC contributions from mean-field backgrounds.

## Key numbers

- Practically all nucleons with momenta ≥ 300 MeV/c belong to SRCs, consisting mostly of two nucleons.
- Probability of such SRCs in medium and heavy nuclei is ~25%.
- Proton removal from two-nucleon SRCs is accompanied by a neutron in 90% of cases and by another proton in only 10% of cases.
- For carbon, the probability of short-range correlations is ~20%.
- The ratio of two-proton SRCs to proton-neutron SRCs is very small, ~1/20.
- The proportionality coefficient a₂(¹²C) for the SRC wave function relative to the deuteron wave function in the range 300 ≤ k ≤ 800 MeV/c is 4 to 5.
- The average recoil energy after removal of a nucleon from a two-nucleon SRC is ⟨E_R⟩ = p_i²/(2m_N).
- For three-nucleon SRCs of type 3N-I, the average recoil energy is ⟨E_R⟩ ~ p_i²/(4m_N); for type 3N-II, ⟨E_R⟩ ~ p_i²/m_N.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical predictions by Frankfurt and Strikman (1977, 1981) that high-energy processes could probe SRCs, and it synthesizes recent experimental results from Jefferson Lab (Kim et al., 2004) and BNL (EIP collaborations) that provided the first unambiguous evidence for SRCs. The findings confirm the dominance of tensor forces in the intermediate-to-short-range NN interaction, as previously discussed in calculations using the Paris potential, AV18, and variational Monte Carlo methods (Pieper, 2003; Forest, 1996; Schiavilla, 2007). The results enable improved modeling of neutron star cooling, requiring modifications to the Landau Fermi liquid approach for highly asymmetric nuclear matter, and motivate future studies of three-nucleon correlations, Δ-isobar admixtures, and isospin effects in SRCs.
