# How reliable is the mean-field Nuclear Matter description for supporting Chiral Effective Lagrangians?

> The link between non-linear chiral effective Lagrangians and the Walecka model description of bulk nuclear matter [1] is questioned. This fact is by itself due to the Mean Field Approximation (MFA) which in nuclear mater makes the picture of a nucleon-nucleon interaction based on scalar(vector) meson exchange, equivalent to the description of a nuclear matter based on attractive and repulsive contact interactions. We present a linear chiral model where this link between the Walecka model and an underlying to chiral symmetry realization still holds, due to MFA.

# How reliable is the mean-field Nuclear Matter description for supporting Chiral Effective Lagrangians?

## Key claim
The mean-field approximation (MFA) makes the Walecka model for infinite nuclear matter equivalent to a zero-range model with contact interactions, regardless of whether the underlying chiral symmetry is realized linearly or non-linearly. Therefore, the apparent connection between non-linear chiral effective Lagrangians and the Walecka model at the MFA level does not constitute evidence for a chiral foundation of the Walecka model, and any such claim requires going beyond MFA.

## Method
The authors compare the equations of state of the Walecka model, a non-linear chiral Lagrangian with four-nucleon contact terms (Gelmini and Ritzi, 1995), and a linear chiral Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with an added vector interaction, all evaluated at the mean-field level for infinite nuclear matter. They show that in MFA, the meson masses become irrelevant and the Walecka model reduces to a zero-range model (ZRM) with attractive scalar and repulsive vector contact interactions. They then demonstrate that both the non-linear and linear chiral Lagrangians produce the same equations of state as the ZRM and the Walecka model when evaluated in MFA, using a cutoff Lambda fixed by the nucleon mass and the Walecka coupling constant C_s^2.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon mass fixed at M = 938.27 MeV for the gap equation calculation.
- Cutoff Lambda = 328.5 MeV obtained from the gap equation using the Walecka coupling constant C_s^2 that reproduces nuclear matter saturation properties (Furnsthal and Serot, 1996; Delfino, Coelho and Malheiro, 1995).
- Critical coupling constant G_crit = (4 pi^2) / (gamma Lambda^2) required for a nontrivial nucleon mass solution, where gamma = 4 is the degeneracy factor.

## Context
This work questions the claim by Gelmini and Ritzi (1995) that a non-linear chiral effective Lagrangian with four-nucleon terms provides a chiral foundation for the Walecka model (Walecka, 1974; Serot and Walecka, 1986). The authors show that the equivalence is an artifact of MFA, which also makes the Walecka model indistinguishable from a zero-range model with contact interactions. They extend the argument to show that a linear chiral model (Nambu and Jona-Lasinio, 1961) with vector interactions also reduces to the same equations of state in MFA, but note that this approach suffers from a zero-frequency Nambu-Goldstone mode that cannot be identified with the pion. The work implies that meaningful tests of chiral effective Lagrangians in nuclear matter require going beyond MFA, where the pion can couple to nucleons and the identification with the Walecka model is lost.
