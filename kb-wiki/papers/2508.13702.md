# Structure of hypernucleus $_Λ^{7}$Li within microscopic three-cluster model

> The structure of bound and resonance states of the hypernucleus $_Λ^{7}$Li is studied within a three-cluster model. This nucleus is considered a three-cluster structure consisting of $^{4}$He, a deuteron, and a lambda hyperon. The chosen three-cluster configuration allows us to describe more accurately the structure of hypernucleus $_Λ^{7}$Li and the dynamics of different processes that involve interactions of lightest nuclei and hypernuclei. The main goal of the present investigations is to find resonance states in the three-cluster continuum of $_Λ^{7}$Li and determine their nature. A set of narrow resonance states is detected at the energy range 0$<E\leq$2 MeV above the three-cluster threshold $^{4}$He+$d$+$Λ$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a set of narrow resonance states exists in the three-cluster continuum of the hypernucleus $_\Lambda^{7}$Li, specifically in the energy range 0 < E ≤ 2 MeV above the $^{4}$He+$d$+$\Lambda$ threshold, but the model generates the weakly bound 1/2$^{+}$ state approximately 2.1 MeV underbound compared to experiment, indicating a limitation in reproducing all bound-state energies precisely.

## Method

The authors employed a microscopic three-cluster model (the AMHHB model) using hyperspherical harmonics to describe $_\Lambda^{7}$Li as a system of $^{4}$He, a deuteron, and a lambda hyperon. The wave function was expanded over hyperspherical harmonics and oscillator functions, and the Schrödinger equation was reduced to a system of linear algebraic equations solved by diagonalization for bound states and by implementing proper boundary conditions for scattering states to determine resonance parameters. The Hasegawa-Nagata potential (HNP) was used for nucleon-nucleon interactions and the YNG-NF potential for nucleon-hyperon interactions, with an oscillator length of b = 1.357 fm.

## Key numbers

- Bound state energies of $_\Lambda^{7}$Li (from the $^{4}$He+$d$+$\Lambda$ threshold): 1/2$^{+}$ ground state at -7.060 MeV (experiment: -7.094 MeV), 3/2$^{+}$ at -6.587 MeV (experiment: -6.402 MeV), 5/2$^{+}$ at -4.856 MeV (experiment: -5.043 MeV), and a weakly bound 1/2$^{+}$ at -1.113 MeV (experiment: -3.217 MeV).
- Mass root-mean-square radii of bound states: deeply bound states have radii between 2.0 and 2.2 fm (specifically 2.183 fm for 1/2$^{+}$, 2.208 fm for 3/2$^{+}$, 2.036 fm for 5/2$^{+}$), while the weakly bound 1/2$^{+}$ state has a radius of 4.524 fm.
- The narrowest resonance state is the 5/2$^{+}$ state at energy E = 0.290 MeV with a total width Γ = 0.00104 MeV (1.0 keV) and Γ/E = 3.6×10$^{-7}$.
- Other resonance states include: 1/2$^{-}$ at E = 1.545 MeV (Γ = 0.264 MeV), 3/2$^{-}$ at E = 1.043 MeV (Γ = 0.279 MeV), 3/2$^{-}$ at E = 1.551 MeV (Γ = 0.249 MeV), 1/2$^{+}$ at E = 1.520 MeV (Γ = 0.547 MeV), and 3/2$^{+}$ at E = 1.604 MeV (Γ = 0.696 MeV).
- The bound state energy of $^{6}$Li from the $^{4}$He+$d$ threshold is E(1$^{+}$) = -1.431 MeV, close to the experimental value of -1.474 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the three-cluster microscopic model previously applied to ordinary light nuclei (Vasilevsky, 2001; Nesterov, 2010; Vasilevsky, 2012, 2017, 2018) to the hypernuclear system $_\Lambda^{7}$Li, which has four bound states compared to only two in ordinary $^{7}$Li. The study fills a gap in experimental and theoretical information about three-cluster resonance states in hypernuclei, which have mostly been studied for bound states using shell models (Gal, 1978; Millener, 2012), mean-field models (Giai, 1993; Vretenar, 2001), and ab initio no-core shell models (Wirth, 2014; Lähde, 2020). The results predict narrow resonance states that can guide future experimental searches for three-cluster decays in hypernuclei.
