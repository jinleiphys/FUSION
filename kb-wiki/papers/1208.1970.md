# Open heavy flavor and J/psi at RHIC and LHC

> The suppression and collective flow of open and hidden heavy flavor in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions is studied with the partonic transport model Boltzmann Approach to MultiParton Scatterings (BAMPS). Charm and bottom quarks interact with the rest of the medium via elastic scatterings with an improved Debye screening and a running coupling. Heavy flavor electron data from RHIC can only be described if the cross section is multiplied with K=3.5 to mimic the contribution from radiative processes. With the same value for K we compare to elliptic flow data of D mesons at the LHC and find a good agreement. However, the nuclear modification factor of D mesons, non-prompt J/psi, muons, and electrons at the LHC is underestimated in our calculations. In addition, we present first BAMPS results on the suppression and elliptic flow of J/psi mesons, which are treated in the same framework.

## Key claim

Elastic scatterings of heavy quarks with an improved Debye screening and running coupling, scaled by a factor K=3.5 to mimic radiative processes, can describe heavy flavor elliptic flow at both RHIC and LHC but systematically underestimate the nuclear modification factor at LHC energies.

## Method

The authors used the partonic transport model BAMPS (Boltzmann Approach to MultiParton Scatterings) to simulate charm and bottom quark interactions with the quark-gluon plasma via elastic 2→2 scatterings (g Q → g Q and q Q → q Q), regularized with a screening mass μ² = κ m_D² where κ ≈ 0.2, and incorporating a running coupling. For J/ψ mesons, dissociation by gluons (J/ψ + g → c + ċ) and regeneration via the back-reaction were implemented, along with cold nuclear matter effects including shadowing, nuclear absorption, and the Cronin effect.

## Key numbers

- Elastic cross section scaled by K = 3.5 to fit heavy flavor electron data from RHIC
- Screening mass parameter κ = 1/(2e) ≈ 0.2, relating μ² to the Debye mass m_D²
- J/ψ formation time τ₀ = 0.6 fm used to prevent early melting when temperature cannot be properly defined
- For the coalescence scenario, secondary J/ψ elliptic flow follows v₂^{J/ψ}(p_T) ≈ 2 v₂^c(p_T/2)

## Context

This work extends the BAMPS framework (Xu 2004, Xu 2007) from light partons to heavy flavor, building on earlier studies of heavy quark energy loss (Uphoff 2010, Uphoff 2011) and J/ψ suppression (Uphoff 2011). The results contradict the regeneration picture where J/ψ flow should inherit charm quark flow, as BAMPS finds small J/ψ v₂ compatible with RHIC data (Powell 2011). The approach enables future inclusion of radiative 2→3 processes for heavy quarks, which the authors note are currently being studied and will be presented separately.
