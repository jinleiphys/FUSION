# Directed and Elliptic Flow

> We compare microscopic transport model calculations to recent data on the directed and elliptic flow of various hadrons in 2 - 10 A GeV Au+Au and Pb (158 A GeV) Pb collisions. For the Au+Au excitation function a transition from the squeeze-out to an in-plane enhanced emission is consistently described with mean field potentials corresponding to one incompressibility. For the Pb (158 A GeV) Pb system the elliptic flow prefers in-plane emission both for protons and pions, the directed flow of protons is opposite to that of the pions, which exhibit anti-flow. Strong directed transverse flow is present for protons and Lambdas in Au (6 A GeV) Au collisions as well. Both for the SPS and the AGS energies the agreement between data and calculations is remarkable.

## Key claim

The UrQMD transport model, when including mean-field potentials corresponding to a single incompressibility of K=380 MeV, consistently describes the transition from squeeze-out to in-plane enhanced elliptic flow across the AGS energy range, contrary to claims that a softening of the equation of state with incident energy is required.

## Method

The authors performed UrQMD (Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) transport model calculations for Au+Au collisions at beam energies from 2 to 10.6 A GeV and for Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A GeV. They compared the calculated directed flow (v1) and elliptic flow (v2) parameters to experimental data from the E895, E877, and NA49 collaborations, running the model both with and without mean-field potentials to isolate the effect of the nuclear equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Elliptic flow parameter v2 for protons at midrapidity in Au+Au collisions shows a transition from negative (squeeze-out) to positive (in-plane enhancement) around 5 A GeV, described with an incompressibility K=380 MeV
- For Pb+Pb at 158 A GeV, proton elliptic flow v2 ≈ 5% and pion elliptic flow v2 ≈ 2%, both positive indicating in-plane emission
- Directed flow of protons is opposite to that of pions at SPS energies, with pions exhibiting anti-flow (negative v1)
- In Au(6 A GeV)Au collisions, both protons and Lambdas show strong positive directed flow, with proton flow larger than Lambda flow near midrapidity (|y/yp| ≤ 0.6)

## Context

This work extends earlier flow studies from the BEVALAC and SATURNE eras (Gustafsson 1984, Doss 1986, Gutbrod 1989) and the EOS collaboration (Liu 1998) to higher energies at the AGS and SPS. The results contradict the interpretation of Pinkenburg (1999) and Danielewicz (1998) that a softening of the equation of state with incident energy is needed, instead showing that a single incompressibility suffices. The species-dependent flow patterns for protons and Lambdas rule out simple fireball-plus-flow models and demonstrate the need for microscopic transport approaches.
