# Neutral-current background induced by atmospheric neutrinos at large liquid-scintillator detectors: II. Methodology for in situ measurements

> Future large liquid-scintillator (LS) detectors are competitive with and complementary to the water-Cherenkov detectors on the searches for diffuse supernova neutrino background and nucleon decay. In a companion paper, we have performed a systematic calculation of the neutral-current (NC) background induced by atmospheric neutrino interactions on $^{12}{\rm C}$ nuclei in LS detectors, which are expected to be crucially important for the experimental searches for the diffuse supernova neutrino background and nucleon decay. In this paper, we perform a systematic study on the measurement of the NC background and evaluate the associated uncertainties. We first exploit the characteristics of the NC background, in particular, the multiplicities of neutrons and pions, and the possible association with unstable residual nuclei. It turns out that the neutron multiplicity distribution is very powerful to discriminate among different models. Then, we develop a maximum-likelihood method to allow an {\it in situ} measurement of the NC interactions with a triple-coincidence signature. Finally, a data-driven approach is proposed to evaluate the uncertainty of the NC background in the search for the diffuse supernova neutrino background. We conclude that future large LS experiments like JUNO (Jiangmen Underground Neutrino Observatory) will be able to make a unique contribution to the worldwide data set to improve the prediction of atmospheric neutrino NC interactions on $^{12}$C.

## Key claim

Future large liquid-scintillator detectors like JUNO can measure the neutral-current background from atmospheric neutrinos *in situ* using a triple-coincidence signature, reducing the uncertainty on the NC background prediction for the diffuse supernova neutrino background search from around 20% to the 10% level, provided that the neutron multiplicity distribution and correlations between different event categories are exploited.

## Method

The authors first characterized the neutral-current interactions of atmospheric neutrinos with ¹²C using six nuclear models from the GENIE and NuWro event generators, with deexcitation handled by TALYS and detector response simulated with GEANT4. They then developed a maximum-likelihood method to extract the NC background using a triple-coincidence signature (prompt signal, neutron capture, and decay of an unstable residual nucleus such as ¹¹C, ¹⁰C, or ⁸Li). Finally, a data-driven approach was proposed to propagate the measured NC background uncertainty into the DSNB signal region, using toy Monte Carlo datasets based on the JUNO detector parameters.

## Key numbers

- The event rate for NC interactions with one neutron production is (16.5 ± 2.8) kt⁻¹ yr⁻¹ in the whole visible energy range, reducing to (3.1 ± 0.5) kt⁻¹ yr⁻¹ in the energy window 11 MeV ≲ E_vis ≲ 30 MeV.
- The overall uncertainty on the NC background prediction is about 20% from model variations, plus 15% from atmospheric neutrino flux uncertainties, giving a total of 25% when added in quadrature.
- The neutron multiplicity distribution shows that GENIE predicts about 23% probability for Category II interactions to produce more than two neutrons, compared to 5–9% for NuWro models.
- The DSNB IBD rate is calculated as 0.30 kt⁻¹ yr⁻¹ for the energy range 7.5 MeV to 100 MeV, using a DSNB model with R_CCSN = 10⁻⁴ yr⁻¹ Mpc⁻³, ⟨E⟩ = 14 MeV, and f_BH = 0.27.
- The ratio of total double-coincidences to total triple-coincidences is about 3/2 for both GENIE and NuWro models.

## Context

This work extends the systematic calculation of atmospheric neutrino NC backgrounds on ¹²C presented in the companion paper Cheng (2020) by developing a methodology for *in situ* measurement and uncertainty reduction. It builds on prior studies of cosmogenic isotopes in liquid scintillator detectors (e.g., KamLAND and Borexino analyses of ⁹Li/⁸He and ¹⁰C) and on the Super-K measurement of neutrino-oxygen NC quasi-elastic cross sections Wan (2019). The method enables future large LS detectors like JUNO to provide unique data for improving atmospheric neutrino NC interaction models, complementing water-Cherenkov detectors such as Super-K and Hyper-K with gadolinium loading.
