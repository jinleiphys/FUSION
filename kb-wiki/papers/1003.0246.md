# Density-matrix formalism with three-body ground-state correlations

> A density-matrix formalism which includes the effects of three-body ground- state correlations is applied to the standard Lipkin model. The reason to consider the complicated three-body correlations is that the truncation scheme of reduced density matrices up to the two-body level does not give satisfactory results to the standard Lipkin model. It is shown that inclusion of the three-body correlations drastically improves the properties of the ground states and excited states. It is pointed out that lack of mean-field effects in the standard Lipkin model enhances the relative importance of the three-body ground-state correlations. Formal aspects of the density-matrix formalism such as a relation to the variational principle and the stability condition of the ground state are also discussed. It is pointed out that the three-body ground-state correlations are necessary to satisfy the stability condition.

# Density-matrix formalism with three-body ground-state correlations

## Key claim

Inclusion of three-body ground-state correlations in the time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM) drastically improves ground-state and excited-state properties for the standard Lipkin model, where truncation at the two-body level produces ground-state energies that are slightly overbound (lower than exact). The authors point out that the lack of mean-field effects in the standard Lipkin model enhances the relative importance of these three-body correlations.

## Method

The authors extend the time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM) by including the three-body correlation matrix C₃ in addition to the occupation matrix n and two-body correlation matrix C₂, truncating the BBGKY hierarchy at the three-body level. They solve the stationary conditions for n, C₂, and C₃ using a gradient method supplemented by a time-dependent adiabatic switching approach, then construct an extended RPA (ERPA) including one-body and two-body excitation operators built on the correlated ground state. The formalism is applied to the standard Lipkin model for N=4 and N=8 fermion systems.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy improvement: TDDM with three-body correlations removes the overbinding seen in TDDM1 (two-body only) for the standard Lipkin model with N=4, bringing results close to exact values across the coupling range χ = (N-1)|V|/ε.
- For N=2 test system, the time-dependent method with τ = 2πħ/ε × 10 reproduces the exact ground-state energy -√(ε²+V²) within numerical accuracy.
- Occupation probability of the upper state and two-body correlation matrix C_{pp'-p-p'} for N=4 show improved agreement with exact results when three-body correlations are included.
- For N=8, similar improvement in ground-state energy is achieved with three-body correlations compared to the two-body truncation.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous applications of TDDM to solvable models (Takahara, 2007; Tohyama and Schuck, 2008), where truncation at the two-body level gave satisfactory results for the extended Lipkin model (which includes a mean-field-generating U term) but produced overbound ground states for the standard Lipkin model (Lipkin, 1965). The paper also relates the formalism to self-consistent RPA (SCRPA) as developed by Rowe (1968) and Dukelsky et al., showing that the one-body part of ERPA is formally similar to SCRPA but differs in how occupation numbers and correlation matrices are determined. The inclusion of three-body correlations enables satisfaction of the stability condition for the ground state and ensures hermiticity of the ERPA Hamiltonian matrix, addressing a known issue in the equation-of-motion method.
