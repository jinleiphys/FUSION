# The reaction $pd\to(pp)n$ at high momentum transfer and short-range NN properties

> A recent cross section measurement of the deuteron breakup reaction $pd\to (pp)(0^\circ)+n(180^\circ)$, in the GeV region in a kinematics similar to backward $pd$ elastic scattering, strongly contradicts predictions of a $pd \to dp$ model based on the one-nucleon exchange, single pN scattering and $Δ$ excitation mechanisms, and on the wave functions of the Reid soft core and Paris $NN$ potentials. We show within the same model that for the CD Bonn $NN$ potential there is qualitative agreement with the data. It is attributed to a reduction of the one-nucleon exchange at energies above 1 GeV and an increase of the $Δ(1232)$-isobar contribution, both related to the short-range properties of the wave functions generated by this potential.

## Key claim

The ONE+SS+Δ model, which previously failed to describe the measured $pd\to(pp)n$ cross section when using the Reid soft core (RSC) and Paris $NN$ potentials, achieves qualitative agreement with the data when using the CD Bonn $NN$ potential, due to the softer high-momentum components of the CD Bonn wave functions.

## Method

The authors calculated the three-fold differential cross section for $pd\to(pp)n$ at forward diproton angles and backward neutron angles, using a model that includes one-nucleon exchange (ONE), single $pN$ scattering (SS), and $\Delta(1232)$-isobar excitation mechanisms. They compared results obtained with deuteron and $pp$ ($^1S_0$) wave functions from the RSC, Paris, and CD Bonn $NN$ potentials, and also included initial and final state interactions in distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) for the ONE contribution.

## Key numbers

- The measured cross section at beam energies $T_p=0.6-1.9$ GeV with forward emission of a proton pair of low excitation energy $E_{pp}=0-3$ MeV was used for comparison.
- At $T_p>1$ GeV, the model with RSC and Paris potentials overestimates the data by a factor of 2-3.
- The ONE cross section at $T_p>0.8$ GeV for the CD Bonn potential is smaller by a factor of 3-4 compared to the result with the Paris potential.
- The $\Delta$ contribution to the breakup cross section increases by approximately 50% for the CD Bonn potential compared to the Paris potential.
- Inclusion of Coulomb interaction in the final $pp$ system decreases the ONE+SS+$\Delta$ cross section by approximately 20%.

## Context

This work extends earlier model calculations by the same group (Uzikov, 2002) that used RSC and Paris potentials and found disagreement with the ANKE-COSY data (Vikhrov et al., 2001). The analysis contradicts the earlier suggestion that the high-momentum components of $NN$ wave functions must be much smaller than those of RSC and Paris potentials; instead, the CD Bonn potential achieves agreement without such extreme softening. The results enable future polarization measurements in $pd\to(pp)n$ to provide further tests of $NN$ dynamics at short distances, and the reaction is proposed as a cleaner probe than $pd\to dp$ because $\Delta$ and $N^*$ contributions are suppressed by an isospin factor of 1/3.
