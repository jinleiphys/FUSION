# Accessing the shape of atomic nuclei with relativistic collisions of isobars

> Nuclides sharing the same mass number (isobars) are observed ubiquitously along the stability line. While having nearly identical radii, stable isobars can differ in shape, and present in particular different quadrupole deformations. We show that even small differences in these deformations can be probed by relativistic nuclear collisions experiments, where they manifest as deviations from unity in the ratios of elliptic flow coefficients taken between isobaric systems. Collider experiments with isobars represent, thus, a unique means to obtain quantitative information about the geometric shape of atomic nuclei.

## Key claim

Even small differences in the quadrupole deformation of stable isobars can be probed by relativistic nuclear collisions, where they manifest as deviations from unity in the ratio of elliptic flow coefficients between isobaric systems; the authors show that for well-deformed nuclei, differences at the level of a few percent in the deformation parameter β will leave visible signatures in this ratio.

## Method

The authors use a standard 2-parameter Fermi (2pF) parametrization for the nuclear matter density with an axial quadrupole deformation term (β Y₂,₀), and the TRENTo model to simulate minimum-bias relativistic collisions of isobaric pairs. For each pair, they compute the rms eccentricity ε₂{2} in centrality classes, and since isobaric systems share the same dynamical response coefficient κ₂, the ratio of elliptic flow coefficients v₂{2} equals the ratio of ε₂{2}. They perform 5 million minimum-bias collisions per system and evaluate the ratio as a function of centrality.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁵⁴Sm and ¹⁵⁴Gd: β = 0.34 for Sm, β = 0.31 for Gd; the ratio of v₂ coefficients shows a deviation from unity of order 5% in central collisions.
- For ⁹⁶Ru and ⁹⁶Zr: β = 0.15 for Ru, β = 0.06 for Zr; the splitting between flow coefficients in central collisions is well above 1%.
- The matter radius difference between two stable isobars is at most 0.5%, leading to a negligible correction to the v₂ ratio of order few per mille.
- For both pairs studied, β_X² - β_Y² ≈ 0.02, with a coefficient c ≈ 3 in the expansion ε₂{2}_X / ε₂{2}_Y ≈ 1 + c(β_X² - β_Y²).

## Context

This work extends the recent realization that elliptic flow in ²³⁸U+²³⁸U collisions at RHIC and ¹²⁹Xe+¹²⁹Xe collisions at LHC is dominated by nuclear deformation effects (Adamczyk 2015, Jia, Acharya 2018, Sirunyan 2019, Aad 2019). The proposed isobar ratio method enables quantitative extraction of relative quadrupole deformations across the nuclide chart, complementing low-energy nuclear structure experiments. The authors specifically note that upcoming data from the 2018 RHIC run of ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr and ⁹⁶Ru+⁹⁶Ru collisions can be directly compared to their predictions, and that the approach can be extended to study octupole deformation in ¹⁴⁶,¹⁴⁸,¹⁵⁰Nd and ¹⁵⁰Sm via triangular flow measurements.
