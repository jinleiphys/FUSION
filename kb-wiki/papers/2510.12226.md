# Unlocking the initial neutron density distribution from the two-pion HBT correlation function in heavy-ion collisions

> Revealing the neutron density distribution in the nucleus is one of the crucial tasks of nuclear physics. Within the framework of the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamic model followed by a correlation afterburner program, we investigate the effects of the initial neutron density distribution on the charged-pion yield ratio $π^{-}/π^{+}$, the two-pion momentum correlation function, and the emission source dimension. It is found that the $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio is sensitive to the initial neutron density distribution and the impact parameter, especially for collisions at large impact parameter. However, the charge splitting in the correlation functions between positively $π^{+}π^{+}$ and negatively $π^{-}π^{-}$, as well as the source radii and volumes extracted exhibit a stronger dependence on the initial neutron density distribution, but a weaker dependence on the impact parameter. The present study highlights that $π^{+}π^{+}$ and $π^{-}π^{-}$ correlation functions in heavy-ion collisions could be used to probe the initial neutron density distribution of nuclei.

# Unlocking the initial neutron density distribution from the two-pion HBT correlation function in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The charge splitting between positively and negatively charged two-pion HBT correlation functions, and the extracted source radii and volumes, exhibit a stronger dependence on the initial neutron density distribution (parameterized by the diffuseness parameter $f_n$) than on the impact parameter, suggesting that two-pion HBT correlations may serve as a more robust probe of the initial neutron density distribution than the charged-pion yield ratio $\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}$, which depends on both $f_n$ and impact parameter.

## Method
The authors used the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model in mean-field mode with a hard equation of state ($K_0=380$ MeV) to simulate Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3$ GeV. The initial neutron density distributions were varied by adjusting the diffuseness parameter $f_n$ from 1 to 6 in a Droplet model parameterization of the Woods-Saxon form, while keeping the proton distribution fixed ($f_p=1.0$). After the transport evolution, the space-time coordinates and four-momenta of pions were fed into the correlation afterburner (CRAB) program to construct two-pion HBT correlation functions, which were then fitted with a three-dimensional Gaussian form in the longitudinally comoving system to extract HBT radii parameters.

## Key numbers
- The diffuseness parameter $f_n$ was varied from 1 to 6 to produce different neutron density distributions and neutron skin thicknesses for $^{197}$Au nuclei.
- Au+Au collisions were simulated at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3$ GeV with impact parameters $b$ ranging from 0 to 10 fm.
- Charged pions were selected within transverse momentum range $0.15 < p_T < 1.0$ GeV/c and rapidity window $-0.5 < y < 0$, following STAR experimental conditions.
- The $\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}$ ratio decreases monotonically with increasing $f_n$ and increases with impact parameter, with the effect most pronounced at $b=4$, 6, and 8 fm.
- The ratio of $\pi^{-}\pi^{-}$ to $\pi^{+}\pi^{+}$ correlation functions is close to unity when $f_n=1$ and systematically decreases as $f_n$ increases, while remaining nearly unchanged across $b$ from 2 to 10 fm.
- The $R_O/R_S$ ratio for $\pi^{+}\pi^{+}$ is larger than that for $\pi^{-}\pi^{-}$, consistent with STAR preliminary data, and the data favor $f_n < 3$ for $^{197}$Au.

## Context
This work extends previous studies using the UrQMD model (Li 2022) that investigated effects of resonance decay widths, in-medium cross sections, and potentials on two-pion interferometry, where Coulomb interaction was identified as the main cause of charge splitting but other factors were suggested to contribute. The study builds on earlier findings that the charged-pion ratio $\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}$ correlates with neutron skin thickness (Wei 2013, Hartnack 2018, Yang 2020) and that nucleon-nucleon correlation functions carry information about neutron or proton halos in exotic nuclei (Ma 2006, Cao 2012). The results enable a new method to probe neutron density distributions and neutron skin thickness through charge splitting in two-pion HBT correlations, which may be less contaminated by impact parameter uncertainties than yield-based observables, and could help constrain the nuclear equation of state and symmetry energy through Bayesian inference approaches.
