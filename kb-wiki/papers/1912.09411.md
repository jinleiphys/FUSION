# Nuclear and neutron-star matter from local chiral interactions

> We report a quantum Monte Carlo calculation of the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter using local interactions derived from chiral effective field theory up to next-to-next-to-leading order fit to few-body observables only. The empirical saturation density and energy are well reproduced within statistical and systematic uncertainties. We have also derived the symmetry energy as a function of the density, finding good agreement with available experimentally derived constraints at saturation and twice saturation density. We find that the corresponding pressure is also in excellent agreement with recent constraints extracted from gravitational waves of the neutron-star merger GW170817 by the LIGO-Virgo detection.

## Key claim

The paper reports that local chiral interactions at next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO), fit only to few-body observables, produce an equation of state for symmetric nuclear matter that reproduces empirical saturation properties and a symmetry energy consistent with experimental constraints, but only within statistical and systematic uncertainties; the saturation density and energy from the calculation lie slightly above the empirical point.

## Method

The authors performed auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations of pure neutron matter (66 neutrons) and symmetric nuclear matter (28 nucleons) in a periodic box, using local chiral effective field theory interactions up to N$^2$LO with a coordinate-space cutoff of 1.0 fm. Two parametrizations of the three-body contact term (E1 and Eτ) were compared to quantify regularization-scheme uncertainties. The trial wave function included spin-backflow correlations, and the ground state was projected via imaginary-time evolution with constrained-path and unconstrained evolution.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density for the E1 interaction: n0 = 0.22(1) fm^{-3}; saturation energy: E0 = -13.96(8) MeV
- Incompressibility for E1: K0 = 223(16) MeV, within the empirical range of 230(20) MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation density for E1: S = 30(3) MeV; slope L = 59(9) MeV
- Symmetry energy at twice saturation density for E1: S = 45(5) MeV, compatible with the empirical range 46-54 MeV
- Pressure of β-equilibrated matter from the E1 interaction is compatible with but at the lower bound of the LIGO-Virgo extraction from GW170817

## Context

This work extends earlier AFDMC calculations of pure neutron matter using local chiral interactions (Lynn 2016) by including symmetric nuclear matter and the symmetry energy with improved wave functions and uncertainty estimates. The results are compared to experimental constraints on the symmetry energy from nuclear masses (Kortelainen 2010), neutron-skin thicknesses (Chen 2010), dipole polarizability (Piekarewicz 2012), giant dipole resonances (Trippa 2008), heavy-ion collisions (Tsang 2008), and isobaric analog states (Danielewicz 2016), as well as to theoretical constraints from Hebeler (2010) and Gandolfi (2012). The pressure from β-equilibrated matter is compared to the LIGO-Virgo extraction from GW170817 (Abbott 2018). The work enables more reliable comparisons between nuclear-matter calculations and both laboratory experiments and astrophysical observations.
