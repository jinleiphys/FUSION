# Estimation of correlation coefficients and spin angular distributions of fission fragments

> This study proposes a theoretical model for studying the spin characteristics and angular correlations of fission fragments of heavy nuclei. The mechanisms of spin formation, including the influence of transverse vibrations, are considered and the relationship between the anisotropy of the angular distribution and the correlation coefficient is revealed. The theoretical predictions are compared with experimental data and various models developed by other research groups.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a theoretical model within the framework of quantum fission theory that predicts a complete absence of correlation between fission fragment spins, consistent with experimental data from Wilson (2021), but the model's generalized correlation coefficients (0.034, 0.020, 0.017) differ by an order of magnitude from those obtained by the Randrup group (0.002, 0.002, 0.001), yet both confirm the same qualitative conclusion of no spin correlation.

## Method

The authors develop a theoretical model based on the "cold" fissioning nucleus concept, where spin distributions are constructed using zero oscillatory wave functions in momentum representation for transverse wriggling and bending vibrational modes. The model calculates the probability distribution of fragment spins by expressing the spins of the two fragments in terms of the wriggling and bending vibration spins, using moments of inertia calculated from the Migdal superfluid nucleus model rather than rigid-body values. The correlation coefficients for each fragment pair are computed individually and then combined into a generalized coefficient weighted by fragment yields for three reactions: neutron-induced fission of 232Th and 238U, and spontaneous fission of 252Cf.

## Key numbers

- Generalized correlation coefficients: 0.034 for 232Th(n,f), 0.020 for 238U(n,f), and 0.017 for 252Cf(sf)
- For comparison, the FREYA model (Randrup 2021) gives correlation coefficients of 0.002 for 235U(n,f) and 238U(n,f), 0.001 for 239Pu(n,f) and 252Cf(sf)
- Mean primary fragment spins from FREYA: light fragment 4.27-5.08 hbar, heavy fragment 5.66-6.33 hbar across the four studied reactions
- Zero-point vibration energies assumed: hbar*omega_w = 2.5 MeV for wriggling, hbar*omega_b = 0.9 MeV for bending
- Individual fragment correlation coefficients range from 0.000 to 0.207 across all fragment pairs and reactions, with most values below 0.05

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Kadmensky (2002, 2003, 2005, 2008, 2009, 2017) on transitional fission states and the "cold" nucleus approach, and directly addresses the experimental finding by Wilson (2021) that fission fragment spins are uncorrelated. The model is compared with the FREYA statistical model by Randrup (2021) and the TDDFT approach by Stetcu (2021), and the results support the conclusion that fragment spins form before scission yet remain effectively uncorrelated, contradicting earlier assumptions that spin correlations should be present if spins are generated by collective vibrations.
