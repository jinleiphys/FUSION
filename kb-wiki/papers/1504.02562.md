# Exotic Nuclei and Matter in a Chirally Effective Approach

> A relativistic approach to describe nuclear and in general strongly interacting matter is introduced and discussed. Here, not only the nuclear forces but also the masses of the nucleons are generated through meson fields. Within this framework it is possible to calculate properties of finite nuclei at a level of accuracy similar to dedicated relativistic nuclear structure models. Due to the more general approach, a wider range of properties of hadronic states can be investigated. A number of results for heavy and neutron-rich nuclei toward the drip line are presented.

## Key claim

The chiral SU(3) hadronic (CMF) model can describe finite nuclei with accuracy comparable to dedicated relativistic nuclear structure models, but only after a careful fine-tuned optimisation of the coupling constants; the abstract's claim of "similar to dedicated relativistic nuclear structure models" depends on this parameter tuning.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic mean-field approach based on an effective chiral SU(3) hadronic (CMF) model, where baryon masses are generated through coupling to scalar fields (σ, ζ, δ) that acquire vacuum expectation values via spontaneous symmetry breaking, analogous to chiral σ models. Baryons interact via meson exchange with the meson fields approximated by their mean-field values, and the vector fields ω, ρ, and φ generate repulsive forces. The coupling constants were recently refitted to reproduce baryon masses and nuclear binding energies, and the model was used to calculate all even-even nuclei up to the proton and neutron drip lines.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy error compared to experimental data: ε = 0.17% for nuclei with baryon number A ≥ 50, and ε = 0.12% for A ≤ 100, representing a 20% improvement over earlier results.
- Measured quadrupole deformation for nobelium isotopes: β₂(²⁵²No) = 0.31 ± 0.02 and β₂(²⁵⁴No) = 0.32 ± 0.02.
- Fission barrier for superheavy nucleus with Z = 120 and N = 174: about 4 to 5 MeV.
- Fission barrier for the extremely neutron-rich superheavy nucleus ³⁷⁸120 (Z = 120, N = 258): about 9 MeV, with a distinct spherical minimum.

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) hadronic model previously developed by Papazoglou (1997, 1998) and applied to deformed nuclei, now with a refitted parameter set that improves binding energy predictions by 20%. The model enables simultaneous study of finite nuclei, neutron stars, and heavy-ion collisions within a single framework, and predicts a strong shell closure at neutron number N = 258, which was first discussed by Schramm (2011) and also found in Skyrme models by Tarasov (2014). This opens the question of whether such extremely stable superheavy nuclei could be produced in neutron star crusts and ejected via violent processes or mergers.
