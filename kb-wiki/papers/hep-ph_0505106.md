# Quark Spectrum near Chiral Transition Points

> Near the critical temperature of the chiral phase transition, a collective excitation due to fluctuation of the chiral order parameter appears. We investigate how it affects the quark spectrum near but above the critical temperature. The calculated spectral function has many peaks. We show this behavior can be understood in terms of resonance scatterings of a quark off the collective mode.

## Key claim

Near but above the critical temperature of the chiral phase transition, the quark spectral function develops multiple peaks that can be understood as resonance scatterings of a quark off the collective soft mode (fluctuation of the chiral order parameter), rather than as genuine quasiparticle states; this behavior is shown to be an interpolating feature between free quark dispersion at zero temperature and the high-temperature limit of hot QCD.

## Method

The authors employ the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the chiral limit to study the quark spectral function above the critical temperature. The fluctuation of the chiral condensate (soft mode) is described by effective quark-antiquark propagators in the scalar-isoscalar (sigma) and pseudoscalar-isovector (pi) channels, evaluated in the random phase approximation. The quark self-energy is computed in the non-self-consistent T-matrix approximation, and the retarded Green function is analytically continued from the imaginary-time formalism to obtain the spectral function.

## Key numbers

* Critical temperature at zero density: T_C = 193.5 MeV (determined from the mean-field thermodynamic potential of the NJL model).
* Tricritical point (TCP) location: T_TCP = 84.2 MeV and mu_TCP = 278.6 MeV.
* The soft-mode spectral peaks are visible up to epsilon = (T - T_C)/T_C = 0.2 (i.e., about 20% above T_C).
* NJL model parameters: coupling constant G_S = 5.5 GeV^{-2}, three-dimensional cutoff Lambda = 631 MeV.
* At T = 196 MeV (1.01 T_C) and p ≈ 0, the dispersion relation shows ten solutions, though not all form peaks in the spectral function.
* At T = 210 MeV (1.09 T_C) and |p| = 50 MeV, the imaginary part of the self-energy Sigma_- has two peaks around omega ≈ -100 MeV and omega ≈ 50 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier finding by Hatsuda (1984) that the fluctuation of the chiral order parameter survives up to epsilon ≈ 0.2 above T_C, and applies it to the single-quark spectrum. The results show that the quark dispersion relations near T_C resemble those from hot QCD in the high-temperature limit (Weldon, 1989), but the mechanism here involves scattering off the chiral soft mode rather than thermal gluons. The analysis is complementary to studies of pseudogap formation via phase fluctuations of the chiral condensate (Babaev, 2000), and it opens the possibility of investigating combined effects of amplitude and phase fluctuations near the critical endpoint.
