# Simple non-perturbative resummation schemes beyond mean-field: case study for scalar $ϕ^4$ theory in 1+1 dimensions

> I present a sequence of non-perturbative approximate solutions for scalar $ϕ^4$ theory for arbitrary interaction strength, which contains, but allows to systematically improve on, the familiar mean-field approximation. This sequence of approximate solutions is apparently well-behaved and numerically simple to calculate since it only requires the evaluation of (nested) one-loop integrals. To test this resummation scheme, the case of $ϕ^4$ theory in 1+1 dimensions is considered, finding approximate agreement with known results for the vacuum energy and mass gap up to the critical point. Because it can be generalized to other dimensions, fermionic fields and finite temperature, the resummation scheme could potentially become a useful tool for calculating non-perturbative properties approximately in certain quantum field theories.

# Simple non-perturbative resummation schemes beyond mean-field: case study for scalar φ⁴ theory in 1+1 dimensions

## Key claim

The paper presents a sequence of non-perturbative resummation schemes (R0 through R3) for scalar φ⁴ theory that systematically improve on the mean-field approximation, but the results are only *approximate*, the R3 scheme yields a critical coupling of approximately g_c ≃ 2.2, whereas precision calculations consistently find g_c ≃ 2.8, indicating quantitative deviations from exact results even at the highest resummation level considered.

## Method

The author introduces auxiliary fields σ, ζ to rewrite the φ⁴ path integral, then integrates out the original φ field and expands around the zero-mode of ζ. This generates a sequence of approximations (R0, R1, R2, R3) where each level resums additional classes of diagrams by introducing self-energies and vertex corrections that are determined self-consistently through gap equations. All schemes require only the evaluation of (nested) one-loop integrals, which are computed numerically using Gauss-Legendre quadrature with up to N=501 stencils for R2 and N=201 stencils for R3.

## Key numbers

- The R2 scheme predicts a phase transition at critical coupling g_c ≃ 1.89, with a critical exponent c_2 = 0.12(5) obtained numerically at that coupling.
- The R3 scheme yields a critical coupling of approximately g_c ≃ 2.2, with critical exponent c_2 = 0.12(5).
- Precision calculations from Refs. Elias-Miro (2017) and Serone (2018) consistently find g_c ≃ 2.8.
- The exact critical exponents for the two-dimensional Ising universality class are c_1 = 1 and c_2 = 1/8.
- Numerical evaluations used N=501 stencils for R2 and N=201 stencils for R3, with N_φ = 48 Fourier stencils for angular discretization in the R3 vertex calculation.
- The R2 and R3 schemes use a momentum cutoff K²_max > 1000 m_R², beyond which results become independent of the specific cutoff value.

## Context

This work extends the mean-field (R1) approximation for scalar φ⁴ theory, which fails to capture symmetry breaking at zero temperature in 1+1 dimensions, by constructing higher-level resummations (R2 and R3) that incorporate RPA-type and vertex corrections. The results are compared to precision calculations from Hamiltonian truncation (Elias-Miro, 2017) and Borel summation (Serone, 2018), as well as lattice studies (Schaich, 2009; Bosetti, 2015). The schemes are designed to be generalizable to other dimensions, fermionic fields, finite temperature, and other observables such as transport coefficients, potentially providing approximate tools for quantum field theories at intermediate coupling where exact methods are unavailable.
