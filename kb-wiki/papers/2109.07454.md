# Definitive Detection of Orbital Angular Momentum States in Neutrons by Spin-polarized $^3$He

> A standard method to detect thermal neutrons is the nuclear interaction $^3$He(n,p)$^3$H. The spin-dependence of this interaction is also the basis of a neutron spin-polarization filter using nuclear polarized $^3$He. We consider the corresponding interaction for neutrons placed in an intrinsic orbital angular momentum (OAM) state. We derive the relative polarization-dependent absorption cross-sections for neutrons in an $L=1$ OAM state. The absorption of those neutrons results in compound states $J^π=0^-$, $1^-$, and $2^-$. Varying the three available polarizations tests that an OAM neutron has been absorbed and probes which decay states are physically possible. We describe the energetically likely excited states of $^4$He after absorption, due to the fact that the compound state has odd parity. This provides a definitive method for detecting neutron OAM states and suggests that intrinsic OAM states offer the possibility to observe new physics, including anomalous cross-sections and new channels of radioactive decay.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the thermal capture of a spin-polarized neutron in an L=1 orbital angular momentum (OAM) state by a spin-polarized 3He nucleus provides a definitive detection method for neutron OAM states, because the resulting polarization-dependent absorption cross-sections for compound states Jπ = 0−, 1−, and 2− vary in a manner that cannot be obtained with ordinary neutrons.

## Method

The authors extend the standard Rose (1948) calculation of spin-dependent neutron absorption cross-sections for 3He to include neutrons in an L=1 OAM state. They derive the relative absorption cross-sections for the three possible compound states (Jπ = 0−, 1−, 2−) as functions of three experimentally controllable polarizations: the neutron spin polarization p, the OAM polarization PL, and the 3He nuclear spin polarization PN. The cross-sections are obtained by evaluating Clebsch-Gordan coefficients for the coupling of the neutron OAM state (jL=1), neutron spin (S=1/2), and 3He nuclear spin (jN=1/2) into the compound nucleus states.

## Key numbers

* The standard n + 3He reaction releases 764 keV, with proton kinetic energy Ep = 573 keV and triton kinetic energy E3H = 191 keV.
* For conventional (non-OAM) neutrons, the singlet cross-section is σ0 = K(j'=0)/4 [1 − PNp], which goes to zero when pPN = 1.
* For OAM neutrons absorbed into Jπ = 2−, the cross-section is σ2 = K(j''=2)/24 [24 − 5(1−pPL) − 4(1−pPN) − 5(1−PLPN)], which never goes to zero.
* For OAM neutrons absorbed into Jπ = 0−, the cross-section is σ0 = K(j''=0)/12 [1 − pPL + pPN − PLPN], which is zero when pPL = 1 or PLPN = 1.
* The 4He compound nucleus formed by conventional thermal neutron absorption lies at 20.578 MeV above the 4He ground state, 368 keV above a broad Jπ = 0+ resonance at 20.21 MeV.
* For OAM neutron absorption, the Jπ = 0− state lies at 21.01 MeV, the Jπ = 2− state at 21.84 MeV, and the Jπ = 1− state at 24.25 MeV in 4He.

## Context

This work extends the spin-dependent neutron absorption formalism of Rose (1948) and the experimental measurements of Passell and Schermer (1960), who showed that conventional thermal neutron capture by 3He occurs exclusively through the Jπ = 0+ singlet channel. The paper addresses the inability of previous experiments (Cappelletti, 2020; Cappelletti, 2020) to rule out non-OAM explanations for observed neutron OAM effects. The proposed method enables definitive detection of neutron OAM states and suggests possibilities for observing anomalous cross-sections and new channels of radioactive decay through the odd-parity compound states of 4He.
