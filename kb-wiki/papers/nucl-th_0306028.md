# A practical approach to the problem of the missing imaginary part of the handbag diagram in the confined Bethe-Salpeter framework

> In the confined Bethe-Salpeter (BS) methods, the quark propagator is an entire function. Hence, the imaginary part of the handbag diagram disappears, leading to a problem of the vanishing parton distribution function (PDF). In contrast, the direct calculation of the light-cone (LC) momentum distribution does give a non-vanishing result even in the confined BS framework. We consider their precise relation and difference, and propose to use the latter as a practical approach to this problem. Our formalism is general enough to be applied to various effective models.

## Key claim

In the confined Bethe-Salpeter (BS) framework, the imaginary part of the handbag diagram vanishes because the quark propagator is an entire function, making the parton distribution function (PDF) obtained via that method zero; however, the direct calculation of the light-cone (LC) momentum distribution yields a non-vanishing result, and the paper proposes using the latter as a practical alternative, noting that the two methods would agree only if the struck quark propagator satisfied the scaling property.

## Method

The author applies the inverse Mellin transformation analytically to the matrix elements of twist-two operators, introducing a "forward Compton-like amplitude" \(T(x)\) and a "handbag-like diagram" where the struck quark is treated with a free propagator rather than the nonperturbative one. The discontinuity of \(T(x)\) along the cut \([-1,1]\) yields \(H(x)\), from which the quark and antiquark LC momentum distributions \(q(x)\) and \(\bar{q}(x)\) are defined. The handbag-like diagram is obtained by summing the vertex insertions \(\gamma^+(k^+)^{n-1}/(p^+)^n\) into a form proportional to \(\gamma^+/(x p^+ - k^+)\), and its imaginary part gives \(\gamma^+ \delta(x p^+ - k^+)\), directly counting partons with LC momentum fraction \(x\).

## Key numbers

- The forward Compton-like amplitude \(T(x)\) is defined as \(T(x) \equiv \frac{1}{2\pi i} \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} A_n / x^n\), where \(A_n\) are the spin-averaged matrix elements of twist-two operators.
- The discontinuity \(H(x) = \text{disc}(T(x)) = T(x-i\epsilon) - T(x+i\epsilon)\) is related to the PDFs via \(q(x) = H(x)\) for \(0 \le x \le 1\) and \(\bar{q}(x) = -H(-x)\).
- The vertex insertion for the handbag-like diagram sums to \(\frac{1}{2\pi i} \frac{\gamma^+}{x p^+ - k^+}\), and its discontinuity yields \(\gamma^+ \delta(x p^+ - k^+)\).
- The moments satisfy \(A_n = \int_0^1 dx\, x^{n-1} \left( q(x) + (-1)^n \bar{q}(x) \right)\).

## Context

This work addresses a known problem in the confined BS framework, where the entire-function nature of the quark propagator (as used by Roberts and Williams (1994), Tandy (1997), and Alkofer and von Smekal (2001)) causes the imaginary part of the handbag diagram to vanish, making the standard PDF extraction impossible. The paper extends the approach of Mineo, Bentz, and Yazaki (1999, 2002) and Schreiber, Signal, and Thomas (1991), who directly calculated LC momentum distributions, and contrasts it with the handbag-diagram method used by Hecht, Roberts, and Schmidt (2001) for the pion. The formalism is general enough to be applied to various effective models and enables practical PDF calculations even when the quark propagator does not satisfy the scaling property discussed by Weigel, Arriola, and Gamberg (1999).
