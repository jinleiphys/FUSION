# QCD inequalities for the nucleon mass and the free energy of baryonic matter

> The positivity of the integrand of certain Euclidean space functional integrals implies that the free energy per unit volume for QCD with a baryon chemical potential $μ_B$ (and zero isospin chemical potential) is greater than the free energy with isospin chemical potential $μ_I = (2 μ_B)/N_c$ (and zero baryon chemical potential). This relation implies a bound on the nucleon mass: there exists a particle X in QCD such that $M_N \ge (N_c m_X)/(2 I_X)$ where $m_X$ is the mass and of the particle and $I_X$ is its isospin.

# QCD inequalities for the nucleon mass and the free energy of baryonic matter

## Key claim
The free energy per unit volume for QCD with a baryon chemical potential (and zero isospin chemical potential) is greater than or equal to the free energy with an isospin chemical potential set to 2μ_B/N_c (and zero baryon chemical potential). This inequality implies a lower bound on the nucleon mass in terms of the particle with the lowest mass per unit isospin, which in practice is the pion, but the bound is proven directly from QCD rather than from models or the large N_c limit.

## Method
The author uses QCD inequality techniques based on the positivity of Euclidean functional integrands, analogous to the Vafa-Witten argument for the θ-vacuum. For two-flavor QCD with degenerate quark masses, the grand partition function at baryon chemical potential is bounded above by the absolute square of the functional determinant, which is then identified with the partition function at an isospin chemical potential μ_I = 2μ_B/N_c. At zero temperature, thermodynamic arguments relating critical chemical potentials to particle masses yield the bound on the nucleon mass.

## Key numbers
- The bound on the nucleon mass is M_N ≥ (N_c m_X)/(2 I_X), where N_c = 3 for physical QCD, m_X is the mass of particle X, and I_X is its isospin.
- For the case where X is the pion, the bound becomes M_N ≥ (3 m_π)/2.
- The critical baryon chemical potential satisfies μ_B^c ≥ (N_c μ_I^c)/2, with μ_I^c = m_π in chiral perturbation theory.
- The inequality for free energies is G_B(T,μ_B) ≥ G_I(T, 2μ_B/N_c) for all temperatures T.

## Context
This work extends the QCD inequality program initiated by Nussinov (1983), Weingarten (1983), and Witten (1983) to bound the nucleon mass directly from QCD with two degenerate light flavors, without requiring the large N_c limit used by Nussinov and Sathiapalan (1985) or the six-flavor requirement of Weingarten's original bound. The result also provides a rigorous constraint on models of QCD at finite baryon density, a regime inaccessible to standard lattice Monte Carlo methods, by relating the baryon chemical potential free energy to the isospin chemical potential free energy which can be computed on the lattice as demonstrated by Alford, Kapustin, and Wilczek (1999) and subsequent lattice studies.
