# Fluctuations of order parameter in an $SU(N_c)$ effective model

> We investigate features of the deconfinement phase transition in an $SU(N_c)$ gauge theory as revealed by fluctuations of the order parameter. The tool of choice is an effective model built from one-loop expressions of the field determinants of gluon and ghost, in the presence of a Polyakov loop background field. We show that the curvature masses associated with the Cartan angles, which serve as a proxy to study the $A_0$-gluon screening mass, show a characteristic dip in the vicinity of the transition temperature. The strength of the observables, which reflects a competition between the confining and the deconfining forces, is sensitive to assumptions of dynamics, thus provides an interesting link between the $Z(N_c)$ vacuum structure and the properties of gluon and ghost propagators.

## Key claim

The curvature masses associated with the Cartan angles, which serve as a proxy for the $A_0$-gluon screening mass, show a characteristic dip near the deconfinement transition temperature $T_d$, with the strength of this observable being sensitive to assumptions about the dynamical properties of gluon and ghost propagators.

## Method

The authors construct an effective model for $SU(N_c)$ gauge theory using one-loop expressions of the field determinants of gluon and ghost in the presence of a Polyakov loop background field. The effective potential combines a confining term from the group invariant measure (ghost contribution) and a deconfining term from the gluon determinant, with the model extended to include wave function renormalizations fitted to lattice QCD results for gluon and ghost propagators. The curvature masses are extracted from the second derivatives of the effective potential with respect to the Cartan angles, which are then related to the longitudinal and transverse susceptibilities of the Polyakov loop order parameter.

## Key numbers

- The gluon mass parameter used in the model is $m_A \approx 0.756$ GeV, supported by calculations in different gauges.
- For $SU(3)$, the confining potential parameter is $b = (0.1745\ \mathrm{GeV})^{3}$.
- The deconfinement transition temperature calculated numerically is $T_d = 0.274$ GeV, while the condition $\bar{u}_2(T) = 0$ gives an approximate value of $T \approx 0.29$ GeV.
- For the ghost wave function renormalization fit, the parameters are $R_1 = 1.335$ GeV and $R_2 = 0.732$ GeV.
- For the gluon wave function renormalization fit, the parameters are $(g_1, R_1, R_2) = (4, 2.615, 1.660)$ and $(g_2, R_3, R_4) = (1, 2.616, 6.794)$, all in GeV.
- As $N_c$ increases, the Polyakov loop at $T_d$ tends to $\approx 0.5$ for model A, and $\ell(T_d, N_c\rightarrow \infty) \approx 0.6$ for models B and C.

## Context

This work extends previous effective model studies of pure gauge systems by Meisinger (2001, 2003), Braun (2007), and Lo (2013), focusing on fluctuation observables rather than just the order parameter itself. The approach builds on the background field method in Landau-DeWitt gauge developed by Reinosa (2014), and the results clarify the connection between curvature masses and eigenvalues of the Polyakov loop operator in a matrix model. The framework enables future studies of how nonperturbative gluon and ghost propagator properties, as determined from lattice QCD or Schwinger-Dyson equations, manifest in the thermodynamics of the deconfinement transition.
