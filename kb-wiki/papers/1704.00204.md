# Nuclear Effects in the Deuteron and Constraints on the d/u Ratio

> We present a detailed study of nuclear corrections in the deuteron (D) by performing an analysis of data from deep inelastic scattering off proton and D, dilepton pair production in $pp$ and pD interactions, and W^+- and Z boson production in pp and p pbar collisions. In particular, we discuss the determination of the off-shell function describing the modification of the parton distribution functions in bound nucleons in the context of global QCD fits. Our results are consistent with the ones obtained independently from the study of deep inelastic scattering data off heavy nuclei with $A\geq4$, further confirming the universality of the off-shell function. We also study the sensitivity to the modeling of the deuteron wave function. As an important application we discuss the impact of nuclear corrections to the deuteron on the determination of the d quark distribution.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the off-shell function describing the modification of parton distribution functions in bound nucleons can be determined independently from deuterium data, and that this determination is consistent with results from heavy nuclear targets (A ≥ 4), confirming the universality of the off-shell function. The analysis includes the hedge that the off-shell function extracted from deuterium data depends on the choice of deuteron wave function, though the results from different wave function models are consistent within uncertainties.

## Method

The authors performed a global QCD fit at next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO) to data from deep inelastic scattering off proton and deuterium targets, dilepton pair production in pp and pD interactions, and W± and Z boson production in pp and p pbar collisions. The nuclear corrections for the deuteron were calculated using a microscopic model that includes Fermi motion and binding effects, the off-shell correction to bound nucleon structure functions, and the dependence on the deuteron wave function, with the off-shell function δf(x) extracted simultaneously with the parton distribution functions and higher-twist terms.

## Key numbers

- The slope of the ratio R(D/N) = F2D/F2N at Q2 = 20 GeV2 in the region 0.35 ≤ x ≤ 0.55 is dR(D/N)/dx = -0.099 ± 0.006, including model uncertainties
- The deuteron binding energy is about 2.2 MeV, accounting for only about 0.1% of its mass
- The average nucleon virtuality v ranges from -0.037 (CD-Bonn wave function) to -0.062 (WJC1 wave function) across different deuteron wave function models
- The average bound nucleon energy ε ranges from -9.96 MeV (CD-Bonn) to -16.16 MeV (WJC1)
- The average kinetic energy ⟨p2⟩/2M ranges from 7.74 MeV (CD-Bonn) to 13.94 MeV (WJC1)
- The analysis includes 1168 HERA I+II inclusive DIS data points on proton targets
- The analysis includes 605 BCDMS, 490 NMC, and multiple SLAC data sets on proton and deuterium targets
- The BONuS experiment data included have Q2 > 1.5 GeV2 and W > 1.6 GeV
- The DIS data cuts require Q2 > 2.5 GeV2 and W > 1.8 GeV to exclude resonance production

## Context

This work extends the microscopic model for nuclear structure functions developed by Kulagin and Petti (2004, 2007, 2010, 2014), which successfully described nuclear DIS data from 4He to 207Pb, nuclear Drell-Yan data, and W±,Z production in p+Pb collisions at the LHC. The analysis enables improved constraints on the d/u ratio at large x by reducing the correlation between nuclear corrections in the deuteron and the d-quark distribution, using complementary data from Drell-Yan and W± production at colliders. The results are consistent with the off-shell function determined from heavy nuclear targets by Kulagin and Petti (2004), further supporting the universality of the off-shell correction.
