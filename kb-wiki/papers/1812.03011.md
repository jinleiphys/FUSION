# Kaons and antikaons in strong magnetic fields

> The in-medium masses of the kaons and antikaons in strongly magnetized asymmetric nuclear matter are studied using a chiral SU(3) model. The medium modifications of the masses of these open strange pseudoscalar mesons arise due to their interactions with the nucleons and scalar mesons within the model. The proton, the charged nucleon, has effects from the Landau energy levels in the presence of the magnetic field. The anomalous magnetic moments (AMM) of the nucleons are taken into consideration in the present study and these are seen to be large at high magnetic fields and high densities. The isospin effects are appreciable at high densities. The density effects are observed to be the dominant medium effects, as compared to the effects from magnetic field and isospin asymmetry. ~

## Key claim

The in-medium masses of kaons and antikaons in strongly magnetized asymmetric nuclear matter are studied using a chiral SU(3) model, with the finding that density effects dominate over magnetic field and isospin asymmetry effects, though the anomalous magnetic moments (AMM) of nucleons produce large effects at high magnetic fields and high densities.

## Method

The authors use a chiral SU(3) model in the mean field approximation to compute the scalar field expectation values (sigma, zeta, delta) from coupled equations of motion in magnetized nuclear matter. The kaon and antikaon self-energies are derived from an interaction Lagrangian including the Weinberg-Tomozawa term, scalar meson exchange, and range terms, with the proton number and scalar densities incorporating Landau energy levels and both nucleons including anomalous magnetic moment contributions. For charged K± mesons, an additional positive mass shift from the lowest Landau level (eB) is added to the medium-modified mass.

## Key numbers

- At zero magnetic field and symmetric nuclear matter (eta=0) at saturation density rho_0, the K+ mass is 521 MeV and the K- mass is 452 MeV
- At zero magnetic field and symmetric nuclear matter at 4rho_0, the K+ mass is 544.6 MeV and the K- mass is 307.9 MeV
- For eB/m_pi^2 = 8 with AMM effects at rho_0 and eta=0, the K+ effective mass is 652.7 MeV and the K- effective mass is 600.7 MeV
- For eB/m_pi^2 = 8 with AMM effects at 4rho_0 and eta=0, the K+ effective mass is 675.9 MeV and the K- effective mass is 501.3 MeV
- For eB/m_pi^2 = 8 with AMM effects at rho_0 and eta=0.5, the K0 effective mass is 535.0 MeV and the anti-K0 effective mass is 444.2 MeV
- The parameters d1 and d2 in the range terms are determined as 2.56/m_K and 0.73/m_K respectively

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kaons and antikaons in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter using the chiral SU(3) model (Kristof 2010, Mishra 2010) by adding strong magnetic fields and nucleon anomalous magnetic moments. It builds on prior investigations of light vector mesons (Mishra 2017) and heavy flavor mesons (Mishra 2017, Singh 2018) in magnetic fields within the same effective model. The results are relevant for understanding kaon properties in noncentral heavy ion collisions and magnetar environments, and enable future studies of kaon condensation and transport in strongly magnetized dense matter.
