# Renormalization and Mixing of Staple-Shaped Wilson Line Operators on the Lattice Revisited

> Transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions and wave functions (TMDPDFs/TMDWFs) can be extracted from lattice calculations of appropriate Euclidean matrix elements of staple-shaped Wilson line operators. We investigate the mixing pattern of such operators under lattice renormalization using symmetry considerations. We perform an analysis for operators with all Dirac structures, which reveals mixings that are not present in one-loop lattice perturbation theory calculations. We also present the relevant one-loop matching in a renormalization scheme that does not introduce extra non-perturbative effects at large distances, both for the TMDPDFs and for the TMDWFs. Our results have the potential to greatly facilitate numerical calculations of TMDPDFs and TMDWFs on the lattice.

## Key claim

The mixing pattern of staple-shaped Wilson line operators under lattice renormalization, derived from symmetry considerations, reveals mixings that are not present in one-loop lattice perturbation theory calculations, particularly that operators with Dirac structure \(\Gamma\) mix with those of \(\gamma_3\Gamma\) when a fermion action that does not preserve chiral symmetry is used.

## Method

The authors analyzed the transformation properties of staple-shaped Wilson line operators under parity (\(\mathcal{P}\)), time-reversal (\(\mathcal{T}\)), charge conjugation (\(\mathcal{C}\)), and axial transformations in Euclidean space, extending the symmetry-based approach previously applied to straight Wilson line operators for quasi-PDFs by Chen et al. (2017). They constructed an operator basis with definite discrete symmetry eigenvalues to identify allowed mixings, and then presented a one-loop matching kernel in a renormalization scheme that avoids introducing extra non-perturbative effects at large distances, following the spirit of the hybrid renormalization scheme proposed by Ji et al. (2020).

## Key numbers

* The renormalization factor \(Z\) for the multiplicative part can be constructed from the ratio \(\tilde h_\Gamma^2(z_0/2, P^z) / \tilde h_\Gamma(z_0, P^z)\), where \(z_0\) is chosen within the perturbative region.
* The conversion factor from the RI/MOM scheme to the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme for \(\Gamma = \gamma^\mu\) is given by \(\tilde C_\Gamma = 1 + \left[\frac{1}{6}\mathcal{V}_\Gamma^{\mu\mu}(\mu, p_0) - Z_q^{(1)}\right]\), where \(\mathcal{V}_\Gamma^{\mu\mu}\) was calculated in Ebert et al. (2019) and no summation over \(\mu\) is implied.

## Context

This work extends the symmetry-based mixing analysis of Chen et al. (2017) from straight Wilson line operators (used for quasi-PDFs) to staple-shaped Wilson line operators (used for quasi-TMDPDFs and quasi-TMDWFs). The results reveal additional mixings beyond those found in one-loop lattice perturbation theory calculations by Constantinou et al. (2019) and Green et al. (2020). The proposed renormalization scheme, following the hybrid renormalization approach of Ji et al. (2020), avoids the non-perturbative effects at large distances that plague the RI/MOM and ratio schemes, and the one-loop matching kernel enables practical lattice calculations of TMDPDFs and TMDWFs.
