# Ab-initio no-core shell model study of $^{18-24}$Ne isotopes

> We report \textit{ab initio} no-core shell model (NCSM) study of $^{18-24}$Ne isotopes for energy spectra, electromagnetic properties, and point-proton radii using three realistic $NN$ interactions. We have used inside nonlocal outside Yukawa (INOY), charge-dependent Bonn 2000 (CDB2K) and the chiral next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO) interactions. We are able to reach basis size up to $N_{max}$ = 6 for $^{18}$Ne and $N_{max}$ = 4 for the $^{19-24}$Ne isotopes with m-scheme dimensions up to 1.0 $\times$ $10^9$ in case of $^{24}$Ne. We observed better results for INOY interaction in terms of the binding energies of ground state (g.s.), and overall all three interactions provide good agreement with the experimental low-energy spectra. Our results for reduced $M1$ transition strengths and magnetic moments are close to the experimental values. We found that for long-range observables such as the $E2$ transition strengths, the electric quadrupole moments, and the point-proton radii ($r_p$), we need higher $N_{max}$ calculations to obtain results comparable to the experimental data. We have observed almost 6 \% increment in the converged $r_p$ as we increase the model space from $N_{max}$ = 4 to $N_{max}$ = 6.

## Key claim

The paper reports that the INOY interaction yields better results for ground-state binding energies of Ne isotopes than CDB2K or N$^3$LO, but for long-range observables such as E2 transition strengths, electric quadrupole moments, and point-proton radii, higher Nmax calculations are needed to obtain results comparable to experimental data, with a caveat that the NCSM results are not fully converged for these observables.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for 18-24Ne isotopes using three realistic NN interactions: INOY, CDB2K, and chiral N$^3$LO. They used the Okubo-Lee-Suzuki (OLS) scheme to derive effective interactions and reached basis sizes up to Nmax = 6 for 18Ne and Nmax = 4 for 19-24Ne, with m-scheme dimensions up to 1.0 × 10^9 for 24Ne. The calculations were performed using the pAntoine code, and the optimal harmonic-oscillator frequencies were determined by minimizing ground-state energies for each interaction and nucleus.

## Key numbers

- For 18Ne, the excitation energy of the 2+1 state obtained using N$^3$LO matches the experimental value of 1.887 MeV; with CDB2K it is lower by 500 keV.
- For 20Ne, the calculated excitation energy of the 2+1 state is 1.034 MeV for INOY and 1.431 MeV for CDB2K, compared to the experimental value of 1.633 MeV.
- For 22Ne, the excitation energies of the 2+1 and 4+1 states increase by 168 keV and 598 keV, respectively, when the basis is increased from Nmax = 0 to 4 for the INOY interaction.
- For 24Ne, the 0+2 and 2+3 states improve by more than 2 MeV on increasing basis size from Nmax = 0 to 4 for the INOY interaction, ending up only 44 keV and 60 keV lower than the experimental values, respectively.
- For 21Ne, the excitation energy of the first excited state obtained with N$^3$LO is 0.339 MeV, only 11 keV less than the experimental value.
- The m-scheme dimension for 24Ne at Nmax = 4 reaches 1.0 × 10^9.
- A 6% increment in the converged point-proton radius (rp) was observed when increasing the model space from Nmax = 4 to Nmax = 6.

## Context

This work extends previous ab initio studies of neon isotopes, including the in-medium no-core shell model (IM-NCSM) and projected generator coordinate method (PGCM) by Jansen et al. (2016), the symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) by Dytrych et al. (2020), and the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) by Stroberg et al. (2017). The results enable systematic comparison of three different NN interactions across the neon isotopic chain from neutron shell closure at N=8 to N=14, and highlight the need for larger model spaces or inclusion of three-nucleon forces to fully converge long-range observables.
