# Dynamical Symmetries Reflected in Realistic Interactions

> Realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions, derived within the framework of meson theory or more recently in terms of chiral effective field theory, yield new possibilities for achieving a unified microscopic description of atomic nuclei. Based on spectral distribution methods, a comparison of these interactions to a most general Sp(4) dynamically symmetric interaction, which previously we found to reproduce well that part of the interaction that is responsible for shaping pairing-governed isobaric analog 0+ states, can determine the extent to which this significantly simpler model Hamiltonian can be used to obtain an approximate, yet very good description of low-lying nuclear structure. And furthermore, one can apply this model in situations that would otherwise be prohibitive because of the size of the model space. In addition, we introduce a Sp(4) symmetry breaking term by including the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction in the analysis and examining the capacity of this extended model interaction to imitate realistic interactions. This provides a further step towards gaining a better understanding of the underlying foundation of realistic interactions and their ability to reproduce striking features of nuclei such as strong pairing correlations or collective rotational motion.

## Key claim

Realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions (CD-Bonn, CD-Bonn+3terms, and GXPF1) in the fp shell contain a large fraction of an Sp(4) dynamically symmetric pairing interaction plus a quadrupole-quadrupole term, but the degree of agreement depends on the specific interaction, the nuclear region (single-j f7/2 versus multi-j upper fp shell), and the isospin of the states considered.

## Method

The authors used spectral distribution theory to compute correlation coefficients between the pure two-body parts of three realistic interactions (CD-Bonn, CD-Bonn+3terms, GXPF1) and model Hamiltonians based on Sp(4) dynamical symmetry (isovector pairing plus isoscalar pn force) and an extended model that adds a traceless quadrupole-quadrupole term breaking Sp(4) symmetry. The analysis was performed for both scalar (averaged over all states) and isospin-scalar (fixed isospin T) distributions in the f7/2 single-j shell and the multi-j upper fp shell (1f5/2, 2p1/2, 2p3/2).

## Key numbers

- In the f7/2 orbit for the highest possible isospin group (T=n/2), the CD-Bonn, CD-Bonn+3terms, and GXPF1 interactions contain on average 59%, 77%, and 78% of the pairing+quadrupole interaction, respectively.
- For those same T=n/2 states, the correlation rises to 91% (CD-Bonn), 97% (CD-Bonn+3terms), and 92% (GXPF1) with the pairing+quadrupole model.
- In the upper fp shell scalar distribution, the correlation of the pure two-body GXPF1 interaction with the Sp(4) model is 0.65, with the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction is 0.67, and with the extended pairing+quadrupole model is 0.83.
- The scalar correlation between the pure two-body parts of CD-Bonn and CD-Bonn+3terms is 0.90, while between CD-Bonn and GXPF1 it is 0.56, and between CD-Bonn+3terms and GXPF1 it is 0.53.
- The extended model Hamiltonian with chi=0.027 reproduced low-lying energy spectra of Ni-58 and Cu-58 in the fpg major shell.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Sviratcheva et al. 2006) on Sp(4) symmetry in isobaric analog 0+ states by comparing three modern realistic interactions (CD-Bonn from Machleidt 2001, CD-Bonn+3terms from Popescu et al. 2005, and GXPF1 from Honma et al. 2004) to model Hamiltonians with pairing and quadrupole terms. The spectral distribution method, developed by French (1971) and Chang et al. (1971), allows assessment of symmetry content without large-scale diagonalization. The results enable using the simpler Sp(4)-based model as a good approximation for low-lying structure in fp-shell nuclei, particularly for GXPF1 in the upper fp shell, and provide a framework for understanding how realistic interactions generate pairing correlations and collective rotational motion.
