# Elimination of spurious modes within QRPA

> We suggest a generalized method for elimination of spurious admixtures (SA) from intrinsic nuclear excitations described within the Quasiparticle-Random-Phase-Approximation (QRPA). Various kinds of SA-corrections are treated at the same theoretical ground. The known corrections are well reproduced. As relevant cases, we consider subtraction of SA related with i) violation of the translational invariance (isovector E1 and isoscalar toroidal and compression E1 modes), ii) pairing-induced non-conservation of the particle number (E2(K=0) and E0 modes), and iii) rotational invariance (E2(K=1) and M1(K=1) modes). The SA subtraction can be done at the level of QRPA states, electromagnetic responses, and even transition operators. The additional deformation-induced corrections are proposed. The accuracy of the method is demonstrated by Skyrme QRPA calculations for axially-deformed $^{154}$Sm.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a generalized method for eliminating spurious admixtures (SA) from intrinsic nuclear excitations described within the Quasiparticle-Random-Phase-Approximation (QRPA), treating various kinds of SA-corrections on the same theoretical ground. The method works even when the spurious mode is not exactly at zero energy (which is the usual case in practice due to limited configuration space and numerical inaccuracy), by requiring orthogonality of physical states to the approximate spurious mode.

## Method

The method refines QRPA states by requiring their orthogonality to the spurious mode, which is approximated by the lowest-energy eigenstate (with small but non-zero energy) using both the generalized coordinate and momentum operators. The SA subtraction can be performed at three levels: directly on QRPA wave functions, on electromagnetic transition matrix elements and responses, or on transition operators themselves. For cases where the symmetry operator and its conjugate are known (e.g., E1 transitions), analytical corrections are derived; otherwise, the two-quasiparticle amplitudes of the unknown conjugate operator are determined from the QRPA matrices.

## Key numbers

* The Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule for dipole excitations is exhausted by 95% and the isoscalar dipole energy-weighted sum rule by 97% in the calculations for $^{154}$Sm.
* The equilibrium quadrupole deformation of $^{154}$Sm is $\beta = 0.339$, obtained by minimization of the system energy.
* The single-particle space includes levels up to 40 MeV (1533 proton and 1722 neutron levels in $^{154}$Sm).
* For $K^{\pi} = 1^-$ states, the two-quasiparticle basis includes approximately 9000 proton and approximately 16000 neutron quasiparticle pairs.
* The energy-dependent folding parameters used for $^{154}$Sm are $\Delta_0 = 0.1$ MeV, $a = 0.15$, and $E_0 = 8.3$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous projection techniques for removing spurious admixtures (e.g., Ju08, Co00, Na07, Mi12) by using a more general expression for the spurious state that includes both the generalized coordinate and momentum operators, allowing a unified treatment of different symmetry violations. The method is demonstrated for translational invariance violation (E1 modes), pairing-induced particle number non-conservation (E0 and E2(K=0) modes), and rotational invariance violation (E2(K=1) and M1(K=1) modes) in axially-deformed $^{154}$Sm using Skyrme QRPA with the SLy6 parametrization. The approach enables systematic removal of spurious admixtures from both spherical and deformed nuclei, and the derived deformation-induced corrections for E1 excitations are shown to be essential for the compression isoscalar mode.
