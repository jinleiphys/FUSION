# Rearranging Pionless Effective Field Theory

> We point out a redundancy in the operator structure of the pionless effective field theory which dramatically simplifies computations. This redundancy is best exploited by using dibaryon fields as fundamental degrees of freedom. In turn, this suggests a new power counting scheme which sums range corrections to all orders. We explore this method with a few simple observables: the deuteron charge form factor, n p -> d gamma, and Compton scattering from the deuteron. Higher dimension operators involving electroweak gauge fields are not renormalized by the s-wave strong interactions, and therefore do not scale with inverse powers of the renormalization scale. Thus, naive dimensional analysis of these operators is sufficient to estimate their contribution to a given process.

## Key claim

The paper identifies a redundancy in the operator structure of pionless effective field theory (EFT) that dramatically simplifies computations; this redundancy is best exploited by using dibaryon fields as fundamental degrees of freedom, which suggests a new power counting scheme that sums range corrections to all orders, with the hedge that higher dimension operators involving electroweak gauge fields are not renormalized by s-wave strong interactions and therefore scale according to naive dimensional analysis rather than with inverse powers of the renormalization scale.

## Method

The authors reformulate pionless EFT by introducing dibaryon fields as explicit degrees of freedom in both the \(^3S_1\) and \(^1S_0\) channels, with the effective range \(r\) taken to be of order \(1/Q\) in the power counting. They demonstrate that using the equations of motion for nucleon fields reduces the number of independent higher-order operators, and they apply this dibaryon formulation to compute the deuteron charge form factor, the radiative capture process \(np \rightarrow d\gamma\), and Compton scattering from the deuteron, working in the zero-recoil limit with non-relativistic kinematics.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter in the \(^3S_1\) channel is \(\gamma r^{(^3S_1)} \sim 0.4\), where \(\gamma^2 = M_N B\) is the deuteron binding momentum.
- The dibaryon coupling is fixed as \(y^2 = 8\pi / (M_N^2 r^{(^3S_1)})\) and the bare dibaryon mass parameter as \(\Delta = (2/(M_N r^{(^3S_1)}))(1/a^{(^3S_1)} - \mu)\), where \(\mu\) is the renormalization scale.
- The measured cross section for \(np \rightarrow d\gamma\) is \(\sigma^{\text{expt}} = 334.2 \pm 0.5\) mb for an incident neutron speed of \(|v| = 2200\) m/s in the laboratory frame, requiring \(L_1 \sim -4.0\) fm.
- The uncertainty in the capture cross section in the energy regime relevant for nucleosynthesis is \(\sim 3\%\).
- The nucleon polarizabilities computed at one-loop order in chiral perturbation theory are \(\alpha_N = 10\beta_N = 5 g_A^2 e^2 / (192\pi^2 f_\pi^2 m_\pi) = 1.2 \times 10^{-3}\) fm\(^3\).
- The differential cross section for Compton scattering is shown for an incident photon energy of \(\omega = 49\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the pionless EFT framework developed by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998-1999) and by Chen, Rupak, and Savage (1999) by introducing a dibaryon field formulation that simplifies higher-order calculations. The approach builds on the observation by Phillips, Rupak, and Savage (1999) that fitting the deuteron binding energy and residue rather than scattering length and effective range improves convergence, and on the suggestion by Kaplan (1997) to treat the effective range as order \(1/Q\). The dibaryon formulation reproduces effective range theory results for the deuteron charge form factor and \(np \rightarrow d\gamma\) while reducing computational complexity, and it enables systematic inclusion of higher-order effects such as the shape parameter and nucleon polarizabilities in Compton scattering, with the potential to determine nucleon isoscalar polarizabilities from low-energy deuteron Compton scattering measurements.
