# Two-body problems with confining potentials

> A formalism is presented that allows an asymptotically exact solution of non-relativistic and semi-relativistic two-body problems with infinitely rising confining potentials. We consider both linear and quadratic confinement. The additional short-range terms are expanded in a Coulomb-Sturmian basis. Such kinds of Hamiltonians are frequently used in atomic, nuclear, and particle physics.

## Key claim

The paper presents a formalism that allows an asymptotically exact solution of non-relativistic and semi-relativistic two-body problems with infinitely rising confining potentials, but the exactness is conditional on taking the basis size to infinity; in practice, the method yields highly accurate results with a finite, relatively small basis.

## Method

The bound-state problem is written in integral equation form (Lippmann-Schwinger equation) with the kinetic energy and confining terms incorporated into the Green's operator. The short-range part of the potential is expanded in a separable form using a Coulomb-Sturmian (CS) basis, which allows the Green's operator to be evaluated analytically via matrix continued fractions. For non-relativistic kinetic energy with linear or quadratic confinement, the Hamiltonian matrix in the CS basis is an infinite symmetric band matrix (pentadiagonal for linear, septadiagonal for quadratic), and the Green's matrix is obtained by inverting this band matrix using block-matrix continued fractions. For semi-relativistic kinetic energy, the CS matrix is asymptotically pentadiagonal dominant, so the same continued-fraction technique applies.

## Key numbers

- Yukawa plus linear confinement, non-relativistic, first three eigenstates (m=1, a1=1, v1=10, μ1=5, v2=-5, μ2=1): 1st = -0.4380692, 2nd = 2.399815, 3rd = 3.840087 (converged to 7 decimal places by N=15 basis states).
- Yukawa plus linear confinement, semi-relativistic, first three eigenstates (same parameters): 1st = -1.596355, 2nd = 1.007884, 3rd = 2.324394 (converged to 6 decimal places by N=30 basis states).
- Yukawa plus quadratic confinement, non-relativistic, first three eigenstates (m=1, a2=0.5, v1=3, μ1=5, v2=-2, μ2=1): 1st = 0.5503106, 2nd = 2.929150, 3rd = 5.056225 (converged to 7 decimal places by N=15 basis states).
- Yukawa plus quadratic confinement, semi-relativistic, first three eigenstates (same parameters): 1st = -0.1080306, 2nd = 1.603199, 3rd = 2.854740 (converged to 5-6 decimal places by N=33 basis states).

## Context

This work extends the Coulomb-Sturmian separable expansion method, previously developed for Coulomb-like potentials by Adhikari and Tomio (1992) and by Konya, Levai, and Papp (1997), to handle infinitely rising confining potentials (linear and quadratic) that appear in quark-quark interaction models and trapped quantum particles. The method enables accurate computation of many bound states (not just the lowest few) for both non-relativistic and semi-relativistic Hamiltonians, which is important for three- or many-body calculations where the full spectrum of the two-body subsystem is needed.
