# A theory vade mecum for PSI experiments

> This article gives a compact introduction and overview of the theory underlying the experiments described in the rest of this review.

## Key claim

This article provides a compact pedagogical overview of the theoretical framework, Standard Model effective field theory, low-energy effective field theory, and chiral perturbation theory, that underlies the experimental program at the Paul Scherrer Institute, but it explicitly does not attempt to be self-contained or to derive new results, serving instead as a guide to key references.

## Method

The authors first present the QED+QCD Lagrangian and the decomposition of the electromagnetic current into form factors for leptons and nucleons, then introduce the Fermi theory of weak interactions at low energies. They generalize the framework to include beyond-Standard-Model physics via the low-energy effective field theory (LEFT) and the Standard Model effective field theory (SMEFT), discussing operator bases, renormalization-group running, and matching between the two EFTs. Finally, they describe chiral perturbation theory as the low-energy effective theory of QCD, writing the leading-order Lagrangian in terms of pion fields and low-energy constants.

## Key numbers

* The fine-structure constant at zero momentum transfer is approximately 1/137.
* The momentum transfer for muon capture on the proton, neglecting binding energies, is \(q_0^2 = -0.88\,m_\mu^2\).
* The leading-order chiral perturbation theory Lagrangian contains two terms, with the low-energy constant \(F\) appearing in the kinetic term and the constant \(B\) relating the quark mass matrix to the pion mass.

## Context

This article is part of the "Review of Particle Physics at PSI" (SciPost Phys. Proc. 5, 2021) and provides the theoretical background for the experimental sections that follow, covering muon decay, muon-to-electron conversion, muonic atom spectroscopy, neutron beta decay, neutron electric dipole moment searches, and pion decays. It extends the pedagogical tradition of Michel (1949) on muon decay parameters and Fetscher (1986) on generalized contact interactions, and it enables readers to connect low-energy measurements to constraints on BSM models through the SMEFT-to-LEFT matching framework of Jenkins, Manohar, and collaborators (2013–2019).
