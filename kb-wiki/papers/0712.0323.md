# Critical view of WKB decay widths

> A detailed comparison of the expressions for the decay widths obtained within the semiclassical WKB approximation using different approaches to the tunneling problem is performed. The differences between the available improved formulae for tunneling near the top and the bottom of the barrier are investigated. Though the simple WKB method gives the right order of magnitude of the decay widths, a small number of parameters are often fitted. The need to perform the fitting procedure remaining consistently within the WKB framework is emphasized in the context of the fission model based calculations. Calculations for the decay widths of some recently found super heavy nuclei using microscopic alpha-nucleus potentials are presented to demonstrate the importance of a consistent WKB calculation. The half-lives are found to be sensitive to the density dependence of the nucleon-nucleon interaction and the implementation of the Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization condition inherent in the WKB approach.

# Critical view of WKB decay widths

## Key claim

While the simple WKB method gives the right order of magnitude for alpha decay widths, the specific numerical results depend strongly on whether the calculation consistently implements the Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization condition, the Langer modification, and the theoretical relation for the vibrational energy, elements that are often neglected or fitted arbitrarily in super-asymmetric fission model (SAFM) calculations.

## Method

The authors compared four WKB-based approaches to alpha decay widths: the two-potential approach (TPA), a path integral method with Jost functions, a comparison-equation method for improved WKB formulae, and the super-asymmetric fission model (SAFM). They applied these methods to calculate half-lives of superheavy nuclei (Z=106–118) using double-folding alpha-nucleus potentials with both free and density-dependent nucleon-nucleon (M3Y) interactions, imposing the Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization condition to fix the potential normalization. They also tested improved WKB formulae near the top of the barrier using the 8Be 2+ resonance.

## Key numbers

- For 271Sg (Q=8.67 MeV, G=22): half-life with free NN = 17638.6 ms, with density-dependent NN = 2794.5 ms, with density-dependent NN but λ=1 (no Bohr-Sommerfeld condition) = 213197.8 ms, experimental = 288000 ms
- For 273Ds (Q=11.368 MeV, G=22): half-life with free NN = 0.021 ms, with density-dependent NN = 0.0034 ms, experimental = 0.17 ms
- For 294118 (Q=11.81 MeV, G=22): half-life with free NN = 0.416 ms, with density-dependent NN = 0.064 ms, experimental = 1.8 ms
- For the 8Be 2+ resonance at 3.03 MeV: experimental width = 1.513 MeV; standard WKB gives 1.232 MeV; the improved formula with positive sign in denominator gives 1.535 MeV; the formula with negative sign gives 0.63 MeV
- Barrier height for 8Be alpha-alpha system = 3.27 MeV; Q-value = 3.122 MeV
- Fitted vibrational energies Eν in SAFM are ~1 MeV (e.g., 0.786 MeV for 271Sg), but the self-consistent value from Eq. (vibenergy) is ~4 MeV (e.g., 4.130 MeV for 271Sg)
- Assault frequencies νTPA are of order 10^21 s^-1 (e.g., 2.923 × 10^21 s^-1 for 271Sg)
- The normalization constant λ from the Bohr-Sommerfeld condition ranges 0.6–0.7 for free NN and 2.0–2.3 for density-dependent NN

## Context

This work extends the critical comparison of WKB decay width formulae initiated by Gurvitz (1988), Froeman (1970), and Shepard (1978), and specifically addresses inconsistencies in the SAFM approach of Poenaru (1980s) as applied to superheavy nuclei. The authors show that SAFM calculations in recent literature (e.g., by various groups studying superheavy elements) often neglect the Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization condition, the Langer modification, and the self-consistency requirement for the vibrational energy, leading to results that are not properly within the WKB framework. The work enables more reliable predictions of superheavy nucleus half-lives by demonstrating which ingredients of the WKB calculation are essential and how sensitive the results are to the density dependence of the nucleon-nucleon interaction.
