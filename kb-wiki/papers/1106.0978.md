# CGC initial conditions at RHIC and LHC

> Monte-Carlo implementations of kT -factorization formula with both KLN and running-coupling BK unintegrated gluon distributions for nucleus-nucleus collisions are used to analyze recent experimental data on the particle multiplicities from RHIC(Au+Au@200GeV) and LHC(Pb+Pb@2.76TeV). We also compare the predicted transverse energy at midrapidity to new data from ALICE.

## Key claim

Monte-Carlo implementations of the kT-factorization formula with both KLN and running-coupling BK unintegrated gluon distributions can reproduce the centrality dependence of charged hadron multiplicity at both RHIC and LHC energies, but the authors note that parameter dependence must be checked carefully and that additional particle production mechanisms during the pre-thermalization evolution may be needed if the evolution speed parameter λ is small.

## Method

The authors implement two Monte-Carlo models (MC-KLN and MCrcBK) that apply the kT-factorized formula locally in the transverse plane, computing gluon production from nucleus-nucleus collisions event-by-event with fluctuations in nucleon positions. The MC-KLN model uses a parameterized saturation scale Q_s^2 with a free parameter λ controlling x-evolution speed, while the MCrcBK model obtains unintegrated gluon distributions from numerical solutions of the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) evolution equation with a McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) initial condition. Both models compute thickness functions at each grid point to obtain local saturation scales and then integrate over transverse momentum to predict charged hadron multiplicities and transverse energy.

## Key numbers

- Charged hadron multiplicity in Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV as a function of number of participants N_part, compared to PHOBOS and ALICE data respectively
- In MC-KLN model, λ = 0.23 used in Eq. (4) for the x-evolution speed parameter Q_s^2 ∝ (0.01/x)^λ, where λ is expected in the range 0.2 < λ < 0.3 from HERA global analysis
- In MCrcBK model, initial saturation scale Q_s0^2 = 0.2 GeV^2 for nucleon and Λ = 0.2 GeV used in MV initial condition
- For Gaussian nucleon shape: B = 0.2 fm^2, σ_NN = 41.94 mb for √s_NN = 200 GeV, σ_NN = 61.36 mb for √s_NN = 2.76 TeV
- Approximately 2.5% (0.5%) of beam energy predicted to be deposited initially into central rapidity region in central collisions at RHIC (LHC)
- Constant gluon multiplication factor κ_g = 5 assumed in MCrcBK model to obtain p⊥-integrated hadron yields

## Context

This work extends earlier KLN and fKLN models (which used average or local saturation scales without nucleon position fluctuations) by implementing Monte-Carlo treatments that account for event-by-event fluctuations of nucleon positions inside nuclei. The MCrcBK model in particular builds on rcBK evolution solutions from Albacete (2007, 2009, 2010) and provides more predictive power than MC-KLN because the x-dependence is obtained dynamically from the evolution equation rather than from a parameterized form. The results enable future studies of initial conditions for hydrodynamic simulations and suggest that entropy production during thermalization may be small, though the authors note that the bottom-up thermalization scenario predicts an additional factor of α_s(Q_s^2)^{-2/5} increase in gluon number during subsequent evolution that could be incorporated.
