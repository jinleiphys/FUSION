# Theoretical reaction rates of the $^{12}$C($α$,$γ$)$^{16}$O reaction from the potential model

> The radiative capture cross sections of $^{12}$C($α$,$γ$)$^{16}$O and derived reaction rates are calculated from the direct capture potential model. The resulting $S$-factor at low energies is found to be dominated by $E$2 transition to the $^{16}$O ground state. The $E$1 and $E$2 $S$-factors at $E_{c.m.}=0.3$ MeV are $S_{E1}\approx3$ keV~b and $S_{E2}=150^{+41}_{-17}$ keV~b, respectively. The sum of the cascade transition through the excited state of $^{16}$O is $S_{\rm casc}= 18\pm4.5$ keV~b. The derived reaction rates at low temperatures seem to be concordant with those from the previous evaluation. For astrophysical applications, our reaction rates below $T_9=3$ are provided in an analytic expression.

## Key claim
The potential model predicts that the $E2$ transition dominates the $^{12}$C($\alpha$,$\gamma$)$^{16}$O $S$-factor at low energies, yielding a very small $E1$ component ($S_{E1}\approx3$ keV b) that differs substantially from previous $R$-matrix analyses, though the total $S$-factor remains consistent with earlier evaluations.

## Method
The radiative capture cross sections were calculated using a direct capture potential model, where scattering wavefunctions were generated from a potential fitted to $\alpha$+$^{12}$C elastic scattering data including nuclear rainbow scattering at high energies. Bound states were described either by $\alpha$-cluster models (for the $0^+_2$ and $2^+_1$ states) or by the separation energy method (for shell-model states). The effective charge in the electromagnetic operator was used as an adjustable parameter to minimize differences with experimental cross section data.

## Key numbers
- $S_{E1} \approx 3$ keV b at $E_{c.m.}=0.3$ MeV for the $E1$ transition to the $^{16}$O ground state
- $S_{E2} = 150^{+41}_{-17}$ keV b at $E_{c.m.}=0.3$ MeV for the $E2$ transition to the $^{16}$O ground state
- $S_{\text{casc}} = 18 \pm 4.5$ keV b at $E_{c.m.}=0.3$ MeV for the sum of cascade transitions through excited states of $^{16}$O
- Total $S$-factor at $E_{c.m.}=0.3$ MeV: $171^{+46}_{-22}$ keV b
- Asymptotic normalization constants: $C^2 = 5.00 \times 10^{28}$ fm$^{-1}$ for the $1^-_1$ state, $C^2 = 2.03 \times 10^{10}$ fm$^{-1}$ for the $2^+_1$ state
- Analytic reaction rate expression provided for temperatures below $T_9 = 3$ (where $T_9$ is temperature in units of $10^9$ K)

## Context
This work extends the potential model approach of Katsuma (2012, 2010) to the $^{12}$C($\alpha$,$\gamma$)$^{16}$O reaction, which is critical for helium burning nucleosynthesis. The results contradict the $R$-matrix analyses of KU02 (Kunz, 2002) and BU96 (Buchmann, 1996), which found $S_{E1} \approx 76-79$ keV b and $S_{E2} \approx 70-85$ keV b, whereas this work finds a much smaller $E1$ component and larger $E2$ component. The derived reaction rates are compared with the CF88 (Caughlan and Fowler, 1988) and NACRE compilations, and the analytic expression enables direct use in astrophysical models of stellar evolution and nucleosynthesis.
