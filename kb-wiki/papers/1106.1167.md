# The Role of Collective Neutrino Flavor Oscillations in Core-Collapse Supernova Shock Revival

> We explore the effects of collective neutrino flavor oscillations due to neutrino-neutrino interactions on the neutrino heating behind a stalled core-collapse supernova shock. We carry out axisymmetric (2D) radiation-hydrodynamic core-collapse supernova simulations, tracking the first 400 ms of the post-core-bounce evolution in 11.2 solar mass and 15 solar mass progenitor stars. Using inputs from these 2D simulations, we perform neutrino flavor oscillation calculations in multi-energy single-angle and multi-angle single-energy approximations. Our results show that flavor conversions do not set in until close to or outside the stalled shock, enhancing heating by not more than a few percent in the most optimistic case. Consequently, we conclude that the postbounce pre-explosion dynamics of standard core-collapse supernovae remains unaffected by neutrino oscillations. Multi-angle effects in regions of high electron density can further inhibit collective oscillations, strengthening our conclusion.

## Key claim
Collective neutrino flavor oscillations due to neutrino-neutrino interactions do not significantly affect the postbounce pre-explosion dynamics of standard core-collapse supernovae, because flavor conversions set in only close to or outside the stalled shock, enhancing heating by not more than a few percent in the most optimistic case.

## Method
The authors performed axisymmetric (2D) radiation-hydrodynamic core-collapse supernova simulations tracking the first 400 ms of post-core-bounce evolution in 11.2 solar mass and 15 solar mass progenitor stars. Using neutrino radiation fields from these 2D simulations, they carried out neutrino flavor oscillation calculations in multi-energy single-angle and multi-angle single-energy approximations to determine the radii at which collective oscillations set in and could influence neutrino heating.

## Key numbers
- Progenitor masses studied: 11.2 solar masses and 15 solar masses
- Post-core-bounce evolution tracked: first 400 ms
- Neutrino heating enhancement in the most optimistic case: not more than a few percent
- Typical gain radius during accretion phase: approximately 100 km
- Typical shock radius during accretion phase: approximately 200 km
- Benchmark mixing angle θ₁₃ used: 0.001
- Absolute value of atmospheric mass-squared difference |Δm²ₐₜₘ| used: 2.6×10⁻³ eV²
- Spectral split energy for νₑ: approximately 6-8 MeV
- Region of majority of net heating remains at least approximately 100 km below the oscillation radius, even in the 11.2 solar mass progenitor

## Context
This work extends the study of self-induced collective neutrino flavor oscillations, previously explored by Duan et al. (2005, 2006) and others, to realistic 2D core-collapse supernova models. It contradicts the assumption that such oscillations could dramatically enhance neutrino heating and revive the stalled shock, as suggested by Suwa et al. (2011) who assumed a neutrino conversion radius at 100 km. The results also address the suppression of collective oscillations by high electron density reported by Chakraborty et al. (2011) based on 1D simulations, finding that such suppression may be significantly weaker in 2D models, though flavor conversion still occurs too far out to affect the explosion mechanism.
