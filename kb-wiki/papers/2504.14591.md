# Kinetic Theory of Quasiparticles, Retarded Correlators and Hydrodynamics

> Within the relaxation time approximation under a constant mass profile, we investigate the collective dynamics of a system of massive relativistic particles described by the Maxwell-Boltzmann equilibrium distribution. We analytically derive the two-point retarded correlation functions for both charge and energy-momentum tensor components at arbitrary momentum and frequency. We expand our results in the limits of very small and very large mass-to-temperature ratios ($m/T$). Similar to the massless case, we identify a critical threshold in ($k τ$) below which the correlators permit physical solutions. This behavior arises from a logarithmic branch cut in the spectral function. At higher momenta, solutions emerge significantly below this cut, corresponding to non-hydrodynamic modes. Our analysis demonstrates that hydrodynamic poles dominate in the strong coupling regime, while the weak coupling regime features a logarithmic branch cut extending along $ω= k$ and $ω= -k$. Notably, in the sound channel, finite mass modifies the standard propagating sound mode, converting it into a purely imaginary mode. In contrast, the shear channel exhibits modes that asymptotically converge to their massless counterparts. Additionally, we compute the transport coefficients for shear and bulk viscosity, along with higher-order gradient corrections up to third order, expressed as perturbative expansions in both the small and large ($m/T$) regimes.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for a system of massive relativistic particles described by the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution within the relaxation time approximation, finite mass modifies the standard propagating sound mode in the sound channel, converting it into a purely imaginary mode, while shear channel modes asymptotically converge to their massless counterparts; this behavior is derived analytically under expansions for very small and very large mass-to-temperature ratios (m/T).

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann equation within the Relaxation Time Approximation (RTA) for a system of particles with a constant mass profile and zero chemical potential in the background limit. Using a variational approach, they derive retarded two-point correlation functions for the charge and energy-momentum tensor components at arbitrary momentum (k) and frequency (omega). To obtain analytical expressions, they expand results for both small and large limits of the mass-to-temperature ratio x = m/T, performing integrals over momentum space using expansions of logarithmic functions and modified Bessel functions of the second kind.

## Key numbers

- The trace anomaly parameter Theta reaches its maximum value Theta_Max ~ 0.058 at x = 2.386, demonstrating significant departure from scale invariance at this point.
- At x = 0 (massless limit), the speed of sound c_s^2 = 1/3 and the trace anomaly Theta = 0.
- As x approaches infinity (heavy quasiparticle limit), both c_s^2 and Theta approach zero.
- The number susceptibility chi(m, T_0) for m << T_0 is chi = (T_0^2 / 2 pi^2) * (2 - (1/2) x^2 + O(x^4)).
- The number susceptibility chi(m, T_0) for m >> T_0 is chi = (T_0^2 / 2 pi^2) * e^{-x} * ( x^{3/2} sqrt(pi/2) + O(x^{1/2}) ).

## Context

This work extends the kinetic theory analysis of collective dynamics from massless systems (Romatschke, 2015) to massive relativistic particles, breaking conformal symmetry and bringing the analysis closer to realistic physical scenarios such as the Quark-Gluon Plasma. The study of massive particles, referred to as quasiparticles, has been explored in contexts including derivation of second-order relativistic viscous hydrodynamics for temperature-dependent quasiparticles (Tinti, 2016) and investigation of hydrodynamization in boost-invariant plasmas with finite particle mass (Jaiswal, 2022). The results enable examination of the validity of relativistic hydrodynamics near critical points where mass serves as an order parameter for chiral symmetry restoration.
