# Non-adiabatic effect in electron screening in fusion reactions at astrophysical energies

> We calculate the electron screening effect in low energy nuclear fusion reactions by taking non-adiabaticity of the tunnleing process into account. In order to investigate deviations from the adiabatic limit, we use the dynamical norm method, which has recently been developed by the present authors. Using $d$+D reaction as an example, we show that the screening energy never exceeds those estimated in the adiabatic approximation. Our calculations indicate that the non-adiabatic effect is important both in classically allowed and classically forbidden processes.

# Non-adiabatic effect in electron screening in fusion reactions at astrophysical energies

## Key claim

The screening energy in low-energy fusion reactions never exceeds the adiabatic approximation estimate, and non-adiabatic effects are important both in classically allowed and classically forbidden processes. The authors show that properly treating the tunneling region yields a significantly smaller screening energy than previous approaches that only considered the classically allowed region.

## Method

The authors apply the dynamical norm method, which they had previously developed, to the d+D reaction system. They solve the time-dependent Schrödinger equation for the electron in the external Coulomb field of two moving deuterons, first along real time to the outer classical turning point and then along imaginary time through the tunneling region to the inner turning point. The tunneling probability is obtained as the product of the adiabatic tunneling probability and the norm of the electronic wave function at the inner turning point, where this norm represents the deviation from the adiabatic limit.

## Key numbers

- The adiabatic screening energy uses binding energy differences of ΔE_g = 40.7 eV (difference between the 1s orbital of He⁺ and the 1s orbital of D) and ΔE_u = 0 eV (difference between the 1p orbital of He⁺ and the 1s orbital of D)
- The initial position for solving the time evolution is chosen to be 10 atomic units for the internuclear separation
- The inner turning point is assumed to be twice the deuteron radius

## Context

This work extends the study of electron screening in low-energy fusion reactions beyond the static adiabatic and sudden approximations. It directly addresses the approach of Shoppa et al. (1993), who studied the incident energy dependence of screening but did not explicitly handle the tunneling process, instead assuming a constant energy shift inside the tunneling region. The results show that non-adiabatic effects further reduce the tunneling rate compared to the adiabatic approximation, which deepens the puzzle of why experimentally observed screening effects are significantly larger than adiabatic estimates for d+D and other systems such as d+³He. The authors suggest that additional ingredients, such as polarization of the colliding deuteron, may be needed to explain the experimental enhancements.
