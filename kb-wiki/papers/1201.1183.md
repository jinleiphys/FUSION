# Four-quark condensates in open-charm chiral QCD sum rules

> Recently, in Hilger et al. (2011) [1] QCD sum rules for chiral partners in the open-charm meson sector have been presented at nonzero baryon net density or temperature up to and including mass dimension 5. Referring to this, details concerning the cancelation of infrared divergences are presented and important technical and conceptional ingredients for an incorporation of four-quark condensates beyond factorization and of other mass dimension 6 condensates are collected.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in chiral partner QCD sum rules for open-charm mesons, infrared divergences cancel exactly when non-normal ordered condensates are introduced, and that up to mass dimension 5 only the chiral condensate mixes with other condensates, leaving the operator product expansion chirally odd; this cancellation is shown explicitly for the pseudoscalar-scalar difference and argued to hold for the vector-axialvector case by analogy.

## Method

The authors evaluate the operator product expansion (OPE) for the difference of chiral partner correlators (pseudoscalar minus scalar, and vector minus axialvector) for heavy-light quark mesons in the fixed-point gauge, using the background field method. They compute the relevant traces of quark propagators up to mass dimension 5, identify infrared-divergent integrals in the limit of vanishing light quark mass, and show that these divergences are absorbed by renormalizing the normal-ordered chiral condensate via the relation between normal-ordered and non-normal-ordered condensates. The analysis is then extended to discuss the structure of four-quark condensates (mass dimension 6) that would enter beyond the present truncation.

## Key numbers

* The infrared-divergent integral limit: \(\lim_{m_d \to 0} m_d \, {\rm I}_{3,0,2}(q^2,m_d^2,m_c^2) = \frac{1}{m_d} \frac{1}{q^2+m_c^2}\), showing a \(1/m_d\) singularity from vacuum D meson sum rules.
* The renormalization of the chiral condensate to order \(\alpha_s^1\) in \(\overline{\rm MS}\)-scheme: \(\langle\bar{q}q\rangle = \langle:\bar{q}q:\rangle + \frac{3}{4\pi^2}m_q^3 \left( \ln{\frac{\mu^2}{m_q^2}}+1 \right) - \frac{1}{12m_q} \langle \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} G^2 \rangle\), where \(\mu\) is the renormalization scale.
* The cancellation of the infrared divergence yields a finite result: \(\Pi^{{\rm P-S}(2)}_{\langle \bar d d \rangle}(q) \to \frac 12 \langle \bar d d \rangle \TrD \left[ S_c(q) \right] - \frac 16 \frac{m_c}{m_d} \frac{1}{q^2+m_c^2} \langle \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} G^2 \rangle\), where the divergent term cancels against the OPE contribution.

## Context

This work extends the chiral partner QCD sum rules for open-charm mesons presented by Hilger et al. (2011), which included terms up to mass dimension 5 at nonzero baryon density or temperature. The present paper provides the technical details of infrared divergence cancellation and collects the ingredients needed to incorporate four-quark condensates beyond the vacuum saturation (factorization) approximation, as well as other mass dimension 6 condensates. This enables future calculations that can determine the numerical impact of four-quark condensates on chiral partner sum rules, which are important for studying dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and its restoration in medium.
