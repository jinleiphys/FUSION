# Dilepton and Photon Emission Rates from a Hadronic Gas

> We analyze the dilepton and photon emission rates from a hadronic gas using chiral reduction formulas and a virial expansion. The emission rates are reduced to pertinent vacuum correlation functions, most of which can be assessed from experiment. Our results indicate that in the low mass region, the dilepton and photon rates are enhanced compared to most of the calculations using chiral Lagrangians. The enhancement is further increased through a finite pion chemical potential. An estimate of the emission rates is also made using Haag's expansion for the electromagnetic current. The relevance of these results to dilepton and photon emission rates in heavy-ion collisions is discussed.

## Key claim

The dilepton and photon emission rates from a hadronic gas at temperatures at or below the pion mass are enhanced in the low-mass region compared to most calculations using chiral Lagrangians, with the enhancement further increased by a finite pion chemical potential. This result is conditional on the use of chiral reduction formulas and a virial expansion truncated to leading order in pion density, where the dimensionless expansion parameter κ = n/(2m_π f_π^2) is approximately 0.3.

## Method

The authors used chiral reduction formulas to relate the forward scattering amplitudes of virtual and real photons off on-shell pions (γ* nπ → γ* nπ) to vacuum correlation functions constrained by experimental data from e+e- annihilation, tau decays, pion radiative decay, and two-photon fusion reactions. They performed a virial expansion of the thermal current-current correlation function in terms of pion states, keeping only the vacuum and one-pion terms (leading order in pion density), and extracted the absorptive parts from measured vector and axial spectral densities using Breit-Wigner fits with momentum-dependent widths.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: f_π = 93 MeV
- Pion mean free path in a pion gas at T ≤ m_π: 2-3 fm over a wide range of momenta
- Pion mean distance at T ≤ m_π: approximately 2 fm
- Dimensionless virial expansion parameter: κ = 0.3
- Axial Breit-Wigner fit parameters: f_A = 190 MeV, m_A = 1210 MeV, Γ_A(m_A^2) = 0.4 GeV
- Rho meson width parameterization: Γ_ρ(q^2) = 0.149 (m_ρ/√(q^2)) ((q^2 - 4m_π^2)/(m_ρ^2 - 4m_π^2))^(3/2) GeV, with γ = 0.4 in the form factor
- At T = 150 MeV, the dielectron rate enhancement above the a1 mass is about an order of magnitude compared to the Haag expansion
- For μ_π = 100 MeV, the virial expansion dilepton rate in the low mass region is enhanced by about a factor of five; the entire Haag rate increases by about a factor of 10
- For μ_π = 100 MeV at T = 100-150 MeV, photon rates are enhanced by about a factor of two throughout the energy spectrum
- The rho peak decreases by about 10% at T = 150 MeV due to negative contributions from Im Π_V terms

## Context

This work extends the model-independent approach of chiral reduction formulas (Master, 1990) to finite-temperature dilepton and photon emission, contrasting with earlier calculations using explicit reaction processes from chiral Lagrangians (Kapusta, 1989; Gale and Kapusta, 1987) and high-temperature QCD (Plasma, 1988). The results are directly relevant to interpreting the dielectron excess observed by CERES and the dimuon excess observed by HELIOS3 at CERN SPS, and motivate more detailed transport-equation calculations for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
