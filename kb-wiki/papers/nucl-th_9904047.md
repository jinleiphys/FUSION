# Electromagnetic probes of hot and dense hadronic matter

> The formalism of photon and dilepton productions in a thermal bath are discussed in the frame work of finite temperature field theory. The emission rate has been expressed in terms of the discontinuities or imaginary part of the photon self energy in the thermal bath. The photon and dilepton emissions have been computed by taking into account the in-medium modifications of the particles appearing in the internal loop of the self energy diagram using a phenomenological effective lagrangian approach. The results has been compared with the photon spectra measured by WA 98 collaboration at CERN SPS energies.

# Electromagnetic probes of hot and dense hadronic matter

## Key claim

The paper argues that photon and dilepton emission rates from hot hadronic gas, computed with in-medium modifications of hadronic masses and decay widths using a phenomenological effective Lagrangian approach, can describe the WA98 photon spectra from Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon, but the transverse momentum spectra do not allow a clear distinction between scenarios with and without in-medium mass modifications given the experimental uncertainties.

## Method

The authors compute photon and dilepton emission rates from a thermal hadronic bath using finite-temperature field theory, expressing the rates in terms of the imaginary part of the photon self-energy. They calculate the in-medium effective masses and decay widths of vector mesons (rho, omega) by evaluating the self-energy from a VNN (vector-nucleon-nucleon) Lagrangian in the mean-field approximation, including nucleon loop contributions and thermal effects via the imaginary-time formalism. The emission rates are then convoluted with a boost-invariant Bjorken hydrodynamical model for the space-time evolution of the system to obtain transverse momentum distributions, which are compared to WA98 data.

## Key numbers

- At T=180 MeV, the total photon emission rate with finite-temperature effects is a factor of approximately 3 higher than the rate calculated without medium effects.
- The omega mass drops at T=180 MeV by about 250 MeV from its free space value of 782 MeV.
- The rho meson mass decreases substantially due to interactions with nucleonic excitations, while it increases only marginally (approximately 10-15 MeV) due to rho-pion interactions.
- At the invariant mass M = m_rho* (the in-medium rho mass), inclusion of Bose Enhancement effects suppresses the dilepton yield by a factor of 3 at T=180 MeV and twice nuclear matter density.
- The initial temperatures used for the hadronic scenario are T_i=230 MeV (with medium effects) and T_i=270 MeV (without medium effects), both at tau_i=1 fm/c; the QGP scenario uses T_i=180 MeV at tau_i=1 fm/c with a critical temperature of 160 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature field theory formalism for electromagnetic probes developed by Weldon (1990) and others, applying it to compute photon and dilepton rates from a hadronic gas with in-medium modifications of vector meson properties. The results are compared to the earlier work of Li, Ko and Brown, who attributed low-mass dilepton enhancement solely to mass modification while neglecting Bose Enhancement effects. The paper shows that the WA98 photon data from Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon can be described by both the hadronic scenario and the QGP scenario, but the hadronic scenario dominates over the entire p_T range, and the data cannot distinguish between in-medium dropping mass and free mass scenarios, motivating future experiments with better statistics.
