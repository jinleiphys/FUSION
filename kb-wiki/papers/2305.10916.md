# Microscopic Encoding of Macroscopic Universality: Scaling Properties of Dirac Eigenspectra near QCD Chiral Phase Transition

> Macroscopic properties of the strong interaction near its chiral phase transition exhibit scaling behaviors, which are the same as those observed close to the magnetic transition in a 3-dimensional classical spin system with $O(4)$ symmetry. We show that the universal scaling properties of the chiral phase transition in Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) at the macroscale are, in fact, encoded within the microscopic energy levels of its fundamental constituents, the quarks. We establish a connection between the cumulants of the chiral order parameter, i.e., the chiral condensate, and the correlations among the energy levels of quarks, i.e., the eigenspectra of the massless QCD Dirac operator. This relation elucidates how the fluctuations of the chiral condensate arise from the correlations within the infrared part of the energy spectra of quarks, and naturally leads to a generalization of the Banks-Casher relation for the cumulants of the chiral condensate. Then, through (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD calculations with varying light quark masses near the QCD chiral transition, we demonstrate that the correlations among the infrared part of the Dirac eigenvalue spectra exhibit same universal scaling behaviors as expected of the cumulants of the chiral condensate. We find that these universal scaling behaviors extend up to the physical values of the up and down quark masses. Our study reveals how the hidden scaling features at the microscale give rise to the macroscopic universal properties of QCD.

## Key claim

The universal scaling properties of the QCD chiral phase transition, which at the macroscale are identical to those of a 3-dimensional O(4) spin system, are encoded within the correlations among the infrared eigenvalues of the massless Dirac operator; this is demonstrated for the O(2) universality class using staggered lattice QCD, and the scaling extends up to physical light quark masses (pion mass 140 MeV).

## Method

The authors derived a theoretical relation connecting the nth-order cumulant of the chiral condensate to n-point correlations of the Dirac eigenvalue spectral density, generalizing the Banks-Casher relation to higher cumulants. They then performed (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD calculations using highly improved staggered quarks on Nτ=8 lattices with light quark masses corresponding to pion masses of 55, 80, 110, and 140 MeV, computing the spectral correlation functions P_n(λ) for n=1,2,3 via Chebyshev filtering with stochastic estimation on approximately 3000 gauge configurations.

## Key numbers

- Pion masses studied: mπ ≈ 55, 80, 110, and 140 MeV, corresponding to light quark mass ratios ml = ms/160, ms/80, ms/40, and ms/27 respectively
- Temperature range: T = 135–176 MeV, with scaling observed in the window 135 MeV ≤ T ≤ 145 MeV
- Chiral transition temperature used for scaling: Tc = 144.2(6) MeV, with scaling parameter z0 = 1.83(9), taken from Clarke (2020)
- Critical exponents adopted: β = 0.349, δ = 4.78 (3-dimensional O(2) universality class)
- Lattice temporal extent Nτ = 8, spatial extents Nσ = (4–7)Nτ

## Context

This work extends the understanding of universal scaling in QCD beyond macroscopic observables by directly connecting it to the microscopic Dirac eigenvalue spectrum, generalizing the classic Banks-Casher relation (Banks and Casher, 1979) to higher-order cumulants. The lattice setup follows the HotQCD Collaboration approach (Bazavov et al., 2010–2018), and the scaling analysis uses the same gauge ensembles and O(2) scaling functions from Engels (2001) and Ejiri (2009) that were previously applied to macroscopic observables such as the static quark free energy by Clarke (2020). The results enable future studies using Dirac spectral correlations for precise determination of critical parameters and can be extended to random matrix theory frameworks (Gasser, 1987; Leutwyler, 1992; Verbaarschot, 2000) and to spin systems near criticality.
