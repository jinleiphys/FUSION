# New concept for pairing anti-halo effect as a localized wave packet of quasi-particle

> The pairing anti-halo effect is a phenomenon that a pairing correlation suppresses a divergence of nuclear radius, which happens for single-particle states with orbital angular momenta of $l$ = 0 and 1 in the limit of vanishing binding energy. While this effect has mainly been discussed in terms of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory, we here use a three-body model and provide its new intuitive concept as a localized wave packet for a quasi-particle, that is, a coherent superposition of a weakly bound and continuum wave functions due to a pairing interaction. We show that the one-particle density in the three-body model can be directly expressed with such quasi-particle wave functions, which have a close analog to wave functions in the HFB approximation.

## Key claim

The pairing anti-halo effect can be understood intuitively as the formation of a localized wave packet through a coherent superposition of a weakly bound state and continuum states due to the pairing interaction, rather than solely through the mathematical formalism of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory where the quasi-particle energy remains finite in the zero binding limit.

## Method

The authors employ a three-body model consisting of a core nucleus (²²O) and two valence neutrons, using a contact pairing interaction with a Woods-Saxon mean-field potential. They solve for the two-particle wave function in a model space including only s-wave states (one bound 2s₁/₂ state at -0.275 MeV and five discretized continuum states up to 10 MeV), then construct the one-particle density and decompose it into bound-bound, bound-continuum, and continuum-continuum components. They also define a "quasi-particle" wave function within the three-body model by expressing the one-particle density in a form analogous to HFB theory.

## Key numbers

- The 2s₁/₂ bound state energy is ε = -0.275 MeV for the ²⁴O system (²²O + n + n)
- The pairing interaction strength is g = 1374 MeV fm³ with a cutoff energy of E_cut = 10 MeV
- The box size for discretizing continuum states is R_box = 30 fm
- The Woods-Saxon potential has radius R = 3.5 fm, diffuseness a = 0.67 fm, and depth V₀ = -34.56 MeV
- The three-body ground state energy is E_g.s. = -2.46 MeV
- The root-mean-square radius for the correlated (paired) case is √⟨r²⟩ = 5.18 fm, compared to 8.83 fm for the uncorrelated case
- The lowest discretized continuum s-wave state is at ε_c = 0.51 MeV

## Context

This work extends the pairing anti-halo effect originally proposed by Bennaceur, Dobaczewski, and Ploszajczak (2000) using HFB theory, which showed that pairing correlations suppress the divergence of nuclear radius for weakly bound s-wave and p-wave states. The three-body model provides a complementary and more intuitive picture to the HFB approach, and the authors note that odd-even staggering in reaction cross sections observed by Takechi (2012, 2014) can be interpreted in terms of this effect. The study enables a clearer physical understanding of how pairing interactions localize halo wave functions in weakly bound even-even nuclei.
