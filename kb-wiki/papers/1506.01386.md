# Dispersion relation for hadronic light-by-light scattering: theoretical foundations

> In this paper we make a further step towards a dispersive description of the hadronic light-by-light (HLbL) tensor, which should ultimately lead to a data-driven evaluation of its contribution to $(g-2)_μ$. We first provide a Lorentz decomposition of the HLbL tensor performed according to the general recipe by Bardeen, Tung, and Tarrach, generalizing and extending our previous approach, which was constructed in terms of a basis of helicity amplitudes. Such a tensor decomposition has several advantages: the role of gauge invariance and crossing symmetry becomes fully transparent; the scalar coefficient functions are free of kinematic singularities and zeros, and thus fulfill a Mandelstam double-dispersive representation; and the explicit relation for the HLbL contribution to $(g-2)_μ$ in terms of the coefficient functions simplifies substantially. We demonstrate explicitly that the dispersive approach defines both the pion-pole and the pion-loop contribution unambiguously and in a model-independent way. The pion loop, dispersively defined as pion-box topology, is proven to coincide exactly with the one-loop scalar QED amplitude, multiplied by the appropriate pion vector form factors.

## Key claim

The paper provides a rigorous dispersive (data-driven) framework for the hadronic light-by-light (HLbL) contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, demonstrating that the pion-pole and pion-loop contributions can be defined unambiguously and model-independently within this approach, provided the scalar coefficient functions are free of kinematic singularities and zeros.

## Method

The authors perform a Lorentz decomposition of the HLbL tensor following the Bardeen-Tung-Tarrach (BTT) recipe, generalizing their previous helicity-amplitude basis. They construct 43 scalar coefficient functions that are free of kinematic singularities and zeros, enabling a Mandelstam double-dispersive representation. The dispersive approach is then applied to define the pion-pole and pion-box (pion-loop) contributions, with the pion-box proven to coincide exactly with the one-loop scalar QED amplitude multiplied by pion vector form factors.

## Key numbers

- The pion-loop contribution, dispersively defined as pion-box topology, is proven to coincide exactly with the one-loop scalar QED amplitude multiplied by the appropriate pion vector form factors (no explicit numerical value given for the contribution itself)
- The double-spectral integrals involve integration thresholds at \(4M_\pi^2\) (the two-pion threshold) for the Mandelstam variables \(s\), \(t\), and \(u\)
- The basis consists of 43 scalar coefficient functions \(\tilde\Pi_i\) derived from a redundant set of 54 Lorentz structures

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous dispersive approach (Colangelo et al., 2014) that was constructed in terms of helicity amplitudes, now reformulated in the BTT tensor basis to make gauge invariance and crossing symmetry fully transparent. The framework enables a data-driven evaluation of the HLbL contribution to \((g-2)_\mu\), providing a model-independent definition of the pion-pole and pion-loop contributions that had previously been subject to ambiguities in other approaches.
