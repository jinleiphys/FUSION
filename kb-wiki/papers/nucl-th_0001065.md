# Nuclear Saturation with QCD sum rules

> The scalar and vectorial self energies obtained through QCD sum rules are introduced in the Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) equations. This QHD and QCD mixing show us that the effect of the density on the coupling constants is very small.

## Key claim

By introducing scalar and vector self-energies derived from QCD sum rules into the simplest Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) model, the saturation properties of nuclear matter can be reproduced using coupling constants from the Bonn potential, which are more realistic than the QHD constants; the effect of density on the coupling constants is claimed to be very small.

## Method

The author takes the scalar and vector self-energies obtained from QCD sum rules at finite density (Eqs. self_qcd_s and self_qcd_v) and inserts them into the energy density expression of the Serot-Walecka mean-field theory (MFT) version of QHD, which includes nucleons coupled to sigma and omega mesons. The self-energies are expressed in terms of the nucleon sigma term sigma_B and the average light quark mass m_q, and are proportional to the baryon density rho_B. The binding energy per nucleon is then calculated as a function of Fermi momentum using the Bonn potential coupling constants, and the resulting saturation point is compared to the standard MFT result.

## Key numbers

* Saturation point with MFT coupling constants: Fermi momentum k_F = 1.12 fm^{-1} and energy per nucleon -15.75 MeV.
* Saturation point using Bonn coupling constants and QCD sum rule self-energies (with sigma_B = 49 MeV and m_q = 6.8 MeV): k_F = 1.42 fm^{-1} and energy per nucleon -15.75 MeV.
* Bonn potential coupling constant ratios: g_s^2/m_s^2 = 3.233 x 10^{-4} and g_v^2/m_v^2 = 4.104 x 10^{-4}.
* MFT coupling constant ratios: g_s^2/m_s^2 = 3.029 x 10^{-4} and g_v^2/m_v^2 = 2.222 x 10^{-4}.
* Empirical coupling constant ratios: g_s^2/m_s^2 = 3.402 x 10^{-4} and g_v^2/m_v^2 = 3.516 x 10^{-4}.
* Pion mass m_pi = 138 MeV, pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV, average quark mass m_q = 7 +/- 2 MeV, sigma term sigma_B = 45 +/- 10 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Cohen, Furnstahl, Griegel, and Jin (1995), who derived QCD sum rules for the nucleon self-energies in nuclear matter, by applying those self-energies to the QHD mean-field model of Serot and Walecka (1986). The paper addresses the known problem that the coupling constants required in the standard Walecka model to achieve nuclear saturation differ significantly from those extracted from the Bonn potential (Machleidt, Holinde, and Elster, 1987) or from empirical data. The result suggests that QCD sum rules can reconcile the QHD model with more realistic free-space coupling constants, and the author proposes that the method could be extended to high-density and high-temperature systems such as neutron stars.
