# Exploring effects of tensor force and its strength via neutron drops

> The tensor-force effects on the evolution of the spin-orbit splittings in the neutron drops are investigated within the framework of the relativistic Hartree-Fock theory. For fair comparisons on the pure mean-field level, the results of the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculation with the Bonn A interaction are adopted as meta-data. Through a quantitative analysis, we certify that the $π$-pseudovector ($π$-PV) coupling affects the evolutionary trend through the tensor force embedded. The strength of the tensor force is explored by enlarging the strength $f_π$ of the $π$-PV coupling. It is found that weakening the density dependence of $f_π$ is slightly better than enlarging it with a factor. We thus provide a semiquantitative support for the \textit{renormalization persistency} of the tensor force within the framework of density functional theory. This will serve as an important guidance for the further development of the relativistic effective interactions with particular focus on the tensor force.

## Key claim

The paper quantitatively certifies that the pi-pseudovector (pi-PV) coupling affects the evolutionary trend of spin-orbit splittings in neutron drops almost entirely through the embedded tensor force, and provides semiquantitative support for the "renormalization persistency" of the tensor force within density functional theory, with the caveat that weakening the density dependence of the pi-PV coupling strength is slightly better than enlarging it by a constant factor.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) calculations for neutron drops confined by a harmonic oscillator potential with hbar-omega = 10 MeV, using the effective interactions PKO1, PKO2, PKA1, and the relativistic mean-field (RMF) interaction PKDD. They quantitatively extracted the tensor-force contributions to the single-particle energies using the method developed in Wang (2018), and compared the spin-orbit splittings with meta-data from relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) calculations using the Bonn A interaction. The strength of the tensor force was explored by multiplying the pi-PV coupling strength f_pi(0) by a factor lambda (1.0 to 1.6) and by multiplying the density-dependence coefficient a_pi by a factor eta (0.1 to 0.7).

## Key numbers

* The rms deviation Delta of the slope of the SO splittings between neighboring (sub)shells for PKO1 with the original pi-PV coupling (lambda = 1.0) is 0.0909 MeV.
* The optimal scaling factor for the pi-PV coupling strength is lambda = 1.42, yielding a minimum rms deviation Delta = 0.0362 MeV.
* The optimal scaling factor for the density-dependence coefficient a_pi is eta = 0.33, yielding a minimum rms deviation Delta = 0.0350 MeV.
* The binding energies of neutron drops with PKO1 range from 60.679 MeV (N=4) to 1445.527 MeV (N=50), with radii from 2.527 fm to 3.895 fm.
* The tensor-force contributions to binding energies and radii are negligible, and almost vanish at spin-saturated neutron numbers N = 8, 20, and 40.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of tensor-force effects in neutron drops previously studied by Shen (2018) and Wang (2019), who used RBHF calculations as meta-data but did not quantitatively extract tensor-force contributions. The paper builds on the method for evaluating tensor-force contributions developed by Wang (2018) and the mechanism of tensor-force effects on spin-orbit splittings established by Otsuka (2005). The results provide guidance for developing relativistic effective interactions with improved tensor-force components, particularly regarding the density dependence of the tensor force and the concept of "renormalization persistency" proposed by Otsuka (2010).
