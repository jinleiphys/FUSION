# Two Lectures on the Phase Diagram of QCD

> The phase diagram of QCD at finite temperature and density is discussed. Large numbers of quark colors, $N_{\rm c} >> 1$, is used to explain generic features of the phase diagram. For temperatures below $ T \le 160$~MeV at zero baryon number density, the three dimensional string model is shown to describe the thermodynamics of QCD, and as well, the integrated spectrum of non-Goldstone mesons and glueballs. The lowest mass state in the spectrum of the open and closed string is treated separately due to the tachyon problem of string theory. This is with no undetermined free parameters. It is argued that there are at least three phases at zero baryon number density characterized by the $N_{\rm c}$ dependence of extensive thermodynamic quantities. It is also argued that the intermediate phase has restored chiral symmetry. At high baryon number density and low temperature, again there are three phases. A Quarkyonic phase, with energy density of order $N_{\rm c}$, is distinguished from its counterpart at low baryon density and temperature by its chiral properties.

# Two Lectures on the Phase Diagram of QCD

## Key claim

The paper argues that QCD at finite temperature and zero baryon density has at least three distinct phases characterized by the N_c dependence of thermodynamic quantities, with an intermediate phase (between approximately 160 MeV and 300 MeV) where chiral symmetry is restored but gluons remain confined inside glueballs, contrary to the conventional two-phase picture of hadron gas and quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The author uses the large N_c (number of quark colors) limit to classify phases by the scaling of thermodynamic quantities with N_c, and applies three-dimensional string theory to compute the density of states for high-mass mesons and glueballs, treating the lowest-mass states separately due to the tachyon problem. The string theory predictions for the cumulative mass spectra, trace anomaly, entropy density, pressure, and chiral condensate are compared to lattice QCD data and experimental particle data, with the string tension fixed from phenomenology at sqrt(sigma) = 440-485 MeV and no other free parameters.

## Key numbers

- The Hagedorn temperature is T_H = sqrt(3*sigma/2*pi) = 304-335 MeV, computed from the string tension range
- The deconfinement temperature for pure gauge theory is T_d/sqrt(sigma) = 0.646, corresponding to approximately 284-313 MeV
- The chiral crossover temperature from lattice QCD is T_chiral = 156.5 +/- 1.5 MeV
- The string tension is sqrt(sigma) = 440-485 MeV
- The Polyakov loop expectation value is approximately 0.1 at T ~ 165 MeV and approximately 0.8 at T ~ 300 MeV
- The averaged sigma term per quark from best fit to lattice chiral condensate data is bar(sigma) ~ 30 MeV, varied within 15-60 MeV for uncertainty

## Context

This work extends the large N_c arguments of 't Hooft (1974) and Witten (1979) to finite temperature QCD, and builds on Thorn's (1980) application of the large N_c limit to argue for a Hagedorn temperature. The three-dimensional string description of the hadron spectrum quantitatively reproduces lattice data from Meyer (2004, 2009) and Athenodorou (2020) for glueballs, and from the HotQCD collaboration (2014, 2018) for thermodynamic quantities. The intermediate phase with restored chiral symmetry provides a quantitative realization of speculations by Cohen and Glozman (2023). This work enables a unified description of the QCD phase diagram from hadron gas through the intermediate "Quark Spaghetti with Glueballs" (SQGP) phase to the quark-gluon plasma, and motivates further lattice studies of the confinement-deconfinement transition in the presence of dynamical quarks.
