# Vector interaction strength in Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models from hadron-quark phase diagrams

> We estimate the vector interaction strength of the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) parametrizations, assuming that its transition curves should be as close as possible of the recently studied RMF-PNJL hadron-quark phase diagrams. Such diagrams are obtained matching relativistic mean-field hadronic models, and the PNJL quark ones. By using this method we found for the magnitude of the vector interaction, often treated as a free parameter, a range of 7.66 GeV$^{-2}\lesssim G_V \lesssim 16.13$ GeV$^{-2}$, or equivalently, $1.52 \lesssim G_V/G_s \lesssim 3.2$, with $G_s$ being the scalar coupling constant of the model. These values are compatible but restricts the range of 4 GeV$^{-2}\lesssim G_V \lesssim 19$ GeV$^{-2}$, recently obtained from lattice QCD data through a different mean-field model approach.

## Key claim
The vector interaction strength \(G_V\) in PNJL models can be constrained by requiring that the PNJLv transition curves match the hadron-quark phase diagrams obtained from matching RMF hadronic models with PNJL quark models (with \(G_V=0\)), yielding a range of \(7.66\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2} \lesssim G_V \lesssim 16.13\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\) that is compatible with but narrower than the range \(4\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2} \lesssim G_V \lesssim 19\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\) from lattice QCD data.

## Method
The authors constructed a PNJL model with an added vector interaction term (PNJLv) and computed the grand thermodynamic potential and equations of motion for the quark condensate, Polyakov loop, and quark density. They varied \(G_V\) and compared the resulting phase transition curves (defined by the condition \(\rho_s/\rho_s^{\mathrm{vac}} = 0.70\)–\(0.73\) depending on the Polyakov potential) to the hadron-quark transition bands previously obtained by matching RMF hadronic models with PNJL models having \(G_V=0\). The overlap region between the PNJLv curves and the RMF-PNJL bands defined the allowed range of \(G_V\).

## Key numbers
- Total range of vector coupling: \(7.66\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2} \lesssim G_V \lesssim 16.13\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\).
- Equivalent ratio range: \(1.52 \lesssim G_V/G_s \lesssim 3.2\), where \(G_s = 5.04\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\).
- For individual Polyakov potentials, the minimum ratios \(G_V^{\mathrm{MIN}}/G_s\) are 1.60 (RRW06), 1.52 (RTW05), 1.60 (FUKU08), 1.54 (DS10); maximum ratios are 3.20 (RRW06), 2.74 (RTW05), 2.86 (FUKU08), 2.98 (DS10).
- Maximum temperatures for good overlap: \(T^{\mathrm{MAX}} \approx 90\) MeV (RRW06), 80 MeV (RTW05), 50 MeV (FUKU08), 80 MeV (DS10).
- Lattice QCD transition temperature at \(\mu=0\) used for calibration: \(T_c(\mu=0)=173\pm 8\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the RMF-PNJL matching approach of Lourenco et al. (2012) by introducing a vector interaction in the quark sector to mimic the repulsive short-range nucleon-nucleon interaction present in RMF hadronic models. The resulting \(G_V\) values are considerably larger than those from Fierz-transformed one-gluon exchange (\(G_V/G_s = 0.25\)–\(0.5\)) used in non-local PNJLv models by KasP (2011), but are compatible with the range extracted from lattice QCD susceptibilities by Ferroni (2012). The results enable improved modeling of proto-neutron stars at \(T \lesssim 50\) MeV and provide a benchmark for future studies of the QCD phase diagram at intermediate temperature and chemical potential.
