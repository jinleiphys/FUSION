# Collective flows of $α$-clustering $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ + $^{197}\mathrm{Au}$ by using different flow analysis methods

> Recently the ratio of triangular flow to the elliptic flow ($v_3/v_2$) of hadrons was proposed as a probe to detect the pattern of $α$-clustering $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ in $^{12}\mathrm{C}$+$^{197}\mathrm{Au}$ collisions at relativistic energy by a participant plane method (Phys. Rev. C 95, 064904 (2017)). In experimental event plane method, Q-cumulant method and two-particle correlation method with rapidity gap always were used for measurement of collective flow only by means of momentum space. By comparing collective flow through the different methods, the ratio of $v_3/v_2$ could be taken as an experimental probe to distinguish different $α$-clustering structure of $^{12}\mathrm{C}$.

## Key claim

The ratio of triangular flow to elliptic flow ($v_3/v_2$) of final charged hadrons, measured via the two-particle correlation method with a rapidity gap, can serve as an experimental probe to distinguish between different $\alpha$-clustering configurations (chain, triangle, or Woods-Saxon) of $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ in $^{12}\mathrm{C}$+$^{197}\mathrm{Au}$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV, provided that the results from this method are consistent with those from the participant plane method used in earlier theoretical work.

## Method

The authors used the multi-phase transport (AMPT) model to simulate $^{12}\mathrm{C}$+$^{197}\mathrm{Au}$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV, configuring the $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ nucleus with three different structures: three $\alpha$ clusters in a chain arrangement, three $\alpha$ clusters in a triangle arrangement, and a standard Woods-Saxon nucleon distribution. They then calculated the elliptic flow ($v_2$) and triangular flow ($v_3$) of final charged hadrons using four different flow analysis methods: the participant plane (PP) method, the event plane (EP) method, the Q-cumulant (QC) method, and the two-particle correlation (2PC) method with a rapidity gap of $2 < |\Delta\eta| < 5$.

## Key numbers

*   For the chain structure, the participant eccentricity $\epsilon_2\{PP\} = 0.5$ and $\epsilon_3\{PP\} = 0.16$; for the triangle structure, $\epsilon_2\{PP\} = 0.23$ and $\epsilon_3\{PP\} = 0.25$; for the Woods-Saxon structure, $\epsilon_2\{PP\} = 0.26$ and $\epsilon_3\{PP\} = 0.22$.
*   The $\alpha$ cluster parameters used were: for the triangle structure, average radial center $r_c = 1.8$ fm with width $\sigma_{r_c} = 0.1$ fm; for the chain structure, $r_c = 2.5$ fm with $\sigma_{r_c} = 0.1$ fm for two clusters, with the third at the center.
*   The ratio $v_3/v_2$ from the 2PC method was found to be closest to that from the PP method across the different $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ configurations.

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal by Zhang et al. (2017) that the $v_3/v_2$ ratio, calculated using the participant plane method, could probe $\alpha$-clustering in $^{12}\mathrm{C}$. The present study tests whether this probe remains viable when using experimental flow analysis methods (event plane, Q-cumulant, two-particle correlation) that rely only on momentum-space information, rather than the initial coordinate-space information required by the participant plane method. The results suggest that the two-particle correlation method with a rapidity gap is the most suitable experimental technique for this purpose, enabling future experimental searches for $\alpha$-clustering nuclear structure in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
