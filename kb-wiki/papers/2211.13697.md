# Helicity conservation in perfect electromagnetic and chiral fluids

> We derive the total helicity conservation law for a perfect electromagnetic relativistic fluid. As the conservation equation contains the derivative of the magnetic helicity, it can be reshaped as having the same form as the chiral anomaly equation if the fluid is isentropic. We also take the non-relativistic limit of the helicity conservation law, and check the agreement with the Abanov-Wiegmann equation at zero temperature, but we provide further corrections in the more general case. We then consider chiral fluids, when the chiral anomaly equation has to be incorporated in the hydrodynamical equations, together with other chiral transport effects which exist in the presence of a chiral imbalance. We finally study how the chiral imbalance modifies the helicity conservation law.

## Key claim

The paper derives a total helicity conservation law for perfect relativistic electromagnetic fluids, showing that in the isentropic limit the conservation equation takes the same mathematical form as the chiral anomaly equation even though it is a purely classical effect; however, the Abanov-Wiegmann equation from prior work is only recovered at zero temperature, and the authors provide further corrections for the general case.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic hydrodynamic equations for a perfect charged fluid, including the charge current and energy-momentum tensor conservation laws, and derive the Carter-Lichnerowicz equation. They then define a generalized Chern-Simons current in terms of an enthalpy vorticity tensor and the electromagnetic field, compute its divergence, and use the Carter-Lichnerowicz equation to obtain the helicity conservation law. The nonrelativistic limit is taken by restoring the speed of light constant and expanding to order v^2/c^2. For chiral fluids, the chiral anomaly equation and additional chiral transport coefficients (chiral vortical, chiral magnetic, axial vortical, and chiral separation effects) are incorporated into the hydrodynamic description, and the helicity evolution is derived from the equations of motion for the vorticity and magnetic field.

## Key numbers

* The chiral anomaly coefficient is C = 1/(2π^2).
* The nonrelativistic expansion is carried out to order v^2/c^2, with the leading term of the helicity density ρ_A = (m c^2/2)[m v·(∇×v) + 2(v/c)·B] plus corrections of order v/c.
* The relativistic enthalpy is expressed as h = m c^2 + h_nr = m c^2 + μ_nr + T s̄, where the rest energy m c^2 is separated from the nonrelativistic enthalpy.

## Context

This work extends the helicity conservation studies of Abanov and Wiegmann (2021, 2022) from barotropic nonrelativistic fluids to general relativistic perfect fluids, and generalizes the relativistic treatment of Abanov (2022) by providing the genuine conservation law for generic isentropic fluids rather than only at zero temperature. The paper also incorporates the chiral anomaly into the helicity conservation framework, building on the chiral hydrodynamics developed by Son and Surowka (2009) and subsequent work on chiral transport effects. The results enable future studies of helicity transfer between fluid and electromagnetic sectors in cosmological scenarios and relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where large vorticity and magnetic fields are generated.
