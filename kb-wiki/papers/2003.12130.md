# Charged multi-hadron systems in lattice QCD+QED

> Systems with the quantum numbers of up to twelve charged and neutral pseudoscalar mesons, as well as one-, two-, and three-nucleon systems, are studied using dynamical lattice quantum chromodynamics and quantum electrodynamics (QCD+QED) calculations and effective field theory. QED effects on hadronic interactions are determined by comparing systems of charged and neutral hadrons after tuning the quark masses to remove strong isospin breaking effects. A non-relativistic effective field theory, which perturbatively includes finite-volume Coulomb effects, is analyzed for systems of multiple charged hadrons and found to accurately reproduce the lattice QCD+QED results. QED effects on charged multi-hadron systems beyond Coulomb photon exchange are determined by comparing the two- and three-body interaction parameters extracted from the lattice QCD+QED results for charged and neutral multi-hadron systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that finite-volume Coulomb effects in lattice QCD+QED calculations of charged multi-hadron systems can be treated perturbatively over a wider range of volumes than previously suggested, specifically when the spatial extent \(L\) satisfies \(\alpha M L / (4\pi) \ll 1\) rather than the more restrictive condition \(L \ll 1/(\alpha M)\) proposed by Beane (2014).

## Method

The authors performed dynamical lattice QCD+QED calculations using the QED\(_L\) formalism on two lattice volumes (\(32^3 \times 64\) and \(48^3 \times 96\)) with a larger-than-physical fine-structure constant \(\alpha \simeq 0.1\) and quark masses tuned to an approximate SU(3) flavor-symmetric point to isolate pure QED effects. They extracted finite-volume energy levels for systems of up to twelve charged pions (\(\pi^+\)) and neutral kaons (\(\overline{K}^0\)), as well as one-, two-, and three-nucleon systems, and compared these results to a non-relativistic effective field theory (NRQED\(_L\)) that includes perturbative finite-volume Coulomb effects, allowing extraction of two-body scattering lengths and three-body interaction parameters.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing \(a = 0.068(2)\ \text{fm}\) obtained from the gauge field ensemble used in the calculations.
- Fine-structure constant set to \(\alpha \simeq 0.1\) (larger than physical) to enhance QED effects.
- Single \(\pi^+\) energy on \(L/a = 48\) volume: \(a E_{1\pi^+} = 0.15082(29)\); on \(L/a = 32\) volume: \(a E_{1\pi^+} = 0.14921(49)\).
- Single \(\overline{K}^0\) energy on \(L/a = 48\) volume: \(a E_{1\overline{K}^0} = 0.13921(30)\); on \(L/a = 32\) volume: \(a E_{1\overline{K}^0} = 0.13910(45)\).
- Twelve-\(\pi^+\) system energy on \(L/a = 48\) volume: \(a E_{12\pi^+} = 2.02(10)\); on \(L/a = 32\) volume: \(a E_{12\pi^+} = 3.20(20)\).
- Twelve-\(\overline{K}^0\) system energy on \(L/a = 48\) volume: \(a E_{12\overline{K}^0} = 2.002(79)\); on \(L/a = 32\) volume: \(a E_{12\overline{K}^0} = 2.7(1.1)\).

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice QCD+QED studies of single-hadron systems by Borsanyi (2015) and Horsley (2015) to multi-hadron systems with up to twelve mesons and up to three nucleons, and provides the first systematic comparison of lattice QCD+QED results with a non-relativistic effective field theory for charged multi-hadron systems. The findings enable future lattice calculations of isospin-breaking effects in nucleon-nucleon scattering and multi-nucleon systems, and refine the practical window for perturbative treatment of Coulomb effects in finite-volume calculations beyond the criteria suggested by Beane (2014).
