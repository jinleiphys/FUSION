# Parameter-free predictions for the collective deformation variables beta and gamma within the pseudo-SU(3) scheme

> The consequences of the short range nature of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, which forces the spatial part of the nuclear wave function to be as symmetric as possible, on the pseudo-SU(3) scheme are examined through a study of the collective deformation parameters beta and gamma in the rare earth region. It turns out that beyond the middle of each harmonic oscillator shell possessing an SU(3) subalgebra, the highest weight irreducible representation (the hw irrep) of SU(3) has to be used, instead of the irrep with the highest eigenvalue of the second order Casimir operator of SU(3) (the hC irrep), while in the first half of each shell the two choices are identical. The choice of the hw irrep predicts a transition from prolate to oblate shapes just below the upper end of the rare earth region, between the neutron numbers N=114 and 116 in the W, Os, and Pt series of isotopes, in agreement with available experimental information, while the choice of the hC irrep leads to a prolate to oblate transition in the middle of the shell, which is not seen experimentally. The prolate over oblate dominance in the ground states of even-even nuclei is obtained as a by-product.

## Key claim
The short-range nature of the nucleon-nucleon interaction forces the use of the highest weight (hw) irreducible representation of SU(3) beyond the middle of each harmonic oscillator shell, rather than the irrep with the highest eigenvalue of the second-order Casimir operator (hC); this choice predicts a prolate-to-oblate shape transition near neutron numbers N=114–116 in W, Os, and Pt isotopes, in agreement with experiment, whereas the hC choice incorrectly places the transition at midshell.

## Method
The authors distributed valence protons and neutrons into pseudo-SU(3) shells and intruder orbitals for rare-earth nuclei (Z=50–82, N=82–126) using Nilsson diagrams and deformations from the D1S Gogny interaction. They computed the SU(3) irreps (λ,μ) for protons and neutrons from the hw and hC choices, then formed the total “stretched” irrep (λ_p+λ_n, μ_p+μ_n). The collective deformation parameters β and γ were obtained by mapping the SU(3) Casimir invariants onto the Bohr–Mottelson collective model variables via Eqs. (1) and (2), with β rescaled by A/50 to account for shell size.

## Key numbers
- For ¹⁵⁸Gd (Z=64, N=94), the predicted β=0.356 from D1S Gogny is close to the experimental value of 0.351 from Pritychenko; the total SU(3) irrep is (28,8) for both hw and hC choices.
- For ¹⁷⁶Yb (Z=70, N=106), the hw choice gives a prolate irrep (30,8) while the hC choice gives an oblate irrep (10,30); the experimental β is 0.3014.
- The hw predictions cross the prolate-to-oblate border (γ=30°) at neutron numbers N=114–116 for Hf, W, Os, and Pt isotopic chains.
- The hw and hC predictions for γ converge again at N=122 for most chains, except Yb, because the hw and hC proton irreps differ in Yb and Hf chains.
- The constant r₀ used in the β formula is 0.87, taken from Castaños and Stone.

## Context
This work extends the particle-hole symmetry breaking previously demonstrated in the proxy-SU(3) scheme (Bonatsos et al., 2017) to the pseudo-SU(3) framework, which recovers SU(3) symmetry via a unitary transformation of normal-parity orbitals (Quesne, Velazquez). It resolves the long-standing question of prolate-over-oblate dominance in even-even nuclei (Hamamoto) and predicts a prolate-to-oblate shape transition near N=114–116, consistent with experimental data (Namenson, Alkhomashi, Wheldon, Podolyak, Linnemann) and earlier proxy-SU(3) results (Bonatsos et al., 2017, 2018). The work enables application of pseudo-SU(3) to the upper half of nuclear shells, where phenomena like the prolate-to-oblate transition occur, and invites comparison with the quasi-SU(3) scheme (Zuker et al., 1995, 1999).
