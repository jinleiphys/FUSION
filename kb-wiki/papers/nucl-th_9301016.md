# Shadowing in Deuterium

> We calculate nuclear shadowing in lepton-deuteron deep inelastic scattering, which arises from the double scattering of the virtual photon from both nucleons in the deuteron. The total correction to the deuteron structure function is found to be less than about 1\% at small Bjorken-x, but dependent on the model deuteron wavefunction. The resulting increase in the corrected neutron structure function is between 1-2\% for x near 0.004, which leads to a 4-10\% decrease in the value of the Gottfried sum obtained recently by the New Muon Collaboration.

## Key claim

The total shadowing correction to the deuteron structure function is less than about 1% at small Bjorken-x, but this result depends on the model deuteron wavefunction used, with realistic wavefunctions producing smaller corrections than earlier estimates.

## Method

The authors calculated nuclear shadowing in lepton-deuteron deep inelastic scattering from the double scattering mechanism where the virtual photon interacts with both nucleons in the deuteron. They combined three contributions: vector meson dominance (VMD) at low Q², Pomeron exchange at higher masses, and meson exchange contributions, using several realistic deuteron wavefunctions (Paris, Bonn, Bochum) and varying the nucleon-meson form factor cutoffs.

## Key numbers

- Total correction to the deuteron structure function is less than about 1% at small x, with the shadowing correction amounting to (0.5-1.0%, 0.4-0.8%, 0.0-0.3%) of the total F₂D at x = (0.004, 0.01, 0.1) respectively
- The resulting increase in the corrected neutron structure function is 1-2% for x near 0.004
- The correction leads to a 4-10% decrease in the value of the Gottfried sum obtained by the New Muon Collaboration, with the overall correction to the NMC value for S_G(0,1) between -0.010 and -0.026
- At Q² = 4 GeV², the VMD contribution from the ρ⁰ meson accounts for approximately 80% of the VMD shadowing correction
- The magnitude of δF₂D(x) is about 4 times smaller than obtained in reference Zoller (1992) and about 2 times smaller compared with the result of Badelek et al. (1992)
- Antishadowing is less than 0.2% of F₂D at x ≈ 0.2

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of deuteron shadowing by Zoller (1992) and Badelek et al. (1992), but finds significantly smaller corrections due to the inclusion of meson exchange contributions (which produce antishadowing at small x) and the use of realistic deuteron wavefunctions. The results are directly relevant to the extraction of the neutron structure function from deuteron data by the New Muon Collaboration (1990, 1992) and the determination of the Gottfried sum rule. The paper enables more precise extraction of neutron structure functions and resolves discrepancies between earlier theoretical estimates of shadowing in deuterium.
