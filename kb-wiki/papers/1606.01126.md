# Superfluid hydrodynamics in the inner crust of neutron stars

> The inner crust of neutron stars is supposed to be inhomogeneous and composed of dense structures (clusters) that are immersed in a dilute gas of unbound neutrons. Here we consider spherical clusters forming a BCC crystal and cylindrical rods arranged in a hexagonal lattice. We study the relative motion of these dense structures and the neutron gas using superfluid hydrodynamics. Within this approach, which relies on the assumption that Cooper pairs are small compared to the crystalline structures, we find that the entrainment of neutrons by the clusters is very weak since neutrons of the gas can flow through the clusters. Consequently, we obtain a low effective mass of the clusters and a superfluid density that is even higher than the density of unbound neutrons. Consequences for the constraints from glitch observations are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors find that, under the assumption of strong pairing where Cooper pairs are small compared to the crystalline structures, the entrainment of neutrons by the clusters is very weak because neutrons of the gas can flow through the clusters, leading to a superfluid density that is even higher than the density of unbound neutrons.

## Method

The authors solve the Laplace equation for the superfluid velocity potential within a primitive cell of a BCC lattice of spherical clusters and a hexagonal lattice of cylindrical rods, using boundary conditions that allow neutron flow across the cluster surface. The numerical solution is obtained by discretizing the cell with a regular mesh and solving the resulting linear system via LU decomposition, with input densities and geometries taken from Extended Thomas-Fermi calculations using the SLy4 Skyrme energy-density functional.

## Key numbers

- The effective number of neutrons entrained by a spherical cluster is given by \(N_{\text{eff}} = N_{\text{r-cluster}} \frac{(1-\gamma)^2}{1+2\gamma}\), where \(\gamma = n_{n,\Out}/n_{n,\In}\) is the ratio of neutron densities in the gas and cluster
- For a cylindrical rod moving perpendicular to its axis, the effective number of entrained neutrons per unit length is \(N_{\text{eff}} = N_{\text{r-cluster}} \frac{(1-\gamma)^2}{1+\gamma}\)
- For the 1D plate (lasagne) phase, the \(zz\) component of the bound neutron density matrix is \(n^{b}_{n,zz} = \bar{n}_n \frac{(1-\gamma)^2 L_\Out L_\In}{(L_\Out+\gamma L_\In)(L_\In+\gamma L_\Out)}\), which is much smaller than \(\bar{n}_n\) (ratio \(\lesssim 0.03\)) because \(\gamma \gtrsim 0.7\) in the deepest crust layers

## Context

This work extends the superfluid hydrodynamics approach of Magierski and Bulgac (2004) from isolated clusters to periodic crystalline lattices, and contrasts with the band-structure theory of Chamel and co-workers (2005, 2006, 2012) which predicts strong entrainment and a reduced superfluid density. The results enable improved constraints on glitch observations in neutron stars, particularly for the Vela pulsar, by providing microscopic input for the effective masses of clusters and superfluid densities in the inner crust.
