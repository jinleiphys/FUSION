# Neutrino induced transitions between the ground states of the A=12 triad

> Neutrino induced reactions on $^{12}$C, an ingredient of liquid scintillators, have been studied in several experiments. We show that for currently available neutrino energies, $E_ν \le$ 300 MeV, calculated exclusive cross sections $^{12}$C$_{gs}(ν,l)$$^{12}$N$_{gs}$ for both muon and electron neutrinos are essentially model independent, provided the calculations simultaneously describe the rates of several other reactions involving the same states or their isobar analogs. The calculations agree well with the measured cross sections, which can be therefore used to check the normalization of the incident neutrino spectrum and the efficiency of the detector.

# Neutrino induced transitions between the ground states of the A=12 triad

## Key claim

The exclusive charged-current cross sections for neutrino-induced transitions between the ground states of the A=12 triad (12C to 12N or 12B) are essentially model independent for neutrino energies up to 300 MeV, provided the calculations simultaneously reproduce the rates of several other reactions involving the same states or their isobar analogs. The agreement between all models and experimental data is good, meaning these exclusive cross sections can serve as a reliable monitor of neutrino flux normalization and detector efficiency, and the known discrepancy between measured and calculated inclusive cross sections on 12C must have a source other than flux normalization.

## Method

The authors calculated the exclusive cross sections 12C(νe,e−)12Ngs and 12C(νμ,μ−)12Ngs using three distinct theoretical approaches: a restricted p-shell shell model with one-body transition densities fitted to beta decay, M1 width, electron scattering form factor, and muon capture data; a continuum Random Phase Approximation (RPA) with a global quenching factor of 0.258 applied to all rates; and the elementary-particle treatment (EPT) extended to higher neutrino energies by including lepton mass and the induced pseudoscalar term. The calculations were constrained by requiring simultaneous description of the beta+ decay of 12N, the M1 radiative width of the 15.11 MeV state in 12C, the electron scattering form factor for that state, and the muon capture rate to the ground state of 12B.

## Key numbers

- Experimental exclusive cross section for 12C(νe,e−)12Ngs from decay-at-rest neutrinos: 8.9 ± 0.6 ± 0.75 × 10−42 cm2 (Karmen) and 9.1 ± 0.4 ± 0.9 × 10−42 cm2 (LSND)
- Experimental exclusive cross section for 12C(νμ,μ−)12Ngs from decay-in-flight neutrinos: 64 ± 10 ± 10 × 10−42 cm2 (LSND)
- Calculated exclusive cross section for 12C(νe,e−)12Ngs using the authors' fitted one-body densities: 9.1 ± 0.1 × 10−42 cm2
- Calculated exclusive cross section for 12C(νμ,μ−)12Ngs using the authors' fitted one-body densities: 63.5 ± 5 × 10−42 cm2
- Experimental muon capture rate to the 1+ ground state of 12B: 6200 ± 200 s−1
- RPA quenching factor applied to all rates: 0.258
- Average momentum transfer for νe reaction: about 50 MeV; for νμ reaction: about 150 MeV

## Context

This work extends the elementary-particle treatment of Fukugita, Kohyama, and Kubodera (1988) to higher neutrino energies relevant to the LSND decay-in-flight muon neutrinos, and addresses a disturbing discrepancy between calculations and the LSND measurement of the inclusive reaction 12C(νμ,μ)12N*. The authors show that the exclusive cross sections, unlike the inclusive ones, are robustly predicted across multiple approaches (shell model, RPA, EPT) and agree with experiment, enabling their use as a flux and efficiency monitor. The paper enables future experiments to use the exclusive channel as a calibration tool and redirects the search for the source of the inclusive cross section discrepancy away from flux normalization issues.
