# Fractional Energy Loss and Centrality Scaling

> The phenomenon of centrality scaling in the high-\pt spectra of $π^0$ produced in Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt s=200$ GeV is examined in the framework of relating fractional energy loss to fractional centrality increase. A new scaling behavior is found where the scaling variable is given a power-law dependence on $N_{\rm part}$. The exponent $γ$ specifies the fractional proportionality relationship between energy loss and centrality, and is a phenomenologically determined number that characterizes the nuclear suppression effect. The implication on the parton energy loss in the context of recombination is discussed.

## Key claim
The paper finds that the fractional energy loss of pions in Au-Au collisions is proportional to the fractional change in centrality (number of participants), characterized by a single exponent γ = 0.077, but this scaling may break into two regimes in the normalization factor, potentially indicating a threshold for quark-gluon plasma formation.

## Method
The authors analyzed finalized PHENIX π⁰ data at √s = 200 GeV for nine centrality bins, constructing a scaling function Φ(z) by applying horizontal shifts J(N) and vertical shifts B(N) to the inclusive p_T spectra in a log-log plot. They derived the scaling variable z = p_T J(N) and enforced a fractional proportionality relation δx/x = γ δN/N, which forces J(N) ∝ N^γ. The exponent γ was extracted from a straight-line fit to J(N) on a log-log plot.

## Key numbers
- γ = 0.077, the exponent characterizing the fractional proportionality between energy loss and centrality increase, extracted from the scaling factor J(N) for N > 10.
- The scaling function Φ(z) is parametrized as Φ(z) = 150 (z² + 1.05)^(-4.18) (1 + 7e^(-4z)).
- The average scaling variable ⟨z⟩ = 0.42.
- The normalization factor B(N) shows two scaling regimes: β₁ = 0.744 for N < 38 and β₂ = 1.292 for N > 38, with N₁ = 1610 and N₂ = 325.
- The inclusive cross section of pions at p_T ≈ 3-4 GeV/c decreases by a factor of 4-5 when varying from most peripheral to most central collisions.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous scaling analysis (Hwa and Yang, 2003) using preliminary PHENIX data, now with finalized tabulated data, and provides a physically motivated interpretation of the scaling variable. The fractional energy loss result (δx/x independent of x) differs from pQCD predictions (Baier et al.) where Δp_T ∝ p_T^(1/2), and from the assumption of constant dE/dL for partons. The scaling behavior supports a recombination model of hadronization (Hwa and Yang, 2003; Fries et al.; Greco et al.; Lin and Molnar) where hard and soft partons recombine without separate fragmentation, and the same γ applies to quarks just before hadronization. The two scaling regimes in B(N) may offer a signature for quark-gluon plasma formation.
