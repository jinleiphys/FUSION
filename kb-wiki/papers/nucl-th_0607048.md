# Connecting the X(5)-$β^2$, X(5)-$β^4$, and X(3) models to the shape/phase transition region of the interacting boson model

> The parameter independent (up to overall scale factors) predictions of the X(5)-$β^2$, X(5)-$β^4$, and X(3) models, which are variants of the X(5) critical point symmetry developed within the framework of the geometric collective model, are compared to two-parameter calculations in the framework of the interacting boson approximation (IBA) model. The results show that these geometric models coincide with IBA parameters consistent with the phase/shape transition region of the IBA for boson numbers of physical interest (close to 10). Nuclei within the rare-earth region and select Os and Pt isotopes are identified as good examples of X(3), X(5)-$β^2$, and X(5)-$β^4$ behavior.

# Connecting the X(5)-β², X(5)-β⁴, and X(3) models to the shape/phase transition region of the interacting boson model

## Key claim

The parameter-free predictions of the X(3) and X(5)-β² geometric models coincide with specific points within the narrow shape/phase transition region of the interacting boson approximation (IBA) model for boson numbers near 10, while X(5)-β⁴ and X(5) do not match IBA predictions inside that region for any parameter values. The correspondence is limited to energy ratios for X(3) and extends to some B(E2) ratios for X(5)-β², but no single geometric model reproduces the full set of IBA observables exactly.

## Method

The authors compared the parameter-independent (up to overall scale) predictions of the X(3), X(5)-β², and X(5)-β⁴ geometric models to two-parameter IBA calculations using the Hamiltonian H(ζ,χ) = c[(1-ζ)n̂_d - (ζ/4N_B) Q̂^χ·Q̂^χ] for boson number N_B=10. They mapped the loci of IBA parameters (ζ,χ) that reproduce the yrast energy ratio R₄/₂ of each geometric model, then compared energy ratios (R₄/₂, E(0₂⁺)/E(2₁⁺), E(2₀₂⁺⁺)/E(2₁⁺)) and B(E2) transition strengths across the IBA symmetry triangle, focusing on the phase coexistence region bounded by ζ* (left border, nearly coincident with ζ_crit) and ζ** (right border).

## Key numbers

- X(3) model yrast energy ratio R₄/₂ = 2.44; IBA at ζ_crit = 0.511 and χ = -1.32 gives R₄/₂ = 2.44, E(0₂⁺)/E(2₁⁺) = 2.65 (X(3): 2.87), and E(2₀₂⁺⁺)/E(2₁⁺) = 4.02 (X(3): 4.83)
- X(5)-β² model R₄/₂ = 2.65; IBA at ζ** = 0.54 and χ = -1.32 gives R₄/₂ = 2.68, E(0₂⁺)/E(2₁⁺) = 3.41 (X(5)-β²: 3.56), and E(2₀₂⁺⁺)/E(2₁⁺) = 5.23 (X(5)-β²: 4.56)
- X(5)-β² B(E2) ratios: B₄/₂ = 1.77 and B₀/₂ = 1.21; IBA at ζ_crit and χ = -1.32 gives B₄/₂ = 1.73 and B₀/₂ = 1.23
- X(5)-β⁴ model R₄/₂ = 2.77; X(5) model R₄/₂ = 2.90
- For χ = -1.32 and N_B = 10: ζ* = 0.507, ζ_crit = 0.511, ζ** = 0.54
- ζ_crit formula for χ = -√7/2: ζ_crit = 16N_B/(34N_B - 27)
- ζ** formula: ζ** = 4N_B/(8N_B + χ² - 8)

## Context

This work extends earlier findings (McCutchan 2003) that X(5) predictions cannot be exactly reproduced by any point in the two-parameter IBA space, with best agreement outside the phase transition region. The study connects the geometric collective model variants X(3) (Bonatsos 2004), X(5)-β², and X(5)-β⁴ (Bonatsos 2005) to the IBA shape/phase transition region previously mapped by Gilmore (1979), Dieperink (1980), and Iachello, Zamfir, and collaborators. The identified candidate nuclei (¹⁷²Os, ¹⁷⁴Os, ¹⁷⁶Os, ¹⁸⁶Pt, ¹⁴⁶Ce, ¹⁵⁸Er) provide experimental tests of these geometric models and suggest that neighboring even-even nuclei can exhibit the predicted X(3) → X(5)-β² → X(5)-β⁴ evolution. The authors propose extending similar comparisons to the E(5) critical point symmetry and related models (Arias 2003, Bonatsos 2004) as well as triaxial Z(5) and Z(4) models.
