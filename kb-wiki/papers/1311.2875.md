# Effect of high frequency modes of medium on an open quantum system

> We present a method to calculate the real time effective propagator of a generic open quantum system, immersed in a medium using a wave function based framework. The medium is characterised by a set of harmonic oscillators having a continuous span of frequencies. This technique has been applied to the Caldeira-Leggett model showing that high frequency modes of the medium do not contribute towards decay of the population of states of the open system. In fact, they cause a Rabi type oscillation. Moreover, our wave function based approach provides an excellent alternative to conventional formalisms involving the density matrix.

# Effect of high frequency modes of medium on an open quantum system

## Key claim
High frequency modes of a medium do not contribute to the decay of population of states of an open quantum system; instead, they cause a Rabi type oscillation in the population dynamics. This result is derived under the condition that the test system is observed at a time scale much higher than the inherent time scale of the medium, allowing a Markovian treatment, and that the lower cutoff of the high frequency modes is set far above the characteristic frequency of the test oscillator.

## Method
The authors construct a real-time effective propagator for a generic open quantum system using a wave function based path integral framework, applied to the Caldeira-Leggett model with a bilinear system-bath interaction. They trace over the degrees of freedom of the medium by imposing periodic boundary conditions on the high frequency oscillator modes, separating the propagator into a product of a medium-dependent exponential factor and the free propagator of the test system. The parameter α encoding the effect of high frequency modes is then calculated from the spectral function for Ohmic and non-Ohmic baths.

## Key numbers
- For an Ohmic bath with spectral function ρ(ω) = η ω, the parameter α = η ln(Ω/Ω_c), where Ω is the upper frequency cutoff and Ω_c is the lower bound of high frequency modes, much larger than the characteristic frequency of the test oscillator.
- For non-Ohmic baths with ρ(ω) ∝ ω^n, the parameter α = ∫_{Ω_c}^{Ω} η ω^{n-2} dω.
- The effective propagator contains a factor exp[∑_j λ² (x_f − x_i)² / (4m ω_j³)] from the high frequency modes, with terms of O(1/ω_j⁴) and higher neglected for sufficiently large ω_j.

## Context
This work extends the Caldeira-Leggett model (Caldeira and Leggett, 1982) by providing a wave function based alternative to the conventional density matrix formalism for open quantum systems. The method avoids the computational demands of density matrix approaches (requiring N² information versus N for wave functions) and the stringent approximations often needed in Heisenberg equation solutions. The technique is applicable to systems such as heavy quark bound states in quark-gluon plasma (Borghini, 2011; Akamatsu, 2011; Young, 2010) and can be generalized to include all medium modes, with alternative treatments given by Dalibard (1992) and Breuer (1999) that handle all frequencies without distinguishing low and high modes.
