# Charge symmetry violation in the doubly charmed cascade masses

> We investigate the electromagnetic contribution to the charge symmetry breaking in the $Ξ_{cc}$ baryon masses using a subtracted dispersion relation based on the Cottingham formula, following the formalism developed in an analysis of the octet baryon mass differences. In the absence of experimental information on the structure of charmed baryons, we use parameters for the electromagnetic structure of the $Ξ_{cc}$ and the difference in its charge states obtained from lattice QCD and estimates of SU(4) symmetry breaking. We report a conservative estimate for the mass splitting of the doubly charmed cascades to be 8 $\pm$ 9 MeV. While a smaller isospin splitting is compatible with this result, surprisingly it does not preclude the large splitting reported by the SELEX Collaboration. We identify those quantities which could be determined from lattice QCD and which would reduce the quoted theoretical uncertainty.

# Charge symmetry violation in the doubly charmed cascade masses

## Key claim
The electromagnetic contribution to the mass splitting between the $\Xi_{cc}^{++}$ and $\Xi_{cc}^{+}$ baryons is estimated to be 8 ± 9 MeV, which is compatible with a small isospin splitting but does not preclude the large splittings of 17 MeV and 21 MeV reported by the SELEX Collaboration, given the current theoretical uncertainties.

## Method
The authors used a subtracted dispersion relation based on the Cottingham formula, following the formalism developed by Erben et al. (2014) for octet baryon mass differences. The electromagnetic self-energy was decomposed into elastic, inelastic, subtraction, and counter-term contributions. Since no experimental data exist on the electromagnetic structure of charmed baryons, the authors used lattice QCD results for charge radii and magnetic moments from Can et al. (2013), combined with estimates of SU(4) symmetry breaking and quark-diquark model predictions for the resonance spectrum.

## Key numbers
- Total electromagnetic mass splitting $\delta M_{\Xi_{cc}^{++}}^{\gamma} - \delta M_{\Xi_{cc}^{+}}^{\gamma} = 8 \pm 9$ MeV
- Elastic contribution to the mass splitting: 3.19 ± 0.35 MeV
- Inelastic contribution: 0.30 ± 0.10 MeV
- Subtraction elastic contribution: 1.65 ± 0.36 MeV
- Subtraction inelastic contribution: 3 ± 9 MeV
- SELEX reported isospin mass splittings of 17 MeV and 21 MeV for two $\Xi_{cc}$ doublets
- LHCb reported a $\Xi_{cc}^{++}$ mass of 3621.40 ± 0.78 MeV
- Magnetic polarizability difference $\beta^{\Xi_{cc}^{++}} - \beta^{\Xi_{cc}^{+}} = -0.5 \pm 1.6 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$
- Mass scale for the polarizability form factor $\Lambda_{\beta}^{\Xi_{cc}} = 1.0 \pm 0.3$ GeV
- Ratio of light quark momentum fractions $\langle x \rangle^{\Xi_{cc}}_l / \langle x \rangle^p_d = 0.30$

## Context
This work extends the Cottingham sum-rule formalism previously applied to octet baryons by Erben et al. (2014) and Walker-Loud et al. (2012) to the doubly charmed cascade system. The calculation addresses the puzzle created by the SELEX Collaboration (2002) reporting anomalously large isospin splittings of 17-21 MeV for $\Xi_{cc}$ baryons, which are much larger than the typical 1-7 MeV splittings seen in other baryon isospin pairs. The results enable future lattice QCD calculations of magnetic polarizabilities and sigma terms for doubly charmed baryons to reduce the dominant theoretical uncertainty and resolve whether the SELEX observations are consistent with standard electromagnetic charge symmetry violation.
