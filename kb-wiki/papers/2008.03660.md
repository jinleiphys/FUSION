# From Ji to Jaffe-Manohar orbital angular momentum in Lattice QCD using a direct derivative method

> A Lattice QCD approach to quark orbital angular momentum in the proton based on generalized transverse momentum-dependent parton distributions (GTMDs) is enhanced methodologically by incorporating a direct derivative technique. This improvement removes a significant numerical bias that had been seen to afflict results of a previous study. In particular, the value obtained for Ji quark orbital angular momentum is reconciled with the one obtained independently via Ji's sum rule, validating the GMTD approach. Since GTMDs simultaneously contain information about the quark impact parameter and transverse momentum, they permit a direct evaluation of the cross product of the latter. They are defined through proton matrix elements of a quark bilocal operator containing a Wilson line; the choice in Wilson line path allows one to continuously interpolate from Ji to Jaffe-Manohar quark orbital angular momentum. The latter is seen to be significantly enhanced in magnitude compared to Ji quark orbital angular momentum, confirming previous results.

## Key claim

The direct derivative method removes a significant numerical bias that had caused the GTMD-based lattice QCD calculation of Ji quark orbital angular momentum to disagree with the independent determination via Ji's sum rule by approximately a factor of 2; after the improvement, the two determinations agree within statistical uncertainty, validating the GTMD approach.

## Method

The authors compute proton matrix elements of a quark bilocal operator containing a Wilson line (GTMD correlators) on a 32^3 x 96 lattice with 2+1-flavor clover fermions at pion mass 317 MeV and lattice spacing 0.114 fm. Instead of using finite differences over discrete momentum increments to evaluate the derivative with respect to transverse momentum transfer Delta_T, they implement a direct derivative method by coupling quarks to an infinitesimal constant U(1) background gauge field, which generates an additional vector current insertion in the propagators and allows exact evaluation of the Delta_T-derivative. The Wilson line path is varied from a straight line (eta=0, corresponding to Ji orbital angular momentum) to staple-shaped paths with staple length parameter eta, with the infinite staple length limit corresponding to Jaffe-Manohar orbital angular momentum.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_pi = 317 MeV (significantly lower than the 518 MeV used in the initial exploration by Engelhardt et al.)
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.114 fm on 32^3 x 96 lattices
- Total of 23224 data samples gathered on 968 gauge configurations
- Source-sink separation: t_f = 1.14 fm (10a)
- Three proton momenta used: P*L/(2pi) = (0,0,n_P) with n_P = 0, 1, 2, corresponding to Collins-Soper parameter zeta_hat = 0, 0.315, 0.63
- Ji quark orbital angular momentum from the GTMD approach agrees with the independent determination via Ji's sum rule at the same pion mass in the MSbar scheme at scale mu^2 = 4 GeV^2 (from LHPC, 2019)
- Jaffe-Manohar orbital angular momentum is significantly enhanced in magnitude compared to Ji orbital angular momentum, confirming previous results

## Context

This work extends the initial GTMD-based lattice QCD exploration by Engelhardt et al. (2016) which used a pion mass of 518 MeV and suffered from a factor-of-2 systematic bias due to finite difference evaluation of the Delta_T-derivative. The direct derivative method follows the approach developed by Hasan et al. for charge radius calculations. The results validate the GTMD framework for computing quark orbital angular momentum and enable future studies at multiple lattice spacings to observe scale evolution, as well as investigations at larger Collins-Soper parameter values to confirm the early onset of asymptotic behavior seen for Jaffe-Manohar orbital angular momentum.
