# Solution of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for pion-nucleon scattering

> A relativistic description of pion-nucleon scattering based on the four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equation is presented. The kernel of the equation consists of s- and u-channel nucleon and delta pole diagrams, as well as rho and sigma exchange in the t-channel. The Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved by means of a Wick rotation, and good fits are obtained to the s- and p-wave phase shifts up to 360 MeV pion laboratory energy. The coupling constants determined by the fits are consistent with the commonly accepted values in the literature.

## Key claim

The authors present a relativistic model of pion-nucleon scattering that solves the four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equation directly, rather than using a three-dimensional reduction, and obtain good fits to s- and p-wave phase shifts up to 360 MeV pion laboratory energy. The hedge is that the coupling constants determined by the fits are "consistent with the commonly accepted values in the literature," but the paper acknowledges significant ambiguities in those accepted values, particularly for the rho and sigma couplings.

## Method

The Bethe-Salpeter equation for pion-nucleon scattering is solved in four dimensions using a Wick rotation to handle the singularities of the kernel. The potential includes s- and u-channel nucleon and Delta(1232) pole diagrams, plus t-channel rho and sigma exchange, all derived from a chirally-invariant Lagrangian with derivative couplings. Form factors of the multipole type (with poles only on the real axis) are used to regularize the divergent integrals, and the bare nucleon mass and coupling constants are renormalized so that the dressed propagator has a pole at the physical nucleon mass.

## Key numbers

* The pion-nucleon coupling constant is fixed to \(g_{\pi N N}^2 / 4\pi = 13.5\), the value advocated by the Nijmegen group.
* The quark-model relation gives \(f_{\pi N \Delta}^2 / 4\pi = 0.21\) for the pi-N-Delta coupling, while the one-pion loop width calculation gives \(f_{\pi N \Delta}^2 / 4\pi = 0.36\).
* The rho coupling product \(g_{\rho}^2 / 4\pi = g_{\rho\pi\pi} g_{\rho NN} / 4\pi\) is estimated as 3.1 from the isospin-odd scattering length, or 2.84 from the rho decay width.
* The KSRF relation gives \(g_{\rho\pi\pi}^2 / 4\pi = 2.7\) using the pion decay constant \(f_{\pi} = 93\) MeV.
* The sigma meson mass is taken to be around \(4m_{\pi}\) to \(6m_{\pi}\) (approximately 560 to 840 MeV).
* The fits cover pion laboratory energies up to 360 MeV, with results also shown up to 600 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter calculation of Nieland and Tjon, which only included the u-channel nucleon pole and could only describe the P33 partial wave. It contrasts with three-dimensional reduction approaches used by Pearce and Jennings, Gross and Surya, and Lee and collaborators, which introduce ambiguities in off-shell behavior and can violate symmetries such as charge conjugation and CPT. The resulting amplitude can be gauged to produce a photoproduction amplitude that satisfies unitarity and gauge invariance, enabling applications to pion photoproduction and other nuclear reactions.
