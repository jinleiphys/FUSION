# A transport model for heavy ion collisions at RHIC

> To study heavy ion collisions at energies to be available from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC), we have developed a transport model that includes both initial partonic and final hadronic interactions. Specifically, the parton cascade model ZPC, which uses as input the parton distribution from the HIJING model, is extended to include the quark-gluon to hadronic matter transition and also final-state hadronic interactions based on the ART model. Comparisons with SPS data and predictions for RHIC are reported.

## Key claim

The authors present a transport model that combines parton cascade (ZPC) with hadronic afterburner (ART) to describe heavy ion collisions at RHIC energies, but the model's predictions are conditional on several adjustable parameters including the diquark-antidiquark pair production probability (set to 80%) and the treatment of double resonance absorption cross sections.

## Method

The model uses HIJING to generate initial parton phase-space distributions, then evolves the partons via the ZPC parton cascade model which includes only gluon-gluon elastic scatterings with a screening mass regulated cross section. After partons stop interacting, they are hadronized using a modified HIJING fragmentation scheme (with diquark-antidiquark pair formation) after an additional proper time of 1.2 fm, and the resulting hadrons are evolved using the ART transport model which was extended to include anti-nucleon annihilation, kaon inelastic interactions, and neutral kaon production.

## Key numbers

- Net baryon peak height at RHIC Au+Au central (b=0) collisions is around 80, smaller than the 100 predicted by Fritiof1.7
- Central rapidity net baryon height is around 15, similar to Venus4.02 prediction
- Anti-proton yield at central rapidities is about 10 after absorption in hadronic matter
- The BM-bar-B probability among produced diquark-antidiquark pairs is set to 80%
- Hadronization occurs after an additional proper time of approximately 1.2 fm
- Screening mass estimated as μ² ≈ 3πα_S/R_A² × (N_G/2Y)

## Context

This work extends the HIJING model (Wang and Gyulassy, 1991) and ZPC parton cascade (Zhang, 1998) by adding a hadronic afterburner based on the ART model (Li and Ko, 1995) which was previously successful at AGS energies. The model enables predictions for RHIC heavy ion collisions and shows that many anti-protons survive final-state interactions, that kaon production is significantly enhanced by hadronic interactions, and that the rapidity plateau is wider than default HIJING predictions. The authors note that future improvements should include parton inelastic scatterings, dynamical screening, and improved hadronization.
