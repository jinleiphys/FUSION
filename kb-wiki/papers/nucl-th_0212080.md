# Relativistic Mean Field Approximation in a Density Dependent Parametrization Model at Finite Temperature

> In this work we calculate the equation of state of nuclear matter for different proton fractions at zero and finite temperature within the Thomas Fermi approach considering three different parameter sets: the well-known NL3 and TM1 and a density dependent parametrization proposed by Typel and Wolter. The main differences are outlined and the consequences of imposing beta-stability in these models are discussed.

## Key claim

The density-dependent parametrization (TW) of Typel and Wolter produces a softer equation of state (EOS) than the constant-coupling NL3 and TM1 models for all proton fractions at both zero and finite temperature, with the softening arising because the TW meson-nucleon coupling constants decrease at high density (approximately to 0.7 of their saturation values for the isoscalar mesons and to zero for the isovector rho meson).

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic mean-field equations in the Thomas-Fermi approximation for infinite nuclear matter, using three parameter sets: the constant-coupling NL3 and TM1 models (which include nonlinear scalar and vector self-interactions) and the density-dependent TW model (where the meson-nucleon couplings are functions of the baryon density). They computed the equation of state (energy density vs. pressure) for fixed proton fractions and for beta-equilibrated matter (including electrons and muons) at temperatures from 0 to 100 MeV, and also evaluated the symmetry energy from the second derivative of the energy density with respect to isospin asymmetry.

## Key numbers

- The TW parametrization yields a symmetry energy of **32 MeV** at saturation density, compared to the NL3 value which lies in the relativistic-model range of 35–42 MeV; the accepted range from mass formula calculations is 27–36 MeV.
- At high densities (ρ/ρ_sat ≫ 1), the TW coupling constants approach approximately **0.7** of their saturation values for the σ and ω mesons, while the ρ-meson coupling goes to **zero**.
- The electron and muon masses used in the beta-equilibrium calculations are **m_e = 0.511 MeV** and **m_μ = 106.55 MeV**.
- Finite-temperature calculations were performed at **T = 10 MeV**, **50 MeV**, and **100 MeV**; at T = 10 MeV the EOS minima shift upward compared to T = 0, and pure neutron matter (Y_p = 0) acquires a minimum at finite temperature that is absent at T = 0.

## Context

This work extends the density-dependent parametrization of Typel and Wolter (2000) from symmetric nuclear matter to asymmetric matter and finite temperature, comparing it with the widely used NL3 model of Lalazissis et al. (1997) and the TM1 model of Sugahara and Toki (1994). The TW EOS is found to be softer than both NL3 and TM1 and closer to the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock results of Brockmann and Machleidt (1990), making it relevant for neutron-star structure calculations and for studies of liquid-gas phase transitions in asymmetric matter (which the authors note is under current investigation).
