# Relaxation for massive particles: transport and causality

> Correlators at finite density and temperature encode important information about a physical system, such as transport and causality. For a massive gas of particles in the relaxation time approximation of kinetic theory, we provide complete analytic results for all first order transport coefficients, including thermoelectric coefficients. We demonstrate the first complete picture of the complex structure of the correlators as a function of mass, providing an interpretation in terms of the causal structure of lightcones. We provide a simple criterion to extract the effective lightcone velocity from the cut of the retarded correlator.

## Key claim

For a massive gas in the relaxation time approximation of kinetic theory, the retarded correlators exhibit an undeformable cut in the complex frequency plane whose endpoints correspond to the speed of light and whose discontinuity profile encodes an effective lightcone velocity for massive particle propagation; the authors provide a complete analytic picture of all first-order transport coefficients including thermoelectric coefficients, and they show that the diffusive mode remains stable for all masses in any spatial dimension \(d>1\), contrary to a claim by Hataei (2025).

## Method

The authors solve the linearized Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation (RTA) with a constant relaxation time \(\tau_R\) for a gas of massive particles obeying the Maxwell–Jüttner equilibrium distribution, turning on external gauge and metric perturbations in Fourier space. They compute the retarded correlators of the number current and energy-momentum tensor using a variational approach, extract the hydrodynamic dispersion relations by expanding the pole equations to quadratic order in frequency \(\omega\) and wave number \(k\), and obtain closed-form expressions for the transport coefficients in terms of modified Bessel functions and Meijer \(G\)-functions. The analytic structure of the correlators is studied by examining the discontinuity along the cut in the complex \(\omega\) plane, and a criterion is proposed to define the effective lightcone velocity \(v_{\rm cut}\) from the discontinuity profile.

## Key numbers

* Charge diffusion coefficient: \(D(m)/\tau_R = 1/3 - (1/6)\mathfrak{m}^2 + \mathcal{O}(\mathfrak{m}^3)\) for \(\mathfrak{m} \ll 1\) and \(D(m)/\tau_R = 1/\mathfrak{m} + \mathcal{O}(\mathfrak{m}^{-2})\) for \(\mathfrak{m} \gg 1\), where \(\mathfrak{m} \equiv m/T_0\).
* Shear diffusion coefficient: \(D_{\rm sh}(m)/\tau_R = 1/5 - (1/60)\mathfrak{m}^2 + \mathcal{O}(\mathfrak{m}^4)\) for \(\mathfrak{m} \ll 1\) and \(D_{\rm sh}(m)/\tau_R = 1/\mathfrak{m} + \mathcal{O}(\mathfrak{m}^{-2})\) for \(\mathfrak{m} \gg 1\).
* Speed of sound squared: \(c_s^2 = 1/3\) in the massless limit, with asymptotic behavior \(c_s^2 = (1/3)(1 - \mathfrak{m}^2/24) + \mathcal{O}(\mathfrak{m}^4)\) for \(\mathfrak{m} \ll 1\) and \(c_s^2 = 1/\mathfrak{m} + \mathcal{O}(\mathfrak{m}^{-5/2})\) for \(\mathfrak{m} \gg 1\).
* Attenuation in the sound channel: \(\Gamma_s/\tau_R = 2/15\) in the massless limit, with \(\Gamma_s/\tau_R = 2/15 (1 - \mathfrak{m}^2/12) + \mathcal{O}(\mathfrak{m}^3)\) for \(\mathfrak{m} \ll 1\) and \(\Gamma_s/\tau_R = 1/\mathfrak{m} + \mathcal{O}(\mathfrak{m}^{-2})\) for \(\mathfrak{m} \gg 1\).
* Bulk viscosity: \(\zeta/(\varepsilon_0 + P_0) = \tau_R (5/432)\mathfrak{m}^4 - (\pi/144)\mathfrak{m}^5 + \mathcal{O}(\mathfrak{m}^6)\) for \(\mathfrak{m} \ll 1\) and \(\zeta/(\varepsilon_0 + P_0) = \tau_R (2/3)\mathfrak{m}^{-1} - (16/3)\mathfrak{m}^{-2} + \mathcal{O}(\mathfrak{m}^{-3})\) for \(\mathfrak{m} \gg 1\).
* Shear viscosity: \(\eta = (\tau_R/(30\pi^2)) \int_m^\infty dx \, x^{-1} (x^2 - m^2)^{5/2} e^{-x/T_0}\).
* The endpoints of the cut in the complex frequency plane are located at \(\omega = \pm k - i/\tau_R\).

## Context

This work extends the massless RTA results of Romatschke (2015) and the massive thermodynamic calculations of Romatschke (2011) by providing closed-form expressions for all first-order transport coefficients, including thermoelectric coefficients, for a massive gas. It corrects an erroneous claim by Hataei (2025) that the diffusive mode becomes unstable at a certain mass, showing instead that the diffusion coefficient remains positive for all masses in any spatial dimension \(d>1\). The analysis of the cut structure and the extraction of an effective lightcone velocity from the discontinuity profile is presented as novel, and the authors suggest that similar analytic structures appear in weakly coupled field theories, referencing the work of Hartnoll (2005) on gluon propagators and Rocha (2024) on scalar field theory. The results enable future studies of how causal arguments in kinetic theory are modified by the presence of cuts.
