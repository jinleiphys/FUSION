# Wilson line correlators beyond the large-$N_c$

> We study hard $1\to 2$ final-state parton splittings in the medium, and put special emphasis on calculating the Wilson line correlators that appear in these calculations. As partons go through the medium their color continuously rotates, an effect that is encapsulated in a Wilson line along their trajectory. When calculating observables, one typically has to calculate traces of two or more medium-averaged Wilson lines. These are usually dealt with in the literature by invoking the large-$N_c$ limit, but exact calculations have been lacking in many cases. In our work, we show how correlators of multiple Wilson lines appear, and develop a method to calculate them numerically to all orders in $N_c$. Initially, we focus on the trace of four Wilson lines, which we develop a differential equation for. We will then generalize this calculation to a product of an arbitrary number of Wilson lines, and show how to do the exact calculation numerically, and even analytically in the large-$N_c$ limit. Color sub-leading corrections, that are suppressed with a factor $N_c^{-2}$ relative to the leading scaling, are calculated explicitly for the four-point correlator and we discuss how to extend this method to the general case. These results are relevant for high-$p_T$ jet processes and initial stage physics at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper develops a method to calculate Wilson line correlators for medium-induced parton splittings to all orders in the number of colors \(N_c\), going beyond the standard large-\(N_c\) approximation; the authors show that color sub-leading corrections, suppressed by a factor \(N_c^{-2}\) relative to the leading scaling, are essential for recovering the correct long-time behavior of the correlators, despite the naive expectation that such corrections are only \(\sim 10\%\) for \(N_c=3\).

## Method

The authors study hard \(1\to 2\) final-state parton splittings (photon splitting to quark-antiquark, quark splitting to quark-gluon, and gluon splitting to two gluons) in a medium modeled by a classical background gauge field with a Gaussian noise approximation. They derive a coupled system of differential equations for the evolution of multi-Wilson line correlators in time, which they solve numerically to all orders in \(N_c\), and also analytically in the large-\(N_c\) limit; the method is applied to compute the medium modification factor \(F_{\text{med}}(z,\theta)\) for the emission spectra using the harmonic oscillator approximation for the medium potential.

## Key numbers

* The color sub-leading corrections are suppressed by a factor \(N_c^{-2}\) relative to the leading scaling, corresponding to \(\sim 10\%\) for \(N_c=3\).
* The formation time of the splitting process is given by \(t_{\rm f} = \frac{2}{z(1-z)E\theta^2}\).
* The jet quenching coefficient for the fundamental representation is defined as \(\hat q_R = C_R n g^2 \int^{q^{\rm max}_{\perp}} \frac{{\rm d}^2 \q}{(2\pi)^2} \, \q^2 \frac{{\rm d}^2 \sigma_{\rm el}}{{\rm d}^2 \q}\), with \(C_F=\frac{N_c^2-1}{2N_c}\) and \(C_A=N_c\).
* The vacuum emission spectrum is \(\frac{{\rm d} I^{\textrm{vac}}}{{\rm d} z \,{\rm d} \theta}=\frac{\alpha}{\pi} \frac{P(z)}{\theta}\), where \(P(z)\) is the Altarelli-Parisi splitting function.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of medium-induced parton splittings, such as those by Mehtar-Tani (2018) and Dominguez (2020), which focused on photon splitting and invoked the large-\(N_c\) approximation to obtain analytical formulas. The method developed here enables exact numerical calculation of multi-Wilson line correlators for arbitrary \(N_c\), relevant for improving jet quenching phenomenology at the LHC and for understanding color dynamics in high-energy QCD; it also connects to techniques for multi-gluon emissions with overlapping formation times (Arnold, 2019) and to sub-leading color corrections in high-energy QCD evolution at next-to-leading order (Lappi, 2020).
