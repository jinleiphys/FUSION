# Static meson correlators in 2+1 flavor QCD at non-zero temperature

> We study correlation functions of various static meson operators of size r at non-zero temperature in 2+1 flavor QCD, including Coulomb gauge fixed operators and Wilson loops with smeared spatial parts. The numerical calculations are performed on 24^3x6 lattices using highly improved staggered quark action. We discuss possible implications of our findings on the temperature dependence of the static energy of QQbar pair.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the temperature dependence of static meson correlators and the effective potential derived from them suggests the energy of a static quark-antiquark pair decreases with increasing temperature, contrary to prior results obtained in SU(3) gauge theory using the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) by Rothkopf (2011). However, the authors qualify that the effective potential does not reach a plateau at finite temperature, making a direct extraction of the static energy ambiguous, and that the observed tau-dependence may arise from periodic boundary conditions rather than the imaginary part of the potential.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations of static meson correlation functions on 24^3 x 6 lattices using the Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) action with 2+1 flavors of dynamical quarks (light quark mass corresponding to pion mass 160 MeV, strange quark mass at its physical value). They computed correlators for various static meson operators including Coulomb gauge fixed operators and Wilson loops with APE and HYP smeared spatial links across a temperature range from 147 MeV to 488 MeV, spanning the chiral crossover region (T_c = 171(1) MeV). The effective potential V_eff was studied as a function of Euclidean time tau for spatial separations rT = 1/2 and rT = 1, and the singlet free energy F_1 was extracted from the correlator at tau = 1/T.

## Key numbers

* The chiral crossover temperature for N_tau = 6 is T_c = 171(1) MeV.
* Temperatures studied range from T = 147 MeV (beta = 6.000) to T = 488 MeV (beta = 7.280).
* At the lowest temperature T = 147 MeV and separation rT = 1/2, the downward shift of the static potential is about 10 MeV.
* For modeling the imaginary part of the potential, the authors used values Im V = T for rT = 1 and Im V = T/2 for rT = 1/2, and also considered Im V = 3T for rT = 1.
* The non-exponential contribution to the correlator (coefficient c) is about 1000 times smaller than the exponential amplitude A, but increases with increasing temperature.
* The lattice spacing is determined using r_1 = 0.3106 fm.

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of static quark-antiquark interactions at non-zero temperature from pure gauge theory (Zantow 2001, Kaczmarek 2002, Digal 2003, Bazavov 2008) to 2+1 flavor QCD with dynamical quarks across the chiral crossover. It contrasts with the MEM-based analysis of Wilson loops in quenched QCD by Rothkopf (2011), which suggested different behavior for the static energy. The results enable future quantitative comparisons with resummed hard thermal loop perturbation theory calculations of the singlet free energy (Burnier 2009, Berwein 2012) and provide input for effective field theory approaches to in-medium quarkonium properties (Brambilla 2008, Petreczky 2010).
