# Pairing in bulk nuclear matter beyond BCS

> The influence of short-range correlations on the spectral distribution of neutrons is incorporated in the solution of the gap equation for the ${}^3P_2-{}^3F_2$ coupled channel in pure neutron matter. This effect is studied for different realistic interactions including one based on chiral perturbation theory. The gap in this channel vanishes at all relevant densities due to the treatment of these correlations. We also consider the effect of long-range correlations by including polarization terms in addition to the bare interaction which allow the neutrons to exchange density and spin fluctuations governed by the strength of Landau parameters allowed to have reasonable values consistent with the available literature. Preliminary results indicate that reasonable values of these parameters do not generate a gap in the ${}^3P_2-{}^3F_2$ coupled channel either for all three realistic interactions although the pairing interaction becomes slightly more attractive.

## Key claim

Short-range correlations (SRC), when properly included via self-consistent ladder-diagram self-energies, cause the pairing gap in the \(^3P_2-^3F_2\) coupled channel of pure neutron matter to vanish at all relevant densities for three realistic interactions (CD-Bonn, N3LO chiral, and Argonne v18); preliminary inclusion of long-range polarization terms does not restore a finite gap, although the pairing interaction becomes slightly more attractive.

## Method

The authors solved the generalized gap equation for the \(^3P_2-^3F_2\) coupled channel in pure neutron matter, replacing the BCS quasiparticle-energy denominator with a convolution of normal spectral functions that incorporate short-range correlations from self-consistent ladder-diagram self-energies at finite temperature, extrapolated to zero temperature. They employed three realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions (CD-Bonn, N3LO chiral-perturbation-theory potential, and Argonne v18) and, for long-range correlations, added polarization terms to the bare interaction using Landau parameters for density and spin fluctuations following the procedure of Cao (2006).

## Key numbers

* For the \(^1S_0\) channel with CD-Bonn interaction, inclusion of SRC reduces the maximum gap by about 0.75 MeV relative to the BCS value of about 3 MeV at \(k_F = 0.8\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).
* For the \(^1S_0\) channel with CD-Bonn interaction, simultaneous inclusion of SRC and LRC yields a maximum gap of about 1.7 MeV at a Fermi momentum slightly above \(0.8\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).
* For the \(^3P_2-^3F_2\) channel with N3LO interaction, the BCS gap (kinetic single-particle energies) has a maximum of about 0.75 MeV; adding LRC without SRC raises the maximum to about 1.3 MeV at a slightly higher density.
* For the \(^3P_2-^3F_2\) channel with CD-Bonn interaction, the BCS maximum gap is about 0.8 MeV but at a substantially higher density than for N3LO.
* For the \(^3P_2-^3F_2\) channel, inclusion of SRC alone yields no nontrivial solution for any of the three interactions; preliminary inclusion of both SRC and LRC also yields no finite gap.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Muther (2005), who showed that SRC suppress the \(^3S_1-^3D_1\) gap in symmetric nuclear matter to zero at normal density, reconciling many-body calculations with empirical evidence from finite nuclei. It applies the same self-consistent treatment to the \(^3P_2-^3F_2\) channel in neutron matter, where earlier BCS calculations (e.g., Dean 2003) gave gaps ranging from about 0.5 to 0.9 MeV. The results challenge the assumption that this channel provides the high-density neutron superfluidity required in some neutron-star cooling scenarios (e.g., Page 2011, Blaschke 2013). The inclusion of three-body forces, as studied by Dong (2013) with approximate SRC treatment, may not alter these conclusions.
