# Topological susceptibility and axion potential in two-flavor superconductive quark matter

> We study the potential of the axion, $a$, of Quantum Chromodynamics, in the two-flavor color superconducting phase of cold and dense quark matter. We adopt a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio-like model. Our interaction contains two terms, one preserving and one breaking the $U(1)_A$ symmetry: the latter is responsible of the coupling of axions to quarks. We introduce two quark condensates, $h_L$ and $h_R$, describing condensation for left-handed and right-handed quarks respectively; we then study the loci of the minima of the thermodynamic potential, $Ω$, in the $(h_L,h_R)$ plane, noticing how the instanton-induced interaction favors condensation in the scalar channel when the $θ-$angle, $θ=a/f_a$, vanishes. Increasing $θ$ we find a phase transition where the scalar condensate rotates into a pseudo-scalar one. We present an analytical result for the topological susceptibility, $χ$, in the superconductive phase, which stands both at zero and at finite temperature. Finally, we compute the axion mass and its self-coupling. In particular, the axion mass $m_a$ is related to the full topological susceptibility via $χ=m_a^2 f_a^2$, hence our result for $χ$ gives an analytical result for $m_a$ in the superconductive phase of high-density Quantum Chromodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper derives an analytical expression for the topological susceptibility in the two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase of dense quark matter, which directly gives the axion mass in this phase; however, this result is obtained within a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio-like model at mean-field level, and the authors note that the axion mass formula depends on the physical value of the superconductive gap and on the parameter ζ that controls the relative strength of the U(1)A-breaking instanton-induced interaction.

## Method

The authors adopt a four-fermion effective Lagrangian with a U(1)A-preserving term (modeling one-gluon exchange) and a U(1)A-breaking term (modeling instanton-mediated interactions), coupling the axion field a to the latter via phases exp(±ia/fa). They introduce separate left-handed and right-handed diquark condensates hL and hR, compute the thermodynamic potential Ω at one-loop (mean-field) level using Nambu-Gorkov formalism, and study its minima in the (hL, hR) plane as a function of θ = a/fa. The topological susceptibility χ = ∂²Ω/∂θ² at θ = 0 is then derived analytically in terms of the gap parameters.

## Key numbers

- The condition for a non-trivial solution of the gap equation requires ζ < 2; for ζ ≥ 2 there exists a range of a where only the trivial solution hL = 0 exists.
- The gaps Δ3 and Δ5 are given by Δ3² = ζ²GD²hL² + 4GD²hR² − 4ζGD²hLhR cos(a/fa) and Δ5² = ζ²GD²hR² + 4GD²hL² − 4ζGD²hLhR cos(a/fa).
- The momentum cutoff Λ is taken as Λ = O(1 GeV), following standard NJL model conventions.
- The quark chemical potential used in the numerical results is μ = 400 MeV.
- The coupling GD is fixed to produce a gap ΔL = 50 MeV at a = 0 and μ = 400 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of axion properties in dense quark matter by Zhang et al. (2023) and others (Lu 2018, Lopes 2022, Bandyopadhyay 2019, Das 2020, Mohapatra 2022) from the chiral symmetry breaking phase to the color-superconducting phase. It also builds on earlier treatments of the 2SC phase using NJL-like models by Blaschke (2005) and Ruester (2005), but introduces separate left- and right-handed condensates to capture the pseudo-scalar condensate that becomes relevant when the axion field is non-zero. The analytical result for the topological susceptibility enables computation of the axion mass in the 2SC phase, which is relevant for axion phenomenology in compact star interiors.
