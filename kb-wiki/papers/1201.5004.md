# Geometrical Effect on Conical Emission of Correlated Hadrons

> Dihadron correlations at intermediate p_T revealed novel structures on the away side of high p_T trigger particles at RHIC. The away-side correlations in central Au+Au collisions are significantly broader than in pp and d+Au collisions and in restricted kinematic range, double-peaked away from Δϕ=π. Three-particle correlations indicate conical emission of the away-side correlated hadrons at angles independent of associated particle p_T, consistent with formation of Mach-cone shock waves. In this talk we further investigate the conical emission phenomenon exploiting dihadron correlations as a function of the trigger particle azimuth from the reaction plane. Such correlations are sensitive to the collision geometry. We study these geometrical effects and discuss how they might be used to further our understanding of the medium created in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The away-side double-peak structure in dihadron correlations, previously interpreted as evidence for Mach-cone shock waves, is shown to depend strongly on the trigger particle's azimuthal angle relative to the reaction plane, suggesting that medium flow effects significantly modify the conical emission angle rather than the angle being a direct measure of the medium's speed of sound.

## Method

The analysis uses dihadron correlations at large pseudorapidity separation (|Δη| > 0.7) in 20-60% central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, dividing events into six slices of the trigger particle azimuth relative to the event plane (φ_s). The correlation functions are fit with four Gaussians: two for back-to-back ridge components at Δφ = 0 and π, and two symmetric Gaussian peaks on the away side about Δφ = π. The elliptic flow background is subtracted using the Zero-Yield-At-Minimum (ZYAM) method, with flow parameters taken as the average between the away-side two-particle method (v_2{AS}) and the four-particle cumulant method (v_2{4}).

## Key numbers

* Conical emission angle from three-particle correlations: θ = 1.37 ± 0.02 (stat.) ± 0.06 (syst.) radians, measured in prior work (STAR, 2011).
* Trigger particle p_T range: 3 < p_T^(t) < 4 GeV/c; associated particle p_T ranges: 1 < p_T^(a) < 2 GeV/c and 1 < p_T^(a) < 1.5 GeV/c.
* The away-side double-peak position is approximately constant for |φ_s| < 45°, then increases with φ_s for |φ_s| > 45°, with different behavior for low and high associated p_T.
* The peak angle for out-of-plane trigger particles increases with associated p_T, contrary to naive expectations from uniform medium flow.

## Context

This work extends prior dihadron correlation studies at RHIC that revealed broad away-side structures and double-peaked correlations in central Au+Au collisions (STAR, 2005; PHENIX, 2006), and three-particle correlation measurements indicating conical emission consistent with Mach-cone shock waves (STAR, 2009). The study by Takahashi (2009) suggested initial-state fluctuations could produce away-side double-hump structures, but those would not generate conical emission in three-particle correlations (Qian, 2011). The present analysis uses the trigger particle orientation relative to the reaction plane to disentangle medium flow effects from the conical emission signal, enabling future extraction of the medium's speed of sound and equation of state.
