# Elucidation of the anomalous A = 9 isospin quartet behaviour

> Recent high-precision mass measurements of $^{9}$Li and $^{9}$Be, performed with the TITAN Penning trap at the TRIUMF ISAC facility, are analyzed in light of state-of-the-art shell model calculations. We find an explanation for the anomalous Isobaric Mass Multiplet Equation (IMME) behaviour for the two $A$ = 9 quartets. The presence of a cubic $d$ = 6.3(17) keV term for the $J^π$ = 3/2$^{-}$ quartet and the vanishing cubic term for the excited $J^π$ = 1/2$^{-}$ multiplet depend upon the presence of a nearby $T$ = 1/2 state in $^{9}$B and $^{9}$Be that induces isospin mixing. This is contrary to previous hypotheses involving purely Coulomb and charge-dependent effects. $T$ = 1/2 states have been observed near the calculated energy, above the $T$ = 3/2 state. However an experimental confirmation of their $J^π$ is needed.

## Key claim

The anomalous cubic term (d = 6.3(17) keV) in the ground-state A = 9 isospin quartet is caused by isospin mixing between the T = 3/2 state and a nearby T = 1/2 state in ⁹B and ⁹Be, contrary to previous hypotheses that invoked purely Coulomb and charge-dependent effects. The vanishing cubic term for the excited J^π = 1/2⁻ quartet arises because no nearby T = 1/2 state exists for that multiplet.

## Method

High-precision mass measurements of ⁹Li and ⁹Be were performed using the TITAN Penning trap mass spectrometer at the TRIUMF ISAC facility. The new masses were combined with existing reaction Q-value data for ⁹B and ⁹C, and analyzed using shell model calculations with isospin-conserving (PJT and CKI Hamiltonians) and isospin-non-conserving interactions to extract the IMME coefficients and identify the isospin mixing mechanism.

## Key numbers

- New ⁹Li ground state mass excess: 24 954.91(20) keV
- New ⁹Be ground state mass excess: 11 348.391(93) keV
- Cubic coefficient d for the J^π = 3/2⁻ quartet: 6.3(17) keV
- Quadratic fit χ² for the J^π = 3/2⁻ quartet: 14.6 (P-value = 1×10⁻⁴)
- Quadratic fit χ² for the J^π = 1/2⁻ quartet (using ENSDF ⁹B excitation energy): 1.2 (P-value = 3×10⁻¹)
- Quadratic fit χ² for the J^π = 1/2⁻ quartet (using alternative ⁹B excitation energy 16 990(30) keV): 6.9 (P-value = 9×10⁻³)
- Isospin non-conserving matrix element magnitude for ⁹B: 116 keV (sum of Coulomb 54 keV, charge-asymmetric isovector 44 keV, charge-dependent isotensor 18 keV)
- Isospin non-conserving matrix element magnitude for ⁹Be: 45 keV (sum of Coulomb -19 keV, charge-asymmetric isovector -44 keV, charge-dependent isotensor 18 keV)
- Resulting downward shifts: approximately 14 keV in ⁹B and 3 keV in ⁹Be

## Context

This work resolves a long-standing anomaly in the A = 9 isospin quartets first documented by Kaschl (1975) and reviewed by Britz (1998), where previous explanations based on Coulomb-dependent Thomas-Ehrman shifts and non-Coulomb charge-dependent forces (Bertsch 1970) proved insufficient. The results extend the understanding of isospin mixing mechanisms previously applied to the A = 8 quintet (Charity 2011) and provide a framework that can be generalized to other isobaric multiplets, with implications for isospin-forbidden proton decays and the determination of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix element V_ud.
