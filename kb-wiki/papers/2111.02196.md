# Shell-model study of titanium isotopic chain with chiral two- and three-body forces

> The even-even Ti isotopic chain, from A = 42 to 70, has been studied within the nuclear shell-model framework by employing an effective Hamiltonian which is derived by way of many-body perturbation theory from a chiral potential with two- and three-body forces, and includes three-body contributions which account for Pauli principle violations in nuclei with more than two valence particles. We consider 40Ca as a closed core and a model space spanned by the neutron and proton 0f1p orbitals with the addition of the 0g9/2 orbital for neutrons. Calculated two-neutron separation energies and excitation energies of the yrast 2+ states are reported and compared with the experimental data, which are available up to 62Ti. The present study intends to investigate the effects of the adopted effective interactions on the evolution of the shell structure.

## Key claim

The chiral-derived shell-model effective Hamiltonian, which includes density-dependent two-body terms that account for three-body correlations, improves agreement with experimental two-neutron separation energies for neutron-rich Ti isotopes beyond N=34, but the omission of the neutron 1d5/2 orbital prevents the model from reproducing the observed decrease in yrast 2+ excitation energies from N=36 to N=40.

## Method

The authors start from the chiral N3LO nucleon-nucleon potential of Entem and Machleidt and the chiral N2LO three-nucleon force, with low-energy constants from Machleidt (2011) and Navratil (2007). They derive a shell-model effective Hamiltonian via the Kuo-Lee-Ratcliff folded-diagram expansion of the Q-box vertex function, calculated up to third order in the NN potential and first order in the NNN potential, within a model space of proton 0f1p orbitals and neutron 0f1p plus 0g9/2 orbitals outside a 40Ca core. Three-body correlations are included as density-dependent two-body matrix elements derived from second-order three-body diagrams, producing a separate effective Hamiltonian for each nucleus.

## Key numbers

* Two-neutron separation energies for Ti isotopes from N=22 to 48 are calculated; the inclusion of three-body correlations increases S2n values starting from N=34, bringing them into good agreement with experimental data for 56-62Ti, which are significantly underestimated when these correlations are omitted.
* The calculated 2+1 excitation energy in 62Ti is reduced by three-body correlations, leading to the disappearance of the shell gap at N=40 in agreement with experiment (Cortez 2020), whereas calculations without these correlations predict an increase.
* The 0g9/2 - 0f5/2 neutron ESPE gap at N=40 is reduced by approximately 400 keV when three-body correlations are included.
* All Ti isotopes up to 70Ti are predicted to be bound in both calculations (with and without three-body correlations).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on Ca isotopes (Coraggio 2020) and on the role of chiral three-body forces in the 0f1p model space (Ma 2019) to the full Ti isotopic chain with both valence neutrons and protons. The results are compared with experimental data from Michi (2020) and Cortez (2020), and with prior shell-model calculations using empirically corrected interactions (Lenzi 2010) and realistic interactions from the CD-Bonn potential (Coraggio 2014). The omission of the neutron 1d5/2 orbital, identified by Caurier (2002) and Lenzi (2010) as crucial for reproducing collectivity at N=40, limits the present model's ability to describe the observed 2+ excitation energies from N=36 to 40; the authors plan to include this orbital in future large-scale calculations using a double-step procedure.
