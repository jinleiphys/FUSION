# The Gallium Solar Neutrino Capture Cross Section Revisited

> Solar neutrino flux constraints from the legacy GALLEX/GNO and SAGE experiments continue to influence contemporary global analyses of neutrino properties. The constraints depend on the neutrino absorption cross sections for various solar sources. Following recent work updating the $^{51}$Cr and $^{37}$Ar neutrino source cross sections, we reevaluate the $^{71}$Ga solar neutrino cross sections, focusing on contributions from transitions to $^{71}$Ge excited states, but also revising the ground-state transition to take into account new $^{71}$Ge electron-capture lifetime measurements and various theory corrections. The excited-state contributions have been traditionally taken from forward-angle $(p,n)$ cross sections. Here we correct this procedure for the $\approx 10\%-20\%$ tensor operator contribution that alters the relationship between Gamow-Teller and $(p,n)$ transition strengths. Using state-of-the-art nuclear shell-model calculations to evaluate this correction, we find that it lowers the $^8$B and hep neutrino cross sections. However, the addition of other corrections, including contributions from near-threshold continuum states that radiatively decay, leads to an overall increase in the $^8$B and hep cross sections of $\approx 10\%$ relative to the values recommended by Bahcall. Uncertainties are propagated using Monte Carlo simulations.

## Key claim

The authors reevaluate the $^{71}$Ga solar neutrino cross sections and find that, after correcting for a tensor operator contribution in $(p,n)$ scattering and including contributions from near-threshold continuum states, the $^8$B and hep neutrino cross sections increase by approximately 10% relative to the values recommended by Bahcall, contrary to the naive expectation that the tensor correction alone would lower them.

## Method

The authors extract Gamow-Teller (GT) transition strengths for $^{71}$Ga($\nu_e,e^-$)$^{71}$Ge from forward-angle $(p,n)$ cross section measurements, correcting for the approximately 10-20% tensor operator contribution that alters the relationship between GT and $(p,n)$ transition strengths using state-of-the-art nuclear shell-model calculations. They revise the ground-state transition using new $^{71}$Ge electron-capture lifetime measurements from three experiments, incorporate radiative corrections and weak-magnetism effects, and include contributions from near-threshold continuum states that radiatively decay rather than undergo particle breakup. Uncertainties are propagated using Monte Carlo simulations.

## Key numbers

- Weighted mean $^{71}$Ge electron-capture half-life: $\tau_{1/2} = 11.465 \pm 0.008$ days, from combining measurements of $11.43 \pm 0.03$ d (PhysRevC.31.666), $11.46 \pm 0.04$ d (PhysRevC.108.L021602), and $11.468 \pm 0.008$ d (PhysRevC.109.055501)
- Effective ground-state $B_{\mathrm{GT}^+}$ value: $0.0863 \pm 0.0013$ (95% C.L.)
- $^{71}$Ge EC Q value: $232.443 \pm 0.093$ keV
- Tensor coupling strength $\delta = 0.074 \pm 0.008$ (1$\sigma$) from PhysRevC.108.035502
- Excited state $B_{\mathrm{GT}^+}$ for the 500 keV $3/2^-$ level: $0.0104 \pm 0.0022$ (68% C.L.)
- Excited state $B_{\mathrm{GT}^+}$ for the 175 keV $5/2^-$ level: $\le 0.0077$ (68% C.L.)
- Overall increase in $^8$B and hep cross sections: approximately 10% relative to Bahcall values
- $^{71}$Ge breakup threshold: 7.416 MeV
- $L/K$ capture ratio: $0.118 \pm 0.004$; $M/K$ capture ratio: $0.019 \pm 0.003$

## Context

This work extends the recent reevaluation of $^{71}$Ga cross sections for $^{51}$Cr and $^{37}$Ar neutrino sources (PhysRevC.108.035502) to solar neutrino sources, addressing the same tensor operator correction to $(p,n)$ cross sections that was applied there. The updated cross sections are needed because the legacy GALLEX/GNO and SAGE experiments continue to influence global neutrino parameter analyses (Esteban 2020, de Salas 2020) and provide direct constraints on low-energy pp neutrinos alongside Borexino. The results enable more precise extraction of solar neutrino fluxes from the gallium experiment data and help resolve whether nuclear physics uncertainties contribute to the approximately 4$\sigma$ "gallium anomaly" seen in neutrino source calibration experiments including the recent BEST experiment.
