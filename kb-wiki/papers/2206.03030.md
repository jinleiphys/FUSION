# Triaxiality induced monopole-quadrupole-hexadecupole coupling in the isoscalar giant resonances for $^{86}\textrm{Ge}$

> The isoscalar giant resonances for $^{86}\textrm{Ge}$ are studied by the quasiparticle finite amplitude method based on the covariant density functional theory. In addition to the well-known monopole-quadrupole coupling that splits the isoscalar giant monopole resonance in axially deformed nuclei, a monopole-quadrupole-hexadecupole coupling is identified in the triaxially deformed nucleus $^{86}\textrm{Ge}$, leading to the emergence of a distinct resonance peak at the low energy side of the isoscalar monopole strength function. The transition density of the triaxiality induced resonance peak shows a strong interplay among monopole, quadrupole, and hexadecupole vibrations. The resonance peak responses to monopole, quadrupole, and hexadecupole perturbations simultaneously, which could be regarded as a fingerprint of the triaxiality in $^{86}\textrm{Ge}$.

## Key claim

In the triaxially deformed nucleus \(^{86}\text{Ge}\), a monopole-quadrupole-hexadecupole (E0-E2-E4) coupling is identified, leading to a distinct resonance peak near 11.5 MeV in the isoscalar giant monopole resonance strength function that does not appear in axially deformed calculations; this peak could serve as a fingerprint of triaxiality.

## Method

The authors performed quasiparticle finite amplitude method (QFAM) calculations based on covariant density functional theory (CDFT) with the DD-ME2 functional and a separable pairing force, using a triaxial harmonic oscillator basis with 12 shells (2,086,560 two-quasiparticle pairs). They computed strength functions for the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR), isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR) with \(K=0\), and isoscalar giant hexadecupole resonance (ISGHR) with \(K=0\) by perturbing the nucleus with the operators \(\hat{Q}_{00}=r^2\), \(\hat{Q}_{20}=r^2Y_{20}\), and \(\hat{Q}_{40}=r^4Y_{40}\), and compared triaxial and axial results. Transition densities at specific energies were analyzed to identify the multipole composition of the resonances.

## Key numbers

- The ground state of \(^{86}\text{Ge}\) has deformation parameters \(\beta = 0.234\) and \(\gamma = 20.9^\circ\) (triaxial), compared to an axial prolate minimum at \(\beta = 0.209\) with \(E = 0.62\) MeV and an axial oblate minimum at \(\beta = 0.191\) with \(E = 1.70\) MeV.
- The triaxiality-induced resonance peak appears at \(\omega \approx 11.5\) MeV in the ISGMR strength function.
- The fraction of the energy-weighted sum rule exhausted by the triaxiality-induced peak (from 10 to 12 MeV, subtracting the axial case) is 0.55% for ISGMR, 1.02% for ISGQR, and 2.58% for ISGHR.
- The lifetime of \(^{86}\text{Ge}\) is about 226 ms.
- The particle-hole configuration \(2d_{5/2} (\Omega_h = 5/2) \rightarrow 1i_{13/2} (\Omega_p = 1/2)\) has an energy of 12.21 MeV, and \(1g_{7/2} (\Omega_h = 7/2) \rightarrow 1i_{13/2} (\Omega_p = 1/2)\) has an energy of 8.99 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of monopole-quadrupole (E0-E2) coupling in axially deformed nuclei, which splits the ISGMR into two branches as observed experimentally in nuclei such as \(^{150}\text{Nd}\) (Garg, 1984), \(^{154}\text{Sm}\) (Youngblood, 1999), and \(^{238}\text{U}\) (Brandenburg, 1982), and explained by RPA calculations (Niksic, 2013; Kvasil, 2016; Colo, 2020). It builds on the triaxial QFAM implementation for CDFT (Sun, 2019) and suggests that triaxiality introduces a new coupling mechanism involving hexadecupole vibrations. The predicted resonance peak at 11.5 MeV could be tested experimentally with radioactive beam facilities, given the 226 ms lifetime of \(^{86}\text{Ge}\), and would provide a signature for identifying triaxial deformation in nuclei.
