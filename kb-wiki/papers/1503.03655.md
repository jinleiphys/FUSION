# Rapidity dependence of elliptic and triangular flow in proton-nucleus collisions from collective dynamics

> The rapidity dependence of elliptic, $v_2$, and triangular, $v_3$, flow coefficients in proton-nucleus (p+A) collisions is predicted in hydrodynamics and in a multi-phase transport model (AMPT). We find that $v_n$ ($n=2,3$) on a nucleus side is significantly larger than on a proton side and the ratio between the two, $v_n^{\rm Pb}/v_n^{\rm p}$, weekly depends on the transverse momentum of produced particles.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that in p+Pb collisions, the elliptic ($v_2$) and triangular ($v_3$) flow coefficients are significantly larger on the nucleus-going side than on the proton-going side, and that the ratio $v_n^{\rm Pb}/v_n^{\rm p}$ depends only weakly on the transverse momentum of produced particles, based on both hydrodynamic and AMPT model calculations.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic simulations (with shear viscosity $\eta/s=0.08$ and bulk viscosity $\eta/s=0.04$ for $T<170$ MeV, freeze-out at 150 MeV) and AMPT model calculations (with string melting and a parton cross-section of $\sigma=3$ mb) for p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV. Flow coefficients were extracted using two-particle correlations between forward ($2.5<\eta<4$, Pb-going), backward ($-4<\eta<-2.5$, p-going), and central ($|\eta|<1$) rapidity bins, with centrality defined by wounded nucleon count $N_w \ge 13$ for $0-20\%$ centrality.

## Key numbers

- $v_2$ and $v_3$ are larger on the nucleus-going side ($2.5<\eta<4$) than on the proton-going side ($-4<\eta<-2.5$) for $0-20\%$ central p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV, across the transverse momentum range $0.25<p_\perp<5$ GeV.
- The ratio $v_n^{\rm Pb}/v_n^{\rm p}$ weakly depends on $p_\perp$ in both models, with the hydrodynamic ratio shown for $0.25<p_\perp<5$ GeV.
- In the AMPT model, the ratio $v_2^{\rm Pb}/v_2^{\rm p}$ approaches unity for peripheral collisions (e.g., $40-60\%$ centrality class).
- The AMPT model uses a parton cross-section of $\sigma=3$ mb; cross-sections of $1.5-3$ mb were found sufficient to reproduce data.
- The hydrodynamic calculation uses shear viscosity $\eta/s=0.08$ and bulk viscosity $\eta/s=0.04$ for $T<170$ MeV, with freeze-out at 150 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior hydrodynamic predictions of larger average transverse momentum on the Pb-going side (Bozek 2013) and CMS observations of asymmetric elliptic flow (Granier de Cassagnac 2014) to a full rapidity-dependent prediction of $v_2$ and $v_3$ as functions of $p_\perp$. The results provide a test to distinguish between final-state collective dynamics (hydrodynamics and AMPT) and initial-state saturation effects (color glass condensate), where a nontrivial rapidity dependence of $v_2$ is expected (Dumitru 2014, Dusling 2013). The predictions extend to rapidities accessible by the ALICE muon spectrometer, beyond current CMS measurements.
