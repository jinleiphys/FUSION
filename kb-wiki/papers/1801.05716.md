# Scaling properties of spectra in new exact solutions of rotating, multi-component fireball hydrodynamics

> We describe fireballs that rehadronize from a perfect fluid of quark matter, characterized by the lattice QCD equation of state, to a chemically frozen, multi-component mixture, that contains various kinds of observable hadrons. For simplicity and clarity, we apply a non-relativistic approximation to describe the kinematics of this expansion. Unexpectedly, we identify a secondary explosion that may characterize fireball hydrodynamics at the QCD critical point. After rehadronization, the multi-component mixture of hadrons keeps on rotating and expanding together, similarly to a single component fluid. After kinetic freeze-out, the effective temperature $T_{i}$ of the single-particle spectra of hadron type $h_i$ is found to be a sum of the kinetic freeze-out temperature $T_f$ (that is independent of the hadron type $h_i$) and a term proportional to the mass $m_i$ of hadron type $h_i$. The coefficient of proportionality to $m_i$ is also found to be independent of the hadron type $h_i$ but be dependent on the radial flow and vorticity of collective dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper derives new exact solutions of non-relativistic fireball hydrodynamics for a multi-component hadronic mixture that expands and rotates together, showing that the effective temperature of each hadron species depends linearly on its mass. The authors identify a secondary explosion that may characterize fireball hydrodynamics at the QCD critical point, but note that their non-relativistic approximation limits direct applicability to low transverse momentum and mid-rapidity.

## Method

The authors start from relativistic perfect fluid hydrodynamics equations and apply a non-relativistic approximation (|v|^2 << 1) to obtain a closed system of partial differential equations for both quark matter (high temperature) and chemically frozen multi-component hadronic matter (low temperature). They use a lattice QCD equation of state for the temperature-dependent energy density to pressure ratio κ(T) = ε/p, and assume that in the hadronic phase the enthalpy density is dominated by the sum of rest masses of chemically frozen hadron species. The complicated partial differential equations are reduced to ordinary differential equations by introducing scaling variables with Gaussian density profiles and spatially homogeneous temperature profiles.

## Key numbers

- The effective temperature of hadron type h_i is given by T_i = T_f + m_i ⟨u_T⟩^2, where T_f is the kinetic freeze-out temperature (independent of hadron type) and ⟨u_T⟩ is the average transverse flow (also independent of hadron type)
- The acceleration parameter D in the hadronic matter phase is D = T/⟨m⟩, where ⟨m⟩ = (∑_i m_i n_i)/(∑_i n_i) is the average mass of the multi-component hadronic mixture
- The vorticity vector's non-vanishing component in the laboratory frame is ω_y(r,t) = ω + (ω/2)(X/Z + Z/X), where ω = ω_0 R_0^2/R^2 and R = (X+Z)/2
- The scaling variable s = r_x^2/X^2 + r_y^2/Y^2 + r_z^2/Z^2 + (1/Z^2 - 1/X^2)[(r_x^2 - r_z^2)sin^2(ϑ) + r_x r_z sin(2ϑ)]

## Context

This work extends earlier single-component exact solutions of non-relativistic fireball hydrodynamics, such as the spherically symmetric solution of Csizmadia (1998) and the ellipsoidally symmetric solutions of Akkelin (2000) and Csorgo (2001), which only treated one kind of hadron. The new multi-component generalization solves a challenge that had not been attempted before. The results enable future studies using lattice QCD equations of state with relativistic kinematics and multi-component hadronic matter, building on the Buda-Lund model family of exact solutions.
