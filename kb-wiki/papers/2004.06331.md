# Hadron resonance gas with van der Waals interactions

> An overview of a hadron resonance gas (HRG) model that includes van der Waals interactions between hadrons is presented. Applications of the excluded volume HRG model to heavy-ion collision data and lattice QCD equation of state are discussed. A recently developed quantum van der Waals hadron resonance gas model is covered as well. Applications of this model in the context of the QCD critical point are elaborated.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how a hadron resonance gas (HRG) model extended with van der Waals interactions, both repulsive (excluded volume) and attractive, can describe lattice QCD data and heavy-ion collision observables, but the author emphasizes that the excluded volume parameters are effective phenomenological quantities rather than direct measures of hadronic hard-core radii, due to significant quantum mechanical corrections.

## Method

The author surveys three classes of excluded-volume HRG models: (1) a common eigenvolume parameter for all hadrons, (2) a diagonal model with species-dependent eigenvolumes, and (3) a nondiagonal model with pair-specific excluded-volume parameters. The review then covers the quantum van der Waals (QvdW) extension, which adds attractive mean-field interactions between baryons, and shows how the nucleon-nucleon QvdW parameters are fixed to reproduce nuclear matter ground-state properties (binding energy and saturation density). The multi-component QvdW-HRG model is formulated in the grand canonical ensemble, with pressure given by a sum of ideal-gas terms evaluated at shifted chemical potentials minus a mean-field attraction term.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear matter ground state: binding energy of −16 MeV and normal nuclear density \(n_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) are used to fix the nucleon-nucleon van der Waals parameters: \(a_{NN} = 329\ \text{MeV fm}^3\) and \(b_{NN} = 3.42\ \text{fm}^3\).
* The quantum van der Waals model of symmetric nuclear matter predicts a critical point at \(T_c \simeq 19.7\ \text{MeV}\), \(\mu_c \simeq 908\ \text{MeV}\), and \(n_c \simeq 0.05\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
* Lattice QCD data on net-baryon susceptibilities at \(\mu_B = 0\) are captured by an EV-HRG model with a baryonic eigenvolume parameter \(b = 1\ \text{fm}^3\) common to all species.
* The chiral pseudocritical temperature from lattice QCD is \(T_{pc} \simeq 155\ \text{MeV}\).
* In the QvdW-HRG model fitted to \(\mu_B = 0\) lattice data, the critical point is located at \(T \sim 60\!-\!65\ \text{MeV}\) and \(\mu_B \sim 700\!-\!715\ \text{MeV}\) (Samanta 2017, Sarkar 2018).

## Context

This review extends the ideal HRG model, whose roots go back to Hagedorn (1965), by incorporating repulsive interactions via excluded volumes and attractive interactions via a van der Waals mean field. It builds on earlier work by Albright (2015), Huovinen (2017), and Vovchenko (2017) showing that deviations of lattice QCD susceptibilities from the uncorrelated hadron gas baseline can be attributed to repulsive interactions. The QvdW-HRG model enables studies of critical phenomena in the QCD phase diagram, including the nuclear liquid-gas transition and its effect on fluctuation observables such as net-baryon kurtosis, which can be compared to STAR collaboration data (Adamczyk 2013, Adam 2020).
