# Review on dynamically generated resonances

> We present recent results on the vector meson-vector meson and vector meson-baryon interaction using a unitary approach based on the hidden-gauge Lagrangians. For the vector-vector case we find that 11 states get dynamically generated, corresponding to poles of the scattering matrices on the second Riemann sheet. In the vector-baryon sector we also find 9 states dynamically generated from the vector-baryon octet interaction and 10 from the vector-baryon decuplet interaction. We also report on baryon states found from the interaction of two mesons and a baryon.

# Dynamically generated resonances

## Key claim
Using a unitary approach based on hidden-gauge Lagrangians, the authors find that 11 vector-vector states and 19 vector-baryon states (9 from octet interaction, 10 from decuplet interaction) are dynamically generated as poles of scattering matrices on the second Riemann sheet, but the association with experimentally observed resonances is tentative and depends on matching mass, width, and decay pattern.

## Method
The authors construct transition amplitudes for vector meson-vector meson and vector meson-baryon interactions using hidden-gauge Lagrangians, which provide contact terms, t(u)-channel vector-exchange amplitudes, and direct box amplitudes with two intermediate pseudoscalar mesons. They solve the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation in the on-shell factorization approach, searching for poles on the second Riemann sheet. For vector-baryon interactions, the spin structure from the factorized polarization vectors produces degenerate states with spin-parity 1/2-, 3/2- for octet baryons and 1/2-, 3/2-, 5/2- for decuplet baryons.

## Key numbers
- 11 states dynamically generated from vector-vector interaction across nine strangeness-isospin-spin channels
- For the vector-vector sector: f2(1270) appears at pole position (1275,2) MeV (mass, width); f0(1710) at (1726,28) MeV; K2*(1430) at (1431,1) MeV
- 9 states from vector-baryon octet interaction: N*(1696) with width 92 MeV; Lambda(1783) with width 9 MeV; Sigma(1830) with width 42 MeV
- 10 states from vector-baryon decuplet interaction: Delta(1971) with width 52 MeV; N*(1850) with width 11 MeV; Omega(2445) with width 13 MeV
- For the vector-vector case, two parameters Lambda and Lambda_b are fixed to reproduce widths of f0(1370) and f2(1270); Lambda_b values of 1.4 GeV and 1.5 GeV are used to quantify uncertainties
- A bound state of K Kbar N appears around 1920 MeV with J^P = 1/2+

## Context
This work extends the successful unitary chiral approach previously applied to pseudoscalar meson-baryon interactions (which generated the N*(1535) and two Lambda(1405) states as shown by Kaiser (1995) and others) to the vector meson sector using hidden-gauge Lagrangians developed by Bando et al. The results build on earlier studies by Molina (2008), Geng (2008), and others, and have been tested against radiative decays into gamma gamma (finding good agreement for f2(1270) and f0(1370)), J/psi decays into phi (omega) plus resonances as studied by Martinez Torres (2009), and helicity amplitudes compared to quark model predictions by Sun (2010). The work enables experimental searches for predicted spin partners of already observed states and suggests specific measurements in gamma p reactions to discriminate between 1/2+ and 3/2+ resonance interpretations around 1920 MeV.
