# The Rarita--Schwinger field: renormalization and phenomenology

> We discuss renormalization of propagator of interacting Rarita--Schwinger field. Spin-3/2 contribution after renormalization takes usual resonance form. For non-leading spin-1/2 terms we found procedure, which guarantees absence of poles in energy plane. The obtained renormalized propagator has one free parameter and is a straight generalization of the famous free propagator of Moldauer and Case. Application of this propagator for production of $Δ^{++}(1232)$ in $π^{+}\particle{p}\to π^{+}\particle{p}$ leads to good description of total cross-section and to reasonable agreement with results of partial wave analysis.

## Key claim

The renormalized Rarita-Schwinger propagator, after removing poles from non-leading spin-1/2 terms, yields a good description of the total cross-section for pi+ p -> pi+ p near the Delta++(1232) resonance, but the quality of the fit depends on including a phenomenological form factor with a pole near threshold that may mimic one-nucleon exchange contributions.

## Method

The authors construct a dressed propagator for the interacting Rarita-Schwinger field using a Lambda-basis decomposition that separates spin-3/2 and spin-1/2 components. They impose renormalization conditions that eliminate unphysical poles in the energy plane for the spin-1/2 sector, leaving one free parameter. The propagator is then applied to compute the resonance contribution to pi N scattering via a standard pi N Delta interaction Lagrangian, with a Blatt-Weisskopf form factor introduced to ensure convergence of loop integrals.

## Key numbers

- Delta mass: M_Delta = 1232.0 +/- 0.2 MeV
- Delta width: Gamma_Delta = 113 +/- 1 MeV
- Free parameter A = -0.576 +/- 0.014
- Form factor parameter Lambda = -0.607 +/- 0.012 GeV^2
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom: chi^2/DOF = 0.99
- Fit performed in energy region from threshold up to 1.32 GeV

## Context

This work extends the free Rarita-Schwinger propagator of Moldauer and Case by including renormalization of interacting spin-3/2 and spin-1/2 components. The resulting amplitude produces partial waves (P33, D33, S31, P31) that show reasonable agreement with the partial wave analysis of Arndt (2006). The approach enables systematic inclusion of spin-1/2 background contributions in resonance phenomenology, though the authors note their simple form factor parameterization should ultimately be made consistent with low-energy dynamical dispersion equations as in the work of Schwager, Stichel, and Mitter (1969).
