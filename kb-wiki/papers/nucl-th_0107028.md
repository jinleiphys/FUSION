# Kaon photoproduction: background contributions, form factors and missing resonances

> The photoproduction p(gamma, K+)Lambda process is studied within a field-theoretic approach. It is shown that the background contributions constitute an important part of the reaction dynamics. We compare predictions obtained with three plausible techniques for dealing with these background contributions. It appears that the extracted resonance parameters drastically depend on the applied technique. We investigate the implications of the corrections to the functional form of the hadronic form factor in the contact term, recently suggested by Davidson and Workman (Phys. Rev. C 63, 025210). The role of background contributions and hadronic form factors for the identification of the quantum numbers of ``missing'' resonances is discussed.

## Key claim

The extracted resonance parameters for the $p(\gamma, K^+)\Lambda$ reaction depend drastically on the chosen technique for handling background contributions, and the identification of a "missing" resonance's quantum numbers is premature based on the existing data set alone.

## Method

The authors describe the $p(\gamma, K^+)\Lambda$ process using an effective Lagrangian approach with hadronic degrees of freedom, employing pseudo-scalar coupling for the $K\Lambda N$ vertex. They compare three models for treating background contributions: model A uses Born terms and $t$-channel vector meson exchange with a soft hadronic form factor cutoff; model B adds $u$-channel hyperon resonances ($\Lambda^*(1800)$ and $\Lambda^*(1810)$) to reduce Born strength with realistic cutoff masses; model C ignores broken SU(3) constraints on the coupling constants. The models are fitted to the SAPHIR data set containing 90 differential and 24 total cross section points plus 12 $\Lambda$-recoil polarization asymmetries for photon lab energies from threshold (0.91 GeV) up to 2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The Born terms with point-like couplings and SU(3)-constrained $g_{K\Lambda p}/\sqrt{4\pi}$ between -4.5 and -3.0 and $g_{K\Sigma^0 p}/\sqrt{4\pi}$ between 0.9 and 1.3 produce cross sections that "largely overshoot the data"
- In model A, the best fits were obtained with a cutoff mass $\Lambda$ approaching the imposed under-limit of 0.4 GeV for the hadronic form factors
- In model B, realistic cutoff masses of order 1.5 GeV were reached, with hyperon coupling constants $G_{\Lambda^*(1800)} = -4.38$ and $G_{\Lambda^*(1810)} = -1.75$
- In model C, the best fit gave $g_{K\Lambda p} = -0.40$, far below the SU(3) prediction of -3.75, with a "hard" cutoff mass of 1.8 GeV
- Introducing additional $s$-channel nucleon resonances beyond the standard set produced $\chi^2$ values worse than 8, compared to typical $\chi^2 \approx 2.9$ for the other models
- The $D_{13}(1895)$ resonance, proposed as a "missing" resonance by Mart and Bennhold, has a mass of 1895 MeV and explains the structure at photon lab energies of about 1.5 GeV in the SAPHIR total cross section data

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Mart and Bennhold (2000) who first proposed the $D_{13}(1895)$ as a "missing" resonance in kaon photoproduction, and addresses the criticism by Davidson and Workman (2001) regarding the functional form of the hadronic form factor in the contact term. The authors show that both a $D_{13}$ and a $P_{13}$ resonance can equally well reproduce the structure in the total cross section, and that the extracted coupling constants are heavily model-dependent, making it premature to assign quantum numbers to the "missing" resonance. The study enables future work to focus on measuring polarization observables to further constrain the reaction mechanisms, as the authors note that a complete meson photoproduction experiment needs at least seven observables to constrain the reaction amplitudes at a fixed photon energy.
