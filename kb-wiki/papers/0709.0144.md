# The thermodynamics for a hadronic gas of fireballs with internal color structures and chiral fields

> The thermodynamical partition function for a gas of color-singlet bags consisting of fundamental and adjoint particles in both $U(N_c)$ and $SU(N_c)$ group representations is reviewed in detail. The constituent particle species are assumed to satisfy various thermodynamical statistics. The gas of bags is probed to study the phase transition for a nuclear matter in the extreme conditions. These bags are interpreted as the Hagedorn states and they are the highly excited hadronic states which are produced below the phase transition point to the quark-gluon plasma. The hadronic density of states has the Gross-Witten critical point and exhibits a third order phase transition from a hadronic phase dominated by the discrete low-lying hadronic mass spectrum particles to another hadronic phase dominated by the continuous Hagedorn states. The Hagedorn threshold production is found just above the highest known experimental discrete low-lying hadronic mass spectrum. The subsequent Hagedorn phase undergoes a first order deconfinement phase transition to an explosive quark-gluon plasma. The role of the chiral phase transition in the phases of the discrete low-lying mass spectrum and the continuous Hagedorn mass spectrum is also considered. It is found crucial in the phase transition diagram. Alternate scenarios are briefly discussed for the Hagedorn gas undergoes a higher order phase transition through multi-processes of internal color-flavor structure modification.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the hadronic density of states exhibits a Gross-Witten critical point leading to a third-order phase transition between a discrete low-lying hadronic mass spectrum phase and a continuous Hagedorn state phase, with the Hagedorn threshold production occurring just above the highest known experimental discrete low-lying hadronic mass spectrum, and that this subsequent Hagedorn phase then undergoes a first-order deconfinement phase transition to a quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors construct the grand-canonical partition function for a gas of color-singlet bags containing fundamental quarks and adjoint gluons in both U(N_c) and SU(N_c) group representations, imposing color-singlet constraints via the Haar measure with the Vandermonde determinant. They evaluate the partition function using steepest-descent methods in the large-N_c limit, solving the stationary condition for the color eigenvalue distribution to locate the Gross-Witten critical point, and then derive the micro-canonical density of states via inverse Laplace transform of the grand-canonical ensemble following the Kapusta approach but with explicit internal color symmetry.

## Key numbers

* The third-order phase transition point occurs at a thermal running parameter value of lambda_tilde = 1/2, where the color eigenvalue distribution changes from uniform over the full circle |theta_i| <= pi to uniform only over an incomplete range |theta_i| <= theta_c < pi
* For the U(N_c) group, the trace over the adjoint representation for gluons is given by tr_c R_adj(g^k) = sum_{i != j} cos k(theta_i - theta_j) + N_c, while for SU(N_c) it is sum_{i != j} cos k(theta_i - theta_j) + (N_c - 1)
* The single-particle partition function for fundamental particles and antiparticles in the color-singlet projection involves the factor (2/N_c) sum_{i=1}^{N_c} cos(theta_i) from the trace over fundamental representations

## Context

This work extends the statistical bootstrap model of Hagedorn (1965) and Frautschi (1971) by incorporating explicit internal color symmetry constraints from the bag model approach of Kapusta (1981, 1982). It builds upon the Gross-Witten (1980) solution for the large-N_c limit of lattice gauge theory and the subsequent work by Azakov, Salomonson and Skagerstam (1987) on singular integral equations for quarks and gluons. The paper enables a unified treatment of the Hagedorn phase transition, chiral symmetry restoration, and deconfinement in the QCD phase diagram, connecting to recent work by Dumitru (2004, 2005) on Polyakov loop matrix models and to AdS/CFT connections suggested by Sundborg (2000) and Aharony (2003, 2005).
