# Deuteron polarizations in the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process for finding the gluon transversity

> The gluon transversity distribution in the deuteron is defined by the matrix element between linearly-polarized deuteron states, and it could be investigated in proton-deuteron collisions in addition to lepton-deuteron scattering. The linear polarization of photon is often used, whereas it is rarely used for the spin-1 deuteron. Therefore, it is desirable to express deuteron-reaction cross sections in term of conventional deuteron spin polarizations for actual experimental measurements. In this work, we investigate how proton-deuteron Drell-Yan cross sections are expressed by the conventional polarizations for finding the gluon transversity distribution. In particular, we show that the gluon transversity can be measured in the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process by taking the cross-section difference between the deuteron spin polarizations along the two-transverse axes. Since the gluon transversity does not exist for the spin-1/2 nucleons, a finite gluon transversity of the deuteron could indicate an "exotic" mechanism beyond the simple bound system of the nucleons in the deuteron. Therefore, the gluon transversity is an interesting and appropriate observable to shed light on a new hadronic mechanism in nuclei.

## Key claim

The gluon transversity distribution in the deuteron can be measured in the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process by taking the cross-section difference between deuteron spin polarizations rotated around the two transverse axes, rather than using the rarely-employed linear polarization states of the deuteron.

## Method

The authors derive expressions for the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan cross section using conventional deuteron vector and tensor polarization parameters, defined in the deuteron rest frame. They show that rotating the deuteron spin vector (initially in the \(s_z=+1\) state) around the \(y\)-axis by angle \(\alpha_y\) or around the \(x\)-axis by angle \(\alpha_x\) produces a finite linear-polarization parameter \(S_{TT}^{xx}\) that couples to the gluon transversity distribution \(\Delta_T g_B(x_b)\). The cross-section difference between the two rotations isolates the gluon transversity contribution, while the sum gives the unpolarized plus tensor-polarized contributions.

## Key numbers

* The cross-section difference for extracting gluon transversity is proportional to \(\sin^2 \alpha \, \cos(2\phi)\), where \(\alpha\) is the rotation angle around either transverse axis and \(\phi\) is the azimuthal angle of the virtual photon in the center-of-momentum frame.
* The gluon transversity term in the cross section contains a factor \(S_{B,TT}^{xx} = \frac{1}{2} \sin^2{\alpha_y}\) for rotation around the \(y\)-axis and \(S_{B,TT}^{xx} = -\frac{1}{2} \sin^2{\alpha_x}\) for rotation around the \(x\)-axis.
* The tensor-polarization parameter \(S_{B,LL}\) for either rotation is given by \(\frac{1}{8} \left\{ 1 + 3\cos(2\alpha) \right\}\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical formalism of Kumano and Song (2019), which expressed proton-deuteron Drell-Yan cross sections using linear polarizations of the deuteron, by recasting those results in terms of conventional spin polarizations more practical for experimental measurements. The gluon transversity distribution does not exist for spin-1/2 nucleons, so a finite measurement in the deuteron would indicate exotic mechanisms beyond a simple proton-neutron bound state, complementing studies of tensor-polarized structure functions like \(b_1\) measured by HERMES and planned at JLab. The results enable experimental searches at hadron facilities including Fermilab, J-PARC, GSI-FAIR, and NICA, as well as potential fixed-target measurements at RHIC, LHC, or EIC.
