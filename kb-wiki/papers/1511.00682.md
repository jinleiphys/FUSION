# Vibrational quantisation of the B=7 Skyrmion

> We consider the inclusion of the most important vibrational modes in the quantisation of the dodecahedral $B=7$ Skyrmion. In contrast to a rigid body quantisation, this formalism allows a spin $\frac{3}{2}$ state to lie below the spin $\frac{7}{2}$ state, in agreement with experimental data. There is also a low lying spin $\frac{1}{2}$ state and two spin $\frac{5}{2} $ states. We find that the excited spin $\frac{7}{2}$ state has a smaller root mean square charge radius than the other states. This prediction is an important signature of the Skyrme model, in conflict with more conventional nuclear models.

## Key claim

By including the lowest-energy vibrational mode in the quantisation of the dodecahedral B=7 Skyrmion, the spin 3/2 state can lie below the spin 7/2 state, in agreement with experimental data for the 7Li/7Be isodoublet, a result that rigid-body quantisation cannot reproduce.

## Method

The author constructs an 11-dimensional configuration space consisting of the 5-dimensional vibrational space V5 of the B=7 Skyrmion (spanned by the lowest-energy fivefold-degenerate vibrational mode) together with rotational and isorotational SU(2) degrees of freedom, modulo the dodecahedral symmetry group. The quantum Hamiltonian is the Laplace-Beltrami operator plus the potential energy; the Finkelstein-Rubinstein constraints are imposed on the wavefunction. The problem is reduced to a one-dimensional Schrödinger equation along either a C3-preserving or D5-preserving line in V5, using gradient flow to compute the metric and potential as functions of geodesic distance.

## Key numbers

- The harmonic frequency of the lowest vibrational mode near the origin of V5 is 0.34 (in Skyrme units), compared with 0.302 found in Kim (prior work); the small difference is attributed to different pion masses used.
- For 7Li, the experimental first excited state has spin 1/2 and lies 0.5 MeV above the ground state, while the spin 7/2 state is the second excited state lying 4.6 MeV above the ground state.
- The spin 3/2 state requires one unit of vibrational excitation (its vibrational wavefunction must vanish at s=0), while the spin 7/2 state can occupy the vibrational ground state.

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model quantisation beyond the rigid-body (zero-mode) approach used by Light (prior work) and others, which predicted a spin 7/2 ground state for the B=7 system in conflict with experiment. The inclusion of vibrational modes resolves this failure and connects the Skyrme model to cluster-model ideas, as the vibrational directions correspond to breakup into B=3+B=4 or B=2+B=2+B=3 clusters. The prediction that the excited spin 7/2 state has a smaller root-mean-square charge radius than the other states is presented as a key signature distinguishing the Skyrme model from conventional nuclear models.
