# Dark Nuclei II: Nuclear Spectroscopy in Two-Colour QCD

> We consider two-colour QCD with two flavours of quarks as a possible theory of composite dark matter and use lattice field theory methods to investigate nuclear spectroscopy in the spin $J=0$ and $J=1$ multi-baryon sectors. We find compelling evidence that $J=1$ systems with baryon number $B=2,3$ (and their mixed meson-baryon counterparts) are bound states - the analogues of nuclei in this theory. In addition, we estimate the $σ$-terms of the $J=0$ and $J=1$ single baryon states which are important for the coupling of the theory to scalar currents that may mediate interactions with the visible sector.

## Key claim

The authors find compelling evidence that in two-colour QCD with two flavours, spin-1 (\(J^P=1^+\)) systems with baryon number \(B=2\) and \(B=3\) are bound states (analogues of nuclei), but this result is conditional on the specific quark mass range studied where the vector meson mass is less than twice the pseudoscalar meson mass, and the binding energies per baryon are comparable to those seen in QCD with large quark masses.

## Method

The authors use lattice field theory with standard Wilson gauge and fermion actions for SU(\(N_c=2\)) gauge theory with two degenerate quark flavours, generating gauge configurations via hybrid Monte-Carlo on multiple lattice volumes ranging from \(12^3\times48\) to \(24^3\times72\). They compute two-point correlation functions for single hadron states (pseudoscalar mesons, vector mesons, and their degenerate baryon counterparts) and for multi-hadron systems with baryon number \(n=2,3\) in the \(J=0\) and \(J=1\) sectors, using established contraction methods to extract energy shifts and binding energies from the time dependence of correlators while accounting for thermal effects from finite temporal extent.

## Key numbers

- PCAC quark masses range from \(a m_q = 0.0280(2)(4)\) (ensemble B, \(\beta=2.0\), \(m_0=-0.9490\)) to \(a m_q = 0.2277(2)(1)\) (ensemble F, \(\beta=2.2\), \(m_0=-0.6000\)) on \(L=16\) ensembles.
- Pion masses range from \(a m_\pi = 0.347(6)(13)\) (ensemble B) to \(a m_\pi = 0.9151(13)(6)\) (ensemble D) in lattice units.
- Pion decay constants range from \(a f_\pi = 0.101(3)(5)\) (ensemble B) to \(a f_\pi = 0.259(1)(1)\) (ensemble A) in lattice units.
- The ratio \(m_\pi/m_\rho\) ranges from 0.663(9)(10) (ensemble B) to 0.951(3)(3) (ensemble F).
- Lattice spacings at the common mass ratio \(m_\pi/m_\rho=0.9\) are \(a = 0.35(2)\times10^{-3}\) fm (\(\beta=1.8\)), \(0.24(1)\times10^{-3}\) fm (\(\beta=2.0\)), \(0.19(1)\times10^{-3}\) fm (\(\beta=2.1\)), and \(0.14(2)\times10^{-3}\) fm (\(\beta=2.2\)).
- The dimensionless couplings for a quark flavour \(q\) in a hadron \(H\), \(f_q^{(H)} \equiv \langle H | m_q \overline{q}q|H\rangle/M_H\), are estimated to be \(\sim 0.15\)–\(0.3\) at the quark masses studied.

## Context

This work extends lattice investigations of nuclear binding in QCD (Beane 2010, 2011, 2012, 2013; Yamazaki 2012) to the two-colour theory as a candidate for composite dark matter, building on earlier studies of two-colour QCD spectroscopy (Lewis 2011; Buckley 2012; Hietanen 2014). The finding that bound states analogous to nuclei form in this theory enables phenomenological studies of strongly interacting dark matter, including nuclear reactions and couplings to scalar currents via the computed \(\sigma\)-terms, which are explored in a companion article (Detmold 2014).
