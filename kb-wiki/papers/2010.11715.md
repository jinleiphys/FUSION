# Relativistic $N$-particle energy shift in finite volume

> We present a general method for deriving the energy shift of an interacting system of $N$ spinless particles in a finite volume. To this end, we use the nonrelativistic effective field theory (NREFT), and match the pertinent low-energy constants to the scattering amplitudes. Relativistic corrections are explicitly included up to a given order in the $1/L$ expansion. We apply this method to obtain the ground state of $N$ particles, and the first excited state of two and three particles to order $L^{-6}$ in terms of the threshold parameters of the two- and three-particle relativistic scattering amplitudes. We use these expressions to analyze the $N$-particle ground state energy shift in the complex $φ^4$ theory.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general method for deriving the finite-volume energy shift of N identical relativistic bosons using nonrelativistic effective field theory (NREFT), with relativistic corrections included perturbatively up to order L^{-6}. The method is applied to the ground state of N particles and the first excited state of two and three particles, but the authors note that the derivation can be straightforwardly extended to higher excited states and systems with more particles.

## Method

The authors match the nonrelativistic effective Lagrangian to relativistic scattering amplitudes in the two- and three-body sectors, expressing the effective couplings g1, g2, g3, and eta_3 in terms of observable quantities such as the scattering length a0, effective range r0, and the subtracted three-particle scattering amplitude at threshold. They then use Rayleigh-Schrodinger perturbation theory within the NREFT framework to calculate the finite-volume energy shift, including relativistic corrections from the expansion of the one-particle energy and from derivative interactions. The method is applied to lattice data from the complex phi^4 theory in the symmetric phase for systems with one to five identical bosons at various box sizes L.

## Key numbers

- The two-body effective couplings are matched as: g1 = 8*pi*a0/m, g2 = pi*a0/(2*m^3), and g3 = -(pi*a0^2/(2*m))*(-1/(a0*m^2) + r0), where a0 is the scattering length and r0 is the effective range.
- The finite parts of two-loop diagrams are computed numerically: delta^(d) approximately equals -1.08964 and delta^(e) approximately equals 3.92587.
- The three-particle threshold amplitude (bar script T) is found to be different from zero at 7 sigma significance, taking into account only statistical error.
- In fits to order L^{-6}, the scattering length a0 is fixed to values around 0.428-0.434 depending on the fit (e.g., a0 = 0.428 for the Delta E_3 fit, a0 = 0.434 for the Delta E_4 fit, a0 = 0.433 for the Delta E_5 fit).
- The fit interval for L is [9, 24] in lattice units.

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume formalism for multiparticle systems beyond the nonrelativistic approximation, building on prior work by Gasser (2007) on matching relativistic and nonrelativistic theories and by Pang (2019) on three-particle energy shifts. The method enables extraction of infinite-volume observables (scattering length, effective range, and three-body threshold amplitude) from lattice QCD calculations of multiparticle spectra, and the authors note that the strong correlation between r0 and bar script T observed in fixed-N fits will also appear in QCD calculations. The study of excited states is identified as a promising direction to break this correlation, since relativistic and effective-range corrections contribute at order L^{-5} in excited states while the three-body coupling first appears at order L^{-6}.
