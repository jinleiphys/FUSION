# Exactly solvable pairing model for superconductors with a p+ip-wave symmetry

> We present the exact Bethe ansatz solution for the two-dimensional BCS pairing Hamiltonian with p_x + i p_y symmetry. Using both mean-field theory and the exact solution we obtain the ground-state phase diagram parameterized by the filling fraction and the coupling constant. It consists of three phases denoted weak coupling BCS, weak pairing, and strong pairing. The first two phases are separated by a topologically protected line where the exact ground state is given by the Moore-Read pfaffian state. In the thermodynamic limit the ground-state energy is discontinuous on this line. The other two phases are separated by the critical line, also topologically protected, previously found by Read and Green. We establish a duality relation between the weak and strong pairing phases, whereby ground states of the weak phase are "dressed" versions of the ground states of the strong phase by zero energy (Moore-Read) pairs and characterized by a topological order parameter.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first exact Bethe ansatz solution for the two-dimensional BCS pairing Hamiltonian with p_x + i p_y symmetry, showing that the ground-state phase diagram contains three phases (weak coupling BCS, weak pairing, and strong pairing) separated by two topologically protected lines, with the exact solution revealing a duality between the weak and strong pairing phases that is not captured by mean-field theory alone.

## Method

The authors derived the exact Bethe ansatz solution for the Hamiltonian using the Quantum Inverse Scattering Method with the trigonometric XXZ R-matrix, in contrast to the rational XXX solution used for the s-wave BCS model. They solved the Bethe ansatz equations numerically for finite systems with L energy levels and M Cooper pairs, and also performed mean-field calculations using Bogoliubov transformation to obtain the gap and chemical potential equations. The exact eigenstates are constructed as products of creation operators with rapidities satisfying the Bethe ansatz equations, with the total energy given by a sum over these rapidities.

## Key numbers

- The Read-Green line occurs at filling fraction x_RG = (1 - 1/g)/2, where g = G L is the rescaled coupling constant, independent of momentum distribution and ultraviolet cut-off
- The Moore-Read line occurs at filling fraction x_MR = 1 - 1/g, again independent of momentum distribution and cut-off
- The duality relation between weak and strong pairing phases is x_I + x_II = x_MR = 1 - 1/g, which holds for rational values of g
- For a one-pair state dressed to give the ground state in the weak pairing region, the filling approaches x_MR = 1 - 1/g as L approaches infinity, with the ground-state energy on the MR line being E(g, x_MR) = 0 while the limit from the weak pairing side gives a nonzero energy CE(g)
- The winding number w = 0 for the strong pairing phase (mu < 0) and w = +1 for the weak pairing and weak coupling BCS phases (mu > 0)

## Context

This work extends the mean-field analysis of Read and Green (2000) for the p+ip BCS model, who found a second-order phase transition governed by the chemical potential between weak and strong pairing phases, and Volovik (1999) who showed the topological structure in k-space. The exact solution builds on the Richardson-Gaudin integrability framework developed for the s-wave BCS model by Richardson (1963) and later generalized by Dukelsky et al. (2004). The results enable future exploration of how vortices can be incorporated into similar models, and provide a new example of a zeroth-order quantum phase transition in a many-body system.
