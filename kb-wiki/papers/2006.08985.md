# Modified Excluded Volume Hadron Resonance Gas Model with Lorentz Contraction

> In this work we discuss a modified version of Excluded Volume Hadron Resonance Gas model and also study the effect of Lorentz contraction of the excluded volume on scaled pressure and susceptibilities of conserved charges. We find that the Lorentz contraction, coupled with the variety of excluded volume parameters reproduce the lattice QCD data quite satisfactorily.

# Modified Excluded Volume Hadron Resonance Gas Model with Lorentz Contraction

## Key claim

The authors claim that a modified excluded volume hadron resonance gas model, which incorporates both unequal hard-core radii for different hadron species and Lorentz contraction of the excluded volume, can reproduce lattice QCD data for scaled pressure and charge susceptibilities "quite satisfactorily", but the agreement is partial and depends on the observable, with the strangeness sector showing persistent discrepancies and the best fits requiring specific choices of hadronic radii (baryon radius 0.35 fm, pion radius 0.2 fm, other meson radius 0.3 fm).

## Method

The authors construct a Modified Excluded Volume Hadron Resonance Gas (MEVHRG) model using virial expansion with unequal hard-core radii for mesons, baryons, and antibaryons, and then extend it to a Lorentz-contracted version (LMEVHRG) where the excluded volume is reduced by a factor of m0/√(p²+m0²) in the direction of motion. They compute scaled pressure P/T⁴ and second- and fourth-order susceptibilities of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness at zero chemical potentials, comparing results from pure HRG, standard EVHRG, MEVHRG, and LMEVHRG models against lattice QCD data from Bazavov (2014) and Borsanyi (2018), using all hadron species up to 3 GeV mass from the particle data book.

## Key numbers

- Baryon hard-core radius: Rb = 0.35 fm; pion radius: Rπ = 0.2 fm; other meson radius: Rm = 0.3 fm
- All hadrons up to 3 GeV mass included from the particle data book
- Scaled pressure for LMEVHRG model is higher than for EVHRG and MEVHRG, with repulsive effects becoming prominent above T = 0.12 GeV
- For χ₂ᴮ, all interacting models agree with lattice QCD up to T = 0.18 GeV
- For χ₂ⱽ, LMEVHRG gives the best match with lattice data at medium temperatures; for χ₂ˢ, lattice data overestimates even pure HRG results
- Inclusion of Lorentz contraction yields a maximum deviation of 10% from lattice data for χ₂ⱽ, χ₂ᴮ, and χ₂ˢ, with deviations less than 5% in some cases
- For χ₄ᴮ, interacting models show excellent match with lattice QCD data; χ₄ˢ lattice data matches pure HRG up to T = 0.165 GeV then approaches interacting results

## Context

This work extends the excluded volume hadron resonance gas approach of Alba (2017) and Vovchenko (2016), who studied unequal hadron sizes, and the Lorentz contraction treatment of Oliinychenko (2012), but using a different implementation. The authors note that while pure HRG explains scaled pressure, repulsive interactions are essential for baryon and electric charge susceptibilities, consistent with findings by Vovchenko (2017) that baryon-only repulsion works well for baryon susceptibilities. The paper identifies that the strangeness sector remains problematic, echoing Alba (2017) who argued that the particle data book spectrum may be incomplete for strangeness thermodynamics. The work enables future studies incorporating temperature-dependent masses or hybrid models combining HRG with quark degrees of freedom.
