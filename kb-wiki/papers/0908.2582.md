# Two-Flavor Chiral Perturbation Theory for Hyperons

> The three-flavor chiral expansion for octet baryons has well-known problems with convergence. We show that this three-flavor chiral expansion can be reorganized into a two-flavor expansion thereby eliminating large kaon and eta loop contributions. Issues of the underlying formulation are addressed by considering the effect of strangeness changing thresholds on hyperon masses. While the spin-3/2 hyperon resonances are considerably more sensitive to these thresholds compared to the spin-1/2 hyperons, we demonstrate that in both cases the essential physics can be captured in the two-flavor effective theory by terms that are analytic in the pion mass squared, but non-analytic in the strange quark mass. Using the two-flavor theory of hyperons, baryon masses and axial charges are investigated. Loop contributions in the two-flavor theory appear to be perturbatively under control. A natural application for our development is to study the pion mass dependence of lattice QCD data on hyperon properties.

# Two-Flavor Chiral Perturbation Theory for Hyperons

## Key claim

The three-flavor chiral expansion for octet baryons suffers from poor convergence due to large kaon and eta loop contributions, but this expansion can be reorganized into a two-flavor expansion that eliminates explicit kaon and eta degrees of freedom. The two-flavor theory captures the essential physics of strangeness-changing thresholds through terms that are analytic in the pion mass squared but non-analytic in the strange quark mass, provided one is suitably far from kaon production thresholds.

## Method

The authors perform a matching procedure between SU(3) and SU(2) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT), expanding kaon and eta loop contributions in powers of the pion mass relative to the eta-s meson mass. They compute baryon masses and axial charges at next-to-leading order (NLO) in the two-flavor theory for each strangeness sector separately, using lattice QCD data to determine the NLO low-energy constants where available. The convergence of the expansion is assessed by evaluating the expansion parameter epsilon_{BB'} for each strangeness-changing transition, which depends on the baryon mass splitting and the eta-s meson mass.

## Key numbers

- The Gell-Mann-Okubo mass relation for octet baryons is satisfied experimentally at the level of 0.90% using neutral baryon masses divided by the weighted average octet mass.
- NLO loop contributions to baryon masses in SU(3) HBChPT at scale mu = Lambda_chi are: delta M_N / M_N = -39% for the nucleon, delta M_Lambda / M_Lambda = -67% for the lambda, delta M_Sigma / M_Sigma = -89% for the sigma, and delta M_Xi / M_Xi = -98% for the cascade.
- The expansion parameters epsilon_{BB'} for strangeness-changing transitions range from 0.04 to 0.23, all smaller than the generic SU(3) expansion parameter epsilon ~ m_eta / M_B = 0.5.
- The eta-s meson mass is estimated as m_{eta_s} = 0.69 GeV using LO ChPT and neutral pion and kaon masses.
- The kaon mass is m_K = 0.50 GeV; the baryon mass splittings delta_{BB'} range from 0.13 GeV (Sigma to Xi) to 0.45 GeV (N to Sigma*).
- Axial couplings used in the two-flavor theory: g_A = 1.25, g_{Sigma Sigma} = 0.78, g_{Xi Xi} = 0.24, g_{Delta N} = 1.48, g_{Lambda Sigma*} = 0.91, g_{Sigma Sigma*} = 0.76, g_{Xi Xi*} = 0.69, and g_{Lambda Sigma} = 1.47.
- The NLO local contribution coefficients for axial charges at scale Lambda_chi are: A_{NN} = -12, A_{Sigma Sigma} = -2.9, and A_{Xi Xi} = -0.22.
- The two non-trivial combinations of axial charges independent of local contributions in SU(3) HBChPT are computed as Delta g = -0.0035 and Delta G = -0.017.

## Context

This work extends the program of applying two-flavor chiral perturbation theory to strange baryons, following earlier developments by Roessl (1999), Frink and Meissner (2002), Beane (2003), and others. It directly addresses the well-known convergence problems of SU(3) chiral perturbation theory for octet baryons, which had been documented in prior lattice QCD and phenomenological studies. The two-flavor formulation enables controlled pion mass extrapolations of lattice QCD data for hyperon properties, and the authors identify the need for future lattice QCD calculations of the lambda-sigma axial coupling using isospin twisted boundary conditions as proposed by Tiburzi (2005).
