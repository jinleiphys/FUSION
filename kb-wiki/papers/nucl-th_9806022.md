# $η^{\prime}$ Production in Proton-Proton Collisions near Threshold

> Within the one-pion exchange model we calculate the near-threshold $η^{\prime}$ production from $pp$ collisions including the final state interaction between the protons. Since the description of the data is quite well we conclude that $η, ρ$ or $ ω$ exchange currents either play no role or cancel each other to a large extent in this reaction.

# Wiki Page: η′ Production in Proton-Proton Collisions near Threshold

## Key claim
Within the one-pion exchange model, including the final-state interaction between the two protons brings the calculated near-threshold pp → pp η′ cross section into agreement with experimental data, implying that η, ρ, or ω exchange currents either contribute negligibly or cancel each other out. This conclusion depends on using the average πN → η′N amplitude extracted from experimental data rather than relying on theoretical estimates.

## Method
The authors calculate the pp → pp η′ cross section using a one-pion exchange model, where the production amplitude is constructed from the NNπ coupling constant (g_NNπ = 13.59), a monopole form factor with cutoff Λ = 1.3 GeV, and the πN → η′N amplitude extracted from experimental πN → η′N cross section data. The final-state interaction between the two protons is incorporated using either the Watson-Migdal approximation (with a correction factor parameterized by α = −21.67 MeV and β = 162.9 MeV from the Nijmegen-93 partial wave analysis) or the inverse S-wave Jost function.

## Key numbers
- Excess energy range considered: ε ≤ 10 MeV (where ε = √s − 2m_N − m_η′).
- NNπ coupling constant: g_NNπ = 13.59.
- Form factor cutoff parameter: Λ = 1.3 GeV.
- FSI parameters from effective range expansion: α = −21.67 MeV, β = 162.9 MeV.
- FSI is dominated by S-wave proton-proton scattering for relative momenta of the final protons below approximately 100 MeV/c.

## Context
This work extends the one-pion exchange model previously applied to η production (Sibirtsev and Cassing, 1998) and addresses a discrepancy noted by Hibou (1998), where calculations without FSI underestimated the pp → pp η′ cross section by up to a factor of 2. The inclusion of FSI resolves this discrepancy, enabling future studies of η′ production in proton-neutron collisions, where the different FSI (Laget, 1998) may explain the large ratio of pp → pp η to pn → pn η cross sections near threshold.
