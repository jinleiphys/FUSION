# Deformation effects in the Coulomb breakup of 31Ne

> We present a fully quantum mechanical theory to study the effects of deformation on various reaction observables in the Coulomb breakup of neutron rich exotic medium mass nuclei on heavy targets within the framework of finite range distorted wave Born approximation by using a deformed Woods-Saxon potential. As an application of this theory, we calculate the one-neutron removal cross section, relative energy spectra, parallel momentum distributions and angular distributions in the breakup of 31Ne on Pb and Au targets at 234 MeV/u. We suggest ways to put constraints on the large uncertainty in the one-neutron separation energy of 31Ne and also argue that if 31Ne is indeed a halo nucleus then it should be a deformed one.

## Key claim

The paper argues that if 31Ne is a halo nucleus, it must be a deformed one, and that Coulomb breakup observables can be used to constrain the large uncertainty in its one-neutron separation energy (Sn = 0.29 ± 1.64 MeV) and quadrupole deformation parameter β2, though the available one-neutron removal cross section data cannot conclusively rule out either a 3/2− or 1/2+ ground state spin-parity.

## Method

The authors extended the post-form finite range distorted wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) for Coulomb breakup to include projectile deformation by introducing a quadrupole-deformed Woods-Saxon potential (truncated to first order in β2) as the interaction between the valence neutron and the charged core. The transition amplitude was factorized into a dynamics part (expressed analytically via the Bremsstrahlung integral) and a structure part containing the deformation parameter, with the radial wave function still calculated from the spherical Woods-Saxon potential to maintain analytic tractability. The theory was applied to calculate one-neutron removal cross sections, relative energy spectra, parallel momentum distributions, and angular distributions for 31Ne on Pb and Au targets at 234 MeV/u.

## Key numbers

- One-neutron separation energy of 31Ne: Sn = 0.29 ± 1.64 MeV (from Jurado) or 0.30 ± 1.6 MeV (from Nuclear Data Sheets)
- Beam energy: 234 MeV/u for breakup on Pb and Au targets
- For the 3/2− (p-wave) configuration with β2 = 0.0, 0.2, and 0.5, the Sn ranges reproducing experimental data are 0.24–0.35 MeV, 0.30–0.42 MeV, and 0.43–0.58 MeV, respectively
- For Sn = 0.29 MeV and 0.35 MeV, the β2 values consistent with data are approximately 0.15 and 0.3, respectively
- FWHM of parallel momentum distribution for Sn = 0.29 MeV: 51.24 MeV/c (β2 = 0.0), 45.09 MeV/c (β2 = 0.2), 42.15 MeV/c (β2 = 0.4 and 0.5)
- FWHM of parallel momentum distribution for Sn = 0.35 MeV: 55.38 MeV/c (β2 = 0.0), 48.03 MeV/c (β2 = 0.2), 44.60 MeV/c (β2 = 0.4 and 0.5)
- Grazing angle for 31Ne on Au at 234 MeV/u: 1.2 degrees
- Woods-Saxon parameters: radius r0 = 1.24 fm, diffuseness a = 0.62 fm; depths 50.41 MeV (p-wave) and 29.88 MeV (s-wave) for Sn = 0.29 MeV

## Context

This work extends the FRDWBA Coulomb breakup formalism of Chatterjee and collaborators to include deformation effects, building on prior studies of 31Ne by Nakamura et al. (2009) who measured one-neutron removal cross sections, and by Hamamoto (2010) who used Nilsson model calculations with a deformed Woods-Saxon potential to suggest possible ground state configurations. The calculations also relate to the particle-rotor model studies of Urata et al. and the microscopic optical potential calculations of Minomo et al., all of which investigated deformation in 31Ne. The results enable future experiments to use relative energy spectra and parallel momentum distributions to simultaneously constrain the one-neutron separation energy and quadrupole deformation parameter of 31Ne.
