# Do deviations of neutron scattering widths distribution from the Porter-Thomas law indicate on failure of the Random Matrix theory?

## Key claim

Deviations of neutron scattering width distributions from the Porter-Thomas law can be explained within the random matrix theory framework itself, without invoking non-chaotic mechanisms, provided that resonance overlapping (parameter κ = ⟨Γ⟩/D) is properly accounted for. The paper shows that for overlapping parameter κ ≲ 0.4, the deviations are quantitatively consistent with RMT predictions, and only at κ approaching 1 (the superradiance transition) do long-range correlations cause significant departures.

## Method

The author starts from the extended RMT joint distribution for energies and widths of neutron resonances with one open channel (Sokolov and Zelevinsky 1989) and reformulates it as an analog statistical mechanics problem of interacting particles in two dimensions (ε, ξ). The global "semicircle" level density is separated from local pairwise interactions, and a circularly modified RMT (cmRMT) model is introduced to eliminate boundary effects. The width distribution for a given resonance is computed analytically by integrating over the positions of the two nearest neighbors explicitly (using the Wigner surmise for their spacing distribution) while treating farther resonances as equidistant, and the result is compared to direct numerical simulations of the full joint distribution.

## Key numbers

- Overlapping parameter κ = ⟨Γ⟩/D, where ⟨Γ⟩ is the mean resonance width and D is the mean level spacing; the analytical estimate works well for κ ≲ 0.4
- For κ = 0.05, the analytical estimate of the ratio Ψ(x|κ) = ℙ(x,κ)/W_PT(x) agrees with numerical simulations for N = 101 levels
- For κ = 0.1 and κ = 0.4, the analytical estimate fits numerical data for N = 321 levels with central domain k ∈ (-30, 30)
- At κ ≳ 0.4, width distributions of central levels become dependent on total number of levels N, and asymptotic behavior does not occur even at N ≳ 200-300
- The superradiance transition is expected at κ ∼ 1 (Sokolov and Zelevinsky 1989)
- For κ ≲ 0.3, the system is far from the width collectivization border at κ ∼ 1

## Context

This work extends the RMT framework of Sokolov and Zelevinsky (1989) for joint statistics of overlapping neutron resonances, and addresses the challenge posed by Koehler et al. (2012) who claimed that deviations from the Porter-Thomas law in neutron scattering data indicate failure of RMT. The paper improves upon the equidistant-level approximation of Shchepkin (2012) by explicitly integrating over nearest-neighbor spacing fluctuations, and provides a practical analytical formula for the width distribution that can be compared with experimental data. The results enable experimental tests of whether observed deviations from Porter-Thomas are consistent with RMT predictions for overlapping resonances, without invoking non-chaotic mechanisms such as neutron strength function effects (Weidenmüller 2010), parent-daughter correlations (Volya 2011), or nonstatistical γ-decays (Volya 2015).
