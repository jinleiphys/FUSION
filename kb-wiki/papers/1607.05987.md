# Testing Lorentz Symmetry using Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We consider the low-energy effects of a selected set of Lorentz- and CPT-violating quark and gluon operators by deriving the corresponding chiral effective lagrangian. Using this effective lagrangian, low-energy hadronic observables can be calculated. We apply this to magnetometer experiments and derive the best bounds on some of the Lorentz-violating coefficients. We point out that progress can be made by studying the nucleon-nucleon potential, and by considering storage-ring experiments for deuterons and other light nuclei.

# Testing Lorentz Symmetry using Chiral Perturbation Theory

## Key claim
The paper derives the low-energy chiral effective lagrangian for a selected set of Lorentz- and CPT-violating quark and gluon operators of mass-dimension five, and uses it to place the current best bounds on some Lorentz-violating coefficients from magnetometer experiments, but notes that the bounds on the D coefficient are many orders of magnitude weaker than the natural Planck-scale expectation and do not probe that scale.

## Method
The authors construct the chiral effective lagrangian in the SO(4) formalism of chiral perturbation theory, matching the transformation properties of the dimension-five Lorentz-violating operators under chiral SO(4) and discrete C, P, and T symmetries. They then calculate the leading-order nucleon and pion-nucleon operators, and apply the resulting effective lagrangian to compute energy-level shifts in atoms for comparison with comagnetometer data. The low-energy constants are estimated using naive dimensional analysis.

## Key numbers
- The natural expected size of Lorentz-violating coefficients is of order 1/Λ_LV, where Λ_LV is commonly identified with the Planck scale Λ_PL ≈ 10^19 GeV.
- Best bounds on components of the C and H coefficients from a ³He/¹²⁹Xe comagnetometer experiment are on the order of 10⁻³³ GeV⁻¹.
- Best bound on some components of the D coefficient is on the order of 10⁻¹⁸ GeV⁻¹, which does not probe the expected size of 1/Λ_PL.
- The pion decay constant F_π ≈ 185 MeV sets the chiral symmetry breaking scale Λ_χ ≈ 2πF_π ~ 1 GeV.
- The low-energy constants are estimated as c^± = O(Λ_χ F_π) and h = O(Λ_χ²).

## Context
This work extends the Standard-Model Extension (SME) framework for Lorentz and CPT violation into the nonperturbative QCD regime by adapting chiral perturbation theory, following the conceptual basis established in the author's prior paper (Noordmans, 2016). The results enable improved constraints on Lorentz-violating coefficients from existing magnetometer data, and point toward future improvements by studying the nucleon-nucleon potential and storage-ring experiments for deuterons and other light nuclei, analogous to successful chiral EFT treatments of other discrete-symmetry violations reviewed in the literature.
