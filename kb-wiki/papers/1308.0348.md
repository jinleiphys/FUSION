# Effective field theory as a limit of R-matrix theory for light nuclear reactions

> We study the zero channel radius limit of Wigner's R-matrix theory for two cases, and show that it corresponds to non-relativistic effective quantum field theory. We begin with the simple problem of single-channel n-p elastic scattering in the 1S0 channel. The dependence of the R matrix width and level energy on the channel radius, "a" for fixed scattering length a0 and effective range r0 is determined. It is shown that these quantities have a simple pole for a critical value of the channel radius. The 3H(d,n)4He reaction cross section, analyzed with a two-channel effective field theory in the previous paper, is then examined using a two-channel, single-level R-matrix parametrization. The resulting S matrix is shown to be identical in these two representations in the limit that R-matrix channel radii are taken to zero. This equivalence is established by giving the relationship between the low-energy constants of the effective field theory (couplings and mass) and the R-matrix parameters (reduced width amplitudes and level energy). An excellent three-parameter fit to the observed astrophysical factor S is found for "unphysical" values of the reduced widths.

# Effective field theory as a limit of R-matrix theory for light nuclear reactions

## Key claim

The zero channel radius limit of Wigner's R-matrix theory is shown to be identical to non-relativistic effective quantum field theory, but only if the reduced-width amplitudes are allowed to become pure imaginary (unphysical) values with negative squared reduced widths, which corresponds to using a "wrong-sign" Lagrangian in the EFT description.

## Method

The authors first study single-channel neutron-proton elastic scattering in the 1S0 channel using a single-level R-matrix parametrization, deriving analytic expressions for the dependence of the R-matrix width and level energy on the channel radius for fixed scattering length and effective range. They then analyze the 3H(d,n)4He reaction cross section using a two-channel, single-level R-matrix parametrization and take the channel radii to zero, establishing the relationship between the R-matrix parameters (reduced width amplitudes and level energy) and the low-energy constants of the effective field theory (couplings and mass). The equivalence is demonstrated by showing the S-matrix becomes identical in both representations in the zero-radius limit.

## Key numbers

- For 1S0 np scattering: scattering length a0 = -23.7 fm and effective range r0 = 2.75 fm; the critical channel radius pole occurs at a_p = 1.30 fm
- At zero channel radius for 1S0 np scattering: E_lambda(0) = -1.27 MeV and g^2(0) = -30.2 MeV-fm [g^2(0)/(hbar c) = -0.153]
- For 3S1 np scattering: scattering length 5.411 fm and effective range 1.74 fm; pole at a_p = 1.07 fm; at zero radius E_lambda(0) = +8.81 MeV and g^2(0) = -47.7 MeV-fm [g^2(0)/(hbar c) = -0.242]
- Best four-parameter R-matrix fit to the dt reaction data at a = 7 fm: chi^2 = 34.94 (chi^2/DOF = 0.713), with E_lambda = 179 +/- 5 keV, gamma_d^2 = 324 +/- 12 keV, gamma_n^2 = 12.2 +/- 0.2 keV, and boundary conditions B_d = -0.59, B_n = -2
- The Bohr radius equivalent for the dt system is b0 = 34.62 fm

## Context

This work extends the companion paper by Brown, Hale, and Paris (2013) which derived a two-channel effective field theory expression for the 3H(d,n)4He reaction cross section. The study establishes an exact identity between the zero-radius limit of single-level R-matrix theory and the EFT description with local interactions mediated by a single unstable field, building on earlier work by Resler (1992) and Karnakov et al. (1990, 1991) who found that excellent fits could be obtained with single R-matrix levels. The results enable a direct correspondence between R-matrix reduced-width amplitudes and EFT coupling constants, and show that the "wrong-sign" Lagrangian in EFT compensates for describing finite-range nuclear forces with zero-range interactions. The authors note that the zero-radius R-matrix description is similar to the model-independent effective range expansion of Karnakov et al. (1990) but requires only three parameters instead of four.
