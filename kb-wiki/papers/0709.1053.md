# Exact Solutions of the Equations of Relativistic Hydrodynamics Representing Potential Flows

> We use a connection between relativistic hydrodynamics and scalar field theory to generate exact analytic solutions describing non-stationary inhomogeneous flows of the perfect fluid with one-parametric equation of state (EOS) $p = p(ε)$. For linear EOS $p = κε$ we obtain self-similar solutions in the case of plane, cylindrical and spherical symmetries. In the case of extremely stiff EOS ($κ=1$) we obtain ''monopole + dipole'' and ''monopole + quadrupole'' axially symmetric solutions. We also found some nonlinear EOSs that admit analytic solutions.

## Key claim
The paper uses a known connection between relativistic hydrodynamics and scalar field theory to generate exact analytic solutions for non-stationary, inhomogeneous perfect fluid flows, but the solutions are restricted to potential flows (where the four-velocity is proportional to a gradient of a scalar function) and many exhibit light-cone singularities that limit their domain of validity.

## Method
The authors reduce the relativistic hydrodynamic equations for a perfect fluid with a one-parametric equation of state \(p = p(\varepsilon)\) to a single scalar field equation by requiring that the four-velocity be proportional to the gradient of a scalar function. For the extremely stiff equation of state \(p = \varepsilon\), this reduces to the linear wave equation, whose solutions are constructed using plane waves, spherical waves, and multipole expansions. For nonlinear equations of state and the linear equation of state \(p = \kappa \varepsilon\), self-similar ansätze of the form \(\varphi = \varphi(\sigma)\) with \(\sigma = (t^2 - r^2)/2\) or \(\varphi = \varphi(r/t)\) are used to obtain ordinary differential equations that yield analytic solutions.

## Key numbers
- For the extremely stiff EOS (\(p = \varepsilon\)) with plane symmetry, the scaling solution gives \(\varepsilon = 2A^2(t^2 - x^2)^{-1}\) and \(v = x/t\) inside the light cone \(|x| < t\).
- For the extremely stiff EOS with spherical symmetry and a combination of outgoing and ingoing waves (\(n=1\)), the solution inside the light cone \(t > r\) yields \(\varepsilon = p = 8A^2/(t^2 - r^2)^3\) and \(v = r/t\).
- For the linear EOS \(p = \kappa \varepsilon\) with scaling ansatz, the energy density is \(\varepsilon = C / \tau^{(1+\kappa)(1+n)}\) with \(\tau = \sqrt{t^2 - r^2}\), \(v = r/t\), and \(0 < r < t\), where \(n=0,1,2\) for plane, cylindrical, and spherical symmetry.
- For the linear EOS with self-similar ansatz \(\varphi = \varphi(r/t)\) outside the light cone (\(0 < t < r\)), the energy density is \(\varepsilon = S^\alpha\) with \(S = C^2 / [2 r^{2n\kappa} (r^2 - t^2)^{1 - n\kappa}]\) and \(v = t/r\).

## Context
This work extends the known scaling solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics for the linear EOS \(p = \kappa \varepsilon\) found by Hwa (1974), Cooper et al. (1975), Chiu (1975), and Bjorken (1983) to include non-spherical geometries via multipole expansions and to cover nonlinear equations of state. The solutions are related to the "accelerating" solutions of Csörgő et al. (2003) and the solutions of Nagy (2007), with the self-similar solution outside the light cone corresponding to equations (24)-(25) of Nagy (2007). The results enable testing of numerical relativistic hydrodynamic codes and provide analytic models for theoretical studies of elementary particle multiple production and astrophysical outflows.
