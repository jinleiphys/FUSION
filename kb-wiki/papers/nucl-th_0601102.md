# Spin-isospin stability of nuclear matter

> We calculate the density-dependent spin-isospin asymmetry energy $J(k_f)$ of nuclear matter in the three-loop approximation of chiral perturbation theory. The interaction contributions to $J(k_f)$ originate from one-pion exchange, iterated one-pion exchange, and irreducible two-pion exchange with no, single, and double virtual $Δ$-isobar excitation. We find that the approximation to $1π$-exchange and iterated $1π$-exchange terms (which leads already to a good nuclear matter equation of state by adjusting an emerging contact-term) is spin-isospin stable, since $J(k_{f0})\simeq 24 {\rm MeV}>0$. The inclusion of the chiral $πNΔ$-dynamics, necessary in order to guarantee the spin-stability of nuclear matter, keeps this property intact. The corresponding spin-isospin asymmetry energy $J(k_f)$ stays positive even for extreme values of an undetermined short-distance parameter $J_5$ (whose possible range we estimate from realistic NN-potentials). The largest positive contribution to $J(k_f)$ (a term linear in density) comes from a two-body contact-term with its strength fitted to the empirical nuclear matter saturation point.

## Key claim

The approximation to one-pion exchange and iterated one-pion exchange (which already yields a good nuclear matter equation of state) is spin-isospin stable, with a positive spin-isospin asymmetry energy at saturation density, and this stability persists after including chiral πNΔ-dynamics even for extreme values of the undetermined short-distance parameter J5.

## Method

The spin-isospin asymmetry energy J(kf) of nuclear matter was calculated in the three-loop approximation of chiral perturbation theory. Interaction contributions were derived from one-pion exchange, iterated one-pion exchange, and irreducible two-pion exchange with no, single, and double virtual Δ-isobar excitation, using dimensional regularization and a twice-subtracted dispersion relation for the two-pion exchange contributions. The short-distance parameter J5 was estimated from the spectroscopic low-energy constants of the CD-Bonn, Nijm-II, and AV-18 realistic NN-potentials.

## Key numbers

- At saturation density (kf0 = 2mπ, ρ0 = 0.173 fm⁻³), the fourth-order approximation (1π-exchange and iterated 1π-exchange) gives J(kf0) = 23.9 MeV, indicating spin-isospin stability.
- The largest positive contribution to J(2mπ) = 23.9 MeV comes from the term linear in density (Eq. 13), amounting to 59.2 MeV at saturation density.
- The largest negative contribution is -32.8 MeV from the iterated 1π-exchange Fock diagram with two medium insertions (Eq. 6).
- Including chiral πNΔ-dynamics with J5 = 0 gives J(2mπ) = 69.5 MeV, with two-body Fock and three-body Hartree contributions totaling 51.3 MeV (30.6 MeV + 20.7 MeV).
- Estimates of J5 from realistic NN-potentials yield J5 = -1.34 (CD-Bonn), -0.57 (Nijm-II), and -0.94 (AV-18), with average J5 = -0.95.
- Even with the extreme choice J5 = -19, the spin-isospin asymmetry energy remains positive, preserving spin-isospin stability.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory approach to nuclear matter previously developed by Lutz (1999) and Kaiser (2002) for the equation of state, and by Kaiser (2005) for spin-stability. It addresses the open question of whether nuclear matter is stable against correlated spin-isospin deformations, complementing earlier studies within Brueckner theory using the Reid soft-core NN-potential by Dabrowski (1975). The finding that spin-isospin stability is robust enables further application of the chiral framework to nuclear matter properties, though foundational questions remain regarding the convergence of the chiral expansion and the relation of fitted short-distance parameters to few-nucleon systems.
