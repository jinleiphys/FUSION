# Time-Dependent Superfluid Band Theory for the Inner Crust of Neutron Stars: Current Status and Future Challenges

> In this contribution, current status and future prospects of our ongoing project is summarized. In the inner crust of neutron stars, a variety of crystalline structures may emerge, as a result of competition of Coulomb and nuclear interactions, which are immersed in a sea of superfluid neutrons. The best quantum mechanical approach to study properties of dripped neutrons under a periodic potential is the band theory of solids. Concerning the band structure effects on transport properties of neutrons, however, situation is complicated and there has not been established a clear consensus yet. To provide a robust conclusion on the band structure effects, we have developed a fully-microscopic time-dependent band theory based on time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT), taking full account of Fermionic superfluditiy. We have successfully developed a parallel computational code and applied it to the slab phase of nuclear matter. We introduce ongoing works and discuss possible future directions.

## Key claim

The authors claim that fully self-consistent superfluid band theory calculations for the slab phase of nuclear matter show the neutron effective mass is *reduced* (anti-entrainment, m_n^star/m_n < 1), contrary to earlier non-self-consistent calculations that predicted entrainment (m_n^star/m_n > 1), but they hedge that this result currently applies only to the 1D slab geometry and that the situation for 2D and 3D geometries remains unknown.

## Method

The authors formulate a time-dependent superfluid band theory by imposing Bloch's boundary condition on quasiparticle wave functions in the superfluid local density approximation (SLDA) equation, which is formally similar to the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) or Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equation. They apply this framework to the slab phase of nuclear matter using a Skyrme-type energy density functional, solving for the quasiparticle wave functions and band structure in one spatial dimension. The macroscopic neutron effective mass is extracted either from the curvature of the bands (static method) or from real-time dynamics where a constant external force is applied to protons and the resulting collective masses of the slab are computed.

## Key numbers

* Neutron effective mass m_n^star/m_n = 0.58 for the slab phase at baryon number density n_b = 0.07 fm^-3, from fully self-consistent superfluid band theory calculations (Yoshimura 2023).
* Neutron effective mass m_n^star/m_n = 0.59 for the slab phase at n_b = 0.04 fm^-3 with fixed proton fraction Y_p = 0.1, from self-consistent non-superfluid TDDFT (Sekizawa 2022).
* Neutron effective mass m_n^star/m_n = 0.65–0.75 for the slab phase at n_b = 0.07–0.08 fm^-3, from fully self-consistent static band theory without superfluidity (Kashiwaba 2019).
* For comparison, earlier non-self-consistent 3D calculations predicted m_n^star/m_n = 1.07–15.4 at n_b = 0.03–0.086 fm^-3 (Chamel 2005) and m_n^star/m_n = 1.21–13.6 at n_b = 0.0003–0.08 fm^-3 (Chamel 2012).
* The number of quasiparticle wave functions for a typical 1D slab calculation is estimated at 2,880,000; for a 2D rod phase the estimate exceeds 1 billion.

## Context

This work extends the fully self-consistent static band theory of Kashiwaba and Nakatsukasa (2019) and the time-dependent band theory of Sekizawa (2022) by incorporating neutron superfluidity via the (time-dependent) superfluid local density approximation. It directly contradicts the earlier non-self-consistent band calculations of Carter (2005) and Chamel (2005, 2012), which predicted large entrainment effects (m_n^star/m_n > 1), and instead finds anti-entrainment (m_n^star/m_n < 1) for the slab phase. The next steps are to extend the framework to 2D and 3D geometries (rod and bubble phases), to finite temperatures for studying pasta melting and neutrino opacities, and to finite magnetic fields for magnetar applications.
