# Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay, the Inverted Hierarchy and Precision Determination of theta(12)

> Ruling out the inverted neutrino hierarchy with neutrinoless double beta decay experiments is possible if a limit on the effective mass below the minimal theoretically possible value is reached. We stress that this lower limit depends strongly on the value of the solar neutrino mixing angle: it introduces an uncertainty of a factor of 2 within its current 3 sigma range. If an experiment is not background-free, a factor of two in effective mass corresponds to a combined factor of 16 improvement for the experimental parameters running time, detector mass, background level and energy resolution. Therefore, a more precise determination of theta(12) is crucial for the interpretation of experimental results and the evaluation of the potential and requirements for future experiments. We give the required half-lifes to exclude (and touch) the inverted hierarchy regime for all double beta decay isotopes with a Q-value above 2 MeV. The nuclear matrix elements from 6 different groups and, if available, their errors are used and compared. We carefully put the calculations on equal footing in what regards various convention issues. We also use our compilation of matrix elements to give the reachable values of the effective mass for a given half-life value.

## Key claim

Ruling out the inverted neutrino hierarchy with neutrinoless double beta decay experiments requires reaching a limit on the effective mass below its minimal theoretically possible value, but this lower limit depends strongly on the solar neutrino mixing angle theta_12, introducing an uncertainty of a factor of 2 within its current 3-sigma range.

## Method

The authors compiled nuclear matrix element calculations from six different groups (QRPA, Nuclear Shell Model, Interacting Boson Model, Generating Coordinate Method, projected-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model, and pseudo-SU(3)) for all 11 neutrinoless double beta decay isotopes with Q-value above 2 MeV. They carefully put the calculations on equal footing by scaling all matrix elements to common values of the axial-vector coupling g_A = 1.25 and nuclear radius parameter r_0 = 1.2 fm, and used consistent phase space factors from Suhonen (1998). The required half-lives to exclude and to touch the inverted hierarchy regime were then calculated for different values of sin^2 theta_12.

## Key numbers

- The lower limit of the effective mass in the inverted hierarchy varies from 0.0100 eV to 0.0240 eV depending on sin^2 theta_12 and other oscillation parameters within their 3-sigma ranges
- For sin^2 theta_12 = 0.270, the minimal effective mass is 0.0196 eV (minimal) to 0.0240 eV (maximal)
- For sin^2 theta_12 = 0.318 (best-fit), the minimal effective mass is 0.0154 eV to 0.0189 eV
- For sin^2 theta_12 = 0.380, the minimal effective mass is 0.0100 eV to 0.0123 eV
- A factor of 2 uncertainty in effective mass translates to a factor of 4 in lifetime reach and a factor of 16 in the combination of experimental parameters (running time, detector mass, background level, energy resolution)
- The phase space factor G^{0nu} for Ge-76 is 0.623 x 10^{-14} yr^{-1}, for Xe-136 is 4.31 x 10^{-14} yr^{-1}, for Mo-100 is 4.36 x 10^{-14} yr^{-1}
- The Nuclear Shell Model matrix element for Ge-76 is 2.81, for Xe-136 is 2.19, for Te-130 is 2.65
- The GERDA and MAJORANA experiments have expected sensitivity of 6.0 x 10^{27} years for Ge-76
- The CUORE experiment has expected sensitivity of 6.5 x 10^{26} years for Te-130
- The EXO experiment has expected sensitivity of 6.4 x 10^{25} years (near) and 8.0 x 10^{26} years (far) for Xe-136

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Petcov (2005) and others on the lower limit of the effective mass in the inverted hierarchy, quantifying how the uncertainty in theta_12 from Schwetz (2010) affects experimental requirements. The authors provide a consistent comparison of nuclear matrix elements from six different groups (Faessler, Suhonen, Caurier, Iachello, Rodriguez, and the PHFB collaboration) that had previously used different conventions for g_A and r_0. The results enable experimental collaborations to evaluate the required sensitivity for their chosen isotope and to understand how improved measurements of theta_12 from proposed experiments such as SNO+, CLEAN, HERON, or LENS would reduce the uncertainty in the physics reach of neutrinoless double beta decay searches.
