# Systematic study of two-proton radioactivity with a screened electrostatic barrier

> In this study, a phenomenological model is proposed based on Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) theory and applied to investigate the two-proton ($2p$) radioactive half-lives of nuclei near or beyond the proton drip line. The total diproton-daughter nucleus interaction potential is composed of the Hulthen-type electrostatic term and the centrifugal term. The calculated $2p$ radioactive half-lives can accurately reproduce the existing 10 experimental datasets of five true $2p$ radioactive nuclei with $σ$ = 0.736. In addition, we extend this model to predict the half-lives of possible $2p$ radioactive nuclei whose $2p$ radioactivity is energetically allowed or observed but not yet quantified in NUBASE2016. The predicted results are in agreement with those obtained using the Gamow-like model, generalized liquid drop model, Sreeja formula, and Liu formula.

## Key claim

The authors propose a phenomenological model using a Hulthen-type electrostatic potential to account for the electrostatic screening effect in two-proton radioactivity, and they claim it reproduces experimental half-lives with a standard deviation of sigma = 0.736, which is a 13.0% improvement over the best previous model (Gamow-like model with sigma = 0.846). However, the model contains one adjustable parameter (the screening parameter a) that is fitted to the same experimental data used for validation.

## Method

The authors apply the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) semiclassical approximation to calculate the penetration probability of a diproton through a barrier composed of a Hulthen-type electrostatic potential (replacing the usual Coulomb potential) plus a Langer-modified centrifugal term. The screening parameter a = 2.9647 x 10^-2 fm^-1 is determined by minimizing the standard deviation between calculated and experimental half-lives for 10 datasets from five true two-proton emitters (19Mg, 45Fe, 48Ni, 54Zn, 67Kr). The model is then used to predict half-lives for 18 additional nuclei whose two-proton radioactivity is energetically allowed but not yet quantified.

## Key numbers

* Screening parameter a = 2.9647 x 10^-2 fm^-1, determined by fitting to 10 experimental datasets of five true two-proton radioactive nuclei.
* Standard deviation sigma = 0.736 between calculated and experimental half-lives for the 10 datasets, compared to sigma = 0.846 for the Gamow-like model, sigma = 0.852 for the generalized liquid drop model, sigma = 1.221 for the Sreeja formula, and sigma = 0.851 for the Liu formula.
* For 45Fe with Q2p = 1.100 MeV, the calculated log10(T1/2) = -2.32 s versus experimental -2.40 s (from GSI 2002 data).
* For 67Kr with Q2p = 1.690 MeV, the calculated log10(T1/2) = -0.95 s versus experimental -1.70 s.
* Predicted half-lives range from log10(T1/2) = -16.76 s for 10N (l=1, Q2p=1.300 MeV) to -1.13 s for 47Co (l=0, Q2p=1.042 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the use of the Hulthen potential, previously applied to alpha decay and proton radioactivity by Deng et al. (recent years) and others, to the case of two-proton radioactivity. The model improves upon the Gamow-like model of Sreeja (2019), the generalized liquid drop model, and the empirical formulas of Sreeja and Liu (2019) by incorporating electrostatic screening effects that these earlier approaches neglected. The predictions for 18 possible two-proton emitters from AME2016 provide targets for future experimental searches at facilities such as GANIL and GSI.
