# Dynamical freeze-out criterion in event-by-event hydrodynamics

> In hydrodynamical modelling of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions the freeze-out is typically assumed to take place on a surface of constant temperature or energy density. In this work we apply a dynamical freeze-out criterion, which compares the hydrodynamical expansion rate with the pion scattering rate, to an event-by-event ideal hydrodynamics at the full RHIC collision energy. We present hadron spectra and elliptic flow calculated using (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics, and show the differences between constant temperature and dynamical freeze-out criteria. We find that when the freeze-out ratio is fixed to one, the different freeze-out criteria lead to slightly different spectra and v_2(p_T) in the event-by-event calculations.

# Dynamical freeze-out criterion in event-by-event hydrodynamics

## Key claim
When the freeze-out ratio (expansion rate over scattering rate) is fixed to one, the dynamical freeze-out criterion leads to slightly different spectra and elliptic flow compared to constant-temperature freeze-out in event-by-event ideal hydrodynamics at full RHIC energy; however, the effects are small and the authors note it is unknown whether the observed ~10% reduction in pion elliptic flow survives if the Knudsen number is treated as a free parameter rather than fixed to one.

## Method
The authors applied a dynamical freeze-out criterion comparing the hydrodynamical expansion rate (theta) with the pion scattering rate (Gamma) to (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics with boost invariance and finite net baryon density, using the s95p-PCE-v1 equation of state with chemical freeze-out at 150 MeV. Initial conditions were generated from a Monte Carlo Glauber model with 75% binary and 25% wounded nucleon profiles, smeared with 0.8 fm Gaussians, and averaged over 1000 fluctuating events; the scattering rate was calculated semiclassically for a chemically frozen hadron-resonance gas using cross sections from the UrQMD hadron cascade, and freeze-out surfaces were defined by constant Knudsen number K = 1 versus constant temperature T = 120 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Initial time of evolution: tau_0 = 0.6 fm
- Fluctuation size parameter (Gaussian width): Sigma = 0.8 fm
- Chemical freeze-out temperature: T_chem = 150 MeV
- Constant temperature freeze-out baseline: T_f = 120 MeV
- Collision system: Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Dynamical freeze-out criterion: Knudsen number K = theta/Gamma = 1
- Pion v_2(p_T) reduced by ~10% when using dynamical criterion compared to constant temperature freeze-out

## Context
This work extends earlier applications of dynamical freeze-out criteria in hydrodynamics (Bondorf 1978, Hung 1997, Eskola 2007) by implementing the criterion in event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions rather than averaged ones, and by using a chemically frozen equation of state. The authors compare their results to blast-wave fits from the STAR collaboration (Adams 2003) and note that the centrality dependence of temperature and flow velocity from the dynamical criterion follows similar slopes to the blast-wave fit. The study enables future work where the freeze-out Knudsen number could be treated as a free parameter to match measured spectra, and raises the question of whether the ~10% reduction in pion elliptic flow survives such tuning.
