# Feeddown contributions from unstable nuclei in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We estimate the feeddown contributions from decays of unstable $A = 4$ and $A = 5$ nuclei to the final yields of protons, deuterons, tritons, $^3$He, and $^4$He produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} > 2.4$ GeV, using the statistical model. The feeddown contribution effects do not exceed 5% at LHC and top RHIC energies due to the large penalty factors involved, but are substantial at intermediate collision energies. We observe large feeddown contributions for tritons, $^3$He, and $^4$He at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} \lesssim 10$ GeV, where they may account for as much as 70% of the final yield at the lower end of the collision energies considered. Sizable ($>10$%) effects for deuteron yields are observed at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} \lesssim 4$ GeV. The results suggest that the excited nuclei feeddown cannot be neglected in the ongoing and future analysis of light nuclei production at intermediate collision energies, including HADES and CBM experiments at FAIR, NICA at JINR, RHIC beam energy scan and fixed-target programmes, and NA61/SHINE at CERN. We further show that the freeze-out curve in the $T$-$μ_B$ plane itself is affected significantly by the light nuclei at high baryochemical potential.

## Key claim

Feeddown from decays of unstable A=4 and A=5 nuclei contributes substantially (up to 70%) to final yields of tritons, ³He, and ⁴He at collision energies √sNN ≲ 10 GeV, but does not exceed 5% at LHC and top RHIC energies due to large penalty factors.

## Method

The authors used the ideal hadron resonance gas (HRG) model implemented in the Thermal-FIST package, adding A=4 and A=5 excited nuclear states with their known spin, parity, energy levels, and decay branching ratios from Refs. Tilley (1992) and Tilley (2002). They calculated the feeddown contributions as the sum over all unstable states of the average number of each stable particle species produced in their decays, along the chemical freeze-out curve of Vovchenko (2015) for collision energies √sNN > 2.4 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Feeddown contributions do not exceed 5% at LHC and top RHIC energies
- Feeddown may account for as much as 70% of the final yield of tritons, ³He, and ⁴He at the lower end of collision energies considered (√sNN ≲ 10 GeV)
- Sizable (>10%) effects for deuteron yields are observed at √sNN ≲ 4 GeV
- About 10% of final proton yield comes from decays of excited nuclei at √sNN = 2.4 GeV (HADES energy)
- The penalty factor is larger than 10 at μB < 600 MeV and reaches values of order 300-400 at the LHC
- The ratio Op,d,t = Np Nt / Nd² saturates at about 0.96 at high energies when feeddown is included, compared to about 0.9 without excited nuclei
- At μB ≃ 800 MeV, the difference in temperature T is almost a factor of 2 between the version without nuclei and the one including nuclei for the E/N = 1 GeV freeze-out criterion

## Context

This work extends the estimates of Shuryak (2019) on feeddown from excited ⁴He states into a full thermal model calculation incorporating ⁴H, ⁴Li, and A=5 excited states (⁵H, ⁵He, ⁵Li). It is consistent with the quantum statistical model analysis of Hahn (1986) for intermediate collision energies with S/A ≲ 5. The results indicate that excited nuclei feeddown cannot be neglected in ongoing and future experiments including HADES, CBM at FAIR, NICA at JINR, RHIC beam energy scan, and NA61/SHINE at CERN. The non-monotonic collision energy dependence of the Op,d,t ratio demonstrates that such behavior is possible without invoking the QCD critical point.
