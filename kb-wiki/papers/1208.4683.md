# Time evolution of the sQGP with hydrodynamic models

> The strongly interacting Quark-Gluon-Plasma (sQGP) created in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions, can be described by hydrodynamic models. Low energy hadrons are created after the so called freeze-out of this medium, thus their distributions reveal information about the final state of the sQGP. Photons are created throughout the evolution, so their distributions carry information on the whole expansion and cool down. We show results from an analytic, 1+3 dimensional perfect relativistic hydrodynamic solution, and compare hadron and photon observables to RHIC data. We extract an average equation of state of the expanding quark matter from this comparison. In the second part of this paper, we generalize the before mentioned analytic solution of relativistic perfect fluid hydrodynamics to arbitrary temperature-dependent Equation of State. We investigate special cases of this class of solutions, in particular, we present hydrodynamical solutions with an Equation of State determined from lattice QCD calculations.

# Time evolution of the sQGP with hydrodynamic models

## Key claim

The paper shows that an analytic, 1+3 dimensional perfect relativistic hydrodynamic solution can simultaneously describe hadron and direct photon observables from RHIC Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, yielding an average speed of sound of the expanding quark matter; however, this average EoS parameter may mask a temperature-dependent speed of sound, as demonstrated by new solutions that accommodate arbitrary temperature-dependent equations of state including those from lattice QCD.

## Method

The author used an existing ellipsoidally symmetric analytic solution of relativistic perfect fluid hydrodynamics with a constant equation of state to calculate source functions for hadron and photon emission, then fitted these to PHENIX data on pion invariant momentum distributions, HBT radii, elliptic flow, and direct photon spectra. In the second part, the author generalized this solution to arbitrary temperature-dependent equation of state by deriving new parametric forms for the temperature evolution, entropy density, and conserved number density, and applied these using the lattice QCD equation of state from the Budapest-Wuppertal group (Borsanyi 2010).

## Key numbers

- Speed of sound extracted from photon fits: \(c_s = 0.36 \pm 0.02_{\text{stat}} \pm 0.04_{\text{syst}}\) (from \(\kappa = 7.9 \pm 0.7_{\text{stat}} \pm 1.5_{\text{syst}}\))
- Initial temperature of the fireball center: \(T_i = 507 \pm 12_{\text{stat}} \pm 90_{\text{syst}}\) MeV at 0.7 fm/\(c\) (maximum \(t_i\) within 95% probability)
- Freeze-out proper-time from hadron fits: \(\tau_0 = 7.7 \pm 0.1\) fm/\(c\)
- Central freeze-out temperature from hadron fits: \(T_0 = 199 \pm 3\) MeV (from \(N_1\) and HBT hadron data) or \(204 \pm 7\) MeV (from elliptic flow hadron data)
- Example calculation with lattice QCD EoS: for \(\tau_f = 8\) fm/\(c\) and \(\tau_{\text{init}} = 1.5\) fm/\(c\), with \(T_f = 170\) MeV, the initial temperature \(T_{\text{init}} \approx 550\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the ellipsoidally symmetric hydrodynamic model of Csorgo (2003) and the hadron and photon observable calculations of Csanad (2009, 2011) by fitting to PHENIX 200 GeV Au+Au data from Adler (2003, 2004) and Adare (2008, 2011). The extracted average speed of sound agrees with lattice QCD calculations (Borsanyi 2010) and earlier hadronic data analyses (Adare 2006, Lacey 2006). The new solutions for general temperature-dependent EoS are the first of their kind and enable direct use of lattice QCD equations of state to compute the time evolution of temperature, allowing extraction of initial conditions from final-state observables. The predicted photon HBT radii (with \(R_{\text{out}}\) significantly larger than \(R_{\text{side}}\)) provide a testable signature for future measurements.
