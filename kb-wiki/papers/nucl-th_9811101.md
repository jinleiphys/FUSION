# Effective interactions for the nuclear shell model

> Various perturbative and non-perturbative many-body techniques are discussed in this work. Especially, we will focus on the summation of so-called Parquet diagrams with emphasis on applications to finite nuclei. Here, the subset of two-body Parquet equations will be discussed. A practical implementation of the corresponding equations for studies of effective interactions for finite nuclei is outlined.

## Key claim

This work outlines how perturbative and non-perturbative many-body techniques, particularly the summation of Parquet diagrams and the \(\hat{Q}\)-box folded-diagram method, can be used to derive effective interactions for shell-model calculations starting from the free nucleon-nucleon interaction, with the caveat that practical implementations for finite nuclei are still being developed and that three-body forces may be needed for systems with many valence nucleons.

## Method

The paper reviews the derivation of effective interactions within degenerate Rayleigh-Schrödinger perturbation theory, using projection operators \(P\) (model space) and \(Q\) (excluded space). It details the summation of ladder diagrams via the \(G\)-matrix (Bethe-Brueckner-Goldstone equation) to handle short-range correlations, the \(\hat{Q}\)-box method for folded diagrams, and the Tamm-Dancoff (TDA) and random-phase (RPA) approximations for long-range particle-hole correlations. The self-consistent summation of all three channels (particle-particle, particle-hole, and hole-hole) through the Parquet equations is presented as the ultimate goal, with angular-momentum recoupling formulas given for the \([12]\), \([13]\), and \([14]\) channels.

## Key numbers

*   Shell-model calculations can now handle systems with some 10 million basis states.
*   In Sn isotopes, one may have up to 31 valence particles or holes interacting via an effective two-body interaction.
*   The major neutron shell between neutron numbers 50 and 82 is being filled beyond the \(^{100}\)Sn closed shell core.
*   Typical interparticle distances for the \(NN\) interaction are of the order of 1–2 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier perturbative approaches to effective interactions, such as the \(\hat{Q}\)-box folded-diagram formulation of Kuo and co-workers (1990, 1995) and the particle-particle RPA studies of Ellis, Mavromatis and Müther (1991, 1995). It aims to enable large-scale shell-model calculations in the \(1p0f\) shell and \(2s1d0g_{7/2}\) shells (including the \(0h_{11/2}\) intruder state) for systems with many valence particles, such as the tin isotopes, where the reliability of realistic two-body effective interactions can be tested against experimental excitation spectra and binding energies.
