# Investigation of entanglement in $N = Z$ nuclei within no-core shell model

> In this work, we explore the entanglement structure of two $N = Z$ nuclei, $^{20}$Ne and $^{22}$Na using single-orbital entanglement entropy within the No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) framework for two realistic interactions, INOY and N$^3$LO. We begin with the determination of the optimal frequencies based on the variation of ground-state (g.s.) binding energy with NCSM parameters, $N_{max}$ and $\hbar Ω$, followed by an analysis of the total single-orbital entanglement entropy, $S_{tot}$, for the g.s. of $^{20}$Ne and $^{22}$Na. Our results show that $S_{tot}$ increases with $N_{max}$ and decreases with $\hbar Ω$ after reaching a maximum. We use $S_{tot}$ to guide the selection of an additional set of optimal frequencies that can enhance electromagnetic transition strengths. We also calculate the low-energy spectra and $S_{tot}$ for four low-lying states of $^{20}$Ne and six low-lying states of $^{22}$Na. Finally, we calculate a few $E2$ and one $M1$ transition strengths, finding that N$^3$LO provides better results for $B(E2; 5^+_1 \to 3^+_1$) and INOY performs well for the $B(M1; 0_1^+ \to 1_1^+)$ transition in the $^{22}$Na nucleus while considering the first set of optimal frequencies. We also observe that the second set of optimal frequencies enhances electromagnetic transition strengths, particularly for the states with large and comparable $S_{tot}$. Also, for both nuclei, the $S_{tot}$ for INOY and N$^3$LO are close while considering the second set of optimal frequencies, suggesting that the calculated $S_{tot}$ are more dependent on $\hbar Ω$ than the interactions employed for the same model space defined by the $N_{max}$ parameter.

## Key claim

The total single-orbital entanglement entropy \(S_{tot}\) for the ground states of \(^{20}\)Ne and \(^{22}\)Na is more dependent on the harmonic oscillator frequency \(\hbar\Omega\) than on the specific realistic interaction (INOY or N\(^3\)LO) when using the second set of optimal frequencies, and large and comparable \(S_{tot}\) values between initial and final states can significantly enhance electromagnetic transition strengths.

## Method

The authors performed no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for the \(N=Z\) nuclei \(^{20}\)Ne and \(^{22}\)Na using two realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions: INOY (inside non-local, outside Yukawa) and N\(^3\)LO (next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order chiral interaction). The Okubu-Lee-Suzuki (OLS) transformation was used to soften the interactions and derive effective Hamiltonians within model spaces up to \(N_{max}=4\) (28 harmonic oscillator orbitals). Ground-state binding energies were computed for \(\hbar\Omega\) from 8 to 28 MeV to determine a first set of optimal frequencies (20 MeV for INOY, 14 MeV for N\(^3\)LO), and the variation of total single-orbital entanglement entropy \(S_{tot}\) with \(N_{max}\) and \(\hbar\Omega\) was used to select a second set of optimal frequencies (10 MeV for both interactions in \(^{20}\)Ne; 8 MeV for INOY and 10 MeV for N\(^3\)LO in \(^{22}\)Na). Low-energy spectra, \(S_{tot}\) for individual states, and selected \(E2\) and \(M1\) transition strengths were then calculated at both frequency sets.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state \(S_{tot}\) of \(^{20}\)Ne at \(N_{max}=4\) and \(\hbar\Omega=8\) MeV: 6.584 (INOY) and 6.598 (N\(^3\)LO).
- Ground-state \(S_{tot}\) of \(^{22}\)Na at \(N_{max}=4\) and \(\hbar\Omega=8\) MeV: higher than for \(^{20}\)Ne at the same \(\hbar\Omega\) and \(N_{max}\).
- For the first set of optimal frequencies, \(S_{tot}\) for INOY is on average 14.9% lower than N\(^3\)LO for the four low-lying states of \(^{20}\)Ne, and 15.4% lower for the six low-lying states of \(^{22}\)Na.
- \(B(E2; 2^+_1 \to 0^+_1)\) in \(^{20}\)Ne: experimental 65.4(32) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\); INOY at 20 MeV gives 10.35 \(e^2\)fm\(^4\); INOY at 10 MeV gives 35.19 \(e^2\)fm\(^4\).
- \(B(E2; 4^+_1 \to 2^+_1)\) in \(^{20}\)Ne: experimental 71.0(64) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\); INOY at 20 MeV gives 12.57 \(e^2\)fm\(^4\); INOY at 10 MeV gives 44.41 \(e^2\)fm\(^4\).
- \(B(E2; 5^+_1 \to 3^+_1)\) in \(^{22}\)Na: experimental 19.0(15) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\); N\(^3\)LO at 14 MeV gives 7.15 \(e^2\)fm\(^4\); N\(^3\)LO at 10 MeV gives 2.75 \(e^2\)fm\(^4\).
- \(B(M1; 0^+_1 \to 1^+_1)\) in \(^{22}\)Na: experimental 4.96(18) \(\mu_N^2\); INOY at 20 MeV gives 4.37 \(\mu_N^2\); INOY at 8 MeV gives 16.32 \(\mu_N^2\).

## Context

This work extends prior entanglement studies in nuclear systems, such as the investigation of mode entanglement in \(^{18}\)O by the authors of Ref. QI3, single-orbital entanglement in \(^8\)Be by the authors of Ref. QI4, and proton-neutron entanglement in \(sd\)- and \(fp\)-shell nuclei by the authors of Ref. QI9. It also builds on the authors' own previous NCSM calculations for \(^{20}\)Ne and \(^{22}\)Na (Sarma and Srivastava, 2023) and on the work of the authors of Ref. QI13, who found that proton-neutron entanglement in \(N=Z\) systems is higher than in \(N\neq Z\) systems using the USDB interaction. The results enable a deeper understanding of how NCSM parameters and interaction choices affect wavefunction structure and observable predictions, and suggest that entanglement entropy can serve as a guide for selecting optimal frequencies to enhance electromagnetic transition strengths in \(N=Z\) nuclei.
