# Asymmetric 1+1-dimensional hydrodynamics in collision

> The possibility that particle production in high-energy collisions is a result of two asymmetric hydrodynamic flows is investigated, using the Khalatnikov form of the 1+1-dimensional approximation of hydrodynamic equations. The general solution is discussed and applied to the physically appealing "generalized in-out cascade" where the space-time and energy-momentum rapidities are equal at initial temperature but boost-invariance is not imposed. It is demonstrated that the two-bump structure of the entropy density, characteristic of the asymmetric input, changes easily into a single broad maximum compatible with data on particle production in symmetric processes. A possible microscopic QCD interpretation of asymmetric hydrodynamics is proposed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that asymmetric (boost-non-invariant) initial conditions for 1+1-dimensional hydrodynamic flow can evolve into a single broad rapidity distribution compatible with data from symmetric collisions, provided the two asymmetric components are linearly superposed; the two-bump structure of the initial entropy density is smoothed out by hydrodynamic diffusion during the expansion.

## Method

The authors use the Khalatnikov form of the 1+1-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic equations, which are linearized via a hodograph transformation to a Klein-Gordon (Telegraphic) equation for a potential. They solve this equation for general Cauchy initial conditions and apply it to a "generalized in-out cascade" where space-time rapidity equals momentum rapidity only at the initial temperature, thereby relaxing boost invariance. The entropy density as a function of rapidity is then computed from the solution and evaluated at freeze-out temperature.

## Key numbers

- The initial entropy distribution is parametrized as \( h(x) = (1-x/x_{max})^a (1+x/x_{max})^b \exp[(x/x_{max}-1)/c] \) with \( a=b=2 \) and \( c = \{0.3, 0.4, 0.5\} \), where \( x_{max} = t_{max} = \log(T_0/T_f) \).
- The entropy vs. rapidity distributions are shown for 5 temperature ratios \( T_0/T_f = 2^k \) with \( k = 0 \) to 4.
- The difference \( \eta - y \) (space-time rapidity minus momentum rapidity) remains below 2-3% of \( y \) throughout most of the evolution, reaching at most 10% at the very edge of phase space.
- The proper time at freeze-out for the symmetric two-component solution is approximately constant in the central rapidity region for not-too-large \( T_0/T_f \).

## Context

This work extends the Khalatnikov approach to hydrodynamics (Khalatnikov, 1954) and the "in-out cascade" (Bjorken, 1983) by allowing asymmetric initial conditions that violate boost invariance. It builds on recent developments of the Khalatnikov equation by Beuf (2009) and Peschanski (2009). The results support the two-source description of particle production used in wounded constituent models (Bialas, 1976; Bialas and Czyz, 2005) and provide a hydrodynamic basis for forward-backward multiplicity correlations and directed flow observed in heavy-ion collisions. The paper enables further study of longitudinal dynamics in asymmetric collisions such as deuteron-gold reactions.
