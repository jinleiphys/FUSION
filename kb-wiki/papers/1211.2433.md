# Phenomenology of quarkyonic percolation at FAIR

> We will give an introduction to the concept of quarkyonic matter, presenting an overview of what is meant by this term in the literature. We will then argue that the quarkyonic phase, as defined in the original paper, is a percolation-type phase transition whose phase transition line is strongly curved in $ρ_B-N_c$ space, where $N_c$ is the number of colors and $ρ_B$ the baryon density. With a toy model estimate, we show that it might be possible to obtain a percolating but confined phase at $N_c=3,N_f=2$ at densities larger than one baryon per one baryon size. We conclude by discussing how this phase can be observed at FAIR.

## Key claim

The quarkyonic phase, originally defined as confined matter at high baryon density, is a percolation-type phase transition whose transition line is strongly curved in baryon-density–number-of-colors space; a percolating but confined phase might be possible at \(N_c=3, N_f=2\) at densities larger than one baryon per baryon size, but the toy model shows that at the chemical potential \(\mu_Q \simeq \Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}\) the critical number of colors required for percolation is \(N_c \simeq \order{10}\), so the existence of quarkyonic matter in our world remains an open possibility rather than a certainty.

## Method

The authors model the transition between normal nuclear matter and quarkyonic matter using percolation theory, positing a generic interaction propagator that falls off at distances larger than \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}^{-1}\) and has interaction probability \(\sim \lambda/N_c\). They integrate physical baryons into a square lattice and perform a renormalization-group analysis to obtain critical percolation probabilities as functions of baryon density and number of colors \(N_c\), comparing these to deconfinement curves derived from the condition \(\mu_Q^{\text{deconfinement}} \simeq \sqrt{N_c/N_f}\,\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}\).

## Key numbers

- At leading order, the chemical potential required for deconfinement is \(\mu_Q^{\text{deconfinement}} \simeq \sqrt{N_c/N_f}\,\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}\).
- At the chemical potential \(\mu_Q \simeq \Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}\), the critical number of colors needed to trigger a percolation transition is \(N_c \simeq \order{10}\), meaning \(N_c=3\) is not sufficient at that density.
- The percolation critical number of colors \(N_c\) is shown for regular hexagonal packing as a function of \(\lambda\) and \(r_T\) at fixed baryon density (Fig. 3); the specific numerical values for \(\lambda\) and \(r_T\) are not given in the text.
- Two-dimensional plots in the (baryon density, \(N_c\)) plane show percolation curves versus deconfinement curves at various temperatures \(\theta = T/T_c\) for \(N_f=1\) and \(N_f=3\) (Fig. 4); no explicit numerical values for the curves are provided in the text.

## Context

This work extends the concept of quarkyonic matter introduced by McLerran and Pisarski (2007) by interpreting the quarkyonic phase transition as a percolation phenomenon, contrasting with earlier ideas such as a confined yet chirally restored phase, chirally inhomogeneous phases, a deconfined but chiral-broken plasma of constituent quarks, and large-\(N_c\) generalizations of nuclear matter. The percolation-based toy model enables a systematic study of the phase diagram in \(\rho_B\)-\(N_c\) space and suggests that dilepton spectral functions, particularly dips at \(M \simeq \rho_B^{1/3}\) due to Bloch-like behavior, could serve as experimental signatures at FAIR, with quantitative predictions requiring further work combining the Dirac equation with classical baryon distributions from models such as uRQMD.
