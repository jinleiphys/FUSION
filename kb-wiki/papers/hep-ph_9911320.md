# Pion-nucleon scattering and the nucleon sigma term in an extended sigma model

> A modified linear sigma model that allows for $g_A = 1.26$ by addition of vector and pseudovector $πN$ coupling terms was discussed by Bjorken and Nauenberg and by Lee. In this extended linear sigma model the elastic $π$N scattering amplitudes satisfy the relevant chiral low-energy theorems, such as the Weinberg-Tomozawa relation for the isovector scattering length and in some cases Adler's "consistency condition". The agreement of the isospin symmetric $πN$ scattering length with experiment is substantially improved in this extended sigma model as compared with the original one. We show that the nucleon sigma term ($Σ_N$) in the linear- and the extended sigma models with three different kinds of chiral symmetry breaking terms are identical. Within the tree approximation the formal operator expression for the $Σ_N$ term and the value extracted from the $πN$ scattering matrix coincide. Large values of $Σ_N$ are easily obtained without any $s\bar s$ content of the nucleon. Using chiral rotations the Lagrangian of this extended sigma model reproduces the lowest-order $πN$ chiral perturbation theory Lagrangian.

## Key claim

The extended linear sigma model, which allows the nucleon axial coupling constant \(g_A\) to differ from unity (specifically \(g_A = 1.26\)) by adding vector and pseudovector \(\pi N\) coupling terms, yields a substantially improved isospin-symmetric \(\pi N\) scattering length compared to the original Gell-Mann–Levy model, and the nucleon sigma term \(\Sigma_N\) extracted from the scattering amplitude in the tree approximation is identical to the operator definition, with large values of \(\Sigma_N\) obtainable without any \(s\bar{s}\) content in the nucleon.

## Method

The authors extended the linear sigma model by adding a non-renormalizable derivative-coupling term (analogous to a Pauli term) that allows \(g_A \neq 1\) while preserving the chiral charge algebra. They computed the elastic \(\pi N\) scattering amplitude in the tree approximation from the four lowest-order Feynman diagrams, using three different chiral symmetry breaking (\(\chi\)SB) terms (\(\varepsilon_1 \sigma\), \(-\varepsilon_2 \pi^2\), \(-\varepsilon_3 \bar{\psi}\psi\)) to generate the pion mass. The scattering lengths and the nucleon sigma term \(\Sigma_N\) were then evaluated from the amplitude and compared with the operator definition via the double commutator of the axial charge with the \(\chi\)SB Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

* The axial coupling constant is set to \(g_A = 1.26\).
* The isospin-symmetric scattering length \(a_0^{(+)}\) is given by \(a_0^{(+)} \simeq \frac{g_{\pi N}^2}{4\pi(1+m_\pi/M)} \left(\frac{m_\pi}{M}\right) \left[ -\left(\frac{m_\pi}{2M}\right)^2 - g_A^{-2} \left(\frac{m_\pi}{m_\sigma}\right)^2 \right] \frac{1}{m_\pi}\) in the \(\varepsilon_2 = \varepsilon_3 = 0\) limit.
* The isospin-antisymmetric scattering length \(a_0^{(-)}\) approximates to \(a_0^{(-)} \simeq \frac{g_{\pi N}^2}{8\pi(1+m_\pi/M)} \left(\frac{m_\pi}{M}\right)^2 \frac{1}{g_A^2 m_\pi}\) (the Weinberg-Tomozawa result).
* The Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner (GMOR) relation is given as \(m_\pi^2 f_\pi^2 = -[m_u^0 \langle 0|\bar{u}u|0\rangle + m_d^0 \langle 0|\bar{d}d|0\rangle]\).
* The bare nucleon mass is estimated as \(\bar{M}_N^0 = \frac{3}{2}(m_u^0 + m_d^0) \simeq 23\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the original Gell-Mann–Levy linear sigma model (Gell-Mann and Levy, 1960) by incorporating an extension due to Bjorken and Nauenberg and Lee (1968) that allows \(g_A \neq 1\), addressing a known phenomenological weakness of the original model (where \(g_A = 1\)). It builds on the analysis of Campbell (1979) for the sigma term and scattering lengths in the linear sigma model, and on studies of the quark condensate in nuclear matter (Delorme et al., 1996; Vautherin and Davesne, 1999). The results enable improved tree-level predictions for \(\pi N\) scattering and the nucleon sigma term without invoking strange-quark content, and are relevant for studies of pion condensation and nuclear matter properties.
