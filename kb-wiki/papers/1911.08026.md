# Time-dependent approaches to open quantum systems

> Couplings of a system to other degrees of freedom (that is, environmental degrees of freedom) lead to energy dissipation when the number of environmental degrees of freedom is large enough. Here we discuss quantal treatments for such energy dissipation. To this end, we discuss two different time-dependent methods. One is to introduce an effective time-dependent Hamiltonian, which leads to a classical equation of motion as a relationship among expectation values of quantum operators. We apply this method to a heavy-ion fusion reaction and discuss the role of dissipation on the penetrability of the Coulomb barrier. The other method is to start with a Hamiltonian with environmental degrees of freedom and derive an equation which the system degree of freedom obeys. For this, we present a new efficient method to solve coupled-channels equations, which can be easily applied even when the dimension of the coupled-channels equations is huge.

# Time-dependent approaches to open quantum systems

## Key claim
Two time-dependent methods for quantum friction are presented and compared: a phenomenological quantum friction model using an effective Hamiltonian, and a more microscopic system-plus-bath (Caldeira-Leggett) model solved with a new efficient coupled-channels basis. The phenomenological model yields a penetrability that does not reach unity at high energies but saturates around 0.9, which the authors note is peculiar to the quantum friction model and differs from classical mechanics where penetrability can only be 0 or 1.

## Method
The first method uses the Kostin model with a coordinate-dependent friction coefficient, solving the time-dependent Schrodinger equation with a wave packet approach for the 16O + 208Pb fusion reaction. The second method employs the Caldeira-Leggett Hamiltonian and introduces a new basis constructed from Bessel functions to expand the total wave function, reducing the dimension of the coupled-channels equations even when the number of environmental oscillator modes is large or continuous.

## Key numbers
- Coulomb barrier height V_B = 74.5 MeV for the 16O + 208Pb system, using an optical potential with V_0 = -901.4 MeV, R_v = 8.44 fm, a_v = 0.664 fm, W_0 = -30 MeV, R_w = 6.76 fm, a_w = 0.4 fm
- Friction coefficient parameter gamma_0 = 4.7 x 10^{-23} s/MeV used in the surface friction model
- Energy width sigma_E = 0.5 MeV used for the initial Gaussian energy distribution
- Penetrability with friction saturates at approximately 0.9 at high energies, compared to unity without friction
- For the damped harmonic oscillator: hbar omega_S = 2 eV, V_I = 1 eV, hbar Omega = 4 eV, with initial conditions q_0/q_S = -1, sigma_0/q_S = 1/sqrt(2), p_0 q_S/hbar = 0
- Coupled-channels calculations for the damped harmonic oscillator used K = 20 Bessel function basis states and phonon truncation at N_max = 5, with contributions from 5-phonon states remaining small throughout the time range shown

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous time-dependent wave packet approach to quantum friction Hamiltonians (Tokieda and Hagino, 2017) and their new efficient coupled-channels method (Tokieda and Hagino, 2019). It builds on the Caldeira-Leggett model (Caldeira and Leggett, 1983) for quantum tunneling suppression and the surface friction model successfully applied to above-barrier fusion reactions and deep inelastic scatterings (Frobrich and Lipperheide, 1996). The phenomenological approach connects to earlier quantum friction models by Kostin (1972) and others (Kan and Griffin, 1972; Albrecht, 1975). The new efficient basis method enables treatment of large-dimensional coupled-channels problems that were previously intractable, potentially allowing quantum descriptions of heavy-ion deep inelastic collisions and fusion reactions for superheavy element synthesis.
