# Determining astrophysical three-body radiative capture reaction rates from inclusive Coulomb break-up measurements

> A relationship between the Coulomb inclusive break-up probability and the radiative capture reaction rate for weakly-bound three-body systems is established. This direct link provides a robust procedure to estimate the reaction rate for nuclei of astrophysical interest by measuring inclusive break-up processes at different energies and angles. This might be an advantageous alternative to the determination of reaction rates from the measurement of $B(E1)$ distributions through exclusive Coulomb break-up experiments. In addition, it provides a reference to assess the validity of different theoretical approaches that have been used to calculate reaction rates. The procedure is applied to $^{11}$Li ($^{9}$Li+n+n) and $^6$He ($^{4}$He+n+n) three-body systems for which some data exist.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a direct mathematical relationship between the inclusive Coulomb break-up probability of a weakly-bound three-body system and its astrophysical radiative capture reaction rate, valid only in the region where first-order dipole Coulomb interaction is dominant and the Equivalent Photon Method holds (semiclassical regime with negligible nuclear forces and higher-order Coulomb effects).

## Method

The authors derive an analytical relation connecting the reduced break-up probability \(P_r(t)\) (where \(t\) is the collision time, a function of scattering angle and incident energy) to the three-body radiative capture reaction rate \(\langle R_3(\varepsilon)\rangle(T)\). They express \(P_r(t)\) as an exponential expansion \(P_r(t) \simeq e^{-|\varepsilon_B|t}(b_0 + b_1 t + b_2 t^2 + \dots)\), fit the parameters to experimental inclusive break-up data, and then use a second derivative transformation to obtain the reaction rate as a function of temperature. The method is applied to existing inclusive break-up data for \(^{11}\)Li (on \(^{208}\)Pb) and \(^{6}\)He (on Pb targets).

## Key numbers

* For \(^{11}\)Li: the quadratic fit parameters from the reduced break-up probability data (collision time range \(t = 5\) to \(15\) MeV\(^{-1}\)) are \(b_0 = 7.8\) e\(^2\)fm\(^2\)MeV\(^{-1}\), \(b_1 = -0.4\) e\(^2\)fm\(^2\), \(b_2 = 0.02\) e\(^2\)fm\(^2\)MeV, using the experimental two-neutron separation energy \(|\varepsilon_B| = 0.37\) MeV.
* For \(^{11}\)Li: the method yields reaction rates in the temperature range from 0.7 to 2.3 GK, corresponding to collision times from \(t = 15\) to \(t = 5\) MeV\(^{-1}\).
* For \(^{6}\)He: the quadratic fit parameters from break-up data (collision time range approximately 2.77 to 3.74 MeV\(^{-1}\)) are \(b_0 = 2.01\) e\(^2\)fm\(^2\)MeV\(^{-1}\), \(b_1 = -0.03\) e\(^2\)fm\(^2\), \(b_2 = -0.13\) e\(^2\)fm\(^2\)MeV, using the experimental two-neutron separation energy \(|\varepsilon_B| = 0.975\) MeV.
* For \(^{6}\)He: the temperature range covered by the data is 3.1 to 4.2 GK; to reach \(T = 1\) GK, break-up measurements starting at \(\theta_{min} = 9.5^\circ\) (collision time 11.6 MeV\(^{-1}\)) would be needed for \(^{6}\)He+\(^{206}\)Pb at \(E_{lab} = 18\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the method of Fernandez-Garcia et al. (2013) for obtaining \(B(E1)\) distributions from low-energy Coulomb excitation to directly determine astrophysical reaction rates, providing an alternative to exclusive Coulomb break-up experiments (e.g., Nakamura et al. 2006, Aumann et al. 1999) which have large uncertainties near threshold. The approach enables estimation of reaction rates for short-lived nuclei where direct measurements are impossible, such as the \(^4\)He(\(2n,\gamma\))\(^6\)He reaction relevant for r-process nucleosynthesis in neutron star mergers (Bartlett et al. 2006), and could be extended to other systems like \(\alpha(\alpha n,\gamma)^9\)Be or \(^{15}\)O(\(2p,\gamma\))\(^{17}\)Ne.
