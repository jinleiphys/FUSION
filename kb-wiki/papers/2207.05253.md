# Deep insight into charge equilibration and the effects on producing neutron-rich isotopes around N = 126 in the multinucleon transfer reactions

> The dynamics of the charge equilibration (CE) and the effects on the production of the neutron-rich isotopes around $N = 126$ in multinucleon transfer reactions are still not well understood. In this Letter, we investigate the mechanism of the CE from different viewpoints by using the extended version of the dinuclear system model (DNS-sysu) and the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model. From the macroscopic and microscopic dynamical viewpoints, we find incomplete CE for the mass asymmetry reaction systems even in the very deep collisions, and the behavior of "inverse CE" that the tendency of the fragments is away from the $N/Z$ value of the compound system in the reaction $^{140}$Xe + $^{198}$Pt. Unlike the slow process presented in the ImQMD model, the behavior of fast equilibration with the characteristic time $\sim$ 0.52 zs is obtained based on the DNS-sysu model, which is consistent with the experimental data. By performing the systematic calculation, the correlation between the CE and the mass asymmetry of the reaction systems is clarified, which not only accounts for the observed intriguing phenomena of the CE but also provides essential information for producing the neutron-rich isotopes around $N = 126$.

# Deep insight into charge equilibration and the effects on producing neutron-rich isotopes around N = 126 in the multinucleon transfer reactions

## Key claim
The authors find that complete charge equilibration is hard to reach in mass asymmetric reaction systems, and they observe a behavior of "inverse CE" where fragments move away from the compound system N/Z value in the reaction 140Xe + 198Pt. The correlation between charge equilibration and mass asymmetry of the reaction systems is clarified for the first time, which accounts for both incomplete CE and inverse CE phenomena.

## Method
The authors used two complementary models: the extended dinuclear system model (DNS-sysu), a macroscopic approach solving master equations for nucleon transfer on a potential energy surface, and the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model, a microscopic dynamical approach. They calculated the evolution of average N/Z values of projectile-like and target-like fragments as a function of contact time for various Xe isotopes on a 198Pt target, and systematically studied the correlation between charge equilibration and mass asymmetry across a broad range of projectile masses and N/Z values. The de-excitation of primary fragments was treated using the GEMINI++ code.

## Key numbers
- Characteristic time for charge equilibration in the DNS-sysu model: approximately 0.52 zs, consistent with experimental data (0.3 zs from Jedele et al. and 0.14 zs from Kratz et al.) and TDHF calculations (0.5 zs)
- For the reaction 140Xe + 198Pt, the driving potentials for neutron stripping channels: Delta U(-1n) = -2.6 MeV, Delta U(-2n) = -4.8 MeV, and Delta U(-3n) = -6.8 MeV
- For the reaction 78Kr + 208Pb at E_c.m. = 347 MeV: characteristic time with shell corrections = 0.31 zs, without shell corrections = 0.22 zs
- For the reaction 129Xe + 198Pt at E_c.m. = 470 MeV: characteristic time with shell corrections = 0.27 zs, without shell corrections = 0.38 zs
- Saturation value of (N/Z_TLF - N/Z_PLF) for all three Xe + 198Pt systems: 0.04
- For reactions 238U + 198Pt and 202Pt + 198Pt, saturation values of (N/Z_TLF - N/Z_PLF): 0.01 and 0, respectively
- Incident energies used: 129Xe + 198Pt at 470 MeV, 136Xe + 198Pt at 476 MeV, 140Xe + 198Pt at 466 MeV, 202Pt + 198Pt at 663 MeV, 238U + 198Pt at 741 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous studies of charge equilibration in multinucleon transfer reactions, which have been investigated using time-dependent Hartree-Fock by Simenel et al. (2020) and experimental measurements by Kratz et al. (1977) and Jedele et al. (2017). The authors compare their DNS-sysu results with the stochastic mean-field approach of Ayik et al. (2021), finding consistency in the equilibration timescale. The systematic correlation between charge equilibration and mass asymmetry provides guidance for selecting optimal projectile-target combinations to produce neutron-rich isotopes around N = 126, with the specific suggestion that 238U + 198Pt is favorable for producing unknown N = 126 isotopes.
