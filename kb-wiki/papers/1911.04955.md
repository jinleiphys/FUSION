# Family of Chiral Two- plus Three-Nucleon Interactions for Accurate Nuclear Structure Studies

> We present a family of nucleon-nucleon (NN) plus three-nucleon (3N) interactions up to N3LO in the chiral expansion that provides an accurate ab initio description of ground-state energies and charge radii up to the medium-mass regime with quantified theory uncertainties. Starting from the NN interactions proposed by Entem, Machleidt and Nosyk, we construct 3N interactions with consistent chiral order, non-local regulator, and cutoff value and explore the dependence of nuclear observables over a range of mass numbers on the 3N low-energy constants. By fixing these constants using the 3-H and 16-O ground-state energies, we obtain interactions that robustly reproduce experimental energies and radii for large range from p-shell nuclei to the nickel isotopic chain and resolve many of the deficiencies of previous interactions. Based on the order-by-order convergence and the cutoff dependence of nuclear observables, we assess the uncertainties due the interaction, which yield a significant contribution to the total theory uncertainty.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a family of chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) plus three-nucleon (3N) interactions up to N3LO that accurately reproduces ground-state energies and charge radii from light to medium-mass nuclei, but the optimal value of the 3N low-energy constant cD depends on the observable: the value that reproduces medium-mass nuclei (cD approximately 4 for N3LO at 500 MeV cutoff) differs from the value extracted from few-body systems (cD approximately 2) and from nuclear-matter saturation studies (cD approximately -3), a discrepancy that remains unresolved.

## Method

Starting from the EMN family of chiral NN interactions at orders from LO to N3LO with non-local regulators and cutoffs of 450, 500, and 550 MeV, the authors constructed consistent 3N interactions at NNLO and N3LO using the same regulator scheme and cutoff. They fixed the 3N low-energy constant cE by fitting the triton ground-state energy for a range of cD values, then selected the optimal cD for each order and cutoff by requiring that simple IM-SRG calculations reproduce the 16O ground-state energy. Ground-state energies and radii were computed using no-core shell model (NCSM) for few-body systems, in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) for closed-shell medium-mass nuclei, and in-medium no-core shell model (IM-NCSM) for open-shell oxygen isotopes.

## Key numbers

- Triton ground-state energy fixed to experimental value (better than 1 keV convergence) for all interactions
- 4He ground-state energies range from -29.05 MeV to -29.64 MeV across all interactions, with radii from 1.437 fm to 1.499 fm
- Optimal cD values: for NNLO at 450 MeV cutoff cD = 10.0, at 500 MeV cD = 5.0, at 550 MeV cD = 2.0; for N3LO at 450 MeV cD = 9.0, at 500 MeV cD = 4.0, at 550 MeV cD = 3.0
- Expansion parameter Q estimated as approximately 1/3 for medium-mass nuclei
- Many-body uncertainties estimated at approximately 2% from comparisons of different many-body methods
- 9Be 1/2- state appears 1.5 MeV too high in excitation spectra compared to experiment

## Context

This work extends the EMN chiral NN interactions (Entem, Machleidt, Nosyk, 2017) by constructing consistent 3N interactions with non-local regulators, addressing systematic deficiencies of previous chiral interactions that showed underestimation of nuclear radii, particularly in the oxygen isotopic chain (Lapoux et al., 2016; Binder et al., 2014). The new family resolves many deficiencies of earlier interactions such as the NNLO_SAT interaction (Ekstrom et al., 2015), which improved ground-state observables but degraded phase-shift reproduction. The order-by-order uncertainty quantification, previously established in few-body systems (Epelbaum et al., 2015; Binder et al., 2016, 2018) and nuclear-matter calculations (Drischler et al., 2019), is now extended to medium-mass nuclei. The interactions enable ab initio nuclear structure calculations with fully quantified uncertainties across a wide mass range, while highlighting the remaining challenge of reconciling the optimal cD values for nuclear matter, medium-mass nuclei, and few-body systems.
