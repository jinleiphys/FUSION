# Helium nuclei, deuteron and dineutron in 2+1 flavor lattice QCD

> We calculate the binding energies for multi-nucleon bound states with the nuclear mass number less than or equal to 4 in 2+1 flavor QCD at the lattice spacing of a = 0.09 fm employing a relatively heavy quark mass corresponding to m_pi = 0.51 GeV. To distinguish a bound state from attractive scattering states, we investigate the volume dependence of the energy shift between the ground state and the state of free nucleons by changing the spatial extent of the lattice from 2.9 fm to 5.8 fm. We conclude that ^4He, ^3He, deuteron and dineutron are bound at m_pi = 0.51 GeV. We compare their binding energies with those in our quenched studies and also with several previous investigations.

## Key claim

The paper concludes that at a heavy pion mass of 0.51 GeV, the ground states of ^4He, ^3He, the deuteron (^3S_1 channel), and the dineutron (^1S_0 channel) are all bound states, but the binding energies for ^3He and the deuteron are much larger than experimental values, and the dineutron bound state contradicts experimental observation.

## Method

The authors performed 2+1 flavor lattice QCD simulations with the Iwasaki gauge action and non-perturbatively O(a)-improved Wilson quarks at a lattice spacing of a = 0.09 fm. They calculated correlation functions for multi-nucleon systems and the nucleon on four lattice volumes with spatial extents from 2.9 fm to 5.8 fm, extracting the energy shift between the multi-nucleon ground state and free nucleons. To distinguish bound states from attractive scattering states, they extrapolated the volume-dependent energy shifts to the infinite spatial volume limit using a linear function of 1/L^3 for helium nuclei and a bound-state finite-volume formula for the two-nucleon channels.

## Key numbers

*   Pion mass: m_pi = 0.51 GeV; nucleon mass: m_N = 1.32 GeV.
*   Lattice spacing: a = 0.08995(40) fm, corresponding to a^{-1} = 2.194(10) GeV.
*   Spatial extents used: 2.9 fm, 3.6 fm, 4.3 fm, and 5.8 fm.
*   Binding energy of ^4He: -Delta_E_infinity = 43(12)(8) MeV.
*   Binding energy of ^3He: -Delta_E_infinity = 20.3(4.0)(2.0) MeV.
*   Binding energy of deuteron (^3S_1 channel): -Delta_E_infinity = 11.5(1.1)(0.6) MeV.
*   Binding energy of dineutron (^1S_0 channel): -Delta_E_infinity = 7.4(1.3)(0.6) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous quenched QCD studies of helium nuclei and two-nucleon systems at m_pi = 0.80 GeV (Yamazaki 2009, Yamazaki 2011) by including dynamical quark effects with 2+1 flavor QCD at a lighter pion mass. It also compares with results from the NPLQCD collaboration, who found bound states in both two-nucleon channels at m_pi = 0.39 GeV (Beane 2011) and deep bound states at m_pi = 0.81 GeV in 3-flavor QCD (Beane 2012). The results enable future work toward physical quark masses to determine whether the dineutron bound state disappears and the deuteron binding energy approaches the experimental value as the up-down quark mass decreases.
