# Second-order perturbation theory for 3He and pd scattering in pionless EFT

> This work implements pionless effective field theory with the two-nucleon system expanded around the unitarity limit at second order perturbation theory. The expansion is found to converge well. All Coulomb effects are treated in perturbation theory, including two-photon contributions at next-to-next-to-leading order. After fixing a three-nucleon force to the 3He binding energy at this order, proton-deuteron scattering in the doublet S-wave channel is calculated for moderate center-of-mass momenta.

## Key claim

This work demonstrates that pionless effective field theory with the two-nucleon system expanded around the unitarity limit converges well at second-order perturbation theory (next-to-next-to-leading order, NNLO), including fully perturbative treatment of Coulomb effects and two-photon exchange contributions, for both the 3He bound state and proton-deuteron scattering in the doublet S-wave channel.

## Method

The author implements pionless EFT with the two-nucleon system expanded around the unitarity limit, treating all Coulomb effects perturbatively including two-photon exchange diagrams at NNLO. The three-nucleon force is fixed to the 3He binding energy at this order, and the proton-deuteron scattering amplitude in the doublet S-wave channel is calculated for moderate center-of-mass momenta using integral-equation techniques that avoid explicit calculation of multi-loop diagrams. The two-photon contributions are extracted from the O(alpha^2) piece of the full off-shell Coulomb T-matrix using the Hostler form.

## Key numbers

* 3He binding energy: used to fix the three-nucleon force at NNLO (no explicit numerical value given in the truncated text)
* Two-nucleon scattering lengths: 5.4 fm (3S1 channel) and -23.7 fm (1S0 channel), compared to the typical scale M_pi^{-1} ~ 1.4 fm
* Coulomb-modified pp scattering length: a_C ~ 7.81 fm
* nn scattering length: a_nn ~ -(22.9 +/- 4.1) fm, adopted from Kirscher (2011)
* Effective ranges: r_C = 2.794 fm (pp channel) and r_t = 2.73 fm (np channel), with difference r_C - r_t ~ 0.06 fm included at NNLO
* Deuteron binding energy in the full-unitarity scheme: at LO the deuteron is at zero energy (kappa^(0) -> 0), at NLO B_1 = 2 kappa^(0) kappa^(1)/M_N, at NNLO B_2 = (kappa^(1))^2/M_N where kappa^(1) = 1/a_d

## Context

This work extends the unitarity-limit counting schemes developed by Konig (2015) and Konig (2016) from NLO to NNLO, establishing convergence of these expansions up to second order. It goes beyond the first-order perturbation theory calculations of Vanasse (2014) and Konig (2014) by including two-photon exchange and other isospin-breaking corrections, and demonstrates that away from zero-energy threshold it is possible to describe pd scattering with fully perturbative Coulomb effects. The paper also establishes the presence of an NNLO three-body force that must be fixed by a single pd doublet datum, building on the work of Ji (2012) who identified an energy-dependent three-body force at NNLO for the analogous three-boson system.
