# Level inversion in kaonic nuclei and the high-density nuclear equation of state

> It is very difficult for any nuclear model to pin down the saturation property and high-density equation of state (EOS) simultaneously because of high nonlinearity of the nuclear many-body problem. In this work, we propose, for the first time, to use the special property of light kaonic nuclei to characterize the relation between saturation property and high-density EOS. With a series of relativistic mean-field models, this special property is found to be the level inversion between orbitals $2S_{1/2}$ and $1D_{5/2}$ in light kaonic nuclei. This level inversion can serve as a theoretical laboratory to group the incompressibility at saturation density and the EOS at supra-normal densities simultaneously.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the level inversion between the $2S_{1/2}$ and $1D_{5/2}$ orbitals in light kaonic nuclei can serve as a theoretical laboratory to simultaneously group the incompressibility at saturation density and the stiffness of the high-density equation of state (EOS), but this inversion occurs only for a specific combination: a stiff high-density EOS combined with a relatively small incompressibility.

## Method

The authors use relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory to calculate ground-state properties of kaonic nuclei, incorporating a Lagrangian density for nucleons and a separate Lagrangian for the $K^-$ meson with couplings determined by SU(3) relations and adjusted to a $K^-$ optical potential depth of -100 MeV at saturation density. They generate a series of nuclear EOSs with different incompressibilities and high-density stiffnesses by varying the nonlinear $\omega$ self-coupling parameter $c_3$ while fitting to saturation properties, then compute the single-particle energy levels in kaonic nuclei such as $^{18}_{K^-}$O, $^{22}_{K^-}$O, and $^{40}_{K^-}$Ca to map the critical boundary for level inversion in the incompressibility-stiffness plane.

## Key numbers

- The $K^-$ optical potential depth at saturation density is adopted as -100 MeV, based on heavy-ion collision data.
- The saturation density used for fitting is $\rho_0 = 0.148\ \text{fm}^{-3}$ with an energy per baryon of -16.3 MeV.
- The nucleon effective mass $M_B^{\ast}/M_B$ is set around 0.6.
- The sound velocity square at $2\rho_0$ is used as the criterion for high-density EOS stiffness.
- The incompressibility values for several best-fit parameter sets are referenced: NL3, NL1, TM1, FSUGold, and IU-FSUGold, with the level inversion appearing for NL1 and NL3 but not for FSUGold, IU-FSUGold, and TM1.
- The core density in $^{18}_{K^-}$O reaches over two times the saturation density.

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Yang et al. (2017) that established a correlation between low-density halo phenomena in kaonic nuclei and EOS stiffness, and it builds on the known deep binding and dense core properties of kaonic nuclei first described by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002). The level inversion phenomenon provides a new theoretical tool to simultaneously constrain the incompressibility at saturation density (previously studied via giant monopole resonances by Blaizot (1980), Youngblood et al. (1999), and Khan et al. (2012)) and the high-density EOS (which has a 50% relative error from heavy-ion collision data as reported by Danielewicz et al. (2002)). The results suggest that experiments to identify or exclude kaonic nuclei, such as those proposed by Nagae (2017) and the J-PARC facility (2019), would be valuable for constraining the nuclear EOS across the full density region.
