# Mass formula for T=0 and T=1 ground states of N=Z nuclei

> An algebraic model is developed to calculate the T=0 and T=1 ground state binding energies for N=Z nuclei. The method is tested in the sd shell and is then extended to 28-50 shell which is currently the object of many experimental studies.

## Key claim

The authors propose a simple algebraic mass formula based on the isospin-invariant Interacting Boson Model (IBM-4) that can reproduce the binding energies of both T=0 and T=1 ground states in N=Z nuclei, but the model is restricted to the L=0 channel and to self-conjugate nuclei, and it suffers from a discontinuity at mid-shell requiring separate fits for each half of a shell.

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian using Casimir operators of U_L(6), SU_TS(4), U_S(3), and SO_T(3) from the IBM-4 dynamical symmetry chain, with five adjustable parameters plus a core energy. They fit these parameters separately for each half of the sd shell (16O to 28Si and 30P to 40Ca) and for each half of the 28-50 shell (56Ni to 78Y and 78Y to 100Sn) to experimental binding energies of the lowest T=0 and T=1 states in even-even and odd-odd N=Z nuclei, after correcting for Coulomb effects.

## Key numbers

*   RMS deviation of 0.876 MeV for the first half of the sd shell (16O to 28Si) and 0.245 MeV for the second half (30P to 40Ca).
*   RMS deviation of 0.396 MeV for the first half of the 28-50 shell (56Ni to 78Y).
*   Predicted energy splitting E(0+,1) - E(1+,0) of 0.847 MeV for 70Br, 1.037 MeV for 74Rb, and 1.214 MeV for 78Y.
*   In 58Cu, the (0+,1) state lies 0.202 MeV above the (1+,0) ground state; in 62Ga, the (1+,0) state is 0.571 MeV above the (0+,1) ground state; in 66As, the E(0+,1)-E(1+,0) splitting is 0.837 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the algebraic approach of Van Isacker and Warner (1997) and the microscopically derived IBM-4 Hamiltonian of Juillet, Van Isacker, and Warner (2001) to provide a simpler mass formula for binding energy predictions at the N=Z line. It contrasts with global mass models such as the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) of Moller et al. (1995) and the Extended Thomas-Fermi plus Strutinsky Integral (ETFSI) method, which include an ad hoc Wigner term for N=Z nuclei, by offering a physically motivated treatment of isoscalar and isovector pairing competition. The results enable predictions for heavier N=Z nuclei currently under experimental study, particularly in the 28-50 shell, though the approach is limited to self-conjugate nuclei and cannot account for deformation effects.
