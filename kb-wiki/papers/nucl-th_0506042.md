# Resonant Fermi gases with a large effective range

> We calculate the equation of state of a Fermi gas with resonant interactions when the effective range is appreciable. Using an effective field theory for large scattering length and large effective range, we show how calculations in this regime become tractable. Our results are model-independent, and as an application, we study the neutron matter equation of state at low densities of astrophysical interest 0.002 fm^{-3} < rho < 0.02 fm^{-3}, for which the interparticle separation is comparable to the effective range. We compare our simple results with those of conventional many-body calculations.

# Resonant Fermi gases with a large effective range

## Key claim

The equation of state of a resonant Fermi gas can be extended to regimes where the effective range is comparable to the interparticle separation (k_F r_e ~ 1) using a di-fermion effective field theory, but the universal factor ξ becomes a function ξ(k_F r_e) rather than a constant, and the theory is tractable because the effective interaction becomes weak for all k_F r_e ≳ 1.

## Method

The authors construct a di-fermion effective field theory (EFT) Lagrangian with a dimer field d, matching the low-energy constants to the effective range expansion. They sum particle-particle ladder diagrams with Pauli blocking to obtain a cutoff-independent in-medium T matrix T_med, then evaluate the Hartree-Fock energy using this T_med as the effective interaction, with the scattering length set to infinity (1/a_s = 0) for the resonant case.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-neutron scattering length: a_nn = -18.5 ± 0.3 fm
- Neutron-neutron effective range: r_nn = 2.7 fm
- Density range studied for neutron matter: 0.002 fm^{-3} < ρ < 0.02 fm^{-3}
- Universal factor from Green's function Monte Carlo: ξ = 0.44 ± 0.01 (Carlson) and ξ = 0.42 ± 0.01 (Astra)
- ξ_HF(1) ≈ 0.47, close to the universal regime value
- ξ_HF(10) = 0.79 and ξ_HF(100) = 0.96 for large effective range
- Estimated pairing correction to ξ: ξ_sf ≈ -0.01 for densities with k_F r_e ~ 1
- Estimated non-ladder corrections: Δξ = 0.077 / (U_0 (1 + 0.27 k_F r_e))^3, with U_0 = 0.56

## Context

This work extends the universal equation of state for resonant Fermi gases (O'Hara et al. 2002, Thomas2, Salomon, Grimm) beyond the regime k_F r_e ≪ 1 to the regime k_F r_e ∼ 1, which is relevant for low-density neutron matter in neutron stars and supernovae. The results are compared with conventional many-body calculations using Fermi hypernetted-chain techniques (FP) and Brueckner theory (Bao), as well as low-momentum interaction V_lowk Hartree-Fock results, all of which lie within the theoretical error estimates. The approach enables model-independent calculations for systems where both scattering length and effective range are large, including trapped atomic Fermi gases with tunable interactions.
