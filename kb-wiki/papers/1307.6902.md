# Alpha-clustered hypernuclei and chiral SU(3) dynamics

> Light hypernuclei with an $α$ cluster substructure of the core nucleus are studied using an accurate cluster approach (the Hyper-THSR wave function) in combination with a density-dependent $Λ$ hyperon-nuclear interaction derived from chiral SU(3) effective field theory. This interaction includes important two-pion exchange processes involving $ΣN$ intermediate states and associated three-body mechanisms as well as effective mass and surface terms arising in a derivative expansion of the in-medium $Λ$ self-energy. Applications and calculated results are presented and discussed for $_Λ^9$Be and $^{13}_Λ$C. Furthermore, the result of the lightest $α$ clustered hypernucleus, $^5_Λ$He using realistic $ab initio$ four nucleon density is shown.

## Key claim

The chiral SU(3) derived density-dependent Λ-nuclear interaction, including a surface derivative term proportional to ∇²ρ_N, can reproduce empirical Λ binding energies in light alpha-clustered hypernuclei, but the extracted surface coupling strength D depends sensitively on the detailed nuclear core density profile and on whether the core density is taken from the full hypernuclear calculation or from the free core nucleus.

## Method

The authors used the Hyper-THSR (Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke) wave function to describe the alpha-clustered core nuclei ⁸Be and ¹²C and their hypernuclei ⁹_ΛBe and ¹³_ΛC. They computed the nuclear core density distributions from these wave functions and then evaluated the expectation value of a Λ-nuclear Hamiltonian derived from chiral SU(3) effective field theory, which includes a central potential U_c, an effective mass term R(ρ_N), and a surface term D ∇²ρ_N. The Λ binding energies were calculated for varying values of the central potential depth U_0 and surface coupling strength D to find combinations reproducing the empirical binding energies.

## Key numbers

- Empirical Λ binding energy for ⁹_ΛBe: 6.71 MeV (experimental value from literature)
- Empirical Λ binding energy for ¹³_ΛC: 11.71 MeV (experimental value from literature)
- Empirical Λ binding energy for ⁵_ΛHe: 3.12 MeV (experimental value from literature)
- Calculated rms radius of ⁸Be core in ⁹_ΛBe: 2.35 fm, compared to 2.87 fm for free ⁸Be (a shrinkage of about 20%)
- Calculated rms radius of ¹²C core in ¹³_ΛC: 2.32 fm, compared to 2.40 fm for free ¹²C
- For ¹³_ΛC with U_0 = -35 MeV and D = 0, the calculated B_Λ = 16.7 MeV; adding the effective mass term gives B_Λ = 16.7 MeV; including D = 54 MeV·fm⁵ brings B_Λ to the empirical 11.7 MeV
- For ⁹_ΛBe with U_0 = -33.3 MeV and D = 0, B_Λ = 6.24 MeV; adding the effective mass term reaches the empirical 6.71 MeV without need for a surface term
- For ⁵_ΛHe with U_0 = -35 MeV, a surface coupling D ≈ 23 MeV·fm⁵ reproduces the empirical B_Λ = 3.12 MeV using the realistic four-body ⁴He density
- The ⁴He matter radius derived from the empirical charge radius: ⟨r²⟩_m^(1/2) = 1.45 fm (from charge radius 1.68 fm and proton charge radius 0.85 fm)

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) effective field theory approach previously applied to medium-mass and heavy hypernuclei from ¹⁶_ΛO to ²⁰⁸_ΛPb by Finelli et al. (2009) down to the light alpha-clustered systems ⁹_ΛBe, ¹³_ΛC, and ⁵_ΛHe. It uses the THSR cluster model wave functions that have successfully described the alpha cluster structure of ⁸Be and ¹²C, including the Hoyle state in ¹²C. The results enable a systematic test of the gradient expansion for the Λ-nuclear interaction in light systems where surface effects are large, and they show that the surface coupling strength D extracted from ¹³_ΛC (40-80 MeV·fm⁵ range) is consistent with heavier hypernuclei, while ⁹_ΛBe and ⁵_ΛHe require smaller D values, indicating the limits of the local density approximation for very light and strongly deformed systems.
