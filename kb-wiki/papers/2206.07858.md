# Driving Potential and Fission-Fragment Charge Distributions

> We propose an efficient approach to describe the fission-fragment charge yields for actinides based on the driving potential of fissioning system. Considering the properties of primary fission fragments at their ground states, the driving potential, which represents the potential energies of the system around scission configuration and closely relates to the yields of fragments, can be unambiguously and quickly obtained from the Skyrme energy-density functional together with the Weizsaecker-Skyrme mass model. The fission-fragment charge distributions for thermal-neutroninduced fission and spontaneous fission of a series of actinides, especially the odd-even staggering in charge distributions can be well reproduced. Nuclear dynamical deformations and pairing corrections of fragments play an important role in the charge distributions.

# Driving Potential and Fission-Fragment Charge Distributions

## Key claim

The fission-fragment charge distributions for actinides, including odd-even staggering, can be well reproduced using a driving potential derived from the Skyrme energy-density functional and the Weizsäcker-Skyrme mass model, but only for thermal-neutron-induced and spontaneous fission at low excitation energies; the model parameter \(K\) is empirically set to \(0.38A_{\rm CN}-83.35\) MeV and the normalization factor requires measured mass distributions.

## Method

The authors compute the driving potential \(U(A_{\rm f},Z_{\rm f})\) for a fissioning system around scission by summing the ground-state energies of the two primary fragments (from the WS mass model and AME2016 masses), the change in energy due to dynamical deformations \(\Delta Q\), and the interaction potential \(V\) obtained from the Skyrme energy-density functional with the extended Thomas-Fermi approximation. Fragment charge yields are then calculated as \(Y(A_{\rm f},Z_{\rm f}) = C \exp[-U/K]\), where the normalization factor \(C\) is determined from measured fragment mass distributions, and the parameter \(K\) is fitted to reproduce charge distributions for a series of actinides.

## Key numbers

- Model parameter \(K = 0.38A_{\rm CN} - 83.35\) (in MeV) for describing fragment yields in fission of actinides at low excitation energies.
- For thermal-neutron-induced fission of \(^{235}\)U, the driving potential valleys are located around \((A_{\rm f}=140, Z_{\rm f}=54)\) and \((A_{\rm f}=96, Z_{\rm f}=38)\), corresponding to the peaks of measured mass and charge distributions.
- Dynamical octupole deformations \(\beta_3^D\) for fission fragments are found to be about \(0.1\) to \(0.2\).
- For \(^{102}\)Mo, the FRDM model predicts quadrupole deformation \(\beta_2 = 0.329\) while the WS model predicts \(\beta_2 = 0.210\).
- A shift of 4.4 MeV is observed between the model parameter \(K\) and the difference \(Q_{g.s.}^{\rm asym} - Q_{g.s.}^{\rm sym}\) for eight actinides (\(^{230}\)Th, \(^{234,236}\)U, \(^{240,242}\)Pu, \(^{246}\)Cm, \(^{250,252}\)Cf).

## Context

This work extends the di-nuclear system (DNS) concept, previously applied to quasi-fission and fusion of super-heavy systems by Adamian (2003) and Kalandarov (2011), to describe fission-fragment charge distributions. It contrasts with the five-dimensional potential-energy-surface approach of Randrup and Möller, which did not reproduce odd-even staggering in charge distributions for \(^{234,236}\)U and \(^{240}\)Pu. The approach enables rapid predictions of charge yields for actinides where mass distributions are known but charge yields have not been measured, and it clarifies that pairing corrections in the ground-state \(Q\) values are essential for odd-even staggering.
