# Global $Λ$ polarization in heavy-ion collisions at energies 2.4--7.7 GeV: Effect of Meson-Field Interaction

> Based on the three-fluid model, the global $Λ$ polarization in Au+Au collisions at 2.4 $\leq\sqrt{s_{NN}}\leq$ 7.7 GeV is calculated, including its rapidity and centrality dependence. Contributions from the thermal vorticity and meson-field term (proposed by Csernai, Kapusta and Welle) to the global polarization are considered. The results are compared with data from recent and ongoing STAR and HADES experiments. It is predicted that the polarization maximum is reached at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\approx$ 3 GeV, if the measurements are performed with the same acceptance. The value of the polarization is very sensitive to interplay of the aforementioned contributions. In particular, the thermal vorticity results in quite strong increase of the polarization from the midrapidity to forward/backward rapidities, while the meson-field contribution considerably flattens the rapidity dependence. The polarization turns out to be very sensitive to details of the equation of state. While collision dynamics become less equilibrium with decreasing collision energy, the present approach to polarization is based on the assumption of thermal equilibrium. It is found that equilibrium is achieved at the freeze-out stage, but this equilibration takes longer at moderately relativistic energies.

## Key claim

The global \(\Lambda\) polarization in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.4\)–\(7.7\) GeV is predicted to reach a maximum at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 3\) GeV, but this result depends on the assumption of thermal equilibrium at freeze-out, which is achieved only after a long equilibration time at these moderately relativistic energies.

## Method

The authors used the three-fluid dynamics (3FD) model, which treats the colliding nuclei as two counterstreaming baryon-rich fluids and a fireball fluid for newly produced particles, coupled by friction terms. The global \(\Lambda\) polarization was calculated from the thermal vorticity and an additional meson-field term (proposed by Csernai, Kapusta, and Welle) using a thermodynamic approach, with averaging over the freeze-out hypersurface and including feed-down corrections from \(\Sigma^*\) and \(\Sigma^0\) decays. Simulations were performed with three different equations of state: a purely hadronic EoS, a first-order phase transition (1PT) EoS, and a crossover EoS.

## Key numbers

- The global \(\Lambda\) polarization in the midrapidity region (\(|y_h| < 0.8\)) at \(b = 8\) fm is reduced by \(\approx 25\%\) at low energies and by \(\approx 15\%\) at 7.7 GeV due to feed-down from higher-lying resonances.
- The natural scale factor for the meson-field contribution is \(\frac{g_{\omega\Lambda}\bar{g}_{\omega} n_0}{m_\Lambda m^2_{\omega}} \approx 0.1\), where \(n_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\) is the normal nuclear density.
- Mechanical equilibration at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.42\) GeV is reached at \(\approx 9\) fm/c, and thermalization at \(\approx 12\) fm/c, with freeze-out completed at \(\approx 20\) fm/c from the beginning of the collision.
- The average freeze-out energy density \(\varepsilon_{\text{frz}}\) is automatically generated in the 3FD simulations, with the only freeze-out parameter being \(\epsilon_{\text{frz}} = 0.4\) GeV/fm\(^3\) for all EoS's and collision energies.

## Context

This work extends previous 3FD calculations of global \(\Lambda\) polarization (Ivanov, 2020) by including the meson-field contribution and refined treatment of feed-down decays. It addresses the discrepancy between model predictions for the polarization peak location: Deng (2020) and Ivanov (2020) predicted a peak at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 3\) GeV, while Guo (2021) predicted it at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 7.7\) GeV. The results are compared with recent data from STAR (2021) at 3 GeV and 7.2 GeV and HADES (2021) at 2.4 GeV, and the calculations enable predictions for future experiments at FAIR and NICA.
