# $nα$ Elastic Scattering: An Application of Variable Phase Approach to Local Potential

> Distance-dependent phase shifts, amplitude functions, and radial wave functions for neutron-alpha elastic scattering are studied using the Variable Phase Approach. The microscopic KKNN potential is employed to calculate scattering properties for the $S_{1/2}$, $P_{3/2}$, and $P_{1/2}$ partial waves over a range of laboratory energies. The variable phase equations are solved numerically using a fifth-order Runge-Kutta method, allowing a direct examination of how the nuclear interaction generates the scattering phase within the finite interaction region. The results exhibit physically consistent behavior of the phase shifts and yield well-behaved amplitude and wave functions. This study demonstrates that the Variable Phase Approach provides a physically transparent and reliable framework for describing neutron-alpha elastic scattering and for applications in inverse scattering problems.

# nα Elastic Scattering: An Application of Variable Phase Approach to Local Potential

## Key claim
The Variable Phase Approach, applied with the microscopic KKNN potential, yields physically consistent distance-dependent phase shifts, amplitude functions, and radial wave functions for neutron-alpha elastic scattering that demonstrate the roles of central interaction, centrifugal barrier, and spin-orbit coupling; however, the results are demonstrated only for the $S_{1/2}$, $P_{3/2}$, and $P_{1/2}$ partial waves over a limited range of laboratory energies, and the approach's reliability for inverse scattering problems is asserted rather than demonstrated with numerical inversion results.

## Method
The Variable Phase Approach transforms the second-order Schrödinger equation into a first-order Riccati-type differential equation for the distance-dependent phase shift $\delta_\ell(k,r)$, which is solved numerically using a fifth-order Runge-Kutta method with initial condition $\delta_\ell(0)=0$. The microscopic KKNN potential, a local potential with central and spin-orbit terms parameterized as sums of Gaussians, is used as the nuclear interaction for neutron-alpha scattering. From the phase shift solutions, amplitude functions $A_\ell(r)$ and radial wave functions $u_\ell(r)$ are reconstructed for the $S_{1/2}$, $P_{1/2}$, and $P_{3/2}$ partial waves.

## Key numbers
- The reduced mass factor $\hbar^2/2\mu$ is taken as 25.9425 MeV·fm² for the neutron-alpha system.
- The KKNN central potential includes a term with strength -96.3 MeV and range parameter 0.36 fm⁻², and another with 77.0 MeV and 0.90 fm⁻².
- The spin-orbit potential includes a term with strength -16.8 MeV and range parameter 0.52 fm⁻².
- Distance-dependent phase shifts, amplitude functions, and radial wave functions are computed at laboratory energies of 1, 5, and 10 MeV for all three partial waves.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous application of the Phase Function Method (PFM) or Variable Phase Approach to nucleon-nucleon scattering using Morse potentials (Khachi, earlier papers on n-p, n-d, and n-α scattering). It specifically builds on a recent study (Khachi, 2025) where Morse potential parameters were obtained for np scattering, now applying the same methodology with the microscopic KKNN potential originally developed by Kanada et al. The approach enables direct examination of how the nuclear interaction generates scattering phase within the finite interaction region and is positioned as suitable for future inverse scattering problems and extension to other systems such as proton-alpha and neutron-nucleus scattering.
