# Surface Symmetry Energy of Nuclear Energy Density Functionals

> We study the bulk deformation properties of the Skyrme nuclear energy density functionals. Following simple arguments based on the leptodermous expansion and liquid drop model, we apply the nuclear density functional theory to assess the role of the surface symmetry energy in nuclei. To this end, we validate the commonly used functional parametrizations against the data on excitation energies of superdeformed band-heads in Hg and Pb isotopes, and fission isomers in actinide nuclei. After subtracting shell effects, the results of our self-consistent calculations are consistent with macroscopic arguments and indicate that experimental data on strongly deformed configurations in neutron-rich nuclei are essential for optimizing future nuclear energy density functionals. The resulting survey provides a useful benchmark for further theoretical improvements. Unlike in nuclei close to the stability valley, whose macroscopic deformability hangs on the balance of surface and Coulomb terms, the deformability of neutron-rich nuclei strongly depends on the surface-symmetry energy; hence, its proper determination is crucial for the stability of deformed phases of the neutron- rich matter and description of fission rates for r-process nucleosynthesis.

## Key claim

The surface-symmetry coefficient of the nuclear energy density functional is poorly constrained by ground-state data, but strongly deformed configurations in neutron-rich nuclei can amplify its contribution and thus provide a way to determine it. The authors show that the macroscopic deformation energy of very neutron-rich nuclei involves a three-way competition between the repulsive surface term and attractive Coulomb and surface-symmetry terms, unlike nuclei near stability where only surface and Coulomb terms compete.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using several Skyrme energy density functionals with a mixed-pairing interaction, fitting the pairing strength to the average neutron pairing gap in 120Sn. They computed potential energy curves for superdeformed band-heads in Hg and Pb isotopes and fission isomers in actinide nuclei, using the HFBTHO and HFODD solvers with a stretched harmonic oscillator basis of 20 shells and a 60 MeV cut-off energy. They then subtracted microscopic shell corrections from the self-consistent results to extract the macroscopic part of the deformation energy and compared it with liquid drop model predictions.

## Context

This work extends the leptodermous expansion analysis of Reinhard et al. (2006), who extracted LDM parameters from DFT calculations for very large nuclei and found that a_ssym could vary by a factor of 3 across parameterizations. It also builds on earlier studies by Farine et al. (1978, 1980, 1981) and Tondeur (1984) who noted a correlation between a_ssym and a_sym. The results indicate that experimental data on strongly deformed configurations in neutron-rich nuclei are essential for optimizing the isospin channel of nuclear energy density functionals, which has implications for predicting fission rates for r-process nucleosynthesis and the structure of neutron star crusts.
