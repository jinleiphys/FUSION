# Charm and Charmonium in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> In the first part of the talk, we briefly review the problem of parton-energy loss and thermalization at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider and discuss how heavy quarks (charm and bottom) can help to resolve the existing experimental and theoretical puzzles. The second part of the talk is devoted to the properties of heavy quarkonia in the (strongly interacting) Quark-Gluon Plasma (sQGP) and their consequences for observables in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Heavy-quark observables (charm and bottom) can resolve puzzles about parton-energy loss and thermalization in the strongly interacting Quark-Gluon Plasma (sQGP) created at RHIC, but the microscopic mechanisms underlying thermalization and energy loss are not well understood yet; the paper argues that nonperturbative elastic interactions (resonance-mediated) are needed to explain the observed suppression and elliptic flow of single-electron spectra, beyond what perturbative QCD alone can account for.

## Method

The authors evaluate heavy-quark diffusion and thermalization in the QGP using a Fokker-Planck approach for Brownian motion, with drag and diffusion coefficients calculated from both perturbative and nonperturbative elastic interactions (resonance-mediated scattering via D- and B-meson-like states). They implement these coefficients into a relativistic Langevin simulation for charm and bottom quarks in an expanding thermal fireball at RHIC, followed by hadronization via quark coalescence and fragmentation at the critical temperature. For quarkonia, they use a T-matrix approach with heavy-quark potentials from lattice QCD to simultaneously describe bound and scattering states, and compute euclidean correlation functions for comparison with lattice results.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density at RHIC: ε₀ ≈ 30 GeV/fm³
- Critical energy density at phase boundary: ε_c ≈ 1 GeV/fm³
- Kinetic equilibration time: τ₀ ≈ 0.5 fm/c
- Charmonium ground-state size: r = 0.25 fm
- Temperature where J/ψ and η_c survive in QGP: up to ~2 T_c (T_c ≈ 180 MeV)
- Thermal relaxation time for charm quarks from pQCD at T=350 MeV: above 10 fm/c, compared to QGP lifetime τ_QGP ≈ 5 fm/c
- Nonperturbative resonance interactions reduce charm thermalization time by factor ~3 compared to pQCD elastic scattering alone
- Charmonium binding energies at T ≈ 1.5 T_c: ε_B ≈ 0.1-0.2 GeV (compared to ~0.6 GeV in vacuum)
- Bottomonium binding energies at T ≈ 1.5 T_c: ε_B ≈ 0.3-0.6 GeV (compared to ~1.1 GeV in vacuum)
- J/ψ width at T ≈ 1.5 T_c: ~0.1 GeV
- Charm contribution to electron R_AA as low as 0.1 and elliptic flow as large as 14% from nonperturbative rescattering
- Crossing between charm and bottom contribution in p-p collisions at p_T ≈ 5 GeV, shifting to p_T ≈ 2.5-3 GeV in Au-Au
- Number of c-cbar pairs in central Au-Au at √s=200 AGeV: N_c-cbar ≈ 20 (compared to 0.2 at SPS)
- QGP suppression factor for J/ψ at RHIC: S_QGP ≈ 0.4

## Context

This work extends earlier perturbative QCD energy-loss calculations (Gyulassy 2003, Wicks 2005, Armesto 2005) by incorporating nonperturbative resonance-mediated elastic interactions for heavy quarks, motivated by the inadequacy of pQCD to explain RHIC data on single-electron suppression and elliptic flow. It also builds on lattice QCD results for charmonium spectral functions (Asakawa 2003, Datta 2003) and potential-model studies (Shuryak 2003, Wong 2004, Mocsy 2005) to develop a T-matrix approach that simultaneously treats color screening, parton-induced dissociation, and in-medium heavy-quark masses. The work enables quantitative predictions for J/ψ and Υ suppression at RHIC, including the possibility of J/ψ regeneration from thermalized charm quarks, and suggests that stronger suppression of Υ relative to J/ψ would be a direct signature of color screening in the sQGP.
