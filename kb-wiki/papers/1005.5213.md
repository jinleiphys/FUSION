# Comparison of different proximity potentials for asymmetric colliding nuclei

> Using the different versions of phenomenological proximity potential as well as other parametrizations within the proximity concept, we perform a detailed comparative study of fusion barriers for asymmetric colliding nuclei with asymmetry parameter as high as 0.23. In all, 12 different proximity potentials are robust against the experimental data of 60 reactions. Our detailed study reveals that the surface energy coefficient as well as radius of the colliding nuclei depend significantly on the asymmetry parameter. All models are able to explain the fusion barrier heights within $\pm10%$ on the average. The potentials due to Bass 80, AW 95, and Denisov DP explain nicely the fusion cross sections at above- as well as below-barrier energies.

# Comparison of different proximity potentials for asymmetric colliding nuclei

## Key claim

All 12 proximity-type potentials can reproduce experimental fusion barrier heights within ±10% on average for asymmetric colliding nuclei with asymmetry parameter up to 0.23, but the surface energy coefficient and nuclear radius depend significantly on the asymmetry parameter, and only the Bass 80, AW 95, and Denisov DP potentials satisfactorily explain fusion cross sections at both above- and below-barrier energies.

## Method

The authors calculated fusion barriers and cross sections for 60 asymmetric reactions (compound mass 29 to 294, asymmetry parameter 0.02 to 0.23) using 12 different proximity-type potentials: four versions of the proximity potential (Prox 77, Prox 88, Prox 00, Prox 00DP), three versions of the Bass potential (Bass 73, Bass 77, Bass 80), three versions of the Winther potential (CW 76, BW 91, AW 95), the Ngo 80 potential, and the Denisov DP potential. The nuclear part of the interaction potential was added to the Coulomb potential, and fusion cross sections were computed using the Wong formula with barrier penetration factors.

## Key numbers

- 60 asymmetric reactions were analyzed, with compound masses between 29 and 294
- Asymmetry parameter A_s varied between 0.02 and 0.23; mass asymmetry eta_A varied between 0.0 and 0.97
- All models reproduced fusion barrier heights within ±10% on average
- Prox 88, Bass 80, AW 95, and Denisov DP reproduced barrier heights within ±5% (shaded regions in Fig. 5)
- Surface energy coefficient gamma in Prox 77: gamma_0 = 0.9517 MeV/fm^2, k_s = 1.7826; for (N-Z)/(N+Z) = 0.5, gamma reduces to 0.5276 MeV/fm^2
- Surface energy coefficient gamma in Prox 88: gamma_0 = 1.2496 MeV/fm^2, k_s = 2.3
- Lightest reaction: 12C + 17O; heaviest reaction: 86Kr + 208Pb
- Prox 00 radius formula (Eq. 8): R_00 = 1.240 A^{1/3} [1 + 1.646/A - 0.191 (A-2Z)/A] fm, valid for even-even nuclei with 8 ≤ Z < 38

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous comparative study of fusion models for symmetric colliding nuclei (Dutt and Puri, 2009) to asymmetric systems with larger neutron/proton content. The study tests refinements of the original proximity potential by Blocki et al. (1977), including modifications by Reisdorf (Prox 88), Myers and Swiatecki (Prox 00), and Royer and Rousseau (Prox 00DP), as well as potentials by Bass (1973, 1977, 1980), Christensen and Winther (1976), Broglia and Winther (1991), Aage Winther (1995), Ngo (1980), and Denisov (2002). The results enable selection of the most reliable proximity-type potentials for predicting fusion barriers and cross sections in reactions involving neutron-rich or neutron-deficient nuclei, which is relevant for radioactive-ion beam experiments.
