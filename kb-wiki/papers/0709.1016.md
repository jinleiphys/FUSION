# Charge Form Factor and Cluster Structure of $^6$Li Nucleus

> The charge form factor of ${}^6$Li nucleus is considered on the basis of its cluster structure. The charge density of ${}^6$Li is presented as a superposition of two terms. One of them is a folded density and the second one is a sum of ${}^4$He and the deuteron densities. Using the available experimental data for ${}^4$He and deuteron charge form factors, a good agreement of the calculations within the suggested scheme is obtained with the experimental data for the charge form factor of ${}^6$Li, including those in the region of large transferred momenta.

## Key claim

The charge form factor of $^6$Li can be described up to large transferred momenta by a superposition of a folded density (from $^4$He and deuteron) and a direct sum of the $^4$He and deuteron densities, but the folding term alone fails for $q > 2.7$ fm$^{-1}$ and the additional shell-model cluster term is needed only with a small weight (about 2.5%–1.5%).

## Method

The charge density of $^6$Li was constructed as a weighted sum of two terms: (i) a folding of the experimental charge densities of $^4$He and the deuteron, and (ii) a direct sum of the $^4$He and deuteron charge densities. The corresponding charge form factor was computed in the Plane-Wave Born Approximation with a center-of-mass correction factor, using experimental $^4$He form factor data and a parametrized fit to the deuteron charge form factor from Jefferson Lab. The single free parameter $c_1$ (with $c_2 = 1 - c_1$) was adjusted to reproduce the $^6$Li charge form factor data over the full measured $q$ range.

## Key numbers

- The best-fit weight coefficients are $c_1 = 0.979$ and $c_2 = 0.021$, with an acceptable interval $c_1 = 0.975$–$0.985$ (and $c_2 = 0.025$–$0.015$).
- The folding term describes the $^6$Li charge form factor well for $0 < q \lesssim 2.7$ fm$^{-1}$; the shell-model cluster term becomes important for $q \gtrsim 3$ fm$^{-1}$.
- The calculated charge rms radius of $^6$Li is $2.684$ fm, compared to the experimental value $2.57(10)$ fm.
- Input rms radii used: $^4$He $1.676(8)$ fm, deuteron $2.116(6)$ fm.
- The minimum of the experimental $^6$Li charge form factor occurs at $q \approx 2.9$ fm$^{-1}$, which is closer to the $^4$He minimum ($q \approx 3.2$ fm$^{-1}$) than to the deuteron minimum ($q \approx 4.2$ fm$^{-1}$).

## Context

This work extends earlier cluster-model studies of $^6$Li, particularly the Model of Nucleon Associations (MNA) which described elastic and inelastic electron scattering only up to $q \sim 2$ fm$^{-1}$ (Krumova and collaborators, earlier work). It also builds on Large-Scale Shell-Model calculations (Antonov and collaborators, 2005) that showed clustering behavior in $^{6,7}$Li. The proposed scheme enables a simple, one-parameter description of the $^6$Li charge form factor over a wider momentum range, and can be tested against future electron-scattering data on radioactive beams at facilities like GSI and RIKEN.
