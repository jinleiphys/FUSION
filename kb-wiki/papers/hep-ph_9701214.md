# Macroscopic Parity Violation and Supernova Asymmetries

> Core collapse supernovae are dominated by weakly interacting neutrinos. This provides a unique opportunity for macroscopic parity violation. We speculate that parity violation in a strong magnetic field can lead to an asymmetry in the explosion and a recoil of the newly formed neutron star. We estimate the asymmetry from neutrino-polarized-neutron elastic scattering, polarized electron capture and neutrino-nucleus elastic scattering in a (partially) polarized electron gas.

# Macroscopic Parity Violation and Supernova Asymmetries

## Key claim

Parity violation from known weak interactions in a strong magnetic field can produce a neutrino flux asymmetry and consequent neutron star recoil, but the calculated asymmetries (a few times 10⁻⁴ B₁₄) are too small to directly explain observed neutron star velocities of ~500 km/s unless very strong non-dipole magnetic fields (up to 10¹⁶ Gauss) exist or nonlinear dynamics amplify the effect.

## Method

The authors estimate the neutrino asymmetry from three microscopic parity-violating processes in a supernova environment with a strong magnetic field: neutrino elastic scattering from polarized neutrons, neutrino-nucleus elastic scattering in a polarized electron gas (using a phase-space estimate of the electron particle-hole polarization tensor), and polarized electron capture on protons. For each reaction, they compute the asymmetry as a function of the magnetic field strength B₁₄ (in units of 10¹⁴ Gauss), temperature, and electron Fermi momentum, then compare the resulting neutrino asymmetry A_ν to the ~1% needed to produce observed neutron star recoil velocities.

## Key numbers

- Neutron star recoil velocity: v/c ≈ 0.1 (A_ν + A_m), where A_ν and A_m are asymmetries in neutrino and matter momentum; observed velocities of ~500 km/s require A_ν or A_m of about 1%.
- Neutrino asymmetry from polarized neutron elastic scattering: |A_ν| ≈ 1.3 × 10⁻⁴ B₁₄ at a temperature near 3 MeV.
- Neutrino asymmetry from neutrino-nucleus elastic scattering in a polarized electron gas: |A_ν| ≈ 5 × 10⁻⁵ B₁₄ for nuclei near ⁵⁶Fe.
- Neutrino asymmetry from polarized electron capture on protons: |A_ν| ≈ 2.4 × 10⁻⁴ B₁₄ at density ~10¹¹ g/cm³ and electron fraction ~0.1 (k_F ≈ 10 MeV).
- Natural size of asymmetry for nondegenerate conditions: eB/E_ν² ≈ 0.6 × 10⁻² B₁₄ for neutrino energy E_ν ≈ 10 MeV.
- Longitudinal asymmetry for neutrino-polarized-neutron elastic scattering: |A_l| = 2 g_v g_a / (g_v² + 3 g_a²) ≈ 0.46, with g_v = -1 and g_a = -1.26.

## Context

This paper extends earlier work on neutrino asymmetries in magnetic fields by Vilenkin (1995) and Bezchastnov and Haensel (1996) on neutrino-electron scattering, and by Horowitz and Wehrberger (1991) on electron screening in neutrino-nucleus scattering. It also connects to the proposal by Burrows and Hayes (1996) that preexisting asymmetries in the collapsing core could produce neutron star kicks and detectable gravitational radiation. The results provide a benchmark for comparing known weak-interaction mechanisms against more speculative possibilities such as neutrino magnetic moments (Chugai, 1984) or matter-enhanced neutrino oscillations (Kusenko and Segre, 1996). The work enables future simulations incorporating small microscopic asymmetries to test whether nonlinear supernova dynamics can amplify them to the ~1% level needed to explain observed neutron star velocities.
