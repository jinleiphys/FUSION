# Angular momentum content of the rho-meson in lattice QCD

> The variational method allows one to study the mixing of interpolators with different chiral transformation properties in the non-perturbatively determined physical state. It is then possible to define and calculate in a gauge-invariant manner the chiral as well as the partial wave content of the quark-antiquark component of a meson in the infrared, where mass is generated. Using a unitary transformation from the chiral basis to the LSJ basis one may extract a partial wave content of a meson. We present results for the ground state of the rho-meson using quenched simulations as well as simulations with two dynamical quarks, all for lattice spacings close to 0.15 fm. We point out that these results indicate a simple 3S1-wave composition of the rho-meson in the infrared, like in the SU(6) flavor-spin quark model.

# Angular momentum content of the rho-meson in lattice QCD

## Key claim

The quark-antiquark component of the ground-state rho-meson in the infrared (at the scale where mass is generated) is almost purely a 3S1 partial wave, consistent with SU(6) flavor-spin quark model expectations. This conclusion holds for both quenched and dynamical simulations at lattice spacings close to 0.15 fm, with an extrapolation toward the physical point giving a ratio of contributions compatible with sqrt(2) within errors.

## Method

The authors used the variational method on a 2x2 correlation matrix built from four interpolating operators (gamma^i and gamma^t gamma^i, each with two smearing widths of 0.25 fm and 0.41 fm) for the rho-meson channel (JPC = 1--). They computed the ratio a1/a3 and a2/a4 from Eq. (13) to determine the relative contributions of the (0,1)+(1,0) and (1/2,1/2)_b chiral representations, then applied a known unitary transformation from the chiral basis to the LSJ basis to extract the partial wave content. Simulations used the Luscher-Weisz gauge action and the chirally improved (CI) Dirac operator on 16^3 x 32 lattices.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacing: a = 0.1480(10) fm (quenched), 0.1507(17) fm (dynamical ensemble A), 0.1500(12) fm (dynamical ensemble B), 0.1440(12) fm (dynamical ensemble C)
* Pion masses: 475-1053 MeV (quenched), 526(7) MeV (dyn. A), 469(4) MeV (dyn. B), 318(5) MeV (dyn. C)
* Rho-meson masses: 912-1251 MeV (quenched), 922(17) MeV (dyn. A), 897(13) MeV (dyn. B), 810(28) MeV (dyn. C)
* Extrapolated ratio a1/a3 toward the physical point: approximately 1.35, compatible with sqrt(2) within errors
* Smearing widths: narrow = 0.25 fm, wide = 0.41 fm
* Ratio from dynamical calculations with point interpolators at a = 0.114 fm: approximately 1.6 (from AlAnAo08)

## Context

This work extends the variational method studies of the hadron spectrum by the same authors in quenched (BuGaGl04a, BuGaGl06, GaGlLa08) and dynamical (GaHaLa08) simulations, applying it specifically to extract the chiral and angular momentum content of the rho-meson. The method provides a model-independent, gauge-invariant explanation for the success of the SU(6) flavor-spin quark model for the rho-meson, and enables future decomposition studies of other mesons. The results contrast with the expectation at higher resolution (Q^2 to infinity) where the pseudotensor current decouples and the (0,1)+(1,0) representation should dominate.
