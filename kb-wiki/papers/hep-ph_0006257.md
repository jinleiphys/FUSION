# The Effective Pressure of a Saturated Gluon Plasma

> The evolution of the gluon plasma produced with saturation initial conditions is calculated via transport theory for nuclear collisions with 0.1 <sqrt(s) <10 A TeV. The effective longitudinal pressure is found to remain significantly below the lattice QCD pressure with these initial conditions until the plasma cools to near the confinement scale. The absolute value of the transverse energy per unit of rapidity and its dependence on beam energy is shown to provide a sensitive test of gluon saturation models since the fractional transverse energy loss due to final state interactions is predicted to be much smaller and exhibit a weaker energy dependence than nondissipative hydrodynamics applied throughout the evolution.

## Key claim

The effective longitudinal pressure of a saturated gluon plasma remains significantly below the lattice QCD equilibrium pressure until the plasma cools to near the confinement scale, because asymptotic freedom causes the ratio of the relaxation rate to the expansion rate to vanish as center-of-mass energy increases, leading to a long period of dissipative, non-equilibrium expansion.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation for a gluon plasma produced with saturation initial conditions from the EKRT model, using a relaxation rate that includes both elastic and inelastic (radiative) energy loss contributions. The energy density evolution is computed from the first energy moment of the transport equation, with the effective longitudinal pressure defined by the exponent delta in the relation E_T(tau)/E_T(tau_0) = (tau_0/tau)^delta. The calculation uses initial conditions at tau_0 = 1/p_sat and evolves the system until the energy density reaches approximately 2 GeV/fm^3 near the confinement scale.

## Key numbers

- For RHIC at sqrt(s) = 200 A GeV, the effective pressure reaches the LQCD curve at tau_L = 1-2 fm, when the energy density has dropped to epsilon_L = 6.5-12 GeV/fm^3 (for K_in = 1-2)
- For LHC at sqrt(s) = 5400 A GeV, tau_L = 3-7 fm, with epsilon_L = 9.5-21.5 GeV/fm^3 (for K_in = 1-2)
- The logarithmic slope kappa = d log E_T^f / d log sqrt(s) is 0.50 for K_in = 1, 0.46 for K_in = 2, compared to 0.40 for isentropic hydrodynamic expansion and 0.59 for the initial EKRT saturated E_T^i
- The saturation scale p_sat from EKRT parametrization: p_sat = 0.208 A^0.128 sqrt(s)^0.191 (p_sat and sqrt(s) in GeV)
- Minijet multiplicities predicted for LHC: dN/dy = 3200 for beta = 1 versus dN/dy = 5100 for beta(p_sat) using the Gexpr formula

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of dissipative effects in heavy-ion collisions (Hosoya and Kajantie 1985, Danielewicz and Gyulassy 1985, Heiselberg and Wang 1996) to the novel initial conditions suggested by gluon saturation models (EKRT 2000, Mueller 2000, Krasnitz and Venugopalan 2000). The results show that the beam energy dependence of the transverse energy per unit rapidity provides a sensitive test of saturation models, since dissipative effects reduce the effective longitudinal pressure and cause the final transverse energy to reflect the initial conditions more accurately than in ideal hydrodynamic scenarios. The work enables using bulk calorimetric observables at RHIC and LHC to discriminate between saturation and non-saturation models of initial conditions.
