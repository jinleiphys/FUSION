# Transport coefficients from the 2PI effective action

> We show that the lowest nontrivial truncation of the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action correctly determines transport coefficients in a weak coupling or 1/N expansion at leading (logarithmic) order in several relativistic field theories. In particular, we consider a single real scalar field with cubic and quartic interactions in the loop expansion, the O(N) model in the 2PI-1/N expansion, and QED with a single and many fermion fields. Therefore, these truncations will provide a correct description, to leading (logarithmic) order, of the long time behavior of these systems, i.e. the approach to equilibrium. This supports the promising results obtained for the dynamics of quantum fields out of equilibrium using 2PI effective action techniques.

## Key claim

The lowest nontrivial truncation of the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action correctly determines transport coefficients in a weak coupling or 1/N expansion at leading (logarithmic) order, but only when the truncation includes the specific diagrams that generate the appropriate ladder integral equations with on-shell kinematic configurations.

## Method

The authors derive integral equations for the 4-point vertex function from the 2PI effective action, then specialize to on-shell momenta where pinching poles dominate. They analyze which diagrams in the kernel of these integral equations contribute at leading order in the weak coupling or 1/N expansion for each model, comparing with known diagrammatic results for transport coefficients from kinetic theory.

## Key numbers

- For a real scalar field with cubic and quartic interactions, the 3-loop approximation of the 2PI effective action is necessary and sufficient to yield the leading-order result for shear viscosity in the weak-coupling limit where lambda ~ (g/m)^2 << 1
- In the O(N) model, the 2PI-1/N expansion at next-to-leading order provides a starting point for computing shear viscosity to first nontrivial order in 1/N without restriction to small lambda
- In QED, the 2-loop approximation in the loop expansion sums the necessary diagrams to obtain shear viscosity and electrical conductivity to leading-logarithmic order
- In QED with N identical fermions and coupling e^2/N, the 2PI-1/N expansion at NLO yields the correct leading-order result for shear viscosity and electrical conductivity

## Context

This work extends the diagrammatic analysis of transport coefficients by Jeon (1995) and Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2000-2001) by showing that the same leading-order results emerge from truncations of the 2PI effective action currently used in far-from-equilibrium simulations. It provides theoretical support for numerical studies of thermalization in scalar field theories by Berges (2001-2002) and Aarts (2002), and enables systematic derivation of integral equations for transport coefficients in the O(N) model and QED that can be solved numerically.
