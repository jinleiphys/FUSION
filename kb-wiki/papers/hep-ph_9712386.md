# Running charm quark mass versus M_D and the D_(s) into μνdecay

> I study (for the first time) the dependence of M_D and of the leptonic decay constant f_D on the variation of the running charm quark mass m_c(ν). I conclude that the present data on f_{D_s} from D_s into μνdecay give a weaker constraint than M_D, where the latter leads to the result, m_c(M_c)=(1.08\pm 0.11) GeV, to two-loop accuracy in the {MS}-bar scheme, in good agreement with the value m_c(M_c)=(1.23^{+0.04}_{-0.05}) GeV extracted directly, within the same approximation, from M_{J/ψ}. The agreement of the corresponding perturbative pole mass with the one extracted directly from the data can indicate that the non-perturbative effects to the pole mass are negligible. Inversely, injecting the average value of m_c(M_c) from M_D and M_{J/ψ} into the m_c behaviour of f_D, I obtain f_D\simeq (1.52\pm 0.16)f_π, which combined with the sum rule prediction for f_{D_s}/f_D, gives f_{D_s}\simeq (1.75\pm 0.18)f_πin good agreement with the data. The extension of the analysis to the case of f_B and f_{D^*} is discussed.

# Running charm quark mass versus M_D and the D_(s) into μν decay

## Key claim

The paper claims that the D meson mass M_D provides a stronger constraint on the running charm quark mass than the leptonic decay constant f_Ds from D_s → μν decay data, and that the extracted running mass agrees with the value obtained from J/ψ mass within two-loop accuracy in the MS-bar scheme, indicating that non-perturbative effects on the pole mass are negligible.

## Method

The author uses QCD spectral sum rules (SVZ-type) for the pseudoscalar two-point correlator of the heavy-light quark current with D meson quantum numbers, evaluated to two-loop accuracy. The Laplace transform sum rules are applied to extract the running charm quark mass m_c(ν) by requiring that the sum rule prediction reproduces the experimental value of M_D = 1.865 GeV, with stability criteria imposed on the sum rule variable τ and the continuum threshold t_c.

## Key numbers

- Running charm quark mass from M_D: m_c(M_c) = (1.08 ± 0.11) GeV at two-loop accuracy in the MS-bar scheme
- Running charm quark mass from M_J/ψ: m_c(M_c) = (1.23^{+0.04}_{-0.05}) GeV within the same two-loop approximation
- Combined average running charm mass from M_D and M_J/ψ: m_c(M_c) = (1.20 ± 0.05) GeV
- Decay constant f_D from combined running mass input: f_D ≃ (1.52 ± 0.16) f_π
- Decay constant f_Ds from combined result and sum rule ratio: f_Ds ≃ (1.75 ± 0.18) f_π
- Experimental average of f_Ds from D_s → μν decay: f_Ds ≃ (1.92 ± 0.23) f_π
- Ratio f_Ds/f_D from QSSR: 1.15 ± 0.04, implying f_D ≃ (1.67 ± 0.24) f_π
- QCD scale used: Λ = (325 ± 100) MeV for four active flavours

## Context

This work extends the determination of the running charm quark mass using heavy-light meson systems, complementing earlier extractions from charmonium (J/ψ) by Narison (1997). The analysis uses the same two-loop QCD spectral sum rule framework previously applied to pole mass determinations (Narison, SNB). The agreement between running mass and pole mass results suggests that non-perturbative corrections to the pole mass are small in this channel. The method enables improved predictions for f_D and f_Ds, and the author discusses extension to f_B and f_D*, though the B meson case is less conclusive due to stronger dependence on the subtraction scale.
