# $CP$ violation in $η^{(\prime)}\toπ^+π^-μ^+μ^-$ decays

> It has been pointed out recently that a certain set of dimension-6 scalar $P$- and $CP$-violating light-quark-muon operators may be tested in $η$ and $η'$ decays to various final states involving $μ^+μ^-$ pairs, at a level not yet excluded by constraints from electric dipole moments. We here work out the hadronic matrix elements required for the predictions for the decays $η, η' \toπ^+π^-μ^+μ^-$. We relate a new asymmetry in the angular distribution between the dipion and dimuon decay planes to the corresponding Wilson coefficients. Despite the advantage of not requiring the measurement of muon polarization, the projected sensitivities are shown to be moderate due to a combination of small phase space and chiral suppression. Such sensitivity studies are most timely in view of upcoming or planned high-statistics $η^{(\prime)}$ experiments such as the JLab eta factory or REDTOP.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a new $CP$-violating angular asymmetry $A_{\phi}$ in $\eta^{(\prime)}\to\pi^+\pi^-\mu^+\mu^-$ decays, arising from dimension-6 scalar quark-muon operators, is sensitive to new physics only at moderate levels due to small phase space and chiral suppression, despite not requiring muon polarization measurements.

## Method

The authors computed the Standard Model amplitude for $\eta^{(\prime)}\to\pi^+\pi^-\mu^+\mu^-$ using a dispersive description with Omnès functions for the $\pi\pi$ final-state interactions and a factorized vector form factor for the dilepton system. They evaluated the beyond-Standard-Model pseudoscalar matrix elements $\langle\pi^+\pi^-|P^{q,s}|\eta^{(\prime)}\rangle$ using SU(3) chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order for the $\eta$ and large-$N_c$ chiral perturbation theory with unitarization for the $\eta'$, then related the angular asymmetry $A_{\phi}$ to the Wilson coefficients of the scalar operators $\mathcal{O}_{\ell equ}^{(1)}$ and $\mathcal{O}_{\ell edq}$.

## Key numbers

- $\Gamma(\eta\to\pi^+\pi^-\mu^+\mu^-) = 8.33(40)\times10^{-15}\,\text{GeV}$ (Standard Model partial width)
- $\Gamma(\eta'\to\pi^+\pi^-\mu^+\mu^-) = 4.23(24)\times10^{-9}\,\text{GeV}$ (Standard Model partial width)
- $\mathcal{B}(\eta\to\pi^+\pi^-\mu^+\mu^-) = 6.36(39)\times 10^{-9}$ (Standard Model branching ratio)
- $\mathcal{B}(\eta'\to\pi^+\pi^-\mu^+\mu^-) = 2.25(14)\times 10^{-5}$ (Standard Model branching ratio)
- $\mathcal{F}_{\eta}(0,0,0;0) = 18.6\,\text{GeV}^{-3}$ (Wess-Zumino-Witten anomaly value for $\eta$)
- $\mathcal{F}_{\eta'}(0,0,0;0) = 26.3\,\text{GeV}^{-3}$ (Wess-Zumino-Witten anomaly value for $\eta'$)
- $B_0 = 2.39(18)\,\text{GeV}$ (quark condensate parameter at scale $2\,\text{GeV}$)
- $\theta_{81} = -17.0(1.5)^\circ$ (octet-singlet mixing angle from lattice)
- $\phi_{qs} = 37.8(1.5)^\circ$ (quark-flavor basis mixing angle)

## Context

This work extends the SMEFT-based analysis of $CP$ violation in $\eta^{(\prime)}$ decays initiated by Sanchez-Puertas (2018) and Sanchez-Puertas (2019), which focused on leptonic decays, and the study of $\eta^{(\prime)}\to\pi^0\mu^+\mu^-$ by Escribano (2022). Unlike the earlier work on $\eta^{(\prime)}\to\pi^0\mu^+\mu^-$, the $\pi^+\pi^-$ final state avoids isospin-breaking suppression and does not require muon polarimetry. The projected sensitivities are moderate, making $\eta\to\mu^+\mu^-$ still the most promising channel for upcoming facilities such as the JLab eta factory and REDTOP.
