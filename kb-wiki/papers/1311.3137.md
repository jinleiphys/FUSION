# QCD effective potential with strong U(1)_{em} magnetic fields

> We derive the analytic expression for the one-loop $SU(N_{c})$ QCD effective potential including $N_{f}$ flavor quarks which non-linearly interact with the chromo-magnetic background field and the external $U(1)_{em}$ magnetic field. After the renormalization of couplings and fields, we obtain the correct one-loop $β$ functions of both QCD and QED, and the resulting effective potential satisfies the renormalization group equation. We investigate the effect of the magnetic field on the QCD vacuum by using the effective potential, in particular for the color $SU(3)$ case with the three flavors ($u, d, s$). Our result shows that the chromo-magnetic field prefers to be parallel to the external magnetic field. Furthermore, quark loop contributions to the effective potential with the magnetic field enhance gluonic contributions, and then the chromo-magnetic condensate increases with an increasing magnetic field. This result supports the recent observed gluonic magnetic catalysis at zero-temperature in lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The one-loop QCD effective potential in a pure chromomagnetic background field and a strong external U(1)ₑₘ magnetic field shows that the chromomagnetic field prefers to be parallel (or antiparallel) to the external magnetic field, and that quark loop contributions enhance gluonic contributions, causing the chromomagnetic condensate to increase with increasing magnetic field, supporting the gluonic magnetic catalysis observed in lattice QCD at zero temperature.

## Method

The author derives the analytic expression for the one-loop SU(Nc) QCD effective potential including Nf flavor quarks that nonlinearly interact with a covariantly constant chromomagnetic background field and a constant external U(1)ₑₘ magnetic field, using Schwinger's proper time method. The quark propagator is evaluated in the combined Abelianized gauge field obtained by diagonalizing the color matrix n̂ATA, and the resulting effective potential is renormalized to obtain the correct one-loop β functions of both QCD and QED. The analysis focuses on the SU(3) case with three flavors (u, d, s) and a pure chromomagnetic background.

- For the SU(3) case, the eigenvalues wa of the color matrix n̂ATA satisfy ∑a wa² = 1/2 and ∑a wa = 0.
- The finite part of the quark effective potential in the massless limit is Vqfin = ∑a ∑i { - (aa,i²)/(24π²) [ log(aa,i) - cq ] }, where cq = 12 log G - log 2 and G = 1.282427... is the Glaisher constant.

## Context

This work extends the analytic calculation of the QCD effective potential in a chromomagnetic background (Savvidy 1977, Matinyan 1976, Nielsen 1978, Leutwyler 1980) to include a strong external U(1)ₑₘ magnetic field via quark loop contributions. It confirms the numerical results of Galilo and Nedelko (2011) that the chromomagnetic field prefers to be parallel to the external magnetic field, and is consistent with recent SU(2) lattice QCD simulations (Ilgenfritz 2012, 2013) and SU(3) lattice QCD simulations (Bali 2013) that observed gluonic magnetic catalysis at zero temperature. The analytic expression enables further investigation of how the QCD vacuum responds to strong magnetic fields without relying on numerical integration of the proper time integral.
