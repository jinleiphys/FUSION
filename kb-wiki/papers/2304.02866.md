# Constraining beyond the Standard Model nucleon isovector charges

> At the TeV scale, low-energy precision observations of neutron characteristics provide unique probes of novel physics. Precision studies of neutron decay observables are susceptible to beyond the Standard Model (BSM) tensor and scalar interactions, while the neutron electric dipole moment, $d_n$, also has high sensitivity to new BSM CP-violating interactions. To fully utilise the potential of future experimental neutron physics programs, matrix elements of appropriate low-energy effective operators within neutron states must be precisely calculated. We present results from the QCDSF/UKQCD/CSSM collaboration for the isovector charges $g_T,~g_A$ and $g_S$ of the nucleon, $Σ$ and $Ξ$ baryons using lattice QCD methods and the Feynman-Hellmann theorem. We use a flavour symmetry breaking method to systematically approach the physical quark mass using ensembles that span five lattice spacings and multiple volumes. We extend this existing flavour breaking expansion to also account for lattice spacing and finite volume effects in order to quantify all systematic uncertainties. Our final estimates of the nucleon isovector charges are $g_T~=~1.010(21)_{\text{stat}}(12)_{\text{sys}},~g_A=1.253(63)_{\text{stat}}(41)_{\text{sys}}$ and $g_S~=~1.08(21)_{\text{stat}}(03)_{\text{sys}}$ renormalised, where appropriate, at $μ=2~\text{GeV}$ in the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme.

## Key claim
The paper presents lattice QCD calculations of the nucleon isovector tensor, axial, and scalar charges with quantified systematic uncertainties from lattice spacing and finite volume effects, but the results are obtained using a flavour symmetry breaking expansion about the SU(3) symmetric point rather than a direct chiral extrapolation, and the quoted systematic errors reflect the fit window dependence and the expansion procedure rather than a full assessment of all possible systematic effects.

## Method
The authors use the Feynman-Hellmann theorem on \(N_f = 2 + 1\) dynamical fermion ensembles with tree-level Symanzik improved gluon action and non-perturbatively O(a) improved Wilson fermions, spanning five lattice spacings (0.052 to 0.082 fm) and multiple volumes. They extract energy shifts from ratios of perturbed to unperturbed two-point correlation functions at multiple values of the background field parameter \(\lambda\), then perform linear fits to obtain the quark-level tensor, axial, and scalar charges for up and down quarks. The nucleon isovector charges are obtained as the difference \(g^{u-d} = g^u - g^d\), and a weighted averaging method over fit windows is used to combine statistical and systematic uncertainties from the choice of Euclidean time ranges.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon isovector tensor charge: \(g_T = 1.010(21)_{\text{stat}}(12)_{\text{sys}}\), renormalised at \(\mu = 2\) GeV in the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme.
- Nucleon isovector axial charge: \(g_A = 1.253(63)_{\text{stat}}(41)_{\text{sys}}\), renormalised at \(\mu = 2\) GeV in the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme.
- Nucleon isovector scalar charge: \(g_S = 1.08(21)_{\text{stat}}(03)_{\text{sys}}\), renormalised at \(\mu = 2\) GeV in the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme.
- For a representative ensemble at \(a = 0.068\) fm: \(g_T^u = 0.822(27)\), \(g_T^d = -0.263(25)\), \(g_A^u = 0.814(56)\), \(g_A^d = -0.316(26)\), \(g_S^u = 4.03(29)\), \(g_S^d = 3.04(17)\), all renormalised at \(\mu = 2\) GeV in the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme.

## Context
This work extends the QCDSF/UKQCD/CSSM collaboration's ongoing program using the Feynman-Hellmann theorem for hadronic matrix elements (Horsley 2012, Chambers 2017, Can 2020) to a dedicated study of the nucleon tensor, scalar, and axial charges. The results are relevant for interpreting neutron beta decay experiments searching for beyond the Standard Model tensor and scalar interactions, as well as for constraining the neutron electric dipole moment from quark electric dipole moments. The flavour symmetry breaking expansion method follows the approach of Bickerton (2019) and provides an alternative to chiral perturbation theory extrapolations used by other lattice collaborations such as those in Chang (2018) and Walker-Loud (2020).
