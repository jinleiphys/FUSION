# Single-flavor CSL phase in compact stars

> We suggest a scenario where the three light quark flavors are sequentially deconfined under increasing pressure in cold asymmetric nuclear matter as e.g. in neutron stars. The basis for our analysis is a chiral quark matter model of Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type with diquark pairing in the spin-1 single flavor (CSL), spin-0 two flavor (2SC) and three flavor (CFL) channels. We find that nucleon dissociation sets in at about the saturation density, n_0, when the down-quark Fermi sea is populated (d-quark dripline) due to the flavor asymmetry induced by beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality. At about 3n_0 u-quarks appear and a two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase is formed. The s-quark Fermi sea is populated only at still higher baryon density, when the quark chemical potential is of the order of the dynamically generated strange quark mass. We construct two different hybrid equations of state (EoS) using the Dirac-Brueckner Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach and the EoS by Shen et al in the nuclear matter sector. The corresponding hybrid star sequences have maximum masses of, respectively, 2.1 and 2.0 M_solar. Two- and three-flavor quark-matter phases exist only in gravitationally unstable hybrid star solutions in the DBHF case, while the Shen-based EoS produce stable configurations with a 2SC phase component in the core of massive stars. Nucleon dissociation due to d-quark drip at the crust-core boundary fulfills basic criteria for a deep crustal heating process which is required to explain superbusts as well as cooling of X-ray transients.

# Single-flavor CSL phase in compact stars

## Key claim

The authors suggest that in cold asymmetric nuclear matter (as in neutron stars), the three light quark flavors are sequentially deconfined under increasing pressure, with down quarks appearing first at the crust-core boundary to form a single-flavor color-spin-locked (CSL) phase mixed with nuclear matter. This scenario depends on the flavor asymmetry induced by beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality, and the authors note that the single-flavor d-quark phase had previously been disregarded because it is negatively charged and cannot be neutralized in a purely leptonic background.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid equations of state by combining a nuclear matter sector (using either the Dirac-Brueckner Hartree-Fock approach with the Bonn A potential or the Shen et al. relativistic mean field EoS) with a three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type chiral quark model that includes diquark pairing in the spin-1 single flavor (CSL), spin-0 two flavor (2SC), and three flavor (CFL) channels. The mean-field thermodynamic potential is minimized under constraints of charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium, and the resulting hybrid EoS are used to solve the Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for hydrostatic equilibrium to obtain compact star sequences.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon dissociation (d-quark dripline) sets in at about the saturation density, n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- u-quarks appear at about 3n_0, forming a two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase
- Maximum hybrid star masses are 2.1 M_sun for the DBHF-based EoS and 2.0 M_sun for the Shen-based EoS
- Energy release from conversion of DBHF nuclear matter to DBHF-CSL hybrid matter gives 12 MeV for a 1.4 M_sun star and 8 MeV for a 2.0 M_sun star
- The direct URCA emissivity for normal quark matter is given by epsilon_0 approximately (914 pi / 1680) G_F^2 mu_e mu_u mu_d T^6 theta_ue^2
- Superburst ignition requires crust temperatures around 6 x 10^8 K and column depths of about 10^12 g cm^{-2}
- The strange star model requires energy release of 1 to 100 MeV per accreted nucleon and thermal conductivity of quark matter in the range 10^19 to 10^22 erg cm^{-1} s^{-1} K^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the hybrid star EoS developed by Klahn et al. (2006) and Blaschke et al. (2005), which used a three-flavor chiral quark model with strong diquark coupling and repulsive vector meanfield to achieve stable hybrid stars up to 2.1 M_sun. The sequential deconfinement scenario contrasts with simple thermodynamic bag models, which are flavor-blind and cannot address this behavior. The authors argue that the d-quark drip effect at the crust-core boundary can serve as a deep crustal heating mechanism, potentially explaining superbursts (previously attributed to strange stars in the CFL phase by Page et al., 2005) and the cooling of X-ray transients, without requiring strange quark matter.
