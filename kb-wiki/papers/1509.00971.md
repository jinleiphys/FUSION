# Towards an ab initio description of the light-nuclei radiative captures

> The ${^3{\rm He}}(α,γ){^7{\rm Be}}$ and ${^3{\rm H}}(α,γ){^7{\rm Li}}$ astrophysical $S$ factors are evaluated at low collision energies (less than 2.5 MeV in the centre-of-mass frame) within the no-core shell model with continuum approach using a renormalized chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Key claim

The no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) approach, using a renormalized chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction restricted to two-body terms, can qualitatively reproduce the experimental ${^3{\rm He}}(\alpha,\gamma){^7{\rm Be}}$ astrophysical $S$ factor but overestimates the ${^3{\rm H}}(\alpha,\gamma){^7{\rm Li}}$ astrophysical $S$ factor; an accurate reproduction would require the inclusion of three-nucleon forces.

## Method

The authors applied the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) approach, combining no-core shell model (NCSM) eigenstates of the compound $^7{\rm Be}$ or $^7{\rm Li}$ systems with $\alpha+{^3{\rm He}}$ or $\alpha+{^3{\rm H}}$ cluster states built via the resonating group method (RGM). They used a chiral N$^3$LO nucleon-nucleon interaction softened by the similarity-renormalization-group (SRG) method with $\Lambda=2.15~{\rm fm}^{-1}$, restricted to two-body terms, and solved the seven-nucleon Schr\"odinger equation variationally with the microscopic $R$-matrix method to impose proper asymptotic boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

- SRG parameter set to $\Lambda=2.15~{\rm fm}^{-1}$ to approximately reproduce separation energies of $^7{\rm Be}$ and $^7{\rm Li}$ bound states.
- Harmonic oscillator frequency set to $\Omega=20$~MeV$/\hbar$ for both NCSM and NCSM/RGM states.
- Positive-parity NCSM states obtained with $N_{\rm max}=11$ and negative-parity states with $N_{\rm max}=10$; NCSM/RGM states used $N_{\rm max}=12$ for $^4{\rm He}$, $^3{\rm He}$, and $^3{\rm H}$ ground states.
- ${^3{\rm He}}(\alpha,\gamma){^7{\rm Be}}$ astrophysical $S$ factors evaluated for collision energies less than 2.5~MeV in the centre-of-mass frame.
- ${^3{\rm H}}(\alpha,\gamma){^7{\rm Li}}$ astrophysical $S$ factors evaluated for collision energies less than 1.2~MeV in the centre-of-mass frame.

## Context

This work extends the NCSMC approach (Barrett, Navrátil, and Quaglioni, 2013) to radiative-capture reactions, providing the first ab initio description of ${^3{\rm He}}(\alpha,\gamma){^7{\rm Be}}$ and ${^3{\rm H}}(\alpha,\gamma){^7{\rm Li}}$ using a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction. The results are compared to experimental data from multiple groups (e.g., Nara Singh et al., 2004; Brown et al., 2007; Bordeanu et al., 2013) and to the microscopic approach of Neff (2011), which showed a similar overestimation for the ${^3{\rm H}}(\alpha,\gamma){^7{\rm Li}}$ reaction. The next step is to include three-nucleon forces to improve the accuracy of the astrophysical $S$ factors.
