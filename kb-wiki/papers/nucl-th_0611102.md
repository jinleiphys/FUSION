# The Van-der-Waals Gas EOS for the Lorentz Contracted Spheres

> The relativistic equation of state (EOS) of the Van-der-Waals gas is suggested and analyzed. In contrast to the usual case, the Lorentz contraction of the sphere's volume is taken into account. It is proven that the suggested EOS obeys the causality in the limit of high densities, i.e., the value of sound velocity of such a media is subluminar. The pressure obtained for the high values of chemical potential has an interesting kinetic interpretation. The suggested EOS shows that for high densities the most probable configuration corresponds to the smallest value of the relativistic excluded volume. In other words, for high densities the configurations with the collinear velocities of the neighboring hard core particles are the most probable ones. This, perhaps, may shed light on the coalescence process of any relativistic hard core constituents.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a relativistic van der Waals equation of state for Lorentz-contracted hard spheres that, unlike previous relativistic generalizations, obeys causality (sound speed does not exceed light speed) in the high-density limit, provided the momentum-dependent excluded volume is averaged inside the exponential of the pressure expression rather than approximated by a constant second virial coefficient.

## Method

The author generalizes the cluster and virial expansions to momentum-dependent hard-core potentials, deriving a transcendental equation for pressure where the excluded volume depends on particle momenta and the angle between their velocities. An analytic approximation for the relativistic excluded volume of two Lorentz-contracted spheres is used to perform momentum integrations analytically in the high-pressure limit, yielding a self-consistent equation for the most probable particle energy. The high chemical potential limit (\(\mu/T \gg 1\)) and the high temperature limit (\(T/\mu \gg 1\)) are analyzed separately to extract the equation of state and sound speed.

## Key numbers

- The second virial coefficient for pions at \(T = 140\) MeV is reduced by 50% compared to the nonrelativistic value due to Lorentz contraction.
- In the high-temperature limit \(T \gg m\), the second virial coefficient approaches \(a_2(T) \to \frac{3\pi\alpha v_{\rm o}}{8} + O(m/T)\), where \(\alpha \approx 1/1.065\) and \(v_{\rm o}\) is the eigenvolume.
- In the high chemical potential limit (\(\mu/T \gg 1\)), the pressure scales as \(p \approx \mu^2 / (32\, m\, v_{\rm o})\) and the sound speed squared is \(c_s^2 = 1\).
- The most probable particle energy in the high chemical potential limit is \(E^* \approx \mu/4\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic virial expansion for Lorentz-contracted hard spheres developed by Bugaev (2000) and addresses the causality problem identified by Raju (1992) in earlier relativistic van der Waals models. The proposed equation of state enables consistent inclusion of relativistic hard-core repulsion in effective models of hadronic matter, such as the modified Walecka model (Rischke, 1991), Nambu–Jona-Lasinio extensions, and quark-meson coupling models, particularly at high densities and temperatures relevant to heavy-ion collisions and the quark-gluon plasma crossover region.
