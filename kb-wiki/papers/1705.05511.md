# Ab initio electromagnetic observables with the in-medium similarity renormalization group

> We present the formalism for consistently transforming transition operators within the in-medium similarity renormalization group framework. We implement the operator transformation in both the equations-of-motion and valence-space variants, and present first results for electromagnetic transitions and moments in medium-mass nuclei using consistently-evolved operators, including the induced two-body parts. These results are compared to experimental values, and--where possible--the results of no-core shell model calculations using the same input chiral interaction. We find good agreement between the equations-of-motion and valence space approaches. Magnetic dipole observables are generally in reasonable agreement with experiment, while the more collective electric quadrupole and octupole observables are significantly underpredicted, often by over an order of magnitude, indicating missing physics at the present level of truncation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that consistently evolved electromagnetic transition operators can be computed within the IMSRG framework for medium-mass nuclei, but electric quadrupole and octupole observables are significantly underpredicted (often by over an order of magnitude), indicating missing physics at the present level of operator truncation to two-body terms.

## Method

The authors implement operator transformation within both the equations-of-motion IMSRG (EOM-IMSRG) and valence-space IMSRG (VS-IMSRG) variants, using commutator expressions for spherical tensor operators truncated at the two-body level. They perform IMSRG evolution of the Hamiltonian and transition operators consistently, then compute electromagnetic transitions and moments by evaluating matrix elements with IMSRG-transformed wave functions, using either EOM ladder operators or valence-space diagonalization with NuShellX@MSU.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic dipole observables show reasonable agreement with experiment across the nuclei studied
- Electric quadrupole and octupole observables are underpredicted "often by over an order of magnitude" compared to experiment
- For the deuteron in a 0s valence space, the IMSRG reproduces full configuration interaction results exactly (the bare quadrupole moment is identically zero, so any non-zero value comes from IMSRG evolution)
- For $^{14}$C, the center-of-mass energy for the ground state is below 100 keV, while the $2^+_1$ state has $E_{\text{c.m.}} = 1.298$ MeV without a center-of-mass trap, reduced to below 100 keV with the Lawson-Gloeckner term
- The chiral NN interaction uses cutoff $\Lambda_{NN}=500$ MeV and 3N interaction cutoff $\Lambda_{3N}=400$ MeV, with SRG evolution to $\lambda_{SRG}=2.0$ fm$^{-1}$
- Three-body energy truncation $E_{3max} \leq 14$ is used

## Context

This work extends the IMSRG framework (previously successful for binding energies, radii, and spectra) to consistently evolved electromagnetic transition operators, addressing a long-standing challenge in nuclear theory. It builds on prior operator transformation efforts using the Okubo-Lee-Suzuki method (Navratil 1997) and other approaches (Anderson 2010, Paar 2006, Schuster 2014, Stetcu 2005, More 2015). The results enable future calculations of quantities relevant for beyond-Standard-Model physics such as neutrinoless double beta decay, and demonstrate that the success of diagrammatic-expansion methods for energies and radii does not automatically carry over to all observables, particularly collective electric transitions.
