# Ab initio study of Z(N) = 6 magicity

> The existence of magic numbers of protons and neutrons in nuclei is essential for understanding nuclear structure and fundamental nuclear forces. Over decades, researchers have conducted theoretical and experimental studies on the new magic number Z(N) = 6, focusing on observables such as radii, binding energy, electromagnetic transition, and nucleon separation energies. We perform the ab initio no-core shell model calculations for the occupation numbers of the lowest single particle states in the ground states of Z(N) = 6 and Z(N) = 8 isotopes (isotones). Our calculations do not support Z(N) = 6 as a magic number over a span of atomic numbers. However, 14C and 14O exhibit the characteristics of double-magic nuclei.

## Key claim
The paper argues that Z(N)=6 is not a universal magic number, but rather a *local magic number* whose magicity is only clearly expressed when the neutron (or proton) number lies in the interval 8 to 9. The authors specifically find that 14C and 14O exhibit double-magic characteristics, but that the magicity weakens for carbon isotopes with N<8 or N>9.

## Method
The authors performed ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations of occupation numbers in the lowest harmonic oscillator single-particle states for Z(N)=6 and Z(N)=8 isotopes and isotones. They used the Daejeon16 NN interaction (derived from chiral effective field theory and softened via similarity renormalization group transformation) in model spaces up to Nmax=10 with hbarOmega=17.5 MeV, and also performed calculations with the JISP16 NN interaction at Nmax=8 for comparison.

## Key numbers
- Proton 0p1/2 occupation number in 14C falls into the range 0.2-0.25 at Nmax=10 with Daejeon16, comparable to the 0d5/2 occupation numbers (~0.27) in Z=8 and N=8 magic nuclei
- Proton 0p1/2 occupation numbers in 10C and 12C are approximately 0.4-0.6, much larger than expected for a closed subshell
- JISP16 calculations give a proton 0p1/2 occupation number in 14C of about 0.16, approximately 30% smaller than the Daejeon16 result from the same model space
- Occupation numbers in 13O differ by less than 2% when varying hbarOmega from 15 to 20 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous studies of Z(N)=6 magicity, including the analysis of separation energy systematics by Kanungo et al. (2002) and the systematic study by Tran et al. (2017) which proposed persistent Z=6 magicity in 13-20C. The authors' NCSM calculations support the proposition of Tran et al. (2017) that 14C and 14O are double-magic nuclei, but contradict the idea of universal Z(N)=6 magicity. The results suggest that Z=6 and N=6 are local magic numbers, and the authors propose that future spectroscopic studies using transfer or knockout reactions could investigate the structural evolution in these nuclei.
