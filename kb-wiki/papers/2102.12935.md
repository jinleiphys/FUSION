# $Z_N$ symmetry in $SU(N)$ gauge theories

> We study $Z_N$ symmetry in $SU(N)$ gauge theories in the presence of matter fields in the fundamental representation, by restricting the lattice partition function integration to matter fields which are uniform in spatial directions and gauge fields with vanishing spatial components. In this approximation the gauge matter field interaction effectively reduces to a 1-dimensional gauged chain. This makes analytical calculations of the matter field contribution to the Polyakov loop free energy possible. We show that in the limit of large number of temporal sites the explicit breaking of $Z_N$ symmetry in this free energy vanishes, driven by dominance of the density of states. We argue that the spatial links as well as the spatial modes of the matter fields determine the boundaries separating regions where $Z_N$ symmetry is realised from rest of the phase diagram.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in a simplified 1-dimensional lattice model where spatial gauge fields are set to zero and matter fields are uniform in spatial directions, the explicit breaking of \(Z_N\) symmetry in the free energy vanishes in the limit of large number of temporal sites \(N_\tau\), even when the relevant couplings are finite and the action breaks the symmetry; this vanishing is driven by dominance of the density of states.

## Method

The authors restrict the lattice partition function for \(SU(N)\) gauge theories with fundamental-representation Higgs or staggered fermions to configurations where spatial gauge links are set to unity and matter fields are uniform in spatial directions, reducing the system to a 1-dimensional gauged chain. They then fix a gauge where all temporal links except the last are set to identity, integrate out the matter fields sequentially (first the interior sites, then the two sites connected to the last gauge link), and evaluate the resulting determinant or polynomial to obtain the free energy as a function of the Polyakov loop \(L = \text{Tr}(U)/N\). Recursion relations for the integration coefficients are derived for arbitrary \(N_\tau\), and explicit results are given for \(N=2\) (Higgs) and for diagonal \(U\) (fermions).

## Key numbers

- For \(SU(2)\) + Higgs, the determinant of the \(8\times 8\) matrix \(M\) is \(\text{Det}(M) = (B_{N_\tau}^2 - A_{N_\tau} C_{N_\tau} + 2b B_{N_\tau} \alpha_0 + b^2)^4\); the \(Z_2\) explicit breaking term is \(2b B_{N_\tau} \alpha_0\), and \(B_{N_\tau}\) vanishes in the large-\(N_\tau\) limit.
- For \(SU(2)\) + fermions, the partition function is \(\mathcal{Z}_L = \tilde{E}^2 + 2\tilde{E}\tilde{A}|U_{11}|^2 + \tilde{A}^2 + 2\tilde{B}\tilde{C}|U_{12}|^2 + 2(1-\tilde{D}\text{Re}(U_{11}^2)) + (\tilde{E}+\tilde{A})(1-\tilde{D})\text{tr}(U)\); the \(Z_2\) breaking term is linear in \(\tilde{E}+\tilde{A}\), which is subdominant for large \(N_\tau\).
- For general \(N\) with diagonal \(U_{rs} = \lambda_r \delta_{rs}\), the free energy is \(V(L) = -T \sum_r \left\{ \log(\tilde{E}+\tilde{A}) + \log\left(1 - (\lambda_r \tilde{D} - \lambda_r^*)/(\tilde{E}+\tilde{A})\right) \right\}\); the second term vanishes in the large-\(N_\tau\) limit for non-zero fermion mass \(m_f\).
- For fixed physical Higgs mass, the lattice parameter \(b = (m_b^2 + 8)^{-1}\) increases to \(b = 1/8\) as \(N_\tau \to \infty\) (since \(m_b \propto a_l \propto 1/N_\tau\)), but this does not prevent the vanishing of \(b B_{N_\tau}\).

## Context

This work extends perturbative one-loop calculations by Weiss (1981) and Green (1983) and non-perturbative lattice studies by Satz (1985) and Biswal et al. (2015, 2016), which showed a trend of decreasing explicit \(Z_N\) breaking with increasing \(N_\tau\). The analytical results here support the conjecture that \(Z_N\) symmetry may reemerge in the continuum limit. The authors argue that spatial links and non-zero spatial modes of matter fields, neglected in this approximation, determine the boundaries of the region where \(Z_N\) symmetry is realised, as seen in recent Monte Carlo simulations by Biswal et al. (2021).
