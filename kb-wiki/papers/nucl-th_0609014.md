# Strangeness contributions to nucleon form factors

> We review a recent theoretical determination of the strange quark content of the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon. These are compared with a global analysis of current experimental measurements in parity-violating electron scattering.

## Key claim

The paper reviews a theoretical determination of the strange quark contributions to nucleon electromagnetic form factors, finding a small negative strange magnetic moment and a tightly bounded strange electric form factor, which are compared with a global analysis of parity-violating electron scattering data; the hedge is that the theoretical result relies on indirect methods (chiral extrapolation of quenched lattice QCD combined with charge symmetry and hyperon data) rather than direct lattice calculations, and the global experimental fit shows the strangeness form factors are consistent with zero within uncertainties.

## Method

The theoretical determination uses a decomposition of baryon three-point functions in QCD, combined with charge symmetry and experimentally measured hyperon magnetic moments, to express the strange magnetic moment in terms of valence quark ratios and a disconnected-insertion ratio. These valence ratios are obtained from high-precision quenched lattice QCD simulations of baryon electromagnetic form factors, corrected for finite-volume effects and the quenched approximation, and extrapolated to the physical pion mass using finite-range regularisation (FRR) chiral perturbation theory. The disconnected-insertion ratio is estimated using the same unquenching correction method that successfully reproduced the full baryon octet magnetic moments. The experimental side performs a global fit of world parity-violating electron scattering data at \(Q^2 < 0.3\ \text{GeV}^2\) to simultaneously extract the strange electric and magnetic form factors along with the axial and anapole form factors, using a Taylor expansion for the \(Q^2\) evolution of the strangeness form factors.

## Key numbers

- Strange magnetic moment of the proton: \(G_M^s = -0.046 \pm 0.022\ \mu_N\) (nuclear magnetons), from the theoretical calculation of Leinweber et al. (2004).
- Strange electric form factor at \(Q^2 = 0.1\ \text{GeV}^2\): \(G_E^s(Q^2 = 0.1\ \text{GeV}^2) = +0.001 \pm 0.004 \pm 0.004\), from the theoretical calculation of Leinweber et al. (2006).
- HAPPEx Collaboration measurement at \(Q^2 = 0.1\ \text{GeV}^2\): \(G_E^s = -0.01 \pm 0.03\).
- Global fit best-fit parameters at \(Q^2 < 0.3\ \text{GeV}^2\): \(\rho_s = -0.06 \pm 0.41 \mp 0.00\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) (strange electric slope), \(\mu_s = 0.12 \pm 0.55 \pm 0.07\) (strange magnetic moment), \(\tilde{g}_A^p = 0.05 \pm 1.38 \mp 0.29\), \(\tilde{g}_A^n = 2.61 \pm 2.27 \mp 0.37\) (axial form factor parameters).
- Lattice simulations used pion masses down to approximately \(350\ \text{MeV}\), with chiral extrapolation from the range \(0.25 \gtrsim m_\pi^2 \gtrsim 1.0\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- FRR extrapolated nucleon mass at the physical point shows less than 1% variation associated with truncation between successive orders in the chiral expansion.

## Context

This work extends the chiral extrapolation and unquenching techniques developed by Young (2002), Leinweber (2003), and Young (2004) for baryon masses and magnetic moments, applying them to the strangeness form factors. The theoretical prediction is compared with a global analysis of parity-violating electron scattering data from the SAMPLE (1997), HAPPEx, and G0 Collaborations, as performed by Young (2006). The results enable a combined determination of the strange and anapole form factors free from theoretical input, and future measurements from HAPPEx in 2006 are expected to further constrain the flavour structure of the nucleon.
