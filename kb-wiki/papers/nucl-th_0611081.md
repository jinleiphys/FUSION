# Application of chiral nuclear forces to light nuclei

> In these proceedings, we discuss the current status of nuclear bound state predictions based on chiral nuclear interactions. Results of ordinary $s$- and $p$-shell nuclei and light hypernuclei are shown.

## Key claim

Chiral nuclear forces, when extended to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO), yield cutoff-dependent variations in binding energies that become negligible (approximately 0.1% of the potential energy), but the description of p-shell nuclei remains incomplete because the two parameter sets for three-nucleon forces that reproduce s-shell binding equally well give different predictions for heavier systems.

## Method

The author solved the Schrodinger equation for s-shell nuclei (3H, 4He) and p-shell nuclei (6Li, 7Li) using chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions at orders Q0 through Q4, combined with leading-order three-nucleon forces (3NFs) whose two unknown low-energy constants (c_D and c_E) were fixed by fitting the 3H and 4He binding energies. For hypernuclei, a leading-order chiral hyperon-nucleon (YN) interaction was used, with scattering lengths constrained to reproduce the 3ΛH binding energy. The no-core shell model (NCSM) was employed for p-shell calculations, and the momentum cutoff Λ was varied between 400 and 700 MeV to study regularization dependence.

## Key numbers

- 3H binding energy variation with cutoff: at Q0, from -7.50 MeV (Λ=500 MeV) to -6.07 MeV (Λ=600 MeV), a change of 1.43 MeV; at Q4, from -7.84 MeV (Λ=500 MeV) to -7.80 MeV (Λ=600 MeV), a change of only 0.04 MeV
- 3NF contribution to 4He potential energy: at most 7.5%, consistent with the power-counting estimate of approximately 2%
- 6Li binding energy: -30.0 MeV without 3NF, -32.3 MeV with 3NF-A, -31.1 MeV with 3NF-B (experimental value: -32.0 MeV)
- 7Li binding energy: -34.6 MeV without 3NF, -38.0 MeV with 3NF-A, -36.7 MeV with 3NF-B (experimental value: -39.2 MeV)
- 4ΛH separation energies for the 0+ state: 2.63 MeV (Λ=500 MeV) to 2.38 MeV (Λ=700 MeV); for the 1+ state: 1.85 MeV (Λ=500 MeV) to 1.04 MeV (Λ=700 MeV)
- Charge-symmetry breaking for 4ΛH-4ΛHe: less than 0.03 MeV for all cutoffs (experimental value: 0.35 MeV for 0+ state)

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to nuclear forces, building on earlier calculations for s-shell nuclei by Nogga (2002) and Pieper (2001) that showed traditional three-nucleon force models fail for p-shell nuclei. The results confirm the power-counting expectations of Epelbaum (2005) and Entem (2003) for NN interactions, and demonstrate that chiral 3NFs improve the description of p-shell spectra compared to calculations without 3NFs. For hypernuclei, this is the first application of a chiral YN interaction (developed by Polinder, 2006) to bound-state calculations, showing improvement over phenomenological models such as Julich 05 and Nijmegen SC97f, which fail to describe the 4ΛH level splitting consistently. The work enables future combined fits of NN and YN interactions at next-to-leading order, which should reduce cutoff dependence and include charge-symmetry breaking terms.
