# Probe Initial Parton Density and Formation Time via Jet Quenching

> Medium modification of jet fragmentation function due to multiple scattering and induced gluon radiation leads directly to jet quenching or suppression of leading particle distribution from jet fragmentation. One can extract an effective total parton energy loss which can be related to the total transverse momentum broadening. For an expanding medium, both are shown to be sensitive to the initial parton density and formation time. Therefore, one can extract the initial parton density and formation time from simultaneous measurements of parton energy loss and transverse momentum broadening. Implication of the recent experimental data on effects of detailed balance in parton energy loss is also discussed.

# Probe Initial Parton Density and Formation Time via Jet Quenching

## Key claim
By simultaneously measuring parton energy loss and transverse momentum broadening in heavy-ion collisions, one can extract both the initial parton density and the formation time of the dense medium; however, the extraction depends on modeling the medium expansion and requires accounting for detailed balance between gluon emission and absorption, which modifies the energy dependence of the energy loss.

## Method
The author extends the twist-4 factorization approach for parton energy loss in cold nuclear matter to a one-dimensional expanding medium in heavy-ion collisions, relating the quark-gluon correlation function to the gluon density profile. The energy loss and transverse momentum broadening are calculated using opacity expansion, assuming a Gaussian nuclear density distribution and a Debye screening mass for the medium, then fitted to PHENIX data on suppression of large transverse momentum pion spectra.

## Key numbers
- Quark energy loss inside a cold gold nucleus: \(dE/dL \approx 0.5\) GeV/fm, extracted from HERMES data with \(\widetilde{C}(Q^2)=0.0060\) GeV\(^2\) and \(\alpha_s(Q^2)=0.33\) at \(Q^2 \approx 3\) GeV\(^2\).
- Transverse momentum broadening in cold nuclear matter: \(\langle \Delta q_\perp^2\rangle \approx 0.011 A^{1/3}\) GeV\(^2\).
- Fitted average energy loss in expanding medium from PHENIX data: \(\langle dE/dL\rangle_{1d} \approx 0.34 \ln E / \ln 5\) GeV/fm.
- Equivalent static-medium energy loss for a 10-GeV parton: \((dE/dL)_0 \approx 7.3\) GeV/fm, assuming initial formation time \(\tau_0 \sim 0.2\) fm and nuclear radius \(R_A \sim 6\) fm.
- Gluon density in initial stage of Au+Au collisions at \(\tau_0 = 0.2\) fm/\(c\) is about 15 times higher than that inside a cold nucleus.

## Context
This work extends the twist-4 formalism of Guo and Wang (2001) for parton energy loss in cold nuclei to hot dense matter created in heavy-ion collisions, building on the opacity expansion approach of Gyulassy, Levai, and Vitev. The analysis uses HERMES data on nuclear modification of fragmentation functions and PHENIX data on suppression of high-\(p_T\) pions at RHIC. The results enable simultaneous extraction of initial parton density and formation time from combined measurements of energy loss and transverse momentum broadening, while highlighting the need to include detailed balance effects as in Wang and Wang (2002) to explain the observed constant suppression factor at large \(p_T\).
