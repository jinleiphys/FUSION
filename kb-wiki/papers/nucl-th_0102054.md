# Analytical approximation for the sphere-sphere Coulomb potential

## Key claim

The paper presents a simple analytical expression that closely approximates the Coulomb potential between two uniformly charged spheres, with the condition that the approximation is analytic on and near the real r-axis, which is required for semiclassical optical potential analyses. The author claims the approximation produces no physically appreciable effects in calculated cross sections, with the caveat that the parameters must be determined by fitting for each ratio of sphere radii.

## Method

The author constructs an analytical form factor for the sphere-sphere Coulomb potential using a hyperbolic tangent function with odd powers of the scaled radial coordinate x = r/(R1+R2), specifically f(x) = (1/x) tanh(a1 x + a3 x^3 + a5 x^5). The three parameters a1, a3, and a5 are determined by fitting this expression to the exact sphere-sphere form factor for each value of the radius ratio a = R1/R2, using 101 x-values equally spaced between 0 and 1.5.

## Key numbers

- For the test case of 16O on 12C at Elab = 132 MeV, with radii R1 = 3.54 fm and R2 = 3.17 fm, the best fit parameters are a1 = 2.387, a3 = 1.071, a5 = 1.683
- The maximum relative error in cross sections calculated from 1 degree to 180 degrees with a step of 0.25 degrees is lower than 0.09 percent for this test case
- In the worst case (alpha = 0, corresponding to the point-sphere limit), the maximum relative error of the best fit form factor is less than 0.8 percent
- For the point-sphere case (alpha = 0), the Brink-Takigawa approximation gives a maximum relative error smaller than 3 percent, while the even-function approximation of Anni and Renna gives a maximum relative error lower than 4 percent
- Using linear interpolation of the tabulated parameter values at intermediate alpha values, the maximum relative error does not exceed 0.21 percent for alpha values above 0.5

## Context

This work extends earlier techniques for handling the non-analyticity of Coulomb potentials in semiclassical analyses. Knoll and Schaeffer (1976) avoided the problem by using point-point Coulomb potentials for all r-values, which is only justified for strongly absorptive interactions. Brink and Takigawa (1977) approximated the point-sphere form factor with an exponential expression achieving 3 percent accuracy, while Anni and Renna (1981) used a hyperbolic tangent form for the point-sphere case. The present work generalizes the even-function approach to the sphere-sphere case by adding a fifth power term, enabling accurate semiclassical analyses of heavy-ion scattering where projectile and target have similar radii, as in the recent analysis of 16O on 12C at 132 MeV by Anni (2001).
