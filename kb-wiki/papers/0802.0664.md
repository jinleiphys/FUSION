# Nuclear expansion and symmetry energy of hot nuclei

> The decrease in the symmetry energy of hot nuclei populated in $^{58}$Ni + $^{58}$Ni, $^{58}$Fe + $^{58}$Ni and $^{58}$Fe + $^{58}$Fe reactions at beam energies of 30, 40, and 47 MeV/nucleon, as a function of excitation energy is studied. It is observed that this decrease is mainly a consequence of increasing expansion or decreasing density rather than the increasing temperature. The results are in good agreement with the recently reported microscopic calculation based on the Thomas-Fermi approach. An empirical relation to study the symmetry energy of finite nuclei in various mass region is proposed.

## Key claim

The observed decrease in the symmetry energy of hot nuclei with increasing excitation energy is mainly a consequence of decreasing density (nuclear expansion) rather than increasing temperature, based on the correlation between the isoscaling parameter, caloric curve, and break-up density extracted from statistical multifragmentation model calculations.

## Method

The authors measured fragment isotopic yields from multifragmentation of hot nuclei (A ~ 100) populated in 58Ni + 58Ni, 58Fe + 58Ni, and 58Fe + 58Fe reactions at beam energies of 30, 40, and 47 MeV/nucleon. They extracted the isoscaling parameter α from the ratio of fragment yields between neutron-rich and neutron-deficient reaction pairs, and used the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) with a multiplicity-dependent break-up density (varying from ~1/2 to 1/3 saturation density) to relate α to the symmetry energy via the relation α = (4C_sym/T)[(Z/A)_1² - (Z/A)_2²]. The excitation energy and density were determined from Boltzmann-Nordheim-Vlasov (BNV) model simulations and an expanding Fermi gas model, respectively.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies used: 30, 40, and 47 MeV/nucleon for all three reaction systems (58Ni + 58Ni, 58Fe + 58Ni, 58Fe + 58Fe)
- The break-up density in the SMM calculation was varied from approximately 1/2 to 1/3 the saturation density (ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³)
- The empirical relation for symmetry energy of finite nuclei uses parameters: α = 31-33 MeV, γ = 0.55-0.69, and α/β = 2.6-3.0 (where α is volume symmetry energy and β is surface symmetry energy at normal nuclear density)
- The empirical relation with α = 31.6 MeV, γ = 0.69, and α/β = 2.6 was found to agree well with Thomas-Fermi calculations for masses A = 40, 150, and 197

## Context

This work extends previous studies of symmetry energy in multifragmentation (Shetty 2004, Iglio 2006, Fevre 2005, Hen 2005) by systematically examining its excitation energy dependence and demonstrating that the decrease is driven by density reduction rather than temperature increase. The results agree with the microscopic Thomas-Fermi calculation of Samaddar (2007) and with the break-up density analysis of Natowitz (2002). The proposed empirical relation enables future studies of symmetry energy across different mass regions and can be tested against measurements for very light and heavy nuclei.
