# Extended Skyrme interaction (I): spin fluctuations in dense matter

> Most of the Skyrme interactions are known to predict spin or isospin instabilities beyond the saturation density of nuclear matter which contradict predictions based on realistic interactions. A modification of the standard Skyrme interaction is proposed so that the ferromagnetic instability is removed. The new terms are density dependent and modify only the spin p-h interaction in the case of spin-saturated system. Consequences for the nuclear response function and neutrino mean free path are shown. The overall effect of the RPA correlations makes dense matter more transparent for neutrino propagation by a factor of 2 to 10 depending of the density.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an extension to the standard Skyrme interaction by adding density-dependent spin and spin-isospin terms that remove the ferromagnetic instability in dense nuclear matter, a pathology present in most standard Skyrme forces. The modification only affects the spin particle-hole interaction in spin-saturated systems, leaving the successful ground-state properties of the original interactions unchanged.

## Method

The authors introduce two new density-dependent terms to the Skyrme functional, proportional to the spin density ρ_s and the spin-isospin density ρ_st, each raised to the power γ_s = 2 and γ_st = 2. They determine the parameters t_3^s, t_3^{st}, and x_3^s by fitting the resulting Landau parameters G_0 and G_0' to microscopic Brueckner Hartree-Fock (G-matrix) calculations. The extended interactions are then applied to compute the RPA response function in nuclear matter and the neutrino mean free path in pure neutron matter.

## Key numbers

- The critical density for ferromagnetic instability ρ_f varies widely among standard Skyrme interactions: near ρ_0 for BSk16 and RATP, around 2ρ_0 for SkM* and SLy5, and nearly 3ρ_0 for SGII and LNS in symmetric nuclear matter.
- The corrected Landau parameter G_0' is increased by about +0.3 compared to the original interactions, bringing it closer to the empirical value of 1.3 ± 0.2 (from Wood-Saxon plus one-pion and rho meson models) or 0.7 ± 0.1 (from self-consistent Skyrme mean-field models).
- At density ρ = ρ_0, the new terms shift the collective spin mode to slightly higher energies by 0.5-1 MeV.
- The overall effect of RPA correlations makes dense matter more transparent for neutrino propagation by a factor of 2 to 10 depending on the density.
- The BSk16 interaction reproduces known nuclear masses with a root-mean-square deviation of 632 keV.

## Context

This work extends the standard Skyrme interaction framework (Skyrme 1956, Vautherin and Brink 1972) to cure spin instabilities that contradict predictions from realistic interactions such as diffusion Monte-Carlo (Fantoni et al. 2001) and Brueckner Hartree-Fock calculations (Vidana et al. 2002, Bombaci et al. 2006). The new terms are adjusted to reproduce G-matrix results (Zuo et al. 2003) and enable self-consistent calculations of spin-dependent excitations and neutrino mean free path in dense matter, building a bridge between finite nuclei and infinite nuclear matter without introducing extra parameters.
