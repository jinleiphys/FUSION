# Isomer shift and magnetic moment of the long-lived 1/2$^{+}$ isomer in $^{79}_{30}$Zn$_{49}$: signature of shape coexistence near $^{78}$Ni

> Collinear laser spectroscopy has been performed on the $^{79}_{30}$Zn$_{49}$ isotope at ISOLDE-CERN. The existence of a long-lived isomer with a few hundred milliseconds half-life was confirmed, and the nuclear spins and moments of the ground and isomeric states in $^{79}$Zn as well as the isomer shift were measured. From the observed hyperfine structures, spins $I = 9/2$ and $I = 1/2$ are firmly assigned to the ground and isomeric states. The magnetic moment $μ$ ($^{79}$Zn) = $-$1.1866(10) $μ_{\rm{N}}$, confirms the spin-parity $9/2^{+}$ with a $νg_{9/2}^{-1}$ shell-model configuration, in excellent agreement with the prediction from large scale shell-model theories. The magnetic moment $μ$ ($^{79m}$Zn) = $-$1.0180(12) $μ_{\rm{N}}$ supports a positive parity for the isomer, with a wave function dominated by a 2h-1p neutron excitation across the $N = 50$ shell gap. The large isomer shift reveals an increase of the intruder isomer mean square charge radius with respect to that of the ground state: $δ\langle r^{2}_{c}\rangle^{79,79m}$ = +0.204(6) fm$^{2}$, providing first evidence of shape coexistence.

## Key claim

Collinear laser spectroscopy on the neutron-rich isotope $^{79}_{30}$Zn$_{49}$ provides the first experimental evidence of shape coexistence near the doubly-magic $^{78}$Ni, as inferred from a large isomer shift that reveals an increase in the mean square charge radius of the long-lived $1/2^{+}$ isomer relative to the $9/2^{+}$ ground state.

## Method

Collinear laser spectroscopy was performed on bunched, cooled $^{79}$Zn ions at the COLLAPS setup at ISOLDE-CERN, using a frequency-doubled cw Ti:Sa laser locked to 480.7254 nm to excite the $4s4p$ $^{3}P_{2}$ $\rightarrow$ $4s5s$ $^{3}S_{1}$ transition in neutral Zn atoms after charge exchange with sodium vapour. Hyperfine structure spectra were recorded by scanning the voltage applied to the charge exchange cell and collecting fluorescence photons; the spectra were fitted with Lorentzian line profiles using a $\chi^{2}$ minimization routine to extract hyperfine constants, nuclear spins, and the centroid shift.

## Key numbers

* Ground state spin $I = 9/2$ and isomeric state spin $I = 1/2$ firmly assigned from hyperfine structure fits.
* Magnetic moment of the ground state: $\mu(^{79}\text{Zn}) = -1.1866(10)~\mu_{\text{N}}$, confirming a $\nu g_{9/2}^{-1}$ configuration.
* Magnetic moment of the isomer: $\mu(^{79m}\text{Zn}) = -1.0180(12)~\mu_{\text{N}}$, supporting a positive-parity intruder configuration.
* Isomer shift: $\delta\nu^{79,79m} = 61.3(31)~\text{MHz}$, corresponding to a change in mean square charge radius $\delta\langle r_{c}^{2}\rangle^{79,79m} = +0.204(6)[36]~\text{fm}^{2}$ (statistical and systematic errors).
* Ground state quadrupole moment: $Q_{\text{s,exp}} = +0.487(53)~b$, yielding a deduced deformation $\beta_2 = 0.15(2)$.
* Estimated deformation of the isomeric state: $\langle\beta_2^{2}\rangle^{1/2} \sim 0.22$, inferred from the increased charge radius.

## Context

This work extends the study of shape coexistence along the $N = 50$ closed shell, previously established along $N = 20$, $N = 28$, and $N = 40$ (e.g., Neyens 2011, Gade 2016), and follows observations of intruder $1/2^{+}$ states in $^{81}$Ge (Hoff 1981) and $^{83}$Se (Meyer 1982). The large isomer shift in $^{79}$Zn is comparable to that seen in thallium isotopes where shape coexistence is well known, and is an order of magnitude larger than the shifts between normal $1/2^{-}$ isomers in $^{85}$Kr and $^{87}$Sr (Keim 1995). The results challenge the magicity of $^{78}$Ni and motivate further measurements of the $1/2^{+}$ isomer properties in $^{81}$Ge and low-lying levels in $^{77}$Ni, as well as Coulomb excitation of the isomeric state to probe its deformation.
