# Carrollian Origins of Bjorken Flow

> Bjorken flow is among the simplest models of fluids moving near the speed of light ($c$) while Carroll symmetry arises as a contraction of Poincaré group when $c \to 0$. We show that Bjorken flow and its phenomenological approximations are completely captured by Carrollian fluids. Carrollian symmetries arise on generic null surfaces and a fluid moving at $c$ is restricted to such a surface, thereby naturally inheriting the symmetries. Carrollian hydrodynamics is thus not exotic, but rather ubiquitous, and provides a concrete framework for fluids moving at or near the speed of light.

## Key claim
Bjorken flow and its phenomenological approximations (including boost/rapidity invariance) are completely captured by Carrollian hydrodynamics, which arises naturally for any fluid moving at or near the speed of light because such fluids are constrained to null hypersurfaces that inherit Carroll symmetry. The authors show that the Bjorken equation and the assumption of rapidity-independence emerge as leading-order results of a systematic Carrollian expansion, rather than being imposed as separate phenomenological assumptions.

## Method
The authors take the vanishing-speed-of-light (c→0) limit of relativistic perfect-fluid and first-order viscous hydrodynamics in the Papapetrou-Randers parametrisation, obtaining Carrollian fluid equations. They then adapt the Carrollian geometric data (degenerate metric, kernel vector, Ehresmann connection) to Milne coordinates (proper time τ, rapidity ρ) by taking a simultaneous limit τ→ετ, ρ→ρ/ε with ε→0, which maps the Milne metric to a Carrollian structure. The resulting Carrollian fluid equations are shown to reproduce the Bjorken flow equation dε/dτ = -(ε+P)/τ and the condition ∂_i p = 0 (rapidity independence) for both perfect and viscous fluids.

## Key numbers
- Bjorken flow equation: dε/dτ = -(ε+P)/τ, where ε is energy density, P is pressure, and τ is proper time.
- For viscous Bjorken flow: dε/dτ = -(ε+P)/τ + (1/τ²)(2η/3 + ζ), where η is shear viscosity and ζ is bulk viscosity.
- In Milne coordinates, the four-velocity becomes u^μ = (1,0,0,0) after boost invariance is imposed.
- The Milne metric is ds² = -dτ² + τ² dρ² + dx² + dy².
- The Carrollian expansion scalar θ = (1/Ω) ∂_t ln√a becomes θ = 1/τ in the Milne-adapted gauge.

## Context
This work establishes that Carrollian hydrodynamics, previously considered exotic and limited to flat-space holography (Ciambelli 2018), is instead ubiquitous and provides a concrete framework for any fluid moving near the speed of light. It directly extends the fluid/gravity correspondence (Bhattacharyya 2007) by showing that the ultrarelativistic regime of fluids corresponds to Carrollian hydrodynamics, which in turn relates to asymptotically flat spacetimes via flat holography (Ciambelli 2018). The paper also connects to the black hole membrane paradigm (Damour 1978, Donnay 2019), where event horizons as null surfaces obey Carrollian hydrodynamics, suggesting a relation between Bjorken flow and black hole horizon dynamics. This opens the way for a systematic c-expansion to describe physics beyond the central rapidity region in heavy-ion collisions and cosmological phase transitions.
