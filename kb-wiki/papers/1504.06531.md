# Double-quarkonium production at a fixed-target experiment at the LHC (AFTER@LHC)

> We present predictions for double-quarkonium production in the kinematical region relevant for the proposed fixed-target experiment using the LHC beams (dubbed as AFTER@LHC). These include all spin-triplet S -wave charmonium and bottomonium pairs, i.e. Psi(n_1S) + Psi(n_2S), Psi(n_1S) + Upsilon(m_1S) and Upsilon(m_1S) + Upsilon(m_2S ) with n_1,n_2 = 1,2 and m_1,m_2 = 1,2,3. We calculate the contributions from double-parton scatterings and single-parton scatterings. With an integrated luminosity of 20 fb-1 to be collected at AFTER@LHC, we find that the yields for double-charmonium production are large enough for differential distribution measurements. We discuss some differential distributions for J/Psi + J/Psi production, which can help to study the physics of double-parton and single-parton scatterings in a new energy range and which might also be sensitive to double intrinsic c-bar(c) coalescence at large negative Feynman x.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that double-quarkonium yields at the proposed AFTER@LHC fixed-target experiment are large enough for differential distribution measurements, but only for double-charmonium production; the predictions for bottomonium pairs should be considered conservative lower estimates due to uncertainties in the energy dependence of the parametrization used to fit single-quarkonium production data.

## Method

The authors computed double-parton scattering (DPS) contributions using the "pocket formula" with σ_eff = 5.0 ± 2.75 mb, where single-quarkonium cross sections were obtained from a data-driven Crystal Ball fit to PHENIX charmonium data at √s = 200 GeV and CDF bottomonium data at √s = 1.8 TeV. Single-parton scattering (SPS) contributions were calculated at leading order in α_s within the colour-singlet model using HELAC-Onia, with feed-down from higher excited spin-triplet S-wave states included via known branching ratios.

## Key numbers

- Centre-of-mass energy for p+p collisions at AFTER@LHC: √s_NN ≃ 115 GeV (and √s_NN ≃ 72 GeV for Pb+p and Pb+A)
- Integrated luminosity assumed: up to 20 fb⁻¹ per year for p + liquid H target with bent crystal extraction
- σ_eff used for DPS predictions: 5.0 ± 2.75 mb (from D0 J/ψ-pair data)
- Radial wave functions squared used: |R(0)|² = 0.81 GeV³ for J/ψ, 0.529 GeV³ for ψ(2S), 6.477 GeV³ for Υ(1S), 3.234 GeV³ for Υ(2S), 2.474 GeV³ for Υ(3S)
- Charm quark mass: m_c = 1.5 ± 0.1 GeV; bottom quark mass: m_b = 4.75 ± 0.25 GeV
- Fit parameters for J/ψ at PHENIC: κ = 0.67 ± 0.08, λ = 0.38, with n = 2 and ⟨P_T⟩ = 4.5 GeV
- Fit parameters for Υ(1S) at CDF: κ = 0.89, λ = 0.084 ± 0.0061, with n = 2 and ⟨P_T⟩ = 13.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous comprehensive comparison of J/ψ-pair production at the Tevatron and LHC (Lansberg 2014) to the fixed-target energy regime of the proposed AFTER@LHC facility, which was advocated by Brodsky (2012) and Lansberg (2012) as a quarkonium observatory. The study enables future measurements of double-parton scattering in a new energy range between RHIC and Tevatron energies, and could probe double intrinsic c-cbar coalescence at large negative Feynman x, complementing studies at collider experiments such as D0 (2014) and CMS (2014).
