# Large-$N_c$ analysis of magnetic and axial two-nucleon currents in pionless effective field theory

> We analyze magnetic and axial two-nucleon contact terms in a combined large-$N_c$ and pionless effective field theory expansion. These terms play important roles in correctly describing, e.g., the low-energy cross section of radiative neutron capture and the deuteron magnetic moment. We show that the large-$N_c$ expansion hints towards a hierarchy between the two leading-order magnetic terms that matches that found in phenomenological fits. We also comment on the issue of naturalness in different Lagrangian bases.

## Key claim

The large-\(N_c\) expansion suggests that the isoscalar magnetic two-nucleon contact term is suppressed by a factor of \(1/N_c\) relative to the isovector term, and that this hierarchy, combined with an additional factor of 8 from Fierz transformations, can reconcile the experimentally observed ratio of the two leading-order magnetic low-energy couplings (LECs) with naturalness expectations.

## Method

The authors combined pionless effective field theory (EFT\(_{\not{\pi}}\)) with the large-\(N_c\) expansion of QCD, where \(N_c\) is the number of colors. They constructed the most general set of two-nucleon contact operators coupling to magnetic and axial external fields, determined the large-\(N_c\) scaling of each operator by matching to a Hartree-form Hamiltonian in the quark basis, and then used Fierz transformations to reduce the operator set to a minimal basis while retaining the manifest large-\(N_c\) hierarchy.

## Key numbers

* The experimentally fitted LECs for magnetic two-nucleon currents at renormalization scale \(\mu = m_\pi\) are \(L_1 = 7.24\ \text{fm}^4\) (isovector) and \(L_2 = -0.149\ \text{fm}^4\) (isoscalar), giving a ratio \(|L_2/L_1|_{\text{exp}} \approx 0.021\).
* The large-\(N_c\) analysis predicts \(|L_2/L_1|_{N_c} \approx 1/(8 N_c) \approx 0.042\) for \(N_c = 3\).
* In the large-\(N_c\) basis, the magnetic LECs are \(C_s^{(M)} = 0.149\ \text{fm}^4\) and \(C_v^{(M)} = 0.905\ \text{fm}^4\), with \(C_v^{(M)}\) larger than \(C_s^{(M)}\) by a factor of approximately 6.
* The NPLQCD collaboration extracted the isovector axial LEC \(L_{1,A}^{\text{NPLQCD}}(\mu=m_\pi) = 3.9(0.2)(1.0)(0.4)(0.9)\ \text{fm}^3\) from a lattice QCD calculation at \(m_\pi^{\text{lat}} = 806\ \text{MeV}\).
* Using the large-\(N_c\) relationship with \(N_c=3\) and the NPLQCD value gives an estimate \(L_{2,A} \sim 0.1625\ \text{fm}^3\) for the isoscalar axial LEC.

## Context

This work extends previous large-\(N_c\) analyses of two-nucleon interactions in pionless EFT, which had focused on parity-conserving scattering (Kaplan 1995, Schindler 2018), parity-violating interactions (Schindler 2015), and time-reversal-violating interactions (Vanasse 2019). The results for magnetic currents agree with the hierarchy found in phenomenological fits by Chen (1999), and the analysis of axial currents provides a prediction for the isoscalar axial LEC \(L_{2,A}\) that has not been determined experimentally. The work also cautions that naturalness assumptions for LECs can appear different depending on the operator basis, which has implications for Bayesian parameter estimation methods that use naturalness as a prior.
