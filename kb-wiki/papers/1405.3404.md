# Direct X(3872) production in e+e- collisions

> Direct production of the charmonium-like state $X(3872)$ in $e^+e^-$ collisions is considered in the framework of the vector meson dominance model. An order-of-magnitude estimate for the width $Γ(X\to e^+e^-)$ is found to be $\gtrsim$0.03 eV. The same approach applied to the $χ_{c1}$ charmonium decay predicts the corresponding width of the order 0.1 eV in agreement with earlier estimates. Experimental perspectives for the direct production of the $1^{++}$ charmonia in $e^+e^-$ collisions are briefly discussed.

# Direct X(3872) production in e+e- collisions

## Key claim
The width for the direct production of the X(3872) in e+e- collisions via two-photon processes is estimated to be at least 0.03 eV, but this should be regarded as no more than an order-of-magnitude estimate with an uncertainty of about 100% due to the model dependence of the vector meson dominance approach and the use of lower bounds on experimental branching fractions.

## Method
The authors employ a vector meson dominance (VMD) model to parametrize the X(3872) to two virtual photons vertex, where the virtual photons are replaced by vector meson pairs (rho J/psi, omega J/psi, gamma* J/psi, and gamma* psi(2S)). The transition amplitude is constructed from one-loop diagrams involving these intermediate vector meson pairs, with coupling constants extracted from measured branching fractions of X(3872) decays into rho J/psi, omega J/psi, gamma J/psi, and gamma psi(2S). A short-ranged contact term is added and regularized using dimensional regularization with the MS-bar scheme at scale mu = M_X, with the uncertainty estimated by varying the scale from M_X/2 to 2M_X.

## Key numbers
- Lower bound on the X(3872) to e+e- width: Gamma(X -> e+e-) >= 0.03 eV, dominated by the gamma* psi(2S) intermediate state
- Contribution from the rho J/psi intermediate state: Gamma(X -> rho J/psi -> e+e-) ~ 10^(-7) eV
- Contribution from the omega J/psi intermediate state: Gamma(X -> omega J/psi -> e+e-) ~ 10^(-7) eV
- For the gamma* J/psi intermediate state: Gamma(X -> gamma* J/psi -> e+e-) >= 10^(-3) eV
- For the gamma* psi(2S) intermediate state: Gamma(X -> gamma* psi(2S) -> e+e-) >= 0.03 eV
- The same VMD approach applied to the chi_c1 charmonium gives Gamma(chi_c1 -> e+e-) ~ 0.1 eV, compared to 0.46 eV from earlier VMD estimates in Kaplan (1978) and Kuehn (1979)
- Ratio of widths: Gamma(X -> e+e-) : Gamma(chi_c1 -> e+e-) >= 1:3
- X(3872) mass: M_X = (3871.68 +/- 0.17) MeV
- Upper bound on total X(3872) width: Gamma_X < 1.2 MeV (the authors use Gamma_X = 1.0 MeV for estimates)
- Lower bounds on branching fractions used: Br(X -> gamma J/psi) > 0.7%, Br(X -> gamma psi(2S)) > 1.7%, Br(X -> rho J/psi) > 2.6%, Br(X -> omega J/psi) > 1.9%

## Context
This work extends VMD-based estimates of leptonic widths for axial-vector charmonia, previously applied to the chi_c1 by Kaplan (1978) and Kuehn (1979), to the exotic charmonium-like state X(3872). The direct production of a non-vector state in e+e- collisions via two-photon processes was previously considered impossible due to low cross sections, but the authors argue that high-luminosity accelerators such as BEPC-II operating in the charmonium energy region may make detection realistic. The calculation uses experimental data on X(3872) decays from Belle, BaBar, and LHCb, and the results enable experimental searches for direct X(3872) production in e+e- collisions, with the chi_c1 production serving as a benchmark for feasibility studies at BESIII.
