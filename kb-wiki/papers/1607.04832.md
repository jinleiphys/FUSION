# Phases of kinky holographic nuclear matter

> Holographic QCD at finite baryon number density and zero temperature is studied within the five-dimensional Sakai-Sugimoto model. We introduce a new approximation that models a smeared crystal of solitonic baryons by assuming spatial homogeneity to obtain an effective kink theory in the holographic direction. The kink theory correctly reproduces a first order phase transition to lightly bound nuclear matter. As the density is further increased the kink splits into a pair of half-kink constituents, providing a concrete realization of the previously suggested dyonic salt phase, where the bulk soliton splits into constituents at high density. The kink model also captures the phenomenon of baryonic popcorn, in which a first order phase transition generates an additional soliton layer in the holographic direction. We find that this popcorn transition takes place at a density below the dyonic salt phase, making the latter energetically unfavourable. However, the kink model predicts only one pop, rather than the sequence of pops suggested by previous approximations. In the kink model the two layers produced by the single pop form the surface of a soliton bag that increases in size as the baryon chemical potential is increased. The interior of the bag is filled with abelian electric potential and the instanton charge density is localized on the surface of the bag. The soliton bag may provide a holographic description of a quarkyonic phase.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new homogeneous approximation for the Sakai-Sugimoto model that models a smeared crystal of solitonic baryons, yielding an effective kink theory in the holographic direction. The kink model predicts only one baryonic popcorn transition (rather than a sequence of pops) and finds that the popcorn transition occurs at a density below the dyonic salt phase, making the dyonic salt phase energetically unfavourable.

## Method

The authors work within the five-dimensional U(2) Yang-Mills version of the Sakai-Sugimoto model, applying a separable approximation A_i(x,z) = R_i(x) psi(z) and then imposing spatial homogeneity at the level of the field strength rather than the gauge potential. This reduces the problem to an effective one-dimensional kink theory for the profile function psi(z) in the holographic direction z, with the abelian electric potential omega(z) eliminated via its field equation. The resulting energy functional is solved numerically using a gradient flow algorithm with a change of variable z = tan u to map the infinite range onto a finite interval, using the fiducial value Lambda = 10 for the rescaled 't Hooft coupling.

## Key numbers

- The optimum baryon number density for the single layer is rho_star = 0.027, corresponding to the analogue of nuclear matter density in QCD.
- The critical chemical potential for the first order phase transition is mu_star = 98 (for Lambda = 10), which is less than the baryon mass M_B = 102, giving a percentage binding energy per nucleon of Delta = 4%.
- The critical density for the baryonic popcorn transition to a double layer is rho_2 = 0.22, well below the density at which dyonic salt appears.
- The critical chemical potential for the popcorn transition is mu_2 = 185 (for Lambda = 10).
- A realistic nuclear binding energy of Delta approximately 0.9% is obtained at Lambda approximately 18.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of cold dense nuclear matter in holographic QCD using the Sakai-Sugimoto model, particularly the approaches of Rozali (2007) using singular homogeneous gauge potentials and Kim (2007) using a three-sphere with fixed self-dual form. The kink model provides a concrete realization of the dyonic salt phase proposed by Rho (2009) and the baryonic popcorn phase suggested by Kaplunovsky (2012, 2015), while contradicting the prediction of multiple popcorn transitions. The soliton bag that emerges from the single pop is similar to the magnetic bag description of large charge non-abelian monopoles by Bolognesi (2005) and may provide a holographic description of the quarkyonic phase proposed by McLerran (2007).
