# Asymmetric Nuclear Matter in the Relativistic Brueckner Hartree Fock approach

> We present a calculation of asymmetric nuclear matter properties in a relativistic Brueckner Hartree Fock framework. Following other calculations the components of the self-energies are extracted by projecting on Lorentz invariant amplitudes. It is shown that for asymmetric nuclear matter one needs a sixth invariant. We present a set of invariants which in the limit of symmetric nuclear matter reduces to the conventional set. We argue that the existence of such a properly behaving set is also crucial for the application of the projection method in symmetric nuclear matter. Results for the equation of state and other observables are presented. Special attention is payed to an analysis in terms of mean-field effective coupling constants. Apart from the usual ones we also find significant strength in the isovector scalar channel, which can be interpreted as an effective $δ$-meson.

# Asymmetric Nuclear Matter in the Relativistic Brueckner Hartree Fock approach

## Key claim

The authors show that a sixth Lorentz invariant amplitude is required to decompose the in-medium T-matrix for asymmetric nuclear matter, and that this sixth invariant must be chosen so that its coefficient vanishes in the limit of equal proton and neutron masses to recover the conventional five-invariant description of symmetric nuclear matter. The contribution of this sixth invariant to the final results is negligible, although it indirectly affects the other coefficients.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) equations for asymmetric nuclear matter using a plane-wave spinor basis with distinguishable protons and neutrons, calculating three different T-matrices (pp, nn, and coupled np channels) with different Fermi momenta and Pauli-blocking operators for each species. The self-energies are extracted by projecting the on-shell T-matrices onto Lorentz invariant amplitudes, requiring a newly introduced sixth invariant for the np channel to account for the six independent helicity amplitudes that arise when proton and neutron masses differ. The calculation uses the Groningen potential and the Bonn C potential, iterating until self-consistent self-energies are obtained.

## Key numbers

- Asymmetry energy coefficient a4 = 25 MeV for the Groningen potential, and a4 = 28 MeV for the Bonn C potential.
- The effective isovector scalar coupling constant (g*δ/mδ)² and isovector vector coupling constant (g*ρ/mρ)² are of comparable strength at saturation density ρ0 = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- The density dependence of effective coupling constants is predominantly linear in the Fermi momentum for densities ranging from half to double normal nuclear matter density (p_f from approximately 0.12 to 0.36 GeV/c).
- Below p_f ~ 0.24 GeV/c, the density dependence changes and an enhancement of isovector couplings is found at large asymmetry.

## Context

This work extends earlier RBHF calculations of symmetric nuclear matter by ter Haar and Malfliet (1987) and asymmetric matter by the same authors (1987), correcting an omission in the earlier asymmetric calculation by introducing the required sixth invariant. The results are compared to other contemporary asymmetric matter calculations by Engvik et al. (1994), Huber et al. (1995), and Lee et al. (1997). The extracted effective coupling constants enable density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DDRH) calculations for finite nuclei as developed by Lenske and Fuchs (1995), and the finding of significant isovector scalar strength supports the inclusion of an effective δ-meson in relativistic mean-field models beyond the standard σ-ω-ρ framework of Serot and Walecka (1986).
