# The role of Delta(1700) excitation and rho production in double pion photoproduction

> Recent information on invariant mass distributions of the $γp\toπ^+π^0 n$ reaction, where previous theoretical models had shown deficiencies, have made more evident the need for new mechanisms, so far neglected or inaccurately included. We have updated a previous model to include new necessary mechanisms. We find that the production of the $ρ$ meson, and the $Δ(1700)$ excitation, through interference with the dominant terms, are important mechanisms that solve the puzzle of the $γp\toπ^+π^0 n$ reaction without spoiling the early agreement with the $γp\toπ^+π^- p$ and $γp\toπ^0π^0 p$ reactions.

## Key claim

The authors claim that including the production of the rho meson and the excitation of the Delta(1700) resonance, through interference with the dominant Delta Kroll Ruderman term, resolves the longstanding discrepancy in the gamma p -> pi+ pi0 n reaction without spoiling the prior agreement for the gamma p -> pi+ pi- p and gamma p -> pi0 pi0 p channels, provided the sign of the N*(1520) -> rho N coupling is chosen positive as indicated by isobar model analyses.

## Method

The authors updated the existing Gomez Tejedor-Oset model for double pion photoproduction by adding two new Feynman diagrams: the N*(1520) -> rho N decay followed by rho -> pi pi, and a rho Kroll Ruderman contact term. They refitted the N*(1520) -> Delta pi coupling constants using a Lagrangian that includes both s-wave and d-wave decay, incorporating the finite width of the Delta resonance, and determined the N*(1520) -> rho N coupling constant from the experimental decay width. The model was then evaluated for the gamma p -> pi+ pi0 n, gamma p -> pi+ pi- p, and gamma p -> pi0 pi0 p channels, with the sign of the rho coupling constant fixed by comparison to isobar model analyses of pi N -> pi pi N reactions.

## Key numbers

- The previous model underestimated the total cross section of the gamma p -> pi+ pi0 n channel by about 40 percent.
- The N*(1520) -> rho N term alone provides a strength at the peak of about 10 microbarns in the gamma p -> pi+ pi0 n channel.
- The N*(1520) decay width into N pi at the resonance mass is 66 MeV, with a pion momentum in the N*(1520) rest frame of 456 MeV.
- The rho pi pi coupling constant f_rho is 6.14, and the N*(1520) N rho coupling constant g_rho is 4.52 when fit from the experimental decay width without form factor, and 5.09 when including the form factor.
- The constant C_rho relating the rho NN coupling to the pion NN coupling is 3.96 when the form factor parameter Lambda_rho is taken as 1.4 GeV.
- The s-wave and d-wave partial amplitudes for N*(1520) -> Delta pi decay are fit to the average experimental values, yielding two sign solutions: (a) f_tilde = 1.061, g_tilde = -0.640 and (b) f_tilde = -1.061, g_tilde = 0.640, with solution (b) being the correct one in the new formalism.

## Context

This work extends the model of Gomez Tejedor and Oset (1996, 1997) which successfully described the gamma p -> pi+ pi- p and gamma p -> pi0 pi0 p channels but failed for gamma p -> pi+ pi0 n. It addresses the same deficiencies noted in the model of Laget (1997) and provides an alternative to the approach of Ochi et al. (1997, 1998) which introduced rho production but used a very small range parameter for the rho coupling to baryons (around 200 MeV) that would be problematic in other areas of rho phenomenology. The results enable a unified description of all three measured double pion photoproduction channels on the proton and support the experimental finding from the analysis of pi0 pi0 invariant mass distributions that the N*(1520) excitation followed by Delta pi decay is the dominant mechanism.
