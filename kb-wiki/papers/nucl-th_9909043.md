# Three-Pion Interferometry of Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> Three-pion interferometry is investigated for new information on the space-time structure of the pion source created in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The two- and three-pion correlations are numerically computed for incoherent source functions based on the Bjorken hydrodynamical model, over a wide range of the kinematic variables. New information provided by three-pion interferometry, different from that provided by two-pion interferometry, should appear in the phases of the Fourier transform of the source function. Variables are identified that would be sensitive to the phases and suitable for observation. For a positive, chaotic source function, however, a variation of the three-pion phase is found to be difficult to extract from experiments. Effects of asymmetry of the source function are also examined.

# Three-Pion Interferometry of Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim

Three-pion interferometry can in principle provide new information about the space-time structure of the pion source through the phase of the source function's Fourier transform, which is inaccessible to two-pion interferometry. However, for a positive, chaotic source function as conventionally assumed, the variation of the three-pion phase is found to be difficult to extract from experiments because the coefficient of the phase-dependent term in the three-particle correlation function becomes small precisely when the phase deviates from zero.

## Method

The authors numerically computed two- and three-pion correlation functions for incoherent source functions based on the Bjorken hydrodynamical model, using five different profile functions (Simple box-type, Gaussian, Heinz, Exponential, and Theta profiles) over a wide range of kinematic variables. They identified optimal variables for observing the phase effect, specifically choosing the constraint q12 = q23 to reduce the three-momentum problem to a single relative momentum variable, and examined both q_z and q_out (q_x) as independent variables. The calculations assumed completely chaotic pion emission with no final-state interactions, with freeze-out occurring on a constant proper-time hypersurface.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature T_f = 140 MeV used in calculations
- Proper time tau_0 = 6 fm used as central value for all profile functions
- Delta_eta = 1.2 used for all profile functions
- Delta_tau = 1 fm used for Heinz, Theta, and Exponential profile functions
- Transverse size R_T = 5 fm used for calculations with q_out as variable
- m_T = 140 MeV used as central value for transverse mass
- For the Heinz profile with tau_0 = 6 fm, Delta_tau = 1 fm, Delta_eta = 1.2, and m_T = T_f = 140 MeV, phi_12 reaches pi/2 around q^z_12 = 150 MeV while cos(Phi) remains above 0.5
- Parameter sets yielding R_long = 3.5 fm (matching CERN NA49 measurements) included (tau_0, Delta_tau) = (6.5, 0.65), (4.8, 2.8), and (3.2, 3.2) fm
- The CERN NA44 experiment reported extraction of cos(Phi) up to about 60 MeV total relative momentum, finding cos(Phi) approximately 0.2

## Context

This work extends the theoretical investigation of three-pion interferometry by Heinz and Zhang (1990s) to a wider range of kinematic variables and multiple source profile functions, confirming their finding that the phase information is difficult to extract for chaotic sources. The study was motivated by the first experimental results from the CERN NA44 experiment and anticipated more detailed measurements from RHIC. The authors identify that the difficulty arises because for a positive source function, the odd part of the source (which generates the phase) has a smaller spatial extent than the even part, causing the amplitude factor F_3 to decrease before the phase variation becomes observable. This suggests the interesting possibility that the source function might be locally negative, which would indicate quantum correlation effects in the dynamics.
