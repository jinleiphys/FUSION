# Distance to the drip lines

> It can be found that with the adequate measure, the beta stability line is equidistant from neutron and proton drip lines. We explore this fact and its predictive potentiality in the simplest case, the classic liquid drop formula.

# Distance to the drip lines

## Key claim
The beta stability line is equidistant from the neutron and proton drip lines when measured by an appropriate mass-based metric, and this equidistance holds within approximately 2% even when proton and neutron masses are treated as different. The property is explored using the classic Weizsäcker liquid drop formula, where the discrepancy between the neutron numbers at the two drip lines for a given mass number A₀ is found to be at most -0.815 near A₀ ~ 300.

## Method
The author fixes a mass number A₀ at the drip lines and solves the second-degree equation for the proton drip line and the third-degree equation for the stability line using the Bethe-Weizsäcker mass formula. The difference d(A₀) = N - N₀ is computed between the neutron number N of the stable nucleus and the neutron number N₀ from the neutron drip line equation, and this function is explored numerically in Mathematica across the four free parameters a₁, a₂, a₃, a₄ of the liquid drop model.

## Key numbers
- For the standard parameter set (a₁ = 15.75 MeV, a₂ = 17.8 MeV, a₃ = 0.711 MeV, a₄ = 23.0 MeV), the maximum discrepancy d(A₀) is -0.815 at A₀ ~ 300 when proton and neutron masses are set equal.
- The equidistance property holds within 2% across the mass range.
- The function d(A₀) has three zeros besides the trivial one, at approximately A = 25, 600, and 2000.
- A numerical minimization of the sum of squared discrepancies at A₀ = 50, 100, 150, 200 yields parameter sets such as a₁ = 15.0299 MeV, a₂ = 17.7684 MeV, a₃ = 0.668602 MeV, a₄ = 30.3896 MeV (mean square discrepancy 0.00175986).
- Another minimum gives a₁ = 18.1636 MeV, a₂ = 12.6318 MeV, a₃ = 0.812369 MeV, a₄ = 41.3469 MeV (mean square discrepancy 0.00108511).

## Context
This work extends the observation that the equidistance property appears in most nuclear mass models, including those from Uno and collaborators (no year given), and tests it in the simplest case of the Bethe-Weizsäcker liquid drop formula. The author notes that the property may be related to enhanced mass-dependent effects at the drip lines and to magic numbers, as nuclei at the proton drip line with magic Z correspond to nuclei at the neutron drip line with magic N (e.g., N=28-30 with Z=28, N=50 with Z=40, N=82 with Z=58, N=126 with Z=82, N=184 with Z=114). The work was motivated by questions from M. Asorey and A. Zuker, and it suggests that the function d(A₀) is a useful parameter for studying any mass model's properties.
