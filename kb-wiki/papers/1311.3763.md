# XYZ States

> In the past decade, many new charmonium (or charmonium-like) and bottomonium (or bottomonium-like) states were observed experimentally. I will review these XYZ states which do not fit into the quark model spectrum easily.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the experimental XYZ states that do not fit easily into the conventional quark model spectrum, but emphasizes that many of these states may be threshold enhancements rather than genuine resonances, and that theoretical interpretations (molecular states, tetraquarks, hybrid charmonium, or conventional charmonium with coupled-channel effects) remain speculative.

## Method

The authors survey experimental results from BaBar, Belle, CLEO, and BESIII collaborations on charmonium-like and bottomonium-like states observed in initial state radiation, double charmonium production, two-photon fusion, B decays, and excited charmonium/bottomonium decays. They compare these observations to predictions from the quark model, lattice QCD, QCD sum rules, and one-boson-exchange models for hadronic molecules, analyzing decay patterns, quantum numbers, and threshold kinematics.

## Key numbers

- Seven vector charmonium states observed between 4 and 4.7 GeV: ψ(4008), ψ(4040), ψ(4160), ψ(4260), ψ(4360), ψ(4415), ψ(4660), while the quark model expects at most five (3S/ψ(4040), 2D/ψ(4160), 4S/ψ(4415), 3D, 5S)
- For X(3872) with a binding energy of 0.3 MeV, the calculated branching fraction ratio between isospin-conserving J/ψ π⁺π⁻π⁰ and isospin-violating J/ψ π⁺π⁻ is 0.42, agreeing with BaBar's measurement 0.8±0.3 and Belle's measurement 1.0±0.4±0.3
- The radiative decay ratio Γ[γ X(3872)]/Γ[ππ J/ψ] for Y(4260) is 11.2%
- The Y(5S) mass is 10.86 GeV, while M_B + M_B* + M_π = 10.744 GeV and 2M_B* + M_π = 10.79 GeV

## Context

This review extends the ongoing effort to understand XYZ states that have been observed since 2003, following the discovery of X(3872) and the charged Z_c and Z_b states by Belle (2011) and BESIII (2013). The paper contrasts the molecular interpretation (analogous to the deuteron in nuclear physics, as proposed by Yukawa in 1935) with tetraquark, hybrid charmonium, and conventional charmonium explanations. It enables future experimental tests at BelleII and BESIII by predicting partner states and decay patterns for the molecular scenario, particularly for the Z_c(3900), Z_c(4020), and Z_b(10610)/Z_b(10650) multiplets.
