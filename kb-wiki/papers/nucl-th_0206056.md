# Nuclear spin-orbit interaction from chiral pion-nucleon dynamics

> Using the two-loop approximation of chiral perturbation theory, we calculate the momentum and density dependent nuclear spin-orbit strength $U_{ls}(p,k_f)$. This quantity is derived from the spin-dependent part of the interaction energy $Σ_{spin} = {i\over 2} \vec σ\cdot (\vec q \times\vec p) U_{ls}(p,k_f)$ of a nucleon scattering off weakly inhomogeneous isospin symmetric nuclear matter. We find that iterated $1π$-exchange generates at saturation density, $k_{f0}=272.7 $MeV, a spin-orbit strength at $p=0$ of $U_{ls}(0,k_{f0})\simeq 35$ MeVfm$^2$ in perfect agreement with the empirical value used in the shell model. This novel spin-orbit strength is neither of relativistic nor of short range origin. The potential $V_{ls}$ underlying the empirical spin-orbit strength $\widetilde U_{ls}= V_{ls} r_{ls}^2$ becomes a rather weak one, $V_{ls}\simeq 17$ MeV, after the identification $r_{ls}= m_π^{-1}$ as suggested by the present calculation. We observe however a strong $p$-dependence of $U_{ls}(p,k_{f0})$ leading even to a sign change above $p=200 $MeV. This and other features of the emerging spin-orbit Hamiltonian which go beyond the usual shell model parametrization leave questions about the ultimate relevance of the spin-orbit interaction generated by $2π$-exchange for a finite nucleus. We also calculate the complex-valued isovector single-particle potential $U_I(p,k_f)+ i W_I(p,k_f)$ in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter proportional to $τ_3 (N-Z)/(N+Z)$. For the real part we find reasonable agreement with empirical values and the imaginary part vanishes at the Fermi-surface $p=k_f$.

# Nuclear spin-orbit interaction from chiral pion-nucleon dynamics

## Key claim

Iterated one-pion exchange calculated in two-loop chiral perturbation theory generates a nuclear spin-orbit strength at saturation density of approximately 35 MeV fm², matching the empirical shell-model value, but this result comes with a strong momentum dependence that leads to a sign change above p=200 MeV, raising questions about whether the spin-orbit interaction from 2π-exchange is ultimately relevant for finite nuclei.

## Method

The author calculates the momentum- and density-dependent nuclear spin-orbit strength U_ls(p,k_f) from the spin-dependent part of the interaction energy of a nucleon scattering off weakly inhomogeneous isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, using the two-loop approximation of chiral perturbation theory. The calculation includes 1π-exchange Fock diagrams and iterated 1π-exchange Hartree and Fock diagrams with one and two medium insertions, organized by the number of Fermi-sphere integrations. The complex-valued isovector single-particle potential in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter is also computed from the same chiral framework.

## Key numbers

- Spin-orbit strength at saturation density (k_f0 = 272.7 MeV) and zero nucleon momentum: U_ls(0,k_f0) = (0.4 + 34.7) MeV fm² = 35.1 MeV fm², dominated by iterated 1π-exchange Hartree diagrams
- The underlying potential V_ls becomes approximately 17 MeV after identifying the effective range r_ls with the pion Compton wavelength m_π⁻¹ = 1.46 fm
- The spin-orbit strength changes sign above p = 200 MeV at saturation density
- Real part of the isovector single-particle potential at saturation density: U_I(0,k_f0) = 47 MeV, comparable to shell-model values of about 33 MeV and optical-model values of about 40 MeV
- The imaginary part of the isovector potential W_I(p,k_f) vanishes quadratically at the Fermi surface p = k_f
- The 1π-exchange Fock diagram contributes only about 1.1% of the empirical spin-orbit strength at saturation density

## Context

This work extends the author's previous chiral perturbation theory treatment of nuclear matter (Kaiser, 2002) which reproduced the empirical saturation point, compressibility K ≈ 255 MeV, and asymmetry energy A(k_f0) = 33.8 MeV using a single momentum cutoff Λ ≈ 0.65 GeV. It also follows the calculation of the spin-independent single-particle potential U(p,k_f) which gave a depth of -53.2 MeV at the bottom of the Fermi sea. The result challenges the conventional relativistic mean-field explanation of the spin-orbit interaction (Walecka, 1974) by showing that iterated pion exchange, which is neither relativistic nor short-range in origin, can reproduce the empirical spin-orbit strength. The strong momentum dependence and the appearance of terms proportional to both ∇f(r) and f(r)∇f(r) in coordinate space go beyond the standard shell-model parametrization, and implementing these results into nuclear structure calculations will clarify the role of 2π-exchange in finite nuclei.
