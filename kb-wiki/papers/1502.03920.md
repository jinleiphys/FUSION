# Slowly rotating superfluid neutron stars with isospin dependent entrainment in a two-fluid model

> We investigate the slowly rotating general relativistic superfluid neutron stars including the entrainment effect in a two-fluid model, where one fluid represents the superfluid neutrons and the other is the charge-neutral fluid called the proton fluid, made of protons and electrons. The equation of state and the entrainment effect between the superfluid neutrons and the proton fluid are computed using a relativistic mean field (RMF) model where baryon-baryon interaction is mediated by the exchange of $σ$, $ω$, and $ρ$ mesons and scalar self interactions are also included. The equations governing rotating neutron stars in the slow rotation approximation are second order in rotational velocities of neutron and proton fluids. We explore the effects of the isospin dependent entrainment and the relative rotation between two fluids on the global properties of rotating superfluid neutron stars such as mass, shape, and the mass shedding (Kepler) limit within the RMF model with different parameter sets. It is observed that for the global properties of rotating superfluid neutron stars in particular, the Kepler limit is modified compared with the case that does not include the contribution of $ρ$ mesons in the entrainment effect.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including the isospin-dependent entrainment effect (via the rho meson contribution) in a relativistic mean field model modifies the mass-shedding (Kepler) limit of slowly rotating superfluid neutron stars compared to calculations that neglect the rho meson contribution, but the qualitative behavior of the Kepler limit as a function of relative rotation rate matches earlier polytropic equation of state results by Prix et al. rather than the earlier sigma-omega model results by Comer (2004).

## Method

The authors adopt the two-fluid formalism of Andersson and Comer (2001) extended to include the entrainment effect, where one fluid represents superfluid neutrons and the other represents the charge-neutral proton fluid (protons and electrons). They compute the equation of state and the isospin-dependent entrainment using a relativistic mean field model with sigma, omega, and rho meson exchange including scalar self-interactions, using both GL and NL3 parameter sets. The slow rotation approximation of Hartle (1967) is applied to Einstein's field equations, retaining terms up to second order in the rotational velocities of the neutron and proton fluids, and the resulting equations are solved numerically to obtain the frame-dragging frequency, metric functions, density corrections, and the Kepler limit.

## Context

This work extends the two-fluid rotating superfluid neutron star formalism of Andersson and Comer (2001) and Comer (2004) by incorporating an isospin-dependent entrainment effect derived from a realistic relativistic mean field equation of state including rho mesons, which was previously calculated by Kheto and Bandyopadhyay (2014). The results differ from Comer (2004) who used the sigma-omega model without rho mesons and found a different qualitative behavior of the Kepler limit, but agree qualitatively with Prix et al. who used polytropic equations of state with constant entrainment values. This enables future studies of rotating superfluid neutron stars with more realistic microphysical inputs, particularly for understanding the role of symmetry energy in neutron star structure and the mass-shedding limit.
