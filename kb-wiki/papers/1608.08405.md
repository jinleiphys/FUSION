# Alpha-decay energies of superheavy nuclei for the Fayans functional

> Alpha-decay energies for several chains of super-heavy nuclei are calculated within the self-consistent mean-field approach by using the Fayans functional FaNDF$^0$. They are compared to the experimental data and predictions of two Skyrme functionals, SLy4 and SkM*, and of the macro-micro method as well. The corresponding lifetimes are calculated with the use of the semi-phenomenological formulas by Parkhomenko and Sobiczewski and by Royer and Zhang.

## Key claim

The Fayans functional FaNDF$^0$, when used in fully self-consistent mean-field calculations, predicts alpha-decay energies for superheavy nuclei with an accuracy comparable to that of the Skyrme functionals SLy4 and SkM*, but the macro-micro method (MMM) still yields a root-mean-square deviation smaller by a factor of 1.5–2; the authors note that FaNDF$^0$ parameters were fitted only to spherical nuclei up to lead, so further tuning including heavy deformed nuclei is needed.

## Method

The authors solved the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations in an axially symmetric harmonic oscillator basis using the HFBTHO code modified for the Fayans functional FaNDF$^0$, with both volume and surface pairing models. For comparison, they performed analogous calculations with the Skyrme functionals SLy4 and SkM* using mixed pairing, and also tabulated predictions from the macro-micro method (MMM) of Muntian et al. (2001, 2003). Alpha-decay lifetimes were then computed from the theoretical Q-values using the semi-phenomenological five-parameter formula of Parkhomenko and Sobiczewski (PS) and the twelve-parameter formula of Royer and Zhang (RZ).

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square deviation (RMSD) of Q-alpha predictions from experiment: FaNDF$^0$ surface pairing = 0.643 MeV; FaNDF$^0$ volume pairing = 0.647 MeV; SLy4 = 0.593 MeV; SkM* = 1.148 MeV; MMM = 0.450 MeV.
- For the chain starting from $^{294}$118, FaNDF$^0$ (surface) predicts Q-alpha = 11.278 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 11.81 (0.06) MeV.
- For $^{291}$Lv, FaNDF$^0$ (surface) predicts Q-alpha = 10.959 MeV, close to the experimental 10.89 (0.07) MeV.
- RMSD of log10(T-alpha) using the PS formula: FaNDF$^0$ surface = 1.52; FaNDF$^0$ volume = 1.53; SLy4 = 1.89; SkM* = 2.46; MMM = 0.70; using experimental Q-alpha directly gives 0.33.
- Nuclear matter properties of FaNDF$^0$: equilibrium density rho_0 = 0.160 fm$^{-3}$, energy per particle mu = -16.0 MeV, incompressibility K_0 = 220 MeV, asymmetry energy a_sym = 30.0 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier partially self-consistent calculations with the Fayans functional by Tolokonnikov et al. (2013) to a fully self-consistent deformed code, enabled by the development of a deformed Fayans EDF solver by the same group. It directly compares with the comprehensive study of superheavy nuclei by Heenen et al. (2015), which used Skyrme functionals SkM* and SLy4 as well as the relativistic DD-PC1 functional, and with the macro-micro method predictions of Muntian et al. (2001, 2003). The results enable more reliable theoretical support for identifying new superheavy nuclei via measured alpha-decay chains, and highlight the need to include heavy deformed nuclei in future fitting of Fayans functional parameters.
