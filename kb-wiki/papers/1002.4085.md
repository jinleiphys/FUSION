# Role of five-quark components in radiative and strong decays of the Lambda(1405) resonance

> Within an extended chiral constituent quark model, three- and five-quark structure of the $S_{01}$ resonance $Λ(1405)$ is investigated. Helicity amplitudes for the electromagnetic decays ($Λ(1405) \to Λ(1116)γ$, $Σ(1194)γ$), and transition amplitudes for strong decays ($Λ(1405)\toΣ(1194)π$, $ K^{-}p$) are drived, as well as the relevant decay widths. The experimental value for the strong decay width, $Γ_{Λ(1405)\to (Σπ)^\circ}=50\pm 2$ MeV, is well reproduced with about 50% of five-quark admixture in the $Λ(1405)$. Important effects due to the configuration mixings among $Λ^{2}_{1}P_{A}$, $Λ^{2}_{8}P_{M}$ and $Λ^{4}_{8}P_{M}$ are found. In addition, transitions between the three- and five-quark components in the baryons turn out to be significant in both radiative and strong decays of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance.

## Key claim

The experimental strong decay width of the Lambda(1405) resonance, Gamma = 50 +/- 2 MeV, is well reproduced only when the resonance contains approximately 50% five-quark admixture, with the remaining 50% being a three-quark component, and when configuration mixings among specific three-quark states and transitions between three- and five-quark components are included.

## Method

Within an extended chiral constituent quark model, the authors constructed wave functions for the Lambda(1405) as an admixture of three-quark configurations (Lambda^2_1 P_A, Lambda^2_8 P_M, Lambda^4_8 P_M) and five-quark components with a [31]_FSX[4]_X[211]_F[22]_S four-quark subsystem. They derived helicity amplitudes for radiative decays (Lambda(1405) -> Lambda(1116)gamma, Sigma(1194)gamma) and transition amplitudes for strong decays (Lambda(1405) -> Sigma(1194)pi, K- p), including diagonal transitions (3q to 3q and 5q to 5q) and nondiagonal transitions (3q to 5q and 5q to 3q). The decay widths were then calculated by summing over all contributing transition amplitudes with the mixing probabilities from the wave function model.

## Key numbers

- Strong decay width Gamma_{Lambda(1405) -> (Sigma pi)^circ} = 50 +/- 2 MeV (experimental value from PDG), reproduced with about 50% five-quark admixture in the Lambda(1405)
- Photon three-momentum magnitude K for Lambda(1405) -> Lambda(1116)gamma is about 259 MeV/c
- Photon three-momentum magnitude K for Lambda(1405) -> Sigma(1194)gamma is about 195 MeV/c
- Mixing coefficients for the three-quark components of Lambda(1405): 0.90 for Lambda^2_1 P_A, -0.43 for Lambda^2_8 P_M, and 0.06 for Lambda^4_8 P_M
- Mixing coefficients for Lambda(1116): 0.93 for Lambda^2_8 S_S, 0.30 for Lambda^2_8 S'_S, and -0.20 for Lambda^2_8 S_M
- Mixing coefficients for Sigma(1193): 0.95 for Sigma^2_8 S_S, 0.18 for Sigma^2_8 S'_S, and -0.16 for Sigma^2_8 S_M

## Context

This work extends the extended chiral constituent quark model approach previously applied to Delta(1232), N(1440), and N(1535) resonances (Li 2005, Li 2006, An 2008, An 2009) to the Lambda(1405) hyperon. It addresses the longstanding puzzle that traditional constituent quark models (Koniuk 1979, Darewych 1983, VanCauteren 2005) predicted strong decay widths much smaller than the experimental value. The finding that five-quark components are essential for Lambda(1405) decays, together with the fact that the lowest-energy five-quark component in N(1535) can only contain s\bar{s} while Lambda(1405) allows u\bar{u}, d\bar{d}, and s\bar{s}, helps explain the observed mass ordering of these two resonances. This work enables future studies of other hyperon resonances and provides a framework for calculating electromagnetic transition form factors that can be compared with experimental data from facilities such as JLab.
