# Photon production spectrum above $T_c$ with a lattice quark propagator

> The photon production rate from the deconfined medium is analyzed with the photon self-energy constructed from the quark propagator obtained by the numerical simulation on the quenched lattice for two values of temperature, $T=1.5T_{\rm c}$ and $3T_{\rm c}$, above the critical temperature $T_{\rm c}$. The photon self-energy is calculated by the Schwinger-Dyson equation with the lattice quark propagator and a vertex function determined so as to satisfy the Ward-Takahashi identity. The obtained photon production rate exhibits a similar behavior as the perturbative results at the energy of photons larger than $0.5$~GeV.

## Key claim

The photon production rate from the deconfined medium, calculated non-perturbatively using a lattice quark propagator and a gauge-invariant vertex, exhibits a similar behavior to perturbative results for photon energies above 0.5 GeV, even though the underlying production mechanism (pair annihilation of quasi-particles, which is forbidden in perturbation theory) is completely different.

## Method

The authors constructed the full photon self-energy via the Schwinger-Dyson equation, using the full quark propagator obtained from quenched lattice QCD simulations in Landau gauge at T=1.5Tc and 3Tc, with a two-pole ansatz for the quark spectral function representing normal and plasmino modes. A photon-quark vertex was constructed to satisfy the Ward-Takahashi identity, ensuring gauge invariance, and the photon production rate was obtained by analytic continuation of the imaginary-time self-energy to real frequencies.

## Key numbers

- Thermal mass ratio m_T/T = 0.768(11) at T=1.5Tc and 0.725(14) at T=3Tc, from extrapolation to infinite volume
- Photon production rate matches perturbative results in slope and magnitude for photon energies ω > 0.5 GeV
- Lattice data available for momentum range p/T ≲ 4.7, with extrapolation to higher momenta using exponential forms
- Residue of plasmino mode Z_-(p) < 0.05 for momenta where the dispersion becomes acausal (p ≳ 3 m_T), making Cherenkov radiation contribution negligible except for ω ≲ 0.1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of dilepton production at zero three-momentum (Kim, 2015) to nonzero momentum for photon production. It provides a non-perturbative alternative to perturbative calculations (Kapusta, 1991; Baier, 1991; Arnold, 2001; Ghiglieri, 2013) used in phenomenological analyses (Paquet, 2015; Shen, 2013), which have not simultaneously reproduced experimental photon yield and anisotropic flow results. The lattice quark propagator data from Karsch (2007, 2009) and Kaczmarek (2012) enables this approach, and the results enable improved modeling of photon emission from the strongly coupled deconfined medium in heavy-ion collisions.
