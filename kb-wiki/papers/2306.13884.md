# Impact of Multiple Phase Transitions in Dense QCD on Compact Stars

> This review covers several recent developments in the physics of dense QCD with an emphasis on the impact of multiple phase transitions on astrophysical manifestations of compact stars. It is conjectured that pair-correlated quark matter in $β$-equilibrium is within the same universality class as spin-imbalanced cold atoms and the isospin asymmetrical nucleonic matter. This then implies the emergence of phases with broken space symmetries and tri-critical (Lifshitz) points. We construct an equation of state (EoS) that extends the two-phase EoS of dense quark matter within the constant speed of sound parameterization by adding a conformal fluid with a speed of sound $c_{\rm conf.}=1/\sqrt{3}$ at densities $\ge 10~n_{\rm sat}$, where $n_{\rm sat}$ is the saturation density. With this input, we construct static, spherically symmetrical compact hybrid stars in the mass--radius diagram, recover such features as the twins and triplets, and show that the transition to conformal fluid leads to the spiraling-in of the tracks in this diagram. Stars on the spirals are classically unstable with respect to the radial oscillations but can be stabilized if the conversion timescale between quark and nucleonic phases at their interface is larger than the oscillation period. Finally, we review the impact of a transition from high-temperature gapped to low-temperature gapless two-flavor phase on the thermal evolution of hybrid stars.

## Key claim

This review argues that multiple sequential phase transitions in dense QCD matter can produce hybrid compact stars with observable "twins" and "triplets" in the mass-radius diagram, and that the transition to a conformal fluid at densities above 10 times nuclear saturation density causes the mass-radius tracks to spiral inward. The author notes that stars on these spirals are classically unstable against radial oscillations but could be stabilized if the conversion timescale between quark and nucleonic phases at the interface exceeds the oscillation period.

## Method

The author constructs an equation of state (EoS) that extends a two-phase constant-speed-of-sound parameterization of dense quark matter by adding a conformal fluid with speed of sound c_conf = 1/sqrt(3) at densities >= 10 n_sat, where n_sat is the nuclear saturation density. Using this EoS, static, spherically symmetric compact hybrid star models are computed to generate mass-radius diagrams. The paper also reviews the impact of a transition from a high-temperature gapped to a low-temperature gapless two-flavor quark phase on the thermal evolution of hybrid stars.

## Key numbers

- Conformal fluid speed of sound: c_conf = 1/sqrt(3) (dimensionless, in units of the speed of light).
- Density threshold for conformal fluid: >= 10 n_sat, where n_sat is the nuclear saturation density.
- The mass-radius diagram exhibits "twins" and "triplets" (multiple stars with the same mass but different radii) as a result of multiple phase transitions.

## Context

This review extends prior work on two-phase EoS models for dense quark matter (e.g., constant-speed-of-sound parameterizations) by adding a third, conformal phase at ultra-high densities. It connects to studies of spin-imbalanced cold atoms and isospin-asymmetric nucleonic matter, suggesting that pair-correlated quark matter in beta-equilibrium belongs to the same universality class. The work enables future investigations of the stability of hybrid stars with slow phase conversion at the quark-nucleon interface, and of the thermal evolution of stars undergoing a transition between gapped and gapless two-flavor quark phases.
