# Amplification of pionic instabilities in high-energy collisions?

> Considering a variety of dynamical scenarios within the linear sigma model, we examine the conditions for pionic modes to be amplified as the chiral field relaxes towards the normal vacuum following the transient restoration of approximate chiral symmetry in a high-energy collision. While Bjorken-type longitudinal expansions appear to be insufficient for amplification for occur, analoguos expansions in two or three dimensions may enhance the low-energy power spectrum by up to an order of magnitude.

## Key claim

Within the linear sigma model, pionic modes can be amplified by up to an order of magnitude during the relaxation of the chiral field after transient chiral symmetry restoration, but only if the expansion is two- or three-dimensional; purely longitudinal (Bjorken-type) expansions are insufficient to cause such amplification.

## Method

The author uses the linear sigma model with a Hartree-type factorization to derive approximate equations of motion for the order parameter and fluctuations. To emulate expanding scenarios in D dimensions, a damping term \(-(D/t)\partial_t\phi\) is added to the equation of motion, and the system is evolved from thermal equilibrium at various initial temperatures. The amplification of pionic modes is quantified by computing the amplification coefficient \(G^\pi_k\) from the time-dependent effective pion mass.

## Key numbers

* Pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92\ \text{MeV}\), pion mass \(m_\pi = 138\ \text{MeV}\), sigma mass \(m_\sigma = 600\ \text{MeV}\) (parameters of the linear sigma model).
* For isotropic expansion (D=3) starting at \(T_0=400\ \text{MeV}\), the amplification coefficient for the zero-momentum pion mode is \(G^\pi_{k=0} = 2.49\), corresponding to a correlation length (FWHM) of 2.1 fm.
* For transverse expansion (D=2) at \(T_0=400\ \text{MeV}\), \(G^\pi_{k=0} = 1.67\) with correlation length 1.3 fm; for longitudinal expansion (D=1) at the same temperature, \(G^\pi_{k=0} = 0.00\) with correlation length 0.6 fm.
* The maximum amplification coefficient for D=3 occurs at \(T_0=500\ \text{MeV}\) with \(G^\pi_{k=0} = 2.61\) and correlation length 1.6 fm.
* The critical temperature in the mean-field treatment is \(T_c = \sqrt{2}v\) with \(v = 86.71\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) formation, particularly the numerical simulations by Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993), Gavin, Gocksch, and Pisarski (1994), and Huang and Wang (1994), as well as analytical approaches by Boyanovsky and de Vega (1995). The analysis confirms the conclusion of Gavin and Muller (1994) that isotropic expansion leads to amplification, while showing that purely longitudinal expansions, as studied by Huang and Wang (1994) and Kluger et al. (1995), are insufficient to cause a quench when initial fluctuations are of thermal magnitude. The results highlight the need for realistic initial conditions based on early partonic dynamics to properly assess DCC formation prospects.
