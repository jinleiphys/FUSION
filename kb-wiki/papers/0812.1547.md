# Photoproduction of hypernuclei within the quark-meson coupling model

> We study the photoproduction of the ^12{_Lambda}B hypernucleus within a fully covariant effective Lagrangian based model, employing Lambda bound state spinors derived from the latest quark-meson coupling model. The kaon production vertex is described via creation, propagation and decay of N*(1650), N*(1710), and N*(1720) intermediate baryonic resonant states in the initial collision of the photon with a target proton in the incident channel. The parameters of the resonance vertices are fixed by describing the total and differential cross section data on the elementary gamma (p, K+) Lambda reaction in the energy regime relevant to the hypernuclear production. It is found that the hypernuclear production cross sections calculated with the quark model based hyperon bound state spinors differ significantly from those obtained with the phenomenological Dirac single particle wave functions.

## Key claim
The hypernuclear photoproduction cross sections for $^{12}\text{C}(\gamma,K^+){^{12}_{\Lambda}}\text{B}$ calculated with $\Lambda$ bound state spinors from the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model differ significantly from those obtained with phenomenological Dirac single-particle wave functions, with the QMC-based cross sections being smaller in magnitude and peaking at lower photon energies.

## Method
The authors employed a fully covariant effective Lagrangian model in which the photon collides with a bound proton, exciting intermediate baryonic resonances $N^*(1650)$, $N^*(1710)$, and $N^*(1720)$ that subsequently decay into a $\Lambda$ hyperon and a $K^+$ meson. The $\Lambda$ bound state spinors were generated from the latest version of the quark-meson coupling model, which includes self-consistent scalar and vector mean fields with different radial shapes, and the resonance coupling constants were fixed by fitting total and differential cross section data for the elementary $\gamma p \to \Lambda K^+$ reaction. The hypernuclear production amplitudes were calculated in momentum space using plane waves for the outgoing kaon, and the results were compared with those obtained using phenomenological Woods-Saxon scalar and vector potentials fitted to experimental binding energies.

## Key numbers
- The binding energy of the $^{12}_{\Lambda}\text{B}(1s_{1/2})$ state is 11.37 MeV (experimental average), while the QMC model predicts 14.93 MeV.
- The binding energy of the $^{12}_{\Lambda}\text{B}(1p_{3/2})$ state is 1.37 MeV (experimental), with QMC predicting 3.62 MeV.
- The binding energy of the $^{12}_{\Lambda}\text{B}(1p_{1/2})$ state is 1.03 MeV (experimental), with QMC predicting 3.62 MeV.
- The binding energy of the $^{12}\text{C}(1p_{3/2})$ nucleon state is 15.96 MeV.
- The phenomenological Dirac vector and scalar field depths for the $^{12}_{\Lambda}\text{B}(1s_{1/2})$ state are $V_v = 171.78$ MeV and $V_s = -212.69$ MeV, with radius $r = 0.983$ fm and diffuseness $a = 0.606$ fm.
- The coupling constants for the $N^*(1650)$ resonance are $g_{p\gamma}^1 = -0.45$ and $g_{K\Lambda} = 0.96$; for $N^*(1710)$: $g_{p\gamma}^1 = 0.25$ and $g_{K\Lambda} = -6.10$; for $N^*(1720)$: $g_{p\gamma}^1 = -0.75$, $g_{p\gamma}^2 = 0.25$, and $g_{K\Lambda} = 0.07$.
- The threshold for kaon photoproduction on $^{12}\text{C}$ is about 695 MeV.
- The momentum transfer at $10^\circ$ kaon angle varies from approximately 2 fm$^{-1}$ to 1.4 fm$^{-1}$ in the photon energy range 0.7 GeV to 1.2 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the covariant model of Shyam (2008) for $^{16}\text{O}(\gamma,K^+){^{16}_{\Lambda}}\text{N}$ by replacing phenomenological Woods-Saxon Dirac spinors with $\Lambda$ bound state spinors from the quark-meson coupling model, which incorporates quark degrees of freedom and self-consistent scalar and vector fields with different radial shapes. The study contrasts with earlier nonrelativistic impulse-approximation models (e.g., Motoba 1994, Lee 2001) and the Dirac-spinor approach of Bennhold (1989), and it enables future comparisons with high-resolution Jefferson Laboratory data on $(e,e^\prime K^+)$ reactions, particularly for constraining the spin-dependent parts of the $\Lambda$-nucleon interaction.
