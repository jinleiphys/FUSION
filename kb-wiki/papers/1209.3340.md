# Nuclear shadowing in deep inelastic scattering on nuclei: a closer look

> The measurement of the nuclear structure function $F_2^A (x,Q^2)$ at the future electron-ion collider (EIC) will be of great relevance to understand the origin of the nuclear shadowing and to probe gluon saturation effects. Currently there are several phenomenological models, based on very distinct approaches, which describe the scarce experimental data quite successfully. One of main uncertainties comes from the schemes used to include the effects associated to the multiple scatterings and to unitarize the cross section. In this paper we compare the predictions of three different unitarization schemes of the nuclear structure function which use the same theoretical input to describe the projectile-nucleon interaction. In particular, we consider as input the predictions of the Color Glass Condensate formalism, which reproduce the inclusive and diffractive $ep$ HERA data. Our results suggest that the experimental analysis of $F_2^A$ will be able to discriminate between the unitarization schemes.

# Nuclear shadowing in deep inelastic scattering on nuclei: a closer look

## Key claim

The three unitarization schemes (Glauber, Schwimmer, and Eikonal) all describe existing E665 experimental data at small x successfully when using the same Color Glass Condensate input for the projectile-nucleon interaction, but their predictions diverge strongly in the kinematic region accessible to a future electron-ion collider, with differences exceeding 20% at very small x, suggesting that experimental measurement of the nuclear structure function F2^A with approximately 10% precision could discriminate between the schemes.

## Method

The authors computed the nuclear structure function ratio R_A/D = (2/A) F2^A / F2^D for calcium and lead nuclei using three distinct unitarization schemes: the Glauber-Gribov formalism (multiple elastic scatterings of a color dipole), the Schwimmer equation (summation of fan diagrams with triple pomeron interactions), and the eikonal unitarized cross section. All three calculations used the same input for the dipole-proton cross section and diffractive structure function, obtained from the solution of the running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation, which reproduces inclusive and diffractive HERA data. The predictions were compared with E665 data and extrapolated to the small-x region (x down to 10^-5) at Q^2 = 1 and 10 GeV^2.

## Key numbers

- At x ≈ 10^-4 and Q^2 = 1 GeV^2, the difference between Glauber and Schwimmer predictions is almost 10% in the ratio R(Ca/D) and increases to approximately 20% in R(Pb/D)
- At the same x and Q^2, the difference between Schwimmer and Eikonal predictions is approximately 5% for R(Ca/D) and 12% for R(Pb/D)
- At smaller x values, the difference between the three predictions exceeds 20%
- The diffractive slope parameter used is B_D = 6.7 GeV^-2
- The integration limits for the diffractive mass are M^2_min = 4 m_pi^2 = 0.08 GeV^2 and x_Pom_max = 0.1
- Existing models coincide within approximately 15% in the region where experimental data exist (x ≥ 10^-2) but differ by almost a factor of 2 at x = 10^-5

## Context

This work extends previous comparisons of nuclear shadowing models (reviewed by Armesto, 2006) by using a single theoretical input (the rcBK solution of the Color Glass Condensate formalism) for all three unitarization schemes, thereby isolating the uncertainty from the multiple-scattering treatment. The Glauber-Gribov approach follows Armesto (2002), while the Schwimmer and Eikonal schemes are based on the Gribov-Regge formalism relating nuclear shadowing to diffractive dissociation (Capella, 1994; Frankfurt, 1998; Armesto, 2008). The results enable future experimental discrimination between unitarization schemes at the EIC, though the authors note that disentangling nonlinear from linear DGLAP dynamics (e.g., EPS09 parametrization) will require combined analysis of multiple observables including the nuclear diffractive structure function and vector meson production.
