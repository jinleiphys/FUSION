# Thermodynamic properties and transport coefficients of QCD matter within the non-extensive Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> We present a non-extensive version of the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model which is based on the non-extentive statistical mechanics. This new statistics is characterized by a dimensionless non-extensivity parameter $q$ that accounts for all possible effects violating the assumptions of the Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics (when $q\rightarrow1$, it returns to the Boltzmann-Gibbs case). Using this q-Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and including two different Polyakov-loop potentials, we discussed the influence of the parameter $q$ on chiral and deconfinement phase transition, various thermodynamic quantities and transport coefficients at finite temperature and zero quark chemical potential. We found that the Stefan-Boltzmann limit is actually related to the choice of statistics. For example, in the Tsallis statistics, the thermodynamic quantities $\fracε{T^{4}}$, $\frac{p}{T^{4}}$ and $\frac{s}{T^{3}}$ all increase with $q$, exceed their usual Stefan-Boltzmann limits and tend to a new $q$-related Tsallis limit at temperature high enough. Interestingly, however, due to a surprising cancellation, the high temperature limit of $c_{s}^{2}$ is still its SB limit $1/3$. In addition, we found some similarities between the non-extensive effect and the finite-size effect. For example, as $q$ increases (size decreases), the criticality of $\frac{c_{v}}{T^{3}}$ and $c_{s}^{2}$ gradually disappears. Besides, in order to better study the non-extensive effect, we defined a new susceptibility and calculated the response of thermodynamic quantities and transport coefficients to $q$. And found that their response patterns are different.

## Key claim

The paper claims that within a non-extensive (Tsallis) version of the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model, thermodynamic quantities such as energy density, pressure, and entropy density exceed their usual Stefan-Boltzmann limits and approach new q-dependent Tsallis limits at high temperature, but the square of the speed of sound still approaches the Stefan-Boltzmann limit of 1/3 due to a cancellation between the growth rates of entropy density and specific heat.

## Method

The authors generalize the two-flavor PNJL model by replacing the Boltzmann-Gibbs exponential factors with q-exponential functions from Tsallis statistics, introducing a non-extensivity parameter q (with q>1 considered). They solve the coupled mean-field gap equations for the constituent quark mass M and the Polyakov-loop expectation value Φ at zero quark chemical potential and finite temperature, using two different Polyakov-loop potentials (polynomial and logarithmic). Thermodynamic quantities and transport coefficients (shear viscosity, electrical conductivity, bulk viscosity) are then computed from the thermodynamic potential and from kinetic theory with a relaxation time approximation, respectively.

## Key numbers

- For the polynomial potential, the chiral pseudo-critical temperature Tχ decreases from 196 MeV at q=1 to 180 MeV at q=1.1; the deconfinement pseudo-critical temperature Td decreases from 170 MeV at q=1 to 154 MeV at q=1.1.
- For the logarithmic potential, Tχ decreases from 200 MeV at q=1 to 188 MeV at q=1.1; Td decreases from 160 MeV at q=1 to 142 MeV at q=1.1.
- At T=350 MeV with the logarithmic potential, the dimensionless energy density ε/T⁴ increases from 11.3 at q=1 to 18.1 at q=1.1 (a 60% increase); pressure p/T⁴ increases by 67% and entropy density s/T³ by 61%.
- The Stefan-Boltzmann limits for the two-flavor PNJL model are: p_SB/T⁴ ≈ 4.06, ε_SB/T⁴ ≈ 12.17, s_SB/T³ ≈ 16.23, and c_v/T³ ≈ 48.69.
- At high temperature (T=350 MeV) with the logarithmic potential, as q increases to 1.1, s/T³ increases by 61.4% and c_v/T³ increases by 59.4%, leading to c_s² still approaching 1/3.

## Context

This work extends the non-extensive generalization of the NJL model (Eur. Phys. J. A, 2016) and the linear sigma model (Shen, 2017) to the PNJL framework, which is more successful at reproducing lattice QCD thermodynamics (Phys. Rev. D, 2006). The study finds similarities between non-extensive effects and finite-size effects (Phys. Rev. D, 2013; Grunfeld, 2018), such as the smoothing of critical behavior in c_v/T³ and c_s² as q increases. The results enable future studies of QCD matter in heavy-ion collisions where Tsallis statistics are known to describe transverse momentum distributions (PHENIX, STAR, ALICE, ATLAS, CMS collaborations).
