# The Impact of Fluctuations on QCD Matter

> We study the effect of quantum and thermal fluctuations as well as the mass dependence of the phase structure of QCD at finite temperature and density within a dynamical Polyakov-loop--extended quark-meson model. The glue dynamics is simulated by the Polyakov-loop potential, also including the back-coupling of the matter sector to the glue dynamics. In the chiral limit, the chiral phase transition at large chemical potential and low temperature splits into two transition branches. For non-vanishing pion masses the chiral transition at small chemical potential changes from a phase transition to a crossover. We close with a discussion of a systematical improvement of the current model towards full QCD.

## Key claim

Quantum and thermal fluctuations, included via the functional renormalization group, push the critical endpoint of the two-flavor QCD phase diagram to low temperatures (around 20-30 MeV) and large chemical potential (around 290 MeV), and the matter back-coupling to the glue sector favors coincidence of the chiral and deconfinement transition lines even at larger chemical potential, casting doubt on mean-field support for a quarkyonic phase.

## Method

The authors solve the functional renormalization group (FRG) flow equation for the free energy of the dynamical Polyakov-loop-extended quark-meson (PQM) model on a sigma grid for general fixed Polyakov-loop backgrounds, without further approximation. The glue dynamics is included via a Polyakov-loop potential whose critical temperature parameter T0 is made flavor- and chemical-potential-dependent to account for matter back-coupling. The flow is integrated from a high initial cutoff scale, with parameters fixed by vacuum phenomenology (f_pi, m_pi, m_sigma), and the equations of motion for the Polyakov loops and sigma field are solved simultaneously.

## Key numbers

- Critical endpoint location: (T^CEP, mu^CEP) approximately (20-30, 290) MeV for physical pion masses (m_pi = 138 MeV).
- At physical pion masses, the deconfinement transition with constant T0 is almost mu-independent and slightly below the chiral crossover; at mu approximately 200 MeV the transitions start to deviate.
- In the chiral limit, the second-order chiral transition at mu=0 splits into two branches for temperatures below 50 MeV, with a critical endpoint on the inner branch.
- For m_pi = 50 MeV, the splitting persists with a critical endpoint again around T_c approximately 20-30 MeV.
- The pressure normalized by its Stefan-Boltzmann value at T=30 MeV and m_pi=50 MeV shows a local minimum in the splitting region, becoming non-monotonic.

## Context

This work extends prior FRG studies of the quark-meson model (Schaefer 2004) and the PQM model (Schaefer 2007, Herbst 2010) by solving the flow without the mean-field approximation for the Polyakov-loop background. It confirms and refines the splitting of the chiral transition in the chiral limit previously seen in pure quark-meson FRG studies. The results contradict mean-field PQM calculations that find a higher-temperature critical endpoint and a separation of chiral and deconfinement transitions; the authors argue that the matter back-coupling to the glue sector, often neglected, is responsible for the difference. The work enables systematic improvement of effective models toward full QCD by constraining the Polyakov-loop potential with first-principle FRG results from QCD (Braun 2009, Pawlowski 2010).
