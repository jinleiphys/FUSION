# From Coulomb excitation cross sections to non-resonant astrophysical rates in three-body systems: $^{17}$Ne case

> Coulomb and nuclear dissociation of $^{17}$Ne on light and heavy targets are studied theoretically. The dipole E1 strength function is determined in a broad energy range including energies of astrophysical interest. Dependence of the strength function on different parameters of the $^{17}$Ne ground state structure and continuum dynamics is analyzed in a three-body model. The discovered dependence plays an important role for studies of the strength functions for the three-body E1 dissociation and radiative capture. The constraints on the $[s^2]/[d^2]$ configuration mixing in $^{17}$Ne and on $p$-wave interaction in the $^{15}$O+$p$ channel are imposed based on experimental data for $^{17}$Ne Coulomb dissociation on heavy target.

## Key claim

The non-resonant astrophysical radiative capture rate for \(^{15}\text{O}+p+p \rightarrow ^{17}\text{Ne}+\gamma\) depends strongly on the low-energy behavior of the E1 strength function, which cannot be reliably extrapolated from experimentally accessible energies (above several hundred keV) without accounting for four specific nuclear-structure parameters: binding energy, energies of natural-parity states in \(^{16}\text{F}\), \(p\)-wave interactions in the \(^{15}\text{O}+p\) channel, and \([s^2]/[d^2]\) configuration mixing in \(^{17}\text{Ne}\).

## Method

The authors combine a three-body hyperspherical harmonic (HH) method for the \(^{17}\text{Ne}\) ground state with a Green's function approach using a simplified three-body Hamiltonian to compute continuum states populated by E1 transitions. Coulomb dissociation cross sections are calculated with the Bertulani-Baur model, and nuclear dissociation is treated via the Glauber model eikonal approximation, using a smooth absorption function derived from eikonal S-matrices for the projectile clusters. The E1 strength function is decomposed into contributions from final states with \(J^\pi = 1/2^+\) and \(3/2^+\), and the dependence on four nuclear-structure parameters is systematically varied.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of \(^{17}\text{Ne}\) ground state: \(E_b = -0.933\) MeV.
- Energies of two-body resonant states in \(^{15}\text{O}+p\) channel: \(s\)-wave \(0^-\) at 0.535 MeV, \(1^-\) at 0.728 MeV; \(d\)-wave \(2^-\) at 0.959 MeV, \(3^-\) at 1.256 MeV.
- Ratio of \(2p\) width to gamma width for the \(3/2^-\) first excited state: experimental limits \(\Gamma_{2p}/\Gamma_{\gamma} \leq 7.7 \times 10^{-3}\) (Chromik 2002) and \(\leq 1.6 \times 10^{-4}\) (Sharov 2017); theoretical prediction \(\Gamma_{2p}/\Gamma_{\gamma} \sim (0.9-2.5) \times 10^{-6}\) (Grigorenko 2007).
- Coulomb dissociation cross section on lead target at 500 AMeV: total Coulomb soft E1 = 322 mb; soft E1 for \(E_T < 7\) MeV = 243 mb; soft E1 for \(E_T < 5\) MeV = 148 mb.
- Nuclear E1 cross section on lead: 1.2 mb; nuclear total: 35 mb.
- For \(V_p = -21\) MeV, the E1 Coulex cross section integrated up to 5 MeV is 148 mb; for \(V_p = 0\) MeV it is 93 mb.
- The E1 strength function at low energies varies by four orders of magnitude when binding energy is varied from 0 to 5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Grigorenko 2006) on the sensitivity of the non-resonant radiative capture rate to the soft dipole mode in \(^{17}\text{Ne}\), now using recent Coulomb dissociation data on a lead target (Marganiec 2016, Wamers 2018). It contradicts the claim by Casal (2016) that the non-resonant rate dominates and the resonant contribution is negligible; the authors argue that the balance between resonant and non-resonant contributions remains important. The analysis demonstrates that the E1 strength function in the experimentally accessible range (1-10 MeV) can have very different low-energy asymptotics depending on dynamical details, which is critical for extrapolating to astrophysical energies below several tens of keV.
