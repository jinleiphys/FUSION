# S-Matrix approach to Compton scattering at the tree level in a strong magnetic field

> We have studied the Compton scattering ($γe^- \longrightarrow γe^-$) at the tree level in a homogeneous background of strong magnetic field ($|eB| \gg { m }^{ 2 },~\text{m is the mass of electron}$) through the S-matrix approach. For that purpose, using the Schwinger propagator for the electron, we have first calculated the square of the S-matrix element in the Landau gauge by summing over the final states of electron and photon and averaging over the initial states of the same. In the strong magnetic field, only the lowest Landau level for electron is considered. Finally we have computed the crosssection for Compton scattering as a function of initial photon energy for the different strengths of strong magnetic fields, where we have found that the crosssection in vacuum gets decreased due to the presence of strong magnetic field. However, for a fixed initial photon energy, the crosssection increases linearly with the magnetic field.

## Key claim
Compton scattering cross-section in a strong magnetic field (|eB| >> m²) is suppressed compared to the vacuum case, but for a fixed initial photon energy, the cross-section increases linearly with the magnetic field strength. This result holds only at tree level and when only the lowest Landau level (n=0) is populated.

## Method
The authors used the Schwinger proper-time propagator for the electron in a constant homogeneous magnetic field along the Z-direction, working in the Landau gauge. They calculated the S-matrix element for both s-channel and u-channel diagrams at tree level, summing over final states and averaging over initial states of electron and photon. In the strong-field limit, only the lowest Landau level (n=0) was considered, and the electron propagator factorized into longitudinal and transverse components. The cross-section was computed as a function of initial photon energy for different magnetic field strengths.

## Key numbers
- The magnetic field strength considered satisfies |eB| >> m², where m is the electron mass (approximately 0.511 MeV)
- The interference term between s-channel and u-channel vanishes in the calculation
- The cross-section in vacuum decreases due to the presence of a strong magnetic field
- For a fixed initial photon energy, the cross-section increases linearly with the magnetic field strength

## Context
This work extends relativistic QED treatments of Compton scattering in strong magnetic fields, following earlier nonrelativistic treatments by Canuto (1971) and Blandford (1976), and relativistic QED calculations by Daugherty (1986) and Bussard (1986). The S-matrix approach using the Schwinger propagator provides a systematic framework for calculating scattering cross-sections in strong magnetic fields relevant to magnetized neutron stars and early-universe electroweak phase transitions. The results enable more accurate modeling of radiation transfer in astrophysical environments with strong magnetic fields, such as magnetars, and could be extended to include finite decay widths for resonant scattering as considered by Gonthier (2014) and Mushtukov (2015).
