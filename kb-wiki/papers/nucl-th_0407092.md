# Damped collective motion of many body systems: A variational approach to the quantal decay rate

> We address the problem of collective motion across a barrier like encountered in fission. A formula for the quantal decay rate is derived which bases on a recently developed variational approach for functional integrals. This formula can be applied to low temperatures that have not been accessible within the former PSPA type approach. To account for damping of collective motion one particle Green functions are dressed with appropriate self-energies.

## Key claim

The paper derives a quantal decay rate formula for collective motion across a barrier (as in fission) that remains valid at temperatures below the crossover temperature \(T_0\), where the earlier Perturbed Static Path Approximation (PSPA) diverges. The improvement relies on a variational approach to functional integrals that accounts for anharmonic terms in the Euclidean action, and on dressing one-particle Green functions with self-energies to incorporate damping of collective motion.

## Method

The authors start from a separable two-body interaction \(\frac{k}{2}\hat{F}^2\) added to an independent-particle Hamiltonian, and use a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce a collective variable \(q(\tau)\). The partition function is expressed as an integral over the static component \(q_0\) times a quantum correction factor \(\zeta(\beta,q_0)\) that contains fluctuations. To go beyond the PSPA (which truncates the action at second order and diverges at \(T_0\)), they apply a variational approach where a reference action with adjustable parameters \(\Omega_\mu^2\) replaces the RPA frequencies, and the effective free energy is minimized. Residual interactions are included by replacing the bare one-body Green functions with dressed ones using a phenomenological self-energy \(\Sigma_k(z)\) with width \(\Gamma_k(\omega)\), leading to a damped collective response function fitted to a harmonic oscillator with inertia \(M\), stiffness \(C\), and damping \(\gamma\). The decay rate is then obtained from Langer's formula using the imaginary part of the free energy evaluated via steepest descent around the barrier.

## Key numbers

- Crossover temperature for the dissipative case: \(T_0(q_0) = \frac{\hbar\varpi(q_0)}{2\pi} \left( \sqrt{1 + \eta^2(q_0)} - \eta(q_0) \right)\), where \(\eta = \gamma/(2\sqrt{M|C|})\) is the damping coefficient.
- Parameters for the phenomenological width: \(\Gamma_0 \approx 33\ \text{MeV}\) and \(c \approx 20\ \text{MeV}\) in \(\Gamma_k(\omega) = \frac{1}{\Gamma_0} \frac{(\hbar\omega - \mu)^2 + \pi^2 T^2}{1 + \frac{1}{c^2}[(\hbar\omega - \mu)^2 + \pi^2 T^2]}\).
- The PSPA convergence condition \(\lambda_1(\beta,q_0) > 0\) translates to \(T > T_0\), with \(T_0 = \text{Max}_{q_0} \frac{\hbar |\varpi_{\text{inst}}(q_0)|}{2\pi}\) for the undamped case, where \(\varpi_{\text{inst}}^2 < 0\) is the unstable local RPA mode.
- The extended PSPA (ePSPA) lowers the breakdown temperature to \(T_0/2\).

## Context

This work extends the PSPA approach of Athanasiou and Liotta (1997) and the Correlated Static Path Approximation of Rossignoli, which fail at the crossover temperature \(T_0\). It builds on a variational method for functional integrals developed by Rummel and Hofmann (2003) and connects to transport theory for damped collective motion (Siemens et al., 1984; Hofmann, 1997) by deriving transport coefficients (inertia \(M\), friction \(\gamma\), stiffness \(C\)) microscopically from the nucleonic response function. Unlike the Caldeira-Leggett model (1983), where the heat bath is fixed and independent of the collective variable, this approach allows the transport coefficients to depend on temperature and the collective coordinate \(q_0\), which is essential for nuclear fission. The resulting rate formula enables calculations at low temperatures previously inaccessible, and the method can be tested on a one-dimensional damped particle as a proxy for more complex many-body systems.
