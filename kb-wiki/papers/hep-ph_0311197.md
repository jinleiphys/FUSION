# Generalized Parton Distributions and the structure of the constituent quark

> In a scenario where the constituent quarks are composite systems, Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) are built from wave functions to be evaluated in a Constituent Quark Model (CQM), convoluted with the GPDs of the constituent quarks themselves. The approach permits to access the kinematical range corresponding to the DGLAP and ERBL regions, so that cross sections for deeply virtual Compton scattering could be estimated.

# Generalized Parton Distributions and the structure of the constituent quark

## Key claim

The authors show that by treating constituent quarks as composite systems with internal structure, rather than point-like particles, one can compute Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) across the full kinematical range including both DGLAP and ERBL regions, which was not possible in earlier Constituent Quark Model calculations that only accessed the DGLAP region.

## Method

The authors construct a convolution formula where the proton GPD $H_q(x,\xi,\Delta^2)$ is expressed as an integral over the constituent quark momentum fraction $z$ of the product of a constituent quark off-forward momentum distribution $H_{q_0}(z,\xi,\Delta^2)$, evaluated using the Isgur-Karl wave functions in a non-relativistic Constituent Quark Model, and a constituent quark GPD $H_{q_0q}(x/z,\xi/z,\Delta^2)$. The constituent quark GPD is modeled using Radyushkin's double distribution formalism with a factorized ansatz involving forward densities from the Altarelli-Cabibbo-Maiani-Petronzio (ACMP) scenario, a monopole form factor corresponding to a constituent quark size of approximately 0.3 fm, and a mesonic amplitude term.

## Key numbers

- The hadronic scale (model scale) is set to $\mu_0^2 = 0.34$ GeV$^2$
- Results are shown for $\xi = 0.2$ and $\Delta^2 = -0.5$ GeV$^2$ at the model scale
- The constituent quark size is taken as $r_Q \simeq 0.3$ fm, supported by analysis of electron scattering data
- NLO QCD evolution is performed up to $Q^2 = 10$ GeV$^2$ for the non-singlet valence $u$-quark distribution

## Context

This work extends the earlier Constituent Quark Model approach of Scopetta and Vento (2002) and the formalism of Ref. [epj], which could only access the DGLAP region because constituent quarks were treated as point-like. The authors build upon the ACMP scenario (Altarelli, Cabibbo, Maiani, Petronzio, 1974) and its update by Scopetta (2001), which showed that deep inelastic scattering data are consistent with composite constituent quarks. The approach enables calculation of cross sections for deeply virtual Compton scattering across a wide kinematical range, providing predictions to guide future experiments at facilities that will measure GPDs through exclusive electron deep inelastic scattering.
