# Superconducting phases of strongly-interacting matter in large magnetic fields

> The large magnetic fields of neutron stars and produced in heavy-ion collisions motivate investigation into the response of strongly-interacting matter to extreme magnetic forces beyond just theoretical interest. Furthermore, the varying temperature $T$, baryon chemical potential $μ_B$, and aforementioned magnetic fields $B$, of these systems leads to questions concerning the phase structure of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) at large. At low temperatures, superconducting phases become a possible candidate for the ground state in the $μ_B$-$B$ plane. This thesis investigates two scenarios where these phases emerge at $T=0$, with an emphasis on the type-II regime. Preceding the presentation of these works, the $μ_B$-$B$ plane of the QCD phase diagram is reviewed such that the results can be placed in the wider context of this plane.

## Key claim
This thesis investigates superconducting phases of strongly-interacting matter at zero temperature in the type-II regime, specifically examining how large magnetic fields (comparable to those in neutron stars and heavy-ion collisions) modify the QCD phase diagram in the baryon chemical potential–magnetic field plane, with the caveat that the analysis is restricted to the $T=0$ limit and emphasizes type-II superconducting behavior.

## Method
The author derives the anomalous baryon current using the Goldstone-Wilczek approach, parameterizing the chiral field as $\Sigma = \Sigma_0 U$ with $\Sigma_0 = e^{i\alpha\tau_3}$ and evaluating traces to obtain the current in terms of a complex scalar field $\varphi$. For flux tube configurations, the asymptotic behavior of condensates and gauge fields is analyzed using an ansatz for $\tilde{a}_3$, $\tilde{a}_8$, $f_2$, and $f_3$ at large radial distances, solving the linearized equations of motion to extract decay lengths. Lattice configuration calculations are performed using Fourier analysis with periodicity $N_x=2$ to determine translational symmetry conditions that minimize free energy, and numerical solutions are obtained via successive over-relaxation (SOR) methods with Newton-Raphson root-finding.

## Key numbers
- Penetration depth: $\ell = 1/(\tilde{q}_{83}\rho_{\rm 2SC})$ (dimensionless form $\tilde{q}_{83}R/\sqrt{\lambda} = r/\ell$)
- Coherence length for $f_3$ condensate: $\xi_3 = 1/(\sqrt{\lambda}\rho_{\rm 2SC})$ (from $R = r/\xi_3$)
- Induced condensate decay length: $\xi_2 = \left[\frac{3eg^2 H}{4(3g^2+e^2)}\right]^{-1/2}$ (from $\Xi^{1/2}R = r/\xi_2$)
- Asymptotic solution for induced $us$ condensate: $u_2(R) \simeq (d_2/R) e^{-\Xi R^2/2}$ (Gaussian suppression, not exponential)
- Asymptotic gauge field deviation: $\tilde{v}_3 \simeq (\tilde{q}_3 d_2^2 / (4\lambda \Xi R^3)) e^{-\Xi R^2}$
- Asymptotic behavior of $\tilde{v}_8$: $\tilde{v}_8(R) \simeq (\tilde{c}_8/\sqrt{R}) e^{-\tilde{q}_{83}R/\sqrt{\lambda}}$
- Asymptotic behavior of $u_3$: $u_3(R) = (d_3/\sqrt{R}) e^{-\sqrt{2}R}$
- Free energy minimization yields $\beta_b = (q/\sqrt{2\pi})(f_0^2 + 2f_0f_1 - f_1^2)$ as the smallest value for $N_x=2$ lattice

## Context
This work extends the study of color superconducting phases in dense QCD matter, specifically the 2SC (two-flavor superconducting) and CFL (color-flavor locked) phases, by incorporating the effects of strong magnetic fields. The asymptotic analysis of flux tubes builds on the approach of Haber (2017, 2018) for CFL flux tubes, while the lattice structure calculations for the pion superconductor relate to the Abrikosov vortex lattice formalism. The results enable future investigation of the transition from flux tube to domain wall solutions at large winding numbers, as studied by Penin (2020) in the abelian Higgs model.
