# Study of η-η' mixing from radiative decay processes

> We perform a thorough analysis of the VPγ(γ*) and Pγγ(γ*) decays in the resonance chiral theory, where V stand for the vector resonances ρ, K*, ω, ϕ, P stand for π, K, η, η' and γ* subsequently decays into lepton pairs. Upon imposing QCD short-distance constraints on resonance couplings, the ω-> πγ(γ*), ρ-> πγ(γ*), K^{*0} -> K^0γprocesses only depend on one free parameter and π-> γγ(γ*) can be completely predicted. The four mixing parameters of the η-η' system, i.e. two mixing angles \theta8, \theta0 and two decay constants F8, F0, are determined from radiative decays involving ηor η'. The higher order low energy constants of the pseudo-Goldstone Lagrangian in the chiral anomaly sector are predicted by integrating out heavy resonances. We also predict the decay widths of ρ-> πe^+ e^-, η' -> γe^+ e^- and ϕ-> ημ^+ μ^-, which can be compared with the future measurement in these channels.

## Key claim
The paper determines the four η-η' mixing parameters (two mixing angles θ₈, θ₀ and two decay constants F₈, F₀) from radiative decay data using Resonance Chiral Theory (RχT), but the results depend on imposing specific QCD short-distance constraints that leave only one free parameter for ω→πγ(γ*), ρ→πγ(γ*), and K*⁰→K⁰γ processes, while π→γγ(γ*) becomes a complete prediction.

## Method
The authors extended the RχT Lagrangian from the SU(3) octet of pseudo-Goldstone bosons to the U(3) nonet including the η' meson, using the antisymmetric tensor formalism for vector resonances. They computed tree-level amplitudes for VPγ(γ*) and Pγγ(γ*) decays, imposed QCD short-distance constraints from operator product expansion matching and high-energy behavior of transition form factors, and performed a global fit to experimental data on V→Pγ, P→Vγ, and P→γγ decays to extract the mixing parameters.

## Key numbers
- The π→γγ decay width is completely predicted from QCD constraints, with no free parameters in the RχT framework.
- The ω→πγ, ρ→πγ, and K*⁰→K⁰γ processes each depend on only one free parameter after imposing short-distance constraints.
- Predicted decay widths are given for ρ→πe⁺e⁻, η'→γe⁺e⁻, and φ→ημ⁺μ⁻, to be compared with future measurements.
- The η₁ mass M₀ (from the U_A(1) anomaly) is treated as non-vanishing even in the chiral limit, with a value higher than the lowest vector resonance mass M_V.
- The vector resonance mass in the chiral limit M_V is estimated using M_ρ (ρ(770) mass).
- The parameter F_V (vector-photon transition strength) is taken from literature values: F_V = √2 F in the minimal leading-order analysis, or F_V = √3 F in next-to-leading order studies.

## Context
This work extends the RχT analysis of VPγ and Pγγ decays from the octet case (Pich, 2003) to the nonet including η', enabling determination of η-η' mixing parameters. It builds on the two-mixing-angle scheme proposed by Leutwyler (1998) and Kaiser (1998), and contrasts with earlier single-constant VMD approaches by incorporating all symmetry-allowed operators in RχT. The results enable predictions for lepton-pair decay channels (ρ→πe⁺e⁻, η'→γe⁺e⁻, φ→ημ⁺μ⁻) that can be tested at facilities like KLOE, Jefferson Lab, and BES-III.
