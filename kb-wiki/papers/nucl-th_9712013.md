# Hadron Polarizabilities and Form Factors

> This is the summary of the working group on Hadron Polarizabilities and Form Factors of the Chiral Dynamics Workshop in Mainz, September 1-5, 1997.

## Key claim
This working group summary reports the status of hadron form factors and polarizabilities as of 1997, highlighting that the electric form factor of the neutron is particularly sensitive to model details and that the unexpectedly large magnetic neutron form factor in the time-like region may indicate a resonance near threshold, though the data still have large error bars.

## Method
The working group reviewed experimental data and theoretical calculations for nucleon form factors in both space-like and time-like momentum transfer regions, using dispersion relations, chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), and the inverse amplitude method (IAM). For polarizabilities, the group examined Compton scattering experiments and theoretical predictions from ChPT and dispersion calculations, including Baldin's sum rule. The group also discussed virtual Compton scattering (VCS) and its potential to measure generalized polarizabilities.

## Key numbers
- Proton electric radius: \(r^{p}_{E} = (0.862 \pm 0.012) \, \text{fm}\) from Mainz experiments in the 1970s, and \(r^{p}_{E} = (0.847 \pm 0.008) \, \text{fm}\) from a dispersion theory global fit (Mergell et al., 1996).
- Neutron electric mean square radius: \(\langle r^2 \rangle^n_E = (-0.113 \pm 0.003 \pm 0.004) \, \text{fm}^2\) from low-energy neutron transmission through Pb atoms (Kopecky et al., 1995).
- Electric form factor of the neutron from the \(^3\vec{\text{He}}(\vec{e}, e'n)\) experiment at MAMI: \(G_E^n = 0.0352 \pm 0.0033 \pm 0.0024\) (Q\(^2\)-averaged result, with \(G_M^n = 1.05 \pm 0.05\) times the dipole fit) over Q\(^2\) = 0.27–0.5 GeV\(^2\) (Becker et al., 1997).
- Baldin sum rule for the proton: \(\alpha_p + \beta_p = (14.2 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-4} \, \text{fm}^3\) from the total photoabsorption cross section.
- Proton polarizabilities from the Illinois/SAL collaboration: \(\alpha_p = (12.1 \pm 0.8 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-4} \, \text{fm}^3\), \(\beta_p = (2.1 \mp 0.8 \mp 0.5) \times 10^{-4} \, \text{fm}^3\) (with Baldin sum rule as constraint).
- Slope of the Dirac isoscalar form factor at \(t=0\): \(F'_{1s}(0) = 1.36 \, \text{GeV}^{-2}\) from Mergell et al. (1996), with \(\omega\) and \(\phi\) contributions giving \(F'_{1s}(0) = (2.00 - 0.683) = 1.32 \, \text{GeV}^{-2}\).

## Context
This summary extends the work on nucleon form factors using dispersion relations (Hoehler et al., 1976; Mergell et al., 1996) and ChPT calculations (Bernard, Kaiser, Meissner, 1996), and addresses discrepancies in the neutron electric form factor between \(^3\)He and deuteron experiments. The results enable future double-polarization experiments at MAMI, MIT/Bates, and Jefferson Lab to measure \(G_E^n\) more model-independently, and motivate further study of the time-like region where the FENICE group plans a high-luminosity asymmetric \(e^+e^-\) collider. For polarizabilities, the ongoing Mainz experiment with tagged photons and the TAPS detector aims to improve precision on the proton scalar polarizabilities.
