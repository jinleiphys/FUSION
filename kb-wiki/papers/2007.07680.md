# Formation of light nuclei at chemical freezeout: Description within a statistical thermal model

> The thermal description of light nuclei at the chemical freeze-out has been investigated. First, I have verified the equilibration of the light nuclei and then introduced a new method to investigate the light nuclei formation. One can study the proximity between the phase space density of light nuclei ratios and their hadronic constituents e.g $\bar{d}/d$ and $(\bar{p}\bar{n}/pn)$. I have found that with the exclusion of the decay feed-down from the hadronic yields in the thermal model, the hadronic representations have good agreement with the light nuclei ratios. I have performed a similar analysis with the ratio of $Λ$-hypernuclei and $^3 He$, which is related to the ratio $Λ/p$. In this context, the strangeness population factor $S_3$ has been studied also. These results indicate that the nuclei and hypernuclei formation may occur near the standard chemical freeze-out and before the decay of the hadronic resonances. This method will serve as a guideline to discuss the light nuclei formation and the inclusion of decay into their hadronic constituents.

## Key claim

The paper argues that light nuclei and hypernuclei are formed near the chemical freeze-out boundary and before the decay of hadronic resonances, based on the finding that ratios of light nuclei to their hadronic constituents show better agreement when using only primary (pre-decay) hadron yields rather than total yields including decay feed-down.

## Method

The author uses a statistical thermal model (Hadron Resonance Gas model) to extract chemical freeze-out parameters (T, μ_B, μ_Q, μ_S) by solving equations that relate net baryon charge to total baryon charge and net baryon charge to total particle multiplicity, alongside constraints of net electric charge to net baryon and strangeness neutrality. The analysis incorporates yields of hadrons and light nuclei (d, ³He, ⁴He, ³H, and ³_ΛH) from AGS, SPS, RHIC, and LHC experiments. The author then compares ratios of light nuclei (such as d̄/d, ³_ΛH/³He) with ratios of their hadronic constituents (such as (p̄/p)², Λ/p) computed both with and without decay feed-down contributions.

## Key numbers

- At LHC 2.76 TeV, the temperature decreases by 1 MeV when incorporating all available light nuclei yields into the fit, which is within estimated variances of prior work.
- The estimated χ² per degree of freedom for representative particle ratios at LHC 2.76 TeV is 8.45/11.
- The ratio d/p varies by two orders of magnitude between LHC and AGS energies, while ⁴He/p rises from 10⁻⁸ at LHC to 10⁻⁴ at AGS.
- The factor C₂ (comparing d̄/d to (p̄/p)²) ranges from approximately 0.8 to 1 when using primary proton yields, but decreases significantly at lower √s_NN when including resonance decay feed-down.
- The strangeness population factor S₃ increases from 0.6 at AGS energies to 1 at RHIC 200 GeV, then drops to 0.6 at LHC when using total yields; when using only primary yields, S₃ stays near 0.9 at all collision energies.

## Context

This work extends the statistical thermal model description of light nuclei previously applied by Andronic et al. (2017) and Cleymans et al. (2011), who analyzed light nuclei ratios assuming Boltzmann approximation and neglecting decay feed-down. The paper addresses concerns raised by Oliinychenko et al. (2018) and Cai et al. (2019) about whether weakly bound light nuclei can survive at chemical freeze-out temperatures near 150 MeV. The method enables future investigation of light nuclei formation mechanisms and can be tested with upcoming data from CBM and NICA collaborations, as well as with preliminary tritium yields from HADES, STAR, and ALICE.
