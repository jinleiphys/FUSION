# The Bose-Einstein correlations from a Quantum Field Theory perspective

> Using a specific version of thermal Quantum Field Theory (QFT), supplemented by operator-field evolution of the Langevin type, we discuss two issues concerning the Bose Einstein correlations (BEC): the origin of different possible coherent behaviour of the emitting source and the origin of the observed shape of the BEC function $C_2(Q)$. We demonstrate that previous conjectures in this matter obtained by other approaches are confirmed and have received complementary explanation.

# The Bose-Einstein correlations from a Quantum Field Theory perspective

## Key claim

Using a thermal Quantum Field Theory supplemented by a Langevin-type operator-field evolution, the authors demonstrate that the two main empirical forms of the two-body Bose-Einstein correlation function \(C_2(Q)\), one with a single \(Q\)-dependent term and one with two such terms, are both correct but correspond to different definitions of coherence: the former arises from dividing the source into coherent and chaotic subsources, while the latter arises from a single source producing partially coherent fields.

## Method

The authors model the hadronizing source by representing the annihilation operator for a selected boson as the sum of a heat-bath part and an external-force part, with time evolution governed by a Langevin equation. Thermal averages are taken over a reservoir of damped oscillators characterized by temperature \(T\) and chemical potential \(\mu\), with the Kubo-Martin-Schwinger condition enforcing bosonic statistics. To obtain a finite-width correlation function, the delta functions appearing in the thermal averages are replaced by smearing functions \(\Omega(Q \cdot r)\) that encode the finite space-time extent of the source, with the 4-vector \(r_\mu\) defining the source size.

## Key numbers

- The correlation function \(C_2(Q)\) reaches its maximum at \(Q=0\) and falls to its asymptotic value \(C_2 = 1\) already at \(Q \sim 1\) GeV/c.
- For the case \(\alpha = 0\) (no external force), the correlation function satisfies \(1 < C_2(Q) < 1 + \gamma \Omega(Q\cdot r)\), where \(\gamma = n^2(\bar{\omega})/[n(\omega)n(\omega')]\).
- The chaoticity parameter \(p\) is related to the coherence parameter \(\alpha\) by \(p = 1/(1+\alpha)\), with example values shown for \(\alpha = 0, 0.25, 1, 4\) corresponding to \(p = 1.0, 0.8, 0.5, 0.2\) respectively.

## Context

This work extends the quantum-statistical approach of Weiner (1999) and the coherent-chaotic subsource model of Andreev, Lednicky, and Sumbera (1994) by providing a common QFT framework that reconciles their different predictions for the shape of \(C_2(Q)\). The authors show that the smearing of delta functions in thermal averages, equivalent to introducing a finite source size, is the origin of the observed \(Q\)-dependence, a point made implicitly in earlier QFT treatments such as that of Ravndal and Wroldsen (1984). The formalism enables future experimental discrimination between the two coherence scenarios, particularly at larger coherence (smaller chaoticity) where the predicted \(C_2(Q)\) shapes differ most visibly.
