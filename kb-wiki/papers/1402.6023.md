# Formulation of transverse mass distributions in Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV/nucleon

> The transverse mass spectra of light mesons produced in Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV/nucleon are analyzed in Tsallis statistics. In high energy collisions, it has been found that the spectra follow a generalized scaling law. We applied Tsallis statistics to the description of different particles using the scaling properties. The calculated results are in agreement with experimental data of PHENIX Collaboration. And, the temperature of emission sources is extracted consistently.

# Formulation of transverse mass distributions in Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV/nucleon

## Key claim

The transverse mass spectra of light mesons in Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV/nucleon can be described by Tsallis statistics, and using the observed m_T scaling properties, the temperature of emission sources can be extracted directly and consistently from pion data alone, with the caveat that the extracted temperature depends on collision centrality and the fit quality varies across meson species (χ²/dof ranges from 0.38 to 0.96).

## Method

The authors fit the transverse mass spectra of charged and neutral pions measured by the PHENIX Collaboration at midrapidity using a Tsallis distribution formula that includes a rapidity integration width. From these fits, they extract the temperature T and the nonequilibrium parameter q for four centrality classes (Minimum Bias, 0-20%, 20-60%, 60-92%). Using the m_T scaling property, they then predict the transverse mass spectra of K±, J/ψ, φ, ω, and η mesons without additional free parameters, and compare these predictions to PHENIX data.

## Key numbers

- Temperature T for Minimum Bias pions: 0.064 ± 0.02 GeV, with q = 1.094 ± 0.02, χ²/dof = 0.80
- Temperature T for 0-20% centrality pions: 0.078 ± 0.03 GeV, with q = 1.086 ± 0.02, χ²/dof = 0.56
- Temperature T for 20-60% centrality pions: 0.072 ± 0.02 GeV, with q = 1.091 ± 0.03, χ²/dof = 0.49
- Temperature T for 60-92% centrality pions: 0.059 ± 0.03 GeV, with q = 1.098 ± 0.02, χ²/dof = 0.38
- Minimum Bias K/π⁰ yield ratio: 0.531 ± 0.003
- Minimum Bias η/π⁰ yield ratio: 0.524 ± 0.038
- Minimum Bias φ/π⁰ yield ratio: 0.342 ± 0.015
- Minimum Bias (J/ψ)/π⁰ yield ratio: 0.0034 ± 0.0004
- χ²/dof for all meson fits ranges from 0.38 (minimum) to 0.96 (maximum)

## Context

This work extends the application of Tsallis statistics to transverse mass spectra in heavy-ion collisions, following earlier statistical model approaches by Adams (2003) and the cylinder model by Liu and collaborators (previous work). The m_T scaling property, previously observed in S-S and S-Au collisions by Albrecht (1995), is here exploited to predict spectra of multiple meson species from pion fits alone. The extracted temperatures increase with centrality, consistent with the cylinder model results of the same group, but now provide direct numerical values rather than relative excitation degrees. This enables more precise estimates of hadronic decay backgrounds in photonic and leptonic probes of quark-gluon plasma at RHIC.
