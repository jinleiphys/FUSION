# Pygmy dipole resonance in 208Pb

> Scattering of protons of several hundred MeV is a promising new spectroscopic tool for the study of electric dipole strength in nuclei. A case study of 208Pb shows that at very forward angles J^pi = 1- states are strongly populated via Coulomb excitation. A separation from nuclear excitation of other modes is achieved by a multipole decomposition analysis of the experimental cross sections based on theoretical angular distributions calculated within the quasiparticle-phonon model. The B(E1) transition strength distribution is extracted for excitation energies up to 9 MeV, i.e., in the region of the so-called pygmy dipole resonance (PDR). The Coulomb-nuclear interference shows sensitivity to the underlying structure of the E1 transitions, which allows for the first time an experimental extraction of the electromagnetic transition strength and the energy centroid of the PDR.

## Key claim

The paper claims that high-energy proton scattering at very forward angles can be used to extract the B(E1) transition strength distribution of the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) in 208Pb up to 9 MeV, and that the Coulomb-nuclear interference in the angular distributions allows for the first experimental separation of PDR and GDR contributions. The hedge is that this separation relies on a multipole decomposition analysis (MDA) using theoretical angular distributions from the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM), and the systematic uncertainty of the MDA approach is estimated to be at most 10%.

## Method

The experiment used a 295 MeV polarized proton beam from the RING cyclotron at RCNP, Osaka University, incident on an isotopically enriched 208Pb foil of 5.2 mg/cm² areal density. Scattered protons were measured with the Grand Raiden spectrometer over an angular range of 0° to 2.5° for excitation energies from 4 to 22 MeV, with an energy resolution of about 25 keV. A multipole decomposition analysis (MDA) was performed on the experimental angular distributions using theoretical cross sections calculated with the code DWBA07, employing RPA amplitudes and single-particle wave functions from the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) and the t-matrix parametrization of Love and Franey at 325 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Energy resolution: ΔE ≈ 25 keV (full width at half maximum)
- Proton beam energy: 295 MeV
- Areal density of 208Pb target: 5.2 mg/cm²
- B(E1) strength for the state at 5.5128 MeV: 0.397(21) e²fm²
- B(E1) strength for the state at 5.2949 MeV: 0.112(8) e²fm²
- B(E1) strength for the state at 6.7184 MeV: 0.095(6) e²fm²
- Extracted PDR centroid energy: Ec = 6.82(2) MeV
- Extracted total PDR strength: Σ B(E1) = 2.18(7) e²fm²
- Excitation energy range of the analysis: 4.8 to 9.0 MeV
- Scattering angle range for cross section integration: 0° to 0.94°

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the PDR in 208Pb using (γ,γ') reactions by Ryezayeva (2002), Enders (2003), Shizuma (2008), and Schwengner (2010), which suffered from uncertainties in ground-state branching ratios and limitations above the neutron separation energy. The new method overcomes these limitations and provides an experimental benchmark for theoretical predictions from the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM), the relativistic time-blocking approximation (RTBA) by Litvinova (2007), and shell-model calculations by Brown (2000). The results enable future studies of the PDR in vibrational and deformed nuclei, and can be combined with isoscalar probes such as (α,α'γ) reactions to further constrain the structure of the PDR.
