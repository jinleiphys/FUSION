# Realistic shell-model calculations for neutron-rich calcium isotopes

> We study the neutron-rich calcium isotopes performing shell-model calculations with a realistic effective interaction. This is derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential renormalized by way of the V-low-k approach, considering 48Ca as an inert core and including the neutron 0g9/2 orbital. We compare our results with experiment and with the results of a previous study where 40Ca was assumed as a closed core and the standard 1p0f model space was employed. The calculated spectroscopic properties are in both cases in very good agreement with the available experimental data and enable a discussion on the role of the g9/2 single-particle state in the heavy-mass Ca isotopes.

## Key claim
Shell-model calculations using a realistic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn potential with a 48Ca inert core and including the neutron 0g9/2 orbital reproduce experimental data for neutron-rich calcium isotopes up to A=56, but the role of the g9/2 orbital becomes significant only from 54Ca onward, where differences between the two model-space calculations increase with neutron number.

## Method
The authors derived a shell-model effective interaction from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential renormalized via the V-low-k approach, using a model space consisting of the neutron 0f5/2, 1p3/2, 1p1/2, and 0g9/2 orbitals and the proton 0f7/2 orbital above a doubly-closed 48Ca core. The two-body matrix elements were calculated through third-order perturbation theory using the Q-box plus folded-diagram method, with the folded series summed to all orders via the Lee-Suzuki iteration method. Results were compared with a previous calculation (Coraggio 2009) that used a 40Ca core and the standard 1p0f model space.

## Key numbers
- Single-particle energies for neutrons: epsilon_3/2 = -4.60 MeV, epsilon_1/2 = -2.86 MeV, epsilon_5/2 = -1.20 MeV, epsilon_9/2 = -0.55 MeV
- B(E2;2+_1 -> 0+_1) in 50Ca: calculated value 7.1 e^2 fm^4, experimental value 7.5 +/- 0.2 e^2 fm^4 (Valiente-Dobon 2009)
- 50Ca 2+_1 energy: calculated I = 0.939 MeV, calculated II = 0.953 MeV, experimental = 1.026 MeV (Rejmund 2007)
- 52Ca 2+_1 energy: calculated I = 2.358 MeV, calculated II = 2.396 MeV, experimental = 2.562 MeV (Rejmund 2007)
- 51Ca 9/2+_1 energy: calculated I = 4.126 MeV, experimental = 4.155 MeV (Fornal 2008)
- Two-neutron separation energy for 56Ca is about 2 MeV larger in calculation I than in calculation II

## Context
This work extends the realistic shell-model approach of Coraggio (2009), which used a 40Ca core and the 1p0f model space, to include the neutron 0g9/2 orbital with a 48Ca core for studying heavy calcium isotopes. The calculations use no empirical modifications to the two-body matrix elements, unlike phenomenological interactions such as KB3G (Poves 2001), FPD6 (Richter 1991), GXPF1 (Honma 2002), and GXPF1A (Honma 2005). The results enable predictions for unmeasured states in 53-56Ca and clarify that the g9/2 orbital becomes important for nuclei beyond 54Ca, motivating future experimental studies of these neutron-rich isotopes.
