# Relativistic BCS-BEC Crossover at Zero Temperature

> We investigate the BCS-BEC crossover at zero temperature in the frame of a relativistic model. The universality of the BCS-BEC crossover for non-relativistic systems breaks down in relativistic case and the crossover can be induced by changing the density. When the effective scattering length is much less than the fermion Compton wavelength, we recover the non-relativistic result if the gas is initially in non-relativistic state. At ultra-strong coupling where the scattering length is of the order of the Compton wavelength, a new BEC state appears. In this state the condensed bosons become nearly massless and anti-fermions are excited. The behavior of the Goldstone mode and the mixing between the amplitude and phase modes are significantly different in different condensed regions.

## Key claim

The universality of BCS-BEC crossover that holds for non-relativistic systems breaks down in the relativistic case because the fermion mass introduces a new length scale (the Compton wavelength). A new relativistic BEC (RBEC) state appears at ultra-strong coupling where the scattering length is of order the Compton wavelength, and this RBEC can occur even in systems initially in a non-relativistic state if the attractive coupling is strong enough.

## Method

The authors extend Leggett's mean-field theory of BCS-BEC crossover at zero temperature to a relativistic Dirac fermion model with a zero-range attractive interaction in the scalar channel. They derive and solve the relativistic gap equation and number equation self-consistently for the pairing gap and chemical potential, including both fermion and anti-fermion degrees of freedom. The model is regularized by subtracting the vacuum contribution and defining a renormalized coupling related to the s-wave scattering length. Collective modes are studied via Gaussian fluctuations around the saddle point.

## Key numbers

* The critical coupling for the RBEC state is given by \(\eta_c \simeq 2\zeta^{-1}\) for cutoff \(\Lambda/m=10\), where \(\eta = 1/(k_f a_s)\) and \(\zeta = k_f/m\).
* The critical coupling satisfies \(\eta_c = (2/\pi)\zeta^{-1} f(\Lambda/m)\) with \(f(x) = \ln(x+\sqrt{x^2+1})\).
* The critical renormalized coupling satisfies \(U_c^{-1} = (2/\pi)U_0^{-1} f(\Lambda/m)\) with \(U_0 = 4\pi/m^2\).
* In the RBEC region with \(\mu \rightarrow 0\), the Goldstone mode velocity is \(c = 1\).
* In the non-relativistic BCS limit, the Goldstone mode velocity is \(c = \zeta/\sqrt{3}\).
* In the ultra-relativistic BCS state (\(k_f \gg m\)), the Goldstone mode velocity is \(c = 1/\sqrt{3}\).
* In the non-relativistic BEC region, the Goldstone mode velocity is \(c = \zeta/\sqrt{3\pi\eta}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of BCS-BEC crossover from non-relativistic systems (Eagles, Leggett) to relativistic Fermi systems, building on earlier finite-temperature work using the Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink theory (RBEC1, RBEC2) and Bose-Fermi models (RBEC3). The paper shows that the RBEC state previously found at finite temperature also exists at zero temperature and can appear in any fermion system with sufficiently strong coupling, not just in relativistic systems. The results enable further study of dense QCD matter at finite isospin density (ISO1, ISO2, ISO3, ISO4) and two-color QCD at finite baryon density, where the quark mass decreases with density due to chiral symmetry restoration.
