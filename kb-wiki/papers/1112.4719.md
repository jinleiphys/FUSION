# Gluon matter plasma in the compact star core within fluid QCD model

> The structure of compact star core filled by gluon matter plasma is investigated within the fluid-like QCD framework. The energy momentum tensor, density and pressure relevant for gluonic plasma having the nature of fluid bulk of gluon sea are derived within the model. It is shown that the model provides a new equation of state for perfect fluid with only a single parameter of fluid distribution ϕ(x). The results are applied to construct the equation of state describing the gluonic plasma dominated compact star core. The equations of pressure and density distribution are solved analytically for small compact star core radius. The phase transition of plasma near the core surface is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a fluid-like QCD model can describe gluon matter plasma in compact star cores, yielding a new equation of state for a perfect fluid with a single parameter of fluid distribution \(\phi(x)\), but only under the condition that the core radius is small (\(r^\prime \ll 1\)) and the system is spherically symmetric and isotropic.

## Method

The authors start from a QCD Lagrangian with SU(3) color gauge symmetry and impose a fluid-like ansatz for the gluon field, \(U_\mu^a = u_\mu^a \phi(x)\), where \(u_\mu^a\) is a relativistic 4-velocity and \(\phi(x)\) is a scalar field distribution. They derive the energy-momentum tensor for the gluonic plasma, then specialize to a static, spherically symmetric Schwarzschild geometry to obtain expressions for density and pressure. The resulting equations are solved analytically via a series expansion near the origin for small normalized core radius, using a particular exponential form for \(\phi(r)\).

## Key numbers

- The phase transition from QGP to hadronic matter is predicted to occur at approximately 175 MeV, cited from lattice QCD calculations (Gottlieb, Petreczky).
- The inner core temperature \(T_0\) is taken as 1 GeV, and the core surface temperature \(T_s\) as 175 MeV, used in the density distribution calculations.
- The density distribution is computed up to 7th order in the series expansion for small normalized core radius \(r^\prime \ll 1\), with the 7th order solution showing the phase transition.
- The distribution function is taken as \(\phi(r) \sim \exp(a r)\) with \(a = 0.4\).
- The normalized star radius used in the temperature-dependent density plot is \(r^\prime = 0.01\).

## Context

This work extends the fluid QCD model developed by Sulaiman et al. (2011) and Djun et al., which treats QGP as a strongly interacting gluon sea. It applies the model to compact star interiors, contrasting with pure hydrodynamic approaches (e.g., Romatschke, Bouras) or pure QCD lattice calculations (Gottlieb, Petreczky). The results enable analytical study of the equation of state and phase transition in gluonic plasma-dominated compact star cores, particularly near the core surface where hadronization occurs.
