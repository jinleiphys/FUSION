# Probing the substructure of $f_0(1370)$

> Within an effective nonlinear chiral Lagrangian framework the substructure of $f_0(1370)$ is studied. The investigation is conducted in the context of a global picture of scalar mesons in which the importance of the underlying connections among scalar mesons below and above 1 GeV is recognized and implemented. These connections are due to the mixings among various quark-antiquarks, four-quarks and glue components and play a central role in understanding the properties of scalar mesons. Iterative Monte Carlo simulations are first performed on the 14-dimensional parameter space of the model and sets of points in this parameter space (the global sets) that give an overall agreement with all experimental data on mass spectrum, various decay widths and decay ratios of all isosinglet scalar states below 2 GeV are determined. Then within each global set, subsets that give closest agreement for the properties of $f_0(1370)$ are studied. Unlike the properties of other isosinglet states that show a range of variation within each global set, it is found that there is a clear signal for $f_0(1370)$ to be predominantly a quark-antiquark state with a substantial $s {\bar s}$ component, together with small remnants of four-quark and glue components.

## Key claim

Within an effective nonlinear chiral Lagrangian framework, the substructure of f0(1370) is found to be predominantly a quark-antiquark state with a substantial s sbar component, together with small remnants of four-quark and glue components. This result is conditional on the specific global fits performed: unlike other isosinglet scalar states below 2 GeV, f0(1370) exhibits the most stable substructure across the Monte Carlo simulations, but the conclusion depends on the particular set of experimental inputs used in the global fits.

## Method

The authors employed an iterative Monte Carlo method to perform a correlated analysis of the 14-dimensional parameter space of a nonlinear chiral Lagrangian that includes two scalar meson nonets (a four-quark nonet below 1 GeV and a quark-antiquark nonet above 1 GeV) plus a scalar glueball field. They first determined global sets of parameter points that simultaneously agree with all experimental data on masses, decay widths, and decay ratios of all five isosinglet scalar states below 2 GeV [f0(500), f0(980), f0(1370), f0(1500), f0(1710)], then within each global set identified subsets that give closest agreement specifically for the properties of f0(1370). Three different global fits were performed: fit I excluded two partial decay widths of f0(1370), fit II included all 23 experimental quantities, and fit III excluded decay ratios from the WA102 collaboration.

## Key numbers

- The mass of f0(1370) used as input: 1312 MeV (from WA102 collaboration) in fits I and II, and 1300 ± 15 MeV (from Bugg 2007) in fit III.
- The ratio Γ[f0(1370)→ππ] / Γ[f0(1370)→K Kbar] = 2.17 ± 0.9 (WA102 collaboration).
- The ratio Γ[f0(1370)→ηη] / Γ[f0(1370)→K Kbar] = 0.35 ± 0.30 (WA102 collaboration).
- The partial decay width Γ[f0(1370)→ππ] = (0.26 ± 0.09) × (230 ± 15) MeV (Bugg 2007).
- The partial decay width Γ[f0(1370)→K Kbar] = (0.35 ± 0.13) × (230 ± 15) MeV (Bugg 2007).
- The mass of f0(1500) used as input: 1502 MeV (WA102) in fits I and II, and 1505 ± 6 MeV (PDG) in fit III.
- The mass of f0(1710) used as input: 1727 MeV (WA102) in fits I and II, and 1690 ± 20 MeV (Oller) in fit III.
- The pion decay constant Fπ = 0.131 GeV is used in the Lagrangian.

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Fariborz and collaborators (2004, 2006) that performed uncorrelated analyses of the isosinglet scalar mass spectrum and decays, and generalizes the mixing framework developed for I=1/2 and I=1 scalar mesons in Mec (2004) to include the I=0 sector with glueball mixing. The results support earlier findings from Fariborz (2004, 2006) that f0(1370) is dominantly a quark-antiquark (mainly s sbar) state, and are consistent with the broader picture from the generalized linear sigma model (Fariborz and collaborators, 2005-2009) that connects scalar mesons below and above 1 GeV through two- and four-quark mixing. The work enables future refinement of the substructure determination for the other isosinglet states, which show more variation across the global fits.
