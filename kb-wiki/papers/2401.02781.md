# Simultaneous Determination of Fragmentation Functions and Test on Momentum Sum Rule

> We perform a simultaneous global analysis of hadron fragmentation functions (FFs) to various charged hadrons at next-to-leading order in QCD. The world data set includes results from electron-positron single-inclusive annihilation, semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering, as well as proton-proton collisions including jet fragmentation measurements which lead to strong constraints on the gluon fragmentations. By carefully selecting hadron kinematics to ensure the validity of QCD factorization and the convergence of perturbative calculations, we achieve a satisfying best fit with $Žć^2/$d.o.f.$=0.90$, in the simultaneous extraction of FFs for light charged hadrons ($ŽĆ^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$ and $p/\bar{p}$). The total momentum of $u$, $d$ quarks and gluon carried by light charged hadrons have been determined precisely. That urges future precision measurements on fragmentation to neutral hadrons, which are crucial for the test of fundamental sum rules in QCD fragmentation.

## Key claim

## Method

The authors perform a global QCD analysis at next-to-leading order using data from electron-positron single-inclusive annihilation (SIA), semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS), and proton-proton collisions including jet fragmentation measurements. They parametrize FFs at an initial scale QŌéĆ = 5 GeV using a flexible functional form with 63 free parameters for ŽĆŌü║, KŌü║, and p together, evolve them using two-loop timelike splitting kernels, and compute theoretical cross sections with the FMNLO program using CT14 NLO parton distribution functions. Kinematic selection criteria are applied to ensure the validity of leading-twist collinear factorization, including z > 0.01 and hadron transverse momentum or energy above 4 GeV for most datasets.

## Context

This work extends previous FF determinations such as DSS (deFlorian 2007), HKNS (Hirai 2007), AKK (Albino 2008), NNFF (Bertone 2018), MAPFF (Khalek 2021), and JAM (Moffat 2021) by including jet fragmentation measurements from LHC experiments (ATLAS, CMS, LHCb) for the first time, which provide strong constraints on gluon fragmentation. The analysis enables tests of fundamental QCD sum rules, particularly the momentum sum rule, and the authors note that the total momentum carried by strange quarks appears anomalously large compared to up and down quarks, potentially inconsistent with the sum rule. Future precision measurements of neutral hadron fragmentation are needed to fully test the momentum sum rule, which is relevant for programs at upcoming electron-ion colliders.
