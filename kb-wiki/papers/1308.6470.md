# Nuclear stopping for heavy-ion induced reactions in the Fermi Energy range : from 1-Body to 2-Body dissipation

> We address the stopping in heavy-ion induced reactions around the Fermi energy using central collisions recorded with \emph{INDRA} $4π$ array. The stopping is minimal around the Fermi energy and corresponds to the disappearance of the Mean-Field effects and the appearance of nucleon-nucleon elastic collisions. This phenomenon is attributed to a change in the dissipation regime going from 1-body (Mean-Field) dissipation at low incident energy to 2- body (nucleonic collisions) above the Fermi energy. A connection to the in-medium transport properties of nuclear matter is proposed and comprehensive values of the nucleon mean free path $λ_{NN}$ and the nucleon-nucleon cross section are extracted.

# Nuclear stopping for heavy-ion induced reactions in the Fermi Energy range: from 1-Body to 2-Body dissipation

## Key claim
The stopping in heavy-ion induced reactions is minimal around the Fermi energy (approximately 30A MeV), marking the transition from 1-body (Mean-Field) dissipation at lower energies to 2-body (nucleon-nucleon elastic collision) dissipation above the Fermi energy. This minimal stopping corresponds to the disappearance of Mean-Field effects and the appearance of nucleonic collisions, with the degree of stopping connected to the in-medium nucleon mean free path and nucleon-nucleon cross section.

## Method
Central collisions of symmetric heavy-ion systems with total masses between 70 and 500 were selected from INDRA 4π array data using total charged-particle multiplicity, covering incident energies from 10A to 100A MeV. The energy isotropy ratio R_E (ratio of transverse to parallel kinetic energy in the center-of-mass frame) was used to quantify stopping, and a normalized distance d from the entrance channel (sudden approximation) was computed. Simple Monte Carlo Glauber simulations of two Fermi spheres were performed to relate the normalized distance d to the in-medium nucleon mean free path λ_NN, from which the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section and quenching factor F were extracted using kinetic theory and Pauli blocking corrections.

## Key numbers
- Incident energy range studied: E_inc/A = 10–100 MeV
- Minimal stopping observed at E_inc/A = 30 MeV (slightly below the Fermi energy)
- Fermi momentum at saturation density: P_Fermi = 266 MeV/c
- Nuclear radius parameter: r_0 = 1.2 fm
- Saturation density: ρ = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Nucleon mean free path λ_NN decreases from approximately 8 fm at E_inc/A = 30 MeV to 5 fm at E_inc/A = 100 MeV
- Quenching factor F ≈ 20% at low energy (near 30A MeV) and F ≈ 80% at the highest incident energy (100A MeV)
- All systems collapse to the same reduced isotropy R_E' = R_E/L at incident energies above 100A MeV

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Lehaut (2010) on stopping in central collisions using the INDRA dataset, providing a systematic extraction of in-medium transport properties across symmetric systems. The extracted nucleon mean free path values agree with Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations by Rios (2012) using realistic interactions. The quenching factor results are compared to theoretical prescriptions from Klakow (1993), Li and Machleidt (1993), Schnell (1998), Xiangzhou (1998), and Coupland (2011), with the Coupland (2011) parametrization (based on Danielewicz's phenomenological assumption) giving the best description of the experimental data. This analysis enables future constraints on in-medium transport properties for microscopic models of heavy-ion collisions and nuclear matter, with implications for supernova collapse and neutron star formation.
