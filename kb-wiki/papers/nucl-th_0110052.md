# Analytic proof of the Weinberg theorem for off mass shell and composite pions

> The Weinberg theorem for the pion-pion scattering is proved analytically in full detail for quark models in the ladder truncation. The proof is displayed with Feynman diagrams. The axial and vector Ward identities, for the quark propagator and for the ladder, exactly cancel any model dependence. Off mass shell and finite size effects are included in the quark-antiquark pion Bethe Salpeter vertices. This is applied to compute the isospin 0, 1 and 2 scattering matrices off the mass shell .

## Key claim

The Weinberg theorem for pion-pion scattering is proven analytically for quark models in the ladder truncation, with the proof extending to off mass shell pions and composite pions with finite size, provided the quark-quark interaction is chirally invariant and the kernel is local.

## Method

The proof uses axial and vector Ward identities for the quark propagator and the ladder truncation to cancel model-dependent terms in the Feynman diagram expansion. The dressed axial vertex Gamma_A is related to the pion Bethe-Salpeter vertex chi, and the loop diagrams for pi-pi scattering are expanded up to order M_pi^2 and P_i P_j in the pion mass and momenta. The analysis computes the Feynman loop with three diagrams (a vertical scalar ladder, a horizontal scalar ladder, and a subtracted empty box) and shows that the finite term of order 1 exactly cancels in the chiral limit, leaving only terms proportional to the current quark mass m and higher-order momentum terms.

## Key numbers

* The offshellness of a virtual pion pi* in pi production at threshold is P^2 - M_pi^2 = -3.32 M_pi^2
* The total box amplitude vanishes in the chiral limit m=0, with the result proportional to 8 i m tr{S} + o(m^2, P_i^4, m P_i^2)
* The proof is valid up to order M_pi^2 and P_i P_j in the pion mass and momenta

## Context

This work extends the original Weinberg theorem (Weinberg, 1966) for pi-pi scattering to quark models with composite pions, including off mass shell effects crucial for experimental extraction from pi-N scattering. The proof generalizes earlier derivations using Ward identities for pion fields (Leutwyler) and functional integration of quarks (Roberts) by incorporating finite-size pions from quark models. The result applies to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Nambu, Jona-Lasinio), conventional quark models with confining instantaneous interactions, Euclidean space models, and covariant models in Minkowski space, enabling microscopic calculations of pi-pi scattering amplitudes with off mass shell pions.
