# Strange mesons in nuclei and neutron stars

> The present status in the field of strange mesons in nuclei and neutron stars is reviewed. In particular, the $\bar K N$ interaction, that is governed by the presence of the $Λ(1405)$, is analyzed and the formation of the $\bar K NN$ bound state is discussed. Moreover, the properties of $\bar K$ in dense nuclear matter are studied, in connection with strangeness production in nuclear collisions and kaon condensation in neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the current status of strange meson research, noting that while the antikaon-nucleon interaction is governed by the Lambda(1405) resonance and may produce bound states, the existence and properties of the KbarNN bound state remain uncertain, with binding energies ranging from 9 to 95 MeV and widths from 16 to 110 MeV depending on the theoretical approach used.

## Method

The paper surveys coupled-channel unitarized theories within meson-exchange models and meson-baryon chiral effective theories (chiEFT) for the KbarN interaction. It compares variational, three-body Faddeev, and coupled-channel Complex Scaling Method (ccCSM) calculations for the KbarNN bound state, and reviews transport model simulations (HSD and PHSD) for antikaon production in heavy-ion collisions. The review also examines relativistic mean-field models and chiEFT approaches for kaon condensation in neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies of KbarNN range from 9 to 95 MeV across different theoretical calculations
- Decay widths of KbarNN range from 16 to 110 MeV across different theoretical calculations
- Chiral interaction calculations give binding energies of 9-50 MeV (Barnea et al. 2012: 16 MeV; Dote et al. 2009: 17-23 MeV; Ikeda et al. 2010: 9-16 MeV)
- Phenomenological potential calculations give binding energies of 40-95 MeV (Yamazaki et al. 2002: 48 MeV; Shevchenko et al. 2007: 50-70 MeV; Ikeda et al. 2007: 60-95 MeV)
- Heavy-ion collision studies focus on beam energies below 2 AGeV for antikaon production

## Context

This review extends the work of Tolos (2020) on strange mesons and updates the status of theoretical and experimental efforts. It contrasts chiral effective theory approaches (Ramos 1999, Tolos 2006, Cabrera 2014) which find less attractive antikaon potentials, with phenomenological models (Schaffner 1996, Tsushima 1997) that predict stronger attraction and support kaon condensation in neutron stars. The review enables future comparisons between transport model predictions and experimental data from KaoS, FOPI, and HADES Collaborations, as well as continued debate on kaon condensation in neutron star interiors.
