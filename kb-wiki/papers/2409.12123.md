# Far-from-equilibrium attractors with Full Relativistic Boltzmann approach in 3+1 D: moments of distribution function and anisotropic flows $v_n$

> We employ the Full Relativistic Boltzmann Transport approach for a conformal system in 3+1D to study the universal behaviour in moments of the distribution function and anisotropic flows. We investigate different transverse system sizes $R$ and interaction strength $η/s$ and identify universality classes based upon the interplay between $R$ and the mean free path; we show that each of this classes can be identified by a particular value of the opacity $\hat γ$, which has been previously introduced in literature. Our results highlight that, at early times, the inverse Reynolds number and momentum moments of the distribution function display universal behaviour, converging to a 1D attractor driven by longitudinal expansion. This indicates that systems of different sizes and interaction strengths tend to approach equilibrium in a similar manner. We provide a detailed analysis of how the onset of transverse flow affects these moments at later times. Moreover, we investigate the system size and $η/s$ dependence for the harmonic flows $v_2$, $v_3$, $v_4$ and their response functions, along with the impact of the $η/s$ and the system transverse size on the dissipation of initial azimuthal correlations in momentum space. Finally, we introduce the normalised elliptic flow $v_2/v_{2,eq}$, showing the emergence of attractor behaviour in the regime of large opacity. These results offer new insights into how different systems evolve towards equilibrium and the role that system size and interaction play in this process.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, for a conformal system simulated with the Full Relativistic Boltzmann Transport approach in 3+1D, the inverse Reynolds number and momentum moments of the distribution function exhibit universal attractor behavior at early times, driven by longitudinal expansion, but this universality is conditional on the system's opacity \(\hat \gamma\), a parameter that encodes the interplay between transverse system size \(R\) and mean free path, and the attractor is eventually broken at later times by the onset of transverse flow.

## Method

The authors solve the Full Relativistic Boltzmann Transport equation for a massless, conformal one-component system using a test-particle method with a stochastic collision integral for \(2\leftrightarrow 2\) isotropic scattering. They initialize the system at proper time \(\tau_0 = 0.1\) fm with a Gaussian transverse density profile of radius \(R\) and a Romatschke-Strickland momentum distribution, then extract local thermodynamic quantities via Landau matching and compute the inverse Reynolds number, integrated momentum moments \(\mathcal{M}^{nm}\), and anisotropic flow coefficients \(v_n\) for \(n=2,3,4\) as functions of time.

## Key numbers

* Opacity \(\hat \gamma\) values studied: 1.13, 3.56, 7.12, and 12.8, each realized for three transverse radii \(R = 1.0, 2.5, 5.5\) fm and corresponding \(\eta/s\) values (e.g., for \(\hat \gamma = 7.12\): \(4\pi\eta/s = 0.503\) at \(R=1.0\) fm, 1.00 at \(R=2.5\) fm, 1.81 at \(R=5.5\) fm).
* At \(t \approx R\), the average transverse flow \(\langle \beta_\perp \rangle \approx 0.5\) for all systems studied; at \(t \sim 2R\), \(\langle \beta_\perp \rangle \approx 0.8\).
* At \(t \sim 2R\), both simulations with \(R=2.5\) fm and \(R=5.5\) fm (for \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\)) have performed approximately 80% of their total number of collisions.
* Initial temperature \(T_0 = 0.5\) GeV and initial proper time \(\tau_0 = 0.1\) fm are fixed throughout.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous 0+1D and 1+1D attractor studies (Nugara 2023) to a full 3+1D geometry, and builds on earlier effective kinetic theory results in RTA and ITA (Kurkela 2018, 2019; Ambrus 2021, 2022) that identified the opacity \(\hat \gamma\) as a universal scaling parameter. By using a full Boltzmann solver rather than a relaxation-time approximation, the paper provides a more microscopic validation of attractor behavior in moments of the distribution function and anisotropic flows, and it enables future studies of how initial momentum anisotropies (e.g., from the Color Glass Condensate model) affect thermalization in small collision systems like \(pp\) and \(pA\).
