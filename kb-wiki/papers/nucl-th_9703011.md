# Rho-meson mass in light nuclei

> The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model is applied to a study of the mass of the $ρ$-meson in helium and carbon nuclei. The average mass of a $ρ$-meson formed in $^{3,4}$He and $^{12}$C is expected to be around 730, 690 and 720 MeV, respectively.

## Key claim

The average mass of a ρ-meson formed in ³He, ⁴He, and ¹²C is expected to be around 730, 690, and 720 MeV respectively, representing a reduction of about 40–80 MeV due to the nuclear medium effect, though the exact value depends on the parameter set chosen for the in-medium σ-meson mass.

## Method

The authors applied the quark-meson coupling (QMC-II) model, in which quarks in non-overlapping MIT bags interact self-consistently with scalar (σ) and vector (ω, ρ) mesons in the mean-field approximation. For ¹²C, the coupled differential equations from the QMC-II Lagrangian were solved self-consistently; for ³He and ⁴He, a local-density approximation was used with Gaussian and modified Gaussian density distributions fitted to the known rms radii. The in-medium ρ-meson mass was then computed from the mean-field σ field at each position using a simple analytic expression.

## Key numbers

- Free ρ-meson mass: 770 MeV; free ω-meson mass: 783 MeV; free nucleon mass: 939 MeV; bag radius: 0.8 fm; quark mass: 5 MeV; bag constant B^(1/4) = 170.0 MeV.
- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³; binding energy at saturation: −15.7 MeV; bulk symmetry energy: 35 MeV.
- Average ρ-meson mass in ³He: ~730 MeV; in ⁴He: ~690 MeV; in ¹²C: ~720 MeV.
- Reduction of ρ-meson mass at the center of the nucleus: about 10–15%.
- σ-meson mass reduction at saturation density: about 2%, 7%, and 10% for parameter sets A, B, and C respectively.
- rms charge radius of ³He: 1.88 fm; rms matter radius of ⁴He: 1.56 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QMC model calculations of hadron masses in medium-mass and heavy nuclei (Saito et al., 1996) to the light nuclei ³He, ⁴He, and ¹²C, motivated by ongoing experiments at INS using tagged photon beams and the TAGX spectrometer to measure ρ⁰ decay into π⁺π⁻. The results are intended for comparison with those experimental data, and the authors note that Asakawa and Ko (1995) have studied the ρ-meson width in nuclear matter using QCD sum rules, finding that the width decreases only slightly with density.
