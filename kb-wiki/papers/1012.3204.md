# Magnetic Tuning of the Relativistic BCS-BEC Crossover

> The effect of an applied magnetic field in the crossover from Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) to Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) pairing regimes is investigated. We use a model of relativistic fermions and bosons inspired by those previously used in the context of cold fermionic atoms and in the magnetic-color-flavor-locking phase of color superconductivity. It turns out that as with cold atom systems, an applied magnetic field can also tune the BCS-BEC crossover in the relativistic case. We find that no matter what the initial state is at B=0, for large enough magnetic fields the system always settles into a pure BCS regime. In contrast to the atomic case, the magnetic field tuning of the crossover in the relativistic system is not connected to a Feshbach resonance, but to the relative numbers of Landau levels with either BEC or BCS type of dispersion relations that are occupied at each magnetic field strength.

## Key claim
The paper claims that an applied magnetic field can tune the BCS-BEC crossover in a relativistic fermion-boson model, but the mechanism is not a Feshbach resonance as in cold atoms; instead, it depends on the relative numbers of occupied Landau levels that have either BCS-type or BEC-type dispersion relations, and for sufficiently large fields the system always ends up in a pure BCS regime regardless of its zero-field state.

## Method
The authors use a two-channel relativistic model with charged fermions and neutral scalar bosons coupled via a Yukawa interaction, minimally coupled to a uniform external magnetic field. They derive the zero-temperature effective potential, gap equation, and chemical equilibrium condition at fixed total baryon number density, then solve these equations numerically for the gap and chemical potential as functions of the renormalized boson mass and magnetic field strength. The crossover is characterized by the fermion and boson number fractions and by the parameter \(x = -(\widetilde{m}_b^2 - 4\mu^2)/(2g^2)\), with a Gaussian regulator cutoff \(\Lambda = 1\) GeV.

## Key numbers
- At zero magnetic field, for \(m_b = 0.8\) GeV (\(\widetilde{m}_b = 0.692\) GeV), the gap \(\Delta_0 = 0.073\) GeV and chemical potential \(\mu_0 = 0.357\) GeV.
- At zero magnetic field, for \(m_b = 1.2\) GeV (\(\widetilde{m}_b = 1.131\) GeV), the gap \(\Delta_0 = 0.029\) GeV and chemical potential \(\mu_0 = 0.555\) GeV.
- The crossover point shifts to a lower \(m_b\) when the magnetic field is large enough (\(\gtrsim 10^{19}\) G), favoring the BCS side over a much larger range of \(m_b\) values.
- The system reaches a pure BCS regime when the magnetic field is strong enough that \(|qB| \geq \mu^2\), putting all fermions in the lowest Landau level.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the BCS-BEC crossover in relativistic systems, such as those by Sun et al. (2008) and the nonrelativistic treatment by Chen et al. (2005), by introducing an external magnetic field. It is motivated by the quark-hadron continuity conjecture of Schafer and Wilczek (1999) and the magnetic-color-flavor-locking phase of color superconductivity. The results enable future investigations of the crossover in more realistic single-channel models (e.g., Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models with diquark and chiral condensates) and in applications to neutron star cores and low-energy heavy-ion collision experiments at facilities such as FAIR and NICA.
