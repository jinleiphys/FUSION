# Alpha-like clustering in $^{20}$Ne from a quartetting wave function approach

> Quartetting ($α$-like clustering) occurs in low density matter ($\le 0.03$ fm$^{-3}$) which exists, e.g., at the surface of nuclei. It is of interest for the $α$ preformation to calculate the $α$ decay of heavy nuclei such as $^{212}$Po, but also in light nuclei (e.g., $^{20}$Ne) which shows strong signatures of quartetting. We analyze the intrinsic structure of the $α$-like cluster and the center of mass motion of the quartet, in particular the role of Pauli blocking. The Thomas-Fermi model for the (daughter) core nucleus is improved introducing quasiparticle nucleon states. Calculations performed for harmonic oscillator basis states show that the effective potential for the quartet center of mass motion remains nearly constant within the core nucleus. The relation to the THSR (Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke) approach is discussed.

## Key claim
The effective potential for the center-of-mass motion of an alpha-like quartet remains nearly constant inside the core nucleus, a result derived from calculations using harmonic oscillator basis states. This flat potential is a consequence of the Thomas-Fermi model, but the paper shows it is approximately reproduced by shell model calculations, though the sharp kink at the critical radius is an artifact of the local density approximation.

## Method
The authors use a quartetting wave function approach, decomposing the four-nucleon wave function into an intrinsic part and a center-of-mass (c.o.m.) part. The intrinsic wave function is treated as an alpha-like cluster for densities below the Mott density (0.02917 fm$^{-3}$) and as four uncorrelated quasiparticles above it. The c.o.m. motion is described by a Schrödinger equation with an effective potential $W(R)$ that includes a mean-field contribution from the core nucleus (via double folding with a M3Y-type interaction) and a Pauli blocking term. The core nucleus density is modeled using a Thomas-Fermi approximation, empirical Fermi functions, and a harmonic oscillator shell model for $^{16}$O and a large $A=80$ system to compare the two approaches.

## Key numbers
- The Mott density for alpha particle dissolution is $n_B^{\rm Mott} = 0.02917$ fm$^{-3}$.
- For $^{20}$Ne ($^{16}$O + $\alpha$), the critical radius where the alpha cluster dissolves is $r_{\rm crit} = 3.302$ fm using a Woods-Saxon density, and $r_{\rm crit}^{\rm h.o.} = 3.3156$ fm using a harmonic oscillator model.
- The binding energy of the free alpha particle is $B_\alpha = 28.3$ MeV.
- The effective potential for the quartet inside the core is set to $\mu_4 = -33.0$ MeV, derived from the difference in binding energies between $^{20}$Ne (160.645 MeV) and $^{16}$O (127.66 MeV).
- For the $^{208}$Pb core, the critical radius is $r_{\rm crit} = 7.4383$ fm.

## Context
This work extends the quartetting wave function approach previously applied to alpha decay in heavy nuclei like $^{212}$Po (Röpke et al., 2014; Xu et al., 2016) to the light nucleus $^{20}$Ne, where both the quartetting approach and the Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke (THSR) container model (Tohsaki et al., 2001) can be compared. It improves upon the earlier Thomas-Fermi model for the core by introducing quasiparticle nucleon states from a harmonic oscillator shell model, addressing the inconsistency of a purely local density approximation. This enables a more consistent microscopic description of alpha clustering in both light and heavy nuclei, and provides a pathway to incorporate quartetting for core nucleons in future work.
