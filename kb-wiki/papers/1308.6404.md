# Novel Six-Quark Hidden-Color Dibaryon States in QCD

> The recent observation of a hadronic resonance $d^*$ in the proton-neutron system with isospin $I = 0$ and spin-parity $J^P = 3^+$ raises the possibility of producing other novel six-quark dibaryon configurations allowed by QCD. A dramatic example of an exotic six-quark color-singlet system is the charge $Q=+4$, isospin I=3, $I^z=+3$ $|uuuuuu>$ state which couples strongly to $ﾎ膿{++}$ + $ﾎ膿{++} .$ The width and decay properties of such six-quark resonances could be regarded as manifestations of "hidden-color" six-quark configurations, a first-principle prediction of QCD -- SU(3)-color gauge theory for the deuteron distribution amplitude. Other implications and possible future experiments are discussed.

# Novel Six-Quark Hidden-Color Dibaryon States in QCD

## Key claim
The recently observed $d^*$ resonance in the proton-neutron system with $I(J^P) = 0(3^+)$ may be a genuine six-quark "hidden-color" state rather than a conventional $\Delta\Delta$ bound state (deltaron), based on its narrow width of 70 MeV being incompatible with the expected 160 MeV width for a deltaron with 90 MeV binding energy.

## Method
The authors review existing experimental data on the $d^*$ resonance observed in $pn$ collisions leading to two-pion production channels ($pn \to d\pi^0\pi^0$, $pn \to d\pi^+\pi^-$, $pn \to pp\pi^-\pi^0$) and analyze its decay properties via Dalitz plots. They apply the quark-model wavefunction decomposition of Harvey, which gives two possible configurations for an $I(J^P) = 0(3^+)$ state: one dominated by $\Delta\Delta$ and one dominated by hidden-color six-quark ($6Q$) components, and compare the predicted widths to the observed 70 MeV width to distinguish between these interpretations.

## Key numbers
- $d^*$ resonance mass: $M = 2.37$ GeV
- $d^*$ resonance width: $\Gamma = 70$ MeV
- $d^*$ mass is approximately 90 MeV below the nominal $2m_{\Delta}$ mass of a $\Delta\Delta$ system
- Expected width for a deltaron with 90 MeV binding energy: $\Gamma_{\Delta\Delta} = 160$ MeV (reduced from 230 MeV for free $\Delta\Delta$)
- Branching ratio $BR(d^* \to \Delta\Delta) / BR(d^* \to pn) = 9:1$
- Predicted mass for the $|uuuuuu>$ state: approximately 2.4 GeV
- Required proton beam energy for $D_{30}$ production: $T_p = 1.7-2$ GeV
- Branching ratio $BR(\Upsilon \to \bar{d} + X) = 2.86 \times 10^{-5}$

## Context
This work extends the original proposal of six-quark dibaryon states by Dyson and Xuong (1964) and the hidden-color formalism developed by Brodsky and collaborators for the deuteron distribution amplitude. It directly follows the experimental observation of the $d^*$ resonance by Bashkanov et al. and the WASA-at-COSY collaboration. The paper discusses how the $d^*$ could be the "inevitable non-strange dibaryon" predicted by Goldman et al. and others, and notes that Gal and Garcilazo (2013) recently succeeded in predicting such a deeply bound $\Delta\Delta$ state in a relativistic three-body calculation. The work enables experimental searches for the charge $Q=+4$ $D_{30}$ state and strange dibaryon partners at facilities including MAMI, JLab, COSY, and JPARC, as well as in $\Upsilon$ decays at B-factories.
