# High Energy Hadron-Hadron Scattering

> The elastic hadron-hadron scattering at high energies is one of the most fundamental subjects of all particle physics problems and yet is least understood in spite of many advances in quantum chromodynamics (QCD) at the conceptual level. We review here the recent theoretical and experimental status of the subject as well as the rigorous results of the high energy hadron-hadron scattering. Surprisingly enough, the high-energy models for the elastic and diffractive scattering abstracted from the Regge-Pomeron field theory is still {\it phenomenologically} successful to explain the high-energy scattering data though no rigorous derivation out of the QCD is yet available.

## Key claim

The paper argues that phenomenological models based on Regge-Pomeron field theory remain successful in describing high-energy elastic and diffractive hadron-hadron scattering data, despite the fact that no rigorous derivation of these models from quantum chromodynamics (QCD) is yet available. The author emphasizes that the gap between phenomenological models and the microscopic gauge theory remains wide, and that matching the hard Pomeron from perturbative QCD to the soft Pomeron from phenomenological models is a central unsolved problem.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical and experimental status of high-energy elastic hadron-hadron scattering, focusing on forward scattering parameters (total cross section, ratio of real to imaginary part, and nuclear slope) for pp and pbar-p scattering. It summarizes the asymptotic amplitude analysis based on derivative dispersion relations (DDR) derived from the Sommerfeld-Watson-Regge representation, and compares this approach with eikonal formalism that guarantees s-channel unitarity. The author also discusses the experimental data from ISR, UA4/2, CDF, and E710 collaborations, and examines the role of the Pomeron and Odderon in describing the high-energy behavior.

## Context

This review extends the long-standing program of understanding high-energy hadron-hadron scattering through Regge theory and the Pomeron concept, which was originally introduced to explain the high-energy behavior of total cross sections. The paper discusses the tension between the CDF and E710 measurements of the total cross section at Tevatron energy, which complicates predictions for the LHC. It also addresses the relationship between the soft Pomeron from phenomenological models and the hard Pomeron from perturbative QCD (Lipatov, 1976), and notes that the new UA4/2 rho measurement has eliminated the need for an Odderon contribution in forward scattering, though the Odderon may still play a role in non-forward regions such as the diffractive minimum. The work enables future experimental tests at LHC and further theoretical efforts to match the phenomenological Regge approach with microscopic QCD.
