# Meson-Meson Scattering in Relativistic Constraint Dynamics

> Dirac's relativistic constraint dynamics have been successfully applied to obtain a covariant nonperturbative description of QED and QCD bound states. We use this formalism to describe a microscopic theory of meson-meson scattering as a relativistic generalization of the nonrelativistic quark-interchange model developed by Barnes and Swanson.

## Key claim

The paper presents a relativistic generalization of the nonrelativistic quark-interchange model for meson-meson scattering, using Dirac's constraint dynamics, but the formalism is a hybrid that combines two-body wave functions with a four-body potential that depends on the component of the relative coordinate transverse to the total four-momentum of the four-quark system, and the authors note that in the Born approximation the energy dependence of the relativistic effective potentials can be ignored.

## Method

The authors start from the two-body Dirac equations of constraint dynamics, which yield a covariant Schrödinger-like form for meson bound states, and combine them with Sazdjian's four-body formalism where interactions depend on the transverse component of the relative coordinate with respect to the total four-momentum of the four-body system. They construct relativistic orthogonality and completeness conditions for the four-quark states, postulate a hybrid overlap between initial and final meson states using the total four-momentum unit vector of the four-quark system, and derive a covariant three-dimensional expression for the meson-meson scattering amplitude that generalizes the nonrelativistic result of Barnes and Swanson.

## Key numbers

- The two-body Dirac equations have an exact solution for singlet positronium in lowest-order QED that agrees with standard perturbative results (no explicit numerical value given).
- The formalism yields "very good results for the entire meson spectrum from the light pion to the heavy upsilon states" (no explicit numerical values provided in the text).

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic quark-interchange model developed by Barnes and Swanson (1990s) and later supplemented by Wong, Barnes, and Swanson, to the relativistic domain using constraint dynamics previously applied to two-body bound states in QED and QCD by Crater and collaborators. The formalism is motivated by the need for a reliable relativistic meson-meson scattering description to rule out false signals for quark-gluon plasma formation, specifically the dissociative process π + J/ψ → D + D̅* that could mimic J/ψ suppression at high temperatures. The next step is to apply the relativistic quark model wave functions developed by the authors to compute meson-meson cross sections.
