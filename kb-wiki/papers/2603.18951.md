# Transverse spin effects and light-quark dipole moments at colliders

> In this talk, we present novel methods to investigate light-quark dipole interactions at colliders. Our approach includes: (1) measuring azimuthal asymmetries of a collinear dihadron in semi-inclusive deep inelastic lepton scattering off an unpolarized proton target at the Electron-Ion Collider, and (2) utilizing azimuthal asymmetries of dihadron $(h_1 h_2)$ produced in association with an additional hadron $h^\prime$ at lepton colliders. These asymmetries provide a unique means to observe transversely polarized quarks, which arise from quantum interference and are exclusively sensitive to dipole interactions at the leading power of the new physics scale. Consequently, they exhibit a linear dependence on the dipole couplings, free from contamination by other new physics effects. This approach has the potential to significantly strengthen current constraints by one to two orders of magnitude. By combining all possible channels of $h^\prime$, this novel approach enables the disentanglement of the up- and down-quark dipole moments. Additionally, by controlling the electron's longitudinal polarization and the center-of-mass energy, it separates the contributions mediated by photon and weak boson. Furthermore, it allows for a simultaneous determination of both real and imaginary parts of the dipole couplings, offering a new avenue for investigating potential $CP$-violating effects at high energies.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that azimuthal asymmetries from dihadron production in SIDIS at the EIC and in associated hadron production at lepton colliders can probe light-quark dipole moments with linear sensitivity at order 1/Lambda^2, free from contamination by other new physics effects, provided that the quark transverse spin arises from quantum interference between dipole and Standard Model amplitudes.

## Method

The authors analyze two processes: (1) semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering of an electron off an unpolarized proton target at the EIC, producing a collinear dihadron pair (pi+ pi-); and (2) associated production of a dihadron pair with an additional hadron h' (pi+, K+, p, or antiproton) in e+e- collisions. In both cases, they compute the azimuthal angle distribution of the hadron plane relative to the scattering plane, which encodes the transverse spin of the fragmenting quark. The transverse spin arises from interference between the Standard Model amplitudes and the dipole operator contributions, and is projected onto the dihadron azimuthal asymmetry through the interference dihadron fragmentation function H^{h1 h2}_q.

## Key numbers

- For the EIC SIDIS analysis at sqrt(s)=105 GeV with L=1000 fb^-1, projected 68% C.L. constraints reach typical bounds of O(10^-2) for Gamma_gamma^{u,d} and O(10^-1) for Gamma_Z^{u,d}.
- For the lepton collider analysis at sqrt(s)=10 GeV with polarized electron beam and L=1 ab^-1, typical bounds are O(10^-2) for Gamma_gamma^{u,d}.
- For the lepton collider analysis at sqrt(s)=91 GeV (Z-pole) with unpolarized beams and L=1 ab^-1, typical bounds are O(10^-3) for Gamma_Z^{u,d}.
- For the electron dipole moment benchmark at sqrt(s)=250 GeV with L=5 ab^-1, expected constraints are O(0.01%) for Gamma_Z^e and O(0.1%) for Gamma_gamma^e.
- The approach claims to strengthen current constraints by one to two orders of magnitude relative to prior work by Boughezal et al. (2021).

## Context

This work extends the transverse spin technique previously applied to lepton dipole moments at e+e- colliders by Wen et al. (2023) to the case of light quarks, where color confinement prevents direct measurement. It addresses the flat direction problem in the EIC-only analysis by adding lepton collider channels with different hadron species (pi+, K+, p) to disentangle up- and down-quark dipole contributions. The method complements existing constraints from Drell-Yan and Z-pole processes studied by Boughezal et al. (2021), and enables simultaneous determination of both real and imaginary parts of the dipole couplings for CP-violation studies.
