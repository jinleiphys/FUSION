# Low energy Kbar N interactions and Faddeev calculation of the K- d scattering length in isospin and particle bases

> $\Kbar N$ interactions are investigated {\it via} an effective non-linear chiral meson-baryon Lagrangian. The adjustable parameters are determined by a fitting procedure on the $K^-p$ threshold branching ratios and total cross-section data for $p^{lab}_K\le$ 250 MeV/c. We produce predictions for the $Σπ$ mass spectrum, and scattering lenghts $a_{K^-p}$, $a_n(K^-n \to K^-n)$, $a_n0(\Kbar0 n \to \Kbar0 n)$, and $a_{ex}(K^-p \to \Kbar0 n)$. The $\Kbar N$ amplitudes thus obtained, as well as those for other two-body channels ($πN$, $NN$, and $YN$) are used as input to predict the scattering length $A_{K^-d}$, for which we have devised a relativistic version of the three-body Faddeev equations. Results for all two- and three-body coupled channels are reported both in isospin and particle bases. All available $\Kbar N$ data are well reproduced and our best results for the $K^-p$ and $K^-d$ scattering lenghts are $a_{K^-p} = (-0.90 + i 0.87) fm$ and $A_{K^-d} = (-1.80 + i 1.55) fm$.

## Key claim

The paper presents a complete Faddeev calculation of the \(K^- d\) scattering length using \(\Kbar N\) amplitudes derived from a chiral Lagrangian, with the hedge that the best results for \(a_{K^-p}\) and \(A_{K^-d}\) are obtained only after fitting parameters to threshold branching ratios and cross-section data, and that the inclusion of \(\eta Y\) channels is necessary to reproduce the experimental values.

## Method

The authors constructed coupled \(s\)-wave separable \(\Kbar N\) interactions using an effective non-linear chiral meson-baryon Lagrangian, with parameters fitted to \(K^-p\) threshold branching ratios and total cross-section data for kaon laboratory momenta up to 250 MeV/c. They then used these amplitudes, along with \(\pi N\), \(NN\), and \(YN\) two-body inputs, in a relativistic version of the three-body Faddeev equations to predict the \(K^-d\) scattering length, performing calculations in both isospin and particle bases to evaluate isospin breaking effects.

## Key numbers

- \(a_{K^-p} = (-0.90 + i\,0.87)\) fm (best result from model OSA in particle basis)
- \(A_{K^-d} = (-1.80 + i\,1.55)\) fm (best result from model OSA in particle basis)
- Experimental \(K^-p\) scattering length (with Coulomb effects): \(a^c_{K^-p} = (-0.78 \pm 0.15 \pm 0.03) + i\,(0.49 \pm 0.25 \pm 0.12)\) fm from KEK experiment (Iwa97)
- Threshold branching ratio \(\gamma = \Gamma(K^-p \rightarrow \pi^+ \Sigma^-) / \Gamma(K^-p \rightarrow \pi^- \Sigma^+) = 2.36 \pm 0.04\) (experimental)
- Threshold branching ratio \(R_c = 0.664 \pm 0.011\) (experimental)
- Threshold branching ratio \(R_n = 0.189 \pm 0.015\) (experimental)
- Previous best multi-channel Faddeev result: \(A_{K^-d} = (-1.51 + i\,1.45)\) fm from Bah90

## Context

This work extends the chiral Lagrangian approach of Oset and Ramos (1998), which dynamically generated the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance, by incorporating those \(\Kbar N\) amplitudes into a full relativistic three-body Faddeev calculation for the \(K^-d\) system. It improves upon earlier multi-channel Faddeev calculations by Tok81, Tor86, and Bah90 by using chirally motivated two-body interactions and by performing the first calculation in the particle basis to quantify isospin breaking effects at the three-body level. The results enable future comparisons with upcoming \(K^-d\) atomic measurements from the DEAR project at DA\(\Phi\)NE, and provide a foundation for extracting the kaon-nucleon sigma term.
