# Exploring the possible two-proton radioactivity of $^{38,39}$Ti

> Two-proton (2$p$) radioactivity represents a rare decay mode that has been experimentally observed only in a selected few nuclei. The exploration of 2$p$ emission is crucial for elucidating the structure, mass, and nucleon-nucleon interactions within exotic proton-rich nuclei. $^{39}$Ti has long been postulated as a potential candidate for 2$p$ emission; however, experimental investigations have yet to confirm its 2$p$ decay. To provide more accurate information for further studies, we utilize the Gamow shell model (GSM) and the Gamow coupled channel (GCC) method to analyze the prospective 2$p$ radioactivity of isotopes $^{38,39}$Ti. Our calculations suggest that $^{39}$Ti is indeed a viable candidate for 2$p$ emission. Notably, the estimated partial 2$p$ decay width for $^{39}$Ti, predicted from the three-body GCC method, suggests that its 2$p$ decay could rival its $β$ decay in likelihood, although this is highly dependent on the specific 2$p$ decay energy. Additionally, our analysis indicates a propensity for pairing between the valence protons in $^{39}$Ti. A similar investigative approach reveals that $^{38}$Ti exhibits a higher 2$p$ decay energy and a broader decay width than $^{39}$Ti, positioning it as a more promising candidate for 2$p$ decay.

## Key claim

The paper claims that $^{39}$Ti is a viable candidate for two-proton (2$p$) emission, but the 2$p$ decay could rival beta decay in likelihood only under specific conditions of the 2$p$ decay energy; the estimated 2$p$ half-life limits of 0.4 to 10 ms at the calculated decay energy closely match previously measured half-lives of $^{39}$Ti, which may explain why 2$p$ decay has not been observed experimentally due to competition with beta decay.

## Method

The authors employed the Gamow shell model (GSM) with an effective field theory (EFT) inspired interaction, using a $^{36}$Ca core plus valence protons and neutrons in an $sdpf$ model space with mixed Berggren and harmonic oscillator bases. They also applied the Gamow coupled channel (GCC) method, a three-body model using Jacobi coordinates with a Woods-Saxon core-proton potential and Minnesota force for the proton-proton interaction, to calculate 2$p$ decay widths and half-lives. The GSM interaction parameters were optimized by fitting to 13 experimental binding energies of Ca, Sc, and Ti isotopes near $^{39}$Ti, achieving a root mean square deviation of approximately 712 keV.

## Key numbers

- The two-proton separation energy ($S_{2p}$) for $^{39}$Ti is $-0.45$ MeV, with an uncertainty of 0.3 MeV, and the single-proton separation energy ($S_p$) is $0.15$ MeV, with an uncertainty of 0.31 MeV.
- The ground state energy of $^{39}$Ti is $-14.31$ MeV relative to the core, and the ground state energy of $^{37}$Ca is $-14.76$ MeV.
- The estimated 2$p$ half-life limits for $^{39}$Ti at the calculated decay energy range between 0.4 and 10 ms.
- The two-proton decay energy ($Q_{2p}$) for $^{38}$Ti is approximately 1.85 MeV, with an uncertainty of 0.26 MeV.
- The 2$p$ half-life for $^{38}$Ti is estimated to range from $2.76 \times 10^{-14}$ seconds to $1.17 \times 10^{-11}$ seconds.
- The dominant configuration for the $^{39}$Ti ground state is ${}^{36}{\rm{Ca}} \otimes \pi \left( {f_{7/2}} \right)^2\nu \left( {d_{3/2}} \right)^1$ with approximately 63% occupation probability, and a secondary configuration ${}^{36}{\rm{Ca}} \otimes \pi \left( {p_{3/2}} \right)^2\nu \left( {d_{3/2}} \right)^1$ with about 22% probability.

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical studies of 2$p$ radioactivity in $^{39}$Ti and $^{38}$Ti, which had been predicted as candidates since the 1990s by Brown (1991), Ormand (1996), and others, but had not been confirmed experimentally despite multiple measurements by Detraz (1990), Giovinazzo (2001), and Dossat (2007). The study improves upon earlier approaches such as the extended R-matrix framework by Zhang (2023) and the WKB approximation by Cui (2020) and Royer (2022) by providing a microscopic treatment of continuum effects using GSM and GCC methods. The results enable future experimental searches for 2$p$ decay in $^{38}$Ti, which is predicted to be a more promising candidate than $^{39}$Ti due to its higher 2$p$ decay energy and broader decay width.
