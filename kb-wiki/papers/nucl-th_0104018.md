# Elliptic flow and freezeout from the parton cascade MPC

> Differential elliptic flow out to p_T ~ 5 GeV/c and particle spectra are calculated using the MPC elastic parton cascade model for Au+Au at Ecm ~ 130A GeV. The evolution is computed from parton transport theory, followed by hadronization either via independent fragmentation or by imposing parton-hadron duality. With pQCD elastic cross sections, very large initial gluon densities dN/deta > 7000 are required to reproduce the data measured by the STAR collaboration. In addition, elliptic flow and the p_T spectra are shown to be very sensit ive to particle subdivision.

# Elliptic flow and freezeout from the parton cascade MPC

## Key claim

The paper shows that pQCD elastic cross sections alone can reproduce STAR elliptic flow data only if initial gluon densities are extremely large (dN_g/dη > 7000), and that both elliptic flow and transverse momentum spectra are highly sensitive to the particle subdivision parameter used to maintain Lorentz covariance in the cascade simulation.

## Method

The authors solve covariant Boltzmann transport theory with elastic 2→2 gluon scattering using the MPC parton cascade, with Debye-screened pQCD matrix elements. They initialize a longitudinally boost-invariant Bjorken tube at T₀ = 700 MeV and τ₀ = 0.1 fm/c, with transverse density from a binary collision profile of two Woods-Saxon distributions, and then hadronize via either local parton-hadron duality (each gluon becomes a pion with equal isospin probability) or independent fragmentation using NLO g→π± fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

- Initial gluon pseudo-rapidity density required to match STAR data: dN_g/dη > 7000 with pQCD elastic cross sections
- Initial temperature: T₀ = 700 MeV at proper time τ₀ = 0.1 fm/c
- HIJING normalization at b=0: dN(0)/dη = 210
- Particle subdivision values used: ℓ = 100 for impact parameters 0, 2, and 4 fm; ℓ = 220, 450, 1100, and 5000 for b = 6, 8, 10, and 12 fm
- Minimum-bias elliptic flow saturates at p_T ∼ 1.5–2 GeV
- Elliptic flow maximum occurs at impact parameter b ≈ 8 fm
- For ξ = 21000 mb, elastic energy loss causes an order of magnitude quenching at p_T > 6 GeV for semicentral collisions
- To obtain covariant results, particle subdivision ℓ > 200 was needed

## Context

This work extends earlier parton cascade studies by Zhang (1999) and Molnar (2001) by computing p_T-differential elliptic flow, modeling hadronization to obtain final hadron observables, and using realistic nuclear geometry. It contrasts with ideal hydrodynamics, which agrees with data up to p_T ∼ 1.5 GeV but fails to saturate at high p_T as STAR data show, and with inelastic parton energy loss calculations by Gyulassy, Vitev, and Wang that are valid only at high p_T. The results enable future studies of whether gluon saturation models by Eskola (2000) with up to five times higher initial densities than HIJING can generate the observed collective flow with pQCD cross sections.
