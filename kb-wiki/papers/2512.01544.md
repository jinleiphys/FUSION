# Fermi-liquid view of viscosity in cold and dense nucleon matter

> We develop a framework to calculate transport properties in cold, dense relativistic quasiparticle system within the Fermi-liquid theory at the mean-field level. Building on our previous study [Phys. Rev. C 111, 044904 (2025)], we start from the linearized relativistic Boltzmann equation tailored to quasiparticles with medium-dependent dispersion relation and implement Landau matching conditions, proving that the bulk viscosity is manifestly nonnegative. A low-temperature expansion then yields leading-order ($T/μ^*$) expressions for the shear ($η$) and bulk ($ζ$) viscosities, where the behavior $ζ/η\propto (T/μ^*)^4$ in the degenerate regime is found to be robust against quasiparticle mass correction. We couple the kinetic framework to a Walecka-type mean-field equation of state and compute $η$ and $ζ$ for cold, dense nucleon matter. The transport properties of nucleonic matter in the degenerate regime can be relevant for intermediate beam-energy nuclear experiments.

# Fermi-liquid view of viscosity in cold and dense nucleon matter

## Key claim
The authors prove that bulk viscosity in cold, dense relativistic quasiparticle systems is manifestly nonnegative when Landau matching conditions are properly implemented, and that the ratio ζ/η ∝ (T/μ*)^4 in the degenerate regime is robust against quasiparticle mass corrections from mean-field interactions.

## Method
The authors start from the linearized relativistic Boltzmann equation for quasiparticles with medium-dependent dispersion relations, solved within the relaxation-time approximation (RTA). They implement Landau matching conditions to fix zero-mode ambiguities in the collision operator, then perform a low-temperature Sommerfeld expansion to obtain leading-order expressions for shear and bulk viscosities. The framework is coupled to a Walecka-type mean-field equation of state with scalar (σ, mσ=550 MeV, gσ=8.69) and vector (ω, mω=783 MeV, gω=8.65) meson exchanges, including self-interaction terms to reproduce empirical nuclear saturation properties.

## Key numbers
- Shear viscosity at leading order: η_LO = p_F*^5 τ_rel / (30π² μ*)
- Bulk viscosity at leading order: ζ_LO = (8π² m*⁴ p_F* T⁴) / (405 μ*⁵) τ_rel
- Ratio ζ_LO/η_LO ∝ (T/μ*)⁴ in the degenerate regime
- Relaxation time parameterization: τ_rel(x) = 388 × (x⁴ − 2.37x³ + 5.44x² − 5.63x + 1.90) / (x⁵ + 2.25x⁴ − 3.60x²) fm, where x = μ_B*/m_N*
- Nucleon mass m_N = 939 MeV; scalar meson mass m_σ = 550 MeV; vector meson mass m_ω = 783 MeV
- Coupling constants: g_σ = 8.69, g_ω = 8.65; self-interaction parameters κ_3 = 7.95×10⁻³, κ_4 = 6.95×10⁻⁴
- Numerical results presented at T = 8 MeV for dimensionless viscosities scaled by mean-field enthalpy and effective Fermi momentum

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Li et al., Phys. Rev. C 111, 044904, 2025) by establishing proper Landau matching conditions that guarantee nonnegative bulk viscosity, resolving a gap identified in that earlier analysis. The framework is relevant for intermediate beam-energy nuclear experiments at facilities such as the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) in the United States and the proposed Electron-Ion Collider in China (EicC), as well as for understanding transport in neutron stars and neutron star mergers where dissipation is typically dominated by slow electroweak processes (Hoyos, 2020; Cruz Rojas, 2024). The results enable hydrodynamic modeling of cold, dense nucleonic matter with microscopically grounded transport coefficients, contrasting with the hot QCD matter regime where temperature is the dominant scale (Baier, 2000; Arnold, 2002; Romatschke, 2017).
