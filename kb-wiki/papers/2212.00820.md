# Far-from-equilibrium slow modes and momentum anisotropy in expanding plasma

> The momentum distribution of particle production in heavy-ion collisions encodes information about thermalization processes in the early-stage quark-gluon plasma. We use kinetic theory to study the far-from-equilibrium evolution of an expanding plasma with an anisotropic momentum-space distribution. We identify slow and fast degrees of freedom in the far-from-equilibrium plasma from the evolution of moments of this distribution. At late times, the slow modes correspond to hydrodynamic degrees of freedom and are naturally gapped from the fast modes by the inverse of the relaxation time, $τ_R^{-1}$. At early times, however, there are an infinite number of slow modes with a gap inversely proportional to time, $τ^{-1}$. From the evolution of the slow modes we generalize the paradigm of the far-from-equilibrium attractor to vector and tensor components of the energy-momentum tensor, and even to higher moments of the distribution function that are not part of the hydrodynamic evolution. We predict that initial-state momentum anisotropy decays slowly in the far-from-equilibrium phase and may persist until the relaxation time.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a far-from-equilibrium, longitudinally expanding plasma, initial-state momentum anisotropy decays slowly and may persist until the relaxation time \(\tau_R\), because early-time slow modes associated with momentum anisotropy are not hydrodynamic modes and their decay rate is set by \(1/\tau_R\) rather than by the faster decay expected from extending hydrodynamic reasoning.

## Method

The authors use kinetic theory in the relaxation-time approximation (RTA) for a massless gluon plasma undergoing boost-invariant longitudinal expansion. They expand the angular distribution function \(F(\tau, \hat{p})\) in spherical harmonics and derive evolution equations for the moments \(L_{lm}\). By analyzing the eigenspectrum of the non-Hermitian matrices \(H_{ms}(\tau)\) that govern the moment evolution, they identify slow and fast modes in each symmetry sector \((m,s)\). They then solve the moment equations numerically for various initial conditions to demonstrate attractor behavior and compute the decay of momentum anisotropy.

## Key numbers

* The ground state eigenvalues for all parity-even sectors with \(m \geq 2\) are \(E^{G}_{m+} = \frac{1}{\tau} + \frac{1}{\tau_R}\) (Eq. \ref{Em}).
* In the early-time limit \(\tau \to 0\), the ground state eigenvalues for the hydrodynamic parity-even sectors \((0,+)\) and \((1,+)\) and for all \(m \geq 2\) parity-even sectors are degenerate: \(\lim_{\tau\to 0} E^{H}_{0+} = E^{H}_{1+} = E^{G}_{m\geq2 +} = \frac{1}{\tau}\) (Eq. \ref{E-slow}).
* The ratio of relaxation time to the timescale for geometry-driven anisotropy generation is estimated as \(\frac{\tau_{\mathcal{E}}}{\tau_R} \sim \left(\frac{R}{\tau_0}\right)^{1/3} \left(\frac{7.14}{\nu \pi} \frac{dN_{\rm ch}}{dy}\right)^{1/3} \frac{1}{4\pi(\eta/s)\,c_{s}}\), and with \(\nu=40\) and \(\eta/s=0.2\), initial momentum anisotropy can be important when \(dN_{\rm ch}/dy \lesssim 20\).
* The evolution of the momentum eccentricity \(\mathcal{E}_{2p}\) follows \(\partial_\tau \ln {\cal E}_{2p} = -\left(E^{G}_{2+}-E^{G}_{0+}\right)\), where the difference \(E^{G}_{2+}-E^{G}_{0+} \sim 1/\tau_R\).

## Context

This work extends the study of far-from-equilibrium attractors, previously focused on scalar components of the energy-momentum tensor such as \(p_L/\epsilon\) (Romatschke 2017, Kurkela 2019), to vector and tensor components. It generalizes the adiabatic analysis of kinetic theory developed by Brewer (2019) and Blaizot (2019). The finding that initial momentum anisotropy persists until \(\tau_R\) addresses a debate about the decay timescale of initial-state anisotropies (Greif 2017, Nie 2019, Liyanage 2022) and has implications for whether anisotropic flow in small collision systems is hydrodynamic in origin or receives contributions from initial-state quantum fluctuations (Dumitru 2008, Dusling 2015, Schlichting 2016, Schenke 2019). The work motivates future studies of the interplay between initial-state momentum anisotropy and anisotropy generated by spatial gradients.
