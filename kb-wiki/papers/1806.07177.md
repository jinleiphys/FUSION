# Jet quenching parameter in an expanding QCD plasma

> We study the phenomenon of transverse momentum broadening for a high-$p_T$ parton propagating through a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma undergoing boost-invariant longitudinal expansion. We propose a boost-invariant description for this phenomenon, in which the broadening refers to the angular variables $η$ (the pseudo-rapidity) and $ϕ$ (the azimuthal angle). The jet quenching parameter $\hat{q}$ which enters this description depends upon the proper time alone. We furthermore consider radiative corrections to $\hat q$. As in the case of a static medium, we find potentially large corrections enhanced by a double logarithm. But unlike for the static medium, these corrections are now local in time: they depend upon the local (proper) time characterizing the expansion, and not upon the overall path length. We resum such corrections to all orders into a renormalized jet quenching parameter. The main effect of this resummation is to slow down the decrease of $\hat q$ with increasing proper time.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a boost-invariant description of transverse momentum broadening for a high-$p_T$ parton in a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma undergoing boost-invariant longitudinal expansion, where the broadening refers to angular variables (pseudo-rapidity $\eta$ and azimuthal angle $\phi$) rather than momentum components. The radiative corrections to the jet quenching parameter $\hat{q}$, which are enhanced by double logarithms as in the static medium case, become local in proper time due to the expansion, meaning they depend on the local proper time rather than the overall path length.

## Method

The authors use light-cone coordinates adapted to the parton's direction of motion and eikonal approximation to describe multiple scattering, representing the effect of collisions via Wilson lines. They compute the transverse momentum distribution via Fourier transform of a dipole S-matrix, first at tree level for a boost-invariant expanding plasma with temperature scaling as $T(\tau)/T_0 = (\tau_0/\tau)^{\beta/3}$, then calculate radiative corrections to double-logarithmic accuracy by integrating over gluon formation times and transverse momenta, resumming these corrections to all orders.

## Key numbers

- The temperature scaling law: $(T/T_0)^3 = (\tau_0/\tau)^\beta$, with $\beta = 3v_s^2 = 1/(1+\Delta_1/3)$, where $\beta=1$ for an ideal gas and $\beta<1$ in perturbative QCD
- For the ideal gas limit ($\beta=1$): $\mathcal{Q}_0^2(L) = \hat{q}_0(\tau_0)\tau_0 \ln(L/\tau_0)$
- For $\beta<1$ in the large time limit: $\mathcal{Q}_0^2(L) = \hat{q}_0(L)L/(1-\beta)$
- The one-loop correction to the jet quenching parameter: $\delta\hat{q}(\tau) = \hat{q}_0(\tau) \cdot (\alpha_s N_c / 2\pi) \cdot \ln^2(\tau/\lambda(\tau))$, where $\lambda=1/T$ is the thermal wavelength

## Context

This work extends previous studies of radiative corrections to $\hat{q}$ in static media (Liou 2013, Iancu 2014, Blaizot 2014, Wu 2014) to the physically relevant case of a longitudinally expanding plasma, building on the Bjorken (1982) boost-invariant expansion model. It generalizes earlier studies of collisional transverse momentum broadening in expanding media (Baier 1998, Zakharov 1998, Arnold 2008, Wu 2010) which were restricted to probes at central rapidity. The key advance is showing that the non-locality of quantum corrections is considerably reduced by expansion, enabling a renormalized $\hat{q}(\tau)$ that remains local in proper time, which enables more realistic phenomenological calculations of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC.
