# Hadron Masses in Strong Magnetic Fields

> Hadron masses under strong magnetic fields are studied. In the presence of strong magnetic fields exceeding the QCD energy scale $ eB \gg Λ^2_{\rm QCD} $, ${\rm SU(3)}_{\rm flavor} \otimes {\rm SU(2)}_{\rm spin}$ symmetry of hadrons is explicitly broken so that the quark components of hadrons differ from those with zero or weak magnetic fields $ eB \lesssim Λ^2_{\rm QCD} $. Also, squeezing of hadrons by strong magnetic fields affects the hadron mass spectrum. We develop a quark model which appropriately incorporates these features and analytically calculate various hadron masses including mesons, baryons and those with strangeness.

# Hadron Masses in Strong Magnetic Fields

## Key claim

Under strong magnetic fields exceeding the QCD energy scale ($eB \gg \Lambda^2_{\rm QCD}$), hadron masses are determined primarily by the number of quarks with $qs<0$ (where $q$ is electric charge and $s$ is spin projection), whose constituent mass grows as $\sqrt{2|qB|}$, while quarks with $qs>0$ have nearly constant mass; this explains the non-trivial mass hierarchy observed in lattice simulations, though the model is only qualitatively consistent and neglects short-range quark interactions and chiral corrections.

## Method

The author constructs a single-particle Dirac Hamiltonian with a linear confinement potential $V(r) = \sqrt{m^2 + \sigma_\perp^2 r^2 + \sigma_\parallel^2 z^2}$ in a constant magnetic field along the $z$-axis, solves analytically for the lowest-energy (constituent) quark mass $M$ and probability density, then computes hadron masses as the sum of constituent quark masses of the appropriate quark components (Table 1), where the quark content of each hadron is reorganized to minimize the number of $qs<0$ quarks. Parameters are fixed to $\sigma_\perp = \sigma_\parallel = (200\ \mathrm{MeV})^2$, $m_u=m_d=0\ \mathrm{MeV}$, $m_s=350\ \mathrm{MeV}$ to reproduce empirical constituent quark masses at $B=0$.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses at $B=0$: $M_u, M_d \sim 350\ \mathrm{MeV}$, $M_s \sim 500\ \mathrm{MeV}$ (set by parameter choice)
- Confinement parameters: $\sigma_\perp = \sigma_\parallel = (200\ \mathrm{MeV})^2 \sim \Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2$
- For strong fields $eB \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2$, constituent quark mass for $qs<0$ quarks: $M \sim \sqrt{2|qB|}$ (independent of $\sigma_\perp$)
- For weak fields $eB \ll \Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2$, Zeeman splitting: $M \sim M(B=0) - qBs/(2M(B=0))$ with $g=2$
- Mass hierarchy for light mesons at strong fields: $M_{\rho^0} \sim M_{\pi^\pm} > M_{\rho^\pm} \sim M_{\pi^0}$
- Mass hierarchy for light baryons at strong fields: $M_{\Delta^0} > M_{\Delta^+} > M_{\Delta^-} \sim M_n \sim M_p \sim M_{\Delta^{++}}$

## Context

This work extends phenomenological studies of hadron masses in strong magnetic fields by Andreichikov et al. (2013) using a relativistic Hamiltonian technique and by Hattori et al. (2014) using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, but provides a simpler analytical quark model that clearly explains the physics. The model reproduces qualitatively the lattice QCD results of Hidaka and Yamamoto (2013) and Luschevskaya et al. (2014) for $\pi$ and $\rho$ meson masses, including the hierarchy $M_{\rho^0} \sim M_{\pi^+} > M_{\rho^+} \sim M_{\pi^0}$. The results enable predictions for decay mode modifications (e.g., suppression of $K^0 \rightarrow \pi^+\pi^-$) and for the equation of state of nuclear matter in neutron stars, though the author notes that gluon dynamics modifications via quark loop corrections (Shovkovy 2002; Clark et al. 2014) and magnetic catalysis effects remain to be incorporated.
