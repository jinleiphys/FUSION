# Chiral phase properties of finite size quark droplets in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model

> Chiral phase properties of finite size hadronic systems are investigated within the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. Finite size effects are taken into account by making use of the multiple reflection expansion. We find that, for droplets with relatively small baryon numbers, chiral symmetry restoration is enhanced by the finite size effects. However the radius of the stable droplet does not change much, as compared to that without the multiple reflection expansion.

## Key claim

Finite size effects, modeled via the multiple reflection expansion (MRE), enhance chiral symmetry restoration in quark droplets with relatively small baryon numbers, but the radius of the stable droplet changes only slightly compared to calculations without the MRE.

## Method

The authors use the two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean-field (Hartree) approximation at zero temperature, with a sharp momentum cutoff Λ = 600 MeV and coupling constant GΛ² = 2.45. Finite size effects for spherical quark droplets are incorporated via the multiple reflection expansion (MRE), which modifies the density of states by adding surface and curvature terms. The Schwinger–Dyson equation for the dynamically generated quark mass is solved self-consistently together with the baryon number constraint for fixed baryon numbers A = 100 and A = 1000.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum values: pion decay constant fπ = 93.9 MeV, constituent quark mass m = 400 MeV, quark condensate ⟨ūu⟩ = ⟨d̄d⟩ = (−244.9 MeV)³.
- With MRE for A = 100: critical radius Rc^(MRE) ≃ 12 fm; critical baryon number density nc^(MRE) ≃ 0.08 n0, where n0 = 0.17 fm⁻³ is normal nuclear matter density.
- Without MRE for A = 100: critical radius Rc ≃ 4 fm (second-order transition).
- With MRE for A = 1000: critical radius Rc^(MRE) ≃ 14 fm; critical baryon number density nc^(MRE) ≃ 0.5 n0.
- Stable droplet radius with MRE for A = 100: Rs^(MRE) ≃ 3.5 fm.
- Stable droplet radius without MRE for A = 100: Rs ≃ 3.7 fm.
- For bulk matter (infinite system): critical density nc^(QM) ≃ 2 n0.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nonstrange quark matter stability in the NJL model by Buballa and Oertel (2000) and by Mishra and collaborators, who found that stable nonstrange quark matter lies in the chirally restored phase. The finite size treatment using the MRE, originally developed for free particles by Balian and Bloch (1970) and applied to massive quarks by Madsen (1993), allows the authors to assess how droplet size affects chiral symmetry restoration. The results support the earlier conclusion that stable droplets are in the chirally symmetric phase, reminiscent of the MIT bag model, and suggest that finite size effects may be important for understanding strangelet stability, though the MRE approximation becomes unreliable for baryon numbers below about 50.
