# Localized $N, Λ, Σ$, and $Ξ$ Single-Particle Potentials in Finite Nuclei Calculated with $SU_6$ Quark-Model Baryon-Baryon Interactions

> Localized single-particle potentials for all octet baryons, $N$, $Λ$, $Σ$, and $Ξ$, in finite nuclei, $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, $^{28}$Si, $^{40}$Ca, $^{56}$Fe, and $^{90}$Zr, are calculated using the quark-model baryon-baryon interactions. $G$-matrices evaluated in symmetric nuclear matter in the lowest order Brueckner theory are applied to finite nuclei in local density approximation. Non-local potentials are localized by a zero-momentum Wigner transformation. Empirical single-particle properties of the nucleon and the $Λ$ hyperon in nuclear medium have been known to be explained semi-quantitatively in the LOBT framework. Attention is focused in the present consideration on predictions for the $Σ$ and $Ξ$ hyperons. The unified description for the octet baryon-baryon interactions by the SU$_6$ quark-model enables us to obtain less ambiguous extrapolation to the $S=-1$ and $S=-2$ sectors based on the knowledge in the $NN$ sector than other potential models. The $Σ$ mean field is shown to be weakly attractive at the surface, but turns to be repulsive inside, which is consistent with the experimental evidence. The $Ξ$ hyperon s.p. potential is also attractive at the nuclear surface region, and inside fluctuates around zero. Hence $Ξ$ hypernuclear bound states are unlikely. We also evaluate energy shifts of the $Σ^-$ and $Ξ^-$ atomic levels in $^{28}$Si and $^{56}$Fe, using the calculated s.p. potentials.

## Key claim

The SU₆ quark-model baryon-baryon interactions predict that the Σ mean field in finite nuclei is weakly attractive at the surface but becomes repulsive inside, consistent with experimental evidence, while the Ξ hyperon single-particle potential is attractive at the nuclear surface region and fluctuates around zero inside, making Ξ hypernuclear bound states unlikely.

## Method

The authors calculated G-matrices in symmetric nuclear matter using lowest-order Brueckner theory with the SU₆ quark-model potential fss2, then applied these to finite nuclei via local density approximation. Non-local single-particle potentials were localized using a zero-momentum Wigner transformation. The method was applied to six nuclei (¹²C, ¹⁶O, ²⁸Si, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁵⁶Fe, ⁹⁰Zr) using nucleon density distributions from density-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations with the Campi-Sprung G-0 force.

## Key numbers

- The Σ single-particle potential at k=0 in symmetric nuclear matter is repulsive by about 15 MeV at normal nuclear density (k_F = 1.35 fm⁻¹)
- The width Γ(0) of the Σ hyperon in nuclear medium at normal density exceeds 30 MeV
- The Ξ effective mass ratio m_Ξ*/m_Ξ is approximately 1.1 at k_F = 1.35 fm⁻¹ and approximately 1.05 at k_F = 1.07 fm⁻¹
- The calculated nucleon potential depth in finite nuclei is 80-90 MeV, corresponding to the single-particle potential in nuclear matter at normal density
- The Λ single-particle potential strength in normal nuclear matter is almost 45 MeV
- The LOBT saturation curve for the fss2 potential shows an energy minimum at k_F = 1.7 fm⁻¹ and E/A = -20 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Kyoto-Niigata group's unified SU₆ quark-model description of octet baryon-baryon interactions (fss2 potential) from nuclear matter to finite nuclei, providing predictions for Σ and Ξ hyperons where experimental data is scarce. The results contrast with most Nijmegen one-boson-exchange potential parameter sets (Hard Core models, Soft Core versions, and ESC06) which predict an attractive Σ-nucleus potential, while the quark-model prediction of repulsive Σ potential agrees with analyses of KEK (π⁻,K⁺) inclusive spectra by Harada (2005) and Kohno (2006). The calculated Σ potential's radial dependence matches the surface-attractive to interior-repulsive behavior deduced from Σ⁻ atomic data analyses by Batty, Friedman, and Gal (1994). The small imaginary strength of the Ξ potential at low momentum is consistent with the empirical small width of about 3 MeV from Ξ⁻p cross-section measurements by Ahn (2006).
