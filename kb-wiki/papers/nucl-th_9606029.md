# Exotic Decays of Strangelets

> The stability of strange matter depends on the implicit assumption that baryon number is conserved. We examine the relevant effective operators which allow strangelets to decay by violating the conservation of baryon number. From the experimental lower limit of 10$^{25}$ years on the stability of nuclei, we find a lower limit of the order 10$^6$ years on the stability of strangelets against such exotic decays.

## Key claim

Strangelets (hypothetical stable lumps of strange quark matter) could decay via baryon-number-violating interactions that are kinematically forbidden for ordinary nuclei, but the lifetime for such exotic decays has a firm lower limit of about 10⁶ years, derived from the experimental lower limit of 1.6 × 10²⁵ years on nuclear stability.

## Method

The authors examine effective operators of dimension 9 that violate baryon number by two units and strangeness by four units, specifically the (uss)² operator. They estimate the lifetime of strangelets against decays such as (A, Nₛ) → (A−2, Nₛ−2) + KK by comparing the suppression of the corresponding nuclear decay (NN → KKKπ) via weak transitions to the known limits on nuclear stability, and also compute explicit lifetimes from supersymmetric and E₆-inspired models.

## Key numbers

- Experimental lower limit on mode-independent nuclear stability: 1.6 × 10²⁵ years
- Lower limit on strangelet lifetime against ΔB = 2, ΔNₛ = 4 decays: > 10⁶ years
- For a supersymmetric model with scalar quark masses > 200 GeV and λᵤₛb < 1, the estimated strangelet lifetime is > 10²⁰ years
- For an E₆-inspired model with M = 1 TeV, the estimated strangelet lifetime is ~ 2 × 10⁷ years
- The ground-state mass difference m₀(A) − m₀(A−2) for strangelets is approximately (1704 + 111 A⁻¹/³ + 161 A⁻²/³) MeV, assuming mₛ = 100 MeV and B¹/⁴ = 145 MeV
- The effective (uss)² operator would allow decays until A ≈ 13, below which the energy per baryon exceeds 930 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of baryon-number-violating interactions beyond ordinary proton decay and neutron-antineutron oscillation, which have been constrained by experiments such as those reviewed by the Particle Data Group (1996) and by direct searches for NN → ππ decay in iron. The authors specifically consider an effective (uss)² operator that is kinematically forbidden for nuclei but allowed for strangelets, building on earlier strange matter stability calculations by Madsen (1994). The result enables future searches for strange matter as long-lived kaon and pion emitters, and suggests that if the mediating particles have masses around 1 TeV, they could be probed at the LHC.
