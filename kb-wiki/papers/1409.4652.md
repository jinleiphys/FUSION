# Diffusion of hidden charm mesons in hadronic medium

> The drag and diffusion coefficients of a hot hadronic medium have been evaluated by using hidden charm mesons as probes. The matrix elements for the evaluation of these coefficients are calculated using an effective theory as well as from scattering lengths. Although the transport coefficients show a significant rise with temperature its effects on the suppression of $J/Ïˆ$ in hadronic matter is not significant.

## Key claim

The drag and diffusion coefficients of $J/\psi$ and $\eta_c$ in a hot hadronic medium are small, and although they show a significant rise with temperature, the resulting suppression of $J/\psi$ at high momentum in the hadronic phase is not significant, meaning any observed suppression likely originates from the quark-gluon plasma phase.

## Method

The drag and diffusion coefficients were computed from the elastic scattering of $J/\psi$ and $\eta_c$ with light vector mesons ($\rho^0$, $\omega$, $\phi$) in a thermal hadronic medium. The scattering amplitudes were obtained using two independent approaches: an effective SU(4) chiral Lagrangian with vector-vector-pseudo-scalar interactions via $\eta_c$ or $J/\psi$ exchange, and T-matrix elements extracted from scattering lengths from quenched lattice QCD calculations for $J/\psi$ and $\eta_c$ interactions with $\pi$, $\rho^0$, and nucleons.

## Key numbers

- Elastic cross sections of $J/\psi$ with $\rho^0$ and $\omega$ are within 1 to 10 mb for $\sqrt{s} \sim 8$ GeV.
- Coupling constants for the effective interaction $g_{JV\eta_c}$ are 2.44 GeV$^{-1}$ for $V=\rho^0$, 7.03 GeV$^{-1}$ for $V=\omega$, and 4.51 GeV$^{-1}$ for $V=\phi$.
- Scattering length for $J/\psi\pi$ is $0.0119 \pm 0.0039$ fm; for $J/\psi\rho^0$ it is $0.23 \pm 0.08$ fm; for $J/\psi N$ it is $0.71 \pm 0.48$ fm.
- Scattering length for $\eta_c\pi$ is $0.0113 \pm 0.0035$ fm; for $\eta_c\rho^0$ it is $0.21 \pm 0.11$ fm; for $\eta_c N$ it is $0.70 \pm 0.66$ fm.
- Taking $\Delta\tau \sim 5$ fm/c and $\gamma \sim 10^{-4}$ fm$^{-1}$, the $R_{AA}$ for $J/\psi$ is close to unity, indicating negligible hadronic suppression.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on drag and diffusion of open charm mesons (Mitra et al., 2012) and $\Lambda_c$ baryons (Mitra et al., 2013) in hadronic matter, using similar effective Lagrangian and scattering-length methods. The finding that $J/\psi$ momentum diffusion is small contrasts with the significant drag found for $D$ mesons and $\Lambda_c$, and implies that the hadronic phase contributes negligibly to $J/\psi$ suppression at high $p_T$, simplifying the characterization of quark-gluon plasma from $J/\psi$ data. The results are compared with earlier estimates of $J/\psi$ absorption by comoving hadrons from Haglin (2000) and Liu and Ko (2001), and with lattice QCD scattering lengths from Yokokawa et al. (2006).
