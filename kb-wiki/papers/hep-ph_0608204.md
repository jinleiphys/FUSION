# Meson-Baryon Effective Chiral Lagrangians to O(q^3)

> We construct the complete and minimal O(q^2) and O(q^3) three-flavour Lorentz invariant chiral meson-baryon Lagrangians for the first time in the literature. We compare with previous three-flavour studies reducing the number of independent monomials and adding new ones that were missing.

# Meson-Baryon Effective Chiral Lagrangians to O(q^3)

## Key claim

The authors construct the complete and minimal three-flavour Lorentz invariant chiral meson-baryon Lagrangians at O(q^2) and O(q^3) for the first time, correcting and extending prior work by Krause (1992) and Frink and Meißner (2004) by reducing the number of independent monomials and adding previously missing terms.

## Method

The authors construct all possible monomials bilinear in baryon fields using the standard non-linear realization of chiral symmetry, building blocks (u_mu, chi_plus/minus, f_plus/minus^mu_nu), covariant derivatives, and Clifford algebra elements. They enforce invariance under parity, charge conjugation, and hermitic conjugation, then reduce the set to a minimal linearly independent basis using equations of motion at lowest chiral order, Cayley-Hamilton relations for SU(3), and the curvature relation [D_mu, D_nu] X = (1/4)[[u_mu, u_nu], X] - (i/2)[f^+_mu_nu, X].

## Key numbers

- The O(q^2) Lagrangian contains 15 independent terms with coefficients b_D, b_F, b_0, b_1 through b_8, and d_1 through d_5.
- The O(q^3) Lagrangian contains 84 independent terms with coefficients h_i (i = 1 to 84).
- 3 structures from Krause (1992) at O(q^2) are shown to be redundant and expressible in terms of others.
- The term with coefficient b_9 in Frink and Meißner (2004) is also redundant and can be written in terms of monomials proportional to b_5 through b_8.

## Context

This work fills a gap in three-flavour baryon chiral perturbation theory, where the Lorentz invariant meson-baryon Lagrangians at O(q^2) and O(q^3) were previously given incompletely or with redundant terms by Krause (1992) and Frink and Meißner (2004). The complete Lagrangians enable systematic one-loop calculations in SU(3) baryon CHPT using Lorentz invariant regularization schemes such as infrared regularization or the extended on-mass-shell scheme, and support non-perturbative resummation methods for studying scattering and production processes involving resonances like the Lambda(1405).
