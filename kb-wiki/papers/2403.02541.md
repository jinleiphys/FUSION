# Shell Effects in Quasi-fission in Reactions Forming 226Th Compound Nucleus

> Quasi-fission (QF) reactions occur in fully damped heavy-ion collisions without the formation of an equilibrated compound nucleus, leading to the formation of fragments with similar properties as in fission reactions. Similar shell effects are expected to affect fragment formation in both fission and QF. Our purpose is to investigate QF dynamics in different reactions forming the same compound nucleus and search for possible signatures of shell effects in fragment formation. 50Ca+176Yb and 96Zr+130Sn QF reactions are simulated with the time-dependent Hartree-Fock code Sky3D near the Coulomb barrier. Evolutions of the quadrupole (Q20) and octupole (Q30) moments are interpreted in terms of features of the potential energy surface (PES) of the 226Th compound nucleus. Both reactions encounter QF. In 50Ca+176Yb, those only occur at finite angular momenta. In the more symmetric 96Zr+130Sn reaction with stronger Coulomb repulsion in the entrance channel, QF also occurs in central collisions. In agreement with earlier predictions, 50Ca+176Yb encounters partial mass equilibration that is stopped when the heavy fragment reaches Z~54 protons, as in the asymmetric fission mode of 226Th. 96Zr+130Sn encounters an inverse QF also leading to similar fragments as in asymmetric fission. In both systems, QF trajectories in the Q20-Q30 plane follow the asymmetric fission valley of 226Th PES. The observation of an inverse QF is a clear prediction that shell effects have a strong influence in QF. The similarity between fragments formed in asymmetric fission and QF supports the idea that the same shell effects are at play in both mechanisms. Interpreting QF dynamics with PES used in fission is naturally limited by the fact that these PES are usually computed with axial symmetry, no angular momentum and no excitation energy, thus motivating future developments of PES for QF.

## Key claim

Shell effects, specifically octupole deformed shell effects at Z=52-56, influence quasi-fission dynamics in reactions forming 226Th, as evidenced by mass equilibration stopping at Z≈54 in 50Ca+176Yb and by an "inverse quasi-fission" (increasing mass asymmetry) in 96Zr+130Sn; however, the comparison between TDHF trajectories and potential energy surfaces remains qualitative because the PES are computed assuming zero excitation energy, zero angular momentum, and axial symmetry.

## Method

The authors performed time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) simulations using the Sky3D code with the SLy4d Skyrme functional and BCS pairing for 50Ca+176Yb and 96Zr+130Sn collisions near the Coulomb barrier. They computed the potential energy surface (PES) of 226Th using constrained Hartree-Fock with the SkyAx code, and compared TDHF trajectories in the quadrupole (Q20) and octupole (Q30) moment plane to features of this PES, including the asymmetric fission valley.

## Key numbers

- Coulomb barriers: VB=151.8 MeV for 50Ca+176Yb and VB=215.4 MeV for 96Zr+130Sn, determined according to Światecki et al.
- In 50Ca+176Yb, quasi-fission occurs only at finite angular momenta; central collisions show a rapid transition from quasi-elastic scattering to fusion with contact times exceeding 35 zs.
- In 96Zr+130Sn, quasi-fission occurs in central collisions at energies from ≈221 MeV to ≈232 MeV, with contact times up to τ≈9 zs at Ec.m.≈231 MeV.
- Mass equilibration in 50Ca+176Yb stops when the heavy fragment reaches Z≈54 protons and N≈82 neutrons, matching the asymmetric fission mode of 226Th.
- Total excitation energy (TXE) in quasi-fission events plateaus at TXE≈70±10 MeV for contact times τ≳5 zs.
- Quasi-fission events in 50Ca+176Yb have TKE close to Viola systematics; inverse quasi-fission events in 96Zr+130Sn have TKE higher than Viola systematics.

## Context

This work extends earlier TDHF predictions by Simenel (2021) for 50Ca+176Yb, which showed mass equilibration stopping at Z≈54, and applies a similar PES-topography interpretation approach as McGlynn (2023) used for reactions forming 294Og. The results support the idea that the same octupole deformed shell effects at Z=52-56 proposed by Scamps (2018) for actinide fission also influence quasi-fission, but the authors note that interpreting quasi-fission dynamics with PES computed under axial symmetry, zero angular momentum, and zero excitation energy is limited, motivating future developments of PES specifically for quasi-fission.
