# Determination of the entanglement entropy in elastic scattering using model-independent method for hadron femtoscopy

> The entanglement entropy of two-body elastic scattering at high energies is studied by using the model-independent Lévy imaging method for investigating the hadron structure. It is considered the finite entropy in the momentum Hilbert space properly regularized and results are compared to recent evaluation using the diffraction peak approximation. We present the entropy for RHIC, Tevatron and LHC energies pointing out the underlying uncertainties.

## Key claim

The entanglement entropy in elastic hadron scattering can be extracted using a model-independent Lévy imaging method rather than relying on the diffraction peak approximation, and the resulting values are somewhat larger than those obtained with the peak approximation but the deviation is not large, indicating the small-\(t\) approximation remains suitable.

## Method

The authors compute the entanglement entropy \(S_{EE}\) for two-body elastic scattering using the volume-regularization scheme, which yields an expression depending only on measurable observables: the total cross section \(\sigma_{tot}\), elastic cross section \(\sigma_{el}\), and differential elastic cross section \(d\sigma_{el}/dt\). They reconstruct the elastic scattering amplitude from experimental differential cross section data using the Lévy expansion method, a model-independent series expansion in terms of orthogonal Lévy polynomials, and then evaluate the entropy integral numerically. The analysis covers proton-proton collisions at ISR energies (23.5 to 62.5 GeV) and LHC energies (7 and 13 TeV), as well as proton-antiproton collisions at ISR (53 GeV), UA4 (546 and 630 GeV), and D0 Tevatron (1960 GeV).

## Key numbers

- At \(\sqrt{s}=13\) TeV (LHC, \(pp\)), the Lévy imaging method gives \(S_{EE} = 1.126\), compared to the volume-regularization value of 1.114 from the diffraction peak approximation.
- At \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV (LHC, \(pp\)), the Lévy imaging method gives \(S_{EE} = 1.020\), compared to the volume-regularization value of 1.031.
- At \(\sqrt{s}=1.8\) TeV (Tevatron, \(p\bar{p}\)), the Lévy imaging method gives \(S_{EE} = 0.953\), compared to the volume-regularization value of 0.918.
- At \(\sqrt{s}=0.2\) TeV (RHIC, \(pp\)), the volume-regularization value is \(S_{EE} = 0.769\), using \(\sigma_{tot}=54.67\pm 1.89\) mb and \(\sigma_{el}=10.85\pm 0.64\) mb.
- The black disk limit gives an asymptotic bound \(S_{EE} \approx 1+\ln(2) \approx 1.693\) for the diffraction peak approximation.
- The one-channel eikonal model fit yields a Pomeron intercept \(\Delta_{\pom}=0.0019\pm 0.0011\) from the energy dependence of the extracted entropy.

## Context

This work extends the entanglement entropy formalism for elastic scattering developed by Peschanski and Seki (2016, 2019), who used the diffraction peak approximation and three regularization schemes (volume regularization, step-function cutoff, Gaussian cutoff). The present study replaces that approximation with the model-independent Lévy imaging method of Csörgő et al. (2018, 2019, 2020), which allows reconstruction of the full \(t\)-dependence of the elastic amplitude. The results enable a more reliable determination of the entropy at both low and high energies and provide input for testing unitarity bounds such as the black disk limit and the gray disc limit from the U-matrix formalism of Troshin and Tyurin (2007, 2019).
