# Study of the ABC Enhancement in the d d -> alpha X reaction

> The polarized d d -> alpha X reaction at beam energies close to the eta threshold shows very strong structure in the missing mass corresponding to the ABC enhancement. The deuteron tensor analysing power A_yy, and the slope of the vector analysing power A_y with respect to angle, have been measured for this reaction around the forward direction. Both signals are small, and their variations with the alpha-particle momentum are in broad agreement with a theoretical model in which each pair of nucleons in the projectile and target deuterons undergoes pion production through the NN -> d pi reaction.

## Key claim

The ABC enhancement observed in the missing mass spectrum of the \(\vec{d}\,d \to \alpha\,X^0\) reaction is consistent with a model where double-pion production proceeds via two independent \(NN \to d\pi\) reactions between nucleon pairs from the projectile and target deuterons, but the measured vector and tensor analysing powers are small and the theoretical agreement is only broad, with a systematic displacement in the slope of the vector analysing power of about 2 rad\(^{-1}\).

## Method

The experiment used the SPESIII magnetic spectrometer at Laboratoire National Saturne (LNS) to measure deuteron vector and tensor analysing powers in the \(\vec{d}\,d \to \alpha\,X^0\) reaction near the forward direction, with beam energies between 1116.7 and 1127 MeV (spanning the \(\eta\) threshold at \(T_d = 1121\) MeV). The tensor analysing power \(A_{yy}\) was integrated over the full spectrometer acceptance, while the slope of the vector analysing power \(A_y\) with respect to angle was extracted by fitting the left-right asymmetry over a horizontal opening angle of \(\pm 50\) mrad. Data from six energies were summed for the slope measurement to improve statistics, and the results were compared to a refined double-\(\Delta\) model including all significant \(NN \to d\pi\) partial wave amplitudes from the C500 solution of Arndt.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron beam had maximum tensor polarisation \(\rho_{20} = 0.649 \pm 0.011\) and vector polarisation \(\rho_{10} = -0.405 \pm 0.011\).
- The mean slope of the tensor analysing power, which should vanish in the forward direction, was \(\langle \overline{A_{yy}'} \rangle = (-0.05 \pm 0.10)\) rad\(^{-1}\), consistent with zero within statistical errors.
- The difference in \(A_{yy}\) between the forward and backward ABC peaks was \(0.05 \pm 0.02\), possibly arising from integration over the finite SPESIII angular acceptance.
- The typical vector analysing power signal was 0.05 or less over the angular range.
- The ABC peaks have masses around 310 MeV and widths about 40 MeV, as described in the theoretical model.

## Context

This work extends the study of the ABC enhancement, first discovered in the \(p\,d \to {}^{3}\text{He}\,X^0\) reaction by Abashian, Booth, and Crowe (1960), and later observed in \(p\,n \to d\,X^0\) by Banaigs (1973) and \(p\,p \to p\,p\,X^0\) by Bergdolt (1984). The most prominent ABC manifestation is in the \(d\,d \to \alpha\,X^0\) reaction near 1 GeV, as reported by Banaigs (1973) and Nicole (1996). The present measurement of analysing powers provides quantitative tests of the double-\(\Delta\) model of Andersson (1996), which describes the ABC as arising from two independent \(pn \to d\pi^0\) reactions. The results support this model but also indicate that further measurements closer to threshold, where the ABC peaks are not seen (Pia, 1997), would be needed for a complete understanding; such measurements are no longer possible at Saturne following its closure in December 1997.
