# Convergence of 2-Body Effective Range Expansions for $nd$ Quartet Scattering

> We examine the convergence properties of the 2-nucleon Effective Range Expansion as used in Effective Theories (ET-ERE's) for 3-nucleon calculations. We accomplish this by accounting for the 2-body dynamics with a simple rank-1 separable 2-body potential where the finite range effects can be incorporated systematically in both the 2- and 3-body problems. We make our initial comparisons in the simple context of the $^3S_1$ 2-nucleon channel and the $^4S_{3/2}$ 3-nucleon channel. We find that convergence problems for some of the 3-nucleon scattering amplitudes using the ET-ERE can be traced to its poor account of finite range effects that soften the momentum dependence of the deuteron propagator in the Faddeev kernel. In contrast, our simple separable potential with dipole form factors works very well in all cases considered.

## Key claim

The paper finds that convergence problems in 3-nucleon scattering amplitudes using the Effective Range Expansion as implemented in Effective Theories (ET-ERE) arise from its poor treatment of finite range effects, which soften the momentum dependence of the deuteron propagator in the Faddeev kernel; a simple separable potential with dipole form factors works well in all cases considered, while the ET-ERE approach shows qualitative failures at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) for some amplitudes.

## Method

The authors model the 2-body interaction in the $^3S_1$ channel using a rank-1 separable potential with a dipole (Yamaguchi) form factor, fitting its single inverse range parameter $\beta$ to the Nijmegen II phase shifts. They then solve the Faddeev equations for $nd$ quartet scattering in the $^4S_{3/2}$ channel, expanding the resulting 3-body scattering amplitude in powers of $1/\beta$ to define leading order (LO), next-to-leading order (NLO), and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) approximations. Separately, they construct the corresponding ET-ERE Faddeev equations by replacing the off-shell 2-body amplitude with the on-shell Effective Range Expansion, and compare the convergence of both approaches against the full separable potential result.

## Key numbers

* The dipole form factor parameter $\beta = 1.3940$ fm$^{-1}$ is determined by fitting to Nijmegen II $^3S_1$ phase shifts for $p_{cm}^2 < 0.85$ fm$^{-2}$, yielding $\beta/\gamma = 6.019$ where $\gamma = 0.231607$ fm$^{-1}$ from the deuteron binding energy.
* The model predicts scattering length $a = 5.421$ fm (compared to 5.403 fm from Babenko and Petrov), effective range $r_0 = 1.772$ fm (compared to 1.749 fm), and shape parameter $v_4 = 0.1357$ fm$^3$ (compared to 0.163 fm$^3$).
* The Effective Range Expansion for the dipole form factor terminates at ${\cal O}(p^4)$, with $v_4 = 1/(2\beta^3(1+\gamma/\beta)^2)$.

## Context

This work extends the long history of separable potential approaches to 3-body problems, going back to the period after Faddeev's seminal work, and contrasts them with modern Effective Theory treatments that rely on contact interactions and the Effective Range Expansion. The authors build on the separable expansion method of Harms (1970) and the Unitary Pole Approximation, and compare their results to ET-ERE formulations such as those reviewed by Griesshammer (2004) and Platter (2006). The findings enable a systematic understanding of how finite range effects enter 3-body calculations and suggest limitations of the ERE-based approach for describing off-shell 2-body physics in the Faddeev kernel.
