# Three-Nucleon Forces

> The role of three-nucleon forces in ab initio calculations of nuclear systems is investigated. The difference between genuine and induced many-nucleon forces is emphasized. Induced forces arise in the process of solving the nuclear many-body problem as technical intermediaries towards calculationally converged results. Genuine forces make up the Hamiltonian; they represent the chosen underlying dynamics. The hierarchy of contributions arising from two-, three- and many-nucleon forces is discussed. Signals for the need of the inclusion of genuine three-nucleon forces are studied in nuclear systems, technically best under control, especially in three-nucleon and four-nucleon systems. Genuine three-nucleon forces are important for details in the decription of some observables. Their contributions to observables are small on the scale set by two-nucleon forces.

# Three-Nucleon Forces

## Key claim
Genuine three-nucleon forces are necessary for details in the description of some nuclear observables, but their contributions are small on the scale set by two-nucleon forces; the paper emphasizes that the distinction between genuine forces (which make up the Hamiltonian) and induced forces (which arise as technical intermediaries in solving the many-body problem) is critical for understanding when and why three-nucleon forces are needed.

## Method
The paper reviews the conceptual foundations of three-nucleon forces, contrasting genuine forces derived from chiral effective field theory (chi-EFT) or meson theory with induced forces arising from methods such as Brueckner theory or similarity renormalization group (SRG) transformations. It examines signals for genuine three-nucleon force effects in three-nucleon and four-nucleon systems, where calculations are technically best under control, and discusses the hierarchy of contributions from two-, three-, and many-nucleon forces.

## Key numbers
- The chiral expansion is ordered as (Q / Lambda)^(n+1), where Lambda is the chiral-symmetry breaking scale of about 1 GeV, phrased as N^nLO.
- The chi-EFT two-nucleon force at N^3LO has 26 tunable parameters (24 charge-independent, 2 charge-dependent).
- The three-nucleon force at N^2LO carries two tunable parameters (one from the one-pi-exchange-contact topology, one from the purely-contact topology).
- The unified description of nuclear phenomena at low and intermediate energies covers energies up to about 0.5 GeV internal energy.
- The SRG-evolved Hamiltonian with induced forces showed that the He-4 binding contribution from the induced four-nucleon force is quantitatively negligible.

## Context
This review extends the line of work on microscopic nuclear theory that began with early phenomenological two-nucleon forces and Brueckner theory (Brueckner, 1955; Bethe, 1956). It contrasts with earlier approaches such as the Paris potential (Lacombe et al., 1980), the Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon force (Coon et al., 1979), and the Brazil three-nucleon force (Coelho et al., 1983), while emphasizing that chi-EFT (Epelbaum et al., 2009; Machleidt and Entem, 2011) now provides the most consistent framework for deriving two- and three-nucleon forces simultaneously. The paper enables future work by clarifying that the three-nucleon force at N^3LO in chi-EFT is close to completion (Bernard et al., 2008) and that SRG-evolved chi-EFT potentials allow applications in more complex nuclei.
