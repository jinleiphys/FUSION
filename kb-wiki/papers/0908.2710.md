# Toward Identifying the QCD Critical Point: attenuation of the sound mode around the critical point

> Motivated by the fact that QCD critical point (CP) belongs to the same universality class as the liquid-gas transition, the dynamical density fluctuations around the CP is analyzed using relativistic fluid dynamics for a viscous fluid. It is shown that relativistic effects on the dynamical structure factor of the density fluctuation appears only in the width of the sound and thermal modes through a modification of the transport/kinetic coefficients. It is found that the mechanical density fluctuation which is enhanced by the relativistic effects are attenuated, whereas the entropy fluctuation in turn becomes more prominent around the QCD CP. This attenuation of the density fluctuation around the QCD CP may imply that if the suppression or even total disappearance of Mach cone is observed as the incident energy of the relativistic heavy-ion collisionsis lowered, it can be a signal of the existence of the QCD critical point.

## Key claim

The mechanical density fluctuation (sound mode) is attenuated near the QCD critical point, while the entropy fluctuation becomes more prominent; this attenuation may imply that suppression or disappearance of Mach cone formation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions as the incident energy is lowered could serve as a signal for the existence of the QCD critical point.

## Method

The authors analyzed dynamical density fluctuations around the QCD critical point using relativistic fluid dynamics for a viscous fluid, employing the Landau (energy frame), Eckart (particle frame), Tsumura-Kunihiro-Ohnishi (TKO, particle frame), and Israel-Stewart (second-order) equations. They derived the dynamical structure factor (spectral function) of the density fluctuation for each equation and examined its behavior near the critical point using static and dynamic scaling laws for the liquid-gas transition universality class.

## Key numbers

* The specific heat at constant density diverges as \(\tilde{c}_n = c_0 t^{-\tilde{\alpha}}\) with \(\tilde{\alpha} \sim 0.11\), where \(t = |(T - T_c)/T_c|\) is the reduced temperature.
* The isothermal compressibility diverges as \(K_T = K_0 t^{-\tilde{\gamma}}\) with \(\tilde{\gamma} \sim 1.2\).
* The correlation length diverges as \(\xi = \xi_0 t^{-\nu}\) with \(\nu \sim 0.63\).
* The bulk viscosity diverges as \(\zeta = \zeta_0 t^{-a_{\zeta}}\) with \(a_{\zeta} \sim 1.8\) (from \(a_{\zeta} = z\nu - \tilde{\alpha}\) with dynamical critical exponent \(z \sim 3\)).
* The thermal conductivity diverges as \(\kappa = \kappa_0 t^{-a_{\kappa}}\) with \(a_{\kappa} \sim 0.63\).
* The width of the Rayleigh peak behaves as \(\Gamma_{\rm R} \sim t^{\tilde{\gamma} - a_{\kappa}}\).
* The width of the Brillouin peaks for Landau and Israel-Stewart equations behaves as \(\Gamma_{\rm B} \sim (\zeta_0 / 2 w_0) t^{-a_{\zeta}}\).
* The ratio of specific heats diverges as \(\gamma = \tilde{c}_p / \tilde{c}_n \sim t^{-\tilde{\gamma} + \tilde{\alpha}} \rightarrow \infty\) in the critical region.
* The spectral function was calculated for \(k = 0.1\) [1/fm], \(\mu_0 = 200\) [MeV], \(T_0 = 200\) [MeV], \(\eta/(n_0 s_0) = \zeta/(n_0 s_0) = 0.3\), and \(\kappa T_0/(n_0 s_0) = 0.6\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of dynamical density fluctuations at the QCD critical point, which belongs to the same universality class (class H of Hohenberg-Halperin) as the liquid-gas phase transition, as established by Fujii (2004) and Son (2004). The authors build on earlier work by Kunihiro (1991) showing that the scalar-vector cross term couples the sigma mode to the density-density correlator at finite chemical potential, and by Kunihiro (2000) and Hatta (2002) on diverging density fluctuations. The analysis uses the critical behavior of transport coefficients, particularly the singular bulk viscosity argued by Moore (2007) based on Onuki's work (1997) on the liquid-gas transition. The results enable predictions for experimental signals of the QCD critical point through possible suppression of Mach cone formation in heavy-ion collisions as the incident energy is lowered from \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV at RHIC.
