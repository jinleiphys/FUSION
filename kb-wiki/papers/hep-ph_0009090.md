# Formation of Quarkonium States at RHIC

> At RHIC the cross section for c-cbar production will be large enough such that approximately 10 pairs will be produced in each central collision. If a region of deconfined quarks and gluons is subsequently formed, one would expect that the mobility of the charm quarks will enable them to form J/Psi through "off-diagonal" combinations, involving a quark and an antiquark which were originally produced in separate incoherent interactions. We present model estimates of this effect, which indicate that the signal for deconfinement at RHIC may possibly be J/Psi enhancement rather than suppression.

# Formation of Quarkonium States at RHIC

## Key claim

If a deconfined quark-gluon plasma is formed at RHIC, the mobility of charm quarks from multiple independent production pairs could enable J/ψ formation through "off-diagonal" combinations, potentially producing an **enhancement** of J/ψ yields rather than the suppression traditionally expected, but this result depends critically on the average number of initially-produced charm quark pairs being substantially above unity (approximately 10 per central collision) and on the efficiency of the new formation mechanism during the deconfinement period.

## Method

The authors model J/ψ production and dissociation in a deconfined medium as an ideal gas of free gluons and light quarks undergoing isentropic longitudinal expansion, with thermal equilibrium momentum distributions for both gluons and charm quarks. They solve a kinetic rate equation coupling J/ψ formation (via capture of a free charm quark and antiquark with gluon emission) and dissociation (via gluon collisions), using cross sections from the operator product expansion with detailed balance, and parameterize the final J/ψ population as a quadratic function of the number of initially-unbound charm quark pairs.

## Key numbers

- Average number of charm quark pairs per central RHIC collision: **10**, from a pQCD estimate.
- Fitted β values (quadratic formation parameter) for J/ψ: **2.0 – 2.6 × 10⁻³**, relatively insensitive to initial temperature in the range 300–500 MeV.
- Predicted enhancement factor S_J for J/ψ production: **3.6 – 5.4**, for initial temperatures between 300 and 500 MeV.
- Final temperature for end of deconfining phase: **150 MeV**.
- Thermalization time τ₀: **0.5 fm**; initial volume V₀ = πR²τ₀ with nuclear radius **R = 6 fm**.
- Initial temperature range explored: **300 MeV < T₀ < 500 MeV**.
- Order-of-magnitude estimate for fraction x of initially-bound charm pairs: **10⁻²**, reduced by a factor of **0.6** for central collisions due to nuclear absorption.

## Context

This work extends the original proposal of Matsui and Satz (1986) that J/ψ suppression via color screening would signal deconfinement, and the subsequent gluon dissociation model of Kharzeev and collaborators. The authors adapt their previous model for B_c meson formation (Thews, Schroedter, Rafelski) to J/ψ production at RHIC, where multiple charm pairs become available. The key new prediction, that J/ψ enhancement rather than suppression could be the deconfinement signal, directly contradicts the standard suppression expectation from CERN SPS experiments (NA50 collaboration) and opens the possibility of experimentally "tuning" the number of initial heavy quark pairs by varying collision centrality or energy to search for nonlinear J/ψ production in total charm.
