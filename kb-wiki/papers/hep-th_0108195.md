# Effective lagrangians for QCD at high density

> We describe low energy physics in the CFL and LOFF phases by means of effective lagrangians. In the CFL case we present also how to derive expressions for the parameters appearing in the lagrangian via weak coupling calculations taking advantage of the dimensional reduction of fermion physics around the Fermi surface. The Goldstone boson of the LOFF phase turns out to be a phonon satisfying an anisotropic dispersion relation.

## Key claim

The paper describes low-energy effective lagrangians for the Color-Flavor Locked (CFL) and LOFF phases of QCD at high density, with the hedge that the effective description is valid only at energies much below the gap energy, and that the LOFF phase exists only for a specific window of chemical potential differences between quark flavors.

## Method

The author constructs effective lagrangians for the CFL phase by introducing Goldstone fields as phases of the condensates in the (3-bar, 3-bar) channel, using gauge-invariant fields to describe the 10 Goldstone bosons from the symmetry breaking pattern. For the LOFF phase, the author introduces a phonon field to restore broken translational and rotational invariance, and derives an effective lagrangian with a tower of spatial derivative terms. The parameters in the CFL effective lagrangian are evaluated via weak-coupling calculations using dimensional reduction of fermion physics around the Fermi surface, where only states near the Fermi surface are retained and the decoupling of negative-energy states is exploited.

## Key numbers

* The gap parameter for the CFL phase is denoted by Δ, with the gluon physical masses found to be of order 1.70Δ.
* The decay constant for the Goldstone bosons in the CFL phase is F_T^2 = μ^2(21 - 8 ln 2)/(36π^2).
* The pion velocity in the CFL phase is v^2 = 1/3, and the same velocity applies to all Nambu-Goldstone bosons including those from U(1)_V and U(1)_A breaking.
* For the LOFF phase with a point-like four-fermi coupling, the window for crystalline color superconductivity is δμ_1 = 0.71Δ and δμ_2 = 0.744Δ, for μ = 0.4 GeV and Δ = 40 MeV.
* The Debye and Meissner masses for gluons in the CFL phase are m_D^2 = g_s^2 F_T^2 and m_M^2 = (1/3) m_D^2.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to color superconductivity, building on the original ideas of Bardeen, Cooper, and Schrieffer applied to QCD at high density as developed by Barrois (1977) and later by Rajagopal and Wilczek (1999). The CFL phase was first described by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999), and the LOFF phase was originally studied by Larkin and Ovchinnikov (1964) and Fulde and Ferrell (1964) in the context of electron superconductivity with magnetic impurities. The effective lagrangian formalism enables the study of quark-hadron continuity between the CFL phase and nuclear matter, and provides a framework for computing properties of Goldstone bosons and gluons in dense QCD matter.
