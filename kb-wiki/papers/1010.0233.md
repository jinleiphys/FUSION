# Flow analysis with cumulants: direct calculations

> Anisotropic flow measurements in heavy-ion collisions provide important information on the properties of hot and dense matter. These measurements are based on analysis of azimuthal correlations and might be biased by contributions from correlations that are not related to the initial geometry, so called non-flow. To improve anisotropic flow measurements advanced methods based on multi-particle correlations (cumulants) have been developed to suppress non-flow contribution. These multi-particle correlations can be calculated by looping over all possible multiplets, however this quickly becomes prohibitively CPU intensive. Therefore, the most used technique for cumulant calculations is based on generating functions. This method involves approximations, and has its own biases, which complicates the interpretation of the results. In this paper we present a new exact method for direct calculations of multi-particle cumulants using moments of the flow vectors.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new exact method for calculating multi-particle cumulants directly from moments of flow vectors, avoiding the approximations and biases of the generating function approach while maintaining linear computational scaling with multiplicity.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for multi-particle azimuthal correlations in terms of Q-vectors (sums of complex exponentials of particle azimuthal angles) by decomposing powers of |Q_n|^2 and separating terms with distinct index combinations. For 4-particle correlations, they include previously neglected terms involving Q-vectors evaluated at different harmonics (such as |Q_{2n}|^2 and Re[Q_{2n}Q_n^*Q_n^*]) which cancel interference between harmonics. The method is generalized to differential flow analysis using separate p-vectors for particles of interest and q-vectors for particles labeled both as reference flow particles and particles of interest.

## Key numbers

- Simulated events with multiplicity M=500, v_2=0.05 and v_4=0.1: direct cumulants (v_2{k,QC}) are unaffected by v_4 interference, while Lee-Yang Zero's Sum method (v_2{LYZS}) and fitting of Q-distribution method (v_2{FQD}) show clear bias
- 10^7 simulated events with v_2=0.05 for a detector with two large acceptance "holes": acceptance-corrected direct cumulants agree with Monte Carlo estimate, while uncorrected estimates are strongly biased
- Therminator simulation results: p_t-integrated flow of protons shows 2nd order cumulant biased by nonflow while higher order cumulants agree with Monte Carlo

## Context

This work extends the cumulant method for anisotropic flow analysis originally developed by Borghini, Dinh, and Ollitrault (2000-2001), which used generating functions with interpolation formulae that introduced numerical uncertainties. The new direct method avoids these approximations and also corrects the bias from interference between different harmonics that affected the earlier Q-vector moment approach of Borghini (2000). The method has been used for real data analysis by the STAR and ALICE Collaborations, and enables exact, fast computation of cumulants up to at least 8th order without the tuning of interpolating parameters required by the generating function approach.
