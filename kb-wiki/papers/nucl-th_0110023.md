# Theoretical description of the double beta decay of 160Gd

> The half-life of the 2 neutrino double-beta-decay of 160Gd, a process which was previously reported as theoretically forbidden, is estimated by including the pairing interaction, which mixes different occupations and opens channels for the decay. Explicit expressions are presented for the pairing mixing, and for the 2 nu and 0 nu double-beta-decay nuclear matrix elements in the present pseudo SU(3) approach. The estimated double-beta-decay half-lives suggest that the planned experiments would succeed in detecting the 2 nu double-beta-decay in 160Gd, and in putting competitive limits for the zero neutrino mode.

## Key claim

The two-neutrino double-beta decay of ¹⁶⁰Gd, previously reported as theoretically forbidden in the pseudo SU(3) model due to selection rules, becomes allowed when the pairing interaction mixes configurations with different occupation numbers, opening new decay channels; the estimated half-lives suggest planned experiments could detect the 2ν mode and set competitive limits on the 0ν mode.

## Method

The authors used the pseudo SU(3) shell model to describe the ground states of ¹⁶⁰Gd and ¹⁶⁰Dy, including the pairing interaction to mix configurations with different numbers of nucleons in normal-parity and abnormal-parity (intruder) orbitals. They calculated the 2νββ and 0νββ nuclear matrix elements first in a simplified one-component model for the initial state and a two-component model for the final state, then in an enlarged model space with four configurations for each nucleus, diagonalizing the pairing-induced mixing matrix. The 2νββ matrix element was evaluated using a summation method that exploits the commutation of the Gamow-Teller operator with the SU(3) Hamiltonian, while the 0νββ matrix element employed a closure approximation with a neutrino potential.

## Key numbers

- The pairing strength parameters used were G_π = 21/A = 0.132 MeV and G_ν = 17/A = 0.106 MeV for ¹⁶⁰Gd.
- The deformation parameter assumed for ¹⁶⁰Gd was ε = 0.26, taken from Moller (1995).
- The energy ΔE to promote a pair of protons from normal to intruder orbitals in ¹⁶⁰Gd was estimated as 0.54 MeV, 0.81 MeV, and 1.35 MeV for different configurations; for ¹⁶⁰Dy the corresponding values were 0.54 MeV, 1.71 MeV, and 2.25 MeV.
- The dominant component of the ¹⁶⁰Gd ground state wave function had amplitude 0.7253 in the enlarged model space (configuration i₁ with 8 protons in normal and 6 in intruder orbitals, and 8 neutrons in normal and 6 in intruder orbitals).
- The dominant component of the ¹⁶⁰Dy ground state wave function had amplitude 0.7639 in the enlarged model space (configuration f₁ with 10 protons in normal and 6 in intruder orbitals, and 8 neutrons in normal and 4 in intruder orbitals).

## Context

This work extends the pseudo SU(3) model calculations of double-beta decay half-lives previously performed by the same group for six heavy deformed nuclei (Cas94, Hir95a, Hir95b, Hir95c, Hir95d), which successfully reproduced experimental data for ¹⁵⁰Nd and ²³⁸U. The earlier model predicted complete suppression of ββ decay for ¹⁶⁰Gd and four other nuclei based on selection rules; the present inclusion of pairing mixing removes that suppression. The results enable experimental groups planning direct-counting experiments for ¹⁶⁰Gd to have theoretical half-life estimates for both the 2ν and 0ν modes, and the authors note that the strong cancellation of the 2ν mode could reduce background for 0ν detection, as argued by Dan (2000).
