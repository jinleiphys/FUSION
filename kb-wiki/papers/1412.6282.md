# Baryon onset in a magnetic field

> The critical baryon chemical potential for the onset of nuclear matter is a function of the vacuum mass and the binding energy. Both quantities are affected by an external magnetic field. We show within two relativistic mean-field models - including magnetic catalysis, but omitting the anomalous magnetic moment - that a magnetic field increases both the vacuum mass and the binding energy. For sufficiently large magnetic fields, the effect on the vacuum mass dominates and as a result the critical baryon chemical potential is increased.

## Key claim

The critical baryon chemical potential for the onset of nuclear matter in a magnetic field is determined by two competing effects: the vacuum mass increases due to magnetic catalysis, and the binding energy also increases; for sufficiently large magnetic fields, the effect on the vacuum mass dominates and the critical chemical potential is increased, but this result omits the anomalous magnetic moment and is model-dependent.

## Method

The authors use two relativistic mean-field models, the Walecka model and an extended linear sigma model with a chiral partner of the nucleon, evaluated in the mean-field approximation at zero temperature. They include the Dirac sea contribution (responsible for magnetic catalysis) via the proper time regularization of the free energy for charged fermions in a homogeneous magnetic field, while omitting the anomalous magnetic moment. The critical chemical potential for the baryon onset is found by extremizing the free energy with respect to meson condensates and requiring equal free energies of the vacuum and nuclear matter phases.

## Key numbers

- At vanishing magnetic field, the critical chemical potential is set to 922.7 MeV by construction, with a nucleon vacuum mass of 939 MeV and binding energy of -16.3 MeV.
- The saturation density used for parameter fitting is n0 = 0.153 fm^{-3}, with binding energy E_bind = -16.3 MeV, compression modulus K = 250 MeV, and effective mass M_N = 0.8 m_N (m_N = 939 MeV).
- De Haas-van Alphen oscillations appear at small magnetic fields, qB less than approximately 0.03 GeV^2, before all nucleons occupy the lowest Landau level at larger fields.
- Compact star magnetic fields up to 10^{15} G at the surface and 10^{18-20} G in the interior are cited as relevant phenomenological context.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the QCD phase diagram in a magnetic field, which had shown that the chiral crossover temperature decreases with B (Bali 2011) and that magnetic catalysis increases the chiral condensate and quark masses (Klimenko 1990, Gusynin 1994, Shovkovy 2012). Previous calculations of magnetized nuclear matter included the anomalous magnetic moment but omitted the vacuum Dirac sea contribution (Broderick 2001, Sinha 2010, Rabhi 2011, Dexheimer 2011, Preis 2011, Dong 2013, de Lima 2013, Casali 2013). The present work incorporates magnetic catalysis into dense nuclear matter for the first time, but the authors note that future studies should include both magnetic catalysis and the anomalous magnetic moment, which is expected to counteract the observed effect (Preis 2010, Andreichikov 2013). The results are not directly applicable to compact star interiors due to the focus on the baryon onset rather than high densities, but the method may inform future equation-of-state calculations for magnetized neutron stars.
