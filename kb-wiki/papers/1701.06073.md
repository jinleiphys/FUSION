# Parton energy loss in QCD matter

> QCD jets, produced copiously in heavy-ion collisions at LHC and also at RHIC, serve as probes of the dynamics of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). Jet fragmentation in the medium is interesting in its own right and, in order to extract pertinent information about the QGP, it has to be well understood. We present a brief overview of the physics involved and argue that jet substructure observables provide new opportunities for understanding the nature of the modifications.

## Key claim

Jet substructure observables, particularly the SoftDrop grooming procedure, offer a new handle to directly measure medium-induced gluon radiation and disentangle the effects of colour coherence in jet fragmentation, rather than merely being sensitive to integrated energy loss. The paper argues that the interplay between coherent and incoherent energy loss of sub-jets leads to qualitatively different predictions for the splitting probability distribution.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework for radiative parton energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma, focusing on the Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigne-Schiff (BDMPS) formalism for medium-induced gluon radiation and the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect. It then analyses the two-gluon emission rate in a dense medium to study colour coherence effects, distinguishing between the collinear limit (where the hard gluon formation time is long) and the soft limit (where the soft gluon formation time is long). Finally, it applies the SoftDrop grooming procedure to two extreme scenarios: one where the whole jet is unresolved by the medium (coherent energy loss) and one where all sub-jets are resolved and lose energy independently (incoherent energy loss).

## Key numbers

* For a medium of length L = 4 fm, jet quenching parameter q̂ = 1 GeV²/fm, and coupling ᾱ = 0.3, the characteristic BDMPS energy scale is ω_c = 80 GeV and the multiple-branching scale is ω_s = 7 GeV.
* For the same parameters, the range of BDMPS emission angles is 0.025 < θ_BDMPS < 0.28, estimated from momentum broadening as θ ∼ √(q̂ L)/ω.
* The decoherence parameter for a dipole is Δ_decoh = 1 - exp[-(L/t_d)³], where the decoherence time t_d = [12/(q̂ θ_H²)]^(1/3) and θ_H is the emission angle of the hard gluon.
* The anomalous dimension for the running of q̂ with medium size is γ = 2√ᾱ, giving q̂(L) ∝ L^γ for a large medium.

## Context

This review extends the BDMPS formalism for radiative energy loss (Baier 1997, Zakharov 1997) and the first-order-in-opacity approach (Gyulassy 2000, Wiedemann 2000) by incorporating recent calculations of two-gluon emission rates (Arnold 2015, 2016) and colour coherence effects (Mehtar-Tani 2010, 2011; Casalderrey-Solana 2011). It builds on the decoherence parameter approach (Casalderrey-Solana 2012) and applies it to the SoftDrop grooming technique (Larkoski 2014, 2015), enabling future comparisons with experimental jet substructure data from LHC and RHIC and with Monte Carlo generators for jets in heavy-ion collisions (Zapp 2012, Casalderrey-Solana 2016).
