# Stochastic fluctuations in relativistic fluids: causality, stability, and the information current

> We develop a general formalism for introducing stochastic fluctuations around thermodynamic equilibrium which takes into account, for the first time, recent developments on the causality and stability properties of relativistic hydrodynamic theories. The method is valid for any covariantly stable theory of relativistic viscous fluid dynamics derived from a covariant maximum entropy principle. We illustrate the formalism with some applications, showing how it could be used to consistently introduce fluctuations in a model of relativistic heat diffusion, and in conformally invariant Israel-Stewart theory in a general hydrodynamic frame. The latter example is used to study the hydrodynamic frame dependence of the symmetric two-point function of fluctuations of the energy-momentum tensor.

## Key claim

The paper develops a general formalism for introducing stochastic fluctuations around thermodynamic equilibrium in relativistic fluids that, for the first time, incorporates recent constraints from causality and stability; the method is valid only for theories derived from a covariant maximum entropy principle, and the resulting noise correlators are frame-dependent in general hydrodynamic frames.

## Method

The authors generalize the Fox-Uhlenbeck approach to relativistic systems by using the thermodynamic information current \(E^\mu\) introduced by Gavassino (2021), which determines the probability distribution for fluctuations around equilibrium via \(w \propto \exp(-\int_\Sigma d\Sigma\, n_\mu E^\mu)\). They then match the long-time limit of the conditional probability distribution from linearized stochastic equations of motion to this thermodynamic distribution to extract the noise correlators. The formalism is applied to a relativistic heat diffusion model and to conformally invariant Israel-Stewart theory in a general hydrodynamic frame.

## Key numbers

* For the Landau-Lifshitz first-order theory, the information current contains no term proportional to \(\delta\sigma_{\mu\nu}\delta\sigma^{\mu\nu}\), leading to violations of the Gibbs stability criterion when the Knudsen number \(\mathrm{Kn} = \ell/L \gtrsim 1\), where \(\ell = 3\eta/(4\epsilon)\) is the microscopic scale.
* In the local rest frame (\(n^\mu = -u^\mu\)), the Landau-Lifshitz information current reduces to \(E_{\mathrm{Landau}} = \frac{1}{8\epsilon T} \delta\epsilon^2 + \frac{2\epsilon}{3T} \delta u^\nu \delta u_\nu\), which is positive semi-definite and hides the instability present in other frames.
* The entropy production in Landau-Lifshitz theory is \(T\,\partial_\mu s^\mu = 2\eta\,\sigma_{\mu\nu}\sigma^{\mu\nu}\), which is positive semi-definite for \(\eta>0\) even for arbitrarily large gradients.

## Context

This work extends the deterministic causality and stability results of Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha, and Kovtun (2020) and Gavassino (2021) into the stochastic domain, providing a framework to include thermal fluctuations in causal relativistic hydrodynamics. It contrasts with prior stochastic formulations based on acausal Landau-Lifshitz or Eckart theories, which exhibit frame-dependent runaway fluctuations as shown by Gavassino (2021). The formalism enables consistent computation of spacelike-separated correlation functions in general hydrodynamic frames, with applications to heavy-ion collision phenomenology and critical dynamics.
