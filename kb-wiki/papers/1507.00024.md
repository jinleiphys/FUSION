# Bottomonium Mesons and Strategies for Their Observation

> The $B$-factories and Large Hadron Collider experiments have demonstrated the ability to observe and measure the properties of bottomonium mesons. In order to discover missing states it is useful to know their properties to develop a successful search strategy. To this end we calculate the masses and decay properties of excited bottomonium states. We use the relativized quark model to calculate the masses and wavefunctions and the $^3P_0$ quark-pair creation model to calculate decay widths to open bottom. We also summarize results for radiative transitions, annihilation decays, hadronic transitions and production cross sections which are used to develop strategies to find these states. We find that the $b\bar{b}$ system has a rich spectroscopy that we expect to be substantially extended by the LHC and $e^+e^-$ experiments in the near future. Some of the most promising possibilities at the LHC are observing the $χ_{b(1,2)}(3P)$, $χ_{b(1,2)}(4P)$ and $η_b(3S)$ states in $γμ^+μ^-$ final states that proceed via radiative transitions through $Υ(nS)$ intermediate states and $1^3D_J$ and $2^3D_J$ into $γγμ^+μ^-$ final states proceeding via $1^3P_J\to 1^3S_1$ and $2^3P_J\to 2^3S_1$ intermediate states respectively. Some of the most interesting possibilities in $e^+e^-$ collisions are studying the $1^3D_J$ states via $4γ$ cascades starting with the $Υ(3S)$ and the $3^3P_J$ states in $γγμ^+ μ^-$ final states starting with the $Υ(4S)$ and proceeding via $Υ(nS)$ intermediate states. Completing the bottomonium spectrum is an important validation of lattice QCD calculations and a test of our understanding of bottomonium states in the context of the quark model.

## Key claim

The paper calculates masses and decay properties of excited bottomonium states using the relativized quark model and the $^3P_0$ quark-pair creation model, but the authors caution that due to neglected coupled channel effects and the crudeness of the relativization procedure, the mass predictions should not be expected to be accurate to better than approximately 10-20 MeV.

## Method

The authors used the relativized quark model to calculate masses and wavefunctions for bottomonium states, incorporating a relativistic kinetic energy term, a Lorentz vector one-gluon-exchange interaction with a QCD-motivated running coupling constant, and a Lorentz scalar linear confining interaction. They then used the $^3P_0$ quark-pair creation model with simple harmonic oscillator wavefunctions (with oscillator parameters fitted to match the rms radii of the relativized quark model wavefunctions) to calculate strong decay widths to open bottom. The results for radiative transitions, annihilation decays, hadronic transitions, and production cross sections were combined to estimate branching ratios and develop experimental search strategies for LHC and $e^+e^-$ collisions.

## Key numbers

- Predicted $\Upsilon(1^3S_1)$ mass: 9465 MeV (experimental: 9460.30 $\pm$ 0.26 MeV)
- Predicted $\chi_{b1}(3^3P_1)$ mass: 10538 MeV (experimental from LHCb: 10515.7$^{+2.2+1.5}_{-3.9-2.1}$ MeV)
- Predicted total width of $\Upsilon(4^3S_1)$: 22.0 MeV (experimental: 20.5 $\pm$ 2.5 MeV)
- Predicted total width of $\Upsilon(5^3S_1)$: 27.4 MeV (experimental: 55 $\pm$ 28 MeV)
- Predicted total width of $\Upsilon(6^3S_1)$: 33.9 MeV (experimental: 79 $\pm$ 16 MeV)
- Predicted $\chi_{b2}(1^3P_2)$ radiative width to $\Upsilon(1^3S_1)\gamma$: 32.8 keV (experimental BR: 19.1 $\pm$ 1.2%)
- Predicted $\chi_{b1}(1^3P_1)$ radiative width to $\Upsilon(1^3S_1)\gamma$: 29.5 keV (experimental BR: 33.9 $\pm$ 2.2%)
- Predicted $\chi_{b0}(1^3P_0)$ total width: 2.6 MeV (dominated by $gg$ decay)
- Predicted $\eta_b(1^1S_0)$ total width: 16.6 MeV (experimental: 10.8$^{+6.0}_{-4.2}$ MeV)
- Predicted $\Upsilon(5^3S_1)$ decay to $BB^*$: 16.6 MeV width (60.6% BR)
- Predicted $\Upsilon(6^3S_1)$ decay to $BB(1P_1^\prime)$: 10.8 MeV width (31.8% BR)

## Context

This work extends the relativized quark model calculations previously applied by Godfrey and collaborators to meson spectroscopy (Godfrey 1985) and uses the $^3P_0$ model approach developed by Micu (1968) and Le Yaouanc (1972) that has been applied to hadron decays by Barnes (2005) and others. The paper provides a comprehensive set of predictions for missing bottomonium states to guide experimental searches at the LHC and Belle II, motivated by recent lattice QCD calculations (Lewis 2012, Dowdall 2013) that require experimental validation. The results enable searches for states such as $\chi_{b(1,2)}(3P)$, $\chi_{b(1,2)}(4P)$, and $\eta_b(3S)$ at the LHC via $\gamma\mu^+\mu^-$ final states, and studies of $1^3D_J$ states in $e^+e^-$ collisions via $4\gamma$ cascades from $\Upsilon(3S)$.
