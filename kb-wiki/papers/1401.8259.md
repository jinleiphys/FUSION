# Nuclear Physics Review

> Anchoring low-energy nuclear physics to the fundamental theory of strong interactions remains an outstanding challenge. I review the current progress and challenges of the endeavor to use lattice QCD to bridge this connection. This is a particularly exciting time for this line of research as demonstrated by the spike in the number of different collaborative efforts focussed on this problem and presented at this conference. I first digress and discuss the 2013 Ken Wilson Award.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations of the nucleon mass splitting \(M_n - M_p\) now show striking evidence of non-analytic light-quark mass dependence (chiral logarithms) in the nucleon spectrum, but only when using a symmetric isospin-breaking scheme that cancels problematic \(m_\pi^3\) terms; this contrasts with earlier nucleon mass fits that required \(g_A \sim 0\) or NNLO corrections to achieve convergence.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD calculations of the nucleon mass and its isospin splitting, using chiral perturbation theory (\(\chi\)PT) to fit lattice results. For the nucleon mass splitting, a symmetric valence-quark isospin breaking scheme is employed (\(m_u^{val} = m_l - \delta\), \(m_d^{val} = m_l + \delta\) with degenerate sea quarks) to improve chiral behavior. Preliminary results use anisotropic clover-Wilson ensembles from the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration (HSC) at three values of \(\delta\) and three light-quark masses corresponding to \(m_\pi \simeq \{244, 426, 498\}\) MeV, fitted with the NNLO \(\chi\)PT formula for \(\delta M_{n-p}\).

## Key numbers

* Nucleon mass from NNLO \(\chi\)PT fit to LHPC results: \(M_N = 941 \pm 42 \pm 17\) MeV, with the lightest pion mass \(m_\pi \sim 300\) MeV.
* "Ruler" approximation for nucleon mass: \(M_N[\text{MeV}] = 800 + m_\pi\), yielding \(\sigma_{\pi N} = 67 \pm 4\) MeV.
* Experimental neutron-proton mass difference: \(M_n - M_p = 1.29333217(42)\) MeV.
* Cottingham formula estimate of electromagnetic self-energy: \(\delta M^\gamma_{p-n} = 1.30 \pm 0.03 \pm 0.47\) MeV.
* Lattice average of strong isospin-breaking contribution: \(\delta M_{n-p}^{m_d - m_u} = 2.39(21)\) MeV.
* Combined prediction of electromagnetic contribution from lattice and experiment: \(\delta M^\gamma_{p-n} = 1.10 \pm 0.21\) MeV.
* Fit of \(\delta M_{n-p}^{m_d - m_u}\) to \(\chi\)PT formula with \(g_A = 1.27\) fixed yields \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 1.34/4 = 0.33\); with \(g_A\) free, fit gives \(g_A^{fit} = 1.5(3)\).
* Primordial baryon-to-photon ratio from CMB: \(\eta = 6.23(17) \times 10^{-10}\).

## Context

This review extends the author's earlier work with the LHPC Collaboration (Walker-Loud 2008) on nucleon mass chiral extrapolation and the "ruler" plot, and updates results presented at Chiral Dynamics 2012. The new symmetric isospin-breaking scheme improves upon prior lattice calculations of \(\delta M_{n-p}^{m_d - m_u}\) (summarized with a FLAG-like color scheme) and enables first-principles connections to Big Bang Nucleosynthesis, allowing constraints on variation of \(m_d - m_u\). The work also reviews the Luscher method and the HAL QCD potential method for extracting scattering phase shifts from finite-volume lattice QCD, comparing results for the \(I=2\) \(\pi\pi\) system (NPLQCD 2011, Dudek 2012) and the H-dibaryon (NPLQCD 2010, HAL QCD 2010).
