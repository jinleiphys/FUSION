# Neutrino-induced coherent pion production off nuclei reexamined

> It is pointed out that so far all theoretical estimates of coherent pion production off nuclei induced by neutrinos rely on the 'local approximation' well known in photonuclear physics. The effects of dropping this approximation are discussed. It is found that in a plane wave approximation for the pion the local approximation overestimates the coherent neutrino-induced pion production on nuclei.

## Key claim

All previous theoretical estimates of neutrino-induced coherent pion production rely on the "local approximation," which factorizes the nuclear form factor out of the production amplitude; dropping this approximation shows that the local approximation overestimates the cross section, with errors reaching up to 100% in the pion momentum distribution at lower neutrino energies, even when both calculations use a plane wave approximation for the pion.

## Method

The authors compare a full calculation, where the hadronic current for coherent pion production is summed over all occupied nucleon states in a mean-field model for ¹²C with a propagating Δ(1232) resonance, against the local approximation, which freezes the initial nucleon momentum in the Δ propagator and vertex to factor out the nuclear form factor. Both calculations use the same relativistic mean-field nuclear structure and a plane wave approximation for the outgoing pion, with no pion final-state interactions, to isolate the effect of the local approximation. The single-nucleon current includes the standard electroweak vertex with vector and axial contributions, and the Δ propagator uses the free Rarita-Schwinger form without in-medium modifications.

## Key numbers

- At a neutrino energy of 1 GeV, the local approximation gives a charged-current pion angular distribution that is about 20% larger than the full calculation at very forward angles.
- At a neutrino energy of 500 MeV, the local approximation overestimates the charged-current pion angular distribution by a factor of approximately 2 at zero degrees.
- For a neutrino energy of 1 GeV, the local approximation overestimates the charged-current pion momentum distribution by about 70% at the peak.
- For charged-current at 500 MeV, the full calculation yields a peak value less than 1/2 of the local approximation result.
- The discrepancy in the pion momentum distribution decreases with neutrino energy: more than a factor of 2 at 500 MeV, about 1.7 at 1 GeV, and less at 2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the scrutiny of the local approximation, previously examined for photon-induced coherent π⁰ and η production by Peters et al. (1998) and Tiator et al. (1984), to neutrino-induced coherent pion production, which had not been tested before. The results directly contradict the assumption used in both PCAC-based models (e.g., Berger and Sehgal, 2008) and nuclear-structure-based models (e.g., Amaro et al., 2008) that the local approximation is accurate for neutrino reactions. The findings motivate future full calculations that include pion final-state interactions and in-medium Δ modifications to definitively resolve the overestimate.
