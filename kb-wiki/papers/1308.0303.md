# Kerman-Onishi conditions in self-consistent tilted-axis-cranking mean-field calculations

> \item[Background] For cranked mean-field calculations with arbitrarily oriented rotational frequency vector $\boldsymbolω$ in the intrinsic frame, one has to employ constraints on average values of the quadrupole-moment tensor, so as to keep the nucleus in the principal-axis reference frame. Kerman and Onishi [Nucl. Phys. A {\bf 361}, 179 (1981)] have shown that the Lagrangian multipliers that correspond to the required constraints are proportional to $\boldsymbolω \times \boldsymbol{J}$, where $\boldsymbol{J}$ is the average angular momentum vector. \item[Purpose] We study the validity and consequences of the Kerman-Onishi conditions in the context of self-consistent tilted-axis-cranking (TAC) mean-field calculations. \item[Methods] We perform a two-dimensional self-consistent calculations (with and without pairing) utilizing the symmetry-unrestricted solver {\sc hfodd}. At each tilting angle, we compare the calculated values of quadrupole-moment-tensor Lagrangian multipliers and $\boldsymbolω \times \boldsymbol{J}$. \item[Results] We show that in self-consistent calculations, the Kerman-Onishi conditions are obeyed with high precision. Small deviations seen in the calculations with pairing can be attributed to the truncation of the quasiparticle spectrum. We also provide results of systematic TAC calculations for triaxial strongly deformed bands in $^{160}$Yb. \item[Conclusions] For non-stationary TAC solutions, Kerman-Onishi conditions link the non-zero values of the angle between rotational-frequency and angular-momentum vectors to the constraints on off-diagonal components of the quadrupole-moment tensor. To stabilize the convergence of self-consistent iterations, such constraints have to be taken into account. Only then one can determine the Routhian surfaces as functions of the tilting angles.

## Key claim

The Kerman-Onishi (KO) conditions, which relate the Lagrange multipliers constraining off-diagonal quadrupole moments to the cross product of rotational frequency and angular momentum, are obeyed with high precision in self-consistent tilted-axis-cranking (TAC) mean-field calculations based on realistic energy density functionals (EDFs), including when pairing is included; small deviations seen with pairing are attributable to truncation of the quasiparticle spectrum, not to a failure of the KO conditions themselves.

## Method

The authors performed two-dimensional self-consistent TAC calculations using the symmetry-unrestricted solver hfodd, with the rotational axis allowed to tilt from the principal axis in the x1-x2 plane. They compared the self-consistently obtained Lagrange multiplier L_{2-2} (which enforces the constraint ⟨Q_{2-2}⟩=0) with the value L'_{2-2} predicted by the KO condition L'_{2-2} = (ω₁j₂ − ω₂j₁)/(2Re⟨Q₂₂⟩), for triaxial bands in ¹⁵⁸Er (Skyrme-Hartree-Fock without pairing) and ¹¹⁰Mo (Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov with Lipkin-Nogami pairing). Systematic TAC calculations were also performed for five triaxial strongly deformed (TSD) configurations in ¹⁶⁰Yb.

## Key numbers

* For most tilting angles θ, the ratio L_{2-2}/L'_{2-2} stays inside the interval [0.999, 1.001], meaning the first three significant digits of L_{2-2} and L'_{2-2} are the same (tested for ¹⁵⁸Er and ¹¹⁰Mo).
* In ¹⁵⁸Er and ¹¹⁰Mo, the angle α between ω and J reaches at most 0.1° and 1°, respectively.
* For the SHFB+LN calculation at θ=40°, the ratio L_{2-2}/L'_{2-2} converges to a limit deviating from one by approximately 0.0006; this deviation is attributed to truncation of the quasiparticle spectrum at a cutoff energy E_cut=60 MeV.
* For ¹⁶⁰Yb, PAC calculations found three typical deformations: (Q_t, γ) ∼ (9 eb, 9°–14°) for TSD1, (Q_t, γ) ∼ (12.2–10.8 eb, −10°) for TSD2, and (Q_t, γ) ∼ (10.0–10.5 eb, 13°) for TSD3.
* TSD3 configurations in ¹⁶⁰Yb become lower in energy than TSD1 and TSD2 already at ω ≈ 0.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the original derivation of Kerman and Onishi (1981), which was based on a Hamiltonian and wave-function formalism, to the framework of Kohn-Sham density functional theory where no many-body Hamiltonian is explicitly defined. It provides the first fully self-consistent EDF calculations (including pairing) that strictly obey the KO conditions, going beyond earlier implementations that used phenomenological potentials or the pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian (Kerman and Onishi 1981; Nazarewicz 1992; Horiuchi et al. 1996; Oi et al. 1998, 2003). The results enable reliable interpretation of triaxial strongly deformed bands seen experimentally, such as those in ¹⁵⁸Er (Paul et al. 2007; Pattabiraman et al. 2007; Aguilar et al. 2008; TEA Collaboration 2008; Ollier et al. 2009, 2011; Wang et al. 2011) and ¹⁶⁰Yb, and demonstrate the existence of θ-soft rotational bands that require going beyond single-reference DFT to understand.
