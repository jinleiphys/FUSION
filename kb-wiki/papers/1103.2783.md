# Progress Report on Computing Excited-State Hadron Masses in Lattice QCD

> Our progress in computing the spectrum of excited baryons and mesons in lattice QCD is described. Sets of spatially-extended hadron operators with a variety of different momenta are used. A new method of stochastically estimating the low-lying effects of quark propagation is utilized which allows reliable determinations of temporal correlations of both single-hadron and multi-hadron operators. The method is tested on the isoscalar mesons in the scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector channels, and on the two-pion system of total isospin I=0,1,2.

## Key claim
The stochastic LapH method for estimating slice-to-slice quark propagators yields nearly an order of magnitude reduction in statistical error over previous methods, with a very mild volume dependence, making it feasible to compute temporal correlations of both single-hadron and multi-hadron operators at lighter pion masses.

## Method
The authors used anisotropic lattice QCD with \(N_f=2+1\) flavors of dynamical quarks, employing spatially-extended hadron operators constructed from covariantly-displaced, smeared quark fields. They introduced a new stochastic estimation scheme combining Laplacian Heaviside (LapH) quark-field smearing with diluted noise vectors restricted to the LapH subspace, using specific dilution schemes (TF, SF, LI8 for forward-time quark lines; TI16, SF, LI8 for same-sink-time quark lines). Correlation matrices were computed and analyzed via the generalized eigenvalue method to extract stationary-state energies.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass \(m_\pi \sim 400\) MeV used for operator selection on a \(16^3\times 128\) anisotropic lattice with spatial spacing \(a_s\sim 0.12\) fm and aspect ratio \(a_s/a_t\sim 3.5\), using 50 to 100 configurations.
- Pion mass \(m_\pi \sim 230\) MeV used for testing on a \(24^3\times 128\) anisotropic lattice with \(a_s\sim 0.12\) fm and \(a_s/a_t\sim 3.5\), using 198 configurations for isoscalar mesons and 584 configurations for two-pion systems.
- The stochastic LapH method produced "nearly an order of magnitude reduction in the statistical error over previous methods."
- For two-pion systems, results were obtained for total isospin \(I=0,1,2\) with zero total momentum, including \(S\)-wave (zero relative momentum) and \(P\)-wave (minimal non-zero on-axis relative momenta) states.

## Context
This work extends the excited-state hadron spectrum calculations reported by the same collaboration in spectrum2009 and wallace2010, which used two flavors of dynamical quarks. The new stochastic LapH method addresses the challenge of evaluating multi-hadron correlators at lighter pion masses, where more multi-hadron states lie below excited resonances. The approach enables future computations on larger \(24^3\times 128\) and \(32^3\times 256\) lattices at pion masses of 400 MeV and 230 MeV, with results for QCD stationary-state energies expected soon.
