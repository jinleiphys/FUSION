# Parton-medium cross section and average QGP viscosity in lead-lead collisions at LHC

> The recent experimental CMS and E-by-E ATLAS data on the elliptic anisotropy of particles in \PbPb collisions are used to extract parton medium cross-section and the quark-gluon plasma viscosity-to-entropy ratio within the incomplete equilibration medium model. Our method assumes extrapolation of the measured pseudorapidity spectrum of charged particles to low-$p_T$ range. Then the rapidity distribution of both charged and neutral hadrons is restored. The extracted value of the parton-medium cross section is found to be $\ds σ= (3.1 \pm 0.2) {\rm mb}$. It yields $η/s = 0.17 \pm 0.02$ for the ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density in QGP phase. The last result is in a good agreement with the estimates obtained by calculations within the viscous hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The authors extract the parton-medium cross section and QGP viscosity-to-entropy ratio from LHC data using the incomplete equilibration medium (IEM) model, finding results that agree with viscous hydrodynamic calculations, but the extraction relies on an extrapolation of the measured pseudorapidity spectrum of charged particles to the low-$p_T$ range (0 to 0.3 GeV/$c$) and conversion to rapidity distributions for both charged and neutral hadrons.

## Method

The authors fit the eccentricity-scaled elliptic flow $v_2/\varepsilon$ as a function of transverse particle density $n_T = (1/S)(dN/dy)|_{y=0}$ using the IEM model equation $v_2/\varepsilon = (v_2/\varepsilon)^{\rm max} \times n_T/(n_T + 1/(c_s \sigma K_0))$, with $c_s = 1/\sqrt{3}$ and $K_0 = 0.70 \pm 0.03$ from Monte-Carlo simulations. They use CMS event-plane data on $v_2$ for PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV, extrapolate the charged particle $p_T$ spectrum from $0.3-3.0$ GeV/$c$ down to $0$ GeV/$c$ using power-law and polynomial fits, convert $dN/d\eta$ to $dN/dy$ assuming pion mass, and obtain the total hadron yield as $dN/dy = 3/2 \times dN_{ch}/dy$ to account for neutral pions. The freeze-out temperature $T_{freez} = 166 \pm 3$ MeV is obtained from a fit of identified charged hadron yields using the Statistical Hadronization Model with UrQMD modification factors, and this temperature is used in the relation $\eta/s = 0.194 / (T^2 \sigma)$ to extract the viscosity-to-entropy ratio.

## Key numbers

- Parton-medium cross section: $\sigma = (3.1 \pm 0.2)$ mb, extracted from the fit of $v_2\{EP\}/\varepsilon_{part}$ distribution to the IEM model for PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV.
- Ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density: $\eta/s = 0.17 \pm 0.02$, corresponding to $4\pi(\eta/s) = 2.14 \pm 0.25$.
- Maximum eccentricity-scaled elliptic flow: $(v_2/\varepsilon)^{\rm max} = 0.58 \pm 0.03$ from the same fit.
- Chemical freeze-out temperature: $T_{freez} = 166 \pm 3$ MeV, obtained from fitting identified charged hadron yields in PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV using the Statistical Hadronization Model with UrQMD correction factors.

## Context

This work extends the IEM model analysis previously applied to RHIC data by Drescher (2007) for AuAu and CuCu collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, now applying it to LHC PbPb data at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV. The extracted $\eta/s = 0.17 \pm 0.02$ agrees with viscous hydrodynamic hybrid model calculations by Song (2011) which found $\eta/s \approx 0.16$ at RHIC and $\eta/s \approx 0.22 \pm 0.02$ at LHC, and with Gale (2012) who found $\eta/s \approx 0.2$ for LHC. The result is also consistent with the AdS/CFT lower bound of $4\pi\eta/s \geq 1$ and the upper limit of $\eta/s \lesssim 0.6$ from theoretical considerations. This enables extraction of QGP transport properties directly from experimental flow data without full hydrodynamic simulations.
