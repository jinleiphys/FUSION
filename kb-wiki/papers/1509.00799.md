# QED effects in the pseudoscalar meson sector

> We present results on the pseudoscalar meson masses from a fully dynamical simulation of QCD+QED. We concentrate particularly on violations of isospin symmetry. We calculate the $π^+$-$π^0$ splitting and also look at other isospin violating mass differences. We have presented results for these isospin splittings in arXiv:1508.06401 [hep-lat]. In this paper we give more details of the techniques employed, discussing in particular the question of how much of the symmetry violation is due to QCD, arising from the different masses of the $u$ and $d$ quarks, and how much is due to QED, arising from the different charges of the quarks. This decomposition is not unique, it depends on the renormalisation scheme and scale. We suggest a renormalisation scheme in which Dashen's theorem for neutral mesons holds, so that the electromagnetic self-energies of the neutral mesons are zero, and discuss how the self-energies change when we transform to a scheme such as $\bar{MS}$, in which Dashen's theorem for neutral mesons is violated.

## Key claim

The paper presents a lattice QCD+QED calculation of pseudoscalar meson masses, focusing on isospin symmetry violations, but emphasizes that the decomposition of mass splittings into QCD and QED contributions is not unique and depends on the renormalisation scheme and scale. The authors propose a "Dashen scheme" in which neutral mesons have zero electromagnetic self-energy by construction, and discuss how results change when transforming to other schemes such as $\overline{MS}$.

## Method

The authors perform fully dynamical lattice simulations of QCD+QED using three clover-improved quark flavours (u, d, s) with a non-compact U(1) gauge action for the photon field, at a single lattice spacing corresponding to $\beta_{QCD}=5.50$ and $\beta_{QED}=0.8$ (equivalent to $\alpha_{EM}\approx 0.10$, much larger than the physical $1/137$). They generate configurations on $32^3\times 64$ and $48^3\times 96$ lattices, then carry out partially quenched calculations with valence quarks having different masses from the sea quarks, and expand meson masses about a symmetric point where all three sea quark masses are equal and the neutral pseudoscalar meson masses are equal to an average pseudoscalar mass $X_\pi^2$.

## Key numbers

- The QED coupling used is $\beta_{QED}=0.8$, giving $e^2=1.25$ and $\alpha_{EM}=e^2/(4\pi)\approx 0.10$, compared to the physical $\alpha_{EM}=1/137$
- The lattice parameters at the symmetric point are $\kappa_u=0.124362$, $\kappa_d=\kappa_s=0.121713$, with $\beta_{QCD}=5.50$
- The multiplicative renormalisation factors to convert bare quark masses to Dashen-scheme masses are $Z_{m_d}^{QED}=Z_{m_s}^{QED}=1.023$ and $Z_{m_u}^{QED}=1.096$
- Simulations were performed on three lattice volumes: $24^3\times 48$, $32^3\times 64$, and $48^3\times 96$, with the smallest volume showing clear finite-size effects

## Context

This work extends the QCDSF-UKQCD collaboration's previous pure-QCD studies of isospin breaking (Horsley et al., 2015) by including dynamical QED effects, and builds on earlier lattice QCD+QED calculations by Duncan (1990s), Blum (2000s), and Borsanyi et al. (2013-2014). The paper provides the methodological foundation for separating QCD and QED contributions to hadron masses, enabling future calculations of baryon isospin splittings and the determination of the $m_u-m_d$ mass difference, which is difficult to extract reliably from pure QCD simulations.
