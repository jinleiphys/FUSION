# A new Skyrme interaction with improved spin-isospin properties

> A correct determination of the spin-isospin properties of the nuclear effective interaction should lead, among other improvements, to an accurate description of the Gamow-Teller Resonance (GTR). These nuclear excitations impact on a variety of physical processes: from the response in charge-exchange reactions of nuclei naturally present in the Earth, to the description of the stellar nucleosynthesis, and of the pre-supernova explosion core-collapse evolution of massive stars in the Universe. A reliable description of the GTR provides also stringent tests for neutrinoless double-$β$ decay calculations. We present a new Skyrme interaction as accurate as previous forces in the description of finite nuclei and of uniform matter properties around saturation density, and that account well for the GTR in ${}^{48}$Ca, ${}^{90}$Zr and ${}^{208}$Pb, the Isobaric Analog Resonance and the Spin Dipole Resonance in ${}^{90}$Zr and ${}^{208}$Pb.

## Key claim

The authors present a new Skyrme energy density functional (SAMi) that achieves accurate descriptions of Gamow-Teller, Isobaric Analog, and Spin Dipole Resonances in medium and heavy nuclei without sacrificing the quality of ground-state properties or nuclear matter saturation characteristics, provided the spin-orbit splittings and the hierarchy of Landau-Migdal parameters (G0 > 0, G0' > 0.25) are explicitly fitted or constrained.

## Method

The SAMi functional was fitted to binding energies and charge radii of doubly-magic nuclei, proton spin-orbit splittings in 90Zr and 208Pb, and pseudo-data for the energy per particle of pure neutron matter from microscopic calculations (Wiringa et al., 1988). The spin and spin-isospin Landau-Migdal parameters G0 and G0' were fixed to 0.15 and 0.35 respectively at saturation density, and the two-component spin-orbit potential (parameters W0 and W0') was allowed to vary independently. The fit was performed using the MINUIT package, and the resulting functional was tested within the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) for charge-exchange and isoscalar/isovector giant resonances.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density rho_inf = 0.159(1) fm^-3; saturation energy e_inf = -15.93(9) MeV; incompressibility K_inf = 245(1) MeV; symmetry energy J = 28(1) MeV; slope L = 44(7) MeV.
- GMR centroid energy in 208Pb: SAMi = 14.48 MeV vs. experimental 14.24 +/- 0.11 MeV (Youngblood et al., 1999).
- GDR centroid energy in 208Pb: SAMi = 13.95 MeV vs. experimental 13.25 +/- 0.10 MeV (Ryez et al., 2002).
- GTR excitation energy in 48Ca: SAMi = 10.2 MeV (high-energy peak) vs. experimental 10.5 MeV; low-energy peak SAMi = 2.0 MeV vs. experimental 3.0 MeV.
- GTR excitation energy in 90Zr: SAMi = 15.5 MeV (high-energy peak) vs. experimental 15.8 +/- 0.5 MeV; low-energy peak SAMi = 7.8 MeV vs. experimental 9.0 +/- 0.5 MeV.
- GTR excitation energy in 208Pb: SAMi = 19.3 MeV vs. experimental 19.2 +/- 0.2 MeV (Akimune et al., 1995).
- IAR in 90Zr and 208Pb reproduced within 1.5% discrepancy with experiment (Krasznahorkay et al., 2001).
- Neutron skin thickness in 208Pb: SAMi = 0.15 fm, compatible with the hadronic extraction of 0.18 +/- 0.03 fm (Tsang et al., 2012).
- Neutron skin thickness in 90Zr: SAMi = 0.07 fm, in perfect agreement with the value 0.07 +/- 0.04 fm extracted from SDR analysis (Yako et al., 2006).

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme functional approach of Chabanat et al. (1998) (SLy family) and Giai and Sagawa (1981) (SGII) by explicitly fitting spin-orbit splittings and fixing the Landau-Migdal parameters to improve spin-isospin properties, a problem identified by Bender et al. (2002). The SAMi functional enables accurate RPA calculations of charge-exchange resonances (GTR, IAR, SDR) across medium and heavy nuclei, which are critical for weak-interaction rates in supernova dynamics (Langanke et al., 2008) and for constraining neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements. The functional also provides predictions for neutron skin thicknesses that can be tested by future parity-violating electron scattering experiments like PREX.
