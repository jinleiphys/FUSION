# Benchmarking rotational correction energies in odd-mass nuclei

> Nuclear energy density functional theory (DFT) provides a microscopic approach to describing nuclear masses. By incorporating pairing correlations and deformation within DFT, nuclear masses can be predicted with sub-MeV accuracy. A crucial factor in achieving this precision is the dynamical correlation energy associated with restoring rotational symmetry, known as rotational correction energy (RCE). This correction is typically estimated using the cranking approximation in perturbation theory. In this work, we benchmark the results of these calculations against exact angular-momentum projection (AMP) for both even-even and odd-mass nuclei from light to heavy mass region, utilizing a covariant DFT framework. We find that the RCE, computed using the full moment of inertia (MoI) in the cranking approximation, closely matches the results from exact AMP for both even-even and odd-mass nuclei, with the exception of near-spherical even-even nuclei. For certain configurations in odd-mass nuclei, however, the RCEs can become abnormally small, a phenomenon linked to the divergence of the MoI. To address this, a regulator is introduced for the MoI of an odd-mass nucleus, the validity of which is examined through exact AMP calculations.

## Key claim
The cranking approximation using the full moment of inertia (MoI) yields rotational correction energies (RCEs) that closely match exact angular-momentum projection (AMP) results for both even-even and odd-mass nuclei, except for near-spherical even-even nuclei; however, for certain configurations in odd-mass nuclei, the RCEs can become abnormally small due to divergence of the MoI, which is addressed by introducing a regulator.

## Method
The authors performed covariant density functional theory (CDFT) calculations using the PC-F1 parameterization with BCS pairing, generating mean-field states for even-even nuclei (quasiparticle vacuum) and odd-mass nuclei (one-quasiparticle states with blocked orbitals). They computed RCEs via exact angular-momentum projection (AMP) with and without particle-number projection (PNP), and compared these to the cranking approximation using several MoI formulas: the Inglis-Belyaev (IB) MoI, the Nilsson-Prior (NP) MoI, the full MoI for odd-mass nuclei (composed of four terms), and a regulated version of the full MoI that removes divergences when quasiparticle energies become nearly degenerate.

## Key numbers
- For even-even nuclei, the MoI from the Nilsson-Prior formula (I_NP) is systematically larger than the Inglis-Belyaev MoI (I_IB) by about 20% for deformed states.
- For the odd-mass nucleus 49Cr with K^pi=5/2^- configuration, the I_3 term in the full MoI causes an abnormally large MoI around beta_2 = 0.55, leading to a dip in RCE; the regulator with parameters k_0 = 0.05 MeV^-4, k_1 = 100, and beta_0 = 0.2 removes this abnormal behavior.
- For 122Nd and 123Nd, RCEs from the cranking approximation agree with exact AMP to within less than 0.2 MeV.
- For Sr and Zr isotopes, the largest discrepancy between cranking and exact AMP RCEs is about 0.5 MeV, observed in 99Sr.
- For a set of deformed odd-mass nuclei from 21Ne to 127Nd, RCEs from all methods fluctuate around 2.0 MeV within a range of 1-4 MeV.
- Including the RCE from the full MoI (E^F_rot) reduces the rms error in binding energies from 2.77 MeV (pure mean-field) to 1.29 MeV, compared to 1.57 MeV achieved with exact AMP RCE.
- The phenomenological formula (E^Q_rot) yields an rms error of 1.20 MeV for the same set of odd-mass nuclei.

## Context
This work extends the benchmarking of rotational correction energies from even-even nuclei (previously studied by Zhang (2013) and others using the phenomenological formula of Chamel (2008) and Goriely (2009)) to odd-mass nuclei, where such validation was lacking. The authors recently extended the multi-reference CDFT framework to odd-mass nuclei (Zhou, 2023), enabling this benchmark. The results validate the use of the full MoI in the cranking approximation for odd-mass nuclei, and the introduced regulator addresses a divergence problem that could affect mass predictions. This work enables more accurate predictions of odd-even mass differences and nucleon separation energies, and could influence understanding of shape coexistence in nuclei (Fu, 2013).
