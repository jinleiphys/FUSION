# Dynamics of Chemical Equilibrium of Hadronic Matter Close to $T_c$

> Quick chemical equilibration times of hadrons (specifically, $p\bar{p}$, $K\bar{K}$, $Λ\barΛ$, and $Ω\barΩ$ pairs) within a hadron gas are explained dynamically using Hagedorn states, which drive particles into equilibrium close to the critical temperature. Within this scheme, we use master equations and derive various analytical estimates for the chemical equilibration times. We compare our model to recent lattice results and find that for both $T_c=176$ MeV and $T_c=196$ MeV, the hadrons can reach chemical equilibrium almost immediately, well before the chemical freeze-out temperatures found in thermal fits for a hadron gas without Hagedorn states. Furthermore the ratios $p/π$, $K/π$, $Λ/π$, and $Ω/ π$ match experimental values well in our dynamical scenario.

## Key claim

The paper claims that Hagedorn states (heavy resonances with an exponentially growing mass spectrum) can dynamically drive hadrons such as protons, kaons, lambdas, and omegas into chemical equilibrium almost immediately near the critical temperature, but this result depends on the assumption that Hagedorn states exist and that their decay widths are large enough to catalyze rapid equilibration before the system cools below chemical freeze-out temperatures.

## Method

The authors construct a statistical model of a hadron gas that includes Hagedorn states with an exponential mass spectrum, fitting the model parameters to lattice QCD results for the trace anomaly and energy density at two different critical temperatures (176 MeV and 196 MeV). They then write master equations (rate equations) for the time evolution of pions, Hagedorn states, and specific hadron pairs (p-pbar, K-Kbar, Lambda-AntiLambda, Omega-AntiOmega), using decay widths and branching ratios derived from micro-canonical and canonical models, and solve these equations both analytically and numerically for static systems and for an expanding fireball with Bjorken expansion.

## Key numbers

- Chemical equilibration times for proton-antiproton, kaon-antikaon, and lambda-antilambda pairs at T = T_c are less than 0.2 fm/c for both T_c = 176 MeV and T_c = 196 MeV.
- At T = 0.9 T_c, the chemical equilibration time for p-pbar and Lambda-AntiLambda pairs is still about t < 1 fm/c.
- The total decay width of Hagedorn states is given by Gamma_i = 0.15 m_i - 0.0584 (in GeV), ranging from 250 MeV to 1800 MeV.
- The average number of pions from Hagedorn decay is given by <n_i> = 0.9 + 1.2 (m_i / m_p), yielding values from approximately 3 to 34 after normalization.
- For the expanding fireball, the total pion number in the 5% most central collisions is 874, obtained from experimental data at RHIC (Bearden, 2004) with dN_pi/dy = 291.5 per charge state averaged over y = ±0.5.
- The entropy per pion in the model is approximately S_pi/N_pi ≈ 6.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Greiner (2004) and Noronha-Hostler (2007) that proposed Hagedorn states as a mechanism for rapid chemical equilibration, addressing the problem that standard hadron gas models without Hagedorn states predict equilibration times of 100-1000 fm/c (Koch, 1986) while the hadronic fireball lifetime is only 4-7 fm/c. The paper also builds on lattice QCD results from the RBC-Bielefeld collaboration (Cheng, 2007; Bazavov, 2009) with T_c = 196 MeV and the BMW collaboration (Zodor) with T_c = 176 MeV. The results enable future work incorporating Hagedorn states into transport approaches such as UrQMD and suggest that the critical temperature and chemical freeze-out temperature may be related through the Hagedorn state mechanism.
