# Comment on 'Induced Pseudoscalar Coupling Constant' by Il-Tong Cheon and Myung Ki Cheoun (nucl-th/9811009)

> In a recent preprint Cheon and Cheoun have derived from a chiral model an additional term, not usually appearing in the standard matrix element for radiative muon capture. Using that term they generate a large correction to the RMC spectrum which tends to resolve the problem caused by the too large value of $g_P$ found in the TRIUMF RMC experiment. In this comment we observe first that their extra term leads to an amplitude which is not gauge invariant and second that such a term should be present, in a gauge invariant way, in an earlier full chiral perturbation theory calculation, which however found negligible differences from the standard approach.

# Comment on 'Induced Pseudoscalar Coupling Constant' by Il-Tong Cheon and Myung Ki Cheoun

## Key claim

The extra seagull term proposed by Cheon and Cheoun (1998) to resolve the discrepancy between the TRIUMF radiative muon capture (RMC) experiment and the Goldberger-Treiman prediction for the induced pseudoscalar coupling constant $g_P$ leads to an amplitude that is not gauge invariant, and a term of the same structure should already be present in a gauge-invariant way in earlier chiral perturbation theory calculations, which found negligible differences from the standard approach.

## Method

The author checks gauge invariance of the Cheon-Cheoun amplitude by making the substitution $\epsilon \rightarrow k$ (replacing the photon polarization vector with the photon momentum) in the new term $\Delta M_e$ and comparing with the standard terms $M_a$ through $M_e$. The author also compares the proposed term against the full chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) calculation to $O(p^3)$ by Ando and Min (1998), and demonstrates via heavy baryon ChPT reduction that the seagull structure already appears in the standard amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The TRIUMF RMC experiment found a value of $g_P$ almost 1.5 times the value predicted by the Goldberger-Treiman relation.
- The ChPT calculation by Ando and Min (1998) was performed to $O(p^3)$ and found negligible effects relative to the standard calculation.

## Context

This comment addresses a proposed solution by Cheon and Cheoun (1998) to the puzzle created by the TRIUMF RMC experiment (Jonkmans et al., 1996), which found a $g_P$ value 1.5 times the Goldberger-Treiman prediction, while other measurements (particularly in non-radiative muon capture) agreed with the expected value. The comment argues that the Cheon-Cheoun term violates gauge invariance and that a full ChPT calculation by Ando and Min (1998) already included such terms and found them negligible. The work enables future RMC analyses to focus on gauge-invariant approaches and clarifies that the proposed solution does not resolve the $g_P$ puzzle.
