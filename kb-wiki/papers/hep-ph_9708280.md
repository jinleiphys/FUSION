# Phase transition of a finite quark-gluon plasma

> The deconfinement transition region between hadronic matter and quark-gluon plasma is studied for finite volumes. Assuming simple model equations of state and a first order phase transition, we find that fluctuations in finite volumes hinder a sharp separation between the two phases around the critical temperature, leading to a rounding of the phase transition. For reaction volumes expected in heavy ion experiments, the softening of the equation of state is reduced considerably. This is especially true when the requirement of exact color-singletness is included in the QGP equation of state.

## Key claim

For finite volumes expected in heavy ion collisions (below about 100 fm³), thermodynamic fluctuations prevent a sharp first-order phase transition between hadronic matter and quark-gluon plasma, causing a rounding of the transition and a considerable reduction in the softening of the equation of state; this effect is even stronger when the requirement of exact color-singletness is included in the QGP equation of state.

## Method

The authors model the deconfinement phase transition by constructing a free energy for a finite system as a function of an order parameter (the hadronic volume fraction ξ), using a hadron gas equation of state (non-interacting Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac gases with a Hagedorn correction) and a simple MIT bag model for the QGP. They compute the probability distribution of the order parameter via the Boltzmann factor exp(-βF(ξ)) and then calculate expectation values of thermodynamic quantities (energy density, entropy density, pressure) as integrals over ξ, both with and without the color-singlet constraint on the QGP phase.

## Key numbers

- For a volume of 25 fm³ at 10 MeV above the critical temperature, the system is composed of about 10% hadron gas and 90% QGP.
- The critical temperature for infinite matter is T_C = 143 MeV (using bag constant B = 200 MeV/fm³).
- Expected reaction volumes at RHIC (√s = 200 AGeV) are V ≈ 750 fm³; at LHC (√s ≈ 5.5 ATeV) V ≈ 1350 fm³; but subsystems in thermal equilibrium are only V < 100 fm³.
- The shift of the critical temperature due to color-singletness follows ΔT_C ~ 1/V^α with α ≈ 0.7.
- For volumes V < 25 fm³ with the color-singlet constraint, the minimum of P(ε)/ε vanishes completely.
- The speed of sound squared c_s² is smoothly damped over the energy density range between 20 MeV/fm³ and 200 MeV/fm³ for V = 100 fm³ without color-singlet constraint.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of finite-size effects on phase transitions in strongly interacting matter, particularly the work of Csernai and Neda (1994) on the deconfinement transition and Csernai and Kapusta (1986) on the liquid-gas transition. It also builds on the analysis of Binder and Landau (1984) on finite-size scaling of first-order phase transitions. The results challenge the viability of the "softest point" lifetime signal proposed by Hung and Shuryak (1995) and Rischke and Gyulassy (1996) as a signature of QGP formation in heavy ion collisions, since finite volumes and color-singletness drastically reduce the softening of the equation of state. The work also provides phenomenological insight for interpreting lattice QCD calculations, which must be extrapolated from finite volumes (currently V ≈ 50-100 fm³) to infinite matter.
