# Role of nuclear deformation and orientation about symmetry axis of target nucleus on heavy-ion fusion dynamics

> Nuclear shape and orientation degrees of freedom are incorporated into the calculation of the double-folding nuclear potential within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) formalism. The quadrupole deformations ($β_2$), nuclear densities and the effective nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction potential are obtained using the RMF approach for the Hybrid, NL3$^*$ and NL3 parameterizations. The calculated quadrupole deformations are included in the target densities through the nuclear radius. The deformation and orientation-dependent microscopic nuclear potentials are further employed to obtain fusion barrier characteristics and cross-sections for 12 even-even heavy-ion reactions with doubly magic spherical $^{16}$O and $^{48}$Ca as projectiles along with deformed targets from different mass regions. The results obtained for the relativistic R3Y NN potential are compared with those of the Reid version of the non-relativistic M3Y NN potential as well as with the available experimental data. A decrease in the barrier height and increase in the cross-section is observed upon the inclusion of target quadrupole deformations in the nuclear density distributions at the target orientation angles, $θ_2\le58^\circ$ for the R3Y NN potential and at $θ_2\le60^\circ$ for the M3Y NN potential. On comparing the $θ_2$-integrated cross-section calculated using M3Y and R3Y NN potentials with spherical and deformed densities, one observes that the deformed densities and the relativistic R3Y NN potential obtained for the Hybrid parameter set provide better agreement with the available experimental data for all the considered reactions. Moreover, the modifications in the characteristics of the fusion barrier and hence in the cross-section with the inclusion of nuclear shape degrees of freedom and orientations are found to become more prominent in reactions forming heavier compound nuclei.

## Key claim
The inclusion of target quadrupole deformations and orientation angles in the double-folding nuclear potential, calculated within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) formalism, lowers the fusion barrier height and increases the cross-section for orientation angles θ₂ ≤ 58° (R3Y potential) and θ₂ ≤ 60° (M3Y potential), with the effect becoming more prominent for reactions forming heavier compound nuclei.

## Method
The authors used the axially deformed RMF formalism with NL3, NL3*, and Hybrid parameter sets to calculate quadrupole deformations (β₂), nuclear densities, and the relativistic R3Y effective nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction potential. The target quadrupole deformations were incorporated into the nuclear densities through the nuclear radius, and the deformation- and orientation-dependent nuclear potential was obtained via the double-folding approach. The resulting total interaction potential (including Coulomb and centrifugal terms) was used to compute fusion barrier characteristics and cross-sections for 12 even-even heavy-ion reactions using the ℓ-summed Wong model, with results compared to those from the non-relativistic M3Y NN potential and experimental data.

## Key numbers
- The increase in barrier height when changing θ₂ from 0° to 90° is smallest (1.94 MeV for R3Y, 1.98 MeV for M3Y) for the ⁴⁸Ca+³²S reaction and largest (20.72 MeV for R3Y, 20.97 MeV for M3Y) for the ⁴⁸Ca+²³⁸U reaction.
- For the ⁴⁸Ca+¹⁶⁸Er reaction (target with highest β₂ = 0.352), the change in barrier height from θ₂ = 0° to 90° is 19.51 MeV (R3Y) and 19.76 MeV (M3Y).
- The deformed barrier height is lower than the spherical barrier height at θ₂ ≤ 58° for the R3Y NN potential and at θ₂ ≤ 60° for the M3Y NN potential.
- The Hybrid parameter set (incompressibility K = 230.01 MeV) gives the lowest barrier height, while NL3 gives the highest, for spherical target densities.
- The surface diffuseness parameter for proton densities is approximately 0.1 fm smaller than experimental charge density values due to the finite size effect of the proton not included in RMF calculations.

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Bhuyan et al. (2018, 2020, 2022) and Rana et al. (2021, 2022) that used spherical nuclear densities and the R3Y effective NN interaction within the RMF formalism to study heavy-ion fusion dynamics. The present study incorporates quadrupole deformation and orientation degrees of freedom of the target nucleus, which were previously neglected. The results enable more reliable descriptions of sub-barrier fusion dynamics, particularly for heavy and superheavy mass regions, and demonstrate that the deformed densities combined with the relativistic R3Y NN potential from the Hybrid parameter set provide better agreement with experimental data than spherical densities or the non-relativistic M3Y potential.
