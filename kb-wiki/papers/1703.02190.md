# QCD mechanisms for accessing the nucleon GPDs with the exclusive pion-induced Drell-Yan process at J-PARC

> Generalized parton distributions (GPDs) encoding multidimensional information of hadron partonic structure appear as the building blocks in a factorized description of hard exclusive reactions. The nucleon GPDs have been accessed by deeply virtual Compton scattering and deeply virtual meson production with lepton beam. A complementary probe with hadron beam is the exclusive pion-induced Drell-Yan process. We discuss recent theoretical advances on describing this process in terms of the partonic subprocess convoluted with the nucleon GPDs and the pion distribution amplitudes. Furthermore, we mention the feasibility study for measuring the exclusive pion-induced Drell-Yan process, $π^- p \to μ^+ μ^- n$, via a spectrometer at the High Momentum Beamline being constructed at J-PARC in Japan. We also point out the possible soft partonic mechanisms beyond the QCD factorization framework, which could give important contributions at J-PARC kinematics, and present an estimate of the soft mechanisms making use of dispersion relations and quark-hadron duality. Realization of the measurement of the exclusive pion-induced Drell-Yan process at J-PARC will provide a new test of QCD descriptions of a novel class of hard exclusive reactions. It will also offer the possibility of experimentally accessing nucleon GPDs at large timelike virtuality.

## Key claim

The exclusive pion-induced Drell-Yan process at J-PARC can provide access to nucleon generalized parton distributions (GPDs) at large timelike virtuality, but soft nonfactorizable mechanisms beyond the QCD factorization framework may give important contributions at J-PARC kinematics and could produce cross sections larger by a factor of approximately 5 than those from the leading-order QCD factorization formula.

## Method

The author presents a theoretical analysis of the exclusive Drell-Yan process π⁻ p → μ⁺ μ⁻ n, describing the leading-order QCD factorization formula where the amplitude is expressed as a convolution of the pion distribution amplitude (DA) with nucleon GPDs. The cross section is estimated using two different GPD parameterizations: the BMP2001 model based on double distributions and the GK2013 parameterization fitted to HERMES electroproduction data. Additionally, the author develops a light-cone QCD sum rule approach to estimate the soft nonfactorizable mechanism, using the operator product expansion for an off-forward virtual Compton amplitude with an axial vector current, combined with dispersion relations and quark-hadron duality.

## Key numbers

- The differential cross section for π⁻ p → γ* n at Q'² = 5 GeV², τ = 0.2, and |t| = 0.2 GeV² is at the pb level using the LO QCD factorization formula with BMP2001 input.
- The updated cross section using the GK2013 parameterization is enhanced compared to the BMP2001 result at the same kinematics (Q'² = 5 GeV², τ = 0.2, |t| = 0.2 GeV²).
- The soft nonfactorizable mechanism gives a cross section larger by a factor of approximately 5 than the LO QCD factorization result at Q'² = 5 GeV², τ = 0.2, and |t| = 0.2 GeV².
- The threshold parameter used in the light-cone QCD sum rule is q_th² = 0.7 GeV².
- The secondary pion beam at J-PARC has energies of approximately 15-20 GeV.
- For the pion pole contribution, Q'² |F_exp_π(Q'²)| ≈ 0.88 GeV² compared with Q'² |F_LO_π(Q'²)| ≈ 0.15 GeV² at moderate Q'².

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for accessing nucleon GPDs via exclusive reactions, complementing the deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and deeply virtual meson production (DVMP) experiments at JLab, HERMES, and COMPASS. The exclusive pion-induced Drell-Yan process was first proposed by Berger, Diehl and Pire (2001) using the LO QCD factorization formula. The author updates those cross section estimates using the GK2013 GPD parameterization from Goloskokov and Kroll (2015) and performs a feasibility study for the E50 spectrometer at J-PARC. The soft nonfactorizable mechanism is estimated using light-cone QCD sum rules, analogous to the treatment of the pion electromagnetic form factor by Braun (1999). The results enable experimental tests of QCD descriptions of hard exclusive reactions and could distinguish between different GPD parameterizations using J-PARC data.
