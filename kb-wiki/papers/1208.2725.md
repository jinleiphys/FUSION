# The thermodynamic properties of weakly interacting quark gluon plasma via the one gluon exchange interaction

> The thermodynamic properties of the quark gluon plasma($QGP$) as well as its phase diagram are calculated as a function of baryon density (chemical potential) and temperature. The $QGP$ is assumed to be composed of the light quarks only, i.e. the up and the down quarks, which interact weakly and the gluons which are treated as they are free. The interaction between quarks is considered in the framework of the one gluon exchange model which is obtained from the Fermi liquid picture. The bag model is used, with fixed bag pressure (${\cal B}$) for the nonperturbative part and the quantum chromodynamics ($QCD$) coupling is assumed to be constant i.e. no dependence on the temperature or the baryon density. The effect of weakly interacting quarks on the $QGP$ phase diagram are shown and discussed. It is demonstrated that the one gluon exchange interaction for the massless quarks has considerable effect on the $QGP$ phase diagram and it causes the system to reach to the confined phase at the smaller baryon densities and temperatures. The pressure of excluded volume hadron gas model is also used to find the transition phase-diagram. Our results depend on the values of bag pressure and the $QCD$ coupling constant which the latter does not have a dramatic effect on our calculations . Finally, we compare our results with the thermodynamic properties of strange quark matter and the lattice $QCD$ prediction for the $QGP$ transition critical temperature.

## Key claim

The one gluon exchange interaction, treated as a repulsive force between massless up and down quarks within the Fermi liquid model, lowers the baryon density and temperature required for the transition to the quark gluon plasma phase compared to the noninteracting case; however, the results depend on the chosen bag pressure and the QCD coupling constant, which does not have a dramatic effect on the calculations.

## Method

The authors model the quark gluon plasma as a system of weakly interacting massless up and down quarks plus free gluons, using the Landau Fermi liquid theory to derive the one gluon exchange interaction energy. The partition function is computed analytically for quarks and gluons, and thermodynamic quantities (energy density, pressure, entropy, free energy) are obtained as functions of baryon density and temperature. The phase transition boundary is found by equating the QGP pressure (including bag pressure) to either zero hadronic pressure or the pressure from an excluded volume hadron gas model.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for noninteracting QGP at zero baryon density: approximately 140 MeV, compared to the lattice QCD prediction of 170 MeV.
- Bag pressure used for comparison with Wong (2000): 208 MeV fm^{-3}.
- Bag pressure required to match the lattice QCD critical temperature of 170 MeV at low baryonic chemical potential: approximately 441 MeV (noted as within the estimated range up to 500 MeV).
- QCD coupling constant fixed at α_c = 0.2 for most calculations.
- Baryon density for transition at zero temperature in noninteracting case: n_B ≈ 0.7 fm^{-3} (four times empirical nuclear matter density).

## Context

This work extends the noninteracting QGP calculations of Wong (2000) by incorporating a repulsive one gluon exchange interaction between quarks, and also goes beyond the zero hadronic pressure approximation by including the excluded volume hadron gas model from earlier studies (E1, E2, E3). The results are compared with the strange quark matter calculations of Modarres (2009) and with lattice QCD predictions for the critical temperature (JHEP, 2006). The approach enables future studies to relate the bag constant and QCD coupling constant, and to generalize to non-constant coupling and gluon interactions.
