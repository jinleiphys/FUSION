# T-odd particle spin rotation in nuclear target

> It is shown that particle spin rotation in nuclear target, caused by the imaginary part of the spin-dependent forward scattering amplitude, is T-odd rotation.

## Key claim

The paper shows that spin rotation of a particle passing through a nuclear target, when caused by the imaginary part of the spin-dependent forward scattering amplitude, is a T-odd rotation, but this is a "dissipative" effect of second order in polarization, distinct from any T-odd terms arising from fundamental time-reversal violation.

## Method

The author derives the time evolution of the polarization vector for a spin-1/2 particle moving through a polarized medium, starting from the effective refractive index expressed in terms of the forward scattering amplitude. The equation of motion for the true polarization is obtained from the spin density matrix, and the specific case of a completely polarized beam is analyzed to isolate the contribution of the imaginary part of the spin-dependent amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The spin rotation frequency is proportional to the real part of the spin-dependent forward scattering amplitude: \( \frac{4\pi\rho v}{k} \re A_s \).
- The dissipative T-odd rotation term is proportional to the imaginary part of the spin-dependent forward scattering amplitude: \( \frac{4\pi\rho v}{k} \im A_s \).
- For the case where initial polarization is perpendicular to \( \boldsymbol J \), the polarization components evolve as: \( O_x(t) = \cos\left(\frac{4\pi\rho v}{k} \re A_s t\right) / \cosh\left( \frac{4\pi\rho v}{k} \im A_s t \right) \), \( O_y(t) = -\sin\left(\frac{4\pi\rho v}{k} \re A_s t\right) / \cosh\left( \frac{4\pi\rho v}{k} \im A_s t \right) \), \( O_z(t) = -\tanh\left(\frac{4\pi\rho v}{k} \im A_s t \right) \).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of neutron and particle spin rotation in polarized nuclear targets, originally developed by Baryshevsky and Podgoretsky (1964) and further explored by Abragam and Goldman (1982) and Forte (1973). The paper clarifies that the imaginary part of the spin-dependent forward scattering amplitude, previously considered in the literature, produces a T-odd rotation that is distinct from fundamental T-violation and is instead a dissipative effect analogous to terms appearing in the Bargmann-Michel-Telegdi equation when radiation reaction is included (Rowe and Rowe, 1987). This result enables absolute calibration of spin rotation measurement equipment, since the imaginary part of the forward scattering amplitude can be determined from total cross-section measurements.
