# Stability and Evolution of Color Skyrmions in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We show the existence of unstable color skyrmions in a class of nonabelian fluid models. Oscillating and expanding solutions are found in the time-dependent case.

## Key claim

The existence and stability of color skyrmions in a nonabelian fluid model depends critically on the value of the adiabatic index gamma in the equation of state; for gamma between 6/5 and 5/3, topological configurations are unstable and the total energy is minimized by infinite dilution, while for gamma=2 a stable skyrmion exists.

## Method

The author considers a nonabelian fluid system with an SU(2) color group and a gamma-law equation of state (pressure proportional to energy density). For time-independent configurations, a variational analysis is performed by scaling the fluid configuration with a single characteristic size R, introducing dimensionless variables, and examining the total energy as a function of R for different values of gamma between 1 and 2. For time-dependent configurations, the Euler-Lagrange equation is derived from a Lagrangian expressed in terms of the generalized velocity field omega_mu, and solved for the special cases gamma=2 and gamma=4/3.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for QGP: T_C ~ 170 MeV
- Critical density for QGP: mu_C ~ 1 GeV/fm^3 (ten times normal nuclear matter density)
- For gamma=4/3, the total energy scales as E = const./R, with no stable configuration at finite R
- For gamma=2, the time-dependent solution yields oscillatory motion described by a one-dimensional Hamiltonian system with potential q^(2/3) where q = R^(3/2)
- For gamma=4/3, the approximate expansion solution is R^(0) = R_0 (t/tau + 1)^(4/3), with the condition epsilon = tau/R_0 << 1

## Context

This work extends the author's previous paper (Dai and Nair, 2006) which showed the existence of color skyrmions for a particular choice of hydrodynamic Hamiltonian and for gamma=2 with a specific ansatz. The analysis is motivated by experimental results from RHIC indicating that the quark-gluon plasma behaves as a near-perfect fluid with shear viscosity close to the AdS/CFT lower bound. The equation of state used is informed by lattice simulations of isentropic QGP expansion, and the paper notes that color skyrmions also arise in the effective action description of Wilson lines (Pisarski, 2000s). The results enable further study of topological soliton dynamics in nonabelian fluids and suggest that expanding solitons may be generic features of QGP created in nuclear collisions.
