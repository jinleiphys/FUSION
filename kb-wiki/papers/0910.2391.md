# Study of neutron-rich calcium isotopes with a realistic shell-model interaction

> We have studied neutron-rich calcium isotopes in terms of the shell model employing a realistic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential. The short-range repulsion of the potential is renormalized by way of the V-low-k approach. The calculated results are in very good agreement with the available experimental data, thus supporting our predictions for the hitherto unknown spectra of 53-56Ca nuclei. In this context, the possible existence of an N=34 shell closure is discussed.

## Key claim

The authors find that their realistic shell-model effective interaction, derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential via the V-low-k renormalization approach, reproduces available experimental data for neutron-rich calcium isotopes without empirical monopole corrections, but their results do not provide evidence for an N=34 shell closure in 54Ca, contrary to predictions from the GXPF1A interaction.

## Method

The authors derived a shell-model effective interaction from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, renormalizing the short-range repulsion using the V-low-k approach and then calculating two-body matrix elements through third-order perturbation theory with the Q-box plus folded-diagram method summed to all orders via the Lee-Suzuki iteration method. The model space consisted of the four neutron single-particle levels 0f7/2, 0f5/2, 1p3/2, and 1p1/2 above a doubly-closed 40Ca core, with single-particle energies determined by reproducing observed states in 47Ca and 49Ca. Shell-model calculations were performed for calcium isotopes from 49Ca to 56Ca using the Oslo shell-model code.

## Key numbers

- Single-particle energy differences: epsilon(1p3/2) - epsilon(0f7/2) = 2.7 MeV, epsilon(1p1/2) - epsilon(0f7/2) = 5.5 MeV, epsilon(0f5/2) - epsilon(0f7/2) = 8.5 MeV; absolute energy of neutron 0f7/2 orbital placed at -8.2 MeV to reproduce 49Ca binding energy relative to 40Ca
- Calculated B(E2; 2+1 -> 0+1) in 50Ca: 10.9 e2 fm4, compared to experimental value 7.5 +/- 0.2 e2 fm4 from Valiente-Dobon (2009)
- Calculated 2+1 excitation energy in 52Ca: 2.396 MeV (experimental: 2.562 MeV); in 54Ca: 2.061 MeV (predicted)
- Monopole matrix element for (0f5/2, 1p3/2) configuration: -0.09 MeV for this interaction, 0.12 MeV for GXPF1A; for (0f5/2, 1p1/2) configuration: 0.02 MeV for this interaction, 0.08 MeV for GXPF1A
- Effective single-particle energy gap between 1p1/2 and 0f5/2 at N=28: about 2 MeV for both interactions; at N=34: remains about 2 MeV for this interaction, increases to about 3.6 MeV for GXPF1A

## Context

This work extends the program of deriving realistic shell-model effective interactions from high-precision nucleon-nucleon potentials using the V-low-k approach, previously applied by Coraggio and collaborators (2007, 2009) to other mass regions. It directly addresses the question of an N=34 shell closure in neutron-rich calcium isotopes, which was predicted by Honma and collaborators (2004, 2005) using the GXPF1A interaction but not supported by calculations with the KB3G interaction (Caurier, 2005) or FPD6 interaction (Poves, 2001). The results enable future experimental studies of 53-56Ca to test whether the N=34 shell closure exists, with the authors' predictions providing spectra for these unobserved nuclei.
