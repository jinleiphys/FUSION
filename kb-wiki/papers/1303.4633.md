# Formulation of the Schwinger mechanism in classical statistical field theory

> In this paper, we show how classical statistical field theory techniques can be used to efficiently perform the numerical evaluation of the non-perturbative Schwinger mechanism of particle production by quantum tunneling. In some approximation, we also consider the back-reaction of the produced particles on the external field, as well as the self-interactions of the produced particles.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Schwinger mechanism, an intrinsically quantum non-perturbative phenomenon of particle production by quantum tunneling, can be reformulated and numerically evaluated using classical statistical field theory techniques, provided one treats the background gauge field exactly and uses a Gaussian ensemble of initial conditions for the classical fields.

## Method

The authors consider scalar electrodynamics with a complex scalar field minimally coupled to an Abelian vector field driven by an external source. They show that the leading-order inclusive particle spectrum, normally obtained from the 1-loop 2-point correlation function of the scalar field, can be recast as a Gaussian functional integral over initial conditions for classical fields that obey the linearized covariant Klein-Gordon equation. The numerical implementation discretizes space on a lattice with forward and backward covariant derivatives preserving gauge invariance, solves the classical equation of motion for each Monte-Carlo sample of initial conditions, and extracts the spectrum via a Lehmann-Symanzik-Zimmermann reduction formula at late times.

## Key numbers

* The computation time for the direct mode-function method scales as \(N_t \times N_{\rm latt}^2\), where \(N_{\rm latt}\) is the number of lattice points and \(N_t\) the number of time-steps.
* The Monte-Carlo method scales as \(N_{\rm samples} \times N_{\rm latt} \times (N_{\rm latt} + N_t)\), and is less costly when \(N_{\rm samples} \ll N_{\rm latt}\) and \(N_{\rm samples} \ll N_t\).
* The lattice used for numerical demonstration has \(N_x = N_y = 32\), \(N_z = 256\) lattice points.
* The Schwinger pair production probability in QED is of order \(\exp(-\pi m^2 / eE)\), requiring fields of order \(E \sim m^2/e \sim 10^{18}\) V/m for electron-positron pair production.

## Context

This work extends the classical statistical field theory approach previously applied to cosmology (Polarski and Starobinsky, 1992; Son, 1996; Micha and Tkachev, 2003), cold atom physics (Norrie et al., 2005, 2006), and quark-gluon plasma thermalization (Dusling et al., 2007; Epelbaum and Gelis, 2011; Berges et al., 2008, 2009) to the Schwinger mechanism. It provides a computationally efficient alternative to the traditional method based on the imaginary part of the Heisenberg-Euler Lagrangian (Heisenberg and Euler, 1936) or Bogoliubov transformations (Tanji, 2008, 2009, 2010), and is motivated by applications to the Color Glass Condensate framework where strong color fields of order \(1/g\) can produce large pair yields. The formulation enables inclusion of back-reaction of produced particles on the external field and self-interactions among produced particles, which are crucial for energy conservation and eventual thermalization.
