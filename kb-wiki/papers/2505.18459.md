# Bridging reaction theory and nuclear structure in $π^\pm$-${}^{48}$Ca scattering

> We extend the pion-nucleus multiple-scattering framework to include detailed second-order rescattering dynamics for nuclei with non-zero isospin. To account for intermediate charge-exchange and nucleon spin-flip effects, we develop a scattering potential that depends on the one- and two-body densities of the target nucleus. We compute one-body densities from coupled-cluster theory and two-body densities within the Hartree-Fock approximation. To estimate theoretical uncertainties, we employ modern nuclear Hamiltonians derived from chiral effective field theory. While the sensitivity to nuclear structure details is mild, second-order corrections are found to be sizeable and essential for accurately reproducing differential cross sections measured in $π^\pm$-${}^{48}$Ca elastic scattering within the $Δ(1232)$-resonance region

## Key claim

The paper extends a second-order pion-nucleus multiple-scattering framework to targets with non-zero isospin, finding that while sensitivity to nuclear structure details is mild, second-order corrections are sizeable and essential for accurately reproducing differential cross sections measured in pi+/- elastic scattering on 48Ca within the Delta(1232)-resonance region.

## Method

The authors generalized the Kerman-McManus-Thaler multiple-scattering formalism to derive a second-order scattering potential that explicitly accounts for intermediate charge-exchange and nucleon spin-flip dynamics in nuclei with non-zero isospin. One-body densities were computed using coupled-cluster theory at the singles-and-doubles level (CCSD), while two-body densities were obtained within the Hartree-Fock approximation, both using chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians (NNLO_sat(450) and Delta NNLO_GO(394)). The three in-medium parameters for the pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes were taken unchanged from a previous fit to pi+/- on 12C data in the 80-180 MeV kinetic energy range.

## Key numbers

* Differential cross sections for pi+/- elastic scattering on 48Ca were computed in the pion laboratory kinetic energy range of 80-180 MeV, where cross sections are particularly sensitive to Delta-resonance properties.
* The one-body density matrix elements in the harmonic oscillator basis satisfy the normalization rho_p(q=0)=Z and rho_n(q=0)=N, with Z=20 and N=28 for 48Ca.
* The exchange correlation function C_(ex) is normalized to C_(ex)(0,0) = min(N,Z) = 20 for 48Ca.
* The harmonic oscillator basis used 15 major shells with frequencies hbar-omega = 16 MeV for Delta NNLO_GO(394) and 22 MeV for NNLO_sat(450).

## Context

This work extends the second-order pion-nucleus scattering formalism previously developed by Tsaran (2023) for spin-isospin-zero nuclei (12C, 16O, 28Si, 40Ca) to targets with non-zero isospin, specifically 48Ca. The approach builds on the Kerman-McManus-Thaler multiple-scattering theory and is motivated by the need for improved pion-nucleus interaction models for neutrino oscillation experiments such as DUNE and MicroBooNE, which use 40Ar-based detectors, and for coherent pi0 photoproduction studies on 48Ca being pursued by the A2@MAMI Collaboration. The work contributes to bridging nuclear structure and reaction theories, following efforts by Vorabbi (2016), Rotureau (2018), Idini (2019), Hebborn (2022), and Vorabbi (2023) who applied first-order scattering theory with one-body densities to nucleon-nucleus cross sections.
