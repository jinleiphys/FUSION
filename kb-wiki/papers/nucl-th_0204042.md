# Scaling in many-body systems and proton response

> The observation of scaling in processes in which a weakly interacting probe delivers large momentum ${\bf q}$ to a many-body system reflects the dominance of incoherent scattering off target constituents. While a suitably defined scaling function can provide rich information on the internal dynamics of the target, in general its extraction from the measured cross section requires careful consideration of the nature of the interaction driving the scattering process. The analysis of deep inelastic electron-proton scattering in the target rest frame within standard many-body theory naturally leads to the emergence of a scaling function that, unlike the commonly used structure functions $F_1$ and $F_2$, can be identified with the intrinsic proton response. The implications for the theoretical analysis of deep inelastic scattering and the interpretation of the data are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that deep inelastic electron-proton scattering can be analyzed within standard many-body theory using a scaling function in the variable \(\widetilde y = \nu - |{\bf q}|\) that, unlike the conventional structure functions \(F_1\) and \(F_2\), can be directly identified with the intrinsic proton response; this scaling occurs irrespective of the constituent mass, contrary to the standard parton model assumption that constituents must be nearly massless.

## Method

The author applies the impulse approximation (IA) formalism from many-body theory to deep inelastic electron-proton scattering, treating the proton as a bound system of pointlike Dirac particles. The scaling function \(F(\widetilde y)\) is extracted by dividing the measured structure functions \(W_1\) and \(W_2\) by the appropriate elementary electron-constituent cross sections evaluated at a fixed constituent momentum \(\overline p\), using constituent mass \(m = 300\) MeV and binding parameter \(B_0 = 200\) MeV. The analysis uses DIS data from SLAC, CERN (NMC and BCDMS collaborations) rearranged in bins of constant momentum transfer \(|{\bf q}|\) centered at 11, 19, and 27 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Constituent mass \(m = 300\) MeV and binding parameter \(B_0 = 200\) MeV used to evaluate elementary cross sections
- Scaling sets in at momentum transfer \(|{\bf q}| > 10\) GeV for both \(W_1/\overline\sigma_1\) and \(W_2/\overline\sigma_2\)
- The difference between Bjorken variable \(x\) and \(-\widetilde y/M\) is less than 2% over the entire range \(0 \leq x \leq 1\) at fixed \(|{\bf q}| = 20\) GeV
- Data span a \(Q^2\) range from 1.7 to 37.5 GeV\(^2\)
- Varying \(m\) and \(B_0\) in the range 10–300 MeV does not appreciably affect the scaling behavior

## Context

This work extends the \(y\)-scaling analysis previously applied to neutron scattering off liquid helium (West, 1975) and electron-nucleus scattering (Day et al., 1987) to the case of deep inelastic electron-proton scattering. The approach provides a physical interpretation of the Nachtman variable \(\xi\) (Nachtman, 1973) and suggests that constituent quark model calculations of proton structure functions (Traini et al., 1997; Thomas, 1984) should be reconsidered, as the data are compatible with a description of the proton in terms of massive bound constituents rather than requiring massless partons.
