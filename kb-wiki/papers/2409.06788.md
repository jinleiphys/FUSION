# Baryon stopping and charge deposition in heavy-ion collisions due to gluon saturation

> We compute baryon and electric charge deposition in high-energy heavy-ion collisions using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) Effective Field Theory, where at leading order charge is deposited through multiple scatterings of valence quarks with a saturated gluon target. A simplified phenomenological formula is derived to describe charge deposition, from which the parametrical dependence with collisional energy and geometry can be extracted. We present an approximate analytical prediction of the so-called baryon stopping parameter $α_B$, which shows excellent agreement with the state-of-the art extractions of $α_B$ from experimental data. These results are further validated using the McDIPPER framework, by computing charge deposition at midrapidity across a range of collision energies ($\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}= 62.4 - 5020$ GeV).

## Key claim

The authors derive an approximate analytical prediction for the baryon stopping parameter α_B ≈ 0.5–0.7 from the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) Effective Field Theory, which is remarkably different from the value α_B/Q ≈ 2 obtained in collinearly factorized pQCD calculations, and shows excellent agreement with state-of-the-art extractions from experimental data.

## Method

The authors compute baryon and electric charge deposition in high-energy heavy-ion collisions using the CGC Effective Field Theory at leading order, where charge is deposited through multiple scatterings of valence quarks with a saturated gluon target. They derive a simplified phenomenological formula for charge deposition based on the Golec-Biernat Wusthoff (GBW) model, where the fundamental dipole gluon distribution is approximated as a Gaussian around the saturation scale, and validate this formula against numerical event-by-event studies using the McDIPPER framework with the IP-Sat dipole model.

## Key numbers

- Baryon stopping parameter α_B ≈ 0.5–0.7 from the CGC-based analytical formula, compared to α_B/Q ≈ 2 from collinearly factorized pQCD calculations
- Collision energy range studied: √s_NN = 62.4 – 5020 GeV for Au-Au collisions
- GBW model parameters: Q_p,0^2 = 0.152 GeV^2, λ = 0.215, σ_0 ≡ 2π B_G with B_G = 0.156 fm^2
- Midrapidity charge deposition follows an exponential decrease with rapidity shift δy = y – y_beam: dQ_i/dη_s ∝ exp[α_i (η_s – y_beam)]

## Context

This work extends the perturbative quark scattering picture by re-imagining it in the context of a saturated gluon target, contrasting with the baryon junction mechanism (Kharzeev 1996) which has received increasing attention for describing baryon charge deposition. The results enable phenomenological studies of baryon stopping at intermediate and high collisional energies, particularly relevant for the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC and future LHC forward detectors such as ALICE FoCal and ALICE 3.
