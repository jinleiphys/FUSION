# On similarities of bulk observables in nuclear and particle collisions

> We study the regularities in the multiparticle production data obtained from different types of collisions indicating the universality of the hadroproduction process. The similarities of such bulk variables like the charged particle mean multiplicity and the pseudorapidity density at midrapidity measured in nucleus-nucleus, (anti)proton-proton and e+e- interactions are analysed according to the dissipating energy of participants and their types. This approach shows a good agreement with the measurements in a wide range of nuclear collision energies from AGS to RHIC. The predictions up to the LHC energies are made and compared to experimental extrapolations.

# Wiki Page: On similarities of bulk observables in nuclear and particle collisions

## Key claim

The paper argues that bulk observables (mean multiplicity and pseudorapidity density at midrapidity) in central nucleus-nucleus collisions, e+e- annihilation, and (anti)proton-proton collisions can be unified by applying a tripling factor to the center-of-mass energy and properly treating the type of participating constituents (quarks vs. nucleons), with the caveat that this scaling works well only for energies above approximately 50 GeV per nucleon, while deviations appear at lower energies.

## Method

The authors compare charged particle mean multiplicity and pseudorapidity density at midrapidity per participant pair across nucleus-nucleus (AGS to RHIC), (anti)proton-proton, and e+e- collisions as a function of center-of-mass energy. They apply a constituent quark picture where in pp collisions only one quark pair interacts (depositing 1/3 of the nucleon energy), while in central nucleus-nucleus collisions all three quarks per nucleon participate coherently, making nucleons effectively structureless like e+e- collisions. The Landau hydrodynamic model is used to derive a correction factor for the midrapidity density that accounts for the different participant masses (constituent quark mass of mp/3 for pp versus proton mass mp for nuclear collisions).

## Key numbers

- The mean multiplicity in pp collisions at center-of-mass energy sqrt(s_pp) is found to be close to that in e+e- collisions at sqrt(s_ee) = sqrt(s_pp)/3, spanning from GeV to TeV energies.
- The averaged mean multiplicity from LEP experiments at sqrt(s_ee) = 130 GeV is 23.35 ± 0.20 ± 0.10, and at sqrt(s_ee) = 200 GeV is 27.62 ± 0.11 ± 0.16.
- The pseudorapidity density at midrapidity in nucleus-nucleus collisions at LHC energy sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV is predicted to be approximately 7.7.
- The pseudorapidity density at midrapidity for pp interactions at LHC at sqrt(s_pp) = 14 TeV is expected to be approximately 6.1 from the CDF fit.
- The mean multiplicity per participant pair in heavy-ion collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV is predicted to be about 3.3 times larger than the data at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.
- The mean multiplicity per participant pair in heavy-ion collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV is about 10% above the N_ch^pp approximation at sqrt(s_pp) = 14 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the observation that bulk observables in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC show similarities to e+e- annihilation data when normalized to participant pairs, as previously noted by PHENIX and STAR collaborations (PHENIX 2002, STAR 2002). The paper contradicts the naive expectation that hadron-hadron collisions at the same energy per nucleon would give identical results, showing instead that pp data at sqrt(s_pp) = 3*sqrt(s_NN) match nuclear data. The constituent quark picture builds on the additive quark model (Levin 1975) and the Landau hydrodynamic model (Landau 1953). The predictions for LHC energies enable comparison with upcoming heavy-ion runs at the LHC and with extrapolations from PHENIX (PHENIX 2004) and ATLAS Monte Carlo tuning (ATLAS 2004).
