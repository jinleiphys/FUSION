# Study of Lattice QCD Form Factors Using the Extended Gari-Krumpelmann Model

> We explore the suitability of a modern vector meson dominance (VMD) model as a method for chiral extrapolation of nucleon electromagnetic form factor simulations in lattice QCD. It is found that the VMD fits to experimental data can be readily generalized to describe the lattice simulations. However, the converse is not true. That is, the VMD form is unsuitable as a method of extrapolation of lattice simulations at large quark mass to the physical regime.

## Key claim

The Extended Gari-Krumpelmann (GKex) vector meson dominance model can be generalized to fit lattice QCD nucleon form factor data at large pion masses, but it is unsuitable as a method for chiral extrapolation to the physical pion mass because the functional form is too sensitive to the fitted parameters.

## Method

The authors took the GKex model of Lomon (2002), which parametrizes nucleon electromagnetic form factors using vector meson pole terms (rho, omega, phi and their excited states) with perturbative QCD asymptotic behavior, and introduced pion-mass dependence into the vector meson masses, coupling constants, and cutoff parameters. They fitted the resulting expressions to quenched lattice QCD data from the QCDSF collaboration (Gockeler et al., 2003) for three lattice spacings, using the Minuit package, and compared the quality of fit to that obtained with the Light Front Cloudy Bag Model (LFCBM) from earlier work by Matevosyan et al. (2005).

## Key numbers

- The best GKex fit (GKex(01-) model) to lattice data at lattice spacing a = 0.26 GeV^{-1} (120 data points) yielded a chi-squared of 103, compared to chi-squared = 81 for the LFCBM fit.
- The lattice data covered pion masses from 0.6 GeV to 1.2 GeV, with corresponding nucleon masses from approximately 1.5 GeV to 2.0 GeV, and momentum transfers Q^2 from 0.6 GeV^2 to 2.3 GeV^2.
- The GKex fits required 10 free parameters (coefficients a and b for the pion-mass dependence of coupling constants and cutoffs) to achieve reasonable descriptions, whereas the LFCBM required only 2 parameters.
- The worst GKex fit (GKex(02L)) gave chi-squared = 671 for the same lattice spacing.

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral extrapolation methods for nucleon electromagnetic form factors, following earlier successful application of the LFCBM by Matevosyan, Thomas, and Miller (2005). It directly tests the vector meson dominance approach of Lomon (2002) against lattice QCD data from the QCDSF collaboration (Gockeler et al., 2003). The finding that VMD is unsuitable for chiral extrapolation, despite fitting lattice data when many parameters are allowed to vary, contrasts with the LFCBM which achieved similar fit quality with far fewer parameters. This result guides future lattice QCD analyses toward models with more constrained quark-mass dependence for extrapolating form factors to the physical regime.
