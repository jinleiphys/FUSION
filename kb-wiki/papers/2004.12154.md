# Bulk viscosity of resonating fermions revisited: Kubo formula, sum rule, and the dimer and high-temperature limits

> The bulk viscosity of two-component fermions with a zero-range interaction is revisited both in two and three dimensions. We first point out that the "standard" Kubo formula employed in recent studies has flaws to give rise to an unphysical divergent bulk viscosity even in a limit where it is supposed to vanish. The corrected Kubo formula as well as the sum rule is then carefully rederived so as to confirm that the bulk viscosity indeed vanishes in the free, unitarity, and dimer limits. We also discuss that the recently found discrepancy between the Kubo formalism and the kinetic theory for the bulk viscosity is attributed to the fact that the quasiparticle approximation assumed by the latter breaks down even in the high-temperature limit.

# Bulk viscosity of resonating fermions revisited: Kubo formula, sum rule, and the dimer and high-temperature limits

## Key claim

The "standard" Kubo formula for bulk viscosity employed in recent studies has flaws that produce an unphysical divergent bulk viscosity even in limits where it is supposed to vanish; the corrected Kubo formula confirms that the bulk viscosity indeed vanishes in the free, unitarity, and dimer limits, and the discrepancy between Kubo formalism and kinetic theory arises because the quasiparticle approximation breaks down even in the high-temperature limit.

## Method

The authors rederive the Kubo formula for bulk viscosity by matching current responses between microscopic and hydrodynamic descriptions, incorporating pressure fluctuations that were neglected in prior work. They evaluate the corrected Kubo formula using the canonical correlation function formalism and the contact-contact response function, applying diagrammatic methods with pair propagators that become exact in the dimer limit and high-temperature limit. The analysis is performed for two-component fermions with zero-range interaction in both two and three dimensions.

## Key numbers

- In the dimer limit (a → +0), the frequency-dependent complex bulk viscosity vanishes at frequencies below the dimer binding energy of 1/(ma²), with no unphysical divergence at zero frequency
- The sum rule for the bulk viscosity simplifies to ∫_{-∞}^{∞} dω/π ζ(ω+i0⁺) = -a^(3-d)/(d²·Ω_{d-1}m) (∂C/∂a)_{N,S} for two-component fermions with zero-range interaction
- In the high-temperature limit, the bulk viscosity at O(z²) gives a term proportional to δ(ω)/a⁴ for a > 0 from bound-bound transitions
- The contact-contact response function in the dimer limit yields R_{CC}(w,0) = (Ω_{d-1}m²N)/a^(4-d) ∫₀^∞ dε/π Im[D(ε-i0⁺)] × (1/(ε+1/(ma²)-w) + 1/(ε+1/(ma²)+w))

## Context

This work corrects the Kubo formula approach used in recent studies by Nishida (2019), Enss (2019), and Hofmann (2020) that employed a "standard" Kubo formula leading to unphysical divergences. The corrected formalism builds on the canonical correlation function approach of Mori (1962) and Luttinger (1964), which was not well appreciated in ultracold atom physics. The paper resolves the discrepancy between Kubo formalism and kinetic theory found in those recent works by showing that the quasiparticle approximation assumed by kinetic theory breaks down even in the high-temperature limit. This enables future systematic resummation of higher-order corrections in fugacity to properly describe the broadening of zero-frequency peaks in the bulk viscosity spectral function.
