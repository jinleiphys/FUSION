# An in-Medium Heavy-Quark Potential from the $Q\bar{Q}$ Free Energy

> We investigate the problem of extracting a static potential between a quark and its antiquark in a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) from lattice-QCD computations of the singlet free energy, $F_{Q\bar{Q}}(r)$. We utilize the thermodynamic $T$-matrix formalism to calculate the free energy from an underlying potential ansatz resummed in ladder approximation. Imaginary parts of both $Q\bar Q$ potential-type and single-quark selfenergies are included as estimated from earlier results of the $T$-matrix approach. We find that the imaginary parts, and in particular their (low-) energy dependence, induce marked deviations of the (real part of the) potential from the calculated free energy. When fitting lattice results of the latter, the extracted potential is characterized by significant long-range contributions from remnants of the confining force. We briefly discuss consequences of this feature for the heavy-quark transport coefficient in the QGP.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the static in-medium heavy-quark potential extracted from lattice-QCD free energies is significantly stronger and longer-ranged than the free energy itself, due to the energy dependence of imaginary parts in both the potential and single-quark selfenergies; this implies that the potential retains a remnant of the confining force well above the critical temperature, contrary to what a weak-coupling analysis would suggest.

## Method

The authors use the thermodynamic T-matrix formalism (finite-temperature Bethe-Salpeter equation in ladder approximation) to relate the static Q\bar{Q} free energy to an underlying potential ansatz. They fit a screened Cornell potential (with real and imaginary parts) to lattice-QCD data for the color-singlet free energy at three temperatures, using complex single-quark selfenergies with energy dependence taken from earlier selfconsistent T-matrix calculations (Riek and Rapp, 2010). The imaginary parts of both the potential and the selfenergies are parameterized with Gaussian energy dependencies and variable strength parameters.

## Key numbers

- String tension: σ = 1.58 GeV/fm (about 60% larger than the vacuum value), kept constant across temperatures.
- Coulomb Debye mass: m_D = 1.22 g T; string screening mass: m_s = 0.8 g T.
- Strong coupling runs with temperature: α_s = 0.288 − 0.05(T/(1.2 T_c) − 1), decreasing from 0.288 at 1.2 T_c to 0.238 at 2.4 T_c.
- Fits performed at T = 1.2, 1.7, and 2.2 T_c, with T_c = 196 MeV, using lattice data from Kaczmarek (2007) with N_f = 2+1 dynamical flavors.
- Dimensionless coefficients: B = 0.73 (rescaling the imaginary part of the potential), C = 2.07 (width of the energy dependence of selfenergies).
- Preliminary heavy-quark diffusion coefficient: D_s (2π T) ≈ 3–5.

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to define an in-medium heavy-quark potential from lattice-QCD free energies, which had been bracketed by using either the free energy or the internal energy as the potential (leading to weakly or strongly coupled systems, respectively). It differs from previous extractions using effective Schrodinger equations for the Wilson loop (Rothkopf, 2011; Burnier, 2012; Bazavov, 2014) and from weak-coupling schemes (Laine, 2006; Beraudo, 2007; Brambilla, 2008) by defining the potential within a field-theoretic scattering equation. The results enable improved calculations of heavy-quark transport coefficients and quarkonium spectral functions in the quark-gluon plasma, with the next step being a fully selfconsistent T-matrix calculation as outlined in Riek and Rapp (2010).
