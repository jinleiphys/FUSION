# Strangeness dynamics and transverse pressure in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions

> We investigate hadron production as well as transverse hadron spectra from proton-proton, proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions from 2 $A\cdot$GeV to 21.3 $A\cdot$TeV within two independent transport approaches (HSD and UrQMD) that are based on quark, diquark, string and hadronic degrees of freedom. The comparison to experimental data on transverse mass spectra from $pp$, $pA$ and C+C (or Si+Si) reactions shows the reliability of the transport models for light systems. For central Au+Au (Pb+Pb) collisions at bombarding energies above $\sim$ 5 A$\cdot$GeV, furthermore, the measured $K^{\pm}$ transverse mass spectra have a larger inverse slope parameter than expected from the default calculations. We investigate various scenarios to explore their potential effects on the $K^\pm$ spectra. In particular the initial state Cronin effect is found to play a substantial role at top SPS and RHIC energies. However, the maximum in the $K^+/π^+$ ratio at 20 to 30 A$\cdot$GeV is missed by ~40% and the approximately constant slope of the $K^\pm$ spectra at SPS energies is not reproduced either. Our systematic analysis suggests that the additional pressure - as expected from lattice QCD calculations at finite quark chemical potential $μ_q$ and temperature $T$- should be generated by strong interactions in the early pre-hadronic/partonic phase of central Au+Au (Pb+Pb) collisions.

## Key claim

The default hadron-string transport models (HSD and UrQMD) fail to reproduce the measured hardening of kaon transverse mass spectra and the maximum in the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio in central Au+Au (Pb+Pb) collisions above \(\sim 5\) A\(\cdot\)GeV, suggesting that additional pressure from strong interactions in the early pre-hadronic/partonic phase, as expected from lattice QCD at finite quark chemical potential \(\mu_q\) and temperature \(T\), is required, rather than purely hadronic rescattering.

## Method

The authors used two independent transport models, HSD and UrQMD (versions 1.3, 2.0, and 2.1), which incorporate hadronic and string degrees of freedom, to simulate hadron production and transverse mass spectra from \(pp\), \(pA\), and \(AA\) collisions across beam energies from 2 \(A\cdot\)GeV to 21.3 \(A\cdot\)TeV. They compared the default calculations to experimental data and tested alternative scenarios including the initial-state Cronin effect, heavy resonance formation, and in-medium potentials to explore their effects on kaon transverse mass spectra and particle ratios.

## Key numbers

- The maximum in the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio at 20 to 30 \(A\cdot\)GeV is missed by the transport models by approximately 40%.
- The inverse slope parameter \(T\) for \(K^\pm\) transverse mass spectra at SPS energies (e.g., 160 \(A\cdot\)GeV) is underestimated severely in both HSD and UrQMD compared to data; for \(pp\) reactions at 160 GeV, experimental slope parameters are \(163\pm 10\) MeV for \(\pi^-\), \(172\pm 17\) MeV for \(K^+\), and \(164\pm 16\) MeV for \(K^-\).
- At RHIC energies (\(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV), UrQMD 1.3 shows a \(\sim 30\%\) higher pion yield at midrapidity than HSD, while 4\(\pi\) integrated yields differ by about 10% between models and compared to data.
- The UrQMD 2.1 scenario reduces pion multiplicity at AGS and SPS energies by about 25% compared to UrQMD 1.3, improving agreement with data.
- The inverse slope parameters from UrQMD 1.3 and HSD differ for pions and antikaons by about 10% at AGS energies (14.6 GeV/\(c\)) in \(pA\) reactions.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by the same group (Weber 2002, MT-prl) to RHIC energies and systematically tests multiple dynamical scenarios. It contradicts the interpretation that the 'step' in kaon slopes and the 'horn' in the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio (proposed by SMES) can be explained by hadronic rescattering alone. The analysis enables future work to incorporate partonic degrees of freedom and lattice QCD-inspired pressure into transport models, and calls for more experimental data in the energy range \(20 \leq \sqrt{s} \leq 200\) GeV to clarify the role of the pre-hadronic phase.
