# Nuclear liquid-gas phase transition within the lattice gas model

> We study the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition on the basis of a two-component lattice gas model. A Metropolis type of sampling method is used to generate microscopic states in the canonical ensemble. The effective equation of state and fragment mass distributions are evaluated in a wide range of temperatures and densities. A definition of the phase coexistence region appropriate for mesoscopic systems is proposed. The caloric curve resulting from different types of freeze-out conditions are presented.

# Nuclear liquid-gas phase transition within the lattice gas model

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a two-component lattice gas model can consistently describe both the nuclear equation of state and fragment mass distributions across the liquid-gas phase transition, but the cluster size distribution alone cannot uniquely identify the phase transition because fragments of intermediate mass can appear outside the coexistence region defined by the common-tangent construction.

## Method

A two-component lattice gas model on a simple cubic lattice with 6 nearest neighbors was simulated using Metropolis Monte Carlo sampling in the canonical ensemble, with nucleon-nucleon interactions represented by a nearest-neighbor square well potential (attractive for like nucleons at -5 MeV, slightly repulsive for proton-neutron pairs at +1 MeV). The system volume was fixed at 6^3 lattice sites with open boundaries, and the density was varied by changing the number of particles for symmetric matter (Z/A = 0.5). Clusters were defined using Hill's pairwise binding criterion based on relative kinetic energy and bond strength.

## Key numbers

- Critical point for the finite system (volume 6^3): (ρ/ρ₀, T, p) ≈ (0.39, 4.8 MeV, 0.15 MeV/fm³)
- Critical point in the macroscopic limit: (ρ/ρ₀, T, P) ≈ (0.58, 6.7 MeV, 0.36 MeV/fm³)
- Lattice spacing a₀ chosen such that fully occupied lattice density equals nuclear ground state density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Interaction parameters: ε_s = -5 MeV for pp and nn pairs, ε_d = +1 MeV for pn pairs
- Fisher exponent τ ≈ 2.2 at the critical point, consistent with the Ising model value τ = 2.21
- χ² per degree of freedom for power-law fits to cluster size distributions: 0.03–0.13
- Isobar studied at p = 0.10 MeV/fm³; isochor studied at ρ/ρ₀ = 0.3
- Maximum fragment mass considered: A_max = 190

## Context

This work extends the lattice gas model approach to nuclear multifragmentation pioneered by Biro (1986) and later developed by Das Gupta and Pan (1990s) and Campi and Krivine (1997), by explicitly calculating the equation of state and phase coexistence region for a finite system. The model connects to the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) and the ALADIN experiment by showing that a plateau in the caloric curve appears only under constant subcritical pressure freeze-out conditions. The paper enables future work incorporating Coulomb repulsion, microcanonical sampling, quantum effects at low excitation energies, and improved cluster de-excitation prescriptions.
