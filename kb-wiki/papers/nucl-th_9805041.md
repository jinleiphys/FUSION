# Self-consistency in the Projected Shell Model

> The Projected Shell Model is a shell model theory built up over a deformed BCS mean field. Ground state and excited bands in even-even nuclei are obtained through diagonalization of a pairing plus quadrupole Hamiltonian in an angular momentum projected 0-, 2-, and 4-quasiparticle basis. The residual quadrupole-quadrupole interaction strength is fixed self-consistently with the deformed mean field and the pairing constants are the same used in constructing the quasiparticle basis. Taking $^{160}Dy$ as an example, we calculate low-lying states and compare them with experimental data. We exhibit the effect of changing the residual interaction strengths on the spectra. It is clearly seen that there are many $J^π= 0^+, 1^+, 4^+$ bandheads whose energies can only be reproduced using the self-consistent strengths. It is thus concluded that the Projected Shell Model is a model essentially with no free parameters.

# Self-consistency in the Projected Shell Model

## Key claim

The Projected Shell Model (PSM) is essentially a parameter-free model for heavy deformed nuclei, because the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction strength is fixed self-consistently from the deformed mean field and the pairing constants are taken from systematics; however, the gamma bandhead energy in 160Dy remains systematically too high even with self-consistent strengths, indicating the Hamiltonian needs additional spin-dependent interactions for fine details.

## Method

The authors diagonalize a pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian in an angular-momentum-projected basis of 0-, 2-, and 4-quasiparticle states built from a Nilsson + BCS deformed mean field. The quadrupole-quadrupole interaction strength χ is determined self-consistently by equating the Hartree potential from the Hamiltonian with the Nilsson stretched potential for a given deformation ε. The monopole pairing constants G_M are taken from systematics with an A-dependent formula, and the quadrupole pairing strength is set to 1/5 of the monopole value with ±10% variation allowed. For 160Dy, the authors artificially vary the quadrupole-quadrupole strength by a factor x and the pairing strength by a factor g to study the sensitivity of bandhead energies.

## Key numbers

- The deformation ε = 0.25 is taken from systematics (ADT95) for 160Dy, with additional calculations at ε = 0.29.
- The Nilsson oscillator energy is ℏω_0 = 41.47 A^{-1/3} MeV.
- The experimental gamma bandhead energy in 160Dy is E_γ(2^+) = 0.974 MeV; the PSM predicts this state at nearly twice that value at x=1.
- A 15% increase in deformation (from ε = 0.25 to ε = 0.29) allows the PSM to reproduce the backbending in the yrast band of 160Dy.
- Quasiparticle pairs with energies lower than 1.5 MeV and two-proton-two-neutron quasiparticle states with energies lower than 3.0 MeV are included in the truncated Hilbert space.
- The quadrupole pairing constant is set as G_Q = γ G_M with γ = 0.18 in the calculations including pairing.

## Context

This work extends the Projected Shell Model formalism of Hara and Sun (1995) by demonstrating that the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction strength can be fixed self-consistently rather than treated as a free parameter, confirming earlier RPA findings by Bes and collaborators (1969) that truncation effects can require renormalization of the interaction. The paper identifies a limitation of the standard pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian for describing the gamma band in 160Dy, suggesting that spin-dependent two-body interactions, as used in the quasiparticle-phonon model of Soloviev (1986), may be needed. The self-consistent approach also removes spurious states in RPA treatments of deformed nuclei as shown by Loiudice and collaborators (1996). This work enables systematic studies of heavy deformed nuclei with a nearly parameter-free shell model, and the authors indicate a forthcoming detailed study of Dy isotopes including B(E2) transitions.
