# A unified algorithm for multi-particle correlations between azimuthal angle and transverse momentum in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions

> Multi-particle correlations between azimuthal angle and mean transverse momentum are a powerful tool for probing size and shape correlations in the initial conditions of heavy-ion collisions. These correlations have also been employed to investigate nuclear structure, including potential nuclear shape phase transitions at the energy frontier. However, their implementation is highly nontrivial, and prior studies have been mostly limited to lower-order correlations, such as the modified Pearson correlation coefficient, $ρ(v_{\rm n}^{2}, [p_{\rm T}])$. This paper presents a unified framework that employs a recursive algorithm, enabling the efficient evaluation of arbitrary-order correlations while maintaining computational efficiency. This framework is demonstrated using widely adopted transport models, including AMPT and HIJING. The proposed unified algorithm for multi-particle correlations between azimuthal angle and transverse momentum provides a systematic and efficient approach for multi-particle correlation analyses. Its application in experiments at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and the Large Hadron Collider facilitates the exploration of nuclear structure at ultra-relativistic energies.

# A unified algorithm for multi-particle correlations between azimuthal angle and transverse momentum in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions

## Key claim

The paper presents a unified recursive algorithm that enables efficient evaluation of arbitrary-order multi-particle correlations between azimuthal angle and transverse momentum, overcoming the previous limitation to lower-order correlations such as the modified Pearson correlation coefficient ρ(v_n^2, [p_T]). The algorithm is demonstrated using AMPT and HIJING transport models, but the authors note that the AMPT results serve as baseline predictions that do not account for specific nuclear shapes of 208Pb or 129Xe.

## Method

The authors develop a bitmask-based recursive algorithm that computes multi-particle moments by filling arrays with all combinations of observables summed over particles, then applies recursion relations to remove self-correlations and extract cumulants. The algorithm is implemented in C++ code and validated using the AMPT (string melting version) and HIJING models for Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV and Xe-Xe collisions at 5.44 TeV. The authors compute specific multi-particle cumulants C(v_n^m, δp_T^k) including C(v_n^2, δp_T), C(v_n^2, δp_T^2), C(v_n^2, δp_T^3), C(v_n^4, δp_T), and C(v_n^4, δp_T^2), with normalization by the mth-order anisotropic flow cumulant c_n{m} and the p_T variance.

## Key numbers

- Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV and Xe-Xe collisions at √s_NN = 5.44 TeV are simulated using AMPT and HIJING models
- The quadrupole deformation β_2 = 0.207 for 129Xe nuclei is cited from prior ALICE measurements (2024)
- For 129Xe, a fixed triaxiality γ = 27° is cited from prior work by Bally et al. (2021)
- The six-particle cumulant C(v_2^4, δp_T^2) shows a sign change in the centrality range of 60-70% in AMPT simulations
- In HIJING simulations, all presented cumulants are consistent with zero in central and mid-central collisions, with non-collective contributions appearing above 60-70% centrality
- The six-particle cumulant C(v_2^4, δp_T^2) from HIJING is consistent with zero across the full centrality range in the standard method

## Context

This work extends the Generic Framework for multi-particle azimuthal correlations (Bilandzic, 2013) and the generalized multi-particle p_T correlations (Nielsen, 2023) to arbitrary-order joint correlations between azimuthal angle and transverse momentum. The algorithm enables experimental measurements at RHIC and the LHC to probe nuclear structure, including potential shape phase transitions around 128-130Xe predicted by the Interacting Boson Model under E(5) symmetry (Iachello, 2001; 2000). The six-particle cumulant C(v_2^4, δp_T^2) is identified as particularly sensitive to γ fluctuations, potentially distinguishing between rigid triaxial deformation and the γ-soft deformation that would indicate a shape phase transition.
