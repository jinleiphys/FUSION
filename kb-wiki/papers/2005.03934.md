# Monopole and dipole transitions of the cluster states of 18O

> On the basis of an extended antisymmetrized molecular dynamics calculation, we study the cluster structure and the of the 0+ and 1- states of 18O. We discuss that several different kinds of cluster states appear in the excitation spectrum, and their monopole and dipole transitions are interesting fingerprints of unique cluster structure. We show that the monopole/dipole transitions are enhanced for the 14C+alpha cluster states, while they are hindered for the molecular-orbit state. We also point out that the ratio of the electric and isoscalar monopole transition strengths gives a good hint for the structure of the excited states.

# Monopole and dipole transitions of the cluster states of 18O

## Key claim
The $0^+_2$ and $1^-_4$ states form the parity doublet of the $^{14}{\rm C}(0^+_1)+\alpha$ cluster states with a 5.5 MeV splitting, but the assignment of the negative-parity partner remains experimentally controversial because the $1^-_1$ state at 4.46 MeV proposed by Gai et al. (1983) is contradicted by all theoretical calculations.

## Method
The authors performed an extended antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculation that couples the $^{14}{\rm C}(0^+_1)+\alpha$ and $^{14}{\rm C}(2^+_1)+\alpha$ channels, using the Gogny D1S effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. They constructed the model space by combining energy-variation-with-constraint AMD wave functions and Brink-type cluster wave functions with varying inter-cluster distance and three orientations of the oblately deformed $^{14}{\rm C}$ cluster, then projected to angular momentum eigenstates and superposed via generator coordinate method.

## Key numbers
- $0^+_2$ state calculated at 6.1 MeV excitation energy (observed at 3.63 MeV)
- $1^-_4$ state at 11.6 MeV identified as the negative-parity doublet partner
- Doublet splitting of 5.5 MeV for the $^{14}{\rm C}(0^+_1)+\alpha$ configuration (compared to 3.5 MeV for $^{12}{\rm C}+\alpha$ in $^{16}{\rm O}$ and 5.9 MeV for $^{16}{\rm O}+\alpha$ in $^{20}{\rm Ne}$)
- $M(E0)$ for $0^+_1\rightarrow 0^+_2$ transition: 0.36 W.u. (experimental values: 0.49 W.u. and 1.01 W.u.)
- $M(E1)$ for $1^-_4\rightarrow 0^+_2$ transition: 17.3 $\times 10^{-2}$ W.u.
- $M(E1)$ for $1^-_4\rightarrow 0^+_5$ transition: 38.7 $\times 10^{-2}$ W.u.
- $M(IS0)$ for $0^+_2\rightarrow 0^+_5$ transition: 2.75 W.u.
- $M(IS1)$ for $1^-_4\rightarrow 0^+_5$ transition: 17.4 W.u.

## Context
This work extends previous AMD calculations by Baba and Kimura (2019) and addresses the long-standing controversy over the negative-parity partner of the $^{14}{\rm C}+\alpha$ cluster band in $^{18}{\rm O}$, following experimental studies by Gai et al. (1983), Curtis et al. (2002), Yildiz et al. (2006), and Avila et al. (2014). The calculation also predicts a molecular-orbit state (MO state) at 11.8 MeV, tentatively corresponding to the structure proposed by von Oertzen et al. (2009), and a higher-nodal $^{14}{\rm C}+\alpha$ state at 13.6 MeV. The results demonstrate that monopole and dipole transition strengths can distinguish between $^{14}{\rm C}+\alpha$ cluster states and molecular-orbit states, enabling future experimental identification of these states through measurement of $E0$, $IS0$, $E1$, and $IS1$ transitions.
