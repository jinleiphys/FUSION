# Spectral function of the $η'$ meson in nuclear medium based on phenomenological models

> The in-medium modification of the spectral function of the $η'$ meson with and without the spatial momentum is studied with the $Tρ$ approximation by employing two phenomenological models for the $η'N$ scattering; one is called coupled channels model and the other the $N(1895)$-dominance model. In the former model, the $η'N$ scattering amplitude is calculated in the unitarized coupled-channel approach involving the $η'N$ channel, while in the latter model the $η'N$ scattering process is dominated by the $N(1895)$ resonance with the spin and parity $J^P=1/2^-$. In the \com{coupled channels model}, one single peak of the in-medium $η'$ mode appears in the spectral function and the peak position shifts to higher energies along with the increase of the nuclear density reflecting the repulsive $η'N$ scattering length of the unitarized coupled-channel amplitude. On the other hand, two branches related to the $η'$ and $N(1895)$-hole modes appear in the $N(1895)$-dominance model. In both models, the shift of the peak position and the width in the spectral function are a few tens of MeV at the normal nuclear density for the $η'$ meson at rest in the nuclear medium. Once the spatial momentum is turned on, the peak positions in the spectral function approach the energies without the nuclear medium effect. Particularly, in the $N(1895)$-dominance model, the peak strength of the $N(1895)$-hole mode gets smaller with the finite momentum and the spectral function comes to have one single peak.

## Key claim

The in-medium modification of the $\eta'$ meson spectral function depends strongly on the chosen model for $\eta'N$ scattering: the coupled channels model yields a repulsive scattering length and a single peak shifting to higher energies with density, while the $N(1895)$-dominance model produces two branches ($\eta'$ and $N(1895)$-hole modes) that merge into a single peak when spatial momentum is applied.

## Method

The $\eta'$ self-energy in symmetric nuclear matter was computed using the $T\rho$ approximation, where the in-medium self-energy is $\Pi_{\eta'}(\omega,\bp;\rho) = T_{\eta'N}(\sqrt{s})\,\rho$ with $\sqrt{s}=(\omega+m_N)^2-\bp^2$. Two phenomenological models for the $s$-wave $\eta'N$ scattering $T$-matrix were employed: (1) a coupled channels model solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a separable interaction kernel derived from the chiral $U(3)$ Lagrangian, including $\pi N$, $\eta N$, $K\Lambda$, $K\Sigma$, and $\eta'N$ channels; (2) an $N(1895)$-dominance model where the amplitude is governed by the $N(1895)$ resonance ($J^P=1/2^-$) near the $\eta'N$ threshold. The spectral function was then obtained from the in-medium propagator $D_{\eta'}(\omega,\bp;\rho) = [\omega^2 - \bp^2 - m_{\eta'}^2 - \Pi_{\eta'}(\omega,\bp;\rho) + i\epsilon]^{-1}$.

## Key numbers

* In the coupled channels model, the $s$-wave $\eta'N$ scattering length is $a_{\eta'N} = (-0.41 + i\,0.04)$ fm, with a negative real part indicating repulsive interaction.
* At normal nuclear density $\rho_0$ for the $\eta'$ at rest, both models yield peak shifts and widths of "a few tens of MeV" (explicitly, the coupled channels model gives a peak near $\omega \approx 0.958$ GeV shifted upward, while the $N(1895)$-dominance model produces two branches).
* The $N(1895)$ resonance parameters used are Breit-Wigner mass $m_{N^*} = 1.8944$ GeV and width $\Gamma_{N^*} = 0.0707$ GeV from the EtaMAID2018 analysis.
* For the scattering-length approximation with $a_{\eta'N} = +0.87$ fm, the peak shifts to $\omega \approx 0.88$ GeV, which is 80 MeV below the vacuum $\eta'$ mass $m_{\eta'} = 0.958$ GeV.
* The experimental scattering length extracted from $pp\to pp\eta'$ data is $a_{\eta'N} = 0^{+0.43}_{-0.43} + i\,0.37^{+0.40}_{-0.16}$ fm.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of in-medium $\eta'$ properties (e.g., Sakai (2013), Jido (2011), Nagahiro (2006)) by incorporating finite-momentum effects on the spectral function, which are crucial for interpreting experimental data from the LEPS2 Collaboration at SPring-8 and the J-PARC E16 experiment. The two models represent distinct scenarios: the coupled channels model (based on Bruns (2019)) yields a repulsive $\eta'N$ interaction, while the $N(1895)$-dominance model (inspired by the EtaMAID2018 analysis of Tiator (2018)) introduces a near-threshold resonance that can create $N(1895)$-hole excitations. The results enable more realistic comparisons with ongoing experiments searching for $\eta'$-nucleus bound states, where no signal has been observed so far (LEPS2/BGOegg (2020), n-PRiME/Super-FRS (2016)).
