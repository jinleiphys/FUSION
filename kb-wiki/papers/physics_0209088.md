# Gauge Invariant Evaluation of Nuclear Polarization with Collective Model

> The nuclear-polarization (NP) energies with the collective model commonly employed in the NP calculations for hydrogenlike heavy ions are found to have serious gauge violations when the ladder and cross diagrams only are taken into account. Using the equivalence of charge-current density with a schematic microscopic model, the NP energy shifts with the collective model are gauge invariantly evaluated for the $1s_{1/2}$ states in $^{208}_{~82}$Pb$^{81+}$ and $^{238}_{~92}$U$^{91+}$.

# Gauge Invariant Evaluation of Nuclear Polarization with Collective Model

## Key claim
The nuclear collective model commonly used for nuclear-polarization (NP) calculations in hydrogenlike heavy ions exhibits serious gauge violations when only ladder and cross diagrams are considered; however, by including the seagull diagram, whose contribution is determined from the ground-state charge distribution and does not depend on the details of the nuclear excitation model, gauge-invariant NP energy shifts can be obtained for the collective model.

## Method
The authors evaluate NP energy shifts for the 1s₁/₂ states of hydrogenlike ²⁰⁸Pb⁸¹⁺ and ²³⁸U⁹¹⁺ using the collective model with charge and current transition densities that satisfy the continuity equation. They compute contributions from three Feynman diagrams (ladder, cross, and seagull) in both Feynman and Coulomb gauges, using the same low-lying states and giant resonances as in prior work (Nefiodov et al. 1996, Yamanaka et al. 2001), and additionally include 4⁻ and 5⁻ giant resonances. The seagull contribution is derived from the equivalence between the collective model transition densities and those of a schematic microscopic model with a separable interaction, ensuring gauge invariance when all three diagrams are included.

## Key numbers
- For ²⁰⁸Pb⁸¹⁺: total NP energy shift is −35.4 meV (Feynman gauge) and −35.0 meV (Coulomb gauge); the Coulomb-only (CNP) result is −30.7 meV.
- For ²³⁸U⁹¹⁺: total NP energy shift is −204.7 meV (Feynman gauge) and −203.0 meV (Coulomb gauge); the Coulomb-only result is −192.4 meV.
- The seagull contribution to the 1⁻ multipole in ²⁰⁸Pb⁸¹⁺ is −42.3 meV (Feynman gauge) and −7.3 meV (Coulomb gauge); in ²³⁸U⁹¹⁺ it is −65.7 meV (Feynman gauge) and −3.9 meV (Coulomb gauge).
- The net transverse effect is about 14–15% of the Coulomb energy shift in ²⁰⁸Pb⁸¹⁺ (−30.7 meV), and about 6% in ²³⁸U⁹¹⁺ due to the dominant Coulomb contribution from the lowest 2⁺ state.
- The lowest 2⁺ state in ²³⁸U⁹¹⁺ has an excitation energy ΔE₂ = 44.9 keV and contributes −131.6 meV (Coulomb gauge).

## Context
This work extends the NP calculations of Plunien et al. (1989, 1995) and Nefiodov et al. (1996) by demonstrating that the collective model, like the microscopic random phase approximation (RPA) model of Haga et al. (2002), requires the seagull diagram to restore gauge invariance. It corrects the earlier conclusion of Yamanaka et al. (2001) regarding transverse effects, which were based on calculations omitting the seagull contribution. The agreement between the collective model and the microscopic RPA model provides stability for NP predictions with respect to the choice of nuclear model, enabling more reliable tests of quantum electrodynamics in strong electromagnetic fields.
