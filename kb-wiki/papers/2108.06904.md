# Connecting Theory to Heavy Ion Experiment

> Only a fraction of all $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ hyperons detected in heavy ion collisions are produced from the hot and dense matter directly at the hadronization. These hyperons are called the {\em primary} hyperons. The rest of the hyperons are products of the decays of heavier hyperon states, which in turn are produced at the hadronization. As such, the polarization of only primary hyperons can be described with the formulae introduced in Sect. 8. For the rest of the hyperons, the polarization transfer in the decays has to be computed, and convoluted with the polarization of the mother hyperon. In this chapter, a derivation of the polarization transfer coefficients, as well as the computation of the mean polarization of all $Λ$ hyperons detected in the experiment, is presented. The chapter is concluded with the calculation of the resonance contributions to the global and local $Λ$ polarizations.

## Key claim

Only a fraction of all Λ and anti-Λ hyperons detected in heavy ion collisions are primary (produced directly at hadronization); the majority come from decays of heavier hyperons, and the polarization of these non-primary hyperons must be computed via polarization transfer coefficients from the mother particles, which may alter the polarization features compared to primary-only predictions.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for the polarization transfer coefficients in two-body decays of hyperons (H → Λ + X) using a helicity formalism and Wigner rotation matrices, treating the strong decay Σ* → Λπ, the electromagnetic decay Σ⁰ → Λγ, and the weak decay Ξ → Λπ. They compute the mean spin vector of the daughter Λ in its rest frame from the spin density matrix of the mother hyperon, assuming local thermodynamic equilibrium and neglecting post-hadronization scattering. For global (momentum-integrated) polarization, they derive a linear proportionality coefficient C_S between the mother's and daughter's mean spin vectors; for local (momentum-dependent) polarization, they use a reduced spin density matrix formalism.

## Key numbers

- For the strong decay Σ* → Λπ, the polarization transfer coefficient is C_S = 1/3, meaning the daughter Λ's mean spin vector is parallel to the mother's.
- For the electromagnetic decay Σ⁰ → Λγ, the polarization transfer coefficient is C_S = -1/3, meaning the daughter Λ's mean spin vector is antiparallel to the mother's.
- At RHIC with √s_NN = 200 GeV, primary Λ hyperons were predicted to contribute only one quarter of all measured Λ hyperons (citing Becattini 2016).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Becattini (2016) on hyperon polarization in heavy ion collisions by providing detailed derivations of polarization transfer in feed-down decays. It addresses the "sign puzzles" where theoretical calculations using only primary Λ (e.g., Karpenko 2016, Becattini 2017, Xia 2018, Florkowski 2019) contradict experimental measurements from STAR (Niida 2018, Adam 2019) for the sign of transverse and longitudinal local polarizations. The results enable quantitative inclusion of resonance feed-down effects in hydrodynamic and transport models of spin polarization, potentially resolving these discrepancies.
