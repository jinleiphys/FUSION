# Possible $η' d$ bound state and its $s$-channel formation in the $γd \to ηd$ reaction

> We theoretically investigate a possibility of an $η^{\prime} d$ bound state and its formation in the $γd \to ηd$ reaction. First, in the fixed center approximation to the Faddeev equations we obtain an $η^{\prime} d$ bound state with a binding energy of 25 MeV and width of 19 MeV, where we take the $η^{\prime} N$ interaction with a coupling to the $ηN$ channel from the linear $σ$ model. Then, in order to investigate the feasibility from an experimental point of view, we calculate the cross section of the $γd \to ηd$ reaction at the photon energy in the laboratory frame around 1.2 GeV. As a result, we find a clear peak structure with the strength $\sim$ 0.2 nb/sr, corresponding to a signal of the $η^{\prime} d$ bound state in case of backward $η$ emission. This structure will be prominent because a background contribution coming from single-step $η$ emission off a bound nucleon is highly suppressed. In addition, the signal can be seen even in case of forward $η$ emission as a bump or dip, depending on the relative phase between the bound-state formation and the single-step background.

## Key claim

The authors predict an $\eta^{\prime} d$ bound state with binding energy 25 MeV and width 19 MeV using the fixed center approximation to the Faddeev equations, but this result depends on the specific $\eta^{\prime} N$ interaction taken from the linear $\sigma$ model; the observable signal in the $\gamma d \to \eta d$ reaction appears as a peak of approximately 0.2 nb/sr only for backward $\eta$ emission, while forward emission may show a bump or dip depending on the unknown relative phase between bound-state formation and background.

## Method

The authors first solved the Faddeev equation in the fixed center approximation for the $\eta^{\prime} p n$ three-body system, using an $\eta^{\prime} N$ interaction derived from the linear $\sigma$ model with coupling to the $\eta N$ channel and parameters from Sakai (2013). They then calculated the $\gamma d \to \eta d$ reaction cross section by constructing amplitudes from phenomenological $\gamma N \to \eta N$ and $\gamma N \to \eta^{\prime} N$ amplitudes (the former from the Bonn-Gatchina partial wave analysis BG2014-02, the latter from a model fitted to experimental data), combined with the multiple scattering amplitude for the $\eta^{\prime} d$ system and the transition to $\eta d$.

## Key numbers

- $\eta^{\prime} d$ bound state binding energy: 25 MeV, measured from the $\eta^{\prime} d$ threshold
- $\eta^{\prime} d$ bound state width: 19 MeV
- $\eta^{\prime} d$ bound state pole position: $2809 - 10i$ MeV in the complex energy plane
- $\eta^{\prime} N$ bound state pole position: $1889 - 6i$ MeV, corresponding to binding energy 8 MeV and width 12 MeV
- Peak cross section for $\gamma d \to \eta d$ via bound state formation: approximately 0.2 nb/sr for backward $\eta$ emission
- Photon energy in laboratory frame for bound-state search: approximately 1.2 GeV
- $\eta^{\prime} d$ threshold energy: 2.833 GeV

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of $\eta^{\prime}$-nucleus bound states motivated by predictions of $\eta^{\prime}$ mass reduction in nuclear medium due to partial chiral symmetry restoration, as calculated in chiral effective models by Jido (2011) and Sakai (2013). The authors previously investigated $\eta^{\prime} n$ bound states in the $\gamma d \to \eta n p$ reaction (Sekihara, 2016), and here extend the approach to the $\eta^{\prime} d$ system. The predicted bound state could be tested experimentally at facilities capable of photon beams around 1.2 GeV, and the reaction mechanism allows center-of-mass energy scans by varying photon energy, which is advantageous for experimental searches.
