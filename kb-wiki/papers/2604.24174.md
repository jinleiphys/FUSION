# Survival of Pairing Correlations and Shell Effects at Scission in Finite-Temperature Nuclear Fission: Implications for Odd-Even Staggering

> We investigate the finite-temperature evolution of microscopic free-energy corrections in nuclear fission, focusing on pairing and shell effects near scission. The analysis is based on a finite-temperature BCS treatment combined with the Strutinsky method and is performed for representative deformation points along the fission path. Both pairing and shell contributions exhibit regular thermal attenuation, but their deformation dependencies differ substantially. In particular, pairing remains strongly deformation-dependent in the scission region, and its free-energy contribution differs markedly between the constant and surface-dependent pairing-strength prescriptions. The shell correction near scission is also significant at low temperature and is progressively suppressed with increasing excitation energy. These results support the interpretation of odd-even staggering in fragment charge yields as a manifestation of pairing correlations surviving into the strongly deformed pre-scission configuration. They also show that pairing and shell effects should be treated separately in finite-temperature dynamical calculations, with distinct deformation- and temperature-dependent attenuation laws.

## Key claim

Pairing correlations survive into the strongly deformed pre-scission configuration and remain deformation-dependent even near scission, but the magnitude of this survival depends critically on whether a constant or surface-dependent pairing-strength prescription is adopted; the surface-dependent prescription predicts pairing persists to substantially higher temperatures and produces a much larger negative free-energy correction in the scission region, especially for neutrons in the symmetric fission valley.

## Method

The authors performed finite-temperature BCS calculations combined with the Strutinsky method for representative deformation points along the fission path of 236U, using the Fourier-over-Spheroid (FoS) shape parametrization. The macroscopic part of the deformation energy came from the Lublin-Strasbourg Drop (LSD) model, while microscopic shell and pairing corrections were obtained from the Yukawa-folded single-particle potential. They compared two pairing prescriptions: constant pairing strength G=const and a surface-dependent pairing strength G∝Surface, computing the temperature-dependent pairing gap, pairing free-energy correction, and shell free-energy correction for the ground state, second saddle, and asymmetric and symmetric scission configurations.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for pairing gap suppression follows Tc = 0.608 Δ0, with a fitted scaling parameter a = 6.098 in the empirical formula Δ(T) = Δ0 tanh[a(Tc - T)/Δ0].
- In the asymmetric scission configuration with the surface-dependent pairing prescription, the pairing free-energy correction reaches values of approximately -3 MeV at low excitation energies for both protons and neutrons.
- In the symmetric scission configuration with the surface-dependent prescription, the neutron pairing free-energy correction exceeds -6 MeV in absolute value at low excitation energies, while the constant-G solutions are close to zero.
- The standard BCS estimate for the critical temperature is Tc ≈ 0.57 Δ(T=0), where Δ(T=0) is the zero-temperature pairing gap.

## Context

This work extends earlier Langevin-based calculations of isotope-resolved fission yields by Pomorski et al. (2024, 2025), which found that odd-even staggering in charge-resolved yields remained systematically weaker in calculations than in evaluated data, pointing to a missing description of pairing-related structure at scission. The study provides a microscopic finite-temperature complement to those phenomenological studies and builds on the systematic analysis of pairing in fission by Zdeb et al. (2025). The results enable improved finite-temperature dynamical calculations where pairing and shell effects are treated separately with distinct deformation- and temperature-dependent attenuation laws, and may help connect pairing persistence to the interpretation of odd-even staggering in fragment charge yields and to prompt-neutron emission systematics.
