# A dynamical, confining model and hot quark stars

> We explore the consequences of an equation of state (EOS) obtained in a confining Dyson-Schwinger equation model of QCD for the structure and stability of nonstrange quark stars at finite-T, and compare the results with those obtained using a bag-model EOS. Both models support a temperature profile that varies over the star's volume and the consequences of this are model independent. However, in our model the analogue of the bag pressure is (T,mu)-dependent, which is not the case in the bag model. This is a significant qualitative difference and comparing the results effects a primary goal of elucidating the sensitivity of quark star properties to the form of the EOS.

## Key claim
A Dyson-Schwinger equation model with a dynamically generated, (T,μ)-dependent bag pressure yields qualitatively different predictions for quark star structure compared to the constant-bag model, but the authors caution that their equation of state applies only to pure quark-matter stars because hadronic contributions are omitted.

## Method
The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for nonstrange quark stars in β-equilibrium with electrons, using an equation of state derived from a confining Dyson-Schwinger equation model (EOS^DC) where the quark pressure vanishes in the confined domain and the bag pressure is (T,μ)-dependent. They compared results with the standard bag model (EOS^BM) using a constant bag pressure B_P = 57 MeV/fm^3, assuming an adiabatic temperature profile with constant entropy per baryon across the star.

## Key numbers
- Critical chemical potential at T=0: μ_c ≈ 0.28 η, where η ≈ 1 GeV is the model mass-scale
- Nuclear saturation density: n_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}
- Critical energy density at T=50 MeV in DC model: ε_c = 1.4 MeV/fm^3
- Bag model critical energy density: ε_c = 4 B_P = 228 MeV/fm^3
- Maximum mass of pure quark star in DC model: M ∼ 0.6-0.7 M_⊙ (approximately one-third of bag model maximum)
- Maximum radius at T(0)=50 MeV: R ∼ 8-10 km (DC model gives lower bound)
- Mass reduction when central temperature increases from 0 to 50 MeV: ∼ 20%
- Gravitational mass radiated during cooling: 2-4%
- For fixed baryon number N^DC = 0.866 N_⊙: M(T=0) = 0.54 M_⊙, M(T=50 MeV) = 0.55 M_⊙
- For fixed baryon number N^BM = 5.766 N_⊙: M(T=0) = 1.62 M_⊙, M(T=50 MeV) = 1.69 M_⊙

## Context
This work extends Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of confinement and chiral symmetry breaking (Roberts and Schmidt, 1998) to astrophysical applications, contrasting with the bag model approach used by Weber (1990) and others. The results contradict the common assumption of isothermal temperature profiles in hot quark stars (Weber, 1990) and show that the maximum mass decreases with temperature rather than increasing. The authors identify the inclusion of hadronic bound states (as proposed by the Regensburg group) as the necessary next step for realistic star modeling.
