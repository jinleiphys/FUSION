# Sound velocity peak induced by the chiral partner in dense two-color QCD

> Recently, the peak structure of the sound velocity was observed in the lattice simulation of two-color and two-flavor QCD at the finite quark chemical potential. The comparison with the chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) result was undertaken, however, the ChPT failed in reproducing the peak structure. In this study, to extend the ChPT framework, we incorporate contributions of the $σ$ meson, that is identified as the chiral partner of pions, on top of the low-energy pion dynamics by using the linear sigma model (LSM). Based on the LSM we derive analytic expressions of the thermodynamic quantities as well as the sound velocity within a mean-field approximation. As a result, we find that those quantities are provided by sums of the ChPT results and corrections, where the latter is characterized by a mass difference between the chiral partners, the $σ$ meson and pion. The chiral partner contributions are found to yield a peak in the sound velocity successfully. We furthermore show that the sound velocity peak emerges only when $m_σ>\sqrt{3}m_π$ and $μ_q > m_π$, with $m_{σ(π)}$ and $μ_q$ being the $σ$ meson (pion) mass and the quark chemical potential, respectively. The correlation between the sound velocity peak and the sign of the trace anomaly is also addressed.

## Key claim

The peak structure of the sound velocity observed in lattice simulations of two-color QCD, which chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) fails to reproduce, can be successfully generated by incorporating the sigma meson as the chiral partner of pions using the linear sigma model (LSM), but only under the conditions that the sigma meson mass satisfies \(m_\sigma > \sqrt{3}m_\pi\) and the quark chemical potential satisfies \(\mu_q > m_\pi\).

## Method

The authors employed the linear sigma model (LSM) for two-flavor two-color QCD, which introduces the sigma meson as the chiral partner of pions alongside the Nambu-Goldstone boson dynamics. They derived analytic expressions for thermodynamic quantities (pressure, number density, susceptibility, energy density) and the sound velocity within a mean-field approximation, showing these quantities are sums of ChPT results plus corrections characterized by the mass difference between the sigma meson and pion. The sound velocity was computed using the zero-temperature relation \(c_s^2 = n/(\mu_q \chi)\) from the baryon number density \(n\) and baryon susceptibility \(\chi\).

## Key numbers

- The critical chemical potential for the baryon superfluid phase transition is \(\mu_q^{\rm cr} = m_\pi/2\).
- The sound velocity peak emerges only when \(m_\sigma > \sqrt{3}m_\pi\) and \(\mu_q > m_\pi\).
- In the ChPT result, the sound velocity approaches \(c_s^2 = 1\) as \(\mu_q \to \infty\), while the lattice simulation shows it should approach the conformal limit \(c_s^2 = 1/3\).
- The ChPT sound velocity is given by \((c_s^{\rm ChPT})^2 = (1 - 1/\bar\mu^4)/(1 + 3/\bar\mu^4)\) where \(\bar\mu = \mu_q/\mu_q^{\rm cr} = 2\mu_q/m_\pi\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) framework of Kogut et al. (1999, 2000) and Son and Stephanov (2000), which describes only Nambu-Goldstone boson dynamics and fails to reproduce the sound velocity peak observed in lattice simulations of two-color QCD by Iida et al. (2022). The LSM approach builds on the work of Suenaga (2022) to incorporate the sigma meson as the chiral partner of pions. This enables a hadronic description of the sound velocity peak in dense baryonic matter, providing a bridge between lattice observations and effective field theory, and addresses the correlation between the sound velocity peak and the sign of the trace anomaly.
