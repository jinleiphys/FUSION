# Is there hydrodynamic flow at RHIC ?

> It is argued that the observation of anisotropic azimuthal distribution of final state particles alone is insufficient to show whether the formed matter at RHIC behaves like hydrodynamic flow. Examining the intrinsic interaction (or correlation) of the formed matter should provide more definite judgement. To the end, a spatial-dependent azimuthal multiplicity-correlation pattern is suggested. It shows clearly in the pattern that there are two kinds of interactions at the early stage of Au + Au collisions at $\sqnn=200$ GeV generated by RQMD with hadron re-scattering and AMPT with string melting. This is out of the expectation from the elliptic flow driven by anisotropic expansion.

## Key claim

The observation of anisotropic azimuthal distributions (elliptic flow $v_2$) alone is insufficient to prove that the formed matter at RHIC behaves like hydrodynamic flow; a spatial-dependent azimuthal multiplicity-correlation pattern reveals two opposite intrinsic interactions in transport models, which is contrary to the expectation that only anisotropic expansion drives the flow.

## Method

The authors introduce a neighboring angular-bin multiplicity correlation pattern $C_{m,m+1}$ defined by dividing the $2\pi$ azimuthal angle into 50 equal bins and computing the normalized bin-bin correlation. They apply this measure to 249,824 events from RQMD with hadron re-scattering and 204,004 events from AMPT with string melting for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, analyzing the centrality, transverse-momentum, and rapidity dependence of the correlation pattern and its Fourier coefficient $u_2$ (anisotropic correlation coefficient).

## Key numbers

* Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV: 249,824 events generated by RQMD with hadron re-scattering and 204,004 events by AMPT with string melting.
* The azimuthal range $2\pi$ is partitioned uniformly into 50 equal-size angular bins for the correlation pattern.
* In peripheral collisions, the correlation pattern is $\cos2\phi$ (in-plane) like; in near-central collisions, it becomes $-\cos2\phi$ (out-of-plane) like; in mid-central collisions, the pattern is flat.
* The anisotropic correlation coefficient $u_2$ is positive in peripheral collisions, negative in mid-central collisions, and more negative in central collisions, and is almost independent of $p_t$ in peripheral and mid-central collisions but decreases rapidly with increasing $p_t$ in central collisions.

## Context

This work extends the debate on whether the matter created at RHIC behaves as a perfect fluid, as suggested by hydrodynamic descriptions of $v_2$ (e.g., Sorge (1999), Molnar and Gyulassy (2002)), or arises from non-equilibrium processes (e.g., Asakawa, Bass, Muller, and Nonaka (2008); Hwa (2007); Anchishkin and Yezhov (2008)). The proposed correlation pattern, building on prior work by Wu Yuanfang et al. (2005), provides a direct experimental test: if the measured correlation pattern has the same anisotropy as the azimuthal distribution, it supports hydrodynamic flow; if not, it contradicts the hydrodynamic interpretation. The results from RQMD and AMPT models suggest that the formed matter in these transport models does not behave like hydrodynamic flow, motivating experimental measurement at RHIC and LHC.
