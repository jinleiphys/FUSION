# Correlations with fluctuating strings

> We present a semi-analytic approach to forward-backward multiplicity correlations in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions, based on particle emission from strings with fluctuating end-points. We show that with the constraints from rapidity spectra, one can obtain bounds for the magnitude of the standard measures of the forward-backward fluctuations. The method is generic under the assumption of independent production from sources. For definiteness, we use the wounded quark model for Au+Au and d+Au collisions at the energy of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV.

# Correlations with fluctuating strings

## Key claim

The paper shows that forward-backward multiplicity correlations in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions can be bounded from above and below using only the constraint of measured one-body pseudorapidity spectra, under the assumption of independent particle emission from strings with fluctuating end-points. The bounds are derived semi-analytically and depend on the specific distribution of string end-points, which cannot be uniquely determined from the one-body spectra alone.

## Method

The authors model particle emission from strings whose end-points fluctuate in spatial rapidity according to distributions extracted from PHOBOS pseudorapidity spectra for d-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, using the wounded quark model. They derive analytic expressions relating the one-body emission profile f(eta) to the cumulative distribution functions of the string end-points, then compute two-body distributions and covariances for three distinct cases: equal end-point distributions (g1=g2), disjoint supports, and an intermediate case using valence quark distributions. The forward-backward correlation measure a_{11} is computed via Legendre expansion of the correlation function C(eta1,eta2) for Au-Au and d-Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- The analysis uses sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV for both Au+Au and d+Au collisions.
- The covariance from string end-point fluctuations dominates over fluctuations in the number of wounded quarks, which contribute to a_{11} at the level of 10-20 percent only.
- The predicted a_{11} coefficient for the disjoint case is about a factor of 3 smaller than for the g1=g2 case.
- The pseudorapidity range used for the a_{nm} coefficients is Y=1.
- The a_{11} coefficient scales approximately with the inverse of the number of sources (strings).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Broniowski (2015), which only considered one-end fluctuations of strings, to include fluctuations of both string end-points. It merges the wounded quark model of Bialas (1976) with string-based particle production models such as the Lund model of Andersson (1983). The approach enables semi-analytic bounds on forward-backward correlation measures that can be tested against experimental data from RHIC and the LHC, and provides a framework for understanding the relative importance of string end-point fluctuations versus fluctuations in the number of sources.
