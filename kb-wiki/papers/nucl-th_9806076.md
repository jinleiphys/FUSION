# Saturnales

## Key claim

This concluding talk at the "20 years of Saturne-2" colloquium argues that the Saturne-2 accelerator was closed for political and budgetary reasons, not because it lacked interesting physics problems or international competitiveness; the author asserts that both justifications are false.

## Method

The author presents a personal selection of memorable physics achievements from the Saturne-2 program over its 20-year lifetime, drawing on presentations given at the colloquium and his own involvement in experiments. He reviews specific experimental programs including elastic proton-nucleus scattering, pick-up reactions, near-threshold meson production, nucleon-nucleon scattering with polarized beams, deuteron polarimetry, charge-exchange reactions, and few-nucleon spin measurements, while also discussing the accelerator's technical capabilities and the fate of its unfinished data analyses.

## Key numbers

- The $^{12}$C$(p,d)^{11}$C$^*$ reaction was measured for half a dozen excited states of $^{11}$C at 700 MeV using SPESI.
- The $\vec{d}p\to\,^3$He$\,\eta$ reaction was measured near threshold at SPESIV, showing an unexpectedly large cross section.
- The $\vec{d}\vec{p}\to\vec{p}d$ triple-spin measurement was performed at 1.6 GeV, representing a unique technical achievement.
- Deuteron tensor polarimeters AHEAD and POLDER were calibrated at Saturne, enabling $T_{20}$ measurements in $ed\to e\vec{d}$ at CEBAF at $q^2$ values twice as high as previous experiments.
- The $\vec{d}p\to\{pp\}n$ charge-exchange reaction was studied for deuterons of 1–2 GeV using two-proton detection in the focal plane of SPESIV.
- The rotating energy degrader ("roue de Beurtey") allowed simultaneous $A_y$ measurements in elastic proton-proton scattering at 16 different energies.
- Polarized ion beams up to $^{129}$Xe (30$^{+}$) were available from MIMAS, though heavy-ion requests represented only 10% of total beam time.

## Context

This talk summarizes and celebrates the physics output of the Saturne-2 accelerator facility at Saclay, which was shut down in December 1997. It extends earlier work by Palevsky and collaborators at the Cosmotron on elastic proton-nucleus scattering, and by the Gatchina-Saclay collaboration on matter densities using the Glauber model. The near-threshold $\eta$ production work inspired subsequent studies at Uppsala, Jülich (COSY), and GSI, including searches for $\eta$-nucleus quasi-bound states and charge-symmetry violation in $dd\to\,^4$He$\,\pi^0$. The deuteron polarimeters calibrated at Saturne were later used at CEBAF for form factor measurements. The talk also notes that many datasets from Saturne remain unanalyzed on tape and calls for resources to complete their analysis.
