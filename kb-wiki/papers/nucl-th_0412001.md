# Different formulations of 3He and 3H photodisintegration

> Different momentum space Faddeev-like equations and their solutions for the radiative pd-capture and the three-nucleon photodisintegration of 3He are presented. Applications are based on the AV18 nucleon-nucleon and the Urbana IX three nucleon forces. Meson exchange currents are included using the Siegert theorem. A very good agreement has been found in all cases indicating the reliability of the used numerical methods. Predictions for cross sections and polarization observables in the pd-capture and the complete three nucleon breakup of 3He at different incoming deuteron/photon energies are presented.

# Different formulations of ³He and ³H photodisintegration

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that three different momentum-space Faddeev-like formulations for computing transition amplitudes in pd-capture and three-nucleon photodisintegration yield very good agreement with each other, but only when sufficient partial waves are included; the agreement serves as an internal benchmark of numerical reliability rather than a claim of agreement with experiment.

## Method

The authors solve Faddeev-like integral equations in momentum space for the radiative pd-capture (equivalent to two-body photodisintegration of the 3N bound state) and for three-body photodisintegration of ³He, using the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential alone and combined with the Urbana IX three-nucleon force. Meson exchange currents are included via the Siegert theorem. Three distinct formulations are compared: method 1 (solving coupled equations for auxiliary states U' and U''), method 2 (using the identity 1+P = ½P(1+P) to reformulate the kernel), and method 3 (computing the radiative capture amplitude directly from the Faddeev equation for the initial Nd channel state). All equations are solved by iteration with Padé summation in a partial-wave decomposition.

## Key numbers

- Incoming deuteron laboratory energy E_d = 300 MeV, equivalent to photon laboratory energy E_γ ≈ 106 MeV in photodisintegration
- At E_d = 17.5 MeV, comparison to Sagara data shows reasonable agreement for tensor analyzing powers A_XX and A_YY
- For three-body photodisintegration at E_γ = 100 MeV, differences between methods 1_{NN+3NF} and 2_{NN+3NF} remain below 1% across about 300,000 configurations
- For predictions based on NN interaction only, differences between methods 1_{NN} and 2_{NN} are below 5% for the majority of configurations
- Convergence in total angular momentum J_max of the three-body system: J_max = 7/2 is very close to final prediction with J_max = 15/2; increasing to J_max = 19/2 produces no perceptible change
- Convergence in two-body subsystem partial waves: method 1_{NN+3NF} requires j_max = 3 for near-convergence; method 2_{NN} requires at least j_max = 5

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Faddeev calculations for electron-induced processes (Golak et al., 1995), pd radiative capture (Golak et al., 2000), and 3N bound state photodisintegration (Skibinski et al., 2003) by systematically comparing different numerical formulations. It complements configuration-space variational calculations by Kievsky, Viviani, and Rosati (2001) and Lorentz integral transform calculations by the Trento group (Efros et al., 2000), with which benchmark comparisons were previously published (Golak et al., 2002). The internal consistency demonstrated here enables reliable tests of nuclear dynamics against data and provides a foundation for future calculations using chiral effective field theory currents and forces, which were under development at the time (Epelbaum et al., 2004; Park, Min, and Rho, 1996).
