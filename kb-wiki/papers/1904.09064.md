# Theory on Hadrons in Nuclear Medium

> After decades-long attempts to measure the mass shift and understand the origin of hadron mass, it became clear that one has to analyze hadrons with small vacuum width. Also, to identify the effect of chiral symmetry breaking, one has to start by looking at chiral partners. In this talk, I will review why such consideration inevitably led us to consider $K^*$ and $K_1$ in nuclear matter [T. Song, T. Hatsuda, S H Lee, PLB792 (2019) 160-169]. With the kaon beam at JPARC, one could observe the mass shift of both particles in a nuclear target experiment. Once the masses and mass difference of $K^*$ and $K_1$ mesons are measured, we will be closer to understanding the origin of the hadron masses and the effects of chiral symmetry breaking in them.

## Key claim

The mass difference between the chiral partner mesons $K^*$ and $K_1$ in nuclear matter is sensitive to the chiral order parameter, and measuring this mass shift with a kaon beam at JPARC could provide a path to understanding the origin of hadron masses and the effects of chiral symmetry breaking, provided one focuses on hadrons with small vacuum width to avoid contamination from final-state interactions.

## Method

The author reviews the QCD sum rule approach for the vector ($K^*$) and axial-vector ($K_1$) currents in the open strange channel, using the operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension-6 operators in both vacuum and nuclear matter. The medium effects are incorporated via the linear density approximation, where condensates are expanded as vacuum values plus nucleon matrix elements times baryon density. The difference between the vector and axial-vector correlation functions is identified as a chiral order parameter, and the mass shifts are extracted from the sum rules by projecting onto the longitudinal component at zero three-momentum.

## Key numbers

- The upper limit of the mass shift of $K_1^-$ in nuclear matter is as large as -249 MeV, and for $K_1^+$ it is -35 MeV, from the QCD sum rule analysis of Song, Hatsuda, and Lee (2019).
- The vacuum widths of $K^*(892)$ and $K_1(1270)$ are 50.3 MeV and 90 MeV, respectively, which are smaller than their non-strange counterparts (the $\rho$ at 150 MeV and $a_1$ at 250-600 MeV).
- The KEK-PS E325 collaboration reported evidence of $\phi$ meson mass shift by 3.4% at normal nuclear density.
- The CBELSA/TAPS Collaboration observed the $\omega$ meson mass decreased by about 60 MeV at an estimated average nuclear density of 0.6 $\rho_0$.

## Context

This work extends the in-medium QCD sum rule approach originally developed by Hatsuda and Lee (1991) for vector mesons, and builds on the experimental strategy of focusing on mesons with small vacuum width as advocated by Metag (2017). The specific calculation for $K_1$ in nuclear matter was performed by Song, Hatsuda, and Lee (2019), and the proposal to use $K^*$ and $K_1$ as chiral partners follows from the general framework of Lee (2013) showing that the vector-axial vector difference in the open strange channel is a chiral order parameter. The paper enables future experiments at JPARC with kaon beams to measure the mass shift of both $K^*$ and $K_1$, which would complement earlier dilepton measurements by KEK-PS E325 and hadronic decay measurements by CBELSA/TAPS.
