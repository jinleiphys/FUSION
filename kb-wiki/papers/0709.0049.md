# On the possibility of P-violation at finite baryon-number densities

> We show how the introduction of a finite baryon density may trigger spontaneous parity violation in the hadronic phase of QCD. Since this involves strong interaction physics in an intermediate energy range we approximate QCD by a σmodel that retains the two lowest scalar and pseudoscalar multiplets. We propose a novel mechanism based on interplay between lightest and heavy meson states which cannot be realized solely in the Goldstone boson (pion) sector and thereby is unrelated to the one advocated by Migdal some time ago. Our approach is relevant for dense matter in an intermediate regime of few nuclear densities where quark percolation does not yet play a significant role.

# On the possibility of P-violation at finite baryon-number densities

## Key claim

The authors show that spontaneous parity violation may occur in the hadronic phase of QCD at finite baryon density, but only in an intermediate density regime of a few times normal nuclear density where quark percolation is not yet significant. This mechanism requires the interplay of at least two scalar/pseudoscalar multiplets and cannot be realized in the Goldstone boson (pion) sector alone, distinguishing it from the earlier mechanism proposed by Migdal.

## Method

The authors approximate QCD by a generalized linear sigma model containing the two lowest-lying scalar and pseudoscalar multiplets, with an effective potential constructed to respect SU(2)L × SU(2)R symmetry. A finite baryon chemical potential is introduced via a quark-meson coupling term, and the one-loop contribution to the effective potential from constituent quarks is computed. The conditions for spontaneous parity breaking are derived by analyzing the vacuum expectation values of the scalar and pseudoscalar fields, and the model parameters are fixed using known meson spectroscopy.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear density: ρB ≈ 0.17 fm⁻³, corresponding to an average distance of 1.8 fm between nucleons
- The critical density for spontaneous parity breaking is estimated at ρB,crit ≈ 0.5 fm⁻³ ≈ 3 ρB,nuclear
- The critical chemical potential is μc ≈ 4.3 F₀, where F₀ = 100 MeV is the pion decay constant in the model
- At the critical point, the scalar condensate σ₁,crit ≈ 1.8 F₀ and the Fermi momentum pF ≈ 3.9 F₀ ≈ 1.44 pF,nuclear
- Input meson masses used: mΣ₁ = 0.7 GeV, mΠ = 1.3 GeV, mΣ₂ = 1.5 GeV
- Derived coupling constants: λ₁ ≈ 0.15, λ₄ ≈ 0.35, λ₃ ≈ 3.6, Δ₁₁ ≈ 2.7 F₀², Δ₂₂ ≈ 11 F₀²

## Context

This work extends the earlier conjecture by Migdal regarding parity violation in nuclear matter, but proposes a novel mechanism requiring heavy meson states that cannot be realized in the pion sector alone. It builds on the Vafa-Witten theorem (Witten, 1984) which excludes spontaneous parity breaking at zero chemical potential, and addresses the regime where lattice QCD methods face difficulties due to the sign problem at finite density (reviewed by Philipsen, 2006). The results are relevant for compact star physics (Itoh, 1975; Greiner et al., 1995) and heavy-ion collisions, and could potentially be tested by lattice expansions in small chemical potential at finite temperature.
