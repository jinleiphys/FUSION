# Aspects of the confinement mechanism in Coulomb-gauge QCD

> Phenomenological consequences of the infrared singular, instantaneous part of the gluon propagator in Coulomb gauge are investigated. The corresponding quark Dyson-Schwinger equation is solved, neglecting retardation and transverse gluons and regulating the resulting infrared singularities. While the quark propagator vanishes as the infrared regulator goes to zero, the frequency integral over the quark propagator stays finite and well-defined. Solutions of the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the pseudoscalar and vector mesons as well as for scalar and axial-vector diquarks are obtained. In the limit of a vanishing infrared regulator the diquark masses diverge, while meson properties and diquark radii remain finite and well-defined. These features are interpreted with respect to the resulting aspects of confinement for colored quark-quark correlations.

## Key claim

In a simplified model of Coulomb-gauge QCD that neglects retardation and transverse gluons, meson masses and charge radii remain finite and well-defined as the infrared regulator goes to zero, while diquark masses diverge like 1/μ_IR, removing colored quark-quark correlations from the physical spectrum. This provides a qualitative demonstration of confinement for colored two-quark states, though the authors caution that the results are qualitatively but not quantitatively significant.

## Method

The authors solved the quark Dyson-Schwinger (gap) equation in Coulomb gauge using an instantaneous approximation with the infrared-singular color-Coulomb potential V_C(k) = σ_c/(k^2)^2, regulated by an infrared cutoff μ_IR. They then solved the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for pseudoscalar and vector mesons as well as scalar and axial-vector diquarks, obtaining bound-state masses and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes as functions of μ_IR and taking the limit μ_IR → 0.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb string tension σ_c is estimated to be approximately 600-750 MeV for three colors, inferred from the generally used asymptotic string tension of about 440 MeV
- Diquark masses diverge like 1/μ_IR as the infrared regulator μ_IR approaches zero, for values of 10^{-4} ≤ μ_IR ≤ 10^{-2} (in units of √σ_c)
- The pion mass is identically zero for all values of μ_IR in the chiral limit
- Meson masses (ρ meson) stabilize in the limit μ_IR → 0, while diquark masses increase without bound
- Charge radii for both pion and scalar diquark remain finite in the limit μ_IR → 0

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations of the gluon propagator in Coulomb gauge by Szczepaniak (2003) and lattice calculations by Greensite (2004) which showed that the color-Coulomb potential rises linearly with distance. The study builds on the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach previously applied extensively in Landau-gauge QCD by Maris (1997, 1999, 2002) and Alkofer (2002), and provides motivation for nucleon studies using a covariant quark-diquark picture. The results enable future investigations including retardation effects and transverse gluons, which the authors note are currently underway.
