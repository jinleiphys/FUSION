# Strange two-baryon interactions using chiral effective field theory

> We have constructed the leading order strangeness S=-1,-2 baryon-baryon potential in a chiral effective field theory approach. The chiral potential consists of one-pseudoscalar-meson exchanges and non-derivative four-baryon contact terms. The potential, derived using SU(3)_f symmetry constraints, contains six independent low-energy coefficients. We have solved a regularized Lippmann-Schwinger equation and achieved a good description of the available scattering data. Furthermore a correctly bound hypertriton has been obtained.

## Key claim

The leading-order chiral effective field theory potential, constrained by SU(3)_f symmetry and fitted to low-energy hyperon-nucleon scattering data, provides a good description of the available scattering data and yields a correctly bound hypertriton, but only for cutoff values between 550 and 700 MeV and for natural values of the low-energy coefficients.

## Method

The authors constructed the leading-order strangeness S=-1 and S=-2 baryon-baryon potential using chiral effective field theory with Weinberg power counting. The potential includes one-pseudoscalar-meson exchanges and non-derivative four-baryon contact terms, with six independent low-energy coefficients derived from SU(3)_f symmetry constraints. They solved a regularized coupled-channels Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a cutoff regulator function exp[-(p'^4 + p^4)/Lambda^4] and fitted five of the six low-energy coefficients to the 35 available low-energy hyperon-nucleon scattering data points.

## Key numbers

- The chiral EFT potential was fitted to 35 low-energy hyperon-nucleon scattering data points.
- Cutoff values Lambda = 550 to 700 MeV were used in the regulator function.
- The sixth low-energy coefficient (present only in isospin zero S=-2 channels) was varied in the range of 2.0 to -0.05 times its natural value, for a cutoff of Lambda = 600 MeV.
- The hypertriton was correctly bound, and reasonable Lambda separation energies for ^4_Lambda H were predicted.

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach, previously applied successfully to nucleon-nucleon interactions by Entem and Machleidt (2003) and Epelbaum et al. (2005), to the strangeness S=-1 and S=-2 baryon-baryon sectors. Prior meson-exchange models for hyperon-nucleon interactions included the Juelich '04 model by Haidenbauer et al. (2005) and the Nijmegen NSC97f model by Rijken et al. (1999). The results enable future next-to-leading order calculations combining nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon interactions in a unified chiral EFT framework.
