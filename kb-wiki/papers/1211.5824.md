# Faddeev-Chiral Unitary Approach to the $K^- d$ scattering length

> Our earlier Faddeev three-body study in the $K^-$-deuteron scattering length, $A_{K^-d}$, is revisited here in the light of the recent developments in two fronts: {\it (i)} the improved chiral unitary approach to the theoretical description of the coupled $\Kbar N$ related channels at low energies, and {\it (ii)} the new and improved measurement from SIDDHARTA Collaboration of the strong interaction energy shift and width in the lowest $K^-$-hydrogen atomic level. Those two, in combination, have allowed us to produced a reliable two-body input to the three-body calculation. All available low-energy $K^-p$ observables are well reproduced and predictions for the $\Kbar N$ scattering lengths and amplitudes, $(πΣ)^\circ$ invariant-mass spectra, as well as for $A_{K^-d}$ are put forward and compared with results from other sources. The findings of the present work are expected to be useful in interpreting the forthcoming data from CLAS, HADES, LEPS and SIDDHARTA Collaborations.

## Key claim

The authors revisit their earlier Faddeev three-body calculation of the $K^-$-deuteron scattering length using improved chiral unitary two-body amplitudes constrained by the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen data, finding that the resulting $A_{K^-d}$ predictions depend on which chiral model ($c$-model vs $s$-model) is used for the $\Kbar N$ input, with the $c$-model giving $A_{K^-d} = (-1.62 + i\,1.26)$ fm and the $s$-model giving $(-1.68 + i\,1.34)$ fm.

## Method

The authors solve coupled Bethe-Salpeter equations for the $\Kbar N$ system using an on-shell ansatz with driving terms from a chiral Lagrangian up to next-to-leading order (NLO), including Weinberg-Tomozawa contact terms, NLO contact terms, and $s$-channel Born terms (the $u$-channel terms are omitted to avoid unphysical branch cuts). The resulting two-body $t$-matrices are then used as input to a relativistic Faddeev three-body calculation of the $K^- d$ scattering length, which includes the deuteron $D$-state and accounts for nucleon binding, target recoil, and intermediate nucleon-hyperon interactions.

## Key numbers

- $K^-$-hydrogen atomic level shift and width from SIDDHARTA: $\Delta E_{1s} = -283 \pm 36$ eV, $\Gamma_{1s} = 541 \pm 92$ eV
- $K^-$-hydrogen atomic level shift and width from KEK: $\Delta E_{1s} = -323 \pm 64$ eV, $\Gamma_{1s} = 470 \pm 231$ eV
- $K^-$-hydrogen atomic level shift and width from DEAR: $\Delta E_{1s} = -193 \pm 37$ eV, $\Gamma_{1s} = 249 \pm 115$ eV
- $K^- d$ scattering length from the $c$-model: $A_{K^-d} = (-1.62 + i\,1.26)$ fm
- $K^- d$ scattering length from the $s$-model: $A_{K^-d} = (-1.68 + i\,1.34)$ fm
- $\chi^2$ per degree of freedom: 1.22 for the $c$-model, 1.21 for the $s$-model
- Threshold branching ratios: $\gamma = 2.36 \pm 0.04$, $R_c = 0.664 \pm 0.011$, $R_n = 0.189 \pm 0.015$

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier Faddeev three-body study (Bahaoui et al., 2002, 2003) by incorporating improved chiral unitary $\Kbar N$ amplitudes that now include NLO terms and are constrained by the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen data (Bazzi et al., 2011), replacing the earlier use of only Weinberg-Tomozawa interactions with a global form factor. The results are compared with the fixed center approximation calculations of Meissner et al. (2006) and Doring et al. (2011), as well as with the separable potential approach of Shevchenko (2012), and are expected to help interpret forthcoming data from CLAS, HADES, LEPS, and SIDDHARTA collaborations.
