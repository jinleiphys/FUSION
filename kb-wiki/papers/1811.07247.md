# Search for the $Σ^*$ state in $Λ^+_c \to π^+ π^0 π^-Σ^+$ decay by triangle singularity

> A $Σ^*$ resonance with spin-parity $J^P = 1/2^-$ and mass in the vicinity of the $\bar{K}N$ threshold has been predicted in the unitary chiral approach and inferred from the analysis of CLAS data on the $γp \to K^+ π^0 Σ^0$ reaction. In this work, based on the dominant Cabibbo favored weak decay mechanism, we perform a study of $Λ_c^+ \to π^+ π^0 Σ^*$ with the possible $Σ^*$ state decaying into $π^- Σ^+$ through a triangle diagram. This process is initiated by $ Λ_c^+ \to π^+ \bar{K}^*N $, then the $\bar{K}^*$ decays into $\bar{K} π$ and $\bar{K} N$ produce the $Σ^*$ through a triangle loop containing $\bar{K}^* N \bar{K}$ which develops a triangle singularity. We show that the $π^- Σ^+$ state is generated from final state interaction of $\bar{K}N$ in $S$-wave and isospin $I=1$, and the $Λ_c^+ \to π^+ π^0 π^- Σ^+$ decay can be used to study the possible $Σ^*$ state around the $\bar{K}N$ threshold. The proposed decay mechanism can provide valuable information on the nature of the $Σ^*$ resonance and can in principle be tested by facilities such as LHCb, BelleII and BESIII.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the $\Lambda_c^+ \to \pi^+ \pi^0 \pi^- \Sigma^+$ decay can be used to study a possible $\Sigma^*$ resonance with $J^P = 1/2^-$ near the $\bar{K}N$ threshold (around 1430–1434 MeV), but the signal appears as a pronounced cusp structure rather than a clear pole, with the narrow width (about 10 MeV) being a consequence of the triangle singularity mechanism and the nearly-bound nature of the state.

## Method

The authors calculate the decay amplitude for $\Lambda_c^+ \to \pi^+ \pi^0 \pi^- \Sigma^+$ using a triangle diagram mechanism: the $\Lambda_c^+$ first decays via external $W^+$ emission into $\pi^+ \bar{K}^* N$ (with $\bar{K}^*N$ in isospin $I=0$), then the $\bar{K}^*$ decays into $\bar{K}\pi$, and the $\bar{K}N$ pair undergoes final-state interaction in $S$-wave and isospin $I=1$ to produce $\pi\Sigma$. The triangle loop integral is regularized with a cutoff of 630 MeV, and the two-body $\bar{K}N \to \pi\Sigma$ amplitudes are obtained from the Bethe-Salpeter equation using tree-level potentials from Oset (1997).

## Key numbers

- The branching ratio $Br(\Lambda_c^+ \to \pi^+ K^{*-} p) = (1.4 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-2}$ is used to determine the coupling constant $|A|^2 = (3.9 \pm 1.4) \times 10^{-16}~\text{MeV}^{-2}$.
- The predicted branching ratio for $\Lambda_c^+ \to \pi^+ \pi^0 \pi^- \Sigma^+$ is $(3 \pm 1) \times 10^{-4}$.
- A narrow peak in the $\pi^- \Sigma^+$ invariant mass distribution appears around 1434 MeV, with a width of about 10 MeV.
- A bump structure in the $\pi^0 \pi^- \Sigma^+$ invariant mass distribution appears around 1880 MeV due to the triangle singularity.
- The cutoff used to regularize the loop functions is 630 MeV, and the coupling $g = m_V/2f_\pi$ uses $m_V = 780$ MeV and $f_\pi = 93$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of triangle singularities in charmed baryon decays, following the approach of Dai (2018) for related $\Lambda_c^+$ decays. It addresses the long-standing question of a possible $\Sigma^*$ resonance near the $\bar{K}N$ threshold, which was predicted in chiral unitary approaches by Oller (2000) and Jido (2003) and inferred from CLAS data on $\gamma p \to K^+ \pi^0 \Sigma^0$ analyzed by Roca (2013). The predicted branching ratio of $10^{-4}$ is within measurable range for LHCb, BelleII, and BESIII, enabling experimental tests of the nature of this state. The situation is compared to the $a_0(980)$ resonance, which also appears as a pronounced cusp both theoretically (Oller, 1997) and experimentally (Kornicer, 2016).
