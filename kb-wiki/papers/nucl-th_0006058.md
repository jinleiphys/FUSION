# Effective range function below threshold

> We demonstrate that the kernel of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, associated with interactions consisting of a sum of the Coulomb plus a short range nuclear potential, below threshold becomes degenerate. Taking advantage of this fact, we present a simple method of calculating the effective range function for negative energies. This may be useful in practice since the effective range expansion extrapolated to threshold allows to extract low-energy scattering parameters: the Coulomb-modified scattering length and the effective range.

# Effective range function below threshold

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for interactions consisting of a Coulomb plus short-range nuclear potential, the kernel of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation becomes degenerate below threshold, enabling a direct calculation of the effective range function at negative energies without encountering Coulomb singularities. The method is limited to momenta where the regular Coulomb wave function does not diverge, with an upper bound determined by the range of the nuclear potential and a practical lower bound where the Sturmian expansion convergence deteriorates.

## Method

The author reformulates the two-potential scattering problem using a Coulomb-modified Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a specially constructed K-matrix Green's function that is real analytic in k² and free from Coulomb bound-state poles. For negative energies (k = ip with p > 0), the Coulomb Green's function is expanded in a Sturmian basis of Laguerre polynomials, making the integral equation kernel degenerate and reducing the problem to solving a finite system of linear algebraic equations. The method is tested on the Reid nucleon-nucleon ¹S₀ potential for three values of the Coulomb strength parameter (Z = +1, 0, -1).

## Key numbers

- The Sturmian expansion method was applied in the momentum range 20 MeV < p < 65 MeV, with the upper bound set by the one-pion-exchange tail of the Reid potential which limits admissible momenta to below 65 MeV.
- The rank N of the Sturmian approximation required for convergence varied from approximately 100 to several hundred at the low-energy end.
- For the nn and pp ¹S₀ scattering lengths (cited from experiment): a_nn = -18.6 ± 0.5 fm and a_pp = -7.828 ± 0.008 fm, with most of the difference attributed to Coulomb interaction.
- The effective range r₀ was found to be little affected by Coulomb interaction, with all three curves (Z = +1, 0, -1) showing nearly identical slopes near zero energy.
- For attractive Coulomb potential (Z < 0), the K-matrix pole lies in the physical region very close to threshold.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of effective range theory for charged particles, building on prior developments of the Coulomb-modified effective range function by Hull (1956), Hamilton (1956), Cornille (1965), Humblet (1963), Lambert (1969), and Kok (1980). The Sturmian expansion technique for Coulomb Green's functions follows the approach of Weinberg (1963), Rotenberg (1961), and Chen (1978). The method enables direct calculation of the effective range function below threshold without relying on the effective range expansion for extrapolation, and can be generalized to complex nuclear potentials for applications to hadronic atoms, where it provides an alternative to the Deser-Trueman shift formula (Deser 1954, Trueman 1961).
