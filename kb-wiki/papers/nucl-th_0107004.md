# N=Z Waiting Point Nucleus $^{68}$Se: Collective Rotation and High-K Isomeric States

> The structure of collective states and quasi-particle excitations in $^{68}$Se is analyzed by using the projected shell model. It is found that the $g_{9/2}$ orbitals play a decisive role for the structure of this nucleus. At the prolate minimum, alignment of the low-K states causes the observed first backbending in moment of inertia and we predict a second backbending that has not yet been reported. At the oblate minimum, quasi-particles having high-K can form long-lived isomeric states. The structure of $^{68}$Se is discussed with respect to the isomers and their possible impact on the determination of its nuclear mass.

# N=Z Waiting Point Nucleus $^{68}$Se: Collective Rotation and High-K Isomeric States

## Key claim
The projected shell model (PSM) calculation reproduces the observed oblate ground-state band and the first backbending at spin I=8 in the prolate band of $^{68}$Se, and predicts a second sharp backbending at I=16 that has not yet been reported. The existence of high-K isomeric states at approximately 5 MeV excitation energy could explain the discrepancy between the GANIL mass measurement of $^{68}$Se and the fragmentation experiments that set an upper limit of 150 ns for the half-life of $^{69}$Br.

## Method
The authors performed projected shell model (PSM) calculations for $^{68}$Se using a quadrupole plus pairing Hamiltonian with particles in three major shells (N=2,3,4) for both neutrons and protons. The basis included 0-, 2-, and 4-quasiparticle states built on deformed Nilsson orbitals with BCS pairing, and rotational symmetry was restored by angular momentum projection. Separate diagonalizations were performed at deformation $\varepsilon_2=0.28$ for the prolate minimum and $\varepsilon_2=-0.24$ for the oblate minimum, neglecting coupling between the two minima based on the approximately 3 MeV barrier calculated by Sarriguren et al. (1999).

## Key numbers
- Deformation parameters: $\varepsilon_2=0.28$ for prolate states, $\varepsilon_2=-0.24$ for oblate states
- Monopole pairing strength: $G_M=[18.0-14.5(N-Z)/A]/A$ for neutrons and $G_M=14.5/A$ for protons
- Quadrupole pairing strength $G_Q$ proportional to $G_M$ with proportionality constant 0.20
- First backbending in the prolate band occurs at spin I=8 (observed)
- Predicted second backbending in the prolate band at spin I=16
- High-K isomeric states predicted at approximately 5 MeV excitation energy with bandhead spin I=8
- Measured mass excess of $^{68}$Se from GANIL: $-52.347\pm0.080$ MeV
- Upper limit for $^{69}$Br half-life from fragmentation experiments: 150 ns
- Theoretical barrier between prolate and oblate minima: approximately 3 MeV (Sarriguren et al., 1999)

## Context
This work extends the application of the projected shell model to the N=Z waiting point nucleus $^{68}$Se, which is critical for understanding the rp-process in type I X-ray bursts. The calculation builds on experimental identification of coexisting oblate and prolate rotational bands by Fischer et al. (2000) and constrained Hartree-Fock calculations by Sarriguren et al. (1999). The prediction of high-K isomeric states addresses a specific discrepancy between the GANIL mass measurement by Lalleman et al. (2000) and fragmentation experiments that set an upper limit on the $^{69}$Br half-life. The results enable future experimental searches for these isomers and network calculations of the rp-process to determine their impact on nucleosynthesis and energy generation.
