# A numerical relativity approach to the initial value problem in asymptotically Anti-de Sitter spacetime for plasma thermalization - an ADM formulation

> This article studies a numerical relativity approach to the initial value problem in Anti-de Sitter spacetime relevant for dual non-equilibrium evolution of strongly coupled non-Abelian plasma undergoing Bjorken expansion. In order to use initial conditions for the metric obtained in arXiv:0906.4423 we introduce new, ADM formalism-based scheme for numerical integration of Einstein's equations with negative cosmological constant. The key novel element of this approach is the choice of lapse function vanishing at fixed radial position, enabling, if needed, efficient horizon excision. Various physical aspects of the gauge theory thermalization process in this setup have been outlined in our companion article arXiv:1103.3452. In this work we focus on the gravitational side of the problem and present full technical details of our setup. We discuss in particular the ADM formalism, the explicit form of initial states, the boundary conditions for the metric on the inner and outer edges of the simulation domain, the relation between boundary and bulk notions of time, the procedure to extract the gauge theory energy-momentum tensor and non-equilibrium apparent horizon entropy, as well as the choice of point for freezing the lapse. Finally, we comment on various features of the initial profiles we consider.

## Key claim

This paper presents a numerical relativity framework for solving Einstein's equations with negative cosmological constant in asymptotically Anti-de Sitter spacetime, specifically designed to evolve initial data from Beuf (2009) that cannot be evolved using the Eddington-Finkelstein characteristic formulation because the initial time hypersurface in Fefferman-Graham coordinates does not align with ingoing null light rays.

## Method

The authors adopt the ADM (Arnowitt-Deser-Misner) formalism with spacelike fixed-time hypersurfaces to solve the 5-dimensional Einstein equations with negative cosmological constant for a boost-invariant, holographic plasma system undergoing Bjorken expansion. The key novel element is a choice of lapse function that vanishes at a fixed radial position, which enables efficient horizon excision without requiring knowledge of the event horizon location. The initial data are specified on a bulk time hypersurface coinciding with the Fefferman-Graham τ = 0 hypersurface, using a single function of radial coordinate C₀(u) that parametrizes the most general initial state subject to regularity constraints and asymptotic AdS boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

- Thermalization criterion: |(τ/w)(dw/dτ) / F_hydro^(3rd order)(w) - 1| < 0.005, where w = T_eff(τ) × τ
- Viscous hydrodynamics applies for Tτ_th ≥ 0.6-0.7, consistent with RHIC assumptions (e.g., T = 500 MeV and τ_th = 0.25 fm gives Tτ = 0.63)
- The Fefferman-Graham coordinate frame breaks down at u₀^(FG) = π/(2γ) for the analytic example where γ = (1/2)√3 π² (T_eff^(i))²
- The early time energy density expansion allows only even powers of proper time: ε(τ)|_(τ≈0) = (3/8)N_c²π² (T_eff^(i) + (1/2)T_eff''(0)τ² + ...)⁴

## Context

This work extends the early-time analysis of Beuf (2009), who used Fefferman-Graham coordinates to derive initial data for boost-invariant holographic thermalization but could not evolve past the coordinate singularity at finite radial position. It provides the gravitational technical details underlying the physical results reported in Heller, Janik, and Witaszczyk (2011). The approach contrasts with the characteristic Eddington-Finkelstein formulation used by Chesler and Yaffe (2009, 2010), which cannot start from τ = 0 at the boundary due to non-commuting limits. The ADM formulation enables future generalizations to other holographic setups and allows comparison with the recently developed Generalized Harmonic formulation adapted to asymptotically AdS spacetimes by Pretorius (2012).
