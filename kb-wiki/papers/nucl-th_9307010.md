# Triton calculations with the new Nijmegen potentials

> Triton properties are calculated using new nucleon-nucleon potentials, which were fit to the world nucleon-nucleon data. All potentials are charge dependent and explicitly incorporate the mass difference between the charged and neutral pions. Three of these models have a nearly optimal chi**2 per degree of freedom and can therefore be considered as alternative partial wave analyses, which in quality can almost compete with the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis. The triton binding energy obtained with three local models (Nijm II, Reid93, AV18) can be summarized as 7.62 +_ 0.01 MeV, which is nearly 900 keV lower than experiment. The non-local model Nijm I binds by 7.72 MeV.

## Key claim

The triton binding energy obtained with three local models (Nijm II, Reid93, AV18) can be summarized as 7.62 ± 0.01 MeV, which is nearly 900 keV lower than the experimental value of 8.48 MeV; the non-local model Nijm I binds by 7.72 MeV, suggesting that local potentials which fit the NN scattering data very well bind the triton by a unique value of about 7.62 MeV, but this does not prove uniqueness.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations for the triton bound state using up to 34 three-nucleon partial-wave states, which guarantees partial-wave convergence at the level of 10 keV, including all partial waves of the NN interaction with J ≤ 4. They used five new nucleon-nucleon potentials (Nijm I, Nijm II, Nijm92, Reid93, and AV18) that were fit to the world nucleon-nucleon data, are charge dependent, and explicitly incorporate the mass difference between charged and neutral pions. Three of these models (Nijm I, Nijm II, Reid93) have a nearly optimal chi-squared per degree of freedom of 1.03 and can be considered as alternative partial-wave analyses.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy for three local potentials (Nijm II, Reid93, AV18): 7.62 ± 0.01 MeV, nearly 900 keV lower than the experimental value of 8.48 MeV
- Triton binding energy for non-local Nijm I potential: 7.72 MeV (34 channels)
- Triton binding energy for Nijm92 potential: 7.68 MeV
- Triton binding energy for original Nijm78 potential: 7.63 MeV (with pp-type 1S0 force, requiring ~100 keV increase)
- Chi-squared per datum: Nijm I = 1.03, Nijm II = 1.03, Reid93 = 1.03, AV18 = 1.30, Nijm92 = 1.92, Nijm PWA93 = 0.99
- Deuteron D-state probabilities: Nijm I = 5.66%, Nijm II = 5.64%, Reid93 = 5.70%, AV18 = 5.65%, Nijm92 = 5.61%
- Epsilon-1 mixing parameter at 50 MeV: Nijm I = 2.09 degrees, Nijm II = 2.00 degrees, Reid93 = 2.03 degrees, AV18 = 2.16 degrees, Nijm92 = 1.98 degrees, Nijm PWA93 = 2.11 ± 0.05 degrees
- Percentage of triton potential energy from tensor force: Nijm I = 72%, Nijm II = 52%, Reid93 = 57%, AV18 = 52%, Nijm92 = 77%
- Percentage of triton potential energy from OPEP: Nijm I = 74%, Nijm II = 67%, Reid93 = 71%, AV18 = 77%, Nijm92 = 113%
- Pion masses determined from consistency check: neutral pion = 135.6 ± 1.3 MeV, charged pion = 139.4 ± 1.0 MeV

## Context

This work extends the program of calculating triton properties with realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials, following earlier calculations by Chen, Payne, Friar, and Gibson (1985-1988) that found triton underbinding varying from 0.2 to 1.1 MeV for a wide variety of potential models. The new Nijmegen potentials are the best ever constructed in terms of fit to scattering data, and the results provide a benchmark against which calculations with other potential models should be compared. The 100 keV difference between the non-local Nijm I and local Nijm II models demonstrates the effect of nonlocality in the NN force, shifting the physics issues in the triton problem toward the origin and presence of nonlocalities, alongside the question of three-nucleon forces.
