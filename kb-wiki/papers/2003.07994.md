# Fermion dynamical symmetry and strongly-correlated electrons: a comprehensive model of high-temperature superconductivity

> We review application of the SU(4) model of strongly-correlated electrons to cuprate and iron-based superconductors. A minimal self-consistent generalization of BCS theory to incorporate antiferromagnetism on an equal footing with pairing and strong Coulomb repulsion is found to account systematically for the major features of high-temperature superconductivity, with microscopic details of the parent compounds entering only parametrically. This provides a systematic procedure to separate essential from peripheral, suggesting that many features exhibited by the high-$T\tsub c$ data set are of interest in their own right but are not central to the superconducting mechanism. More generally, we propose that the surprisingly broad range of conventional and unconventional superconducting and superfluid behavior observed across many fields of physics results from the systematic appearance of similar algebraic structures for the emergent effective Hamiltonians, even though the microscopic Hamiltonians of the corresponding parent states may differ radically from each other.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a minimal self-consistent generalization of BCS theory that incorporates antiferromagnetism on an equal footing with pairing and strong Coulomb repulsion can account systematically for the major features of high-temperature superconductivity, with the caveat that microscopic details of the parent compounds enter only parametrically and many observed features may be peripheral rather than central to the superconducting mechanism.

## Method

The authors apply fermion dynamical symmetry methods based on Lie algebras and generalized coherent states to construct an SU(4) model of strongly-correlated electrons. They implement an emergent-symmetry truncation of the Hilbert space that identifies collective modes associated with pairing, antiferromagnetism, and charge degrees of freedom, then solve the resulting model using spontaneously-broken symmetry approximations analogous to those used in nuclear structure physics and BCS theory.

## Key numbers

- Cuprate superconductivity onset occurs at approximately 3-5% hole density per copper site in the copper-oxygen plane
- The pseudogap temperature T* lies above the superconducting transition temperature Tc in the underdoped region
- The superconducting gap and pseudogap exhibit opposite doping dependence at low doping

## Context

This work extends the fermion dynamical symmetry model (FDSM) previously applied to nuclear structure physics into condensed matter systems, providing a unified framework for cuprate and iron-based superconductors. The SU(4) model contains much of the physics of resonating valence bond (RVB) models without some of their specific assumptions, provides a microscopic derivation of the Zhang SO(5) model as an approximate solution, and relates to Hubbard or t-J models through different approaches to Hilbert space truncation. The paper enables a systematic procedure to separate essential from peripheral features in the high-Tc data set and proposes that diverse superconducting and superfluid phenomena across physics may share a common algebraic description.
