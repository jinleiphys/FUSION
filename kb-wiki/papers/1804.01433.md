# A simple approach to the chaos-order contributions in nuclear spectra

> The simple one-parameter nearest neighbor-spacing distribution (NNSD) is suggested for statistical analysis of nuclear spectra. This distribution is derived within the Wigner-Dyson approach in the linear approximation for the level repulsion density of quantum states. The obtained NNSD gives the individual information on the Wigner and Poisson contributions in agreement with that of the statistical experimental distributions of collective states in deformed nuclei. Using this NNSD, one finds that the symmetry breaking due to the fixing of projections of the angular momentum of collective states enhances a chaos as a shift of the NNSD from the Poisson to Wigner distribution behavior.

## Key claim

The paper derives a one-parameter nearest-neighbor spacing distribution (NNSD) from a linear approximation to the level repulsion density within the Wigner-Dyson approach, which provides separate quantitative information on Poisson (order) and Wigner (chaos) contributions to nuclear spectra, but only under the assumption of fast convergence of the normalization integral over spacings to infinity.

## Method

The authors start from the general Wigner-Dyson formula for the NNSD, which depends on the level repulsion density g(s). They approximate g(s) as a linear function a + b s, substitute this into the Wigner-Dyson formula, and impose both normalization conditions (integral over p(s) equals 1 and integral over s p(s) equals 1) to reduce the two parameters a and b to a single parameter w. The resulting one-parameter LWD1 distribution is then fitted to experimental NNSDs from collective states in deformed actinide nuclei and compared with the two-parameter LWD2 and Brody distributions.

## Key numbers

* For the circular billiard (Poisson limit): a_1 = 0.98, b_1 = 0.02, w = 4.79, chi^2_1 = 0.99 percent.
* For the heart billiard (Wigner limit): a_1 = 0.08, b_1 = 1.41, w = 0.05, chi^2_1 = 3.6 percent.
* For the nuclear data ensemble (NDE) of 1726 neutron and proton resonance energies: a_1 = 0.07, b_1 = 1.44, w = 0.04, chi^2_1 = 0.99 percent.
* For experimental 0^+ collective states in actinides (0-3 MeV): a_1 = 0.32, b_1 = 0.98, w = 0.23, chi^2_1 = 11.4 percent.
* For experimental 2^+ collective states: a_1 = 0.58, b_1 = 0.54, w = 0.56, chi^2_1 = 11.8 percent.
* For experimental 4^+ collective states: a_1 = 0.68, b_1 = 0.40, w = 0.76, chi^2_1 = 9.1 percent.
* For 4^+ states with fixed K=0 projection: a_1 = 0.32, b_1 = 0.97, w = 0.23, chi^2_1 = 11.0 percent.
* For 4^+ states with fixed K=2 projection: a_1 = 0.07, b_1 = 1.44, w = 0.04, chi^2_1 = 11.3 percent.
* For 4^+ states with fixed K=4 projection: a_1 = 0.14, b_1 = 1.30, w = 0.08, chi^2_1 = 12.3 percent.

## Context

This work extends the two-parameter LWD2 approach of Magner et al. (2017) by reducing it to a one-parameter form, and it is compared with the Brody distribution (Brody, 1973) and the Berry-Robnik distribution (Berry and Robnik, 1984). The analysis confirms the intermediate order-chaos structure previously reported by Shriner et al. (1991) for collective states in deformed nuclei, and demonstrates that fixing the projection K of angular momentum enhances chaos, shifting the NNSD toward the Wigner distribution, consistent with symmetry breaking effects seen in single-particle spectra by Magner et al. (2012). The results enable systematic study of symmetry breaking phenomena in collective nuclear states.
