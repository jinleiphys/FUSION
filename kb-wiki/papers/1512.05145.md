# Introductory lectures on lattice QCD at nonzero baryon number

> These lecture notes contain an elementary introduction to lattice QCD at nonzero chemical potential. Topics discussed include chemical potential in the continuum and on the lattice; the sign, overlap and Silver Blaze problems; the phase boundary at small chemical potential; imaginary chemical potential; and complex Langevin dynamics. An incomplete overview of other approaches is presented as well. These lectures are meant for postgraduate students and postdocs with an interest in extreme QCD. A basic knowledge of lattice QCD is assumed but not essential. Some exercises are included at the end.

## Key claim

These lecture notes provide an elementary introduction to lattice QCD at nonzero chemical potential, focusing on why the QCD phase diagram has not yet been determined from first principles due to the sign problem, and discussing partial or possibly complete solutions to this problem. The author explicitly states that the lectures are incomplete and limited by time and knowledge, and that very recent material still in development has been omitted.

## Method

The lectures introduce the grand canonical partition function for QCD at nonzero temperature and baryon density, showing how chemical potential couples to the conserved charge and appears as an imaginary abelian vector field in the temporal direction. The sign problem is demonstrated by showing that the fermion determinant becomes complex at nonzero chemical potential, with the property det M(μ)* = det M(-μ*). The lectures then review several approaches to circumvent the sign problem: reweighting methods, Taylor expansion of the phase boundary at small chemical potential, simulations at imaginary chemical potential where the determinant is real, and complex Langevin dynamics for gauge theories.

## Key numbers

- The baryon chemical potential equals μ_B = 3μ_q, where μ_q is the quark chemical potential
- For free fermions at zero temperature, the onset of nonzero density occurs at μ = m, with the density given by (μ² - m²)^(3/2)/(3π²) Θ(μ - m)
- For QCD with two degenerate flavours, the critical quark chemical potential for onset is μ_c = m_N/3 (nucleon mass minus binding energy) for baryon number, and μ_c = m_π/2 for isospin chemical potential (pion condensation)
- The average phase factor in the phase-quenched theory is given by ⟨e^{iφ}⟩_{pq} = Z_full/Z_pq = e^{-ΩΔf}, where Ω is the spacetime volume and Δf is the difference in free energy densities

## Context

These lectures extend the pedagogical literature on lattice QCD at nonzero chemical potential, building on the foundational work of Hasenfratz and Kogut (1983) on introducing chemical potential on the lattice, and the formulation of the Silver Blaze problem by Cohen (2003). The lectures enable postgraduate students and postdocs to understand the sign problem, overlap problem, and Silver Blaze problem, and to evaluate approaches such as reweighting (Barbour 1997, Fodor and Katz 2002), imaginary chemical potential methods, and complex Langevin dynamics as potential routes toward determining the QCD phase diagram from first principles.
