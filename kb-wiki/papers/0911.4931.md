# The horn, the hadron mass spectrum and the QCD phase diagram - the statistical model of hadron production in central nucleus-nucleus collisions

> We present the status of the description of hadron production in central nucleus-nucleus collisions within the statistical model . The recent inclusion of very high-mass resonances and the sigma meson leads to an improved description of the data, in particular the energy dependence of the K+/pi+ ratio at SPS energies (``the horn''). The connection to the QCD phase diagram is discussed.

# The horn, the hadron mass spectrum and the QCD phase diagram - the statistical model of hadron production in central nucleus-nucleus collisions

## Key claim
The previously problematic "horn" structure in the energy dependence of the K+/pi+ ratio near sqrt(s_NN) approximately 10 GeV is now well described by the statistical hadro-chemical equilibrium model, but only after including the sigma meson and many higher-mass resonances in the hadronic mass spectrum. This explanation is rooted in the detailed features of the hadronic mass spectrum that produce a limiting temperature, rather than requiring the onset of deconfinement as an explanation.

## Method
The authors performed thermal fits to hadron yields from central nucleus-nucleus collisions across AGS to RHIC energies using a hadro-chemical equilibrium model with three parameters: chemical freeze-out temperature T, baryo-chemical potential mu_b, and fireball volume V. They employed an improved hadronic mass spectrum that includes the sigma meson and many higher-lying resonances, and parametrized the extracted T and mu_b values as functions of sqrt(s_NN) using the expressions given in Equation (1) of the paper.

## Key numbers
- The limiting temperature T_lim = 164 MeV, extracted from the parametrization of chemical freeze-out temperatures.
- The baryo-chemical potential parametrization: mu_b [MeV] = 1303 / (1 + 0.286 * sqrt(s_NN [GeV])).
- The K+/pi+ ratio maximum occurs naturally at sqrt(s_NN) approximately 8 GeV in the thermal model.
- The Lambda/pi- ratio shows a pronounced maximum at sqrt(s_NN) approximately 5 GeV.
- The horn structure is observed at a beam energy of 30 AGeV (corresponding to sqrt(s_NN) approximately 10 GeV).
- At SPS beam energy of 40 AGeV, mu_b approximately 400 MeV, coinciding with the approach to saturation in T.

## Context
This work extends the statistical model analyses of Andronic, Braun-Munzinger, and Stachel (2008) and earlier studies (2005) that could not reproduce the sharp horn structure observed by the NA49 collaboration (2002). The improved description contradicts interpretations by Gazdzicki and Gorenstein (1999) that the horn signals the onset of deconfinement and QGP formation. The results connect to the QCD phase diagram via the limiting temperature concept from Hagedorn (1985) and lattice QCD calculations of the phase boundary and critical point. Open questions remain whether the chemical freeze-out curve at larger mu_b traces the QCD phase boundary or is influenced by exotic phases such as those predicted by McLerran and Pisarski (2007).
