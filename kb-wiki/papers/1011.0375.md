# The "Ridge" in Proton-Proton Scattering at 7 TeV

> One of the most important experimental results for proton-proton scattering at the LHC is the observation of a so-called "ridge" structure in the two particle correlation function versus the pseudorapidity difference $Δη$ and the azimuthal angle difference $Δϕ$. One finds a strong correlation around $Δϕ=0$, extended over many units in $Δη$. We show that a hydrodynamical expansion based on flux tube initial conditions leads in a natural way to the observed structure. To get this result, we have to perform an event-by-event calculation, because the effect is due to statistical fluctuations of the initial conditions, together with a subsequent collective expansion. This is a strong point in favour of a fluid-like behavior even in $pp$ scattering, where we have to deal with length scales of the order of 0.1 fm.

## Key claim

The observed "ridge" structure in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at 7 TeV can be explained by a hydrodynamical expansion based on flux tube initial conditions, provided one performs an event-by-event calculation that accounts for statistical fluctuations of the initial conditions together with subsequent collective expansion.

## Method

The authors used a 3+1 dimensional hydrodynamical evolution with initial conditions obtained from an EPOS 2 multiple scattering approach, where parton ladders are identified as elementary flux tubes treated as classical strings. For each event, the energy density and flow velocity from overlapping flux tubes serve as initial conditions for the hydrodynamic expansion, which evolves until freeze-out according to the Cooper-Frye prescription. The calculation was performed event-by-event, which is computationally demanding because the cell sizes must be small even though the system volume is small.

## Key numbers

- The flux tube radius was chosen as approximately 0.2 fm to obtain the best overall picture for all flow-dependent observables.
- The high multiplicity trigger in the simulation corresponds to 5.3 times the average multiplicity (events with 10 elementary scatterings), compared to the experimental high multiplicity bin of about 7 times average.
- The integrated elliptical flow v2 at midrapidity is approximately 0.01, compatible with values of about 0.05 at intermediate transverse momentum from other calculations.
- The ridge effect is largest for particles with transverse momentum between 1 and 3 GeV/c, and disappears towards large and small values.
- The cutoff for string segments escaping the core was reduced from 3 GeV/c to 1 GeV/c to produce an awayside correlation without destroying the pt spectra.

## Context

This work extends the same hydrodynamic approach that has been successfully applied to AuAu collisions at RHIC (Werner et al., EPOS2) and to pp collisions at 0.9 TeV (Werner et al., EPOS2pp). The ridge structure was first observed by the CMS collaboration in pp scattering at 7 TeV, and a similar structure had been seen in heavy ion collisions at RHIC where it is attributed to hydrodynamical evolution. The approach differs from Glauber-type models (d'Enterria et al., Prasad et al.) where asymmetry comes from geometry rather than statistical fluctuations, and from parametrized initial conditions (Kodama et al.). The work enables further study of collective fluid-like behavior in small systems and requires future development of a continuous core-corona procedure rather than a sharp cutoff.
