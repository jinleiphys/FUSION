# Light Baryon Resonances: Restrictions and Perspectives

> The problem of nucleon resonances N' with masses below the Delta is considered. We derive bounds for the properties of such states. Some of these are new, while others improve upon existing limits. We discuss the nature of N' states, and their unitary partners, assuming their existence can be verified.

## Key claim

The paper derives new and improved bounds on the properties of hypothetical nucleon resonances \(N'\) with masses below the \(\Delta(1232)\), showing that if such states exist, their couplings to standard hadrons must be suppressed by more than a factor of \(10^{-2}\) in amplitude, but the present \(\pi N\) scattering data cannot exclude narrow resonances with widths below 50 keV.

## Method

The authors performed a reanalysis of the \(\pi N\) SAID database by inserting trial resonances with fixed masses (1100–1300 MeV) and widths (50–300 keV) into individual partial-wave amplitudes (\(S_{11}, S_{31}, P_{11}, P_{13}, P_{31}, P_{33}, D_{13}, D_{15}\)) and refitting all other parameters to minimize \(\chi^2\). They also applied dispersion relations to \(\pi N\) scattering amplitudes to extract bounds on the \(\pi NN'\) coupling constant, and compared results with the pion-exchange contribution to \(np\) scattering. For subthreshold states, they analyzed the Panofsky ratio and the \(n2\gamma\) final state from stopped-pion capture to constrain radiative decays.

## Key numbers

* For elastic \(\pi N\) resonances between \(\pi N\)-threshold and 1300 MeV, no evidence was found for widths \(\Gamma \geq 50\) keV; the bound \(\Gamma(N') < 50\) keV gives \(\Gamma(N')/\Gamma(\Delta) < 4 \times 10^{-4}\).
* From Compton scattering data up to \(E_\gamma = 290\) MeV, the limit \(\text{Br}^2_\gamma(N') \cdot \Gamma_{N'} < 10\) eV holds for \(m_N < m_{N'} < 1200\) MeV, compared to \(\text{Br}^2_\gamma(\Delta) \cdot \Gamma_\Delta = 3.6\) keV.
* The \(\pi NN'\) coupling is bounded by \(g^2_{\pi NN'}/(4\pi) \leq 0.16\), giving \(g^2_{\pi NN'}/g^2_{\pi NN} \leq 10^{-2}\), improving on an earlier bound of 0.1.
* From stopped-pion capture, the ratio \(W(\pi^- p \to n'\gamma)/W(\pi^- p \to n\gamma) < 8 \times 10^{-5}\) (with a more stringent estimate of \(\sim 10^{-5}\)).
* For subthreshold \(N'\) states, the radiative width bound is \(\Gamma(N' \to N\gamma) < 5\) eV over the whole interval from \(m_N\) to the \(\pi N\)-threshold.
* The Gell-Mann-Okubo mass formula using \(\Lambda(1330)\), \(\Sigma(1480)\), and \(\Xi(1620)\) gives an \(N'\) mass of about 1100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical bounds on light baryon resonances proposed by Azimov (1970s) and others, and responds to contradictory experimental reports from TRIUMF, Saclay, INR Moscow, and JLab. The authors connect the \(N'\) problem to the then-recent claimed discovery of the \(\Theta^+\) pentaquark at SPring-8, ITEP, and JLab, and discuss the chiral soliton model prediction of Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997). The results provide quantitative constraints for future searches at facilities such as COSY, JLab, and MAMI, and suggest that if \(N'\) exists, it would likely be a 5-quark state with suppressed couplings, possibly belonging to a unitary octet including \(\Sigma(1480)\), \(\Xi(1620)\), and \(\Lambda(1330)\).
