# Skewness of the elliptic flow distribution in $\sqrt{s_{_{\mathrm{NN}}}}$ = 5.02~TeV PbPb collisions from HYDJET++ model

> The elliptic flow ($v_{2}$) event-by-event fluctuations in PbPb collisions at 5.02~TeV are analyzed within the HYDJET++ model. Using the multi-particle, so called Q-cumulant method, $v_{2}\{2\}$, $v_{2}\{4\}$, $v_{2}\{6\}$ and $v_{2}\{8\}$ are calculated and used to study their ratios and to construct skewness ($γ^{exp}_{1}$) as a measure of the asymmetry of the elliptic flow distribution. Additionally, in order to check if there is a hydrodynamics nature in the elliptic collectivity generated by the HYDJET++ model, the ratio of $v_{2}\{6\} - v_{2}\{8\}$ and $v_{2}\{4\} - v_{2}\{6\}$ distribution is calculated. The analysis is performed as a function of the collision centrality. In order to check the HYDJET++ model responses, the results of this analysis are compared to the corresponding experimental measurements by CMS. A good qualitative and rather good quantitative agreement is found.

## Key claim

The HYDJET++ model reproduces the qualitative centrality dependence of elliptic flow skewness observed by CMS, but yields a significantly larger magnitude of the skewness than the experimental data, indicating that the model's event-by-event elliptic flow fluctuations are more non-Gaussian than those in real PbPb collisions.

## Method

The authors simulated approximately 60 million PbPb collisions at 5.02 TeV using the HYDJET++ model with its "flow+quenched jets" switch. They applied the Q-cumulant method to extract two-, four-, six-, and eight-particle elliptic flow harmonics (v2{2}, v2{4}, v2{6}, v2{8}) within the kinematic range 0.3 < pT < 3.0 GeV/c and |η| < 1.0, covering centralities from 5-10% to 55-60%. The standardized skewness γexp1 was computed from these cumulants using the formula from Giacalone (2016), and the ratio (v2{6} - v2{8})/(v2{4} - v2{6}) was used to test for ideal hydrodynamic behavior.

## Key numbers

* The ratio (v2{6} - v2{8})/(v2{4} - v2{6}) from HYDJET++ has a mean value of 0.127 ± 0.002 over the 5-60% centrality range, compared to the ideal hydrodynamic prediction of approximately 0.091.
* The HYDJET++ ratio (v2{6} - v2{8})/(v2{4} - v2{6}) increases from about 0.11 at the most central 5-10% collisions to about 0.14 for the most peripheral 55-60% collisions.
* The differences between higher-order cumulant ratios (v2{6}/v2{4}, v2{8}/v2{4}, v2{8}/v2{6}) are smaller than 1% in HYDJET++, while experimental CMS ratios deviate from unity by up to a few percent in peripheral events.
* The skewness γexp1 from HYDJET++ is negative with magnitude increasing toward peripheral collisions, but is significantly larger in magnitude than the CMS experimental results from Sirunyan (2017).

## Context

This work extends the study of elliptic flow fluctuations beyond Gaussian models by comparing the HYDJET++ Monte Carlo generator to experimental CMS data from Sirunyan (2017) and ALICE data from Acharya (2018). The analysis tests the universal equality v2{6} - v2{8} = (1/11)(v2{4} - v2{6}) proposed by Giacalone (2016) for ideal hydrodynamics, finding that HYDJET++ predictions for this relation agree with ALICE data. The results enable future comparisons between hydrodynamic and hybrid models to understand the origin of non-Gaussian flow fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions.
