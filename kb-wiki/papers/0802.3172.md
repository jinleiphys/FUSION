# Lambda hyperonic effect on the normal driplines

> A generalized mass formula is used to calculate the neutron and proton drip lines of normal and lambda hypernuclei treating non-strange and strange nuclei on the same footing. Calculations suggest existence of several bound hypernuclei whose normal cores are unbound. Addition of Lambda or, Lambda-Lambda hyperon(s) to a normal nucleus is found to cause shifts of the neutron and proton driplines from their conventional limits.

# Lambda hyperonic effect on the normal driplines

## Key claim
Using the BWMH generalized mass formula, the authors calculate that addition of one or two Lambda hyperons to a normal nucleus shifts the neutron and proton driplines from their conventional limits, but this shift is not uniform across the nuclear chart and depends on the specific nucleus. The abstract's claim of "shifts of the neutron and proton driplines" is qualified by the finding that the effect is more prominent on the neutron dripline than the proton dripline, and that for heavier nuclei the proton dripline of single-Lambda hypernuclei often merges with the normal proton dripline.

## Method
The authors employed the BWMH (Samanta Formula) mass formula, which treats non-strange normal nuclei and strange hypernuclei on the same footing, to compute one-neutron and one-proton separation energies for isotopes of each element from Li to Bi. For each element with fixed proton number Z, the neutron number was varied until the last bound nucleus was found at the neutron-rich and neutron-deficient sides, defining the driplines for normal nuclei, single-Lambda hypernuclei, and double-Lambda hypernuclei.

## Key numbers
- Normal neutron drip nucleus $^{44}$Si (N=30) shifts to $^{47}_{\Lambda}$Si (N=32) with addition of a single Lambda, with $S_n$ = 0.242 MeV
- Normal neutron drip nucleus $^{193}$La (N=136) shifts to $^{196}_{\Lambda}$La (N=138) with addition of a single Lambda
- $^{47}_{\Lambda}$Si (N=32) has $S_n$ = 0.242 MeV while $^{48}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$Si (same N=32) has $S_n$ = 0.513 MeV, showing different mean-field modification from single versus double Lambda
- Normal neutron drip nucleus $^{23}$N (N=16) shifts to $^{27}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$N (N=18) with double Lambda addition
- Normal neutron drip nucleus $^{232}$Er (N=164) shifts to $^{236}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$Er (N=166) with double Lambda addition
- Normal proton drip nucleus $^{39}$Ti (N=17) becomes $^{40}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$Ti (N=16) after adding double Lambda, not $^{41}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$Ti (N=17)
- The hyperon separation energy for $^{13}_{\Lambda}$O is predicted to be $S_\Lambda$ = 14.1 MeV

## Context
This work extends the BWMH mass formula previously developed by Samanta and Adhikari (2002) and Samanta et al. (2005, 2006) to predict dripline locations for hypernuclei, complementing earlier relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations by Vretenar et al. (1998) which suggested that a Lambda hyperon could shift the neutron drip line by stabilizing an otherwise unbound core nucleus. The results provide guidelines for future experiments at facilities such as GSI (HypHI), FAIR (PANDA), and J-PARC (Hyperball) that aim to explore very neutron-rich hypernuclei relevant for understanding neutron star cores. The non-uniform shift of hypernuclear driplines calls for microscopic investigations beyond the liquid-drop approach used here.
