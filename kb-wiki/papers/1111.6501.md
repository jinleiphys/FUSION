# Solutions of the Dirac equation with spin and pseudospin symmetry for trigonometric Scarf potential in D-dimensions

> Solutions of the Dirac equation with spin and pseudospin symmetry for the scalar and vector trigonometric scarf potential in $D$-dimensions within the framework of an approximation scheme to the centrifugal barrier are obtained. The energy spectral and the two-component spinor eigenfunctions are obtained. The bound state energy levels for various values of dimension $D$, $n$, $κ$ and the potential range parameter $α$ are also presented.

## Key claim

The paper claims to obtain approximate analytical solutions of the Dirac equation in D-dimensions for the trigonometric Scarf potential under spin and pseudospin symmetry conditions, but only by using an approximation scheme for the centrifugal barrier (1/r² ≈ α²/cos² αr) that limits the results to small values of the potential range parameter α.

## Method

The authors solve the D-dimensional Dirac equation with scalar and vector trigonometric Scarf potentials by applying the Nikiforov-Uvarov (N-U) method after introducing a coordinate transformation z = -tan² αr and using an approximation for the centrifugal term. Under spin symmetry (Δ(r) = V(r) - S(r) = constant) and pseudospin symmetry (Σ(r) = V(r) + S(r) = constant), they reduce the coupled Dirac equations to second-order differential equations and obtain energy eigenvalues and two-component spinor wavefunctions expressed in terms of Jacobi polynomials.

## Key numbers

- For spin symmetry with D=3, n_r=1, α=0.0001, M=C_s=V_o=S_o=1: energy E = 3.0004 (dimensionless units)
- For spin symmetry with D=3, n_r=5, α=0.01, M=C_s=V_o=S_o=1: energy E = 3.1057 (dimensionless units)
- For spin symmetry with D=6, n_r=5, α=0.01, M=C_s=V_o=S_o=1: energy E = 3.1300 (dimensionless units)
- For pseudospin symmetry with D=3, n_r=1, α=0.0001, M=C_ps=V_o=S_o=1: energy E = -2.00000001 (dimensionless units)
- For pseudospin symmetry with D=3, n_r=5, α=0.01, M=C_ps=V_o=S_o=1: energy E = -2.00119952 (dimensionless units)
- For pseudospin symmetry with D=6, n_r=5, α=0.01, M=C_ps=V_o=S_o=1: energy E = -2.00187383 (dimensionless units)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of spin and pseudospin symmetric solutions of the Dirac equation for potentials including the Hulthen potential (Soylu et al., 2007), Eckart potential (Jia et al., 2006), Poschl-Teller potential (Jia et al., 2007; Agboola, 2011), Rosen-Morse potential (Oyewumi and Akoshile, 2010; Ikhdair, 2010), and Wood-Saxon potential (Guo and Sheng, 2005) to the trigonometric Scarf potential in arbitrary dimensions. The results enable further investigation of nuclear shell structure in higher-dimensional spaces and provide a framework for studying PT-symmetric and q-deformed versions of the Scarf potential.
