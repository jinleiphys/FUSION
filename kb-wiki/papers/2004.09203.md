# Effects of anomalous magnetic moment of quarks on the dilepton production from hot and dense magnetized quark matter using the NJL model

> Dilepton production rate (DPR) from hot and dense quark matter is studied in the presence of an arbitrary external magnetic field using the 2-flavour Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. The anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of the quarks is taken into consideration while calculating the constituent quark mass as well as the DPR from the thermo-magnetic medium. An infinite number of quark Landau levels is incorporated so that no approximations are made on the strength of the background magnetic field. The analytic structure of the two point vector current correlation function in the complex energy plane reveals that, in addition to the usual Unitary cut, a non-trival Landau cut appears in the physical kinematic domains solely due to the external magnetic field. Moreover, these kinematic domains of the Unitary and Landau cuts are found to be significantly modified due to the AMM of the quarks. With finite AMM of the quarks, for certain values of the external magnetic field, the kinematically forbidden gap between the Unitary and Landau cuts are shown to vanish leading to the generation of a continuous spectrum of dilepton emission over the whole invariant mass region not observed earlier.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of quarks in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model calculation of dilepton production from hot and dense magnetized quark matter leads to a significant modification of the kinematic domains of the Unitary and Landau cuts, and for certain values of the external magnetic field, the kinematically forbidden gap between these cuts vanishes, producing a continuous dilepton emission spectrum over the whole invariant mass region not observed in previous studies.

## Method

The authors calculate the dilepton production rate (DPR) using the 2-flavour NJL model in the presence of an arbitrary external magnetic field at finite temperature and baryon density, incorporating the AMM of quarks. They employ the Real Time Formalism (RTF) of finite temperature field theory and the full Schwinger proper time propagator (including AMM of charged fermions) with all Landau levels to compute the vector-vector current correlator. The temperature-, density-, and magnetic-field-dependent effective quark mass is obtained from the NJL model and used as input in the DPR expression, with no approximation on the magnetic field strength.

## Key numbers

* The DPR at zero magnetic field is given by an analytical expression containing step functions that restrict dilepton production to invariant masses satisfying \(q^2 > 4m_L^2\) and \(q^2 > 4M^2\), where \(m_L\) is the lepton mass and \(M\) is the constituent quark mass.
* The kinematic domain for the Unitary-I cut at non-zero magnetic field is \(\sqrt{q_z^2 + 4(M - \kappa_u B)^2} < q^0 < \infty\), where \(\kappa_u\) is the AMM of the up quark and \(B\) is the magnetic field.
* The kinematic domain for the Landau cut at non-zero magnetic field is \(|q^0| < \sqrt{q_z^2 + (\sqrt{M^2 + 2|e_u B|} + \kappa_u B - |M - \kappa_u B|)^2}\), where \(e_u = 2e/3\) is the up quark charge.
* The DPR at non-zero magnetic field is expressed as \(\text{DPR}_{B \neq 0} = \frac{1}{4\pi^4 q_\parallel^4} \left( \frac{1}{e^{q^0/T} + 1} \right) \left[ g_\parallel^{\mu\nu} \text{Im} \mathcal{W}^{11}_{\mu\nu}(q_\parallel) \, g_\parallel^{\alpha\beta} \text{Im} \mathcal{L}_{\alpha\beta}(q_\parallel) + \frac{1}{2} g_\perp^{\mu\nu} \text{Im} \mathcal{W}^{11}_{\mu\nu}(q_\parallel) \, g_\perp^{\alpha\beta} \text{Im} \mathcal{L}_{\alpha\beta}(q_\parallel) \right]\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dilepton production in magnetized quark matter by Tuchin (2012, 2013), Sadooghi (2016), Bandyopadhyay (2016, 2017), Ghosh (2018), Islam (2018), and Das (2019) by incorporating the AMM of quarks and using the NJL model with all Landau levels. It also builds on recent studies by Sadooghi and Chaudhuri (2019) that examined the effect of quark AMM on the phase diagram of the NJL model. The results enable more realistic predictions for dilepton observables in heavy-ion collision experiments where strong transient magnetic fields are present, and may help distinguish between different models of the quark-gluon plasma in the presence of magnetic fields.
