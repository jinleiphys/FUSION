# Instability-induced fermion production in quantum field theory

> Nonequilibrium instabilities are known to lead to exponential amplification of boson occupation numbers for low momentum modes on time scales much shorter than the asymptotic thermal equilibration time. We show for Yukawa-type interactions that this growth induces very efficient fermion production, which proceeds with the maximum primary boson growth rate. The description is based on a 1/N expansion of the 2PI effective action to NLO including boson-fermion loops, which are crucial to observe this phenomenon. For long enough amplification in the boson sector, fermion production terminates when the thermal occupancy is reached in the infrared. At higher momenta, where boson occupation numbers are low, the fermion modes exhibit a power-law regime with exponent two.

## Key claim

For Yukawa-type interactions, the exponential amplification of low-momentum boson occupation numbers following a nonequilibrium instability induces very efficient fermion production that proceeds with the maximum primary boson growth rate, but this mechanism requires the inclusion of boson-fermion loop contributions in the 2PI effective action at next-to-leading order, which had not been done before.

## Method

The authors employed a 1/N expansion to next-to-leading order (NLO) of the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action for a SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R symmetric Yukawa model with N=4 scalar fields and N_f=2 massless Dirac fermions in 3+1 dimensions, solving the coupled integro-differential equations of motion for the boson and fermion two-point correlation functions. They induced a spinodal (tachyonic) instability by a sudden quench in the sign of the mass-squared term, starting from vacuum-like initial conditions with zero occupation numbers, and evolved the system numerically through the exponential growth regime into the nonlinear stage. The approximation includes an infinite resummation of boson loop diagrams and the leading fermion-boson loop contributions, which are crucial for observing the induced fermion production.

## Key numbers

- The primary exponential growth rate for the lowest momentum boson mode is γ₀ = m, where m is the mass parameter setting the scale; the dominant solution for the boson statistical propagator at early times behaves as F_φ(t,t';p) = A₀ exp[γ(p)(t+t')] with γ(p) = √(m²−p²) for |p| < m.
- The time at which nonlinearities become nonperturbatively large (t_φ) is estimated as t_φ ≈ 7.2/m for λ=10⁻², t_φ ≈ 8.5/m for λ=10⁻³, t_φ ≈ 9.7/m for λ=10⁻⁴, and t_φ ≈ 11.0/m for λ=10⁻⁵, where λ is the boson self-coupling.
- At higher momenta where boson occupation numbers are low, the fermion modes exhibit a power-law regime with exponent κ_ψ = 2, i.e., n_ψ(|p|) ∼ |p|⁻².

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nonequilibrium instabilities and thermalization in bosonic theories (Berges 2002, Arrizabalaga 2004, Berges 2008) by including fermion fluctuations for the first time in the 2PI 1/N expansion at NLO, going beyond earlier approaches that solved the Dirac equation in the presence of a classical macroscopic field (Boyanovsky 1995, Greene 1998, Garcia-Bellido 2000). The results enable future studies that include non-vanishing macroscopic field values to estimate the importance of quantum corrections to the Dirac equation, and provide a controlled framework for understanding fermion production in early-universe reheating and heavy-ion collision contexts.
