# A general procedure for detector-response correction of higher order cumulants

> We propose a general procedure for the detector-response correction (including efficiency correction) of higher order cumulants observed by the event-by-event analysis in heavy-ion collisions. This method makes use of the moments of the response matrix characterizing the property of a detector, and is applicable to a wide variety of response matrices such as those having non-binomial responses and including the effects of ghost tracks. A procedure to carry out the detector-response correction of realistic detectors is discussed. In test analyses, we show that this method can successfully re- construct the cumulants of true distribution for various response matrices including the one having multiplicity-dependent efficiency.

## Key claim

The authors propose a general procedure for detector-response correction of higher order cumulants that works exactly for response matrices where the moments \(R_m(N)\) are polynomials of order \(m\), and approximately for realistic detectors via truncation of the polynomial expansion, provided the truncation reproduces the response moments within statistical errors.

## Method

The method relates the moments of the observed distribution \(\llangle n^m \rrangle\) to the moments of the true distribution \(\langle N^m \rangle\) via the moments \(R_m(N)\) of the response matrix \(\mathcal{R}(n;N)\), expanded as \(R_m(N) = \sum_j r_{mj} N^j\). For exactly solvable models (binomial, hypergeometric, beta-binomial) the expansion terminates at order \(m\), giving a closed lower-triangular matrix equation that is inverted order-by-order. For realistic detectors where the expansion does not close, the series is truncated at order \(L\), and the coefficients \(r_{mj}\) are determined by fitting \(R_m(N)\) obtained from Monte Carlo simulations; the inversion then yields the corrected moments up to order \(L\).

## Key numbers

* For the hypergeometric and beta-binomial test models with \(p = 0.7\) and \(Y = 140\), the corrected cumulants \(\langle N^m \rangle_{\rm c}\) up to fourth order reproduce the true value \(\langle N^m \rangle_{\rm c} = 40\) within statistics for all \(Y\) values tested.
* For the multiplicity-dependent efficiency model with \(p(N) = 0.7 - \varepsilon(N - \langle N \rangle)\) and \(\varepsilon = 0.002\), the corrected cumulants up to fourth order reproduce \(\langle N^m \rangle_{\rm c} = 40\) within statistics using a fourth-order polynomial truncation.
* The typical slope \(\varepsilon\) for protons at the STAR detector is \(\varepsilon \simeq 0.0002\) at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7\) GeV and \(\varepsilon \simeq 0.0003\) at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV in Au+Au collisions; the maximal \(\varepsilon\) in the test is about one order larger than these values.

## Context

This work extends the binomial-model efficiency correction (Kitazawa and Asakawa, 2015; Nonaka et al., 2017) to non-binomial response matrices, including those with multiplicity-dependent efficiency and ghost tracks, which violate the binomial assumption. The method provides an alternative to the unfolding approach (D'Agostini, 1995; Adye, 2011) that avoids reconstructing the full distribution and instead works directly with cumulants, enabling application to realistic detectors whose response is estimated via Monte Carlo simulations such as GEANT.
