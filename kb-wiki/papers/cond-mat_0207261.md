# Thomas-Fermi approximation to static vortex states in superfluid trapped atomic gases

> We revise the Thomas-Fermi approximation for describing vortex states in Bose condensates of magnetically trapped atoms. Our approach is based on considering the hbar -> 0 limit rather than the N -> infinity limit as Thomas-Fermi approximation in close analogy with the Fermi systems. Even for relatively small numbers of trapped particles we find good agreement between Gross-Pitaevskii and Thomas-Fermi calculations for the different contributions to the total energy of the atoms in the condensate. We also discuss the application of our approach to the description of vortex states in superfluid fermionic systems in the Ginzburg-Landau regime.

## Key claim

The authors reinterpret the Thomas-Fermi (TF) approximation for vortex states in Bose-Einstein condensates as the \(\hbar \to 0\) limit rather than the usual \(N \to \infty\) limit, and claim that this approach yields good agreement with Gross-Pitaevskii (GP) calculations for the total energy even for relatively small numbers of trapped particles, though the agreement for the kinetic energy worsens at large particle numbers.

## Method

The authors derive a semiclassical TF density for vortex states with quantized circulation \(\kappa\) by starting from the single-particle propagator at lowest order in \(\hbar\), projecting onto states of definite angular momentum \(L_z\), and using a Bessel-function expansion to obtain the local spectral density. For the interacting case, they determine the chemical potential \(\mu\) and normalization constant \(c_\kappa\) self-consistently by requiring that the integrated level density matches that of the non-interacting harmonic oscillator, then solve the non-linear equation for the TF density iteratively. They compare their results to numerical solutions of the Gross-Pitaevskii equation (GPE) obtained via imaginary-time step techniques, and also to two large-\(N\) approximation methods: the standard \(N\to\infty\) TF limit (neglecting radial and axial kinetic energy) and the method of matched asymptotics (MA).

## Key numbers

* For a condensate of \(10^2\) \(^{87}\)Rb atoms with \(\kappa = 1\) in a spherical trap (\(\aHO = 0.791\ \mu\)m, \(a = 100\,a_0\)), the \(\hbar\to 0\) TF approach gives total energy per particle \(\etot = 2.61\ \hbar\omega\) and chemical potential \(\mu = 2.72\ \hbar\omega\), compared to GPE values of \(\etot = 2.62\ \hbar\omega\) and \(\mu = 2.74\ \hbar\omega\).
* For \(10^4\) atoms, the \(\hbar\to 0\) TF approach gives \(\etot = 6.19\ \hbar\omega\) and \(\mu = 8.28\ \hbar\omega\), versus GPE values of \(\etot = 6.30\ \hbar\omega\) and \(\mu = 8.40\ \hbar\omega\).
* For \(10^6\) atoms, the \(\hbar\to 0\) TF approach gives \(\etot = 35.86\ \hbar\omega\) and \(\mu = 50.13\ \hbar\omega\), versus GPE values of \(\etot = 35.93\ \hbar\omega\) and \(\mu = 50.18\ \hbar\omega\).
* The radial and axial kinetic energy per particle \(\ekin\) from the \(\hbar\to 0\) TF approach is \(0.673\ \hbar\omega\) for \(10^2\) atoms (GPE: \(0.686\ \hbar\omega\)), but becomes \(-0.024\ \hbar\omega\) for \(10^6\) atoms (GPE: \(0.059\ \hbar\omega\)), indicating a breakdown of the approximation for this quantity at large \(N\).

## Context

This work extends the \(\hbar\to 0\) interpretation of the TF approximation previously applied to the ground state of Bose-Einstein condensates by Schuck and Vinas (2001) to the case of vortex states, and contrasts with the standard \(N\to\infty\) TF limit used by Fetter (1998), Rokhsar (1997), Ho (1998), and Sinha (1998), which neglects radial and axial kinetic energy. The method also enables application to vortex states in superfluid trapped Fermi gases via the Ginzburg-Landau equation, as discussed by Baranov (2002) and Rodriguez (2001). The approach provides a semiclassical framework that works for small particle numbers where large-\(N\) methods fail, but the kinetic energy results for large \(N\) are limited by non-analytic \(\hbar\) corrections that a pure TF expansion cannot capture.
