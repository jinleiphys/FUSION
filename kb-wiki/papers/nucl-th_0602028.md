# Quantum calculation of vortices in the inner crust of neutron stars

> We study, within a quantum mechanical framework based on self-consistent mean field theory, the interaction between a vortex and a nucleus immersed in a sea of free neutrons, a scenario representative of the inner crust of neutron stars. Quantal finite size effects force the vortex core outside the nucleus, influencing vortex pinning in an important way.

## Key claim

Quantal finite size effects, particularly spatial quantization leading to shell structure, force the vortex core outside the nucleus in the inner crust of neutron stars, contrary to the expectation that the lower pairing gap inside the nucleus would favor pinning; the pinning energy is positive but small, amounting to 3.3 MeV at the Fermi energy studied.

## Method

The authors solved the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (De Gennes) equations self-consistently within a cylindrical box of radius 30 fm and height 40 fm, using the SkII Skyrme force for the mean field and a density-dependent contact pairing interaction that reproduces the Gogny-force pairing gap in uniform neutron matter. They considered a single Wigner cell containing one nucleus and a sea of free neutrons, imposing axial symmetry for the vortex-nucleus system while constraining the proton density to spherical symmetry. Calculations were performed for vortex numbers ν=1 and ν=2, with the Fermi energy fixed at E_F = 5.8 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Vortex core radius in uniform neutron matter: ρ_core ≈ 2 fm, defined as the radius where Δ(ρ_core) = Δ_unif/2
- Asymptotic pairing gap far from the nucleus: Δ_unif ≈ 2.2 MeV; at the nuclear surface Δ ≈ 1 MeV
- Nuclear radius: R ≈ 7.5 fm, with diffusivity about 0.9 fm
- Energy cost to create an interstitial vortex (no nucleus): E_IU = 50.1 MeV, composed of ΔE_pair = 118.2 MeV, ΔE_kin = -65.9 MeV, ΔE_pot = -2.2 MeV
- Energy cost to create a pinned vortex (on nucleus): E_PN = 53.4 MeV, composed of ΔE_pair = 154.0 MeV, ΔE_kin = -78.5 MeV, ΔE_mean = -22.1 MeV
- Pinning energy at E_F = 5.8 MeV: E_PN - E_IU = 3.3 MeV
- Estimated error on pinning energy: less than 2 MeV
- Lattice step ranges from about 90 fm at n = 5 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³ to about 30 fm at n = 7 × 10⁻² fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the earlier mean-field calculations of vortex structure in uniform neutron matter by Bulgac (2002) and Elgaroy (2003) to the inhomogeneous environment of the inner crust, incorporating a nucleus in the Wigner cell. It also builds on the Negele-Vautherin (1973) description of the spherically symmetric Wigner cell. The results enable more realistic modeling of vortex pinning and glitch mechanisms in neutron stars, showing that the simple picture of pinning due to lower pairing energy inside nuclei is counteracted by quantal shell effects that expel the vortex, leading to a small positive pinning energy.
