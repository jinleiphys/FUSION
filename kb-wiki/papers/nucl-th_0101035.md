# Mutual heavy ion dissociation in peripheral collisions at ultrarelativistic energies

> We study mutual dissociation of heavy nuclei in peripheral collisions at ultrarelativistic energies. Earlier this process was proposed for beam luminosity monitoring via simultaneous registration of forward and backward neutrons in zero degree calorimeters at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. Electromagnetic dissociation of heavy ions is considered in the framework of the Weizsacker-Williams method and simulated by the RELDIS code. Photoneutron cross sections measured in different experiments and calculated by the GNASH code are used as input for the calculations of dissociation cross sections. The difference in results obtained with different inputs provides a realistic estimation for the systematic uncertainty of the luminosity monitoring method. Contribution to simultaneous neutron emission due to grazing nuclear interactions is calculated within the abrasion model. Good description of CERN SPS experimental data on Au and Pb dissociation gives confidence in predictive power of the model for AuAu and PbPb collisions at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The mutual electromagnetic dissociation of heavy nuclei in peripheral collisions at ultrarelativistic energies can serve as a practical method for beam luminosity monitoring, but the accuracy of the calculated cross sections depends heavily on the quality of input photoneutron cross section data and the choice of critical impact parameter, with systematic uncertainties arising from discrepancies between different experimental measurements and theoretical models.

## Method

The authors used the Weizsacker-Williams method of equivalent photons to calculate electromagnetic dissociation cross sections, implemented in the RELDIS code, with photoneutron cross sections from Saclay and Livermore experiments and from the GNASH code as input. They also calculated the contribution from grazing nuclear interactions using an abrasion model based on Glauber multiple scattering theory, with nuclear densities approximated by Fermi functions and a hypergeometrical model for determining the numbers of abraded protons and neutrons. The total cross sections for mutual dissociation were computed by integrating over impact parameters with a Poisson distribution for multiphoton absorption probabilities.

## Key numbers

- The cross sections for simultaneous dipole-dipole excitation of AuAu and PbPb collisions are 0.49 mb and 0.54 mb, respectively, from Ref. [Benesh]
- A rough estimation of 10^{-5}A^2 mb gives 0.39 mb for Au and 0.43 mb for Pb
- The leading order (LO) mechanism gives approximately 70% of the total mutual dissociation cross section at RHIC energies and approximately 60% at LHC energies
- The sum of next-to-leading-order (NLO) contributions adds approximately 25-27% to the total cross section
- At RHIC energies, the remaining contribution of approximately 5% (or about 0.2 b) is due to triple excitations
- The ratio E_max/E_A is close to 10^{-4} for heavy nuclei
- The diffuseness parameter d = 0.54 fm is used in the Fermi function for nuclear densities
- The Levinger parameter L = 6.5 is used in the quasideuteron model
- The systematic error in total photoabsorption cross section measured via neutron detection is approximately 3-5%
- The discrepancy in (gamma,2n) cross sections between different experiments reaches up to approximately 50% for Pb-208 target
- The Saclay data for both Au-197 and Pb-208 should be used with a correction factor of 0.93 according to Ref. [Berman]
- The peak height inconsistency between Livermore and Saclay data is approximately 3% for Au-197 and approximately 20% for Pb-208

## Context

This work extends the formalism previously used in Pshenichnov et al. (2000) for single dissociation to the case of mutual dissociation, and provides the theoretical basis for the luminosity monitoring method proposed by Baltz, White, and Baltz2 (1990s). The calculations are tested against CERN SPS experimental data on Au and Pb dissociation, and the results enable predictions for AuAu and PbPb collisions at RHIC and LHC, where the electromagnetic dissociation cross section by far exceeds the dissociation cross section due to direct nuclear overlap as shown by Krauss, Pshenichnov, and Weneser (1990s).
