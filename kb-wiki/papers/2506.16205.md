# Holographic Baryons as Quantum Hall Droplets

> We provide a first-principle construction of baryons as quantum Hall droplets in single-flavor holographic QCD. The baryons are described as charged D6-branes with a circular boundary on a flavor D8-brane in the Type IIA backgrounds dual to the confining and non-confining phases. The holographic description allows us to calculate precisely their properties, such as mass and size. We also consider other objects with baryonic charge, such as vortons, domain walls with holes, and "sandwich vortons", and discuss the relative (meta)stability of all these configurations.

## Key claim
The paper provides a first-principle holographic construction of baryons as quantum Hall droplets in single-flavor QCD-like theories, but only for the gluonic core of the baryons, leaving the study of their mesonic shell for future work. The construction relies on the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model and requires charged D6-brane configurations with a circular boundary on a flavor D8-brane, where the baryon charge, quark charge, and spin satisfy the relation \(J = n_B^2 N/2\) (reducing to \(J = N/2\) for the elementary \(n_B=1\) baryon).

## Method
The authors model baryons as D6-branes wrapping the \(S^4\) of the Type IIA background, extended along the holographic coordinate \(u\) with a circular boundary at the tip of a U-shaped D8-brane in both the confining and deconfined phases of the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model. They solve the Dirac-Born-Infeld and Chern-Simons equations of motion for the D6-brane embedding profile \(\rho(u)\) and the \(U(1)\) gauge field components \(a_t(u)\) and \(a_\psi(u)\), imposing boundary conditions that ensure gauge invariance and vanishing curvature at the boundary. The numerical solutions yield the baryon mass and size, and the analysis is extended to other baryonic configurations such as vortons, domain walls with holes, and "sandwich vortons."

## Key numbers
- The baryon mass scales linearly with the number of colors \(N\), while the radius is \(N\)-independent.
- The baryon size is suppressed by the large value of the 't Hooft coupling \(\lambda\), with a different power law suppression compared to the standard \(N_f \geq 2\) baryons in the WSS model (Hata, 2007; Hashimoto, 2008).
- The critical temperature for the confinement/deconfinement phase transition is \(T_c = M_{KK}/(2\pi)\).
- The critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration in the deconfined phase is \(T_a \simeq 0.154/L\), where \(L\) is the asymptotic separation of the flavor branes.
- The relation between baryon charge \(n_B\), quark charge \(q_s\), and spin \(J\) is \(J = n_B^2 N/2\).
- The KK mass scale \(M_{KK}\) is related to background parameters by \(M_{KK} = (3/2)\sqrt{u_0/R^3}\).

## Context
This work extends the proposal by Komargodski (2018) that one-flavor baryons are analogous to quantum Hall droplets with a \(U(1)_N\) Chern-Simons theory on their worldvolume, providing a first-principle holographic realization in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model (Witten, 1998; Sakai, 2004, 2005). It builds on previous studies of domain walls, strings, and vortons in the same model (Bigazzi, 2022, 2024) and enables precise quantitative predictions for baryon properties in holographic QCD, as well as phase diagrams comparing the stability of baryons, sandwich vortons, ordinary vortons, and quarks. The results suggest that similar quantum Hall droplet baryons may exist in real QCD and can be reinterpreted as defects in beyond-the-Standard-Model theories.
