# Decoherence and Entropy Production in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> Short thermalization times of less than 1 fm/c for quark and gluon matter have been suggested by recent experiments at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). It has been difficult to justify this rapid thermalization in first-principle calculations based on perturbation theory or the color glass condensate picture. Here, we address the related question of the decoherence of the gluon field, which is a necessary component of thermalization. We present a simplified leading-order computation of the decoherence time of a gluon ensemble subject to an incoming flux of Weizsacker-Williams gluons. We also discuss the entropy produced during the decoherence process and its relation to the entropy in the final state which has been measured experimentally.

## Key claim

The decoherence time of the gluon field in relativistic nuclear collisions is of order the inverse saturation scale, $\tau_{\rm dec} \sim Q_s^{-1}$, and is numerically smaller than 1 fm/$c$ at RHIC, but the authors emphasize this is an upper bound derived from a simplified leading-order computation within the McLerran-Venugopalan model, with the result depending on the running coupling and the width of the initial gluon momentum distribution.

## Method

The authors compute the time evolution of the gluon density matrix for a nucleus at rest under the perturbation of an incoming flux of Weizsacker-Williams gluons from a fast-moving second nucleus, using second-order perturbation theory at leading order in the coupling. They evaluate the decoherence time as the inverse decay time of the ratio $\tr D^2 / (\tr D)^2$, using an ansatz for the initial gluon density matrix with a Gaussian momentum distribution of width $1/\zeta$, and compute the relevant integrals within the McLerran-Venugopalan model with running coupling following the prescription of Kovchegov and Weigert (2007). The entropy produced by decoherence is estimated by modeling the initial gluon field as a collection of coherent states and computing the entropy of the diagonal density matrix after complete loss of off-diagonal elements.

## Key numbers

- The decoherence time satisfies $\tau_{\rm dec} \lesssim 0.25 \, (g_\mu^2/g_\Lambda^2) (\beta_0 g_\mu^2/16\pi^2) (1/\zeta Q_s) (1/Q_s)$, with all factors in parentheses of order unity, implying $\tau_{\rm dec} \sim Q_s^{-1}$.
- The saturation scale at RHIC is estimated as $Q_s^2 \approx 2$ GeV$^2$.
- The average number of decohering partons per coherence domain is $\bar n \approx C_{\rm F}\ln 2/(\pi\alpha_s^2) \approx 3$, using $C_{\rm F}=4/3$ and $\alpha_s \approx 0.3$.
- The total entropy per unit rapidity produced by decoherence in Au+Au collisions at RHIC is estimated as $dS_{\rm dec}/dy \approx 1500$, using $Q_s^2 \approx 2$ GeV$^2$, $R=7$ fm, and $\alpha_s \approx 0.3$.
- This decoherence entropy accounts for about one-third of the entropy measured in the final hadron distribution.

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation by Muller and Schafer (2005) of the decoherence time in leading-order perturbation theory, providing a more general result expressed in terms of the gluon two-point correlation function and evaluating it within the McLerran-Venugopalan model using running coupling results from Kovchegov and Weigert (2007). The authors also revisit their previous arguments from Fries, Muller, and Schafer (2006) about entropy production via decoherence, now using the short decoherence times established at RHIC. The paper addresses the puzzle of rapid thermalization at RHIC (less than 1 fm/$c$) that has been difficult to justify from perturbative QCD or color glass condensate calculations alone, and suggests that decoherence of the initial gluon field is a necessary precursor to thermalization that can generate a large fraction of the observed entropy.
