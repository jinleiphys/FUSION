# Faddeev fixed-center approximation to the $D\bar{D}K$ system and the hidden charm $K_{c\bar{c}}(4180)$ state

> We perform a theoretical study on the $D\bar{D}K$ three body system, using the fixed center approximation to the Faddeev equations, considering the interaction between $D$ and $K$, $D$ and $\bar{D}$ from the chiral unitary approach. We assume the scattering of $K$ meson on a clusterized system $D\bar{D}$, where a scalar meson $X(3720)$ could be formed. Thanks to the strong $DK$ interaction, where the scalar $D^*_{s0}(2317)$ meson is dynamically generated, a resonance structure shows up in the modulus squared of the three body $K$-$(D\bar{D})_{X(3720)}$ scattering amplitude and supports that a $D\bar{D}K$ bound state can be formed. The result is in agreement with previous theoretical studies, which claim a new excited hidden charm $K$ meson, $K_{c\bar{c}}(4180)$ with quantum numbers $I(J^P) = \frac{1}{2}(0^-)$ and mass about $4180$ MeV. It is expected that these theoretical results motivate its search in experimental measurements.

# Faddeev fixed-center approximation to the $D\bar{D}K$ system and the hidden charm $K_{c\bar{c}}(4180)$ state

## Key claim
The $D\bar{D}K$ three-body system can form a bound state with mass around 4180–4191 MeV, provided that the $D\bar{D}$ subsystem is treated as a clusterized bound state $X(3720)$ and the $DK$ interaction is strong enough to dynamically generate the $D^*_{s0}(2317)$ meson. The result is obtained within the fixed-center approximation (FCA) to the Faddeev equations, which assumes the $D\bar{D}$ cluster is not disrupted by the scattering of the $K$ meson.

## Method
The authors solve the Faddeev equations using the fixed-center approximation (FCA), treating the $D\bar{D}$ bound state $X(3720)$ as a fixed cluster and allowing the $K$ meson to undergo multiple scattering with the $D$ and $\bar{D}$ constituents. The two-body $D\bar{D}$ and $DK$ scattering amplitudes are taken from the chiral unitary approach, with a cutoff $\Lambda = 850$ MeV to regularize the loop functions. The total $K$-$X(3720)$ scattering amplitude $T$ is computed from the coupled FCA equations $T_1 = t_1 + t_1 G_0 T_2$ and $T_2 = t_2 + t_2 G_0 T_1$, where $t_1$ and $t_2$ are the $DK$ and $\bar{D}K$ amplitudes, and $G_0$ is the $K$ meson propagator in the cluster rest frame.

## Key numbers
- The peak in $|T|^2$ for the $K$-$X(3720)$ scattering amplitude appears at $\sqrt{s} = 4191$ MeV, associated with the $K_{c\bar{c}}(4180)$ state.
- The $D\bar{D}$ bound state $X(3720)$ is obtained with mass $M = 3720$ MeV using a cutoff $\Lambda = 850$ MeV; varying $\Lambda$ to 950 MeV shifts the peak to around 3700 MeV.
- The $DK$ subsystem invariant mass at the peak is $\sqrt{s_{DK}} = 2342$ MeV, where the $DK$ interaction is strong.
- The root-mean-square radius of the $DK$ subsystem in the bound state is 1.26 fm; the $\bar{D}K$ and $D\bar{D}$ subsystems have radii 2.27 fm and 2.10 fm, respectively.
- Particle masses used: $D$ ($\bar{D}$) = 1869.12 MeV, $K$ = 495.6455 MeV, $\pi$ = 138.04 MeV, $\eta$ = 547.862 MeV, $\eta_c$ = 2980.916 MeV, $D_s^+$ = 1969.49 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the study of Wu (2020), who solved the Schrödinger equation with the Gaussian expansion method for the $D\bar{D}K$ system and predicted a bound $K_{c\bar{c}}(4180)$ state with $I(J^P) = \frac{1}{2}(0^-)$. The FCA approach has been previously applied to similar three-body systems such as $DKK$ and $DK\bar{K}$ (Debastiani, 2017), $D\bar{D}^*K$ (Ren, 2018), and $BD\bar{D}$ (Dias, 2017). The results agree with the earlier Gaussian expansion calculation, validating the FCA as a simpler alternative for this system. The work enables future experimental searches for the $K_{c\bar{c}}(4180)$ state in reactions such as $B^+ \to D^+D^-K^+$ (LHCb) or $e^+e^-$ collisions (BESIII).
