# Probing QED Vacuum with Heavy Ions

> We recall how nearly half a century ago the proposal was made to explore the structure of the quantum vacuum using slow heavy-ion collisions. Pursuing this topic we review the foundational concept of spontaneous vacuum decay accompanied by observable positron emission in heavy-ion collisions and describe the related theoretical developments in strong fields QED.

# Probing QED Vacuum with Heavy Ions

## Key claim
The paper reviews the proposal, made nearly half a century ago, that slow heavy-ion collisions can be used to explore the quantum vacuum structure, specifically through spontaneous vacuum decay accompanied by observable positron emission. The authors note that the experimental effort over 20 years was inconclusive, and the account focuses on the theoretical developments that preceded that experimental work.

## Method
The authors solve the Dirac equation for electrons in the strong electrostatic potential of superheavy nuclei, using finite-size nuclear charge distributions to avoid singularities. They apply the Fano embedding method to describe the quasi-bound state that appears as a resonance in the negative energy continuum when the nuclear charge exceeds a critical value, and they also compute scattering phase shifts directly from the Dirac equation. The formalism is extended to quasi-molecular systems formed in heavy-ion collisions, where supercritical fields arise transiently as nuclei graze each other.

## Key numbers
- Critical nuclear charge for the 1s state diving into the negative energy continuum: Z_cr(1s_{1/2}) ≈ 171.5
- Critical nuclear charge for the 2p_{1/2} state: Z_cr(2p_{1/2}) ≈ 185.5
- For Z = 184, the 1s_{1/2} resonance is centered at energy ε = -926 keV with full width at half maximum Γ = 4.8 keV
- Resonance parameters for 1s_{1/2}: δ = 29.0 keV, τ = 0.33 keV, γ = 0.04 keV
- Resonance parameters for 2p_{1/2}: δ = 37.8 keV, τ = 0.22 keV, γ = 0.08 keV
- The energy shift of the bound 1s-state due to additional charge Z' is ΔE_0 = -Z' × δ, with δ ≈ 30 keV
- The width of the resonance is Γ = Z'^2 × γ, with γ ≈ 0.05 keV

## Context
This work extends the foundational studies of the Klein paradox from Klein (1929) and the early calculations of Dirac eigenstates for superheavy nuclei from the late 1960s. The Frankfurt strong fields group, led by Walter Greiner, developed these ideas in parallel with independent work by Zeldovich, Gershtein, and Popov in the Soviet Union. The review enables future experimental searches for spontaneous positron emission in heavy-ion collisions and provides the theoretical framework for understanding the charged vacuum state that forms in supercritical fields.
