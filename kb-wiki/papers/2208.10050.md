# Bottomonium sequential suppression and strong heavy-quark potential in heavy-ion collisions

> We employ the time-dependent Schrödinger equation with different complex potentials to study the bottomonium sequential suppression in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV and 5.02 TeV and Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. Both color screening effect and the random scatterings with thermal partons are considered in the real and imaginary parts of the heavy-quark potentials. As the real part of the heavy-quark potential is between the free energy $F(T,r)$ and the internal energy $U(T,r)$ of heavy quarkonium, we parametrize different potentials with a function of $F$ and $U$ to evolve the bottomonium wave packages in the medium. We find that when the real part of the potential is close to $U(T,r)$, it can explain well the pattern of bottomonium sequential suppression where their nuclear modification factors satisfy the relation $R_{AA}(1s)>R_{AA}(2s)>R_{AA}(3s)$ observed in experiments. In the other limit of $F(T,r)$, bottomonium wave packages tend to expand due to weak attracted force, which results in evident transitions from $Υ(2s)$ to $Υ(3s)$ components and does not satisfy the sequential suppression pattern. We suggest that the bottomonium sequential suppression can be a probe of strong heavy-quark potential in the medium.

## Key claim
The sequential suppression pattern of bottomonium states (R_AA(1s) > R_AA(2s) > R_AA(3s)) can be explained only when the real part of the heavy-quark potential is close to the internal energy U(T,r), indicating a strong attractive force; when the potential approaches the free energy F(T,r), the wave packages expand and the pattern is lost, suggesting that bottomonium suppression can serve as a probe of the in-medium heavy-quark potential.

## Method
The authors solved the time-dependent Schrodinger equation for bottomonium wave packages in a hot QGP medium, using a complex potential whose real part is parametrized as V_R = xF + (1-x)U (with x between 0 and 1) and whose imaginary part is fitted to lattice QCD data with two uncertainty bands. Wave packages were initialized as bottomonium vacuum eigenstates at tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c, evolved event-by-event along trajectories in a (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic medium, and projected onto vacuum eigenstates after the medium to obtain nuclear modification factors including feed-down contributions.

## Key numbers
- Bottom mass m_b = 4.62 GeV used in the reduced mass m_mu = m_b/2
- Maximum initial temperature at center of fireball: T_0 = 510 MeV at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb, T_0 = 484 MeV at 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb, T_0 = 390 MeV at 200 GeV Au-Au
- Critical temperature T_c = 165 MeV for the first-order phase transition
- Time step Delta t = 0.001 fm/c and radial step Delta r = 0.03 fm in the Crank-Nicolson solver
- Mean transverse momentum squared values: <p_T^2>_pp = 80 (GeV/c)^2 at 5.02 TeV, 55 (GeV/c)^2 at 2.76 TeV, 28 (GeV/c)^2 at 200 GeV
- Direct production cross sections at 5.02 TeV: sigma_direct(1s) = 37.97 nb, sigma_direct(1p) = 44.2 nb, sigma_direct(2s) = 18.27 nb, sigma_direct(2p) = 37.68 nb, sigma_direct(3s) = 8.21 nb

## Context
This work extends potential-model studies of heavy quarkonium in heavy-ion collisions, where previous approaches include the Schrodinger-Langevin equation (Katz 2015) and open quantum system methods using Lindblad equations (Brambilla 2016, 2020) or stochastic Schrodinger equations (Akamatsu 2018, Xie 2022). The authors build on lattice QCD calculations of the heavy-quark free energy F and internal energy U (Burnier 2016) and use a hydrodynamic model previously applied to charmonium and bottomonium at RHIC (Liu 2010) and LHC (Chen 2013, 2018). The results suggest that measuring R_AA for Upsilon(2s) and Upsilon(3s) with better precision can discriminate between strong and weak in-medium potentials, enabling extraction of the true heavy-quark potential from experimental data.
