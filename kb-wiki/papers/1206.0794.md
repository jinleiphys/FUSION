# Semi-realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions with improved neutron-matter properties

> New parameter-sets of the semi-realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction are developed, by modifying the M3Y interaction but maintaining the tensor channels and the longest-range central channels. The modification is made so as to reproduce microscopic results of neutron-matter energies, in addition to the measured binding energies of doubly magic nuclei including $^{100}$Sn and the even-odd mass differences of the Z=50 and N=82 nuclei in the self-consistent mean-field calculations. Separation energies of the proton- or neutron-magic nuclei are shown to be in fair agreement with the experimental data. With the new parameter-sets M3Y-P6 and P7, the isotropic spin-saturated symmetric nuclear matter remains stable in the density range as wide as $ρ\lesssim 6ρ_0$, while keeping desirable results of the previous parameter-set on finite nuclei. Isotope shifts of the Pb nuclei and tensor-force effects on shell structure are discussed.

## Key claim

New parameter-sets M3Y-P6 and M3Y-P7 of the semi-realistic M3Y-type nucleon-nucleon interaction are developed by fitting to microscopic neutron-matter energies (FP and APR results, respectively) while maintaining the tensor channels and longest-range central channels, yielding symmetric nuclear matter stable up to densities of about 6 times saturation density, provided the isotropic spin-saturated condition holds.

## Method

The author starts from the M3Y-Paris interaction (M3Y-P0) and modifies the central and spin-orbit channels while keeping the tensor channels and the longest-range central channels (identical to the one-pion exchange potential) unchanged. The new parameters are determined by fitting to microscopic neutron-matter energies from Friedman-Pandharipande (FP) for M3Y-P6 and from Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall (APR) for M3Y-P7, in addition to measured binding energies of doubly magic nuclei including 100Sn and even-odd mass differences of Z=50 and N=82 nuclei, using self-consistent mean-field (Hartree-Fock and Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov) calculations with a Gaussian expansion method basis.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: k_F0 = 1.340 fm^-1 for both M3Y-P6 and M3Y-P7
- Saturation energy: E_0 = -16.24 MeV (M3Y-P6) and -16.22 MeV (M3Y-P7)
- Incompressibility at saturation: K_0 = 239.7 MeV (M3Y-P6) and 254.7 MeV (M3Y-P7)
- Symmetry energy at saturation: a_t0 = 32.14 MeV (M3Y-P6) and 31.74 MeV (M3Y-P7)
- Effective mass at saturation: M*_0/M = 0.596 (M3Y-P6) and 0.589 (M3Y-P7)
- Symmetry energy slope parameter: L_t0 = 44.64 MeV (M3Y-P6) and 51.53 MeV (M3Y-P7)
- Binding energy of 100Sn: 822.5 MeV (M3Y-P6) and 822.8 MeV (M3Y-P7), compared to experimental 824.8 MeV
- Binding energy of 208Pb: 1634.5 MeV (M3Y-P6) and 1635.5 MeV (M3Y-P7), compared to experimental 1636.4 MeV
- Neutron-skin thickness in 208Pb: 0.158 fm (M3Y-P6) and 0.161 fm (M3Y-P7)
- Symmetric nuclear matter remains stable up to rho less than about 6 rho_0 for M3Y-P6 and P7

## Context

This work extends the M3Y-type semi-realistic interactions previously developed by Nakada (2003, 2008) by improving the density dependence of the symmetry energy, which was insufficiently reliable in earlier parameter-sets such as M3Y-P5 as noted by Tsang et al. (2009). The new interactions are fitted to microscopic neutron-matter calculations by Friedman-Pandharipande (1981) and Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall (1998), and they enable more reliable studies of neutron star structure, charge-exchange reactions, and pygmy dipole resonances in neutron-rich nuclei. The parameter-sets also allow investigation of tensor-force effects on shell structure and isotope shifts in Pb nuclei within self-consistent mean-field and random-phase approximation frameworks.
