# Conformal symmetry and the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation

> Solutions to the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation are considered which respect an SO(3) subgroup of the conformal group. The symmetry dictates a specific dependence of the saturation scale on the impact parameter. Applications to deep inelastic scattering are considered.

## Key claim

Solutions to the leading-order Balitsky-Kovchegov equation exist that respect an SO(3) subgroup of the conformal group, which dictates a specific dependence of the saturation scale on impact parameter; however, this conformally invariant form leads to a total cross-section that increases too quickly at small Q, indicating that confinement effects are needed to capture the rapid falloff of energy density at large transverse radius.

## Method

The paper imposes an SO(3) subgroup of the PSL(2,C) conformal symmetry group on the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation, which requires the dipole scattering amplitude S(r1,r2;Y) to depend only on a chordal distance function d_q(r1,r2) rather than on the simple dipole size |r1-r2|. The author then derives the impact-parameter dependence of the saturation scale Q_s(b;Y) from this symmetry and computes the total cross-section for deep inelastic scattering by integrating the dipole amplitude over the photon wave functions, comparing the results to HERA data.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale growth exponent c ≈ 4.88, appearing in Q_s(Y) = Q_0 exp((c/2) ᾱ_s Y)
- The anomalous dimension γ_c ≈ 0.628, defined as the solution to γ χ'(γ) = χ(γ)
- The Golec-Biernat-Wüsthoff model uses Q_s(Y) = (1 GeV) exp(0.14(Y-8.1)) with σ_0 = 23 μb and quark masses m_f = 140 MeV
- A cutoff b_max = 0.69 fm provides a good fit to DIS data when using the SO(3)-invariant form with a hard cutoff
- The parameter q is chosen approximately as the inverse proton radius, q ≈ 0.2 GeV
- The HERA data covers Q between 0.2 GeV and 20 GeV, with Q_s on the order of 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the use of conformal symmetry from relativistic hydrodynamics (Gubser, 2010) to the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation, similar in spirit to Hatta (2009) who used an SO(2,1) subgroup for the Banfi-Marchesini-Smye equation. The SO(3)-invariant form for the saturation scale Q_s(b;Y) = Q_s^max(Y)/(1+q^2 b^2) was previously considered by Iancu (2007) and Bondarenko (2003), but this paper provides its symmetry justification. The approach enables systematic study of impact-parameter dependence in saturation physics, though the conformal prediction overestimates cross-section growth at small τ compared to HERA data (Stasto, 2000), indicating the need for confinement effects.
