# Examination of the influence of the f$_{0}$(975) and $ϕ$(1020) mesons on the surface gravitional redshift of the neutron star PSR J0348+0432

> The effect of the mesons $f_{0}(975)$ and $ϕ(1020)$ on the surface gravitional redshift of the neutron star PSR J0348+0432 is examined in the framework of the relativistic mean field theory by choosing the suitable hyperon coupling constants. We find that compared with that without considering the mesons $f_{0}(975)$ and $ϕ(1020)$, the value range of the radius $R$ of the neutron star PSR J0348+0432 would be changed from a narrow range 12.964 km $\sim$ 12.364 km to a wider range 12.941 km $\sim$ 11.907 km corresponding to the observation mass M=1.97 M$_{\odot}$$\sim$2.05 M$_{\odot}$. We also find that the value range of the surface gravitational redshift $z$ of the neutron star PSR J0348+0432 changes from 0.3469 $\sim$ 0.3997 to 0.3480 $\sim$ 0.4263 corresponding to the observation mass M=1.97 M$_{\odot}$$\sim$2.05 M$_{\odot}$ as the mesons $f_{0}(975)$ and $ϕ(1020)$ being considered. These mean the radius $R$ and the surface gravitational redshift $z$ all will be constrained in a wider scope as the mesons $f_{0}(975)$ and $ϕ(1020)$ being considered. We also can see that the difference of the radius and the surface gravitational redshift is not so large whether the mesons $f_{0}(975)$ and $ϕ(1020)$ being considered or not. This indicates that the mesons $f_{0}(975)$ and $ϕ(1020)$ do not play a major role in the massive neutron star PSR J0348+0432.

# Examination of the influence of the f0(975) and φ(1020) mesons on the surface gravitational redshift of the neutron star PSR J0348+0432

## Key claim
Including the f0(975) and φ(1020) mesons in the relativistic mean field description of the neutron star PSR J0348+0432 widens the allowed ranges of radius and surface gravitational redshift, but the differences are small enough that these mesons "do not play a major role" in this massive neutron star, indicating that nucleon-hyperon interactions mediated by these mesons are very weak.

## Method
The author used relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with the GL85 nucleon coupling constant set and the baryon octet, solving the Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute neutron star structure. Hyperon coupling constants for the σ, ω, and ρ mesons were determined by fitting to hyperon well depths in nuclear matter (UΛ(N) = -30 MeV, UΣ(N) = +40 MeV, UΞ(N) = -28 MeV) and using SU(6) symmetry relations, while the f0(975) and φ(1020) couplings were obtained from quark model relations and phenomenological potential depth constraints. The parameter set NO.40 (xσΛ=0.8, xωΛ=0.9319; xσΣ=0.4, xωΣ=0.8250; xσΞ=0.8383, xωΞ=0.999) was used to compute radius and surface gravitational redshift for the observed mass range of PSR J0348+0432.

## Key numbers
- Without f0(975) and φ(1020) mesons: radius range 12.964 km to 12.364 km for mass range 1.97 M⊙ to 2.05 M⊙
- With f0(975) and φ(1020) mesons: radius range 12.941 km to 11.907 km for mass range 1.97 M⊙ to 2.05 M⊙
- Without f0(975) and φ(1020) mesons: surface gravitational redshift z range 0.3469 to 0.3997 for mass range 1.97 M⊙ to 2.05 M⊙
- With f0(975) and φ(1020) mesons: surface gravitational redshift z range 0.3480 to 0.4263 for mass range 1.97 M⊙ to 2.05 M⊙
- Central energy density εc without mesons: 1.2470×10^15 g cm^-3 to 1.6721×10^15 g cm^-3
- Central energy density εc with mesons: 1.2754×10^15 g cm^-3 to 2.0660×10^15 g cm^-3
- The maximum mass obtained with mesons included (parameter set NO.40) is 2.05 M⊙

## Context
This work extends the theoretical study of the massive neutron star PSR J0348+0432, first observed by Antoniadis et al. (2013), within the RMF framework previously applied to PSR J1614-2230 by Demorest et al. (2010) and many subsequent authors. The study uses the GL85 parameter set from Glendenning (1985) and follows the hyperon coupling methodology of Schaffner et al. (1994) and Zhao (2012). The results show that the f0(975) and φ(1020) mesons, which mediate hyperon-hyperon interactions, have only a minor effect on the structure of this particular massive neutron star, suggesting that future work on even more massive neutron stars or different compositions may be needed to constrain these couplings further.
