# Description of the isotope chain $^{180-196}$Pt within some solvable approaches

> Energies of the ground, $β$ and $γ$ bands as well as the associated B(E2) values are determined for each even-even isotope of the $^{180-196}$Pt chain by the exact solutions of some differential equations which approximate the generalized Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian. The emerging approaches are called the Sextic and Spheroidal Approach (SSA), the Sextic and Mathieu Approach (SMA), the Infinite Square Well and Spheroidal Approach (ISWSA) and the Infinite Square Well and Mathieu Approach (ISWMA), respectively. While the first three methods were formulated in some earlier papers of the present authors, ISWMA is an inedited approach of this work. Numerical results are compared with those obtained with the so called X(5) and Z(5) models. A contour plot for the probability density as function of the intrinsic dynamic deformations is given for a few states of the three considered bands with the aim of evidencing the shape evolution along the isotope chain and pointing out possible shape coexistence.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Sextic and Spheroidal Approach (SSA) and the Infinite Square Well and Spheroidal Approach (ISWSA) describe the ground, beta, and gamma bands of the lighter Pt isotopes (180-188) better than the X(5) model, while the Sextic and Mathieu Approach (SMA) and the Infinite Square Well and Mathieu Approach (ISWMA) perform better than the Z(5) model for the heavier isotopes (190-196), but this conclusion is qualified by the observation that for some specific isotopes and transitions, such as Z(5) for 192Pt and 196Pt, the competing models yield smaller r.m.s. deviations.

## Method

The authors apply four solvable approaches (SSA, SMA, ISWSA, ISWMA) derived from the Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian with separated beta and gamma potentials to the even-even isotope chain 180-196Pt. The beta equation uses either an infinite square well (ISWSA, ISWMA) or a sextic oscillator plus centrifugal barrier (SSA, SMA), while the gamma equation employs spheroidal functions (SSA, ISWSA) or Mathieu functions (SMA, ISWMA) obtained from a second-order expansion of the rotor term and a periodic potential. Excitation energies and B(E2) values are calculated and compared with experimental data and with the X(5) and Z(5) models.

## Key numbers

- For 180Pt, the r.m.s. deviation for excitation energies is 58 keV for SSA, 108 keV for ISWSA, and 128 keV for X(5).
- For 182Pt, the r.m.s. deviation for excitation energies is 47 keV for SSA, 156 keV for ISWSA, and 164 keV for X(5).
- For 192Pt, the r.m.s. deviation for excitation energies is 76 keV for ISWMA, 158 keV for Z(5), and 193 keV for SMA.
- For 196Pt, the r.m.s. deviation for excitation energies is 92 keV for SMA, 135 keV for ISWMA, and 274 keV for Z(5).
- The experimental energy ratio R_{4_g+/2_g+} for 180Pt is 2.69, for 182Pt is 2.71, and for 184Pt is 2.67, compared to the X(5) prediction of 2.90.
- The experimental energy ratio R_{0_beta+/2_g+} for 192Pt is 3.78, compared to the Z(5) prediction of 3.91.
- For 192Pt, the quantity |Delta E| = |E_{3_gamma+} - E_{2_gamma+} - E_{2_g+}| equals 8 keV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on Os isotopes (Raduta and Buganu, 2011; 2012) using SSA, DSA, and ISWSA to the Pt isotope chain, and introduces ISWMA as a new approach. The results are compared with the X(5) critical point symmetry (Iachello, 2001) and the Z(5) model (Bonatsos et al., 2004), and are motivated by experimental evidence for shape coexistence in Pt isotopes from studies such as Petkov (2012) and Dracoulis (2011). The analysis enables further investigation of the prolate-to-oblate shape phase transition along the Pt chain and the identification of candidates for triaxial deformation near gamma_0 = 30 degrees.
