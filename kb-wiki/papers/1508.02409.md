# Conserved Charge Susceptibilities in a Chemically Frozen Hadronic Gas

> In a hadronic gas with three conserved charges (electric charge, baryon number, and strangeness) we employ the hadron resonance gas model to compute both diagonal and off-diagonal susceptibilities. We model the effect of chemical freeze-out in two ways: one in which all particle numbers are conserved below the chemical freeze-out temperature and one which takes into account resonance decays. We then briefly discuss possible implications these results may have on two active areas of research, hydrodynamic fluctuations and the search for the QCD critical point.

## Key claim
The paper shows that including chemical freeze-out in the hadron resonance gas model generally enhances conserved charge susceptibilities at low temperatures compared to full equilibrium, but this effect is reduced by about 10% when resonance decays are included via partial chemical equilibrium. The authors note that for net proton fluctuations used in the search for the QCD critical point, the skewness and kurtosis are independent of whether the system is in chemical equilibrium or frozen out within the Boltzmann approximation used.

## Method
The authors employed the hadron resonance gas model including all particles and resonances from the Particle Data Group with masses below 2 GeV, using the Boltzmann approximation throughout. They implemented two chemical freeze-out scenarios: Full Freeze-Out (FFO) where each hadron species has fixed numbers below the freeze-out temperature, and Partial Chemical Equilibrium (PCE) where only long-lived stable hadrons (pions, kaons, eta, omega, nucleons, eta-prime, phi, Lambda, Sigma, Xi, Omega) are conserved while resonances decay. The chemical potentials enforcing these conditions were solved analytically for FFO and numerically for PCE using Mathematica's FindRoot function.

## Key numbers
- Chemical freeze-out temperature assumed: T_ch = 160 MeV
- The PCE scenario amounts to roughly an O(10%) correction to the FFO scenario for temperatures above 100 MeV
- For high mass particles (m ~ 1 GeV) and low temperatures (T ~ 100 MeV), the approximate formula for FFO chemical potentials differs from the analytic result by less than 3%
- The HRG model is applicable for temperatures below deconfinement (T less than about 180 MeV) and above kinetic freeze-out (T greater than about 100 MeV)
- For the baryon-baryon susceptibility ratio chi_BB T / s, the FFO and PCE curves lie exactly on top of each other within numerical precision

## Context
This work extends the long-standing discussion of chemical freeze-out effects on thermodynamics in heavy ion collisions, which previously focused on pressure and energy density (Bebie 1991, Hirano 2002, Teaney 2002, Huovinen 2007, Huovinen 2009). The authors reopen this line of inquiry with attention to susceptibilities, which are needed for studies of hydrodynamic fluctuations (Ling 2013) and the search for the QCD critical point at RHIC's Beam Energy Scan (Stephanov 1998, Stephanov 1999, Hatta 2003). The results enable more accurate modeling of thermal fluctuations and balance functions in heavy ion collisions, and the authors note that work exploring implications for particle correlations and the critical point search is underway.
