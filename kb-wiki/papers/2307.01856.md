# Long-Lived Particles and the Quiet Sun

> The nuclear reaction network within the interior of the Sun is an efficient MeV physics factory, and can produce long-lived particles generic to dark sector models. In this work we consider the sensitivity of satellite instruments, primarily the RHESSI Spectrometer, that observe the Quiet Sun in the MeV regime where backgrounds are low. We find that Quiet Sun observations offer a powerful and complementary probe in regions of parameter space where the long-lived particle decay length is longer than the radius of the Sun, and shorter than the distance between the Sun and Earth. We comment on connections to recent model-building work on heavy neutral leptons coupled to neutrinos and high-quality axions from mirror symmetries.

## Key claim
Quiet Sun observations by satellite instruments like the RHESSI Spectrometer can probe long-lived particles (LLPs) from dark sectors, but only in the specific parameter region where the LLP decay length is longer than the Sun's radius and shorter than the Earth-Sun distance, and where the signal flux exceeds the total observed background in any energy window.

## Method
The authors performed a rate-only analysis of RHESSI front-segment data, comparing the predicted photon flux from LLP decays against the observed background flux. For neutrino upscattering via a transition dipole moment, they implemented a Monte Carlo simulation sampling neutrino energy, production location, and upscattering location, accounting for flavor transformation. For solar axions produced via the p d -> 3He gamma reaction in the pp chain, they related the axion flux to the pp neutrino flux and accounted for axion absorption, Primakoff scattering, and electron scattering. For model-independent LLP searches, they considered mono-energetic and power-law production spectra and computed the resulting photon flux at Earth.

## Key numbers
- The pp neutrino flux used for axion calculations is 6 x 10^10 cm^-2 s^-1
- RHESSI has 3 keV FWHM energy resolution below 1 MeV
- The axion photon flux threshold for exclusion above 1 MeV is 1.8 x 10^-3 s^-1 cm^-2 keV^-1
- For the neutrino dipole portal, bounds end near m_N = 1 MeV because decay lengths become smaller than the Sun's radius beyond this mass
- The axion sensitivity is exhausted for g_{3aN} ~ 1 x 10^-5
- For axions with m_a = 1 MeV and f_a = 3 x 10^4 GeV, the corresponding couplings are g_{a gamma gamma} ~ 2 x 10^-8 and g_{3aN} ~ 3 x 10^-5

## Context
This work extends the original idea by Stodolsky and Raffelt (1982) for detecting 200 keV axions from the Sun, which had remained unexplored with new data for decades. It provides complementary constraints to terrestrial searches by CAST (2009) and Borexino (2012), which are sensitive to shorter decay lengths, while RHESSI probes LLPs with decay lengths between the Sun's radius and Earth-Sun distance. The results connect to recent model-building on heavy neutral leptons coupled to neutrinos and high-quality axions from mirror symmetries, and the authors suggest future missions like COSI could substantially improve sensitivity through larger surface area, better background understanding, and angular profile analysis.
