# Dipole response in neutron-rich nuclei with new Skyrme interactions

> We investigate the isoscalar and isovector E1 response of neutron-rich nuclei, within a semi-classical transport model employing effective interactions for the nuclear mean-field. In particular, we adopt the recently introduced SAMi-J Skyrme interactions, whose parameters are specifically tuned to improve the description of spin-isospin properties of nuclei. Our analysis evidences a relevant degree of isoscalar/isovector mixing of the collective excitations developing in neutron-rich systems. Focusing on the low-lying strength emerging in the isovector response, we show that this energy region essentially corresponds to the excitation of isoscalar-like modes, which also contribute to the isovector response owing to their mixed character. Considering effective interactions which mostly differ in the isovector channels, we observe that these mixing effects increase with the slope L of the symmetry energy at saturation density, leading to a larger strength in the low-energy region of the isovector response. This result appears connected to the increase, with L, of the neutron/proton asymmetry at the surface of the considered nuclei, i.e., to the extension of the neutron skin.

## Key claim

The low-lying strength observed in the isovector dipole response of neutron-rich nuclei essentially corresponds to the excitation of isoscalar-like modes that contribute to the isovector response due to their mixed isoscalar/isovector character, and this mixing increases with the slope L of the symmetry energy at saturation density.

## Method

The authors solved the semi-classical Vlasov equation for neutron and proton distribution functions using a test-particle method with 1500 test particles per nucleon, employing three SAMi-J Skyrme interactions (SAMi-J27, SAMi-J31, SAMi-J35) that differ primarily in the isovector channel. Ground state configurations for the neutron-rich nuclei 68Ni, 132Sn, and 208Pb were obtained by tuning neutron and proton radii to minimize total energy, and the dipole response was studied by applying isoscalar and isovector initial perturbations.

## Key numbers

- SAMi-J interactions predict saturation density rho_0 = 0.159 fm^-3, energy per nucleon E/A = -15.9 MeV, and incompressibility K = 245 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter.
- The symmetry energy coefficient at average density rho_c = 0.65 rho_0 is approximately C(rho_c) ≈ 22 MeV for all three SAMi-J parametrizations.
- The slope parameter L of the symmetry energy at saturation density takes values of 29.9 MeV (SAMi-J27), 74.5 MeV (SAMi-J31), and 115.2 MeV (SAMi-J35).
- Neutron skin thickness increases with the slope parameter L for all studied nuclei (68Ni, 132Sn, 208Pb).

## Context

This work extends previous semi-classical transport studies of dipole response in neutron-rich nuclei (e.g., Urban (2012), Baran (2012)) by employing the recently introduced SAMi-J Skyrme interactions, which were specifically tuned to improve spin-isospin properties (Roca-Maza (2012)). The analysis connects the mixed isoscalar/isovector character of collective excitations to the density dependence of the symmetry energy and the neutron skin extension, complementing both earlier semi-classical and RPA studies (Urban (2012), Roca-Maza (2012)). The results enable further investigation of the relationship between low-energy dipole strength and neutron skin thickness across different mass regions.
