# A Survey of Nuclear Pasta in the Intermediate Density Regime I: Shapes and Energies

> Background: Nuclear pasta, emerging due to the competition between the long-range Coulomb force and the short-range strong force, is believed to be present in astrophysical scenarios, such as neutron stars and core-collapse supernovae. Its structure can have a high impact e.g. on neutrino transport or the tidal deformability of neutron stars. Purpose: We study several possible pasta configurations, all of them minimal surface configurations, which are expected to appear in the mid-density regime of nuclear pasta, i.e. around 40% of the nuclear saturation density. In particular we are interested in the energy spectrum for different pasta configurations considered. Method: Employing the density functional theory (DFT) approach, we calculate the binding energy of the different configurations for three values of the proton content XP = 1/10, 1/3 and 1/2, by optimizing their periodic length. We study finite temperature effects and the impact of electron screening. Results: Nuclear pasta lowers the energy significantly compared to uniform matter, especially for $X_P \geq 1/3$. However, the different configurations have very similar binding energies. For large proton content, $X_P \geq 1/3$, the pasta configurations are very stable, for lower proton content temperatures of a few MeV are enough for the transition to uniform matter. Electron screening has a small influence on the binding energy of nuclear pasta, but increases its periodic length. Conclusion: Nuclear pasta in the mid-density regime lowers the energy of the matter for all proton fractions under study. It can survive even large temperatures of several MeV. Since various configurations have very similar energy, it is to expected that many configurations can coexist simultaneously already at small temperatures.

## Key claim

Nuclear pasta configurations in the intermediate density regime (around 40% of nuclear saturation density) lower the energy significantly compared to uniform matter, especially for proton fractions \(X_P \geq 1/3\), but the different pasta shapes have binding energies within a few hundred keV per nucleon of each other, implying that many configurations can coexist simultaneously even at small temperatures.

## Method

The authors used density functional theory (DFT) at the Hartree-Fock level with the Skyrme-type TOV-min parametrization, which is fitted to both stable nuclei and neutron star mass-radius relations. They calculated binding energies for slab, P-surface, Gyroid, Diamond, and double P-surface and double Gyroid configurations by optimizing the periodic box length for three proton fractions (\(X_P = 1/10, 1/3, 1/2\)) at mean densities between \(0.04\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) and \(0.08\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\). Finite-temperature effects were studied using the free energy with Fermi-Dirac occupations, and electron screening was included via the Thomas-Fermi approximation to the Poisson equation.

## Key numbers

- For \(X_P=1/2\) and \(X_P=1/3\) at low densities, the slab pasta configuration is lower in energy than uniform matter by as much as \(5\ \mathrm{MeV}\) per particle.
- At the highest density studied (\(0.08\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\)), the energy difference between slab pasta and uniform matter is about \(1\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(X_P=1/2\) and \(1/3\).
- For \(X_P=1/10\) at low densities, the energy is lowered by about \(1\ \mathrm{MeV}\) relative to uniform matter.
- At zero temperature, the free energy difference between slab and uniform matter is about \(4.4\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(X_P=1/2\), \(3.9\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(X_P=1/3\), and only about \(0.8\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(X_P=1/10\) at \(\rho=0.04\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\).
- At \(T=10\ \mathrm{MeV}\), the free energy difference shrinks to \(0.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(X_P=1/2\) and \(0.35\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(X_P=1/3\); for \(X_P=1/10\) the transition to uniform matter occurs between \(T=6\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(T=8\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For a slab configuration with \(\rho=0.05\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) and \(X_P=1/2\), electron screening changes the total energy from \(-12.54\ \mathrm{MeV}\) to \(-12.61\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (about 0.4% reduction) and increases the optimal periodic length from \(17.61\ \mathrm{fm}\) to \(18.22\ \mathrm{fm}\) (2.3% increase).
- For the most extreme case with \(\rho=0.08\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) and \(X_P=1/2\), electron screening increases the periodic length by 4.8%.
- For \(X_P \gtrsim 1/3\), none of the pasta configurations differ in binding energy by more than \(0.3\ \mathrm{MeV}/A\) from the slab configuration; most differ by not more than \(0.1\ \mathrm{MeV}/A\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nuclear pasta using density functional theory, particularly the investigation of triply periodic minimal surface (TPMS) configurations such as the Gyroid and Diamond (Schuetrumpf 2015) and the P-surface (Schuetrumpf 2013). It provides a comprehensive survey of single and double TPMS configurations in the intermediate density regime, complementing classical liquid drop model and molecular dynamics calculations (Dorso 2012, Caplan 2018). The results enable improved modeling of neutron star crust properties relevant to gravitational wave observations (GW170817) and neutrino transport in core-collapse supernovae, and suggest that pasta matter at finite temperature is not well ordered but has an amorphous character with constant transformations between configurations.
