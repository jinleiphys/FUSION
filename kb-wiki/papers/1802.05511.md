# Charge Symmetry Breaking Effects in Pion and Kaon Structure

> Charge symmetry breaking (CSB) effects associated with the $u$ and $d$ quark mass difference are investigated in the quark distribution functions and spacelike electromagnetic form factors of the pion and kaon. We use a confining version of the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model, where CSB effects at the infrared scale associated with the model are driven by the dressed $u$ and $d$ quark mass ratio, which because of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking is much closer to unity than the associated current quark mass ratio. The pion and kaon are given as bound states of a dressed quark and a dressed antiquark governed by the Bethe-Salpeter equation, and exhibit the properties of Goldstone bosons, with a pion mass difference given by $m_{π^+}^2 - m_{π^0}^2 \propto (m_u - m_d)^2$ as demanded by dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. We find significant CSB effects for realistic current quark mass ratios ($m_u/m_d \sim 0.5$) in the quark flavor-sector electromagnetic form factors of both the pion and kaon. For example, the difference between the $u$ and $d$ quark contributions to the $π^+$ electromagnetic form factors is about 8\% at a momentum transfer of $Q^2 \simeq 10\,$GeV$^2$, while the analogous effect for the light quark sector form factors in the $K^+$ and $K^0$ is about twice as large. For the Parton distribution functions, we find CSB effects which are considerably smaller than those found in the electromagnetic form factors.

## Key claim

Charge symmetry breaking (CSB) effects in pion and kaon structure, driven by the \(u\) and \(d\) quark mass difference, are found to be significant in the electromagnetic form factors at large momentum transfer but considerably smaller in the parton distribution functions; the size of these effects is determined by the dressed quark mass ratio, which due to dynamical chiral symmetry breaking is much closer to unity than the current quark mass ratio.

## Method

The authors use a confining version of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model regularized with the proper-time scheme, which includes an infrared cutoff to simulate quark confinement. Pion and kaon bound states are obtained by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the pseudoscalar channel, and the dressed quark-photon vertex is determined from the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation. For a given current quark mass ratio \(m_u/m_d\), the model parameters are fixed by the physical \(\pi^+\) mass, leptonic decay constant, and \(\rho^0\) mass, with the strange quark mass constrained to give the physical kaon mass.

## Key numbers

- The difference between the \(u\) and \(d\) quark contributions to the \(\pi^+\) electromagnetic form factor is about 8% at a momentum transfer of \(Q^2 \simeq 10\ \text{GeV}^2\) for a realistic current quark mass ratio \(m_u/m_d \sim 0.5\).
- The analogous CSB effect for the light quark sector form factors in the \(K^+\) and \(K^0\) is about twice as large as that in the pion at large \(Q^2\).
- For \(m_u/m_d = 0.5\), the dressed quark masses are \(M_u = 396\ \text{MeV}\) and \(M_d = 404\ \text{MeV}\), giving \((M_d - M_u)/(M_u + M_d) = 1\%\).
- The CSB effect in the quark-sector charge radii of the \(\pi^+\) is \([|r^u_{\pi^+}| - |r^d_{\pi^+}|]/[|r^u_{\pi^+}| + |r^d_{\pi^+}|] \simeq 0.7\%\) for \(m_u/m_d \simeq 0.5\), with \(r^u_{\pi^+} = 0.625\ \text{fm}\) and \(r^d_{\pi^+} = -0.616\ \text{fm}\).
- The kaon mass splitting from CSB alone is \(m_{K^0} - m_{K^\pm} = 7.8\ \text{MeV}\) for \(m_u/m_d = 0.5\), about twice the empirical splitting of \(3.93\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of CSB in hadron structure, such as lattice QCD calculations by Borsanyi (2013) and Horsley (2015) for hadron masses, and earlier NJL model studies of meson properties. The results show that CSB effects in the electromagnetic form factors grow logarithmically with \(Q^2\) at accessible scales, in contrast to the asymptotic QCD prediction that they vanish at very high \(Q^2\) (Farrar 1979, Lepage 1980). These findings are relevant for extracting CSB effects and strange quark content in the nucleon through processes such as pion-induced Drell-Yan reactions, and for planning experiments at Jefferson Lab and a future electron-ion collider.
