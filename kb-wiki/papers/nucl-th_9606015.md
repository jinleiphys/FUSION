# Unitary ambiguity in the extraction of the E2/M1 ratio for the $γN\leftrightarrowΔ$ transition

> The resonant electric quadrupole amplitude in the transition $γN\leftrightarrowΔ(1232)$ is of great interest for the understanding of baryon structure. Various dynamical models have been developed to extract it from the corresponding photoproduction multipole of pions on nucleons. It is shown that once such a model is specified, a whole class of unitarily equivalent models can be constructed, all of them providing exactly the same fit to the experimental data. However, they may predict quite different resonant amplitudes. Therefore, the extraction of the E2/M1($γN\leftrightarrowΔ$) ratio (bare or dressed) which is based on a dynamical model using a largely phenomenological $πN$ interaction is not unique.

# Unitary ambiguity in the extraction of the E2/M1 ratio for the γN↔Δ transition

## Key claim

The extraction of the resonant E2/M1 ratio for the γN↔Δ(1232) transition from pion photoproduction data is not unique, because once a dynamical model with a phenomenological πN interaction is specified, a whole class of unitarily equivalent models can be constructed that give exactly the same fit to experimental data but predict quite different resonant amplitudes. The ambiguity persists even with perfectly accurate data and affects both bare and dressed resonant multipoles.

## Method

The authors construct a simple dynamical model in a Hilbert space of Δ ⊕ πN ⊕ γN states, with a Hamiltonian containing a background πN interaction, a πNΔ vertex, a nonresonant γN→πN driving term, and a γNΔ vertex, using a separable form for the background interaction as in Tanabe and Ohta (1985). They then apply a unitary transformation U(α)=exp(iαχ) with a generator χ that mixes resonant and background πN interactions, chosen to be odd under time reversal, and evaluate how the bare and dressed resonant multipoles M_{1+}^{3/2} and E_{1+}^{3/2} change under this transformation while the total multipoles remain invariant.

## Key numbers

* The bare resonant ratio R_{EM}^Δ at resonance varies between -1.5% and -5% for transformation angles |α̃| ≤ 10°.
* The original model (α̃ = 0°) predicts R_{EM}^Δ = -3.1% at resonance, identical to the result of Nozawa, Blankleider and Lee (1990).
* For α̃ = +10°, the ratio is approximately -5%; for α̃ = -10°, the ratio is approximately -1.5%.
* The transformation parameter α̃ is varied over the range 0°, ±5°, ±10° in the numerical examples.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical model approach of Tanabe and Ohta (1985), Yang (1985), and Nozawa, Blankleider and Lee (1990) for extracting the γN↔Δ transition amplitudes from pion photoproduction, and contradicts the implicit assumption in Bernstein, Nozawa and Moinester (1993) that improved data will allow favoring one model and yield a unique R_{EM}. The paper demonstrates a fundamental unitary ambiguity analogous to representation dependence seen in the deuteron D-wave or isobaric components in nuclei (Friar, 1979; Amado, 1979). This result calls into question the possibility of cleanly testing microscopic hadron models against extracted resonant amplitudes, and suggests that future work should instead calculate the complete γN→πN amplitude including background contributions within the same dynamical model. The ambiguity may also affect separation of resonant and nonresonant amplitudes in other reactions such as η photoproduction on nucleons via the S_{11}(1535) resonance.
