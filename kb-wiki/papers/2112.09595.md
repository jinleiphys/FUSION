# A Bayesian analysis of the properties of hybrid stars with the NJL model

> We implement a Bayesian analysis of the properties of non-rotating hybrid stars at equilibrium with quark matter cores, as described by the SU(3) Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. The hadronic phase is described by a unified metamodeling approach, with a prior parameter space covering the present uncertainties on nuclear matter properties with nucleonic degrees of freedom. The parameter space of the NJL model includes vector-isoscalar and vector-isovector couplings and, additionally, an effective bag constant for the quark pressure is introduced as a free parameter. The phase transition is assumed to be first order with charge neutral phases, following the Maxwell construction. Our Bayesian framework includes filters on the experimental and theoretical low-density nuclear physics knowledge (atomic masses, ab initio calculations of the EoS) and high density constraints from astrophysical observations (maximum mass of J0348+0432, binary tidal deformability of the GW170817 event). We find that microscopic vector interactions play an important role in quark matter in order to stiffen the equation of state sufficiently to reach high star masses, in agreement with previous studies. Even within a very large prior for both the hadronic phase and the quark phase and the important freedom brought by the effective bag constant, our posterior quark cores tend to be relatively small and only appear in very heavy stars ($M\gtrsim 2 M_\odot$). Coincidentally, the inclusion of the nucleon-quark transition (deconfinement transition) only weakly affects the radii of compact stars, foreshadowing a very low observability of a possible phase transition using x-ray radii measurements.

## Key claim

Even with a very large prior parameter space for both the hadronic and quark phases, and the additional freedom of an effective bag constant, the posterior quark cores in hybrid stars tend to be relatively small and only appear in very heavy stars (M ≳ 2 M⊙), which foreshadows a very low observability of a possible deconfinement phase transition using x-ray radii measurements.

## Method

The authors constructed hybrid equations of state by combining a unified metamodeling approach for the hadronic phase (with a prior parameter space covering nuclear matter uncertainties) with the SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for the quark phase, assuming a first-order phase transition via the Maxwell construction. They performed a Bayesian analysis that included filters from low-density nuclear physics knowledge (atomic masses, ab initio calculations of the EoS) and high-density astrophysical constraints (maximum mass of J0348+0432, binary tidal deformability from GW170817), with the NJL parameter space including vector-isoscalar and vector-isovector couplings plus an effective bag constant as free parameters.

## Key numbers

- The posterior quark cores only appear in stars with mass M ≳ 2 M⊙
- The prior range for the effective bag constant B* was set to [-20, 20] MeV fm⁻³, with the physical limitation B* < B_max
- The vector to scalar coupling ratios were varied in flat distributions: ξ_ω in [0.0, 0.5] and ξ_ρ in [0.0, 1.0]
- The NJL model parameters were fixed with cutoff Λ = 630 MeV, G_S Λ² = 1.781, and K Λ⁵ = 9.29
- The nuclear prior ranges included n_sat from 0.15 to 0.17 fm⁻³, E_sat from -17.5 to -14.5 MeV, and K_sat from 190 to 300 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of hybrid stars by Ferreira (2020, 2021) using a fully controlled nucleonic EoS with a unified treatment of the neutron star crust, including constraints from nuclear mass measurements and ab initio nuclear theory. The results confirm earlier findings that quark cores can exist in high-mass neutron stars respecting current astrophysical constraints, but strengthen the conclusion that observations are still not accurate enough to decide on the existence of a deconfined core, and that the deconfinement transition only weakly affects compact star radii.
