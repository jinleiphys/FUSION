# Covariant Spectator Theory of np scattering: Isoscalar interaction currents

> Using the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST), one boson exchange (OBE) models have been found that give precision fits to low energy np scattering and the deuteron binding energy. The boson-nucleon vertices used in these models contain a momentum dependence that requires a new class of interaction currents for use with electromagnetic interactions. Current conservation requires that these new interaction currents satisfy a two-body Ward-Takahashi identity, and using principals of simplicity and picture independence, these currents can be uniquely determined. The results lead to general formulae for a two-body current that can be expressed in terms of relativistic np wave functions, Psi, and two convenient truncated wave functions, ${\it Ψ}^{(2)}$ and $\widehat {\it Ψ}$, which contain all of the information needed for the explicit evaluation of the contributions from the interaction current. These three wave functions can be calculated from the CST bound or scattering state equations (and their off-shell extrapolations). A companion paper uses this formalism to evaluate the deuteron magnetic moment.

## Key claim
Using the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST), the paper derives isoscalar interaction currents required by off-shell couplings in one-boson-exchange models, claiming these currents can be uniquely determined through principles of simplicity and picture independence without introducing undetermined parameters, provided the currents satisfy a two-body Ward-Takahashi identity.

## Method
The author constructs isoscalar two-body interaction currents by requiring they satisfy a two-body Ward-Takahashi identity derived from current conservation, using the principles of simplicity and picture independence to eliminate transverse ambiguities. The interaction current is factorized into products of the nucleon current multiplied by truncated NN interaction kernels constructed from the full OBE kernel, with only scalar and vector meson exchanges contributing. The resulting formulae express the deuteron current as traces over products of relativistic np wave functions (Ψ), truncated wave functions (Ψ^(2) and Ψ̂), propagators, and nucleon currents.

## Key numbers
- The deuteron binding energy is 2.2246 MeV, used to establish the anomalous threshold at approximately 1.7 m_π²
- The OBE models fit np scattering data up to lab energies of 350 MeV
- The isoscalar charge e₀ = 1/2, consistent with Eq. (2.17) of Gross (1987)
- The new off-shell nucleon form factor F₃ is subject to the constraint F₃(0) = 1

## Context
This work extends the CST formalism developed by Gross (1964-1965) and subsequent calculations by Gross, Arnold, and Carlson (1980) and Gross, Van Orden, and Devine (1995) for deuteron electromagnetic form factors. It specifically addresses the interaction currents required by off-shell couplings at scalar meson-nucleon vertices (parameter ν_s), which were discovered by Stadler and Gross (1996) to be necessary for correctly predicting the triton binding energy without three-body forces. The companion paper (Ref. II) applies this formalism to evaluate the deuteron magnetic moment as the first numerical test.
