# Photo- and electro-production of medium mass Lambda-hypernuclei

> The characteristic and selective nature of the electro-magnetic production of Lambda-hypernuclei in exciting states is demonstrated assuming the medium-mass targets 28Si, 40Ca, and 52Cr. Formalism of DWIA is used adopting the Saclay-Lyon, Kaon-MAID, Adelseck-Saghai, and Williams-Ji-Cotanch models for the elementary amplitudes and various nuclear and hypernuclear wave functions. The elementary amplitudes are discussed in detail presenting their basic properties and comparison with data. The unique features of the electro-magnetic production of Lambda-hypernuclei shown on examples are the slective excitation of unnatural parity highest-spin states (natural parity ones for the LS-closed targets) and a possibilty to investigate the Lambda single-particle energies including a spin-orbit splitting using variety of medium-mass targets.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that photo- and electro-production of Lambda-hypernuclei on medium-mass targets (28Si, 40Ca, 52Cr) selectively excite unnatural parity highest-spin states (or natural parity ones for LS-closed targets), a feature not available in the more established (K-,pi-) or (pi+,K+) reactions, provided the elementary amplitudes are described by one of several isobaric models (Saclay-Lyon, Kaon-MAID, Adelseck-Saghai, Williams-Ji-Cotanch) and the momentum transfer is about 350 MeV/c.

## Method

The authors used the Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation (DWIA) formalism to calculate cross sections for the (gamma,K+) and (e,e'K+) reactions on 28Si, 40Ca, and 52Cr targets at a photon laboratory energy of 1.3 GeV and a kaon laboratory angle of 3 degrees. They employed four different isobaric models for the elementary gamma p -> Lambda K+ amplitude (Saclay-Lyon A, Kaon-MAID, Adelseck-Saghai AS1, and Williams-Ji-Cotanch C4) and used both simplified single-j configurations and full (sd)^n shell-model wave functions for the nuclear and hypernuclear states, including distortion effects for the incoming photon and outgoing kaon.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer to the Lambda hyperon in the (gamma,K+) reaction at P_gamma = 1.3 GeV/c: 353 MeV/c at theta_K^L = 0 degrees and 425 MeV/c at theta_K^L = 10 degrees.
- For the n(pi+,K+)Lambda reaction at P_pi = 1.05 GeV/c: momentum transfer 406 MeV/c at theta^L = 0 degrees and 447 MeV/c at theta^L = 10 degrees.
- At E_gamma^L = 1.3 GeV and theta_K^L = 3 degrees, the Saclay-Lyon A model gives a 40% larger elementary cross section than the Kaon-MAID model.
- At theta_K^L = 3 degrees and E_gamma^L = 1.3 GeV, the uncertainty between the Kaon-MAID and Williams-Ji-Cotanch models for the elementary forward-angle cross section can amount to 80%.
- For the 28Si(gamma,K+)28_Lambda Al reaction, use of the full (sd)^n shell-model wave functions reduces the cross sections of the major peaks by a factor of about 0.65 compared to the single-j (0d5/2)^6 estimate.
- The Kaon-MAID amplitude gives approximately 30% smaller hypernuclear cross sections than the Saclay-Lyon A values at theta_K^L = 3 degrees for 28Si.
- Assumed proton-hole widths for the 28Si target: Gamma_p(0s1/2^-1) = 10 MeV, Gamma_p(0p3/2^-1) = 6 MeV, Gamma_p(0p1/2^-1) = 3 MeV, Gamma_p(0d5/2^-1) = 0 MeV.
- Assumed Lambda bound-state width: Gamma_Lambda(j) = 0.3 MeV for bound states, 1.0 MeV for 0 < E_Lambda < 2 MeV, and 3 MeV for E_Lambda > 2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier photo-production predictions for light p-shell targets (7Li, 10B, 12C, 16O) by Motoba, Sotona, and collaborators (1990s) to medium-mass nuclei, motivated by the successful first experiment on 12C at Jefferson Lab (JLab) which confirmed the theoretical predictions. The paper contrasts the electromagnetic production mechanism with the established (K-,pi-) and (pi+,K+) reactions (reviewed by Hashimoto and Tamura (2006) and Bando, Motoba, and Zofka (1990)), noting that the strong spin-flip and large momentum transfer in photo-production allow selective excitation of unnatural parity high-spin states and provide access to proton-deficient hypernuclei not reachable by other reactions. The results enable future experimental programs at JLab (CEBAF proposals) to measure Lambda single-particle energies and spin-orbit splittings in medium-mass hypernuclei with an expected energy resolution of about 400 keV.
