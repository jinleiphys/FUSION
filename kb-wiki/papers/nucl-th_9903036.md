# Model Calculations for the Two-Fragment Electro-Disintegration of $^4$He

> Differential cross sections for the electro-disintegration process $e + {^4He} \longrightarrow {^3H}+ p + e'$ are calculated, using a model in which the final state interaction is included by means of a nucleon-nucleus (3+1) potential constructed via Marchenko inversion. The required bound-state wave functions are calculated within the integrodifferential equation approach (IDEA). In our model the important condition that the initial bound state and the final scattering state are orthogonal is fulfilled. The sensitivity of the cross section to the input $p{^3H}$ interaction in certain kinematical regions is investigated. The approach adopted could be useful in reactions involving few cluster systems where effective interactions are not well known and exact methods are presently unavailable. Although, our Plane-Wave Impulse Approximation results exhibit, similarly to other calculations, a dip in the five-fold differential cross-section around a missing momentum of $\sim 450 MeV/c$, it is argued that this is an artifact of the omission of re-scattering four-nucleon processes.

## Key claim

The authors calculate differential cross sections for the electro-disintegration of $^4$He into $^3$H + p using a model where final state interaction is included via a nucleon-nucleus potential constructed by Marchenko inversion, but they argue that the dip in the Plane-Wave Impulse Approximation cross section around a missing momentum of approximately 450 MeV/c is an artifact of omitting re-scattering four-nucleon processes, not necessarily a manifestation of strong final state interaction effects or meson exchange currents.

## Method

The authors used the integrodifferential equation approach (IDEA) with the Malfliet-Tjon MT I-III potential to calculate the three- and four-nucleon bound state wave functions. For the final scattering state, they replaced the plane wave by scattering states generated from a p-$^3$H potential obtained via the Marchenko inverse scattering procedure, using phase shifts extrapolated from the optical potential of van Oers et al. (1982). The transition matrix elements were evaluated by solving a Lippmann-Schwinger-type equation in momentum space, and the cross section was computed using the McVoy and van Hove Hamiltonian for the electron-nucleon interaction.

## Key numbers

- The dip in the PWIA five-fold differential cross section occurs around a missing momentum of approximately 450 MeV/c
- For kinematics R: incident electron energy $E_i = 524.9$ MeV, scattered electron energy $E_f = 423.9$ MeV, scattering angle $\theta = 49.60^\circ$, momentum transfer $q = 408$ MeV/c
- For kinematics (A,B,C,D): $E_i = 525.0$ MeV, $E_f = 310.0$ MeV, $\theta = 49.60^\circ$, $q = 401$ MeV/c
- For kinematics S: $E_i = 560.0$ MeV, $E_f = 360.0$ MeV, $\theta = 25.00^\circ$, $q = 278$ MeV/c
- The Marchenko potentials are phase equivalent up to approximately 300 MeV
- At missing momenta below 300 MeV/c, all calculations show good agreement with experimental data
- The phase shifts from the optical potential become negative for energies greater than 200 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of three-nucleon electro-disintegration by Griffy and Oakes (1964), Lehman (1969, 1971), and Heimbach et al. (1977) to the four-nucleon system, where rigorous four-body calculations beyond breakup threshold are not yet available. The model is similar in spirit to the approach of Schiavilla (1990) using the Variational Monte Carlo method with the Argonne and Urbana interactions, and to the diagrammatic method of Laget (1994). The results enable future investigations using the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas formalism to include the 2+2 channel, which the authors suggest should remove the apparent zero in the PWIA cross section.
