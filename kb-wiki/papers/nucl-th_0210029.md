# Threshold open charm production in nucleon-nucleon collisions

> The associative charm particles production in nucleon-nucleon collisions $N+N\to Λ_c(Σ_c)+\bar{D}+N$, is described in a general way and the spin and isospin structure of the corresponding matrix elements are derived. Using an analogy with strange particle ($ΛK$)-production, the $D-$meson exchange model is considered in detail. Estimations of the energy behavior of the threshold cross sections show a large dependence on the form of the phenomenological hadron form factors and indicate that, at threshold, the cross section is three order of magnitude smaller than for strange particle production.

# Threshold open charm production in nucleon-nucleon collisions

## Key claim

The cross section for associative charm production in nucleon-nucleon collisions near threshold is estimated to be three orders of magnitude smaller than for strange particle production, but this result depends strongly on the choice of phenomenological hadron form factors and on the ratio of D-meson to K-meson scattering lengths, which are not experimentally determined.

## Method

The authors derive the spin and isospin structure of the threshold matrix elements for the reactions N+N → Λc(Σc) + D̅ + N using symmetry properties of the strong interaction and the Pauli principle. They then model the reaction via D-meson exchange in the t-channel, in analogy with K-exchange for strange particle production, and compute the threshold cross sections using the D̅N elastic scattering amplitudes and SU4-symmetry relations for coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of total cross sections for Λc+D̅0 production versus ΛK+ production in pp collisions at the same energy excess Q is estimated as: σ(pp→Λc+D̅0p)/σ(pp→ΛK+p) = 3×10⁻² × [a₁(DN)/a₁(KN)]², assuming SU4-symmetry for coupling constants
- The predicted energy dependence for the open charm cross section is: σ(pp→Λc+D̅0p) ≈ 0.2 (Q/0.1 GeV)² nb
- At the lowest measured energy (Ep=70 GeV, √s=11.46 GeV, Q=6 GeV), the model extrapolates to σ ≈ 0.7 μb, compared to the experimental value σ(pp→charm) = 1.6⁺¹·¹₋₀·₇ (stat) ± 0.3 (syst) μb from Amaglobeli et al. (2001)
- The ratio of threshold matrix elements for D-exchange versus K-exchange is: R_DK = 0.11 × (g_pΛc+D̅0/g_pΛK+)² × [a₁(DN)/a₁(KN)]²
- The suppression factor from monopole hadronic form factors with cutoff λ=1.5 GeV is D_F ≈ 0.08
- The ratio of cross sections for np versus pp collisions in the D-exchange model is: R_D = (1/4)(1 + a₀²/3a₁²) > 1/4

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed for strange particle production in nucleon-nucleon collisions, particularly the K-exchange model used by Moskal et al. (2002) and the spin-isospin analysis by Rekalo, Arvieux, and Tomasi-Gustafsson (1997). It provides a model-independent framework for threshold open charm production that can be tested at future facilities such as those planned at GSI. The results enable predictions for the elementary cross sections needed as input for simulations of charm dynamics in nucleus-nucleus collisions, which Cassing et al. (2001) identified as mandatory for reliable conclusions about open charm in heavy-ion reactions.
