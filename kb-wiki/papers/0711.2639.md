# Strangeness in Relativistic Astrophysics

> In these lecture notes, the role of strangeness in relativistic astrophysics of compact stars is addressed. The appearance of strange particles, as hyperons, kaons, and strange quarks, in the core of compact stars is examined and common features as well as differences are presented. Impacts on the global properties of compact stars and signals of the presence of exotic matter are outlined for the various strange phases which can appear in the interior at high densities.

## Key claim

These lecture notes argue that the appearance of strange particles (hyperons, kaons, and strange quarks) in the core of compact stars is a generic feature of dense matter, but the quantitative impact on stellar properties depends strongly on poorly constrained interactions such as the hyperon-hyperon attraction and the kaon optical potential in dense nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral SU(3)×SU(3) effective Lagrangian for baryonic matter, treating hyperons as part of the baryon octet with coupling constants fixed by SU(3) symmetry relations and hypernuclear data. For kaon condensation, they adopt a relativistic mean-field model with Yukawa couplings of kaons to scalar and vector fields, using the kaon optical potential at saturation density as a control parameter. For quark matter, they employ perturbative QCD with corrections from one-gluon exchange and the MIT bag model, solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations for each scenario.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter properties from the chiral model: binding energy E_B/A = -16 MeV at n_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}, effective mass m*_N/m_N = 0.61, compression modulus K = 276 MeV, asymmetry term a_sym = 40.4 MeV
- Hyperons appear in neutron star matter at about 2n_0 (twice normal nuclear matter density), with fractions reaching around 10% at larger densities
- Maximum mass with nucleons and leptons only: 1.84 M_sun (chiral model) and 2.16 M_sun (RMF model); with hyperons included: 1.52 M_sun (chiral) and 1.55 M_sun (RMF)
- Kaon condensation sets in at 3n_0 for a kaon optical potential of U_K = -120 MeV at n_0
- For U_K = -130 MeV, kaon-condensed stars can reach radii well below 10 km

## Context

These lecture notes extend the line of work on strange matter in compact stars initiated by Glendenning (1984) and Kaplan & Nelson (1986), and build upon the textbook treatments by Glendenning, Weber, and Haensel. The chiral SU(3) model connects hypernuclear data from experiments on hypernuclei to neutron star structure, while the discussion of kaon condensation follows the field-theoretical approach of Glendenning (1997, 1998). The work enables future comparisons between different exotic phases (hyperons, kaon condensates, quark matter) in the mass-radius diagram and highlights how future observations of neutron star masses and radii could distinguish between these scenarios.
