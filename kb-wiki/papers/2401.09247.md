# Hydrodynamical duals of the gravitational axial anomaly and the cosmological constant

> We construct the hydrodynamic expansion for a rotating and accelerated medium in a curved space-time, and establish a duality between the currents related to the cosmological constant and the acceleration. Then we consider the more general case with a non-zero Weyl tensor, and show the relationship between the current in flat space-time and the gravitational axial anomaly. This generalizes the previous derivation to the case with a non-zero Ricci tensor.

## Key claim

The paper extends the derivation of the kinematic vortical effect (KVE), a third-order gradient expansion term in the axial current linking vorticity and acceleration, from Ricci-flat spacetimes to the more general case where the Ricci tensor is proportional to the cosmological constant, showing that the key relation between the transport coefficients and the gravitational axial anomaly remains valid even when the Ricci tensor is nonzero.

## Method

The authors construct a hydrodynamic gradient expansion for the axial current of an uncharged, non-dissipative fluid of massless fermions in global thermodynamic equilibrium, using the beta-frame where the inverse temperature vector satisfies the Killing equation. They first analyze the case of vanishing Weyl tensor (de Sitter or anti-de Sitter spacetime) and then the general case of Einstein manifolds with nonzero Weyl tensor, imposing the condition \(R_{\mu\nu} = \Lambda g_{\mu\nu}\). By computing the covariant divergence of the third-order axial current and equating it to the gravitational chiral anomaly expressed in terms of the Weyl tensor decomposition, they derive a system of differential equations for the transport coefficients, which they solve in terms of dimensionless parameters.

## Key numbers

* For massless Dirac fields, the gravitational chiral anomaly coefficient is \(\mathcal{N} = 1/(384 \pi^2)\).
* For massless Dirac fields, the flat-space transport coefficient \(\lambda_1 = -1/(24 \pi^2)\).
* The relation between the cosmological constant term and the acceleration term is \(\lambda_{\Lambda} = -\lambda_2 / 3\).
* The general relation linking the transport coefficients to the anomaly is \(\lambda_1 - \lambda_2 = 32 \mathcal{N}\).
* For the Rarita-Schwinger-Adler model (spin 3/2 plus two spin 1/2 modes), the gravitational chiral anomaly is \(\mathcal{N} = -19/(384 \pi^2)\).

## Context

This work generalizes the derivation of the kinematic vortical effect from Prokhorov et al. (2022), which was restricted to Ricci-flat spacetimes (\(R_{\mu\nu}=0\)), to the case of Einstein manifolds with a cosmological constant. It also extends the analysis of Khakimov et al. (2023) on the stress-energy tensor in curved spacetime with zero Weyl tensor to the axial current. The results enable the complete determination of the third-order axial current in curved spacetime from a single flat-space transport coefficient and the gravitational anomaly, and they confirm a duality between the cosmological constant and acceleration terms that was previously found in the simpler case.
