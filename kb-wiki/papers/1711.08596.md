# Proxy-SU(3): A symmetry for heavy nuclei

> The SU(3) symmetry realized by J. P. Elliott in the sd nuclear shell is destroyed in heavier shells by the strong spin-orbit interaction. On the other hand, the SU(3) symmetry has been used for the description of heavy nuclei in terms of bosons in the framework of the Interacting Boson Approximation, as well as in terms of fermions using the pseudo-SU(3) approximation. A new fermionic approximation, called the proxy-SU(3), has been recently introduced and applied to the even rare earths. We show that the applicability of proxy-SU(3) can be extended to even nuclei in the 28-50 proton shell, to even superheavy elements, as well as to odd-odd and odd rare earths. Parameter free predictions for the beta and gamma deformation parameters are presented and compared to alternative theoretical predictions and to existing data.

## Key claim

The proxy-SU(3) fermionic symmetry, previously applied only to even rare earths, is shown to be extendable to even nuclei in the 28-50 proton shell, even superheavy elements, and odd-odd and odd rare earths, with parameter-free predictions for beta and gamma deformation parameters that generally agree with alternative calculations and data, though deviations occur near the Z=40 subshell closure.

## Method

The authors apply the proxy-SU(3) scheme, which approximates the full shell model spaces by replacing orbitals with their proxy counterparts to recover SU(3) symmetry broken by the spin-orbit interaction, to compute deformation parameters beta and gamma without free parameters. They compare these predictions to results from D1S Gogny calculations, Relativistic Mean Field with NL3 parametrization, the FRDM(2012) mass tables, covariant density functional theory, and a microscopic-macroscopic method, as well as to experimental data from Raman (2001).

## Key numbers

- For even nuclei in the 28-50 proton shell, proxy-SU(3) predicts gamma values near 30 degrees around N=74, indicating triaxial shapes, and approaching 60 degrees (oblate shapes) in Ru and Pd isotopes.
- For odd-odd rare earths, largest deviations from FRDM(2012) predictions appear for Au (Z=79), near the end of the 50-82 proton shell.
- For odd-N rare earths, highest deviations appear near the end of the 50-82 proton shell at Pt (Z=78) isotopes.
- For superheavy elements, results are shown for Z from 100 to 114 and N from 128 to 220, compared to D1S Gogny, covariant density functional theory (PC-PK1 and DD-PC1 functionals), MMM, and FRDM(2012) predictions.

## Context

This work extends the proxy-SU(3) scheme introduced by Bonatsos and collaborators (2015, 2017) from even rare earths to additional nuclear regions, including the 28-50 proton shell where shape coexistence around Z=40 is known from Wood and Heyde (1999). The parameter-free predictions enable comparison with multiple theoretical approaches (Gogny D1S, Relativistic Mean Field, FRDM) and provide a symmetry-based framework for understanding deformation in heavy and superheavy nuclei, with open questions remaining near subshell closures and at the ends of neutron shells.
