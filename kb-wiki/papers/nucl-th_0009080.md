# What can we learn from Nuclear Matter Instabilities?

> We discuss the features of instabilities in binary systems, in particular, for asymmetric nuclear matter. We show its relevance for the interpretation of results obtained in experiments and in "ab initio" simulations of the reaction between $^{124}Sn+^{124}Sn$ at 50AMeV.}

# What can we learn from Nuclear Matter Instabilities?

## Key claim

The paper argues that studying instabilities in asymmetric (two-component) nuclear matter reveals that chemical instabilities are triggered by isoscalar-like density fluctuations (protons and neutrons fluctuating in phase) rather than isovector-like fluctuations, due to the attractive interaction between protons and neutrons. This dynamical behavior is shown to be relevant for interpreting multifragmentation in heavy-ion collisions, but the authors emphasize that the time scales of the process are of the order of or shorter than relaxation times, so equilibrium phase-transition approaches alone are insufficient.

## Method

The authors analyze the spinodal border of asymmetric nuclear matter using Landau theory for two-component Fermi liquids, solving two coupled Landau-Vlasov equations for protons and neutrons to determine stability conditions. They then perform "ab initio" simulations using the Stochastic Iso-BNV code, a microscopic transport equation that includes asymmetry effects and fluctuation dynamics, for collisions of $^{124}Sn+^{124}Sn$ at 50AMeV with varying impact parameters (b=2fm, 4fm, 6fm). The simulations track the time evolution of density, mass in liquid and gas phases, asymmetry, and fragment multiplicity, averaging over 100 events per impact parameter.

## Key numbers

- The spinodal lines are computed for three asymmetries: I=(N-Z)/(N+Z)=0.0, 0.5, and 0.8 of nuclear matter.
- The reaction studied is $^{124}Sn+^{124}Sn$ at 50AMeV, with impact parameters b=2fm (semi-central), b=4fm, and b=6fm (semi-peripheral).
- The "liquid" phase is defined as clusters with Z >= 3; intermediate mass fragments (IMFs) are defined as 3 <= Z <= 12.
- The "central" region for analysis is defined as a cubic box of side 20fm around the center of mass.
- Freeze-out times are observed to decrease with increasing impact parameter.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of liquid-gas phase transitions in nuclear matter (Pethick 1987, 1988, 1993; Colonna 1994, 1998) by focusing on the binary (two-component) character of asymmetric nuclear matter and its dynamical evolution. The authors connect their findings to experimental data measured at NSCL-MSU for Sn+Sn reactions (referred to as "betty" in the text). The results enable future studies of the symmetry term of the equation of state (EOS) under laboratory conditions, with implications for astrophysical systems, by showing that isospin dynamics in fragment formation is sensitive to the stiffness of the asymmetry term.
