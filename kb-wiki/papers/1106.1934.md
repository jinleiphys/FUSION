# A momentum-space Argonne V18 interaction

> This paper gives a momentum-space representation of the Argonne V18 potential as an expansion in products of spin-isospin operators with scalar coefficient functions of the momentum transfer. Two representations of the scalar coefficient functions for the strong part of the interaction are given. One is as an expansion in an orthonormal basis of rational functions and the other as an expansion in Chebyshev polynomials on different intervals. Both provide practical and efficient representations for computing the momentum-space potential that do not require integration or interpolation. Programs based on both expansions are available as supplementary material. Analytic expressions are given for the scalar coefficient functions of the Fourier transform of the electromagnetic part of the Argonne V18. A simple method for computing the partial-wave projections of these interactions from the operator expressions is also given.

# A momentum-space Argonne V18 interaction

## Key claim
The paper provides two practical analytic approximations (Chebyshev polynomial expansions and rational function expansions) for the momentum-space representation of the Argonne V18 potential that avoid integration or interpolation when computing the potential for any pair of initial and final momenta. The Chebyshev expansion is preferred for precision calculations, while the rational function expansion is less accurate, particularly at higher momentum transfers.

## Method
The authors Fourier-transform the 18-term configuration-space Argonne V18 potential, which yields 24 momentum-space scalar coefficient functions of the momentum transfer q (the extra six arise from the L^2 and (L·S)^2 operators splitting into two distinct momentum-space contributions each). They compute these coefficient functions numerically using Gauss-Legendre quadrature between zeros of spherical Bessel functions, then approximate them by two expansions: (1) Chebyshev polynomials on three intervals (0-10, 10-50, 50-100 fm^-1), with additional sub-intervals for potentials V4, V6, and V17; (2) expansions in orthonormal rational basis functions derived from associated Laguerre polynomials in configuration space with scale parameter Λ = 7 fm^-1, using 70 expansion coefficients per potential. The electromagnetic part of the Argonne V18 is treated separately with analytic Fourier transforms.

## Key numbers
- The radial integrals are evaluated with a finite cutoff at 20 fm; momentum-space potentials are computed for momentum transfers q < 100 fm^-1.
- The Chebyshev expansion uses 101 coefficients per interval on three intervals [0,10], [10,50], [50,100] fm^-1; for V4, additional sub-intervals use 21, 31, 41, and 71 polynomials; for V6, 31, 41, and 41 polynomials; for V17, 31, 51, and 51 polynomials.
- The rational basis expansion uses 70 expansion coefficients per potential, with scale parameter Λ = 7 fm^-1.
- At q = 1 fm^-1, the Chebyshev expansion agrees with the direct numerical Fourier transform to between 5-7 significant figures for all 24 potentials; at q = 5 fm^-1, similar agreement holds.
- At q = 15 fm^-1, potential 17 (the isotensor tensor term) has magnitude about -3 × 10^-10 MeV fm^5, several orders of magnitude smaller than other potentials at that momentum transfer.
- Deuteron binding energy and wave functions computed with both expansions agree with the configuration-space partial-wave calculation to the sixth significant figure; for example, the s-wave at k = 0.0 fm^-1 is 1.2695 × 10^1 (Chebyshev and rational) versus 1.2693 × 10^1 (partial-wave).

## Context
This work extends the Argonne V18 potential (Wiringa et al., 1995), originally formulated in configuration space for variational Monte Carlo calculations, into a momentum-space representation suitable for Faddeev calculations, relativistic few-body calculations, and calculations involving electromagnetic probes. Previous momentum-space applications of the V18 potential (Huber et al., 1995) required interpolation or separate Fourier transforms for each pair of momenta. The analytic expansions provided here enable direct computation of the momentum-space interaction for any pair of initial and final momenta, and the supplementary programs (chebyshev-argonne.c and rational-argonne.c) make the potential readily usable in momentum-space nuclear structure and reaction calculations.
