# Spatial correlation of a particle-hole pair with a repulsive isovector interaction

> We study the spatial correlation of a particle-hole pair in the isovector channel in $^{56}$Co and $^{40}$K nuclei. To this end, we employ the Hartree-Fock+Tamm-Dancoff approximation with the Skyrme interaction. We find a large concentration of the two-body density at positions where the neutron particle and the proton hole states locate on the opposite side to each other with respect to the core nucleus. This feature originates from a repulsive nature of the isovector residual interaction, which is in stark contrast to the dineutron correlation with an attractive pairing interaction between the valence neutrons discussed e.g., in $^{11}$Li and $^6$He. A possible experimental implication of the repulsive correlation is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a repulsive isovector particle-hole interaction produces a strong anti-correlation in the spatial distribution of a neutron particle and a proton hole, analogous to the Coulomb hole in atomic physics, but the authors note that this feature had been qualitatively argued earlier (B67) without being demonstrated in actual numerical calculations until now.

## Method

The authors employ the Hartree-Fock (HF) plus Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (TDA) with the Skyrme SIII interaction to construct wave functions for neutron-particle proton-hole states in ⁵⁶Co (with ⁵⁶Ni core) and ⁴⁰K (with ⁴⁰Ca core). They diagonalize the many-body Hamiltonian in a basis of 1p-1h configurations, using only the t₀ and t₃ terms of the Skyrme residual interaction for the TDA calculations, and discretize the continuum for neutron particle states with a box boundary condition at 15 fm, truncating at εₚ = 40 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For ⁵⁶Co, the 4₁⁺ state wave function is 96.4% (2p₃/₂)ₙ (1f₇/₂)ₚ⁻¹ configuration, and the 2₁⁺ state is 95.5% the same configuration.
- For ⁴⁰K, the 3⁻ state wave function consists of 81.8% (1f₇/₂)ₙ (1d₃/₂)ₚ⁻¹, 17.9% (1f₇/₂)ₙ (2s₁/₂)ₚ⁻¹, and 0.112% (1f₇/₂)ₙ (1d₅/₂)ₚ⁻¹ configurations.
- The TDA calculation yields the 3⁻ state in ⁴⁰K at +0.052 MeV above the ⁴⁰Ca ground state after correcting the neutron-proton mass difference; the empirical energy is -1.28 MeV, while the TDA energy before the mass difference correction is -1.24 MeV.
- For the two-proton-hole nucleus ⁵⁴Fe, the pairing interaction strength is adjusted to g = 262 MeV fm³ to reproduce the empirical two-proton separation energy S₂ₚ = 12.23 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of spatial correlations in nuclei from the well-known dineutron correlation in neutron-rich nuclei like ¹¹Li and ⁶He (Hagino and Sagawa 2005; Zhukov 1993) to the repulsive isovector channel, providing the first numerical demonstration of an anti-correlation analogous to the Coulomb hole in atomic physics (Moiseiwitsch 1963; Roothaan and Bagus 1979). The results enable future calculations of deuteron transfer reaction cross sections using coupled-reaction-channel methods or second-order distorted wave Born approximation, which could test the qualitative argument of B67 that ground-state-to-ground-state deuteron transfer is suppressed by this repulsive correlation.
