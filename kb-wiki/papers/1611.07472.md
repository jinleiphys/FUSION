# QCD, with Strings Attached

> In the nearly twenty years that have elapsed since its discovery, the gauge-gravity correspondence has become established as an efficient tool to explore the physics of a large class of strongly-coupled field theories. A brief overview is given here of its formulation and a few of its applications, emphasizing attempts to emulate aspects of the strong-coupling regime of quantum chromodynamics (QCD). To the extent possible, the presentation is self-contained, and in particular, it does not presuppose knowledge of string theory.

## Key claim

The gauge-gravity (AdS/CFT) correspondence provides a geometric implementation of the renormalization group that translates strongly-coupled field theory problems into tractable gravitational calculations, but holographists do not claim to have solved QCD itself; the theories accessible via this duality are distant cousins or toy models of real-world theories, with clear limitations that are partly evolving and partly rigid.

## Method

The paper presents a pedagogical overview of the holographic correspondence, starting from the idea that the energy scale of a field theory becomes an extra spatial coordinate in a higher-dimensional gravitational spacetime. It explains how conformal field theories map to anti-de Sitter (AdS) spacetime, how generic field theories correspond to asymptotically AdS geometries with nontrivial radial dependence, and how non-Abelian gauge theories with large 't Hooft coupling and large number of colors N_c admit a classical string theory description. The concrete example discussed is the duality between maximally supersymmetric Yang-Mills (MSYM) theory with gauge group SU(N_c) and a string theory on AdS_5.

## Key numbers

- The AdS metric is given by ds^2 = (L^2/z^2)(-dt^2 + d\vec{x}^2 + dz^2), where L is the AdS radius of curvature and z is the radial coordinate (z=0 corresponds to the UV boundary)
- The conformal group for 3+1 dimensional CFTs is SO(4,2), matching the isometry group of AdS_5
- The 't Hooft coupling is defined as lambda = g_YM^2 N_c, where g_YM is the Yang-Mills coupling and N_c is the number of colors
- The string coupling g_s controls the perturbative expansion of string theory, with brane tensions proportional to 1/g_s for D-branes and 1/g_s^2 for other solitonic branes
- The string length l_s sets the mass scale for the infinite tower of string modes, with masses proportional to 1/l_s

## Context

This review extends the pedagogical lineage of earlier overviews of the gauge-gravity correspondence (Maldacena 1998, Gubser-Klebanov-Polyakov 1998, Witten 1998) and situates holography as complementary to lattice QCD, which is the method of choice for first-principle calculations in strongly-coupled QCD but struggles with time dependence and finite density. The paper enables newcomers to access the vast literature of over 12,000 papers on holographic duality, citing textbooks by Nastase, Erdmenger, Mateos, and Zaanen as entry points, while noting that lattice calculations have helped verify some holographic predictions for various gauge theories.
