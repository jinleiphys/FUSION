# Non-empirical nuclear energy functionals, pairing gaps and odd-even mass differences

> First, we briefly outline some aspects of the starting project to design non-empirical energy functionals based on low-momentum vacuum interactions and many-body perturbation theory. Second, we present results obtained within an approximation of such a scheme where the pairing part of the energy density functional is constructed at first order in the nuclear plus Coulomb two-body interaction. We discuss in detail the physics of the odd-even mass staggering and the necessity to compute actual odd-even mass differences to analyze it meaningfully.

## Key claim

The paper outlines a non-empirical approach to constructing nuclear energy density functionals from low-momentum vacuum interactions and many-body perturbation theory, but the results presented are limited to an approximation where the pairing part is built only at first order in the nuclear plus Coulomb two-body interaction, meaning the scheme is not yet a complete non-empirical functional.

## Method

The authors construct the pairing part of the energy density functional at first order in the two-body interaction (nuclear plus Coulomb) within a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework. They compute actual odd-even mass differences rather than using the standard three-point formula for pairing gaps, and analyze the physics of odd-even mass staggering in finite nuclei. The functional form is expressed as an orbital-dependent energy functional with explicit dependence on the one-body density matrix, pairing tensor, and quasiparticle energies.

## Key numbers

- The effective vertices at second order in the NN interaction are given by expressions such as $\bar{v}^{\rho\rho\rho\rho}_{ijklijkl} \equiv 6 \, |\bar{v}^{NN}_{ijkl}|^2 / (\epsilon_{i} + \epsilon_{j} - \epsilon_{k} - \epsilon_{l})$, where $\epsilon_i$ are single-particle energies determined self-consistently.
- The effective two-body vertex $\bar{v}^{\rho\rho}_{ijij}$ is expressed as $\bar{v}^{NN}_{ijij} + \frac{1}{6} \sum_{k} \bar{v}^{\rho\rho\rho}_{ijkijk}$, showing the perturbative renormalization from three-body contributions.
- The pairing field $\Delta_{ij}$ is defined as $\frac{1}{2} \sum_{kl} \overline{v}^{pp}_{ij kl} \kappa_{kl}$, where $\overline{v}^{pp}$ is an effective vertex with its own diagrammatic expansion.

## Context

This work extends the program initiated by Duguet (2006) to build non-empirical energy functionals from low-momentum interactions, contrasting with empirical parameterizations such as Skyrme or Gogny functionals reviewed by Bender et al. (2003). The approach bridges with ab-initio many-body techniques applicable to light nuclei and aims to enable controlled calculations with theoretical error bars for heavy and complex nuclei. The paper specifically addresses the need to go beyond the standard three-point formula for pairing gaps, as emphasized in prior work by the authors (Duguet 2004, 2007; Lesinski 2008, 2009).
