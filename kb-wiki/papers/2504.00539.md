# Illustrating the liquid gas transition of nuclear matter in QCD

> We demonstrate that the liquid-gas transition of nuclear matter can be rigorously described with the quantum chromodynamics by combining the quark gap equation and the Faddeev equation of nucleon. Our investigation focuses on this transition at zero temperature and finite chemical potential, revealing a finite difference between the gas and liquid solution of the quark propagator. This difference emerges from the shift of the nucleon pole mass in medium, which is generated in the nucleon channel of the quark gap equation. We prove that such a difference is precisely the contour contribution from the shift of the nucleon pole. The resulting discontinuity manifests as a first-order phase transition and fundamentally determines both the nuclear binding energy and the saturation density. We then derive an analytical relation between the binding energy and the sigma term of the nucleon, yielding a binding energy of $E/A=15.9\,\textrm{MeV}$. Furthermore, by establishing the relation between the nuclear saturation density and the vector charge of nucleon in association with the binding energy, we determine the saturation density to be $n_{\textrm{B}}^{0}=0.15\,\textrm{fm}^{-3}$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the liquid-gas transition of nuclear matter can be rigorously described directly from quantum chromodynamics by combining the quark gap equation with the Faddeev equation of the nucleon, but this demonstration relies on a simplified approximation that treats the contour contribution from the nucleon pole as a constant at its infrared limit, rather than a full numerical solution of the coupled equations.

## Method

The authors combine the Dyson-Schwinger equation (quark gap equation) with the three-body Faddeev equation for the nucleon, explicitly including a nucleon channel in the quark self-energy. At zero temperature and finite chemical potential, they analyze the analytic structure of the quark propagator and show that a singularity from the nucleon propagator triggers a deviation from the vacuum solution, producing a first-order phase transition. They then approximate the contour contribution from the shifted nucleon pole as a constant at infrared momentum and derive analytic relations connecting the binding energy and saturation density to the nucleon scalar and vector charges.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon: \(E/A = 15.9\) MeV, derived from the analytic relation using the nucleon sigma term.
- Saturation density: \(n_{\text{B}}^{0} = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\), determined from the relation involving the vector charge of the nucleon.
- In-medium nucleon pole mass: \(M_N^* = 939 - 15.9\) MeV, giving a transition chemical potential \(\mu_B = M_N^* = 923.1\) MeV.
- The constant contour contribution \(\delta\bar{f} = 0.0164\), computed using the GMOR relation with pion mass \(M_\pi = 135\) MeV, pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92\) MeV, and nucleon sigma term \(\sigma_N = 43.7\) MeV from lattice QCD.
- Nakanishi normalization factor for the nucleon: \(Z = 0.97\).

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger and Faddeev equation framework, previously used for studying nucleon properties in vacuum (Roberts 2007, Eichmann 2012, Qin 2018), to describe the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition directly from QCD. It contrasts with phenomenological approaches such as the relativistic mean field model (Walecka 1974, Serot 1984) and Hartree-Fock methods based on nucleon-nucleon potentials (Haftel 1970, Baldo 1999), which do not start from the fundamental quark-gluon degrees of freedom. The derived analytic relation \(n_B^0 = M_\pi^2 f_\pi^2 / (3\sigma_N)\) provides a new constraint for low-energy effective models of QCD and can be tested against future lattice QCD calculations of the nucleon sigma term.
