# Nuclear Matter Mean Field with Extended NJL Model

> An extended version of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model is applied to describe both nuclear matter and surface properties of finite nuclei. Several parameter sets are discussed and a comparison of the saturation properties and equation of state (EOS) with the NL3 parametrization of the non-linear Walecka model is made. The properties of asymmetric matter are discussed.

# Nuclear Matter Mean Field with Extended NJL Model

## Key claim

The extended NJL (ENJL) model can reproduce nuclear matter saturation properties and surface properties of finite nuclei almost perfectly for bulk static quantities, but it fails for dynamical properties such as scalar excitation masses (which come out at twice the nucleon mass) and the nuclear surface description, requiring replacement by an extended chiral sigma (ECS) model for those aspects.

## Method

The authors extend the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model by adding a vector-isoscalar term \(G_v\) and a density-dependent scalar coupling term \(G_{sv}\) to the Lagrangian, treating nucleons as elementary particles with quark clustering put in by hand via a reduced cutoff. They solve the mean-field gap equation and thermodynamic potential to obtain the equation of state, then compute surface properties using a Thomas-Fermi approximation with gradient terms for the scalar meson field. Two parameter sets (EOSI and EOSII) are explored, and results are compared to the NL3 parametrization of the non-linear Walecka model and to the quark meson coupling (QMC) model.

## Key numbers

- Saturation energy per nucleon \(E/A - M_0 = -16.12\) MeV for EOSI (compared to \(-16.3\) MeV for NL3)
- Saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.148\) fm\(^{-3}\) for EOSI (same as NL3)
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation \(M/M_0 = 0.75\) for EOSI (compared to 0.60 for NL3)
- Incompressibility \(K = 295\) MeV for EOSI (compared to 272 MeV for NL3)
- Surface energy \(W_{surf} = 19.43\) MeV for EOSI (compared to 19.38 MeV for NL3)
- Surface thickness \(t = 2.64\) fm for EOSI (compared to 2.65 fm for NL3)
- Nuclear radius for \(A=100\): \(R = 5.33\) fm for EOSI (compared to 5.328 fm for NL3)
- Coupling constants for EOSI: \(G_s = 3.880\) fm\(^2\), \(G_v = 3.952\) fm\(^2\), \(G_{sv} = -4.901\) fm\(^8\), cutoff \(\Lambda = 418.9\) MeV
- Symmetry energy \(a_{sym} = 35\) MeV (input value)
- Pion decay constant from ECS model: \(F_\pi = 106.1\) MeV (empirical value 93 MeV); from ENJL model: \(F_\pi = 33.49\) MeV
- Critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration at zero density: \(T_c = 196\) MeV
- Volume integral of NN scalar interaction: \(\widetilde{V}_s = -1705.4\) MeV fm\(^3\) (ENJL), compared to \(-1719\) MeV fm\(^3\) in the Guichon QMC model
- Density ratio where nucleon mass vanishes: \(\rho/\rho_0 = 3.0\) for EOSI

## Context

This work extends the NJL model (Nambu and Jona-Lasinio, 1961) to nuclear matter by incorporating density-dependent couplings, following earlier extensions by Koch et al. (1987) and Bentz et al. (2001). It directly compares its EOSI parametrization to the NL3 relativistic mean-field model of Ring (1996) and to the quark meson coupling model of Guichon (1989). The paper shows that the ENJL model's \(G_{sv}\) term plays an analogous role to the confinement modification in Guichon's model, and connects the approach to the derivative coupling model of Zimanyi and Moszkowski (1990) and the hybrid model of Glendenning et al. (1992). The results enable future applications to finite nuclei at finite temperature and to neutron-star matter with strangeness and beta-equilibrium.
