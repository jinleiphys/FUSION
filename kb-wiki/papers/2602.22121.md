# Trade-offs in Gauss's law error correction for lattice gauge theory quantum simulations

> Gauss's law-based quantum error correction (GLQEC) offers a promising approach to reducing qubit overhead in lattice gauge theory simulations by leveraging built-in symmetries. For applications of GLQEC to 1+1D lattice quantum electrodynamics (QED), we identify two significant trade-offs. First, we prove via dimension-counting arguments that GLQEC requires periodic electric fields, thereby constraining the design space for lattice QED simulations. Second, we numerically compare GLQEC with a universal quantum error correction (UQEC) code, specifically the $d=3$ bitflip repetition code, and find that while GLQEC can achieve lower logical error rates in single-round error correction, it exhibits faster decoherence to the steady-state mixed ensemble under multiple rounds. The mixing speed penalty is manifest in observables of interest for both memory experiments and Hamiltonian evolution. We identify a mixing speed threshold, $p_{th}=0.277(2)$, above which using GLQEC exhibits even faster decoherence than without error correction. Our results highlight fundamental limitations of symmetry-based error correction schemes and inform corresponding constraints on formulations of lattice gauge theories compatible with error-robust quantum simulation techniques.

## Key claim

Gauss's law-based quantum error correction (GLQEC) for 1+1D lattice QED requires periodic electric fields to form a valid stabilizer code, and while it achieves lower logical error rates than a universal quantum error correction (UQEC) code in single-round error correction, it exhibits faster decoherence to a steady-state mixed ensemble under multiple rounds, with a mixing speed threshold of p_th = 0.277(2) above which GLQEC causes even faster decoherence than no error correction at all.

## Method

The authors analyzed the RRW protocol for Gauss's law QEC applied to the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian formulation of the massive Schwinger model (1+1D lattice QED) with staggered fermions and binary (qubit) gauge field truncation. They proved via dimension-counting arguments that the dimension of the physical Hilbert space for non-periodic U(1) theories follows Lucas numbers, which cannot be the dimension of a stabilizer code space, establishing incompatibility. They numerically compared GLQEC to a d=3 bitflip repetition code (UQEC) under bitflip noise using single-round memory experiments, multi-round memory experiments, and noisy Hamiltonian evolution, analyzing logical error rates, mixing speeds, and steady-state ensembles.

## Key numbers

* Mixing speed threshold: p_th = 0.277(2), above which GLQEC exhibits faster decoherence than having no error correction at all.
* For single-round error correction under bitflip noise, the UQEC-to-GLQEC logical error rate ratio converges to 1.2 asymptotically at low physical error rates and to 1.0 at high physical error rates.
* The GLQEC code for binary lattice gauge theories with periodic boundary conditions and 2n fermionic sites is a [[4n, 2n, 3:1]] (for n>1) bitflip code, with the Gauss's law checks forming a 2n x 4n binary parity check matrix.

## Context

This work extends the RRW protocol introduced by Rajput, Roggero, and Wiebe (2023) and Spagnoli et al. (2024), who proposed using Gauss's law as a stabilizer code for Z_2 and periodic Lambda=1-truncated U(1) theories. The authors identify fundamental limitations of symmetry-based error correction schemes that were not previously studied, including the incompatibility with non-periodic electric fields and the mixing speed penalty under multiple rounds of error correction. These results inform constraints on formulations of lattice gauge theories compatible with error-robust quantum simulation techniques and highlight trade-offs between qubit savings and error correction performance that must be considered when designing quantum simulations of gauge theories.
