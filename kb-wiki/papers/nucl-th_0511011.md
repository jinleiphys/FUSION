# Can one extract the pi-neutron scattering length from pi-deuteron scattering?

> We give a prove of evidence that the original power counting by Weinberg can be applied to estimate the contributions of the operators contributing to the pi-deuteron scattering length. As a consequence, pi-deuteron observables can be used to extract neutron amplitudes--in case of pi-deuteron scattering this means that the pi-neutron scattering length can be extracted with high accuracy. This result is at variance with recent claims. We discuss the origin of this difference.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that when the one-pion exchange is included non-perturbatively in the deuteron wave function, the logarithmic regulator dependence of the leading few-body correction to the π-deuteron scattering length disappears, contrary to recent claims that a counter term is needed at that order. This supports the original Weinberg power counting and enables high-accuracy extraction of the π-neutron scattering length from π-deuteron data.

## Method

The authors numerically evaluated the static contribution to the leading few-body correction to the π-deuteron scattering length using deuteron wave functions obtained from solving the Schrödinger equation with a leading-order NN potential that includes both one-pion exchange and a contact term. They varied the regulator cutoff Λ from 2 to 20 fm⁻¹ (400–4000 MeV) and compared results using wave functions with non-perturbative pions (Ψ_Λ^π) against wave functions from only contact interactions (Ψ_Λ^{no π}).

## Key numbers

- For wave functions with non-perturbative pions (Ψ_Λ^π), the static three-body contribution was fitted as a constant for Λ ≥ 6 fm⁻¹, yielding a^(static) = 2.12 m_π⁻¹, in good agreement with previous calculations by Baru et al. (2004).
- The cutoff range studied was Λ = 2–20 fm⁻¹ (400–4000 MeV), extending well above the chiral symmetry breaking scale Λ_χ ≈ 1000–1200 MeV.
- For wave functions with only contact interactions (Ψ_Λ^{no π}), the results showed a clear ln(Λ) behavior, consistent with the findings of Grießhammer et al. (2004) and Rusetsky et al. (2005).
- The deuteron binding energy was reproduced to 2.225 MeV for all wave functions, with the asymptotic S-state normalization A_S ranging from 0.839 to 0.866 fm⁻¹/² for Ψ_Λ^π and from 0.69 to 0.76 fm⁻¹/² for Ψ_Λ^{no π}.

## Context

This work addresses a controversy in chiral effective field theory for few-nucleon systems: whether the leading few-body correction to π-deuteron scattering requires a counter term at the same order, as claimed by Grießhammer et al. (2004) and Rusetsky et al. (2005) based on perturbative treatment of one-pion exchange. The authors show that when one-pion exchange is treated non-perturbatively in the wave function, as originally proposed by Weinberg (1990, 1991), the logarithmic divergence disappears and the original power counting holds. This result validates the approach used in previous calculations of reactions such as γd → π⁰d (Beane et al., 2004), π³He → π³He (Baru et al., 2004), π⁻d → γnn (Gardestig et al., 2005), and γd → π⁺nn (Lensky et al., 2005), and enables high-precision extraction of the π-neutron scattering length from π-deuteron data.
