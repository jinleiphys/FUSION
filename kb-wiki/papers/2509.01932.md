# Discrete and continuous spectrum of lightest hypernuclei

> We analyze the peculiarities of the interaction of the lambda hyperon with s-shell nuclei. The spectra of bound and scattering states are studied in the hypernuclei $_Λ^{2}$H, $_Λ^{3}$H, $_Λ^{4}$H, $_Λ^{4}$He and $_Λ^{5}$He, which is considered as two-cluster configurations $p+Λ$, $d$+$Λ$, $^{3}$H+$Λ$, $^{3}$He+$Λ$, $^{4}$He+$Λ$, respectively. The explicit form of the folding potentials of such an interaction is presented in coordinate and oscillator representations, which help us to understand the structure of hypernuclei of interest. We compare energies of bound states, phase shifts of the elastic lambda hyperon, and neutron scattering from s-shell nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the algebraic version of the resonating group method (RGM) can successfully describe both bound and scattering states of the lightest hypernuclei ($_{\Lambda}^{2}$H through $_{\Lambda}^{5}$He) using two-cluster configurations, but the results depend on the chosen nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon potentials and the common oscillator length parameter (b = 1.357 fm) selected to minimize the $^{4}$He + d threshold energy.

## Method

The authors employed the algebraic version of the resonating group method (RGM) to study hypernuclei as two-cluster systems: p+$\Lambda$, d+$\Lambda$, $^{3}$H+$\Lambda$, $^{3}$He+$\Lambda$, and $^{4}$He+$\Lambda$. They used the Hasegawa-Nagata potential (HNP) for nucleon-nucleon interactions and the YNG-NF potential for lambda-nucleon interactions, expanding the relative motion wave functions over harmonic oscillator basis functions to transform the integro-differential equations into systems of linear algebraic equations. Folding potentials were derived analytically in both coordinate and oscillator representations for each hypernuclear system.

## Key numbers

* $_{\Lambda}^{2}$H: resonance state at 4.052 MeV (p+$\Lambda$ channel, $J^{\pi}=1^{+}$)
* $_{\Lambda}^{3}$H: bound state at -0.164 MeV (d+$\Lambda$ channel, $J^{\pi}=1/2^{+}$)
* $_{\Lambda}^{4}$H: bound state at -2.169 MeV (t+$\Lambda$ channel, $J^{\pi}=0^{+}$)
* $_{\Lambda}^{4}$He: bound state at -2.347 MeV ($^{3}$He+$\Lambda$ channel, $J^{\pi}=0^{+}$)
* $_{\Lambda}^{5}$He: bound state at -3.102 MeV ($^{4}$He+$\Lambda$ channel, $J^{\pi}=1/2^{+}$)
* Oscillator length b = 1.357 fm, selected as a common value for all nuclei and hypernuclei to minimize the $^{4}$He+d threshold energy for the HNP potential

## Context

This work extends the algebraic version of the resonating group method, previously applied to $_{\Lambda}^{9}$Be (Nesterov, 2021) and $_{\Lambda}^{4}$H (Nesterov, 2021), to a systematic study of all s-shell hypernuclei. The results are compared with experimental data from the hypernuclear chart (ChartHyperN2021) and with ab initio calculations by Nogga (2002) using four lambda-nucleon potentials with $\Sigma$-N mixing, as well as with chiral effective field theory calculations by Li (2025) that demonstrated the necessity of hyperon-nucleon-nucleon three-body forces. The present work enables future three-cluster studies of heavier hypernuclei such as $_{\Lambda}^{7}$Li in the $^{4}$He+d+$\Lambda$ configuration.
