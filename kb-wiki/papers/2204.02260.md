# Heavy baryons in hot stellar matter with light nuclei and hypernuclei

> The production of light nuclei and hypernuclei together with heavy baryons, both hyperons and $Δ$-baryons, in low density matter as found in stellar environments such as supernova or binary mergers is studied within relativistic mean-field models. Five light nuclei were considered together with three light hypernuclei. The presence of both hyperons and $Δ$-baryons shift the dissolution of clusters to larger densities and increase the abundance of clusters. This effect is larger the smaller the charge fraction and the higher the temperature. The couplings of the $Δ$-baryons were chosen imposing that the nucleon effective mass remains finite inside neutron stars.

# Heavy baryons in hot stellar matter with light nuclei and hypernuclei

## Key claim

The presence of both hyperons and Δ-baryons in low-density stellar matter shifts the dissolution of light clusters to larger densities and increases cluster abundance, with the effect being larger for smaller charge fractions and higher temperatures; however, the Δ-baryon couplings must be constrained so that the nucleon effective mass remains finite inside neutron stars, which eliminates several coupling combinations.

## Method

The authors employed two relativistic mean-field (RMF) models, DD2 (density-dependent couplings) and FSU2H (nonlinear meson terms), to describe warm hadronic matter including nucleons, five light nuclei (²H, ³H, ³He, ⁴He, ⁶He), three light hypernuclei (³_ΛH, ⁴_ΛH, ⁴_ΛHe), the full spin-1/2 baryonic octet, and the spin-3/2 Δ-isobar quadruplet. Clusters were treated as point-like particles with in-medium binding energy shifts following the approach of Pais (2018), with chemical equilibrium imposed at fixed charge fraction and temperature. The Δ-meson couplings were varied within intervals (0.9 ≤ x_σΔ ≤ 1.2, 0.9 ≤ x_ωΔ ≤ 1.2, x_ρΔ = 0.8, 1, 2) and constrained by requiring that the nucleon effective mass remains positive up to the central density of the maximum-mass neutron star.

## Key numbers

- For DD2 with x_σΔ = x_ωΔ = 1, x_ρΔ = 1: maximum mass 2.02 M_⊙, radius at 1.4 M_⊙ is 12.93 km, onset density of Δ⁻ is 0.28 fm⁻³, onset density of Λ is 0.36 fm⁻³.
- For DD2 with x_σΔ = x_ωΔ = 1.2, x_ρΔ = 1: maximum mass 2.05 M_⊙, radius at 1.4 M_⊙ is 12.97 km, onset density of Δ⁻ is 0.27 fm⁻³.
- For FSU2H with x_σΔ = x_ωΔ = 1.2, x_ρΔ = 1: maximum mass 1.98 M_⊙, radius at 1.4 M_⊙ is 12.97 km, onset density of Δ⁻ is 0.26 fm⁻³.
- The nuclear saturation density ρ₀ is 0.15 fm⁻³ for FSU2H and 0.149 fm⁻³ for DD2; binding energy per particle is 16.28 MeV (FSU2H) and 16.02 MeV (DD2).
- For DD2 without Δ-baryons, the baryonic octet gives maximum mass 2.04 M_⊙ and Λ onset at 0.33 fm⁻³.
- The scalar cluster-meson coupling was fixed to x_s = 0.93 for DD2 and x_s = 0.91 for FSU2H.

## Context

This work extends the study of Custódio (2021), which calculated light cluster and hypernuclei abundances with hyperons but without Δ-isobars, by incorporating Δ-baryons into the RMF framework. It builds on the cluster formalism of Pais (2018), which introduced in-medium binding energy shifts calibrated to NIMROD experiment data (Qin 2011), and on the hypernuclei treatment of Custódio (2021). The results enable more complete equations of state for core-collapse supernova and neutron star merger simulations, where temperatures of 50–100 MeV and non-β-equilibrium conditions allow heavy baryons to appear at lower densities than in cold neutron stars.
