# Neutron star structure with nuclear force mediated by hypothetical X17 boson

> A reported ${17~}$MeV boson, which has been proposed as an explanation to the $^{8}$Be and $^{4}$He anomaly, is investigated in the context of its possible influence to neutron stars structure. Implementing a $m_{X}$=17 MeV to the nuclear equation of state using different incompressibility values K$_{0}$=245 MeV and K$_{0}$=260 MeV and solving Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations, we estimate an upper limit of ${M_{TOV}\thickapprox 2.4M\odot}$ for a non rotating neutron star with span in radius ${R}$ between ${11.5~}$km to ${14~}$km. # Neutron star structure with nuclear force mediated by hypothetical X17 boson

## Key claim
If the nuclear force is mediated by a hypothetical 17 MeV boson (the X17 particle proposed to explain anomalies in $^{8}$Be and $^{4}$He), then the maximum mass of a non-rotating neutron star is approximately 2.4 solar masses, with radii between 11.5 km and 14 km, provided the nuclear incompressibility lies between 245 MeV and 260 MeV. The authors note that these limits fit well with observations from the GW190814 merger event, but that rotation could overestimate radii by 3–3.5 km, and that hyperon softening could reduce the maximum mass to about 2 solar masses.

## Method
The authors constructed equations of state for pure neutron matter using relativistic mean field theory, assuming nuclear force mediation by a 17 MeV boson, with scalar and vector couplings constrained by nuclear saturation properties (binding energy −16 MeV, saturation density 0.16 fm$^{-3}$). They implemented cubic and quartic scalar self-interactions to obtain two incompressibility values, K$_0$ = 245 MeV and K$_0$ = 260 MeV, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations using polytropic parameterizations of the resulting equations of state. They also considered cases with a 10% admixture of protons and simulated hyperon softening by reducing the polytropic exponents at high densities.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass of non-rotating neutron star: M$_{TOV}$ ≈ 2.4 M$_\odot$ (for pure neutron matter, both incompressibility values)
- Radius range for pure neutron matter: R = 11.5 km to 14 km
- Incompressibility values used: K$_0$ = 245 MeV and K$_0$ = 260 MeV
- With 10% proton admixture: radius reduced by approximately 3% relative to pure neutron matter
- With simulated hyperon softening: maximum mass reduced to M$_{TOV}$ ≈ 2 M$_\odot$
- Vector boson mass: m$_v$ = 17 MeV; scalar boson mass: m$_s$ = 25.58 MeV (for both parameter sets)
- Couplings for K$_0$ = 245 MeV: g$_v$ = 0.2407, g$_s$ = 0.4666, $\kappa$ = 0.0039 MeV, $\lambda$ = −0.001396
- Couplings for K$_0$ = 260 MeV: g$_v$ = 0.2417, g$_s$ = 0.4684, $\kappa$ = 0.00374 MeV, $\lambda$ = −0.001204
- Transition densities for polytropes: $\rho_1$ = 2.8 × 10$^{14}$ g/cm$^3$, $\rho_2$ = 10$^{14.7}$ g/cm$^3$, $\rho_3$ = 10$^{15}$ g/cm$^3$

## Context
This work extends the investigation of the X17 boson, first reported by Krasznahorkay et al. (2016) in $^{8}$Be and later in $^{4}$He, from nuclear structure to compact astrophysical objects. The authors build on their own prior work (Petousis et al.) and use constraints from the GW170817 merger event (Abbott et al., 2017) and the GW190814 event to test the viability of the X17-mediated nuclear force. The results are compared to neutron star mass-radius constraints from Lattimer, Cromartie, Ozel, Guillot, and the NICER collaboration (Riley et al.), and the hyperon softening analysis references Fortin (2018). The paper enables future tests of the X17 hypothesis through neutron star observations, particularly from the eXTP mission, and suggests that a 2.6 M$_\odot$ neutron star (as possibly observed in GW190814) would be compatible with this model if hyperons are absent.
