# Landau Migdal Theory of Interacting Fermi Systems: A Framework for Effective Theories in Nuclear Structure Physics

> We review Migdal's Theory of Finite Fermi Systems and its application to the structure of nuclei. The theory is an extension of Landau's Theory of Interacting Fermi Systems. In the first part the basic formulas are derived within the many body Green functions approach. The theory is applied to isovector electric giant resonances in medium and heavy mass nuclei. The parameterizations of the enormalized effective ph-interaction and the effective operators are discussed. It is shown that the number of free parameters are restricted due to conservation laws. We also present an extension of Migdal's theory, where the low-lying phonons are considered in a consistent manner. The extended theory is again applied to the same isovector electric giant resonances and to the analysis of $(α,α^\prime)$ reaction data. We point out that the extended theory is the appropriate frame for self consistent nuclear structure calculations starting from effective Lagrangians and Hamiltonians.

# Landau Migdal Theory of Interacting Fermi Systems: A Framework for Effective Theories in Nuclear Structure Physics

## Key claim

The Landau-Migdal Theory of Finite Fermi Systems provides a rigorous, microscopically derived framework for nuclear structure calculations that can accommodate both phenomenological and self-consistent approaches, but its practical implementation requires phenomenological input (quasi-particle energies and renormalized interactions) because first-principles calculations from the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction remain too involved to achieve quantitative agreement without severe approximations.

## Method

The authors derive the basic equations of Migdal's theory using many-body Green functions, starting from the Dyson equation and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the response function in the particle-hole channel. They apply Landau's renormalization procedure to separate the singular (pole) part of the one-particle Green function near the Fermi surface from the remainder, yielding renormalized effective particle-hole interactions and effective one-body operators. The resulting equations are solved in coordinate space (r-space) to compute transition strengths for isovector electric giant resonances in doubly magic nuclei, with the single-particle continuum included via the method of Shlomo and Bertsch (1975).

## Key numbers

- The Fermi momentum is given by p_F = (3π²ℏ³N/V)^(1/3) for an infinite Fermi system.
- The compression modulus K is related to the Landau parameter f_0 by K = 6(ℏ²p_F²/2m*)(1 + 2f_0).
- The effective mass m* is related to the Landau parameter f_1 by m*/m = 1 + (2/3)f_1.
- Numerical results are presented for the giant electric dipole resonance in three doubly magic nuclei: ⁴⁰Ca, ¹³²Sn, and ²⁰⁸Pb.

## Context

This review extends Landau's Theory of Interacting Fermi Systems (Landau and Lifshitz, 1957) to finite nuclei, as originally formulated by Migdal (1967). The work builds on earlier demonstrations by Backman, Jackson and Speth (1975) that Skyrme force parameterizations could be tested against Landau-Migdal parameters, and by Krewald et al. (1988) who calculated these parameters from G-matrices based on meson exchange models. The extended theory (ETFFS) incorporating low-lying phonons provides a framework for self-consistent nuclear structure calculations starting from effective Lagrangians, including chiral Lagrangians (Meissner, 2005), and enables testing of effective interactions against universal Landau-Migdal parameters that connect to bulk nuclear properties such as the compression modulus and symmetry energy.
