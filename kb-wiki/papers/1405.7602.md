# Three-nucleon forces and spectroscopy of neutron-rich calcium isotopes

> We study excited-state properties of neutron-rich calcium isotopes based on chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions. We first discuss the details of our many-body framework, investigate convergence properties, and for two-nucleon interactions benchmark against coupled-cluster calculations. We then focus on the spectroscopy of 47-56Ca, finding that with both 3N forces and an extended pfg9/2 valence space, we obtain a good level of agreement with experiment. We also study electromagnetic transitions and find that experimental data are well described by our calculations. In addition, we provide predictions for unexplored properties of neutron-rich calcium isotopes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that chiral two- and three-nucleon (3N) forces, when combined with an extended pfg9/2 valence space, yield good agreement with experimental spectroscopy of neutron-rich calcium isotopes 47-56Ca, but the agreement is conditional on including both 3N forces and the extended valence space; calculations with only two-nucleon forces or in the smaller pf-shell valence space produce spectra that are too compressed and in poor agreement with experiment.

## Method

The authors construct valence-space Hamiltonians using many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) up to third order, based on chiral effective field theory interactions: a low-momentum N3LO two-nucleon potential evolved via renormalization group to a cutoff of 2.0 fm^-1, and leading N2LO three-nucleon forces with couplings fit to the 3H binding energy and 4He radius. They include normal-ordered one- and two-body parts of 3N forces and perform exact diagonalization in both the pf-shell (0f7/2, 1p3/2, 0f5/2, 1p1/2) and extended pfg9/2 valence spaces on top of a 40Ca core, using the shell model codes ANTOINE and NATHAN.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energies of calcium isotopes calculated with NN+3N forces in the pfg9/2 space agree with experiment for 48-54Ca, with the repulsive effect of normal-ordered 3N forces being evident
- The 2+1 state in 48Ca is overpredicted by approximately 500 keV relative to experiment
- In 47Ca, the lowest 3/2- state is approximately 1 MeV below experiment in the MBPT calculation
- The 2+1 state in 50Ca is overpredicted by approximately 500 keV in all MBPT calculations
- The 2+1 energy in 52Ca is found to be more than 1.5 MeV higher than in neighboring 50Ca, consistent with the N=32 subshell closure
- In 54Ca, the 2+1 energy is approximately 500 keV below that in 52Ca
- The change in ground-state energies from four to five major shells for 3N forces is approximately 10 keV for two-body matrix elements and approximately 50 keV for single-particle energies
- The MBPT ground-state energies are within 5% of coupled-cluster calculations for calcium isotopes up to 55Ca

## Context

This work extends previous valence-space calculations for oxygen isotopes (Holt et al., 2012-2013) and calcium ground-state energies (Gallant et al., 2012; Wienholtz et al., 2013) to excited-state properties of neutron-rich calcium isotopes. It provides microscopic predictions for spectroscopy in the region of the N=32 and N=34 shell closures, complementing phenomenological shell-model calculations with GXPF1A and KB3G interactions, and ab initio coupled-cluster and self-consistent Green's function calculations. The results enable future experimental tests at rare isotope beam facilities and provide predictions for unexplored excited states in 51-56Ca.
