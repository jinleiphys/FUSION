# Highest weight SU(3) irreducible representations for nuclei with shape coexistence

> The SU(3) irreducible representations (irreps) are characterised by the (lambda, mu) Elliott quantum numbers, which are necessary for the extraction of the nuclear deformation, the energy spectrum and the transition probabilities. These irreps can be calculated through a code which requires high computational power. In the following text a hand-writing method is presented for the calculation of the highest weight (h.w.) irreps, using two different sets of magic numbers, namely proxy-SU(3) and three-dimensional isotropic harmonic oscillator.

## Key claim

The paper presents a hand-writing method for calculating the highest weight SU(3) irreducible representation (irrep) for a given nucleus, using either proxy-SU(3) magic numbers or three-dimensional isotropic harmonic oscillator magic numbers, and proposes that the two sets of magic numbers together can describe both the ground state band and the first excited 0+ band of shape coexistence without invoking particle-hole excitations across major shells.

## Method

The method uses Gel'fand-Tsetlin triangles for the U(10) algebra (corresponding to the n=3 harmonic oscillator shell) to determine the occupation of orbitals by valence protons and neutrons. For the highest weight irrep, the first k/2 orbitals (where k is the number of valence particles) are fully occupied with 2 particles each, and the remaining orbitals are empty. The total quanta in each Cartesian axis are summed from the occupied orbitals, arranged in decreasing order to give U(3) quantum numbers [f1,f2,f3], from which the (λ, μ) Elliott quantum numbers are obtained via λ = f1 - f2 and μ = f2 - f3. The total irrep is the sum of the proton and neutron irreps.

## Context

This work extends the proxy-SU(3) symmetry proposed by Bonatsos et al. (2017) and the Elliott SU(3) model (Elliott, 1958) to address shape coexistence, a phenomenon reviewed by Heyde and Wood (2011) and Wood et al. (1992). The method provides an alternative to the traditional particle-hole excitation approach of Heyde et al. (1985), which works well near magic numbers but cannot explain shape coexistence in open shell regions. The paper enables predictions of shape coexistence in open shell regions without requiring particle-hole excitations across major shells, by using the harmonic oscillator magic numbers as a complementary set to the proxy-SU(3) magic numbers.
