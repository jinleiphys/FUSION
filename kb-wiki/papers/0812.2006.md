# Whence the odd-even staggering in nuclear binding?

> We explore the systematics of odd-even mass staggering with a view to identifying the physical mechanisms responsible. The BCS pairing and mean field contributions have A- and number parity dependencies which can help disentangle the different contributions. This motivates the two-term parametrization c_1 + c_2/A as a theoretically based alternative to the inverse power form traditionally used to fit odd-even mass differences. Assuming that the A-dependence of the BCS pairing is weak, we find that mean-field contributions are dominant below mass number A~40 while BCS pairing dominates in heavier nuclei.

# Whence the odd-even staggering in nuclear binding?

## Key claim

The odd-even staggering (OES) of nuclear binding energies can be decomposed into a constant term (attributed to BCS pairing) and a term proportional to 1/A (attributed to mean-field effects including single-particle energy spacing and time-odd orbital interactions), with mean-field contributions dominant below mass number A ~ 40 and BCS pairing dominant in heavier nuclei, assuming the A-dependence of BCS pairing is weak.

## Method

The authors fit experimental odd-even staggering data from the Audi 2003 mass table using three parametrizations: a constant (naive BCS form), a one-term power law c_α A^{-α} with adjustable exponent, and a two-term form c_1 + c_2/A. They analyze four OES measures separately: neutron odd (Δ^{(3)}_{on}), neutron even (Δ^{(3)}_{en}), proton odd (Δ^{(3)}_{op}), and proton even (Δ^{(3)}_{ep}), excluding N=Z nuclei, odd-odd nuclei, and nuclei with binding energy errors above 200 keV.

## Key numbers

- For neutron odd OES (Δ^{(3)}_{on}, 443 data points): constant fit c_0 = 1.04 MeV with rms residual 0.313 MeV; two-term fit gives c_1 = 0.82 MeV, c_2 = 24 MeV with rms 0.270 MeV
- For neutron even OES (Δ^{(3)}_{en}, 442 data points): constant fit c_0 = 1.32 MeV with rms 0.420 MeV; two-term fit gives c_1 = 0.94 MeV, c_2 = 41 MeV with rms 0.308 MeV
- For proton odd OES (Δ^{(3)}_{op}, 418 data points): constant fit c_0 = 0.96 MeV with rms 0.275 MeV; two-term fit gives c_1 = 0.75 MeV, c_2 = 25 MeV with rms 0.231 MeV
- For proton even OES (Δ^{(3)}_{ep}, 407 data points): constant fit c_0 = 1.64 MeV with rms 0.455 MeV; two-term fit gives c_1 = 1.11 MeV, c_2 = 59 MeV with rms 0.270 MeV
- The averaged constant term c_{1+} = 0.88 MeV for neutrons and 0.93 MeV for protons; the dividing point where constant and 1/A terms contribute equally is A ~ 40
- Extracted time-odd interaction matrix elements: v_{i\bar i,i\bar i} ≈ -49 MeV, \bar v ≈ -30 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of odd-even mass staggering by Hilaire (2002), Dobaczewski (2001), and Satula (1998), who had suggested contributions beyond BCS pairing. The authors propose a theoretically motivated two-term parametrization as an alternative to the traditional inverse power forms (such as 1/A^{1/2} or A^{-1/3}) used by Hilaire (2002) and others. The analysis enables future work to disentangle BCS pairing from mean-field effects in nuclear binding, and the extracted interaction matrix elements provide constraints for density functional theory and the UNEDF collaboration's nuclear modeling efforts.
