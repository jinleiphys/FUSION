# Multigluon correlations in JIMWLK

> We discuss applications of the JIMWLK renormalization group equation to multigluon correlations in high energy collisions. This includes recent progress in computing the energy dependence of higher point Wilson line correlators from the JIMWLK renormalization group equation. We find that the large Nc approximation used so far in the phenomenological literature is not very accurate. On the other hand a Gaussian finite Nc approximation is surprisingly close to the full result. We also discuss correlations at large rapidity separations, relevant for the "ridge" correlations observed in experiments.

## Key claim
The large Nc approximation used in phenomenological literature for higher-point Wilson line correlators is not very accurate, while a Gaussian finite Nc approximation is surprisingly close to the full JIMWLK result; however, the implications for ridge phenomenology remain uncertain due to a problematic infrared behavior of the decorrelation speed in rapidity.

## Method
The author solved the JIMWLK renormalization group equation numerically to compute the energy (rapidity) dependence of higher-point Wilson line correlators, specifically the quadrupole operator. The quadrupole was evaluated in two special coordinate configurations (a "line" and a "square") and compared to both a "naive large Nc" approximation (quadrupole as product of two dipoles) and a Gaussian finite Nc approximation based on the MV model. For unequal rapidity correlations, the decorrelation speed of dipole operators at different rapidities was extracted from exponential fits to JIMWLK simulations.

## Key numbers
- The JIMWLK evolution was run for 5.18 units of rapidity from the initial condition (MV model at y=0).
- The characteristic length/momentum scale in the naive large Nc approximation differs by a factor of approximately 2 from the actual JIMWLK result.
- The decorrelation speed ζ for unequal rapidity correlations is proportional to αs and is compared to the natural evolution speed λ ≡ d ln Qs²/dy measured in the same simulation.
- The decorrelation speed shows a slight logarithmic dependence on the infrared cutoff given by the system size L, persisting over a variety of initial conditions, lattice sizes, and infrared running coupling freeze-out values.

## Context
This work extends the numerical study of Dumitru (2011) on quadrupole correlators in JIMWLK evolution and addresses approximations used in phenomenological calculations by Tuchin (2009) and Albacete (2010). The results on unequal rapidity correlations are relevant for the "ridge" long-range rapidity correlations observed in nucleus-nucleus and high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions, previously modeled using MV model arguments by Dumitru (2008), Dusling (2009), and Lappi (2009). The paper identifies an unresolved infrared sensitivity in the decorrelation speed that leaves the implications for ridge phenomenology uncertain.
