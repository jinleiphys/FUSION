# The Pseudoscalar Decay Constant

> We discuss insights that may be drawn from our recent 2 flavour O(a)--improved Wilson quark simulations. We discuss the evidence of the onset of chiral logarithms in the pion decay constant. An overview is given of current extrapolation methods and a modification of chiral perturbation theory is presented as an approach for sensibly extrapolating to the physical quark masses.

## Key claim

The paper presents evidence for the onset of chiral logarithms in the pion decay constant from 2-flavor O(a)-improved Wilson quark simulations, but the data remain at quark masses too large for standard chiral perturbation theory to apply directly, necessitating a modified extrapolation method that suppresses the chiral logarithm above a certain energy scale.

## Method

The authors performed UKQCD simulations at three quark masses (κ = 0.1358, 0.1355, 0.1350) on 16³ × 32 lattices at β = 5.2 using a non-perturbatively improved clover action, extracting the pseudoscalar decay constant from the order-a improved expression (1 + b_A m_PS)(a f_A + c_A a f_P). They compare their results with JLQCD data and with predictions from standard chiral perturbation theory, Dyson-Schwinger equation approaches, and propose a modified chiral perturbation theory form that introduces an additional parameter to suppress the chiral logarithm at higher masses.

## Key numbers

- Three κ values used: 0.1358, 0.1355, and 0.1350, with corresponding axial-vector decay constant values a f_A = 0.0829(26), 0.1055(14), and 0.1336(11) respectively
- Pseudoscalar decay constant contributions a f_P = 0.1457(78), 0.1835(44), and 0.2468(33) at the same κ values
- The product m_PS L ≈ 4 for each simulation, indicating expected finite volume effects
- The modified chiral perturbation theory fit yields Λ = 0.93 ± 0.42 GeV, compared to Λ₄ = 1.25 +0.15/-0.13 GeV from standard chiral perturbation theory (Colangelo 2003)
- The perturbative renormalisation factor Z_A gives a 25% correction at first order with an expected systematic error up to 5% at second order
- The value of r₀ has an uncertainty of 5%

## Context

This work extends the UKQCD collaboration's earlier results (Allton 2004) on the pseudoscalar decay constant and compares with JLQCD simulations (Aoki 2002) that used a polynomial fit (A + B m_PS² + C m_PS⁴) omitting chiral logarithms. The paper contrasts the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach of Tandy (2003) and Maris (1999), which excludes quark loops, with standard chiral perturbation theory predictions from Colangelo (2003) and Bijnens (1998). The proposed modified chiral perturbation theory, building on the Adelaide group's finite-range regularisation work (Leinweber 1999, 2001; Young 2002), aims to enable sensible extrapolations to physical quark masses while respecting chiral perturbation theory near the chiral limit, though the authors note that continuum extrapolation and the difference between equal-mass and strange-light mesons remain open issues.
