# Limiting fragmentation from the Color Glass Condensate

> We show how the limiting fragmentation phenomenon can arise from the Color Glass Condensate model of high energy QCD. We consider the very forward rapidity region in relativistic heavy ion collisions and argue that in this region, nucleus-nucleus collisions are similar to proton-nucleus collisions (up to shadowing corrections). We then use the known results for proton-nucleus cross sections to show that it leads to the phenomenon of limiting fragmentation in the very forward region of heavy ion collisions as observed at RHIC.

## Key claim

Limiting fragmentation in the very forward rapidity region of heavy ion collisions can arise from the Color Glass Condensate model, provided one treats the projectile nucleus as a dense Color Glass Condensate and the target nucleus as a dilute system of partons, with the caveat that the approach breaks down within about three units of rapidity from the target nucleus.

## Method

The author argues that in the very forward rapidity region, nucleus-nucleus collisions are similar to proton-nucleus collisions (up to shadowing corrections). The known cross section for quark-nucleus scattering, which is the multiple scattering generalization of perturbative QCD and remains finite as transverse momentum goes to zero due to higher twist effects, is convoluted with the quark and gluon distributions of the target nucleus. After integrating over transverse momentum and converting to hadron rapidity, the resulting differential cross section depends only on the parton distributions of the target nucleus evaluated at a specific momentum fraction x.

## Key numbers

- The maximum observed value of (eta_h - y_beam) at RHIC is about +2 units of rapidity, leading to x_q = x_g approximately equal to exp(-2 + eta_h - y_beam).
- A scale of Q = 2.3 GeV was fixed by fitting the limiting fragmentation data at sqrt(s) ~ 20 GeV for a couple of units of rapidity.
- For the running saturation scale, Q_s0^2 = 2.0 GeV^2 at mid rapidity with lambda = 0.3 was used, and alternatively Q_s0^2 = 3.0 GeV^2 or lambda = 0.45 were considered but disfavored by data.
- The saturation scale of the target nucleus becomes appreciable (about 1 GeV) approximately three units of rapidity away from the target nucleus.

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate model, previously applied to mid-rapidity heavy ion collisions by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and to proton-nucleus collisions by Dumitru and Jalilian-Marian (2002), to explain the limiting fragmentation phenomenon observed by the PHOBOS collaboration at RHIC. The approach enables a semi-quantitative understanding of forward rapidity particle production and suggests that the blackness of the projectile nucleus combined with the growth of target nucleus parton distributions with rapidity drives the limiting fragmentation behavior.
