# Hadronic parity violation in $\vec{n} p \to d γ$ with effective field theory

> The parity-violating nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential is considered up to next-to-next-to leading order in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory. We include one-pion exchange at leading order and the two-pion exchange and two-nucleon contact terms at next-to-next-to-leading order. The effects of intermediate (two-pion exchange) and short-range (two-nucleon contact) terms are probed by calculating the photon asymmetry $A_γ$ in $\vec{n} p \to d γ$ employing Siegert's theorem and an accurate phenomenological potential for the parity-conserving NN interaction. We explore in detail the uncertainties due to the parameters that control the contribution of the short-range interaction. We obtain about 20% uncertainty in the value of $A_γ$ up to the next-to-next-to leading order. We discuss the implication of this uncertainty for the determination of the weak pion-nucleon coupling constant and how the uncertainty can be reduced.

# Hadronic parity violation in $\vec{n} p \to d \gamma$ with effective field theory

## Key claim
The parity-violating photon asymmetry $A_\gamma$ in $\vec{n} p \to d \gamma$ receives contributions from one-pion exchange (OPE) at leading order and from two-pion exchange (TPE) plus short-range contact terms at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), but the total asymmetry carries about 20% uncertainty because the renormalized low-energy constant (LEC) for the contact term cannot yet be fixed from data or underlying theory, leaving the result dependent on the choice of cutoff and renormalization scheme.

## Method
The authors constructed the parity-violating nucleon-nucleon potential up to NNLO in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, including OPE at LO and TPE plus two-nucleon contact terms at NNLO. They employed the Argonne v18 potential for the parity-conserving interaction and Siegert's theorem for the electromagnetic current operators, then calculated the photon asymmetry $A_\gamma$ in $\vec{n} p \to d \gamma$ at threshold. The renormalized LEC $C^R_6$ was treated in both maximal-subtraction (MX) and minimal-subtraction (MN) schemes, with its value estimated from $\rho$-meson resonance saturation, from the MN scheme's scale-dependent constant $C_{\rm mn}$, and from a term restoring cutoff independence of the OPE contribution.

## Key numbers
- The OPE contribution to the asymmetry coefficient $a_\gamma$ (where $A_\gamma = a_\gamma h^1_\pi$) ranges from $-0.0992$ at $\Lambda = 500$ MeV to $-0.1119$ at $\Lambda = 2000$ MeV, showing satisfactory cutoff independence.
- The TPE contribution to $a_\gamma$ varies from $-0.0022$ at $\Lambda = 500$ MeV to $+0.0133$ at $\Lambda = 2000$ MeV, changing sign and reaching about 10% of the OPE contribution.
- The total asymmetry coefficient $a^{\rm tot}_\gamma$ in the MX scheme ranges from $-0.1149$ at $\Lambda = 500$ MeV to $-0.0988$ at $\Lambda = 2000$ MeV.
- In the MN scheme with $\mu = m_\rho$, $a^{\rm tot}_\gamma$ ranges from $-0.0885$ at $\Lambda = 500$ MeV to $-0.0952$ at $\Lambda = 2000$ MeV.
- The overall range of $a^{\rm tot}_\gamma$ across all considered parameters is $-(0.08 \sim 0.11)$.
- The $\rho$-meson contribution to the LEC gives $C^R_6(\rho) = -1.20 \times 10^{-9} \, \text{MeV}^{-3}$ in units of $h^1_\pi = 4.6 \times 10^{-7}$.
- The MN scheme constant $C_{\rm mn}$ at $\mu = m_\rho$ equals $4.174 \times 10^{-8} \, \text{MeV}^{-3}$ in units of $h^1_\pi$.

## Context
This work extends the parity-violating effective field theory approach of Zhu et al. (2005) by calculating the photon asymmetry $A_\gamma$ in $\vec{n} p \to d \gamma$ with the full NNLO potential, including TPE and contact terms. Prior calculations using the DDH one-meson-exchange model (Desplanques, Donoghue, Holstein, 1980) had found the asymmetry dominated by the weak pion-nucleon coupling $h^1_\pi$ with negligible heavy-meson contributions, and earlier TPE estimates by Desplanques and collaborators (1972-1975) gave about -7% of the OPE contribution. The present results show that NNLO corrections are about 10-20% of the OPE contribution but that the contact-term uncertainty prevents a precise determination of $h^1_\pi$ until the LEC can be fixed by experiment, lattice calculations, or renormalization group methods.
