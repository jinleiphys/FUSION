# Pre-hydrodynamic jet momentum broadening beyond the jet quenching parameter

> We obtain the collision kernel and related dipole cross section during the initial nonequilibrium stages in heavy-ion collisions. These quantities are a crucial input for jet quenching calculations. We further compute the gluon splitting rates in the AMY formalism resulting from this nonequilibrium kernel. Comparing with thermal and commonly used forms, we find that particularly the gluon splitting rate for parton energies of the order of the hard effective temperature significantly differs from these approximations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that during the initial nonequilibrium stages of heavy-ion collisions, the gluon splitting rate for parton energies of the order of the hard effective temperature significantly differs from thermal and commonly used approximations, but this result depends on the assumption of an infinite medium and a static collision kernel during the splitting process.

## Method

The authors compute the collision kernel \(C(\mathbf{q}_\perp)\) and dipole cross section \(C(\mathbf{b})\) using QCD kinetic theory simulations with the isoHTL screening prescription, extracting these quantities from the overoccupied gluon fields that dominate the earliest instants after a heavy-ion collision. They then solve the integral equation for gluon splitting rates in the AMY formalism using a novel numerical method, feeding in the nonequilibrium collision kernel and comparing the results to thermal equilibrium and isotropic approximations.

## Key numbers

- For coupling \(\lambda=2\), the dipole cross section at fixed small \(b=0.01/T_\varepsilon\) is initially above the thermal value, then drops below and approaches the thermal value from below.
- For coupling \(\lambda=10\) (solid lines), the dipole cross section is reduced throughout the whole time evolution compared to the thermal value.
- The gluon splitting rate for fixed parton energy, normalized to the equilibrium rate, shows strongly qualitatively and quantitatively different evolution when using the nonequilibrium collision kernel compared to the commonly used isotropic approximation (denoted \(T_*\)).

## Context

This work extends previous computations of the jet quenching parameter \(\hat q\) during the early stages in heavy-ion collisions by Ipp (2020), Carrington (2021), Avramescu (2023), and Boguslavski (2024), going beyond \(\hat q\) to obtain the full collision kernel and dipole cross section. The results enable more realistic modeling of jet-medium interactions during the initial nonequilibrium stages and may improve QCD kinetic theory simulations that currently rely on thermal equilibrium approximations, as used in the work of Kurkela (2015, 2018) to connect the system to later hydrodynamic stages.
