# Parity-Violating Pion-Nucleon Coupling h_{πNN}^{(1)} from π^+ Electroproton Production Near the Threshold

> We study the possibility of measuring parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling h_{πNN}^{(1)} from π^+ electroproton production in the threshold region. We calculate the electron-helicity asymmetry in terms of h_{πNN}^{(1)} and the Z-boson exchange between the electron and proton in leading-order heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory. From the result, we demonstrate that the coupling can be determined to an accuracy of about 2 10^{-7} in an experiment with incident electron energy 250 MeV, luminosity 10^{38} sec^{-1} cm^{-2}, and a running time of 10^7 sec.

## Key claim

The parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling \(h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}\) can be determined to an accuracy of about \(2 \times 10^{-7}\) from the electron-helicity asymmetry in \(\pi^+\) electroproduction on a proton target, but only if the incident electron energy is 250 MeV (not 200 MeV) because at the lower energy the Z-exchange and hadronic contributions cancel almost entirely, making the total asymmetry too small to measure.

## Method

The authors performed a leading-order (LO) heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB\(\chi\)PT) calculation of the electron-helicity asymmetry in the reaction \(\vec{e}p \rightarrow e'\pi^+ n\) near threshold, including both the parity-violating hadronic vertex from \(h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}\) and the semi-leptonic Z-boson exchange between the electron and the nucleon system. They computed the unpolarized cross section from LO Feynman diagrams and the parity-violating asymmetry from the interference of LO parity-conserving and next-to-leading-order (NLO) parity-violating amplitudes, using standard values for the axial coupling \(g_A=1.257\), pion decay constant \(f_\pi=93\) MeV, and the \(\gamma\)-\(N\)-\(\Delta\) and \(\pi\)-\(N\)-\(\Delta\) couplings \(G_1=3.85\) and \(g_{\pi N\Delta}=1.05\).

## Key numbers

- The semi-inclusive \(\pi^+\) production cross section is about 100 nb at a forward steradian for incident electron energy \(E_e = 250\) MeV.
- The electron-helicity asymmetry from Z-exchange alone is on the order of \(-2.5 \times 10^{-7}\) at \(E_e = 200\) MeV, and increases by approximately a factor of 2 (to about \(-5 \times 10^{-7}\)) at \(E_e = 250\) MeV due to larger \(Q^2\).
- With the "best guess" value \(h_{\pi NN}^{(1)} = 5 \times 10^{-7}\) from DDH, the total asymmetry at \(E_e = 250\) MeV is around \(-2 \times 10^{-7}\) in the forward direction.
- The coupling can be determined to an accuracy of \(2 \times 10^{-7}\) (50% of the asymmetry) in an experiment with luminosity \(10^{38}\) sec\(^{-1}\) cm\(^{-2}\) and running time \(10^7\) sec.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of \(h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}\) extraction from pion photoproduction \(\vec{\gamma}p \rightarrow n\pi^+\) (Chen and Ji, 2000) to the electroproduction channel, which offers a larger cross section and experimental practicality. It contrasts with earlier theoretical studies by Li, Henley and Hwang and by Hammer and Drechsel, who used meson exchange models for the same process, and with the ongoing few-body experiments at LANSCE (\(\vec{n}p \rightarrow d\gamma\)), Jefferson Lab (\(\vec{\gamma}d \rightarrow np\)), and NIST (neutron spin rotation in helium) that aim to determine the same coupling. The result enables a dedicated measurement of the leading parity-violating hadronic coupling using a semi-inclusive \(\pi^+\) detection setup with a 250 MeV polarized electron beam.
