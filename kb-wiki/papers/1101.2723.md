# Symplectic structure and monopole strength in 12C

> The relation between the monopole transition strength and existence of cluster structure in the excited states is discussed based on an algebraic cluster model. The structure of $^{12}$C is studied with a 3$α$ model, and the wave function for the relative motions between $α$ clusters are described by the symplectic algebra $Sp(2,R)_z$, which corresponds to the linear combinations of $SU(3)$ states with different multiplicities. Introducing $Sp(2,R)_z$ algebra works well for reducing the number of the basis states, and it is also shown that states connected by the strong monopole transition are classified by a quantum number $Λ$ of the $Sp(2,R)_z$ algebra.

## Key claim

The strong monopole transition strength in $^{12}$C is closely related to the symplectic quantum number $\Lambda$ of the $Sp(2,R)_z$ algebra; states with large monopole strength from the ground state dominantly contain components of ladder states with the same $\Lambda$ value ($5/2$) as the ground state, rather than higher $\Lambda$ values.

## Method

The structure of $^{12}$C was studied using a 3$\alpha$ cluster model, where the wave functions for the relative motions between $\alpha$ clusters were described by harmonic oscillator basis states forming the symplectic algebra $Sp(2,R)_z$. This algebra corresponds to linear combinations of $SU(3)$ states with different multiplicities, classified by a quantum number $\Lambda$. The model space was truncated to the three lowest $\Lambda$ values for each $\mu$ state, allowing inclusion of total harmonic oscillator quanta up to $N_{max}=100$ and $\mu$ values up to 30, which is not feasible with pure $SU(3)$ bases. The Hamiltonian included a two-body $\alpha$-$\alpha$ folding potential, Coulomb interaction, and a three-body interaction whose strength was adjusted separately for each $J$ to reproduce experimental ground band energies.

## Key numbers

- The calculated monopole transition matrix element (proton part) from the ground state to the second $0^+$ state is $\sim$5.9 fm$^2$, compared to the experimental value of $5.4\pm0.2$ fm$^2$.
- The ground state has a 93% squared overlap with the $\Lambda=5/2$ state whose band head is $(\lambda,\mu)=(0,4)$.
- The second $0^+$ state is calculated at $E_f = 0.96$ MeV above the 3$\alpha$ threshold.
- The ground state root mean square radius is 2.39 fm; the second $0^+$ state has $R_{r.m.s.}=3.61$ fm.
- The three-body interaction strengths used are 31.7 MeV for $J=0^+$, 63.0 MeV for $J=2^+$, and 150.0 MeV for $J=4^+$.
- The $\alpha$-$\alpha$ folding potential parameters are $\alpha=0.2009$ fm$^{-2}$ and $V_2=-106.1$ MeV; the Coulomb parameter is $\beta=0.5972$ fm$^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the algebraic cluster model approach to $^{12}$C by introducing $Sp(2,R)_z$ algebra to efficiently describe cluster states with large harmonic oscillator quanta, building on prior studies of symplectic structure in light nuclei by SP2 and Arickx. The relation between monopole strength and cluster structure was previously proposed by Kawabata and discussed theoretically by YIO, Uegaki, HO, and Yamada-1, with the Bayman-Bohr theorem (1959) providing the foundation. The present analysis enables future combination with the complex scaling method to correctly treat unbound states and resonance boundary conditions.
