# Measuring Double Parton Distributions in Nucleons at Proton-Nucleus Colliders

> We predict a strong enhancement of multijet production in proton-nucleus collisions at collider energies, as compared to a naive expectation of a cross section $\propto A$. The study of the process would allow to measure, for the first time, the double parton distribution functions in a nucleon in a model independent way and hence to study both the longitudinal and the transverse correlations of partons.

# Measuring Double Parton Distributions in Nucleons at Proton-Nucleus Colliders

## Key claim
The study of the A-dependence of double parton scattering in proton-nucleus collisions will allow a model-independent measurement of double parton distribution functions in nucleons, provided that the kinematic regime is chosen where shadowing corrections to nuclear structure functions can be neglected or are small (e.g., $x_i \geq 0.1$, $p_t \geq 5-7$ GeV/c).

## Method
The authors calculate the double parton scattering cross section in proton-nucleus collisions by separating two contributions: scattering off one nucleon of the nucleus ($\sigma_1^D \propto A$) and scattering off two different nucleons ($\sigma_2^D \propto A^{1.5}$). The second term is computed solely from nuclear geometry using the nuclear thickness function $T(B)$, without model assumptions about parton correlations. The relative importance of the two terms is estimated using the CDF measurement of $\sigma_{eff}$ and a factorized form of the double parton distribution.

## Key numbers
- $\sigma_{eff} = 14.5 \pm 1.7^{+1.7}_{-2.3}$ mb, measured by CDF for photon+jet and dijet scattering in $p\bar{p}$ collisions at Tevatron, in the kinematic range $x=0.01-0.40$ (photon+jet) and $x=0.002-0.20$ (dijet).
- $\sigma_2^D / \sigma_1^D \approx 0.45 \cdot (A/10)^{0.5}$ for $A \geq 10$, using the Fermi step parameterization of nuclear matter densities.
- For heavy nuclei at RHIC, the $\sigma_2^D$ term constitutes about 70% of the total double parton scattering cross section.
- The triple scattering terms scale as $\sigma_1^T : \sigma_2^T : \sigma_3^T = 1 : 1.45(A/10)^{0.5} : 0.25(A/10)$ for $A \geq 10$, assuming $\sigma_{eff}$ as the transverse scale.

## Context
This work extends the study of double parton scattering, previously investigated in $pp$ and $p\bar{p}$ collisions by experiments such as CDF (1997), by proposing proton-nucleus collisions as a cleaner probe. The key limitation of proton-proton studies was the need to model both longitudinal and transverse partonic correlations simultaneously. The authors show that the large nuclear radius ($R_A \gg R_N$) allows separation of these correlations through the distinct A-dependence of the two scattering mechanisms. This enables future measurements at RHIC and LHC to test the factorization approximation of Eq. (1) and to extract the transverse separation between two partons in a nucleon. The approach also opens the possibility of measuring triple parton densities at LHC energies with a large-acceptance detector.
