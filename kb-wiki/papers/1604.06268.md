# Shells, orbit bifurcations and symmetry restorations in Fermi systems

> The periodic-orbit theory based on the improved stationary-phase method within the phase-space path integral approach is presented for the semiclassical description of the nuclear shell structure, concerning the main topics of the fruitful activity of V. G. Solovjov. We apply this theory to study bifurcations and symmetry breaking phenomena in a radial power-law potential which is close to the realistic Woods-Saxon one up to about the Fermi energy. Using the realistic parametrization of nuclear shapes we explain the origin of the double-humped fission barrier and the asymmetry in the fission isomer shapes by the bifurcations of periodic orbits. The semiclassical origin of the oblate-prolate shape asymmetry and tetrahedral shapes is also suggested within the improved periodic-orbit approach. The enhancement of shell structures at some surface diffuseness and deformation parameters of such shapes are explained by existence of the simple local bifurcations and new non-local bridge-orbit bifurcations in integrable and partially integrable Fermi systems. We obtained good agreement between the semiclassical and quantum shell-structure components of the level density and energy for several surface diffuseness and deformation parameters of the potentials, including their symmetry breaking and bifurcation values.

# Shells, orbit bifurcations and symmetry restorations in Fermi systems

## Key claim

The improved stationary-phase method (ISPM) within periodic-orbit theory can explain the origin of the double-humped fission barrier, asymmetry in fission isomer shapes, oblate-prolate shape asymmetry, and tetrahedral shapes through bifurcations of periodic orbits, but only for specific surface diffuseness and deformation parameters where simple local bifurcations and new non-local bridge-orbit bifurcations occur in integrable and partially integrable Fermi systems.

## Method

The authors extended the Gutzwiller periodic-orbit theory using the improved stationary-phase method (ISPM) within a phase-space path integral approach, applying it to a radial power-law potential (RPLP) that approximates the Woods-Saxon potential up to about the Fermi energy. They derived trace formulas for the level density and shell-correction energy for all periodic-orbit families found by scaling of the RPLP classical dynamics, using the ISPM to handle bifurcations and symmetry-breaking points where the standard stationary-phase method diverges. The semiclassical results were compared with quantum calculations for several surface diffuseness and deformation parameters, including symmetry-breaking and bifurcation values.

## Key numbers

- The enhancement of the level density amplitude at bifurcation points scales as \(A^{(\mathcal{K})}_{\rm PO} \sim A^{\rm G}_{\rm PO} \; \hbar^{-\mathcal{K}/2}\), where \(\mathcal{K}\) is the classical degeneracy and \(A^{\rm G}_{\rm PO}\) is the Gutzwiller amplitude for isolated periodic orbits.
- The averaging parameter \(\Gamma\) for the level density must be much smaller than the Fermi energy \(\varepsilon_F\).
- The shell-correction energy is given by \(\delta U_{\rm scl} = 2\sum_{\rm PO} \frac{\hbar^2}{t_{\rm PO}^2}\, \delta g^{}_{\rm PO}(\varepsilon_F)\), where \(t_{\rm PO}=M T_{\rm PO}\) is the period for a periodic orbit with repetition number \(M\).

## Context

This work extends the periodic-orbit theory developed by Gutzwiller (1971), Balian and Bloch (1972), and Berry and Tabor (1976) to handle bifurcations and symmetry breaking in nuclear mean-field potentials. It builds on the shell-correction method of Strutinsky (1967) and the improved stationary-phase method previously applied to spheroidal and elliptical potentials by Magner and collaborators (2006-2013). The results enable semiclassical understanding of nuclear deformation energies, fission barriers, and shape isomerism, and provide a framework for extending periodic-orbit calculations to more realistic deformed potentials with surface diffuseness.
