# Thermalization of the quark-gluon plasma and dynamical formation of Bose-Einstein Condensate

> We report recent progress on understanding the thermalization of the quark-gluon plasma during the early stage in a heavy ion collision. The initially high overpopulation in the pre-equilibrium gluonic matter (``glasma'') is shown to play a crucial role. The strongly interacting nature (and thus fast evolution) naturally arises as an {\em emergent property} of this pre-equilibrium matter where the intrinsic coupling is weak but the highly occupied gluon states coherently amplify the scattering. A possible transient Bose-Einstein Condensate is argued to form dynamically on a rather general ground. We develop the kinetic approach for describing this highly overpopulated system and find approximate scaling solutions as well as numerically study the onset of condensation. Finally we discuss possible phenomenological implications.

# Thermalization of the quark-gluon plasma and dynamical formation of Bose-Einstein Condensate

## Key claim
The strongly interacting nature (and thus fast evolution) of the pre-equilibrium gluonic matter (glasma) arises as an emergent property where the intrinsic coupling is weak but the highly occupied gluon states coherently amplify the scattering, with the caveat that this holds only when the occupation number reaches values as high as 1/α_s, rendering the power counting in coupling constant different from normal perturbation theory.

## Method
The author develops a kinetic approach assuming dominance of 2↔2 elastic processes, deriving a transport equation in the small-angle approximation analogous to the Landau approach for non-relativistic electromagnetic plasma, but including full nonlinearity in the f(1+f) terms arising from the bosonic nature of gluons. Scaling solutions are found analytically for both static box and longitudinally expanding cases, and the kinetic equation is solved numerically to study the onset of condensation under varied initial conditions including different distribution shapes, overpopulation parameters, and anisotropies.

## Key numbers
- Initial glasma distribution: f(p ≤ Q_s) = 1/α_s, f(p > Q_s) = 0
- Overpopulation parameter for glasma: n ε^{-3/4} ~ 1/α_s^{1/4}
- Thermal Bose gas overpopulation parameter: n ε^{-3/4} ≈ 0.28 (massless case)
- Critical coupling for overpopulation condition: 1/α_s > 0.154
- Realistic α_s values considered: 0.2~0.3
- Static box scaling solution: Λ_s ~ Q_s (t_0/t)^{3/7}, Λ ~ Q_s (t_0/t)^{-1/7}
- Static box thermalization time: t_th ~ (1/Q_s) (1/α_s)^{7/4}
- Expanding case scaling solution: Λ_s ~ Q_s (t_0/t)^{(4+δ)/7}, Λ ~ Q_s (t_0/t)^{(1+2δ)/7}
- Expanding case thermalization time: t_th ~ (1/Q_s) (1/α_s)^{7/(3-δ)}
- Critical initial overpopulation for BEC onset in static box: n ε^{-3/4} > 0.28
- Critical initial overpopulation for BEC onset in expanding isotropic case: n ε^{-3/4} > 0.40

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Blaizot (2011) on overpopulated glasma and the kinetic approach developed by the author with collaborators (BLM). It contrasts with the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario of Baier (2000) and Mueller (2005) by showing that purely elastic scatterings aided by high overpopulation can drive thermalization, without requiring inelastic processes as the primary mechanism. The numerical findings on Bose condensate formation are consistent with classical-statistical lattice simulations of scalar field theory by Epelbaum (2011) and Gelis (2011), while the case for non-Abelian gauge theory remains under investigation by Berges (2011, 2012), Kurkela (2012), and Schlichting (2012). The work enables pre-equilibrium phenomenology including photon and dilepton production from glasma, as first attempted by Chiu (2012), and opens avenues for studying early-time flow generation and pre-equilibrium jet energy loss.
