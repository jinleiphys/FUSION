# Photoproduction of heavy quarks in ultraperipheral pp, pA, and AA collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> Photoproduction of heavy quarks in ultraperipheral collisions can help elucidate important features of the physics of heavy quarks in Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). Due to the dependence on parton distributions it can also potentially offer some constraining ability in the determination of nuclear parton distributions. In the present study we consider next-to-leading order (NLO) photoproduction of heavy quarks in ultraperipheral proton-proton (pp), proton-nucleus (pA), and nucleus-nucleus (AA) collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC). Total cross sections and rapidity distributions are considered and the influence of nuclear modifications of parton distributions on these quantities are explored for pA and AA collisions. We find that photoproduction of heavy quarks in PbPb collisions exhibit significant sensitivity to nuclear effects, and in conjunction with photoproduction in pPb collisions, affords good constraining potential for gluon shadowing determination.

## Key claim

The paper claims that next-to-leading order (NLO) photoproduction of heavy quarks in ultraperipheral PbPb collisions exhibits significant sensitivity to nuclear effects, and when combined with pPb collisions, offers good constraining potential for gluon shadowing determination, though the sensitivity in pPb collisions alone is very small (reducing the no-modification total cross section by approximately 0.9% to 1.7% for charm and 0.05% to 1.4% for bottom depending on the nuclear modification set).

## Method

The authors calculated total cross sections and rapidity distributions for inclusive photoproduction of c\bar{c} and b\bar{b} pairs in ultraperipheral pp, pPb, and PbPb collisions at the LHC using next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD. They convolved equivalent photon fluxes from protons and nuclei with NLO photoproduction cross sections (both direct and resolved contributions), using MSTW08 parton distributions for the proton, and two nuclear modification sets (EPS09 central fit and FGS10 strong gluon shadowing model) for nuclear parton distributions, with the AFG04 NLO photon parton distributions for resolved contributions.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for c\bar{c} in pp collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=7 TeV: 2728.0 nb (direct 2050.9 nb, resolved 677.1 nb)
- Total cross section for c\bar{c} in pp collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=14 TeV: 3962.3 nb (direct 2837.6 nb, resolved 1124.7 nb)
- Total cross section for b\bar{b} in pp collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=7 TeV: 51.3 nb (direct 31.2 nb, resolved 20.1 nb)
- Total cross section for b\bar{b} in pp collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=14 TeV: 96.8 nb (direct 52.8 nb, resolved 44.0 nb)
- Ratio of total cross sections at 14 TeV to 7 TeV in pp: R_c\bar{c}=1.452, R_b\bar{b}=1.887
- Total cross section for c\bar{c} in pPb at sqrt(s_NN)=5 TeV: 3170.3 microbarns (MSTW08), 3143.4 microbarns (EPS09), 3115.3 microbarns (FGS10)
- Total cross section for c\bar{c} in pPb at sqrt(s_NN)=8.8 TeV: 4709.5 microbarns (MSTW08), 4668.9 microbarns (EPS09), 4627.3 microbarns (FGS10)
- Total cross section for b\bar{b} in pPb at sqrt(s_NN)=5 TeV: 31.6 microbarns (MSTW08), 31.4 microbarns (EPS09), 31.2 microbarns (FGS10)
- Total cross section for b\bar{b} in pPb at sqrt(s_NN)=8.8 TeV: 59.8 microbarns (MSTW08), 59.4 microbarns (EPS09), 59.0 microbarns (FGS10)
- Gluon shadowing in FGS10 reaches about 0.49 at x approximately 10^{-5} at Q^2=4m_c^2, while EPS09 gluon shadowing is almost constant at about 0.8 at small x
- Heavy quark masses used: m_c=1.4 GeV, m_b=4.75 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous leading-order (LO) studies of heavy quark photoproduction in ultraperipheral pPb and PbPb collisions (Adeluyi 2011, 2012) by providing next-to-leading order (NLO) calculations, and adds results for pp collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=7 TeV and 14 TeV. The study uses the EPS09 nuclear parton distribution set from Eskola, Paukunnen, and Salgado (2009) and the FGS10 set from Frankfurt, Guzey, and Strikman (2011), which differ significantly in their gluon shadowing predictions. The results enable future experimental measurements at the LHC to help constrain nuclear gluon distributions, particularly gluon shadowing, which remains the least constrained aspect of nuclear parton distributions.
