# Role of T=0 pairing in Gamow-Teller states in N=Z nuclei

> Gamow-Teller (GT) states in N=Z nuclei with the mass number A from 48 to 64 are studied by using Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov + quasi-particle random phase approximation (HFB+QRPA) with Skyrme interactions. The isoscalar spin-triplet (T=0,S=1) pairing interaction is taken into account in QRPA calculations. It is found in the context of SU(4) symmetry in the spin-isospin space that the GT strength of lower energy excitations is largely enhanced by the T=0 pairing interaction which works cooperatively with the T=1 pairing interaction in the ground state. A two-peaked structure observed recently in $(p,n)$ reaction on $^{56}$Ni can be considered as a manifestation of the role of T=0 pairing in the GT collective states.

## Key claim

The low-energy Gamow-Teller (GT) strength in N=Z nuclei is largely enhanced by the isoscalar spin-triplet (T=0, S=1) pairing interaction, which works cooperatively with the isovector (T=1) pairing in the ground state, but this enhancement is framed within the context of SU(4) symmetry in spin-isospin space and the effect is conditional on the T=0 pairing strength parameter f being sufficiently large (f >= 1.0).

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations in coordinate space followed by quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) with Skyrme interactions (SGII+Te1 and T21) for N=Z nuclei with mass numbers A=48 to 64. Density-dependent contact pairing interactions were adopted for both T=1 and T=0 channels, with the T=0 pairing strength varied by a factor f from 0.0 to 1.7, where f=1.7 was the maximum value to avoid spin-isospin instability of the ground state of ⁵⁶Cu.

## Key numbers

- The T=1 pairing strength was set to V₀ = -550 MeV·fm³, determined to reproduce the average odd-even mass staggering gap Δ⁽³⁾ = 12/A¹/³ MeV in the N=28 region.
- In ⁵⁶Ni without T=0 pairing (f=0), the main QRPA peak appeared at 20.4 MeV with a small peak at 18.0 MeV; the unperturbed strength had groups at E_x = 9 MeV and 17 MeV.
- Experimental data for ⁵⁶Ni show two peaks at 18.7 MeV and 20.8 MeV from (p,n) reaction measurements (Sasano 2011).
- With T=0 pairing at f=1.5, the lower peak in ⁵⁶Ni appeared at 16.6 MeV with B(GT)=4.82, and the higher peak at 20.5 MeV with B(GT)=4.10.
- The GT sum rule value S_-(GT) for ⁵⁶Ni decreased from 18.28 (f=0) to 14.75 (f=1.5), a decrease of about 20% at f=1.5 and up to 30% at f=1.7.
- For ⁴⁸Cr with f=1.5, the lower peak at E_x=17.4 MeV had B(GT)=5.68 and S_-(GT)=11.77.
- For ⁶⁴Ge with f=1.5, the lower peak at E_x=16.1 MeV had B(GT)=2.15 and S_-(GT)=17.26.
- The extracted T=0 pairing strength from p-n scattering length makes a bound deuteron state with binding energy 1.41 MeV, somewhat smaller than the experimental value 2.2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent HF+RPA calculations by Bai (2009, 2010, 2011) for closed-shell nuclei to open-shell N=Z nuclei using HFB+QRPA. The results are compared with shell model calculations using KB3G and GXPF1A interactions by Sasano (2011) and a particle-vibration coupling model by Niu (2012) for ⁵⁶Ni. The paper enables future studies of T=0 pairing effects in medium-heavy and heavy N=Z nuclei, with the empirical GT strength data providing constraints to determine the phenomenological T=0 pairing strength for HFB and QRPA calculations.
