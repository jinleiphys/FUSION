# Implications of Neutron Star Properties for the Existence of Light Dark Matter

> It was recently suggested that the discrepancy between two methods of measuring the lifetime of the neutron may be a result of an unseen decay mode into a dark matter particle which is almost degenerate with the neutron. We explore the consequences of this for the properties of neutron stars, finding that their known properties are in conflict with the existence of such a particle.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the proposed dark matter particle almost degenerate with the neutron, suggested to explain the neutron lifetime discrepancy, cannot exist because its presence would reduce the maximum mass of neutron stars to about 0.7 M$_\odot$, which is incompatible with the well-established existence of neutron stars with masses around 1.4 M$_\odot$ and up to 2 M$_\odot$.

## Method

The authors used the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model to calculate the equation of state (EoS) of dense nuclear matter in beta-equilibrium, including neutrons, protons, electrons, muons, and a dark matter particle assumed to be degenerate with the neutron. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations with this EoS to compute the mass versus radius relation for neutron stars, comparing the cases with and without dark matter.

## Key numbers

- The maximum neutron star mass without dark matter is around 2.2 M$_\odot$; with dark matter included, the maximum mass drops to 0.7 M$_\odot$.
- The total baryon number of a maximum-mass neutron star (around 2 M$_\odot$) is of order $3.2 \times 10^{57}$; the maximum-mass dark matter star contains only $9.2 \times 10^{56}$ dark matter particles plus nucleons, corresponding to a pre-decay mass of about 0.7 M$_\odot$.
- Observed neutron star masses are typically around 1.4-1.5 M$_\odot$, with maximum masses around 2 M$_\odot$ (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013).
- Dark matter begins to dominate the composition beyond a baryon density of 2 $\rho_0$ (twice nuclear saturation density).

## Context

This work tests the dark matter explanation for the neutron lifetime discrepancy proposed by Fornal and Grinstein (2018), which had already been partially constrained by Czarnecki et al. (2018) and ruled out for the decay mode $n \rightarrow DM + \gamma$ by Tang et al. (2018). The paper extends the analysis to neutron star environments, finding that the scenario is incompatible with observed neutron star masses. The result is model-independent, as the authors verified that alternate parameter sets and inclusion of hyperons do not change the conclusion.
