# Exact and asymptotic local virial theorems for finite fermionic systems

> We investigate the particle and kinetic-energy densities for a system of $N$ fermions confined in a potential $V(\bfr)$. In an earlier paper [J. Phys. A: Math. Gen. {\bf 36}, 1111 (2003)], some exact and asymptotic relations involving the particle density and the kinetic-energy density locally, i.e. at any given point $\bfr$, were derived for isotropic harmonic oscillators in arbitrary dimensions. In this paper we show that these {\it local virial theorems} (LVT) also hold exactly for linear potentials in arbitrary dimensions and for the one-dimensional box. We also investigate the validity of these LVTs when they are applied to arbitrary smooth potentials. We formulate generalized LVTs that are supported by a semiclassical theory which relates the density oscillations to the closed non-periodic orbits of the classical system. We test the validity of these generalized theorems numerically for various local potentials. Although they formally are only valid asymptotically for large particle numbers $N$, we show that they practically are surprisingly accurate also for moderate values of $N$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that local virial theorems (LVTs) relating particle density and kinetic-energy density at any given point hold exactly only for isotropic harmonic oscillators, linear potentials, and the one-dimensional box; for arbitrary smooth potentials they are only asymptotically valid for large particle numbers \(N\), but the authors demonstrate numerically that they are surprisingly accurate even for moderate \(N\).

## Method

The authors derive exact quantum-mechanical expressions for the particle density \(\rho(\mathbf{r})\) and kinetic-energy densities \(\tau(\mathbf{r})\), \(\tau_1(\mathbf{r})\), and \(\xi(\mathbf{r})\) for isotropic harmonic oscillators, linear potentials in arbitrary dimensions, and the one-dimensional infinite square well. They then formulate generalized LVTs guided by a semiclassical theory that expresses density oscillations in terms of closed non-periodic orbits of the classical system, and test these generalized theorems numerically for spherical and non-spherical quartic potentials and the two-dimensional circular billiard.

## Key numbers

* For the 3D isotropic harmonic oscillator with \(N = 79\,422\) particles (corresponding to \(M = 60\)), the asymptotic relation \(\delta\tau_{\mathrm{as}}(r) \simeq [\lambda_M - V(r)] \delta\rho_{\mathrm{as}}(r)\) is tested and shows excellent agreement except near the classical turning point (units: \(\hbar = \omega = m = 1\)).
* For a 3D spherical billiard with \(N = 100\,068\) particles, the kinetic-energy density \(\xi(r)\) exhibits irregular, long-ranged oscillations around its constant Thomas-Fermi value \(\xi_{\mathrm{TF}}\) (units: \(\hbar^2/2m = R = 1\)).
* The oscillating part of the density for the one-dimensional box asymptotically satisfies \(-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \delta\rho''_{\mathrm{as}}(x) = 4\lambda_{\mathrm{TF}} \delta\rho(x)\), with \(\lambda_{\mathrm{TF}}\) being the Thomas-Fermi Fermi energy.

## Context

This work extends earlier exact results for isotropic harmonic oscillators by Brack et al. (2003) to linear potentials and the one-dimensional box, and generalizes the local virial theorems to arbitrary smooth potentials using a semiclassical theory developed by the same group (Roccia, Brack, and Koch, 2007-2008). The findings enable the application of local virial relations to mean-field descriptions of interacting fermion systems within density functional theory, and provide a semiclassical understanding of density oscillations in terms of classical closed orbits.
