# On the quarkonium-in-jet collinear fragmentation at moderate-to-large transverse momentum

> We report progress on the Heavy-Flavor Non-Relativistic Evolution (HF-NRevo) setup, a novel methodology to address quarkonium formation within the fragmentation approximation. Our study sheds light on the moderate to large transverse-momentum sector, where the leading-twist collinear fragmentation of a single parton prevails over the higher-twist fragmentation from a constituent heavy-quark pair produced in the hard scattering. As for the initial energy-scale inputs, we rely on nonrelativistic next-to-leading calculations for all the parton-to-quarkonia fragmentation channels. Preliminary sets of variable-flavor number-scheme (VFNS) fragmentation functions, named NRFF1.0, are built via an evolution-threshold enhanced DGLAP scheme. Taking NRFF1.0 as a starting point, we use HF-NRevo to address the collinear fragmentation of quarkonia inside jets.

## Key claim

The Heavy-Flavor Non-Relativistic Evolution (HF-NRevo) setup provides a novel methodology for quarkonium formation within the fragmentation approximation, specifically targeting the moderate-to-large transverse-momentum sector where leading-twist collinear fragmentation of a single parton dominates over higher-twist fragmentation from a constituent heavy-quark pair produced in the hard scattering.

## Method

The author develops preliminary sets of variable-flavor number-scheme (VFNS) fragmentation functions, named NRFF1.0, using nonrelativistic next-to-leading-order (NLO) calculations for all parton-to-quarkonia fragmentation channels as initial energy-scale inputs. These are built via an evolution-threshold enhanced DGLAP scheme, with the DGLAP evolution proceeding in two stages: first an expanded and decoupled evolution performed symbolically using symJETHAD, then a numerical all-order evolution. Missing Higher-Order Uncertainties (MHOUs) are quantified through a Monte-Carlo-like approach by scanning the factorization and renormalization scales in the initial FF inputs, varying them by a factor of 1/2 to 2.

## Key numbers

- The DGLAP evolution of quarkonium FFs proceeds from the lowest scale, μ₀, to a matching value μ_M, where μ_M can be set approximately equal to Q × R_J (with Q the process-typical hard scale and R_J the jet radius)
- The factorization scale μ_F is varied from 30 to 120 GeV for the (b → Υ) and (g → Υ) NRFF1.0 NLO fragmentation functions shown in the figures
- The initial-scale inputs are varied by a factor of 1/2 to 2 for quantifying MHOUs

## Context

This work extends the NRQCD framework for quarkonium hadronization, which was developed by Caswell (1985) and Bodwin (1994), by providing a consistent DGLAP evolution scheme for fragmentation functions. The NRFF1.0 sets will supersede the ZCW19+ and ZCFW22 functions currently used in studies of vector quarkonia and charmed B mesons. The future release of NRFF1.0jet Semi-Inclusive Fragmenting Jet Functions (SIFJFs) will enable precision studies of jet substructure via resummation-sensitive observables such as jet angularities, with applications at the HL-LHC, the EIC, and new-generation lepton colliders.
