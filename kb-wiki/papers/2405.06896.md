# Energy momentum tensor on and off the light cone: exposition with scalar Yukawa theory

> We compute the gravitational form factors $A_i$, $D_i$ and $\bar c_i$ of the scalar Yukawa theory using both the light-cone and covariant perturbation theory at the one-loop level. The light-cone formalism provides a potential approach to access these form factors beyond the perturbative regime. However, unlike the covariant formulation, the Poincaré symmetry on the light cone is not manifest. In this work, we use perturbation theory as a benchmark to extract the gravitational form factors from the light-front energy-momentum tensor. By comparing results on and off the light cone, we identify $T^{++}, T^{+a}, T^{+-}, T^{12}$ as the "good currents" that are properly renormalized and can be used to extract the gravitational form factors.

## Key claim

The authors compute gravitational form factors (GFFs) of scalar Yukawa theory at one-loop order using both light-cone perturbation theory (LCPT) and covariant perturbation theory (CPT), and identify that only the "good currents" \(T^{++}, T^{+a}, T^{+-}, T^{12}\) are properly renormalized and can be used to extract GFFs, while components \(T^{11}\) and \(T^{22}\) contain uncanceled divergences and violate current conservation in the light-front formalism.

## Method

The authors evaluate the energy-momentum tensor (EMT) of scalar Yukawa theory at one-loop order using both covariant perturbation theory (CPT) and light-cone perturbation theory (LCPT) with Pauli-Villars regularization. They compute the hadronic matrix elements of the EMT for a nucleon (complex scalar) dressed by a pion (real scalar) and extract the gravitational form factors \(A_i(Q^2)\), \(D_i(Q^2)\), and \(\bar c_i(Q^2)\) for each constituent. The light-front results are analyzed using covariant light-front dynamics (CLFD), which introduces a null vector \(\omega^\mu\) to parametrize the reduced Poincaré symmetry and spurious form factors \(S_{1i}\) and \(S_{2i}\).

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass \(m = 0.94\,\mathrm{GeV}\) and pion mass \(\mu = 0.14\,\mathrm{GeV}\) are assigned as tentative physical masses for the scalar Yukawa model.
- The dimensionless coupling is \(\alpha = g^2/(16\pi m^2)\), appearing in the tree-level Yukawa potential \(V(r) = -(\alpha/r)e^{-\mu r}\).
- The total \(\bar c(Q^2) = \bar c_N(Q^2) + \bar c_\pi(Q^2)\) vanishes with both dimensional regularization and Pauli-Villars regularization, as required by current conservation.
- In the standard light-front formulation, the violation of current conservation leads to a divergent total \(\bar c(-q^2) = (Z/m^2)\Sigma(m^2)\).
- The GFFs \(\bar c_i(Q^2)\) for both nucleon and pion are constant (independent of \(Q^2\)), consistent with the ultra-local character of scalar Yukawa theory.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous non-perturbative calculation of GFFs in scalar Yukawa theory (Cao 2023) by using perturbation theory as a benchmark to identify which EMT components are properly renormalized on the light front. It builds on the covariant light-front dynamics framework developed by Karmanov and collaborators (Carbonell 1998) and the Lorentz decomposition for CLFD by Lorce (2015). The results enable future non-perturbative extraction of all three GFFs \(A_i\), \(D_i\), and \(\bar c_i\) from light-front Hamiltonian calculations, including the force between constituents encoded in \(\bar c_i\), which requires the "good current" \(T^{+-}\) in addition to \(T^{++}\) and \(T^{12}\).
