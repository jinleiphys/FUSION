# Neutron stars with a crossover equation of state

> The question of whether quark matter exists in neutron stars is a long standing one. Generally one finds that a first order phase transition from baryons to quarks softens the equation of state so much that the star would collapse into a black hole. We consider a crossover equation of state, similar to the crossover that is found in lattice QCD studies at finite temperature and zero or small baryon chemical potentials. We find that with reasonable parameters it may be possible to support neutron stars up to about 2.2 solar masses. In that case 1 to 10% of the pressure would be contributed by quark matter in the central core of the highest mass stars.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a smooth crossover equation of state (EoS) from hadronic matter to quark matter, analogous to the crossover seen in lattice QCD at finite temperature, can support neutron stars up to about 2.2 solar masses, whereas a first-order phase transition would soften the EoS too much and cause collapse. The hedge is that this result depends on the choice of the switching parameter μ₀, which must be at least about 1.8 GeV to be consistent with observed 2-solar-mass stars, and that the hadronic EoS used is a simplified relativistic mean-field model.

## Method

The authors construct a zero-temperature crossover EoS by combining a hadronic pressure from relativistic mean-field theory (with scalar σ, vector ω and ρ mesons, fit to nuclear saturation properties) with a perturbative QCD quark pressure (up to αₛ² order, with massless quarks), using a smooth switching function S(μ) = exp[-(μ₀/μ)⁴]. They then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute neutron-star mass, radius, and central energy density for four values of μ₀ (1.65, 1.8, 1.95, and 2.1 GeV).

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron-star masses for the four μ₀ values: 2.01, 2.11, 2.17, and 2.20 M⊙ (solar masses).
- The observed neutron-star masses cited: 1.928±0.017 M⊙ (Demorest, updated by Fonseca), 2.01±0.04 M⊙ (Antoniadis), and 2.14±0.10 M⊙ (Cromartie).
- The switching parameter μ₀ must be at least about 1.8 GeV to support the observed 2.01 and 2.14 M⊙ stars.
- For the highest-mass stars, 1 to 10% of the pressure in the central core is contributed by quark matter.
- The hadronic EoS is fit to normal nuclear saturation density n₀ = 0.153 fm⁻³, binding energy 16.3 MeV, Landau mass 0.83 mₙ, compressibility K = 250 MeV, and symmetry energy S = 32.5 MeV.
- The asymptotic speed of sound squared for quark matter approaches 1/3 from below, with a correction of order αₛ².

## Context

This work extends the idea of a crossover EoS, previously applied to finite-temperature QCD by matching hadronic gas to quark-gluon plasma (Ref. matchingpaper), to the zero-temperature, high-density regime relevant for neutron stars. It contrasts with earlier work (JEllis) showing that first-order phase transitions or hyperon formation soften the EoS too much to reach 2 M⊙, and with the quarkyonic matter model (quarkyonic1) which yields a second-order transition. The results enable future studies using more sophisticated hadronic EoS (including protons, electrons, muons, and hyperons) and motivate observational searches for signatures of a crossover transition in neutron-star mergers or mass-radius measurements.
