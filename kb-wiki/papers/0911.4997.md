# The Single Flavor Color Superconductivity in a Magnetic Field

> We investigate the single flavor color superconductivity in a magnetic field. Because of the absence of the electromagnetic Meissner effect, forming a nonspherical CSC phase, polar, A or planar, does not cost energy of excluding magnetic flux. We found that these nonspherical phases do occupy a significant portion of the phase diagram with respect to magnetic field and temperature and may be implemented under the typical quark density and the magnetic field inside a compact star.

## Key claim

Nonspherical single-flavor color superconducting phases (polar, A, and planar) can occupy a significant portion of the temperature-magnetic field phase diagram in compact stars because they lack the electromagnetic Meissner effect and therefore do not pay the energy cost of expelling magnetic flux, unlike the spherical color-spin locked (CSL) phase.

## Method

The authors compute the Gibbs free energy density for each pairing state (CSL, polar, A, planar) in a magnetic field, using a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL)-like effective action that captures the dominant transverse pairing channel in the ultrarelativistic limit. They minimize the free energy with respect to the magnetic field and color fields subject to constraints from the Meissner effect, then compare the free energies of different phases for two-flavor (u,d) and three-flavor (u,d,s) quark matter under charge neutrality. The phase boundaries are determined by balancing the Gibbs free energies of competing phases as functions of temperature and magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- At zero temperature, the relative pressure ratios (normalized to the CSL gap) are: CSL phase ρ_CSL(0)=1, planar phase ρ_planar(0)=0.98, polar phase ρ_polar(0)=0.88, A phase ρ_A(0)=0.65.
- The critical temperature for all single-flavor pairing states is T_c = (e^γ_E/π) Δ_0, where γ_E is the Euler-Mascheroni constant.
- For the two-flavor case (u,d): the critical magnetic field scale H_0 = 5.44 × 10^14 G and critical temperature T_C = 1.35 × 10^(-1) MeV.
- For the three-flavor case (u,d,s): H_0 = 1.97 × 10^14 G and T_C = 0.49 × 10^(-1) MeV.
- The characteristic field scale is defined as H_0 = μΔ_0/π, with μ ~ 400-500 MeV for quark matter in compact stars.

## Context

This work extends the study of single-flavor color superconductivity (CSC) by Schmitt, Wang, and Rischke (2005) and by Buballa, Dietrich, and Hošek (2006) to include the effects of a magnetic field, which is present in compact stars at strengths exceeding 10^15 G. The authors show that nonspherical phases (polar, A, planar) can compete with the previously favored CSL phase when magnetic fields are present, enabling future investigations of neutron star cooling behavior and r-mode instability as discussed by Alford, Schmitt, Rajagopal, and Schäfer (2006). The analysis also suggests connections to domain wall structures proposed by Son and Stephanov (2008) in the context of two-flavor and three-flavor CSC in magnetic fields.
