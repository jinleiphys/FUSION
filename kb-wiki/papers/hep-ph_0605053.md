# Baryon Stopping in Proton-Nucleus Collisions

> We calculate the inclusive small-x valence quark production cross section in proton-nucleus collisions at high energies. The calculation is performed in the framework of the Color Glass Condensate formalism. We consider both the case when the valence quark originates inside the nucleus and the case when it originates inside the proton. We first calculate the cross section in the quasi-classical approximation resumming the multiple rescatterings with the nucleus. Then we include the the effects of double logarithmic reggeon evolution and leading logarithmic gluon evolution in the obtained cross section. The calculated nuclear modification factor for the stopped baryons exhibits Cronin enhancement in the quasi-classical approximation and suppression at high energies/rapidities when quantum evolution corrections are included, providing a new observable which can be used to test Color Glass physics.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the inclusive small-x valence quark production cross section in proton-nucleus collisions, showing that the nuclear modification factor for stopped baryons exhibits Cronin enhancement in the quasi-classical approximation but turns into suppression at high energies/rapidities when quantum evolution corrections are included, providing a new observable for testing Color Glass Condensate dynamics.

## Method

The calculation is performed in the Color Glass Condensate formalism using the light-cone gauge of the proton (A+=0), which is equivalent to covariant gauge for the nucleus. The authors first derive the cross section in the quasi-classical approximation by resumming multiple rescatterings with the nucleus via Glauber-Mueller propagators, considering separately the cases where the valence quark originates in the proton versus in the nucleus. They then include double logarithmic reggeon evolution and leading logarithmic gluon evolution effects into the obtained cross section, following the non-linear evolution equation derived in their prior work (IKMT, 2005).

## Key numbers

* The valence quark distribution from the proton scales as ~ e^(y-Y) while valence quarks from the nucleus scale as ~ e^(-y), where Y is the total rapidity of the collision and y is the quark rapidity
* At large transverse momentum (k_T >> Q_s), the valence quark production cross section scales as ~ 1/k_T^3, compared to the ~ 1/k_T^4 scaling of inclusive gluon production
* The saturation scale for a quark dipole is Q_sq^2 = 4 pi alpha_s^2 (C_F/N_c) rho T_A(b), where rho is nuclear density and T_A(b) is nuclear thickness
* The gluon dipole saturation scale is Q_sg^2 = 4 pi alpha_s^2 rho T_A(b)
* At central rapidity (y=Y/2) and k_T << Q_sq, the nuclear modification factor R_pA approximately equals k_T^2/Q_sq^2 + O(k_T^4/Q_sq^4), indicating suppression in the saturation region
* At central rapidity and k_T >> Q_sq, R_pA approximately equals 1 + (15/8)(Q_sq^2/k_T^2) ln^2(k_T^2/(4 Lambda^2)) + ..., showing enhancement above unity

## Context

This work extends the perturbative approach to baryon stopping initiated in IKMT (2005), which first studied valence quark distributions at small Bjorken x in the quasi-classical approximation of the McLerran-Venugopalan model. The calculation follows techniques for inclusive cross sections developed in KM (2001) and KT (2003) for gluon production, and is complementary to the work of DJ (2004) which considered hard valence quark production in the fragmentation region. The prediction that small-x evolution turns Cronin enhancement into suppression at forward rapidities parallels the findings of KLM (2001), KKT (2002), and AAKSW (2002) for gluon production, which were confirmed by RHIC d+Au experiments (BRAHMS, PHOBOS, PHENIX, STAR). The results enable future work implementing fragmentation mechanisms to convert valence quarks into hadrons for comparison with RHIC data and predictions for the LHC.
