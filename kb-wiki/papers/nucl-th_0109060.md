# Monopoles and Knots in Skyrme Theory

> We show that the Skyrme theory actually is a theory of monopoles which allows a new type of solitons, the topological knots made of monopole-anti-monopole pair,which is different from the well-known skyrmions. Furthermore, we derive a generalized Skyrme action from the Yang-Mills action of QCD, which we propose to be an effective action of QCD in the infra-red limit. We discuss the physical implications of our results.

## Key claim

The Skyrme theory is actually a theory of monopoles that admits a new type of soliton, topological knots made of monopole-anti-monopole pairs, distinct from the well-known skyrmions; the author also derives a generalized Skyrme action from the Yang-Mills action of QCD, proposed as an effective action of QCD in the infra-red limit, but notes that the derivation relies on inserting a mass term for the restricted potential that violates gauge invariance until dynamical symmetry breaking via monopole condensation is assumed.

## Method

The author first shows that by setting the scalar field ξ = π in the Skyrme Lagrangian, the equation of motion reduces to the knot equation of the Skyrme-Faddeev action, proving that knots in that theory are also solutions of the Skyrme theory. He then reparametrizes the SU(2) Yang-Mills gauge potential using the Cho decomposition (also called the Cho-Faddeev-Niemi decomposition) into a restricted potential that makes a topological unit vector field n̂ covariant constant and a covariant valence potential, and by inserting a mass term for the restricted potential, derives a generalized Skyrme Lagrangian from the Yang-Mills action.

## Key numbers

* The mass of the lightest knot in Skyrme theory is estimated to be about 1.42 times the mass of the nucleon.
* The baryonic charge of skyrmions is given by Q_s = (1/24π²) ∫ ε_{ijk} tr(L_i L_j L_k) d³x.
* The knot charge is Q_k = (1/32π²) ∫ ε_{ijk} C_i H_{jk} d³x, and the monopole charge is Q_m = (1/8π) ∫ ε_{ijk} H_{ij} dσ_k.

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme theory beyond the well-known skyrmions (Skyrme 1961; Brown 1980s) by showing it also contains monopoles and knot solitons previously found in the Skyrme-Faddeev nonlinear sigma model (Faddeev 1990s). The derivation of a generalized Skyrme action from QCD via the Cho decomposition (Cho 1980; Cho 2000) builds on earlier attempts to derive the Skyrme action from QCD (Simic 1980s) and on the author's own work deriving a generalized Skyrme-Faddeev action from the one-loop effective action of QCD with a monopole background (Cho 2000; Cho 2001). The results enable a new interpretation of skyrmions as monopoles with a U(1) charge from the Hopf fiber, and suggest that the knots should be interpreted as magnetic glueballs, whose experimental search in the hadron spectrum is proposed as the next step.
