# One fluid might not rule them all

> In this proceeding, we present our recent investigations on hydrodynamic collectivity in high-multiplicity proton--proton collisions at $\sqrt{s}=$ 13 TeV using the VISHNU hybrid model with different initial condition models, called HIJING, super-MC and TRENTo. We find that with carefully tuned parameters, hydrodynamic simulations can give reasonable descriptions of the measured two-particle correlations. However, multi-particle single and mixed harmonics cumulants can not be described by hydrodynamics with these three initial conditions, even for the signs in a few cases. Further studies show that the non-linear response plays an important role in the hydrodynamic expansion of the p--p systems. Such an effect can change $c_2\{4\}$ from a negative value in the initial state to a positive value in the final state. The failure of the hydrodynamic description of multi-particle cumulant triggers the questions on whether the hydrodynamics can rule all collision systems, including p--p collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim

The authors find that while hydrodynamic simulations with three different initial condition models can reasonably describe two-particle correlations in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at 13 TeV, they fail to reproduce multi-particle cumulants (including signs in some cases), raising the question of whether hydrodynamics can describe all collision systems including proton-proton collisions at the LHC.

## Method

The authors used the iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model, which combines 2+1D viscous hydrodynamics with UrQMD hadron cascades, to simulate high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV. They implemented three different initial condition models (modified HIJING, super-MC, and TRENTo) and tuned parameters for each. The specific shear viscosity eta/s was assumed constant, while bulk viscosity, net baryon density, and heat conductivity were neglected.

## Key numbers

- The study focuses on proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV
- Hydrodynamic calculations of c2{4} (four-particle cumulant of second harmonics) are always positive regardless of initial condition model, while experimental data show negative values
- A clear deviation of v2 from linear relationship with eccentricity epsilon_2 is seen in the region of epsilon_2 > 0.45, where a cubic response becomes significant
- The non-linear response can change c2{4} from a negative value in the initial state to a positive value in the final state

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic modeling studies of small collision systems, following observations of flow phenomena in proton-proton and proton-lead collisions that challenged both the hydrodynamic model as the "standard model" in heavy-ion physics and the PYTHIA model for minimum-bias proton-proton physics. The authors note that the hydrodynamic model MUSIC with IP-Glasma initial conditions also does not generate a negative c2{4} in proton-proton collisions (Schenke 2020). The results suggest that further developments on both initial conditions and hydrodynamic modeling are necessary to determine whether hydrodynamic flow is produced in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions.
