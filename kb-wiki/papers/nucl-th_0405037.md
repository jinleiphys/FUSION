# Seniority scenario for the 68-72Zn and 66-68Ni B(E2)^ difference

> The very simple product of the number of particles by the number of holes appearing in the expression of the reduced B(E2: 0+1 -> 2+1) (B(E2)^) transition probability of even-even nuclei obtained from the extension of the seniority scheme is used to analyze in a same time the experimental B(E2)^ values of 56-68 Ni and those of 62-72Zn. The evolution of these B(E2)^ values with neutron number fits in with a scenario involving p-n interaction.

# Seniority scenario for the 68-72Zn and 66-68Ni B(E2)↑ difference

## Key claim

The difference in B(E2)↑ evolution between Ni and Zn isotopes above N=38 can be explained by a neutron sub-shell closure at N=38 (rather than a doubly magic 68Ni at N=40), combined with proton-neutron interaction effects that are suppressed in Ni by the Z=28 gap but active in Zn.

## Method

The authors apply the seniority scheme expression B(E2)↑ ∝ (number of particle pairs) × (number of hole pairs) to a composite valence shell, summing proton and neutron contributions separately. They compare experimental B(E2)↑ values for 56-68Ni and 62-72Zn with calculations using either a full 28-50 major shell or a sub-shell closure at N=38 for neutrons and at Z=40 for protons, normalizing to the Ca isotopic chain.

## Key numbers

- 68Ni B(E2)↑ value at N=40 is the lowest in the Ni series, nearly equal to the value at N=28 and N=38
- In 69Zn, the N=38 spacing (E38) is 100 keV smaller than in 67Ni, and the N=40 spacing (E40) is twice smaller than in 67Ni
- 70Ni B(E2)↑ is expected either near 400 e²fm⁴ (on the N=40 sub-shell closure curve) or around 800 e²fm⁴ (on the N=38 sub-shell closure curve)
- 68Ni B(E2)↑ lies exactly between the two calculated curves (N=38 and N=40 sub-shell closure assumptions), interpreted as a transitional point

## Context

This work extends the seniority-based analysis of B(E2)↑ systematics previously applied to Ca isotopes (Casten, 1990; Talmi, 1993) and addresses the puzzle raised by Sorlin et al. (2001, 2002) regarding the opposite B(E2)↑ behavior of Ni and Zn above N=38. The authors propose a N=38 sub-shell closure scenario that contrasts with the N=40 doubly magic interpretation of 68Ni. The analysis enables predictions for 70Ni and heavier Zn isotopes, and recent lifetime measurements by Kenn et al. (2004) for 58-64Ni are noted as potentially supporting the N=38 sub-shell closure interpretation.
