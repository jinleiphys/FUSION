# Influence of the nuclear matter equation of state on the r-mode instability using the finite-range simple effective interaction

> The characteristic physical properties of rotating neutron stars under the r-mode oscillation are evaluated using the finite-range simple effective interaction. Emphasis is given on examining the influence of the stiffness of both the symmetric and asymmetric parts of the nuclear equation of state on these properties. The amplitude of the r-mode at saturation is calculated using the data of particular neutron stars from the considerations of "spin equilibrium" and "thermal equilibrium". The upper limit of the r-mode saturation amplitude is found to lie in the range 10^{-8}-10^{-6}, in agreement with the predictions of earlier work.

## Key claim

The upper limit of the r-mode saturation amplitude for rotating neutron stars lies in the range 10^{-8} to 10^{-6}, but this result depends on the specific choices of the nuclear equation of state stiffness (both symmetric and asymmetric parts) and on the assumptions of "spin equilibrium" and "thermal equilibrium" for particular neutron star data.

## Method

The authors used the finite-range simple effective interaction (SEI) with a Gaussian form factor to construct the nuclear equation of state for neutron star matter composed of neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons in beta equilibrium. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations to obtain neutron star properties and computed the r-mode instability boundary by balancing gravitational radiation time scales against viscous damping from shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and crust-core boundary layer dissipation. The r-mode saturation amplitude was then calculated under thermal equilibrium conditions where viscous heating equals neutrino cooling, and the spin-down rate was derived from the angular momentum loss due to gravitational radiation.

## Key numbers

- The upper limit of the r-mode saturation amplitude lies in the range 10^{-8} to 10^{-6}
- For the EOS with gamma=1/2: saturation density rho_0 = 0.1571 fm^{-3}, incompressibility K(rho_0) = 245.6 MeV, symmetry energy E_s(rho_0) = 35.0 MeV, slope parameter L(rho_0) = 76.26 MeV
- For the EOS with gamma=2/3: saturation density rho_0 = 0.1552 fm^{-3}, incompressibility K(rho_0) = 262.6 MeV, symmetry energy E_s(rho_0) = 35.0 MeV, slope parameter L(rho_0) = 77.31 MeV
- The symmetry energy slope parameter L(rho_0) was varied in the range 70-110 MeV for the gamma=1/2 EOS to examine its influence
- The effective mass at saturation density is m*/m = 0.7111 for gamma=1/2 and 0.7119 for gamma=2/3

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Wen (2012), Vidana (2012), and Moustakidis (2015) that examined the influence of the symmetry energy slope parameter L on the r-mode instability boundary, but uses the finite-range simple effective interaction rather than zero-range Skyrme-type forces. The results are compared with observational data from neutron stars in low-mass X-ray binaries and millisecond radio pulsars. The study enables future work to constrain the nuclear equation of state by comparing predicted r-mode instability windows with observed neutron star spin frequencies and temperatures.
