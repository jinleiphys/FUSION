# Quark transverse spin-orbit correlations

> We extend the study of quark spin-orbit correlations in the nucleon to the case of transverse polarization. At the leading-twist level, this completes the spin structure of the quark kinetic energy-momentum tensor. In particular, we revisit the transversity decomposition of angular momentum proposed a decade ago by Burkardt and introduce a new transverse correlation, namely between quark transversity and orbital angular momentum. We also provide for the first time the Wandzura-Wilczek expression for the second Mellin moment of twist-3 transversity generalized parton distributions, along with a new sum rule. Based on lattice calculation results, we conclude that the quark transverse spin-orbit correlation is negative for both up and down flavors, just like in the longitudinal case.

## Key claim

The paper extends the study of quark spin-orbit correlations to the case of transverse polarization, introducing a new correlation between quark transversity and orbital angular momentum (OAM). Based on lattice QCD results, the authors conclude that the quark transverse spin-orbit correlation is negative for both up and down flavors, but note that this correlation involves transversity rather than transverse spin, so spherical symmetry does not require equality with the longitudinal case.

## Method

The authors decompose the asymmetric quark kinetic energy-momentum tensor into transversity states, parametrizing its non-forward matrix elements in terms of seven generalized form factors (GFFs). They relate these GFFs to specific Mellin moments of twist-2 and twist-3 tensor generalized parton distributions (GPDs) using QCD equations of motion, and derive the Wandzura-Wilczek expression for the second Mellin moment of twist-3 transversity GPDs. The transverse spin-orbit correlation is then evaluated numerically using lattice QCD results for the relevant moments and scalar charges.

## Key numbers

* Quark transverse spin-orbit correlation for up quarks: \(C^u_x \approx -3.6\), obtained using lattice QCD results at scale \(\mu^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\) from the QCDSF/UKQCD Collaboration and Abdel-Rehim et al.
* Quark transverse spin-orbit correlation for down quarks: \(C^d_x \approx -2.2\), from the same lattice data.
* Longitudinal spin-orbit correlation for comparison: \(C^u_z \approx -0.9\) and \(C^d_z \approx -0.53\) from prior work by Lorce (2014).
* Burkardt's transverse correlation from lattice data: \(\langle\delta^x J^x_u\rangle_\text{latt.} = 0.344\) and \(\langle\delta^x J^x_d\rangle_\text{latt.} = 0.104\).
* Second Mellin moment of transversity GPD \(H_T^u\) from lattice: \(\int dx\, x H_T^u = 0.268(6)\) at \(\mu^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\).
* Second Mellin moment of transversity GPD \(H_T^d\) from lattice: \(\int dx\, x H_T^d = -0.052(2)\) at \(\mu^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the study of longitudinal spin-orbit correlations by Lorce (2014) to the transverse case, completing the leading-twist spin structure of the quark kinetic energy-momentum tensor. It revisits the transversity decomposition of angular momentum proposed by Burkardt (2005, 2006), introducing a new correlation between quark transversity and OAM that differs from Burkardt's correlation between transversity and total angular momentum. The results enable future experimental extraction of these correlations from GPD measurements at facilities such as Jefferson Lab, RHIC, COMPASS, and the future Electron-Ion Collider, and provide lattice-based predictions for comparison with phenomenological models.
