# Electromagnetic transition form factors and dilepton decay rates of nucleon resonances

> Relativistic, kinematically complete phenomenological expressions for the dilepton decay rates of nucleon resonances with arbitrary spin and parity are derived in terms of the magnetic, electric, and Coulomb transition form factors. The dilepton decay rates of the nucleon resonances with masses below 2 GeV are estimated using the extended vector meson dominance model for the transition form factors. The model provides a unified description of the photo- and electroproduction data, the vector meson decays, and the dilepton decays of the nucleon resonances. The constraints on the transition form factors from the quark counting rules are taken into account. The parameters of the model are fixed by fitting the available photo- and electroproduction data and using results of the multichannel partial-wave analysis of the $πN$ scattering. Where experimental data are not available, predictions of the non-relativistic quark models are used as an input. The vector meson coupling constants of the magnetic, electric, and Coulomb types are determined. The dilepton widths and the dilepton spectra from decays of nucleon resonances with masses below 2 GeV are calculated.

## Key claim

The paper derives relativistic, kinematically complete phenomenological expressions for dilepton decay rates of nucleon resonances with arbitrary spin and parity, and provides numerical estimates for resonances with masses below 2 GeV using an extended vector meson dominance (VMD) model; however, the reliability of these estimates depends on the availability of experimental photo- and electroproduction data, and where such data are absent, predictions from non-relativistic quark models are used as input.

## Method

The authors first derive general expressions for the electromagnetic transition form factors (magnetic, electric, and Coulomb) for nucleon resonances of arbitrary spin and parity, connecting them to covariant form factors and helicity amplitudes. They then implement an extended VMD model that includes contributions from radially excited vector mesons to satisfy quark counting rules for the asymptotic behavior of form factors. The model parameters are fixed by fitting available photo- and electroproduction data and using results from multichannel partial-wave analysis of πN scattering, with non-relativistic quark model predictions filling gaps where experimental data are unavailable. Finally, the dilepton decay widths and spectra for nucleon resonances below 2 GeV are calculated from the fitted form factors.

## Key numbers

- The dilepton decay rates are calculated for nucleon resonances with masses below 2 GeV.
- The fine-structure constant α = 1/137 is used in the electromagnetic coupling.
- The photon energy for real photons is given by ω₀ = (m*² − m²)/(2m*), where m* is the resonance mass and m is the nucleon mass.
- The extended VMD model includes ground-state ρ, ω, and φ mesons plus radially excited vector mesons (ρ′, ρ′′, etc.) to achieve dipole behavior for Sachs form factors, as required by quark counting rules.

## Context

This work extends the same extended VMD philosophy previously applied to meson decays in Faessler (2000), which successfully predicted the φ → η e⁺ e⁻ and η → π⁺ π⁻ e⁺ e⁻ decay rates later confirmed by the CMD-2 Collaboration (Akhmetshin, 2001). The paper addresses the discrepancy noted in Faessler (2000) that standard VMD overestimates photon branching ratios of nucleon resonances, and provides a unified description of photo- and electroproduction data, vector meson decays (N* → Nρ(ω)), and dilepton decays (N* → Nℓ⁺ℓ⁻). The results enable improved modeling of dilepton production in proton-proton and heavy-ion collisions, particularly relevant for interpreting data from the DLS experiment at BEVALAC and the future HADES experiment at GSI.
