# Valence parton distribution function of pion from fine lattice

> We present a lattice QCD study of valence parton distribution inside the pion within the framework of Large Momentum Effective Theory. We use a mixed action approach with 1-HYP smeared valence Wilson clover quarks on 2+1 flavor HISQ sea with the valence quark mass tuned to 300 MeV pion mass. We use $48^3 \times 64$ lattice at a fine lattice spacing $a=0.06$ fm for this computation. We renormalize the quasi-PDF matrix element in the non-perturbative RI-MOM scheme. As a byproduct, we test the validity of 1-loop matching procedure by comparing the RI-MOM renormalized quasi-PDF matrix element with off-shell quark external states as computed in the continuum 1-loop perturbation theory with the lattice results at $a=0.04$ and 0.06 fm. By applying the RI-MOM to ${\bar{\rm MS}}$ one-loop matching, implemented through a fit to phenomenologically motivated PDFs, we obtain the valence PDF of pion.

## Key claim

The paper presents a lattice QCD calculation of the pion valence parton distribution function (PDF) using the Large Momentum Effective Theory (LaMET) framework, but the result is obtained through a fit to phenomenologically motivated PDFs rather than a direct model-independent extraction, and the authors explicitly note that the methodology's feasibility depends on assumptions about higher-twist corrections and perturbative matching that are still being tested.

## Method

The authors used a mixed action approach with 1-HYP smeared valence Wilson clover quarks on 2+1 flavor HISQ sea configurations at a lattice spacing of 0.06 fm and volume 48^3 x 64, with valence quark mass tuned to a pion mass of 300 MeV. They computed the quasi-PDF matrix element for the pion at momenta up to 2.15 GeV using boosted Gaussian sources, extracted the bare matrix elements from three-point functions using two-state and three-state fits as well as the summation method, and renormalized the quasi-PDF operator in the non-perturbative RI-MOM scheme. The renormalized quasi-PDF was then matched to the MSbar PDF using one-loop perturbative matching, implemented through a fit to phenomenologically motivated PDFs.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.06 fm (primary ensemble) and a = 0.04 fm (for renormalization study)
- Lattice volume: 48^3 x 64
- Pion mass: m_pi = 300 MeV (valence), 160 MeV (sea)
- Pion momenta studied: P_z = 0, 0.43, 0.86, 1.29, 1.72, and 2.15 GeV
- Source-sink separations used for three-point functions: t = 8a, 10a, and 12a (0.48, 0.60, and 0.72 fm)
- Number of gauge configurations: 216 for |z|/a in [0,8], 100 for (8,16], and 48 for (16,24]
- The ratio of gamma_z to gamma_t matrix elements at z=0 shows 3-4% deviations from the expected P_z/E_pi(P_z) relation at small P_z

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD approach to PDFs using LaMET, following the methodology developed by Ji (2013) and the renormalization strategy outlined by Chen (2017) and Liu (2018). It uses finer lattices (a=0.06 fm) than previous qPDF studies and focuses on the pion as a test case because its smaller mass makes it easier to achieve large boosts and suppress target mass corrections. The results enable future comparisons with phenomenological pion PDFs from analyses by Aicher (2010) and others, and help assess the validity of the one-loop matching procedure by comparing RI-MOM renormalized quasi-PDF matrix elements with continuum perturbation theory at two lattice spacings (0.04 and 0.06 fm).
