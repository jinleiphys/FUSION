# Mean field and two-body nuclear effects in inclusive electron scattering on argon, carbon and titanium: the superscaling approach

> We compare the predictions of the SuSAv2 model including two-particle two-hole meson-exchange currents with the recent JLab data for inclusive electron scattering on three different targets (C, Ar and Ti). The agreement is very good over the full energy spectrum, with some discrepancy seen only in the deep inelastic region. The 2p2h response, peaked in the dip region between the quasielastic and $Δ$-resonance peak, is essential to reproduce the data. We also analyze the $k_F$ (Fermi momentum) dependence of the data in terms of scaling of second kind, showing that the 2p2h response scales very differently from the quasielastic one, in full accord with what is predicted by the model. The results represent a valuable test of the applicability of the model to neutrino scattering processes on different nuclei.

## Key claim

The SuSAv2-MEC model, which combines mean-field and two-body meson-exchange current contributions, reproduces JLab inclusive electron scattering data on carbon, titanium, and argon with very good agreement over the full energy spectrum, except for some discrepancy in the deep inelastic region at the highest energy transfers.

## Method

The authors compared predictions of the SuSAv2 model, which uses a blending of Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) and Relativistic Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (RPWIA) descriptions, with two-particle two-hole (2p2h) meson-exchange currents added, against JLab inclusive electron scattering data for carbon, titanium, and argon at a beam energy of 2.222 GeV and scattering angle 15.541 degrees. They also extracted the superscaling function from the data and analyzed its dependence on the Fermi momentum to test scaling of second kind for both the quasielastic and 2p2h regimes.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momenta used: 0.228 GeV/c for carbon, 0.241 GeV/c for titanium and argon.
- Beam energy: E = 2.222 GeV; scattering angle: theta = 15.541 degrees.
- The RMF model is reliable up to knock-out nucleon kinetic energy T_N of about 150 MeV; for higher momenta (>= 500 MeV) the RPWIA description is used.
- The 2p2h response scales as k_F^3, confirmed by the data collapsing into a single curve when the superscaling function is divided by eta_F^3 (where eta_F = k_F/m_N) in the dip region.

## Context

This work extends the SuSAv2-MEC model, previously validated for symmetric nuclei like carbon and oxygen (Megias 2016), to asymmetric nuclei with non-zero isospin (titanium and argon) for the first time. The results confirm the scaling predictions of Amaro (2017) that the 2p2h response scales as k_F^3, and demonstrate the model's applicability to neutrino scattering processes relevant for experiments such as DUNE, T2K, and MicroBooNE. The model is being implemented in Monte Carlo event generators including NEUT, GENIE, and NuWro for use in neutrino oscillation analyses.
