# The internal composition of proto-neutron stars under strong magnetic fields

> In this work, we study the effects of magnetic fields and rotation on the structure and composition of proto-neutron stars (PNS's). A hadronic chiral SU(3) model is applied to cold neutron stars (NS) and proto-neutron stars with trapped neutrinos and at fixed entropy per baryon. We obtain general relativistic solutions for neutron and proto-neutron stars endowed with a poloidal magnetic field by solving Einstein-Maxwell field equations in a self-consistent way. As the neutrino chemical potential decreases in value over time, this alters the chemical equilibrium and the composition inside the star, leading to a change in the structure and in the particle population of these objects. We find that the magnetic field deforms the star and significantly alters the number of trapped neutrinos in the stellar interior, together with strangeness content and temperature in each evolution stage.

## Key claim
Strong magnetic fields (central values up to ~10^18 G) significantly alter the internal composition of proto-neutron stars by reducing central baryon density, which can suppress strangeness content entirely, while also modifying the number of trapped neutrinos and temperature distribution at each evolutionary stage.

## Method
The authors used a hadronic chiral SU(3) model to compute equations of state for cold neutron stars and proto-neutron stars with trapped neutrinos and fixed entropy per baryon (sB=1 and sB=2). They solved the coupled Einstein-Maxwell field equations in a self-consistent way using the LORENE C++ library with a pseudo-spectral method, assuming stationary, axisymmetric poloidal magnetic fields and fixing the stellar baryonic mass to MB=2.35 solar masses.

## Key numbers
- Central magnetic fields up to 1.10 x 10^18 G were obtained for the highest current function f0=39000 (for the sB=2, YL=0.4 case)
- Surface magnetic fields reached up to 0.47 x 10^18 G for the same configuration
- The maximum gravitational mass of cold beta-equilibrated matter with hyperons is 2.08 solar masses (baryon mass MB=2.41 solar masses)
- Without hyperons, the gravitational mass reaches 2.14 solar masses (baryon mass MB=2.50 solar masses)
- For the cold neutron star (T=0) with f0=39000, central baryon density dropped to 0.424 fm^-3 from 0.694 fm^-3 in the unmagnetized case
- The equatorial coordinate radius for the cold unmagnetized star was req=9.13 km, with circular radius Rcirc=12.37 km

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous general-relativistic study of magnetized hybrid stars (Franzon et al., 2016) to hot proto-neutron stars with trapped neutrinos, building on the formalism developed by Bonazzola (1993) and Bocquet (1995). It contrasts with earlier work by Pons (2001) on quark effects in proto-neutron star evolution and by Thompson and Duncan (1992) on dynamo-generated magnetic fields. The results enable future studies of phase transitions from quark to hadronic matter in magnetized proto-neutron stars and predictions for neutrino signals detectable by next-generation neutrino detectors.
