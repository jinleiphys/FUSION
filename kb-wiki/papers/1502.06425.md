# Microscopic analysis of $^{10,11}$Be elastic scattering on protons and nuclei and breakup processes of $^{11}$Be within the $^{10}$Be+$n$ cluster model

> The density distributions of $^{10}$Be and $^{11}$Be nuclei obtained within the quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) model and the generator coordinate method (GCM) are used to calculate the microscopic optical potentials (OPs) and cross sections of elastic scattering of these nuclei on protons and $^{12}$C at energies $E<100$ MeV/nucleon. The real part of the OP is calculated using the folding model with the exchange terms included, while the imaginary part of the OP that reproduces the phase of scattering is obtained in the high-energy approximation (HEA). In this hybrid model of OP the free parameters are the depths of the real and imaginary parts obtained by fitting the experimental data. The well known energy dependence of the volume integrals is used as a physical constraint to resolve the ambiguities of the parameter values. The role of the spin-orbit potential and the surface contribution to the OP is studied for an adequate description of available experimental elastic scattering cross section data. Also, the cluster model, in which $^{11}$Be consists of a $n$-halo and the $^{10}$Be core, is adopted. Within the latter, the breakup cross sections of $^{11}$Be nucleus on $^{9}$Be, $^{93}$Nb, $^{181}$Ta, and $^{238}$U targets and momentum distributions of $^{10}$Be fragments are calculated and compared with the existing experimental data.

## Key claim

The hybrid optical model, combining a folded real potential with an imaginary part from the high-energy approximation, can describe elastic scattering of the halo nucleus $^{11}$Be and its core $^{10}$Be on protons and $^{12}$C at energies below 100 MeV/nucleon, but only when both spin-orbit and surface imaginary terms are included to account for the halo structure and breakup effects.

## Method

The real part of the optical potential was calculated using the folding model with the CDM3Y6 effective Paris nucleon-nucleon interaction, including direct and exchange terms, using density distributions of $^{10}$Be from the quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) model and of $^{10,11}$Be from the generator coordinate method (GCM). The imaginary part was obtained from the high-energy approximation (HEA) using nuclear densities and averaged nucleon-nucleon cross sections. The depths of the real, imaginary, spin-orbit, and surface imaginary components were fitted to elastic scattering data, with the energy dependence of volume integrals used as a physical constraint to resolve ambiguities. For breakup calculations, $^{11}$Be was treated as a $^{10}$Be core plus a halo neutron in a cluster model, and the momentum distributions of $^{10}$Be fragments were computed.

## Key numbers

- Neutron separation energy of $^{11}$Be: $S_n = 504 \pm 6$ keV from the ground $s_{1/2}$ state.
- Elastic scattering data for $^{10}$Be+p at 39.1 and 59.4 MeV/nucleon (Lapoux 2008, Cortina-Gil 1997) and for $^{11}$Be+p at 38.4 and 49.3 MeV/nucleon (Lapoux 2008, Cortina-Gil 1997).
- For $^{10}$Be+p at 39.1 MeV/nucleon with GCM density, including both $ls$ and surface terms: $N_R = 0.995$, $N_I = 0.266$, $J_V = 394.161$ MeV.fm$^3$, $J_W^{(b)} = 122.321$ MeV.fm$^3$, total reaction cross section $\sigma_R = 298.65$ mb.
- For $^{11}$Be+p at 38.4 MeV/nucleon with GCM density, including both $ls$ and surface terms: $N_R = 0.849$, $N_I = 0.106$, $J_V = 349.685$ MeV.fm$^3$, $J_W^{(b)} = 269.903$ MeV.fm$^3$, $\sigma_R = 493.01$ mb.
- For $^{10}$Be+p at low proton energies 7.5 and 10.7 MeV (Schmitt 2013), with GCM density and $ls$ term: $N_R = 2.287$ and $2.232$, respectively; $J_V = 1215.283$ and $1144.742$ MeV.fm$^3$.
- The average distance between the halo neutron and the $^{10}$Be core in $^{11}$Be is around 7 fm, while the core radius is 2.5 fm (Nortershauser 2009).

## Context

This work extends the hybrid optical model approach previously applied to exotic He and Li isotopes (Lukyanov 2007, 2009, 2010, 2013) to the Be isotopic chain, using realistic microscopic densities from QMC (Pieper 2002) and GCM (Descouvemont 1997). It improves upon earlier phenomenological optical potential calculations for $^{10,11}$Be+p scattering (Cortina-Gil 1997) and folding model studies (Farag 2012, 2014, 2014a) by including both spin-orbit and surface imaginary terms, and by using the energy dependence of volume integrals as a constraint. The breakup calculations for $^{11}$Be on various targets (Kelley 1995) enable tests of the cluster model description of the halo structure and can be compared with future measurements of momentum distributions.
