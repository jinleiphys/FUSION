# Magicity versus superfluidity around $^{28}$O viewed from the study of $^{30}$F

> The neutron-rich unbound fluorine isotope $^{30}$F$_{21}$ has been observed for the first time by measuring its neutron decay at the SAMURAI spectrometer (RIBF, RIKEN) in the quasi-free proton knockout reaction of $^{31}$Ne nuclei at 235 MeV/nucleon. The mass and thus one-neutron-separation energy of $^{30}$F has been determined to be $S_n = -472\pm 58 \mathrm{(stat.)} \pm 33 \mathrm{(sys.)}$ keV from the measurement of its invariant-mass spectrum. The absence of a sharp drop in $S_n$($^{30}$F) shows that the ``magic'' $N=20$ shell gap is not restored close to $^{28}$O, which is in agreement with our shell-model calculations that predict a near degeneracy between the neutron $d$ and $fp$ orbitals, with the $1p_{3/2}$ and $1p_{1/2}$ orbitals becoming more bound than the $0f_{7/2}$ one. This degeneracy and reordering of orbitals has two potential consequences: $^{28}$O behaves like a strongly superfluid nucleus with neutron pairs scattering across shells, and both $^{29,31}$F appear to be good two-neutron halo-nucleus candidates.

## Key claim

The one-neutron-separation energy of $^{30}$F is $S_n = -472 \pm 58 \text{(stat.)} \pm 33 \text{(sys.)}$ keV, and the absence of a sharp drop in this value after $N=20$ shows that the "magic" $N=20$ shell gap is not restored close to $^{28}$O, contrary to what might be expected from the behavior of heavier isotones such as phosphorus.

## Method

The neutron-unbound isotope $^{30}$F was produced via quasi-free proton knockout from a $^{31}$Ne beam at 235 MeV/nucleon on a liquid hydrogen target at the SAMURAI spectrometer (RIBF, RIKEN). The invariant-mass spectrum of the $^{29}$F + n decay products was reconstructed from the four-momenta of the fragment and neutron, and the resonance energy was extracted by fitting a single-level Breit-Wigner line shape plus a broad unresolved component, folded with the experimental response matrix from Geant4 simulations.

## Key numbers

- One-neutron-separation energy of $^{30}$F: $S_n = -472 \pm 58 \text{(stat.)} \pm 33 \text{(sys.)}$ keV, extracted from the resonance energy $E^r_{fn} = 472 \pm 58 \text{(stat.)} \pm 33 \text{(sys.)}$ keV in the $^{29}$F + n relative-energy spectrum.
- Resonance width: $\Gamma^r = 477^{+358}_{-177} \text{(stat.)}$ keV (poorly constrained due to unresolved contributions).
- One-neutron detection efficiency of the total array: $\varepsilon_n = 54\%$ for decay energies below 1 MeV, decreasing to $30\%$ at 3.5 MeV.
- Shell-model predicted $S_n$ for $^{30}$F is $-892$ keV, which is 420 keV lower than the experimental value.
- Shell-model predicted $S_{2n}$ values: 776 keV for $^{29}$F (compared to experimental $1130(540)$ keV) and 32 keV for $^{31}$F.
- Neutron occupancy in $^{29}$F from shell model: $0f_{7/2}$ orbital occupied by 0.2 neutrons, $1p_{3/2}$ orbital by 0.8 neutrons; in $^{31}$F: $0f_{7/2}$ by 0.8, $1p_{3/2}$ by 2.1 neutrons.

## Context

This work extends the study of neutron-rich fluorine isotopes beyond $N=20$, following prior measurements of $^{27,28}$F by Revel (2020) and the observation of a low-lying $1/2^+$ state in $^{29}$F by Doornenbal (2017). The finding that the $N=20$ shell gap is not restored near $^{28}$O contradicts the behavior seen in phosphorus isotopes (where a $4.915(13)$ MeV gap is observed) and supports the interpretation that $^{28}$O behaves as a strongly superfluid nucleus with neutron pairs scattering across shells. The results enable future searches for a transition to a BEC-like superfluid phase in the fluorine chain and for two-neutron halo structures in $^{29}$F and $^{31}$F, as previously suggested by Bagchi (2020), Fortunato (2020), and Singh (2022).
