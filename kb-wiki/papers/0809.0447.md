# Further explorations of Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mass formulas. IX: Constraint of pairing force to $^1S_0$ neutron-matter gap

> In this latest of our series of Skyrme-HFB mass models, HFB-16, we introduce the new feature of requiring that the contact pairing force reproduce at each density the $^1S_0$ pairing gap of neutron matter as determined in microscopic calculations with realistic nucleon-nucleon forces. We retain the earlier constraints on the Skyrme force of reproducing the energy-density curve of neutron matter, and of having an isoscalar effective mass of $0.8M$ in symmetric infinite nuclear matter at the saturation density; we also keep the recently adopted device of dropping Coulomb exchange. Furthermore, the correction term for the spurious energy of collective motion has a form that is known to favour fission barriers that are in good agreement with experiment. Despite the extra constraints on the effective force, we have achieved a better fit to the mass data than any other mean field model, the rms error on the 2149 measured masses of nuclei with $N$ and $Z \ge$ 8 having been reduced to 0.632 MeV; the improvement is particularly striking for the most neutron-rich nuclei. Moreover, it turns out that even with no flexibility at all remaining for the pairing force, the spectral pairing gaps that we find suggest that level densities in good agreement with experiment should be obtained. This new force is thus particularly well-suited for astrophysical applications, such as stellar nucleosynthesis and neutron-star crusts.

## Key claim

The authors construct a new Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mass model (HFB-16) that imposes the additional constraint that the contact pairing force exactly reproduce the $^1S_0$ pairing gap of neutron matter from microscopic calculations, while retaining all previous constraints on the Skyrme force; despite this extra constraint, the model achieves a better fit to measured nuclear masses than any other mean-field model, with the improvement being particularly striking for the most neutron-rich nuclei.

## Method

The authors solve the HFB equations in uniform neutron matter at each density to determine the strength of the contact pairing force that reproduces the microscopic $^1S_0$ pairing gap from the Argonne $v_{14}$ interaction in the BCS approximation, using Eq. (3.6) to compute $v^{\pi q}[\rho_q]$ from the effective mass and the target gap. The Skyrme force parameters are fitted to the 2149 measured masses from the 2003 Atomic Mass Evaluation, while simultaneously constraining the force to reproduce the energy-density curve of neutron matter and an isoscalar effective mass of $0.8M$ in symmetric infinite nuclear matter at saturation density, and dropping Coulomb exchange. The proton pairing strength is taken as the neutron pairing strength multiplied by a constant density-independent fitting parameter.

## Key numbers

- RMS deviation of 0.632 MeV on the 2149 measured masses of nuclei with $N$ and $Z \ge 8$ from the 2003 Atomic Mass Evaluation
- Isoscalar effective mass $M_s^*/M = 0.8$ in symmetric infinite nuclear matter at saturation density
- Coefficient $b = 0.8$ in the collective correction term of Eq. (4.2), matching the value found for model HFB-14
- Previous best fit (model HFB-8) had RMS deviation 0.635 MeV on the same 2149 masses
- For comparison, the SLy4 force with its pairing prescription gives RMS deviation 5.1 MeV for the subset of even-even nuclei

## Context

This work extends the authors' series of Skyrme-HFB mass models (HFB-1 through HFB-15) by adding the constraint that the pairing force reproduce microscopic neutron-matter pairing gaps, following the scheme proposed by Duguet (2004). Previous models in the series had introduced constraints on the effective mass (starting with HFB-9), the neutron-matter energy-density curve (HFB-9), level densities (HFB-12), fission barriers (HFB-14), and dropping Coulomb exchange (HFB-15). The new model enables reliable investigations of superfluid phases in neutron-star inner crusts and improves confidence in extrapolations to neutron-rich nuclei relevant for the r-process of stellar nucleosynthesis and neutron-star crust composition.
