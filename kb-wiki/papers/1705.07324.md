# Time-dependent mean-field investigations of the quasifission process

> We demonstrate that the microscopic Time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory provides an important approach to shed light on the nuclear dynamics leading to the formation of superheavy elements. In particular, we discuss studying quasifission dynamics and calculating ingredients for compound nucleus formation probability calculations. We also discuss possible extensions to TDHF to address the distribution of observables.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory, a fully microscopic approach, can provide quantitative insights into quasifission dynamics that are essential for understanding superheavy element formation, but the approach is limited to mean values of observables and requires extensions such as the stochastic mean-field method to address distributions of fragment properties.

## Method

The authors performed TDHF calculations on a 3D Cartesian grid without symmetry restrictions for heavy-ion collisions, including reactions such as 50Ti+249Bk, 40Ca+238U, and 54Cr+186W. They extracted capture cross-sections using the density-constrained TDHF (DC-TDHF) method, computed fragment total kinetic energies (TKE), mass-angle distributions (MAD), and contact times, and calculated time-dependent moments of inertia by diagonalizing the inertia tensor from the TDHF density. For fluctuations beyond mean field, they employed the stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach to compute transport coefficients and fragment distributions in the neutron-proton plane.

## Key numbers

- Contact time for 50Ti+249Bk at E_c.m.=233 MeV and impact parameter b=1.0 fm is 16 zs.
- For 40Ca+238U, TKE predictions from TDHF agree with Viola systematics for central collisions at E_c.m./V_B=1.0-1.1.
- For 48Ca+249Bk non-central collisions at E_c.m.=218 MeV, the moment-of-inertia ratio Im_0/Im_eff at the final touching configuration ranges from 1.4 to 1.8.
- For 40Ca+238U at E_cm=202 MeV, the mean neutron and proton numbers at the exit channel are (N=125, Z=82) and (N=137, Z=86) for target-like fragments.
- For 40Ca+238U at E_c.m./V_B=1.142, and 54Cr+186W at E_c.m./V_B=1.130, TDHF calculations reproduce experimental mass-angle distributions.

## Context

This work extends the program of applying TDHF to heavy-ion fusion and quasifission that has been developed by groups including Simenel (2012), Nakatsukasa (2016), and others, building on comparisons with experimental data from Wakhle (2014) and Hammerton (2015). The paper enables future calculations of compound nucleus formation probability (P_CN) from first principles, and the SMF extension provides a framework for computing fragment distributions that goes beyond the mean-field approximation, addressing limitations noted by Dasso (1979) and Simenel (2011) regarding underestimated fragment mass distributions in dissipative collisions.
