# Salty Water Cerenkov Detectors

> The addition of certain solutes to a water Cerenkov detector will introduce new charge-current channels for the detection of $ν_e$s. The experimental conditions necessary to exploit such signals - large volumes and very low backgrounds - should be reached for the first time in SuperKamiokande and SNO. I compare some of the more attractive possibilities ($^7$Li, $^{11}$B, $^{35}$Cl) in terms of counting rate, reliability of the cross section, specificity of signal, and potential for distinguishing competing solutions to the solar neutrino puzzle.

## Key claim
Adding certain solutes to large water Cerenkov detectors like SuperKamiokande and SNO could introduce new charge-current channels for detecting electron neutrinos, but the feasibility depends critically on achieving the extraordinary fiducial volumes and radiopurities these detectors are expected to reach for the first time.

## Method
The paper compares three candidate solutes (lithium-7, boron-11, and chlorine-35) as neutrino targets in water Cerenkov detectors, evaluating each on counting rate, cross section reliability, signal specificity, and ability to distinguish between competing solar neutrino solutions. For each candidate, the author calculates expected event rates assuming a 22 kiloton fiducial volume (SuperKamiokande), a reduced boron-8 neutrino flux of 3 × 10^6/cm^2 sec, and specific solute concentrations, then simulates how the charge-current signals would compare with ν_x - e elastic scattering under four different oscillation scenarios.

## Key numbers
- For a 5% LiOH solution in SuperKamiokande: total event rate of 8480/year for 8B ν_e flux of 3 × 10^6/cm^2 sec, with 87.4% detected above a 5 MeV threshold
- The 7Li(ν_e, e-)7Be cross section for a normalized 8B neutrino spectrum is known to about 1%, with σ(8B) = 3.50 × 10^{-42} cm^2
- For a 5% H3BO3 solution: total event rate of 1430/year, with 60% detected above 5 MeV threshold; total cross section σ(8B) = 1.41 × 10^{-42} cm^2
- For a 10% NH4Cl solution: 400 events/year total, with 109 detected events/year after accounting for detection efficiencies of 43.7% (prompt electron) and 62.2% (coincident positron); cross section σ(8B) = 2.23 × 10^{-43} cm^2
- The 35Ar β+ decay has a half-life of 1.775 sec and maximum positron energy of 5.454 MeV
- The 7Li angular distribution parameter α(E_e) is approximately -0.02 ± 0.02 for electron energies between 5 and 10 MeV
- The 11B angular distribution parameter α(E_e) = 0.31 + 0.063(E_e - 7.5) where E_e is in MeV, giving σ(0°)/σ(180°) ~ 1.9 at E_e = 7.5 MeV
- The 35Cl prompt electron angular distribution has α(E_e) ~ 0.86

## Context
This paper extends the discussion of using deuterium as a neutrino target in SNO to other light mirror nuclei with T=1/2, building on earlier work from the Borex proposal (a predecessor to Borexino) which discussed boron as a solar neutrino target, and from the SUNLAB project which investigated concentrated LiCl solutions. The proposed solute signals would allow SuperKamiokande to compare electron and heavy-flavor neutrino signals as a function of energy, a capability not present in either SuperKamiokande or SNO as originally designed, since SNO's neutral current signal from deuterium breakup measures only an integrated rate. The analysis demonstrates that 7Li data could distinguish between the small-angle MSW solution and the large-angle flavor oscillation solution at confidence levels of approximately 8σ, 6σ, and 4σ using data above 10, 11, and 12 MeV respectively.
