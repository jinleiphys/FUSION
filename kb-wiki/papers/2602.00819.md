# Spin alignment, tensor polarizabilities, and local equilibrium for spin-1 particles

> Different bases for the spin-1 density matrix are discussed to clarify the connection between its components and observables measured in heavy-ion collisions. The theoretical advantage of using the adjoint representation for spin matrices is emphasized. Next, the equilibrium spin density matrix and the corresponding Wigner function are introduced. With appropriate definitions of the energy-momentum and spin tensors, this framework allows for the formulation of perfect spin hydrodynamics in the same way as previously done for spin-1/2 particles. Together, these results provide a unified description of spin-1/2 and spin-1 particles.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a local equilibrium state for spin-1 particles can be defined analogously to spin-1/2 particles, where the spin part of the total angular momentum is conserved separately, and that this equilibrium spin density matrix uniquely determines both the spin polarization vector and the tensor polarizabilities, thereby establishing a connection between hyperon and vector-meson measurements without requiring dissipation.

## Method

The authors formulate the spin-1 density matrix in the adjoint representation using spin operators defined by (S^i)_{jk} = -i epsilon_{ijk}, which directly expresses the antisymmetric part as the spin polarization vector and the symmetric part as tensor polarizabilities. They then construct a Lorentz-covariant spin density matrix and Wigner function for a relativistic gas of spin-1 particles described by the Proca field, using the canonical boost to connect particle rest frame quantities to laboratory frame quantities. The local equilibrium spin density matrix is derived from an exponential form exp[alphav dot S] normalized by its trace, and the resulting energy-momentum and spin tensors are obtained through integration over momentum space with the Maxwell-Juttner distribution function.

## Key numbers

- The spin density matrix in the adjoint representation in the particle rest frame is given by rho^S_* = (1/3)[1 + (3/2) P^i_* S^i + sqrt(3/2) T^{ij}_* (S^i S^j + S^j S^i)], where P^i_* are spin polarization components and T^{ij}_* are tensor polarizabilities
- The equilibrium spin polarization vector magnitude is |P_*| = 2 sinh(alpha)/(2 cosh alpha + 1), where alpha = |alphav| is the magnitude of the angular velocity parameter
- The spin alignment observable A = rho_{00} - 1/3 = -sqrt(2/3) T^{22}_*, which for local equilibrium becomes A = -(3(hat_alpha_2)^2 - 1)(cosh alpha - 1)/(3(2 cosh alpha + 1))
- For small sqrt(-a^2), the polarization per particle for spin-1 is dDelta Pi_mu/dDelta N = (4/3) a_mu, while for large sqrt(-a^2) it approaches a_mu/sqrt(-a^2), bounded by 1 (compared to 1/2 for spin-1/2)

## Context

This work extends the spin hydrodynamics framework previously developed for spin-1/2 particles by Florkowski (2017) and Bhadury (2025) to spin-1 particles, providing a unified description of both types. The authors build on the spin density matrix formalism from Leader (2001) and the Wigner function approach from Weickgenannt (2022), while contrasting their local equilibrium picture with the coalescence model of Xia (2020) that previously connected hyperon and vector-meson measurements. The results enable the construction of perfect spin hydrodynamics incorporating both spin-1/2 and spin-1 particles, and establish that non-trivial spin alignment can occur in local equilibrium without requiring dissipative effects.
