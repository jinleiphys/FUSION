# Transport equations and linear response of superfluid Fermi mixtures in neutron stars

> We study transport properties of a strongly interacting superfluid mixture of two Fermi-liquids. A typical example of such matter is the neutron-proton liquid in the cores of neutron stars. To describe the mixture, we employ the Landau theory of Fermi-liquids, generalized to allow for the effects of superfluidity. We formulate the kinetic equation and analyze linear response of the system to vector (e.g., electromagnetic) perturbation. In particular, we calculate the transverse and longitudinal polarization functions for both liquid components. We demonstrate, that they can be expressed through the Landau parameters of the mixture and polarization functions of noninteracting matter (when the Landau quasiparticle interaction is neglected). Our results can be used, e.g., for studies of the kinetic coefficients and low-frequency long-wavelength collective modes in superfluid Fermi-mixtures.

## Key claim

The paper derives a collisionless kinetic equation for a strongly interacting superfluid mixture of two Fermi-liquids (such as the neutron-proton liquid in neutron star cores) and shows that the transverse and longitudinal polarization functions for both components can be expressed through the Landau parameters of the mixture and the polarization functions of noninteracting matter, but only under the simplifying assumption that both particle species pair in the spin-singlet \(^1\)S\(_0\) state (whereas neutrons in neutron star cores are believed to pair in the spin-triplet \(^3\)P\(_2\) state).

## Method

The author generalizes the Landau theory of Fermi-liquids to allow for superfluidity in a two-component mixture, formulating a kinetic equation for the matrix deviation of the quasiparticle distribution function from equilibrium. The system of four coupled integral equations is simplified by introducing new variables (\(\delta n_{\pm}\), \(\delta s_{\pm}\)) and solved in the collisionless approximation to calculate the linear response to a vector (electromagnetic) perturbation, yielding the longitudinal and transverse polarization functions for each component.

## Key numbers

* Neutron star radius \(R \sim 10\) km and mass \(M \sim M_\odot\) with \(M_\odot \approx 1.99 \times 10^{33}\) g.
* Nuclear saturation density \(\rho_0 \approx 2.8 \times 10^{14}\) g cm\(^{-3}\).
* Superfluid transition temperatures for neutrons and protons are \(T \lesssim (10^8-10^{10})\) K.
* The Fermi momentum for species \(i\) is \(n_i = p_{{\rm F}i}^3 / (3\pi^2)\).
* The effective mass relation is \(m_i / m_i^\ast = 1 - \sum_j (m_j G_{ij}) / n_i\) with \(G_{ij} \equiv (1/9\pi^4) p_{{\rm F}i}^2 p_{{\rm F}j}^2 f_1^{ij}\).

## Context

This work extends the one-component superfluid Fermi-liquid response calculations of Leinson (2009) and the earlier formalism of Leggett (1965, 1966) to the case of a strongly interacting two-component mixture, addressing a gap in the literature where prior studies (e.g., by Kolomeitsev and Voskresensky, 2008) neglected neutron-proton interactions. The derived kinetic equation and polarization functions enable future studies of kinetic coefficients, low-frequency long-wavelength collective modes, and neutrino emission processes (such as Cooper-pairing and bremsstrahlung) in superfluid neutron star cores.
