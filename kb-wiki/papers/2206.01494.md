# New insights into the oscillation of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors

> The electromagnetic form factors of the proton and the neutron in the timelike region are investigated. Electron-positron annihilation into antinucleon-nucleon ($\bar NN$) pairs is treated in distorted wave Born approximation, including the final-state interaction in the $\bar NN$ system. The latter is obtained by a Lippmann-Schwinger equation for $\bar{N}N$ potentials derived within SU(3) chiral effective field theory. By fitting to the phase shifts and (differential) cross section data, a high quality description is achieved. With these amplitudes, the oscillations of the electromagnetic form factors of the proton and the neutron are studied. It is found that each of them can be described by two fractional oscillators. One is characterized as \lq overdamped' and dominates near the threshold, while the other is \lq underdamped' and plays an important role in the high-energy region. These two oscillators are essential to understand the distributions of polarized electric charges induced by hard photons for the nucleons.

## Key claim

The oscillatory behavior of the proton and neutron electromagnetic form factors in the timelike region can be described by two fractional oscillators, one "overdamped" dominating near threshold and one "underdamped" dominating at higher energies, with the phase delay between proton and neutron oscillations confirmed.

## Method

The authors treat electron-positron annihilation into antinucleon-nucleon pairs in distorted wave Born approximation, including final-state interactions in the antinucleon-nucleon system obtained by solving a Lippmann-Schwinger equation for potentials derived within SU(3) chiral effective field theory up to next-to-leading order. The low-energy constants and bare electromagnetic vertices are fixed by fitting to antinucleon-nucleon phase shifts and scattering lengths, as well as to electron-positron annihilation cross sections and electromagnetic form factor data. The resulting form factors from threshold to 2.2 GeV are combined with experimental data up to 3.2 GeV and fitted with fractional oscillation functions using Mittag-Leffler functions.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff parameter for the Lippmann-Schwinger equation regulator is Lambda = 850 MeV, with values explored in the range 750-950 MeV
- The damping factor for the overdamped oscillator is alpha_1^p = 1.263 +/- 0.002 for the proton and alpha_1^n = 1.060 +/- 0.001 for the neutron
- The damping factor for the underdamped oscillator is alpha_2 = 1.880 +/- 0.001 for both proton and neutron
- The oscillation frequency for the overdamped oscillator is omega_1 = (5.371 +/- 0.015) x 10^{-2} (dimensionless)
- The oscillation frequency for the underdamped oscillator is omega_2 = (7.472 +/- 0.022) x 10^{-3} (dimensionless)
- The momentum shift for the neutron underdamped oscillator is p_0^n = 1035.93 +/- 2.44 MeV, with p_0^p = 0 for the proton
- The initial values for the subtracted form factors at threshold are G_osc^{0,p}(0) = 0.1918 and G_osc^{0,n}(0) = 0.3138 from chiral effective field theory

## Context

This work extends studies of nucleon electromagnetic form factor oscillations first emphasized by Bianconi (2015) and observed by BaBar (2013) and BESIII (2019, 2021), where the BESIII Collaboration found a phase difference between proton and neutron oscillations. The authors improve upon previous analyses by including final-state interactions from chiral effective field theory to describe the near-threshold region, which earlier approaches using ordinary oscillators could not handle. The fractional oscillator model enables future studies of the distributions of polarized electric charges in nucleons and provides clues about the underlying dynamics, with the overdamped component dominated by chiral effective field theory potentials and the underdamped component by one-gluon exchange between constituent quarks.
