# Elastic $Œ±$-$^{12}$C scattering at low energies with the sharp resonant $0_3^+$ state of $^{16}$O

> An inclusion of sharp resonant $0_3^+$ state of $^{16}$O and first excited $2_1^+$ state of $^{12}$C in a study of $s$-wave elastic $Œ±$-$^{12}$C scattering at low energies is investigated in an effective Lagrangian approach. The elastic scattering amplitude is parted into two; one is for the sharp resonant $0_3^+$ state of $^{16}$O parameterized by Breit-Wigner formula, and the other is for a non-resonant part of the amplitude parameterized by effective range expansion. In the non-resonant part of the amplitude, a contribution from the $2_1^+$ state of $^{12}$C is included. We discuss a large correlation between a coupling for the $2_1^+$ state of $^{12}$C and an effective range parameter $Q_0$ as well as a necessity of inclusion of a vertex correction for the initial and final $Œ±$-$^{12}$C states. After fixing parameters appearing in the amplitudes by using experimental data, we calculate asymptotic normalization coefficients for ground $0_1^+$ state and first excited $0_2^+$ state of $^{16}$O and compare them to previous results found in literature.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the sharp resonant $0_3^+$ state of $^{16}$O and the first excited $2_1^+$ state of $^{12}$C in an effective Lagrangian approach for $s$-wave elastic $\alpha$-$^{12}$C scattering reproduces experimental phase shift data well, but the coupling constant $\tilde g_0$ for the $2_1^+$ state of $^{12}$C cannot be fixed from elastic scattering data alone due to a large correlation with the effective range parameter $Q_0$.

## Method

The elastic scattering amplitude was separated into two parts: a Breit-Wigner amplitude for the sharp resonant $0_3^+$ state of $^{16}$O, and a non-resonant amplitude parameterized by effective range expansion up to $p^6$ order. A bubble diagram contribution from the $2_1^+$ state of $^{12}$C and a phenomenological vertex correction for initial and final $\alpha$-$^{12}$C states were included in the non-resonant amplitude. Parameters were fixed using binding energies of the $0_1^+$ and $0_2^+$ states of $^{16}$O, the resonance energy and width of the $0_3^+$ state, and fits to experimental phase shift data from Tischhauser et al. (2009) for $2.6 \le E_\alpha \le 6.62$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- Resonance energy of the $0_3^+$ state of $^{16}$O: $E_r = 4.887(2)$ MeV (center-of-mass), corresponding to laboratory energy $E_{\alpha,r} = 6.516(3)$ MeV
- Width of the $0_3^+$ state: $\Gamma_r = 1.5(5)$ keV
- Excitation energy of the $2_1^+$ state of $^{12}$C: $\Delta_2 = 4.440$ MeV
- Fitted effective range parameter $P_0$ ranges from $-0.03573(3)$ fm$^3$ (for $\tilde g_0 = 0$) to $-0.03584(3)$ fm$^3$ (for $\tilde g_0 = 60$)
- Fitted effective range parameter $Q_0$ ranges from $0.002055(9)$ fm$^5$ (for $\tilde g_0 = 0$) to $0.009598(9)$ fm$^5$ (for $\tilde g_0 = 60$)
- Fitted vertex correction parameter $R_2$ ranges from $0.976(14)$ fm$^2$ (for $\tilde g_0 = 0$) to $0.731(16)$ fm$^2$ (for $\tilde g_0 = 60$)
- Calculated ANC for the $0_1^+$ state of $^{16}$O ranges from $41.0(1)$ fm$^{-1/2}$ (for $\tilde g_0 = 0$) to $14.2(0)$ fm$^{-1/2}$ (for $\tilde g_0 = 60$)
- Calculated ANC for the $0_2^+$ state of $^{16}$O ranges from $443(3)$ fm$^{-1/2}$ (for $\tilde g_0 = 0$) to $115(0)$ fm$^{-1/2}$ (for $\tilde g_0 = 60$)
- Reduced chi-squared for all fits: $\chi^2/N = 1.60$ with $N = 351$ data points

## Context

This work extends the author's previous effective field theory studies of the $^{12}$C($\alpha$,$\gamma$)$^{16}$O reaction (Ando, 2016; Ando, 2018; Ando, 2019) by incorporating the sharp resonant $0_3^+$ state of $^{16}$O and the $2_1^+$ state of $^{12}$C, which were not included in earlier treatments. The calculated ANCs for the $0_1^+$ and $0_2^+$ states of $^{16}$O agree with the "small value group" in the literature, including results from Schurmann et al. (2011) and Orlov, Irgaziev, and Nabi (2017), but are significantly smaller than the "large value group" results from Avila et al. (2015) and deBoer et al. (2017). The strong correlation between $\tilde g_0$ and $Q_0$ means that elastic scattering data alone cannot determine the $2_1^+$ state contribution, and future work using inelastic $\alpha$-$^{12}$C scattering data or a fixed ANC value is needed to resolve this ambiguity.
