# Modeling a Realistic Dynamical Model for High Energy Heavy Ion Collisions

> In this article, we outline the modeling of a realistic dynamical model for comprehensive description of high energy heavy ion collisions. Comparing theoretical calculations and experimental data at RHIC, we give detailed discussions on the key ingredients for the construction of a multi-module model: initial condition, hydrodynamical expansion, hadronization, and freezeout processes.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a comprehensive multi-module dynamical model, combining initial conditions, hydrodynamical expansion, hadronization, and freezeout, is indispensable for describing the entire history of high energy heavy ion collisions, but acknowledges that such a model is still missing despite accumulated knowledge of each stage.

## Method

The authors review and compare existing ideal and viscous hydrodynamical models (listed in Tables 1 and 2) by examining their dimensionality, initial condition schemes (Glauber-type, color glass condensate, event-by-event fluctuations), equations of state (bag model, lattice QCD inspired), numerical schemes (SPH, PPM, SHASTA, Kurganov-Tadmor), freezeout treatments, and calculated observables. They then discuss each key ingredient, initial conditions, hydrodynamical equations, equation of state, transport coefficients, jet energy loss, hadronization via recombination, and freezeout with final state interactions, by comparing theoretical calculations with experimental data from SPS, RHIC, and LHC.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature ranges for hydrodynamical models at RHIC: T ~ 300–600 MeV
- Inverse-slope parameter extracted from PHENIX direct photon spectra in Au+Au at sqrt(s)=200 GeV: T = 221 ± 19(stat) ± 19(syst) MeV
- Three sets of (initial temperature T_i, freezeout temperature T_f) with tau_0 that yield almost identical differential elliptic flow in viscous hydrodynamics: (0.29, 0.14) GeV with tau_0=2 fm; (0.36, 0.15) GeV with tau_0=1 fm; (0.43, 0.16) GeV with tau_0=0.5 fm
- Initial time tau_0 given by inverse saturation scale: tau=0.18 fm at RHIC, tau=0.10 fm at LHC
- Critical temperature of phase transition: approximately 160 MeV

## Context

This review extends the line of work on relativistic hydrodynamical modeling of heavy ion collisions that began with early RHIC-era models by Kolb (2001), Hirano (2002), Teaney (2000), and Nonaka (2006). It contrasts ideal hydrodynamics with the more recent viscous hydrodynamical approaches by Romatschke (2007), Dusling (2007), Luzum (2008), Schenke (2010), and Song (2010). The paper enables future construction of a fully integrated multi-module model that can simultaneously describe initial state fluctuations, viscous hydrodynamical expansion, recombination hadronization, and final state interactions, which would allow more rigorous constraints on transport coefficients and the QCD equation of state from experimental data at RHIC and LHC.
