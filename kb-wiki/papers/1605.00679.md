# Aspects of the momentum dependence of the equation of state and of the residual $NN$ cross section, and their effects on nuclear stopping

> With the semiclassical Landau-Vlasov transport model we studied the stopping observable $R_E$, the energy-based isotropy ratio, for the $^{129}$Xe\,+\,$^{120}$Sn reaction at beam energies spanning 12$A$ to 100$A$ MeV. We investigated the impacts of the nonlocality of the nuclear mean field, of the in-medium modified nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) cross section and of the reaction centrality. A fixed set of model parameters yields $R_E$ values that favorably compare with the experimental ones, but only for energies below the Fermi energy $E_F$. Above $E_F$ agreement is readily possible, but by a smooth evolution with energy of the parameter that controls the in-medium modification of $NN$ cross section. By comparing the simulation correction factor ${\cal F}$ applied to the free $NN$ cross section with the one deduced from experimental data [Phys.\ Rev.\ C\,{\bf 90}, 064602 (2014)], we infer that the zero-range mean field almost entirely reproduces it. Also, in accordance with what has been deduced from experimental data, around $E_F$ a strong reduction of the free $NN$ cross section is found. In order to test the impact of sampling central collisions by multiplicity an event generator (HIPSE) was used. We obtain that high multiplicity events are spread over a broad impact parameter range, but it turns out that this has a small effect on the observable $R_E$ and, thus, on ${\cal F}$ as well.

## Key claim

The Landau-Vlasov transport model can reproduce experimental stopping ratios \(R_E\) for the \(^{129}\)Xe + \(^{120}\)Sn reaction below the Fermi energy with a fixed set of parameters, but above the Fermi energy agreement requires a smooth evolution with energy of the parameter controlling the in-medium modification of the nucleon-nucleon cross section, meaning no single parameter set works across the full 12\(A\) to 100\(A\) MeV range.

## Method

The authors used the semiclassical Landau-Vlasov (LV) transport model to simulate the \(^{129}\)Xe + \(^{120}\)Sn reaction at beam energies from 12\(A\) to 100\(A\) MeV, computing the energy-based isotropy ratio \(R_E\) (transverse-to-longitudinal energy ratio) for central collisions. They tested two nuclear mean fields (the momentum-dependent D1-G1 Gogny force with \(K_\infty = 228\) MeV and \(m^\ast/m = 0.67\), and the zero-range Skyrme Zamick force with \(K_\infty = 200\) MeV and \(m^\ast/m = 1.0\)) and several parametrizations of the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section, including those from Chen (1968), Cugnon (1987), Li and Machleidt (1993-1994), and Klakow (1993), as well as a simple multiplicative scaling factor \(\mathcal{F}\) applied to the free cross section. The HIPSE event generator was used to study how multiplicity-based centrality selection affects the extracted \(\mathcal{F}\) values.

## Key numbers

- The D1-G1 EOS has incompressibility \(K_\infty = 228\) MeV and effective mass \(m^\ast/m = 0.67\); the Zamick EOS has \(K_\infty = 200\) MeV and \(m^\ast/m = 1.0\).
- For the D1-G1 EOS with the Klakow parametrization, \(\alpha\) values between \(-0.6\) and \(-0.5\) reproduce experimental \(R_E\) at \(E_{\rm inc} \lesssim E_F\) (Fermi energy \(\sim 38\) MeV).
- The experimental correction factor \(\mathcal{F}_{\rm exp}\) from Lopez (2014) reaches a minimum of 0.17 around the Fermi energy, then increases to about 0.5 at 100\(A\) MeV.
- For the D1-G1 EOS, \(\mathcal{F}_{\rm sim} > 1\) at \(E_{\rm inc} \gtrsim 80A\) MeV, while the Zamick EOS gives \(\mathcal{F}_{\rm sim} < 1\) at all energies.
- The HIPSE simulation shows that high-multiplicity events selected to match the number of events with \(b \leq 2\) fm (about 2% of \(\sigma_R\), or 104–116 mb) have a broad impact parameter distribution extending up to \(b \approx 5\) fm, with the Gaussian maximum near \(b \approx 3\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the experimental study of nuclear stopping by the INDRA Collaboration (Lehaut, 2010; Lopez, 2014), which found a minimum in the isotropy ratio \(R_E\) around the Fermi energy and a strong reduction of the free nucleon-nucleon cross section. The authors compare their simulation results to the experimental \(\mathcal{F}\) factor extracted by Lopez (2014) and find that the zero-range Zamick mean field reproduces the experimental \(\mathcal{F}\) values above 35\(A\) MeV, while the momentum-dependent D1-G1 force gives about twice higher \(\mathcal{F}\) values. Previous theoretical studies using IQMD (Su, 2013; Liu, 2001; Zhang, 2011; Kaur, 2011; Vinayak, 2012) and AMD (Zhao, 2014) also failed to reproduce the experimental \(R_E\) with a single parameter set across the full energy range. The results enable future work to refine in-medium cross section parametrizations by requiring a smooth energy-dependent modification rather than a fixed reduction factor.
