# New results from a number operator interpretation of the compositeness of bound and resonant states

> A novel theoretical approach to the problem of the compositeness ($X$) of a resonance or bound state is developed on the basis of the expectation values of the number operators of the free particles in the continuum. This formalism is specially suitable for effective field theories in which the bare elementary states are integrated out but that give rise to resonance and bound states when implemented in nonperturbative calculations. We demonstrate that $X=1$ for finite-range energy-independent potentials, either regular or singular. A non-trivial example for an energy-dependent potential is discussed where it is shown that $X$ is independent of any type of cutoff regulator employed. The generalization of these techniques to relativistic states is developed. We also explain how to obtain a meaningful compositeness with respect to the open channels for resonances, even if it is complex in a first turn, by making use of suitable phase-factor transformations. Defining elementariness as $X=0$, we derive a new universal criterion for the elementariness of a bound state. Along the same lines, a necessary condition for a resonance to be qualified as elementary is given. The application of the formalism here developed might be of considerable practical interest.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the compositeness \(X\) of a bound or resonant state can be reinterpreted as the expectation value of the number operator of free particles in the continuum, and that for finite-range energy-independent potentials (regular or singular) the total compositeness is exactly \(X=1\), contrary to earlier expectations that \(Z\) (elementariness) might be nonzero in such cases.

## Method

The author defines compositeness \(X\) as the expectation value of the number operator of free-particle constituents in the bound state, divided by the nominal number of constituents. This definition is shown to be equivalent to the standard wave-function normalization approach. The formalism is developed within non-relativistic quantum field theory, using the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in partial waves and the analytic properties of the coupling function \(g(k)\). For zero-range energy-independent potentials, the author solves the algebraic matrix form of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation and evaluates the compositeness integral by contour integration in the complex momentum plane.

## Key numbers

- For finite-range energy-independent potentials, the total compositeness \(X = 1\) (exact result, no numerical value given).
- The compositeness in a partial wave is given by \(X_{\ell S} = \frac{1}{2\pi^2} \int_0^\infty dk \, k^2 \frac{g_{\ell S}^2(k^2)}{(k^2/2\mu - E_B)^2}\), where \(E_B\) is the bound-state energy and \(\mu\) the reduced mass.
- For zero-range potentials regularized with a convergence factor \(e^{i\epsilon(k+k')}\) (\(\epsilon \to 0^+\)), the contour integration yields \(X = \frac{2i\mu^2}{\pi} \frac{\partial}{\partial k} \left[ \frac{k^2 g^2(k^2)}{(k+\varkappa)^2} \right]_{k=\varkappa}\), where \(\varkappa = \sqrt{2\mu E_B}\).

## Context

This work extends the foundational compositeness framework of Weinberg (1965) and earlier work by Castillejo, Dalitz, and Dyson (1956) by providing a number-operator interpretation that avoids the need to introduce bare elementary states explicitly. The formalism is particularly suited for effective field theories where elementary states are integrated out, as in the approaches of Oller (2000) and Kaiser (1998). The result that \(X=1\) for energy-independent potentials clarifies the limitations of perturbative renormalization theory in nonperturbative contexts, and the method enables practical calculation of compositeness from scattering data without requiring knowledge of the full Hamiltonian.
