# How the QCD trace anomaly behaves at the core of twin stars?

> We investigate the behavior of the dense and cold (normalized) QCD trace anomaly, $Î”$, in the interior of twin neutron stars (obtained from several sets of equations of state in agreement with modern compact-star and multimessenger data) satisfying static and dynamic stability conditions. We scan the formed twin-star parameter space in order to look for effects caused by the presence of a strong first-order phase transition connecting hadron and quark phases by means of a Maxwell construction. We found robustly that $Î”$ suffers an abrupt decrease around the transition point, even reaching large negative values ($Î”\simeq-0.35$), in marked contrast to current studies pointing out a smooth behavior with $Î”\gtrsim 0$ at all densities. Besides, we characterize the behavior of {the recently defined} conformal factor, $d_{c}$, in our four categories of twin stars for which we perform comparisons with theoretical constraints put, e.g. by Bayesian studies sometimes adjusted to agree with perturbative QCD at high densities. These analyses allow us to hypothesize modifications in the strong QCD coupling in dense nuclear matter with a strong thermodynamic discontinuity.

## Key claim

The authors find that the QCD trace anomaly $\Delta$ suffers an abrupt decrease around the hadron-quark phase transition point in twin neutron stars, even reaching large negative values ($\Delta\simeq-0.35$), in marked contrast to current studies that point to a smooth behavior with $\Delta\gtrsim 0$ at all densities; this result is conditional on the presence of a strong first-order phase transition via Maxwell construction and the satisfaction of both static and dynamic stability conditions.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid equations of state (EoS) for twin neutron stars by matching a hadronic phase (described by generalized piecewise polytropes connected to the stiffest chiral effective theory EoS) to a quark phase (described by the constant-speed-of-sound parametrization) through a Maxwell construction with a strong first-order phase transition. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for hydrostatic equilibrium and the Gondek equations for radial oscillations, applying rapid and slow junction conditions at the hadron-quark interface to determine dynamically stable configurations. The parameter space is scanned over transition pressures $P_t$ in the range [10, 100] MeV fm$^{-3}$ and energy density jumps $\Delta\epsilon$ between 1.1 and 10 times the initial value, with quark-phase speeds of sound $c_s^2=1$ and $c_s^2=0.5$, classifying twin stars into four categories based on their mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- The normalized QCD trace anomaly $\Delta$ reaches values as low as $\Delta\simeq-0.35$ around the transition point in twin stars, in contrast to the $\Delta\gtrsim 0$ behavior found in studies without strong first-order transitions.
- Transition pressures $P_t$ are scanned in the range [10, 100] MeV fm$^{-3}$.
- Energy density jumps $\Delta\epsilon$ are varied between 1.1 and 10 times the initial energy density at the transition.
- Sample parameters for Category I: $\epsilon_t = 333.08$ MeV fm$^{-3}$, $\Delta\epsilon = 274$ MeV fm$^{-3}$, $P_t = 70$ MeV fm$^{-3}$, $c_s^2 = 1$.
- Sample parameters for Category II: $\epsilon_t = 333.08$ MeV fm$^{-3}$, $\Delta\epsilon = 545$ MeV fm$^{-3}$, $P_t = 70$ MeV fm$^{-3}$, $c_s^2 = 1$.
- Sample parameters for Category III: $\epsilon_t = 263.73$ MeV fm$^{-3}$, $\Delta\epsilon = 178$ MeV fm$^{-3}$, $P_t = 30$ MeV fm$^{-3}$, $c_s^2 = 1$.
- Sample parameters for Category IV: $\epsilon_t = 212.91$ MeV fm$^{-3}$, $\Delta\epsilon = 157$ MeV fm$^{-3}$, $P_t = 10$ MeV fm$^{-3}$, $c_s^2 = 1$.
- The Seidov criterion for twin-star existence requires $\Delta\epsilon \geq \Delta\epsilon_{\rm crit} \equiv \frac{1}{2}\epsilon_t + \frac{3}{2}P_t$.

## Context

This work extends studies of the QCD trace anomaly in dense matter, particularly challenging the $\Delta \geq 0$ conjecture of Fujimoto (2022) which was based on smooth EoS interpolations without strong first-order transitions. It contrasts with Bayesian studies by Takatsy (2023) and Albino (2024) that found $\Delta$ almost always positive in hybrid stars, and with the work of Lope-Oter (2024) that found only slightly negative values ($\Delta\approx -0.1$) in twin stars without verifying radial stability. The results enable future investigations into modifications of the strong QCD coupling in dense nuclear matter with thermodynamic discontinuities and motivate extending the Komoltsev-Kurkela (2021) pQCD consistency band to twin-star extreme densities.
