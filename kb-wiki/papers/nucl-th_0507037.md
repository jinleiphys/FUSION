# Delta-33 medium mass modification and pion spectra

> We study the pi+- spectra obtained in 2,4,6,8 A GeV Au-Au collisions within the thermal model. We find that the main features of the data can be well described after we include the pions from the decay of the Delta-resonance with medium mass modification.

## Key claim

The main features of the charged pion spectra from Au-Au collisions at 2-8 A GeV can be well described by a thermal model that includes pions from Delta-33 resonance decay, but only when a uniform multiplicative reduction of all hadron masses (pion, nucleon, and Delta-33) is applied; the mass modification is the new physics element allowing a significant improvement of the data description using a single thermal source.

## Method

The authors model the high transverse-momentum component of the pion m_t spectra with a thermal Bose distribution to extract an effective inverse slope T, then subtract this direct thermal component from the experimental spectra. The difference is modeled as the decay spectrum of thermal Delta-33 resonances, using a Boltzmann distribution for the Delta-33 with fugacities determined by isospin weights. All hadron masses (pion, nucleon, Delta-33) are scaled by a common multiplicative factor m/m_0, and longitudinal flow is introduced via a two-fluid rapidity profile with parameter eta_0.

## Key numbers

- The mass reduction factor m/m_0 ranges from 0.72 at 2 A GeV to 0.57 at 8 A GeV, corresponding to modified Delta-33 masses from 0.880 GeV down to 0.704 GeV (free-space mass 1.232 GeV).
- The slope temperature T (comprising thermal and flow effects) increases from 122 MeV at 2 A GeV to 141 MeV at 8 A GeV.
- The longitudinal flow parameter eta_0 is 0.15 at 2 A GeV and 0.3 at 4, 6, and 8 A GeV.
- The chi-squared per degree of freedom ranges from 1.04 (6 A GeV) to 1.20 (4 A GeV) across the four beam energies.
- The normalization factor N_Delta e^{mu_B/T_ch} decreases from 2,170 at 2 A GeV to 536 at 8 A GeV.

## Context

This work extends the two-component thermal model used by the experimental E895 collaboration (Klay, 2002) by replacing the second thermal source with Delta-33 resonance decay, following earlier recognition by Brown (1991) and Hong (1997) that Delta decay is a significant pion production mechanism in this energy range. The mass modification approach is contrasted with quantum-hadrodynamic model calculations by Serot (1997) and Rapp (2000), where the authors note that their bag-model-inspired mass reduction differs from hadron model calculations that find small mass shifts but large width increases. The analysis enables future reconstruction of the mass-modified Delta-33 resonance via invariant mass methods and extension to nucleon spectra.
