# Octupole deformation of nuclei near the spherical closed-shell configurations

> The origin of octupole deformation for even-even nuclei near the doubly-closed shell configurations are investigated by means of the semiclassical periodic orbit theory. In order to focus on the change of shell structure due to deformation, a simple infinite-well potential model is employed with octupole shape parametrized by merging a sphere and a paraboloid. Attention is paid to the contributions of the degenerate families of periodic orbits (POs) confined in the spherical portion of the potential, that are expected to partially preserve the spherical shell effect up to considerably large value of the octupole parameter. The contribution of those POs to the semiclassical trace formula plays an important role in bringing about shell energy gain due to octupole deformation in the system with a few particles added to spherical closed-shell configurations.

# Octupole deformation of nuclei near the spherical closed-shell configurations

## Key claim

Octupole deformation in nuclei just above spherical closed-shell configurations arises from a strong shell effect associated with classical periodic orbits confined in the spherical portion of the potential, which partially preserves the spherical shell structure up to considerably large octupole deformation. The mechanism is analogous to the prefragment shell effect previously identified in nuclear fission, where the spherical subsystem provides shell-energy gain when its size matches that of a spherical magic nucleus.

## Method

The author employs a simple infinite-well (cavity) potential model with an axially symmetric octupole shape parameterized by smoothly merging a sphere and a paraboloid, where the relative thickness of the paraboloid tip κ serves as the octupole parameter. Single-particle energy levels are computed by solving the Laplace equation with Dirichlet boundary conditions, and the semiclassical periodic orbit theory (POT) is applied using the Balian-Bloch and Gutzwiller trace formulas to express the oscillating part of the level density as a sum over classical periodic orbits. The Fourier transform of the quantum level density is used to identify which periodic orbits dominate the shell structure, and the shell energy is evaluated from the trace formula including contributions from degenerate orbit families confined in the spherical part of the cavity.

## Key numbers

- The spherical magic numbers relevant to the analysis are N = 58, 92, and 138, which correspond to shell gaps in the cavity model where approximately degenerate Δl = 3 pairs of levels appear (e.g., 2f and 1i above the N = 92 gap, 1j and 2g above the N = 138 gap).
- Complete parity mixing of the Δl = 3 pair (1j and 2g orbitals above N = 138) is achieved already at κ ≈ 0.1, as shown by the wave function contour plots.
- The Fourier transform calculation uses a Gaussian cutoff with k_c R_0 = 20 (R_0 being the spherical-limit radius) and k_max = 1.5 k_c.
- The shell energy δE(N) multiplied by N^(-2/3) is plotted against N^(1/3) for κ = 0, 0.2, and 0.5, showing that the quantum results are reproduced by the contribution of periodic orbits confined in the sphere part.
- The Fermi wave number k_F is approximately independent of particle number, given by k_F ≈ (9π/2)^(1/3) r_0^(-1).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies on prefragment shell effects in nuclear fission (Arita 2018a, 2018b, 2020), where periodic orbits confined in spherical subsystems were shown to explain asymmetric fission in actinides. The analysis builds on the established understanding that octupole deformation near closed shells is driven by Δl = 3 parity-mixing correlations (Butler 1996, Hamamoto and Mottelson 1991, Frisk 1994), but provides a new semiclassical explanation in terms of gross shell structure rather than individual orbital behavior. The results enable systematic predictions of octupole-deformed regions on the nuclear chart and connect to recent work on octupole shapes in fission fragments (Scamps 2018, 2019), explaining why the heavier fission fragment mass concentrates around 140 (slightly above doubly magic 132Sn). The shape parametrization is to be generalized to spheroid+paraboloid for combined quadrupole and octupole studies (Arita 2023b).
