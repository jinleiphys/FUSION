# Modeling final-state interactions with a relativistic multiple-scattering approximation

> We address the issue of nuclear attenuation in nucleon and pion knockout reactions. A selection of results from a model based on a relativistic multiple-scattering approximation is presented. We show transparency calculations for pion electroproduction on several nuclei, where data are in very good agreement with calculations including color transparency. Secondly, we discuss the density dependence of reactions involving one or double proton knockout. The latter reaction succeeds in probing the high density regions in the deep interior of the nucleus.

# Modeling final-state interactions with a relativistic multiple-scattering approximation

## Key claim

The relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approximation (RMSGA) model, which has no free parameters, can reproduce pion electroproduction transparency data on several nuclei only when color transparency (CT) effects are included, with CT-related enhancements up to 20% at the highest energies; the agreement is "very good" for light nuclei but the model overestimates the data for gold somewhat, though the slope remains consistent with measurements.

## Method

The authors extend Glauber multiple-scattering theory to a relativistic framework using eikonal approximation for high-energy hadrons, describing all hadrons with relativistic wave functions from the Serot-Walecka model. The Glauber phase encodes nuclear attenuation through profile functions for nucleon-nucleon and pion-nucleon scattering, with three energy-dependent parameters fitted to scattering data. Color transparency is incorporated via the quantum diffusion model, replacing the total cross section with an effective one that evolves from a reduced value to its normal value over a formation length. Short-range correlations (SRC) are included by replacing single-particle densities with effective densities derived from correlated two-body densities using a Jastrow correlation function.

## Key numbers

- Pion transparency calculations cover a pion momentum range of 2.8 ≤ p_π ≤ 4.4 GeV for the A(e,e'π⁺) reaction on ¹²C, ²⁷Al, ⁶³Cu, and ¹⁹⁷Au
- Including SRC increases transparencies by about 2% for ¹²C, ²⁷Al, and ⁶³Cu, and about 4% for ¹⁹⁷Au
- Color transparency produces enhancements up to 20% at the highest energies
- For the quantum diffusion model, the average transverse momentum of a quark inside a hadron is k_t = 0.350 GeV/c
- Formation length parameters: ΔM² = 1 GeV² for the proton and ΔM² = 0.7 GeV² for the pion
- The proton root-mean-square radius in ¹²C from elastic electron scattering is ⟨r²⟩^{1/2} = 2.464 ± 0.012 fm (Reuter, 1982)
- For density dependence studies, the energy transfer is 1.5 GeV for single-nucleon knockout and 3 GeV for the ¹²C(γ,pp) reaction
- For the (p,2p) calculations, both final-state protons have 1.5 GeV kinetic energy and are detected at 32° on opposite sides of the incoming proton

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RMSGA model applications to A(e,e'p) reactions (Ryckebusch, 2003; Lava, 2004), A(p,2p) processes (Van Overmeire, 2006), pion photo- and electroproduction (Cosyn, 2006; Cosyn, 2007), and neutrino-induced nucleon knockout (Martinez, 2005). The pion transparency results are compared with the semiclassical model of Larson, Miller, and Strikman (2006) and with JLab data from Clasie (2007), as well as with a more recent model by Kaskulov (2008) that also finds excellent agreement with CT-included calculations. The density-mapping technique enables future work to identify which nuclear regions contribute to specific knockout reactions, showing that A(γ,pp) probes the deep interior, A(p,2p) is peripheral, and A(e,e'p) is intermediate between these two.
