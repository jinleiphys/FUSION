# Matter Mass Generation and Theta Vacuum: Dynamical Spontaneous Symmetry Breaking

> This work proposes a stringent concept of matter mass generation and Theta vacuum in the context of local gauge theory for the strong force under the constraint of the flat universe. The matter mass is generated as the consequence of dynamical spontaneous symmetry breaking (DSSB) of gauge symmetry and discrete symmetries, which is motivated by the parameter Theta representing the surface term. Matter mass generation introduces the typical features of constituent particle mass, dual Meissner effect, and hyperfine structure. The Theta term plays important roles on the DSSB of the gauge group and on the quantization of the matter and vacuum space. The Theta vacuum exhibits the intrinsic principal number and intrinsic angular momentum for intrinsic space quantization in analogy with the extrinsic principal number and extrinsic angular momentum for extrinsic space quantization.

## Key claim

This paper proposes that matter mass is generated through dynamical spontaneous symmetry breaking (DSSB) of gauge and discrete symmetries driven by the nonperturbative Theta term, under the specific constraint that the universe is extremely flat with Omega - 1 = -10^{-61}. The claim is hedged by the condition that this mechanism applies specifically to strong interactions and requires the Theta term as a surface term to resolve the gauge-invariance problem of conventional fermion mass terms.

## Method

The author adds the nonperturbative Theta term to the QCD Lagrangian density and implements DSSB through two simultaneous mechanisms: explicit symmetry breaking of gauge fields (represented by the strong coupling constant) and spontaneous symmetry breaking via condensation of singlet gauge bosons. The formalism uses fermion condensation as an order parameter, with the gauge boson mass expressed as M_G^2 = M_H^2 - c_f g_s^2 <phi>^2, where <phi> is the condensation of odd-parity singlet gauge bosons. The approach also introduces intrinsic space quantization with principal quantum numbers for color, isospin, and spin spaces, and relates the Theta parameter to the flat universe constraint through Theta = 10^{-60} rho_G / rho_m.

## Key numbers

- Flat universe constraint: Omega - 1 = -10^{-61}
- Theta parameter defined as Theta = 10^{-60} rho_G / rho_m
- Matter energy density: rho_m approximately 10^{-47} GeV^4
- For a nucleon: m_h = 0.94 GeV, c_f = 1/3, alpha_s = 0.48, N_sd = 1, M_G = 0.27 GeV
- Theta_QCD approximately 10^{-12} at the strong scale
- Baryon asymmetry delta_B approximately 10^{-10}
- Proton Lande spin g-factor: g_s^p = 5.59; neutron: g_s^n = -3.83
- Proton to constituent quark mass ratio: m_p/m_q approximately 2.79
- Nuclear radius relation: r = r_0 A^{1/3} with r_0 approximately 1.2 fm
- Nuclear density: n_B = 1.95 x 10^{38} cm^{-3}
- Theta < 10^{-9} from electric dipole moment of the neutron (consistent with observed results)

## Context

This work extends the concept of the Theta vacuum introduced by 't Hooft (1976) and the DSSB approach of Nambu and Jona-Lasinio (1961), while addressing the fermion mass generation problem that the Glashow-Weinberg-Salam model and the Higgs mechanism (Higgs, 1964) leave as input parameters. The approach enables a unified description of hadron masses through the constituent quark model (Dashen and Gell-Mann, 1966) and provides a framework for understanding the baryon asymmetry (Steinberg, 1976) and the electric dipole moment of the neutron (Altarev et al., 1986; Smith et al., 1990) through the Theta vacuum structure.
