# A schematic model for QCD at finite temperature

> The simplest version of a class of toy models for QCD is presented. It is a Lipkin-type model, for the quark-antiquark sector, and, for the gluon sector, gluon pairs with spin zero are treated as elementary bosons. The model restricts to mesons with spin zero and to few baryonic states. The corresponding energy spectrum is discussed. We show that ground state correlations are essential to describe physical properties of the spectrum at low energies. Phase transitions are described in an effective manner, by using coherent states. The appearance of a Goldstone boson for large values of the interaction strength is discussed, as related to a collective state. The formalism is extended to consider finite temperatures. The partition function is calculated, in an approximate way, showing the convenience of the use of coherent states. The energy density, heat capacity and transitions from the hadronic phase to the quark-gluon plasma are calculated.

# A schematic model for QCD at finite temperature

## Key claim

The paper presents a toy model for QCD that can describe phase transitions from hadronic matter to a quark-gluon plasma at finite temperature, but only in an effective manner using coherent states and with the restriction that the model is not derived from field theory but rather represents interactions between effective degrees of freedom. The model shows that ground state correlations are essential for describing physical properties of the spectrum at low energies, and that a Goldstone boson appears for large values of the interaction strength.

## Method

The authors construct a Lipkin-type model where the quark-antiquark sector uses SU(2) algebra operators and the gluon sector treats spin-zero gluon pairs as elementary bosons, restricting to mesons with spin zero and few baryonic states. Two Hamiltonians are considered: H_I which commutes with a symmetry operator P and H_II which contains all terms required by symmetry including ground state correlations. The partition function is calculated approximately using coherent states and a re-exponentiation technique, with numerical diagonalization performed for comparison.

## Key numbers

- The degeneracy parameter Omega = 9, corresponding to n_c = 3 colors and n_fl = 3 flavors
- The effective quark mass omega_f = 1/3 GeV
- The boson (gluon pair) energy omega_b = 1.6 GeV
- The critical interaction strength for H_I is V^c_1 = 0.035 GeV, where a level crossing occurs
- For H_II, three regimes are identified: equal population of fermion and gluon pairs for V_1 < 0.008 GeV, fermionic dominance for 0.008 < V_1 < 0.015 GeV, and gluonic dominance for V_1 > 0.015 GeV
- The approximate partition function works reasonably well for temperatures T > 0.15 GeV
- The heat capacity shows a Schottky bump typical of a two-level system for V_1 = 0 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Lipkin model (Lipkin, 1965) familiar in nuclear physics to QCD at finite temperature, and builds upon earlier work by Schutte (related to pion effects in nuclei). The model is intended to mock up basic features of non-perturbative QCD in a similar way that schematic models do in the nuclear many-body problem, and can be used to test microscopic many-body techniques for describing realistic QCD scenarios that cannot be accessed by lattice gauge calculations or other methods. The approach enables the study of the transition from hadronic phase to quark-gluon plasma, though the authors note that the model does not result from a field theory.
