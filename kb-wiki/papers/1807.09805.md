# Transverse Momentum Spectra at Threshold for Groomed Heavy Quark Jets

> We present the transverse momentum spectrum for a heavy hadron at threshold in a groomed jet initiated by a heavy quark. The cross section is doubly differential in the energy fraction of an identified heavy hadron in the jet and its transverse momentum measured with respect to the groomed (recoil free) jet axis. The grooming is implemented using a soft-drop grooming algorithm and helps us in mitigating the effects of Non-Global logarithms and pile up. For the particular case of a $B$ meson, we identify two distinct regimes of the transverse momentum spectrum and develop an EFT within the formalisms of Soft Collineat Effective Theory (SCET) and Heavy Quark Effective Theory (HQET) for each of these regions. We show how each region can be matched smoothly into the other to provide a prediction for the perturbative transverse momentum spectrum. The EFT also predicts the scaling behavior of the leading non-perturbative power corrections and implements a simple shape function to account for hadronization. We work in the threshold region where the heavy hadron carries most of the energy of the jet since in this regime, we have a very good discriminating power between heavy quark and gluon initiated jets. We observe that the shape of the spectrum is independent of the energy of the jet over a large range of transverse momentum. We propose that this spectrum can be used as a probe of evolution for heavy quark TMD fragmentation function. At the same time, it can be treated as a jet substructure observable for probing Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP).

## Key claim

The paper develops a factorization theorem for the transverse momentum spectrum of a heavy hadron (specifically a B meson) inside a groomed jet initiated by a heavy quark, working in the threshold region where the hadron carries most of the jet energy (z ~ 0.8). The authors claim that the shape of the transverse momentum spectrum is independent of the jet energy over a large range of transverse momentum, but this result is conditional on the grooming procedure (soft-drop algorithm) and the specific threshold regime.

## Method

The authors use Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) and Heavy Quark Effective Theory (HQET) to develop factorization theorems for two distinct regimes of transverse momentum: Region 1 where q_perp ~ m(1-z) and Region 2 where q_perp >> m(1-z). They implement soft-drop grooming with parameters z_cut ~ 0.1 and beta = 0 to remove contaminating soft radiation and non-global logarithms, then match the two regions smoothly to obtain a prediction for the perturbative transverse momentum spectrum. The resummation of large logarithms is performed using renormalization group equations in Laplace space.

## Key numbers

- The threshold region studied is z ~ 0.8, where the heavy hadron carries most of the jet energy
- The soft-drop grooming parameter is z_cut ~ 0.1, with beta = 0
- For a jet energy of approximately 100 GeV, the transverse momentum range probed is q_perp ~ (0, m_b) where m_b is the b-quark mass
- The canonical scales for resummation are mu_cs = q_perp and mu_B = m * z_cut
- The fixed order cross section in Region 2 scales as 1/q_perp^2 times theta((1-z)-z_cut)/(1-z)

## Context

This work extends jet substructure calculations from light/massless partons to heavy quark jets, building on prior developments in soft-drop grooming by Larkoski (2014) and factorization theorems for groomed jets by Makris (2017). The paper addresses the lack of attention paid to massive quark jets compared to extensive studies of light parton jets by Feige (2012), Field (2012), Dasgupta (2013), and Larkoski (2014). The proposed observable can serve as a probe of heavy quark TMD fragmentation function evolution and as a jet substructure observable for studying Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) properties.
