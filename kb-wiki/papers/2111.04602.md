# Self-consistent description of high-spin states in doubly magic $^{208}$Pb

> We analyze recent data on a long series of high-spin states in $^{208}$Pb with a self-consistent phonon-coupling model for nuclear excitations based on the Skyrme functionals. The model is the renormalized time-blocking approximation (RenTBA) which takes the coherent one-particle-one-hole (1p1h) states of the random-phase approximation (RPA) as starting point and develops from that more complex configurations beyond RPA. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first investigation of high spin states in $^{208}$Pb using self-consistent nuclear models. The interesting point here is that complex configurations are compulsory to describe the upper end of the long spin series at all. The data thus provide an ideal testing ground for phonon-coupling models as they give direct access to complex configurations. We find that standard Skyrme functionals which perform well in ground state properties and giant resonance excitations deliver at once an agreeable description of these high spin states.

## Key claim

Standard Skyrme energy-density functionals that perform well for ground states and giant resonances can describe the high-spin yrast states in $^{208}$Pb up to $I=30$ without refitting parameters, provided complex configurations beyond RPA (1p1h$\otimes$phonon and 2p2h$\otimes$phonon) are included; the authors note this is the first self-consistent investigation of these states.

## Method

The authors used the renormalized time-blocking approximation (RenTBA), a phonon-coupling model that starts from RPA 1p1h states and adds 1p1h$\otimes$phonon configurations, with phonons self-consistently optimized within the RenTBA loop. For spins $14 < I < 27$, the coupling between 1p1h$\otimes$phonon configurations was neglected (approximate RenTBA$_0$) because these states lie in the nucleon continuum and couple only weakly. For $I \geq 27$, 2p2h$\otimes$phonon configurations were included using an uncoupled generalization of RenTBA$_0$. Calculations employed three Skyrme parameter sets: SV-bas, SV-bas$_{-0.44}$, and SKXm$_{-0.49}$, with wave functions represented on a spherical grid in coordinate space (box radius 18 fm, particle energies up to 100 MeV).

## Key numbers

*   Experimental yrast state energies in $^{208}$Pb range from 8.027 MeV ($15^-_1$) to 13.5360 MeV ($26^-_1$) (Broda 2017).
*   The $26^-_2$ state has a 1p1h$\otimes$phonon configuration energy of 15.16 MeV and a 2p2h$\otimes$phonon configuration energy of 13.51 MeV (SV-bas), with the latter being the relevant configuration.
*   Discrete RPA (DRPA) calculations for the $15^-_1$ state give energies of 21.5 MeV (box radius 18 fm) and 14.7 MeV (box radius 27 fm), showing strong box-size dependence and inability to describe the experimental 8.027 MeV value.
*   The $13^-_1$ and $14^-_1$ phonons are pure 1p1h states (100% $\nu\, 1j_{15/2} \, 1i_{13/2}{}^{-1}$) according to the composition table.

## Context

This work extends the experimental and shell-model study of high-spin states in $^{208}$Pb by Broda (2017), which used large-scale shell model calculations with tuned single-particle energies and a microscopic two-body interaction. The present paper provides the first self-consistent description using Skyrme energy-density functionals and the RenTBA phonon-coupling model, previously successful for giant resonance spreading widths (Lyutorovich 2012). The results show that self-consistent models can compete with shell-model calculations for these states, and that the rotational trend observed in the data arises from single-particle rotation rather than collective cranking rotation, which is inhibited by the spherical shape and large shell gap of $^{208}$Pb.
