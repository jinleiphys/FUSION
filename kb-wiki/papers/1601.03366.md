# Short-distance RG-analysis of $X(3872)$ radiative decays

> We present a renormalization-group analysis of the $X(3872)$ radiative decays into $J/ ψγ$ and $ψ(2S) γ$. We assume a $D\bar{D}^{*}$ molecule for the $X$ long-distance structure and parametrize its short-distance physics as contact interactions. Using effective field theory techniques and power-divergence subtraction scheme, we find that short- and long-distance physics are equally important in these decays. Our calculations set a lower limit to the corresponding decay widths, which can in principle be tested experimentally. Our results may be used as guide to build models for the $X$ short-distance.

## Key claim

The paper argues that short- and long-distance physics are equally important in the radiative decays of the X(3872) into J/ψγ and ψ(2S)γ, contradicting earlier claims that a large ratio R would disfavor a molecular nature; the authors show that the ratio R from loop diagrams alone is not an observable because it depends strongly on the renormalization scale when power-divergence subtraction is used, and that proper renormalization requires including short-distance contact interactions.

## Method

The authors assume the X(3872) is a D D̄* molecule and use effective field theory with heavy-meson and chiral symmetries to compute loop diagrams for the radiative decays, regularizing loop integrals with the power-divergence subtraction (PDS) scheme. They impose the renormalization-group condition that the decay width be independent of the renormalization scale μ, combined with the experimental constraint R ≈ 2.46 from LHCb, to extract the short-distance contact couplings C_J/ψ and C_ψ' as functions of μ.

## Key numbers

- The ratio R ≡ Γ[X → γ ψ(2S)] / Γ[X → γ J/ψ] measured by LHCb is 2.46 ± 0.64 ± 0.29, where the first uncertainty is statistical and the second is systematic.
- The ratio R measured by BaBar in 2009 is 3.4 ± 1.4.
- Belle set an upper limit R < 2.1 at 90% confidence level.
- The non-relativistic coupling x_nr^(0) = 0.97^(+0.40)_(-0.97) GeV^(-1/2) was obtained from a non-relativistic relation with the binding energy at scale μ_0 = M_X.
- The ratio g_ψ'/g_J/ψ ≈ 1.67 is adopted as the central value from leptonic decay widths, with a natural band 1 ≲ g_ψ'/g_J/ψ ≲ 2.5.
- The renormalization scale range used is 3 GeV ≤ μ ≤ 5 GeV.
- To keep the short-distance couplings C_ψ real-valued within the EFT range, the constraints are Γ_γJ/ψ ≳ 7.5 keV and Γ_γψ' ≳ 18.5 keV.
- The total decay width of the X has an upper limit of 1.2 MeV.
- Swanson's molecular model calculation gives Γ(X → γ J/ψ) = 8 keV and Γ(X → γ ψ') = 0.03 keV; his quark model calculations give Γ(X → γ J/ψ) ∼ 70-140 keV and Γ(X → γ ψ') ∼ 95 keV.
- Dong and collaborators obtained an upper limit of 118.9 keV for Γ(X → γ J/ψ).

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory analysis of Guo et al., who studied only long-distance loop contributions to the radiative decays without explicitly including short-distance contact interactions. The authors adopt the PDS regularization scheme, originally developed for nucleon-nucleon interactions by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) and later applied to cold-atom and exotic meson systems by Braaten and collaborators (2004, 2005), to handle power-law divergences that are set to zero in the standard MS scheme. The results provide lower limits on the decay widths that can be tested experimentally and serve as a guide for building models of the X short-distance structure, including possible charmonium, tetraquark, or heavier molecular components.
