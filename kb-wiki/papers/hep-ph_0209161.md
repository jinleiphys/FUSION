# High-p_T Tomography of d+Au and Au+Au at SPS, RHIC, and LHC

> The interplay of nuclear effects on the p_T > 2 GeV inclusive hadron spectra in d+Au and Au+Au reactions at root(s) = 17, 200, 5500 GeV is compared to leading order perturbative QCD calculations for elementary p+p (p-bar+p) collisions. The competition between nuclear shadowing, Cronin effect, and jet energy loss due to medium-induced gluon radiation is predicted to lead to a striking energy dependence of the nuclear suppression/enhancement pattern in A+A reactions. We show that future d+Au data can used to disentangle the initial and final state effects.

## Key claim

The interplay of nuclear shadowing, Cronin effect, and jet energy loss due to medium-induced gluon radiation is predicted to lead to a striking energy dependence of the nuclear suppression/enhancement pattern in A+A reactions, with the surprising result that at RHIC energies these three effects conspire to yield an approximately constant suppression factor for neutral pions in the 4 to 20 GeV transverse momentum range.

## Method

The authors compute inclusive hadron spectra at leading order in perturbative QCD using GRV98 parton distribution functions and BKK fragmentation functions, incorporating nuclear shadowing via the EKS98 parameterization and modeling the Cronin effect through initial-state parton kT-broadening with a logarithmic enhancement term. Jet energy loss due to medium-induced gluon radiation is included via the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) formalism, where the fractional energy loss distribution P(epsilon,E) is computed accounting for longitudinal Bjorken expansion of the plasma with gluon rapidity density dN^g/dy as the free parameter. The nuclear modification ratio R_BA(p_T) is then calculated for d+Au and central Au+Au at sqrt(s)_NN = 17, 200, and 5500 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Fixed kT broadening in p+p: <k_T^2>_pp = 1.8 GeV^2 reproduces spectral shapes to within 30% for p_T > 2 GeV over the energy range of interest
- Cronin effect transport parameters: mu^2/lambda = 0.05 GeV^2/fm, lambda = 3.5 fm, c/mu^2 = 0.18 /GeV^2, consistent with mu^2/lambda = 0.064 +/- 0.036 GeV^2/fm from Drell-Yan fits
- At SPS (sqrt(s)_NN = 17 GeV), Cronin enhancement in Au+Au may reach a factor ~4 at p_T ~ 4-5 GeV
- At RHIC (sqrt(s)_NN = 200 GeV), Cronin enhancement peaks at p_T ~ 3 GeV with maximum values of 1.3 in d+Au and 1.6 in Au+Au
- At RHIC, predicted suppression pattern for central Au+Au: R_AA(p_T) ~ 0.3-0.2 for dN^g/dy ~ 800-1200
- At LHC (sqrt(s)_NN = 5500 GeV), shadowing and Cronin effect give <= 15% correction in the 6 <= p_T <= 100 GeV range
- Reducing plasmon mass by factor of two leads to ~25% more suppression at p_T = 5 GeV and <10% increased suppression at p_T = 20 GeV for RHIC energies

## Context

This work extends the jet tomography framework developed by Gyulassy, Levai, and Vitev (2002) and the GLV energy loss formalism to systematically compare d+Au and Au+Au reactions across three energy scales (SPS, RHIC, LHC). The authors address the puzzling absence of quenching in central Pb+Pb at SPS observed by WA98 (1998), explaining it as Cronin enhancement dominating over suppression, and predict that future d+Au data at RHIC can disentangle initial-state effects (shadowing and Cronin) from final-state jet quenching. This enables tomographic determination of initial gluon densities produced in heavy ion collisions.
