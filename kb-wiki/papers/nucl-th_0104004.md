# J/psi suppression at SPS and RHIC in the comovers approach

> The NA50 collaboration data on the $J/ψ$ suppression are compared with the results obtained in a comovers approach based on the Dual Parton Model (DPM). Predictions for the $J/ψ$ suppression versus the charged multiplicity - measured in the rapidity region of the dimuon trigger - are given for SPS and RHIC energies.

# J/ψ suppression at SPS and RHIC in the comovers approach

## Key claim
The NA50 data on J/ψ suppression in Pb-Pb collisions from peripheral collisions up to the knee of the transverse energy (E_T) distribution can be described in a comovers approach based on the Dual Parton Model, but beyond the knee the model underestimates the suppression, though theoretical uncertainties are large and the data in that region rely on a theoretical input that has not been experimentally verified.

## Method
The J/ψ survival probability is computed as the product of two factors: nuclear absorption of the c-cbar pair via the probabilistic Glauber model (with absorptive cross-section σ_abs = 4.5 mb), and suppression from interaction with comovers using an exponential formula involving the comover density in the dimuon trigger rapidity region (0 < y* < 1). The comover densities are determined in the Dual Parton Model as a linear combination of the number of participants and the number of binary collisions, with coefficients that depend on impact parameter and rapidity. The transverse energy E_T is related to the comover multiplicity in the NA50 calorimeter region via E_T(b) = (1/3) q N_co(b) with q = 0.6 GeV, and fluctuations are introduced to describe the tail of the E_T distribution.

## Key numbers
- σ_abs = 4.5 mb (absorptive cross-section for nuclear absorption of the c-cbar pair)
- σ_co = 1 mb (averaged interaction cross-section for comover suppression)
- N_f = 1.15 fm⁻² (comover density in pp collisions)
- q = 0.6 GeV (transverse energy per particle from fit to E_T distribution)
- a = 0.88 (parameter in the E_T-b correlation)
- E_in = 158 GeV (beam energy at SPS)
- For E_ZDC < 28 TeV, the J/ψ suppression is significantly steeper than the NA50 absorption model, indicating anomalous suppression already present in very peripheral collisions

## Context
This work extends the comovers approach previously developed by the same authors (Capella et al., earlier references) to describe J/ψ suppression in heavy-ion collisions, and compares with NA50 collaboration data at SPS energies. The approach contrasts with interpretations invoking one or two deconfining phase transitions. The model successfully reproduces the centrality dependence from peripheral collisions up to the knee of the E_T distribution, where both the NA50 absorption model and an alternative model (referred to as reference 7r) fail to describe peripheral data. The work provides predictions for J/ψ suppression versus charged multiplicity at both SPS and RHIC energies, enabling future experimental tests with the dimuon and multiplicity triggers in the same rapidity region.
