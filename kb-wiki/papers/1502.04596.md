# Quarks Production in the Quark-Gluon Plasma Created in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> In this article we report on our results about quark production and chemical equilibration of quark-gluon plasma. Our initial condition corresponds to a classic Yang-Mills spectrum, in which only gluon degrees of freedom are considered; the initial condition is then evolved to a quark-gluon plasma by means of relativistic transport theory with inelastic processes which permit the conversion of gluons to $q\bar{q}$ pairs. We then compare our results to the ones obtained with a standard Glauber model initialization. We find that regardless of the initial condition the final stage of the system contains an abundant percentage of $q\bar{q}$ pairs; moreover spanning the possible coupling from weak to strong we find that unless the coupling is unrealistically small, both production rate and final percentage of fermions is quite large.

## Key claim

The authors find that regardless of whether the initial condition is a thermal Glauber model or a classical Yang-Mills (CYM) glasma spectrum, the final stage of the quark-gluon plasma contains an abundant percentage of quark-antiquark pairs; this result holds unless the strong coupling constant is unrealistically small.

## Method

The authors initialize a pure gluon plasma using either a standard Glauber model or a CYM glasma spectrum, then evolve the system via relativistic transport theory (Boltzmann-Vlasov equation) with inelastic QCD processes (specifically $gg \leftrightarrow q\bar{q}$) that convert gluons to quark-antiquark pairs. They consider massless quasiparticles, massive quasiparticles with temperature-independent masses, and massive quasiparticles with temperature-dependent masses that reproduce the lattice QCD equation of state. The simulations are performed for Au-Au collisions at RHIC energy with impact parameter $b=2.5$ fm.

## Key numbers

* Initial gluon multiplicity: $dN_g/dy = 1040$ for Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200A$ GeV and $b=2.5$ fm.
* For $\alpha_s = 0.7$ (corresponding to $\eta/s \approx 2/4\pi$ at $T=0.3$ GeV) with CYM initialization, the quark formation time $\tau_q$ (time to reach 50% quarks plus antiquarks and 50% gluons) is approximately 0.15 fm/c.
* For a temperature-dependent running coupling (Eq. 4, with $\Lambda=0.2$ GeV, giving $\alpha_s \approx 0.4$ at $T=0.3$ GeV) with CYM initialization, $\tau_q \approx 0.6$ fm/c.
* For $\alpha_s = 0.3$ with CYM initialization, $\tau_q \approx 2$ fm/c.
* For massless quasiparticles with $\alpha_s = 0.7$, the ratio $R = (N_q + N_{\bar q})/N_g$ reaches approximately $9/4$ (the equilibrium value for massless gas) within about 1 fm/c.
* For massless quasiparticles with running coupling, $R$ saturates at approximately 1.7.
* For massless quasiparticles with $\alpha_s = 0.3$, $R$ reaches approximately 1.2.
* For massive quasiparticles with temperature-independent masses ($m_q = 0.5$ GeV, gluon-to-quark mass ratio 3/2), $R$ increases by about 30% compared to the massless case.
* For massive quasiparticles with temperature-dependent masses and $\alpha_s = 0.7$, $R$ reaches approximately 3 at the end of the evolution.
* Regardless of initial condition, at later times the fireball consists of about 80% quarks and 20% gluons.

## Context

This work extends the study of Scardina et al. (2013) by investigating the role of the initial non-equilibrium distribution (CYM glasma spectrum versus Glauber model) on quark production times and chemical equilibration, as well as the coupling dependence of these quantities. The results show that QCD inelastic processes are efficient enough to produce abundant quark-antiquark pairs even when neglecting possible quark formation before $\tau_0 = 0.2$ fm/c (CYM) or $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm/c (Glauber). The authors note that future work should include Bose-Einstein enhancement factors in the collision integral and compare with quark production via the Schwinger mechanism from flux tube shattering, following Ryblewski et al. (2013).
