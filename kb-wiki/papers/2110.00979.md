# Competition between $(γ,p)$ and $(γ,n)$ photo-disintegration yields

> A comprehensive analysis of the competition between photo-proton and photo-neutron disintegration yields is undertaken by constraining photo-absorption data with physics principles -- namely, dipole sum rules, decay properties to open channels, isospin selection rules and statistical evaporation from compound nucleus formation. The trend and magnitude of disintegration yields for self-conjugate nuclei are in agreement with the evaporation model of Blatt and Weisskopf. A substantial improvement is found between the exponential trend presented in this work and that determined by Morinaga using a similar approach. This work allows for the evaluation of frequently missing or inconsistent photo-proton contributions, which impacts the photo-disintegration of light and neutron-deficient nuclei, where photo-proton contributions are relevant.

## Key claim
The exponential trend of the photo-proton to photo-neutron yield ratio for self-conjugate nuclei derived from the evaporation model of Blatt and Weisskopf shows substantial improvement over the earlier trend determined by Morinaga (1955), but the work acknowledges that the ratio is constrained by physics principles rather than providing a definitive prediction, and that missing or inconsistent photo-proton data remain a limitation.

## Method
The author compiled experimental photo-proton and photo-neutron cross sections for A=4n self-conjugate nuclei from 12C to 40Ca, selecting data from at least two experiments with an upper integration limit of 30 MeV. The consistency of the selected data was benchmarked against three physics principles: decay properties to open channels (counting discrete levels in residual nuclei below excitation energies), isospin selection rules (using Clebsch-Gordan coefficients for T=1 GDR decay), and statistical evaporation from compound nucleus formation (using the evaporation model of Blatt and Weisskopf with level density parameters determined by counting levels within the relevant excitation energies).

## Key numbers
- For 12C: experimental ratio r_pn = 2.0(4), with sigma(gamma,p) = 110 MeV·mb and sigma(gamma,n) = 54 MeV·mb
- For 16O: experimental ratio r_pn = 1.7(3), with sigma(gamma,p) = 133 MeV·mb and sigma(gamma,n) = 81 MeV·mb
- For 20Ne: experimental ratio r_pn = 2.0(4), with sigma(gamma,p) = 231 MeV·mb and sigma(gamma,n) = 115 MeV·mb
- For 24Mg: experimental ratio r_pn = 2.4(6), with sigma(gamma,p) = 180 MeV·mb and sigma(gamma,n) = 76 MeV·mb
- For 28Si: experimental ratio r_pn = 2.6(4), with sigma(gamma,p) = 230 MeV·mb and sigma(gamma,n) = 90 MeV·mb
- For 32S: experimental ratio r_pn = 3.1(6), with sigma(gamma,p) = 350 MeV·mb and sigma(gamma,n) = 112 MeV·mb
- For 40Ca: experimental ratio r_pn = 5.3(7), with sigma(gamma,p) = 470 MeV·mb and sigma(gamma,n) = 88 MeV·mb
- The total photo-absorption cross sections sigma_0 exhaust the TRK sum rule (60 NZ/A (1+Delta)) within uncertainties for most nuclei, e.g., 12C: sigma_0 = 164(17) MeV·mb vs. TRK = 180 MeV·mb; 40Ca: sigma_0 = 560(90) MeV·mb vs. TRK = 600 MeV·mb
- The exponential fit from the evaporation model yields r_pn values ranging from 1.1 (12C) to 4.6 (40Ca), compared to the open-channels ratio N_p/N_n ranging from 1.0 (12C, 16O) to 3.5 (40Ca) and the isospin intensity ratio I(T+1)/I(T) ranging from 2.3 (12C) to 2.7 (40Ca)

## Context
This work extends the earlier approach of Morinaga (1955) for estimating photo-proton to photo-neutron yield ratios in self-conjugate nuclei using the evaporation model, finding substantial improvement in the exponential trend. It also builds on the systematic evaluation of photo-neutron data by Dietrich and Berman (1988) and the IAEA-led update to the photo-absorption data library (Kawano et al.). The work enables evaluation of frequently missing or inconsistent photo-proton contributions, which is relevant for light and neutron-deficient nuclei where photo-proton contributions are important, and motivates further systematic studies at modern facilities such as HIGS, ELBE, and ELI-NP.
