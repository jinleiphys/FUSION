# Effect of Coriolis force on the shear viscosity of quark matter: A nonrelativistic description

> Shear viscosity becomes anisotropic in a rotating medium. It is discovered here that for rotating thermalized quantum systems such as those created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, the coeffficient of shear viscosity breaks up into five independent components. Similar phenomena were also discovered for quark-gluon plasma in the presence of the magnetic field. Like the Lorentz force at a finite magnetic field, the Coriolis force also creates anisotropic viscosity at nonzero rotation. As a first approach, for simplicity, the calculations are done in the nonrelativistic prescription, with a future proposal to extend it toward a relativistic description. Introducing the Coriolis force term in relaxation time approximated Boltzmann transport equation, we have found different effective relaxation times along the parallel, perpendicular, and Hall directions in terms of actual relaxation time and rotating time period. Comparing the present formalism with the finite magnetic field picture, we have shown the equivalence of roles between the rotating and cyclotron time periods, where the rotating time period is inverse of twice the angular velocity.

## Key claim

For a rotating thermalized quantum system such as quark matter created in heavy-ion collisions, the shear viscosity coefficient becomes anisotropic and breaks into five independent components due to the Coriolis force, analogous to the effect of the Lorentz force in a magnetized medium. The authors note that this is a first nonrelativistic approach, with a future proposal to extend it toward a relativistic description.

## Method

The authors introduced the Coriolis force term into the Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation (RTA), writing the equation in a rotating frame with constant angular velocity. They solved for the perturbed distribution function by expressing it as a sum of five basis tensors constructed from the fluid velocity and the angular velocity unit vector, then derived the five independent shear viscosity coefficients by equating coefficients of independent tensor structures. The calculation was performed for nonrelativistic matter, with the centrifugal and Euler forces neglected for simplicity.

## Key numbers

* The rotating time period is defined as tau_Omega = 1/(2*Omega), where Omega is the angular velocity of the medium.
* The effective relaxation times along the parallel, perpendicular, and Hall directions are expressed in terms of the actual relaxation time tau_c and the rotating time period tau_Omega, with denominators containing factors of 1+4(tau_c/tau_Omega)^2 and 1+(tau_c/tau_Omega)^2.
* The orbital angular momentum in typical off-central heavy-ion collisions is of the order of 10^3 to 10^7 hbar, depending on impact parameter, collision energy, and system size.

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of shear viscosity in magnetized matter (Tuchin 2011, Ghosh 2018, Mohanty 2018, Dey 2019, Dash 2020) to the case of rotating matter, exploiting the formal similarity between the Lorentz force (q v x B) and the Coriolis force (2m v x Omega). The results enable future calculations of anisotropic transport coefficients for spin-hydrodynamics simulations of quark-gluon plasma created in peripheral heavy-ion collisions, where both large angular momentum and magnetic fields are present.
