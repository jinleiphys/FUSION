# Double -- photon exclusive processes with heavy quark -- heavy antiquark pairs in high-energy Pb-Pb collisions at LHC

> The cross section for exclusive heavy quark and heavy antiquark pair ($Q \bar Q$) production in peripheral ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions is calculated for the LHC energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.5 TeV. Here we consider only processes with photon--photon interactions and omit diffractive contributions. We present results in the impact parameter equivalent photon approximation (EPA) and compare some of them with results obtained by exact calculations of the Feynman diagrams in the momentum space. We include both $Q \bar Q$, $Q \bar Q g$ and $Q \bar Q q \bar q$ final states as well as photon single-resolved components. Realistic charge densities in nuclei were taken in the calculation. The different components give contributions of the same order of magnitude to the nuclear cross section. The cross sections found here are smaller than those for the diffractive photon-pomeron mechanism and larger than diffractive pomeron-pomeron discussed in the literature.

## Key claim

The cross sections for exclusive heavy quark pair production in peripheral Pb-Pb collisions at LHC are calculated for the photon-photon mechanism only, omitting diffractive contributions; the authors find that the different components (direct Born, QCD corrections, four-quark final states, and single-resolved processes) give contributions of the same order of magnitude to the nuclear cross section, with the total cross sections being smaller than those for diffractive photon-pomeron processes but larger than those for diffractive pomeron-pomeron processes.

## Method

The authors calculate the cross section for exclusive heavy quark-antiquark pair production in peripheral Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energy using the impact parameter equivalent photon approximation (b-space EPA) with realistic nuclear charge densities and form factors. They include the direct Born process, leading-order QCD corrections (gluon bremsstrahlung and virtual corrections), four-quark final states (Q Qbar q qbar) calculated in the color dipole framework, and photon single-resolved components using gluon distributions. The b-space EPA results for the direct Born contribution are compared with exact Feynman diagram calculations in momentum space.

## Key numbers

* Total cross section for PbPb → PbPb c cbar (Born direct, b-space EPA with absorption b>14 fm): 1.00 mb (for b<1000 fm)
* Total cross section for PbPb → PbPb b bbar (Born direct, b-space EPA with absorption b>14 fm): 2.05 μb (for b<1000 fm)
* Leading-order QCD corrections (b-space EPA with absorption): 0.36 mb for c cbar, 0.83 μb for b bbar
* Four-quark component (b-space EPA with absorption): 0.67 mb for c cbar q qbar, 6.98 μb for b bbar q qbar
* Single-resolved component (b-space EPA with absorption): 0.39 mb for c cbar, 0.97 μb for b bbar
* Total cross section including all mechanisms (b-space EPA with absorption): 2.47 mb for c cbar, 10.83 μb for b bbar
* Event rates over 10^6 s run at LHC (luminosity 4.2×10^26 cm^-2 s^-1): N(c cbar) = 10.4 × 10^5, N(b bbar) = 4.6 × 10^3
* Heavy quark masses used: m_c = 1.5 GeV, m_b = 4.75 GeV
* Lead nucleus radius: R_A = 1.2 A^(1/3) ≈ 7 fm

## Context

This work extends previous studies of exclusive lepton pair production in heavy ion collisions (Klusek-Gawenda and Szczurek, 2010) to heavy quark production, where the inclusion of realistic form factors was shown to be essential. The authors compare their photon-photon mechanism results with diffractive mechanisms estimated by Machado and coworkers (2010), finding that the photon-photon cross sections are smaller than photon-pomeron diffractive processes but larger than pomeron-pomeron diffractive processes. The results enable future combined analyses of all production mechanisms using differential distributions, with the reliably calculable QED processes serving as a baseline for understanding the more complex diffractive contributions.
