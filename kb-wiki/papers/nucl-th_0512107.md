# Scattering processes in antiprotonic hydrogen - hydrogen atom collisions

> The elastic scattering, Stark transitions and Coulomb deexcitation of excited antiprotonic hydrogen atom in collisions with hydrogenic atom have been studied in the framework of the fully quantum-mechanical close-coupling method for the first time. The total cross sections $σ_{nl \to n'l'}(E)$ and averaged on the initial angular momentum $l$ cross sections $σ_{n\to n'}(E)$ have been calculated for the initial states of $(\bar{p}p)_{n}$ atoms with the principal quantum number $n=3 - 14 $ and at the relative energies $E=0.05 - 50$ eV. The energy shifts of the $ns$ states due to the strong interaction and relativistic effects are taken into account. Some of our results are compared with the semiclassical calculations.

## Key claim

For the first time, the elastic scattering, Stark transitions, and Coulomb deexcitation of excited antiprotonic hydrogen atoms colliding with hydrogenic atoms have been calculated using a fully quantum-mechanical close-coupling method, with the caveat that the strong interaction energy shifts of the \(ns\) states (taken as repulsive and real-valued) are included, which substantially suppresses transitions involving those states.

## Method

The authors solved the four-body Schrödinger equation for the \((\bar{p}p) + H\) system using a close-coupling expansion in a basis of hydrogen-like wave functions for the exotic atom and the target hydrogen atom, with the total wave function expanded in terms of total angular momentum \(J\) and parity. The coupled radial equations were integrated numerically via the Numerov method, and the \(T\)-matrix was obtained from the real \(K\)-matrix. The interaction potential included all two-body Coulomb terms, and the target electron was fixed in the \(1s\) state; only open channels with \(n' \le n\) were included, and the \(ns\)-state energy shifts were taken from the measured spin-averaged \(1s\) shift scaled as \(\epsilon_{1s}/n^3\).

## Key numbers

- The spin-averaged \(1s\) energy shift used for the antiprotonic hydrogen atom is \(\epsilon_{1s} = (721 \pm 14)\ \text{eV}\).
- Total cross sections \(\sigma_{nl \to n'l'}(E)\) and \(l\)-averaged cross sections \(\sigma_{n \to n'}(E)\) were calculated for initial principal quantum numbers \(n = 3\) to \(14\).
- The relative collision energy range covered is \(E = 0.05\) to \(50\ \text{eV}\).
- All cross sections were calculated with an accuracy better than \(0.1\%\).
- For \(n=8\) at \(E_{\text{cm}} = 1.4\ \text{eV}\), the contribution of \(\Delta n > 1\) transitions to Coulomb deexcitation is approximately \(50\%\) of the \(\Delta n = 1\) contribution.

## Context

This work extends the fully quantum-mechanical close-coupling treatment previously applied to muonic hydrogen (Popov and Pomerantsev, 2004) and pionic hydrogen (Popov and Pomerantsev, 2005) to the antiprotonic hydrogen system. It contrasts with the semiclassical straight-line trajectory approximation of Leon and Bethe (1962) and the semiclassical model used by others (e.g., 2004) for higher \(n\) states, finding that the semiclassical results give a different energy dependence and smaller cross sections at low energies. The results provide a first-principles input for realistic cascade kinetics calculations of antiprotonic hydrogen, enabling improved analysis of precision spectroscopy data and X-ray yields.
