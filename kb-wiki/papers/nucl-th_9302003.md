# Spin and Flavour: Concluding Remarks

> Summmary talk at the Workshop on Sin and Flavour in Hadronic and Electromagnetic Interactions, Turin (Italy), september 1992, to appear in the Proceedings,

## Key claim
The workshop summary highlights that while perturbative QCD (pQCD) fails to explain many observed spin effects in hadronic collisions, a combination of modern concepts (pQCD, Skyrmions) and conventional approaches (meson exchanges, potential models, Regge trajectories) remains necessary; the equivalence between quark and hadron pictures is the basis of QCD sum rules but remains to be demonstrated for the actual number of colours and dimensions.

## Method
The paper is a summary talk reviewing results presented at the Workshop on Spin and Flavour in Hadronic and Electromagnetic Interactions (Turin, September 1992). It surveys experimental and theoretical contributions on strangeness (hidden strangeness, hyperons, hypernuclei, dibaryons), charm (charmonium spectroscopy from E760 at Fermilab, open charm, charm in nuclei), spin physics (antiproton-proton scattering, Lambda production, high-energy spin effects), and future facilities (SuperLEAR, DAΦNE, CEBAF, EEF). The author provides commentary on the underlying physics and open questions.

## Key numbers
- The mass of the charmonium 1P1 state coincides with the centre-of-gravity of the triplet states to within 1 MeV, confirming that the spin-spin term is very short-ranged and does not affect ℓ>0 partial waves.
- The ratio (Ψ' - η'_c)/(J/ψ - η_c) = 93 MeV / 112 MeV from e+e- data is too large to be easily reproduced in potential models.
- For S=-2 hypernuclei, the double-Lambda binding energy difference δB_ΛΛ ≡ B_ΛΛ - 2B_Λ ≈ 4-5 MeV, implying |V_ΛN| < |V_ΛΛ| < |V_NN|.
- The H dibaryon (uuddss) was predicted by Jaffe (1977) with a binding of ~150 MeV from chromomagnetic interactions, but stability suffers from chromoelectric forces, kinetic energy, and SU(3) breaking.
- The ppbar → ΛΛbar reaction always occurs in the triplet state, rather than 75% of the time as expected without spin-dependent forces.

## Context
This summary extends the discussions from the 1992 Turin Workshop, reviewing results from LEAR (Amsler, Bressani), the E760 experiment at Fermilab (Palestini), and theoretical work on hypernuclei (Gal), charmonium (MR), and spin effects (Anselmino, Bradamante). It enables future work on SuperLEAR for quarkonium and hybrid spectroscopy, DAΦNE for kaon decay and CP violation, and CEBAF/EEF for quark confinement studies. The paper notes that simple potential models often work better than sophisticated approaches including continuum coupling, and that the H dibaryon remains controversial with no experimental confirmation.
