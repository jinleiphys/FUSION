# The Juelich hyperon-nucleon model revisited

> A one-boson-exchange model for the hyperon-nucleon (ΛN, ΣN) interaction is presented. The model incorporates the standard one boson exchanges of the lowest pseudoscalar and vector meson multiplets with coupling constants fixed by SU(6) flavor symmetry relations. As the main new feature of the model, the contributions in the scalar--isoscalar (σ) and vector--isovector (ρ) exchange channels are now constrained by a microscopic model of correlated ππand K \bar K exchange. Additional short-ranged ingredients of the model in the scalar--isovector (a_0) and scalar--isospin-1/2 (κ) channels are likewise viewed as arising from meson-meson correlations but are treated phenomenologically. With this model a satisfactory reproduction of the available hyperon-nucleon data can be achieved.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new one-boson-exchange model for hyperon-nucleon interactions where the scalar-isoscalar (σ) and vector-isovector (ρ) exchange contributions are constrained by a microscopic model of correlated ππ and K̅K exchange, rather than treated as free parameters; however, the short-ranged contributions in the scalar-isovector (a₀) and scalar-isospin-1/2 (κ) channels are still treated phenomenologically.

## Method

The authors computed correlated ππ and K̅K exchange potentials for the ΛN and ΣN systems using a dispersion-theoretical approach, starting from a field-theoretical model for B̅B' → ππ, K̅K Born amplitudes and ππ–K̅K coupled-channel scattering amplitudes. The resulting spectral functions were parameterized as effective σ and ρ exchanges with sharp masses (m_σ = 550 MeV, m_ρ = 770 MeV) and t-dependent effective coupling strengths fixed at t=0. The remaining pseudoscalar and vector meson exchanges (π, K, ω, K*) were included with coupling constants determined by SU(6) flavor symmetry, while the ω', a₀(980), and κ exchanges were treated phenomenologically with free parameters fitted to hyperon-nucleon scattering data.

## Key numbers

- Effective σ coupling strength G_{ΛN→ΛN}/4π = 3.52 at t=0
- Effective σ coupling strength G_{ΣN→ΣN}/4π = 2.92 at t=0
- Effective ρ coupling strengths for ΣN channel: VV = 1.26, VT = 1.24, TV = 8.09, TT = 8.07 (all divided by 4π)
- Effective ρ coupling strengths for ΛN→ΣN transition: VV = −0.43, VT = 3.72, TV = −1.32, TT = 21.00 (all divided by 4π)
- σ meson mass set to 550 MeV; ρ meson mass set to 770 MeV
- Cutoff masses: Λ_σ = 2.8 GeV (for spectral function parameterization), Λ_ρ = 2.5 GeV; for effective potential: Λ_σ = 2.5 GeV (ΛN) and 1.6 GeV (ΣN), Λ_ρ = 1.25 GeV (ΛN→ΣN) and 1.8 GeV (ΣN)
- ω' meson mass = 1120 MeV; a₀(980) mass = 983 MeV; κ meson mass = 1000 MeV
- NNπ coupling constant g_{NNπ}/√(4π) = 3.795
- NΛK coupling constant g_{NΛK}/√(4π) = −3.944

## Context

This work extends the earlier Jülich hyperon-nucleon models (Holz, 1993) by replacing the phenomenological σ and ρ exchanges with microscopically derived correlated ππ and K̅K exchange contributions, following the formalism developed by Reuber et al. (1997) for baryon-baryon channels with strangeness S=0, −1, and −2. The model differs from the Nijmegen group's approach (Nijmegen models D, F, NSC, NSC97) and the Ehime potential, which treat scalar mesons as genuine SU(3) multiplet members, and from the Tübingen constituent quark model, by instead deriving the intermediate-range attraction from correlated meson-meson dynamics. This enables a more consistent extension to strangeness S=−2 systems and the H-dibaryon problem, and provides a framework for future calculations of hyperon-nucleon scattering without undetermined σ-coupling parameters at the strange vertices.
