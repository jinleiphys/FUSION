# Electron Beam Single-Spin Asymmetries in the Resonance Region with Final Hadrons Observed

> We study beam-transverse and normal single-spin asymmetry for electron-proton inelastic scattering in the resonance region for the case where a pion is observed in the final state. There is no beam single-spin asymmetry if the observed particle has single-photon exchange as its only interaction, but this is naturally circumvented when one observes a strongly interacting final state particle. The asymmetries are not large as there is an electron mass divided by momentum transfer suppression. We present results for the cases where the final electron and a pion are both observed, with asymmetries of order tens of ppm, and where only a final pion is observed, with asymmetries of order one ppm.

# Electron Beam Single-Spin Asymmetries in the Resonance Region with Final Hadrons Observed

## Key claim

Beam single-spin asymmetries in electron-proton inelastic scattering can arise from one-photon exchange when a strongly interacting final-state particle (a pion) is observed, circumventing the usual theorem that such asymmetries vanish for single-photon exchange. The asymmetries are suppressed by the electron mass divided by momentum transfer, yielding values of order tens of ppm when both electron and pion are observed, and order one ppm when only the pion is observed.

## Method

The authors calculate beam-normal and beam-sideways single-spin asymmetries for the reaction $ep \to e\pi N$ in the resonance region using the one-photon exchange approximation, with strong interaction phases obtained from the MAID multipole amplitude parametrization (Drechsel and Tiator, 2007). For the case where both final electron and pion are observed, the cross section is evaluated directly at fixed hadronic mass $W = 1.232$ GeV (the $\Delta(1232)$ resonance). For the case where only the pion is observed, a two-dimensional phase space integral over the unobserved electron and nucleon directions is performed in the electron-nucleon center-of-mass frame, with kinematic transformations between the lab frame, the electron-nucleon CM, and the pion-nucleon CM.

## Key numbers

- Beam-normal asymmetry $B_n$ for $ep \to e\pi N$ with both electron and pion observed reaches a peak magnitude of approximately **37 ppm** at $\theta_\pi = \pi/2$ rad, for $\epsilon_i = 1.155$ GeV, $\theta_e = 7.9^\circ$, and $W = 1.232$ GeV.
- Beam-sideways asymmetry $B_s$ for the same kinematics reaches a maximum value of approximately **16 ppm**.
- For the case where only the pion is observed ($e + p \to \pi^+ + X$), the beam-normal asymmetry achieves a maximum magnitude on the order of **1 ppm** for $\epsilon_i = 1.155$ GeV and outgoing pion lab angle $7.9^\circ$.
- The maximum hadronic mass $m_X$ for the pion-only case is approximately **1.58 GeV** for the kinematics used.
- The upper limit of pion momentum $k_\pi$ for the pion-only case is approximately **1.132 GeV** (retaining non-zero pion mass) or **1.142 GeV** (neglecting pion mass).

## Context

This work extends calculations of beam single-spin asymmetries from elastic electron-proton scattering (where two-photon exchange is required) into the inelastic resonance region, using the MAID multipole analysis (Drechsel and Tiator, 2007) for strong interaction phases. The results are relevant to experiments such as QWeak (Androic, 2013) and Jefferson Lab measurements of beam-normal asymmetries in the resonance region (Nuruzzaman, 2015). The small asymmetries found (tens of ppm or less) indicate that pion mis-identification contamination in electron-observed asymmetry experiments is negligible for most experiments, though corrections may be needed for experiments measuring asymmetries at the 10 ppm level. Future work could extend the calculation to two-photon exchange for a more accurate description of beam-spin asymmetries when only the final electron is observed.
