# Collisions of protons with light nuclei shed new light on nucleon structure

> The high rates of multi-parton interactions at the LHC can provide a unique opportunity to study the multi-parton structure of the hadron. To this purpose high energy collisions of protons with nuclei are particularly suitable. The rates of multi-parton interactions depend in fact both on the partonic multiplicities and on the distributions of partons in transverse space, which produce different effects on the cross section in pA collisions, as a function of the atomic mass number A. Differently with respect to the case of multi-parton interactions in pp collisions, the possibility of changing the atomic mass number provides thus an additional handle to distinguish the diverse contributions. Some relevant features of double parton interactions in pD collisions have been discussed in a previous paper. In the present paper we show how the effects of double and triple correlation terms of the multi-parton structure can be disentangled, by comparing the rates of multiple parton interactions in collisions of protons with D, Tritium and 3He.

## Key claim

By comparing the rates of multiple parton interactions (MPI) in collisions of protons with Deuterium, Tritium, and Helium-3, the effects of double and triple correlation terms in the multi-parton structure of hadrons can be disentangled from the effects due to correlations in transverse parton coordinates, which are unavoidably linked in proton-proton collisions.

## Method

The authors derived analytical expressions for double and triple parton scattering cross sections in proton collisions with light nuclei (Deuterium, Tritium, and Helium-3), using a covariant Feynman diagram formalism with effective vertices for non-perturbative dynamics. They distinguished cases based on the number of bound nucleons participating in the hard interaction (one or two for double scattering; one for triple scattering) and separated diagonal terms from interference terms arising from the nuclear wave function. The analysis used non-relativistic nuclear wave functions in the rest frame of the nucleus, with all particles treated as spinless bosons and proton/neutron masses considered equal.

## Key numbers

- The cross section for double parton scattering on a Deuteron with one interacting nucleon and one spectator is expressed as an integral over parton densities, hard scattering cross sections, and the nuclear wave function squared \(|\tilde\Psi_D(Z;B)|^2/Z^2\), with the nuclear fractional momentum \(Z\) integrated from 0 to 2.
- The diagonal term of double scattering on a Deuteron with both nucleons interacting involves the nuclear density \(|\Psi(Z;B)|^2\) and the one-body parton densities \(\Gamma(\bar x_1/Z;b_1)\) and \(\Gamma(\bar x_2/(2-Z);b_2)\).
- The interference term for double scattering on a Deuteron with both nucleons interacting requires the nuclear wave function at different \(Z\) values, with the mismatch \(\delta(Z-Z'-\bar x_1+\bar x_2)\) appearing in the cross section expression.
- For triple scattering on Tritium with one interacting nucleon, the three-body parton densities \(\Gamma(x_1,x_2,x_3;\beta_1,\beta_2,\beta_3)\) and \(\Gamma(z_1,z_2,z_3;b_1,b_2,b_3)\) are defined with factors \(x_1x_2x_3/(1-x_1-x_2-x_3)\) and \(z_1z_2z_3/(1-z_1-z_2-z_3)\) respectively.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of double parton interactions in proton-Deuteron collisions (Calucci and Treleani, 2010) to include triple parton interactions and the nuclei Tritium and Helium-3. It builds on the general formulation of multiple parton interactions within QCD developed by Diehl, Blok, Gaunt, and others, and on the observation by Strikman (2001) that proton-nucleus collisions can provide a unique handle on multi-parton structure. The paper enables experimental extraction of correlation parameters in multi-parton distributions by comparing MPI rates across different light nuclear targets at LHC energies.
