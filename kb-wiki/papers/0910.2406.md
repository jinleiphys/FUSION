# Improved transverse (e,e') response function of 3He at intermediate momentum transfers

> The transverse electron scattering response function of 3He is studied in the quasi-elastic peak region for momentum transfers between 500 and 700 MeV/c. A conventional description of the process leads to results at a substantial variation with experiment. To improve the results, the present calculation is done in a reference frame (the ANB or Active Nucleon Breit frame) which diminishes the influence of relativistic effects on nuclear states. The laboratory frame response function is then obtained via a kinematics transformation. In addition, a one-body nuclear current operator is employed that includes all leading order relativistic corrections. Multipoles of this operator are listed. It is shown that the use of the ANB frame leads to a sizable shift of the quasi-elastic peak to lower energy and, contrary to the relativistic current, also to an increase of the peak height. The additionally considered meson exchange current contribution is quite small in the peak region. In comparison with experiment one finds an excellent agreement of the peak positions. The peak height agrees well with experiment for the lowest considered momentum transfer (500 MeV/c), but tends to be too high for higher momentum transfer (10% at 700 MeV/c).

## Key claim

The authors show that using the Active Nucleon Breit (ANB) reference frame, combined with a one-body current operator including all leading-order relativistic corrections, yields excellent agreement with experimental peak positions for the transverse (e,e') response of 3He at momentum transfers between 500 and 700 MeV/c, but the theoretical peak height overestimates experiment by up to 10% at the highest momentum transfer (700 MeV/c), suggesting that unincluded relativistic effects (wave function boost, dynamical effects) are increasing in importance.

## Method

The transverse response function R_T of 3He was calculated in the quasi-elastic peak region using the AV18 NN potential plus UIX three-nucleon force, with full final state interaction included via the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method. The calculation was performed in the ANB reference frame to minimize relativistic effects on nuclear states, then transformed to the laboratory frame; the one-body current operator included all relativistic corrections up to order M^{-3}, and non-relativistic meson exchange currents consistent with the AV18 potential were added.

## Key numbers

- Peak position shifts from lab to ANB frame: 8.7 MeV at q=500 MeV/c, 16.7 MeV at q=600 MeV/c, and 29.3 MeV at q=700 MeV/c.
- Peak height increase from lab to ANB frame: 5.6% at q=500 MeV/c, 10.3% at q=600 MeV/c, and 16.7% at q=700 MeV/c.
- Relativistic corrections to the one-body current reduce peak heights by 6.2% at q=500 MeV/c, 8.5% at q=600 MeV/c, and 11.3% at q=700 MeV/c.
- Meson exchange current contributions increase peak heights by 3.2% at q=500 MeV/c, 2.7% at q=600 MeV/c, and 2.2% at q=700 MeV/c.
- Theoretical peak height overestimates experiment by about 5% at q=600 MeV/c and 10% at q=700 MeV/c.
- Pion production thresholds: approximately 180 MeV at q=500 MeV/c, 200 MeV at q=600 MeV/c, and 220 MeV at q=700 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the longitudinal response of trinucleons (Efros et al., 2005) to the transverse response of 3He, using the same ANB frame method that proved efficient for the longitudinal case. It improves upon earlier non-relativistic calculations of the transverse response (Efros et al., 2004; Hann et al., 2004; Bochum group) by including all leading-order relativistic corrections to the one-body current, which had previously been applied only to deuteron electrodisintegration (Ritz et al., 1997) and elastic magnetic form factors of trinucleons. The results enable future investigation of remaining relativistic effects (wave function boost, dynamical effects) to improve agreement with experiment at higher momentum transfers.
