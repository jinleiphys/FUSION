# Fitting and selecting scattering data

> The main purpose of scattering experiments is to unveil the underlying structure of the colliding particles and their interaction. Typically one measures scattering observables (cross sections and polarizations) at discrete angles and energies and mutually consistent data may validate or falsify proposed theories or models. However, the accumulation of data from different laboratories while potentially improves the statistical significance it may sometimes generate mutually inconsistent data as a side-effect. Thus, some decision has to be made on what are the maximal amount of data which are mutually compatible. We show elastic $πN $ and $NN$ scattering as prominent examples where this selection is called for. We discuss how it can be done in a self-consistent manner invoking a principle of maximal consensus of the database and with the help of a sufficiently flexible model involving a minimal number of theoretical assumptions. In the NN case this has become possible with a combination of long distance field theoretical constraints at the hadronic level such as pion exchanges and electromagnetic effects and a coarse graining of the unknown interaction over the shortest de Broglie wavelength being probed in the scattering process.

# Fitting and selecting scattering data

## Key claim
The paper argues that when scattering data from multiple laboratories accumulate over time, mutually inconsistent data inevitably arise, and a self-consistent selection procedure based on a principle of maximal consensus can identify the largest subset of mutually compatible data. The authors show that this selection is possible using a sufficiently flexible model with minimal theoretical assumptions, and that in the NN case the coarse-graining approach combined with long-distance field-theoretical constraints provides such a model.

## Method
The authors apply a multi-energy partial-wave analysis to elastic πN and NN scattering data, using a coarse-grained potential that separates the interaction into a short-range piece represented by delta-shell potentials at discrete radii and a long-range piece from quantum field theory (pion exchanges and electromagnetic effects). They implement an iterative data-selection procedure: fit all data, remove data sets with improbably high or low contributions to the total χ² using a 3σ criterion, refit the remaining data, reapply the 3σ criterion to all data, and repeat until no further data are excluded or recovered.

## Key numbers
- For NN scattering at T_LAB ≤ 350 MeV, the fit to all 8173 data points yields χ²/ν = 1.41; after selection, 6713 data points remain with χ²/ν = 1.04.
- When pion-nucleon coupling constants are included as fitting parameters, the selected database yields χ²/ν = 1.025.
- The coarse-graining boundary radius r_c is taken between 1.8 fm and 3 fm, with the grid spacing Δr ~ 1/p_max corresponding to the shortest de Broglie wavelength probed.
- For πN scattering at T_LAB^π ≤ 200 MeV and NN scattering at T_LAB ≤ 350 MeV, the standard deviation N_σ from Eq. (3) is plotted over 5-year steps from 1955–2015, showing values far above the 1σ confidence band.

## Context
This work extends the long tradition of phase-shift analysis dating back to Fermi and Metropolis (1952) and the Livermore fits, and directly builds on the Granada group's NN coarse-graining approach (Perez 2013, Perez 2014). The paper addresses the persistent problem of mutually inconsistent scattering data, which was recognized as early as Perring (1963). The selection procedure enables the construction of the largest self-consistent NN database below 350 MeV (the Granada-2013 database) and provides a framework that could be extended to other hadronic systems such as πN, ππ, or K N scattering.
