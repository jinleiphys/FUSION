# The calculation of prompt fission neutron spectrum for 233U(n,f) reaction by the semi-empirical method

> The prompt fission neutron spectra for neutron-induced fission of 233U for low energy neutrons (below 6 MeV) are calculated using the nuclear evaporation theory with a semi-empirical method, in which the partition of the total excitation energy between the fission fragments for the nth+233U fission reactions are determined with the available experimental and evaluation data. The calculated prompt fission neutron spectra agree well with the experimental data. The proportions of high- energy outgoing neutrons of prompt fission neutron spectrum versus incident neutron energies are investigated with the theoretical spectra, and the results are consistent with the systematics. We conclude that the semi-empirical method is sound, and it could be a useful tool for prompt fission neutron spectra evaluation.

## Key claim

The semi-empirical method, which determines the partition of total excitation energy between fission fragments from available experimental and evaluated data, produces prompt fission neutron spectra for 233U(n,f) that agree well with experimental data for incident neutron energies below 6 MeV, but the authors note that for the 0.55 MeV case the calculated spectrum appears somewhat harder from 6.0 to 9.0 MeV, and no experimental data exist above 12 MeV to validate the tail region.

## Method

The authors extract the energy partition of total excitation energy between complementary fission fragments for thermal neutron-induced 233U fission from experimental and evaluated data on prompt neutron multiplicity, average neutron kinetic energy, and gamma-ray energy. For other incident neutron energies below 6 MeV, they extend this partition using systematic parameters of fission fragment mass distributions. They calculate nuclear temperatures for each fragment using the Fermi gas model at higher excitation energies and a constant temperature model at lower energies, then compute the center-of-mass neutron spectrum via the Weisskopf evaporation formula, transform to the laboratory system, and synthesize the total spectrum using chain yields and prompt neutron numbers.

## Key numbers

- The prompt fission neutron spectra were calculated for incident neutron energies from 0.0253 eV to 6 MeV.
- For thermal neutrons (0.0253 eV) and 0.55 MeV incident energy, calculated spectra were compared with experimental data from the EXFOR database (entry numbers: 22688, 30704, 40872, 40873, 40930).
- The proportion of high-energy outgoing neutrons (7 MeV to 20 MeV) increases from 1.9% to 2.7% as incident neutron energy increases from 0.0253 eV to 6 MeV.
- The calculated total average prompt fission neutron multiplicity agrees with evaluated data for incident neutron energies below 6 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the semi-empirical method previously applied by the same group to 235U(n,f) (Chen et al., 2012) to the 233U(n,f) reaction, which is important for fusion-fission hybrid reactor design. It differs from the Los Alamos model (Madland and Nix, 1982) by using experimentally-determined excitation energy partition rather than assuming a triangular nuclear temperature distribution, and from prior calculations for 233U (Zheng et al., 2009) which still assumed a simple triangular temperature shape. The method enables evaluation of prompt fission neutron spectra for other actinides where experimental data are scarce.
