# Van der Waals type PV diagrams of PNJL matter

> Keeping in the mind about Van der Walls gas equation, we have explore PV diagram of quark matter by using PNJL model and we find a qualitative similarity.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the pressure-volume (PV) diagram of quark matter, computed using the Polyakov–Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model, shows a qualitative similarity to the Van der Waals gas PV diagram, but this similarity is conditional: it appears only for isotherms below a reduced temperature of \(T_r = 1\) (corresponding to \(T \approx 160\) MeV), where non-monotonic behavior indicates a phase transition and a critical point.

## Method

The authors used the PNJL model to compute the temperature- and chemical-potential-dependent constituent quark mass \(M(T, \mu)\), then calculated the net quark number density \(n(T, \mu)\) and pressure \(P(T, \mu)\) via standard integrals over momentum space with a degeneracy factor \(g = 18\). For fixed net quark number \(N = 100\), they varied the chemical potential \(\mu\) to obtain the corresponding volume \(V\) and pressure \(P\) at three characteristic temperatures (0.120, 0.200, and 0.400 GeV), generating PV isotherms. The results were also presented in reduced variables \(P_r\) and \(V_r\) to mimic Van der Waals plots.

## Key numbers

*   Net quark number fixed at \(N = 100\) for the PV calculations.
*   Volume range considered: \(V = 1\) fm\(^3\) to \(100\) fm\(^3\), corresponding to number densities \(n = 100\) to \(1\) fm\(^{-3}\).
*   Three temperatures used for isotherms: \(T = 0.120\) GeV, \(0.200\) GeV, and \(0.400\) GeV.
*   The cross-over transition temperature at \(\mu = 0\) is identified as \(T \sim 160\) MeV, consistent with lattice QCD results.

## Context

This work extends the study of phase transitions in quark matter using the PNJL model, building on prior studies by the same group (Seth et al., earlier works) and lattice QCD results (LQCD1, LQCD2). The qualitative Van der Waals-like PV diagrams provide a visual analogy for the interactions and phase structure of quark matter, and the identification of \(T \sim 160\) MeV as the cross-over temperature at zero chemical potential aligns with continuum lattice QCD. The approach enables future exploration of metastable and unstable states in the QCD phase diagram, similar to earlier work by NM (no year given) on normal nuclear matter with quantum statistical corrections.
