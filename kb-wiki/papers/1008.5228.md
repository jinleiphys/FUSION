# Electroproduction of Resonances at Large Momentum Transfers

> Measurements of transition form factors for the electro-excitation of nucleon resonances in the Q^2=5-14 GeV^2 region can provide one with the information on quark wave functions at small transverse separations. In particular a comparison of form factors for the states of opposite parity can give insight in the mechanisms of chiral symmetry breaking. # Electroproduction of Resonances at Large Momentum Transfers

## Key claim
Measurements of transition form factors for electroexcitation of nucleon resonances in the Q^2=5-14 GeV^2 region can provide information on quark wave functions at small transverse separations, and comparing form factors for states of opposite parity can give insight into chiral symmetry breaking mechanisms. However, the standard perturbative QCD factorization approach based on hard gluon exchange is not reached at present energies, and the reduction of full light-cone wave functions to simpler distribution amplitudes does not seem to work in the Q^2=5-15 GeV^2 range.

## Method
The author discusses a theoretical approach combining lattice QCD calculations of nucleon and resonance distribution amplitudes (DAs) with light-cone sum rules (LCSR) to compute transition form factors. Lattice calculations use nonperturbatively renormalized three-quark operators with up to two derivatives on gauge configurations with two flavors of clover fermions, identifying baryon states of different parity by their propagation direction in imaginary time. The LCSR method derives form factors from correlation functions using dispersion relations and quark-hadron duality, expressing them as convolutions of coefficient functions with DAs of increasing twist.

## Key numbers
- The proposed Q^2 range for measurements is 5-14 GeV^2.
- Lattice calculations used 600 N_f=2 gauge configurations on a 32^3 x 64 lattice with beta=5.29 (a=0.0753 fm) and pion mass m_pi approx 282 MeV (m_pi L=3.44).
- After nonperturbative renormalization, the sum of quark momentum fractions x1+x2+x3 is approx 0.94 instead of unity, indicating O(a) discretization errors.
- The duality parameter s_0 is approx (1.5 GeV)^2, which sets the scale for higher-twist contributions rather than Lambda_QCD^2/Q^2.
- The left-most lattice points for wave functions at the origin are at m_pi^2 approx 0.08 GeV^2, closer to the physical point than previous calculations.

## Context
This work extends the approach of combining lattice QCD with LCSR first demonstrated in Braun (2009) for the electroproduction of N*(1535), the parity partner of the nucleon. It builds on earlier QCD sum rule calculations by Chernyak (1983) and lattice calculations of nucleon DAs by Braun (2008). The method has previously been applied to nucleon electromagnetic and axial form factors (Braun 2001, 2006), N-gamma-Delta transitions (Braun 2005), and various baryon decay processes. The planned measurements at Jefferson Lab using the CLAS12 detector will provide experimental data in the Q^2=5-14 GeV^2 region. Future work requires extending LCSR to next-to-leading order accuracy, reducing O(a) discretization errors for second moments, and addressing the separation of N*(1535) from N*(1650) in lattice calculations with three flavors of dynamic fermions.
