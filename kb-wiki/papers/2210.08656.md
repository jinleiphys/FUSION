# Multi-Neutrino Entanglement and Correlations in Dense Neutrino Systems

> The time-evolution of multi-neutrino entanglement and correlations are studied in two-flavor collective neutrino oscillations, relevant for dense neutrino environments, building upon previous works. Specifically, simulations performed of systems with up to 12 neutrinos using Quantinuum's H1-1 20 qubit trapped-ion quantum computer are used to compute n-tangles, and two- and three-body correlations, probing beyond mean-field descriptions. n-tangle re-scalings are found to converge for large system sizes, signaling the presence of genuine multi-neutrino entanglement.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that genuine multi-neutrino entanglement, quantified by n-tangles, is present in dense neutrino systems and that the n-tangle re-scalings converge for large system sizes, signaling that beyond-mean-field effects are essential for describing collective neutrino oscillations.

## Method
The authors performed quantum simulations of two-flavor collective neutrino oscillations for systems of up to 12 neutrinos using Quantinuum's H1-1 20-qubit trapped-ion quantum computer, along with its noisy emulator H1-1E and classical simulations. They computed flavor inversion probabilities, two-body correlations, three-body correlations, and n-tangles by implementing Trotterized time evolution of the neutrino Hamiltonian, comparing results from the quantum hardware (240 shots) and emulator (1200 shots) against exact classical calculations using Mathematica and Julia.

## Key numbers
- Flavor inversion probability for N=8 system at t=40: P1(t)=0.683(30) from H1-1, compared to single-Trotter exact value of 0.694
- Flavor inversion probability for N=12 system at t=40: P1(t)=0.663(30) from H1-1, compared to single-Trotter exact value of 0.668
- Two-body correlation C^(2nu)_1,8(t) for N=8 at t=20: -0.504(56) from H1-1, compared to single-Trotter exact value of -0.454
- Two-body correlation C^(2nu)_1,12(t) for N=12 at t=20: -0.300(58) from H1-1, compared to single-Trotter exact value of -0.360
- Three-body correlation C^(3nu)_1,7,8(t) for N=8 at t=20: 0.105(52) from H1-1, compared to single-Trotter exact value of 0.068
- Three-body correlation C^(3nu)_1,2,12(t) for N=12 at t=20: 0.061(58) from H1-1, compared to single-Trotter exact value of 0.020

## Context
This work extends previous studies of collective neutrino oscillations in dense environments, such as supernovae and neutron star mergers, by moving beyond mean-field descriptions to explicitly compute quantum entanglement measures. The use of a trapped-ion quantum computer to simulate up to 12 neutrinos enables direct probing of genuine multi-neutrino entanglement (n-tangles) that cannot be captured by mean-field approaches. The results enable future studies of larger neutrino systems and provide benchmarks for validating approximate methods against exact quantum dynamics, opening the door to understanding how entanglement scales in astrophysical neutrino environments.
