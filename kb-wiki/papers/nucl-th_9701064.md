# Low-Mass $e^+e^-$ Pairs from in-Medium $ρ$ Meson Propagation

> Based on a realistic model for the rho meson in free space we investigate it's medium modifications in a hot hadron gas generated by hadronic rescattering processes, i.e. renormalization of intermediate two-pion states as well as direct rho meson scattering off hadrons. Within the vector dominance model the resulting in-medium rho spectral function is applied to calculate $e^+e^-$ spectra as recently measured in heavy-ion collisions at CERN-SpS energies in the CERES experiment.

# Low-Mass $e^+e^-$ Pairs from in-Medium $\rho$ Meson Propagation

## Key claim
Conventional hadronic rescattering mechanisms of the $\rho$ meson in a hot and dense hadronic environment appear sufficient to account for the experimentally observed $e^+e^-$ excess in central S-Au and Pb-Au collisions at CERN-SpS energies, without requiring a dropping $\rho$ mass from the Brown-Rho scaling conjecture.

## Method
The authors construct a free $\rho$ meson propagator by renormalizing a bare $\rho$ through coupling to intermediate two-pion states, then introduce medium modifications from three sources: (1) in-medium pion propagation via p-wave nucleon-nucleonhole and delta-nucleonhole excitations at finite temperature, (2) direct $\rho$ scattering off nucleons and deltas including $\rho N(1720)N^{-1}$ and $\rho\Delta(1905)N^{-1}$ excitations, and (3) $\rho$ scattering off pions and (anti)kaons via $a_1$(1260) and $K_1$/$\bar{K_1}$(1270) formation. The in-medium $\rho$ spectral function is then used within the vector dominance model to compute $e^+e^-$ production rates from $\pi^+\pi^-$ annihilation, integrated over a temperature/density evolution from transport calculations for central S-Au (200 GeV/u) and Pb-Au (158 GeV/u) collisions.

## Key numbers
- Free $\rho$ meson lifetime: $\tau_\rho^{free}=1.3$ fm/c, compared to fireball lifetime $\tau_{fireball}\approx10$ fm/c, $\tau_\omega^{free}=23$ fm/c, and $\tau_\phi^{free}=44$ fm/c
- In p-Be and p-Au collisions, the event generator successfully describes data, but in S-Au collisions an overall factor of about 5 enhancement is observed, reaching a maximum factor of $\sim10$ around invariant masses $M_{e^+e^-}\simeq0.4$ GeV
- At highest temperature considered ($T=170$ MeV), $\rho$-meson scattering produces a $\sim60$ MeV broadening of the $\rho$ spectral function
- The in-medium $\rho$ propagator calculation at normal nuclear matter density uses $\rho=\rho_0$ and small temperature $T=5$ MeV for the cold nuclear matter case
- The $\rho$ spectral function is shown at temperatures $T=127$ MeV, $T=149$ MeV, and $T=170$ MeV, with a pronounced enhancement over the free curve for invariant masses below $M\simeq0.6$ GeV

## Context
This work extends prior studies of $\rho$ meson medium modifications in cold nuclear matter by He et al., Asakawa et al., and Chanfray and Schuck, and addresses the discrepancy between experimental $e^+e^-$ spectra from the CERES/NA45 collaboration and theoretical predictions based on free $\pi^+\pi^-$ annihilation. Previous transport simulations by Cassing et al. and hydrodynamical simulations by Li and Ko and Prakash et al. reduced but did not eliminate the discrepancy. The authors argue against the Brown-Rho scaling conjecture of a dropping $\rho$ mass as the only explanation, and suggest that measuring invariant mass spectra in various $p_T$ bins could distinguish between a uniform mass shift and the dynamic broadening mechanisms presented here.
