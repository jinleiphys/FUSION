# Neutron star properties from optimized chiral nuclear interactions

> We adopt two- and three-body nuclear forces derived at the next-to-next-to-leading-order (N2LO) in the framework of effective chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) to calculate the equation of state (EOS) of $β$-stable neutron star matter using the Brueckner--Hartree--Fock many-body approach. We use the recent optimized chiral two-body nuclear interaction at N2LO derived by \cite{ekstrom1} and two different parametrizations of the three-body N2LO interaction: the first one is fixed to reproduce the saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter while the second one is fixed to reproduce the binding energies of light atomic nuclei. We show that in the second case the properties of nuclear matter are not well determined whereas in the first case various empirical nuclear matter properties around the saturation density are well reproduced. We also calculate the nuclear symmetry energy $E_{sym}$ as a function of the nucleonic density and compare our results with the empirical constraints obtained using the excitation energies of isobaric analog states in nuclei and the experimental data on the neutron skin thickness of heavy nuclei. We next calculate various neutron star properties and in particular the mass-radius and mass-central density relations. We find that the adopted interactions based on a fully microscopic framework, are able to provide an EOS which is consistent with the present data of measured neutron star masses and in particular with the mass $M=2.01\pm0.04 M_\odot$ of the neutron star in PSR J0348+0432. We finally consider the possible presence of hyperons in the stellar core and we find a softening of the EOS and a substantial reduction of the stellar maximum mass in agreement with similar calculations present in the literature.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that chiral nuclear interactions at N2LO, when the three-body force is fitted to the saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter (N2LO1 parametrization), can reproduce empirical nuclear matter properties and produce neutron star masses consistent with the 2.01 solar mass measurement of PSR J0348+0432; however, when the three-body force is fitted to light-nuclei binding energies (N2LO parametrization), the nuclear matter properties are not well determined and the saturation density becomes too low.

## Method

The authors used the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) many-body approach to calculate the equation of state of beta-stable neutron star matter, employing the optimized chiral two-body N2LO potential from Ekstrom et al. (2015) with a 550 MeV cutoff, combined with two different parametrizations of the N2LO three-nucleon force. The three-body force was converted into an effective density-dependent two-body force by averaging over the coordinates of one nucleon, and the Bethe-Goldstone equation was solved self-consistently including partial waves up to total angular momentum J_max = 8.

- Saturation density for N2LO_opt+N2LO1 model: rho_0 = 0.163 fm^-3, with energy per particle E/A = -15.20 MeV
- Saturation density for N2LO_opt+N2LO model: rho_0 = 0.110 fm^-3, with energy per particle E/A = -10.72 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation for N2LO_opt+N2LO1: E_sym = 34.38 MeV, with slope L = 79.01 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation for N2LO_opt+N2LO: E_sym = 24.03 MeV, with slope L = 35.70 MeV
- Incompressibility for N2LO_opt+N2LO1: K_infinity = 222 MeV
- Incompressibility for N2LO_opt+N2LO: K_infinity = 134 MeV
- The neutron star mass M = 2.01 +/- 0.04 M_sun of PSR J0348+0432 is used as a constraint
- The NN potential was optimized to scattering data below 125 MeV laboratory energy

## Context

This work extends the application of chiral effective field theory interactions to neutron star structure, using the optimized N2LO potential of Ekstrom et al. (2015) which achieved chi^2/datum near 1 for NN scattering, unlike previous N2LO potentials with chi^2/datum around 10. The results are compared to empirical constraints on the symmetry energy from isobaric analog states (Danielewicz et al., 2014) and neutron skin thickness (Roca-Maza et al., 2013), and to heavy-ion collision flow data (Danielewicz et al., 2002). The inclusion of hyperons is noted to soften the equation of state and reduce the maximum mass, consistent with similar calculations in the literature.
