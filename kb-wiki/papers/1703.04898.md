# Coulomb effects in high-energy $e^+e^-$ electroproduction by a heavy charged particles in an atomic field

> The cross section of high-energy $e^+e^-$ pair production by a heavy charged particle in the atomic field is investigated in detail. We take into account the interaction with the atomic field of $e^+e^-$ pair and a heavy particle as well. The calculation is performed exactly in the parameters of the atomic field. It is shown that, in contrast to the commonly accepted point of view, the cross section differential with respect to the final momentum of a heavy particle is strongly affected by the interaction of a heavy particle with the atomic field. However, the cross section integrated over the final momentum of a heavy particle is independent of this interaction.

## Key claim

The cross section of high-energy $e^+e^-$ pair production by a heavy charged particle in an atomic field, when differential with respect to the final momentum of the heavy particle, is strongly affected by the interaction of that heavy particle with the atomic field (parameter $\eta_p = Z Z_p \alpha$), contrary to the commonly accepted view; however, the cross section integrated over the final momentum of the heavy particle remains independent of this interaction.

## Method

The authors used a quasiclassical approximation, exact in the parameters $\eta = Z\alpha$ and $\eta_p = Z Z_p \alpha$, to compute the matrix element for electroproduction in a pure Coulomb field and then extended the result to a screened atomic potential. The calculation was performed for definite helicities of the electron and positron, and the amplitude was separated into contributions from transverse and longitudinal components relative to the initial heavy-particle momentum.

## Key numbers

- For a gold target ($Z = 79$) with $\gamma = 100$, $\omega = m_e \gamma / 4$, and $\varepsilon_3 = \varepsilon_4 = \omega/2$, the differential cross section $\Sigma$ (in units $S = (Z_p \alpha)^2 / (\omega^2 m_e^3)$) shows strong dependence on $Z_p$: at small $p_{2\perp}/m_e$, the exact result is essentially smaller than the $\eta_p \rightarrow 0$ limit, while at large $p_{2\perp}/m_e$ the relation is opposite.
- The integral $\int_0^\infty \Sigma \, dp_{2\perp}$ is independent of $\eta_p$ for the same parameters ($Z=79$, $\gamma=100$, $\omega = m_e \gamma/4$, $\varepsilon_3 = \varepsilon_4 = \omega/2$).
- Screening becomes important for $\gamma \gg m_e r_{scr} \sim Z^{-1/3}/\alpha$, but for the heavy-particle interaction with the atomic field, the unscreened Coulomb form $A_{as}(\bm\Delta_\perp)$ can be used up to very large $\gamma$.

## Context

This work extends the earlier Born-approximation results of Bhabha (1935) and Racah (1937) and the Coulomb-correction calculations of Nikishov (1982) and Ivanov et al. (1998), which treated the heavy particle's interaction with the atomic field only in the plane-wave limit. The present paper provides the first exact treatment of that interaction for the differential cross section, using the quasiclassical method developed by Krachkov and Milstein (2016) for electroproduction by electrons. The results enable experimental tests of the strong $\eta_p$ dependence in the differential cross section, which the authors suggest is feasible for moderate $\gamma$ values.
