# Structure of virtual photon polarization in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Anisotropy in heavy-ion collisions leads to polarization of both direct and virtual photons, the latter detected via internal conversion to dileptons pairs. Thus measurement of photon polarization probes the anisotropy at all stages of collisions. In order to characterize the polarization of virtual photons, we derive here the general structure of the photon polarization operator, rho^(mu nu), in an anisotropic medium, in terms of the four spectral functions, two transverse and one longitudinal, as usual, plus a new spectral function that reflects anisotropy in asymmetric collisions; and derive the local production rate of dilepton pairs in terms of these four functions.

## Key claim

The paper derives the general structure of the virtual photon polarization operator in an anisotropic medium, showing that it requires four spectral functions (two transverse, one longitudinal, and a new one reflecting anisotropy) rather than the three needed in isotropic media; the new spectral function \(\rho_n\) is expected to be non-zero only in asymmetric collisions (e.g., \(A\) on \(A'\)), not in symmetric collisions of identical nuclei.

## Method

The authors construct an orthonormal basis of polarization vectors in the local rest frame of the matter, assuming anisotropy along a single axis \(\hat n\) (typically the beam axis), and decompose the photon polarization operator \(\rho^{\mu\nu}\) into components along these vectors. They then derive the local production rate of dilepton pairs from virtual photons by contracting \(\rho^{\mu\nu}\) with the lepton-pair matrix element \(L^{\mu\nu}\), expressing the result in terms of the four spectral functions and the angular variables of the dilepton system.

## Key numbers

- The estimated net polarization of direct photons from a schematic model with heavy scattering centers and anisotropic temperature was "of order 10% or larger" (no precise number given), but more realistic calculations showed the final polarization to be "only on the order of 3-5%".
- The dilepton production rate formula (Eq. saniso2) contains a \(\cos 2\phi_s\) term with coefficient \(\delta\rho^{\rm T} \equiv (\rho_1^{\rm T} - \rho_2^{\rm T})/2\), which quantifies the anisotropy from the difference of the two transverse spectral functions.
- For massless leptons (\(m=0\)) with \(\rho_n\) absent, the angular distribution is parameterized by coefficients \(\lambda_s = (\bar{\rho}^{\rm T} - \rho^{\rm L})/\zeta\), \(\nu_s = -2\delta\rho^{\rm T}/\zeta\), and \(\mu_s = 0\), where \(\zeta = \bar{\rho}^{\rm T}(1 - 2s_0^2/\vec s\,^2) + \rho^{\rm L}\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of photon polarization in heavy-ion collisions from real photons (Baym and Hatsuda, 2015) to virtual photons detected via dilepton pairs, providing the general framework for interpreting angular distributions of dileptons as probes of plasma anisotropy. The formalism generalizes prior calculations of real photon production in anisotropic quark-gluon plasma (Schenke et al., 2006; Bhattacharya et al., 2015) and is intended to enable full space-time integrated calculations of dilepton rates using three-dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamics, with work in progress (future publication).
