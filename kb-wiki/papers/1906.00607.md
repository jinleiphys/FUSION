# An update on fine-tunings in the triple-alpha process

> The triple-alpha process, whereby evolved stars create carbon and oxygen, is believed to be fine-tuned to a high degree. Such fine-tuning is suggested by the unusually strong temperature dependence of the triple-alpha reaction rate at stellar temperatures. This sensitivity is due to the resonant character of the triple-alpha process, which proceeds through the so-called "Hoyle state" of $^{12}$C with spin-parity $0^+$. The question of fine-tuning can be studied within the {\it ab initio} framework of nuclear lattice effective field theory, which makes it possible to relate {\it ad hoc} changes in the energy of the Hoyle state to changes in the fundamental parameters of the nuclear Hamiltonian, which are the light quark mass $m_q$ and the electromagnetic fine-structure constant. Here, we update the effective field theory calculation of the sensitivity of the triple-alpha process to small changes in the fundamental parameters. In particular, we consider recent high-precision lattice QCD calculations of the nucleon axial coupling $g_A$, as well as new and more comprehensive results from stellar simulations of the production of carbon and oxygen. While the updated stellar simulations allow for much larger {\it ad hoc} shifts in the Hoyle state energy than previously thought, recent lattice QCD results for the nucleon S-wave singlet and triplet scattering lengths now disfavor the scenario of no fine-tuning in the light quark mass $m_q$.

## Key claim

The paper updates the effective field theory calculation of the triple-alpha process sensitivity to fundamental parameters, finding that while recent stellar simulations allow for much larger ad hoc shifts in the Hoyle state energy than previously thought (up to approximately 500 keV for carbon production), new lattice QCD results for nucleon S-wave scattering lengths now disfavor the scenario of no fine-tuning in the light quark mass \(m_q\).

## Method

The authors use nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) to relate ad hoc changes in the Hoyle state energy of \(^{12}\)C to changes in the light quark mass \(m_q\) and the electromagnetic fine-structure constant \(\alpha_\mathrm{em}\). They update the hadronic input parameters \(x_1\) and \(x_2\) (describing pion mass dependence of nucleon mass and one-pion exchange strength) using recent lattice QCD calculations of the nucleon axial coupling \(g_A\) and Roy-Steiner-equation analyses of the pion-nucleon \(\sigma\)-term. The inverse singlet and triplet neutron-proton scattering lengths \(\bar A_s\) and \(\bar A_t\) are extracted from lattice QCD binding energy data for deuteron and dineutron at unphysical pion masses via quadratic and cubic interpolations, then combined with updated stellar nucleosynthesis simulations from Huang et al. (2018) to determine allowed ranges of \(\delta m_q/m_q\) and \(\delta \alpha_\mathrm{em}/\alpha_\mathrm{em}\).

## Key numbers

- Physical Hoyle state resonance energy: \(E_R^\mathrm{ph} = 379.47(18)\) keV
- Radiative width of Hoyle state: \(\Gamma_\gamma \simeq 0.0037\) eV
- For low metallicity (\(\mathcal{Z} = 10^{-4}\)), carbon production allowed for \(-300~\mathrm{keV} \leq \delta E_R \leq 500~\mathrm{keV}\); oxygen production allowed for \(-300~\mathrm{keV} \leq \delta E_R \leq 300~\mathrm{keV}\)
- For solar metallicity (\(\mathcal{Z} = 0.02\)), carbon production allowed for \(-300~\mathrm{keV} \leq \delta E_R \leq 160~\mathrm{keV}\); oxygen production allowed for \(-150~\mathrm{keV} \leq \delta E_R \leq 200~\mathrm{keV}\)
- Updated hadronic parameter: \(x_1 = 0.84(7)\) from \(\sigma_{\pi N} = (59.1 \pm 3.5)\) MeV
- Updated hadronic parameter: \(x_2 = -0.053(16)\) lattice units (l.u.)
- Interpolated lattice QCD values: \(\bar A_s = 0.54(24)\), \(\bar A_t = 0.33(16)\)
- Derivative of Hoyle state energy with respect to pion mass: \(\partial E_R/\partial M_\pi = -0.572(19) \bar A_s - 0.933(15) \bar A_t + 0.068(7)\)
- \(K_{M_\pi}^q = 0.494^{+0.009}_{-0.013}\)

## Context

This work extends the earlier NLEFT analysis of Epelbaum et al. (2013) and Berengut et al. (2013) by incorporating high-precision lattice QCD results for the nucleon axial coupling \(g_A\) from Chang et al. (2018) and new comprehensive stellar nucleosynthesis simulations from Huang et al. (2018). The updated stellar simulations allow for much larger Hoyle state energy shifts (up to approximately 500 keV) compared to the earlier bound of approximately \(\pm 100\) keV from Oberhummer et al. (2000, 1999), suggesting less fine-tuning in the Hoyle state energy itself. However, the new lattice QCD data for \(\bar A_s\) and \(\bar A_t\) now disfavor the scenario of no fine-tuning in the light quark mass, enabling more precise constraints on the allowed variation of fundamental constants compatible with carbon-oxygen based life.
