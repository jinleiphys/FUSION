# Visualisation of an entangled channel spin-1 system

> Co-variance matrix formalism gives powerful entanglement criteria for continuous as well as finite dimensional systems. We use this formalism to study a mixed channel spin-1 system which is well known in nuclear reactions. A spin-j state can be visualized as being made up of 2j spinors which are represented by a constellation of 2j points on a Bloch sphere using Majorana construction. We extend this formalism to visualize an entangled mixed spin-1 system.

# Visualisation of an entangled channel spin-1 system

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a mixed channel spin-1 system, formed from the direct product of two spin-1/2 subsystem density matrices (a beam and a target), can exhibit entanglement arising solely from the projection of the combined density matrix onto the spin-1 space, even though the two subsystems themselves are not entangled. The entanglement is diagnosed via negativity of the covariance matrix, which is a necessary and sufficient condition for symmetric two-qubit states.

## Method
The authors employ the covariance matrix formalism for a symmetric two-qubit state, using spherical tensor (statistical tensor) representation of the density matrix in the Madison convention. They consider a channel spin-1 system formed by the direct product of two spin-1/2 density matrices representing a polarized beam and a polarized target, then project onto the spin-1 subspace. Working in the special Lakin frame (SLF), they derive analytic expressions for the diagonal covariance matrix elements as functions of the beam/target polarization magnitude p and the inclusive angle 2θ between the polarization vectors. They also apply the Majorana construction to visualize the mixed spin-1 density matrix as constellations of spinors on the Bloch sphere.

## Key numbers
- Covariance matrix diagonal element \(C_{x_0x_0} = \frac{1-p^2(1+2\sin^2\theta)}{3+p^2\cos 2\theta}\); negativity of this element signals entanglement.
- Covariance matrix diagonal element \(C_{y_0y_0} = \frac{1-p^2\cos 2\theta}{3+p^2\cos 2\theta}\).
- Covariance matrix diagonal element \(C_{z_0z_0} = \frac{1+p^2(1+2\cos^2\theta)}{3+p^2\cos 2\theta} - \left(\frac{4p\cos\theta}{3+p^2\cos 2\theta}\right)^2\).
- Eigenvalues of the spin-1 density matrix: \(\lambda_{1,2} = \frac{1}{N}[1+p^2\cos^2\theta \pm \sqrt{p^4\sin^4\theta + 4p^2\cos^2\theta}]\) and \(\lambda_3 = \frac{1-p^2}{N}\), where \(N = 3+p^2\cos 2\theta\).
- For pure states (p=1), the system is entangled except when \(\theta = 0\) and \(\theta = \pi\) (polarization vectors parallel or antiparallel).

## Context
This work extends the covariance matrix entanglement criterion developed by Gittsovich et al. (2008) and the spin squeezing inequalities for spin-1 systems (Vitagliano et al., 2010) to the specific case of channel spin-1 systems arising in nuclear reactions. It builds on the statistical tensor formalism standard in nuclear reaction theory (Madison convention, Satchler 1983) and the special Lakin frame used in analyzing polarized scattering experiments (Lakin 1955, Ohlsen 1972). The paper enables visualization of mixed-state entanglement in terms of Majorana constellations on the Bloch sphere, which may lead to a geometric understanding of entanglement for higher-spin systems. The authors note that no single entanglement measure fully captures mixed-state entanglement, and their geometric characterization could provide intuitive insight.
