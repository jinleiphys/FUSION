# Medium-induced emissions of hard gluons

> We present a derivation of the medium-induced gluon radiation spectrum beyond the current limitation of soft gluon emission. Making use of the path integral approach to describe the propagation of high-energy particles inside a medium, we study the limiting case of a hard gluon emission. Analytical and numerical results are presented and discussed within the multiple soft scattering approximation. An ansatz interpolating between soft and hard gluon emissions is provided. The Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect is observed in the expected kinematic region.

## Key claim

The paper extends the derivation of medium-induced gluon radiation beyond the usual soft-gluon limit (x → 0) to the hard-gluon limit (x → 1), providing an interpolating ansatz between both regimes; the authors note that the extension is heuristic and that the hard limit calculation assumes the final quark is much softer than the initial quark and the gluon.

## Method

Using the path-integral formalism with Wilson lines to describe high-energy parton propagation through a medium, the authors compute the medium-induced gluon radiation spectrum in the multiple soft scattering approximation. They first derive the spectrum in the soft limit (x → 0) and then perform a parallel derivation for the hard limit (x → 1), where the final quark's transverse momentum is kept in the propagator denominator to allow transverse motion. An ansatz interpolating between the two limits is constructed and evaluated both analytically and numerically.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum splitting function in the hard limit is given by \(P^{vac}_{g \leftarrow q}(x) = C_F \left[ \frac{1 + (1-x)^2}{x} \right] \xrightarrow{x \rightarrow 1} C_F\)
- The vacuum contribution in the hard limit yields \(\left. x \frac{dI}{dx d^2 \mathbf{k_\perp}} \right|_{x \rightarrow 1} \simeq \frac{C_F \alpha_s}{2 \pi^2} \frac{1}{\mathbf{k_\perp}^2}\)
- The soft-limit vacuum contribution is \(\left. k_+ \frac{dI^{vac}}{d k_+ d^2 \mathbf{k_\perp}} \right|_{x \rightarrow 0} = \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{\pi^2} \frac{1}{\mathbf{k_\perp^2}}\)
- The characteristic gluon energy scale is \(\omega_c = \frac{1}{2} \hat{q} L^2\), where \(\hat{q}\) is the transport coefficient and \(L\) the medium length

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS-Z formalism (Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, Schiff, 1997; Zakharov, 1997; Wiedemann, 2000) beyond the soft-gluon emission limit, addressing a limitation noted in reviews of jet quenching models (Brick, 2011). The finite-energy corrections are needed to put Monte Carlo jet quenching models (such as Q-PYTHIA and JEWEL) on more solid theoretical grounds, and complement ongoing work on interferences from multiple emitters (Mehtar-Tani, 2010; Casalderrey-Solana, 2011) and color-exchange effects with the medium (Beraudo, 2011; Aurenche, 2011).
