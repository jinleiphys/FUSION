# Ab initio calculation of the $^3$He$(α,γ)^7$Be astrophysical S factor with chiral two- and three-nucleon forces

> The $^3$He$(α,γ)^7$Be radiative capture reaction plays a key role in the creation of elements in stars as well as in the production of solar neutrinos, the observation of which is one of the main tools to study the properties of our sun. Since accurate experimental measurements of this fusion cross section at solar energies are difficult due to the strong Coulomb repulsion between the reactants, the onus falls on theory to provide a robust means for extrapolating from the region where experimental data is available down to the desired astrophysical regime. We present the first microscopic calculations of $^3$He$(α,γ)^7$Be with explicit inclusion of three-nucleon forces. Our prediction of the astrophysical $S$ factor qualitatively agrees with experimental data. We further incorporate experimental bound-state and scattering information in our calculation to arrive at a more quantitative description. This process reveals that our current model lacks sufficient repulsion in the $1/2^+$ channel of our model space to simultaneously reproduce elastic-scattering data. This deficit suggests that $^3$He$(α,γ)^7$Be probes aspects of the nuclear force that are not currently well-constrained.

# Ab initio calculation of the $^3$He$(α,γ)^7$Be astrophysical S factor with chiral two- and three-nucleon forces

## Key claim
The first microscopic calculation of $^3$He$(α,γ)^7$Be that explicitly includes three-nucleon forces yields qualitative agreement with experimental data, but the model lacks sufficient repulsion in the $1/2^+$ channel to simultaneously reproduce elastic-scattering data, suggesting this reaction probes aspects of the nuclear force that are not currently well-constrained.

## Method
The authors applied the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) framework to describe the $^3$He+$α$ system and calculate the radiative capture reaction, using a chiral Hamiltonian with nucleon-nucleon interactions at next-to-next-to-next-to leading order (NN-N$^3$LO) and three-nucleon forces at next-to-next-to leading order (N$^2$LO) with simultaneous local and nonlocal regularization. The interaction was softened via similarity renormalization group (SRG) technique at momentum scale $λ_{SRG}=2.0$ fm$^{-1}$, and the NCSMC model space included microscopic seven-body cluster states built from ground states of $^4$He and $^3$He plus the ten lowest positive and negative parity eigenstates of $^7$Be. Phenomenological shifts were applied to NCSM energy eigenvalues to reproduce experimental bound-state energies and elastic-scattering data.

## Context
This work extends previous NCSMC calculations of $^3$He$(α,γ)^7$Be by Dohet-Eraly (2016) by including three-nucleon forces for the first time in such calculations. It builds on prior theoretical approaches including external-capture models by Tombrello (1963), halo-EFT approaches by Zhang (2020) and Higa (2018), microscopic approaches by Nollett (2001), Neff (2011), Kajino (1986), and Csoto (2000), and ab initio approaches by Dohet-Eraly (2016). The results are compared with the Solar Fusion III evaluation by Acharya (2024) and experimental data from the SONIK collaboration (2022) and earlier measurements. The finding that the $1/2^+$ channel lacks sufficient repulsion indicates that this reaction provides a new probe of nuclear forces not constrained by typical nucleon-nucleon scattering data, motivating further theoretical work to reduce the theoretical uncertainty in standard solar model predictions.
