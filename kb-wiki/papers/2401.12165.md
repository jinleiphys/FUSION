# Bayesian analysis of nontrivial features in the speed of sound inside neutron stars in light of astrophysical and pQCD constraints

> Functional forms of the neutron star Equation of State (EoS) are required to extract the viable EoS band from neutron star observations. Realistic nuclear EoS, containing deconfined quarks or hyperons, present nontrivial features in the speed of sound such as bumps, kinks, and plateaus. Using modified Gaussian processes to model EoS with nontrivial features, we show in a fully Bayesian analysis incorporating measurements from X-ray sources, gravitational wave observations, and perturbative QCD results that these features are compatible with current constraints. We find nontrivial behavior in the EoS plays a role in understanding the possible phase structure of neutron stars at densities around 2 $n_{\rm sat}$.

## Key claim

Using modified Gaussian processes (mGPs) to model neutron star equations of state with nontrivial speed-of-sound features, a fully Bayesian analysis incorporating X-ray, gravitational wave, and perturbative QCD constraints finds that such features are compatible with current data but are neither favored nor disfavored over a benchmark model with only long-range correlations.

## Method

The authors constructed two equation-of-state models: a benchmark model using standard Gaussian processes with long-range correlations across density, and an mGP model where piecewise modifications (spikes and plateaus) were introduced to the speed-of-sound functional to produce multi-scale correlations. They performed Bayesian model comparison by computing the Bayes factor between the two models, incorporating likelihood factors from symmetry energy estimates, high-mass pulsar measurements, simultaneous mass-radius measurements from NICER, tidal deformability data from GW170817 and GW190425, and perturbative QCD constraints propagated to neutron-star densities via causality and thermodynamic consistency.

## Key numbers

- Bayes factor \( K = \mathcal{E}_{\text{benchmark}}/\mathcal{E}_{\text{mGP}} = 1.5 \), indicating current constraints do not favor either model over the other
- Nuclear saturation density \( n_{\text{sat}} \equiv 0.16 \) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Symmetry energy constraint of \( 32 \pm 2 \) MeV from low-energy nuclear experiments
- The mGP posterior for the location of the global maximum in \( c_s^2 \) is bimodal, with support for a maximum below 3 \( n_{\text{sat}} \) (consistent with a crossover phase transition), while the benchmark model posterior peaks at \( 4-7 \) \( n_{\text{sat}} \)

## Context

This work extends the mGP methodology introduced by Mroczek (2023) for modeling multi-scale correlations in the neutron star equation of state, building on Gaussian process approaches developed by Landry (2018) and Essick (2019). The analysis incorporates perturbative QCD constraints from Gorda (2022) and astrophysical data from NICER and LIGO/Virgo. The results show that current data cannot distinguish between equations of state with and without nontrivial speed-of-sound features, but the mGP framework enables future searches for signatures of crossover phase transitions to quark matter at densities around 2 \( n_{\text{sat}} \).
