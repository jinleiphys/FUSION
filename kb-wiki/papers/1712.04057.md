# World-line formulation of chiral kinetic theory in topological background gauge fields

> In heavy-ion collisions, an interesting question of phenomenological relevance is how the chiral imbalance generated at early times persists through a fluctuating background of sphalerons in addition to other "non-anomalous" interactions with the QGP. To address this question, we construct a relativistic chiral kinetic theory using the world-line formulation of quantum field theory. We outline how Berry's phase arises in this framework, and how its effects can be clearly distinguished from those arising from the chiral anomaly. We further outline how this framework can be matched to classical statistical simulations at early times and to anomalous chiral hydrodynamics at late times.

# World-line formulation of chiral kinetic theory in topological background gauge fields

## Key claim

The authors construct a relativistic chiral kinetic theory using the world-line formulation of quantum field theory, demonstrating that Berry's phase arises from the real part of the fermion effective action while the chiral anomaly arises from the imaginary part, showing these two topological effects are distinct and should not be confused, contrary to prior claims that tied the anomaly to Berry's phase.

## Method

The authors use the world-line representation of the one-loop effective action for massless Dirac fermions in background vector and axial-vector fields, expressing both the real and imaginary parts of the effective action as quantum mechanical path integrals over bosonic coordinates and anticommuting Grassmann variables. They take the nonrelativistic adiabatic limit of the real part to recover Berry's phase, while showing the chiral anomaly emerges from fermionic zero modes in the imaginary part. They then extend the framework to a Schwinger-Keldysh formulation to derive a Liouville equation for a many-body phase space distribution that includes spin via Grassmann variables, splitting the distribution and gauge fields into background and fluctuation components to obtain coupled kinetic equations.

## Key numbers

- The chiral anomaly relation is derived as ∂_μ ⟨j^5_μ(y)⟩ = −(1/16π²) ε^{μνρσ} F_{μν}(y) F_{ρσ}(y), with no numerical coefficient other than the standard 1/(16π²) factor.
- The nonrelativistic Hamiltonian obtained includes terms such as −(B·S)/m and (S·([v/c − A/(mc²)] × E))/(2mc), with explicit factors of c and m.
- The world-line Lagrangian for the real part of the effective action in the nonrelativistic adiabatic limit yields a Berry phase term −ṗ·A(p) in the effective Hamiltonian, where A(p) is the Berry connection.

## Context

This work extends the world-line approach previously applied to spinless colored particles for Bodeker effective kinetic theory (Bodeker, 1999; Litim, 1999) to include spin via Grassmann variables, building on seminal work by Berezin and Marinov (1976) and related formulations by Barducci et al. (1976). It provides a framework that can be matched to classical-statistical simulations at early times (Berges, 2013; Mace, 2016) and to anomalous chiral hydrodynamics at late times (Son, 2009; Nair, 2011), enabling systematic study of the Chiral Magnetic Effect in heavy-ion collisions. The authors explicitly distinguish their result from prior chiral kinetic theories (Son, 2012; Stephanov, 2012; Chen, 2013) that tied the anomaly to Berry's phase, agreeing instead with Fujikawa (2005) that the two topologies are distinct.
