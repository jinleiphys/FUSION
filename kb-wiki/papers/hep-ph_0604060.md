# The pressure of deconfined QCD for all temperatures and quark chemical potentials

> We present a new method for the evaluation of the perturbative expansion of the QCD pressure which is valid at all values of the temperature and quark chemical potentials in the deconfined phase and which we work out up to and including order g^4 accuracy. Our calculation is manifestly four-dimensional and purely diagrammatic -- and thus independent of any effective theory descriptions of high temperature or high density QCD. In various limits, we recover the known results of dimensional reduction and the HDL and HTL resummation schemes, as well as the equation of state of zero-temperature quark matter, thereby verifying their respective validity. To demonstrate the overlap of the various regimes, we furthermore show how the predictions of dimensional reduction and HDL resummed perturbation theory agree in the regime T~\sqrt{g}*mu. At parametrically smaller temperatures T~g*mu, we find that the dimensional reduction result agrees well with those of the nonstatic resummations down to the remarkably low value T~0.2 m_D, where m_D is the Debye mass at T=0. Beyond this, we see that only the latter methods connect smoothly to the T=0 result of Freedman and McLerran, to which the leading small-T corrections are given by the so-called non-Fermi-liquid terms, first obtained through HDL resummations. Finally, we outline the extension of our method to the next order, where it would include terms for the low-temperature entropy and specific heats that are unknown at present.

## Key claim

The authors present a new four-dimensional, purely diagrammatic method for computing the perturbative expansion of the QCD pressure that is valid at all temperatures and quark chemical potentials in the deconfined phase, working to order g^4 accuracy, but the method's validity is conditional on the coupling g being sufficiently small and on working at sufficiently high temperatures and/or densities where the perturbative expansion is applicable.

## Method

The calculation uses a manifestly four-dimensional diagrammatic approach, evaluating all relevant classes of Feynman graphs contributing to the partition function up to and including order g^4, independent of any effective theory descriptions such as dimensional reduction or HTL/HDL resummation. The pressure is expressed as the sum of an analytic power series in g^2 and a logarithmic sum-integral that is treated numerically, with the numerical evaluation of multi-dimensional integrals involving the one-loop gluon polarization tensor. The method is worked out in the Feynman gauge using dimensional regularization at d=4-2ε dimensions in Euclidean metric, with the MSbar renormalization scheme.

## Key numbers

* The Debye mass at zero temperature is given by m_D = sqrt(N_f/2) g μ / π
* The dimensional reduction result agrees with nonstatic resummations down to T ≈ 0.2 m_D, where m_D is the Debye mass at T=0
* At T ~ sqrt(g) μ, the pressure expansion contains half-integer powers of g, with the g^4 term involving 72 T_F^2 ln g
* The zero-temperature pressure result of Freedman and McLerran is given to order g^4, with the g^4 coefficient containing terms such as (17/2) C_F and (C_A/36)(568-264 ln 2)
* The anomalous specific heat contributions to the pressure involve a T ln T term in the entropy, with the scale under the logarithm determined in Ref. Ipp (2003)

## Context

This work connects and verifies several disjoint perturbative calculations of the QCD pressure: dimensional reduction (Kajantie et al., 2003; Vuorinen, 2003) valid at high T, the zero-temperature result of Freedman and McLerran (1977), and HDL resummation results (Ipp et al., 2003; Gerhold et al., 2004) for low temperatures. The method enables a smooth interpolation between all these regimes and provides the first independent verification of the Freedman-McLerran result, while also outlining the extension to next order where it would include currently unknown terms for low-temperature entropy and specific heats.
