# Symmetries and Supersymmetries of the Dirac Hamiltonian with Axially-Deformed Scalar and Vector Potentials

> We consider several classes of symmetries of the Dirac Hamiltonian in 3+1 dimensions, with axially-deformed scalar and vector potentials. The symmetries include the known pseudospin and spin limits and additional symmetries which occur when the potentials depend on different variables. Supersymmetries are observed within each class and the corresponding charges are identified.

## Key claim
The paper identifies and classifies four distinct symmetry classes of the Dirac Hamiltonian with axially-deformed scalar and vector potentials, and demonstrates that supersymmetric structures emerge within each class when specific conditions on the potentials are met. The hedge is that these symmetries and supersymmetries are exact only under idealized conditions (e.g., constant sum or difference of potentials, or potentials depending on different variables), while in realistic physical systems they are only approximately realized.

## Method
The Dirac Hamiltonian is analyzed for axially-symmetric scalar and vector potentials \(V_S(\rho,z)\) and \(V_V(\rho,z)\) in 3+1 dimensions. The paper identifies four symmetry classes: pseudospin symmetry (class I, when \(V_S + V_V = \Delta_0\)), spin symmetry (class II, when \(V_S - V_V = \Xi_0\)), and two additional classes (III and IV) where the potentials depend on different variables (\(V_S(z), V_V(\rho)\) or \(V_S(\rho), V_V(z)\)). For each class, the conserved operators and the form of the Dirac wave functions are derived, and supersymmetric structures are constructed using conserved, anticommuting operators \(\hat{R}\) and \(\hat{B}\) that satisfy \([H,\hat{R}] = [H,\hat{B}] = \{\hat{R},\hat{B}\} = 0\).

## Key numbers
- For class III with \(V_V(\rho) = \alpha_V/\rho\) and arbitrary \(V_S(z)\), the energy eigenvalues are \(E^{(\epsilon)}_{n_\rho,\Omega} = |\epsilon|/\sqrt{1 + \alpha_V^2/(n_\rho+\gamma)^2}\) with \(\gamma = \sqrt{\Omega^2 - \alpha_V^2}\) and \(n_\rho = 0,1,2,\ldots\).
- For class IV with \(V_S(\rho) = \alpha_S/\rho\) (\(\alpha_S < 0\)) and arbitrary \(V_V(z)\), the allowed values are \(\tilde{\epsilon} = \pm M\sqrt{1 - \alpha_S^2/(n_\rho+\tilde{\gamma})^2}\) with \(\tilde{\gamma} = \sqrt{\Omega^2 + \alpha_S^2}\) and \(n_\rho = 0,1,2,\ldots\).
- For class III, the separation constant \(\epsilon\) plays the role of a mass for transverse motion; for class IV, the quantum number \(\tilde{\epsilon}\) plays the role of an energy for transverse motion.
- The pseudospin symmetry limit (class I) produces degenerate doublets with \(\Omega_1 = \tilde{\Lambda} - 1/2\) and \(\Omega_2 = \tilde{\Lambda} + 1/2\), with a non-degenerate nodeless state at the bottom of the \(\Omega_1\) tower for each \(\tilde{\Lambda} \neq 0\).
- The spin symmetry limit (class II) produces degenerate doublets with \(\Omega_1 = \Lambda - 1/2\) and \(\Omega_2 = \Lambda + 1/2\), with no non-degenerate state in either tower.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of pseudospin symmetry (Bell and others, 1975; Ginocchio and Leviatan, 1998) and spin symmetry (Page and others, 2001) from spherical to axially-deformed potentials, and generalizes earlier supersymmetric analyses of spherical Dirac Hamiltonians (Sukumar, 1985; Leviatan, 2004). The results are relevant to axially-deformed nuclei, where pseudospin doublets explain features such as superdeformation and identical bands (Bohr and others, 1982; Ginocchio and others, 2005), and to heavy-light quark mesons where spin symmetry is observed (Page, 2001). The additional symmetries (classes III and IV) connect to electron channeling in crystals, the Schwinger mechanism for particle production in strong fields (Wang and others, 1988; Gato and others, 1992), and quark-antiquark pair creation in flux tubes (Pavel and others, 1991).
