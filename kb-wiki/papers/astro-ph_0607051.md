# Bulk viscosity of Mixed nucleon-hyperon-quark Matter in Neutron stars

> We calculate the coefficient of bulk viscosity by considering the non-leptonic weak interactions in the cores of hybrid stars with both hyperons and quarks. We first determine the dependence of the production rate of neutrons on the reaction rate of quarks in the non-leptonic processes, that is $Γ_{n}=K_{s}Γ_{s}+Γ_Λ+2Γ_{Σ^{-}}$. The conversion rate, $K_{s}$ in our scenario is a complicated function of baryon number density. We also consider medium effect of quark matter on bulk viscosity. Using these results, we estimate the limiting rotation of the hybrid stars, which may suppress the r-mode instability more effectively. Hybrid stars should be the candidates for the extremely rapid rotators .

# Bulk viscosity of Mixed nucleon-hyperon-quark Matter in Neutron stars

## Key claim
The bulk viscosity of mixed nucleon-hyperon-quark matter in hybrid stars is large enough to suppress r-mode instability at low temperatures, creating a low-temperature instability window that allows submillisecond pulsars to exist, but only if the conversion rate K_s between quarks and neutrons is treated as a density-dependent function rather than a constant, and the medium effect of quark matter is included.

## Method
The authors used the Lindblom-Owen relaxation time formalism to compute the bulk viscosity coefficient from non-leptonic weak interactions involving both hyperons (n+n↔p+Σ⁻, n+p↔p+Λ) and strange quarks (u+d↔u+s) in the mixed hadron-quark phase of neutron star cores. They employed the Ghosh-Phatak-Sahu (GPS) equation of state for hadronic matter and the Schertler-Greiner-Thoma (SGT) effective mass bag model with medium effects for quark matter, imposing β-equilibrium, Gibbs conditions, and global charge neutrality to determine compositions. The production rate of neutrons was expressed as Γ_n = K_s Γ_s + Γ_Λ + 2Γ_Σ⁻, where K_s was determined numerically as a function of baryon number density from the constraint equations linking density variations of all particle species.

## Key numbers
- The transition temperature between low and high temperature regimes for bulk viscosity is about 10⁸ to 10⁹ K for the models considered.
- The perturbation frequency used for bulk viscosity calculations was ω̂ = 10⁴ s⁻¹.
- The saturation density of nucleons is ε₀ = 140 MeV/fm³ ≈ 2.5×10¹⁴ g/cm³; the GPS and BPS equations of state are matched at ε ≈ 10¹⁴ g/cm³ ≈ 0.4 ε₀.
- The Hulse-Taylor pulsar has a measured mass of 1.44 M_sun, used as a reference for rejecting equations of state that produce smaller maximum masses.
- The bulk viscosity of the mixed nucleon-hyperon-quark phase is of almost the same order as the bulk viscosity of pure strange quark matter.

## Context
This work extends prior calculations of bulk viscosity in neutron star matter that considered only single reaction channels, either hadron non-leptonic processes (Lindblom and Owen, 2002; Nayyar and Owen, 2005) or quark non-leptonic processes (Drago et al., 2005), by treating the general case where both hyperons and quarks are present simultaneously. The authors use the GPS equation of state (Ghosh, Phatak, and Sahu, 1995) for hadrons and the SGT model (Schertler, Greiner, and Thoma, 1997) for quarks, with a BPS crust (Baym, Pethick, and Sutherland, 1971). The results enable predictions of r-mode instability windows for hybrid stars, suggesting that submillisecond pulsars may exist as hybrid stars with mixed nucleon-hyperon-quark cores.
