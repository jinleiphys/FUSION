# Possibility of DCC formation in pp collisions at LHC energy via reaction-diffusion equation

> There are indications of formation of a thermalized medium in high multiplicity pp collisions at LHC energy. It is possible that such a medium may reach high enough energy density/temperature so that a transient stage of quark-gluon plasma, where chiral symmetry is restored, may be achieved. Due to rapid 3-dimensional expansion, the system will quickly cool undergoing spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking transition. We study the dynamics of chiral field, after the symmetry breaking transition, for such an event using reaction-diffusion equation approach which we have recently applied for studying QCD transitions in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We show that the interior of such a rapidly expanding system is likely to lead to the formation of a single large domain of disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) which has been a subject of intensive search in earlier experiments. We argue that large multiplicity pp collisions naturally give rise to required boundary conditions for the existence of slowly propagating front solutions of reaction-diffusion equation with resulting dynamics of chiral field leading to the formation of a large DCC domain.

# Possibility of DCC formation in pp collisions at LHC energy via reaction-diffusion equation

## Key claim

The authors argue that in high-multiplicity pp collisions at LHC energy, a single large domain of disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) may form, provided that the system reaches a transient stage of chiral symmetry restoration and that the subsequent dynamics of the chiral field is governed by a reaction-diffusion equation with appropriate boundary conditions. The claim is hedged by the assumption that the required dissipation and boundary conditions naturally arise from rapid three-dimensional expansion, and that thermal fluctuations and quantum decay do not destroy the domain before it grows to observable size.

## Method

The authors model the chiral field using the linear sigma model with a finite-temperature effective potential, including explicit symmetry breaking. They evolve the field equations in spherical coordinates with a Hubble-like dissipation term (3/tau) from three-dimensional expansion, plus an additional dissipation parameter eta-prime representing coupling to other field modes. The initial profile at tau = 2 fm interpolates between the true vacuum value and the saddle point opposite to the true vacuum on the vacuum manifold, with the field lying in the sigma-pi3 plane. The temperature is taken to decrease as T(tau) = T0 * tau0 / tau, and the system undergoes scale-invariant three-dimensional expansion.

## Key numbers

* Initial temperature for field evolution: T0 = 150 MeV at proper time tau0 = 2 fm, corresponding to the chiral symmetry broken phase.
* Critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration: Tc approximately 200 MeV.
* True vacuum value of the sigma field: sigma = 75.18 MeV; saddle point opposite to true vacuum: sigma = -49.25 MeV (with pion fields zero at both boundaries).
* Initial radius of the parton system: approximately 2.5 fm at tau0 = 2 fm.
* With Hubble damping only (eta-prime = 0), the DCC domain nearly doubles in size by tau = 4 fm, and decay sets in at tau approximately 7.2 fm.
* With constant eta-prime = 20 fm^{-1}, significantly larger DCC domain stretching is observed; with eta-prime = 40 fm^{-1}, even larger domains result, with the field still not significantly deviating from the disoriented value by tau = 7.2 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous application of reaction-diffusion equations to QCD phase transitions in relativistic heavy-ion collisions (Bagchi et al., 2014), where propagating front solutions were shown to exist for chiral and confinement-deconfinement transitions. It revisits the long-standing search for DCC, which was motivated by Centauro events in cosmic ray experiments (Bjorken et al., 1993) and extensively studied in both hadronic and heavy-ion collisions. The key advance is that high-multiplicity pp collisions at LHC energy may provide a cleaner environment than heavy-ion collisions, where multiple small DCC domains wash out coherent pion signals. The results suggest that LHC pp data should be examined for DCC signatures, though the authors note that thermal fluctuations and quantum decay of the DCC domain into pions will set the ultimate limit on domain growth.
