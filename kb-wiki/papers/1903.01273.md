# Reply to "Comment on "How (not) to renormalize integral equations with singular potentials in effective field theory"

> We offer a brief response to the criticism put forward by Pavon Valderrama about our recent paper on "How (not) to renormalize integral equations with singular potentials in effective field theory".

# Reply to "Comment on 'How (not) to renormalize integral equations with singular potentials in effective field theory'"

## Key claim

The authors defend their original claim that taking the cutoff limit Λ → ∞ in non-perturbative expressions for the scattering amplitude without first subtracting all ultraviolet divergences (step ii) leads to results that are not properly renormalized and are incompatible with effective field theory principles, even if a finite limit exists. They argue that the critic's counterexamples rely on either irrelevant regularization schemes or on fitting parameters without demonstrating convergence of the cutoff limit.

## Method

The authors address two specific criticisms from Pavon Valderrama (2019). First, they clarify that the power divergence subtraction scheme (PDS) used by Kaplan (1998) employs dimensional regularization, which yields finite loop integrals in four dimensions and is therefore not applicable to the cutoff-regularized framework under dispute. Second, they construct a toy-model amplitude expansion to test the convergence of the perturbative approach proposed by the critic, deriving two possible sets of coefficients for the amplitude expansion that reproduce the same full phase shift but show no sign of convergence when truncated at next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers

- Phase shift at k_cm = 0.22 GeV: δ_LO = -63.61° and full δ = -14.46° for the toy model with cutoff R_c = 0.3 fm.
- Two possible amplitude expansions (in units of 4π/m) at order ε^3 that reproduce the full unitary amplitude -(4π/m)(1/T) = -3.90 - i: (24.3 - 49.0 i) ε^{-1} + (28.0 + 36.8 i) ε^1 + (-37.5 + 8.4 i) ε^3, or (24.3 - 49.0 i) ε^{-1} + (-28.0 - 36.8 i) ε^1 + (18.4 + 82.0 i) ε^3.
- At next-to-leading order (ε^1), the unitarity measure -(4π/m)(1/T_NLO) gives -1.11 - 0.26 i for the first solution and 0.03 - 0.71 i for the second, both far from the unitary value.

## Context

This reply responds to a comment by Pavon Valderrama (2019) on the authors' earlier paper Epelbaum et al. (2018) about renormalization of integral equations with singular potentials. The authors maintain that their original criticism applies to non-perturbative calculations in spin-triplet nucleon-nucleon channels based on one-pion exchange, where no complete subtraction of divergences has been demonstrated except in the approach of Epelbaum (2012). The work reinforces the requirement that EFT calculations must demonstrate controlled convergence and reliable error estimates rather than merely fitting data with adjustable parameters.
