# Double giant resonances in deformed nuclei

> We report on the first microscopic study of the properties of two-phonon giant resonances in deformed nuclei. The cross sections of the excitation of the giant dipole and the double giant dipole resonances in relativistic heavy ion collisions are calculated. We predict that the double giant dipole resonance has a one-bump structure with a centroid 0.8 MeV higher than twice energy for the single giant dipole resonance in the reaction under consideration. The width of the double resonance equals to 1.33 of that for the single resonance.

# Double giant resonances in deformed nuclei

## Key claim
The authors predict that the double giant dipole resonance (DGDR) in deformed nuclei such as 238U has a one-bump structure in relativistic heavy ion collisions, with a centroid 0.8 MeV higher than twice the energy of the single giant dipole resonance (GDR) centroid, and a width equal to 1.33 times that of the single GDR. These effects arise from the energy dependence of the virtual photon spectra in the reaction mechanism, not from anharmonicities of the two-phonon states.

## Method
The authors used the Quasiparticle Phonon Model (QPM) with a Woods-Saxon average field and monopole pairing to construct the phonon basis for the GDR in 238U by solving RPA equations, using deformation parameters beta_2 = 0.22 and beta_4 = 0.08. They then constructed DGDR states by folding two 1- phonons, yielding approximately 1.5 x 10^6 non-degenerate two-phonon states, and calculated excitation cross sections in second-order perturbation theory for 238U projectiles at 0.5 GeV per nucleon incident on 120Sn and 208Pb targets.

## Key numbers
- GDR in 238U splits into two components: K=0 with centroid 11.0 MeV and m2 = 2.1 MeV; K=±1 with centroid 12.3 MeV and m2 = 2.6 MeV
- Total GDR centroid in RHIC: 12.0 MeV with m2 = 2.6 MeV; total GDR cross section: 1991.4 mb (120Sn target) and 4614.5 mb (208Pb target)
- Total DGDR centroid in RHIC: 24.8 MeV with m2 = 3.4 MeV; total DGDR cross section: 102.5 mb (120Sn target) and 501.3 mb (208Pb target)
- DGDR centroid is 0.8 MeV higher than twice the GDR centroid energy in the reaction under consideration
- Ratio of DGDR width to GDR width equals 1.33
- The GDR K=±1 component has experimental width Gamma_2 = 5.10 MeV; the K=0 component has Gamma_1 = 2.99 MeV (from Gurevich 1976)
- The phonon basis exhausts 32.6% (K=0) and 76.3% (K=±1) of the energy-weighted sum rule (14.8 * NZ/A e^2 fm^2 MeV)

## Context
This work extends previous microscopic studies of two-phonon giant resonances in spherical nuclei by Ponomarev (1994, 1996) and Bertulani (1996) to the case of deformed nuclei, specifically 238U. The calculation uses the Quasiparticle Phonon Model of Soloviev (1992) and follows the experimental conditions of the LAND collaboration experiment at GSI/SIS by Emling (private communication). The results enable experimental identification of the DGDR in deformed nuclei by predicting a one-bump structure with a measurable energy shift and narrower width than expected from simple harmonic scaling, which should simplify separation of the DGDR from the total cross section in relativistic heavy ion collisions.
