# Angular distribution in two-particle emission induced by neutrinos and electrons

> The angular distribution of the phase space arising in two-particle emission reactions induced by electrons and neutrinos is computed in the laboratory (Lab) system by boosting the isotropic distribution in the center of mass (CM) system used in Monte Carlo generators. The Lab distribution has a singularity for some angular values, coming from the Jacobian of the angular transformation between CM and Lab systems. We recover the formula we obtained in a previous calculation for the Lab angular distribution. This is in accordance with the Monte Carlo method used to generate two-particle events for neutrino scattering\cite{Sob12}. Inversely, by performing the transformation to the CM system, it can be shown that the phase-space function, which is proportional to the two particle-two hole (2p-2h) hadronic tensor for a constant current operator, can be computed analytically in the frozen nucleon approximation, if Pauli blocking is absent. The results in the CM frame confirm our previous work done using an alternative approach in the Lab frame. The possibilities of using this method to compute the hadronic tensor by a boost to the CM system are analyzed.

## Key claim

The isotropic angular distribution of two-nucleon emission in the center-of-mass (CM) frame, as used in Monte Carlo event generators, is shown to be exactly equivalent to the angular distribution obtained in the laboratory (Lab) frame after integration of the energy-conservation Dirac delta function, provided the normalization constant is chosen appropriately; this equivalence holds in the frozen nucleon approximation (initial nucleons at rest) and in the absence of Pauli blocking.

## Method

The authors compute the phase-space angular distribution for two-particle emission in the relativistic Fermi gas by boosting the isotropic CM distribution to the Lab frame using a Lorentz transformation. They compare this boosted distribution to the Lab-frame angular distribution derived from the Jacobian of the energy-conservation delta function, showing they are identical up to a normalization factor. They then reverse the procedure: by boosting the Lab-frame phase-space integral to the CM frame, they perform the angular integration analytically in the frozen nucleon approximation, obtaining a closed-form expression for the phase-space function.

* For q = 3 GeV/c, the analytic result without Pauli blocking agrees with the numerical Lab-frame calculation including Pauli blocking, except for a small region around the quasielastic peak where the Pauli-blocked result is slightly below the analytic value (Fig. 3).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of the Lab-frame angular distribution for two-particle emission in the frozen nucleon approximation (Ruiz Simo et al., 2014), which showed integrable singularities in the angular distribution. It provides the theoretical justification for the isotropic CM angular distribution used in Monte Carlo event generators such as those described by Sobczyk (2012), GENIE, and Katori (2013). The CM integration method offers an alternative approach to computing the 2p-2h hadronic tensor for neutrino and electron scattering, potentially simplifying calculations that currently require 7-dimensional numerical integration.
