# Hot QCD Phase Diagram From Holographic Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton Models

> In this review, we provide an up-to-date account of quantitative holographic descriptions of the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (QGP) produced in heavy-ion collisions, based on the class of gauge-gravity Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton (EMD) models. Holography is employed to tentatively map the QCD phase diagram at finite temperature onto a dual theory of charged, asymptotically AdS black holes in 5D. With a quantitative focus on the hot QCD phase diagram, the EMD models reviewed are adjusted to describe lattice results for the finite-temperature QCD equation of state, with 2+1 flavors and physical quark masses, at zero chemical potential and vanishing electromagnetic fields. The predictive power of EMD models is tested by quantitatively comparing their predictions for the hot QCD equation of state at nonzero baryon density and the corresponding state-of-the-art lattice QCD results. The shear and bulk viscosities predicted by these EMD models are also compared to the corresponding profiles favored by the latest phenomenological multistage models describing different heavy-ion data. We report preliminary results from a Bayesian analysis which provide systematic evidence that lattice results at finite temperature and zero baryon density strongly constrains the free parameters of EMD models. Remarkably, the set of parameters constrained by lattice results at zero chemical potential produces EMD models in quantitative agreement with lattice QCD results also at finite baryon density. We also review results for equilibrium and transport properties from magnetic EMD models, describing the QGP at finite temperatures and magnetic fields. Finally, we provide a critical assessment of the main limitations and drawbacks of the holographic models reviewed in the present work, and point out some perspectives we believe are of fundamental importance for future developments.

## Key claim

This review argues that holographic Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton (EMD) models, whose free parameters are constrained by lattice QCD results at zero baryon density, can quantitatively reproduce lattice QCD results for the equation of state at finite baryon density and also produce shear and bulk viscosity profiles consistent with those favored by phenomenological multistage models of heavy-ion collisions, though the authors provide a critical assessment of the models' limitations and drawbacks.

## Method

The authors review a class of bottom-up holographic EMD models that map the QCD phase diagram at finite temperature onto a dual theory of charged, asymptotically Anti-de Sitter black holes in five dimensions. The models are adjusted to reproduce lattice QCD results for the finite-temperature equation of state with 2+1 flavors and physical quark masses at zero chemical potential and vanishing electromagnetic fields, and their predictions are then compared to lattice results at nonzero baryon density and to phenomenological viscosity profiles from Bayesian analyses of heavy-ion data.

## Key numbers

- The QCD deconfinement crossover transition at zero baryon density occurs at a temperature scale \(T_c\sim 150\) MeV.
- The strongly coupled QGP window at zero baryon density is approximately \(T_c \sim 150\,\text{MeV} < T < 2T_c \sim 300\) MeV.
- State-of-the-art lattice simulations for the continuum-extrapolated QCD equation of state with 2+1 flavors and physical quark masses are available up to \(\mu_B/T\le 3.5\) from a novel expansion scheme and up to \(\mu_B/T\le 3\) from a traditional Taylor expansion.
- The holographic benchmark value for the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\).
- The relativistic string tension is \(\sigma = (2\pi\alpha')^{-1} \approx (440\,\text{MeV})^2\).

## Context

This review extends the program of using holographic gauge-gravity duality, originally motivated by the AdS-CFT correspondence of Maldacena (1997), Gubser, Klebanov, and Polyakov (1998), and Witten (1998), to describe the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions. It builds upon lattice QCD results for the equation of state at zero and finite baryon density from Borsanyi et al. (2021) and Bollweg et al. (2022), and compares its viscosity predictions to phenomenological Bayesian analyses from the JETSCAPE Collaboration (2020). The work enables future quantitative holographic studies of the QCD phase diagram at higher baryon densities and in the presence of magnetic fields, while also identifying key limitations and open questions for further development.
