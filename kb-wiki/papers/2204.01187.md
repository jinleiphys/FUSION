# Ab Initio Nuclear Reaction Theory with Applications to Astrophysics

> We present an introduction to ab initio nuclear theory with a focus on nuclear reactions. After a high-level overview of ab initio approaches in nuclear physics, we give a more detailed description of the no-core shell model technique equivalent to a large extent to configuration-interaction methods applied in quantum chemistry. We then introduce the no-core shell model with continuum approach that provides a quantum many-body description of nuclear reactions. After a brief review of nuclear reactions important for astrophysics, we present examples of results of ab initio calculations of radiative capture and transfer reactions in light nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper presents an introduction to *ab initio* nuclear reaction theory, focusing on the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) approach, which provides a quantum many-body description of nuclear reactions for light nuclei, but the authors note that the NCSM eigenstates used as input lack correct asymptotic behavior for weakly-bound states and always have incorrect asymptotic behavior for resonances.

## Method

The authors describe the no-core shell model (NCSM) technique, which expands the many-body wave function over a complete set of antisymmetric A-nucleon harmonic oscillator (HO) basis states containing up to Nmax HO excitations above the lowest configuration, with the Hamiltonian diagonalized to obtain eigenstates. They then introduce the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) method, which combines the NCSM with the resonating-group method (RGM) to describe binary-cluster scattering and reactions, using channel states that represent two clusters in relative motion with appropriate inter-cluster antisymmetrization. The interactions used are primarily chiral effective field theory (chiral EFT) nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) forces, softened via similarity renormalization group (SRG) transformations.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state energy of 6He calculated with SRG-evolved N3LO NN potential (Entem 2003) with Lambda = 2.02 fm^-1, showing convergence with increasing Nmax basis size and HO frequency dependence; exponential extrapolation E(Nmax) = E_infinity + a e^{-b Nmax} is used.
* Typical HO frequency used in NCSM calculations is hbar-Omega = 20 MeV, corresponding to oscillator length b0 = 1.44 fm.
* Cutoff for chiral EFT interactions typically varies in the range of 400-600 MeV; most calculations use NN interactions with Lambda_cut = 500 MeV.
* SRG flow parameter Lambda = s^{-(1/4)} is used, with low SRG parameters Lambda less than or approximately 2 fm^-1 noted as requiring caution for wave function renormalization.
* NCSM basis sizes typically reach Nmax ~ 10-14 for practical calculations.

## Context

This work extends the line of *ab initio* nuclear structure methods, particularly the no-core shell model (NCSM) developed by Navratil and collaborators (2000), to include continuum and reaction capabilities through the NCSMC approach (Baroni 2013). It contrasts with exact methods for A=3,4 systems such as Faddeev (Glöckle 2001), Faddeev-Yakubovsky (Lazauskas 2005), and hyperspherical harmonics (Marcucci 2009), and with other *ab initio* reaction approaches like the coupled cluster method with Gamow basis (Hagen 2012) and nuclear lattice EFT (Elhatisari 2015). The work enables *ab initio* calculations of radiative capture and transfer reactions in light nuclei relevant to astrophysics, building on the NCSM/RGM framework (Quaglioni 2008, 2009).
