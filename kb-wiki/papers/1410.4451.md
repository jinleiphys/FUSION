# Proton and Neutron Momentum Distributions in A = 3 Asymmetric Nuclei

> A proposal approved by the Jefferson Lab (JLab) PAC to study the proton-to-neutron momentum distribution ratio in A=3 nuclei via (e,e'p) scattering off 3He and 3H mirror nuclei. The experiment will measure the 3H(e,e'p) and 3He(e,e'p) cross-sections and cross-section ratios at Q2 = 2 and xB>1 kinematics, over a missing momentum range of 0 - 450 MeV/c. The experiment was approved in 2014 at part of the JLab Hall-A Tritium run period for a total run time of 12 days.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that in asymmetric A=3 nuclei, the minority nucleon species (protons in tritium, neutrons in helium-3) will have a higher average kinetic energy than the majority species, due to the dominance of neutron-proton short-range correlated pairs populating the high-momentum tail equally between the two species. This prediction depends on selecting kinematics that minimize final state interactions, specifically at small recoil angles relative to the momentum transfer.

## Method
The experiment will measure quasielastic (e,e'p) cross sections from both 3He and 3H mirror nuclei at Q2 = 2 (GeV/c)2 and xB = 1.2, using the MARATHON target and two High Resolution Spectrometers in Jefferson Lab Hall A. The missing momentum range covered is 0 to 450 MeV/c, and the kinematics are chosen at small theta_rq (angle between recoil momentum and momentum transfer) to suppress final state interaction effects. The ratio of 3He(e,e'p) to 3H(e,e'p) cross sections will be used to extract the ratio of ground state minority to majority nucleon momentum distributions, where residual final state interactions largely cancel.

## Key numbers
- Q2 = 2 (GeV/c)2 for the electron scattering kinematics
- xB = 1.2 for the Bjorken scaling variable
- Missing momentum range: 0 to 450 MeV/c
- Total requested beam time: 12 days (1 day calibration, 9 days for 3H(e,e'p) and 3He(e,e'p) measurements, plus additional time)
- From Variational Monte Carlo calculations: average kinetic energy of protons in 3He is 14.71 MeV (majority) and in 3H is 19.61 MeV (minority), giving a ratio of 0.76 for 3He and 1.31 for 3H
- From VMC calculations: average kinetic energy of neutrons in 3He is 19.35 MeV (minority) and in 3H is 14.96 MeV (majority)

## Context
This experiment extends the study of short-range correlated pairs from symmetric nuclei (4He, 12C) studied by Subedi (2008), Piasetzky (2006), and Egiyan (2006) to asymmetric A=3 mirror nuclei, where the np-SRC dominance predicts an inversion of momentum sharing between majority and minority nucleons. The work complements the MARATHON experiment's inclusive (e,e') measurements on 3He and 3H and will help correct for off-shell effects in deep inelastic scattering data. Previous 3He(e,e'p) measurements by Rvachev (2005) and Benmokhtar (2005) at lower Q2 were dominated by final state interactions, while the only prior 3H(e,e'p) measurement by Johansson (1964) had limited statistics and low momentum transfer.
