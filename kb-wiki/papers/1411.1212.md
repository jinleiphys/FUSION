# Low energy chiral two pion exchange potential with statistical uncertainties

> We present a new phenomenological Nucleon-Nucleon chiral potential fitted to 925 pp and 1743 np scattering data selected from the Granada-2013 NN-database up to a laboratory energy of $125$ MeV with 20 short distance parameters and three chiral constants $c_1$, $c_3$ and $c_4$ with $χ^2/ν= 1.02$. Special attention is given to testing the normality of the residuals which allows for a sound propagation of statistical errors from the experimental data to the potential parameters, phase-shifts, scattering amplitudes and counter-terms. This fit allows for a new determination of the chiral constants $c_1$, $c_3$ and $c_4$ compatible with previous determinations from NN data. This new interactions is found to be softer than other high quality potentials by undertaking a Weinberg eigenvalue analysis. We further explore the interplay between the error analysis and the assumed form of the short distance interaction. The present work shows that it is possible to fit NN scattering with a TPE chiral potential fulfilling all necessary statistical requirements up to 125 MeV and shows unequivocal non-vanishing D-wave short distance pieces.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a chiral two-pion-exchange (TPE) nucleon-nucleon potential fitted to scattering data up to 125 MeV laboratory energy can achieve a statistically satisfactory fit (chi-squared per degree of freedom = 1.02) and pass normality tests, but the reduction in fitted energy range leads to larger statistical uncertainties and increased correlations among parameters compared to fits up to 350 MeV.

## Method

The authors constructed a coarse-grained delta-shell potential with a chiral TPE tail starting at 1.8 fm, fitted to 925 pp and 1743 np scattering data from the Granada-2013 database up to 125 MeV laboratory energy. They used 20 short-distance delta-shell parameters plus three chiral constants (c1, c3, c4) as fitting parameters, and applied a Tail-Sensitive normality test to validate the residuals before propagating statistical uncertainties via Monte Carlo sampling from the covariance matrix.

## Key numbers

- Chi-squared per degree of freedom: chi^2/nu = 1.02 for the fit to 2668 scattering data points
- Fitted chiral constants: c1 = -0.27 +/- 2.87 GeV^-1, c3 = -5.77 +/- 1.58 GeV^-1, c4 = 4.24 +/- 0.73 GeV^-1
- Number of short-distance parameters: 20 (reduced from 30 in the 350 MeV fit)
- Tail-Sensitive test statistic: T = 0.0068, with critical value T_c = 0.0008 at significance level alpha = 0.05 for sample size N = 2668
- Boundary radius between short-range and long-range parts: r_c = 1.8 fm
- Delta-shell spacing: Delta r = 0.6 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous coarse-grained delta-shell potentials (Perez 2013, 2014) by restricting the fitting range to 125 MeV, which improves the softness of the interaction as measured by Weinberg eigenvalue analysis. The potential is softer than traditional high-quality potentials such as Reid93, NijmII, and AV18, making it more suitable for mean-field and ab initio nuclear structure calculations. The approach enables propagation of statistical uncertainties from scattering data into few-body calculations, as demonstrated in the authors' prior work (Perez 2014), and provides a framework for comparing chiral TPE versus OPE potentials at low energies.
