# The $β^4$ potential at the U(5)-O(6) critical point of the Interacting Boson Model

> Exact numerical results of the interacting boson model Hamiltonian along the integrable line from U(5) to O(6) are obtained by diagonalization within boson seniority subspaces. The matrix Hamiltonian reduces to a block tridiagonal form which can be diagonalized for large number of bosons. We present results for the low energy spectrum and the transition probabilities for systems up to 10000 bosons, which confirm that at the critical point the system is equally well described by the Bohr Hamiltonian with a $β^4$ potential.

# The β⁴ potential at the U(5)-O(6) critical point of the Interacting Boson Model

## Key claim

The paper confirms that at the critical point of the U(5) to O(6) transition in the Interacting Boson Model (IBM), the low-energy spectrum and transition probabilities converge in the large-boson-number limit to the predictions of the Bohr Hamiltonian with a β⁴ potential, rather than to the E(5) critical point symmetry based on a square well potential. This conclusion is reached by extending calculations to systems of up to 10000 bosons, overcoming the previous limitation of N=40 for transition rates.

## Method

The authors diagonalize two IBM Hamiltonians (one using a pairing operator and one using a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction) along the integrable line from U(5) to O(6). They exploit the su(1,1) pair algebra of s- and d-bosons to construct a basis classified by boson seniority quantum numbers (ν_s, ν_d), which reduces the Hamiltonian matrices to block tridiagonal form within each seniority subspace. This allows exact diagonalization for very large boson numbers, yielding both energy eigenvalues and wave functions needed to calculate electric quadrupole transition probabilities.

## Key numbers

- Systems studied up to N = 10000 bosons (previous work limited to N = 40 for transition rates)
- For Hamiltonian H_I, the critical control parameter is x_c^I = 0.5, independent of boson number N
- For Hamiltonian H_II, the critical control parameter is x_c^II = (4N-8)/(5N-8), which approaches 4/5 in the large N limit
- Four energy ratios and two B(E2) ratios (R_1 = B(E2;4⁺_{1,2} → 2⁺_{1,1}) / B(E2;2⁺_{1,1} → 0⁺_{1,0}) and R_2 = B(E2;0⁺_{2,0} → 2⁺_{1,1}) / B(E2;2⁺_{1,1} → 0⁺_{1,0})) are computed and shown to converge to the β⁴ potential predictions

## Context

This work extends a previous study by the same authors (Garcia-Ramos, Dukelsky, Arias, 2004) that used Richardson equations for energies but could only compute transition rates up to N=40 using standard IBM codes. The results confirm that the IBM critical point corresponds to the Bohr Hamiltonian with a β⁴ potential rather than the E(5) critical point symmetry proposed by Iachello (2000). The formalism can be generalized to other two-level boson models, and the issue has also been studied from a different perspective by Rowe et al. (2004).
