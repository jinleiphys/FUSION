# Gapless superfluidity in neutron stars: Normal-fluid fraction

> Our previous investigation within the time-dependent nuclear energy-density functional theory showed that the nuclear superfluids contained inside cold neutron stars could become gapless under certain circumstances. The absence of a gap in the energy spectrum of quasiparticle excitations leads to a specific heat that is comparable to that in the normal phase in sharp contrast with the exponential suppression in the BCS phase of type $^1S_0$ pairing. Here, we further study gapless superfluidity within the same microscopic framework focusing on hydrodynamic properties. In particular, we calculate the mass fraction transported by the normal fluid of quasiparticle excitations, and we find that it can be finite even at zero temperature. We derive an approximate analytical formula for arbitrary neutron-proton superfluid mixtures. We also present numerical results for neutron stars. Our study suggests that the dynamics of neutron stars may be much more complicated than previously thought. The realization of gapless superfluidity in neutron stars and its implications are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors show that in neutron stars, nuclear superfluids can enter a gapless phase where a normal fluid of quasiparticle excitations carries mass even at zero temperature, contradicting the usual assumption that the normal fluid vanishes at T=0. This effect occurs only when the effective superfluid velocity exceeds Landau's critical velocity but remains below the critical velocity for superfluidity destruction.

## Method

The authors solve the self-consistent time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) equations within nuclear energy-density functional theory using the Brussels-Montreal functional BSk24, computing the entrainment matrix and normal-fluid densities for homogeneous neutron-proton superfluid mixtures. They derive an analytic expression for the function Y_q that determines the normal-fluid fraction at zero temperature in the gapless phase, and obtain approximate formulas in the weak-coupling regime where the effective superfluid velocity is small compared to the Fermi velocity.

## Key numbers

- Landau's effective superfluid velocity: V_Lq = V_Fq * sqrt( (mu_bar_0/2) * [ sqrt(1 + (Delta_bar_0/mu_bar_0)^2) - 1 ] ), where mu_bar_0 and Delta_bar_0 are the zero-temperature reduced chemical potential and gap parameter in the absence of superflow
- Critical velocity in weak-coupling approximation: V_cq ≈ (e/2) * V_Lq ≈ 1.35914 * V_Lq, where e ≈ 2.71828 is Euler's number
- Maximum true nucleon velocity: v_q^(Max) ≈ 1.0111 * V_Lq, reached at effective superfluid velocity V_q^(Max) ≈ 1.0283 * V_Lq
- The function Y_q at zero temperature in the gapless phase: Y_q = (mu_bar + Vbar^2 - (1/4)*(Deltabar^2/Vbar^2))^(3/2), where Vbar = V_q/V_Fq

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation (Allard and Chamel, 2023) which first demonstrated gapless superfluidity in neutron stars using TDHFB theory, where they focused on thermal properties. The present study examines hydrodynamic properties, specifically the normal-fluid fraction, which was previously calculated within Fermi liquid theory by Leinson (2018) and using relativistic mean-field models by Sourie and collaborators (2016). The results generalize the expression derived by Vollhardt and Maki (1978) for superfluid He-3 to neutron-proton superfluid mixtures, and show that the dynamics of neutron stars may be much more complicated than previously thought, with implications for pulsar glitches and gravitational-wave emission.
