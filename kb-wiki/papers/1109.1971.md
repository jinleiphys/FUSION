# Screening Masses in Gluonic Plasma

> Both electric and magnetic screening masses in a nonperturbative gluonic background are investigated using operator product expansion. The magnetic screening mass is found to agree with lattice results whereas the electric screening mass is somewhat smaller than the one found on the lattice.

## Key claim

The authors use operator product expansion (OPE) to compute nonperturbative contributions to both electric and magnetic screening masses in a gluonic plasma; the magnetic screening mass agrees with lattice results, but the electric screening mass is about 20% below the lattice data points, which themselves show a rather large spread.

## Method

The nonperturbative gluon self-energy was computed using OPE, including contributions from dimension-four gluon condensates (chromoelectric and chromomagnetic), ghost condensates, and mixed ghost-gluon condensates, with the nonperturbative ghost-gluon vertex fixed by renormalization. The screening masses were extracted from the pole position of the gluon propagator in the spacelike static limit (p₀ = 0, p² = –M²), solving the coupled equations for the longitudinal (electric) and transverse (magnetic) components. The electric and magnetic condensates were related to lattice-measured plaquette differences for pure SU(3) gauge theory, and the perturbative part was evaluated using a two-loop running coupling with scale μ̄ = 2πT and Λ = 1.03 T_c.

## Key numbers

- Electric screening mass m_D from OPE is about 20% below lattice QCD data points (LAT-I from Nakamura (2003) and LAT-II from Kaczmarek (2005)) over the temperature range considered.
- Magnetic screening mass m_m = b^(1/4) is purely nonperturbative and agrees relatively well with lattice data; it shows weak gauge dependence between Landau gauge (ξ = 0) and Feynman gauge (ξ = 1).
- The nonperturbative coefficient a in π_l^(np) is a = (4π² N_c / F_A) [ (8/3)(α_s/π)⟨E²⟩_T + (8/30)(α_s/π)⟨B²⟩_T ].
- The nonperturbative coefficient b in π_t^(np) is b = (4π² N_c / F_A) [ W_E (α_s/π)⟨E²⟩_T – W_B (α_s/π)⟨B²⟩_T ], with W_E = (2 + ξ/3) and W_B = (1/15)(38 + 9ξ).
- The zero-temperature gluon condensate is taken as ⟨G²⟩_0 / T_c⁴ = 2.5, with critical temperature T_c = 260 MeV for pure SU(3) gauge theory.
- Perturbative leading-order electric mass is m_D^(pert) = 4πα_s T².

## Context

This work extends the OPE approach to finite-temperature screening masses, building on earlier nonperturbative studies of the gluon propagator by Schmidt (1999) and on lattice QCD results for screening masses by Nakamura (2003), Kaczmarek (2005), and Cucchieri (2000). It contrasts with perturbative hard thermal loop methods and with dimensionally reduced QCD approaches by Arnold (1995) and Laine (2009), which work at much higher temperatures. The results provide nonperturbative input for calculating thermodynamic quantities, spectral properties, jet quenching, and quarkonium suppression in hot QCD matter produced in heavy-ion collisions.
