# Four-nucleon scattering: Ab initio calculations in momentum space

> The four-body equations of Alt, Grassberger and Sandhas are solved for $\nH$ scattering at energies below three-body breakup threshold using various realistic interactions including one derived from chiral perturbation theory. After partial wave decomposition the equations are three-variable integral equations that are solved numerically without any approximations beyond the usual discretization of continuum variables on a finite momentum mesh. Large number of two-, three- and four-nucleon partial waves are considered until the convergence of the observables is obtained. The total $\nH$ cross section data in the resonance region is not described by the calculations which confirms previous findings by other groups. Nevertheless the numbers we get are slightly higher and closer to the data than previously found and depend on the choice of the two-nucleon potential. Correlations between the $A_y$ deficiency in $\nd$ elastic scattering and the total $\nH$ cross section are studied.

## Key claim

The authors solve the four-body Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for neutron-triton (n-3H) scattering without approximations beyond discretization of continuum variables, but the total cross section data in the resonance region is still not described by the calculations, confirming previous findings; however, the numbers obtained are slightly higher and closer to the data than previously found and depend on the choice of the two-nucleon potential.

## Method

The AGS four-particle scattering equations are solved in momentum space after partial wave decomposition, yielding three-variable integral equations that are discretized on a finite momentum mesh and solved via Neumann series summation with the Pade method. The two-nucleon transition matrix is treated exactly, and the subsystem transition operators for 1+3 and 2+2 partitions are calculated through Neumann series rather than full off-shell evaluation to avoid storage problems. Large numbers of two-, three-, and four-nucleon partial waves are included until convergence of observables is obtained, with the permutation operators P1 and P34 handled using spline interpolation methods.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section sigma_t for n-3H scattering at E_n = 4 MeV with AV18 potential converges to 2.286 barns with I <= 5 + 3H_6 partial waves
- At E_n = 3.5 MeV, sigma_t values range from 2.216 b (INOY04) to 2.377 b (N3LO), with AV18 giving 2.331 b
- Triton binding energies range from 7.621 MeV (AV18) to 8.493 MeV (INOY04)
- Alpha particle binding energies range from 24.24 MeV (AV18) to 29.11 MeV (INOY04)
- Singlet scattering lengths a_0 range from 4.02 fm (INOY04) to 4.28 fm (AV18)
- Triplet scattering lengths a_1 range from 3.51 fm (INOY04) to 3.71 fm (AV18)
- At E_n = 0 MeV, sigma_t values range from 1.67 b (INOY04) to 1.88 b (AV18)
- Modified P-wave potentials give sigma_t = 2.375 b (AV18') and 2.191 b (INOY04') at E_n = 3.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the AGS equation approach of Fonseca (1999, 2002) from rank-one approximations to exact treatment of the two-nucleon interaction, and confirms the findings of the Grenoble group (Cieselski, 1999) and Lazauskas (2004, 2005) that realistic interactions miss the total cross section peak by at least 0.2 b. The calculations enable systematic study of correlations between three-nucleon and four-nucleon observables, including the Tjon line (Tjon, 1975) correlating triton and alpha binding energies, and correlations between the A_y deficiency in n-d elastic scattering and the total n-3H cross section.
