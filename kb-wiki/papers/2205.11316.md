# Resonance form factors from finite-volume correlation functions with the external field method

> A novel method for the extraction of form factors of unstable particles on the lattice is proposed. The approach is based on the study of two-particle scattering in a static, spatially periodic external field by using a generalization of the Lüscher method in the presence of such a field. It is shown that the resonance form factor is given by the derivative of the resonance pole position in the complex plane with respect to the coupling constant to the external field. Unlike the standard approach, this proposal does not suffer from problems caused by the presence of the triangle diagram.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that resonance form factors can be extracted from lattice calculations by using the Feynman-Hellmann theorem in a spatially periodic external field, where the derivative of the resonance pole position with respect to the coupling constant to the external field gives the form factor, provided the local (contact) contribution is isolated from the problematic triangle diagram contribution.

## Method

The authors use a non-relativistic effective field theory (NREFT) framework for two spinless particles interacting via S-wave scattering, with a static spatially periodic external field \(A^0(\mathbf{x}) = A_0 \cos(\omega x_\parallel)\). They derive a generalized Lüscher quantization condition in the presence of the external field, which relates the two-particle energy spectrum in a finite cubic volume to the contact coupling \(\kappa\) that encodes the resonance form factor. The extraction of \(\kappa\) is performed at real energies without analytic continuation, and the infinite-volume limit is trivial because contact terms contain only exponentially suppressed finite-volume corrections.

## Key numbers

* The single-particle form factor vertex is given by \(\Gamma(\mathbf{p},\mathbf{q}) = 1 - \frac{C_R}{6m^2} (\mathbf{p}-\mathbf{q})^2\), where \(C_R\) is related to the mean charge radius through \(C_R = m^2 \langle r^2 \rangle\).
* The S-wave scattering amplitude is expressed as \(T(q_0) = \frac{8\pi/m}{K^{-1}(q_0,q_0) - i q_0}\), with \(C_0 = -\frac{2\pi a}{m}\) and \(C_2 = \frac{\pi r a^2}{2m}\), where \(a\) is the scattering length and \(r\) is the effective range.
* The resonance pole position is determined from \(-\frac{1}{a} + \frac{1}{2} r q_R^2 - \sqrt{-q_R^2} = 0\), with \(\text{Re}\,q_R^2 > 0\), \(\text{Im}\,q_R^2 < 0\), and \(|\text{Im}\,q_R^2| \ll |\text{Re}\,q_R^2| \ll m^2\).
* The resonance energy in moving frames is \(P^0_R = 2m + \frac{\mathbf{P}^2}{4m} + \frac{q_R^2}{m} = \text{Re}\,P^0_R - \frac{i}{2} \Gamma_R\), where \(\Gamma_R\) is the resonance width.
* The wave function renormalization constant of the resonance is \(Z = \frac{8\pi/m}{[K^{-1}(q_R,q_R)]' - [\sqrt{-q_R^2}]'}\), where primes denote derivatives with respect to \(q_0^2\).

## Context

This work extends the Feynman-Hellmann method previously applied to stable hadrons (QCDSF Collaboration, 2017) to the case of unstable particles, generalizing the approach of Ruiz de Elvira (2017) who used the theorem to define sigma-terms for resonances. The method avoids the difficulties with triangle diagrams identified in earlier work by Hoja (2010), Bernard (2012), Briceno (2015), and Baroni (2018), where the finite-volume matrix elements exhibited irregular behavior requiring subtraction and re-addition of the triangle contribution. The approach enables extraction of resonance form factors from lattice QCD without performing analytic continuation, using only the two-particle energy spectrum in an external field and previously determined elastic phase shifts and single-particle form factors.
