# The mean square radius of the neutron distribution and the skin thickness derived from electron scattering

> The second-order moment of the nuclear charge density($R^2_c$) is dominated by the mean square radius(msr) of the point proton distribution($R_p^2$), while the fourth-order moment($Q^4_c$) depends on the msr of the point neutron one($R_n^2$) also. Moreover, $R^2_n$ is strongly correlated to $R^2_c$ in nuclear models. According to these facts, the linear relationship between various moments in the nuclear mean field models are investigated with use of the least squares method for $^{40}$Ca, $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb. From the intersection points of the obtained straight lines with those of the experimental values for $R^2_c$ and $Q^4_c$ determined through electron scattering, the values of $R_p$ and $R_n$ are estimated. Since relativistic and non-relativistic models provide different lines, the obtained values of $R_n$ and the skin thickness($R_n-R_p$) differ from each other in the two frameworks.

## Key claim

The authors estimate the mean square radius of the point neutron distribution ($R_n^2$) and the neutron skin thickness ($R_n - R_p$) by exploiting the linear relationship between the second- and fourth-order moments of the nuclear charge density in relativistic and non-relativistic mean field models, but the extracted values differ between the two frameworks because relativistic and non-relativistic models produce different regression lines.

## Method

The authors used 11 relativistic and 9 non-relativistic mean field models to calculate the second-order moment ($R_c^2$) and fourth-order moment ($Q_c^4$) of the nuclear charge density for $^{40}$Ca, $^{48}$Ca, and $^{208}$Pb. They performed least-squares linear regression between various calculated moments and determined the intersection points of these regression lines with the experimental values of $R_c^2$ and $Q_c^4$ from conventional electron scattering, thereby extracting $R_p$, $R_n$, and the skin thickness $\delta R = R_n - R_p$.

## Key numbers

* For $^{48}$Ca, relativistic models predict $R_n = 3.587$ to $3.605$ fm, while non-relativistic models predict $R_n = 3.492$ to $3.502$ fm.
* For $^{208}$Pb, relativistic models predict $R_n = 5.723$ to $5.749$ fm, while non-relativistic models predict $R_n = 5.587$ to $5.627$ fm.
* For $^{48}$Ca, the skin thickness $\delta R$ is $0.206$ to $0.232$ fm in relativistic models and $0.115$ to $0.139$ fm in non-relativistic models.
* For $^{208}$Pb, the skin thickness $\delta R$ is $0.258$ to $0.306$ fm in relativistic models and $0.128$ to $0.194$ fm in non-relativistic models.
* The deviation from the mean value of $R_n$ is less than $\pm 0.5\%$; for $^{208}$Pb in relativistic models it is $\pm 0.227\%$, much smaller than in previous parity-violating electron scattering studies.
* The experimental errors of $R_c$ and $Q_c$ from Ref. [emrich] are $\pm (0.009$ to $0.022)$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the approach used by Roca-Maza et al. (2011) for parity-violating electron scattering asymmetry ($A_{pv}$) to conventional electron scattering data, using the fourth-order moment $Q_c^4$ which depends on the neutron distribution. The authors note that this is the first analysis of neutron density based on conventional electron scattering data, and the extracted $R_n$ values have much smaller uncertainties than those from parity-violating experiments on $^{208}$Pb (Abrahamyan et al., 2012). The results enable more precise constraints on neutron distributions in nuclei, which are relevant for nuclear structure studies and neutron star physics.
