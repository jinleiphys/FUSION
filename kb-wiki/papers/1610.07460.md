# Partial Wave Decomposition in Friedrichs Model With Self-interacting Continua

> We consider the nonrelativistic model of coupling bare discrete states with continuum states in which the continuum states can have interactions among themselves. By partial-wave decomposition and constraint to the conserved angular momentum eigenstates, the model can be reduced to Friedrichs-like model with additional interactions between the continua. If a kind of factorizable form factor is chosen, the model can be exactly solvable, that is, the generalized discrete eigenstates including bound states, virtual states, and resonances, can all be represented using the original bare states, and so do the in-state and out-state. The exact $S$ matrix is thus obtained. We then discuss the behaviors of the dynamically generated $S$-wave and $P$-wave discrete states as the coupling is varying when there is only one self-interacting bare continuum state. We find that even when the potential is repulsive there could also be resonances and virtual states. In the $P$-wave cases with attractive interaction, we find that when there is a near-threshold bound state, there will always be an accompanying virtual state and we also give a more general argument of this effect.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in a nonrelativistic Friedrichs model with self-interacting continua, after partial-wave decomposition, the Hamiltonian reduces to a solvable form when factorizable form factors are chosen, and that even with a repulsive potential there can be resonances and virtual states, while in P-wave attractive cases a near-threshold bound state always has an accompanying virtual state.

## Method

The authors perform partial-wave decomposition of the nonrelativistic Hamiltonian coupling discrete bare states with continuum states that interact among themselves, restricting to fixed total angular momentum eigenstates. They choose factorizable form factors of the form \(g_{ij}(\omega)=u^*_{ij} f_j^*(\omega)\) and \(f_{j'j}(\omega',\omega)=v_{j'j}f_{j'}(\omega')f_j^*(\omega)\), which allows exact solution of the eigenstate equation for discrete eigenstates (bound states, virtual states, resonances), in-states, and out-states. The exact partial-wave S-matrix is then obtained, and the authors study pole trajectories for S-wave and P-wave dynamically generated states using an exponential form factor \(f(\omega)=(\omega-a)^{(l+1/2)/2}\exp\{-(\omega-a)/(2\Lambda)\}\) with no bare discrete state.

## Key numbers

- For S-wave with repulsive potential: resonance poles exist on the second Riemann sheet; as coupling decreases, poles move toward negative infinity on the complex plane (no explicit numerical coupling values given).
- For S-wave with attractive potential: a bound state exists on the first sheet for large coupling; as coupling decreases, it moves through threshold to become a virtual state on the second sheet, then moves toward negative infinity (no explicit numerical coupling values given).
- For P-wave with repulsive potential: a virtual state exists on the second sheet; as coupling goes to positive infinity, the virtual state approaches a limiting point determined by \(G_P^{II}(E)=0\) which is independent of \(\lambda\) (no explicit numerical value for the limiting point given).
- For P-wave with attractive potential: one bound state and one virtual state exist for large coupling; as coupling decreases, the two solutions move through threshold and become a pair of resonance poles on the second sheet, then move toward negative infinity (no explicit numerical coupling values given).
- All calculations use dimensionless parameters: threshold \(a=0.5\), cutoff \(\Lambda=2\), reduced mass \(\mu=1\).

## Context

This work extends the Friedrichs model (Friedrichs, 1948) by including interactions among continuum states after partial-wave decomposition, generalizing previous solvable multi-continuum Friedrichs models (Xiao, 2016) and models without bare discrete states (Aceti, 2012; Sekihara, 2014). The results enable systematic study of dynamically generated states in different partial waves, with applications to understanding exotic hadron resonances such as X(3872) and the relation between bound states and virtual states in P-wave channels, generalizing compositeness and elementariness analyses (Weinberg, 1962; Sekihara, 2014) to arbitrary partial waves.
