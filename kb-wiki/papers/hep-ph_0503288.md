# Color-spin locking phase in two-flavor quark matter for compact star phenomenology

> We study a spin-1 single flavor color superconducting phase which results from a color-spin locking (CSL) interaction in two-flavor quark matter. This phase is particularly interesting for compact star cooling applications since the CSL phase may survive under charge neutrality constraints implying a mismatch between up- and down-quark chemical potentials which can destroy the scalar diquark condensate. CSL gaps are evaluated within an NJL model and they are found to be consistent with cooling phenomenology if a density dependent coupling constant is used.

## Key claim

The CSL (color-spin locking) phase in two-flavor quark matter can satisfy compact star cooling constraints, specifically that all quarks are paired with the smallest gap in the range of several keV and decreasing with density, but only if a density-dependent coupling constant, motivated by the running of the QCD coupling, is introduced; with constant coupling the effective gaps do not decrease with density as required.

## Method

The authors employ a two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with four-point interactions in the quark-antiquark and diquark channels, using a color-current relation \(H_v = 3G/8\) to fix the diquark coupling. They solve the mean-field gap equations for the dynamical quark mass \(M_f\) and the CSL diquark gap \(\Delta_f\) at zero temperature, using exact dispersion relations for two quasiparticle modes and approximate (expanded to order \(|\Delta_f|^2\)) dispersion relations for the remaining four modes. The effective gap \(\Delta_{f,\rm eff}\), which is the smallest gap in the spectrum and controls cooling, is computed from \(\Delta_{f,\rm eff} = (M_f / \sqrt{\mu_f^2 + |\Delta_f|^2})\,|\Delta_f|\).

## Key numbers

* For constant coupling, the CSL gap \(\Delta_f\) at the critical chemical potential \(\mu_{f,\rm crit}\) ranges from about 0.1 MeV (Set 1) to about 6 MeV (Set 3), while at \(\mu_f = 500\) MeV it reaches about 10 MeV for all parameter sets.
* The effective gap \(\Delta_{f,\rm eff}\) is up to two orders of magnitude smaller than \(\Delta_f\); for Set 2 with constant coupling it is nearly constant over the relevant chemical potential range.
* With a density-dependent coupling and \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD} = 280-300\) MeV, the effective CSL gaps change by more than two orders of magnitude in the range \(\mu_f = 325-500\) MeV, matching the cooling phenomenology requirement that the smallest gaps be in the range 10-100 keV.
* Three NJL parameter sets are used, with vacuum constituent quark masses of 300 MeV (Set 1), 350 MeV (Set 2), and 400 MeV (Set 3), corresponding to cutoff \(\Lambda = 664.3, 613.4, 587.9\) MeV respectively.

## Context

This work extends studies of spin-1 color superconducting phases in quark matter, particularly the CSL phase found to be the most stable spin-1 phase at high densities by Schmitt (2004). It addresses the phenomenological "2SC+X" phase introduced by Grigorian (2004) to explain compact star cooling data, for which no microscopic calculation previously existed. The CSL phase is contrasted with the 2SC phase, which is fragile under charge neutrality constraints (Alford, 2002), and with the A-phase spin-1 pattern (Schmitt, 2005), which leaves ungapped modes. The results enable future construction of electrically neutral beta-equilibrium quark matter for hybrid star cooling simulations.
