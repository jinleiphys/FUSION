# Elastic scattering on a quantum computer

> Scattering probes the internal structure of quantum systems. We calculate the two-particle elastic scattering phase shift for a short-ranged interaction on a quantum computer. Short-ranged interactions with a large scattering length or shallow bound state describe a universality class that is of interest in atomic, condensed matter, nuclear, and particle physics. The phase shift is calculated by relating the ground state energy of the interacting particles in a harmonic trap. The relaxation method is used as the variational quantum eigensolver for the ground state calculation. Schmidt decomposition is used to reduce quantum circuits nominally requiring tens of qubits to 2-qubit circuits, thus reducing the noise in quantum measurements. Calculations in multi-particle systems with many-body interactions would benefit from this reduction of qubits in noisy quantum processors.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the two-particle elastic scattering phase shift for a short-ranged interaction on a quantum computer, using the Busch formula that relates the ground state energy of interacting particles in a harmonic trap to the phase shift, but only for the ground state of the relative motion, requiring variation of the harmonic oscillator frequency to access different momenta.

## Method

The authors discretize the two-particle Hamiltonian on a lattice and compute the ground state energy using a variational quantum eigensolver (VQE) implemented via the relaxation method, where the wave function amplitudes in position space are iteratively updated classically. The energy expectation value is computed on a quantum processor, and Schmidt decomposition is applied to reduce the required quantum circuits from tens of qubits to at most 2-qubit circuits by tracing out spectator qubits. The phase shift is then extracted from the computed relative energy using the Busch formula.

## Key numbers

* For a system with particle masses m1 = m2 = 940 MeV, lattice spacing b = 1.25 fm, lattice size L = 17, and oscillator frequencies ω = 3, 4, 5 MeV, the lowest three momentum phase shift data points were calculated on an ideal simulator.
* For b = 1.0 fm, L = 15, and ω = 6 to 9 MeV in 1 MeV intervals, the next four phase shift data points were obtained.
* The highest momentum point used b = 0.8 fm, L = 13, and ω = 10 MeV.
* On the IBMQ-Brisbane quantum processor, with c0 = 0.2, the SVD-reduced circuit measurements gave total energy Etotal = 22.5 MeV when combining Run 2 and Run 3 measurements, compared to the exact result of 22.33 MeV for the trial wave function.
* The Hilbert space for L = 17 is approximately 2 × 10^10 dimensional, yet expectation values were computed using at most 2^2 dimensional mixed states after Schmidt decomposition.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Bedaque (2022) for calculating inelastic reactions on quantum computers by demonstrating a practical calculation of elastic scattering phase shifts using current noisy quantum processors. The method builds on the Busch formula (Busch, 1998) that relates energy shifts in a harmonic trap to scattering phase shifts, previously used in classical calculations. The Schmidt decomposition technique for reducing qubit requirements enables calculations on NISQ-era devices and could benefit multi-particle systems with many-body interactions.
