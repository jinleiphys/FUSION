# Probing the Nuclear Equation of State in the quasi-elastic nucleus-nucleus scattering

> Large-angle elastic scattering of alpha-particle and strongly-bound light nuclei at a few tens MeV/nucleon has shown the pattern of rainbow scattering. This interesting process was shown to involve a significant overlap of the two colliding nuclei, with the total nuclear density well above the saturation density of normal nuclear matter (NM). For a microscopic calculation of the nucleus-nucleus potential within the folding model, we have developed a density dependent nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction based on the G-matrix interaction M3Y. Our folding analysis of the refractive 4He, 12C, and 16O elastic scattering shows consistently that the NM incompressibility K should be around 250 MeV which implies a rather soft nuclear Equation of State (EOS). To probe the symmetry part of the nuclear EOS, we have used the isovector coupling to link the isospin dependence of the proton optical potential to the cross section of (p,n) charge-exchange reactions exciting the isobaric analog states in nuclei of different mass regions. With the isospin dependence of the NN interaction fine tuned to reproduce the charge exchange data, a realistic estimate of the NM symmetry energy has been made.

## Key claim

The nuclear matter incompressibility \(K\) is constrained to around 230–270 MeV from folding model analyses of refractive elastic scattering of alpha particles and light nuclei, implying a soft equation of state; the symmetry energy at saturation density is deduced to be \(E_{\text{sym}} \approx 31 \pm 1\) MeV from charge-exchange reactions, with the isovector strength of the density-dependent NN interaction needing to be about 10% stronger than the isoscalar strength to reproduce the data.

## Method

The authors performed folding model calculations of the real nucleus-nucleus optical potential using a density-dependent M3Y-type NN interaction (CDM3Y6) to describe elastic scattering data for \(^4\)He, \(^{12}\)C, and \(^{16}\)O at a few tens of MeV/nucleon, extracting the nuclear matter incompressibility \(K\) from the best-fit density dependence. For the symmetry energy, they used a coupled-channel analysis of \((p,n)\) charge-exchange reactions exciting isobaric analog states in \(^6\)He, \(^{48}\)Ca, \(^{90}\)Zr, \(^{120}\)Sn, and \(^{208}\)Pb, fine-tuning the isovector strength \(C_1\) of the CDM3Y6 interaction to reproduce the measured angular distributions and total cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter incompressibility \(K \approx 252\) MeV from the best-fit CDM3Y6 interaction for elastic \(^{16}\)O+\(^{16}\)O scattering at \(E_{\text{lab}} = 350\) MeV.
- The most realistic value for \(K\) is around 230–270 MeV, corresponding to a soft equation of state.
- Symmetry energy at saturation density \(E_{\text{sym}} \approx 31 \pm 1\) MeV from HF calculations using the best-fit isovector strength.
- The best-fit isovector strength \(C_1\) of the CDM3Y6 interaction is about 10% stronger than the isoscalar strength \(C_0\).
- For the \(^{48}\)Ca\((p,n)^{48}\)Sc reaction at 45 MeV, the experimental total cross section is \(8.4 \pm 1.0\) mb.
- The total overlap density in the \(^{16}\)O+\(^{16}\)O system at 350 MeV reaches up to \(2\rho_0\) (with \(\rho_0 \approx 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\)) at impact parameters as small as 2 fm.

## Context

This work extends the folding model approach of Khoa (1994, 1995, 1997) to systematically constrain both the isoscalar and isovector parts of the nuclear equation of state from elastic and charge-exchange scattering data. The deduced symmetry energy \(E_{\text{sym}} \approx 31 \pm 1\) MeV is complementary to structure studies of the neutron skin in \(^{208}\)Pb by Brown (2000) and Fur02, which give \(E_{\text{sym}} \approx 27–31\) MeV, and to heavy-ion fragmentation studies by Ono (2003) using antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. The results enable more reliable predictions for the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter relevant to neutron star structure and supernova dynamics.
