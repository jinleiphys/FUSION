# The Non-Quenching of $g_A$ in Nuclei and Emergent Scale Symmetry in Dense Baryonic Matter

> How the axial coupling constant $g_A$ in nuclear Gamow-Teller transitions described in shell model gets "quenched" to a universal constant close to 1 can be explained by nuclear correlations in Fermi-liquid fixed point theory using a scale-symmetric chiral Lagrangian supplemented with hidden local symmetric vector mesons. Contrary to what one might naively suspect -- and has been discussed in some circles, there is no fundamental quenching at nuclear matter density due to QCD condensates. When the density of many-body systems treated with the same Lagrangian increases beyond the density $n=n_{1/2}\gsim 2n_0$ (where $n_0$ is the normal nuclear matter density) at which skyrmions representing baryons fractionize to half-skyrmions, with the $ρ$ meson driven toward the vector manifestation fixed point and a scalar meson $σ$ driven to the dilaton-limit fixed point with the nucleons parity-doubled, the dense matter supports the "pseudo-conformal" sound velocity for $n\gsim n_{1/2}$ while the trace of the energy momentum tensor remains non-vanishing. A plausible interpretation is that this signals the emergence of scale symmetry not explicitly present or hidden in QCD in the vacuum. The fundamental constant $g_A$, unaffected by QCD condensates for $n< n_{1/2}$, does go to 1 as the dilaton-limit fixed point is approached before arriving at chiral restoration, but it is not directly related to the "quenched $g_A$" in nuclei which can be explained as a Fermi-liquid fixed point quantity. The mechanism that produces a precocious pseudo-conformal sound velocity is expected to impact on the tidal deformability $Λ$ in gravity waves from coalescing neutron stars.

## Key claim

The observed "quenching" of the axial coupling constant \(g_A\) in nuclear Gamow-Teller transitions to a universal value near 1 is not a fundamental QCD effect from condensates at nuclear matter density, but rather a Fermi-liquid fixed-point phenomenon arising from nuclear correlations; the fundamental \(g_A\) remains unscaled by density for \(n < n_{1/2} \sim 2n_0\), only approaching 1 at the dilaton-limit fixed point near chiral restoration.

## Method

The authors construct a scale-symmetric chiral Lagrangian with hidden local symmetry (sHLS) for vector mesons (\(\rho\), \(\omega\)) and a scalar dilaton \(\sigma\), treated in the "leading-order-scale symmetry" (LOSS) approximation. They embed this Lagrangian in baryonic matter using "sliding vacua" where bare parameters acquire intrinsic density dependence (IDD) from QCD condensates, then apply a mean-field treatment corresponding to Landau Fermi-liquid fixed-point theory, supplemented by \(V_{\text{lowk}}\) renormalization-group decimations to incorporate higher-order correlations. The topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions at density \(n_{1/2}\) is transcribed into the density dependence of the Lagrangian parameters, particularly the \(\rho\)-meson mass approaching the vector manifestation fixed point and the nucleon mass approaching a parity-doubling constant \(m_0\).

## Key numbers

- The "quenched" effective coupling in simple shell model is \(g_A^{\text{eff}} \approx 1.0 \pm 0.2\) for light nuclei, compared to the free-space value \(g_A = 1.2755(11)\).
- The dilaton condensate ratio at normal nuclear matter density is \(\Phi(n_0) = \langle \chi \rangle^\ast / \langle \chi \rangle \simeq 0.80\), determined from deeply bound pionic systems.
- The pion Fock contribution to the Landau parameter at \(n_0\) is \(\tilde{F}_1^\pi(n_0) = -0.459\).
- The predicted anomalous orbital gyromagnetic ratio for the proton is \(\delta g_l^p = 0.21\), in agreement with the experimental value \(\delta g_l^{\text{exp}} = 0.23 \pm 0.03\) in heavy nuclei.
- The topology change density is \(n_{1/2} \gsim 2n_0\), where \(n_0 \approx 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) is normal nuclear matter density.

## Context

This work extends the Fermi-liquid fixed-point approach of Friman and Rho (1996) and the scale-chiral effective field theory of Crewther and Tunstall (2012) to connect the long-standing "quenched \(g_A\)" problem in nuclear Gamow-Teller transitions (reviewed by Wilkinson (1973) and others) with dense matter relevant to neutron stars. The mechanism predicts a precocious pseudoconformal sound velocity for \(n \gsim n_{1/2}\), which impacts the tidal deformability \(\Lambda\) in gravitational waves from coalescing neutron stars (GW170817). The approach contrasts with conventional explanations involving core-polarization, \(\Delta\)-hole coupling (Bohr and Mottelson, Auerbach), and two-body meson-exchange currents, instead attributing the quenching to a Fermi-liquid fixed-point quantity with no fundamental QCD quenching at nuclear matter density.
