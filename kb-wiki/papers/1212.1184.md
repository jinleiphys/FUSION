# Phase diagram and surface tension in the three-flavor Polyakov-quark-meson model

> We obtain the in-medium effective potential of the three-flavor Polyakov-Quark-Meson model as a real function of real variables in the Polyakov loop variable, to allow for the study of all possible minima of the model. At finite quark chemical potential, the real and imaginary parts of the effective potential, in terms of the Polyakov loop variables, are made apparent, showing explicitly the fermion sign problem of the theory. The phase diagram and other equilibrium observables, obtained from the real part of the effective potential, are calculated in the mean-field approximation. The obtained results are compared to those found with the so-called saddle-point approach. Our procedure also allows the calculation of the surface tension between the chirally broken and confined phase, and the chirally restored and deconfined phase. The values of surface tension we find for low temperatures are very close to the ones recently found for two-flavor chiral models. Some consequences of our results for the early Universe, for heavy-ion collisions, and for proto-neutron stars are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the three-flavor Polyakov-quark-meson model at finite quark chemical potential has a complex effective potential with no true minima, so the authors propose neglecting the imaginary part to obtain a real effective potential that can be minimized; this approximation yields a phase diagram and surface tension values that differ from those obtained with the saddle-point approach.

## Method

The authors compute the in-medium effective potential of the three-flavor Polyakov-quark-meson model in the mean-field approximation, expressing the Polyakov loop variables in terms of their real and imaginary parts (alpha and beta). They neglect the imaginary part of the quark contribution to the effective potential, which is odd in beta, to obtain a real function of real variables that can be minimized. The minima of this real effective potential are then located as functions of temperature and quark chemical potential, and the surface tension between the chirally broken/confined phase and the chirally restored/deconfined phase is calculated along the first-order phase transition line.

## Key numbers

* The constituent light quark mass is set to m_l = 300 MeV in vacuum, giving m_s approximately 417 MeV for the strange quark.
* The sigma meson mass is taken as m_sigma = 500 MeV, within the range 400-550 MeV from the Particle Data Group.
* The parameter T_0 for the Polyakov loop potential with 2+1 quark flavors is T_0 = 182 MeV.
* The surface tension values found for low temperatures are very close to those recently found for two-flavor chiral models (no explicit numerical value given for the surface tension itself).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Roessner (2008) for the PNJL model to the three-flavor Polyakov-quark-meson model, providing an alternative to the saddle-point approach used by Fukushima (2004) and others. The calculation of surface tension enables studies of first-order phase transition dynamics relevant for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, NICA, and FAIR, as well as for proto-neutron stars and the early Universe, following methods applied to two-flavor quark-meson models by Palhares (2010) and to the NJL model by Pinto (2012).
