# Rapidity loss, spin and angular asymmetries in scattering of a quark from color field of a proton (nucleus)

> We calculate the helicity amplitudes for scattering of a quark from both large and small $x$ gluons of a target proton or nucleus using spinor helicity formalism. We show that scattering from large $x$ gluons of the target results in non-zero spin asymmetry at intermediate $p_t$ as well as rapidity loss of the projectile quark. We comment on how this can also generate angular asymmetries in particle production in high energy collisions.

## Key claim

Scattering of a quark from large \(x\) gluons of a target proton or nucleus generates non-zero spin asymmetry at intermediate transverse momentum and rapidity loss of the projectile quark, effects that vanish in the standard eikonal (small \(x\)) approximation of the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism.

## Method

The author calculates helicity amplitudes for a quark scattering from a target color field composed of both small \(x\) gluons (treated via CGC methods as a classical field summed into Wilson lines) and large \(x\) gluons (treated as single-scattering partons in QCD-improved parton model). Using spinor helicity formalism, the Dirac structures of the four contributing diagrams (one eikonal and three non-eikonal) are evaluated explicitly for positive and negative helicity incoming quarks, and the squared amplitudes are derived. The analysis focuses on the non-eikonal diagrams, which vanish in the small \(x\) limit and involve longitudinal momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

* The eikonal contribution to the helicity amplitude scales as \(\mathcal{N}^{\pm}_{eik} \sim p^+ \sim \sqrt{s}\), where \(p^+\) is the large light-cone momentum of the incoming quark.
* The non-eikonal helicity amplitudes \(\mathcal{N}_2^{+ , b}\) and \(\mathcal{N}_3^{+ , b}\) contain factors proportional to \(1/q_\perp^2\) and \(1/p_{1\perp}^2\), respectively, where \(q_\perp\) and \(p_{1\perp}\) are transverse momenta of the scattered quark.
* The squared amplitude \(|\mathcal{N}_2^{+ , b c}|^2\) includes a term proportional to \((p^+ - q^+)^2 \, q_\perp^2\, A_\perp^b (x) \cdot A_\perp^c (y)\), where \(p^+\) and \(q^+\) are the incoming and outgoing quark light-cone momenta, and \(A_\perp\) is the transverse component of the large \(x\) gluon field.
* The spin asymmetry \(A_{LL}\) is defined as \((d\sigma^{++} - d\sigma^{--}) / (d\sigma^{++} + d\sigma^{--})\), where the imaginary terms proportional to \(i \epsilon^{ij}\) in the squared amplitudes survive for polarized quarks.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid CGC formalism, which treats small \(x\) gluons eikonally and is limited to \(x \le 0.01\), by incorporating large \(x\) gluons of the target as in Jalilian-Marian (2019) and Martin (2019). It enables calculation of spin asymmetries and rapidity loss in quark scattering at intermediate to high transverse momentum, relevant for interpreting data from RHIC and the LHC and for future Electron Ion Collider studies. The approach also opens the way to compute photon-jet and di-jet angular correlations across the full transverse momentum range, unifying small \(x\) CGC results (e.g., \(k_t\)-factorized CGC) with collinearly-factorized pQCD at large \(x\).
