# Exotic hadrons and SU(3) chiral dynamics

> We explore a possibility to generate exotic hadrons dynamically in the scattering of hadrons. The s-wave scattering amplitude of an arbitrary hadron with the Nambu-Goldstone boson is constructed so as to satisfy the unitarity condition and the chiral low energy theorem. We find that the chiral interaction for the exotic channels is in most cases repulsive, and that the strength of the possible attractive interaction is uniquely determined. We show that the attractive interaction in exotic channels is not strong enough to generate a bound state, while the interaction in nonexotic channel generate bound states which are considered to be the origin of some resonances observed in nature.

## Key claim

The leading-order chiral (Weinberg-Tomozawa) interaction in exotic hadron channels is in most cases repulsive, and where it is attractive its strength is uniquely fixed to a value too small to generate a bound state for any target hadron with mass below 6 GeV.

## Method

The authors constructed the s-wave scattering amplitude for an arbitrary hadron scattering with a Nambu-Goldstone boson, imposing both the chiral low-energy theorem (via the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction) and exact two-body unitarity (via the N/D method with one subtraction constant). They then computed the group-theoretical coupling strength C for each exotic channel and searched for poles in the resulting amplitude to determine whether a bound state is generated.

## Key numbers

- The attractive interaction strength for exotic channels is uniquely fixed to C_exotic = 1 (dimensionless group-theoretical factor).
- The critical interaction strength required to generate a bound state is given by C_crit = 2f^2 / [m * (-G(M_T + m))], where f is the NG boson decay constant, m is the NG boson mass, M_T is the target hadron mass, and G is the once-subtracted dispersion integral.
- The attractive interaction C_exotic = 1 is not strong enough to generate a bound state for target hadron masses smaller than 6 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach, previously applied successfully to nonexotic channels by Kaiser (1995), Oset (1998), Oller (2000), and Lutz (2001), to the systematic study of exotic hadrons. The result provides a dynamical explanation for the empirical difficulty in observing exotic hadrons, complementing quark-model approaches, while noting that exotic states generated by other mechanisms (e.g., quark dynamics) are not excluded.
