# Production of charmed baryons in $\bar p p$ collisions close to their thresholds

> Cross sections for the charm-production reactions $\bar p p \to \bar Λ_c^- Σ_c^+$, $\bar Σ_cΣ_c$, $\bar Ξ_cΞ_c$, and $\bar Ξ_c'Ξ_c'$ are presented, for energies near their respective thresholds. The results are based on a calculation performed in the meson-exchange framework in close analogy to earlier studies of the Jülich group on the strangeness-production reactions $\bar p p \to \bar ΛΣ$, $\bar ΣΣ$, $\bar ΞΞ$ by connecting the two sectors via SU(4) flavor symmetry. The cross sections are found to be in the order of $0.1 - 1$ $μb$ at energies of $100$ MeV above the respective thresholds, for all considered channels. Complementary to meson-exchange, where the charmed baryons are produced by the exchange of $D$ and $D^*$ mesons, a charm-production potential derived in a quark model is employed for assessing uncertainties. The cross sections predicted within that picture turned out to be significantly smaller.

## Key claim

Cross sections for charmed-baryon production in antiproton-proton collisions near threshold are predicted to be in the order of 0.1–1 μb at 100 MeV excess energy, but the results depend strongly on the choice of transition mechanism: the meson-exchange model yields significantly larger cross sections than the quark-model approach, with the discrepancy growing for channels with higher thresholds.

## Method

The authors solve a multi-channel Lippmann–Schwinger equation for the reactions \(\bar p p \to \bar\Lambda_c^-\Sigma_c^+\), \(\bar\Sigma_c\Sigma_c\), \(\bar\Xi_c\Xi_c\), and \(\bar\Xi_c'\Xi_c'\), using transition potentials from \(t\)-channel \(D\) and \(D^*\) meson exchange with SU(4) flavor symmetry to relate coupling constants to the strangeness sector. As a complementary approach, they employ a charm-production potential derived from a constituent quark model (the \(^3S_1\) mechanism of Kohno and Weise), with the effective coupling strength reduced by \((m_s/m_c)^2 \approx 1/9\) relative to the strangeness case. The initial \(\bar N N\) interaction is described by four variants (A, A', B, C) of a meson-exchange potential with a phenomenological optical potential for annihilation, fitted to \(\bar p p\) data at laboratory momenta 6–10 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Cross sections for \(\bar p p \to \bar\Lambda_c^-\Sigma_c^+\) at 100 MeV excess energy: 0.60–0.91 μb (meson exchange), 0.02–0.04 μb (quark model).
- Cross sections for \(\bar p p \to \bar\Sigma_c^{--}\Sigma_c^{++}\) at 100 MeV excess energy: 0.91–1.58 μb (meson exchange), 0.002 μb (quark model).
- Cross sections for \(\bar p p \to \bar\Xi_c^-\Xi_c^+\) at 100 MeV excess energy: 0.62–0.87 μb (meson exchange), 0.007–0.010 μb (quark model).
- Cross sections for \(\bar p p \to \bar\Lambda_c^-\Lambda_c^+\) at 100 MeV excess energy: 5.65–8.37 μb (meson exchange), 2.22–3.49 μb (quark model).
- Cross sections for \(\bar p p \to \bar\Xi_c^{\prime -}\Xi_c^{\prime +}\) at 100 MeV excess energy: 0.27–0.39 μb (meson exchange), (0.1–0.2) × 10⁻³ μb (quark model).
- The reduction factor for the quark-model effective coupling strength in the charm sector relative to strangeness is \((m_s/m_c)^2 \approx (550\ \text{MeV} / 1600\ \text{MeV})^2 \approx 1/9\).
- The cutoff mass \(\Lambda\) at the \(NY_cD\) and \(NY_cD^*\) vertices is 3 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Jülich group’s earlier coupled-channel studies of strangeness-production reactions \(\bar p p \to \bar\Lambda\Sigma\), \(\bar\Sigma\Sigma\), \(\bar\Xi\Xi\) (Haidenbauer 1991, 1992, 1993) to the charm sector via SU(4) flavor symmetry, providing the first systematic predictions for \(\bar\Xi_c\Xi_c\) and \(\bar\Xi_c'\Xi_c'\) production. The results enable feasibility assessments for the \(\bar{\text{P}}\)ANDA experiment at FAIR, which will operate with antiproton beam momenta up to 15 GeV/c (center-of-mass energy 5.5 GeV). The predictions are compared with those of Khodjamirian (2012) using QCD light-cone sum rules and with earlier work by Kroll (1988) and Titov and Kämpfer (2008, 2011), highlighting the need for experimental data to discriminate between the meson-exchange and quark-model descriptions.
