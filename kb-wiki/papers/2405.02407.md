# Lepton-neutron interaction and S-wave low energy parameters

> A lepton-neutron potential in configuration space is obtained. It is based on the Coulomb plus hyperfine interaction Hamiltonian integrated over the neutron charge and magnetic densities. Different parametrisations of the neutron electromagnetic form factors are compared. It is given in the operator form with a central, spin-spin, tensor and spin-orbit terms. The potentials for lowest partial waves states are presented. We compute the lepton-neutron lepton ($ln$) low-energy parameters for the S-waves, estimate the zero-energy cross sections for higher angular momentum states, and point out a possible divergence in the partial wave summation due to the spin-orbit potential.

## Key claim

The paper derives a lepton-neutron potential in configuration space from the Coulomb plus hyperfine interaction Hamiltonian integrated over neutron charge and magnetic densities, and computes S-wave low-energy parameters; however, the authors note that for higher angular momentum states the standard low-energy parameters are not defined due to the asymptotic \(1/r^3\) behavior of the tensor and spin-orbit terms, and they point out a possible divergence in the partial wave summation from the spin-orbit potential.

## Method

The authors constructed a lepton-neutron potential by convolving the point-lepton Coulomb and hyperfine interaction Hamiltonians with neutron charge and magnetic densities obtained from Fourier transforms of Sachs form factors (Friar, Kelly, and Atac et al. parametrizations for electric; dipole and Kelly for magnetic). They then solved the coupled radial Schrodinger equations in the \(|SLJ\rangle\) partial wave basis to compute scattering observables for electrons, muons, and taus (and their antiparticles) on neutrons.

## Key numbers

*   Neutron charge mean squared radius: \(\langle r^2_n\rangle = -0.116 \pm 0.002 \; \text{fm}^2\) (experimental value from PDG 2022).
*   Friar form factor gives \(\langle r^2_n\rangle = -0.113 \; \text{fm}^2\); Kelly form factor gives \(\langle r^2_n\rangle = -0.112 \pm 0.003 \; \text{fm}^2\); Atac et al. parametrization gives \(\langle r^2_n\rangle = -0.110 \; \text{fm}^2\).
*   Coulomb potential depth at origin: \(V^C_{ln}(0) \approx -0.266\) MeV (same for all leptons).
*   Electron-neutron magnetic potential prefactor: \(C_{MM}^{en} = 4359.4109\) MeV.
*   Electron-neutron spin-orbit prefactor: \(C_{LS}^{en} = -34853.82\) MeV.
*   \(^1\)S\(_0\) scattering length for \(e^-n\): \(a_0 = 2.926 \times 10^{-3}\) fm (Friar+Dipole); for \(\mu^- n\): \(a_0 = 2.501 \times 10^{-3}\) fm; for \(\tau^- n\): \(a_0 = 1.574 \times 10^{-4}\) fm.
*   \(^1\)S\(_0\) effective range for \(e^-n\): \(r_0 = -149\) fm (Friar+Dipole); for \(\mu^- n\): \(r_0 = -170\) fm; for \(\tau^- n\): \(r_0 = 4814\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Friar (1975) and the electromagnetic corrections to nucleon-nucleon S-wave low-energy parameters from the AV18 potential (1995) by providing a full configuration-space lepton-neutron potential with central, spin-spin, tensor, and spin-orbit terms. It enables future calculations of electronic effects in low-energy neutron scattering on atomic targets, and supports applications in precision atomic spectroscopy (e.g., muonic atom measurements of nuclear charge radii by the CREMA collaboration, 2016 and 2021) and beyond-standard-model searches for new bosons. The paper notes that the spin-orbit potential does not conserve total spin \(S\), unlike standard spin-orbit interactions, and that the \(1/r^3\) tail of the tensor and spin-orbit terms invalidates standard low-energy parameter definitions for higher partial waves, referencing the work of Sommerfeld (1960, 1961) and others on scattering with long-range potentials.
