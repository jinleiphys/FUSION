# Hadronic Lorentz Violation in Chiral Perturbation Theory Including the Coupling to External Fields

> If any violation of Lorentz symmetry exists in the hadron sector, its ultimate origins must lie at the quark level. We continue the analysis of how the theories at these two levels are connected, using chiral perturbation theory. Considering a two-flavor quark theory, with dimension-4 operators that break Lorentz symmetry, we derive a low-energy theory of pions and nucleons that is invariant under local chiral transformations and includes the coupling to external fields. The pure meson and baryon sectors, as well as the couplings between them and the couplings to external electromagnetic and weak gauge fields, contain forms of Lorentz violation which depend on linear combinations of quark-level coefficients. In particular, at leading order the electromagnetic couplings depend on the very same combinations as appear in the free particle propagators. This means that observations of electromagnetic processes involving hadrons--such as vacuum Cerenkov radiation, which may be allowed in Lorentz-violating theories--can only reliably constrain certain particular combinations of quark coefficients.

## Key claim

The paper derives the low-energy hadronic effective theory from quark-level Lorentz-violating operators, showing that at leading order the electromagnetic couplings of pions and nucleons depend on the same linear combinations of quark coefficients as appear in the free particle propagators, which means that observations of electromagnetic processes involving hadrons can only reliably constrain certain particular combinations of quark coefficients.

## Method

The authors start from a two-flavor quark theory with dimension-4 CPT-even Lorentz-violating operators and use chiral perturbation theory to construct a low-energy effective Lagrangian for pions and nucleons that is invariant under local chiral transformations. They promote the global chiral symmetry to a local one by treating the Lorentz-violating coefficients as spurion fields with defined transformation properties, and include couplings to external electromagnetic and weak gauge fields. The effective Lagrangian is constructed at leading chiral order by writing all terms consistent with the local chiral symmetry, parity, and charge conjugation, and reducing them to a minimal set using trace relations and integration by parts.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order mesonic Lagrangian contains a single low-energy constant \(\beta^{(1)}\) multiplying the term \(\frac{F^2}{4} (\CRsing^{\mu\nu} + \CLsing^{\mu\nu}) \tr[(D_\mu U)^\dagger D_\nu U]\)
- The leading-order pion-nucleon Lagrangian contains four dimensionless low-energy constants \(\alpha^{(1)}\), \(\alpha^{(2)}\), \(\alpha^{(3)}\), and \(\alpha^{(4)}\), which are the same as those appearing in the free nucleon sector from previous work (Kamand, 2016)
- The pion decay constant in the chiral limit is denoted \(F\), and the nucleon mass in the chiral limit is \(m_N\)
- The pion mass is related to quark masses by \(M_\pi^2 = B(m_u + m_d)\), where \(B = -\frac{1}{2} \langle \bar{Q} Q \rangle\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Kamand, 2016) which constructed hadronic Lagrangians with global chiral symmetry and no external fields, by promoting to local chiral invariance and including couplings to external electromagnetic and weak gauge fields. The results enable translation of experimental bounds on Lorentz violation in hadronic systems (such as vacuum Cerenkov radiation constraints) into bounds on fundamental quark-level coefficients in the Standard Model Extension (SME), and complement related work using chiral perturbation theory for Lorentz violation (Noordmans, 2015).
