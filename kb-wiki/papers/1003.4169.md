# Chiral Magnetic Effect and QCD Phase Transitions with Effective Models

> We study the influence of the chiral phase transition on the chiral magnetic effect. The chiral electric current density along the magnetic field, the electric charge difference between on each side of the reaction plane, and the azimuthal charged-particle correlations as functions of the temperature during the QCD phase transitions are calculated. It is found that with the decrease of the temperature, the chiral electric current density, the electric charge difference, and the azimuthal charged-particle correlations all get a sudden suppression at the critical temperature of the chiral phase transition, because the large quark constituent mass in the chiral symmetry broken phase quite suppresses the axial anomaly and the chiral magnetic effect. We suggest that the azimuthal charged-particle correlations (including the correlators divided by the total multiplicity of produced charged particles which are used in current experiments and another kind of correlators not divided by the total multiplicity) can be employed to identify the occurrence of the QCD phase transitions in RHIC energy scan experiments.

## Key claim
The chiral magnetic effect (CME) signals, chiral electric current density, electric charge difference across the reaction plane, and azimuthal charged-particle correlations, all experience a sudden suppression at the critical temperature of the chiral phase transition, because the large quark constituent mass in the chirally broken phase suppresses the axial anomaly. This suppression is predicted to occur regardless of the value of the chiral chemical potential, and the authors suggest that azimuthal charged-particle correlations can be used to identify QCD phase transitions in RHIC beam energy scan experiments.

## Method
The authors employed the 2+1 flavor Polyakov–Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model in the mean-field approximation, incorporating a chiral chemical potential μ₅ to study left-right asymmetry and a homogeneous external magnetic field B. They derived the thermodynamic potential and calculated the chiral electric current density j₃, the electric charge difference Δ₊/V between the two sides of the reaction plane, and the azimuthal charged-particle correlations as functions of temperature, using only the lowest Landau level for charge separation contributions.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature of the chiral phase transition: T_C = 185 MeV (for μ₅ = 250 MeV, μ_i = 0).
- Magnetic field strength used in calculations: eB = 10⁴ MeV².
- Chiral chemical potential values used: μ₅ = 150, 200, and 250 MeV.
- PNJL model parameters: m₀ˡ = 5.5 MeV, m₀ˢ = 140.7 MeV, GΛ² = 1.835, KΛ⁵ = 12.36, Λ = 602.3 MeV; fitted to m_π = 135.0 MeV, m_K = 497.7 MeV, m_η′ = 957.8 MeV, f_π = 92.4 MeV.
- Polyakov-loop potential parameters: a₀ = 6.75, a₁ = -1.95, a₂ = 2.625, a₃ = -7.44, b₃ = 0.75, b₄ = 7.5; T₀ = 270 MeV.
- In the high-temperature massless limit, j₃ = e²Bμ₅/π² and Δ₊/V = eBμ₅/(2π²) for the three-flavor system with μ_i = 0.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier study (Fu 2010) by providing detailed calculations of how the chiral magnetic effect is influenced by the chiral phase transition, using the PNJL model which has been validated against lattice QCD data (Ratti 2006, Röner 2007, Ghosh 2006, Fu 2008, Fu 2009). The results contradict the naive expectation that the CME current is temperature-independent, showing instead that the finite quark mass in the broken phase suppresses the effect. This enables experimental identification of the QCD phase transition via azimuthal charged-particle correlations in RHIC energy scan experiments, building on the charge separation measurements reported by the STAR Collaboration (Abelev 2009a, Abelev 2009b).
