# The Superfluid and Conformal Phase Transitions of Two-Color QCD

> The phase structure of two-color QCD is examined as a function of the chemical potential and the number of light quark flavors. We consider effective Lagrangians for two-color QCD containing the Goldstone excitations, spin-one particles and negative intrinsic parity terms. We discuss the possibility of a conformal phase transition and the enhancement of the global symmetries as the number of flavors is increased. The effects of a quark chemical potential on the spin-one particles and on the negative intrinsic parity terms are analyzed. It is shown that the phase diagram that is predicted by the linearly realized effective Lagrangian at tree-level matches exactly that predicted by chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for two-color QCD, the phase diagram predicted by a linearly realized effective Lagrangian at tree-level exactly matches that predicted by chiral perturbation theory, but this agreement assumes the vector spectrum is heavy compared to the pseudo-Goldstone excitations, an assumption that should break down near the critical number of flavors (approximately \(N_f \simeq 8\)).

## Method

The authors constructed the most general effective Lagrangian for two-color QCD containing Goldstone bosons, spin-one vector fields, and negative intrinsic parity (epsilon) terms, using both nonlinear and linear realizations of chiral symmetry. They introduced a baryon chemical potential via a covariant derivative and determined the vacuum structure by minimizing the static potential with respect to a variational parameter \(\alpha\) that interpolates between chiral and diquark condensates. The vector mass gaps and dispersion relations were calculated, and the enhanced global symmetry scenario was identified by setting the effective Lagrangian parameters to \(s=4\) and \(h=2\).

## Key numbers

* The critical chemical potential for the onset of diquark condensation is \(\mu_B = m_\pi\); for \(\mu_B \geq m_\pi\), the vacuum angle satisfies \(\cos(\alpha) = m_\pi^2 / \mu_B^2\).
* The baryon density is \(n_B = 8 N_f v^2 \mu_B \sin^2\alpha\).
* The chiral condensate is \(\langle \bar{\psi}\psi \rangle = 2 N_f G \cos\alpha\) and the diquark condensate is \(\langle \psi\psi \rangle = 2 N_f G \sin\alpha\), where \(G\) is the chiral condensate in the chiral limit.
* The one-loop beta function for two-color QCD changes sign at \(N_f > 11\), and the critical number of flavors for the conformal phase transition is estimated to be about \(N_f \simeq 8\).
* The enhanced global symmetry occurs for \(s=4\) and \(h=2\), yielding degenerate vector masses \(M_S^2 = g^2(m_V^2 - 2v^2) = M_X^2\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory analysis of two-color QCD at nonzero chemical potential by Kogut, Stephanov, Toublan, Verbaarschot, and Zhitnitsky (KSTVZ) to include spin-one particles and negative parity terms. It also builds on the enhanced global symmetry scenario proposed by Appelquist, Ratnaweera, Sannino, and collaborators (ARS, AS) for gauge theories near the conformal phase transition. The results enable lattice simulations to test whether deviations from chiral perturbation theory appear for \(N_f \simeq 8\), where the vector spectrum becomes light and the conformal phase transition may suppress diquark condensation.
