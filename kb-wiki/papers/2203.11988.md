# Some Conceptual Aspects of Operator Design for Quantum Simulations of Non-Abelian Lattice Gauge Theories

> In the Kogut-Susskind formulation of lattice gauge theories, a set of quantum numbers resides at the ends of each link to characterize the vertex-local gauge field. We discuss the role of these quantum numbers in propagating correlations and supporting entanglement that ensures each vertex remains gauge invariant, despite time evolution induced by operators with (only) partial access to each vertex Hilbert space. Applied to recent proposals for eliminating vertex-local Hilbert spaces in quantum simulation, we describe how the required entanglement is generated via delocalization of the time evolution operator with nearest-neighbor controls. These hybridizations, organized with qudits or qubits, exchange classical operator preprocessing for reductions in quantum resource requirements that extend throughout the lattice volume.

## Key claim

The paper argues that vertex-local quantum numbers (color-isospin and hypercharge) in the Kogut-Susskind formulation provide an entangled fabric that protects Gauss's law, and that these degrees of freedom can be removed via classical preprocessing of plaquette operators, trading reduced quantum resource requirements for delocalized nearest-neighbor controls, but only if the resulting operator design is organized with qudits or qubits and the classical preprocessing is performed once per operator.

## Method

The authors analyze the role of Clebsch-Gordan (CG) sums in maintaining gauge invariance when plaquette operators act on only two-link subspaces of a vertex, showing how destructive interference among projection quantum numbers prevents gauge-violating transitions. They then describe a hybrid approach where vertex-local quantum numbers are integrated out of the Hilbert space, and the required CG correlations are instead encoded into the matrix elements of the plaquette operator, which is extended to include controls on nearest-neighbor links in all spatial dimensions. The resulting time evolution operator is decomposed into control sectors and implemented via Givens rotations, with gauge invariance preserved through Trotterization.

## Key numbers

- At truncation \(\Lambda = 1\) (including irreps \(\{\mathbf{1}, \mathbf{3}, \bar{\mathbf{3}}, \mathbf{8}\}\)), the irrep quantum numbers require 2 qubits per link while the vertex-local quantum numbers require 16 qubits per link.
- The qubit count per link scales as \(N_q^{\text{(link)}} = 2 \lceil \log_2 (1+\Lambda) \rceil + 2 \sum_{j=1}^3 \lceil \log_2(1 + 2j\Lambda) \rceil\), with the local quantum number registers exceeding the irrep registers by a multiplicative factor scaling roughly as \(\sim 3 + \log_\Lambda(48)\).
- The number of Givens rotations per plaquette operator scales polynomially as \(\mathcal{O}(\Lambda^{16})\) with the field truncation.
- Vertex CG coefficients for the \(\mathbf{6}\)-\(\mathbf{6}\)-\(\mathbf{8}\) configuration are: \(\mathcal{N}_{\bar{\mathbf{3}},\bar{\mathbf{3}}} = 1/2\), \(\mathcal{N}_{\mathbf{6}, \mathbf{6}} = \sqrt{10}/4\), \(\mathcal{N}_{\bar{\mathbf{3}}, \mathbf{6}} = \sqrt{3}/2\), \(\mathcal{N}_{\mathbf{6}, \bar{\mathbf{3}}} = \sqrt{3}/(2\sqrt{2})\).

## Context

This work extends the hybrid multiplet basis approach previously developed for SU(2) by Banuls (2017) and Klco (2019) to SU(3) lattice gauge theories, building on the scalability analysis of Ciavarella (2021) for truncated SU(3) dynamics on IBM quantum devices. The method is intermediate between the full link-based representation of Byrnes (2005), which places all gauge field information at the link, and the loop-string-hadron formulation of Raychowdhury (2018, 2019), which captures the field through gauge-invariant operators local to each vertex. The paper enables practical quantum simulations of non-Abelian gauge theories with reduced qubit requirements while retaining a built-in mechanism for detecting local errors via post-selection into the gauge-invariant subspace.
