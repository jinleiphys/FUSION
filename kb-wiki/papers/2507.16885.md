# Angular correlations in neutron-gamma reactions

> Angular correlations for low-energy neutron induced $γ$ reactions are calculated using nuclear reaction formalism for multi level neutron capture with arbitrary multiplicities of $γ$ transitions. We confirm previous calculations [V. V. Flambaum and O. P. Sushkov, Nucl. Phys.A 435 , 352 (1985)] for dipole transitions obtained with different formalism. The possible applications of n-$γ$ correlations for studies of symmetry violations and electromagnetic transitions are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents an independent calculation of angular correlations for low-energy neutron-induced gamma reactions using nuclear reaction formalism, confirming the earlier results of Flambaum and Sushkov (1985) for dipole transitions but noting that the sign of terms involving interference between electric and magnetic transitions depends on an arbitrary phase choice in the transformation between massive spin-1 particles and photons, which has been a source of confusion in the literature.

## Method

The authors derived a general expression for statistical tensors of outgoing spin-1 massive particles using the reaction matrix formalism with Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, Racah coefficients, and 9j symbols. They then transformed this expression to the case of gamma rays by applying a transformation function that converts the channel-spin representation to the multipole representation for electromagnetic transitions, explicitly addressing the sign ambiguity in the transformation that arises from the curl relation between electric and magnetic multipole fields. The final expression for neutron-gamma angular correlations was obtained by summing over channel spins and using the identity relating Clebsch-Gordan coefficients to Racah and 9j symbols.

## Key numbers

* The parity violating asymmetry in the 0.734 eV p-wave resonance in La-139 was measured to be (9.56 plus or minus 0.35) times 10 to the power of minus 2, cited as an example of observed enhancement in parity violating effects.
* The discovery potential for time reversal invariance violating experiments is estimated to be 10 squared to 10 to the power of 4, more sensitive than current limits due to enhancement near neutron p-wave resonances.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Flambaum and Sushkov (1985) for angular correlations in neutron-gamma reactions, providing an independent calculation using nuclear reaction theory rather than the alternative formalism used previously. The paper addresses inconsistencies and confusions in the literature, particularly regarding the sign of interference terms between electric and magnetic transitions, which had led to discrepancies between calculations and measurements of left-right and forward-backward asymmetries in radiative neutron capture on Sn-117 at the 1.33 eV p-wave resonance, as discussed by Alfimenkov (1984) and later by Skoy (1991) and Barabanov (1992). The results enable more reliable extraction of neutron partial widths from ongoing and future experiments studying neutron resonances, which are needed to determine the spin structure in the relation of parity violating and time reversal invariance violating effects in neutron scattering.
