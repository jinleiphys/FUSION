# Normalization of scattering states and Levinson's theorem: reply to a Comment by R. G. Newton

> Recently R. G. Newton published a comment criticizing the methods and the results of a paper published by the author. His criticism touches on a few key points of the subject and hence deserves a detailed reply. Here is the reply, point by point, to his criticism.

# Normalization of scattering states and Levinson's theorem: reply to a Comment by R. G. Newton

## Key claim

The paper defends the author's previous derivation of a modified normalization condition for scattering states that includes a finite correction term at k = k', arguing that the Dirac delta function in quantum theory must be understood as a particular limiting process rather than a strict mathematical distribution, and that the previously reported possibility of a q = 1/4 fractional contribution to Levinson's theorem in the presence of a threshold resonance is ruled out by an overlooked constraint sin[2η_l(0)] = 0.

## Method

The author responds point by point to Newton's criticism by re-deriving the normalization integral for scattering states from the exact identity involving the radial wave function and its derivative at a finite cutoff radius R, then taking the limit R → ∞. For the threshold resonance case, the author examines the asymptotic behavior of the k = 0 solution and shows that the derivation of the modified normalization integral implies a constraint sin[2η_l(0)] = 0 that eliminates the q = 1/4 solution from Levinson's theorem. The high-energy behavior of phase shifts is defended by noting that the equations used are local in k and singularities at finite k do not affect the k → ∞ limit.

## Key numbers

- For the Schrodinger equation without threshold resonance: η_l(0) = n_l π, where n_l is the number of bound states.
- For the Dirac equation without threshold resonance: η_{mκ}(0) + η_{-m,κ}(0) = (N_κ^+ + N_κ^-)π, where N_κ^+ and N_κ^- are numbers of positive and negative energy bound states.
- For the Schrodinger equation with a threshold resonance: η_0(0) = n_0 π + (π/2) sin^2 η_0(0), with the additional constraint sin[2η_l(0)] = 0.
- For the Dirac equation with a threshold resonance: η_{±mκ}(0) = n_κ^± π + (π/2) sin^2 η_{±mκ}(0), with the analogous constraint sin[2η_{±mκ}(0)] = 0.
- The previously reported possible values for q in η_l(0) = (n_l + q)π were q = 0, q = 1/4, or q = 1/2; the constraint rules out q = 1/4.

## Context

This paper is a direct reply to Newton's comment criticizing Poliatzky's earlier work (Poliatzky, 1994) on the normalization of scattering states and Levinson's theorem. The author extends the discussion to the case of a threshold resonance (half-bound state or zero-energy solution) for both the Schrodinger and Dirac equations, correcting the earlier claim that q = 1/4 was possible. The constraint that rules out q = 1/4 was independently confirmed by Sassoli de Bianchi (year not given) in one spatial dimension. The derivation of the modified Levinson theorem for the threshold resonance case was first given by Ni (year not given), who did not check for all solutions. The work enables a complete classification of Levinson's theorem for both Schrodinger and Dirac equations with and without threshold resonances, with the constraint sin[2η_l(0)] = 0 providing the necessary consistency condition.
