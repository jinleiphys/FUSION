# MC-EKRT: Monte Carlo event generator with saturated minijet production for initializing 3+1 D fluid dynamics in high energy nuclear collisions

> We present a novel Monte-Carlo implementation of the EKRT model, MC-EKRT, for computing partonic initial states in high-energy nuclear collisions. Our new MC-EKRT event generator is based on collinearly factorized, dynamically fluctuating pQCD minijet production, supplemented with a saturation conjecture that controls the low-$p_T$ particle production. Previously, the EKRT model has been very successful in describing low-$p_T$ observables at mid-rapidity in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC and RHIC energies. As novel features, our new MC implementation gives a full 3-dimensional initial state event-by-event, includes dynamical minijet-multiplicity fluctuations in the saturation and particle production, introduces a new type of spatially dependent nuclear parton distribution functions, and accounts for the conservation of energy/momentum and valence-quark number. In this proof-of-principle study, we average a large set of event-by-event MC-EKRT initial conditions and compute the rapidity and centrality dependence of the charged hadron multiplicities and elliptic flow for the LHC Pb+Pb and RHIC Au+Au collisions using 3+1 D viscous fluid-dynamical evolution. Also event-by-event fluctuations and decorrelations of initial eccentricities are studied. The good agreement with the rapidity-dependent data suggests that the same saturation mechanism that has been very successful in explaining the mid-rapidity observables, works well also at larger rapidities.

## Key claim

The paper presents a proof-of-principle study showing that the new MC-EKRT event generator, which includes dynamical minijet-multiplicity fluctuations and spatially dependent nuclear parton distribution functions, produces charged hadron multiplicities and elliptic flow in good agreement with rapidity-dependent data from LHC Pb+Pb and RHIC Au+Au collisions, suggesting the same saturation mechanism that works at mid-rapidity also works at larger rapidities.

## Method

The authors implement a Monte Carlo event generator that samples nucleon positions from Woods-Saxon distributions, generates multiple dijet candidates from each nucleon-nucleon pair using Poissonian statistics and LO pQCD cross sections with spatially dependent nuclear PDFs, then filters excess dijets by imposing a local geometric EKRT saturation criterion and conservation of energy and valence quark number. The surviving minijets are propagated as free particles to a proper time surface and converted into energy density via Gaussian smearing to initialize 3+1 D shear-viscous fluid dynamics, with averaged initial states used for each centrality class in this study.

- Nuclear radius R_A = 6.624 fm and diffusion parameter d = 0.550 fm for Pb-208; R_A = 6.380 fm and d = 0.535 fm for Au-197
- Minimum nucleon separation d_min = 0.4 fm used in sampling nucleon configurations

## Context

This work extends the EKRT model (Eskola, Kajantie, Ruuskanen, Tuominen, 1999-2000) and its event-by-event version (Niemi et al., 2015) by providing a full 3-dimensional initial state with dynamical fluctuations, addressing shortcomings of the earlier EbyE-EKRT approach that only had geometrical fluctuations from nucleon positions. The new MC-EKRT generator enables future global analyses of rapidity-dependent observables and studies of smaller collision systems, and the authors note that the Bayesian global analysis by Bernhard et al. (2019) confirmed that initial density profiles from the earlier EbyE-EKRT and IP-Sat models (Schenke et al., 2012) gave the best match with Bayesian inference results.
