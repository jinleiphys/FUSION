# Quasideuteron states with deformed core

> The M1 transitions between low-lying T=1 and T=0 states in deformed odd-odd N=Z nuclei are analyzed in the frames of the rotor-plus-particle model. Using the representation of an explicit coupling of angular momenta we show that strong coupling of the quasideuteron configurations to the axially deformed core results in a distribution of the total 0+ --> 1+ strength among a few low-lying 1+ states. Simple analytical formulae for B(M1) values are derived. The realization of the M1 sum rule for the low-lying 1+,T=0 states is indicated. The calculated B(M1) values are found to be in good agreement with experimental data and reveal specific features of collectivity in odd-odd N=Z nuclei.

# Quasideuteron states with deformed core

## Key claim

The paper shows that in deformed odd-odd N=Z nuclei, the strong coupling of quasideuteron configurations to an axially deformed core fragments the total M1 transition strength from the lowest 0+, T=1 state among several low-lying 1+, T=0 states, in contrast to the spherical case where the strength concentrates in a single transition. The authors derive analytical B(M1) formulae and demonstrate that the calculated values agree well with experimental data, but note that the agreement is worse for K=1 states than for K=0 states, indicating larger admixtures of other configurations.

## Method

The authors employ a rotor-plus-particle model with an axially deformed even-even core and one proton plus one neutron outside, neglecting Coriolis interaction and residual proton-neutron interaction. They transform the wave functions into a representation of explicit angular momentum coupling, allowing them to work with spherical shell model configurations and derive analytical expressions for B(M1) values. The Nilsson single-particle wave functions are expanded in a spherical basis using projection coefficients, and the M1 transition operator is separated into core and valence particle contributions, with the core contributing zero for ΔT=1 transitions.

## Context

This work extends the spherical shell model quasideuteron formalism of Lisetskiy (1999) to axially deformed mean fields, explaining the fragmentation of M1 strength observed in deformed odd-odd N=Z nuclei such as 10B, 14N, 22Na, and 26Al, where the total M1 strength is distributed among two or three low-lying states rather than concentrated in a single transition as in 6Li, 18F, and 42Sc. The rotor-plus-quasideuteron model reproduces the Ω²-dependence of B(M1) values for ΔT=1, ΔK=0 transitions and demonstrates a sum rule for low-lying 1+ states analogous to that noted by Zamick and Auerbach (1999) for 12C. The results enable predictions for heavier proton-rich nuclei up to 100Sn and suggest that forbidden isovector M1 transitions between K=0, T=1 and K=2Ω, T=0 bands can serve as indicators of K quantum number goodness in odd-odd N=Z nuclei.
