# Coulomb energy difference as a probe of isospin-symmetry breaking in the upper fp-shell nuclei

> The anomaly in Coulomb energy differences (CED) between the isospin T=1 states in the odd-odd N=Z nucleus 70Br and the analogue states in its even-even partner 70Se has remained a puzzle. This is a direct manifestation of isospin-symmetry breaking in effective nuclear interactions. Here, we perform large-scale shell-model calculations for nuclei with A=66-78 using the new filter diagonalization method based on the Sakurai-Sugiura algorithm. The calculations reproduce well the experimental CED. The observed negative CED for A=70 are accounted for by the cross-shell neutron excitations from the fp-shell to the g9/2 intruder orbit with the enhanced electromagnetic spin-orbit contribution at this special nucleon number.

## Key claim

The anomalous negative Coulomb energy difference (CED) observed for the A=70 pair ⁷⁰Br/⁷⁰Se is explained by enhanced neutron excitations from the fp-shell to the g₉/₂ intruder orbit combined with the electromagnetic spin-orbit contribution, rather than by shape differences or the Thomas-Ehrman effect as previously suggested.

## Method

Large-scale shell-model calculations were performed in the pf₅/₂g₉/₂ model space using the JUN45 effective interaction based on the Bonn-C potential. The filter diagonalization method based on the Sakurai-Sugiura algorithm was employed to overcome the difficulty of obtaining T=1 states in odd-odd N=Z nuclei, which is impossible with the standard Lanczos method due to numerous T=0 states at lower energies. The Coulomb interaction was separated into a multipole term V_CM, a single-particle correction ε_ll, and an electromagnetic spin-orbit term ε_ls, with the radial term V_Cr omitted as it is not important in this mass region.

## Key numbers

- The proton g₉/₂ orbit is lowered by approximately 66 keV and the f₅/₂ orbit raised by approximately 66 keV due to the spin-orbit term, decreasing the proton gap by roughly 132 keV
- The neutron g₉/₂ orbit is raised by approximately 55 keV and the f₅/₂ orbit lowered by approximately 55 keV, enhancing the neutron shell gap by roughly 110 keV
- The total difference between neutron and proton orbit shifts is about 242 keV
- Estimated spin-orbit components from occupation number differences: -33.4 keV for J=2, -58.8 keV for J=4, and -77.2 keV for J=6 in the ⁷⁰Br/⁷⁰Se pair
- Calculated B(E2) values for ⁷⁰Se: 345 e²fm⁴ (2₁⁺), 539 e²fm⁴ (4₁⁺), and 594 e²fm⁴ (6₁⁺), agreeing with experimental values 342(19), 370(24), and 530(96) e²fm⁴
- Calculated quadrupole moments for ⁷⁰Se: 37.5 efm² (2₁⁺), 50.2 efm² (4₁⁺), and 55.5 efm² (6₁⁺), corresponding to oblate deformations of -0.21, -0.22, and -0.22

## Context

This work extends previous studies of mirror energy differences (MED) in the A~60 mass region by Ekman (2005) and Andersson (2005), and the authors' own earlier work on MED in ⁶⁷Se/⁶⁷As (Kaneko, 2010). It contradicts earlier suggestions by Nara Singh (2007) that the CED anomaly could be explained by shape-dependent Coulomb energy variations, and by de Angelis (2001) attributing it to the Thomas-Ehrman effect. The calculations enable future studies of isospin-symmetry breaking in heavier N=Z nuclei and demonstrate the filter diagonalization method as a practical tool for accessing T=1 states in odd-odd nuclei.
