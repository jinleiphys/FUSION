# Extensivity of Irreversible Current and Stability in Causal Dissipative Hydrodynamics

> We extended our formulation of causal dissipative hydrodynamics [T. Koide \textit{et al.}, Phys. Rev. \textbf{C75}, 034909 (2007)] to be applicable to the ultra-relativistic regime by considering the extensiveness of irreversible currents. The new equation has a non-linear term which suppresses the effect of viscosity. We found that such a term is necessary to guarantee the positive definiteness of the inertia term and stabilize numerical calculations in ultra-relativistic initial conditions. Because of the suppression of the viscosity, the behavior of the fluid is more close to that of the ideal fluid. Our result is essentially same as that from the extended irreversible thermodynamics, but is different from the Israel-Stewart theory. A possible origin of the difference is discussed.

# Extensivity of Irreversible Current and Stability in Causal Dissipative Hydrodynamics

## Key claim

The authors extend their previous formulation of causal dissipative hydrodynamics (Koide et al., 2007) to the ultra-relativistic regime by applying memory effects to the extensive (integrated) irreversible current rather than to densities, which introduces a nonlinear term that suppresses viscosity and guarantees positive definiteness of the inertia term; this result is essentially the same as extended irreversible thermodynamics but differs from the Israel-Stewart theory.

## Method

The authors rederive the causal dissipative hydrodynamic equations for bulk viscosity in 1+1 dimensions by introducing a memory function applied to the extensive measure of the irreversible current (integrated over a finite fluid cell volume) rather than to densities. This yields a nonlinear differential equation for the bulk viscosity that includes an additional term proportional to the product of the relaxation time and the bulk viscosity multiplied by the divergence of the four-velocity. The resulting nonlinear causal dissipative hydrodynamics (NLCDH) is solved numerically using the Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics (SPH) method for shock formation, Landau initial condition expansion, and nonperiodic oscillation scenarios.

## Key numbers

- The minimum bulk viscosity in NLCDH is bounded from below by $\Pi_{\mathrm{min}} = -\zeta / \tau_{\mathrm{R}}$, where $\zeta$ is the bulk viscosity coefficient and $\tau_{\mathrm{R}}$ is the relaxation time.
- For the scaling solution of the Bjorken model, the initial condition uses energy density $\varepsilon(\tau_0) = 1~\mathrm{GeV/fm}^3$ and $\Pi(\tau_0) = 0$ at initial proper time $\tau_0 = 0.1$ fm, with massless ideal gas equation of state $\alpha = 1/3$ and parameters $b = 6$, $a = 1$.
- The propagation speed of sound in NLCDH is $v_{\mathrm{c}} = \sqrt{1/b + \alpha}$, and causality requires $b < 1/(1-\alpha)$, which for $\alpha = 1/3$ gives $b < 3/2$.
- For the Landau initial condition, the initial temperature is 590 MeV and the initial size is 0.7 fm, with parameters $a = 0.1$ and $b = 6$.
- In the shock formation simulation, LCDH becomes unstable at $t = 0.75$ fm for initial trigger velocity $\gamma = 5$ with $a = 1$, while NLCDH remains stable under the same conditions.
- The coarse-graining scale for the SPH kernel is taken as $h \simeq 0.1$ fm, corresponding to the mean-free path of partons in QGP.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous formulation of causal dissipative hydrodynamics (Koide et al., 2007) by incorporating the finite size of fluid cells, which was previously neglected. The resulting NLCDH differs from the truncated Israel-Stewart theory by the presence of a nonlinear term that suppresses viscosity and stabilizes numerical calculations in ultra-relativistic regimes. The approach is related to extended irreversible thermodynamics (Jou) and enables stable numerical simulations of relativistic heavy-ion collisions at ultra-relativistic energies, including shock formation and expansion to vacuum, while also showing that nonperiodic oscillations (similar to turbulence) persist in the new scheme.
