# Spin dependence of the pbar-d and pbar-3He interactions

> Elastic scattering of antiprotons on deuteron and 3He targets is studied within the Glauber-Sitenko theory. In case of pbar-d scattering, the single- and double pbar-N scattering mechanisms and the full spin dependence of the elementary pbar-N scattering amplitudes are taken into account on the basis of an appropriately modified formalism developed for p-d scattering. Differential cross sections and analyzing powers are calculated for antiproton beam energies between 50 and 300 MeV, using the Nbar-N model of the Juelich group as input. Results for total polarized cross sections are obtained via the optical theorem. The efficiency of the polarization buildup for antiprotons in a storage ring is discussed.

# Spin dependence of the pbar-d and pbar-3He interactions

## Key claim

The Glauber-Sitenko theory, including single and double scattering mechanisms with full spin dependence of elementary antiproton-nucleon amplitudes, can provide reliable predictions for antiproton-deuteron elastic scattering observables only up to center-of-mass angles of 50-60 degrees, where the sensitivity to the backward-angle behavior of the elementary amplitudes is minimal.

## Method

The authors adapted the Glauber-Sitenko formalism originally developed for proton-deuteron scattering by Platonova and Kukulin, modifying it for antiproton-deuteron and antiproton-3He elastic scattering. They included single and double scattering mechanisms, the S- and D-wave components of the deuteron wave function, and the full spin structure of the elementary antiproton-nucleon scattering amplitude, using two different antinucleon-nucleon models (A and D) from the Juelich group as input for beam energies between 50 and 300 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross section for pbar-d scattering at 179 MeV shows first minimum at q^2 approximately 0.12-0.13 (GeV/c)^2, corresponding to theta_c.m. approximately 55 degrees
- Reliable predictions from Glauber-Sitenko approach obtained for theta_c.m. up to 50-60 degrees in the pbar-d system for energies 50-300 MeV
- Tensor analyzing powers A_xx and A_yy reduced by one order of magnitude when deuteron D-wave is omitted

## Context

This work extends earlier single-scattering approximation studies by the same authors for pbar-d (Haidenbauer and Uzikov, 2009) and pbar-3He (Haidenbauer and Uzikov, 2010) by including double scattering effects and full spin observables. It builds on the Glauber-Sitenko formalism successfully applied to proton-deuteron scattering by Platonova and Kukulin. The results are relevant for the PAX collaboration's planned test experiment at the CERN AD ring for spin-filtering with antiprotons, and the calculated spin observables could discriminate between different antinucleon-nucleon interaction models once experimental data become available.
