# Chaotic Scattering in Heavy--Ion Reactions

> We discuss the relevance of chaotic scattering in heavy--ion reactions at energies around the Coulomb barrier. A model in two and three dimensions which takes into account rotational degrees of freedom is discussed both classically and quantum-mechanically. The typical chaotic features found in this description of heavy-ion collisions are connected with the anomalous behaviour of several experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that chaotic scattering is a ubiquitous phenomenon in heavy-ion reactions at energies around the Coulomb barrier, but only when the two characteristic time scales (relative motion and internal rotational motion) are comparable; for heavier systems where the Coulomb repulsion is stronger and the moment of inertia is larger, the motion remains regular.

## Method

The authors model the scattering between a spherical and a deformed nucleus using a Hamiltonian with rotational degrees of freedom, first in two dimensions (3 degrees of freedom: radial distance, rotor orientation, and azimuthal angle) and then in three dimensions (5 degrees of freedom). They solve the classical equations of motion numerically using the proximity potential for the nuclear interaction, and for the quantum treatment they solve coupled-channels Schrodinger equations. The fractal dimension of the repeller is calculated using both the sandbox method and the uncertainty dimension method, and the escape rate is extracted from exponential fits of the reaction time probability distributions.

## Key numbers

- For the system $^{28}Si + ^{24}Mg$, the Coulomb barrier is approximately $V_B \sim 26.5$ MeV; chaotic scattering appears just above this barrier.
- The fractal dimension for planar scattering of $^{28}Si + ^{24}Mg$ at E=25 MeV and L=0 is D=0.73 (sandbox method) and D=0.84 (uncertainty dimension method).
- The escape rate $\Gamma$ for the same system ranges between values reported in tables 2 and 3 (exact numerical values not fully extractable from truncated text).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of chaotic scattering in simple Hamiltonian systems (Eckhardt 1988, Jung 1987, Blümel 1988, Gaspard 1989, Bleher 1990, Smilansky 1991) to realistic nuclear potentials with actual nuclear physics units, using the proximity potential of Blocki (1977) and the nuclear structure parameters of Bohr and Mottelson (1978). The paper connects classical chaotic scattering features to the anomalous behaviour of experimental heavy-ion data, and shows that the quantum coupled-channels calculations produce S-matrix fluctuations reminiscent of Ericson's fluctuations (Ericson 1960, 1963, 1966) from compound nucleus theory. The results enable future studies of the interplay between hyperbolic and nonhyperbolic chaotic features in realistic nuclear systems, and suggest that chaotic scattering should be expected for light heavy-ions (atomic mass number A between 4 and 60) but not for heavier systems.
