# Vector-like Leptons in Light of the Cabibbo-Angle Anomaly

> The first row of the Cabibbo-Cobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix shows a discrepancy of $\sim $$3\,σ$ with unitarity, known as the "Cabibbo Angle Anomaly" (CAA). This tension can be explained via modified $W$ couplings to leptons, but in order to consistently assess the validity of this solution, a global fit including the electroweak (EW) precision constraints and the low energy tests of lepton flavour universality (LFU) is necessary. Performing such a fit for gauge-invariant dimension-6 operators, we find that even when assuming LFU, including the CKM elements $V_{us}$ and $V_{ud}$ into the electroweak fit has a relevant impact, shifting the best fit point significantly. Vector-like leptons (VLLs) are prime candidates for a corresponding UV completion since they can affect the $W$ and $Z$ couplings to leptons already at tree-level. Studying each pattern of new physics (NP) given by the six possible representations of VLLs (under the SM gauge group) we find that any single representation describes experimental data at most slighlty better than the SM hypothesis. However, allowing for several representations of VLLs at the same time, we find a simple scenario consisting of a singlet, $N$, coupling to electrons and a triplet, $Σ_1$, coupling to muons which not only explains the CAA but also improves the electroweak fit in such a way that its best fit point is preferred by more than $4\,σ$ with respect to the SM.

## Key claim

The Cabibbo Angle Anomaly (CAA), a roughly 3 sigma discrepancy from first-row CKM unitarity, can be explained by modified W boson couplings to leptons, but a global electroweak fit including CKM elements V_us and V_ud shifts the best fit point significantly even when lepton flavour universality is assumed. While no single vector-like lepton representation fits data much better than the Standard Model, a combination of a singlet N coupling to electrons and a triplet Sigma_1 coupling to muons yields a best fit point preferred by more than 4 sigma over the Standard Model.

## Method

The author performed a global fit of gauge-invariant dimension-6 operators that modify W and Z boson couplings to leptons, including electroweak precision constraints and low-energy tests of lepton flavour universality. For the ultraviolet completion, the six possible representations of vector-like leptons under the SM gauge group were studied individually and in combination, integrating them out at tree-level to obtain the Wilson coefficients for the effective operators.

## Key numbers

- The first row of the CKM matrix shows a discrepancy of approximately 3 sigma with unitarity (the Cabibbo Angle Anomaly)
- The combined scenario of a singlet N coupling to electrons and a triplet Sigma_1 coupling to muons is preferred by more than 4 sigma with respect to the Standard Model
- The Higgs vacuum expectation value used is v approximately 246 GeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Coutinho, Crivellin and Manzari (2020) which showed that modified gauge boson couplings to neutrinos can solve the CAA while satisfying electroweak precision bounds. It also connects to studies of vector-like leptons in the context of the muon anomalous magnetic moment (g-2) by Czarnecki and Marciano (2001) and others, as well as to B-physics anomalies in b to s l+ l- transitions. The results enable further exploration of correlations between the CAA, lepton flavour violation, and other flavour anomalies, and provide a concrete UV-complete framework for future collider searches.
