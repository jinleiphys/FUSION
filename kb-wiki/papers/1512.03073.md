# Spectra and elliptic flow of charmed hadrons in HYDJET++ model

> Heavy-flavour quarks are predominantly produced in hard scatterings on a short time-scale and traverse the medium interacting with its constituents, thus they are one of the effective probes of the transport properties of the medium formed in relativistic heavy ion collisions. On the other hand, the thermal production of heavy-flavour quarks in quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is itself of interest. In this report, the production and elliptic flow of the prompt charmed mesons $D^0$, $D^+$, $D^{*+}$ and $J/ψ$ in PbPb collisions at the center-of-mass energy 2.76 TeV per nucleon pair are described in the framework of two-component HYDJET++ model. The model combines thermal and pQCD production mechanisms. The spectra and elliptic flow of charmed mesons are presented, the results are compared with LHC data.

## Key claim
The HYDJET++ model, combining thermal (statistical coalescence) and pQCD hard-scattering charm production, can simultaneously reproduce the transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flow of both open-charm D mesons and hidden-charm J/ψ mesons in PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV, but only if J/ψ mesons are assumed to undergo an early thermal freeze-out at chemical freeze-out temperature rather than at the kinetic freeze-out temperature used for light hadrons and D mesons.

## Method
The two-component HYDJET++ model was used: a soft hydro-type component with statistical hadronization including a charm enhancement factor (fugacity γc), and a hard pQCD component using PYTHIA for initial parton production and PYQUEN for parton energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma. The charm fugacity γc = 11.5 was fixed from the J/ψ yield, and the model parameters for soft and hard components were previously tuned to inclusive hadron data at RHIC and LHC. For J/ψ mesons, an early thermal freeze-out scenario was adopted where freeze-out occurs at the chemical freeze-out temperature with reduced collective velocities, while D mesons used the same freeze-out parameters as inclusive hadrons.

## Key numbers
- Charm enhancement factor (fugacity) γc = 11.5, fixed from J/ψ yield in 20% most central PbPb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV
- J/ψ pT spectrum in 0-20% central PbPb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV is reproduced up to pT ∼ 3 GeV/c using early thermal freeze-out scenario
- D-meson pT spectra (D0, D±, D*±) in 0-20% central PbPb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV are reproduced using the same freeze-out parameters as for inclusive hadrons
- Elliptic flow v2(pT) of J/ψ mesons for 20-40% centrality and D0 mesons for 10-30% centrality at √sNN = 2.76 TeV agree with ALICE data within experimental uncertainties
- Significant part of D-mesons up to pT ∼ 4 GeV/c appears to be in kinetic equilibrium with the medium

## Context
This work extends the HYDJET++ model application from inclusive hadrons to charmed hadrons, following earlier successful description of J/ψ pT and y spectra at RHIC energy √sNN = 200 GeV (PHENIX data) using the same early freeze-out scenario previously suggested for SPS data at 158 GeV/nucleon (early freeze-out, 2000). The results enable further differential studies of charm production mechanisms and thermalization in heavy-ion collisions, and support the interpretation that J/ψ mesons are not in kinetic equilibrium with the medium while a significant fraction of D mesons are.
