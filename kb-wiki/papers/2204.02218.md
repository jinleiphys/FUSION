# Local and global polarization of $Λ$ hyperons across RHIC-BES energies: the roles of spin hall effect, initial condition and baryon diffusion

> We perform a systematic study on the local and global spin polarization of $Λ$ and $\overlineΛ$ hyperons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at beam energy scan energies via the (3+1)-dimensional CLVisc hydrodynamics model with AMPT and SMASH initial conditions. Following the quantum kinetic theory, we decompose the polarization vector as the parts induced by thermal vorticity, shear tensor and the spin Hall effect (SHE). We find that the polarization induced by SHE and the total polarization strongly depends on the initial conditions. At $7.7$GeV, SHE gives a sizeable contribution and even flips the sign of the local polarization along the beam direction for AMPT initial condition, which is not observed for SMASH initial condition. Meanwhile, the local polarization along the out-of-plane direction induced by SHE with AMPT initial condition does not always increase with decreasing collision energies. Next, we find that the polarization along the beam direction is sensitive to the baryon diffusion coefficient, but the local polarization along the out-of-plane direction is not. Our results for the global polarization of $Λ$ and $\overlineΛ$ agree well with the STAR data. Interestingly, the global polarization of $\overlineΛ$ is not always larger than that of $Λ$ due to various competing effects. Our findings are helpful for understanding the polarization phenomenon and the detailed structure of quark-gluon plasma in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The spin Hall effect (SHE) contribution to local polarization strongly depends on the initial condition model used; at 7.7 GeV, SHE can flip the sign of the local polarization along the beam direction for AMPT initial conditions but not for SMASH initial conditions, and the global polarization of anti-Lambda is not always larger than that of Lambda due to competing effects.

## Method

The authors performed (3+1)-dimensional CLVisc hydrodynamic simulations with AMPT and SMASH initial conditions for Au+Au collisions at RHIC-BES energies. They decomposed the polarization vector into contributions from thermal vorticity, shear tensor, fluid acceleration minus temperature gradient, and the spin Hall effect (gradient of chemical potential over temperature), and studied the dependence on initial conditions and the baryon diffusion coefficient.

## Key numbers

- At sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV, SHE gives a sizeable contribution and flips the sign of local polarization along the beam direction for AMPT initial condition, but this sign flip is not observed for SMASH initial condition
- The local polarization along the out-of-plane direction induced by SHE with AMPT initial condition does not always increase with decreasing collision energies across the studied range (7.7, 27, 62.4 GeV)
- The global polarization results for Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons agree well with STAR data across BES energies (7.7-200 GeV)
- Calculations were performed for 20-50% centrality with p_T in [0.5, 3.0] GeV and y in [-1, 1], with Lambda mass taken as m = 1.116 GeV
- The specific shear viscosity C_eta_v and baryon diffusion coefficient C_B were treated as free parameters; C_B values of 0 and 1.2 were compared

## Context

This work extends previous studies of spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions that used thermal vorticity alone (Becattini 2017, Xia 2018) and recent work incorporating shear-induced polarization (Fu 2021, Becattini 2021) and the spin Hall effect (Fu 2022, Yi 2021). The strong dependence on initial conditions found here, particularly the sign change at low energies with AMPT versus SMASH initial conditions, suggests that local polarization measurements can probe the initial baryon density profile of the quark-gluon plasma. The sensitivity of beam-direction polarization to the baryon diffusion coefficient provides a new way to constrain transport properties of the QGP.
