# What's new at small x

## Key claim

Small-x QCD may be approaching a new regime of high parton densities where classical field methods or multi-Pomeron exchanges become necessary, but the leading-order BFKL resummation (predicting a power rise ~x^{-0.5}) is too rapid to match HERA data, and the next-to-leading order correction is so large it changes sign, indicating the resummation procedure as developed is flawed.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical approaches to small-x QCD: DGLAP double-log evolution, BFKL and NLO BFKL resummations, the classical effective field theory (EFT) of the Color Glass Condensate with Wilsonian renormalization group improvement, the Onium dipole model, and hard diffraction factorization. It compares these frameworks against HERA structure function data and discusses their application to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.

## Key numbers

- The phenomenological Pomeron intercept from Donnachie and Landshoff fits: α₀ ≈ 1.08, with string tension α′ = 0.25 GeV⁻², giving σ(s) ~ s^{0.08} for total cross-sections.
- The BFKL characteristic function at γ=1/2 gives a gluon distribution power −ᾱ_S χ(1/2) = −0.5 for α_S = 0.2, too rapid to be compatible with HERA data.
- The NLO BFKL correction gives χ(1/2) = χ₀(1/2) [1 − 6.47 ᾱ_S]; for ᾱ_S = 0.2 the power becomes −0.15, a large correction that changes sign.
- A NLO DGLAP fit to HERA double asymptotic scaling extracts α_S(M_Z) = 0.120 ± 0.05 (exp.) ± 0.09 (theory).
- Hard diffraction at HERA comprises ~10% of the cross-section; the diffractive structure function shows very weak Q² dependence (leading twist).
- In heavy-ion collisions, the saturation scale is estimated as Q_s ∼ 1 GeV for RHIC and Q_s ∼ 2–3 GeV for LHC.
- Gluon production per unit area per unit rapidity: dE/(πR² dη) = [(N_c²−1)/N_c] [c(Q_s² R²)/(4π² α_S)] Q_s³, with c ≈ 4.5 approximately constant.
- Gluon number per unit area per unit rapidity: dN/(πR² dη) = [c̃/(4π² α_S)] [(N_c²−1)/N_c] Q_s², with c̃ ∼ 1.08.

## Context

This plenary talk at WHEPP-6 (2000) surveys the state of small-x QCD after the first years of HERA operation. It extends the BFKL framework of Lipatov and collaborators (1990s) and the DGLAP evolution of Dokshitzer, Gribov, Lipatov, Altarelli, and Parisi (1970s), while highlighting the NLO BFKL crisis identified by Fadin, Lipatov, and collaborators (1998). The paper contrasts the classical EFT approach of McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and the Onium dipole model of Mueller (1994), noting that the non-linear Wilson RG equation of Jalilian-Marian, Kovner, Weigert, and collaborators (1997–1999) can recover multi-Pomeron fan diagrams as a limit. It enables future experimental tests at RHIC and eRHIC, where nuclear targets allow access to parton densities otherwise reachable only at LHC ep energies.
