# Direct photon spectra in Pb-Pb at sqrt(s) = 5.5 TeV: hydrodynamics+pQCD predictions

> 2 pages, 2 figures. Contribution to "Workshop on Heavy Ion Collisions at the LHC: Last Call for Predictions", Geneva, Switzerland, 14 May - 8 Jun 2007. To appear in J.Phys.G

## Key claim

The paper predicts that in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC, thermal (hydrodynamic) photon emission dominates over prompt (pQCD) photon production up to a transverse momentum of about 4 GeV/\(c\) in central collisions and 1.5 GeV/\(c\) in peripheral collisions, with the thermal-prompt crossing point moving up from about 2.5 GeV/\(c\) at RHIC to about 4.5 GeV/\(c\) at the LHC.

## Method

The authors combine a 2+1-dimensional ideal relativistic hydrodynamical model (starting at \(\tau_0 \approx 0.1\) fm/\(c\)) with next-to-leading-order (NLO) perturbative QCD calculations. Thermal photon emission is computed using AMY complete leading-log rates for the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase and the Turbide et al. parametrization for the hadron resonance gas (HRG) phase, with a first-order phase transition at \(T_{\rm crit} \approx 170\) MeV. Prompt photon production from parton-parton scatterings is calculated at NLO using CTEQ6.5M parton distribution functions (with nuclear shadowing and isospin corrections via the nDSg parametrization) and BFG parton-to-photon fragmentation functions (modified by BDMPS quenching weights to account for parton energy loss in the medium).

## Key numbers

- Initial entropy density: \(s_0 = 1120\) fm\(^{-3}\), corresponding to a maximum central temperature \(T_0 \approx 650\) MeV (average \(\langle T_0 \rangle \approx 470\) MeV).
- Number of incoherent nucleon-nucleon collisions: \(N_{\rm coll} = 1670\) for 0-10% central Pb-Pb (mean impact parameter \(\langle b \rangle = 3.2\) fm) and \(N_{\rm coll} = 12.9\) for 60-90% peripheral Pb-Pb (\(\langle b \rangle = 13\) fm).
- Quenching parameter for parton energy loss: \(\omega_c = \langle \hat{q} \rangle L^2 \approx 50\) GeV, extrapolated from RHIC.
- Nuclear modification factor for prompt photons: \(R_{PbPb} \approx 0.2\) at \(p_T = 10\) GeV/\(c\) and \(R_{PbPb} \approx 0.8\) at \(p_T = 100\) GeV/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamics+pQCD approach previously applied to RHIC energies (d'Enterria and Peressounko, 2005) to the LHC energy of \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.5\) TeV. The predictions enable experimental searches for thermal photons from the quark-gluon plasma at LHC, where the thermal contribution is expected to dominate over prompt photons up to higher transverse momenta than at RHIC, making semi-hard direct photons a valuable probe of the thermodynamic properties of the system.
