# A new perspective on the $Δ_{5/2^{+}}(2000)$ puzzle

> We argue that $Δ_{5/2^{+}}(2000)(\ast\ast),$ cataloged as a resonance in the Particle Data Book Review (PDG), should be interpreted instead as two distinctive resonances, $Δ_{5/2^{+}}(\sim1740)$ and $Δ_{5/2^{+}}(\sim2200)$. Our argument is based on a solution of the $πΔρ$ problem in a Fixed Center Approximation (FCA) to the Fadeev equations. $Δ_{5/2^{+}}(\sim1740)$ can then be interpreted as a $π-(Δρ)_{N(1675)}$ bound state. As a corollary $Δ_{1/2^{+}}(1750)(\ast)$ can be understood as a $πN_{1/2^{-}}(1650)$ bound state.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the PDG-listed single resonance Δ₅/₂⁺(2000) should be reinterpreted as two distinct resonances, Δ₅/₂⁺(~1740) and Δ₅/₂⁺(~2200), based on a solution of the π-Δ-ρ three-body problem; the lower state is specifically interpreted as a π-(Δρ) bound state where the Δρ subsystem forms N(1675), and the authors note that their results depend on two subtraction constants (a_Δπ and a_ρπ) treated as effective parameters that could differ significantly from prior values.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations for the π-Δ-ρ three-body system using the Fixed Center Approximation (FCA), where the π meson scatters sequentially off the ρ and Δ particles that form the bound N(1675) state. The π-Δ and π-ρ unitarized s-wave scattering amplitudes were taken from prior chiral unitary approaches (Sar05, Roc05), and the loop function G₀ for the π propagating inside N(1675) was computed. The subtraction constants a_Δπ and a_ρπ were varied to reproduce the observed resonance masses, with a_Δπ = -4.3 needed to obtain the average mass of 1740 MeV for the lower state.

## Key numbers

- Δ₅/₂⁺(~1740) is extracted from data analyses giving masses of 1724±61 MeV and 1752±32 MeV from two independent analyses (Vra00, Man92), and a pole position at 1738 MeV from a recent analysis (Suz10).
- Δ₅/₂⁺(~2200) is extracted from a separate analysis giving 2200±125 MeV (Cut80).
- The π-N(1675) threshold is at 1814 MeV, lying between the 3q model prediction (~1910 MeV) and the experimental candidate masses.
- The subtraction constant a_Δρ = -2.28 was fixed to reproduce the N(1675) mass in the Δρ bound state calculation.
- With a_ρπ = -2.0 and a_Δπ values of -2.6, -3.0, and -3.4, the first peak (assigned to Δ₅/₂⁺(1740)) varies from 1770 MeV to 1800 MeV; forcing a_Δπ = -4.3 gives an average mass of 1740 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Pej09, which explained Δ₅/₂⁻(1930) and its partners as ρΔ bound states, and also described N₁/₂⁻(1650), N₃/₂⁻(1700), and N₅/₂⁻(1675) as ρΔ bound states in the I=1/2 sector. The present paper applies a similar three-body formalism to the π-N(1675) system in the I=3/2 sector, predicting that Δ₁/₂⁺(1750) can be understood as a π-N₁/₂⁻(1650) bound state (a state that quark models from Capstick cannot describe), and that Δ₃/₂⁺(~1740) may correspond to Δ₃/₂⁺(1600). The results provide a theoretical basis for reinterpreting the PDG entry and suggest that only data analyses reproducing ππN production cross sections will extract the lower Δ₅/₂⁺ state.
