# What can we learn from global spin alignment of $ϕ$ meson in heavy-ion collisions?

> We propose that a significant positive deviation from 1/3 for the spin density matrix element $ρ_{00}$ of the $ϕ$ meson may indicate the existence of a mean field of the $ϕ$ meson generated in heavy-ion collisions. This explains why STAR preliminary data for the $ϕ$ meson's $ρ_{00}$ are much larger than 1/3 while the data of $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ polarization seem not to allow such a significant and positive deviation. The contribution may be from the polarization of the strange quark and antiquark through the $ϕ$ field, an effective mode of the gluon field in strong interaction. We show that $ρ_{00}$ for the $ϕ$ meson is a good analyzer for fields even if they may strongly fluctuate in space-time.

## Key claim

A significant positive deviation of the spin density matrix element ρ₀₀ for the φ meson from 1/3 may indicate the existence of a mean φ meson field generated in heavy-ion collisions, rather than arising from vorticity or electromagnetic fields alone. This reconciles the apparent contradiction between STAR preliminary data showing ρ₀₀ > 1/3 and the small Λ and anti-Λ polarization values that would otherwise predict ρ₀₀ < 1/3.

## Method

The authors derive an expression for ρ₀₀ of the φ meson in the coalescence model, relating it to the polarization of strange and anti-strange quarks. They compute contributions from thermal vorticity, electromagnetic fields, and a proposed mean φ meson field (analogous to the vector dominance model) using the spin polarization formula from Yang et al. (2017). The φ field contribution is estimated using a strangeness current density derived from the asymmetry of s and anti-s parton distribution functions in nucleons, with the φ meson wave function taken from the quark potential model.

## Key numbers

- The STAR data give P_Λ ≈ (1.08 ± 0.15 ± 0.11)% and P_anti-Λ ≈ (1.38 ± 0.30 ± 0.13)% for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 7.7–200 GeV, yielding (4/9)P_Λ P_anti-Λ ≈ 6.6 × 10⁻⁵, which is negligible compared to 1/3.
- The mean squared momentum of the φ meson from the quark potential model is ⟨p²⟩_φ ≈ 0.18 GeV² ≈ 9.45 m_π², with m_π ≈ 138.05 MeV.
- The constituent strange quark mass is taken as m_s ≈ 450 MeV.
- The effective temperature T_eff is calibrated to 300 MeV at √s_NN = 200 GeV.
- The c_ε term from fluid vorticity is estimated using CLVisc hydrodynamic simulations giving ⟨ε_z² + ε_x²⟩ ~ 10⁻⁴, yielding c_ε even more suppressed than 10⁻⁴.
- The c_E term from electric fields is estimated using PHSD transport model simulations with peak eE ~ m_π², giving c_E ~ 10⁻⁵.
- Three values of the φ field parameter G_s^(y) are chosen: 2.05, 3.08, and 5.13 m_π⁴.

## Context

This work extends the study of spin alignment in heavy-ion collisions beyond the conventional vorticity and electromagnetic field contributions considered by Yang et al. (2017) and the STAR collaboration (2017, 2018). The proposed φ meson field mechanism builds on the vector dominance model of Sakurai (1960) and Bauer et al. (1977), and on the proposal by Csernai et al. (2018) regarding vector meson mean fields in hyperon polarization. The result enables experimental tests using φ meson spin alignment as a clean probe of fluctuating fields, in contrast to the K*⁰ meson which has stronger interactions and more complicated spin structure.
