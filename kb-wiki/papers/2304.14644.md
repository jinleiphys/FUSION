# Gravitational waves from non-radial perturbations in glitching pulsars

> The Rossby mode (r-mode) perturbations in pulsars as a steady gravitational wave (GW) sources have been explored. The time evolution and the intensity of the emitted GWs in terms of the strain tensor amplitude have been estimated with the approximation of slow rotation adopting the equation of state derived using the Skyrme effective interaction with NRAPR parameter set. The core of the neutron star has been considered to be $β$-equilibrated nuclear matter composed of neutrons, protons, electrons and muons, which is surrounded by a solid crust. Calculations have been made for the critical frequencies, the evolution of frequencies and frequency change rates with time as well as the fiducial viscous and gravitational timescales, across a broad spectrum of pulsar masses. Our findings reveal that the r-mode instability region is associated with rotating young and hot pulsars. Furthermore, it is noteworthy that pulsars with low $L$ value emit gravitational radiation and fall within the r-mode instability region if the primary dissipative mechanism is shear viscosity along the crust-core interface boundary layer. The r-mode perturbation amplitude increases because of GW emissions, in contrast to other non-radial perturbations which transport to infinity the star's angular momentum. Thus the presence of these stellar perturbations implies a non-negative rate of change in transfer of rotational angular momentum. This observation suggests that for a glitching pulsar, the GW emission intensity evolves increasingly with time till the angular frequency diminishes to a value that is below a crucial threshold, after which the compact star ceases to emit radiation.

## Key claim
The paper claims that for glitching pulsars, gravitational wave emission from r-mode perturbations increases with time until the star's angular frequency drops below a critical threshold, after which emission ceases. This behavior depends on the condition that shear viscosity at the crust-core boundary layer is the primary dissipative mechanism, and applies specifically to pulsars with low values of the symmetry energy slope parameter \(L\).

## Method
The authors model r-mode oscillations in slowly rotating neutron stars using the Skyrme effective interaction with the NRAPR parameter set for the nuclear equation of state. They compute critical frequencies, frequency evolution, frequency change rates, and fiducial viscous and gravitational timescales for a range of neutron star masses, considering a \(\beta\)-equilibrated \(npe\mu\) core surrounded by a solid crust. The analysis focuses on the \(l=2\) quadrupole r-mode, solving the time evolution of angular velocity under the combined effects of gravitational radiation and shear viscosity at the crust-core boundary layer.

## Key numbers
- The NRAPR parameter set yields a symmetric nuclear matter incompressibility \(K_\infty = 225.65\) MeV at saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.1606\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The symmetry energy at saturation is \(E_{sym}(\rho_0) = 32.79\) MeV, with slope parameter \(L = 59.63\) MeV.
- The isobaric incompressibility is \(K_\tau = -385.32\) MeV.
- The fiducial shear viscous timescale \(\widetilde{\tau}_{SV}\) and gravitational radiation timescale \(\widetilde{\tau}_{GR}\) are computed as functions of neutron star gravitational mass (shown in Figure 6, numerical values not given in text).
- The critical angular velocity \(\Omega_c\) for the \(l=2\) mode is given by \(\left(\frac{\Omega_c}{\Omega_0}\right) = \left(-\frac{\widetilde{\tau}_{GR}}{\widetilde{\tau}_{SV}}\right)^{2/11} \left(\frac{10^8 K}{T}\right)^{2/11}\), where \(\Omega_0 = \sqrt{\pi G \bar{\rho}}\).
- The critical temperature \(T_c\) satisfies \(\frac{T_c}{10^8 K} = \left(\frac{\Omega_0}{\Omega_c}\right)^{11/2} \left(-\frac{\tilde{\tau}_{GR}}{\widetilde{\tau}_{SV}}\right) \approx (3/2)^{11/2} \left(-\frac{\widetilde{\tau}_{GR}}{\widetilde{\tau}_{SV}}\right)\).
- The time to evolve from initial angular velocity \(\Omega_{in}\) to critical \(\Omega_c\) is \(t_c = \frac{1}{\textsl{C}}\left(\Omega_{in}^{-6} - \Omega_{c}^{-6}\right)\), where \(\textsl{C} = \frac{12\alpha_r^2 Q}{\widetilde{\tau}_{GR}(1-\alpha_r^2 Q)}\frac{1}{\Omega_0^6}\).

## Context
This work extends the r-mode instability analysis of Moustakidis (2015) by using the NRAPR Skyrme interaction to compute the full time evolution of gravitational wave emission from glitching pulsars, including the spin-down rate. It builds on the foundational r-mode studies of Andersson (1998) and Friedman and Morsink (1998), and the boundary layer shear viscosity treatment of Bildsten and Ushomirsky (2000) and Lindblom (2000). The results enable future comparisons with LIGO and VIRGO gravitational wave detections, potentially constraining the high-density behavior of the nuclear equation of state.
