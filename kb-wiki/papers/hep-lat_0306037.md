# Scalar Mesons as qbar^2 q^2? Insight from the Lattice

> I describe some insight obtained from a lattice calculation on the possibility that the light scalar mesons are \qbar^2 q^2 states rather than \qbar q. First I review some general features of \qbar^2 q^2 states in QCD inspired quark models. Then I describe a lattice QCD calculation of pseudoscalar meson scattering amplitudes, ignoring quark loops and quark annihilation, which finds indications that for sufficiently heavy quarks there is a stable four-quark bound state with JPC=0{++} and non-exotic flavor quantum numbers.

## Key claim

The lattice calculation, performed in the quenched approximation with heavy quark masses and ignoring quark annihilation, finds indications of a stable four-quark bound state with quantum numbers \(J^{PC}=0^{++}\) in the non-exotic flavor channel, but the authors caution that the results are suggestive rather than conclusive and require confirmation on larger lattices.

## Method

The authors performed a quenched lattice QCD calculation of pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar scattering amplitudes using Symanzik-improved glue and quark actions with tadpole-improved coefficients, working at a quark mass where the pseudoscalar-to-vector meson mass ratio \(m_P/m_V\) is 0.76. They constructed four-quark (\(\bar q^2 q^2\)) sources, discarded quark annihilation diagrams, and studied the large Euclidean time falloff of \(\bar q^2 q^2-\bar q^2 q^2\) correlators for both exotic (\(I=2\)) and non-exotic (\(I=0\)) channels on several lattice volumes. The binding energies \(\delta E\) were extracted from ratios of correlators and examined as a function of lattice size \(L\) to distinguish a bound state from a scattering state described by the Luscher formula.

## Key numbers

* The pseudoscalar-to-vector meson mass ratio used in the calculation is \(m_P/m_V = 0.76\), corresponding to a quark mass close to the physical strange quark.
* For the exotic (\(I=2\)) channel, the binding energy \(\delta E_E\) follows a \(1/L^3\) dependence expected for a scattering state, with the fitted scattering amplitude close to the chiral perturbation theory prediction \(4 f_P^2 T = 1\) using \(f_P = 148\) MeV.
* For the non-exotic (\(I=0\)) channel, the binding energy \(\delta E_N\) departs from \(1/L^3\) falloff and appears to approach a negative constant as \(L\to\infty\), indicating a bound state.
* The toy model with a square well potential of range \(b = 1/m_\pi \approx 1.4\) fm, tuned to give 10 MeV binding at \(m_P \sim 800\) MeV, shows the bound state disappears when \(m_P\) falls below 330 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of four-quark sources by Gupta et al. (2000) and Fukugita et al. (1995), which each examined only a single lattice volume and could not distinguish bound states from scattering states. The results support the \(\bar q^2 q^2\) interpretation of light scalar mesons originally proposed by Jaffe (1977) and contrast with the conventional quark model assignment of scalar mesons as orbitally excited \(\bar q q\) states. The findings enable future unquenched lattice calculations to determine whether these bound states survive when quark loops and annihilation are included, and whether they correspond to physical states such as the \(f_0(980)\) and \(a_0(980)\) mesons.
