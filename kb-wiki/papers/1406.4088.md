# Ab initio calculation of the neutron-proton mass difference

> The existence and stability of atoms rely on the fact that neutrons are more massive than protons. The measured mass difference is only 0.14\% of the average of the two masses. A slightly smaller or larger value would have led to a dramatically different universe. Here, we show that this difference results from the competition between electromagnetic and mass isospin breaking effects. We performed lattice quantum-chromodynamics and quantum-electrodynamics computations with four nondegenerate Wilson fermion flavors and computed the neutron-proton mass-splitting with an accuracy of $300$ kilo-electron volts, which is greater than $0$ by $5$ standard deviations. We also determine the splittings in the $Σ$, $Ξ$, $D$ and $Ξ_{cc}$ isospin multiplets, exceeding in some cases the precision of experimental measurements.

## Key claim

The neutron-proton mass splitting of approximately 1.51 MeV results from a subtle cancellation between electromagnetic effects (which favor a lighter neutron) and quark mass isospin breaking effects (which favor a heavier neutron), with the calculation achieving a 5-standard-deviation signal for a nonzero splitting after accounting for systematic uncertainties through a histogram method with Akaike information criterion weighting.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD+QED simulations with 1+1+1+1 flavors of nondegenerate clover-improved Wilson quarks (up, down, strange, charm) at four lattice spacings from 0.06 fm to 0.10 fm, using four values of the bare fine structure constant including zero and values near 1/137, 1/10, and 1/6. They generated gauge configurations with an improved Hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm incorporating Fourier acceleration for the photon field, used two frameworks for handling the photon field's long-range nature, and corrected for power-law finite-volume effects using one-loop analytical QED calculations and high-precision QED simulations.

## Key numbers

* Neutron-proton mass splitting: 1.51(16)(23) MeV, where the first error is statistical and the second systematic
* QCD contribution to the neutron-proton splitting: 2.52(17)(24) MeV; QED contribution: -1.00(07)(14) MeV
* Sigma splitting (Sigma- minus Sigma+): 8.09(16)(11) MeV, entirely from QCD
* Xi splitting (Xi- minus Xi0): 6.66(11)(09) MeV, with QCD contribution 5.53(17)(17) MeV and QED contribution 1.14(16)(09) MeV
* D meson splitting (D+ minus D0): 4.68(10)(13) MeV
* Xi_cc splitting (Xi_cc++ minus Xi_cc+): 2.16(11)(17) MeV
* Coleman-Glashow difference: 0.00(11)(06) MeV, consistent with zero at 130 keV accuracy
* Ratio of QCD to QED contributions for the nucleon splitting, using the experimental value: (M_n-M_p)_QCD/(M_n-M_p)_QED = -2.49(23)(29)
* Smallest pion mass used: approximately 195 MeV with more than 20,000 trajectories
* Largest lattice spatial extent: 8 fm

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice calculations that used the quenched approximation for QED (Duncan 1996) or electroquenched approaches (Blum 2010, Basak 2013, Borsanyi 2013) by including fully dynamical QED and nondegenerate quark masses in the sea, reducing systematic uncertainties from O(1/N_c/m_c^2, alpha, m_d-m_u) to O(1/N_c/m_b^2, alpha^2). The results enable precise separation of QCD and QED contributions to isospin splittings, provide predictions for the Xi and Xi_cc splittings that exceed experimental precision, and allow constraints on fundamental constants from astrophysical and cosmological arguments about the universe's composition.
