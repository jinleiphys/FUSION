# Asymmetry and Spin-Orbit Effects in Binding Energy in the Effective Nuclear Surface Approximation

> Isoscalar and isovector particle densities are derived analytically by using the approximation of a sharp edged nucleus within the local energy density approach with the proton-neutron asymmetry and spin-orbit effects. Equations for the effective nuclear-surface shapes as collective variables are derived up to the higher order corrections in the form of the macroscopic boundary conditions. The analytical expressions for the isoscalar and isovector tension coefficients of the nuclear surface binding energy and the finite-size corrections to the $β$ stability line are obtained.

# Asymmetry and Spin-Orbit Effects in Binding Energy in the Effective Nuclear Surface Approximation

## Key claim

The paper extends the effective nuclear surface (ES) approximation to include proton-neutron asymmetry and spin-orbit effects analytically, deriving isoscalar and isovector tension coefficients and finite-size corrections to the beta-stability line, but the precision of the spin-orbit and semiclassical terms in the isovector energy density is not accurate enough for all considered Skyrme interactions, making the isovector surface tension inconsistent with the capillary isovector pressure.

## Method

The authors use the local energy density approach within the extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) theory with Skyrme forces, employing a sharp-edged nucleus approximation and expanding in the small parameter a/R ~ A^{-1/3} for heavy nuclei. They derive first-order differential equations for the isoscalar and isovector particle densities in the normal-to-surface coordinate, solve them analytically, and obtain macroscopic boundary conditions for the effective nuclear surface shape. The surface tension coefficients are then expressed as integrals over the density profiles, and the finite-size correction to the beta-stability line is derived from the equivalence of neutron and proton chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- Volume binding energy per particle: b_v = 16 MeV
- Symmetry energy constant of nuclear matter: b_sym = 60 MeV
- Saturation density of infinite nuclear matter: rho_bar = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Spin-orbit coupling constant range: W_0 = 100-130 MeV fm^5
- Isoscalar surface energy constant b_s^+ for SkM*: 17.1 MeV (analytical), 18.5 MeV (numerical)
- Isoscalar surface energy constant b_s^+ for SLy7: 15.9 MeV (analytical), 17.0 MeV (numerical)
- Ratio b_s^-/X^2 for SkM*: -3.23 MeV
- Ratio b_s^-/X^2 for SLy7: -10.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the effective surface approximation developed by Strutinsky, Tyapin, Magner, and collaborators (1970s-1990s) by adding asymmetry and spin-orbit terms that were previously omitted. The approach builds on the ETF method with Skyrme forces from Brack, Guet, and Hakansson (1985) and Chabanat et al. (1997-1998). The results enable improved macroscopic components for nuclear collective dynamical macro-micromodels combining the ETF or liquid-drop model with the Strutinsky shell correction method, applicable to studies of low-lying collective excitations and fission processes.
