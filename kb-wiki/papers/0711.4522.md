# Estimating Consequences of 3-Body Forces

> Classifying the strengthes of three-body forces 3BFs with the condition that observables must be cut-off independent, i.e. renormalised at each order, leads to surprising results with relevance for example for thermal neutron capture on the deuteron. Details and a better bibliography in Ref. [1].

# Estimating Consequences of 3-Body Forces

## Key claim

Three-body forces (3BFs) in effective field theories of nuclear physics are not added out of phenomenological necessity but are required if and only if they are needed to cancel cut-off dependence in low-energy observables; this renormalisation-based power counting leads to the surprising result that about half of the 3BFs for partial waves with l ≤ 2 are weaker (demoted) and half are stronger (promoted) than a simplistic dimensional estimate would suggest.

## Method

The author starts from the Faddeev equation for nucleon-deuteron scattering in the spin-doublet and quartet channels at leading order, with the NN amplitude given by the leading term of the effective-range expansion. The asymptotic behaviour of the three-nucleon amplitude is determined by solving an algebraic equation for the exponent s₀, which depends only on the partial wave l and the spin-isospin factor λ. The order n at which a 3BF is required is then determined by the superficial degree of divergence of higher-order corrections, computed from the condition Re[n − s_l(λ) − s_{l′}(λ′)] ≥ 0, where the exponents s_l come from the hyper-radial Schrödinger equation in position space.

## Key numbers

- The total cross-section for thermal neutron capture on the deuteron (nd → tγ) computed in pion-less EFT converges order by order to σ_tot = [0.485(LO) + 0.011(NLO) + 0.007(N2LO)] mb = [0.503 ± 0.003] mb, compared to the experimental value [0.509 ± 0.015] mb.
- Nuclear potential models give a spread of [0.49 to 0.66] mb for the same cross-section, which reduces to [0.52 to 0.56] mb when gauge-invariance is restored.
- The S-wave to S-wave 3BF in the spin-doublet channel is promoted to LO (compared to a simplistic estimate of N2LO), with a typical size of 100% in pion-less EFT versus 10% from the simplistic estimate.
- The P-wave to P-wave 3BF in the spin-doublet channel is demoted to N5.7LO (compared to a simplistic N4LO), with a typical size of 0.2% versus 1% from the simplistic estimate.
- The quartet S-wave to S-wave 3BF is demoted to N4.3+2LO (compared to a simplistic N2+2LO), with a typical size of 0.1% versus 1% from the simplistic estimate.

## Context

This work extends the renormalisation-group approach to three-body forces in effective field theories, building on the well-known Efimov and Thomas effects for three bosons where a 3BF must be added at leading order to stabilise the system. The author applies the methodology to the pion-less EFT of nuclear physics (EFT(π/)), showing that the power counting for 3BFs can be derived rigorously from cut-off independence rather than from educated guesswork. The results enable systematic, cut-off independent predictions for low-energy three-nucleon observables such as thermal neutron capture on the deuteron, and they pose a puzzle for potential models: those models reproduce the same input as EFT(π/) (nucleon magnetic moments, deuteron and triton binding energies, NN and nd scattering lengths, and the np → dγ cross-section) yet give results that vary dramatically, contradicting the EFT expectation of universality.
