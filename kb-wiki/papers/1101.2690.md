# Decoupling of Spurious Deep Bound States with the Similarity Renormalization Group

> The Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) is a continuous series of unitary transformations that can be implemented as a flow equation. When the relative kinetic energy ($\Trel$) is used in the SRG generator, nuclear structure calculations have shown greatly improved convergence with basis size because of the decoupling of high-energy and low-energy physics. However this generator can sometimes be problematic. A test case is provided by a study of initial interactions from chiral effective field theories with large cutoffs, which can lead to spurious deep bound states. We would like the SRG to decouple these from the physical shallow bound states. However, with $\Trel$ the high- and low-energy bound states are not decoupled in the usual sense. Replacing $\Trel$ by the momentum-space diagonal of the Hamiltonian ($H_d$) in the SRG generator does produce decoupling, such that the shallow states are in the low-momentum region and the deep bound states are at higher momentum. The flow toward universal low-momentum interactions is also restored.

## Key claim

The Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) with the standard relative kinetic energy generator fails to decouple spurious deep bound states that arise in chiral effective field theory interactions with large cutoffs, but replacing the generator with the momentum-space diagonal of the Hamiltonian restores proper decoupling and the flow toward universal low-momentum interactions.

## Method

The authors evolved leading-order chiral effective field theory nucleon-nucleon potentials using SRG flow equations with two different generators: the relative kinetic energy and the momentum-space diagonal of the Hamiltonian. They examined the decoupling properties in the 1S0 channel (no spurious states), the coupled 3S1-3D1 channel, and the 3P0 channel (both with spurious bound states at large cutoffs), analyzing contour plots of the evolved potentials, momentum probability densities of bound states, and phase shifts after applying momentum cuts.

## Key numbers

- For the 3S1-3D1 channel with Lambda_EFT = 20.0 fm^-1, the spurious state momentum distribution after H_diag evolution is confined to a small region near k = 1.6 fm^-1, while the deuteron low-momentum distribution remains essentially unchanged.
- Decoupling with H_diag in the presence of spurious bound states is delayed until lambda = 1.2 fm^-1 for a 2.5 fm^-1 cut, compared to lambda = 2.0 fm^-1 when no spurious states are present.
- The 3P0 channel develops a spurious bound state for Lambda_EFT between 3 and 4 fm^-1.
- Bound state energies for spurious states in the 3S1-3D1 and 3P0 channels reach values of two hundred MeV or more.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Glazek and Perry (2008), who identified the generic failure of the kinetic-energy-based SRG generator for bound states in simple model Hamiltonians, to realistic nuclear interactions from chiral effective field theory. The study builds on the renormalization analysis of Nogga et al. (2005), who explored large-cutoff chiral EFT and found spurious deep bound states that had to be removed by hand. The results enable future investigations of SRG evolution in few-body systems (A >= 3) and further studies of effective field theory using the diagonal-generator approach.
