# Sketching pion and proton mass distributions

> A light-front holographic model is used to illustrate an algebraic scheme for constructing a representation of a hadron's zero-skewness generalised parton distribution (GPD) from its valence-quark distribution function (DF) and electromagnetic form factor, $F_H$, without reference to deeply virtual Compton scattering data. The hadron's mass distribution gravitational form factor, $A_H$, calculated from this GPD is harder than $F_H$; and, for each hadron, the associated mass-density profile is more compact than the analogous charge profile, with each pion near-core density being larger than that of its proton partner. These features are independent of the scheme employed.

# Sketching pion and proton mass distributions

## Key claim

A light-front holographic model (LFHM) can algebraically construct a hadron's zero-skewness generalised parton distribution (GPD) from its valence-quark distribution function (DF) and electromagnetic form factor without reference to deeply virtual Compton scattering data, and the resulting mass-density profile is more compact than the charge profile for both pion and proton, with each pion near-core density being larger than that of its proton partner; these features are claimed to be independent of the scheme employed, though the model does not incorporate the logarithmic scaling violations characteristic of QCD.

## Method

The authors use a light-front holographic model (LFHM) where hadron electromagnetic form factors are expressed via the Euler Beta function with a mass scale parameter λ, and the zero-skewness GPD is constructed algebraically from the valence-quark DF and a profile function w(x) that is determined by solving an integral equation relating the DF to the form factor representation. The GPD is then used to compute the mass distribution gravitational form factor A(Q²) via the first Mellin moment, and two-dimensional transverse densities are obtained through Fourier transforms. The approach is applied to both pion (τ=2) and proton (τ=3) using continuum Schwinger function method (CSM) predictions for hadron-scale valence-quark DFs.

## Key numbers

- For the pion mass distribution form factor radius ratio: (r_A^π/r_F^π)² = 0.58 (LFHM), compared to 0.54 from CSM (Xu 2023)
- For the proton mass-to-charge radius ratio: (r_mass^p/r_ch^p)² = 0.87 (LFHM), compared to 0.81(5) from CSM (Yao 2024)
- The LFHM mass scale parameter is √λ = 0.548 GeV, determined from the ρ-meson mass m_ρ = 0.775 GeV
- Transverse density peak positions: pion mass profile at b_⟂^max = 0.116 fm, pion charge profile at 0.151 fm; proton mass profile at 0.226 fm, proton charge profile at 0.260 fm
- Transverse density peak heights: pion mass profile 2.40 × fm, pion charge profile 1.84 × fm; proton mass profile 1.85 × fm, proton charge profile 1.55 × fm
- The pion radius ratio bound is given as 1/2 ≤ (r_A^π/r_F^π)² ≤ 1

## Context

This work extends the data-driven numerical method for GPD determination from Xu (2023) by introducing an algebraic simplification using the light-front holographic model, and extends the analysis from the pion to the proton. The results are compared with continuum Schwinger function method predictions from Xu (2023) for the pion and Yao (2024) for the proton, and with lattice QCD results from Hackett (2023) for both hadrons. The approach enables model-independent features to be identified before precise deeply virtual Compton scattering data become available from modern and anticipated facilities, though the authors note that lattice QCD results at the physical pion mass are desirable for more reliable comparisons.
