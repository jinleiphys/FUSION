# Tsallis statistics, fractals and QCD

> We study the non-extensive Tsallis statistics and its applications to QCD and high energy physics, and analyze the possible connections of this statistics with a fractal structure of hadrons. Then, we describe how scaling properties of Yang-Mills theories allow the appearance of self-similar structures in gauge fields, which actually behave as fractals. The Tsallis entropic index, $q$, is deduced in terms of the field theory parameters, resulting in a good agreement with the value obtained experimentally.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Tsallis entropic index \(q\) can be derived from the parameters of Yang-Mills field theory via a comparison of the 1-loop beta function, yielding a value of \(q = 1.14\) that agrees with experimental fits, but this derivation relies on the assumption that gauge fields exhibit self-similar fractal structures and that the effective coupling follows a specific power-law form.

## Method

The authors first review the non-extensive self-consistent thermodynamics (NESCT) approach, which replaces the Boltzmann-Gibbs exponential factor with the Tsallis \(q\)-exponential to fit hadron transverse momentum distributions. They then introduce the concept of thermofractals, self-similar thermodynamic systems whose energy distribution naturally obeys Tsallis statistics. Finally, they analyze the renormalization group invariance of Yang-Mills fields, showing that the scaling properties of vertex functions imply a fractal structure, and compute the 1-loop beta function from an effective coupling derived from self-similarity, equating it to the standard QCD beta function to solve for \(q\).

## Key numbers

*   The Tsallis entropic index from fits to \(pp\) collision data is \(q = 1.14 \pm 0.01\) with a temperature \(T = 62 \pm 5 \, \text{MeV}\).
*   The freeze-out line in the \(T - \mu_B\) diagram spans the region \(0 < \mu_B < 1039.2 \, \text{MeV}\).
*   The fractal dimension from intermittency analysis of rapidity distributions is \(D = 0.65\), while the thermofractal model with \(q = 1.14\) gives \(D = 0.69\).
*   The derived 1-loop beta function from the fractal formalism is \(\beta_{\bar{g}} = - \frac{1}{16\pi^2} \frac{1}{q-1} g^{\tilde{N}+1}\), and equating it to the QCD result \(\beta_{\text{QCD}} = - \frac{1}{16\pi^2} \left[ \frac{11}{3}c_1 - \frac{4}{3} c_2 \right] \bar{g}^{3}\) with \(N_c = N_f/2 = 3\) yields \(q = 1.14\).

## Context

This work extends the Hagedorn self-consistent thermodynamics (Hagedorn, 1965) by replacing Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics with Tsallis statistics to resolve discrepancies with power-law transverse momentum distributions in \(pp\) collisions. It connects the phenomenological success of Tsallis statistics in high-energy physics to a fundamental origin in the renormalization group properties of Yang-Mills theories, building on the renormalizability proof by 't Hooft (1972) and the Callan-Symanzik equation (Callan, 1970; Symanzik, 1970). The results enable a unified description of hadron spectra, multiparticle production, and the thermodynamics of QCD, with potential applications to neutron star structure and hadron structure models.
