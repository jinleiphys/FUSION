# Shell structure and shape transition in odd-$Z$ superheavy nuclei with proton numbers $Z=117, 119$: insights from deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov in continuum

> We present a systematic study on the structural properties of odd-$Z$ superheavy nuclei with proton numbers $Z=117, 119$, and neutron numbers $N$ increasing from $N=170$ to the neutron dripline within the framework of axially deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc). The results are compared with those of even-even superheavy nuclei with proton numbers $Z=118$ and $120$. We analyze various bulk properties of their ground states, including binding energies, quadrupole deformations, root-mean-square radii, nucleon separation energies, and $α$-decay energies. The coexistence of competing prolate and oblate or spherical shapes leads to abrupt changes in both quadrupole deformations and charge radii as functions of neutron numbers. Compared to even-even nuclei, the odd-mass ones exhibit a more complicated transition picture, in which the quantum numbers of $K^π$ of the lowest-energy configuration may change with deformation. This may result in the change of angular momentum in the ground-state to ground-state $α$-decay and thus quench the decay rate in odd-mass nuclei. Moreover, our results demonstrate a pronounced proton shell gap at $Z=120$, instead of $Z=114$, which is consistent with the predictions of most covariant density functional theories. Moreover, large neutron shell gaps are found at $N=172$ and $N=258$ in the four isotopic chains, as well as at $N=184$ in the light two isotopic chains with $Z=117$ and $Z=118$, attributed to the nearly-degenerate $3d$ and $4p$ spin-orbit doublet states due to the presence of bubble structure.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for odd-Z superheavy nuclei with Z=117 and 119, the ground-state quadrupole deformations and charge radii undergo abrupt changes as a function of neutron number due to shape coexistence, and that the quantum numbers K^π of the lowest-energy configuration may change with deformation, potentially quenching α-decay rates in odd-mass nuclei. The authors hedge that these predictions are based on the specific PC-PK1 energy density functional within the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov in continuum (DRHBc) framework, and that the results differ quantitatively from the WS4 mass model.

## Method

The authors performed systematic calculations using axially deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) with the PC-PK1 energy density functional, employing a density-dependent δ force for isovector pairing with strength V0 = -325 MeV fm^3. For odd-Z nuclei, the blocking effect from the unpaired proton was treated using the equal-filling approximation, and the ground state for each isotope was determined by starting from 11 different initial quadrupole deformations ranging from -0.4 to +0.6 with step size 0.1, using an automatic blocking procedure to find the lowest-energy state. The quasiparticle wave functions were expanded in a spherical Dirac Woods-Saxon basis with angular momentum cutoff Jmax = 23ħ/2, energy cutoff E_cut^+ = 300 MeV, box size Rmax = 20 fm, Legendre expansion truncation Lmax = 10, and mesh size Δr = 0.1 fm.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies per nucleon B/A for Z=117-120 isotopes peak at either N=174 or N=176, then decrease smoothly with increasing neutron number
- The linear regression of total binding energies for 460 isotopes using a six-parameter liquid drop model (LDM6) yields an rms error of 2.63 MeV
- Ground states of the four isotopic chains with N < 186 are spherical or weakly deformed; for N around 194, Z=119 and 120 isotopes shift to oblate states with β2 ≈ -0.4
- For N around 206, ground states shift back to prolate deformed states with β ≈ 0.3; for N up to 248, quadrupole deformations decrease smoothly to zero
- In 365119 (Z=119, N=246), the global energy-minimum state has K^π = 7/2^- at β20 ≈ 0.15, while the second minimum at β20 ≈ -0.10 has K^π = 11/2^+
- The second energy minimum with β20 ≈ 0.55 in 289119 is higher than the global minimum with β20 = 0.05 by 1.15 MeV
- Charge radii of Z=119 and 120 isotopes with N ≈ 200 are approximately 0.1 fm greater than those of their isotones with Z=117 and 118
- Large neutron shell gaps are found at N=172 and N=258 in all four isotopic chains, and at N=184 in the Z=117 and Z=118 isotopic chains

## Context

This work extends the DRHBc mass table calculations previously performed for even-even nuclei (Zhang et al., 2020; DRHBcMassTable, 2022), even-Z odd-N nuclei (Pan et al., 2022; DRHBcMassTable, 2024), and odd-odd nuclei with Z=106-112 (He et al., 2024) to the heavier odd-Z superheavy nuclei with Z=117 and 119. The results are compared with the WS4 mass model (Wang et al., 2014) and with even-even superheavy nuclei with Z=118 and 120. The finding of a pronounced proton shell gap at Z=120 rather than Z=114 is consistent with most covariant density functional theories, and the identification of large neutron shell gaps at N=172, N=184, and N=258 attributed to nearly-degenerate 3d and 4p spin-orbit doublet states due to bubble structure provides predictions for future experimental searches for superheavy elements beyond Z=118.
