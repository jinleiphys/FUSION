# Large-$N_c$ operator analysis of hyperon-nucleon interactions in SU(3) chiral effective field theory

> We study octet-octet baryon ($J^P = {\textstyle \frac12}^+$) contact interactions in SU(3) chiral effective field theory by using large-$N_c$ operator analysis. Applying the $1/N_c$ expansion of the Hartee Hamiltonian, we find 15 operators in the octet-octet baryon potential where 4 operators for leading order (LO) and 11 for and net-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). The large-$N_c$ operator analysis of octet-octet baryon matrix elements reduces the number of free parameters from 15 to 6 at LO of the $1/N_c$ expansion. The application of large-$N_c$ sum rules to the Jülich model of hyperon-nucleon (YN) interactions at the LO of the chiral expansion reduces the model parameters to 3 from 5 at the LO of $1/N_c$ expansion. We find that the values of LECs fitted to YN scattering data in Ref. \cite{Li:2016paq} in the relativistic covariant ChEFT (EG) approach is more consistent with the predictions of large-$N_c$ than in the heavy baryon (HB) formalism approach.

## Key claim

The large-$N_c$ operator analysis reduces the number of free parameters in the SU(3) octet-octet baryon contact interactions from 15 to 6 at leading order of the $1/N_c$ expansion, and the resulting sum rules are more consistent with the low-energy constants fitted to hyperon-nucleon scattering data in the relativistic covariant chiral effective field theory approach than in the heavy baryon formalism approach, with the caveat that these relations hold up to corrections of order $1/N_c^2 \approx 10\%$.

## Method

The authors constructed the octet-octet baryon contact potential from the SU(3) chiral Lagrangian using relativistic covariant constraints, obtaining 6 operators at leading order and 21 at next-to-leading order in the small momentum expansion. They then built a Hartree Hamiltonian in the $1/N_c$ expansion up to next-to-next-to-leading order, yielding 4 operators at leading order and 11 at next-to-next-to-leading order in $1/N_c$. By matching the spin and flavor structures of the chiral potential to the $1/N_c$ expansion, they derived 9 sum rules relating the low-energy constants at leading order of the $1/N_c$ expansion, and applied these sum rules to the Juelich model of hyperon-nucleon interactions at leading order of the chiral expansion.

## Key numbers

* The SU(3) octet-octet baryon potential from the chiral Lagrangian contains 15 free parameters (low-energy constants) at leading order of the chiral expansion.
* The large-$N_c$ operator analysis reduces the number of free parameters from 15 to 6 at leading order of the $1/N_c$ expansion.
* Applying large-$N_c$ sum rules to the Juelich model of hyperon-nucleon interactions at leading order of the chiral expansion reduces the model parameters from 5 to 3 at leading order of the $1/N_c$ expansion.
* The sum rules are held up to corrections of $1/N_c^2 \approx 10\%$ at $N_c = 3$.

## Context

This work extends the large-$N_c$ operator analysis previously applied to nucleon-nucleon potentials by Kaplan (1995, 1996) and to three-nucleon potentials by Phillips (2013) to the SU(3) octet-octet baryon sector, including hyperon-nucleon interactions. The results enable future fits of hyperon-nucleon scattering data with fewer free parameters constrained by large-$N_c$ arguments, and can be applied to hyperon-nucleon interactions at next-to-leading order and to hyperon-hyperon interactions. The analysis builds on the SU(3) chiral Lagrangian for octet-octet baryon contact interactions developed by Polinder (2006) and the relativistic covariant chiral effective field theory approach of Li (2016).
