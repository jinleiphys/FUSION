# Effects of high-momentum tail of nucleon momentum distribution on initiation of cluster production in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies

> Based on the transport model isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck coupled with a phase-space coalescence afterburner, we studied the effects of the high-momentum tail (HMT) of nucleon momentum distribution in initialization in 197Au+197Au reactions at a beam energy of 400 MeV/nucleon with different impact parameters.We found remarkable impact parameter-dependent HMT effects on the fragment multiplicity distribution. The average neutron to proton ratio of produced isotopes is also affected by the HMT. The rapidity distributions of triton and 3He and their elliptic flows are all evidently affected by the HMT. All the effects of the HMT on the cluster production in heavy-ion collisions are centrality dependent.

## Key claim

The high-momentum tail (HMT) of the nucleon momentum distribution in the initial state of heavy-ion collisions produces centrality-dependent effects on fragment multiplicity, the average neutron-to-proton ratio of isotopes, and the rapidity distributions and elliptic flows of triton and ³He, but the magnitude of these effects depends on the impact parameter and is more prominent in peripheral collisions.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model coupled with a phase-space coalescence afterburner to simulate ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon. The initial nucleon momentum distribution included a high-momentum tail extending to 2.2 times the Fermi momentum, with 20% of nucleons in the HMT following a 1/k⁴ distribution and obeying the n-p dominance model. The single-nucleon potential was derived from a modified Gogny effective interaction, and the phase-space coalescence used parameters P₀ = 263 MeV/c and R₀ = 3 fm.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 400 MeV/nucleon for ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au reactions
- Impact parameters studied: b = 3, 5, and 7 fm
- HMT cutoff parameter: λ = 2.2, so the HMT extends to λk_Fₙ,ₚ = 2.2 k_Fₙ,ₚ
- 20% of nucleons (equal numbers of neutrons and protons) are placed in the HMT
- In ¹⁹⁷Au, approximately 25% of protons and 17% of neutrons have momenta larger than their respective Fermi momenta
- Coalescence parameters: P₀ = 263 MeV/c and R₀ = 3 fm
- 100,000 events per case with 250 test particles per nucleon

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Yong (2015, 2016) that examined HMT effects on nucleonic collective flows and free neutron-to-proton ratios, and by the CLAS Collaboration (2018) which measured isospin-dependent high-momentum fractions in medium-to-heavy nuclei. The results enable experimental verification of nucleon-nucleon short-range correlation effects on cluster production in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for triton and ³He elliptic flows at transverse momenta above 250 MeV/c in semicentral and semiperipheral collisions.
