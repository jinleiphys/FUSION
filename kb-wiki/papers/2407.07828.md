# Inhibition of splitting of the chiral and deconfinement transition due to rotation in QCD: the phase diagram of linear sigma model coupled to Polyakov loop

> We discuss the effect of rigid rotation on the critical temperatures of deconfinement and chiral transitions in the linear sigma model coupled to quarks and the Polyakov loop. We point out the essential role of the causality condition, which requires that any point of the system should rotate slower than the velocity of light. We show that imposing this physical requirement leads to inhibition of the splitting between the chiral and confining transitions, which becomes negligibly small (ΔT ~ 1 MeV or less) for experimentally relevant, slow angular velocities Ω ~ 10 MeV of a 5-10 fm-sized systems. Moreover, the boundedness of the system has a much bigger effect on temperature splitting than the rotation itself: the splitting reaches 10 MeV in a small, one-fermi-sized non-rotating system. The temperature splitting may, however, become enhanced in an academic limit of ultra-relativistic regimes when the boundary of the system rotates at near-to-light velocities.

## Key claim

The splitting between the chiral and deconfinement transition temperatures is inhibited by the causality condition (requiring that no point rotates faster than light) and becomes negligibly small (ΔT ~ 1 MeV or less) for experimentally relevant slow angular velocities Ω ~ 10 MeV in 5-10 fm-sized systems; the boundedness of the system has a much larger effect on temperature splitting than rotation itself.

## Method

The authors employ the Polyakov-loop enhanced linear sigma model with quarks (PLSM_q) in a mean-field approximation, treating the meson fields (σ, π) and Polyakov loop as classical fields while quarks are quantum fields. They impose rigid rotation with cylindrical boundaries to enforce causality, quantizing transverse radial modes via spectral boundary conditions, and solve the saddle-point equations for the σ field and Polyakov loop to determine the free energy minima as functions of temperature, angular velocity, system size, and chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The splitting between chiral and deconfinement transition temperatures is ΔT ~ 1 MeV or less for angular velocities Ω ~ 10 MeV in systems of size 5-10 fm
- The splitting reaches 10 MeV in a small, one-fermi-sized non-rotating system
- The pion mass is set to m_π = 138 MeV and the sigma meson mass to m_σ = 600 MeV in the vacuum
- The quartic coupling is λ = 20, derived from m_σ = 600 MeV and f_π
- The constituent quark mass in vacuum is 307 MeV, giving Yukawa coupling g ≃ 3.3
- The critical temperature for pure gauge theory is T_0 = 270 MeV
- At chemical potential μ = 0.25 GeV, the phase transition occurs at T_c ≃ 0.15 GeV

## Context

This work extends the study of rotation effects on QCD phase transitions by Sun (2023), who proposed that rotation can split the deconfinement and chiral transitions but did not consider boundary effects. The authors show that imposing causality via finite cylindrical boundaries fundamentally alters the conclusions, with boundary size dominating over rotation in determining transition splitting. The results contradict the expectation from most theoretical models (Chen 2015, Jiang 2016, Chernodub 2017) that predict critical temperature decreases with angular velocity, and instead align qualitatively with lattice simulations (Braguta 2020, 2021, 2022) showing increasing critical temperature with rotation. The work enables future studies incorporating rotation-dependent Polyakov loop potentials and inhomogeneous confinement effects observed in recent lattice data (Braguta 2023).
