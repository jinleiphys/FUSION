# Second Order Twist Contributions to the Balitsky-Kovchegov Equation at small-x: Deterministic and Stochastic Pictures

> We study the second order twist corrections to a toy model of a dipole-dipole interaction in the context of a both deterministic and stochastic effects. This work is done in the high $N_C$ limit in the Bjorken picture. We show that the correction to the second twist terms of the stochastic picture suggest additional importance of the second twist correction in the stochastic model as compared to the deterministic model.

## Key claim

The second-order twist correction in the stochastic (front-fluctuation) picture of the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation is significantly larger relative to the deterministic picture, suggesting that factorization approaches may not properly capture the dynamics in the stochastic regime. The claim is made in the context of a toy model of dipole-dipole scattering in the high \(N_C\) limit and the Bjorken frame, not in full deep inelastic scattering.

## Method

The author computes the forward scattering amplitude for a single dipole (leading twist) and for a system of two dipoles (second-order twist) using an operator product expansion approach in light-cone gauge, following the formalism of Balitsky (1996). The deterministic scattering amplitudes are then extended to include stochastic front fluctuations following the branching-diffusion model of Munier (2014), where the probability of a delayed front is taken as \(P(\delta)= e^{-\gamma\delta}\). Ratios of stochastic to deterministic amplitudes for both twist orders are compared in the geometric scaling region where the dipole size is smaller than the inverse saturation momentum.

## Key numbers

* The deterministic second-order twist amplitude is a factor of \(10^3\) smaller than the first-order twist amplitude in the scaling region (from comparison of Figures 3 and 2, with normalization set so the first-order maximum is 1).
* The ratio of stochastic to deterministic first-order twist amplitudes is proportional to \(\ln(1/(x_\perp^2 Q_{S0}^2))\) (equation 13).
* The ratio of stochastic to deterministic second-order twist amplitudes is proportional to \(1/[\gamma (x_\perp Q_S)^{2\gamma} \ln(x_\perp^2 Q_S^2)]\) (equation 17).
* The ratio of second-order stochastic to first-order stochastic amplitudes is proportional to \(1/\ln(1/(x_\perp^2 Q_S^2))\) (equation 18).

## Context

This work extends the operator product expansion approach of Balitsky (1996) for twist expansions in the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation by incorporating stochastic front fluctuations, following the branching-diffusion model of Munier (2014). It connects the stochastic corrections to the operator representation of the gluon distribution, building on the work of Balitsky (2017) and Ducloué et al. (2017) on deep inelastic scattering. The results suggest that for lower values of \(Q^2\), the stochastic second-order twist corrections may require ladder-diagram contributions analogous to those in the BFKL approach of Kuraev, Lipatov, and Fadin (1977) and the GGM approach of Kovchegov and Levin (2005), motivating further study of the stochastic BK equation and the JIMWLK equation for nuclear fluctuations.
