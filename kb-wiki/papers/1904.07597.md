# Systematic study of proton radioactivity of spherical proton emitters within various versions of proximity potential formalisms

> In this work we present a systematic study of the proton radioactivity half-lives of spherical proton emitters within the Coulomb and proximity potential model. We investigate 28 different versions of the proximity potential formalisms developed for the description of proton radioactivity, $\mathcalα$ decay and heavy particle radioactivity. It is found that 21 of them are not suitable to deal with the proton radioactivity, because the classical turning points $r_{\text{in}}$ cannot be obtained due to the fact that the depth of the total interaction potential between the emitted proton and the daughter nucleus is above the proton radioactivity energy. Among the other 7 versions of the proximity potential formalisms, it is Guo2013 which gives the lowest rms deviation in the description of the experimental half-lives of the known spherical proton emitters. We use this proximity potential formalism to predict the proton radioactivity half-lives of 13 spherical proton emitters, whose proton radioactivity is energetically allowed or observed but not yet quantified, within a factor of 3.71.

## Key claim

Among 28 versions of proximity potential formalisms tested for describing proton radioactivity half-lives of spherical proton emitters, only 7 are suitable because the other 21 produce total interaction potentials whose depth lies above the proton radioactivity energy, preventing the determination of the classical turning point \(r_{\text{in}}\). The Guo2013 formalism yields the lowest root-mean-square deviation from experimental half-lives, and predictions for 13 unquantified spherical proton emitters are given within a factor of 3.71.

## Method

The authors employed the Coulomb and proximity potential model combined with the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) method to calculate proton radioactivity half-lives for spherical proton emitters. They tested 28 different versions of proximity potential formalisms, including Bass73, CW76, Denisov, Denisov DP, Guo2013, Prox.00 DP, and Prox.2010, and compared the calculated half-lives with experimental data from NUBASE2016 and AME2016. The standard deviation \(\sigma = \sqrt{\sum (\log_{10}(T_{1/2}^{\text{calc}}/T_{1/2}^{\text{exp}}))^2 / n}\) was used to quantify agreement.

## Key numbers

- 21 of the 28 proximity potential formalisms were found unsuitable for proton radioactivity because the classical turning point \(r_{\text{in}}\) could not be obtained.
- The Guo2013 formalism gave the lowest standard deviation \(\sigma = 0.569\) for 27 known spherical proton emitters.
- Predicted half-lives for 13 spherical proton emitters are within a factor of 3.71 based on the Guo2013 formalism.
- For \(^{145}\text{Tm}\) (\(Q_p = 1.741\) MeV, \(l_{\text{min}} = 5\)), the experimental half-life is \(3.17 \times 10^{-6}\) s; the Guo2013 calculation is not explicitly listed in the truncated text, but the Denisov calculation gives \(4.21 \times 10^{-7}\) s and the Denisov DP calculation gives \(8.43 \times 10^{-10}\) s.
- For \(^{146}\text{Tm}\) (\(Q_p = 0.891\) MeV, \(l_{\text{min}} = 0\)), the experimental half-life is \(1.55 \times 10^{-1}\) s; the Bass73 calculation gives \(4.69 \times 10^{0}\) s, CW76 gives \(3.12 \times 10^{1}\) s, Denisov gives \(1.87 \times 10^{-1}\) s, and Denisov DP gives \(1.47 \times 10^{-3}\) s.
- For \(^{147}\text{Tm}\) (\(Q_p = 1.059\) MeV, \(l_{\text{min}} = 5\)), the experimental half-life is \(3.74 \times 10^{0}\) s; the Bass73 calculation gives \(6.50 \times 10^{2}\) s, CW76 gives \(2.31 \times 10^{3}\) s, Denisov gives \(1.26 \times 10^{0}\) s, and Denisov DP gives \(2.11 \times 10^{-3}\) s.

## Context

This work extends previous comparative studies of proximity potential formalisms for \(\alpha\) decay and heavy particle radioactivity by Yao (2015), Ghodsi (2016), and Santhosh (2017) to the case of proton radioactivity. The authors find that only 7 of 28 formalisms are applicable, with Guo2013 performing best, consistent with earlier findings that the Coulomb and proximity potential model (CPPM) reproduces experimental data better than the deformed version (CPPMDN) as reported by Santhosh and Sukumaran (2017). The predictions for 13 unquantified spherical proton emitters provide guidance for future experiments and are validated by the Geiger-Nuttall law, which shows linear relationships between \(\log_{10} T_{1/2}\) and \(Q_p^{-1/2}\) for isotopes with the same angular momentum.
