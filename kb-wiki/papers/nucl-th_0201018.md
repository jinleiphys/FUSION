# ESC NN-Potentials in Momentum Space. I. PS-PS Exchange Potentials

> A momentum space representation is derived for the Nijmegen Extended-Soft-Core (ESC) interactions. The partial wave projection of this representation is carried through, in principle for Two-Meson-Exchange (TME) in general. Explicit results for the momentum space partial wave NN-potentials from PS-PS-Exchange are given.

## Key claim

The paper derives a momentum space representation for the Nijmegen Extended-Soft-Core (ESC) interactions, specifically for Two-Meson-Exchange (TME) potentials with Gaussian form factors, showing that the otherwise numerically costly double convolution integrals can be reduced to a finite two-parameter (t,u) integration after analytic elimination of the λ-parameter.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic two-body Bethe-Salpeter equation and reduce it to a three-dimensional Thompson equation, then to the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in Pauli spinor space. They represent the TME potentials as convolutive integrals over momentum Δ with Gaussian form factors and Yukawa functions, then exploit an exponential integral identity to rewrite the integrands and perform the λ-integration analytically, leaving only a double integral over parameters t and u over a finite domain. Explicit partial wave projection formulas on the LSJ basis are developed for pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar (PS-PS) exchange, including adiabatic terms, non-adiabatic corrections, pseudovector vertex corrections, and off-shell 1/M corrections.

## Key numbers

- The fit to pp- and np-data covers laboratory kinetic energies 0 ≤ T_lab ≤ 350 MeV, achieving χ²/N_data = 1.15 using 20 physical parameters (coupling constants and cut-off masses).
- The effective field theory restricts to mesons with mass M ≤ 1 GeV/c², based on the chiral symmetry breaking scale Λ_χSB² ≈ 1 GeV².
- The Gaussian form factors suppress high momentum transfers strongly, with the (t,u)-integration domain effectively finite due to exponentials exp(-m₁² t) and exp(-m₂² u) in the integrands.

## Context

This work extends the Nijmegen soft-core models previously developed in configuration space for nucleon-nucleon (Rijken 1993, Rijken and Stoks 1996a, 1996b, Stoks and Rijken 1997) and hyperon-nucleon (Rijken 1999) scattering. The momentum space representation enables direct application in momentum space computations, avoiding the problematic numerical Fourier transformation from configuration space. The companion paper II (Rijken and Nagata 2000) extends the representation to Meson-Pair-Exchange potentials and presents the full fit to NN data.
