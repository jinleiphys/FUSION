# Anomalous Chiral Transport in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Chiral anomaly is a very fundamental aspect of quantum theories with chiral fermion, from the Standard Model to supersymmetric field theories or even string theories. How such microscopic anomaly manifests itself in a macroscopic many-body system with chiral fermions, is a highly nontrivial question that has recently attracted significant interest. As it turns out, unusual transport currents can be induced by chiral anomaly under suitable conditions in such systems, with the notable example of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) where a vector current (e.g. electric current) is generated along an external magnetic field. The CME has been enthusiastically studied in two very different physical systems: the Dirac and Weyl semimetals in condensed matter physics as well as the quark-gluon plasma in heavy ion collisions. In this contribution, we report the latest theoretical and experimental status for the search of CME in heavy ion collisions.

# Anomalous Chiral Transport in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME), where an electric current is generated along an external magnetic field in a chiral fermion system, can be observed in heavy ion collisions, but its experimental signal remains entangled with background correlations. The paper reports that quantitative predictions from the Anomalous-Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD) framework, using realistic initial conditions and magnetic field lifetime, are consistent with existing measurements from RHIC, though subject to current theoretical uncertainties.

## Method
The authors developed the Anomalous-Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD) simulation framework, which couples data-validated VISHNU simulations for bulk QGP evolution with anomalous fluid dynamic equations for chiral currents of up and down flavor quarks. The framework incorporates second-order viscous corrections for normal viscous currents and treats the magnetic-field-driven anomalous transport that distinguishes left-handed from right-handed currents with opposite sign. The CME-induced charge separation signal was computed by evolving initial chirality imbalances (estimated from gluonic field fluctuations) under realistic magnetic field profiles from simulations, and the resulting H-correlations were compared with STAR experimental data.

## Key numbers
- The magnetic field strength in isobaric collisions (ZrZr versus RuRu) differs by about 10%, leading to a predicted shift of about 15% in CME-driven correlations in peripheral collisions between the two systems.
- The AVFD-predicted CME signal with realistic initial conditions and magnetic field lifetime is quantitatively consistent with measurements from 200 AGeV AuAu collisions at RHIC.
- A simple phenomenological formula for the beam energy dependence of the CME signal, H ∝ [1+tanh((√s−√s_l)/Δ_l)] × [1+tanh((√s_h−√s)/Δ_h)], provides a reasonable fit to RHIC Beam Energy Scan data and the ALICE data point.
- The fitting trend implies that at LHC √s = 5.05 TeV collisions, the CME signal may become negligibly small.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework for anomalous chiral transport first formalized by Son and Surowka (2009), who derived the fluid dynamic equations for anomalous currents. It builds upon earlier attempts to apply this framework to heavy ion collisions by Hirono et al. (2014) and Yin and Liao (2015). The AVFD simulations enable quantitative comparison with experimental measurements from STAR (2014, Beam Energy Scan) and ALICE (2013), and provide predictions for the planned isobaric collision experiment at RHIC described by Skokov et al. (2016). The results also connect to studies of the Chiral Magnetic Wave by Burnier et al. (2011) and vorticity-induced transport by Kharzeev et al. (2010).
