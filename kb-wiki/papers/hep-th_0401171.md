# Short-range Interaction and Nonrelativistic Phi**4 Theory in Various Dimensions

> We employ the effective field theory method to systematically study the short-range interaction in two-body sector in 2, 3 and 4 spacetime dimensions, respectively. The phi**4 theory is taken as a specific example and matched onto the nonrelativistic effective theory to one loop level. An exact, Lorentz-invariant expression for the S-wave amplitude is presented, from which the nonperturbative information can be easily extracted. We pay particular attention to the renormalization group analysis in the 3 dimensions, and show that relativistic effects qualitatively change the renormalization group flow of higher-dimensional operators. There is one ancient claim that triviality of the 4-dimensional phi**4 theory can be substantiated in the nonrelativistic limit. We illustrate that this assertion arises from treating the interaction between two nonrelativistic particles as literally zero-range, which is incompatible with the Uncertainty Principle. The S-wave effective range in this theory is identified to be approximately 16/3pi times the Compton wavelength.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the claimed triviality of 4D φ⁴ theory in the nonrelativistic limit, previously asserted by Beg and Furlong, arises from incorrectly treating the two-body interaction as literally zero-range; this treatment is incompatible with the Uncertainty Principle, which sets the shortest meaningful distance in nonrelativistic quantum mechanics at the Compton wavelength.

## Method

The author employs effective field theory (EFT) methods to systematically study short-range two-body interactions in 2, 3, and 4 spacetime dimensions, using φ⁴ theory as a concrete example matched onto a nonrelativistic EFT at one-loop level. The bubble chain diagrams in the nonrelativistic EFT are summed analytically to obtain an exact, Lorentz-invariant expression for the S-wave scattering amplitude, with relativistic corrections implemented by retaining the ∂²/∂t² operator in the Lagrangian rather than performing field redefinitions.

## Key numbers

- The S-wave effective range in 4D φ⁴ theory is identified to be approximately 16/3π times the Compton wavelength.
- The one-loop integral I₀ in 4D with cutoff regularization gives a linearly divergent term −(m/4π²)Λ and a finite term (m/8π)√(−2mE−iϵ).
- In dimensional regularization with minimal subtraction, the one-loop integral I₀ in 4D yields (m/8π)√(−2mE−iϵ), with no pole at D=4.
- The tree-level matching gives C₀ = λ/(4m²) + O(λ²) and C₂ = 0 + O(λ²), holding for arbitrary spacetime dimensions.

## Context

This work extends the EFT approach to short-range interactions previously applied to few-nucleon systems (Beane, 2000) and cold dilute Bose gas (Braaten, 1996) to lower-dimensional cases, and provides a systematic treatment of relativistic effects that were only incorporated to first order in prior work. The paper directly contradicts the claim of Beg and Furlong (circa 1984) that triviality of 4D φ⁴ theory can be substantiated in the nonrelativistic limit, showing that their argument fails because it incorrectly treats the interaction as zero-range rather than short-range with range ∼1/m. The exact Lorentz-invariant amplitude enables nonperturbative insights into 2D and 3D scattering where perturbative expansions suffer from zero-momentum singularities.
