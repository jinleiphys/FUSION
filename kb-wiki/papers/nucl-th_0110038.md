# Singlet-to-triplet ratio in the deuteron breakup reaction $pd\to pnp$ at 585 MeV

> Available experimental data on the exclusive $pd\to pnp$ reaction at 585 MeV show a narrow peak in the proton-neutron final-state interaction region. It was supposed previously, on the basis of a phenomenological analysis of the shape of this peak, that the final spin-singlet $pn$ state provided about one third of the observed cross section. By comparing the absolute value of the measured cross section with that of $pd$ elastic scattering using the Fäldt-Wilkin extrapolation theorem, it is shown here that the $pd\to pnp$ data can be explained mainly by the spin-triplet final state with a singlet admixture of a few percent. The smallness of the singlet contribution is compatible with existing $pN\to pNπ$ data and the one-pion exchange mechanism of the $pd\to pnp$ reaction.

## Key claim

By comparing the absolute cross section of the exclusive $pd\to pnp$ reaction at 585 MeV with that of $pd$ elastic scattering using the Fäldt-Wilkin extrapolation theorem, the authors show that the data can be explained mainly by a spin-triplet final state with a singlet admixture of only a few percent, contradicting the earlier phenomenological estimate of about one third from the peak shape alone.

## Method

The authors used the Fäldt-Wilkin extrapolation theorem to relate the triplet $pn$ final-state interaction cross section in $pd\to pnp$ to the measured $pd\to dp$ elastic scattering cross section at the same beam energy and similar scattering angle. They then performed a five-dimensional integration of the theoretical triplet cross section over the experimental angular and momentum acceptances using Gaussian smearing parameters from the original SREL experiment. By comparing the smeared triplet cross section plus a variable singlet admixture to the measured five-fold differential cross section, they extracted the singlet-to-triplet ratio $\zeta = |A_s|^2/|A_t|^2$ via a $\chi^2$ analysis.

## Key numbers

- The $pd\to dp$ elastic cross section at $T_p=590$ MeV and $\theta^*=92.7^\circ$ is $\left( 30.4 { \pm 0.8(\text{stat.}) \atop \pm 2.9(\text{syst.})} \right) \mu$b/sr (Alder et al.).
- For one data set, a pure triplet contribution ($\zeta=0$) gives $\chi^2=0.7$; $\zeta=0.02$ gives $\chi^2=0.9$; $\zeta=0.05$ gives $\chi^2=1.8$; $\zeta=0.10$ gives $\chi^2=4.6$.
- For the other data set, ($\zeta$, $\chi^2$) values are: (0.0, 2.4), (0.02, 2.1), (0.05, 2.3), (0.10, 4.0).
- Scaling the measured cross section by factors 0.85 to 1.15 yields $\zeta$ values for minimum $\chi^2$ ranging from $+0.035$ to $-0.030$, with uncertainties $\Delta\zeta$ of order a few percent.
- The spin-statistical factor of $\zeta=1/3$ assumed in the earlier analysis (Witten et al.) overestimates the cross section in the peak region by a factor of 2.5 to 3.

## Context

This work reanalyzes the exclusive $pd\to pnp$ data from Witten et al. (SREL, 585 MeV), which had previously been interpreted using the Migdal-Watson approximation to yield a singlet fraction of about one third based on the peak shape alone. The present result, a singlet admixture of a few percent, is consistent with the small singlet-to-triplet ratios observed in $pp\to pn\pi^+$ reactions at COSY, CELSIUS, and LAMPF (analyzed by Fäldt, Wilkin, and collaborators), and with the theoretical expectation of $\frac{1}{27}$ from the one-pion exchange triangle diagram with $\Delta$-isobar excitation. The authors propose that a new measurement of the $\vec{p}\vec{d}\to pnp$ reaction at the ANKE spectrometer at COSY-Jülich at beam energies of 0.5-2.5 GeV could put more stringent limits on the singlet-to-triplet ratio.
