# Neutrinoless Double-Beta Decay and Realistic Shell Model

> We report on the calculation of the neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix element for 76Ge within the framework of the realistic shell model. The effective shell-model Hamiltonian and the two-body transition operator describing the decay are derived by way of many-body perturbation theory. Particular attention is focused on the role played by the so-called Pauli blocking effect in the derivation of the effective operator.

## Key claim

The realistic shell-model calculation of the neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix element for ⁷⁶Ge, using an effective operator derived from many-body perturbation theory, shows that including Pauli blocking diagrams reduces the matrix element by about 10%, mainly through the Gamow-Teller contribution.

## Method

The authors start from the high-precision CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, softened via the V_low-k approach with a cutoff of 2.6 fm⁻¹, and derive an effective shell-model Hamiltonian and a two-body 0νββ decay operator using many-body perturbation theory up to third and second order, respectively. The model space consists of the four orbitals (1p₃/₂, 1p₁/₂, 0f₅/₂, 0g₉/₂) outside a ⁵⁶Ni core, and the Pauli blocking effect is incorporated by converting three-body connected diagrams into density-dependent two-body operators summed over occupied model-space orbitals.

## Key numbers

- The bare-operator NME for ⁷⁶Ge is 3.40, compared to 2.96 (Menendez 2009) and 3.25 (Senkov 2014), with the largest discrepancy not exceeding 15%.
- Using the second-order effective operator without Pauli blocking: M⁰ν_GT = 2.39, M⁰ν_F = -0.64, total M⁰ν = 2.79.
- Including Pauli blocking diagrams: M⁰ν_GT = 2.03, M⁰ν_F = -0.66, total M⁰ν = 2.44, a reduction of about 10%.
- The density-dependent Pauli blocking diagrams (a) and (b) for the (f₅/₂,f₅/₂) → (g₉/₂,g₉/₂) configuration yield values of 0.157 and -1.096, respectively, while the Pauli-violating diagrams (a₁) and (b₁) give -0.337 and 0.335.

## Context

This work extends the realistic shell-model approach previously applied by the same group to Gamow-Teller and 2νββ decays of nuclei from A=48 to A=136 (Coraggio 2017, Coraggio 2019), where no empirical quenching of g_A was needed. It builds on the pioneering effective-operator method of Kuo and coworkers (Song 1989, Staudt 1992) and the more recent work of Holt and Engel (2013). The results enable more reliable extraction of the effective Majorana neutrino mass from the ongoing GERDA and CDEX-1 experiments on ⁷⁶Ge.
