# Momentum transport away from a jet in an expanding nuclear medium

> We study the transport dynamics of momenta deposited from jets in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. Assuming that the high-energy partons traverse expanding quark-gluon fluids and are subject to lose their energy and momentum, we simulate dijet asymmetric events by solving relativistic hydrodynamic equations numerically without linearization in the fully (3+1)-dimensional coordinate. Mach cones are formed and strongly broadened by radial flow of the background medium. As a result, the yield of low-$p_{T}$ particles increases at large angles from the jet axis and compensates the dijet momentum imbalance inside the jet-cone. This provides an intimate link between the medium excitation by jets and results in dijet asymmetric events observed by the CMS Collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the low-\(p_T\) particle enhancement at large angles from the jet axis, which compensates dijet momentum imbalance in asymmetric dijet events observed by the CMS Collaboration, arises from energy and momentum deposited by jets and transported by collective flow in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). This claim is hedged by the use of a simplified equation of state (massless ideal gas) and a simple collisional energy loss model, with the authors noting that the basic feature of momentum transport away from the jet axis due to radial expansion does not change when employing a lattice QCD equation of state.

## Method

The authors solved (3+1)-dimensional relativistic ideal hydrodynamic equations with source terms representing energy and momentum deposition from a back-to-back pair of massless partons (initial energy 200 GeV each) traversing an expanding QGP fluid. The hydrodynamic equations were solved numerically without linearization in Milne coordinates using a new high-precision scheme, with initial conditions for central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies based on a Monte Carlo Glauber model and a Bjorken-like rapidity profile. The parton energy loss was modeled using collisional energy loss in the local rest frame of the fluid, and the Cooper-Frye formula was applied at a fixed freeze-out proper time to obtain particle momentum distributions.

## Key numbers

- Initial parton energy: \(p^{0}_{a} (\tau=0) = 200\:{\rm GeV}\) for each parton in the back-to-back pair.
- Initial proper time for hydrodynamics: \(\tau_0 = 0.6\:{\rm fm}/c\).
- Freeze-out proper time: \(\tau_{f}=9.6\:{\rm fm}/c\).
- Energy loss parameters: strong coupling \(\alpha_{s}=0.3\), number of active flavors \(n_{f}=3\), and energy loss strength parameter \(A=15\).
- Jet cone radius used for analysis: \(\Delta R = 0.8\).
- Transverse momentum ranges analyzed: \(0\)–\(0.5\), \(0.5\)–\(1\), \(1\)–\(2\), \(2\)–\(4\), \(4\)–\(8\) GeV/\(c\), and \(p_{T}>8\:{\rm GeV}/c\).
- The total \(\langle \Slash{p}^{||}_{T} \rangle\) for the whole system vanishes within uncertainties for all values of the dijet asymmetry ratio \(A_{J}\), demonstrating energy-momentum conservation.
- Out-of-cone (\(\Delta R \geq 0.8\)) contribution to \(\langle \Slash{p}^{||}_{T} \rangle\) is positive and comes only from particles with \(p_{T}<2\:{\rm GeV}/c\).

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic studies of Mach cones in a static or uniform medium (e.g., CasalderreySolana (2004), Neufeld (2008), Qin (2009)) by incorporating a fully (3+1)-dimensional expanding QGP background and solving the nonlinear equations without linearization. It directly addresses the dijet asymmetry observed by the CMS Collaboration (Chatrchyan 2011) and provides a dynamical explanation for the low-\(p_T\) particle enhancement at large angles that balances the momentum imbalance, linking medium response to jet quenching with experimental observables. The results enable future extraction of QGP transport coefficients and sound velocity by focusing on low-momentum particles at large angles from the jet axis.
