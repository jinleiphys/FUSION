# Chiral and $U(1)_A$ restoration for the scalar/pseudoscalar meson nonets

> We analyze the restoration pattern of the members of the scalar and pseudoscalar meson nonets under chiral $O(4)$ and $U(1)_A$ symmetries. For that purpose, we exploit QCD Ward Identities (WI), which allow one to relate susceptibilities with quark condensates, as well as susceptibility differences with meson vertices. In addition, we consider the low-energy realization of QCD provided by $U(3)$ Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) at finite temperature to perform a full analysis of the different correlators involved. Our analysis suggests $U(1)_A$ partner restoration if chiral symmetry partners are also degenerated. This is also confirmed by the ChPT analysis when the light chiral limit is reached. Partner degeneration for the $I=1/2$ sector, the behavior of $I=0$ mixing and the temperature scaling of meson masses predicted by WI are also studied. Special attention is paid to the connection of our results with recent lattice analyses.

## Key claim

The paper argues that if chiral $O(4)$ symmetry partners become degenerate (as expected near the chiral transition), then $U(1)_A$ partners must also become degenerate, implying an $O(4) \times U(1)_A$ restoration pattern; this conclusion is derived from QCD Ward Identities and holds in the ideal chiral restoration regime, though it may also apply approximately in a crossover scenario.

## Method

The authors exploit QCD Ward Identities (WI) to relate pseudoscalar and scalar susceptibilities to quark condensates and to susceptibility differences involving meson vertices. They also perform a full analysis using $U(3)$ Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) at finite temperature to provide a model-independent hadronic realization of the WI and to study the temperature behavior of the relevant observables for chiral and $U(1)_A$ restoration, including new explicit expressions for scalar susceptibilities derived for the first time in this formalism.

## Key numbers

* The crossover transition temperature for physical quark masses ($N_f=2+1$) is about $T_c \sim 155$ MeV at vanishing baryon density.
* The relation $\chi_{5,disc}(T) = \frac{1}{\hat m ^2}\chi_{top}(T)$ connects the disconnected pseudoscalar susceptibility to the topological susceptibility, where $\hat m$ is the light quark mass.
* The mixed light-strange pseudoscalar susceptibility satisfies $\chi_P^{ls}(T) = -2\frac{\hat m}{m_s} \chi_{5,disc}(T) = -\frac{2}{\hat m m_s}\chi_{top}(T)$, linking $O(4)$ and $U(1)_A$ restoration.
* At $T=0$ and away from $T_c$, $\chi_{5,disc} \sim 1/{\hat m}$ and $\chi_{top} \sim {\hat m}$ in the chiral limit $\hat m \rightarrow 0^+$.

## Context

This work extends the $SU(2)$ study of Nicola (2013) where Ward Identities described $\sigma$-$\pi$ degeneration, and the $U(3)$ WI analysis of Nicola and de Elvira (2016) relating quark condensates and pseudoscalar susceptibilities. It also connects with recent lattice analyses by Buchoff (2013) and Bhattacharya (2014) suggesting $U(1)_A$ restoration above $T_c$, and by Aoki (2012), Cossu (2013), and Tomiya (2016) finding $U(1)_A$ restoration at or near $T_c$ in the chiral limit. The results enable future lattice checks of the predicted WI relations and clarify the nature of the scalar meson nonet through the restoration pattern.
