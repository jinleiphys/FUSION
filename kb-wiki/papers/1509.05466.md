# Dileptons and Chiral Symmetry Restoration

> We report on recent work relating the medium effects observed in dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions to potential signals of chiral symmetry restoration. The key connection remains the approach to spectral function degeneracy between the vector-isovector channel with its chiral partner, the axialvector-isovector channel. Several approaches are discussed to elaborate this connection, namely QCD and Weinberg sum rules with input for chiral order parameters from lattice QCD, and chiral hadronic theory to directly evaluate the medium effects of the axialvector channel and the pertinent pion decay constant as function of temperature. A pattern emerges where the chiral mass splitting between rho and a_1 burns off and is accompanied by a strong broadening of the spectral distributions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the medium effects observed in dilepton spectra from heavy-ion collisions are compatible with chiral symmetry restoration, but the claim is hedged by the condition that this compatibility is established through a combined broadening and "burning-off" of the chiral mass splitting between the rho and a_1 mesons, rather than through a simple mass shift of the rho meson alone.

## Method

The authors used two complementary theoretical frameworks. First, they applied QCD sum rules and Weinberg sum rules, using lattice QCD input for the temperature dependence of the quark condensate and pion decay constant, to test whether a previously constructed in-medium rho spectral function (which successfully describes dilepton data) is compatible with a degenerate vector-axialvector spectral function. Second, they employed a Massive Yang-Mills chiral Lagrangian approach to compute the in-medium vector and axialvector spectral functions in a hot pion gas using the Matsubara formalism, including unitarity cuts, Landau cuts, vertex corrections, and tadpole diagrams.

## Key numbers

* The pseudo-critical temperature for chiral restoration at vanishing baryon chemical potential is T_pc approximately 155 MeV.
* The energy density at the pseudo-critical temperature is epsilon_pc approximately 0.3 GeV, comparable to cold nuclear matter at twice saturation density.
* The chiral condensate at T_pc is still at about half of its vacuum value.
* In the Massive Yang-Mills approach, the rho resonance undergoes a moderate broadening of up to approximately 50 MeV at T = 160 MeV.
* In the same approach, the pion decay constant and scalar condensate drop by up to 15-20 percent at T = 160 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous successful description of dilepton spectra from experiments at SIS, SPS, and RHIC (HADES, CERES, NA60, STAR) using a strongly broadened rho spectral function. It connects these phenomenological findings to the fundamental question of chiral symmetry restoration by testing compatibility with lattice QCD results for chiral order parameters. The sum rule analysis builds on the finite-temperature extension of Weinberg sum rules by Kapusta (1993) and the QCD sum rule technique of Shifman (1978), while the chiral Lagrangian approach extends the Massive Yang-Mills framework of Gomm (1984) and Bando (1987). The results enable future work including baryonic contributions and suggest a general hadronic mechanism where chiral restoration proceeds via broadening and mass-splitting reduction rather than ground-state mass shifts.
