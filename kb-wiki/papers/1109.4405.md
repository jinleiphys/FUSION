# Viscous photons in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Theoretical studies of the production of real thermal photons in relativistic heavy ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) are performed. The space-time evolution of the colliding system is modelled using MUSIC, a 3+1D relativistic hydrodynamic simulation, using both its ideal and viscous versions. The inclusive spectrum and its azimuthal angular anisotropy are studied separately, and the relative contributions of the different photon sources are highlighted. It is shown that the photon v2 coefficient is especially sensitive to the details of the microscopic dynamics like the equation of state, the ratio of shear viscosity over entropy density, eta/s, and to the morphology of the initial state.

## Key claim

The photon elliptic flow coefficient $v_2$ is especially sensitive to the details of microscopic dynamics including the equation of state, the ratio of shear viscosity over entropy density $\eta/s$, and the morphology of the initial state; however, the viscous corrections to the distribution functions can become perturbatively unreliable for photon energies of 3 GeV and above, where up to 80% of high-temperature fluid cells show $\delta f/f_0 \geq 1$.

## Method

The space-time evolution of Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV was modeled using MUSIC, a 3+1D relativistic hydrodynamic simulation, in both ideal and viscous (Israel-Stewart formalism) versions. Photon emission rates from the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) were calculated using Compton and quark-antiquark annihilation processes with out-of-equilibrium distribution functions $f_0 + \delta f$, while hadronic gas photon rates were computed using a Massive Yang-Mills model with mesonic degrees of freedom $\{\pi, K, \rho, K^*, a_1\}$. Both smooth averaged initial conditions (AICs) and fluctuating initial conditions (FICs) were employed, with the viscous pressure tensor initialized to zero at $\tau_0 = 0.2$ fm/c.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, impact parameter $b=4.47$ fm (0-20% centrality)
- Initial time $\tau_0 = 0.2$ fm/c; freeze-out energy density $0.12$ GeV/fm$^3$ (approximately $T=137$ MeV)
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $\eta/s = 1/4\pi$ used for viscous calculations
- For a photon energy of 2 GeV, approximately 20% of fluid cells have $\delta f/f_0 \geq 1$ at early times ($T>250$ MeV)
- For a photon energy of 3 GeV, 80% of high-temperature cells have $\delta f/f_0 \geq 1$ and approximately 30% have $\delta f/f_0 \geq 2$
- The difference between ideal and viscous total thermal photon spectra grows to approximately 100% at $p_T=4$ GeV
- Ensembles of 50 events for FIC studies yield uncertainties of order 5% on thermal photon spectra and 15% on thermal photon $v_2$

## Context

This work extends earlier photon production calculations by incorporating viscous hydrodynamic evolution and event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions, building on the hydrodynamic framework of Schenke (2010) and the fluctuating initial condition approach of Schenke (2011). The results show that extracting shear viscosity from photon spectra alone will be difficult, but that photon $v_2$ provides a sensitive probe of the equation of state and $\eta/s$, complementing hadronic flow analyses. The study enables future work to include all photon sources in a theoretically consistent way with full viscous corrections, and to use the complete leading-order QGP photon rates of Arnold (2001) rather than the subset of diagrams used here.
