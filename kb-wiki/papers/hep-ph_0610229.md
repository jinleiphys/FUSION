# Scalar meson properties from D-meson decays

> Decay amplitudes of D(D_s)->f0(980)X, X=pi, K, are compared to experimental branching ratios with the aim of singling out the poorly known D->f0(980) transition form factor in these amplitudes. Since the other elements of the amplitudes are either calculable in an effective QCD theory using operator product expansion or are known from experiment (e.g. the pion and kaon decay constants), we can take advantage of these reactions to constrain the transition form factors obtained in relativistic quark models. In these models, the f0(980) wavefunction requires an unknown size parameter for both its non-strange ubar u(dbar d) and strange sbar s components, which we fit to the D(D_s) decay data.

## Key claim

The authors use experimental branching ratios of $D(D_s)\to f_0(980)X$ decays to constrain the unknown size parameters of the $f_0(980)$ wavefunction in relativistic quark models, but note that the results are strongly dependent on the poorly known $f_0\to\pi^+\pi^-$ and $f_0\to K^+K^-$ branching fractions and that large experimental uncertainties yield sizable parameter errors.

## Method

The decay amplitudes for $D(D_s)\to f_0(980)X$ ($X=\pi,K$) are constructed within the naive QCD factorization approach at tree level, neglecting hard-scattering effects and annihilation topologies. The amplitudes are products of a short-distance part (Wilson coefficients at scale $\mu=m_c$) and hadronic matrix elements including the $D\to f_0(980)$ transition form factor $F_0^{D\to f_0}(m_P^2)$, which is computed from relativistic quark models where the $f_0(980)$ wavefunction has Gaussian spatial components with size parameters $\alpha_n$ (non-strange) and $\alpha_s$ (strange) and a mixing angle $\theta_m$. These three parameters are fitted to the experimental branching ratios listed in Table 1.

- $\mathcal{B}(D^0 \to f_0 \bar{K}^0) \times \mathcal{B}(f_0 \to \pi^+ \pi^-) = (3.2 \pm 0.9)\times 10^{-3}$ from ARGUS and $(2.5^{+0.8}_{-0.5})\times 10^{-3}$ from CLEO
- $\mathcal{B}(D_s^+ \to f_0 \pi^+) \times \mathcal{B}(f_0 \to \pi^+ \pi^-) = (5.7 \pm 1.7)\times 10^{-3}$ from E791 and $(9.5 \pm 2.7)\times 10^{-3}$ from FOCUS

- The mixing angle $\theta_m$ has been previously estimated from $D_s^+\to f_0(980)\pi^+$ and $D_s^+\to \phi\pi^+$ decays to cover $20^\circ \lesssim \theta \lesssim 40^\circ$ and $140^\circ \lesssim \theta \lesssim 160^\circ$

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic quark model calculations of $B\to f_0(980)$ and $D\to f_0(980)$ transition form factors by the same authors (El-Bennich et al., 2006, "mesonichp"), using both a dispersion relation approach and Covariant Light-Front Dynamics. The constituent structure of scalar mesons remains controversial, with competing descriptions as orbitally excited $\bar qq$ states (Tornqvist and Roos, 1995), four-quark configurations (Black et al., 1998), or molecular states (Weinstein and Isgur, 1990); the present analysis assumes a $\bar qq$ picture with non-strange and strange components. The results enable predictions for $B\to f_0(980)$ and $D\to f_0(980)$ transition form factors at space- and time-like momenta, but the authors emphasize that higher-statistics data from three-body $D$-decays are needed to reduce the large parametric uncertainties.
