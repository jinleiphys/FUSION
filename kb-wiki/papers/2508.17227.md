# Effects of Geometric configuration in relativistic isobaric collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV

> In this work, we present a study on the effects of nuclear deformation ($β_2$,$β_3$) and surface diffuseness ($a$) on the charged hadron multiplicity ($N_{\mathrm{ch}}$) and elliptic flow ($v_2$), obtained in symmetric isobaric collisions of ${}^{96}_{44}\mathrm{Ru} + {}^{96}_{44}\mathrm{Ru}$ and ${}^{96}_{40}\mathrm{Zr} + {}^{96}_{40}\mathrm{Zr}$. The two extreme configurations (tip-tip and body-body) were used to determine the correlation between the final state observables and initial geometry using the HYDJET++ model. The octupole deformation parameter ($β_3$) enhances $N_{\mathrm{ch}}$ in central tip-tip Zr+Zr collisions and suppresses it in peripheral ones. In mid-central to peripheral body-body Zr+Zr collisions, $β_3$ leads to a reduction in charged hadron production. Surface-diffuseness ($a$), along with quadrapole deformation ($β_2$), also shows a significant impact on multiplicity and elliptic flow. The octupole deformation enhances elliptic flow in Zr's body-body collisions. Results are compared with the STAR blind-analysis data where available.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nuclear deformation parameters (quadrupole β₂ and octupole β₃) and surface diffuseness (a) produce systematic but modest modifications to charged-hadron multiplicity and elliptic flow in isobaric collisions, with the effects being configuration-dependent and most visible in specific centrality ranges rather than universally across all collision geometries.

## Method

The authors used the HYDJET++ Monte Carlo event generator, which superposes a soft hydro-type state and a hard partonic state, to simulate symmetric collisions of ⁹⁶Ru + ⁹⁶Ru and ⁹⁶Zr + ⁹⁶Zr at √s_NN = 200 GeV. They implemented a modified Woods-Saxon density profile incorporating quadrupole (β₂) and octupole (β₃) deformation parameters, and considered two extreme geometric configurations (tip-tip and body-body) along with a spherical reference case, using three parameter sets from prior literature. Centrality classes were determined independently for each configuration to account for orientation-dependent overlap geometries.

## Key numbers

- Surface diffuseness parameter a for Zr varies from 0.46 fm (case-1) to 0.536 fm (case-2), while Ru varies from 0.46 fm (case-1) to 0.485 fm (case-2)
- Quadrupole deformation β₂ for Ru is 0.158 (case-1) and 0.154 (case-2); for Zr it is 0.080 (case-1) and 0.062 (case-2)
- Octupole deformation β₃ = 0.202 is included for Zr in case-2 only; Ru has β₃ = 0 in all cases
- Nuclear radius parameters R: Ru = 5.085 fm (case-1), 5.063 fm (case-2), 5.067 fm (case-3); Zr = 5.020 fm (case-1), 4.960 fm (case-2), 4.965 fm (case-3)
- In central (0-5%) body-body collisions with case-1 parameters, Zr yields higher charged-hadron multiplicity than Ru at η=0 due to Zr's smaller β₂
- In peripheral (50-60%) tip-tip collisions with case-2, Zr's octupole deformation (β₃ = 0.202) suppresses charged-particle production below what would be expected from its larger surface diffuseness alone
- The ratio P(N_ch)^Ru/P(N_ch)^Zr in spherical (case-3) configuration rises at high multiplicities (N_ch > 300) and shows a dip in peripheral collisions (N_ch = 0-50)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nuclear deformation effects in isobaric collisions motivated by the STAR collaboration's blind analysis (STAR 2021), which found that ⁹⁶Ru+⁹⁶Ru and ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr systems exhibited different background signals due to variations in charged-particle production and flow harmonics. The study uses deformation parameters from Deng (2016), van der Schee (2023), and Xu (2021) to systematically probe how quadrupole and octupole deformations affect observables, enabling better isolation of possible Chiral Magnetic Effect signals in future analyses. The results show that surface diffuseness can play a more dominant role than quadrupole deformation in certain configurations, and that octupole deformation introduces asymmetry effects that reduce effective overlap in peripheral collisions.
