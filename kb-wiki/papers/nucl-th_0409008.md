# Electromagnetic properties of diquarks

> Diquark correlations play an important role in hadron physics. The properties of diquarks can be obtained from the corresponding bound state equation. Using a model for the effective quark-quark interaction that has proved successful in the light meson sector, we solve the scalar diquark Bethe-Salpeter equations and use the obtained Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes to compute the diquarks' electromagnetic form factors. The scalar ud diquark charge radius is about 8% larger than the pion charge radius, indicating that these diquarks are somewhat larger in size than the corresponding mesons. We also provide analytic fits for the form factor over a moderate range in Q^2, which may be useful, for example, in building quark-diquark models of nucleons.

## Key claim

The scalar ud diquark charge radius is about 8% larger than the pion charge radius, indicating that these diquarks are somewhat larger in size than the corresponding mesons. This result is obtained within a specific model for the effective quark-quark interaction that has proved successful in the light meson sector, and the calculation is performed using the rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations.

## Method

The author solves the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for scalar diquarks in the ladder truncation, using a phenomenological effective interaction that was previously fitted to reproduce the pion decay constant and the chiral condensate. The resulting diquark Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes are then used in the impulse approximation to compute the electromagnetic form factors, with the dressed quark-photon vertex obtained from the inhomogeneous ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation to satisfy the Ward-Takahashi identity. The calculation is performed for ud, us, and ds scalar diquarks in the colour anti-triplet configuration.

## Key numbers

- The scalar ud diquark charge radius squared is 0.51 fm^2 (for all parameter sets), corresponding to r_ud = 0.71 fm, which is 8% larger than the pion charge radius r_pi = 0.66 fm
- The ud diquark mass ranges from 0.688 GeV to 0.988 GeV depending on the interaction parameters (omega from 0.30 to 0.50 GeV, D adjusted to keep D*omega approximately constant at (0.72 GeV)^3)
- The us diquark mass is 1.07 GeV (for omega=0.42 GeV, D=0.888 GeV^2) and the ds diquark mass is the same within numerical uncertainty
- The ud diquark form factor is best fitted by [M^2/(Q^2 + M^2)]^a with M ≈ 0.79 GeV and a = 1.4, with chi^2 of 0.001 over the range -0.3 GeV^2 < Q^2 < 1.5 GeV^2
- The pion form factor is well fitted by a monopole with M^2 = 0.545 GeV^2 (close to the rho meson mass squared in this model)

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach, previously applied successfully to light pseudoscalar and vector mesons by Maris and Tandy (1999), to the calculation of diquark electromagnetic properties. The results disagree with earlier Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model calculations by Weiss et al. (1993), which suggested that the pion and ud scalar diquark form factors are almost identical and gave a significantly smaller charge radius than the measured pion charge radius. The calculated diquark form factors and charge radii can be used to improve quark-diquark models of nucleons and other baryons, and the author estimates that a pentaquark in a diquark-diquark-antiquark configuration would have a radius of approximately 1 fm, about 15% larger than the proton.
