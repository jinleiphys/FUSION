# Renormalization of the one-pion-exchange interaction

> A renormalization scheme for the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction based on a subtracted T-matrix equation is proposed and applied to the one-pion-exchange potential supplemented by contact interactions. The singlet and triplet scattering lengths are given to fix the renormalized strengths of the contact interactions. With only one scaling parameter ($μ$), the results show an overall very good agreement with neutron-proton data, particularly for the observables related to the triplet channel. The agreement is qualitative in the $^1S_0$ channel. Between the low-energy NN observables we have examined, the mixing parameter of the $^3S_1-^3D_1$ states is the most sensible to the scale. The scheme is renormalization group invariant for $μ\to\infty$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the leading-order nucleon-nucleon interaction (one-pion-exchange potential plus a contact term) can be fully renormalized using a single subtraction scale μ, and that the scheme is renormalization-group invariant only in the limit μ → ∞, not at the finite scale of the rho meson mass (~4 fm⁻¹) originally suggested by Weinberg (1990).

## Method

The authors derived a subtracted T-matrix integral equation where the subtraction is performed at energy −μ² in the center-of-mass system. They set the T-matrix at the subtraction point equal to the one-pion-exchange potential plus a delta-function contact interaction, with the renormalized strengths of the contact terms fixed by the singlet and triplet scattering lengths. The coupled-channel equations for the ³S₁–³D₁ states and the single-channel equation for the ¹S₀ state were solved numerically in momentum space using a Gaussian mesh with up to 100 points, for various values of μ ranging from 4 to 60 fm⁻¹.

## Key numbers

- Singlet scattering length fixed to a_s = −23.7 fm; triplet scattering length fixed to a_t = 5.4 fm
- For μ = 4 fm⁻¹: singlet effective range r_{0,s} = 1.332 fm; triplet effective range r_{0,t} = 1.364 fm; deuteron binding energy B_D = 1.977 MeV; D/S ratio η_D = 0.02808
- For μ = 10 fm⁻¹: r_{0,s} = 1.345 fm; r_{0,t} = 1.536 fm; B_D = 2.084 MeV; η_D = 0.02904
- For μ = 30 fm⁻¹: r_{0,s} = 1.347 fm; r_{0,t} = 1.582 fm; B_D = 2.114 MeV; η_D = 0.02933
- Experimental reference values (Nijmegen): r_{0,s} = 2.73 fm; r_{0,t} = 1.75 fm; B_D = 2.2246 MeV; η_D = 0.0256
- The mixing parameter ε₁ shows sensitivity to μ for laboratory energies above 10 MeV, with convergence requiring μ → ∞

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory program for nuclear forces proposed by Weinberg (1990), who suggested cutting virtual NN propagation at the rho meson mass scale (~4 fm⁻¹). The authors show that while this scale works qualitatively for diagonal phase shifts (¹S₀, ³S₁, ³D₁), the mixing parameter ε₁ requires μ → ∞ for proper convergence. The results confirm earlier findings by Ballot and Robillota (1990) that the ³D₁ phase shift is dominated by OPEP, and support the need for higher-order terms (e.g., the Laplacian of the delta potential) to improve the singlet effective range, as noted in approaches by Kaplan et al. (1996) and others. The work enables a systematic program where higher-order chiral corrections can be added to the renormalized leading-order T-matrix without affecting the already well-described triplet channel.
