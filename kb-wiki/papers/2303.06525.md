# Finding the best basis states for the variation after projection nuclear wave functions

> The variation after projection (VAP) method is expected to be an efficient way of getting the optimized nuclear wave functions, so that they can be as close as possible to the exact shell model ones. However, we found there are two additional problems that may seriously affect the convergence of the VAP iteration. The first problem is, if a randomly selected projected basis state does not mix with a VAP wave function in the VAP calculation, then it is likely that this basis state will never mix with the VAP wave function even after the VAP iteration converges, which means such selected projected basis state is useless. The other problem is the poor orthonormality among the projected basis states that seriously affect the accuracy of the calculated VAP wave function. In the present work, solutions for these two problems are proposed and some examples are presented to test the validity. It turns out that, with the present solutions, the most important projected basis states can be reliably obtained and the fully optimized VAP wave functions can be accurately and efficiently calculated.

## Key claim

The authors identify two previously unrecognized problems that can prevent convergence of the variation after projection (VAP) method: (1) a randomly selected projected basis state that does not mix with the VAP wave function at the start of iteration will likely never mix even after convergence, making it useless; and (2) poor orthonormality among projected basis states seriously affects the accuracy of the calculated VAP wave function. They propose solutions for both problems, but note that the effectiveness depends on the specific implementation and the constraint parameter choice.

## Method

The authors propose an algorithm to vary a candidate projected Slater determinant (SD) by maximizing a coupling strength quantity C, defined as the sum over calculated states of the squared matrix element connecting the candidate to the VAP wave function, using gradient and Hessian information already available in the VAP calculation. To address orthonormality, they replace two previous constraint terms with a single new constraint term proportional to the inverse of the determinant of the norm matrix, which strictly forbids redundant basis states by diverging when any eigenvalue of the norm matrix becomes zero. The method is tested by performing VAP calculations for the ground 0+ state in 64Ge using the GXPF1A interaction in the fp model space, for 8+ states in 24Mg using the USDB interaction in the sd model space, and for 4+ states in 56Ni using the GXPF1A interaction.

## Key numbers

- For the ground 0+ state in 64Ge with n=1, the converged VAP energy is -302.983 MeV; after adding a second projected SD using the new algorithm, the energy lowers to -303.522 MeV.
- The initial C value between a random projected SD and the converged n=1 VAP wave function in 64Ge is as tiny as 10^-10, but becomes large enough after only 4 iterations of the C-maximization procedure.
- For the 8+ states in 24Mg with 10 projected SDs, using the new constraint with chi = 10^-20 allows the VAP iteration to converge, whereas without any constraint the gradient of Q cannot be reduced below 10^-3 MeV.
- For the yrast 4+ state in 56Ni, the energy decreases steadily as projected SDs are added one by one, with the constraint parameter taken as chi = 10^(-2n) where n is the number of included projected states.

## Context

This work extends the VAP method developed by the same group in previous work (Gao et al., 2022) by solving two convergence problems that arise in large model spaces. The basis selection algorithm provides an alternative to the stochastic candidate selection used in the quantum Monte Carlo diagonalization method (Otsuka et al., 2001), where many useless candidates may need to be checked before finding an important one. The new constraint using the determinant of the norm matrix replaces two previous constraints from Gao et al. (2022) and is shown to more strictly forbid redundant basis states. The method enables systematic improvement of VAP wave functions toward exact shell model results by adding important projected basis states one by one, as demonstrated for 4+ states in 56Ni.
