# Form factors in the "point form" of relativistic quantum mechanics: single and two-particle currents

> Electromagnetic and Lorentz-scalar form factors are calculated for a bound system of two spin-less particles exchanging a zero-mass scalar particle. Different approaches are considered including solutions of a Bethe-Salpeter equation, a ``point form'' approach to relativistic quantum mechanics and a non-relativistic one. The comparison of the Bethe-Salpeter results, which play the role of an ``experiment'' here, with the ones obtained in ``point form'' in single-particle approximation, evidences sizable discrepancies, pointing to large contributions from two-body currents in the latter approach. These ones are constructed using two constraints: ensuring current conservation and reproducing the Born amplitude. The two-body currents so obtained are qualitatively very different from standard ones. Quantitatively, they turn out not to be sufficient to remedy all the shortcomings of the ``point form'' form factors evidenced in impulse approximation.

## Key claim

The "point form" approach to relativistic quantum mechanics, when using only single-particle (impulse approximation) currents, produces form factors for a two-body bound system that disagree significantly with the "exact" Bethe-Salpeter results, especially at high momentum transfer and for large binding energies; the authors construct two-body currents to remedy these discrepancies but find them quantitatively insufficient to fix all the shortcomings.

## Method

The authors calculate electromagnetic and Lorentz-scalar form factors for a system of two distinguishable spinless particles exchanging a zero-mass scalar boson (the Wick-Cutkosky model), using solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation as the "exact" reference. They compare these to results from the "point form" approach using three different mass operators (a Coulomb-like potential v0, a non-local one-boson-exchange potential v1, and an improved version v2) and to non-relativistic calculations. Two-body currents are constructed in the "point form" framework by imposing two constraints: ensuring current conservation and reproducing the Born amplitude.

## Key numbers

- For elastic form factor F1 at Q^2/m^2 = 100 with coupling alpha=1: Bethe-Salpeter gives 0.213e-03, non-relativistic gives 0.169e-03, "point form" gives 0.343e-04
- For elastic form factor F1 at Q^2/m^2 = 100 with coupling alpha=3: Bethe-Salpeter gives 0.503e-02, non-relativistic gives 0.338e-02, "point form" gives 0.228e-03
- For elastic form factor F1 at Q^2/m^2 = 100 with coupling alpha=2pi (binding energy E=1.90m): Bethe-Salpeter gives 0.285e-01, non-relativistic gives 0.190e-01, "point form" gives 0.127e-09
- For elastic scalar form factor F0 at Q^2/m^2 = 0.01 with alpha=3: Bethe-Salpeter gives 1.123, non-relativistic gives 0.996, "point form" gives 0.682
- The "point form" elastic form factor F1 asymptotically falls as 1/Q^6 instead of the "exact" 1/Q^4 behavior
- The "point form" elastic scalar form factor F0 asymptotically falls as 1/Q^8 instead of the "exact" 1/Q^4 behavior

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Desplanques 2001) by including Lorentz-scalar probes and different mass operators, and by constructing explicit two-body currents. It was motivated by puzzling features of the "point form" calculation of nucleon form factors by Wagenbrunn (2000), which showed surprisingly good agreement with experiment despite omitting vector meson dominance contributions and violating current conservation. The Wick-Cutkosky model used here was previously employed by Karmanov and Smirnov (1994) to test light-front form factor calculations. The results enable future work on improving the "point form" implementation, with proposals sketched in Desplanques (2001) and Amghar (2002).
