# Comment on "Sequential Single-Pion Production Explaining the Dibaryon "$d^*(2380)$" Peak"

> In arxiv: 2102.05575 a two-step process $pn \to (pp) π^- \to (ΔN) π^- \to (d π^+) π^-$ was calculated by using experimental total cross sections for the single-pion production processes $pn \to pp π^-(I=0)$ and $pp \to d π^+$. As a result the authors obtain a resonance-like structure for the total $pn \to dπ^+π^-$ cross section of about the right size and width of the observed $d^*(2380)$ peak at an energy about 40 MeV below the $d^*(2380)$ mass. We object both the results of the sequential process calculation and its presentation as an alternative to the dibaryon interpretation.

## Key claim

The sequential two-step process proposed in arXiv:2102.05575 cannot explain the dibaryon resonance \(d^*(2380)\) because the calculated peak misses the measured resonance mass by about 40 MeV, the width of the first-step reaction is artificially narrowed by an unjustified error treatment, and the sequential process fails to reproduce signals observed in multiple other channels and partial-wave analyses.

## Method

The authors critique the sequential process calculation of \(pn \to (pp)\pi^- \to (\Delta N)\pi^- \to (d\pi^+)\pi^-\) by examining the experimental total cross sections used for the single-pion production steps \(pn \to pp\pi^-(I=0)\) and \(pp \to d\pi^+\). They analyze the systematic error treatment in the isoscalar \(pn \to pp\pi^-\) data, the kinematic mismatch between the partial waves required for the second step and those actually present, and the failure of the sequential ansatz to account for signals in non-fusion channels and partial-wave analyses.

## Key numbers

- The calculated peak in the \(pn \to d\pi^+\pi^-\) cross section from the sequential process lies about 40 MeV below the \(d^*(2380)\) mass.
- The width of the structure in the isoscalar \(pn \to pp\pi^-\) cross section is about 150 MeV in the authors' fits, not 70 MeV as claimed in the sequential-process paper.
- The isovector part of the \(np \to pp\pi^-\) reaction is larger than the isoscalar part by roughly a factor of four at the \(d^*(2380)\) energy, yet the isovector part of the \(d\pi^+\pi^-\) channel is smaller by a factor of ten.
- The binning used for the total cross section presentation in the experimental papers was 10 MeV, and the COSY beam had a resolution in the sub-MeV range.

## Context

This comment extends the debate over the nature of the dibaryon resonance \(d^*(2380)\) with \(I(J^P) = 0(3^+)\), which has been observed in all hadronic decay channels except the isoscalar \(NN\pi\) channel, consistent with a \(\Delta\Delta\) intermediate configuration as discussed in Bashkanov et al. (2013). The comment contradicts the sequential-process interpretation of Molina and Oset (2021) by pointing to consistent signals in non-fusion channels such as \(np\pi^0\pi^0\) and \(pp\pi^0\pi^-\) (WASA-at-COSY, 2013-2016), the pole in the \(^3D_3-^3G_3\) \(np\)-partial waves at 2380 MeV (WASA-at-COSY, 2014), and photoexcitation signals in \(\gamma d \to d\pi^0\pi^0\) (Ishikawa et al., 2015; MAMI, 2018). The comment enables future work to focus on the \(\Delta\Delta\) dibaryon interpretation and partial-wave analyses rather than sequential single-pion production.
