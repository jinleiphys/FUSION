# Jet Vetoes Interfering with H->WW

> Far off-shell Higgs production in $H \rightarrow WW,ZZ$, is a particularly powerful probe of Higgs properties, allowing one to disentangle Higgs width and coupling information unavailable in on-shell rate measurements. These measurements require an understanding of the cross section in the far off-shell region in the presence of realistic experimental cuts. We analytically study the effect of a $p_T$ jet veto on far off-shell cross sections, including signal-background interference, by utilizing hard functions in the soft collinear effective theory that are differential in the decay products of the $W/Z$. Summing large logarithms of $\sqrt{\hat s}/p_T^{veto}$, we find that the jet veto induces a strong dependence on the partonic centre of mass energy, $\sqrt{\hat s}$, and modifies distributions in $\sqrt{\hat s}$ or $M_T$. The example of $gg\rightarrow H \rightarrow WW$ is used to demonstrate these effects at next to leading log order. We also discuss the importance of jet vetoes and jet binning for the recent program to extract Higgs couplings and widths from far off-shell cross sections.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a jet veto on transverse momentum ($p_T$) introduces a strong dependence on the partonic center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{\hat s}$ in far off-shell Higgs production, modifying differential distributions in $\sqrt{\hat s}$ or transverse mass $M_T$ through a Sudakov suppression factor that the abstract does not quantify as being strongly $\sqrt{\hat s}$-dependent.

## Method

The authors use soft collinear effective theory (SCET) to derive a factorization theorem for the exclusive zero-jet cross section with a $p_T$ jet veto, separating the hard matrix element from universal beam and soft functions. They resum large logarithms of $\sqrt{\hat s}/p_T^{\text{veto}}$ to next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) order using renormalization group evolution, and apply the formalism to the process $gg\rightarrow H \rightarrow WW \rightarrow l\nu l\nu$ including signal-background interference for two Higgs mass scenarios ($m_H=126$ GeV and $m_H=600$ GeV).

## Key numbers

* The jet veto scale used in experimental analyses is typically $p_T^{\text{veto}} \sim 25-30$ GeV, with jet radius $R \simeq 0.4-0.5$.
* The Standard Model Higgs width is approximately 4 MeV, and the CMS bound on the Higgs width from off-shell production is $\Gamma_H \leq 4.2\, \Gamma_H^{\text{SM}}$.
* The NLL resummation formula (Eq.~\ref{eq:intro_fact}) shows the cross section depends on the Sudakov factor $e^{-2\text{Re} K^i_{\text{NLL}}(\sqrt{\hat s},\, p_T^{\text{veto}})}$, which suppresses the cross section with a strong $\sqrt{\hat s}$ dependence.
* The off-shell cross section scales as $\sigma_H^{\text{off-shell}} \sim g_i^2 g_f^2$ and the signal-background interference scales as $\sigma^{\text{int}} \sim g_i g_f$, compared to the narrow width approximation scaling $\sigma^{\text{nwa}} \sim g_i^2 g_f^2 / \Gamma_H$.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of jet veto effects from on-shell Higgs production (studied by Berger (2010), Banfi (2012), Becher (2012), Tackmann (2012), Stewart (2013)) to the far off-shell region relevant for Higgs width extraction. The method proposed by Caola (2013) and Campbell (2013) to bound the Higgs width using off-shell $H\rightarrow WW$ and $H\rightarrow ZZ$ production requires accurate modeling of jet veto effects, which this paper provides analytically for the first time. The results enable improved theoretical predictions for experimental analyses that use jet binning to reduce backgrounds, such as the CMS bound on the Higgs width from $H\rightarrow ZZ\rightarrow 2l2\nu$ (CMS, 2014).
