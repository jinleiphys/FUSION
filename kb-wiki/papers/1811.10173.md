# The $\bar{D}^* \bar{D}^* Σ_c$ Three-Body System

> In the molecular picture the hidden-charm, pentaquark-like $P_c(4450)$ resonance is a $\bar{D}^* Σ_c$ bound state with quantum numbers $I=\tfrac{1}{2}$ and $J^P = \tfrac{3}{2}^-$. If this happens to be the case, it will be natural to expect the existence of $\bar{D}^* \bar{D}^* Σ_c$ three-body bound states. The most probable quantum numbers for a bound $\bar{D}^* \bar{D}^* Σ_c$ trimer are the isoscalar $J^P = \tfrac{1}{2}^+$, $\tfrac{5}{2}^+$ and the isovector $J^P = \tfrac{3}{2}^+$, $\tfrac{5}{2}^+$ configurations. Calculations within a contact-range theory indicate a trimer binding energy $B_3 \sim 3-5\,{\rm MeV}$ and $14-16\,{\rm MeV}$ for the isoscalar $\tfrac{1}{2}^+$ and $\tfrac{5}{2}^+$ states and $B_3 \sim 1-3\,{\rm MeV}$ and $3-5\,{\rm MeV}$ for the isovector $\tfrac{3}{2}^+$ and $\tfrac{5}{2}^+$ states, respectively, with $B_3$ relative to the $\bar{D}^* P_c(4450)$ threshold. These predictions are affected by a series of error sources that we discuss in detail.

## Key claim

If the $P_c(4450)$ resonance is indeed a $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c$ molecular bound state with quantum numbers $I=1/2$ and $J^P = 3/2^-$, then the $\bar{D}^* \bar{D}^* \Sigma_c$ three-body system is expected to form bound trimers, though these predictions depend on the molecular nature of the $P_c(4450)$ being correct and are subject to several sources of uncertainty discussed in the paper.

## Method

The author solves Faddeev equations for the $\bar{D}^* \bar{D}^* \Sigma_c$ system using a contact-range theory where the $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c$ interaction in the $P_c(4450)$ channel is treated as leading order, while all other two-body interactions (including $\bar{D}^* \bar{D}^*$ and $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c$ in other channels) are assumed subleading. The $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c$ potential is described by a Gaussian regulator with cut-off $\Lambda = 0.5-1.0$ GeV, and the coupling strength is fixed by reproducing the $P_c(4450)$ binding energy $B_2 = 12 \pm 3$ MeV. The Faddeev equations reduce to single-channel integral equations via diagonalization of spin-isospin coupling factors $\lambda$.

## Key numbers

- $P_c(4450)$ two-body binding energy: $B_2 = 12 \pm 3$ MeV, relative to the $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c$ threshold
- Isoscalar $J^P = 5/2^+$ trimer binding energy: $B_3 = 14-16$ MeV (relative to $\bar{D}^* P_c(4450)$ threshold)
- Isoscalar $J^P = 1/2^+$ trimer binding energy: $B_3 = 3-5$ MeV
- Isovector $J^P = 5/2^+$ trimer binding energy: $B_3 = 3-5$ MeV
- Isovector $J^P = 1/2^+$ trimer binding energy: $B_3 = 1-3$ MeV
- Expected size of the $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c$ molecule: $1/\sqrt{2\mu B_2} \sim 1.2$ fm
- Binding momentum of the $5/2^+$ trimer: $\sqrt{2\mu_1 B_3} \sim 200-210$ MeV, corresponding to size $0.9-1.0$ fm

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of the $P_c(4450)$ resonance, originally proposed by Roca (2015), He (2015), and Xiao (2015) as a $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c$ bound state, to predict three-body $\bar{D}^* \bar{D}^* \Sigma_c$ bound states. The calculations use contact-range effective field theory methods previously applied to heavy meson systems by Valderrama (2018) and Ren (2018). The predictions enable experimental searches for these trimers, which if observed would strongly support the molecular nature of the $P_c(4450)$, or alternatively lattice QCD studies to test the existence of these states. The analysis also discusses the Efimov effect in the unitary limit, finding that only the $J=5/2$, $I=0$ trimer is potentially Efimov-like, with a discrete scaling factor $e^{\pi/s_0} \simeq 5711$ for momenta.
