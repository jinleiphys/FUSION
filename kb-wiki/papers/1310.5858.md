# Recent developments in jet quenching theory

> Motivated by the new results obtained in heavy-ion collision experiments at the LHC, several extensions of the standard calculations of energy loss have been made recently. In this manuscript, I provide a short overview of some of the recent developments in jet quenching theory. First, I discuss some improvements computed by different groups to implement energy-momentum conservation in a rigorous way, relaxing some of the assumptions that were made in the standard jet quenching calculations. Second, quantum interference effects between different parton emitters when propagating through an extended coloured medium will be considered, with a quark- antiquark antenna as a model setup. Finally, other recent extensions as the modification of the colour flow inside a jet in a QCD medium with respect to vacuum, and the use of SCET to compute transverse momentum broadening and medium-induced gluon radiation, will be very briefly presented.

## Key claim

The paper reviews recent extensions to standard jet quenching calculations that relax key assumptions, specifically, the soft-gluon and eikonal approximations, to incorporate finite energy corrections, non-eikonal parton propagation, and colour coherence effects, but notes that these improvements are still limited and phenomenological implementation is ongoing.

## Method

The author surveys several theoretical developments within the path-integral formalism and Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) that compute medium-induced gluon radiation spectra beyond the soft-gluon limit, including an interpolation function between soft and hard regimes and a Green's function description that allows Brownian transverse motion for all partons. The paper also examines quantum interference effects using a quark-antiquark antenna model to study colour decoherence, and analyses modifications to colour flow in the Lund string model when medium interactions break colour connections after gluon emission.

## Key numbers

- The medium-induced gluon radiation spectrum is computed as a function of the dimensionless variables \(p_+/\omega_{c+} = 2p_+ / (\hat{q}_A L_+^2)\) and \(\kappa^2 = \mathbf{q}^2 /(\hat{q}_A L_+)\), where \(p_+\) is the initial quark energy, \(L_+\) the medium length, and \(\hat{q}_A\) the transport coefficient.
- For the quark-antiquark antenna in the soft limit, the decoherence parameter is \(\Delta_{med} \approx 1 - \exp\left[ -\frac{1}{12} \hat{q} \, \theta_{q \bar{q}} L^3 \right]\), where \(\theta_{q\bar{q}}\) is the cone opening angle and \(L\) the medium length.
- The total gluon spectrum off an antenna in the soft limit is given by \(\left. dN_q^{tot} \right|_{\omega \rightarrow 0} = \frac{ \alpha_s C_F}{ \pi} \frac{ d\omega }{\omega} \frac{ \sin \theta \, d \theta }{ 1 - \cos \theta } \left[ \Theta (\cos \theta - \cos \theta_{q \bar{q}} ) + \Delta_{med} \Theta ( \cos \theta_{q \bar{q}} - \cos \theta) \right]\).

## Context

This review extends the standard jet quenching formalisms (e.g., those referenced as "models1", "models2", "models3" in the text) that assumed soft-gluon emissions and eikonal propagation, and addresses limitations identified in prior work such as the lack of energy-momentum conservation and colour coherence. The developments enable more realistic Monte Carlo implementations of parton shower evolution in a quark-gluon plasma, and the colour-flow modifications suggest an extra source of energy loss beyond the standard \(\hat{q}\) parameter approach, with phenomenological applications currently under development.
