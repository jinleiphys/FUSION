# Bubble dynamics and the quark-hadron phase transition in nuclear collisions

> We study the nucleation of a quark gluon plasma (QGP) phase in a hadron gas at low temperatures and high baryon densities. This kind of process will presumably happen very often in nuclear collisions at FAIR and NICA. When the appropriate energy densities (or baryon densities) and temperatures are reached the conversion of one phase into another is not instantaneous. It is a complex process, which involves the nucleation of bubbles of the new phase. One important element of this transition process is the rate of growth of a QGP bubble. In order to estimate it we solve the Relativistic Rayleigh$-$Plesset equation which governs the dynamics of a relativistic spherical bubble in a strongly interacting medium. The baryon rich hadron gas is represented by the nonlinear Walecka model and the QGP is described by the MIT bag model and also by a mean field model of QCD.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the quark-hadron phase transition in nuclear collisions at FAIR and NICA is not instantaneous, as often assumed in hydrodynamical codes, but involves a finite transition time governed by bubble nucleation and growth; however, the authors qualify that their calculations assume the bubble surface moves slowly compared to the sound velocity (approximately 0.4-0.6 times the speed of light), allowing an adiabatic approximation where pressure in each phase is treated as constant.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Rayleigh-Plesset (RRP) equation, which describes the dynamics of a spherical bubble in a relativistic fluid, for both QGP bubbles expanding in a hadron gas and hadron gas bubbles collapsing in QGP. The hadron gas is modeled using the nonlinear Walecka model (NLWM), while the QGP is described by the MIT bag model and a mean field theory of QCD (mQCD) at zero temperature, and by lattice QCD parametrizations at finite temperature. The RRP equation is solved numerically for various initial bubble radii and velocities, and the non-relativistic limit is also solved analytically using the Sundman transformation method.

## Key numbers

- The collapse time of a hadron gas bubble in a soft QGP (MIT bag model) is approximately 3.3 fm in the relativistic case and 3.0 fm in the non-relativistic case.
- Using the stiffer mQCD equation of state for the QGP, the collapse occurs sooner: at approximately 2.5 fm in the relativistic case and 2.2 fm in the non-relativistic case.
- QGP bubbles with initial radius 1 fm or 2 fm, using the hardest equation of state (mQCD), take at least 10 fm to reach a typical system size of approximately 6 fm.
- The typical total collision times at FAIR are in the range of 15-20 fm.
- The critical temperature used in the lattice QCD parametrization is T_c = 200 MeV.
- The baryon densities used in the zero-temperature calculations are rho_ex = 0.35 fm^{-3} for the external medium and rho_in = 1.0 fm^{-3} for the bubble interior.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of bubble dynamics in nuclear collisions, particularly those of Refs. [bub2] and [bub3], by applying the relativistic Rayleigh-Plesset equation to the specific scenario of low-temperature, high-baryon-density collisions expected at FAIR and NICA. The authors note that most existing hydrodynamical codes assume instantaneous phase conversion once a critical energy density is exceeded, and their results suggest this assumption may be problematic because the calculated bubble growth times (approximately 10 fm) are comparable to or longer than the total collision time (15-20 fm). The work also connects to extensive prior studies of QGP nucleation in heavy ion collisions (Kapusta, Madsen, and others) and in compact star contexts (Lugones, Heiselberg, and others), but focuses specifically on the time evolution of bubbles rather than just nucleation rates or critical radii.
