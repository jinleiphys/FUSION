# Generalized Ladder Operators for Shape-invariant Potentials

> A general form for ladder operators is used to construct a method to solve bound-state Schrödinger equations. The characteristics of supersymmetry and shape invariance of the system are the start point of the approach. To show the elegance and the utility of the method we use it to obtain energy spectra and eigenfunctions for the one-dimensional harmonic oscillator and Morse potentials and for the radial harmonic oscillator and Coulomb potentials.

# Generalized Ladder Operators for Shape-invariant Potentials

## Key claim

The paper presents a general algebraic method for solving bound-state Schrödinger equations exactly, using generalized ladder operators constructed from supersymmetric quantum mechanics and the shape invariance condition, but only for potentials where the shape invariance involves translations of parameters (not scaling), and the method is demonstrated only for four specific potentials.

## Method

The authors start from the supersymmetric factorization of a Hamiltonian into bosonic operators A± and impose the shape invariance condition with a translation step η between parameters. They define generalized ladder operators B± by composing the bosonic operators with translation operators T that act on the parameter space. The ground state wavefunction is obtained from the condition B−Ψ0 = 0, and excited states are generated by repeated application of the creation operator B+. The energy eigenvalues are computed from the sum of the remainders R(ak) that appear in the shape invariance condition, without constructing the usual hierarchy of Hamiltonians.

## Key numbers

- One-dimensional harmonic oscillator: ground state energy E0 = 1, energy spectrum En = 2n + 1, with parameter step η = 0 and R(ak) = 2
- Morse potential: ground state energy E0 = λ − 1/4, energy spectrum En = 2λ(n + 1/2) − (n + 1/2)², with parameter step η = 1 and a0 = 1/2
- Radial harmonic oscillator (3D): ground state energy E0(l) = 2l + 3, energy spectrum En(l) = 2l + 3 + 4n, with parameter step η = 1 and R(lk) = 4
- Radial Coulomb potential: ground state energy E0(l) = 1/[4(l+1)²], energy spectrum En(l) = −1/[4(n+l+1)²], with parameter step η = 1

## Context

This work extends the algebraic structure for shape-invariant potentials introduced by Balantekin (1998), who showed that shape invariance has an underlying algebraic structure. The method provides an alternative to the standard supersymmetric hierarchy of Hamiltonians approach (Dutt, Khare, and Sukhatme, 1988) and to earlier algebraic treatments of the Morse potential (Nieto and Simmons, 1979; Cooper, 1993) and the radial Coulomb potential (Nieto and Simmons, 1979). The approach enables the construction of coherent states for one-dimensional potentials and can be extended to radial harmonic oscillator and Coulomb potentials in arbitrary dimensions.
