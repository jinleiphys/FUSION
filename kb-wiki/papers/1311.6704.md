# BCS-BEC crossover in relativistic Fermi systems

> We review the BCS-BEC crossover in relativistic Fermi systems, including the QCD matter at finite density. In the first part we study the BCS-BEC crossover in a relativistic four-fermion interaction model and show how the relativistic effect affects the BCS-BEC crossover. In the second part, we investigate both two-color QCD at finite baryon density and pion superfluid at finite isospin density, by using an effective Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. We will show how the model describes the weakly interacting diquark and pion condensates at low density and the BEC-BCS crossover at high density.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in relativistic Fermi systems, the BCS-BEC crossover depends not only on the dimensionless coupling parameter \(\eta = 1/(k_f a_s)\) but also on a relativistic parameter \(\zeta = k_f/m\), breaking the universality found in nonrelativistic theories; a relativistic BEC (RBEC) state emerges when the coupling becomes strong enough that \(\eta \sim \zeta^{-1}\), even if the initial noninteracting gas satisfies the nonrelativistic condition \(k_f \ll m\).

## Method

The authors study the BCS-BEC crossover in a relativistic four-fermion interaction model with a contact interaction in the scalar \(J^P = 0^+\) channel, using a mean-field approximation at zero temperature. They derive and solve the relativistic gap and number equations self-consistently for the pairing gap \(\Delta_0\) and chemical potential \(\mu\), introducing a momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\) for regularization and defining a renormalized coupling \(U\) to connect with the \(s\)-wave scattering length \(a_s\). They then apply an effective Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to investigate two-color QCD at finite baryon density and pion superfluid at finite isospin density.

## Key numbers

* The critical coupling for the relativistic BEC state is estimated as \(\eta_c \simeq 2\zeta^{-1}\) for cutoff \(\Lambda/m = 10\), where \(\zeta = k_f/m\).
* The universal constant \(U_0 = 4\pi/m^2 = 4\pi\lambda_c^2\) appears in the analytical expression for the critical coupling: \(U_c^{-1} = (2/\pi)U_0^{-1} f(\Lambda/m)\) with \(f(x) = \ln(x + \sqrt{x^2 + 1})\).
* In the RBEC region near onset with \(\Delta_0 < m\), the fermion and anti-fermion densities are estimated as \(n_- \simeq n_+ \simeq \Delta_0^2 \Lambda / (8\pi^2)\) for \(m \ll \Lambda\).
* For quark matter at moderate density, the ratio \(\kappa = T_c/E_f\) is estimated as \(\kappa \sim 10^{-1}\), compared to \(\kappa \sim 10^{-5}\) for ordinary BCS superconductors and \(\kappa \sim 10^{-2}\) for high-temperature superconductors.

## Context

This work reviews and extends the BCS-BEC crossover framework originally developed by Eagles (1969) and Leggett (1980) for nonrelativistic Fermi gases, applying it to relativistic systems including QCD matter. It builds on prior studies of color superconductivity in dense quark matter (e.g., early work by Bailin and Love, and later by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek) and on the Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink (NSR) theory and boson-fermion models for relativistic BCS-BEC crossover (e.g., Abuki, and the RBCSBEC series). The paper enables future lattice simulations of two-color QCD and QCD at finite isospin density to be interpreted within the BCS-BEC crossover framework, and it suggests that relativistic effects may become important in ultracold atomic gases if sufficiently strong attraction could be achieved.
