# Canonical-basis solution of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equation on three-dimensional Cartesian mesh

> A method is presented to obtain the canonical-form solutions of the HFB equation for atomic nuclei with zero-range interactions like the Skyrme force. It is appropriate to describe pairing correlations in the continuum in coordinate-space representations. An improved gradient method is used for faster convergences under constraint of orthogonality between orbitals. To prevent high-lying orbitals to shrink into a spatial point, a repulsive momentum dependent force is introduced, which turns out to unveil the nature of high-lying canonical-basis orbitals. The asymptotic properties at large radius and the relation with quasiparticle states are discussed for the obtained canonical basis.

## Key claim

The paper presents a canonical-basis solution of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equation on a three-dimensional Cartesian mesh for zero-range interactions, claiming that the method requires far fewer single-particle states than quasiparticle-based approaches because canonical-basis orbitals are spatially localized even at positive energies, but this advantage depends on introducing a repulsive momentum-dependent pairing force to prevent high-lying orbitals from collapsing to a spatial point.

## Method

The authors formulate the HFB problem in the canonical representation using the Bloch-Messiah theorem, expressing the nuclear wave function as a BCS-type product of canonical orbitals with occupation amplitudes \(u_i\) and \(v_i\). They employ a Skyrme interaction (SIII parameter set without spin-orbit term) for the mean field and a modified zero-range pairing interaction that includes a momentum-dependent term proportional to \(1/k_c^2\) to suppress high-momentum components. An improved gradient method under orthogonality constraints is used to minimize the total energy functional, with the number of canonical orbitals \(N_w\) proportional to the nuclear volume rather than the normalization box volume.

## Key numbers

- The Skyrme SIII parameters used: \(t_0 = -1128.75\) MeV fm\(^3\), \(t_3 = 14000\) MeV fm\(^{3+3\alpha}\) with \(\alpha=1\), and the combinations \(f_1 = 44.375\) MeV fm\(^5\) and \(f_2 = -62.969\) MeV fm\(^5\) for the momentum-dependent terms.
- The pairing interaction strength is typically \(v_p = -1000\) MeV fm\(^3\) with a momentum cut-off parameter \(k_c = 2\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- The particle-density cut-off parameter \(\rho_c = 0.32\) fm\(^{-3}\) is used in the pairing interaction.
- The condition for needing continuum states applies when the neutron Fermi level is higher than approximately \(\frac{41}{2} A^{-1/3}\) MeV, which affects about half of the \(10^4\) nuclides between proton and neutron drip lines.

## Context

This work extends the canonical-basis HFB method originally developed for spherical nuclei by Reinhard et al. (1997) to deformed nuclei using a three-dimensional Cartesian mesh representation, building on earlier reports by Tajima (1998, 1999, 2000). The method addresses the difficulty of solving HFB for deformed nuclei in coordinate space, where quasiparticle-based approaches require a number of states proportional to the normalization box volume; the canonical-basis approach reduces this to a number proportional to the nuclear volume. The paper also resolves the "point collapse" problem of high-lying canonical orbitals that occurs with pure delta-function pairing forces, which was previously identified in Tajima (2000).
