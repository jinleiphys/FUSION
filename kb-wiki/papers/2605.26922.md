# Experimental and theoretical studies of hyperfine structures in $^{21}$Na

> We measured the hyperfine structure constants, $A(3s^2S_{1/2})$ and $A(3p^2P_{1/2})$, of the neutron-deficient isotope $^{21}\text{Na}$ using CLaSsy, a setup dedicated to collinear laser spectroscopy at RAON. The hyperfine structure constants of $^{21}\text{Na}$ were measured to be $103.6(10)_{\mathrm{stat}}(9)_{\mathrm{syst}}$ MHz for $A(3p^2P_{1/2})$ and $954.9(11)_{\mathrm{stat}}(25)_{\mathrm{syst}}$ MHz for $A(3s^2S_{1/2})$. A systematic comparison with the state-of-the-art ab-initio relativistic coupled cluster calculations shows the role of higher-order correlation effects such as triple excitations in $^{21}$Na. Furthermore, the measurement demonstrates a capability of the CLaSsy setup to conduct collinear laser spectroscopy experiments with a radioactive beam.

## Key claim

The hyperfine structure constants of the neutron-deficient isotope $^{21}$Na were measured with improved precision for the $A(3p^2P_{1/2})$ state compared to previous studies, and the results show that incorporating triple excitations in relativistic coupled-cluster calculations is essential to achieve theoretical agreement with experiment, though the ground-state constant $A(3s^2S_{1/2})$ remains dominated by the higher-precision measurement from Ames (1965).

## Method

Collinear laser spectroscopy was performed at the RAON facility using the CLaSsy setup with a radio-frequency quadrupole cooler-buncher (RFQ-CB). Singly charged $^{21}$Na and $^{23}$Na ions were produced via the isotope separation on-line (ISOL) technique using a silicon carbide target irradiated with a 70 MeV proton beam, extracted at 20 keV kinetic energy, neutralized in a rubidium charge-exchange cell, and resonantly excited on the D1 transition ($3s^2S_{1/2} \leftrightarrow 3p^2P_{1/2}$) using a tunable continuous-wave dye laser at 589 nm. Spectra were recorded in both collinear and anticollinear geometries using the voltage scan method, and the hyperfine constants were extracted via simultaneous linked fits of skewed Voigt profiles using the SATLAS2 package. Theoretical calculations were performed using relativistic coupled-cluster theory at the singles-and-doubles (RCCSD) and singles-doubles-and-triples (RCCSDT) levels, with corrections for the Breit interaction, vacuum polarization, self-energy, and Bohr-Weisskopf effects.

## Key numbers

* $A(3p^2P_{1/2})$ of $^{21}$Na: $103.6(10)_{\mathrm{stat}}(9)_{\mathrm{syst}}$ MHz
* $A(3s^2S_{1/2})$ of $^{21}$Na: $954.9(11)_{\mathrm{stat}}(25)_{\mathrm{syst}}$ MHz
* $A(3p^2P_{1/2})$ of $^{23}$Na (reference): $95.9(10)_{\mathrm{stat}}(2)_{\mathrm{syst}}$ MHz
* $A(3s^2S_{1/2})$ of $^{23}$Na (reference): $884.9(16)_{\mathrm{stat}}(4)_{\mathrm{syst}}$ MHz
* Final theoretical value for $^{21}$Na $A(3s^2S_{1/2})$: $952.29(70)$ MHz (RCCSDT with QED corrections)
* Final theoretical value for $^{21}$Na $A(3p^2P_{1/2})$: $101.64(27)$ MHz (RCCSDT with QED corrections)
* $^{21}$Na beam intensity: $1.0 \times 10^7$ to $2.75 \times 10^7$ particles per second at 10 Hz repetition rate
* Lorentzian FWHM fixed at 37 MHz, varied over 26.4--53.1 MHz for systematic study
* Laser frequency drift: $1.6$ MHz/h during data acquisition

## Context

This work extends previous laser spectroscopy measurements of $^{21}$Na by Huber (1978) and Touchard (1982), and the more precise ground-state measurement by Ames (1965) using a different technique. The new measurement of $A(3p^2P_{1/2})$ achieves reduced uncertainty compared to those earlier studies, and the combined weighted mean of all $A(3p^2P_{1/2})$ values (102.9(10) MHz) now clearly distinguishes between RCCSD and RCCSDT theoretical predictions, demonstrating the importance of triple excitations. The successful demonstration of the CLaSsy setup with a radioactive beam at RAON enables future hyperfine structure studies of more exotic neutron-deficient sodium isotopes and other rare isotopes, with improved resolution and detection efficiency needed for sub-MHz precision measurements that could further benchmark atomic many-body calculations.
