# Temperature Dependence of Beam on Plasma Stopping Power in the Resonance Regions of Fusion Reactions

> A recent proposal of accelerator based fusion reactor considers a scheme where an ion beam from the accelerator hits the target plasma on the resonance of the fusion reaction so that the reactivity ($σv$) can be an order of magnitude larger than that of a thermonuclear reactor. One of the important inputs is the stopping power which is needed to assess the energy loss of the beam in the plasma. In this work, we shall use the analytic formulation of Brown, Preston and Singleton~\cite{Brown:2005ji} to calculate the temperature dependence of the stopping power due to the target $t, {}^3H_e$, and ${}^{11}B$ plasmas in the resonance regions of their respective fusion reactions, i.e., $ d + t \rightarrow n + α, d + {}^3H_e \rightarrow p + α$, and $p + {}^{11}B \rightarrow 3 α$. It is found that the calculated stopping power, especially when the quantum corrections are included, does not go down with temperature as fast at $T^{-3/2}$. Instead it decreases slower, more like $T^{-x}$ with $x \le 1$ in the range of T from $\sim$ 5 to 50 keV for $d$ on $t$ and ${}^3H_e$ plasmas around their resonance energies.

# Temperature Dependence of Beam on Plasma Stopping Power in the Resonance Regions of Fusion Reactions

## Key claim
The stopping power of ion beams on target plasmas at fusion resonance energies does not decrease with temperature as fast as the previously assumed $T^{-3/2}$ scaling; instead it decreases more slowly, like $T^{-x}$ with $x \le 1$ in the temperature range from approximately 5 to 50 keV for deuterium on tritium and helium-3 plasmas, especially when quantum corrections are included. This slower decrease, combined with non-negligible ion contributions to stopping power, renders the original accelerator-based fusion reactor proposal less feasible than previously thought.

## Method
The author numerically implemented the analytic formulation of Brown, Preston, and Singleton (BPS, 2005) for stopping power, which uses dimensional continuation to combine long-distance collective plasma behavior and short-distance Coulomb scattering. The total stopping power was calculated as the sum of classical and quantum contributions for both electrons and ions in the plasma, for deuterium projectiles on tritium, helium-3, and deuterium plasmas, and for proton projectiles on boron-11 plasma, at the respective resonance energies of the fusion reactions $d + t \rightarrow n + \alpha$, $d + {}^3H_e \rightarrow p + \alpha$, and $p + {}^{11}B \rightarrow 3 \alpha$.

## Key numbers
- For the $d + t$ reaction at resonance energy $E_R = 107$ keV (lab frame), the stopping power power-law exponent $b$ in $aT^{-b}$ is approximately 1.3 at low temperature (1-3 keV), decreasing to around 1 and down to approximately 0.4 in the 7-20 keV range.
- The $d + t$ reaction has a resonance at $E_R = 107$ keV with full width at half maximum $\Gamma = 210$ keV and maximum cross section $\sigma_{\text{max}} = 5.1$ barns.
- The $d + {}^3H_e$ reaction has a resonance at $E_R = 439$ keV with $\Gamma = 430$ keV and $\sigma_{\text{max}} = 0.81$ barns.
- The $p + {}^{11}B$ reaction has a resonance at $E_R = 625$ keV with $\Gamma = 300$ keV and $\sigma_{\text{max}} = 1.28$ barns.
- For $d$ on $t$ plasma at $T = 10$ keV, the ratio $R_{sp} = 0.42$, which is less than unity; $R_{sp}$ reaches unity at approximately $T \sim 60$ keV.
- For $d$ on ${}^3H_e$ plasma, $R_{sp} = 0.12$ at $T = 10$ keV and $0.21$ at $T = 30$ keV.
- For $p$ on ${}^{11}B$ plasma, $R_{sp} = 0.063$ at $T = 30$ keV.
- The stopping power of ${}^{11}B$ on $p$ plasma at 7.5 MeV projectile energy is about an order of magnitude larger than that of $p$ on ${}^{11}B$ plasma at $T = 10$ keV.

## Context
This work extends the BPS formalism (Brown, Preston, and Singleton, 2005) for stopping power to the specific fusion resonance energies relevant to accelerator-based fusion reactor proposals (Liu, 2017). The earlier proposal (Liu, 2017) assumed a $T^{-3/2}$ scaling for stopping power based on Chu (1972) and Hamada (1978), which led to predictions of favorable critical temperatures for net energy gain. The present calculation shows that the actual temperature dependence is weaker ($T^{-x}$ with $x \le 1$), and that ion contributions to stopping power are non-negligible, resulting in $R_{sp} < 1$ for all cases considered. This contradicts the earlier feasibility assessment and suggests that alternative schemes, such as using the stopping power to heat and maintain plasma in a magnetic mirror configuration (Liu, 2023), need to be explored. The BPS formalism has been validated by recent experiments: Cayzac et al. (2017) found agreement for nitrogen projectiles at $v_b/v_e^{th} \simeq 1.2$, and Frenje et al. (2019) found BPS provided better description than other formalisms for low-Z ions, except at $v_i \sim 0.3 v^{th}$ where it underpredicted by approximately 20%.
