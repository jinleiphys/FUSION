# Multiple parton interactions and forward double pion production in pp and dA scattering

> We estimate the contributions by double-parton interactions to the cross sections for pp->pi^0 pi^0 X and dA->pi^0 pi^0 X at RHIC. We find that such contributions become important at large forward rapidities of the produced pions. This is in particular the case for dA scattering, where they strongly enhance the azimuthal-angular independent "pedestal" component of the cross section, providing a natural explanation of this feature of the RHIC dA data. We argue that the discussed processes open a window to studies of double quark distributions in nucleons. We also briefly address the roles of shadowing and energy loss in dA scattering, which we show to affect the double-inclusive pion cross section much more strongly than the single-inclusive one. We discuss the implications of our results for the interpretation of pion azimuthal correlations.

## Key claim

Double-parton interactions (two separate hard scatterings in a single collision) can dominate over the leading-twist (single hard scattering) mechanism for forward double-pion production at RHIC, particularly in dA collisions, providing a natural explanation for the azimuthal-angle-independent "pedestal" component observed in the data. The authors emphasize that this interpretation depends on the effective transverse area parameter πR²_int and the model for double-parton correlations, and that the double-scattering contribution is a higher-twist (power-suppressed) effect that becomes competitive only in specific kinematic regimes.

## Method

The authors computed leading-twist (LT) cross sections for pp → π⁰π⁰X using LO perturbative QCD with CTEQ6L parton distribution functions and de Florian-Sassot-Stratmann (DSS) fragmentation functions, and estimated double-parton contributions using a factorized ansatz with an effective transverse area πR²_int = 15 mb and a model for double-parton distributions that enforces the kinematic constraint x_a + x_a' ≤ 1. For dA scattering, they distinguished three contributions: (a) two quarks from one nucleon striking the same target nucleon, (b) independent scattering of the deuteron's proton and neutron off separate target nucleons, and (c) two quarks from one nucleon striking two different target nucleons, using the nuclear thickness function T(b) to account for nuclear geometry.

## Key numbers

- At √S = 200 GeV, the ratio r_pp of double-scattering to leading-twist contributions for pp → π⁰π⁰X reaches values of order 1 or larger for forward kinematics (η₁ ~ 3-4, p_T,1 ~ 2.5 GeV, with 1.5 GeV ≤ p_T,2 ≤ p_T,1 and 2.5 ≤ η₂ ≤ 4)
- At √S = 500 GeV, double-scattering effects are overall smaller than at 200 GeV but remain significant at large rapidities
- The effective transverse area parameter πR²_int = 15 mb, taken from experimental measurements by Abe (1997) and Abazov (2009)
- For dA scattering at impact parameter b = 0, the ratio r_dA of total double-scattering to leading-twist contributions reaches values of 3-10 or higher for forward kinematics (η₁ ~ 3-4, p_T,1 ~ 2.5 GeV)
- The uncorrelated mean-field estimate gives πR²_int = 34 mb (Frankfurt 2003), which is an upper limit when only momentum fractions larger than ~10⁻² are relevant
- For heavy nuclei at b ≈ 0, T(0) ≈ 2.2 fm⁻² = 1/(4.54 pb), giving r_a/r_c ≈ 0.3

## Context

This work extends the analysis of forward pion production in pp and dA scattering at RHIC, building on the observation that single-inclusive forward pion production is described well by NLO pQCD (Werner, d'Enterria 2006) while showing strong suppression in dA collisions (RHIC forward data). The authors previously demonstrated (GSV) that the dominant mechanism for single-inclusive forward pion production is scattering of a leading quark off a gluon with x_g ~ 0.01-0.03. The double-parton mechanism studied here provides an alternative to the Color Glass Condensate explanation (Tuchin 2009, Albacete 2010) for the depletion of the away-side peak in central dA collisions, and offers a natural explanation for the pedestal component that has received less attention. The work opens a window to studies of double quark distributions in nucleons, connecting to broader efforts on multi-parton interactions (Strikman 2001, BDFS, Sjostrand 2004) that are also important for LHC physics.
