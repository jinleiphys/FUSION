# Formation time scaling and hadronization in cold nuclear matter

> I propose a scaling analysis of the hadron multiplicity ratio measured in Deep Inelastic Scattering on nuclear targets as a tool to distinguish energy loss and nuclear absorption effects on hadron suppression in cold nuclear matter. The proposed scaling variable is a function of the hadron fractional energy and of the virtual photon energy. Its functional form, which depends on a parameter λ, can be fixed by general theoretical considerations and encompasses both energy loss and absorption models. The parameter λis fitted to HERMES experimental data and shown to favor prehadron nuclear absorption as leading mechanism for hadron suppression as opposed to quark energy loss.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the hadron multiplicity ratio in nuclear Deep Inelastic Scattering (nDIS) scales with a single variable tau = C * z_h^lambda * (1-z_h) * nu, and that the fitted exponent lambda can distinguish between prehadron nuclear absorption (lambda > 0) and quark energy loss (lambda < 0) as the dominant mechanism for hadron suppression, with HERMES data favoring absorption.

## Method

The author defines a scaling variable tau as a function of the hadron fractional energy z_h and the virtual photon energy nu, with a free exponent lambda. The multiplicity ratio R_M is assumed to depend only on tau, not on z_h and nu separately. The exponent lambda is extracted by fitting a polynomial function to experimental R_M data points from HERMES, computing tau for each z_h and nu bin, and minimizing chi-squared. The same procedure is applied to theoretical model calculations for comparison.

## Key numbers

* For pi+ and pi- production on Kr target at E_lab = 27 GeV, the best-fit scaling exponent is lambda_best = 0.34 (from Fig. 1 caption), with chi-squared per degree of freedom less than 1.6 for all data sets.
* For absorption models, the scaling exponent falls in the range 0.5 less than or approximately equal to lambda less than or approximately equal to 1.2.
* For energy loss models, the best-fit lambda is less than or approximately equal to 0.
* For charged hadrons on Kr target at E_lab = 27 GeV with a cut z_h > 0.5, the fit yields lambda_best = 0.34 +/- 0.13 with reduced chi-squared/d.o.f. = 1.18.
* For h- on Kr target at E_lab = 12 GeV, lambda_best = 0.34 +/- 0.11; for h+ on the same target, lambda_best = 0.02 +/- 0.09.
* The prehadron formation time in absorption models is estimated as t_* less than or approximately equal to 5 fm, smaller than the nuclear radius.

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing ambiguity between energy loss models (e.g., Wang, Arleo) and nuclear absorption models (e.g., Kopeliovich, Falter et al.) for hadron suppression in nDIS, where previous A^(2/3) scaling analyses could not distinguish the two mechanisms (Accardi, 2005; AGMP, 2005). The proposed scaling analysis provides a new discriminative tool. The results enable future experimental studies with higher statistics (e.g., from CLAS or upcoming HERMES data on Xe targets) to further constrain the space-time evolution of hadronization and to cross-check measurements of hadron p_T-broadening as proposed by Kopeliovich.
