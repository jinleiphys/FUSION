# Resonance parameters from K-matrix and T-matrix poles

> We extract K-matrix poles from our fits to elastic pion-nucleon scattering and eta-nucleon production data in order to test a recently proposed method for the determination of resonance properties, based on the trace of the K-matrix. We have considered issues associated with the separation of background and resonance contributions, the correspondence between K-matrix and T-matrix poles, and the complicated behavior of eigenphases.

# Resonance parameters from K-matrix and T-matrix poles

## Key claim

The authors test the method proposed by Ceci and collaborators (2007) for extracting resonance parameters from K-matrix poles and find that K-matrix poles are not generally independent of background contributions, and that a pole in the T-matrix does not necessarily imply a pole in the K-matrix, undermining the claimed model independence of the method.

## Method

The authors take T-matrix amplitudes determined from fits to elastic pion-nucleon scattering and eta-nucleon production data (the SP06 solution from SAID) and compute the corresponding K-matrix via the relation T = K(1 - iK)^{-1}. They then search for poles in the resulting K-matrix at real energies and compare these to known T-matrix pole positions in the complex energy plane. They also analytically compare two different parameterizations of the resonant eigenphase: the form used by Ceci and collaborators (2007) and an alternative form following Dalitz (1963), Goebel and McVoy (1968), and Weidenmuller (1967).

## Key numbers

- T-matrix pole for the Delta(1232) resonance: (1210, 50) MeV (real part, -Gamma/2) in the P33 partial wave; corresponding K-matrix pole found at 1232 MeV
- T-matrix pole for the N(1535) resonance: (1500, 50) MeV in the S11 partial wave; corresponding K-matrix pole found at 1535 MeV
- T-matrix pole for the N(1650) resonance: (1650, 40) MeV in the S11 partial wave; corresponding K-matrix pole found at 1675 MeV
- T-matrix pole for the Delta(1620) resonance: (1595, 70) MeV in the S31 partial wave; corresponding K-matrix pole found at 1660 MeV
- No K-matrix poles found near T-matrix poles for the P11 partial wave (T poles at (1360, 80) and (1390, 80) MeV), the D13 partial wave (T pole at (1515, 55) MeV), or the F15 partial wave (T poles at (1675, 60) and (1780, 130) MeV)
- K-matrix pole found at 1760 MeV for the D15 partial wave, where the T-matrix pole is at (1655, 70) MeV

## Context

This work directly tests the method proposed by Ceci and collaborators (2007) for using K-matrix pole positions and residues as model-independent characterizations of resonance structure. The authors find that the claimed model independence depends on the specific parameterization of the resonant eigenphase, and they provide an explicit counterexample using the phase-addition rule from Dalitz (1963), Goebel and McVoy (1968), and Weidenmuller (1967). The results contradict the assumption of a simple one-to-one correspondence between K-matrix and T-matrix poles, and the authors note that eigenphase behavior may be more complicated than the single-resonant-eigenphase form assumed in the Ceci method, citing Weidenmuller's application of Wigner's no-crossing theorem. This work enables future studies of eigenphase behavior using S-matrices from scattering amplitudes to determine how common eigenphase repulsion is in practice.
