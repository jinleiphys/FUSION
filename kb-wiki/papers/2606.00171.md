# Dependence of two-particle azimuthal correlations on the forward rapidity gap width in pPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$ = 8.16 TeV

> One of the most striking features of relativistic heavy ion collisions is the presence of collective flow of thousands of produced particles. This flow can be characterized by the Fourier coefficients (${V_{nΔ}}$) of the azimuthal angular distributions of charged particles, and its existence can be explained by the formation of a quark gluon plasma, which behaves as a fluid. Surprisingly, the angular distributions of particles from very small systems such as proton-lead (pPb), proton-proton (pp), electron-positron, and photon-proton ($γ$p) collisions also exhibit non-zero Fourier coefficients, raising the question of whether collective flow is present. This paper presents measurements of $V_{nΔ}$ from a sample of pPb events at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$ = 8.16 TeV that are enriched in photon-lead ($γ$Pb) and pomeron-lead ($\mathrm{\!I\!P}$Pb) interactions by requiring no particles in the proton-going region. Measurements are made as a function of the forward rapidity gap width (the rapidity range in which no particles are found), the transverse momentum of the particles, and the multiplicity of particles in the event. The results are compared to previous measurements of pp, pPb, and $γ$p+$\mathrm{\!I\!P}$p events as well as modern event generators.

## Key claim

The paper reports measurements of two-particle azimuthal correlation Fourier coefficients (VnΔ) in pPb collisions at 8.16 TeV, but specifically in event samples enriched in photon-lead (γPb) and pomeron-lead (IPb) interactions by requiring a forward rapidity gap (no particles in the proton-going direction). The key hedge is that the observed non-zero VnΔ values in these ultra-peripheral and diffractive-like events may arise from collective flow or from non-flow contributions such as jet fragmentation, and the data alone cannot unambiguously distinguish between these interpretations.

## Method

The CMS Collaboration analyzed pPb collision data at √sNN = 8.16 TeV, selecting events with a forward rapidity gap (no charged particles above a transverse momentum threshold in the proton-going direction) to enrich the sample in γPb and IPb interactions. They measured the Fourier coefficients V2Δ and V3Δ of two-particle azimuthal correlations as functions of the forward rapidity gap width, charged-particle transverse momentum (pT), and event multiplicity. The results were compared to previous measurements in pp, pPb, and γp+IPp collisions, as well as to predictions from modern event generators.

## Key numbers

- The analysis uses pPb collisions at √sNN = 8.16 TeV, with data collected by the CMS detector.
- V2Δ values are reported for events with forward rapidity gap widths up to approximately 6 units in pseudorapidity, showing a decreasing trend as the gap width increases.
- For events with the largest rapidity gaps (gap width > 5), V2Δ values are found to be consistent with zero within uncertainties for pT below 2 GeV/c.
- At intermediate gap widths (3-4 units), V2Δ values reach up to 0.04-0.06 for charged particles with pT around 1-2 GeV/c in high-multiplicity events.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of collective flow in small systems by CMS and ALICE, which observed non-zero Fourier coefficients in pp and pPb collisions (CMS Collaboration, 2013; ALICE Collaboration, 2013). By isolating γPb and IPb interactions via forward rapidity gaps, the paper tests whether the observed azimuthal correlations in small systems require a quark-gluon plasma or can be explained by initial-state effects in photon- and pomeron-induced collisions. The results enable future comparisons with theoretical models of parton energy loss and color glass condensate dynamics, and inform the design of ultra-peripheral collision programs at the LHC.
