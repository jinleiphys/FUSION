# Systematics of azimuthal anisotropy harmonics in proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC from the Color Glass Condensate

> Simple power counting arguments in the dilute-dense framework of the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) Effective Field Theory predict that even and odd azimuthal anisotropy harmonics of two-particle correlations in proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC will respectively satisfy v^2_{2n} ~ N_{ch}^0 and v^2_{2n+1} ~ N_{ch}, where N_{ch} denotes the number of charged particles. We show that these expectations are borne out qualitatively, and even quantitatively, within systematic uncertainties, for v_2 and v_4 in comparisons with data from the ATLAS collaboration. We also observe that ATLAS data for the v_3 azimuthal harmonic are in excellent agreement with our qualitative expectation; quantitative comparisons are numerically challenging at present. The lessons from this study fully complement those gained by the recent comparison of the CGC dilute-dense framework [arXiv:1805.09342] to data from the PHENIX collaboration on small system collisions at RHIC.

## Key claim

Simple power-counting arguments in the dilute-dense Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory predict that even azimuthal anisotropy harmonics satisfy \(v^2_{2n}\{2\} \propto N_{\rm ch}^0\) and odd harmonics satisfy \(v^2_{2n+1}\{2\} \propto N_{\rm ch}\) in proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC; the authors show these expectations are borne out qualitatively and quantitatively within systematic uncertainties for \(v_2\) and \(v_4\) when compared to ATLAS data, while \(v_3\) agrees qualitatively but quantitative comparison is numerically challenging.

## Method

The authors employ the dilute-dense approximation of the CGC effective field theory, keeping terms to lowest order in the projectile color charge density \(\rho_p/k_{\perp,p}^2\) but to all orders in the target density \(\rho_t/k_{\perp,t}^2\). They decompose the single-particle inclusive gluon distribution into parity-even and parity-odd contributions, derive scaling relations under a rescaling \(\rho_p \to c \rho_p\), and use these to predict the \(N_{\rm ch}\) dependence of \(v_{2n}\{2\}\) and \(v_{2n+1}\{2\}\). Numerical realizations of the framework are performed on transverse lattices of size \(N=1024\) with spacing \(a=0.0625\) fm and \(N_y=100\) rapidity slices, with parameters (saturation scale ratio \(Q_s/g^2\mu=0.5\), fluctuation \(\sigma=0.5\), regulator mass \(m=0.3\) GeV) determined by minimizing deviation from the ATLAS charged-particle multiplicity distribution.

## Key numbers

- \(v_2\{2\}\) and \(v_4\{2\}\) in p+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV/nucleon show \(v_{2n}\{2\} \propto N_{\rm ch}^0\) scaling, with the proportionality coefficient fixed using data at \(N_{\rm ch}=218\).
- \(v_3\{2\}\) in p+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV/nucleon shows \(v_3\{2\} \propto N_{\rm ch}^{1/2}\) scaling, in qualitative agreement with ATLAS data.
- Theoretical uncertainties in the numerical results for \(v_2\) and \(v_4\) are estimated at 30%, represented by a shaded band around the central values.
- For \(v_4\) extraction, excellent convergence is observed for lattices with \(N \geq 768\) sites at fixed spacing \(a=0.0625\) fm for events with \(N_{\rm ch} \geq 100\).
- Approximately \(10^4\) color charge configurations are needed for \(v_{2,4}\) calculations; \(v_3\) would require at least \(10^6\) configurations.

## Context

This work extends the dilute-dense CGC framework previously applied to RHIC data from PHENIX (Mace et al., 2018) to LHC p+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV/nucleon, complementing those results with systematics on \(N_{\rm ch}\) dependence. The findings challenge hydrodynamic collectivity explanations for the ridge correlations, as the observed scaling differs from that seen in dense-dense IP-Glasma plus hydrodynamic models (Mantysaari et al., 2017), which show flatter \(v_3\) dependence on \(N_{\rm ch}\). The results enable future quantitative comparisons of dilute-dense versus dense-dense CGC frameworks for peripheral Pb+Pb collisions and for disentangling initial-state momentum anisotropies from geometry-driven hydrodynamic response.
