# Factorization of shell-model ground-states

> We present a new method that accurately approximates the shell-model ground-state by products of suitable states. The optimal factors are determined by a variational principle and result from the solution of rather low-dimensional eigenvalue problems. The power of this method is demonstrated by computations of ground-states and low-lying excitations in sd-shell and pf-shell nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper presents a variational method that approximates shell-model ground-states as products of a small number of proton and neutron states, achieving exponential convergence in the ground-state energy as the number of retained factors increases, but the method is an approximation whose accuracy depends on the truncation parameter Omega (the number of factors) and the specific nucleus.

## Method

The authors divide the single-particle orbitals into proton and neutron subsets, then expand the nuclear many-body state as a sum over products of proton and neutron basis states. They make a variational ansatz with a truncated number Omega of such product terms, and solve the resulting coupled nonlinear eigenvalue problems iteratively: first solving for the proton states with fixed neutron states, then vice versa, using singular value decompositions to maintain orthonormality. The method is extended to include axial symmetry (m-scheme) by considering only products of proton and neutron states with opposite angular momentum projections that sum to zero.

## Key numbers

- For 44Ti with Omega=1 factorization: ground-state energy E0 = -10.74 MeV (dimension d=190) vs exact E0 = -13.88 MeV (d=4000)
- For 48Cr with Omega=1 factorization: E0 = -29.55 MeV (d=4,845) vs exact E0 = -32.95 MeV (d=1,963,461)
- For 56Ni with Omega=1 factorization: E0 = -76.23 MeV (d=125,970) vs exact E0 = -78.46 MeV (d=1,087,455,228)
- For 24Mg with USD interaction: exact ground-state energy E0 = -87.08 MeV (full m-scheme dimension d_max=28,503)
- For 56Ni with m-scheme factorization: ground-state energy converges to within 100 keV of exact result while eigenvalue problem dimension is reduced by a factor of 1000
- For 48Cr with m-scheme factorization: ground-state energy converges to within 30 keV of exact result while eigenvalue problem dimension is reduced by a factor of 12
- Exponential convergence rate for 48Cr: c approximately -31.38 (about a factor eight larger than the Exponential Convergence Method rate reported by Horoi (2002))

## Context

This work extends non-stochastic approximation methods for nuclear shell-model calculations, specifically building on the density matrix renormalization group approach of White (1992) and its nuclear applications by Dukelsky (2001, 2002) and Dimitrova (2002). The method improves upon the Exponential Convergence Method of Horoi (1994, 1999, 2002, 2003) by achieving faster exponential convergence rates (about a factor eight faster for 48Cr and about a factor 200 faster for 56Ni). The approach also relates to the work of Andreozzi and Porrini (2002) who approximated shell-model ground-states by products of eigenstates of the proton-proton and neutron-neutron Hamiltonian. The factorization method enables accurate approximations to ground-states and low-lying excitations in sd-shell and pf-shell nuclei using eigenvalue problems whose dimensions are reduced by orders of magnitude compared to exact diagonalization.
