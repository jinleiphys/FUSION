# Theoretical study of the decay-out spin of superdeformed bands in the Dy and Hg regions

> Decay of the superdeformed bands have been studied mainly concentrating upon the decay-out spin, which is sensitive to the tunneling probability between the super- and normal-deformed wells. Although the basic features are well understood by the calculations, it is difficult to precisely reproduce the decay-out spins in some cases. Comparison of the systematic calculations with experimental data reveals that values of the calculated decay-out spins scatter more broadly around the average value in both the $A \approx$ 150 and 190 regions, which reflects the variety of calculated tunneling probability in each band.

## Key claim

Although the basic features of superdeformed band decay are well understood within the authors' theoretical framework, the decay-out spin is not precisely reproduced in individual cases; the calculated decay-out spins scatter more broadly around the average value than observed experimentally in both the A ≈ 150 and A ≈ 190 mass regions, reflecting the variety of calculated tunneling probability in each band.

## Method

The authors use a compound mixing model based on two-well mixing between superdeformed (SD) and normal-deformed (ND) states, where the coupling strength is estimated from the transmission coefficient for tunneling between the wells. They calculate the relative gamma-ray intensity inside the SD band as a function of angular momentum using four quantities: the spreading width due to the coupling, the level density of ND compound states, the rotational E2 width of the SD band, and the statistical E1 width of the ND band. The potential energy surface is obtained from Nilsson-Strutinsky calculations with pairing correlations included in RPA order, and the mass tensor is calculated using the pairing hopping model with a Fermi-gas estimate of the number of level-crossings.

## Context

This work extends the compound mixing model of Vigezzi, Broglia, and collaborators (1990) and the authors' own earlier calculations (1993, 1996) for the decay of superdeformed bands in the A ≈ 150 and A ≈ 190 mass regions. The systematic comparison reveals that while basic features are reproduced, the calculated decay-out spins scatter more broadly than the experimentally observed narrow range, and the average decay-out spin for A ≈ 190 nuclei is overestimated even with a 1 MeV back-shift in the level density formula. The results suggest that microscopic calculations of the mass tensor based on counting level crossings are needed, and that improvement of the potential energy surface using Woods-Saxon or Skyrme Hartree-Fock methods may be necessary; the authors also note that modification of the compound mixing model to account for non-chaotic ND states at higher spins could help reduce the discrepancy.
