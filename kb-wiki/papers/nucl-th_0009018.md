# Antiflow of kaons in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We compare relativistic transport model calculations to recent data on the sideward flow of neutral strange K^0_s mesons for Au+Au collisions at 6 AGeV. A soft nuclear equation of state is found to describe very well the positive proton flow data measured in the same experiment. In the absence of kaon potential, the K^0 flow pattern is similar to that of protons. The kaon flow becomes negative if a repulsive kaon potential determined from the impulse approximation is introduced. However, this potential underestimates the data which exhibits larger antiflow. An excellent agreement with the data is obtained when a relativistic scalar-vector kaon potential, that has stronger density dependence, is used. We further find that the transverse momentum dependence of directed and elliptic flow is quite sensitive to the kaon potential in dense matter.

# Antiflow of kaons in relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The observed large antiflow of neutral strange K⁰_s mesons in 6 AGeV Au+Au collisions can be reproduced by a relativistic scalar-vector kaon potential with stronger density dependence than the impulse approximation, but only when a soft nuclear equation of state (incompressibility K=210 MeV) is used; the impulse-approximation potential underestimates the antiflow, and a stiff equation of state (K=380 MeV) overpredicts proton flow.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic transport model ART (A Relativistic Transport model) to simulate Au+Au collisions at 6 AGeV, incorporating a Skyrme-type nucleon mean field with either a soft (K=210 MeV) or stiff (K=380 MeV) nuclear equation of state at normal nuclear matter density ρ₀=0.16 fm⁻³. Kaon production was treated with a formation time of τ^K_fo=1.2 fm/c, and two forms of kaon potential were compared: one from the impulse approximation using the isospin-averaged kaon-nucleon scattering length (ā_KN ≈ -0.255 fm), and a relativistic scalar-vector potential from the chiral Lagrangian with parameters fitted to give the same repulsive potential of +30 MeV at ρ₀. Calculations were restricted to impact parameters b ≤ 7 fm and transverse momentum p_t ≤ 700 MeV/c to match the E895 experimental cuts.

## Key numbers

- Proton directed flow slope at midrapidity: F = d⟨p_x⟩/d(y/y_cm) ≈ 180 MeV/c (data); reproduced by soft EOS (K=210 MeV), overpredicted by stiff EOS (K=380 MeV)
- K⁰_s directed flow slope: F ≈ -127 ± 20 MeV/c (E895 data)
- Kaon potential at normal nuclear density: U_K(ρ₀) = +30 MeV at zero momentum (both impulse approximation and scalar-vector potential)
- Isospin-averaged kaon-nucleon scattering length: ā_KN ≈ -0.255 fm
- Scalar-vector potential parameter: b_K = 3/(8f²_π) ≈ 0.333 GeV fm³
- Scalar-vector potential parameter: a_K ≈ 0.173 GeV² fm³ (fitted to give U_K(ρ₀)=+30 MeV)
- Normal nuclear matter density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Kaon formation time: τ^K_fo = 1.2 fm/c
- Passage time of spectators: t ≈ 2R/(γv) ≈ 6.4 fm/c

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical predictions by Li et al. (1995) that repulsive kaon potentials would produce anticorrelated flow, and directly addresses the E895 Collaboration measurements of K⁰_s directed flow in 6 AGeV Au+Au collisions (Chung et al., 2000; Liu et al., 2000). The study contrasts with findings at lower beam energies (1-2 AGeV) from the FOPI and KaoS Collaborations, where smaller antiflow was observed, and with earlier AGS experiments that found nearly vanishing kaon transverse flow. The results enable using kaon directed and elliptic flow as probes of the kaon dispersion relation in dense matter at densities below about 4ρ₀, and complement studies of kaon properties at GSI energies where the scalar-vector potential had already been successful (Li, Lee, and Brown, 1997).
