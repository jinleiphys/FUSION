# Nucleon-to-Roper electromagnetic transition form factors at large-$Q^2$

> High-precision nucleon-resonance electroproduction data on a large kinematic domain of energy and momentum transfer have proven crucial in revealing novel features of strong interactions within the Standard Model and unfolding structural details of baryon excited states. Thus, in anticipation of new data reaching to unprecedented photon virtuality, we employ a quark-diquark approximation to the three valence-quark bound-state problem to compute $γ^\ast p \to R^+$ and $γ^\ast n \to R^0$ transition form factors on $Q^2/m_N^2 \in [0,12]$, where $m_N$ is the nucleon mass. Having simultaneously analysed both charged and neutral channels, we also provide a quark-flavour separation of the transition form factors. The results should be useful in planning new-generation experiments.

## Key claim

Using a quark-diquark approximation to the three valence-quark bound-state problem, the authors compute the $\gamma^\ast p \to R^+$ and $\gamma^\ast n \to R^0$ transition form factors on $Q^2/m_N^2 \in [0,12]$, but these results represent only the dressed-quark core contributions, omitting meson-baryon final-state interactions which are expected to be important at low $Q^2$ (below $Q^2/m_N^2 \approx 2$).

## Method

The authors solve the Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation for the nucleon and Roper resonance as three-valence-quark bound states, using a quark-diquark approximation where the baryon consists of a dressed quark correlated with either a scalar $[ud]$ or pseudovector $\{qq\}$ diquark. The electromagnetic current is constructed from six diagrams that probe the dressed quarks and diquarks in various ways, and the transition form factors are computed by evaluating this current between the Faddeev amplitudes of the initial nucleon and final Roper states. The calculations are performed on the domain $Q^2/m_N^2 \in [0,12]$, with projections to higher $Q^2$ obtained via a novel numerical technique.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon dressed-quark core mass: $m_N = 1.18$ GeV
- Roper dressed-quark core mass: $m_R = 1.73$ GeV
- Charged-Roper core magnetic moment: $\mu_{R^+} = 2.67$ (compared to proton core $\mu_p = 2.50$)
- Neutral-Roper core magnetic moment: $\mu_{R^0} = -1.24$ (compared to neutron core $\mu_n = -1.83$)
- Charged-Roper core electric radius: $r_E^{R^+} M_N = 6.23$ (compared to proton core $r_E^p M_N = 3.65$)
- Charged-Roper core magnetic radius: $r_M^{R^+} M_N = 4.49$ (compared to proton core $r_M^p M_N = 3.17$)
- Probability of scalar diquark $[ud]$ in charged-Roper: ${\cal P}_{[ud]}^{R^+} = 0.60$ (compared to proton $0.62$)
- Probability of pseudovector diquark $\{qq\}$ in charged-Roper: ${\cal P}_{\{qq\}}^{R^+} = 0.33$ (compared to proton $0.29$)

## Context

This work extends the Poincaré-covariant continuum analyses of Segovia et al. (2015, 2016) and Chen et al. (2017) by providing the previously missing predictions for the $\gamma^\ast n \to R^0$ transition form factors and the Roper elastic form factors on the full $Q^2$ domain expected to be mapped by experiments at the upgraded Jefferson Lab (JLab 12). The results enable quark-flavour separation of the transition form factors and should be useful for planning new-generation experiments. The calculations are independent of but consistent with dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) results from Suzuki et al. (2009), which give a bare-Roper mass of 1.76 GeV, and with lattice QCD simulations that suppress meson-baryon final-state interactions.
