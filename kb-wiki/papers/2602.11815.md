# Two-Pion Exchange Contributions to the Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction from the Roper Resonance

> We derive the long-range components of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) two-pion-exchange potential with an intermediate Roper resonance. Leading-order interactions in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory are considered. NN phase shifts with orbital angular momentum $L\geq 2$ are calculated in first-order perturbation theory and compared to those obtained without the Roper resonance. We show that the Roper contribution is sizeable for $D$ waves and improves the description of phase shifts for all the partial waves slightly. We also discuss the role of the Roper resonance in the NN interaction in the framework of resonance saturation.

# Two-Pion Exchange Contributions to the Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction from the Roper Resonance

## Key claim

The Roper resonance contributes sizeably to D-wave nucleon-nucleon phase shifts and slightly improves the description of phase shifts for all partial waves with orbital angular momentum L ≥ 2, but these contributions are smaller than those of purely nucleonic two-pion exchange at the same order and are increasingly suppressed in higher partial waves.

## Method

The authors derive the long-range components of the nucleon-nucleon two-pion-exchange potential with one and two intermediate Roper resonances using leading-order interactions in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory. They calculate NN phase shifts for partial waves with L ≥ 2 in first-order perturbation theory, comparing results obtained with and without explicit Roper contributions, and also compare to subleading two-pion exchange with resonance-saturated low-energy constants.

## Key numbers

- Roper-nucleon mass difference ρ = 690 MeV and axial-vector nucleon-Roper coupling g_A' = 1.06 adopted as the primary parameter set ("N^2LO EFT" values from fits to πN phase-shift analysis near the Delta resonance)
- Alternative parameter sets: g_A' = 0.32 with ρ = 470 MeV ("N^3LO EFT") and g_A' = 0.54 with ρ = 550 MeV ("N^2LO GW")
- Pion decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV, pion mass m_π = 139 MeV, nucleon mass m_N = 939 MeV, nucleon axial coupling g_A = 1.29
- Renormalization scale μ = 1 GeV used for illustration
- Roper saturation yields c_3^R ≈ -0.41 GeV^{-1} and c_4^R ≈ 0.82 GeV^{-1} from the "N^2LO EFT" parameters
- Roper contributions account for nearly one half of conventional two-pion exchange in D waves, except in ^3D_1 where very small, and in ^3D_3 where larger than conventional two-pion exchange
- Phase shifts calculated up to laboratory energy T_lab ≈ 280 MeV (near pion-production threshold)

## Context

This work extends chiral effective field theory treatments of the nucleon-nucleon interaction by explicitly including the Roper resonance N(1440) as a low-energy degree of freedom for the first time, following prior inclusion of the Delta isobar in chiral nuclear forces by Ordonez (1993, 1995), Kaiser (1997, 1998), and others. The Roper contribution to NN interactions was previously studied only in coupled-channel approaches predating chiral EFT, such as those by Lomon (1981), Lee (1984), and Sitarski (1987). This derivation enables future extensions of chiral nuclear forces into the pion-production region and improves understanding of resonance saturation of the subleading two-pion-exchange low-energy constants c_3 and c_4, building on the work of Bernard (1996) and Long (2011) on Roper saturation in πN scattering.
