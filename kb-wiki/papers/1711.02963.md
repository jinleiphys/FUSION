# Transverse momentum spectra of hadrons in $p+p$ collisions at CERN SPS energies from the UrQMD transport model

> The UrQMD transport model, version 3.4, is used to study the new experimental data on transverse momentum spectra of $π^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, $p$ and $\bar p$ produced in inelastic $p+p$ interactions at SPS energies, recently published by the NA61/SHINE Collaboration. The comparison of model predictions to these new measurements is presented as a function of collision energy for central and forward particle rapidity intervals. In addition, the inverse slope parameters characterizing the transverse momentum distributions are extracted from the predicted spectra and compared to the corresponding values obtained from NA61/SHINE distributions, as a function of particle rapidity and collision energy. A complex pattern of deviations between the experimental data and the UrQMD model emerges. For charged pions, the fair agreement visible at top SPS energies deteriorates with the decreasing energy. For charged $K$ mesons, UrQMD significantly underpredicts positive kaon production at lower beam momenta. It also underpredicts the central rapidity proton yield at top collision energy and overpredicts antiproton production at all considered energies. We conclude that the new experimental data analyzed in this paper still constitute a challenge for the present version of the model.

## Key claim

The UrQMD transport model version 3.4 shows a complex pattern of deviations from the new NA61/SHINE data on hadron transverse momentum spectra in inelastic p+p collisions at SPS energies, with the fair agreement for charged pions at top energies deteriorating as collision energy decreases, and the model significantly underpredicting positive kaon production at lower beam momenta while overpredicting antiproton production at all considered energies.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD v3.4 transport model, which solves the relativistic Boltzmann equation with hadronic and string degrees of freedom (55 baryon and 32 meson species up to 2.25 GeV mass), to simulate inelastic p+p collisions at beam momenta of 20, 31, 40, 80, and 158 GeV/c. They compared the model predictions for transverse momentum spectra of π±, K±, p, and p̅ to the NA61/SHINE experimental data at central (0<y<0.2) and forward (1.4<y<1.6) rapidity intervals, and extracted inverse slope parameters by fitting the exponential function d²N/(dp_T dy) = (S p_T)/(T²+mT) exp[-(m_T-m)/T] to the predicted spectra.

## Key numbers

- For charged pions at central rapidity (0<y<0.2), the UrQMD model describes well the transverse momentum spectra at 158 GeV/c but underestimates yields at lower beam energies for the whole p_T range.
- At forward rapidity (1.4<y<1.6), the model overestimates pion yield for p_T>0.8 GeV/c at all considered beam energies (31, 40, 80, 158 GeV/c).
- For mean π⁻ and π⁺ multiplicities at 20-31 GeV/c beam momentum, discrepancies may reach 30% for mean multiplicities and a factor of two (up to 30%) for negative (positive) pion dN/dy values at y≈0.
- For K⁺ mesons, the model underestimates mean multiplicities by up to 40% at lower energies, with dN/dy(y≈0) discrepancies reaching a factor of two.
- For antiprotons, the model overpredicts mean multiplicities by factors from 1.5 to 2.2 for all beam momenta (20-158 GeV/c).
- For protons at 158 GeV/c, the discrepancy reaches almost a factor of two for dN/dy(y≈0).
- The π⁻ inverse slope at forward rapidity shows deviations of about 20% from experimental data at lower beam momenta.

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses by Vovchenko and Uzh that used UrQMD to study a previous NA61/SHINE dataset including only negatively charged pion spectra, as well as NA49 data on p+p interactions at top SPS energy. The new NA61/SHINE data covering all identified particles across the full SPS energy range (20-158 GeV/c) provide a baseline for studying the onset of deconfinement in heavy-ion collisions, previously observed by the NA49 Collaboration at about 30 GeV/c in central Pb+Pb reactions. The results show that the present UrQMD v3.4 model still cannot describe these data quantitatively, and the authors suggest that improvements such as including low-mass diffraction dissociation and adjusting binary inelastic cross sections could be tested, though a complex tuning process involving both p+p and A+A collisions would be needed.
