# Dispersive Analysis of $D$- and $B$-Meson Form Factors with Chiral and Heavy-Quark Constraints

> We analyze the isovector vector form factors of $D$, $D^*$, $B$, and $B^*$ mesons at low energies. We employ all constraints due to chiral and heavy-quark symmetry, and include the physics of resonant pion-pion rescattering in a model-independent way, using dispersion theory. Special attention is paid to the analytic properties of these form factors, which include anomalous thresholds due to triangle diagrams that are located on the physical Riemann sheets in some of the form factors. We extract the couplings of the $ρ(770)$ resonance to all these heavy mesons by determining the appropriate pole residues.

## Key claim

The isovector vector form factors of $D$, $D^*$, $B$, and $B^*$ mesons at low energies can be determined model-independently using dispersion theory, but only when chiral and heavy-quark symmetry constraints are combined with the physics of resonant pion-pion rescattering, and special attention is paid to anomalous thresholds that appear on the physical Riemann sheets for some of the form factors.

## Method

The authors construct the isovector vector form factors for pseudoscalar and vector heavy-light mesons (D, D*, B, B*) by first decomposing the matrix elements into Bardeen-Tung-Tarrach (BTT) Lorentz structures free of kinematic constraints. They then derive unitarity relations for the form factors in terms of the pion vector form factor and the P-wave projections of the M(*)M(*) → ππ scattering amplitudes, which are modeled using heavy-meson chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order in the chiral counting and leading order in the heavy-quark expansion, including both Born exchange terms and contact interactions. The resulting dispersion relations are solved using the Omnès formalism, incorporating the ππ P-wave phase shift and the analytic structure including anomalous thresholds from triangle diagrams.

## Key numbers

- Averaged meson masses used: M_D = 1.86725(4) GeV, M_D* = 2.00856(4) GeV, M_B = 5.27956(5) GeV, M_B* = 5.32475(20) GeV, M_π = 139.57039(18) MeV
- Pion decay constant: F_π = 92.32(10) MeV
- Heavy-meson-pion coupling constant: |g| = 0.547(6), determined from D*+ → D+π0 and D*+ → D0π+ partial decay widths

## Context

This work extends the dispersive approach previously applied to nucleon form factors (Granados 2017, Alarcon 2017, Leupold 2017, Junker 2019) and other hadronic systems to the heavy-light meson sector, combining heavy-quark chiral perturbation theory (Wise 1992, Yan 1992, Burdman 1992) with dispersion theory. The framework enables model-independent extraction of ρ(770) coupling constants to heavy mesons via pole residues on the second Riemann sheet, and provides the necessary input for future investigations of the electromagnetic form factors and binding forces of X, Y, Z exotic states with hidden charm or bottomness.
