# Dynamics of pion production in heavy-ion collisions around 1A GeV energies

> Within the framework of the improved isospin dependent quantum molecular dynamics (ImIQMD) model, the dynamics of pion emission in heavy-ion collisions in the region 1 A GeV as a probe of nuclear symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities is investigated systematically. The total pion multiplicities and the $π^{-}/π^{+}$ yields are calculated for selected Skyrme parameters SkP, SLy6, Ska and SIII, and also for the cases of different stiffness of symmetry energy with the parameter SLy6. The influence of Coulomb potential, symmetry energy and in-medium pion potential on the pion production is investigated and compared each other by analyzing the distributions of transverse momentum and longitudinal rapidity and also the excitation functions of the total pion and the $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio. The directed flow, elliptic flow and polar angle distributions are calculated at the cases of different collision centralities and symmetry energies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}$ ratio in heavy-ion collisions around 1A GeV is a sensitive probe of the nuclear symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities, but the sensitivity is conditional: the ratio increases with the stiffness of the symmetry energy only up to a point, and a "superhard" symmetry energy actually reduces the ratio because it lowers the probability of neutron-neutron collisions in the high-density region.

## Method

The authors used the improved isospin dependent quantum molecular dynamics (ImIQMD) model to simulate heavy-ion collisions, incorporating Skyrme energy-density functionals with different symmetry energy stiffness parameters (soft, linear, hard, superhard) and four specific Skyrme forces (SkP, SLy6, Ska, SIII). Pion production was modeled via the decay of $\Delta$(1232) and N*(1440) resonances created in inelastic nucleon-nucleon scattering, and the evolution of pions was governed by Hamilton's equations including Coulomb, symmetry energy, and in-medium pion optical potential terms. Calculations were compared with experimental data from the FOPI collaboration for systems such as $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au and $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy at saturation density is set to $E_{sym}(\rho_{0})=31.5$ MeV at $\rho_{0}=0.165$ fm$^{-3}$ for the parameterized cases with $\gamma_{s}=0.5$ (soft), 1 (linear), 2 (hard), and 3 (superhard).
- The total pion multiplicity in $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca head-on collisions is calculated for incident energies from near threshold up to 1.5A GeV and compared with FOPI data; the model "predicts rather well the total yields at higher incident energies, but slightly overestimates the values near threshold energies."
- The $\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}$ ratio for Sn isotope collisions at $E_{lab}=0.4$A GeV varies with the N/Z of the system and with the stiffness of the symmetry energy; the ratio decreases with incident energy and approaches the isobar model value at $E_{lab}>1.0$A GeV.
- In $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au at 1A GeV, the transverse momentum distributions show that the pion optical potential enhances high-momentum pion production, while the Coulomb potential reduces high-momentum $\pi^{-}$ production.
- The directed flow $v_{1}$ of charged pions in $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au at 1.5A GeV shows an "S-shape" consistent with FOPI data, with $\pi^{+}$ antiflow relative to protons observed at larger impact parameters (b=8-10 fm).

## Context

This work extends previous transport model studies of pion production as a probe of the symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities, specifically addressing the conflicting results between the IBUU04 model (which found a very soft symmetry energy from FOPI data, as reported by Xiao et al. (2009)) and the RBUU model from the Catania group (which found a hard symmetry energy, as reported by Ferini et al. (2006) and Providencia et al. (2010)). The ImIQMD model enables a systematic comparison of different Skyrme forces and symmetry energy stiffness parameters, and the results enable future experimental studies at facilities such as FAIR, RIKEN, and FRIB to constrain the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy more precisely.
