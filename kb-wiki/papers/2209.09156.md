# Nuclear DFT electromagnetic moments in heavy deformed open-shell odd nuclei

> Within the nuclear DFT approach, we determined the magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments for paired nuclear states corresponding to the proton (neutron) quasiparticles blocked in the p11/2- (n13/2+) intruder configurations. We performed calculations for all deformed open-shell odd nuclei with 63<=Z<=82 and 82<=N<=126. Time-reversal symmetry was broken in the intrinsic reference frame and self-consistent shape and spin core polarizations were established. We determined spectroscopic moments of angular-momentum-projected wave functions and compared them with available experimental data. We obtained good agreement with data without using effective g-factors or effective charges in the dipole or quadrupole operators, respectively. We also showed that the intrinsic magnetic dipole moments, or those obtained for conserved intrinsic time-reversal symmetry, do not represent viable approximations of the spectroscopic ones.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that nuclear density-functional theory (DFT) can reproduce experimental magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments for heavy deformed open-shell odd nuclei across a broad region of the nuclear chart without using effective g-factors or effective charges, provided that time-reversal symmetry is broken and angular-momentum projection is performed. However, the agreement is qualified by notable exceptions in light Hg isotopes and in 147Gd, and the authors note that the calculated magnetic dipole moments of heavier nuclei appear to call for stronger time-odd polarization effects than those produced by the simple spin-spin interaction employed.

## Method

The authors performed tagged quasiparticle-blocking calculations using the Skyrme functional UNEDF1 with pairing correlations, focusing on the π11/2⁻ and ν13/2⁺ intruder configurations in all deformed open-shell odd nuclei with 63≤Z≤82 and 82≤N≤126. They broke time-reversal symmetry in the intrinsic reference frame, established self-consistent shape and spin core polarizations, and determined spectroscopic moments by performing angular-momentum projection (AMP) of the intrinsic paired and unpaired states using the code hfodd (v3.16n). The isovector Landau parameter g₀'=1.7(4) was used for the spin-spin interaction, with no other parameters adjusted in the work.

## Key numbers

- Calculations performed for all nuclei with 63≤Z≤82 and 82≤N≤126, covering 450 heavy open-shell deformed and paired odd nuclei
- Fixed average constant neutron and proton pairing gaps at Δn=Δp=1 MeV used in the initial step
- Intrinsic mass quadrupole moments constrained to Q₂₀=-10 or +10 b for initial solutions
- Single-particle states cut at 60 MeV for the pairing treatment
- Neutron and proton pairing strengths increased by 20% with respect to the original UNEDF1 parameter adjustment
- Particle-number projection (PNP) performed together with AMP gave magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments within about 1% of the AMP-only values
- Calculated magnetic dipole moment of 147Gd is -1.30 μN, disagreeing with two measured values of -0.24(7) μN and +0.49(2) μN
- For the π11/2⁻ configurations near N=126, departures from Schmidt limits depend relatively strongly on proton numbers; for ν13/2⁺ near N=82, the dependence is relatively weak
- Transition to oblate shapes in Hg isotopes occurs at N=115; below N=107 the prolate minimum disappears entirely

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous DFT approach (Sas22b) that was applied to selected nuclei without pairing correlations, now including pairing to cover all nuclei in the region. It contrasts with traditional shell-model calculations that often resort to effective g-factors or effective charges tuned to specific data (Ney03, Pap13, Klo19, Rod20b), and with ab initio approaches that remain challenging for heavy systems (Car15, Hen18, Pas13, Str22). The results enable systematic DFT calculations of electromagnetic moments across the entire Segrè chart for all odd nuclei and spins and parities, with work on this extensive goal reported as in progress.
