# Quark-quark correlations and baryon electroweak observables

> The simple independent quark models have difficulties explaining simultaneously the totality of the known hyperon magnetic moments and hyperon semi-leptonic decay rates. We show that both the Goldstone boson loop contributions and the two-quark effective exchange currents are essential in explaining these observables.

# Quark-quark correlations and baryon electroweak observables

## Key claim

The paper shows that both Goldstone boson loop contributions and two-quark effective exchange currents (denoted J2hat) are essential to explain hyperon magnetic moments and semileptonic decay rates, contrary to the assumption in Lipkin (1999) that these observables cannot be simultaneously explained within a simple additive quark picture.

## Method

The authors calculate baryon magnetic moments and weak decay axial-vector coupling ratios using the cloudy bag (chiral bag) model, which includes both pion-loop corrections and a two-body exchange current arising from gluon-exchange-induced spin-dependent quark-quark interactions. The strength of the two-body current is constrained by the octet-decuplet mass splitting (e.g., the N-Delta mass difference). The results are compared to experimental data for octet baryons.

## Key numbers

- Experimental magnetic moments: mu_Lambda = -0.613 +/- 0.004 n.m., mu_Xi- = -0.6507 +/- 0.0025 n.m., giving R = mu_Xi-/mu_Lambda = 1.062 +/- 0.005
- The two-body current contribution G = 0.2 n.m. in the cloudy bag model
- The pion-loop correction delta_mu_pi = 0.59 n.m.
- The ratio R' = (mu_Sigma+ + 2 mu_Sigma-)/mu_Lambda changes from the additive quark model value of -1 to approximately -0.23 experimentally; the combined pion-cloud and J2hat contributions resolve this discrepancy
- The ratio R'' = (g_A/g_V for Sigma- -> n)/(g_A/g_V for Lambda -> p) has experimental value -0.473 +/- 0.026, while the additive quark model gives -1/3; the cloudy bag calculation including J2hat gives R'' = -0.47

## Context

This work extends the chiral phenomenology approach of Thomas et al. (2000) and Cloet et al. (2002), who found that Goldstone boson loops alone could only increase the Xi-/Lambda magnetic moment ratio R to 0.99, still below the experimental value of 1.062. The paper resolves the "Xi-Lambda puzzle" and the "Sigma-Lambda problem" identified by Lipkin (1999), who argued that the magnetic moment ratio R' and the weak decay ratio R'' could not be simultaneously explained. The results demonstrate that quark-quark spin-spin correlations mediated by gluon exchange produce two-body exchange currents analogous to nuclear Z-graph contributions, and these must be included alongside chiral loop corrections for a complete description of baryon electroweak observables.
