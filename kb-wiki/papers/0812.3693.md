# Description of Four-Body Breakup Reaction with the Method of Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels

> We present a method for smoothing discrete breakup $S$-matrix elements calculated by the method of continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC). This smoothing method makes it possible to apply CDCC to four-body breakup reactions. The reliability of the smoothing method is confirmed for two cases, $^{58}$Ni($d$, $p n$) at 80 MeV and the $E1$ transition of $^6$He. We apply CDCC with the smoothing method to $^6$He breakup reaction at 22.5 MeV. Multi-step breakup processes are found to be important.

## Key claim

The authors present a smoothing method for converting discrete breakup S-matrix elements from continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) calculations into continuous functions, enabling application of CDCC to four-body breakup reactions; the method's reliability is confirmed for two test cases, but the smoothing procedure requires solving for the overlap between pseudostates and exact continuum states within a truncated model space, which is an approximation.

## Method

The authors extend CDCC to four-body breakup reactions by replacing the average discretization method (infeasible for three-body projectiles) with a pseudostate discretization method using Gaussian basis functions to diagonalize the projectile's internal Hamiltonian. They then develop a "model space smoothing method" that constructs continuous breakup S-matrix elements by summing discrete CDCC S-matrix elements weighted by smoothing factors, which are overlaps between pseudostates and exact continuum states obtained by solving the three-body Lippmann-Schwinger equation within the same model space. The method is tested on the 58Ni(d,pn) reaction at 80 MeV and the E1 transition of 6He, then applied to 6He breakup on 209Bi at 22.5 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For the 58Ni(d,pn) test at 80 MeV: the maximum momentum kmax is taken to be 1.3 fm^-1, with 17 states for each of ℓ=0 and ℓ=2; the scattering wave functions are connected with asymptotic forms at Rmax=30 fm.
- For the 6He E1 transition test: three parameter sets for the 1^- state use rmax and ymax values of 50.0 fm (Set I), 70.0 fm (Set II), and 80.0 fm (Set III), with imax and jmax ranging from 17 to 19.
- For the 6He+209Bi application: the incident energy is 22.5 MeV.
- The smoothing factor difference between the model space smoothing method and exact calculation for the two-body projectile test is below 1% level.

## Context

This work extends the CDCC method, previously limited to three-body breakup processes (two-body projectiles), to four-body breakup reactions involving three-body projectiles like 6He. It builds on prior pseudostate discretization methods using Gaussian basis functions (Matsumoto, 2003; Egami, 2004) and THO basis functions (Moro, 2007) that successfully described elastic scattering of three-body projectiles, but could not handle real four-body breakup processes. The new smoothing method enables calculation of quintuple differential cross sections for four-body breakup, and the application to 6He+209Bi at 22.5 MeV shows that multistep breakup processes are significant, opening the door to more complete analyses of halo nucleus scattering experiments.
