# Shape phase transition in the odd Sm nuclei: effective order parameter and odd-even effect

> Some binding-energy-related quantities serving as effective order parameters have been used to analyze the shape phase transition in the odd Sm nuclei. It is found that the signals of phase transition in the odd Sm nuclei are greatly enhanced in contrast to the even Sm nuclei. A further analysis shows that the transitional behaviors related to pairing in the Sm nuclei can be well described by the mean field plus pairing interaction model, with a monotonic decrease in the pairing strength $G$.

## Key claim

The first-order shape phase transition occurs in odd Sm isotopes as it does in even Sm isotopes, but the phase transition signals in the odd species are greatly enhanced by the odd neutron effect, with the amplitude of the deformation contribution to the two-neutron separation energy increasing about 25% near the critical point compared to the even Sm nuclei.

## Method

The authors analyzed binding-energy-related quantities (two-neutron separation energy S2n, its deformation part S(2n)def, and odd-even mass difference D) as effective order parameters for shape phase transitions in odd Sm nuclei. They fitted the odd-even mass differences using a Nilsson mean-field plus pairing interaction Hamiltonian solved with two approximation schemes: the exactly solvable nearest-orbit pairing interaction (ENO) and the constant pairing interaction solved by BCS theory (CBCS). The pairing strength G was extracted as a function of neutron number for both even and odd Sm isotopes.

## Key numbers

- The deformation contribution S(2n)def was obtained by fitting with parameters A = -19.8 MeV for even Sm nuclei and A = -19.4 MeV for odd Sm nuclei, with B = 0.66 MeV
- The amplitude of S(2n)def in odd Sm nuclei increases about 25% near the critical point (N=90, Np=4) compared to that in even Sm nuclei
- The critical point of the phase transition in Sm nuclei is at Np = 4 (N = 90)
- The odd-even mass differences D are all negative for odd Sm nuclei but positive for even Sm nuclei, with extreme values around N=90
- The pairing strength G in the ENO scheme is almost ten times that in the CBCS scheme

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shape phase transitions in even-even Sm isotopes (Casten 2007) to odd-A Sm nuclei, using binding-energy-related effective order parameters introduced by Iachello (2004) and Zhang (2013). The analysis shows that the pairing interaction with monotonic decrease in pairing strength G drives the phase transition, and that exactly solvable pairing models (ENO scheme) reproduce experimental pairing-excitation energies better than BCS-based constant pairing models. The results enable microscopic analysis of shape phase transitions in odd-A nuclei and call for more realistic shell model calculations with different truncation schemes (Sun 2008, Zhao 2000) to confirm the predictions.
