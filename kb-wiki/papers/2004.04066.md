# The Superconducting Critical Temperature

> Two principles govern the critical temperature for superconducting transitions: (1)~intrinsic strength of the pair coupling and (2)~effect of the many-body environment on the efficiency of that coupling. Most discussions take into account only the first but we argue that the properties of unconventional superconductors are governed more often by the second, through dynamical symmetry relating normal and superconducting states. Differentiating these effects is essential to charting a path to the highest-temperature superconductors.

# The Superconducting Critical Temperature

## Key claim
The critical temperature for unconventional superconductors is governed more by the many-body environment's effect on pairing efficiency, specifically through dynamical symmetry relating normal and superconducting states, than by intrinsic pair coupling strength, which is the factor most discussions emphasize.

## Method
The authors apply fermion dynamical symmetry, truncating the Hilbert space to a collective subspace defined by an SU(4) Lie algebra whose generators represent antiferromagnetic (AF), spin-singlet and spin-triplet bondwise pairs, spin, and charge operators. They derive exact solutions for three dynamical symmetry chains (SU(4) ⊃ SO(4) for AF Mott insulator, SU(4) ⊃ SU(2) for d-wave singlet superconductivity, and SU(4) ⊃ SO(5) for a critical dynamical symmetry) and compare the resulting phase diagram and order parameters to cuprate data using only two adjustable parameters: effective singlet pairing strength G₀ and effective AF correlation strength χ.

## Key numbers
- The cuprate phase diagram is described quantitatively with only two significant parameters: effective singlet pairing strength G₀ and effective AF correlation strength χ.
- Superconductivity exists only for doping less than approximately 25% holes, as SU(4) symmetry breaks when more than one-quarter of lattice sites are occupied by holes.
- The derivative of the pairing order parameter Δ with respect to doping x at x=0 is infinite (∂Δ/∂x|x=0 = ∞), indicating a fundamental Cooper-like instability at zero doping.
- The derivative of the AF order parameter Q with respect to doping x at the critical doping x = x_q is negative infinity (∂Q/∂x|x=x_q = -∞), indicating a quantum phase transition at the critical doping point labeled P_q in the phase diagram.

## Context
This work extends the fermion dynamical symmetry approach previously developed by the authors (Guidry, Sun, and collaborators, 2001–2011) for cuprate superconductors, providing a unified framework that relates conventional BCS superconductivity, unconventional superconductivity, and nuclear structure collective states through shared Lie algebras. The SU(4) model predicts that compounds with similar symmetries but different microscopic structure should exist, a prediction validated by the 2008 discovery of iron-based superconductors, and suggests that the high critical temperatures in cuprates, iron-based, heavy-fermion, and organic superconductors all arise from the same mechanism: a collective rotation in symmetry space between a low-entropy competing-order state and a low-entropy superconducting state, rather than from strong intrinsic pairing alone.
