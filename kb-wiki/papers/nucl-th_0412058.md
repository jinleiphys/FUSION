# Pseudoscalar and vector mesons as q\bar{q} bound states

> Two-body bound states such as mesons are described by solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation. We discuss recent results for the pseudoscalar and vector meson masses and leptonic decay constants, ranging from pions up to c\bar{c} bound states. Our results are in good agreement with data. Essential in these calculation is a momentum-dependent quark mass function, which evolves from a constituent-quark mass in the infrared region to a current-quark mass in the perturbative region. In addition to the mass spectrum, we review the electromagnetic form factors of the light mesons. Electromagnetic current conservation is manifest and the influence of intermediate vector mesons is incorporated self-consistently. The results for the pion form factor are in excellent agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

The rainbow-ladder truncation of Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations, combined with a momentum-dependent effective quark-quark interaction that reproduces perturbative QCD in the ultraviolet, yields pseudoscalar and vector meson masses and leptonic decay constants in good agreement with experimental data, provided the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity is preserved, which guarantees that excited pseudoscalar meson decay constants vanish in the chiral limit.

## Method

The authors solved the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for pseudoscalar and vector meson bound states in rainbow-ladder truncation, using a dressed quark propagator obtained from the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation with a momentum-dependent effective interaction that reduces to the one-loop perturbative running coupling at large momenta. The electromagnetic form factors were computed in a generalized impulse approximation using the dressed quark-photon vertex obtained from an inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation, which automatically satisfies electromagnetic current conservation via the vector Ward-Takahashi identity.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state pion mass: 0.14 GeV (experiment: 0.14 GeV); pion decay constant: 0.131 GeV (experiment: 0.131 GeV)
- First radially excited pion mass: 1.10 GeV (experiment: 1.3 GeV); its decay constant: -0.002 GeV
- Ground-state rho meson mass: 0.74 GeV (experiment: 0.77 GeV); rho decay constant: 0.206 GeV (experiment: 0.22 GeV)
- Ground-state eta_c mass: 2.98 GeV (experiment: 2.98 GeV); eta_c decay constant: 0.33 GeV (experiment: 0.34 GeV)
- Pion charge radius squared: 0.44 fm^2 (experiment: 0.44 fm^2)
- Charged kaon charge radius squared: 0.38 fm^2 (experiment: 0.34(5) fm^2)
- Neutral kaon charge radius squared: -0.085 fm^2 (experiment: -0.052(26) fm^2)
- Partial decay width rho^+/- -> pi gamma: 53 keV (experiment: 68 keV)
- Partial decay width omega -> pi gamma: 479 keV (experiment: 757 keV)

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach of Maris and Roberts (1997) and Maris and Tandy (1999) to include radially excited pseudoscalar mesons and electromagnetic form factors of light mesons, demonstrating that the rainbow-ladder truncation preserves both the vector and axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identities. The results confirm the prediction that excited pseudoscalar meson decay constants vanish in the chiral limit, as derived from the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity by Holl et al. (2004). The calculations enable systematic studies of meson properties within a Poincare-covariant framework that can be extended to incorporate dressed quark-gluon vertex corrections, which lattice simulations by Bowman et al. (2002) and Zhang et al. (2003) suggest are important for quantitative agreement with the quark propagator.
