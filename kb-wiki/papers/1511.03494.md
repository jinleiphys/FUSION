# Solvababe supersymmetric algebraic model for descriptions of transitional even and odd mass nuclei near the critical point of the spherical to unstable shapes

> Exactly solvable solution for the spherical to gamma - unstable transition in transitional nuclei based on dual algebraic structure and nuclear supersymmetry concept is proposed. The duality relations between the unitary and quasispin algebraic structures for the boson and fermion systems are extended to mixed boson-fermion system. It is shown that the relation between the even-even and odd-A neighbors implied by nuclear supersymmetry in addition to dynamical symmetry limits can be also used for transitional regions. The experimental evidences are presented for even- even [E(5)] and odd-mass [E(5/4)] nuclei near the critical point symmetry.

# Solvable supersymmetric algebraic model for descriptions of transitional even and odd mass nuclei near the critical point of the spherical to unstable shapes

## Key claim

The authors propose that nuclear supersymmetry, previously applied only to dynamical symmetry limits, can be extended to describe transitional regions between spherical and gamma-unstable shapes in both even-even and odd-A nuclei, using a solvable Richardson-Gaudin approach based on generalized quasispin algebras. The claim is hedged by the fact that the model is demonstrated only for the specific case of fermions with spin j=3/2 coupled to a boson core, and the authors state that a detailed group theoretical description will be given in a subsequent publication.

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian for the U(5) to O(6) transitional region using generators of an affine generalized quasispin algebra (GQA) for mixed boson-fermion systems, with duality relations connecting the unitary and quasispin algebraic structures. The Hamiltonian is diagonalized via the Bethe-ansatz method, yielding eigenvalues expressed in terms of solutions to a set of nonlinear Bethe-ansatz equations. The model is applied simultaneously to the even-even nucleus and its odd-A neighbor using the same set of fit parameters, consistent with the nuclear supersymmetry concept, with control parameters Cs and Cf varied between 0 and 1 to interpolate between the U(5) and O(6) dynamical symmetry limits.

## Key numbers

- For the pair 134Ba-135Ba: control parameters Cs = 0.52, Cf = 0.8; Hamiltonian parameters alpha = 333.12 keV, beta = 1.945 keV, gamma = 0.73 keV; fit quality sigma = 155.42 keV, phi = 10.73%.
- For the pair 130Xe-131Xe: control parameters Cs = 0.55, Cf = 0.9; Hamiltonian parameters alpha = 187.84 keV, beta = 0.9564 keV, gamma = 10.98 keV; fit quality sigma = 143.29 keV, phi = 12.4%.
- The total boson number N = 10 was used for the energy surface calculations shown in the figures.
- The R4/2 ratio (E(nu_d=2)/E(nu_d=1)) values of 2.2-2.3 indicate transitional nuclei, consistent with the U(5)-O(6) transition.
- The expectation value of the d-boson number operator remains approximately constant for Cs less than 0.45 and changes rapidly over the range 0.65 to 1.0.

## Context

This work extends the nuclear supersymmetry approach, originally developed by Iachello and collaborators in the early 1980s for dynamical symmetry limits, to transitional regions between spherical and gamma-unstable shapes. It builds on the E(5) critical point symmetry for even-even nuclei proposed by Iachello (2000) and the E(5/4) critical supersymmetry for odd-A nuclei proposed by Iachello (2001), as well as earlier studies of quantum phase transitions in odd-even nuclei using supersymmetric schemes by Frank et al. and Jolie et al. The model enables simultaneous description of even-even and odd-A neighboring nuclei across the entire U(5)-O(6) transitional region using a single set of parameters, and the authors identify 134Ba-135Ba and 130Xe-131Xe as the best candidates for this supersymmetric transitional scheme.
