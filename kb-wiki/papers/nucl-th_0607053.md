# The relativistic self-energy in nuclear dynamics

> It is a well known fact that Dirac phenomenology of nuclear forces predicts the existence of large scalar and vector mean fields in matter. To analyse the relativistic self-energy in a model independent way, modern high precision nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) potentials are mapped on a relativistic operator basis using projection techniques. This allows to compare the various potentials at the level of covariant amplitudes were a remarkable agreement is found. It allows further to calculate the relativistic self-energy in nuclear matter in Hartree-Fock approximation. Independent of the choice of the nucleon-nucleon interaction large scalar and vector mean fields of several hundred MeV magnitude are generated at tree level. In the framework of chiral EFT these fields are dominantly generated by contact terms which occur at next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion. Consistent with Dirac phenomenology the corresponding low energy constants which generate the large fields are closely connected to the spin-orbit interaction in $NN$ scattering. The connection to QCD sum rules is discussed as well.

## Key claim
Large scalar and vector mean fields of several hundred MeV magnitude are generated at tree level in nuclear matter from modern high-precision nucleon-nucleon potentials, independent of the specific potential choice, and in chiral effective field theory these fields arise dominantly from contact terms at next-to-leading order rather than from pion-exchange dynamics.

## Method
The authors mapped modern high-precision nucleon-nucleon potentials (Bonn A, CD-Bonn, Nijmegen potentials, Argonne v18, and chiral EFT potentials up to N3LO) onto a relativistic operator basis using projection techniques based on the Clifford algebra in Dirac space. Starting from the angular-momentum representation of each potential, they transformed to plane-wave helicity states and then to Lorentz-invariant amplitudes in Dirac space, allowing comparison of the covariant amplitudes across different potential models. The relativistic self-energy in nuclear matter was then calculated in the Hartree-Fock approximation at tree level.

## Key numbers
- At nuclear saturation density (ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³), empirical scalar and vector mean fields from relativistic mean field fits are Σs ≈ -350 MeV and Σ₀ ≈ +300 MeV.
- The central single-particle potential U_cent ≈ Σ₀ + Σs is of order -50 MeV.
- The spin-orbit potential U_S.O. ∝ (Σ₀ - Σs) ≈ 750 MeV.
- The ratio (Σ₀ + Σs)/(Σ₀ - Σs) is less than 10%, characterizing pseudo-spin symmetry breaking.
- The Fermi momentum over nucleon mass ratio is k_F/M ≈ 0.25.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Plohl, Fuchs, and van Dalen (2006) by providing a detailed formalism for mapping various nucleon-nucleon potentials onto Dirac phenomenology and computing the resulting relativistic self-energy. It connects Dirac phenomenology (Serot and Walecka, 1986; Ring, 1996) and relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations (Brockmann and Machleidt, 1990; Gross-Boelting, Fuchs, and Faessler, 1999) with chiral effective field theory (Entem and Machleidt, 2002, 2003; Epelbaum, Glöckle, and Meißner, 2005) and QCD sum rules (Cohen, Furnstahl, and Griegel, 1991; Drukarev and Levin, 1991). The results enable a model-independent understanding of how large scalar and vector mean fields arise from the vacuum nucleon-nucleon interaction and clarify the connection between low-energy constants in chiral EFT and the spin-orbit interaction in nucleon-nucleon scattering.
