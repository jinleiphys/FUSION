# Neutron Transversity at Jefferson Lab

> Nucleon transversity and single transverse spin asymmetries have been the recent focus of large efforts by both theorists and experimentalists. On-going and planned experiments from HERMES, COMPASS and RHIC are mostly on the proton or the deuteron. Presented here is a planned measurement of the neutron transversity and single target spin asymmetries at Jefferson Lab in Hall A using a transversely polarized $^3$He target. Also presented are the results and plans of other neutron transverse spin experiments at Jefferson Lab. Finally, the factorization for semi-inclusive DIS studies at Jefferson Lab is discussed.

# Neutron Transversity at Jefferson Lab

## Key claim
The paper presents a planned measurement of neutron transversity via single-spin asymmetry in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) on a transversely polarized $^3$He target at Jefferson Lab Hall A, which would provide the first measurement of neutron transversity complementary to existing proton and deuteron measurements from HERMES and COMPASS. The authors note that the experiment focuses on the valence quark region ($x = 0.19 - 0.34$) at moderate $Q^2$ ($1.77 - 2.73$ GeV$^2$), and that the success of extracting transversity depends on independent measurements of Collins fragmentation functions, such as those from the Belle collaboration.

## Method
The planned experiment uses a 6 GeV continuous-wave electron beam with polarization up to 85% incident on a transversely polarized $^3$He target (effective polarized neutron target with in-beam polarization of about 40%) in Hall A. The left-side high resolution spectrometer (HRS$_L$) at 16$^\circ$ detects hadrons ($\pi^-$, $K^-$) while the BigBite spectrometer at 30$^\circ$ detects scattered electrons, with vertical coils added to the target to rotate polarization direction in the transverse plane for separating Collins and Sivers effects. Inclusive transverse spin experiments (E97-103, E99-117, E94-010) measured the spin structure function $g_2^n$ and its moment $d_2^n$ to study higher-twist effects, while unpolarized SIDIS measurements test factorization at JLab kinematics.

## Key numbers
- Polarized $^3$He target in-beam polarization: about 40%
- Polarized luminosity reached: $10^{36}$ s$^{-1}$cm$^{-2}$
- Electron beam energy: up to 6 GeV (with planned upgrade to 12 GeV)
- Electron beam current: up to 180 $\mu$A
- Electron beam polarization: up to 85%
- Planned experiment kinematics: $x = 0.19 - 0.34$, $Q^2 = 1.77 - 2.73$ GeV$^2$
- HRS momentum range: up to 4 GeV/c
- HRS$_L$ angle: 16$^\circ$; BigBite angle: 30$^\circ$
- Beamtime: 24 days for ${\vec n}(e,e^\prime \pi^-)X$ single spin asymmetry
- Inclusive $g_2^n$ experiment (E97-103): $Q^2$ range 0.58 to 1.36 GeV$^2$ at $x \approx 0.2$
- $d_2^n$ extraction (E99-117): average $Q^2$ of 5 GeV$^2$
- Typical JLab SIDIS kinematics: $Q^2$ around 2 GeV$^2$, $W^2$ of 4-10 GeV$^2$, $W'^2$ around 4 GeV$^2$, $x$ range 0.1-0.4, $z$ range 0.4-0.6
- Factorization tests from Hall C and Hall B: data consistent at the 10% level

## Context
This work extends the transversity program from HERMES (2003) and COMPASS (2002-2004) measurements on proton and deuteron targets to the neutron, using the $^3$He target technique developed at Jefferson Lab Hall A. The inclusive transverse spin experiments (E97-103, E99-117, E94-010) provided precision measurements of $g_2^n$ and $d_2^n$ that show higher-twist effects become significantly positive at $Q^2$ below 1 GeV$^2$, contradicting bag model and Chiral Soliton model predictions of negative or near-zero values, while lattice QCD predictions for $d_2^n$ were also negative. The planned neutron transversity measurement, combined with HERMES and COMPASS data, will constrain both $u$-quark and $d$-quark transversity distributions in the valence region, and the factorization tests from Hall C and Hall B provide essential validation for SIDIS studies at JLab energies.
