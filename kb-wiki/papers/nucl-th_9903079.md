# Partial Wave Decomposition for Meson Exchange Currents in Few-Nucleon Systems

> We develop an approach for calculating matrix elements of meson exchange currents between 3N basis states in (jJ)-coupling and a 3N bound state. The contribution generated by $π$- and $ρ$-exchange are included in the consideration. The matrix elements are expressed in terms of multiple integrals in the momentum space. We apply a technique of the partial wave decompositions and carry out some angular integrations in closed form. Different representations appropriate for numerical calculations are derived for the matrix elements of interest. The momentum dependences of the matrix elements are studied and benchmark results are presented. The approach developed is of interest for the investigations of deuteron- proton radiative capture and ^3He photo- and electrodisintegration when the interaction in the initial or final nuclear states is taken into account by solving the Faddeev equations.

## Key claim

The paper develops a method for calculating matrix elements of two-body meson exchange currents (MEC) between three-nucleon (3N) basis states and a 3N bound state, specifically for pion and rho exchange, with the aim of enabling gauge-invariant calculations of electromagnetic reactions when final-state interactions are treated via Faddeev equations. The abstract's claim of "benchmark results" is qualified by the fact that the numerical results presented are limited to the pion-exchange current and serve as a test of the partial-wave decomposition technique, not as a full calculation of reaction observables.

## Method

The authors express the matrix elements of the two-body current operator between 3N basis states in (jJ)-coupling as multiple integrals over momentum-space angles. They perform a partial-wave decomposition of the pion- and rho-exchange currents, carrying out three of the angular integrations analytically in closed form and leaving the remaining integrals for numerical evaluation. The method is designed to be compatible with the existing Faddeev-based approach for one-body currents used in studies of deuteron-proton radiative capture and helium photo- and electrodisintegration.

## Context

This work extends the momentum-space Faddeev formalism for three-nucleon reactions developed by Gloeckle and collaborators (Gloeckle, 1983; Gloeckle, Phys. Rep.) to include explicit two-body meson exchange currents, which were previously treated only via the Siegert theorem or omitted in calculations such as those by Ishikawa (1996) and Golak (1997). The method enables future calculations of deuteron-proton radiative capture and helium photo- and electrodisintegration that simultaneously include both MEC and full final-state interactions from realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials, moving beyond the separate treatments of MEC (e.g., by Riska, 1985; Mathiot, 1989) and rescattering (e.g., by Fonseca, 1993; Sandhas and Schadow, 1996).
