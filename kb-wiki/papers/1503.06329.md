# Chiral Corrections to Nucleon Two- and Three-Point Correlation Functions

> We consider multi-particle contributions to nucleon two- and three-point functions from the perspective of chiral dynamics. Lattice nucleon interpolating operators, which have definite chiral transformation properties, can be mapped into chiral perturbation theory. Using the most common of such operators, we determine pion-nucleon and pion-delta couplings to nucleon two- and three-point correlation functions at leading order in the low-energy expansion. The couplings of pions to nucleons and deltas in two-point functions are consistent with simple phase-space considerations, in accordance with the Lehmann spectral representation. An argument based on available phase space on a torus is utilized to derive the scaling of multiple-pion couplings. While multi-pion states are indeed suppressed, this suppression scales differently with particle number compared to that in infinite volume. For nucleon three-point correlation functions, we investigate the axial-vector current at vanishing momentum transfer. The effect of pion-nucleon and pion-delta states on the extraction of the nucleon axial charge is assessed. We show that couplings to finite volume multi-particle states could potentially lead to overestimation of the axial charge. Hence pion-nucleon excited states cannot explain the trend seen in lattice QCD calculations of the nucleon axial charge.

## Key claim

The paper shows that pion-nucleon and pion-delta excited-state contamination in lattice QCD nucleon correlation functions, computed using chiral perturbation theory at leading order, drives the axial-vector three-point function upward and could lead to overestimation of the nucleon axial charge; therefore such contamination cannot explain the trend of underestimation seen in lattice QCD calculations of the axial charge.

## Method

The author maps standard lattice nucleon interpolating operators with definite chiral transformation properties into heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory operators. Using one-loop heavy-nucleon chiral perturbation theory on a finite periodic torus, the spectral weights for pion-nucleon and pion-delta contributions to nucleon two-point functions are computed at leading order in the low-energy expansion, and the axial-vector current three-point function at vanishing momentum transfer is evaluated with all possible intermediate states arising at one-loop order.

## Key numbers

- The pion-nucleon spectral weight in finite volume is given by $|Z_{\vec{n}}|^2 = \frac{3 g_A^2}{4 f^2 L^3} \frac{\vec{k}^2}{[\vec{k}^2 + m_\pi^2]^{3/2}}$, where $f = 130$ MeV, $g_A = 1.25$, and $\vec{k} = 2\pi \vec{n}/L$
- The pion-delta spectral weight in finite volume is $|Z^\D_{\vec{n}}|^2 = \frac{2 g_{\D N}^2}{3 f^2 L^3} \frac{\vec{k}^2}{\sqrt{\vec{k}^2 + m_\pi^2} [\sqrt{\vec{k}^2 + m_\pi^2} + \D]^2}$, with $g_{\D N} = 1.5$ and $\D = 0.29$ GeV
- Pion-nucleon contamination in the nucleon two-point function effective mass is at the level of a few percent for lattice parameters $a = 0.1$ fm and $L = 48a$ at the physical pion mass
- For multi-particle states on a torus near threshold, the spectral weights scale as $\sqrt{E - E_{\text{Th}}}^{\, N - 2}$, compared to $\sqrt{E - E_{\text{Th}}}^{\, 3N - 5}$ in infinite volume

## Context

This work extends the author's prior analysis of chiral corrections to nucleon correlation functions in infinite volume (Tiburzi, 2009) and builds on the model-independent determination of pion-nucleon contributions to nucleon two-point functions by Bar (2015). The results contradict the hypothesis that pion-nucleon excited-state contamination causes the underestimation of the axial charge observed in many lattice QCD calculations, instead showing such contamination would lead to overestimation. The analysis enables lattice practitioners to better isolate and control systematic uncertainties from excited-state contamination in calculations of nucleon structure, particularly for the axial charge benchmark quantity.
