# Neutron Star vs Quark Star in the Multimessenger Era

> Neutron stars (NSs) which could contain exotic degrees of freedom in the core and the self-bound quark stars (QSs) made purely of absolutely stable deconfined quark matter are still two main candidates for the compact objects observed in pulsars and gravitational wave (GW) events in binary star mergers. We perform a Bayesian model-agnostic inference of the properties of NSs and QSs by combining multi-messenger data of GW170817, GW190425, PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0740+6620, PSR J1614-2230, PSR J0348+0432 as well as ab initio calculations from perturbative quantum chromodynamics and chiral effective field theory. We find the NS scenario is strongly favored against the QS scenario with a Bayes factor of NS over QS $\mathcal{B}^\text{NS}_\text{QS} = 11.5$. In addition, the peak of the squared sound velocity $c_s^2 \sim 0.5c^2$ around $3.5$ times nuclear saturation density $n_0$ observed in the NS case disappears in the QS case which suggests that the $c_s^2$ first increases and then saturates at $c_s^2 \sim 0.5c^2$ above $\sim 4n_0$. The sound velocity and trace anomaly are found to approach the conformal limit in the core of heavy NSs with mass $M \gtrsim 2M_{\odot}$, but not in the core of QSs.

## Key claim
The paper finds that the current multimessenger data and ab initio calculations from perturbative quantum chromodynamics and chiral effective field theory strongly favor the neutron star scenario over the quark star scenario for compact objects, with a Bayes factor of 11.5. The authors note this preference is mainly driven by the NICER measurement of PSR J0030+0451, and that removing this constraint reduces the Bayes factor to 1.3.

## Method
The authors perform a Bayesian model-agnostic inference using a speed-of-sound extension approach to construct equations of state for both neutron star and quark star matter. For neutron stars, they match chiral effective field theory results at low densities (0.58n0 to 1.1n0) to a piecewise-linear interpolation of the squared sound velocity up to 12n0, with perturbative QCD constraints at high density. For quark stars, they sample the sound velocity and chemical potential at zero pressure assuming the self-bound property, with the same high-density constraints. They combine data from gravitational wave events GW170817 and GW190425, NICER mass-radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, Shapiro delay mass measurements of several pulsars, and ab initio calculations.

## Key numbers
- Bayes factor of neutron star over quark star: B^NS_QS = 11.5, indicating strong evidence for the neutron star hypothesis
- Maximum mass for neutron stars: M_TOV,NS = 2.17^{+0.26}_{-0.15} M_sun (90% credible interval)
- Maximum mass for quark stars: M_TOV,QS = 2.49^{+0.47}_{-0.35} M_sun (90% credible interval)
- Radius of 1.4 M_sun neutron star: R_1.4,NS = 12.44^{+0.74}_{-0.71} km (90% credible interval)
- Radius of 1.4 M_sun quark star: R_1.4,QS = 11.41^{+0.64}_{-0.61} km (90% credible interval)
- Tidal deformability of 1.4 M_sun neutron star: Lambda_1.4,NS = 504^{+223}_{-174} (90% credible interval)
- Tidal deformability of 1.4 M_sun quark star: Lambda_1.4,QS = 642^{+260}_{-204} (90% credible interval)
- Peak squared sound velocity for neutron star matter: c_s^2 ~ 0.5c^2 at n ~ 3.5n0 (n_pk,NS = 0.55^{+0.19}_{-0.14} fm^{-3})
- Central density of 2 M_sun neutron star: n_c,NS,2M_sun = 0.56^{+0.14}_{-0.10} fm^{-3}
- Central density of maximum mass neutron star: n_c,NS,max = 0.90^{+0.11}_{-0.12} fm^{-3}
- Energy per baryon at zero pressure for quark star matter: [654, 812] MeV (68% credible interval)
- Number density at zero pressure for quark star matter: [0.24, 0.35] fm^{-3} (68% credible interval)

## Context
This work extends the Bayesian model-agnostic approach of Annala (2019) and Gorda (2022) by directly comparing neutron star and quark star hypotheses using the same multimessenger dataset. The results contradict the quark star hypothesis proposed by Bodmer (1971) and Witten (1984) that deconfined quark matter might be the true ground state of QCD matter. The finding that sound velocity and trace anomaly approach the conformal limit in heavy neutron stars with M > 2 M_sun is consistent with the conclusions of Fujimoto (2022), Marczenko (2022), and Annala (2023). The Bayes factor analysis enables future work to incorporate additional multimessenger data to further distinguish between compact star scenarios.
