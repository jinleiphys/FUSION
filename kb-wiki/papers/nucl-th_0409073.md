# Neutron Star Properties from an NJL Model Modified to Simulate Confinement

> The NJL model has recently been extended with a method to simulate confinement. This leads in mean field approximation to a natural mechanism for the saturation of nuclear matter. We use the model to investigate the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter and then use it to compute the properties of neutron stars.

# Neutron Star Properties from an NJL Model Modified to Simulate Confinement

## Key claim

The NJL model extended with a proper-time cutoff scheme to simulate confinement can, in mean field approximation, produce stable neutron stars up to a maximum mass of 2.19 solar masses, but only for central densities below the threshold where phase transitions to quark matter or color superconducting states would occur.

## Method

The authors use a flavor SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model modified with a proper-time cutoff regularization to simulate quark confinement, constructing nucleons as quark-diquark bound states via the Faddeev equation in the static approximation. The equation of state for asymmetric nuclear matter is derived from the quark-level Lagrangian using the path integral formalism, with electrons introduced as a Fermi gas to enforce charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium. Neutron star masses, radii, and profiles are then computed by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass: 2.19 solar masses, at a central baryon density of 0.97 fm^{-3}
- Typical mass neutron star (1.4 solar masses): central density of 0.4 fm^{-3} and radius around 12.4 km
- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: (0.16 fm^{-3}, -15 MeV) per nucleon
- Symmetry energy coefficient: 32.5 MeV, set by fitting the rho meson coupling constant G_rho at saturation density
- Constituent quark mass at zero density: M_0 = 400 MeV
- Current quark mass: m = 16.93 MeV
- Ultraviolet cutoff: Lambda_UV = 638.5 MeV; infrared cutoff: Lambda_IR = 200 MeV
- Coupling constants: G_pi = 19.60 GeV^{-2}, G_s = 0.51 G_pi, G_omega = 0.37 G_pi, G_rho = 0.092 G_pi

## Context

This work extends the NJL model with confinement simulation developed by Bentz and Thomas (2001) to the study of neutron stars, building on earlier work that showed this approach naturally produces nuclear matter saturation. The results are compared to other quark-level models such as the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model of Guichon (1987) and the chiral soliton model of Smith (2004), and the computed neutron star masses and radii are found to be in basic agreement with astrophysical data. The model enables future investigation of phase transitions to quark matter and color superconducting states at higher central densities, as previously explored by Bentz (2002).
