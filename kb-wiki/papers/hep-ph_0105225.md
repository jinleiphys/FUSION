# Chiral symmetry restoration and the linear form of baryonic Regge trajectories

> It has recently been suggested that the observed structure of parity doublets, seen in the spectrum of highly excited baryons, may be due to the effective restoration of chiral symmetry for these states. This chiral symmetry restoration high in the spectrum is consistent with the concept of quark-hadron duality. Moreover, if QCD dynamics implies the linear Regge trajectories for highly excited baryons, then the MacDowell symmetry requires the parity doubling to appear, which shows that linear Regge trajectories and chiral symmetry restoration are well compatible. On the contrary, in the low-energy part of baryon spectrum the parity doublets are absent because of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. Then the MacDowell symmetry implies that there should be no linear parallel Regge trajectories. Experimental data shows that this is indeed the case.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed parity doubling in highly excited baryons, interpreted as effective chiral symmetry restoration, is consistent with linear Regge trajectories for those states, while the absence of parity doublets in the low-energy spectrum (due to spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking) implies that low-lying Regge trajectories must be curved and non-parallel.

## Method

The author uses the Ioffe current for baryons to construct the two-point correlation function, separating it into chiral even and odd terms. The operator product expansion (OPE) is applied to show that the chiral odd term, which violates chiral symmetry, is suppressed by powers of momentum in the deep space-like region. The MacDowell symmetry, a consequence of analyticity in Regge theory, is then applied to baryonic Regge trajectories: for linear trajectories (α(t) = α₀ + α′ t), the symmetry forces positive- and negative-parity trajectories to coincide, implying degenerate parity doublets, which is the same condition as chiral symmetry restoration in the Wigner mode.

## Key numbers

- The OPE for the chiral odd term is given as Π^(odd)(q²) = −(1/(4π²)) ⟨q̅q⟩ q² ln(−q²), showing suppression by a power of q² relative to the chiral even term.
- The OPE for the chiral even term includes a gluonic condensate contribution ⟨(α_s/π) G^a_{μν} G^a_{μν}⟩ and a term (2/(3q²)) ⟨q̅q⟩².
- The "asymptotic regime" for e⁺e⁻ → hadrons is stated to set up at s ∼ 2–3 GeV².
- The masses of the low-lying parity partners N(939), 1/2⁺ and N(1535), 1/2⁻ are given as explicit examples of non-degenerate parity doublets.

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Cohen and Glozman (2000) that parity doublets in highly excited baryons indicate effective chiral symmetry restoration, and the concept of quark-hadron duality from Poggio, Quinn, and Weinberg (1976). It contradicts the expectation from potential constituent quark models (Glozman and Riska, 1996) that predict many missing states around 2 GeV without parity doubling. The result enables a unified understanding where the linear Regge trajectories expected from confinement are compatible with chiral symmetry restoration high in the spectrum, while spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking (Ioffe, 1981) explains the curved trajectories and absence of parity doublets at low energies.
