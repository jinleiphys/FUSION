# Pion photoproduction off the proton in a gauge-invariant chiral unitary framework

> We investigate pion photoproduction off the proton in a manifestly gauge-invariant chiral unitary extension of chiral perturbation theory. In a first step, we consider meson-baryon scattering taking into account all next-to-leading order contact interactions. The resulting low-energy constants are determined by a fit to s-wave pion-nucleon scattering and the low-energy data for the reaction pi- p --> eta n. To assess the theoretical uncertainty, we perform two different fit strategies. Having determined the low-energy constants, we then analyse the data on the s-wave multipole amplitudes E0+ of pion and eta photoproduction. These are parameter-free predictions, as the two new low-energy constants are determined by the neutron and proton magnetic moments.

## Key claim

The authors present parameter-free predictions for s-wave multipole amplitudes of pion and eta photoproduction off the proton, after first determining low-energy constants from fits to meson-baryon scattering data; however, the predictions are only parameter-free because the two new low-energy constants appearing in the photoproduction sector are fixed by the neutron and proton magnetic moments, and the hadronic sector itself requires fitting 17 parameters (14 low-energy constants plus three subtraction scales) to scattering data.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for six coupled meson-baryon channels (π⁰p, π⁺n, ηp, K⁺Λ, K⁺Σ⁰, K⁰Σ⁺) using a kernel derived from the next-to-leading order chiral Lagrangian contact terms, without employing the on-shell approximation common in the literature. Low-energy constants are determined by fitting to s-wave πN partial waves from the SAID WI08 analysis and to differential cross sections for π⁻p → ηn from Prakhov et al. (2005). The photoproduction amplitudes are then constructed by coupling the hadronic scattering solution to the electromagnetic field, with the two additional low-energy constants fixed by the proton and neutron magnetic moments.

## Key numbers

* πN scattering lengths: a_{1/2} = +174.5^{+15.2}_{-32.8} × 10⁻³/M_{π⁺} and a_{3/2} = -87.0^{+4.3}_{-4.2} × 10⁻³/M_{π⁺} (fit strategy I)
* Pole position of N*(1535): W = (1.547^{+0.004}_{-0.021} − i 0.046^{+0.004}_{-0.017}) GeV (fit strategy I)
* Pole position of N*(1650): W = (1.597^{+0.017}_{-0.020} − i 0.045^{+0.010}_{-0.015}) GeV (fit strategy I)
* Fit performed for elastic πN scattering in S₁₁ and S₃₁ partial waves up to W_cms < 1.56 GeV (strategy I) or S₁₁ up to 1.70 GeV and S₃₁ only below 1.20 GeV (strategy II)
* Systematic error assigned to πN partial wave amplitudes: 0.005 below W = 1.28 GeV, 0.030 above that energy
* Systematic error on π⁻p → ηn data: 6% as reported by Prakhov et al. (2005)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous gauge-invariant chiral unitary coupled-channel approach from kaon photoproduction (Borasoy et al., 2007) and eta photoproduction (Ruic et al., 2011) to pion photoproduction, incorporating next-to-leading order chiral Lagrangian contributions that were developed for meson-baryon scattering in Bruns et al. (2010). The approach dynamically generates the N*(1535) and N*(1650) resonances, in contrast to constituent quark model pictures where these states are not naturally explained. The method enables parameter-free predictions for s-wave photoproduction multipoles, and the authors note that future improvements require inclusion of crossed-channel dynamics (as in Roy-Steiner equation approaches by Ditsche et al., 2012) and explicit resonance degrees of freedom for states not generated dynamically.
