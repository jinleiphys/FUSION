# Azimuthal distribution of mean transverse rapidity in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> By the sample generated by the AMPT with string melting model, we compare the azimuthal distributions of multiplicity, total transverse rapidity, and mean transverse rapidity. It is demonstrated that the azimuthal distribution of mean transverse rapidity is a good probe of the radial kinetic expansion. The anisotropic part of the distribution characterizes the anisotropic nature of the radial expansion, and isotropic part is combinations of thermal motion and isotropic radial expansion.

## Key claim

Using AMPT with string melting model simulations, the azimuthal distribution of mean transverse rapidity is demonstrated to be a good probe of radial kinetic expansion, with its anisotropic part characterizing the anisotropic nature of radial expansion and its isotropic part combining thermal motion and isotropic radial expansion.

## Method

The authors generated approximately 0.2 million minimum bias events of Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV using the AMPT model with string melting, which implements a partonic phase and reproduces elliptic flow data from RHIC. They computed and compared the azimuthal distributions of multiplicity, total transverse rapidity, and mean transverse rapidity in the full rapidity range y in [-5,5], expanding each distribution in Fourier series with the dominant second harmonic term. The centrality dependence of the anisotropic coefficients and the mass dependence of the isotropic part were then examined for pions, kaons, and protons.

## Key numbers

* The anisotropic coefficient v2 of mean transverse rapidity is the smallest among the three distributions at each centrality, with v2 of total transverse rapidity the largest and v2 of multiplicity in the middle (Fig. 2).
* At centralities 0-5%, 30-40%, and 60-70%, the azimuthal distribution of mean transverse rapidity shows nearly isotropic behavior for central collisions, largest anisotropy in mid-central collisions, and smaller anisotropy in peripheral collisions (Fig. 3).
* For three particle species (pion, kaon, proton), the isotropic parameter v0 of mean transverse rapidity is largest for the lightest particle (pion) and smallest for the heaviest particle (proton), with kaon values between them (Fig. 4).

## Context

This work extends the approach of using azimuthal distributions of mean transverse momentum to study radial kinetic expansion, as suggested by Ollitrault (1998), by replacing transverse momentum with transverse rapidity which is more convenient under Lorentz transformations. The study uses the AMPT model with string melting developed by Zhang, Ko, Li, and Lin (2000, 2005), which reproduces RHIC elliptic flow data. The results provide a model-independent way to extract anisotropic radial rapidity, which is helpful for hydrodynamic calculations and for model-independent determination of shear viscosity in relativistic heavy ion collisions.
