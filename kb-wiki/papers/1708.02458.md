# Gluon quasidistribution function at one loop

> We study the unpolarized gluon quasidistribution function in the nucleon at one loop level in the large momentum effective theory. For the quark quasidistribution, power law ultraviolet divergences arise in the cut-off scheme and an important observation is that they all are subjected to Wilson lines. However for the gluon quasidistribution function, we first point out that the linear ultraviolet divergences also exist in the real diagram which is not connected to any Wilson line. We then study the one loop corrections to parton distribution functions in both cut-off scheme and dimensional regularization to deal with the ultraviolet divergences. In addition to the ordinary quark and gluon distributions, we also include the quark to gluon and gluon to quark splitting diagrams. The complete one-loop matching factors between the quasi and light cone parton distribution functions are presented in the cut-off scheme. We derive the $P^z$ evolution equation for quasi parton distribution functions, and find that the $P^z$ evolution kernels are identical to the DGLAP evolution kernels.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first complete one-loop calculation of the unpolarized gluon quasidistribution function in the large momentum effective theory (LaMET), showing that while the factorization between quasi and light-cone parton distribution functions holds for gluons at one loop, the gluon case introduces a linear ultraviolet divergence in a real diagram that is not connected to any Wilson line, unlike the quark case where such divergences are always associated with Wilson lines.

## Method

The authors computed one-loop corrections to both quasi and light-cone gluon parton distribution functions in Feynman gauge, using a finite gluon mass to regulate infrared divergences and employing both a momentum cut-off scheme and dimensional regularization to handle ultraviolet divergences. They included all real and virtual diagrams for gluon-to-gluon, quark-to-quark, quark-to-gluon, and gluon-to-quark splitting processes, and derived the complete one-loop matching factors between quasi and light-cone distributions. The calculation was performed for a nucleon target with momentum \(P^z\) in the large momentum limit.

## Key numbers

- The linear ultraviolet divergence in the gluon quasidistribution from Fig. (a) real correction scales as \(\frac{3}{4}\frac{\Lambda}{P^z}\) in the cut-off scheme, where \(\Lambda\) is the UV cut-off and \(P^z\) is the nucleon momentum.
- The one-loop gluon-to-gluon quasi distribution in the cut-off scheme for \(0<x<1\) contains the term \(\frac{3}{4}\frac{\Lambda}{P^z}\) plus logarithmic terms in \(m_g^2/(P^z)^2\).
- The one-loop gluon self-energy virtual correction in dimensional regularization after \(\overline{\mathrm{MS}}\) subtraction gives \(\delta \mathcal{C}^{(1)}_{gg} = \frac{\alpha_s}{4\pi}\left[\left(\frac{5}{3}C_A \ln\frac{\mu^2}{m_g^2} - \frac{4}{3}T_F n_f \ln\frac{\mu^2}{-m_g^2}\right) + \left(\frac{55}{9} - \sqrt{3}\pi\right)C_A - \frac{20}{3}T_F n_f\right]\) for the light-cone PDF.
- The \(P^z\) evolution kernels for quasi parton distribution functions are found to be identical to the DGLAP evolution kernels.

## Context

This work extends the LaMET framework, previously applied to quark quasidistributions by Ji (2013) and Xiong (2013), to the gluon case for the first time. It builds on the one-loop matching for nonsinglet quark distributions studied by Xiong (2013) and Ma (2014), and addresses the renormalization issues raised by Ji (2017) regarding linear divergences. The results enable lattice QCD calculations of gluon parton distribution functions, which are essential for Higgs boson and heavy quarkonium production at hadron colliders, by providing the matching coefficients needed to extract light-cone PDFs from lattice-computable quasi PDFs.
