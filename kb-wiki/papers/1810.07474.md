# DIS at small x and hadron-hadron scattering at high energies via the holographic Pomeron exchange

> We present our analysis on the high energy scattering processes in the framework of the holographic QCD, assuming that the Pomeron exchange gives a dominant contribution to the cross sections. Focusing on the two-body scattering, we employ the Brower-Polchinski-Strassler-Tan Pomeron exchange kernel to describe the strong gluonic interaction, and utilize the bottom-up AdS/QCD models to obtain the density distributions of the involved hadrons in the five-dimensional AdS space, to calculate the total cross sections. Considering the unpolarized deep inelastic scattering at small Bjorken $x$ and the high energy hadron-hadron scattering, we show that our calculations are consistent with the presently available experimental data. Our predictions made via this framework can be tested at various experimental facilities in the future.

## Key claim

The holographic QCD framework, combining the BPST Pomeron exchange kernel with bottom-up AdS/QCD hadron density distributions, can simultaneously describe both DIS structure functions at small Bjorken x and hadron-hadron total cross sections at high energies, with the longitudinal structure function predicted without any additional adjustable parameters beyond those fixed by the proton F2 data.

## Method

The authors employed the Brower-Polchinski-Strassler-Tan (BPST) Pomeron exchange kernel in five-dimensional AdS space to describe the dominant gluonic interaction in two-body scattering. They used bottom-up AdS/QCD models to obtain density distributions of hadrons (nucleon, pion) and the U(1) vector field wave function for the photon, then calculated total cross sections via the optical theorem and structure functions from the eikonal representation, including a modified kernel with a confinement term characterized by sharp cutoffs z0 and z0'.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon F2 and FL structure functions were calculated for Q2 values ranging from 0.2 to 12 GeV2 and compared with HERA data
- The nucleon-nucleon total cross section was calculated as a function of sqrt(s) and compared with data up to sqrt(s) approximately 10 TeV, where the model shows substantial deviation from the COMPETE empirical fit above 10 TeV
- The photon structure function was calculated for various Q2 and compared with OPAL data at LEP and the GRS PDF set at NLO accuracy
- The pion structure function and pion-nucleon and pion-pion total cross sections were calculated without any adjustable parameters using the bottom-up AdS/QCD meson model

## Context

This work extends the holographic QCD approach of Brower, Polchinski, Strassler, and Tan (2006) for the Pomeron exchange kernel, combined with the bottom-up AdS/QCD models of Erlich (2005) and Hong (2006) for hadron density distributions. It builds on prior analyses by Watanabe (2012, 2013, 2015, 2018) and uses the nucleon gravitational form factors from Abidin (2008, 2009). The framework enables predictions for structure functions of other hadrons (pion, photon) and hadron-hadron cross sections that can be tested at future experimental facilities including the LHC and linear colliders.
