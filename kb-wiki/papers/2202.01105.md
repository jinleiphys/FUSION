# Nuclear Forces for Precision Nuclear Physics -- a collection of perspectives

> This is a collection of perspective pieces contributed by the participants of the Institute of Nuclear Theory's Program on Nuclear Physics for Precision Nuclear Physics which was held virtually from April 19 to May 7, 2021. The collection represents the reflections of a vibrant and engaged community of researchers on the status of theoretical research in low-energy nuclear physics, the challenges ahead, and new ideas and strategies to make progress in nuclear structure and reaction physics, effective field theory, lattice QCD, quantum information, and quantum computing. The contributed pieces solely reflect the perspectives of the respective authors and do not represent the viewpoints of the Institute for Nuclear theory or the organizers of the program.

## Key numbers
- The HAL QCD Collaboration reported that di-nucleon systems do not support bound states at heavy pion masses, while earlier works had claimed deeply bound di-nucleons with binding energies of approximately 20 MeV (NPLQCD 2012).
- For the H dibaryon in the SU(3)-flavor symmetric limit, the continuum-limit binding energy was 4.6(1.3) MeV, whereas on the coarsest lattice spacing it exceeded 30 MeV (Green 2021).
- The lowest excited-state gap in two-baryon systems is approximately 20–50 MeV for typical lattice volumes, corresponding to a decay time scale of 4–10 fm.
- The noise of two-baryon systems overwhelms the signal before approximately 2 fm for calculations at larger-than-physical pion mass, with signal degradation scaling as exp(-2(M_N - 1.5 m_pi) t) at asymptotically large times.
- The t-channel cut for nucleon-nucleon scattering at physical pion mass starts about 5 MeV below threshold.

## Context
This collection addresses the long-standing discrepancy between lattice QCD groups that reported deeply bound di-nucleon systems at heavy pion masses (NPLQCD 2012, Yamazaki 2012, Orginos 2015, Berkowitz 2015, Wagman 2017) and the HAL QCD Collaboration (Inoue 2011, Ishii 2012), which found no such bound states. The volume discusses how recent calculations using variational methods with momentum-space operators (Francis 2018, Horz 2020, Amarasinghe 2021) have not identified deeply bound di-nucleons, suggesting that earlier results suffered from uncontrolled systematic errors due to false plateaus. The perspectives outline the need for reliable spectral analysis using the variational method combined with the Luscher quantization condition, and highlight open questions about discretization effects, quantization conditions on left-hand cuts, and the extension of these methods to three-nucleon systems and matrix elements.
