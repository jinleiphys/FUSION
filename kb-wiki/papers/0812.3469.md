# A new approach to bulk viscosity in strange quark matter at high densities

> A new method is proposed to compute the bulk viscosity in strange quark matter at high densities. Using the method it is straightforward to prove that the bulk viscosity is positive definite, which is not so easy to accomplish in other approaches especially for multi-component fluids like strange quark matter with light up and down quarks and massive strange quarks.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new method for computing bulk viscosity in strange quark matter that guarantees a positive-definite result, which is not easily achieved in conventional approaches for multi-component fluids where the strange quark mass is large compared to temperature and chemical potential.

## Method

The method relates energy dissipation to entropy production in a quasi-control number system, where each cell maintains constant baryon number but allows particle exchange. The bulk viscosity is derived from the entropy production formula in dissipative hydrodynamics, using the reaction rates of weak processes (d↔s transition, Urca I, and Urca II) and the chemical potential differences between reacting species.

## Key numbers

- The resulting bulk viscosity from the multi-component fluid approach is about **one order of magnitude larger** than those from conventional approaches (Wang 1985, Sawyer 1989, Madsen 1992, Dai 1996, Anand 1999, Xiaoping 2004).
- The calculation assumes the strange quark mass \(m_s\) is much larger than light quark masses, and that chemical potential differences satisfy \(\Delta\mu_k \ll T \ll \mu\).
- The oscillation period is \(\tau = 2\pi/\omega\), where \(\omega\) is the pulsation frequency.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous multi-component fluid treatment of strange quark matter (Dong 2007) by providing a method that guarantees positive bulk viscosity, which could not always be ensured in that earlier approach. It contrasts with conventional simple-fluid treatments (Wang 1985, Sawyer 1989, Madsen 1992) that implicitly assume equal fluid velocity divergences for all particle species, an approximation that breaks down when the strange quark mass is large. The new method enables reliable calculations of bulk viscosity for compact star applications, particularly for understanding r-mode instability damping (Andersson 1997, Friedman 1997, Lindblom 1998).
