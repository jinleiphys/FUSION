# Some intriguing aspects of multiparticle production processes

> Multiparticle production processes provide valuable information about the mechanism of the conversion of the initial energy of projectiles into a number of secondaries by measuring their multiplicity distributions and their distributions in phase space. They therefore serve as a reference point for more involved measurements. Distributions in phase space are usually investigated using the statistical approach, very successful in general but failing in cases of small colliding systems, small multiplicities, and at the edges of the allowed phase space, in which cases the underlying dynamical effects competing with the statistical distributions take over. We discuss an alternative approach, which applies to the whole phase space without detailed knowledge of dynamics. It is based on a modification of the usual statistics by generalizing it to a superstatistical form. We stress particularly the scaling and self-similar properties of such an approach manifesting themselves as the phenomena of the log-periodic oscillations and oscillations of temperature caused by sound waves in hadronic matter. Concerning the multiplicity distributions we discuss in detail the phenomenon of the oscillatory behaviour of the modified combinants apparently observed in experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a superstatistical generalization of standard Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics, resulting in Tsallis (quasi-power law) distributions, can describe multiparticle production across the whole phase space without detailed knowledge of dynamics, but the approach is phenomenological and the physical meaning of the necessary constraint in the maximum-entropy derivation remains not fully understood.

## Method

The authors review the derivation of Tsallis distributions from several mechanisms: fluctuations of temperature (superstatistics), fluctuations of multiplicity, preferential attachment in networks, multiplicative noise in Langevin equations, and maximum-entropy with Shannon entropy under a specific constraint. They then apply these formalisms to analyze transverse momentum distributions and multiplicity distributions in hadronic and nuclear collisions, focusing on the oscillatory behavior of modified combinants and log-periodic oscillations of distributions as functions of transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

* The Tsallis distribution parameter \(q\) is related to temperature fluctuations by \(q = 1 + \omega_T^2\) where \(\omega_T^2 = Var(T)/\langle T \rangle^2\).
* For a system with fixed total energy \(U\) and fixed multiplicity \(N\), the single-particle energy distribution is a Tsallis distribution with \(q = (N-3)/(N-2) < 1\) and temperature \(T = U/(N-2)\).
* For negative binomial multiplicity fluctuations, the resulting energy distribution is a Tsallis distribution with \(q = 1 + 1/k > 1\) where \(k\) is the NBD parameter.
* The relation between multiplicity fluctuations and the nonextensivity parameter is \(q - 1 = Var(N)/\langle N \rangle^2 - 1/\langle N \rangle\).

## Context

This review extends the authors' previous work on superstatistics and nonextensive approaches to multiparticle production (Wilk and Włodarczyk, 2012; 2015) and builds on the Tsallis statistics framework (Tsallis, 1988; 2009). It contrasts with the standard statistical approach that fails for small colliding systems and at phase-space boundaries, and with purely QCD-based hard scattering descriptions. The work enables further investigation of log-periodic oscillations in transverse momentum distributions and oscillatory modified combinants in multiplicity distributions as signatures of self-similarity and sound waves in hadronic matter.
