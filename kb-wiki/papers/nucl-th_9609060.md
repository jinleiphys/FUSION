# On the symmetries of the 1+1 dimensional relativistic fluid dynamics

> A very intuitive description of nucleus-nucleus collision phenomena is provided by the relativistic fluid dynamics. We consider a 1+1 dimensional relativistic imperfect fluid flow to approximate the high energy heavy ion collision. The article investigates the application of the continuous symmetry group on the relativistic fluid energy-momentum tensor conservation equations in the ultrarelativistic limit $γ\rightarrow \infty$.

# On the symmetries of the 1+1 dimensional relativistic fluid dynamics

## Key claim
The authors claim that the 1+1 dimensional relativistic imperfect fluid dynamics equations, in the ultrarelativistic limit (γ → ∞), admit a nine-parameter continuous symmetry group, but only after discarding all but the highest power of the four-velocity U and the free terms from the energy-momentum conservation equations, an approximation they justify by asserting that U³ ≫ U² ≫ U ≫ 1 in that limit.

## Method
The authors start from the relativistic energy-momentum tensor conservation equations for an imperfect fluid in the Eckart frame, including shear viscosity η, bulk viscosity ζ, and heat conduction χ. They reduce the equations to 1+1 dimensions and then take the ultrarelativistic limit by retaining only the terms proportional to the highest power of the four-velocity components (u, v) and the free terms. They apply the standard Lie symmetry method (Olver, 1986) to this simplified system, solving the defining equations for the infinitesimal generators of the symmetry group and computing the second-order prolongation to find seven global differential invariants.

## Key numbers
- The mean free path Λ is estimated at about 1 fm, compared to the size of a heavy nucleus (uranium) of 15 fm.
- The symmetry group has nine parameters, with infinitesimal generators V₁ through V₉ listed explicitly.
- Seven global independent invariants (I₁ through I₇) of the symmetry group are obtained, each expressed in terms of the dependent variables (u, v, T, p) and their partial derivatives.

## Context
This work extends the Lie symmetry analysis previously applied by the same authors (Alexa and Vrinceanu, 1996) to a simpler form of the energy-momentum tensor conservation equations. It builds on the relativistic fluid dynamics approach to heavy ion collisions reviewed by Strottman (1990), and on the general symmetry-group methodology for differential equations developed by Olver (1986). The results provide a set of differential invariants that can be used to reduce the order of the original fluid dynamics equations, potentially enabling integration by quadratures for special types of symmetry groups.
