# Entropy Production in Dissipationless Hydrodynamics with an order parameter

> We study hydrodynamics coupled to order parameter based on linear sigma model. We obtain numerical solutions for both boost invariant and non-boost invariant solutions. Both solutions show the order parameter rises with oscillations, which persist at late time. The temperature drops with correlated oscillations, which can be approximated by a power law at mid-rapidity. We also find the entropy is conserved in the boost invariant case, but entropy production is seen in non-boost invariant solution. We interpret the entropy production as due to smoothening of inhomogeneity in the off-equilibrium state.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in dissipationless hydrodynamics coupled to an order parameter (the sigma field from the linear sigma model), entropy is strictly conserved only under boost-invariant (Bjorken) flow; in non-boost-invariant solutions, entropy is produced even without any explicit dissipative terms, because the evolution irreversibly smoothens out initial inhomogeneity in rapidity.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled equations of motion for the sigma mean field and the quark-fluid stress-energy tensor in Milner coordinates, using the linear sigma model at mean-field level with zero baryon chemical potential. They obtain numerical solutions for both boost-invariant (Bjorken) flow, where the flow velocity is fixed to \(u^\mu = (1,0,0,0)\), and non-boost-invariant flow, where the longitudinal velocity \(u^\tau\) is a dynamical field with Gaussian initial profiles for temperature, sigma field, and flow velocity. Entropy production is evaluated by integrating the entropy current over constant proper-time slices and over isothermal contours.

## Key numbers

*   For boost-invariant solutions, the temperature drop follows an approximate power law \(T \sim \tau^{-\alpha}\); the exponent \(\alpha\) increases with initial temperature \(T_0\) and approaches \(1/3\) in the high-temperature limit (chiral symmetry restored).
*   For non-boost-invariant solutions starting from the same initial temperature at mid-rapidity, the exponent \(\alpha\) is larger than in the boost-invariant case, indicating faster cooling (e.g., \(T_0 = 240\ \text{MeV}\) yields a larger \(\alpha\) than the Bjorken case).
*   The pion mass \(M_\pi = 138\ \text{MeV}\), sigma mass \(M_\sigma = 600\ \text{MeV}\), and pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 93\ \text{MeV}\) are used as experimental inputs to fix the linear sigma model parameters.
*   Initial conditions for non-boost-invariant solutions use a Gaussian variance \(\Delta^2 = 5\) in rapidity, with maximum temperature \(T_0 = 240\ \text{MeV}\), minimum temperature \(T_\text{min} = 40\ \text{MeV}\), and maximum flow velocity \(u_\text{max} = 2\).
*   Entropy per transverse area \(\int d\eta\, \tau s\) shows mild production over proper time for \(T_0 = 200, 240, 280, 340\ \text{MeV}\) (all with \(u_\text{max}=2\)), with saturation at later time for higher initial temperatures.
*   Entropy production is more pronounced when evaluated on isothermal contours; for \(T_0 = 280\ \text{MeV}\) and \(u_\text{max}=2\), the total entropy per transverse area increases significantly with contour time.

## Context

This work extends studies of hydrodynamics with a critical mode near the QCD phase transition, following the approach of Nahrgang (2011) and Mishustin (1998) who coupled the sigma field to hydrodynamic equations. It contrasts with the standard Bjorken picture (Bjorken, 1983) by showing that when boost invariance is broken, entropy is produced even in the absence of dissipation, analogous to the irreversible expansion of an ideal gas into vacuum. The results enable future studies of entropy production in systems with a first-order phase transition, where larger initial inhomogeneity is expected to generate even more entropy.
