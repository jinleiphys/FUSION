# QCD equation of state at finite chemical potential from unbiased exponential resummation of the lattice QCD Taylor series

> Exponential resummation of the QCD finite-density Taylor series has been recently introduced as an alternative way of resumming the finite-density lattice QCD Taylor series. Unfortunately the usual exponential resummation formula suffers from stochastic bias which must be subtracted before identifying genuine higher-order contributions. In this paper, we present a new way of subtracting the stochastic bias at the level of each individual gauge configuration, up to a certain order of either the Taylor series or the cumulant expansion, by modifying the argument of the exponential. Retaining the exponential form of the resummation allows us to also calculate the phase factor of the fermion determinant on each gauge configuration. We present our results for the excess pressure, number density, and the average phase factor and show that the new results contain less stochastic bias and are in better agreement with the QCD Taylor series compared to the previous exponential resummation.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method to subtract stochastic bias from the exponential resummation of the lattice QCD Taylor series at finite chemical potential, achieving results that are in better agreement with the standard Taylor series than previous biased exponential resummation, but only up to a finite order of either the Taylor or cumulant expansion rather than fully unbiased.

## Method

The authors modify the argument of the exponential in the resummation formula on a configuration-by-configuration basis, replacing the biased averages of the derivatives \(D_n^Y\) with modified coefficients \(C_n^Y\) (Eqs. 7-10) that subtract stochastic bias up to order \(N\) in the chemical potential, or with cumulant-like quantities \(L_m\) (Eqs. 13-16) that subtract bias up to order \(M\) in the cumulant expansion. They apply this formalism to lattice QCD data from the HotQCD collaboration using 2+1 flavors of rooted staggered quarks with the HISQ action on \(N_\tau=8\) lattices at temperatures of approximately 135, 157, and 176 MeV, using 500 to 2000 stochastic random volume sources per configuration to estimate the first eight derivatives of the fermion determinant.

## Key numbers

- Results are presented for excess pressure \(\Delta P/T^4\) and number density \(N/T^3\) in the range \(0 \leq |\mu_{B,I}/T| \leq 2\) for both baryon and isospin chemical potentials.
- Calculations use \(N_\tau=8\) lattices at three temperatures: \(T \sim 157\) MeV (approximately the chiral crossover temperature \(T_{pc}=156.5(1.5)\) MeV), \(T \sim 176\) MeV, and \(T \sim 135\) MeV.
- For the isospin case at \(T=157\) MeV, unbiased resummation results lie between second and fourth order Taylor results for \(0 \leq |\hat{\mu}_I^2| \leq 4\).
- For the baryon case at \(T=135\) MeV, biased results with 500 stochastic estimates are "close to zero" while unbiased results are "clearly non-zero and closer to the Taylor expansion results" for \(|\hat{\mu}_B^2| \leq 1.5\).
- The unbiased results were obtained using only 500 stochastic estimates for \(D_1^B\) and \(D_2^B\), compared to 2000 estimates used for some biased calculations.

## Context

This work extends the exponential resummation approach introduced by Mondal (2021) for the QCD equation of state at finite chemical potential, which resums the contributions of the first \(N\) derivatives of the fermion determinant to all orders. The new method corrects for the stochastic bias present in the original exponential resummation formula, which can be significant for finite numbers of random volume sources. The approach is tested against standard Taylor expansion results from the HotQCD collaboration (Bollweg 2021, 2022) and compared to an alternative cumulant expansion approach by Mitra (2022) that avoids bias but loses all-orders resummation and phase factor information. The unbiased exponential resummation preserves the ability to calculate the average phase factor of the fermion determinant, which is related to the sign problem and the location of singularities of the QCD partition function in the complex chemical potential plane.
