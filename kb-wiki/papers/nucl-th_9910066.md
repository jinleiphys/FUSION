# Possible $ΔΔ$ dibaryons in the quark cluster model

> In the framework of RGM, the binding energy of one channel $ΔΔ_{(3,0)}$($d^*$) and $ΔΔ_{(0,3)}$ are studied in the chiral SU(3) quark cluster model. It is shown that the binding energies of the systems are a few tens of MeV. The behavior of the chiral field is also investigated by comparing the results with those in the SU(2) and the extended SU(2) chiral quark models. It is found that the symmetry property of the $ΔΔ$ system makes the contribution of the relative kinetic energy operator between two clusters attractive. This is very beneficial for forming the bound dibaryon. Meanwhile the chiral-quark field coupling also plays a very important role on binding. The S-wave phase shifts and the corresponding scattering lengths of the systems are also given.

## Key claim
The paper claims that both the $\Delta\Delta_{(3,0)}$ (known as $d^*$) and the $\Delta\Delta_{(0,3)}$ systems form bound dibaryon states in the one-channel approximation of the chiral SU(3) quark cluster model, with binding energies of a few tens of MeV. The authors hedge that these states would have very broad widths because their masses lie above the thresholds for strong decay channels such as $\Delta\Delta \rightarrow \Delta N\pi$ and $\Delta\Delta \rightarrow NN\pi\pi$.

## Method
The authors employed the Resonating Group Method (RGM) within the chiral SU(3) quark cluster model, solving the bound-state equation for the six-quark system. The effective Hamiltonian includes confinement, one-gluon-exchange (OGE), pseudoscalar chiral field, and scalar chiral field induced potentials. Model parameters were fixed by reproducing the mass splittings among N, $\Delta$, $\Sigma$, and $\Xi$ baryons, and the stability conditions of octet and decuplet baryons. The binding energies and root-mean-square radii were calculated for the one-channel $\Delta\Delta_{(3,0)}$ and $\Delta\Delta_{(0,3)}$ systems, and results were compared across three chiral quark models (SU(3), extended SU(2), and SU(2)).

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of $\Delta\Delta_{(3,0)}$ ($d^*$) in Model I (SU(3) chiral quark model): 22.2 MeV (Set 1) and 18.5 MeV (Set 2), with corresponding RMS radii of 1.01 fm and 1.05 fm.
- Binding energy of $\Delta\Delta_{(0,3)}$ in Model I: 16.0 MeV (Set 1) and 13.5 MeV (Set 2), with corresponding RMS radii of 1.10 fm and 1.14 fm.
- In Model II (extended SU(2) chiral quark model), binding energy of $\Delta\Delta_{(3,0)}$ is 64.8 MeV (RMS 0.84 fm) and of $\Delta\Delta_{(0,3)}$ is 6.3 MeV (RMS 1.25 fm).
- In Model III (SU(2) chiral quark model), binding energy of $\Delta\Delta_{(3,0)}$ is 62.7 MeV (RMS 0.86 fm) and of $\Delta\Delta_{(0,3)}$ is 13.2 MeV (RMS 1.11 fm).
- Scattering lengths for $\Delta\Delta_{(3,0)}$ in Model I: -1.30 fm (Set 1) and -1.16 fm (Set 2).
- Scattering lengths for $\Delta\Delta_{(0,3)}$ in Model I: -2.56 fm (Set 1) and -2.72 fm (Set 2).

## Context
This work extends prior theoretical predictions of the $d^*$ dibaryon by Yazaki (1987) and others, who found attractive color magnetic interactions in the $\Delta\Delta_{(3,0)}$ system using OGE and confinement potentials. The authors also build on earlier calculations of the H dibaryon by Jaffe (1977) and subsequent studies of strangenessless and strange dibaryons. The prediction of a new bound $\Delta\Delta_{(0,3)}$ state, with lower spin (S=0) than the $d^*$ (S=3), suggests a candidate that may be more favorable for experimental detection, though both states are expected to have very broad widths due to strong decay channels.
