# Hadron production at the LHC: Any indication of new phenomena

> We confront soft Pomeron and gluon saturation models with the first LHC data on inclusive hadron production. We claim that while the first type of models are not able to describe some part of the LHC data, the Colour-Glass-Condensate (gluon saturation) approach gives an adequate description of the data. Here, we compare our published predictions with the recently available 7 TeV data. We firmly believe that if further experimental measurements confirm that the gluon saturation works, it will be a major discovery.

## Key claim

The authors claim that Colour-Glass-Condensate (gluon saturation) models adequately describe the first LHC 7 TeV data on inclusive hadron production, while soft Pomeron models cannot describe some part of the data; however, they qualify that the claim that soft models are unable to describe the LHC data is premature, because the experimental selection of measured events depends strongly on Monte Carlo simulations that cannot consistently describe diffraction production processes.

## Method

The authors confront two classes of models with LHC data on inclusive hadron production in proton-proton collisions: soft Pomeron models based on Reggeon field theory and N=4 Super Yang Mills, and high-density QCD models based on the Colour-Glass-Condensate (CGC) approach. For the CGC approach, they calculate inclusive mini jet cross-sections via k_T factorization, using the b-CGC saturation model for the dipole amplitude that describes HERA deep inelastic scattering data at small Bjorken-x, and relate the mini jet cross-section to hadron production via the Local Parton-Hadron Duality principle with two free parameters (overall factor C and mini jet mass m_jet) fixed at lower energy.

## Key numbers

* The soft Pomeron intercept from N=4 SYM is approximately 0.3, with slope approximately 0, in the limit of 2/sqrt(lambda) much less than 1.
* The GLMM model predicts sigma_ND = 51.47 mb at sqrt(s)=7 TeV, compared to Pythia6 (48.5 mb) and Phojet (61.6 mb).
* The GLMM model predicts sigma_sd = 10.2 mb and sigma_dd = 6.46 mb at sqrt(s)=7 TeV.
* The CGC model predictions for charged hadron multiplicity at midrapidity at sqrt(s)=7 TeV show agreement with CMS data, with theoretical uncertainties less than 6% at high energies.
* The average fraction of mini jet energy carried by hadrons is <z> = 0.48 to 0.5, describing the average transverse momentum of charged hadrons across a wide energy range.
* The mini jet mass used in the CGC model is m_jet = 0.4 GeV, fixed at lower energy.
* The peak in the differential yield of charged hadrons occurs at approximately p_T = m_jet * <z>.
* For Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC, the model predicts charged hadron multiplicity at midrapidity for central 0-6% collisions at sqrt(s)=2.75 and 5.5 TeV, with theoretical uncertainties less than 7%.

## Context

This work extends the Colour-Glass-Condensate approach previously applied to heavy ion-ion collisions at RHIC (Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi, 2001) and to proton-proton collisions at lower energies (Levin and Rezaeian, 2010), and confronts it with the first LHC 7 TeV data from CMS. The soft Pomeron models compared include the GLMM model (Gotsman, Levin, Maor, Miller, 2008), the Durham group models (KMR, 2007 and 2010), and the model of Ostapchenko and Seniuk (2009). The paper enables future tests of the CGC approach through its predictions for nucleus-nucleus collisions at the LHC, which the authors consider a crucial test for the discovery of a new phase of QCD.
