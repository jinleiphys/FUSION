# Gluodissociation and Screening of Upsilon States in PbPb Collisions at sqrt (s_NN) = 2.76 TeV

> We suggest that gluon-induced dissociation and screening of the Y(nS) states explain the suppression of the Y(2S+3S) states relative to the Y(1S) ground state that has been observed by CMS in PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)= 2.76 TeV at the CERN LHC. The minimum-bias gluodissociation cross sections of the 1S-3S states are calculated using a screened Cornell potential and a thermal gluon distribution. The 3S state dissolves due to screening before sizeable gluodissociation occurs, but for the 2S and 1S states there is an interplay between screening, gluodissociation, and feed-down from the chi_b(2P) and chi_b(1P) states. Based on a schematic approach, we find that the calculated suppression of the Y(2S) and Y(3S) states relative to Y(1S) is consistent with the CMS result, but allows for additional suppression mechanisms. The Y(1S) suppression through gluodissociation is, however, in good agreement with the CMS data.

## Key claim

The authors suggest that gluon-induced dissociation and screening of Y(nS) states, combined with feed-down from chi_b states, can explain the observed suppression of excited Upsilon states relative to the ground state in PbPb collisions, but their schematic approach allows for additional suppression mechanisms beyond those considered.

## Method

The authors calculated minimum-bias gluodissociation cross sections for the 1S-3S Upsilon states using a screened Cornell potential with a thermal gluon distribution, solving the Schrodinger equation for the singlet wave functions and computing the dissociation cross sections via a modified Bhanot-Peskin approach. They then obtained thermally averaged cross sections by integrating over gluon momenta weighted with a Bose-Einstein distribution, and computed suppression factors by considering impact-parameter dependent initial temperatures, interaction times, and a radiative feed-down cascade from chi_b(1P) and chi_b(2P) states.

## Key numbers

* The rms radius of the 2S state increases from 0.50 fm at T=0 to 0.77 fm at T=200 MeV, while the 3S state rms radius increases from 0.73 fm to 1.99 fm
* Thermally averaged gluodissociation cross section for the 1S state ranges from 0.080 mb at T=170 MeV to 0.141 mb at T=300 MeV (with m_g=0)
* Thermally averaged gluodissociation cross section for the 2S state ranges from 0.783 mb at T=170 MeV to 0.041 mb at T=300 MeV (with m_g=0)
* The calculated minimum-bias suppression factor R_AA(1S) ranges from 0.62 for T_0=500 MeV to 0.50 for T_0=800 MeV (with m_g=0)
* The calculated ratio Y(2S+3S)/Y(1S) in PbPb ranges from 0.58 for T_0=500 MeV to 0.45 for T_0=800 MeV (with m_g=0)
* The experimental CMS value for R_AA(1S) is 0.62 +/- 0.11(stat) +/- 0.10(sys) in minimum-bias PbPb collisions
* The experimental CMS value for Y(2S+3S)/Y(1S) in PbPb is 0.24 +0.13/-0.12(stat) +/- 0.02(sys)

## Context

This work extends the study of quarkonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions, which began with charmonium studies since 1986 at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies, and follows the first observations of Upsilon suppression by STAR at RHIC and CMS at LHC. The calculation is complementary to the approach of solving a Schrodinger equation with an imaginary-valued contribution to the potential due to Landau damping as performed by Strickland (2011) for the Y(1S) and chi_b(1P) states. The results enable using precise measurements of 2S and 3S state populations as a thermometer for the initial QGP temperature, with better statistics expected from the 2011 PbPb run at LHC.
