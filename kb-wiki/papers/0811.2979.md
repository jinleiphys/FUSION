# The "Perfect" Fluid Quenches Jets Almost Perfectly

> The QCD matter produced in nuclear collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) has been found to have a very low shear viscosity, which is close to the lower bound allowed by unitarity. The matter has also been found to strongly suppress the emission of energetic hadrons. This phenomenon, called "jet quenching" is interpreted to be the result of a large energy loss by the precursor parton on its path through the dense matter, primarily due to gluon radiation. I discuss how the two phenomena are related. The RHIC data suggest, in some scenarios of jet quenching, that the quark-gluon plasma created in nuclear collisions is characterized by strong coupling, but still admits a quasi-particle description.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the shear viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio (\(\eta/s\)) and the jet-quenching transport coefficient (\(\hat{q}\)) are inversely related in a weakly coupled quasi-particle quark-gluon plasma, but this relation breaks down at strong coupling. The RHIC data are consistent with either scenario depending on which jet-quenching scheme is adopted, leaving the nature of the plasma ambiguous.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical relations between shear viscosity and the jet-quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\) derived from kinetic theory, assuming a quasi-particle medium dominated by small-angle scattering. Values of \(\hat{q}\) extracted from PHENIX \(\pi^0\) suppression data in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV using three different radiative energy-loss schemes (ASW, HT, AMY) are compared with the \(\eta/s\) range inferred from elliptic-flow measurements in viscous hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density in RHIC collisions exceeds 5 GeV/fm\(^3\), more than 30 times normal nuclear matter density.
- Chemical freeze-out temperature: \(T_\textrm{ch} \approx 160\) MeV.
- Shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio from elliptic flow: \(0 < \eta/s < 0.3\) (Glauber initial conditions near lower bound; saturation models near upper bound); a narrower range \(0.15 \leq \eta/s \leq 0.3\) from multiplicity-dependent \(v_2\) analysis.
- Extracted \(\hat{q}\) values (normalized to gluons at \(T = 400\) MeV, in GeV\(^2\)/fm): ASW scheme gives 10 (\(T^3\) scaling) or 18.5 (\(\varepsilon^{4/3}\) scaling); HT scheme gives 2.3 or 4.5; AMY scheme gives 4.1 (\(T^3\) scaling).
- Using the AMY scheme including collisional loss: \(\hat{q} \approx 2.75\) GeV\(^2\)/fm, yielding \(1.25\,T^3/\hat{q} \approx 0.145\), which falls within the \(\eta/s\) range from flow.
- Lattice QCD result (Meyer, 2007) at \(T = 1.65\,T_c \approx 300\) MeV: \(\eta/s = 0.134 \pm 33\) (presumably \(0.134 \pm 0.033\)).

## Context

This work extends the relation between shear viscosity and jet quenching proposed by Majumder, Müller, and Bass (2007) and compares it with RHIC data. It contrasts the weak-coupling quasi-particle picture with the strong-coupling scenario from supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory (Policastro, Son, and Starinets, 2001) and the KSS bound (Kovtun, Son, and Starinets, 2004). The analysis enables future discrimination between these scenarios using more precise measurements from RHIC and the LHC.
