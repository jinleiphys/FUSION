# Theoretical exploration of $S$-factors for nuclear reactions of astrophysical importance

> We present here a robust analytical model based on nuclear reaction theory for non-resonant fusion cross sections near Coulomb barrier. The astrophysical $S$-factors involving stable and neutron rich isotopes of C, O, Ne, Mg and Si for fusion reactions have been calculated in the centre of mass energy range of 2-30 MeV. The model is based on the tunneling through barrier arising out of nuclear, Coulomb and centrifugal potentials. Our formalism predicts precisely the suppression of $S$-factor at sub-barrier energies which are of astrophysical interest. The cross sections can be convoluted with Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution of energies to obtain thermo- or pycno- nuclear reaction rates relevant to nucleosynthesis at high density environments and stellar burning at high temperatures as well as for $^{34}$Ne $+$ $^{34}$Ne fusion occurring in the inner crust of accreting neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors present a robust analytical model for non-resonant fusion cross sections near the Coulomb barrier that predicts suppression of the astrophysical S-factor at sub-barrier energies, but the model relies on a phenomenological simulation of higher partial wave contributions and a five-parameter fit to existing S-factor data from the Sao Paulo (SP) calculations (ADNDT10) rather than being fully predictive from first principles.

## Method

The model assumes a parabolic barrier for the nuclear plus Coulomb potential, matched to a pure Coulomb tail at a matching radius R_m, and adds the centrifugal barrier separately. The s-wave transmission coefficient T_0(E) is computed exactly via quantum mechanical barrier penetration, while contributions from higher partial waves (l>0) are approximated by an overall normalization factor N(E) ≈ N_0 = 1 + n_0 sqrt(E_c/E_0), where n_0 is a fitted parameter. The five model parameters (r_0, ε, n_0, Δ_1, Δ_2) are obtained by fitting the astrophysical S-factors to experimental data and to the nine-parameter phenomenological Sao Paulo (SP) calculations (ADNDT10) for each reaction system.

## Context

This work extends the barrier penetration model approach of Be10 and Be12 by reducing the number of free parameters from seven to five and using exact analytic expressions for barrier penetration rather than the approximations used in those earlier works. The model is fitted to the Sao Paulo (SP) phenomenological calculations (ADNDT10) and to experimental data for C+C fusion from multiple experiments (Pa69, Ma73, Hi77, Ke80, Be81, Er81, Da82, Sa82, Ro03, Ag06, Ba06, Sp07). The results enable calculation of Maxwellian-averaged thermonuclear reaction rates for nucleosynthesis in high-density environments such as stellar burning, type Ia supernovae, and pycnonuclear burning of neutron-rich nuclei like 34Ne+34Ne in the inner crust of accreting neutron stars.
