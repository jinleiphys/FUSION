# Study on Lambda(1405) in the flavor SU(3) limit in the HAL QCD method

> We study interactions between the S-wave octet pseudo-scalar (PS) meson and octet baryon in the flavor SU(3) limit using the HAL QCD method at the PS meson mass $m_M\approx 670~\textrm{MeV}$. We focus on the singlet and two octet channels, where the poles corresponding to $Λ(1405)$ have been predicted in the chiral unitary model. For calculations with $Λ$-baryon source operators with zero momentum, we employ the conventional stochastic calculation combined with the covariant-approximation averaging to calculate the all-to-all propagators. Due to a zero of the R-correlator (a kind of wave function), the leading order (LO) potential obtained by the single channel analysis has a singular point in all channels, which makes it difficult to obtain reliable binding energies. To overcome this problem, we take a linear combination of two octet R-correlators with a relative weight such that it does not cross zero, as two octet channels are suggested to couple to the same low-energy states with different weights. The potential calculated from such the linear combination shows strong attraction without singularities, though its shape depends on the relative weight. Our estimation for the binding energy in the octet channel is $E^{8_{s(a)}}_{\textrm{bind}}=163(7)(^{+16} _{-64})~\textrm{MeV}$, which is consistent with 156(8) MeV estimated from the two-point correlation function within errors.

## Key claim
The authors claim that the binding energy in the octet channel of the meson-baryon system in the flavor SU(3) limit is consistent with the two-point correlation function estimate, but only after constructing a mixed R-correlator to avoid singularities in the potential; the result depends on the choice of the mixing parameter \(c\), introducing a systematic uncertainty.

## Method
The authors used the time-dependent HAL QCD method on gauge configurations in the flavor SU(3) limit with a pseudo-scalar meson mass of approximately 670 MeV on a \(32^4\) lattice. They computed all-to-all propagators using stochastic techniques with covariant-approximation averaging, and extracted leading-order potentials from R-correlators for the singlet and two octet channels (\(8_s\) and \(8_a\)). To avoid singularities from zeros in the R-correlators, they formed a linear combination of the two octet R-correlators with a relative weight \(c\), then fitted the resulting potential with Gaussian functions and solved the Schrödinger equation to obtain binding energies.

## Key numbers
- Pseudo-scalar meson mass: \(m_M = 671.2(1.5)\) MeV.
- Octet baryon mass (positive parity): \(m^{+}_{8,B} = 1488.8(3.9)\) MeV.
- Octet baryon mass (negative parity): \(m^{-}_{8,B} = 2013.2(11.2)\) MeV.
- Singlet baryon mass (negative parity): \(m^{-}_{1,B} = 1923.4(9.1)\) MeV.
- Binding energy in the octet channel from the mixed R-correlator method: \(E^{8_{s(a)}}_{\text{bind}} = 163(7)(^{+16}_{-64})\) MeV, where the first error is statistical and the second is systematic.
- Binding energy estimated from the two-point correlation function: 156(8) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the chiral unitary model predictions by Jido et al. (2003), which suggested two poles corresponding to \(\Lambda(1405)\) in the singlet and octet channels in the SU(3) limit. The study uses the HAL QCD method to extract interaction potentials, building on earlier lattice QCD techniques by Ishii et al. (2006) and Aoki et al. (2009). The results enable future investigations of the two-pole structure of \(\Lambda(1405)\) in the SU(3) limit and provide a foundation for extending the analysis to the physical \(2+1\)-flavor case, though the dependence of the potential shape on the mixing parameter \(c\) and the presence of non-local effects remain open questions.
