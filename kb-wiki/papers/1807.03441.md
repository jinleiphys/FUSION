# Theoretical study of the capture of stable $^{39}$K and neutron-rich radioactive $^{46}$K by $^{181}$Ta

> The empirical coupled-channel (ECC) model and the universal fusion function (UFF) prescription are used to analyse the data of capture cross sections for reactions ${}^{39}$K$+{}^{181}$Ta and ${}^{46}$K$+{}^{181}$Ta reported recently by A. Wakhle {\it et al.} [Phys. Rev. C 97, 021602(R) (2018)]. The results of the ECC model are in good agreement with the data of ${}^{39}$K$+{}^{181}$Ta while, for ${}^{46}$K$+{}^{181}$Ta, the predictions of the ECC model overestimate the above-barrier capture cross sections. Comparing the reduced data of these two reactions, it is found that the above-barrier cross sections of ${}^{39}$K$+{}^{181}$Ta are consistent with the UFF and are larger than those of ${}^{46}$K$+{}^{181}$Ta. This implies that the capture cross sections of ${}^{46}$K$+{}^{181}$Ta are suppressed at energies above the Coulomb barrier. Furthermore, at sub-barrier energies, the reduced calculated capture cross sections of ${}^{39}$K$+{}^{181}$Ta are a little larger than those of ${}^{46}$K$+{}^{181}$Ta, which is owing to the coupling to the positive $Q$-value two-neutron transfer channel.

## Key claim

The empirical coupled-channel (ECC) model reproduces the capture cross sections for the stable-beam reaction 39K+181Ta well, but overestimates the above-barrier capture cross sections for the neutron-rich radioactive-beam reaction 46K+181Ta, indicating a suppression of capture for the latter system at energies above the Coulomb barrier.

## Method

The authors applied the empirical coupled-channel (ECC) model, which treats coupled-channel effects by introducing an asymmetric Gaussian barrier weight function whose parameters are determined from a two-dimensional potential energy surface. For the 39K reaction, the positive Q-value for two-neutron transfer (3.67 MeV) broadens the barrier weight function; for the 46K reaction, the two-neutron transfer Q-value is negative, so no such broadening is applied. The data were also reduced using the universal fusion function (UFF) prescription based on the Sao Paulo potential to compare the two systems on a common scale.

## Key numbers

- For 39K+181Ta, the ECC barrier weight function parameters are: left width Δ1 = 3.56 MeV, right width Δ2 = 23.46 MeV, most probable barrier height Bm = 146.78 MeV.
- For 46K+181Ta, the ECC barrier weight function parameters are: Δ1 = 2.38 MeV, Δ2 = 22.20 MeV, Bm = 143.25 MeV.
- The Q-value for two-neutron transfer is +3.67 MeV for 39K+181Ta and negative for 46K+181Ta.
- The Sao Paulo potential barrier parameters for 39K+181Ta are: VB = 155.651 MeV, ℏω = 4.328 MeV, RB = 12.030 fm; for 46K+181Ta: VB = 152.141 MeV, ℏω = 4.029 MeV, RB = 12.333 fm.
- The barrier parameters extracted from the experimental data in Wakhle et al. (2018) are: for 39K+181Ta, VB = 152.690 MeV, RB = 12.82 fm; for 46K+181Ta, VB = 146.484 MeV, RB = 10.16 fm.

## Context

This work extends the ECC model previously developed by Wang et al. (2017) for 217 reaction systems to the specific case of a neutron-rich radioactive beam (46K) compared to its stable isotope (39K). The results contradict the conclusion of Wakhle et al. (2018) that the reduced excitation functions of the two reactions show no significant difference; instead, the UFF reduction reveals a clear suppression of above-barrier capture for 46K+181Ta. The finding highlights the need to account for suppression effects when using neutron-rich radioactive beams to synthesize heavy and superheavy nuclei, and calls for further experimental and theoretical studies.
