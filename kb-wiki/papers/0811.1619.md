# Alpha decay chains from superheavy nuclei

> Magic islands for extra-stable nuclei in the midst of the sea of fission-instability were predicted to be around Z=114, 124 or, 126 with N=184, and Z=120, with N=172. Whether these fission-survived superheavy nuclei with high Z and N would live long enough for detection or, undergo alpha-decay in a very short time remains an open question. Alpha-decay half lives of nuclei with 130 < Z < 100 have been calculated in a WKB framework using density-dependent M3Y interaction with Q-values from different mass formulae. The results are in excellent agreement with the experimental data. Fission survived Sg nuclei with Z=106, N=162 is predicted to have the highest alpha-decay half life ~ 3.2 hrs in the Z=106-108, N=160-164 region called, small island/peninsula. Superheavy nuclei with Z > 118 are found to have alpha-decay half lives of the order of microseconds or, less.

## Key claim

Alpha-decay half-lives of superheavy nuclei with Z between 100 and 130, calculated using a WKB framework with density-dependent M3Y interaction and Q-values from three different mass formulae, agree excellently with experimental data; however, the predicted half-lives are highly sensitive to the chosen Q-value, and for some decay chains discrepancies remain that may indicate transitions from excited states rather than ground-state-to-ground-state decays.

## Method

Alpha-decay half-lives were calculated using a quantum tunneling model within the WKB approximation, where the nuclear interaction potential was obtained by double folding the density distributions of the alpha particle and daughter nucleus with a density-dependent M3Y effective interaction. The total interaction energy included the nuclear potential, Coulomb potential, and centrifugal barrier for spin-parity conservation. Theoretical Q-values were taken from three mass formulae: Muntian-Hofmann-Patyk-Sobiczewski (Q-MMM), Myers-Swiatecki (Q-MS), and Koura-Uno-Tachibana-Yamada (Q-KUTY), and zero-point vibration energies were scaled by Q with coefficients depending on the even-odd character of the parent nucleus.

## Context

This work extends earlier predictions of magic islands for superheavy nuclei around Z=114, 124, 126 with N=184 and Z=120 with N=172, originally proposed by Myers and Swiatecki (1965) and later by Nilsson et al. (1969), and provides systematic alpha-decay half-life calculations for 1700 isotopes with Z=100-130 using a consistent WKB framework. The calculations use the density-dependent M3Y interaction method previously developed by the author and collaborators, and compare Q-values from three mass models (Muntian-Hofmann-Patyk-Sobiczewski, Myers-Swiatecki, and Koura-Uno-Tachibana-Yamada) against experimental data from GSI, RIKEN, and JINR. The results suggest that the center of the magic island may be near Z=110, N=183 rather than Z=114, N=184 as earlier thought, and that nuclei with Z=120, 124, 126 at N=184 would undergo alpha-decay within microseconds, motivating future searches for long-lived isomeric states in heavier nuclei.
