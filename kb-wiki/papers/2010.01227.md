# Parametrization of the surface energy in the ETF approximation

> We perform extended Thomas-Fermi calculations in the Wigner-Seitz cell below and above neutron drip with realistic functionals. The resulting energy density is decomposed as a sum of bulk terms and a surface term, and a compressible liquid drop analytical formula is used to fit the surface tension. The effect of curvature terms and neutron skin is studied in detail. A very good reproduction of the microscopic data is obtained using an expression that depends only on the mass and charge of the cluster, showing that the in-medium modifications of the nuclear energy in the presence of an external neutron gas can be effectively accounted for in the isospin dependence of the surface tension. In this first application, aimed at establishing the fitting protocol, we concentrate on the Sly4 energy functional, but the study can be easily generalized to different functionals, and the resulting parametrizations can be used for direct applications in pasta calculations in neutron star crusts and supernova matter.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the surface tension of neutron-rich nuclei in stellar matter can be accurately parametrized using a compressible liquid drop formula that depends only on the cluster mass and charge, effectively accounting for in-medium modifications from an external neutron gas through the isospin dependence of the surface tension. This claim is qualified as a first application establishing the fitting protocol, limited to the Sly4 energy functional.

## Method

Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) calculations were performed in spherical Wigner-Seitz cells using Fermi-Dirac density profiles for both protons and neutrons, with the Sly4 Skyrme functional. The variational parameters (diffusivities, central densities, background densities, and cell radius) were optimized by minimizing the total energy per particle for fixed total baryon density and proton fraction, both below and above neutron drip. The resulting energy density was decomposed into bulk terms (nuclear and Coulomb) and a surface term, and the surface tension was extracted by fitting the microscopic ETF energies to a compressible liquid drop model (CLDM) expression with surface and curvature contributions.

## Key numbers

- The neutron background density along the I=0.4 path varied between ρ_b = 3.8×10⁻⁶ fm⁻³ and ρ_b = 4.0×10⁻⁴ fm⁻³ for total masses ranging from A=118 to A=637
- The total isospin asymmetry was varied from I=0.00 to I=0.95 in steps of 0.05
- The global density was varied from 1.0×10⁻⁴ fm⁻³ to 4.2×10⁻² fm⁻³
- The proton form factor S_p = 0.8 fm was used to obtain charge radii from the proton density profiles
- The Taylor expansion for the potential energy was taken to order N=4

## Context

This work extends previous ETF calculations by Centelles (1998), Warda (2009), Mondal (2016), and Raduta (2018) for nuclei in vacuum and in a neutron gas, and builds on the CLDM parametrization approach of Ravenhall (1983), Lattimer and Swesty (1991), and Newton (2013). The resulting parametrization enables direct applications in pasta calculations for neutron star crusts and supernova matter, and can be generalized to different functionals for systematic studies of model dependence, as done by Carreau (2019) and Fortin (2016).
