# Diffractive incoherent vector meson production off protons: a quark model approach to gluon fluctuation effects

> Fluctuations play an important role in diffractive production of vector mesons. It was in particular recently suggested, based on the Impact-Parameter dependent Saturation model (IPSat), that geometrical fluctuations triggered by the motion of the constituent quarks within the protons could explain incoherent diffractive processes observed at HERA. We propose a variant of the IPSat model which includes spatial and symmetry correlations between constituent quarks, thereby reducing the number of parameters needed to describe diffractive vector meson production to a single one, the size of the gluon cloud around each valence quark. The application to $J/Ψ$, $ρ$ and $ϕ$ diffractive electron and photon production cross sections reveal the important role of geometrical fluctuations in incoherent channels, while other sources of fluctuations are needed to fully account for electroproduction of light mesons, as well as photo production of $J/Ψ$ mesons at small momentum transfer.

## Key claim

Geometrical fluctuations of the gluon density, driven by the motion of constituent quarks within the proton, can explain incoherent diffractive vector meson production at HERA, but only if the model includes spatial and symmetry correlations between quarks; even then, additional non-geometrical fluctuation sources are needed to fully describe electroproduction of light mesons and photoproduction of J/Ψ at small momentum transfer.

## Method

The authors modify the Impact-Parameter dependent Saturation (IPSat) model by replacing the phenomenological Gaussian proton profile with a two-harmonic-oscillator (2 h.o.) quark model wave function that incorporates SU(6) breaking via hyperfine interactions, fixing quark positions from proton and neutron charge radii. They then attach a Gaussian gluon cloud of width B_q around each valence quark, sample quark positions from the correlated 2 h.o. density, and evolve parton distributions from the bare quark model scale to experimental scales using DGLAP evolution at LO, NLO, and NNLO.

## Key numbers

- Proton charge r.m.s. radius: ⟨r²⟩_p = (0.862 ± 0.012)² fm², used to fix α² ≈ 1.35 fm⁻².
- Neutron charge r.m.s. radius: ⟨r²⟩_n = −(11.94 ± 0.18) × 10⁻² fm², used to fix β² ≈ 1.99 fm⁻².
- Gluon cloud width for the lumpy configuration: B_q = 0.7 GeV⁻² = (0.1651 fm)².
- Two-dimensional gluon root mean square radius of the proton fixed to √⟨b²⟩ = 2√2 GeV⁻¹ ≈ 0.55 fm.
- Number of configurations used for averaging: N_conf = 10000.
- Coherent J/Ψ photoproduction cross section at ⟨W⟩ = 100 GeV reproduced with B_G = 4 GeV⁻² for the Gaussian profile.
- Incoherent J/Ψ photoproduction cross section at ⟨W⟩ = 100 GeV reproduced with lumpy parameters B_qc = 3.3 GeV⁻², B_q = 0.7 GeV⁻².

## Context

This work extends the approach of Mantysaari and Schenke (2016), who first suggested that geometrical fluctuations from constituent quark motion could explain incoherent diffractive processes at HERA using a Gaussian IPSat model with uncorrelated quarks. The authors introduce a quark-model-based approach (QMBA) that includes SU(6)-breaking correlations from the hyperfine interaction, reducing the free parameters to a single one (B_q). The results confirm the sensitivity of incoherent cross sections to gluon fluctuations and show that the 2 h.o. model reproduces J/Ψ photoproduction data, but light meson electroproduction and J/Ψ photoproduction at small t require additional fluctuation sources beyond geometry.
