# Thermodynamics of a gas of hadrons with attractive and repulsive interaction within S-matrix formalism

> We report the effect of including repulsive interactions on various thermodynamic observables calculated using a S-matrix based Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model to already available corresponding results with only attractive interactions [A. Dash, S. Samanta, and B. Mohanty, Phys. Rev. C 97, 055208 (2018)]. The attractive part of the interaction is calculated by parameterizing the two body phase shifts using K-matrix formalism while the repulsive part is included by fitting to the experimental phase shifts which carry the information about the nature of the interaction. We find that the bulk thermodynamic variables for a gas of hadrons such as energy density, pressure, entropy density, speed of sound and specific heat are suppressed by the inclusion of repulsive interactions and are more pronounced for second and higher order correlations and fluctuations, particularly for the observables $χ^2_Q$, $χ^2_B-χ^4_B$ and $C_{BS}$ in the present model. We find a good agreement between lattice QCD simulations and the present model for $C_{BS}$. We have also computed two leading order Fourier coefficients of the imaginary part of the first order baryonic susceptibility at imaginary baryon chemical potential within this model and compared them with the corresponding results from lattice. Additionally, assuming that the value of interacting pressure versus temperature for a gas of hadrons calculated in S-matrix formalism is same as that from a van der Waals HRG (VDWHRG) model, we have quantified the attractive and repulsive interactions in our model in terms of attractive and repulsive parameters used in the VDWHRG model. The values of parameters thus obtained are $a=1.54\pm 0.064$ GeV $\text{fm}^{3}$ and $r=0.81\pm 0.014$ fm.

## Key claim

The inclusion of repulsive interactions via experimental phase shifts in an S-matrix based Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model suppresses bulk thermodynamic variables and higher-order fluctuations compared to a model with only attractive interactions, and brings certain observables like the baryon-strangeness correlation \(C_{BS}\) into better agreement with lattice QCD data, though the effect is conditional on the specific interaction channels included (e.g., \(\pi N\), \(KN\), \(NN\), \(\pi\pi\)).

## Method

The authors extended their previous K-matrix based HRG model (Dash, Samanta, and Mohanty, 2018), which handled only attractive interactions via resonance phase shifts parameterized from PDG masses and widths, by adding repulsive interactions fitted to experimental phase shifts from the SAID database for \(NN\), \(\pi N\), and \(KN\) scattering, and from Garcia-Martin et al. (2011) for the repulsive isotensor \(\pi\pi\) channel. The total pressure and number density were computed using the S-matrix formalism's second virial coefficient, summing ideal contributions from all stable hadrons with interacting contributions from binary channels where the derivative of the phase shift with respect to energy determines the sign (attractive if positive, repulsive if negative). The resulting thermodynamic observables were compared to lattice QCD data and to ideal HRG models with different hadron spectra.

## Key numbers

- The van der Waals parameters extracted by matching the S-matrix interacting pressure to a VDWHRG model are \(a = 1.54 \pm 0.064\) GeV fm\(^3\) (attractive parameter) and \(r = 0.81 \pm 0.014\) fm (repulsive parameter).
- For \(\chi^2_B\), the contribution from repulsive interactions follows the order \(\pi N > KN > NN\), with the dominant \(\pi N\) repulsion active in the energy range \(1.07\) GeV \(< \sqrt{s} < 1.67\) GeV, while \(NN\) repulsion is active between \(1.88\) GeV \(< \sqrt{s} < 2.34\) GeV.
- The Fourier coefficient \(b_2(T)\) from the S-matrix formalism is positive for \(T < 135\) MeV and negative above this temperature, contrary to the purely negative result of Vovchenko et al. (2017).
- The observable \(\chi^2_B - \chi^4_B\) changes sign from negative to positive at \(T > 110\) MeV, indicating the hard core nature of \(NN\) interaction, and the inclusion of Fermi-Dirac statistics shifts this sign change to a lower temperature.

## Context

This work extends the K-matrix HRG model of Dash, Samanta, and Mohanty (2018) by adding repulsive interactions, addressing a limitation of the earlier approach which only included attractive channels. It builds on prior studies using excluded volume approaches (e.g., Rischke et al., 1991; Cleymans et al., 1992; Vovchenko et al., 2015-2018) and van der Waals HRG models (e.g., Vovchenko et al., 2015-2017; Samanta et al., 2017) that included repulsion phenomenologically, but here the repulsion is derived directly from experimental phase shifts. The results enable more precise comparisons with lattice QCD data for fluctuations and correlations of conserved charges, and the extracted van der Waals parameters provide a bridge between the S-matrix approach and simpler phenomenological models. Future work is needed to include other baryon-baryon interactions (e.g., via chiral effective theory) for which experimental phase shift data are unavailable.
