# Event-by-event direct photon anisotropic flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We consider directly emitted and hadronic decay photons from event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations. We compute the direct photon anisotropic flow coefficients and compare with recent experimental measurements. We find that it is crucial to include the photon multiplicity as a weighting factor in the definition of $v^γ_n$. We also investigate the sensitivity of the direct photon spectrum and elliptic flow to the theoretical uncertainty of the photon emission rate in the quark-hadron transition region and to the pre-equilibrium dynamics of relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the standard experimental subtraction procedure for extracting direct photon anisotropic flow coefficients only yields the correct direct photon \(v_n\) if the event-averaging includes a photon multiplicity weighting factor; without this weighting, the direct and subtraction methods disagree, and even with the weighting the theoretical calculations still severely underestimate the measured direct photon elliptic flow at both RHIC and LHC.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, including both directly emitted thermal photons and photons from hadronic decays. They computed direct photon anisotropic flow coefficients using the scalar-product method, comparing definitions with and without photon multiplicity weighting. They also varied the temperature window for interpolating between QGP and hadron gas photon emission rates and introduced a free-streaming pre-equilibrium stage matched to viscous hydrodynamics at \(\tau_\mathrm{th}=0.6\) fm/\(c\) to study sensitivity to early-time dynamics.

## Key numbers

- The photon multiplicity weighted direct photon \(v_2\{\mathrm{SP, mult}\}\) is \(\sim 10\%\) smaller than the unweighted \(v_2\{\mathrm{SP}\}\) for 0-40% centrality in Au+Au collisions.
- The \(p_T\)-integrated thermal photon yield increases by about 30% when the rate-switching window is lowered to \(T_\mathrm{sw}=150-170\) MeV compared to \(T_\mathrm{sw}=180-220\) MeV.
- The direct photon \(v_2\) changes by almost a factor of 2 between the two rate-switching windows (\(T_\mathrm{sw}=150-170\) MeV vs. \(T_\mathrm{sw}=180-220\) MeV).
- Including photons from short-lived resonance decays (all resonances with masses below 2 GeV) increases the direct photon yield by 5-10%.

## Context

This work extends the event-by-event hydrodynamic approach of Shen (2013) and Shen (2013) to address the "direct photon flow puzzle" first observed in Au+Au collisions at RHIC by Adare (2011) and Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC by Lohner (2012). The paper shows that even after including photon multiplicity weighting, pre-equilibrium dynamics, and short-lived resonance decays, the theoretical calculations still severely underestimate the measured direct photon spectrum and elliptic flow, highlighting the need for improved photon emission rates in the quark-hadron transition region using nonperturbative techniques as suggested by Lin.
