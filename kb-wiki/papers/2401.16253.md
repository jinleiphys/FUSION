# Astrophysical Equation-of-State Constraints on the Color-Superconducting Gap

> We demonstrate that astrophysical constraints on the dense-matter equation of state place an upper bound on the color-superconducting gap in dense matter above the transition from nuclear matter to quark matter. Pairing effects in the color-flavor locked (CFL) quark matter phase increase the pressure at high density, and if this effect is sufficiently large then the requirements of causality and mechanical stability make it impossible to reach such a pressure in a way that is consistent with what is known at lower densities. The intermediate-density equation of state is inferred by considering extensions of chiral effective field theory (CEFT) to neutron star densities, and conditioning these using current astrophysical observations of neutron star radius, maximum mass, and tidal deformability (PSR J0348+0432, PSR J1624-2230, PSR J0740+6620, GW170817). At baryon number chemical potential $μ= 2.6~\text{GeV}$ we find a 95% upper limit on the CFL pairing gap $Δ$ of $457~\text{MeV}$ using overly conservative assumptions and $216~\text{MeV}$ with more reasonable assumptions. This constraint may be strengthened by future astrophysical measurements as well as by future advances in high density QCD calculations.

## Key claim
Astrophysical constraints on the dense-matter equation of state place an upper bound on the color-superconducting gap in quark matter above the transition from nuclear matter to quark matter, but only if the pairing effects in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase increase the pressure sufficiently that causality and mechanical stability requirements make it impossible to reach such a pressure consistently with lower-density knowledge.

## Method
The authors used a Bayesian analysis combining chiral effective field theory (CEFT) calculations up to 1.1 times nuclear saturation density with astrophysical observations of neutron star masses, radii, and tidal deformabilities from PSR J0348+0432, PSR J1624-2230, PSR J0740+6620, and GW170817. They then applied integral constraints from perturbative QCD (pQCD) at high chemical potentials, requiring that any interpolating equation of state between low-density and high-density regimes be mechanically stable, thermodynamically consistent, and causal, with the CFL pairing contribution to pressure given by pCFL = (1/(3π²))Δ²μ².

## Key numbers
- At baryon number chemical potential μ = 2.6 GeV, the 95% upper limit on the CFL pairing gap Δ is 457 MeV using overly conservative assumptions and 216 MeV with more reasonable assumptions.
- The analytic estimate using CEFT at nL = 1.1 ns (μL = 0.97 GeV) with pL ≈ (2.2-3.5) MeV/fm³ gives Δmax ≈ 880 MeV at μH = 2.6 GeV.
- Using μL = 1.45 GeV and pL = 160 MeV/fm³ (estimates for the center of a 2.1 M⊙ neutron star) gives Δmax ≈ 440 MeV at μH = 2.6 GeV.
- The CEFT-only analysis (no astrophysical input) yields a 95% upper limit of 799 MeV at μH = 2.6 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the model-agnostic Bayesian analyses of Gorda (2022) and Komoltsev (2021, 2023) that used CEFT, pQCD, and astrophysical observations to constrain the dense matter equation of state, but which previously neglected color superconductivity effects. The authors show that the integral constraints from pQCD calculations combined with current neutron star observations can already rule out the highest model predictions for the CFL gap (which range from 20 to 300 MeV in model calculations by Alford (1997), Rapp (1997), and Braun (2021)), and that future astrophysical measurements and advances in N3LO pQCD calculations will further tighten these constraints.
