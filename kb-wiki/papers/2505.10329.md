# Confronting recent light compact star observations with color-flavor locked quark matter

> Recent analyses on the properties of the central compact object in the HESS J1731-347 remnant and the PSR J1231-1411 pulsar indicated that these two compact objects are characterized by similar (low) masses and possibly different radii. This paper aims at reconciling the aforementioned measurements by utilizing the widely employed color-flavor locked (CFL) MIT bag model. The main objective is related to the examination of the acceptable values for the color superconducting gap $Δ$ and the bag parameter $B$. Furthermore, our analysis involves two distinct hypotheses for the nature of compact stars. Firstly, we considered the case of absolute stability for strange quark matter and we found that it is possible to explain both measurements, while also respecting the latest astronomical constraints on the masses and radii of compact stars. Secondly, we studied the case of hybrid stellar matter (transition from hadrons to quarks), and concluded that, when early phase transitions are considered, the simultaneous reconciliation of both measurements leads to results that are inconsistent to the existence of massive compact stars. However, we showed that all current constraints may be satisfied under the consideration that the HESS J1731-347 remnant contains a slow stable hybrid star.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the HESS J1731-347 central compact object and the PSR J1231-1411 pulsar can be simultaneously explained by color-flavor locked (CFL) quark matter, but only under specific conditions: either assuming absolutely stable strange quark matter with large superconducting gaps, or by considering the HESS J1731-347 remnant as a slow stable hybrid star with extreme values of the bag parameter and gap, provided these values remain compatible with perturbative QCD results.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations using the CFL MIT bag model equation of state, scanning a wide range of bag constants B (57-300 MeV·fm⁻³) and color superconducting gaps Δ (50-300 MeV) for two values of the perturbative QCD parameter a₄ (1.0 and 0.7). They considered two hypotheses: absolutely stable strange quark matter (strange stars) and hybrid stars with a first-order hadron-to-quark phase transition, using the Ska Skyrme model for the hadronic phase. For each scenario, they identified parameter pairs producing mass-radius curves compatible at the 1σ level with observations of HESS J1731-347, PSR J1231-1411 (using two different estimates labeled Case I and Case II), and other multimessenger constraints including PSR J0952-0607, PSR J0030+0451, and PSR J0437-4715.

## Key numbers

- HESS J1731-347: M = 0.77⁺⁰·²⁰₋₀·₁₇ M⊙, R = 10.4⁺⁰·⁸⁶₋₀·₇₈ km (1σ, from Doroshenko 2022)
- PSR J1231-1411 Case I (Salmi 2024): M = 1.04⁺⁰·⁰⁵₋₀·⁰³ M⊙, R = 12.6⁺⁰·³₋₀·₃ km
- PSR J1231-1411 Case II (Qi 2025): M = 1.12⁺⁰·⁰⁷₋₀·⁰⁷ M⊙, R = 9.91⁺⁰·⁸⁸₋₀·⁸⁶ km
- For absolutely stable strange stars compatible with both HESS J1731-347 and PSR J1231-1411 (Case I), the allowed parameter region requires small bag constants near the minimum of 57 MeV·fm⁻³ and large gaps, yielding maximum masses above 3 M⊙
- For Case II with a₄ = 0.7, the common parameter space yields maximum masses spanning approximately 2.25-3.25 M⊙
- For the slow stable hybrid star scenario, reconciliation of HESS J1731-347 requires extremely large B and Δ values (e.g., Δ up to 600 MeV), with compatibility to pQCD results only for Δ up to 400 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies that attempted to explain the unusually low mass and radius of the HESS J1731-347 central compact object, which was first reported by Doroshenko (2022) and has been interpreted as a possible strange quark star (Di Clemente 2023, Horvath 2023), a hybrid star with early phase transition (Tsaloukidis 2023, Sagun 2023, Laskos-Patkos 2024, 2025), or a soft neutron star (Brodie 2023, Huang 2023). The authors specifically build on the slow stable hybrid star concept introduced by Mariani (2024) and test it within the CFL MIT bag model framework. The results enable future work on formation scenarios for slow stable hybrid stars and more precise cooling analyses of CFL strange stars to resolve tensions between the large gaps required for maximum mass constraints and the small gaps suggested by thermal evolution of the HESS J1731-347 central compact object.
