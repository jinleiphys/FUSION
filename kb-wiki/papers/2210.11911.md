# Exclusive $ρ$ and $J/Ψ$ photoproduction in ultraperipheral $pO$ and $OO$ collisions at energies available at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> Collisions with Oxygen ions at the LHC opens the possibility of investigating the description of the QCD dynamics in an unexplored regime, complementary to those performed in $pp$, $pPb$ and $PbPb$ collisions. In this paper we estimate the exclusive $ρ$ and $J/Ψ$ photoproduction in ultraperipheral $pO$ and $OO$ collisions at the Large Hadron Collider using the color dipole formalism and considering distinct models for the dipole - target scattering amplitude and different approaches for the modelling of the vector meson wave function. We present our predictions for the rapidity and transverse momentum distributions, as well as for the total cross sections considering the rapidity ranges covered by the ALICE and LHCb detectors. Such results indicate that a future experimental analysis of these final states is feasible and that its study can be useful to improve our understanding of the QCD dynamics at high energies.

## Key claim

The authors estimate that exclusive rho and J/Psi photoproduction in ultraperipheral pO and OO collisions at the LHC is feasible for future experimental analysis, but they hedge that the dipole formalism for rho photoproduction is not fully justified perturbatively because dipoles larger than 0.7 fm contribute approximately 40% of the amplitude, making the perturbative treatment dependent on the saturation scale being large enough at current collider energies.

## Method

The authors used the color dipole formalism to calculate exclusive vector meson photoproduction cross sections, employing the bCGC and IP-Sat models for the dipole-proton scattering amplitude and the Glauber-Gribov formalism to extend these to an oxygen target. They considered two models for the vector meson wave function overlap (Boosted Gaussian and Gauss-LC) and computed rapidity and transverse momentum distributions for pO collisions at sqrt(s) = 9 TeV and OO collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.52 TeV.

## Key numbers

- For OO collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.52 TeV, the total cross section for rho photoproduction in the ALICE rapidity range (-2.5 ≤ Y ≤ 2.5) ranges from 2545.44 μb (IP-SAT + GLC) to 2837.62 μb (bCGC + BG)
- For OO collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.52 TeV, the total cross section for J/Psi photoproduction in the ALICE rapidity range ranges from 7.01 μb (IP-SAT + GLC) to 8.06 μb (bCGC + BG)
- For pO collisions at sqrt(s) = 9 TeV, the total cross section for rho photoproduction in the ALICE rapidity range ranges from 130.45 μb (IP-SAT + GLC) to 152.70 μb (bCGC + BG)
- For pO collisions at sqrt(s) = 9 TeV, the total cross section for J/Psi photoproduction in the ALICE rapidity range ranges from 0.63 μb (bCGC + GLC and IP-SAT + GLC) to 0.75 μb (IP-SAT + BG)
- The peak of the overlap function W(r) occurs at r ≈ 0.09 fm for J/Psi and r ≈ 0.7 fm for rho
- Assuming a luminosity of 7 × 10^5 μb^{-1}, the authors predict more than 17 × 10^7 rho events and more than 49 × 10^5 J/Psi events at ALICE for OO collisions

## Context

This work extends previous studies of exclusive vector meson photoproduction in pp, pPb, PbPb, and XeXe collisions (Goncalves et al., 2020; Goncalves et al., 2019) to oxygen-ion collisions, which probe a new kinematical regime where the saturation scale Q_s is predicted to be energy and atomic number dependent. The results enable future experimental analyses at the LHC during Run 3, where pO and OO collisions are expected to achieve large integrated luminosities (Citron et al., 2018), and can help discriminate between the bCGC and IP-Sat models of QCD dynamics, particularly through the asymmetry of rapidity distributions for rho production and the position of dips in t-distributions.
