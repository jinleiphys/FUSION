# On the Off-Mass-Shell Deformation of the Nucleon Structure Function

> The off-mass-shell behavior of the nucleon structure function, $F_2^N$, is studied within an approach motivated by the Sullivan model. Deep inelastic scattering on the nucleon is considered in the second order in the pion-nucleon coupling constant, corresponding to the dressing of the bare nucleons by the mesonic cloud. The inclusive and semi-inclusive deep inelastic processes on the deuteron involving off-shell nucleons are considered. A deformation of the mesonic cloud for the off-mass-shell nucleon, compared to the free one, generates observable effects in deep inelastic scattering. In particular, it leads to the breakdown of the convolution model, i.e. the deuteron structure functions are not expressed through the free nucleon structure function. Analysis of the semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering on the deuteron, in the spectator approximation, shows that this reaction opens new possibilities to study the role of the off-shell effects in determining in detail the nucleon structure function.

## Key claim

The off-mass-shell nucleon structure function, calculated in a pion-nucleon model motivated by the Sullivan model, shows a deformation of the mesonic cloud that leads to a breakdown of the convolution model for the deuteron; the effect is small in inclusive deuteron scattering but becomes significantly larger in semi-inclusive scattering with a spectator proton, where the virtuality of the struck nucleon can be much greater than in heavy nuclei.

## Method

The truncated nucleon tensor was calculated to second order in the pion-nucleon coupling constant using pseudoscalar coupling, including diagrams for the bare nucleon, the nucleon with a pion loop, and the pion with a nucleon loop. The resulting operator was inserted into the deuteron amplitude using a non-relativistic deuteron wave function from the Bonn potential, and the inclusive and semi-inclusive structure functions were evaluated with parametrizations of the bare nucleon and pion structure functions from Ambrogiani (1991) and Mes (1989). Vertex formfactors were introduced to regulate divergences and to connect with the Sullivan model, with calculations performed both with and without an additional formfactor for the external nucleon line.

## Key numbers

- The average shift from the mass shell for a nucleon in the deuteron is approximately 3-4%: $\langle p^2 \rangle \approx m^2 (1 - (3\div 4)\cdot 10^{-2})$.
- For a spectator momentum $p_s \sim 300$ MeV/c, the shift from the mass shell of the interacting nucleon is about 20%; for $p_s \sim 500$ MeV/c, the shift is about 40%.
- The normalization factor for the free nucleon structure function in the one-loop approximation with the chosen formfactor gives $N_N^{-1} - 1 \approx 0.24$.
- The cut-off mass for the pion formfactor is $\Lambda_\pi = 1$ GeV; the cut-off mass for the nucleon formfactor that preserves baryon charge is $\Lambda_N = 1.475$ GeV; the additional formfactor for the external nucleon line uses $\tilde\Lambda_N^2 = 1.65$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of off-mass-shell effects in the nucleon structure function beyond the "binding effects" described by Vagradov (1979) and the quark-model approaches of Kulagin (1994) and Kwon (1994), which found off-shell corrections of only a few percent. The authors use a dynamical pion-nucleon model motivated by the Sullivan model (Sullivan, 1972; Thomas, 1983) to provide a consistent treatment of off-shell behavior governed by meson-nucleon dynamics. The results enable experimental tests of off-shell effects through semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering on the deuteron, particularly at facilities such as CEBAF, where the spectator momentum can be varied to probe different virtualities of the struck nucleon.
