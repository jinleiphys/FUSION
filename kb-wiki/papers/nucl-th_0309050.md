# Probing the shell valence structure underlying the B(E2: 0+->2+) for N,Z~40: preponderance of the p-n interaction over the sub-shell closures

> The very simple product of the number of particles by the number of holes appearing in the expression of the reduced B(E2:0+->2+) transition probability of even-even nuclei obtained from the extension of the seniority scheme is used to analyze the experimental B(E2:0+->2+) values in the Cr up to Se isotopes. A new interpretation is given to the B(E2:0+->2+) measured in 68Ni and 70Zn. The B(E2:0+->2+) features of the even-even nuclei between Ni and Se with neutron number ranging from 28 up to 50 fit in with a global scenario involving p-n interaction. The evolution of the B(E2:0+->2+) curves presenting very large values is amazingly reproduced by very schematic binomial calculations.

# Probing the shell valence structure underlying the B(E2: 0+→2+) for N,Z~40: preponderance of the p-n interaction over the sub-shell closures

## Key claim

The B(E2: 0+→2+) values in even-even nuclei from Cr to Se with neutron numbers 28-50 can be globally interpreted using a simple product of valence particle pairs and hole pairs, without invoking sub-shell closures as the primary explanation; instead, the proton-neutron interaction dominates the behavior, and the decrease in B(E2) between ⁶⁶Ni and ⁶⁸Ni represents an exception that reveals a complex neutron valence space structure rather than a simple sub-shell closure effect.

## Method

The authors extend the seniority scheme formula B(E2) ∝ (N/2) × (Ω/2 − N/2) from single-j shell configurations to complex valence shells by summing over all j-shells (Ω = Σ Ωⱼ) without weighting. They calculate approximate B(E2) values as the sum of proton and neutron contributions: B(E2)ₐₚₚᵣₒₓ = [Nₚ/2 × (Ωₚ/2 − Nₚ/2)] + [Nₙ/2 × (Ωₙ/2 − Nₙ/2)], using different combinations of major shell (Ω=22 for the 28-50 shell) and sub-shell valence spaces (Ω=12 for protons below Z=40, Ω=10 for neutrons below N=38). They compare these calculations to experimental B(E2) values from the literature for isotopic chains from Ca to Zr.

## Key numbers

- The 28-50 major shell gives Ωₚ = 22 for protons and Ωₙ = 22 for neutrons; the proton sub-shell closure at Z=40 gives Ωₚ = 12 for Z below 40 and Ωₚ = 10 for Z above; the neutron sub-shell closure at N=38 gives Ωₙ = 10 for N below 38 and Ωₙ = 12 for N above
- The scaling factor used to compare calculations to experiment is 100 e²fm⁴ per unit of (Nₚₐᵣₜ × Nₕₒₗ)ₚ + (Nₚₐᵣₜ × Nₕₒₗ)ₙ, chosen to reproduce the Ca isotope curve where only the f₇/₂ single-j shell (Ω=8) plays a role
- The maximum of the B(E2) curve occurs at N=38-40 for a neutron major shell, at N=32-34 for the lowest neutron sub-shell from a N=38 closure, and at N=44 for the highest neutron sub-shell
- Deformation changes in Kr (Z=36) and Sr (Z=38) isotopes from β≈0.4 at N=40 down to β≈0.15 at N=50 produce no irregularities in the B(E2) ratios between consecutive isotopes

## Context

This work extends the seniority scheme approach previously applied to single-j shell configurations (Talmi, Casten) to complex valence shells in the N,Z≈40 region. It directly addresses the interpretation of recent Coulomb excitation measurements at GANIL for ⁶⁸Ni (Sorlin 2002) and ⁷⁰Zn (Sorlin 2001), which had been explained by parity change from the ν1g9/2 orbital and deformation onset respectively. The analysis contradicts the interpretation of a N=40 sub-shell closure as the dominant effect, instead showing that the B(E2) features across multiple isotopic chains (Ni, Zn, Ge, Se, Kr, Sr, Zr) are consistent with a global scenario where the proton-neutron interaction governs the valence space structure. The work enables future systematic analyses of B(E2) systematics in this region and provides criteria for identifying valence space changes from experimental B(E2) curves.
