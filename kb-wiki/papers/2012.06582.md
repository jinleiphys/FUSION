# Explainable machine learning of the underlying physics of high-energy particle collisions

> We present an implementation of an explainable and physics-aware machine learning model capable of inferring the underlying physics of high-energy particle collisions using the information encoded in the energy-momentum four-vectors of the final state particles. We demonstrate the proof-of-concept of our White Box AI approach using a Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) which learns from a DGLAP-based parton shower Monte Carlo event generator. We show, for the first time, that our approach leads to a network that is able to learn not only the final distribution of particles, but also the underlying parton branching mechanism, i.e. the Altarelli-Parisi splitting function, the ordering variable of the shower, and the scaling behavior. While the current work is focused on perturbative physics of the parton shower, we foresee a broad range of applications of our framework to areas that are currently difficult to address from first principles in QCD. Examples include nonperturbative and collective effects, factorization breaking and the modification of the parton shower in heavy-ion, and electron-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate a proof-of-concept that a Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) trained on the final-state four-vectors of a DGLAP-based parton shower can learn not only the final particle distributions but also the underlying parton branching mechanism, including the Altarelli-Parisi splitting function, the ordering variable of the shower, and the scaling behavior. The claim is explicitly hedged as a proof-of-concept focused on perturbative physics, with the caveat that the current work uses a simplified pure gluon shower and does not yet address nonperturbative effects or experimental data.

## Method

The authors trained a GAN on the final-state four-vectors produced by a DGLAP-based parton shower Monte Carlo event generator for pure gluon showers at energies Q = 200-800 GeV. The generator network was designed with two components: a time-independent part to learn the Altarelli-Parisi splitting function and azimuthal angle, and a time-dependent part to learn the ordering variable (splitting angle) that depends on the shower evolution scale. The discriminator used deep sets to achieve permutation invariance for the variable-length sets of particle four-vectors. The network was trained on 3.5 × 10^7 parton shower events and evaluated against 3.5 × 10^8 GAN-generated events after 700 training epochs.

## Key numbers

- Shower evolution from hard scale Q down to hadronization scale of 1 GeV
- Cutoff parameter epsilon = 0.03 on momentum fraction z to avoid singular endpoints
- Training data generated for energies in the range Q = 200-800 GeV
- Generator network used 5 hidden layers with 50 neurons each
- 3.5 × 10^7 parton shower events used for training; 3.5 × 10^8 GAN events evaluated after 700 epochs
- Deep sets allowed to contain up to 200 four-vectors per event

## Context

This work extends the application of GANs in high-energy physics (de Oliveira 2015, Komiske 2016, Kasieczka 2017, Metodiev 2017) from simulating event distributions to actually extracting underlying physics mechanisms, departing from the typical black-box paradigm. It builds on efforts to infer physics information from data using probabilistic models based on jet clustering (Andreassen 2018) and convolutional autoencoders within showers (Monk 2018). The framework enables future studies of nonperturbative effects, collective effects, and modification of the parton shower in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC, as well as electron-nucleus collisions at the future Electron-Ion Collider.
