# Neutron capture cross sections from surrogate reaction data and theory: connecting the pieces with a Markov-Chain Monte Carlo approach

> The neutron capture cross section for $^{90}Zr(n, γ)$ has recently been determined using surrogate $^{92}Zr(p, dγ)$ data and nuclear reaction theory. That work employed an approximate fitting method based on Bayesian Monte Carlo sampling to determine parameters needed for calculating the $^{90}Zr(n, γ)$ cross section. Here, we improve the approach by introducing a more sophisticated Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling method. We present preliminary results.

## Key claim
The authors improve upon a previous Bayesian Monte Carlo fitting method for determining the $^{90}Zr(n,\gamma)$ cross section from surrogate $^{92}Zr(p,d\gamma)$ data by introducing a Markov-Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampling approach, but present only preliminary results.

## Method
The authors model the decay of the compound nucleus $^{91}Zr$ using a Hauser-Feshbach statistical model with a composite Gilbert-Cameron level density (five varied parameters) and an enhanced generalized Lorentzian (EGLO) plus standard Lorentzian (SLO) gamma-ray strength function (nine varied parameters). They employ a Metropolis-Hastings MCMC algorithm to sample the 14-parameter space, using finite flat prior distributions centered on recommended values from RIPL-3. The posterior parameter distribution is then used to calculate the $^{90}Zr(n,\gamma)$ cross section.

## Key numbers
- 14 parameters were varied simultaneously: 5 level density parameters (asymptotic level density parameter $\tilde{a}$, shell correction energy $\delta W$, level density damping parameter $\gamma$, pairing energy shift $\Delta$, and effective moment of inertia) and 9 gamma-ray strength function parameters (3 each for two EGLO peaks and one SLO peak).
- The preliminary $^{90}Zr(n,\gamma)$ cross section result is shown with a 68% confidence interval (16th and 84th quartiles of the sampled distribution).
- The result agrees with the TENDL 2015 and ENDF/B-VII.1 evaluations, both based on directly-measured data.

## Context
This work extends the surrogate method of Escher (2012) and the Bayesian Monte Carlo approach of the prior $^{90}Zr(n,\gamma)$ determination from $^{92}Zr(p,d\gamma)$ data (Gorton and Escher, 2018). The improved MCMC sampling method propagates all constraints encoded in the joint probability distribution of parameters, including correlations. Future work will investigate correlations between model parameters and between cross sections at different energies.
