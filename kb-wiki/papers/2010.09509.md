# The high energy spectrum of internal positrons from radiative muon capture on nuclei

> The Mu2e and COMET collaborations will search for nucleus-catalyzed muon conversion to positrons ($μ^-\rightarrow e^+$) as a signal of lepton number violation. A key background for this search is radiative muon capture where either: 1) a real photon converts to an $e^+ e^-$ pair "externally" in surrounding material, or 2) a virtual photon mediates the production of an $e^+e^-$ pair "internally". If the $e^+$ has an energy approaching the signal region then it can serve as an irreducible background. In this work we describe how the near end-point internal positron spectrum can be related to the real photon spectrum from the same nucleus, which encodes all non-trivial nuclear physics.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the high-energy (near end-point) spectrum of positrons from internal pair production in radiative muon capture can be predicted directly from the measured real photon spectrum from the same nucleus, with errors controlled by the small parameter δ = (E− − me)/q0. This allows nuclear physics uncertainties to be circumvented empirically, but the approximation neglects Coulomb corrections from the nuclear field, which may be insufficient near the endpoint.

## Method
The authors relate the S-matrix element for radiative muon capture producing a real photon to the corresponding element for internal e+e− pair production via a virtual photon. They decompose the four-body phase space for the e+e− final state into a three-body phase space for a massive virtual photon times the two-body decay of that photon, then integrate over the lepton tensor and virtual-photon mass m* to derive an internal conversion probability P_int. By expanding in the small parameter δ near the positron endpoint, they show that the leading-order conversion probability depends only on the real photon spectrum and is independent of unconstrained nuclear matrix elements (transverse T*² and longitudinal L*² corrections).

## Key numbers
- The endpoint shift for positrons relative to photons is me = 0.511 MeV.
- The maximal virtual photon mass m*(+) near the endpoint scales as m*(+) = q0 × [2√((1−δ)δ) + O(ε)] where ε = me/q0.
- The ratio m*(+)/m*(−) in the regime δ ≫ ε scales as 2δ/ε.
- The energy resolution of Mu2e is O(100 keV), meaning electron mass effects can be resolved.
- The expected sensitivity for the ratio R_eμ of μ→e events to total muon captures is at the level of 10⁻¹⁷.

## Context
This work addresses a key background for the Mu2e (Bernstein 2019) and COMET (Lee 2018) experiments searching for lepton number violation via μ⁻ → e⁺ conversion. It extends the classic Kroll-Wada (1955) analysis of e+e− production relative to single photon production, but disagrees with their conclusion that longitudinal matrix elements are negligible, finding instead that L*² is O(1) in the m*→0 limit. The result enables experiments to use direct measurements of the real photon spectrum from radiative muon capture on aluminum to predict both external and internal positron backgrounds, or conversely to use measured positron spectra to infer the photon spectrum. Future work is needed to incorporate Coulomb distortion effects on the outgoing electron/positron wavefunctions.
