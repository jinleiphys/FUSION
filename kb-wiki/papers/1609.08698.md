# Dependence of fusion on isospin dynamics

> We introduce a new microscopic approach to calculate the dependence of fusion barriers and cross-sections on isospin dynamics. The method is based on the time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory and the isoscalar and isovector properties of the energy density functional (EDF). The contribution to the fusion barriers originating from the isoscalar and isovector parts of the EDF is calculated. It is shown that for non-symmetric systems the isovector dynamics influence the sub-barrier fusion cross-sections. For most systems this results in an enhancement of the sub-barrier cross-sections, while for others we observe differing degrees of hindrance. We use this approach to provide an explanation of recently measured fusion cross sections which show a surprising enhancement at low $E_\mathrm{c.m.}$ energies for the system $^{40}$Ca+$^{132}$Sn as compared to the more neutron-rich system $^{48}$Ca+$^{132}$Sn, and discuss the dependence of sub-barrier fusion cross-sections on transfer.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a microscopic approach based on time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory to show that isovector dynamics can either enhance or hinder sub-barrier fusion cross-sections depending on the system, and that the puzzling experimental enhancement of sub-barrier fusion for ⁴⁰Ca+¹³²Sn compared to ⁴⁸Ca+¹³²Sn arises from the vanishing of the isovector contribution in the latter system due to negative Q-values for neutron pickup.

## Method

The authors used the density-constrained TDHF (DC-TDHF) method with the Skyrme SLy4 energy density functional (EDF) decomposed into isoscalar and isovector parts. They calculated fusion barriers for several systems (⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁸Ca, ¹⁶O+²⁰⁸Pb, ⁴⁰Ca+¹³²Sn, ⁴⁸Ca+¹³²Sn, and ⁵⁴Ca+¹³²Sn) by performing TDHF time-evolutions in a three-dimensional Cartesian geometry with no symmetry assumptions, then constraining the densities to obtain the ion-ion interaction potential. They also analyzed microscopic neutron and proton currents in the neck region to understand the isovector dynamics.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁸Ca at E_c.m. = 55 MeV: the isovector contribution reduces the isoscalar barrier, resulting in narrowing of the fusion barrier and enhancement of sub-barrier fusion cross-sections.
- For ¹⁶O+²⁰⁸Pb at E_c.m. = 75, 90, and 120 MeV: the isovector contribution decreases with increasing beam energy; at 75 MeV there is substantial sub-barrier fusion enhancement, while at 120 MeV the isovector contribution vanishes and the total barrier approaches the frozen density barrier.
- For ⁴⁰Ca+¹³²Sn at E_c.m. = 120 MeV: strong isovector reduction of the isoscalar barrier is observed, leading to a narrower barrier.
- For ⁴⁸Ca+¹³²Sn at E_c.m. = 120 MeV: the isovector contribution vanishes, making the fusion barrier much wider than for ⁴⁰Ca+¹³²Sn.
- For ⁵⁴Ca+¹³²Sn at E_c.m. = 120 MeV: the isovector effect is reversed, causing widening of the barrier and hindrance of sub-barrier fusion.
- In the neck region at R = 11.5 fm and E_c.m. = 120 MeV: the isovector current density for ⁴⁸Ca+¹³²Sn is an order of magnitude lower than for ⁴⁰Ca+¹³²Sn; the proton current density for ⁴⁸Ca+¹³²Sn is 30%–50% smaller than for ⁴⁰Ca+¹³²Sn.

## Context

This work extends previous DC-TDHF studies (Oberacker 2013) that showed similar barrier heights but different widths for the Ca+Sn systems, and provides a microscopic explanation for experimental results (Kolata 2012) where sub-barrier fusion enhancement for ⁴⁰Ca+¹³²Sn could not be explained by Q-value arguments alone. The approach enables quantitative assessment of the role of neutron transfer in sub-barrier fusion, which had been difficult with earlier coupled-channels calculations (Rowley 1992, Hagino 2012) and models of intermediate neutron rearrangements (Zagrebaev 2003, 2007; Karpov 2015). The method also connects to studies of pre-equilibrium giant-dipole resonance (Dasso 1985, Chomaz 1993, Baran 1996–2009, Simenel 2001, 2007, Sekizawa 2013) and provides a framework for future work on cross-section ratios and other systems.
