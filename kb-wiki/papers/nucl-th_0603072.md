# Predictions of striking energy and angular dependence in pp --> (pp)_S-wave pi^0 production

> A phenomenological calculation from threshold to 800 MeV of the initial proton beam energy is presented to describe recent data on the reaction pp --> (pp)_S-wave pi^0 with a low energy cut on the final state diproton excitation energy. A strong forward dip is obtained in the differential cross section as in the data from COSY at 800 MeV, although the absolute value of the forward cross section is too low. Earlier low energy data from CELSIUS are reasonably well reproduced. In the unexplored energy interval between these two experiments the model predicts a spectacular energy dependence both in the forward direction and in the angle-integrated cross section.

## Key claim
The model predicts a spectacular energy dependence in both the forward differential cross section and the angle-integrated cross section for the reaction pp → (pp)_S-wave π^0 in the unexplored energy interval between the CELSIUS data (310–425 MeV) and the COSY data at 800 MeV, though the absolute value of the forward cross section at 800 MeV is too low compared to experiment.

## Method
A phenomenological calculation was performed from threshold to 800 MeV initial proton beam energy, using a production operator that includes direct production from each nucleon with distorted initial and final pp states, Δ(1232) resonance excitation via coupled channels with pion and ρ meson exchange, pion s-wave rescattering parameterized on the energy shell, and heavy meson exchange effects. The final state diproton was constrained to the ¹S₀ state by applying a low energy cut (3 MeV) on the relative pp excitation energy, and the long-range free nucleon wave functions and Coulomb interaction were treated explicitly.

## Key numbers
- The model reproduces CELSIUS data at energies between 310 and 425 MeV with a cut of 3 MeV on the diproton energy.
- At 800 MeV, the COSY data show the cross section dropping by 30% in the interval where cosθ changed from 0.97 to 1.
- The forward cross section at 800 MeV is predicted to be about 700 nb/sr (outside the figure range).
- The Δ resonance excitation is displaced by about 80 MeV in the c.m.s. to appear most prominently at about 800 MeV laboratory energy.
- The model fits data well up to η = q_max/m_π ≈ 0.6, where Ss-wave production is dominant.

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological model previously applied to pion absorption on a bound diproton (Niskanen, 1990s) and to free pp reactions (Niskanen, 2000) to higher energies with a final-state cut. It reproduces the forward dip seen in COSY/ANKE data (Dymov, 2005) and the CELSIUS data (Bilger, 2000), and predicts strong energy dependence in the uncharted region between 500 and 800 MeV. The results enable future experiments at COSY to test the predicted extreme angular and energy variations, and may allow extraction of reaction matrix elements from spin observables due to the simplified spin structure of the final ¹S₀ state.
