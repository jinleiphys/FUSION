# Medium Modification of Nucleon Properties in Skyrme Model

> A Skyrme type Lagrangian for a skyrmion imbedded in a baryon rich environment is proposed. The dependence of static nucleon properties and nucleon - nucleon tensor interaction on nuclear density is investigated.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a Skyrme-type Lagrangian for a skyrmion embedded in a baryon-rich environment, claiming that the modification of nucleon properties in nuclear matter arises from the influence of the medium on nonlinear pion fields via the pion self-energy, rather than from a trivial scale renormalization of model parameters.

## Method

The authors modify the free-space Skyrme Lagrangian by incorporating the pion self-energy operator (polarization operator) into the chiral-symmetry-breaking term, using the nonlocal Kisslinger optical potential to represent medium effects. For homogeneous nuclear matter, they treat the resulting dipole susceptibility and S-wave susceptibility as constants, solve the Euler-Lagrange equation for the spherically symmetric hedgehog ansatz, and compute static nucleon properties and the nucleon-nucleon tensor interaction using the product approximation for two skyrmions.

## Key numbers

- Critical nuclear density for skyrmion stability: \(\rho_{crit} \geq 1.3 \rho_0\) for \(g_0' = 1/3\) and \(\rho_{crit} \geq 3 \rho_0\) for \(g_0' = 0.7\), where \(\rho_0 = 0.5 m_\pi^3\) is normal nuclear density.
- In-medium nucleon mass at normal nuclear density: \(M_N^* = 572\) MeV for \(g_0' = 1/3\) (free value is 939 MeV).
- Ratio of in-medium to free axial coupling constant: \(g_A^*/g_A = 0.62\) at \(\rho = \rho_0\) with \(g_0' = 1/3\).
- Swelling of nucleon size: approximately 30% increase in root-mean-square radii at normal nuclear density.
- Effective P-wave scattering volume \(c_o\) must be reduced by a factor of 2 to reproduce the experimentally observed quenching ratio \(g_A^*/g_A = 0.8\).

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model approach of Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983) to nuclear matter by incorporating medium effects through the pion self-energy, similar to the treatment of pions in nuclei by Ericson and Weise (1988). The results contradict the pion excess model of Ericson (1987), which predicted much larger nucleon swelling, and are consistent with the nuclear binding model of Noble (1979) and quantum hadrodynamics results of Song, Zhang, and Su (1995) for the nucleon effective mass. The suppression of the tensor interaction in nuclear matter agrees with the scaling model of Brown and Rho (1991) and the analysis of Hosaka and Toki (1991).
