# Extracting the Mass Dependence and Quantum Numbers of Short-Range Correlated Pairs from A(e,e'p) and A(e,e'pp) Scattering

> The nuclear mass dependence of the number of short-range correlated (SRC) proton-proton (pp) and proton-neutron (pn) pairs in nuclei is a sensitive probe of the dynamics of short-range pairs in the ground state of atomic nuclei. This work presents an analysis of electroinduced single-proton and two-proton knockout measurements off 12C, 27Al, 56Fe, and 208Pb in kinematics dominated by scattering off SRC pairs. The nuclear mass dependence of the observed A(e,e'pp)/12C(e,e'pp) cross-section ratios and the extracted number of pp- and pn-SRC pairs are much softer than the mass dependence of the total number of possible pairs. This is in agreement with a physical picture of SRC affecting predominantly nucleon-nucleon pairs in a nodeless relative-S state of the mean-field basis.

## Key claim

The nuclear mass dependence of the number of short-range correlated (SRC) proton-proton and proton-neutron pairs is much softer than the combinatorial scaling based on the total number of possible pairs, consistent with SRC affecting predominantly nucleon-nucleon pairs in a nodeless relative-S state of the mean-field basis, with the caveat that the extracted number of pp pairs is very sensitive to single charge-exchange corrections.

## Method

The authors analyzed electroinduced single-proton and two-proton knockout measurements from 12C, 27Al, 56Fe, and 208Pb targets using the CLAS detector at Jefferson Lab with a 5.014 GeV electron beam, selecting events with Q2 > 1.5 GeV2, xB > 1.2, and missing momentum between 300 and 600 MeV/c to isolate scattering off SRC pairs. They extracted A(e,e'pp)/12C(e,e'pp) and A(e,e'p)/12C(e,e'p) cross-section ratios, corrected for final-state interactions using a relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approximation (RMSGA) including single charge-exchange effects, and compared the results to factorized calculations using independent-particle model (IPM) wave functions with correlation operators.

## Key numbers

- The measured Pb/C A(e,e'pp) cross-section ratio is 3.8 ± 0.5, compared to a combinatorial scaling based on the number of pp pairs that would yield a ratio of over 200.
- The ZRA-RMSGA calculation yields a Pb/C ratio of 4.96 (+0.11, -0.14).
- The extracted number of pp SRC pairs relative to Carbon for 208Pb is 7.73 (+5.92, -7.23), compared to S_n=0 counting of 45.29 and ZRA calculation of 17.44.
- The extracted number of pn SRC pairs relative to Carbon for 208Pb is 24.87 (+3.89, -3.42), compared to S_n=0 counting of 37.62 and ZRA calculation of 18.80.
- The ZRA predicts a mass dependence proportional to A^(1.01 ± 0.02), while S_n=0 counting gives A^(1.12 ± 0.02).

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of SRC pair abundances by Hen (2014) and the universal high-momentum tail scaling observed by Fomin (2011) and Egiyan (2003, 2005), providing the first systematic extraction of the mass dependence of pp and pn SRC pairs from medium and heavy nuclei. The results support the theoretical framework of Vanhalst (2011, 2012) and Roth (2010) where correlation operators act on IPM S_n=0 pairs, and are consistent with evidence from 16O(e,e'pp)14C measurements by Onderwater (1997) for 1S0(1) dominance in SRC-prone pp pairs. This enables future studies of the contact term in nuclear systems and connections to ultra-cold atomic systems as discussed by Hen (2014).
