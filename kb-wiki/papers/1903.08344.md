# Sensitivity of Type Ia supernovae to electron capture rates

> The thermonuclear explosion of massive white dwarfs is believed to explain at least a fraction of Type Ia supernovae (SNIa). After thermal runaway, electron captures on the ashes left behind by the burning front determine a loss of pressure, which impacts the dynamics of the explosion and the neutron excess of matter. Indeed, overproduction of neutron-rich species such as $^{54}$Cr has been deemed a problem of Chandrasekhar-mass models of SNIa for a long time. I present the results of a sensitivity study of SNIa models to the rates of weak interactions, which have been incorporated directly into the hydrodynamic explosion code. The weak rates have been scaled up/down by a factor ten, either globally for a common bibliographical source, or individually for selected isotopes. In line with previous works, the impact of weak rates uncertainties on sub-Chandrasekhar models of SNIa is almost negligible. The impact on the dynamics of Chandrasekhar-mass models and on the yield of $^{56}$Ni is also scarce. The strongest effect is found on the nucleosynthesis of neutron-rich nuclei, such as $^{48}$Ca, $^{54}$Cr, $^{58}$Fe, and $^{64}$Ni. The species with the highest influence on nucleosynthesis do not coincide with the isotopes that contribute most to the neutronization of matter. Among the last ones, there are protons, $^{54,55}$Fe, $^{55}$Co, and $^{56}$Ni, while the main influencers are $^{54,55}$Mn and $^{55-57}$Fe, in disagreement with Parikh et al (2013), who found that SNIa nucleosynthesis is most sensitive to the $β^+$-decay rates of $^{28}$Si, $^{32}$S, and $^{36}$Ar. An eventual increase in all weak rates on pf-shell nuclei would affect the dynamical evolution of hot bubbles, running away at the beginning of the explosion, and the yields of SNIa.

## Key claim

The sensitivity of Type Ia supernova models to electron capture rates is strongest for the nucleosynthesis of neutron-rich nuclei such as ⁴⁸Ca, ⁵⁴Cr, ⁵⁸Fe, and ⁶⁴Ni, while the impact on dynamics and ⁵⁶Ni yield is scarce; however, the species that most influence nucleosynthesis (⁵⁴,⁵⁵Mn and ⁵⁵-⁵⁷Fe) do not coincide with the isotopes that contribute most to neutronization (protons, ⁵⁴,⁵⁵Fe, ⁵⁵Co, and ⁵⁶Ni), in disagreement with Parikh et al (2013) who found maximal sensitivity to β⁺-decay rates of ²⁸Si, ³²S, and ³⁶Ar.

## Method

The author computed one-dimensional spherical explosion models of sub-Chandrasekhar and Chandrasekhar-mass white dwarfs using a hydrocode that integrates hydrodynamics and a large nuclear network simultaneously, avoiding post-processing. Weak interaction rates were scaled up or down by a factor of ten, either globally for a common bibliographic source (FFN82, Oda94, MPLD00, PF03) or individually for selected isotopes, and the hydrodynamic model was rerun for each modification to maintain consistency between hydrodynamics and nucleosynthesis.

## Key numbers

- Sub-Chandrasekhar model S (1.06 M☉, central density 4.8×10⁷ g cm⁻³): kinetic energy 1.32×10⁵¹ erg, ⁵⁶Ni mass 0.664 M☉
- Sub-Chandrasekhar model S+ (1.15 M☉, central density 9.5×10⁷ g cm⁻³): kinetic energy 1.46×10⁵¹ erg, ⁵⁶Ni mass 0.894 M☉
- Chandrasekhar-mass model C3 (1.37 M☉, central density 3.0×10⁹ g cm⁻³): kinetic energy 1.42×10⁵¹ erg, ⁵⁶Ni mass 0.685 M☉
- Scaling all MPLD00 rates up by factor 10 in model C3: kinetic energy decreases by 3.0%, ⁵⁶Ni yield decreases by 13%, ⁴⁸Ca yield increases by factor 4.9×10⁵, ⁵⁴Cr yield increases by factor 8.9
- Scaling all MPLD00 rates up by factor 10 except protons in model C3: ⁵⁶Ni yield decreases by 6.6%, ⁴⁸Ca yield increases by factor 2.6×10³, ⁵⁴Cr yield increases by factor 3.8
- Scaling all MPLD00 rates down by factor 10 in model C3: ⁵⁶Ni yield increases by 10.4%, ⁵⁴Cr yield decreases by factor 1.7×10⁻⁵
- Sub-Chandrasekhar model S with MPLD00 rates scaled up by factor 10: kinetic energy changes by 0.2%, ⁵⁶Ni yield changes by 2.1%
- Model C3 with MPLD00 rates scaled up by factor 10 except protons: ⁵⁶Ni yield decreases by 6.6%, ⁴⁸Ca yield increases by factor 2.6×10³, ⁵⁴Cr yield increases by factor 3.8
- Model C5 (central density 5.0×10⁹ g cm⁻³) with MPLD00 rates scaled up by factor 10 except protons: ⁸⁰Se yield increases by factor 7.3×10³, ⁴⁸Ca yield increases by factor 14, ⁶⁴Ni yield increases by factor 2.1, ⁵⁴Cr yield increases by factor 1.6

## Context

This work extends the sensitivity studies of Bravo (2000) and Parikh et al (2013) by incorporating weak rate modifications directly into the hydrodynamic explosion code rather than using nuclear post-processing, and by exploring a wider range of central densities (2-5×10⁹ g cm⁻³) for Chandrasekhar-mass models. The results contradict Parikh et al (2013), who found maximal sensitivity to electron capture rates on α-elements ²⁸Si, ³²S, and ³⁶Ar, instead identifying ⁵⁴,⁵⁵Mn and ⁵⁵-⁵⁷Fe as the main influencers. The work enables future studies to focus experimental and theoretical efforts on measuring weak rates for these specific iron-group nuclei, and suggests that an increase in all weak rates on pf-shell nuclei could affect the dynamical evolution of hot bubbles in three-dimensional models.
