# Electroweak structure of light nuclei within chiral effective field theory

> We review the results of the most recent calculations for the electromagnetic structure of light nuclei, the weak muon capture on deuteron and 3He and the weak proton-proton capture reaction at energies of astrophysical interest, performed within the chiral effective field theory framework.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory (χEFT) calculations of electroweak observables in light nuclei (A=2,3) achieve good agreement with experimental data, except for the A=3 magnetic form factors at high momentum transfer, a region the author notes is "well beyond the applicability range of χEFT." The weak-sector results rely on the older Park et al. model (up to N2LO), not the newer N3LO electromagnetic operators, making a new derivation of axial currents "highly desirable."

## Method

The author reviews calculations of electromagnetic form factors and static properties of the deuteron, triton, and ³He using χEFT electromagnetic current operators up to N3LO, derived via time-ordered perturbation theory by Pastore et al. Weak observables, muon capture on deuteron and ³He, and the proton-proton weak capture reaction, are computed using the Park et al. axial current operators up to N2LO, with nuclear wave functions from the N3LO two-nucleon potential of Entem and Machleidt and the N2LO three-nucleon interaction of Navrátil et al. Low-energy constants in the electromagnetic currents are fixed to the deuteron magnetic moment, the isoscalar A=3 magnetic moment, and either the neutron-proton radiative capture cross section or the isovector A=3 magnetic moment; the axial current LEC d_R is related to the three-nucleon interaction LEC c_D, fitted to the triton binding energy and the Gamow-Teller matrix element in tritium β-decay.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron r.m.s. radius: 1.972 ± 0.004 fm (experiment: 1.9733 ± 0.0044 fm)
- Deuteron quadrupole moment: 0.2836 ± 0.0016 fm² (experiment: 0.2859 ± 0.0003 fm)
- ³He charge radius: 1.962 ± 0.004 fm (experiment: 1.959 ± 0.030 fm)
- ³H charge radius: 1.756 ± 0.006 fm (experiment: 1.755 ± 0.086 fm)
- Doublet capture rate for muon capture on deuteron (Γ^D): 399 ± 3 sec⁻¹ (total, with all partial waves)
- Total capture rate for muon capture on ³He (Γ_0): 1494 ± 21 sec⁻¹ (experiment: 1496 ± 4 sec⁻¹)
- Astrophysical S-factor for pp capture at zero energy: S(0) = (4.030 ± 0.006) × 10⁻²³ MeV fm², with a P-wave contribution of 0.020 × 10⁻²³ MeV fm²

## Context

This work extends the χEFT approach to electroweak observables beyond earlier "hybrid" calculations that used phenomenological potentials (e.g., Argonne v18 from Wiringa et al., 1995) with χEFT currents, and beyond the original Park et al. (1996, 2003) derivations. The electromagnetic results improve upon the phenomenological AV18 calculation, which gave a deuteron quadrupole moment of 0.275 fm² in significant disagreement with experiment, whereas the χEFT result (0.2836 fm²) agrees well. The weak capture results enable more precise predictions for solar neutrino physics and stellar evolution, and the framework is noted to be extendable to radiative proton-deuteron and weak proton-³He captures.
