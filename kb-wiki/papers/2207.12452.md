# Multi-Stage Heavy Quark Transport in Ultra-relativistic Heavy-ion Collisions

> The quark gluon plasma (QGP) is one of the most interesting forms of matter providing us with insight on quantum chromodynamics (QCD) and the early universe. It is believed that the heavy-ion collision experiments at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) have created the QGP medium by colliding two heavy nuclei at nearly the speed of light. Since the collision happens really fast, we can not observe the QGP directly. Instead, we look at the hundreds or even thousands of final hadrons coming out of the collision. In particular, jet and heavy flavor observables are excellent probes of the transport properties of such a medium. On the theoretical side, computational models are essential to make the connections between the final observables and the plasma. Previously studies have employed a comprehensive multistage modeling approach of both the probes and the medium. In this dissertation, heavy quarks are investigated as probes pf the QGP. First, the framework that describes the evolution of both soft and hard particles during the collision is discussed, which includes initial condition, hydrodynamical expansion, parton transport, hadronization, and hadronic rescattering. It has recently been organized into the Jet Energy-loss Tomography with a Statistically and Computationally Advanced Program Envelope (JETSCAPE) framework, which allows people to study heavy-ion collision in a more systematic manner. To study the energy loss of hard partons inside the QGP medium, the linear Boltzmann transport model (LBT) and in medium DGLAP evolution (implemented in the MATTER model) are combined and have achieved a simultaneous description of both charged hadron, D meson, and inclusive jet observables. To further extract the transport coefficients, a Bayesian analysis is conducted which constrains the parameters in the transport models.

## Key claim

The dissertation claims that a multi-stage heavy quark transport model, combining the linear Boltzmann transport model (LBT) and in-medium DGLAP evolution (MATTER model) within the JETSCAPE framework, can simultaneously describe charged hadron, D meson, and inclusive jet observables, but the Bayesian analysis reveals that parameters such as $c_1$ and $c_2$ remain the most difficult to constrain, with their posterior distributions often close to a uniform posterior.

## Method

The author implements a multi-stage modeling approach within the JETSCAPE framework that includes initial conditions, hydrodynamical expansion, parton transport, hadronization, and hadronic rescattering. Heavy quark energy loss is modeled by combining the linear Boltzmann transport model (LBT) with in-medium DGLAP evolution (MATTER model). A Bayesian analysis is conducted using Gaussian process emulators trained on Latin hypercube sampling design points, with principal component analysis to reduce output dimensionality, and Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling to extract posterior distributions of transport parameters.

## Key numbers

*   For the simple analytical bulk model with three parameters ($a$, $b$, $c$), 50 design points were sampled and four levels of statistical model uncertainty (1%, 5%, 10%, 15%) were investigated.
*   With just 1% statistical model uncertainty in the simple bulk model, $\langle\Delta\rangle(c)$ is around 0.13, which translates to a Gaussian posterior with $\sigma\approx 0.2$ on average.
*   When reducing statistics to 1/3 of events per design point, 5 principal components explain around 86% of the total variance, compared to 95% before.
*   Additional Gaussian noise with 0.02 standard deviation was already larger than the model statistical fluctuation for low $p_T$ $R_{AA}$ data.
*   For the full heavy-ion model, the switching scale $Q_s$ has a prior constraint $Q_s > 1.5 \approx m_c$ where $m_c$ is the mass of the charm quark.

## Context

This work extends the multi-stage modeling approach previously organized into the JETSCAPE framework, which allows systematic study of heavy-ion collisions. The combination of LBT and MATTER models for heavy quark transport builds on prior work in parton energy loss modeling. The Bayesian analysis methodology, including Gaussian process emulators and principal component analysis, follows techniques used in earlier studies such as those by Paquet (2020) for effective bulk models. The results enable future extraction of transport coefficients with quantified uncertainties and highlight the need for more design points and statistics to constrain parameters like $c_1$ and $c_2$ that affect only a small temperature range.
