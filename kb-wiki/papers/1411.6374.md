# Anisotropic flow in pp-collisions at the LHC

> We discuss collective effects in $pp$--collisions at the LHC energies and derive an upper bound for the anisotropic flow coefficients $v_n$. A possibility of its verification via comparison with the measurements of $v_2$ is considered. We use an assumption on the relation of the two--particle correlations with the rotation of the transient state of matter.

# Anisotropic flow in pp-collisions at the LHC

## Key claim
The paper derives an upper bound of 1/4 for the anisotropic flow coefficients v_n in pp-collisions at LHC energies, but this bound is only valid in the high-energy limit where reflective scattering becomes the dominant mode and the impact parameter is fixed at the value where the elastic S-matrix element vanishes (S(s,b)=0). The bound is not applicable at lower energies where reflective scattering is absent.

## Method
The authors use the U-matrix approach to unitarization, where the elastic S-matrix element is written in rational form S(s,b) = (1-U(s,b))/(1+U(s,b)). They relate the measured anisotropic flow coefficients v_n to the bare coefficients via a weight function w(s,b) = [1+S(s,b)]^2/4, which takes the value 1/4 at the impact parameter b=r(s) where S(s,b)=0. Using the asymptotic relation that observables in multiparticle production are dominated by collisions at b=r(s), they obtain the bound v_n ≤ 1/4. The authors also propose using three-particle correlations, with the normal to the reaction plane determined from two particles exhibiting the ridge effect, to measure v_2 experimentally.

## Key numbers
- Upper bound for anisotropic flow coefficients: v_n(p_T) ≤ 1/4 in the high-energy limit
- The value of r(s) (impact parameter where S(s,b)=0) is about 0.2 fm at center-of-mass energy √s = 7 TeV
- Reflective scattering mode turns on starting at energy √s ≃ 2 TeV
- In nuclear collisions, v_2 values are in the region 0.1-0.2 at p_T ∼ 1.5 GeV/c
- From the constituent quark scaling relation v_2(p_T) ≃ n_Q v_2^Q(p_T/n_Q), the upper bound for the quark elliptic flow v_2^Q is 1/12

## Context
This work extends the discussion of collective effects from heavy-ion collisions to proton-proton collisions, following the observation of the ridge effect by the CMS Collaboration (2010) in pp-collisions at √s = 7 TeV. The authors build on their previous work on reflective scattering (Troshin and Tyurin, 2007) and the rotating transient state mechanism for the ridge (Troshin and Tyurin, 2012). The paper provides a theoretical upper bound that can be tested against future measurements of anisotropic flow in pp-collisions at the LHC, particularly at √s = 13 TeV where the authors predict the ridge should appear in events with average multiplicities rather than only high multiplicities.
