# Updated analysis of jet quenching at RHIC and LHC within the light cone path integral approach

> We present results of a detailed analysis of experimental data on the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ and the flow coefficient $v_2$ for light hadrons from RHIC for $0.2$ TeV Au+Au collisions and from LHC for $2.76$ and $5.02$ TeV Pb+Pb, and $5.44$ TeV Xe+Xe collisions. We perform calculations within the light-cone path integral approach to induced gluon emission. We use running $α_s$ which is frozen at low momenta at some value $α_{s}^{fr}$. We find that the RHIC data support somewhat larger value of $α_{s}^{fr}$. For the $χ^2$ optimized values of $α_{s}^{fr}$, the theoretical predictions are in reasonable agreement with data on $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$. Calculations made for different formation times and life-times of the QGP show that jet quenching at the RHIC and LHC energies is only weakly sensitive to the initial and final stages of the QCD matter evolution.

## Key claim

The light-cone path integral approach to induced gluon emission, using running \(\alpha_s\) frozen at low momenta at a value \(\alpha_s^{fr}\), can achieve reasonable agreement with experimental data on both the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) and the flow coefficient \(v_2\) for light hadrons at RHIC and LHC energies, but only if a somewhat larger value of \(\alpha_s^{fr}\) is used for RHIC data compared to LHC data.

## Method

The author calculates the induced gluon spectrum using the light-cone path integral (LCPI) approach, which goes beyond the harmonic oscillator and single-rescattering approximations by treating an arbitrary number of rescatterings with a realistic dipole cross section. The QGP fireball is modeled with a uniform transverse density distribution and Bjorken 1+1D longitudinal expansion, using a flat entropy density derived from the Glauber wounded nucleon model. The medium-modified fragmentation functions are computed from the one-gluon spectrum accounting for multiple gluon emission in the independent radiation approximation, and the collisional energy loss is included by renormalizing the initial QGP temperature.

## Key numbers

* For RHIC 0.2 TeV Au+Au collisions, the optimal \(\alpha_s^{fr}\) is larger than for LHC collisions, as demonstrated by a \(\chi^2\) analysis of \(R_{AA}\) data with \(p_{T,min}=7\) and 10 GeV.
* For LHC 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions, and 5.44 TeV Xe+Xe collisions, the \(\chi^2\) analysis is performed for two upper \(p_T\) bounds: \(p_{T,max}=120\) GeV and 20 GeV.
* The collisional energy loss reduces \(R_{AA}\) by \(\sim 15-20\%\) at \(p_T\sim 10-20\) GeV for RHIC, and by \(\sim 5-15\%\) for \(p_T\sim 10-100\) GeV at LHC energies.
* The freeze-out time is taken as \(\tau_{f.o.}\approx 1.05\times(dN_{ch}/d\eta)^{1/3}\), with a thermalization time \(\tau_0=0.5\) fm (and also tested with \(\tau_0=0.8\) fm).
* The analysis uses data for centralities less than 30%, with a lower \(p_T\) bound of \(p_{T,min}=10\) GeV (and 7 GeV for PHENIX data).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous jet quenching analyses within the LCPI approach (Zakharov 2008, 2011, 2013) by including recent LHC data for 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb and 5.44 TeV Xe+Xe collisions, and by performing an accurate \(\chi^2\) fit rather than visual comparison. The approach contrasts with models based on the harmonic oscillator approximation (BDMPS) or the single-rescattering GLV formalism (CUJET3, Djordjevic), which may have theoretical uncertainties in the \(p_T\)- and centrality-dependence of \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\). The results enable more precise modeling of jet quenching by demonstrating that the LCPI approach can simultaneously describe both \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\) data, while highlighting the need for a collision-energy-dependent \(\alpha_s^{fr}\).
