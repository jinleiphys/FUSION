# $α$ and triton clustering in $^{35}$Cl

> Coupling of cluster and deformed structures are important for dynamics of nuclear structure. Threshold energy has been discussed to explain cluster structures coupling to deformed states but relation between threshold energy and excitation energy has open problems. Negative-parity superdeformed (SD) states were observed by a $γ$-spectroscopy experiment in $^{35}$Cl but its detailed structure is unclear. By analyzing coupling of cluster structures in deformed states and high-lying cluster states in $^{35}$Cl, cluster structures coupling to deformed states and excitation energy of high-lying cluster states are investigated. The antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) and the generator coordinate method (GCM) are used. An AMD wave function is a Slater determinant of Gaussian wave packets. By energy variational calculations with constraints on deformation and clustering, wave functions of deformed structures and $α$- and $t$-cluster structures are obtained. Adopting those wave functions as GCM basis, wave functions of ground and excited states are calculated. Various deformed bands are obtained and predicted. A $K^π= \frac{1}{2}^-$ deformed band, which corresponds to the observed SD band, dominates deformed structure and compact $α$- and $t$-cluster structure components. Particle-hole configurations of the dominant components with deformed and cluster structures are similar. In high-lying states, almost pure $α$- and $t$-cluster states are obtained in negative-parity states, and excitation energies of the $t$-cluster states are higher than those of $α$-cluster states. In conclusions, particle-hole configurations of cluster structure with small intercluster distance are important for coupling to low-energy deformed states. Threshold energies reflect to excitation energies of high-lying almost pure cluster states.

## Key claim

The paper argues that coupling of α-³¹P and t-³²S cluster structures to deformed states in ³⁵Cl is governed primarily by particle-hole configurations rather than by threshold energies alone, with compact cluster structures (small intercluster distance) coupling to low-energy deformed states when their particle-hole configurations match, while threshold energies only reflect the excitation energies of high-lying, almost pure cluster states.

## Method

The authors performed energy variational calculations using antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with constraints on quadrupole deformation β and on intercluster distances for α-³¹P and t-³²S systems, using the Gogny D1S effective interaction. Wave functions of deformed structures and cluster structures were obtained and then used as basis states in a generator coordinate method (GCM) calculation with parity and angular momentum projection to compute wave functions of ground and excited states.

## Key numbers

- In negative-parity states, the 1ħω, 3ħω, 5ħω, and 7ħω excited configurations have local minima at β ≈ 0.2, 0.4, 0.6, and 0.7, respectively
- Energies of α-³¹P and t-³²S cluster structures around the Coulomb barrier are −269 MeV and −263 MeV, respectively
- In-band B(E2) values of the theoretical K^π = 1/2⁻ band are 40–60 W.u., while experimental values for the negative-parity SD band are 32.9 ± 4.6 W.u. (19/2⁻ → 15/2⁻) and 25.3 ± 3.2 W.u. (23/2⁻ → 19/2⁻)
- Excitation energies of the "t" band are approximately 10 MeV higher than those of the "α" band
- For the K^π = 1/2⁻ band, overlaps with L-type α-³¹P and t-³²S cluster structure wave functions are more than 0.8 in the short intercluster distance region

## Context

This work extends the discussion of cluster structure coupling to deformed states previously studied in ⁴⁰Ca (where α-³⁶Ar clustering of the ND band was predicted by cluster model and confirmed experimentally), as well as in ³²,³⁴S, ³⁶Ar, ⁴²Ca, and ⁴⁴Ti. The paper addresses the open problem of why the threshold rule, which works well in light nuclei like ¹²C and ¹⁶O, fails quantitatively in the sd-shell region (e.g., the 0₂⁺ state in ⁴⁰Ca at 3.35 MeV is far below the α+³⁶Ar threshold of 7.04 MeV). The results enable understanding of how particle-hole configurations, rather than threshold energies alone, determine which cluster structures couple to deformed states, and clarify the structure of the negative-parity superdeformed band observed experimentally in ³⁵Cl by PhysRevC.88.034303.
