# The $D_{sJ}^*(2860)$ Mesons as Excited D-wave $c\bar{s}$ States

> A new charm-strange meson, the $D_{s1}^*(2863)$, has recently been observed by the LHCb collaboration which also determined the $D_{sJ}^*(2860)$ to have spin 3. One of the speculations about the previously observed $D_{s1}^*(2710)$ is that it is the $1^3D_1(c\bar{s})$ state. In this paper we reexamine the quark model properties and assignments of these three states in light of these new measurements. We conclude that the $D_{s1}^*(2863)$ and $D_{s3}^*(2863)$ are the $1^3D_1 (c\bar{s})$ and $1^3D_3 (c\bar{s})$ states respectively and the $D_{s1}^*(2710)$ is the $2^3S_1(c\bar{s})$ state. In addition to these three states there are another three excited $D_s$ states in this mass region still to be found; the $2^1S_0$ and two $1D_2$ states. We calculate the properties of these states and expect that LHCb has the capability of observing these states in the near future.

## Key claim

The authors conclude that the newly observed $D_{s1}^*(2863)$ and $D_{s3}^*(2863)$ are the $1^3D_1 (c\bar{s})$ and $1^3D_3 (c\bar{s})$ states respectively, and the previously observed $D_{s1}^*(2710)$ is the $2^3S_1(c\bar{s})$ state, but note that a small $2^3S_1 - 1^3D_1$ mixing angle of about 10 degrees is needed to bring the predicted branching ratio of the $D_{s1}^*(2710)$ into agreement with the measured value.

## Method

The authors compared the observed masses and decay widths of the $D_{s1}^*(2710)$, $D_{s1}^*(2860)$, and $D_{s3}^*(2860)$ to the predictions of the relativized quark model for masses and the $^3P_0$ quark pair creation model for strong decay widths. They calculated partial decay widths for the $2S$ and $1D$ charm-strange meson multiplets using harmonic oscillator wave functions and a quark creation parameter $\gamma=0.4$, using both the predicted masses and the experimentally measured masses as input.

## Key numbers

- Mass of $D_{s1}^*(2860)$: $2859 \pm 12 \pm 6 \pm 23$ MeV (statistical, systematic, model uncertainties).
- Width of $D_{s1}^*(2860)$: $159 \pm 23 \pm 27 \pm 72$ MeV.
- Mass of $D_{s3}^*(2860)$: $2860.5 \pm 2.6 \pm 2.5 \pm 6.0$ MeV.
- Width of $D_{s3}^*(2860)$: $53 \pm 7 \pm 4 \pm 6$ MeV.
- Mass of $D_{s1}^*(2710)$: $2709 \pm 4$ MeV; width: $117 \pm 13$ MeV.
- Ratio $\Gamma(D_{s1}^*(2710) \to D^* K) / \Gamma(D_{s1}^*(2710) \to D K) = 0.91 \pm 0.13 (\text{stat}) \pm 0.12 (\text{syst})$.
- Predicted total width for $D_s^*(2^3S_1)$ using measured mass: 125 MeV (compared to experimental $117 \pm 13$ MeV).
- Predicted total width for $D_s^*(1^3D_3)$ using measured mass: 34 MeV (compared to experimental $58 \pm 11$ MeV).
- Predicted total width for $D_s^*(1^3D_1)$ using measured mass: 187 MeV (compared to experimental $159 \pm 80$ MeV).
- Predicted mass of the $2^1S_0$ state: approximately 2650 MeV.
- Predicted masses of the two $J=2$ D-wave states: $D_2'$ at approximately 2872 MeV and $D_2$ at approximately 2846 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the quark-model analysis of excited charm-strange mesons by Godfrey and Moats (2013) and others, and directly responds to the LHCb collaboration's 2014 observation that the $D_{sJ}^*(2860)$ is actually two overlapping resonances with spin 1 and spin 3. The authors contradict earlier studies (e.g., Zhang 2006, Zhong 2009, Yuan 2012) which concluded that the $D_{s1}^*(2710)$ was the $1^3D_1$ state rather than the $2^3S_1$ state. The paper enables future experimental searches by LHCb for the three remaining undiscovered excited $D_s$ states in this mass region: the $2^1S_0$ and the two $1D_2$ states.
