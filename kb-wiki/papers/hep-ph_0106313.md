# Classical Initial Conditions for Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> We present the results of an analytical calculation of the distribution of gluons in the state immediately following a heavy ion collision in the quasi-classical limit of QCD given by McLerran-Venugopalan model. We show that the typical transverse energy Et of the produced gluons is of the order of the saturation scale of the nuclei Qs, as predicted by Mueller. We demonstrate that due to multiple rescatterings the obtained gluon distribution remains finite (up to logarithms of k) in the soft transverse momentum limit of k << Q_s. We compare the gluon spectrum in the single nuclear wave function before the collision to the spectrum of the produced gluons. The total number of produced gluons turns out to be proportional to the total number of gluons inside the nuclear wave function before the collision with the proportionality coefficient c = 2 ln 2.

# Classical Initial Conditions for Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

## Key claim

The paper provides an analytical calculation of the gluon distribution immediately after a heavy ion collision in the quasi-classical limit of QCD (McLerran-Venugopalan model), showing that the distribution remains finite in the soft transverse momentum limit due to multiple rescatterings, and that the total number of produced gluons is proportional to the total number of gluons in the nuclear wave function before the collision with a proportionality coefficient of approximately 1.39. The result is derived in the logarithmic approximation and is valid only for transverse momenta not much larger than the saturation scale.

## Method

The calculation resums all multiple rescatterings of gluons with nucleons in both colliding nuclei, corresponding to resumming powers of both Q_s1^2/k_perp^2 and Q_s2^2/k_perp^2. The author generalizes previous proton-nucleus results obtained in covariant gauge (Kovchegov and Mueller, 1998) to the nucleus-nucleus case using light cone perturbation theory in light cone gauge, where initial state interactions dominate. ## Key numbers

- The average transverse energy per produced gluon for two identical nuclei is <E_T> = 1.06 Q_s, where Q_s is the saturation scale.
- The total number of produced gluons per unit rapidity and impact parameter is dN^{AA}/(d^2 b dy) = (C_F Q_s^2 ln 2)/(alpha_s pi^2).
- The proportionality coefficient between the total number of produced gluons and the total number of gluons in a single nuclear wave function before the collision is c = 2 ln 2 ≈ 1.39.
- In the soft limit k_perp << Q_s, the gluon distribution approaches a constant value of C_F/(alpha_s 2 pi^3).
- In the ultraviolet limit k_perp/Q_s → infinity, the distribution falls off as 1/k_perp^4, scaling as Q_s1^2 Q_s2^2/(alpha_s k_perp^4).
- The transverse area of the nuclei used in the numerical plot is S_perp = 50 fm^2 ≈ 1250 GeV^{-2}.

## Context

This work extends the classical field approach to nucleus-nucleus collisions formulated by Kovchegov, McLerran, and Weigert (1999), who found the classical gluon field to order g^3 in perturbation theory. It generalizes the proton-nucleus gluon production cross section derived by Kovchegov and Mueller (1998) to the symmetric nucleus-nucleus case, confirming the conjecture by Mueller (2000) that the typical transverse energy of produced gluons is of order the saturation scale. The results provide initial conditions for possible thermalization of the gluonic system at later times in heavy ion collisions.
