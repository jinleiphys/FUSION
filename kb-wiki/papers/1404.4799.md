# Scalar strangeness content of the nucleon and baryon sigma terms

> The scalar strangeness content of the nucleon, characterized by the so-called strangeness-nucleon sigma term, is of fundamental importance in understanding its sea-quark flavor structure. We report a determination of the octet baryon sigma terms via the Feynman-Hellmann theorem by analyzing the latest high-statistics $n_f=2+1$ lattice QCD simulations with covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order. In particular, we predict $σ_{πN}=55(1)(4)$ MeV and $σ_{sN}=27(27)(4)$ MeV, while the first error is statistical and the second systematic due to different lattice scales. The predicted $σ_{sN}$ is consistent with the latest LQCD results and the results based on the next-to-next-to-leading order chiral perturbation theory. Several key factors in determining the sigma terms are systematically taken into account and clarified for the first time, including the effects of lattice scale setting, systematic uncertainties originating from chiral expansion truncations, and constraint of strong-interaction isospin breaking effects.

## Key claim

The authors determine the octet baryon sigma terms using covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) by analyzing high-statistics nf=2+1 lattice QCD simulations, finding that the predicted strangeness-nucleon sigma term is consistent with the latest LQCD results and NNLO chiral perturbation theory, but that the systematic uncertainties from lattice scale setting are small, in contrast with previous studies.

## Method

The authors apply the Feynman-Hellmann theorem to the quark mass dependence of octet baryon masses computed from lattice QCD simulations by the PACS-CS, LHPC, and QCDSF-UKQCD collaborations, using covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory up to N3LO with 19 unknown low-energy constants. They fit the LQCD data with three different lattice scale setting methods (mass independent with fixed lattice spacings, mass independent with self-consistently determined lattice spacings, and mass dependent scale setting) and further constrain the low-energy constants using LQCD results on strong isospin-splitting effects on octet baryon masses.

## Key numbers

* Pion-nucleon sigma term: σπN = 55(1)(4) MeV, where the first error is statistical and the second systematic due to different lattice scales
* Strangeness-nucleon sigma term: σsN = 27(27)(4) MeV, with the same error conventions
* Pion-lambda sigma term: σπΛ = 32(1)(2) MeV
* Pion-sigma sigma term: σπΣ = 34(1)(3) MeV
* Pion-xi sigma term: σπΞ = 16(1)(2) MeV
* Strangeness-lambda sigma term: σsΛ = 185(24)(17) MeV
* Strangeness-sigma sigma term: σsΣ = 210(26)(42) MeV
* Strangeness-xi sigma term: σsΞ = 333(25)(13) MeV
* χ2/d.o.f. for the mass independent scale setting with fixed lattice spacings: 0.87
* χ2/d.o.f. for the mass dependent scale setting: 0.53

## Context

This work extends previous studies of baryon sigma terms using covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (Ren 2012, Ren 2013) by including the QCDSF-UKQCD data that provide constraints on the strange-quark mass dependence, and by systematically studying the effects of lattice scale setting methods that had been shown to change the strangeness-nucleon sigma term by a factor of two to three in finite-range regularization BChPT (Shanahan 2012, Shanahan 2013). The predicted σπN = 55(1)(4) MeV is in reasonable agreement with the πN scattering study (Alarcon 2011) giving 59(7) MeV and with the systematic LQCD study (Alvarez-Ruso 2013) giving 52(3)(8) MeV, but larger than the N3LO partial summation approach (Lutz 2014) giving 39+2-1 MeV. The results enable improved predictions for dark matter detection cross sections and for Higgs boson searches at the LHC.
