# Nuclear matter in relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory with Bonn potential in the full Dirac space

> Starting from the Bonn potential, relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) equations are solved for nuclear matter in the full Dirac space, which provides a unique way to determine the single-particle potentials and avoids the approximations applied in the RBHF calculations in the Dirac space with positive-energy states (PESs) only. The uncertainties of the RBHF calculations in the Dirac space with PESs only are investigated, and the importance of the RBHF calculations in the full Dirac space is demonstrated. In the RBHF calculations in the full Dirac space, the empirical saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter are reproduced, and the obtained equation of state agrees with the results based on the relativistic Green's function approach up to the saturation density.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that solving relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock equations in the full Dirac space (including both positive-energy and negative-energy states) provides a unique determination of single-particle potentials, thereby removing the uncertainties inherent in calculations restricted to positive-energy states only, and reproduces empirical saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors solved the in-medium relativistic Thompson equation for the G matrix using the Bonn A potential, including both positive-energy and negative-energy states in the initial and final states while excluding negative-energy states in the intermediate propagator. The single-particle potentials (scalar, timelike vector, and spacelike vector components) were extracted uniquely from three matrix elements of the potential operator (Σ⁺⁺, Σ⁻⁺, Σ⁻⁻) calculated from the G matrix, and the coupled set of equations was iterated to self-consistency. The matrix elements of the bare NN interaction were treated in the center-of-mass frame with an approximation that neglects Lorentz invariance violations from retardation effects.

## Key numbers

- Saturation binding energy per nucleon for symmetric nuclear matter with Bonn A: the result is within the empirical range of -16 ± 1 MeV (the paper states the empirical saturation properties are "reproduced" but does not give a single numerical value for the binding energy in the abstract or introduction)
- Non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculation with Bonn A gives saturation energy -23.55 MeV and saturation Fermi momentum k_F = 1.85 fm⁻¹ (from the comparison in Section IV)
- The approximation for Lorentz transformation of matrix elements (Eq. 4) introduces a deviation in binding energy per nucleon smaller than 0.2 MeV in the density region k_F = 0.6 to 1.6 fm⁻¹ (from the RHF test in Section III)
- Numerical precision of the binding energy per nucleon is less than 0.2 MeV (from Section III)
- Cutoff of total angular momentum in partial-wave decomposition: 10 ħ (from Section III)

## Context

This work extends the pioneering full-Dirac-space RBHF calculations of the Brooklyn group (Anastasio et al., 1980-1983) and the later work of Poschenrieder (1988) and Huber, Weber, and Weigel (1995), but uses the Thompson equation (rather than the Brueckner propagator) and achieves full self-consistency. It directly addresses the discrepancy between the results of Huber et al. (1995), which gave good saturation properties, and Katayama et al. (2015), which found an underestimation of binding energy by about 5 MeV at saturation for Bonn A. The method enables future fully self-consistent RBHF calculations for finite nuclei and neutron drops without the approximations required by positive-energy-state-only approaches.
