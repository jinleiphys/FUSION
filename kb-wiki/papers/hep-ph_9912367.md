# Large Scale Rapidity Correlations in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We discuss particle production mechanisms for heavy ion collisions. We present an argument demonstrating how the fluctuations of the number of produced particles in a series of classical emissions can account for KNO scaling. We predict rapidity correlations in the particle production in the event by event analysis of heavy ion collisions on the rapidity scales of the order of one over the strong coupling constant.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the fluctuations in the number of produced particles from a series of classical emissions can account for KNO scaling in heavy ion collisions, but they note that this scaling will slowly break down at very high energies because the running coupling constant becomes smaller, making the relative width of the multiplicity distribution larger.

## Method

The authors develop a stochastic evolution equation for the total particle multiplicity as a function of rapidity, modeling the BFKL evolution kernel as a deterministic growth term plus a Gaussian stochastic term representing fluctuations induced at each stage of the renormalization group evolution. They solve this equation to derive the multiplicity distribution function and compute the two-particle distribution function from classical field theory, then define a correlation coefficient to quantify rapidity correlations on an event-by-event basis.

## Key numbers

- The predicted multiplicity per unit rapidity in a given event is of order \(dN/dy \sim 10^3\), with statistical fluctuations \(\sigma/(dN/dy) \sim 10^{-1} - 10^{-2}\).
- The saturation momentum scale \(\Lambda^2 = (1/\pi R^2)(dN/dy)\) is argued to be large compared to \(\Lambda^2_{QCD}\), with \(\alpha_s(\Lambda) \ll 1\).
- The saturation regime occupation number is \( (1/\pi R^2)(dN/d^2p_T dy) \sim 1/\alpha_s\).
- The relative width of the KNO multiplicity distribution scales as \(\delta N^2 / \overline{N}^2(y) \sim 1/\alpha_s^2\) parametrically, and more precisely \(\delta N^2 / \overline{N}^2(y) \sim 1/(\alpha_s(\Lambda) \alpha_s(\Lambda_{target}))\).
- The width of the multiplicity distribution scales as \(\delta N \sim \overline{N}(E)/\sqrt{A^\delta}\), where \(\delta = 2/3, 1,\) or \(4/3\) depending on the saturation model.
- The correlation coefficient \(\mathcal{C}(b; y_1, y_2)\) is predicted to be 1 for rapidity separations \(|y_1 - y_2| \lesssim 1/\alpha_s\) and smaller for wider intervals.
- The two-particle distribution function factorizes as \(D(y_1,y_2) = \langle dN/dy_1 \rangle \langle dN/dy_2 \rangle\) up to corrections of order \(A^{-2/3}\).

## Context

This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan model (1994) of classical gluon fields in heavy ion collisions by incorporating the renormalization group approach developed by Jalilian-Marian, Kovchegov, McLerran, and Weigert (1997-1999) to account for a series of classical emissions. The authors connect their results to the BFKL equation (Balitsky, Fadin, Kuraev, Lipatov, 1975-1978) and to KNO scaling originally observed in hadron-hadron collisions by Koba, Nielsen, and Olesen (1972). The predictions for rapidity correlations and the correlation coefficient \(\mathcal{C}\) are intended to be testable at RHIC and LHC experiments.
