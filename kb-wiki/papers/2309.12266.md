# Heavy $Ξ_{c/b}$ and $Ξ'_{c/b}$ baryons in the quark model

> We present a study of the low-lying $Ξ_{c/b}$ and $Ξ'_{c/b}$ baryon states based on masses, strong and radiative decays. In a harmonic oscillator quark model analysis it is found that the majority of the detected states can be interpreted as $S$- and $P$-wave excitations.

## Key claim

In a harmonic oscillator quark model analysis, the majority of the detected low-lying Ξc/b and Ξ'c/b baryon states can be interpreted as S- and P-wave excitations, with the explicit caveat that the assignment of quantum numbers is based on a combination of energy systematics, strong decay widths, and electromagnetic decay widths, not on direct experimental determination.

## Method

The authors employed a harmonic oscillator quark model with a Hamiltonian that includes spin, spin-orbit, isospin, and flavor SUf(3) interactions, expressed in Jacobi coordinates for the qqQ three-quark system. They calculated masses for ground-state configurations with (nρ,nλ)=(0,0) and for states with one quantum of excitation in either the λ mode (0,1) or the ρ mode (1,0). Strong decay widths were computed using two models: the elementary emission model (EEM) and the 3P0 model, and radiative decay widths were calculated for S- and P-wave single-charm and single-bottom cascade baryons.

## Key numbers

- The three newly observed negative parity Ξ0c states (Ξ0c(2923), Ξ0c(2939), and Ξ0c(2965)) follow a mass rule of roughly 126 MeV with respect to the excited negative parity Ω0c states.
- For the Ξc(2645)+ state, the calculated strong decay width is 1.23 MeV (EEM) and 0.02 MeV (3P0 model), compared to the experimental value of 2.14 ± 0.19 MeV.
- For the Ξc(2939)0 state (assigned as 2λ(Ξ'c)3/2-), the calculated strong decay width is 11.53 MeV (EEM) and 3.08 MeV (3P0 model), compared to the experimental value of 10.2 ± 1.4 MeV.
- For the Ξc(2965)0 state (assigned as 4λ(Ξ'c)3/2-), the calculated strong decay width is 3.88 MeV (EEM) and 2.04 MeV (3P0 model), compared to the experimental value of 14.1 ± 1.6 MeV.
- The radiative decay width of Ξc(2790)0 → 2Ξc0 + γ is calculated as 239.3 keV, compared to the experimental value of 800 ± 320 keV.
- The radiative decay width of Ξc(2815)0 → 2Ξc0 + γ is calculated as 344.6 keV, compared to the experimental value of 320 ± 45+45-80 keV.
- For the Ξb(6227)0 state (assigned as 4λ(Ξ'b)5/2-), the calculated strong decay width is 13.80 MeV (EEM) and 3.36 MeV (3P0 model), compared to the experimental value of 18.6+5.2-4.3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the quark model analysis of singly-heavy baryons previously applied to other systems by Santopinto (2019) and Ortiz-Pacheco (2020), and provides a systematic classification of the Ξc/b and Ξ'c/b states within the harmonic oscillator framework. The results are compared with the chiral quark model calculations of Wang (2017) and with experimental data from the LHCb collaboration and the Particle Data Group compilation (2020). The study enables future experimental searches for predicted states, particularly the ρ-mode excitations and the bottom-sector counterparts of the observed charmed states, and provides a basis for testing the SUf(3) multiplet assignment suggested by the equal spacing mass rule for the three new negative parity Ξ0c states.
