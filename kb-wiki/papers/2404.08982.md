# Correlation between the charge radii difference in mirror partner nuclei and the symmetry energy slope

> A correlation between the charge radii difference of mirror partner nuclei $Δ{R_{\mathrm{ch}}}$ and the slope parameter $L$ of symmetry energy has been built to ascertain the equation of state of isospin asymmetric nuclear matter. In this work, the influences of pairing correlations and isoscalar compression modulus on the $Δ{R_{\mathrm{ch}}}$ are systematically investigated based on the Skyrme energy density functional theory. The calculated results suggest that the linear correlation between $Δ{R_{\mathrm{ch}}}$ and $L$ is decreased by the surface pairing correlations. The slope parameter deduced from the difference of charge radii of mirror-pair nuclei $^{32}$Ar-$^{32}$Si, $^{36}$Ca-$^{36}$S, $^{38}$Ca-$^{38}$Ar, and $^{54}$Ni-$^{54}$Fe falls into the range of $L$=42.57-50.64 MeV, that is, the rather soft equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter. Besides, the range of the slope parameter can also be influenced by the effective forces classified by various isoscalar incompressibility coefficients.

# Correlation between the charge radii difference in mirror partner nuclei and the symmetry energy slope

## Key claim
The linear correlation between the charge radii difference of mirror partner nuclei and the symmetry energy slope parameter L is decreased by surface pairing correlations, and the extracted slope parameter L = 42.57–50.64 MeV represents a rather soft equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter, but this range is influenced by the choice of pairing force and by the isoscalar incompressibility coefficient of the effective Skyrme forces used.

## Method
The authors performed spherical Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with density-dependent zero-range pairing forces of volume, mixed, and surface types, using 21 Skyrme parameter sets grouped into three families with isoscalar incompressibility coefficients K ≈ 230, 240, and 250 MeV. They computed charge radii for the mirror pairs ³²Ar-³²Si, ³⁶Ca-³⁶S, ³⁸Ca-³⁸Ar, and ⁵⁴Ni-⁵⁴Fe, then extracted the slope parameter L by comparing calculated ΔRch with experimental data and evaluating Pearson correlation coefficients.

## Key numbers
- The extracted slope parameter L from the four mirror pairs falls into the range L = 42.57–50.64 MeV under mixed-type pairing (η = 0.5).
- For ⁵⁴Ni-⁵⁴Fe, the Pearson coefficient R between ΔRch and L is 0.993 without pairing, but drops to 0.882 with surface-type pairing (η = 1.0).
- For ³²Ar-³²Si, ³⁶Ca-³⁶S, and ³⁸Ca-³⁸Ar with surface-type pairing, Pearson coefficients are R = 0.887, 0.791, and 0.743 respectively.
- The upper limit of L from ³²Ar-³²Si alone is 96.65 MeV, which the authors note is overestimated compared to the L ≤ 60 MeV constraint from König et al. (2023).
- The deviation in L caused by varying K from ≈230 to ≈250 MeV is approximately 38.0 MeV for the ³⁶Ca-³⁶S and ³⁸Ca-³⁸Ar pairs.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Brown et al. (2020), Pineda et al. (2021), and König et al. (2023), who proposed using charge radii differences of mirror nuclei to constrain the symmetry energy slope L. It systematically investigates two previously under-explored influences: pairing correlations (volume, mixed, and surface types) and the isoscalar incompressibility coefficient K of the Skyrme forces. The results enable more precise constraints on the nuclear equation of state by identifying which mirror pairs and which pairing treatments yield the most reliable L values, and by showing that the incompressibility must be accounted for in future calibrations.
