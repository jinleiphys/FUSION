# An Effective Equation of State for Dense Matter with Strangeness

> An effective equation of state (EoS) which generalizes the Lattimer-Swesty equation for nuclear matter is presented for matter at supernuclear densities including strange baryons. It contains an adjustable baryon potential energy density, based on models of local potentials for the baryon-baryon interactions. The features of the equation rely on the properties of nuclei for the nucleon-nucleon interactions, and mainly on experimental data from hypernuclei for the hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions. The EoS is used to calculate equilibrium compositions and thermodynamic properties of high density matter with strangeness in two astrophysical contexts: neutron star matter (transparent to neutrinos) and proto-neutron star matter (opaque to neutrinos). The effective EoS reproduces typical properties of high density matter found in theoretical microscopic models. Of these, the main result is that hyperons appear in both types of matter at about twice the nuclear saturation density, and that their appearance significantly softens the EoS. The range of maximal masses of neutron stars found in a comprehensive parameter survey is 1.4-1.7 $M_\odot$. Another typical result is that the maximal mass of a proto-neutron star with strange baryons is higher than that of an evolved neutron star (opposite to the case of nuclear matter), setting the stage for a "delayed collapse" scenario.

## Key claim

The paper presents an effective equation of state (EoS) that generalizes the Lattimer-Swesty equation to include strange baryons (Λ, Σ, Ξ) at supernuclear densities, but the results depend strongly on the choice of parameters for the baryon-baryon interactions, particularly the exponent γ of the repulsion term, which cannot be uniquely determined from available experimental data.

## Method

The authors construct an analytic, parameterized EoS by modeling the baryon potential energy density using local density-dependent potentials for nucleon-nucleon, hyperon-nucleon, and hyperon-hyperon interactions, with parameters calibrated to nuclear properties at saturation density and hypernuclear data. Equilibrium compositions are calculated by solving chemical equilibrium conditions (beta-equilibrium, charge neutrality, baryon number conservation) for two astrophysical scenarios: neutrino-transparent neutron star matter and neutrino-opaque proto-neutron star matter. The EoS is then used to compute thermodynamic properties and neutron star masses across a parameter survey varying the repulsion exponents and interaction strengths.

## Key numbers

- Hyperons appear in both neutron star matter and proto-neutron star matter at about twice the nuclear saturation density (ρ_s = 0.155 fm⁻³)
- The range of maximal masses of neutron stars found in the parameter survey is 1.4–1.7 M_⊙
- The Λ hyperon well depth in nuclear matter at saturation density is −27 to −28 MeV
- The Ξ hyperon well depth in nuclear matter at saturation density is −20 to −25 MeV
- The cross-over density where the Λ potential becomes zero varies from about 2ρ_s for γ=2 to about 3.5ρ_s for γ=4/3
- The nuclear saturation density is taken as ρ_s = 0.155 fm⁻³ (following the Skyrme I' force)

## Context

This work extends the Lattimer-Swesty (1991) effective EoS for nuclear matter to include strange baryons, building on the local potential approach of Millener, Dover and Gal (1988) for Λ hypernuclei. The EoS is designed for rapid use in hydrodynamical simulations and parameter surveys, complementing more computationally expensive microscopic models such as relativistic mean field and Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations. The finding that proto-neutron star maximal masses exceed those of evolved neutron stars (opposite to the purely nuclear case) enables a "delayed collapse" scenario for neutron star formation.
