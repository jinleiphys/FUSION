# Exact Analytical Solution of the Constrained Statistical Multifragmentation Model

> A novel powerful mathematical method is presented, which allows us to find an analytical solution of a simplified version of the statistical multifragmentation model with the restriction that the largest fragment size cannot exceed the finite volume of the system. A complete analysis of the isobaric partition singularities is done for finite system volumes. The finite size effects for large fragments and the role of metastable (unstable) states are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents an exact analytical solution for a constrained statistical multifragmentation model (CSMM) in which the largest fragment size is explicitly limited by the finite system volume, extending prior work that was restricted to the thermodynamic limit. The solution is achieved via a novel Laplace-Fourier transformation method that decouples the volume dependence, but the analysis is limited to a simplified version of the SMM that neglects symmetry and Coulomb contributions.

## Method

The author introduces a Fourier representation of the Dirac delta function to decouple the additional volume dependence in the grand canonical partition function of the constrained SMM, then applies a Laplace transformation to obtain the isobaric partition. The singularities of the isobaric partition are analyzed for finite volumes by solving the transcendental equation lambda*_n = F(V, lambda*_n), where the function F depends on the maximum fragment size K_up = alpha V/b. The Euler-MacLaurin formula is used to study finite volume effects on the thermodynamic functions.

## Key numbers

- Bulk binding energy per nucleon W_o = 16 MeV, with excited state contribution T^2/epsilon_o where epsilon_o = 16 MeV
- Surface tension parameterization: sigma_o = 18 MeV, T_c = 18 MeV (sigma = 0 at T >= T_c)
- Normal nuclear density rho_o = 0.16 fm^{-3}, giving excluded volume parameter b = 1/rho_o
- Fisher exponent tau = 1.825 used in the graphical analysis at T = 10 MeV
- The liquid phase singularity gives pressure p_l(T,mu) = (mu + W(T))/b

## Context

This work extends the exact analytical solution of the statistical multifragmentation model found by Bugaev (2000, 2001), which was limited to infinite system volumes, to finite volumes by explicitly constraining the largest fragment size relative to the system volume. The method enables study of finite size effects for large fragments and the role of metastable and unstable states, which could not be addressed within the mean-field approach or numerically. The results may allow more confident recovery of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition diagram from experiments on finite nuclei, and may relate to spinodal instabilities discussed by Randrup (2004).
