# Asymptotic behavior of Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions for multi-particles in quantum field theories

> We derive asymptotic behaviors of the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave function at large space separations for systems with more than 2 particles in quantum field theories. To deal with $n$-particles in the center of mass flame coherently, we introduce the Jacob coordinates of $n$ particles and then combine their $3(n-1)$ coordinates into the one spherical coordinate in $D=3(n-1)$ dimensions. We parametrize on-shell $T$-matrix for $n$-particle system of scalar fields at low energy, using the unitarity constraint of the $S$-matrix. We then express asymptotic behaviors of the NBS wave function for $n$ particles at low energy, in terms of parameters of $T$-matrix, and show that the NBS wave function carry the information of $T$-matrix such as phase shifts and mixing angles of the $n$-particle system in its own asymptotic behavior, so that the NBS wave function can be considered as the scattering wave of $n$-particles in quantum mechanics. This property is one of the essential ingredients of the HAL QCD scheme to define "potential" from the NBS wave function in quantum field theories such as QCD. Our results, together with an extension to systems with spin 1/2 particles, justify the HAL QCD's definition of potentials for 3 or more nucleons(baryons) in terms the NBS wave functions.

## Key claim

The paper derives the asymptotic behavior of Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions for systems of three or more scalar particles at large spatial separations, showing that these wave functions carry the information of the on-shell T-matrix (phase shifts and mixing angles) and can therefore be interpreted as scattering waves in quantum mechanics. This result is derived under the non-relativistic approximation for the energy and assumes no bound states exist in the two-or-more-particle systems.

## Method

The authors introduce modified Jacobi coordinates for n particles in the center-of-mass frame and combine the 3(n-1) coordinates into a single spherical coordinate in D = 3(n-1) dimensions. They parametrize the on-shell T-matrix for n scalar particles at low energy by solving the unitarity constraint of the S-matrix using hyperspherical harmonic functions in D dimensions. Using the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, they then compute the NBS wave function and evaluate its asymptotic form at large separations by performing the momentum integrals with the Bessel function integration formula.

## Key numbers

* For n=2: the T-matrix is parametrized as T_l(q) = - (4 × 2^(3/2))/(q E) e^(i δ_l(E)) sin δ_l(E), where δ_l(E) is the phase shift for partial wave l at energy E = 2√(m² + q²/2)
* For general n: the diagonalized T-matrix element is T_[L](Q) = - (2 n^(3/2))/(m Q^(3n-5)) e^(i δ_[L](Q)) sin δ_[L](Q), where Q is the hyper-radius in D = 3(n-1) dimensional momentum space and δ_[L](Q) is the generalized phase shift
* The asymptotic NBS wave function for n particles at large hyper-radius R behaves as Ψ^n ∝ (1/R^((D-1)/2)) sin(Q_A R - Δ_L + δ_[N](Q_A)), where D = 3(n-1) and Δ_L = (2L_D - 1)π/4 for odd n cases

## Context

This work provides the theoretical foundation for the HAL QCD method's definition of potentials for three or more nucleons (baryons) in terms of NBS wave functions, extending previous derivations that existed only for two-particle systems (Lin 2001, Aoki 2005, Ishizuka 2009). The results justify the extraction of three-nucleon forces (3NF) by the HAL QCD method as previously attempted by Doi (2010, 2011), and together with an extension to spin-1/2 particles, enable the study of hadronic interactions above inelastic thresholds where particle production such as NN → NNπ can occur.
