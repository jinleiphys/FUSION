# Probing DDM and ML quantum concepts in shape phase transitions of $γ$-unstable nuclei

> In a recent paper (S. Ait El Korchi et al. 2020 EPL 132 52001), we explored, inside the context of Critical Point Symmetries (CPSs) X(3) and Z(4), a correlation between two exceedingly known quantum concepts, the Minimal Length (ML) and the Deformation-Dependent Mass (DDM), that are commonly applied in various areas of physics. Such a correlation has been strongly identified in transition nuclei by calculating some physical observables of that quantum system, like as energy spectra, moments of inertia and transition probabilities. In this paper we extend that study to E(5) dynamical symmetry corresponding to the shape phase transition U(5)$\leftrightarrow$O(6). The experimental realization of the models was found to occur in some nuclei, using the existing phenomenological potentials : Infinite Square Well, Davidson and Kratzer, whose models fits provide the best agreement. Importantly the calculations performed in this work using these potentials corroborate the fact that the revealed correlation between both quantum concepts is not destructively affected by the presence of other model parameters and hence its existence is independent of the form or type of the used potential. Undoubtedly, the present work will open the way for more investigations of this correlation in the limits of other critical points symmetries in nuclear shape phase transitions which play today a major role in nuclear structure research from theoretical as well as experimental point of view.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previously discovered correlation between Deformation-Dependent Mass (DDM) and Minimal Length (ML) formalisms from the X(3) and Z(4) critical point symmetries to the E(5) dynamical symmetry, and claims that this correlation persists regardless of which phenomenological potential (Infinite Square Well, Davidson, or Kratzer) is used, meaning the correlation is independent of the potential form.

## Method

The authors solve the Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian for gamma-unstable nuclei in the E(5) critical point symmetry using three phenomenological potentials (Infinite Square Well, Davidson, and Kratzer) under both the Deformation-Dependent Mass (DDM) formalism and the Minimal Length (ML) formalism. For the DDM case, the asymptotic iteration method (AIM) is used to obtain energy spectra and wave functions; for the ML case, a combination of AIM and quantum perturbation theory (QPM) is employed. The resulting theoretical predictions for energy spectra, B(E2) transition probabilities, and moments of inertia are compared with experimental data for selected nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For the E(5)-DDM with Infinite Square Well potential, the energy spectrum is given by \(E_{s,L} = \frac{\hbar^2}{2B_0}\bar{k}_{s,\tilde{\eta}}^2\) with \(\bar{k}_{s,\tilde{\eta}} = \frac{(1+a)}{\beta_{\omega}^{(1+a)}}\chi_{s,\tilde{\eta}}\), where \(\chi_{s,\tilde{\eta}}\) is the s-th zero of the Bessel function of the first kind.
- For the E(5)-DDM with Davidson potential, the energy spectrum formula is \(E_{n,\tau}=\frac{\hbar^2}{2B_m}[k_0+\frac{a}{2}(3+2\sigma_{1}+2\sigma_{2}+\sigma_{1}\sigma_{2})+2a(2+\sigma_{1}+\sigma_{2})n+4an^2]\).
- For the E(5)-DDM with Kratzer potential, the energy spectrum is \(E_{n,\tau} =-\frac{\hbar^2}{2B_m \left(n+\frac{1}{2} + \sqrt{ \left(\tau+\frac{3}{2} \right)^2+2\tilde B} \right)^2}\).
- For the E(5)-ML with Infinite Square Well potential, the energy spectrum is \(E_{s,L}=\frac{\hbar^2}{2B_{m}}\frac{\left(\frac{\chi_{s,\eta}}{\beta_{w}}\right)^{2}}{1-2\alpha \left(\frac{\chi_{s,\eta}}{\beta_{w}}\right)^{2}}\).
- For the E(5)-ML with Davidson potential, the unperturbed energy is \(E^{0}_{n,L}=\sqrt{\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2B_{m}}}\rho(4\mu+2+4n)\) with \(\mu=\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{2}\sqrt{4\Lambda+8\beta_{0}^{2}+9}\) and \(\rho=\sqrt{\frac{a}{2}}\).
- For the E(5)-ML with Kratzer potential, the unperturbed energy is \(E^{0}_{n,\tau}=\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2B_{m}}\left[\frac{1}{2(n+\mu)}\right]^{2}\) with \(\mu=\frac{1}{2}+\sqrt{\tilde{B}+\left(\tau+\frac{3}{2}\right)^{2}}\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Korchi et al. (2020) which found a correlation between DDM and ML formalisms in the X(3) and Z(4) critical point symmetries. The present paper applies the same investigation to the E(5) dynamical symmetry, which describes the shape phase transition from spherical U(5) to gamma-unstable O(6) shapes. The results enable future investigations of this DDM-ML correlation in other critical point symmetries of nuclear shape phase transitions, potentially providing a unified framework for understanding these quantum concepts across different nuclear structure regimes.
