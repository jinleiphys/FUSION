# Critical temperature of deconfinement in a constrained space using a bag model at vanishing baryon density

> The geometry of fireballs in relativistic heavy ion collisions is approximated by a static box, which is infinite in two directions while finite in the other direction. The critical temperature of deconfinement phase transition is calculated explicitly in the MIT bag model at vanishing baryon density. It is found that the critical temperature shifts to a value higher than that in an unconstrained space.

## Key claim

The critical temperature of the deconfinement phase transition in the MIT bag model shifts to a higher value when the system is constrained in one spatial direction (as a simplified model of a heavy-ion collision fireball), compared to the unconstrained case; the shift is driven by the suppression of the zero-momentum mode under Dirichlet boundary conditions, which reduces the grand potential density more strongly for the light partons in the QGP phase than for the hadron gas.

## Method

The authors model the fireball as a static box infinite in two transverse directions but with a finite thickness \(L\) in the longitudinal direction, imposing Dirichlet boundary conditions on the particle wave functions. They compute the grand partition function for both the QGP phase (massless gluons, massless \(u/d\) quarks, and a 150 MeV \(s\) quark) and the hadron gas phase (all hadrons below 2 GeV) using the density of states modified by the finite-length constraint. The critical temperature is found by solving the mechanical equilibrium condition \(\omega_{\text{QGP}} = \omega_{\text{HG}}\) (grand potential density equality) as a function of \(L\), with the bag constant fixed at \(B = (236\ \text{MeV})^4\).

## Key numbers

* In the unconstrained (\(L = +\infty\)) limit, the critical temperature is \(T_c = 165\ \text{MeV}\).
* At a box thickness of \(L = 4\ \text{fm}\), the critical temperature shifts to \(T_c = 179\ \text{MeV}\).
* For \(L = 8\ \text{fm}\), the shift of \(T_c\) is still above \(5\ \text{MeV}\) relative to the unconstrained value.
* A rough analytic estimate for the shift at large \(L\) is \(\Delta T_c \approx \frac{\pi}{16L}\); for \(L = 4\ \text{fm}\) this gives \(\Delta T_c \approx 10\ \text{MeV}\), qualitatively consistent with the full calculation.
* Under periodic boundary conditions at \(L = 4\ \text{fm}\), the critical temperature is \(T_c = 164.9\ \text{MeV}\), only \(0.1\ \text{MeV}\) below the unconstrained value.

## Context

This work extends finite-size studies of the QCD phase transition (e.g., He (1996), Kiriyama (2006), Palhares (2009)) by focusing on the specific geometry of a pancake-shaped fireball in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where the longitudinal size is much smaller than the transverse size at early times. The results provide a phenomenological estimate of how the deconfinement temperature is modified by the finite longitudinal extent of the system, and the analytic formula \(\Delta T_c \approx \pi/(16L)\) offers a simple benchmark for comparing with lattice QCD or other model calculations of finite-volume effects. The next step would be to include a more realistic expansion dynamics or to test the sensitivity to the bag constant and the hadron resonance spectrum.
