# Improved lattice operators for non-relativistic fermions

> In this work I apply a recently proposed improvement procedure, originally conceived to reduce finite lattice spacing effects in transfer matrices for dilute Fermi systems, to tuning operators for the calculation of observables. I construct, in particular, highly improved representations for the energy and the contact, as a first step in an improvement program for finite-temperature calculations. I illustrate the effects of improvement on those quantities with a ground-state lattice calculation at unitarity.

# Improved lattice operators for non-relativistic fermions

## Key claim

The paper extends the improvement procedure of Endres et al. (2011) from designing improved transfer matrices to also designing improved operators for observables, specifically the energy and the contact, in lattice Monte Carlo calculations of dilute Fermi systems at unitarity. The improvement is demonstrated for ground-state calculations, but the method is presented as a first step toward reducing finite lattice spacing effects in finite-temperature calculations, with the caveat that the operator improvement for the contact shows limited benefit beyond the lowest fitted eigenvalues.

## Method

The author applies the improvement procedure of Endres et al. (2011) by expanding the Hubbard-Stratonovich function A(p) in a set of momentum-dependent operators O_n(p) = [2 sin(p/2)]^{2n} and fitting the expansion coefficients C_n to match the two-body energy spectrum from Luscher's formula at unitarity. For observables, the author takes formal derivatives of the transfer matrix with respect to the imaginary time step tau (for the energy) and the inverse scattering length a^{-1} (for the contact), expands those derivatives in the same operator basis, and fits the new coefficients D_n and F_n to reproduce the exact two-body derivative spectra from Luscher's formula using the Feynman-Hellmann theorem.

## Key numbers

- For 80 particles in a 10^3 lattice volume at unitarity with the simple Hubbard model, k_F * r_eff = 0.54, showing the effective range is not negligible
- The coefficients C_n for the transfer matrix at N_O = 5: C_0 = 0.45853, C_1 = 0.03331, C_2 = 0.00718, C_3 = 0.00132, C_4 = 0.00129 (for N_x = 16, tau = 0.05 in lattice units)
- The coefficients D_n for the energy operator at N_O = 5: D_0 = -10.03874, D_1 = -0.74266, D_2 = -0.16064, D_3 = 0.02948, D_4 = -0.02878 (for N_x = 16, tau = 0.05 in lattice units)
- The coefficients F_n for the contact operator at N_O = 5: F_0 = 0.08205, F_1 = 0.01278, F_2 = 0.00406, F_3 = -0.00023, F_4 = 0.00180 (for N_x = 16, tau = 0.05 in lattice units)
- For the simple Hubbard model at unitarity, the effective range r_eff = 0.40l (where l is the lattice spacing)

## Context

This work extends the improvement method of Endres et al. (2011, 2012) for transfer matrices in non-relativistic fermion lattice calculations to the construction of improved operators for observables, following the spirit of the Symanzik improvement program from lattice QCD. The approach enables systematic reduction of lattice spacing effects beyond the simple Hubbard model approach used in earlier work by Bulgac, Drut, and Magierski (2006) and others, where only the scattering length could be tuned. The improved operators are intended for use in finite-temperature Monte Carlo calculations, where previous studies by Privitera et al. (2010) using dynamical mean-field theory and by Braun (2012) using functional renormalization group techniques have addressed related issues. The method leaves only finite-volume effects unaccounted for, eliminating the need for extrapolations to the dilute limit.
