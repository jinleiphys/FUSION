# Role of particle diffusion in shaping the gravitational wave signal from neutron star inspirals

> It is commonly believed that the dissipative properties of superdense matter play a negligible role in modeling gravitational waveforms from neutron star inspirals. This study aims to investigate whether this presumption holds true for the often neglected dissipative process associated with particle diffusion in superconducting neutron stars. As we demonstrate, diffusion effects can significantly impact the phase of the gravitational wave from the inspiral, manifesting at a magnitude of a few tens of milliradians at large orbit separations, equivalent to orbital frequencies of a few hertz. We also find that dissipation resulting from particle diffusion might increase the neutron star's temperature to approximately $10^7\rm K$ during the inspiral.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that particle diffusion in superconducting neutron stars can produce a gravitational wave phase shift of a few tens of milliradians at orbital frequencies of a few hertz, contrary to the common belief that dissipative properties of superdense matter are negligible for waveform modeling. This result is conditional on the assumption that the redshifted temperature is uniform (which likely overestimates the effect, since diffusion is less efficient at higher temperatures) and on the validity of the slow-diffusion approximation.

## Method
The authors solve the linearized perturbation equations for a non-rotating, strongly superconducting neutron star in a binary system, adopting the Cowling approximation and treating diffusion as a small correction. They expand the Lagrangian displacement into eigenmodes of the non-dissipative operator, use the asymptotic solution for large orbit separations, and compute the diffusion-driven energy dissipation rate from the chemical potential perturbations. The orbital evolution is then integrated in the Newtonian limit to obtain the accumulated gravitational wave phase shift.

## Key numbers
- Phase shift from diffusion: a few tens of milliradians at orbital frequencies of a few hertz (e.g., at approximately 2–5 Hz).
- Neutron star temperature increase during inspiral: approximately \(10^7\ \mathrm{K}\).
- Stellar masses considered: \(1.0\ M_\odot\) and \(1.4\ M_\odot\) for identical binary neutron stars.
- Equation of state used: BSk24.
- Diffusion coefficients taken from DGS20 (2020) for three-component \(ne\mu\)-matter, applied to superconducting four-component \(npe\mu\)-matter.

## Context
This work extends the study of dissipative effects in neutron star inspirals beyond shear viscosity (Lai 1994, Yagi & Yunes 2017, Alford & Wang 2019, Most 2022) and mutual friction (Kantor et al. 2022) to the previously neglected process of particle diffusion. It builds on the formalism for diffusion in superdense matter developed by Dommes et al. (2020), Dommes & Gusakov (2021), and Gusakov et al. (2023). The results suggest that diffusion may be the leading dissipative mechanism at large orbit separations (low orbital frequencies), and that its imprint on the gravitational wave phase could be detectable by next-generation detectors such as the Einstein Telescope, Cosmic Explorer, or LISA.
