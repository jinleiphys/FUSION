# A coherent study of $χ_{c0,2}\to VV$, $PP$ and $SS$

> We investigate the decays of $χ_{c0,2}$ into vector meson pairs ($VV$), pseudoscalar pairs ($PP$), and scalar pairs ($SS$) in a general factorization scheme. The purpose is to clarify the role played by the OZI-rule violations and SU(3) flavour breakings in the decay transitions, and their correlations with the final state meson wavefunctions. For $χ_{c0,2}\to VV$ and $PP$, we obtain an overall self-contained description of the experimental data. Applying this factorization to $χ_{c0}\to f_0^i f_0^j$, where $i, j=1, 2, 3$ denotes $f_0(1710)$, $f_0(1500)$ and $f_0(1370)$, respectively, we find that specific patterns will arise from the model predictions for the decay branching ratio magnitudes, and useful information about the structure of those three scalars can be abstracted.

## Key claim

The paper presents a factorization scheme for χc0,2 decays into vector, pseudoscalar, and scalar meson pairs that can describe existing experimental data, but the extracted OZI-rule violation parameter r carries large uncertainties (r = 0.45 ± 0.48 for χc0 and r = 0.24 ± 0.29 for χc2) due to the limited precision of current measurements, meaning the role of OZI violations remains poorly constrained.

## Method

The author distinguishes singly OZI-disconnected (SOZI) and doubly OZI-disconnected (DOZI) transition amplitudes, introducing parameters r (relative strength of DOZI to SOZI) and R (SU(3) flavour symmetry breaking factor, where R = 1 is the symmetry limit). For scalar meson decays, an additional parameter t accounts for direct glueball production via gg → GG. The transition amplitudes are factorized into products of coupling strengths, and a Gaussian form factor with β = 0.5 GeV accounts for spatial wavefunction effects. Experimental branching ratios from BES for χc0,2 → φφ, ωω, and K*K̅* (for VV) and for χc0,2 → ππ, KK̅, and ηη (for PP) are used to fit the parameters.

## Context

This work extends the glueball-QQ̅ mixing phenomenology developed by Close, Amsler, Kirk, and Zhao (2000s) for J/ψ decays into scalar mesons, and the intermediate meson exchange explanation of OZI-rule violations in J/ψ → Vf0 by Zhao and Zou (2004). The factorization scheme allows systematic study of correlations between OZI violations and SU(3) breaking across χc0,2 decay channels. The predicted pattern for χc0 → f0(1710)f0(1370) being the largest scalar-pair channel under large OZI violations provides a testable signature for glueball-QQ̅ mixing in the scalar mesons, which can be checked by BES data. The author notes that pQCD approaches (Anselmino, Chernyak, Cao-Huang-Wu) systematically underestimate the data, while a 3P0 quark pair creation model (Zhou-Ping-Zou) also shows nonperturbative contributions.
