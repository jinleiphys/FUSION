# Optical potentials, retarded Green's functions and nonorthogonality

> An approach is outline to constructing an optical potential that includes the effects of antisymmetry and target recoil. it is based on the retarded Green's function, which could make it a better starting point for applications to direct nuclear reactions, particularly when extended to coupled channels. Its form retains a simple connection to folding potentials, even in the presence of three-body forces.

## Key claim

The paper outlines an approach to constructing an optical potential that includes antisymmetry and target recoil, based on the retarded Green's function, where the resulting potential retains a simple connection to folding potentials even with three-body forces, unlike previous coupled-channel approaches by Cederbaum (2001) and Johnson (2019) which had different combinatoric factors that broke this connection.

## Method

The author starts from a set of off-diagonal retarded Green's functions for a projectile nucleon interacting with an A-body nucleus in various energy eigenstates, including recoil effects. A complete set of (A+1)-body states is inserted into the equations, yielding coupled-channel equations in an effective one-body space spanned by channels of relative motion. Feshbach's projection formalism is then applied to eliminate all but a small number of channels, producing an optical potential whose Born term has the form of the usual folding potential including contributions from three- and more-body forces, without requiring separate inversion of the nonorthogonal overlap matrix.

## Key numbers

- The two-body term in the potential operator V'_{kk'} from Cederbaum (2001) and Johnson (2019) is multiplied by 1/2, whereas the present approach uses the full folding term without this factor.
- The three-body term in V'_{kk'} from those prior approaches is multiplied by 1/12 instead of 1/4 as in the present work's Eq. (1b).
- The free propagator G_0 has the form 1/(ω - (A+1)/A ε(k) + iη), where A is the target mass number.

## Context

This work extends the retarded Green's function approach of Redish and Villars (1970) and Johnson (2017, 2019) to coupled channels, enabling treatment of direct reactions including inelastic scattering and charge exchange. It contrasts with approaches based on time-ordered Green's functions used by Rotureau (2020) and Whitehead (2018), which work well for heavier nuclei but neglect recoil effects important for light nuclei. The resulting Hermitian-analytic optical potential could serve as a starting point for practical reaction calculations, though it inherits a linear energy dependence from the nonorthogonal basis that Capuzzi and Mahaux (1995) and Cederbaum (2001) had previously deprecated.
