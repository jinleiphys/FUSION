# Compact star and compact star matter properties from a baryonic extended linear sigma model with explicit chiral symmetry breaking

> Based on a baryonic extended linear sigma model including explicit chiral symmetry breaking effect, the structure of neutron star with the emergence of hyperons is investigated using the relativistic mean field approximation. It's found that, except the lightest scalar meson $σ$ whose structure is not well understood so far, the vacuum mass spectra of relevant hadrons and NM properties around saturation density can be well reproduced. Nevertheless, we found that, to have a realistic mass-radius relation of neutron stars, the $πN$ sigma term $σ_{πN}$, which denotes the contribution of explicit symmetry breaking, should deviate from its empirical values at vacuum. Specifically, $σ_{πN}\sim -600$ MeV, rather than $(32-89) \rm \ MeV$ at vacuum. With an appropriate choice of $σ_{πN}$ and $K(n_0)$, our framework can give a more data favored mass-radius relation of neutron stars with the emergence of hyperons. The effect of explicit symmetry breaking at densities is hoped to provide a new insight to the relation between microscopic symmetries in medium and macroscopic phenomena.

## Key claim

To obtain a realistic mass-radius relation for neutron stars that satisfies the constraints from MSP J0740+6620 and GW170817, the pion-nucleon sigma term \(\sigma_{\pi N}\) must deviate from its empirical vacuum value of (32-89) MeV to approximately -600 MeV, or alternatively the incompressibility \(K(n_0)\) must deviate from its empirical range of (200-260) MeV to about 500 MeV, when using the baryonic extended linear sigma model with explicit chiral symmetry breaking under the relativistic mean field approximation.

## Method

The authors constructed a three-flavor baryonic extended linear sigma model (bELSM-\(\xi\)) that includes explicit chiral symmetry breaking at leading order by introducing a constant scalar background field \(\xi\) matched to quark mass terms in QCD. They applied the relativistic mean field (RMF) approximation to compute the equation of state, determined free parameters from vacuum hadron mass spectra and nuclear matter properties at saturation density \(n_0 \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations with hyperon emergence under beta equilibrium.

## Key numbers

* The empirical vacuum value of the pion-nucleon sigma term is \(\sigma_{\pi N} \sim (32-89)\) MeV, but the model requires \(\sigma_{\pi N} \sim -600\) MeV to produce a realistic mass-radius relation.
* The empirical incompressibility at saturation density is \(K(n_0) \approx (200-260)\) MeV, but the model alternatively requires \(K(n_0) \sim 500\) MeV.
* The saturation density is \(n_0 \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), and the model reproduces the binding energy \(E_0 = -16.00\) MeV and symmetry energy \(E_{\rm sym} = 30.00\) MeV at this density.
* The hyperon potential is set to \(U_{\Lambda} = -28\) MeV, and hyperons emerge at densities around \(2.5 n_0\) to \(3 n_0\) depending on the parameter set.
* The model reproduces the nucleon masses \(m_n = 939.565\) MeV and \(m_p = 938.272\) MeV, and the \(\Lambda\) hyperon mass \(m_{\Lambda} = 1115.58\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the baryonic extended linear sigma model (bELSM) previously developed by Ma, Ma, and Zhang (2023) to include explicit chiral symmetry breaking, enabling the study of hyperon emergence in neutron stars. It contrasts with Walecka-type models such as TM1-H1 and TM1-H0.6 (Glendenning, 1991) by incorporating chiral symmetry patterns of QCD with consistent power counting, and finds that the hyperon emergence density is higher than in those models due to the relation \(g_{\omega NN} \simeq g_{\omega \Lambda\Lambda}\). The results suggest that the behavior of explicit chiral symmetry breaking at high densities may provide new insight into the connection between microscopic symmetries in medium and macroscopic neutron star phenomena.
