# On Nonlinear Evolution of Axisymmetric Nuclear Surface

> We consider an uniformly charged incompressible nuclear fluid bounded by a closed surface. It is shown that an evolution of an axisymmetric surface $Γ(\bbox{r},t)\equiv σ- Σ(z,t) = 0,\quad \bbox{r}=(σ,ϕ,z)$ can be approximately reduced to a motion of a curve in the $(σ,z)$-plane. A nonlinear integro-diffrerential equation for the contour $Σ(z,t)$ is derived. It is pointed on a direct correspondence between $Σ(z,t)$ and a local curvature, that gives possibility to use methods of differential geometry to analyze an evolution of an axisymmetric nuclear surface.

# On Nonlinear Evolution of Axisymmetric Nuclear Surface

## Key claim

The paper shows that the evolution of an axisymmetric, uniformly charged, incompressible nuclear fluid bounded by a closed surface can be approximately reduced to the motion of a curve in the (sigma, z)-plane, leading to a nonlinear integro-differential equation for the contour. The authors state this as a "short report" presenting only the principal line, with an extended paper still in progress, meaning the derivation is formal and no explicit solutions or numerical tests are provided.

## Method

The authors start from the Vlasov kinetic equation for the one-body Wigner distribution function and truncate the moment hierarchy to obtain fluid-dynamical equations for density, collective velocity, and pressure tensors. They assume a uniformly charged, incompressible nuclear fluid with a sharp surface described by the contour Sigma(z,t) in cylindrical coordinates, replace the nuclear mean-field potential by a surface tension term with coefficient gamma_s related to the mass formula, and derive the boundary conditions involving the stress tensor and the principal curvatures. The final equation of motion for the contour is obtained by combining the continuity equation, Euler equations, and boundary conditions, and the authors note the direct correspondence between Sigma(z,t) and the local curvature, enabling the use of Frenet-Serret differential geometry.

## Key numbers

- Surface tension coefficient: b_s = 4 pi r_0^2 gamma_s approximately 22 MeV, from the mass formula.
- Nuclear matter density: n_0 = (2 p_F^3) / (3 pi hbar^3), where p_F is the Fermi momentum.
- Equilibrium spherical radius R_0 defined by 4 R_0^3 = 3 integral dz Sigma(z,t)^2.
- Equilibrium pressure tensor: P^0_ij = (2/5) epsilon_F n_0 delta_ij, where epsilon_F is the Fermi energy.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior studies of nonlinear nuclear hydrodynamics, specifically their reduction of 2D vortical motion to 1D boundary evolution (Kartavenko, 1993) and their treatment of localized vortices on a spherical nuclear surface (Kartavenko, Gridnev, Maruhn, and Greiner, 1996). The paper aims to generalize these approaches to fully three-dimensional axisymmetric surfaces, enabling the use of differential geometry methods (Frenet-Serret formalism) to analyze nuclear surface dynamics. The approach is related to the liquid-drop model of Rayleigh (1879) and the classical work on ellipsoidal figures of equilibrium by Chandrasekhar (1969), as well as contemporary applications of soliton concepts to nuclear hydrodynamics reviewed by Kartavenko (1993).
