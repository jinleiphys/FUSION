# Influence of density dependent symmetry energy on Elliptical flow

> The effect of density dependent symmetry energy on elliptical flow is studied using isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics model(IQMD). We have used the reduced isospin- dependent cross-section with hard(H) equation of state to study the sensitivity of elliptical flow towards symmetry energy in the energy range of 50 - 1000 MeV/nucleon. The elliptical flow becomes zero at a particular energy termed as transition energy. A systematic effort has been made to pin down the transition energy for the density dependent symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The density dependent symmetry energy influences elliptical flow in heavy-ion collisions, with the transition energy (where elliptical flow vanishes) showing sensitivity to the stiffness parameter \(\gamma\) that controls how symmetry energy varies with density; the effect is most pronounced at incident energies above 50 MeV/nucleon where binary collisions contribute alongside the mean field.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate collisions of \(Au^{179}_{97}+Au^{179}_{97}\), \(Sn^{124}_{50}+Sn^{124}_{50}\), and \(Xe^{124}_{54}+Xe^{124}_{54}\) at beam energies from 50 to 1000 MeV/nucleon. They employed a hard (H) equation of state with reduced isospin-dependent cross-sections (0.9 of \(\sigma_{NN}\)) and varied the density dependence of the symmetry energy using the parametrization \(E(\rho)=E(\rho_o)(\rho/\rho_o)^{\gamma}\) with \(\gamma\) values of 0.66, 1.33, and 2. The phase space was clusterized using the minimum spanning tree method with a 4 fm binding distance, and elliptical flow was analyzed for free nucleons, light charged particles (LCPs, \(2 \leq A \leq 4\)), and intermediate mass fragments (IMFs, \(5 \leq A \leq A_{tot}/6\)).

## Key numbers

- Transition energy for free nucleons and LCPs increases with \(\gamma\) up to a certain point then decreases for very stiff symmetry energy (\(\gamma = 2\)), while for IMFs the transition energy increases monotonically with \(\gamma\).
- At 100 MeV/nucleon, the elliptical flow shows clear sensitivity to the N/Z ratio: the neutron-rich system (\(Xe^{124}_{54}+Xe^{124}_{54}\)) exhibits weaker squeeze-out flow compared to \(Sn^{124}_{50}+Sn^{124}_{50}\) for LCPs.
- For \(\gamma = 0.66\), the density dependent symmetry energy produces larger squeeze-out flow than the constant symmetry energy (32 MeV), and this parametrization shows better agreement with experimental data from INDRA, FOPI, and PLASTIC BALL collaborations for \(Au+Au\) collisions at energies from 50 to 1000 MeV/nucleon.
- At incident energies of 800-1000 MeV/nucleon, the system reaches densities 2-3 times normal nuclear matter density (\(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)), where the density dependent symmetry energy effect becomes significant.

## Context

This work extends prior studies by S. Kumar et al. (2010) and others who established elliptical flow as a probe for symmetry energy but did not address its density dependence. The authors compare their results with experimental data from the INDRA, FOPI, and PLASTIC BALL collaborations, and with the constant symmetry energy assumption of 32 MeV at normal density. The study enables future work to constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy (parameterized by \(\gamma\)) using transition energy measurements, building on the range \(\gamma = 0.4-1.05\) suggested by Shetty et al. (2007) and Tsang et al. (2009).
