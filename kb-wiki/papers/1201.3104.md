# Comparison of Viscosities from the Chapman-Enskog and Relaxation Time Methods

> A quantitative comparison between the results of shear viscosities from the Chapman-Enskog and relaxation time methods is performed for selected test cases with specified elastic differential cross sections: (i) the non-relativistic, relativistic and ultra-relativistic hard sphere gas with angle and energy independent differential cross section, (ii) the Maxwell gas, (iii) chiral pions and (iv) massive pions. Our quantitative results reveal that the extent of agreement (or disagreement) depends very sensitively on the energy dependence of the differential cross sections employed.

# Comparison of Viscosities from the Chapman-Enskog and Relaxation Time Methods

## Key claim
The extent of agreement or disagreement between shear viscosities calculated using the Chapman-Enskog and relaxation time methods depends very sensitively on the energy dependence of the differential cross sections employed, not merely on the angular weighting difference between the two approaches.

## Method
The authors performed a quantitative comparison of shear viscosities from the Chapman-Enskog approximation (first order) and the relaxation time approximation for four test cases with specified elastic differential cross sections: (i) non-relativistic, relativistic, and ultra-relativistic hard sphere gas with angle- and energy-independent cross section, (ii) Maxwell gas, (iii) chiral pions, and (iv) massive pions with experimental cross sections. Analytical results were obtained where possible in the non-relativistic or extremely relativistic limits, and numerical calculations using the full relativistic formalism were performed for massive pions.

## Key numbers
- Non-relativistic hard sphere gas: Chapman-Enskog viscosity = 0.078 sqrt(m k_B T / pi) (1/a^2); relaxation time viscosity = 0.049 sqrt(m k_B T / pi) (1/a^2); ratio = 1.59
- Non-relativistic Maxwell gas: both methods give k_B T / (2 pi Gamma); ratio = 1.00
- Ultra-relativistic hard sphere gas: Chapman-Enskog viscosity = 1.2 (k_B T) / (pi a^2 c); relaxation time viscosity = (8/5) (k_B T) / (pi a^2 c); ratio = 1.33
- Ultra-relativistic chiral pions: Chapman-Enskog viscosity = (15 pi / 184) (f_pi^4 / T) (1 / hbar^2 c^3); relaxation time viscosity = (12 pi / 25) (f_pi^4 / T) (1 / hbar^2 c^3); ratio = 0.169

## Context
This work extends prior comparisons of transport coefficient methods by Kox (1976) for hard sphere gases and by Prakash, Prakash, Venugopalan, and Welke (1993) for chiral pions, and by Gavin (1985), Kapusta (2009), and Chakraborty and Kapusta (2010) for the relaxation time approach. The results call for checks using the more exact Green-Kubo calculations of shear viscosity and stress the need to combine all available experimental knowledge of differential cross sections for low mass hadrons to establish the temperature-dependent shear viscosity to entropy ratio for use in viscous hydrodynamics.
