# Equations of state for hot neutron stars -- II. The role of exotic particle degrees of freedom

> Explosive astrophysical systems - such as supernovae or compact star binary mergers - provide conditions where exotic degrees of freedom can be populated. Within the covariant density functional theory of nuclear matter we build several general purpose equations of state which, in addition to the baryonic octet, account for $Δ(1232)$ resonance states. The thermodynamic stability of $Δ$-admixed nuclear matter is investigated in the limiting case of vanishing temperature for charge fractions $Y_Q=0.01$ and $Y_Q=0.5$ and wide ranges of the coupling constants to the scalar and vector mesonic fields. General purpose equation of state models with exotica presently available on the \textsc{CompOSE} database are further reviewed; for a selection of them we then investigate thermal properties for thermodynamic conditions relevant for core-collapse supernovae and binary neutron star mergers. Modifications induced by hyperons, $Δ(1232)$, $K^-$, pions and quarks are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first publicly available general-purpose equations of state that include both the full baryonic octet and Delta(1232) resonances, but the thermodynamic stability of Delta-admixed nuclear matter is only guaranteed when the neutralizing electron gas is included; without it, one of the three models exhibits Delta-driven instabilities even at zero temperature.

## Method

The author builds three equation of state models within covariant density functional theory, incorporating the baryonic octet and Delta(1232) resonances with density-dependent meson couplings. The coupling constants for Deltas to scalar and vector mesonic fields are expressed as ratios relative to nucleon couplings, with values (1.1;1.1;1.0), (1.2;1.1;1.0), and (1.2;1.3;1.0). The paper also reviews 36 existing general-purpose equation of state tables with exotic degrees of freedom available on the CompOSE database, then compares thermal properties of selected models for thermodynamic conditions relevant to core-collapse supernovae and binary neutron star mergers.

## Key numbers

- Maximum masses of cold beta-equilibrated neutron stars for the three new R(DD2YDelta) models: 2.04, 2.05, and 2.03 solar masses respectively
- Radii of canonical 1.4 solar mass neutron stars for the new models: 12.97 km, 12.30 km, and 13.25 km respectively
- Combined tidal deformability for GW170817 (mass ratio q=0.73): 697, 470, and 786 for the three new models
- Well depth of Lambda hyperon in symmetric saturated nuclear matter: approximately -30 MeV, calibrated from experimental binding energies of single-Lambda hypernuclei
- Well depth of Xi hyperon: between -18.8 MeV and -14.6 MeV from calibration to single-Xi nuclei, consistent with -15 MeV from inclusive (K-, K+) spectra
- Well depth of Sigma hyperon: approximately 30 +/- 20 MeV from Sigma- atoms and inclusive (pi-, K+) spectra
- Well depth of K- meson at saturation density: -120 MeV used for MBB(DD2K) model
- Lambda-Lambda bond energy: revised to 0.67 +/- 0.17 MeV from the original 1.01 +/- 0.2 MeV

## Context

This work extends the author's previous paper (Raduta, 2021) on nucleonic equations of state and provides the first publicly available general-purpose tables including Delta resonances, filling a gap in the CompOSE database. The paper reviews and compares existing models with hyperons (Oertel, 2012; Hempel, 2010; Ishizuka, 2008), kaon condensates (Malik, 2021), pions (Ishizuka, 2008), and quark-hadron phase transitions (Sagert, 2009; Bastian, 2017; Dexheimer, 2017), enabling future numerical simulations of core-collapse supernovae and binary neutron star mergers to systematically compare the effects of different exotic degrees of freedom on gravitational wave signals, neutrino emissions, and remnant dynamics.
