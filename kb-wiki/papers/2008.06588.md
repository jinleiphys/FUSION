# Ab initio neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements for 48Ca, 76Ge, and 82Se

> We calculate basis-space converged neutrinoless $ββ$ decay nuclear matrix elements for the lightest candidates: 48Ca, 76Ge and 82Se. Starting from initial two- and three-nucleon forces, we apply the ab initio in-medium similarity renormalization group to construct valence-space Hamiltonians and consistently transformed $ββ$-decay operators. We find that the tensor component is non-negligible in 76Ge and 82Se, and resulting nuclear matrix elements are overall 25-45% smaller than those obtained from the phenomenological shell model. While a final matrix element with uncertainties still requires substantial developments, this work nevertheless opens a path toward a true first-principles calculation of neutrinoless $ββ$ decay in all nuclei relevant for ongoing large-scale searches.

## Key claim

The nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay in 48Ca, 76Ge, and 82Se, computed from chiral two- and three-nucleon forces via the ab initio valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group, are 25-45% smaller than those from the phenomenological shell model, but the authors state that a final matrix element with uncertainties still requires substantial developments before claims of a definitive value can be made.

## Method

The authors start from chiral effective field theory two-nucleon plus three-nucleon forces (the 1.8/2.0(EM) Hamiltonian) and apply the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) in the IMSRG(2) approximation to construct valence-space Hamiltonians and consistently transformed neutrinoless double-beta decay operators. They use a harmonic-oscillator basis with Hartree-Fock transformation, ensemble normal-ordering at the two-body level, and the Magnus formulation of the IMSRG, followed by valence-space diagonalization using the KSHELL shell-model code. The standard closure approximation is employed with closure energies of 7.72 MeV for 48Ca and 9.41 MeV for the heavier isotopes.

## Key numbers

* Total NME for 48Ca: 0.58(1) (dimensionless, multiplied by nuclear radius R=1.2 A^(1/3) fm), converged at e_max=14 and E_3max=20
* Total NME for 76Ge: 2.14(9) (dimensionless), converged at e_max=14 and E_3max=24
* Total NME for 82Se: 1.24(5) (dimensionless), converged at e_max=14 and E_3max=24
* Reduction compared to phenomenological shell model: 25% in 48Ca, 30% in 76Ge, and 45% in 82Se
* Tensor component accounts for 20% of the total NME in 48Ca, and is non-negligible in 76Ge and 82Se at approximately the same magnitude as the Fermi term
* Ground-state energies agree with experiment to better than 1% for A=48 systems and to 2% for A=76,82 systems

## Context

This work extends ab initio calculations of neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements beyond the lightest systems to three experimental candidates, using the VS-IMSRG approach previously developed by Stroberg (2019) and others. The results are consistent with complementary ab initio calculations using the in-medium generator coordinate method by Yao (2020) and coupled-cluster theory by Novario (2020) for 48Ca when starting from the same input forces. The paper opens a path toward first-principles calculations for the remaining major experimental candidates 130Te and 136Xe, though significant work remains to quantify uncertainties from neglected higher-order chiral contributions, the recently discovered leading-order short-range contact term, and three-body operator effects in the IMSRG(3) approximation.
