# Configuration and bandhead spin assignments for the 2-quasiparticle rotational bands in the neutron-rich nuclei $^{154, 156}$Pm

> The rotational bands in the neutron-rich nuclei $^{153-157}$Pm are investigated by a particle-number conserving method. The kinematic moments of inertia for the 1-quasiparticle bands in odd-$A$ Pm isotopes $^{153, 155, 157}$Pm are reproduced quite well by the present calculation. By comparison between the experimental and calculated moments of inertia for the three 2-quasiparticle bands in the odd-odd nuclei $^{154, 156}$Pm, their configurations and bandhead spins have been assigned properly. For the 2-quasiparticle band in $^{154}$Pm, the configuration is assigned as $\pi5/2^-[532]\otimes \nu3/2^-[521]$ ($K^π=4^+$) with the bandhead spin $I_0=4\hbar$. In $^{156}$Pm, the same configuration and bandhead spin assignments have been made for the 2-quasiparticle band with lower excitation energy. The configuration $\pi5/2^+[413]\otimes \nu5/2^+[642]$ ($K^π=5^+$) with the bandhead spin $I_0=5\hbar$ is assigned for that with higher excitation energy.

## Key claim

By comparing experimental and calculated kinematic moments of inertia, the configurations and bandhead spins for three 2-quasiparticle rotational bands in the odd-odd nuclei 154Pm and 156Pm are assigned, with the caveat that the proton-neutron residual interaction is not included in the particle-number conserving cranked shell model (PNC-CSM), so the K quantum numbers are assigned based on Gallagher-Moszkowski coupling rules.

## Method

The cranked shell model with pairing correlations treated by a particle-number conserving (PNC) method was used, where the pairing Hamiltonian is diagonalized directly in a truncated cranked many-particle configuration space. The kinematic moments of inertia for the 1-quasiparticle bands in 153,155,157Pm were first calculated and compared with experimental data to validate the parameter set. Then, for each 2-quasiparticle band in 154,156Pm, the experimental moments of inertia were extracted assuming different bandhead spins and K values, and compared with PNC-CSM calculations using different possible 2-quasiparticle configurations to determine the best match.

## Key numbers

- For the 2-quasiparticle band in 154Pm: configuration assigned as π5/2-[532]⊗ν3/2-[521] (Kπ=4+) with bandhead spin I0=4ħ.
- For band 1 in 156Pm: configuration assigned as π5/2-[532]⊗ν3/2-[521] (Kπ=4+) with bandhead spin I0=4ħ.
- For band 2 in 156Pm: configuration assigned as π5/2+[413]⊗ν5/2+[642] (Kπ=5+) with bandhead spin I0=5ħ.
- Deformation parameters used: ε2=0.250 for 153,154Pm; ε2=0.258 for 155,156Pm; ε2=0.267 for 157Pm; ε4 values range from -0.073 to -0.047 across the isotopes.
- Pairing strengths: G0p=0.25 MeV and G2p=0.01 MeVfm-4 for protons; G0n=0.30 MeV and G2n=0.02 MeVfm-4 for neutrons.
- The neutron orbital ν5/2+[642] was shifted upwards by 0.07ħω0 for all Pm isotopes.

## Context

This work extends the PNC-CSM method, previously applied to neighboring Nd, Sm, and Gd isotopes by Zhang (2018) and He (2018), to the neutron-rich Pm isotopes. The assignments resolve ambiguities left by Bhattacharyya (2018), who observed the rotational bands but could not assign configurations due to lack of firm spin and parity. The results enable further experimental studies of these odd-odd nuclei and provide calculated moments of inertia for other possible low-lying 2-quasiparticle bands as a guide for future measurements.
