# Initial conditions for dipole evolution beyond the McLerran-Venugopalan model

> We derive the scattering amplitude N(r) for a QCD dipole on a dense target in the semi-classical approximation. We include the first subleading correction in the target thickness arising from ~ρ^4 operators in the effective action for the large-x valence charges. Our result for N(r) can be matched to a phenomenological proton fit by Albacete et al over a broad range of dipole sizes r and provides a definite prediction for the A-dependence for heavy-ion targets. We find a suppression of N(r) for finite A for dipole sizes a few times smaller than the inverse saturation scale, corresponding to a suppression of the classical bremsstrahlung tail.

# Initial conditions for dipole evolution beyond the McLerran-Venugopalan model

## Key claim
The dipole scattering amplitude N(r) derived from including the first subleading correction in target thickness (arising from ~ρ⁴ operators in the effective action) can be matched to the phenomenological AAMQS proton fit over a broad range of dipole sizes, and predicts that the AAMQS modification of the McLerran-Venugopalan model should vanish like β_A ~ A^{-2/3} for nuclear targets.

## Method
The authors compute the dipole scattering amplitude N(r) in the semi-classical approximation by expanding the Wilson lines perturbatively to order g⁸, including the first correction from quartic (ρ⁴) operators in the effective action for large-x valence charges. They evaluate the expectation value of the dipole operator D(r) = (1/N_c)⟨tr V(x⊥)V†(y⊥)⟩ to leading logarithmic accuracy in log(1/rΛ), obtaining an analytic expression for the T-matrix N(r) = 1 - D(r) valid in the short-distance regime r²Q_s² < 1.

## Key numbers
- The saturation scale is defined as Q_s² ≡ (g⁴/2π) C_F ∫ dz⁻ μ̃²(z⁻), where C_F = (N_c²-1)/(2N_c)
- The matching parameter β for a proton target (A=1) is β ≃ 1/100, defined as β ≡ (C_F²/6π³) (g⁸/(Q_s² κ₄)) [∫ dz⁻ μ⁴(z⁻)]²
- For nuclei, β_A ∼ A^{-2/3}, while Q_s² ∼ A^{1/3}
- The dipole scattering amplitude N(r) = (Q_s² r²/4) log(1/rΛ) - (C_F²/6π³)(g⁸/κ₄)[∫ dz⁻ μ⁴(z⁻)]² r² log³(1/rΛ) for r²Q_s² < 1
- The AAMQS model uses γ ≃ 1.119 for the initial condition N_AAMQS(r, x₀=0.01) = 1 - exp[-(1/4)(r²Q_s²(x₀))^γ log(e + 1/rΛ)]
- The MV model initial condition overshoots LHC p+p data at p⊥ ≳ 6 GeV by roughly an order of magnitude

## Context
This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1994) by including non-Gaussian corrections from quartic Casimir operators in the effective action for valence charges, following the approach of Jeon and Venugopalan (2005) for random walks in SU(3) representation space. The result can be matched to the phenomenological AAMQS fit (Albacete, Armesto, Milhano, Quiroga, and Salgado, 2010) which describes deep-inelastic scattering data and charged hadron distributions in p+p collisions at 7 TeV. This enables predictions for the nuclear modification factor R_pA in p+Pb collisions at LHC and for heavy-ion structure functions at a future electron-ion collider, where the ρ⁴ correction is predicted to become negligible for large nuclei.
