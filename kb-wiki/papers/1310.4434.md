# An effective theory for QCD with an axial chemical potential

> We consider the low energy realization of QCD in terms of meson fields when an axial chemical potential is present; a situation that may be relevant in heavy ion collisions. We shall demonstrate that the presence of an axial charge constitutes an explicit source of parity breaking. The eigenstates of strong interactions do not have a definite parity and interactions that would otherwise be forbidden compete with the familiar ones. In this work, we first focus on scalars and pseudoscalars that are described by a generalized linear sigma model; and next, we give some hints on how the Vector Meson Dominance model describes the vector sector.

# An effective theory for QCD with an axial chemical potential

## Key claim

The presence of an axial chemical potential μ₅, generated by topological fluctuations in a hot QCD medium, constitutes an explicit source of parity breaking in the low-energy hadronic sector; however, the effect is only relevant for light u and d quarks because the strange quark's chiral charge oscillates to zero on timescales much shorter than the fireball lifetime (τ_fireball ∼ 5–10 fm, while 1/m_s ∼ 1 fm).

## Method

The authors construct a generalized linear sigma model for scalar and pseudoscalar mesons, extending the derivative operator to include an isosinglet axial chemical potential μ₅ as the time component of an external axial-vector field. They fit the coupling constants to phenomenology via χ² minimization using MINUIT, assuming isospin symmetry and v_u = v_d = v_s = f_π ≈ 92 MeV at μ₅ = 0. For the vector meson sector, they employ the Vector Meson Dominance model and introduce a Chern-Simons term proportional to μ₅ to capture parity-breaking effects.

## Key numbers

- Fireball lifetime: τ_fireball ∼ 5–10 fm; strange quark oscillation time: 1/m_s ∼ 1 fm; light quark oscillation time: 1/m_q ∼ 40 fm (for m_q ∼ 5 MeV)
- Pion decay constant at μ₅ = 0: f_π ≈ 92 MeV
- Fitted coupling constants (in MeV units): b = -3510100/m, M² = 1255600, c = 1252.2, λ₁ = 67.007, λ₂ = 9.3126, d₁ = -1051.7/m, d₂ = 523.21/m, with m ≡ (m_u + m_d)/2 and m/m_s ≈ 1/25
- For μ₅ = 50 MeV, the in-medium η̃ decay width at rest is approximately 60 MeV, corresponding to a mean free path of 3 fm
- For μ₅ ≈ 240 MeV and momentum q = 500 MeV, an extra bump appears in the η̃ width
- The π̃ mass becomes tachyonic for momenta above threshold k^c_π̃ ≡ m₁m₂/(4μ₅); the η̃ mass becomes tachyonic above k^c_η̃ ≡ m₃/(4μ₅ m₅) √(m₄² m₅² − 8c² v_q²)
- Vector meson dispersion relation: m_{V,ε}² − m_V² ∝ ε μ₅ |k|, where ε = 0, ±1 is the meson polarization
- Example calculation shown for μ₅ = 290 MeV in the ρ⁰ → e⁺e⁻ channel

## Context

This work extends the hypothesis that local parity breaking (LPB) in QCD arises from large topological fluctuations in a hot environment, as opposed to stable P- and CP-odd pion condensates studied by earlier authors (pioncond references). The Vafa-Witten theorem does not apply for μ ≠ 0, so parity breaking in a finite volume is not forbidden. The paper connects to the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) proposed by Kharzeev (kharz) for peripheral heavy ion collisions, but focuses on central collisions where topological charge fluctuations generate an axial chemical potential. The results enable experimental searches for parity-breaking signatures in heavy ion collisions, particularly through distorted ρ⁰ spectral functions in dilepton channels and through enhanced η → ππ decays that could thermalize within the fireball.
