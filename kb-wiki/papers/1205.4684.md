# Probing Two Holographic Models of Strongly Coupled Anisotropic Plasma

> Quark-gluon plasma during its initial phase after its production in heavy-ion collisions is expected to have substantial pressure anisotropies. In order to model this situation by a strongly coupled N=4 super-Yang-Mills plasma with fixed anisotropy by means of AdS/CFT duality, two models have been discussed in the literature. Janik and Witaszczyk have considered a geometry involving a comparatively benign naked singularity, while more recently Mateos and Trancanelli have used a regular geometry involving a nontrivial axion field dual to a parity-odd deformation of the gauge theory by a spatially varying theta parameter. We study the (rather different) implications of these two models on the heavy-quark potential as well as jet quenching and compare their respective predictions with those of weakly coupled anisotropic plasmas.

# Probing Two Holographic Models of Strongly Coupled Anisotropic Plasma

## Key claim

The two holographic models of anisotropic strongly coupled plasma (the singular Janik-Witaszczyk geometry and the regular Mateos-Trancanelli axion-dilaton geometry) produce qualitatively different predictions for heavy-quark potential and jet quenching, and neither fully agrees with weak-coupling results; the Janik-Witaszczyk model shows opposite anisotropy effects for prolate versus oblate plasmas, while the Mateos-Trancanelli model is more uniform in this respect.

## Method

The authors compute the heavy-quark potential from the Wilson-Polyakov loop dual to a fundamental string with spacelike separated endpoints at the AdS boundary, using the generic metric form for stationary spatially anisotropic geometries. For jet quenching, they compare the momentum broadening parameters derived from the two holographic backgrounds. They also compute the electrostatic propagator and heavy-quark potential in two weak-coupling models: hard anisotropic loop effective theory and a zero-coupling version of the Mateos-Trancanelli theta-deformed gauge theory.

## Key numbers

- In the JW model with constant energy density, the heavy-quark potential is computed for oblate anisotropy with $P_z=0$ ($B=\sqrt{2}$) and prolate anisotropy with $P_\perp=0$ ($B=-\sqrt{6}$), where $B$ is the dimensionless anisotropy parameter.
- In the MT model at approximately constant temperature $T=0.318$ (in units with $\mu=1$), the anisotropy parameter $a/T$ ranges from $0$ (isotropic) to $50.51$, with pressure anisotropy $\Delta$ ranging from $0$ to $-1.29$.
- In the MT model at approximately constant entropy density $N_c^{-2}s=0.159$, the anisotropy parameter $a N_c^{2/3}/s^{1/3}$ ranges from $0$ to $27.37$, with $\Delta$ ranging from $0$ to $-1.29$.
- In the weak-coupling hard anisotropic loop model, the anisotropy parameter $\xi$ ranges from $-0.9$ (prolate, $\Delta=-0.8365$) to $100$ (oblate, $\Delta=55.72$).
- In the zero-coupling theta-deformed model, the pressure anisotropy at high temperature is $P_z^{(0)}-P_\perp^{(0)} = a^2 T^2/24 - 3a^3 T/64 + O(a^4)$, indicating prolate anisotropy for $T\gg a$, while the holographic MT model gives $P_z-P_\perp = -a^2 T^2/16 + O(a^4)$ (oblate for $a\ll T$).

## Context

This work extends the comparison of two holographic approaches to anisotropic quark-gluon plasma: the singular gravity dual of Janik and Witaszczyk (2008) and the regular axion-dilaton gravity dual of Mateos and Trancanelli (2011). The authors had previously studied electromagnetic signatures of the JW model (Rebhan and Steineder, 2011) and viscosity properties of the MT model (Rebhan and Steineder, 2011). The heavy-quark potential in the MT model had been explored by Giataganas (2012) and Chernicoff et al. (2012), while weak-coupling results for the heavy-quark potential were obtained by Dumitru, Guo, and Hidaka (2007) and Philipsen and Tassler (2009). The results enable assessment of which holographic model better captures the qualitative features expected from heavy-ion collision phenomenology, and highlight that the JW model's anisotropy effects reverse between prolate and oblate plasmas while the MT model does not show this reversal.
