# Thermal model at RHIC: particle ratios and pT spectra

> Predictions of the single-freeze-out model for the particle spectra at RHIC are presented. The model assumes that the chemical and thermal freeze-outs occur simultaneously, and incorporates in simple terms the longitudinal and transverse flow. All resonance decays are included. The model predictions and the data are in striking agreement in the whole available range of momenta.

## Key claim

The single-freeze-out model, which assumes chemical and thermal freeze-outs occur simultaneously, reproduces RHIC particle spectra with "striking agreement" across the whole available momentum range, but the authors caution that results at \(p_\perp > 2\) GeV should be taken "with a grain of salt" since hard processes are expected to be important there.

## Method

The model uses a freeze-out hypersurface parameterized as a generalization of the Bjorken model with two geometric parameters: invariant time \(\tau\) and transverse radius \(\rho_{\rm max}\), assuming Hubble-like expansion where four-velocity is proportional to the coordinate. Particles are distributed according to statistical distribution functions at freeze-out with thermal parameters \(T=165 \pm 7\) MeV and \(\mu_B=41 \pm 5\) MeV obtained from particle ratio analysis, and all resonance decays from PDG tables are included exactly in semi-analytic fashion. The Cooper-Frye formalism is applied to compute spectra, with the ratio \(\rho_{\rm max}/\tau\) determining the spectral shape.

## Key numbers

- Thermal parameters from particle ratio analysis: \(T = 165 \pm 7\) MeV, \(\mu_B = 41 \pm 5\) MeV, with \(\mu_S = 9\) MeV and \(\mu_I = -1\) MeV.
- For PHENIX minimum-bias data at \(\sqrt{s} = 130\) GeV A: fitted geometric parameters \(\tau = 5.55\) fm and \(\rho_{\rm max} = 4.50\) fm.
- For most central collisions at RHIC: fitted geometric parameters \(\tau = 7.66\) fm and \(\rho_{\rm max} = 6.69\) fm.
- Average transverse flow velocity \(\langle \beta_\perp \rangle = 0.49\) and maximum transverse flow velocity \(\beta^{\rm max}_\perp = 0.66\) from the most-central fit.
- The model reproduces the crossing of \(p\) and \(\pi^+\) around \(p_\perp = 2\) GeV, and of \(K^+\) and \(\pi^+\) around 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the thermal model approach to RHIC energies, confirming the particle ratio results of previous studies (Braun-Munzinger et al., 2001) and providing a unified description of both ratios and spectra. The single-freeze-out assumption contradicts the traditional picture with two distinct freeze-outs at noticeably different temperatures, and the model solves the "anti-baryon puzzle" discussed by Rapp by naturally reproducing \(\overline{p}\) yields without requiring additional mechanisms. The approach enables future work on HBT radii, where the authors note that including resonance decays and Van der Waals corrections (excluded volume effects) should increase the predicted transverse size from the current \(\rho_{\rm max}\) value to resolve the discrepancy with measured HBT radii.
