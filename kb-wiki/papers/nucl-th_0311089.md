# Dispersion Relation Constrained Partial Wave Analysis of piN Elastic and piN\to\etaN Scattering Data: The Baryon Spectrum

> We present results from a comprehensive partial-wave analysis of pi+-p elastic scattering and charge-exchange data, covering the region from threshold to 2.1 GeV in the lab pion kinetic energy, employing a coupled-channel formalism to simultaneously fit pi-p\toηn data to 0.8 GeV. Our main result, solution FA02, utilizes a complete set of forward and fixed-t dispersion relation constraints, from threshold to 1 GeV, and from t = 0 to -0.4 (GeV/c)^2, applied to the piN elastic amplitude. A large number of systematic checks have been performed, including fits with no charge-exchange data and other database changes, fits with few or no dispersion relation constraints, and changes to the Coulomb correction scheme. We have also reexamined methods used to extract Breit-Wigner resonance parameters. The quality of fit to both data and dispersion relation constraints is superior to our earlier work. The results of these analyses are compared with previous solutions in terms of their resonance spectra and preferred values for couplings and low-energy parameters, including the piNN coupling constant.

# Dispersion Relation Constrained Partial Wave Analysis of πN Elastic and πN→ηN Scattering Data: The Baryon Spectrum

## Key claim

The FA02 solution provides a superior fit to both πN elastic and πN→ηN scattering data and dispersion relation constraints compared to earlier work (SM95), but the resonance parameters extracted depend on the method used (pole versus Breit-Wigner), and the results for the N(1535) and N(1650) resonances differ significantly from the SM95 analysis, with widths now closer to their Review of Particle Properties averages.

## Method

A coupled-channel Chew-Mandelstam K-matrix formalism was used to simultaneously fit π±p elastic scattering and charge-exchange data from threshold to 2.1 GeV lab pion kinetic energy, along with π−p→ηn data up to 0.8 GeV. The energy-dependent partial-wave fits were constrained by a complete set of forward and fixed-t dispersion relations (C±, E±, B±, and fixed-t C±) applied from threshold to 1 GeV and t = 0 to -0.4 (GeV/c)^2, with the π−p S-wave scattering length fixed near the PSI pionic-atom result. Coulomb corrections employed the Nordita prescription supplemented by Gibbs extended-source barrier factors, with the Δ(1232) charge splitting treated phenomenologically.

## Key numbers

- Overall χ² of 45874 for 23979 data points (π± elastic, charge-exchange, and η production) to 2.1 GeV, with ηN data included to 800 MeV; this is significantly lower than the previous SM95 fit χ²/data = 2.2
- πNN coupling constant g²/4π = 13.75 ± 0.10
- P-wave scattering volumes: a⁺₁₊ = 0.133 ± 0.001 μ⁻³, a⁻₁₊ = -0.074 ± 0.001 μ⁻³
- S-wave scattering lengths: 3a⁻₀₊ = 0.2650 ± 0.0014 μ⁻¹, a_π⁻p = 0.0870 ± 0.0013 μ⁻¹, a⁺₀₊ = -0.0010 ± 0.0012 μ⁻¹
- Δ⁰−Δ⁺⁺ mass difference = 1.74 ± 0.15 MeV; width difference Γ⁰−Γ⁺⁺ = 1.09 ± 0.64 MeV
- 172 parameters varied in the energy-dependent analysis (94 for isospin 1/2, 78 for isospin 3/2)
- 138 differential and 35 total cross sections for π−p→ηn were selected for analysis

## Context

This work extends the previous SM95 solution (Arndt et al., 1995) by incorporating a larger database including new high-quality data in the Δ(1232) resonance region from TRIUMF, LAMPF, and PSI, and by adding fixed-t C± dispersion relation constraints and forward derivative E± dispersion relations over broader energy and t ranges. The coupled-channel approach with explicit ηN channel fitting addresses discrepancies between single-channel πN analyses and multi-channel studies of eta photoproduction that claimed different resonance properties for N(1535) and N(1520). The results enable improved extraction of baryon resonance parameters and low-energy constants, and provide a foundation for future extensions to higher energies and additional reaction channels.
