# $η^\prime$ photoproduction near threshold

> In this work, the $η^\prime$ meson photoproduction near threshold is studied in the quark model framework. A pseudovector effective Lagrangian is introduced for the $η^\prime NN$ coupling and the newly published data from the SAPHIR Collaboration provide good constraints to this parameter. Corrections of order $O(1/m_q^2)$ for the electromagnetic interaction vertex are taken into account, which produce corrections of order $O(1/m_q^3)$ to the transition amplitude for $γp\to η^\prime p$. Some low-lying resonances, $S_{11}(1535)$, $P_{13}(1720)$, and $P_{13}(1900)$ are found to have significant contributions. A bump structure around $E_γ\approx$ 2 GeV is found arising from the $n=3$ terms in the harmonic oscillator basis. The beam polarization asymmetries are predicted and can be tested against the forthcoming data from GRAAL.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the steep peak in the total cross section for γp → η′p near threshold is dominated by the S11(1535) resonance, contrary to an earlier explanation that invoked higher-mass resonances S11(2090) and P11(2100). The author qualifies that the η′NN coupling constant is treated as a free parameter constrained by the SAPHIR data, and that the model includes corrections of order O(1/mq³) to the transition amplitude.

## Method

The author uses a nonrelativistic constituent quark model (NRCQM) with a pseudovector effective Lagrangian for the η′NN coupling at the quark level. The electromagnetic interaction is expanded to O(1/mq²), and the equal velocity frame (EVF) is adopted for Lorentz boosts to avoid an ad hoc form factor. The s-channel resonance excitations are systematically included, with the harmonic oscillator basis used to classify resonances up to n=3 shells, and the nucleon pole terms are also incorporated.

## Key numbers

- The η′NN coupling constant derived from the fit is gη′NN = 1.66, corresponding to a coupling αη′ = 0.22.
- The harmonic oscillator strength is α = 384.5 MeV and the constituent quark mass is mq = 330 MeV, adopted from η meson production studies.
- The S11(1535) branching ratios used are 0.45 to πN and 0.55 to ηN, the same as in η meson production.
- A bump structure in the total cross section is found automatically at Eγ ≈ 2 GeV, arising from n=3 terms in the harmonic oscillator basis.
- The exclusive total cross section for γp → N* → η′p is used to derive the quantity ξ = {|A1/2|² + |A3/2|²}¹/² for the P13(1900) and F15(2000) resonances, with branching ratios to η′N possibly as large as 20%.
- The photon energy range covered by the SAPHIR data is from threshold (1.45 GeV) to 2.6 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the quark model approach of Li (1997) for η′ photoproduction, which had predicted a strong near-threshold peak dominated by S11(1535) and a small bump at Eγ = 2 GeV, but had not included the P13(1900) and F15(2000) resonances. The author contrasts this with the hadronic model of the SAPHIR Collaboration (1998), which attributed the resonance structure to S11(2090) and P11(2100) and neglected low-lying resonances. The results enable future tests of the predicted beam polarization asymmetries against data from GRAAL and JLab, and suggest that η′ photoproduction can help clarify the existence and properties of the P13(1900) and F15(2000) resonances.
