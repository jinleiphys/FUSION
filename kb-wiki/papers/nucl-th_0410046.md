# Shape transition and oblate-prolate coexistence in N=Z fpg-shell nuclei

> Nuclear shape transition and oblate-prolate coexistence in $N=Z$ nuclei are investigated within the configuration space ($2p_{3/2}$, $1f_{5/2}$, $2p_{1/2}$, and $1g_{9/2}$). We perform shell model calculations for $^{60}$Zn, $^{64}$Ge, and $^{68}$Se and constrained Hartree-Fock (CHF) calculations for $^{60}$Zn, $^{64}$Ge, $^{68}$Se, and $^{72}$Kr, employing an effective pairing plus quadrupole residual interaction with monopole interactions. The shell model calculations reproduce well the experimental energy levels of these nuclei. From the analysis of potential energy surface in the CHF calculations, we found shape transition from prolate to oblate deformation in these $N=Z$ nuclei and oblate-prolate coexistence at $^{68}$Se. The ground state of $^{68}$Se has oblate shape, while the shape of $^{60}$Zn and $^{64}$Ge are prolate. It is shown that the isovector matrix elements between $f_{5/2}$ and $p_{1/2}$ orbits cause the oblate deformation for $^{68}$Se, and four-particle four-hole ($4p-4h$) excitations are important for the oblate configuration.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the oblate deformation observed in the ground state of $^{68}$Se and the oblate-prolate coexistence in that nucleus are driven specifically by the isovector (T=1) monopole interaction matrix element between the $f_{5/2}$ and $p_{1/2}$ orbits, contrary to earlier VAMPIR calculations that attributed the oblate-driving force to T=0 monopole matrix elements between $g_{9/2}$ and $f_{5/2}(f_{7/2})$ orbits.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell model diagonalizations in the $fpg$-shell model space ($2p_{3/2}$, $1f_{5/2}$, $2p_{1/2}$, $1g_{9/2}$) for $^{60}$Zn, $^{64}$Ge, and $^{68}$Se, using an extended pairing-plus-quadrupole (P+QQ) Hamiltonian with monopole interactions. They also carried out constrained Hartree-Fock (CHF) calculations for $^{60}$Zn, $^{64}$Ge, $^{68}$Se, and $^{72}$Kr to map potential energy surfaces (PES) as functions of the intrinsic quadrupole moment $q$ and the triaxial angle $\gamma$, and analyzed spectroscopic quadrupole moments and occupation numbers to identify the microscopic origin of oblate deformation.

## Key numbers

- Single-particle energies used: $\varepsilon_{p3/2}=0.0$ MeV, $\varepsilon_{f5/2}=0.77$ MeV, $\varepsilon_{p1/2}=1.11$ MeV, $\varepsilon_{g9/2}=2.50$ MeV (adjusted from the earlier 3.70 MeV to fit odd-mass Ge isotopes)
- Force strengths: $g_{0}=0.270(64/A)$, $\chi_{2}=0.250(64/A)^{5/3}/b^{4}$, $\chi_{3}=0.05(64/A)^{2}/b^{6}$, $k^{0}=1.44(64/A)$
- Monopole shifts: $V_{m}(f_{5/2},p_{1/2};T=1)=-0.4$ MeV, $V_{m}(p_{3/2},f_{5/2};T=1)=V_{m}(p_{3/2},p_{1/2};T=1)=-0.3$ MeV, $V_{m}(g_{9/2},g_{9/2};T=1)=-0.2$ MeV, $V_{m}(g_{9/2},g_{9/2};T=0)=-0.1$ MeV
- For $^{60}$Zn and $^{64}$Ge: deformation $\beta\sim 0.2$ (prolate) for the $2_{1}^{+}$ state and $\beta\sim -0.2$ (oblate) for the $2_{2}^{+}$ state
- For $^{68}$Se: oblate deformation with $\beta\sim -0.2$ for the $2_{1}^{+}$ state; prolate deformation with $\beta\sim 0.2$ for the $2_{2}^{+}$ state
- $B(E2;2_{1}^{+}\rightarrow 0_{1}^{+})$ for $^{68}$Se gives $\beta\sim 0.21$, smaller than the $\beta\sim 0.27$ from Fischer et al. and $\beta\sim 0.33$ from Petrovici et al.
- Maximum shell model dimension for $^{68}$Se: 0.165 billion
- Occupation numbers for $^{68}$Se $2_{1}^{+}$ with $v=-0.4$ MeV: $p_{3/2}=2.31$, $f_{5/2}=2.38$, $p_{1/2}=1.20$, $g_{9/2}=0.11$; with $v=0.0$ MeV: $p_{3/2}=2.65$, $f_{5/2}=2.78$, $p_{1/2}=0.42$, $g_{9/2}=0.15$

## Context

This work extends earlier shell model studies using the extended P+QQ interaction (Kaneko, Hasegawa) and addresses the long-standing predictions of oblate deformation in $N=Z$ nuclei near $A=68$, recently confirmed experimentally by Fischer et al. The results contradict the VAMPIR calculations of Petrovici et al., which attributed oblate deformation to T=0 monopole matrix elements between $g_{9/2}$ and $f_{5/2}(f_{7/2})$ orbits, a difference the authors trace to the inversion of the $p_{1/2}$ and $g_{9/2}$ single-particle energies in that work. The CHF results are consistent with Skyrme HFB calculations by Yamagami et al. The finding that $4p-4h$ excitations from $(p_{3/2}f_{5/2})$ to $(p_{1/2}g_{9/2})$ are essential for oblate configurations enables future investigations of oblate and triaxial deformation in $N\neq Z$ nuclei where the Fermi surface for neutrons lies between $f_{5/2}$ and $p_{1/2}$ in the $fpg$ shell.
