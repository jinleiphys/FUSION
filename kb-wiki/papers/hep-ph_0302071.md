# Triangular and Y-shaped hadrons with static sources

> The structure of hadrons consisting of three static color sources in fundamental (baryons) or adjoint (three-gluon glueballs) representations is studied. The static potentials of glueballs as well as gluon field distributions in glueballs and baryons are calculated in the framework of field correlator method.

## Key claim

The static potentials and gluon field distributions for three-gluon glueballs (both Y-shaped and triangular) and baryons are calculated using the field correlator method, but the nondiagonal (interference) terms are neglected in the glueball potentials, and the triangular glueball field distribution is obtained only in the leading order of the 1/N_c expansion.

## Method

The authors use the field correlator method (FCM) to compute Wilson loops for three-static-source systems, expressing static potentials in terms of two-point gluon field strength correlators. For the gluon field distributions, they employ the connected probe technique, where a small probe plaquette is joined to the hadron Wilson loop via parallel transporters, and evaluate the resulting expressions in the bilocal approximation of FCM using a parametrization of the correlators with string tension σ and gluon correlation length T_g.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of Y-type glueball potential to baryon potential: V_Y^(G)/V_Y^(B) = C_A/C_F = 9/4, where C_A = 3 and C_F = 4/3
- String tension used throughout: σ = 0.18 GeV^2
- Gluon field correlation length range: T_g = 0.12 to 0.2 fm
- Ratio of energy density at string junction to density in middle of string in baryon: w_sj / w_string = 9/8
- Saturated field profile for meson: E^string(ρ) = 2σ (1 + ρ/T_g) exp(-ρ/T_g), where ρ is distance from quark-antiquark axis
- Parameter relation from Gauss law: 3 σ T_g = C_F α_s, yielding T_g ≈ 0.2 fm for α_s ≈ 0.4
- Perturbative potential for three-gluon glueballs: V^pert_(adj)(r) = -(3/2) C_2(adj) α_s / r with α_s = 0.3

## Context

This work extends the field correlator method previously applied to mesons and baryons (Kuzmenko and Simonov, 2003) to three-gluon glueballs, and builds on lattice results for baryon flux structure obtained in the maximally abelian gauge (Ichie, 2002). The calculated field distributions enable future study of nuclear structure and can be extended to arbitrary numbers of colors, while the interference terms neglected in the glueball potentials are left for subsequent publications.
