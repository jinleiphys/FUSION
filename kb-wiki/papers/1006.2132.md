# Quark fragmentation in the $θ$-vacuum

> The vacuum of Quantum Chromodynamics is a superposition of degenerate states with different topological numbers that are connected by tunneling (the $θ$-vacuum). The tunneling events are due to topologically non-trivial configurations of gauge fields (e.g. the instantons) that induce local $\p$-odd domains in Minkowski space-time. We study the quark fragmentation in this topologically non-trivial QCD background. We find that even though QCD globally conserves $\p$ and $\cp$ symmetries, two new kinds of $\p$-odd fragmentation functions emerge. They generate interesting dihadron correlations: one is the azimuthal angle correlation $\sim \cos(ϕ_1 + ϕ_2)$ usually referred to as the Collins effect, and the other is the $\p$-odd correlation $\sim \sin(ϕ_1 + ϕ_2)$ that vanishes in the cross section summed over many events, but survives on the event-by-event basis. Using the chiral quark model we estimate the magnitude of these new fragmentation functions. We study their experimental manifestations in dihadron production in $e^+e^-$ collisions, and comment on the applicability of our approach in deep-inelastic scattering, proton-proton and heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

Even though QCD globally conserves parity and CP, local parity-odd domains induced by topological fluctuations (instantons) allow two new parity-odd quark fragmentation functions to emerge; these generate a parity-odd azimuthal correlation \(\sin(\phi_1+\phi_2)\) that survives only on an event-by-event basis, not in the event-averaged cross section.

## Method

The authors derive the most general Lorentz-invariant decomposition of the quark fragmentation matrix without imposing the parity constraint, obtaining two new parity-odd fragmentation functions \(\widetilde{D}(z,p_\perp^2)\) and \(\widetilde{H}_1^\perp(z,p_\perp^2)\) in addition to the usual spin-averaged function \(D(z,p_\perp^2)\) and the Collins function \(H_1^\perp(z,p_\perp^2)\). They estimate the magnitude of these new functions using the chiral quark model of Manohar and Georgi (1984), incorporating a modified quark propagator that accounts for an effective space-time dependent \(\theta\) field. The observable effects are then computed for back-to-back dihadron production in \(e^+e^-\) collisions at leading order.

## Key numbers

- The effective transverse gradient of the \(\theta\) field is estimated as \(\bar{\theta}_\perp \sim 10\ \text{MeV}\), derived from instanton vacuum parameters: mean instanton size \(\rho \sim 1/3\ \text{fm}\) and typical separation \(R\) satisfying \(\rho/R \sim 1/3\), with \(N_f=3\).
- The relative magnitude of the parity-odd azimuthal correlation in \(e^+e^-\) collisions is estimated as \(I(\bar{\theta}, z_1, z_2) \sim 1.5\%\) for typical values \(z_1 = z_2 = 0.5\) with final hadrons \(\pi^+\) and \(\pi^-\) at BELLE kinematics.
- The pion decay constant used in the chiral quark model is \(f_\pi \approx 93\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of QCD factorization to include local parity violation from topological fluctuations, building on the proposal of Kharzeev (1998) that local parity-odd domains can be observed through multi-particle correlations. It provides operator definitions for the new fragmentation functions and connects them to the instanton vacuum model reviewed by Schafer and Shuryak (1996). The results enable experimental searches for event-by-event parity violation in \(e^+e^-\) collisions at BELLE, as well as in deep-inelastic scattering, proton-proton, and heavy ion collisions, and offer an alternative interpretation of the Collins effect measured by the BELLE Collaboration (Seidl et al., 2008).
