# Electrical conductivity of hot relativistic plasma in a strong magnetic field

> We employ first-principles quantum field theoretical methods to investigate the longitudinal and transverse electrical conductivities of a strongly magnetized hot quantum electrodynamics (QED) plasma at the leading order in coupling. The analysis employs the fermion damping rate in the Landau-level representation, calculated with full kinematics and exact amplitudes of one-to-two and two-to-one QED processes. In the relativistic regime, both conductivities exhibit an approximate scaling behavior described by $Ïƒ_{\parallel,\perp} = T \tildeÏƒ_{\parallel,\perp}$, where $\tildeÏƒ_{\parallel,\perp}$ are functions of the dimensionless ratio $|eB|/T^2$ (with $T$ denoting temperature and $B$ magnetic field strength). We argue that the mechanisms for the transverse and longitudinal conductivities differ significantly, leading to a strong suppression of the former in comparison to the latter.

## Key claim

The longitudinal and transverse electrical conductivities of a strongly magnetized hot QED plasma exhibit an approximate scaling behavior described by $\sigma_{\parallel,\perp} = T \tilde{\sigma}_{\parallel,\perp}$, where $\tilde{\sigma}_{\parallel,\perp}$ are functions only of the dimensionless ratio $|eB|/T^2$, but this scaling holds only in the ultrarelativistic regime where $T \gg m_e$ and $\sqrt{|eB|} \gg m_e$, and the leading-order approximation is valid only when $|eB|/T^2 \gg \alpha$ (with $\alpha = 1/137$).

## Method

The authors used the Kubo linear-response formula to relate the electrical conductivity tensor to the imaginary part of the polarization tensor at one-loop order, omitting vertex corrections which give subleading contributions in strong magnetic fields. The fermion damping rates $\Gamma_n(k_z)$ in the Landau-level representation were calculated from first principles at leading order in coupling, using full kinematics and exact amplitudes of one-to-two and two-to-one QED processes ($\psi_n \to \psi_{n'} + \gamma$, $\psi_n + \gamma \to \psi_{n'}$, and $\psi_n + \bar{\psi}_{n'} \to \gamma$). Numerical data for damping rates were generated across magnetic fields from $|eB| = 225 m_e^2$ to $51200 m_e^2$ and temperatures from $T = 20 m_e$ to $80 m_e$, for Landau levels up to $n = 50$ (with phase space up to $n_{\rm max} = 100$), and these rates were then substituted into the Kubo-derived expressions for longitudinal and transverse conductivities.

## Key numbers

- The longitudinal conductivity scaling function $\tilde{\sigma}_{\parallel,0} \approx 2.4$ and the transverse conductivity scaling function $\tilde{\sigma}_{\perp,0} \approx 0.076$ at the reference point (the zero-field limit of the PadÃ© fits).
- In QED, the ratio of longitudinal to transverse conductivity reaches values of order $10^3$ at $|eB|/T^2 \simeq 1$ and grows to about $10^7$ at $|eB|/T^2 \simeq 50$.
- The transverse conductivity reaches a minimum around $b_{\rm min} \simeq 12$ (where $b = |eB|/T^2$) and then starts to grow slowly.
- The PadÃ© fit for $\tilde{\sigma}_{\perp}$ has a quality of about $4\%$ in the range $0.1 \lesssim |eB|/T^2 \lesssim 45$, while the fit for $\tilde{\sigma}_{\parallel}$ has a quality of about $20\%$ in the same range.
- Two low-temperature data points are given: at $T = 2.5 m_e \approx 1.28$ MeV with $|eB| = 12.5 m_e^2$ ($B \approx 5.52 \times 10^{14}$ G) and $|eB| = 25 m_e^2$ ($B \approx 1.10 \times 10^{15}$ G).

## Context

This work extends first-principles quantum field theoretical calculations of electrical conductivity from the zero-field case (studied by Ahonen (1996), Baym (1997), Arnold (2000, 2003), and Boyanovsky (2002)) and weak-field cases (van Erkelens (1984), Pike (2016)) to the regime of strong magnetic fields where Landau-level quantization is important. It builds on the authors' own recent calculation of fermion damping rates in strongly magnetized plasmas (Ghosh, 2024) and is similar in approach to the gauge theory study of Hattori (2016) but goes beyond their lowest Landau-level approximation. The results enable more realistic simulations of pulsar magnetospheres and provide a perturbative baseline for understanding charge transport in strongly magnetized quark-gluon plasma, where lattice QCD calculations (Astrakhantsev, 2019) and kinetic theory approaches (Fukushima, 2017, 2019) have been attempted but with significant discrepancies in magnitude and detail.
