# Boost invariant spin hydrodynamics within the first order in derivative expansion

> Boost-invariant equations of spin hydrodynamics confined to the first-order terms in gradients are numerically solved. The spin equation of state, relating the spin density tensor to the spin chemical potential, is consistently included in the first order. Depending on its form and the structure of the spin transport coefficients, we find solutions which are both stable and unstable within the considered evolution times of 10 fm/c. These findings are complementary to the recent identification of stable and unstable modes for perturbed uniform spin systems described by similar hydrodynamic frameworks.

## Key claim

The authors numerically solve boost-invariant spin hydrodynamics equations at first order in gradients, finding that solutions can be either stable or unstable depending on the form of the spin equation of state and the structure of spin transport coefficients, but only within the limited evolution time of 10 fm/c.

## Method

The authors implement Bjorken (boost-invariant) flow in the first-order spin hydrodynamic framework of Hattori et al. (2019), using a novel spin equation of state where the spin density tensor is proportional to the normalized spin chemical potential, \(S^{\mu\nu} = S_0(T) \, \omega^{\mu\nu} / \sqrt{\omega:\omega}\), to maintain consistent gradient counting. They reduce the full set of conservation equations to a single second-order differential equation for temperature evolution coupled to a spin evolution equation, which they solve numerically for different choices of the spin equation of state and the spin transport coefficient \(\gamma\).

## Key numbers

* The evolution time considered is 10 fm/c, within which both stable and unstable solutions are found.
* The spin chemical potential magnitude \(C\) evolves according to \(C = -\frac{1}{4\sqrt{2}\gamma\beta}\left(S_0^\prime(T) \frac{dT}{d\tau} + \frac{S_0}{\tau}\right)\), where \(\beta = 1/T\).
* The energy conservation equation includes shear and bulk viscosity terms as \(\frac{d\varepsilon}{d\tau} + \frac{\varepsilon+p}{\tau} - \frac{s_0}{\tau^2}\left(\frac{2}{3}\frac{\eta_s}{s_0} + \frac{\zeta}{s_0}\right) = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the first-order spin hydrodynamic framework of Hattori et al. (2019) and the subsequent studies by Fukushima et al. (2020), Wang et al. (2021), and Daher et al. (2022) by introducing a new spin equation of state that respects consistent gradient counting, making it complementary to the earlier boost-invariant analysis by Wang et al. (2021). The results enable future dynamic simulations of spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions and are directly comparable to the stability analysis of perturbed uniform spin systems recently performed by the same community.
