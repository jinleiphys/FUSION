# Dilaton-Limit Fixed Point in Hidden Local Symmetric Parity Doublet Model

> We study nucleon structure with positive and negative parities using a parity doublet model endowed with hidden local symmetry (HLS) with the objective to probe dense baryonic matter. The model -- that we shall refer to as "PDHLS model" for short -- allows a chiral-invariant mass of the nucleons unconnected to spontaneously broken chiral symmetry which comes out to be m_0 ~ 200 MeV at tree level from fitting to the decay width of the parity doubler, N(1535), to nucleon-pion and nucleon axial coupling g_A=1.267. The presence of a substantial m_0 that remains non-vanishing at chiral restoration presents a deep issue for the origin of the nucleon mass as well as will affect nontrivially the equation of state for dense baryonic matter relevant for compact stars. We construct a chiral perturbation theory at one-loop order and explore the phase structure of the model using renormalization group equations. We find a fixed point that corresponds exactly to the "dilaton limit" at which the HLS vector mesons decouple from the nucleons before reaching the vector manifestation fixed point. We suggest that cold baryonic system will flow to this limit as density increases toward that of chiral restoration.

## Key claim

The paper identifies a fixed point in the one-loop renormalization group equations of the parity-doublet hidden local symmetric (PDHLS) model that corresponds exactly to the "dilaton limit," at which the hidden local symmetry vector mesons decouple from nucleons before reaching the vector manifestation fixed point; the authors suggest that cold baryonic systems will flow to this limit as density increases toward chiral restoration, but note that this is a proposal and that the set of equations may possess other fixed points consistent with QCD.

## Method

The authors constructed a parity-doublet nonlinear sigma model with hidden local symmetry (PDHLS model) for nucleons of both parities, including vector mesons (rho, omega) as gauge bosons of the hidden local symmetry. They extracted the chiral-invariant mass parameter m0 at tree level by fitting the decay width of N(1535) to nucleon-pion and the nucleon axial coupling gA = 1.267. They then constructed a chiral perturbation theory at one-loop order and derived renormalization group equations for the nucleon masses, vector coupling gV, and axial-vector coupling gA, analyzing the fixed-point structure of these coupled equations.

## Key numbers

* Chiral-invariant mass m0 = 204 ± 39 MeV, extracted from the decay width of N(1535) → N+π with experimental width Γ = 70 ± 26.25 MeV.
* Tree-level scattering lengths: a0+ = (-6.0 ± 0.4) × 10⁻⁵ MeV⁻¹ and a0- = (57 ± 1) × 10⁻⁵ MeV⁻¹, compared to experimental values a0,exp+ = (-2.446 ± 0.504) × 10⁻⁵ MeV⁻¹ and a0,exp- = (66.043 ± 0.935) × 10⁻⁵ MeV⁻¹.
* The dilaton-limit fixed point is identified as (1-gV, gA-gV, MS, MD) = (0, 0, m0², 0), where MS = (mN+ + mN-)²/4 and MD = (mN+ - mN-)²/4.
* The decay width bound corresponds to tanh δ in the range 0.981 to 0.991.

## Context

This work extends the hidden local symmetry (HLS) approach of Harada and Yamawaki (2003) to include parity-doubled nucleons, motivated by the half-skyrmion phase found in skyrmion crystal simulations by Lee, Park, and Rho (2010) where a non-vanishing chiral-invariant mass m0 appears. The dilaton-limit fixed point found here is the same as the "dilaton limit" considered by Sasaki (2011) that encodes Weinberg's mended symmetry, and the result that vector-meson coupling to nucleons gets suppressed at high density is expected to have a large impact on the equation of state for compact stars. The extracted m0 value of ~200 MeV differs from the ~800 MeV required in mean-field linear sigma model approaches to nuclear matter, and the authors suggest that including both the dilaton scalar and vector mesons in a dilaton-implemented HLS model may resolve this discrepancy.
