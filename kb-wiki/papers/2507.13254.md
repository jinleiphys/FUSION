# High-precision baryon number cumulants from lattice QCD in a finite box: cumulant ratios, Lee-Yang zeros and critical endpoint predictions

> We have performed high-statistics lattice simulations using 4HEX improved staggered fermions on $16^3 \times 8$ lattices. We calculated the Taylor expansion coefficients of the pressure with respect to the baryochemical potential to the tenth order at zero, and fourth order at purely imaginary chemical potentials. We used this data to construct rational function approximations of the free energy. We use a rational ansatz that explicitly satisfies the charge conjugation symmetry and the Roberge-Weiss periodicity, which are exact properties of the QCD free energy. We use this ansatz to estimate the position of Lee-Yang zeros in the complex chemical potential plane. The temperature dependence of the imaginary part of the Lee-Yang zeros is then fitted with ansätze motivated by the universal behavior of the free energy near a 3D Ising critical point. In principle, this allows one to estimate the temperature of the critical endpoint. We consider several sources of systematic errors. On this single lattice spacing we find that with $84\%$ probability, the chiral critical endpoint is either below $103$~MeV temperature or it does not exist. We also identify some caveats of the method, which do not disappear even with the extremely high statistics of this present study. We discuss to what extent these can be eliminated by future high statistics lattice analyses.

## Key claim

Using a single lattice spacing and finite volume, the authors find with 84% probability that the chiral critical endpoint is either below 103 MeV temperature or does not exist, but they identify caveats in the method that persist even with extremely high statistics and cannot be eliminated without future analyses.

## Method

The authors performed high-statistics lattice QCD simulations using 4HEX improved staggered fermions on a fixed 16^3 x 8 lattice (physical volume LT=2), generating approximately 2 million configurations per temperature at zero chemical potential and 100,000 configurations per temperature at eight imaginary chemical potentials. They calculated Taylor expansion coefficients of the pressure with respect to baryochemical potential to tenth order at zero chemical potential and fourth order at purely imaginary chemical potentials, then constructed rational function approximations (Padé approximants) of the free energy using a cosh-based variable that explicitly satisfies charge conjugation symmetry and Roberge-Weiss periodicity. From these rational approximations they estimated Lee-Yang zero positions in the complex chemical potential plane, then fitted the temperature dependence of the imaginary part of the Lee-Yang zeros with ansätze motivated by 3D Ising universal behavior to estimate the critical endpoint temperature.

## Key numbers

* Taylor coefficients calculated to unprecedented tenth order (chi_10) at mu_B=0 on 16^3 x 8 lattices, with approximately 2 million configurations per temperature
* At purely imaginary chemical potentials, Taylor coefficients calculated up to fourth order with approximately 100,000 configurations per temperature and chemical potential
* The physical volume is fixed in temperature units to LT=2
* With 84% probability, the chiral critical endpoint is either below 103 MeV temperature or does not exist
* The number of terms in the expression for chi_2 through chi_10 is 2, 8, 25, 79, and 230 respectively
* For the 3D Ising universality class, the product of critical exponents beta*delta is approximately 1.56
* The radius of convergence of the Taylor expansion of the ratio chi_4^B/chi_2^B in mu_B/T is close to pi/2 at low temperatures
* Temperatures below 130 MeV were excluded from the scaling ansatz fits because lattice results were consistent with the hadron resonance gas baseline

## Context

This work extends the Lee-Yang zero approach to the QCD phase diagram previously used by Basar (2023) and Clarke (2024), applying it to a high-statistics lattice dataset. The authors compare their results to the hadron resonance gas model baseline and find that below 130 MeV the data cannot distinguish QCD from the HRG prediction. The study identifies substantial systematic uncertainties in both steps of the two-step extrapolation (analytic continuation to complex chemical potentials and subsequent extrapolation to the critical endpoint), showing that the method currently provides only an upper bound on the critical endpoint temperature rather than a sharp prediction. This contrasts with chiral effective model calculations by Kovacs (2016) and Fu (2023), truncated Dyson-Schwinger equation approaches by Isserstedt (2019) and Gao (2020), and holography-based modeling by Hippert (2023), which tend to predict a critical endpoint at higher baryochemical potentials.
