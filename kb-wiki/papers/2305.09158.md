# Statistical approach of nuclear multifragmentation with realistic nuclear equation of state

> In this work, Canonical Thermodynamical model for nuclear multifragmentation has been updated with realistic nuclear equation of state. Mass distribution, intermediate mass fragment multiplicity as well as isospin sensitive observables have been investigated with semi-microscopic approach of determining nuclear binding and excitation energies. Production of neutron rich isotopes as well as isoscaling and isobaric yield ratio parameters have been significantly modified due to inclusion of this realistic nuclear equation of state.

# Statistical approach of nuclear multifragmentation with realistic nuclear equation of state

## Key claim
The Canonical Thermodynamical Model (CTM) for nuclear multifragmentation, when updated with a semi-microscopic compressible liquid drop approach using the Sly5 functional for nuclear binding and excitation energies, produces significantly modified predictions for neutron-rich isotope production, isoscaling parameters, and isobaric yield ratios compared to the conventional Bethe-Weizsacker liquid drop formula, particularly for isospin-asymmetric fragmenting systems at higher temperatures and freeze-out volumes.

## Method
The author replaced the conventional liquid-drop binding energy formula in the CTM with a semi-microscopic approach where the Helmholtz free energy of each composite nucleus is decomposed into bulk, surface, and Coulomb contributions. The bulk part is derived from a meta-modelling of the nuclear equation of state using Sly5 functional parameters, with the baryonic density adjusted for isospin asymmetry via saturation properties. The surface free energy uses a temperature- and isospin-dependent surface tension optimized for the Sly5 functional, and the Coulomb term retains the Wigner-Seitz correction. Fragment multiplicities are then calculated from the canonical partition function using recursion relations, and the excited fragments are subsequently decayed through an evaporation model using the same realistic binding and excitation energies.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy per nucleon comparisons are shown for isotopes of Carbon, Oxygen, Silicon, Calcium, Nickel, and Tin, with experimental values from AME2020 used as reference; the realistic Sly5 approach modifies binding significantly for neutron-rich nuclei.
- Mass distributions are calculated for the fragmenting system with Z0=75, A0=168 at temperatures T=3 MeV, 5 MeV, and 8 MeV at freeze-out volume 6V0.
- Intermediate mass fragment (IMF) multiplicity dependence is shown for temperatures from approximately 2 to 8 MeV, freeze-out volumes from 4V0 to 10V0, and isospin asymmetry (N0/Z0) from approximately 1.2 to 1.5.
- Isoscaling ratios R21 are computed for two fragmenting systems with Z0=75, A0=168 and A0=186 at T=5 MeV and Vf=6V0; the isoscaling parameters alpha and beta decrease with the realistic Sly5 binding.
- Isobaric yield ratio ln R[1,-1,A] is plotted as a function of A^(2/3) for both fragmenting systems at T=5 MeV and Vf=6V0, showing reduced values with realistic binding.
- The Sly5 functional parameters used include: sigma0=1.09191, bs=15.36563, p=3.0 for surface tension; Ksat, rho0, and Tc(y_c,p) expression with Tc=87.76*(Ksat/375)^(1/2)*(0.155/rho0)^(1/3)*y_cp*(1-y_cp) in MeV.

## Context
This work extends the Canonical Thermodynamical Model (CTM) originally developed by Das et al. and the Copenhagen Statistical Multifragmentation Model (Bondorf et al.) by replacing the conventional Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula with a realistic nuclear equation of state based on the Sly5 functional. The approach builds on the meta-modelling framework of Margueron (2018) and the surface tension parameterization of Carreau (2019) from Thomas-Fermi calculations. The updated model enables more consistent coupling between dynamical transport models and statistical multifragmentation calculations, as both stages can now use the same nuclear interaction. Future work will investigate the effect of different nuclear equation of state parametrizations on fragmentation observables and enable hybrid model calculations with identical interactions in both the dynamical and statistical stages.
