# Chiral Perturbation Theory and the pp -> pp pi0 Reaction Near Threshold

> A chiral-perturbative consideration of the near-threshold pp -> pp pi0 reaction indicates that the pion-rescattering term has a substantial energy and momentum dependence. The existing calculations that incorporate this dependence give pion rescattering contributions significantly larger than those of the conventional treatment, and this enhanced rescattering term interferes destructively with the one-body impulse term, leading to theoretical cross sections that are much smaller than the observed values. However, since the existing calculations are based on coordinate-space representation, they involve a number of simplifying assumptions about the energy-momentum flow in the rescattering diagram, even though the delicate interplay between the one-body and two-body terms makes it desirable to avoid these kinematical assumptions. We carry out here a momentum-space calculation that retains the energy-momentum dependence of the vertices as predicted by chiral perturbation theory. Our improved treatment increases the rescattering amplitude by a factor of 3 over the value obtained in the r-space calculations. The pp -> pp pi0 transition amplitude, which is now dominated by the rescattering term, leads to the cross section much larger than what was reported in the approximate r-space calculations. Thus, the extremely small cross sections obtained in the previous chiral perturbative treatments of this reaction should be considered as an accidental consequence of the approximations employed rather than a general feature.

## Key claim
The extremely small cross sections for pp → ppπ⁰ near threshold obtained in previous chiral perturbation theory calculations (Park et al. 1996; Cohen et al. 1996) are an accidental consequence of the coordinate-space kinematical approximations employed, rather than a general feature of chiral perturbation theory. A full momentum-space calculation that retains the energy-momentum dependence of the vertices increases the rescattering amplitude by a factor of approximately 3 over the r-space calculations, leading to much larger cross sections.

## Method
The authors performed a momentum-space calculation of the pp → ppπ⁰ reaction near threshold using chiral perturbation theory in the heavy-fermion formalism, retaining the full energy-momentum dependence of the pion-nucleon vertices. The transition operator included the ν=-1 impulse term and the ν=+1 pion-rescattering term, with the reduced matrix elements evaluated between pp scattering wavefunctions generated from the Argonne V18 potential, using the low-energy coefficients c1, c2, and c3 determined from pion-nucleon scattering data.

## Key numbers
- The rescattering amplitude in the full momentum-space calculation is increased by a factor of approximately 3 over the value obtained in the coordinate-space calculations.
- The low-energy coefficients used (parameter set I) are: c1 = -0.87 ± 0.11 GeV⁻¹, c2 = 3.34 ± 0.27 GeV⁻¹, c3 = -5.25 ± 0.22 GeV⁻¹.
- An alternative determination of c2 from the pion-nucleon effective range parameter b⁺ gives c2 = (4.5 ± 0.7) GeV⁻¹, significantly larger than the value in parameter set I.
- The pion decay constant fπ = 93 MeV is used throughout.
- The conventional treatment yields cross sections smaller than experimental values by a factor of approximately 5.

## Context
This work extends the chiral perturbation theory calculations of Park et al. (1996) and Cohen et al. (1996) for the pp → ppπ⁰ reaction, which found that the enhanced rescattering term almost cancels the impulse term, leading to theoretical cross sections much smaller than observed values. The present calculation demonstrates that the coordinate-space kinematical simplifications used in those prior works are unreliable for this reaction, and that a proper momentum-space treatment resolves the discrepancy. This enables future calculations to properly incorporate the energy-momentum dependence of chiral perturbation theory vertices without the need for the fixed-kinematics approximation.
