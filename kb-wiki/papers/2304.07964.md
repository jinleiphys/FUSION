# First characterization of the scattering length distribution of vector meson interaction with deuteron

> Under the framework of the vector meson dominance model (VMD), the absolute scattering lengths of light vector mesons ($ϕ$, $ω$, and $ρ$) with the deuteron are calculated from the cross sections of vector meson photoproduction off a deuteron. Additionally, a fitting function is used to predict the scattering lengths of the heavy vector mesons $J/ψ$-$d$ and $% Υ$-$d$. Based on these results, the distribution of the scattering lengths $|α_{V d}|$ between vector mesons and the deuteron is presented for the first time and compared with the scattering lengths $|α_{V p}|$ between vector mesons and the proton. It is found that as the mass of the vector meson increases, the scattering lengths $|α_{V d}|$ become closer to $|α_{V p}|$. In addition, the correlation analysis indicates a strong positive relationship between the scattering length and the vector meson radius, with a high correlation coefficient. The implications of results are essential for improving our understanding of the interaction between hadron and nucleus.

# First characterization of the scattering length distribution of vector meson interaction with deuteron

## Key claim

Under the vector meson dominance (VMD) model, the absolute scattering lengths of light vector mesons (φ, ω, ρ) with the deuteron are extracted from photoproduction cross sections, and a fitted exponential function predicts the scattering lengths for heavy vector mesons (J/ψ, Υ) with the deuteron; however, the ρ-d result is excluded from the fit due to its large uncertainty and the broad width of the ρ meson, and the authors note that the close agreement between ρ-d and ω-d scattering lengths may be coincidental.

## Method

The VMD model is used to relate the differential cross section dσ/dt of vector meson photoproduction off a deuteron at the production threshold to the absolute scattering length |α_{Vd}|. Experimental differential cross section data for ρ-d (from Chetry 2019, at W = 3.02–3.49 GeV) and ω-d (from CLAS 2018, at W = 3.09–3.89 GeV) are fitted with an exponential function dσ/dt = A exp(b(t - t_thr)) to extract the threshold value. The scattering lengths for J/ψ-d and Υ-d are then predicted using a fitting function derived from the ω-d and φ-d results.

## Key numbers

- RMS scattering length for ρ-d: |α_{ρd}| = 0.060 ± 0.017 fm (from five W values between 3.02 and 3.49 GeV)
- RMS scattering length for ω-d: |α_{ωd}| = 0.052 ± 0.019 fm (from four W values between 3.09 and 3.89 GeV)
- Predicted scattering length for J/ψ-d: |α_{J/ψ d}| = 0.697 ± 0.071 am (attometers)
- Predicted scattering length for Υ-d: |α_{Υ d}| = 0.260 ± 0.084 am (attometers)
- Fitting function for V-d scattering length: |α_{Vd}| = exp[-8.73 + (4.54 ± 0.30) × (1/M_V)], where M_V is the vector meson mass
- Strong interaction radii from literature: ρ = 0.72 ± 0.05 fm, ω = 0.72 ± 0.05 fm, φ = 0.46 ± 0.02 fm, J/ψ = 0.20 ± 0.02 fm, proton = 0.82 ± 0.02 fm; deuteron RMS radius = 1.953 fm

## Context

This work extends previous VMD-based studies of vector meson-proton scattering lengths (Strakovsky 2014, 2020, 2021; Wang 2022) and the authors' own extraction of φ-d scattering length (Wang 2022) to provide the first systematic distribution of vector meson-deuteron scattering lengths. The results enable comparison of V-d and V-p interactions, showing that |α_{Vd}| is nearly an order of magnitude smaller than |α_{Vp}| for each meson, and that the difference decreases as vector meson mass increases. The authors suggest these findings may provide theoretical references for understanding pentaquark state production (LHCb 2015, 2019) and molecular state binding energies, but note that additional experimental data on γd → Vd cross sections are needed for definitive conclusions.
