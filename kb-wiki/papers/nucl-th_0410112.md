# Gauge dependence of calculations in relativistic Coulomb excitation

> Before a quantum-mechanical calculation involving electromagnetic interactions is performed, a choice must be made of the gauge to be used in expressing the potentials. If the calculation is done exactly, the observable results it predicts will be independent of the choice of gauge. However, in most practical calculations approximations are made, which can destroy the gauge invariance of the predictions. We compare here the results of coupled-channel time-dependent relativistic Coulomb excitation calculations, as performed in either Lorentz or Coulomb gauges. We find significant differences when the bombarding energy per nucleon is $\geq$ 2 GeV, which indicates that the common practice of relying completely on the Lorentz gauge can be dangerous.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that coupled-channel time-dependent relativistic Coulomb excitation calculations yield significantly different results when performed in Lorentz versus Coulomb gauges at bombarding energies per nucleon ≥ 2 GeV, contradicting the common assumption that the Lorentz gauge is always reliable for such calculations.

## Method

The authors derived the gauge transformation connecting Lorentz and Coulomb gauge potentials for both point and finite-size projectiles, then computed interaction matrix elements in both gauges using multipole expansions. They applied these matrix elements to coupled-channel calculations of multiphonon giant dipole resonance excitation in ⁴⁰Ca bombarded by ²⁰⁸Pb, comparing the gauge-dependent predictions.

## Key numbers

- Bombarding energy per nucleon ≥ 2 GeV: the threshold above which significant gauge-dependent differences appear in the calculations
- γ (Lorentz factor): the key parameter controlling the divergence; the Lorentz gauge matrix element [V^L(ω)]_{γα} diverges as log γ for M_γ = M_α when ω ≠ ω_{γα}, while the Coulomb gauge matrix element [V^C(ω)]_{γα} remains finite due to a 1/γ² factor that overpowers the log γ divergence
- The Lorentz gauge potential predicts non-zero one-step population of excited J=0 states from a J=0 ground state, whereas the Coulomb gauge potential predicts these matrix elements vanish identically

## Context

This work extends the classic relativistic Coulomb excitation formalism of Winther and Alder (1979), which used first-order perturbation theory and was gauge invariant, to the coupled-channel regime where gauge invariance is lost due to truncation of the target state basis. The paper addresses concerns raised by Baltz, Rhoades-Brown, and Weneser (1991) and Rumrich and Greiner (1991) about gauge dependence in relativistic Coulomb excitation, and provides explicit formulas showing that the Lorentz gauge develops a logarithmic divergence at high energy that the Coulomb gauge avoids. The results enable more reliable coupled-channel calculations for multi-phonon giant resonance excitation at relativistic energies.
