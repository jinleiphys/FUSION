# Ab initio computations of strongly deformed nuclei around $^{80}$Zr

> Nuclei around $N\approx Z\approx 40$ are strongly deformed and exhibit coexistence of shapes. These phenomena have challenged nuclear models. Here we perform ab initio coupled-cluster computations of low-lying collective states and electromagnetic quadrupole transitions of the even-even nuclei $^{72}$Kr, $^{76,78}$Sr, $^{78,80}$Zr and $^{84}$Mo starting from chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces. Our calculations reproduce the coexistence of oblate and prolate shapes in these nuclei, yield rotational bands and strong electromagnetic transitions, but are not accurate for some observables and nuclei. These results highlight the advances and challenges of ab initio computations of heavy deformed nuclei.

# Ab initio computations of strongly deformed nuclei around $^{80}$Zr

## Key claim

The authors perform ab initio coupled-cluster computations that reproduce shape coexistence and rotational bands in heavy nuclei around mass 80, but the calculations are not precise enough to unambiguously identify the ground-state shape because 1% uncertainties in total energies for different shapes are larger than the small energy differences between them.

## Method

The authors start from chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces (the 1.8/2.0(EM) interaction), perform axially-symmetric Hartree-Fock calculations with a quadrupole constraint to map potential energy surfaces, then include short-range correlations via coupled-cluster with singles and doubles (CCSD) and restore rotational symmetry through angular momentum projection. The three-nucleon force is treated via a normal-ordered two-body approximation modified for deformed nuclei, and the calculations are performed in harmonic-oscillator basis spaces up to Nmax = 12.

## Key numbers

- For $^{80}$Zr, the Hartree-Fock minima have deformation parameters $\beta_2 \approx$ -0.17 (oblate), 0.0 (spherical), 0.33 (prolate), and 0.46 (larger prolate)
- The $B(E2;2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1)$ value for $^{80}$Zr from the larger prolate shape is 3044 $e^2$fm$^4$ (with uncertainties +143/-274) in the 1.8/2.0(EM) interaction, and 3449 $e^2$fm$^4$ with the $\Delta$NNLO$_{\rm GO}$ interaction
- The experimental $B(E2)$ for $^{80}$Zr is 1910(180) $e^2$fm$^4$ from Llewellyn (2020)
- For $^{72}$Kr, the computed $B(E2)$ is 1012 $e^2$fm$^4$ (with uncertainties +36/-50), compared to experimental values of 810(150) $e^2$fm$^4$ from Iwasaki (2014) and 999(129) $e^2$fm$^4$ from Gade (2005)
- For $^{76}$Sr, the computed $B(E2)$ is 2444 $e^2$fm$^4$ (with uncertainties +145/-248), compared to the experimental value 2390(240) $e^2$fm$^4$
- In $^{72}$Kr, the oblate and prolate $0^+$ states are about 1 MeV apart and the $2^+$ states about 0.8 MeV apart
- The three-nucleon interaction is truncated to excitation energies up to $E_{\rm 3max}=28\hbar\omega$
- The energy from triples excitations is estimated as 10% of the CCSD correlation energy

## Context

This work extends ab initio coupled-cluster methods to nuclei about twice as heavy as the deformed neon and magnesium isotopes in the first island of inversion previously computed by Miyagi (2020), Novario (2020), Stroberg (2021), Frosini (2022), and Sun (2024). The results are compared to beyond-mean-field calculations by Delaroche (2010) and Rodriguez (2011), and to large-scale shell model calculations by Kaneko (2021). The paper addresses the mass anomaly near N=Z=40 reported by Hamaker (2021), where theoretical approaches struggle to reproduce the four-point mass difference and two-proton shell gap. The computations provide a step toward describing deformed nuclei in heavy-ion collisions and tests of fundamental symmetries.
