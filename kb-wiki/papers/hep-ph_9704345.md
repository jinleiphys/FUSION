# The Linear Meson Model and Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We compare the linear meson model and chiral perturbation theory in next to leading order in the quark mass expansion. In particular, we compute the couplings L_4--L_8 of chiral perturbation theory as functions of the parameters of the linear model. They are induced by the exchange of 0^{++} scalar mesons. We use a phenomenological analysis of the effective vertices of the linear model in terms of pseudoscalar meson masses and decay constants. Our results for the L_i agree with previous phenomenological estimates.

## Key claim

The paper computes the low-energy constants L4 through L8 of chiral perturbation theory as functions of the parameters of the linear meson model, finding that these couplings are induced by the exchange of 0++ scalar mesons and that the results agree with previous phenomenological estimates, though the couplings L5 and L8 from the linear model are somewhat smaller than the central values from chiral perturbation theory at a renormalization scale of 400 MeV.

## Method

The authors express the complex 3x3 matrix field of the linear sigma model in a polar decomposition into a hermitian scalar matrix S and a unitary matrix U containing the pseudoscalar mesons. They then solve the field equations for the scalar fields as functionals of the pseudoscalar fields and insert the result into the effective action, expanding to low orders in derivatives and quark masses. The parameters of the linear model are determined phenomenologically from pseudoscalar meson masses and decay constants, including f_pi, f_K, f_eta, f_eta-prime, and the eta-prime mass, without requiring input from the scalar sector.

## Key numbers

- The average pseudoscalar octet mass used for momentum evaluation is q0^2 = -(2M_K±^2 + M_pi±^2)/3
- The compositeness scale k_Phi for the linear meson model is around (600-700) MeV
- The coupling L7 is given by L7 = -sigma_0/(18 nu), essentially fixed by f_pi and the eta-prime mass
- The combination (Z_h/Z_m) m_h^2 appearing in L5 and L8 is approximately (2/3)(Z_p/Z_m) M_eta-prime^2 + 3 lambda_2 sigma_0^2

## Context

This work extends the comparison between the linear sigma model and chiral perturbation theory beyond lowest order, building on the phenomenological analysis of the linear meson model by Jungnickel and Wetterich (1996). The results are in overall agreement with an earlier analysis by Ecker, Gasser, Pich, and de Rafael (1989) which hypothesized that the L_i couplings are dominated by scalar and vector meson exchange. The paper enables a precise quantitative estimate of the low-energy couplings L4 through L8 within the linear model framework, and the results have also been compared with estimates from extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models.
