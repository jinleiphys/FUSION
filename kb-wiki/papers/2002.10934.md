# Study of diffusion coefficients of identified particles at energies available at BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

> Using event-by-event fluctuations, we study the diffusion parameters of net-charge, net-pion, net-kaon, and net-proton in the heavy-ion jet interaction generator (HIJING), and ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) models at different collision energies \sqsn available at BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). The diffusion parameter ($σ$) of net-charge and identified particles are estimated in rapidity space at various \sqsn. It is observed that, the $σ$ values are independent of collision energies but emphasises the particle-species dependence of diffusion coefficient in the QGP medium. The present work on particle-species dependence of diffusion coefficient provides a baseline for comparison with the experimental data.

## Key claim
The diffusion parameter (σ) of net-charge and identified particles (net-pion, net-kaon, net-proton) in HIJING and UrQMD models is independent of collision energy from √sNN = 7.7 to 200 GeV, but shows a clear particle-species dependence, with heavier particles exhibiting systematically higher σ values than lighter ones.

## Method
The authors used the HIJING (V.1.37) and UrQMD (V.1.30) event generators to simulate Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 7.7, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV for the 0-5% most central events. They computed the dynamical fluctuation measure ⟨Nch⟩ν(±,dyn) for net-charge, net-pion, net-kaon, and net-proton as a function of pseudorapidity window Δη, and extracted the diffusion coefficient σ by fitting these data with an error function Erf(Δη/√8σ) over specified Δη ranges.

## Key numbers
- Diffusion coefficient σ values for net-charge and net-pion are close to each other at all studied energies (√sNN = 7.7 to 200 GeV)
- σ values for net-proton and net-kaon are systematically higher than net-pion at all energies in both HIJING and UrQMD models
- Fits were performed over Δη ranges: 0.35–3.5 at √sNN = 7.7 GeV, 0.35–4.5 at 19.6 GeV, and 0.35–5.0 at 27–200 GeV
- Beam rapidity is approximately 5.2 at 200 GeV and approximately 2 at 7.7 GeV
- Typical δy_coll for baryon charge is 0.2 and for electric charge is 0.8

## Context
This work extends prior studies of net-charge fluctuation strength and diffusion parameter from transport and hadron resonance gas models (Mishra 2017) and identified particle fluctuations (Singh 2019) by specifically extracting diffusion coefficients for net-pion, net-kaon, and net-proton. The results provide a model baseline for comparison with experimental data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, where baryon chemical potential can reach up to μB ∼ 400 MeV at lower energies, enabling study of charge particle diffusion properties of nuclear matter. The diffusion coefficient matrix for baryon, electric charge, and strangeness has been calculated using kinetic theory (Greif 2017), and diffusion has been studied using net-charge fluctuations in LHC data (Abelev 2012).
