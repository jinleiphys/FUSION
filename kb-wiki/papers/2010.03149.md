# M1 resonance in $^{208}$Pb within the self-consistent phonon-coupling model

> The main goal of the paper is to investigate theoretically the experimentally observed fragmentation of the isovector $M1$ resonance in $^{208}$Pb within a self-consistent model based on an energy-density functional (EDF) of the Skyrme type. This fragmentation (spread of the $M1$ strength) is not reproduced in a conventional one-particle--one-hole ($1p1h$) random-phase approximation (RPA) and thus has to be investigated in the framework of more complicated models. However, previously applied models of this type were not self-consistent. In the present work, we use a recently developed renormalized version of the self-consistent time blocking approximation (RenTBA) in which the $1p1h\otimes$phonon configurations are included on top of the RPA $1p1h$ configurations. We have determined several sets of the parameters of the modified Skyrme EDF fitted within the RenTBA and RPA and have found the necessary condition of producing the fragmentation of the $M1$ resonance in $^{208}$Pb in our model. We present also the results of the RenTBA and RPA calculations for the first excited states of the natural parity modes in $^{208}$Pb obtained with these modified parametrizations.

## Key claim

The self-consistent RenTBA model can reproduce the experimentally observed splitting of the isovector M1 resonance in 208Pb into two main peaks, but only when the spin-related parameters of the Skyrme EDF are tuned to specific values; the fragmentation is not a generic result and depends on the particular parametrization used.

## Method

The authors solved the fully self-consistent random-phase approximation (RPA) and renormalized time blocking approximation (RenTBA) equations using Skyrme energy-density functionals, with wave functions represented on a spherical grid in coordinate space and a box radius of 18 fm. The spin-related EDF parameters (x_W, W_0, g, g') were refitted to reproduce the experimental energy and strength of the 1_1^+ state and the mean energy and summed strength of the M1 resonance in 208Pb, while the phonon space in RenTBA included RPA phonons with energies up to 50 MeV and multipolarities L ≤ 15 selected by a collectivity criterion.

## Key numbers

- The experimental summed M1 strength in 208Pb in the interval 6.6–8.1 MeV is 15.3 μ_N^2, used as a benchmark for fitting.
- The RenTBA with parametrization SKXm_{-0.49} yields summed strengths of 7.6 μ_N^2 below 7.37 MeV and 7.8 μ_N^2 above 7.37 MeV, compared to experimental values of 9.2 μ_N^2 and 6.2 μ_N^2 respectively.
- The theoretical Gaussian width Γ of the M1 strength distribution in the interval 6.6–8.1 MeV is 0.20 MeV, while the experimental width is 0.44 MeV.
- The theoretical mean energy difference ΔĒ = Ē_> - Ē_< is 0.14 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 0.31 MeV.
- The escape widths of the main peaks in the RenTBA calculation with SKXm_{-0.49} are less than 1 keV, as determined from calculations with Δ = 0.1 keV smearing.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent RPA calculations of Tselyaev (2019) for M1 excitations in 208Pb, which reproduced the energy and strength of the 1_1^+ state and the summed M1 strength but could not describe the observed fragmentation. The present study uses the RenTBA framework developed by Tselyaev (2018) to include 1p1h⊗phonon configurations, maintaining full self-consistency. The results show that reproducing the fragmentation requires specific tuning of the spin-orbit parameter x_W to negative values, and that the single-particle continuum has negligible impact on the strength distributions at the 20 keV smearing scale used. The authors note that additional correlations beyond the current model, such as ground-state correlations (Kamerdzhiev, 1991; Kamerdzhiev, 1993a) or two-phonon configurations (Tselyaev, 2007; Soloviev, 1992), may be needed to fully describe the detailed fragmentation structure.
