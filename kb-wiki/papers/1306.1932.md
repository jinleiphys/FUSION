# Finite temperature Mott transition in a nonlocal PNJL model

> We provide a novel calculation of the Mott effect in non-local PNJL models. We find that the "deconfinement" transition temperature in these models is lower than the Mott temperature. Furthermore, the mass and the width of the $σ$ and the $π$ meson modes is calculated with the result that the width in nonlocal models is in general reduced as compared to local models. Difficulties encountered while attempting to "Wick rotate" covariant models are carefully discussed.

# Finite temperature Mott transition in a nonlocal PNJL model

## Key claim

The "deconfinement" transition temperature in nonlocal PNJL models is lower than the Mott temperature, and the width of the σ and π meson modes is reduced compared to local models, but this result is conditional on the presence of complex conjugate mass poles in the quark propagator, which only appear when the gap parameter σ exceeds a critical value σ_c = Λ/√(2e).

## Method

The authors study the Mott transition in a covariant, nonlocal Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (nl-PNJL) model with a Gaussian form factor f(p²) = exp(-p²/Λ²). They compute the spatial (screening) meson masses from the Bethe-Salpeter equation 1 - G_S Π_M(ν_m=0, |q|=-i m_M) = 0 and calculate the meson widths from the imaginary part of the polarization function at rest (q=0), using the relation Γ_M = g_{M\bar{q}q} Im[Π_M]/m_M.

## Key numbers

- The critical gap value for the appearance of complex conjugate mass poles is σ_c = Λ/√(2e), where Λ is the Gaussian form factor cutoff.
- The continuum threshold temperature T_cont is defined by σ(T_cont) = σ_c = Λ/√(2e).
- At T ~ 300 MeV, the width-to-mass ratio for both π and σ mesons is Γ_M/M_M ~ 0.1.
- The light state mass in the chiral limit is given by m_L²(σ) = -(1/2) W₀(-2σ²/Λ²), where W₀ is the principal branch of the Lambert W-function.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the Mott effect in local PNJL models (Hansen 2006) by introducing a nonlocal interaction kernel, finding that nonlocality significantly reduces the meson width. The calculation builds on earlier nl-PNJL model developments (Horvatic 2010, Radzhabov 2010, Hell 2011, Carlomagno 2013) and aims toward a virial expansion approach to quark-hadron transitions (Wergieluk 2012, Yamazaki 2012, Blaschke 2013). The authors identify a key open problem: covariant models with Gaussian regulators produce unphysical singularities that hinder a proper Wick rotation, suggesting that Coulomb gauge formulations (Guo 2009, Pak 2011, Watson 2012) may be more appropriate for fully describing the Mott transition.
