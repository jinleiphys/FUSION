# Unstable Quark-Gluon Plasma at LHC

> Coupling of the quark-gluon plasma from the early stage of heavy-ion collisions is argued to be weaker at LHC than at RHIC. For this reason, the role of instabilities - the pre-equilibrium plasma is unstable with respect to chromomagnetic modes - will be enhanced. The instabilities isotropize the system and speed up the process of equilibration. A possibility to observe direct signals of the instabilities is considered.

# Unstable Quark-Gluon Plasma at LHC

## Key claim

The pre-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma at LHC will be significantly more weakly coupled than at RHIC, making chromomagnetic (Weibel) instabilities much more effective in isotropizing and equilibrating the system; however, the author states that his estimates "do not allow to draw a firm conclusion" that the instabilities will play an important role, only that conditions are "much more preferable" than at RHIC.

## Method

The author estimates the QGP coupling strength at LHC versus RHIC using two approaches: (1) comparing the lattice QCD measure (epsilon_T - 3p)/T^4 at the expected thermal temperatures, and (2) computing the running coupling constant alpha from the initial energy density epsilon_0 using a parameterized formula with Q^2 proportional to sqrt(epsilon_0). The initial momentum anisotropy is quantified via the Landau model of rapidity distribution width, and the growth rate of Weibel instabilities is compared to collisional and expansion timescales.

## Key numbers

- Thermal energy density at RHIC: epsilon_T^RHIC ≈ 30 GeV/fm^3 at T ≈ 350 MeV; at LHC: epsilon_T^LHC ≈ 130 GeV/fm^3 at T ≈ 500 MeV
- Lattice QCD measure (epsilon_T - 3p)/T^4 ≈ 3 at T = 350 MeV (RHIC), reduced to about 1 at T = 500 MeV (LHC)
- Initial energy density estimate: epsilon_0^RHIC ≈ 130 GeV/fm^3 for sqrt(s) = 200 GeV; epsilon_0^LHC ≈ 3600 GeV/fm^3 for sqrt(s) = 5500 GeV
- Running coupling: alpha^RHIC ≈ 0.3; alpha^LHC ≈ 0.16 (using the parameterization with a = 4.12)
- Rapidity distribution width: sigma_y = 2.2 at top RHIC energy; sigma_y = 2.8 at LHC (sqrt(s) = 5500 GeV)
- Anisotropy parameter 2⟨p_L^2⟩/⟨p_T^2⟩ = 2×10^4 at RHIC; 7×10^6 at LHC
- Typical parton momentum from initial energy density: p^RHIC = 1.0 GeV; p^LHC = 2.3 GeV
- Instability growth rate inverse: gamma^{-1} ∼ 0.1 fm/c

## Context

This paper extends the author's earlier work on chromomagnetic Weibel instabilities in heavy-ion collisions (Mrowczynski, 2005) and the proposal that instabilities can explain fast thermalization and low viscosity (Asakawa, 2006). It directly contrasts the LHC regime with RHIC findings, where the equilibrium QGP coupling was inferred as alpha = 0.3 from jet quenching analysis (Qin, 2007). The work enables experimental searches for instability signatures through elliptic flow fluctuations (previously predicted by Mrowczynski, 2002 and 2005), azimuthal fluctuations of radial flow, and jet broadening effects (Majumder, 2006).
