# Velocity-like maximum polarization: irreversibility and quantum measurements

> The polarization emerging in the subsequent scattering processes can never exceed $1$ which corresponds to the fully polarized pure state. This property is shown to be provided by the addition rule similar to that for relativistic velocities never exceeding the speed of light. The cases of spin $1/2$ and $1$ are considered. The photon linear polarization in Thomson scattering is monotonically increasing. This directness is shown to be a consequence of spin measurement procedure and may be the particular example of ithe anticipated relation between quantum measurement and time irreversibility. The emergent polarization may be considered as a case of opposing time's arrows corresponding to microscopic (spin) and macroscopic (momentum) degrees of freedom, respectively.

# Velocity-like maximum polarization: irreversibility and quantum measurements

## Key claim
The polarization emerging in successive scattering processes can never exceed 1 (the fully polarized pure state) because it follows an addition rule mathematically identical to the relativistic velocity-addition formula, which naturally enforces a maximum value. This irreversibility in polarization growth arises from the quantum measurement procedure (projection of the final-state density matrix onto the detector's density matrix) rather than from time-reversal violation in the underlying dynamics.

## Method
The author derives the composition rule for spin-1/2 polarization in non-relativistic elastic scattering of a spin-1/2 beam on a spin-0 target, starting from the standard scattering amplitude with spin-conserving and spin-flip terms. The resulting formula for final polarization as a function of initial polarization and the polarization generated in the scattering is shown to be identical to the relativistic velocity-addition formula, including non-commutativity for non-collinear cases analogous to Thomas precession. The analysis is extended to relativistic Thomson scattering of photons, where the Stokes parameter for linear polarization is shown to obey the same addition rule in the low-frequency limit, and to fermion helicity scattering via a similar generic cross-section structure.

## Key numbers
- For spin-1/2 non-relativistic scattering with initial polarization parallel to the scattering normal, the final polarization is given by P_f = (P + P_0)/(1 + P P_0), where P_0 = 2 Im(a b*)/(|a|^2 + |b|^2) is the polarization generated from unpolarized initial particles.
- For non-collinear spin-1/2 polarization, the squared final polarization is P_f^2 = ((P + P_0)^2 - [P x P_0]^2)/(1 + (P·P_0))^2.
- In Thomson scattering, the cross-section coefficients are F_0 = ω/ω' + ω'/ω - sin^2 θ, F_3 = sin^2 θ, and F_33 = 1 + cos^2 θ.
- The inequality F_33 ≤ F_0 holds, with equality in the limit ω/m → 1 and ω' → ω, giving the exact velocity-like addition rule ξ_3^s = (ξ_3^{s,0} + ξ_3)/(1 + ξ_3^{s,0} ξ_3).
- For 90-degree Thomson scattering, the emergent linear polarization ξ_3 = 1.

## Context
This work extends the author's prior approach to polarization in heavy-ion collisions based on axial vortical effect and quark-hadron duality (Sorin 2016), and the treatment of quantized vortices in pionic superfluid (Teryaev 2017, 2022). The composition rule for polarization generated at different stages of hyperon production connects to earlier work on inclusive polarization (Nazarova 2021) and the suppression of polarization due to randomization of scattering planes (Hoyer 1986). The relation between irreversibility and quantum measurement references the classical textbook discussion in Landau and Lifshitz (1980). The results enable consistent quantum-theoretical treatment of polarization in hadronic collisions (where multiple mechanisms like Sivers and Collins functions contribute) and in heavy-ion collisions (where vorticity and statistical approaches are used), and suggest a framework for opposing time's arrows between microscopic (spin) and macroscopic (momentum) degrees of freedom.
