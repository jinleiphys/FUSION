# Cluster Production with Coalescence and Breakup

> The problem of hadronic cluster production in quark-hadron phase transition in heavy-ion collisions is studied by cellular automata. Previous result on the scaling behavior is extended to include variation in the drift speed. It is also shown that coalescence is more important than growth in generating scaling. A new set of rules is adopted to free the clusters from being rigid. It is found that the scaling exponent is independent of not only the shapes of the clusters, but also the probability of breakup of the clusters. The universality of the scaling behavior is now extended to a wide range of physical properties characterizing the geometry and dynamics of the phase transition process.

# Cluster Production with Coalescence and Breakup

## Key claim
The scaling exponent of hadronic cluster size distributions in quark-hadron phase transition is independent not only of cluster shapes but also of the probability of cluster breakup, provided nucleation probability is sufficiently high (p > 0.05). The universality of scaling behavior is extended to a wide range of physical properties characterizing the geometry and dynamics of the phase transition process, but only when the mixed (M) region is large enough relative to the drift step.

## Method
The authors model the quark-hadron mixed phase in heavy-ion collisions using a 2D cellular automaton on an L x L lattice, where occupied sites represent hadronic clusters and unoccupied sites represent quark phase. They introduce a new approach where each cluster is represented as a stack at a single lattice site with an effective radius R = α√(S/π), allowing for variable cluster shapes via the parameter α. The simulation includes nucleation, growth, drift with random walk, coalescence when cluster effective radii overlap, emission at the right boundary, and spontaneous fission parameterized by a breakup probability B(S, S₁, S₂) = β(√(S₁/S) + √(S₂/S))⁻ᵇ with b=1.

## Key numbers
- Scaling exponent γ = 1.86 ± 0.18 for the standard case (L=16, S₀=1, drift step=1)
- Scaling behavior holds for nucleation probability p in the range 0.05 ≤ p ≤ 0.5
- When drift step is doubled to 2 with L=16, scaling fails for p ≤ 0.05 but is restored when L is doubled to 32
- For α = 1.0 (circular clusters), the scaling exponent is nearly identical to the earlier cellular automaton result
- For α = 1.2 and 1.4, there is a slight increase in slope but very little change; with α = 1.4 the effective area is double that for α = 1.0
- Breakup parameter β varied from 0 to 1.0; at p = 0.01 scaling cannot be achieved even without breakup, and breakup makes it worse
- At p = 0.05, scaling appears for β = 0 but is disrupted by increasing β
- At p = 0.2, breakup has no effect for any β value

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous results (Hwa and Pan, 1994) on scaling behavior in cluster production during quark-hadron phase transition, which was based on the concept of self-organized criticality (Bak, Tang, and Wiesenfeld, 1987). The earlier 1D study (Hwa, Lee, and Pan, 1994) included breakup but the 2D study (Hwa and Pan, 1994) did not. The new cellular automaton with effective radii frees clusters from rigid shapes and allows simple implementation of breakup. The results suggest a possible connection between self-organized criticality in phase transition and percolation in nuclear fragmentation, and provide motivation for further theoretical and experimental study of how hadronic clusters produced at high temperature transform to cold hadrons at the detector.
