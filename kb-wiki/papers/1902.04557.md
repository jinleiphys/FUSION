# Inferring neutron star properties from GW170817 with universal relations

> Because all neutron stars share a common equation of state, tidal deformability constraints from the compact binary coalescence GW170817 have implications for the properties of neutron stars in other systems. Using equation-of-state insensitive relations between macroscopic observables like moment of inertia ($I$), tidal deformability ($Λ$) and stellar compactness, we derive constraints on these properties as a function of neutron-star mass based on the LIGO-Virgo collaboration's canonical deformability measurement, $Λ_{1.4} = 190^{+390}_{-120}$. Specific estimates of $Λ$, $I$, dimensionless spin $χ$, and stellar radius $R$ for a few systems targeted by radio or X-ray studies are extracted from the general constraints. We also infer the canonical neutron-star radius as $R_{1.4} = 10.9^{+1.9}_{-1.5}$ km at 90$\%$ confidence. We further demonstrate how a gravitational-wave measurement of $Λ_{1.4}$ can be combined with independent measurements of neutron-star radii to tighten constraints on the tidal deformability as a proxy for the equation of state. We find that GW170817 and existing observations of six thermonuclear bursters in low-mass X-ray binaries jointly imply $Λ_{1.4} = 196^{+92}_{-63}$ at the 90$\%$ confidence level.

## Key claim

The paper uses equation-of-state insensitive universal relations to translate the GW170817 tidal deformability measurement into constraints on neutron-star properties across different masses, finding that the canonical radius is $R_{1.4} = 10.9^{+1.9}_{-1.5}$ km at 90% confidence, but this inference assumes the universal relations hold for weakly magnetized isolated neutron stars long after formation and binary neutron stars long before merger, and may be violated by nonbarotropic equations of state or strong phase transitions.

## Method

The authors computed binary Love, I-Love, and I-compactness universal relations using 53 unified equations of state from relativistic mean-field theory and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock theory, then applied a Bayesian inference scheme that marginalizes over the gravitational-wave posterior for $\Lambda_{1.4}$ from GW170817 and electromagnetic mass measurements to derive posterior distributions for tidal deformability, moment of inertia, dimensionless spin, and radius as functions of neutron-star mass. They also combined the GW170817 constraint with independent radius measurements from six thermonuclear bursters in low-mass X-ray binaries to produce joint multimessenger constraints on the tidal deformability.

## Key numbers

- $\Lambda_{1.4} = 190^{+390}_{-120}$ (median and symmetric 90% confidence interval) from the LIGO-Virgo collaboration's canonical deformability measurement based on GW170817
- $R_{1.4} = 10.9^{+1.9}_{-1.5}$ km at 90% confidence for the canonical neutron-star radius
- Joint multimessenger constraint from GW170817 and six thermonuclear bursters: $\Lambda_{1.4} = 196^{+92}_{-63}$ at 90% confidence level
- The I-compactness relation fit residuals are at most 3% over the relevant range
- The binary Love relation fractional errors are approximately 1% near $1.4\,M_{\odot}$ and rise to about 50% at the high-mass edge of the relation
- The I-Love relation maximum residuals are no more than 0.6% error over the relevant range of $\Lambda$

## Context

This work extends the technique of Landry (2019) who combined I-Love and binary Love relations to infer the moment of inertia of PSR J0737-3039A from GW170817 constraints. The paper provides general constraints on neutron-star properties as functions of mass, enabling comparison with future moment-of-inertia measurements from the Square Kilometre Array and radius measurements from the NICER observatory. The joint multimessenger analysis demonstrates a simpler alternative to full Bayesian equation-of-state modeling, building on prior work by Annala (2018), Most (2018), and LandryEssick (2019) who placed different constraints on $\Lambda_{1.4}$ using various equation-of-state priors.
