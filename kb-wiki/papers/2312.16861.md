# Search for the 6$α$ condensed state in $^{24}$Mg using the $^{12}\rm{C}+{}^{12}\rm{C}$ scattering

> We searched for the 6$α$-condensed state in $^{24}$Mg by measuring the $^{12}\rm{C}+{}^{12}\rm{C}$ scattering with the SAKRA Si detector array at $E_\rm{cm}$ = 17.5-25.0 MeV. By using the invariant-mass method for the detected 3$α$ particles, the inclusive cross sections for the $^{12}\rm{C}+{}^{12}\rm{C}\to{}^{12}\rm{C}(0^+_2)+X$ and $^{12}\rm{C}(3^-_1)+X$ reactions were determined. In addition, the missing-mass spectroscopy was successfully utilized to determine the excitation energy of the residual $^{12}\rm{C}$ nucleus and the exclusive cross sections for the $^{12}\rm{C}+{}^{12}\rm{C}\to{}^{12}\rm{C}(0^+_2)+{}^{12}\rm{C}(0^+_1)$, $^{12}\rm{C}(0^+_2)+{}^{12}\rm{C}(2^+_1)$, and $^{12}\rm{C}(0^+_2)+{}^{12}\rm{C}(0^+_2)$ reactions. In both the inclusive $^{12}\rm{C}(0^+_2)+X$ channel and the exclusive $^{12}\rm{C}(0^+_2)+{}^{12}\rm{C}(0^+_1)$ channel, the cross section peaked at $E_rm{cm}$ = 19.4 MeV, which correspond to the excitation energy of $E_x$ = 33.3 MeV in $^{24}\rm{Mg}$. This 19.4-MeV state is a candidate for the 6$α$-condensed state because of the agreement of the excitation energy with the theoretical value and its decay property. In the exclusive $^{12}\rm{C}(0^+_2)+{}^{12}\rm{C}(0^+_2)$ channel, a broad state was observed at $E_\rm{cm}$ = 22.5 MeV, which correspond to the excitation energy of $E_x$ = 36.4 MeV in $^{24}\rm{Mg}$. From the angular distribution of the differential cross section, the spin and parity of this 22.5-MeV state was assigned to be $4^+$. In addition, a $2^+$ state was suggested at the low-energy side of the 22.5-MeV state. Because their excitation energies are higher than the theoretical value of the 6$α$-condensed state, these states might be excited states of the 6$α$-condensed state such as the $2^+_2$ and $4^+_1$ states in $^{12}\rm{C}$.

## Key claim

The paper reports a candidate for the 6α-condensed state in ²⁴Mg at E_cm = 19.4 MeV (E_x = 33.3 MeV), but the spin and parity of this state could not be determined due to contamination from continuous background components, so the assignment remains tentative.

## Method

The authors measured ¹²C+¹²C scattering at E_cm = 17.5–25.0 MeV using the SAKRA Si detector array at the JAEA tandem accelerator. They identified 3α particles from the decay of excited ¹²C nuclei via pulse shape analysis, reconstructed the invariant mass of the 3α particles to identify the 0⁺₂ (Hoyle) and 3⁻₁ states in ¹²C, and used missing-mass spectroscopy to determine the excitation energy of the residual ¹²C nucleus and separate exclusive reaction channels.

## Key numbers

- Inclusive cross section for ¹²C+¹²C → ¹²C(0⁺₂)+X peaked at E_cm = 19.4 MeV, corresponding to E_x = 33.3 MeV in ²⁴Mg.
- Inclusive cross section for ¹²C+¹²C → ¹²C(3⁻₁)+X also peaked at E_cm = 19.4 MeV.
- Exclusive cross section for ¹²C+¹²C → ¹²C(0⁺₂)+¹²C(0⁺₁) peaked at E_cm = 19.4 MeV.
- Exclusive cross section for ¹²C+¹²C → ¹²C(0⁺₂)+¹²C(0⁺₂) showed a broad peak at E_cm = 22.5 MeV, corresponding to E_x = 36.4 MeV in ²⁴Mg.
- The spin and parity of the 22.5-MeV state was assigned as 4⁺ from Legendre polynomial fitting of angular distributions.
- A 2⁺ state was suggested at the low-energy side of the 22.5-MeV state.
- The angular acceptance for the ¹²C(0⁺₂)+¹²C(0⁺₁) channel was 44° ≤ θ_cm ≤ 144°, for ¹²C(0⁺₂)+¹²C(2⁺₁) was 52° ≤ θ_cm ≤ 144°, and for ¹²C(0⁺₂)+¹²C(0⁺₂) was 68° ≤ θ_cm ≤ 140°.

## Context

This work extends previous searches for α-condensed states in ²⁴Mg, particularly following the statistically limited inelastic alpha scattering study by Kawabata (2013) and the indirect 3α measurement by Kolata (1980) which observed a peak at E_cm ≈ 19.5 MeV. The present results provide direct detection of 3α particles with invariant-mass determination, enabling identification of decay channels. The candidate 6α-condensed state at 19.4 MeV and the proposed excited states at 22.5 MeV (4⁺) and a suggested 2⁺ state parallel the structure of the Hoyle band in ¹²C (the 2⁺₂ and 4⁺₁ states). The work calls for microscopic theoretical calculations of the 6α-condensed state, which are currently unavailable.
