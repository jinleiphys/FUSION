# Recent progress on superstrange dynamics

> We review the present activities related to hypernuclear production in hadronic in-medium reactions at intermediate energies. The status of theoretical predictions and experimental evidences for the in-medium formation of bound superstrange matter is discussed. Heavy-ion collisions and antiproton-induced reactions at energies close to strangeness production thresholds create the conditions of hypermatter formation. This allows to understand better in-medium hyperon interactions and sets constraints on the strangeness sector of the nuclear equation of state.

## Key claim

The paper reviews theoretical and experimental progress on the formation of bound superstrange matter (multi-strangeness hypernuclei) in hadronic in-medium reactions at intermediate energies, but notes that while single-Λ hypernuclei can probe the |S|=1 hyperon-nucleon sector, the higher strangeness domain (S=-2 and beyond) remains largely unobserved experimentally, with only theoretical predictions available.

## Method

The author describes a hybrid simulation approach combining the Relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) transport equation, solved via the test-particle method, with the Statistical Multi-fragmentation Model (SMM) for clusterization. The transport dynamics are derived from Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) mean-field theory, using the Giessen-BUU (GiBUU) model for simulations of heavy-ion collisions and antiproton-induced reactions. The mean-field for hyperons is constructed from the nucleonic one using SU(3) or SU(6) symmetry arguments, and the collision integral includes exclusive elastic and inelastic channels up to baryonic resonances of 2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear equation of state at high baryon densities up to (2-3) times saturation density was predicted from transport theoretical strangeness production analyses
- For nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction (strangeness content S=0), high precision 4300 scattering data are available
- In the S=-1 sector (hyperon-nucleon), only 38 scattering data are available
- Neutron star masses of 1.97 ± 0.04 M⊙ and 2.01 ± 0.04 M⊙ provide lower bounds for maximum neutron star mass, excluding a soft EoS at high baryon densities
- Heavy-ion collisions at 0.6 GeV beam energy per nucleon (Au+Au) are used as an example for thermodynamic extraction from RBUU calculations

## Context

This review extends the line of work on the nuclear equation of state (EoS) initiated at BEVALAC in the early 1970s and continued through heavy-ion experiments on collective flow and kaon dynamics (FOPI, KAOS collaborations). It addresses the "hyperon puzzle" where nuclear models including hyperons cannot explain observed neutron star masses (Fonseca 2016, Oertel 2016). The work enables future experimental programs including the PANDA experiment at FAIR, J-PARC, HypHI collaboration, STAR at RHIC, ALICE at LHC, and CBM at FAIR for studying multi-strangeness hypernuclear physics.
