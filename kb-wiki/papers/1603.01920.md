# Full jet evolution in quark-gluon plasma and nuclear modification of jet production and jet shape in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 ATeV at the LHC

> We study the evolution of full jet shower in quark-gluon plasma via solving a set of coupled differential transport equations for the three-dimensional momentum distributions of quarks and gluons contained in the full jets. In our jet evolution equations, we include all partonic splitting processes as well as the collisional energy loss and transverse momentum broadening for both the leading and radiated partons of the full jets. Combining with a realistic (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulation for the space-time profiles of the hot and dense nuclear medium produced in heavy-ion collisions, we apply our formalism to calculate the nuclear modification of single inclusive full jet spectra, the momentum imbalance of photon-jet and dijet pairs, and jet shape function (at partonic level) in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 ATeV. The roles of various jet-medium interaction mechanisms on the full jet modification are studied. We find that the nuclear modification of jet shape is sensitive to the interplay of different interaction mechanisms as well as the energies of the full jets.

## Key claim

The authors find that the nuclear modification of jet shape is sensitive to the interplay of different interaction mechanisms as well as the energies of the full jets, with the caveat that their calculation is performed at partonic level and uses a single free parameter (the jet transport coefficient \(\hat{q}_0\)) tuned to reproduce single inclusive jet suppression data.

## Method

The authors solve a set of coupled differential transport equations for the three-dimensional momentum distributions of quarks and gluons within full jets, including all partonic splitting processes (gluon radiation, \(g \to q\bar{q}\)) as well as collisional energy loss and transverse momentum broadening for both leading and radiated partons. They combine this with a (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulation (VISH2+1) for the space-time profiles of the quark-gluon plasma produced in Pb+Pb collisions, using initial jet conditions from PYTHIA simulations tuned to reproduce the jet shape function in p+p collisions at 2.76 TeV.

## Key numbers

* The transport parameter for a quark jet at the center of the medium at initial time \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/c in most central (0-10%) Pb+Pb collisions is extracted as \(\hat{q}_0 \approx 1.7\) GeV\(^2\)/fm.
* A minimum cutoff of \(\omega_{\rm min} = 2\) GeV is imposed for medium-induced radiation, below which partons are assumed to be thermalized and treated as part of the medium.
* The jet-medium interaction is turned off when the local medium temperature drops below \(T_c = 160\) MeV.
* Results are presented for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 ATeV at the LHC, with jet energy cuts of \(p_T > 100\) GeV and \(p_T > 50\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Qin (2010) by including gluon radiation and the \(g \to q \bar{q}\) channel, and by allowing radiated partons to also experience inelastic collisions with the medium after formation. The extracted \(\hat{q}_0\) value is consistent with the range obtained by the JET Collaboration (2013) from comparing five different energy loss models to single inclusive hadron suppression data at RHIC and LHC. The framework enables systematic study of how different jet-medium interaction mechanisms (collisional energy loss, transverse momentum broadening, and medium-induced splitting) contribute to the modification of jet internal structure, particularly the jet shape function.
