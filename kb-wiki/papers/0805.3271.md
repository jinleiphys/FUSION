# Systematic Comparison of Jet Energy-Loss Schemes in a 3D hydrodynamic medium

> We utilize a 3D hydrodynamic model to provide the medium evolution for a systematic comparison of jet energy-loss calculations in the BDMPS/ASW, HT and AMY approaches. We find that the parameters of all three calculations can be adjusted to provide a good description of inclusive data on $R_{AA}$ versus transverse momentum. However, we do observe slight differences in their predictions for the azimuthal angular dependence of $R_{AA}$ vs. $p_T$. We also note that the value of the transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ needed in the three approaches to describe the data differs significantly.

# Systematic Comparison of Jet Energy-Loss Schemes in a 3D Hydrodynamic Medium

## Key claim
All three jet energy-loss schemes (BDMPS/ASW, HT, and AMY) can be tuned to describe inclusive nuclear modification factor data, but they show slight differences in the azimuthal angular dependence of R_AA versus transverse momentum, and the extracted transport coefficient values differ significantly across the approaches.

## Method
The authors used a 3D hydrodynamic model to provide the time evolution of the medium produced at RHIC, then implemented jet energy-loss calculations in the BDMPS/ASW, Higher Twist (HT), and AMY formalisms. They computed the inclusive and azimuthally differential nuclear suppression factor R_AA of pions as a function of transverse momentum p_T, using the same medium evolution, structure functions, and fragmentation functions for all three approaches.

## Key numbers
- For a gluon jet, the extracted transport coefficient values are: HT approach requires q̂_0 ≈ 3.4 GeV²/fm, ASW formalism requires q̂_0 ≈ 20 GeV²/fm, and AMY approach uses α_S ≈ 0.33 (convertible to q̂_0 ≈ 5.5 GeV²/fm)
- The ASW value for q̂_0 at τ = 0.6 fm/c and ε_0 = 55 GeV/fm³ lies a factor of 3.6 higher than the Baier estimate for an ideal QGP (q̂ ≈ 2·ε^(3/4))
- The HT calculation lies about a factor of 1.6 below the Baier ideal QGP estimate
- AMY and HT exhibit the same peak-to-valley ratio for out-of-plane versus in-plane R_AA, though absolute R_AA values differ by approximately 10%
- The ASW calculation systematically shows a stronger azimuthal dependence than HT and AMY

## Context
This work extends earlier jet-quenching studies that used simplified one- or two-dimensional Bjorken expansion models, building on the 3D hydrodynamic evolution code of Nonaka (2006). Previous calculations by Hirano (2002, 2003) estimated 3D expansion effects on R_AA but used a simplified energy-loss treatment with linear dE/dx dependence. The systematic comparison enables future work to understand why the extracted transport coefficient q̂_0 values differ by up to a factor of 6 between approaches, with a factor of two attributable to different scaling prescriptions (temperature versus energy-density) for coupling the medium to the transport coefficient.
