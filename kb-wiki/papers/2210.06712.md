# Far-from-equilibrium attractors with a realistic non-conformal equation of state

> Using anisotropic hydrodynamics, we examine the existence of early-time attractors of non-conformal systems undergoing Bjorken expansion. In the case of a constant mass, we find that the evolution of the scaled longitudinal pressure is insensitive to variations of initial conditions converging onto an early-time universal curve and eventually merging with the late-time Navier-Stokes attractor (the hydrodynamic attractor). On the other hand, the bulk and the shear viscous corrections do not show an early-time attractor behavior. These results are consistent with previous studies considering a constant mass. When a realistic equation of state is included in the dynamics with a thermal mass, we demonstrate for the first time the absence of strict late-time universal attractors. However, a semi-universal feature of the evolution at very late times remains.

# Far-from-equilibrium attractors with a realistic non-conformal equation of state

## Key claim

Using anisotropic hydrodynamics for systems undergoing Bjorken expansion, the author demonstrates that when a realistic non-conformal equation of state with a thermal mass is included, strict late-time universal attractors are absent; however, a semi-universal feature of the evolution at very late times remains, with a spread of approximately 10% in the scaled longitudinal pressure at scaled time tau-bar = 5.

## Method

The author solves the 0+1D quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP) dynamical equations for a boost-invariant system undergoing Bjorken expansion, using a relaxation-time approximation collision kernel with a non-conformal relaxation time tau_eq(T). The thermal mass m(T) is obtained by tuning to the equation of state from lattice QCD calculations via the trace anomaly, and the background field B is evolved consistently to maintain thermodynamic stability. For comparison, constant-mass cases are also solved using the same anisotropic hydrodynamics framework.

## Key numbers

- For constant mass m = 200 MeV with initial temperature T0 = 500 MeV and 4 pi eta/s = 10, trajectories of scaled longitudinal pressure P_L/P converge onto an early-time universal curve within a scaled time tau/tau_eq ~ 1, then merge with the late-time Navier-Stokes attractor.
- For the thermal mass case with T0 = 600 MeV and 4 pi eta/s = 2, the scaled longitudinal pressure solutions at tau/tau_eq = 5 differ by approximately 10% when initial times and anisotropy parameters are varied.
- The constant mass test cases reproduce results from prior work (Alqahtani et al., 2015) for pressure anisotropy and scaled bulk pressure using constant tau_eq = 0.5 fm/c.
- The conformal relaxation time approximation tau_eq = 5 eta / (4 P_eq) = 5 bar-eta / T is shown to be a good approximation for small masses when m/T < 1, but non-conformal corrections become important at later times as temperature decreases.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of far-from-equilibrium attractors in conformal systems (Romatschke, 2017; Strickland, 2018) and non-conformal systems with constant mass (Alqahtani et al., 2021; Jaiswal et al., 2022) to the case of a realistic temperature-dependent mass from lattice QCD. The constant-mass results agree with exact kinetic theory solutions (Alqahtani et al., 2021; Jaiswal et al., 2022), while the thermal-mass case reveals that the strict attractor found in simpler models becomes a semi-universal band. This finding motivates future exact solutions of the Boltzmann equation with thermal masses and comparisons with modified anisotropic hydrodynamics approaches (Alqahtani et al., 2021).
