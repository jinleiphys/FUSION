# Geometric Quantum Information Structure in Quantum Fields and their Lattice Simulation

> An upper limit to distillable entanglement between two disconnected regions of massless non-interacting scalar field theory has an exponential decay defined by a geometric decay constant. When regulated at short distances with a spatial lattice, this entanglement abruptly vanishes beyond a dimensionless separation, defining a negativity sphere. In two spatial dimensions, we determine this geometric decay constant between a pair of disks and the growth of the negativity sphere toward the continuum through a series of lattice calculations. Making the connection to quantum field theories in three-spatial dimensions, assuming such quantum information scales appear also in quantum chromodynamics (QCD), a new relative scale may be present in effective field theories describing the low-energy dynamics of nucleons and nuclei. We highlight potential impacts of the distillable entanglement structure on effective field theories, lattice QCD calculations and future quantum simulations.

## Key claim

The exponential decay of distillable entanglement between disconnected regions of massless scalar field theory is controlled by a purely geometric constant that increases with spatial dimension, and when the field is regulated on a lattice this entanglement abruptly vanishes beyond a finite separation (the "negativity sphere"), with the sphere radius scaling with region size in a dimension-dependent way.

## Method

The logarithmic negativity between two circular regions of a massless non-interacting scalar field in two spatial dimensions was computed using the symplectic spectrum of the partially transposed covariance matrix, constructed from two-point vacuum expectation values of the field and conjugate momentum operators. The field was discretized on a spatial lattice with two different pixelation schemes (integer-truncated and half-integer-truncated boundaries) to approach the continuum limit, and the eigenvalues of the matrix product \(GH^\Gamma\) were evaluated with high-precision arithmetic. The exponential decay constant \(\beta_{2D}\) was extracted from the negativity as a function of separation \(\tilde{r}/d\) and extrapolated to the continuum limit.

## Key numbers

- The geometric decay constant for the exponential suppression of logarithmic negativity in two spatial dimensions is \(\beta_{2D} = 5.29(4)\).
- In one spatial dimension, the previously calculated decay constant is \(\beta_{1D} = 2.82(3)\).
- The negativity sphere radius in two dimensions scales as \(\tilde{r}_{\mathcal{N}}/d_{lat} \sim 0.59(4) d_{lat}\).
- In one dimension, the negativity sphere radius scales as \(\tilde{r}_{\mathcal{N}}/d_{lat} \sim 1.118(1) d_{lat}\).
- For a 2D lattice with spacing \(a \sim 0.1\) fm and nucleon radius set by the chiral symmetry breaking scale (\(r_\chi \sim 0.2\) fm), the negativity sphere radius is \(\tilde{r}_{\mathcal{N}} \sim 0.8\) fm with logarithmic negativity \(\mathcal{N} \sim 10^{-7}\).
- For the same lattice spacing but nucleon radius set by the pion mass (\(r_\chi \sim 1.4\) fm), the negativity sphere radius is \(\tilde{r}_{\mathcal{N}} \sim 47\) fm with \(\mathcal{N} \sim 1 \times 10^{-40}\).

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of entanglement in harmonic chains and scalar field theory on lattices (Srednicki 1993, Audenaert 2002, Botero 2004, Marcovitch 2008, Calabrese 2009, 2012) by determining the geometric decay constant in two spatial dimensions and quantifying the finite-volume "negativity sphere" effect. The results motivate future non-perturbative computation of \(\beta_{3D}\) in both scalar field theory and QCD, and suggest that an entanglement-derived scale may influence the convergence of low-energy effective field theories for nuclear forces (Weinberg 1990, 1991, Kaplan 1998, Epelbaum 2019) and the design of lattice QCD calculations and quantum simulations of field theories.
