# Nuclear energy functional with a surface-peaked effective mass: Global properties

> A correction to the nuclear functional is proposed in order to improve the density of states around the Fermi surface. The induced effect of this correction is to produce a surface-peaked effective mass, whose mean value can be tuned to get closer to 1 for the states close to the Fermi energy. In this work we study the effect of the correction term on global properties of nuclei such as the density of states in $^{40}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb, pairing and specific heat at low temperature in $^{120}$Sn. In the latter application, an explicit temperature-dependent form of the correction term is employed and it is shown that the critical temperature is reduced by 40-60~keV.

# Nuclear energy functional with a surface-peaked effective mass: Global properties

## Key claim
The authors propose a correction term added to the Skyrme energy density functional that produces a surface-peaked effective mass, whose mean value can be tuned to approach 1 for states near the Fermi energy, thereby improving the density of states. The correction is energy-independent and designed to have only a moderate effect on the mean field, with the caveat that the Skyrme parameters are not refitted in this exploratory study.

## Method
The authors add an isoscalar correction term to the standard Skyrme energy density functional, consisting of a term proportional to τ(∇ρ)² that induces a surface-peaked effective mass and a second term proportional to ρ²(∇ρ)² that moderates the effect on the mean field. They solve self-consistent Kohn-Sham equations for spherical nuclei using the BSk14 interaction, studying the correction's impact on the density of states in ⁴⁰Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb, and on pairing properties in ¹²⁰Sn within a finite-temperature HF+BCS framework using a density-dependent contact pairing interaction.

## Key numbers
- The effective mass at the surface increases up to about 1.2–1.3 for the maximum strength of the correction term used (C₀^{τ(∇ρ)²} = -800 MeV fm¹⁰).
- The coefficient C₀^{τ(∇ρ)²} is varied from 0 to -800 MeV fm¹⁰; values with |C₀^{τ(∇ρ)²}| larger than 800 MeV fm¹⁰ produce instabilities in the Hartree-Fock iterations.
- The proportionality constant between the two correction coefficients is set to 12 fm, with reasonable values lying in the range 10–20 fm.
- For the pairing interaction at T=0 in ¹²⁰Sn, the average neutron pairing gap is fixed to 1.3 MeV; the renormalized pairing strengths for C₀^{τ(∇ρ)²} = -400 MeV fm¹⁰ are (η=0: -248 MeV fm³), (η=0.5: -362 MeV fm³), (η=1: -670 MeV fm³).
- The temperature-dependent correction reduces the critical temperature by 40–60 keV, with the ratio T_c/Δ̃_n(T=0) varying from approximately 0.55 (volume interaction) to 0.51 (surface interaction) when using the temperature-dependent prescription.
- The typical scale T₀ for the exponential temperature dependence of the correction coefficient is 2 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Ma and Wambach (1983), who parametrized the ω-mass as a gradient of the nuclear profile within a shell model, by implementing a surface-peaked effective mass directly in the energy density functional framework. The authors compare their results to those of Zalewski et al. (2010), who studied similar correction terms with a different Skyrme interaction (SkX) and a refitting procedure. The paper enables future studies of temperature-dependent nuclear properties, particularly the critical temperature of pairing which could be extracted experimentally as suggested by Guttormsen (2001) and Schiller (2001), and motivates consistent treatment of the surface-peaked effective mass with induced pairing interactions in non-empirical approaches.
