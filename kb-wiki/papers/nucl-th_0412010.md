# Relativistic continuum-continuum coupling in the dissociation of halo nuclei

> A relativistic coupled-channels theory for the calculation of dissociation cross sections of halo nuclei is developed. A comparison with non-relativistic models is done for the dissociation of $^{8}$B projectiles. It is shown that neglecting relativistic effects leads to seizable inaccuracies in the extraction of the astrophysical S-factor for the proton+beryllium radiative capture reaction.

## Key claim
Neglecting relativistic effects in coupled-channels calculations of halo-nuclei dissociation leads to inaccuracies of 3-15% in cross sections, which may require re-evaluation of astrophysical S-factors extracted from Coulomb breakup experiments at high bombarding energies.

## Method
The author develops a relativistic continuum discretized coupled-channels (RCDCC) method based on the Klein-Gordon equation with a potential that transforms as the time-like component of a four-vector, using the eikonal approximation. The method is applied to the dissociation of ⁸B projectiles on lead targets at 50, 80, and 250 MeV/nucleon, with the continuum discretized into energy bins and including s, p, d, and f partial waves. The results are compared to non-relativistic calculations (with Lorentz factor γ set to unity) and to data from the RIKEN and GSI experiments.

## Key numbers
- At 50 MeV/nucleon, non-relativistic potentials yield cross sections 3-6% lower than the full RCDCC calculation.
- At 83 MeV/nucleon, the difference between relativistic and non-relativistic calculations is 4-9%.
- At 250 MeV/nucleon, the relativistic correction factor Δ = (σ_RCDCC - σ_CDCC)/σ_CDCC reaches 14.6% for a relative energy E = 1 MeV between proton and ⁷Be.
- At 250 MeV/nucleon, Δ = 5.3% for E = 0.1 MeV and Δ = 6.9% for E = 2 MeV.
- At 50 MeV/nucleon, Δ = 1.5% for E = 0.1 MeV, 4.2% for E = 1 MeV, and 3.4% for E = 2 MeV.
- At 80 MeV/nucleon, Δ = 3% for E = 0.1 MeV, 5.5% for E = 1 MeV, and 4.1% for E = 2 MeV.
- The RCDCC and direct solution of the Schrödinger equation (DSSE) methods agree to within less than 2% for impact parameters b > 30 fm.

## Context
This work extends the continuum discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method introduced by Rawitscher (1974) and applied to unstable nuclei by Bertulani and collaborators (1992), by incorporating relativistic dynamics that were previously neglected in dynamical calculations. The paper enables more accurate extraction of the astrophysical S-factor for the ⁷Be(p,γ)⁸B reaction from Coulomb breakup experiments at facilities like GSI (254 MeV/nucleon), and suggests that previous analyses by Davids et al. (2001) and Schumann et al. (2003) may need revision. The comparison with the DSSE method of Bertulani and Baur (1993) validates both approaches for large impact parameters.
