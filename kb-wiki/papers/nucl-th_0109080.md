# Extended van Royen-Weisskopf formalism for lepton-antilepton meson decay widths within non-relativistic quark models

> The classical van Royen-Weisskopf formula for the decay width of a meson into a lepton-antilepton pair is modified in order to include non-zero quark momentum contributions within the meson as well as relativistic effects. Besides, a phenomenological electromagnetic density for quarks is introduced. The meson wave functions are obtained from two different models: a chiral constituent quark model and a quark potential model including instanton effects. The modified van Royen-Weisskopf formula is found to improve systematically the results for the widths, giving an overall good description of all known decays.

# Extended van Royen-Weisskopf formalism for lepton-antilepton meson decay widths within non-relativistic quark models

## Key claim

The classical van Royen-Weisskopf formula for meson leptonic decay widths is modified to include non-zero quark momentum contributions and relativistic effects, plus a phenomenological electromagnetic density for quarks; the modifications systematically improve the theoretical results for all known decays, but the calculated widths for D-wave mesons remain much smaller than experimental values, indicating that further considerations such as tensor forces are needed.

## Method

The authors start from the second-order Feynman diagram for quark-antiquark annihilation into a virtual photon that produces a lepton-antilepton pair, using non-relativistic meson wave functions augmented with relativistic Dirac spinors. They derive a modified expression replacing the wave function at the origin with a term that includes an integral over the momentum-space wave function weighted by relativistic factors, and further introduce a Gaussian electromagnetic density for constituent quarks to account for their finite size. Meson wave functions are obtained from two distinct non-relativistic quark models: a chiral constituent quark model (χQM) and a quark potential model with instanton effects (DNR), with parameters fixed to other observables (meson spectra and NN interaction).

## Key numbers

- The modification to the van Royen-Weisskopf results for S-waves gives an effect of order 10%–30% depending on the state.
- For the DNR potential, the fitted electromagnetic size parameters are (σ₀, m₀) = (0.6 GeV, 1.25 GeV); for χQM they are (0.7 GeV, 2.11 GeV).
- The DNR potential systematically gives a wave function at the origin that can exceed experimental widths by a factor of 3 in the van Royen-Weisskopf formula.
- For heavy quarkonia, the non-zero momentum and relativistic corrections give a negative contribution between 10% and 30%, smaller than the 60% difference reported in light-front dynamics calculations by the Bonn group.
- The ratio of ρ to ω widths is expected to be 9 from flavour content, but the experimental value is approximately 11; DNR gives identical wave functions for both, while χQM gives m_ρ > m_ω and Ψ(0)_ρ < Ψ(0)_ω, making the ratio even smaller than 9.

## Context

This work extends the classical van Royen-Weisskopf formula (Royen and Weisskopf, 1960s) by incorporating momentum-dependent and relativistic corrections previously considered by the Bonn group (Beylich et al.) and by light-front dynamics approaches (Lou et al.). It also introduces a phenomenological quark electromagnetic size motivated by vector-meson dominance and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model studies of Vogl et al. The results are compared with two non-relativistic quark models that had previously been applied to meson strong decays (Blanco et al., 2000) and baryon spectra; the improved formalism enables parameter-free tests of meson wave functions through leptonic decays, which are more sensitive than energy spectra alone.
