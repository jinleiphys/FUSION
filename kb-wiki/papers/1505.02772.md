# Directional detection of dark matter in universal bound states

> It has been suggested that several small-scale structure anomalies in $Λ$CDM cosmology can be solved by strong self-interaction between dark matter particles. It was shown by Braaten and Hammer that the presence of a near threshold S-wave resonance can make the scattering cross section at nonrelativistic speeds come close to saturating the unitarity bound. This can result in the formation of a stable bound state of two asymmetric dark matter particles (we call the bound state as darkonium). Laha and Braaten studied the nuclear recoil energy spectrum in dark matter direct detection experiments due to this incident bound state. Here we study the angular recoil spectrum, and show that it is uniquely determined up to normalization by the S-wave scattering length. Observing this angular recoil spectrum in a dark matter directional detection experiment will uniquely determine many of the low-energy properties of dark matter independent of the underlying dark matter microphysics.

## Key claim

The angular recoil spectrum from darkonium (a bound state of two asymmetric dark matter particles) scattering with a nucleus in a directional detection experiment is uniquely determined in shape by the S-wave scattering length, up to an overall normalization that requires a complete underlying theory.

## Method

The author derives analytical expressions for the double differential scattering rate (with respect to nuclear recoil energy and solid angle) for three processes: elastic scattering of a single dark matter particle, elastic scattering of darkonium, and break-up scattering of darkonium into its two constituent dark matter particles. The calculation uses nonrelativistic quantum mechanics with universal bound-state formalism, where the S-wave scattering length governs the low-energy dynamics, and the laboratory-frame rates are obtained by boosting Galactic-frame expressions using a Maxwellian dark matter velocity distribution with standard parameters (v_E = 242 km/s, v_max = 600 km/s, v_0 = 230 km/s).

## Key numbers

- For dark matter particle mass m = 100 GeV and self-interaction cross section sigma_el/m = 1 cm^2 g^{-1} at relative velocity v = 10 km/s, the darkonium binding energy is 0.52 keV, and the bound state breaks apart during collision with the nucleus.
- For dark matter particle mass m = 10 GeV and sigma_el/m = 1 cm^2 g^{-1} at v = 10 km/s, the darkonium binding energy is 52 keV, and the bound state does not break apart during collision with the nucleus.
- The spin-dependent cross section with the proton is taken as sigma_SD^p = 10^{-39} cm^2 for normalization purposes (arbitrary overall scale).
- The minimum dark matter speed required for darkonium break-up scattering is given by v_min = (gamma^2/(m q) + q/(2 mu_2) + v_E cos(theta_{v_E q}))/cos(theta_{vq}), where gamma = 1/a is the inverse S-wave scattering length.

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear recoil energy spectra from darkonium scattering in direct detection experiments by Laha and Braaten (2013) to the angular recoil spectrum relevant for directional detection. It builds on the universal bound-state framework of Braaten and Hammer (2013), which showed that a near-threshold S-wave resonance can make dark matter self-interaction cross sections saturate the unitarity bound. The results enable future directional detection experiments to determine low-energy dark matter properties (binding energy, self-interaction cross section) from the observed angular recoil spectrum shape, independent of the underlying dark matter microphysics.
