# Effects of the particle-particle channel on properties of low-lying vibrational states

> Making use of the finite rank separable approach for the quasiparticle random phase approximation enables one to perform nuclear structure calculations in very large two-quasiparticle spaces. The approach is extended to take into account the residual particle-particle interaction. The calculations are performed by using Skyrme interactions in the particle-hole channel and density-dependent zero-range interactions in the particle-particle channel. To illustrate our approach, we study the properties of the lowest quadrupole states in the even-even nuclei $^{128}$Pd, $^{130}$Cd, $^{124-134}$Sn, $^{128-136}$Te and $^{136}$Xe.

## Key claim

Extending the finite-rank separable quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) to include the residual particle-particle (p-p) interaction reduces the collectivity of low-lying quadrupole states, but the effect is modest for nuclei near closed shells and becomes more important for nuclei far from stability; the approach reproduces the general trend of experimental energies and B(E2) values but systematically overestimates them, suggesting that coupling to two-phonon components is needed for quantitative agreement.

## Method

The authors start from Hartree-Fock (HF) plus BCS calculations using the SLy4 Skyrme interaction for the particle-hole (p-h) channel and a surface-peaked density-dependent zero-range force for pairing. They then construct the residual interaction in both p-h and p-p channels by taking second derivatives of the energy density functional and approximating the p-h part by its Landau-Migdal form keeping only l=0 terms. The residual interaction is reduced to a finite-rank separable form using a Gauss integration over the radial coordinate, which allows the QRPA equations to be solved in a space whose matrix dimensions never exceed 6N x 6N (with N=45 integration points) regardless of the two-quasiparticle configuration space size.

## Key numbers

* Pairing strength V0 = -940 MeV fm^3, fitted to reproduce odd-even mass differences for both protons and neutrons in the region.
* Energy cut-off for the active pairing space: Delta E = 10 MeV above the Fermi level, with width parameter mu = 0.5 MeV.
* For 130Te, including the p-p channel without cut-off gives E(2+_1) = 1.15 MeV and B(E2 up) = 4000 e^2 fm^4; with the cut-off these become 1.27 MeV and 3600 e^2 fm^4, compared to the p-h-only values of 1.49 MeV and 3400 e^2 fm^4.
* In 132Sn, the main neutron configuration {2f7/2,1h11/2} contributes about 61% and the main proton configuration {2d5/2,1g9/2} contributes about 33% to the 2+_1 state.
* In 134Sn, the leading neutron configuration {2f7/2,2f7/2} contributes about 96% of the wave function.
* In 134Te, the dominant proton configuration {1g7/2,1g7/2} gives about 65% of the wave function, while neutron configurations contribute less than 3%.
* (Mn/Mp)/(N/Z) ratios for the first 2+_1 states: 0.47 for 128Pd, 0.49 for 130Cd, 0.81 for 132Sn, 0.54 for 134Te, 0.55 for 136Xe.

## Context

This work extends the finite-rank separable QRPA approach of Severyukhin et al. (2002) and Severyukhin et al. (2004) by adding the residual particle-particle interaction, building on the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) of Soloviev. The method enables calculations in very large configuration spaces that were previously impractical. The results for Sn and Te isotopes and N=82 isotones around 132Sn are compared with experimental data from Radford et al. (2002, 2005) and Raman et al. (2001), and with earlier QRPA calculations using Skyrme forces by Terasaki et al. (2006) and Colo et al. (2003) and Gogny forces by Giamatiempo et al. (2003). The predictions for N=82 isotones below 132Sn are relevant for stellar nucleosynthesis studies as noted by Jungclaus et al. (2007).
