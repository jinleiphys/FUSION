# Comparison of Models of Critical Opacity in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> In this work we discuss two methods of calculation of quark propagation in the quark-gluon plasma. Both methods make use of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. The essential difference of these calculations is the treatment of deconfinement. A model of confinement is not included in the work of Gastineau, Blanquier and Aichelin [hep-ph/0404207], however, the meson states they consider are still bound for temperatures greater than the deconfinement temperature T_c. On the other hand, our model deals with unconfined quarks and includes a description of the q(bar)q resonances found in lattice QCD studies that make use of the maximum entropy method (MEM). We compare the q{bar)q cross sections calculated in these models.

# Comparison of Models of Critical Opacity in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim

The two models compared both use the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) framework but differ fundamentally in their treatment of deconfinement: the model of Gastineau, Blanquier and Aichelin retains bound meson states above the deconfinement temperature, while the authors' model uses only unconfined quarks and incorporates $q\overline{q}$ resonances extracted from lattice QCD via the maximum entropy method (MEM), leading to different predictions for the quark mean-free path.

## Method

The authors compare two NJL-based calculations of $q\overline{q}$ scattering cross sections in the quark-gluon plasma. In the model of Gastineau, Blanquier and Aichelin, the scattering amplitude includes $s$, $t$, and $u$-channel exchange of $\pi$ and $K$ mesons with temperature-dependent masses, using scalar and pseudoscalar meson propagators of the form $\mathcal{D}^{S,P} = G_{S,P}/(1 - G_{S,P} J^{S,P})$. The authors' own model instead uses a simplified NJL Lagrangian with $N_f=1$, replaces the Gell-Mann matrices by unity, and calculates the total cross section via a Breit-Wigner parametrization of resonances, summing over scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector channels with a statistical factor $N=8$, using a Gaussian regulator rather than a sharp cutoff.

## Key numbers

- Deconfinement temperature is $T_c \sim 170$ MeV in unquenched QCD calculations and $T_c = 270$ MeV in quenched calculations.
- The model of Gastineau, Blanquier and Aichelin calculates $q\overline{q}$ interactions for temperatures in the range $0$ to $350$ MeV.
- The authors' model uses a Gaussian regulator (rather than the sharp cutoff typical of NJL models) and a statistical factor $N = \sum_J (2J+1) = 8$ for the sum over scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector resonances.
- Quark mean-free path results are shown for antiquark chemical potentials $\mu = 1.1$ GeV, $1.3$ GeV, and $1.5$ GeV (Fig. 4).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier studies (Li and Shakin, 2003-2004) of hadronic current correlation functions and quark mean-free paths in the deconfined phase, and directly contrasts with the approach of Gastineau, Blanquier and Aichelin (2004), who retain bound meson states above $T_c$. The authors' model is closer in spirit to the work of the Stony Brook group (Rapp et al., 1998-2001), who also reference lattice QCD data to argue for very small quark mean-free paths. The comparison enables assessment of how the treatment of deconfinement affects predictions for critical opacity and transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma formed in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC.
