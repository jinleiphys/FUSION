# Study of Collective Phenomena in Heavy-Ion Collisions Using CMS Open Data

> In this work, we present preliminary results from a measurement of the recently proposed observable, $v_0(p_T)$, in lead-lead (PbPb) collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76 \text{ TeV}$, using public data from the CMS Open Data portal. This observable is directly sensitive to radial flow and characterizes the transverse momentum ($p_T$) dependence of radial flow fluctuations, serving as probe of collective phenomena in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formed in heavy-ion collisions. Consistently with the ATLAS results at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02 \text{ TeV}$, we observe the three key features of collective radial flow: long-range correlations in pseudorapidity ($η$), a centrality-independent shape as a function of $p_T$, and factorization in $p_T$. The results presented in this work are generally compatible, within uncertainties, with the ATLAS measurements at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\text{ TeV}$ reported in Phys. Rev. Lett. 136, 032301 (2026).

## Key claim

The authors present preliminary results for the newly proposed observable \(v_0(p_T)\) in lead-lead collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV using CMS Open Data, finding compatibility within uncertainties with ATLAS measurements at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV; however, the analysis is restricted to events with collision centrality greater than 50% due to data availability, and the results are preliminary with statistical uncertainties only.

## Method

The analysis uses approximately three million minimum-bias PbPb events at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV from the CMS 2011 data, selecting charged-particle tracks with \(0.5 < p_T < 10\) GeV and \(|\eta| < 2.4\). The observable \(v_0(p_T)\) is constructed from the normalized covariance between the fraction of tracks in a given \(p_T\) bin and the event mean transverse momentum, using a 2-subevent method with a pseudorapidity gap to suppress non-flow correlations. The factorization assumption is tested by varying the \(p_T^{ref}\) range used to compute the global amplitude \(v_0\) and the event mean \(p_T\).

## Key numbers

- The analysis uses approximately three million minimum-bias PbPb events at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV.
- Selected tracks satisfy \(0.5 < p_T < 10\) GeV and \(|\eta| < 2.4\).
- The default parameters for the main results are \(\eta_{gap}=1\) and \(p_T^{ref}\) range within \(0.5\text{-}2.0\) GeV.
- The observable \(v_0(p_T)/v_0\) shows centrality independence at low \(p_T\) for the two available centrality ranges (50-60% and >60%).
- The zero-crossing point of \(v_0(p_T)v_0\) occurs at approximately the same \(p_T\) value for both collision energies due to similar \(\langle p_T \rangle\) values.

## Context

This work extends the ATLAS measurement of \(v_0(p_T)\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV (ATLAS Collaboration, Phys. Rev. Lett. 136, 032301, 2026) to a lower collision energy of 2.76 TeV using CMS Open Data, providing a cross-experiment validation of the observable. The results confirm the three key features of collective radial flow (long-range correlations in pseudorapidity, centrality-independent shape, and factorization in \(p_T\)) at a different energy, and the authors plan to incorporate systematic uncertainties, increase the event sample, and compare with theoretical predictions at 2.76 TeV.
