# Electroweak form factors of large nuclei as BPS skyrmions

> We employ the Bogomolnyi-Prasad-Sommerfield (BPS) Skyrme model within the framework of semi-classical quantization to compute both electromagnetic and neutral current form factors for heavy nuclei. Our results show excellent agreement with the experimental data for low- to moderate momentum transfer. Further, we present an analytic expression of the neutral current form factor for generic nuclei, expressed as a power series in the momentum transfer. Our method provides an alternative to existing phenomenological approaches, and is particularly relevant for precision neutrino experiments where control over model-dependent systematics is essential for probing physics beyond the Standard Model.

## Key claim

The BPS Skyrme model, despite being a drastic simplification that eliminates propagating pion degrees of freedom and predicts zero classical binding energies, reproduces experimental electromagnetic and neutral current form factors for heavy nuclei with excellent agreement for low- to moderate momentum transfer, provided that small quantum and Coulomb corrections are added to achieve realistic binding energies.

## Method

The authors employ the BPS submodel of the generalized Skyrme model, which consists of a potential term and a term proportional to the square of the baryon current, and use the pion mass potential V(ξ) = 1 − cos ξ. They introduce an axially symmetric ansatz for the Skyrme field, solve the first-order BPS equation analytically to obtain a compacton solution with radius R_B, then perform rigid rotor semi-classical quantization to introduce spin and isospin degrees of freedom. The electroweak charge densities are computed as expectation values of the vector and baryon current operators, and the form factors are obtained by spatial Fourier transform of these densities, with an analytic power series expansion in momentum transfer for the neutral current case.

## Key numbers

- Compacton radius: R_B = 1.3599 × B^(1/3) fm, where B is the baryon number
- The isospin moment of inertia for the pion mass potential: I_3 = (64π/105) × (μ^2/B^2) × R_B^5
- The weak neutral current form factor moments for any nucleus with quantum numbers (B, i_3) are given analytically, with Q_NC = (1/2)(s^2_W B − (1−2s^2_W)i_3) where s^2_W = 0.223
- The electromagnetic form factor moments are given analytically, with Q_EM = B/2 + i_3
- The Taylor series approximation for form factors shows very good agreement assuming terms up to n = 10
- Agreement with experimental data is demonstrated for momentum transfers up to approximately 150 MeV

## Context

This work extends the BPS Skyrme model approach previously developed by Adam et al. (2013) for nuclear binding energies and by GarciaMartin-Caro et al. (2023) for electroweak form factors. It provides an alternative to phenomenological approaches such as the Klein-Nystrand form factor (Klein, 1999) and the Helm form factor (Helm, 1956), as well as to more computationally intensive microscopic methods like density functional theory (Patton, 2012), relativistic mean field methods (Yang, 2019), coupled-cluster theory (Payne, 2019), and shell-model calculations (AbdelKhaleq, 2024). The analytic single-parameter formula enables rapid computation of form factors for precision neutrino experiments such as CEνNS and PVES, where controlling model-dependent systematics is essential for probing physics beyond the Standard Model.
