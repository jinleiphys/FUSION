# Short-range and tensor correlations in 4He and 8Be studied with antisymmetrized quasi cluster model

> We apply tensor version of antisymmetrized quasi cluster model (AQCM-T) to 4He and 8Be while focusing on the NN correlations in alpha clusters. We adopt the NN interactions including realistic ones containing a repulsive core for the central part in addition to the tensor part. In 4He, the pn pair in the 3D channel has been known to play a decisive role in the tensor correlation and the framework is capable of treating not only this channel but also the NN correlations in the 1S and 3S channels. In 8Be, when two alpha clusters approach, the 3D pair is suppressed because of the Pauli blocking effect, which induces the decrease of the 3S component through the 3S-3D coupling. This effect results in the reduction of the attractive effect of the central-even interaction in the middle-range region.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the antisymmetrized quasi cluster model with tensor correlations (AQCM-T) can simultaneously treat short-range correlations from the repulsive core of the central interaction and tensor correlations in alpha clusters, but the framework is less satisfactory for the G3RS1-3R interaction with a 2000 MeV repulsive core height because the ansatz of correlating only a single NN-pair may not hold for extremely hard cores.

## Method

The authors apply the AQCM-T framework to 4He and 8Be, using Gaussian wave packets with complex conjugate centroid parameters to generate correlated NN-pair wave functions containing S-wave and D-wave components. For 4He, they perform a generator coordinate method (GCM) calculation superposing basis states with momentum parameters k = 0.5 to 5.5 fm−1 across three channels (1S, 3S, and 3D), using realistic G3RS interactions with a repulsive core. For 8Be, they place a correlated alpha cluster (αk) and an uncorrelated (0s)4 alpha cluster (α0) at variable distances d = 1 to 8 fm and angles θα, performing both full GCM and fixed-d calculations to separate internal excitation from external potential effects.

## Key numbers

- For 4He with G3RS2-3R interaction: binding energy and radius are "reasonably reproduced" (exact numerical values not given in truncated text)
- For 4He with G3RS1-3R interaction: results are "not satisfactory" due to the 2000 MeV repulsive core height
- For 8Be with G3RS2-3R interaction: the energy difference between calculations with and without θα=0 is 0.03 MeV at d=6 fm
- For 8Be calculations: 200 basis states (V2m-3R) or 392 basis states (G3RS2-3R) are used, corresponding to 584 or 1032 Slater determinants respectively
- The width parameter ν is set to 0.25 fm−2 and fixed for all calculations
- For 4He: 11 basis states with k = 0.5, 1.0, ..., 5.5 fm−1 are adopted for each channel
- For 8Be: eight distance values d = 1, 2, ..., 8 fm are adopted, with dmax = 8 fm near the Coulomb barrier position

## Context

This work extends the AQCM-T framework previously introduced by the same group (Itagaki et al., 2017) to include short-range correlations from the repulsive core of realistic NN interactions, going beyond effective interactions like Volkov No.2 that lack repulsive cores. The study builds on earlier cluster model developments including the Brink alpha-cluster model (Brink, 1966), antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (Kanada-En'yo, 1995), and fermionic molecular dynamics with unitary correlation method (Neff, Roth, Chernykh). The finding that tensor suppression contributes to alpha-alpha repulsion at short distances is consistent with pioneering works by Bando (1970), the quantum Monte Carlo approach (QMC), and Yamamoto (1977). The results enable future studies of how short-range and tensor correlations modify cluster structures in heavier nuclei beyond 8Be.
