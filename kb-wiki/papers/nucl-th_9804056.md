# Pion Correlations and Two-Particle Bound States

> A method to investigate possible loosely bound dibaryon states is suggested. The momentum dependence of a two-pion correlation function is shown to depend on the (non)existence of a bound state.

## Key claim

The momentum dependence of the two-pion correlation function can reveal the existence of a loosely bound dibaryon state, but only if the bound state's spatial extent is large enough that the relative momentum of the emitted pions scans the wave function of the baryon pair; the method is specifically applicable to states with widths too large to appear as resonance peaks.

## Method

The author models a bound state of two baryon resonances (e.g., a Delta-Delta system) as heavy sources of pions in the large-Nc limit, applying an adiabatic approximation that separates the fast Delta-to-nucleon-pion decay from the slow relative motion of the two baryons. Using the formalism of two-particle correlations from heavy-ion collisions, the two-pion correlation function is derived in terms of the spatial distribution of the pion-emitting sources. The observable correlation function is obtained by averaging over the bound-state wave function of the baryon pair, yielding an expression that depends on the relative momentum of the two pions and the binding momentum of the bound state.

## Key numbers

- The candidate dibaryon resonance from pionic double charge exchange has a width Gamma less than or approximately 0.5 MeV.
- A naive estimate for a Delta-Delta bound state width gives Gamma greater than or approximately 2 * Gamma_Delta, approximately 300 MeV.
- For an S-wave bound state with wave function phi(R) = sqrt(kappa/(2pi)) * exp(-kappa R)/R, the correlation function is C = 1 + arctan(chi)/chi, where chi = q/(2kappa) and q is the relative pion momentum.
- For small relative momentum (chi << 1), the correlation function approaches C approaches 2 (100% interference).
- For large relative momentum (q >> 1/R_bar), the correlation function approaches C approaches 1.

## Context

This work extends the technique of pion interferometry, routinely applied in heavy-ion collisions to extract source sizes, to the search for dibaryon bound states. The only established dibaryon is the deuteron, while a candidate from pionic double charge exchange has been suggested (DCX reference). The method addresses the problem of observing bound states of baryon resonances (such as a Delta-Delta bound state) that would have widths too large to appear as resonance peaks, unlike the narrow candidate from double charge exchange. The formalism builds on the two-particle correlation approach developed by the authors cited as "co" and uses the large-Nc approximation from the work cited as "ma".
