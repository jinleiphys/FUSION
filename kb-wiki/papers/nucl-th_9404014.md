# Critical Enhancement of the In-medium Nucleon-Nucleon Cross Section at low Temperatures

> The in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section is calculated starting from the thermodynamic T-matrix at finite temperatures. The corresponding Bethe-Salpeter-equation is solved using a separable representation of the Paris nucleon-nucleon-potential. The energy-dependent in-medium N-N cross section at a given density shows a strong temperature dependence. Especially at low temperatures and low total momenta, the in-medium cross section is strongly modified by in-medium effects. In particular, with decreasing temperature an enhancement near the Fermi energy is observed. This enhancement can be discussed as a precursor of the superfluid phase transition in nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section at low temperatures and zero total momentum exhibits a resonance-like enhancement that diverges at a critical temperature, which is identical to the critical temperature for the onset of superfluidity in symmetric nuclear matter as given by the Thouless criterion.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the thermodynamic T-matrix in the ladder approximation using a separable representation of the Paris nucleon-nucleon potential. The calculation incorporated two in-medium effects: Pauli blocking via the factor \(1-f(\epsilon(k_1))-f(\epsilon(k_2))\) and quasiparticle energy renormalization from the self energy, both evaluated within the Matsubara-Green's function technique at finite temperature. The in-medium total cross section was then obtained from the on-shell T-matrix after a partial wave decomposition up to \(L=2\).

## Key numbers

- At density \(n=0.5 n_0\) (with saturation density \(n_0=0.17\ \text{fm}^{-3}\)) and zero total momentum, the in-medium cross section diverges at a critical temperature \(T_c=4.5\ \text{MeV}\) at laboratory energy \(E_{\text{LAB}}=90\ \text{MeV}\).
- At density \(n=0.2 n_0\) and zero total momentum, the critical temperature is \(T_c=4.2\ \text{MeV}\) with the divergence at \(E_{\text{LAB}}=46\ \text{MeV}\).
- For temperatures above \(T=20\ \text{MeV}\) at \(n=0.5 n_0\) and \(K=0\), the in-medium cross section approaches the free cross section.
- For a total momentum \(K=400\ \text{MeV}/c\) at \(n=0.5 n_0\) and \(T=5\ \text{MeV}\), the in-medium cross section approaches the free cross section.

## Context

This work extends non-relativistic Brueckner-theory calculations of the in-medium cross section at zero temperature by Faessler (1989) and Bohnet et al. (1989) to finite temperatures, and contrasts with relativistic Dirac-Brueckner calculations by ter Haar and Malfliet (1987) and Li and Machleidt (1993) which did not find such an enhancement. The results enable the inclusion of temperature-dependent in-medium cross sections in BUU simulations of heavy-ion reactions, and directly link the cross-section divergence to the Thouless criterion for superfluidity, as previously applied to nuclear matter by Alm et al. (1993) and Baldo et al. (1990).
