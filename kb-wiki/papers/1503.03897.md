# Anisotropic particle production and azimuthal correlations in high-energy pA collisions

> We summarize some recent ideas relating to anisotropic particle production in high-energy collisions. Anisotropic gluon distributions lead to anisotropies of the single-particle azimuthal distribution and hence to disconnected contributions to multi-particle cumulants. When these dominate, the four-particle elliptic anisotropy $c_2\{4\}$ changes sign. On the other hand, connected diagrams for $m$-particle cumulants are found to quickly saturate with increasing $m$, a ``coherence'' quite unlike conventional ``non-flow'' contributions such as decays. Finally, we perform a first exploratory phenomenological analysis in order to estimate the amplitude ${\cal A}$ of the $\cos(2φ)$ anisotropy of the gluon distribution at small $x$, and we provide a qualitative prediction for the elliptic asymmetry from three-particle correlations, $c_2\{3\}$.

## Key claim

Anisotropic gluon distributions in the target nucleus can generate azimuthal asymmetries in particle production that produce disconnected contributions to multi-particle cumulants; when these dominate, the four-particle elliptic anisotropy $c_2\{4\}$ changes sign, but this requires the anisotropy amplitude ${\cal A}$ to exceed a parametric threshold of order $1/N_c^{3/2}$ relative to the connected contributions.

## Method

The authors compute the S-matrix for dipole scattering off a semi-classical color field in the eikonal approximation, expanding to leading order in the dipole radius $r$. They separate the target field ensemble into subclasses with a preferred anisotropy direction $\hat a$, characterized by an amplitude ${\cal A}$ for the $\cos(2\varphi)$ modulation of the electric field correlator. They then evaluate connected and disconnected diagrams for $m$-particle cumulants $c_2\{m\}$, including a detailed calculation of the three-particle cumulant $c_2\{3\}$, and perform a numerical Monte-Carlo simulation of the McLerran-Venugopalan model on a lattice to extract the Fourier harmonics of the dipole S-matrix.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale is determined numerically as $Q_s \approx 0.7125 \, g^2 \mu$ from the condition $\langle{\cal S}_\rho\rangle(r = \sqrt{2}/Q_s) = e^{-1/2}$.
- The four-particle cumulant changes sign when ${\cal A} \sim 1/N_c^{3/2}$ parametrically, with the connected contribution scaling as $1/({\cal A}^4 N_c^6)$ relative to the disconnected term.
- The two-particle cumulant factorizes when ${\cal A} > N_c^{-1}$, while the four-particle cumulant factorizes when ${\cal A} > N_c^{-3/2}$.
- The three-particle cumulant $c_2\{3\}$ at high transverse momentum scales as $1/k_3^2$ when the S-matrix is expanded to order $r_3^4$, with the disconnected contribution proportional to ${\cal A}^4/2$ and the connected contributions proportional to ${\cal A}^2/(2(N_c^2-1))$ and $1/(16(N_c^2-1)^2)$.
- The large-$m$ limit of the connected harmonics gives $\lim_{m\to\infty} |v_2\{m\}| = j_{0,1} / [2 N_D (N_c^2-1)]$, where $j_{0,1}$ is the first zero of the Bessel function $J_0$.

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate framework for small-$x$ QCD to explain azimuthal anisotropies observed in p+Pb collisions at the LHC (ALICE, ATLAS, CMS collaborations) and d+Au collisions at RHIC, where elliptic asymmetries persist up to $p_\perp = 10$ GeV (ATLAS, 2014). It builds on the McLerran-Venugopalan model (1994) and the JIMWLK evolution equation to show that angular fluctuations of the target color fields constitute slow variables that must be averaged over only after computing multi-particle cumulants, unlike conventional isotropic averaging. The results enable phenomenological extraction of the anisotropy amplitude ${\cal A}$ from experimental data and provide a framework to distinguish these QCD-based correlations from conventional non-flow effects such as resonance decays or jet fragmentation.
