# Nuclear Physics and Astrophysics of Neutrino Oscillations

> For a long time very little experimental information was available about neutrino properties, even though a minute neutrino mass has intriguing cosmological and astrophysical implications. This situation has changed in recent decades: intense experimental activity to measure many neutrino properties took place. Some of these developments and their implications for astrophysics and cosmology are briefly reviewed with a particular emphasis on neutrino magnetic moments and collective neutrino oscillations

# Nuclear Physics and Astrophysics of Neutrino Oscillations

## Key claim
The paper reviews recent progress in neutrino physics, emphasizing that while the three-flavor mixing framework is now well-established with precise measurements of the mixing angle θ₁₃, open questions remain, particularly the reactor antineutrino anomaly (a ~5% flux deficit and a ~5 MeV spectral bump) which may or may not be explained by sterile neutrinos, and the impact of neutrino magnetic moments and collective oscillations on astrophysical and cosmological processes.

## Method
The paper surveys experimental results from reactor neutrino experiments (Daya Bay, RENO, Double Chooz) that measured the mixing angle θ₁₃ and the mass-squared difference δm²ₑₑ, discusses the reactor antineutrino anomaly and sterile neutrino searches, reviews experimental and astrophysical limits on neutrino magnetic moments from reactor recoil experiments and globular cluster cooling, and presents the theoretical formalism for collective neutrino oscillations using the neutrino flavor isospin algebra and Bethe ansatz solutions for the many-neutrino Hamiltonian.

- Daya Bay simultaneous fit to neutron capture on hydrogen and gadolinium: sin²2θ₁₃ = 0.082 ± 0.004
- RENO measurement: |δm²ₑₑ| = [2.62⁺⁰·²¹₋₀·₂₃ (stat.) ⁺⁰·¹²₋₀·₁₃ (syst.)] × 10⁻³ eV² and sin²2θ₁₃ = 0.082 ± 0.009 (stat.) ± 0.006 (syst.)

- Planck collaboration value N_eff = 3.30 ± 0.27 yields limit on Majorana magnetic moment: μ_Majorana ≤ 6 × 10⁻¹⁰ μ_B (Vassh 2015)

## Context
This review extends the discussion of neutrino oscillations beyond the standard three-flavor framework, addressing the reactor antineutrino anomaly first noted by Mention (2011) and the possibility of sterile neutrinos hinted by LSND and MiniBooNE experiments (Conrad 2013). It connects neutrino magnetic moments to astrophysical constraints from red giant cooling in globular clusters (Viaux 2013) and Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (Morgan 1981), and presents the theoretical framework for collective neutrino oscillations in core-collapse supernovae, which is essential for understanding r-process nucleosynthesis sites, whether core-collapse supernovae or neutron star mergers (Argast 2003, Ji 2016). The work enables future searches for sterile neutrinos and improved modeling of neutrino transport in supernovae, with upcoming facilities like the Thirty Meter Telescope and LIGO gravitational wave detections of neutron star mergers.
