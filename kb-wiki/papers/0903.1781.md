# The role of the background in the extraction of resonance contributions from meson-baryon scattering

> The separation of background and resonance contributions in pion-nucleon scattering is an often discussed issue. We investigate to what extent the background can be separated from the pole contribution. For illustration we use results from an analytic model for the meson-baryon interaction derived from meson exchange. We focus on the two distinct cases of an elastic and a highly inelastic resonance, namely the Delta(1232) and the Delta*(1700). Our results are also relevant for studies of dynamically generated resonances and attempts to extract bare quantities from hadronic models to be compared to quark model results.

## Key claim
The separation of a meson-baryon scattering amplitude into a pole part (resonance) and a non-pole part (background) is model dependent and has no unique physical meaning; the non-pole part can contain dynamically generated poles that are systematically cancelled when the genuine pole term is added, making the naive identification of the non-pole part as "background" questionable.

## Method
The authors use the Jülich coupled-channel meson-exchange model for pion-nucleon scattering, which solves a Lippmann-Schwinger-type scattering equation. They decompose the full amplitude into a pole part (from s-channel baryon resonances) and a non-pole part (from meson exchange and u-channel processes), then perform Laurent expansions around resonance poles to extract the residue and constant term. They compare the full amplitude with two approximations: one adding the non-pole part to the pole residue term, and one using only the Laurent expansion up to the constant term.

## Key numbers
- For the Δ(1232) P33 resonance, the pole position is at z0 = 1218 + 45i MeV.
- The non-pole part T^NP for the Δ(1232) has a dynamically generated pole at z̃0 = 1074 + 229i MeV.
- The ratio |(T^NP + a0^P)/T^NP| for the Δ(1232) is 0.4, indicating strong cancellation between the pole and non-pole contributions to the constant term.
- For the Δ*(1700) D33 resonance, the pole position is at z0 = 1637 - 118i MeV, and the ratio is 1.3, indicating little cancellation.
- The renormalization of the bare vertex for the Δ(1232) is 45%, with |(Γ_D - γ_B)/γ_B| = 0.45 at the pole position.
- The ratio |(g - Γ_D)/g| for the Δ(1232) is 0.40, where g = √(a_{-1}).

## Context
This work extends the long-standing discussion of background separation in pion-nucleon partial wave analyses, such as those by Arndt (2006) and Cutkosky (1979). It directly addresses the question of whether bare quantities from hadronic models can be compared to quark model results, as discussed by Leenew and Svarcundress. The results are relevant for unitarized chiral perturbation theory studies of dynamically generated resonances, such as those by Kaiser (1995), Inoue (2001), and Nieves (2001), showing that genuine pole terms can strongly repel dynamically generated poles and alter the interpretation of resonance nature.
