# Hadronization conditions in relativistic nuclear collisions and the QCD pseudo-critical line

> We compare the reconstructed hadronization conditions in relativistic nuclear collisions in the nucleon-nucleon centre-of-mass energy range 4.7-2760 GeV in terms of temperature and baryon-chemical potential with lattice QCD calculations, by using hadronic multiplicities. We obtain hadronization temperatures and baryon chemical potentials with a fit to measured multiplicities by correcting for the effect of post-hadronization rescattering. The post-hadronization modification factors are calculated by means of a coupled hydrodynamical-transport model simulation under the same conditions of approximate isothermal and isochemical decoupling as assumed in the statistical hadronization model fits to the data. The fit quality is considerably better than without rescattering corrections, as already found in previous work. The curvature of the obtained "true" hadronization pseudo-critical line kappa is found to be 0.0048 +- 0.0026, in agreement with lattice QCD estimates; the pseudo-critical temperature at vanishing mu_B is found to be 164.3+-1.8 MeV.

## Key claim

The authors reconstruct the hadronization conditions (the "latest chemical equilibrium point") in relativistic nuclear collisions by correcting statistical hadronization model fits for post-hadronization rescattering, and find that the curvature of the resulting pseudo-critical line in the temperature–baryon-chemical-potential plane agrees with lattice QCD estimates, but only when the lowest-energy data point (AGS at 4.75 GeV) is excluded from the fit.

## Method

The authors perform statistical hadronization model (SHM) fits to measured hadron multiplicities from central Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions at five center-of-mass energies (4.75, 7.6, 8.7, 17.3, and 2760 GeV). They correct the SHM yields using modification factors calculated from a hybrid hydrodynamical-transport (UrQMD) simulation, where the hydrodynamic decoupling is set at an approximately isothermal hypersurface corresponding to a fixed energy density. The resulting "true" hadronization points (latest chemical equilibrium points, LCEPs) are then fitted to a quadratic parametrization of the QCD pseudo-critical line to extract the curvature parameter kappa and the pseudo-critical temperature at zero baryon-chemical potential.

## Key numbers

* Pseudo-critical temperature at vanishing baryon-chemical potential: 164.3 ± 1.8 MeV (from the 4-point fit excluding the AGS point)
* Curvature of the pseudo-critical line (4-point fit): kappa = 0.0048 ± 0.0026
* Curvature of the pseudo-critical line (5-point fit including AGS): kappa = 0.0111 ± 0.0055
* Pseudo-critical temperature from the 5-point freeze-out fit (without afterburning corrections): 162.1 ± 4.4 MeV
* Chi-squared per degree of freedom for the LHC point (2760 GeV) with afterburning corrections: 4.7/8
* Chi-squared per degree of freedom for the LHC point without afterburning corrections: 15.2/8
* Decoupling temperature used for the LHC point in the hybrid simulation: 165 MeV
* Decoupling temperature used for the AGS point (4.75 GeV): 135 MeV
* Modification factor for antiprotons at the AGS energy: 0.341
* Modification factor for antiprotons at the LHC energy: 0.754

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Becattini et al., 2012, 2013, 2015) by implementing an improved isothermal decoupling condition in the hybrid hydrodynamical-transport simulation, yielding better fit quality than both plain SHM fits and earlier non-isothermal afterburning corrections. The extracted curvature is compared with multiple lattice QCD calculations (Kaczmarek et al., 2014; HotQCD collaboration, 2014; Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration, 2010, 2012, 2014; Pisa group, 2014; Bari group, 2014) and shows preference for the lower curvature estimates. The results enable future experiments (NA61 at SPS, NICA, FAIR) to probe whether the lowest-energy point at 4.75 GeV lies below the deconfinement onset, and may constrain the location of the QCD critical point.
