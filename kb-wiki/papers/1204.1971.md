# Nonequilibrium Phase Transitions and a Nonequilibrium Critical Point from Anti-de Sitter Space and Conformal Field Theory Correspondence

> We find novel phase transitions and critical phenomena that occur only outside the linear-response regime of current-driven nonequilibrium states. We consider the strongly-interacting (3+1)-dimensional N=4 large-Nc SU(Nc) supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory with a single flavor of fundamental N=2 hypermultiplet as a microscopic theory. We compute its nonlinear non-ballistic quark-charge conductivity by using the AdS/CFT correspondence. We find that the system exhibits a novel nonequilibrium first-order phase transition where the conductivity jumps and the sign of the differential conductivity flips at finite current density. A nonequilibrium critical point is discovered at the end point of the first-order regime. We propose a nonequilibrium steady-state analogue of thermodynamic potential in terms of the gravity-dual theory in order to define the transition point. Nonequilibrium analogues of critical exponents are proposed as well. The critical behavior of the conductivity is numerically confirmed on the basis of these proposals. The present work provides a new example of nonequilibrium phase transitions and nonequilibrium critical points.

## Key claim

The paper reports the discovery of first-order and second-order nonequilibrium phase transitions between negative differential conductivity (NDC) and positive differential conductivity (PDC) in a current-driven strongly coupled gauge theory plasma, occurring only outside the linear-response regime. The transitions and a nonequilibrium critical point are found using the AdS/CFT correspondence, but the author notes that the microscopic theory is an idealized supersymmetric gauge theory not realized in nature, chosen for the well-established nature of its holographic dual rather than for direct experimental relevance.

## Method

The authors study the nonlinear, non-ballistic quark-charge conductivity of (3+1)-dimensional N=4 large-Nc SU(Nc) supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory with a single flavor of fundamental N=2 hypermultiplet, using the AdS/CFT correspondence. The gravity dual is a D3-D7 system: the gluon sector is mapped to a five-dimensional AdS-Schwarzschild black hole (providing a heat bath at temperature T), and the quark sector is mapped to a probe D7-brane embedded in this geometry. The nonlinear conductivity is computed by solving the D7-brane equations of motion for the embedding function θ(z) and the U(1) gauge field A_x, imposing reality of the D7-brane action to determine the relation between current density J and electric field E. A nonequilibrium analogue of thermodynamic potential is proposed using the Hamiltonian density of the D7-brane in Minkowski signature, and nonequilibrium analogues of critical exponents are defined and numerically extracted.

## Key numbers

* The critical temperature for the second-order transition is T_c = 3.4365 × 10^{-1} (in units where m_q = 1 and natural units c = ħ = k_B = 1).
* The critical current density at the critical point is J_c = 1.86 × 10^{-2} (in the same units).
* The critical exponent β for the conductivity difference along the first-order transition line is β = 0.52 ± 0.03.
* The critical exponent δ̃ for the conductivity along the T = T_c line is δ̃ = 3.1 ± 0.2.
* Preliminary values for the chiral condensate exponents are β_chiral ∼ 0.4 and δ̃_chiral ∼ 3.

## Context

This work extends the earlier finding of smooth crossover between NDC and PDC in the same system by Nakamura (2010) to a regime where the transition becomes first-order, with a critical point at its endpoint. The author notes that such current-driven nonequilibrium phase transitions between NDC and PDC have not been reported experimentally or theoretically before. The proposed formalism for a nonequilibrium thermodynamic potential and critical exponents provides a framework for analyzing nonequilibrium steady-state phase transitions, and the results suggest possible universality with liquid-gas transitions and Mott insulator-to-metal transitions (referencing Papanikolaou and others). The work enables future studies of the relationship between the proposed thermodynamic potential and steady-state thermodynamics, as well as construction of phenomenological Landau-type models for nonequilibrium phase transitions.
