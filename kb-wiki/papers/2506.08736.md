# Microscopic analysis of the giant monopole resonance excitation energy

> A systematic study of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) in a wide range of nuclei from various isotopic chains is performed within the microscopic self-consistent Skyrme HF+BCS method and coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM). The calculations for the nuclear incompressibility are based on the Brueckner and Barcelona-Catania-Paris-Madrid (BCPM) energy density functionals for nuclear matter using the capability of the CDFM to make a transition to the corresponding incompressibility in finite nuclei. The results obtained by applying of different definitions of the ISGMR energy, as well as the two energy-density functionals, are analyzed and compared with the available experimental data. The consideration includes the peculiarities of the proton and neutron density distributions and their corresponding linear size characteristics. In general, a connection with the measured neutron skin thicknesses is proposed as a possible way for realistic estimations of the energy of ISGMR.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM), combined with Skyrme HF+BCS density distributions and either the Brueckner or BCPM energy-density functional, can reproduce the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) centroid energies across a wide range of nuclei from Ca to Pb, but the agreement with experimental data depends strongly on which functional and which definition of the ISGMR energy is used, with the Brueckner functional performing better for lighter nuclei and BCPM(v) for heavier ones.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Skyrme HF+BCS calculations with the SLy4 interaction to obtain proton and neutron density distributions for even-even nuclei from Ca to Pb. They then used the CDFM weight function, derived from these densities, to integrate nuclear-matter incompressibility (from either the Brueckner or BCPM energy-density functional) into finite-nucleus incompressibility K^A. The ISGMR centroid energy was calculated using two definitions: one involving a radius parameter r_0 and the other using the mean-square mass radius from the HF+BCS densities.

## Key numbers

- For Ca-40, the Brueckner functional gives E_ISGMR = 19.76 MeV (experiment: 19.18 ± 0.37 MeV), while BCPM(v) gives 24.92 MeV
- For Ni-68, Brueckner gives E_ISGMR = 16.80 MeV (experiment: 21.1 ± 1.9 MeV), while BCPM(v) gives 21.74 MeV
- For Sn-124, Brueckner gives E_ISGMR = 13.80 MeV (experiment: 15.3 ± 0.1 MeV), while BCPM(v) gives 17.87 MeV
- For Pb-208, Brueckner gives E_ISGMR = 11.34 MeV (experiment: 13.96 ± 0.2 MeV), while BCPM(v) gives 14.93 MeV
- The symmetry energy S^A for Pb-208 is 28.91 MeV (Brueckner) and 30.20 MeV (BCPM(v)), compared to 31.3 ± 0.8 MeV from chiral EFT (Sammarruca 2022)
- The slope parameter L^A_sym for Pb-208 is 33.27 MeV (Brueckner) and 54.47 MeV (BCPM(v)), compared to 52.6 ± 4.0 MeV from chiral EFT (Sammarruca 2022)
- The neutron skin thickness from PREX-II for Pb-208 is 0.283 ± 0.071 fm, and from CREX for Ca-48 is 0.121 ± 0.026 ± 0.024 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CDFM study of ISGMR in Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopes (Gaidarov 2023) by adding the BCPM energy-density functional alongside the Brueckner functional, and by expanding the nuclear range from Ca-40 to Pb-208. The results are compared with experimental data from multiple groups (Anders 2013, Howard 2019, Button 2017, Cao 2012, Youngblood 2004) and with chiral EFT predictions (Sammarruca 2022). The paper proposes using measured neutron skin thicknesses from PREX-II and CREX as an alternative route to determine the rms radii needed for ISGMR energy calculations, which could reduce uncertainties from theoretical neutron density distributions.
