# Nucleation rate of color superconducting droplets in protoneutron stars

> We analyse the nucleation of quark matter droplets under protoneutron star conditions. We adopt a two-phase framework in which the hadronic phase is described through a non-linear Walecka model and the just deconfined matter by the MIT bag model including color superconductivity. Surface tension and curvature energy are calculated self-consistently within the MRE formalism. We impose flavour conservation during the transition, which means that the just deconfined quark droplet is transiently out of equilibrium with respect to weak interactions. Our results show that trapped neutrinos slightly increase the critical density for deconfinement and that color superconductivity significantly decreases such density at low temperatures. We also show that the nucleation rate is negligible for droplets larger than 100-200 fm and is huge for smaller droplets provided that the temperature is low enough. We compare our results with previous calculations using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with color superconductivity and the MIT bag model without color superconductivity. We conclude that the deconfinement transition should be triggered instantaneously when a density slightly larger than the bulk transition density is reached at some layer of a protoneutron star. Since color superconductivity lowers the transition density at low temperatures, the transition is likely to occur after significant cooling in a massive enough protoneutron star.

## Key claim

The nucleation rate of quark-matter droplets in protoneutron stars is negligible for droplets larger than about 100–200 fm but huge for smaller droplets at low enough temperature, implying that the deconfinement transition should occur instantaneously when a density slightly above the bulk transition density is reached, provided the star is massive enough and has cooled significantly.

## Method

The authors model the hadronic phase with a non-linear Walecka model including the baryon octet, electrons, and electron neutrinos in weak equilibrium, and the just-deconfined quark phase with the MIT bag model including u, d, and s quarks, electrons, and electron neutrinos. Flavor conservation is imposed during the transition (so the quark droplet is transiently out of beta equilibrium), and the most likely configuration is taken to be the two-flavor color superconductor (2SC) state. Finite-size effects of the droplet are treated via the multiple reflection expansion (MRE) formalism, which yields self-consistent surface tension and curvature energy as functions of the thermodynamic state, and the nucleation rate is computed from the free energy barrier.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the color-superconducting gap is \(T_c = 0.57 \Delta_0\) (e.g., for \(\Delta_0 = 100\) MeV, \(T_c \approx 57\) MeV).
- Surface tension values are \(\sigma \lesssim 18\ \mathrm{MeV\ fm}^{-2}\) and curvature energy \(\zeta \sim 4\)–\(12\ \mathrm{MeV\ fm}^{-1}\), which are an order of magnitude smaller than those obtained with the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model in Lugones and Grunfeld (2011).
- The nucleation rate \(\Gamma\) is negligible for droplets larger than \(R^* \sim 100\)–\(200\) fm and huge for smaller droplets at temperatures as low as \(T = 1\) MeV.
- The bag constant is set to \(B = 100\), \(160\), and \(353\ \mathrm{MeV/fm}^3\); the strange quark mass is \(m_s = 150\) MeV; the gap parameter \(\Delta_0 = 100\) MeV is used in most calculations.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quark-matter nucleation in protoneutron stars by incorporating color superconductivity and self-consistent finite-size effects within the MIT bag model, comparing directly with results from the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model in Lugones and Grunfeld (2011) and with earlier MIT bag model calculations without color superconductivity (e.g., Logoteta et al.). The finding that the nucleation rate is negligible above a few hundred fm but huge below it, and that color superconductivity lowers the transition density at low temperatures, enables a clearer prediction of when and where deconfinement occurs in a cooling protoneutron star, suggesting the transition is triggered after significant cooling in sufficiently massive stars.
