# On mass polarization effect in three-body systems

> We evaluate the mass polarization term of the kinetic-energy operator for different three-body nuclear $AAB$ systems by employing the method of Faddeev equations in configuration space. For a three-boson system this term is determined by the difference of the doubled binding energy of the $AB$ subsystem $2E_{2}$ and the three-body binding energy $E_{3}(V_{AA}=0)$ when the interaction between the identical particles is omitted. In this case: $\left\vert E_{3}(V_{AA}=0)\right\vert >2\left\vert E_{2}\right\vert$. In the case of a system complicated by isospins(spins), such as the kaonic clusters $ K^{-}K^{-}p$ and $ppK^{-}$, the similar evaluation impossible. For these systems it is found that $\left\vert E_{3}(V_{AA}=0)\right\vert <2\left\vert E_{2}\right\vert$. A model with an $AB$ potential averaged over spin(isospin) variables transforms the later case to the first one. The mass polarization effect calculated within this model is essential for the kaonic clusters. Besides we have obtained the relation $|E_3|\le |2E_2|$ for the binding energy of the kaonic clusters.

## Key claim

For three-body nuclear AAB systems, the mass polarization term of the kinetic-energy operator can be evaluated as the difference between twice the two-body AB binding energy and the three-body binding energy when the AA interaction is omitted, but this evaluation is impossible for systems with isospin- or spin-dependent interactions such as kaonic clusters, where a model with an averaged AB potential must be used instead.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations in configuration space for three-body AAB systems with two identical particles, using an s-wave partial wave approach with spin-isospin dependent potentials. For systems complicated by isospin or spin degrees of freedom, such as the kaonic clusters K⁻K⁻p and ppK⁻, they introduced an effective AB potential averaged over spin or isospin variables to reduce the system to a bosonic-like form. The calculations employed the Akaishi-Yamazaki (AY) and Hyodo-Weise (HW) effective potentials for kaon-nucleon and kaon-kaon interactions, and the Malfliet-Tjon MT I-III potential for nucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Key numbers

- For the ΛΛα system (⁶_ΛΛHe) with the TH(M) Λα potential: E₂ = -3.12 MeV, E₃(V_ΛΛ=0) = -6.49 MeV, δB = 0.25 MeV, giving δB/|E₃| = 3.8%.
- For the ΛΛα system with the TH potential: E₂ = -3.03 MeV, E₃(V_ΛΛ=0) = -6.335 MeV, δB = 0.275 MeV, giving δB/|E₃| = 4.3%.
- For the ΛΛα system with the Isle potential: E₂ = -3.10 MeV, E₃(V_ΛΛ=0) = -6.341 MeV, δB = 0.141 MeV, giving δB/|E₃| = 2.2%.
- For the K⁻K⁻p system with AY potentials (m_K = 493.667 MeV): E₂ = -30.3 MeV, E₃ = -31.66 MeV (with V_KK ≠ 0), E₃(V_KK=0) = -35.18 MeV.
- For the K⁻K⁻p system with HW potentials (m_K = 493.667 MeV): E₂ = -11.16 MeV, E₃(V_KK=0) = -12.18 MeV (no bound state with V_KK ≠ 0).
- For the K⁻K⁻p system with averaged AY potential: E₂ᵃᵛ = -9.63 MeV, E₃ᵃᵛ(V_KK=0) = -21.7 MeV, δBᵃᵛ = 2.44 MeV.
- For the K⁻K⁻p system with averaged HW potential: E₂ᵃᵛ = -1.11 MeV, E₃ᵃᵛ(V_KK=0) = -3.07 MeV, δBᵃᵛ = 0.85 MeV.
- For the nnp system (³H) with MT I-III potential: E₃ = -8.534 MeV, consistent with the value of -8.535 MeV from Filikhin et al. (FGP).

## Context

This work extends the study of the mass polarization term from atomic physics, where it was first studied by Hughes and Eckart (1930), to nuclear three-body systems. It builds on prior calculations for the ΛΛα system by Filikhin and Gal (2002) and Hiyama (2002), and addresses the omission of the mass polarization term in theoretical analyses of kaonic clusters by Dote (2015) and Dote et al. (2015). The results enable more accurate treatment of kaonic clusters such as K⁻K⁻p and ppK⁻, where the mass ratio of kaon to nucleon is about 1/2, making the mass polarization effect essential.
