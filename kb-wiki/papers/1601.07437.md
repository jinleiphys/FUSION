# The QCD Critical Point and Related Observables

> The search for the critical point of QCD in heavy-ion collision experiments has sparked enormous interest with the completion of phase I of the RHIC beam energy scan. Here, I review the basics of the thermodynamics of the QCD phase transition and its implications for experimental multiplicity fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions. Several sources of noncritical fluctuations impact the observables and need to be understood in addition to the critical phenomena. Recent progress has been made in dynamical modeling of critical fluctuations, which ultimately is indispensable to understand potential signals of the QCD critical point in heavy-ion collision.

## Key claim

The search for the QCD critical point in heavy-ion collisions requires not only understanding static thermodynamic fluctuations but also incorporating dynamical effects of critical fluctuations and noncritical sources, because the systems created in collisions are highly dynamical and undergo multiple stages that impact final particle spectra.

## Method

The paper reviews the thermodynamics of the QCD phase transition and its connection to experimental multiplicity fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions. It examines susceptibilities derived from derivatives of the pressure with respect to chemical potential, which relate to cumulants of event-by-event multiplicity distributions. The review analyzes critical fluctuations via coupling of the order parameter sigma to protons, noncritical sources such as limited detector acceptance, resonance decays, volume fluctuations, and global net-baryon number conservation, and dynamical modeling approaches including nonequilibrium chiral fluid dynamics (NchiFD) and fluctuating viscous fluid dynamics.

## Key numbers

- The crossover transition temperature from lattice QCD at zero baryo-chemical potential is in the range T_c = 145-165 MeV, depending on the thermodynamic quantity.
- Higher-order cumulants of the critical mode sigma scale with correlation length as: <Delta sigma^2> proportional to xi^2, <Delta sigma^3> proportional to xi^{9/2}, and <Delta sigma^4>_c proportional to xi^7.
- The maximal correlation length in a dynamical setup was found to extend to 1.5-2.5 fm (Berdnikov 1999).
- Net-proton fluctuation calculations used xi_max = 2.6 fm at mu_B = 0.15 GeV with width 0.4 GeV, 3d Ising model parameters lambda_3 = 1.5 and lambda_4 = 13, and coupling g_p = 9.
- STAR data for net-proton fluctuations were taken with |y| < 0.5 and 0.4 < p_T < 2 (GeV/c).

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework of critical fluctuations developed by Stephanov (1998, 1999, 2008) and Athanasiou (2010), which provided the main motivation for experimental searches at RHIC. It addresses the need to go beyond static equilibrium expectations by incorporating dynamics, as demonstrated by Mukherjee (2015) showing that nonequilibrium memory effects can alter the magnitude and sign of critical fluctuation signals. The work enables future quantitative comparisons between dynamical models and experimental data from the RHIC beam energy scan phase II, NA61 at CERN-SPS, CBM at FAIR, and NICA at JINR.
