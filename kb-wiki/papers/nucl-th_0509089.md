# A q-deformed NJL model at finite temperature: chiral symmetry restoration and pion properties

> We review the implementation of a q-deformed fermionic algebra in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model (NJL). The gap equations obtained from a deformed condensate as well as from the deformation of the NJL Hamiltonian are discussed. The effect of both temperature and deformation in the chiral symmetry restoration process as well as in the pion properties is studied.

# A q-deformed NJL model at finite temperature: chiral symmetry restoration and pion properties

## Key claim

The paper shows that applying a q-deformation (with q < 1) to the fermionic algebra in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model reduces the quark condensate and dynamical mass, and that this deformation can compensate for low condensation to keep the pion massive even when the condensate is approximately 35% lower than in the standard NJL model. The authors explicitly note that the truncation at first order in Q may not be granted for smaller q values.

## Method

The authors implement a q-deformed fermionic algebra based on the SU_q(2) quantum group, where only negative-helicity quark and antiquark operators are modified. They derive two distinct gap equations: one by directly deforming the condensate in the self-consistent mass equation, and another by deforming the NJL Hamiltonian and applying the Bogoliubov-Valatin variational procedure. Finite temperature is introduced via the thermal Green function formalism with Fermi-Dirac distributions, and the coupled gap equations for effective mass and chemical potential are solved with a non-covariant three-momentum cutoff regularization.

## Key numbers

- For q = 0.5, the condensation is approximately 35% lower than in the non-deformed case.
- For q = 0.5, the pion mass obtained is about 190 MeV, compared to about 100 MeV that the standard NJL model would give in such a condensation scenario.
- The cutoff parameter Lambda is used as the regularization scale, with the coupling G having a critical value G_c related to the cutoff.
- The deformation parameter Q is defined as Q = q^{-1} - 1, with q < 1 corresponding to Q > 0.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Timoteo and Lima, 2004) on q-deformation in the NJL model by adding finite temperature effects and pion property calculations. The chiral symmetry restoration behavior as a function of temperature and deformation is compared to the density-dependent restoration observed by Klimt et al. (1990). The paper addresses the low-condensation scenario discussed by Stern (1998), where the standard NJL model fails to describe pionic observables, and shows that the deformed model can maintain a reasonable pion mass even with reduced condensate. This enables further studies of hadronic properties in regimes where the quark condensate is not the sole order parameter.
