# Jet quenching effects on the direct, elliptic, and triangular flow at RHIC

> In this paper we investigate how the energy and momentum deposited by partonic dijets in the quark-gluon plasma may affect the direct, elliptic and triangular flow of low (and intermediate) $p_T$ hadrons in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC. The dijets are modeled as external sources in the energy-momentum conservation equations for hydrodynamics, which are solved on an event-by-event basis within the ideal fluid approximation. We focus our investigation at mid-rapidity and solve the hydrodynamic equations imposing boost invariance. Differential anisotropic flow coefficients for $p_T \gtrsim 1$ GeV are found to be significantly enhanced if the dijets deposit on average more than 12 GeV in the QGP (or more than 6 GeV per jet). Because this jet-induced extra anisotropic flow is not related to the fluctuations of the initial geometry of the collision, the correlation between the $v_2$ and $v_3$ coefficients and their corresponding eccentricities is considerably weakened. In addition, we argue that the extra amount of direct flow induced by dijets may be quantified by comparing the azimuthal dependence of dihadron correlations in dijet events with the corresponding quantity obtained in events without dijets. This comparison could be used to give a rough estimate of the magnitude of the effective coupling between the jets and the medium.

## Key claim

The energy and momentum deposited by partonic dijets in the quark-gluon plasma can significantly enhance the differential anisotropic flow coefficients for hadrons with transverse momentum above approximately 1 GeV, but only if the dijets deposit on average more than 12 GeV in the QGP (more than 6 GeV per jet); this jet-induced extra flow is not related to fluctuations of the initial collision geometry.

## Method

The authors modeled dijets as external source terms in the ideal hydrodynamic energy-momentum conservation equations, solved on an event-by-event basis using a 2+1 smoothed particle hydrodynamics (SPH) approach with boost invariance at mid-rapidity. Initial conditions were generated from a Monte Carlo Glauber model for central Au+Au collisions at 200A GeV, and the dijet energy loss rate was parametrized as proportional to the local entropy density, with the reference energy loss rate varied from 5 to 20 GeV/fm. The final hadron spectra for direct positively charged pions were computed using the Cooper-Frye prescription at a freeze-out temperature of 0.14 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The average energy deposited in the medium by the dijet at mid-rapidity for the highest coupling studied (reference energy loss rate 20 GeV/fm) is approximately 16 GeV (8 GeV per jet on average).
- For the lowest coupling studied (reference energy loss rate 5 GeV/fm), the average deposited energy is slightly smaller than 4 GeV.
- The linear correlation coefficient between elliptic flow v2 and eccentricity ε2,2 for the mixed ensemble at 2 < pT < 3 GeV with reference energy loss rate 20 GeV/fm is λ2 = 0.378.
- The freeze-out temperature used is Tfo = 0.14 GeV, giving a total expansion time around 15 fm/c in the (0-5)% centrality window.
- The smoothing SPH parameter is h = 0.3 fm, and the source width is σ = 0.6 fm.
- The reference entropy density is s0 = 70 fm^-3, corresponding to the maximum of the average entropy density distribution in the (0-5)% centrality window.

## Context

This work extends the study of jet-medium interactions beyond jet quenching to examine how dijets modify the hydrodynamic evolution of the quark-gluon plasma, building on the approach of Chaudhuri (2005) who first introduced source terms for jets in hydrodynamics. The results show that for sufficiently large energy deposition, the correlation between anisotropic flow coefficients and initial geometry eccentricities is considerably weakened, which contradicts the standard picture where flow is primarily determined by initial geometry fluctuations (Alver 2010, Teaney 2010). The authors propose that comparing dihadron correlations in dijet events versus events without dijets could provide a rough estimate of the effective coupling between jets and the medium, enabling future experimental tests at RHIC.
