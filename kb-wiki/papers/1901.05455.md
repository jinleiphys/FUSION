# Calculation of Nucleon Electric Dipole Moments Induced by Quark Chromo-Electric Dipole Moments and the QCD $θ$-term

> Electric dipole moments (EDMs) of nucleons and nuclei, which are sought as evidence of CP violation, require lattice calculations to connect constraints from experiments to limits on the strong CP violation within QCD or CP violation introduced by new physics from beyond the standard model. Nucleon EDM calculations on a lattice are notoriously hard due to large statistical noise, chiral symmetry violating effects, and potential mixing of the EDM and the anomalous magnetic moment of the nucleon. In this report, details of ongoing lattice calculations of proton and neutron EDMs induced by the QCD $θ$-term and the quark chromo-EDM, the lowest-dimension effective CP-violating quark-gluon interaction are presented. Our calculation employs chiral-symmetric fermion discretization. An assessment of feasibility of nucleon EDM calculations at the physical point is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents ongoing lattice QCD calculations of nucleon electric dipole moments induced by the QCD \(\theta\)-term and the quark chromo-electric dipole moment, but emphasizes that the results for the \(\theta\)-term at the physical pion mass are extremely challenging and currently yield no signal, requiring a factor of 25–100 more statistics or alternative methods such as a dynamical \(\theta^I\)-term.

## Method

The authors use lattice QCD with chiral-symmetric (Möbius domain wall) fermions on ensembles generated by the RBC/UKQCD collaboration, employing the Iwasaki gauge action with \(N_f=2+1\) dynamical quarks. They compute nucleon electric dipole form factors \(F_3(Q^2)\) from \(\CP\)-odd three-point correlation functions, treating the \(\CP\)-violating interactions as first-order perturbations to the QCD action. For the \(\theta\)-term, they restrict the topological charge estimator to a cylindrical volume around the nucleon to reduce statistical noise; for the chromo-EDM, they evaluate only connected quark contractions at the physical pion mass.

## Key numbers

- Heavy-pion ensemble: \(m_\pi \approx 340\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(m_N \approx 1.178\ \mathrm{GeV}\), lattice spacing \(a = 0.1105(6)\ \mathrm{fm}\), 1400 configurations.
- Physical-pion ensemble: \(m_\pi \approx 139.2(4)\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(m_N \approx 0.945(6)\ \mathrm{GeV}\), lattice spacing \(a = 0.1141(3)\ \mathrm{fm}\), 130 configurations.
- Very preliminary estimate for the \(\theta\)-induced neutron EDM form factor at the heavy pion mass: \(\hat{F}^\theta_{3n}(0) \approx 0.05\), with a stated 100% uncertainty.
- Leading-order chiral extrapolation of this estimate to the physical point yields \(|\hat{F}_{3n}^{\theta,\mathrm{phys}}| \approx 0.01\) or \(|\hat{d}^{\theta,\mathrm{phys}}_n| \approx 0.001\ e\cdot\mathrm{fm}\).
- At the physical point with \(\approx 33,000\) samples, the neutron EDFF signal is consistent with zero with statistical uncertainty \(\delta F_{3n} \approx 0.05\ldots 0.10\).
- For the chromo-EDM calculation, 33,280 statistical samples are used (256 low-precision + 4 high-precision per configuration), with nucleon momenta up to \(|\vec p| \approx 0.72\ \mathrm{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice QCD calculations of nucleon EDMs, such as those by Shintani (2015), Liu (2017), and Abramczyk (2017), which identified spurious contributions from parity mixing. The authors specifically compare their \(\theta\)-induced neutron EDM estimate to the corrected result from Guo (2015) using Wilson fermions, which constrains \(|\hat{F}^\theta_3(0)| \lesssim 0.06\) at \(m_\pi \approx 360\ \mathrm{MeV}\). The calculations enable future constraints on the strong CP-violating \(\theta_\text{QCD}\) angle and beyond-the-Standard-Model theories from experimental EDM searches, but the authors note that direct physical-point \(\theta\)-term calculations may be at the limit of current computing capabilities, potentially requiring chiral perturbation theory extrapolations or dynamical \(\theta^I\)-term simulations as first explored by Aoki (2008).
