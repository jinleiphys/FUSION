# In-medium Spectral Functions in a Coarse-Graining Approach

> We use a coarse-graining approach to extract local thermodynamic properties from simulations with a microscopic transport model by averaging over a large ensemble of events. Setting up a grid of small space-time cells and going into each cell's rest frame allows to determine baryon and energy density. With help of an equation of state we get the corresponding temperature $T$ and baryon-chemical potential $μ_{\mathrm{B}}$. These results are used for the calculation of the thermal dilepton yield. We apply and compare two different spectral functions for the $ρ$ meson, firstly a calculation from hadronic many-body theory and secondly a calculation from experimental scattering amplitudes. The results obtained with our approach are compared to measurements of the NA60 Collaboration. A relatively good description of the data is achieved with both spectral functions. However, the hadronic many-body calculation is found to be closer to the experimental data with regard to the in-medium broadening of the spectral shape.

## Key claim

The coarse-graining approach, which combines a microscopic transport model with in-medium spectral functions, achieves a relatively good description of NA60 dilepton data using both spectral functions; however, the hadronic many-body calculation (Rapp SF) is found to be closer to the experimental data with regard to the in-medium broadening of the spectral shape, while the Eletsky SF overestimates the yield near the rho pole mass by almost a factor of two.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model to simulate In+In collisions at 158 A GeV, averaging over approximately 1000 events. They set up a space-time grid with cell size Delta t = 0.2 fm/c and Delta x = 0.8 fm, boosted into each cell's rest frame using Eckart's definition, and extracted local temperature T and baryon-chemical potential mu_B using a hadron resonance gas equation of state (with a lattice-based EoS for T > 170 MeV). The thermal dilepton yield was then calculated using two different rho meson spectral functions: the hadronic many-body calculation by Rapp and Wambach (Rapp SF) and the model-independent approach by Eletsky and others (Eletsky SF) based on empirical scattering amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- In+In collisions at E_lab = 158 A GeV (SPS energy)
- Space-time grid: Delta t = 0.2 fm/c, Delta x = 0.8 fm
- Ensemble size: approximately 1000 UrQMD events
- The Rapp SF overshoots NA60 data by about 20% in the mass range from 0.6 to 0.8 GeV
- The Eletsky SF gives a yield almost a factor of two above the data near the rho pole mass
- Two transverse momentum bins analyzed: p_t < 0.2 GeV and p_t between 1.6 and 1.8 GeV
- Transition temperature between hadron gas and QGP equation of state: 170 MeV

## Context

This work extends the coarse-graining approach of Endres (2014) following a similar ansatz as proposed by Huovinen (2002), combining microscopic transport with in-medium spectral functions as a compromise between transport approaches (where medium modifications are difficult) and fireball models (which use simplified reaction evolution). The results are compared to NA60 Collaboration data (Arnaldi 2008, Specht 2010) and show that the Rapp and Wambach (1999) hadronic many-body calculation describes the in-medium broadening more realistically than the Eletsky (2001) approach, which uses a low-density virial expansion valid only for low densities. This enables future studies of dilepton production at other beam energies and collision systems using the same coarse-graining framework.
