# Sketch of the resolution of the axial U(1) problem without chiral anomaly

> We propose a mechanism which explains the masses of $η$ and $η'$ mesons without invoking the explicit violation of $U(1)_A$ symmetry by the chiral anomaly. It is shown that the U(1) problem, the problem for which the prediction of $η$ and $η'$ masses in the simple chiral perturbation theory largely deviates from the experimental values, is actually resolved by considering the first order contribution of the disconnected meson correlator with respect to the quark mass. The bound of Weinberg $m_η^2 \le 3 m_π^2$ is fulfilled by considering the negative squared mass of $η$ or $η'$ which is just the saddle point of the QCD effective potential, and 20% level agreements with experimental data are obtained by just fitting one low energy constant. We provide the leading chiral Lagrangian due to the disconnected contribution in 3-flavor QCD, and also discuss the 2- and 4-flavor cases as well as the consistency of our mechanism with the chiral restoration at high temperature found in lattice calculations.

# Sketch of the resolution of the axial U(1) problem without chiral anomaly

## Key claim
The masses of the η and η' mesons can be explained without invoking explicit U(1)_A violation by the chiral anomaly, by instead considering the first-order contribution of the disconnected meson correlator with respect to the quark mass. The mechanism requires interpreting the negative squared mass of either η or η' as a saddle point of the QCD effective potential, not as an unphysical result.

## Method
The author constructs the leading chiral Lagrangian for 3-flavor QCD including both connected and disconnected diagram contributions to the NG boson mass matrix. The disconnected contribution, which is linear in the current quark masses, is parameterized by a single low-energy constant B_disc that is fitted to reproduce either m_η or m_η'. The mass matrix is diagonalized, and the negative squared mass eigenvalue is reinterpreted as the curvature at a saddle point on the chiral circle, with its sign reverted to obtain the physical meson mass.

## Key numbers
- The connected diagram alone yields m_π ≈ 148 MeV, m_η ≈ 98 MeV, and m_η' ≈ 589 MeV, with η-η' mixing angle θ ≈ -51°, showing the U(1) problem.
- Fit IV with B_disc = -5.58 GeV gives m_η = 573 MeV and m_η' = 959 MeV (after sign reversion), with mixing angle θ = -18°, both within 10% of experimental values.
- The 2-photon decay rate for η → γγ predicted with θ = -18° is 418 eV, compared to the experimental value of 516(20) eV; for η' → γγ the prediction is 4640 eV versus 4340(140) eV experimentally.
- For N_f = 2 with B_disc = -5.58 GeV, the diagonalization yields m_π = 134 MeV and m_η = 244 MeV.
- The low-energy constants use f_π = 92.2 MeV, f_8 = 115 MeV, f_0 = 100 MeV, and quark masses m_l = 3.43 MeV, m_s = 93.5 MeV at μ = 2 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the author's earlier arguments (Yamanaka, 2022) that the topological charge of QCD is unphysical, implying the absence of the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft interaction and the topological susceptibility. It contradicts the conventional resolution of the U(1) problem via instantons (t Hooft, 1976) and the Witten-Veneziano mechanism (Witten, 1979; Veneziano, 1979). The mechanism enables a return to ordinary chiral perturbation theory for η and η' without requiring the 1/N_c expansion, and is consistent with lattice QCD results showing chiral restoration at high temperature (Cossu, 2013; Brandt, 2016; Aoki, 2020).
