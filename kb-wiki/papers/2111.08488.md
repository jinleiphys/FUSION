# Coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CE$ν$NS) event rates for Ge, Zn and Si detector materials

> Realistic nuclear structure calculations are presented for the event rates due to coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CE$ν$NS), assuming neutrinos from pion-decay at-rest, from nuclear reactors and from Earth's interior. We focus on the currently interesting Germanium isotopes, $^{70,73,76}$Ge, which constitute detector materials of the recently planned CE$ν$NS experiments. We study in addition the potential use of $^{64,70}$Zn and $^{28}$Si isotopes as promising CE$ν$NS detectors. From nuclear physics perspectives, recently, calculations have been carried out within the framework of the deformed shell-model (DSM), based on realistic nuclear forces, and assessed on the reproducibility of spectroscopic nuclear properties. The high confidence level acquired by their agreement with experimental results and by their comparison with other mostly phenomenological calculations encouraged the use of DSM to extract predictions for the CE$ν$NS event rates of the above isotopes. Our detailed estimation of the nuclear physics aspects of the recently observed neutral current coherent neutrino-nucleus scattering may shed light on unravelling the still remaining uncertainties for the CE$ν$NS process within and beyond the Standard Model.

## Key claim

The deformed shell model (DSM) provides reliable predictions for CEνNS event rates in Ge, Zn, and Si isotopes, with percentage differences relative to the Klein-Nystrand form factor approximation reaching up to 8% for π-DAR neutrinos on ⁶⁴Zn and ⁷⁶Ge, while for reactor neutrinos the nuclear structure uncertainties remain below the sub-percentage level.

## Method

The authors performed deformed shell model (DSM) calculations based on Hartree-Fock states with angular momentum projection and band mixing, using realistic nuclear forces and effective two-body interactions specific to each isotope (jj44b, modified Kuo, GXPF1A, and USD interactions). They computed proton and neutron nuclear form factors for ⁷⁰,⁷³,⁷⁶Ge, ⁶⁴,⁷⁰Zn, and ²⁸Si, then convolved these with neutrino energy distributions from π-DAR sources (SNS specifications), reactor antineutrinos, and geoneutrinos to obtain differential and integrated event rates as functions of nuclear recoil energy.

## Key numbers

- For SNS π-DAR neutrinos, the proportion of differential event rates for ⁷⁰Ge:⁷³Ge:⁷⁶Ge at recoil energy 0.1 keV is 2.96:3.64:4.0, equivalent to 1:1.230:1.351
- The SNS neutrino flux at a detector baseline of L ≈ 20 m is Φ_ν ≈ 1 × 10⁷ s⁻¹ cm⁻²
- Reactor antineutrino flux assumed is Φ_ν ≈ 1 × 10¹³ s⁻¹ cm⁻²
- Maximum neutrino energies: 52.8 MeV for π-DAR, ≈ 9.5 MeV for reactor, and (1.3, 2.3, 4.5) MeV for (K, Th, U) geoneutrinos respectively
- Harmonic oscillator length parameters: 1.894 fm (⁷⁰Ge), 1.907 fm (⁷³Ge), 1.920 fm (⁷⁶Ge), 1.865 fm (⁶⁴Zn), 1.894 fm (⁷⁰Zn), 1.625 fm (²⁸Si)
- Experimental charge radii: 4.041 fm (⁷⁰Ge), 4.063 fm (⁷³Ge), 4.081 fm (⁷⁶Ge), 3.928 fm (⁶⁴Zn), 3.985 fm (⁷⁰Zn), 3.122 fm (²⁸Si)
- Percentage difference R between DSM and Klein-Nystrand form factor can reach 8% for ⁶⁴Zn and ⁷⁶Ge with π-DAR neutrinos

## Context

This work extends previous DSM calculations for spectroscopic properties and double-beta decay (Sahu 2013, 2014) and for WIMP scattering off ⁷³Ge (Sahu 2017) to CEνNS event rates for isotopes relevant to current and planned experiments including COHERENT, CONUS, νGEN, TEXONO, RICOCHET, MINER, and NUCLEUS. The results enable precision comparisons between full nuclear structure calculations and approximate form factor parametrizations (such as the Klein-Nystrand approximation used by the COHERENT Collaboration), and provide input for constraining nonstandard interactions and for estimating neutrino backgrounds in dark matter direct detection experiments like SuperCDMS at SNOLAB, which aims for nuclear recoil thresholds as low as 40 eV (germanium) and 78 eV (silicon).
