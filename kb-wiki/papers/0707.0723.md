# Clustering and Triaxial Deformations of $^{40}$Ca

> We have studied the positive-parity states of $^{40}$Ca using antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) and the generator coordinate method (GCM). Imposing two different kinds of constraints on the variational calculation, we have found various kinds of $^{40}{\rm Ca}$ structures such as a deformed-shell structure, as well as $α$-$^{36}$Ar and $^{12}$C-$^{28}$Si cluster structures. After the GCM calculation, we obtained a normal-deformed band and a superdeformed band together with their side bands associated with triaxial deformation. The calculated $B(E2)$ values agreed well with empirical data. It was also found that the normal-deformed and superdeformed bands have a non-negligible $α$-$^{36}$Ar cluster component and $^{12}$C-$^{28}$Si cluster component, respectively. This leads to the presence of an $α$-$^{36}$Ar higher-nodal band occurring above the normal-deformed band.

## Key claim

The normal-deformed (ND) and superdeformed (SD) bands in 40Ca, while appearing as deformed mean-field states in the beta-constrained calculation, contain non-negligible cluster components: the ND band has approximately 40% squared overlap with the alpha-36Ar cluster configuration space, and the SD band has approximately 59% squared overlap with the 12C-28Si cluster configuration space, even though spatially developed clustering is not visible in the density distributions of the beta-constrained minima.

## Method

The authors used antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with triaxially deformed Gaussian wave packets and the generator coordinate method (GCM), employing two types of constraints on the variational calculation: a constraint on the quadrupole deformation parameter beta and a constraint on the distance d between cluster centers of mass. They used the Gogny D1S and Skyrme SLy7 effective interactions, performed angular momentum projection, and solved the Hill-Wheeler equation to obtain the final GCM wave functions from 28 basis states including beta-constrained, alpha-36Ar d-constrained, and 12C-28Si d-constrained wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The ND minimum appears at beta = 0.39 with gamma = 25.2 degrees; the SD minimum appears at beta = 0.62 with gamma = 14.5 degrees
- The excitation energies of the ND and SD minima before angular momentum projection are approximately 14 MeV and 18 MeV, respectively
- After GCM calculation, the theoretical band-head excitation energies are 11.2 MeV for the Kpi = 0_ND+ band and 12.1 MeV for the Kpi = 0_SD+ band
- The squared overlap of ND states with beta-constrained wave functions is 99%, while the overlap with alpha-36Ar cluster wave functions is approximately 40% for the Kpi = 0_ND+ band
- The squared overlap of SD states with beta-constrained wave functions is 95%, while the overlap with 12C-28Si cluster wave functions is approximately 59% for the Kpi = 0_SD+ band
- The alpha-36Ar higher-nodal band (Kpi = 0_hn+) has squared overlap of 49% with alpha-36Ar cluster wave functions and 41% with beta-constrained wave functions

## Context

This work extends prior studies of 40Ca structure including cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations by Inakura (2002), SHF-BCS plus GCM calculations by Bender (2003) which assumed axial symmetry and could not study triaxiality, and spherical-basis AMD calculations by Kanada-En'yo (2005) which did not produce triaxial deformation. The present calculation demonstrates that triaxial deformation is essential for understanding the excited states of 40Ca and that the ND and SD bands have significant cluster components, supporting earlier suggestions from the alpha-36Ar orthogonal condition model by Sakuda (1994) and the local potential model by Ohkubo (1988). The results predict a Kpi = 2_SD+ side band and an oblate band (Kpi = 0_ob+) not yet observed experimentally, and identify candidates for the alpha-36Ar higher-nodal bands observed by Yamaya (1994).
