# Three-body resonances in pionless effective field theory

> We investigate the appearance of resonances in three-body systems using pionless effective field theory at leading order. The Faddeev equation is analytically continued to the unphysical sheet adjacent to the positive real energy axis using a contour rotation. We consider both, the three-boson system and the three-neutron system. For the former, we calculate the trajectory of Borromean three-body Efimov states turning into resonances as they cross the three-body threshold. For the latter, we find no sign of three-body resonances or virtual states at leading order. This result is validated by exploring the level structure of three-body states in a finite volume approach.

## Key claim

Using pionless effective field theory at leading order, the authors find no evidence for three-neutron resonances or virtual states in the \(J^\pi = \frac{1}{2}^-\), \(\frac{3}{2}^-\), and \(\frac{1}{2}^+\) channels up to the physical \(nn\) scattering length, with the caveat that the leading-order calculation has an estimated uncertainty of about 15% due to the ratio of effective range to scattering length.

## Method

The authors analytically continued the Faddeev equation to the unphysical sheet adjacent to the positive real energy axis using a contour rotation in momentum space, with the two-body interaction described by a separable Gaussian regulator in pionless EFT at leading order. For the three-boson system, a momentum-independent three-body contact interaction was included, while for the three-neutron system only an \(S\)-wave two-body interaction in the \(^1S_0\) channel was used. The results were validated by a complementary finite-volume approach using a discrete variable representation to search for avoided level crossings in periodic boxes.

## Key numbers

- For the three-neutron system, the physical \(nn\) scattering length is \((-18.9 \pm 0.4)\) fm with an effective range of approximately 2.7 fm, giving a leading-order uncertainty of about 15%.
- The two-body binding momentum \(\gamma\) is related to the scattering length by \(\gamma = 1/a\), with a two-body bound state at energy \(E_2 = -\gamma^2 = -1/a^2\) for positive \(\gamma\).
- For the three-boson system, the trajectory of Borromean Efimov states turning into resonances was calculated for a broad range of negative scattering length.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of three-neutron resonances using the complex scaling method (Csoto 1995, Witala 1999) and analytical continuation in the coupling constant (Lazauskas 2005), which gave conflicting results. It also builds on the finite-volume formalism developed by Klos (2018) for identifying few-body resonances. The model-independent pionless EFT approach provides a systematic framework that can be improved by higher-order corrections, and the absence of three-neutron resonances at leading order supports the conclusions of Deltuva (2018) and Higgins (2020) while contradicting suggestions by Gandolfi (2016) and Li (2019) that a three-neutron resonance might exist.
