# A background estimator for jet studies in p+p and A+A collisions

> Experimentally, jet physics studies face an unavoidable task: distinguishing, at the detector level, the particles produced in the hard partonic scattering from the ones created in unrelated soft processes such as pileup interactions in high-luminosity proton-proton scattering or the underlying event in heavy-ion collisions. The fluctuating nature of the background constitutes the main source of uncertainty for any subtraction algorithm. Aiming at mitigating the effect of such fluctuations, we present a new method to estimate the background contribution to the transverse momentum on a jet-by-jet basis. Our approach is based on estimating the median background momentum density stored above a $p_{\rm T}$-cut applied at the constituent level and an experimentally accessible correction term related to the signal contribution below the cut. This allows to trade part of the uncertainty due to background contamination for that of the signal below the cut, similarly to SoftKiller method. We propose to reduce the fluctuations of the latter by exploiting intrinsic correlations among the soft and hard sectors of QCD jets. Our data-driven approach is tested against PYTHIA8 and JEWEL di-jet events embedded in a thermal background and compared to the area-median and SoftKiller methods. The main result of this study is a $\sim 5\!-\!40\%$ improvement on the resolution of the reconstructed jet $p_T$ compared to previous methods in a high-luminosity proton-proton scenario. Its applicability in a heavy-ion context is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new background estimator, called the $\rho$-correction method, that improves the resolution of reconstructed jet transverse momentum by 5-35% compared to the area-median and SoftKiller methods, but this improvement is conditional on exploiting intrinsic correlations between the soft and hard sectors of QCD jets, and the method's applicability in heavy-ion collisions requires using p+p data to tune free parameters due to the inability to determine the true jet $p_T$ in the presence of energy loss.

## Method

The method estimates the background contribution to a jet's $p_T$ by first dividing the event into $k_T$ patches, computing the median momentum density $\rho_>$ using only particles above a soft $p_T$ cut, and then adding the total $p_T$ of particles below that cut within the jet. A correction term $p_{T,<}^{\rm shift}$ is subtracted to account for signal contamination below the cut, which can be determined inclusively or using a width-dependent hard-soft correlation measured from low-pileup p+p events. The method was tested on PYTHIA8 di-jets embedded in minimum bias events (simulating p+p with pileup) and JEWEL di-jets embedded in a thermal background (simulating heavy-ion collisions), and compared to the area-median and SoftKiller methods.

## Key numbers

* For PYTHIA8 events with $\rm{n_{PU}=200}$ (high-luminosity LHC scenario), the $\rho$-correction HS$_{\rm corr}$ method yields a standard deviation of $10.31 \pm 0.18$ GeV/c for the $p_T$ reconstruction distribution, compared to $15.35 \pm 0.26$ GeV/c for area-median and $10.37 \pm 0.18$ GeV/c for SoftKiller $R^{\rm adj}$.
* For PYTHIA8 events with $\rm{n_{PU}=60}$, the $\rho$-correction HS$_{\rm corr}$ method yields a standard deviation of $6.21 \pm 0.11$ GeV/c, compared to $7.87 \pm 0.13$ GeV/c for area-median and $6.36 \pm 0.11$ GeV/c for SoftKiller $R^{\rm adj}$.
* For JEWEL di-jets embedded in a thermal background (simulating central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt s = 5.02$ TeV with $\langle \rho \rangle = 250$ GeV), the $\rho$-correction HS$_{\rm corr}$ method reduces the standard deviation by 0.6 GeV/c compared to SoftKiller $R^{\rm adj}$ and by 4.4 GeV/c compared to area-median.
* The optimal soft $p_T$ cut for the $\rho$-correction method is $p_T^{\rm cut,soft} = 1.4$ GeV/c for $\rm{n_{PU}=60}$ and $p_T^{\rm cut,soft} = 2.2$ GeV/c for $\rm{n_{PU}=200}$.

## Context

This work extends the area-median method (Cacciari, 2007) and the SoftKiller method (Cacciari, 2014) by introducing a correction for signal contamination below a momentum cut, which is the main source of bias in SoftKiller. The method exploits intrinsic hard-soft correlations in QCD jets, as demonstrated by the correlation between the soft signal contribution and the jet width (Larkoski, 2014), to reduce fluctuations in the signal contamination term. This enables an unbiased background estimator for heavy-ion collisions that does not rely on the common trade-off between resolution improvement and a shift in the mean due to energy loss, making it applicable to experimental jet analyses at the LHC.
