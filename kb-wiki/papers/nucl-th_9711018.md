# A study of the phase transition in the usual statistical model for nuclear multifragmentation

> We use a simplified model which is based on the same physics as inherent in most statistical models for nuclear multifragmentation. The simplified model allows exact calculations for thermodynamic properties of systems of large number of particles. This enables us to study a phase transition in the model. A first order phase transition can be tracked down. There are significant differences between this phase transition and some other well-known cases.

# A study of the phase transition in the usual statistical model for nuclear multifragmentation

## Key claim

A first-order phase transition exists in the standard statistical model for nuclear multifragmentation, but this transition is qualitatively different from the liquid-gas phase transition seen in the lattice gas model: the specific heat diverges at the boiling point while the second moment remains finite, which is the opposite behavior of the lattice gas model.

## Method

The authors construct a simplified statistical model where nucleons can form composites (clusters of k nucleons) with ground-state binding energy, temperature-dependent surface tension, and Fermi-gas excited states. They compute the canonical partition function exactly for systems up to 2800 particles using a recursion relation, avoiding Monte Carlo sampling, and extract thermodynamic quantities including free energy, specific heat, average largest cluster size, and fragment yield distributions.

## Key numbers

- The volume energy coefficient is W0 = 16 MeV, the surface tension at zero temperature is σ0 = 18 MeV, and the critical temperature for surface tension is Tc = 18 MeV
- The freeze-out density used in most calculations is ρ = A/V = 0.05 fm⁻³, giving an effective available density ρ̃ = 0.0727 fm⁻³
- For a system of 2800 particles, the break in the first derivative of the free energy occurs at 7.3 MeV; for 1400 particles, at 7.15 MeV
- The boiling temperature in the infinite-matter limit, obtained from the grand canonical ensemble, is 8.187 MeV at ρ̃ = 0.07273 fm⁻³
- The specific heat maxima occur at 6.95 MeV (700 particles), 7.15 MeV (1400 particles), and 7.3 MeV (2800 particles)
- The effective τ minimum is 1.84 for the case shown, using fragment masses from A=5 to A=30
- The second moment M2 saturates at approximately 11.6 and does not grow with particle number

## Context

This work extends the statistical multifragmentation model of Bondorf et al. (1995) and Gross (1997) by rigorously demonstrating the existence and character of its phase transition, which was briefly addressed by Das Gupta, Pan, Kvasnikova, and Gale (1997). The results contrast with the lattice gas model of Pan and Das Gupta (1995), which maps onto an Ising model and shows a standard liquid-gas transition where the second moment diverges but specific heat only jumps discontinuously. The finding that the minimum of effective τ and the maximum of the second moment M2 both occur near the specific heat maximum provides experimental proxies for locating the boiling temperature, a feature shared with the lattice gas model despite the different transition character.
