# Correlation function for the $n \bar{D}_{s0}^*(2317)$ interaction and the issue of elastic unitarity

> We study the interaction of a neutron with the $\bar D_{s0}^*(2317)$ resonance and look at the amplitude below threshold and close above threshold. The study is done from the perspective that the $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ resonance is a molecular state of $DK$ in $I=0$. To study this interaction, we use the Fixed Center Approximation to Faddeev equations that considers the $DK$ molecule as the cluster and the neutron as the external particle. We improve the Fixed Center approach to implement elastic unitarity around threshold, which is needed to obtain scattering parameters and to evaluate the $n \bar D_{s0}^*(2317)$ correlation function that we determine here. One interesting result of the study is the appearance of a resonant state below threshold with a binding of about 130 MeV and a width of about 80 MeV, which we suggest to look at in reactions measuring the invariant mass of $πΣ\bar D$. The ALICE collaboration has initiated studies of this type, by looking at the $p f_1(1285)$ correlation function, and we can only encourage work in this direction which should provide much valuable information on the nature of many resonant states.

# Correlation function for the \(n \bar{D}_{s0}^*(2317)\) interaction and the issue of elastic unitarity

## Key claim

The \(n \bar{D}_{s0}^*(2317)\) system develops a resonant state below threshold with a binding of about 130 MeV and a width of about 80 MeV, but only under the assumption that the \(D_{s0}^*(2317)\) resonance is a molecular state of \(DK\) in isospin \(I=0\); if the \(D_{s0}^*(2317)\) were an ordinary meson state, the bound state would be expected to be very narrow, with a width coming only from the natural decay of the \(\bar{D}_{s0}^*(2317)\) into \(\bar{D}_s \pi\), which is smaller than 3.8 MeV.

## Method

The authors used the Fixed Center Approximation (FCA) to the Faddeev equations, treating the \(DK\) molecule as the cluster and the neutron as the external particle, with the \(n\bar{D}\) and \(n\bar{K}\) scattering matrices as inputs. They improved the FCA by implementing a coherent propagation of the \(n\bar{D}_{s0}^*\) system through a Lippmann–Schwinger equation, which enforces elastic unitarity around threshold and allows extraction of scattering parameters and the correlation function. The correlation function was computed using the Koonin–Pratt formalism adapted to the half-off-shell \(\tilde{T}'\) matrix.

## Key numbers

* The resonant state below the \(n \bar{D}_{s0}^*(2317)\) threshold has a binding of about 130 MeV and a width of about 80 MeV.
* The scattering length for \(n \bar{D}_{s0}^*(2317)\) scattering is \(a = 0.60 - i\,0.29\) fm.
* The effective range for \(n \bar{D}_{s0}^*(2317)\) scattering is \(r_0 = 1.14 - i\,0.11\) fm.
* The \(D_{s0}^*(2317)\) is bound by about 42 MeV with respect to the \(D^0 K^+\) threshold, and its width is smaller than 3.8 MeV according to the Particle Data Group.
* The probability of \(DK\) in the wave function of the \(D_{s0}^*(2317)\) is about 72%, from lattice QCD analysis by MartinezTorres (2014).
* The regulator \(q_{\text{max}} = 710\) MeV was used in the loop functions for the dynamical generation of the \(D_{s0}^*(2317)\) from \(DK\).
* The \(\pi \Sigma\) channel opens up at \(\sqrt{s} = 3063\) MeV in the \(n \bar{D}_{s0}^*(2317)\) system.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Encarnacion (2025), who studied the \(p f_1(1285)\) correlation function using the FCA and found a bound state around 40 MeV below threshold with a width of about 70 MeV, but raised the issue of elastic unitarity being only approximately satisfied. The present paper provides a general framework that respects elastic unitarity and applies it to the \(n \bar{D}_{s0}^*(2317)\) system, where the \(D_{s0}^*(2317)\) is treated as a molecular state of \(DK\) as supported by lattice QCD simulations by Mohler (2013), Lang (2014), Bali (2017), and Cheung (2020). The three-body system \(n \bar{D} \bar{K}\) was previously studied by Yamagata-Sekihara (2018) who found a bound state, but the present work focuses specifically on the \(n \bar{D}_{s0}^*(2317)\) configuration. The results enable experimental searches for the predicted resonant state in reactions measuring the invariant mass of \(\pi \Sigma \bar{D}\), following the approach initiated by the ALICE collaboration for the \(p f_1(1285)\) correlation function.
