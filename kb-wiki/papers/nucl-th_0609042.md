# Dynamical phase trajectories for relativistic nuclear collisions

> Central collisions of gold nuclei are simulated by several existing models and the central net baryon density rho and the energy density eps are extracted at successive times, for beam kinetic energies of 5-40 GeV per nucleon. The resulting trajectories in the (rho,eps) phase plane are discussed from the perspective of experimentally exploring the expected first-order hadronization phase transition with the planned FAIR at GSI or in a low-energy campaign at RHIC.

## Key claim

The dynamical phase trajectories in the (net baryon density, energy density) plane for central Au+Au collisions at beam kinetic energies of 5–40 GeV per nucleon are extracted from several existing models, and the authors argue that these mechanical quantities (rather than thermodynamic ones) provide a robust basis for assessing whether the planned FAIR facility or a low-energy RHIC campaign can explore the expected first-order hadronization phase transition, though the degree of local equilibration must be separately analyzed before thermodynamic implications can be drawn.

## Method

Central collisions of gold nuclei were simulated using five dynamical models: three-fluid hydrodynamics, Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD), Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD), the Quark-Gluon String Model (QGSM), and the Giessen Boltzmann–Ühling–Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) model. For each model, the central net baryon density ρ(t) and total energy density ε(t) were extracted at successive times at the collision center (origin in the CM frame), without requiring local thermal equilibrium, and combined to form phase trajectories in the (ρ, ε) plane. These trajectories were compared to adiabatic expansion paths from a chiral hadronic SU(3) model and to schematic phase coexistence boundaries from a constructed equation of state.

## Key numbers

* For the three-fluid model at 5 GeV/A, the phase trajectory just reaches the hadronic boundary of the schematic phase coexistence region; at 10 GeV/A, the turning point lies on the plasma side beyond the schematic phase coexistence region, and this trajectory spends the longest time traversing the phase coexistence region during expansion.
* In the PHSD model, the 5 GeV/A trajectory turns around just at the hadronic border of the schematic phase coexistence region, while the 10 GeV/A trajectory turns around at the schematic plasma phase boundary of that region.
* The UrQMD model yields compressions somewhat higher than PHSD and more similar to QGSM; the symbols on the UrQMD phase trajectories are separated by Δt = 1 fm/c.
* The QGSM expansion trajectories at lower energies fall significantly below those of the other models, attributed to the absence of hadron repulsion and the dominance of nucleon and pion propagation over resonance production.
* The GiBUU model reaches slightly higher densities than PHSD, and the difference between outward and inward trajectory grows faster as collision energy increases.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of phase evolution in nuclear collisions, notably the analysis by Dorso et al. (1989) who extracted thermodynamic phase evolution from the breakup of an initially compressed nucleus and observed spinodal behavior associated with the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition. The present study uses multiple dynamical models (three-fluid hydrodynamics from Ivanov et al. (2005), HSD/PHSD from Cassing et al. (1999), UrQMD from Bass et al. (1998) and Bleicher et al. (1999), QGSM from Amelin et al. (1990), and GiBUU from various authors) to assess the thermodynamic environments achievable at FAIR and low-energy RHIC, and it enables future work to determine whether the phase trajectories actually cross the predicted first-order phase coexistence region and whether signals of the phase transition can survive through freeze-out.
