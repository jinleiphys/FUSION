# Hadron structure from lattice QCD - outlook and future perspectives

> We review results on hadron structure using lattice QCD simulations with pion masses close or at to the physical value. We pay particular attention to recent successes on the computation of the mass of the low-lying baryons and on the challenges involved in evaluating energies of excited states and resonance parameters, as well as, in studies of nucleon structure.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD simulations with quark masses fixed at their physical values are now feasible, eliminating the need for chiral extrapolation and its associated systematic uncertainty, but these simulations introduce new challenges including increased statistical noise and the need to handle unstable particles on a finite lattice in Euclidean time.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD results obtained using improved discretization schemes, primarily Wilson-type twisted mass fermions (TMF) and domain wall fermions, with simulations performed at multiple lattice spacings and volumes to control lattice artifacts. Hadron masses are extracted from two-point correlation functions in the large Euclidean time limit, while nucleon structure observables require three-point functions that include both connected and disconnected quark loop contributions, with the latter computed using stochastic noise techniques and GPU-accelerated multi-GPU codes.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass of the physical point TMF ensemble: m_pi = 130 MeV, with lattice spacing a = 0.094 fm and spatial extent L = 4.5 fm
- N_f=2+1+1 TMF ensemble with m_pi = 373 MeV, a = 0.082 fm, L = 2.6 fm (B55 ensemble) used for high-statistics disconnected contribution analysis
- Preliminary charmed baryon masses from the physical point ensemble: Xi_cc* = 3.678(8) GeV, Omega+_cc = 3.708(10) GeV, Omega*+_cc = 3.767(11) GeV, Omega++_ccc = 4.746(3) GeV
- Preliminary isovector tensor charge from TMF at the physical point: g_T^{u-d} = 1.048(34) in MS-bar at 2 GeV
- Disconnected contributions to the nucleon axial charge from light quarks (g_A^{u+d}) and strange quarks (g_A^s) are O(10%) for u- and d-quarks, obtained from 4700 gauge configurations yielding approximately 150,000 statistics

## Context

This review extends the work of multiple lattice QCD collaborations including MILC (Bazavov 2014), BMW (Durr 2013), ETM (Abdel-Rehim 2013), QCDSF (Bali 2012), PACS-CS (Aoki 2009), and RBC/UKQCD (Syritsyn) who have generated simulations with physical light quark masses using different discretization schemes. The results demonstrate that lattice QCD can now reproduce experimental baryon masses and the nucleon axial charge without chiral extrapolation, enabling future predictions for beyond-Standard-Model physics probes such as the tensor charge and nucleon sigma-terms, though higher statistics and further development of noise reduction techniques are needed for reliable predictions at the physical point.
