# Perturbative Expansion of Irreversible Work in Fokker-Planck Equation a la Quantum Mechanics

> We discuss the systematic expansion of the solution of the Fokker-Planck equation with the help of the eigenfunctions of the time-dependent Fokker-Planck operator. The expansion parameter is the time derivative of the external parameter which controls the form of an external potential. Our expansion corresponds to the perturbative calculation of the adiabatic motion in quantum mechanics. With this method, we derive a new formula to calculate the irreversible work order by order, which is expressed as the expectation value with a pseudo density matrix. Applying this method to the case of the harmonic potential, we show that the first order term of the expansion gives the exact result. Because we do not need to solve the coupled differential equations of moments, our method simplifies the calculations of various functions such as the fluctuation of the irreversible work per unit time. We further investigate the exact optimized protocol to minimize the irreversible work by calculating its variation with respect to the control parameter itself.

## Key claim

The paper develops a perturbative expansion of the Fokker-Planck equation solution using eigenfunctions of the time-dependent Fokker-Planck operator, where the expansion parameter is the time derivative of the external control parameter; the method is claimed to give the exact result for the harmonic potential at first order, but the general applicability is limited by the condition that the system evolves from an equilibrium initial state.

## Method

The solution of the Fokker-Planck equation is expanded in a bi-orthogonal basis formed by eigenfunctions of the time-dependent Fokker-Planck operator and its adjoint, which are constructed from eigenfunctions of an associated Hermitian Hamiltonian operator. The expansion coefficients are determined iteratively through an integral equation involving matrix elements of the time derivative of the Fokker-Planck operator. The method is applied to the harmonic potential to compute the irreversible work and its fluctuation, and the variation of the irreversible work with respect to the control parameter is calculated to derive an equation for the optimized protocol.

## Key numbers

- The eigenvalue of the Fokker-Planck operator for the harmonic potential is given by \(\bar{\lambda}_n(t) = a_t n / \nu\), where \(a_t\) is the control parameter and \(\nu\) is the friction coefficient.
- For the harmonic potential, the irreversible work is expressed as \(W_{irr} = \int^{t_f}_{t_i} dt \int^t_{t_i} dt' \dot{a}_{t'} \left[e^{-\int^t_{t'} ds\frac{2a_s}{\nu}} \frac{1}{2\beta a^2_{t'}} \right] \dot{a}_{t}\).
- In the large time-scale limit \(\tau_{op} \rightarrow \infty\), the irreversible work for the harmonic potential is approximated by \(W_{irr} \approx \frac{\nu}{4\beta \tau_{op}} \int^{1}_{0} d\tau \frac{ \dot{\bar{a}}_{\tau}^2}{\bar{a}^3_{\tau}}\).
- In the instantaneous jump limit \(\tau_{op} \rightarrow 0\), the ratio of irreversible work to free energy change is \(\lim_{\tau_{op} \rightarrow 0} \frac{W_{irr}}{|\Delta F|} = \frac{\left( \frac{\bar{a}_1}{\bar{a}_0} -1 \right)}{|\ln \frac{\bar{a}_1}{\bar{a}_0}|}\).

## Context

This work extends the perturbative approach to irreversible work calculations beyond the moment method used by Schmiedl and Seifert (2007, 2008) for exactly solvable harmonic potentials, and provides an alternative to the Chapman-Enskog-type expansion proposed by Sekimoto and Sasa (1997). The method enables systematic calculation of irreversible work and its fluctuations for more general potentials without solving coupled differential equations of moments, and it allows investigation of the optimized protocol that minimizes irreversible work for a fixed time period, which is relevant for designing efficient nanomachines.
