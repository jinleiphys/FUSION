# The quark-meson coupling model

> We review the quark-meson coupling model, in which the quark degrees of freedom are explicitly involved to describe the properties of not only nuclear matter but also finite nuclei. Then, we present the electric and magnetic form factors for the proton bound in specific orbits for several closed-shell nuclei.

## Key claim

The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which explicitly includes quark degrees of freedom, predicts that the electromagnetic form factors of a proton bound in a finite nucleus are modified by the nuclear medium, with the magnitude of the modification depending on the specific nuclear orbit and the local baryon density; the abstract presents the calculation of these form factors but glosses over the fact that the results are shown as ratios to free-space values to reduce theoretical uncertainties, and that the exploratory study ignores possible effects of off-shell form factors and meson exchange currents.

## Method

The QMC model treats the nucleon as a composite object of three quarks confined by a scalar potential, with the quarks additionally feeling scalar and vector mean fields generated by surrounding nucleons via meson exchange (sigma, omega, rho mesons and the Coulomb field). For finite nuclei, the Born-Oppenheimer approximation is used: the nucleon's internal structure is solved for constant scalar and vector fields at the nucleon's centre, with first-order perturbation theory correcting for field variation across the bag. The electromagnetic form factors for a proton bound in a specific orbit are then computed in local density approximation by integrating the density-dependent form factor of a proton in nuclear matter over the local baryon density and the proton shell-model wave function.

## Key numbers

- Free nucleon mass set to 939 MeV; free bag radius set to 0.8 fm; bare quark mass chosen as 5 MeV.
- Bag constant B^(1/4) = 170.0 MeV; parameter z_N = 3.295.
- Coupling constants fitted to symmetric nuclear matter: g_sigma^2/4pi = 5.40, g_omega^2/4pi = 5.31, g_rho^2/4pi = 6.93.
- Saturation density rho_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}; average binding energy at saturation = -15.7 MeV; nuclear incompressibility about 280 MeV.
- At saturation density, the nucleon bag radius changes by delta R_N^*/R_N = -0.02, the lowest eigenvalue changes by delta x_N^*/x_N = -0.16, and the root-mean-square radius of the quark wave function changes by delta r_q^*/r_q = +0.02.
- For the 1s-orbit proton in ^16O, the magnetic moment increases by about 7%; for the 1p-orbits, it is reduced by about 2-3% from the 1s-orbit value.
- For a proton in the 1s-orbit in ^16O, the ratio G_E/G_M runs roughly from 0.41 at Q^2 = 0 to 0.28 at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2.

## Context

This work reviews and extends the QMC model, first proposed by Guichon (1988) and later developed by the Adelaide group, which explicitly incorporates quark degrees of freedom to describe nuclear matter and finite nuclei, in contrast to Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) (Walecka) where the nucleon is treated as a point-like particle. The calculation of bound-nucleon electromagnetic form factors enables comparison with ongoing and future electron-nucleus scattering experiments at TJNAF and Mainz, which could detect differences between form factors in different shell model orbits and provide a signature for the role of quarks in nuclei. The paper also notes a modified version, MQMC (Jin and Jennings), where the bag constant is allowed to decrease in nuclear matter, and shows that a 10% reduction in the bag constant at saturation density has a significant effect on the form factors.
