# Thermodynamical properties of a mean-field plus pairing model and applications for the Fe nuclei

> A mean-field plus pairing model for atomic nuclei in the Fe region was studied using a finite-temperature quantum Monte-Carlo method. We present results for thermodynamical quantities such as the internal energy and the specific heat. These results give indications of a phase transition related to the pairing amongst nucleons, around temperatures of 0.7 MeV. The influence of the residual interaction and of the size of the model space on the nuclear level densities is discussed too.

## Key claim

The paper presents indications of a phase transition related to pairing correlations among nucleons at temperatures around 0.7 MeV, but the authors explicitly state that their results are not yet conform to realistic Hamiltonians and are limited by a too-simple mean-field plus pairing model and modest computer facilities.

## Method

The authors used a finite-temperature quantum Monte-Carlo method based on the Shell-Model Quantum Monte-Carlo approach, with a discrete Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation and Suzuki-Trotter decomposition to separate one- and two-body parts of the Hamiltonian. They applied this method to a mean-field (Woods-Saxon potential) plus pairing Hamiltonian for nuclei in the Fe region, specifically modeling ⁵⁶Fe with 6 valence protons and 10 valence neutrons in the fp-shell (1f₇/₂, 2p₃/₂, 2p₁/₂, 1f₅/₂ orbitals), with the pairing strength taken as G = 20 MeV/A following Bes and Sorensen (1969).

## Key numbers

- Phase transition related to pairing is indicated around temperatures of **0.7 MeV**.
- For the neutron specific heat with G = 20 MeV/56, the peak shifts to a slightly higher temperature and becomes more pronounced as G increases; for G ≥ 20 MeV/56, pairing correlations seem to induce a phase transition.
- For protons with G = 10 MeV/56, a second peak in the specific-heat curve develops around **T = 0.2 MeV**, attributed to pair correlations among the 6 particles in the 1f₇/₂ orbital.
- The ground-state energy shift for the system with 10 valence neutrons, calculated from mass excesses, is **1.776 MeV**; the quantum Monte-Carlo results approach this value at temperatures below **0.5 MeV**.
- For the largest model space (78 single-particle states), at high temperatures (T ≥ 2 MeV), the specific-heat curve coincides with the G=0 case, and the proton and neutron energy are some **5 MeV** lower than in the G=0 case.
- The fp-shell model space is too small to describe the system at temperatures T ≥ **1.3 MeV**.
- Extended orbitals beyond the core and fp shell contribute only about **1%** of the ground state density.

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature quantum Monte-Carlo approach for nuclear shell models, as developed by Dean et al. (1995) and others, to a mean-field plus pairing model for Fe-region nuclei. The results are compared with BCS theory (Ring and Schuck, 1980) and with backshifted Bethe formula level-density parametrizations fitted to experimental data by Woosley et al. (1975). The method enables exact treatment of many-body correlations with fixed particle number, but the authors note that more realistic interactions (as used in shell-model Monte-Carlo calculations by Nakada and Alhassid, 1997) are needed for accurate level densities, and that core excitations must be included in the model space for reliable results above 20 MeV excitation energy.
