# The Fermi function and the neutron's lifetime

> The traditional Fermi function ansatz for nuclear beta decay describes enhanced perturbative effects in the limit of large nuclear charge $Z$ and/or small electron velocity $β$. We define and compute the quantum field theory object that replaces this ansatz for neutron beta decay, where neither of these limits hold. We present a new factorization formula that applies in the limit of small electron mass, analyze the components of this formula through two loop order, and resum perturbative corrections that are enhanced by large logarithms. We apply our results to the neutron lifetime, supplying the first two-loop input to the long-distance corrections. Our result can be summarized as \begin{equation*} τ_n \times |V_{ud}|^2\big[1+3λ^2\big]\big[1+Δ_R\big] = \frac{5263.284(17)\,{\rm s}} {1 + 27.04(7)\times 10^{-3} }~, \end{equation*} with $|V_{ud}|$ the up-down quark mixing parameter, $τ_n$ the neutron's lifetime, $λ$ the ratio of axial to vector charge, and $Δ_R$ the short-distance matching correction. We find a shift in the long-distance radiative corrections compared to previous work, and discuss implications for extractions of $|V_{ud}|$ and tests of the Standard Model.

## Key claim

The traditional Fermi function ansatz for nuclear beta decay, which describes enhanced perturbative effects in the limit of large nuclear charge Z and/or small electron velocity β, does not provide a controlled approximation for neutron beta decay where neither limit holds; the authors define and compute the quantum field theory object that replaces this ansatz, finding a shift in long-distance radiative corrections compared to previous work.

## Method

The authors decompose the hard amplitude for neutron beta decay in the static limit (Δ/m_p → 0 with Δ fixed) and present a factorization formula that applies in the limit of small electron mass (m_e²/Δ² ≈ 0.156). They compute the hard and jet functions through two-loop order, resum perturbative corrections enhanced by large logarithms using renormalization group methods, and combine these results with known recoil corrections and the short-distance matching correction Δ_R to obtain updated predictions for the neutron lifetime and |V_ud|.

## Key numbers

- The phase space factor in the static limit: f_static ≈ 0.0157528
- The long-distance radiative correction in the static limit: δ_R,static(μ_UV = Δ) = (29.18 ± 0.07 ± 0.01 ± 0.02) × 10⁻³
- The short-distance matching correction: Δ_R(μ_UV = Δ) = 45.37(27) × 10⁻³
- The combined correction factor: [1 + 27.04(7) × 10⁻³]
- The normalization constant: 5263.284(17) s
- Extracted |V_ud| = 0.97393(41) using τ_n = 877.82(30) s from UCNτ and λ = -1.27641(56) from PERKEO-III
- The mean electron velocity in neutron decay: ⟨β⟩ ≈ 0.73
- Less than 0.1% of the total decay rate involves electron velocity β < 0.1; less than 10% involves β < 0.5

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Hill (2023) and Hill (2023) that identified the traditional Fermi function for nuclear beta decay as the leading-in-Z contribution to a quantum field theory factorization formula. The results modify the long-distance radiative correction compared to previous work by Czarnecki (2019) and Cirigliano (2023), replacing the Fermi function ansatz with a controlled resummation. The improved prediction enables more precise extractions of |V_ud| from neutron beta decay and tests of the Standard Model, with implications for the discrepancy between in-beam and ultracold neutron measurements of the neutron lifetime discussed by Czarnecki (2018) and Fornal (2018).
