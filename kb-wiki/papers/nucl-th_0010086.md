# Isovector multiphonon excitations in near spherical nuclei

> The lowest isoscalar and isovector quadrupole and octupole excitations in near spherical nuclei are studied within the the proton-neutron version of the interacting boson model including quadrupole and octupole bosons (sdf-IBM-2). The main decay modes of these states in near spherical nuclei are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the existence and decay properties of mixed-symmetry (isovector) multiphonon states with negative parity in near spherical nuclei, specifically the $(2^+_{\rm ms} \otimes 3^-_1)$ quintuplet and the $(2^+_1 \otimes 3^-_{\rm ms})$ quintuplet, within the proton-neutron interacting boson model including quadrupole and octupole bosons (sdf-IBM-2). The abstract's claim of "discussing" decay modes glosses over the fact that these states are predicted theoretically and have not yet been observed experimentally, with the paper providing analytical decay ratios in a dynamical symmetry limit.

## Method

The authors employ the sdf-IBM-2, which includes s (l^π = 0^+), d (l^π = 2^+), and f (l^π = 3^-) bosons for protons and neutrons, with a Hamiltonian containing Majorana operators to set excitation energies of proton-neutron non-symmetric states. States are classified by group reduction chain quantum numbers from U_π(13) ⊗ U_ν(13) down to U_πν(6) ⊗ U_πν(7), distinguishing three types of mixed-symmetry states: those with mixed symmetry in the sd-sector coupled to symmetric octupole bosons, those with mixed symmetry in the f-sector, and those symmetric separately in sd- and f-sectors but coupled non-symmetrically. Electromagnetic decay rates are derived analytically in the U(5) dynamical symmetry limit of the model.

## Key numbers

- The excitation energy of the $(2^+_{\rm ms} \otimes 3^-)$ states is expected to be close to the sum energy of the constituting phonons, around 3.5–4 MeV, in nuclei such as 142Ce, 144Nd, 94Mo, and 134,136Ba.
- The ratio of E1 excitation strengths to the $1^-_{\rm ms}$ state versus the symmetric two-phonon $1^-_1$ state in the U(5) limit is given by $\frac{B(E1;0^+_1 \rightarrow 1^-_{\rm ms})}{B(E1;0^+_1 \rightarrow 1^-_1)} = \frac{N(N-2)}{(N-1)^2} \frac{(1-\eta^2)N_\pi N_\nu}{(N_\pi + \eta N_\nu)^2}$, where $\eta \equiv e_\nu/e_\pi$ is the ratio of E2 effective boson charges.
- The fundamental $2^+_{\rm ms}$ state decays by a strong M1 transition to the isoscalar $2^+_1$ state with $B({\rm M1}) \sim 1 \mu_N^2$ (from prior work cited as Iachello and Arima (1987)).

## Context

This work extends the standard IBM-2 description of mixed-symmetry states (Iachello and Arima, 1987) by including octupole (f) bosons, building on prior identification of the $2^+_{\rm ms}$ state and the $1^+$ and $3^+$ members of the $(2^+_1 \otimes 2^+_{\rm ms})$ quintuplet in 94Mo (Pietralla et al., cited as PiFr). The predictions enable experimental searches for negative-parity mixed-symmetry states in near spherical nuclei where both isovector quadrupole and isoscalar octupole phonons are already known, such as 142Ce, 144Nd, 94Mo, and 134,136Ba.
