# Neutral-Current Neutrino Scattering from the Deuteron

> Neutral-current neutrino scattering from the deuteron leading to proton-neutron final states is considered using an approach that incorporates relativistic dynamics and consequently provides robust modeling at relatively high energies and momenta. In this work the focus is placed on the fully exclusive reaction where both the proton and neutron in the final state are assumed to be detected. Accordingly, the incident neutrino energy, the neutrino scattering angle and the scattered neutrino's energy can all be reconstructed. It is shown that for specific choices of kinematics the reaction proceeds mainly via scattering from the proton, while for other choices of kinematics it proceeds mainly from the neutron. Specific asymmetries are introduced to focus on these attributes. Measurements in both regions have the potential to yield valuable information on the nucleon's electroweak form factors at momentum transfers up to a (GeV/c)$^2$. In particular, the cross sections are shown to be very sensitive to the isoscalar axial-vector form factor, and sensitive but less so to the magnetic strangeness form factor. Comparisons with other reactions, specifically charge-changing neutrino reactions and both parity-conserving and -violating electron scattering, have the potential to provide new ways to test the Standard Model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that neutral-current neutrino scattering from the deuteron, with detection of both final-state proton and neutron, can be used to reconstruct the full lepton kinematics and isolate scattering from the proton or neutron in specific kinematic regions, thereby providing access to nucleon electroweak form factors, particularly the isoscalar axial-vector form factor, at momentum transfers up to about 1 (GeV/c)², though the sensitivity to the magnetic strangeness form factor is smaller than to the axial-vector one.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic, Lorentz-covariant model of deuteron electrodisintegration originally developed for electron scattering, extended to neutral-current neutrino reactions. The model includes plane-wave contributions and final-state interactions via a proton-neutron t-matrix from SAID fits or a Regge parameterization, and employs dipole parameterizations for the axial-vector and strangeness form factors with coupling constants ρ_s, μ_s, and g^s_A constrained by parity-violating electron scattering data. Cross sections and isospin asymmetries are calculated for both single-nucleon targets (as a baseline) and for the exclusive deuteron disintegration reaction ν_ℓ + ²H → ν_ℓ + p + n, assuming detection of both nucleons.

## Key numbers

- The cross sections for neutrino scattering from nucleons at θ_N = 0° peak at about p_N = 0.75 GeV and fall to about 10% of the maximum at about p_N = 1.2 GeV, corresponding to |Q²| up to about 1 (GeV/c)².
- For the single-nucleon case at θ_N = 0°, the range of |Q²| is up to about 1 (GeV/c)², and the range of neutrino energy k is up to about 0.75 GeV at the cross-section peak.
- At θ_N = 40°, the ranges of k and |Q²| increase compared to θ_N = 0°, with a kinematical singularity possible when the denominator of k = m_N T_N / (p_N cosθ_N - T_N) vanishes.
- The strangeness parameters tested are ρ_s = -0.3 to 0.9, μ_s = -0.1 to 0.3, and g^s_A = -0.4 to 0.0, with central values ρ_s = 0.3, μ_s = 0.1, g^s_A = -0.2.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic model for deuteron electrodisintegration (Jeschonnek et al., 2008, 2009) and charged-current neutrino reactions on the deuteron (Moreno et al., 2014; Van Orden et al., 2017) to neutral-current neutrino scattering. It builds on parity-violating electron scattering constraints on strangeness form factors (Gonzalez-Jimenez et al., 2014) and lattice QCD calculations of strangeness contributions (Sufian et al., 2016, 2018; Djukanovic et al., 2019). The approach enables future experimental tests of the Standard Model by comparing neutral-current neutrino scattering with charge-changing neutrino reactions and parity-conserving or parity-violating electron scattering.
