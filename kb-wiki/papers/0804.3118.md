# Current quark mass dependence of nucleon magnetic moments and radii

> A calculation of the current-quark-mass-dependence of nucleon static electromagnetic properties is necessary in order to use observational data as a means to place constraints on the variation of Nature's fundamental parameters. A Poincare' covariant Faddeev equation, which describes baryons as composites of confined-quarks and -nonpointlike-diquarks, is used to calculate this dependence The results indicate that, like observables dependent on the nucleons' magnetic moments, quantities sensitive to their magnetic and charge radii, such as the energy levels and transition frequencies in Hydrogen and Deuterium, might also provide a tool with which to place limits on the allowed variation in Nature's constants.

## Key claim

The paper calculates how nucleon static electromagnetic properties (magnetic moments and charge/magnetic radii) depend on the current quark mass, finding that these quantities all decrease with increasing quark mass, and that the response from the dressed-quark core is only about 8 ± 3% of that arising from the pseudoscalar meson cloud. The authors hedge that their results are "reliable estimates" and that their estimation of loop contributions is "cruder" than prior work, but provide independent confirmation of the sign and magnitude of effects reported in Flambaum (2004).

## Method

A Poincaré covariant Faddeev equation was solved for the nucleon as a bound state of dressed quarks and nonpointlike scalar and axial-vector diquark correlations, with the kernel specified by dressed-quark propagators and diquark Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes. The nucleon-photon vertex was constructed to satisfy the Ward-Takahashi identity for on-shell nucleons, and the electromagnetic form factors were computed on Q² ∈ (0,1] GeV² using adaptive Monte Carlo integration with 10⁸ sample points for two-loop diagrams. Pseudoscalar meson loop contributions were added via a regulated chiral perturbation theory estimate with a regularization scale λ = 0.3 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Diquark masses: scalar diquark m_{[ud]₀⁺} = 0.7–0.8 GeV, axial-vector diquark m_{(uu)₁⁺} = m_{(ud)₁⁺} = m_{(dd)₁⁺} = 0.9–1.0 GeV
- Diquark electromagnetic sizes: r_{[ud]₀⁺} ≈ 0.7 fm, r_{(ud)₁⁺} ∼ 0.8 fm
- Nucleon and Δ masses used in the model: M_N = 1.18 GeV, m_Δ = 1.33 GeV (inflated to allow for pion cloud)
- Quark-core magnetic moments at physical current-quark mass: μ_n^{q(qq)} = -1.51, μ_p^{q(qq)} = 2.55
- Pseudoscalar loop contributions at physical pion mass: μ_n^π = -0.40, μ_p^π = 0.24
- Total magnetic moments (core + loop): μ_n = -1.91, μ_p = 2.79 (matching experiment)
- Renormalization group invariant ratios for total quantities: -δμ_n/μ_n / δm/m = 0.186, -δμ_p/μ_p / δm/m = 0.139, -δr_n²/r_n² / δm/m = 0.483, -δr_p²/r_p² / δm/m = 0.202, -δ(r_n^μ)²/(r_n^μ)² / δm/m = 0.350, -δ(r_p^μ)²/(r_p^μ)² / δm/m = 0.356
- Diquark σ-term: σ_qq ≈ 26 MeV
- Current-quark mass domain: m = 3 MeV + j × 1 MeV, j = 0,1,…,7

## Context

This work extends the approach of Flambaum (2004), which extrapolated lattice QCD results from near the strange-quark mass, by performing an internally consistent calculation directly at the physical light-quark current mass. The results confirm the magnitude and sign of the magnetic moment variation reported there, with the authors' values larger by a factor of about 1.5. The calculation enables the use of observational data from atomic clocks (Flambaum 2006, 2007), quasar absorption spectra (Tzanavaris 2004), and the Oklo natural nuclear reactor (Flambaum 2002, Dmitriev 2002) to place constraints on the temporal variation of Nature's fundamental parameters, and suggests that observables sensitive to nucleon charge and magnetic radii (such as hydrogen and deuterium energy levels) may provide additional tools for such constraints.
