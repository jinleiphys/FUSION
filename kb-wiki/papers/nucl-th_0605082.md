# Extended Partial-Wave Analysis of piN Scattering Data

> We present results from a comprehensive partial-wave analysis of pi+-p elastic scattering and charge-exchange data, covering the region from threshold to 2.6 GeV in the lab pion kinetic energy, employing a coupled-channel formalism to simultaneously fit pi-p-->eta n data to 0.8 GeV. Our main result, solution SP06, utilizes a complete set of forward and fixed-t dispersion relation constraints applied to the piN elastic amplitude. The results of these analyses are compared with previous solutions in terms of their resonance spectra and preferred values for couplings and low-energy parameters.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new energy-dependent partial-wave analysis solution (SP06) for pion-nucleon scattering that extends the energy range from 2.1 GeV to 2.6 GeV in lab pion kinetic energy, but the abstract glosses over the fact that the fit to higher energies is possible without degrading the description of data below 2.1 GeV only because the analysis employs a coupled-channel formalism and dispersion relation constraints, and that some resonance structures remain ambiguous or questionable.

## Method

The authors performed a comprehensive partial-wave analysis of pi+-p elastic scattering and charge-exchange data from threshold to 2.6 GeV in lab pion kinetic energy, using a coupled-channel formalism to simultaneously fit pi-p to eta n data up to 0.8 GeV. They applied a complete set of forward and fixed-t dispersion relation constraints to the piN elastic amplitude, and fitted partial waves up to l=8. The analysis included 13344 pi+p, 11967 pi-p, 2933 charge-exchange, and 257 eta-production data points, with systematic uncertainties treated as normalization factors in a modified chi-squared minimization.

## Key numbers

- Energy range extended from 2.1 GeV to 2.6 GeV in lab pion kinetic energy, corresponding to a 200 MeV increase in center-of-mass energy
- Database includes 13344 pi+p, 11967 pi-p, 2933 charge-exchange, and 257 eta-production data points
- Added 274 pi+p and 271 pi-p differential cross sections from 20 to 40 MeV from TRIUMF
- Added 648 charge-exchange data between 520 and 620 MeV from BNL-AGS Crystal Ball Collaboration
- Added 84 eta-production data between 560 and 620 MeV from BNL-AGS Crystal Ball Collaboration
- Extracted piN coupling constant g^2/4pi = 13.76 +/- 0.01, consistent with previous FA02 result
- Sigma term changed by less than 2 MeV between FA02 and SP06 solutions
- Dispersion relation constraints applied up to T_pi = 1 GeV and t = -0.4 (GeV/c)^2

## Context

This work extends the ongoing George Washington University (GW) partial-wave analyses (FA02, SM95, FA93, SM90, FA84) and compares results with the Karlsruhe-Helsinki (KH) and Carnegie-Mellon-Berkeley (CMB) analyses. The extended energy range to 2.6 GeV allows coverage of the resonance region more completely, enabling future fits to single-pion photoproduction data from Jefferson Lab experiments. The analysis addresses the "missing resonance" problem by showing that many 3-star and lower-rated states claimed in KH and CMB fits are not required to fit the piN database, suggesting these states should be established in reactions where they couple more strongly.
