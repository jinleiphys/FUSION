# Ground-gamma band mixing and evolution of collectivity in even-even neutron-rich nuclei with 40<Z<50

> We propose an extended band mixing formalism capable of describing the ground-gamma band interaction in a wide range of collective spectra beyond the regions of well deformed nuclei. On this basis we explain the staggering effects observed in the gamma bands of Mo, Ru and Pd nuclei providing a consistent interpretation of new experimental data in the neutron rich region. As a result the systematic behavior of the odd-even staggering effect and some general characteristics of the spectrum such as the mutual disposition of the bands, the interaction strength and the band structures is explained as the manifestation of respective changes in collective dynamics of the system.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an extended band mixing formalism that can describe ground-gamma band interaction in collective spectra beyond well-deformed nuclei, but the key hedge is that the additional term generating the energy dependence of the type L(L+n) is not derived in explicit form, and the authors state that any particular assumption for its construction would impose a limitation on the generality of analysis.

## Method

The authors modify the Vector Boson Model (VBM) formalism by introducing a global parameter of collectivity `n` that replaces the rotational term L(L+1) with a generalized expression L(L+n), where n=1 gives rotor-like spacing and large n gives vibrator-like spacing. They apply a chi-squared minimization procedure at fixed discrete n-values (1-100) to fit the model parameters A, B, C, a, and b to experimental ground band and gamma band energy levels up to angular momentum L=8 hbar for Mo, Ru, and Pd isotopes in the region 40<Z<50.

## Key numbers

* For Mo isotopes, the collectivity parameter n ranges from n=26 (100Mo, near U(5) vibrational limit) to n=3 (106Mo and 108Mo, near SU(3) rotational limit), with RMS deviations sigma_E between 8.07 keV and 31.65 keV.
* For Ru isotopes, n ranges from n=15 (104Ru) to n=5 (110Ru and 112Ru), with sigma_E between 15.52 keV and 23.16 keV.
* For Pd isotopes, n ranges from n=16 (108Pd) to n=12 (110Pd), with sigma_E between 22.75 keV and 28.68 keV.
* The experimental band splitting ratio Delta E_2 (relative displacement of gamma band 2+ and ground band 2+) varies from 2.0-3.2 in Mo, 1.2-2.0 in Ru, and 1.09-1.18 in Pd, compared to values of 7-25 in rare-earth and actinide regions.
* The experimental excitation energy ratio R_4 = E(4)/E(2) ranges from 2.12 (100Mo) to 3.04 (106Mo) for Mo, 2.48 (104Ru) to 2.75 (108Ru, 110Ru) for Ru, and 2.41 (108Pd) to 2.57 (116Pd) for Pd.

## Context

This work extends the Vector Boson Model band mixing formalism previously applied to rare-earth and actinide nuclei (Minkov 1997, 2000) into the transitional region 40<Z<50, where nuclei like 104Mo have been suggested as X(5) phase transition candidates (Bizzeti 2002) and 104Ru and 108Pd as possible E(5) critical behavior candidates (Fortunato 2001, Zhang 2002). The formalism enables systematic interpretation of new experimental data from fission fragment studies using Eurogam, Euroball, and Gammasphere arrays, and provides a framework for understanding how the same ground-gamma band coupling mechanism persists while collective band structure changes from rotational to vibrational modes.
