# In-medium kinetic theory of $D$ mesons and heavy-flavor transport coefficients

> We extend the kinetic theory of $D$ mesons to accommodate thermal and off-shell effects due to the medium modification of the heavy-meson spectral functions. From the Kadanoff-Baym approach we derive the off-shell Fokker-Planck equation which encodes the heavy-flavor transport coefficients. We analyze the thermal width (damping rate) of $D$ mesons due to their scattering off light mesons, focusing on new in-medium effects: off-shell corrections, inelastic channels, and the contribution of the Landau cut. We obtain that the latter effect (absent for vacuum scattering amplitudes) brings sizable corrections at moderate temperatures. We discuss how the heavy-flavor transport coefficients, like the drag and diffusion coefficients, are modified in matter. We find that the $D$-meson spatial diffusion coefficient matches smoothly to the latest results of lattice-QCD calculations and Bayesian analyses at higher temperatures.

## Key claim

The authors extend the kinetic theory of $D$ mesons to include thermal and off-shell effects from medium-modified spectral functions, finding that the Landau cut contribution (absent for vacuum scattering amplitudes) brings sizable corrections at moderate temperatures, and that the $D$-meson spatial diffusion coefficient matches smoothly to lattice-QCD and Bayesian analysis results at higher temperatures.

## Method

The authors derive an off-shell Fokker-Planck equation from the Kadanoff-Baym approach using a $T$-matrix approximation with self-consistent in-medium unitarized scattering amplitudes. They compute the $D$-meson thermal width (damping rate) from scattering off light mesons ($\pi$, $K$, $\bar{K}$, $\eta$) in a hot mesonic bath, incorporating off-shell corrections, inelastic channels, and the Landau cut contribution, then extract heavy-flavor transport coefficients such as drag and diffusion coefficients.

## Key numbers

* The $D$-meson spatial diffusion coefficient matches lattice-QCD results from Banerjee (2011), Kaczmarek (2014), Francis (2015), Brambilla (2020), and Altenkort (2020) and Bayesian analyses from Ke (2018) at temperatures close to and above $T_c$.
* The thermal width (damping rate) receives sizable corrections from the Landau cut contribution at moderate temperatures, where this effect is absent for vacuum scattering amplitudes.
* A total of 10 coupled channels are needed for the $D$-meson interaction, including $D$ or $D_s$ mesons scattering with $\pi$, $K$, $\bar{K}$, and $\eta$ mesons.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous self-consistent calculations of $D$-meson properties in a hot mesonic bath (Montana 2020, Montana 2020) by deriving the off-shell kinetic equation and transport coefficients. It contrasts with earlier approaches by Laine (2011), He (2011), and Ghosh (2011) that used vacuum scattering amplitudes or parametrized interactions, and with the unitarized calculations by Tolos (2013) and Torres-Rincon (2013) that neglected off-shell and Landau cut effects. The results enable more consistent modeling of heavy-flavor dynamics in heavy-ion collisions and provide a bridge between hadronic and quark-gluon plasma phases.
