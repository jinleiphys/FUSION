# Electric and magnetic dipole strength in $^{58}$Ni from forward-angle inelastic proton scattering

> The aim of the present work is a state-by-state analysis of possible E1 and M1 transitions in $^{58}$Ni with a high-resolution (p,p') experiment at 295 MeV and very forward angles including 0° and a comparison to results from studies of the dipole strength with the $(γ,γ')$ and (e,e') reactions. The E1 and M1 cross sections of individual peaks in the spectra are deduced with a multipole decomposition analysis and converted to reduced E1 and spin-M1 transition strengths using the virtual photon and the unit cross-section method, respectively. Despite the high level density good agreement is obtained for the deduced excitation energies of J = 1 states in the three types of experiments indicating that the same states are excited. The B(E1) and B(M1) strengths from the $(γ,γ^\prime)$ experiments are systematically smaller than in the present work because of the lack of information on branching ratios to lower-lying excited states and the competition of particle emission. Fair agreement with the B(M1) strengths extracted from the (e,e') data is obtained after removal of E1 transitions uniquely assigned in the present work, which belong to a low-energy toroidal mode with unusual properties mimicking M1 excitations in electron scattering. The experimental M1 strength distribution is compared to large-scale shell-model calculations with the effective GXPF1A and KB3G interactions. They provide a good description of the isospin splitting and the running sum of the M1 strength. A quenching factor 0.74 for the spin-isospin part of the M1 operator is needed to attain quantitative agreement with the data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that high-resolution forward-angle inelastic proton scattering at 295 MeV can resolve individual E1 and M1 transitions in 58Ni up to about 13 MeV excitation energy, but the extracted B(E1) and B(M1) strengths are systematically larger than those from (γ,γ') experiments because the photon-scattering results lack information on branching ratios to lower-lying states and competition from particle emission.

## Method

A 295 MeV proton beam was scattered on an enriched 58Ni target, and scattered protons were detected with the Grand Raiden magnetic spectrometer at angles of 0°, 2.5°, and 4.5°. Multipole decomposition analysis (MDA) of angular distributions extracted E1 and M1 cross sections for individual peaks, which were converted to reduced transition strengths using the virtual photon method for E1 and the unit cross-section method for M1. The experimental M1 strength distribution was compared to large-scale shell-model calculations using the GXPF1A and KB3G effective interactions.

## Key numbers

- Energy resolution of 22 keV FWHM achieved for the (p,p') spectra
- 11 E1 and 26 M1 transitions uniquely identified in the excitation energy region 6-13 MeV
- 22 dipole transitions with preference for either E1 or M1 multipolarity and 57 transitions with uncertain multipolarity were found
- A quenching factor of 0.74 for the spin-isospin part of the M1 operator was needed to achieve quantitative agreement between shell-model calculations and experimental data
- Total systematic uncertainty of 6.5% for the differential cross sections
- Unit cross section σ̂_GT = 4.34 mb/sr for 58Ni at 295 MeV incident proton energy

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dipole strength in 58Ni using (γ,γ') experiments by Bauwens (2000), Schiller (2013), and Shizuma (2024), and (e,e') experiments by Metzger (1987). It resolves discrepancies between these earlier measurements by identifying E1 transitions belonging to a low-energy toroidal mode that mimic M1 excitations in electron scattering, as discussed by von Neumann-Cosel (2023). The results enable improved comparisons with shell-model calculations of spin-M1 strength and provide independent constraints on the quenching of axial strength in nuclei, complementing Gamow-Teller studies by Fujita (2007) and Hagemann (2005).
