# Giant dipole resonance in $^{88}$Mo from phonon damping model's strength functions averaged over temperature and angular momentum distributions

> The line shapes of giant dipole resonance (GDR) in the decay of the compound nucleus $^{88}$Mo, which is formed after the fusion-evaporation reaction $^{48}$Ti + $^{40}$Ca at various excitation energies $E^{*}$ from 58 to 308 MeV, are generated by averaging the GDR strength functions predicted within the phonon damping model (PDM) using the empirical probabilities for temperature and angular momentum. The average strength functions are compared with the PDM strength functions calculated at the mean temperature and mean angular momentum, which are obtained by averaging the values of temperature and angular momentum using the same temperature and angular-momentum probability distributions, respectively. It is seen that these two ways of generating the GDR linear line shape yield very similar results. It is also shown that the GDR width approaches a saturation at angular momentum $J\geq$ 50$\hbar$ at $T=$ 4 MeV and at $J\geq$ 70$\hbar$ at any $T$.

## Key claim
The GDR line shape obtained by averaging strength functions over the full temperature and angular-momentum probability distributions is nearly identical to the strength function calculated at the single pair of mean values (average temperature and average angular momentum), so the computationally expensive averaging over many cascades can be avoided.

## Method
The phonon damping model (PDM) was used to calculate GDR strength functions for the compound nucleus ⁸⁸Mo formed in the fusion-evaporation reaction ⁴⁸Ti + ⁴⁰Ca at nine excitation energies from 58 to 308 MeV. The temperature and angular-momentum probability distributions were generated by the GEMINI++ statistical decay code, and the average strength function was computed by weighting individual PDM strength functions with these empirical probabilities. This average was then compared to the PDM strength function evaluated at the mean temperature and mean angular momentum obtained from the same distributions.

## Key numbers
- Excitation energies studied: 58, 67, 80, 126, 172, 194, 217, 262, and 308 MeV (from beam energies 150–700 MeV).
- Average temperatures range from \(\overline{T} = 1.46\) MeV at \(E^* = 58\) MeV to \(\overline{T} = 3.37\) MeV at \(E^* = 308\) MeV.
- Average angular momenta range from \(\overline{J} = 22\hbar\) at \(E^* = 58\) MeV to \(\overline{J} = 38\hbar\) at \(E^* = 308\) MeV, saturating at 38–39\(\hbar\) for \(E^* \geq 172\) MeV.
- GDR width saturates at angular momentum \(J \geq 50\hbar\) when \(T = 4\) MeV, and at \(J \geq 70\hbar\) at any temperature.
- PDM parameters: \({\cal F}_1 = 0.071\) MeV, \({\cal F}_2 = 0.163\) MeV, \(E_{GDR} = 15\) MeV, smoothing parameter \(\varepsilon = 0.5\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the phonon damping model (PDM) previously developed by Dang and collaborators (2001–2013) to describe GDR broadening via coupling to noncollective particle-hole, particle-particle, and hole-hole configurations. It directly addresses a question left open by Gervais et al. (2004) and Eisenman et al. (2007), who incorporated theoretical strength functions into full statistical decay calculations for ¹²⁰Sn but did not test whether averaging over distributions is equivalent to using mean values. The result enables simpler comparisons between theory and experiment: once average temperature and angular momentum are known, the PDM strength function at those averages can be compared directly to data without performing the full cascade averaging. The experimental data for ⁸⁸Mo, ⁹²Mo, ¹⁰⁰Mo, and ¹⁰⁶Cd are taken from recent measurements by the Krakow group (2012–2013) and from earlier work by Kicinska-Habior et al. (1997) and Lucchini et al. (2012).
