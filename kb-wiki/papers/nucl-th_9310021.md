# Minijet-associated Dilepton Production in Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> Dilepton production associated with minijets is calculated in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions using the first order approximation of the dilepton fragmentation functions of quarks and gluons. The full QCD evolution of the fragmentation functions is also studied. We find that the dilepton pairs from the fragmentation of minijets are comparable to direct Drell-Yan at $\sqrt{s}=200$ AGeV for small dilepton invariant mass $M\sim$ 1-2 GeV/c$^2$ while dominant over a large range of mass at $\sqrt{s}=6400$ AGeV.

## Key claim

Dilepton pairs from the fragmentation of minijets are comparable to direct Drell-Yan production at RHIC energies (sqrt(s)=200 AGeV) for small dilepton invariant mass M ~ 1-2 GeV/c^2, but become dominant over a large range of mass at LHC energies (sqrt(s)=6400 AGeV), with the caveat that the first-order calculation of the z-integrated dilepton fragmentation functions is a very good approximation to the full QCD-evolved result across the whole M^2 range.

## Method

The authors calculated dilepton fragmentation functions for quarks and gluons in perturbative QCD using the leading logarithm approximation in an axial gauge, deriving both the first-order result and the full QCD evolution via coupled evolution equations. They then convolved the z-integrated dilepton fragmentation functions with 2-to-2 minijet production cross sections, using Duke-Owens parton distributions and nuclear shadowing parametrizations, to compute the associated dilepton production in heavy ion collisions at midrapidity (Y=0) as a function of invariant mass M.

## Key numbers

- At sqrt(s)=200 AGeV (RHIC), minijet-associated dileptons are comparable to direct Drell-Yan for M ~ 1-2 GeV/c^2
- At sqrt(s)=6400 AGeV (LHC), minijet-associated dileptons dominate Drell-Yan up to M ~ 10 GeV/c^2
- The first-order QCD corrections to the QED fragmentation function are controlled by a factor C ~ 1 - kappa/(e^kappa - 1), where kappa = ln[ln(Q^2/Lambda^2)/ln(M^2/Lambda^2)]; for Q=5 GeV and M=1 GeV/c^2, the overall QCD corrections to the lowest-order result are about 10%, except near z=0 and 1
- The minijet-associated dilepton production uses a lower cutoff p_0 = 2 GeV/c for the transverse momentum integration
- The z-integrated fragmentation functions are proportional to ln^2(Q^2_max/M^2), with Q_max chosen as either p_T or 2p_T

## Context

This work extends the study of electromagnetic signals from quark-gluon plasma, specifically addressing the background from initial parton scatterings (minijets) to thermal dilepton signals. The authors use a different approach from the complete O(alpha^2 alpha_s^2) matrix element calculations of Drell-Yan processes (DYK2), instead calculating dilepton fragmentation functions similar to real photon fragmentation functions (OWEN, REYA) but with the advantage that the fixed invariant mass M provides a natural infrared cutoff. The results are qualitatively similar to minijet-associated photon production in Gupta (1993) and enable better discrimination of thermal dilepton signals from QGP in the mass window M ~ 1-3 GeV/c^2 at RHIC and LHC energies.
