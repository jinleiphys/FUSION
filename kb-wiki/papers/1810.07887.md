# Consistent mean-field description of the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C optical potential at low energies and the astrophysical $S$ factor

> The nuclear mean-field potential built up by the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C interaction at energies relevant for the carbon burning process is calculated in the double-folding model (DFM) using the realistic ground-state density of $^{12}$C and the CDM3Y3 density dependent nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction, with the rearrangement term properly included. To validate the use of a density dependent NN interaction in the DFM calculation in the low-energy regime, an adiabatic approximation is suggested for the nucleus-nucleus overlap density. The reliability of the nuclear mean-field potential predicted by this low-energy version of the DFM is tested in a detailed optical model analysis of the elastic $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C scattering data at energies below 10 MeV/nucleon. The folded mean-field potential is then used to study the astrophysical $S$ factor of the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C fusion in the barrier penetration model. Without any adjustment of the potential strength, our results reproduce very well the non-resonant behavior of the $S$ factor of the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C fusion over a wide range of energies.

# Consistent mean-field description of the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C optical potential at low energies and the astrophysical $S$ factor

## Key claim

The double-folding model using the CDM3Y3 density dependent nucleon-nucleon interaction with an adiabatic density approximation (ADA) for the dinuclear overlap density predicts the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C mean-field potential that, without any renormalization of the potential strength, reproduces both the elastic scattering data below 10 MeV/nucleon and the non-resonant behavior of the astrophysical $S$ factor over a wide energy range, provided the rearrangement term is properly included.

## Method

The authors calculated the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C optical potential using the double-folding model (DFM) with the CDM3Y3 density dependent nucleon-nucleon interaction and the ground-state density of $^{12}$C from a no-core shell model calculation. To treat the dinuclear overlap density at low energies, they introduced an adiabatic density approximation (ADA) where the overlap density transitions smoothly from the frozen density approximation (FDA) at the grazing distance ($R_\text{cut} \approx 6$ fm) to the central density of $^{24}$Mg at zero distance, using an exponential interpolation. The folded potential including the rearrangement term was tested in optical model analyses of elastic $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C scattering data at laboratory energies from 16 to 117.1 MeV, and then used in the barrier penetration model (BPM) to compute the astrophysical $S$ factor for $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C fusion.

## Key numbers

- The grazing distance for the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C system is chosen as $R_\text{cut} \approx 6$ fm, close to the touching distance from coupled channel analyses of sub-barrier fusion.
- The DF3 potential (HF-type plus rearrangement term with ADA) is about 90 MeV deeper at the center than the DF2 potential (HF-type plus rearrangement term with FDA).
- The volume integral per interacting nucleon pair $J_V$ of the DF3 potential ranges from 368.9 MeV fm$^3$ at $E_\text{lab}=16$ MeV to 349.6 MeV fm$^3$ at $E_\text{lab}=117.1$ MeV.
- At the lowest energies, the $J_V$ value of the DF3 potential agrees with the empirical value of $360 \pm 5$ MeV fm$^3$ of the deep Woods-Saxon potential that describes both elastic scattering and the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C resonance band.
- The total reaction cross section $\sigma_R$ from the optical model ranges from 369.0 mb at $E_\text{lab}=16$ MeV to 1399.6 mb at $E_\text{lab}=117.1$ MeV.
- The RMS charge radius used for $^{12}$C in the Coulomb potential is $R_\text{C}=3.17$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the double-folding model approach of Khoa et al. (2007, 2016) to low energies relevant for stellar carbon burning, where previous DFM calculations used density independent nucleon-nucleon interactions (e.g., Gasques et al., 2005; Aziz et al., 2015). The adiabatic density approximation is similar to that proposed by Reichstein and Malik (1971, 1976) for nuclear fission studies, and was previously attempted by Ohtsuka et al. (1987) in the energy density formalism. The deep real optical potential family obtained here is consistent with the mean-field potential shown by Kondo et al. (1998) to smoothly match the deep family from medium energies down to near the Coulomb barrier, and with the global optical potential for $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C by Brandan and McVoy (1990, 1992). The results enable a reliable mean-field prediction of the astrophysical $S$ factor for $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C fusion down to the Gamow energy (about 1.5 MeV), which can help resolve the current discrepancy between the extrapolations of Fowler et al. (1975) and Jiang et al. (2007, 2018), and the indirect Trojan horse method results of Tumino et al. (2018).
