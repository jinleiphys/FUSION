# Bounding Gauged Skyrmion Masses

> Normally, standard (ungauged) skyrmion masses are proportional to the coupling of the Skyrme term needed for stability, and so can grow to infinite magnitude with increasing coupling. In striking contrast, when skyrmions are gauged, their masses are bounded above for any Skyrme coupling, and, instead, are of the order of monopole masses, O(v/g), so that the coupling of the Skyrme term is not very important. This boundedness phenomenon and its implications are investigated.

## Key claim

When skyrmions are gauged (by promoting the diagonal subgroup of the chiral group to a gauge group), their masses are bounded above for any Skyrme coupling, in striking contrast to ungauged skyrmions whose masses grow without bound; the mass instead stabilizes to a "monopolic" scale of order O(v/g), making the Skyrme coupling strength largely irrelevant for the mass in the large-coupling limit.

## Method

The authors study the simplest SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R chiral model with the diagonal SU(2)_V subgroup gauged, using the spherically symmetric Skyrme-Wu-Yang hedgehog Ansatz for unit winding/baryon number. They derive the energy functional, obtain topological lower bounds via Bogomol'ny-type completions, and perform numerical solutions of the coupled Euler-Lagrange equations for the gauge field profile a(r) and the chiral field profile f(r) over a wide range of Skyrme coupling κ. The asymptotic behavior for large κ is analyzed analytically by noting the decoupling of the two field scales, with the gauge field attenuating inside a core radius R ~ 1.34 before the chiral field builds up.

## Key numbers

- The upper bound for the gauged skyrmion mass in the limit κ → ∞ is E(g,v,∞) ~ 12.95 v/g (in natural units), which is approximately 2.06 times the highest topological lower bound of 2π v/g.
- The subleading correction to the asymptotic mass is E(g,v,κ) = E(g,v,∞) - 6.68 (v/g) κ^{-1/2} + O(κ^{-1}).
- The Skyrme term contribution E_sk decays as ~ 1.62 κ^{-1/2} for large κ.
- The core radius where the gauge field decays to zero is R ~ 1.34, very weakly dependent on κ for large κ² ~ 1000.
- With a pion mass term added (λ = 2(m_π/gv)² = 1), the upper bound increases to E(g,v,κ) = 17.5 v/g - 6.0 v/g κ^{-1/2} + O(κ^{-1}).

## Context

This work extends and contrasts with the standard ungauged skyrmion results of Skyrme (1961), where masses are proportional to the Skyrme coupling and grow without bound. The boundedness phenomenon is reminiscent of the 't Hooft-Polyakov monopole studied by Kirkman (1979), where the mass stabilizes as the Higgs mass is taken to infinity. The numerical branch structure of gauged skyrmions had been explored in earlier work by Arthur, Brihaye, and collaborators (various years), but the present paper provides the first explicit demonstration and analytic understanding of the upper bound. The results enable further study of additional operators involving the Yang-Mills field strength at the same dimension as the gauged Skyrme term, which the authors note should be considered in future work.
