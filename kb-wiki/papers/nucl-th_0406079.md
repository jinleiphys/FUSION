# Application of wavelets to singular integral scattering equations

> The use of orthonormal wavelet basis functions for solving singular integral scattering equations is investigated. It is shown that these basis functions lead to sparse matrix equations which can be solved by iterative techniques. The scaling properties of wavelets are used to derive an efficient method for evaluating the singular integrals. The accuracy and efficiency of the wavelet transforms is demonstrated by solving the two-body T-matrix equation without partial wave projection. The resulting matrix equation which is characteristic of multiparticle integral scattering equations is found to provide an efficient method for obtaining accurate approximate solutions to the integral equation. These results indicate that wavelet transforms may provide a useful tool for studying few-body systems.

## Key claim

Wavelet basis functions can reduce the two-body T-matrix integral equation without partial wave projection to a sparse linear system solvable by iterative techniques, with the caveat that the method's efficiency depends on preconditioning the system by inverting the overlap matrix **N** before applying the wavelet transform.

## Method

The authors solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the two-body T-matrix in momentum space using a Malfliet-Tjon III potential, without performing a partial wave decomposition. They mapped the semi-infinite momentum interval and the angular interval onto finite rectangles, projected the resulting singular integral equation onto a direct-product basis of Daubechies-3 wavelet functions, and used the scaling properties of the wavelets to evaluate the singular integrals. The dense linear system was preconditioned by inverting the overlap matrix, transformed to the wavelet basis via a fast orthogonal transformation, truncated by discarding matrix elements below a threshold, and solved using the complex biconjugate gradient method.

## Key numbers

* At 300 MeV lab energy, the on-shell T-matrix at 90 degrees converged to Re(T) = 0.491760404 and Im(T) = 0.286116271 with M_k = 7 and M_u = 7 basis functions in the momentum and angular variables, respectively.
* At 800 MeV lab energy, the on-shell T-matrix at 90 degrees converged to Re(T) = 0.454931334 and Im(T) = 0.110788571 with M_k = 7 and M_u = 7 basis functions.
* With a truncation threshold of epsilon = 10^{-5}, the wavelet-transformed matrix at 800 MeV (M_k=4, M_u=3) retained only 19% of the full matrix elements.
* The preconditioned biconjugate gradient method reduced the residual error to 8.09 x 10^{-13} after 50 iterations, compared to 1.01 x 10^{-4} for the non-preconditioned method.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous test of wavelet methods on the s-wave Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a Malfliet Tjon V potential (Kessler, Payne, and Polyzou, 2003), where 96% of kernel matrix elements could be eliminated with errors of a few parts per million. The present calculation addresses the more challenging case of the full two-variable integral equation without partial waves, which has the same number of continuous variables as the three-body Faddeev equations with partial waves. The results suggest that wavelet methods could be applied to relativistic three-body calculations, which are just beginning to be solved (Stadler; Gloeckle), by reducing the size of momentum-space calculations to levels comparable to configuration-space banded-matrix methods.
