# Coulomb screening effect on the nuclear-pasta structure

> Using the density functional theory (DFT) with the relativistic mean field (RMF) model, we study the non-uniform state of nuclear matter, ``nuclear pasta''. We self-consistently include the Coulomb interaction together with other interactions. It is found that the Coulomb screening effect is significant for each pasta structure but not for the bulk equation of state (EOS) of the nuclear pasta phase.

# Coulomb screening effect on the nuclear-pasta structure

## Key claim

The Coulomb screening effect significantly alters the sequence and existence of individual pasta geometries (droplet, rod, slab, tube, bubble) in symmetric nuclear matter, but has only a small effect on the bulk equation of state (EOS) of the nuclear pasta phase. The spherical-hole (bubble) configuration disappears entirely when Coulomb screening is included self-consistently.

## Method

The authors used density functional theory (DFT) with the relativistic mean field (RMF) model, solving the coupled equations of motion for nucleons, electrons, and meson mean fields together with the Poisson equation for the Coulomb potential in a self-consistent manner. They employed the Wigner-Seitz cell approximation with three geometries (3D sphere, 2D cylinder, 1D slab) and optimized the cell size with 1 fm precision, selecting the lowest-energy configuration among geometries. To isolate the Coulomb screening effect, they compared full calculations against a uniform-electron approximation where the Coulomb potential was discarded only in the Poisson equation but retained elsewhere.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: approximately 0.16 fm^-3 (context: reference density for pasta dissolution into uniform matter)
- Sigma meson mass adjusted to approximately 400 MeV to reproduce finite nuclei binding energies; using the popular value of approximately 500 MeV would overbind finite nuclei by about 3 MeV per nucleon
- Wigner-Seitz cell grid points: approximately 100 (context: numerical resolution for solving field equations)
- Cell size optimization precision: 1 fm (context: used to determine phase diagram boundaries)

## Context

This work extends prior studies of nuclear pasta structures (Ravenhall 1983; Hashimoto 1984; Williams 1985; Oyamatsu 1993; Lorenz 1993; Maruyama 1998; Gentile 2000; Gentile 2002) that treated the electron density as a uniform background and discarded the rearrangement effect of charged particles by the Coulomb interaction. It is part of a broader project by the authors on structured mixed phases in first-order phase transitions, including hadron-quark deconfinement and kaon condensation (Voskresensky et al.; Maruyama et al. for kaon condensation). The results enable more realistic modeling of supernova matter and neutron star crust properties, though the authors note that the effective interaction choice also influences pasta structure appearance, as discussed by Oyamatsu (2004).
