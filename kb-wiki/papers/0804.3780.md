# Self-consistent single-nucleon and single-Lambda potentials in strange nuclear matter with the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach

> We calculate self-consistent single-nucleon and single-Lambda potentials in hyperonic matter for different Lambda concentrations. The predictions include relativistic Dirac effects as reported previously in a calculation of the Lambda binding energy in symmetric nuclear matter. We discuss the dependence on momentum, density, and Lambda fraction.

## Key claim

Self-consistent single-nucleon and single-Lambda potentials in hyperonic matter are calculated including relativistic Dirac effects, but the Lambda-Lambda interaction is neglected and the Sigma hyperon is treated as a virtual particle with free-space energies, limiting the calculation to low Lambda concentrations.

## Method

The author solves the coupled self-consistency problem for the nucleon and Lambda single-particle potentials using the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach, with the Bonn B potential for the nucleon-nucleon sector and the Juelich NY05 potential for the nucleon-hyperon sector. The Bethe-Goldstone equation is solved with angle-averaged Pauli blocking and dispersion, and Dirac effects are applied to both the NN and N-Lambda potentials through the Lambda effective mass. The calculation starts from symmetric nuclear matter with a Lambda impurity and then increases the Lambda concentration while keeping the total density constant.

## Key numbers

- At zero Lambda density and nucleon Fermi momentum 1.35 fm^-1, the Lambda binding energy (U_Lambda at k=0) is approximately -50 MeV (depending on the NY potential used)
- The Lambda single-particle potential at zero momentum is about -30 MeV in the calculations of Catania (2000) using different NY potentials
- Calculations are performed at nucleon Fermi momenta of 1.25 fm^-1 and 1.4 fm^-1 with Lambda fraction fixed at 20%
- Lambda fractions studied: Y_Lambda = 0, 0.1, 0.15, and 0.2, corresponding to asymmetry parameters alpha = 1, 0.8, 0.7, and 0.6
- The average energy of a nucleon in nuclear matter is approximately -16 MeV

## Context

This work extends the author's previous calculation of the Lambda binding energy in symmetric nuclear matter (Sammarruca 2008) by exploring the behavior of self-consistent single-particle potentials with varying Lambda fraction. It contrasts with earlier microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations from the Catania group (2000) which used the Nijmegen (1989) and older Juelich (1989, 1994) NY meson-exchange potentials, finding large quantitative differences due to the different NY potentials. The results provide a foundation for studies of hypernuclei based on generalized mass formulas and predictions of stability for systems where nucleons are replaced with Lambdas, and may inform calculations of the equation of state for neutron star matter.
