# Thouless-Valatin moment of inertia and removal of the spurious mode in the linear response theory

> Symmetry breaking at the mean-field level leads to an appearance of a symmetry restoring Nambu-Goldstone (NG) mode in the linear response theory. These modes represent a special kind of collective motion of the system. However, they can interfere with the calculated intrinsic physical excitations and, hence, they are often called as spurious modes. I discuss translational and rotational NG mode and the inertia parameter associated with these modes, by using the finite amplitude method formalism. I will also discuss how to remove spurious mode from the calculated transition strength function.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the Thouless-Valatin (TV) inertia parameter for translational and rotational Nambu-Goldstone modes can be obtained from the finite amplitude method (FAM) strength function at zero energy, and that the associated spurious modes can be removed from the calculated transition strength function, but the removal procedure requires the conjugate operator Q, which for the rotational case must be constructed from the response to the P operator at zero energy rather than from an analytic form.

## Method
The author uses the finite amplitude method (FAM) within the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) framework, starting from a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) ground state obtained with the SkM* Skyrme energy density functional. The FAM equations are solved iteratively for each frequency omega, and the TV inertia parameter M_NG is extracted from the strength function S(P;0) at zero energy for the symmetry-breaking operator P. For the rotational case, where the conjugate operator Q has no analytic form, Q is constructed from the FAM amplitudes at zero energy using equation (8). The spurious mode is removed by subtracting projections onto the P and Q operators from the calculated FAM amplitudes.

## Key numbers
- For the translational NG mode in 168Er, the TV inertia mass parameter obtained was M_NG = 4.074379 MeV^-1 fm^-2, corresponding to a mass number A_NG ≈ 167.951, deviating only 0.03% from the actual mass A = 168.
- For the rotational NG mode in 168Er, the rotational moment of inertia obtained was M_NG = 34.917269 MeV^-1, which in a simple rotational picture corresponds to a 2+ state energy of E(2+) = 86 keV for the ground state rotational band.
- The experimental energy of the 2+ state in the ground state rotational band of 168Er is 79.8 keV (IAEA data).
- The HFB ground state of 168Er has a deformation parameter beta = 0.32.

## Context
This work extends the finite amplitude method formalism introduced by Nakatsukasa (2007) to compute the Thouless-Valatin inertia parameter and remove spurious modes, building on earlier demonstrations by Hinohara (2015) that the TV inertia can be obtained from the FAM strength function. The method enables microscopic input for collective Hamiltonian models (Hinohara 2010, Washiyama 2017) and allows computation of transition strength functions free from spurious translational and rotational modes for any electromagnetic operator, with the rotational TV inertia shown to coincide with the result from self-consistent cranking calculations at zero cranking frequency (Petrik 2018).
