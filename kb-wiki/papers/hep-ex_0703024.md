# Small-x QCD studies with CMS at the LHC

> The capabilities of the CMS experiment to study the low-x parton structure and QCD evolution in the proton and the nucleus at LHC energies are presented through four different measurements, to be carried out in Pb-Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV: (i) the charged hadron rapidity density $dN_{ch}/dη$ and (ii) the ultraperipheral (photo)production of Upsilon; and in p-p at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV: (iii) inclusive forward jets and (iv) Mueller-Navelet dijets (separated by $Deltaη\gtrsim$ 8).

## Key claim

The CMS experiment can probe parton saturation effects in the proton and nucleus at LHC energies through four specific measurements, but the actual sensitivity to saturation depends on the ability to measure low-x parton densities down to x ~ 10^{-6} in forward rapidity regions, with systematic uncertainties as large as ~30% for some channels.

## Method

The paper presents simulation studies for four observables: (i) charged hadron rapidity density dN_ch/dη in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV, using hit counting in the innermost Si pixel layer; (ii) ultraperipheral photoproduction of Upsilon mesons in Pb-Pb, using the STARLIGHT Monte Carlo with full CMS detector simulation; (iii) inclusive forward jet production in p-p at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV, using PYTHIA 6.403 at particle-level with HF calorimeter granularity; and (iv) Mueller-Navelet dijet production in p-p at 14 TeV with large pseudorapidity separation, using PYTHIA events with specific kinematic cuts.

## Key numbers

- Expected PbPb charged hadron multiplicity at mid-rapidity: dN/dη|_(η=0) ≈ 2000
- CMS can reconstruct dN_ch/dη on an event-by-event basis within ~2% of the true primary multiplicity
- For Upsilon photoproduction in Pb-Pb: Lorentz factor γ = 2930, maximum photon energies ω_max ≈ 100 GeV, gamma-gamma c.m. energy W_max ≈ 160 GeV, gamma-Pb c.m. energy W_max ≈ 1 TeV
- x values probed in gamma-Pb → Upsilon processes at y = 2.5 can be as low as x ~ 10^{-4}
- Expected Upsilon yield: ~400 events with 0.5 nb^{-1} integrated PbPb luminosity
- Forward jet production in p-p at 14 TeV probes PDFs down to x_2 ≈ 10^{-6} for jets with E_T ≈ 20-100 GeV
- Systematic uncertainties for forward jet studies can be as large as ~30%
- Mueller-Navelet dijet cross section reduction from gluon saturation effects: factor of ~2 for jets separated by Δη ≈ 9

## Context

This work extends the low-x QCD program from HERA DIS measurements (which observed fast gluon density growth) to hadron collider energies, where saturation effects are predicted to be enhanced in nuclei by factors of A^(1/3) ≈ 6 for Pb compared to protons. The proposed measurements build on theoretical frameworks including DGLAP and BFKL evolution equations, the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism with BK/JIMWLK non-linear equations, and prior work by Kharzeev and Levin (2001) and Armesto et al. (2004) on CGC predictions at RHIC energies. These measurements would enable direct experimental tests of gluon saturation and constrain nuclear parton distribution functions, which are essential for interpreting heavy-ion collision final-state properties.
