# Primordial deuterium abundance from calculations of $p(n,γ)$ and $d(p,γ)$ reactions within potential-model approach

> The $p(n,γ)$ and $d(p,γ)$ reactions are key nuclear inputs for Big Bang nucleosynthesis. In this work, both reactions are analyzed within a consistent two-body potential framework based on the Malfliet-Tjon interaction, including contributions from both $E1$ and $M1$ transitions. A single scaling factor $λ$ controlling the low-energy scattering dynamics is constrained by the $p(n,γ)$ and propagated consistently to the $d(p,γ)$. The obtained abundance, $\mathrm{D/H} = 2.479^{+0.350}_{-0.177}\times 10^{-5}$, is in good agreement with values inferred from metal-poor damped Lyman-$α$ systems. The modest variations of $λ$ lead to a significant change in the predicted $\mathrm{D/H}$ ratio and light-element abundances.

## Key claim
The authors use a consistent two-body potential-model framework based on the Malfliet-Tjon interaction to calculate both the p(n,γ) and d(p,γ) reactions, finding that a single scaling factor λ constrained by the thermal p(n,γ) cross section can be propagated to the d(p,γ) reaction, yielding a primordial deuterium abundance of D/H = 2.479^{+0.350}_{-0.177} × 10^{-5} that agrees with observational values, though the uncertainty is asymmetric and reaches up to 14% due to nonlinear propagation through the BBN network.

## Method
The authors solve the two-body Schrödinger equation with Malfliet-Tjon potentials for both bound and scattering states, including E1 and M1 electromagnetic transitions. The bound-state potential is fixed by reproducing the empirical nucleon separation energy, while the scattering potential is scaled by a factor λ constrained by the measured thermal neutron capture cross section of p(n,γ) at 25.3 meV. For the d(p,γ) reaction, the effective pd scattering interaction is approximated as Vpd = Vnp + Vpp = 2Vnp, and the resulting cross sections are used to derive Maxwellian-averaged reaction rates implemented in the PArthENoPE BBN code.

## Key numbers
- Thermal neutron capture cross section for p(n,γ): σth = 332.6 mb, in agreement with experimental value of 332.6 ± 0.7 mb
- Scaling factor for p(n,γ): λpng = 0.734 ± 0.023 (δλ/λ = 0.0313)
- Scaling factor for d(p,γ): λdpg = 1.470 ± 0.046
- Astrophysical S factor at zero energy for d(p,γ): S(0) = 0.228 ± 0.015 eV·b
- Primordial deuterium abundance: D/H = 2.479^{+0.350}_{-0.177} × 10^{-5}
- Observed deuterium abundance from Cooke et al. (2018): D/H = (2.527 ± 0.030) × 10^{-5}
- Cross section at En = 550 keV (Ec.m. = 275 keV): calculated 37.54 μb versus experimental 35.2 ± 2.4 μb from Nagai et al. (1997)

## Context
This work extends previous potential-model studies of radiative capture reactions (Huang 2010, Le Anh 2024) by consistently treating both p(n,γ) and d(p,γ) within the same framework and propagating uncertainties to BBN predictions. The results are compared with ab initio calculations (Marcucci 2016) and χEFT calculations (Acharya 2022), showing qualitative agreement. The approach enables systematic exploration of how nuclear-model uncertainties in low-energy capture reactions affect cosmological observables, and the predicted deuterium abundance is consistent with observational constraints from metal-poor damped Lyman-α systems (Cooke 2018) and with recent BBN predictions using the PRIMAT Monte Carlo framework (Shen and He 2024).
