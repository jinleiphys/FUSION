# Photon production from a non-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma

> We calculate leading-order medium photon yields from a quark-gluon plasma using (3+1)-dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamics. Non-equilibrium corrections to the photon rate are taken into account using a self-consistent modification of the particle distribution functions and the corresponding anisotropic hard-loop fermionic self-energies. We present predictions for the high-energy photon spectrum and photon elliptic flow as a function of transverse momentum, shear viscosity, and initial momentum-space anisotropy. Our findings indicate that high-energy photon production is sensitive to the assumed level of initial momentum-space anisotropy of the quark-gluon plasma. As a result, it may be possible to experimentally constrain the early-time momentum-space anisotropy of the quark-gluon plasma generated in relativistic heavy-ion collisions using high-energy photon yields.

## Key claim

High-energy photon production from a quark-gluon plasma is sensitive to the assumed level of initial momentum-space anisotropy, and it may therefore be possible to experimentally constrain the early-time momentum-space anisotropy of the QGP using high-energy photon yields, but only if the non-equilibrium modifications to both the distribution functions and the screening (via the hard-loop fermionic self-energies) are treated self-consistently.

## Method

The authors compute leading-order photon production rates from Compton scattering and quark-antiquark annihilation, separating the contributions into hard-momentum and soft-momentum exchange regimes and regulating the infrared divergence using a separation scale set by the principle of minimal sensitivity. They use a spheroidal (Romatschke-Strickland) form for the non-equilibrium one-particle distribution function, which is positive definite at all momenta, and evaluate the rates using (3+1)-dimensional leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) with a relaxation-time approximation collisional kernel to describe the space-time evolution of the QGP. The differential photon spectrum is obtained by integrating the local rate over the QGP space-time volume, boosting the photon momentum to the local rest frame of each fluid cell.

## Key numbers

* The photon rate shows a variation of less than or equal to 30% at scaled photon energy E/Λ = 1 and less than or equal to 20% at E/Λ = 10 when the separation scale p* is varied by a factor of 2, for realistic coupling α_s = 0.32.
* The critical temperature used to define the QGP phase boundary is T_c = 175 MeV.
* The system volume at creation is approximately 4000 fm^3, and the initial time is τ ~ 0.2 fm/c.
* The pseudo-critical temperature for the QCD phase transition is T_c ~ 165 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of photon production from an anisotropic QGP by Schenke et al. (2006, 2008) and Bhattacharya et al. (2008, 2009), which used simple analytic models with adjustable isotropization times, by instead employing a full (3+1)-dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamics evolution. It goes beyond viscous hydrodynamics studies by Shen, Paquet, Heinz, and Gale (2013, 2014, 2015) that used a Grad-14 δf form for viscous corrections, which can become negative at high momentum; the spheroidal distribution used here is positive definite. The results enable future experimental constraints on the early-time momentum-space anisotropy of the QGP using high-energy photon spectra from relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
