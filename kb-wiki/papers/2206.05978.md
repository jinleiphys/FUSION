# Laurent+Pietarinen Partial Wave Analysis

> A new energy-dependent fit strategy, independent of any specific microscopic theory, is applied to kaon photoproduction data with center-of-mass energies ranging from 1625 MeV to 2296 MeV. Experimental data are fitted in terms of a modified Laurent expansion (Laurent+Pietarinen expansion) which previously has been successfully applied to multipoles. The present aim is to extract resonance pole parameters directly from the data, rather than from sets of multipoles. A constrained single-energy fit is then used to search for missing structures. In this proof-of-principle study, the data are well-described by the initial L+P fit, and it is shown that only a moderate amount of structure, mostly in higher multipoles, is missing from the original fit. Problems due to an unmeasurable overall phase, plaguing single-channel multipole analyses, are mitigated by implementing a form of phase limitation, fixing the initial values of fit parameters using a multi-channel analysis.

## Key claim
The authors present a proof-of-principle study showing that a simplified Laurent+Pietarinen (L+P) expansion, fitted directly to kaon photoproduction data rather than to pre-extracted multipoles, can extract resonance pole parameters while requiring only moderate additional structure in higher multipoles, provided the overall phase ambiguity is mitigated by initializing fit parameters using a multi-channel analysis (Bonn-Gatchina).

## Method
The authors performed a two-step procedure: first, a simplified energy-dependent L+P fit was applied directly to the full set of measured observables for $\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda$ (differential cross section and seven polarization observables) in the center-of-mass energy range 1625–2296 MeV, using one Pietarinen expansion per multipole and only 4-star PDG resonances. Second, a constrained single-energy partial-wave analysis was performed using the smooth L+P amplitudes as penalty functions to search for missing structure, with the penalty factor set so that the penalty contribution to total $\chi^2$ was no larger than 10%. The initial L+P parameters were determined by fitting the Bonn-Gatchina transversity amplitudes, providing phase information to resolve the continuum ambiguity.

## Key numbers
- Energy range: 1625 MeV to 2296 MeV (center-of-mass)
- Database: approximately 8000 data points, including 3615 differential cross section points, 400 $\Sigma$ points, 408 $T$ points, 1597 $P$ points, 415 $O_{x'}$ points, 415 $O_{z'}$ points, 138 $C_x$ points, and 138 $C_z$ points
- Fit used 36 interpolated energy points (reduced from 121 measured energies) and about 3000 minimization points (reduced from 8000)
- CPU time: 12–14 hours for the simplified L+P fit using Mathematica 11.0
- Penalty factor $\lambda = 150$ in the constrained single-energy fit
- For the $E_{0+}$ multipole, the full L+P fit gave pole positions at (1.688, 0.073) GeV and (1.869, 0.043) GeV (real and imaginary parts of pole masses)
- For the $M_{1-}$ multipole, the full L+P fit gave a pole at (1.930, 0.063) GeV
- For the $E_{2+}$ multipole, the full L+P fit gave poles at (1.671, 0.318) GeV, (2.070, 0.093) GeV, and (1.888, 0.072) GeV

## Context
This work extends the Laurent+Pietarinen method previously applied to multipole amplitudes (Svarc et al., 2013, 2016) to direct fitting of experimental data, bypassing the need for pre-extracted partial waves. It addresses the continuum ambiguity problem in single-channel analyses (discussed by Svarc et al., 2018) by using phase information from the Bonn-Gatchina multi-channel analysis (Anisovich et al., 2017). The approach enables experimental groups to extract resonance pole parameters without relying on elaborate multi-channel formalisms, and the iterative procedure combining energy-dependent and single-energy fits can identify where simplifications in the analytic expansion are insufficient.
