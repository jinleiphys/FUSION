# Momentum Distribution in Nuclear Matter and Finite Nuclei

> A simple method is presented to evaluate the effects of short-range correlations on the momentum distribution of nucleons in nuclear matter within the framework of the Green's function approach. The method provides a very efficient representation of the single-particle Green's function for a correlated system. The reliability of this method is established by comparing its results to those obtained in more elaborate calculations. The sensitivity of the momentum distribution on the nucleon-nucleon interaction and the nuclear density is studied. The momentum distributions of nucleons in finite nuclei are derived from those in nuclear matter using a local-density approximation. These results are compared to those obtained directly for light nuclei like $^{16}O$.

## Key claim

The paper presents a simplified method for calculating the effects of short-range correlations on nucleon momentum distributions, claiming that a perturbative approach including self-energy terms up to second order in a nuclear matter G-matrix yields results comparable to much more sophisticated self-consistent Green's function calculations, but only after careful treatment of double-counting and with the caveat that the method's reliability depends on the specific nucleon-nucleon interaction and nuclear density considered.

## Method

The authors calculate the single-particle Green's function for nuclear matter using a self-energy that includes Hartree-Fock (first-order) and second-order terms (2-particle-1-hole and 2-hole-1-particle) in a nuclear matter G-matrix, discretizing the integrals to represent the self-energy as a sum of discrete poles. They then solve the Dyson equation via a matrix eigenvalue problem and apply a Lanczos-based BAGEL (BAsis GEnerated by Lanczos) scheme to obtain an efficient representation of the Green's function with a reduced number of poles. For finite nuclei, they compute the momentum distribution both directly for 16O using a similar second-order self-energy approach in a spherical box basis, and via a local density approximation (LDA) that derives finite-nucleus momentum distributions from nuclear matter results at varying local Fermi momenta.

## Key numbers

- Average occupation of states below the Fermi momentum in nuclear matter at saturation density (kF = 1.36 fm^-1): 0.83 for the Reid soft-core potential, 0.86 for the OBE potential B
- At momentum k ≈ 4 fm^-1, the momentum distribution from the Reid soft-core potential is more than a factor of 2 larger than that from the OBE potential
- Experimental single-particle energies used for hole states in 16O: -47 MeV for s1/2, -21.8 MeV for p3/2, -15.7 MeV for p1/2 states
- The Fermi momentum at empirical saturation density: kF = 1.36 fm^-1

## Context

This work extends the Green's function approach to momentum distributions previously applied to nuclear matter by Vonderfecht et al. (1993) and to finite nuclei by Polls et al. (1994), addressing inconsistencies between those calculations such as different densities, interactions, and self-energy treatments. The method enables systematic studies of how the momentum distribution depends on the nucleon-nucleon interaction and nuclear density, and provides a computationally efficient way to estimate finite-nucleus momentum distributions via the local density approximation, which can be compared to direct calculations for light nuclei like 16O.
