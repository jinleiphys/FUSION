# Excitation and probing of low-energy nuclear states at high-energy storage rings

> $^{229}$Th with a low-lying nuclear isomeric state is an essential candidate for a nuclear clock as well as many other applications. Laser excitation of the isomeric state has been a long-standing goal. With relativistic $^{229}$Th ions in storage rings, high-power lasers with wavelengths in the visible range or longer can be used to achieve high excitation rates of $^{229}$Th isomers. This can be realized through direct resonant excitation, or excitation via an intermediate nuclear or electronic state, facilitated by the tunability of both the laser-beam and ion-bunch parameters. Unique opportunities are offered by highly charged $^{229}$Th ions due to the nuclear-state mixing. The significantly reduced isomeric-state lifetime corresponds to a much higher excitation rate for direct resonant excitation. Importantly, we propose electric dipole transitions changing both the electronic and nuclear states that are opened by the nuclear hyperfine mixing. We suggest using them for efficient isomer excitation in Li-like $^{229}$Th ions, via stimulated Raman adiabatic passage or single-laser excitation. We also propose schemes for probing the isomers, utilizing nuclear radiative decay or laser spectroscopy on electronic transitions, through which the isomeric-state energy can be determined with an orders-of-magnitude higher precision than the current value. The schemes proposed here for $^{229}$Th could also be adapted to low-energy nuclear states in other nuclei, such as $^{229}$Pa.

## Key claim

Laser excitation of the isomeric state in \({}^{229}\)Th can be achieved using relativistic highly charged ions in storage rings with visible-range or longer-wavelength lasers, provided that the nuclear hyperfine mixing in highly charged ions (such as H-like or Li-like) is exploited to significantly reduce the isomeric-state lifetime and thereby increase excitation rates, or that repeated pumping schemes are used to relax the demand on individual laser-pulse intensity.

## Method

The authors model the population transfer from the nuclear ground state to the isomeric state using a density-matrix master equation in the ion frame, accounting for the relativistic Doppler shift of laser photons in near-head-on collisions with stored ion bunches. They consider direct resonant excitation of the isomer, excitation via the second nuclear excited state at 29 keV, and excitation via electric-dipole transitions opened by nuclear hyperfine mixing in Li-like \({}^{229}\)Th ions, including stimulated Raman adiabatic passage (STIRAP) and single-laser schemes. The calculations assume Gaussian laser pulses with durations tuned to match the Doppler width arising from the ion energy spread, and the population evolution under repeated pumping is computed iteratively.

## Key numbers

* Isomeric-state energy: \(E_{\text{iso}} = 8.19(12)\ \text{eV}\) (average of two experimental values).
* Radiative half-life of the isomeric state in bare nuclei: \(T_{1/2}^{\text{rad}} \approx 5.2 \times 10^3\ \text{s}\) (theoretical, from reduced transition probability \(B(M1) \approx 0.0076\ \text{W.u.}\)).
* Second nuclear excited state energy: \(E_{\text{2nd}} = 29,189.93(7)\ \text{eV}\).
* Radiative width of the cross-band transition from the second excited state to the ground state: \(\Gamma_{\gamma}^{\text{cr}} = 1.70(40)\ \text{neV}\).
* Branching ratio for the cross-band transition: \(\text{BR}^{\text{cr}}_\gamma = 9.3(6)\%\); for the in-band transition to the isomeric state: \(\text{BR}^{\text{in}}_\gamma = 90.7(6)\%\).
* For repeated pumping at SPS (\(\gamma \approx 35\), \(\Delta\gamma/\gamma \approx 10^{-4}\)) with a CO\(_2\) laser (\(\hbar\omega = 0.117\ \text{eV}\)) and \(E_{\text{p}} = 10\ \text{J}\) per pulse inside a Fabry–Perot cavity: average population-transfer fraction per pulse \(\rho_0 \approx 8.9 \times 10^{-4}\), maximal population fraction \(\rho_{\text{max}} \approx 0.50\), reached after \(n_{\text{col}} \approx 2600\) collisions (excitation time \(t_{\text{ex}} \approx 60\ \text{ms}\)).
* For repeated pumping at HESR (\(\gamma \approx 6\), \(\Delta\gamma/\gamma \approx 10^{-5}\)) with \(\hbar\omega \approx 0.683\ \text{eV}\) and \(E_{\text{p}} = 10\ \text{J}\): similar characteristic excitation time of about \(60\ \text{ms}\).
* Proposed precision for determining the isomeric-state energy: better than \(10^{-4}\) or down to below \(10^{-6}\), representing an orders-of-magnitude improvement over the current relative uncertainty of \(2\%\) (or \(0.3\%\) recently reported).

## Context

This work extends proposals for laser excitation of \({}^{229m}\)Th, which have previously focused on nonrelativistic atoms or low-charge ions (e.g., Jeet 2015, Yamaguchi 2015, Stellmer 2018, von der Wense 2017) and on electron-bridge processes in low-charge or highly charged ions (Porsev 2010, Bilous 2018, Bilous 2020). It also builds on the observation of radiative decay of \({}^{229m}\)Th at ISOLDE (Kraemer 2022). The schemes proposed here leverage existing or planned storage-ring facilities (ESR at GSI, HESR at FAIR, SPS and LHC at CERN, HIAF in China) and could be adapted to other nuclei with low-energy nuclear states, such as \({}^{229}\)Pa. The work enables a path toward a nuclear clock and fundamental-physics tests by providing concrete excitation and probing schemes that use currently available laser and accelerator technology.
