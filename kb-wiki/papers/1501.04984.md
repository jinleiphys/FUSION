# Pairing versus quarteting coherence length

> We systematically analyse the coherence length in even-even nuclei. The pairing coherence length in the spin-singlet channel for the effective density dependent delta (DDD) and Gaussian interaction is estimated. We consider in our calculations bound states as well as narrow resonances. It turns out that the pairing gaps given by the DDD interaction are similar to those of the Gaussian potential if one renormalizes the radial width to the nuclear radius. The correlations induced by the pairing interaction have in all considered cases a long range character inside the nucleus and decrease towards the surface. The mean coherence length is larger than the geometrical radius for light nuclei and approaches this value for heavy nuclei. The effect of the temperature and states in continuum is investigated. Strong shell effects are evidenced, especially for protons. We generalize this concept to quartets by considering similar relations, but between proton and neutron pairs. The quartet coherence length has a similar shape, but with larger values on the nuclear surface. We evidence the important role of proton-neutron correlations by estimating the so-called alpha coherence length, which takes into account the overlap with the proton-neutron part of the $α$-particle wave function. It turns out that it does not depend on the nuclear size and has a value comparable to the free $α$-particle radius. We have shown that pairing correlations are mainly concentrated inside the nucleus, while quarteting correlations are connected to the nuclear surface.

## Key claim

The paper systematically analyzes the pairing coherence length in even-even nuclei and generalizes the concept to quartets, showing that pairing correlations are mainly concentrated inside the nucleus while quarteting correlations are connected to the nuclear surface. The authors hedge that the pairing gaps given by the density dependent delta (DDD) interaction are similar to those of the Gaussian potential only if one renormalizes the radial width to the nuclear radius.

## Method

The authors solved the BCS equations separately for protons and neutrons using a Woods-Saxon mean field with universal parametrization, considering bound states and narrow resonances up to 10 MeV with decay width less than or equal to 1 MeV. They computed the pairing coherence length from the spin-singlet component of the two-body pairing density using both "normal" and "anomalous" densities, and generalized this to quarteting coherence length by overlapping proton and neutron pair wave functions with the alpha-particle wave function components. The analysis covered all even-even nuclei with atomic numbers between 20 and 100.

## Key numbers

- The strength parameter of the Gaussian interaction with r0=2 fm is close to the singlet "bare" value in free space v0 approximately 35 MeV for very light nuclei, decreasing to v0 approximately 20 MeV for heavy nuclei
- The ratio of mean coherence length to nuclear radius decreases from 1.4 for light nuclei to around unity for heavy nuclei for the Gaussian interaction with r0=2 fm
- The "critical" temperature for 220Ra is approximately 0.57 MeV, where the gap decreases below 10^-3 MeV
- The mean alpha coherence length for even-even alpha-emitters above A=100 has a quasi-constant value around 1.7 fm
- The alpha-particle geometrical radius is approximately 1.9 fm, estimated as 1.2 times 4^(1/3)
- The free alpha-particle harmonic oscillator parameter beta_alpha is approximately 0.5 fm^-2

## Context

This work extends the analysis of coherence length in superfluid nuclei from Pillet et al. (2007, 2010) and Vinas et al. (2010), who showed that the pairing coherence length is relatively large and comparable to nuclear size inside the nucleus. The authors compare their results to the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach using the Gogny force for Ni isotopes from Pillet et al. (2007), finding similar shape with mean coherence length of about 6 fm in the internal region decreasing to 2 fm outside the nucleus. The study enables systematic comparison between pairing and quarteting correlations, confirming the crucial role of proton-neutron correlations in alpha-particle formation as suggested by Ropke et al. (1998) and Delion et al. (2013).
