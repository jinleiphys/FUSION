# Gauging the spectator equations

> We show how to derive relativistic, unitary, gauge invariant, and charge conserving three-dimensional scattering equations for a system of hadrons interacting with an electromagnetic field. In the method proposed, the spectator equations describing the strong interactions of the hadrons are gauged using our recently introduced gauging of equations method. A key ingredient in our model is the on-mass-shell particle propagator. We discuss how to gauge this on-mass-shell propagator so that both the Ward-Takahashi and Ward identities are satisfied. We then demonstrate our gauging procedure by deriving the gauge-invariant three-dimensional expression for the deuteron photodisintegration amplitude within the spectator approach.

## Key claim

The paper claims to derive relativistic, unitary, gauge invariant, and charge conserving three-dimensional scattering equations for hadrons interacting with an electromagnetic field, but the key condition is that this is achieved only by constructing a specific gauged on-mass-shell propagator that satisfies both the Ward-Takahashi and Ward identities, which the prior Gross-Riska prescription failed to do for charge conservation.

## Method

The authors start from the four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equation and convert it to the three-dimensional spectator (Gross) equations by replacing the Feynman propagator of one particle with an on-mass-shell propagator containing a positive energy delta-function. They then apply their previously developed "gauging of equations" method to these spectator equations, which requires constructing a gauged on-mass-shell propagator that satisfies both the Ward-Takahashi identity and the Ward identity. The procedure is demonstrated by deriving the gauge-invariant three-dimensional expression for the deuteron photodisintegration amplitude within the spectator approach.

## Key numbers

* The on-mass-shell propagator for a spinor particle is defined as $\delta(p)=2\pi(\pslash+m)\delta^+(p^2-m^2)$, replacing the Feynman propagator $d(p)=i(\pslash+m)/(p^2-m^2+i\epsilon)$.
* The proposed gauged on-mass-shell propagator is $\delta^\mu(p',p)=i[\delta(p')\Gamma^\mu(p',p)\Lambda(p)-\Lambda(p')\Gamma^\mu(p',p)\delta(p)]/(p^2-p'^2)$, which is explicitly regular at $p^2-p'^2=0$.
* The Gross-Riska prescription $\delta'^\mu(p',p)=\delta(p')\Gamma^\mu(p',p)d(p)+d(p')\Gamma^\mu(p',p)\delta(p)$ differs from the authors' by $-\delta(p')\Gamma^\mu(p',p)\delta(p)$, which vanishes for $q^2>0$ and $q^2=0$ (with $q\neq0$) but not for $q^2<0$, where it diverges as $\mathbf{q}\rightarrow 0$.
* For the scalar particle case in the Breit frame with $q_0=0$ and $\mathbf{P}'=-\mathbf{P}=\mathbf{q}/2$, the difference term $\Delta j^\mu(P',P)$ contains a factor $|\mathbf{q}|^{-1}\delta(2p_z+|\mathbf{q}|)$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous four-dimensional gauging of equations method (Kvinikhidze and Blankleider, 1997) to three-dimensional spectator equations, building on the Gross reduction scheme (Gross, 1982) which preserves covariance and unitarity. The paper corrects the Gross-Riska prescription (Gross and Riska, 1987) for gauging the on-mass-shell propagator, which satisfied the Ward-Takahashi identity but not the Ward identity, thus breaking charge conservation. The method enables practical three-dimensional calculations of electromagnetic interactions with hadronic systems, such as deuteron photodisintegration, and is directly applicable to three-nucleon problems and weak interactions with W or other gauge bosons.
