# Medium modification of pion production in low energy Au+Au collisions

> There is a major mismatch between the charged pion yields in Au+Au collisions at low energies calculated by various transport models and the experimental measured values from the Hades collaboration. In this work, reasonable improvements on the equation of state, in-medium modification of cross sections, and the influence of the nuclear potential for Delta resonances will be investigated in the framework of the GiBUU transport model. As a result, we demonstrate that theoretical calculations can indeed describe the charged pion yields measured by Hades for Au+Au collisions rather well, but that a mismatch then remains between calculations and data for the yields of neutral pions extracted from dileptons within the same experiment.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that charged pion yields in Au+Au collisions at 1.23 AGeV measured by HADES can be described by the GiBUU transport model when combining in-medium cross-section suppression via effective Dirac masses with a reduced baryonic potential for Delta resonances, but a mismatch then remains between calculations and data for neutral pion yields extracted from dileptons within the same experiment.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic mean-field (RMF) mode of the GiBUU transport model to simulate Au+Au collisions at 1.23 AGeV incident energy. They compared different nuclear equations of state (EOS), implemented two types of in-medium modifications to pion production cross sections (exponential density suppression and effective-mass suppression), and introduced a reduced scalar and vector coupling for Delta resonances (r_S = r_V = 2/3) to match the phenomenological Delta potential of approximately -30 MeV. The simulations were compared to experimental data from HADES and FOPI collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy: E_kin = 1.23 AGeV for Au+Au collisions
- Saturation density: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Nuclear incompressibility K = 240 MeV for the Liu EOS, compared to K = 210 MeV for the NL2 Lang EOS
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation: m_N*/m_N = 0.75 for Liu EOS, 0.83 for NL2 Lang EOS
- Delta potential in nuclei: U_Delta approximately -30 MeV, about 2/3 of the nucleon potential
- Central baryon density reduction from 2.82 rho/rho_0 (no modifications) to 2.54 rho/rho_0 (with exponential suppression), a 10% decrease
- Exponential suppression parameters: alpha = 2.0, beta = 1 in f = exp(-alpha (rho/rho_0)^beta)

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing problem (known for almost 30 years, e.g. Bass (1995), Teis (1996)) that transport models systematically overpredict pion yields measured by HADES in Au+Au collisions at 1.23 AGeV. The authors extend prior work by Godbey (2021) who used ad hoc density-dependent suppression factors, and by Larionov (2020) who reported a 50% mismatch. The study enables a physically motivated framework for medium modifications in transport models, and the favored Liu EOS (Liu, 2001) simultaneously satisfies astrophysical constraints from neutron star mergers (Li, 2021; Xie, 2020).
