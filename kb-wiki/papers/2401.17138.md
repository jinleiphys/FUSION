# Nuclear scattering via quantum computing

> We propose a hybrid quantum-classical framework to solve the elastic scattering phase shift of two well-bound nuclei in an uncoupled channel. Within this framework, we develop a many-body formalism in which the continuum scattering states of the two colliding nuclei are regulated by a weak external harmonic oscillator potential with varying strength. Based on our formalism, we propose an approach to compute the eigenenergies of the low-lying scattering states of the relative motion of the colliding nuclei as a function of the oscillator strength of the confining potential. Utilizing the modified effective range expansion, we extrapolate the elastic scattering phase shift of the colliding nuclei from these eigenenergies to the limit when the external potential vanishes. In our hybrid approach, we leverage the advantage of quantum computing to solve for these eigenenergies from a set of many-nucleon Hamiltonian eigenvalue problems. These eigenenergies are inputs to classical computers to obtain the phase shift. We demonstrate our framework with two simple problems, where we implement the rodeo algorithm to solve the relevant eigenenergies with the IBM Qiskit quantum simulator. The results of both the spectra and the elastic scattering phase shifts agree well with other theoretical results.

## Key claim

The authors propose a hybrid quantum-classical framework to compute elastic scattering phase shifts of two well-bound nuclei, where the continuum scattering states are discretized by a weak external harmonic oscillator potential and the phase shift is extrapolated via the modified effective range expansion (MERE) formula. The framework is demonstrated only for toy problems where the colliding nuclei are treated as structureless particles, with the caveat that full many-body applications require future fault-tolerant quantum computers.

## Method

The continuum scattering states of two colliding nuclei are regulated by adding a weak external harmonic oscillator potential with varying strength ω to the relative-motion Hamiltonian, which discretizes the low-lying scattering states. The eigenenergies of these discretized states are computed using the rodeo algorithm implemented on the IBM Qiskit quantum simulator. These eigenenergies are then input to the MERE formula on classical computers to extrapolate the elastic scattering phase shift to the limit of vanishing external potential.

## Context

This work extends the approach of using confined scattering states and the MERE formula (Busch 1998, Stetcu 2007, Suzuki 2009, Blume 2012) to a hybrid quantum-classical setting, addressing the difficulty that quantum computing provides only probabilities (not phases) from projective measurements. It builds on prior quantum eigensolver techniques (rodeo algorithm from 2021) and enables future first-principles nuclear scattering calculations on fault-tolerant quantum computers, going beyond the structureless-particle toy models demonstrated here.
