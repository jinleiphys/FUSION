# Production of $K^+K^-$ Pairs Through Decay of $ϕ$ Meson

> We develop a theoretical framework for the production of $K^+K^-$ pairs through the decay of $ϕ$ mesons produced from a thermal background, based on the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. The differential production rate of $K^+K^-$ is related to the self-energy of the $ϕ$ meson, incorporating the contributions of the quark loop at leading order and the kaon loop at next-to-leading order in the $1/N_c$ expansion. We numerically evaluate the invariant mass spectrum of the $K^+K^-$ pair both in vacuum and at finite temperature. The inclusion of the kaon loop results in a finite width of the spectrum, improving agreement with experimental data. We also investigate the spin alignment of the $ϕ$ meson induced by its motion relative to the thermal background. In the NJL model with only chiral condensates, we find that deviations from the unpolarized limit of 1/3 are negligible.

## Key claim
The paper develops a theoretical framework for K⁺K⁻ pair production through φ meson decay in a thermal medium using the NJL model, finding that the inclusion of kaon-loop contributions at next-to-leading order in the 1/N_c expansion is crucial for reproducing the finite width of the invariant mass spectrum. The authors report that deviations of the φ meson spin alignment from the unpolarized limit of 1/3 are negligible within the NJL model with only chiral condensates, despite experimental observations showing significant positive deviations.

## Method
The authors compute the differential production rate of K⁺K⁻ pairs by relating it to the φ meson's self-energy and spectral function, evaluated using the SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. The self-energy includes the leading-order s-quark loop contribution and next-to-leading order kaon-loop contributions (including K⁺K⁻ and K⁰_L K⁰_S loops) in the 1/N_c expansion, with the φK⁺K⁻ vertex calculated from a triangle quark-loop diagram. The model parameters are determined by fitting physical meson masses and decay constants in vacuum, with an additional cutoff parameter Λ_M = 0.719 GeV and vector coupling G_V = -1.0621 GeV⁻² fixed by comparing the K⁺K⁻ invariant mass spectrum to experimental data.

## Key numbers
- Quark bare masses: m_u = m_d = 5.5 MeV, m_s = 140.7 MeV; momentum cutoff Λ = 602.3 MeV; scalar coupling G_S = 5.058 GeV⁻²; KMT coupling K = 155.9 GeV⁻⁵ (from Rehberg (1995))
- Vector coupling G_V = -1.0621 GeV⁻² and meson-loop cutoff Λ_M = 0.719 GeV, determined by fitting the K⁺K⁻ invariant mass spectrum
- φ meson lifetime approximately 45 fm/c, much longer than the fireball lifetime
- Branching ratio for φ → K⁺K⁻ is 49.1%; branching ratio for φ → K⁰_L K⁰_S is 33.9%
- For temperatures below 0.05 GeV, variations of kaon mass M_K are δM_K ∼ 10⁻⁵ GeV and coupling g_{Kus} variations are δg_{Kus} ∼ 10⁻³

## Context
This work extends the NJL model treatment of vector mesons beyond the leading-order quark-loop approximation by including kaon-loop contributions, which are necessary to describe the physical φ → K⁺K⁻ decay channel that is inaccessible via the unphysical s + s̄ channel at low temperatures. The study is motivated by the STAR Collaboration's (2023) observation of significant positive deviation from 1/3 in φ meson spin alignment, which previous theoretical estimates from vorticity and magnetic fields (Sheng (2019), Xia (2020)) found to be orders of magnitude smaller than experiment. The framework enables future calculations of the φ meson spectral function and spin alignment in thermal medium, potentially addressing the discrepancy between theory and experimental data.
