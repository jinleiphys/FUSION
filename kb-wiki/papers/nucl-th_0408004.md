# Deuteron Compton Scattering in Effective Field Theory: Spin-Dependent Cross Sections and Asymmetries

> Polarized Compton scattering on the deuteron is studied in nuclear effective field theory. A set of tensor structures is introduced to define 12 independent Compton amplitudes. The scalar and vector amplitudes are calculated up to ${\cal O}((Q/Λ)^2)$ in low-energy power counting. Significant contribution to the vector amplitudes is found to come from the spin-orbit type of relativistic corrections. A double-helicity dependent cross section $Δ_1 σ= (σ_{+1-1}-σ_{+1+1})/2$ is calculated to the same order, and the effect of the nucleon isoscalar spin-dependent polarizabilities is found to be smaller than the effect of isoscalar spin-independent ones. Contributions of spin-independent polarizabilities are investigated in various asymmetries, one of which has as large as 12 (26) percent effect at the center-of-mass photon energy 30 (50) MeV.

# Deuteron Compton Scattering in Effective Field Theory: Spin-Dependent Cross Sections and Asymmetries

## Key claim

The double-helicity dependent cross section on the deuteron is calculated up to ${\cal O}((Q/\Lambda)^2)$ in low-energy power counting, and the effect of nucleon isoscalar spin-dependent polarizabilities is found to be smaller than the effect of isoscalar spin-independent ones, contrary to what one might hope for extracting neutron spin polarizabilities from deuteron data. The vector amplitudes receive significant contributions from spin-orbit type relativistic corrections, which are nominally higher-order in power counting but become numerically important for certain spin-dependent observables.

## Method

The authors construct 12 independent tensor structures for real photon Compton scattering on the deuteron using parity and time-reversal symmetries, classifying them into scalar (2), vector (4), and tensor (6) amplitudes. They calculate the vector Compton amplitudes up to $(Q/\Lambda)^4$ in a low-energy expansion using the dibaryon formulation of pionless effective field theory (dEFT($\not\!\pi$)), including electric current and magnetic couplings, spin-orbit relativistic corrections, and nucleon polarizability interactions. Power counting is performed in two kinematic regions: Region I where photon energy is far below the deuteron binding energy (2.2 MeV), and Region II where photon energy is above binding but below the pion mass (around 50 MeV), with the paper focusing on Region II.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron binding energy is $B = 2.2$ MeV, defining the low-energy scale.
- One asymmetry shows a 12 percent effect at center-of-mass photon energy 30 MeV and a 26 percent effect at 50 MeV from spin-independent polarizabilities.
- The coupling constant $L_1$ is fixed to $-4.42$ fm from the measured $n+p\rightarrow d+\gamma$ cross section of $334.2 \pm 0.5$ mb at an incident neutron speed of 2200 m/s.
- The coupling constant $L_2$ is fixed to $-0.03$ fm from the deuteron magnetic moment.
- Chiral perturbation theory values used for isoscalar nucleon polarizabilities: $\alpha_0 = 12 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$, $\beta_0 = 1.2 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$, $\gamma^{(s)}_{E1} = -3.1 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^4$, $\gamma^{(s)}_{M1} = 0.4 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^4$, $\gamma^{(s)}_{E2} = 2.1 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^4$, $\gamma^{(s)}_{M2} = 0.6 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^4$.

## Context

This work extends previous effective field theory treatments of deuteron Compton scattering, particularly the unpolarized cross section calculation by Chen, Ji, and Li (2004) which showed that vector amplitudes contribute significantly (order 15 percent or more) due to enhancement from the square of the isovector magnetic moment. It also builds on the dibaryon formulation of Savage (1999) and the power counting scheme of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998). The paper enables future experimental extraction of nucleon spin polarizabilities from polarized deuteron Compton data, though it cautions that the enhancement from magnetic moment contributions makes accessing the spin-dependent polarizabilities more difficult than previously expected. The tensor structures introduced provide a complete basis for future calculations of all 12 independent Compton amplitudes on spin-1 targets.
