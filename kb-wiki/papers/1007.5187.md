# Intercepts of the momentum correlation functions in μ-Bose gas model and their asymptotics

> The so-called μ-deformed oscillator (or μ-oscillator) introduced by A.Jannussis, though possesses rather exotic properties with respect to other better known deformed oscillator models, also has good potential for diverse physical applications. In this paper, the corresponding μ-Bose gas model based on μ-oscillators is explored. Within this model, the intercepts λ^(2)(K) and λ^(3)(K) of two- and three-particle momentum correlation functions are calculated with the goal of possible application for modeling the non-Bose type behavior of the intercepts of two- and three-pion correlations, observed in the experiments on relativistic heavy ion collisions. In derivation of intercepts, different orders of approximation in the deformation parameter μare considered. Our analysis also yields asymptotical behavior of the intercepts λ^(2)(K) and λ^(3)(K). The results for λ^(2)(K) are compared with experimental data, and with earlier known results drawn using other deformed Bose gas models.

## Key claim

The μ-Bose gas model, based on μ-deformed oscillators, can reproduce the non-Bose type behavior of two- and three-pion correlation intercepts observed in relativistic heavy ion collisions, but only within a fixed order of approximation in the deformation parameter μ (up to μ⁵ in this paper) and with the asymptotics approaching a constant value determined solely by μ, independent of momentum, mass, or temperature.

## Method

The authors construct a μ-Bose gas model using μ-deformed oscillators defined by the structure function φ(N) = N/(1+μN). They calculate the intercepts λ⁽²⁾(K) and λ⁽³⁾(K) of two- and three-particle momentum correlation functions by expressing these intercepts in terms of thermal averages of powers of the number operator N, expanding the structure function as a power series in μ up to order μ⁵, and evaluating the resulting expressions using the known thermal averages ⟨Nᵏ⟩ for a Bose gas. The exact asymptotic formulas for large momentum (βω → ∞) are derived by summing the infinite series.

## Key numbers

- For μ = 0.1, the asymptotic intercept for two-particle correlations is λ⁽²⁾_as. = 0.8331; for μ = 0.15, λ⁽²⁾_as. = 0.7664.
- For μ = 0.1, the asymptotic intercept for three-particle correlations is λ⁽³⁾_as. = 3.6365; for μ = 0.15, λ⁽³⁾_as. = 2.9964.
- The exact asymptotic formula for the two-particle intercept is λ⁽²⁾_as.(μ) = 1/(1+2μ).
- The exact asymptotic formula for the three-particle intercept is λ⁽³⁾_as.(μ) = (5+7μ)/[(1+2μ)(1+3μ)].
- For μ = 0.1, the asymptotic value of the function r⁽³⁾ is r⁽³⁾_as. = 0.7472; for μ = 0.15, r⁽³⁾_as. = 0.5194.
- The exact asymptotic formula for r⁽³⁾ is r_as.⁽³⁾(μ) = [(1-μ)/(1+3μ)]√(1+2μ).
- A comparison with experimental data from Abelev (2009) for 62.4 GeV Au+Au collisions uses μ = 0.255 and T = 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the program of using deformed Bose gas models to describe non-Bose behavior of pion correlation intercepts, previously developed with q-deformed oscillators (Arik-Coon and Biedenharn-Macfarlane types) by Gavrilik and collaborators (2005, 2006) and with p,q-deformed oscillators by Gavrilik and Rebesh (2007). The μ-oscillator, introduced by Jannussis (1993), differs from these Fibonacci-type oscillators and belongs to the class of quasi-Fibonacci oscillators. The results enable future comparison with experimental data from RHIC and LHC experiments, particularly for three-pion correlations where data are currently insufficient, and may find applications in ⁴He theory and quantum optics.
