# Jet suppression of pions and single electrons at Au+Au collisions at RHIC

> Jet suppression is considered to be a powerful tool to study the properties of a QCD medium created in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. However, theoretical predictions obtained by using jet energy loss in static QCD medium show disagreement with experimental data, which is known as the heavy flavor puzzle at RHIC. We calculate the suppression patterns of pions and single electrons for Au+Au collisions at RHIC by including the energy loss in a finite size dynamical QCD medium, with finite magnetic mass effects taken into account. In contrast to the static case, we here report a good agreement with the experimental results, where this agreement is robust with respect to magnetic mass values. Therefore, the inclusion of dynamical QCD medium effects provides a reasonable explanation of the heavy flavor puzzle at RHIC.

## Key claim

Including energy loss in a finite size *dynamical* QCD medium (rather than a static one) yields suppression predictions for pions and single electrons that agree with RHIC experimental data, and this agreement is robust with respect to the value of the magnetic mass. This resolves the "heavy flavor puzzle at RHIC," where static-medium predictions had previously disagreed with data.

## Method

The author integrates radiative and collisional energy loss in a finite size dynamical QCD medium (developed in prior work by Djordjevic and Heinz) into a computational framework that also includes multi-gluon fluctuations and path length fluctuations. The quenched spectra of partons, hadrons, and leptons are computed from a pQCD convolution of the initial parton spectrum, an energy loss probability, fragmentation functions, and (for heavy quarks) decay functions to single electrons. Suppression predictions for pions and single electrons are generated for central 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC and compared directly with PHENIX and STAR experimental data.

## Key numbers

* Temperature of the quark-gluon plasma is taken as T = 225 MeV, with N_f = 2.5 effective light quark flavors and strong coupling constant alpha_S = 0.3.
* Debye screening mass mu = gT sqrt(1+N_f/6) is approximately 0.5 GeV; gluon mass is m_g = mu/sqrt(2).
* Charm quark mass is 1.2 GeV; bottom quark mass is 4.75 GeV.
* Inclusion of magnetic mass can decrease jet suppression for all types of quarks and gluons by 25-50% compared to the case of zero magnetic mass.
* At jet energies above 15 GeV, predicted pion and single electron suppressions become very similar; below 10 GeV, the obtained suppressions differ by less than or equal to 2.

## Context

This work extends the author's prior development of radiative energy loss in a finite size dynamical QCD medium (Djordjevic 2009, Djordjevic and Heinz 2008) and collisional energy loss in the same medium (Djordjevic 2006), and applies it to the "heavy flavor puzzle at RHIC" identified by Gyulassy (2009) and others. The static-medium predictions that disagreed with data were from models such as those by Djordjevic, Gyulassy, Wicks, and Vogt (2006) and by Rapp, Greco, and van Hees (2006). The results enable future tests against upcoming high-luminosity RHIC and LHC data.
