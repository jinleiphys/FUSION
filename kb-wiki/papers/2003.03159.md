# Radial Fulde-Ferrell-Larkin-Ovchinnikov state in a population-imbalanced Fermi gas

> The possibility of a Fulde-Ferrell-Larkin-Ovchinnikov (FFLO) state in a population imbalanced Fermi gas with a vortex is proposed. Employing the Bogoliubov-de-Gennes formalism we self-consistently determine the superfluid order parameter and the particle number density in the presence of a vortex. We find that as increasing population imbalance, the superfluid order parameter spatially oscillates around the vortex core in the radial direction, indicating that the FFLO state becomes stable. We find that the radial FFLO states cover a wide region of the phase diagram in the weak-coupling regime at $T=0$ in contrast to the conventional case without a vortex. We show that this inhomogeneous superfluidity can be detected as peak structures of the local polarization rate associated with the node structure of the superfluid order parameter. Since the vortex in the 3D Fermi gas with population imbalance has been already realized in experiments, our proposal is a promising candidate of the FFLO state in cold atom physics.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a quantum vortex in a three-dimensional population-imbalanced Fermi gas can stabilize a radial Fulde-Ferrell-Larkin-Ovchinnikov (FFLO) state, where the superfluid order parameter oscillates spatially around the vortex core. The authors claim this FFLO state covers a wide region of the phase diagram in the weak-coupling regime at zero temperature, in contrast to the conventional case without a vortex where the FFLO state occupies only a narrow region.

## Method

The authors employ the Bogoliubov-de-Gennes (BdG) formalism to self-consistently determine the superfluid order parameter and particle number density for a singly isolated quantum vortex with winding number w=1 in a two-component Fermi gas with population imbalance. They solve the gap equation and particle number equations for a given interaction strength and population imbalance P=(N_up-N_down)/(N_up+N_down) at zero temperature, using a cylindrical coordinate system with system size Rk_F=50 in the radial direction and L_z k_F=20 in the axial direction, with cutoff energy E_c=9 epsilon_F.

## Key numbers

- System size: Rk_F=50 (radial direction) and L_z k_F=20 (axial direction), where k_F is the Fermi momentum
- Cutoff energy: E_c=9 epsilon_F, where epsilon_F=k_F^2/(2m) is the Fermi energy
- Interaction strength: (k_F a_s)^{-1}=-0.5 for the weak-coupling regime studied, where a_s is the s-wave scattering length
- Temperature: T=0 (fixed throughout calculations)
- The number N of nodes (where Delta(rho)=0) increases with population imbalance: N=1 and N=2 cases are shown
- Local polarization rate P(rho) shows peak structures at radial positions rho k_F approximately 13 and 24 for the N=2 case
- LDOS peaks are observed at omega=-0.16 epsilon_F for up-spin and omega=0.24 epsilon_F for down-spin components

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of FFLO states in three-dimensional Fermi gases, which predicted only a narrow region of stability at zero temperature (PhysRevLett.94.060404, SHEEHY20071790) that vanishes with increasing temperature (Parish2007). The authors contrast their proposal with earlier work on angular-FFLO states in toroidal traps (yanase1, yanase2) and FFLO states at trap edges (doi:10.1143/JPSJ.76.104006), where the oscillation amplitude was much smaller than the bulk order parameter. The key advance is that the vortex provides a natural localization mechanism for excess atoms near the core, making the FFLO state experimentally accessible since vortices in population-imbalanced 3D Fermi gases have already been realized in experiments (Zwierlein492, Zwierlein2006).
