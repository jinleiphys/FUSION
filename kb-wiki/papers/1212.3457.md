# Universal nature and finite-range corrections in elastic atom-dimer scattering below the dimer breakup threshold

> We investigate universal behavior in elastic atom-dimer scattering below the dimer breakup threshold calculating the atom-dimer effective-range function $ak\cotδ$. Using the He-He system as a reference, we solve the Schrödinger equation for a family of potentials having different values of the two-body scattering length $a$ and we compare our results to the universal zero-range form deduced by Efimov, $ak\cotδ=c_1(ka)+c_2(ka)\cot[s_0\ln(κ_*a)+ϕ(ka)]$, for selected values of the three-body parameter $κ_*$. Using the parametrization of the universal functions $c_1,c_2,ϕ$ given in the literature, a good agreement with the universal formula is obtained after introducing a particular type of finite-range corrections. Furthermore, we show that the same parametrization describes a very different system: nucleon-deuteron scattering below the deuteron breakup threshold. Our analysis confirms the universal character of the process, and relates the pole energy in the effective-range function of nucleon-deuteron scattering to the three-body parameter $κ_*$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the universal zero-range Efimov formula for the atom-dimer effective-range function can quantitatively describe numerical calculations for both atomic helium and nucleon-deuteron scattering only after introducing a specific finite-range correction: a shift in the three-body parameter of the form \(\kappa'_*a = \kappa_*a + \kappa_*r_*\), where \(r_* \approx 2r_0\) is extracted from the helium calculations.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrödinger equation for a family of two-body potentials derived from the LM2M2 helium potential by scaling the interaction strength \(\lambda\), producing scattering lengths from approximately 50 \(a_0\) to 440 \(a_0\). They used an attractive Gaussian two-body potential plus a repulsive hypercentral three-body force to reproduce the two-body scattering length and three-body binding energy, then computed atom-dimer scattering lengths and s-wave phase shifts using the hyperspherical harmonic method with the Kohn variational principle. The same universal parametrization was then applied to nucleon-deuteron scattering data from Chen (1989) by extracting effective values of the scattering length and three-body parameter from the pole energy and doublet scattering length.

## Key numbers

- The LM2M2 helium potential at \(\lambda=1\) gives two-body scattering length \(a = 189.41\) \(a_0\), dimer energy \(E_2 = -1.303\) mK, and effective range \(r_s = 13.845\) \(a_0\).
- The three-body parameter extracted at the unitary limit is \(\kappa_* = 0.002119\) \(a_0^{-1}\) for the TBG potential and \(\kappa_* = 0.001899\) \(a_0^{-1}\) for the TBG+H3B potential.
- The finite-range shift parameter is \(r_* = 21\) \(a_0\), approximately twice the two-body range \(r_0 = 10\) \(a_0\).
- The ratio \(a_*/a_- \approx -0.32\) is obtained for both values of \(\kappa_*\), compared to the zero-range universal prediction of \(-1.07\).
- For nucleon-deuteron scattering, the extracted effective scattering length is \(a = 4.075\) fm and \(\kappa'_*a = 0.5779\), with the pole energy \(E_p = -160\) keV from Chen (1989).

## Context

This work extends the zero-range universal theory of Efimov (1970, 1971) and the parametrization of Braaten (2006) by quantifying finite-range corrections in atom-dimer scattering. The results address discrepancies between theoretical predictions and experimental measurements of the ratio \(a_*/a_-\) in cold-atom systems, as reported by Zaccanti (2009) and Machtey (2012). The analysis also connects the universal description to nucleon-deuteron scattering, building on earlier studies of the pole structure by Barton (1969), Whiting (1976), Girard (1979), and Chen (1989), and enables application to halo nuclei such as the \(n-^{19}\mathrm{C}\) system studied by Yamashita (2008).
