# Does stability of relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics imply causality?

> We investigate the causality and stability of relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics in the absence of conserved charges. We perform a linear stability analysis in the rest frame of the fluid and find that the equations of relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics are always stable. We then perform a linear stability analysis in a Lorentz-boosted frame. Provided that the ratio of the relaxation time for the shear stress tensor, $τ_π$, to the sound attenuation length, $Γ_s = 4η/3(\varepsilon+P)$, fulfills a certain asymptotic causality condition, the equations of motion give rise to stable solutions. Although the group velocity associated with perturbations may exceed the velocity of light in a certain finite range of wavenumbers, we demonstrate that this does not violate causality, as long as the asymptotic causality condition is fulfilled. Finally, we compute the characteristic velocities and show that they remain below the velocity of light if the ratio $τ_π/Γ_s$ fulfills the asymptotic causality condition.

## Key claim

Stability of relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics in the rest frame does not guarantee causality; the theory remains causal only if the ratio of the shear relaxation time \(\tau_\pi\) to the sound attenuation length \(\Gamma_s\) satisfies an asymptotic causality condition, even though the group velocity may exceed the speed of light in a finite wavenumber range.

## Method

The authors performed a linear stability analysis of second-order relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics (without conserved charges) around a hydrostatic equilibrium state, first in the local rest frame and then in a Lorentz-boosted frame. They derived dispersion relations for shear and sound modes from the linearized equations of motion, computed group velocities, and examined the asymptotic large-wavenumber limit to determine conditions for causality and stability.

## Key numbers

- The critical wavenumber for shear modes is \(k_c = \sqrt{(\varepsilon+P)/(4\eta\tau_\pi)} \equiv \sqrt{b}/(2\tau_\pi)\), where \(b = \tau_\pi(\varepsilon+P)/\eta\).
- The asymptotic group velocity for sound modes is \(v_{g,\text{sound}}^{\text{as}} = c_s \sqrt{1 + \Gamma_s/(\tau_\pi c_s^2)}\).
- The asymptotic causality condition is \(\Gamma_s/\tau_\pi \le 1 - c_s^2\), which for conformal fluids (\(c_s^2 = 1/(D-1)\)) simplifies to \(b \ge 2\).
- For the AdS/CFT values \(\eta = s/(4\pi)\), \(\tau_\pi = (2 - \ln 2)/(2\pi T)\), the parameter \(b = 2(2-\ln 2) \approx 2.614 > 2\), satisfying the condition.
- The asymptotic group velocity for shear modes is \(v_{g,\text{shear}}^{\text{as}} = 1/\sqrt{b}\).
- For bulk and shear together in \(D=3\), the asymptotic causality condition becomes \(\zeta/(\tau_\Pi(\varepsilon+P)) + \eta/(\tau_\pi(\varepsilon+P)) \le 1 - c_s^2\).

## Context

This work extends the stability and causality analysis of Hiscock, Lindblom, and Olson (1985, 1990) for relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics, who studied only low- and high-wavenumber limits and missed the divergence of the group velocity of shear modes at intermediate wavenumbers. It also generalizes the analysis of Koide, Denicol, Kodama, and Rischke (2007) for bulk viscosity to the case of nonvanishing shear viscosity. The results show that second-order theories (such as Israel-Stewart) are not automatically causal; they can violate causality and become unstable if \(\tau_\pi\) is too small, explaining why relativistic Navier-Stokes theory (\(\tau_\pi \rightarrow 0\)) is acausal.
