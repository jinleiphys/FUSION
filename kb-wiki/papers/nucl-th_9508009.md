# Neutrinos from Protoneutron Stars: A Probe of Hot and Dense matter

> Neutrino processes in dense matter play a key role in the dynamics, deleptonization and early cooling of hot protoneutron stars formed in the gravitational collapse of massive stars. Here we calculate neutrino mean free paths from neutrino-hyperon interactions in dense matter containing hyperons. Significant contributions to the neutrino opacity arise from scattering and absorption reactions involving the sigma minus particle. The estimates given here emphasise the need for (a) opacities which incoparate many-body effects in a multi-component mixture, and (b) new calculations of thermal and leptonic evolution of protoneutron stars with neutrino transport and equations of state with strangeness-rich matter.

## Key claim

Neutrino mean free paths in hot protoneutron star matter are significantly reduced (by 30-50%) when hyperons are present, primarily due to scattering and absorption reactions involving the Sigma-minus hyperon, but these estimates are made under degenerate conditions and neglect many-body corrections beyond effective mass renormalization.

## Method

The authors calculated neutrino mean free paths for both scattering (neutral current) and absorption (charged current) reactions on baryons (nucleons and hyperons) in degenerate matter using tree-level weak interaction matrix elements from the Weinberg-Salam theory. They employed a non-relativistic approximation for baryons and considered both degenerate and non-degenerate neutrino limits, with baryon effective masses incorporated to account for medium modifications. The relaxation times were derived by linearizing the Boltzmann equation and summing over all baryonic species present in the multicomponent mixture.

## Key numbers

* Neutrino Fermi energies at the protoneutron star core are approximately 200-300 MeV for trapped electron neutrinos.
* Nuclear matter saturation density is n0 = 0.16 fm^-3; strange particles are expected to appear at 2-4 times this density in the absence of trapped neutrinos.
* The weak coupling constant is GF = 1.436 x 10^-49 erg cm^-3.
* The scattering mean free path in matter containing Lambda, Sigma-minus, and Sigma-zero hyperons is reduced by about 30-50% from its value in nucleons-only matter.
* Up to 50% reduction in mean free path may be expected from absorption reactions involving hyperons.
* Absorption reactions dominate over scattering reactions by a factor of about three to five, even in the presence of hyperons.
* Neutrino absorption on single hyperons can proceed when the Lambda hyperon concentration exceeds a critical value less than 3% (typically about 1%).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of neutrino opacities in dense matter that considered only nucleons and leptons (Sawyer 1975, Iwamoto and Pethick 1982) or charged-current reactions on hyperons (Maxwell and Prakash 1993). It directly addresses the omission in the protoneutron star cooling simulations of Keil and Janka (1995), who assessed only structural changes from the equation of state but ignored neutrino interactions with strange particles. The results enable future full simulations of thermal and leptonic evolution of protoneutron stars incorporating neutrino transport and equations of state with strangeness-rich matter, which could be compared with observations from next-generation neutrino detectors.
