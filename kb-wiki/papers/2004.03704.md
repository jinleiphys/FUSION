# Mass sum rules of the electron in quantum electrodynamics

> Different decompositions of the nucleon mass, in terms of the masses and energies of the underlying constituents, have been proposed in the literature. We explore the corresponding sum rules in quantum electrodynamics for an electron at one-loop order in perturbation theory. To this end we compute the form factors of the energy-momentum tensor, by paying particular attention to the renormalization of ultraviolet divergences, operator mixing and scheme dependence. We clarify the expressions of all the proposed sum rules in the electron rest frame in terms of renormalized operators. Furthermore, we consider the same sum rules in a moving frame, where they become energy decompositions. Finally, we discuss some implications of our study on the mass sum rules for the nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the three proposed mass decompositions of the nucleon (Ji 1995, Lorcé 2017, Hatta-Rajan-Tanaka 2018) can be studied in QED for an electron at one-loop order, but the interpretation of the separate electron and photon contributions depends critically on the renormalization scheme and operator mixing; in particular, the four-term decompositions of both Ji (1995) and Lorcé (2017) actually reduce to three non-trivial terms at this order.

## Method

The authors compute the forward matrix elements of the energy-momentum tensor (EMT) for an electron in QED at one-loop order in perturbation theory, using dimensional regularization in \(d=4-2\varepsilon\) dimensions. They evaluate the separate electron and photon contributions to the EMT form factors \(A_i(0)\) and \(\bar C_i(0)\) from the Feynman diagrams shown in Figure 1, then perform operator renormalization including mixing between electron and photon composite operators, working in both the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme and a newly defined "diagonal" (DS) scheme. The renormalized form factors are used to compute the terms appearing in the mass sum rules of Ji (1995), Lorcé (2017), and Hatta-Rajan-Tanaka (2018).

## Key numbers

* The electron form factor \(A_e^R(0) = 1 - \frac{2\alpha}{3\pi}\mathscr{L} - \frac{17}{18}\frac{\alpha}{\pi}\) and the photon form factor \(A_\gamma^R(0) = \frac{2\alpha}{3\pi}\mathscr{L} + \frac{17}{18}\frac{\alpha}{\pi}\) at one-loop in the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme, where \(\mathscr{L} = \log(\mu^2/m^2)\) and \(\alpha\) is the fine structure constant.
* In the two-term decomposition of \(m^2\) by Hatta-Rajan-Tanaka (2018), the electron contribution \(\bar{m}_e^2/m^2 = 1 - 7\alpha/(6\pi)\) in the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme and \(\bar{m}_e^2/m^2 = 1\) in the DS scheme at \(O(\alpha)\).
* The photon contribution to the same decomposition \(\bar{m}_\gamma^2/m^2 = 7\alpha/(6\pi)\) in the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme and \(\bar{m}_\gamma^2/m^2 = 0\) in the DS scheme at \(O(\alpha)\).
* In the two-term energy decomposition of Lorcé (2017), the electron partial energy \(U_e = m[1 - \alpha\mathscr{L}/(2\pi) - \alpha/\pi]\) in the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme and \(U_e = m[1 - \alpha\mathscr{L}/(2\pi) - 17\alpha/(24\pi)]\) in the DS scheme.
* The photon partial energy \(U_\gamma = m[\alpha\mathscr{L}/(2\pi) + \alpha/\pi]\) in the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme and \(U_\gamma = m[\alpha\mathscr{L}/(2\pi) + 17\alpha/(24\pi)]\) in the DS scheme.

## Context

This work extends the one-loop QED calculation of the EMT form factors first discussed by Ji (1998) for the Ji (1995) mass sum rule, now including the more recent decompositions proposed by Lorcé (2017) and Hatta-Rajan-Tanaka (2018). The study clarifies that operator mixing and scheme dependence are essential for properly defining the separate electron and photon contributions, and that the DS scheme diagonalizes the trace mixing equations, making it a natural choice for the Hatta-Rajan-Tanaka (2018) decomposition. The results provide a perturbative QED analogue that can inform the interpretation of nucleon mass sum rules in QCD, where the same operator mixing issues arise.
