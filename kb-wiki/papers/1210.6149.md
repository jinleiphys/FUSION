# On Impact of Nuclear Effects on Weak Pion Production in Sub 1 GeV Energy Region

> Charged-current single pion production in scattering off 12C is investigated for neutrino energies up to 1 GeV. An impact of nuclear effects with in-medium modifications of the Delta(1232) resonance properties as well as an effective field theory nonresonant background contribution are discussed. Dependence of the fraction of Delta(1232) decays into npnh states on incident neutrino energy is estimated. A model of Nieves et al. [1] is further developed by performing exact integration avoiding several approximations. The effect of exact integration is investigated both for double-differential and total neutrino-nucleus cross sections.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the approximations used in the Nieves et al. (2011) model for single pion production in neutrino-nucleus scattering are inadequate for double-differential cross sections, though they produce results close to exact calculations for total cross sections; additionally, the assumption of a constant fraction of pionless Delta decay cannot be applied for experiments with large flux contributions from neutrino energies below 1 GeV.

## Method

The authors developed the model of Nieves et al. (2011) by performing exact six-dimensional Monte Carlo integration using the Vegas algorithm, avoiding the earlier approximation of replacing nucleon momentum with an average value in the local Fermi sea. They incorporated in-medium modifications of the Delta(1232) resonance properties, including Pauli blocking, two- and three-nucleon absorption channels parameterized by Oset (1987), and an effective field theory nonresonant background contribution from Hernandez et al. (2007). The calculations were performed for charged-current single pion production on carbon-12 for neutrino energies up to 1 GeV.

## Key numbers

- At 1 GeV on an isoscalar target, single pion production channels account for approximately 36% of the total cross section.
- The free Delta(1232) width used in the model is 0.118 GeV, obtained with a pi-N-Delta coupling constant f* = 2.16.
- The real part of the Delta self-energy in the nuclear medium is taken as Re(Sigma) = 40 * (rho(r)/rho(0)) MeV.
- For electron (anti)neutrinos at 400 MeV, in-medium effects produce an almost constant reduction of the cross section on the level of 30-40%.
- For muon neutrino/antineutrino energies above approximately 0.6 GeV, the in-medium reduction of the cross section is of the order of 30%.
- The pion form factor uses a monopole form with cutoff Lambda_pi = 1.25 GeV; the rho-meson mass is m_rho = 0.7758 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the model of Nieves et al. (2011) for weak single pion production on nuclei by removing approximations in the numerical integration, and incorporates the nonresonant background from the chiral effective field theory approach of Hernandez et al. (2007). The in-medium Delta self-energy treatment follows the parameterization of Oset (1987) originally developed for real photon and pion scattering. The results enable experimental groups at T2K, MINOS, NOvA, MiniBooNE, and MicroBooNE to better evaluate systematic errors in neutrino oscillation analyses, particularly regarding the contamination of charged-current quasi-elastic event samples by pionless Delta decays and the energy reconstruction of neutrino events.
