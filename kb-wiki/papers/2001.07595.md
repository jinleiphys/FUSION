# Electromagnetic transition rates of Carbon-12 and Oxygen-16 in rotational-vibrational models

> We develop a formalism to calculate electromagnetic (EM) transition rates for rotational-vibrational models of nuclei. The formalism is applied to recently proposed models of Carbon-12 and Oxygen-16 which are inspired by nuclear dynamics in the Skyrme model. We compare the results to experimental data, as well as other nuclear models. The results for Carbon-12 are in good agreement with the data across all models, making it difficult to differentiate the models. More experimental data is needed to do this, and we suggest which transitions would be most interesting to measure. The models of Oxygen-16 are less successful in describing the data, and we suggest some possible improvements to our approximations which may help.

## Key claim

The paper develops a general formalism for calculating electromagnetic transition rates in rotational-vibrational models of nuclei, but the models of Oxygen-16 are less successful in describing experimental data than those for Carbon-12, and the authors suggest possible improvements to their approximations.

## Method

The authors construct a formalism for electromagnetic transition rates using rotational-vibrational wavefunctions expanded in a basis of rigid-body wavefunctions with body-fixed angular momentum projection, combined with vibrational wavefunctions on a shape space. They apply this formalism to a quantum graph model of Carbon-12, where three alpha-particles form isosceles triangles interpolating between equilateral triangle and linear chain configurations, and to an E-manifold model of Oxygen-16, where four alpha-particles form configurations on a six-punctured sphere including tetrahedral and square arrangements. The alpha-particles are treated as point charges for calculating the multipole tensor of the charge density.

## Key numbers

- Carbon-12: B(E2, 2_1^+ → 0_1^+) = 11.7 e^2 fm^4 in the quantum graph model, compared to experimental value 7.6 ± 0.42 e^2 fm^4
- Carbon-12: B(E3, 3_1^- → 0_1^+) = 62.4 e^2 fm^6 in the quantum graph model, compared to experimental value 103 ± 13.7 e^2 fm^6
- Carbon-12: B(E2, 2_1^+ → 0_2^+) = 1.10 e^2 fm^4 in the quantum graph model, compared to experimental value 2.7 ± 0.28 e^2 fm^4
- Carbon-12: B(E2, 2_2^+ → 0_2^+) = 24.7 e^2 fm^4 in the quantum graph model, compared to ab initio value 6 e^2 fm^4
- Carbon-12: B(E1, 2_3^+ → 1_1^-) = 0 in the quantum graph model, compared to experimental value (3.1 ± 0.78) × 10^{-3} e^2 fm^2
- Oxygen-16: the scale parameter κ is fixed to match the B(E3; 3_1^- → 0_1^+) transition rate

## Context

This work extends the quantum graph model for Carbon-12 introduced by Rawlinson (2019) and the E-manifold model for Oxygen-16 from Halcrow, King, and Manton (2017), both inspired by nuclear dynamics in the Skyrme model. The results are compared to the Algebraic Cluster Model (ACM) of Bijker and Iachello (2014) and ab initio lattice calculations from Epelbaum et al. (2012). The formalism enables differentiation between competing alpha-particle models of light nuclei, and the authors suggest that measuring the B(E2; 2_2^+ → 0_2^+) transition in Carbon-12 would be particularly useful for distinguishing models, as the quantum graph model predicts a value four times larger than the ab initio calculation.
