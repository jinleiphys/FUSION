# Coulomb dissociation of 9Li and the rate of the 8Li(n,g)9Li reaction

> We calculate the Coulomb dissociation of 9Li on Pb and U targets at 28.5 MeV/A beam energy within a finite range distorted wave Born approximation formalism of the breakup reactions. Invoking the principle of detailed balance, these cross sections are used to determine the excitation function and subsequently the rate of the radiative capture reaction 8Li(n,g)9Li at astrophysical energies. Our method is free from the uncertainties associated with the multipole strength distributions of the 9Li nucleus. The rate of this reaction at a temperature of 10^9K is found to be about 2900 cm^3 mole^{-1} s^{-1}.

## Key claim

The rate of the 8Li(n,γ)9Li reaction at a temperature of 10^9 K is about 2900 cm^3 mole^{-1} s^{-1}, but this value is derived from Coulomb dissociation data that carry experimental uncertainties of approximately a factor of two, and the theoretical uncertainty is much smaller than the experimental one.

## Method

The authors calculated Coulomb dissociation cross sections of 9Li on Pb and U targets at 28.5 MeV/A beam energy using a post-form finite range distorted wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) formalism. The photodissociation cross sections were extracted from the calculated breakup cross sections via the virtual photon method, and the radiative capture cross sections for 8Li(n,γ)9Li were obtained by applying the principle of detailed balance. The ground state wave function of 9Li was generated from a Woods-Saxon potential whose depth was adjusted to reproduce the one-neutron separation energy of 4.05 MeV, and a spectroscopic factor of 0.68 ± 0.14 from a transfer reaction measurement was used.

## Key numbers

- Reaction rate at T₉ = 1 (10^9 K): approximately 2900 cm^3 mole^{-1} s^{-1}, averaged over Pb and U targets.
- Reaction rate varies in the range 2800–3100 cm^3 mole^{-1} s^{-1} for T₉ between 0.5 and 2.
- Maximum contribution to the reaction rate at T₉ = 1 comes from a relative energy of 45 keV.
- One-neutron separation energy of 9Li: 4.05 MeV.
- Woods-Saxon potential depth: V₀ = -45.21 MeV, with radius parameter r₀ = 1.25 fm, diffuseness a = 0.52 fm, core radius R = 2.49 fm, and spin-orbit strength F_s.o. = 0.351 fm.
- RMS distance of core-neutron relative motion: 3.10 fm; RMS size of 9Li: 2.55 fm.
- Spectroscopic factor for the 9Li → 8Li + n partition: 0.68 ± 0.14.
- Ratio of E2 to E1 excitation at the resonance peak (E_γ = 0.26 MeV): 0.018; over E_γ = 0–1 MeV: about 0.0023.

## Context

This work extends earlier Coulomb dissociation analyses of 9Li by Bertulani (1999), who used first-order Coulomb excitation theory and obtained a rate of 2200 cm^3 mole^{-1} s^{-1}, and by Zecher et al. (1998) and Kobayashi et al. (2003), who reported experimental upper limits of 7200 and 790 cm^3 mole^{-1} s^{-1}, respectively. The present FRDWBA method avoids uncertainties in multipole strength distributions that affect structure-based calculations by Descouvemont (1993) and Rauscher et al. (1994), and yields a rate about 30% higher than Bertulani (1999) but much lower than the shell-model result of Mao and Champagne (1991) and the systematic estimate of Malaney and Fowler (1989). The result implies that the 8Li(n,γ)9Li reaction does not significantly reduce the production of elements with A > 12 through the 8Li(α,n)11B chain in inhomogeneous big bang nucleosynthesis.
