# The self-consistent analysis for sub-barrier fusion enhancement effect in Ca + Ca and Ni + Ni

> The fusion dynamic mechanism of heavy-ions at energies near the Coulomb barrier is complicated and still not very clear up to now. Accordingly, a self-consistent method based on the CCFULL calculations has been developed and applied for an ingoing study of the effect of the positive Q-value neutron transfer (PQNT) channels in this work. The typical experimental fusion data of Ca + Ca and Ni + Ni is analyzed within the unified calculation scheme. The PQNT effect in near-barrier fusion is further confirmed based on the self-consistent analysis and extracted quantitatively.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that positive Q-value neutron transfer (PQNT) channels cause sub-barrier fusion enhancement in Ca + Ca and Ni + Ni systems, but only after the inelastic coupling effects are calibrated using reference systems that lack PQNT channels (such as the symmetric systems). The enhancement is extracted quantitatively using the residual enhancement (RE) method, which reduces uncertainty from inelastic couplings.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-channel (CC) calculations using the CCFULL code with the Akyuz-Winther proximity potential parameterized into Woods-Saxon form. They selected the near-spherical systems 40,48Ca + 40,48Ca and 58,64Ni + 58,64Ni, using the symmetric systems (40Ca + 40Ca and 48Ca + 48Ca) as benchmarks to calibrate the inelastic coupling schemes. The RE ratio (experimental cross section divided by CC calculation result considering major inelastic couplings) was then computed for the asymmetric systems to isolate the PQNT effect.

## Key numbers

- For 40Ca + 48Ca, the Qgg-value for the +2n transfer channel is +2.62 MeV, and for the +4n channel is +3.88 MeV, indicating positive Q-value neutron transfer channels exist
- For 58Ni + 64Ni, the Qgg-value for the +2n transfer channel is +3.89 MeV, and for the +4n channel is +3.89 MeV
- The uncoupled Coulomb barrier for 40Ca + 48Ca is 53.189 MeV with barrier radius 10.080 fm
- The uncoupled Coulomb barrier for 58Ni + 64Ni is 97.713 MeV with barrier radius 10.754 fm
- Deformation parameters used: 40Ca 3- state at 3.737 MeV with beta = 0.270; 48Ca 2+ state at 3.832 MeV with beta = 0.104; 58Ni 2+ state at 1.454 MeV with beta = 0.154; 64Ni 2+ state at 1.346 MeV with beta = 0.158

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent RE method proposed by Jia (2016) to systematically analyze the PQNT effect in fusion reactions, building on earlier experimental studies by Beckerman (1980) who first discovered the effect in 58,64Ni + 58,64Ni systems. The analysis addresses inconsistencies in previous experimental data and theoretical descriptions, particularly the limitations of standard coupled-channel models that require external parameters for the nucleus-nucleus potential and couplings as noted by Simenel (2013). The results enable quantitative extraction of PQNT enhancement, which can help constrain theoretical models for superheavy element synthesis and astrophysical nucleosynthesis.
