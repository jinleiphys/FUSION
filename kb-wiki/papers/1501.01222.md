# Clusters in nuclear matter and Mott points

> Light clusters (mass number $A \leq 4$) in nuclear matter at subsaturation densities are described using a quantum statistical approach. In addition to self-energy and Pauli-blocking, effects of continuum correlations are taken into account to calculate the quasiparticle properties and abundances of light elements. Medium-modified quasiparticle properties are important ingredients to derive a nuclear matter equation of state applicable in the entire region of warm dense matter below saturation density. The influence of the nucleon-nucleon interaction on the quasiparticle shift is discussed.

## Key claim

Light clusters (A ≤ 4) in nuclear matter at subsaturation densities are described using a quantum statistical approach that includes self-energy, Pauli-blocking, and continuum correlations; the paper argues that the Mott line (where bound states dissolve) is not very sensitive to the details of the nucleon-nucleon interaction potential, but depends significantly on whether the occupation numbers used in the Pauli blocking term include only free nucleons or also correlated nucleons.

## Method

The author derives the in-medium Schrödinger equation from a Green function approach, obtaining cluster quasiparticle shifts as the sum of self-energy, Pauli blocking, and Coulomb contributions. The Pauli blocking term is evaluated using different separable nucleon-nucleon interaction potentials (Yamaguchi with Lorentzian form factors, Gaussian, and Mongan type with repulsive core) to compute the deuteron Mott line. The generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck formula is used to calculate the composition of symmetric nuclear matter, separating bound state and continuum contributions to the correlated density.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: n_sat ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³
- Deuteron vacuum binding energy: E_d = -2.225 MeV
- For T = 10 MeV, Mott line from Schmidt et al. using separable Paris (PEST) potential: n_Mott_d = 0.013 fm⁻³
- For T = 10 MeV, Mott line from Stein et al. using Yamaguchi potential: n_Mott_d = 0.02 fm⁻³
- For T = 10 MeV, first-order Pauli blocking shift: 352.5 MeV fm³ × n_B, giving Mott line n_Mott_d,(1) = 0.0063 fm⁻³
- For T = 10 MeV, exact solution with Gaussian potential: n_Mott_d = 0.009585 fm⁻³
- For T = 10 MeV, Mongan potential (with repulsive core): n_Mott_d = 0.0094967 fm⁻³
- For T = 10 MeV, Yamaguchi Lorentzian potential: n_Mott_d = 0.0095542 fm⁻³
- For T = 10 MeV, Mott line including correlations in occupation: n_Mott_d = 0.0087987 fm⁻³
- Effective shift Mott point at T = 10 MeV: n_B,d_Mott_point = 0.005225 fm⁻³
- Effective shift Mott point at T = 5 MeV: n_B,d_Mott_point = 0.00292 fm⁻³
- Experimental Mott point from Natowitz at T ≈ 4.5 MeV: n_B,d_Mott_point ≈ 0.001 fm⁻³
- First-order Mott line values for symmetric matter: n_Mott_d,(1)(20 MeV) = 0.0128 fm⁻³, n_Mott_d,(1)(50 MeV) = 0.0385 fm⁻³, n_Mott_d,(1)(100 MeV) = 0.0968 fm⁻³, n_Mott_d,(1)(140 MeV) = 0.154 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the quantum statistical approach to nuclear matter equation of state developed by Röpke, Schmidt, and others (Röpke 1982, Röpke 1983) by systematically comparing different nucleon-nucleon interaction potentials for the Pauli blocking calculation. The results enable improved parametrizations of effective cluster binding energies for use in equation of state models for heavy-ion collisions and core-collapse supernovae, as discussed by Natowitz (2014) and Typel (2010). The paper also relates the microscopic QS approach to semi-empirical excluded volume models (Hempel 2012) and effective binding energy shifts (Grigo 2007, Sumiyoshi 2009).
