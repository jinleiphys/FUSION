# Instanton interactions in dense-matter QCD

> A Coulomb gas representation of dense-matter QCD is derived from a dual transformation of the low-energy effective Lagrangian. The point-like charges Q=+1,-1 of the gas are identified with the instantons and anti-instantons of such topological charges. An instanton repels another instanton with the same force as it attracts an anti-instanton, in contrast to the semiclassical interaction.

## Key claim

The authors derive a Coulomb gas representation of high-density QCD from a dual transformation of the low-energy effective Lagrangian, and identify the point-like charges of the gas with instantons and anti-instantons. The key hedge is that this identification and the resulting Coulomb-like interactions between same-sign instantons hold only at large distances and in the high-density regime where the instanton gas is dilute and analytically controllable, in contrast to the semiclassical approximation where instantons interact only with anti-instantons.

## Method

The authors start from the low-energy effective Lagrangian for high-density QCD with two massless quark flavors, which describes the pseudoscalar isoscalar excitations (the \(\eta\) boson) and the dependence on the QCD vacuum angle \(\theta\). They rewrite the partition function of this sine-Gordon-type theory as that of a four-dimensional Coulomb gas by expanding the cosine term, performing a binomial expansion, and integrating out the \(\varphi\) field via a Gaussian path integral. The resulting Coulomb gas partition function contains charges \(Q = \pm 1\) with fugacity \(\lambda\) proportional to the instanton-induced potential coefficient \(a\), and the interaction between charges is mediated by the four-dimensional Coulomb potential \(G(x) = 1/(4\pi^2 x^2)\).

## Key numbers

- The fugacity \(\lambda\) is given by \(\lambda = a \mu^2 \Delta^2 / u\), where \(a = 5 \times 10^4 (\ln(\mu/\Lambda_{\rm QCD}))^7 (\Lambda_{\rm QCD}/\mu)^{29/3}\) for \(N_f = 2\).
- The instanton size density at finite chemical potential is \(n_0(\rho) = [0.466 e^{-1.679 N_c} 1.34^{N_f} / ((N_c-1)!(N_c-2)!)] (8\pi^2/g^2)^{2N_c} \rho^{-5} \exp(-8\pi^2/g^2(\rho)) e^{-N_f \mu^2 \rho^2}\).
- The hierarchy of scales for large \(\mu\) is: instanton size \(\mu^{-1} \ll\) cutoff \(\Delta^{-1} \ll\) average inter-instanton distance \((\sqrt{a} \mu \Delta)^{-1/2} \ll\) Debye screening length \((\sqrt{a} \Delta)^{-1}\).
- For the \(N_f = 3\) color-flavor-locking case, the coefficient is \(a' = 7 \times 10^3 (m_s/\mu) (\ln(\mu/\Lambda_{\rm QCD}))^7 (\Lambda_{\rm QCD}/\mu)^9\).

## Context

This work extends the instanton calculus to the high-density regime of QCD, where the instanton gas becomes arbitrarily dilute and analytically controllable, in contrast to the QCD vacuum where the instanton gas is more like a liquid (Schaefer and Shuryak, 1998). The authors build on their earlier derivation of the effective Lagrangian for high-density QCD (Son, Stephanov, and Zhitnitsky, 2001) and on prior work using a Coulomb gas representation for zero-density QCD (JZ, 2000), which lacked theoretical control. The results enable precise calculations of instanton interactions and theta dependence in dense matter, and can be generalized to the color-flavor-locking state of \(N_f=3\) QCD and to QCD at large isospin density, where lattice simulations are possible.
