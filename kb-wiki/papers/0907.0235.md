# Sommerfeld enhancement from Goldstone pseudo-scalar exchange

> We point out that the exchange of a Goldstone pseudo-scalar can provide an enhancement in the dark matter annihilation rate capable of explaining the excess flux seen in high energy cosmic ray data. The mechanism of enhancement involves the coupling of s and d waves through the tensor force that is very strong and, in fact, singular at short distances. The results indicate that large enhancements require some amount of fine tuning. We also discuss the enhancement due to other singular attractive potentials, such as WIMP models with a permanent electric dipole.

## Key claim

The exchange of a Goldstone pseudo-scalar boson between dark matter particles can produce a Sommerfeld enhancement in the annihilation rate large enough to explain the observed cosmic ray excess, but only if the model parameters are fine-tuned so that a bound state lies close to threshold.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled-channel Schrödinger equation for a spin-1/2 Dirac fermion dark matter particle and its antiparticle interacting via one-Goldstone-boson exchange, which produces both a central Yukawa potential and a tensor potential that mixes s-wave (L=0) and d-wave (L=2) components in the J=1, S=1 channel. Because the tensor potential is singular (∼1/r³ at short distances), the calculation requires a renormalization procedure: a short-distance constant potential V₀ (from a four-fermion operator) is introduced at a cutoff scale R∼1/M, and its strength is adjusted to make low-energy observables cutoff-independent. The Sommerfeld enhancement is then computed as the ratio of the interacting to free s-wave wavefunction squared, smeared over the short-distance region.

## Key numbers

- Dark matter mass M = 1000 GeV, Goldstone boson mass m = 1 GeV, and incoming momentum k = 0.2 m (0.2 GeV) are used in the single-channel example.
- For the coupled-channel Goldstone boson case, M = 1000 GeV, m = 1 GeV, and k = 1 GeV are used, with cutoff R = 1/M = 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹.
- The Goldstone boson coupling is α = g²/(16π f), with natural values g∼1 and 4π f ∼ TeV; the potential strength parameter G is defined via α = G/M².
- The Goldstone boson mass is constrained to m ≳ 0.3 GeV from Big-Bang nucleosynthesis considerations.
- The short-distance potential V₀ is varied; in the coupled-channel results shown, V₀ = -10/(M R²) for the G-scan and the coupling range explored is 1 < G < 55.

## Context

This work extends the Sommerfeld enhancement mechanism, previously studied for central Yukawa potentials (Hisano et al., 2004; Cirelli et al., 2007; Arkani-Hamed et al., 2009), to the case of pseudo-scalar exchange where the tensor force dominates. The problem is closely analogous to the one-pion exchange potential in nuclear physics (Bedaque et al., 2003; Epelbaum, 2006), but with opposite sign for particle-antiparticle interactions and a larger hierarchy between the heavy and light mass scales. The results show that, unlike the universal enhancement from non-singular potentials, the enhancement from singular tensor potentials depends on a short-distance parameter and requires fine-tuning, which limits the predictive power of such models for dark matter annihilation.
