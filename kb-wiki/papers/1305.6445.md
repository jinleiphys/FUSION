# Gluon bremsstrahlung by heavy quarks - its effects on transport coefficients and equilibrium distribution

> The effects of gluon radiation by charm quarks on the transport coefficients {\it e.g.} drag, longitudinal and transverse diffusion and shear viscosity have been studied within the ambit of perturbative quantum chromodynamics (pQCD) and kinetic theory. We found that while the soft gluon radiation has substantial effects on the transport coefficients of the charm quarks in the quark gluon plasma its effects on the equilibrium distribution function is insignificant.

## Key claim

The authors find that including soft gluon bremsstrahlung from charm quarks substantially increases the drag, longitudinal diffusion, and transverse diffusion coefficients compared to elastic collisions alone, but that this radiative contribution has an insignificant effect on the shape of the charm quark's equilibrium distribution function, because the shape depends on ratios of transport coefficients rather than their absolute magnitudes.

## Method

The transport coefficients (drag, longitudinal diffusion, transverse diffusion) for charm quarks in a quark-gluon plasma are calculated using perturbative QCD and kinetic theory via the Fokker-Planck equation. Elastic (2-to-2) scattering amplitudes are combined with radiative (2-to-3) amplitudes for soft gluon emission, where the latter is factorized as the elastic amplitude multiplied by a gluon emission spectrum including a dead-cone factor and LPM suppression via a formation-time cutoff. The total effective transport coefficient is taken as the sum of the collisional and radiative contributions.

## Key numbers

- For a charm quark with momentum \(p = 5\) GeV at bath temperature \(T = 300\) MeV, the total drag coefficient (collisional plus radiative) is almost double the collisional-only value.
- At \(T = 600\) MeV for the same momentum, the total drag becomes 2.12 times the collisional drag.
- For a charm quark with momentum 10 GeV at \(T = 525\) MeV, the collisional drag is 0.038 fm\(^{-1}\) and the radiative contribution is 0.047 fm\(^{-1}\).
- At the same conditions, the radiative transverse diffusion coefficient is about 1.33 times the collisional one, and the radiative longitudinal diffusion coefficient is 1.2 times the collisional one.
- Fitting the equilibrium distribution to a Tsallis form using only collisional transport coefficients at \(T = 525\) MeV gives \(q = 1.101\) and \(T_T = 184\) MeV; including radiative coefficients does not alter these values.
- At \(T = 725\) MeV, the Tsallis parameter \(q = 1.095\) (nearly unchanged) and \(T_T = 335\) MeV; the ratio \(T/T_T\) is 2.85 at 525 MeV and 2.164 at 725 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of charm quark transport coefficients that considered only elastic collisions (e.g., Moore and Teaney (2005), Gossiaux and Aichelin (2008), Hees et al. (2006)) or estimated radiative effects via the Einstein relation from drag (e.g., Mazumder et al. (2012), Abir et al. (2012)). It also revisits the generalized Einstein relation derived by Rafelski et al. (2012) to include radiative processes. The results enable more realistic modeling of heavy-quark observables (nuclear modification factor, elliptic flow) at RHIC and LHC, and provide a direct calculation of \(\eta/s\) from the transverse diffusion coefficient that brings theory closer to experimental values from Adare et al. (2010).
