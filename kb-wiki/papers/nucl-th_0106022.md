# Isospin dependence of nuclear matter symmetry energy coefficients

> Generalized symmetry energy coefficients of asymmetric nuclear matter are obtained as screening functions. The dependence of the isospin symmetry energy coefficient on the neutron proton (n-p) asymmetry may be determined unless by a constant (exponent) $Z$ which depend on microscopic properties. The dependence of the generalized symmetry energy coefficients with Skyrme forces on the n-p asymmetry and on the density, only from .5 up to 1.5 $ρ_0$, are investigated in the isospin and scalar channels. The use of Skyrme-type effective forces allows us to obtain analytical expressions for these parameters as well as their dependences on the neutron-proton (n-p) asymmetry, density and even temperature. Whereas the density dependence of these coefficients obtained with Skyrme forces is not necessarily realistic the dependence on the n-p asymmetry exhibit a more consistent behaviour. The isospin symmetry energy coefficient (s.e.c.) increases as the n-p asymmetry acquires higher values whereas the isoscalar s.e.c. decreases. Some consequences for the Supernovae mechanism are discussed.

# Isospin dependence of nuclear matter symmetry energy coefficients

## Key claim

The generalized symmetry energy coefficients of asymmetric nuclear matter can be obtained as screening functions, and the isospin symmetry energy coefficient increases with neutron-proton asymmetry; however, the exact functional form of this dependence contains a constant exponent Z that cannot be determined from the Skyrme-force calculations presented, and the derived analytical expression for the asymmetry dependence (Equation isosb) is not compatible with the numerical results obtained from the Skyrme forces used.

## Method

The static limit of the dynamical polarizability of asymmetric nuclear matter was derived from the Hartree-Fock equation with an external perturbation, yielding generalized symmetry energy coefficients as screening functions for the isovector and scalar channels. Analytical expressions for these coefficients were obtained using Skyrme-type effective interactions (SLyb, SkSC4, SkSC6, SkSC10) with different density dependences, and their dependence on neutron-proton asymmetry b and density (from 0.5 ρ₀ to 1.5 ρ₀) was computed at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- The isospin symmetry energy coefficient at saturation density is nearly 30 MeV for the parameterization of Pal (1998), with S₀ ≈ 30 MeV
- The SkSC forces yield different symmetry energies at saturation: SkSC4 gives 27 MeV, SkSC6 gives 30 MeV, and SkSC10 gives 32 MeV
- For the scalar channel incompressibility parameterization from reference BCK, the coefficient a' ranges from 1.28 up to 1.99 for several Skyrme interactions at zero temperature
- The SkSC forces give incompressibility parameters: SkSC4 (K_v=234.7 MeV, K_s=-334.9 MeV); SkSC10 (K_v=235.4 MeV, K_s=-203.5 MeV); SkSC6 (K_v=235.8 MeV, K_s=-136.6 MeV)
- The density range investigated is from 0.5 ρ₀ up to 1.5 ρ₀

## Context

This work extends the author's preliminary results (Braghin 2000) on nuclear matter screening functions and generalizes the approach to Skyrme forces with more involved density dependence, including those from Chabanat (1997) and the SkSC parameterizations from Onsi and Pearson (1998) which were fitted to nuclear masses and neutron star properties. The study contrasts with the density-dependent parameterization of Pal (1998) and with microscopic calculations by Bombaci and Lombardo (1991) and Wiringa (1993). The results enable improved modeling of the supernova mechanism, suggesting that the increase of the isospin symmetry energy coefficient with asymmetry suppresses deleptonization and helps successful explosion, consistent with analysis of SN 1987a by Bethe (1990), Dean (1998), and Donati (1998).
