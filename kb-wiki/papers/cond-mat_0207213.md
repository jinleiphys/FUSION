# Effects of the trapping potential on a superfluid atomic Fermi Gas

> We examine a dilute two-component atomic Fermi gas trapped in a harmonic potential in the superfluid phase. For experimentally realistic parameters, the trapping potential is shown to have crucial influence on various properties of the gas. Using an effective hamiltonian, analytical results for the critical temperature, the temperature dependence of the superfluid gap, and the energy of the lowest collective modes are derived. These results are shown to agree well with numerical calculations. We furthermore discuss in more detail a previous proposed method to experimentally observe the superfluid transition by looking at the collective mode spectrum. Our results are aimed at the present experimental effort to observe a superfluid phase transition in a trapped atomic Fermi gas.

# Effects of the trapping potential on a superfluid atomic Fermi Gas

## Key claim

For a dilute two-component atomic Fermi gas in a harmonic trap, the critical temperature in the intrashell regime can be orders of magnitude higher than the bulk BCS prediction, but this result holds only when the Cooper pair coherence length is comparable to the system size (ξ_BCS ≃ R) and the condition k_B T_c ≪ ħω_T is satisfied.

## Method

The author derives an effective Hamiltonian for pairing between particles with time-reversed angular momentum quantum numbers (l,m) and (l,-m) within the same harmonic oscillator shell. Analytical expressions for the critical temperature, temperature-dependent superfluid gap, and collective mode energies are obtained from the gap equation in the intrashell regime and compared to numerical solutions of the Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations using a pseudopotential scheme. The collective mode spectrum is calculated within a random-phase approximation (RPA) framework including superfluid correlations.

## Key numbers

- For k_F|a| = 0.1, the crossover particle number between intrashell and bulk regimes is N_σ^* = 4 × 10^20; for k_F|a| = 0.3, N_σ^* = 9 × 10^6
- Current experiments have N ∼ O(10^5) particles trapped, placing them in the intrashell regime
- The effective coupling strength for pairing is G = 32π^(−2) k_F|a|/15
- The critical temperature in the intrashell regime is given by k_B T_c/ħω_T ≃ G/(2 − 4 ln(e^γ n_F)G) where γ ≃ 0.577
- For G → 0, k_B T_c/ħω_T = G/2
- At T=0, the gap Δ_0/k_B T_c ≃ 2
- For (T_c − T)/T_c ≪ 1, Δ(T)/k_B T_c = √12 √(1 − T/T_c)
- Numerical calculations used n_F ∼ 30, giving N = 2N_σ ∼ 10^4 particles trapped
- The low-energy quadrupole mode frequency ħω_Q ≃ 2Δ(T) in the intrashell regime
- Trapping frequencies in optical traps are ω_T ∼ O(10^3 Hz), yielding ħω_T ∼ O(10^2 nK)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Bruun and Heiselberg (2002) and Heiselberg and Bennett (1998) on the nature of the superfluid state in trapped Fermi gases, showing that the shell structure of the harmonic trap dramatically enhances the critical temperature compared to the homogeneous BCS result of Gorkov (1958) and Stoof (1994). The paper enables experimental detection of the superfluid transition through collective mode spectroscopy, specifically the emergence of low-energy monopole and quadrupole pair vibration modes at ħω ∼ 2Δ, and provides analytical formulas that agree with numerical BdG calculations for systems with ∼10^4 particles.
