# Updated analysis of NN elastic scattering to 3 GeV

> A partial-wave analysis of NN elastic scattering data has been updated to include a number of recent measurements. Experiments carried out at the Cooler Synchrotron (COSY) by the EDDA Collaboration have had a significant impact above 1 GeV. Results are discussed in terms of the partial-wave and direct-reconstruction amplitudes.

## Key claim

The updated partial-wave analysis (SP07) provides a much improved fit to recent polarized data from the EDDA collaboration at COSY, but the resulting partial-wave amplitudes, particularly the \(^1D_2\) and \(^1G_4\) waves, have changed dramatically above 1 GeV; the solution should be considered at best qualitative between 2.5 and 3 GeV due to the absence of data in that region.

## Method

The authors performed an energy-dependent partial-wave analysis (PWA) of proton-proton (pp) and neutron-proton (np) elastic scattering data, incorporating new measurements from the EDDA collaboration at COSY, SATURNE II, PSI, Uppsala, IUCF, Ohio University, TUNL, and JINR. The analysis used the standard SAID facility formalism, constructing observables from partial-wave amplitudes with allowed combinations of spin, angular momentum, and isospin, and compared results with direct amplitude reconstruction (DAR) amplitudes from the Saclay group.

## Key numbers

- The SP07 solution fits 24,916 pp data points with a total \(\chi^2\) of 44,463 over the energy range 0–3000 MeV laboratory kinetic energy.
- The SP07 solution fits 12,693 np data points with a total \(\chi^2\) of 21,496 over the energy range 0–1300 MeV.
- For pp data in the 2500–3000 MeV range, the normalized \(\chi^2\)/data is 3.3 for SP07 compared to 3.6 for the previous SP00 solution.
- For pp data in the 1500–2000 MeV range, the normalized \(\chi^2\)/data is 2.0 for SP07 compared to 2.6 for SP00.
- The EDDA collaboration contributed 2888 unpolarized cross section data points (d\(\sigma\)/d\(\Omega\)) from 240–2577 MeV with a \(\chi^2\) of 3327.
- The EDDA collaboration contributed 403 data points each for \(A_{xx}\), \(A_{yy}\), and \(A_{zx}\) observables from 481–2492 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the previous SP00 analysis (Arndt et al., 2000) which extended the upper energy limit from 2.5 to 3.0 GeV, and compares with the Saclay DAR study (Lehar et al.) that gave amplitudes to 2.7 GeV. The new fit resolves ambiguities found in SP00 where energy-dependent and single-energy solutions followed different branches of DAR amplitudes, particularly for the imaginary parts of amplitudes \(a\) and \(b\). The results enable further progress on np scattering, which will require extending measurements above 1.3 GeV, as proposed for the Nuclotron at JINR, Dubna using a polarized deuteron beam and polarized proton target.
