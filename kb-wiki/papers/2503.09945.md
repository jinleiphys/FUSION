# Nucleon resonance structure to 2 GeV and the nature of the Roper

> The study of the spectrum of excited states of the nucleon is vital to our understanding of how QCD is realized in the baryon spectrum. Using a simultaneous analysis of the pion nucleon scattering data up to 2 GeV as well as the results of lattice QCD calculations, we obtain new insight into the nature of the Roper resonance as well as the excited states around 1.9 GeV.

## Key claim
By simultaneously fitting experimental pion-nucleon scattering data up to 2 GeV and lattice QCD results within a Hamiltonian Effective Field Theory (HEFT) framework, the authors claim new insight into the Roper resonance and nearby excited states, but note that multiple valid parameter solutions exist and the physical interpretation depends on the model-dependent eigenvector decompositions.

## Method
The authors constructed a Hamiltonian with two bare baryon states (ground state nucleon and first radial excitation) and four two-body coupled channels (πN, πΔ, ηN, σN) in a HEFT framework. They constrained the 32 free parameters by fitting experimental πN → πN scattering phase shifts from the WI08 partial-wave analysis in the energy range 1200–1900 MeV, resonance pole positions from the Particle Data Group, and finite-volume energy eigenstates from lattice QCD calculations by CSSM, Lang et al., and PACS-CS. Pole trajectories were traced by varying interaction strengths from 0% to 100% to identify the origins of resonances.

## Key numbers
- The Roper resonance physical mass is 1.44 GeV, while the bare excited nucleon state core mass is approximately 1.75 GeV, with the meson cloud reducing it to the physical value.
- The first radially excited nucleon state from lattice QCD lies at approximately 2 GeV, significantly higher than both the Roper core mass of 1.75 GeV and the physical Roper mass of 1.44 GeV.
- The analysis covers the energy range 1200 MeV to 1900 MeV for scattering phase shifts.
- A dipole regulator with u_min = 0.01 was adopted for finite-volume calculations.
- The pion decay constant used is f_π = 92.4 MeV.
- Pole tracing used rotation angles of approximately -70 degrees to access unphysical Riemann sheets.

## Context
This work extends the dynamical coupled-channels analysis of Suzuki (2010), who traced Roper resonance poles to a bare excited nucleon state with mass 1.763 GeV. It also builds on previous HEFT studies by Wu (2014), Abell (2022), and Hockley (2024) that established the correspondence between HEFT and Lüscher's formalism. The analysis enables a unified description of the positive-parity nucleon spectrum from first-principles lattice QCD and experimental scattering data, addressing the long-standing Roper mass problem where constituent quark models predicted a core mass near 1.75 GeV that required large meson-cloud corrections to reach the physical 1.44 GeV.
