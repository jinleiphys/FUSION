# Shell model study for neutron-rich sd-shell nuclei

> The microscopic structure of neutron-rich sd-shell nuclei is investigated by using the spherical shell-model in the sd-pf valence space with the extended pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole forces accompanied by the monopole interaction (EPQQM). The calculation reproduces systematically the known energy levels for even-even and odd-mass nuclei including the recent data for 43S, 46S and 47Ar. In particular, the erosion of the N=28 shell closure in 42Si can be explained. Our EPQQM results are compared with other shell-model calculations with the SDPF-NR and SDPF-U effective interactions.

# Shell model study for neutron-rich sd-shell nuclei

## Key claim

The extended pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole forces with monopole interaction (EPQQM) can provide a unified effective interaction that consistently describes collectivity at N=28 for both Z>14 and Z≤14 isotopic chains, where previous approaches such as SDPF-NR and SDPF-U required separate interactions for different proton ranges. The erosion of the N=28 shell closure in 42Si is explained through attractive T=0 monopole interactions between πd3/2-νf7/2 and πd5/2-νf7/2 orbitals, though the calculation deviates from experiment for 34Si and 44S, and the RMS deviations for higher-lying states (2₂⁺, 4₁⁺) are larger than for the 2₁⁺ states.

## Method

The authors performed spherical shell-model calculations in the sd-pf valence space (¹⁶O core) using the ANTOINE code, with valence protons restricted to the sd shell (0d5/2, 1s1/2, 0d3/2) and neutrons occupying the sd-pf space (0d5/2, 1s1/2, 0d3/2, 0f7/2, 1p3/2, 0f5/2, 1p1/2) with twelve sd frozen neutrons. The Hamiltonian consisted of single-particle energies, J=0 and J=2 pairing forces, quadrupole-quadrupole forces, and a monopole interaction, with strengths determined by fitting experimental energy levels for odd-mass nuclei near shell closures. Results were compared with experimental data and with other shell-model calculations using the SDPF-NR and SDPF-U effective interactions.

## Key numbers

- RMS deviations between experimental and theoretical excitation energies: 0.24 MeV for 3/2⁻ states, 0.14 MeV for 1/2⁻ states, and 0.20 MeV for 5/2⁻ states in odd-mass nuclei near shell closures
- Interaction strengths: g₀ = 0.540 MeV, g₂ = 0.678 MeV, χ₂ = 0.474 MeV
- Monopole strengths: kₘᵀ⁼⁰(f7/2,d3/2) = -0.65 MeV, kₘᵀ⁼⁰(f7/2,d5/2) = -0.3 MeV
- In ⁴³S: low-lying isomer at 320.5 keV, ground state is 3/2⁻ (not the expected 7/2⁻)
- B(E2;2⁺→0⁺) for ⁴⁴S: EPQQM gives 98 e²fm⁴, experiment gives 63 e²fm⁴
- B(E2;2⁺→0⁺) for ⁴⁶Ar: EPQQM gives 108 e²fm⁴, experiment gives 114 e²fm⁴ (new value from Mengoni (2010))
- B(E2;2⁺→0⁺) for ⁴²S: EPQQM gives 72 e²fm⁴, experiment gives 79 e²fm⁴
- RMS deviations for 2₁⁺, 2₂⁺, and 4₁⁺ states: 0.22 MeV, 0.71 MeV, and 0.92 MeV respectively
- Effective charges: eπ=1.35e, eν=0.35e for S isotopes; eπ=1.50e, eν=0.50e for Ar isotopes; eπ=1.15e, eν=0.15e for Si isotopes
- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment Q(2₁⁺) for ⁴²Si: +16 e fm² (EPQQM), indicating oblate deformation

## Context

This work extends the EPQQM model previously applied to fp-shell nuclei (Hasegawa, 2001) and fpg-shell nuclei (Kaneko, 2002) into the neutron-rich sd-pf region, and follows the approach of Retamosa (1997) and Caurier (2002, 2005) using the SDPF-NR interaction. It addresses the limitation that SDPF-NR failed for silicon isotopes and that SDPF-U required two separate interactions (one for Z>14, one for Z≤14) as proposed by Nowacki and Poves. The monopole interactions are connected to the tensor and central forces discussed by Otsuka (2001, 2005, 2008, 2010) and Smirnova (2010). The results enable predictions for shape changes in N=28 isotones and for nuclei near the neutron drip-line, though the authors note that none of the current interactions can reproduce the 2₁⁺ energy of ³⁴Si, suggesting the need to include neutron excitations across the N=20 energy gap.
