# Can the massive neutron star PSR J0348+0432 be a hyperon star?

> Whether the massive neutron star PSR J0348+0432 can change into a hyperon star is studied in the framework of the relative mean field theory by choosing the suitable hyperon coupling constants. We find that whether the mesons $σ^{*}$ and $ϕ$ being considered or not, the neutron star PSR J0348+0432 all can change into a hyperon star and the hyperon star transition density are the same for the two cases. We also find that the canonical mass neutron star also can change into a hyperon star in a minor hyperon star transition density as the mesons $σ^{*}$ and $ϕ$ are not considered. Our results confirms some of recent conclusions.

## Key claim
The massive neutron star PSR J0348+0432 can be a hyperon star under relativistic mean field theory, but only for specific tuned hyperon coupling constants; the transition density at which hyperons first appear is identical (3.041 ρ₀) whether or not the hyperon-hyperon mediating mesons σ* and φ are included.

## Method
The author used relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with the GL85 nucleon coupling constant set and hyperon coupling constants fitted to empirical hyperon well depths (U_Λ^(N) = -30 MeV, U_Σ^(N) = +40 MeV, U_Ξ^(N) = -28 MeV). For the massive neutron star PSR J0348+0432, parameters were fine-tuned to reproduce its observed mass of 2.01 M_⊙ by solving the Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations, both with and without the σ* and φ mesons that mediate hyperon-hyperon interactions. The baryon number density at which Λ hyperons first appear (the hyperon star transition density) was then computed for each case.

## Key numbers
- The massive neutron star PSR J0348+0432 has a mass of 2.01 ± 0.04 M_⊙ (observed by Antoniadis 2013).
- Without σ* and φ mesons, the tuned parameter set (x_σΛ=0.8, x_ωΛ=0.9319; x_σΣ=0.4, x_ωΣ=0.825; x_σΞ=0.6946, x_ωΞ=0.7964) yields a maximum mass of 2.01 M_⊙.
- With σ* and φ mesons, the tuned parameter set (x_σΛ=0.8, x_ωΛ=0.9319; x_σΣ=0.4, x_ωΣ=0.825; x_σΞ=0.7447, x_ωΞ=0.8671) also yields a mass of 2.01 M_⊙.
- The hyperon star transition density for PSR J0348+0432 is ρ_t = 3.041 ρ₀ in both cases (with and without σ* and φ), where ρ₀ = 0.145 fm⁻³.
- At this transition density, the relative number density of Λ hyperons is 0.001358% (without σ*, φ) and 0.001374% (with σ*, φ).
- The central baryon density of PSR J0348+0432 is ρ_c = 6.3142 ρ₀ (without σ*, φ).
- For a canonical mass neutron star (M = 1.4843 M_⊙), the hyperon star transition density is ρ_t = 2.1586 ρ₀ (without σ*, φ), with a central density ρ_c = 4.8 ρ₀.

## Context
This work extends studies of hyperon formation in massive neutron stars, following observations of PSR J0348+0432 by Antoniadis (2013) and PSR J1614-2230 by Demorest (2010). It uses the RMF framework with the GL85 parameter set from Glendenning (1985) and hyperon coupling schemes from Schaffner (1994). The results confirm conclusions by Bednarek (2012), Kolomeitsev (2005), Dexheimer (2008), and Weissenborn (2012) that massive neutron stars can accommodate hyperons when larger vector coupling constants provide sufficient repulsion. The finding that the transition density is insensitive to σ* and φ mesons enables simpler models for hyperon star structure calculations.
