# Solving The High Energy Evolution Equation Including Running Coupling Corrections

> We study the solution of the nonlinear BK evolution equation with the recently calculated running coupling corrections [hep-ph/0609105, hep-ph/0609090]. Performing a numerical solution we confirm the earlier result of [hep-ph/0408216] that the high energy evolution with the running coupling leads to a universal scaling behavior for the dipole scattering amplitude. The running coupling corrections calculated recently significantly change the shape of the scaling function as compared to the fixed coupling case leading to a considerable increase in the anomalous dimension and to a slow-down of the evolution with rapidity. The difference between the two recent calculations is due to an extra contribution to the evolution kernel, referred to as the subtraction term, which arises when running coupling corrections are included. These subtraction terms were neglected in both recent calculations. We evaluate numerically the subtraction terms for both calculations, and demonstrate that when the subtraction terms are added back to the evolution kernels obtained in the two works the resulting dipole amplitudes agree with each other! We then use the complete running coupling kernel including the subtraction term to find the numerical solution of the resulting full non-linear evolution equation with the running coupling corrections. Again the scaling regime is recovered at very large rapidity.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the two recent calculations of running coupling corrections to the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) evolution equation, which appeared to disagree, actually yield identical results once the previously neglected "subtraction terms" are included; the full evolution equation including these subtraction terms recovers universal scaling behavior at high rapidity, with the scaling function unaltered by the subtraction term.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the nonlinear BK evolution equation for the dipole scattering amplitude, first including only the running coupling terms from Balitsky (2006) and Kovchegov and Weigert (2006), then adding the subtraction terms derived from the full NLO kernel with all-orders resummation of fermion bubble chains. The subtraction terms are evaluated by performing transverse momentum integrals of the NLO kernel, expanding in powers of the running coupling, and using the Brodsky-Lepage-Mackenzie prescription to set the scale. The running coupling is frozen at a constant value in the infrared to avoid the Landau pole.

## Key numbers

- The anomalous dimension extracted from the running coupling solution is approximately gamma = 0.85, compared to gamma = 0.64 for the fixed coupling case.
- The beta function used is beta_2 = (11 N_c - 2 N_f) / (12 pi), with N_c = 3 and N_f the number of flavors.
- The subtraction terms are shown to be important at moderate rapidities but their relative contribution decreases with increasing rapidity, becoming small at high enough rapidities.

## Context

This work extends the earlier numerical study of Albacete (2004) which first showed universal scaling behavior for the dipole amplitude with running coupling, but using ad hoc prescriptions for the coupling scale. The paper resolves the apparent discrepancy between the running coupling calculations of Balitsky (2006) and Kovchegov and Weigert (2006) by showing they agree when subtraction terms are included. The results enable future studies of the full nonlinear evolution with running coupling corrections, and the authors note that the extracted anomalous dimension disagrees with analytical predictions from Mueller (2002), Triantafyllopoulos (2002), Iancu (2002), Munier (2004), and Beuf (2007).
