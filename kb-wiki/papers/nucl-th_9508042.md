# On the relationship of the scaled phase space and Skyrme-coherent state treatments of proton antiproton annihilation at rest

> We discuss pion multiplicities and single pion momentum spectra from proton antiproton annihilation at rest. Both the scaled phase space model and the Skyrme-coherent state approach describe these observables well. In the coherent state approach the puzzling size of the scale parameter relating the phase space integrals for different multiplicities is replaced by a well defined weight function. The strength of this function is determined by the intensity of the classical pion field and its spatial extent is of order 1 fm.

## Key claim

The scaled phase space (SPS) model and the Skyrme-coherent state approach both describe pion multiplicities and single pion momentum spectra from proton antiproton annihilation at rest equally well, but the coherent state approach replaces the ad hoc scaling parameter of SPS with a physically motivated form factor whose strength is fixed by the total energy release (2M) and whose spatial extent is of order 1 fm.

## Method

The authors compare two theoretical frameworks for proton antiproton annihilation at rest into pions. The scaled phase space model introduces a scaling length L (set to 1.2 fm) to relate phase space integrals for different pion multiplicities, with the average pion multiplicity fixed to the experimental value of 5. The Skyrme-coherent state approach uses a classical pion field from Skyrmion dynamics, quantized via coherent states projected onto states of definite energy-momentum, with a form factor f(k) parameterized by a size parameter alpha and a strength C0 determined by the energy release.

## Key numbers

- The scaling length in the SPS model is L = 1.2 fm, adjusted to give the correct average pion number of 5.
- The average pion multiplicity from experiment is hat{n} = 5.
- The variance of the pion multiplicity distribution in the SPS model is 1.
- The volume in the SPS model is V = (2 pi L)^3, which is of order (2 pi)^3 fm^3.
- The form factor parameter alpha = 3.0 mu, where mu is the pion mass.
- The form factor strength C0 = 0.061 GeV^5, determined by requiring the energy release of the classical pion source to be 2M.
- The r.m.s. size of the pion source in the Skyrme-coherent state approach is 0.7 fm.
- The classical mean pion multiplicity without energy-momentum conservation is 4.5 for alpha = 3 mu.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Skyrme-coherent state calculations (Amado et al., 1995) and compares them to the statistical phase space approach originally introduced by Fermi. The scaled phase space model had historically been unable to explain the large scaling volume needed (Dover, 1989; older references), while the Skyrme-coherent state approach provides a dynamical origin for the multiplicity weights. The results enable future complete Skyrme dynamics calculations of annihilation with no free parameters, and have already been extended to calculate pion charge branching ratios and vector meson production (Lu and Amado, 1995) and two-pion correlations (Amado et al., 1994).
