# Improving a radiative plus collisional energy loss model for application to RHIC and LHC

> With the QGP opacity computed perturbatively and with the global entropy constraints imposed by the observed dNch/dy~1000, radiative energy loss alone cannot account for the observed suppression of single non-photonic electrons. Collisional energy loss is comparable in magnitude to radiative loss for both light and heavy jets. # Improving a radiative plus collisional energy loss model for application to RHIC and LHC

## Key claim
Radiative energy loss alone cannot account for the observed suppression of single non-photonic electrons at RHIC when the QGP opacity is computed perturbatively and global entropy constraints from dNch/dy~1000 are imposed; collisional energy loss is comparable in magnitude to radiative loss for both light and heavy jets, and two refinements, fluctuations in the number of collisions and a running QCD coupling, significantly alter the predicted collisional energy loss.

## Method
The authors model the QGP as a weakly coupled medium and compute collisional energy loss using a Gyulassy-Wang model with hard-thermal-loop (HTL) medium-modified propagators, generating single-collision probability distributions for longitudinal momentum loss. These distributions are Poisson-convoluted to obtain energy loss distributions for finite numbers of expected collisions, and the resulting R_AA values are compared to the Gaussian approximation used in the earlier WHDG model. Separately, the authors evaluate Bjorken-style collisional energy loss formulas with a running QCD coupling at one-loop, including finite-energy kinematic bounds, and compare results to fixed-alpha_s=0.3 calculations.

## Key numbers
- The expected mean free path of a quark in the Gyulassy-Wang model is ~2 fm.
- For light quarks with average number of collisions <n>=2, the Gaussian approximation overpredicts the R_AA suppression by ~0.1, which is around a 30% effect.
- For bottom quarks with <n>=10 collisions, the Gaussian approximation and the full Poisson-convoluted distribution differ in R_AA by only ~0.01.
- Including a running QCD coupling increases the collisional energy loss by up to a factor of ~1.5 compared to fixed alpha_s=0.3.
- The running coupling results are evaluated for temperatures T~0.2-0.3 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the WHDG model (Wicks, Horowitz, Djordjevic, Gyulassy, 2005), which first argued that pQCD collisional energy loss cannot be neglected for heavy quarks and that full geometric integration over the QGP is required. The paper addresses two specific theoretical uncertainties identified in WHDG: the treatment of collisional fluctuations via a Fokker-Planck/Gaussian approximation versus a discrete collision-by-collision treatment, and the use of a fixed QCD coupling alpha_s=0.3 versus a running coupling. The results are relevant to interpreting non-photonic electron data from PHENIX and STAR at RHIC, and to predictions for LHC. The running coupling analysis follows the approach of Peshier (2006) and Braun (2006) but adds finite-energy kinematic bounds. The combination of these refinements, together with uncertainties in charm-to-bottom cross-section ratios, suggests that both pion and electron R_AA may be describable within purely perturbative calculations.
