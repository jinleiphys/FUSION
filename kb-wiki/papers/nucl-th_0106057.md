# The equivalence theorem and the Bethe-Salpeter equation

> We solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for two-particle scattering in a field-theoretical model using two lagrangians related by a field transformation. The kernel of the equation consists of the sum of all tree-level diagrams for each lagrangian. The solutions differ even if all four external particles are put on the mass shell, which implies that observables calculated by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation depend on the representation of the theory. We point out that this violation of the equivalence theorem has a simple explanation and should be expected for any Bethe-Salpeter equation with a tree-level kernel. Implications for dynamical models of hadronic interactions are discussed.

## Key claim

The on-shell scattering amplitude obtained by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) with a tree-level kernel depends on the representation of the theory, even when the two representations are related by a field transformation that guarantees identical on-shell tree-level amplitudes; this violation of the equivalence theorem arises because the BSE with a tree-level kernel does not generate the full set of loop diagrams required for representation independence.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for two-particle scattering of neutral spinless scalar fields (phi and sigma) using two lagrangians related by a non-local field transformation that included form factors. The kernel of the BSE was constructed as the sum of all tree-level diagrams from each lagrangian, with the s-channel pole diagram using bare mass and coupling while u-channel and contact diagrams used renormalized physical parameters. The S-wave phase shifts for phi-sigma scattering were computed in both representations and compared as a function of the transformation parameter f and scattering energy.

## Key numbers

- Masses used: M = 1000 MeV (phi), m = 150 MeV (sigma); coupling constant g = 13
- Cut-off parameters: Lambda_T = Lambda_phi = Lambda_sigma = 2 GeV
- Transformation parameter f varied between 0 and 0.13
- The S-wave phase shifts delta^(I) and delta^(II) differed between representations; the difference delta^(II) - delta^(I) depended on f and energy
- The representation-dependence decreased at lower energies as loop contributions became less important
- Scattering lengths a^(I) and a^(II) differed between the two representations
- It was impossible to find transformation parameters Lambda_T and f (except f=0) that made the phase shifts identical in both representations

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the equivalence theorem for tree-level and one-loop calculations in hadronic models (Davies 1996, Scherer 1995, Fearing 1998, Adam 1998) to the multi-loop context of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, which is used in dynamical models of pion-nucleon (Pearce 1991) and photon-nucleon (Nozawa 1990) interactions. The results show that the choice of representation in BSE-based models is an additional model assumption that affects on-shell observables, and that the K-matrix approach (Goudsmit 1994) preserves the equivalence theorem because it uses on-shell tree-level amplitudes.
