# The Predictive Power of Effective Field Theory in NN Scattering: ^3S_1-^3D_1 Mixing at Next-to Next-to Leading Order

## Key claim

Although the NNLO calculation of the ^3S_1-^3D_1 mixing angle ε₁ describes scattering data well at momenta much less than 50 MeV, varying the renormalization condition (i.e., the momentum at which the free parameter ζ₆ is fit) produces 100% uncertainties at momenta as low as 100 MeV, implying a fundamental lack of predictive power at this order in the Q expansion. This lack of predictive power is traced to large cancellations between NNLO terms that are several times larger than the NLO terms, and is consistent with simple power counting arguments given the small empirical size of ε₁.

## Method

The authors compute the ^3S_1-^3D_1 mixing angle ε₁ in nucleon-nucleon scattering using the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) Q-counting scheme of chiral effective field theory, working to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). They use the expressions derived by Fleming, Mehan, and Stewart (FMS) for the s-d mixing amplitude, which includes one-pion-exchange potential pions, radiation pions, and a single undetermined short-distance counterterm C₂^(SD) (parametrized as ζ₆). To test the sensitivity to higher-order terms, the authors vary the renormalization condition by fitting ζ₆ so that the NNLO prediction agrees with the Nijmegen partial wave analysis at different fixed momenta p_R ranging from 20 to 200 MeV, rather than using a single global fit.

## Key numbers

- The NNLO calculation describes data well at momenta much less than 50 MeV, but varying the renormalization condition results in 100% expected uncertainties at momenta as low as 100 MeV.
- By 200 MeV, differences between fits at different renormalization points reach 200%.
- The values of ζ₆ for fits crossing the data vary only from 0.359 (for p_R = 20 MeV) to 0.412 (for p_R = 200 MeV).
- The NNLO counterterm contribution ε₁_(CT)^(2) and the remaining NNLO piece ε̃₁^(2) are typically more than an order of magnitude larger than the NLO prediction, the full NNLO prediction, and the data-extracted value.
- The deuteron binding energy of 2.22 MeV gives γ = √(MB) = 45.7 MeV.
- The FMS global fit used ζ₁ = 0.2345, ζ₂ = -0.1038, and ζ₆ = 0.385.
- A power counting estimate with Λ ∼ 300 MeV gives ε₁ ∼ 25°, more than an order of magnitude larger than the observed value of ∼ 2°.

## Context

This work extends the NNLO calculation of ε₁ by Fleming, Mehan, and Stewart (FMS), who found good agreement with the Nijmegen partial wave analysis and concluded this supported perturbative pions in Q counting. The authors show that this agreement is misleading because it depends sensitively on the choice of renormalization condition, and that the large cancellations between NNLO terms indicate poor convergence. The result is consistent with earlier work by Kaplan and Steele (KS) using a toy potential with one-pion-exchange, which showed no sign of convergence at NNLO for S-wave scattering, and with the failure of NLO Q counting to predict the shape function S introduced by Cohen and Hansen. The authors argue that the unnaturally small size of ε₁ (∼ 2° versus a naive estimate of ∼ 25°) means that even if Q counting converges, one would need to work to order (Q/Λ)^5 or (Q/Λ)^7 to achieve 20% accuracy, making such calculations intractable.
