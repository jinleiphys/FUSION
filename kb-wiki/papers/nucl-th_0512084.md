# Back-to-Back Correlations for Finite Expanding Fireballs

> Back-to-Back Correlations of particle-antiparticle pairs are related to the in-medium mass-modification and squeezing of the quanta involved. They are predicted to appear when hot and dense hadronic matter is formed in high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions. The survival and magnitude of the Back-to-Back Correlations of boson-antiboson pairs generated by in-medium mass modifications are studied here in the case of a thermalized, finite-sized, spherically symmetric expanding medium. We show that the BBC signal indeed survives the finite-time emission, as well as the expansion and flow effects, with sufficient intensity to be observed at RHIC.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Back-to-Back Correlation (BBC) signal from boson-antiboson pairs, generated by in-medium mass modifications, survives finite-time emission and expansion/flow effects with sufficient intensity to be observed at RHIC, but only under the approximation that the squeezing parameter is approximately independent of position (valid for small mass shifts where \((m_* - m)/m \ll 1\)).

## Method

The authors generalize previous studies of BBC from infinite, static media to a finite, spherically symmetric, non-relativistically expanding fireball with a Gaussian density profile and constant freeze-out temperature. They model the system using a Bogoliubov-Valatin transformation between in-medium and asymptotic quanta, evaluate the squeezed correlation amplitudes \(G_c\) and \(G_s\) via Fourier transforms of Gaussian emission functions, and compute the BBC correlation function \(C_2(\mathbf{k}, -\mathbf{k})\) for \(\phi\) meson pairs under two scenarios: mass shift occurring over the entire fireball volume, and mass shift occurring only in a sub-volume.

## Key numbers

* For a fireball with radius \(R = 7\) fm/c, temperature \(T = 140\) MeV, and finite emission time \(\Delta t = 2\) fm/c, the BBC correlation \(C^V_{BBC}\) is higher with radial flow \(\langle u \rangle = 0.5\) than with no flow for momenta \(|\mathbf{k}| \lesssim 1000\) MeV/c, but the no-flow case surpasses it for \(|\mathbf{k}| \gtrsim 1000\) MeV/c.
* In the large momentum limit, the BBC correlation reduces to \(C(k,-k) = 1 + |c_0/s_0|^2\), which has no upper limit and diverges for small but non-vanishing squeezing where \(|c_0| \rightarrow 1\) and \(|s_0| \rightarrow 0\).
* For large in-medium mass modifications and large momenta, the BBC strength approaches \(C(k,-k) \rightarrow 2\), similar to the HBT effect, when \(|c_0|, |s_0| \rightarrow \infty\) (since \(|c_0|/|s_0| \rightarrow 1\)).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Asakawa and Csorgo (1999) and others that were restricted to infinite, static homogeneous media, by incorporating finite-size effects and hydrodynamic expansion using the non-relativistic hydrodynamical model of Csorgo (1996). The results enable experimental searches for BBC signals at RHIC as an independent probe of in-medium hadron mass modifications, complementing information from dilepton yields and spectra. The paper also notes that the formalism can be generalized to cylindrically and ellipsoidally symmetric expansions, as well as relativistic expansions with inhomogeneous temperature profiles.
