# Examination of level density prescriptions in the interpretation of high energy gamma-ray spectra

> High energy $γ$-ray spectra measured by our group involving the compound nuclei (CN) $^{63}$Cu at excitation energy ($E^*$) $\sim$ 36 MeV with average angular momentum ($J$) = 12 - 17 $\hbar$, $^{97}$Tc at $E^* \sim$ 29 - 50 MeV with $J$ = 12 - 14 $\hbar$, $^{113}$Sb at $E^*$ = 109 MeV and 121 MeV with $J$ = 49 - 59 $\hbar$ and $^{201}$Tl at $E^*$ = 39.5, 47.5 MeV with $J$ = 18 - 24 $\hbar$ have been analyzed utilizing the level density prescriptions of (i)Ignatyuk, Smirenkin and Tishin (IST), (ii)Budtz-Jorgensen and Knitter (BJK), and (iii) Kataria, Ramamurthy and Kapoor (KRK). These three prescriptions have been tested for correct statistical model description of high energy $γ$-rays in the light of extracting the giant dipole resonance (GDR) parameters at low excitation energy and spin where shell effects might play an important role as well as at high excitation energy where shell effects have melted. Interestingly, only the IST level density prescription could explain the high energy $γ$-ray spectra with reasonable GDR parameters for all the four nuclei.

## Key claim

The IST level density prescription is the only one among the three tested (IST, KRK, BJK) that can consistently explain high-energy gamma-ray spectra with GDR centroid energies in agreement with existing systematics across all four compound nuclei, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific excitation energy and angular momentum ranges studied (29.3-121 MeV and 12-59 hbar) and on the nuclei being non-fissioning with spherical ground states.

## Method

High-energy gamma-ray spectra (4-32 MeV) from the decay of compound nuclei 63Cu, 97Tc, 113Sb, and 201Tl, populated via alpha and neon beams from the K-130 cyclotron at Variable Energy Cyclotron Centre, Kolkata, were measured using the LAMBDA array (49 detectors) and a 50-element low-energy gamma multiplicity detector. The spectra were fitted with CASCADE statistical model calculations using three level density prescriptions (IST, KRK, BJK), with GDR parameters (centroid energy, strength, width) extracted by chi-squared minimization over E_gamma = 10-20 MeV, while the bremsstrahlung slope parameter was fixed from systematics and the inverse level density parameter for IST was determined from neutron evaporation data where available.

## Key numbers

* For 63Cu at E* = 36 MeV and J = 12-17 hbar, IST gave E_GDR = 16.7-16.9 MeV (close to systematics of 17.0 MeV), while KRK gave E_GDR = 17.9 MeV (5% larger than systematics).
* For 97Tc at E* = 29.3-50.4 MeV and J = 12-14 hbar, IST gave E_GDR = 15.6-16.4 MeV (systematics: 15.6 MeV), KRK gave 17.0-17.5 MeV (13% larger), and BJK gave 15.0-16.8 MeV.
* For 113Sb at E* = 109-121 MeV and J = 49-59 hbar, IST gave E_GDR = 15.5 MeV (systematics: 15.1 MeV), KRK gave 17.0-17.3 MeV (12% larger), and BJK gave 14.0 MeV.
* For 201Tl at E* = 39.5-47.5 MeV and J = 18-24 hbar, IST gave E_GDR = 13.5-13.9 MeV (systematics: 13.4 MeV), while KRK gave 14.4-14.7 MeV (10% larger).
* The IST asymptotic level density parameter tilde{a} for 97Tc was taken as A/8.0, A/9.7, A/9.0, and A/9.2 MeV^-1 at E_lab = 28, 35, 42, and 50 MeV, respectively, extracted from neutron evaporation data.

## Context

This work extends the comparative study of Dioszegi et al. (2000), who tested Dilg, IST, and BJK level densities for A = 110-130 at E* = 58-62 MeV and J = 16.9-20.9 hbar, by testing KRK and BJK prescriptions for the first time on high-energy gamma-ray spectra across a wider range of excitation energies (29.3-121 MeV) and angular momenta (12-59 hbar), including lower energies where shell effects are important. The results enable reliable extraction of GDR parameters using the IST prescription for future studies of nuclear symmetry energy and damping mechanisms, while cautioning against using KRK or BJK prescriptions which systematically shift GDR centroid energies.
