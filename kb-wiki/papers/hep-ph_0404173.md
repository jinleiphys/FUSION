# Exotic Baryons and Monopole Excitations in a Chiral Soliton Model

> We compute the spectra of exotic pentaquarks and monopole excitations of the low--lying ${1/2}^+$ and ${3/2}^+$ baryons in a chiral soliton model. Once the low--lying baryon properties are fit, the other states are predicted without any more adjustable parameters. This approach naturally leads to a scenario in which the mass spectrum of the next to lowest--lying $J^π={1/2}^+$ states is fairly well approximated by the ideal mixing pattern of the $\mathbf{8}\oplus\bar{\mathbf{10}}$ representation of flavor SU(3). We compare our results to predictions obtained in other pictures for pentaquarks and speculate about the spin--parity assignment for $Ξ(1690)$ and $Ξ(1950)$

## Key claim

The chiral soliton model predicts the masses of exotic pentaquarks and monopole excitations without any adjustable parameters once low-lying baryon properties are fit, but the agreement with data is only within about 30-50 MeV and the model's predictions for the Roper resonance are somewhat low compared to the Breit-Wigner mass (though consistent with the pole position estimate).

## Method

The Skyrme model extended by a scalar glueball field was used to construct a static soliton solution with hedgehog structure. Collective coordinates for flavor rotations (A(t) in SU(3)) and monopole vibrations (x(t) for spatial extension) were quantized canonically, yielding a Hamiltonian that was diagonalized exactly in a basis of SU(3) representations (octet, antidecuplet, 27-plet, and higher) combined with radial excitation states up to 4 GeV above the ground states.

## Key numbers

- Predicted mass of Θ⁺ pentaquark: 1.57 GeV (e=5.0) and 1.59 GeV (e=5.5), compared to experimental 1.537±0.010 GeV
- Predicted mass of Ξ₃/₂ pentaquark: 1.89 GeV (e=5.0) and 1.91 GeV (e=5.5), compared to experimental 1.862±0.002 GeV
- Mass difference between Ξ₃/₂ and Θ⁺ reproduced within 10 MeV of experiment
- Roper resonance N(1440) predicted at 413 MeV (e=5.0) and 445 MeV (e=5.5) above nucleon mass, compared to Breit-Wigner 501 MeV and pole position 426 MeV
- Ground state octet baryon mass differences: Λ at 175/173 MeV (e=5.0/5.5) vs 177 MeV expt; Σ at 284/284 MeV vs 254 MeV expt; Ξ at 382/380 MeV vs 379 MeV expt
- Ground state decuplet baryon mass differences: Δ at 258/276 MeV vs 293 MeV expt; Σ* at 445/460 MeV vs 446 MeV expt; Ξ* at 604/617 MeV vs 591 MeV expt; Ω at 730/745 MeV vs 733 MeV expt
- First excited nucleon state (|N,1⟩) at 413/445 MeV above nucleon; first excited Δ at 640/680 MeV vs 661 MeV expt

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral soliton model studies by Schvellinger (1991) and Weigel (1998) that included monopole excitations, and contrasts with model-independent approaches (Diakonov 1997, Ellis 2004, Walliser 2003) that treat operator coefficients as adjustable parameters. The results support the 8⊕10̅ mixing scenario proposed by Jaffe (2003) and Diakonov (2003) for pentaquarks, but provide a dynamical origin for the additional octet as a monopole excitation. The model enables predictions for the spin-parity assignment of Ξ(1690) and Ξ(1950), suggesting Ξ(1950) is J^π=1/2^+ while Ξ(1690) likely belongs to a different (D-wave) multiplet.
