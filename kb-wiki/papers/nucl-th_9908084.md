# Charge conjugation invariance of the Spectator Equations

> In response to recent critcism, we show how to define the spectator equations for negative energies so that charge conjugation invariance is preserved. The result, which emerges naturally from the application of spectator principles to systems of particles with negative energies, is to replace all factors of the external energies $W_i$ by $\sqrt{W^2_i}$, insuring that the amplitudes are independent of the sign of the energies $W_i$.

## Key claim

The spectator equations satisfy charge conjugation invariance exactly, provided that the on-shell internal particle(s) are on their positive energy mass-shell when the external energies are positive, and on their negative energy mass-shell when the external energies are negative; this requirement is equivalent to replacing all external energies \(W_i\) by \(|W_i| = \sqrt{W_i^2}\).

## Method

The author analyzes the one-loop bubble and crossed-bubble self-energy diagrams in a \(\lambda \phi^2 \Psi^\dagger \Psi\) theory, examining the pole structure in the complex \(k_0\) plane for both positive and negative external total energy \(W\). He shows that for \(W>0\) the spectator approximation picks the positive-energy nucleon pole, while for \(W<0\) the same physical argument picks the negative-energy nucleon pole, and demonstrates that the resulting scalar functions \(A(W)\) and \(B(W)\) satisfy the charge conjugation transformation property \(C\Sigma(P)C^{-1} = \Sigma^T(-P)\). Numerical examples in 1+1 dimensions are presented to illustrate the cancellation between the bubble and crossed-bubble contributions and the failure of the naive extension to negative energies proposed by Pascalutsa and Tjon.

## Key numbers

- The scalar function \(B_S(W)\) for the spectator approximation in 1+1 dimensions is given by \(B_S(W) = -\frac{m\lambda^2}{4\pi} \int \frac{dk}{E(k)[\mu^2 - m^2 - W^2 + 2E(k)W]}\), with \(M = m/\mu = 7\) and \(\lambda^2/(2\pi\mu^2) = 3\) used in the numerical example.
- The numerical example shows that the sum of the exact bubble and crossed-bubble diagrams (heavy dotted line) is almost identical to the positive-energy nucleon pole contribution \(B_S(W)\) for \(W>0\).
- For a \(\Psi^\dagger \gamma_5 \Psi \phi\) theory with no exchange term, the positive-energy meson pole contribution to the direct bubble is shown to agree very well with the exact result (solid and dotted lines in Fig. 4), while using the positive-energy pole for both \(W>0\) and \(W<0\) violates C invariance significantly.

## Context

This paper responds to criticism by Pascalutsa and Tjon (1999) that the spectator formalism violates charge conjugation invariance, and shows that their proposed extension to negative energies does indeed violate C, but a more natural extension does not. The work extends the spectator theory originally developed by Gross (1982) to negative energies for the first time, and clarifies that the spectator approximation with the heavy particle on-shell should be used when exchange forces are present, while the light-particle on-shell version (or the Bethe-Salpeter equation) should be used when there are no exchange forces. This enables consistent application of the spectator formalism to systems involving both positive and negative energies, such as \(q\bar{q}\) systems, though the author notes that a four-channel spectator equation (Gross and Milana, 1991) is required for accurate treatment near \(W=0\).
