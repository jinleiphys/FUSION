# Nucleon to $Δ$ axial and pseudoscalar transition form factors

> A symmetry-preserving continuum approach to the calculation of baryon properties in relativistic quantum field theory is used to predict all form factors associated with nucleon-to-$Δ$ axial and pseudoscalar transition currents, thereby unifying them with many additional properties of these and other baryons. The new parameter-free predictions can serve as credible benchmarks for use in analysing existing and anticipated data from worldwide efforts focused on elucidation of $ν$ properties.

## Key claim

Using a symmetry-preserving continuum approach to baryon properties in relativistic quantum field theory, the authors deliver parameter-free predictions for all form factors associated with nucleon-to-Δ axial and pseudoscalar transition currents, unifying them with other baryon properties; the predictions are intended as credible benchmarks for neutrino experiments, but the authors note that no material improvement may be envisaged before the same observables can be calculated using explicit three-body Faddeev equation approaches or lattice QCD simulations.

## Method

The authors solve a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation that describes baryon structure via dressed-quark and diquark correlations, using a framework that preserves chiral symmetry and its breaking pattern. The transition form factors are obtained from nucleon elastic weak form factor expressions by replacing all inputs connected with the final state by those for the Δ-baryon, with the interaction current included. The diquark masses are fixed at m_{[ud]} = 0.80 GeV for isoscalar-scalar diquarks and m_{{uu}} = m_{{ud}} = m_{{dd}} = 0.89 GeV for isovector-axialvector diquarks, with uncertainties estimated from ±5% variations in these masses.

## Key numbers

- C^A_5(0) = 1.16^{+0.09}_{-0.03}, which is 0.94(5) times the nucleon axial coupling g_A predicted by the same framework
- The axial mass for C^A_5 in a dipole representation is 0.95(3) times the analogous scale in the nucleon axial form factor G_A
- C^A_3(0) = 0.26^{+0.17}_{-0.04}
- C^A_4(0) = -0.66^{+0.03}_{-0.10}
- The off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation is satisfied: G_{πNΔ}(0)/m_N = 25.2^{+1.9}_{-0.7} compared with 2C^A_5(0)/f_π = 25.1^{+1.9}_{-0.6}
- The parity-violating asymmetry at Q^2=0 is R_PV(0) = 0.45(4)
- The πNΔ form factor has a zero at x = 0.84(6), where x = Q^2/[(m_N + m_Δ)/2]^2
- The πNΔ radius is r_{πNΔ} = 1.4(1) r_{πNN}

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous unified predictions for nucleon and Δ-baryon elastic weak-interaction form factors (Chen 2021, ChenChen 2022, Yin 2023) to the inelastic N→Δ transition, which is crucial for interpreting neutrino oscillation experiments in the few-GeV range. The only prior related calculations were lattice QCD results from Alexandrou (2007, 2010) using pion masses m_π ≥ 297 MeV, which the authors find lie lower at x≈0 and are harder than their predictions, likely due to unphysically large pion masses. The predictions enable improved analysis of existing and anticipated data from neutrino experiments such as DUNE and T2K, and can serve as benchmarks for future lattice QCD calculations at the physical pion mass.
