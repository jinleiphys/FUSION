# Jet-based measurements of Sivers and Collins asymmetries at the future Electron-Ion Collider

> We present predictions and projections for hadron-in-jet measurements and electron-jet azimuthal correlations at the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC). These observables directly probe the three-dimensional (3D) structure of hadrons, in particular, the quark transversity and Sivers parton distributions and the Collins fragmentation functions. We explore the feasibility of these experimental measurements by detector simulations and discuss detector requirements. We conclude that jet observables have the potential to enhance the 3D imaging EIC program.

## Key claim

Jet-based measurements at the future Electron-Ion Collider can separate transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions from fragmentation functions in a single measurement, but the feasibility depends on achieving sufficient detector resolution and acceptance, particularly for large-radius jets in forward pseudorapidity regions.

## Method

The authors performed leading-order perturbative QCD calculations at next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL') accuracy for the Sivers asymmetry via electron-jet azimuthal correlations and the Collins asymmetry via hadron-in-jet measurements, using the anti-kT jet algorithm with radius R=1. They generated neutral-current deep-inelastic scattering events with Pythia8 using DIRE dipole shower and Lund string hadronization, with beam energies of 10 GeV electrons and 275 GeV protons at center-of-mass energy 105 GeV. Fast detector simulations were performed using the Delphes package with parametrized momentum and energy resolutions following baseline EIC general-purpose detector specifications.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy: sqrt(s) = 105 GeV from 10 GeV electron and 275 GeV proton beams
- Integrated luminosity assumed: 100 fb^-1, corresponding to about one year at 10^34 cm^-2 s^-1
- Event selection: Q^2 > 25 GeV^2 and 0.1 < y < 0.85
- Minimum transverse momentum cut: 5 GeV for both electron and jet
- Jet radius parameter: R = 1.0
- Average proton-beam polarization assumed: 70%
- Overall efficiency assumed: 50% for trigger, data quality, and reconstruction
- Sea-quark region probed at x approximately 0.05 with pT approximately 7 GeV; valence region x > 0.1 reached with pT approximately 15 GeV; x > 0.3 probed with pT > 25 GeV
- Projected statistical uncertainties for Sivers asymmetry are much smaller than current theoretical uncertainties from the extraction of Echevarria (2014)
- Projected statistical uncertainties for Collins asymmetry are much smaller than uncertainties from the extraction of Kang (2015)

## Context

This work extends previous EIC projection studies that focused on semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering observables (Anselmino 2011, Accardi 2012) by providing the first predictions for hadron-in-jet asymmetries at the EIC. The jet-based approach complements SIDIS measurements by separating TMD PDFs from TMD FFs, which is not possible in SIDIS alone where the observed hadron transverse momentum receives contributions from both. The measurements will enable tests of TMD universality and evolution, and can help confirm the sign-change of the Sivers function between SIDIS and Drell-Yan reactions predicted by Brodsky (2002) and Collins (2002).
