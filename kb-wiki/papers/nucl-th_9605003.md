# Virtual Compton scattering off nuclei in the $Δ$-resonance region

> Virtual Compton scattering in the $Δ$-resonance region is considered in the case of a target nucleus. The discussion involves generalized polarizabilities and is developed for zero-spin nuclei, focusing on the new information coming from virtual Compton scattering in comparison with real Compton scattering.

## Key claim

Virtual Compton scattering (VCS) off zero-spin nuclei in the Δ-resonance region can access new information through the scalar polarizability \(P_0(EL,CL;q'^\mu,q^\mu)\), which involves the coupling between the longitudinal virtual photon and the nuclear transition operator; however, within the Δ-hole model used, only small contributions to the longitudinal (\(W_{\rm L}\)) and longitudinal-transverse (\(W_{\rm LT}\)) structure functions arise from the resonant \(E2\) and \(C2\) multipoles, and the \(W_{\rm LT}\) structure function is predicted to be rather small and peaked in the forward hemisphere.

## Method

The authors generalize the formalism of nuclear polarizabilities from real Compton scattering to VCS by including the Coulomb (longitudinal) multipole in the multipole expansion of the absorbed virtual photon. They apply a modified Δ-hole model with one-body interaction mechanisms between the incident photon and single nucleons, deriving the nuclear polarizabilities from nucleon polarizability operators. For zero-spin nuclei, they compute the four response functions (\(W_{\rm L}\), \(W_{\rm T}\), \(W_{\rm LT}\), \(W_{\rm TT}\)) and evaluate them for \(^4\)He across the Δ-resonance region, including resonant \(M1\), \(E2\), and \(C2\) multipoles for the \(N\Delta\) transition and background contributions from the Kroll-Ruderman and seagull terms.

## Key numbers

- The structure function \(W_{\rm LT}\) for \(^4\)He is predicted to be "rather small and peaked in the forward hemisphere" (no explicit numerical value given for the magnitude).
- The paper references a recent experiment on \(^4\)He in the Δ-resonance region (Schaerf) that achieved separation of \(W_{\rm T}\) and \(W_{\rm TT}\), and precise data obtained at Mainz with tagged photons (Selke).
- The analysis within the Δ-hole model (Pasquini) shows "rather satisfactory agreement between theory and data under resonance conditions, while below the Δ-resonance energy discrepancies are found at backward angles."

## Context

This work extends the formalism of nuclear polarizabilities from real Compton scattering (Pasquini, 1995) to virtual Compton scattering, following the approach developed for nucleon targets (Guichon, 1995; Audit, 1995). It enables the extraction of new information about nuclear structure through the longitudinal response \(W_{\rm L}\) and the longitudinal-transverse interference \(W_{\rm LT}\), which are not accessible in real Compton scattering. The authors note that the separation of \(W_{\rm LT}\) requires high-precision experiments to overcome the overwhelming photon bremsstrahlung contribution, and that the situation is similar to studying the \(E2/M1\) ratio for Δ excitation in pion photoproduction (Laget, 1995).
