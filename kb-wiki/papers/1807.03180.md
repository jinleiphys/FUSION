# Heavy-flavor flow-harmonics in high-energy nuclear collisions: time-development and eccentricity fluctuations

> We study the development of heavy-flavor flow harmonics in high-energy nuclear collisions. The elliptic and triangular flow of heavy-flavor hadrons, arising from the finite impact parameter of the two nuclei and from event-by-event fluctuations of the initial geometry, is analyzed in detail, considering the contribution from particles decoupling from the fireball at various times. We also study the dependence of the flow harmonics on the event-shape fluctuations, considering events belonging to the same centrality class but characterized by very different eccentricities (or vice-versa).

## Key claim

Heavy-flavor flow harmonics in high-energy nuclear collisions arise from a combination of the finite impact parameter and event-by-event fluctuations of the initial geometry, but the final elliptic flow signal results from the interplay of different (opposite-sign) effects: at the very beginning it is dominated by the anisotropic escape probability and only later the interaction with the medium plays a role.

## Method

The authors generated several thousands of Glauber Monte Carlo initial conditions, dividing them into centrality classes according to the number of nucleon-nucleon collisions. For each centrality class they built an average initial condition summing all events rotated to align the reference angle along the x-axis, then calculated the hydrodynamic evolution of the fireball using the ECHO-QGP code and simulated heavy quark propagation through the Langevin equation implemented in the POWLANG setup, which includes in-medium hadronization modeling.

## Key numbers

* For the 30-50% centrality class, the subsample of most eccentric events had epsilon_2 >= 0.58 and the least eccentric events had epsilon_2 <= 0.50, based on Glauber-MC simulations of the initial state
* The analysis was performed for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV
* Results for D-meson v_2 and v_3 in non-central Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC were compared to ALICE and CMS data

## Context

This work extends the POWLANG transport setup (Beraudo 2017) to study heavy-flavor flow harmonics beyond the standard nuclear modification factor R_AA and elliptic flow v_2 in non-central collisions. It builds on earlier studies of light hadron event-shape engineering (Adam 2015) which found that final v_2 largely reflects fluctuations in initial geometry. The analysis enables future studies of how heavy quarks, with their longer relaxation times compared to light partons, respond differently to initial geometry depending on medium density.
