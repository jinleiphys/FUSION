# Virtual Photons Shed Light on the Early Temperature of Dense QCD Matter

> Dileptons produced during heavy-ion collisions represent a unique probe of the QCD phase diagram, and convey information about the state of the strongly interacting system at the moment their preceding off-shell photon is created. In this study, we compute thermal dilepton yields from Au+Au collisions performed at different beam energies, employing a (3+1)-dimensional dynamic framework combined with emission rates accurate at next-to-leading order in perturbation theory and which include baryon chemical potential dependencies. By comparing the effective temperature extracted from the thermal dilepton invariant mass spectrum with the average temperature of the fluid, we offer a robust quantitative validation of dileptons as effective probe of the early quark-gluon plasma stage.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a quantitative linear relationship between the effective temperature extracted from thermal dilepton spectra in the intermediate invariant mass region (IMR) and the initial average temperature of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in Au+Au collisions, but the extracted effective temperature is consistently above the mean hydrodynamic temperature throughout the evolution and the relationship is validated only for the specific (3+1)-dimensional dissipative hydrodynamic framework used.

## Method

The authors compute thermal dilepton yields from Au+Au collisions at beam energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV using a (3+1)-dimensional dissipative hydrodynamic framework with shear viscosity and baryon diffusion, combined with dilepton emission rates at next-to-leading order (NLO) in perturbative QCD that include baryon chemical potential dependencies. The emission rates interpolate between strict perturbative calculations for large invariant mass and hard thermal loop (HTL) resummation with Landau-Pomeranchuck-Migdal (LPM) effects for small invariant mass, using a fixed QCD coupling of alpha_s = 0.3. The effective temperature is extracted from the slope of the invariant mass spectrum in the IMR (1 GeV to 3 GeV) and compared with the average hydrodynamic temperatures of the fluid cells.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy range: sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 to 200 GeV for Au+Au collisions
- Invariant mass region for temperature extraction: 1 GeV to 3 GeV
- Global fit parameters for the linear relation between initial mean temperature and effective temperature: kappa = 1.55 +/- 0.02, c = -(9.3 +/- 0.3) x 10^(-2) GeV
- Fixed QCD coupling: alpha_s = 0.3
- Estimated theoretical uncertainty in the perturbative scheme for the IMR: below approximately 10%
- The final freeze-out temperature remains relatively stable down to sqrt(s_NN) = 19 GeV but experiences a sudden drop at 7.7 GeV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of dileptons as thermometers for the QGP, building on prior studies by Rapp (2013), Vujanovic (2013, 2016) that established the QGP source dominance for M > 1.1 GeV, and by Kajantie (1981) and Hwa (1985) who anticipated the relationship between dilepton spectra and initial temperatures. The results complement findings by Giacalone (2019) on correlations between initial state energy and particle multiplicities, and by Du (2022) on constraining initial baryon density using directed flows. The linear relationship between effective temperature and initial mean temperature provides a means to extract early QGP temperatures from dilepton spectra across multiple beam energies and centrality classes, though the authors note that future work should examine the influence of the pre-hydrodynamic stage on dilepton spectra.
