# NA61/SHINE results on search for critical point

> The NA61/SHINE experiment at the CERN SPS is a multipurpose fixed-target spectrometer for charged and neutral hadron measurements. Its research program includes studies of strong interactions as well as reference measurements for neutrino and cosmic-ray physics. One major goal of its strong interaction program is to determine the existence and pinpoint the location of the QCD critical point, an object of both experimental and theoretical studies. This contribution will summarize the current status of NA61/SHINE critical point searches in nucleus-nucleus collisions, in the collision energy range $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5-17$~GeV. The review includes studies of fluctuations of net-electric charge, femtoscopy analysis of $π-π$ pairs, as well as intermittency of protons and negatively charged hadrons. No clear indication of the critical point has been observed so far. Finally, we report on the development of novel methods aimed at solving the long-standing problem of bin-by-bin correlations in experimental intermittency analysis, and for a more accurate handling of systematics and uncertainties.

## Key claim

No clear indication of the QCD critical point has been observed so far in the NA61/SHINE scan of collision energies \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5-17\) GeV and various system sizes, but the results point to the crucial importance of properly understanding non-critical baseline effects before any definitive conclusion can be drawn.

## Method

The NA61/SHINE experiment performed a two-dimensional scan in collision energy (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5-17\) GeV) and system size (p+p, p+Pb, Be+Be, O+O, Ar+Sc, Xe+La, Pb+Pb) to search for signatures of the QCD critical point. Three complementary analyses were conducted: fluctuations of net-electric charge via cumulant ratios up to fourth order, femtoscopy of \(\pi-\pi\) pairs using Levy-distributed source fits to extract the Levy exponent \(\alpha\), and intermittency analysis of protons and negatively charged hadrons using scaled factorial moments in transverse momentum space.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy range scanned: \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5-17\) GeV
- For net-charge fluctuations in central Ar+Sc collisions, cumulant ratios \(\kappa_3/\kappa_1\) and \(\kappa_4/\kappa_2\) show a hint of non-monotonic behavior at mid-SPS energies, but with large statistical uncertainties
- For femtoscopy, the Levy exponent \(\alpha\) fitted to \(\pi^+\pi^+\) and \(\pi^-\pi^-\) pairs in Ar+Sc collisions is close to Gaussian (\(\alpha = 2\)), far from the critical point predicted value of \(\alpha = 0.5\)
- For intermittency, proton multiplicity moments \(\Delta F_2(M)_c\) were studied in Ar+Sc collisions across beam momenta \(13A-150A\) GeV/\(c\) (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5-17\) GeV) with transverse momentum bins ranging from \(1^2 \leq M^2 \leq 150^2\) in a window \(-1.5 \leq p_{x,y} \leq 1.5\) GeV/\(c\) at mid-rapidity; no intermittency signal was observed
- For negatively charged hadrons in Xe+La collisions at \(150A\) GeV/\(c\) (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 16.8\) GeV), no intermittency effect remained after applying cumulative transform or short-range correlation cuts

## Context

This work extends the critical point search program of the NA49 experiment (the predecessor of NA61/SHINE) and provides systematic comparisons with the EPOS1.99 model for net-charge fluctuations. The intermittency analysis of negatively charged hadrons in Xe+La collisions serves as a check of published STAR Collaboration intermittency results for all charged hadrons in Au+Au collisions (STAR, 2023). The development of a Principal Component Analysis method to handle bin-by-bin correlations in intermittency analysis enables more reliable extraction of intermittency indices from correlated data, which can be applied by NA61/SHINE and other high-energy physics experiments.
