# Binding of eta_c meson with light nucleus

> Binding energy of eta_c with ^4 He has been calculated on the basis of Yukawa type potential. Final Rank Approximation (FRA) has been used for microscopical treatment of the initial 5-body problem. The results are compared with the results of the folding model and variational calculations.

## Key claim

The binding energy of the $\eta_c$ meson with $^4$He is calculated using a Yukawa-type meson-nucleon potential, and the Finite Rank Approximation (FRA) yields a binding energy of -7.5 MeV for a specific set of potential parameters, which is deeper than the folding model result of -5 MeV for the same parameters, but far shallower than a previous phenomenological estimate of -143 MeV.

## Method

The five-body problem (one $\eta_c$ meson plus four nucleons) is treated microscopically using the Finite Rank Approximation (FRA), where the nuclear subsystem Hamiltonian $H_A$ is approximated by a rank-one operator projecting onto the ground state of $^4$He. The meson-nucleon interaction is taken as an attractive Yukawa potential $V_{mN}(r) = -V_0 e^{-\mu r}/r$ with parameters $V_0$ and $\mu$ varied in the ranges 0.4–0.6 (dimensionless) and 0.4–0.6 GeV respectively. The resulting Schrödinger equation is reduced to a system of one-dimensional differential equations by expanding the total wavefunction in a set of orthonormal auxiliary functions built from Gaussians, and the binding energy is extracted from the solution.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of $\eta_c$–$^4$He from the present FRA approach: $E_{\rm present} = -7.5$ MeV for $V_0 = 0.6$, $\mu = 0.6$ GeV.
- Binding energy from the folding model: $E_{\rm fold} = -5$ MeV for the same parameters $V_0 = 0.6$, $\mu = 0.6$ GeV.
- Previous phenomenological estimate from Belyaev et al. (2005, Ref. [1]): $E_{\rm phen} = -143$ MeV, which the authors consider not fully adequate.
- The Yukawa potential parameters are varied in the intervals $0.4 \le V_0 \le 0.6$ and $0.4 \le \mu \le 0.6$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier folding-model calculations of $\eta_c$ binding with light nuclei (A=3,4) reported in Belyaev et al. (2005, Ref. [2]) by introducing a more elaborate microscopic treatment via the Finite Rank Approximation, which includes meson rescattering effects neglected in the folding model. The results show that the folding model underestimates the binding energy by about 2.5 MeV for the chosen parameters, and the present method converges rapidly with the number of auxiliary functions. This approach enables more reliable predictions for charmonium-nucleus bound states and may help constrain the role of gluonic degrees of freedom in meson-nucleon interactions.
