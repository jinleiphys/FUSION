# Momentum dependence of the symmetry potential and nuclear reactions induced by neutron-rich nuclei at RIA

> Effects of the momentum-dependence of the symmetry potential in nuclear reactions induced by neutron-rich nuclei at RIA energies are studied using an isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model. It is found that symmetry potentials with and without the momentum-dependence but corresponding to the same density-dependent symmetry energy $E_{sym}(ρ)$ lead to significantly different predictions on several $E_{sym}(ρ)$-sensitive experimental observables. The momentum-dependence of the symmetry potential is thus critically important for investigating accurately the equation of state (${\rm EOS}$) and novel properties of dense neutron-rich matter at RIA.

# Momentum dependence of the symmetry potential and nuclear reactions induced by neutron-rich nuclei at RIA

## Key claim
Symmetry potentials with and without momentum-dependence but corresponding to the same density-dependent symmetry energy $E_{sym}(\rho)$ lead to significantly different predictions on several $E_{sym}(\rho)$-sensitive experimental observables; the momentum-dependence of the symmetry potential is critically important for accurately investigating the equation of state of dense neutron-rich matter at RIA energies.

## Method
The authors implemented a single nucleon potential parameterized from Gogny Hartree-Fock and Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations (the MDI interaction) into an isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model. They compared results from this full momentum-dependent potential with results from a constructed potential that has the same momentum-dependent isoscalar part but a momentum-independent symmetry potential giving the same $E_{sym}(\rho)$, using the MDI(1) and MDI(0) parameter sets to vary the density dependence of the symmetry energy. Simulations were performed for $^{132}Sn+^{124}Sn$ reactions at a beam energy of 400 MeV/nucleon and an impact parameter of 5 fm.

## Key numbers
- The symmetry energy at normal nuclear matter density $\rho_0=0.16/fm^3$ is set to 30 MeV.
- The compressibility of symmetric nuclear matter $K_0$ is 211 MeV for the MDI interaction and 215 MeV for the MDYI interaction.
- Interaction strength parameters: $C_{unlike}=-103.4$ MeV, $C_{like}=-11.7$ MeV.
- The slope of the neutron-proton differential flow $F^x_{n-p}(y)$ at y=0 changes from -1.9 to 21.4 MeV/c using the momentum-dependent potential when changing from MDI(1) to MDI(0), compared to a change from 26 to 39 MeV/c with the momentum-independent symmetry potentials.

## Context
This work extends prior isospin-dependent transport model studies that identified observables such as the neutron/proton ratio (Li 1997, Tani 2001, Bar 2002), neutron-proton differential flow (Li 2000, Gre 2003), and neutron-proton correlation functions (Chen 2003) as probes of the symmetry energy, but which had never included momentum-dependence of the symmetry potential. The momentum-dependence of the isoscalar potential was already well-known (Gale 1990, Pan 1993, Zhang 1994), but the momentum-dependence of the symmetry potential was only recently parameterized in a form usable in transport models (Das 2003). This work enables more accurate extraction of the density-dependent symmetry energy from future RIA experiments by showing that momentum-dependence must be included and that observables are actually more sensitive to $E_{sym}(\rho)$ variations when momentum-dependent symmetry potentials are used.
