# Near-threshold J/Psi production in proton-nucleus collisions

> We study the J/Psi production from nuclei near the kinematic threshold within the collision model, based on the nuclear spectral function, for incoherent primary proton-nucleon charmonium creation processes. The model takes into account the initial proton and final J/Psi absorption, target nucleon binding and Fermi motion, the formation length of J/Psi mesons as well as the effect of their nuclear mean-field potential on these processes. We calculate the A dependences of the absolute and relative (transparency ratio) charmonium yields as well as its excitation function within the different scenarios for the J/PsiN absorption cross section, for the J/Psi formation length and for J/Psi in-medium modification. We demonstrate that the former observables, on the one hand, are not practically affected by the charmonium formation length effects and, on the other hand, they are appreciably sensitive to the genuine J/PsiN absorption cross section at beam energies of interest, which means that these observables can be useful to help determine the above cross section from the comparison of the results of our calculations with the future data from the CBM experiment at FAIR-the upcoming accelerator facility at GSI-Darmstadt, Germany. We also show that the excitation function for J/Psi subthreshold production in pA reactions reveals some sensitivity to adopted its in-medium modification scenarios. Therefore, such observable may be an important tool to get valuable information on the charmonium in-medium properties.

## Key claim
The absolute and relative (transparency ratio) yields of J/Psi mesons in proton-nucleus collisions near threshold are practically unaffected by charmonium formation length effects but are appreciably sensitive to the genuine J/Psi-nucleon absorption cross section; the excitation function for subthreshold production shows sensitivity to the adopted in-medium mass shift scenarios, but only at beam energies where the predicted cross sections are extremely small (0.01–1 pb).

## Method
The authors employ a first-collision model based on the nuclear spectral function, accounting for initial proton and final J/Psi absorption, target nucleon binding and Fermi motion, J/Psi formation length, and the effect of a nuclear mean-field potential. They calculate the A-dependence of absolute yields and transparency ratios for 14 GeV protons on targets from carbon to uranium, using four values of the J/Psi-nucleon absorption cross section (3.5, 7, 14 mb) and two formation length options (0 and 0.8 fm). The excitation function for J/Psi production on carbon and niobium is computed for beam energies from 8 to 14 GeV with four scenarios for the in-medium mass shift (V0 = 0, -50, -100, -150 MeV).

## Key numbers
- Threshold kinetic energy for J/Psi production in pp collisions: 11.3 GeV.
- At 14 GeV beam energy, absolute J/Psi yields for targets heavier than Al are in the range 100–400 pb across the four absorption cross section scenarios.
- The J/Psi formation length at threshold momentum (7.6 GeV/c) is approximately 0.8 fm.
- For the transparency ratio, differences of 20–30% between absorption cross section options appear only for targets heavier than Ca.
- Subthreshold excitation function cross sections (T0 ~ 8–10 GeV) are in the range 0.01–1 pb.
- The ratio of average nucleon density to saturation density is 0.5 for carbon and 0.7 for niobium.

## Context
This work extends the spectral function approach previously applied to photoproduced omega and eta-prime mesons (Paryev, 2014) to the case of J/Psi production in proton-nucleus collisions near threshold. It provides predictions for the upcoming CBM experiment at FAIR, where high-intensity proton beams will enable measurements of these extremely small cross sections. The calculations contradict earlier assumptions that formation length effects dominate near threshold, and instead show that the A-dependence of yields and transparency ratios can directly constrain the genuine J/Psi-nucleon absorption cross section, while the excitation function can probe the in-medium mass shift predicted by Sibirtsev and Voloshin (2005).
