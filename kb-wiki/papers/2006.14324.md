# Jet quenching parameter from a soft wall AdS/QCD model

> We study the effect of chemical potential and nonconformality on the jet quenching parameter in a holographic QCD model with conformal invariance broken by a background dilaton. It turns out that the presence of chemical potential and nonconformality both increase the jet quenching parameter thus enhancing the energy loss, consistently with the findings of the drag force.

## Key claim
The paper claims that both chemical potential and nonconformality increase the jet quenching parameter in a soft wall AdS/QCD model, but this result is derived under the dipole approximation (valid only for small quark-antiquark separation L with LT << 1) and within a specific holographic model that does not solve the Einstein equations consistently.

## Method
The authors compute the jet quenching parameter using the holographic dual of light-like adjoint Wilson loops in the SW_T,μ model, which is an AdS-Reissner-Nordstrom black hole metric multiplied by a warp factor h(r)=exp(c²R⁴/r²) that breaks conformal invariance. They extract the jet quenching parameter from the regulated Nambu-Goto action of a string worldsheet in the light-cone coordinates, taking the low-energy limit (C→0) and expanding to leading order in 1/B. The calculation reproduces the known SYM result when both chemical potential and nonconformality are set to zero.

## Key numbers
- The jet quenching parameter for N=4 SYM at finite temperature is reproduced as: $\hat{q}_{SYM} = \frac{\pi^{3/2}\Gamma(3/4)}{\Gamma(5/4)}\sqrt{\lambda}T^3$
- Typical numerical values of $\hat{q}$ in GeV²/fm are tabulated for various combinations of temperature T, chemical potential μ, and deformation parameter c (all in GeV): at T=0.3 GeV with μ=c=0, $\hat{q}=4.50$ GeV²/fm; at T=0.5 GeV with μ=c=0, $\hat{q}=20.69$ GeV²/fm; at T=0.5 GeV with μ=0.3 GeV and c=0.7 GeV, $\hat{q}=23.10$ GeV²/fm
- The chemical potential is related to black hole charge by $\mu = \sqrt{3}Q r_h / R^2$, with Q constrained to $0 \leq Q \leq \sqrt{2}$
- The temperature is given by $T = (r_h / \pi R^2)(1 - Q^2/2)$
- The deformation parameter c/T is studied in the range $0 \leq c/T \leq 2.5$

## Context
This work extends previous holographic studies of jet quenching in N=4 SYM by Liu et al. (2006) to a nonconformal soft wall model with chemical potential, following the SW_T,μ model introduced by Colangelo et al. (2011) for studying heavy quark free energy and QCD phase diagrams. The results are consistent with earlier findings on the drag force in the same model by Zhang et al. (2020), showing that both chemical potential and nonconformality enhance energy loss for both heavy and light quarks. The theoretical estimates agree with experimental values extracted from RHIC data (5-25 GeV²/fm), and the work suggests that increasing μ and c may lower the allowed temperature range for agreement with experiment.
