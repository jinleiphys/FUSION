# Statistical nature of cluster emission in nuclear liquid-vapour phase coexistence

> The emission of nuclear clusters is investigated within the framework of isospin dependent lattice gas model and classical molecular dynamics model. It is found that the emission of individual cluster which is heavier than proton is almost Poissonian except near the transition temperature at which the system is leaving the liquid-vapor phase coexistence and the thermal scaling is observed by the linear Arrhenius plots which is made from the average multiplicity of each cluster versus the inverse of temperature in the liquid vapor phase coexistence. The slopes of the Arrhenius plots, {\it i.e.} the "emission barriers", are extracted as a function of the mass or charge number and fitted by the formula embodied with the contributions of the surface energy and Coulomb interaction. The good agreements are obtained in comparison with the data for low energy conditional barriers. In addition, the possible influences of the source size, Coulomb interaction and "freeze-out" density and related physical implications are discussed.

## Key claim

The emission of individual clusters heavier than protons is almost Poissonian (i.e., independent and stochastic) except near the transition temperature where the system leaves the liquid-vapor phase coexistence; thermal scaling via linear Arrhenius plots holds only within the coexistence phase, not in the single-phase (gas) regime.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent lattice gas model (I-LGM) and the isospin-dependent classical molecular dynamics model (I-CMD) of Das Gupta and Pan to simulate the disassembly of a 129Xe nucleus (and systems with A=80 and A=274) at a freeze-out density of about 0.38 ρ0. They extracted cluster multiplicity distributions as a function of temperature, tested Poissonian reducibility via the ratio of variance to mean multiplicity, and constructed Arrhenius plots (ln⟨n⟩ vs. 1/T) to extract emission barriers from the slopes in the low-temperature (coexistence) region.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out density chosen as 0.38 ρ0, corresponding to a 7³ cubic lattice for 129Xe, 6³ for A=80, and 9³ for A=274.
- Interaction constants: ε_nn = ε_pp = 0 MeV, ε_pn = -5.33 MeV; the np potential minimum is -5.33 MeV at r0 = 1.842 fm.
- For I-LGM and I-CMD without Coulomb, the emission barrier B for clusters classified by mass number A follows B = c1 × A^(2/3) with c1 = 8.469 MeV; for charge number Z, B = c1 × ((A/Z)_fit × Z)^(2/3) with (A/Z)_fit = 1.866.
- For I-CMD with Coulomb, the barrier fit uses c2 = 12.921 MeV, r_Coul = 1.22 fm, and Z_res ≈ 41 for 129Xe.
- Additional freeze-out densities tested: 0.177 ρ0 (9³ lattice) and 0.597 ρ0 (6³ lattice) for 129Xe.

## Context

This work extends the experimental observations of Moretto et al. (1997, 1999) on Poissonian reducibility and thermal scaling in multifragmentation, which were originally based on transverse energy as a temperature proxy, by using the true temperature as a model input in thermal-equilibrium models. It supports the statistical nature of cluster emission and provides a theoretical foundation for the emission barrier systematics, showing that barriers scale with Z^(2/3) (surface energy) when Coulomb is absent and include an additional Coulomb term when present. The results enable future comparisons with dynamical transport models such as quantum molecular dynamics, fermion molecular dynamics, and antisymmetrized molecular dynamics, and motivate microcanonical treatments of the lattice gas model to study ensemble effects on multiplicity fluctuations.
