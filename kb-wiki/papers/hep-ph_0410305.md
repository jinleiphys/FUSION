# Kaonic hydrogen and K^- p scattering

> Chiral SU(3) effective field theory in combination with a relativistic coupled channels approach is used to perform a novel analysis of the strong interaction shift and width in kaonic hydrogen in view of the new accurate DEAR measurements. Questions of consistency with previous K^- p data are examined. Coulomb and isospin breaking effects turn out to be important and are both taken into account in this work.

## Key claim
The authors find that a global fit to all available low-energy K^- p data, including the new DEAR kaonic hydrogen measurements, cannot simultaneously satisfy the elastic K^- p total cross sections and the DEAR strong-interaction shift and width; the DEAR width is particularly difficult to reconcile with the scattering data, even after including Coulomb and isospin breaking corrections.

## Method
The authors use a relativistic chiral SU(3) effective field theory combined with a coupled-channels Bethe-Salpeter approach, resumming tree-level amplitudes from the next-to-leading order Lagrangian via unitarized loop integrals. They perform a global chi-squared fit to K^- p scattering cross sections, threshold branching ratios, the pi Sigma mass spectrum, and the DEAR kaonic hydrogen shift and width, including Coulomb corrections to the elastic K^- p amplitude and isospin breaking effects.

## Key numbers
- Strong-interaction scattering length from the global fit: a_{K^- p} = (-0.51 + 0.82 i) fm.
- Strong-interaction shift and width from the global fit: Delta E = 236 eV, Gamma = 580 eV (with isospin breaking corrections).
- When constrained to stay within the DEAR error band: Delta E = 235 eV, Gamma = 390 eV, giving a_{K^- p} = (-0.57 + 0.56 i) fm.
- Threshold branching ratios from the global fit: gamma = 2.35, R_c = 0.653, R_n = 0.194, compared to experimental values gamma = 2.36 +/- 0.04, R_c = 0.664 +/- 0.011, R_n = 0.189 +/- 0.015.
- Fitted decay constant in the chiral limit: f = 103.1 MeV, between F_pi = 92.4 MeV and F_K = 112.7 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the chiral coupled-channels approach of Kaiser, Siegel, and Weise (1995) and the later analysis by Borasoy, Nissler, and Weise (2004) that included eta photoproduction, by adding crossed Born terms and electromagnetic corrections. It confronts the new DEAR kaonic hydrogen data (2004) with older K^- p scattering data from Humphrey and Ross (1962), Sakitt et al. (1965), and others, revealing a tension between the precise DEAR width and the bulk of scattering cross sections. The results point to the need for future precision measurements at DA Phi NE to resolve the discrepancy.
