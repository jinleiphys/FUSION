# Core-crust transition and crustal fraction of moment of inertia in neutron stars

> The crustal fraction of moment of inertia in neutron stars is calculated using $β$-equilibrated nuclear matter obtained from density dependent M3Y effective interaction. The transition density, pressure and proton fraction at the inner edge separating the liquid core from the solid crust of the neutron stars determined from the thermodynamic stability conditions are found to be $ρ_t=$ 0.0938 fm$^{-3}$, P$_t=$ 0.5006 MeV fm$^{-3}$ and $x_{p(t)}=$ 0.0308, respectively. The crustal fraction of the moment of inertia can be extracted from studying pulsar glitches and is most sensitive to the pressure as well as density at the transition from the crust to the core. These results for pressure and density at core-crust transition together with the observed minimum crustal fraction of the total moment of inertia provide a new limit for the radius of the Vela pulsar: $R \geq 4.10 + 3.36 M/M_\odot$ kms.

## Key claim
The crustal fraction of moment of inertia in neutron stars, calculated using the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective interaction, can accommodate large Vela pulsar glitches without invoking an additional angular-momentum reservoir beyond the solid crust, provided the neutron star mass is 1.8 solar masses or less.

## Method
The authors used the DDM3Y effective nucleon-nucleon interaction to derive the equation of state for beta-equilibrated nuclear matter. The core-crust transition density, pressure, and proton fraction were determined by setting the thermodynamic stability condition (the thermal potential) to zero. The mass-radius relation and total moment of inertia were obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation and the slow-rotation Hartle formalism, and the crustal fraction of moment of inertia was then computed using an approximate formula depending on the transition pressure and density.

## Key numbers
- Core-crust transition density: rho_t = 0.0938 fm^{-3}
- Core-crust transition pressure: P_t = 0.5006 MeV fm^{-3}
- Proton fraction at transition: x_{p(t)} = 0.0308
- Symmetric nuclear matter incompressibility: K_infinity = 274.7 +/- 7.4 MeV
- Nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density: E_sym(rho_0) = 30.71 +/- 0.26 MeV
- Slope of symmetry energy: L = 45.11 +/- 0.02 MeV
- Isospin-dependent part of isobaric incompressibility: K_tau = -408.97 +/- 3.01 MeV
- Vela pulsar radius constraint: R >= 4.10 + 3.36 M/M_sun km
- Maximum neutron star mass from this EoS: 1.9227 M_sun (at central density 1.31 fm^{-3})
- Crustal fraction of moment of inertia exceeds 0.014 for neutron stars with masses 1.8 M_sun or less

## Context
This work extends the line of research on neutron star core-crust transitions and pulsar glitch modeling, following the approach of Link et al. (1999) who showed that glitches in the Vela pulsar require more than 1.4% of the star's moment of inertia. The DDM3Y interaction used here was previously shown by Basu et al. (2009) to satisfy constraints from giant monopole resonances, neutron skin thickness, and heavy-ion collision data. The results provide a new radius constraint for the Vela pulsar that differs from earlier estimates by Link et al. (1999) and Xu et al. (2009), and support the recent suggestion by Piekarewicz (2014) that the crust alone may be sufficient to explain large Vela glitches.
