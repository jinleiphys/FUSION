# Probing gluon saturation via diffractive jets in ultra-peripheral nucleus-nucleus collisions

> We argue that semi-inclusive photo-production of a pair of hard jets via coherent diffraction in nucleus-nucleus ultra-peripheral collisions at high energy is a golden channel to study gluon saturation. The dominant contribution is the diffractive production of three jets in an asymmetric configuration. Two of the jets are hard and propagate at nearly central pseudo-rapidities. The third jet is semi-hard, with transverse momentum comparable to the nuclear saturation momentum, and is well separated in pseudo-rapidity from the hard dijets. The emission of the semi-hard jet allows for strong scattering, thus avoiding the "higher-twist" suppression of the exclusive dijet production due to colour transparency. We compute the trijet cross-section using the diffractive TMD factorisation which emerges from the CGC effective theory at high energy. The cross-section is controlled by gluon saturation, which leaves its imprints on the structure of the final state, notably on the rapidity distribution.

## Key claim

Semi-inclusive diffractive photo-production of a hard quark-antiquark pair accompanied by a semi-hard gluon jet in nucleus-nucleus ultra-peripheral collisions is a "golden channel" to study gluon saturation, provided the third jet is actually observed. The cross-section avoids the higher-twist suppression of exclusive dijet production because the semi-hard gluon emission allows for strong scattering, and the process is controlled by gluon saturation which leaves imprints on the final state structure, notably the rapidity distribution.

## Method

The authors compute the cross-section for diffractive (2+1)-jet production (two hard jets from a quark-antiquark pair plus a semi-hard gluon jet) in coherent ultra-peripheral nucleus-nucleus collisions using diffractive transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) factorization that emerges from the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory. The cross-section factorizes into a photon flux from one nucleus, a hard factor describing the photon decay into the quark-antiquark pair and gluon emission, and a semi-hard factor representing the unintegrated gluon distribution of the Pomeron (the gluon diffractive TMD), which is computed using the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model for the gluon-gluon dipole scattering amplitude. The analysis focuses on Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC with center-of-mass energy per nucleon-nucleon collision of 5 TeV.

## Key numbers

- The saturation momentum for a lead nucleus at small x is expected to be $Q_s^2(x,A) \gtrsim 2$ GeV$^2$ for $x \lesssim 10^{-2}$ and $A \simeq 200$.
- The total transverse momentum transferred from the target to the jets is $\Delta_{\perp} \sim 1/R_A \simeq 30$ MeV for a Pb nucleus with $R_A \simeq 6$ fm.
- The critical photon energy fraction from the nucleus is $x_{\gamma}^{*} \equiv 1/(2 M_N R_A) \simeq 0.016$, giving a critical photon energy $\omega^{*} = x_{\gamma}^{*} E_N \simeq 40$ GeV for $E_N = 2.5$ TeV.
- The rapidity separation between the hard dijets and the semi-hard gluon jet is $\Delta\eta_{\rm jet} \sim \ln(P_{\perp}/Q_s)$, estimated as 2 to 3 units of pseudo-rapidity.
- The integrated gluon distribution of the Pomeron in the MV model scales like $A \ln A$ and vanishes like $(1-x)^2$ when $x \to 1$, compared to $A^{4/3}$ and $(1-x)^3$ without saturation.
- The dijet measurements at the LHC explore values of $x_{\mathbb{P}}$ in the ballpark of $x_{\mathbb{P}} = 0.01$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of diffractive (2+1)-jet production in deep inelastic scattering at the Electron-Ion Collider (Iancu et al., 2021; Iancu et al., 2022) to ultra-peripheral nucleus-nucleus collisions at the LHC. It contrasts with exclusive dijet production, which suffers from higher-twist suppression due to color transparency, and with earlier collinear factorization approaches to diffractive parton distributions that relied on models (Frankfurt et al., 2011; Guzey et al., 2016). The paper enables experimental searches for gluon saturation via the semi-hard gluon jet's rapidity distribution and the leading-twist scaling of the cross-section with nuclear mass number A.
