# Non-trivial aspects of the onset of nuclear collectivity: Static moments

> We consider several topics concerning static magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments ($μ$ and $Q$) as signatures of the onset of nuclear collectivity. Having previously noted that in $^{50}\mbox{Cr}$ there is an abrupt change of sign in $Q$ of yrast states with $J^π=10^+, 12^+$, and $14^+$ relative to lower $J$ states, we discuss whether these states are oblate or prolate. We next show that configuration mixing leads to much larger changes in $Q$ than in $μ$. We then look for other bands of interest in $^{50}\mbox{Cr}$. Finally we discuss the Jolos-von Brentano relationship which relates $Q$ of $2^+_1$ states to $B({\rm E2})$'s for transitions from and to the $2^+_1$ states.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the onset of nuclear collectivity in $^{50}\text{Cr}$ produces large changes in electric quadrupole moments $Q$ but much smaller changes in magnetic $g$ factors, contradicting the expectation that collectivity would bring $g$ factors close to the rotational value $g_R = Z/A$.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed for $^{50}\text{Cr}$ allowing up to three nucleons to be excited from the $f_{7/2}$ shell to the rest of the $f$-$p$ shell, using both the FPD6 and KB3 interactions. The authors computed static quadrupole moments $Q$ and magnetic $g$ factors for yrast states up to $J^\pi = 14^+$, and tested the Jolos-von Brentano relation linking $Q(2^+_1)$ to $B(\text{E2})$ values by comparing its predictions with shell-model results and experimental data for nuclei in the $s$-$d$ and $f$-$p$ shells.

## Key numbers

- Static quadrupole moments in $^{50}\text{Cr}$ with FPD6 interaction ($t=3$): $Q(2^+_1) = -27.5\ e\,\text{fm}^2$, $Q(4^+_1) = -34.8\ e\,\text{fm}^2$, $Q(6^+_1) = -8.1\ e\,\text{fm}^2$, $Q(8^+_1) = -20.7\ e\,\text{fm}^2$, $Q(10^+_1) = +45.7\ e\,\text{fm}^2$, $Q(12^+_1) = +18.6\ e\,\text{fm}^2$, $Q(14^+_1) = +11.4\ e\,\text{fm}^2$
- Static quadrupole moments in $^{50}\text{Cr}$ with KB3 interaction ($t=3$): $Q(2^+_1) = -24.8\ e\,\text{fm}^2$, $Q(4^+_1) = -30.0\ e\,\text{fm}^2$, $Q(6^+_1) = -15.6\ e\,\text{fm}^2$, $Q(8^+_1) = -14.7\ e\,\text{fm}^2$, $Q(10^+_1) = +26.5\ e\,\text{fm}^2$, $Q(12^+_1) = +13.0\ e\,\text{fm}^2$, $Q(14^+_1) = +8.2\ e\,\text{fm}^2$
- Experimental $g$ factors in $^{50}\text{Cr}$: $g(2^+_1) = 0.54(11)$, $g(4^+_1) = 0.43(9)$, $g(6^+_1) = 0.54(16)$, $g(8^+_1) = 0.54(9)$
- Calculated $g$ factors for $^{50}\text{Cr}$ with KB3 interaction ($t=3$, free $g$ values): $g(2^+_1) = 0.58$, $g(4^+_1) = 0.80$, $g(6^+_1) = 0.79$, $g(8^+_1) = 0.83$
- Percent change in $g$ factors from $t=0$ to $t=3$ for $^{50}\text{Cr}$: $2^+_1$: 18.1%, $4^+_1$: 15.3%, $6^+_1$: 10.4%, $8^+_1$: 7.6%
- Experimental $Q(2^+_1)$ for $^{50}\text{Cr}$: $-36 \pm 7\ e\,\text{fm}^2$
- J-vB prediction for $|Q(2^+_1)|$ in $^{50}\text{Cr}$ using experimental data: 22.5 $e\,\text{fm}^2$; rotational formula prediction: 29.8 $e\,\text{fm}^2$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous shell-model study of $^{50}\text{Cr}$ (Zamick, Fayache, and Zheng, 1996) which first noted the sign change in quadrupole moments at high spin. The paper addresses experimental measurements by Pakou et al. (1994) who found $g$ factors in $^{50}\text{Cr}$ close to $Z/A$ and interpreted this as evidence for collectivity. The study enables future reconciliation between shell-model calculations and experimental data on magnetic moments, and provides a systematic test of the Jolos-von Brentano relation (Jolos and von Brentano, preprint) across the $s$-$d$ and $f$-$p$ shells, finding it works better with experimental input than with shell-model $B(\text{E2})$ values.
