# Chiral effective theory predictions for deuteron form factor ratios at low Q^2

> We use chiral effective theory to predict the deuteron form factor ratio G_C/G_Q as well as ratios of deuteron to nucleon form factors. These ratios are calculated to next-to-next-to-leading order. At this order the chiral expansion for the NN isoscalar charge operator (including consistently calculated 1/M corrections) is a parameter-free prediction of the effective theory. Use of this operator in conjunction with NLO and NNLO chiral effective theory wave functions produces results that are consistent with extant experimental data for Q^2 < 0.35 GeV^2. These wave functions predict a deuteron quadrupole moment G_Q(Q^2=0)=0.278-0.282 fm^2-with the variation arising from short-distance contributions to this quantity. The variation is of the same size as the discrepancy between the theoretical result and the experimental value. This motivates the renormalization of G_Q via a two-nucleon operator that couples to quadrupole photons. After that renormalization we obtain a robust prediction for the shape of G_C/G_Q at Q^2 < 0.3 GeV^2. This allows us to make precise, model-independent predictions for the values of this ratio that will be measured at the lower end of the kinematic range explored at BLAST. We also present results for the ratio G_C/G_M.

## Key claim

Chiral effective theory predictions for the deuteron form factor ratio \(G_C/G_Q\) are parameter-free at next-to-next-to-leading order after renormalizing the quadrupole moment via a short-distance two-nucleon operator, but the prediction is robust only for \(Q^2 < 0.3\) GeV\(^2\); at \(Q^2 \approx 0.4\) GeV\(^2\) the uncertainty becomes sizeable.

## Method

The author computed the deuteron charge operator \(J_0\) up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO, \(O(eP^3)\)) in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, including consistently calculated \(1/M\) corrections and the two-body pion-exchange graph. Wave functions from NLO, NNLO, and N\(^3\)LO chiral potentials (Epelbaum et al., 2005) were used, with spectral-function regularization and a momentum cutoff. The deuteron quadrupole moment was renormalized by fitting a short-distance two-nucleon operator that couples to quadrupole photons, and results were presented as ratios to isoscalar nucleon form factors to isolate deuteron structure.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron quadrupole moment predicted by chiral wave functions: \(G_Q(Q^2=0) = 0.278\)–\(0.282\) fm\(^2\), with the variation arising from short-distance contributions.
- Experimental deuteron quadrupole moment: \(Q_d = 0.2859(3)\) fm\(^2\) (from molecular physics, CR71 and BC79).
- The two-pion-exchange contribution at \(O(eP^4)\) to \(Q_d\) was computed by Park et al. (2000) as \(\Delta Q_d^{(4)} = -0.002\) fm\(^2\), insufficient to repair the discrepancy.
- The chiral expansion for form factor ratios is consistent with experimental data for \(Q^2 < 0.35\) GeV\(^2\).
- Robust predictions for \(G_C/G_Q\) are obtained for \(Q^2 < 0.3\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends chiral effective theory calculations of deuteron electromagnetic form factors (Phillips, 2003; Walzl and Meissner, 2001; Park et al., 1996) to include consistent \(1/M\) corrections and renormalization of the quadrupole moment. It addresses the persistent underprediction of the deuteron quadrupole moment found in all modern potential models (e.g., Nijm93 by Stoks et al., 1994; CD-Bonn by Machleidt, 2001; AV18 by Wiringa et al., 1995) by introducing a short-distance two-body operator. The predictions are designed to be tested by upcoming polarized electron-deuteron scattering data from the BLAST experiment at Bates, enabling model-independent extraction of \(G_C/G_Q\) at low \(Q^2\).
