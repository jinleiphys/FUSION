# Fluctuations and the QCD phase diagram

> In this contribution the role of quantum fluctuations for the QCD phase diagram is discussed. This concerns in particular the importance of the matter back-reaction to the gluonic sector. The impact of these fluctuations on the location of the confinement/deconfinement and the chiral transition lines as well as their interrelation are investigated. Consequences of our findings for the size of a possible quarkyonic phase and location of a critical endpoint in the phase diagram are drawn.

# Fluctuations and the QCD phase diagram

## Key claim
Including the matter back-reaction to the gluonic sector via a running Polyakov-loop scale parameter \(T_0(N_f,\mu)\) causes the chiral and deconfinement transition lines to coincide over the whole phase diagram, leaving little room for a chirally symmetric and confining (quarkyonic) region and excluding a critical endpoint at small chemical potentials. This result depends on the truncation level of the functional renormalization group (FRG) flow and the omission of baryonic degrees of freedom at low temperatures and high densities.

## Method
The author employs a Polyakov-quark-meson (PQM) model with quantum fluctuations treated via the functional renormalization group (FRG) approach. The FRG flow equation includes quark-meson dynamics in a Polyakov-loop background, and the matter back-reaction is encoded in a flavor- and chemical-potential-dependent scale parameter \(T_0(N_f,\mu)\) in the effective Polyakov-loop potential, based on HTL/HDL perturbation theory and one-loop running of the QCD \(\beta\)-function. Phase diagrams are computed with and without this back-reaction and compared to mean-field results.

## Key numbers
- The pure gauge deconfinement transition temperature is \(T_c = 270\) MeV for \(N_c = 3\).
- The chiral (pseudo)critical transition lines curve downwards with increasing chemical potential for physical quark masses.
- The grey band representing the width of the temperature derivative of the Polyakov-loop variable at 80% of its peak height shrinks with increasing \(\mu\) when the back-reaction is included.

## Context
This work extends Polyakov-loop extended chiral models such as the PNJL model (Fukushima, 2003) and the PQM model (Schaefer, 2007; Herbst, 2010) beyond mean-field by including quantum fluctuations via FRG. It directly builds on the first phenomenological estimate of the \(N_f\)- and \(\mu\)-dependence of \(T_0\) (Schaefer, 2007), which was later confirmed by first-principle QCD FRG computations (Braun, 2009) and by a combination of PNJL with a statistical model (Fukushima, 2010). The results enable systematic improvement of effective models toward full dynamical QCD and constrain the possible location of a critical endpoint and the size of a quarkyonic phase.
