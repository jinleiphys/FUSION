# Statistical Ensembles With Finite Bath: A Description for an Event Generator

> A Monte Carlo event generator has been developed assuming thermal production of hadrons. The system under consideration is sampled grand canonically in the Boltzmann approximation. A re-weighting scheme is then introduced to account for conservation of charges (baryon number, strangeness, electric charge) and energy and momentum, effectively allowing for extrapolation of grand canonical results to the microcanonical limit. This method has two strong advantages compared to analytical approaches and standard microcanonical Monte Carlo techniques, in that it is capable of handling resonance decays as well as (very) large system sizes.

## Key claim

The paper presents a Monte Carlo event generator that samples hadron production grand canonically in the Boltzmann approximation and then applies a re-weighting scheme to extrapolate results toward the microcanonical limit, but the method is explicitly limited to the large volume limit where the central limit theorem approximation for the weight factor is valid.

## Method

The authors sample particle multiplicities from Poisson distributions and momenta from Boltzmann spectra for a hadron resonance gas in a fixed volume V1 at temperature T and chemical potentials μj. They then calculate a weight factor W for each event based on the deviation of its extensive quantities (baryon number, strangeness, electric charge, energy, and momentum components) from the equilibrium values, using a multivariate Gaussian approximation derived from the central limit theorem. By varying the parameter λ = V1/Vg, where Vg is the volume of a fictitious total system, they interpolate between the grand canonical ensemble (λ=0) and the microcanonical limit (λ→1).

## Key numbers

- Temperature T = 0.160 GeV used for the static neutral system studied
- Volume V1 = 2000 fm³ used for the demonstration system
- 2.5 × 10⁴ events generated per Monte Carlo run, with 20 runs per λ value
- 19 different equally spaced values of λ investigated
- L = 7 extensive quantities considered for re-weighting (B, S, Q, E, Px, Py, Pz)
- The maximal weight a microstate can assume is Wmax = (1-λ)^(-L/2)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of ideal particle and anti-particle gases (Hauer 2007, 2008) and massless particle gases (Hauer 2008) by implementing the re-weighting scheme into the THERMUS package (Wheaton 2004). The method offers advantages over analytical approaches and standard microcanonical Monte Carlo techniques (Becattini 2004, 2006) by handling resonance decays and large system sizes, and enables systematic study of how fluctuation and correlation observables depend on the size of the thermodynamic bath, which is relevant for interpreting heavy-ion collision data from SIS through RHIC energies.
