# Role of momentum in the generator-coordinate method applied to barrier penetration

> Nuclear fission at barrier-top energies is conventionally modeled by a one-dimensional Schrödinger equation applied to internal fission channels, but that treatment is hard to justify in the configuration-interaction approach to nuclear Hamiltonians. Here we show that inclusion of states of finite momentum by the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) considerably extends the range of energies at which GCM-based Hamiltonians could reproduce the Schrödinger treatment. The transmission probabilities for crossing the barrier are calculated by a discrete version of Kohn's variational method, which may also be useful for other systems of interacting fermions.

## Key claim

Inclusion of a second momentum-carrying state (the first excited harmonic oscillator wave function) at each site of a Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) chain doubles the useful energy range over which the discrete-basis Hamiltonian can reproduce the one-dimensional Schrödinger transmission probabilities, extending the range from barrier heights of a few times the zero-point energy to at least ten times the zero-point energy.

## Method

The authors construct a discrete-basis Hamiltonian using Gaussian wave packets on a mesh of equally spaced points along a collective coordinate, with the Gaussian overlap approximation (GOA) for the Hamiltonian and overlap matrix elements. They add a second state per site corresponding to the first excited harmonic oscillator wave function to introduce finite momentum. Transmission probabilities for barrier penetration are calculated using a discrete version of Kohn's variational method, where asymptotic channel wave functions are included explicitly and the unknown amplitudes for reflected and transmitted waves are obtained by solving a truncated matrix equation.

## Key numbers

- The mesh spacing is set to Δx = 5^(1/2) s, where s is the Gaussian width parameter.
- The zero-point kinetic energy is E_q = ħ²/(4 M s²).
- For a barrier with height V₀ = 3 E_q and width σ = 2.0 s, the two-state GCM treatment reproduces the Schrödinger transmission probability for energies up to E/E_q ≈ 3, while the previous single-state treatment failed above that energy.
- For a barrier with height V₀ = 6 E_q and width σ = 2.0 s, the two-state treatment gives excellent agreement with the Schrödinger result up to at least E/E_q ≈ 10.
- The ring Hamiltonian used to construct plane waves uses N_q = 30 sites with next-to-nearest neighbor interactions (N_od = 2).
- The interior region for the barrier penetration calculation uses N_φ = 60 basis states, giving a truncated Hamiltonian matrix of minimum dimensions 62 × 66.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Hagino 2023, Bertsch 2024) which showed that a GCM Hamiltonian with only one state per site could reproduce Schrödinger transmission probabilities only for barrier heights no higher than 2-3 times the zero-point energy. The present two-state construction overcomes that limitation and provides an energy range sufficient to study fission channels in actinide nuclei. The Kohn variational method used here has been previously applied to nucleus-nucleus collisions with GCM Hamiltonians (Beck 1975, Kamimura 1977, Viñas 2020) and to the no-core shell model (Efros 2019, Sharaf 2024). The work enables microscopic configuration-interaction studies of nuclear fission dynamics, addressing fundamental questions about whether fission should be treated as diffusive (Kramer 1940) or as motion along a collective coordinate (Bohr and Wheeler 1939).
