# Two-photon exchange on neutron and the hyperfine splitting

> We calculate the contribution from the two-photon exchange on the neutron to the hyperfine splitting of S energy levels. We update the value of the neutron Zemach radius, estimate total recoil and polarizability corrections. The resulting two-photon exchange in electronic atoms exceeds by an order of magnitude the leading Zemach term and has different sign both in electronic and muonic hydrogen.

# Two-photon exchange on neutron and the hyperfine splitting

## Key claim
The two-photon exchange (TPE) contribution to the hyperfine splitting from the neutron intermediate state exceeds the leading Zemach term by an order of magnitude in electronic atoms and has opposite sign in both electronic and muonic hydrogen, but the polarizability correction is estimated with a 60% uncertainty due to the lack of complete data on neutron spin structure functions.

## Method
The author calculates the TPE correction to the hyperfine splitting of S energy levels by decomposing the effective neutron-structure radius into Zemach, recoil, and polarizability radii. The Zemach radius is evaluated using modern neutron form factor parametrizations that satisfy a consistency plateau criterion at low momentum transfer, while the polarizability contribution is estimated from the MAID partial-wave analysis of pion-nucleon, kaon-nucleon, and eta-nucleon channels combined with the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule and low-momentum-transfer expansion of the first moment of the g1 structure function.

## Key numbers
- Neutron Zemach radius: -0.0449(13) fm (same for both electronic and muonic atoms)
- Neutron recoil radius in electronic atoms: 0.328(2) fm; in muonic atoms: 0.0823(8) fm
- Total neutron TPE radius in electronic atoms: 0.347(38) fm; in muonic atoms: 0.102(39) fm
- Neutron squared electric charge radius: -0.1161 ± 0.0022 fm²
- Neutron magnetic radius: 0.864 ± 0.009 fm
- The r_F2² term alone: 0.987(7) fm for electronic atoms and 0.260(6) fm for muonic atoms
- The polarizability radius: 0.064(38) fm for electronic atoms and 0.065(39) fm for muonic atoms

## Context
This work extends the TPE calculations previously performed for the proton (Tomalak 2017, Tomalak 2018) to the neutron, which had only received the leading Zemach correction from Friar (2005). The neutron TPE contribution is needed for complete structure corrections to hyperfine splitting in light atoms such as hydrogen and deuterium, where the proton-neutron discrepancy in charge radius measurements (Pohl 2010, Antognini 2013) motivates revisiting higher-order hadronic corrections. The results enable improved extraction of the neutron Zemach radius from future hyperfine splitting measurements and can be refined with better neutron form factor data and spin structure function measurements.
