# Quark mass dependence of Spin-Orbit force in parity-odd NN system from 2+1 flavor QCD

> We report our recent study of spin-orbit force between two nucleons in the parity-odd sector from lattice QCD. To examine the quark mass dependence of spin-orbit force, we construct it from Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions in $^3P_0$, $^3P_1$ and $^3P_2-^3F_2$ channels calculated from lattice QCD in the quark mass region $m_π$=702-411 MeV. The calculation is performed on Blue Gene/Q at KEK by using $N_f=2+1$ PACS-CS gauge configuration generated by O(a) improved Wilson quark action with RG improved (Iwasaki) gauge action. We find that the potentials tend to become stronger as the quark mass decreases.

# Quark mass dependence of Spin-Orbit force in parity-odd NN system from 2+1 flavor QCD

## Key claim
The spin-orbit, central, and tensor potentials in the parity-odd nucleon-nucleon sector tend to become stronger as the quark mass decreases, based on lattice QCD calculations at pion masses of 702, 570, and 411 MeV, though the time slice saturation of the central potential has not yet been achieved.

## Method
The authors extract the spin-orbit, central, and tensor potentials in the spin-triplet parity-odd sector from equal-time Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions calculated in lattice QCD. They use NBS wave functions in the $^3P_0$, $^3P_1$, and $^3P_2-^3F_2$ channels, obtained from 4-point nucleon correlation functions on $32^3\times 64$ lattices with $N_f=2+1$ PACS-CS gauge configurations generated with $\mathcal{O}(a)$ improved Wilson quark action and RG improved (Iwasaki) gauge action at $\beta=1.90$, and solve the coupled Schr\"odinger equation via the time-dependent method to determine the three potentials simultaneously.

## Key numbers
- Pion masses studied: $m_\pi = 702(1)(10)$ MeV, $570(2)(8)$ MeV, and $411.3(2)(6)$ MeV, with corresponding nucleon masses $m_N = 1538(5)(23)$ MeV, $1411(12)(20)$ MeV, and $1215(12)(17)$ MeV, respectively (first error statistical, second systematic from lattice scale ambiguity).
- Lattice spacing: $a^{-1} = 2.176(31)$ GeV ($a = 0.0907(13)$ fm), with lattice extension $L \simeq 2.9$ fm.
- Quark hopping parameters: $\kappa_{ud} = 0.13700$, $0.13727$, and $0.13754$ with $\kappa_s = 0.13640$; clover coefficient $C_{\rm SW} = 1.715$.
- The spin-orbit potential is negative and strong, the tensor potential is weak and positive, and the central potential has a repulsive core at short distance, consistent with earlier results at $m_\pi=1133$ MeV from Murano (2013).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of parity-odd NN potentials at a single heavy pion mass of 1133 MeV (Murano, 2013) to three lighter quark masses, using 2+1 flavor configurations from the PACS-CS collaboration (Aoki, 2008). The method builds on the HAL QCD approach for defining energy-independent non-local potentials from NBS wave functions (Ishii, 2006; Aoki, 2009), which has previously been applied to central and tensor forces in the parity-even NN sector. Achieving time slice saturation and increasing statistics will enable quantitative comparison with phenomenological potentials such as AV18 and allow calculation of P-wave phase shifts relevant for neutron star superfluidity.
