# The chiral critical point of Nf=3 QCD at finite density to the order (mu/T)^4

> QCD with three degenerate quark flavours at zero baryon density exhibits a first order thermal phase transition for small quark masses, which changes to a smooth crossover for some critical quark mass m^c_0, i.e. the chiral critical point. It is generally believed that as an (even) function of quark chemical potential, m_c(mu), the critical point moves to larger quark masses, constituting the critical endpoint of a first order phase transition in theories with m\geq m^c_0. To test this, we consider a Taylor expansion of m_c(mu) around mu=0 and determine the first two coefficients from lattice simulations with staggered fermions on N_t=4 lattices. We employ two different techniques: a) calculating the coefficients directly from a mu=0 ensemble using a novel finite difference method, and b) fitting them to simulation data obtained for imaginary chemical potentials. The mu^2 and mu^4 coefficients are found to be negative by both methods, with consistent absolute values. Combining both methods gives evidence that also the mu^6 coefficient is negative. Hence, on coarse N_t=4 lattices a three-flavour theory with m > m^c_0 does not possess a chiral critical endpoint for quark chemical potentials mu\lsim T. Simulations on finer lattices are required for reliable continuum physics. Possible implications for the QCD phase diagram are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for three-flavor QCD on coarse \(N_t=4\) lattices, the region of first-order chiral phase transitions *shrinks* with increasing chemical potential, meaning there is **no** chiral critical endpoint for quark chemical potentials \(\mu \lesssim T\) in a theory with quark masses larger than the zero-density critical mass. This contradicts the previously expected scenario where the critical line moves to larger quark masses, creating a critical endpoint.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with staggered fermions on \(8^3\times 4\) and \(12^3\times 4\) lattices, using two complementary techniques to extract the Taylor expansion coefficients of the critical quark mass \(m_c(\mu)\) around \(\mu=0\). The first method directly computed the derivative \(\partial B_4/\partial (a\mu)^2\) via reweighting from a \(\mu=0\) ensemble using a novel finite difference method. The second method fitted the Binder cumulant \(B_4\) and pseudo-critical coupling \(\beta_c\) to simulation data obtained at imaginary chemical potentials \(\mu = i\mu_i\), where no sign problem exists.

## Key numbers

- Leading coefficient \(c_1 = -3.3(3)\) for the expansion \(\frac{m_c(\mu)}{m_c(0)} = 1 + c_1 (\mu/\pi T_c)^2 + c_2 (\mu/\pi T_c)^4 + \ldots\)
- Next-to-leading coefficient \(c_2 = -47(20)\) for the same expansion
- Critical quark mass at zero density \(am_0^c = 0.0259(4)\) on the \(8^3\times 4\) lattice
- Binder cumulant derivative \(b_{01} = 1.6(1)\) from the direct derivative method on the \(L=8\) lattice
- The \(\mu^6\) coefficient \(c_3'\) is also found to be negative, further shrinking the first-order region

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by de Forcrand and Philipsen (2007) which first found a negative curvature for the chiral critical line using imaginary chemical potentials, contradicting the continuity arguments of derivs (2002) that predicted the critical line should move to larger quark masses. The paper provides a systematic investigation of higher-order terms up to \((\mu/T)^4\) and finds all coefficients negative, confirming the scenario where no chiral critical endpoint exists for \(\mu \lesssim T\). The results are limited to coarse \(N_t=4\) lattices, and the authors note that simulations on finer lattices are required for reliable continuum physics, as cut-off effects may be stronger than finite density effects.
