# Volume, Coulomb, and volume-symmetry coefficients of nucleus incompressibility in the relativistic mean field theory with the excluded volume effects

> The relation among the volume coefficient $K$(=incompressibility of the nuclear matter), the Coulomb coefficient $K_c$, and the volume-symmetry coefficient $K_{vs}$ of the nucleus incompressibility are studied in the framework of the relativistic mean field theory with the excluded volume effects of the nucleons, under the assumption of the scaling model. It is found that $K= 300\pm 50$MeV is necessary to account for the empirical values of $K$, $K_c$, and $K_{vs}$, simultaneously, as is in the case of the point-like nucleons. The result is independent on the detail descriptions of the potential of the $σ$-meson self-interaction and is almost independent on the excluded volume of the nucleons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a nuclear matter incompressibility of \(K = 300 \pm 50\) MeV is necessary to simultaneously reproduce the empirical values of the volume coefficient \(K\), the Coulomb coefficient \(K_c\), and the volume-symmetry coefficient \(K_{vs}\) of nucleus incompressibility, under the scaling model and within relativistic mean field theory (RMF) that includes excluded volume effects (EVE) of nucleons. This conclusion holds for any form of the \(\sigma\)-meson self-interaction potential and is almost independent of the excluded volume parameter \(R_n\) (up to 0.8 fm).

## Method

The authors use the \(\sigma\)-\(\omega\)-\(\rho\) model of RMF with nonlinear \(\sigma\) terms and include excluded volume effects by replacing the baryon density \(\rho\) with \(\tilde{\rho}/(1+V_n\tilde{\rho})\), where \(V_n\) is the excluded volume per nucleon. They derive analytic expressions for \(K\), \(K_c\), and \(K_{vs}\) under the scaling model, which depend only on nuclear matter properties (density, binding energy, symmetry energy, effective mass) and not on the detailed form of the \(\sigma\) self-interaction \(U(\phi)\). They then compute \(K_{vs}\) as a function of the effective nucleon mass \(M^*\) for various combinations of \(K\) and \(K_c\) taken from empirical fits, with the excluded volume radius \(R_n\) varied from 0 to 0.8 fm.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter incompressibility \(K = 300 \pm 50\) MeV is required to simultaneously account for empirical \(K\), \(K_c\), and \(K_{vs}\).
- For \(K = 300\) MeV and \(K_c = -3.990\) MeV, the calculated \(K_{vs}\) ranges from \(-247\) to \(+176\) MeV for \(R_n = 0.8\) fm, from \(-292\) to \(-53\) MeV for \(R_n = 0.6\) fm, and from \(-298\) to \(-115\) MeV for point-like nucleons (\(R_n = 0\)).
- For \(K = 350\) MeV and \(K_c = -7.274\) MeV, the calculated \(K_{vs}\) ranges from \(-652\) to \(-484\) MeV for \(R_n = 0.8\) fm, from \(-673\) to \(-610\) MeV for \(R_n = 0.6\) fm, and from \(-671\) to \(-632\) MeV for point-like nucleons.
- The normal baryon density is fixed at \(\rho_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\), the binding energy per nucleon at \(a_1 = 15.75\) MeV, and the symmetry energy at \(a_4 = 30.0\) MeV.
- The excluded volume radius \(R_n\) is restricted to \(0 \leq R_n \leq 0.8\) fm; values above 0.9 fm may cause divergences.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Kouno et al., 1995) on the relation between \(K\) and \(K_c\) in RMF with nonlinear \(\sigma\) and \(\omega\) terms, and with excluded volume effects. It generalizes those results by showing that the conclusion \(K = 300 \pm 50\) MeV is independent of the detailed form of the \(\sigma\) meson self-interaction potential, and is almost unaffected by the excluded volume. The finding agrees with the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculation by the authors' reference [19], with the generator coordinate calculation for GMR by Stoitsov, Ring and Sharma (1994) suggesting \(K \approx 300\) MeV, and with earlier work by Sharma (1993). The result contradicts the low incompressibility (\(K \approx 200\) MeV) favored by Von-Eiff et al. (1993) for nuclear surface properties, and the paper notes that no coupling parameters could be found to reproduce \(K = 200\) MeV with the quartic-cubic potential. The analysis enables future work to focus on whether specific coupling parameters exist that reproduce the required \(K\) and \(K_c\) values for a given \(U(\phi)\).
