# Viscosity spectral functions of the dilute Fermi gas in kinetic theory

> We compute the viscosity spectral function of the dilute Fermi gas for different values of the s-wave scattering length $a$, including the unitarity limit $a\to\infty$. We perform the calculation in kinetic theory by studying the response to a non-trivial background metric. We find the expected structure consisting of a diffusive peak in the transverse shear channel and a sound peak in the longitudinal channel. At zero momentum the width of the diffusive peak is $ω_0\simeq (2ε)/(3η)$ where $ε$ is the energy density and $η$ is the shear viscosity. At finite momentum the spectral function approaches the collisionless limit and the width is of order $ω_0\sim k(T/m)^{1/2}$.

## Key claim

The paper computes the viscosity spectral function of the dilute Fermi gas using kinetic theory, finding that at zero momentum the width of the diffusive peak is approximately $\omega_0 \simeq (2\varepsilon)/(3\eta)$ where $\varepsilon$ is the energy density and $\eta$ is the shear viscosity, with the caveat that this result applies in the regime of high temperature and small frequency and momentum where kinetic theory methods are valid.

## Method

The authors solve the linearized Boltzmann equation in a curved background metric to compute the retarded stress tensor correlation function via linear response theory. They make an ansatz for the deviation of the distribution function that is quadratic in momentum, take moments of the linearized Boltzmann equation, and extract the spectral functions in the transverse shear channels ($xy$ and $xz$) and the longitudinal channel ($zz$). The collision integral uses elastic $2\leftrightarrow 2$ scattering characterized by the $s$-wave scattering length $a$, and the integrals are evaluated both analytically in the $k\to 0$ limit and numerically for finite momentum.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity in the high temperature limit: $\eta = \frac{15 (mT)^{3/2}}{32 \sqrt{\pi}}$ for the unitarity limit $a\to\infty$, and $\eta = \frac{15 (mT)^{3/2}}{32 \sqrt{\pi}} \cdot \frac{1}{3mTa^2}$ for $a\to 0$
- Sum rule: $\frac{1}{\pi}\int_0^{\infty} d\omega\, \eta(\omega) = \frac{\varepsilon}{3}$, where $\varepsilon$ is the energy density
- In the Kubo limit: $\lim_{\omega,k\to 0} \rho_T/(2\omega) = \eta$ for transverse channels and $\lim_{\omega,k\to 0} \rho_L/(2\omega) = \frac{4}{3}\eta$ for the longitudinal channel (bulk viscosity $\zeta=0$)
- At finite momentum the spectral function width is of order $\omega_0 \sim k(T/m)^{1/2}$ in the collisionless limit
- For the free spectral function at $k\to 0$: $\lim_{k\to 0} \rho^{xyxy}(\omega,k)/(2\omega) = (\varepsilon/3)\pi\delta(\omega)$

## Context

This work extends kinetic theory calculations of transport coefficients for the dilute Fermi gas at unitarity, building on prior calculations of shear viscosity by Bruun (2005, 2006), Rupak (2007), and Escobedo (2009). The results complement diagrammatic calculations of the shear viscosity using the Kubo formula by Enss (2010) and a sum rule for the stress tensor spectral function proved by Taylor (2010). The study is motivated by experiments with trapped Fermi gases near Feshbach resonances by O'Hara (2002) and Cao (2010) that indicate nearly perfect fluidity with $\eta/s \lesssim 0.5\,\hbar/k_B$, approaching the AdS/CFT bound proposed by Kovtun (2004). The spectral functions computed here serve as a default model for numerical calculations of the Euclidean correlator in imaginary time Monte Carlo simulations.
