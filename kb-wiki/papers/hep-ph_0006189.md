# Heavy-Meson Observables via Dyson-Schwinger Equations

> We summarise a Dyson-Schwinger-equation-based calculation of an extensive range of light- and heavy-meson observables, characterised by heavy-meson leptonic decays, semileptonic heavy-to-heavy and heavy-to-light transitions, radiative and strong decays, and the rare flavour-changing neutral-current process. In the calculation the heavy-quark mass functions are approximated by constants, interpreted as their constituent mass.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a Dyson-Schwinger-equation-based framework, in which heavy-quark mass functions are approximated by constants (interpreted as constituent masses), can provide a unified and uniformly accurate description of an extensive range of light- and heavy-meson observables, but with the caveat that corrections to the heavy-quark symmetry limit of up to 30% are encountered in b-to-c transitions and can be as large as a factor of two in c-to-d transitions.

## Method

The authors employed an algebraic characterisation of dressed-quark propagators and bound-state Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes using ten parameters plus four quark masses, fitted via chi-squared minimization to 42 heavy- and light-meson observables. Heavy-quark propagators were approximated by a constant constituent-mass form, while light-quark propagators were represented by an algebraic parametrisation incorporating confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. Bound states were described via Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes with simple functional forms (exponential for heavy mesons, one-parameter for vector mesons), and the model parameters were fixed by fitting to experimental data including leptonic decay constants, semileptonic transition form factors, and strong decay widths.

## Key numbers

- Constituent heavy-quark masses: \(\hat M_c = 1.32\) GeV and \(\hat M_b = 4.65\) GeV
- Heavy-meson binding energies: \(E_D = 0.67\) GeV, \(E_B = 0.70\) GeV
- Ratio \(E_D/\hat M_c = 0.51\) and \(E_B/\hat M_b = 0.15\)
- Fitted light-quark current masses: \(m_u = 5.4\) MeV, \(m_s = 119\) MeV
- Euclidean constituent-light-quark masses: \(M^E_u = 0.36\) GeV, \(M^E_s = 0.49\) GeV
- Leptonic decay constants: \(f_D = 0.213\) GeV (observed \(0.200 \pm 0.030\)), \(f_{D_s} = 0.234\) GeV (observed \(0.251 \pm 0.030\)), \(f_B = 0.182\) GeV (observed \(0.170 \pm 0.035\))
- Semileptonic form factor \(f_+^{B\to D}(0) = 0.58\) (observed 0.73)
- Isgur-Wise function slope parameter \(\rho^2 = 1.84\) (observed \(1.53 \pm 0.36\))
- \(\xi^{f_+}(1) = 1.08\), deviating from the heavy-quark symmetry prediction of 1.00
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom: \(\chi^2/{\rm d.o.f} = 1.75\), \(\chi^2/N_{\rm obs} = 1.17\)

## Context

This work extends Dyson-Schwinger equation phenomenology, previously applied extensively to light-meson physics (reviewed in Bastirev and Echaya), to heavy-meson observables, building on prior studies by Ivanov, Kalinovsky, and Roberts (1999) and Maris and Roberts (1997). The calculation provides a unified framework for heavy-meson leptonic decays, semileptonic heavy-to-heavy and heavy-to-light transitions, radiative and strong decays, and the rare flavour-changing neutral-current process \(B \to K^\ast \gamma\). The results enable improved extraction of CKM matrix elements \(V_{cb}\) and \(V_{ub}\) from experimental data, and demonstrate that heavy- and light-mesons can be treated as finite-size bound states of dressed quarks without qualitative distinction, while also quantifying the size of corrections to heavy-quark symmetry.
