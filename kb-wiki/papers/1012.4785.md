# Inverse magnetic catalysis in dense holographic matter

> We study the chiral phase transition in a magnetic field at finite temperature and chemical potential within the Sakai-Sugimoto model, a holographic top-down approach to (large-N_c) QCD. We consider the limit of a small separation of the flavor D8-branes, which corresponds to a dual field theory comparable to a Nambu-Jona Lasinio (NJL) model. Mapping out the surface of the chiral phase transition in the parameter space of magnetic field strength, quark chemical potential, and temperature, we find that for small temperatures the addition of a magnetic field decreases the critical chemical potential for chiral symmetry restoration - in contrast to the case of vanishing chemical potential where, in accordance with the familiar phenomenon of magnetic catalysis, the magnetic field favors the chirally broken phase. This "inverse magnetic catalysis" (IMC) appears to be associated with a previously found magnetic phase transition within the chirally symmetric phase that shows an intriguing similarity to a transition into the lowest Landau level. We estimate IMC to persist up to 10^{19} G at low temperatures.

## Key claim

In the Sakai-Sugimoto model in the NJL limit (small separation of flavor D8-branes), the chiral phase transition exhibits inverse magnetic catalysis at finite chemical potential: for small temperatures, increasing the magnetic field *decreases* the critical chemical potential for chiral symmetry restoration, opposite to the magnetic catalysis seen at zero chemical potential. This effect is estimated to persist up to magnetic field strengths of \(10^{19}\) G at low temperatures.

## Method

The authors study the chiral phase transition within the Sakai-Sugimoto model (a holographic top-down approach to large-\(N_c\) QCD) in the deconfined phase, using the probe brane approximation with \(N_f=1\) flavor. They work in the limit of small asymptotic separation \(L\) of the D8- and \(\overline{\text{D8}}\)-branes (the "NJL limit"), where the tip of the connected branes \(u_0\) is far from the horizon \(u_T\), allowing the approximation \(f(u) \simeq 1\). The equations of motion for the gauge fields \(a_0\), \(a_3\) and the embedding function \(x_4\) are solved semi-analytically, reducing to coupled algebraic equations for the tip position \(u_0\) and a parameter \(\eta\) related to the derivative ratio at the tip. The free energy of the chirally broken phase is compared to that of the chirally symmetric phase to map out the critical surface in the parameter space of temperature, chemical potential, and magnetic field.

## Key numbers

* The inverse magnetic catalysis effect is estimated to persist up to magnetic fields of \(10^{19}\) G at low temperatures.
* The critical separation \(L\) below which chiral and deconfinement transitions decouple is \(L \lesssim 0.3\pi/M_{\text{KK}}\) (citing Aharony 2006).
* At \(b=\mu=0\), the approximation \(f\simeq 1\) yields a critical temperature that deviates by about 10% from the full result.
* The dimensionless temperature is defined as \(t \equiv TR\), with \(u_T = (4\pi/3)^2 t^2\).
* The free energy and chemical potential scale as \(\Omega \to \ell^7 \Omega\) and \(\mu \to \ell^2 \mu\) under rescaling by the dimensionless separation \(\ell \equiv L/R\).

## Context

This work extends previous Sakai-Sugimoto model studies of the chiral phase transition in the \(T\)-\(\mu\) plane at \(B=0\) (Horigome 2006) and the \(T\)-\(B\) plane at \(\mu=0\) (Bergman 2008, Johnson 2008) to the full \(T\)-\(\mu\)-\(B\) parameter space. The results show striking qualitative resemblance to NJL model calculations (Ebert 1999, Ebert 2003, Inagaki 2003, Boomsma 2009, Frolov 2010, Fayazbakhsh 2010, Chatterjee 2011) and predict a discontinuity in the quark density interpreted as a transition to the lowest Landau level, supporting earlier findings in the Sakai-Sugimoto model (Lifschytz 2009, Thompson 2008). The work enables further holographic studies of magnetic effects in dense QCD matter relevant for heavy-ion collisions and compact stars.
