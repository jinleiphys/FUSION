# Renormalized critical dynamics and fluctuations in model A

> In the context of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, we explore the stochastic and dissipative relaxational dynamics of a non-conserved order parameter in a $λφ^4$ interaction. The cutoff of the theory is provided by the lattice spacing chosen for our numerical simulations. As a consequence, observables become dependent on that scale. We consider a possible first-order phase transition and an evolution close to a critical point. We demonstrate that using a lattice counterterm restores the expected behavior of the mean, variance and kurtosis: the mean and the variance become lattice spacing independent, and we recover the correct expectation value of the mean, the growth of the variance with the correlation length and the expected minimum in the kurtosis. Our findings hold true in equilibrium and during the dynamical relaxation, and therefore mark an important step towards a fully fluctuating fluid dynamical setup.

## Key claim
Using a lattice mass counterterm restores the expected lattice-spacing-independent behavior of the mean, variance, and kurtosis of a non-conserved order parameter in model A dynamics, both in equilibrium and during dynamical relaxation; however, the counterterm is derived perturbatively and its effectiveness near a critical point, where perturbation theory breaks down, is tested empirically.

## Method
The authors numerically solved the stochastic Ginzburg-Landau-Langevin equation for a scalar field with a λφ⁴ interaction (model A in the Hohenberg-Halperin classification) on a cubic lattice with periodic boundary conditions. They introduced a mass counterterm derived from perturbative renormalization to cancel the ultraviolet cutoff dependence introduced by the finite lattice spacing, and compared the bare and renormalized results for the mean, variance, and kurtosis of the volume-averaged order parameter in equilibrium and during relaxational evolution.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing values used: dx = 0.625, 0.417, 0.3125, 0.25, and 0.208 (dimensionless units, corresponding to N = 32, 48, 64, 80, and 96 cells per side for a cubic system of side L = 20).
- Coupling constant λ = 0.25; temperature T = 1; damping coefficient η = 1; renormalization scale M = 1.
- For the chirally broken phase: ε = -1; for near-critical cases: ε = 0.1 and ε = 0.01.
- Number of noise configurations: 10⁴.
- Time step dt = 0.1; equilibrium reached by t_fin = 60 for most cases, extended to t_fin = 300 for the ε = 0.01 renormalized kurtosis study.
- Constants in the counterterm: Σ ≈ 3.1759, ζ ≈ 0.09.

## Context
This work extends the lattice renormalization approach of Cassol-Seewald (2007), who demonstrated restoration of lattice-spacing independence for the mean in equilibrium in a double-well potential, to also include the variance and kurtosis, and to dynamical relaxation near a critical point. It addresses the unphysical lattice-spacing dependence that arises in stochastic hydrodynamics approaches to heavy-ion collisions, which has previously been treated by noise smearing (Murase, 2016; Hirano, 2018) or coarse-graining (Nahrgang, 2017; Bluhm, 2018). The results enable the future coupling of renormalized chiral dynamics to a full hydrodynamic evolution, as in nonequilibrium chiral fluid dynamics (Nahrgang, 2011), and ultimately application to model H dynamics for the QCD critical point.
