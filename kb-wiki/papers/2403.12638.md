# An example of the convergence of hydrodynamics in strong external fields

> The anti-de-Sitter/conformal field theory (AdS/CFT) correspondence is used to provide an estimate of the radius of convergence of the linearized gradient expansion of the hydrodynamic description of $\mathcal{N}=4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills (SYM) theory minimally coupled to $U(1)$ gauge theory subjected to strong magnetic fields. The results of this work demonstrate that the dispersion relations of hydrodynamic modes continue to converge for magnetic field strengths far beyond those values for which a hydrodynamic description is expected. For magnetic field strengths much larger then the temperature scale the bulk dual interpolates between AdS$_{4+1}$ and the product of a Bañados-Teitelboim-Zanelli (BTZ) black hole and a two dimensional manifold (BTZ$_{2+1}\times \mathbb{R}^2$), and may be regarded as a renormalization group flow of the $3+1$ dimensional CFT to a $1+1$ dimensional CFT. In this regime, we clarify past literature on the quasi-normal mode (QNM) spectrum of bulk scalar fields by introducing a new way to classify the behavior of QNM collisions in the complex frequency and momentum plane.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the hydrodynamic description of strongly coupled plasma remains convergent for magnetic field strengths far beyond the regime where hydrodynamics is conventionally expected to be valid (B/T² ≪ 1), using the AdS/CFT correspondence to compute the radius of convergence of the linearized gradient expansion.

## Method

The authors construct anisotropic black brane solutions of Einstein-Maxwell theory in asymptotically AdS₅ spacetime, which are holographically dual to thermal states of N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory minimally coupled to U(1) gauge theory in strong external magnetic fields. They compute the quasinormal mode spectrum of linearized fluctuations about these backgrounds and determine the critical points of the spectral curve, points where the implicit function P(ω,q)=0 and its frequency derivative vanish simultaneously, using a pseudo-spectral discretization and a two-dimensional Newton-Raphson method. The critical points provide the radius of convergence of the hydrodynamic dispersion relations via the theory of plane analytic curves, following the method of Grozdanov (2019).

## Key numbers

- For B/T² ≪ 1, the energy density follows ε/B² = a (B/T²)^b with a = 292.206 and b = -2.000, matching the Schwarzschild black brane result ε = 3(πT)⁴ to a relative difference of 7.135×10⁻⁵.
- For B/T² ≫ 1, the energy density follows ε/B² = a + b (B/T²)^c with a = 0.374, b = 7.027, and c = -1.007, consistent with the BTZ₂₊₁ × ℝ² geometry.
- The numerical solver achieves convergence when the residual measure ς = (1/3N) Σᵢ Σⱼ |Eⁱⱼ| ≤ 5×10⁻¹², with constraint residuals routinely of order O(e⁻²⁰).
- Finite difference derivatives use δ = 10⁻¹⁵ for computing the Jacobian in the Newton-Raphson method.

## Context

This work extends the program of Grozdanov (2019) and Grozdanov (2019) on characterizing hydrodynamic breakdown via spectral curve analysis, applying it to the case of strong external magnetic fields where the bulk dual interpolates between AdS₅ and BTZ₂₊₁ × ℝ², representing a renormalization group flow from a 3+1 dimensional CFT to a 1+1 dimensional CFT. The results clarify past literature on quasi-normal mode collisions in the complex frequency and momentum plane, and are relevant for understanding the regime of validity of chiral magnetohydrodynamics used to model the chiral magnetic effect in heavy ion collisions, where magnetic field strengths can reach B/T² ∼ 1/10 to 1 as noted by Fukushima (2008).
