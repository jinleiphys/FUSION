# Wake potential in collisional anisotropic quark-gluon plasma

> Within the framework of Boltzmann transport equation with a Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook (BGK) collisional kernel, we study the wake potential induced by fast partons traveling through the high-temperature QCD plasma which is anisotropic in momentum-space. We calculate the dielectric response function of a collisional anisotropic quark-gluon plasma (AQGP) for small $ξ$ (anisotropic parameter) limit. Using this, the wake potential for various combinations of the anisotropy parameter ($ξ$) and the collision rate ($ν$) is evaluated both for parallel and perpendicular directions of motion of the fast parton. It is seen that the inclusion of the collision modifies the wake potential and the amount as well as the nature of the potential depends on the combinations of $ξ$ and $ν$

## Key claim

The paper shows that including collisions (via a BGK kernel) in an anisotropic quark-gluon plasma modifies the wake potential induced by a fast parton, with the depth and oscillatory nature of the potential depending on the specific combination of the anisotropy parameter (ξ) and the collision rate (ν), but only for small values of the anisotropy parameter (ξ).

## Method

The authors start from the Boltzmann transport equation with a Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook (BGK) collisional kernel to derive the gluon polarization tensor for a momentum-space anisotropic quark-gluon plasma. They calculate the dielectric response function analytically to linear order in the small anisotropy parameter ξ, then use this dielectric function in the Poisson equation to obtain the wake potential for a fast parton moving with constant velocity. The wake potential is evaluated numerically for both parallel and perpendicular directions of the parton's motion relative to the anisotropy axis, for two parton velocities (v = 0.55 and v = 0.99) and various combinations of ξ and ν.

## Key numbers

- Parton velocities considered: v = 0.55 and v = 0.99 (in units of c).
- Anisotropy parameter values used: ξ = 0 (isotropic), ξ = 0.4, and ξ = 0.8 (within the small ξ limit).
- Collision rate values used: ν = 0 (collisionless), ν = 0.2, and ν = 0.8 (in units of the Debye mass m_D).
- The isotropic Debye mass m_D is defined as m_D² = -(g²/2π²)∫₀^∞ dp p² df_iso(p²)/dp.
- The scaled wake potential Φ^a_0 is given by (2π²/m_D)Φ^a.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of wake potentials in isotropic collisional QGP (Can. J. Phys. 1982, J. Phys. G 2007) and in collisionless anisotropic QGP (Mandal and Roy, Phys. Rev. D 2012) by including both anisotropy and collisions simultaneously. The results enable reevaluation of heavy quarkonium suppression and dihadron correlations in heavy-ion collisions, as the depth of the wake potential's negative minimum affects the binding of diquarks and the dissociation temperature required to separate heavy quark bound states.
