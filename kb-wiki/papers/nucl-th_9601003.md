# Green Function Monte Carlo Method for Excited States of Quantum System

> A novel scheme to solve the quantum eigenvalue problem through the imaginary-time Green function Monte Carlo method is presented. This method is applicable to the excited states as well as to the ground state of a generic system. We demonstrate the validity of the method with the numerical examples on three simple systems including a discretized sine-Gordon model.

# Green Function Monte Carlo Method for Excited States of Quantum System

## Key claim
The paper presents a novel extension of the imaginary-time Green function Monte Carlo method that can compute excited states of quantum systems, not just the ground state, by introducing a step-wise orthogonalization procedure that filters out each successive eigenstate. The method is claimed to converge to true eigenvalues for any excited state in the large-sample limit, though the author notes convergence is slower for higher states and that the method is exact only in the first line of the evolution equation, with the sampling-based implementation including contamination from lower eigenstates.

## Method
The method works in configuration space using imaginary-time evolution, where the standard Green function Monte Carlo evolution operator exp(-Δτ H) is applied iteratively. For excited states, the author introduces a Gram-Schmidt-like orthogonalization at each time step that subtracts contributions from all previously converged lower eigenstates, using eq. (8) to recursively filter out the α-th energy eigenstate. The wave functions are represented by sampling points with associated sign information (s = ±1) via importance sampling, and the Metropolis algorithm generates new sampling sets at each evolution step. Energy eigenvalues are computed from the ratio of integrals involving H exp(-Δτ H) and exp(-Δτ H) evaluated with the sampling points and signs.

## Key numbers
- Morse oscillator: first five eigenvalues with Δτ = 0.5, M = 200, N = 400 gave E₁ = -7.00, E₂ = -5.29, E₃ = -3.90, E₄ = -2.86, E₅ = -1.84; with Δτ = 0.2, M = 1000, N = 80 gave E₁ = -7.01, E₂ = -5.27, E₃ = -3.79, E₄ = -2.56, E₅ = -1.53; exact analytical values are (-7.031, -5.281, -3.781, -2.531, -1.531)
- Statistical errors estimated at 0.2% at most for both Morse oscillator calculations
- Gaussian-coupled two-particle system (v₀ = 1, a = 0.5): first five eigenvalues with Δτ = 0.5, 2M = 1600, Nₛ = 40 are E₁ = 2.44, E₂ = 3.34, E₃ = 3.43, E₄ = 3.56, E₅ = 3.69; corresponding 4S² values are 0.11, 7.56, 0.73, 7.27, 0.28
- Sine-Gordon model: calculations performed for N = 4, 6, 8 oscillators with Δτ = 0.5, sampling number NM = 360N, iteration number Nₛ = 400

## Context
This work extends the standard imaginary-time Green function Monte Carlo method, which had previously been applicable only to ground state calculations, to excited states of Hamiltonian systems. The author cites prior applications of stochastic quantization to problems from quantum chromodynamics to plasmon oscillation (BI92, CR79, PC94), and envisions applications in non-relativistic quantum few-body problems (ZK81, LP81, SW93) and lattice field theories (LA94). The sine-Gordon results are compared to semi-classical trace formula calculations by Dashen, Hasslacher, and Neveu (1975), and the numerical agreement supports their claim that the semi-classical results are exact in the full quantum sense. The method enables future calculations on more realistic many-body quantum systems and field theories where direct diagonalization becomes impractical for large degrees of freedom.
