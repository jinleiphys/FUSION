# Onset of $J/ψ$ Melting in Quark-Gluon Fluid at RHIC

> A strong $J/ψ$ suppression in central Au+Au collisions has been observed by the PHENIX experiment at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). We develop a hydro+$J/ψ$ model in which hot quark-gluon matter is described by the full (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamics and $J/ψ$ is treated as an impurity traversing through the matter. The experimental $J/ψ$ suppression pattern in mid-rapidity is reproduced well by the sequential melting of $χ_{\rm c}$, $ψ'$, and $J/ψ$ in dynamically expanding fluid. The melting temperature of directly produced $J/ψ$ is well constrained by the participant-number dependence of the $J/ψ$ suppression and is found to be about $2.T_{\rm c}$ with $T_{\rm c}$ being the pseudo-critical temperature.

## Key claim

The melting temperature of directly produced J/psi is constrained by the participant-number dependence of J/psi suppression and found to be about 2 T_c, where T_c is the pseudo-critical temperature, with the sequential melting of chi_c, psi', and J/psi in dynamically expanding fluid reproducing the experimental suppression pattern.

## Method

The authors developed a hydro+J/psi model in which hot quark-gluon matter is described by full (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamics, and J/psi, chi_c, and psi' are treated as impurities traversing through the matter. Initial J/psi's were distributed in the transverse plane according to the spatial distribution of binary collisions from the Glauber model, with transverse momentum distributed according to the PHENIX measurement. The survival probability was calculated using a threshold ansatz where the dissociation width is infinite above the melting temperature and zero below, with the melting temperatures treated as free parameters to fit the experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Best fit parameters: (T_J/psi, T_chi, f_FD) = (2.02 T_c, 1.22 T_c, 0.30) with chi^2 = 0.86 for N_part >= 50
- The onset of genuine J/psi suppression occurs around N_part ~ 160, where the highest temperature of the matter reaches T_J/psi
- A 6% change in the melting temperature of J/psi from the best fit value 2.02 T_c causes substantial deviation from experimental data
- For nuclear absorption cross section sigma_abs = 0 mb: (T_J/psi, T_chi, f_FD) = (2.00 T_c, 1.02 T_c, 0.35) with chi^2 = 0.97
- For sigma_abs = 2 mb: (T_J/psi, T_chi, f_FD) = (2.02 T_c, 1.02 T_c, 0.15) with chi^2 = 0.87
- The thermal width parameter alpha > 0.1 GeV does not reproduce the data well when using the parametrization Gamma_dis(T < T_J/psi) = alpha(T/T_c - 1)^2

## Context

This work extends the sequential melting scenario proposed for SPS energies (where chi_c and psi' melting was ascribed to J/psi suppression) to RHIC energies, incorporating for the first time a dynamical space-time evolution of the medium via full 3D relativistic hydrodynamics. The result that J/psi survives up to about 2 T_c is consistent with lattice QCD studies (lattice1, lattice2, lattice3) which suggested J/psi would survive even up to about 2 T_c while chi_c and psi' dissociate at different temperatures. This enables quantitative comparison between experimental suppression patterns and lattice QCD predictions for charmonia melting temperatures, and provides a baseline for future studies of charmonium regeneration, azimuthal anisotropy (v_2), and forward rapidity suppression in connection with color glass condensate effects.
