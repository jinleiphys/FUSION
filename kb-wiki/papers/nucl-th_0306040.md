# Field-theoretical three-body relativistic equations for the multichannel $πN <-> γN <-> ππN <-> γπN$ reactions

> A new kind of the relativistic three-body equations for the coupled $πN$ and $γN$ scattering reactions with the $ππN$ and $γπN$ three particle final states are suggested. These equations are derived in the framework of the standard field-theoretical $S$-matrix approach in the time-ordered three dimensional form. Therefore corresponding relativistic covariant equations are three-dimensional from the beginning and the considered formulation is free of the ambiguities which appear due to a three dimensional reduction of the four dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equations. The solutions of the considered equations satisfy the unitarity condition and are exactly gauge invariant even after the truncation of the of the multiparticle ($n>3$) intermediate states. Moreover the form of these three-body equations does not depend on the choice of the model Lagrangian and it is the same for the formulations with and without quark degrees of freedom. The effective potential of the suggested equations is defined by the vertex functions with two on-mass shell particles. It is emphasized that these INPUT vertex functions can be constructed from experimental data. Special attention is given to the construction of the intermediate on shell and off shell $Δ$ resonance states. These intermediate $Δ$ states are obtained after separation of the $Δ$ resonance pole contributions in the intermediate $πN$ Green function. The resulting amplitudes for the $Δ\Longleftrightarrow Nπ$; $Δ\Longleftrightarrow Nγ;$ $% Δ^{\prime}\Longleftrightarrow Δγ$ transition have the same structure as the vertex functions for transitions between the on mass shell particle states with spin 1/2 and 3/2.

## Key claim

The paper derives a new set of relativistic three-body equations for coupled πN, γN, ππN, and γπN reactions that are three-dimensional from the outset, avoiding ambiguities from reducing four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equations, and that remain exactly gauge invariant even after truncating multiparticle intermediate states with n>3.

## Method

The authors start from the standard field-theoretical S-matrix approach and use time-ordered three-dimensional reduction formulas to extract two external particles from asymptotic in/out states. They then apply spectral decomposition (off-shell unitarity conditions) to obtain quadratically nonlinear three-dimensional equations, which are subsequently linearized into Lippmann-Schwinger type equations. The effective potential is constructed from vertex functions with two on-mass-shell particles, and the intermediate Δ resonance states are obtained by separating the Δ resonance pole contributions in the intermediate πN Green function.

## Key numbers

- The paper presents a formal theoretical framework with no specific numerical results for cross sections, scattering amplitudes, or resonance parameters.
- Review-type paper: no original numerics.

## Context

This work extends the field-theoretical three-dimensional approach previously developed for two-body πN and NN scattering by the same authors (Machavariani and Faessler, earlier works) to include three-body final states ππN and γπN. It provides a unified framework that can describe multichannel reactions up to the threshold for creating a third pion, and is motivated by experimental proposals to determine the magnetic moment of the Δ+ resonance in the γp → γ'π°'p' reaction, as investigated by Kondratyuk and collaborators and by the A2/TAPS collaboration at MAMI (Kotulla et al.). The formulation enables future comparison with models that describe two-body πN and γN data without reproducing three-body γπN and ππN data.
