# Thermal hadron production in relativistic nuclear collisions: the hadron mass spectrum, the horn, and the QCD phase transition

> We present, using the statistical model, a new analysis of hadron production in central collisions of heavy nuclei. This study is motivated by the availability of final measurements both for the SPS (beam energies 20-160 AGeV) and for the RHIC energies ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=130 and 200 GeV) and by updates in the hadron mass spectrum, which is a crucial input for statistical models. Extending previous studies by inclusion of very high-mass resonances (m> 2 GeV), and the up-to-now neglected scalar $σ$ meson leads to an improved description of the data. In particular, the hitherto poorly reproduced energy dependence of the $K^+/π^+$ ratio at SPS energies ("the horn") is now well described through the connection to the hadronic mass spectrum and, implicitly, Hagedorn's limiting temperature. We thereby demonstrate the intimate connection between the horn and the QCD phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the previously poorly reproduced "horn" structure in the energy dependence of the $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio at SPS energies is now well described by the statistical model, but only after including very high-mass resonances (m > 2 GeV) and the scalar $\sigma$ meson in the hadronic mass spectrum, thereby connecting the horn to the QCD phase transition through Hagedorn's limiting temperature.

## Method

The authors performed a new statistical model analysis of hadron production yields in central heavy-ion collisions, fitting data from SIS to RHIC energies (beam energies 2 AGeV to $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV). They updated the hadronic mass spectrum by including all known resonances from the Particle Data Group compilation, including states above 2 GeV mass and the $\sigma$ meson ($f_0(600)$) with nominal mass 484 MeV and width 510 MeV, and performed thermal fits with temperature $T$ and baryochemical potential $\mu_b$ as free parameters.

## Key numbers

- The limiting temperature extracted from the parametrization is $T_{lim}$ = 164 MeV, slightly higher than the earlier value of 161 ± 4 MeV
- High-mass resonances (m > 2 GeV) increase calculated pion yields by about 13%, while the $\sigma$ meson adds a further 3.5% increase
- For $m_{\sigma}$ = 600 MeV, $\Gamma_{\sigma}$ = 600 MeV, the calculations produce about 1% fewer pions compared to the nominal $\sigma$ parameters
- High-mass resonances increase calculated $\Lambda$ hyperon production by about 22%, proton production by up to 6%, and kaon production by about 7%
- At top RHIC energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV), a fit of STAR yields alone gives $T$ = 162 MeV, $\mu_b$ = 32 MeV, $V$ = 2400 fm$^3$, with $\chi^2/N_{df}$ = 9.0/11
- The $\mu_b$ parametrization is: $\mu_b \mathrm{[MeV]} = \frac{1303}{1+0.286\sqrt{s_{NN}(\mathrm{GeV})}}$
- The $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio fit yields $\chi^2/N_{df}$ = 21/12 over the full energy range

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous statistical model analyses (Andronic et al. 2005) by incorporating an updated hadronic mass spectrum including the $\sigma$ meson, which had been neglected in earlier thermal model studies. The paper resolves a long-standing discrepancy where the horn structure in $K^+/\pi^+$ could not be reproduced by thermal models (Andronic et al. 2005, Becattini et al. 2004, Letessier and Rafelski 2005) or by hadronic cascade models (as discussed by NA49 collaboration 2006). The results support the interpretation that the chemical freeze-out curve reflects the QCD phase boundary, and provide predictions for LHC energies ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.5 TeV) including ratios such as $K^+/\pi^+$ = 0.164 and $\Lambda/\pi^-$ = 0.042.
