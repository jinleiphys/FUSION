# Heavy Quark Radiative Energy Loss in QCD Matter

> Heavy quark medium induced radiative energy loss is derived to all orders in opacity, $(L/λ_g)^n$. The analytic expression generalizes the GLV opacity expansion for massless quanta to heavy quarks with mass $M$ in a QCD plasma with a gluon dispersion characterized by an asymptotic plasmon mass, $m_g=gT/\sqrt{2}$. Remarkably, we find that the general result is obtained by simply shifting all frequencies in the GLV series by $(m_g^2+x^2 M^2)/(2 x E)$. Numerical evaluation of the first order in opacity energy loss shows that both charm and bottom energy losses are much closer to the incoherent radiation limit than light partons in nuclear collisions at both RHIC and LHC energies. However, the radiation lengths of heavy quarks remain large compared to nuclear dimensions and hence high $p_T$ heavy quark production is volume rather than surface dominated.

## Key claim

Heavy quark medium induced radiative energy loss can be derived to all orders in opacity by simply shifting all frequencies in the massless GLV series by \((m_g^2 + x^2 M^2)/(2 x E)\), a result that holds under the assumptions of high \(p_T\) eikonal jets and an asymptotic plasmon mass \(m_g = gT/\sqrt{2}\) for transverse gluon modes.

## Method

The authors generalized the GLV opacity expansion for massless partons to massive quarks by computing Feynman diagrams for associated radiation (zeroth order in opacity) and first-order in opacity induced radiation, incorporating both the heavy quark mass \(M\) and a finite gluon plasmon mass \(m_g\). They then extended the result to all orders in opacity using a reaction operator formalism, showing that the finite masses modify the phase factors and current amplitudes in a universal way via the frequency shift \(\Delta\omega = (m_g^2 + x^2 M^2)/(2 x E)\). Numerical evaluation of the first-order energy loss was performed for charm and bottom quarks under RHIC and LHC conditions using a Yukawa screened potential with Debye mass \(\mu = 0.5\) GeV and \(\alpha_s = 0.3\).

## Key numbers

- For charm quarks at RHIC with \(L/\lambda = 4\), \(\mu = 0.5\) GeV, and \(E \sim 5-15\) GeV, the first-order fractional energy loss \(\Delta E^{(1)}/E \approx 0.15\).
- For bottom quarks under the same conditions, \(\Delta E^{(1)}/E\) is about half that of charm, approximately \(0.075\).
- The Ter-Mikayelian effect changes \(\delta(\Delta E^{(1)}/E) < 0.05\) for heavy quarks, which is negligible on an absolute scale.
- Including finite kinematic bounds on \(|\mathbf{q}_1| < \sqrt{6ET}\) reduces \(\Delta E^{(1)}/E\) by less than \(10\%\).
- The effective static plasma opacity for light quark quenching in \(Au+Au\) at 130 GeV is in the range \(L/\lambda = 3-4\).

## Context

This work extends the GLV opacity expansion (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev, 2000-2001) from massless partons to heavy quarks, incorporating both the dead cone effect predicted by Dokshitzer and Kharzeev (2001) and the Ter-Mikayelian plasmon effect derived in the authors' earlier papers (Djordjevic and Gyulassy, 2003). The result enables quantitative predictions for open charm and bottom observables at RHIC and LHC, showing that heavy quark energy loss is closer to the incoherent Gunion-Bertsch limit than light parton energy loss, but that radiation lengths remain large compared to nuclear dimensions, implying volume-dominated rather than surface-dominated high \(p_T\) heavy quark production.
