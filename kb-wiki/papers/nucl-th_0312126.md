# Nonstrange and other unitarity partners of the exotic Theta+ baryon

> Given presently known empirical information about the exotic Theta+ baryon, we analyze possible properties of its SU(3)F partners, paying special attention to the nonstrange member of the antidecuplet N*. The modified PWA analysis presents two candidate masses, 1680 MeV and 1730 MeV. In both cases the N* should be highly inelastic. The theoretical analysis, based on the soliton picture and assumption of Gamma(Theta+) < 5 MeV, shows that most probably Gamma(N*) < 30 MeV. Similar analysis for Xi3/2 predicts its width to be not more than about 10 MeV. Our results suggest several directions for experimental studies that may clarify properties of the antidecuplet baryons, and structure of their mixing with other baryons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the state commonly identified as N(1710) is not the appropriate nonstrange partner of the exotic Θ⁺ baryon in the flavor antidecuplet; instead, two candidate masses near 1680 MeV and 1730 MeV are proposed, with the 1680 MeV candidate being more promising, though the analysis cannot definitively establish the existence of either resonance.

## Method

The authors performed a modified partial-wave analysis (PWA) of πN elastic scattering data, inserting a narrow resonance with Breit-Wigner form into the P₁₁ partial wave and refitting over the database to observe changes in χ². They also applied the chiral soliton approach with octet–antidecuplet mixing to calculate decay widths of the antidecuplet members, using coupling constants constrained by the assumed narrow width of the Θ⁺.

## Key numbers

- Two candidate masses for the nonstrange N* resonance: 1680 MeV and 1730 MeV, from modified PWA of πN elastic scattering
- For the 1680 MeV candidate, the elastic partial width Γ_el ≤ 0.5 MeV; for the 1730 MeV candidate, Γ_el ≤ 0.3 MeV
- Using the soliton picture with Γ(Θ⁺) < 5 MeV, the total width of N* is most probably Γ(N*) < 30 MeV
- For the Ξ₃/₂ (mass 1862 ± 2 MeV), the predicted width is not more than about 10 MeV
- For M_N* = 1680 [1730] MeV, the πΔ decay width Γ(N* → πΔ) ≈ 2.8 [3.5] MeV
- For G_at = 2.9 (compatible with Γ(Θ⁺) = 1 MeV), the KΛ decay width Γ(N* → KΛ) ≈ 0.70 [1.56] MeV for M_N* = 1680 [1730] MeV
- For G_at ≈ 2.9, the Ξ₃/₂ decay widths are Γ(Ξ₃/₂ → πΞ) ≈ 2.6 MeV and Γ(Ξ₃/₂ → K̅Σ) ≈ 2.0 MeV
- For G_at ≈ -1.4, the Ξ₃/₂ decay widths are Γ(Ξ₃/₂ → πΞ) ≈ 0.6 MeV and Γ(Ξ₃/₂ → K̅Σ) ≈ 23 keV

## Context

This work extends the chiral soliton prediction of Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997) that the Θ⁺ baryon belongs to a flavor antidecuplet with Jᴾ = 1/2⁺, and reexamines the identification of the nonstrange member N* with the PDG-listed N(1710) state. The analysis contradicts the assignment of N(1710) as the antidecuplet partner due to its large width (~100 MeV) compared to the narrow Θ⁺, and instead suggests narrow, highly inelastic states near 1680 or 1730 MeV. The results enable experimental searches for these narrow states in ππN, ηN, and KΛ final states, and provide predictions for the decay branching ratios of the Ξ₃/₂ (1862) that can be tested in future measurements.
