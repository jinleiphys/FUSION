# Jet Quenching: the medium modification of the single and double fragmentation functions

> The physics of the quenching of hard jets in dense matter is briefly reviewed. This is presented within the framework of the partonic medium modification of the fragmentation functions. Modifications in both deeply inelastic scattering (DIS) off large nuclei and high-energy heavy-ion collisions are presented.

## Key claim

Jet quenching and the modification of jet structure can be described through the medium modification of fragmentation functions, where a single unknown normalization constant fitted to one experimental data point allows prediction of both single and double hadron modifications across different systems and kinematic variables.

## Method

The author applies generalized factorization and twist expansion to calculate the medium modification of single and dihadron fragmentation functions in deeply inelastic scattering (DIS) off nuclei, then extends the formalism to heavy-ion collisions by including thermal gluon absorption effects. The next-to-leading twist contributions from multiple scattering of the struck quark off soft gluons inside the nucleus with induced gluon radiation are reorganized into effective modified fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

- In DIS off nuclei, the suppression of the single inclusive spectrum increases quadratically with the size of the nuclear target
- In Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, the suppression ratio R_AA for pi0's beyond p_T of 3 GeV and for charged hadrons beyond 6 GeV is almost constant and independent of p_T
- For the most central Au+Au events, R_AA assumes a value of barely one fifth
- The near side two particle correlation (I_AA) is moderately enhanced in central Au+Au collisions relative to p+p collisions, increasing with N_part due to increased total energy loss

## Context

This work extends the twist expansion formalism of Guo and Wang (2001) for single hadron fragmentation functions to dihadron fragmentation functions, building on earlier screened potential scattering models by Gyulassy (1993), Baier (1996), and Wiedemann (2000). The calculations are compared with experimental data from HERMES (Dinezza, 2004) for DIS and from STAR and PHENIX for heavy-ion collisions. The formalism enables predictions of both the suppression of single inclusive spectra and the modification of two-hadron correlations within a unified framework with a single fitted parameter.
