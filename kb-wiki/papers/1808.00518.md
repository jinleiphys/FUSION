# Exact restoration of Galilei invariance in density functional calculations with quantum Monte Carlo

> Galilean invariance is usually violated in self-consistent mean-field calculations that employ effective density-dependent nuclear forces. We present a novel approach, based on variational quantum Monte Carlo techniques, suitable to preserve this symmetry and assess the effect of its violation, seldom attempted in the past. To this aim, we generalize the linear optimization method to encompass the density-dependence of effective Hamiltonians, and study $^4$He, $^{16}$O, and $^{40}$Ca ground-state properties employing the Gogny interaction.

## Key claim

The authors present a variational quantum Monte Carlo (VMC) approach that can exactly restore Galilean invariance in density functional calculations using the Gogny effective interaction, overcoming the violation that is inherent in standard self-consistent mean-field treatments; the method is demonstrated for closed-shell nuclei up to \(^{40}\)Ca, but the authors note that the density-dependent term of the Gogny interaction must be modified to depend on the intrinsic density \(\rho^\alpha(\mathbf{R}_{ij}-\mathbf{R}_{\mathrm{CM}})\) rather than \(\rho^\alpha(\mathbf{R}_{ij})\), which introduces an \(A\)-body force that makes Hartree-Fock calculations impractical but is tractable in QMC.

## Method

The authors generalize the linear optimization method (LM) for wave function optimization in VMC to handle density-dependent Hamiltonians, by computing the single-nucleon densities for each variational parameter shift before each LM iteration. They use a trial wave function consisting of a single Slater determinant with single-particle orbitals expanded in cubic splines, and they project the wave function into the center-of-mass frame by subtracting the center-of-mass coordinate from all spatial coordinates. The Gogny D1S interaction is employed, with the delta function in the density-dependent term regularized by a Gaussian of width \(\mu_3 = 0.1\) fm.

## Key numbers

- \(^{4}\)He: VMC total energy \(E = -30.74(10)\) MeV, compared to HF value \(-30.30\) MeV and experimental \(-28.30\) MeV; point-proton radius \(\langle r_{\mathrm{pt}}^2\rangle_p = 3.60(1)\) fm\(^2\) (experimental 2.14 fm\(^2\) from Sick (2008))
- \(^{16}\)O: VMC total energy \(E = -128.8(5)\) MeV, compared to HF value \(-129.1\) MeV and experimental \(-127.6\) MeV; point-proton radius \(\langle r_{\mathrm{pt}}^2\rangle_p = 7.15(1)\) fm\(^2\) (experimental 6.77 fm\(^2\) from Sick (1970))
- \(^{40}\)Ca: studied but no numerical results reported in the truncated text
- The Gaussian regulator width for the density-dependent term is \(\mu_3 = 0.1\) fm, chosen because finite-size effects are small for \(\mu_3 \lesssim 0.15\) fm
- The linear method converges to the HF energy for \(^{4}\)He within approximately 10 optimization steps

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Galilean invariance violation in mean-field calculations, previously studied analytically by Schmid and collaborators (2001-2004) using harmonic oscillator wave functions with the Gogny interaction and the schematic Brink-Boeker interaction, but which could not be applied to Hartree-Fock wave functions with the full Gogny force. The present QMC approach enables self-consistent calculations with the Gogny D1S interaction while exactly preserving Galilean invariance, and it opens the possibility of studying the effects of this symmetry violation on binding energies, form factors, and spectral functions for nuclei up to mass \(A \approx 50\).
