# T=0 Neutron-Proton Correlations at High Angular Momenta

> The properties of T=0 neutron-proton correlations are discussed within the frame work of different model calculations. Single-j shell calculations reveal that the T=0 correlations remain up to the highest frequencies. They are more complex and cannot be restricted to L=0 pairs only. Whereas it may be difficult to find clear evidence for T=0 pairing at low spins, T=0 correlations are found to induce a new excitation scheme at high angular momenta.

## Key claim

Single-j shell calculations reveal that T=0 neutron-proton correlations remain up to the highest rotational frequencies, but they are more complex than T=1 correlations and cannot be restricted to L=0 pairs only; while clear evidence for T=0 pairing may be difficult to find at low spins, T=0 correlations induce a new excitation scheme at high angular momenta.

## Method

The paper presents single-j shell calculations in the f7/2 shell that diagonalize the two-body interaction and decompose the expectation value of the interaction with respect to contributions from different angular momenta J, relating a gap parameter ΔJT to the expectation energy. Cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations in R-space based on the Skyrme force were performed, extending the two-body particle-particle interaction to incorporate both T=0 and T=1 channels with only parity as a symmetry restriction. Cranked Woods-Saxon calculations with simultaneous T=0 and T=1 pairing and approximate particle number projection via the Lipkin-Nogami procedure were also carried out.

## Key numbers

- The T=0 pairing energy at I=24ħ in 48Cr has approximately the same value as the neutron-neutron (proton-proton) T=1 pairing at I=0ħ, which is 2.2 MeV.
- For 44Ti, the T=0 solution has a calculated quadrupole moment of approximately 50 eb, whereas the T=1 solution gives spherical shape.
- In total routhian surface calculations for super-deformed shapes, a pairing phase transition from T=1 to T=0 occurs at ħω ≈ 0.5 MeV when using strength GT=0 = 1.1 GT=1.
- The moment of inertia for a rotational band with T=0 pairing exceeds the value obtained for rigid rotation (no pairing correlations).

## Context

This work extends earlier generalized pairing theories from the 1960s and 1970s (Goodman 1979) and recent renewed interest following experimental studies of 100Sn and heavy N=Z nuclei (Engel 1996, Satula 1997, Goodman 1999). The paper contradicts the restricted L=0 treatment of T=0 correlations used in recent shell-model calculations of 48Cr (Povh 1998) and enables investigation of rotational bands beyond the terminating states in N=Z nuclei, particularly the new collective excitation mode where T=0 pairing allows smooth occupation of high-j orbitals.
