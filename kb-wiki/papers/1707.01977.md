# A Two-band Model for p-wave Superconductivity

> In this paper we study the effects of hybridization in the superconducting properties of a two-band system. We consider the cases that these bands are formed by electronic orbitals with angular momentum, such that, the hybridization $V(\mathbf{k})$ among them can be symmetric or antisymmetric under inversion symmetry. We take into account only intra-band attractive interactions in the two bands and investigate the appearance of an induced inter-band pairing gap. We show that (inter-band) superconducting orderings are induced in the total absence of attractive interaction between the two bands, which turns out to be completely dependent on the hybridization between them. For the case of antisymmetric hybridization we show that the induced inter-band superconductivity has a p-wave symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper shows that an inter-band p-wave superconducting gap can be induced purely through antisymmetric hybridization between two bands that each have only intra-band s-wave attractive interactions, with no direct attractive interaction between the bands. This induced p-wave gap depends on the difference between the two intra-band gaps and vanishes when the intra-band gaps have equal magnitude, regardless of hybridization strength.

## Method

The authors construct a two-band mean-field Hamiltonian with intra-band s-wave pairing gaps Δ_a and Δ_b, and a momentum-dependent hybridization term V(k) that can be either symmetric (V(-k)=V(k)) or antisymmetric (V(-k)=-V(k)). They diagonalize the Hamiltonian to obtain quasiparticle energies and derive self-consistent gap equations at zero temperature, solving them numerically for both symmetric and antisymmetric hybridization cases. For the antisymmetric case, they take a pure imaginary hybridization V(k)=iγ(k_x k_x + k_y k_y + k_z k_z) to preserve time-reversal symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Critical hybridization parameter α_c ≈ 1.08 for symmetric hybridization (from f(α) analysis), at which both intra-band gaps vanish
- For symmetric hybridization with constant V, critical hybridization V_c = ω_D (the Debye frequency), at which superconductivity is destroyed
- BCS-like result recovered at α→0: Δ(0) = 2ω e^{-1/λ}
- For symmetric hybridization with equal gaps, critical temperature expression: T_c = (2e^γ/π) √(ω_D² - V²) e^{-1/λ}, valid for V < ω_D
- Numerical solutions shown for parameters: X=10, λ_a=0.58, λ_b=0.6, μ̃_a=1.2, μ̃_b=1.6

## Context

This work extends prior studies of multi-band superconductivity in materials like MgB₂ (Choi, 2002) where hybridization between orbitals determines the number of distinct superconducting gaps. It builds on findings that symmetric hybridization suppresses intra-band superconductivity (Ramos, 2010) while antisymmetric hybridization can enhance it. The induced p-wave gap is compared to similar p-wave pairing gaps derived in semiconductor-superconductor hybrid setups proposed by Sau (2010) and Alicea (2011) for realizing topological superconductivity and Majorana fermions, as originally proposed by Kitaev (2001). The results enable controlled generation of p-wave pairing from conventional s-wave superconductors through band hybridization, relevant for topological quantum computing applications.
