# $χ$PT Calculations with two-pion Loops for S-wave $π^0$ Production in pp Collision

> The total cross section for the $pp \to pp π^0$ reaction at energies close to threshold is calculated within the frame of a chiral perturbation theory, taking into account tree and one loop diagrams up to chiral order D=2. Two-pion loop contributions dominate $π^0$ production at threshold. The calculated cross section reproduces data, both scale and energy dependence, fairly well.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the previous failure of chiral perturbation theory (χPT) calculations to reproduce the total cross section for the $pp \to pp \pi^0$ reaction near threshold was not due to limitations of the theory itself, but because two-pion loop contributions had been omitted; when these loop diagrams are included up to chiral order D=2, the calculated cross section reproduces the data fairly well.

## Method

The authors performed χPT calculations using the heavy-fermion formalism (HFF) for the pion-nucleon sector, including tree and one-loop diagrams up to chiral order D=2, involving only pions and nucleons. The production amplitude was factorized into contributions from impulse, rescattering, one-loop (two-pion exchange) terms, and short-range interaction terms, with the short-range parameter d1 fixed by modeling vector meson exchanges (rho and omega). Final state interactions (FSI) were treated approximately by assuming factorization of the S-wave production amplitude with an S-wave FSI factor identified with the on-mass-shell amplitude for πNN → πNN elastic scattering.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop contribution to the on-mass-shell conversion amplitude for π⁰p → π⁰p is T_L^(1) = 3m³/64πF⁴ = 0.1 fm.
- Off mass shell at q = (-m/2, -√(Mm)), the one-loop term becomes T_L^(1) ≈ 2.43 fm.
- The impulse and rescattering terms off mass shell are T_I^(1) = -1.21 fm and T_R^(1) = 0.54 fm, respectively.
- The ratios of the off-mass-shell contributions are M_I^(1) : M_L^(1) : M_R^(1) ≈ 5 : 2 : 1.
- The short-range interaction parameter d1 was determined to be d1 = 1.16 fm³.
- Without contributions from loop diagrams, the cross section is lower by a factor of approximately 2.5.
- The low energy coupling constants used are c'_1 = -1.63, c'_2 = 6.20, and c'_3 = -9.86.
- The pion decay constant F = 93 MeV, pion mass m = 135 MeV, nucleon mass M = 938 MeV, and axial vector coupling g_A = 1.26.

## Context

This work extends previous χPT calculations by Park (1996), Cohen (1996), and Sato (1997), which underestimated the cross section data by a factor of 3-6, by including two-pion loop diagrams that had been omitted. The results contrast with traditional one-boson exchange (OBE) model calculations (Lee 1993, Horowitz 1994, Gedalin 1996), where heavy meson exchanges resolved the discrepancy; the authors note that the two-pion loop mechanism simulates the t-pole term from the OBE model. The calculations enable future work to include contributions from other degrees of freedom such as the Delta (1232 MeV) nucleon isobar and to verify convergence of the HFF expansion at higher chiral orders (D=3).
