# Relative space-time asymmetries in pion and nucleon production in non-central nucleus-nucleus collisions at high energies

> We propose to use the ratio of the pion-proton correlation functions evaluated under different conditions to study the relative space-time asymmetries in pion and proton emission (pion and nucleon source relative shifts) in high energy heavy ion collision. We address the question of the non-central collisions, where the sources can be shifted spatially both in the longitudinal and in the transverse directions in the reaction plane. We use the RQMD event generator to illustrate the effect and the technique.

## Key claim

The ratio of pion-proton correlation functions evaluated under different cuts on the relative momentum component can measure the relative spatial shift between pion and proton sources in non-central nucleus-nucleus collisions, but the method cannot extract all four space-time shifts in the laboratory frame without additional assumptions.

## Method

The authors used the RQMD v1.08 event generator to simulate Au+Au collisions at 11.4 GeV/nucleon, selecting particles in the rapidity region 2.8 < y_lab < 3.2 with a narrow azimuthal sector (p_x > 0, |p_y/p_x| < 0.5). They created two event subsamples based on reaction plane orientation ("Ψ_r=0" and "Ψ_r=π") and calculated π⁺p correlation functions using the Lednicky-Lyuboshitz formalism including both Coulomb and strong final-state interactions, applying cuts on the sign of the relative momentum components k*_x and k*_z in the pair rest frame.

## Key numbers

- Bohr radius for the π⁺p system: a ≈ +222 fm; for π⁻p: a ≈ -222 fm
- In RQMD events, each 1 fm of source shift leads to approximately 0.9% change in the ratio of correlation functions at small k*
- For the "Ψ_r=0" subsample, the expected ratio R^{(-)}_z/R^{(+)}_z ≈ 1.11 and R^{(-)}_x/R^{(+)}_x ≈ 1.05 at small k*
- For the "Ψ_r=π" subsample, the expected ratio R^{(-)}_z/R^{(+)}_z ≈ 1.09 and R^{(-)}_x/R^{(+)}_x ≈ 0.99 at small k*
- Average pair velocity components in the center of mass system: V_z ≈ 0.89 and V_x ≈ 0.17
- Approximately 200K pairs analyzed in each subsample
- The analysis restricted pairs to k* < 15 MeV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical proposal by Lednicky, Lyuboshitz, and collaborators (1990s) for using non-identical particle correlations to study time delays in particle emission, and applies it specifically to the case of non-central collisions where sources are shifted both longitudinally and transversely in the reaction plane. The technique builds on earlier observations from two-pion interferometry in non-central collisions (Voloshin, 1996) which suggested that the pion source is effectively shifted relative to the proton source due to screening by nucleons in the flow direction. The method enables experimental extraction of relative source shifts between different particle species, which is important for testing models of thermalization and chemical equilibration in heavy ion collisions.
