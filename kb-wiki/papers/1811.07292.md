# Nucleon form factors and root-mean-square radii on a (10.8 fm$)^4$ lattice at the physical point

> We present the nucleon form factors and root-mean-square (RMS) radii measured on a (10.8 fm$)^4$ lattice at the physical point. We compute the form factors at small momentum transfer region in $q^2\le 0.102$ GeV$^2$ with the standard plateau method choosing four source-sink separation times $t_{\rm sep}$ from 0.84 to 1.35 fm to examine the possible excited state contamination. We obtain the electric and magnetic form factors and their RMS radii for not only the isovector channel but also the proton and neutron ones without the disconnected diagram. We also obtain the axial-vector coupling and the axial radius from the axial-vector form factor. We find that these three form factors do not show large $t_{\rm sep}$ dependence in our lattice setup. On the other hand, the induced pseudoscalar and pseudoscalar form factors show the clear effects of the excited state contamination, which affect the generalized Goldberger-Treiman relation.

## Key claim

The authors claim that on a (10.8 fm)^4 lattice at the physical point (pion mass 135 MeV), the electric, magnetic, and axial-vector form factors and their root-mean-square radii do not show large source-sink separation dependence, while the induced pseudoscalar and pseudoscalar form factors exhibit clear excited state contamination that affects the generalized Goldberger-Treiman relation.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using 2+1 flavor stout-smeared O(a)-improved Wilson-clover quarks and Iwasaki gauge action on a 128^3 x 128 lattice with lattice spacing a = 0.08457(67) fm. They computed nucleon two- and three-point correlation functions using the sequential source method with four source-sink separations t_sep/a = 10, 12, 14, 16 (0.84 to 1.35 fm) and employed the all-mode-averaging technique with deflated SAP-GCR to reduce computational cost. Form factors were extracted from plateau fits of ratios of three- to two-point functions at eight momentum transfers up to q^2 = 0.102 GeV^2, and root-mean-square radii were determined using linear, dipole, quadratic, and z-expansion fits.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass tuned to 135 MeV (physical point), compared to 146 MeV in previous work (Ishikawa 2018)
* Lattice volume (10.8 fm)^4 with spatial extent 128^3 sites and lattice spacing a = 0.08457(67) fm (a^{-1} = 2.333(18) GeV)
* Minimum momentum transfer q^2_min = 0.013 GeV^2, half of the previous work's value
* Nucleon mass M_N = 0.9416(110) GeV from smeared-local correlator fit
* Source-sink separations t_sep = 0.84, 1.01, 1.18, 1.35 fm (t_sep/a = 10, 12, 14, 16)
* Eight momentum transfer values: q^2 = 0, 0.013, 0.026, 0.039, 0.052, 0.064, 0.077, 0.102 GeV^2
* Renormalization factors Z_V = 0.95153(76)(1487) and Z_A = 0.9650(68)(95) from Schrodinger functional scheme

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Ishikawa (2018) on a (8.1 fm)^4 lattice with m_pi = 146 MeV, using a larger volume (10.8 fm)^4 at the physical pion mass (135 MeV) to reduce finite volume effects and achieve smaller minimum momentum transfer. The results are compared to experimental fits by Kelly (2004) for electromagnetic form factors and to experimental values from ep scattering and muonic hydrogen spectroscopy for the proton charge radius puzzle. The study enables model-independent lattice QCD determination of nucleon form factors and radii at the physical point, providing theoretical input for neutrino-nucleus scattering and muon capture experiments.
