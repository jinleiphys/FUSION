# Parton energy loss in glasma

> We study the synchrotron-like gluon emission in $AA$-collisions from fast partons due to interaction with the coherent glasma color fields. Our results show that for RHIC and LHC conditions the contribution of this mechanism to parton energy loss is much smaller than the radiative energy loss in the plasma phase.

## Key claim

The synchrotron-like gluon emission from fast partons interacting with coherent glasma color fields is much smaller than the radiative energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma phase for RHIC and LHC conditions, despite the very large Lorentz force (~5-10 GeV/fm) in the glasma. This smallness arises partly from strong finite-size effects that suppress the radiation rate by a factor of 3-5 when the gluon formation length is comparable to the glasma lifetime.

## Method

The authors model the glasma as a slab of thickness L ~ 2/Q_s with a time-dependent uniform color field, using the τ-dependence of the glasma energy density from lattice simulations by Lappi (2008). They evaluate the synchrotron-like gluon emission spectrum for the q → gq' transition using a quasiclassical formalism based on the small-angle approximation, with the S-matrix element expressed via two-dimensional Schrödinger-like equations for the transverse wave functions. The gluon emission spectra are computed for both RHIC (Q_s = 1 GeV) and LHC (Q_s = 1.4 GeV) conditions, with the Lorentz force determined from the glasma energy density and the coupling constant taken as g = 2 (α_s ≈ 0.318).

## Key numbers

- For RHIC conditions (Q_s = 1 GeV, L ≈ 0.4 fm): total energy loss ΔE ≈ 184, 234, and 276 MeV for quark energies E_q = 10, 20, and 50 GeV respectively (without kinematic constraint); with kinematic constraint ΔE ≈ 48, 49, and 45 MeV for the same energies
- For LHC conditions (Q_s = 1.4 GeV, L ≈ 0.28 fm): total energy loss ΔE ≈ 320, 414, and 495 MeV for E_q = 10, 20, and 50 GeV respectively (without kinematic constraint); with kinematic constraint ΔE ≈ 63, 59, and 39 MeV
- The glasma energy loss is smaller by a factor of ~10-20 compared to the induced gluon emission in the QGP, which gives ΔE ~ 5-15 GeV for RHIC and 10-30 GeV for LHC at E_q ~ 10-50 GeV
- Finite-size suppression factor S = 3-5 in the ω ≲ 5 GeV region dominating energy loss
- Quark mass taken as m_q = 0.3 GeV; gluon mass m_g = 0.75 GeV (with a test at m_g = 0.4 GeV giving only ~10-20% increase in ΔE)
- Increasing slab thickness to L = 3/Q_s increases energy loss by at most a factor ~1.5-2

## Context

This work extends the study of synchrotron-like gluon emission in uniform color fields (synQCD, 1998) to the glasma phase predicted by the Color Glass Condensate model (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1994; Kovchegov, 1996). It addresses an open question about additional mechanisms of parton energy loss beyond the dominant radiative energy loss in the QGP (BDMPS, 1997; LCPI, 1997; GLV, 2000; AMY, 2001) and elastic collisions (Ecol1, Ecol2). The results enable future work on jet structure at large angles (ridge effect and conical emission) where the wide-angle distribution of synchrotron-like gluon emission may be important, though accurate study would require calculations beyond the small-angle approximation.
