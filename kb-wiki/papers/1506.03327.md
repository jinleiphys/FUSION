# Quark production in heavy ion collisions I. Formalism and boost invariant fermionic light-cone mode functions

> We revisit the problem of quark production in high energy heavy ion collisions, at leading order in $α_s$ in the color glass condensate framework. In this first paper, we setup the formalism and express the quark spectrum in terms of a set of mode functions of the Dirac equation. We determine analytically their initial value in the Fock-Schwinger gauge on a proper time surface $Q_sτ_0\ll 1$, in a basis which makes manifest the boost invariance properties of this problem. We also describe a statistical algorithm to perform the sampling of the mode functions.

## Key claim

The paper sets up a formalism for computing quark production in heavy ion collisions at leading order in the strong coupling constant within the color glass condensate framework, but the actual result is limited to deriving the mode functions analytically only on the initial proper time surface where the proper time satisfies Q_s τ_0 ≪ 1, with numerical results deferred to a forthcoming publication.

## Method

The authors express the inclusive quark spectrum in terms of mode functions that are solutions of the Dirac equation in a classical color background field obtained from the Yang-Mills equations with light-cone color currents representing the two colliding nuclei. They introduce a new basis of mode functions that depend on proper time τ, spatial rapidity η, and transverse position x_⊥, indexed by transverse momentum k_⊥ and a wavenumber ν that is the Fourier conjugate to η, making boost invariance manifest. The initial values of these mode functions are determined analytically in the Fock-Schwinger gauge on the forward light-cone, and a statistical Monte-Carlo algorithm is described to sample the mode functions using random linear superpositions with Gaussian-distributed coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The initial condition for the mode functions is derived on a proper time surface satisfying Q_s τ_0 ≪ 1, where Q_s is the saturation scale.
- The computational cost of the direct deterministic algorithm scales as (N_x N_y)^2 N_η N_τ, where N_x, N_y are the transverse grid sizes, N_η is the number of rapidity points, and N_τ is the number of timesteps.
- The statistical sampling method replaces a power of N_x N_y with N_conf (the number of Monte-Carlo samples), making it advantageous when N_conf ≪ N_x N_y.
- The variance of the statistical estimator decreases as 1/N_conf, following from the central limit theorem.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of quark production in heavy ion collisions by Gelis, Kajantie, and Lappi (2005) that were limited by using a basis of mode functions expressed in Cartesian coordinates (making boost invariance non-obvious), restricting the sum over modes to a subset, and potential contamination from lattice doublers at high momentum. The new formalism enables a lattice implementation where the grid covers a fixed range in rapidity, with numerical results to be presented in a forthcoming paper. The approach is analogous to earlier studies of electron production in nuclear collisions by Baltz, Gelis, McLerran, and Peshier (2001) using the equivalent photon approximation.
