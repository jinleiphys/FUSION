# Elliptic Flow from Minijet Production in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We calculate the contribution to the elliptic flow observable v2 from two-particle correlations in minijet production in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. We use a minijet production cross section derived in a model inspired by saturation approach to high energy scattering. Resulting differential elliptic flow v2(pT) is an increasing function of pT for transverse momenta below the saturation scale Qs. At higher transverse momenta (pT > Qs) differential flow stops growing and becomes approximately constant, reproducing the elliptic flow saturation data reported by STAR. The centrality dependence of the minijet contribution to v2 is also in good agreement with the data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the elliptic flow observable \(v_2\) measured in heavy ion collisions can be largely explained by non-flow two-particle correlations from minijet production in the early stages of the collision, rather than solely by collective hydrodynamic expansion of a quark-gluon plasma. The authors claim that this minijet contribution is large enough to account for most of the high-\(p_T\) elliptic flow data from STAR, but they hedge that a more detailed numerical analysis is still needed to confirm this.

## Method

The authors calculate the contribution to the elliptic flow \(v_2\) from two-particle correlations in minijet production using a \(k_T\)-factorized expression for double inclusive gluon production, with unintegrated gluon distributions given by the quasi-classical Glauber-Mueller expression characterized by the saturation scale \(Q_s\). They derive the differential elliptic flow \(v_2(p_T)\) from the second-order cumulant of the two-particle correlation function, which they show is equivalent to the standard flow analysis method in the limit of large multiplicity. The model uses a cylindrical nucleus approximation and assumes that in any given event either \(y_1 \gg y_2\) or \(y_1 \ll y_2\) to simplify the calculation.

## Key numbers

* The differential elliptic flow \(v_2(p_T)\) is an increasing function of \(p_T\) for transverse momenta below the saturation scale \(Q_s\), and becomes approximately constant for \(p_T > Q_s\), saturating at \(p_T \approx 1.5\ \text{GeV}\) and remaining constant up to \(p_T \approx 4.5\ \text{GeV}\) (STAR data range).
* The fit to STAR data uses \(\alpha_s = 0.3\) and \(Q_s = 1\ \text{GeV}^2\), consistent with values used in saturation-inspired analysis of multiplicity data.
* The integrated flow scales as \(v_2(B) \sim 1/\sqrt{S_\perp Q_s^2} \sim 1/\sqrt{N_{part}}\), where \(S_\perp\) is the overlap transverse area and \(N_{part}\) is the number of participating nucleons.
* The two-particle correlation cross section in the collinear limit matches the collinear factorization result with a \(K\)-factor \(K_2 = 2\), giving \(\frac{d \sigma_{corr}}{d p_T^2 \, d y_1 \, d y_2} = K_2 \, \frac{9 \, \pi \, \alpha_s^2}{4 \, p_T^4} \, [xG (x, p_T^2)]^2\).
* The total multiplicity per unit rapidity is given by \(\frac{dN}{dy} = c \, \frac{S_\perp\, C_F\, Q_s^2}{\alpha_s\, 2\, \pi^2}\) with the gluon liberation coefficient \(c = 2 \ln 2\).

## Context

This work extends the saturation approach to high energy scattering, previously used to describe total charged multiplicity at RHIC by Dumitru and collaborators (2002), to the elliptic flow observable. It contradicts the interpretation that high-\(p_T\) elliptic flow is primarily due to hydrodynamic expansion of quark-gluon plasma, as suggested by hydrodynamic simulations by Huovinen and Teaney, and instead proposes that minijet correlations from the initial quasi-classical gluon fields can explain the saturation of \(v_2(p_T)\) at high \(p_T\) observed by STAR. The paper enables future work to disentangle the contributions of initial-state saturation effects from final-state collective effects in heavy ion collisions, and suggests that flow analysis could be used to determine nuclear saturation scales.
