# Radius-Flow Entanglement in Hadron States and Gravitational Form Factors

> We define an operational integer-$n$ lattice observable for state-dependent spatial entanglement in QCD hadrons and a falsifiable local-window analysis of its radius dependence. The observable is the vacuum-subtracted logarithmic radius derivative of the ball Rényi entropy, $\mathfrak{s}_n(R;h)=R\partial_RΔS_n(B_R;h)$, evaluated in a rest-frame momentum-projected one-hadron state. At fixed replica/cut prescription, $ΔS_n$ is a direct integer-$n$ replica ratio and $\mathfrak{s}_n$ is obtained as its correlated radius derivative; the intended first target is $n=2$, and spin averaging is performed only after forming the flow. Continuum trace-response identities motivate a trace-channel organization, but the proposed lattice observable and fits are defined directly at fixed integer $n$. We test boundary dominance by fitting local $R$ windows to a low-curvature remainder plus two candidate endpoint templates, $\mathfrak{t}_h^{(0)}(R)=R^3ρ_S(R)$ from the trace gravitational form factor $A^S(t)$ and $\mathfrak{t}_h^{(2)}(R)=R^3ρ_A(R)$ from the spin-2 gravitational form factor $A(t)$. A soft-wall AdS/QCD trace--energy calculation motivates this two-function basis and gives an optional model-dependent fixed-ratio benchmark; the lattice coefficients are left free. For representative nucleon dipole gravitational form factors, the pure endpoint-template peak scales are $R_{\rm peak}^{(0)}\simeq0.84~{\rm fm}$ and $R_{\rm peak}^{(2)}\simeq0.43~{\rm fm}$, so lattice data can distinguish scalar dominance, spin-2 dominance, genuine mixing, or failure of the boundary-dominance ansatz.

# Radius-Flow Entanglement in Hadron States and Gravitational Form Factors

## Key claim

The paper defines a lattice-QCD observable for the vacuum-subtracted spatial Rényi response of a momentum-projected hadron, and proposes that the radius derivative of this response can distinguish between scalar-dominance, spin-2-dominance, or mixed contributions from gravitational form factors, but only if the boundary-dominance ansatz holds, which the lattice data itself must test rather than assume.

## Method

The authors construct an integer-\(n\) replica observable on the lattice: the vacuum-subtracted logarithmic radius derivative of the ball Rényi entropy, \(\mathfrak{s}_n(R;h)=R\partial_R\Delta S_n(B_R;h)\), evaluated in a rest-frame momentum-projected one-hadron state. They test boundary dominance by fitting local \(R\) windows to a low-curvature remainder plus two candidate endpoint templates: \(\mathfrak{t}_h^{(0)}(R)=R^3\rho_S(R)\) from the trace gravitational form factor \(A^S(t)\) and \(\mathfrak{t}_h^{(2)}(R)=R^3\rho_A(R)\) from the spin-2 gravitational form factor \(A(t)\). A soft-wall AdS/QCD trace-energy calculation motivates this two-function basis but leaves the lattice coefficients free.

## Key numbers

- For representative nucleon dipole gravitational form factors, the pure endpoint-template peak scales are \(R_{\rm peak}^{(0)}\simeq 0.84~{\rm fm}\) (scalar template) and \(R_{\rm peak}^{(2)}\simeq 0.43~{\rm fm}\) (spin-2 template).
- The intended first numerical target is the two-sheet \(n=2\) measurement of the replicated source-sink correlator ratio.
- The expected finite-volume scaling for a rest-frame momentum eigenstate in a periodic volume \(L^3\) is \(\Delta S^{\cutC}_{n,L}(B_R;h,\lambda) = (1/L^3)\,\mathcal{F}^{\cutC}_{n}(R;h,\lambda) + \cdots\), with the leading coefficient having dimensions of volume.

## Context

This work extends the flux-tube entanglement construction of Amorosso et al. (2024), which used Polyakov-loop correlators for static \(Q\bar Q\) pairs in pure gauge theory, by replacing static sources with dynamical hadron source-sink operators. It provides a first-principles lattice target for questions usually discussed in approximate language such as partonic entanglement, small-\(x\) saturation, and rapidity-space entropy (Kharzeev, 2017; Gursoy, 2023). The two-dimensional QCD benchmark (Goykhman, 2015; Liu, 2022) gives analytic support for inverse-volume scaling after vacuum subtraction in an interacting confining gauge theory, but the four-dimensional exponent must be measured from lattice data rather than assumed.
