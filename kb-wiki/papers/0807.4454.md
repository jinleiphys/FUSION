# Paris nucleon-antinucleon potential constrained by recent antiprotonic-atom data and antineutron-proton total cross sections

> We report on an updated Paris nucleon-antinucleon optical potential. The long- and intermediate-range real parts are obtained by G-parity transformation of the Paris nucleon-nucleon potential based on a theoretical dispersion-relation treatment of the correlated and uncorrelated two-pion exchange. The short-range imaginary potential parametrization results from the calculation of the nucleon-antinucleon annihilation box diagram into two mesons with a nucleon-antinucleon intermediate state in the crossed channel. The parametrized real and imaginary short range parts are determined by fitting not only the existing experimental data included in the 1999 version of the Paris nucleon-antinucleon potential, but also the recent antiprotonic-hydrogen data and antineutron-proton total cross sections. The description of these new observables is improved. Only this readjusted potential generates an isospin zero 1S0, 52 MeV broad quasibound state at 4.8 MeV below the threshold. Recent BES data on J/psi decays could support the existence of such a state.

## Key claim

The updated Paris nucleon-antinucleon optical potential, constrained by recent antiprotonic-hydrogen data and antineutron-proton total cross sections, generates an isospin zero 1S0 quasibound state at 4.8 MeV below threshold with a 52 MeV width, a state absent in the 1999 version; the existence of such a state has some support from BES data on J/psi decays, though the low-energy pbar-p spectrum could also be reproduced using a T=1 S-wave model with no such bound state.

## Method

The long- and intermediate-range real parts of the optical potential are obtained by G-parity transformation of the Paris nucleon-nucleon potential based on a dispersion-relation treatment of correlated and uncorrelated two-pion exchange. The short-range imaginary potential parametrization results from calculation of the nucleon-antinucleon annihilation box diagram into two mesons with a nucleon-antinucleon intermediate state in the crossed channel. The parametrized real and imaginary short-range parts are determined by fitting 4259 data points used in the 1999 version plus 64 total antineutron-proton cross section data and ten antiprotonic-hydrogen level shifts and widths.

## Key numbers

* Best fit to the full data set yields chi-squared/data = 4.52 for the present potential, compared to 4.59 for the Paris 99 version.
* The isospin zero 1S0 quasibound state has binding energy 4.8 MeV and total width 52 MeV; this state is absent in the Paris 99, Paris 94, and Paris 82 potentials.
* For antiprotonic-hydrogen S-waves, the present model gives a spin-singlet shift of 0.778 keV and width of 0.519 keV (experimental: 0.440 plus or minus 0.075 keV shift, 0.60 plus or minus 0.12 keV width).
* For the 64 recent antineutron-proton total cross section data, chi-squared/data = 2.03 for the present model versus 4.29 for Paris 99.
* The spin-singlet (not Coulomb corrected) scattering length a(11S0) is 1.115 minus i 0.856 fm in the present work, compared to 0.844 minus i 0.329 fm in Paris 99.

## Context

This work extends the Paris nucleon-antinucleon potential lineage (Paris 82, Paris 94, Paris 99) by incorporating recent antiprotonic-hydrogen data from Augsburger (1999) and Gotta (1999) and antineutron-proton total cross sections from Iazzi (2000). The updated potential generates a near-threshold quasibound 11S0 state that was absent in all previous Paris versions, which the authors argue could explain the peak observed in J/psi to gamma pbar-p decays by the BES Collaboration (2003). This enables further study of nucleon-antinucleon bound states and resonances in reaction processes that select specific partial wave contributions, such as heavy meson decays.
