# Generalized polarizabilities of the nucleon in baryon chiral perturbation theory

> The nucleon generalized polarizabilities (GPs), probed in virtual Compton scattering (VCS), describe the spatial distribution of the polarization density in a nucleon. They are accessed experimentally via the process of electron-proton bremsstrahlung ($ep\to epγ$) at electron-beam facilities, such as MIT-Bates, CEBAF (Jefferson Lab), and MAMI (Mainz). We present the calculation of the nucleon GPs and VCS observables at next-to-leading order in baryon chiral perturbation theory (B$χ$PT), and confront the results with the empirical information. At this order our results are predictions, in the sense that all the parameters are well-known from elsewhere. Within the relatively large uncertainties of our calculation we find good agreement with the experimental observations of VCS and the empirical extractions of the GPs. We find large discrepancies with previous chiral calculations---all done in heavy-baryon $χ$PT (HB$χ$PT)---and discuss the differences between B$χ$PT and HB$χ$PT responsible for these discrepancies.

# Generalized polarizabilities of the nucleon in baryon chiral perturbation theory

## Key claim
The authors present a next-to-leading order calculation of nucleon generalized polarizabilities (GPs) and virtual Compton scattering (VCS) observables using baryon chiral perturbation theory (BχPT) that is predictive, meaning all parameters are fixed from other sources, and find good agreement with experimental data within relatively large theoretical uncertainties. This stands in contrast to previous heavy-baryon χPT (HBχPT) calculations, with which they find large discrepancies.

## Method
The authors compute the non-Born VCS amplitude and six skewed GPs using manifestly Lorentz-invariant BχPT with SU(2) chiral symmetry, including nucleon and Δ(1232) degrees of freedom. The calculation includes pion-nucleon loops at O(p³) and pion-Delta loops plus the Delta pole graph at O(p⁴/Δ), with renormalization performed using the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) scheme. The tensor decomposition of the VCS amplitude follows the 12-basis of Drechsel et al. (1997), and the results are compared with experimental VCS data from MAMI and with fixed-t dispersive relation calculations by Pasquini et al. (2001).

## Key numbers
- The dipole mass parameter for the magnetic γNΔ coupling is set to Λ² = 0.71 GeV², mimicking vector-meson dominance behavior.
- The pion decay constant used is fπ ≈ 92 MeV, and the chiral symmetry breaking scale is Λχ ∼ 4πfπ.
- The fine structure constant αem = e²/4π ≈ 1/137 is used in relations connecting GPs to static polarizabilities.
- The Delta-nucleon mass difference Δ = MΔ − M is treated as a light scale with mπ ≪ Δ ≪ Λχ.

## Context
This work extends previous BχPT calculations by Lensky et al. (2009, 2014, 2015) for real Compton scattering and forward doubly-virtual Compton scattering to the case of VCS and skewed GPs. It contradicts earlier HBχPT calculations by Hemmert et al. (1996, 1997, 1999) and Kao et al. (2002, 2004), which showed poor convergence, and instead finds better agreement with the empirically successful fixed-t dispersive framework of Pasquini et al. (2001). The results enable improved chiral effective-field theory predictions for ongoing and future VCS experiments at facilities such as MAMI and Jefferson Lab.
