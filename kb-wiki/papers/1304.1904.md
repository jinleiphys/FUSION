# Fate of chiral critical point under the strong isospin asymmetry

> We study the influence of the isospin asymmetry on the phase structure of strongly interacting quark matter near the critical point (CP) using a Ginzburg-Landau approach. The effect is found to be drastic, not only bringing about the shift of the location of the CP, but resulting in a rich phase structure in the vicinity of the CP. In particular, new tricritical and triple points emerge as soon as the isospin density becomes finite. Moreover, we find the CP being washed out from the phase diagram due to the stabilization of a homogeneous charged pion condensate when the isospin chemical potential exceeds a critical value. We derive a model-independent universal relation between the critical isospin chemical potential and the chiral condensate at the CP. We also study the effect of the $\mathrm{U}(1)_\mathrm{A}$ anomaly on the phase transition to the pion condensate in the vicinity of chiral crossover.

# Fate of chiral critical point under the strong isospin asymmetry

## Key claim

The chiral critical point (CP) is washed out from the QCD phase diagram when the isospin chemical potential exceeds a critical value, due to stabilization of a homogeneous charged pion condensate; this disappearance occurs at a universal ratio of critical isospin chemical potential to chiral condensate at the CP, independent of Ginzburg-Landau parameters to sixth order.

## Method

The author constructs a Ginzburg-Landau (GL) effective potential for two chiral four-vectors (σ, π) and their parity partner (η′, a), expanding systematically in the isospin chemical potential μI as a perturbative field. GL couplings are computed using a quark loop approximation, which is argued to be valid at high fugacity. The analysis is performed first at fourth order in fields to study the chiral crossover and pion condensate (PIC) transition, then extended to sixth order to capture the fate of the CP, restricting to the case of strong U(1)A breaking (the standard Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model).

## Key numbers

- Pseudocritical point at μI = 0: α₂^pc = 1.191 h^{2/3} α₄^{1/3}, with M_σ^pc = 1.54 h^{1/3} α₄^{1/6}, M_π^pc = 1.26 h^{1/3} α₄^{1/6}, and σ_pc = 0.630 h^{1/3} α₄^{-1/3}.
- Critical isospin chemical potential for PIC onset (case I, fourth order): μI^c = 1.782 h^{1/3} α₄^{-1/3} = 2.83 σ_pc.
- Tricritical point coordinates (case II, fourth order): μI^TCP = 2.02 h^{1/3} α₄^{-1/3} = 3.21 σ_pc; λ^TCP = 0.67 h^{1/3} α₄^{-1/3} = 1.06 σ_pc; ratio λ^TCP/μI^TCP = 0.33 (universal).
- Location of the CP at μI = 0 (sixth order): α₂^CP = (5/4)(3^{4/5}/2^{2/5}) h^{4/5} ≈ 2.28 h^{4/5}; α₄^CP = -5/(2^{1/5} 3^{3/5}) h^{2/5} ≈ -2.25 h^{2/5}; chiral condensate σ₀ = 0.822 h^{1/5}.
- Critical isospin chemical potential for CP disappearance: μI^c = 1.48 σ₀ (μI² = 1.477 h^{2/5}), a universal ratio independent of GL parameters.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous GL analysis (Iwata and Abuki, 2012) to the case of finite quark mass and finite isospin asymmetry. It builds on earlier studies of the QCD critical point (Asakawa and Yazaki, 1989) and of pion condensation at finite isospin density (Son and Stephanov, 2000; He, Jin, and Zhuang, 2005). The results are consistent with model calculations by Ohnishi et al. (2011) showing the CP shifts to lower temperature and higher chemical potential under isospin asymmetry, and with Ueda (2013) showing the CP can touch the μ axis. The universal relations derived enable model-independent predictions for the behavior of the CP under isospin imbalance, and the work opens the question of how inhomogeneous phases (Nakano and Tatsumi, 2004; Nickel, 2009) and three-flavor effects (Hatsuda, Tachibana, and Yamamoto, 2006) modify these conclusions.
