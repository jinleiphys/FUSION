# Fermion self-energy in magnetized chirally asymmetric QED matter

> The fermion self-energy is calculated for a cold QED plasma with chiral chemical potential in a magnetic field. It is found that a momentum shift parameter dynamically generated in such a plasma leads to a modification of the chiral magnetic effect current. It is argued that the momentum shift parameter can be relevant for the evolution of magnetic field in the chirally asymmetric primordial plasma in the early Universe.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in a cold QED plasma with a chiral chemical potential in a magnetic field, interactions dynamically generate a momentum shift parameter that modifies the chiral magnetic effect current; however, the NJL model results are regularization-dependent, and the more robust QED one-loop calculation shows the effect is several orders smaller than the shift produced by an ordinary chemical potential.

## Method

The author first analyzes a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a point-like four-fermion interaction in a magnetic field, solving the gap equation in the one-loop approximation using both cutoff and proper-time regularization schemes. Then, a renormalizable QED calculation is performed in the weak-field limit (linear in magnetic field) at zero temperature, computing the one-loop fermion self-energy and solving for the modified dispersion relations and Fermi surfaces for left- and right-handed fermions.

## Key numbers

- The momentum shift parameter in the NJL model with cutoff regularization is approximately $\kappa = - g \mu_5 s_\bot / (\Lambda l)^2 / (1 - a g)$, where $g \equiv G_{\rm int}\Lambda^2/(4\pi^2)$, $a=2(\sqrt{2}-1)$, and $l=1/\sqrt{|eB|}$ is the magnetic length.
- In the proper-time regularization of the NJL model, $\kappa = - g \mu_5 s_\bot / (\Lambda l)^2$.
- The QED calculation uses a magnetic field strength $eB = 0.01 \mu_5^2$ and fine-structure constant $\alpha = 1/137$.
- The Fermi-surface shift induced by the magnetic field in QED is of order $\alpha eB/(\pi \mu_5)$, which is several orders weaker than the shift produced by an ordinary chemical potential as studied in chiral-shift-2.
- The uniform shrinking (expansion) of the Fermi surface for right- (left-)handed particles is of order $\alpha/(2\pi\sqrt{1-p_\bot^2/\mu_5^2})$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of chiral shift generation in magnetized relativistic matter, which were performed using NJL-like models (chiral-shift-1, chiral-shift-2) and QED with an ordinary chemical potential (chiral-shift-QED, Gorbar 2013, Xia 2014), to the case of a chiral chemical potential $\mu_5$. The results suggest that interaction-induced corrections to the chiral magnetic effect involve higher Landau levels, not just the lowest Landau level as in the standard CME (Fukushima 2008, Kharzeev 2007), and could be relevant for magnetic field evolution in the early Universe plasma, heavy-ion collisions, or Weyl semimetals.
