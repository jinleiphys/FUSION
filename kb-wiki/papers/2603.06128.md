# Inverse-mapped density-dependent relativistic mean-field inference of the neutron-star equation of state with multi-messenger constraints

> We perform a Bayesian inference of the equation of state (EOS) of cold dense matter within a density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DD-RMF) model. An explicit inverse-mapping procedure reconstructs the density-dependent couplings from a physically interpretable ten-dimensional parameter set while enforcing thermodynamic consistency together with stability and causality conditions. The EOS is constrained by complementary multi-messenger data including chiral effective field theory calculations at low density, heavy-ion collision flow information at intermediate densities, NICER mass-radius posteriors, and the existence of approximately two-solar-mass pulsars. The combined constraints strongly restrict both isoscalar and isovector sectors. In particular, the chiral effective field theory band favors a relatively soft symmetry-energy slope around 38 MeV, corresponding to a compact canonical neutron-star radius of about 11.6 km. To reconcile the intermediate-density softness suggested by heavy-ion data with the high-density stiffness required by massive pulsars, the posterior prefers a moderately large Dirac effective mass at saturation together with correlated high-density limits of the scalar and vector couplings. The resulting sound-speed profile remains causal and shows significant stiffening above the conformal limit at several times nuclear saturation density, indicating strongly interacting matter in neutron-star cores. Evidence diagnostics indicate strong compatibility among the adopted constraints within the present DD-RMF framework.

# Inverse-mapped density-dependent relativistic mean-field inference of the neutron-star equation of state with multi-messenger constraints

## Key claim

The paper claims that a Bayesian inference framework using a density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DD-RMF) model with an explicit inverse-mapping procedure can simultaneously satisfy chiral effective field theory, heavy-ion collision, NICER, and massive pulsar constraints, but this compatibility is conditional on the specific DD-RMF model class and adopted priors, with the posterior favoring a moderately large Dirac effective mass at saturation (M*/M ~ 0.64) and correlated high-density limits of scalar and vector couplings to reconcile intermediate-density softness with high-density stiffness.

## Method

The authors perform Bayesian inference using nested sampling (MultiNest) within a DD-RMF model, where the density-dependent couplings are reconstructed from a 10-dimensional physically interpretable parameter set via an exact inverse-mapping procedure that enforces thermodynamic consistency, stability, and causality. The equation of state is constrained by combining low-density chiral effective field theory bands, intermediate-density heavy-ion collision flow information, NICER mass-radius posteriors for PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0740+6620, and PSR J0437-4715, and a soft penalty for maximum masses below approximately two solar masses. The posterior exploration yields reconstructed coupling shape functions, equation-of-state bands, and sound-speed profiles, with Bayesian evidence diagnostics used to assess dataset compatibility.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry-energy slope: L = 38 (+11, -7) MeV at 68% confidence from the combined analysis
- Canonical neutron-star radius: R_1.4 ~ 11.6 km (correlated with the soft symmetry-energy slope)
- Dirac effective mass at saturation: M*/M = 0.638 (+0.008, -0.012) at 68% confidence
- Saturation density: n_0 = 0.1503 (+0.0031, -0.0025) fm^{-3} at 68% confidence
- Incompressibility: K_0 = 241 (+12, -13) MeV at 68% confidence
- Symmetry energy curvature: K_sym = -84 (+44, -30) MeV at 68% confidence
- Asymptotic scalar coupling limit: f_sigma,infinity = 0.802 (+0.018, -0.020) at 68% confidence
- Asymptotic vector coupling limit: f_omega,infinity = 0.787 (+0.016, -0.020) at 68% confidence
- Asymptotic isovector coupling limit: f_rho,infinity = 0.264 (+0.062, -0.041) at 68% confidence
- Sound speed exceeds the conformal limit c_s^2 = 1/3 around n ~ 3 n_0 (several times nuclear saturation density)
- Bayesian evidence diagnostics yield ln R >= 9 for the combined NS + chiEFT + HIC sectors

## Context

This work extends the line of Bayesian equation-of-state inference using covariant density functional theory, building on the Typel-Wolter (1999) density-dependent RMF parametrization and the DD-ME2 functional of Lalazissis (2005). It directly incorporates the chiral EFT bands from Keller (2023) and the heavy-ion collision flow constraints from Danielewicz (2002) and the community synthesis of Sorensen (2024). The inverse-mapping procedure distinguishes this approach from earlier Bayesian analyses such as Huth (2022) and Koehn (2025) by enforcing that every sampled equation of state exactly matches specified nuclear-matter properties at saturation, enabling transparent decoupling of low-density, intermediate-density, and high-density physics. The results enable future work to test whether the inferred coupling functions and sound-speed profile remain consistent with additional constraints from gravitational-wave tidal deformabilities and perturbative QCD bounds.
