# Equation of state at finite baryon density based on lattice QCD

> We employ the lattice QCD data on Taylor expansion coefficients to extend our previous parametrization of the equation of state to finite baryon density. When we take into account lattice spacing and quark mass dependence of the hadron masses, the Taylor coefficients at low temperature are equal to those of hadron resonance gas. Thus the equation of state is smoothly connected to the hadron resonance gas equation of state at low temperatures. We also show how the elliptic flow is affected by this equation of state at the maximum SPS energy.

# Equation of state at finite baryon density based on lattice QCD

## Key claim
The lattice QCD Taylor expansion coefficients at low temperature, once corrected for lattice spacing and quark mass effects via a 30 MeV temperature shift, match the hadron resonance gas values, enabling a parametrized equation of state that is smoothly connected to the hadron resonance gas at low temperatures. At the maximum SPS energy, the elliptic flow is almost insensitive to whether the equation of state features a crossover or a first-order phase transition, which is problematic for experimental searches for the critical point.

## Method
The authors used lattice QCD Taylor expansion coefficients (second, fourth, and sixth order in baryon and strangeness chemical potentials) from the p4 action and HISQ action calculations. They shifted the p4 data points below 206 MeV by 30 MeV and the 209 MeV point by 15 MeV toward lower temperature to account for discretization effects, then parametrized the shifted coefficients using inverse polynomials matched to hadron resonance gas values at a switching temperature. The resulting equation of state was used in a boost-invariant ideal hydrodynamic model to compute elliptic flow in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 17 GeV.

## Key numbers
- The temperature shift applied to p4 lattice data to approximate continuum limit was 30 MeV for points below 206 MeV, and 15 MeV for the 209 MeV point.
- The parametrization required that Taylor coefficients reach 95% of their Stefan-Boltzmann value at 800 MeV temperature.
- Elliptic flow calculations were performed at impact parameter b = 7 fm for sqrt(s_NN) = 17 GeV Pb+Pb collisions.
- Freeze-out temperatures used were 130 MeV for the lattice-based equation of state and 120 MeV for the bag model equation of state.
- Isentropic curves were shown at entropy per baryon ratios s/n_B = 400, 65, and 40, corresponding to collision energies sqrt(s_NN) = 200, 39, and 17 GeV respectively.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier parametrization of the equation of state at zero chemical potential (Huovinen, 2009) to finite baryon density using lattice QCD Taylor coefficients from the most comprehensive analysis available at the time (Cheng, 2008; Miao, 2008). It contrasts with bag model equations of state featuring a first-order phase transition, and shows that at SPS energies the elliptic flow does not distinguish between a crossover and a first-order transition, unlike at RHIC energies where proton elliptic flow was sensitive to the equation of state (Huovinen, 2005; Kolb, 2000). The work enables more realistic hydrodynamic simulations at finite baryon density but identifies the need for a smoother parametrization to avoid unphysical structure near the transition temperature.
