# Constraints on T-Odd, P-Even Interactions from Electric Dipole Moments

> We construct the relationship between nonrenormalizable,effective, time-reversal violating (TV) parity-conserving (PC) interactions of quarks and gauge bosons and various low-energy TVPC and TV parity-violating (PV) observables. Using effective field theory methods, we delineate the scenarious under which experimental limits on permanent electric dipole moments (EDM's) of the electron, neutron, and neutral atoms as well as limits on TVPC observables provide the most stringent bounds on new TVPC interactions. Under scenarios in which parity invariance is restored at short distances, the one-loop EDM of elementary fermions generate the most severe constraints. The limits derived from the atomic EDM of $^{199}$Hg are considerably weaker. When parity symmetry remains broken at short distances, direct TVPC search limits provide the least ambiguous bounds. The direct limits follow from TVPC interactions between two quarks.

## Key claim

Under the scenario where parity symmetry is restored at short distances (Scenario A), the one-loop electric dipole moment of elementary fermions provides the most stringent constraints on new time-reversal violating parity-conserving (TVPC) interactions, while many-quark contributions to the neutron and atomic EDMs are suppressed by at least five orders of magnitude; under the alternative scenario where parity remains broken at short distances (Scenario B), direct TVPC search limits provide the least ambiguous bounds and EDM limits cannot be used to derive unambiguous information about TVPC new physics.

## Method

The authors construct the relationship between nonrenormalizable, effective, time-reversal violating parity-conserving interactions of quarks and gauge bosons and various low-energy TVPC and TV parity-violating observables using effective field theory (EFT) methods. They expand the effective Lagrangian in inverse powers of the new physics mass scale Lambda_TVPC, identify the lowest-dimension TVPC operators at dimension seven, and compute their contributions to electric dipole moments of elementary fermions, neutrons, and atoms through one-loop and two-loop radiative corrections involving Standard Model gauge bosons. They also relate the effective TVPC and TVPV operators to hadronic coupling constants (grhobar for rho-meson exchange and gpibar for pion exchange) using the quark model, factorization, and current algebra.

## Key numbers

* Electron EDM limit: |d_e| < 4 x 10^{-27} e-cm, yielding a lower bound on the new physics scale Lambda_TVPC > 10^{14} kappa^2 GeV under tree-level relations (where kappa^2 ~ 1 for strong new physics)
* Under Scenario A, the one-loop elementary fermion EDM implies Lambda_TVPC > 260 TeV from d_e limits and > 110 TeV from single-quark neutron EDM limits for strong new TVPC physics
* Many-quark effects in the neutron EDM and atomic EDM of Hg-199 give lower bounds on Lambda_TVPC roughly 1000 times weaker than the elementary fermion bounds
* Under Scenario A, the expected ratio of TVPC nuclear matrix elements to strong interaction matrix elements (alpha_T) is less than or approximately 10^{-15}
* Under Scenario B, direct limits from neutron transmission and charge symmetry breaking in np scattering give Lambda_TVPC > 1 GeV for strong new TVPC physics

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Ramsey-Musolf (1999) to include many-quark TVPC contributions to the neutron EDM (first studied by Khriplovich (1991)) and to atomic EDMs, and corrects the power-counting errors in the earlier analyses of Khriplovich (1991) and Conti (1992) who used a cut-off regulator that did not preserve the EFT scale separation. The paper enables a systematic classification of which experimental constraints (EDM versus direct TVPC searches) are most relevant under different assumptions about the short-distance parity symmetry restoration scale, and provides lower bounds on the new physics scale Lambda_TVPC that can guide future experimental searches.
