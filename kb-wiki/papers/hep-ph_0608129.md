# A further analysis of $Θ^+$ production in $γ+D \to Λ+n+K^{+}$ reaction

> We analyse Theta+ production in the gamma+D -> Λ+n+K+ reaction and study the dependence of the gamma+D -> Λ+n+K+ differential cross section on the nK+ invariant mass and on the momentum of the final neutron p_n. We examine the important role of the interference between the signal and background contributions to the gamma+D -> Λ+n+K+ amplitude in the extraction of the Theta+ signal from the gamma+D -> Λ+n+K+ cross section. We demonstrate that as a result of the cancellation between the interference and signal contributions, the Theta+ signal almost completely washes out after the integration over p_n. This is consistent with the CLAS conclusion that no statistically significant structures in the analysis of the gamma+D -> Λ+n+K+ reaction were observed. Therefore, there is no disagreement between the theory and the experiment and the CLAS result does not refute the existence of the Theta+.

## Key claim

The CLAS experiment's non-observation of a statistically significant $\Theta^+$ signal in the $\gamma+D \to \Lambda+n+K^+$ reaction does not refute the existence of the $\Theta^+$, because the signal is almost completely washed out after integration over the final neutron momentum $p_n$ due to cancellation between the signal and interference contributions, even when a $p_n > 0.2$ GeV cut is imposed.

## Method

The author calculated the triple differential cross section $d\sigma/(dt\,dp_n\,dM^2_{NK})$ for $\gamma+D \to \Lambda+n+K^+$ using a model where the $\Theta^+$ signal amplitude is given by a loop diagram with a spectator nucleon and the background by quasi-free $\gamma+p \to \Lambda+K^+$ scattering. The signal and background amplitudes were combined with their interference, using the Paris deuteron wave function and on-shell $\gamma+N \to \Lambda+K$ amplitudes from MAID, then integrated over $t$ and $p_n$ to simulate the CLAS analysis cuts.

## Key numbers

- The $\Theta^+$ signal deviation from background at $E_\gamma=1.2$ GeV is 5-7% for $p_n=0.1-0.2$ GeV and 45% at $p_n=0.3$ GeV.
- The $\Theta^+$ signal deviation from background at $E_\gamma=1.6$ GeV is 7% at $p_n=0.1$ GeV, 27% at $p_n=0.2$ GeV, and 12% at $p_n=0.3$ GeV.
- After integration over $p_n$ with a $p_n > 0.2$ GeV cut, the $\Theta^+$ peak at $M_{nK}=1.540$ GeV is only a 2% deviation from background at $E_\gamma=1.2$ GeV and a 1% positive peak at $E_\gamma=1.6$ GeV.
- The $\Theta^+$ mass is taken as $M_\Theta=1540$ MeV; calculations use $\Gamma_\Theta=1$ MeV and $\Gamma_\Theta=5$ MeV.
- The theoretical upper bound on $\Gamma_\Theta$ from the chiral quark soliton model is $\Gamma_\Theta < 15$ MeV; recent estimates range 2-5 MeV and 1 MeV; the DIANA collaboration reports $\Gamma_\Theta=0.36 \pm 0.11$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier analysis (Guzey, 2004) of $\Theta^+$ production in $\gamma+D \to \Lambda+n+K^+$ to address the CLAS measurement (Niccolai, 2006) which found no statistically significant $\Theta^+$ signal. The analysis shows that the CLAS result is consistent with theory when the destructive interference between signal and background is accounted for, resolving the apparent disagreement. The paper suggests that tighter cuts on $p_n$ and $t$ than those used by CLAS are needed to extract the $\Theta^+$ signal, and notes a small deviation near $M_{nK} \approx 1.53$ GeV in the CLAS data that merits further study.
