# Structure of Lambda(1405) and threshold behavior of piSigma scattering

> The scattering length and effective range of the piSigma channel are studied in order to characterize the strangeness S=-1 meson-baryon scattering and the Lambda(1405) resonance. We examine various off-shell dependence of the amplitude in dynamical chiral models to evaluate the threshold quantities with the constraint at the KbarN threshold. We find that the piSigma threshold parameters are important to the structure of the Lambda(1405) resonance and provide further constraints on the subthreshold extrapolation of the KbarN interaction.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the $\bar{K}N$ scattering length alone does not fully constrain the subthreshold behavior of the $\bar{K}N$-$\pi\Sigma$ coupled-channel amplitudes, and that the $\pi\Sigma$ scattering length and effective range provide essential additional constraints on the structure of the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance and the extrapolation of the $\bar{K}N$ interaction below threshold.

## Method

The authors constructed two-channel models of $\bar{K}N$ and $\pi\Sigma$ scattering with isospin $I=0$ using the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction from chiral effective theory. They solved the coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation in two different regularization schemes: Model A used dimensional regularization with subtraction constants, while Model B used separable form factors with cut-off parameters. All model parameters were fixed to reproduce the $\bar{K}N$ scattering length $a_{\bar{K}N} = -1.70 + 0.68i$ fm, and the resulting $\pi\Sigma$ threshold parameters and pole positions were compared across the different schemes.

## Key numbers

* $\bar{K}N$ scattering length (input): $a_{\bar{K}N} = -1.70 + 0.68i$ fm, determined by Martin (1980)
* Model A1: $a_{\pi\Sigma} = 0.934$ fm, $r_e = -5.02$ fm; poles at $z = 1422 - 16i$ MeV and $z = 1375 - 72i$ MeV
* Model A2: $a_{\pi\Sigma} = -2.30$ fm, $r_e = -5.89$ fm; poles at $z = 1425 - 11i$ MeV and $z = 1321$ MeV (bound state)
* Model B E-dep: $a_{\pi\Sigma} = 1.44$ fm, $r_e = -3.96$ fm; poles at $z = 1422 - 22i$ MeV and $z = 1349 - 54i$ MeV
* Model B E-indep: $a_{\pi\Sigma} = 5.50$ fm, $r_e = -0.458$ fm; poles at $z = 1423 - 29i$ MeV and $z = 1325$ MeV (virtual state)
* Meson decay constant: $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV; isospin-averaged masses: $m_\pi = 138$ MeV, $M_\Sigma = 1193$ MeV, $M_{\bar{K}} = 496$ MeV, $M_N = 939$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach to $\bar{K}N$-$\pi\Sigma$ scattering developed by Kaiser (1995), Oset (1998), Oller (2000), and Jido (2003), which established the dynamically generated nature of $\Lambda(1405)$ and its double-pole structure. The paper directly addresses the model dependence of the lower $\Lambda(1405)$ pole noted by Hyodo (2007) and the sensitivity to energy-dependent versus energy-independent potentials shown by Ikeda (2010). The results enable future experimental constraints from $\pi\Sigma$ threshold measurements, which are relevant for ongoing experiments at Jefferson Laboratory, GSI, and J-PARC, and for improving predictions of $\bar{K}NN$ quasibound states.
