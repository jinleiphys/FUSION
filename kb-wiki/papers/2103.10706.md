# New effective interactions for hypernuclei in density dependent relativistic mean field model

> New effective $ΛN$ interactions are proposed for the density dependent relativistic mean field model. The multidimensionally constrained relativistic mean field model is used to calculate ground state properties of eleven known $Λ$ hypernuclei with $A\ge 12$ and the corresponding core nuclei. Based on effective $NN$ interactions DD-ME2 and PKDD, the ratios $R_σ$ and $R_ω$ of scalar and vector coupling constants between $ΛN$ and $NN$ interactions are determined by fitting calculated $Λ$ separation energies to experimental values. We propose six new effective interactions for $Λ$ hypernuclei: DD-ME2-Y1, DD-ME2-Y2, DD-ME2-Y3, PKDD-Y1, PKDD-Y2 and PKDD-Y3 with three ways of grouping and including these eleven hypernuclei in the fitting. It is found that the two ratios $R_σ$ and $R_ω$ are correlated well and there holds a good linear relation between them. The statistical errors of the ratio parameters in these effective interactions are analyzed. These new effective interactions are used to study the equation of state of hypernuclear matter and neutron star properties with hyperons.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the ratios \(R_\sigma\) and \(R_\omega\) of scalar and vector coupling constants between \(\Lambda N\) and \(NN\) interactions deviate from the quark-model value of 2/3, and that these ratios become smaller when more light hypernuclei are included in the fitting, indicating that in-medium \(\Lambda N\) couplings are suppressed by structure effects in light hypernuclei.

## Method

The multidimensionally constrained relativistic mean field (MDC-RMF) model with density dependent couplings was used to calculate ground state properties of eleven known \(\Lambda\) hypernuclei with \(A\ge 12\) and their core nuclei, allowing for axial and reflection symmetric deformations. Based on the effective \(NN\) interactions DD-ME2 and PKDD, the ratios \(R_\sigma\) and \(R_\omega\) were determined by fitting calculated \(\Lambda\) separation energies to experimental values via minimization of the average square deviation, using three different groupings of the hypernuclei (all eleven, nine with \(A\ge 16\), and six with \(A\ge 40\)).

## Key numbers

- The \(\Lambda\) separation energy of \(^{12}_{\Lambda}\mathrm{C}\) is \(11.36 \pm 0.20\) MeV experimentally; DD-ME2-Y1 gives 10.789 MeV and PKDD-Y1 gives 10.854 MeV.
- The \(\Lambda\) separation energy of \(^{208}_{\Lambda}\mathrm{Pb}\) is \(26.9 \pm 0.8\) MeV experimentally; DD-ME2-Y3 gives 26.746 MeV and PKDD-Y3 gives 26.729 MeV.
- For DD-ME2-Y3, the ratios are \(R_\sigma = 0.577\) and \(R_\omega = 0.611\); for PKDD-Y3, \(R_\sigma = 0.659\) and \(R_\omega = 0.712\).
- The root mean square deviation \(\Delta\) for all eleven hypernuclei ranges from 0.668 MeV (DD-ME2-Y3) to 0.816 MeV (PKDD-Y1).
- The root of relative square deviation \(\delta\) for all eleven hypernuclei ranges from 3.759% (DD-ME2-Y1) to 5.419% (PKDD-Y3).
- The statistical error \(\sigma_{R_\sigma}^+\) for DD-ME2-Y1 is 0.053 and \(\sigma_{R_\sigma}^-\) is 0.079.

## Context

This work extends the density dependent relativistic mean field approach to hypernuclei, which previously had few effective interactions for \(\Lambda\) hypernuclei (Keil 2000, Hofmann 2001, Finelli 2009, Colucci 2013, Banik 2014, van Dalen 2014, Fortin 2017, Providencia 2019). Unlike prior RMF studies that imposed spherical symmetry, this work uses the MDC-RMF model to account for deformation in hypernuclei. The new interactions enable studies of the equation of state of hypernuclear matter and neutron star properties with hyperons.
