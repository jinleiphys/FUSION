# Inhomogeneous Thermalization in Strongly Coupled Field Theories

> To describe theoretically the creation and evolution of the quark-gluon plasma, one typically employs three ingredients: a model for the initial state, non-hydrodynamic early time evolution, and hydrodynamics. In this paper we study the non-hydrodynamic early time evolution using the AdS/CFT correspondence in the presence of inhomogeneities. We find that the AdS description of the early time evolution is well-matched by free streaming. Near the end of the early time interval where our analytic computations are reliable, the stress tensor agrees with the second order hydrodynamic stress tensor computed from the local energy density and fluid velocity. Our techniques may also be useful for the study of far-from-equilibrium strongly coupled systems in other areas of physics.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the early-time evolution of the stress-energy tensor in a strongly coupled conformal field theory with inhomogeneous initial conditions is well-described by free streaming, and that near the end of the analytically tractable time window the stress tensor agrees with second-order viscous hydrodynamics; however, the authors cannot exclude the possibility of violent equilibration dynamics at later times, and the results are restricted to times short compared to the inverse local temperature and to spatial variations large compared to that scale.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence to model gravitational collapse in an asymptotically AdS₄ spacetime, driven by a spatially inhomogeneous scalar field source at the boundary. They solve the coupled Einstein-scalar field equations perturbatively to second order in the source amplitude and to fourth order in spatial gradients, extracting the boundary energy-momentum tensor from the Fefferman-Graham expansion of the metric. The resulting stress tensor is compared to that of a free-streaming model (massless noninteracting dust) and to first- and second-order viscous hydrodynamics using the local energy density and fluid velocity computed from the holographic solution.

## Key numbers

- The injection time scale for the Gaussian source is set by σ² = 0.1 (in units where the AdS radius is set to 1), with the source centered at ν = 0.5 and spatial width parameter μ = 0.01.
- The amplitude of the scalar source is ε = 0.005.
- The shear viscosity for the 2+1 dimensional CFT is η = (1/(16πG_N)) (4πT/3)², with bulk viscosity ζ = 0.
- The relaxation time for shear stress in second-order hydrodynamics is τ_Π = (3/(4πT)) [1 + (1/3) Harmonic(-1/3)].
- The energy density in the local rest frame is related to the local temperature by ε = (2/(16πG_N)) (4πT/3)³.

## Context

This work extends the homogeneous AdS-Vaidya thermalization model of Bhattacharyya (2009) to include spatial inhomogeneities, complementing the boost-invariant, homogeneous studies of Chesler (2009), Beuf (2009), and Heller (2011) that found hydrodynamization on timescales of 0.3-0.5 fm/c. The results support the standard phenomenological approach of using free streaming followed by viscous hydrodynamics for early-time evolution in heavy ion collisions, but the authors note that the short time window accessible analytically prevents ruling out later violent equilibration as seen in van der Schee (2013) for homogeneous boost-invariant collisions.
