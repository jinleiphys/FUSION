# Gribov Confinement and Chiral Perturbation Theory

> I discuss the chiral dynamics of Gribov's theory of confinement. At a critical coupling $α_s^c$ the light quark vacuum undergoes a series of phase transitions which should be taken into account in the application of chiral perturbation theory. The Gribov theory offers a simple explanation of the value of the pion nucleon sigma term without need to invoke a large strange quark component in the nucleon.

# Gribov Confinement and Chiral Perturbation Theory

## Key claim
The Gribov theory of confinement predicts a series of phase transitions in the light quark vacuum at a critical coupling, which means that chiral perturbation theory is an expansion about the difference between a critical mass and the running quark mass rather than about zero quark mass. This resolves the long-standing discrepancy in the pion-nucleon sigma term without requiring a large strange quark component in the nucleon.

## Method
The author analyzes the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the quark propagator and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states within Gribov's confinement framework, identifying multiple solutions that correspond to new vacuum states at a critical coupling. The mass of the Goldstone pion state is derived as a function of the running quark mass and the critical mass for each phase transition, and this result is compared to the standard chiral perturbation theory relation between the pion mass and the current-quark mass.

## Key numbers
- The critical coupling for the phase transitions is α_s^c ≈ 0.6.
- The measured pion-nucleon sigma term from πN scattering is σ_πN = 45 ± 8 MeV.
- The theoretical prediction from baryon spectroscopy (including pion corrections) is σ_πN^{th} = 35 ± 5 MeV.
- The strange quark content parameter y = 2⟨N|s̄s|N⟩ / ⟨N|ūu + d̄d|N⟩ = 0.2 ± 0.2.
- The running light quark mass at μ² = 1 GeV² is m̂ = 6 MeV.
- The critical mass for the first chiral phase transition, evolved to μ² = 1 GeV², is m_P^c ≈ 16.5 MeV.
- The light quark mass extracted from the sigma term using the linear mass formula is m_q ≈ 10.5 MeV at μ² = 1 GeV².
- The strange quark mass is m_s ≈ 150 MeV, above the critical mass m_P^c.

## Context
This work extends Gribov's confinement theory (Gribov, 1994) to explain the sigma term discrepancy that has been debated in the literature, where the measured value from pion-nucleon scattering disagrees with the prediction from baryon octet mass splittings. The analysis enables a consistent treatment of chiral perturbation theory where the pion mass squared is proportional to (m_P^c - m_P) rather than m_P, and it removes the need to invoke a large strange quark component in the nucleon as suggested by previous interpretations of the sigma term data.
