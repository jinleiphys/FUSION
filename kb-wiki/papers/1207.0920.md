# Three -Pion Correlations

> First of all, we mention the situation of empirical analyses on 3rd order BEC (Bose-Einstein Correlation) at RHIC. Second, we introduce several theoretical formulae / approaches. Third we present our analyses of data in Au+Au at 130 GeV by STAR and preliminary data in Au+Au at 200 GeV by PHENIX Collaborations. Our results also contain analyses by means of core-halo model. Finally, we estimate that the volume of interaction in Au + Au collisions at 130 GeV is 500 fm^3, which is compared with V = R_{long} R_{out} R_{side} \sim 300 fm^3 in Pb + Pb collision at 2.76 TeV by ALICE Collaboration. Moreover, usefullness of empirical analyses on (2π^+)π^- and (2π^-)π^+ combinations at RHIC and LHC energies is remarked.

## Key claim

The authors estimate the interaction volume in Au+Au collisions at 130 GeV as 500 fm^3 from three-pion Bose-Einstein correlations, but note that this result depends on the specific analysis method and that the interaction ranges extracted from PHENIX data at 200 GeV are smaller than those from STAR data, making a concrete physical picture difficult to draw at present.

## Method

The authors analyze both raw and Coulomb-corrected three-pion correlation data from STAR (Au+Au at 130 GeV) and PHENIX (Au+Au at 200 GeV) collaborations. They fit the data using conventional plane-wave formulae, a laser-optical (LO/GL) approach with a degree of chaoticity parameter, and formulae based on Coulomb wave functions including the degree of coherence and interaction range. Additionally, they apply a core-halo model to extract the core fraction and degree of coherence.

## Key numbers

* Interaction volume from STAR data at 130 GeV: V = R_{3pi}^3 ~ 500 fm^3, compared with V = R_long R_out R_side ~ 300 fm^3 in Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV by ALICE Collaboration.
* For STAR corrected data at 130 GeV: R_{2pi} = 8.75 +/- 0.31 fm with lambda = 0.58 +/- 0.02 (chi^2/ndf = 23.0/25); R_{3pi} = 8.26 +/- 0.39 fm with lambda = 0.50 +/- 0.02 (chi^2/ndf = 1.88/35).
* For PHENIX corrected data at 200 GeV using conventional formulae: R_{2pi} = 4.77 +/- 0.04 fm with lambda = 0.39 +/- 0.01 (chi^2/ndf = 178/40); R_{3pi} = 6.92 +/- 0.82 fm with lambda = 0.34 +/- 0.10 (chi^2/ndf = 6.5/14).
* For PHENIX raw data at 200 GeV using Coulomb wave function formulae: R_{2pi} = 3.77 +/- 0.03 fm with lambda = 0.253 +/- 0.004 (chi^2/ndf = 129/40); R_{3pi} = 5.77 +/- 0.32 fm with lambda = 0.19 +/- 0.02 (chi^2/ndf = 84/14).

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of third-order Bose-Einstein correlations at RHIC by the STAR Collaboration (Adams 2003) and PHENIX Collaboration (Csanad 2005), comparing different theoretical approaches including plane-wave, laser-optical, and Coulomb wave function formulations. The authors also build on their own prior work on Coulomb wave function treatments (Biyajima 2003, 2005) and core-halo model analyses (Mizoguchi 2000). The results enable future comparisons with LHC data from ALICE and motivate empirical analyses of mixed-charge pion combinations (2pi^+)pi^- and (2pi^-)pi^+ at both RHIC and LHC energies.
