# Signatures of fireball fragmentation at the phase transition

> It is explained why and how the fireball created in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions can fragment when passing the phase transition. It can happen at the first-order phase transition but is not excluded even at high collision energies where the smooth crossover is present. Two potential observables sensitive to the appearance of fragmentation are reviewed: event-by-event changes of rapidity distributions and proton correlation in relative rapidity.

## Key claim
Fireball fragmentation at the phase transition is a realistic scenario at any ultrarelativistic collision energy, though it may be more natural at higher baryochemical potential where a first-order phase transition occurs; even at high collision energies with a smooth crossover, a sharp peak in bulk viscosity at the critical temperature could cause fragmentation.

## Method
The author reviews theoretical arguments for fireball fragmentation during the phase transition, considering both first-order transitions (where spinodal fragmentation can occur if expansion outpaces nucleation) and smooth crossovers (where a bulk viscosity peak may induce fragmentation). A Monte Carlo model called DRAGON (DRoplet an hAdron GeneratOr for Nuclear collisions) was developed to simulate particle production from both a bulk fireball and droplets, using a blast-wave expansion pattern with droplets inheriting local flow velocities. Two observables were tested on DRAGON-generated events: event-by-event fluctuations of rapidity distributions via the Kolmogorov-Smirnov test, and proton correlations in relative rapidity.

## Key numbers
- Transverse flow velocity reaches about 0.7c at \(\sqrt{s}_{NN} = 200\) GeV.
- Total lifetime of the hot fireball is about 10 fm/\(c\).
- Droplet average volume tested: 5 fm\(^3\), 10 fm\(^3\), 25 fm\(^3\), and 50 fm\(^3\).
- Simulations used chemical composition corresponding to \(\sqrt{s}_{NN} = 130\) GeV and \(\sqrt{s}_{NN} = 9\) GeV (FAIR energy).
- The Kolmogorov-Smirnov test was evaluated on 10\(^5\) event pairs from samples of 10\(^4\) events.
- Proton correlation functions were calculated for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}_{NN} = 130\) GeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier theoretical proposals of cavitation or fragmentation at the phase transition, such as Torrieri (2007) and Rajagopal (2009), and builds on studies of spinodal fragmentation by Mishustin (1998, 2001) and Scavenius (2000). It also references the sharp peak in bulk viscosity at \(T_c\) found by Pratt, Kharzeev, and Meyer (2007). The paper enables future experimental searches for fragmentation signatures in heavy-ion collisions at both RHIC and LHC energies, and suggests further investigation of imaging, elliptic flow fluctuations, and correlations in rapidity and azimuthal angle.
