# Aspects of Superfluid Cold Atomic Gases in Optical Lattices

> We review our studies on Bose and Fermi superfluids of cold atomic gases in optical lattices at zero temperature. Especially, we focus on superfluid Fermi gases along the crossover between the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) and the Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) states, which enable us to study the Bose and the Fermi superfluids in a unified point of view. We discuss basic static and long-wavelength properties (such as the equation of state, incompressibility, and effective mass), energetic stability, and energy band structures of the superfluid Fermi gases in an optical lattice periodic along one spatial direction. The periodic potential causes pairs of atoms to be strongly bound, and this can affect the static and long-wavelength properties and the stability of the superflow. Regarding the band structure, a peculiar loop structure called "swallowtail" can appear in superfluid Fermi gases and in the Bose case, but the mechanism of emergence in the Fermi case is very different from that in bosonic case. Other quantum phases that the cold atomic gases in optical lattices can show are also briefly discussed based on their roles as quantum simulators of Hubbard models.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the authors' own theoretical studies on superfluid cold atomic gases in optical lattices at zero temperature, focusing on how the periodic potential modifies static properties, stability, and band structures across the BCS-BEC crossover, with the hedge that the mean-field and hydrodynamic frameworks used are valid only in specific parameter regimes where quantum fluctuations are small.

## Method

The authors employ mean-field theory using the Gross-Pitaevskii equation for Bose superfluids and the Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations for Fermi superfluids in a continuum model, treating a three-dimensional system with a one-dimensional periodic optical lattice potential. For regimes where the lattice spacing is much larger than the healing length, they also apply hydrodynamic theory within the local density approximation to obtain analytic predictions for static and long-wavelength properties.

## Key numbers

- Lattice height is parameterized as \(V_0 = s E_{\rm R}\) with \(s \sim 0.1\) to \(5\) and recoil energy \(E_{\rm R} = \hbar^2 q_{\rm B}^2 / 2m\), covering \(\eta_{\rm height} = V_0/E_{\rm F} \sim 10^{-2}\) to \(10\) and \(\eta_{\rm fine} \sim 0.1\) to \(10\).
- For unitary Fermi gases in a strong lattice (\(s=5\)), the chemical potential shows a linear density dependence \(\mu \propto n\) in the low-density limit, contrasting with the uniform case \(\mu \propto n^{2/3}\).
- In the weak-lattice limit, the incompressibility of unitary Fermi gases is \(\kappa^{-1} \simeq \frac{2}{3}(1+\beta) E_{\rm F} \left[ 1 + \frac{1}{32} (1+\beta)^{-2} (sE_{\rm R}/E_{\rm F})^2 \right] + O[(sE_{\rm R}/E_{\rm F})^4]\).
- The effective mass of unitary Fermi gases in the weak-lattice limit is \(m^*/m \simeq 1 + \frac{9}{32} (1+\beta)^{-2} (sE_{\rm R}/E_{\rm F})^2 + O[(sE_{\rm R}/E_{\rm F})^4]\).
- The sound velocity of unitary Fermi gases in a lattice is given by \(c_s = \sqrt{\kappa^{-1}/m^*}\), and in the weak-lattice limit \(c_s^2 \simeq {c_s^{(0)}}^2 \left[1 - \frac{1}{4}(1+\beta)^{-2} (sE_{\rm R}/E_{\rm F})^2 + O[(sE_{\rm R}/E_{\rm F})^4]\right]\), where \(c_s^{(0)} = [(2/3)(1+\beta)E_{\rm F}/m]^{1/2}\).

## Context

This review extends the authors' prior work on superfluid Fermi gases in optical lattices (Watanabe and Yoon, 2013) and builds on earlier studies of BCS-BEC crossover in uniform gases (Giorgini, 2008) and optical lattice physics (Bloch, 2008; Morsch, 2006). The results enable quantitative predictions for experiments on trapped unitary Fermi gases in optical lattices, such as those by Miller (2007), and provide a framework for studying lattice-induced bound states and their effects on compressibility and effective mass, which differ from ideal Fermi gases and repulsive Bose gases (Kramer, 2003; Orso, 2005).
