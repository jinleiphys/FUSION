# Shell-model calculations of neutrino scattering from 12C

> Neutrino reaction cross-sections, $(ν_μ,μ^-)$, $(ν_e,e^-)$, $μ$-capture and photoabsorption rates on $^{12}$C are computed within a large-basis shell-model framework, which included excitations up to $4\hbarω$. When ground-state correlations are included with an open $p$-shell the predictions of the calculations are in reasonable agreement with most of the experimental results for these reactions. Woods-Saxon radial wave functions are used, with their asymptotic forms matched to the experimental separation energies for bound states, and matched to a binding energy of 0.01 MeV for unbound states. For comparison purposes, some results are given for harmonic oscillator radial functions. Closest agreement between theory and experiment is achieved with unrestricted shell-model configurations and Woods-Saxon radial functions. We obtain for the neutrino-absorption inclusive cross sections: $\barσ = 13.8 \times 10^{-40}$ cm$^2$ for the $(ν_μ,μ^{-})$ decay-in-flight flux in agreement with the LSND datum of $(12.4 \pm 1.8) \times 10^{-40}$ cm$^2$; and $\barσ = 12.5 \times 10^{-42}$ cm$^2$ for the $(ν_{e},e^{-})$ decay-at-rest flux, less than the experimental result of $(14.4 \pm 1.2) \times 10^{-42}$ cm$^2$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a large-basis shell-model calculation including excitations up to 4ħω, with unrestricted configurations and Woods-Saxon radial wave functions, brings the predicted (ν_μ,μ⁻) inclusive cross section into agreement with the LSND measurement, but this improvement comes with a modest deterioration in the predicted (ν_e,e⁻) decay-at-rest cross section, which falls about one standard deviation below the experimental value.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations in four model spaces: closed-shell TDA, closed-shell RPA, closed-shell RPA plus 2p-2h configurations, and a fully unrestricted shell model, all including excitations up to 4ħω (except the unrestricted model, which was limited to 2ħω and then extrapolated). Woods-Saxon radial wave functions were used, with asymptotic forms matched to experimental separation energies for bound states and to a binding energy of 0.01 MeV for unbound states; harmonic oscillator results were given for comparison. The one-body density matrix elements from the shell-model calculations were combined with single-particle matrix elements to evaluate neutrino-absorption cross sections, muon-capture rates, and photoabsorption cross sections, with Coulomb distortion corrections applied to the outgoing leptons.

## Key numbers

* Flux-averaged (ν_μ,μ⁻) inclusive cross section for the decay-in-flight flux: 13.8 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm² (unrestricted shell model, extrapolated from 2ħω), compared to the LSND experimental value of (12.4 ± 1.8) × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm².
* Flux-averaged (ν_e,e⁻) inclusive cross section for the decay-at-rest flux: 12.5 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (unrestricted shell model), compared to the experimental result of (14.4 ± 1.2) × 10⁻⁴² cm².
* Closed-shell RPA (4ħω) result for (ν_μ,μ⁻): 18.2 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm²; closed-shell RPA + 2p-2h (4ħω): 16.7 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm².
* Closed-shell RPA (4ħω) result for (ν_e,e⁻): 21.9 × 10⁻⁴² cm²; closed-shell RPA + 2p-2h (4ħω): 20.4 × 10⁻⁴² cm².
* Total photoabsorption cross section (E1 only, integrated to E_γ = 50 ± 5 MeV): experimental value of 21 ± 1 fm².

## Context

This work extends earlier continuum RPA calculations by Kolbe et al. (1995, 1999), which overestimated the (ν_μ,μ⁻) cross section by almost a factor of two when using a closed p₃/₂ ground state, and which were later modified with partial p₁/₂ occupancies to reduce the discrepancy. The present shell-model approach, by including ground-state correlations and unrestricted configurations, achieves better agreement for the DIF cross section but slightly underpredicts the DAR cross section. The results enable improved constraints on neutrino-oscillation parameter extraction at LSND and KARMEN, and demonstrate the sensitivity of predicted cross sections to the treatment of ground-state spin structure and radial wave functions.
