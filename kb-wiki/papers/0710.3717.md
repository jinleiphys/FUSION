# A calculation of the bulk viscosity in SU(3) gluodynamics

> We perform a lattice Monte-Carlo calculation of the trace-anomaly two-point function at finite temperature in the SU(3) gauge theory. We obtain the long-distance properties of the correlator in the continuum limit and extract the bulk viscosity zeta via a Kubo formula. Unlike the tensor correlator relevant to the shear viscosity, the scalar correlator depends strongly on temperature. If s is the entropy density, we find that zeta/s becomes rapidly small at high T, zeta/s<0.15 at 1.65T_c and zeta/s<0.015 at 3.2T_c. However zeta/s rises dramatically just above T_c, with 0.5<zeta/s<2.0 at 1.02T_c.

## Key claim
The bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio zeta/s in SU(3) gluodynamics rises dramatically just above T_c (0.5 < zeta/s < 2.0 at 1.02 T_c) but becomes rapidly small at high T (zeta/s < 0.015 at 3.2 T_c), with the caveat that the extraction relies on reconstructing the real-time spectral function from Euclidean correlators, requiring assumptions of smoothness.

## Method
The author performed lattice Monte-Carlo simulations of the SU(3) gauge theory using the isotropic Wilson action with a two-level algorithm, employing the bare-plaquette discretization of the energy-momentum tensor. The trace-anomaly two-point function C_theta(x_0) was computed on L_0/a = 8, 10, and 12 lattices in the temperature range T_c < T < 3 T_c, and the bulk viscosity was extracted via a Kubo formula by reconstructing the spectral function rho_theta(omega) from the Euclidean correlator using a singular-value decomposition method with a default model m(omega) for the high-frequency behavior.

## Key numbers
- At T = 1.02 T_c on L_0/a = 8 lattice with LT = 3: zeta/s = 0.73(3) with conservative bounds [0.5, 2.0]
- At T = 1.24 T_c on L_0/a = 12 lattice with LT = 8/3: zeta/s = 0.065(17) with conservative bounds [0.01, 0.37]
- At T = 1.65 T_c on L_0/a = 12 lattice with LT = 16/3: zeta/s = 0.008(7) with conservative bounds [0, 0.15]
- Continuum extrapolated first moment <omega^0> of the spectral function: 16.3(5) at T = 1.24 T_c and 8.5(8) at T = 1.65 T_c
- Entropy density in continuum: s/T^3 = 4.46(6) at T = 1.24 T_c and 5.48(7) at T = 1.65 T_c

## Context
This work extends the lattice calculation of shear viscosity in SU(3) gluodynamics by Meyer (2007) to the bulk viscosity, using similar methodology. It tests the prediction by Kharzeev (2006) based on an exact sum rule that the bulk viscosity rises sharply close to the first-order phase transition, and finds agreement with that qualitative behavior. The results enable comparisons with leading-order perturbative QCD results by Arnold (2006) and with AdS/CFT calculations by Buchel (2005), showing that the perturbative formula zeta/s = 0.020 alpha_s^2 (for N_f = 0) lies below the lattice data and does not capture the sharp rise near T_c.
