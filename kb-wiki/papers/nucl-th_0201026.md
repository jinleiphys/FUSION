# Fragment Distribution in Coexistence Phase of Supernova Matter

> We propose a new nucleosynthesis process in which various nuclei are formed through the liquid-gas phase transition of supernova matter as a preprocess of the standard r-process, and study its possibility of realization qualitatively. In the relativistic mean field and statistical model calculations, we find that this process may take place and help the later r-process to proceed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a liquid-gas phase transition in supernova matter, acting as a preprocess to the standard r-process, may help produce heavy nuclei; however, the authors state this is a qualitative study and that the ejection of such matter through the coexistence phase is "marginal but still possible" given the constraints of neutrino trapping densities.

## Method

The authors first used Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) theory with the TM1 interaction parameter set to map the liquid-gas coexistence region in the (baryon density, temperature) plane for supernova matter under the Gibbs condition, assuming three conserved quantities (baryon number, lepton number, and charge). They then applied a statistical model based on a fragment-based grand canonical ensemble, using the mass table of Myers and Swiatecki (1994) containing 9000 nuclei and including a Coulomb energy correction for electron screening, to calculate fragment distributions at various freeze-out densities and temperatures.

## Key numbers

- The liquid-gas coexistence region extends up to a critical temperature of approximately 15 MeV at maximum, and remains up to about 1 MeV even at very low densities.
- The adiabatic path with entropy per baryon S/B ~ 10 crosses the coexistence boundary at around (baryon density, temperature) = (10^-5 fm^-3, 1.5 MeV).
- At a freeze-out density of 10^-2 fm^-3, the temperature is 7.0 MeV (critical temperature 7.2 MeV) and nuclei around the third r-process peak are formed.
- At a freeze-out density of 10^-3 fm^-3, the temperature is 3.8 MeV (critical temperature 4.0 MeV) and nuclei around the second r-process peak are formed.
- At a freeze-out density of 10^-5 fm^-3, the temperature is 1.44 MeV (critical temperature 1.48 MeV) and nuclei around the first r-process peak are formed.
- At a freeze-out density of 10^-7 fm^-3, the temperature is 0.80 MeV (critical temperature 0.84 MeV) and iron, nickel plus first peak nuclei are formed.
- The Coulomb energy correction by electrons amounts to 90 percent of the total Coulomb energy at baryon density equal to nuclear saturation density and 10 MeV at baryon density 10^-6 times saturation density, for an electron-baryon ratio of 0.3.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the liquid-gas phase transition in supernova matter, which had focused on modifications to the equation of state (Lattimer, year not given) rather than on nuclear distribution as an initial condition for the r-process. The authors note that prior mean field treatments assumed one dominant fragment configuration, whereas their statistical model considers an ensemble of fragment configurations important near the critical temperature. The results suggest that the proposed LG process could provide an initial condition for the standard r-process, and the authors state that works for a more quantitative discussion, including combining with the later r-process, are in progress.
