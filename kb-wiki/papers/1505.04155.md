# Manifestation of proton structure in the initial-state anisotropies in high-energy proton-proton collisions

> Ridge-like correlations in high-energy proton-proton collisions reported by the CMS collaboration suggest a collective flow that resembles the one in heavy-ion collisions. If the hydrodynamic description is valid then the effect results from the initial anisotropy of the colliding matter which depends on the structure of protons. Following recent theoretical developments, we propose several phenomenological models of the proton structure and calculate the anisotropy coefficients using the Monte Carlo Glauber model. Our estimates suggest that the event multiplicity dependence allows one to discriminate between different proton models.

## Key claim

If the hydrodynamic description of ridge-like correlations in high-energy proton-proton collisions is valid, then the initial spatial anisotropies of the colliding matter depend on the internal structure of protons, and the event multiplicity dependence of these anisotropies may allow discrimination between different phenomenological proton models.

## Method

The authors used a Monte Carlo Glauber model to simulate proton-proton collisions, treating each proton as a collection of partons with a specified spatial density profile. For each simulated event, they calculated the eccentricity (ε₂) and triangularity (ε₃) of the binary collision density in the impact plane, and estimated the charged hadron multiplicity assuming linear scaling with the number of binary collisions. They compared three phenomenological proton models: a fixed rod-like (quark-diquark) configuration (I), a fixed triangular configuration (Y), and a Gaussian-fluctuating configuration (G-f) where quark positions vary event-by-event.

## Key numbers

- Partonic cross section fixed at σ_gg = 4.3 mb
- Experimental inelastic pp cross section reproduced at σ_pp ≈ 60 mb
- Effective quark radius r_q = 0.25 fm and quark-diquark separation d = 1.5 fm chosen to reproduce proton charge rms radius of ≈ 0.9 fm
- Minimum bias RMS eccentricities: √⟨ε₂²⟩_MB = 0.37 (II), 0.40 (YY), 0.31 (G-f)
- Minimum bias RMS triangularities: √⟨ε₃²⟩_MB = 0.14 (II), 0.23 (YY), 0.15 (G-f)
- Number of partons per proton N_g in range 7-10 to reproduce σ_pp = 60 mb
- Scaling parameter α from multiplicity relation N = α N_coll varies between 5 and 11
- Expected minimum bias v₂ ≈ 0.11 (II), 0.12 (YY), 0.09 (G-f) assuming v₂/ε₂ ∼ 0.3
- Bożek extracts v₂ in range 0.04-0.08 from ridge effect
- 300,000 events generated for each collision class
- For G-f model, R_P = 0.43 fm to obtain experimental rms proton charge radius

## Context

This work extends the approach of Dent (2012) to link proton models to experimental data on ridge-like correlations in pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV reported by the CMS collaboration. The authors build on the quark-diquark model of Białkowska (2013) and the renormalization group procedure for effective particles (RGPEP) of Głazek (2013) to construct phenomenological proton densities. The results enable future comparison with experimental flow coefficients v₂ and v₃ from multi-particle correlations, and suggest that a realistic model should include mixtures of different proton configurations rather than a single type.
