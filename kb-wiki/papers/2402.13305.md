# Behavior of the continuum coupling correlation energy in the vicinity of the particle emission threshold -- Gamow shell model study

> The Gamow shell model provides the open quantum system formulation of nuclear shell model. In the coupled-channel representation, Gamow shell model provides the unified theory of nuclear structure and reactions which is well suited for the study of resonances and clusterization. In this work, we apply this approach to study the continuum-coupling correlation energy for selected near-threshold states of $^7$Li, $^7$Be, using a translationally invariant Hamiltonian with an effective finite-range two-body interaction.

## Key claim

The continuum-coupling correlation energy, defined as the energy difference between the full Gamow shell model coupled-channel (GSM-CC) solution and the solution without a specific reaction channel, exhibits a minimum near the particle emission threshold; however, the position of this minimum is shifted above the threshold by approximately 0.35 MeV for the one-neutron channel in \(^7\)Li and by about 0.2 MeV between mirror states, indicating that the optimal energy for the strongest coupling to the decay channel is not exactly at the threshold itself.

## Method

The authors applied the Gamow shell model in its coupled-channel representation (GSM-CC) with multi-mass binary partitions, using a translationally invariant Hamiltonian formulated in the cluster orbital shell model (COSM) framework with an inert \(^4\)He core. The effective Hamiltonian employed a Woods-Saxon one-body potential and the FHT finite-range two-body interaction for valence nucleons, while the internal structure of \(^3\)He and \(^3\)H projectiles was calculated using the N\(^3\)LO interaction; the coupled-channel equations were solved by expanding channel states onto a Berggren basis of single-particle states, and the continuum-coupling correlation energy was computed as the difference between the full GSM-CC eigenvalue and the eigenvalue obtained when a specific reaction channel was removed.

## Key numbers

- The minimum of the correlation energy for the \(5/2^-_2\) state in \(^7\)Li, associated with the one-neutron channel \([^6\text{Li}(1^+_1)\otimes\text{n}(\ell j)]^{5/2^-}\), is shifted by about **0.35 MeV** above the neutron emission threshold.
- The positions of the minima of the correlation energy in the mirror \(5/2^-_2\) states of \(^7\)Li and \(^7\)Be differ by about **0.2 MeV**.
- For the \(7/2^-_1\) state in \(^7\)Li, the probability of the triton channel \([^4\text{He}(0^+_1)\otimes^3\text{H}(^2F_{7/2})]^{7/2^-}\) has a maximum approximately **4.5 MeV** above the \(^3\)H emission threshold energy.
- The minimum of the correlation energy for the \(7/2^-_1\) state in \(^7\)Li, associated with the triton channel, is seen at approximately **6 MeV** above the cluster emission threshold.

## Context

This work extends previous GSM studies by Michel (2007, 2010) that demonstrated the necessity of continuum coupling to reproduce Wigner cusps in spectroscopic factors, and builds on the conjecture by Okolowicz (2012, 2013) that near-threshold clustering is a general open quantum system phenomenon. The GSM-CC framework with multi-mass partitions, first applied by Linares Fernandez (2023), enables the unified description of nuclear structure and reactions for states near particle emission thresholds, and the present study lays groundwork for investigating how continuum-coupling correlations relate to clusterization in light nuclei such as \(^7\)Li and \(^7\)Be.
