# Mass spectra and Regge trajectories of $Λ_{c}^{+}$, $Σ_{c}^{0}$, $Ξ_{c}^{0}$ and $Ω_{c}^{0}$ Baryons

> We calculate the mass spectra of the singly charmed baryons ($Λ_{c}^{+}$, $Σ_{c}^{0}$, $Ξ_{c}^{0}$ and $Ω_{c}^{0}$) using Hypercentral constituent quark model(hCQM). The hyper color coloumb plus linear potential is used to calculate the masses of positive(upto $J^{p}=\frac{7}{2}^{+}$) and negative parity(upto $J^{p}=\frac{9}{2}^{-}$) excited states. The spin-spin, spin-orbital and tensor interaction terms are also incorporated for mass spectra. We have compared our results with other theoretical predictions and Lattice QCD for each baryons. Moreover, the known experimental results are also reasonably closed to our predicted masses. By using the radial and orbital excitation, we construct Regge trajectories for the baryons in (n,$M^{2}$) plane and find their slopes and intercepts. The other properties like, magnetic moments, radiative transitions and radiative decay widths of these baryons are also calculated successfully.

## Key claim

The hypercentral constituent quark model (hCQM) with a color Coulomb plus linear potential, augmented by spin-spin, spin-orbit, and tensor interactions, reproduces the mass spectra of singly charmed baryons up to high radial and orbital excitations, with predicted masses that are "reasonably closed" to known experimental results and in line with other theoretical predictions and lattice QCD, though some discrepancies (e.g., 102 MeV for the first orbital state of Λc+) remain.

## Method

The authors solved the six-dimensional hyperradial Schrödinger equation for the three-quark system using the hypercentral constituent quark model (hCQM). The confining potential was taken as a hyper-Coulomb plus linear term, with spin-dependent corrections (spin-spin, spin-orbit, and tensor) added. Constituent quark masses were set to mu=0.338 GeV, md=0.350 GeV, ms=0.500 GeV, and mc=1.275 GeV, and the strong coupling constant was fixed at αs=0.6. The mass spectra for Λc+, Σc0, Ξc0, and Ωc0 were computed for states up to 5S, 5P, 2D, and 1F, and Regge trajectories in the (n, M²) plane were constructed from the results.

## Key numbers

- Λc+ ground state (1²S₁/₂): predicted 2.287 GeV, compared to experimental 2.28646±0.00014 GeV.
- Λc+ first orbital excited state (1²P₁/₂): predicted 2.694 GeV, compared to experimental Λc(2595)+ at 2.59225±0.00028 GeV (difference of 102 MeV).
- Σc0 ground state (1²S₁/₂): predicted 2.452 GeV, compared to experimental 2.45374±0.00016 GeV.
- Σc0 ground state (1⁴S₃/₂): predicted 2.518 GeV, compared to experimental 2.5188±0.0006 GeV.
- Ξc0 ground state (1²S₁/₂): predicted 2.471 GeV, compared to experimental 2.47088 GeV.
- Ωc0 ground state (1²S₁/₂): predicted 2.695 GeV, compared to experimental 2.6952±1.7 GeV.
- Ωc0 ground state (1⁴S₃/₂): predicted 2.767 GeV, compared to experimental 2.7659±2.0 GeV.
- Regge slope for Λc+ S-states (Jᴾ=1/2⁺): β=0.440±0.0029 GeV⁻², intercept β₀=-2.327±0.030.
- Regge slope for Ωc0 S-states (Jᴾ=1/2⁺): β=0.453±0.005 GeV⁻², intercept β₀=-3.320±0.054.

## Context

This work extends the hypercentral constituent quark model (hCQM), previously applied to ground-state baryons by the same group (Shah et al., earlier work), to higher radial and orbital excitations of singly charmed baryons. It compares its results with several prior approaches, including the relativistic quark-diquark model of Ebert et al. (2011), the relativistic flux tube model of Chen et al. (2015), the non-relativistic quark model of Yoshida et al., and lattice QCD calculations from multiple groups (Brown et al., PACS, Alexandrou et al., Mathur et al.). The computed Regge trajectories provide a tool to assign quantum numbers to experimentally observed but unclassified states, such as Λc(2765)+ and Σc(2800), and enable future comparisons with upcoming data from J-PARC, PANDA, and LHCb.
