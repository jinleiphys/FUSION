# Relativistic Density Dependent Hartree-Fock Approach for Finite Nuclei

> The self-energy of the Dirac Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculation in nuclear matter is parametrized by introducing density-dependent coupling constants of isoscalar mesons in the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approach where isoscalar meson $σ$ , $ω$ and isovector meson $π$, $ρ$ contributions are included. The RHF calculations with density dependent coupling constants obtained in this way not only reproduce the nuclear matter saturation properties , but also provide the self-energy with an appropriate density dependence. The relativistic density dependent Hartree-Fock (RDHF) approach contains the features of the relativistic G matrix and in the meantime simplifies the calculation. The ground state properties of spherical nuclei calculated in the RDHF are in good agreement with the experimental data. The contribution of isovector mesons $π$ and $ρ$ , especially the contribution of the tensor coupling of $ρ$ meson , are discussed in this paper.

## Key claim

The relativistic density dependent Hartree-Fock (RDHF) approach, which parametrizes the self-energy of Dirac Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations in nuclear matter by introducing density-dependent coupling constants of isoscalar and isovector mesons, can reproduce ground state properties of spherical nuclei in good agreement with experimental data, provided that the extrapolation of coupling constants at very low densities is handled carefully and the tensor coupling of the ρ meson is included.

## Method

The authors start from an effective Lagrangian density coupling nucleons to σ, ω, π, and ρ mesons plus the electromagnetic field. They parametrize the self-energy from DBHF calculations in nuclear matter (using the Bonn A potential) by introducing density-dependent coupling constants for the isoscalar mesons, then extend this to include isovector mesons π and ρ with both vector and tensor couplings. The coupled Dirac-Hartree-Fock equations are solved self-consistently in coordinate space for spherical, closed-subshell nuclei, with the coupling constants at each baryon density determined from the DBHF parametrization.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic density dependent Hartree (RDH) approach of Brockmann and Toki (1992) and the relativistic density dependent Hartree-Fock (RDHF) calculations of Fritz, Muther, and Machleidt (1993) by including isovector mesons π and ρ, particularly the tensor coupling of the ρ meson, which were neglected in those earlier studies. The approach addresses the "Coester band" problem observed in both nonrelativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations (Kummel et al., 1978) and standard relativistic mean field calculations, where binding energies and charge radii could not be simultaneously reproduced. The RDHF approach with density-dependent coupling constants enables parameter-free calculations of finite nuclei that incorporate the features of the relativistic G matrix while maintaining computational simplicity, and the inclusion of isovector mesons improves the description of isospin-dependent properties such as neutron skin thickness.
