# A Fully Solvable Model of Fermionic Interaction in $3+1d$

> Recently, Romatschke found that the poles in $O(N)$ scalar theories do not affect observables such as temperature and pressure. Romatschke went on to show this result holds for marginal, relevant, and irrelevant operators in $3+1d$ $(O(N)$ scalar theories. We continue in this direction by studying large-$N$ fermi-interactions in $3+1d$. To do so, we produce a model of marginally coupled fermi-interactions which is fully renormalizable at large-$N$. This theory contains poles in the running coupling, however we argue these poles do not affect any physical observables. Further, our theory contains first order phase transition which separates a stable, meta-stable, and unstable phase.

## Key claim

The authors construct a large-\(N\) fermionic theory in \(3+1d\) with marginal four-fermi interactions that is fully renormalizable and contains poles in the running coupling; they argue these poles do not affect any physical observables, and the theory exhibits a first-order phase transition separating stable, meta-stable, and unstable phases.

## Method

The authors start from a Euclidean action for a \(U(N)\) fermionic theory with a marginal interaction term \((\bar{\psi}_f\psi_f)^{d/(d-1)}\) in \(d\) dimensions, then use a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce auxiliary fields \(\sigma\) and \(\xi\). They take the large-\(N\) limit, which makes the saddle-point integration exact, and compactify the Euclidean time dimension to a thermal circle to study finite-temperature thermodynamics. The theory is renormalized using dimensional regularization in \(d=4-2\epsilon\) with the \(\overline{MS}\) scheme, and a \(\mathcal{PT}\)-symmetric extension is constructed by analytically continuing the coupling \(\alpha \rightarrow -g \pm i0^+\) following the Ai-Bender-Sarkar conjecture, yielding a second theory with opposite-sign beta-function that shares identical thermodynamics.

## Key numbers

- The beta-function for the Hermitian theory is \(\beta(\alpha_R) = -\alpha_R^2/(2\pi^2)\), and for the \(\mathcal{PT}\)-symmetric theory it is \(\beta(g_R) = g_R^2/(2\pi^2)\).
- The running coupling in the Hermitian theory has a pole at a cutoff \(\Lambda_c\): \(\alpha_R(\bar{\mu}) = 4\pi^2 / \ln(\bar{\mu}^2 / \Lambda_c^2)\).
- At zero temperature and zero bare mass, the gap equation has two solutions: \(m_0(T=0)=0\) and \(m_1(T=0)=\sqrt{e}\,\Lambda_c\).
- For bare mass \(M = -0.01\Lambda_c\), the zero-temperature gap equation yields three solutions: \(m_0=0\), \(m_1=0.47945\Lambda_c\), and \(m_2=0.74319\Lambda_c\).
- The first-order phase transition occurs at critical temperature \(T_c \approx 0.139\Lambda_c\), where the \(m=0\) solution becomes thermodynamically favored.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Romatschke (2022), who showed that poles in the running coupling of \(O(N)\) scalar theories do not affect observables like temperature and pressure, and that \(\mathcal{PT}\)-symmetric extensions allow one to "see around" such poles. The authors apply this strategy to fermionic interactions, constructing a model inspired by the Gross-Neveu model in \(1+1d\) (Moshe, 2003; Gross and Neveu, 1974) and using fractional interaction terms as suggested by Parisi (1975). The result is a fully solvable, renormalizable, and UV/IR-complete fermionic theory in \(3+1d\) that enables future work on pseudo-scalar four-fermi interactions (generalizing the NJL model), relevant deformations, finite chemical potential, and toy models for QCD.
