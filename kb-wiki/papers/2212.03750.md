# Collisional flavor instability in dense neutrino gases

> Charged-current neutrino processes such as $ν_e + n \rightleftharpoons p + e^-$ and $\barν_e + p \rightleftharpoons n + e^+$ destroy the flavor coherence among the weak-interaction states of a single neutrino and thus damp its flavor oscillation. In a dense neutrino gas such as that inside a core-collapse supernova or the black hole accretion disk formed in a compact binary merger, however, these "collision" processes can trigger large flavor conversion in cooperation with the strong neutrino-neutrino refraction. We show that there exist two types of collisional flavor instability in a homogeneous and isotropic neutrino gas which are identified by the dependence of their real frequencies on the neutrino density $n_ν$. The instability transitions from one type to the other and exhibits a resonance-like behavior in the region where the net electron lepton number of the neutrino gas is negligible. In the transition region, the flavor instability grows exponentially at a rate $\propto n_ν^{1/2}$. We find that the neutrino gas in the black hole accretion disk is susceptible to the collision-induced flavor conversion where the neutrino densities are the highest. As a result, large amounts of heavy-lepton flavor neutrinos may be produced through flavor conversion, which can potentially have important ramifications in the subsequent evolution of the remnant.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that charged-current collision processes, which normally damp neutrino flavor coherence, can instead trigger large flavor conversion in dense neutrino gases when combined with strong neutrino-neutrino refraction. The authors identify two distinct types of collisional flavor instability (CFI) in a homogeneous and isotropic neutrino gas, which transition between each other and exhibit a resonance-like behavior where the net electron lepton number of the neutrino gas is negligible.

## Method

The authors analyze the linearized equation of motion for neutrino flavor coherence in a homogeneous and isotropic neutrino gas, incorporating both the neutrino-neutrino refraction term (proportional to the neutrino density parameter μ) and the effective oscillation frequency ω_eff that includes the vacuum oscillation term and a complex collision rate Γ_E from charged-current processes. They derive a self-consistency equation for collective oscillation modes and obtain approximate analytic solutions for the complex frequency Ω in the limit |Ω| ≫ |ω_eff|, identifying two branches of the dispersion relation. As a concrete example, they solve the full dispersion relation numerically using a Fermi-Dirac energy distribution for neutrinos and antineutrinos with different temperatures and collision rates. They then apply their analysis to the BH accretion disk model M3A8m3a5 from Just (2014), computing the normal mode frequencies from the discrete energy-group version of the equation.

## Key numbers

- The growth rate γ of the CFI in the transition region scales as γ ∝ n_ν^{1/2}, where n_ν is the neutrino density (proportional to μ)
- In the numerical example with μ = 10^3 μs^{-1}, the CFI exists in the range D ≈ -1.13 to D ≈ 0.50, where D = 1 - (n_ν̄_e - n_ν̄_x)/(n_ν_e - n_ν_x) is the dimensionless net νELN parameter
- The average collision rate ratio in the numerical example is Γ/Γ̄ ≈ 2.13, with Γ and Γ̄ being the average collision rates for neutrinos and antineutrinos respectively
- The BH accretion disk model M3A8m3a5 is analyzed at three snapshots: t = 20, 35, and 50 ms
- The vacuum oscillation parameters used are Δm² = 2.5 × 10^{-3} eV² and θ = 8.6°
- The transition between CFI types occurs at x ≈ 103 km in the equatorial plane at t = 20 ms, where n_ν_e ≈ n_ν̄_e

## Context

This work extends the discovery of collision-induced flavor conversion in monochromatic neutrino gases by Johns (2021) and the recent demonstration of CFI in continuous energy spectra by Lin (2022). The authors show that both types of CFI found in the monochromatic case exist in a homogeneous, isotropic gas with continuous energy distributions. The results apply to core-collapse supernovae and black hole accretion disks from compact binary mergers, where the CFI can operate in regions where fast flavor instabilities (which require a crossing of the νELN angular distribution) do not yet exist, as noted by Just (2022). The work motivates integrating flavor oscillations into neutrino transport simulations of these astrophysical environments.
