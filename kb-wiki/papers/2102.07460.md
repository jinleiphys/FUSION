# Isovector electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon from lattice QCD and the proton radius puzzle

> We present results for the isovector electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon computed on the CLS ensembles with $N_f=2+1$ flavors of $\mathcal{O}(a)$-improved Wilson fermions and an $\mathcal{O}(a)$-improved vector current. The analysis includes ensembles with four lattice spacings and pion masses ranging from 130 MeV up to 350 MeV and mainly targets the low-$Q^2$ region. In order to remove any bias from unsuppressed excited-state contributions, we investigate several source-sink separations between 1.0 fm and 1.5 fm and apply the summation method as well as explicit two-state fits. The chiral interpolation is performed by applying covariant chiral perturbation theory including vector mesons directly to our form factor data, thus avoiding an auxiliary parametrization of the $Q^2$ dependence. At the physical point, we obtain $μ=4.71(11)_{\mathrm{stat}}(13)_{\mathrm{sys}}$ for the nucleon isovector magnetic moment, in good agreement with the experimental value and $\langle r_\mathrm{M}^2\rangle~=~0.661(30)_{\mathrm{stat}}(11)_{\mathrm{sys}}\,~\mathrm{fm}^2$ for the corresponding square-radius, again in good agreement with the value inferred from the $ep$-scattering determination [Bernauer et~al., Phys. Rev. Lett., 105, 242001 (2010)] of the proton radius. Our estimate for the isovector electric charge radius, $\langle r_\mathrm{E}^2\rangle = 0.800(25)_{\mathrm{stat}}(22)_{\mathrm{sys}}\,~\mathrm{fm}^2$, however, is in slight tension with the larger value inferred from the aforementioned $ep$-scattering data, while being in agreement with the value derived from the 2018 CODATA average for the proton charge radius.

## Key claim

The lattice QCD calculation yields an isovector electric charge radius that is in slight tension with the larger value inferred from ep-scattering data (Bernauer et al., 2010), while agreeing with the value derived from the 2018 CODATA average for the proton charge radius; the isovector magnetic moment and magnetic radius agree well with experimental and phenomenological values.

## Method

The authors computed isovector electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon using CLS ensembles with Nf=2+1 flavors of O(a)-improved Wilson fermions and an O(a)-improved vector current, covering four lattice spacings and pion masses from 130 MeV to 350 MeV. They investigated source-sink separations between 1.0 fm and 1.5 fm, applying both the summation method and explicit two-state fits to remove excited-state contamination. The chiral interpolation was performed by applying covariant chiral perturbation theory including vector mesons directly to the form factor data, avoiding an auxiliary parametrization of the Q2 dependence, and final results were obtained via model averaging using the Akaike Information Criterion.

## Key numbers

*   Nucleon isovector magnetic moment: mu = 4.71(11)_stat(13)_sys, in good agreement with the experimental value.
*   Isovector magnetic square-radius: <r_M^2> = 0.661(30)_stat(11)_sys fm^2, in good agreement with the value inferred from the ep-scattering determination of the proton radius (Bernauer et al., 2010).
*   Isovector electric charge radius: <r_E^2> = 0.800(25)_stat(22)_sys fm^2, in slight tension with the larger value from ep-scattering data but in agreement with the 2018 CODATA average for the proton charge radius.
*   The isovector anomalous magnetic moment kappa = 3.71 +/- 0.11 +/- 0.13, where the first and second errors refer to statistical uncertainty and total systematic error, respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of nucleon form factors, including the authors' own earlier work on Nf=2 ensembles (Capitani, 2015), by using Nf=2+1 ensembles with physical pion masses and improved control over excited-state contamination. The results address the proton radius puzzle by providing a lattice determination of the isovector electric charge radius that favors the smaller value from muonic hydrogen spectroscopy over the larger value from electron-proton scattering (Bernauer et al., 2010). The direct covariant BChPT fitting method developed here enables more precise extractions of low-Q2 observables and can be applied to future lattice calculations with higher statistics or additional ensembles.
