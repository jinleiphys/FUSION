# Neutron star tidal deformabilities constrained by nuclear theory and experiment

> We confront observational data from gravitational wave event GW170817 with microscopic modeling of the cold neutron star equation of state. We develop and employ a Bayesian statistical framework that enables us to implement constraints on the equation of state from laboratory measurements of nuclei and state-of-the-art chiral effective field theory methods. The energy density functionals constructed from the posterior probability distributions are then used to compute consistently the neutron star equation of state from the outer crust to the inner core, assuming a composition consisting of protons, neutrons, electrons, and muons. In contrast to previous studies, we find that the 95% credibility range of predicted neutron star tidal deformabilities ($136 < Λ< 519$) for a 1.4 solar-mass neutron star is already consistent with the upper bound deduced from observations of the GW170817 event. However, we find that lower bounds on the neutron star tidal deformability will very strongly constrain microscopic models of the dense matter equation of state. We also demonstrate a strong correlation between the neutron star tidal deformability and the pressure of beta-equilibrated matter at twice saturation density.

## Key claim

The 95% credibility range of predicted neutron star tidal deformabilities (136 < Λ < 519) for a 1.4 solar-mass neutron star is already consistent with the upper bound deduced from observations of GW170817, but lower bounds on the tidal deformability will very strongly constrain microscopic models of the dense matter equation of state.

## Method

The authors constructed parametric equations of state for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter using a Bayesian statistical framework. Prior distribution functions were obtained from chiral effective field theory predictions up to twice nuclear saturation density, while likelihood functions incorporated empirical information on nuclear matter properties near saturation density. The energy density functionals from the posterior probability distributions were used to compute the neutron star equation of state from the outer crust to the inner core, assuming a composition of protons, neutrons, electrons, and muons.

## Key numbers

- Tidal deformability Λ for a 1.4 solar-mass neutron star: 95% credibility range 136 < Λ < 519; 68% credibility range 256 < Λ < 442
- Radius for a 1.4 solar-mass neutron star: range 10.42 km < R < 12.80 km, distribution peaks at R ≈ 11.89 km
- Binary tidal deformability distribution peaks at Λ̃ = 402.23 (+147.72, -183.49) for high-spin priors and Λ̃ = 418.11 (+142.02, -172.46) for low-spin priors
- Empirical correlation between tidal deformability Λ of a 1.4 solar-mass neutron star and pressure at twice saturation density: Λ = 31.59 (p/MeV fm⁻³) - 272.36
- Nuclear matter saturation density n₀ = 0.160 ± 0.003 fm⁻³, saturation energy B = 15.939 ± 0.149 MeV, incompressibility K = 232.65 ± 7.00 MeV, skewness Q = -373.26 ± 13.91 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Lim (2017), Zhang (2018), and Du (2018) that implemented chiral effective field theory constraints in mean field modeling of the nuclear energy density functional. In contrast to Annala (2018), the authors find that the upper bound on tidal deformability from GW170817 does not strongly constrain their modeling, likely due to differences in the transition density where chiral EFT results are replaced by polytropic extrapolations. The framework enables future statistical inferences of neutron star properties through combination of laboratory measurements and microscopic modeling, and the authors note a similar concurrent study by Most (2018) that reaches some of the same conclusions.
