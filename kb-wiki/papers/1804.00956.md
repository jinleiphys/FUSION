# Elastic Compton Scattering from 3He and the Role of the Delta

> We report observables for elastic Compton scattering from $^3$He in Chiral Effective Field Theory with an explicit $Δ(1232)$ degree of freedom ($χ$EFT) for energies between 50 and 120 MeV. The $γ\,{}^3$He amplitude is complete at N3LO, $\mathcal{O}(e^2δ^3)$, and in general converges well order by order. It includes the dominant pion-loop and two-body currents, as well as the Delta excitation in the single-nucleon amplitude. Since the cross section is two to three times that for deuterium and the spin of polarised $^3$He is predominantly carried by its constituent neutron, elastic Compton scattering promises information on both the scalar and spin polarisabilities of the neutron. We study in detail the sensitivities of 4 observables to the neutron polarisabilities: the cross section, the beam asymmetry and two double asymmetries resulting from circularly polarised photons and a longitudinally or transversely polarised target. Including the Delta enhances those asymmetries from which neutron spin polarisabilities could be extracted. We also correct previous, erroneous results at N2LO, i.e.~without an explicit Delta, and compare to the same observables on proton, neutron and deuterium targets. An interactive Mathematica notebook of our results is available from hgrie@gwu.edu.

## Key claim

Elastic Compton scattering from 3He in Chiral Effective Field Theory with an explicit Delta(1232) degree of freedom provides observables that promise information on both the scalar and spin polarisabilities of the neutron, with the cross section being two to three times that for deuterium and the spin of polarised 3He predominantly carried by its constituent neutron.

## Method

The authors computed the gamma-3He amplitude at N3LO, O(e^2 delta^3), in Chiral Effective Field Theory including an explicit Delta(1232) degree of freedom, for photon energies between 50 and 120 MeV. The calculation incorporated the dominant pion-loop and two-body currents, as well as the Delta excitation in the single-nucleon amplitude, and evaluated the photonuclear operator between 3He wave functions derived from NN and 3N potentials using the same perturbative ChiEFT expansion.

## Key numbers

* The cross section for elastic Compton scattering from 3He is two to three times that for deuterium in the energy range 50 to 120 MeV.
* The calculation is complete at N3LO, O(e^2 delta^3), for energies between 50 and 120 MeV.
* The Delta-nucleon mass splitting is approximately 300 MeV.
* The extracted neutron electric dipole polarisability from deuterium data is alpha_E1^(n) = 11.55 +/- 1.25 (stat.) +/- 0.2 (Baldin) +/- 0.8 (theory) in units of 10^{-4} fm^3.
* The extracted neutron magnetic dipole polarisability from deuterium data is beta_M1^(n) = 3.65 +/- 1.25 (stat.) +/- 0.2 (Baldin) +/- 0.8 (theory) in units of 10^{-4} fm^3.
* The Baldin sum rule value for the neutron is alpha_E1^(n) + beta_M1^(n) = 15.2 +/- 0.4.
* The extracted proton electric dipole polarisability is alpha_E1^(p) = 10.65 +/- 0.35 (stat.) +/- 0.2 (Baldin) +/- 0.3 (theory) in units of 10^{-4} fm^3.
* The extracted proton magnetic dipole polarisability is beta_M1^(p) = 3.15 +/- 0.35 (stat.) +/- 0.2 (Baldin) +/- 0.3 (theory) in units of 10^{-4} fm^3.
* The Baldin sum rule value for the proton is alpha_E1^(p) + beta_M1^(p) = 13.8 +/- 0.4.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Shukla et al. (2008, 2017) at O(Q^3) in ChiEFT by one order in the chiral counting, incorporating the leading effects of the Delta(1232) resonance. It also corrects previous erroneous results at N2LO (without an explicit Delta) and compares observables to those on proton, neutron, and deuterium targets. The results enable planning of experiments at MAMI and HIgammaS facilities to extract neutron polarisabilities from elastic gamma-3He scattering, complementing deuteron data which only provides access to isoscalar polarisabilities.
