# Empirical constraints on the high-density equation of state from multi-messenger observables

> We search for possible correlations between neutron star observables and thermodynamic quantities that characterize high density nuclear matter. We generate a set of model-independent equations of state describing stellar matter from a Taylor expansion around saturation density. Each equation of state which is a functional of the nuclear matter parameters is thermodynamically consistent, causal and compatible with astrophysical observations. We find that the neutron star tidal deformability and radius are strongly correlated with the pressure, the energy density and the sound velocity at different densities. Similar correlations are also exhibited by a large set of mean-field models based on non-relativistic and relativistic nuclear energy density functionals. These model independent correlations can be employed to constrain the equation of state at different densities above saturation from measurements of NS properties with multi-messenger observations. In particular, precise constraints on the radius of PSR J0030+0451 thanks to NICER observations would allow to better infer the properties of matter around two times the nuclear saturation density.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that neutron star tidal deformability and radius are strongly correlated with the pressure, energy density, and sound velocity at specific densities above saturation, and these correlations appear to be model-independent across both meta-models and a diverse set of nuclear mean-field models, though the correlations are weaker at high densities for the meta-models compared to nuclear models.

## Method

The authors generated a set of 2121 thermodynamically consistent, causal equations of state by randomly sampling an 8-dimensional parameter space of nuclear matter empirical parameters via a multivariate Gaussian with zero covariance, then imposing filters for thermodynamic stability, causality, maximum mass above 1.97 solar masses, tidal deformability between 70 and 580 for a 1.4 solar mass star, and positive symmetry energy. They computed neutron star mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations, then calculated Pearson correlation coefficients between thermodynamic quantities (pressure, energy density, sound velocity) at each baryonic density and neutron star observables (radius, tidal deformability, Love number) for masses from 1.0 to 1.8 solar masses.

## Key numbers

- The set of 2121 valid equations of state was obtained from 10 million sampled EoS after applying all filters
- Mean radius for a 1.4 solar mass star: R = 12.18 ± 0.21 km (mean ± standard deviation)
- Mean tidal deformability for a 1.4 solar mass star: Λ = 467.53 ± 57.89
- Maximum correlation between pressure and Λ for 1.4 solar masses occurs at n = 0.340 fm⁻³ with correlation coefficient 0.982 for meta-models and 0.992 for nuclear models
- Maximum correlation between pressure and R for 1.4 solar masses occurs at n = 0.311 fm⁻³ with correlation coefficient 0.962 for meta-models and 0.980 for nuclear models
- Maximum correlation between energy density and R for 1.4 solar masses occurs at n = 0.413 fm⁻³ with correlation coefficient 0.984 for meta-models and 0.995 for nuclear models
- Maximum correlation between sound velocity and Λ for 1.4 solar masses occurs at n = 0.268 fm⁻³ with correlation coefficient 0.953 for meta-models and 0.987 for nuclear models
- Constrained pressure at n = 0.340 fm⁻³ from Λ range 243.53 to 579.93: P = [17.82, 34.56] MeV/fm³
- Constrained energy density at n = 0.456 fm⁻³ from Λ range 243.53 to 579.93: E = [452.22, 468.22] MeV/fm³
- Constrained sound velocity at n = 0.268 fm⁻³ from Λ range 243.53 to 579.93: v_s = [0.325, 0.446] c

## Context

This work extends the empirical correlation first identified by Lattimer and Prakash (2001) between neutron star radius and pressure at densities between 1.5 and 2-3 times saturation density, showing that similar strong correlations exist for tidal deformability and energy density. The study uses a meta-model approach from Margueron et al. (2018) that avoids the model dependence seen in earlier correlation studies using specific nuclear models such as those by Vidana et al. (2009), Xu et al. (2009), and Alam et al. (2016). The results enable future multi-messenger observations, particularly precise radius measurements from the NICER mission for pulsars like PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0437-4715, to directly constrain the equation of state at specific densities above saturation, complementing constraints from the GW170817 gravitational wave event analyzed by the LIGO/Virgo collaboration (2017, 2018).
