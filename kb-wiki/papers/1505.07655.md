# QCD Equation of State and Cosmological Parameters in Early Universe

> The time evolution of cosmological parameters in early Universe at the deconfinement transition is studied by an equation of state (EoS) which takes into account the finite baryon density and the background magnetic field. The non perturbative dynamics is described by the Field Correlator Method (FCM) which gives, with a small number of free parameters, a good fit of lattice data. The entire system has two components, i.e. the quark-gluon plasma and the electroweak sector, and the solutions of the Friedmann equation show that the scale factor, $a(t)$, and $H(t)= (1/a)da/dt$ are weakly dependent on the EoS, but the deceleration parameter, $q(t)$, and the jerk, $j(t)$, are strongly modified above the critical temperature $T_c$, corresponding to a critical time $t_c \simeq 20-25 μs$. The time evolution of the cosmological parameters suggest that above and around $T_c$ there is a transient state of acceleration typical of a matter dominated Universe; this is entailed by the QCD strong interaction driven by the presence of massive colored objects.

# QCD Equation of State and Cosmological Parameters in Early Universe

## Key claim
The scale factor a(t) and Hubble parameter H(t) are weakly dependent on the equation of state during the QCD deconfinement transition, but the deceleration parameter q(t) and jerk j(t) are strongly modified above the critical temperature Tc, suggesting a transient matter-dominated state driven by massive colored objects rather than a purely radiation-dominated expansion.

## Method
The authors solve the Friedmann equation using an equation of state derived from the Field Correlator Method (FCM), which provides a non-perturbative description of QCD thermodynamics with a small number of free parameters and fits lattice data. The full system includes two components: the quark-gluon plasma (described by FCM with finite baryon chemical potential μB and background magnetic field B) and the electroweak sector (treated as free massless particles with g_ew = 14.45 degrees of freedom). The time evolution of temperature T(t), the ratio p/ε, and the cosmological parameters q(t) and j(t) are computed for various combinations of μB and B.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature Tc ≈ 155 ± 15 MeV (from lattice QCD, cited as Fodor and Karsch)
- Critical time tc ≈ 20-25 μs for the deconfinement transition at μB = 0, B = 0
- Critical times for different parameter sets: tc = 24 μs (μB = 0, B = 0), 21.5 μs (μB = 0.4 GeV, B = 0), 15.6 μs (μB = 0, B = 0.6 GeV^2), and 13.6 μs (μB = 0.4 GeV, B = 0.6 GeV^2)
- Initial conditions: ti = 1 μs, Ti ≈ 500 MeV, εi ≈ 110 GeV/fm^3
- Background magnetic field value considered: eB = 0.6 GeV^2
- Chemical potential considered: μB = 0.4 GeV
- Quark masses used in FCM: m_d = 5 MeV, m_u = 10 MeV, m_s = 140 MeV
- For a matter-dominated flat Universe: q = 1/2, j = 1; for radiation-dominated: q = 1, j = 3
- The parameter R (radiation term) decreases from ≈ 1 to ≈ 0.3 at t ≈ 25 μs and to ≈ 0 at t ≈ 32 μs; A (matter term) increases to ≈ 1 at t ≈ 25 μs with a maximum A ≈ 1.8 at t ≈ 35 μs
- Quasi-particle effective masses near Tc are in the range ≈ 0.6 - 1.2 GeV for μB = 0, B = 0

## Context
This work extends previous studies of density fluctuations in the early Universe that used the MIT bag model equation of state (Castorina et al. 2014) and older lattice QCD equations of state with a first-order deconfinement transition. It improves upon those analyses by using the Field Correlator Method, which provides a realistic QCD equation of state that includes finite baryon density and background magnetic field effects, and by focusing on the cosmological parameters q(t) and j(t) rather than solely on thermodynamic fluctuations or the scale factor. The results enable future work incorporating the hadronic phase below Tc, which the authors note would likely extend the matter-dominated dynamics beyond t ≈ 100 fm/c, and suggest that the transient matter-dominated state near Tc may be relevant for gravitational wave emission studies.
