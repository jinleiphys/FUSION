# Viscous hydrodynamics description of $ϕ$ meson production in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions

> In the Israel-Stewart's theory of 2nd order dissipative hydrodynamics, we have simulated $ϕ$ production in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at $\sqrt{s}_{NN}$=200 GeV. Evolution of QGP fluid with viscosity over the entropy ratio $η/s$=0.25, thermalised at $τ_i$=0.2 fm, with initial energy density $ε_i$=5.1 $GeV/fm^3$ explains the experimental data on $ϕ$ multiplicity, integrated $v_2$, mean $p_T$, $p_T$ spectra and elliptic flow in central and mid-central Au+Au collisions. $η/s$=0.25 is also consistent with centrality dependence of $ϕ$ $p_T$ spectra in Cu+Cu collisions. The central energy density in Cu+Cu collisions is $ε_i$=3.48 $GeV/fm^3$.

# Viscous hydrodynamics description of φ meson production in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions

## Key claim

The paper claims that a specific value of shear viscosity to entropy ratio, η/s = 0.25, is consistent with STAR measurements of φ meson multiplicity, mean pT, pT spectra, and elliptic flow in both Au+Au and Cu+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, but only for central and mid-central collisions and only up to pT ≈ 3 GeV; the model fails for peripheral collisions and for the N(Ω)/N(φ) ratio.

## Method

The authors solved the Israel-Stewart 2nd-order dissipative hydrodynamic equations (boost-invariant, using the AZHYDRO-KOLKATA code) for a QGP fluid with constant η/s = 0.25, initial thermalization time τi = 0.2 fm, and a lattice-based equation of state with a crossover transition. Initial energy density profiles were taken from a Glauber model (75% participants, 25% binary collisions), with the central energy density εi fitted to reproduce φ multiplicity in the most central collisions: 5.1 GeV/fm³ for Au+Au and 3.48 GeV/fm³ for Cu+Cu. Freeze-out was set at TF = 150 MeV, and the Cooper-Frye formalism was used to compute particle spectra.

## Key numbers

- η/s = 0.25: the viscosity over entropy ratio used for all simulations, consistent with STAR φ data in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions.
- εi = 5.1 GeV/fm³: central energy density in b = 0 Au+Au collisions at τi = 0.2 fm.
- εi = 3.48 GeV/fm³: central energy density in b = 0 Cu+Cu collisions at τi = 0.2 fm.
- τi = 0.2 fm: initial thermalization time used in all simulations.
- TF = 150 MeV: freeze-out temperature used in all simulations.
- εco ≈ 1.5 GeV/fm³: energy density for the confinement-deconfinement crossover transition.
- εi(Au)/εi(Cu) ≈ A^(1/3)_Au / A^(1/3)_Cu: the ratio of central energy densities scales with nuclear radius.
- φ pT spectra are reproduced up to pT ≈ 3 GeV in both Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions; beyond 3 GeV, viscous hydrodynamics underpredicts the spectra.
- Integrated v2 in Au+Au is reproduced for 0-5%, 10-40%, and 0-80% centrality but overpredicted in 40-80% centrality.
- Predicted elliptic flow in Cu+Cu collisions is approximately 10% less than in Au+Au collisions.
- The N(Ω)/N(φ) ratio in Au+Au collisions is underpredicted by about 40% in 0-12% centrality at pT ≤ 3 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier analysis (Chaudhuri, 2009) which showed that φ mean pT in Au+Au collisions rejects ideal fluid or low-viscosity fluids (η/s ≤ 0.08-0.16) and requires η/s = 0.25. It provides the first viscous hydrodynamic description of φ production in Cu+Cu collisions at RHIC. The result η/s = 0.25 is consistent with the lower bound from ADS/CFT (η/s ≥ 1/4π ≈ 0.08) and with estimates from elliptic flow scaling (Drescher, 2007: η/s = 0.11-0.19; Lacey, 2006: η/s ≈ 0.09 ± 0.015) and from heavy quark energy loss (PHENIX, 2006: η/s ≈ 0.1-0.16). The initial energy density (5.1 GeV/fm³) is much lower than the ideal hydrodynamics estimate of ~30 GeV/fm³ (QGP3), consistent with entropy production in viscous evolution. The work enables future tests of viscous hydrodynamics predictions for φ elliptic flow in Cu+Cu collisions and highlights the need for improved modeling of Ω production and the N(Ω)/N(φ) ratio.
