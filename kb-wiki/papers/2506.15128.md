# Ambiguities in the Partial-Wave Analysis of the Photoproduction of Pairs of Pseudoscalar Mesons

> Applying the technique of partial-wave analysis, there are cases where more than one set of underlying complex-valued amplitudes can describe the measured observables. These ambiguities can sometimes be resolved using additional information, but assumptions are often required. It is known that the partial-wave analysis of two-pseudoscalar meson systems produced in photoproduction with a linearly polarized photon beam is free from discrete ambiguities stemming from the Barrelet zeros when the nucleon spin is ignored. In this article, we show that continuous ambiguities are possible for certain wave sets, even though the discrete ambiguities do not appear. We also explore ways to resolve these ambiguities and determine the maximal amount of information that can be obtained from analyses that suffer from these continuous ambiguities.

## Key claim

The paper shows that continuous ambiguities (not just discrete Barrelet-zero ambiguities) can arise in partial-wave analysis of two-pseudoscalar meson photoproduction with linearly polarized photons when both reflectivities are populated, even though the discrete ambiguities are absent when nucleon spin is ignored.

## Method

The authors formulate the intensity distribution for photoproduction of two-pseudoscalar meson systems in terms of moments of spherical harmonics and partial-wave amplitudes in the reflectivity basis. They construct a matrix representation relating the moments to bilinear products of partial-wave amplitudes and determine the rank of the coefficient matrix relative to the number of free parameters. Monte Carlo pseudo-data are generated using the intensity formula with a polarization degree of 0.35, and unbinned extended maximum likelihood fits are performed using the AmpTools framework with 100 random starting values per wave set.

## Key numbers

- Degree of linear polarization \(P_{\gamma}\) set to 0.35, matching the GlueX experiment
- \(10^6\) events generated for each wave set, with sum of squared magnitudes of all amplitudes normalized to 1
- For the \(\{S_0^+, D_{+1}^+, D_0^+, D_{-1}^+\}\) wave set: 18 non-zero moments, rank of coefficient matrix is 10, with \(N_{\text{par}} = 7\) free parameters (4 magnitudes, 3 phase differences)
- For the \(\{P_{+1}^{\pm}, P_{-1}^{\pm}\}\) wave set: 7 non-zero moments, rank of coefficient matrix is 5, with \(N_{\text{par}} = 6\) free parameters (4 magnitudes, 2 phase differences)
- For the \(\{S_0^{\pm}, P_{+1}^{\pm}, P_0^{\pm}, P_{-1}^{\pm}\}\) wave set: no continuous or discrete ambiguities found

## Context

This work extends the ambiguity study of Chung (1997) on Barrelet zeros in spinless beam production and the JPAC collaboration study (2023) on discrete ambiguities in linearly polarized photoproduction. The JPAC study showed that discrete ambiguities from Barrelet zeros do not appear when nucleon spin is ignored, but only considered single-reflectivity wave sets. The present paper demonstrates that continuous ambiguities emerge when both reflectivities are populated, as in the simple \(\{P_{+1}^{\pm}, P_{-1}^{\pm}\}\) wave set used for vector meson photoproduction like \(\rho(770)\). This enables experimentalists to identify which wave sets in GlueX analyses may yield non-unique amplitude solutions and to understand the maximal information retrievable from such ambiguous fits.
