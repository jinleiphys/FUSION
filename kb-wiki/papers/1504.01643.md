# Eta photoproduction in a combined analysis of pion- and photon-induced reactions

> The $ηN$ final state is isospin-selective and thus provides access to the spectrum of excited nucleons without being affected by excited $Δ$ states. To this end, the world database on eta photoproduction off the proton up to a center-of-mass energy of $E\sim 2.3$ GeV is analyzed, including data on differential cross sections, and single and double polarization observables. The resonance spectrum and its properties are determined in a combined analysis of eta and pion photoproduction off the proton together with the reactions $πN\to πN$, $ηN$, $KΛ$ and $KΣ$. For the analysis, the so-called Jülich coupled-channel framework is used, incorporating unitarity, analyticity, and effective three-body channels. Parameters tied to photoproduction and hadronic interactions are varied simultaneously. The influence of recent MAMI $T$ and $F$ asymmetry data on the eta photoproduction amplitude is discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The combined analysis of eta photoproduction data together with pion-induced reactions allows one to determine resonance parameters and hadronic branching ratios without relying on the problematic pion-induced eta production data, but the authors note that this argumentation is limited by the fact that even for pion photoproduction the database is not yet fully complete, and the extension to eta photoproduction will lead to changes both in helicity couplings and branching ratios.

## Method

The authors use the Jülich dynamical coupled-channel (DCC) framework, which iterates hadronic scattering potentials in a Lippmann-Schwinger equation formulated in time-ordered perturbation theory, incorporating two- and three-body unitarity, analyticity, and effective three-body channels (ρN, σN, πΔ). The photoproduction kernel is approximated by energy-dependent polynomials, with the hadronic final-state interaction provided by the Jülich DCC model. Parameters tied to both photoproduction and hadronic interactions are varied simultaneously in a fit to the world database on πN → πN, πN → ηN, KΛ, KΣ, and the photon-induced reactions γp → π⁰p, π⁺n, and ηp up to a center-of-mass energy of about 2.3 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The world database on eta photoproduction off the proton is analyzed up to a center-of-mass energy of E ∼ 2.3 GeV.
- Fit A includes 29,392 data points for γp → ηp (differential cross section, recoil polarization P, beam asymmetry Σ); fit B includes 29,680 data points by adding the recent MAMI T and F asymmetry data.
- The number of s-channel resonance parameters amounts to 128, with 11 genuine I=1/2 and 10 genuine I=3/2 resonances.
- The number of parameters tied to the photon interaction is 443 in fit A and 456 in fit B, for a total of 580 (fit A) and 593 (fit B) free parameters.
- An additional systematic error of 5% is applied to all photon-induced data to account for discrepancies.
- The T and F data in eta photoproduction are weighted with generic factors around 10 to compensate for their smaller number of data points.

## Context

This work extends the Jülich coupled-channel approach, previously applied to pion-induced reactions (Jülich2012 model, Rönchen et al. 2012) and pion photoproduction (Rönchen et al. 2014), to include eta photoproduction in a simultaneous analysis. The combined fit allows the higher-quality photoproduction data to constrain the hadronic πN → ηN amplitude, which is poorly determined from the problematic pion-induced data (Clajus 1992, Arndt 2006). The analysis enables a more reliable extraction of N* resonance parameters and ηN branching ratios, and the inclusion of recent MAMI T and F asymmetry data (Akondi 2014) demonstrates their influence on the extracted resonance spectrum.
