# Violation of Factorization Theorem in QCD and QED For Non-Light-Like Wilson Line

> By using path integral formulation of QCD and QED we prove that the factorization theorem is valid for light-like Wilson line but is not valid for non-light-like Wilson line. This conclusion is shown to be consistent with Ward identity and Grammer-Yennie approximation. Hence we find that the factorization theorem is violated in all the previous studies which used the non-light-like Wilson line in the definition of the (transverse momentum dependent) parton distribution function and fragmentation function at high energy colliders.

## Key claim

The factorization theorem for soft-collinear divergences is valid when a light-like Wilson line is used (which produces pure gauge potential with zero field strength), but is not valid when a non-light-like Wilson line is used (which produces non-zero field strength of arbitrary form that cannot be gauged away). This conclusion is shown to be consistent with Ward identity and the Grammer-Yennie approximation, implying that all previous studies using non-light-like Wilson lines in the definition of transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions and fragmentation functions violate the factorization theorem.

## Method

The author uses the path integral formulation of QCD and QED to prove factorization of soft-collinear divergences at all orders in the coupling constant, working in multiple gauges including covariant gauge, general axial gauge, light-cone gauge, general non-covariant gauge, and general Coulomb gauge. The proof relies on the property that a light-like Wilson line produces a pure gauge potential (U(1) pure gauge in QED, with field strength \(F_{\mu\nu}^a(x)=0\)), which can be absorbed via a field redefinition \(\psi'(x)=e^{ie\omega(x)}\psi(x)\) to factorize the soft-collinear divergences. For non-light-like Wilson lines, the background field is not pure gauge (\(F_{\mu\nu}^a(x)\neq 0\)), so the same field redefinition cannot eliminate the dependence on the Wilson line path, and the factorization fails.

## Key numbers

- The Eikonal Feynman rule for soft-collinear divergences is given by \(e\frac{p^\mu}{p\cdot k+i\epsilon}\) (equation for QED), where the collinear divergence condition requires \(m=0\) and \(\theta=0\) (the angle between \(\vec{p}\) and \(\vec{k}\)).
- For a massive electron, \(p\cdot k = |\vec{p}||\vec{k}|(\sqrt{1+m^2/\vec{p}^2} - \cos\theta)\), showing that collinear divergence does not appear in QED because \(m\neq 0\).
- The light-like Wilson line in QED for soft-collinear divergences is expressed as \(e^{ie\int_{x_i}^{x_f} dx^\mu A_\mu(x)} = e^{-ie\int_0^\infty d\lambda\, l\cdot A(x_f+l\lambda)} e^{ie\int_0^\infty d\lambda\, l\cdot A(x_i+l\lambda)}\), where \(l^\mu\) is the light-like four-velocity.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous findings on factorization in NRQCD heavy quarkonium production, where factorization was proven for light-like Wilson lines (Nayak and Sterman, 2012) and shown to break down for non-light-like Wilson lines (Nayak and Sterman, 2013). The paper directly contradicts the approach of Collins (2011) and Collins (2013), who used non-light-like Wilson lines in diagrammatic one-loop perturbative QCD calculations of transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions. The author argues that perturbative methods are insufficient for non-perturbative quantities like parton distribution functions, and that the path integral method (following Tucci, 1982) provides an all-orders proof. This work enables a re-evaluation of factorization proofs in high-energy collider physics, particularly for processes involving transverse momentum dependent distributions.
