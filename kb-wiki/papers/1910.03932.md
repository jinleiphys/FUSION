# Crust of accreting neutron stars within simplified reaction network

> Transiently accreting neutron stars in low mass X-ray binaries are generally believed to be heated up by nuclear reactions in accreted matter during hydrostatic compression. Detailed modeling of these reactions is required for the correct interpretation of observations. In this paper, we construct a simplified reaction network, which can be easily implemented and depends mainly on atomic mass tables as nuclear physics input. We show that it reproduces results of the detailed network by Lau et al. (2018) very well, if one applies the same mass model. However, the composition and the heating power are shown to be sensitive to the mass table used and treatment of mass tables boundary, if one applies several of them in one simulation. In particular, the impurity parameter $Q_\mathrm{imp}$ at density $ρ=2\times 10^{12}$ g cm$^{-3}$ can differ for a factor of few, and even increase with density increase. The profile of integrated heat realize shown to be well confined between results by Fantina et al. (2018) and Lau et al. (2018).

# Crust of accreting neutron stars within simplified reaction network

## Key claim

The authors construct a simplified reaction network that reproduces results of the detailed network by Lau et al. (2018) very well when the same mass model is applied, but the composition and heating power are sensitive to the mass table used and the treatment of mass table boundaries when several tables are combined in one simulation.

## Method

The authors implement a stepwise reaction network that applies priority rules based on typical reaction timescales (neutron emission fastest, then neutron capture, two-neutron capture, electron capture, and pycnonuclear fusion slowest) to mimic fast equilibration over the fastest reactions. At each pressure step, only a "chunk" of 0.001 of total nuclei undergoes reaction, and the Gibbs energy change determines whether reactions are allowed. The network depends mainly on atomic mass tables as nuclear physics input, with the accretion rate fixed to 8.8 × 10^4 g cm^−2 s^−1 and temperature assumed to be 5 × 10^8 K throughout the crust.

## Key numbers

- Impurity parameter Q_imp at density ρ = 2 × 10^12 g cm^−3 can differ by a factor of few depending on the mass table used
- Initial pressure set to P = 6 × 10^26 dynes cm^−2 for starting calculations
- Simulations stop at maximal pressure P = 3.9 × 10^30 dynes cm^−2, matching Lau et al. (2018)
- Accretion rate fixed to ṁ = 8.8 × 10^4 g cm^−2 s^−1 (0.3 of the Eddington accretion rate)
- Gravitational acceleration g = 1.85 × 10^14 cm s^−2
- Chunk size α = 0.001 of total nuclei amount used for stepwise reactions
- First unbound neutrons form at density ρ ≈ 8 × 10^11 g cm^−3 from electron capture by ^56Ar
- Pycnonuclear fusion occurs at density ∼ 2 × 10^12 g cm^−3 in form of fusion-SEC cycle
- Electron capture threshold for ^56Fe increases from 4.0 MeV to 4.1 MeV when experimental data for excited states of ^56Mn are applied
- Theoretical model for excited states of ^56Mn defers electron capture to μ_e = 6.2 MeV in the model applied by Lau et al. (2018)

## Context

This work extends the multicomponent reaction network approach of Lau et al. (2018) by providing a simplified model that depends mainly on atomic mass tables, making it easier to analyze nuclear physics uncertainties. The results are compared with the one-component composition models of Haensel and Zdunik (1990, 2003, 2008) and Fantina et al. (2018), and the profile of integrated heat release is shown to be well confined between results by Fantina et al. (2018) and Lau et al. (2018). The simplified network enables systematic studies of how different atomic mass models (FRDM92, FRDM12, HFB21, DZ31) affect predictions for accreted neutron star crust composition and heating, which is crucial for interpreting thermal emission from quiescent low-mass X-ray binaries.
