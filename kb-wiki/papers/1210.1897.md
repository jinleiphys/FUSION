# Ab initio description of the exotic unbound 7He nucleus

> The neutron rich exotic unbound 7He nucleus has been the subject of many experimental investigations. While the ground-state 3/2- resonance is well established, there is a controversy concerning the excited 1/2- resonance reported in some experiments as low-lying and narrow (E_R ~ 1 MeV, Gamma < 1 MeV) while in others as very broad and located at a higher energy. This issue cannot be addressed by ab initio theoretical calculations based on traditional bound-state methods. We introduce a new unified approach to nuclear bound and continuum states based on the coupling of the no-core shell model, a bound-state technique, with the no-core shell model/resonating group method, a nuclear scattering technique. Our calculations describe the ground-state resonance in agreement with experiment and, at the same time, predict a broad 1/2- resonance above 2 MeV.

## Key claim

The authors predict a broad \(1/2^-\) resonance in \(^7\)He above 2 MeV, contradicting the hypothesis of a low-lying narrow \(1/2^-\) state near 1 MeV, but the calculation uses a two-nucleon potential without three-nucleon forces and depends on the SRG evolution parameter \(\Lambda = 2.02\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).

## Method

The authors couple the no-core shell model (NCSM), a bound-state technique, with the no-core shell model/resonating group method (NCSM/RGM), a nuclear scattering technique, to form the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC). They solve the coupled-channel equations using a microscopic R-matrix method on a Lagrange mesh, employing a similarity-renormalization-group (SRG) evolved chiral N\(^3\)LO \(NN\) potential with \(\Lambda = 2.02\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) and a harmonic oscillator basis with \(N_{\text{max}} = 12\) and \(\hbar\Omega = 16\ \text{MeV}\). The NCSMC wave function includes an expansion over \(A\)-body NCSM eigenstates of \(^7\)He coupled to \(n+^6\)He binary-cluster states, with the \(^6\)He target described by its three lowest eigenstates (\(0^+\), \(2^+_1\), \(2^+_2\)).

## Key numbers

- Ground-state (\(3/2^-\)) resonance of \(^7\)He: NCSMC centroid \(E_R = 0.71\ \text{MeV}\) and width \(\Gamma = 0.30\ \text{MeV}\), compared to experimental values \(E_R = 0.430(3)\ \text{MeV}\) and \(\Gamma = 0.182(5)\ \text{MeV}\) from Cao (2012).
- Excited \(5/2^-\) resonance: NCSMC centroid \(E_R = 3.13\ \text{MeV}\) and width \(\Gamma = 1.07\ \text{MeV}\), compared to experimental \(E_R = 3.35(10)\ \text{MeV}\) and \(\Gamma = 1.99(17)\ \text{MeV}\) from Tilley (2002).
- Excited \(1/2^-\) resonance: NCSMC centroid \(E_R = 2.39\ \text{MeV}\) and width \(\Gamma = 2.89\ \text{MeV}\), in fair agreement with the neutron pick-up experiment of Wuosmaa (2005) (\(E_R = 3.03(10)\ \text{MeV}\), \(\Gamma \approx 2\ \text{MeV}\)) and not supporting the low-lying narrow resonance (\(E_R \sim 1\ \text{MeV}\), \(\Gamma \leq 1\ \text{MeV}\)) reported by Meister (2002).
- NCSM ground-state energy of \(^7\)He at \(N_{\text{max}} = 12\): \(-27.33\ \text{MeV}\); extrapolated value: \(-28.27(25)\ \text{MeV}\) (experimental: \(-28.84\ \text{MeV}\)).
- NCSM ground-state energy of \(^6\)He at \(N_{\text{max}} = 12\): \(-28.63\ \text{MeV}\); extrapolated value: \(-29.25(15)\ \text{MeV}\) (experimental: \(-29.27\ \text{MeV}\)).

## Context

This work extends ab initio calculations of unbound nuclei beyond traditional bound-state methods such as Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC), the no-core shell model (NCSM), and the coupled cluster method (CCM), which could not address the controversy over the \(1/2^-\) resonance in \(^7\)He. The NCSMC approach resolves the discrepancy between experiments reporting a low-lying narrow \(1/2^-\) state (e.g., Markenroth (2001), Meister (2002)) and those finding a broad resonance at higher energy (e.g., Wuosmaa (2005), Boutachkov (2005)). The method enables future studies of composite projectiles and inclusion of three-nucleon forces.
