# Theory Uncertainty in Extracting the Proton's Weak Charge: White Paper

> We review the state-of-the-art and address open questions relative to the theory uncertainty of the $γ-Z$ box contribution to the $A_{PV}$ measurement within the QWEAK experiment at Jefferson Lab. This white paper summarizes the contributions by participants and discussion sessions on this topic within the MITP Workshop on Precision Electroweak Physics held in Mainz, Germany, September 23 - October 11, 2013 \url{http://indico.cern.ch/conferenceDisplay.py?confId=248072

# Theory Uncertainty in Extracting the Proton's Weak Charge: White Paper

## Key claim
The three groups that recalculated the gamma-Z box contribution to the parity-violating asymmetry in the QWEAK experiment agree on the size of the correction, but their uncertainty estimates differ by up to a factor of 6, and the largest uncertainty estimate constitutes 2.6% of the Standard Model value of the asymmetry, interfering with the overall 2% theory uncertainty target.

## Method
The authors review and critically assess three independent calculations of the gamma-Z box diagram contribution to the parity-violating asymmetry in elastic electron-proton scattering at QWEAK kinematics (1.165 GeV electrons, 8 degree scattering angle). All three calculations use the same resonance data parametrization by Christy and Bosted, perform isospin decomposition of resonance excitations using conservation of the vector current, and differ primarily in their treatment of the non-resonant background's isospin/flavor decomposition, which is constrained either by vector meson dominance, by deep inelastic scattering data, or by non-relativistic constituent quark models.

## Key numbers
- The QWEAK experiment aims at a 0.3% determination of sin^2 theta_W(0) via a 2.5% measurement of the parity-violating asymmetry in elastic e-p scattering of 1.165 GeV polarized electrons off a hydrogen target at a scattering angle of 8 degrees.
- Hall et al. claim Re Box_gammaZ^V = (5.57 +/- 0.36) x 10^-3
- Carlson and Rislow claim Re Box_gammaZ^V = (5.7 +/- 0.9) x 10^-3
- Gorchtein et al. claim Re Box_gammaZ^V = (5.4 +/- 2.0) x 10^-3
- A previous calculation by Sibirtsev et al. obtained (4.7 +1.1 -0.4) x 10^-3
- The most relevant kinematics within the loop corresponds to Q^2 less than or equal to 3 GeV^2 and W less than or equal to 5 GeV
- The vector meson dominance sum rule measured at DESY with 82 GeV photons revealed a 21% incompleteness of the vector meson basis
- Gorchtein et al. assign a 100% uncertainty to the continuum contribution, resulting in 35% uncertainty relative to the size of Box_gammaZ^V
- Hall et al. obtain a 6% uncertainty relative to the size of Box_gammaZ^V

## Context
This white paper summarizes discussions from the MITP Workshop on Precision Electroweak Physics held in Mainz, Germany in September-October 2013, addressing the theory uncertainty in extracting the proton's weak charge from the QWEAK experiment at Jefferson Lab. It extends the original analysis by Marciano and others, and documents that a 7% effect relative to the Standard Model predicted asymmetry was missed in the original analysis. The paper identifies open questions for a planned December 2013 mini-workshop at Jefferson Lab, and notes that a future measurement of Q_W^p with electron energy below 200 MeV is planned at Mainz MESA/P2 to minimize the uncertainty from the gamma-Z box correction.
