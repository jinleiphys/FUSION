# Lattice QCD constraints on the heavy quark diffusion coefficient

> We report progress towards computing the heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient from the correlator of two chromo-electric fields attached to a Polyakov loop in pure SU(3) gauge theory. Using a multilevel algorithm and tree-level improvement, we study the behavior of the diffusion coefficient as a function of temperature in the wide range $1.1 < T / T_c < 10^4$ in order to compare it to perturbative expansions at high temperature. We find that within errors the lattice results are remarkably compatible with the next-to-leading order perturbative result.

## Key claim

The lattice results for the heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient are "remarkably compatible" with the next-to-leading order perturbative result within errors, but only after accounting for an overall normalization factor that corrects the one-loop renormalization constant; the Euclidean correlator is dominated by the vacuum part of the spectral function, making thermal effects that encode the value of kappa small (at the few-percent level) and thus challenging to extract.

## Method

The heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient was extracted from the Euclidean correlator of two chromoelectric fields attached to a Polyakov loop in pure SU(3) gauge theory, using the standard Wilson gauge action with a multilevel algorithm for noise reduction. Lattice calculations were performed on 48^3 x Nt lattices with Nt = 12, 16, 20, and 24 at temperatures from 1.1 Tc to 10^4 Tc, using tree-level improvement to reduce discretization errors and linear continuum extrapolations in 1/Nt^2. The spectral function was modeled by matching the low-frequency behavior (proportional to kappa times omega) to the zero-temperature NLO perturbative result at high frequency, and the diffusion coefficient was obtained from the intercept of the spectral function at omega = 0 via the Kubo formula.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range studied: 1.1 < T/Tc < 10^4
- Lattice sizes: Nt = 12, 16, 20, 24 with Ns = 48 (except Nt = 12 where Ns = 24, 32, 48 were used for finite-volume checks)
- The continuum-extrapolated lattice result at T = 10^4 Tc differs from the NLO perturbative curve by a factor of 1.2, attributed to inaccuracy in the one-loop renormalization constant
- The double ratio R2(Nt) deviates from 1 at the few-percent level at lower temperatures, with deviations increasing as temperature decreases and as tauT increases
- For tauT < 0.2, the double ratio is close to 1, indicating the correlator is dominated by the T = 0 part of the spectral function
- The continuum extrapolation is considered reliable only for tauT >= 0.175, based on the behavior of the a^2 dependence slope

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of the heavy quark diffusion coefficient in SU(3) gauge theory (Meyer 2010, Francis 2011, Banerjee 2011, Francis 2015, Shu 2019) by covering a much wider temperature range (1.1 Tc to 10^4 Tc) to enable direct comparison with perturbative calculations. The study uses the NLO spectral function from Burnier (2010) to constrain the functional form of the spectral function at high frequencies, and the NLO perturbative result for kappa from Caron-Huot (2008) as a benchmark. The results enable validation of the lattice methodology for extracting kappa and provide temperature-dependent constraints needed for phenomenological models of heavy quark energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma, where a constant kappa/T^3 cannot simultaneously explain the elliptic flow and nuclear modification factor.
