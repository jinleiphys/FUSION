# Charmed hadron production from secondary anti-proton + proton annihilations in p+A reactions at FAIR

> We present estimates for the production cross sections of exotic states ($Λ_c, Σ_c, Ξ_c, D\, \mathrm{and}\, D_s$) from secondary $\overline B + B$ annihilations in p+A reactions from $E_\mathrm{lab}=10-30A$~GeV. We focus specifically on the newly planned hadron physics program of CBM at FAIR. These estimates for the production of exotic states are based on the achievable number of $\overline B + B$ annihilations and their invariant mass distributions calculated in the UrQMD transport model.

## Key claim

Secondary anti-baryon+baryon annihilations in p+Au reactions at FAIR beam energies (10-30A GeV) can produce measurable yields of charmed hadrons (Lambda_c, Sigma_c, Xi_c, D, D_s) and double phi mesons, even though the primary p+p cross sections for charm are strongly suppressed; the authors explicitly state that their estimates provide a lower limit because they do not include production from proton+nucleon interactions.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD transport model (v. 3.4) in cascade mode to simulate minimum-bias p+Au reactions at beam energies of 10, 20, and 30A GeV, extracting the invariant mass distribution of all anti-baryon+baryon annihilations. They then folded this simulated annihilation spectrum with published cross sections for charmed hadron production in antiproton+proton annihilations from Haidenbauer and Krein (2014, 2016), using both meson/baryon exchange and quark model predictions, and scaled by the total antiproton+proton annihilation cross section (approximately 50 mb) to obtain yields per 90-day running period at a design collision rate of 10 MHz.

## Key numbers

- Approximately 25% of produced anti-protons annihilate in the target nucleus in minimum bias p+Au reactions
- For E_lab = 30A GeV, predicted yields per 90 days for Lambda_c+ anti-Lambda_c-: 3.1 x 10^4 (meson/baryon exchange) and 9.0 x 10^3 (quark model)
- For E_lab = 30A GeV, predicted yields per 90 days for D_s+ D_s-: 1.2 x 10^3 (meson/baryon exchange) and 1.6 x 10^3 (quark model)
- For E_lab = 30A GeV, predicted yields per 90 days for Xi_c+ anti-Xi_c-: 1.5 x 10^3 (meson/baryon exchange) and 1.2 x 10^1 (quark model)
- For E_lab = 30A GeV, predicted yields per 90 days for phi phi: 3.9 x 10^7
- For E_lab = 20A GeV, predicted yields per 90 days for Lambda_c+ anti-Lambda_c-: 4.9 x 10^3 (meson/baryon exchange) and 1.4 x 10^3 (quark model)
- For E_lab = 20A GeV, predicted yields per 90 days for phi phi: 1.7 x 10^7
- For E_lab = 10A GeV, the obtained yields are too low to be accessible by the experiment
- The antiproton+proton annihilation cross section used for normalization is approximately 50 mb
- The cross section for antiproton+proton to phi phi from JETSET (1998) is 3 microbarns at center-of-mass energy approximately 2.12 GeV

## Context

This work extends the exploration of charmed hadron production from antiproton+proton annihilations, previously studied for the PANDA experiment at FAIR, to a new experimental scenario using secondary anti-baryons produced and annihilated within the same p+Au target. The authors build on the theoretical cross sections for charmed hadron production in antiproton+proton annihilations calculated by Haidenbauer and Krein (2014, 2016), and on the JETSET (1998) observation of anomalously large phi phi production cross sections that violate the OZI rule. The results enable a potential hadron physics program within the CBM collaboration at FAIR as a bridge toward the delayed PANDA experiment, allowing exploratory studies of charmed mesons and baryons (including D_s and Xi_c) at collision energies where they have never been observed.
