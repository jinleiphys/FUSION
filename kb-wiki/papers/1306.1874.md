# Applications of a nonlinear evolution equation II: the EMC effect

> The EMC effect is studied by using the GLR-MQ-ZSR equation with minimum number of free parameters, where the nuclear shadowing effect is a dynamical evolution result of the equation, and nucleon swelling and Fermi motion in the nuclear environment deform the input parton distributions. Parton distributions of both proton and nucleus are predicted in a unified framework. We show that the parton recombination as a higher twist correction plays an essential role in the evolution of parton distributions either of proton or nucleus. We find that the nuclear antishadowing contributes a part of enhancement of the ratio of the structure functions around $x\sim 0.1$, while the other part origins from the deformation of the nuclear valence quark distributions. We point out that the nuclear shadowing and antishadowing effects in the gluon distribution are not stronger than that in the quark distributions.

# Applications of a nonlinear evolution equation II: the EMC effect

## Key claim
The EMC effect can be explained using the DGLAP equation with the Zhu-Ruan-Shen (ZRS) corrections (a nonlinear parton recombination term) with only two free parameters added to those already used for the proton, where nuclear shadowing emerges dynamically from QCD evolution rather than from a parameterized input, and the antishadowing enhancement around x ~ 0.1 arises partly from the nonlinear evolution and partly from the deformation of valence quark distributions due to nucleon swelling.

## Method
The authors generalized the DGLAP equation with the ZRS nonlinear corrections (parton recombination terms) to nuclear targets, introducing an effective nucleon number A_eff = 1 + beta(A^{1/3} - 1) with beta = 0.21 to account for multi-nucleon correlations. They applied Fermi motion smearing to the input parton distributions using a Fermi gas model with nuclear binding energies and Fermi momenta from nuclear physics, and modified the input valence quark distributions at the low scale mu^2 by a nucleon swelling mechanism where the dispersion of the distributions scales inversely with the confinement radius R_A = R_p + delta R_A, with the swelling coefficient delta_A determined from local nuclear density. The evolution was run from a low scale where the nucleon consists only of valence quarks, and the resulting parton distributions were used to compute the structure function ratios F_2^A / F_2^{2H} at Q^2 = 5 GeV^2.

## Key numbers
- The effective nucleon number coefficient beta = 0.21 (dimensionless) for the factor A_eff = 1 + beta(A^{1/3} - 1) in the nonlinear terms.
- The free swelling parameter delta_0 = 0.217, determined from the EMC ratio of calcium to deuterium.
- The proton valence quark input distributions at mu^2: x f_{v_u}^p(x, mu^2) = 24.30 x^{1.98} (1-x)^{2.06} and x f_{v_d}^p(x, mu^2) = 9.10 x^{1.31} (1-x)^{3.80}.
- Binding energies per nucleon range from 1.10 MeV (^2H) to 8.79 MeV (^56Fe); Fermi momenta range from 0.6 fm^{-1} (^2H) to 1.36 fm^{-1} (^208Pb); swelling coefficients delta_A range from 0.01 (^2H) to 0.113 (^208Pb) (Table 1).
- The EMC effect ratios F_2^A / F_2^{2H} are presented at Q^2 = 5 GeV^2 for multiple nuclei (^4He, ^9Be, ^12C, ^27Al, ^40Ca, ^56Fe, ^64Cu, ^84Kr, ^108Ag, ^118Sn, ^131Xe, ^197Au, ^208Pb) and compared with experimental data from EMC, E49, E87, E139, E140, NMC, BCDMS, HERMES, and Jefferson Lab.
- The Q^2-dependence of the EMC effect for F_2^{Ca} / F_2^D is shown to be weak at x > 0.1 (Fig. 8).
- The ratio x f_g^{Pb} / x f_g^p at Q^2 = 1.69 GeV^2 is predicted and compared with global models EPS09, EKS98, HKN07, and nDS (Fig. 7).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous application of the DGLAP equation with ZRS corrections to the proton (Chen et al., 2013) to nuclear targets, providing a unified dynamical framework for both proton and nuclear parton distributions. It contrasts with earlier approaches such as the binding model (which was ruled out by Drell-Yan measurements as noted by the authors) and the pion model, and with global DGLAP analyses like EPS09 (Eskola et al.), EKS98 (Eskola et al.), HKN07 (Hirai et al.), and nDS (de Florian et al.) that extract nuclear gluon distributions from scaling violations with many ad hoc free parameters. The work enables dynamically predicted nuclear gluon distributions with reduced uncertainty, which are important for interpreting heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and for studies of quark-gluon plasma and color glass condensation.
