# Comments on the holographic shear viscosity to entropy density ratio

> We revisit the membrane paradigm calculations of the holographic shear viscosity tensor of strongly coupled isotropic plasmas with Einstein gravity dual by emphasizing the fact which was overlooked in the previous literatures that the shear viscosity is a fourth-rank tensor. Using the membrane paradigm we show that depending on whether the holographic shear viscosity tensor to entropy density ratio is η^j_i^j_i/s or η^{jiji}/s or η_{jiji}/s, we can derive three distinct formulae for the holographic shear viscosity tensor to entropy density ratios given explicitly in terms of the background metric g_{ij}. We find that the widely studied η^j_i^j_i/s holographic shear viscosity tensor to entropy density ratio takes the universal value 1/(4π) for isotropic background metric g_{ij} but η^{jiji}/s and η_{jiji}/s holographic shear viscosity tensor to entropy density ratios take non-universal values which depend on the details of the isotropic background metric g_{ij}.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the holographic shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is not uniquely defined because shear viscosity is a fourth-rank tensor; depending on which index structure (η^{j_i}^{j_i}/s, η^{jiji}/s, or η_{jiji}/s) is used, one obtains different values, with only the mixed-index ratio η^{j_i}^{j_i}/s taking the universal value 1/(4π) for isotropic Einstein gravity duals, while the other two are non-universal and depend on the background metric.

## Method

The author revisits the membrane paradigm for holographic shear viscosity in strongly coupled isotropic plasmas with Einstein gravity duals, treating the metric perturbations h_{xy}, h^{xy}, and h^{x}_y as distinct fields with different effective actions and equations of motion. Using the membrane paradigm, the conjugate momenta for each perturbation are evaluated at the horizon, and the shear viscosities are extracted by comparing these momenta to the Kubo formulae for the corresponding two-point functions of the energy-momentum tensor. The resulting shear viscosity to entropy density ratios are expressed explicitly in terms of the background metric components g_{ij} evaluated at the horizon.

## Key numbers

- For isotropic background metrics, η^{x}_y^{x}_y/s = 1/(4π) universally, independent of the metric details.
- For N=4 super-Yang-Mills plasma on flat space: η^{xyxy}/s = (1/(4π)) × 1/(πTR)^4, which is ≥ 1/(4π); η_{xyxy}/s = (1/(4π)) × (πTR)^4, which is ≤ 1/(4π); η^{x}_y^{x}_y/s = 1/(4π).
- For ABJM plasma on flat space: η^{xyxy}/s = (1/(4π)) × 1/((2/3)πTR)^4; η_{xyxy}/s = (1/(4π)) × ((2/3)πTR)^4; η^{x}_y^{x}_y/s = 1/(4π).
- For (2,0) superconformal plasma on flat space: η^{xyxy}/s = (1/(4π)) × 1/((4/3)πTR)^4; η_{xyxy}/s = (1/(4π)) × ((4/3)πTR)^4; η^{x}_y^{x}_y/s = 1/(4π).

## Context

This work extends the membrane paradigm calculations of Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2003, 2004) by emphasizing that shear viscosity is a fourth-rank tensor, leading to distinct shear viscosity to entropy density ratios depending on the index structure. The paper contradicts the implicit assumption in prior literature that all definitions yield the same universal value 1/(4π) for isotropic Einstein gravity duals, and it suggests that the non-universal ratios η^{xyxy}/s and η_{xyxy}/s may have physical meaning for gauge theories on compact spaces (like spheres with radius R), while for flat spaces they should be discarded as unphysical due to their dependence on the AdS radius R.
