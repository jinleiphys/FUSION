# Arbitrary mass Majorana neutrinos in neutrinoless double beta decay

> We revisit the mechanism of neutrinoless double beta (NLDBD) decay mediated by the exchange with the heavy Majorana neutrino N of arbitrary mass mN, slightly mixed with the electron neutrino. By assuming the dominance of this mechanism, we update the well-known NLDBD-decay exclusion plot in the mass-mixing angle plane taking into account recent progress in the calculation of nuclear matrix elements within quasiparticle random phase approximation and improved experimental bounds on the NLDBD-decay half-life of Ge-76 and Xe-136. We also consider the known formula approximating the mN dependence of the NLDBD-decay nuclear matrix element in a simple explicit form. We analyze its accuracy and specify the corresponding parameters, allowing one to easily calculate the NLDBD-decay half-life for arbitrary mN for all the experimentally interesting isotopes without resorting to real nuclear structure calculations.

# Arbitrary mass Majorana neutrinos in neutrinoless double beta decay

## Key claim

The authors update the exclusion limits in the mass-mixing plane for heavy Majorana neutrinos contributing to neutrinoless double beta decay, assuming this mechanism dominates and that no significant cancellation occurs between different terms in the amplitude. The updated limits are derived using improved nuclear matrix elements from quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations and the latest experimental half-life bounds for Ge-76 and Xe-136, but the results depend on the choice of nucleon-nucleon potential and the value of the axial-vector coupling constant in nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors calculate the nuclear matrix element for neutrinoless double beta decay mediated by a heavy Majorana neutrino of arbitrary mass within the QRPA framework with partial restoration of isospin symmetry, using two different nucleon-nucleon potentials (CD-Bonn and Argonne) and two values of the axial-vector coupling constant (1.269 and 1.0). They then derive exclusion limits in the mixing-mass plane from the experimental lower bounds on the half-life of Ge-76 and Xe-136, and also analyze an interpolating formula that approximates the mass dependence of the matrix element for arbitrary neutrino mass using only the asymptotic light and heavy mass limits.

## Key numbers

* Experimental lower bound on the half-life of Ge-76: T_{1/2}^{0nu} >= 3.0 x 10^{25} years (combined GERDA + Heidelberg-Moscow)
* Experimental lower bound on the half-life of Xe-136: T_{1/2}^{0nu} >= 3.4 x 10^{25} years (combined EXO + Kamland-ZEN)
* For Ge-76, the light neutrino matrix element M'^{0nu}_nu ranges from 3.886 (Argonne, g_A=1.00) to 5.571 (CD-Bonn, g_A=1.269)
* For Ge-76, the heavy neutrino matrix element M'^{0nu}_N ranges from 204.0 (Argonne, g_A=1.00) to 433.0 (CD-Bonn, g_A=1.269)
* For Xe-136, the light neutrino matrix element M'^{0nu}_nu ranges from 1.643 (Argonne, g_A=1.00) to 2.460 (CD-Bonn, g_A=1.269)
* For Xe-136, the heavy neutrino matrix element M'^{0nu}_N ranges from 108.0 (Argonne, g_A=1.00) to 228.0 (CD-Bonn, g_A=1.269)
* The parameter sqrt(<p^2>) for Ge-76 ranges from 159 MeV to 193 MeV across the four combinations of potential and g_A
* The parameter sqrt(<p^2>) for Xe-136 ranges from 178 MeV to 211 MeV across the four combinations
* The interpolating formula reproduces the exact QRPA result to within 20% to 25% accuracy in the transition region

## Context

This work extends the previous analysis of Benes (2005) by incorporating updated nuclear matrix elements from QRPA calculations that include constraints from two-neutrino double beta decay half-lives, short-range correlations, and isospin symmetry restoration. The results are compared with other laboratory constraints on heavy neutrino mixing from searches summarized by the Particle Data Group and Atre (2009), showing that the neutrinoless double beta decay limits are more stringent for most masses except a narrow interval around 300-400 MeV. The interpolating formula enables easy calculation of half-lives for arbitrary neutrino masses without full nuclear structure calculations, which is particularly useful for analyzing scenarios like the nuMSM model of Asaka (2005) that involve multiple neutrino mass eigenstates.
