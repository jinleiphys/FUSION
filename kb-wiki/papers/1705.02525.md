# Composition of nuclear matter with light clusters and Bose-Einstein condensation of $α$ particles

> The Bose-Einstein condensation of $α$ partciles in the multicomponent environment of dilute, warm nuclear matter is studied. We consider the cases of matter composed of light clusters with mass numbers $A\leq 4$ and matter that in addition these clusters contains $\isotope[56]{Fe}$ nuclei. We apply the quasiparticle gas model which treats clusters as bound states with infinite life-time and binding energies independent of temperature and density. We show that the $α$ particles can form a condensate at low temperature $T\le 2$ MeV in such matter in the first case. When the $\isotope[56]{Fe}$ nucleus is added to the composition the cluster abundances are strongly modified at low temperatures, with an important implication that the $α$ condensation at these temperatures is suppressed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that alpha particles can form a Bose-Einstein condensate in dilute warm nuclear matter composed only of light clusters (A ≤ 4) at temperatures T ≤ 2 MeV, but this condensation is suppressed when heavy nuclei such as iron-56 are added to the composition, because the cluster abundances are strongly modified at low temperatures.

## Method

The authors applied the quasiparticle gas model, which treats clusters as bound states with infinite lifetime and binding energies independent of temperature and density. They solved coupled nonlinear equations for the densities of neutrons, protons, deuterons, tritons, helions, and alpha particles (and optionally iron-56) at fixed temperature, total nucleon density, and proton fraction, using chemical equilibrium conditions and including mean-field effects on nucleon masses via a Skyrme functional.

## Key numbers

- The density regime studied is n ≤ 0.3 n_sat, where n_sat ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³ is the saturation density
- The temperature regime studied is 2 ≤ T ≤ 10 MeV
- Alpha particles can form a condensate at low temperature T ≤ 2 MeV in matter composed only of light clusters
- Binding energies used: B_d = 2.225 MeV (deuteron), B_t = 8.482 MeV (triton), B_h = 7.718 MeV (helion), B_α = 28.3 MeV (alpha particle)
- The critical temperature for BEC scales as T_c ≈ n_α^(2/3) ≈ n^(2/3)
- At T = 2 MeV with iron-56 present, alpha particles constitute about 10% of matter throughout most of the density range, while iron-56 dominates
- At T = 10 MeV, there is a negligible amount of iron-56 nuclei

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle gas model of Hempel et al. (2009) to study Bose-Einstein condensation of alpha particles in clustered nuclear matter, updating and improving on those results. The findings are relevant for nuclear structure calculations, heavy ion collisions, and astrophysics of supernovas and neutron stars, where the composition of warm low-density nuclear matter affects neutrino emission and absorption. The paper shows that while alpha condensation can occur in matter with only light clusters, it is suppressed when heavy nuclei like iron-56 are present, though dynamical (nonequilibrium) situations such as heavy-ion collisions may still allow condensation.
