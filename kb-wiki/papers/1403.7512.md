# Remarks on higher-order hadronic corrections to the muon g-2

> Recently, it was shown that insertions of hadronic vacuum polarization at O(alpha^4) generate non-negligible effects in the calculation of the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon. This result raises the question if other hadronic diagrams at this order might become relevant for the next round of g-2 measurements as well. In this note we show that a potentially enhanced such contribution, hadronic light-by-light scattering in combination with electron vacuum polarization, is already sufficiently suppressed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that hadronic light-by-light (HLbL) scattering combined with electron vacuum polarization, a potentially enhanced higher-order hadronic correction at O(alpha^4) to the muon g-2, is sufficiently suppressed and does not significantly affect the next round of g-2 measurements.

## Method

The authors approximated the HLbL tensor by the pion-pole contribution using a simple vector-meson-dominance (VMD) model for the pion transition form factor, with pion decay constant F_pi = 92.2 MeV and rho meson mass M_rho = 775.26 MeV. They then modified the photon propagators by the lepton vacuum polarization factor and computed the resulting correction to the muon anomalous magnetic moment. They also compared with renormalization-group arguments and checked diagrams with muon/tau loops and hadronic vacuum polarization.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order HLbL contribution is a_mu^HLbL, LO = (116 +/- 39) x 10^-11
- The pion-pole contribution alone gives a_mu^pi0-pole = 57.2 x 10^-11
- The NLO correction from the electron loop to the pion-pole contribution is a_mu^pi0-pole, NLO = 1.5 x 10^-11, a 2.6% correction
- The renormalization-group estimate gives a suppression factor of 3 x (alpha/pi) x (2/3) x log(m_mu/m_e) ≈ 2.5%
- The final estimate for the full NLO HLbL contribution is a_mu^HLbL, NLO = (3 +/- 2) x 10^-11
- This lies a factor 5 below the accuracy goal of the next round of g-2 experiments

## Context

This work extends the calculation of hadronic vacuum polarization at O(alpha^4) by Kurz (2014), which found a non-negligible NNLO HVP contribution of (12.4 +/- 0.1) x 10^-11, significantly larger than the naive estimate from the NLO/LO suppression ratio of about 1/70 from Hagiwara (2011). The paper shows that the potentially enhanced HLbL contribution with electron vacuum polarization is already sufficiently suppressed, and the remaining radiative corrections to HLbL are unlikely to be significant. This enables the community to focus on other O(alpha^4) contributions for the upcoming g-2 experiments at Fermilab (Venanzoni, 2012) and J-PARC (Saito, 2012).
