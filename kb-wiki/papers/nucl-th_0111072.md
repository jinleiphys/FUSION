# On Cooper Pairing in Finite Fermi Systems

> In order to analyse the role of the quasiparticle-phonon interaction in the origin of nuclear gap, we applied an approach which is similar to the Eliashberg theory for usual superconductors. We obtained that the averaged contribution of the quasiparticle-phonon mechanism to the observed value of the pairing gap for $^{120}$Sn is 26% and the BCS-type mechanism gives 74% . Thus, pairing is of a mixed nature at least in semi-magic nuclei -- it is due to the quasiparticle-phonon and BCS mechanisms, the first one being mainly a surface mechanism and the second one mainly a volume mechanism. The calculations of the strength distribution for the odd-mass nuclei $^{119}Sn$ and $^{121}Sn$ have shown that the quasiparticle-phonon mechanism mainly improves the description of the observed spectroscopic factors in these nuclei. For the case of nuclei with pairing in both proton and neutron systems it is necessary to go beyond the Eliashberg-Migdal approximations and include the vertex correction graphs in addition to the rainbow ones. The estimations for spectroscopic factors performed within a three-level model have shown that the contribution of the vertex correction graphs was rather noticeable.

# On Cooper Pairing in Finite Fermi Systems

## Key claim

The quasiparticle-phonon interaction contributes 26% of the observed pairing gap in $^{120}$Sn, while the BCS-type mechanism contributes 74%, indicating that pairing in semi-magic nuclei is of mixed nature. This result is obtained under the approximation $g^{2}<1$ (weak coupling) and relies on a simple averaging procedure and a simplified determination of the new particle-particle interaction.

## Method

The authors applied an approach analogous to Eliashberg theory for superconductors, starting from general equations for one-particle Green functions in a Fermi system with pairing. They separated the mass operators into energy-independent terms (mean field and BCS-type pairing) and energy-dependent terms containing the quasiparticle-phonon interaction (QPI). For $^{120}$Sn, they solved the system of equations that "refine" the phenomenological single-particle energies and gaps by removing the QPI contribution, using 21 collective phonons with energies below the neutron binding energy. For the strong coupling case ($g^{2}>1$), they employed a schematic three-level model with vertex corrections beyond the Eliashberg-Migdal approximation.

## Key numbers

- The averaged contribution of the quasiparticle-phonon mechanism to the observed pairing gap for $^{120}$Sn is 26% (composed of +31% from phonon exchange and -5% from self-energy diagrams)
- The BCS-type mechanism contributes 74% of the average phenomenological gap of 1.42 MeV for $^{120}$Sn
- In the three-level model with strong coupling ($\alpha=2$), inclusion of vertex corrections decreased the spectroscopic factor for the dominant level from 0.49 to 0.40
- The vertex correction factor $w$ for the three-level model with $j=11/2$ is 0.76, based on the 6j-symbol result from Belyaev and Zelevinsky

## Context

This work extends earlier phenomenological discussions of nuclear pairing as a volume versus surface effect within the theory of finite Fermi systems, as discussed by the authors' previous work (Avdeenkov and Kamerdzhiev, 1999) and by Baran (1998) who solved a BCS equation with phonon-exchange pp interaction. The paper also builds on the approach of Belyaev and Zelevinsky for vertex corrections. The results enable improved calculations of spectroscopic factors in odd-mass nuclei and suggest that experiments with gamma-ray arrays like EUROBALL could further test the mixed nature of pairing. For nuclei with pairing in both proton and neutron systems, the work indicates that vertex correction graphs must be included beyond the Eliashberg-Migdal approximation.
