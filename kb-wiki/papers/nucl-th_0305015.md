# Dilepton production in heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies

> We present a unified description of the vector meson and dilepton production in elementary and in heavy ion reactions. The production of vector mesons ($ρ,ω$) is described via the excitation of nuclear resonances ($R$). The theoretical framework is an extended vector meson dominance model (eVMD). The treatment of the resonance decays $R\longmapsto NV$ with arbitrary spin is covariant and kinematically complete. The eVMD includes thereby excited vector meson states in the transition form factors. This ensures correct asymptotics and provides a unified description of photonic and mesonic decays. The resonance model is successfully applied to the $ω$ production in $p+p$ reactions. The same model is applied to the dilepton production in elementary reactions ($p+p, p+d$). Corresponding data are well reproduced. However, when the model is applied to heavy ion reactions in the BEVALAC/SIS energy range the experimental dilepton spectra measured by the DLS Collaboration are significantly underestimated at small invariant masses. As a possible solution of this problem the destruction of quantum interference in a dense medium is discussed. A decoherent emission through vector mesons decays enhances the corresponding dilepton yield in heavy ion reactions. In the vicinity of the $ρ/ω$-peak the reproduction of the data requires further a substantial collisional broadening of the $ρ$ and in particular of the $ω$ meson.

## Key claim

The paper presents a unified description of vector meson and dilepton production in both elementary and heavy ion reactions using an extended vector meson dominance model (eVMD), but the model significantly underestimates the experimental dilepton spectra measured by the DLS Collaboration at small invariant masses when applied to heavy ion reactions in the BEVALAC/SIS energy range, suggesting that destruction of quantum interference in a dense medium and collisional broadening of the ρ and ω mesons may resolve this discrepancy.

## Method

The production of vector mesons (ρ, ω) is described via the excitation of nuclear resonances (R) with arbitrary spin, using an extended vector meson dominance model that includes excited vector meson states in the transition form factors to ensure correct asymptotics and provide a unified description of photonic and mesonic decays. The reaction dynamics of heavy ion collisions is described within the QMD transport model, which includes the complete set of baryonic resonances (Δ and N*) with masses below 2 GeV. The resonance model is applied to ω production in p+p reactions and dilepton production in elementary reactions (p+p, p+d), and then extended to heavy ion reactions where decoherent emission through vector meson decays and collisional broadening are introduced as possible solutions to the DLS puzzle.

## Key numbers

- The resonance model includes baryonic resonances with masses below 2 GeV, with spins ranging from 1/2 up to 7/2, requiring maximally 6 ρ states with masses: 0.769, 1.250, 1.450, 1.720, 2.150, and 2.350 GeV.
- The exclusive pp→ppω cross section obtained with strong N*(1535)Nω coupling shows that at small excess energies the full cross section is about one order of magnitude larger than the measurable pole part.
- The parameterization of the inclusive cross section σ^{pp→ωX} = 2.5(s/s0 - 1)^{1.47}(s/s0)^{-1.11} from Sibirtsev (1997) is shown for comparison with the resonance model results.
- The pn→pnρ^0 channel is about two times larger and the pn→pnρ^+ channel about four times larger than the measured pp→ppρ^0 channel.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework developed in Krivoruchenko (2000, 2002) for meson decay channels and resonance decays, and addresses the DLS puzzle first identified by the DLS Collaboration at the BEVALAC, where low mass dilepton spectra in heavy ion reactions are underestimated by present transport calculations. The paper builds on prior work by Rapp (1997), Brown and Rho (1991), and others on in-medium vector meson properties, and enables future extraction of information on in-medium ρ and ω meson widths directly from BEVALAC data, as well as investigation of quantum interference effects such as ρ-ω mixing in dense matter.
