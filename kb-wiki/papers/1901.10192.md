# Weak production of strange and charm ground-state baryons in nuclei

> We present results for the quasi-elastic weak production of $Λ$ and $Σ$ hyperons induced by $\barν$ scattering off nuclei, in the kinematical region of interest for accelerator neutrino experiments. We employ realistic hole spectral functions and we describe the propagation of the hyperons in the nuclear medium by means of a Monte Carlo cascade. The latter strongly modifies the kinematics and the relative production rates of the hyperons, leading to a non-vanishing $Σ^+$ cross section, to a sizable enhancement of the $Λ$ production and to a drastic reduction of the $Σ^0$ and $Σ^-$ distributions. We also compute the quasi-elastic weak $Λ_c$ production cross section, paying special attention to estimate the uncertainties induced by the model dependence of the vacuum $n\to Λ_c$ weak matrix element. In this regard, the recent BESIII measurements of the branching ratios of $Λ_c\rightarrow Λl^+ν_l$ ($l=e,μ$) are used to benchmark the available theoretical predictions.

# Weak production of strange and charm ground-state baryons in nuclei

## Key claim
The authors show that final-state interactions (FSI) from a Monte Carlo cascade strongly modify hyperon kinematics and relative production rates in antineutrino-nucleus scattering, leading to a non-vanishing Σ⁺ cross section and sizable enhancement of Λ production, while drastically reducing Σ⁰ and Σ⁻ distributions, effects that depend on the nuclear species and are not captured by simpler Local Fermi Gas models. For Λc production, the theoretical uncertainty from different form-factor models is quantified using BESIII branching ratio measurements as a benchmark, with the spread among predictions reaching approximately 30% or more.

## Method
The authors compute quasi-elastic weak production of Λ and Σ hyperons from antineutrino scattering off ¹²C, ¹⁶O, and ⁴⁰Ca nuclei using realistic hole spectral functions (semi-phenomenological LDA-SF and Correlated Basis Function theory) and a Monte Carlo cascade algorithm to simulate hyperon propagation through the nuclear medium, treating rescattering classically with experimental hyperon-nucleon cross sections. For Λc production from neutrino scattering, they evaluate the νμ + n → μ⁻ Λc cross section using four different form-factor models (LQCD, RCQM, MBM, NRQM) and two approaches: SU(3) rotation from Λc→Λ form factors or direct Λc→N form factors, with the LQCD results providing statistical uncertainties that are propagated to the nuclear cross sections.

## Context
This work extends the pioneering study of Singh and Vicente-Vacas (2006), which first included nuclear effects in ΔS=1 processes using a Local Fermi Gas model, by employing realistic hole spectral functions and a Monte Carlo cascade for hyperon propagation. The results are relevant for interpreting data from SciBooNE, MicroBooNE, MINERvA, and ArgoNeuT experiments with antineutrino beams. For Λc production, the authors use recent BESIII measurements (2015, 2016) of Λc→Λ semileptonic branching ratios to benchmark form-factor models from constituent quark models (Hussain 2017, Gutsche 2014/2015, Perez-Marcial 1989) and the first lattice QCD calculations (Meinel 2016/2017), enabling improved predictions for neutrino-nucleus cross sections at energies near threshold.
