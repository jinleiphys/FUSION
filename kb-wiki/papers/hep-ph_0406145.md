# Influence of tensor interactions on masses and decay widths of dibaryons

> The influence of gluon and Goldstone boson induced tensor interactions on the dibaryon masses and D-wave decay widths has been studied in the quark delocalization, color screening model. The effective S-D wave transition interactions induced by gluon and Goldstone boson exchanges decrease rapidly with increasing strangeness of the channel. The tensor contribution of K and $η$ mesons is negligible in this model. There is no six-quark state in the light flavor world studied so far that can become bound by means of these tensor interactions besides the deuteron. The partial D-wave decay widths of the $IJ^p={1/2}2^+$ N$Ω$ state to spin 0 and 1 $ΛΞ$ final states are 12.0 keV and 21.9 keV respectively. This is a very narrow dibaryon resonance that might be detectable in relativistic heavy ion reactions by existing RHIC detectors through the reconstruction of the vertex mass of the decay product $ΛΞ$ and by the COMPAS detector at CERN or at JHF in Japan and the FAIR project in Germany in the future.

# Influence of tensor interactions on masses and decay widths of dibaryons

## Key claim
Within the extended quark delocalization, color screening model (QDCSM), the tensor interactions from gluon and pion exchanges are not strong enough to bind any six-quark state in the light flavor (u,d,s) world into a strong-interaction-stable bound state besides the deuteron. The NΩ state with quantum numbers IJp=1/2 2+ remains a narrow dibaryon resonance with D-wave decay widths of tens of keV rather than becoming bound.

## Method
The authors used the extended quark delocalization, color screening model (QDCSM) with a Hamiltonian including confinement, one-gluon exchange, and octet Goldstone boson (π, K, η) exchange interactions, including tensor terms. They solved the resonating-group method (RGM) equation by expanding the relative motion wave function in Gaussian basis functions with 15 generating coordinates over 0-9 fm, and calculated D-wave partial decay widths using Fermi's golden rule in the nonrelativistic approximation with final state interactions included.

## Key numbers
- The partial D-wave decay width of the NΩ (IJp=1/2 2+) state to ΛΞ with spin 0 is 12.0 keV.
- The partial D-wave decay width of the NΩ (IJp=1/2 2+) state to ΛΞ with spin 1 is 21.9 keV.
- The D-wave decay width of the d* (IJp=03+) to NN is 6.57 MeV.
- The deuteron mass calculated with tensor interactions is 1876.770 MeV (with a boundary at 9 fm and no ΔΔ channel coupling).
- The NΩ mass in single-channel approximation is about 2566 MeV; including S-wave channel coupling reduces it to 2549 MeV, and adding the ΛΞ D-wave channel changes the mass by less than 1 MeV.
- Model parameters: light quark mass mq=313 MeV, strange quark mass ms=634 MeV, size parameter b=0.6022 fm, confinement strength ac=25.03 MeV·fm-2, strong coupling αs=1.5547, quark-pion coupling g8^2/4π=0.5926, short-range cutoff r0=0.8 fm, color screening parameter μ=0.90 fm-2.

## Context
This work extends previous QDCSM calculations of dibaryon candidates (Pang, 2003; Zhang, 2003) by systematically including tensor interactions from gluon and Goldstone boson exchanges. It contradicts the possibility that tensor forces could bind near-threshold states such as the SIJp=-401+ ΞΞ, confirming that only the deuteron becomes bound through π-exchange tensor interactions, consistent with the known role of pion exchange in nuclear physics (Ping, 2003). The predicted narrow NΩ resonance with tens-of-keV width enables experimental searches via invariant mass reconstruction of ΛΞ decay products at RHIC, COMPASS at CERN, JHF in Japan, and FAIR in Germany.
