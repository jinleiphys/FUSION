# Modeling inclusive electron-nucleus scattering with Bayesian artificial neural networks

> We introduce a Bayesian protocol based on artificial neural networks that is suitable for modeling inclusive electron-nucleus scattering on a variety of nuclear targets with quantified uncertainties. Unlike previous applications in the field, which directly parameterize the cross sections, our approach employs artificial neural networks to represent the longitudinal and transverse response functions. In contrast to cross sections, which depend on the incoming energy, scattering angle, and energy transfer, the response functions are determined solely by the energy and momentum transfer to the system, allowing the angular component to be treated analytically. We assess the accuracy and predictive power of our framework against the extensive data in the quasielastic inclusive electron-scattering database. Additionally, we present novel extractions of the longitudinal and transverse response functions and compare them with previous experimental analysis and nuclear ab-initio calculations.

# Modeling inclusive electron-nucleus scattering with Bayesian artificial neural networks

## Key claim
The authors introduce a Bayesian artificial neural network (ANN) protocol that represents the longitudinal and transverse response functions separately (rather than directly parameterizing cross sections), enabling extraction of these response functions for nuclei where traditional Rosenbluth separation has not been possible, with quantified uncertainties that automatically increase in extrapolation regimes.

## Method
The ANN takes as input the energy transfer, momentum transfer, nucleon number A, and proton number Z, and outputs the longitudinal (R_L) and transverse (R_T) response functions through separate multilayer perceptron networks with 32 neurons per hidden layer and hyperbolic tangent activation. The network is trained on inclusive electron-nucleus scattering cross sections from the quasielastic database for five symmetric nuclei (^4He, ^6Li, ^12C, ^16O, ^40Ca) using Bayesian inference with Hamiltonian Monte Carlo sampling, where the posterior distribution of the 6787 network parameters is sampled via the No-U-Turn Sampler in NumPyro. Coulomb distortion effects are accounted for using an effective momentum approximation.

## Key numbers
- The ANN architecture contains 6787 total parameters (weights and biases).
- Training data is split 80% training and 20% test, with experimental errors augmented by an additional term of 0.05 times the experimental cross section value.
- The network covers nuclei with A ranging from 4 to 40 (^4He, ^6Li, ^12C, ^16O, ^40Ca).
- Response functions are extracted at momentum transfers including q=400 MeV/c for ^4He and ^16O, q=380 MeV/c for ^12C and ^40Ca, and q=300, 500, 600 MeV/c for ^4He.

## Context
This work extends prior ANN applications that directly parameterized inclusive (e,e') cross sections (AlHammal 2023, Kowal 2023) by instead learning the underlying response functions, which depend only on energy and momentum transfer rather than also on scattering angle. The extracted responses are compared with ab initio Green's Function Monte Carlo calculations (Lovato 2015, 2016) using the Argonne v18 + Illinois 7 Hamiltonian and with Lorentz Integral Transform Coupled Cluster calculations (Sobczyk 2021, 2023) using the NNLO_sat chiral Hamiltonian. The framework enables first-time extraction of longitudinal and transverse response functions for ^16O, where no Rosenbluth separation has been performed, and suggests a potential 10-15% underestimation of transverse strength in previous ^40Ca Rosenbluth extractions, which could help resolve tension between Saclay (Meziani 1984) and Bates (Williamson 1997) data.
