# Equilibrium and non-equilibrium properties of the QGP in a magnetic field: a holographic approach

> This manuscript reviews recent theoretical progress on the understanding of the quark gluon plasma in a magnetic field that I presented on the conference Hot Quarks 2016, held at South Padre Island, Texas, USA, 12-17 September 2016. It is shown that, using a holographic bottom-up Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton model, one can have a good quantitative agreement with Lattice data for QCD equation of state and Polyakov loop with nonzero magnetic field. I also present results for the anisotropic shear viscosity ratio $η_{\parallel}/η_{\perp}$, with the conclusion that $η_{\parallel}<η_{\perp}$ for $B>0$.

## Key claim
Using a holographic bottom-up Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton (EMD) model, one can achieve good quantitative agreement with lattice QCD data for the equation of state and Polyakov loop at nonzero magnetic field, provided the model's free parameters are first fixed to lattice data at zero magnetic field. The model predicts that the shear viscosity parallel to the magnetic field is smaller than the shear viscosity perpendicular to it for B > 0.

## Method
The authors construct a 5-dimensional gravitational EMD action where the dilaton field breaks conformal symmetry and the Maxwell field introduces a magnetic field. The dilaton potential, 5-dimensional Newton's constant, and gauge-dilaton coupling are fixed by matching lattice QCD data for the equation of state and magnetic susceptibility with (2+1) flavors at zero magnetic field from Borsanyi (2013) and Bonati (2013). With these inputs fixed, the model then makes predictions for thermodynamics, the Polyakov loop, heavy quark entropy, and the anisotropic shear viscosity ratio at nonzero magnetic field.

## Key numbers
- Magnetic fields created at top LHC collision energies may reach B ~ 10^19 G, or eB ~ 15 m_pi^2 ~ 0.3 GeV^2 in natural units.
- The shear viscosity over entropy density ratio for the QGP is approximately eta/s ≈ 0.095 according to hydrodynamic simulations by Ryu (2015).
- The holographic model yields eta/s = 1/(4π) for conformal plasmas, following Kovtun (2004) and Buchel (2003).
- The ratio eta_parallel / eta_perp is computed as a function of magnetic field and temperature, with the result that eta_parallel < eta_perp for B > 0.

## Context
This work extends the holographic EMD approach of Gubser (2008) and collaborators, previously applied to QGP transport coefficients by Finazzo (2014) and to baryonic chemical potential by DeWolfe (2010, 2011) and Rougemont (2015), to include magnetic field effects. The model enables quantitative predictions for both equilibrium properties (thermodynamics, Polyakov loop) and non-equilibrium properties (anisotropic shear viscosity) of the QGP near the crossover region, with the authors planning to next investigate Bjorken expansion dynamics.
