# Study of participant-spectator matter and thermalization in isospin asymmetric systems

> We study the behavior of participant and spectator matter and thermalization in neutron rich systems at the energy of vanishing flow. Our study indicates that participant-spectator matter follows a similar behavior for neutron-rich systems and for systems lie on the stability line.

# Study of participant-spectator matter and thermalization in isospin asymmetric systems

## Key claim
The behavior of participant and spectator matter in neutron-rich systems at the energy of vanishing flow follows a similar pattern to that of systems on the stability line, with a nearly mass-independent power-law scaling for all N/Z ratios studied.

## Method
The authors used the Isospin-dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate collisions of Ca+Ca, Ni+Ni, Zr+Zr, Sn+Sn, and Xe+Xe series with N/Z ratios of 1.0, 1.6, and 2.0 at impact parameters of b/b_max = 0.2-0.4. Reactions were run at the balance energy (energy of vanishing flow) for each system, and participant-spectator matter was defined using a nucleonic concept where participants are nucleons that suffer at least one collision. The anisotropy ratio was used as an indicator of global equilibrium.

## Key numbers
- Power-law exponent for spectator matter: -0.12 ± 0.04 (N/Z = 1.0), -0.14 ± 0.06 (N/Z = 1.6), -0.03 ± 0.06 (N/Z = 2.0)
- Power-law exponent for participant matter: 0.02 ± 0.01 (N/Z = 1.0), 0.02 ± 0.01 (N/Z = 1.6), 0.01 ± 0.01 (N/Z = 2.0)
- Balance energies for specific systems: 105 MeV for 40Ca+40Ca, 98 MeV for 58Ni+58Ni, 86 MeV for 81Zr+81Zr, 82 MeV for 100Sn+100Sn, 76 MeV for 110Xe+110Xe
- Balance energies for neutron-rich variants: 73 MeV for 60Ca+60Ca, 72 MeV for 84Ni+84Ni, 67 MeV for 120Zr+120Zr, 64 MeV for 150Sn+150Sn, 61 MeV for 162Xe+162Xe

## Context
This work extends prior studies of isospin effects in balance energy by Pak et al. and Liewen et al., who investigated disappearance of flow in neutron-rich systems, and by Gautam and collaborators who demonstrated the dominance of Coulomb potential in isobaric pairs and sensitivity to symmetry energy in isotopic pairs. The present study is the first to examine participant-spectator matter and thermalization at balance energy for neutron-rich colliding pairs, enabling a more complete understanding of how isospin asymmetry affects heavy-ion reaction dynamics at the energy where attractive mean field and repulsive nucleon-nucleon interactions are balanced.
