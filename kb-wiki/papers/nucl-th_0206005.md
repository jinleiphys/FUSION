# Strangeness Enhancement in the Parton Model

> Strangeness enhancement in heavy-ion collisions is studied at the parton level by examining the partition of the new sea quarks generated by gluon conversion into the strange and non-strange sectors. The CTEQ parton distribution functions are used as a baseline for the quiescent sea before gluon conversion. By quark counting simple constraints are placed on the hadron yields in different channels. The experimental values of particle ratios are fitted to determine the strangeness enhancement factor. A quantitative measure of Pauli blocking is determined. Energy dependence between SPS and RHIC energies is well described. No thermal equilibrium or statistical model is assumed.

## Key claim

Strangeness enhancement in heavy-ion collisions can be described at the parton level via gluon conversion into quark pairs, without assuming thermal equilibrium or a statistical model; the enhancement factor is determined phenomenologically by fitting experimental particle ratios, and a quantitative measure of Pauli blocking in the light quark sector is extracted.

## Method

The authors use CTEQ parton distribution functions at a chosen wee-region cutoff \(x_0\) as a baseline for the quiescent sea before gluon conversion. They apply linear quark-counting relations that connect the numbers of hadrons (nucleons, pions, kaons, hyperons) to the numbers of valence, sea, and converted quarks, and fit the free parameters \(x_0\) and \(\gamma\) (the fraction of gluons converted into strange quarks) to experimental particle ratios at SPS and RHIC energies.

## Key numbers

- At SPS (Pb-Pb, \(\sqrt s = 17\) GeV): \(x_0 = 0.135\), \(\gamma = 0.08\), strangeness enhancement factor \(E_s = 2.3\), Pauli blocking factor \(B = 0.94\).
- At RHIC (\(\sqrt s = 130\) GeV): \(x'_0 = 0.0177\), \(E_s = 2.1\), \(B = 0.93\).
- At SPS: \(p/K^+ = 1.0\), \(\pi^+/K^+ = 4.76\), \(K/\bar K \approx K^+/K^- = 1.8\), \(\bar\Lambda/\Lambda = 0.13\), \(\bar N/N = 0.07\).
- At RHIC: \(K^+/\pi^+ = 0.18\) (calculated) vs. \(0.176 \pm 0.004\) (data), \(p/K^+ = 0.71\) (calculated) vs. \(0.73 \pm 0.1\) (inferred from STAR data).
- At RHIC: \(r = K/\bar K = 1.136\), \(R = \bar Y/Y = 0.77\), \(\rho = \bar N/N = 0.64\), \(\kappa = 0.628\), \(\bar\kappa = 0.713\).

## Context

This work extends the parton-model and recombination-model approach to strangeness enhancement, contrasting with statistical thermal models (e.g., Braun-Munzinger et al., 1999; Becattini et al., 2001) and earlier coalescence models (e.g., A. Bialas, 1990; Zimanyi et al., 1990) that either assume thermal equilibrium or introduce unknown factors. It uses CTEQ parton distributions as a baseline, building on prior valon-recombination studies by Hwa and Yang (2002) for hadronic collisions. The results enable a quantitative, energy-dependent description of strangeness enhancement from SPS to RHIC without invoking quark-gluon plasma formation, and suggest that the small suppression of gluon conversion into non-strange quarks (Pauli blocking) is sufficient to produce the observed enhancement.
