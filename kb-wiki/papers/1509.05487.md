# Eta-mesic nuclei: past, present, future

> Eta-mesic nucleus or the quasibound nuclear state of an eta ($η$) meson in a nucleus is caused by strong-interaction force alone. This new type of nuclear species, which extends the landscape of nuclear physics, has been extensively studied since its prediction in 1986. In this paper, we review and analyze in great detail the models of the fundamental $η$--nucleon interaction leading to the formation of an $η$--mesic nucleus, the methods used in calculating the properties of a bound $η$, and the approaches employed in the interpretation of the pertinent experimental data. In view of the successful observation of the $η$--mesic nucleus $^{25}$Mg$_η$ and other promising experimental results, future direction in searching for more $η$--mesic nuclei is suggested.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the history, models, and experimental status of eta-mesic nuclei, asserting that the existence of such states is possible based on attractive eta-nucleon interaction, but emphasizes that the real part of the eta-nucleon scattering length is strongly model-dependent and not directly constrained by data, with values ranging from 0.20 to 1.14 fm across different theoretical approaches.

## Method

The authors analyze low-energy eta-nucleon interaction models, focusing on the coupled-channels isobar model of Bhalerao and Liu (1986) which treats pi N, pi pi N, and eta N channels unitarized through coupled T-matrices. They then review three approaches to calculating eta-nucleus bound states: a full covariant optical potential using the inverse-iteration method, a factorization approximation with an energy-shift parameter Delta, and a static approximation. The calculations use the BL model parameters and nuclear form factors from Hofstadter (1956) and others.

## Key numbers

- The eta-nucleon scattering length from Bhalerao and Liu (1986) is 0.27 + i0.22 fm (one solution) and 0.28 + i0.19 fm (another solution)
- The most recent coupled-channel isobar model by Durand et al. gives a_etaN = 0.30 + i0.18 fm
- The chiral perturbation theory model of Kaiser et al. (improved) gives a_etaN = 0.20 + i0.26 fm
- For 12C, the full off-shell calculation gives binding energy E - iGamma/2 = -(1.19 + i3.67) MeV
- For 208Pb, the full off-shell calculation gives 1s state at -(18.46 + i10.11) MeV, 2s at -(2.37 + i5.82) MeV, 1p at -(12.28 + i9.28) MeV, and 1d at -(3.99 + i6.90) MeV
- The factorization approximation with Delta = 30 MeV gives results close to the full calculation, e.g., for 26Mg: -(7.11 + i7.46) MeV
- No bound state solutions were found for nuclei with mass number A less than 12

## Context

This review extends the original prediction of eta-mesic nuclei by Haider and Liu (1986) and the earlier eta-nucleon interaction model of Bhalerao and Liu (1986). It contrasts various theoretical approaches including chiral perturbation theory (Kaiser et al., 1995; Mai et al., 2012), K-matrix methods (Arndt et al., 2005; Green and Wycech, 1999), and meson-exchange models (Gasparyan et al., 2003; Sibirtsev et al., 2002). The paper enables future experimental searches for eta-mesic nuclei beyond the observed 25Mg_eta system, suggesting that heavier nuclei should be investigated and that the subthreshold eta-nucleon interaction at about 30 MeV below threshold is crucial for accurate predictions.
