# Impact of the neutron star crust on the tidal polarizability

> The first detection of a binary neutron star merger has opened the brand new era of multimessenger astronomy. This historic detection has been instrumental in providing constraints on the tidal polarizability of neutron stars. In turn, the tidal polarizability has been used to impose limits on stellar radii and ultimately on the equation of state (EOS). The tidal polarizability is also sensitive to the second tidal Love number k2. It is the main purpose of this work to perform a detailed study of k2 which, for a given compactness parameter, encodes the entire sensitivity of the tidal polarizability to the EOS. In particular, we examine the role that the crustal component of the EOS plays in the determination of k2. A set of realistic models of the equation of state that yield an accurate description of the properties of finite nuclei and support neutron stars of two solar masses is used. Given that the tidal polarizability scales as the fifth power of the compactness parameter, a universal relation exists among the tidal polarizability and the compactness parameter that is highly insensitive to the underlying EOS. Thus, besides an extraction of the tidal polarizabilities, a measurement of the individual stellar masses is also required to impact the mass-radius relation. However, we observe a strong sensitivity of k2 to the EOS, particularly to the contribution from the inner crust. Although by itself the tidal polarizability can not contribute to the determination of the mass-radius relation, future detections of binary neutron star mergers are poised to provide significant constraints on both the tidal polarizabilities and masses of the individual stars, and thus ultimately on the mass-radius relation. Yet, subleading corrections to the tidal polarizability are encoded in the second Love number k2 which displays a large sensitivity to the entire (crust-plus-core) EOS.

## Key claim

The second tidal Love number \(k_2\) displays a strong sensitivity to the equation of state (EOS), particularly to the contribution from the inner crust, even though the tidal polarizability itself is dominated by the compactness parameter and exhibits a universal relation that is highly insensitive to the underlying EOS.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations together with the differential equation for the induced quadrupole gravitational field to extract the second Love number \(k_2\) for a set of relativistic density functional models. The EOS was constructed from three components: an outer crust using the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland prescription with the Duflo-Zuker mass formula, an inner crust modeled via a polytropic or cubic interpolation between the outer crust and the liquid core, and a uniform liquid core described by a relativistic effective field theory with nonlinear meson interactions. The crust-core transition density was determined from a relativistic random-phase-approximation stability analysis.

## Key numbers

- The outer crust spans densities from about \(10^{4}\,\mathrm{g/cm^{3}}\) to \(4\times 10^{11}\,\mathrm{g/cm^{3}}\), with the neutron-drip density marking the transition to the inner crust at \(4\times 10^{11}\,\mathrm{g/cm^{3}}\).
- The minimum pressure and energy density used for the outer crust EOS are \(P_{\mathrm{min}} = 6.08\times 10^{-15}\,\mathrm{MeV/fm^{3}}\) and \(\mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{min}} = 5.86\times 10^{-9}\,\mathrm{MeV/fm^{3}}\), following Baym, Pethick, and Sutherland (1971).
- For a \(1.4\,M_{\odot}\) neutron star predicted by the FSUGarnet model, the uniform liquid core dominates up to nearly 12 km (about 90% of the stellar radius), while the inner crust spans the \(11.8\) to \(12.6\) km region.
- The function \(y(r)\) at the stellar surface \(\yR\) for a \(1.4\,M_{\odot}\) star predicted by FSUGarnet heals to a value of 0.19 after dropping below zero in the inner crust region.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of tidal deformability following the first binary neutron star merger detection GW170817 by Abbott et al. (2017), which provided initial constraints on the tidal polarizability and stellar radii. The authors build on prior studies of the neutron star crust, including the polytropic interpolation approach of Link (1999) and Carriere et al. (2002), and the relativistic RPA method for determining the crust-core transition. The results demonstrate that future detections of binary neutron star mergers by LIGO-Virgo can provide constraints on both tidal polarizabilities and individual stellar masses, ultimately impacting the mass-radius relation, but that subleading corrections encoded in \(k_2\) require careful treatment of the inner crust EOS.
