# The electro production of d* dibaryon

> $d^*$ dibaryon study is a critical test of hadron interaction models. The electro production cross sections of $ed\to ed^*$ have been calculated based on the meson exchange current model and the cross section around 30 degree of 1 GeV electron in the laboratory frame is about 10 nb. The implication of this result for the $d^*$ dibaryon search has been discussed.

## Key claim

The electroproduction cross section of the d* dibaryon via the reaction ed → ed* is calculated to be about 10 nb for a 1 GeV electron scattered around 30 degrees in the laboratory frame, but this result is an estimate based on several simplifications (neglect of deuteron D-wave, neglect of the Δ-Δ component of the deuteron, use of a single Gaussian wave function for d*, and omission of the NN*(1440) intermediate state) that may affect the precise value.

## Method

The authors used the meson exchange current model, following the hadronic approach of Gomez Tejedor and Oset (GO), to calculate the transition current for ed → ed*. They included only the NN and NN*(1520) intermediate states (Figs. 5 and 6), as the Kroll-Ruderman and meson exchange terms (Figs. 3 and 4) cancel due to isospin and angular momentum conservation. The transition form factors were computed using a single Gaussian wave function for d* (size parameter b* = 0.7 fm) and a three-Gaussian fit for the deuteron, with the d* mass taken as 2.1 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The differential cross section for ed → ed* around 30 degrees in the laboratory frame for a 1 GeV electron is about 10 nb.
- The d* mass is taken to be 2.1 GeV.
- The d* internal wave function uses a single Gaussian with size parameter b* = 0.7 fm.
- The deuteron wave function uses three Gaussian size parameters: b = (4.0975, 1.8252, 0.8837) fm with normalization coefficients c = (0.31491, 0.49716, 0.36926).
- The pion form factor cutoff is Λ_π ~ 1250 MeV; the nucleon form factor uses Λ^2 = 0.71 GeV^2.
- The effective coupling constants used: f* = 2.13, f̃_{N*Δπ} = 0.677; for the proton: g̃_γ = 0.108, g̃_σ = -0.049; for the neutron: g̃_γ = -0.129, g̃_σ = -0.0073.
- The cross section for d* production is about two orders of magnitude smaller than that of quasi-elastic ed → e' NN and Δ resonance production ed → e' NΔ → e' NNπ processes.

## Context

This work extends the hadronic model calculations of Gomez Tejedor and Oset (GO) for γd → ΔΔ and ed → e' pΔ to the specific d* dibaryon channel (IJ^p = 03^+), which is predicted by the quark delocalization model of Wang et al. (1990s) as a narrow resonance near the NNπ threshold. The calculated cross section of ~10 nb is intended to guide experimental searches at GeV-energy electron facilities, but the authors note that the d* signal will be buried in the much larger quasi-elastic and Δ resonance backgrounds, requiring special kinematics and detector systems to extract it. The paper also contrasts the d* mass predictions of different models: meson-baryon coupling (binding 11-340 MeV), quark-meson-gluon hybrid (binding 37-80 MeV), pure gluon exchange (binding 18 MeV), MIT bag (mass 2340 MeV), R-matrix bag (mass 2840 MeV), and skyrmion (binding ~10 MeV).
