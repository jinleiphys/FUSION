# $πωρ$ vertex in nuclear matter

> Medium modifications of the $πωρ$ vertex are analyzed in context of the $ω\to π^0 γ^\ast$ and $ρ\to πγ^\ast$ decays in nuclear matter. A relativistic hadronic model with mesons, nucleons, and $Δ(1232)$ isobars is applied. A substantial increase of the widths for the decays $ω\to π^0 γ^\ast$ and $ρ\to πγ^\ast$ is found for photon virtualities in the range $0.3-0.6$GeV. This enhancement has a direct importance for the description of dilepton yields from dalitz decays in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

# πωρ vertex in nuclear matter

## Key claim

The in-medium πωρ vertex is substantially enhanced at nuclear matter density and zero temperature, leading to an increase of the dilepton yield from Dalitz decays of ω and ρ mesons by factors of approximately 3 and 5 respectively, but only for vector meson momenta relative to the medium below about 200 MeV and for photon virtualities in the range 0.3–0.6 GeV.

## Method

The author applies a relativistic hadronic model including mesons, nucleons, and Δ(1232) isobars, working at leading baryon density and zero temperature. The nucleon propagator in nuclear matter is decomposed into free and density parts, and the Rarita-Schwinger propagator is used for the Δ(1232) with its width incorporated via a complex mass. The diagrams include one nucleon density propagator per diagram, and the decays ω → π⁰γ* and ρ → πγ* are analyzed for both transverse and longitudinal polarizations of the decaying vector meson.

## Key numbers

- The enhancement factor for the ω → π⁰γ* decay width in nuclear matter is approximately 3.
- The enhancement factor for the ρ → πγ* decay width in nuclear matter is approximately 5.
- The medium effects remain large for vector meson momenta |q| lower than 200 MeV, which are the relevant values for heavy-ion collisions.
- The enhancement occurs for photon virtualities in the range 0.3–0.6 GeV.
- The pion decay constant Fπ is taken as 93 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by the same author (Bieniek, 2002) on the in-medium πωρ coupling, motivated by the finding that the ρππ coupling is considerably enhanced in medium. The results address a known problem in the description of dilepton production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where existing calculations by the CERES and HELIOS collaborations have serious problems supplying enough strength in the 0.3–0.6 GeV region. The author uses the fire cylinder evolution model from Rapp and Wambach (1999) to estimate the dilepton spectrum, and emphasizes that the Dalitz decays of the ρ meson should not be neglected as is frequently done.
