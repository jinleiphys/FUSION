# Virtual photons in baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We construct the general Lagrangian for relativistic and heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory with virtual photons to fourth order. We work out the electromagnetic and strong isospin violating contributions to the nucleon self-energy, the nucleon mass and the scalar form factor of the nucleon. Electromagnetic effects for the shift to the Cheng-Dashen point can be as large as 2 MeV. We also discuss the corrections to Weinberg's prediction for the scattering length difference $a(π^0 p) - a(π^0 n)$ and show that they are small.

## Key claim
The paper constructs the complete fourth-order pion-nucleon Lagrangian with virtual photons in both relativistic and heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, showing that electromagnetic effects on the shift to the Cheng-Dashen point can be as large as 2 MeV, while corrections to Weinberg's prediction for the scattering length difference \(a(\pi^0 p) - a(\pi^0 n)\) remain small.

## Method
The authors constructed the general effective Lagrangian for baryon chiral perturbation theory (both relativistic and heavy baryon formulations) including virtual photons up to fourth order in small momenta and electric charge, counting \(e = \mathcal{O}(q)\). They worked out the electromagnetic and strong isospin-violating contributions to the nucleon self-energy, nucleon mass, and scalar form factor, and applied the formalism to compute corrections to the Cheng-Dashen point shift and to Weinberg's scattering length difference prediction.

## Key numbers
- Electromagnetic effects for the shift to the Cheng-Dashen point can be as large as 2 MeV.
- The low-energy constant \(f_2\) from the third-order calculation of the proton-neutron mass difference is \(f_2 = -(0.45 \pm 0.19)\,\text{GeV}^{-1}\).
- Weinberg's prediction for the scattering length difference \(a(\pi^0 p) - a(\pi^0 n)\) gave an effect of order 30%; the authors show the corrections to this prediction are small.

## Context
This work extends the third-order electromagnetic pion-nucleon Lagrangian of Meißner and Steininger (MS) to fourth order, motivated by the need for precision in two-loop calculations of processes like pion-pion scattering and by the fact that one-loop graphs with dimension-two insertions (encoding \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance effects) are often large. The construction enables systematic inclusion of virtual photon effects in low-energy pion-nucleon observables, such as the nucleon scalar form factor and isospin-violating scattering lengths, and provides the foundation for future calculations of electromagnetic corrections to nucleon properties and reactions.
