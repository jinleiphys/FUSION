# Effects of three-baryon forces on kaon condensation in hyperon-mixed matter

> Possibility of kaon-condensed phase in hyperon-mixed matter is considered on the basis of chiral symmetry for kaon-baryon and kaon-kaon interactions, being combined with the relativistic mean-field theory for two-body baryon interaction. In addition, universal three-baryon repulsive force in the string-junction model and phenomenological three-nucleon attractive force are introduced. It is shown that softening of the equation of state stemming from both kaon condensation and mixing of hyperons is compensated with the repulsive effect of the three-baryon force and the relativistic effect for two-body baryon-baryon interaction. The latter effect reflects the density-dependence of scalar and vector meson mean-fields, which is constrained by the contribution of the attractive three-nucleon force to the binding energy at saturation density. The kaon-condensed phase in hyperon-mixed matter becomes stiff enough to be consistent with recent observations of massive neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the kaon-condensed phase in hyperon-mixed matter can be made stiff enough to be consistent with recent observations of massive neutron stars, but only when universal three-baryon repulsive force and relativistic effects for two-body baryon-baryon interaction are included to compensate for the softening of the equation of state that would otherwise occur from both kaon condensation and hyperon mixing.

## Method

The authors combine an effective chiral Lagrangian for kaon-baryon and kaon-kaon interactions with a minimal relativistic mean-field model for two-body baryon interactions mediated by meson exchange. They introduce a universal three-baryon repulsive force derived from the string-junction model (SJM2 parameterization) and a phenomenological three-nucleon attractive force, constructing a baryon interaction model that reproduces saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter. The ground state energy for the hyperon-mixed matter with kaon condensates is obtained under charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium conditions at a given baryon density.

## Key numbers

- Onset density of kaon condensation for Sigma_Kn = 300 MeV: rho_B^c(K) = 0.56, 0.62, 0.70 fm^{-3} for L = 60, 65, 70 MeV respectively
- Onset density of kaon condensation for Sigma_Kn = 400 MeV: rho_B^c(K) = 0.46, 0.48, 0.50 fm^{-3} for L = 60, 65, 70 MeV respectively
- Three-nucleon repulsion energy at saturation density: E(TNR) = 4.1 MeV per baryon (SJM2 model) compared to 3.5 MeV in Lagaris-Pandharipande (1981)
- Three-nucleon attraction energy at saturation density: E(TNA) = -6.6 MeV per baryon (SJM2 model) compared to -6.1 MeV in Lagaris-Pandharipande (1981)
- For a 2.0 solar mass neutron star with L = 65 MeV and Sigma_Kn = 300 MeV, the (Y+K) core radius is 3.8 km; for L = 70 MeV and Sigma_Kn = 400 MeV, the core radius is 4.8 km
- The effective mass ratio for nucleon at saturation density ranges from (M_N*/M_N)_0 = 0.71 to 0.78 depending on the slope parameter L

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of coexistent phases with kaon condensates and hyperon-mixed matter (Muto 2008, Maruyama 2019, Muto 2021), which showed significant softening of the equation of state. It addresses the "hyperon puzzle" by introducing universal three-baryon repulsion, following the approach of Tamagaki (2008) and Tamagaki, Takatsuka and Nishizaki (2008), and builds on earlier work by Nishizaki, Takatsuka and Hiura (1994) for three-nucleon attraction. The results enable predictions for neutron star mass-radius relations that can be tested against observations from GW170817, NICER, and massive neutron star measurements, and provide a framework for studying rapid cooling mechanisms in neutron stars with kaon-condensed cores.
