# Temperature and density dependence of asymmetric nuclear matter and protoneutron star properties within an extended relativistic mean field model

> The effect of temperature and density dependence of the asymmetric nuclear matter properties is studied within the extended relativistic mean field (ERMF) model, which includes the contribution from the self and mixed interaction terms by using different parametrizations obtained by varying the neutron skin thickness $Δ$r and $ω$-meson self-coupling ($ζ$). We observed that the symmetry energy and its slope and incompressibility coefficients decrease with increasing temperatures up to saturation densities. The ERMF parametrizations were employed to obtain a new set of equations of state (EOS) of the protoneutron star (PNS) with and without inclusion of hyperons. In our calculations, in comparison with cold compact stars, we obtained that the gravitational mass of the protoneutron star with and without hyperons increased by $\sim 0.4M_{\odot}$ and its radius increased by $\sim 3$km. Whereas in case of the rotating PNS, the mass shedding limit decreased with increasing temperature, and this suggested that the keplerian frequency of the PNS, at T = 10 MeV should be smaller by $14-20%$ for the EOS with hyperon, as compared to the keplerian frequency of a cold compact star.

# Temperature and density dependence of asymmetric nuclear matter and protoneutron star properties within an extended relativistic mean field model

## Key claim

The symmetry energy, its slope, and the incompressibility coefficient of asymmetric nuclear matter decrease with increasing temperature up to saturation densities, but these bulk properties remain almost unchanged up to 20 MeV and only vary significantly above that temperature. The gravitational mass of protoneutron stars (PNS) increases by approximately 0.4 solar masses and radius by approximately 3 km compared to cold compact stars, but these increases are conditional on the specific parametrizations of the extended relativistic mean field (ERMF) model and the inclusion or exclusion of hyperons.

## Method

The authors employed the extended relativistic mean field (ERMF) model, which includes self and mixed interaction terms for the scalar-isoscalar sigma meson, vector-isoscalar omega meson, and vector-isovector rho meson, using 21 parametrization sets (BSR1-BSR21) generated by varying the omega-meson self-coupling (zeta = 0.0, 0.03, 0.06) and the neutron skin thickness of Pb-208 (0.16 to 0.28 fm in 0.02 fm intervals). They solved the Euler-Lagrange equations for ground state expectation values of meson fields at finite temperatures (0 to 30 MeV), computed the equation of state for charge-neutral matter in beta equilibrium, and integrated the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for non-rotating PNS properties. For rotating PNS, they solved the Einstein field equations for stationary axisymmetric spacetime using the rotating neutron star (RNS) code.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- Symmetry energy at saturation: E_sym = 30 +/- 5 MeV (from experimental constraints cited in the paper)
- Slope of symmetry energy: L = 88 +/- 25 MeV (from experimental constraints cited in the paper)
- Incompressibility coefficient: K = 240 +/- 20 MeV (from experimental constraints cited in the paper)
- Incompressibility coefficient decreases by up to 12.5% at T = 30 MeV relative to T = 0 MeV for the BSR1 parametrization (stiffest EOS, maximum gravitational mass M = 2.5 solar masses)
- Incompressibility coefficient decreases by a minimum of 7% at T = 30 MeV for the BSR21 parametrization (softest EOS, maximum gravitational mass M = 1.74 solar masses)
- Gravitational mass of PNS with and without hyperons increases by approximately 0.4 solar masses compared to cold compact stars
- Radius of PNS with and without hyperons increases by approximately 3 km compared to cold compact stars
- Radius at canonical mass (1.4 solar masses) increases by 3-6 km at finite temperature
- Gravitational redshift at finite temperature decreases by approximately 0.03-0.07
- Keplerian angular velocity of PNS without hyperons decreases by 5%-8% compared to cold compact stars
- Keplerian angular velocity of PNS with hyperons at T = 10 MeV decreases by 14%-20% compared to cold compact stars
- In the Kepler limit sequences, gravitational maximum mass of PNS increases by 20%-23% with temperature, and equatorial radius increases by 25%-46% relative to non-rotating values
- Threshold density for Lambda hyperons in cold matter: 0.376 fm^-3; at T = 10 MeV: decreases to 0.112 fm^-3
- Threshold density for Sigma- hyperons in cold matter: 0.482 fm^-3; at T = 10 MeV: increases to 0.902 fm^-3
- Threshold density for Xi- hyperons in cold matter: 0.490 fm^-3; at T = 3 MeV: Xi- hyperons disappear
- Central energy density upper limit from Tolman VII solution: 1.92 x 10^15 g cm^-3 for BSR1, 2.73 x 10^15 g cm^-3 for BSR15

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous ERMF model (Dhiman 2007) to finite temperatures, studying protoneutron star properties in addition to cold compact stars. It builds on the relativistic mean field framework of Walecka (1974) and Ring (1996), and compares results with the NL3 parametrization (Lalazissis 1997) and TM1 parametrization (Sugahara 1994) of RMF theory. The paper addresses the open question of how temperature affects the density dependence of symmetry energy and incompressibility, which are critical for understanding supernova explosion mechanisms, the liquid-gas phase transition of asymmetric nuclear matter, and the dynamical evolution of massive stars. The results enable future studies of protoneutron star cooling and rotation, particularly the finding that hyperon-containing PNS can satisfy the PSR J1614-2230 mass constraint (Demorest 2010) of 1.97 +/- 0.04 solar masses if the omega-meson self-coupling zeta = 0.0 or 0.03 and the hyperon-meson coupling parameter X_omega_y >= 0.50.
