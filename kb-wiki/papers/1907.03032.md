# The role of the local conservation laws in fluctuations of conserved charges

> In this report we present the first quantitative determination of the correlations between baryons and anti-baryons induced by local baryon number conservation. This is important in view of the many experimental studies aiming at probing the phase structure of strongly interacting matter. We confront our results with the recent measurements of net-proton fluctuations reported by the CERN ALICE experiment. The role of local baryon number conservation is found to be small on the level of second cumulants.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first quantitative determination of correlations between baryons and anti-baryons induced by local baryon number conservation, finding that the effect on second cumulants of net-proton fluctuations measured by ALICE is small; the data are consistent with global baryon number conservation and with local conservation only if the correlation length in rapidity is at least 5 units.

## Method

The authors used a canonical ensemble (CE) partition function with fixed net-baryon number to sample baryon and anti-baryon multiplicities, then introduced finite acceptance by correlating each baryon with an anti-baryon within a variable rapidity correlation length Δy_corr. They compared the resulting normalized second cumulant κ₂(B−B̄)/⟨n_B+n_B̄⟩ as a function of acceptance fraction α to ALICE net-proton fluctuation data, using the charged-particle pseudorapidity distribution shape from ALICE as a proxy for baryon rapidity distributions.

## Key numbers

- For Δy_corr = ∞ (global conservation), the normalized second cumulant κ₂(B−B̄)/⟨n_B+n_B̄⟩ follows Eq.(4) of Braun-Munzinger (2018) and is consistent with ALICE data within experimental uncertainties.
- ALICE data are consistent with Δy_corr ≥ 5; values of Δy_corr smaller than 5 lead to disagreement with experimental measurements.
- The HIJING generator, which corresponds to Δy_corr = 2, underestimates the experimental data.
- Simulations used 10⁷ events with ⟨N_B⟩ = ⟨N_B̄⟩ = 50 for baryons and anti-baryons respectively.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Braun-Munzinger 2018) which described STAR net-proton cumulant data above √s_NN = 11.5 GeV using global baryon number conservation and participant fluctuations. It confronts the ALICE net-proton fluctuation measurements (Rustamov 2017) and shows that local baryon number conservation effects are small for second cumulants, contrary to the stronger correlations seen in the HIJING event generator (Gyulassy 1994, Deng 2010). The results enable quantitative separation of conservation-law effects from possible critical fluctuations in searches for the QCD critical point.
