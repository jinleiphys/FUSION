# Magnetic field dependence of Delta isobars properties in a Skyrme model

> The properties of $Δ$ isobars in a uniform magnetic field are investigated. In the weak magnetic field region, the general relations between magnetic moment of nucleons and $Δ$ isobars are given. In the strong magnetic field region, the mass and size of $Δ$ isobars depend on the increasing of magnetic field strength in different ways: the effective mass of $Δ^{++}$, $Δ^{+}$ and $Δ^{0}$ first decreases and then increases, consequently, the size of $Δ^{++}$, $Δ^{+}$ and $Δ^{0}$ first increases and then decreases; whereas, the effective mass of $Δ^{-}$ always increases, and consequently, the size of $Δ^{-}$ always decreases. The estimation shows in the core part of the magnetar, the equation of state for $Δ$ isobars depends on the magnetic field, which affects the mass limit of the magnetar.

## Key claim
The properties of Delta isobars in a uniform magnetic field depend on the magnetic field strength in a charge-dependent manner, with the effective mass of Delta++, Delta+, and Delta0 first decreasing then increasing, while the effective mass of Delta- always increases; these results are obtained within the semi-classical quantization approach of the Skyrme model and are conditional on the specific parameter set used (m_pi=138 MeV, f_pi=108 MeV, g=4.84).

## Method
The study uses the Skyrme model with a minimal action including pion dynamics and the Wess-Zumino-Witten term, employing a semi-classical quantization approach where spin and isospin are obtained by rotating the ansatz equations in both spatial and isospin space. The Hamiltonian is expanded up to order N_C^{-1}, and the baryon mass and magnetic moment are computed as expectation values of the Hamiltonian and its derivative with respect to the magnetic field, respectively. The equations of motion are obtained from the action at order N_C, and the parameter c_z is fixed to minimize the Delta mass for each magnetic field strength.

## Key numbers
- Minimal mass of Delta++, J_3=3/2 is about 1147 MeV when |eB| ~ 3.2 m_pi^2.
- At |eB| ~ 0, using experimental mu_p=2.793 [mu_N] and mu_n=-1.913 [mu_N], the predicted magnetic moments are: mu_Delta++,J_3=3/2 = 5.42 [mu_N] (experimental 5.6 +/- 1.9 [mu_N]), mu_Delta+,J_3=3/2 = 2.69 [mu_N] (experimental 2.7^{+1.0}_{-1.3} +/- 1.5 +/- 3 [mu_N]), mu_Delta0,J_3=3/2 = -0.05 [mu_N], mu_Delta-,J_3=3/2 = -2.78 [mu_N].
- At |eB| ~ 0, using Skyrme model values mu_p=1.94 [mu_N] and mu_n=-1.21 [mu_N], the predicted magnetic moments for J_3=3/2 states are: mu_Delta++=3.80 [mu_N], mu_Delta+=2.00 [mu_N], mu_Delta0=0.20 [mu_N], mu_Delta-=-1.61 [mu_N].
- At |eB| ~ 0, the mean square electric charge radii are: Delta++ = 3.113 [fm^2], Delta+ = 1.313 [fm^2], Delta0 = -0.487 [fm^2], Delta- = -2.287 [fm^2].
- At |eB| ~ 10^{-2} [GeV^2], the density changes compared to vacuum are: Delta++,J_3=3/2 decreases 8.5%, Delta+,J_3=3/2 decreases 0.9%, Delta0,J_3=3/2 decreases 7.4%, Delta-,J_3=3/2 increases 19.7%.

## Context
This work extends the Skyrme model study of nucleons in a uniform magnetic field by He (2016) to Delta isobars, and provides general relations between nucleon and Delta magnetic moments that are consistent with experimental results from Patrignani (2016) and Tanabashi (2018). The results show that the magnetic field modifies the equation of state for Delta isobars in magnetar cores, which affects the mass limit of magnetars, building on studies of Delta baryon media in neutron stars by Kolomeitsev (2016) and Li (2018). The work also addresses the wide range of experimental magnetic moment values for Delta isobars noted by Pascalutsa (2006), Aubin (2008), Slaughter (2011), and Kaur (2015).
