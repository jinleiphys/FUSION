# Hyperonization in compact stars

> We review the covariant density functional approach to the equation of state of the dense nuclear matter in compact stars. The main emphasis is on the hyperonization of the dense matter, and the role played by the $Δ$-resonances. The implications of hyperonization for the astrophysics of compact stars, including the equation of state, composition, and stellar parameters are examined. The mass-radius relation and tidal deformabilities of static and rapidly rotating (Keplerian) configurations are discussed in some detail. We briefly touch upon some other recent developments involving hyperonization in hot hypernuclear matter at high- and low-densities.

## Key claim
The paper reviews how covariant density functional (CDF) methods can resolve the "hyperon puzzle", the contradiction between observations of two-solar-mass pulsars and the softening of the equation of state caused by hyperon onset, by showing that modern density functionals for hypernuclear matter can simultaneously account for high pulsar masses, gravitational wave data, and X-ray observations.

## Method
The authors construct a relativistic mean-field Lagrangian for dense stellar matter that includes the full spin-1/2 baryon octet (nucleons and hyperons) and spin-3/2 Δ-resonances, with density-dependent meson-baryon coupling constants. The DDME2 parametrization is adopted for the nucleonic sector, while hyperon and Δ couplings are determined via SU(3) flavor symmetry relations with ideal vector mixing. The equations of state are obtained by solving the mean-field equations in the Hartree approximation, from which stellar structure and tidal deformabilities are computed using general relativity.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear saturation density: ρ_sat = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Central densities in compact stars may reach up to about 10 times ρ_sat
- Pulsar mass lower limits from timing observations: PSR J1614-2230 (2.08 ± 0.07 M_⊙ at 68.3% credibility), PSR J0348+0432 (1.928 ± 0.017 M_⊙), PSR J0740+6620 (2.08 ± 0.07 M_⊙ at 68.3% credibility)
- GW170817 binary total mass: 2.73⁺⁰·⁰⁴₋₀·₀₁ M_⊙; component masses 1.16–1.6 M_⊙; upper limit Λ₁.₄ < 700 for a 1.4 M_⊙ star (90% confidence)
- GW190425 binary total mass: 3.4⁺⁰·³₋₀·₁ M_⊙; component masses 1.46–1.87 M_⊙; effective tidal deformability Λ̃ ≤ 600
- NICER radius measurements for PSR J0030+0451: R = 13.02⁺¹·²⁴₋₁·₀₆ km (Miller 2019) and R = 12.71⁺¹·¹⁴₋₁·₁₉ km (Riley 2019) at 68% credible interval
- NICER radius for PSR J0740+6620: R = 13.7⁺²·⁶₋₁·₅ km (Miller 2021) and R = 12.39⁺¹·³₋₀·₉₈ km (Riley 2021) at 68% credible interval

## Context
This review extends the covariant density functional approach to hypernuclear matter originally developed by Glendenning (1985) and Schaffner (1995), and addresses the hyperon puzzle identified by Baldo and Schulze after the 2010 discovery of the two-solar-mass pulsar PSR J1614-2230 by Demorest. The work enables future comparisons of hypernuclear equations of state with gravitational wave constraints from binary neutron star mergers (GW170817, GW190425) and X-ray pulse profile measurements from NICER, while also providing a framework for studying rapidly rotating configurations and finite-temperature hypernuclear matter.
