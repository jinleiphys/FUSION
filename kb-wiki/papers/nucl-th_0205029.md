# RPA vs. exact shell-model correlation energies

> The random phase approximation (RPA) builds in correlations left out by mean-field theory. In full 0-hbar-omega shell-model spaces we calculate the Hartree-Fock + RPA binding energy, and compare it to exact diagonalization. We find that in general HF+RPA gives a very good approximation to the ``exact'' ground state energy. In those cases where RPA is less satisfactory, however, there is no obvious correlation with properties of the HF state, such as deformation or overlap with the exact ground state wavefunction.

# RPA vs. exact shell-model correlation energies

## Key claim

HF+RPA generally gives a very good approximation to the exact ground-state binding energy in full 0-ℏω shell-model spaces, but in cases where RPA is less satisfactory there is no obvious correlation with properties of the HF state such as deformation or overlap with the exact ground-state wavefunction.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock calculations followed by random phase approximation (RPA) in full 0-ℏω shell-model spaces for nuclei in the sd-shell (using the Wildenthal USD interaction with an ¹⁶O core) and the lower pf-shell (using the modified KB3 interaction with a ⁴⁰Ca core). They compared the HF+RPA binding energies against exact shell-model diagonalization in the same valence spaces, computing the correlation energy as the difference between the HF energy and the ground-state energy from each method.

## Key numbers

- For 41 sd-shell nuclides (excluding oxygen isotopes), the rms deviation between HF+RPA and exact shell-model correlation energies is 870 keV.
- For 11 lower pf-shell nuclides (excluding calcium isotopes), the rms deviation is 480 keV.
- For 6 oxygen isotopes (neutrons only), the rms deviation is 1800 keV.
- For 7 calcium isotopes (neutrons only), the rms deviation is 730 keV.
- For spherical nuclei, RPA overestimates the correlation energy by as much as a factor of two (e.g., ²²O ratio 1.67, ²⁴O ratio 1.83, ⁴⁸Ca ratio 1.66).
- For deformed nuclei, the ratio R = E_corr_RPA / E_corr_SM ranges from 0.66 (⁴⁴Ti, β=0.44, prolate) to 1.17 (²⁴Na, β=0.24, γ=13°).

## Context

This work extends tests of HF+RPA binding energies beyond simple schematic models such as the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model (Lipkin 1965) and two-level pairing models (Bertsch 2000) to realistic shell-model spaces with full diagonalization. The results support the proposal by Bertsch and Hagino (2000) to use mean-field theory corrected through RPA for nuclear binding-energy systematics. The authors find that the kinetic energy of RPA zero modes cannot be identified with the correlation energy obtained by projecting the HF state onto good angular momentum, which has implications for removing spurious center-of-mass motion in multi-ℏω spaces.
