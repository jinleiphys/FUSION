# Toward a generative modeling analysis of CLAS exclusive $2π$ photoproduction

> AI-supported algorithms, particularly generative models, have been successfully used in a variety of different contexts. In this work, we demonstrate for the first time that generative adversarial networks (GANs) can be used in high-energy experimental physics to unfold detector effects from multi-particle final states, while preserving correlations between kinematic variables in multidimensional phase space. We perform a full closure test on two-pion photoproduction pseudodata generated with a realistic model in the kinematics of the Jefferson Lab CLAS g11 experiment. The overlap of different reaction mechanisms leading to the same final state associated with the CLAS detector's nontrivial effects represents an ideal test case for AI-supported analysis. Uncertainty quantification performed via bootstrap provides an estimate of the systematic uncertainty associated with the procedure. The test demonstrates that GANs can reproduce highly correlated multidifferential cross sections even in the presence of detector-induced distortions in the training datasets, and provides a solid basis for applying the framework to real experimental data.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates for the first time that generative adversarial networks (GANs) can successfully unfold detector effects from multi-particle final states in high-energy experimental physics while preserving correlations between kinematic variables in multidimensional phase space, but only in a closure test using pseudodata rather than real experimental data.

## Method
The authors generated two sets of Monte Carlo pseudodata for two-pion photoproduction at CLAS g11 experiment kinematics: a phase-space MC (PS-MC) and a realistic MC (RE-MC) that includes the three dominant intermediate resonances (p rho0, Delta++ pi-, Delta0 pi+). They trained a detector-simulation GAN (DS-GAN) on PS-MC pseudodata passed through a GEANT simulation of the CLAS detector, then used an unfolding GAN (UNF-GAN) that takes generator-level events through the trained DS-GAN to produce synthetic detector-level events. Uncertainty quantification was performed via bootstrap resampling.

## Key numbers
- The CLAS g11 experiment collected approximately 40 million events identified as exclusive two-pion photoproduction in the photon energy range 3.0-3.8 GeV
- The primary electron beam energy was 4.02 GeV, providing a tagged photon beam in the energy range from 0.8 to 3.8 GeV
- The CLAS detector momentum resolution sigma_p/p ranged from 0.5 to 1.0 percent depending on kinematics
- The photon energy resolution of the bremsstrahlung tagging system was 0.1 percent
- The detector geometrical acceptance for each positive particle was about 40 percent and somewhat less for negative particles
- The relative momentum resolution for detected pi+ was delta p/p approximately 0.8 percent and angular resolution delta theta/theta approximately 0.5 percent from double Gaussian fits
- The nonresonant two-pion photoproduction contribution was added at approximately 10 percent constant level
- The yield ratio for different detection topologies was 100:37:30:35 for p pi+(pi-), p pi-(pi+), pi+ pi-(p), and all three detected respectively

## Context
This work extends previous GAN-based unfolding analyses that were limited to inclusive electroproduction and single-variable cross sections (Alanazi 2020) to the more challenging case of multiparticle final states with higher-dimensional phase space. The two-pion photoproduction process is important for addressing the missing baryons problem in hadron spectroscopy, and previous analyses by the CLAS Collaboration (2009, 2018) were limited to fitting theoretical curves to marginal 1D distributions, losing correlations between variables. The successful closure test provides a basis for applying the framework to real experimental data from Jefferson Lab.
