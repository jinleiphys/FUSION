# Bottomonium in a Bethe-Salpeter-equation study

> Using a well-established effective interaction in a rainbow-ladder truncation model of QCD, we fix the remaining model parameter to the bottomonium ground-state spectrum in a covariant Bethe-Salpeter equation approach and find surprisingly good agreement with the available experimental data including the 2^{--} Upsilon(1D) state. Furthermore, we investigate the consequences of such a fit for charmonium and light-quark ground states.

## Key claim

Using a rainbow-ladder truncation model of QCD with a fixed effective interaction, the authors find that a single parameter (ω = 0.61 GeV) fitted to bottomonium ground states yields agreement with experimental data at the level of 3 per-mill for all states considered, without fine-tuning. The hedge is that this agreement is "surprisingly good" but the authors acknowledge that the same parameter set performs less well for charmonium and light mesons, and that corrections beyond rainbow-ladder truncation will be needed for a comprehensive description.

## Method

The authors solve the rainbow-truncated quark Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) and the ladder-truncated quark-antiquark Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) in Euclidean momentum space using Landau-gauge QCD. They employ the effective interaction parameterization of Maris and Tandy (1999) with the product D×ω fixed to 0.372 GeV³, and vary ω to fit bottomonium ground-state masses for J^P = 0⁻, 0⁺, 1⁻, 1⁺, and 2⁺ states via least-squares minimization. For higher-lying states where direct numerical solution is inaccessible, they use an extrapolation method based on the eigenvalue behavior of the BSE near the bound-state pole.

## Key numbers

- Bottomonium ground-state masses (in MeV): η_b (0⁻⁺) = 9405, Υ (1⁻⁻) = 9488, χ_b0 (0⁺⁺) = 9831, χ_b1 (1⁺⁺) = 9878, h_b (1⁺⁻) = 9873, χ_b2 (2⁺⁺) = 9927, η_b2 (2⁻⁺) = 10184(8), Υ(1D) (2⁻⁻) = 10188(8)
- Hyperfine splitting between 0⁻⁺ and 1⁻⁻ bottomonium ground states: reproduced to 20% of the experimental value of 69 MeV
- Leptonic decay constant for Υ (1⁻⁻): 687 MeV, within 4% of the experimental value of 715 MeV
- Pseudoscalar bottomonium decay constant f_η_b: 708 MeV (experimental value unknown)
- Charmonium pseudoscalar decay constant: 399 MeV (experimental: 361 MeV, off by 11%)
- Charmonium vector decay constant: 448 MeV (experimental: 416 MeV, off by 8%)

## Context

This work extends the rainbow-ladder DSE-BSE approach, previously applied mainly to light mesons by Maris and Tandy (1999) and to tensor mesons by Krassnigg (2010), into the heavy-quark domain of bottomonium. It provides a baseline for future studies that will include corrections beyond rainbow-ladder truncation, as explored by Fischer and Williams (2009) and Chang and Roberts (2009), and enables systematic fine-tuning of model parameters anchored to the heavy-quark sector while maintaining consistency with lighter systems.
