# Hamiltonian hierarchy and the Hulthen potential

> We deal with the Hamiltonian hierarchy problem of the Hulthén potential within the frame of the supersymmetric quantum mechanics and find that the associated superymmetric partner potentials simulate the effect of the centrifugal barrier. Incorporating the supersymmetric solutions and using the first-order perturbation theory we obtain an expression for the energy levels of theHulthén potential which gives satisfactory values for the non-zero angular momentum states.

# Hamiltonian hierarchy and the Hulthén potential

## Key claim

The authors show that supersymmetric partner potentials of the Hulthén potential simulate the effect of the centrifugal barrier, enabling an approximate analytical treatment for non-zero angular momentum states; however, the exact supersymmetry is broken for ℓ ≠ 0 due to the potential barrier term, so the energy expression obtained via shape invariance alone deviates from accurate numerical values and must be corrected using first-order perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors construct a superpotential ansatz within supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SUSYQM) to generate a Hamiltonian hierarchy for the Hulthén potential, where the (ℓ+1)-th member of the hierarchy yields an effective potential that approximates the centrifugal barrier. They obtain exact analytical eigenvalues and eigenfunctions for the ℓ = 0 case via shape invariance, then treat the deviation between the effective supersymmetric potential and the true Hulthén effective potential for ℓ ≠ 0 as a perturbation, computing first-order corrections to the energy levels.

## Key numbers

- The bound-state energy expression for the effective supersymmetric potential is: E_{ℓ+1}^n = −(me⁴)/(2ℏ²) × [1/(n+ℓ+1) − (n+ℓ+1)β/2]², where β = ℏ²δ/(me²) and n = 0,1,2,...
- The critical screening parameter in atomic units is given by δ_c = 2/(n+ℓ+1)², which works well for ℓ = 0 but fails for non-zero angular momentum states.
- For small δ, the effective Hulthén potential approximates the effective Coulomb potential: V_H^{eff}(r, δ ≅ 0) → V_C^{eff}(r) = −e²/r + ℓ(ℓ+1)ℏ²/(2mr²).

## Context

This work extends the Hamiltonian hierarchy approach previously applied to the Coulomb potential by Sukumar (1985) to the short-range Hulthén potential, which is a special case of the Eckart potential (1930). The method builds on the shape invariance condition of Gendenshtein (1983) and the approximate effective potential introduced by Greene and Aldrich (1976) for generating pseudo-Hulthén wave functions. The results enable analytical treatment of non-zero angular momentum states for the Hulthén potential, complementing earlier numerical approaches by Varshni (1990) and others, and the authors note that work on broken supersymmetry for exact energies of ℓ ≠ 0 states is in progress.
