# Analysis of the low-energy $π^- p$ charge-exchange data

> We analyse the charge-exchange (CX) measurements $π^- p\rightarrow π^0 n$ below pion laboratory kinetic energy of 100 MeV. After the removal of five degrees of freedom from the initial database, we combine it with the truncated $π^+ p$ database of Ref. \cite{mrw1} and fit the ETH model \cite{glmbg} to the resulting data. The set of the parameter values of the ETH model, as well as the predictions derived on their basis for the hadronic phase shifts and for the low-energy $πN$ constants, are significantly different from the results obtained in the analysis of the truncated $π^\pm p$ elastic-scattering databases. The main difference in the hadronic phase shifts occurs in $\tildeδ_{0+}^{1/2}$. We discuss the implications of these findings in terms of the violation of the isospin invariance in the hadronic part of the $πN$ interaction. The effect observed amounts to the level of $7-8 %$ in the CX scattering amplitude below 70 MeV. The results and conclusions of this study agree well with those obtained in the mid 1990s, when the isospin invariance was first tested by using $πN$ experimental data, and disagree with the predictions obtained within the framework of the heavy-baryon Chiral-Perturbation Theory.

## Key claim

The analysis of the combined π⁺p elastic-scattering and charge-exchange (CX) databases using the isospin-invariant ETH model yields parameter values and hadronic phase shifts that differ significantly from those obtained from the π⁺p elastic-scattering data alone, with the main difference occurring in the I=1/2 s-wave phase shift δ̃₀₊^(1/2); the authors interpret this as evidence for a 7–8% violation of isospin invariance in the hadronic part of the πN interaction below 70 MeV, consistent with mid-1990s tests but disagreeing with heavy-baryon Chiral-Perturbation Theory predictions.

## Method

The authors performed a combined fit of the ETH model (an isospin-invariant effective Hamiltonian with seven parameters) to the truncated π⁺p elastic-scattering database (340 data points) and the truncated CX database (328 data points, after removing five outliers from the original 333 CX data points below 100 MeV pion laboratory kinetic energy). The CX data included differential cross sections, total cross sections, analyzing powers, and one threshold constant from experiments spanning BUGG71 to MEKTEROVIĆ09, with normalisation uncertainties assigned where not reported. The fit used K-matrix parameterisations as an intermediate step, then applied the ETH model, and the results (labelled ZUAS12a) were compared to the earlier ZUAS12 solution from π⁺p elastic-scattering only.

- The CX database increased from 47 data points (mid-1990s) to 333 data points in the present work.

- The ETH model fit to tDB₊/₀ yielded χ² = 960.5 for 661 degrees of freedom.
- The isovector s-wave scattering length from the combined fit: ãᶜ⁰ = -0.11660(79) μc⁻¹.
- The isoscalar s-wave scattering length from the combined fit: ãᶜᶜ = 0.0884(26) μc⁻¹.

- The weighted average of CX scale factors below 70 MeV lies (15.6 ± 1.4)% above ZUAS12 predictions.
- Relative difference between ZUAS12a and ZUAS12 CX DCS predictions at 30 MeV: 22.1% at θ=0°, 10.4% at θ=180°.
- Relative difference in CX total cross section between the two predictions: 12.2% at 20 MeV, 11.2% at 30 MeV, 10.0% at 40 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous phase-shift analysis of π⁺p elastic scattering (Matsinos and Rasche, 2012, labelled ZUAS12) and the self-consistency study of π⁺p differential cross sections (Matsinos and Rasche, 2012, labelled ZUAS12b). It directly tests the isospin invariance hypothesis in the hadronic πN interaction, following the mid-1990s analyses by Gibbs, Kaufmann, and collaborators (1995) and Matsinos (1997) that first reported evidence for isospin breaking using a much smaller CX database (47 points). The results contradict the predictions of heavy-baryon Chiral-Perturbation Theory, and the authors suggest that the observed discrepancies between the ZUAS12 and ZUAS12a solutions cannot be resolved by any single experiment due to large normalisation uncertainties (typically 10%). The findings motivate further low-energy CX measurements, particularly around the s- and p-wave interference minimum near 45 MeV, to distinguish between the two prediction sets.
