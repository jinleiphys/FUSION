# Role of meson exchange and final-state interaction in $J/ψ$-meson photoproduction

> We investigate $J/ψ$ photoproduction off the nucleon using a dynamical model based on a Hamiltonian framework. First, Pomeron exchange is regarded as a background contribution that accounts for the gradual rise of the total cross section with increasing beam energy. Then, the meson-exchange and direct $J/ψ$-radiation contributions are included as part of the Born term. More specifically, we examine the contributions of the light-meson [$π^0(135)$, $η(548)$, $η'(958)$, $f_1(1285)$] and charmonium-meson [$η_c(1S)$, $χ_{c0}(1P)$, $χ_{c1}(1P)$, $η_c(2S)$, $χ_{c1}(3872)$] exchanges in the $t$-channel diagram. Most of the coupling constants are determined from the radiative decays of the $J/ψ$ or other relevant charmonium states. Finally, we consider the $J/ψN$ final-state interaction (FSI) within the leading-order approximation, which includes gluon-exchange and direct $J/ψN$-coupling terms. The model parameters of the Hamiltonian are fitted to the data from the GlueX and $J/ψ$-007 experiments at Jefferson Laboratory (JLab). The light mesons $η(548)$ and $η'(958)$ are found to make the most significant contributions, whereas the charmonium mesons play only a minor role. The direct $J/ψ$-radiation term begins to come into play only in the backward scattering regions. The FSI contribution is suppressed by a factor of $10^1 - 10^2$ in comparison to the Born-term contribution when a Yukawa-type potential is employed for the charmonium-nucleon interaction. Our results agree well with the available JLab data on the total and $t$-dependent differential cross sections. High-precision, angle-dependent data in the threshold region ($E_γ\leqslant 8.9$ GeV) are crucial for confirming the FSI effect.

## Key claim

Light mesons (η and η') dominate the meson-exchange contribution to J/ψ photoproduction, while charmonium-meson exchange is suppressed by approximately three orders of magnitude; the final-state interaction (FSI) effect is essential for describing the threshold region only when a specific Yukawa potential parameter set (v0=0.42, α=0.6 GeV) is employed, and high-precision angle-dependent data near threshold (Eγ ≤ 8.9 GeV) are needed to confirm this effect.

## Method

The author constructs a dynamical model within a Hamiltonian framework that includes Pomeron exchange as a background, t-channel light-meson and charmonium-meson exchanges, and direct J/ψ-radiation terms in the s- and u-channels as the Born contribution. The J/ψN final-state interaction is treated at leading order in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation using a Yukawa-type charmonium-nucleon potential motivated by gluon exchange, with coupling constants determined from radiative decays of J/ψ and other charmonium states. Model parameters are fitted to total and differential cross section data from the GlueX and J/ψ-007 experiments at Jefferson Laboratory.

## Key numbers

- Light mesons η(548) and η'(958) make the most significant contributions to the cross section; the ηc(1S) charmonium exchange is suppressed by approximately three orders of magnitude compared to the η'(958) exchange
- The direct J/ψ-radiation contribution is suppressed by a factor of 10^2 compared to the meson-exchange contribution
- The FSI contribution is suppressed by a factor of 10^1 to 10^2 compared to the Born-term contribution when a Yukawa-type potential is employed
- Two Yukawa potential parameter sets are tested: fit-1 (v0=0.10, α=0.6 GeV) and fit-2 (v0=0.42, α=0.6 GeV)
- The direct J/ψ-radiation term becomes visible in the backward scattering region at -t ≥ 5 GeV^2
- High-precision data in the threshold region (Eγ ≤ 8.9 GeV) are identified as crucial for confirming the FSI effect

## Context

This work extends the author's previous dynamical model for φ photoproduction (Kim 2021, Kim 2024) to the J/ψ case, addressing the ongoing debate about hidden-charm pentaquark states Pc observed by LHCb (2015, 2019) and the interpretation of cusp structures at the D̄Λc and D̄*Λc thresholds seen in GlueX data (2019, 2023). The results enable more precise extraction of the gluonic gravitational form factor of the nucleon from threshold J/ψ photoproduction measurements, as pursued by Duran (2022), and provide a framework for distinguishing between meson-exchange and FSI contributions in future high-statistics experiments.
