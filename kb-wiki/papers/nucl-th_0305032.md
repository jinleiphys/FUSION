# Goldstone Bosons in the 3P2 Superfluid Phase of Neutron Matter and Neutrino Emission

> At the high densities present in the interior of neutron stars, the neutrons are condensed into the 3P2 superfluid phase. While this condensation has little impact on the equation of state, it can have an important role in determining the low-temperature energy-momentum transport properties. The spontaneous breaking of baryon number by the condensate gives rise to the familiar Goldstone boson, but in addition, the spontaneous breaking of rotational invariance by the condensate gives rise to three Goldstone bosons, in general, one for each broken generator of rotations. These Goldstone bosons, which couple to the Z0, provide a new mechanism for neutrino emission. Using a low-energy effective field theory to describe the dynamics of these Goldstone bosons we estimate the neutrino emissivity of dense neutron matter and show that their annihilation is the dominant energy-loss mechanism over a range of temperatures.

# Goldstone Bosons in the 3P2 Superfluid Phase of Neutron Matter and Neutrino Emission

## Key claim

The authors argue that in the 3P2 superfluid phase of neutron matter, the spontaneous breaking of rotational invariance produces three Goldstone bosons (angulons) whose annihilation into neutrino-antineutrino pairs provides a new neutrino emission mechanism that is not exponentially suppressed at temperatures below the critical temperature; however, the calculation depends on a particular choice of the gap parameter form, and a true model-independent calculation is not possible at the moment.

## Method

The authors construct a low-energy effective field theory for the Goldstone bosons arising from spontaneous breaking of baryon number (one phonon) and rotational invariance (three angulons) in the 3P2 neutron superfluid phase. They match the low-energy constants of the effective theory to a microscopic model of neutron interactions near the Fermi surface, using fictitious gauge fields to relate the decay constants and propagation speeds to the Fermi momentum, effective neutron mass, and gap parameter. The neutrino emissivity from angulon annihilation is then computed from the effective theory using the weak neutral current couplings of the Goldstone bosons to the Z0 boson.

## Key numbers

- The phonon decay constant is $f_0^2 = M k_F / \pi^2$, where $M$ is the neutron mass and $k_F$ is the Fermi momentum.
- The phonon speed is $v_0 = v_F^2 / 3$, where $v_F$ is the Fermi velocity.
- The angulon decay constant is $f^2 = M k_F / (3\pi^2)$.
- The angulon propagation speeds are $v^2 = v_F^2 / 5$ and $w^2 = v_F^2 / 5$.
- The neutrino emissivity from angulon annihilation scales as $\mathcal{E} \sim T^9 / v^3$, with a numerical estimate of $\mathcal{E} \cong 10^{17} \, T_9^9 \, (0.15 / v)^3 \, \mathrm{erg \, cm^{-3} \, s^{-1}}$, where $T_9 = T / (10^9 \, \mathrm{K})$ and $v = 0.15$ for $k_F \sim 308 \, \mathrm{MeV}$ and $M = 940 \, \mathrm{MeV}$.
- At $T \sim 3 \times 10^8 \, \mathrm{K}$ (approximately $T_c / 10$), the angulon annihilation emissivity is $\mathcal{E}_{\alpha\alpha} \sim 10^{12} \, \mathrm{erg \, cm^{-3} \, s^{-1}}$, compared to electron bremsstrahlung emissivity $\mathcal{E}_e \sim 10^{10} \, \mathrm{erg \, cm^{-3} \, s^{-1}}$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the 3P2 neutron superfluid phase (condensate formation known from nuclear potentials, with gap calculations by various authors) by identifying the Goldstone bosons from rotational symmetry breaking and their role in neutrino emission. The authors note that at temperatures much below the critical temperature, processes involving gapped fermions (modified Urca, neutron bremsstrahlung) are exponentially suppressed as $\sim e^{-2\bar\Delta/T}$, while angulon annihilation provides a power-law temperature dependence. The results highlight that determining the actual phase of cold neutron matter (the form of the gap parameter) is more important for understanding neutron star cooling than a precise determination of the gap size, and the computed rates need to be inserted into realistic cooling codes for a full assessment.
