# Lower Excitation Spectrum of the Nucleon and Delta in a Relativistic Chiral Quark Model

> The lower excitation spectrum of the nucleon and $Δ$ is calculated in a relativistic chiral quark model. Contributions of the second order self-energy and exchange diagrams due-to pion fields to the mass spectrum of the SU(2) baryons are estimated. A splitting between N(939) and positive parity nucleon resonance (Roper resonance) $N^*(1440)$ is reproduced with a reasonable accuracy. The obtained structure of one-meson exchange interaction confirms a prediction of the large $N_c$ limit approach stating that the mass splitting between various baryon states receive contributions from operators which simultaneously couple spin, isospin and orbital momentum. It is shown that one-meson exchange interaction generates a splitting between negative parity $N^*(1/2^-)$ and $N^*(3/2^-)$ states, and also between $Δ^*(3/2^-)$ and $Δ^*(1/2^-)$ states in contrast to the non-relativistic Goldstone-Boson Exchange based quark models. This splitting is due-to a relativistic operator which couples the lower and upper orbital momentum of two interacting valence quarks.

## Key claim

The relativistic one-meson exchange interaction generates a splitting between negative parity nucleon and Delta resonances (e.g., between N*(1/2^-) and N*(3/2^-) states) due to a relativistic operator coupling the lower and upper orbital momentum of two interacting valence quarks, in contrast to non-relativistic Goldstone-Boson Exchange quark models which predict no such splitting. However, the model does not reproduce the correct ordering of the lowest positive and negative parity nucleon resonances, indicating a new mechanism is needed.

## Method

The authors develop a relativistic chiral quark model based on an effective chiral Lagrangian with quarks as relativistic fermions moving in a confining static potential (scalar plus vector). The quark-pion interaction is introduced via the nonlinear sigma model, and second-order perturbative corrections (self-energy and exchange diagrams) due to pion fields are calculated using the Gell-Mann and Low theorem. The model parameters (confining potential strength c, constant m, and Coulomb parameter α=0.26) are fitted to reproduce the proton axial charge, the empirical pion-nucleon coupling constant G²πNN/4π ≈ 14, and a quark core RMS radius of 0.5 fm for the proton.

## Key numbers

- The splitting between N(939) and the Roper resonance N*(1440)(1/2^+) is reproduced with a quark core result of 415 MeV in Model A, which after center-of-mass correction restricts to about 200-250 MeV, plus a perturbative correction of 284 MeV.
- Exchange diagram energy shifts for nucleon states with structure (1S)²1P₃/₂ yield -132.8 MeV for N*(1520)(3/2^-) and -150 MeV for N*(1535)(1/2^-).
- The radially excited nucleon N*(1440)(1/2^+) (with one valence quark in 2S₁/₂) is less bound by 120.35 MeV than the lowest orbital excitations N*(1520)(3/2^-) and N*(1535)(1/2^-) (with last valence quark in 1P₃/₂).
- The pion decay constant is fπ = 93 MeV.
- The Coulomb-type vector potential parameter is α = 0.26 ≈ π/12.
- The three methods for center-of-mass correction agree within 50 MeV for the nucleon ground state.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic chiral quark model originally suggested by Oset (1984) and used for nucleon ground state calculations by Gutsche (1987, 1989), and is related to the Perturbative Chiral Quark Model of Lyubovitskij (2001) and Inoue (2004). The results confirm a prediction of the large Nc limit approach (Schat, 2002) that mass splittings between baryon states receive contributions from operators coupling spin, isospin, and orbital momentum. The model contrasts with the non-relativistic Goldstone Boson Exchange model of Glozman (1996) by generating splitting between negative parity nucleon and Delta states, and suggests that future work should include Instanton-induced exchange or one-gluon exchange forces to resolve the correct ordering of resonances and address the long-standing "three-body spin-orbit puzzle" in baryons.
