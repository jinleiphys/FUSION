# Breakdown of collinear factorization in the exclusive photoproduction of a $ π^{0}γ$ pair with large invariant mass

> We study the exclusive photoproduction of a $ π^{0}γ$ pair with large invariant mass $ M^{2}_{γπ} $, which is sensitive to the exchange of either two quarks or two gluons in the $ t $-channel. In this paper, we show that the process involving two-gluon exchanges does not factorize in the Bjorken limit at the leading twist. This can be explicitly demonstrated by the fact that there exist diagrams, which contribute at the leading twist, for which Glauber gluons are trapped, due to the pinching of the contour integration of both the plus and minus component of the Glauber gluon momentum. For the same reason, $π^0$-nucleon scattering to two photons also suffers from the same issue. On the other hand, we stress that there are no issues with respect to collinear factorization for the quark channels. By considering an analysis of all potential reduced diagrams of leading pinch-singular surfaces, we argue that the quark channel is safe from Glauber pinches, and therefore, a collinear factorization in that case follows through without any problems. This means that processes where gluon exchanges are forbidden, such as the exclusive photoproduction of $ π^{\pm}γ$ and $ ρ^{0,\,\pm} γ$, are unaffected by the factorization breaking effects we point out in this paper.

## Key claim

The exclusive photoproduction of a π⁰γ pair with large invariant mass, when mediated by two-gluon exchange in the t-channel, does not factorize in the Bjorken limit at leading twist, because Glauber gluons become trapped due to pinching of the contour integration of both the plus and minus components of the Glauber gluon momentum; the quark-exchange channels remain safe from this factorization breaking.

## Method

The authors performed an all-order analysis of leading pinch-singular surfaces using the reduced diagram technique of Libby and Sterman, combined with power counting in the hard scale Q and the small parameter λ (the ratio of soft scales to Q). They identified all superficially leading reduced graphs for the process γ + N → γ + π⁰ + N', classified the relevant momentum scalings (collinear, soft, ultrasoft, and Glauber), and examined whether Ward identity cancellations suppress the Glauber region. They then verified the existence of the Glauber pinch explicitly in a specific two-loop Feynman diagram and checked that the soft region is power-suppressed while the Glauber region contributes at leading power.

## Key numbers

* The hard scale Q is defined as Q ∼ √|q'_⊥²| or √|p_{π,⊥}²| in the center-of-mass frame with respect to Δ and q, with the limit Q → ∞ while √|t|, m_π, m_N, Λ_QCD are fixed.
* The small parameter λ is defined as λ ∼ {√|t|, m_π, m_N, Λ_QCD} / Q → 0.
* The Glauber scaling for a momentum k is defined by |k⁺ k⁻| ≪ |k_⊥²|, with specific examples including k ∼ Q (λ², λ², λ) for an n̄-collinear to n-collinear Glauber and k ∼ Q (λ, λ², λ) for an n̄-collinear to soft Glauber.
* The leading reduced diagrams (a) and (b) scale like λ¹ in the power counting.

## Context

This work extends the proof of factorization for 2 → 3 exclusive processes by Qiu and Yu (2022) by identifying a specific counterexample where that proof fails. It contradicts the naive expectation that collinear factorization holds for all such processes at leading twist, and it shows that the breakpoint (cross-over line) singularities, previously harmless in DVCS and DVMP (Collins, 1998; Müller, 2014), can cause a breakdown when Glauber pinches are present. The result implies that processes where two-gluon exchange is forbidden by symmetry, such as exclusive photoproduction of π±γ and ρ⁰,±γ studied by Duplancic (2018, 2022, 2023) and Boussarie (2016), remain factorizable, while π⁰-nucleon scattering to two photons (Qiu, 2022) suffers from the same Glauber pinch problem.
