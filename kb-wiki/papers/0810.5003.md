# Entropy production in 2D $λϕ^4$ theory in the Kadanoff-Baym approach

> We study non-equilibrium quantum dynamics of the single-component scalar field theory in 1+1 space-time dimensions on the basis of the Kadanoff-Baym equation including the next-to-leading-order (NLO) skeleton diagrams. As an extension of the non-relativistic case, we derive relativistic kinetic entropy at the first order in the gradient expansion of the Kadanoff-Baym equations. The derived entropy satisfies the H theorem. Next we perform numerical simulations in spatially homogeneous configurations to investigate thermalization properties of the system by evaluating the system entropy. We find that at later times the kinetic entropy increases approaching the equilibrium value, although the limited time interval in the early stage invalidates the use of it.

## Key claim

The paper derives a relativistic kinetic entropy from the Kadanoff-Baym equations at first order in gradient expansion for 2D λφ⁴ theory, and shows that this entropy satisfies the H-theorem; however, the authors note that the gradient expansion is invalid at very early times, and the limited time interval in the early stage of their numerical simulations prevents reliable use of the entropy there.

## Method

The authors start from the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the two-point function in the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism, using the 2PI effective action truncated to next-to-leading order (NLO) skeleton diagrams (tadpole and sunset). They perform a gradient expansion to first order in the center-of-mass coordinate, derive the retarded propagator in Breit-Wigner form, and construct an entropy current from the difference of the Kadanoff-Baym equations for the Wightman functions. They then numerically solve the Kadanoff-Baym equations in 1+1 dimensions for spatially homogeneous configurations with two different non-thermal initial conditions (tsunami and Woods-Saxon), computing the particle number distribution, spectral function, and the derived entropy.

## Key numbers

- Coupling values used: λ/m² = 4, 2, and 1, with mass m a_s = 0.3
- Lattice size: N=40 grid points (with N=80 check showing no appreciable differences)
- Time step ratio: a_t/a_s = 0.1
- Tsunami initial condition parameters: σ²/m² = 4.4 × (2π/(mL))², p_T = 7 × 2π/L, normalization factor N_T = 0.25
- Woods-Saxon initial condition parameters: p_WS/m = 2.936, κ/m = 0.35, normalization factor N_WS = 0.5
- Total energy conserved within 0.5 percent, with initial total energy ε_tot/m ∼ 260 in the discretization
- Equilibrium temperature from thermalization: T/m ∼ 2.5

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic kinetic entropy formalism of IKV4 (2004) and Kita (2004) to relativistic scalar field theory, building on prior Kadanoff-Baym simulations of λφ⁴ thermalization in 1+1 dimensions by Aarts and Berges (2002) and Berges and Cox (2001). The derived entropy provides a quantitative criterion for how microscopic processes contribute to thermalization, and the numerical results confirm that the system approaches a Bose distribution independently of initial conditions, consistent with earlier findings. The approach enables future study of entropy production in relativistic quantum field theories and may help understand rapid thermalization in heavy ion collisions, though the authors note that defining entropy at higher orders in the skeleton expansion remains an open problem.
