# Spin-isospin resonances: A self-consistent covariant description

> For the first time a fully self-consistent charge-exchange relativistic RPA based on the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approach is established. The self-consistency is verified by the so-called isobaric analog state (IAS) check. The excitation properties and the non-energy weighted sum rules of two important charge-exchange excitation modes, the Gamow-Teller resonance (GTR) and the spin-dipole resonance (SDR), are well reproduced in the doubly magic nuclei $^{48}$Ca, $^{90}$Zr and $^{208}$Pb without readjustment of the particle-hole residual interaction. The dominant contribution of the exchange diagrams is demonstrated.

## Key claim
For the first time, a fully self-consistent charge-exchange relativistic RPA model based on the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approach is established, with self-consistency verified by the isobaric analog state (IAS) check. The pion is included in both the ground-state description and the particle-hole residual interaction, and the zero-range pionic counter-term with strength g'=1/3 is maintained self-consistently without treating it as an adjustable parameter.

## Method
The authors constructed a charge-exchange relativistic RPA model derived from the same Lagrangian as the RHF ground state, using one-meson exchange interactions (sigma, omega, rho, and pi mesons) with finite-range Yukawa propagators and a zero-range pionic counter-term. They applied this RHF+RPA framework to the doubly magic nuclei 48Ca, 90Zr, and 208Pb using three parametrizations (PKO1, PKO2, PKO3) to calculate Gamow-Teller resonance (GTR) and spin-dipole resonance (SDR) excitation properties and sum rules without readjusting the particle-hole residual interaction.

## Key numbers
- IAS degeneracy in 208Pb restored to 4 keV within single-particle energy truncation [-M, M+80 MeV] using PKO1 parametrization; setting g'=0 gives IAS energy of -801 keV, breaking the degeneracy.
- GTR excitation energies: 48Ca at 10.72 MeV (PKO1), 90Zr at 15.80 MeV (PKO1), 208Pb at 18.15 MeV (PKO1); experimental values are approximately 10.5 MeV, 15.6±0.3 MeV, and 19.2±0.2 MeV respectively.
- GTR strengths contain 60-70% of the Ikeda sum rule (3(N-Z)) for all three nuclei across PKO1, PKO2, and PKO3 parametrizations.
- Ikeda sum rule reduction factors from Dirac sea effects: 5.9% for 48Ca (PKO1), 7.0% for 90Zr (PKO1), 7.2% for 208Pb (PKO1).
- SDR sum rule reduction factor for 90Zr is approximately 6.5% across PKO1, PKO2, and PKO3; for 208Pb the reduction factor is about 5.5%.
- Neutron skin thickness for 90Zr: 0.092 fm (PKO1), 0.080 fm (PKO2), 0.079 fm (PKO3); experimental SD value is 0.07±0.04 fm.
- Neutron skin thickness for 208Pb: 0.234 fm (PKO1), 0.194 fm (PKO2), 0.202 fm (PKO3).

## Context
This work extends the relativistic RPA approach beyond the RMF+RPA models of Conti (1998), Vretenar (2003), Ma (2004), and Paar (2004), which lacked full self-consistency because the pion was absent from the ground-state description and the counter-term strength g' had to be treated as an adjustable parameter (approximately 0.6). The present RHF+RPA approach achieves full self-consistency by including the pion in both ground-state and residual interactions, maintaining g'=1/3 as required by the pseudovector coupling. This enables reliable predictions for spin-isospin resonances without re-fitting, and the SDR sum rule provides a method to extract neutron skin thickness, relevant for neutron star and supernova evolution studies as well as the r-process path of stellar nucleosynthesis.
