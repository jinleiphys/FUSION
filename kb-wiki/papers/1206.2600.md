# Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov solution of the pairing Hamiltonian in finite nuclei

> We present an overview of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory of nucleonic superfluidity for finite nuclei. After introducing basic concepts related to pairing correlations, we show how the correlated pairs are incorporated into the HFB wave function. Thereafter, we present derivation and structure of the HFB equations within the superfluid nuclear density functional formalism and discuss several aspects of the theory, including the unitarity of the Bogoliubov transformation in truncated single-particle and quasiparticle spaces, form of the pairing functional, structure of the HFB continuum, regularization and renormalization of pairing fields, and treatment of pairing in systems with odd particle numbers.

## Key claim

This review paper presents an overview of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory of nucleonic superfluidity for finite nuclei, emphasizing that the superfluid wave function represents a fundamentally different phase described by new quasiparticle degrees of freedom, while noting that the correlation energy due to pairing is a small correction to the nuclear binding energy.

## Method

The authors derive the HFB equations within the superfluid nuclear density functional theory (DFT) framework by applying the variational principle to a two-body Hamiltonian using Thouless states as trial wave functions. They discuss the structure of the HFB Hamiltonian and Bogoliubov transformation, the quasiparticle-quasihole symmetry, and the treatment of the Bogoliubov sea in truncated single-particle and quasiparticle spaces. The paper also covers the form of the pairing energy density functional, the structure of the HFB continuum, regularization and renormalization of pairing fields, and the treatment of pairing in systems with odd particle numbers.

## Key numbers

* The pairing correlation P_{μν} for z_μ=1 equals 1/4, meaning it is twice more likely to find a pair of fermions (v²_{μ̃μ}=1/2) than to find these two fermions independently (v²_{μ̃}v²_μ=1/4).
* The bound HFB solutions exist only in the energy region |E_i| ≤ -λ, where λ is the chemical potential (always negative for a particle-bound system).
* The density-dependent pairing force uses ρ₀=0.16 fm⁻³ as the saturation density parameter.
* The pairing interaction parameters η=0, 0.5, and 1 correspond to volume, mixed, and surface pairing, respectively.

## Context

This review extends the traditional BCS theory for electrons (Bardeen, Cooper, and Schrieffer, 1957) and nucleons (Bohr, Mottelson, and Pines, 1958) to the nuclear DFT framework, building on the Bogoliubov-de Gennes formalism (de Gennes, 1966). The work discusses the Bloch-Messiah-Zumino theorem (Bloch and Messiah, 1962; Zumino, 1962) for canonical pairs and addresses practical challenges in HFB calculations such as truncation of the Bogoliubov sea (Dobaczewski et al., 2005; Borzov et al., 2006). The paper enables future studies of pairing in exotic nuclei near the drip lines, including the pairing-antihalo effect (Bennaceur et al., 1999, 2000; Yamagami et al., 2005; Rotival and Duguet, 2009), and provides the theoretical foundation for global surveys of pairing strength (Bertsch et al., 2009).
