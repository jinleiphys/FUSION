# Thermal analysis of particle ratios and p_T spectra at RHIC

> The thermal model of particle production is used to analyze the particle ratios and the p_T spectra measured recently at RHIC. Our fit of the particle ratios yields the temperature at the chemical freeze-out T_chem = 165 +- 7 MeV with the corresponding baryon chemical potential mu^B_chem = 41 +- 5 MeV. The quality of the fit shows that the model works well for RHIC. The p_T spectra are evaluated in an approach which takes into account the modifications of the initial thermal distributions by the secondary decays of resonances. All two- and three-body decays are included. This leads to an effective ``cooling'' of the spectra in the data region by about 35-40 MeV. We find that the pion spectrum is characterized by the inverse slope which agrees well with the value inferred from the RHIC data.

## Key claim

The thermal model of particle production, including all two- and three-body resonance decays, successfully describes particle ratios at RHIC with a chemical freeze-out temperature of 165 ± 7 MeV, but the static fireball model without expansion or flow cannot reproduce the experimentally observed convex shape of the pion transverse-momentum spectrum.

## Method

The authors fit particle ratios measured at RHIC using an ideal-gas thermal model with chemical potentials for baryon number, strangeness, and isospin, minimizing χ² over 16 data points. For the transverse-momentum spectra, they computed the primordial thermal distributions and then added contributions from all two- and three-body resonance decays using an analytic Lorentz-covariant formula, starting from the heaviest resonances and proceeding downward in mass.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out temperature: T_chem = 165 ± 7 MeV
- Baryon chemical potential: μ^B_chem = 41 ± 5 MeV
- χ²/n for the particle ratio fit: 0.97
- Energy density at freeze-out: ε = 0.5 GeV/fm³
- Pressure at freeze-out: P = 0.08 GeV/fm³
- Baryon density at freeze-out: ρ_B = 0.02 fm⁻³
- Average energy per hadron at freeze-out: ⟨E⟩/⟨N⟩ = 1.0 GeV
- Average energy per baryon: ⟨E_B⟩/⟨N_B⟩ = 1.6 GeV
- Average energy per meson: ⟨E_M⟩/⟨N_M⟩ = 0.9 GeV
- Pion inverse slope before decays (0.3-0.9 GeV p⊥ range): 219 MeV
- Pion inverse slope after all decays: 185 MeV (cooling of 34 MeV)
- π⁻/π⁺ ratio: thermal model 1.02, experiment 1.00 ± 0.02 (PHOBOS) and 0.99 ± 0.02 (Bearden)
- p̄/π⁻ ratio: thermal model 0.09, experiment 0.08 ± 0.01 (Harris)
- K⁻/K⁺ ratio: thermal model 0.92, experiment ranges from 0.78 ± 0.12 to 0.92 ± 0.06
- K⁻/π⁻ ratio: thermal model 0.16, experiment 0.15 ± 0.02 (Caines)
- p̄/p ratio: thermal model 0.65, experiment ranges from 0.54 ± 0.08 to 0.61 ± 0.07
- Λ̄/Λ ratio: thermal model 0.69, experiment 0.73 ± 0.03 (Caines)
- Ξ̄/Ξ ratio: thermal model 0.76, experiment 0.82 ± 0.08 (Caines)

## Context

This work extends the thermal model analyses previously applied at AGS and SPS energies to the new RHIC data, confirming the Cleymans-Redlich conjecture (2000) that the energy per hadron at chemical freeze-out is 1 GeV. The authors find T_chem = 165 MeV, which is 9 MeV lower than the value of 174 MeV reported by Braun-Munzinger et al. (2001) and 25 MeV lower than the 190 MeV obtained by Xu and Kaneta (2001), though all results are consistent within errors. The analysis of resonance decay contributions to the p⊥ spectra is introductory to the authors' subsequent work (Florkowski and Broniowski, 2002) where expansion effects are incorporated to achieve full agreement with experimental data.
