# Improved limits on interactions of low-mass spin-0 dark matter from atomic clock spectroscopy

> Low-mass (sub-eV) spin-0 dark matter particles, which form a coherently oscillating classical field $ϕ= ϕ_0 \cos(m_ϕt)$, can induce oscillating variations in the fundamental constants through their interactions with the Standard Model sector. We calculate the effects of such possible interactions, which may include the linear interaction of $ϕ$ with the Higgs boson, on atomic and molecular transitions. Using recent atomic clock spectroscopy measurements, we derive new limits on the linear interaction of $ϕ$ with the Higgs boson, as well as its quadratic interactions with the photon and light quarks. For the linear interaction of $ϕ$ with the Higgs boson, our derived limits improve on existing constraints by up to $2-3$ orders of magnitude.

## Key claim

The paper derives new limits on the linear interaction of a low-mass spin-0 dark matter field with the Higgs boson, as well as its quadratic interactions with the photon and light quarks, using atomic clock spectroscopy data; the improvement over existing constraints for the Higgs interaction is up to 2-3 orders of magnitude, but only under the assumption that the dark matter field saturates the local galactic cold dark matter density.

## Method

The authors calculated how oscillating variations in fundamental constants (fermion masses, fine-structure constant, and QCD scale) induced by a coherently oscillating classical dark matter field affect atomic and molecular transition frequencies, using sensitivity coefficients derived from relativistic atomic theory and nuclear models. They then used experimental data from dysprosium (Dy) spectroscopy (Budker 2015) and rubidium/cesium (Rb/Cs) atomic clock comparisons (Bize 2016) to set limits on the interaction parameters appearing in the Lagrangian for linear and quadratic couplings of the spin-0 field to Standard Model fields.

## Key numbers

- Most sensitive limit on the Higgs interaction parameter A from Dy data: A less than or equal to 1.4 x 10^{-10} eV for dark matter mass m_phi approximately 6 x 10^{-23} eV
- Most sensitive limit on A from Rb/Cs data: A less than or equal to 4 x 10^{-13} eV for m_phi approximately 1.4 x 10^{-23} eV
- Most sensitive limit on the quadratic photon interaction scale Lambda'_gamma from Rb/Cs: Lambda'_gamma greater than or equal to 2 x 10^{19} GeV for m_phi less than or equal to 7 x 10^{-24} eV
- Most sensitive limit on the quadratic light quark interaction scale hat(Lambda')_q from Rb/Cs: hat(Lambda')_q greater than or equal to 4 x 10^{18} GeV for m_phi less than or equal to 7 x 10^{-24} eV
- The Rb/Cs bound on Lambda'_gamma improves on existing limits from Dy spectroscopy (Lambda'_gamma greater than or equal to 3 x 10^{18} GeV for m_phi less than or equal to 3 x 10^{-23} eV) by up to a factor of 6

## Context

This work extends the approach of Stadnik and Flambaum (2015) and Arvanitaki et al. (2015) for using atomic spectroscopy to search for oscillating variations in fundamental constants due to dark matter. The new limits on the Higgs interaction improve on constraints from fifth-force searches (Pospelov 2010), while the quadratic photon limits improve on earlier Dy spectroscopy limits (Stadnik 2015) and Big Bang nucleosynthesis constraints (Stadnik 2015). The results enable further searches using other existing atomic clock data (Al+/Hg+, Yb+/Cs, Yb+(E3)/Yb+(E2), Sr/Yb/Hg) and suggest that atomic optical/hyperfine frequency ratio comparisons and certain molecular transitions offer particularly sensitive platforms for detecting the Higgs interaction.
