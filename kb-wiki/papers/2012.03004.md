# Quasinormal modes of scalar field coupled to Einstein's tensor in the non-commutative geometry inspired black hole

> We investigate the quasinormal modes (QNMs) of the scalar field coupled to the Einstein's tensor in the non-commutative geometry inspired black hole spacetime. It is found that the lapse function of the non-commutative black hole metric can be represented by a Kummer's confluent hypergeometric function, which can effectively solve the problem that the numerical results of the QNMs are sensitive to the model parameters and make the QNMs values more reliable. We make a careful analysis of the scalar QNM frequencies by using several numerical methods, and find that the numerical results obtained by the new WKB method (the Padé approximants) and the Mashhoon method (P$\ddot{\text{o}}$schl-Teller potential method) are quite different from those obtained by the asymptotic iterative method (AIM) and time-domain integration method when the non-commutative parameter $θ$ and coupling parameter $η$ are large. The most obvious difference is that the numerical results obtained by the AIM and the time-domain integration method appear a critical value $η_c$ with an increase of $η$, which leads to the dynamical instability. After carefully analyzing the numeral results, we conclude that the numerical results obtained by the AIM and the time-domain integration method are closer to the theoretical values than those obtained by the WKB method and the Mashhoon method, when the $θ$ and $η$ are large. Moreover, through a numerical fitting, we obtain that the functional relationship between the threshold $η_c$ and the non-commutative parameter $θ$ satisfies $η_{c}=aθ^{b}+c$ for a fixed $l$ approximately. We find that the stability of dynamics can be ensured in the $η<η_c(θ, l)$ region.

## Key claim

The quasinormal mode frequencies of a scalar field coupled to Einstein's tensor in a non-commutative geometry inspired black hole spacetime are reliably computed only when using the asymptotic iterative method (AIM) or time-domain integration, because the WKB method (even with Padé approximants) and the Mashhoon method produce results that deviate significantly from theoretical values when the non-commutative parameter θ and the coupling parameter η are large; the AIM and time-domain methods reveal a critical coupling value η_c beyond which dynamical instability occurs.

## Method

The authors derived the Schrödinger-like wave equation for a massless scalar field non-minimally coupled to Einstein's tensor via the term ±η G^{μν} in a static spherically symmetric non-commutative black hole background, using the negative coupling potential V(r)_- to avoid singularities. They computed quasinormal mode frequencies by four numerical methods: the WKB method with Padé approximants (up to 13th order), the asymptotic iterative method (AIM), the Mashhoon method (Pöschl-Teller potential approximation), and time-domain integration, for various values of θ and η at fixed multipole number l=3 and overtone n=0.

## Key numbers

- For θ=0.02, l=3, n=0, η=0: the 6th WKB frequency is 0.675366 − 0.0964997i, the 8th WKB frequency is 0.675366 − 0.0964997i, and the AIM frequency is 0.675366 − 0.0964996i (all agreeing to six significant digits).
- For θ=0.02, l=3, n=0, η=100: the 6th WKB frequency is 0.675366 − 0.0964997i, the 8th WKB frequency is 0.675366 − 0.0964996i, and the AIM frequency is 0.675366 − 0.0964996i (showing no change with η at small θ).
- For θ=0.2, l=3, n=0, η=1: the 6th WKB frequency is 0.674385 − 0.099339i, and the 7th WKB frequency is 0.674401 − 0.098854i.
- For θ=0.2, l=3, n=0, η=3: the 6th WKB frequency is 0.674157 − 0.104089i, and the 7th WKB frequency is not fully shown in the truncated text.
- For θ=0.2, l=3, n=0, η=5: the 6th WKB frequency is 0.675310 − 0.108057i.
- The functional relationship between the threshold η_c and the non-commutative parameter θ for fixed l is approximately η_c = a θ^b + c (specific fitted values of a, b, c are not given in the truncated text).
- The range of non-commutative parameters for which the black hole has an event horizon (with M=1) is 0 < θ ≲ 0.275811.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quasinormal modes for scalar fields coupled to Einstein's tensor in Reissner-Nordström black holes (Chen 2010, Konoplya 2018) and in Reissner-Nordström-AdS black holes (Abdalla 2019) to the non-commutative geometry inspired black hole background. It also addresses the known issue that WKB method results for non-commutative black holes are non-convergent at higher orders (Das 2018, Batic 2019), and it enables reliable computation of the dynamical instability threshold η_c as a function of the continuous non-commutative parameter θ rather than the discrete multipole number l.
