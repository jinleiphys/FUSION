# New Insight about the Effective Restoration of U_A(1) Symmetry

> The effective restoration of the U_{A}(1) symmetry is revisited by implementing the functional renormalization group approach combining with the 2+1 flavor Polyakov-loop quark-meson model. A temperature-dependent 't Hooft term is taken to imitate the restoration of the U_{A}(1) symmetry. Order parameters, meson spectrum and mixing angles, especially the pressure and the entropy density of the system are calculated to explore the effects of different U_{A}(1) symmetry restoration patterns. We show then that the temperature for the restoration of the U_{A}(1) symmetry is much higher than that for the chiral symmetry SU_{A}(3).

## Key claim

The temperature for the effective restoration of the U_A(1) symmetry is much higher than that for the chiral symmetry SU_A(3), and the U_A(1) symmetry remains broken when the chiral phase transition occurs; this conclusion is drawn from the observation that unphysical negative pressure and entropy density appear if the U_A(1) symmetry is restored before the chiral crossover is completed.

## Method

The authors employ the functional renormalization group (FRG) approach combined with the 2+1 flavor Polyakov-loop quark-meson (PQM) model, using the local potential approximation (LPA). A temperature-dependent 't Hooft term is parameterized to imitate the restoration of the U_A(1) symmetry, with the starting restoration temperature T_r and a width parameter b controlling the decay of the anomaly strength c_a(T). The flow equation for the effective potential is solved numerically by the Taylor method, and order parameters, meson spectrum, mixing angles, pressure, and entropy density are computed for different values of T_r.

## Key numbers

- The chiral pseudo-critical temperature T_c^χ = 208 MeV when the anomaly strength c_a is kept constant.
- For T_r = 150 MeV, the chiral pseudo-critical temperature is lowered to T_c^χ = 177 MeV.
- For T_r = 200 MeV and T_r = 250 MeV, the chiral pseudo-critical temperature is nearly unchanged compared to the constant c_a case.
- The parameter b is set to 50 MeV; different choices of b do not induce much difference.
- The ideal mixing angle for the pseudo-scalar mesons is 35° when the 't Hooft term is constant, and -55° when the U_A(1) symmetry is restored.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of U_A(1) symmetry restoration using effective models such as the linear sigma model, NJL model, and quark-meson model (Bielich 2000, Costa 2004, Schaefer 2009, Fukushima 2001) by employing the FRG approach which goes beyond mean-field approximation and incorporates meson fluctuations. The results are consistent with lattice QCD simulations (Bhattacharya 2014) showing that the (a_0, π) degeneracy occurs later than the (σ, π) degeneracy. The new insight is that thermodynamic quantities (pressure and entropy density) provide a constraint: the U_A(1) symmetry must remain broken until a temperature much higher than T_c^χ to avoid unphysical negative pressure, which enables future investigations of the Columbia plot in the chiral limit.
