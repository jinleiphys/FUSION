# Nuclear chiral dynamics and phases of QCD

> This presentation starts with a brief review of our current picture of QCD phases, derived from lattice QCD thermodynamics and from models based on the symmetries and symmetry breaking patterns of QCD. Typical approaches widely used in this context are the PNJL and chiral quark-meson models. It is pointed out, however, that the modeling of the phase diagram in terms of quarks as quasiparticles misses important and well known nuclear physics constraints. In the hadronic phase of QCD governed by confinement and spontaneously broken chiral symmetry, in-medium chiral effective field theory is the appropriate framework, with pions and nucleons as active degrees of freedom. Nuclear chiral thermodynamics is outlined and the liquid-gas phase transition is described. The density and temperature dependence of the chiral condensate is deduced. As a consequence of two- and three-body correlations in the nuclear medium, no tendency towards a first-order chiral phase transition is found at least up to twice the baryon density of normal nuclear matter and up to temperatures of about 100 MeV. Isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter are also discussed. An outlook is given on new tightened constraints for the equation-of-state of cold and highly compressed matter as implied by a recently observed two-solar-mass neutron star.

## Key claim

In-medium chiral effective field theory, applied to nuclear matter with pions and nucleons as active degrees of freedom, finds no tendency towards a first-order chiral phase transition at least up to twice the baryon density of normal nuclear matter and up to temperatures of about 100 MeV, contradicting the generic first-order transition line that quark-based models (such as PNJL and Polyakov-quark-meson models) predict in this region.

## Method

The free energy density of nuclear matter was computed using in-medium chiral perturbation theory, with the in-medium nucleon propagator incorporating the filled Fermi sea via a medium insertion. The calculation included one- and two-pion exchange processes up to three-loop order in the energy density, with intermediate virtual Delta(1232) isobar excitations and resulting three-body terms, while short-distance dynamics were encoded in a limited set of NN contact terms fitted to reproduce the binding energy per nucleon in equilibrium nuclear matter at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition in symmetric (N=Z) matter: T_c approximately 15 MeV, close to the range of empirical values.
- Equilibrium baryon density at T=0: rho_0 approximately 0.16 fm^{-3}.
- Nuclear compression modulus: kappa = 260 +/- 30 MeV, about twice the pion mass.
- Binding energy per nucleon in equilibrium nuclear matter: |E|/A approximately 16 MeV.
- Fermi momentum of symmetric nuclear matter: p_F approximately 1.36 fm^{-1}, corresponding to about 0.27 GeV.
- The first-order liquid-gas phase transition ceases to exist at a proton fraction Z/A = 0.05, below which neutron matter behaves as an interacting Fermi gas.
- The chiral expansion is trusted up to p_F less than 0.3 GeV, well below the chiral symmetry breaking scale 4*pi*f_pi approximately 1 GeV (with f_pi = 0.09 GeV).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous review (Weise 2010) and directly contradicts the phase diagrams produced by PNJL models (Fukushima 2003, Ratti et al. 2006) and Polyakov-quark-meson models (Schaefer et al. 2007, Herbst et al. 2011), which predict a first-order chiral transition at baryon chemical potentials around the nucleon mass. The approach builds on chiral effective field theory for the nucleon-nucleon interaction (Lutz et al. 2000, Kaiser et al. 2002, Fritsch et al. 2005) and enables a controlled description of nuclear thermodynamics up to twice saturation density, providing constraints for the equation of state of neutron stars.
