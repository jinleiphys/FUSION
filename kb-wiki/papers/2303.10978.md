# Taming Dyson-Schwinger equations with null states

> In quantum field theory, the Dyson-Schwinger equations are an infinite set of coupled equations relating $n$-point Green's functions in a self-consistent manner. They have found important applications in non-perturbative studies, ranging from quantum chromodynamics and hadron physics to strongly correlated electron systems. However, they are notoriously formidable to solve. One of the main problems is that a finite truncation of the infinite system is underdetermined. Recently, Bender et al. [Phys. Rev. Lett. 130, 101602 (2023)] proposed to make use of the large-$n$ asymptotic behaviors and successfully obtained accurate results in $D=0$ spacetime. At higher $D$, it seems more difficult to deduce the large-$n$ behaviors. In this paper, we propose another avenue in light of the null bootstrap. The underdetermined system is solved by imposing the null state condition. This approach can be extended to $D>0$ more readily. As concrete examples, we show that the cases of $D=0$ and $D=1$ indeed converge to the exact results for several Hermitian and non-Hermitian theories of the $gϕ^n$ type, including the complex solutions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the underdetermined Dyson-Schwinger equations can be solved by imposing a null state condition, which can be extended to higher spacetime dimensions more readily than the large-\(n\) asymptotic method of Bender et al. (2023), and demonstrates convergence to exact results for several Hermitian and non-Hermitian \(g\phi^n\) theories at \(D=0\) and \(D=1\).

## Method

The author truncates the infinite Dyson-Schwinger equations to a finite system and imposes the null state condition, which requires that a constructed null operator be orthogonal to a set of test operators. For \(D=0\) theories, null and test operators are polynomials in \(\phi\); for \(D=1\) theories, they involve derivatives of \(\phi\). The resulting polynomial equations are solved for the Green's functions, and the best estimate is extracted either from the median of a dense group of roots or by requiring that the null polynomial's roots (interpreted as energy gaps) be positive.

## Key numbers

- For the \(D=0\) Hermitian \(\phi^4\) theory, the exact two-point Green's function is \(G_2 = \pm 0.675978240\ldots\); at \(K=19\) the median reproduces \(0.6759782403\ldots\).
- For the \(D=0\) non-Hermitian \(i\phi^3\) theory, the exact one-point function is \(G_1 = -0.729011133\ldots e^{i2k\pi/3} i\); at \(K=10\) the median gives \(-0.729011134\ldots i\).
- For the \(D=0\) sextic \(\phi^6\) theory, at \(K=10\) the median gives \(G_2 = 0.57861625\ldots\), close to the exact value \(6^{1/3}\sqrt\pi/\Gamma(1/6) = 0.57861652\ldots\).
- For the \(D=0\) quartic theory without parity symmetry, at \(K=10\) the median of the \((-i)\) group gives \(-0.977741049\ldots i\), close to the exact \(-0.977741067\ldots i\).
- For the \(D=1\) Hermitian quartic theory with \(m=1\) and \(g=1/2\), at \(K=6\) the ground-state expectation \(\langle\phi^2\rangle = 0.305813644\ldots\) and energy gaps \(\{1.628230589\ldots, 5.882239\ldots, 10.9536\ldots, 16.661\ldots, 23.3\ldots, 32.5\ldots\}\) are obtained; the exact values are \(0.3058136507\ldots\) and \(\{1.628230531\ldots, 5.882226\ldots, 10.9525\ldots, 16.624\ldots, 22.8\ldots, 29.4\ldots\}\).
- For the \(D=1\) non-Hermitian cubic theory with \(g=i/2\), at \(K=6\) the one-point function is \(\langle\phi\rangle = -0.590072522\ldots i\) and energy gaps \(\{1.4764812\ldots, 3.202970\ldots, 5.081\ldots, 7.008\ldots, 9.45\ldots \pm 1.05\ldots i\}\); the exact values are \(-0.590072533\ldots i\) and \(\{1.4764809\ldots, 3.202996\ldots, 5.079\ldots, 7.059\ldots, 9.16\ldots, 10.09\ldots\}\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Bender et al. (2023), who used large-\(n\) asymptotic behaviors to close the Dyson-Schwinger equations at \(D=0\) but found extension to higher dimensions challenging. The null state condition is inspired by the null bootstrap of Li (2022) and parallels the role of null states in 2D minimal model conformal field theories as in Belavin, Polyakov, and Zamolodchikov (1984). The method enables reconstruction of time-dependent two-point Green's functions and spectral information, and the author suggests it may provide new insights for truncation approaches to the conformal bootstrap as initiated by Gliozzi (2013).
