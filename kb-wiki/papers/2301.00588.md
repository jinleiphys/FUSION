# Hydrodynamic model of heavy-ion collisions with low momentum components

> Relativistic heavy-ion collisions suggest that low momentum regions of the observed particle spectra are thermal and hydrodynamic, while medium-high momentum regions are non-thermal and perturbative. In this study, I construct a hydrodynamic model of heavy-ion collisions by cutting off the medium-high momentum contributions and investigate the phenomenological consequences. Numerical simulations indicate that the temperature of the quark matter can be higher at earlier times owing to the modification of the equation of state. It is also suggested that direct photon elliptic flow can be sensitive to the momentum dependence of thermalization.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a hydrodynamic model of heavy-ion collisions where only low-momentum particles (below a cutoff momentum \(p_c\)) are assumed to be thermalized, showing that while the temperature of quark matter can be higher at early times due to the modified equation of state, the radial flow development is mostly insensitive to the cutoff; direct photon elliptic flow \(v_2(p_T)\) is suggested to be sensitive to the momentum dependence of thermalization, but the effect on direct photon \(p_T\) spectra is minimal.

## Method

The author develops a (2+1)-dimensional inviscid hydrodynamic model where the equation of state is constructed using hadron resonance gas and parton gas models with an upper momentum cutoff \(p_c\) on constituent particles, ranging from 2 to 5 GeV. The grand-canonical partition function is integrated only up to \(p_c\), and the hadronic and QGP pressures are connected with an exponential damping function. Thermal photon emission rates in the QGP phase are truncated with a hyperbolic factor \(h(p)\), and non-thermal photons from medium-high momentum components are estimated as the difference between the thermal rate at infinite \(p_c\) and that at finite \(p_c\), assumed to have zero azimuthal anisotropy. Event-averaged initial conditions for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV Pb+Pb collisions at impact parameter 4.6 fm are used, with initial time \(\tau_\mathrm{hyd} = 0.4\) fm/\(c\) and initial energy density \(e_0 = 126.6\) GeV/fm\(^3\).

## Key numbers

* The initial energy density at the center of the medium is \(e_0 = 126.6\) GeV/fm\(^3\) for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV Pb+Pb collisions at impact parameter 4.6 fm.
* The initial time is set to \(\tau_\mathrm{hyd} = 0.4\) fm/\(c\) and the kinetic freeze-out temperature is \(T_f = 140\) MeV.
* The equation of state with momentum cutoff differs from lattice QCD data by within 10% for the temperature range \(0.13 \leq T \leq 0.4\) TeV when \(T_c = 0.14\) GeV in the limit \(p_c \to \infty\).
* The effects of the momentum cutoff on pressure and temperature are mostly negligible above \(p_c \sim 4\) GeV, and results converge around \(\tau \sim 1\) fm/\(c\) and \(T \sim 0.3\) GeV.
* The effective sound velocity \(c_1\) from fitting the equation of state increases as \(p_c\) is lowered, ranging from 0.300 (no cutoff) to 0.506 (for \(p_c = 2\) GeV).

## Context

This work extends the standard relativistic hydrodynamic description of heavy-ion collisions, which typically assumes thermalization at all momenta, by introducing a momentum cutoff motivated by the observation that low momentum particles below 2-4 GeV are thermalized while medium-high momentum regions are described by perturbative QCD (Eskola 2002, 2004, 2005). The model addresses the "photon puzzle" (PHENIX 2011, 2015; ALICE 2018), where direct photon spectra and flow harmonics are not completely described by hydrodynamic models. The results suggest that direct photon \(v_2\) may be an observable sensitive to the momentum dependence of thermalization, and future work could include viscosity, event-by-event fluctuations, and coupling with transport models for the high-momentum sector.
