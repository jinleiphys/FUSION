# Universality of the Collins-Soper kernel in lattice calculations

> The Collins-Soper (CS) kernel is a nonperturbative function that characterizes the rapidity evolution of transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions (TMDPDFs) and wave functions. In this Letter, we calculate the CS kernel for pion and proton targets and for quasi-TMDPDFs of leading and next-to-leading power. The calculations are carried out on the CLS ensemble H101 with dynamical $N_f=2+1$ clover-improved Wilson fermions. Our analyses demonstrate the consistency of different lattice extractions of the CS kernel for mesons and baryons, as well as for twist-two and twist-three operators, even though lattice artifacts could be significant. This consistency corroborates the universality of the lattice-determined CS kernel and suggests that a high-precision determination of it is in reach.

## Key claim

The Collins-Soper (CS) kernel extracted from lattice calculations of pion and proton quasi-TMDPDFs, using both leading-power (twist-2) and next-to-leading-power (twist-3) operators, are consistent with each other, corroborating the universality of the CS kernel even though lattice artifacts could be significant.

## Method

The CS kernel was extracted from the ratio of the first Mellin moments of quasi-TMDPDFs computed on the CLS ensemble H101 (Nf=2+1 clover-improved Wilson fermions, a=0.0854 fm, m_pi=422 MeV) at two different hadron momenta. The matrix elements were calculated using the sequential source method with HYP smearing and momentum smearing, for both pion and proton targets, using twist-2 (f1) and twist-3 (e) operators. The CS kernel was obtained from the ratio R = (P1+/P2+)^K(b,mu) * r(b,mu;P1,P2), where r is computed at NLO in perturbation theory, with the constant M determined by normalizing to the phenomenological SV19 extraction at b0=3a=0.26 fm.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacing a = 0.0854 fm, with lattice volume 32^3 x 96, and sea pion mass m_pi = 422 MeV
* Pion valence quark mass corresponds to m_pi^val = 686 MeV
* Hadron momenta used: P_x = {2,3} x 2pi/(a N_sigma), corresponding to P+/P- ratios of 0.13 (pion) and 0.25 (proton)
* Normalization point b0 = 3a = 0.26 fm = 1.3 GeV^{-1}
* Extracted M values: -0.83(0.73) for f1(pion), -4.98(0.61) for e(pion), -0.57(0.34) for f1(proton), -1.04(1.32) for e(proton)
* Resulting correlated uncertainty delta K: 0.15 (f1 pion), 0.10 (e pion), 0.08 (f1 proton), 0.27 (e proton)
* Valid region for extracted CS kernel: b > 0.15 fm (from power correction condition), with main results in intermediate region 0.8 GeV^{-1} < b < 2.6 GeV^{-1}

## Context

This work extends previous lattice extractions of the CS kernel from unpolarized quasi-TMDPDFs of the proton (Ebert 2018, LatticeParton 2020, Schlemmer 2021, Li 2021, LPC 2022) by providing the first comparison across hadron types (pion vs proton) and operator twists (twist-2 vs twist-3). The results confirm the theoretical prediction that the CS kernel is independent of hadron and twist (Vladimirov 2021, Ebert 2021, Rodini 2022) and enable future high-precision determinations of this fundamental nonperturbative function, which is essential for TMD phenomenology and for testing the universality assumptions underlying factorization theorems.
