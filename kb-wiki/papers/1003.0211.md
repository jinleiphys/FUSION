# A Theoretical Determination of N_{nn}/N_{np} in Hypernuclear Non--Mesonic Weak Decay

> The ratio N_{nn}/N_{np} between the number of neutron-neutron and neutron--proton pairs emitted in the non--mesonic weak decay of Λ-hypernuclei is calculated within a nuclear matter formalism extended to ^{12}_{Lambda}C via the local density approximation. The single--nucleon emission spectra, N_p and N_n, are also evaluated. Our formalism takes care of both ground state correlations (gsc) and final state interactions (FSI). The evaluation of N_{nn}/N_{np} which, unlike Gamma_n/Gamma_p = Gamma(Lambda n -> nn)/Gamma(Lambda p -> np), is an actual observable quantity in non--mesonic decay is performed within a fully microscopic model where a proper treatment of FSI, gsc and ground state normalization is considered. All the isospin channels contributing to one-- and two--nucleon induced decays are included. Our final result for the coincidence number ratio, N_{nn}/N_{np}=0.374, is in agreement with the KEK--E508 datum, (N_{nn}/N_{np})^{\rm exp}=0.40 +/- 0.10.

## Key claim

The ratio \(N_{nn}/N_{np}=0.374\) for \({}^{12}_{\Lambda}\mathrm{C}\) is calculated using a nuclear matter formalism extended via the local density approximation, and this value agrees with the KEK-E508 experimental datum of \(0.40\pm 0.10\) only when final state interactions (FSI) and ground state correlations (gsc) are included; without FSI the ratio would be \(0.321\) (equal to \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p\)).

## Method

The calculation employs a nuclear matter formalism extended to \({}^{12}_{\Lambda}\mathrm{C}\) via the local density approximation. The weak transition potential \(V^{\Lambda N \to NN}\) is represented by exchange of \(\pi\), \(\eta\), \(K\), \(\rho\), \(\omega\), and \(K^*\) mesons with coupling constants from the Nijmegen soft-core interaction NSC97f, while the residual strong interaction \(V^{NN}\) uses a \(\pi+\rho\) potential with a Landau-Migdal parameter \(g'=0.7\). The model includes all isospin channels for one- and two-nucleon induced decays and accounts for FSI and gsc through a set of Feynman diagrams; the resulting observables \(N_{nn}\), \(N_{np}\), \(N_n\), and \(N_p\) are computed from the decay widths and FSI contributions.

## Key numbers

- \(N_{nn}/N_{np} = 0.374\) for \({}^{12}_{\Lambda}\mathrm{C}\) with FSI and gsc included, using a nucleon kinetic energy detection threshold \(T^{\rm th}_N = 30\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and opening angle condition \(\cos(\theta_{NN}) \leqslant -0.8\).
- Experimental value from KEK-E508: \((N_{nn}/N_{np})^{\rm exp} = 0.40 \pm 0.10\) under the same kinematic cuts.
- Without FSI, the ratio equals \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p = 0.321\) (for \(T^{\rm th}_N = 0\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(\cos(\theta_{NN}) \leqslant 1.0\)).
- Without quantum interference terms (but with FSI), \((N_{nn}/N_{np})^{\rm no-int} = 0.376\) for \(T^{\rm th}_N = 30\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(\cos(\theta_{NN}) \leqslant -0.8\).
- Partial contributions to \(N_{nn}\) and \(N_{np}\) are tabulated for various FSI diagram types (e.g., \(pp\), \(ph\), \(hh\), \(pp'\), \(ph'\), \(hh'\)) at two kinematic conditions.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Bauer (2007) to compute the observable coincidence number ratio \(N_{nn}/N_{np}\), which is distinct from the unobservable partial width ratio \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p\). The result agrees with the KEK-E508 experiment (Kim 2006; Okada 2004) and demonstrates that FSI are essential for reproducing the data, while quantum interference terms have a small net effect. The approach enables future improvements by incorporating a more realistic residual strong interaction, Pauli exchange terms, and the effect of the \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance, and it sets constraints on both the weak transition potential \(V^{\Lambda N \to NN}\) and the strong interaction \(V^{NN}\).
