# Three-Nucleon Bound State in a Spin-Isospin Dependent Three Dimensional Approach

> A spin-isospin dependent Three-Dimensional approach based on momentum vectors for formulation of the three-nucleon bound state is presented in this paper. The three-nucleon Faddeev equations with two-nucleon interactions are formulated as a function of vector Jacobi momenta, specifically the magnitudes of the momenta and the angle between them with the inclusion of the spin-isospin quantum numbers, without employing a partial wave decomposition. As an application the spin-isospin dependent Faddeev integral equations are solved with Bonn-B potential. Our result for the Triton binding energy with the value of -8.152 MeV is in good agreement with the achievements of the other partial wave based methods.

## Key claim

The paper presents a three-dimensional (3D) formulation of the three-nucleon Faddeev equations that includes spin and isospin degrees of freedom without using a partial wave decomposition; the authors claim that their result for the Triton binding energy of -8.152 MeV using the Bonn-B potential is in good agreement with partial-wave-based methods, but this agreement is conditional on the specific choice of potential and the particular set of spin-isospin channels included.

## Method

The authors formulated the three-nucleon Faddeev equations for the bound state as a function of the magnitudes of the two Jacobi momenta and the angle between them, incorporating spin-isospin quantum numbers via Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. They derived a set of coupled three-dimensional integral equations for the Faddeev component and solved them numerically for the Triton using the Bonn-B two-nucleon potential, with the two-body t-matrix computed in a helicity representation. The total three-nucleon wave function was then constructed from the Faddeev component using permutation operators and spin-isospin recoupling coefficients.

## Key numbers

* Triton binding energy: -8.152 MeV, calculated with the Bonn-B potential in the 3D spin-isospin dependent approach.
* The number of coupled Faddeev equations in the 3D formalism can be 8, 12, 16, or 24, depending on which spin-isospin states (S-T combinations) are included.
* For comparison, the number of partial wave channels needed for convergence in the PW approach ranges from 5 (for j12max=1) to 42 (for j12max=5), with total isospin restricted to T=1/2.

## Context

This work extends the earlier 3D Faddeev formalism for three identical bosons (Elster, 2000; Hadizadeh, 2006) to include spin and isospin degrees of freedom, making it applicable to real three-nucleon systems like the Triton and Helium-3. The approach avoids the complex angular momentum algebra required in traditional partial wave decomposition methods (such as those used by Sammarruca, 1992 and Fachruddin, 2004) and provides a finite number of coupled equations that can be solved directly in momentum vector variables. This formalism enables future inclusion of three-nucleon forces in a more straightforward manner than in partial wave approaches.
