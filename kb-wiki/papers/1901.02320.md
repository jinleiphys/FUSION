# Solar neutrino problem as evidence of new interaction

> A new concept is proposed to solve the solar neutrino problem, that is based on a hypothesis about the existence of a new interaction of electron neutrinos with nucleons mediated by massless pseudoscalar bosons. At every collision of a neutrino with nucleons of the Sun, its handedness changes from left to right and vice versa, and its energy decreases. The postulated hypothesis, having only one free parameter, provides a good agreement between the calculated and experimental characteristics of all five observed processes with solar neutrinos.

# Solar neutrino problem as evidence of new interaction

## Key claim

The discrepancy between standard solar model predictions and experimental rates for all five observed solar neutrino processes can be explained by a new "semi-weak" interaction of electron neutrinos with nucleons, mediated by massless pseudoscalar bosons, using only one free parameter (the effective number of neutrino-nucleon collisions). The author claims that no model or hypothesis in the last 40 years has given good agreement with results of three, four, or five completely different experiments with only one free parameter.

## Method

The author postulates a Lagrangian describing a massless pseudoscalar boson that couples to electron neutrinos and nucleons (with opposite coupling constants for protons and neutrons) but not to electrons at tree level. Using this interaction, the author calculates that each neutrino-nucleon collision flips the neutrino handedness and reduces its energy according to Δω/ω ≈ ω/M. The energy distribution of solar neutrinos after a fixed effective number of collisions n₀ (the single free parameter) is computed using a binomial distribution over the allowed kinematic energy interval, and these modified spectra are folded with cross sections for each detection process to predict event rates.

## Key numbers

- The product of coupling constants g_{νₑps}g_{Nps}/4π is smaller by three orders than the electromagnetic and weak coupling constants α and g²/4π
- The total cross section for elastic νₑN scattering is σ = (g_{νₑps}g_{Nps})²/(16πM²) · 1/(1+2ω₁/M), of order 10⁻³⁶ cm²
- The best agreement with all five experiments is achieved with effective number of collisions n₀ = 11
- For the ³⁷Cl → ³⁷Ar process: SSM prediction 7.9 SNU; calculated with n₀=11 gives 2.72 SNU; experimental value 2.56 ± 0.16 ± 0.16 SNU
- For the ⁷¹Ga → ⁷¹Ge process: SSM prediction 132 SNU; calculated with n₀=11 gives 62.6 SNU; experimental values 65.4⁺³·¹₋₃·₀⁺²·⁶₋₂·₈ SNU and 62.9⁺⁶·⁰₋₅·₉ SNU
- For νₑe⁻ elastic scattering at Super-Kamiokande III (E_c = 5.0 MeV): experimental effective ⁸B flux 2.32 ± 0.04 ± 0.05 × 10⁶ cm⁻²s⁻¹; calculated with n₀=11 gives 2.27 × 10⁶ cm⁻²s⁻¹
- The relative single-shot energy loss for ⁸B neutrinos (average energy 6.7 MeV) is one order of magnitude higher than for pp neutrinos (maximum energy 0.423 MeV)

## Context

This work proposes an alternative to the neutrino oscillation hypothesis for solving the solar neutrino problem, which was previously addressed by the Wolfenstein-Mikheev-Smirnov mechanism and vacuum oscillations requiring at least two massive neutrinos and two free parameters. The author argues that no publication has presented a single-valued calculation procedure for the survival probability of solar electron neutrinos with all free parameters specified and compared to all five experimental processes. The semi-weak interaction model specifically contradicts the oscillation-based interpretation of solar neutrino data while noting that the absence of significant solar neutrino oscillations does not deny small oscillations in other experiments. The author also criticizes the KamLAND reactor antineutrino experiment, citing prior work on fluorescent light attenuation in the liquid scintillator as a source of uncontrolled event loss.
