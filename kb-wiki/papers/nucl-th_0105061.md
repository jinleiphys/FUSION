# On the muon capture in 28^Si and extraction of g_P

> Comparison of the new data for the negative muon capture in 28^Si with the recent shell model calculations using a full 1s-0d model space and the USD empirical effective interaction and the one-nucleon weak current provides the induced pseudoscalar g_P substantially smaller than the value predicted by PCAC and pion-pole dominance. We find that adding the effect from the one-pion exchange axial charge density and using the same scheme of calculations does not change situation to the value of g_P.

## Key claim
The induced pseudoscalar coupling constant \(g_P\) extracted from muon capture in \(^{28}\mathrm{Si}\) remains substantially below the PCAC and pion-pole dominance prediction even after including the one-pion exchange axial charge density, contrary to the expectation from \(A=12\) nuclei where such exchange effects are large.

## Method
The authors performed shell-model calculations using a full 1s-0d model space with the USD empirical effective interaction and the OXBASH code. They computed the \(\gamma\)-\(\nu\) correlation coefficient \(a^0_2\) for the reaction \(\mu^- + ^{28}\mathrm{Si}(0^+) \rightarrow \nu_\mu + ^{28}\mathrm{Al}^*(1^+; 2201\ \mathrm{keV}) \rightarrow \gamma + ^{28}\mathrm{Al}^*(0^+; 972.4\ \mathrm{keV})\) using one-nucleon weak currents and then added the weak axial one-pion exchange charge density, comparing the results to experimental data.

## Key numbers
- Experimental correlation coefficient: \(a^0_2 = 0.360 \pm 0.059\) from the \(\gamma\)-\(\nu\) correlation measurement in the \(^{28}\mathrm{Si}\) muon capture reaction.
- Previous analysis using full 1s-0d model space with USD interaction gave \(g_P/g_A = 0.0 \pm 3.2\).
- For \(A=12\) nuclei, the weak axial one-pion exchange charge density enhanced the matrix element by almost \(40\%\), but in \(^{28}\mathrm{Si}\) the effect was negligibly small.
- Consistent renormalization by Siiskonen et al. (2001) using a realistic NN interaction and G-matrix for the 1s-0d shell yielded \(4.4 \le g_P/g_A \le 5.9\).

## Context
This work extends earlier comparisons of muon capture data in \(^{28}\mathrm{Si}\) with shell-model calculations by Ciechanowicz (1976) and by P. Vogel and collaborators, which had already indicated a low \(g_P\). It contrasts with the \(A=12\) case studied by Guichon and Samour (1982), where the one-pion exchange axial charge density produced a large effect. The failure to reproduce the PCAC prediction in \(^{28}\mathrm{Si}\) points to the need for consistent renormalization of wavefunctions and operators, as pursued by Siiskonen et al. (2001), and leaves open the question of whether other nuclear renormalization effects (core polarization, etc.) are required.
