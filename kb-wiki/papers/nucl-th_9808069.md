# Coulomb and nuclear breakup of $^8$B

> The cross sections for the ($^8$B,$^7$Be-$p$) breakup reaction on $^{58}$Ni and $^{208}$Pb targets at the beam energies of 25.8 MeV and 415 MeV have been calculated within a one-step prior-form distorted-wave Born approximation. The relative contributions of Coulomb and nuclear breakup of dipole and quadrupole multipolarities as well as their interference have been determined. The nuclear breakup contributions are found to be substantial in the angular distributions of the $^7$Be fragment for angles in the range of 30$^\circ$ - 80$^\circ$ at 25.8 MeV beam energy. The Coulomb-nuclear interference terms make the dipole cross section larger than that of quadrupole even at this low beam energy. However, at the incident energy of 415 MeV, these effects are almost negligible in the angular distributions of the ($^7$Be-p) coincidence cross sections at angles below 4$^\circ$.

## Key claim

The Coulomb-nuclear interference effects make the dipole component of the total breakup cross section larger than the quadrupole component at all angles for the $^8$B + $^{58}$Ni reaction at 25.8 MeV, contrary to the expectation from semiclassical Alder-Winther theory that the $E2$ component would dominate at sub-Coulomb energies. At the higher beam energy of 415 MeV on $^{208}$Pb, nuclear effects and quadrupole contributions are almost negligible only for angles below 4 degrees.

## Method

The authors performed one-step prior-form distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations for the breakup of $^8$B into $^7$Be and a proton on $^{58}$Ni and $^{208}$Pb targets at beam energies of 25.8 MeV and 415 MeV respectively. The breakup was treated as a single proton excitation from the ground state to discretized continuum states using the method of continuum bins, with Coulomb and nuclear form factors calculated by single-folding fragment-target interactions over the projectile wave functions. Three-body kinematics were used for the final state in the $^{58}$Ni case, and the calculations included excitations to $\ell = 3$ continuum states.

## Key numbers

- For $^8$B + $^{58}$Ni at 25.8 MeV: the ratio of experimental integrated breakup cross section of $^7$Be (integrated over the angular range (45 $\pm$ 6)$^\circ$) to Rutherford elastic scattering of $^8$B is (8.1 $\pm$ 0.8$^{+2.0}_{-0.5}$) $\times$ 10$^{-3}$; the DWBA calculation with three-body kinematics gives 7.0 $\times$ 10$^{-3}$, in close agreement.
- For $^8$B + $^{208}$Pb at 415 MeV: the point-like projectile approximation (PLPA) becomes inaccurate beyond 4$^\circ$; the $E2$ component of pure Coulomb excitation becomes increasingly important after this angle.
- For the RIKEN data analysis: the quadrupole component is inconsequential in the relative energy bin 0.5-0.75 MeV, significant at angles beyond 5$^\circ$ in the bin 1.25-1.50 MeV, and significant at almost all angles in the bin 2.0-2.25 MeV.
- The astrophysical S-factor resulting from the analysis of the RIKEN data is 17 eV.barn (not a fitting parameter).

## Context

This work extends prior DWBA calculations by Nunes and Thompson (1998) by including $f$-wave final states and proper three-body kinematics, and applies the model to both the Notre Dame experiment on $^{58}$Ni at 25.8 MeV (von Schwarzenberg et al., 1996) and the RIKEN experiment on $^{208}$Pb at 415 MeV (Kikuchi et al., 1997). The results contradict the conclusions of Kikuchi et al. (1997) and Bertulani and Gai (1998) regarding the smallness of the quadrupole component, and demonstrate that the Notre Dame experiment cannot reliably determine the $E2$ component due to Coulomb-nuclear interference effects. The work enables more reliable extraction of the astrophysical S-factor $S_{17}(0)$ from breakup data taken at forward angles below 4$^\circ$ at RIKEN energies.
