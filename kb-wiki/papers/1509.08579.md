# A study of transverse momentum distributions of jets produced in p-p, p-\bar p, d-Au, Au-Au, and Pb-Pb collisions at high energies

> The transverse momentum distributions of jets produced in p-p, p-\bar p, d-Au, Au-Au, and Pb-Pb collisions at high energies with different selected conditions are analyzed by using a multi-source thermal model. The multi-component (mostly two-component) Erlang distribution used in our description is in good agreement with the experimental data measured by the STAR, D0, CDF II, ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS Collaborations. Related parameters are extracted from the transverse momentum distributions and some information on different interacting systems are obtained. In the two-component Erlang distribution, the first component has usually two or more sources which are contributed by strong scattering interactions between two quarks or more quarks and gluons, while the second component has mostly two sources which are contributed by harder head-on scattering between two quarks.

## Key claim

The transverse momentum distributions of jets across multiple collision systems (p-p, p-anti-p, d-Au, Au-Au, Pb-Pb) and energies (0.2–8 TeV) can be described by a multi-component (mostly two-component) Erlang distribution within a multi-source thermal model, where the first component corresponds to strong scattering among multiple partons and the second to harder head-on scattering between two quarks; the model's effective temperature parameter T depends on center-of-mass energy, system size, centrality, jet order, and jet type, but the paper hedges that T "slightly decreases with increase of the size of interacting system, or T is approximately not related to the size in the error range."

## Method

The authors applied a multi-source thermal model in which emission sources are grouped by interaction mechanism, each group producing an Erlang distribution from the folding of exponential source contributions. They used Monte Carlo methods to generate two-component (or, in one case, three-component) Erlang distributions and fitted them to published jet pT spectra from the STAR, D0, CDF II, ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS Collaborations, extracting free parameters (source numbers m1, m2; mean transverse momenta per source; and weights k1, k2) and defining an effective temperature T as the weighted sum of mean source transverse momenta.

## Key numbers

- For p-p collisions at sqrt(s)=7 TeV, the effective temperature T extracted from the 1st leading jet spectra is significantly larger than that from the 2nd leading jet; T for the 1st leading jet in e+jets channels is approximately in agreement with that in dilepton channels within uncertainties.
- For Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, T extracted from charged jet pT spectra slightly increases with centrality percentage C, fitted by T = (-0.125 ± 0.020) exp[-C/(10.500 ± 3.000)] + (5.878 ± 0.010) GeV with chi^2/dof = 0.0002.
- For p-p collisions at sqrt(s)=7 TeV, T decreases with jet order O from 1st to 5th, fitted by T = (54.30 ± 3.00) exp[-O/(1.43 ± 0.10)] + (4.55 ± 1.00) GeV with chi^2 = 0.129.
- For the inclusive large-R jet pT distribution in p-p at sqrt(s)=8 TeV (ATLAS, 20 fb^-1), a three-component Erlang distribution was needed with parameters: m1=29, p_ti1=(6.65±0.60) GeV/c, k1=0.396±0.036; m2=14, p_ti2=(19.50±1.00) GeV/c, k2=0.204±0.036; m3=2, p_ti3=(111.00±10.00) GeV/c; T=51.011±11.853 GeV; chi^2/dof=0.377.
- For p-anti-p collisions at sqrt(s)=1.96 TeV, all four cases (D0 and CDF II data) had second-component source numbers m2=2, and T values were approximately the same across channels.

## Context

This work extends the multi-source thermal model previously developed by the same group (Wei and Liu, referencing their own prior work [37–39]) to jet pT distributions across a wide range of collision systems and energies, from RHIC (0.2 TeV) to LHC (8 TeV). The model successfully describes data from multiple collaborations (STAR, D0, CDF II, ALICE, ATLAS, CMS) that had previously been analyzed individually, and it enables systematic comparison of effective temperatures across different jet types, orders, centralities, and system sizes, revealing trends such as the decrease of T with jet order and the slight increase with centrality in Pb-Pb collisions. The paper notes that the effective temperature parameter includes both thermal motion and flow effects, and is not the true source temperature.
