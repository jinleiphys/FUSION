# Quasi-$γ$ band in $^{\text{114}}$Te

> The low-lying non-yrast states in $^{114}$Te have been investigated using the Indian National Gamma Array through the fusion-evaporation reaction $^{112}$Sn($^{4}$He, 2n) at a beam energy of 37 MeV. Eight new $γ$-transitions have been placed in the level scheme to establish the quasi-$γ$ band in this nucleus. Spin and parity of several excited states have been assigned from the present spectroscopy measurements. The comparison of experimental results on the observed bands with the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) and Triaxial Projected Shell Model (TPSM) confirming the existence of the quasi-$γ$ band structure in the $^{114}$Te nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the low-lying non-yrast states in \(^{114}\)Te form a quasi-\(\gamma\) band structure, but the evidence is based on the similarity of dynamic moments of inertia and energy staggering patterns to known quasi-\(\gamma\) bands, not on direct observation of pure \(\gamma\)-vibrational phonon excitations; the authors hedge by stating the bands "correspond to" the quasi-\(\gamma\)-vibrational structure based on indirect indicators.

## Method

The excited states in \(^{114}\)Te were populated via the fusion-evaporation reaction \(^{112}\)Sn(\(^{4}\)He, 2n) at a beam energy of 37 MeV using the Indian National Gamma Array (INGA) with seven Compton-suppressed HPGe clover detectors and one LEPS detector. Gamma-gamma coincidence data were collected, and spin-parity assignments were made using directional correlation from oriented states (DCO) ratios and linear polarization asymmetry measurements. The experimental level scheme was compared with Interacting Boson Model (IBM) and Triaxial Projected Shell Model (TPSM) calculations.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 37 MeV for the \(^{112}\)Sn(\(^{4}\)He, 2n) reaction.
- Target thickness: 4.5 mg/cm\(^2\) of isotopically-enriched (99.6%) \(^{112}\)Sn.
- Approximately 1.5 \(\times\) 10\(^8\) coincidence events were acquired.
- Energy ratio R\(_{4/2}\) = 2.09 for the yrast band, indicating proximity to U(5) symmetry.
- Ratio of quasi-\(\gamma\) bandhead energy to first 2\(^+\) state energy: 1.96.
- RMS deviation for 14 excited states in the IBM fit: 117.63 keV.
- TPSM axial deformation parameter \(\epsilon = 0.150\) and non-axial deformation parameter \(\epsilon' = 0.09\).
- TPSM calculated band-head energies: \(\gamma\)-band at 1.2452 MeV and \(\gamma\gamma\)-band at 2.7252 MeV above ground state.
- DCO ratio for pure dipole transition (936.2 keV, 7\(^-\) \(\rightarrow\) 6\(^+\), E1): 0.70 (0.08).
- DCO ratio for pure quadrupole transition (774.8 keV, 4\(^+\) \(\rightarrow\) 2\(^+\), E2): 1.60 (0.18).

## Context

This work extends the systematic search for quasi-\(\gamma\) bands in Te isotopes, which had previously been identified in heavier isotopes \(^{124,126}\)Te but without in-band \(\gamma\) transitions, and only tentatively reported in \(^{118}\)Te. The study contradicts earlier interpretations that \(^{114}\)Te mimics pure U(5) dynamical symmetry based on the R\(_{4/2}\) ratio, as the B(E2) values from lifetime measurements were inconsistent with the U(5) limit of IBM (Moller et al., prior work). The results enable future investigations of shape evolution in neutron-deficient Te isotopes and place \(^{114}\)Te between the U(5) and O(6) dynamical symmetries in the Casten symmetry triangle.
