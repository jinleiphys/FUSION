# Radiative transfer in stars by feebly interacting bosons

> Starting from first principles, we study radiative transfer by new feebly-interacting bosons (FIBs) such as axions, axion-like particles (ALPs), dark photons, and others. Our key simplification is to include only boson emission or absorption (including decay), but not scattering between different modes of the radiation field. Based on a given distribution of temperature and FIB absorption rate in a star, we derive explicit volume-integral expressions for the boson luminosity, reaching from the free-streaming to the strong-trapping limit. The latter is seen explicitly to correspond to quasi-thermal emission from a "FIB sphere" according to the Stefan-Boltzmann law. Our results supersede expressions and approximations found in the recent literature on FIB emission from a supernova core and, for radiatively unstable FIBs, provide explicit expressions for the nonlocal ("ballistic") transfer of energy recently discussed in horizontal-branch stars.

## Key claim

The authors derive explicit volume-integral expressions for the luminosity of feebly interacting bosons (FIBs) from stars, valid from the free-streaming to the strong-trapping limit, under the simplifying assumption that only boson emission and absorption (including decay) are included, but not scattering between different modes of the radiation field.

## Method

Starting from the Boltzmann collision equation for a single FIB mode along a ray, the authors solve for the stationary occupation number as an integral over the optical depth, using the reduced absorption rate (which includes stimulated emission) as the only particle-physics input. They then generalize to plane-parallel and spherical geometries by angular integration, expressing the angular moments of the radiation field as linear transformations (Schwarzschild-Milne equations) of the blackbody intensity. The transition to the diffusion regime is shown by expanding the integral kernels in Taylor series, recovering the standard diffusive flux proportional to the temperature gradient.

## Key numbers

* For a power-law temperature profile T(τ) = T₁ τ^p with p = 0.2, the Stefan-Boltzmann optical depth where the SB flux matches the escaping true flux is τ_SB ≃ 0.60.
* For the special case p = 1/4, the Stefan-Boltzmann optical depth is exactly τ_SB = 2/3.
* The diffusive flux in the trapped regime is F_diff(τ,ω) = (v²_ω/3) dB_ω(τ)/dτ, where v_ω is the boson velocity and B_ω is the blackbody intensity.
* The escaping flux at the surface (τ = 0) for a power-law profile with index p is F_true(0,p) = p Γ(4p)/(1+2p), which for p = 1/4 gives approximately 1/6.

## Context

This work extends the standard theory of radiative transfer (Mihalas 1978; Shapiro and Teukolsky 1983) to feebly interacting bosons such as axions, ALPs, and dark photons, superseding expressions and approximations used in recent studies of FIB emission from supernova cores (Chang 2016; Lucente 2020; Bollig 2020; Caputo 2021). The results provide explicit expressions for nonlocal ("ballistic") energy transfer in horizontal-branch stars, as recently discussed by Lucente (2022), and enable more accurate calculations of stellar energy-loss bounds for new bosonic particles.
