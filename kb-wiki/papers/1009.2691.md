# Low energy shape oscillations of negative parity in the main and shape-isomeric minima in actinides

> We study low energy shape oscillations of negative parity in the first and second (isomeric) minima in actinides. As a main tool we use the phenomenological Woods-Saxon potential with a variety of shape deformations. This allows to include a mixing of various multipolarities when considering oscillations with a fixed $K$ quantum number. The phonon energies are determined either from the collective Hamiltonian with the microscopic-macrocopic energy and cranking mass parameters, or from its simplified version with the constant mass parameters. The results for $K^π=0^-$,$1^-$ in the first minima are in a reasonable agreement with experimental data, including predicted E1 transitions; the $K^π=2^-$ energies are systematically overestimated. In the second minimum, as compared to the data for $^{240}$Pu and $^{236}$U, our calculated $K=$1,2 energies are overestimated while the $K=0$ energies are three or more times too large. This signals either a non-collective character of the experimentally assigned $K=0$ states or a serious flaw of the model in the second minimum. More data on the $K=0$, $I^π=1^-$ collective states in the second minima of other nuclei are necessary to resolve this issue.

## Key claim

The authors find that their microscopic-macroscopic model, based on the Woods-Saxon potential with multipole mixing up to λ=7, reproduces Kπ=0− and 1− phonon energies in the first (ground-state) minima of actinides to within ~100 keV, but systematically overestimates Kπ=2− energies. In the second (shape-isomeric) minimum, the calculated K=0 energies are three or more times too large compared to data for 240Pu and 236U, which signals either a non-collective character of the experimentally assigned K=0 states or a serious flaw of the model in the second minimum.

## Method

The authors use a deformed Woods-Saxon single-particle potential with a nuclear surface parametrized by multipole deformations βλμ for λ=3,5,7 and K=0,1,2,3, combined with the Yukawa-plus-exponential macroscopic energy and Strutinsky shell corrections. They construct a collective Hamiltonian with cranking mass parameters and either constant or deformation-dependent masses, diagonalizing it in the space of reflection-asymmetric deformations to obtain phonon energies for each K quantum number. The coupling between different multipolarities (λ=3,5,7) is included via a 3D harmonic oscillator model that yields the lowest eigenmode for each K.

## Key numbers

- For 240Pu in the first minimum, the Coriolis coupling between K=0 and K=1 states is estimated as 49.6 keV (using a=7.156 keV and λ=3); in the second minimum it is 23.1 keV for λ=3 and 36.5 keV for λ=5 (using a=3.33 keV).
- The calculated excitation energy of the superdeformed state in 240Pu is EthII=2.0 MeV, compared to the experimental value EexpII=2.25 MeV; the calculated first and second barriers are EthA=6.4 MeV and EthB=5.0 MeV, compared to experimental values EexpA=5.8 MeV and EexpB=5.45 MeV.
- In 240Pu, the calculated Kπ=0− phonon energy in the second minimum is nearly three times the experimental bandhead energy of 555 keV (Iπ=1−); for 236U the calculated Kπ=0− energy is even larger relative to the experimental 290 keV.
- The stiffness coefficient C3030 at the second minimum is roughly two times larger than at the first minimum for all studied nuclei, while the mass parameter B3030 is two to three times smaller at the second minimum than at the ground-state minimum.
- For 234U, the root-mean-square deviation of the harmonic fit to the octupole energy surface is 1.3 keV at the first minimum and 0.45 keV at the second minimum.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic-macroscopic approach previously applied to octupole vibrations in nuclei like 222Ra (collPom, 1990s) by including higher odd-multipolarities (λ=5 and 7) and applying it systematically to both the first and second minima in actinides. The results contradict the interpretation of the low-lying Kπ=0− states observed in the second well of 240Pu and 236U (Thirolf, 2000s) as collective octupole vibrations, suggesting instead that these states may be non-collective or that the model fails for superdeformed minima. The study enables future tests by calling for more experimental data on K=0, Iπ=1− collective states in the second minima of other nuclei to resolve the discrepancy.
