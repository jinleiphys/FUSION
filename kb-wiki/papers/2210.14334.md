# Numerical study of the twist-3 asymmetry $A_{LT}$ in single-inclusive electron-nucleon and proton-proton collisions

> We provide the first rigorous numerical analysis of the longitudinal-transverse double-spin asymmetry $A_{LT}$ in electron-nucleon and proton-proton collisions for the case where only a single pion, jet, or photon is detected in the final state. Given recent extractions of certain, previously unknown, non-perturbative functions, we are able to compute contributions from all terms relevant for $A_{LT}$ and make realistic predictions for the observable at Jefferson Lab (JLab) 12 GeV, COMPASS, the future Electron-Ion Collider, and the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. We also compare our results to a JLab 6 GeV measurement, which are the only data available for this type of reaction. The twist-3 nature of $A_{LT}$ makes it a potentially fruitful avenue to probe quark-gluon-quark correlations in hadrons as well as provide insights into dynamical quark mass generation in QCD.

## Key claim

This paper provides the first rigorous numerical analysis of the longitudinal-transverse double-spin asymmetry \(A_{LT}\) in single-inclusive electron-nucleon and proton-proton collisions, but the predictions still require approximations for or neglect of certain twist-3 PDFs or FFs due to lack of input for them, meaning the results are realistic but not complete.

## Method

The authors compute \(A_{LT}\) using the collinear twist-3 formalism for reactions where a single pion, jet, or photon is detected in the final state, including \(\vec{e}N^\uparrow \to \pi X\) and \(p^\uparrow \vec{p} \to \pi X\). They use recent extractions of the kinematical twist-3 PDF \(g_{1T}^{(1)}(x)\) from Bhattacharya et al. (2021) and the twist-3 FF \(\tilde{H}(z)\) from the JAM3D-22 analysis to construct two scenarios for the PDFs (a "quark-gluon-quark" scenario and a Wandzura-Wilczek approximation scenario) and three scenarios for the FF \(E(z)\) (equal to \(-\tilde{H}(z)\), zero, or \(\tilde{H}(z)\)). The numerical integrations are performed over the relevant kinematic variables using standard PDF and FF sets from LHAPDF.

## Key numbers

- For the qgq scenario of \(g_{1T}^{(1)}(x)\) at \(Q^2 = 4\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), the up quark distribution \(xg_{1T}^{(1)u}(x)\) is larger and falls off slower at larger \(x\) compared to the WW scenario, while both scenarios are compatible within error bands for the down quark.
- The lattice QCD calculation of \(g_T^{u-d}(x)\) from Bhattacharya et al. (2020) shows agreement with both the qgq and WW scenarios up to \(x \approx 0.4\), but at larger \(x\) the WW scenario goes to zero fastest while the qgq scenario exhibits a change in sign.
- The only existing data for \(A_{LT}\) in single-inclusive reactions are from Jefferson Lab 6 GeV on \(\vec{e} n^\uparrow \to \pi X\) (JeffersonLabHallA, 2015).

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of \(A_{LT}\) in electron-nucleon collisions by Kang et al. (2011) and Kanazawa et al. (2014, 2015), which were limited by the lack of input for non-perturbative functions and had to rely on Wandzura-Wilczek approximations or neglect certain terms entirely. It also provides the first numerical predictions for \(A_{LT}\) in proton-proton collisions, where only analytical formulas existed from Metz et al. (2012), Koike et al. (2015, 2016), and Hatta et al. (2013). The results enable future measurements at JLab 12 GeV, COMPASS, the Electron-Ion Collider, and RHIC to probe quark-gluon-quark correlations in hadrons and potentially provide insight into dynamical quark mass generation in QCD through the FF \(E(z)\).
