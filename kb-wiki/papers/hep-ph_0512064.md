# eta and eta' mesons and dimension 2 gluon condensate <A^2>

> The study of light pseudoscalar quark-antiquark bound states in the Dyson-Schwinger approach with the effective QCD coupling enhanced by the interplay of the dimension 2 gluon condensate <A^2> and dimension 4 gluon condensate <F^2>, is extended to the eta-eta' complex. We include the effects of the gluon axial anomaly into the Dyson-Schwinger approach to mesons. The calculated masses, mixing and two-photon decay widths of eta and eta' mesons are in agreement with experiment. Also, in a model-independent way, we give the modification of the Gell-Mann--Okubo and Schwinger nonet relations due to the interplay of the gluon anomaly and SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking.

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger approach extended to the eta-eta' complex, using an effective QCD coupling enhanced by the interplay of dimension-2 and dimension-4 gluon condensates, can reproduce experimental masses, mixing, and two-photon decay widths of eta and eta' mesons, provided the gluon axial anomaly effects are included as a parameterized mass shift that does not vanish in the chiral limit.

## Method

The authors solved the Dyson-Schwinger (gap) equation for dressed quark propagators of u, d, and s quarks in the rainbow-ladder approximation, using an effective strong coupling alpha_eff(Q^2) derived from the gluon and ghost polarization functions incorporating contributions from the dimension-2 gluon condensate <A^2> and dimension-4 gluon condensate <F^2>. They then solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for pseudoscalar quark-antiquark bound states to obtain masses and decay constants of pi, K, and the unphysical s\bar{s} state, and constructed the eta-eta' mass matrix in the octet-singlet basis with the gluon anomaly included as an additional diagonal mass shift 3\beta on the singlet component, with flavor symmetry breaking parameterized by a factor X for the strange quark.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass M_pi = 0.1350 GeV, decay constant f_pi = 0.09293 GeV, two-photon decay amplitude T_pi^{gamma gamma} = 0.2560 GeV^{-1}
* Kaon mass M_K = 0.4949 GeV, decay constant f_K = 0.1115 GeV
* Unphysical s\bar{s} pseudoscalar mass M_{s\bar{s}} = 0.7221 GeV, decay constant f_{s\bar{s}} = 0.1329 GeV
* Constituent quark masses: M_u(0) = M_d(0) = 0.3842 GeV, M_s(0) = 0.5922 GeV
* Bare quark masses: m_{u,d} = 3.046 MeV, m_s = 67.70 MeV
* Non-anomalous mass matrix elements: M_88^2 = (594.72 MeV)^2, M_80^2 = -(487.05 MeV)^2, M_00^2 = (431.22 MeV)^2
* Gluon condensate parameters: C_A = (0.6060 GeV)^4 = C_G, m_A = 0.8402 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger approach of Kekez and Klabucar (2003) from the pion and kaon sector to the eta-eta' complex, incorporating the gluon axial anomaly treatment developed by Klabucar (1997) and Kekez (2000). The approach enables calculation of eta and eta' properties without refitting model parameters, and provides model-independent modifications of the Gell-Mann-Okubo and Schwinger nonet relations due to the interplay of the gluon anomaly and SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking.
