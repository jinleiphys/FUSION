# Transition radiation as a probe of chiral anomaly

> A fast charged particle crossing the boundary between the chiral matter and vacuum radiates the transition radiation. Its most remarkable features --- the resonant behavior at a certain emission angle and the circular polarization of the spectrum --- depend on the parameters of the chiral anomaly in a particular material/matter. The chiral transition radiation can be used to investigate the chiral anomaly in such diverse media as the quark-gluon plasma, the Weyl semimetals, and the axionic dark matter.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a fast charged particle crossing a boundary between chiral matter and vacuum emits a distinctive "chiral transition radiation" whose resonant angular peak and circular polarization depend on the parameters of the chiral anomaly, but only when the photon polarization satisfies a sign condition relative to the chiral conductivity or the gradient of the theta field.

## Method

The authors derive the photon emission spectrum for an ultrarelativistic fermion crossing a planar boundary into vacuum, using the Maxwell-Chern-Simons effective theory to incorporate the chiral anomaly. They compute the scattering matrix element in the radiation gauge, treating the chiral-medium modification of the photon dispersion relation as a small parameter, and isolate the resonant contribution that arises when the effective mass-squared parameter kappa_lambda becomes negative. The analysis covers two cases: a spatially uniform but time-dependent theta field (case i, giving chiral conductivity sigma_chi) and a time-independent but anisotropic theta field (case ii, giving a vector b proportional to the gradient of theta).

## Key numbers

- For quark-gluon plasma with estimated chiral conductivity sigma_chi ~ 10 MeV, 1 GeV photons are emitted at angle theta ~ 0.1 rad relative to the jet direction.
- For a Weyl semimetal with b = (alpha/pi) * 80 eV, an electron of about 1 GeV energy moving parallel to b radiates 10 MeV photons at angle theta = 1.3 x 10^{-4} rad.
- For axionic dark matter with theta_0 ~ 10^{-19}, axion mass m_a ~ 10^{-6} eV, and photon energy omega ~ 1 TeV, the emission angle is theta ~ 10^{-15} rad.

## Context

This work extends the classical transition radiation effect of Ginzburg and Frank (1945) to chiral media, building on the photon dispersion relations derived by Tuchin (2018) and earlier work on Maxwell-Chern-Simons theory by Wilczek (1987), Carroll, Field, and Jackiw (1989), and Sikivie (1984). The proposed chiral transition radiation provides a new experimental probe of the chiral anomaly in quark-gluon plasma, Weyl semimetals (as studied by Xu et al. 2015 and Lv et al. 2015), and axionic dark matter (as discussed by Chang et al. 2015 and Graham et al. 2013). The method enables direct measurement of the chiral conductivity or the Weyl node separation via the angular and polarization properties of emitted photons.
