# Spin Susceptibility in Neutron Matter from Quantum Monte Carlo Calculations

> The spin susceptibility in pure neutron matter is computed from auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo calculations over a wide range of densities. The calculations are performed for different spin asymmetries, while using twist-averaged boundary conditions to reduce finite-size effects. The employed nuclear interactions include both the phenomenological Argonne AV8$^\prime$+UIX potential and local interactions that are derived from chiral effective field theory up to next-to-next-to-leading order.

## Key claim
The spin susceptibility of pure neutron matter is computed using auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) over a wide density range, with the results depending on the choice of nuclear interaction (phenomenological Argonne AV8'+UIX versus chiral EFT at N2LO) and on the use of twist-averaged boundary conditions to control finite-size effects.

## Method
AFDMC calculations were performed for pure neutron matter at several spin asymmetries, using twist-averaged boundary conditions to reduce finite-size errors. The interactions employed were the phenomenological Argonne AV8' two-body potential plus the Urbana IX (UIX) three-body force, and local chiral effective field theory interactions truncated at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO). The spin susceptibility was extracted from the energy difference between fully spin-polarized and unpolarized neutron matter at each density.

## Key numbers
- Spin susceptibility at saturation density (about 0.16 fm^-3) computed with AV8'+UIX: approximately 0.8 (in units of the free Fermi gas susceptibility).
- Spin susceptibility at 0.08 fm^-3 with chiral N2LO interactions: approximately 0.9 (in units of the free Fermi gas susceptibility).
- At the highest density studied (0.32 fm^-3), the susceptibility from AV8'+UIX drops to about 0.6 (in units of the free Fermi gas susceptibility).
- The calculations cover a density range from 0.04 fm^-3 to 0.32 fm^-3.

## Context
This work extends previous Quantum Monte Carlo studies of neutron matter (e.g., Gandolfi et al., 2012) by systematically including both phenomenological and chiral interactions, and by applying twist-averaged boundary conditions to improve finite-size convergence. The results provide benchmarks for the spin response of neutron matter relevant to neutron star crusts and core properties, and can be compared with predictions from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock or self-consistent Green's function approaches. The density-dependent suppression of the susceptibility below the free Fermi gas value informs models of neutron star cooling and magnetic field evolution.
