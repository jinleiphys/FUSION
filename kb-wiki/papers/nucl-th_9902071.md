# Radiative production of the Lambda(1405) resonance in K collisions on protons and nuclei

> We have carried a theoretical study of the K^- p\to M B γreaction with M B = K^-p, \bar{K}^0 n, π^- Σ^+, π^+ Σ^-, π^0 Σ^0, π^0 Λ, for K^- lab. momenta between 200 and 500 MeV/c, using a chiral unitary approach for the strong K^-p interaction with its coupled channels. The Λ(1405) resonance, which is generated dynamically in this approach, shows up clearly in the dσ/dM_I spectrum, providing new tests for chiral symmetry and the unitary approach, as well as information regarding the nature of the resonance. The photon detection alone, summing all channels, is shown to reproduce quite accurately the strength and shape of the Λ(1405) resonance. Analogous reactions in nuclei can provide much information on the properties of this resonance in a nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The $K^- p \rightarrow M B \gamma$ reaction, with only photon detection and summing over all final meson-baryon channels, can reproduce the shape and strength of the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance, provided the $K^-$ lab momentum is above 200 MeV/c to avoid the Bremsstrahlung tail of the $K^- p \gamma$ channel obscuring the signal.

## Method

The authors used a chiral unitary approach, solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with coupled channels ($K^-p$, $\bar{K}^0 n$, $\pi^0\Lambda$, $\pi^0\Sigma^0$, $\eta\Lambda$, $\eta\Sigma^0$, $\pi^+\Sigma^-$, $\pi^-\Sigma^+$, $K^+\Xi^-$, $K^0\Xi^0$) for the strong interaction, and added photon couplings to mesons, baryons, and a contact term required by gauge invariance. They computed the amplitude for $K^- p \rightarrow M B \gamma$ in the Coulomb gauge, retaining the dominant Bremsstrahlung diagrams from mesons and baryons and the magnetic part from internal loops, and evaluated the cross section as a function of the final meson-baryon invariant mass $M_I$ for $K^-$ lab momenta between 200 and 500 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- The $\Lambda(1405)$ peak appears clearly in the $\pi\Sigma$ spectrum of $d\sigma/dM_I$ for $K^-$ lab momenta between 200 and 500 MeV/c.
- The $K^-p\rightarrow\bar{K}^0 n\gamma$ cross section is very small, around 0.1 mb GeV$^{-1}$ in the range 1.44 - 1.52 GeV.
- The Bremsstrahlung tail of the $K^-p\rightarrow K^- p\gamma$ reaction overlaps with the $\Lambda(1405)$ signal for $K^-$ lab momenta below 200 MeV/c; if $\pi\Sigma$ final particles are detected in coincidence, one can go down to $K^-$ lab momenta of 150 MeV/c.
- The photon momentum in the center-of-mass frame is of the order of 150 MeV or below for the energy regime studied.
- The contact term corrections are below the 8% level, and the loop contribution from diagram row (8) is less than 10% of the cross section.
- The effects of Fermi motion in nuclei were shown for a density $\rho = \rho_0/4$, producing a widening of the $\Lambda(1405)$ distribution.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Oset and Ramos (1998) for the $S=-1$ strong interaction to radiative production of the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance, complementing the photoproduction study of the $\gamma p \rightarrow K^+ \Lambda(1405)$ reaction by Nacher et al. (1999). The reaction studied here uses low-energy $K^-$ beams (momenta below 500 MeV/c) and produces photons of about 100 MeV, making Fermi motion effects in nuclei much weaker than in the photoproduction case, so that photon detection alone can still reveal the resonance shape. The results enable experimental tests at facilities like KEK or Brookhaven, where data from $K^- p$ experiments with photon detection are being analyzed, and provide a baseline for studying modifications of the $\Lambda(1405)$ properties in a nuclear medium.
