# Centrality Dependence of Direct Photons in Au+Au Collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV

> We calculate the spectra of high energy photons emitted in relativistic Au+Au collisions for various centralities and compare to data recently collected at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider by the PHENIX collaboration. Our results for photons from primary hard scatterings and photons from interactions of jets with the medium are consistent with the measurements of neutral pion and direct photon production in p+p collisions and give a good description of direct photon spectra measured in Au+Au collisions. The contribution of photons from jet-to-photon conversion in the medium can be as large as the photon yield from hard scatterings in the momentum range p_T = 2...6 GeV/c. We show that this novel mechanism is not ruled out by any existing data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a calculation including jet-photon conversion in the medium, along with primary hard photons and thermal radiation, gives a good description of the direct photon spectra measured by PHENIX in Au+Au collisions, but the authors caution that primary hard processes alone may also be sufficient within current experimental uncertainties.

## Method

The authors performed a leading-order perturbative QCD calculation of prompt photons (Compton and annihilation processes) and jet production, using CTEQ5L parton distributions with EKS98 nuclear corrections and phenomenological K-factors (K_jet=1.8, K_gamma=1.5) fixed by p+p data. They modeled the fireball evolution assuming boost-invariant longitudinal expansion with azimuthally symmetric transverse expansion, using initial conditions (tau_0, T_0) derived from measured charged hadron multiplicities for each centrality bin. The jet-photon conversion yield was calculated using the rate formula from Fries, Muller, and Srivastava (2003), integrated over the space-time history of the fireball from initial time tau_0 until either the critical temperature T_f=160 MeV or the jet exits the fireball.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperatures T_0 range from 434 MeV (0-10% centrality) to 297 MeV (40-50% centrality)
- Initial times tau_0 range from 0.151 fm/c (0-10%) to 0.221 fm/c (40-50%)
- Nuclear thickness T_AA values: 22.75 mb^-1 (0-10%), 14.35 mb^-1 (10-20%), 8.00 mb^-1 (20-30%), 5.23 mb^-1 (30-40%), 2.86 mb^-1 (40-50%)
- Jet-photon conversion contributes roughly 50% on top of primary hard photons between p_T=4 and 6 GeV/c in central collisions
- Energy loss effects on jet-photon conversion yield are found to be small, about 20%
- The nuclear modification factor R_AA for p_T > 6 GeV/c is compatible with binary collision scaling (R_AA ~ 1) for all centralities

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier prediction of jet-photon conversion in the medium (Fries, Muller, and Srivastava 2003) and provides a systematic centrality-dependent calculation to compare with the first PHENIX data on direct photon production in Au+Au collisions at RHIC (PHENIX 2005). The results are consistent with the NLO calculations by Gordon and Vogelsang (1993) and the work of Turbide et al. (2005), while showing that the jet-photon conversion mechanism is not ruled out by existing data. The calculations enable future high-statistics measurements of the nuclear modification factor R_AA at intermediate p_T to distinguish between different photon production mechanisms, and predict that jet-photon conversion will become the dominant contribution below p_T ~ 12 GeV/c at the LHC.
