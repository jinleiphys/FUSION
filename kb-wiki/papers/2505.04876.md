# Point-proton density distributions of stable nuclei

> Point-proton density distributions are deduced for 130 stable nuclei from $^{7}\mathrm{Li}$ to $^{232}\mathrm{Th}$ from nuclear charge densities determined in elastic electron scattering. There are 171 cases are presented in model-dependent forms, including the modified Harmonic-oscillator function, two-parameter Femi function (2pF), three-parameter Femi function, three-parameter Gaussian function, and 97 in Fourier-Bessel series model-independent forms. Independent of density functions, the point-proton root-mean-square (rms) radii of the derived point-proton density show excellent agreement with each other. We identify cases where the tabulated data of charge densities and charge radii are inconsistent, and the deduced point-proton density distributions are inaccurate due to insufficient experimental momentum transfer coverage or inconsistent scattering experiments. For the widely used 2pF distribution, it is found that the surface diffuseness parameters can be empirically calculated from those of charge density, while the half-density radius parameters follow the $A^{1/3}$ rule. The derived point-proton density distributions can be used as input in nuclear reaction studies and compared with nuclear model predictions.

## Key claim

The authors deduce point-proton density distributions for 130 stable nuclei from charge densities obtained via elastic electron scattering, but caution that the deduced distributions are inaccurate for cases where the tabulated charge density and charge radius data are inconsistent, or where experimental momentum transfer coverage is insufficient.

## Method

The point-proton densities are derived by unfolding the nuclear charge density distributions, using the Fourier transform relation between charge form factors and charge densities, with nucleon electric form factors taken from parameterizations in the literature. The free parameters of the point-proton density (in the same functional form as the original charge density) are determined by fitting the calculated charge form factor to the experimental one using nonlinear least-squares fitting, over the same momentum transfer range as the original electron scattering experiment. The point-neutron density is assumed to follow ρ_n = (N/Z) ρ_p, and spin-orbit density contributions are neglected.

## Key numbers

- Point-proton density distributions are deduced for 130 stable nuclei from ⁷Li to ²³²Th.
- 171 cases are presented in model-dependent forms (MHO, 2pF, 3pF, 3pG) and 97 in Fourier-Bessel series model-independent form.
- For the 2pF distribution, the radius accuracy is within 0.002 fm except for marked outliers; for MHO (Z ≤ 8 nuclei) the accuracy is 0.002 fm; for 3pF and 3pG the accuracy is 0.004 fm.
- Outliers in 2pF cases show radius deviations up to 0.039 fm (⁵⁸Fe, Δ⟨r²⟩_p^{1/2} = -0.039(22) fm) and 0.036 fm (⁶³Cu, Δ⟨r²⟩_p^{1/2} = -0.036(17) fm).
- For ¹⁴⁸Sm in FB form, the radius difference is 0.005(8) fm; for ¹⁵²Sm it is 0.017(8) fm.
- The proton charge radius used is r_p = 0.8414 fm; using r_p = 0.88 fm changes ⟨r²⟩_p^{1/2} by approximately 0.015 fm for A ≤ 10 and 0.006 fm for A ≈ 200.

## Context

This work extends the charge density compilations of De Vries et al. (1987) and Fricke et al. (1995) by systematically deriving point-proton densities from those charge densities, providing a consistent database for nuclear reaction studies and comparisons with nuclear model predictions. The authors identify specific cases where tabulated charge radii deviate from those calculated from the tabulated density parameters (e.g., ²⁰Ne, ⁵⁹Co, ⁷⁴Se) and flag data from a particular study (sh78a) as outliers due to insufficient momentum transfer coverage. The database enables future work on nuclear reaction modeling and tests of nuclear structure theories, while also highlighting the need for caution when using certain existing charge density data.
