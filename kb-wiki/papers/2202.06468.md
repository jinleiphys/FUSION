# Asymmetric nuclear matter in relativistic mean-field models with isoscalar- and isovector-meson mixing

> Using the relativistic mean-field model with nonlinear couplings between the isoscalar and isovector mesons, we study the properties of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter. Not only the vector mixing, $ω_μω^μ\mathbfρ_ν\mathbfρ^ν$, but also the quartic interaction due to the scalar mesons, $σ^{2}\mathbfδ^{2}$, is taken into account to investigate the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy, $E_{\rm sym}$, and the neutron-star properties. It is found that the $δ$ meson increases $E_{\rm sym}$ at high densities, whereas the $σ$-$δ$ mixing makes $E_{\rm sym}$ soft above the saturation density. Furthermore, the $δ$ meson and its mixing have a large influence on the radius and tidal deformability of a neutron star. In particular, the $σ$-$δ$ mixing reduces the neutron-star radius, and, thus, the present calculation can simultaneously reproduce the dimensionless tidal deformabilities of a canonical $1.4M_{\odot}$ neutron star observed from the binary neutron star merger, GW170817, and from the compact binary coalescence, GW190814.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including the scalar-meson mixing term σ²δ² in relativistic mean-field models can simultaneously reproduce the dimensionless tidal deformabilities of a canonical 1.4 M⊙ neutron star observed from both GW170817 and GW190814, but only when the σ-δ mixing parameter Λσδ is positive and less than about 60, because larger values cause the symmetry energy to become negative at high densities.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model including σ, ω, δ, and ρ mesons with nonlinear couplings: the vector mixing ωμωμρνρν and the scalar mixing σ²δ². They fix the isoscalar coupling constants to reproduce saturation properties (binding energy −16.0 MeV, incompressibility 230 MeV, effective nucleon mass 0.65 MN at ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³), then determine the isovector couplings and mixing parameters by requiring Esym = 32.0 MeV and L = 50 MeV at saturation. They vary gδ²/4π from 0 to 10 and Λσδ from −100 to 100, then compute the density dependence of the symmetry energy, neutron-star mass-radius relations, and tidal deformabilities under β-equilibrium and charge neutrality.

## Key numbers

* Symmetry energy at saturation: Esym = 32.0 MeV and slope L = 50 MeV, used as input constraints.
* Saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, binding energy E₀ = −16.0 MeV, incompressibility K₀ = 230 MeV, effective nucleon mass M*N/MN = 0.65.
* The δ meson coupling gδ²/4π is varied in the range 0 ≤ gδ²/4π ≤ 10, with specific values A=1.3, B=2.488, C=4.722 taken from the one-boson exchange potential.
* The σ-δ mixing parameter Λσδ is varied in the range −100 ≤ Λσδ ≤ 100.
* The curvature parameter Ksym varies in the range −450 ≤ Ksym (MeV) ≤ 50 depending on the mixing parameters.
* The constraint from neutron-star observations gives Esym(2ρ₀) ≃ 51 ± 13 MeV at a 68% confidence level.
* Maximum neutron-star masses lie in the range 2.45 ≤ Mmax/M⊙ ≤ 2.47, with radii 11.66 ≤ Rmax (km) ≤ 11.98.
* The radius of a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star, R₁.₄, satisfies the NICER/XMM-Newton constraint of 12.45 ± 0.65 km for all cases except gδ²/4π = 10.
* The dimensionless tidal deformability Λ₁.₄ from GW170817 is 190⁺³⁹⁰₋₁₂₀ and from GW190814 is 616⁺²⁷³₋₁₅₈.

## Context

This work extends previous RMF studies that included the δ meson (Kubis 1997, Hofmann 2000, Liu 2001) and the σ-δ mixing (Zabari 2018, Zabari 2019, Kubis 2020) by systematically investigating both σ²δ² and ωμωμρνρν mixing together. It enables simultaneous consistency with the tidal deformability constraints from GW170817 (LIGO Scientific 2018) and GW190814 (LIGO Scientific 2020), which previous models without σ-δ mixing could not achieve. The results also show that positive Λσδ delays the direct Urca process in neutron-star cooling, and that the σ-δ mixing can partly replace the role of ω-ρ mixing in describing asymmetric nuclear matter properties.
