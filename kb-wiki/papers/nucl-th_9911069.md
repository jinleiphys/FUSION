# Exotic Stochastic Processes from Complex Quantum Environments

> Stochastic processes are shown to emerge from the time evolution of complex quantum systems. Using parametric, banded random matrix ensembles to describe a quantum chaotic environment, we show that the dynamical evolution of a particle coupled to such environments displays a variety of stochastic behaviors, ranging from turbulent diffusion to Lévy processes and Brownian motion. Dissipation and diffusion emerge naturally in the stochastic interpretation of the dynamics. This approach provides a derivation of a fractional kinetic theory in the classical limit and leads to classical Lévy dynamics.

## Key claim

Stochastic processes, including Levy processes and turbulent diffusion, emerge from the time evolution of a quantum particle coupled to a quantum chaotic environment described by parametric banded random matrix ensembles, with the specific type of stochastic behavior determined by the short-distance decorrelation exponent alpha of the environment's correlation function.

## Method

The authors model the quantum chaotic environment using a parametric banded random matrix ensemble with a Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (GOE) interaction term, where the correlation function G(x) between matrix elements at different positions has short-distance behavior G(x) approximately 1 - c_alpha |x|^alpha. They derive the evolution equation for the test particle's density matrix using influence functional techniques in the weak-coupling limit, then solve for the probability distribution P(X,t) and take the semiclassical limit via Wigner transform to obtain a fractional Fokker-Planck equation.

## Key numbers

- The short-distance decorrelation exponent alpha is restricted to the range 0 < alpha ≤ 2 by Bochner's theorem on positive definite functions
- For alpha = 2 and beta = 0, the mean square displacement is ⟨X^2;t⟩ = sigma^2 + (hbar^2)/(4M^2 sigma^2) t^2 + (Gamma^downarrow hbar)/(3M^2 X_0^2) t^3, where the t^3 term is characteristic of turbulent diffusion
- The Levy process diffusion coefficient a(t) = (Gamma^downarrow)/((alpha+1) hbar) (hbar/(M X_0))^alpha t^(alpha+1)
- The generalized friction coefficient gamma_alpha = (beta Gamma^downarrow hbar alpha)/(2M X_0^alpha)

## Context

This work extends the connection between random matrix theory and stochastic processes by showing that quantum chaotic environments can generate Levy stable laws and fractional kinetic theory, providing a microscopic foundation for phenomenological approaches such as fractional Fokker-Planck equations proposed by Zaslavsky (1994) and others. The derivation bridges quantum chaos (RMT), stochastic processes, and fractional kinetic theory, enabling a better understanding of anomalous diffusion in complex classical and quantum systems. The approach contrasts with earlier work that postulated fractional kinetic equations through scaling arguments without a microscopic underpinning.
