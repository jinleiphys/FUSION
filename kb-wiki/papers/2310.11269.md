# Conservation Laws and Hydrodynamics

> Recent years have seen much development in analyzing the structure of relativistic hydrodynamics. In this proceeding, some of the developments are highlighted including issues related to pseudo-gauge transformations and spin hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while the pseudo-gauge freedom makes local current densities mathematically ambiguous, physically observable fields (like the electromagnetic field or gravitational field) select a unique physical current density; however, identifying the quantum current density remains ambiguous at \(O(\hbar)\), which complicates the formulation of spin hydrodynamics.

## Method

The author reviews the mathematical structure of pseudo-gauge transformations in relativistic hydrodynamics, showing that a conserved current density \(J^{\mu,q}\) can be modified by adding \(\partial_\lambda B^{\lambda\mu,q}\) with an antisymmetric pseudo-gauge potential without changing the conservation law or total charge. The analysis then applies this formalism to the energy-momentum tensor, deriving the Belinfante energy-momentum tensor from the canonical tensor \(\Theta^{\mu\nu}\) and the spin current density \(\mathcal{S}^{\lambda\mu\nu}\) via Eq. (6), and discusses two current approaches to spin hydrodynamics: semi-classical kinetic theory and gradient expansion of the non-symmetric energy-momentum tensor.

## Key numbers

- The spin current density \(\mathcal{S}^{\lambda\mu\nu}\) is of \(O(\hbar)\), as stated in the text: "the anti-symmetric part of the energy-momentum tensor ... is then necessarily of \(O(\hbar)\) since the spin current density is \(O(\hbar)\)."
- The Belinfante energy-momentum tensor is given by \(T^{\mu\nu} = \Theta^{\mu\nu}_{(s)} - \frac{1}{2}\partial_\lambda\left( \mathcal{S}^{\mu\lambda\nu} + \mathcal{S}^{\nu\lambda\mu} \right)\) (Eq. 6), which incorporates spin contributions into the energy density.

## Context

This proceeding extends the long history of hydrodynamics in heavy ion collisions, starting with Landau (1953) and Bjorken (1982), and addresses recent puzzles from the STAR collaboration (2017) regarding \(\Lambda\) polarization measurements that show a surprising azimuthal pattern opposite to the vorticity tensor. The work connects to presentations at the 2022 Strangeness in Quark Matter conference by Palermo (2022), Liao (2021), Buzzegoli (2022), and others, and enables future full spin hydrodynamics simulations needed to resolve the \(\Lambda\) polarization puzzle.
