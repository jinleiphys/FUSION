# Study of $K^-$ absorption at rest in nuclei followed by $p Λ$ emission

> $p\,Λ$ emission in coincidence following $K^-$ absorption at rest in nuclei is studied using quantum mechanical scattering theory and nuclear wave functions. $K^-$ absorption is assumed to occur on two protons in the nucleus. In the formalism, emphasis is put on the study of the final state interaction (FSI) effects of $p$ and $Λ$ with the recoiling nucleus. We include elastic scattering and single nucleon knock-out (KO) channels in the FSI. Calculations are presented for the $^{12}$C nucleus, using shell model wave functions, and without any extra mass modification of the $K^-\,pp$ system in the nucleus. Calculated results are presented for the angular correlation distribution between $p$ and $Λ$, their invariant mass distribution and the momentum spectra of $p$ and $Λ$. These results are compared with the corresponding experimental measurements \cite{agnello}. With only elastic scattering FSI included, the angular correlation distribution and the momentum spectra are found to be in good accord with the corresponding measurements. With full FSI the calculated $p\,Λ$ invariant mass distribution is found to have two peaks, one corresponding to the elastic scattering FSI and another to single nucleon KO FSI. The KO peak agrees fully, in position and shape, with the peak observed in Ref. \cite{agnello}. The peak corresponding to elastic scattering FSI does not seem to exist in the measured distribution. Considering that such a two peak structure is always seen in the inclusive ($p$, $p^\prime $) and ($e$, $e^\prime $) reactions in nuclei at intermediate energies, absence of the elastic scattering peak in the $p\,Λ$ reaction is intriguing.

## Key claim

The observed down-shift in the invariant mass of the \(p\Lambda\) pair from the FINUDA experiment can be reproduced by including single-nucleon knock-out (KO) in the final-state interaction (FSI) of the outgoing proton and \(\Lambda\) with the residual nucleus, without invoking a deeply bound \(K^-pp\) system; however, the calculated invariant mass distribution shows a two-peak structure (elastic scattering peak plus KO peak), whereas the experimental data show only the KO peak, leaving the absence of the elastic peak unexplained.

## Method

The authors used quantum mechanical scattering theory with shell-model wave functions for \(^{12}\)C, assuming \(K^-\) absorption at rest on two protons in the nucleus. The FSI of the outgoing proton and \(\Lambda\) with the recoiling nucleus was treated via the eikonal approximation, including both elastic scattering and single-nucleon knock-out channels; the absorption vertex was taken as a constant factor because the relative momentum \(q\) varies little over the measured kinematic range.

## Key numbers

* Attenuation factor \(\eta_A(T_p)\) for \(^{12}\)C is approximately 0.7 for proton kinetic energies in the 100–200 MeV range relevant to this reaction.
* The Q-value of the \(K^- + pp \rightarrow \Lambda p\) reaction is 317 MeV, leading to outgoing proton and \(\Lambda\) momenta centered around 570 MeV/c.
* The \(K^-\) absorption from the \(2p\) atomic orbit dominates over the \(3d\) orbit by about two orders of magnitude in overlap probability; the \(2p\) orbit contributes \(\sim 7\%\) of the kaon population but its absorption yield is still much larger than that from the \(3d\) orbit.
* For \(^{12}\)C, the binding energies of protons in the \(1p\) and \(1s\) shells are taken as 15.96 MeV and 34.0 MeV, respectively; the oscillator length parameter \(b\) is 1.67 fm.
* The number of absorbing proton pairs in \(^{12}\)C is in the ratio \((1p)^2 : (1s1p) : (1s)^2 = 6 : 8 : 1\).

## Context

This work reexamines the hypothesis of Magas et al. (2008) that the observed \(p\Lambda\) invariant-mass peak in FINUDA data arises from single-nucleon knock-out in the FSI, rather than from a deeply bound \(K^-pp\) state. Unlike the classical intranuclear-cascade simulations of Magas et al., the present calculation uses quantum mechanical scattering theory and realistic shell-model wave functions. The results support the KO explanation for the peak position and shape, but the predicted elastic-scattering peak is absent in the data, which is puzzling because such a two-peak structure is routinely seen in inclusive \((p,p')\) and \((e,e')\) reactions at intermediate energies. This work enables further theoretical study of the reaction dynamics and the role of FSI in interpreting kaon-nucleus bound-state searches.
