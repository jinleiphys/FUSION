# New insight into the N/Z and mass equilibration in heavy-ion collisions

> The dynamics of N/Z and mass equilibration are investigated in the reactions 112,124Sn + 239Pu by employing the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics model. It is found that N/Z and mass equilibration take place at different collision stages. The N/Z relaxation is observed in the approaching phase (from first contact to deepest contact) with a very short time, whereas interestingly we find for the first time that mass equilibration only takes place in the separation phase (from the deepest contact to re-separation), which are explained by investigating the dynamical asymmetry between the approaching and separation phases. The mass equilibration also could be clarified with a dynamical potential energy surface. Our results provide a new insight into the equilibration dynamics of the quantum systems.

## Key claim
The authors claim that N/Z equilibration and mass equilibration in heavy-ion collisions occur at different collision stages, with N/Z relaxation saturating during the approaching phase (before deepest contact) and mass equilibration occurring only during the separation phase (after deepest contact). This finding is conditional on the specific reactions studied (112,124Sn + 239Pu) and the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model used.

## Method
The authors employed the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate the reactions 112,124Sn + 239Pu near the Coulomb barrier. They analyzed the time evolution of the N/Z ratio and mass asymmetry by tracking the projectile-like fragment (PLF) and target-like fragment (TLF) using a window plane segmentation method, and calculated nucleon drift coefficients to quantify the net transfer of protons and neutrons during the approaching and separation phases. A dynamical potential energy surface (DyPES) was constructed as a function of relative distance and mass asymmetry to explain the mass equilibration mechanism.

## Context
This work extends previous studies on N/Z equilibration in deep inelastic collisions (DIC) by Kratz (1977), Jedele (2017), and Umar (2017), which typically analyzed equilibration based on contact period rather than evolution time. The finding that mass equilibration only occurs in the separation phase contradicts the common assumption that statistical mechanisms govern mass equilibration throughout the collision, as proposed by Schroder (1977). The results enable a more precise understanding of equilibration dynamics in quantum systems and suggest that characteristic times should be evaluated from evolution time rather than contact period in both experimental and theoretical studies.
