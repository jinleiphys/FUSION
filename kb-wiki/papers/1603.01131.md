# $X(5568)$ and and its partner states

> Stimulated by the recent observation of the $X(5568)$, we study the $X(5568)$ and its partners under the tetraquark scenario. In the framework of the color-magnetic interaction, we estimate the masses of the partner states of the $X(5568)$ and discuss their decay pattern, which provide valuable information on the future experimental search of these states.

## Key claim

Under the tetraquark scenario using a color-magnetic interaction model, the observed $X(5568)$ can be accommodated as a tetraquark candidate, but the authors note that the state corresponding to $X(5568)$ is not the most tightly bound tetraquark in their framework; the analysis is conditional on the signal being real, as the LHCb collaboration was unable to confirm it.

## Method

The authors adopted a chromomagnetic interaction (CMI) model with a contact interaction Hamiltonian to estimate masses of tetraquark states with quark content $qq\bar{q}\bar{b}$. They constructed flavor, color, and spin wave functions for four different diquark configurations (classified by the symmetry of the two light quarks), calculated the CMI matrix elements, and extracted the model parameters from known ground-state baryon and meson mass splittings. The tetraquark masses were then estimated by adding the CMI contribution to the sum of constituent quark masses.

## Key numbers

- The $X(5568)$ has mass $m = 5567.8 \pm 2.9(\text{stat})^{+0.9}_{-1.9}(\text{syst})$ MeV and width $\Gamma = 21.9 \pm 6.4(\text{stat})^{+5.0}_{-2.5}(\text{syst})$ MeV, as reported by the D0 Collaboration.
- In case (1), the $B_{s,\bar{6}}$ state with $J=0$ (corresponding to $X(5568)$) is estimated at 5619 MeV, which is 51 MeV higher than the observed mass.
- In case (2), the $B_{s,\bar{6}}$ state with $J=1$ is estimated at 5518 MeV, which is 49 MeV lower than the observed $X(5568)$.
- The most attractive CMI in case (1) occurs for $B_{\bar{s},\bar{6}}$ (J=0) with CMI = -166 MeV; in case (2), the largest CMI (-300 MeV) occurs for the $B^\ell$ state with $J=0$.
- Quark masses used: $m_q = 310$ MeV, $m_s = 450$ MeV, $m_c = 1430$ MeV, $m_b = 4630$ MeV.
- The $BK$ threshold is around 5774 MeV; the $B\pi$ threshold is 5420 MeV; the $B_s\pi$ threshold is 5506 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the tetraquark interpretation previously applied to the $D_{sJ}(2632)$ signal by Liu et al. (2004) to the bottom sector following the D0 Collaboration's observation of $X(5568)$. The authors contrast their compact tetraquark approach with molecular interpretations: Liu et al. (2009) found that the Weinberg-Tomozawa term for $\bar{B}K$ interaction in the isovector sector is zero, and Zhang et al. (2006) found no S-wave $\bar{B}K$ bound state solution via $\rho$ and $\omega$ exchange. The results enable experimental searches for partner states within the same SU(3) flavor multiplet, particularly in $B\pi$, $B^*\pi$, and $B_s\pi$ channels for bottom tetraquarks, and in $D_s\pi$, $D_s^*\pi$, and isovector $\bar{D}\bar{K}$ channels for charmed partners.
