# Extracting Meson Distribution Amplitudes from Nonlocal Euclidean Correlations at Next-to-Next-to-Leading Order

> We present the first complete result for the next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) hard matching kernel indispensable for a precision extraction of light meson distribution amplitudes from lattice calculations of equal-time nonlocal Euclidean correlation functions. The results are given in both coordinate and momentum space, with the renormalization and matching accomplished in a state-of-the-art scheme. Our results can be used in both large-momentum effective theory and short-distance factorization approaches. Notably, our coordinate space kernel is directly applicable to nonsinglet quark unpolarized and helicity generalized parton distributions as well. We also illustrate the numerical impact of the NNLO matching, using the pion distribution amplitude as an example.

## Key claim

The authors present the first complete next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) hard matching kernel for extracting light meson distribution amplitudes from lattice calculations of equal-time nonlocal Euclidean correlation functions, with the caveat that the results are applicable only at short distances where the factorization formula holds and higher-twist contributions of order \( \mathcal{O}(z_{12}^2 \Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2) \) are suppressed.

## Method

The authors compute two-loop QCD corrections to the leading-twist quasi-light-front operator in coordinate space using dimensional regularization with \( d = 4 - 2\epsilon \). They employ the ratio renormalization scheme at short distances and the hybrid scheme for large distances, then obtain the momentum-space matching kernel via Fourier transform. The renormalized result in the \( \overline{\rm MS} \) scheme is derived by applying renormalization constants for the strong coupling, UV divergences, and the lightcone operator, with the cancellation of all \( 1/\epsilon^2 \) and \( 1/\epsilon \) poles serving as a consistency check.

## Key numbers

* The NNLO contribution to the coordinate-space matching amounts to approximately 30% of the NLO correction, evaluated at scale \( \mu = 2 \) GeV with \( z = 0.2 \) fm.
* The NNLO correction in momentum space introduces modifications of about 10% relative to the NLO prediction, using lattice data at \( P_z = 2.15 \) GeV with \( \mu = 2 \) GeV varied from 2 to 4 GeV.
* The renormalization scale is varied from 2 GeV to 4 GeV to estimate systematic uncertainty in both coordinate and momentum space analyses.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative matching kernel for light meson distribution amplitudes from next-to-leading order (NLO), previously available from Ji (2015), Liu (2018), Yao (2022), and Ma (2022), to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). The results enable precision extractions of pion and kaon distribution amplitudes from lattice QCD calculations using either large-momentum effective theory (LaMET) or short-distance factorization approaches, and are directly applicable to nonsinglet quark unpolarized and helicity generalized parton distributions. The coordinate-space kernel reproduces the NNLO unpolarized quark PDF matching from Li (2020), providing a cross-check of the calculation.
