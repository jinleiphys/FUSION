# The Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum-rule with nuclear targets

> Hadron properties are modified when the hadron is embedded in a nuclear medium. Here we discuss the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn, GDH, sum rule for polarised photoproduction from a polarised nucleon within a polarised nuclear target. Strong enhancement is expected with the suppression of the proton and nucleon resonance masses and enhancement of the proton's anomalous magnetic moment in medium. This could be tested in polarised photoproduction experiments with interesting targets being polarised deuterons, $^3$He, $^6$Li and $^7$Li. The largest contribution to the GDH sum rule comes from the $Î”$ resonance excitation. In existing data with polarised deuterons and $^3$He the $Î”$ excitation is shifted to slightly lower energy when compared to model predictions where the $Î”$ is treated with its free mass.

## Key claim

The Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule for bound nucleons in polarised nuclear targets is expected to be enhanced by up to a factor of about two at nuclear matter density, with existing data from deuteron and $^3$He targets showing a shift of the $\Delta$ resonance excitation to lower incident photon energies by up to approximately -20 MeV, which is qualitatively different from the smearing expected from Fermi motion.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework of the GDH sum rule for polarised photoproduction from polarised nuclear targets, discussing how medium modifications of nucleon and $\Delta$ resonance masses, as well as nucleon magnetic moments, affect both sides of the sum rule. They analyse existing inclusive photoproduction data from Mainz (MAMI) and Bonn (ELSA) for proton, deuteron, and $^3$He targets, comparing the measured helicity-dependent cross sections $\sigma_{\rm p} - \sigma_{\rm a}$ and the individual spin cross sections $\sigma_{\rm p}$ and $\sigma_{\rm a}$ to model predictions that include Fermi motion smearing.

## Key numbers

- The free proton GDH integral from threshold to infinity is measured as $+209 \pm 13\ \mu$b, combining data from 0.2 to 2.9 GeV ($+253.5 \pm 5 \pm 12\ \mu$b) with model estimates for the near-threshold region ($-29.5 \pm 2\ \mu$b) and a high-energy Regge estimate ($-15 \pm 2\ \mu$b).
- The deuteron GDH integral from 0.2 to 1.8 GeV is measured as $+452 \pm 9 \pm 24\ \mu$b, compared to the free-space sum of proton and neutron GDH integrals of $+437\ \mu$b.
- The $\Delta$ resonance peak in the spin-dependent cross section for deuteron and $^3$He targets is shifted to lower incident photon energies by up to approximately $-20$ MeV relative to model predictions using free $\Delta$ masses.
- Chiral models predict a $\Delta$ mass shift in medium of $-33 \times \rho/\rho_0$ MeV, with the proton mass shift about 1.5 times larger (range -50 to -40 MeV at nuclear matter density).
- The effective nucleon polarisations for $^3$He are $P_n = 0.876$ and $P_p = -0.052$; for $^6$Li, $P_p = P_n = 0.848$; for $^7$Li, $P_p = 0.868$ and $P_n = -0.038$.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of medium modifications of hadron properties, previously studied in the context of the EMC effect (Cloet, 2019) and the Bjorken sum rule (Ericson, 1998), to the GDH sum rule for polarised photoproduction. The authors identify a possible shift of the $\Delta$ excitation in existing deuteron data (Ahrens, 2009) and $^3$He data (Aguar Bartolome, 2013) that is not explained by Fermi motion smearing models (Arenhoevel, 2004) or the $\Delta$-hole model (Oset, 1981). The paper motivates future experiments with heavier polarised targets such as $^6$Li and $^7$Li to study the $A$ dependence of the effect and to test predictions of enhanced GDH integrals at nuclear matter density.
