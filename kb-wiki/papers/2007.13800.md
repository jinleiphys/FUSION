# Parton distribution functions from lattice QCD using Bayes-Gauss-Fourier transforms

> We present a new method, based on Gaussian process regression, for reconstructing the continuous $x$-dependence of parton distribution functions (PDFs) from quasi-PDFs computed using lattice QCD. We examine the origin of the unphysical oscillations seen in current lattice calculations of quasi-PDFs and develop a nonparametric fitting approach to take the required Fourier transform. The method is tested on one ensemble of maximally twisted mass fermions with two light quarks. We find that with our approach oscillations of the quasi-PDF are drastically reduced. However, the final effect on the light-cone PDFs is small. This finding suggests that the deviation seen between current lattice QCD results and phenomenological determinations cannot be attributed solely on the Fourier transform.

# Parton distribution functions from lattice QCD using Bayes-Gauss-Fourier transforms

## Key claim
The unphysical oscillations seen in lattice quasi-PDF calculations are drastically reduced by replacing the discrete Fourier transform with a continuous Fourier transform based on Gaussian process regression; however, the final effect on the light-cone PDFs is small, suggesting that the deviation between current lattice QCD results and phenomenological determinations cannot be attributed solely to the Fourier transform procedure.

## Method
The authors apply Gaussian process regression (GPR) to reconstruct a continuous form of the renormalized lattice matrix element as a function of Wilson line length z, then compute its Fourier transform analytically (Bayes-Gauss-Fourier transform, BGFT). The method is tested on one ensemble of maximally twisted mass fermions with two light quarks (cA2.09.48 ensemble) at momentum P3 = 10π/L ≈ 1.38 GeV, using the squared exponential covariance function with hyperparameters tuned via maximum likelihood estimation type II. The approach exploits the Hermitian property of the matrix element and the observed linear behavior of its phase φ(z) to improve stability.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing a = 0.0938(3)(2) fm, lattice size 48³ × 96, spatial extent L = 4.5 fm
- Nucleon momentum P3 = 10π/L ≈ 1.38 GeV
- Source-sink time separation ts = 12a ≈ 1.1 fm
- Nconf = 811 gauge configurations analyzed with Nmeasures = 73000 total measurements
- Pion mass mπ = 0.1304(4) GeV, nucleon mass mN = 0.932(4) GeV
- Net quark number from NNPDF3.1 phenomenological unpolarized PDF: I(PDF) ≈ 1.02, with upper bound I(PDF+σ) ≈ 1.61
- Real part of matrix element at z=0 from NNPDF3.1 at P3=2.75 GeV: Re[h(z=0)] = 0.99(8)

## Context
This work extends the LaMET-based lattice QCD approach to PDFs pioneered by Ji (2013) and implemented by Lin (2014), Alexandrou (2015, 2016), and others. It directly addresses the unphysical oscillations identified in Alexandrou (2019) as an open systematic issue in quasi-PDF calculations. The BGFT method differs from the approach of Ambrogioni (2017) by using maximum likelihood estimation type II to tune hyperparameters rather than fitting a spectral density model to DFT data. The finding that the Fourier transform is not the dominant source of discrepancy redirects attention to other systematic effects such as higher-twist contributions, renormalization, and the need for larger nucleon boosts.
