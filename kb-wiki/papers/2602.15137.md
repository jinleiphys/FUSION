# Back-to-back dijet production in DIS at arbitrary Bjorken-x: TMD gluon distributions to twist-3 accuracy

> We derive the gluon transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) operator structure of back-to-back quark-antiquark dijet production in deep inelastic scattering at arbitrary Bjorken-x to twist-3 accuracy. Working at leading order in the strong coupling and in the kinematic regime where the transverse momentum imbalance of the jets is much smaller than their individual transverse momenta, we perform a systematic gradient expansion of the quark propagator in a background gluon field. This expansion organizes multiple interactions with the target in terms of longitudinal Wilson lines and gauge-invariant field-strength insertions, yielding a TMD description valid beyond the strict high-energy eikonal (x -> 0) approximation. We obtain explicit cross sections for longitudinally and transversely polarized virtual photons, identifying all contributing gluon TMD operators up to twist-3, including structures involving F_+-, F_ij, and three-gluon correlators. The full longitudinal phase associated with Bjorken-x is retained throughout. In the small-x limit, our results reproduce the known sub-eikonal expressions obtained in the Color Glass Condensate framework, establishing a direct connection between the general-x TMD expansion and high-energy factorization. We further reduce the operator basis using equations of motion, minimizing the number of independent nonperturbative matrix elements entering the cross section. This work provides a systematic foundation for extending TMD analyses of dijet production beyond leading twist, establishing a unified operator framework valid at arbitrary Bjorken x that smoothly interpolates between moderate- and small-x descriptions of gluon TMDs.

## Key claim

The authors derive a systematic operator expansion for gluon transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) distributions in back-to-back quark-antiquark dijet production in deep inelastic scattering that is valid at arbitrary Bjorken-x, not only in the strict eikonal (x → 0) limit, and they show that in the small-x limit their results reproduce known sub-eikonal expressions from the Color Glass Condensate framework.

## Method

The authors work at leading order in the strong coupling and in the kinematic regime where the transverse momentum imbalance of the jets is much smaller than their individual transverse momenta. They perform a systematic gradient expansion of the quark propagator in a background gluon field using the background field method, organizing multiple interactions with the target in terms of longitudinal Wilson lines and gauge-invariant field-strength insertions. They retain the full longitudinal phase factor e^{ixP^+z^-} associated with Bjorken-x throughout, and they reduce the operator basis using equations of motion to minimize the number of independent nonperturbative matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- The calculation is performed at leading order in the strong coupling α_s, with the kinematic condition that the transverse momentum imbalance |k_{1⊥}+k_{2⊥}| is much smaller than the individual jet momenta k_{1,2⊥} ~ P_⊥
- The virtual photon virtuality is Q^2 ≡ -q^2, and the hard scale is set by the relative transverse momentum P_⊥ of the quark-antiquark pair
- The expansion parameter for the back-to-back limit is Δ_⊥/P_⊥, where Δ_⊥ ≡ k_{1⊥}+k_{2⊥} ≪ P_⊥
- At realistic EIC kinematics, accessible values of x are typically ≳ 10^{-2} at perturbatively large k_{1⊥}^2 ~ k_{2⊥}^2
- Leading sub-eikonal corrections are suppressed by 1/k_{1,2⊥}^2

## Context

This work extends the TMD factorization framework recently proposed by Mukherjee, Skokov, Tarasov, and Tiwari (2023, 2025), referred to as the MSTT framework, which is based on the background field method of Abbott (1980). It builds on earlier studies of dijet production in DIS within the soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) framework at large-x by del Castillo et al. (2020) and within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory by Dominguez et al. (2011), Boussarie et al. (2021), Caucal et al. (2021, 2023), and Altinoluk et al. (2022, 2024). The results establish a unified operator framework that smoothly interpolates between moderate-x and small-x descriptions of gluon TMDs, enabling future phenomenological applications at the Electron-Ion Collider.
