# LAQGSM03.03 Upgrade and its Validation

> This paper presents part of an internal LANL Progress Report on LAQGSM03.03, an upgrade of the Los Alamos version of the Quark-Gluon String Model event generator for MCNPX/6 and MARS15 transport codes and on its validation and testing against a large variety of recent measurements. We present here an analysis with LAQGSM03.03 of the recent PHENIX mid-rapidity spectra of pi+, pi-, K+, K-, p, and p-bar produced in ultra-relativistic p + p interactions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV; GSI cross sections for the fragmentation of Pb208 at 1 GeV/nucleon on Be9; fragmentation cross sections of Si28 on H, C, Al, Cu, Sn, and Pb at energies from 290 to 1200 MeV/nucleon measred recently at HIMAC and BNL; recent HIMAC data on B, Be, Li, and He production cross sections from fragmentation of C12 on H, C, Al, Cu, Sn, and Pb at 290 and 400 MeV/nucleon; BNL data on fragmentation cross sections of Fe56 on H, C, Al, Cu, and Pb targets at 1.05 GeV/nucleon; recent pi+ and pi- spectra from 6.4, 12.3, and 17.5 GeV/c p + Be9 from the E910 BNL measurements; and fragmentation cross sections of Ca40, Ca48, Ni58, and Ni64 on Be9 and Ta181 at 140 MeV/nucleon, and of Kr86 at 64 MeV/nucleon on the same targets measured recently at NSCL-MSU and RARF-RIKEN, respectively.

## Key claim

LAQGSM03.03, the latest upgrade of the Los Alamos Quark-Gluon String Model event generator, describes a wide variety of nuclear reactions from approximately 10 MeV/nucleon to about 1 TeV/nucleon with generally good agreement against recent experimental data, but the authors acknowledge that the agreement is "good but not perfect" and that "there is room for future improvements."

## Method

The code models nuclear reactions as a three-stage process: an intra-nuclear cascade (INC) stage using an improved version of the Dubna Cascade Model, followed by preequilibrium emission described by the Modified Exciton Model, and finally evaporation or fission using an extended version of Furihata's GEM2 code. For very light excited nuclei (A < 13), the code uses a Fermi breakup model, and for high-energy reactions it employs a coalescence model to form composite particles from emitted cascade nucleons. The upgrade specifically modified the INC for energies above 20 GeV/nucleon by incorporating new fits to evaluated cross sections and introducing an energy-dependent transverse momentum parameter for constituent quarks.

## Key numbers

- For p + p interactions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV (T_p approximately 21314 GeV), the transverse momentum parameter sigma_perp must be increased from 0.51 GeV/c (used at T_p <= 200 GeV) to 2.0 GeV/c to describe the data properly
- The coalescence model uses momentum cutoffs of 90 MeV/c for deuterons, 108 MeV/c for tritons and He-3, and 115 MeV/c for He-4
- In a test of 10^7 simulated inelastic events for 1 GeV/nucleon Pb-208 + Be-9, the previous version LAQGSM03.02 produced 60 unstable light fragments (one H-5, twenty-three H-6, one Li-5, thirty Be-6, one Be-13, and four B-6), representing less than 0.0006% of the total yield
- Fragmentation cross sections were validated for Si-28 on H, C, Al, Cu, Sn, and Pb at energies from 290 to 1200 MeV/nucleon
- Fragmentation cross sections were validated for Fe-56 on H, C, Al, Cu, and Pb targets at 1.05 GeV/nucleon
- Pion spectra were validated for p + Be-9 at beam momenta of 6.4, 12.3, and 17.5 GeV/c
- Fragmentation cross sections were validated for Ca-40, Ca-48, Ni-58, and Ni-64 on Be-9 and Ta-181 at 140 MeV/nucleon, and for Kr-86 at 64 MeV/nucleon on the same targets

## Context

This work extends the Los Alamos version of the Quark-Gluon String Model (LAQGSM) series, building on earlier versions such as LAQGSM03.02 and the Cascade-Exciton Model CEM03.01/CEM03.02 developed by the same group for applications including accelerator transmutation of waste, spallation neutron sources, and NASA space radiation studies. The code is being incorporated into the MARS15, MCNP6, and MCNPX transport codes. The validation against recent data from PHENIX, GSI, HIMAC, BNL, NSCL-MSU, and RARF-RIKEN enables improved predictions for nuclear fragmentation, pion production, and heavy-ion reactions needed for space radiation protection, cancer therapy with carbon ions, and rare isotope production. The authors compare their results against phenomenological systematics EPAX2, the NUCFRG2 transport code, the PHITS code, and the Abrasion-Ablation model as implemented in LISE++, noting that LAQGSM03.03 describes the data better than these other models for the Mocko (2006, 2007) fragmentation measurements.
