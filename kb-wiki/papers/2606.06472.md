# When positive and negative pairs differ in femtoscopy: residual Coulomb and isospin effects

> We study charge-dependent modifications of identical-pion and identical-kaon femtoscopic correlation functions from two sources: the residual Coulomb field of the charged source and isospin-related hadronic dynamics. The residual Coulomb effect is modeled with a modified Retiere--Lisa blast-wave source, where the same emitted particles are propagated with positive and negative charge signs through an effective residual field. The residual Coulomb field produces a small but systematic positive-to-negative splitting of the correlation functions, strongest at low $k_T$ and sensitive to the effective charge, spatial width, and expansion velocity of the residual source. It also modifies the height and shape of the correlation function, complicating interpretation of the fitted radii. UrQMD 3.4 cascade calculations for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7~\mathrm{GeV}$ show that charge-dependent splittings can also appear even without a residual Coulomb field. For pions, this difference is mainly driven by the initial isospin composition, while for kaons it is strongly affected by different $K^+$ and $K^-$ production mechanisms and subsequent hadronic evolution. These results show that residual Coulomb and isospin-related effects can compete, and neither can be interpreted reliably without constraining the other within the same model framework.

## Key claim
Residual Coulomb fields from the charged source and isospin-related hadronic dynamics both produce charge-dependent splittings in identical-pion and identical-kaon femtoscopic correlation functions, and these two effects can compete; neither can be reliably interpreted without constraining the other within the same model framework.

## Method
The authors model the residual Coulomb effect using a modified Retiere--Lisa blast-wave source, where the same emitted particles are propagated with positive and negative charge signs through an effective, time-dependent Gaussian residual field. They also perform UrQMD 3.4 cascade calculations for Au+Au collisions at a fixed energy to study isospin-related effects. The resulting correlation functions for positive and negative pairs are compared to isolate the charge-dependent distortions.

## Key numbers
- The residual Coulomb effect is modeled with an effective net positive charge of the residual source set to \(Z_{\mathrm{res}}=60\) as a baseline for a central heavy-ion collision.
- The default initial width of the residual charge distribution is \(\sigma_0=5.0~\mathrm{fm}\), corresponding to an rms radius of \(\sqrt{3}\,\sigma_0 \approx 8.7~\mathrm{fm}\).
- The residual charge distribution expands with time at a default rate of \(v_{\mathrm{exp}}=0.3\) (in units where \(c=1\)).
- The UrQMD calculations are performed for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7~\mathrm{GeV}\).
- The Coulomb force is integrated up to a maximum time \(t_{\max}=30~\mathrm{fm}/c\) with a base time step of \(\Delta t=0.01~\mathrm{fm}/c\).

## Context
This work extends previous studies on third-body Coulomb effects in femtoscopy (Barz 1996, Barz 1997, Shoppa 1998, HADES 2019) by modeling the distortion of the correlation function itself rather than just the fitted HBT radii. It also connects to studies of isospin effects on pion production ratios (FOPI 2006, SpiRIT 2021). The results enable a more reliable interpretation of charge-dependent femtoscopic signals by demonstrating that residual Coulomb and isospin effects must be constrained simultaneously within a single model framework.
