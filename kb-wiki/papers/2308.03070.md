# The effect of initial nuclear deformation on dielectron photoproduction in hadronic heavy-ion collisions

> Significant excesses of $e^+e^-$ pair production at very low transverse momentum ($p_T <$ 0.15 GeV/c) were observed by the STAR collaboration in hadronic heavy-ion collisions. Such enhancement is assumed to be a sign of photon-photon production in heavy-ion collisions with hadronic overlap, based on comparisons with model calculations for spherical Au + Au collisions. However, there is a lack of calculations for $e^+e^-$ pair production from coherent photon-photon interactions in hadronic U + U collisions, due to the deformity of Uranium nuclei. In this article, we present calculations for $e^+e^-$ pair photoproduction at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 193 GeV in both spherical and deformed U + U collisions within STAR detector acceptance using the equivalent photon approximation (EPA). We conduct event-by-event analysis to investigate the effects of initial nuclear deformation on pair production. Our numerical results show good agreement with experimental data for the 40%--60% and 60%--80% centrality classes in U + U collisions, and the differences between spherical and deformed configurations are approximately 3\%. We also calculate the yields of the photoproduced $e^+e^-$ pair in hadronic deformed Ru + Ru and Zr + Zr collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV. The results reveal that the ratios of the yields of Ru to Zr exhibit very small differences ($<$ 1%) between spherical and deformed cases.

## Key claim
The observed excess of low-pT dielectrons in hadronic heavy-ion collisions can be explained by coherent photon-photon interactions even when initial nuclear deformation is taken into account, with the difference between spherical and deformed configurations being only about 3% for U+U collisions and less than 1% for the ratio of Ru+Ru to Zr+Zr yields.

## Method
The authors used the equivalent photon approximation (EPA) combined with the Glauber model to calculate dielectron photoproduction in hadronic heavy-ion collisions. They performed event-by-event calculations for randomly oriented deformed nuclei by dividing the unit sphere into 400 bins, yielding 160000 collision configurations, and computed the photon flux using nuclear form factors obtained from Fourier transforms of deformed Woods-Saxon charge distributions. The centrality definition for deformed collisions was based on the cumulative distribution of the number of participating nucleons (Npart) rather than impact parameter alone.

## Key numbers
- The difference between spherical and deformed configurations in U+U collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 193 GeV is approximately 3%
- The ratio of dielectron yields between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV shows less than 1% difference between spherical and deformed cases
- In tip-tip U+U collisions, the dielectron yield is approximately 25% higher than in spherical collisions
- In body-body U+U collisions, the dielectron yield is approximately 10% lower than in spherical collisions
- The deformed configuration yields approximately 5% increases compared to the spherical case in Ru+Ru collisions
- The calculations cover the invariant mass region 0.4-2.6 GeV/c^2 and the STAR acceptance (p_T^e > 0.2 GeV/c, |eta^e| < 1, |y^ee| < 1)

## Context
This work extends previous calculations by Zha (2018) and Klein (2018) for spherical Au+Au collisions to deformed uranium and isobaric collisions, addressing the lack of calculations for U+U collisions noted by the STAR collaboration (2018). The results confirm that the low-pT dielectron excess observed by STAR can be attributed to photon-photon interactions even with nuclear deformation, and the negligible effect on Ru/Zr ratios enables future studies of photoproduction in isobaric collisions without needing precise deformation knowledge.
