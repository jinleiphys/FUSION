# Procedure for measuring particle's circular polarization variation with respect to the reaction plane in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The EM field pattern created by spectators in relativistic heavy-ion collisions plants a seed of positive (negative) magnetic helicity in the hemisphere above (below) the reaction plane. Owing to the chiral anomaly, the magnetic helicity interacts with the fermionic helicity of the collision system, and causes photons emitted in upper- and lower-hemispheres to have different preferences in the circular polarization. In this paper, we lay down a procedure to measure the variation of the circular polarization w.r.t the reaction plane in relativistic heavy-ion collisions for massless photons, as well as similar polarization patterns for vector mesons decaying into two daughters. We propose to study the yield differentially and compare the yield between upper- and lower-hemispheres in order to identify and quantify such effects.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a procedure to measure the variation of photon and vector meson circular polarization with respect to the reaction plane in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, but the actual signal strength depends on the competition between the magnetic helicity effect and dilution from background sources such as \(\pi_0\) decays, which must be corrected for on an experiment-by-experiment basis.

## Method

The authors derive a parametrization for the cross-section of photon conversion into \(e^+e^-\) pairs, where the yield is modulated by a term \(H \sin\zeta \sin\phi\) (with \(\zeta\) the electron-positron opening angle and \(\phi\) the photon azimuthal angle relative to the reaction plane). For vector mesons decaying into two daughters (e.g., \(J/\psi \to e^+e^-\)), they introduce an asymmetry parameter \(A\) that modulates the left- and right-circular polarization contributions as a function of \(\phi\), and derive the probability distribution \(y(\phi,\beta)\) in the helicity frame. The signal is extracted by taking the yield difference between upper and lower hemispheres (\(\Delta Y_{\rm up-down}\)), which isolates the polarization asymmetry while canceling symmetric backgrounds.

## Key numbers

- The photon cross-section modulation parameter \(H \equiv ab\) lies in the range \(H \in [0,1]\), where \(a\) (from pair production) and \(b\) (from magnetic helicity modulation) are both in \([0,1]\).
- For vector mesons, the asymmetry parameter \(A\) is defined in \([0,1]\), with \(x = A \sin\phi\) representing the fraction of right-circular polarization.
- The yield difference for \(J/\psi\) between upper and lower hemispheres is given by \(\Delta Y_{\rm up-down}^{J/\psi} = \frac{A}{4\pi}(3-2v_2)(1+\lambda_\beta)\cos\beta\), where \(v_2\) is the elliptic flow coefficient and \(\lambda_\beta \in [-1,1]\) is the polarization parameter.
- For the more general case including reaction-plane dependence of \(\lambda_\beta\), the expression becomes \(\Delta Y_{\rm up-down}^{J/\psi} = \frac{3-2v_2}{8\pi}[2A(1+\lambda_\beta^0)\cos\beta + \lambda_\beta^0 u(1+3\cos 2\beta)]\), with \(u\) a new parameter.

## Context

This work extends theoretical predictions by Hirono, Kharzeev, and Yin (2014), Tuchin (2015), Manuel (2015), and McLerran (2014) that magnetic helicity from the chiral anomaly can produce opposite circular polarizations in photons above and below the reaction plane. It also builds on earlier work by Wang (2005) on global quark polarization and by Ipp (2008) on photon polarization from vorticity. The proposed measurement procedure enables experimental tests at RHIC and the LHC, using existing techniques for anisotropic flow analysis (Poskanzer and Voloshin, 1998) and vector meson polarization measurements (Faccioli, 2010, 2011).
