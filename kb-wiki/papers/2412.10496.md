# On the negative coupling O(N) model in 2d at high temperature

> In this work, I consider N-component scalar quantum field theory in two dimensions interacting with an upside-down quartic potential. Working in the large N limit, the model can be solved non-perturbatively using the saddle-point method for sufficiently strong negative coupling. At high temperature, the O(N) model dimensionally reduces to ${\cal PT}$-symmetric quantum mechanics, for which powerful non-perturbative solution methods exist. It is found that the solution from quantum mechanics can be matched by the saddle-point method in quantum field theory when allowing for saddles beyond the principal Riemann sheet. I show that saddle points on non-principal Riemann sheets lead to a fully consistent solution of the 2d negative-coupling O(N) model for all temperatures.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the high-temperature limit of the negative-coupling O(N) model in two dimensions can be consistently solved by allowing saddle-point solutions on non-principal Riemann sheets, which resolves the problem of complex free energies and complex masses that plague the principal-sheet solutions; the correct physical free energy is real and given by the saddle that is analytically connected to the non-principal root of the dimensionally reduced quantum mechanics.

## Method

The author studies the Euclidean O(N) model with an upside-down quartic potential (negative coupling) in the large N limit, using the saddle-point method for the auxiliary field. At high temperature, the theory dimensionally reduces to PT-symmetric quantum mechanics, which is solved by rewriting the path integral via a field transformation that yields a Hermitian equivalent Hamiltonian with a quartic-plus-linear potential. The saddle-point condition in the original 2d field theory is analytically continued beyond the principal Riemann sheet by replacing \(\sqrt{iz_0}\to m\) and using an alternative representation of the thermal sum, allowing identification of a real-valued saddle \(m_2\) that connects to the high-temperature quantum-mechanical ground state.

## Key numbers

* The critical coupling for the zero-temperature saddle-point degeneracy is \(g_{\rm crit} \equiv \Lambda_{\overline{\rm MS}}^2 \pi e^1\).
* At zero temperature and \(g = g_{\rm crit}\), the free energy density is \(\Omega[\bar z_{-}] = N e \Lambda_{\overline{\rm MS}}^2 / (16\pi)\).
* In the high-temperature limit, the saddle-point solutions are \(i\bar z_\pm = (-2 T g)^{2/3} = e^{\pm 2 i\pi/3} (2 T g)^{2/3}\) on the principal sheet, while the non-principal sheet gives \(m = -(2 T g)^{1/3}\).
* The large-N ground state energy of the dimensionally reduced quantum mechanics is \(E_0 = -3 N (2g)^{1/3} / 8\).
* For \(N=1\), the ground state energy in units of \(g^{1/3}\) is \(E_0/(N g^{1/3}) = 0.9305\); for \(N=3\) it is approximately 0; for \(N=10^3\) it is \(-0.4709\).
* The high-temperature free energy density at large N is \(\Omega = -N \pi T^2/6 - 3 N (2g)^{1/3} T^{4/3}/8 + \ldots\).

## Context

This work extends the study of negative-coupling scalar field theories, which are asymptotically free in four dimensions (Symanzik, 1973) but develop complex saddle points at high temperature (Romatschke, 2022; Weller, 2023). It directly addresses the failure of the ABS conjecture (Ai, 2022) for interpreting complex free energies, as previously shown in quantum mechanics (Lawrence, 2023; Kamata, 2023). The resolution via non-principal Riemann sheets enables a consistent thermodynamic and dynamic (NLO stability) description for all temperatures, and the results can be tested by lattice simulations (Romatschke, 2023; Giachello, 2024).
