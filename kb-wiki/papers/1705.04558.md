# Strong Diffusion Effect of Charm Quarks on $J/ψ$ Production in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC

> We study the $J/ψ$ production based on coalescence model at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 and 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions. With the colliding energy increasing from 2.76 TeV to 5.02 TeV, the number of charm pairs is enhanced by more than 50%. However, the ratio of $J/ψ$ inclusive nuclear modification factors $R^{5.02TeV}_{AA}/R^{2.76TeV}_{AA}$ is only about 1.1 $\sim$ 1.2. We find that the regeneration of $J/ψ$ is proportional to the densities of charm and anti-charm quarks, instead of their total numbers. The charm quark density is diluted by the strong expansion of quark gluon plasma, which suppresses the combination probability of heavy quarks and $J/ψ$ regeneration. This effect is more important in higher colliding energies where QGP expansion is strong. We also propose the ratio $N_{J/ψ}/(N_D)^2$ as a measurement of $c$ and $\bar c$ coalescence probability, which is only affected by the heavy quark diffusions in QGP, and does not depend on the inputs such as cold nuclear matter effects and cross sections of charm quark production. Further more, we give the predictions at the energy of Future Circular Collider ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 39 TeV).

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed ratio of $J/\psi$ inclusive nuclear modification factors between 5.02 TeV and 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions is only about 1.1–1.2, despite a more than 50% increase in charm pair production, because the strong expansion of the quark-gluon plasma at higher energies dilutes the spatial density of charm quarks and suppresses their coalescence probability.

## Method

The authors employ a coalescence model in which $J/\psi$ mesons are formed from charm and anti-charm quarks on the hadronization hypersurface at the critical temperature $T_c$, using the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation with a Cornell potential to obtain the $J/\psi$ wave function and its Wigner function. The space-time evolution of the QGP is simulated with 2+1 dimensional ideal hydrodynamics assuming Bjorken longitudinal expansion, and charm quarks are assumed to reach kinetic thermalization at the initial time $\tau_0$ and follow a conservation equation $\partial_\mu (\rho_c u^\mu)=0$ that governs their spatial diffusion. The inclusive nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ is computed from the coalescence yield plus a 10% contribution from B-hadron decays, using measured pp cross sections for $J/\psi$ and charm pairs.

## Key numbers

- The number of charm pairs increases by more than 50% when the collision energy rises from $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV to 5.02 TeV.
- The ratio of inclusive nuclear modification factors $R_{AA}^{5.02\text{ TeV}} / R_{AA}^{2.76\text{ TeV}}$ is only about 1.1–1.2 in semi-central and central collisions.
- Initial maximum QGP temperatures in central collisions: $T_0^{2.76\text{ TeV}}(\tau_0) = 485$ MeV, $T_0^{5.02\text{ TeV}}(\tau_0) = 510$ MeV, and $T_0^{39\text{ TeV}}(\tau_0) = 650$ MeV, all at $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm/c.
- Charm pair production cross sections in pp collisions at forward rapidity: $d\sigma_{pp}^{c\bar c}/dy(2.76\text{ TeV}) = 0.33$ mb and $d\sigma_{pp}^{c\bar c}/dy(5.02\text{ TeV}) = 0.57$ mb.
- $J/\psi$ inclusive cross sections in pp collisions: $d\sigma^{J/\psi}_{pp}/dy = 2.3\ \mu$b at $2.5<|y|<4$ for 2.76 TeV, and $3.65\ \mu$b at $2.5<|y|<4$ for 5.02 TeV.
- The charm quark mass used in the Cornell potential is $m_c = 1.25$ GeV, with $\alpha = \pi/12$ and $\sigma = 0.2\ \text{GeV}^2$.

## Context

This work extends the coalescence model approach to charmonium production (e.g., Fries (2003), Greco (2003)) by explicitly connecting the suppression of $J/\psi$ regeneration at higher LHC energies to the spatial diffusion of charm quarks in the expanding QGP, an effect previously noted qualitatively by Zhu (2007) and Liu (2014). The proposed observable $N_{J/\psi}/(N_D)^2$ is designed to isolate the coalescence probability from uncertainties in charm production cross sections and cold nuclear matter effects such as shadowing, enabling a cleaner probe of heavy quark dynamics in future measurements at the LHC and the Future Circular Collider (39 TeV).
