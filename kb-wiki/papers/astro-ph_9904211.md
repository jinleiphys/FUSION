# Quasi Periodic Oscillations in Low Mass X-Ray Binaries and Constraints on the Equation of State of Neutron Star Matter

> Recently discovered quasi periodic oscillations in the X-ray brightness of low mass X-ray binaries are used to derive constraints on the mass of the neutron star component and the equation of state of neutron star matter. The observations are compared with models of rapidly rotating neutron stars which are calculated by means of an exact numerical method in full relativity. For the equations of state we select a broad collection of models representing different assumptions about the many-body structure and the complexity of the composition of super dense matter. The mass constraints differ from their values in the approximate treatment by \sim 10%. Under the assumption that the maximum frequency of the quasi periodic oscillations originates from the innermost stable orbit the mass of the neutron star is in the range: $M\sim 1.92-2.25 M_\odot$. Especially the quasi periodic oscillation in the Atoll-source 4U 1820-30 is only consistent with equations of state which are rather stiff at high densities which is explainable, so far, only with pure nucleonic/leptonic composition. This interpretation contradicts the hypothesis that the protoneutron star formed in SN 1987A collapsed to a black hole, since this would demand a maximum neutron star mass below $1.6 M_\odot$. The recently suggested identification of quasi periodic oscillations with frequencies around 10 Hz with the Lense-Thirring precession of the accretion disk is found to be inconsistent with the models studied in this work, unless it is assumed that the first overtone of the precession is observed.

## Key claim

Under the assumption that the maximum frequency of the kilohertz quasi periodic oscillations originates from the innermost stable circular orbit, the mass of the neutron star is constrained to the range M ~ 1.92-2.25 solar masses, with the specific source 4U 1820-30 requiring equations of state that are rather stiff at high densities and consistent only with pure nucleonic/leptonic composition.

## Method

The authors computed models of rapidly rotating neutron stars using an exact numerical method in full general relativity, solving the Einstein equations via a finite difference scheme with compactification of outer space. They compared these models against observational data from 16 low-mass X-ray binary sources, using a broad collection of equations of state representing different assumptions about many-body structure, composition (including nucleons, hyperons, delta isobars, pion condensation, and quark matter), and the possibility of strange stars.

## Key numbers

- Neutron star mass range under the innermost stable orbit interpretation: M ~ 1.92-2.25 solar masses
- Mass constraints from the approximate treatment differ from exact relativistic values by approximately 10 percent
- Frequency separation in 4U 1636-536: delta-nu = 251 Hz, with burst frequency nu_burst = 581 Hz (differing by approximately 15 percent)
- Maximum kilohertz QPO frequency observed in 4U 1820-30: nu_QPO1_max = 1060 Hz, with spin frequency nu_s = 275 Hz
- For the EOS RHFpn (pure nucleonic), mass range from 4U 1820-30 constraints: M = 1.0 to 2.25 solar masses
- For the EOS RHF8 (including hyperons), maximum mass from 4U 1820-30 constraints: M_max = 1.65 solar masses
- Neutron star spin periods from frequency separations: 2.5 ms to 4 ms
- Frequency separation in 4U 0614+091: 323 +/- 4 Hz
- Frequency separation in 4U 1728-34: 355 +/- 5 Hz, with burst frequency 363 Hz
- Frequency separation in 4U 1820-30: 275 +/- 8 Hz

## Context

This work extends the approach of using kilohertz QPOs to constrain neutron star properties, following the beat-frequency model proposed by Strohmayer (1996) and the identification of the upper QPO with orbital frequency at the inner accretion disk edge. The authors improve upon earlier approximate treatments by using fully relativistic rotating neutron star models, finding that mass constraints differ by about 10 percent from approximate values. The results contradict the hypothesis that the protoneutron star formed in SN 1987A collapsed to a black hole, which would require a maximum neutron star mass below 1.6 solar masses. The identification of low-frequency QPOs (around 10 Hz) with Lense-Thirring precession, as suggested by Stella and Vietri (1997), is found inconsistent with the models unless the first overtone of the precession is observed.
