# Statistical Error analysis of Nucleon-Nucleon phenomenological potentials

> Nucleon-Nucleon potentials are commonplace in nuclear physics and are determined from a finite number of experimental data with limited precision sampling the scattering process. We study the statistical assumptions implicit in the standard least squares fitting procedure and apply, along with more conventional tests, a tail sensitive quantile-quantile test as a simple and confident tool to verify the normality of residuals. We show that the fulfilment of normality tests is linked to a judicious and consistent selection of a nucleon-nucleon database. These considerations prove crucial to a proper statistical error analysis and uncertainty propagation. We illustrate these issues by analyzing about 8000 proton-proton and neutron-proton scattering published data. This enables the construction of potentials meeting all statistical requirements necessary for statistical uncertainty estimates in nuclear structure calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the residuals from fitting nucleon-nucleon scattering data with phenomenological potentials can satisfy a normal distribution with high confidence, but only after applying a self-consistent 3-sigma rejection criterion to the database; this normality condition is a necessary prerequisite for meaningful statistical error propagation and uncertainty estimates in nuclear structure calculations.

## Method

The authors performed least-squares chi-squared fits of phenomenological nucleon-nucleon potentials (using delta-shell, OPE-delta-shell, chi-TPE-delta-shell, and OPE-Gaussian forms) to approximately 8000 published proton-proton and neutron-proton scattering data from 1950-2013 up to T_LAB = 350 MeV. They applied a self-consistent 3-sigma rejection criterion to build a consistent database, then tested the normality of the fit residuals using standardized moments, the Pearson test, the Kolmogorov-Smirnov test, and a tail-sensitive quantile-quantile test with confidence bands.

## Key numbers

* Total database of 8124 fitting data points (7709 experimental measurements plus 415 normalizations) was reduced to N = 6713 data points after applying the self-consistent 3-sigma rejection criterion.
* For the 3-sigma self-consistent database with the OPE-delta-shell potential (N = 6713), the standardized moments fell within expected 1-sigma confidence intervals: empirical third moment = 0.007 (expected 0 ± 0.030), fourth moment = 2.975 (expected 3 ± 0.059), fifth moment = 0.059 (expected 0 ± 0.330), sixth moment = 14.405 (expected 15 ± 0.939), seventh moment = 0.658 (expected 0 ± 4.324), eighth moment = 98.687 (expected 105 ± 15.591).
* For the complete database (N = 8125), the standardized moments showed large deviations from normality: third moment = -0.176, fourth moment = 4.305, fifth moment = -3.550, sixth moment = 42.839, seventh moment = -78.766, eighth moment = 671.864.
* The minimal resolution for the delta-shell potential was estimated as Δr_pi ~ 0.6 fm, corresponding to the shortest de Broglie wavelength at pion production threshold.

## Context

This work extends the partial wave analysis approach of the Nijmegen group (Bergervoet 1988, Stoks 1992), who tested squared residuals with a chi-squared distribution for pp data up to T_LAB = 30 and 350 MeV using N = 389 and 1787 data points respectively, but whose method was insensitive to sign asymmetries. The authors' previous work (NavarroPerez 2012, Perez 2012, Amaro 2013) using delta-shell potentials suggested NN uncertainties propagate into nuclear binding energy uncertainties of ΔB/A ~ 0.1-0.5 MeV. This paper enables the construction of smooth potentials with statistically meaningful error bands directly inferred from experiment, which can be used for uncertainty quantification in nuclear structure calculations.
