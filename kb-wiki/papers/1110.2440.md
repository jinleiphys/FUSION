# Electromagnetic emission from hot medium measured by the PHENIX experiment at RHIC

> Electromagnetic radiation has been of interest in heavy ion collisions because they shed light on early stages of the collisions where hadronic probes do not provide direct information since hadronization and hadronic interactions occur later. The latest results on photon measurement from the PHENIX experiment at RHIC reflect thermodynamic properties of the matter produced in the heavy ion collisions. An unexpectedly large positive elliptic flow measured for direct photons can not be explained by any of the current models.

## Key claim

The PHENIX experiment measured a large positive elliptic flow (\(v_2\)) for direct photons in Au+Au collisions at RHIC that is comparable to hadron flow for \(p_T < 3\) GeV/\(c\), a result that cannot be explained by current models and is significantly larger than hydrodynamic predictions.

## Method

Direct photons were measured in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV using two complementary techniques: calorimetric measurement of real photons at high \(p_T\) and internal conversion of virtual photons into electron-positron pairs at low \(p_T\). For the virtual photon method, the invariant mass distribution of electron pairs was fit with a cocktail of hadron decay contributions plus a direct photon component using the Kroll-Wada formula, extracting the direct photon fraction \(r_\gamma\) as a function of \(p_T\) and centrality. The elliptic flow \(v_2\) of direct photons was obtained by subtracting the \(v_2\) of hadron decay photons from inclusive photon \(v_2\) using the measured \(\gamma/\pi^0\) ratio.

## Key numbers

- Direct photon yield in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV shows an excess over scaled p+p yield for \(p_T < 2.5\) GeV/\(c\), with exponential slope parameters of \(\sim 220\) MeV for 0–20% and 20–40% centrality and minimum-bias events.
- Signal-to-background ratio for direct photons is \(\sim 1\%\) at 2 GeV and \(\sim 10\%\) at 5 GeV in p+p collisions; the QGP signal is predicted to be \(\sim 10\%\) of inclusive photons.
- The elliptic flow \(v_2\) of direct photons is large and positive, comparable to hadron flow for \(p_T < 3\) GeV/\(c\), and cannot be explained by current models.

## Context

This work extends the PHENIX program of direct photon measurements at RHIC, which previously established that high-\(p_T\) direct photons from initial hard scattering follow NLO pQCD calculations scaled by nuclear thickness (Adler 2005). The low-\(p_T\) excess observed in Au+Au collisions, absent in \(d\)+Au collisions, indicates a thermal source from the hot medium. The unexpectedly large positive \(v_2\) challenges existing models including hydrodynamic calculations (Chatterjee 2009) and suggests that hadron-gas interactions (vanHees 2011) may play a significant role; resolving this puzzle will require improved theoretical descriptions of photon production in the quark-gluon plasma and hadronic phases.
