# Left-hand cut and the HAL QCD method

> We investigate how the left-hand cut (LHC) problem is treated in the HAL QCD method. For this purpose, we first consider the effect of the LHC to the scattering problem in non-relativistic quantum mechanics with potentials. We show that the $S$-matrix or the scattering phase shift obtained from the potential including the Yukawa term ($e^{- m_πr}/r$) with the infra-red (IR) cutoff $R$ is well-defined even for the complex momentum $k$ as long as $R$ is finite, and they are compared with those obtained by the analytic continuation without the IR cutoff. In the $R\to\infty$ limit, the phase shift approaches the result from the analytic continuation at ${\rm Im}\, k < m_π/2$, while they differ at ${\rm Im}\, k > m_π/2$, except $k= k_b$, where $k_b$ is the binding momentum. We also observe that $k_b$ can be correctly obtained even at finite but large $R$. Using knowledge obtained in the non-relativistic quantum mechanics, we present how we should treat the LHC in the HAL QCD potential method.

## Key claim

The HAL QCD method's extraction of binding energies is not affected by the left-hand cut (LHC) even when a bound state lies on the LHC, because the binding momentum \(k_b\) can be correctly obtained from the potential with a finite infrared cutoff \(R\); however, the analytic continuation of the \(S\)-matrix and the finite-\(R\) result differ at \(\mathrm{Im}\, k > m_\pi/2\) except exactly at \(k = k_b\).

## Method

The authors first study the LHC problem in non-relativistic quantum mechanics using a Yukawa potential \(g e^{-m_\pi r}/r\) with an infrared cutoff \(R\) (setting the potential to zero for \(r > R\)). They compute the \(S\)-matrix \(S(k,R)\) and the scattering phase shift \(\delta_R(k)\) for complex momenta \(k\) and compare them to the analytic continuation \(S_{\mathrm{anal}}(k)\) obtained without the cutoff. They then apply these insights to the HAL QCD potential method for the \(D^*D\) system relevant to the \(T_{cc}^+\) tetraquark, fitting the lattice potential with a 2-Gaussian plus Yukawa-squared form and estimating LHC positions from one-pion and two-pion exchange kinematics.

## Key numbers

* The inverse scattering length for the \(S\)-wave \(D^*D\) system (\(I=0\)) extrapolated to the physical pion mass is \(1/a_0 = -0.01(9)\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) from the chiral extrapolation, consistent with \(1/a_0 = -0.03(4)\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) from the potential extrapolation (Lyu 2023).
* The LHC from one-pion exchange in the \(D^*D\) system appears at \(k^2/m_\pi^2 \simeq -0.02\), using \(m_\pi = 146.4\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(m_D = 1878.2\ \mathrm{MeV}\), and \(m_{D^*} = 2018.1\ \mathrm{MeV}\) from the simulation (Lyu 2023).
* The LHC from two-pion exchange appears at \(k^2/m_\pi^2 \simeq -0.77\) for the same simulation parameters.
* The phase shift from the finite-\(R\) potential converges to the analytic continuation result for \(\mathrm{Im}\, k < m_\pi/2\) as \(R \to \infty\), while they differ for \(\mathrm{Im}\, k > m_\pi/2\) except at the binding momentum \(k_b\).

## Context

This work addresses concerns raised by Du (2023) that left-hand cuts may invalidate lattice QCD analyses of the \(T_{cc}^+\) tetraquark, particularly those using effective range expansion on finite-volume energies (Padmanath 2022). The authors show that the HAL QCD method (Lyu 2023) is robust because the binding energy is obtained by directly solving the Schrodinger equation with the potential, not by analytic continuation of the phase shift. The study enables future applications of the HAL QCD method to systems where bound or virtual states may lie on or below the LHC, and it provides a procedure to include LHC effects by fitting the long-distance behavior of the potential.
