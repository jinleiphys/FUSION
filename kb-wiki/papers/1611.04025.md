# Transverse Momentum Distribution in Heavy Ion Collision using q-Weibull Formalism

> We have implemented the Tsallis q-statistics in the Weibull model of particle production known as the q-Weibull distribution to describe the transverse-momentum (pT ) distribution of the charged hadrons at mid-rapidity measured at RHIC and LHC energies. The model describes the data remarkably well for the entire pT range measured in nucleus-nucleus and nucleon-nucleon collisions. The proposed distribution is based on the non-extensive Tsallis q-statistics which replaces the usual thermal equilibrium assumption of hydrodynamical models. The parameters of the distribution can be related to the various aspects of complex dynamics associated with such collision process.

# Transverse Momentum Distribution in Heavy Ion Collision using q-Weibull Formalism

## Key claim

The q-Weibull distribution, combining Tsallis non-extensive statistics with the Weibull model of particle production, describes the transverse-momentum (pT) distribution of charged hadrons across the entire measured pT range for pp, pA, and AA collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, but the physical interpretation of the parameters depends on whether the fit is constrained to low pT (below 2 GeV/c) or includes the full pT range.

## Method

The authors replaced the exponential factor in the standard two-parameter Weibull distribution with a q-exponential from Tsallis statistics, yielding a three-parameter (q, lambda, k) q-Weibull function. They performed chi-squared fits of this function to published charged-hadron pT spectra from the UA1 experiment (ppbar at 0.2, 0.5, 0.9 TeV), ALICE experiment (pp at 0.9, 2.76, 7, 13 TeV; pPb at 5.02 TeV; PbPb at 2.76 TeV), and PHENIX experiment (AuAu at 200 GeV), for both the full measured pT range and a restricted low-pT range (pT < 2 GeV/c).

## Key numbers

- In pp collisions at ALICE, the q parameter ranges from 1.133 +/- 0.004 at 0.9 TeV to 1.1565 +/- 0.005 at 7 TeV, with lambda between 0.157 +/- 0.006 GeV/c (13 TeV) and 0.1711 +/- 0.008 GeV/c (2.76 TeV), and k between 1.010 +/- 0.024 (13 TeV) and 1.09 +/- 0.029 (2.76 TeV)
- In ppbar collisions at UA1, q ranges from 1.07 +/- 0.014 at 0.2 TeV to 1.080 +/- 0.013 at 0.9 TeV, with lambda between 0.130 +/- 0.015 GeV/c (0.5 TeV) and 0.132 +/- 0.019 GeV/c (0.9 TeV), and k between 0.906 +/- 0.045 (0.9 TeV) and 0.921 +/- 0.065 (0.5 TeV)
- For PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV, the q parameter increases from peripheral to central collisions when fitting the full pT range, but shows a slight decreasing trend or remains constant around 1.0 when constrained to pT < 2 GeV/c
- The lambda parameter increases from peripheral to central collisions in both PbPb and AuAu for both the full pT range and the low-pT regime

## Context

This work extends prior applications of Tsallis statistics to particle production, including the Tsallis Blast Wave model used by Zebo (2013) for RHIC energies and Zebo (2014) for pp collisions at LHC, and the Weibull model previously applied to pp and e+e- collisions by Weib1 and Weib2 (2013-2014). The q-Weibull approach provides a unified framework that can describe both small systems (pp, pA) and large systems (AA) across all measured pT, unlike hydrodynamical models based on Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics which are generally limited to low pT. The results enable systematic studies of identified particle spectra to better understand the physical meaning of the k parameter and to predict spectra for future experiments.
