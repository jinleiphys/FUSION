# Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay

> We review the potential to probe new physics with neutrinoless double beta decay $(A,Z) \to (A,Z+2) + 2 e^-$. Both the standard long-range light neutrino mechanism as well as short-range mechanisms mediated by heavy particles are discussed. We also stress aspects of the connection to lepton number violation at colliders and the implications for baryogenesis.

## Key claim

An observation of neutrinoless double beta decay will either imply lepton number violation at the LHC and low-scale baryogenesis (potentially observable in the near future), or will point toward a high-scale origin of both neutrino masses and baryogenesis; however, various loopholes exist in specific models, including scenarios where lepton number violation exists only for specific flavors, models with hidden sectors or new symmetries, and models where lepton number is broken below the electroweak phase transition.

## Method

The paper reviews the standard long-range light neutrino mechanism for neutrinoless double beta decay as well as short-range mechanisms mediated by heavy particles, and analyzes the connection between these mechanisms and lepton number violation at colliders. The authors examine the decomposition of the effective d=9 operator into two topologies (topology 1 with two bosons and a fermion in internal lines, topology 2 with an internal 3-boson-vertex) and apply this to LHC analogues of neutrinoless double beta decay. They then combine these results with arguments about the washout of baryon asymmetry through lepton number violation and sphaleron processes.

- No specific numerical results from experiments or calculations are presented in the provided text; the paper is a review that discusses qualitative relationships between neutrinoless double beta decay, LHC signals, and baryogenesis scenarios.

## Context

This review extends the discussion of connections between neutrinoless double beta decay and collider physics previously explored in left-right symmetric models by Keung and Senjanovic (1983) and Tello et al. (2010), and in R-parity violating supersymmetry by Allanach, Kom and Pas (2009). The work builds on the systematic decomposition of the neutrinoless double beta decay operator by Bonnet et al. (2013) and the LHC analogue studies by Helo et al. (2013). The argument that low-energy lepton number violation is dangerous for baryogenesis, previously noted by Fukugita and Yanagida (1990) and others, is extended to all short-range contributions and the long-range contribution as recently realized by Deppisch et al. (2015). The review enables a logical framework connecting future observations of neutrinoless double beta decay, LHC signals of lepton number violation, and the scale of baryogenesis.
