# Induced magnetic moment in the magnetic catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking

> The chiral symmetry breaking in a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio effective model of quarks in the presence of a magnetic field is investigated. We show that new interaction tensor channels open up via Fierz identities due to the explicit breaking of the rotational symmetry by the magnetic field. We demonstrate that the magnetic catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking leads to the generation of two independent condensates, the conventional chiral condensate and a spin-one condensate. While the chiral condensate generates, as usual, a dynamical fermion mass, the new condensate enters as a dynamical anomalous magnetic moment in the dispersion of the quasiparticles. Since the pair, formed by a quark and an antiquark with opposite spins, possesses a resultant magnetic moment, an external magnetic field can align it giving rise to a net magnetic moment for the ground state. The two condensates contribute to the effective mass of the LLL quasiparticles in such a way that the critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration becomes enhanced.

## Key claim

The magnetic catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking in a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model of quarks under a magnetic field inevitably generates not only the conventional chiral condensate but also a spin-one condensate that acts as a dynamical anomalous magnetic moment, and the two condensates evaporate at the same critical temperature because the spin-one condensate does not break any symmetry not already broken by the chiral condensate and the magnetic field.

## Method

The authors studied a one-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model of massless quarks in a constant uniform magnetic field, introducing a four-fermion interaction channel with coupling constant G' that becomes relevant only when rotational symmetry is broken by the magnetic field. They solved the system gap equations in the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation to find the two independent condensates, then derived the thermodynamic potential and analytically computed the critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration.

## Key numbers

- The chiral condensate is given by \(\overline{\sigma}=A\exp{-\left[\frac{2\pi^2}{(G+G')N_cqB}\right]}\) with \(A=2G\Lambda/(G+G')\)
- The spin-one condensate is given by \(\overline{\xi}=A'\exp{-\left[\frac{2\pi^2}{(G+G')N_cqB}\right]}\) with \(A'=2G'\Lambda/(G+G')\)
- The effective dynamical mass of LLL quasiparticles is \(M_{\xi}=\overline{\sigma}+\overline{\xi}=2\Lambda\exp{-\left[\frac{2\pi^2}{(G+G')N_cqB}\right]}\)
- Using \(G\Lambda^2=1.835\), \(\Lambda=602.3\) MeV, \(N_c=3\), \(q=|e|/3\simeq 0.1\), and \(G'=\eta G\) with \(\eta \simeq 1\) for \(qB/\Lambda^2\sim 1\), the dynamical mass increases sixfold due to the spin-one condensate
- The critical temperature is \(T_{C_\chi}=1.16\Lambda \exp{-\left[\frac{2\pi^2}{(G+G')N_cqB}\right]}=0.58M_\xi\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous findings in QED (Ferrer and de la Incera, 2010) that magnetic catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking produces both a dynamical fermion mass and a dynamical anomalous magnetic moment, to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model of QCD. The result that the critical temperature increases with magnetic field contrasts with lattice QCD results showing a dropping temperature with field, but the authors note that incorporating the running of the coupling with magnetic field (as discussed in their related work) can reconcile these findings. The work enables further study of electrical conductivity in strongly magnetized quark matter and the role of the spin-one condensate in the chiral phase transition.
