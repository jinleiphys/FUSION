# Treatment of the Coulomb interaction in three-nucleon reactions

> The Coulomb interaction between the two protons is included in the calculation of three-nucleon hadronic and electromagnetic reactions using screening and renormalization approach. Calculations are done using integral equations in momentum space. The reliability of the method is demonstrated. The Coulomb effect on observables is discussed.

# Treatment of the Coulomb interaction in three-nucleon reactions

## Key claim
The screening and renormalization approach can reliably include the Coulomb interaction between the two protons in momentum-space calculations of three-nucleon hadronic and electromagnetic reactions, provided the screening radius is sufficiently large and the screening function is chosen with smoothness parameter n between 3 and 6. The method converges for most observables at screening radius R = 20 fm, but requires larger radii for very low-energy elastic scattering and for breakup configurations near the proton-proton final-state interaction regime.

## Method
The authors solve Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) three-particle integral equations in momentum space, using a screened Coulomb potential of the form w_R(r) = w(r) exp[-(r/R)^n] with n=4 as the standard choice. The three-particle transition operators are decomposed into long-range and Coulomb-distorted short-range parts, where the diverging two-body Coulomb contributions are isolated and renormalized using phase factors, allowing the R → ∞ limit to be taken numerically. The hadronic interaction used is the realistic coupled-channel potential CD Bonn + Δ, which allows for single virtual Δ-isobar excitation.

## Key numbers
- Screening radius R = 20 fm is sufficient for convergence in most cases, with R = 10 fm, 20 fm, and 30 fm compared for elastic scattering at 3 MeV proton lab energy
- For breakup at 13 MeV proton lab energy, convergence requires R up to 60 fm in the proton-proton final-state interaction configuration
- The smoothness parameter n = 4 is the standard choice, with values 3 ≤ n ≤ 6 found to provide sufficiently smooth but rapid screening
- At 3 MeV proton lab energy, the differential cross section, proton analyzing power A_y(N), and deuteron analyzing power T_21 are shown as functions of center-of-mass scattering angle

## Context
This work extends the screening and renormalization approach previously applied to two-charged-particle scattering by Taylor (1974) and to three-nucleon systems by Alt and collaborators (1978, 1994, 2002), but differs from those earlier calculations by using realistic interactions with fully converged calculations in terms of screening radius and partial waves. The results agree well with configuration-space calculations by Kievsky and collaborators (2001) that include an unscreened Coulomb potential with proper boundary conditions, validating both methods. The approach enables calculations of proton-deuteron elastic scattering and breakup reactions above the deuteron breakup threshold, where configuration-space methods are less efficient, and has been extended to four-nucleon scattering by Fonseca. The Coulomb effect converts the proton-proton final-state interaction peak into a minimum with zero cross section, but the space star anomaly in proton-deuteron breakup remains unresolved by inclusion of the Coulomb interaction.
