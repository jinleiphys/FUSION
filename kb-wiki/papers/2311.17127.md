# Intertwined quantum phase transitions in odd-mass Nb isotopes

> A detailed analysis of odd-mass Nb isotopes, in the framework of the interacting boson-fermion model with configuration mixing, discloses the effects of an abrupt crossing of states in normal and intruder configurations (Type~II QPT), on top of which superimposed a gradual evolution from spherical- to deformed-core shapes within the intruder configuration (Type~I QPT). The pronounced presence of both types of QPTs demonstrates, for the first time, the occurrence of intertwined QPTs in odd-mass nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates, for the first time, the occurrence of intertwined quantum phase transitions (QPTs) in odd-mass nuclei, specifically in Nb isotopes, where an abrupt crossing of normal and intruder configurations (Type II QPT) is superimposed on a gradual shape evolution from spherical to deformed cores within the intruder configuration (Type I QPT).

## Method

The authors employed the interacting boson-fermion model with configuration mixing (IBFM-CM), coupling a single proton in the π(1g9/2) orbit to Zr cores with boson numbers N=1–7 for 93–105Nb isotopes. The Hamiltonian was cast in matrix form with normal A configuration (N boson space) and intruder B configuration (N+2 boson space, representing 2p-2h excitations across the Z=40 sub-shell gap), with parameters determined from a fit to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The ground state angular momentum changes from 9/2+1 to 5/2+1 at neutron number 60, signaling the Type II QPT
- For 93Nb, the center of gravity (CoG) of the quintuplet states (5/2+1, 7/2+1, 9/2+3, 11/2+1, 13/2+1) built on 2+1;A is 0.976 MeV, in agreement with the observed energy 0.935 MeV of 2+1 in 92Zr
- For 93Nb, the CoG of the nonet built on 4+1;A is 1.591 MeV, close to 1.495 MeV of 4+1;A
- For 93Nb, the observed B(E2) rate for 9/2+2 → 9/2+1 is 1.03(9) W.u., close to the calculated value 0.85 W.u.
- For 93Nb, the observed B(E2) rate for 9/2+3 → 9/2+1 is 1.52(10) W.u.
- For 93Nb, the CoG of the quintuplet built on 2+2;B is 1.705 MeV, compared to 1.847 MeV of 2+2;B

## Context

This work extends the intertwined QPT scenario previously found in even-even Zr isotopes (Gavrielov 2019, Gavrielov 2020, Gavrielov 2022) to the adjacent odd-even Nb isotopes, demonstrating that the same intricate pattern of coexisting Type I and Type II QPTs persists in odd-mass nuclei. The results motivate further experiments on non-yrast spectroscopy in such nuclei and provide a physically transparent description within the IBFM-CM framework, which improves upon mean-field approaches (Guzman 2011) that fail to reproduce the ground state spin change from 9/2+ to 5/2+ in Nb isotopes.
