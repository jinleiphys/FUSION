# Appearance of novel modes of $\bar{D}$ mesons in the Dual Chiral Density Wave

> We calculate dispersion relations for $\bar{D}(0^-)$, $\bar{D}^*(1^-)$, $\bar{D}_0^*(0^+)$ and $\bar{D}_1(1^+)$ mesons in the Dual Chiral Density Wave (DCDW) where the chiral symmetry is spontaneously broken by inhomogeneous chiral condensate. Employing the Bloch's theorem, we show that all the modes have opposite group velocity to the momentum in the low-momentum region and the energy is minimized at non-zero momentum. Furthermore, the magnitude of momentum which realizes the minimum energy is equal to the wave number of the density wave.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the Dual Chiral Density Wave (DCDW) phase, all four types of \(\bar{D}\) mesons (\(\bar{D}(0^-)\), \(\bar{D}^*(1^-)\), \(\bar{D}_0^*(0^+)\), and \(\bar{D}_1(1^+)\)) develop dispersion relations where the group velocity is opposite to the momentum in the low-momentum region, and the energy minimum occurs at a non-zero momentum whose magnitude equals the wave number of the density wave. This result is derived using Bloch's theorem and depends on the specific chiral partner structure and heavy quark symmetry assumptions in the effective Lagrangian.

## Method

The authors constructed a relativistic effective Lagrangian for \(\bar{D}\) mesons interacting with a chiral field \(M = \sigma + i\tau^a\pi^a\), incorporating chiral partner structure between \((\bar{D}, \bar{D}^*)\) and \((\bar{D}_0^*, \bar{D}_1)\) doublets. They replaced the chiral field with its classical DCDW configuration \(M = \phi \cos(2fx) + i\tau^3 \phi \sin(2fx)\), where \(f\) is the wave number of the density wave, and derived equations of motion for the meson fields. Using Bloch's theorem due to the periodic potential, they solved the resulting infinite matrix equations in momentum space to obtain dispersion relations for the neutral \(\bar{D}\) mesons.

## Key numbers

* The average mass of the \(G\) doublet (\(\bar{D}_0^*\) and \(\bar{D}_1\)) and \(H\) doublet (\(\bar{D}\) and \(\bar{D}^*\)) is \(m = 2.19\) GeV, with a mass splitting parameter \(\Delta_m = 430\) MeV.
* The pion decay constant is fixed at \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV, and the coupling constant \(|g| = 0.50\) is estimated from the experimental width \(\Gamma(D^{*+} \to D^0\pi^+) = 56\) keV.
* The spin-averaged masses are \(m_G = 2.40\) GeV for the \(G\) doublet and \(m_H = 1.97\) GeV for the \(H\) doublet.
* Dispersion relations are computed for density wave numbers \(f = 100\) MeV, \(f = 200\) MeV, and \(f = 400\) MeV, with \(\tilde{\phi} = \phi/f_\pi = 1\) (full chiral condensate) and \(k_\perp^2 = 0\).
* The group velocity at zero momentum is given by \(v_x = \pm \frac{K}{2E} \left[ 1 \pm \frac{K^2}{\sqrt{K^4 + 4\tilde{\phi}^2 m^2 \Delta_m^2}} \right]\), where \(K = 2f\).
* The energy minimum occurs at momentum \(q_x = \pm f\), equal to the wave number of the density wave.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on charmed mesons in inhomogeneous chiral condensates (Suenaga 2014, Suenaga 2014) by computing full dispersion relations rather than just mass spectra. It builds on earlier work showing that the DCDW phase may appear at densities around 2.4 times normal nuclear matter density (Heinz 2013) and that the half-Skyrmion phase shows similarity to DCDW (Harada 2015). The results suggest that \(\bar{D}\) mesons could serve as probes for detecting inhomogeneous chiral condensates in experiments such as the planned RISP facility in Korea.
