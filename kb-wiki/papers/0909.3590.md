# Color symmetric superconductivity in a phenomenological QCD model

> In this paper, we construct a theory of the NJL-type where superconductivity is present, and yet the super-conducting state remains, in the average, color symmetric. This shows that the present approach to color superconductivity is consistent with color singlet-ness. Indeed, quarks are free in the deconfined phase, but the deconfined phase itself is believed to be a color singlet. The usual description of the color superconducting state violates color singlet-ness. On the other hand, the color superconducting state here proposed, is color symmetric in the sense that an arbitrary color rotation leads to an equivalent state, with precisely the same physical properties.

## Key claim

The authors construct an NJL-type model of color superconductivity in which the superconducting state remains, on average, color symmetric (i.e., invariant under arbitrary color rotations), thereby satisfying the condition that the deconfined phase must be a color singlet. This contrasts with the usual BCS description of color superconductivity, which violates color singlet-ness.

## Method

The authors apply a generalized Bogoliubov transformation, symmetric in the three color indices, to a mean-field constrained Hamiltonian of the NJL type that includes a diquark pairing interaction antisymmetric in color and isospin. They enforce the stronger condition that the expectation values of all eight SU(3) generators vanish (color singlet-ness), rather than only the two associated with color neutrality. A Lagrange multiplier is introduced to enforce color neutrality, and the gap equation and thermodynamic potential are derived for the color-symmetric BCS vacuum.

## Key numbers

* The gap equation for color-symmetric superconductivity is \(1 = \frac{G_C}{V} \left( \sum_{m \leq \Omega'} + \sum_{m > \Omega'} \right) \frac{1}{\sqrt{(\varepsilon_m - \lambda)^2 + 3\Delta^2}}\), where \(\Delta\) is the common gap for all three colors and \(\lambda\) is a Lagrange multiplier enforcing color neutrality.
* The expectation value of the constrained Hamiltonian at the extremum is \(\langle \hat K_{MFA} \rangle = 3\sum_{m \leq \Omega'} \varepsilon_m + \sum_{m > \Omega'} (|\varepsilon_m| - E_m) + \sum_{m \leq \Omega'} (\varepsilon_m - E_m) + V \frac{3\Delta^2}{4G_C}\), with \(E_m = \sqrt{(\varepsilon_m - \lambda)^2 + 3\Delta^2}\).
* For the usual (color-symmetry-breaking) approach, the gap equation is \(1 = \frac{G_C}{V} \sum_m \frac{1}{\sqrt{\varepsilon_m^2 + \Delta^2}}\), with only one gap \(\Delta\) from pairing of two colors.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Bohr et al. (2009) (the corrigendum cited in the paper) to a realistic NJL model, correcting an earlier error in the generalized Bogoliubov transformation. It contrasts with the standard color-superconductivity treatments of Alford et al. (2001) and Buballa (2005), which break color symmetry, and with the color-neutrality approaches of Iida and Baym (2001) and Buballa and Shovkovy (2005), which use different chemical potentials for different colors but still fail to ensure full color singlet-ness. The method also differs from the projection techniques of Amore et al. (2002). The work enables future comparisons of the equation of state and other properties of the color-symmetric superconducting phase with conventional color-superconductivity in realistic NJL models, with potential applications to neutron star interiors.
