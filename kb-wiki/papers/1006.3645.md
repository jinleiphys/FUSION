# Strange meson production at high density and temperature

> The properties of strange mesons ($K$, $\bar K$ and $\bar K^*$) in dense matter are studied using a unitary approach in coupled channels for meson-baryon scattering. The kaon-nucleon interaction incorporates $s$- and $p$-wave contributions within a chiral model whereas the interaction of $\bar K^*$ with nucleons is obtained in the framework of the local hidden gauge formalism. The in-medium solution for the scattering amplitude accounts for Pauli blocking effects, mean-field binding on baryons, and meson self-energies. We obtain the $K$, $\bar K$ and $\bar K^*$ (off-shell) spectral functions in the nuclear medium and study their behaviour at finite density, temperature and momentum. We also analyze the energy weighted sum rules of the kaon propagator as a quality test of model calculations. We finally estimate the transparency ratio of the $γA \to K^+ K^{*-} A^\prime$ reaction, which we propose as a feasible scenario at present facilities to detect in-medium modifications of the $\bar K^*$ meson.

# Strange meson production at high density and temperature

## Key claim
The properties of strange mesons (K, anti-K, and anti-K*) in dense nuclear matter are studied using coupled-channel unitary approaches, with the claim that the anti-K* width increases by up to a factor of five at normal nuclear matter density relative to its free-space value of about 50 MeV, though this enhancement depends on the self-consistent treatment of in-medium effects and the inclusion of multiple absorption channels.

## Method
The kaon-nucleon interaction is treated with s-wave and p-wave contributions within a chiral unitary model, solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with on-shell amplitudes and cutoff regularization. The anti-K* interaction with nucleons is obtained using the local hidden gauge formalism, with the in-medium self-energy computed self-consistently from two contributions: the decay mode anti-K* → anti-K pi modified by medium effects, and the interaction of anti-K* with nucleons including quasi-elastic and absorption channels (anti-K* N → rho Y, omega Y, phi Y, etc.). The in-medium scattering amplitudes account for Pauli blocking, mean-field binding on baryons via a sigma-omega model, and meson self-energies, with the spectral functions derived from the imaginary part of the in-medium propagators.

## Key numbers
- Normal nuclear matter density: rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}
- Anti-K* free-space width: approximately 50 MeV
- Anti-K* width at normal nuclear matter density: five times the free value (approximately 250 MeV)
- Anti-K* optical potential at rho_0: moderate, -50 MeV
- Transparency ratio calculated for center-of-mass energies sqrt(s) = 3 GeV and 3.5 GeV, corresponding to photon lab energies of 4.3 GeV and 6 GeV respectively
- Sum rules tested at rho = rho_0, T = 0 MeV, and q = 150 MeV/c

## Context
This work extends previous studies of strange pseudoscalar mesons (K and anti-K) in dense matter by Tolos et al. (2006, 2008) and Ramos et al. (1999), which found an attractive anti-K potential of about -50 MeV at normal density from unitarized chiral dynamics. It also builds on the hidden gauge formalism for vector meson interactions with baryons by Oset et al. (2009) and the recent medium calculations by Tolos et al. (2010). The paper enables experimental tests of in-medium anti-K* modifications through the gamma A → K+ K*- A' reaction at facilities such as GSI, where the transparency ratio can be measured to probe the predicted strong broadening of the anti-K* meson.
