# Chiral dynamics in few-nucleon systems

> We report on recent progress achieved in calculating various few-nucleon low-energy observables from effective field theory. Our discussion includes scattering and bound states in the 2N, 3N and 4N systems and isospin violating effects in the 2N system. We also establish a link between the nucleon-nucleon potential derived in chiral effective field theory and various modern high-precision potentials.

## Key claim
Chiral effective field theory provides a systematic and controlled method for calculating few-nucleon low-energy observables, but the numerical values of the low-energy constants \(c_{1,3,4}\) from pion-nucleon analyses remain uncertain, leading to two possible versions (NNLO and NNLO*) of the nucleon-nucleon potential that differ in whether they produce spurious deeply bound states.

## Method
The authors construct an effective nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential using chiral perturbation theory with Weinberg's power counting and a unitary transformation to avoid energy dependence. The potential is iterated in a regularized Lippmann-Schwinger equation to generate bound and scattering states for 2N, 3N, and 4N systems. Low-energy constants are fitted to low-energy \(np\) phase shifts, and isospin-violating effects are included via quark mass differences and electromagnetic corrections.

## Key numbers
- The \(c_i\) constants from \(Q^2\) analyses of \(\pi N\) scattering: \(c_1 = -0.64\), \(c_3 = -3.90\), \(c_4 = 2.25\) (all in GeV\(^{-1}\)).
- The \(c_i\) bands from \(Q^3\) calculations: \(c_1 = -0.81\) to \(-1.53\), \(c_3 = -4.70\) to \(-6.19\), \(c_4 = 3.25\) to \(4.12\) (all in GeV\(^{-1}\)).
- For the NNLO* potential, reduced values: \(c_3 = -1.15\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_4 = 1.20\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- Triton binding energy at NLO varies between \(-7.55\) and \(-8.28\) MeV for cut-off 540 to 600 MeV.
- \(^4\)He binding energy at NLO varies between \(-24.0\) and \(-28.1\) MeV; at NNLO* it varies between \(-29.96\) and \(-27.87\) MeV for cut-off 500 to 600 MeV.
- The \(pp\) scattering length uncertainty from scheme dependence: \(|\delta a_{pp} / a_{pp}| \simeq 0.6\ \text{fm} / 17\ \text{fm} \simeq 3\%\).
- The LECs from contact interactions scale naturally with \(l\) values between 0.3 and 3.5, except \(f_\pi^2 C_T\) which is unnaturally small: \(-0.002\) to \(0.147\) at NLO and \(0.002\) to \(0.040\) at NNLO*.

## Context
This work extends the chiral EFT approach to few-nucleon systems pioneered by Weinberg (1990, 1991) and Ordonez et al. (1994), and provides a link to modern high-precision potentials such as CD-Bonn, AV-18, and Nijmegen potentials. The results enable systematic inclusion of three-nucleon forces at NNLO and beyond, and allow parameter-free predictions for 3N and 4N observables at NLO. The analysis also connects the low-energy constants to resonance parameters in one-boson-exchange models, as discussed in Epelbaum et al. (2000).
