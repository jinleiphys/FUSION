# Determining the density content of symmetry energy and neutron skin: an empirical approach

> The density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy remains poorly constrained. Starting from precise empirical values of the nuclear volume and surface symmetry energy coefficients and the nuclear saturation density, we show how in the ambit of microscopic calculations with different energy density functionals, the value of the symmetry energy slope parameter $L$ alongwith that for neutron skin can be put in tighter bounds. The value of $L$ is found to be $L$= 64$\pm $5 MeV. For $^{208}$Pb, the neutron skin thickness comes out to be 0.188 $\pm $0.014 fm. Knowing $L$, the method can be applied to predict neutron skins of other nuclei.

# Determining the density content of symmetry energy and neutron skin: an empirical approach

## Key claim

The authors show that by using precise empirical values of the volume and surface symmetry energy coefficients (32.10 ± 0.31 MeV and 58.91 ± 1.08 MeV, respectively) together with the nuclear saturation density, the symmetry energy slope parameter L can be constrained to 64 ± 5 MeV, but this result depends on the specific choice of nuclear density functionals and the assumption that the density dependence of symmetry energy follows a power-law form around saturation.

## Method

The authors employ a power-law ansatz for the density dependence of the volume symmetry energy, C_v(ρ) = C_v(ρ_0)(ρ/ρ_0)^γ, and relate the surface symmetry coefficient C_s to the equivalent density ρ_A of a finite nucleus through an expansion around saturation density. Using the experimental proton density distribution of 208Pb and neutron density profiles from several relativistic mean-field (RMF) interactions (BSR8-BSR14, FSUGOLD, NL3, TM1), they solve coupled equations linking C_s, ρ_A, and γ to extract L and the neutron skin thickness simultaneously.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy slope parameter L = 64 ± 5 MeV
- Neutron skin thickness of 208Pb: 0.188 ± 0.014 fm
- Volume symmetry energy coefficient C_v(ρ_0) = 32.10 ± 0.31 MeV (from Jiang et al. double-difference analysis)
- Surface symmetry energy coefficient C_s = 58.91 ± 1.08 MeV (from Jiang et al. double-difference analysis)
- Nuclear saturation density ρ_0 = 0.155 ± 0.008 fm^{-3}
- Predicted neutron skin for 124Sn: 0.196 ± 0.014 fm
- Predicted neutron skin for 90Zr: 0.107 ± 0.007 fm
- Predicted neutron skin for 48Ca: 0.182 ± 0.008 fm

## Context

This work extends the empirical determination of symmetry energy parameters by using the precise volume and surface symmetry coefficients from Jiang et al. (2012), which were obtained from double differences of experimental symmetry energies. The results contradict the larger L values from isospin diffusion (88 ± 25 MeV, Chen et al. and Li et al.) and the smaller values from neutron star observations (43-52 MeV, Steiner et al.), while being consistent with isoscaling results (L ~ 65 MeV, Shetty et al.) and pygmy dipole resonance analyses (64.8 ± 15.7 MeV, Carbone et al.). The method enables precision predictions of neutron skins for other neutron-rich nuclei, which can be tested by future parity-violating electron scattering experiments like PREX.
