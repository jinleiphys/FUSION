# TDHFB-Langevin approach to the nuclear collective dynamics

> A restricted TDHFB-Langevin formalism is presented, and applied to estimate the nuclear shape diffusion coefficient.

## Key claim

The paper derives a stochastic mean-field formalism (TDHFB-Langevin) that includes frictional and random forces from a classical heat bath, and shows that the effective friction coefficient for nuclear shape diffusion with configuration change depends on temperature and is not the same as the model parameter \(\gamma_\beta\), contrary to simpler assumptions.

## Method

The author applies the time-dependent variational principle to a quantum many-body system bilinearly coupled to a classical heat bath of harmonic oscillators, obtaining Langevin-like equations of motion for the collective coordinates. The formalism is tested on a charged particle in a thermal radiation field, then restricted to a 14-dimensional submanifold of the Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov trial manifold for the sd-shell nucleus \(^{28}\)Si, including quadrupole and pairing interactions. An ensemble of 300 Brownian trajectories is generated from the TDHFB-Langevin equations at the giant quadrupole resonance (GQR) energy, and the diffusion coefficient for the quadrupole deformation is computed from the mean-first-passage-time for configuration switches between oblate and prolate shapes.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{28}\)Si, the metastable ground state (mgs) oblate configuration has quadrupole deformation \(\beta^0 = -0.43\).
- The model Hamiltonian uses interaction strengths \(\chi = 0.186\) MeV and \(G_{pair} = 1.23\) MeV.
- At \(T=0\), the GQR decay width is \(\Gamma_{GQR} \approx 4.5\) MeV when \(\gamma_\beta \sim 6.4 \hbar\).
- The friction coefficient relation is \(\gamma_K = 0.0083 \gamma_\beta\).
- The GQR energy is 19.1 MeV; the temperature range studied is \(k_B T\) between 1.5 and 2.5 MeV.
- The diffusion coefficient interpolation formula is \(D_\beta^*(T) = k_B T / [u + v k_B T + \gamma_\beta (a + b k_B T)]\) with \(u = 0.27 \hbar\), \(v = 1.1 \hbar /\)MeV, \(a = 0.14\), \(b = 0.1\) MeV\(^{-1}\).
- At \(k_B T = 2.5\) MeV, \(D_\beta^* = 453\) keV\(/\hbar\), about a factor ten larger than the microscopic estimate of 30 keV\(/\hbar\) in \(^{24}\)Mg from earlier work.
- Assuming an \(A^{-1}\) dependence, for \(^{158}\)Er one obtains \(D_\beta^* \sim 80\) keV\(/\hbar\), close to the experimental value of \(\sim 70\) keV\(/\hbar\).

## Context

This work extends the stochastic mean-field approach by deriving TDHFB-Langevin equations from a variational principle with a bilinear coupling to a classical heat bath, building on the model used by Hanggi (1985) for the classical Langevin equation and by Grigorescu and collaborators for the GQR. It addresses the discrepancy noted by the author (1995) that microscopic estimates of the shape diffusion coefficient from residual interactions gave only about 10% of the Einstein formula value, and provides a dynamical calculation that yields results consistent with experimental data for heavy nuclei like \(^{158}\)Er. The formalism enables future calculations of nuclear collective dynamics with explicit treatment of dissipation, noise, and configuration changes beyond the static ensemble approach.
