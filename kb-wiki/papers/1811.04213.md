# Event Shape Engineering and Multiplicity dependent Study of Identified Particle Production in proton+proton Collisions at $\sqrt{s}$= 13 TeV using PYTHIA8

> Small system collectivity observed at the LHC energies along with enhancement of strangeness makes high-multiplicity proton+proton (pp) collisions very interesting in order to look for QGP-like features, usually found in heavy-ion collisions. It may be interesting to perform a double differential study of different observables in pp collisions in terms of charged particle multiplicity and event shape in order to understand the new dimensions in high-multiplicity pp physics. We study the correlation between the number of multi-partonic interactions (nMPI), event shape (transverse spherocity) and charged particle multiplicity classes. For the first time, we report the simulation results on the spherocity and charged particle multiplicity dependent study of ($π^{+}+π^{-}$), (K$^{+}$+K$^{-}$), (p+$\mathrm{\bar{p}}$), K$^{*0}$, $ϕ$ and ($Λ+\barΛ$) production in pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 13 TeV using PYTHIA8. We explore the event shape and charged particle multiplicity dependence of the transverse momentum ($p_{\rm{T}}$) spectra, integrated yield, mean transverse momentum ($\langle p_{\rm{T}} \rangle$) and particle ratios of the identified particles. This study provides a baseline for exploring the the event topology and final state multiplicity dependence of identified particle production in the LHC pp collisions.

## Key claim
The paper reports PYTHIA8 simulation results on identified particle production in pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV, showing that event shape (transverse spherocity) and charged particle multiplicity together provide a more selective classification of events than multiplicity alone. The authors explicitly note that the observed QGP-like features (such as a flat proton-to-φ ratio at low pT in isotropic high-multiplicity events) arise from the color reconnection mechanism in PYTHIA8, not from a hydrodynamic medium.

## Method
The authors generated 250 million inelastic non-diffractive pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV using PYTHIA 8.235 with the Monash 2013 tune and MPI-based color reconnection. Events were classified into ten V0M multiplicity classes (based on V0 detector acceptance) and into jetty (S0 < 0.29) and isotropic (S0 > 0.64) event classes using transverse spherocity. The pT spectra, integrated yields, mean pT, and particle ratios were studied for pions, kaons, protons, K*0, φ, and Λ at mid-rapidity (|η| < 0.5).

## Key numbers
- Charged particle multiplicity ranges for V0M classes: V0M-I (50–140), V0M-X (0–9) in the V0 detector acceptance.
- Spherocity cuts: jetty events defined as 0 ≤ S0 < 0.29 (lowest 20% of minimum bias events); isotropic events defined as 0.64 < S0 ≤ 1 (highest 20%).
- Scaled simulated charged particle pT spectra agree with ALICE data within 10–20% at low pT and within unity at intermediate and high pT (scaling factor 1.35 applied).
- Crossing point of jetty-to-integrated and isotropic-to-integrated pT-spectra ratios for pions, kaons, and protons moves from ~1 GeV/c (low multiplicity, V0M-X) to ~3 GeV/c (high multiplicity, V0M-I).
- For resonances (K*0, φ) and Λ, the crossing point in high-multiplicity (V0M-I) events is around 5 GeV/c.

## Context
This simulation study extends the experimental observation of QGP-like features in high-multiplicity pp collisions at the LHC (ALICE Collaboration, 2017) and prior work on transverse spherocity and multi-partonic interactions (Cuautle, 2014; Cuautle, 2015). It provides a PYTHIA8 baseline for future experimental double-differential (event shape and multiplicity) measurements of identified particle production at the LHC. The results enable comparison with hydrodynamical calculations (Bzdak, 2014; Bozek, 2013) and initial-state models (Dumitru, 2010) to disentangle final-state from initial-state effects in small systems.
