# Non-abelian Topological Strings and Metastable States in Linear Sigma Model

> Non-abelian (NA) topological string defects exist in QCD with the spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking $U(N_f)_L \times U(N_f)_R \to U(N_f)_V$. Anomaly effects lead to domain walls connected to these strings. In the framework of linear sigma model we find the configuration of these defects. Also we show that in this model metastable CP violating vacuua exist. Strings as well as extended regions of metastable vacuua may form during the chiral transition which could have interesting effects.

# Non-abelian Topological Strings and Metastable States in Linear Sigma Model

## Key claim

Non-abelian topological string defects exist in QCD with spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, and in the linear sigma model with anomaly effects these strings are connected to domain walls (one or two for elementary non-abelian strings with three flavors), while metastable CP-violating vacua exist at zero temperature for realistic quark masses.

## Method

The authors use a $U(3)_L \times U(3)_R$ linear sigma model Lagrangian with a determinant term to account for the axial anomaly and a term for explicit symmetry breaking by quark masses. They find static non-abelian string configurations by evolving an initial cylindrically symmetric configuration with a dissipative term until the configuration becomes approximately static, and they search for metastable vacua by fixing the chiral phase angles $\theta_1$ and $\theta_2$ and minimizing the potential while relaxing the scalar condensate values $\bar{\sigma}_0$ and $\bar{\sigma}_8$.

## Key numbers

- For the non-abelian string calculation: $\lambda = 53.5322$, $c = 6685.19\ \mathrm{MeV}$, $m^2 = -(591.256\ \mathrm{MeV})^2$, giving $\sigma_0 = 110.23\ \mathrm{MeV}$, $m_\sigma = 1000\ \mathrm{MeV}$, and $m_{\eta^\prime} = 950\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
- For the metastable state calculation: $\bar{\sigma}_0 \sim 130\ \mathrm{MeV}$, $\bar{\sigma}_8 \sim -24\ \mathrm{MeV}$, $h_0 = (286.094\ \mathrm{MeV})^3$, $h_8 = -(310.960\ \mathrm{MeV})^3$, $\lambda_1 = 1.4$, $\lambda_2 = 46.484$.
- The metastable state at $\theta_2 = \pi$ has minimized values $\bar{\sigma}_0 \sim 120\ \mathrm{MeV}$ and $\bar{\sigma}_8 = -28\ \mathrm{MeV}$, close to the initial values.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of abelian topological strings in QCD by Brandenberger et al. (1996) and the authors' own previous work on abelian strings with anomaly effects (Balachandran and Digal, 2002), which found that abelian strings for $N_f=3$ are connected to three domain walls. The non-abelian strings studied here differ crucially: in the string core only a partial chiral symmetry is restored (not the full symmetry), and the number of attached domain walls is one or two rather than three. Metastable CP-violating states were previously found in the non-linear sigma model by other authors (2001) for temperatures near the deconfinement or chiral transition; this work shows they exist in the linear sigma model at zero temperature as well. The results suggest that strings and extended metastable domains may form during the chiral phase transition in heavy-ion collisions and the early Universe, though formation of one suppresses formation of the other.
