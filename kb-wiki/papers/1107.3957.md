# The high energy frontier of ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions

> An outlook on physics at the high energy frontier of nucleus-nucleus collisions is presented, on the basis of the new results presented at Quark Matter 2011 by the LHC and RHIC experiments.

## Key claim

Based on the first LHC results presented at Quark Matter 2011, many previously accepted ideas about ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions are being seriously questioned, including the interpretation of the Mach cone and ridge, thermal particle yields, and parton energy loss mechanisms; the paper provides a subjective perspective on the high energy frontier defined as RHIC top energy and above.

## Method

The author reviews new experimental results from the LHC (ALICE, ATLAS, CMS) and RHIC (PHENIX, STAR) experiments presented at Quark Matter 2011, organized into seven physics areas: global observables, correlations, quarkonia, high pT suppression, identified particles, jets, and heavy flavours. For each area, the author compares LHC measurements at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV to RHIC results and identifies open questions and future measurements needed to advance the field.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle multiplicity per unit pseudorapidity in central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC: about 8, roughly twice the RHIC value
- Average transverse energy per unit pseudorapidity per participant pair in central collisions: about 9 GeV, a factor 2.7 larger than at RHIC
- Energy density achieved: about 16 GeV/fm^3 (assuming 1 fm/c plasma formation time)
- Elliptic flow coefficient v2 at LHC: roughly 20% larger than at RHIC for semi-central events
- High pT suppression R_AA minimum: about 0.14 for transverse momenta around 6-7 GeV/c, rising to about 0.5 above 30 GeV/c
- J/psi nuclear modification factor R_AA at LHC: about 0.5 for pT > 0 and 2.5 < y < 4 (ALICE), as low as 0.2 for central collisions with pT > 6.5 GeV/c (CMS)
- Upsilon(1S) suppression at LHC: R_AA around 0.6; excited states Upsilon(2S+3S) suppressed by about a factor 3 relative to Upsilon(1S)
- Upsilon(1S+2S+3S) suppression at RHIC (STAR): about a factor 3 in nuclear modification factor
- D meson suppression at LHC (ALICE): R_AA values compatible with pions out to 12 GeV/c
- Heavy flavour muon suppression at LHC (ALICE): about a factor 3 in the 5-10 GeV/c pT range
- Jet nuclear modification factor R_cp at LHC: about 0.5 for central (10%) events, flat out to 250 GeV transverse energy
- Measured p/pi ratio at LHC: about 0.05, at odds with thermal model predictions assuming 160-170 MeV temperatures

## Context

This paper extends the Quark Matter 2009 understanding by incorporating the first LHC heavy-ion results, which challenge several established ideas from RHIC-era physics. The author notes that the logarithmic multiplicity scaling from RHIC has broken down, that triangular flow (v3) from initial state fluctuations provides a natural explanation for both the ridge and Mach cone structures previously attributed to medium response effects, and that jet and heavy flavor energy loss results at LHC are puzzling compared to BDMPS predictions. The work enables future precision measurements with higher LHC luminosity and p-Pb reference runs, which could lead to paradigm shifts by Quark Matter 2012.
