# Initial-state-driven spin correlations in high-energy nuclear collisions

> In the study of spin-polarization phenomena in heavy-ion collisions, it is typically assumed that final-state particles are polarized through thermal vorticity and shear. In this sense, polarization is a final-state effect. Here, we propose a different mechanism. We postulate that the collision of spin-carrying nucleons generates an initial transverse spin density, inducing a net polarization of the QCD fireball along a random direction. If the net spin is conserved throughout the evolution of the fireball, the final-state particles should exhibit measurable polarization. Within a wounded nucleon picture, we estimate that initial-state fluctuations induce a net polarization of $Λ$ baryons which is around $1\%$ in central collisions and over $10\%$ in noncentral collisions, significantly exceeding the contributions from thermal vorticity and shear. We introduce a two-particle angular correlation observable designed to reveal initial net-spin fluctuations, and emphasize the main signatures to look for in experiments. We argue that the discovery of these phenomena would have profound implications for nuclear structure and our understanding of spin in relativistic hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The authors propose that initial-state spin fluctuations from colliding nucleons, rather than final-state thermal vorticity or shear, can produce measurable polarization of Λ baryons in heavy-ion collisions, provided the net spin is conserved throughout the fireball evolution. The mechanism relies on event-by-event fluctuations in the net spin of participant nucleons, which average to zero over many events but produce nonzero polarization in individual events.

## Method

The authors construct a Glauber-type model where each nucleon in a colliding nucleus (208Pb or 129Xe) carries a random spin projection (up or down), with equal numbers of each to satisfy the J=0 ground state of the nucleus. From the participant nucleons in each event, they define an initial transverse spin density at mid-rapidity and compute the event-by-event polarization parameter P = (1/Npart) Σ si, where si = ±1. They then propose a two-particle angular correlation observable vΛ² = (9/α₁α₂)⟨⟨cos(Δθ)⟩⟩, where Δθ is the angle between decay protons from pairs of Λ hyperons, which isolates the fluctuation of the polarization magnitude without requiring knowledge of its random direction.

## Key numbers

- In Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, the standard deviation of the polarization parameter P is around 1% in central collisions and over 10% in noncentral collisions.
- For Xe+Xe collisions, the standard deviation of P is larger than in Pb+Pb by approximately a factor √(208/129) ≈ 1.27 across the centrality range.
- The orbital angular momentum of the fireball is estimated to be 10⁴⁻⁵ ℏ, while maximal initial net spin values are of order 100 ℏ/2.
- The relevant parameter for polarization from vorticity, ω/T, is of order 10⁻³ or smaller at top RHIC and LHC energies.

## Context

This work extends the study of spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions beyond the standard paradigm where polarization arises from thermal vorticity and shear at freeze-out (Liang and Voloshin, 2004; Becattini et al., 2013-2019). The authors propose that initial-state spin fluctuations, previously unexplored in this context, can produce polarization signals exceeding those from vorticity and shear by orders of magnitude. The observable vΛ², analogous to spin correlation measurements used in top-antitop studies at the LHC (ATLAS 2023; CMS 2024), could reveal these fluctuations and enable tests of spin conservation in relativistic hydrodynamics, as well as probe two-body spin correlations in nuclear ground states that are inaccessible at lower-energy nuclear experiments.
