# Simple Description of Pion-Pion Scattering to 1 GeV

> Motivated by the 1/Nc expansion, we present a simple model of pion-pion scattering as a sum of a `current-algebra' contact term and resonant pole exchanges. The model preserves crossing symmetry as well as unitarity up to 1.2 GeV. Key features include chiral dynamics, vector meson dominance, a broad low energy scalar (sigma) meson and a `Ramsauer-Townsend' mechanism for the understanding of the 980 MeV region. We discuss in detail the `regularization' (corresponding to rescattering effects) necessary to make all these nice features work.

## Key claim

The authors present a simple model of pion-pion scattering as a sum of a current-algebra contact term and resonant pole exchanges, motivated by the 1/Nc expansion, which preserves crossing symmetry and unitarity up to 1.2 GeV. The key hedge is that the model requires "regularization" (corresponding to rescattering effects that are higher order in 1/Nc) to make the tree-level amplitude unitary, and the comparison with experiment is made by matching the real part of the predicted amplitude to the real part of the experimental amplitude rather than the full complex amplitude.

## Method

The authors construct a crossing-symmetric tree-level amplitude from a nonlinear chiral Lagrangian of pseudoscalar mesons and resonances, including a current-algebra contact term and exchanges of the rho(770), a broad low-energy scalar sigma(550), and the f0(980) scalar resonance. To compare with experiment, they regularize the propagators by introducing imaginary widths (Breit-Wigner forms) and, for the f0(980), incorporate a background phase via a rescattering formula that yields a Ramsauer-Townsend mechanism. The real part of the resulting partial wave amplitude for the I=J=0 channel is compared with experimental data extracted from phase shift analyses up to about 1.2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the truncated 1/Nc inspired approach of Sannino and Schechter (1995), which included resonances up to the p-wave region and satisfied unitarity bounds up to about 1.3 GeV via "local cancellation," but could only properly predict phase shifts up to about 800 MeV. The present paper improves the description in the 800-1200 MeV region by properly including the f0(980) resonance via a Ramsauer-Townsend mechanism, where a background phase of about 90 degrees causes the resonance contribution to reverse sign relative to a standard Breit-Wigner form. This enables a simple description of the sharp dip in the I=J=0 partial wave cross section near 1 GeV and opens the possibility of extending such truncated resonance models to higher energies by including successive groups of resonances that cancel among themselves.
