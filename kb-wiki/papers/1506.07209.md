# Equally Spaced Levels with T=1 two-body Matrix elements

> If one examines two-body matrix elements from experiment one notices that not only J=0 T=1 lies low but also J=1T=0 and J=J_{max} =2j T=0. It is sometimes thought that one needs both T=1 and T=0 two-body matrix elements to get equally spaced spectra of even I states i.e. vibraitonal spectra. We here attempt to get equally spaced levels with only those that have T=1 (even J).As an example we perform single-j calculations (f_{7/2}) in ^{44} T and ^{46} Ti. We then shift gears and decide to play around with the input two particle matrix, elements (not worrying about experiment) to generate interesting spectra e.g. rotaional spectra with and then without T=0 two-body matrix element. Of special interest is a new partial dynamical symmetry found when the "123", the "1234" etc.,interactions are used.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that nearly equally spaced (vibrational) spectra of even-spin states can be obtained using only T=1 two-body matrix elements, contrary to the common assumption that both T=1 and T=0 matrix elements are required; however, the equally spaced pattern is only approximate and does not extend perfectly to all states, as shown by the I=12 state in Ti-44 lying at 4.8524 MeV versus the perfect vibrator prediction of 4.9812 MeV.

## Method

Single-j shell-model calculations were performed for two protons and two neutrons in the f7/2 shell (Ti-44 and Ti-46) using only T=1 two-body matrix elements with the T=0 matrix elements set to zero. The authors then systematically varied the input two-body matrix elements, including interactions labeled "123", "1234", and "12345" (where T=1 matrix elements are set to J/2, J/3, etc.), and examined the resulting spectra and wave functions for rotational and vibrational patterns. Calculations were extended to the g9/2 shell (Cd-96) and h11/2 shell with analogous interactions.

## Key numbers

- For Ti-44 with T=1 only matrix elements (J=0,2,4,6 set to 0.00, 1.00, 1.60, 2.00 MeV respectively), the I=2 state lies at 0.8302 MeV and the I=12 state at 4.8524 MeV (perfect vibrator would place I=12 at 4.9812 MeV)
- The 8 to 6 splitting in Ti-44 with T=1 only is 1.3105 MeV, significantly different from the 2 to 0 splitting of 0.8397 MeV
- With the "123" interaction in f7/2, levels from I=6 to I=12 are equally spaced with a separation of 1.5 MeV
- With the "135" interaction in f7/2, the spacings are 3 MeV, double those of the "123" interaction
- For the perfect rotator with J(J+1) two-body interaction in Ti-44, the I=2 state is at 6 MeV and I=12 at 156 MeV
- In the g9/2 shell with the "1234" interaction, special states appear at energies 4.29, 5.79, 7.29, 8.79, and 10.29 MeV with constant Jp+Jn values

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Bayman et al. (1963-1964), McCullen et al. (1964), and French et al. (1964) on f7/2 shell calculations, and responds to findings by Cederwall et al. (Nature) on equally spaced levels in Pd-92 and by Robinson et al. on Cr-48 and Pd-92. The authors build on a previously identified partial dynamical symmetry (Pereira, Garcia, Zamick, Escuderos, and Neergaard, 2017) where setting all T=0 matrix elements to zero yields degenerate states with good dual quantum numbers (Jp, Jn). The new finding is that with more restrictive T=1 interactions like "123", additional angular momenta (I=6 and I=8) join the equally spaced spectrum, with wave functions constrained by constant (Jp+Jn) rather than the stricter condition of fixed individual (Jp, Jn). This enables future exploration of how simple two-body interactions can generate collective vibrational and rotational patterns in N=Z nuclei.
