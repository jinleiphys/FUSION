# Causality of polarizeable dissipative fluids from Lagrangian hydrodynamics

> We perform a causality analysis on the dispersion relation of hydrodynamics with spin well as shear and bulk viscosity, including the relaxation times for all these quantities. We find that the interplay of the three relaxational scales, for shear and bulk viscosity as well as polarization, leads to non-trivial effects on the dispersion relation. Unexpectedly, the presence of polarization leads to lower effective viscosity and a longer relaxation time, and the presence of viscosity leads to lower limits as well as upper ones on the group velocity and constraints relating polarization to viscosity relaxation times. We conclude with a qualitative discussion on how these results impact phenomenology, specifically the low effective viscosity in strongly interacting matter as well as shear-vorticity coupling.

## Key claim

The interplay of three relaxational scales (for shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and polarization) in a Lagrangian formulation of hydrodynamics with spin leads to nontrivial modifications of the dispersion relation, where the presence of polarization lowers effective viscosity and lengthens relaxation time, while viscosity imposes both upper and lower limits on group velocity and constraints linking polarization to viscosity relaxation times.

## Method

The authors construct a Lagrangian effective field theory for a polarizable, dissipative fluid by linearly combining Lagrangians for ideal hydrodynamics, bulk viscosity, Navier-Stokes shear viscosity, and polarization, with Israel-Stewart-type relaxation times introduced for each dissipative channel. They linearize the equations of motion around a hydrostatic equilibrium background, perform a Fourier decomposition into longitudinal and transverse perturbations, and derive the dispersion relations for both bulk and shear viscosity cases separately. The group velocities are then analyzed in the asymptotic limits of small and large wave numbers relative to temperature, and causality constraints are extracted from requiring subluminal propagation.

## Key numbers

* The transverse group velocity for bulk viscosity in the large-k limit is given by \( \nu_g = 1 / \sqrt{ 1 + A^\prime \frac{\tau^2_\chi}{\tau_\xi} + C \tau^2_\chi } \), where \(A^\prime\) and \(C\) are coefficients defined from the Lagrangian parameters.
* The longitudinal group velocity for bulk viscosity in the large-k limit is \( \nu_g = \sqrt{ \frac{ D \tau^2_\chi + 1 + ( B^\prime + 8 A) \frac{\tau^2_\chi}{\tau_\xi} }{ C \tau^2_\chi - 1 - A \frac{\tau^2_\chi}{\tau_\xi} } } \), with \(D\) another coefficient from the Lagrangian.
* The causality constraint on bulk relaxation time is \( \tau_\xi \geq \frac{ \xi}{(1- c_s^2) (\epsilon + P) - c \frac{ 8 \chi^2}{\tau^2_\chi} } \), where \(c_s\) is the speed of sound, \(\epsilon+P\) the enthalpy, \(\chi\) the polarization susceptibility, and \(c = \pm 1\) indicates spin-vorticity alignment or anti-alignment.
* The causality constraint on shear relaxation time is \( \tau_\eta \geq \frac{ 4 \eta}{(1- c_s^2) (\epsilon + P) - c \frac{ 8 \chi^2}{\tau^2_\chi} } \).
* The transverse group velocity for shear viscosity in the large-k limit is \( \nu^{(x,y)}_g = \sqrt{ \frac{1 + 2A \frac{\tau^2_\chi}{\tau_\eta} (\delta^x_I \delta^y_J + \delta^x_J \delta^y_I ) }{ 1 + C \tau^2_\chi + \frac{A \tau^2_\chi}{\tau_\eta} (\delta^x_I \delta^y_J + \delta^x_J \delta^y_I ) } } \), with indices \(x \neq y = \{1,2,3\}\).
* The longitudinal group velocity for shear viscosity in the large-k limit is \( \nu^{(x,y)}_g = \sqrt{ \frac{D \tau^2_\chi + 1 + (B+2A) \frac{\tau^2_\chi}{\tau_\eta} (\delta^x_I \delta^y_J + \delta^x_J \delta^y_I ) }{ C \tau^2_\chi - 1 - \frac{A \tau^2_\chi}{\tau_\eta} (\delta^x_I \delta^y_J + \delta^x_J \delta^y_I ) } } \).

## Context

This work extends the Lagrangian hydrodynamics approach for polarizable fluids developed by Montenegro and Torrieri (2024) and the earlier causality analysis of polarizable fluids without viscosity by the same authors, by incorporating shear and bulk viscosity with their associated relaxation times in an Israel-Stewart-like framework. The results generalize the dispersion relations and causality constraints previously derived by Koide and Fogaca for viscous fluids without spin, and by Lin and Chiarini for Eulerian approaches to spin hydrodynamics. The findings enable phenomenological connections to the low effective viscosity observed in strongly interacting matter produced in heavy-ion collisions, and to shear-vorticity coupling effects that can be probed through polarization measurements.
