# Nuclear Many-Body Dynamics constrained by QCD and Chiral Symmetry

> We present a novel description of nuclear many-body systems, both for nuclear matter and finite nuclei, emphasizing the connection with the condensate structure of the QCD ground state and spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. Lorentz scalar and vector mean-fields are introduced in accordance with QCD sum rules. Nuclear binding arises from pionic fluctuations, using in-medium chiral perturbation theory up to three-loop order. Ground state properties of $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca are calculated. The built-in QCD constraints reduce the number of input parameters significantly in comparison with purely phenomenological relativistic mean-field approaches.

## Key claim

Nuclear binding and saturation arise almost entirely from chiral (two-pion exchange) fluctuations calculated via in-medium chiral perturbation theory, while the large scalar and vector mean-fields from QCD condensates (approximately equal in magnitude and opposite in sign) are essential for producing the spin-orbit splitting in finite nuclei, though the approach reproduces only about two-thirds of the empirical spin-orbit splitting.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic mean-field Lagrangian with density-dependent scalar and vector four-nucleon contact couplings, separating contributions from QCD condensates (constrained by QCD sum rules) and from pionic fluctuations (calculated using in-medium chiral perturbation theory to three-loop order). The density-dependent couplings for the pionic fluctuation terms are determined by equating the corresponding self-energies in the single-nucleon Dirac equation with those from chiral perturbation theory, using a polynomial fit up to order \(k_f^5\). Ground states of \(^{16}\)O and \(^{40}\)Ca are calculated in the no-sea approximation, with derivative terms introduced for finite nuclei using a single scale parameter \(\Lambda \approx 0.65\) GeV.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: \(\rho_0 = 0.178\) fm\(^{-3}\) (from chiral perturbation theory calculation with cutoff \(\Lambda \simeq 0.65\) GeV)
- Compression modulus at saturation: \(K = 255\) MeV
- Asymmetry energy at saturation: \(A(k_{f0}) = 33.8\) MeV
- Condensate scalar coupling: \(G_S^{(0)} = 10.52\) fm\(^2\); condensate vector coupling: \(G_V^{(0)} = 10.00\) fm\(^2\) (fitted to binding energies and charge radii of \(^{16}\)O and \(^{40}\)Ca)
- QCD sum rule estimate for scalar coupling: \(G_S^{(0)} \simeq 11\) fm\(^2\) at \(\rho_S \simeq \rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) (for \(\sigma_N = 50\) MeV)
- Binding energy deviation from experiment: 7.8% for \(^{16}\)O, 5.3% for \(^{40}\)Ca
- Charge radius deviation from experiment: 2.5% for \(^{16}\)O, 4.6% for \(^{40}\)Ca

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field approach of Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) by imposing QCD sum rule constraints from Drukarev and Levin (1990), Cohen, Furnstahl, and Griegel (1991), and Jin (1994), and by incorporating explicit pionic fluctuations following the in-medium chiral perturbation theory calculation of Kaiser, Fritsch, and Weise (2001). The approach enables a parameter-minimized description of both nuclear matter and finite nuclei, with the next steps being inclusion of higher-dimensional QCD condensates and extension to \(N > Z\) systems.
