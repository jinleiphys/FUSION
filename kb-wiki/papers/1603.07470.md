# Searching for high-$K$ isomers in the proton-rich $A\sim80$ mass region

> Configuration-constrained potential-energy-surface calculations have been performed to investigate the $K$ isomerism in the proton-rich $A\sim80$ mass region. An abundance of high-$K$ states are predicted. These high-$K$ states arise from two and four-quasi-particle excitations, with $K^π=8^{+}$ and $K^π=16^{+}$, respectively. Their excitation energies are comparatively low, making them good candidates for long-lived isomers. Since most nuclei under studies are prolate spheroids in their ground states, the oblate shapes of the predicted high-$K$ states may indicate a combination of $K$ isomerism and shape isomerism.

## Key claim
Configuration-constrained potential-energy-surface calculations predict an abundance of high-K states in the proton-rich A~80 mass region, but the authors hedge that none have been observed experimentally yet, possibly because many nuclides are very proton-rich and some have not even been discovered, or because the predicted isomers may be very long-lived and their gamma rays delayed and subdued.

## Method
Configuration-constrained potential-energy-surface (PES) calculations were performed using a macroscopic-microscopic approach with the standard liquid drop model as the macroscopic part and the Strutinsky shell correction as the microscopic part. Single-particle levels were obtained from a triaxially deformed Woods-Saxon potential with the universal parameter set, and monopole pairing was incorporated with the Lipkin-Nogami method for approximate particle-number projection. At each grid point on the quadrupole deformation plane, the total potential energy was minimized with respect to hexadecapole deformation, and specific single-particle orbitals were traced and blocked for all calculated deformations to produce configuration-constrained PES.

## Key numbers
- Two-quasi-particle states (K^π = 8^+) have excitation energies generally around 3 MeV; four-quasi-particle states (K^π = 16^+) have excitation energies around 6 MeV.
- For ^72Kr, the two-quasi-proton state (K^π = 8^+) has an excitation energy of 2.452 MeV, the two-quasi-neutron state (K^π = 8^+) has 2.484 MeV, and the four-quasi-particle state (K^π = 16^+) has 4.921 MeV.
- For ^80Zr, the ground state is prolate with β2 = 0.405, while the high-K states are oblate with β2 ~ -0.18; the two-quasi-proton state has excitation energy 4.110 MeV, the two-quasi-neutron state has 4.140 MeV, and the four-quasi-particle state has 6.187 MeV.
- Experimentally observed lowest-lying J^π = 8^+ and 16^+ states in this mass region are typically at about 3 MeV and 8 MeV (^78Kr), respectively.

## Context
This work extends configuration-constrained PES calculations, previously successful for describing quasi-particle states in other mass regions (Xu et al., 1998; Wang et al., 2016), to the proton-rich A~80 region where shape coexistence has been intensively studied (Wood et al., 1992; Bender et al., 2015) but K isomerism has been less discussed (Sun, 2004). The predictions enable experimental searches for high-K isomers in this region, particularly in nuclei like ^72Kr and ^80Zr, and suggest that the oblate shapes of the high-K states may indicate a combination of K isomerism and shape isomerism, which could be tested by future experiments.
