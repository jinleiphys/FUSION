# Influence of Meson's Widths on Yukawa-like Potentials and Lattice Correlation Functions

> Euclidean point-to-point propagators or wall-to-wall correlators related to exchange by an unstable particle(sigma,rho,omega-mesons) are modified by presence of particle width. In particular, the usual method of deriving particle masses from logarithmic derivatives need to be modified. Similarly Yukawa-like potentials of nuclear physics due to exchange of those mesons are significantly modified since the coupling to the decay products is strong. For example, the large distance asymptotic changes, exp(-M_{min} r), where M_{min} is the sum of the decay product masses (2 m_π, 2 m_{e}, 2 m_ν). In the area M_{min}r<1the potential has a long-range tail 1/r^3. Similar effects appear due to the virtual decays in the elecroweak sector of the Standard model. The $Z-γ$ mixing via electron loop gives the parity violation potential with the range $1/2 m_{e}$, i.e. the range of the weak interaction increases $10^{5}$ times.

# Influence of Meson's Widths on Yukawa-like Potentials and Lattice Correlation Functions

## Key claim

The width of unstable mesons (sigma, rho, omega) significantly modifies Euclidean correlation functions and Yukawa-like nuclear potentials even when the meson is virtual and cannot physically decay, because the spectral density integral over all possible masses replaces the simple single-mass propagator; the large-distance asymptotic behavior changes from exp(-m_r r) to exp(-M_min r) where M_min is the sum of the decay product masses (e.g., 2 m_pi), and in the intermediate region M_min r < 1 the potential develops a long-range 1/r^3 tail.

## Method

The authors use standard dispersion relations to connect the Euclidean coordinate-space propagator (point-to-point, wall-to-wall, or line-to-line) to an integral over the physical spectral density Im K_mom(s), which contains a Breit-Wigner resonance with a running width. For meson-exchange potentials, they write the potential U(r) as an integral over the meson mass M of the spectral density times the Yukawa-like factor exp(-M r)/r, using a modified Breit-Wigner form with energy-dependent widths (P-wave for rho, S-wave for sigma). They evaluate the integral analytically in four distinct mass intervals (threshold, sub-resonance, resonance, and high-mass) and numerically for realistic parameters.

## Key numbers

- For the rho meson: width Gamma_r = 0.14 GeV, mass m_r = 0.77 GeV, ratio Gamma_r/m_r = 0.19, threshold 2m_pi/m_r = 0.36, P-wave exponent q = 3/2.
- For the sigma meson: ratio Gamma_r/m_r = 1, threshold 2m_pi/m_r = 0.5, S-wave exponent q = 1/2.
- The rho meson potential at m_r r < 3 is about 10% smaller than the Yukawa potential; the sigma meson potential is about 40% smaller.
- The short-range delta-function coefficient B = 0.93 for rho meson and B = 0.62 for sigma meson.
- The simple estimate factor 1 - Gamma_r/(pi m_r) gives 0.94 for rho and 0.68 for sigma.
- The Z-gamma mixing parity-violating potential has range 1/(2 m_e), extending the weak interaction range by a factor of 10^5.
- The thermal mass shift formula: delta m_max approx m_0 - Gamma^2/(8 T).

## Context

This work extends the standard Yukawa potential approach of nuclear physics, where meson-exchange potentials are usually taken as exp(-m_r r)/r with a fixed mass, by incorporating the finite width of the meson through dispersion relations. It also addresses the lattice QCD community's standard method of extracting hadron masses from the logarithmic derivative of wall-to-wall correlators, showing that for realistic widths (especially for the sigma meson with width comparable to its mass) the mass plateau disappears. The authors build on the coordinate-space sum rule approach of Shuryak (Shu_cor) and the SVZ sum rules of Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979), and note that only very recent lattice calculations (Cristoforetti, 2007) have sufficient quality to discuss rho meson width effects. The results enable more realistic extraction of both masses and widths from future lattice simulations, and suggest modifications to nuclear potentials used in mean-field calculations.
