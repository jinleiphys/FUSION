# A new tetra-quark interpretation of X(3872)

> A new tetra-quark interpretation of X(3872) is presented. In this model, X(3872) consists of two degenerate tetra-quark mesons, {[cn](cbar nbar) +- (cn)[cbar nbar]}_{I=0}, and, therefore, it is naturally understood that X(3872) decays into two different eigenstates of G-parity.

## Key claim

X(3872) is not a single meson but consists of two nearly degenerate tetra-quark mesons, \(X(\pm)\sim\{[cn](\bar c\bar n)\pm (cn)[\bar c\bar n]\}_{I=0}\), with opposite G-parities; this allows the same physical state to decay into final states with opposite G-parities without violating G-parity conservation, provided the mass difference between the two components is small enough (predicted to be about 3 MeV by a chiral model).

## Method

The paper classifies four-quark mesons into three types based on flavor symmetry and color configurations, then focuses on the hidden-charm axial-vector sector with \(J^P=1^+\). It proposes that the two states \([cn](\bar c\bar n)_{I=0}\) and \((cn)[\bar c\bar n]_{I=0}\) mix under G-parity conserving strong interactions to form eigenstates \(X(+)\) and \(X(-)\) with opposite G-parities. The mass difference from this mixing is argued to be small because the decay interactions are weak due to small overlap of color and spin wavefunctions, analogous to the narrow width of \(D_{s0}^+(2317)\).

## Key numbers

* Mass of X(3872): \(m = 3871.2 \pm 0.5\) MeV (PDG 2006 compilation).
* Width of X(3872): \(\Gamma < 2.3\) MeV at 90% CL (PDG 2006).
* Ratio of branching fractions: \(Br(X(3872)\rightarrow \pi^+\pi^-\pi^0J/\psi) / Br(X(3872)\rightarrow \pi^+\pi^-J/\psi) = 1.0 \pm 0.4 \pm 0.3\) (Belle).
* Ratio of amplitudes for decays to \(\rho^0 J/\psi\) vs \(\omega J/\psi\): \(|A(X\rightarrow \rho^0 J/\psi) / A(X\rightarrow \omega J/\psi)| = 0.27 \pm 0.02\).
* Mass of a candidate X(3872) signal in \(D^0\bar D^0\pi^0\): \(M = (3875.9 \pm 0.7^{+0.3}_{-1.6} \pm 0.4)\) MeV (Belle).
* Predicted mass difference between \(X_d\) and \(X_u\) in the diquark-antidiquark model (Maiani): \(m_{X_d} - m_{X_u} \simeq (7 \pm 2)\) MeV, compared to measured \((2.7 \pm 1.3 \pm 0.2)\) MeV (Babar).
* Experimental ratio \(\mathcal{R} \equiv Br(B^0\rightarrow X(3872)K^0) / Br(B^+\rightarrow X(3872)K^+)\): \(0.13 < \mathcal{R}_{\rm exp} < 1.10\) at 90% CL (Babar).
* Predicted mass difference between the two G-parity eigenstates from a chiral model: 3 MeV.
* Ratio for \(D_{s1}^+(2460)\): \(\Gamma(D_{s1}^+(2460)\rightarrow D_s^{+}\gamma) / \Gamma(D_{s1}^+(2460)\rightarrow D_s^{*+}\pi^0) = 0.31 \pm 0.06\) (PDG).

## Context

This work extends the tetra-quark approach previously applied to open-charm scalar mesons like \(D_{s0}^+(2317)\) (Terasaki, 2007) and addresses problems in existing models of X(3872): the molecular model (molecule) predicts \(\mathcal{R} < 0.1\) in conflict with experiment, the diquark-antidiquark model (Maiani, 2005) predicts a mass difference of \(7 \pm 2\) MeV larger than observed and charged partners not seen, and all single-meson models violate G-parity conservation. The proposed two-state interpretation enables a consistent description of X(3872) decays without assuming large isospin or G-parity violation, and predicts a rich spectrum of axial-vector tetra-quark mesons including charm-strange states that may correspond to \(D_{s1}^+(2460)\).
