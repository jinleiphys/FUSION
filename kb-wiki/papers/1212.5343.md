# Measurement of transparency ratios for protons from short-range correlated pairs

> Nuclear transparency, Tp(A), is a measure of the average probability for a struck proton to escape the nucleus without significant re-interaction. Previously, nuclear transparencies were extructed for quasi-elastic A(e,e'p) knockout of protons with momentum below the Fermi momentum, where the spectral functions are well known. In this paper we extract a novel observable, the transparency ratio, Tp(A)/T_p(12C), for knockout of high-missing-momentum protons from the breakup of short range correlated pairs (2N-SRC) in Al, Fe and Pb nuclei relative to C. The ratios were measured at momentum transfer Q^2 > 1.5 (GeV/c)^2 and x_B > 1.2 where the reaction is expected to be dominated by electron scattering from 2N-SRC. The transparency ratios of the knocked-out protons coming from 2N-SRC breakup are 20 - 30% lower than those of previous results for low missing momentum. They agree with Glauber calculations and agree with renormalization of the previously published transparencies as proposed by recent theoretical investigations. The new transparencies scale as A^-1/3, which is consistent with dominance of scattering from nucleons at the nuclear surface.

## Key claim

The paper extracts transparency ratios for high-missing-momentum protons knocked out from short-range correlated pairs (2N-SRC) in Al, Fe, and Pb relative to C, finding these ratios are 20-30% lower than previously published mean-field proton transparencies. This supports the claim that earlier mean-field transparency values were overestimated due to incorrect correction factors for 2N-SRC effects.

## Method

The authors measured semi-exclusive A(e,e'p) cross sections using a 5.014 GeV electron beam in CLAS at Jefferson Lab, selecting events with kinematics dominated by scattering from 2N-SRC pairs (x_B >= 1.2, Q^2 >= 1.5 (GeV/c)^2, missing momentum 300-600 MeV/c). The transparency ratio T_p(A)/T_p(12C) was extracted by taking the ratio of measured cross sections per nucleon for each nucleus relative to carbon, scaled by the relative number of 2N-SRC pairs per nucleon (a_2 factors) taken from a compilation of world data on (e,e') cross section ratios.

## Key numbers

- Transparency ratio for Al/C: 0.776 +/- 0.019 (stat) +/- 0.043 (syst)
- Transparency ratio for Fe/C: 0.579 +/- 0.010 (stat) +/- 0.036 (syst)
- Transparency ratio for Pb/C: 0.385 +/- 0.010 (stat) +/- 0.034 (syst)
- A-dependence exponent alpha = -0.34 +/- 0.02, consistent with A^(-1/3) scaling and with the Glauber result of alpha = -0.322 +/- 0.007
- The a_2 scaling factors used: a_2(Al/C) = a_2(Fe/C) = 1.100 +/- 0.055, a_2(Pb/C) = 1.080 +/- 0.054
- Momentum transfer range: Q^2 from approximately 1.5 to 3.5 (GeV/c)^2

## Context

This work extends previous nuclear transparency measurements for mean-field protons (ONeill 1995, Garino 1992, Abbott 1998, Garrow 2002) to the novel regime of high-missing-momentum protons from 2N-SRC breakup. The results support theoretical claims by Frankfurt (2000) and Lava (2004) that published mean-field transparencies were too high due to overestimated SRC correction factors. The A^(-1/3) scaling indicates dominance of scattering from nucleons at the nuclear surface, consistent with Glauber calculations (Pandharipande 1992). This enables improved understanding of final-state interactions in nuclei and provides a cleaner observable for studying nuclear attenuation effects.
