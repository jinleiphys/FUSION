# $D_S$ Mesons in Asymmetric Hot and Dense Hadronic Matter

> The in-medium properties of $D_S$ mesons are investigated within the framework of an effective hadronic model, which is a generalization of a chiral SU(3) model, to SU(4), in order to study the interactions of the charmed hadrons. In the present work, the $D_s$ mesons are observed to experience net attractive interactions in a dense hadronic medium, hence reducing the masses of the $D_S^+$ and $D_S^-$ mesons from the vacuum values. While this conclusion holds in both nuclear and hyperonic media, the magnitude of the mass drop is observed to intensify with the inclusion of strangeness in the medium. Additionally, in hyperonic medium, the mass degeneracy of the $D_S$ mesons is observed to be broken, due to opposite signs of the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction term in the Lagrangian density. Along with the magnitude of the mass drops, the mass splitting between $D_S^+$ and $D_S^-$ mesons is also observed to grow with an increase in baryonic density and strangeness content of the medium. However, all medium effects analyzed are found to be weakly dependent on isospin asymmetry and temperature. We discuss the possible implications emanating from this analysis, which are all expected to make a significant difference to observables in heavy ion collision experiments, especially the upcoming Compressed Baryonic Matter (CBM) experiment at the future Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (FAIR), GSI, where matter at high baryonic densities is planned to be produced.

## Key claim

The authors claim that \(D_S\) mesons experience net attractive interactions in dense hadronic matter, leading to a reduction of their masses from vacuum values, but they hedge that all medium effects are found to be **weakly dependent** on isospin asymmetry and temperature, with the magnitude of the mass drop and mass splitting between \(D_S^+\) and \(D_S^-\) intensifying primarily with baryonic density and strangeness content of the medium.

## Method

The in-medium properties of \(D_S\) mesons were studied using an effective hadronic model that generalizes a chiral SU(3) model to SU(4) to incorporate interactions of charmed hadrons. The interaction Lagrangian density for the \(D_S\) mesons was constructed including the Weinberg-Tomozawa term, scalar meson exchange term, first range term, and \(d_1\) and \(d_2\) range terms, and the mean-field approximation was applied to replace baryon fields by their number and scalar densities. The dispersion relations for \(D_S^+\) and \(D_S^-\) were then derived and solved for nuclear matter and hyperonic matter at finite baryon density, temperature, and isospin asymmetry.

## Key numbers

* The vacuum mass of the \(D_S\) meson is 1968.5 MeV (the lightest pseudoscalar \(D_S\) state).
* The mass of the charm quark is approximately 1.3 GeV, used to justify explicit SU(4) symmetry breaking.
* The mass of the strange quark is approximately 150 MeV, used in the context of explicit symmetry breaking for kaons.
* The masses of up and down quarks are less than 10 MeV, used in the context of explicit symmetry breaking for pions.

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) effective model approach, previously applied to kaons and antikaons (Sambuddha et al., 2006; Mishra et al., 2006, 2008) and to charmed \(D\) mesons (Mishra et al., 2004; Arindam et al., various), to the strange-charmed \(D_S\) meson system. It fills a void in the literature, as prior studies of \(D_S\) mesons in medium were limited to coupled-channel analyses of their excited states (Lutz and Korpa, 2006; Hofmann and Lutz, 2008; Jimenez-Tejero et al., 2009, 2011), while the ground-state pseudoscalar \(D_S\) mesons had not been investigated within this chiral effective framework. The results are expected to inform observables in heavy ion collision experiments, particularly the Compressed Baryonic Matter (CBM) experiment at FAIR, GSI.
