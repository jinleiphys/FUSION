# Uncover the correlation between jet energy correlators and multiplicity fluctuations

> The energy-energy correlator (EEC) and multiplicity are two fundamental observables probing complementary aspects of QCD jets: the former characterizes the angular structure of energy flows in a scale-dependent manner, while the latter is sensitive to the entire history of particle production. In this \emph{Letter}, we uncover a nontrivial correlation between them by studying the EEC as a function of jet internal multiplicity. We introduce the multiplicity-conditioned EEC jet function (MCJF) and perform a factorization calculation to next-to-leading order accuracy. It is found that, for jet samples selected at a given normalized multiplicity $ν= N_{\rm ch}/\langle N_{\rm ch} \rangle$, the EEC in the angular region $Λ_{\rm QCD}/p_{T,\rm jet}\llχ\ll R$ acquires a $ν$-dependent anomalous dimension. Thus the $ν$-conditioned EEC provides a direct and robust probe to the multiplicity generating function in the perturbative regime. In addition, understanding $ν$ dependence of the EEC is also crucial for isolating possible multiplicity-dependent bias effects in the EEC measurements in nuclear environment.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the energy-energy correlator (EEC) within a jet, when conditioned on a fixed normalized charged-particle multiplicity ν = N_ch / ⟨N_ch⟩, acquires a ν-dependent anomalous dimension in the angular region Λ_QCD / p_T,jet ≪ χ ≪ R, providing a perturbatively controlled probe of the multiplicity generating function. The hedge is that this correlation is generated specifically by emissions with angles between the EEC angle χ and the jet cone radius R, and the LO+LL calculation captures the essential trend but does not fully agree with Pythia8 simulations.

## Method

The authors introduce the multiplicity-conditioned EEC jet function (MCJF) and its generating function, performing a factorization calculation to next-to-leading order (NLO) accuracy in the collinear limit. They derive renormalization group equations that govern the joint evolution of the EEC and multiplicity, using angular-ordered evolution for the multiplicity generating function and a linear differential-integral equation for the MCJF. The calculation is validated by comparing LO+LL predictions with Pythia8 simulations for jets with p_T > 500 GeV and cone radius R = 0.4, and the ν-dependent exponent γ(ν) is extracted from the power-law behavior Σ(χ|ν) ∼ χ^{γ(ν)}.

## Key numbers

- Jets with p_T > 500 GeV and cone radius R = 0.4 are used for all numerical results.
- The standard EEC calculation agrees with Pythia8 across a wide angular range, except for χ ≲ 10^{-2} where non-perturbative effects become important.
- The multiplicity-conditioned EEC exponent γ(ν) shows a clear monotonic decrease with increasing ν for both LO+LL predictions and Pythia8 simulations.
- For the multiplicity bias study, a Gaussian reference distribution uses mean ⟨N_ch⟩ = 20 and width σ = 3; variations test width σ = 4 and changes in skewness.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of jet energy correlators (EEC), previously studied in e+e- annihilation (Basham 1978), hadronic collisions (Liu 2022, Liu 2023), and nuclear environments (Barata 2023, Ke 2025), by establishing an analytic connection between EEC and jet multiplicity fluctuations. It builds on the multiplicity generating function formalism developed by the same authors (Duan 2025) and the semi-inclusive jet factorization framework (Kang 2016, Dai 2016). The results enable experimental extraction of the scale-dependent parton production dynamics via the ν-conditioned EEC and highlight the need to account for multiplicity biases when interpreting EEC measurements in proton-lead or electron-ion collisions.
