# SU(3) Polyakov Linear Sigma-Model in an External Magnetic Field

> In the present work, we analyse the effects of an external magnetic field on the chiral critical temperature $T_c$ of strongly interacting matter. In doing this, we can characterize the magnetic properties of the quantum chromodynamics (QCD) strong interacting matter, the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). We investigate this in the framework of the SU(3) Polyakov linear sigma-model (PLSM). To this end, we implement two approaches representing two systems, in which the Polyakov-loop potential added to PLMS either renormalized or non-normalized. The effects of Landau quantization on the strongly interacting matter is conjectures to reduce the electromagnetic interactions between quarks. In this case, the color interactions will be dominant and increasing, which - in turn - can be achieved by increasing of the Polyakov-loop fields. Obviously, each of them equips us with a different understanding about the critical temperature under the effect of an external magnetic field. In both systems, we obtain a paramagnetic response. In one system, we find that $T_c$ increases with increasing the magnetic field. In the other one, $T_c$ significantly decreases with increasing the magnetic field.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the chiral critical temperature of strongly interacting matter can either increase or decrease with an external magnetic field, depending on whether the Polyakov-loop potential is implemented in its standard (non-renormalized) form or with a renormalization factor; the renormalized approach reproduces the recent lattice QCD result that the chiral critical temperature decreases with increasing magnetic field, while the non-renormalized approach yields the opposite trend.

## Method

The authors study the SU(3) Polyakov linear sigma-model (PLSM) at finite temperature and in a constant external magnetic field, using the mean-field approximation and incorporating Landau quantization for the quarks. They implement two versions of the model: one with the standard polynomial Polyakov-loop potential and one where the Polyakov-loop field is renormalized by a temperature- and magnetic-field-dependent factor Z, and a fermionic vacuum loop term is added. The chiral condensates (non-strange sigma_x and strange sigma_y) and the Polyakov-loop expectation value are obtained by minimizing the thermodynamic potential at each temperature and magnetic field value.

## Key numbers

* The chiral critical temperature in the renormalized approach decreases with increasing magnetic field, as shown for eB = 0.019 GeV^2, 0.2 GeV^2, and 0.4 GeV^2 (Fig. 3, left panel).
* The chiral critical temperature in the non-renormalized approach increases with increasing magnetic field, as shown for eB = 0.019 GeV^2, 0.2 GeV^2, and 0.4 GeV^2 (Fig. 3, right panel).
* The Polyakov-loop potential parameters are fitted to pure gauge lattice QCD: a_0 = 6.75, a_1 = -1.95, a_2 = 2.625, a_3 = -7.44, b_3 = 0.75, b_4 = 7.5, with T_0 = 187 MeV (Table I and Table II).
* The renormalized approach uses additional parameters T_s = 270 MeV and phi_0 = 0.99 (Table II).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the magnetic field effect on the QCD phase transition using the PNJL model (Marco 2010, Marco 2011) and PLSM (Fraga 2013, Skokov 2011), which all found that the chiral critical temperature increases with the magnetic field. The authors aim to reconcile these model results with the newer lattice QCD calculations by Bali (2011) that show the chiral critical temperature decreasing with the magnetic field. The renormalized Polyakov-loop approach introduced here reproduces the lattice QCD trend, while the standard approach reproduces the earlier model results. This enables further study of the magnetic properties of the quark-gluon plasma and the role of Polyakov-loop renormalization in effective models.
