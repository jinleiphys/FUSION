# Isospectral Potentials and Quantum Mechanical Functions for Neutron-Neutron Scattering

> In this paper we have constructed inverse isospectral potentials for 1S0-nn state by fitting the experimental SPS using Variational Monte-Carlo technique in tandem with PFM technique. The isospectral potentials are obtained such that the cost measure i.e., mean absolute error (MAE) between the obtained and experimental SPS are less than 1 and the parameters give the low energy scattering parameters (`a' and `re') very close to the experimental values. The S-channel SPS for 1S0-nn have been obtained, with a MAE with respect to experimental data for lab energies up to 350 MeV, to less than 1.

# Isospectral Potentials and Quantum Mechanical Functions for Neutron-Neutron Scattering

## Key claim

The paper constructs inverse isospectral potentials for the 1S0 neutron-neutron state that reproduce experimental scattering phase shifts (SPS) with a mean absolute error (MAE) less than 1 for lab energies up to 350 MeV, but the claim is conditional: the potentials are obtained via a combination of Variational Monte-Carlo (VMC) and Phase Function Method (PFM) such that the cost measure (MAE) between obtained and experimental SPS is less than 1, and the resulting low-energy scattering parameters (scattering length `a` and effective range `re`) are only "very close to" (not exactly matching) the experimental values.

## Method

The authors model the neutron-neutron interaction using a Morse potential with three parameters (depth V0, equilibrium distance rm, and range parameter am). They optimize these parameters by fitting to the experimental SPS data of Wiringa et al. using two approaches: Global Optimization Approach (GOA), which fits all 11 SPS data points simultaneously, and Combinatorial Data Analysis (CDA), which fits only three SPS data points at a time to generate a family of isospectral potentials. The optimization is performed by integrating the first-order Riccati-type phase equation (Phase Function Method) using a 5th-order Runge-Kutta method, with a Variational Monte-Carlo procedure that varies the potential parameters to minimize the mean percentage error between simulated and experimental phase shifts.

## Key numbers

* GOA optimized Morse parameters: V0 = 67.523 MeV, rm = 0.925 fm, am = 0.376 fm, yielding MAE = 0.5.
* GOA results: scattering length a = -17.64 fm (experimental: -18.5(0.4) fm), effective range re = 2.58 fm (experimental: 2.8(0.11) fm), virtual state energy Enn = 0.117 MeV (experimental: 0.1293(158) MeV).
* CDA results (from 21 combinations with MAE < 1): average scattering length a = -16.49(1.56) fm, average effective range re = 2.56(0.02) fm, virtual state energy Enn = 0.135(22) MeV.
* CDA extreme parameter sets: [V0 = 74.427 MeV, rm = 0.915 fm, am = 0.359 fm] with MAE = 0.7, and [V0 = 65.191 MeV, rm = 0.915 fm, am = 0.381 fm] with MAE = 0.9.
* The SPS fits cover lab energies up to 350 MeV; at lab energies above 250 MeV the 1S0 phase shift becomes negative.

## Context

This work extends prior inverse-potential studies by Darewych et al. (who found substantial difference between np and nn potential curves, observing "violation of charge independence") and by Babenko and Petrov (who observed charge dependence of nuclear forces associated with pion mass differences). The method improves upon earlier square well, exponential, Gaussian, or Hulthen potential fits by using the more realistic Morse potential, and it enables future application of the GOA plus CDA approach to higher partial waves of neutron-neutron scattering. The paper also references the work of Henley and others on small departures from charge symmetry and charge independence, and the sensitivity of scattering length calculations to potential changes noted by Konobeevski et al.
