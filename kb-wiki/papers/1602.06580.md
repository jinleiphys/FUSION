# Rotating quark-gluon plasma in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We study the rotational collective motion of the quark-gluon plasma in relativistic heavy ion collisions using the widely-adopted AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model. The global angular momentum, the average vorticity carried by the quark-gluon plasma, and the locally defined vorticity fields are computed for Au+Au collisions, with detailed information of their time evolution, spatial distribution, as well as the dependence on beam energy and collision centrality.

## Key claim

The paper quantifies the rotational motion of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in non-central Au+Au collisions using the AMPT model, but the abstract's claim of "average vorticity" is qualified by the fact that the local vorticity field is dominated by background radial flow contributions unrelated to global rotation; only after a carefully chosen fireball average (using a moment-of-inertia-like weighting) does the residual vorticity reflect the true global rotation.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at various beam energies and impact parameters. They extracted the partonic matter's position and momentum at each time step, coarse-grained the particle distributions into fluid cells (0.8 fm × 0.8 fm on the transverse plane over rapidity slices of 0.4 units), and computed the velocity field as the ratio of momentum density to energy density per cell. The local vorticity was obtained via finite-difference derivatives of the velocity field, and the fireball-averaged out-of-plane vorticity was computed using a weighting function of ρ² ε(r), where ρ is the distance from the rotation axis and ε is the energy density.

## Key numbers

- The QGP fireball carries about 10–20% of the total angular momentum of the colliding system (J = A b √s_NN / 2), and this fraction is essentially constant in time.
- For Au+Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV and b = 7 fm, the averaged out-of-plane vorticity ⟨ω_y⟩ peaks around 1 fm/c at approximately 0.04–0.05 fm⁻¹ (depending on centrality) and then decays steadily.
- The averaged vorticity increases from central to peripheral collisions: at √s_NN = 200 GeV, ⟨ω_y⟩ at b = 9 fm is roughly twice that at b = 5 fm at early times.
- The averaged vorticity increases with decreasing beam energy: at b = 7 fm, ⟨ω_y⟩ at √s_NN = 39 GeV is about 2–3 times larger than at √s_NN = 200 GeV at early times.
- The angular momentum J_y from AMPT is about 2–3 times larger than that from a simple hard sphere model at the same beam energy and impact parameter.
- The maximum J_y occurs at b ≈ 4 fm for √s_NN = 200 GeV in the AMPT model.

## Context

This work extends earlier estimates of QGP rotation from simple hard sphere models (Becattini 2007) and hydrodynamic simulations (Becattini 2013, Csernai 2013, Becattini 2015) by providing the first systematic quantification using the AMPT transport model, which includes parton cascade and realistic nuclear profiles. The results provide crucial input for modeling vorticity-driven phenomena such as the Chiral Vortical Effect (Kharzeev 2007, Son 2009) and the Chiral Vortical Wave (Jiang 2015), enabling quantitative predictions for baryon separation and elliptic flow splitting measurements at RHIC and LHC.
