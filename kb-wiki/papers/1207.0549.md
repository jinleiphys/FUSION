# Nuclear Quasi-Elastic Electron Scattering Limits Nucleon Off-Mass Shell Properties

> The use of quasi-elastic electron nucleus scattering is shown to provide significant constraints on models of the proton electromagnetic form factor of off-shell nucleons. Such models can be constructed to be consistent with constraints from current conservation and low-energy theorems, while also providing a contribution to the Lamb shift that might potentially resolve the proton radius puzzle in muonic hydrogen. However, observations of quasi-elastic scattering limit the overall strength of the off-shell form factors to values that correspond to small contributions to the Lamb shift.

# Nuclear Quasi-Elastic Electron Scattering Limits Nucleon Off-Mass Shell Properties

## Key claim

While models of off-shell proton electromagnetic form factors can be constructed to satisfy current conservation and low-energy theorems, and could in principle contribute to the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen, quasi-elastic electron-nucleus scattering data constrain the overall strength of such off-shell form factors to values that produce Lamb shift contributions far too small (by a factor of about 100) to resolve the proton radius puzzle.

## Method

The authors computed the medium modification of the proton electromagnetic vertex in quasi-elastic electron scattering using a relativistic Fermi gas model for the nuclear wave function, with the bound nucleon's off-shell behavior parameterized by a strength parameter λ. They constructed a new gauge-invariant off-shell vertex operator that satisfies the constraint that the ratio G_E/G_M remains unchanged in the medium, then used this model to compute the two-photon exchange contribution to the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen via a Wick-rotated integral over the virtual photon momentum.

## Key numbers

- The model of Miller (2011) used λ/b² = 2/(79 MeV)² and Λ = 841 MeV, yielding a predicted medium modification of G_E/G_M between 6 and 25 times larger than the observed ~10% reduction in the double ratio from Paolone (2010) for Q² between 0.4 and 2.6 GeV²
- The constraint from quasi-elastic scattering gives |λ| F(Q²) < 7 for Q² < 10 GeV², using ε = -0.007 (the ratio of average nuclear binding energy 7 MeV to nucleon mass)
- With λ = 7, the computed Lamb shift contribution is -0.001 meV, approximately 100 times smaller than the 0.31 meV needed to resolve the proton radius puzzle
- The maximum possible shift from this class of models is -0.005 meV, with larger λ producing a positive shift of the wrong sign

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Miller (2011), who proposed that off-shell proton form factors in two-photon exchange could explain the proton radius puzzle from Pohl (2010). It addresses constraints from Carlson (2011) using dispersion relations and chiral perturbation theory, and from Birse and McGovern (2012) at fourth order. The paper shows that quasi-elastic scattering data from Paolone (2010) on ⁴He(e,e'p)³H provide independent and stringent limits on such off-shell models, ruling out the original Miller (2011) model and showing that even improved models consistent with chiral perturbation theory cannot produce a large enough Lamb shift without contradicting nuclear scattering observations.
