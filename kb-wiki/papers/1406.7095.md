# First applications of Fayans functional to deformed nuclei

> First calculations for deformed nuclei with the Fayans functional are carried out for the uranium and lead isotopic chains. The ground state deformations and deformation energies are compared to Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov results of HFB-17 and HFB-27 functionals. For the uranium isotopic chain, the Fayans functional predictions are rather similar properties compared to HFB-17 and HFB-27. However, there is a disagreement for the lead isotopic chain. Both of the Skyrme HFB functionals predict rather strong deformations for the light Pb isotopes which does not agree with the experimental data on charge radii and magnetic moments of the odd Pb isotopes. On the other hand, the Fayans functional predicts a spherical ground state for all of the lead isotopes, in accordance with the data and the known in literature results obtained with the Gogny D1S force and SLy6 functional as well. The deformation energy curves are calculated and compared to four Skyrme functionals, SLy4, Sly6, SkM* and UNEDF1, for $^{238}$U nucleus and several lead deficient Pb isotopes. In the first case, the Fayans functional result is rather close to SkM* and UNEDF1 which, in particularly the latter one, describe the first and second barriers in $^{238}$U rather well. For the light lead isotopes, the Fayans deformation energy curves are qualitatively close to those of the SLy6 functional.

## Key claim

The Fayans functional (FaNDF$^0$) predicts a spherical ground state for all lead isotopes, in agreement with experimental data on charge radii and magnetic moments, whereas the Skyrme HFB-17 and HFB-27 functionals predict rather strong deformations for light Pb isotopes that disagree with experiment.

## Method

The authors implemented the Fayans functional FaNDF$^0$ in the axially-deformed HFBTHO code, using a localized form of the Yukawa function to make the surface part of the energy density functional similar to Skyrme functionals. They calculated ground state deformations, deformation energies, and two-neutron separation energies for even uranium and lead isotopes, and computed deformation energy curves up to the second fission barrier for $^{238}$U. Results were compared with Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations using HFB-17, HFB-27, SLy4, SLy6, SkM*, and UNEDF1 functionals.

## Key numbers

- For the uranium chain, the FaNDF$^0$ functional gives two-neutron separation energies deviating by 0.5 MeV from experimental values for heavy U isotopes.
- The FaNDF$^0$ functional uses nuclear matter parameters: incompressibility $K_0 = 220$ MeV, equilibrium density $\rho_0 = 0.160$ fm$^{-3}$, energy per particle $\mu = -16.0$ MeV, and asymmetry energy coefficient $a_{\rm sym} = 30.0$ MeV.
- The skewness coefficient for symmetric nuclear matter with FaNDF$^0$ is $Q_0 = -427.14$ MeV, compared to $-386.09$ MeV for SkM* and $-363.11$ MeV for SLy4.
- The symmetry energy slope parameter for FaNDF$^0$ is $L_0 = 29.96$ MeV, compared to $45.78$ MeV for SkM* and $45.94$ MeV for SLy4.
- For the lead chain, HFB-17 and HFB-27 predict deformations $|\beta_2| \simeq 0.2$--$0.3$ for light $^{180-192}$Pb isotopes, while FaNDF$^0$ predicts spherical ground states for all lead isotopes.
- The pairing strength parameters used are $f^{\xi} = -0.440$ for U isotopes and $f^{\xi} = -0.448$ for Pb isotopes, with cutoff energy $E_{\rm cut} = 60$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Fayans functional, previously applied only to spherical nuclei, to deformed nuclei for the first time. It builds on the self-consistent theory of finite Fermi systems (TFFS) developed by Khodel and Saperstein, and on the DF3 and DF3a parameter sets of Fayans and collaborators. The results for lead isotopes agree with generator coordinate method calculations using the Gogny D1S force by Robledo and with SLy6 calculations by Bender, both of which found spherical ground states for neutron-deficient Pb isotopes, contradicting the HFB-17 and HFB-27 predictions from the Brussels-Montreal Collaboration. This work enables future systematic studies of deformation properties across the nuclear chart using the Fayans functional.
