# Properties of heavy mesons at finite temperature

> We study the properties of heavy mesons using a unitarized approach in a hot pionic medium, based on an effective hadronic theory. The interaction between the heavy mesons and pseudoscalar Goldstone bosons is described by a chiral Lagrangian at next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion and leading order in the heavy-quark mass expansion so as to satisfy heavy-quark spin symmetry. The meson-meson scattering problem in coupled channels with finite-temperature corrections is solved in a self-consistent manner. Our results show that the masses of the ground-state charmed mesons $D(0^-)$ and $D_s(1^-)$ decrease in a pionic environment at $T\neq 0$ and they acquire a substantial width. As a consequence, the behaviour of excited mesonic states (i.e. $D_{s0}^*(2317)^\pm$ and $D_0^*(2300)^{0,\pm}$), generated dynamically in our heavy-light molecular model, is also modified at $T\neq 0$. The aim is to test our results against Lattice QCD calculations in the future.

## Key claim

The masses of ground-state charmed mesons decrease in a hot pionic medium and they acquire a substantial width, which in turn modifies the properties of excited mesonic states (such as the \(D_{s0}^*(2317)^\pm\) and \(D_0^*(2300)^{0,\pm}\)) that are dynamically generated in the heavy-light molecular model; the authors aim to test these results against future Lattice QCD calculations.

## Method

The interaction between heavy mesons (\(D^{(*)}\), \(D_s^{(*)}\)) and pseudoscalar Goldstone bosons (\(\pi, K, \bar{K}, \eta\)) was described by a chiral Lagrangian at next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion and leading order in the heavy-quark mass expansion, satisfying heavy-quark spin symmetry. The meson-meson scattering problem in coupled channels was solved using the Bethe-Salpeter equation, with finite-temperature corrections included via the imaginary time formalism (Matsubara frequencies). The self-energy of the heavy mesons was computed from the unitarized \(T\)-matrix, and the propagators were dressed iteratively until self-consistency was achieved, using a momentum cutoff regularization.

## Key numbers

*   At \(T=150\) MeV, the mass shift of the \(D\)-meson is about \(13\%\) of its mass, while for the \(D_s\)-meson it is about \(1\%\).
*   At \(T=150\) MeV, the width of the \(D\)-meson is \(\Gamma_D \sim 50\) MeV, and the width of the \(D_s\)-meson is \(\Gamma_{D_s} \sim 7\) MeV.
*   For the charmed vector mesons at \(T=150\) MeV, mass shifts are \(\sim 10\%\) for \(D^*\) and \(\sim 1\%\) for \(D_s^*\), with widths of \(\sim 50\) MeV and \(\sim 10\) MeV, respectively.
*   At zero temperature, the \(D_{s0}^*(2317)^\pm\) appears as a bound state with mass \(M_R = 2252.5\) MeV and zero width, with a large \(DK\) component (compositeness \(X_{DK} = 0.66\)).
*   At zero temperature, the two-pole structure of the \(D_0^*(2300)^{0,\pm}\) gives poles at \(M_R = 2081.9\) MeV (width \(\Gamma_R/2 = 86.0\) MeV) and \(M_R = 2521.2\) MeV (width \(\Gamma_R/2 = 121.7\) MeV).
*   At \(T=150\) MeV, the \(D_{s0}^*(2317)^\pm\) moves \(\sim 5\%\) towards lower energies and acquires a width of \(\sim 30\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature unitarized chiral approach of Guo (2018) and Albaladejo (2016) to finite temperature, using the method described by Cleven (2017, 2019). The results show that the dilution of the excited states (the \(D_0^*(2300)^{0,\pm}\) and \(D_{s0}^*(2317)^\pm\)) is tied to the molecular description of the model, which would not be attained within quark models. The findings are relevant for calculating heavy-ion collision observables, and the authors plan to test their results against future Lattice QCD calculations and to extend the model to the bottom sector and hidden-charm mesons like the \(X(3872)\).
