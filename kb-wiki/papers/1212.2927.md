# Nucleons near the QCD deconfinement transition

> Using non-perturbative lattice method we studied hadronic screening correlators above and immediately below the deconfinement transition temperature, Tc, in the quenched approximation with lattice spacing of 1/(8T) using clover improved Wilson fermions. Simulations were performed at temperatures T /Tc = 0, 0.95 and 1.5. Mesonic screening correlators show no statistically significant thermal effects below Tc, and clear evidence for weakly interacting quarks above Tc . Baryon screening correlators yield similar physics above Tc, but show precursor effects for chiral symmetry restoration below Tc .

## Key claim

Using quenched lattice QCD with clover-improved Wilson fermions at a lattice spacing of 1/(8T), the authors find that mesonic screening correlators show no statistically significant thermal effects below the deconfinement temperature Tc, while baryon screening correlators reveal precursor effects for chiral symmetry restoration below Tc that are not seen in the meson sector.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations in the quenched approximation using clover-improved Wilson fermions on lattices with Nτ = 8 for finite temperature and Nτ = 32 for zero temperature, with spatial sizes such that LT = 4. They measured hadronic screening correlators at temperatures T/Tc = 0, 0.95, and 1.5, extracting screening masses from fits to cosine-hyperbolic forms for mesons and from two-state fits for nucleons, using wall sources in Coulomb gauge to improve statistics.

## Key numbers

- At T = 0.95Tc, the ratio μV/mV = 0.97 ± 0.02 for mπ/Tc = 2.20, indicating no significant thermal shift in the vector meson mass
- At T = 0.95Tc, the ratio μN+/mN+ = 1.05 ± 0.05 for mπ/Tc = 2.20, showing no significant thermal effect on the nucleon ground state
- The mass splitting between the nucleon and its parity partner at T = 0.95Tc is (μN- - μN+)/Tc = 1.15 ± 0.14 for mπ/Tc = 2.20, compared to (mN- - mN+)/Tc = 1.53 ± 0.09 at T = 0, showing a thermal shift
- At T = 1.5Tc, meson screening masses are within 2-3% of free field theory values (e.g., μV/T = 5.88 ± 0.02 for mπ/Tc = 1.52, compared to FFT value of 5.99)
- At T = 1.5Tc, nucleon screening masses are within 3-4% of free field theory values (e.g., μN+/T = 8.72 ± 0.10 for mπ/Tc = 1.52, compared to FFT value of 8.44)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of meson screening masses by Detar and Karsch (2001), Gavai et al. (2002), and others, while rebooting the study of baryon screening correlators that had not been extended to the low-temperature phase since the work of Detar and Karsch (2001) and Gavai et al. (2002). The observation of precursor effects in the nucleon parity partner mass splitting below Tc provides the first evidence of chiral symmetry restoration precursors in the closing of mass gaps of parity-related states, which constrains models of quantum hadrodynamics by Rapp and Wambach (2000) and could impact analysis of heavy-ion collision data from the LHC.
