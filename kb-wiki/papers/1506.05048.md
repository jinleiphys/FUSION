# Neutrino-driven winds in the aftermath of a neutron star merger: nucleosynthesis and electromagnetic transients

> We present a comprehensive nucleosynthesis study of the neutrino-driven wind in the aftermath of a binary neutron star merger. Our focus is the initial remnant phase when a massive central neutron star is present. Using tracers from a recent hydrodynamical simulation, we determine total masses and integrated abundances to characterize the composition of unbound matter. We find that the nucleosynthetic yields depend sensitively on both the life time of the massive neutron star and the polar angle. Matter in excess of up to $9 \cdot 10^{-3} M_\odot$ becomes unbound until $\sim 200~{\rm ms}$. Due to electron fractions of $Y_{\rm e} \approx 0.2 - 0.4$ mainly nuclei with mass numbers $A < 130$ are synthesized, complementing the yields from the earlier dynamic ejecta. Mixing scenarios with these two types of ejecta can explain the abundance pattern in r-process enriched metal-poor stars. Additionally, we calculate heating rates for the decay of the freshly produced radioactive isotopes. The resulting light curve peaks in the blue band after about $4~{\rm h}$. Furthermore, high opacities due to heavy r-process nuclei in the dynamic ejecta lead to a second peak in the infrared after $3-4~{\rm d}$.

## Key claim

The nucleosynthetic yields from the neutrino-driven wind in a binary neutron star merger remnant depend sensitively on both the lifetime of the massive neutron star and the polar angle, with the caveat that the very small amount of heavy elements (A > 130) produced in the initial phase of the Newtonian simulation may not be present in a general relativistic simulation due to faster evolution toward equilibrium electron fraction.

## Method

The authors used tracer particles from a three-dimensional Newtonian hydrodynamical simulation of a binary neutron star merger remnant, employing the FISH grid code with the TM1 nuclear equation of state and the multiflavor Advanced Spectral Leakage scheme for neutrino interactions. They performed nucleosynthesis post-processing calculations for over 17,000 ejected tracers using a complete network of over 5,800 nuclei from hydrogen to roentgenium, with reaction rates from the Finite Range Droplet Model and weak interaction rates including neutrino absorption on nucleons. The tracers were divided into four angular bins covering polar angles from 0 to 60 degrees to study the angular dependence of the nucleosynthesis.

## Key numbers

- Up to 9 × 10⁻³ M⊙ of matter becomes unbound until approximately 200 ms after merger
- The accretion disk initial mass is M_disk ≈ 0.19 M⊙
- Electron fractions in the wind range from Y_e ≈ 0.2 to 0.4
- At the end of the simulation (t_sim ≈ 190 ms), electron neutrino luminosity from cooling is ~2.2 × 10⁵² erg/s and electron antineutrino luminosity is ~2.9 × 10⁵² erg/s
- Neutrino mean energies are approximately 11 MeV for electron neutrinos and 15.5 MeV for electron antineutrinos
- The resulting light curve peaks in the blue band after about 4 hours
- A second peak in the infrared occurs after 3-4 days
- The neutrino-driven wind ejecta are confined within a polar angle of 60 degrees from the rotational axis
- Radial velocities of unbound tracers range from 0.04c to 0.08c
- Entropy of unbound tracers ranges from 10 k_B/baryon to 30 k_B/baryon
- The threshold electron fraction for producing heavy r-process elements (A > 130) is Y_e ~ 0.25

## Context

This work extends the preliminary nucleosynthesis results of Perego et al. (2014) by using 17,000 trajectories instead of ten representative ones, providing a comprehensive study of the neutrino-driven wind component in binary neutron star mergers. The results complement studies of dynamic ejecta from simulations by Korobkin et al. (2012), Bauswein et al. (2013), and Hotokezaka et al. (2013), which produce the heaviest r-process nuclei through fission cycling. The mixing of neutrino-driven wind ejecta (producing nuclei with A < 130) with dynamic ejecta (producing heavy r-process nuclei) can explain the abundance pattern observed in r-process enriched metal-poor stars, and the predicted two-peaked light curve enables future observational discrimination between different ejecta components in kilonovae.
