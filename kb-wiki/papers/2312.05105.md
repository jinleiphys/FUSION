# Measurement of yields and angular distributions of $γ$-quanta from the interaction of $14.1$ MeV neutrons with oxygen, phosphorus and sulfur

> A study of the inelastic scattering of neutrons with an energy of $14.1$~MeV on the nuclei of oxygen, phosphorus and sulfur was carried out at the TANGRA facility at JINR (Dubna). The purpose of the experiment was to refine existing and obtain new data on the yields and angular distributions of $γ$-quanta emitted by the studied nuclei as a result of neutron-induced nuclear reactions using the tagged neutron method. Two types of detector systems were used to register $γ$-quanta. The $γ$-ray yields were measured using a high-purity germanium (HPGe) detector. The angular distributions of $γ$-rays were obtained using a system of 18 scintillation detectors based on bismuth germanite Bi$_{4}$Ge$_{3}$O$_{12}$ (BGO) located around the sample. As a result of the studies carried out, the yields of two transitions for the reaction of tagged neutrons with $^{16}$O, nine transitions for the reaction with $^{31}$P, and nine transitions for the reaction with $^{32}$S were measured for the first time. The angular anisotropy of the $γ$-radiation accompanying the inelastic scattering of neutrons with an energy of $14.1$~MeV on $^{31}$P nuclei was also measured for the first time.

## Key claim

The paper reports new measurements of gamma-ray yields and angular distributions from 14.1 MeV neutron interactions with oxygen, phosphorus, and sulfur, claiming first-time determinations for several transitions; however, the yields are normalized to the strongest transition for each element rather than being absolute cross sections, and the angular anisotropy for phosphorus is reported as a first measurement.

## Method

The experiment used the tagged neutron method (TNM) at the TANGRA facility (JINR, Dubna) with a portable ING-27 neutron generator producing 14.1 MeV neutrons via the d+t reaction. Gamma-ray yields were measured with a high-purity germanium (HPGe) detector (energy resolution 1.9 keV at 1.33 MeV), while angular distributions were obtained using a system of 18 bismuth germanate (BGO) scintillation detectors arranged in 14-degree increments around the sample. Data were collected in coincidence with a position-sensitive alpha-particle detector (64 pixels) to tag neutron direction, and background was subtracted using time-of-flight spectra and separate measurements without samples.

## Key numbers

- HPGe detector energy resolution: 1.9 keV at 1.33 MeV; BGO detector resolution: about 110 keV at 1.33 MeV
- For oxygen (normalized to the 6129.9 keV transition = 100%): yield of 169.3 keV transition from 16O(n,alpha)13C = 32.9% (3.7% statistical uncertainty); yield of 298.2 keV transition from 16O(n,p)16N = 43.4% (3.9% statistical uncertainty); yield of 2742.0 keV transition = 34.7% (4.3%); yield of 3684.5 keV transition = 35.9% (6.3%); yield of 3853.8 keV transition = 33.0% (4.1%); yield of 6917.1 keV transition = 31.9% (3.9%); yield of 7116.9 keV transition = 34.5% (5.1%)
- For phosphorus (normalized to the sum of 2233.6, 2235.3, and 2240.0 keV transitions = 100%): yield of 1263.3/1266.1 keV doublet = 56.9% (9.0%); yield of 2028.9 keV transition = 14.8% (2.5%); yield of 2148.5 keV transition = 14.0% (3.0%)
- HPGe time resolution: between 26 ns (600-900 keV energy interval) and 17 ns (3600-3900 keV interval)
- Sample dimensions: 6 x 6 x 14 cm3; P2O5 bulk density 0.903 g/cm3, mass 455 g; sulfur bulk density 1.244 g/cm3, mass 627 g
- Estimated systematic uncertainties do not exceed 5% of the yield values for oxygen

## Context

This work extends the TANGRA project's systematic study of 14 MeV neutron inelastic scattering on light and medium nuclei, following previous measurements by Grozdanov (2018), Fedorov (2021), Fedorov (2020), Grozdanov (2020), and Dabylova (2021). The results are compared with calculations using the TALYS 1.96 code with default parameters and with published data from Simakov (1998), Nyberg-Ponnert, Kozlowski (1965), Morgan (1964), and McDonald (1966), as well as with ENDF/B-VIII.0 evaluated data. The new measurements provide data for nuclear reaction modeling and for applications in fast neutron reactor design and elemental analysis, particularly for oxygen, phosphorus, and sulfur which are common in ores and industrial materials.
