# Isospin mixing in a particle-number conserving microscopic approach

> We calculate the isospin-mixing parameter for several Tz=-1, Tz=0 and Tz=1 nuclei from Mg to Sn in the particle-number conserving Higher Tamm-Dancoff approach taking into account the pairing correlations. In particular we investigate the role of the Coulomb interaction and the |Tz|=1 pairing correlations. To do so the HTDA approach is implemented with the SIII Skyrme effective nucleon-nucleon interaction in the mean-field channel and a delta interaction in the pairing channel. We conclude from this investigation that the pairing correlations bring a large contribution to isospin-symmetry breaking, whereas the Coulomb interaction turns out to play a less important role. Moreover we find that the isospin-mixing parameters for Tz=-1 and Tz=1 nuclei are comparable while they are about twice as large for Tz=0 nuclei (between 3% and 6%, including doubly magic nuclei).

## Key claim

The paper calculates isospin-mixing parameters for nuclei from Mg to Sn using a particle-number conserving Higher Tamm-Dancoff approach, finding that pairing correlations contribute substantially to isospin-symmetry breaking while the Coulomb interaction plays a less important role, with the caveat that the study focuses on |Tz|=1 pairing correlations which gives an upper limit for the mixing parameter since proton-neutron pairing correlations are expected to reduce it.

## Method

The authors implemented the Higher Tamm-Dancoff approach (HTDA) with the SIII Skyrme effective nucleon-nucleon interaction in the mean-field channel and a delta interaction in the pairing channel, building correlated ground states as superpositions of Slater determinants on top of Hartree-Fock solutions. They calculated the expectation value of the square of the isospin operator in the HTDA ground state for 24 even-even nuclei with Tz=-1, Tz=0, and Tz=1 across eight elements (Z=12 to Z=50), then extracted the isospin-mixing parameter alpha^2 from the deviation of this expectation value from the pure isospin value T0(T0+1).

## Key numbers

- Isospin-mixing parameter alpha^2 for Tz=0 nuclei ranges between 3% and 6%, including doubly magic nuclei (e.g., 4.2% for 40Ca, 5.7% for 100Sn)
- For Tz=-1 and Tz=1 nuclei, alpha^2 values are comparable and about a factor of two smaller than for Tz=0 nuclei (e.g., 1.3% for 22Mg, 1.5% for 26Mg, 2.3% for 98Sn and 102Sn)
- Without Coulomb interaction, alpha^2 for 40Ca drops from 4.2% to 3.5%; for 100Sn it drops from 5.7% to 1.8%
- The hydrodynamical model of Bohr, Damgaard and Mottelson gives alpha^2 = 3.5 x 10^(-7) Z^2 A^(2/3), yielding 0.16% for 40Ca and 0.04% for 48Ca

## Context

This work extends prior mean-field calculations of isospin mixing that included RPA-type correlations (Sagawa, van Giai) but omitted pairing correlations, which the authors show contribute substantially to isospin-symmetry breaking. The hydrodynamical approach of Bohr, Damgaard and Mottelson (1958) and the shell-model calculations of Ormand, Zuker, and others are discussed as alternative methods with different limitations. The particle-number conserving HTDA approach avoids the spurious isospin mixing that would arise from BCS or Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov treatments, and the results are relevant for correcting superallowed 0+-to-0+ beta decay ft-values in tests of the CVC hypothesis, providing the delta_C corrective term.
