# Isoscalar dipole transition as a probe for asymmetric clustering

> Background: The sharp $1^-$ resonances with enhanced isoscalar dipole transition strengths are observed in many light nuclei at relatively small excitation energies, but their nature was unclear. Purpose: We show those resonances can be attributed to the cluster states with asymmetric configurations such as $帢$+$^{16}{\rm O}$. We explain why asymmetric cluster states are strongly excited by the isoscalar dipole transition. We also provide a theoretical prediction of the isoscalar dipole transitions in $^{20}{\rm Ne}$ and $^{44}{\rm Ti}$. Method: The transition matrix is analytically derived to clarify the excitation mechanism. The nuclear model calculations by Brink-Bloch wave function and antisymmetrized molecular dynamics are also performed to provide a theoretical prediction for $^{20}{\rm Ne}$ and $^{44}{\rm Ti}$. Results: It is shown that the transition matrix is as large as the Weisskopf estimate even though the ground state is an ideal shell model state. Furthermore, it is considerably amplified if the ground state has cluster correlation. The nuclear model calculations predict large transition matrix to the $帢$+$^{16}{\rm O}$ and $帢$+$^{40}{\rm Ca}$ cluster states comparable with or larger than the Weisskopf estimate. Conclusion: We conclude that the asymmetric cluster states are strongly excited by the isoscalar dipole transition. Combined with the isoscalar monopole transition that populates the $0^+$ cluster states, the isoscalar transitions are promising probe for asymmetric clusters.

## Key claim
Sharp 1- resonances with enhanced isoscalar dipole transition strengths observed in many light nuclei can be attributed to cluster states with asymmetric configurations such as 帢+16O, and the transition matrix is as large as the Weisskopf estimate even when the ground state is an ideal shell model state, with further amplification if the ground state has cluster correlation.

## Method
The authors analytically derived the isoscalar dipole transition matrix using shell model and cluster model wave functions for 20Ne and 44Ti, expressing the ground state via the Bayman-Bohr theorem as equivalent to 帢+16O and 帢+40Ca cluster wave functions. They performed nuclear model calculations using the Brink-Bloch wave function with the generator coordinate method (GCM) and antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD), both employing the Gogny D1S effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, to provide theoretical predictions for the transition strengths.

## Key numbers
- For 20Ne, the analytically estimated reduced matrix element M^IS1 = 5.82 fm^3, compared to the Weisskopf estimate of approximately 8.44 fm^3.
- For 44Ti, the analytically estimated reduced matrix element M^IS1 = 14.6 fm^3, compared to the Weisskopf estimate of approximately 18.6 fm^3.
- The oscillator parameter 彖 = 0.16 fm^-2 for 20Ne and 彖 = 0.12 fm^-2 for 44Ti, chosen to minimize ground state energies.
- The principal quantum number of intercluster motion is N0 = 8 for 20Ne and N0 = 12 for 44Ti.
- The 1-1 state of 20Ne is at 5.8 MeV (1.1 MeV above the 帢 threshold), and the 0+4 state is at 8.7 MeV (4 MeV above the 帢 threshold).

## Context
This work extends the explanation by Yamada et al. (2008) of how isoscalar monopole transitions strongly excite cluster states, applying a similar mechanism to the isoscalar dipole transition for asymmetric cluster systems. It provides a theoretical foundation for interpreting experimental observations of low-lying 1- resonances in light nuclei such as 32S (Itoh et al., 2013) and supports the use of isoscalar dipole transitions, combined with isoscalar monopole transitions, as a probe for asymmetric clustering in N=Z nuclei where electric dipole transitions are not available. The results enable future experimental searches for asymmetric cluster states in nuclei such as 20Ne and 44Ti.
