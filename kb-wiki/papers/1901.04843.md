# In-medium properties of the low-lying bottom baryons in the quark-meson coupling model

> In-medium properties of the low-lying baryons are studied in the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, focusing on the $Σ_b$ and $Ξ_b$ baryons. It is predicted that the Lorentz-scalar effective mass of $Σ_b$ becomes smaller than that of $Ξ_b$ at moderate nuclear matter density, and as the density increases, namely, $m^*_{Σ_b} < m^*_{Ξ_b}$, although in vacuum $m_{Σ_b} > m_{Ξ_b}$. We also study the effects of the repulsive Lorentz-vector potentials on the excitation energies of these bottom baryons.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the Lorentz-scalar effective mass of the Σ_b baryon becomes smaller than that of the Ξ_b baryon at nuclear matter densities above about 0.3 ρ₀, reversing their vacuum mass ordering (m_Σ_b > m_Ξ_b in vacuum). This reversal is conditional on the specific quark-meson coupling model treatment and the density threshold of approximately 0.3 times normal nuclear matter density.

## Method

The author uses the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, a quark-based model of nuclear matter where meson fields (σ, ω, ρ) couple only to the light u and d quarks within baryons, while heavy quarks (b) remain unaffected. Within the Hartree mean-field approximation for symmetric nuclear matter, the Dirac equations for quarks in MIT bags are solved self-consistently to compute the in-medium baryon masses and excitation energies, with the bag radii and eigenvalues adjusted at each density. The Lorentz-scalar effective masses and Lorentz-vector potentials are then extracted for the Σ_b (qqb) and Ξ_b (qsb) baryons.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³
- Vacuum masses: m_Σ_b = 5813.4 MeV, m_Ξ_b = 5793.2 MeV (Σ_b heavier than Ξ_b in vacuum)
- At ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³: Lorentz-scalar effective mass m*_Σ_b = 5692.8 MeV, m*_Ξ_b = 5732.7 MeV (Σ_b effective mass becomes lighter)
- The reversal m*_Σ_b < m*_Ξ_b occurs at baryon density larger than about 0.3 ρ₀
- For the realistic case without Pauli potentials, excitation energies of Σ_b and Ξ_b are nearly degenerate in the density range 0.5 ρ₀ < ρ_B < 1.5 ρ₀

## Context

This work extends the QMC model, previously applied to nucleons and strange hyperons by Guichon (1987) and Saito (2005), to the bottom baryon sector (Σ_b and Ξ_b). It builds on the author's recent article (Tsushima, 2018) and uses updated baryon masses from the Particle Data Group (2018) and recent LHCb data for Σ_b (Aaij, 2018). The predictions enable future experimental searches for medium modifications of bottom baryons, such as measuring valence quark distributions in medium or strangeness-changing semileptonic weak decays of Ξ_b → Σ_b in dense matter, which could test dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and partial restoration of chiral symmetry.
