# Renormalization Group Approach to Confinement

> While we have several complementary models of confinement, some of which are phenomenologically appealing, we do not have the ability to calculate analytically even simple aspects of confinement, let alone have a framework to eventually prove confinement. The problem we are facing is to evolve the theory from the perturbative regime to the long distance confining regime. This is generally achieved by renormalization group transformations. With the gradient flow we now have a technique to address the problem from first principles. The primary focus is on the running coupling $α_S(μ)$, from which confinement can be concluded alone. A central point is that the gluon condensate is scale invariant, which reflects its self-similar behavior across different scales. Building on that, we derive $α_S(μ) \simeq Λ_S^2/μ^2$, which evolves to the infrared fixed point $1/α_S = 0$ in accordance with infrared slavery. The only important factor appears to be the presence of the gluon condensate, which is a universal feature that QCD shares with many other models. The analytical results are supported by numerical simulations.

# Renormalization Group Approach to Confinement

## Key claim
The paper claims that the running coupling in QCD follows $\alpha_S(\mu) \simeq \Lambda_S^2/\mu^2$ in the infrared, evolving to the fixed point $1/\alpha_S = 0$ (infrared slavery), but this result depends critically on the assumption that the gluon condensate $G$ is scale invariant under renormalization group transformations, which the author argues holds nonperturbatively using the gradient flow as a regularization tool.

## Method
The author uses the gradient flow, a continuous realization of Wilson's renormalization group transformation, to evolve gauge fields from the perturbative regime to long distances. The running coupling $\alpha_{GF}(\mu)$ is defined via the vacuum expectation value of the energy density $E(t)$ at flow time $t$, with $\mu = 1/\sqrt{8t}$. The scale invariance of the gluon condensate $G$ is exploited to derive the beta function $\beta(\alpha_{GF}) = -2\alpha_{GF}$, which yields the infrared behavior of the coupling. Numerical lattice simulations of pure SU(3) gauge theory at $\beta = 6.0$ on volumes $16^4$, $24^4$, and $32^4$ support the analytical results.

## Key numbers
- The running coupling in the gradient flow scheme: $\alpha_{GF}(\mu) = \Lambda_{GF}^2/\mu^2$, with $\Lambda_{GF}^2 = (\pi/8)\sqrt{G/3}$
- In the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme: $\Lambda_{\overline{\text{MS}}} = 0.534\,\Lambda_{GF}$ for pure gauge theory
- Gluon condensate in $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme: $G = 19.45\,\Lambda_{\overline{\text{MS}}}^4$, giving $G = 0.082(3)\,\text{GeV}^4 \equiv (535(4)\,\text{MeV})^4$ for $\Lambda_{\overline{\text{MS}}} = 0.255(4)\,\text{GeV}$
- Ratio of gluon condensate to topological susceptibility: $G/\chi_t = 75.7$, compared to $\chi_t = (180(4)\,\text{MeV})^4$
- String tension from the static potential: $\sigma = (2/3)\Lambda_V^2$, with $\Lambda_V = 1.61\,\Lambda_{\overline{\text{MS}}} = 0.41(1)\,\text{GeV}$
- Approximately 60% of the gluon condensate magnitude is due to instantons, based on $G_{Q=0}/G \approx 0.4$ at $t/L^2 = 0.21$

## Context
This work extends the author's previous results (Schierholz 2024) and provides a first-principles renormalization group derivation of confinement, building on the gradient flow technique introduced by Luscher (2010). The result that $\alpha_S(\mu) \propto 1/\mu^2$ in the infrared, bypassing the Landau pole, realizes the long-standing concept of infrared slavery. The approach contrasts with earlier models of confinement based on monopoles and vortices (t Hooft 1981, Kronfeld 1987) and with the operator product expansion derivations of the gluon condensate's scale invariance (Shifman 1978, Novikov 1979). The numerical results for the gluon condensate are compared to lattice calculations by Horsley (2012) and Bali (2014), and to the original sum rule estimate by Shifman (1978). The work enables a framework to potentially prove confinement by establishing the existence of a nonvanishing gluon condensate, and provides a connection to the dual superconductor model of confinement through abelian projection.
