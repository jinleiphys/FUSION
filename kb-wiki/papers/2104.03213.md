# Genuine empirical pressure within the proton

> A phenomenological extraction of pressure within the proton has recently been performed using JLab CLAS data (arXiv:2104.02031 [nucl-ex]). The extraction used a 3-dimensional Breit frame description in which the initial and final proton states have different momenta. Instead, we obtain the two-dimensional transverse light front pressure densities that incorporate relativistic effects arising from the boosts that cause the initial and final states to differ. The mechanical radius is then determined to be $0.518~ \pm 0.062_{\mathrm{fit}} \pm 0.126_{\mathrm{sys}}~\mathrm{fm}$, which is smaller than the electric charge radius and larger than the light front momentum radius. The forces within the proton are shown to be predominantly repulsive at distances less than $0.43~\pm 0.12~\mathrm{fm}$ from the center, and predominantly attractive further out.

## Key claim

The authors use a light front formalism to obtain relativistically correct two-dimensional transverse pressure densities from the same Jefferson Lab CLAS data that previously yielded a three-dimensional Breit frame extraction, finding that the mechanical (pressure) radius is 0.518 fm, which is smaller than the electric charge radius but larger than the light front momentum radius.

## Method

The authors take the empirical D(t) form factor extracted from deeply virtual Compton scattering data by Burkert et al. (2021) and apply the light front formalism of Freese and Miller (2021) to compute two-dimensional transverse pressure densities. They decompose the pure stress tensor into isotropic, radial, and tangential pressure components, and compute the mechanical radius from the radial pressure distribution using the relation ⟨x⊥²(mech)⟩ = 4(α-1)/Λ².

## Key numbers

- Mechanical (pressure) radius: 0.518 ± 0.062(fit) ± 0.126(sys) fm
- Distance where isotropic pressure changes sign (repulsive to attractive): 0.43 ± 0.12 fm from center
- Distance where tangential pressure changes sign: 0.23 ± 0.11 fm from center
- Central isotropic pressure: P⁺p(0) = 2.35564 × 10³⁵ Pa
- D(0) = -1.47 ± 0.06 ± 0.14
- Λ² = 1.02 ± 0.13 ± 0.21 GeV²
- α = 2.76 ± 0.23 ± 0.48

## Context

This work extends the Breit frame pressure extraction of Burkert et al. (2018, 2021) by incorporating relativistic effects from boosts that were neglected in the three-dimensional analysis. The light front formalism, developed by Freese and Miller (2021) and based on earlier work by Burkardt (2002) and Miller (2007, 2009), provides relativistically correct densities that integrate out the light front direction. The results enable comparisons between the mechanical radius and other proton radii (mass, axial, charge) and motivate higher-precision DVCS measurements to better constrain the proton's internal pressure distribution.
