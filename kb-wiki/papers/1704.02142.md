# Vorticity in the QGP liquid and $Λ$ polarization at the RHIC Beam Energy Scan

> Polarization of $Λ$ hyperons and their antiparticles is calculated in a 3+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model with initial state from UrQMD hadron/string cascade. We find that, along with recent results from STAR, the mean polarization at fixed centrality decreases as a function of collision energy from 1.5% at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=7.7$ GeV to 0.2% at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV. We explore the effects which lead to such collision energy dependence, feed-down corrections and a difference between $Λ$ and $\barΛ$.

## Key claim

The mean polarization of Lambda hyperons in noncentral Au-Au collisions decreases with collision energy from about 1.6% at 7.7 GeV to less than 0.2% at 200 GeV, but this result depends on feed-down corrections from heavier hyperon decays and does not include hadronic rescattering effects which may further reduce the polarization.

## Method

The authors used a 3+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model (vHLLE code) with initial conditions from the UrQMD hadron/string cascade, tuned to reproduce basic hadron observables at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies. The mean spin vector of Lambda hyperons was calculated at a constant energy density particlization hypersurface (0.5 GeV/fm^3) using a Cooper-Frye-like formula that couples the thermal vorticity tensor to the particle production. Feed-down corrections from resonance decays (Sigma(1385), Sigma^0, and resonances up to Sigma(1670)) were applied using linear spin transfer coefficients derived from Clebsch-Gordan coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Mean polarization of primary Lambda decreases from about 1.6% at sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV to less than 0.2% at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV
- Primary Lambda constitute only 25-28% of all Lambda produced
- Feed-down corrections from resonances up to Sigma(1670) produce an additional 15% dilution of the resulting polarization
- The particlization hypersurface was set at energy density epsilon_sw=0.5 GeV/fm^3

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical calculations of Lambda polarization in hydrodynamic models (Becattini 2013, Becattini 2015, Pang 2016, Karpenko 2016, Xie 2017) and directly compares with recent STAR measurements from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program. The results enable future studies of hadronic rescattering effects on polarization and the role of magnetic field coupling to magnetic moments, which may explain the observed splitting between Lambda and anti-Lambda polarization that the current model does not reproduce.
