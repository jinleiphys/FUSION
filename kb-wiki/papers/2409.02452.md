# Deformation probes for light nuclei in their collisions at relativistic energies

> We have investigated the performance of anisotropic flows $\langle v_n^2 \rangle$, transverse momentum fluctuations $\langle δp_T^2 \rangle $, and their correlations $\langle v_n^2 δp_T \rangle$ in central collisions at relativistic energies as probes of deformation parameters $β_n$ of colliding nuclei, if these nuclei are light nuclei with large $β_n$ and different configurations of $α$ clusters. The effects from higher-order $β_n$ terms are illustrated by derived relations based on the overlap of two nuclei with uniform density distributions and by dynamic simulations of collisions of heavy nuclei whose density distributions are of a deformed Woods-Saxon (WS) form. While the linear relations between $β^2_n$, $\langle v_n^2 \rangle$, and $\langle δp_T^2 \rangle$ and that between $β^3_n$ and $\langle v_n^2 δp_T \rangle$ can be violated for extremely large $β_{n}$, they are mostly valid for realistic values of $β_n$, as long as the density distribution of colliding nuclei can be described by a deformed WS form. However, these linear relations are generally not valid with more realistic density distributions of light nuclei with $α$ clusters, and the amount of deviation depends on the detailed $α$-cluster configurations. Care must be taken when one tries to extract the deformation of light nuclei, and specific probes for $α$-cluster structures in these nuclei are very much needed.

## Key claim

The linear relations between deformation parameters squared ($\beta_n^2$) and observables like anisotropic flow squared ($\langle v_n^2 \rangle$) and transverse momentum fluctuations ($\langle \delta p_T^2 \rangle$), as well as between $\beta_n^3$ and the correlation $\langle v_n^2 \delta p_T \rangle$, are mostly valid for realistic values of $\beta_n$ when the density distribution can be described by a deformed Woods-Saxon form, but these linear relations generally fail for light nuclei with $\alpha$-cluster configurations, with the deviation depending on the detailed cluster structure.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (a multiphase transport) model with string melting to simulate central (0-5% centrality) collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. For $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr collisions, the nuclear density was parameterized by a deformed Woods-Saxon form with varying $\beta_2$ (0 to 1.5) and $\beta_3$ (0 to 0.9). For light nuclei ($^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, $^{20}$Ne, $^{10}$Be), density distributions were obtained from microscopic cluster models using Bloch-Brink or THSR wave functions with different $\alpha$-cluster configurations (triangle, chain, tetrahedron, etc.), and nucleons were sampled preserving the cluster structure.

## Key numbers

- For $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr collisions, the linear relation between $\beta_2^2$ and $\langle \epsilon_2^2 \rangle$ holds until $\beta_2^2 \gtrsim 1$; the linear relation between $\beta_3^2$ and $\langle \epsilon_3^2 \rangle$ holds until $\beta_3^2 \gtrsim 0.4$.
- The linear relation between $\beta_2^2$ and $\langle v_2^2 \rangle$ is violated for $\beta_2^2 \gtrsim 1$; the linear relation between $\beta_3^2$ and $\langle v_3^2 \rangle$ is violated for $\beta_3^2 \gtrsim 0.4$.
- The linear relation between $\beta_2^2$ and $\langle \delta p_T^2 \rangle$ is approximately valid until $\beta_2^2 \gtrsim 1$, after which the increasing trend becomes slower.
- The linear relation between $\beta_2^3$ and $\langle v_2^2 \delta p_T \rangle$ is valid until $\beta_2^3 \gtrsim 0.5$, then the slope becomes much smaller for larger $\beta_2$.
- For $^{96}$Zr, the Woods-Saxon parameters used were $R_0=5.02$ fm and $a=0.52$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the deformation-probe methodology previously applied to heavy nuclei like $^{96}$Ru, $^{96}$Zr, $^{197}$Au, and $^{238}$U (Zhang 2021, Giacalone 2021, STAR 2024) to light nuclei with large deformation and $\alpha$-cluster structures. It shows that the linear relations derived from deformed Woods-Saxon densities (Jia 2021) break down for realistic $\alpha$-cluster configurations in $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, $^{20}$Ne, and $^{10}$Be, motivating the need for new, specific probes of $\alpha$-cluster structures in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
