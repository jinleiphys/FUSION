# Color Correlations in the Proton

> A general QCD light front formalism to compute many-body color charge correlation functions due to quarks in the proton was constructed~\cite{Dumitru:2018vpr}. These enable new studies of color charge distributions in the nucleon. The analogies between such correlation functions to electric and magnetization charge densities in the proton and also to nucleon-nucleon correlations in the nucleus are discussed. Including the color charge correlations leads naturally to the removal of infrared divergences that occurs in two- and three-gluon exchange interactions in $q\bar q$-proton scattering. Extensions to include gluonic color charge correlations are discussed.

# Color Correlations in the Proton

## Key claim
The paper constructs a general QCD light-front formalism for computing many-body color charge correlation functions due to quarks in the proton, but the formalism explicitly ignores anti-quarks and the gluonic contributions are only discussed as an extension, not implemented. The quadratic and cubic correlation functions are evaluated only for a simple three-quark Fock state wave function, not for the full proton state including gluons.

## Method
The author uses light-front dynamics at infinite momentum to define a color charge density operator for quarks, then computes two-point and three-point correlation functions of this operator in the proton. The evaluation is performed using a simple three-quark light-front wave function from Frank (1995), and the resulting correlation functions are expressed in terms of functions G1 and G2 that separate same-quark and different-quark contributions. The formalism is an extension of the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model from relativistic heavy ion physics to nucleon structure.

## Key numbers
- The two-quark color charge correlation function takes the form: ⟨ρ̃^a(K_⊥ − k_⊥) ρ̃^b(k_⊥)⟩_{K_⊥} = (1/2) δ^{ab} (G1(K_⊥) − G2(k, K_⊥)), where the first term comes from both density operators acting on the same quark and the second from action on two different quarks.
- In the forward scattering limit K_⊥ = 0, the correlation function becomes G(k, 0) = 1 − G2(k, 0), and at k_⊥ = 0 the result is G(0, 0) = 0, which is a consequence of color neutrality needed to suppress infrared divergences from gluon propagators.
- The color charge density operator in the infinite momentum frame contains a factor 2π δ(x_p − x_q) δ(r^−), making it a thin disk in longitudinal coordinate.

## Context
This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model (McLerran 1993, 1994) from relativistic heavy ion physics to the structure of a single nucleon. The formalism complements the standard generalized parton distribution (GPD) approach to proton structure. The quadratic color charge correlator corresponds to Pomeron exchange and the cubic correlator to Odderon exchange, enabling new studies of color charge distributions that are analogous to electromagnetic charge densities and to short-ranged nucleon-nucleon correlations in nuclei (Hen 2016, Cruz-Torres 2017).
