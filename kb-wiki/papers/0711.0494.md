# Is Z^+(4430) a loosely bound molecular state?

> Since $Z^+(4430)$ lies very close to the threshold of $D^\ast{\bar D}_1$, we investigate whether $Z^+(4430)$ could be a loosely bound S-wave state of $D^\ast{\bar D}_1$ or $D^\ast{\bar D}^\prime_1$ with $J^P=0^-, 1^-, 2^-$, i.e., a molecular state arising from the one-pion-exchange potential. The potential from the crossed diagram is much larger than that from the diagonal scattering diagram. With various trial wave functions, we notice that the attraction from the one pion exchange potential alone is not strong enough to form a bound state with realistic pionic coupling constants deduced from the decay widths of $D_1$ and $D^\prime_1$.

## Key claim

The authors investigate whether the $Z^+(4430)$ resonance could be a loosely bound S-wave molecular state of $D^*\bar{D}_1$ or $D^*\bar{D}_1'$ with $J^P=0^-,1^-,2^-$ arising from one-pion-exchange potential, but find that the attraction from this potential alone is not strong enough to form a bound state with realistic pionic coupling constants deduced from the decay widths of $D_1$ and $D_1'$.

## Method

The authors derived the one-pion-exchange potential (OPEP) between $D^*$ and $\bar{D}_1$ (or $\bar{D}_1'$) mesons using effective chiral Lagrangians in the heavy quark limit, considering both direct scattering and crossed diagrams. They then applied a variational method with three different trial wave functions to search for bound state solutions in the $J^P=0^-,1^-,2^-$ channels for both the $D^*\bar{D}_1$ and $D^*\bar{D}_1'$ systems, using coupling constants extracted from experimental decay widths.

## Key numbers

- $Z^+(4430)$ mass: $m=4433\pm4(\mathrm{stat})\pm1(\mathrm{syst})$ MeV, width: $\Gamma=44^{+17}_{-13}(\mathrm{stat})^{+30}_{-11}(\mathrm{syst})$ MeV
- Product branching fraction: ${\cal B}(B\to KZ^+(4430))\cdot {\cal B}(Z^+(4430)\to\pi^+\psi')=(4.1\pm 1.0(\mathrm{stat})\pm1.3(\mathrm{syst}))\times 10^{-5}$
- Coupling constants used: $g=0.59\pm0.07\pm0.01$, $|g'|=|g|/3$, $|g''|=|g|$, $h=-0.56\pm0.28$, $h'=(h_1+h_2)/\Lambda_{\chi}=0.55$ GeV$^{-1}$
- Masses used: $m_{D^*}=2007$ MeV, $m_{D_1'}=2430$ MeV, $m_{D_1}=2420$ MeV, $f_{\pi}=132$ MeV, $m_{\pi}=135$ MeV
- $q^0\approx M_{D_1}-M_{D^\ast}\approx 410$ MeV $\approx 3 m_\pi$ for the crossed diagram

## Context

This work provides a dynamical study of the $Z^+(4430)$ signal, which had previously been interpreted as a threshold effect (Rosner 2007), a tetraquark state (Maiani, Polosa and Riquer 2007; Gershtein, Likhoded and Pronko 2007), a baryonium state (Qiao 2007), a molecular state in QCD sum rules (Lee, Mihara, Navarra and Nielsen 2007), or a threshold cusp (Bugg 2007). The authors find that unlike the $X(3872)$ case where one-pion exchange may contribute to binding, the OPEP alone cannot bind the $D^*\bar{D}_1$ or $D^*\bar{D}_1'$ systems, suggesting that additional dynamics (analogous to sigma meson exchange in nuclear physics) would be necessary if $Z^+(4430)$ is confirmed as a molecular state.
