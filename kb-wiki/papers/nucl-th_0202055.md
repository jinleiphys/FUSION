# Quasi free electro fission of 238U

> We present the result of a theoretical study of the quasi free electrofission of $^{238}$U. The exclusive differential cross sections for the quasi free scattering reaction stage have been calculated in PWIA, using a Macroscopic-Microscopic approach for the description of the proton bound states. The nuclear shape was parametrized in terms of cassinian ovaloids. The equilibrium deformation parameters have been calculated by minimizing the total nuclear energy. In the calculation the axially deformed Woods-Saxon single particle potential was used. The obtained single particle momentum distributions were averaged over the nuclear symmetry axis direction. The occupation numbers were calculated in the BCS approach. The fissility for the single hole excited states of the residual nucleus $^{237}$% Pa was calculated on the statistical theory grounds both without taking into account the pre-equilibrium emission of the particle, and with preequilibrium emission in the framework of the exciton model.

## Key claim
The authors present a theoretical framework for calculating exclusive quasi-free electrofission cross sections in \(^{238}\)U, but emphasize that their results serve only as a "first order magnitude guide-line" because the required exclusive \((e,e'pf)\) experiment has never been performed and involves extremely difficult practical conditions.

## Method
The exclusive differential cross sections for the quasi-free scattering stage were calculated in the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) using a macroscopic-microscopic approach for proton bound states, with the nuclear shape parametrized by Cassini ovoids and equilibrium deformation parameters obtained by minimizing the total nuclear energy. Single-particle momentum distributions were computed from an axially deformed Woods-Saxon potential, averaged over the nuclear symmetry axis, with occupation numbers from BCS theory. Fissility for single-hole excited states of the residual nucleus \(^{237}\)Pa was calculated on statistical theory grounds both without pre-equilibrium emission and with pre-equilibrium emission using the exciton model.

## Key numbers
- Equilibrium deformation parameters for \(^{238}\)U: \(\varepsilon = 0.227\) and \(\alpha_4 = 0.059\), obtained by minimizing the total nuclear energy.
- The spin-orbit potential strength constant \(\kappa = 0.63\) was used in the central potential depth parametrization.
- The anomalous magnetic moment of the proton \(\kappa_p = 1.793\) (in units of the Bohr magneton) and proton magnetic moment \(\mu_p = 2.793\) were used in the off-shell electron-nucleon cross section.
- The neutron separation energy for the first step (\(^{237}\)Pa) was taken as \(5.78\) MeV.
- The total nuclear binding energy for \(^{237}\)Pa was \(B = 1794\) MeV.
- The parameter \(K_0 = 14.39\) MeV was used in the fission-to-neutron width ratio expression.
- The incident electron energy \(\varepsilon_1 = 2000\) MeV and electron scattering angle \(\theta_e = 23^\circ\) were used for the differential cross section calculations.
- Fixed proton angles of \(\theta_{P_p k_1} = 0.98\) rad and \(\theta_{P_p k_1} = 0.82\) rad were chosen for two groups of proton states in the parallel kinematics.

## Context
This work extends theoretical studies of quasi-free scattering (Cardman) and inclusive electrofission experiments \((e,f)\) (Likha) and \((e,e'f)\) (Hansen) by providing the first detailed PWIA calculation of exclusive \((e,e'pf)\) cross sections for deformed nuclei. The calculations are intended as a guide for future experiments at high-energy CW electron accelerators such as the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility (TJNAF), and the approach enables extraction of single-hole doorway state contributions to fission that are inaccessible in conventional reactions where structure effects are averaged over many states.
