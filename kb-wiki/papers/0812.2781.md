# Spectroscopy of the unbound: One-neutron knockout reaction of $^{14}$Be

> Full Faddeev-type calculations are performed for one-neutron knockout reaction of $^{14}$Be on proton target at 69 MeV/u incident energy. Inclusive transverse momentum distributions for the outgoing ($^{12}$Be + n) system and semi-inclusive cross sections are presented. A significant proton-core single scattering contribution emerges where the valence neutron has non zero angular momentum relative to the core. This indicates that distorted-wave impulse approximation is inadequate and the complete multiple scattering series must be taken into account for the considered reaction. The magnitude of the semi-inclusive cross section at quasifree scattering conditions is a clear signature of the angular momentum of the valence nucleon.

## Key claim

Full Faddeev-type calculations for the one-neutron knockout reaction of 14Be on a proton target at 69 MeV/u show that the single scattering approximation (SSA) overestimates the full multiple scattering results for all configurations, and that distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) is inadequate because it neglects a significant proton-core single scattering contribution that emerges when the valence neutron has nonzero angular momentum relative to the core.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body Faddeev/Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) scattering equations exactly in momentum space after partial wave decomposition, treating the 14Be nucleus as a 13Be core plus a valence neutron interacting with a proton target. They included nuclear interactions between all three pairs (p-n, p-13Be, and n-13Be) using the CD Bonn potential for p-n, a Watson global optical potential for p-13Be, and Woods-Saxon potentials for n-13Be, with the Coulomb interaction between the proton and 13Be also included. Semi-inclusive breakup cross sections and inclusive transverse momentum distributions were calculated for three possible single-particle configurations of 14Be(0+): S-wave (1/2+), P-wave (1/2-), and D-wave (5/2+) 13Be+n bound states.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy: 69 MeV/u for 14Be scattering from a proton target
- QFS kinematic condition for 13Be: (ECLAB, θCLAB, φCLAB)QFS = (897 MeV, 0°, 0°)
- 13Be resonances used: 1/2- (P-wave) with Erel = 0.45 MeV, 1/2+ (S-wave) with Erel = 1.17 MeV, and 5/2+ (D-wave) with Erel = 2.34 MeV
- Two-neutron separation energy of 14Be: S2n = 1.26 MeV
- Woods-Saxon potential depths for n-13Be: 32.922 MeV (1p1/2), 61.667 MeV (2s1/2), 68.980 MeV (1d5/2), with r0 = 1.2 fm and a0 = 0.6 fm
- Partial waves included: n-p with ℓnp ≤ 8, p-13Be with L ≤ 10, n-13Be with ℓ ≤ 3, three-body total angular momentum up to 100
- The semi-inclusive cross section with an S-wave bound state is one order of magnitude larger than with P-wave, and two orders of magnitude larger than with D-wave

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev/AGS approach previously applied to p-11Be elastic scattering and breakup by Crespo (2008), Crespo (2007b), and Del07 to the 14Be system, providing a benchmark for approximate methods like DWIA commonly used in knockout reactions (Chant 1977). The calculations were motivated by experimental measurements at RIKEN by Kondo (2007) using the invariant mass method to study the unbound 13Be system. The results enable future experimental work to use the magnitude of semi-inclusive cross sections at quasifree scattering conditions as a clear signature of the valence neutron angular momentum, provided full three-body dynamics are controlled.
