# A new and practical formulation for overlaps of Bogoliubov vacua

> In this letter we present a new expression for the overlaps of wavefunctions in Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov based theories. Starting from the Pfaffian formula by Bertsch et al (Phys. Rev. Lett. 108,042505 (2012)), an exact and computationally stable formula for overlaps is derived. We illustrate the convenience of this new formulation with a numerical application in the context of the particle-number projection method. This new formula allows for substantially increased precision and versatility in chemical, atomic, and nuclear physics applications, particularly for methods dealing with superfluidity, symmetry restoration and uses of non-orthogonal many-body basis states.

## Key claim

The paper derives an exact and numerically stable expression for overlaps of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) vacua that eliminates the numerical instability present in the earlier Pfaffian formula of Bertsch and Robledo (2012), which arises from products of very small occupation numbers falling below double-precision range.

## Method

Starting from the Pfaffian formula of Bertsch and Robledo (2012), the authors factorize the norms of the HFB vacua out of the Pfaffian by introducing diagonal matrices \(\Lambda\) and \(\Lambda'\) containing the square roots of the occupation numbers \(v_i\). This factorization cancels the problematic denominator \(\prod_{i,i'} v_i v'_{i'}\) analytically, yielding a new expression (Eq. final_over-1 for even-A and Eq. odd7 for odd-A systems) that remains well-defined even when occupation numbers approach zero. The derivation uses the Bloch-Messiah decomposition to express the Bogoliubov transformation matrices in canonical form, and the Pfaffian identity \(\text{pf}(ABA^T) = \det(A)\,\text{pf}(B)\) to manipulate the matrix argument.

## Key numbers

- The numerical test uses a basis of 17 major oscillator shells for \(^{192}\text{Pb}\), with a total of \(N = 1938\) single-particle states.
- For the particle-number projection matrix element at gauge angle \(\phi = \pi/10\), approximately 800 canonical basis states are needed to achieve a relative error below 1%.
- The denominator \(\prod_{i,i'}^n v_i v'_{i'}\) in the original Bertsch-Robledo formula reaches \(10^{-322}\) (the smallest representable number in double precision) when \(n = 323\) states are included, at which point \(v_i = 0.097593\).
- The new formula allows inclusion of all 1938 states without numerical instability.

## Context

This work extends the Pfaffian formulation of overlaps introduced by Bertsch and Robledo (2012) and earlier work by Robledo (2009), and addresses numerical stability issues previously handled by ad-hoc cutoffs or replacement of small occupation numbers with a tiny parameter \(\epsilon\) as in Gao (2014). The new formula enables precise calculations in the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) and symmetry-restoration methods, particularly for applications requiring accurate description of the radial tail of wavefunctions such as alpha decay and nucleon-nucleus optical potentials.
