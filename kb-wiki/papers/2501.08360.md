# Studying the impact of background field on coupling constants in EiC and EicC

> We studied the influence of background fields on coupling constants in various kinematic regions. We then evaluated these effects through the Bethe-Heitler process in electron-nucleus collisions at the EIC and EicC.

# Studying the impact of background field on coupling constants in EiC and EicC

## Key claim
The background photon field in electron-nucleus collisions modifies the effective electromagnetic coupling constant in a kinematic-dependent way, with the enhancement or suppression determined by whether the average photon distribution function in a given kinematic interval is greater or less than unity; the effect can be large enough that perturbation theory may break down when the average distribution exceeds 137.

## Method
The authors computed the effective coupling constant as α_eff(k) = α_e f(k), where f(k) is the photon distribution function in the nuclear background field, obtained from the equivalent photon approximation (EPA) with a nuclear charge form factor from the STARlight Monte Carlo generator. They evaluated the Bethe-Heitler process in electron-nucleus collisions using TMD factorization with Coulomb corrections incorporated via transverse gauge links, and included Sudakov resummation of soft photon radiation. Numerical cross sections and azimuthal asymmetries were computed for lead targets at EIC (18 GeV electron, 100 GeV ion) and EicC (3.5 GeV electron, 8 GeV ion) energies.

## Key numbers
- The electromagnetic coupling constant in vacuum is α_e ≈ 1/137.
- For the EIC with a Pb target, in the kinematic region P_⊥ ∈ [0.3, 0.4] GeV and y_γ ∈ [0.5, 1], the average photon distribution function is f̄(k) = 97.9, indicating strong enhancement of the coupling constant.
- For the EIC with a Pb target, in the kinematic region P_⊥ ∈ [0.2, 0.3] GeV and y_γ ∈ [0.1, 0.5], the average photon distribution function is f̄(k) = 0.4, indicating suppression of the coupling constant.
- The azimuthal asymmetry ⟨cos(2φ)⟩ at EIC energy is approximately 3% when present, and is reduced by a factor of three after including the background field correction.
- The nuclear charge form factor uses R_A = 1.1 A^(1/3) fm and a = 0.7 fm, closely resembling the Woods-Saxon distribution.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Li, prior work) on quantifying background field effects on coupling constants, and builds on earlier treatments (cc1, cc2, cc3) that considered the peripheral photon field of the nucleus as a background field with Coulomb scattering corrections. The analysis uses the equivalent photon approximation (EPA1, EPA2, EPA3, EPA4) and the STARlight Monte Carlo form factor (starlight) for nuclear charge distributions. The results enable experimental tests at the EIC and EicC that could confirm whether exchanged bosons originate from the vacuum rather than from charge radiation, and may improve precision in QCD predictions.
