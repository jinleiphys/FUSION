# Near-threshold $η^\prime$ meson production in ${π^-}A$ reactions

> We study the inclusive near-threshold production of $η^\prime$ mesons in $π^-$ meson-nucleus reactions on the basis of the first-collision model relying on the nuclear spectral function and including incoherent processes of $η^\prime$ production in $π^-$ meson--proton collisions. The model accounts for the absorption of initial $π^-$ and final $η^\prime$ mesons, the binding of intranuclear protons and their Fermi motion, as well as the effect of the scalar $η^\prime$--nucleus potential (or the in-medium shift of the $η^\prime$ meson mass) on these processes. We calculate the differential and total cross sections for $η^\prime$ production off carbon and tungsten nuclei at laboratory angles of 0$^{\circ}$--10$^{\circ}$ and 10$^{\circ}$--45$^{\circ}$ by $π^-$ mesons with momentum of 1.7 GeV/c, which is close to the threshold momentum for $η^\prime$ creation on a free target proton at rest. We show that the calculated $η^\prime$ production cross sections are larger than those, studied earlier in the $η^\prime$ photoproduction reactions, by about two orders of magnitude. We also demonstrate that the high absolute values of the present differential and total $η^\prime$ production cross sections in the momentum ranges of 0.2--0.3 GeV/c and 0.1--0.6 GeV/c, respectively, possess a high sensitivity to changes in the in-medium shift of the $η^\prime$ mass. This offers the possibility of evaluating the above shift at these momenta from the respective data, which could be taken in future experiments at the GSI pion beam facility or at J-PARC.

# Near-threshold $\eta^\prime$ meson production in $\pi^-A$ reactions

## Key claim

The inclusive differential and total cross sections for $\eta^\prime$ meson production in $\pi^-$ meson-nucleus reactions at 1.7 GeV/c beam momentum are predicted to be larger than those from $\eta^\prime$ photoproduction by about two orders of magnitude, and the low-momentum parts of the spectra (0.2–0.3 GeV/c for differential, 0.1–0.6 GeV/c for total) show high sensitivity to the in-medium shift of the $\eta^\prime$ mass, offering a way to determine this shift from future data at GSI or J-PARC, provided the first-collision model with a spectral function description of nuclear targets is valid and the $\eta^\prime$–nucleon inelastic cross section is taken as 13 mb.

## Method

The author employs a first-collision model based on the nuclear spectral function, including only the primary $\pi^- p \to \eta^\prime n$ channel (since multi-pion channels are energetically suppressed at 1.7 GeV/c). The model accounts for absorption of the initial pion and final $\eta^\prime$ meson, Fermi motion and binding of intranuclear protons, and the effect of a scalar $\eta^\prime$–nucleus potential (in-medium mass shift) on the production kinematics. The off-shell elementary cross section is taken as isotropic in the $\pi^- p$ center-of-mass system, with the free total cross section parametrized from existing data and the in-medium threshold adjusted by the average effective $\eta^\prime$ mass. Calculations are performed for $^{12}$C and $^{184}$W targets at laboratory polar angles of $0^\circ$–$10^\circ$ and $10^\circ$–$45^\circ$.

## Key numbers

- The free threshold momentum for the $\pi^- p \to \eta^\prime n$ reaction is 1.43 GeV/c; the beam momentum used is 1.7 GeV/c, corresponding to an excess energy of 128 MeV above threshold for a free target nucleon at rest.
- The free total cross section $\sigma_{\pi^- p \to \eta^\prime n}$ at 1.7 GeV/c is approximately 110 $\mu$b.
- The $\eta^\prime$–nucleon inelastic cross section adopted for final-state absorption is $\sigma_{\eta^\prime N} = 13$ mb, motivated by CBELSA/TAPS results.
- The average nucleon density ratio $\langle \rho_N \rangle / \rho_0$ is 0.55 for $^{12}$C and 0.76 for $^{184}$W.
- Three values of the $\eta^\prime$ scalar potential $V_0$ at normal nuclear density $\rho_0$ are considered: 0, -40, and -80 MeV.
- At $\eta^\prime$ momentum of 0.25 GeV/c and angles $10^\circ$–$45^\circ$, the differential cross section with $V_0 = -40$ MeV is enhanced by a factor of about 2.4 compared to the case with no mass shift; going from $V_0 = -40$ MeV to $V_0 = -80$ MeV gives an additional enhancement factor of about 1.5.
- In the low-momentum region (0.1–0.6 GeV/c) at angles $10^\circ$–$45^\circ$, the total cross section with $V_0 = -80$ MeV is enhanced by a factor of about 1.8 compared to the zero-potential case; in the full momentum range (0.1–1.35 GeV/c) the enhancement factor is about 1.3.
- The differential cross sections at $\eta^\prime$ momenta around 0.2–0.3 GeV/c are of order 1 $\mu$b/(GeV/c) or larger for both targets and both angular ranges.

## Context

This work extends the first-collision model previously applied to $\phi$ meson production in $\pi^- A$ reactions at 1.7 GeV/c (Paryev, 2013) to the $\eta^\prime$ channel, motivated by CBELSA/TAPS photoproduction experiments that extracted $V_0 = -(39 \pm 7_{\rm stat} \pm 15_{\rm syst})$ MeV (Nanova et al., 2013) and $V_0 = -(44 \pm 16_{\rm stat} \pm 15_{\rm syst})$ MeV (Metag et al., 2017) at higher $\eta^\prime$ momenta (1.1 and 0.6 GeV/c, respectively). The calculations predict that pion-induced reactions can probe the $\eta^\prime$ scalar potential at substantially lower momenta (200–300 MeV/c) than accessible in photoproduction, and with higher statistics due to the larger elementary cross section. The results are intended to guide future measurements at the GSI pion beam facility (using the HADES spectrometer) or at J-PARC, and could complement searches for $\eta^\prime$ mesic bound states such as those attempted in the $^{12}$C($p,d$) reaction at FRS/GSI (Tanaka et al., 2016).
