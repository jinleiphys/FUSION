# Projected shell model study on nuclei near the N = Z line

> Study of the N ~ Z nuclei in the mass-80 region is not only interesting due to the existence of abundant nuclear structure phenomena, but also important in understanding the nucleosynthesis in the rp-process. It is not feasible to apply a conventional shell model due to the necessary involvement of the g_{9/2} sub-shell. In this paper, the projected shell model is introduced to this study. Calculations are systematically performed for the collective levels as well as the quasi-particle excitations. It is demonstrated that calculations with this truncation scheme can achieve a comparable quality as the large-scale shell model diagonalizations for 48Cr, but the present method can be applied for much heavier mass regions. While the known experimental data of the yrast bands in the N ~ Z nuclei (from Se to Ru) are reasonably described, the present calculations predict the existence of K-isomeric states, some of which lie low in energy under certain structure conditions.

## Key claim

The projected shell model (PSM) can achieve a comparable quality to large-scale shell model diagonalizations for light nuclei such as ⁴⁸Cr, but the method can be applied to much heavier mass regions where conventional shell model calculations are not feasible; the calculations predict the existence of K-isomeric states, some of which lie low in energy under certain structure conditions, though the author notes that it remains an open question whether the standard set of PSM interactions is sufficient to describe the yrast properties of N = Z nuclei.

## Method

The PSM builds its model space in a deformed Nilsson+BCS quasiparticle basis, then restores rotational symmetry by standard angular momentum projection techniques, and finally diagonalizes the shell model Hamiltonian (containing quadrupole-quadrupole, monopole pairing, and quadrupole pairing interactions) in this projected basis. For the mass-80 region, three major shells (N=2,3,4) were used for both neutrons and protons, with the monopole pairing strengths G₁=20.25 and G₂=16.20, and the quadrupole-pairing constant G_Q=0.16 G_M for all nuclei considered. The multi-quasiparticle basis included 0-quasiparticle, two-neutron, two-proton, and four-quasiparticle (two neutrons plus two protons) states built from low-lying Nilsson orbitals near the Fermi surface.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁸Cr, the PSM calculation used three major shells (N=1,2,3) for both neutrons and protons, with monopole pairing strengths G₁=22.5 and G₂=18.0, quadrupole pairing G_Q=0.2 G_M, and deformation ε₂=0.25; the results matched large-scale pf-shell model calculations and experimental data for γ-ray transition energies and B(E2) values over the entire spin region.
- For the mass-80 region, the PSM calculations used pairing strengths G₁=20.25 and G₂=16.20, and G_Q=0.16 G_M for all nuclei; deformations ranged from ε₂=0.36 (for ⁷²Kr, ⁷⁶Sr, ⁸⁰Zr, ⁷⁴Kr, ⁷⁸Sr) down to ε₂=0.16 (for ⁹⁰Ru).
- Predicted energies of the I^π = 6⁺ isomeric state (relative to ground state) include: ⁸⁰Zr at 4.19 MeV, ⁸²Zr at 3.71 MeV, ⁸⁴Mo at 3.53 MeV, ⁸⁶Mo at 3.21 MeV, ⁸⁸Ru at 3.14 MeV, and ⁹⁰Ru at 2.38 MeV.
- For ⁶⁸Se, calculations used ε₂=0.28 for prolate states and ε₂=-0.24 for oblate states, with slightly reduced pairing strengths G₁=19.58 and G₂=15.66; the calculations predicted a second sharp backbending at spin 16 in the prolate band, and high-K bands (K=8) with a bandhead of spin 8 and excitation energy of 5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the projected shell model approach, originally developed by Hara and Iwasaki (1980) and systematized in later PSM references, to the study of N ≈ Z nuclei in the mass-80 region, which are important for understanding the rp-process nucleosynthesis in X-ray bursts as discussed by Schatz and collaborators. The calculations systematically describe yrast bands from Se to Ru, building on earlier PSM studies of ⁴⁸Cr and odd-mass nuclei in the fp-shell, and predict K-isomeric states that could significantly affect β-decay half-lives and nucleosynthesis along the rp-process path, similar to the known case of ¹⁸⁰Taᵐ studied by Walker and others.
