# Thermodynamics of an updated hadronic resonance list and influence on hadronic transport

> Hadron lists based on experimental studies summarized by the Particle Data Group (PDG) are a crucial input for the equation of state and thermal models used in the study of strongly-interacting matter produced in heavy-ion collisions. Modeling of these strongly-interacting systems is carried out via hydrodynamical simulations, which are followed by hadronic transport codes that also require a hadronic list as input. To remain consistent throughout the different stages of modeling of a heavy-ion collision, the same hadron list with its corresponding decays must be used at each step. It has been shown that even the most uncertain states listed in the PDG from 2016 are required to reproduce partial pressures and susceptibilities from Lattice Quantum Chromodynamics with the hadronic list known as the PDG2016+. Here, we update the hadronic list for use in heavy-ion collision modeling by including the latest experimental information for all states listed in the Particle Data Booklet in 2021. We then compare our new list, called PDG2021+, to Lattice Quantum Chromodynamics results and find that it achieves even better agreement with the first principles calculations than the PDG2016+ list. Furthermore, we develop a novel scheme based on intermediate decay channels that allows for only binary decays, such that PDG2021+ will be compatible with the hadronic transport framework SMASH. Finally, we use these results to make comparisons to experimental data and discuss the impact on particle yields and spectra.

## Key claim

The updated PDG2021+ hadronic list, which includes all experimentally observed states from the 2021 Particle Data Booklet, achieves even better agreement with lattice QCD results for partial pressures and susceptibilities than the previous PDG2016+ list, but the improvement is most notable in the triply strange baryon sector where a new Omega baryon at approximately 2012 MeV was added.

## Method

The authors constructed the PDG2021+ hadronic list by updating masses, widths, decay channels, and branching ratios for all hadronic resonances from the 2021 PDG edition, including states with limited experimental support. They compared the thermodynamic observables from this list to lattice QCD results using the ideal hadron resonance gas (HRG) model in the Boltzmann approximation, computing partial pressures and susceptibilities of conserved charges. They also developed a novel scheme based on intermediate decay channels that allows only binary decays, making the list compatible with the SMASH hadronic transport framework.

## Key numbers

- The PDG2021+ list includes a new Omega baryon at mass approximately 2012 MeV, representing a 25% increase in the number of triple-strange hadrons
- The Hagedorn temperature from fitting the PDG2021+ spectrum up to 1.9 GeV is T_H = 167.2 MeV, compared to T_H = 174.2 MeV for the SMASH hadron list
- The fit parameters for the PDG2021+ spectrum were A = 0.4735 GeV^(3/2) and T_H = 167.2 MeV
- The strangeness S=3 baryon partial pressure is more than 20% higher at T approximately 150 MeV for PDG2021+ compared to PDG2016+
- The pseudo-critical temperature from lattice QCD is T_p.c. approximately 155 MeV, below which the HRG model is applicable

## Context

This work extends the PDG2016+ hadronic list developed by Alba (2017) and Alba (2020), which demonstrated that including all PDG states (even uncertain ones) was necessary to reproduce lattice QCD partial pressures and susceptibilities. The new PDG2021+ list updates the experimental information to the 2021 PDG edition and is designed for consistent use across all stages of heavy-ion collision simulations, from hydrodynamics through hadronic transport in SMASH. The improved agreement with lattice QCD, particularly in the strange baryon sector, enables more accurate thermal model fits to experimental data and better predictions for particle yields and spectra in heavy-ion collisions.
