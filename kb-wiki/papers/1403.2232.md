# Initial state geometry and fluctuations in Au+Au, Cu+Au and U+U collisions at RHIC

> We study within the IP-Glasma and two-component MC-Glauber models the effects of initial state geometry and fluctuations on multiplicities and eccentricities for several collision species at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). These include copper-gold (Cu+Au), gold-gold (Au+Au), and uranium-uranium (U+U) collisions. The multiplicity densities per participant pair are very similar in all systems studied. Ellipticities vary strongly between collision systems, most significantly for central collisions, while fluctuation driven odd moments vary little between systems. Event-by-event distributions of eccentricities in mid-central collisions are wider in Cu+Au relative to Au+Au and U+U systems. An anti-correlation between multiplicity and eccentricity is observed in ultra central U+U collisions which is weaker in the IP-Glasma model than the two-component MC-Glauber model. In ultra central Au+Au collisions the two models predict opposite signs for the slope of this correlation. Measurements of elliptic flow as a function of multiplicity in such central events can therefore be used to discriminate between models with qualitatively different particle production mechanisms.

## Key claim

The IP-Glasma model predicts that multiplicity densities per participant pair are very similar across Au+Au, Cu+Au, and U+U collisions at RHIC, and that the anti-correlation between multiplicity and eccentricity in ultra-central U+U collisions is weaker in the IP-Glasma model than in the two-component MC-Glauber model; in ultra-central Au+Au collisions the two models predict opposite signs for the slope of this correlation, providing a way to discriminate between particle production mechanisms.

## Method

The authors employed the IP-Glasma model, which combines the IP-Sat dipole model of gluon distributions within nuclei with classical Yang-Mills dynamics of Glasma fields after the collision, solving the equations up to time τ=0.4 fm/c and fixing transverse Coulomb gauge to compute gluon multiplicity per unit rapidity (converted to charged particle multiplicity by a factor of 2/3). They also implemented a two-component MC-Glauber model with negative binomial multiplicity fluctuations, where parameters k=8 and x=0.135 were extracted by fitting the IP-Glasma min-bias multiplicity distribution for Au+Au and U+U collisions. Nucleon positions were sampled from deformed Woods-Saxon distributions with parameters for 238U (β2=0.28, β4=0.093), 197Au (symmetric or with β2=-0.13, β4=-0.03), and 63Cu (spherical), and the four orientation angles of deformed nuclei were sampled randomly for unpolarized collisions.

## Key numbers

- Multiplicity density per participant pair (2/Npart) dNch/dη at η=0 is very similar across all systems studied; for Cu+Au collisions the centrality dependence flattens above Npart>180
- Maximal multiplicity for U+U collisions is approximately 20% larger than for Au+Au collisions; difference between tip-tip and random U+U collisions is ≤5%
- For min-bias Cu+Au collisions, dNch/dη ≈ 90 at η=0; for 0-5% centrality dNch/dη = 340±18, for 20-30% centrality dNch/dη = 150±17
- Ellipticity ε2 for random U+U collisions is larger than for tip-tip configurations by a factor of approximately 1.5 at the highest multiplicity
- ε2 values merge for Au+Au and U+U collisions towards peripheral bins
- Triangularity ε3 values for all systems coincide over the entire range of dNch/dη
- For 20-30% centrality, the scaled ε2 distribution for Cu+Au collisions is noticeably wider than for Au+Au and U+U systems
- In ultra-central U+U collisions (0-0.1% centrality), a distinct anti-correlation between eccentricity and multiplicity is observed; slopes for deformed and spherical Au+Au collisions are consistent with zero within statistical uncertainties

## Context

This work extends the IP-Glasma model studies previously applied to Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV (Schenke 2012, 2013) to asymmetric Cu+Au collisions and collisions of highly deformed 238U nuclei at 200 GeV and 193 GeV respectively. It contrasts with earlier studies of U+U collisions using the Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (KLN) model (Kuhlman 2006, Hirano 2010) and the AMPT model (Haque 2011), finding that the IP-Glasma model does not show the higher multiplicities per participant pair in tip-tip configurations predicted by the two-component MC-Glauber model. The predictions for event-by-event distributions of scaled ellipticity can be compared with future measurements of v2 distributions at RHIC, similar to the successful description of ATLAS vn distributions at LHC energies (Schenke 2013, Gale 2012).
