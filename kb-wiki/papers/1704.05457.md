# The spin-dependent quark beam function at NNLO

> We calculate the beam function for longitudinally-polarized quarks through next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in QCD perturbation theory. This is the last missing ingredient needed to apply the factorization theorem for the $N$-jettiness event-shape variable in polarized collisions through the NNLO level. We present all technical details of our derivation. As a by-product of our calculation we provide the first independent check of the previously-obtained unpolarized quark beam function. We anticipate that our result will have phenomenological applications in describing data from polarized collisions.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the polarized quark beam function matching coefficients through NNLO in QCD perturbation theory, which is the last missing ingredient needed to apply the N-jettiness factorization theorem to polarized collisions at NNLO; however, they note that in apparent contrast to a previous result for the unpolarized case (Gaunt 2014), they do not find any need for an additional regulator beyond dimensional regularization in their calculation.

## Method

The authors compute the spin-dependent beam function by evaluating cut Feynman diagrams in light-cone gauge with n-dot A = 0, using the HV scheme for gamma_5 in dimensional regularization. They extract the matching coefficients by replacing the proton state with perturbative quark and gluon states, then performing renormalization in the MSbar scheme and applying a scheme transformation to restore helicity conservation relations. The calculation includes both real-real and real-virtual diagrams at NNLO, organized into singlet and non-singlet flavor channels.

## Key numbers

- The non-cusp anomalous dimension for the quark beam function at one loop: gamma_{B0}^q = 6 C_F
- The non-cusp anomalous dimension for the quark beam function at two loops: gamma_{B1}^q = C_F [C_A (146/9 - 80 zeta_3) + C_F (3 - 4 pi^2 + 48 zeta_3) + beta_0 (121/9 + 2 pi^2/3)]
- The cusp anomalous dimension at one loop: Gamma_0^q = 4 C_F
- The cusp anomalous dimension at two loops: Gamma_1^q = C_F (4/3) [(4 - pi^2) C_A + 5 beta_0]
- The NLO matching coefficient for the qq channel: Delta I_{qq}^{(1)}(z) = 2 C_F [L_1(1-z)(1+z^2) - ((1+z^2)/(1-z)) ln z - 3(1-z) - (pi^2/6) delta(1-z)] + 8 C_F (1-z)
- The NLO matching coefficient for the qg channel: Delta I_{qg}^{(1)}(z) = 2 T_R [(2z-1) ln((1-z)/z) + 2(1-z)]

## Context

This work extends the N-jettiness subtraction framework (Boughezal 2015, Gaunt 2015) to polarized collisions, providing the last missing ingredient for NNLO calculations of observables such as the double-spin asymmetry A_LL in lepton-proton collisions. The result enables NNLO phenomenological studies at RHIC and future EIC experiments, and will be necessary for extending global fits of polarized proton structure (de Florian 2014) to NNLO. The calculation also provides the first independent check of the previously-obtained unpolarized quark beam function (Gaunt 2014), finding complete agreement.
