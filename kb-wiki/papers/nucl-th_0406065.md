# Three-body electrodisintegration of the three-nucleon bound state with $Δ$-isobar excitation: Processes below pion-production threshold

> Electron scattering from the three-nucleon bound state with two- and three-body disintegration is described. The description uses the purely nucleonic charge-dependent CD-Bonn potential and its coupled-channel extension CD-Bonn + $Δ$. Exact solutions of three-particle equations are employed for the initial and final states of the reactions. The current has one-baryon and two-baryon contributions and couples nucleonic with $Δ$-isobar channels. $Δ$-isobar effects on the observables are isolated. The $Δ$-isobar excitation yields an effective three-nucleon force and effective two- and three-nucleon currents beside other $Δ$-isobar effects; they are mutually consistent.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Δ-isobar excitation yields an effective three-nucleon force and effective two- and three-nucleon currents that are mutually consistent, but the description is only valid below pion-production threshold; above that threshold the coupled-channel potential CD Bonn + Δ shows unphysical resonating structures that cast doubt on the calculated Δ-isobar effects.

## Method

Exact solutions of three-particle scattering equations were solved by Chebyshev expansion of the two-baryon transition matrix for initial and final hadronic states, using the purely nucleonic charge-dependent CD-Bonn potential and its coupled-channel extension CD Bonn + Δ. The electromagnetic current included one-baryon and two-baryon contributions with e.m. form factors, coupling nucleonic with Δ-isobar channels, and was expanded in multipoles up to total three-baryon angular momentum 25/2. The baryonic potential was taken into account in partial waves up to total two-baryon angular momentum I=3, while the e.m. current acted between partial waves up to I=6.

## Key numbers

- Trinucleon binding energies for ³He: -7.941 MeV (CD Bonn) and -8.225 MeV (CD Bonn + Δ); with Coulomb interaction shifted to -7.261 MeV and -7.544 MeV, respectively, compared to experimental -7.718 MeV
- Magnetic moments of ³He: -2.073 μ_N (CD Bonn), -2.139 μ_N (CD Bonn + Δ), experimental -2.127 μ_N
- Magnetic moments of ³H: 2.906 μ_N (CD Bonn), 2.970 μ_N (CD Bonn + Δ), experimental 2.979 μ_N
- Nucleon-Δ transition form factor coupling strength g_ΔN^{M1}(0) = 4.59 μ_N, corresponding to experimental transition magnetic moment G_M^*(0) = 3.06 μ_N
- Δ-isobar rest mass m_Δ c^2 = 1232 MeV
- Electron lab energy 390 MeV with momentum transfer |Q| = 250.2 MeV/c and energy transfer Q_0 = 113 MeV/c for exclusive three-body electrodisintegration of ³He
- Electron lab energy 563.7 MeV with |Q| = 305 MeV/c and Q_0 = 220 MeV/c for higher-energy exclusive measurements

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Yuan (2002) to higher energies and includes three-nucleon breakup, using the same dynamics as Deltuva (2004) for photoreactions. The coupled-channel potential CD Bonn + Δ was fitted to experimental two-nucleon data up to 350 MeV nucleon lab energy with quality matching the original CD-Bonn potential. Alternative descriptions of e.m. processes in the three-nucleon system by Golak (1995, 2002) using different two-nucleon potentials and e.m. currents yield qualitatively similar theoretical predictions where comparable. The results enable comparison with experimental data from Groep (2000) above pion-production threshold, though the authors caution that theoretical predictions in that regime require a two-baryon potential extended realistically above threshold.
