# Kinetics of the chiral phase transition in a quark-meson $σ$ model

> Using the two-particle irreducible (2PI) $Φ$-functional formalism for self-consistent approximations of a linear-$σ$ model for quarks and mesons in and out of equilibrium, the build-up of fluctuations of net-baryon number during the time evolution of an expanding fireball is studied within a kinetic theory for the order parameter ($σ$ field) and quark distribution functions. Initializing the system with purely Gaussian fluctuations a fourth-order cumulant is temporarily built up due to the evolution of the $σ$-field. This is counterbalanced, however, by the dissipative evolution due to collisions between quarks, anti-quarks, mesons, and the mean field, depending on the speed of the fireball expansion.

## Key claim

Using a 2PI Φ-functional approach to a linear-σ model, the authors show that a fourth-order cumulant of net-baryon number fluctuations can be temporarily built up from initially Gaussian fluctuations due to the evolution of the σ field, but this buildup is counterbalanced by dissipative collisions; the observable final-state fluctuations depend on the speed of the fireball expansion and the type of phase transition traversed.

## Method

The authors derived coupled kinetic equations for the mean σ field (order parameter) and generalized Boltzmann equations for quark and meson phase-space distributions from the 2PI Φ-functional formalism, using a first-order gradient expansion and on-shell approximation. They simulated an expanding homogeneous fireball using a Friedmann-Lemaître-Robertson-Walker metric with Hubble expansion, initializing the system in thermal equilibrium with Monte-Carlo sampled Gaussian net-quark number fluctuations. The coupled integro-differential equations were solved numerically on a momentum grid for different expansion velocities and initial conditions corresponding to crossover, second-order, and first-order chiral phase transitions.

## Key numbers

- Critical point at (T, μ_B) = (108, 157) MeV for quark-meson coupling g = 3.3
- Initial fireball radius R₀ = 5 fm, expanding with velocity v such that final radius R ≥ 11 fm at vt ≥ 6 fm
- Largest fluctuations occur for smallest expansion velocity v = 0.05 c (quasi-adiabatic expansion)
- For the case near the critical point (μ_q = 160 MeV), largest final-state cumulant ratio observed for intermediate expansion velocities v = 0.2–0.4 c
- Most interesting momentum range for fluctuations: p = 200–600 MeV
- Pion mass m_π = 138 MeV, pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV, coupling λ = 20

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous 2PI Φ-functional studies of the quark-meson σ model (Meistrenko 2020) to the kinetic evolution of net-baryon number fluctuations in an expanding fireball. It addresses the challenge raised by lattice QCD calculations (Fodor 2002, Aoki 2006) showing a smooth crossover at low baryochemical potential, while effective models predict a first-order transition line ending in a critical point at higher μ_B. The results suggest that higher-order cumulant ratios in different momentum bins could provide experimental signatures to distinguish between crossover, second-order, and first-order phase transitions in heavy-ion collisions at facilities such as LHC, RHIC, and FAIR.
