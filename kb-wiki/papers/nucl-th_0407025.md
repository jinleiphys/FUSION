# Baryonic Resonances from Baryon Decuplet-Meson Octet Interaction

> We study $S$-wave interactions of the baryon decuplet with the octet of pseudoscalar mesons using the lowest order chiral Lagrangian. In the S=1 sector, we find an attractive interaction in the $ΔK$ channel with I=1 while it is repulsive for I=2. The attractive interaction leads to a pole in the second Riemann sheet of the complex plane and is manifested as a large strength in the scattering amplitude close to the $ΔK$ threshold, which is not the case for I=2. We use the unitarized coupled channel approach to also investigate all the other possible values of strangeness and isospin. We find two bound states in the SU(3) limit corresponding to the octet and decuplet representations. These are found to split into eight different trajectories in the complex plane when the SU(3) symmetry is broken gradually. Finally, we are able to provide a reasonable description for a good number of 4-star ${3/2}^-$ resonances listed by the Particle Data Group. In particular, the $Ξ(1820)$, the $Λ(1520)$ and the $Σ(1670)$ states are well reproduced. We predict a few other resonances and also evaluate the couplings of the observed resonances to the various channels from the residues at the poles of the scattering matrix from where partial decay widths into different channels can be evaluated.

## Key claim

The paper claims that many four-star \(3/2^-\) baryon resonances listed by the Particle Data Group can be dynamically generated as quasibound states from the \(S\)-wave interaction of the baryon decuplet with the pseudoscalar meson octet, using only the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian and a unitarized coupled-channel approach. The key hedge is that the description is "reasonable" and that some predicted resonances are very broad and produce small effects in scattering amplitudes, which could explain why they have not been observed experimentally.

## Method

The authors used the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian for the interaction of the baryon decuplet (Delta, Sigma*, Xi*, Omega) with the pseudoscalar meson octet (pion, kaon, eta) and derived the \(S\)-wave transition amplitudes. They then solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels with a unitarization scheme using dimensional regularization (subtraction constant \(a = -2\), scale \(\mu = 700\) MeV) to obtain the scattering matrix \(T = (1 - VG)^{-1} V\). Poles of the \(T\)-matrix were searched in the second and third Riemann sheets of the complex energy plane, and residues at the poles were evaluated to extract couplings to different channels.

## Key numbers

* In the \(SU(3)\) symmetric limit (average masses \(M_0 = 1382\) MeV for decuplet, \(m_0 = 368\) MeV for mesons), two bound-state poles appear on the real axis: one at 1673 MeV (octet representation) and one at 1738 MeV (decuplet representation).
* For \(S=1\), \(I=1\) (27-plet representation), a pole is found in the third Riemann sheet at 1635 MeV on the real axis, corresponding to an attractive \(\Delta K\) interaction with coefficient \(C = 1\).
* For \(S=0\), \(I=3/2\), two poles are found in the second Riemann sheet: one at \((1478 - i165)\) MeV (decuplet representation, coupling mostly to \(\Delta\pi\) with \(|g| = 2.8\)) and one at \((1827 - i108)\) MeV (mixed representation, coupling mostly to \(\Sigma^* K\) with \(|g| = 3.4\)).
* The \(\Lambda(1520)\), \(\Sigma(1670)\), and \(\Xi(1820)\) resonances are "well reproduced" by the model, though no explicit pole positions for these states are given in the truncated text.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to the baryon octet-meson octet interaction (which generated \(J^P = 1/2^-\) resonances such as the \(\Lambda(1405)\) and \(N^*(1535)\)) to the baryon decuplet-meson octet system. It builds on the earlier study by Lutz and collaborators (2002) which first suggested that such interactions could generate \(3/2^-\) resonances, but goes beyond that work by systematically searching for poles in the complex plane, evaluating residues to obtain partial decay widths, and studying the evolution of poles as \(SU(3)\) symmetry is broken. The results enable predictions for new resonances and provide input for calculations of processes such as \(\gamma p \to K^+ K^- p\) in the region above the \(\Lambda(1520)\) peak, which is relevant for pentaquark searches.
