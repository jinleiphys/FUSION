# Weisskopf units for neutron-proton pair transfers

> We introduce the concept of neutron-proton two-particle units ($np$-Weisskopf units) to be used in the analysis of the ($^3$He,$p)$ and $(p,^3$He) \added{reactions on nuclei} along the N=Z line. These are presented for the conditions relevant to the $(n,j,\ell$) orbits expected from $^{16}$O to $^{100}$Sn. As is the case of the Weisskopf units for electromagnetic transitions, the $np$-WU's will provide a simple, yet robust, measure of isoscalar and isovector $np$ pairing collective effects.

## Key claim

The paper introduces neutron-proton two-particle units (np-Weisskopf units) as a simple, robust measure of isoscalar and isovector np pairing collective effects in the analysis of (³He,p) and (p,³He) reactions on nuclei along the N=Z line, but the authors note that the ratio of cross sections for populating the 0⁺(T=1) and 1⁺(T=0) states is the key observable because experimental systematic uncertainties and kinematic conditions largely cancel out.

## Method

The authors performed second-order DWBA calculations using the code FRESCO for (³He,p) reactions at 25 MeV and (p,³He) reactions at 65 MeV/A, with conditions relevant to the filling of different (n,j,ℓ) orbits along the N=Z line from ¹⁶O to ¹⁰⁰Sn. They computed unit cross sections for the 0⁺(T=1) and 1⁺(T=0) states and the ratio R₀₁ = dσ⁰¹/dσ¹⁰, assuming a single j² configuration for the transferred np pair. Optical potentials were taken from CH89 for protons, Daehnick for deuterons, and Bechetti-Greenlees for ³He, with Q-values from experimental data or systematics.

## Key numbers

- Unit cross sections and np-Weisskopf units were calculated for target masses from ¹⁶O to ¹⁰⁰Sn, covering orbits from s₁/₂ to g₉/₂.
- The (³He,p) reaction was calculated at 25 MeV beam energy; the (p,³He) reaction at 65 MeV/A.
- The approximate analytical expression for the np-Weisskopf unit ratio is R₀₁,₂ₛₚ ≈ (1/9) × (9-j coefficient ratio), which shows overall agreement with the full DWBA calculations.
- For the representative cases of s₁/₂ and d₅/₂ orbits, the ratios remain stable even when cross sections change by factors of 10–100 as a function of bombarding energy plus Q-value to the 1⁺ state.

## Context

This work extends the concept of Weisskopf units for electromagnetic transitions (Bohr and Mottelson) and the two-particle units for two-neutron transfers in (t,p) and (p,t) reactions (Broglia et al., 1972) to the case of neutron-proton pair transfers. The np-Weisskopf units provide a baseline for interpreting experimental data from systematic measurements of (³He,p) and (p,³He) reactions in N=Z nuclei, such as those carried out at RCNP (Ayyad et al., 2017) and initial measurements at ATLAS/ANL and GANIL, enabling future studies of np pairing collectivity in the region from ⁵⁶Ni to ¹⁰⁰Sn at rare isotope facilities.
