# Study of the shape coexistence in the 96Zr, 96Mo, 96Ru isobars

> Three stable isobars, $^{96}_{40}$Zr$_{56}$, $^{96}_{42}$Mo$_{54}$ and $^{96}_{44}$Ru$_{52}$, which are in the vicinity of the harmonic oscillator proton shell closure Z=40 and the spin-orbit neutron shell closure N=50, are investigated for the presence of the shape coexistence and mixing phenomena. The ground state deformation of these isobars is extracted from the potential energy surface determined with the Covariant Density Functional Theory using a density-dependent point-coupling interaction, while the excited states are described involving the Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian with octic potential for both axially symmetric and $γ$-unstable quadrupole deformations. Within the broader view of the two approaches, the obtained results clearly highlight the significant contribution of these phenomena to the structure of the states of these nuclei.

# Study of the shape coexistence in the 96Zr, 96Mo, 96Ru isobars

## Key claim

The paper claims that shape coexistence and mixing phenomena significantly contribute to the structure of low-lying states in 96Zr, 96Mo, and 96Ru, but the evidence is conditional on the specific combination of microscopic (CDFT with DD-PCX) and phenomenological (Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian with octic potential) approaches used, and the degree of mixing varies strongly between nuclei, from negligible mixing in 96Zr to moderate mixing in 96Mo.

## Method

The ground-state deformation of each isobar was extracted from potential energy surfaces computed with Covariant Density Functional Theory (CDFT) using the density-dependent point-coupling interaction DD-PCX within the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov framework. Excited states were then described using the Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian with an octic potential, solved numerically in a Bessel-function basis, applied separately for axially symmetric (prolate/oblate) and γ-unstable quadrupole deformations depending on the nucleus. The octic potential parameters were fitted to experimental energies normalized to the first excited 2+ state, and the βM scaling factor was fixed to reproduce the B(E2) transition between that state and the ground state.

## Key numbers

- 96Zr: CDFT ground-state deformation (β2, γ) = (0.200, 48°); BMH-OP average β2 = 0.086; experimental β2 = 0.060 (from ENSDF)
- 96Mo: CDFT ground-state deformation (β2, γ) = (0.200, 18°); BMH-OP average β2 = 0.163; experimental β2 = 0.172 (from ENSDF)
- 96Ru: CDFT ground-state deformation (β2, γ) = (0.100, 0°); BMH-OP average β2 = 0.148; experimental β2 = 0.155 (from ENSDF)
- 96Mo: experimental ΔE = E(0₂⁺) − E(2₁⁺) = 370 keV; theoretical ΔE = 459 keV (threshold for shape coexistence island is < 800 keV)
- 96Mo: B(E2; 4₁⁺ → 2₁⁺) = 41 W.u. (theory) matches 41(7) W.u. (experiment)
- 96Mo: B(E2; 0₂⁺ → 2₁⁺) = 40 W.u. (theory) compared to 51(7) W.u. (experiment)
- 96Mo: B(E2; 2γ⁺ → 2₁⁺) = 16.4(24) W.u. (experiment), more than double in theory
- 96Zr: BMH-OP scaling factor ħ²/(2B) = 1054 keV; βM = 0.075; rms = 0.050
- 96Mo: BMH-OP scaling factor ħ²/(2B) = 198 keV; βM = 0.144; rms = 0.108
- 96Ru: BMH-OP scaling factor ħ²/(2B) = 471 keV; βM = 0.103; rms = 0.047

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shape coexistence in the A=96 mass region, which lies near the Z=40 harmonic oscillator proton shell closure and the N=50 spin-orbit neutron shell closure, a region where the dual-shell mechanism facilitates shape coexistence as proposed by Martinou and collaborators. The authors build on prior applications of the Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian with octic potential to Ru and Cd isotopic chains (Buganu and Budaca, 2021-2022) and on earlier CDFT studies of these isobars by Thomas, Cheng, Barbecho, Kumar, Kremer, Sazonov, and Ramos. The results for 96Zr are compared with those of Sazonov using a different approach, and the present study confirms the picture of shape coexistence with negligible mixing for the 0⁺ states in 96Zr, while for 96Mo the results show shape coexistence with moderate mixing, consistent with the island-of-shape-coexistence criterion of Martinou. The work enables future systematic application of the octic-potential Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian to other candidate nuclei in this mass region.
