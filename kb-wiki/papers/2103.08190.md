# A polynomial in transverse momentum manifesting thermalisation in nuclear collisions

> A specific value of light front variable $ζ_c(p_T,m)$ of the inclusively produced hadrons in a relativistic nuclear collision is constructed as a polynomial in transverse momentum $p_T$ of the particle within the UrQMD transport model. It is shown that those particles with the absolute value of their light front variable $ζ$ greater than the corresponding $ζ_c(p_T,m)$ follow a Fermi-Dirac or Bose-Einstein distribution depending upon the spin of the particle.

## Key claim

The paper claims that within the UrQMD transport model, a specific polynomial function of transverse momentum \(p_T\) can define a threshold value of the light front variable \(\zeta_c(p_T,m)\); particles with \(|\zeta| > \zeta_c(p_T,m)\) then follow Fermi-Dirac or Bose-Einstein distributions. The claim is explicitly conditional on the use of the UrQMD model and has not yet been verified with experimental data.

## Method

The author simulated 55,000 minimum bias Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) GeV using the UrQMD-3.4 transport model. For each hadron species (\(\pi^{\pm}\), \(K^{\pm}\), \(\eta^{0}\), \(p(\bar{p})\), \(\Lambda^{0}\), \(\Sigma^{0}\)) in the pseudorapidity region \(|\eta| < 1\), \(\zeta\) distributions were constructed in several \(p_T\) intervals and fitted with an integral of a thermal distribution (Fermi-Dirac for baryons, Bose-Einstein for mesons). The lowest \(\zeta\) value yielding a successful fit was taken as \(\tilde{\zeta}\); if the \(p_T^2\) and polar angle distributions of particles with \(\zeta > \tilde{\zeta}\) were also describable by the thermal form, \(\tilde{\zeta}\) was assigned as \(\zeta_c\) for that \(p_T\) range. This procedure was repeated across multiple \(p_T\) ranges for each species, and a cubic polynomial \(\zeta_c(p_T,m) = a_0 + a_1 p_T + a_2 p_T^2 + a_3 p_T^3\) was fitted to the resulting \(\zeta_c\) values.

## Key numbers

- 55,000 minimum bias Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) GeV were simulated.
- For \(\pi^{\pm}\), the fitted temperature was \(T = 170 \pm 4\) MeV with \(\chi^2/n.d.f = 10/22\).
- For \(K^{\pm}\), the fitted temperature was \(T = 199 \pm 6\) MeV with \(\chi^2/n.d.f = 4/14\).
- For \(\eta^{0}\), the fitted temperature was \(T = 185 \pm 2\) MeV with \(\chi^2/n.d.f = 40/38\).
- For \(p(\bar{p})\), the fitted temperature was \(T = 232 \pm 3\) MeV with \(\chi^2/n.d.f = 12/23\).
- For \(\Lambda^{0}\), the fitted temperature was \(T = 219 \pm 4\) MeV with \(\chi^2/n.d.f = 10/22\).
- For \(\Sigma^{0}\), the fitted temperature was \(T = 233 \pm 5\) MeV with \(\chi^2/n.d.f = 20/19\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Garsevanishvili (1978, 1979), Amaglobeli (1999), Djobava (2003), and Chkhaidze (2006) which used a constant value of the light front variable \(\tilde\zeta\) to select thermally equilibrated particles in experimental data; here the author quantifies the transverse momentum dependence of that threshold. The measured temperatures exceed the Hagedorn temperature and lattice QCD predictions for the phase transition temperature, as discussed by Gazdzicki (2016) and Ratti (2018), suggesting the possible presence of a deconfined quark-gluon plasma. The analysis enables future tests with experimental data from RHIC and LHC, as well as studies of collectivity in hadron-hadron collisions at LHC, and further investigations using HBT interferometry, two-particle correlations, and collectivity for particles on either side of the \(\zeta_c\) threshold.
