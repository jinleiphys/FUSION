# Method to search for the triple-neutron state in an electron scattering experiment

> An electron scattering experiment to search for the trineutron state $^3n$ by reaction ${\rm ^4He}(e,~e'pπ^{+})^{3}n$ is designed for the A1 facility at Mainzer Microtron. The detailed principles, setup, and simulation of this experiment are presented. With the momenta of the scattered electron, the produced proton and $π^+$ from the reaction measured by three spectrometers with their triple coincidence, the missing mass spectrum of $^3n$ can be obtained. The production rate of $^3n$ based on the cross section of the reaction and a MC simulation is estimated to be about 1.5 per day, which can provide a confidence level of the signal greater than 5$σ$ with a beam time longer than 16 days. According to a MC simulation that evaluates the energy losses of particles in materials and the performance of three spectrometers, the estimated resolution and the predicted shape of the missing mass spectrum are presented. This work provides a new experimental concept for the search for multineutron states in future experiments with an electron beam.

## Key claim

The authors propose that a trineutron ($^3n$) state could be produced and detected for the first time via the reaction ${\rm ^4He}(e,~e'p\pi^{+})^{3}n$ at the MAMI-A1 facility, but only if the signal exceeds a 5$\sigma$ confidence level after at least 16 days of beam time, with an estimated production rate of about 1.5 events per day.

## Method

The experiment uses the 855 MeV electron beam at the Mainzer Microtron (MAMI) and three high-precision spectrometers (SpekA, SpekB, SpekC) in triple coincidence to measure the momenta of the scattered electron, the produced proton, and the $\pi^+$ from the reaction ${\rm ^4He}(e,~e'p\pi^{+})^{3}n$. The missing mass of the $^3n$ system is reconstructed from these measured momenta. A Monte Carlo simulation incorporating the Bethe-Bloch energy loss formula, Landau fluctuations, multiple scattering, and the spectrometer acceptances is used to estimate the production rate, resolution, and expected missing mass spectrum.

## Key numbers

*   Production rate of $^3n$ in the region of interest: approximately 1.5 per day.
*   Required beam time for a signal with confidence level greater than 5$\sigma$: longer than 16 days.
*   Missing mass resolution of the experiment (from simulation with 0 MeV $^3n$ width): about 0.4 MeV.
*   Momentum transfer from the electron to the proton: $Q^2 = 0.025$ (GeV/$c$)$^2$.
*   Cross section for the reaction: $\frac{{\rm d}^5\sigma}{{\rm d}E'{\rm d}\Omega_{e'}{\rm d}\Omega_{\pi^{+}}} = 0.185$ nb/(MeV sr$^2$).
*   Luminosity of the experiment: $\mathcal{L} = 3.01 \times 10^{36}$ (cm$^2$ s)$^{-1}$.
*   Expected total systematic uncertainty of $^3n$ energy: about 0.4 to 0.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the experimental method used by the A1 collaboration to produce the neutron-rich hydrogen isotope $^6$H via the reaction ${\rm ^{7}Li}(e,~e'p\pi^{+}){\rm ^{6}H}$ (Shao 2025), applying a similar principle to search for the trineutron. It directly addresses the long-standing question of multineutron existence, where previous searches for $^3n$ using neutron-proton transfer reactions (e.g., Ajdacic 1965, Thornton 1966, RIBF-SHARAQ11 2024) and pion double charge exchange reactions (Stetz 1986, Yuly 1997) have yielded no evidence. The proposed experiment offers a new reaction mechanism that suppresses the initial neutron correlation effects that complicated the interpretation of recent $^4n$ observations (Duer 2022, Lazauskas 2022). If successful, this work would provide the first experimental evidence for a $^3n$ state and open a new avenue for studying multineutron systems with electron beams, potentially at future facilities like the Shanghai soft X-ray Free-Electron Laser (SXFEL).
