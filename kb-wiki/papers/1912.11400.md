# N$^*$ Experiments and what they tell us about Strong QCD Physics

> I give an overview on experimental studies of the spectrum and the structure of the excited states of the nucleon and what we can learn about their internal structure. One focus is on the efforts to obtain a more complete picture of the light-quark baryon excitation spectrum employing electromagnetic beams that will allow us to draw some conclusions on the symmetries underlying the spectrum. For the higher mass excitations, the full employment of coupled channel approaches is essential when searching for new excited states in the large amounts of data already accumulated in different channels involving a variety of polarization observables. The other focus is on the study of transition form factors and helicity amplitudes and their dependences on $Q^2$, especially on some of the more prominent resonances, especially $Δ(1232)\frac{3}{2}^+$, $N(1440)\frac{1}{2}^+$, and negative parity states $N(1535)\frac{1}{2}^-$, and $N(1675)\frac{5}{2}^-$. These were obtained in pion and eta electroproduction experiments off proton targets and have already led to further insights in the active degrees-of-freedom as a function of the distance scale involved.

# N* Experiments and what they tell us about Strong QCD Physics

## Key claim

The light-quark baryon excitation spectrum below 2.2 GeV has been significantly expanded through photoproduction and electroproduction experiments, but the observed masses of new states deviate from lattice QCD predictions because those calculations use a pion mass of 396 MeV and do not include multi-channel coupling; dynamical coupled-channel effects can produce large mass shifts, as demonstrated by the Roper resonance pole mass shifting downward from a bare mass of 1736 MeV to 1365 MeV.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental studies of nucleon resonance (N* and Δ*) spectroscopy using electromagnetic beams at CEBAF (JLab), ELSA (Bonn), and MAMI (Mainz), focusing on exclusive meson photoproduction and electroproduction reactions with polarization observables. For higher-mass excitations above 2 GeV, coupled-channel partial wave analyses (e.g., Bonn-Gatchina group) are employed to extract resonance parameters from data in multiple final states including K+Λ, K+Σ0, pω, pφ, and Nππ. Transition form factors and helicity amplitudes for specific resonances (Δ(1232)3/2+, N(1440)1/2+, N(1535)1/2-, N(1675)5/2-) are obtained from pion and eta electroproduction off proton targets as a function of Q2.

## Key numbers

- Roper resonance N(1440)1/2+ pole mass shifted downward from bare mass of 1736 MeV to 1365 MeV due to dynamical coupled-channel effects (Suzuki 2009)
- Resonances in the light-quark baryon spectrum are typically 200-300 MeV wide
- New nucleon states discovered in mass range 1.85 to 2.15 GeV (eight baryon states over five years)
- Δ(2200)7/2- state couples at only 3.5% level to Nπ
- Magnetic NΔ transition form factor data show agreement with quark-based calculations at Q2 > 4 GeV2
- For N(1440) and N(1535), QCD-based modeling agrees with data at Q2 > 1.5 GeV2
- Lattice QCD predictions for nucleon spectrum use pion mass of 396 MeV
- Cross-over temperature Tc ≈ 155 MeV for quark-hadron transition in early universe

## Context

This review extends the experimental N* program described in earlier reviews by Klempt (2009), Tiator (2011), Aznauryan (2011, 2012), and Crede (2013), and builds on the quark model predictions of Capstick (1986) and lattice QCD calculations by Dudek (2012) and Edwards (2011). The work enables further searches for "missing" resonances predicted by SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry but not yet observed, and provides input for hadron resonance gas models used to describe the QCD phase transition in the early universe. The new states discovered since 2016 and included in the 2018 Review of Particle Physics bring hadron resonance gas model calculations closer to lattice QCD results for the baryon chemical potential ratio near the crossover temperature.
