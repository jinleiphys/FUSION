# Constraining the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy with heavy-ion reactions and its astrophysical impact

> Recent analyses of several isospin effects in heavy-ion reactions have allowed us to constrain the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy at sub-saturation densities within a narrow range. Combined with constraints on the Equation of State (EOS) of symmetric nuclear matter obtained previously from analyzing the elliptic flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, the EOS of neutron-rich nuclear matter is thus partially constrained. Here we report effects of the partially constrained EOS of neutron-rich nuclear matter on the mass-radius correlation, moment of inertia, elliptical deformation and gravitational radiation of (rapidly) rotating neutron stars.

# Constraining the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy with heavy-ion reactions and its astrophysical impact

## Key claim
The density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy at sub-saturation densities has been constrained within a narrow range by analyzing isospin diffusion, isoscaling in heavy-ion reactions, and neutron skin sizes, but these constraints are only valid for densities below about 1.2 times saturation density and still suffer from some uncertainties; the resulting partially constrained equation of state of neutron-rich matter can already place useful limits on neutron star observables such as mass-radius correlation, moment of inertia, and gravitational wave emission.

## Method
The authors used the parabolic approximation to express the equation of state of isospin asymmetric nuclear matter as the sum of the symmetric matter EOS and a density-dependent symmetry energy term. They combined experimental constraints on symmetric matter pressure from collective flow data in relativistic heavy-ion collisions (Danielewicz, Lacey, Lynch 2002) with constraints on the symmetry energy from isospin diffusion (Tsang 2004, Liu 2007), isoscaling (Shetty 2007), and neutron skin measurements (Steiner 2005) to produce a partially constrained EOS for neutron-rich matter. They then applied this EOS to calculate neutron star properties including mass-radius relations, moment of inertia, ellipticity, and gravitational wave strain amplitudes for rotating neutron stars.

## Key numbers
- The symmetry energy at sub-saturation densities is constrained between approximately $31.6(\rho/\rho_0)^{0.69}$ and $31.6(\rho/\rho_0)^{1.05}$, labelled by parameters $x=0$ and $x=-1$ respectively.
- For a neutron star rotating at 1122 Hz, the allowed mass range is between 1.7 and 2.1 solar masses.
- The maximum radius for a rotating neutron star is given by $R_{\rm max}(M)=\chi(M/1.4M_{\odot})^{1/3}$ km, with $\chi=20.94$ for rotational frequency 716 Hz and $\chi=15.52$ for 1122 Hz.
- The maximal gravitational wave strain amplitude $h_0$ for millisecond pulsars within 0.4 kpc is in the range approximately $[0.4-1.5]\times 10^{-24}$ for neutron star models of $1.4M_{\odot}$.
- The neutron star crust breaking strain $\sigma$ is taken as $10^{-2}$ (maximum value), with the actual range being $\sigma=[10^{-5}-10^{-2}]$.
- The moment of inertia empirical relation is $I_{zz}\approx (0.237\pm 0.008)MR^2[1+4.2(M km)/(M_{\odot}R)+90(M km/(M_{\odot}R))^4]$ for masses above $1M_{\odot}$.
- The ellipticity lower limit from observations is $4\times 10^{-7}$ for PSR J2124-3358.

## Context
This work extends the constraints on symmetric nuclear matter EOS from Danielewicz, Lacey and Lynch (2002) by adding symmetry energy constraints from terrestrial experiments, producing a partially constrained EOS for neutron-rich matter. The authors apply this EOS to neutron star astrophysics, building on prior calculations of neutron star structure and gravitational wave emission (Owen 2005, Lattimer 2005). The results enable predictions for gravitational wave observables that can be tested by LIGO and GEO detectors, and the paper identifies future experiments including parity violating electron scattering at Jefferson Lab and heavy-ion reactions with high energy radioactive beams as pathways to map the full density dependence of the symmetry energy.
