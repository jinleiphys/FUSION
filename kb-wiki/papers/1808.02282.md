# Studies of the Giant Dipole Resonance in $^{27}$Al, $^{40}$Ca, $^{56}$Fe, $^{58}$Ni and $^{208}$Pb with high energy-resolution inelastic proton scattering under 0$^\circ$

> A survey of the fine structure of the Isovector Giant Dipole Resonance (IVGDR) was performed, using the recently commissioned zero-degree facility of the K600 magnetic spectrometer at iThemba LABS. Inelastic proton scattering at an incident energy of 200 MeV was measured on $^{27}$Al, $^{40}$Ca, $^{56}$Fe, $^{58}$Ni and $^{208}$Pb. A high energy resolution ($\rmΔ\it{E} \simeq$ 40 keV FWHM) could be achieved after utilising faint-beam and dispersion-matching techniques. Considerable fine structure is observed in the energy region of the IVGDR and characteristic energy scales are extracted from the experimental data by means of a wavelet analysis. The comparison with Quasiparticle-Phonon Model (QPM) calculations provides insight into the relevance of different giant resonance decay mechanisms. Photoabsorption cross sections derived from the data assuming dominance of relativistic Coulomb excitation are in fair agreement with previous work using real photons.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that high energy-resolution inelastic proton scattering at 0° can resolve fine structure in the Isovector Giant Dipole Resonance across a wide mass range, but the extracted photoabsorption cross sections require a scaling factor of 0.6 ± 0.1 to match previous data, indicating systematic uncertainties in absolute normalization due to limited angular resolution.

## Method

Inelastic proton scattering at 200 MeV incident energy was measured on ²⁷Al, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁵⁶Fe, ⁵⁸Ni, and ²⁰⁸Pb using the K600 magnetic spectrometer at iThemba LABS operated at 0° with laboratory scattering angles up to 1.91°. Faint-beam and dispersion-matching techniques achieved energy resolutions of 40–50 keV FWHM. Background from beam halo and small-angle atomic scattering was subtracted using vertical focal-plane gating, and a lineshape correction removed kinematic broadening. Double differential cross sections were converted to equivalent photoabsorption cross sections via the equivalent photon method using Eikonal approximation, and characteristic energy scales of the fine structure were extracted using continuous wavelet transform analysis with the Morlet wavelet.

## Key numbers

- Energy resolution achieved: ΔE ≃ 40 keV FWHM (45 keV after lineshape correction for ⁴⁰Ca)
- Incident proton energy: Ep = 200 MeV
- Angular acceptance: θlab = 0° – 1.91°
- Normalization factor for equivalent photoabsorption cross sections: 0.6 ± 0.1 for all targets
- Target areal densities: 1 – 3 mg/cm² with >95% enrichment (natural calcium for ⁴⁰Ca, 97% ⁴⁰Ca)
- Resolution improvement from lineshape correction: from 80 keV to 45 keV FWHM for the 10.319 MeV state in ⁴⁰Ca
- IVGDR centroid energy follows systematics E_C = 31.2 A⁻¹/³ + 20.6 A⁻¹/⁶ MeV

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of giant resonance fine structure from the Isoscalar Giant Quadrupole Resonance (previously studied by Shevchenko et al. (2004, 2009) and Usman et al. (2011)) to the Isovector Giant Dipole Resonance across a wide mass range, following pioneering fine-structure work on ²⁰⁸Pb by Poltoratska et al. (2014). The results enable comparison with Quasiparticle-Phonon Model calculations to test the relevance of different decay mechanisms (Landau damping, escape width, spreading width) and provide a foundation for future studies of the nuclear dipole polarizability and neutron skin thickness, as demonstrated in subsequent work on Nd isotopes by Donaldson et al. (2018).
