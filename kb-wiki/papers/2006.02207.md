# On the minimum radius of very massive neutron stars

> Prospects of establishing the radii of massive neutron stars in PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0740+6620 from NICER and Chandra observatories hold the potential to constrain the equation of state (EoS) of matter to densities well beyond those encountered in canonical stars of mass $\sim 1.4\,{\rm M}_{\odot}$. In this work, we investigate the relation between the radii of very massive neutron stars up to the maximum mass, $M_{\rm max}$, supported by dense matter EoSs. Results from models with hadronic matter are contrasted with those that include a first-order hadron-to-quark phase transition. We find that a lower bound on $M_{\rm max}$ with an upper bound on the radius of massive pulsars serves to rule out too soft quark matter, and an upper bound on $M_{\rm max}$ with a lower bound on the radius of massive pulsars strongly disfavors a transition into too-stiff quark matter appearing at low densities. The complementary role played by radius inferences from future gravitational wave events of inspiraling binary neutron stars is also briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper investigates the relation between radii of very massive neutron stars (up to the maximum mass) and finds that a lower bound on the maximum mass combined with an upper bound on the radius of massive pulsars can rule out too-soft quark matter, while an upper bound on the maximum mass with a lower bound on the radius strongly disfavors a transition into too-stiff quark matter appearing at low densities; these conclusions depend on the specific hadronic baseline and the stiffness of quark matter assumed.

## Method

The authors solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations of hydrostatic equilibrium for neutron star models using the constant-sound-speed (CSS) parametrization for hybrid stars with a first-order hadron-to-quark phase transition, contrasting results with purely hadronic models (HLPS and DBHF) and self-bound strange quark stars. They systematically vary the squared sound speed in quark matter between 0.33 and 1, the transition density, and the maximum mass to map the allowed parameter space for the radius of a 2 solar mass star.

## Key numbers

- For a self-bound strange quark star with maximally stiff quark matter (squared sound speed = 1) and maximum mass fixed at 2.0 solar masses, the radius of a 2 solar mass star is 8.34 km and the typical radius for a 1.4 solar mass star is 8.09 km.
- For a self-bound strange quark star with very soft quark matter (squared sound speed = 0.33) and maximum mass = 2.0 solar masses, the radius of a 2 solar mass star is 10.95 km.
- For hybrid stars with soft hadronic matter (HLPS) transitioning to stiff quark matter (squared sound speed = 1) at saturation density n0, the minimum radius of a 2 solar mass star is 8.58 km when the maximum mass is 2.0 solar masses.
- The purely hadronic DBHF EoS yields a radius of 12.82 km for a 2 solar mass star.
- The chirp mass from GW170817 is 1.186 solar masses with total mass 2.73 solar masses; the maximum gravitational mass from that event is constrained to less than about 2.17 solar masses (Margalit 2017) or less than about 2.3 solar masses (Shibata 2019).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron star mass-radius relations (Lattimer 2012, Ozel 2016) and the constraints from GW170817 (Margalit 2017, Shibata 2019) by focusing specifically on the minimum radius of very massive stars up to the maximum mass. The results are relevant for upcoming NICER and Chandra observations of PSR J1614-2230 (mass about 1.93 solar masses) and PSR J0740+6620 (mass about 2.14 solar masses), and for future gravitational wave events like GW190425 (total mass about 3.4 solar masses), enabling discrimination between hadronic, hybrid, and self-bound quark matter scenarios.
