# Phase transitions in the Interacting Boson Fermion Model: the gamma-unstable case

> The phase transition around the critical point in the evolution from spherical to deformed gamma-unstable shapes is investigated in odd nuclei within the Interacting Boson Fermion Model. We consider the particular case of an odd j=3/2 particle coupled to an even-even boson core that undergoes a transition from spherical U(5) to gamma-unstable O(6) situation. The particular choice of the j=3/2 orbital preserves in the odd case the condition of gamma-instability of the system. As a consequence, energy spectrum and electromagnetic transitions, in correspondence of the critical point, display behaviours qualitatively similar to those of the even core. The results are also in qualitative agreement with the recently proposed E(5/4) model, although few differences are present, due to the different nature of the two schemes.

## Key claim

The phase transition from spherical U(5) to gamma-unstable O(6) shapes in odd nuclei, studied within the Interacting Boson Fermion Model (IBFM) with a single j=3/2 fermion coupled to a boson core, preserves gamma-instability and yields energy spectra and electromagnetic transitions qualitatively similar to the even-core case; however, the results show only qualitative agreement with the E(5/4) model, with sizeable quantitative differences in the relative positions of excited multiplets and in interband B(E2) values.

## Method

The authors used the IBFM Hamiltonian with a boson part H_B = x n_d - (1-x)/N Q_B·Q_B, a fermion part for a single j=3/2 orbital, and a boson-fermion coupling V_BF = -2(1-x)/N Q_B·q_F. They diagonalized this Hamiltonian for N=7 bosons as a function of the control parameter x (from 1 to 0), preserving Spin^BF(5) symmetry for all x. Energy levels and B(E2) transition rates were computed at the critical point and compared with the E(5/4) model of Iachello.

## Key numbers

- For the odd system at x=0 (Spin^BF(6) limit), the energy ratios in the ground band are E(tau1=5/2,tau2=1/2)/E(tau1=3/2,tau2=1/2)=2.4 and E(tau1=7/2,tau2=1/2)/E(tau1=3/2,tau2=1/2)=4.2, compared to even-core values of E(tau=2)/E(tau=1)=2.5 and E(tau=3)/E(tau=1)=4.5.
- At the critical point, the first tau1=5/2 multiplet in the odd system lies at relative energy 2.29, close to the E(5/4) value of about 2.20.
- The two first multiplets of the xi=2 family of excited states in the odd system lie at relative energies 1.40 and 2.64, compared to E(5/4) values of about 3.33 and 5.02.
- B(E2) values for transitions between states with the same xi and Delta tau1=1 are normalized to 100 for the transitions between the first multiplet and the ground state; interband transitions are one or two orders of magnitude smaller.

## Context

This work extends the study of shape phase transitions in even-even nuclei (Iachello, 2000-2001; Bohr Hamiltonian) to odd-A nuclei within the IBFM framework, specifically for the U(5)-to-O(6) transition. It contrasts with the E(5/4) supersymmetric model proposed by Iachello (2005), which uses a Bohr Hamiltonian with a gamma-independent square well potential and a Spin(5) scalar boson-fermion coupling. The results enable future comparisons between the algebraic IBFM approach and geometric collective models for odd nuclei at critical points, and highlight that the j=3/2 orbital is a special case preserving gamma-instability, unlike more general orbitals such as j=5/2.
