# Shape coexistence in Ne isotopes and hyperon impurity effect on low-lying states

> Based on the beyond-mean-field Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model, we investigate the shape coexistence in Ne isotopes and the effect of $\la$ hyperon on the energy level structure in the nuclei. The up-to-date Skyrme-type $N\la$ interaction SLL4 and the $NN$ interaction SGII are employed. Low-lying energy spectra of $^{20,22,24,26,28,30,32,34}$Ne, including the low-lying states with $J\leq 6$, are predicted, discussed in detail, and found in good agreement with experimental results. The electric quadrupole transition rate is also examined. The coexistences of a ground state rotational band and a $\be$ vibrational band are revealed in $^{20,22,24}$Ne. Unlike the previously discovered shrinkage effect of $\la_{s}$ on the ground state nuclei, it is found that the $\la_{s}$ may alter the excitation mode of the second band by affecting the distribution of the collective wave function, thereby causing the $\be$ vibrational band transitions to a vibrational band with equidistant energy levels.

## Key claim
The paper claims that shape coexistence (a ground-state rotational band and a beta vibrational band) occurs in \(^{20,22,24}\)Ne, and that adding a \(\Lambda\) hyperon can change the second band from a beta vibrational band into a vibrational band with equidistant energy levels, rather than simply causing the previously known shrinkage effect on the ground state.

## Method
The authors performed beyond-mean-field calculations using the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model with angular momentum projection and the generator coordinate method. They employed the Skyrme-type \(N\Lambda\) interaction SLL4 and the \(NN\) interaction SGII, and constrained the mean-field wave functions to axially deformed shapes parameterized by the quadrupole deformation \(\beta\). Low-lying energy spectra for \(^{20,22,24,26,28,30,32,34}\)Ne and their corresponding \(\Lambda\) hypernuclei were computed for states with \(J \leq 6\).

## Key numbers
- Ground-state deformation \(\beta_{\rm MF}\) for \(^{20}\)Ne: 0.53; for \(^{24}\)Ne: \(-0.22\) (oblate at mean-field level).
- Experimental absolute deformation \(|\beta_{\rm exp.}|\) for \(^{20}\)Ne: 0.72; for \(^{24}\)Ne: 0.41 (from NuDat).
- Excitation energy of the \(0_{\rm II}^+\) state in \(^{20}\)Ne: 3.65 MeV; in \(^{22}\)Ne: 3.74 MeV; in \(^{24}\)Ne: 0.0 MeV (ground state is \(0_{\rm I}^+\)).
- \(B(E2; 2_1^+ \to 0_1^+)\) for \(^{20}\)Ne: 56 \(e^2\text{fm}^4\) (experimental 65 \(e^2\text{fm}^4\)); for \(^{22}\)Ne: 54.9 \(e^2\text{fm}^4\) (experimental 46.7 \(e^2\text{fm}^4\)); for \(^{24}\)Ne: 30.3 \(e^2\text{fm}^4\) (experimental 28.4 \(e^2\text{fm}^4\)).
- For the \(\Lambda\) hypernucleus \(_{\Lambda}^{21}\)Ne, the \(1/2_{\rm I}^+\) state energy is \(-15.58\) MeV relative to the core, with rms charge radius 2.88 fm and average deformation \(\bar{\beta} = 0.48\).
- For \(_{\Lambda}^{25}\)Ne, the \(1/2_{\rm I}^+\) state energy is \(-17.10\) MeV, with rms charge radius 2.95 fm and average deformation \(\bar{\beta} = -0.02\).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of shape coexistence in Ne isotopes using the deformed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model (Sagawa, 2004) and the Gogny-force-based generator coordinate method (Rodriguez, 2003), which predicted strong shape coexistence in \(^{24}\)Ne with an oblate ground state and a prolate isomeric state separated by 77 keV. It also builds on mean-field studies of hyperon impurity effects on shape coexistence (Chen, 2021) by incorporating angular momentum projection and configuration mixing, enabling the first detailed investigation of how a \(\Lambda\) hyperon alters the low-lying energy spectra and band structures in these nuclei. The results enable future experimental searches for hyperon-induced changes in vibrational band patterns in light hypernuclei.
