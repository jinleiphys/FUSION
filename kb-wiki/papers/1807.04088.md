# Energy and system size dependence of subnucleonic fluctuations

> The energy evolution of the fluctuating proton structure is studied by solving the JIMWLK renormalization group equation. The initial condition at moderate $x$ is obtained by fitting the charm reduced cross section data from HERA, requiring that the proton size remains compatible with the diffractive vector meson production measurements. Additionally, we show that the nucleon shape fluctuations are visible in exclusive vector meson production off nuclei.

## Key claim

The energy evolution of the fluctuating proton structure is studied by solving the JIMWLK renormalization group equation, with the initial condition at moderate x obtained by fitting charm reduced cross section data from HERA while requiring that the proton size remains compatible with diffractive vector meson production measurements. The authors show that nucleon shape fluctuations are visible in exclusive vector meson production off nuclei, but note that their framework underestimates inclusive structure functions and the absolute J/Psi production cross section.

## Method

The authors solve the JIMWLK renormalization group equation in its Langevin form to evolve Wilson lines that encode the target's color field, using an initial condition at Bjorken-x = 0.01 fitted to HERA charm structure function data. The dipole-target scattering amplitude is computed and used to calculate cross sections for inclusive deep inelastic scattering and exclusive vector meson production via the dipole picture of the Color Glass Condensate effective theory. The free parameters controlling the proton size and saturation scale at the initial x, as well as the coupling constant or the coordinate space scale for running coupling, are constrained by requiring compatibility with the t-slope of coherent J/Psi production from HERA.

## Key numbers

- The initial condition for JIMWLK evolution is set at Bjorken-x = 0.01.
- The proton geometry is fixed at W = 75 GeV (corresponding to x approximately 10^{-3}) by comparing with HERA J/Psi data.
- Nucleon shape fluctuations in lead-lead ultraperipheral collisions at the LHC significantly enhance the incoherent vector meson production cross section starting at moderate |t| approximately 0.2 GeV^2.
- The ratio of incoherent to coherent cross section from JIMWLK evolution shows good agreement with H1 data, while the IPsat model (with no geometry evolution) gives a practically flat ratio.

## Context

This work extends the study of event-by-event fluctuations in the proton structure from Mantysaari (2016) by incorporating energy evolution via JIMWLK, and builds on the analysis of nucleon shape fluctuations in nuclei from Mantysaari (2017). The results enable future measurements at the Electron Ion Collider to constrain non-linear QCD effects and the initial state of heavy ion collisions, and suggest that LHC measurements of incoherent J/Psi spectra in ultraperipheral collisions can directly test whether nucleon shape fluctuations are visible within heavy nuclei.
