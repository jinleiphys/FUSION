# Evaporation Prescription for Time-Dependent Density Functional Calculations

> Collisions between $^{248}$Cm and $^{48}$Ca are systematically calculated by time-dependent density functional calculations with evaporation prescription. Depending on the incident energy and impact parameter, fusion, fusion-fission, and quasi-fission events are expected to appear. In this paper, the evaporation prescription is introduced, which is expected to be rather important to heavy-ion reactions producing superheavy nuclei, where the heavier total mass can be related to the higher total excitation energy.

## Key claim

The paper introduces an evaporation prescription to be applied to the final products of time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) calculations, arguing that thermal effects such as particle emission operate on a much longer timescale (several 10^-19 s) than the collective dynamics captured by TDDFT (several 10^-21 s), so the prescription can be applied as a post-processing step rather than being included dynamically.

## Method

The authors performed TDDFT calculations with a Skyrme interaction (SLy6) for collisions between 248Cm and 48Ca in a spatial box of 48 × 48 × 24 fm^3 with periodic boundary conditions, using a spatial spacing of 1.0 fm and time step of 2/3 × 10^-24 s. After obtaining TDDFT fragments with their mass numbers, proton numbers, excitation energies, and kinetic energies, they applied an evaporation prescription that determines the maximum integer number of alpha-particle and neutron emissions each fragment can undergo based on its excitation energy, using the cooling energies for one alpha-particle and one neutron emission respectively.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb barrier for the 248Cm + 48Ca system is 209.00 MeV
- The charge equilibration upper energy limit is calculated to be E/A = 3.7 MeV
- An incident energy of 1692 MeV (E/A = 5.7 MeV) shows disappearance of charge equilibration, producing 19O (N/Z = 1.375) outside the initial N/Z range of 1.40 to 1.58
- The spatial box used in calculations is 48 × 48 × 24 fm^3
- The unit spatial spacing is 1.0 fm and the unit time spacing is 2/3 × 10^-24 s
- The initial 248Cm has diameters of 19 fm, 19 fm, and 18 fm in the x, y, and z directions respectively
- The N/Z ratios are 1.58 for 248Cm, 1.40 for 48Ca, and 1.55 for the fusion product 296Lv

## Context

This work extends TDDFT calculations for superheavy element synthesis by addressing the limitation that TDDFT does not directly account for thermal instability and particle emission. The evaporation prescription is motivated by the standard three-factor decomposition of evaporation residue cross-sections (capture cross-section, compound nucleus formation probability, and survival probability against fission) as described in the work of Anto and colleagues, where the survival probability requires additional thermal treatment for superheavy systems. The approach enables future generalization of TDDFT to include cooling effects from neutron and alpha-particle evaporation in heavy-ion reactions producing superheavy nuclei.
