# Impact of weak interactions of free nucleons on the r-process in dynamical ejecta from neutron-star mergers

> We investigate beta-interactions of free nucleons and their impact on the electron fraction (Y_e) and r-process nucleosynthesis in ejecta characteristic of binary neutron star mergers (BNSMs). For that we employ trajectories from a relativistic BNSM model to represent the density-temperature evolutions in our parametric study. In the high-density environment, positron captures decrease the neutron richness at the high temperatures predicted by the hydrodynamic simulation. Circumventing the complexities of modelling three-dimensional neutrino transport, (anti)neutrino captures are parameterized in terms of prescribed neutrino luminosities and mean energies, guided by published results and assumed as constant in time. Depending sensitively on the adopted neutrino-antineutrino luminosity ratio, neutrino processes increase Y_e to values between 0.25 and 0.40, still allowing for a successful r-process compatible with the observed solar abundance distribution and a significant fraction of the ejecta consisting of r-process nuclei. If the electron neutrino luminosities and mean energies are relatively large compared to the antineutrino properties, the mean Y_e might reach values >0.40 so that neutrino captures seriously compromise the success of the r-process. In this case, the r-abundances remain compatible with the solar distribution, but the total amount of ejected r-material is reduced to a few percent, because the production of iron-peak elements is favored. Proper neutrino physics, in particular also neutrino absorption, have to be included in BNSM simulations before final conclusions can be drawn concerning r-processing in this environment and concerning observational consequences like kilonovae, whose peak brightness and color temperature are sensitive to the composition-dependent opacity of the ejecta.

## Key claim

The impact of neutrino and antineutrino captures on free nucleons in dynamical ejecta from binary neutron star mergers depends sensitively on the adopted neutrino-antineutrino luminosity ratio; for certain ratios the electron fraction can reach values above 0.40, which seriously compromises the r-process but still allows r-abundances compatible with the solar distribution, though with the total ejected r-material reduced to a few percent.

## Method

The authors used density and temperature trajectories from a relativistic smoothed-particle hydrodynamics simulation of a symmetric 1.35-1.35 solar mass binary neutron star merger with the DD2 equation of state. They parameterized neutrino and antineutrino captures on free nucleons using prescribed, time-independent luminosities and mean energies taken from published simulations by Wanajo (2014) and Ruffert (2001), and evolved the electron fraction from an initial density of 10^12 g cm^-3 down to the density where the temperature reaches 10 GK, where a full nuclear reaction network was initiated.

## Key numbers

- For Case 1 (Wanajo 2014 at 5 ms): L_nu_e = 0.6 x 10^53 erg/s, L_anti-nu_e = 1.3 x 10^53 erg/s, mean energy nu_e = 12 MeV, mean energy anti-nu_e = 16 MeV, giving asymptotic Y_e^nu_infinity approximately 0.31
- For Case 2 (Wanajo 2014 at 6 ms): L_nu_e = 2.6 x 10^53 erg/s, L_anti-nu_e = 4.0 x 10^53 erg/s, mean energy nu_e = 13 MeV, mean energy anti-nu_e = 16 MeV, giving asymptotic Y_e^nu_infinity approximately 0.42
- For Case 3 (Ruffert 2001 at 6 ms): L_nu_e = 0.3 x 10^53 erg/s, L_anti-nu_e = 10^53 erg/s, mean energy nu_e = 12.5 MeV, mean energy anti-nu_e = 17.4 MeV, giving asymptotic Y_e^nu_infinity approximately 0.21
- For Case 4 (Ruffert 2001 at 10 ms): L_nu_e = 0.6 x 10^53 erg/s, L_anti-nu_e = 1.6 x 10^53 erg/s, mean energy nu_e = 13.5 MeV, mean energy anti-nu_e = 16.3 MeV, giving asymptotic Y_e^nu_infinity approximately 0.29
- The total ejected mass in the merger model was 4.9 x 10^-3 solar masses
- Weak magnetism and recoil corrections increase the asymptotic Y_e^nu_infinity by up to 20 percent

## Context

This parametric study extends the work of Wanajo (2014) and Seki (2015), who first included neutrino interactions in relativistic merger simulations and found that neutrino captures can increase the electron fraction in dynamical ejecta. The paper systematically explores how different neutrino luminosity ratios, taken from the simulations of Wanajo (2014) and Ruffert (2001), affect the electron fraction distribution and subsequent r-process nucleosynthesis. The results motivate the need for full three-dimensional neutrino transport in merger simulations before final conclusions can be drawn about r-processing and observational consequences such as kilonova light curves, whose peak brightness and color temperature depend on the composition-dependent opacity of the ejecta.
