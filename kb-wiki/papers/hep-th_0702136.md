# Is there a "most perfect fluid" consistent with quantum field theory?

> It was recently conjectured that the ratio of the shear viscosity to entropy density, $ η/ s$, for any fluid always exceeds $\hbar/(4 πk_B)$. This conjecture was motivated by quantum field theoretic results obtained via the AdS/CFT correspondence and from empirical data with real fluids. A theoretical counterexample to this bound can be constructed from a nonrelativistic gas by increasing the number of species in the fluid while keeping the dynamics essentially independent of the species type. The question of whether the underlying structure of relativistic quantum field theory generically inhibits the realization of such a system and thereby preserves the possibility of a universal bound is considered here. Using rather conservative assumptions, it is shown here that a metastable gas of heavy mesons in a particular controlled regime of QCD provides a realization of the counterexample and is consistent with a well-defined underlying relativistic quantum field theory. Thus, quantum field theory appears to impose no lower bound on $η/s$, at least for metastable fluids.

## Key claim

The strong KSS conjecture that the ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density, η/s, has a universal lower bound of ℏ/(4πk_B) for any fluid is shown to be violated by a metastable gas of heavy mesons in a particular controlled regime of QCD, meaning quantum field theory appears to impose no lower bound on η/s, at least for metastable fluids.

## Method

The author constructs a theoretical counterexample using a nonrelativistic gas of heavy mesons in a generalized QCD with N_c colors, N_h degenerate flavors of heavy quark, and one flavor of light quark. The parameters are scaled with a parameter ξ such that N_c, N_h, and the heavy quark mass m_h all scale as e^{ξ^4}, while temperature scales as e^{-ξ^4}/ξ^2 and density as ξ^{-4}, creating a regime where the system behaves as a nonrelativistic gas of pseudoscalar heavy mesons with dynamics equivalent to classical hard sphere scattering. The shear viscosity and entropy density are then computed using the Boltzmann equation in the regime where binary collisions dominate.

## Key numbers

- The KSS conjectured bound is η/s ≥ ℏ/(4πk_B), which in natural units (ℏ = k_B = 1) becomes η/s ≥ 1/(4π)
- For the nonrelativistic gas with N_s species, η/s = C_hs ξ^3 √(m T_0) / [a^2 n_0 (log((m T_0)^{3/2}/n_0) + 5/2 + log(ξ) + log(N_s))], where C_hs ≈ 0.179
- With N_s = exp(ξ^4), the ratio simplifies to η/s = (1/ξ) × C_hs √(m T_0) / (a^2 n_0), which can be made arbitrarily small by increasing ξ
- For the heavy meson system in QCD, η/s = (1/ξ) × C_hs √(m_h0 T_0) / (a^2 n_0), again violating the bound at large ξ
- The ratio τ_fl/τ_met for the metastable system scales as n R_0^3 ∼ ξ^{-4}, making the system a well-defined fluid at large ξ

## Context

This paper directly challenges the KSS conjecture by Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2005), which proposed a universal lower bound on η/s based on AdS/CFT correspondence results and empirical data from real fluids. The author extends a theoretical counterexample briefly noted by KSS themselves involving a nonrelativistic gas with many species, and shows it can be realized within a consistent relativistic quantum field theory (QCD with scaled parameters). The work enables further investigation into whether the bound might still hold for absolutely stable fluids, though the author notes this would exclude most ordinary fluids like water, which are metastable under the standard model.
