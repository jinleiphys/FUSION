# Jet Structure in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We review recent theoretical developments in the study of the structure of jets that are produced in ultra relativistic heavy ion collisions. The core of the review focusses on the dynamics of the parton cascade that is induced by the interactions of a fast parton crossing a quark-gluon plasma. We recall the basic mechanisms responsible for medium induced radiation, underline the rapid disappearance of coherence effects, and the ensuing probabilistic nature of the medium induced cascade. We discuss how large radiative corrections modify the classical picture of the gluon cascade, and how these can be absorbed in a renormalization of the jet quenching parameter $\hat q $. Then, we analyze the (wave)-turbulent transport of energy along the medium induced cascade, and point out the main characteristics of the angular structure of such a cascade. Finally, color decoherence of the in-cone jet structure is discussed. Modest contact with phenomenology is presented towards the end of the review.

## Key claim

The review argues that in the limit of asymptotically large or dense media, successive medium-induced branchings are quasi-local and independent from one another, providing a theoretical basis for approximations made in some models of jet quenching, while also showing that color coherence characterizing jet structure in vacuum is significantly altered in a QCD medium.

## Method

The authors review theoretical developments in the study of jet structure in heavy ion collisions, focusing on the dynamics of parton cascades induced by a fast parton crossing a quark-gluon plasma. They analyze the BDMPS-Z mechanism for medium-induced gluon radiation, develop a probabilistic picture of in-medium gluon cascades, examine radiative corrections to momentum broadening and parton energy loss that can be reabsorbed in a renormalization of the jet quenching parameter, and study the time evolution of angular and energy distributions along the medium-induced cascade.

## Key numbers

* The radiative energy loss for a parton traversing a dense QCD medium scales as \(\Delta E = \frac{\pi}{4}\bar\alpha \hat q L^2\), where \(\bar\alpha \equiv \alpha_s N_c/\pi\) and \(L\) is the medium length.
* The characteristic frequency \(\omega_c \equiv \hat q L^2\) defines the scale where the formation time equals the medium size, with \(\tau_\br(\omega_c)=L\).
* The branching time for induced emission is given by \(\tau_\br = \sqrt{\omega/\hat q}\), and the inelastic mean-free path (time to branch) is \(t_\ast(\omega) = \frac{1}{\bar\alpha}\sqrt{\omega/\hat q}\).
* The medium-induced gluon radiation spectrum in the BDMPS-Z regime scales as \(\omega \frac{dI}{d\omega} \simeq \bar\alpha \sqrt{\omega_c/\omega}\) for \(\omega \ll \omega_c\).
* The number of radiated gluons with frequency larger than \(\omega\) scales as \(N(\omega) \simeq \sqrt{\omega_s(L)/\omega}\) where \(\omega_s(L) \equiv \bar\alpha^2 \omega_c(L)\).

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on medium-induced gluon bremsstrahlung by Gyulassy and Wang (1993-1994), Baier, Dokshitzer, Peigné, Mueller, Schiff (1994-1996), and Zakharov (1996-1997), as well as the opacity expansion formalism of Gyulassy, Levai and Vitev (1999-2000) and the High-Twist approach of Guo and Wang (2000-2001). It addresses limitations of standard energy loss approaches by calculating higher-order corrections and establishing the probabilistic nature of the medium-induced cascade, enabling more systematic studies of jet observables and providing theoretical foundations for Monte Carlo event generators such as JEWEL, Q-PYTHIA, and MARTINI.
