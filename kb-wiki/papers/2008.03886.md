# A Hybrid Renormalization Scheme for Quasi Light-Front Correlations in Large-Momentum Effective Theory

> In large-momentum effective theory (LaMET), calculating parton physics starts from calculating coordinate-space-$z$ correlation functions $\tilde h(z, a,P^z)$ in a hadron of momentum $P^z$ in lattice QCD. Such correlation functions involve both linear and logarithmic divergences in lattice spacing $a$, and thus need to be properly renormalized. We introduce a hybrid renormalization procedure to match these lattice correlations to those in the continuum $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme, without introducing extra non-perturbative effects at large $z$. We analyze the effect of ${\cal O}(Λ_{\rm QCD})$ ambiguity in the Wilson line self-energy subtraction involved in this hybrid scheme. To obtain the momentum-space distributions, we recommend to extrapolate the lattice data to the asymptotic $z$-region using the generic properties of the coordinate space correlations at moderate and large $P^z$, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a hybrid renormalization scheme for quasi-light-front correlation functions in LaMET that separates short-distance and long-distance renormalization, avoiding the non-perturbative effects introduced by RI/MOM or ratio methods at large \(z\), while acknowledging that the Wilson line self-energy subtraction involves an \({\cal O}(\Lambda_{\rm QCD})\) ambiguity in the \(a\)-independent term \(m_0\).

## Method

The authors propose to renormalize lattice correlation functions \(\tilde h(z,a,P^z)\) using two distinct procedures matched at an intermediate distance \(z_{\rm S}\): at short distances (\(0 \le |z| \le z_{\rm S}\)), they use a ratio method (e.g., RI/MOM, hadron matrix element at \(P^z=0\), or vacuum expectation value) where the leading-twist approximation holds and perturbative matching to \(\overline{\rm MS}\) is reliable; at large distances (\(z \ge z_{\rm S}\)), they subtract the linear divergence \(\delta m|z|\) explicitly using the auxiliary field formalism, where \(\delta m = m_{-1}(a)/a + m_0\) is extracted from fits to the exponential decay of matrix elements at large \(z\). The two procedures are matched at \(z_{\rm S}\) within the leading-twist region, estimated to be about \(0.25\) to \(0.33\) fm.

## Key numbers

* The leading-twist region where matching is valid is estimated to be \(z_{\rm LT} \approx 0.25\) to \(0.33\) fm.
* For the MILC ensemble with \(a \approx 0.12\) fm, the fitted linear divergence coefficient is \(m_{-1} = 0.234 \pm 0.012\) and the \(a\)-independent term is \(m_0 = (350 \pm 60)\) MeV, obtained from fitting the quark RI/MOM renormalization factor at scale \(\mu_R = 1.8\) GeV and \(p^z_R = 0\).
* The lattice spacings used for the \(\delta m\) fit are \(a \approx \{0.045, 0.06, 0.09, 0.12\}\) fm with 310 MeV pion mass from the MILC collaboration.

## Context

This work extends the LaMET framework (Ji 2013, Ji 2014) by addressing the renormalization of nonlocal operators, which previously relied on RI/MOM (Constantinou 2017, Stewart 2017) or ratio methods (Radyushkin 2017, Orginos 2017, Braun 2018, Li 2020) that introduce non-perturbative effects at large \(z\) through infrared logarithms. The hybrid scheme enables systematic extraction of parton distribution functions from lattice QCD without these artifacts, and the authors recommend extrapolating lattice data to the asymptotic \(z\)-region using generic properties of coordinate-space correlations at moderate and large \(P^z\).
