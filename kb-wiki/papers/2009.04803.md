# Global polarization effect and spin-orbit coupling in strong interaction

> In non-central high energy heavy ion collisions the colliding system posses a huge orbital angular momentum in the direction opposite to the normal of the reaction plane. Due to the spin-orbit coupling in strong interaction, such huge orbital angular momentum leads to the polarization of quarks and anti-quarks in the same direction. This effect, known as the global polarization effect, has been recently observed by STAR Collaboration at RHIC that confirms the theoretical prediction made more than ten years ago. The discovery has attracted much attention on the study of spin effects in heavy ion collision. It opens a new window to study properties of QGP and a new direction in high energy heavy ion physics -- Spin Physics in Heavy Ion Collisions. In this chapter, we review the original ideas and calculations that lead to the predictions. We emphasize the role played by spin-orbit coupling in high energy spin physics and discuss the new opportunities and challenges in this connection.

## Key claim

The global polarization effect (GPE) of Λ and anti-Λ hyperons in heavy-ion collisions, recently observed by the STAR Collaboration at RHIC, confirms a theoretical prediction made more than ten years ago that the huge orbital angular momentum in non-central collisions leads to quark and anti-quark polarization via spin-orbit coupling in strong interaction; the authors review the original ideas and calculations that led to this prediction, emphasizing that the effect opens a new window to study properties of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and a new direction in high energy heavy ion physics.

## Method

The authors calculated the global orbital angular momentum of the colliding system in non-central heavy-ion collisions using both hard-sphere and Woods-Saxon nuclear distributions, then estimated the transverse gradient of the average longitudinal momentum per parton in two scenarios: the Landau fireball model and the Bjorken scaling model. For the Bjorken scaling model, they used the HIJING Monte Carlo model and the Brodsky-Gunion-Kuhn (BGK) model to compute the rapidity distributions of produced partons as a function of transverse coordinate, from which they derived the local relative orbital angular momentum between neighboring partons.

## Key numbers

- The global orbital angular momentum of the colliding system is of the order of 10^4 at most impact parameters for Au+Au collisions.
- In Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV, with a hard-sphere nuclear distribution, the transverse gradient of the average longitudinal momentum per parton is dp₀/dx ≈ 0.34 GeV/fm, giving a local orbital angular momentum l₀ ≈ -1.7 for two partons separated by Δx = 1 fm.
- In the Bjorken scaling model using HIJING simulations of non-central Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV, the local relative longitudinal momentum shear ∂⟨p_z⟩/∂x ≈ 0.003 GeV/fm in the central rapidity region.
- The BGK model parameters for the pp rapidity distribution at √s = 200 GeV are a₁ = 4.584, a₂ = 26.112, and a₃ = 9.70 × 10⁻⁸.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical predictions of Liang and Wang (2004) and Gao, Liang, Wang, and Wang (2007) that led to the prediction of global polarization in heavy-ion collisions, which was recently confirmed by the STAR Collaboration at RHIC. The review connects the global polarization effect to earlier puzzles in spin physics such as the proton spin crisis, single spin left-right asymmetry, and transverse hyperon polarization, and it enables future studies of QGP properties through spin effects in heavy-ion collisions, including new experiments being carried out or planned.
