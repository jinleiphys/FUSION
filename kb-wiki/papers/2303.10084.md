# Momentum dependent nucleon-nucleon contact interaction from a relativistic Lagrangian

> A complete set of parity- and time-reversal conserving relativistic nucleon-nucleon contact operators is identified up to the order $O(p^4)$ of the expansion in soft momenta $p$. A basis is also provided for the corresponding non-relativistic operators contributing in the general reference frame. We show that the non-relativistic expansions of the relativistic operators involve twenty-six independent combinations, two starting at $O(p^0)$, seven at order $O(p^2)$ and seventeen at order $O(p^4)$. This gives supporting evidence to the existence of two free low-energy constants which parametrize an interaction depending on the total nucleon pair momentum ${\bf P}$, and were recently found to be instrumental for the resolution of the long standing $A_y$ problem in low-energy $p-d$ scattering. Furthermore, all remaining ${\bf P}$-dependent interactions at the same order are uniquely determined as relativistic corrections.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a manifestly Lorentz-invariant nucleon-nucleon contact Lagrangian, when reduced to non-relativistic form up to order \(O(p^4)\), yields exactly two unconstrained low-energy constants (LECs) that depend on the total pair momentum \(\mathbf{P}\), supporting the claim that these LECs are instrumental for resolving the long-standing \(A_y\) puzzle in low-energy \(p-d\) scattering, while all other \(\mathbf{P}\)-dependent interactions at the same order are uniquely determined as relativistic corrections.

## Method
The authors constructed a complete (though non-minimal) set of parity- and time-reversal conserving relativistic NN contact operators up to order \(O(p^4)\) in soft momenta, using products of fermion bilinears with derivatives and elements of the Clifford algebra. They then performed a non-relativistic reduction of these operators using the Foldy-Wouthuysen expansion of the Dirac field, expressing the results in terms of a basis of 45 non-relativistic operators (including 15 \(\mathbf{P}\)-independent ones) in the general reference frame. The algebraic calculations were automated with the software FORM, and the linearly independent combinations were identified by matching the relativistic expansions to the non-relativistic basis.

## Key numbers
- The non-relativistic expansions of the relativistic operators involve **26 independent combinations**: 2 starting at \(O(p^0)\), 7 at \(O(p^2)\), and 17 at \(O(p^4)\).
- The non-relativistic operator basis in the general reference frame consists of **45 members** respecting isospin, parity, and time reversal symmetry, of which **15 are \(\mathbf{P}\)-independent**.
- The two unconstrained LECs, named \(D_{16}\) and \(D_{17}\), parametrize \(\mathbf{P}\)-dependent interactions at \(O(p^4)\); their explicit expressions in terms of relativistic operators are given, e.g., \(O''_{16}/m^4 = -\tilde{O}_{10} - \frac{1}{2}\tilde{O}_{11} - \frac{3}{2}\tilde{O}_{12} + \tilde{O}_{13} - \tilde{O}_{16} + \frac{1}{2}\tilde{O}_{24} - \tilde{O}_{28} - 2\tilde{O}_{32} + \tilde{O}_{33} - 2\tilde{O}_{35} - 2\tilde{O}_{36} + 2\tilde{O}_{37} + \frac{3}{2}\tilde{O}_{39} + \tilde{O}_{46}\).
- The remaining **28 \(O(p^4)\) LECs** (denoted \(D_i^*\) in the paper) are fixed as \(1/m\) corrections to lower-order interactions; for example, \(D_1^* = \frac{3}{8}(\gamma_S - \gamma_T) - \frac{1}{2}(C_1 - C_3)\).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of relativistic constraints on NN contact interactions initiated by Girlanda (2010) and Girlanda (2020), where the two extra LECs \(D_{16}\) and \(D_{17}\) were first identified. It contrasts with the approach of Xiao (2018), which was conducted in the NN center-of-mass system and thus could not analyze \(\mathbf{P}\)-dependent operators. The results confirm that the \(\mathbf{P}\)-dependent LECs are unconstrained by relativity and cannot be determined from NN scattering data, but their effect in few-nucleon systems (as shown by Girlanda (2023) for \(p-d\) scattering) is crucial for resolving the \(A_y\) puzzle. The paper also determines the magnitude of the "drift" LECs \(C_i^*\) and \(D_i^*\), enabling future quantification of their impact in systems with \(A>2\).
