# Photoabsorption on a Nucleon in the D_{13} Resonance Energy Region

> We present a simple model for the $γN \to ππN$ reaction which reproduces the cross sections of the $π^+ π^- p, π^+ π^- n, π^+ π^0 n$ and $π^- π^0 p$ channels over the range of the energies 0.41-0.85 GeV. We use the dynamical model for the resonances, Δ(1232), N^*(1520) and ρ-meson. The total photoabsorption off a nucleon is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a simple model for the γN → ππN reaction that reproduces cross sections for multiple isospin channels in the 0.41–0.85 GeV energy range, but the model's success depends on treating the ρ-meson self-energy with a finite-range ρππ form factor and treating the N*(1520) resonance with explicit inclusion of s-wave πΔ, d-wave πΔ, and ρN decay channels.

## Method

The authors constructed an isobar model for the γN → ππN reaction that includes Δ(1232) and N*(1520) as intermediate baryon resonances, with the N*(1520) treated via a dynamical model incorporating its self-energy from πN, s-wave πΔ, d-wave πΔ, and ρN decay channels. The ρ-meson propagation was handled with a finite-range ρππ form factor, and the Δ Kroll-Ruderman term was constructed from a finite-ranged πNΔ form factor by requiring gauge invariance. The effective γNN* coupling constants were determined phenomenologically by fitting to experimental multipole amplitudes from prior partial-wave analyses.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy range covered: 0.41–0.85 GeV
- N*(1520) resonance energy used: 1520 MeV; width used: 120 MeV
- Branching fractions adopted for N*(1520): 58% to πN, 10% to s-wave πΔ, 10% to d-wave πΔ, 22% to ρN
- ρ-meson experimental mass: approximately 770 MeV; width: 154 MeV
- Helicity 3/2 coupling constant for proton target at 750 MeV: \(\tilde{g}_{3/2} = 0.1621 + i0.0522\)
- Three parameter sets for ρππ form factor used: \(q_{\rho\pi\pi} = \infty\), 100 MeV/c, and 200 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends prior isobar models by Bhalerao et al. and Arima et al. for πN scattering in the D13 channel, which treated the ππN channel only as πΔ, by explicitly including s-wave πΔ, d-wave πΔ, and ρN decay modes. The model addresses discrepancies in earlier calculations by Tejedor et al. and Murphy et al., which reproduced γp → π⁺π⁻p cross sections but failed to explain γp → π⁺π⁰n data. The results enable improved understanding of the suppression of D13 and F15 resonances observed in nuclear photoabsorption experiments.
