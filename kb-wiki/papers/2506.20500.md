# Comparison between Causal and Acausal Diffusion: a Schwinger-Keldysh Effective Field Theory Perspective

> In Fick's time-honored theory of diffusion, the system responds instantaneously to external perturbations, resulting in acausal behavior. Maxwell-Cattaneo theory addresses this issue by introducing a relaxation time, rendering the diffusion process causal. We focus on systems where this relaxation time is comparable to the diffusion time and significantly larger than the relaxation times of all other non-conserved operators. In such systems, late-time diffusion is influenced by this relaxation process, leading to a theory of quasi-diffusion. Using the Schwinger-Keldysh Effective Field Theory (SK-EFT) framework, we compare the theories of diffusion and quasi-diffusion by analyzing the real-time dynamics of correlation functions both in linear response and at one-loop order. In particular, we show that the one-loop corrections in the causal (quasi-diffusion) theory, in both the underdamped and the overdamped cases, are governed by two universal functions. In the overdamped case, the behavior mirrors that of the acausal (diffusion) theory, which has recently been applied for precision tests of SK-EFT in diffusive systems. We suggest that our results in the underdamped limit can also be used for precision tests in quasi-diffusive systems.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in diffusive systems where the relaxation time is comparable to the diffusion time and much larger than other non-hydrodynamic relaxation times, the causal Maxwell-Cattaneo theory (quasi-diffusion) yields physically distinct predictions from acausal Fickian diffusion, particularly at one-loop order where the overdamped causal case mirrors the acausal theory but the underdamped case is governed by different universal functions.

## Method

The authors construct Schwinger-Keldysh Effective Field Theories (SK-EFTs) for both acausal (Fickian) diffusion and causal (Maxwell-Cattaneo) diffusion, deriving quadratic and cubic Lagrangians for density fluctuations. They compute linear-response correlation functions analytically and evaluate one-loop corrections to the retarded response function, focusing on the non-analytic parts of the self-energy in one spatial dimension.

## Key numbers

* The collision momentum where the diffusive mode and the fast mode meet in Theory B is given by \(D k_c^2 = 1/(2\tau)\), with \(D=1\) and \(\tau=1\) yielding \(k_c=0.5\).
* The overdamped/underdamped transition in the causal theory occurs at \(\tau D k^2 = 1/4\); for \(\tau D k^2 < 1/4\) the correlation is overdamped, for \(\tau D k^2 > 1/4\) it is underdamped.
* The one-loop correction to the diffusion constant in the acausal theory is \(\delta D(\omega,\mathbf{k}) = \frac{\lambda_D^2 T \chi}{4 D^{3/2}} (-i\omega) F_d(\omega,\mathbf{k})\), while in the causal theory it is \(\delta D(\omega,\mathbf{k}) = \frac{\lambda_D^2 T \chi}{16 D^{3/2}} (-i\omega) F_d(\omega,\mathbf{k}) \times [2 + \tau D \mathbf{k}^2 - \tau \omega(3i + \tau \omega)]^2\).
* The non-analytic part of the one-loop correction in the acausal theory is \(F_1(\omega,k) = [D k^2 - 2i\omega]^{-1/2}\), while in the causal theory it is \(F_1(\omega,k) = [D k^2 - i\omega(2 - i\tau\omega)]^{-1/2} \times [\tau D k^2 + (1 - i\tau\omega)^2]^{-3/2}\).

## Context

This work extends the SK-EFT framework for diffusive systems developed by Chen-Lin et al. (2018) for acausal diffusion and by Abbasi et al. (2022) for causal diffusion, placing the comparison on a systematic field-theoretic footing. It connects to the quasi-hydrodynamics or Hydro+ approach of Grozdanov et al. (2018) and Stephanov et al. (2017), where a slow non-hydrodynamic mode is retained alongside the diffusion mode. The results enable precision tests of SK-EFT in quasi-diffusive systems, analogous to the tests already performed in acausal diffusive systems, and can be applied to experimental data from ultracold atomic gases such as the Hubbard model measurements by Brown et al. (2019).
