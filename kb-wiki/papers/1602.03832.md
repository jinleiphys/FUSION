# Improved empirical parametrizations of the $Î³^\ast N \to Î”(1232)$ and $Î³^\ast N \to N(1520)$ helicity amplitudes and the Siegert's theorem

> In the nucleon electroexcitation reactions, $Î³^\ast N \to R$, where $R$ is a nucleon resonance ($N^\ast$), the electric amplitude $E$, and the longitudinal amplitude $S_{1/2}$, are related by $E \propto \fracÏ‰{|{\bf q}|}S_{1/2}$, at the pseudo-threshold limit ($|{\bf q}| \to 0$), where $Ï‰$ and $|{\bf q}|$ are respectively the energy and the magnitude of three-momentum of the photon. The previous relation is usually refereed as the Siegert's theorem. The form of the electric amplitude, defined in terms of the transverse amplitudes $A_{1/2}$ and $A_{3/2}$, and the explicit coefficients of the relation, depend on the angular momentum and parity ($J^P$) of the resonance $R$. The Siegert's theorem is the consequence of the structure of the electromagnetic transition current, which induces constraints between the electromagnetic form factors in the pseudo-threshold limit. In the present work, we study the implications of the Siegert's theorem for the $Î³^\ast N \to Î”(1232)$ and $Î³^\ast N \to N(1520)$ transitions. For the $Î³^\ast N \to N(1520)$ transition, in addition to the relation between electric amplitude and longitudinal amplitude, we obtain also a relation between the two transverse amplitudes: $A_{1/2}= A_{3/2} /\sqrt{3}$, at the pseudo-threshold. % The constraints at the pseudo-threshold are tested for the MAID2007 parametrizations of the reactions under discussion. New parametrizations for the amplitudes $A_{1/2}$, $A_{3/2}$ and $S_{1/2}$, for the $Î³^\ast N \to Î”(1232)$ and $Î³^\ast N \to N(1520)$ transitions, valid for small and large $Q^2$, are proposed. The new parametrizations are consistent with both: the pseudo-threshold constraints (Siegert's theorem) and the empirical data.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the Siegert's theorem imposes specific constraints on the helicity amplitudes for the $\gamma^\ast N \to \Delta(1232)$ and $\gamma^\ast N \to N(1520)$ transitions at the pseudo-threshold limit ($|{\bf q}| \to 0$), and that the MAID2007 parametrization fails to satisfy these constraints, particularly for the $\Delta(1232)$ transition where the correct relation is $\frac{E_{1+}}{|{\bf q}|} = \lambda_R \frac{S_{1/2}}{|{\bf q}|^2}$ rather than the previously assumed $E_{1+} = \lambda_R \frac{S_{1/2}}{|{\bf q}|}$.

## Method

The author derived the pseudo-threshold constraints from the covariant form of the electromagnetic transition current using Rarita-Schwinger and Dirac spinors for spin-3/2 resonances, obtaining the explicit relations between electric, longitudinal, and transverse helicity amplitudes. These constraints were tested against the MAID2007 parametrizations, and new parametrizations (MAID-SG) for the helicity amplitudes $A_{1/2}$, $A_{3/2}$, and $S_{1/2}$ were constructed using polynomial-exponential forms that enforce the Siegert's theorem at the pseudo-threshold while fitting empirical data for $Q^2 \le 2$ GeV$^2$.

## Key numbers

- For $\gamma^\ast N \to \Delta(1232)$: the pseudo-threshold occurs at $Q^2_{PS} \simeq -0.09$ GeV$^2$, with the constraint $G_E = \frac{M_R-M}{2 M_R} G_C$ (Eq. 31), leading to $\frac{E_{1+}}{|{\bf q}|} = \lambda_R \frac{S_{1/2}}{|{\bf q}|^2}$ where $\lambda_R = \sqrt{2}(M_R - M)$.
- For $\gamma^\ast N \to N(1520)$: at pseudo-threshold, $G_M = 0$ and $G_E = \frac{M_R - M}{M_R} G_C$, giving $\frac{1}{2} E_{2-} = \lambda_R \frac{S_{1/2}}{|{\bf q}|}$ and $A_{1/2} = A_{3/2} /\sqrt{3}$.
- The MAID-SG parametrization for $\Delta(1232)$ uses $G_E$ coefficients: $b_0 = 14.72 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$, $b_1 = -0.0566$ GeV$^{-2}$, $b_2 = 1.91$ GeV$^{-4}$, $b_3 = 0.0164$ GeV$^{-6}$, $b_4 = 1.31$ GeV$^{-2}$; $G_C$ coefficient $c_0 = 21.82 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ is fixed by the Siegert constraint.
- The PDG result for the $\Delta(1232)$ electric quadrupole form factor at the photon point is $G_E(0) = 0.076 \pm 0.015$, corresponding to $R_{EM}(0) = -(2.5 \pm 0.5)\%$.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of pseudo-threshold constraints previously applied to the $\gamma^\ast N \to N(1535)$ transition (Ramalho, 2016) to the $\Delta(1232)$ and $N(1520)$ resonances. The new parametrizations correct the MAID2007 parametrizations (Tiator, 2006; Drechsel, 2007) which violate the Siegert's theorem at the pseudo-threshold. The results enable improved modeling of Dalitz decays of resonances ($\Delta \to e^+ e^- N$) and provide constraints for quark model calculations, where the Siegert's theorem requires inclusion of two-body currents beyond the impulse approximation (Buchmann, 1998).
