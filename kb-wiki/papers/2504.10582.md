# Hadronic light-by-light scattering in the anomalous magnetic moments of electron and $τ$

> In Refs. [1,2] we provided a complete dispersive evaluation of the hadronic light-by-light (HLbL) contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon. While the evaluation strategy was developed for the kinematic situation determined by the muon mass, a similar approach also applies to the HLbL corrections to the anomalous magnetic moments of the electron and $τ$ lepton, shifting the sensitivity in the loop integrals to smaller and larger momenta, respectively. In this Letter, we propagate the corresponding uncertainties of the various contributions, obtaining $a_e^\text{HLbL}= 3.51(23)\times 10^{-14}$ and $a_τ^\text{HLbL}= 3.77(29)\times 10^{-8}$.

# Hadronic light-by-light scattering in the anomalous magnetic moments of electron and $\tau$

## Key claim

The dispersive evaluation of hadronic light-by-light (HLbL) scattering previously developed for the muon is extended to the electron and tau lepton, yielding $a_e^\text{HLbL}=3.51(23)\times 10^{-14}$ and $a_\tau^\text{HLbL}=3.77(29)\times 10^{-8}$, with the caveat that the relative precision remains nearly identical across all three leptons at 7-8% despite the vastly different kinematic regimes probed by the loop integrals.

## Method

The authors adapt the dispersive formalism for HLbL scattering from Hoferichter et al. (2024) to the electron and tau masses, replacing the muon mass in the kernel functions of the master formula. For the electron, numerical instabilities arising from the small mass are resolved by either expanding problematic expressions or using higher-precision arithmetic (DoubleFloats with ~31 decimal digits). The HLbL tensor is reconstructed from its singularities using exclusive hadronic states at low virtualities (pseudoscalar poles, two-meson intermediate states, axial-vector resonances, scalar and tensor states), matched onto perturbative QCD and operator product expansion (OPE) at higher momenta, with the charm loop evaluated by replacing the pQCD quark loop with $\eta_c(1S)$ and $\eta_c(2S)$ hadronic descriptions below a matching scale.

## Key numbers

* $a_e^\text{HLbL} = 3.51(23) \times 10^{-14}$ (total, combining light-quark and charm contributions)
* $a_\tau^\text{HLbL} = 3.77(29) \times 10^{-8}$ (total, combining light-quark and charm contributions)
* Light-quark contribution to $a_e^\text{HLbL}$: $3.45(9)_\text{low}(8)_\text{exp}(6)_\text{match}(15)_\text{sys}(11)_\text{eff}[23]_\text{total} \times 10^{-14}$
* Light-quark contribution to $a_\tau^\text{HLbL}$: $3.36(4)_\text{low}(13)_\text{exp}(13)_\text{match}(17)_\text{sys}(11)_\text{eff}[27]_\text{total} \times 10^{-8}$
* Charm-loop contribution: $a_e^\text{HLbL}[c] = 0.06(2) \times 10^{-14}$, $a_\tau^\text{HLbL}[c] = 0.41(9) \times 10^{-8}$
* Pseudoscalar pole ($\pi^0$, $\eta$, $\eta'$) contribution to $a_e^\text{HLbL}$: $3.63^{+0.10}_{-0.08} \times 10^{-14}$; to $a_\tau^\text{HLbL}$: $1.19^{+0.07}_{-0.05} \times 10^{-8}$
* Relative precision: 7% for electron, 8% for muon, 8% for tau light-quark contributions

## Context

This work extends the complete dispersive evaluation of HLbL scattering for the muon from Hoferichter et al. (2024) to the electron and tau, where the loop integrals probe lower and higher momenta respectively due to the mass hierarchies $m_\mu/m_e = 206.768$ and $m_\tau/m_\mu = 16.817$. The results agree with earlier estimates by Jegerlehner (2017) and Prades (2009) for the electron ($3.7(5)\times 10^{-14}$ and $3.5(1.0)\times 10^{-14}$) and by Eidelman (2007) for the tau ($5(3)\times 10^{-8}$), but with significantly improved precision and a full error analysis. For the electron, the HLbL uncertainty is now two orders of magnitude below the current experimental precision, while for the tau the improved evaluation directly impacts the Standard Model prediction, though experimental sensitivity to $a_\tau$ remains out of reach for the foreseeable future.
