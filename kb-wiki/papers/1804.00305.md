# A pseudo-conformal equation of state in compact-star matter from topology change and hidden symmetries of QCD

> We construct a new effective field theory approach to the equation of state (EoS), dubbed pseudo-confomal model "PCM," for nuclear and compact star matter entirely in terms of effective hadron degrees of freedom. The possible transition at $n\sim (2-4) n_0$ (where $n_0$ is the normal nuclear matter density) from hadron degrees of freedom to strongly-coupled quark degrees of freedom, giving rise to a soft-to-hard changeover in the EoS that can accommodate the massive stars observed, is effectuated by the topology change at $n_{1/2}\gsim 2n_0$ from skyrmions to half-skyrmions without involving local order-parameter fields. The mechanism exploits possible emergence of hidden scale and local symmetries of QCD at high density, leading to a precocious "pseudo-conformal" sound velocity $v_s^2=1/3$ (in unit of $c=1$) for $n\gsim 3n_0$. The resulting prediction signals a drastic departure from standard nuclear many-body theory in the density regime involved in the massive stars. We suggest that the tidal deformability implemented in gravitational waves coming from coalescing neutron stars in LIGO/Virgo-type observations could pin down the location of the topology change density $n_{1/2}$.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a "pseudo-conformal model" (PCM) for the equation of state of compact-star matter, arguing that a topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions at a density \(n_{1/2} \gsim 2n_0\) (where \(n_0\) is normal nuclear matter density) leads to a precocious sound velocity \(v_s^2 = 1/3\) for \(n \gsim 3n_0\), a result the authors claim is "totally foreign" to standard chiral effective field theory (S\(\chi\)EFT) but fully compatible with observed massive stars.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory Lagrangian, denoted \(bs\)HLS, which includes pions, nucleons, vector mesons (as hidden local gauge fields), and a scalar dilaton field (as a pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone boson for broken scale symmetry). The Lagrangian's bare parameters are matched to QCD via current correlators at a scale \(\Lambda^\ast\), and the topology change at \(n_{1/2}\) is implemented by replacing the full \(V_{lowk}\) renormalization-group results in the high-density region (R-II, \(n \geq n_{1/2}\)) with a simple analytic form for the energy per nucleon, \(E/A = X^\alpha (n/n_0)^{1/3} + Y^\alpha (n/n_0)^{-1} - 939\) MeV, which by construction yields \(v_s^2 = 1/3\). The coefficients \(X^\alpha\) and \(Y^\alpha\) for proton fraction \(\alpha = (n_n - n_p)/(n_n + n_p) = (0,1)\) are fixed by requiring continuity of chemical potential and pressure at \(n = n_{1/2}\) between the low-density region (R-I) and R-II.

## Key numbers

* The topology change density is taken as \(n_{1/2} \gsim 2n_0\), with a specific trial value of \(n_{1/2} = 2.6 n_0\) used for tidal deformability calculations.
* The sound velocity reaches the "pseudo-conformal" value \(v_s^2 = 1/3\) (in units of \(c=1\)) for densities \(n \gsim 3n_0\).
* For the PCM with \(n_{1/2} = 2.6 n_0\), the maximum neutron star mass is \(M_{\rm max} = 2.02 M_\odot\) with radius \(R = 11.86\) km and central density \(n_{\rm cent} = 5.6 n_0\).
* For a \(1.4 M_\odot\) star with \(n_{1/2} = 2.6 n_0\), the dimensionless tidal deformability is \(\Lambda \sim 640\) with central density \(n_{\rm cent} = 2.3 n_0\) and radius \(\sim 12.8\) km.
* The predicted symmetry energy at \(2n_0\) is \(E_{sym}^{pred}(2n_0) \simeq 49\) MeV, consistent with the experimental bound \(E_{sym}(2n_0) = 46.9 \pm 10.1\) MeV.
* For comparison, the \(V_{lowk}\)RG result with \(n_{1/2} = 2.0 n_0\) gave \(M_{\rm max} = 2.05 M_\odot\), \(R = 12.19\) km, \(n_{\rm cent} = 5.1 n_0\), and \(\Lambda \sim 790\) for a \(1.4 M_\odot\) star.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous \(V_{lowk}\)RG approach (PKLMR, 2017) and the skyrmion crystal simulations of Park, Lee, Rho, and Sin (2010) by constructing a simplified analytic "pseudo-conformal model" that captures the same physics. It contrasts with standard nuclear many-body theory and S\(\chi\)EFT, which the authors argue cannot produce \(v_s^2 = 1/3\) at non-asymptotic densities without a change of degrees of freedom (citing Tews, 2017). The model enables predictions for the tidal deformability \(\Lambda\) measurable in LIGO/Virgo gravitational-wave observations, which could pin down the topology change density \(n_{1/2}\) and provide information on the hadron-quark crossover density, a quantity currently inaccessible to reliable theories or experiments.
