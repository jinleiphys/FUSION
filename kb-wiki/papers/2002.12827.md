# Redshift factor and diffusive equilibrium of unbound neutrons in the single nucleus model of accreting neutron star crust

> Using a Wigner-Seitz approximation with spherical cells, we re-analyze a widely used single nucleus model of accreting neutron star crust. We calculate beta disequilibrium within the crust, which is sizable, and implies that neutron and baryon chemical potentials, $μ_n$ and $μ_{\rm b}$, are not equal. We include also non-equilibrium reactions, driven by matter compression, and proceeding in the reaction layers. The constancy of $e^Φμ_n$, where the spacetime metric component $g_{00}=e^{2Φ}$, in the shells between the reaction layers is not applicable, because single electron captures are blocked, so that the neutron fraction is fixed, and therefore neutrons are not an independent component of the crust matter. The absence of neutron diffusion in the shells between the reaction layers, stems from the constancy of the neutron fraction (concentration) in these shells. In the reaction layers, the outward force resulting from neutron fraction gradient is balanced by the inward gravitational force acting on unbound neutrons. Neglecting the thickness of the reaction layers compared to the shell thickness, we obtain condition $e^{Φ(r)}f_Q(r)g(r)=$constant, where $g$ is Gibbs energy per nucleon, undergoing discontinuous drops on the reaction surfaces, and $f_Q(r)g(r)={\widetilde{g}}(r)$ is a continuous function, due to the factor $f_Q(r)$ canceling the discontinuities (drops) in $g(r)$. The function $f_Q(r)$ is calculated using the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations from $f_Q(P)$ and $g(P)$ obtained from the equation of state (EOS) with discontinuites. The constancy of of $e^{Φ(r)}{\widetilde{g}}(r)$ is an extension of the standard relation $e^{Φ(r)}{μ}_{\rm b}(r)=$constant, valid in hydrostatic equilibrium for catalyzed crust.

## Key claim

The widely used condition that $e^{\Phi}\mu_n$ is constant throughout the accreted neutron star crust is not applicable in the shells between reaction layers, because single electron captures are blocked and the neutron fraction is fixed, meaning neutrons are not an independent component. Instead, the authors derive a generalized condition $e^{\Phi(r)}\widetilde{g}(r)=$constant, where $\widetilde{g}(r)=f_Q(r)g(r)$ is a continuous function that cancels the discontinuous drops in the Gibbs energy per nucleon $g(r)$ at reaction surfaces.

## Method

The authors use a Wigner-Seitz approximation with spherical cells to re-analyze the single nucleus model (SNM) of a fully accreted neutron star crust, simulating compressional evolution of a cell under increasing pressure. They calculate beta disequilibrium within the crust and include non-equilibrium reactions driven by matter compression that proceed in thin reaction layers, using the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations to relate the equation of state (EOS) with discontinuities to the metric function $\Phi(r)$. The analysis treats the crust as consisting of shells with fixed $(A_{\rm cell}, Z)$ separated by spherical reaction surfaces where $g(r)$ undergoes discontinuous drops, and derives the regularizing factor $f_Q(r)$ from the EOS.

## Key numbers

- The deviation of $e^{\Phi(r)}\mu_n(r)$ from constancy in the fully accreted inner crust fluctuates with maximum amplitude $\sim 0.4\,{\rm MeV}$ reached after the neutron drip point, then decreasing with density and depth.
- The thickness of a reaction layer is estimated as $d_j = \Delta\mu_n^{j} / (m_n g_z) \simeq 10\,{\rm m}$.
- For accreted crusts, the ratio $Q_j/g_j \lesssim 10^{-3}$, so approximating the regularizing factor $f_Q$ by $1 + \sum_i Q_i/g_i \Theta(P>P^+_j)$ is precise within $10^{-5}$.
- The neutron drip pressure occurs at densities exceeding $5\times 10^{11}\,{\rm g\,cm^{-3}}$, and the crust extends up to $10^{14}\,{\rm g\,cm^{-3}}$ at the bottom.
- In the outer crust, nuclear ashes at $\sim 10^8\,{\rm g\,cm^{-3}}$ are compressed during accretion.

## Context

This work extends the standard relation $e^{\Phi(r)}\mu_{\rm b}(r)=$constant derived by Harrison (1965) for cold catalyzed matter in hydrostatic equilibrium, and addresses criticisms by Fortin (2019) regarding the validity of the SNM for calculating crust structure and deep crustal heating. The authors show that the simplest SNM does not violate general relativistic hydrostatic equilibrium or diffusive equilibrium of unbound neutrons, contrary to recent claims. The results enable continued use of the SNM for modeling the thermal state of neutron stars in low-mass X-ray binaries (LMXBs) in quiescence, while noting that extensions including finite reaction layer thickness (Bildsten, 1998) and temperature effects for sub-threshold electron captures remain to be incorporated.
