# Two-body correlation functions in nuclear matter with $np$ condensate

> The density, spin and isospin correlation functions in nuclear matter with a neutron-proton ($np$) condensate are calculated to study the possible signatures of the BEC-BCS crossover in the low-density region. It is shown that the criterion of the crossover (Phys. Rev. Lett. {\bf 95}, 090402 (2005)), consisting in the change of the sign of the density correlation function at low momentum transfer, fails to describe correctly the density-driven BEC-BCS transition at finite isospin asymmetry or finite temperature. As an unambiguous signature of the BEC-BCS transition, there can be used the presence (BCS regime) or absence (BEC regime) of the singularity in the momentum distribution of the quasiparticle density of states.

## Key claim

The criterion proposed by MGB (2005) that the density correlation function changes sign at low momentum transfer across the BEC-BCS crossover fails to describe correctly the density-driven BEC-BCS transition at finite isospin asymmetry or finite temperature, and therefore cannot be considered a universal signature of the transition.

## Method

The author calculated the density, spin, and isospin correlation functions in superfluid nuclear matter with neutron-proton (np) pairing correlations in the S=1, T=0 channel, using a zero-range effective force parametrized to reproduce the pairing gap from the Paris NN potential. The gap equation was solved self-consistently with equations for total density and neutron excess, and the correlation functions were then determined from the normal and anomalous distribution functions at zero and finite temperature for various isospin asymmetries.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density used as reference: $\varrho_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}$
- Zero-range force parameters: $v_0 = -530\ \text{MeV}\cdot\text{fm}^3$, $\eta = 0$, with cutoff $\varepsilon_c = 60\ \text{MeV}$
- At zero temperature and zero momentum transfer, the transverse spin correlation function satisfies the sum rule $S^t(q=0) = -1/4$, independent of density and isospin asymmetry
- The transverse isospin correlation function at zero momentum transfer satisfies $T^t(q=0) = -(1-\alpha)/4$, independent of density, where $\alpha$ is the asymmetry parameter

## Context

This work extends the study of BEC-BCS crossover signatures from ultracold fermionic atom gases (MGB, 2005) to nuclear matter with np pairing, and builds on prior studies of np pairing correlations and the BEC-BCS crossover in nuclear matter (BLS, AFRS, LNS, IYB). The results show that the density correlation function sign-change criterion is not universal, and the author proposes instead that the presence or absence of a singularity in the momentum distribution of the quasiparticle density of states serves as an unambiguous signature of the BEC-BCS transition, relevant for low-density nuclear systems such as exotic nuclei, heavy ion collisions, and neutron star crusts.
