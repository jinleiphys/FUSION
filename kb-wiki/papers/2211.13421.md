# Dynamical fermions, centre vortices, and emergent phenomena

> The non-trivial ground-state vacuum fields of QCD form the foundation of matter. Here we examine the centre vortices identified within the ground-state fields of lattice QCD. We aim to understand the manner in which dynamical fermions in the QCD vacuum alter the centre-vortex structure. Using modern visualisation techniques, the centre-vortex structure of pure-gauge and dynamical-fermion fields is quantified and compared. We then explore the impact this modified structure has on measures of confinement and dynamical mass generation. The string tension of the static quark potential, positivity-violation in the gluon propagator, and dynamical mass generation in the overlap quark propagator are of particular interest. The impact of dynamical fermions is significant and provides new insights into the role of centre vortices in underpinning both confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in QCD.

## Key claim

The paper argues that including dynamical fermions in lattice QCD significantly alters the centre-vortex structure of the ground-state vacuum fields, and that this modification brings the phenomenology of centre vortices much closer to a perfect encapsulation of both confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, whereas in pure-gauge theory the vortex-only string tension is only about 60% of the full string tension and vortex removal leaves residual nonperturbative effects.

## Method

The authors identify centre vortices on matched \(32^3 \times 64\) lattice ensembles by gauge-fixing to Maximal Centre Gauge and projecting link variables to the centre elements of SU(3). They compare a pure-gauge ensemble using the Iwasaki gauge action with a \(2+1\)-flavor dynamical-fermion ensemble from the PACS-CS Collaboration at a pion mass of 156 MeV, using the same gauge action and non-perturbatively improved Wilson quarks. They then compute the static quark potential, the Landau-gauge gluon propagator and its Euclidean correlator, and the overlap quark propagator mass function on the untouched, vortex-only, and vortex-removed configurations.

## Key numbers

* The pure-gauge ensemble has a lattice spacing of \(a = 0.100\) fm; the dynamical-fermion ensemble has \(a = 0.0933\) fm, set via the Sommer parameter with \(r_0 = 0.4921\) fm.
* The dynamical-fermion ensemble uses the lightest \(u\)- and \(d\)-quark-mass ensemble from PACS-CS, with a pion mass of 156 MeV.
* The average number of vortices composing the primary percolating cluster in \(32^2 \times 64\) spatial slices roughly doubles from \(\sim 3,000\) in pure gauge to \(\sim 6,000\) in full QCD.
* The branching probability in full QCD is higher than in pure gauge by a ratio of \(\sim 3/2\).
* In pure gauge, the vortex-only string tension is about \(60\%\) of the full string tension; with dynamical fermions, vortices alone capture both the screening of the pure-gauge string tension and the full string tension of the original untouched ensemble.
* The number of spatial plaquettes on these lattices is \(32^2 \times 64 \times 3 = 196,608\).

## Context

This work extends the centre-vortex program initiated by 't Hooft (1977, 1979) and Nielsen and Olesen (1979) and developed on the lattice by Greensite, Faber, and collaborators (1996-2003). It directly builds on recent studies by Biddle et al. (2020, 2022), Leinweber et al. (2021, 2022), and Virgili et al. (2021) that examine the impact of dynamical fermions on centre vortices. The improved separation of perturbative and nonperturbative physics achieved with dynamical fermions enables a cleaner test of whether centre vortices are the fundamental mechanism underlying confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, and opens the door to using vortex-modified ensembles to study hadron structure and the nature of the QCD vacuum.
