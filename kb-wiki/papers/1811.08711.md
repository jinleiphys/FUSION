# Statistical fluctuations of correlators in the Color Glass Condensate

> In the McLerran-Venugopalan model, correlators of Wilson lines are given by an average over a Gaussian ensemble of random color sources. In numerical implementations, these averages are approximated by a Monte-Carlo sampling. In this paper, we study the statistical error made with such a sampling, with emphasis on the momentum dependence of this error. Using the example of the dipole amplitude, we consider various approximants that are all equivalent in the limit of infinite statistics but differ with finite statistics and compare their statistical errors. For correlation functions that are translation invariant, we show that averaging over the barycenter coordinate drastically reduces the statistical error and more importantly modifies its momentum dependence.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in Monte-Carlo evaluations of Wilson line correlators within the McLerran-Venugopalan model, the statistical error on the dipole amplitude at large transverse momentum scales as \(p_\perp^{-4}\) rather than decaying as the exact result \(p_\perp^{-8}\), making naive sampling worthless at high momentum; however, averaging over the barycenter coordinate in translation-invariant systems drastically reduces this error and makes the relative error momentum-independent.

## Method

The authors study the dipole amplitude \(C(\mathbf{x}_\perp,\mathbf{y}_\perp) = \frac{1}{N_c}\langle \mathrm{tr}(U(\mathbf{x}_\perp)U^\dagger(\mathbf{y}_\perp))\rangle\) in the MV model, discretized on a \(64\times 64\) transverse lattice with spacing \(Q_s a = 1\) and \(L=20\) longitudinal slices, using \(N_c=2\). They compare several Monte-Carlo approximants (the raw complex measurement, its real part, its modulus, and the modulus-squared) against the known analytic result, computing the variance \(\sigma_N^2(\mathbf{p}_\perp)\) analytically from four-point correlators of Wilson lines. They then introduce an alternative estimator that integrates over the barycenter coordinate \((\mathbf{x}_\perp+\mathbf{y}_\perp)/2\) and evaluate its statistical fluctuations.

## Key numbers

* The transverse lattice size is \(N_\perp = 64\) with spacing \(Q_s a = 1\), and \(L = 20\) slices in the \(x^-\) direction.
* The infrared regulator mass is \(m^2 = 0.2\, Q_s^2\).
* The number of colors used in numerical calculations is \(N_c = 2\).
* Without barycenter averaging, the variance \(\sigma_N^2(\mathbf{p}_\perp)\) contains a term \(\frac{1}{N N_c^2} \mathcal{S}_\perp C(\mathbf{p}_\perp)\) that at large \(p_\perp\) behaves as \(\sim p_\perp^{-4}\), while the exact squared result \(C^2(\mathbf{p}_\perp)\) decays as \(\sim p_\perp^{-8}\).
* With barycenter averaging, a single measurement using only \(N=10\) configurations tracks the exact result remarkably well, whereas without barycenter averaging even \(N=10^3\) configurations produce large scatter at high momentum.

## Context

This work extends the standard McLerran-Venugopalan model (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1993) by quantifying the statistical errors inherent in Monte-Carlo implementations of Wilson line correlators, which are central to CGC phenomenology. The analytical expressions for the four-point correlator rely on methods from Blaizot, Iancu, and Weigert (2004). The results enable practitioners to design more efficient numerical schemes for observables such as gluon production in nucleus-nucleus collisions (Krasnitz and Nara, 2000; Lappi, 2003), where the dependence on color sources can only be obtained numerically.
