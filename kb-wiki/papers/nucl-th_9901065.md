# Testing Low Energy Theorems in Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering

> Low energy theorems have been derived for the coefficients of the effective range expansion in s-wave nucleon-nucleon scattering valid to leading nontrivial order in an expansion based $Q$ counting, a scheme in which both $m_π$ and $1/a$ (where $a$ is the scattering length) are treated as small mass scales. Previous tests of these theorems based on coefficients extracted from scattering data indicate a pattern of gross violations which suggested serious problems for the perturbative treatment of pions implicit in $Q$ counting. We discuss the possibility that uncertainties associated with extracting the coefficients from the scattering data make such tests invalid. Here we show that errors in the s-wave phase shift extractions are sufficiently small to test direct test predictions from $Q$ counting at next to leading order. In particular we show that there exist low energy theorems for the sum of all terms in the effective range expansion beyond the first two which allow for precise tests. These low energy theorems fail badly which suggests that pionic aspects of $Q$ counting are not under control.

# Testing Low Energy Theorems in Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering

## Key claim

The low energy theorems derived from Q counting for the shape function (the sum of all terms in the effective range expansion beyond the first two) fail badly in the triplet channel, suggesting that the pionic aspects of Q counting are not under control. The authors show that uncertainties in phase shift extractions are sufficiently small to permit a direct test, contrary to earlier claims by Mehen and Stewart (1998) that errors were too large.

## Method

The authors define the shape function S(k^2) = k cot(delta) + 1/a - (1/2) r_e k^2, which isolates the contributions of all higher-order effective range expansion coefficients (v_2 and above). They compare the Q counting prediction for S(k^2) at next-to-leading order (order Q^2) against values extracted from the Nijmegen partial wave analysis for the triplet (^3S_1) channel, using the precisely known scattering length a and effective range r_e. They also test at the deuteron pole (k = i gamma) where the binding energy is known with high precision.

## Key numbers

- Triplet channel scattering length: a = 5.420 +/- 0.001 fm; effective range: r_e = 1.753 +/- 0.002 fm
- Deuteron binding energy: B = 2.224575(9) MeV
- At the deuteron pole: extracted S(-gamma^2) = -0.017 +/- 0.012 MeV; low energy theorem prediction = -0.743 MeV (disagreement by more than 6 sigma)
- At T_lab = 50 MeV: extracted S = 11.03 +/- 0.24 MeV; low energy theorem prediction = -20.10 MeV (disagreement by more than 100 sigma)
- Low energy theorem prediction for v_2 = -0.95 fm^3; extracted values range from 0.033 to 0.046 fm^3 depending on the fit
- Low energy theorem prediction for v_3 = 4.6 fm^5; extracted values range from 0.671 to 0.675 fm^5
- Low energy theorem prediction for v_4 = -25 fm^7; extracted values range from -3.90 to -3.97 fm^7

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous derivation of low energy theorems for effective range expansion coefficients (Cohen and Hansen, 1998) and directly addresses the criticism by Mehen and Stewart (1998) that uncertainties in phase shift extractions were too large to test these theorems. The results contradict the Q counting scheme of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998), which treats both m_pi and 1/a as small mass scales of order Q. The failure suggests that the effective range r_e (approximately 1.7 fm in the triplet channel) represents a short-distance scale comparable to or larger than 1/m_pi, making the chiral expansion uncontrolled. This is consistent with the finding by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) that a simple effective range expansion without explicit pions describes deuteron form factors better than the next-to-leading order calculation including pions. The work enables future tests comparing theories with and without explicit pions using the same number of fitted parameters.
