# Neutron stars including the effects of chaotic magnetic fields and the anomalous magnetic moments

> In this work the relativistic mean field (RMF) FSUGold model extended to include hyperons is employed to study the properties of neutron stars with strong magnetic fields. The chaotic magnetic field approximation is utilized. The effect of the anomalous magnetic moments (AMMs) is also investigated. It is shown that the equation of state (EOS)of neutron star matter is stiffened by the presence of the magnetic field, which increases the maximum mass of neutron star by around 6%. The AMMs only have a small influence on the EOS of neutron star matter, and increase the maximum mass of neutron star by 0.02 M$_{sun}$. Neutral particles are spin polarized due to the presence of the AMMs.

## Key claim

Using the chaotic magnetic field approximation and an energy density-dependent magnetic field model within the relativistic mean field (RMF) FSUGold model extended to include hyperons, the magnetic field stiffens the equation of state and increases the maximum mass of neutron stars by about 6% for a range of the free parameter gamma (2 ≤ γ ≤ 5), while the anomalous magnetic moments (AMMs) only increase the maximum mass by 0.02 solar masses.

## Method

The authors employed the RMF FSUGold model, extended to include the full baryon octet, to compute the equation of state (EOS) of neutron star matter under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality. They used the chaotic magnetic field approximation, where the magnetic field contributes a pressure of B²/6 instead of B²/2, to maintain an isotropic energy-momentum tensor for the TOV equations, and adopted an energy density-dependent magnetic field profile rather than the more common number density-dependent profile. The effect of the anomalous magnetic moments of baryons and leptons was included via a coupling term in the Lagrangian, and the resulting EOS was used to solve the TOV equations for the mass-radius relation.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass without magnetic field: 1.36 M_sun (radius 11.4 km, central energy density 4.86 fm⁻⁴).
- For 2 ≤ γ ≤ 5 with magnetic field (no AMMs): maximum mass around 1.44 M_sun, an increase of about 6% from the field-free case; corresponding radius around 10.8 km.
- For γ = 1 with magnetic field (no AMMs): maximum mass 1.62 M_sun (radius 12.2 km, central energy density 4.76 fm⁻⁴).
- Including AMMs increases the maximum mass by 0.02 M_sun for 1 ≤ γ ≤ 5 and increases the corresponding radius by about 0.8 km.
- Fixed magnetic field value used for computation: 3.1 × 10¹⁸ G; surface magnetic field: 1 × 10¹⁴ G.

## Context

This work extends the chaotic magnetic field approximation, previously applied to quark stars by Lopes and Menezes (2015), to hadronic neutron stars with hyperons, and incorporates the anomalous magnetic moments of baryons first calculated for hadronic EOS by Broderick (2000). It contrasts with most prior studies (e.g., Bandyopadhyay (1997), Rabhi (2009), Menezes (2009)) that used a number density-dependent magnetic field, instead adopting an energy density-dependent model. The results enable further studies of magnetized neutron stars with more realistic magnetic field profiles and highlight that the extended FSUGold model cannot reach the observed 2.01 M_sun maximum mass, suggesting the need for modifications like the sigma-cut scheme proposed by Maslov (2015).
