# Global description of bottomonium suppression in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at LHC energies

> We show that we can reach a global and coherent description of bottomonium suppression in both proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at the LHC energies. The measured relative suppression of the excited bottomonium states as compared to their ground state allows us to constrain the scattering cross sections between the bottomonia and the comovers created during the collisions. Our result hints at a similar energy distribution of these comovers in the environment created by proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions. Along the way of our study, we also update our knowledge for the bottomonium feed-down pattern and our comprehension on the modification of the nuclear parton distribution functions in proton-nucleus collisions. We present our results for $p$Pb collisions at 5.02 and 8.16 TeV and for PbPb collisions at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV.

# Global description of bottomonium suppression in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at LHC energies

## Key claim
The relative suppression of excited bottomonium states compared to the ground state in both proton-lead and lead-lead collisions at LHC energies can be explained by a single interaction with comoving particles, using a common effective temperature parameter. The model suggests a similar energy distribution of these comovers in the environment created by both collision systems, though the nature of the comovers (partons or hadrons) remains ambiguous.

## Method
The Improved Comover Interaction Model (ICIM) evolves the density of each bottomonium state via a rate equation that depends on the comoving particle density and an energy-averaged scattering cross section. The cross section is parametrized with two free parameters: an effective temperature T_eff for the Bose-Einstein energy distribution of comovers, and an exponent n controlling how quickly the cross section approaches the geometrical cross section. The model includes feed-down contributions from higher bottomonium states and chi_b decays, and is fit to CMS and ATLAS data on relative nuclear suppression factors for Upsilon(2S)/Upsilon(1S) and Upsilon(3S)/Upsilon(1S) in pPb collisions at 5.02 TeV and PbPb collisions at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers
- Fitted T_eff values for all combinations of pion or gluon comovers and n from 0.5 to 2 fall in the range 200 to 300 MeV.
- For n=1 and T_eff = 250 +/- 50 MeV, the model gives: in pPb at 5.02 TeV, Upsilon(2S)/Upsilon(1S) = 0.91 +/- 0.03 (CMS data: 0.83 +/- 0.05 stat. +/- 0.05 syst.) and Upsilon(3S)/Upsilon(1S) = 0.72 +/- 0.02 (CMS data: 0.71 +/- 0.08 stat. +/- 0.09 syst.).
- For pPb at 5.02 TeV with ATLAS acceptance, Upsilon(2S)/Upsilon(1S) = 0.90 +/- 0.03 (ATLAS data: 0.76 +/- 0.07 stat. +/- 0.05 syst.) and Upsilon(3S)/Upsilon(1S) = 0.71 +/- 0.02 (ATLAS data: 0.64 +/- 0.14 stat. +/- 0.06 syst.).
- Feed-down fractions used: Upsilon(1S) receives 70% direct, 8% from Upsilon(2S), 1% from Upsilon(3S), 15% from chi_b1, 5% from chi_b2, 1% from chi_b3; Upsilon(2S) receives 63% direct, 4% from Upsilon(3S), 30% from chi_b2, 3% from chi_b3; Upsilon(3S) receives 40% from chi_b3 decays.

## Context
This work extends the comover interaction model previously applied to charmonium by Ferreiro (2012, 2014, 2018) to the bottomonium family at LHC energies. It addresses the puzzle that sequential suppression of bottomonium states, originally proposed by Matsui and Satz (1986) as a quark-gluon plasma signature, was also observed by CMS in proton-lead collisions (2013) where no QGP is expected. The model enables predictions for absolute suppression of each Upsilon state when combined with nuclear parton distribution function shadowing from nCTEQ15 or EPS09LO, and provides predictions for pPb collisions at 8.16 TeV that can be tested against LHCb data.
