# Formation and Collapse of False Vacuum Bubbles in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> It is possible that under certain situations, in a relativistic heavy-ion collision, partons may expand out forming a shell like structure. We analyze the process of hadronization in such a picture for the case when the quark-hadron transition is of first order, and argue that the inside region of such a shell must correspond to a supercooled (to $T = 0$) deconfined vacuum. Hadrons from that region escape out, leaving a bubble of pure deconfined vacuum with large vacuum energy. This bubble undergoes relativistic collapse, with highly Lorentz contracted bubble walls, and may concentrate the entire energy into extremely small regions. Eventually different portions of bubble wall collide, with the energy being released in the form of particle production. Thermalization of this system can lead to very high temperatures. With a reasonably conservative set of parameters, at LHC, the temperature of the hot spot can reach as high as 3 GeV, and well above it with more optimistic parameters. Such a hot spot can leave signals like large $P_T$ partons, dileptons, and enhanced production of heavy quarks. We also briefly discuss a speculative possibility where the electroweak symmetry may get restored in the highly dense region resulting from the decay of the bubble wall via the phenomenon of non-thermal symmetry restoration (which is usually employed in models of pre-heating after inflation). If that could happen then the possibility may arise of observing sphaleron induced baryon number violation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors argue that under specific conditions in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, a shell-like expansion of partons can lead to the formation of a pure false vacuum bubble of deconfined vacuum (supercooled to T = 0) which undergoes relativistic collapse, potentially producing extremely hot spots with temperatures reaching several GeV, but this scenario depends critically on the quark-hadron transition being first order and on the parton system forming a shell structure before hadronization completes.

## Method

The authors model the expansion of a parton system formed in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, assuming the deconfinement-confinement transition is first order with a barrier surviving to zero temperature. They use a color dielectric field effective potential with parameters from the bag model (B^(1/4) = 122.3 MeV, m_gb = 978.6 MeV) to compute the surface tension and false vacuum energy density of the interface. The bubble wall profile is obtained by solving the Euclidean equation of motion for quantum nucleation (kappa = 3) and inverting the solution to represent a false vacuum bubble of deconfined vacuum. The total energy available for bubble formation is estimated from scaling relations for initial energy density as a function of nuclear mass number A and center-of-mass energy sqrt(s).

## Key numbers

- For LHC with A = 208 and sqrt(s) = 5.5 TeV, initial energy density epsilon_i = 1.3 TeV/fm^3
- Total energy E_tot for LHC: approximately 25 TeV for Delta z_i = 0.15 fm, and 175 TeV for Delta z_i = 1 fm
- Total energy E_tot for RHIC with sqrt(s) = 200 GeV: approximately 3 TeV for Delta z_i = 0.22 fm, and 10 TeV for Delta z_i = 1 fm
- Surface tension sigma = 64.8 MeV/fm^2 and false vacuum energy density rho = 27.8 MeV/fm^3 (approximately (122 MeV)^4) from the bag model parameters
- The action for quantum nucleation of a true vacuum bubble is about 100 in natural units, suppressing bubble nucleation
- The radius of the T = 0 quantum bubble of true vacuum is about 5 fm; the finite temperature bubble radius is about 3.8 fm
- With conservative parameters at LHC, the temperature of the hot spot can reach as high as 3 GeV

## Context

This work extends the "baked Alaska" model for disoriented chiral condensates proposed by Bjorken et al. and the shell structure discussions by the same authors, but applies it to a first-order confinement-deconfinement transition rather than a second-order chiral transition. The formation of false vacuum bubbles by coalescence of true vacuum bubbles was previously discussed by Witten in the context of the quark-hadron transition in the early universe, but the key difference is that the present model produces a pure false vacuum bubble with no quark-gluon plasma inside, enabling dissipationless relativistic collapse. The paper opens the possibility of observing extremely hot spots with signals such as large transverse momentum partons, dileptons, enhanced heavy quark production, and speculatively, electroweak symmetry restoration and sphaleron-induced baryon number violation in heavy-ion collisions at LHC.
