# Estimation of the ratio of the pn -> pn pi0 pi0/pn -> d pi0 pi0 cross sections

> Evidence has recently been presented for the existence of a dibaryon of mass 2380 MeV/c^2 and width 70 MeV/c^2, which decays strongly into the d pi0 pi0 channel [M. Bashkanov et al., Phys.Rev.Lett. 102 (2009) 052301; P. Adlarson et al., arXiv:1104.0123]. The decay rate of such a hypothesised dibaryon into the {pn}_{I=0} pi0 pi0 channel is estimated in a weakly model-dependent way by using final state interaction theory. It is shown that, if the resonance exists, it should then show up as strongly in this channel as in d pi0 pi0. The sum of the two decay modes would saturate most of the inelasticity predicted in the relevant partial waves in the 2380 MeV/c^2 region.

## Key claim

If the dibaryon resonance at 2380 MeV/c² exists, its decay into the {pn}_{I=0} π⁰π⁰ channel should be as strong as its decay into dπ⁰π⁰, with the sum of the two modes saturating most of the predicted inelasticity in the relevant partial waves. The authors use final-state interaction theory to estimate this ratio in a weakly model-dependent way, but the result depends on whether the decay angular momentum is L=0 or L=2.

## Method

The authors apply a previously established relation between the deuteron bound-state wave function and the spin-triplet S-wave proton-neutron scattering state at low relative energy, which connects the squared matrix elements for production of dX and {pn}_qX final states for short-range production operators. They integrate this relation over relativistic phase space for the two-pion final state, treating separately the cases of L=0 and L=2 angular momentum between the deuteron (or pn pair) and the dipion, and including an ABC-like distortion of the π⁰π⁰ invariant mass spectrum.

## Key numbers

- Dibaryon mass: 2380 MeV/c², width: 70 MeV/c² (from WASA collaboration, Bashkanov et al. 2009 and Adlarson et al. 2011)
- Peak excess energy Q ≈ 234 MeV (W = 2.38 GeV, with Q = W - (m_d + 2m_π⁰)c²)
- Deuteron binding energy B = 2.225 MeV (used in the dimensionless variable x = Q/B)
- The predicted cross section ratio R_L(Q) = σ(pn → {pn}_{I=0}π⁰π⁰) / σ(pn → dπ⁰π⁰) at the peak Q ≈ 234 MeV is of order unity for both L=0 and L=2, with L=0 giving a larger ratio than L=2
- The ABC-like distortion of the π⁰π⁰ spectrum changes predictions by typically 10%
- The inelastic cross sections from SAID partial wave analysis are scaled by an isospin factor of 1/6 for comparison

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous final-state interaction approach used for single-meson production in pn → {pn}_qη/dη (Faldt and Wilkin 2001) and pp → {pn}_qπ⁺/dπ⁺ (Faldt and Wilkin 1997) to the two-pion production case studied by the WASA collaboration (Bashkanov et al. 2009, Adlarson et al. 2011). The analysis enables experimental searches for the dibaryon signal in the pnπ⁰π⁰ channel using existing WASA data, and favors the J^p = 3⁺ assignment (L=2 decay) over J^p = 1⁺ (L=0 decay) based on consistency with SAID partial wave predictions.
