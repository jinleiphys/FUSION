# Deformation of C isotopes

> Systematic analysis of the deformations of proton and neutron densities in even-even C isotopes was done based on the method of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. The $E2$ transition strength was discussed in relation to the deformation. We analyze the $B(E2;2^+_1\to 0^+_1)$ in $^{16}$C, which has been recently measured to be abnormally small. The results suggest the difference of the deformations between proton and neutron densities in the neutron-rich C isotopes. It was found that stable proton structure in C isotopes plays an important role in the enhancement the neutron skin structure as well as in the systematics of $B(E2)$ in the neutron-rich C.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that the abnormally small $B(E2;2^+_1\to 0^+_1)$ in $^{16}$C, recently measured as 0.63 e$^2$fm$^4$, can be explained by a difference between the oblate proton shape and the prolate neutron shape in that nucleus, rather than by a spherical or uniformly deformed structure.

## Method

The authors performed antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations for even-even C isotopes ($^{10}$C through $^{20}$C) using a variation-before-projection (VBP) method with parity projection, followed by total-angular-momentum projection to obtain $0^+_1$ and $2^+_1$ states. For $^{16}$C, they also performed variation-after-projection (VAP) calculations to improve the level spacing. The effective interactions used were the MV1 central force with a Majorana parameter $m=0.576$ and a G3RS spin-orbit force with strengths $u_{ls}=900$ or 1500 MeV.

## Key numbers

* $B(E2;2^+_1\rightarrow 0^+_1)$ in $^{16}$C: experimental value 0.63 e$^2$fm$^4$; VBP calculation with $u_{ls}=1500$ MeV gives 1.4 e$^2$fm$^4$, VAP with same interaction gives 3.7 e$^2$fm$^4$.
* Excitation energy $E_x(2^+_1)$ in $^{16}$C: experimental value 1.766 MeV; VBP calculation with $u_{ls}=1500$ MeV gives 0.65 MeV.
* $B(E2;2^+_1\rightarrow 0^+_1)$ in $^{18}$C: VBP calculation with $u_{ls}=1500$ MeV gives 0.6 e$^2$fm$^4$, predicted to be as small as in $^{16}$C.
* $B(E2;2^+_1\rightarrow 0^+_1)$ in $^{20}$C: VBP calculation with $u_{ls}=1500$ MeV gives 5.0 e$^2$fm$^4$, not as small as in $^{16}$C and $^{18}$C.
* Experimental $B(E2;2^+_1\rightarrow 0^+_1)$ for other C isotopes: $^{10}$C: 12.4$\pm$0.2 e$^2$fm$^4$; $^{12}$C: 8.2$\pm$0.1 e$^2$fm$^4$; $^{14}$C: 3.74$\pm$0.5 e$^2$fm$^4$.
* Width parameter $\nu$ used in AMD wave functions: 0.185 for $^{10}$C, 0.190 for $^{12}$C, 0.180 for $^{14}$C, 0.175 for $^{16}$C, 0.170 for $^{18}$C, 0.165 for $^{20}$C.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous AMD study of proton-rich C isotopes (Kanada-En'yo, 2003) which suggested shape differences between proton and neutron densities could suppress $B(E2)$. It directly addresses the recent measurement of an abnormally small $B(E2)$ in $^{16}$C by Imai et al. (2004) and the neutron-to-proton transition matrix element ratio from $^{208}$Pb+$^{16}$C inelastic scattering by Elekes et al. (2004). The results contradict mean-field HF+BCS calculations by Tajima et al. (1996) which predicted spherical shapes for $^{12}$C and $^{14}$C, and they enable predictions for $B(E2)$ in $^{18}$C and $^{20}$C as well as the existence of a side-band $K^\pi=2^+$ state in $^{16}$C that could be tested experimentally.
