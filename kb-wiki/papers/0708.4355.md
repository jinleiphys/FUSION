# Quantum tunneling in ^{277}112 and its alpha-decay chain

> The $α$-decay half lives of nuclei in the decay from element $^{277}112$ are calculated in a WKB framework using DDM3Y interaction and experimental Q-values. Theoretical estimation of half lives in the same quantum tunneling model, using Q-values from the mass formula of Muntian-Hofmann-Patyk-Sobiczewski, are also presented. Calculated results furnish corroborating evidence for the experimental findings at RIKEN and GSI. Certain discrepancies indicate necessity of a better mass formula. Further experimental data with higher statistics would also be useful.

## Key claim

The WKB framework with DDM3Y interaction can reproduce the experimental alpha-decay half lives of the chain from $^{277}112$ when experimental Q-values are used, but the theoretical Q-values from the Muntian-Hofmann-Patyk-Sobiczewski mass formula show systematic discrepancies (over- or under-prediction) that indicate the mass formula needs improvement and that non-zero angular momentum transfer may be required for the first decay.

## Method

Alpha-decay half lives for the chain from $^{277}112$ were calculated using the WKB barrier penetration formalism with microscopic double-folded nuclear potentials obtained from the DDM3Y effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. The calculations used both experimental Q-values (derived from measured alpha-particle kinetic energies with recoil and electron shielding corrections) and theoretical Q-values from the mass formula of Muntian-Hofmann-Patyk-Sobiczewski, assuming zero orbital angular momentum transfer unless otherwise noted.

## Key numbers

- For $^{277}112$ ($\alpha_1$): experimental mean half life $0.69^{+0.69}_{-0.23}$ ms; calculated with $Q^{ex}$ values range from $0.06^{+0.01}_{-0.01}$ ms (Chain 3) to $0.43^{+0.19}_{-0.13}$ ms (Chain 1); calculated with $Q^M = 12.11$ MeV gives $T^{Q^M}_{1/2} = 0.01$ ms
- For $^{273}110$ ($\alpha_2$): experimental mean half life $0.17^{+0.17}_{-0.06}$ ms; $Q^M = 11.1$ MeV gives $T^{Q^M}_{1/2} = 0.30$ ms
- For $^{269}108$ ($\alpha_3$): experimental mean half life $9.7^{+9.7}_{-3.2}$ s; $Q^M = 9.14$ MeV gives $T^{Q^M}_{1/2} = 10.7$ s
- For $^{265}106$ ($\alpha_4$): experimental mean half life $22^{+22}_{-8}$ s; $Q^M = 8.63$ MeV gives $T^{Q^M}_{1/2} = 83.8$ s; using $Q^{ex}=8.888(40)$ MeV (Chain 1) gives $T^{Q^{ex}}_{1/2}=12.59^{+4.32}_{-3.52}$ s
- For $^{261}Rf$ ($\alpha_5$): $Q^M = 8.53$ MeV gives $T^{Q^M}_{1/2} = 31.7$ s; using $Q^{ex}=8.696(21)$ MeV gives $T^{Q^{ex}}_{1/2}=9.34^{+1.50}_{-1.29}$ s
- For $^{257}No$ ($\alpha_6$): $Q^M = 8.19$ MeV gives $T^{Q^M}_{1/2} = 78.0$ s; using $Q^{ex}=8.514(21)$ MeV gives $T^{Q^{ex}}_{1/2}=6.41^{+1.08}_{-0.89}$ s
- For $^{277}112$ with non-zero angular momentum: $T_{1/2}^{Q^M}=0.01$ ms for $l=0$, $0.34$ ms for $l=6$, and $1.23$ ms for $l=7$

## Context

This work provides theoretical corroboration for the experimental alpha-decay chains of $^{277}112$ observed at RIKEN (Morita et al., 2007) and GSI (Hofmann et al., 1996, 2000, 2002), extending earlier WKB-DDM3Y calculations by Samanta et al. (2007) and Chowdhury et al. (2006, 2007) for nuclei with Z=102-112. The results highlight the extreme sensitivity of alpha-decay half lives to Q-values (approximately 0.2 MeV variation changes half lives by factors of 4-7) and suggest that the Muntian-Hofmann-Patyk-Sobiczewski mass formula requires improvement, while also calling for higher-statistics experimental data to resolve remaining discrepancies.
