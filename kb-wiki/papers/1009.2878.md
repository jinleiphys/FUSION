# Deducing spectroscopic factors from wave-function asymptotics

> In a coupled-channel model, we explore the effects of coupling between configurations on the radial behavior of the wave function and, in particular, on the spectroscopic factor (SF) and the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC). We evaluate the extraction of a SF from the ratio of the ANC of the coupled-channel model to that of a single-particle approximation of the wave function. We perform this study within a core + n collective model, which includes two states of the core that connect by a rotational coupling. To get additional insights, we also use a simplified model that takes a delta function for the coupling potential. Calculations are performed for 11Be. Fair agreement is obtained between the SF inferred from the single-particle approximation and the one obtained within the coupled-channel models. Significant discrepancies are observed only for large coupling strength and/or large admixture, that is, a small SF. This suggests that reliable SFs can be deduced from the wave-function asymptotics when the structure is dominated by one configuration, that is, for a large SF.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that spectroscopic factors (SFs) can be reliably deduced from the ratio of the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of a coupled-channel model to that of a single-particle approximation, but only when the nuclear structure is dominated by one configuration (i.e., for a large SF); significant discrepancies arise only for large coupling strength or large admixture, corresponding to a small SF.

## Method

The authors used a core + n collective rotational model for 11Be, treating the 10Be core as a deformed rotor with its 0+ ground state and 2+ excited state coupled via rotational coupling. They solved coupled-channel equations to obtain overlap functions, SFs, and ANCs, then compared the "exact" SF from the coupled-channel model to the SF inferred from the ratio of the coupled-channel ANC to the single-particle ANC. To gain qualitative insight, they also employed a simplified schematic model with square-well potentials and a delta-function coupling between two channels.

## Key numbers

- The 1/2+ ground state of 11Be has a binding energy of ε^{Bn}_{1/2+} = -504 keV.
- The 1/2- first excited state of 11Be has a binding energy of ε^{Bn}_{1/2-} = -184 keV.
- The 5/2+ resonance in 11Be is located at ε^{Bn}_{5/2+} = 1.274 MeV in the continuum.
- The 2+ excited state of 10Be has an excitation energy of ε_{2+} = 3.368 MeV.
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters used: radius R0 = 2.585 fm, diffuseness a = 0.6 fm, spin-orbit depth V_SO = 21.0 MeV fm^2, spin-orbit radius R_SO = 2.585 fm.
- The quadrupole deformation parameter β = 0.67 is the physical value for 10Be, but was treated as a variable parameter in the study.

## Context

This work extends the rotational-coupling model developed by Nunes et al. (1996) for one-neutron halo nuclei, applying it to 11Be as the archetypal case. It addresses the controversy about the use of spectroscopic factors in nuclear structure (see Fortune and Sherr, 2010; Furnstahl and Schwenk, 2010) and the suggestion by Mukhamedzhanov and Nunes (2005) and Capel and Nunes (2007) that direct reactions probe only the ANC of the overlap function. The results enable a better understanding of how reliably SFs can be extracted from ANC measurements in transfer or breakup reactions, particularly for loosely-bound nuclei far from stability.
