# Coarse grained short-range correlations

> We develop a scheme to take into account the effects of short-range nucleon-nucleon correlations in the nucleon-pair wave function by solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation for a coarse grained delta shell potential in S-wave configuration. The S-wave delta shell potential has been adjusted to reproduce the $^1$S$_0$ phase shifts of the AV18 potential for this partial wave up to 2 GeV in the laboratory kinetic energy. We show that a coarse grained potential can describe the high momentum tail of the back-to-back correlated pairs and the $G$-matrix in momentum space. We discuss the easiness and robustness of the calculation in coordinate space and the future improvements and utilities of this model. This work suggests the possibility of using perturbation theory for describing the short-range correlations, and related to this, to substitute the $G$-matrix by an appropriate coarse-grained potential.

## Key claim

The authors show that a coarse-grained delta-shell potential, fitted to reproduce the $^1$S$_0$ phase shifts of the AV18 potential up to 2 GeV in laboratory kinetic energy, can describe the high-momentum tail of back-to-back correlated nucleon pairs and the $G$-matrix in momentum space, suggesting that perturbation theory could substitute the $G$-matrix by an appropriate coarse-grained potential.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Goldstone equation in coordinate space for a coarse-grained delta-shell potential in the $^1$S$_0$ partial wave, restricting to the back-to-back configuration (zero center-of-mass momentum) to avoid complications from the Pauli blocking operator. The delta-shell potential parameters were fitted to reproduce the AV18 phase shifts up to 2 GeV laboratory kinetic energy using a chi-squared minimization. The solution was obtained iteratively, requiring less than six iterations for convergence, and the high-momentum components of the correlated pair wave function were computed analytically from the resulting wave function values at the delta-shell positions.

## Key numbers

- The delta-shell potential was fitted to reproduce $^1$S$_0$ phase shifts of the AV18 potential up to 2 GeV in laboratory kinetic energy.
- The coarse-grained potential required N ~ a/Delta r = a * p_CM^max points, where a is the range of the interaction and Delta r is the resolution wavelength.
- Convergence of the iterative Bethe-Goldstone solution required less than six iterations for all NN potentials considered.
- The large-distance boundary condition was set at r_max = 50 to 100 fm, with results independent of this choice.

## Context

This work extends the coarse-graining approach previously developed for NN scattering analysis (Aviles 1973, Navarro Perez 2011-2014) to the in-medium problem of short-range correlations. The method provides a simpler alternative to solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation with realistic potentials like AV18 (Wiringa 1995), which requires a large number of mesh points for fine-grained solutions. The approach enables future studies of short-range correlations using perturbation theory and could be extended beyond S-wave configurations and the back-to-back kinematic limit.
