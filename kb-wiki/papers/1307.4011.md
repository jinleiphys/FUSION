# Neutrino pair emission from thermally excited nuclei in stellar collapse

> We examine the rate of neutrino-antineutrino pair emission by hot nuclei in collapsing stellar cores. The rates are calculated assuming that only allowed charge-neutral Gamow-Teller (GT$_0$) transitions contribute to the decay of thermally excited nuclear states. To obtain the GT$_0$ transition matrix elements, we employ the quasiparticle random phase approximation extended to finite temperatures within the thermo field dynamics formalism. The decay rates and the energy emission rates are calculated for the sample nuclei ${}^{56}$Fe and $^{82}$Ge at temperatures relevant to core collapse supernovae.

# Neutrino pair emission from thermally excited nuclei in stellar collapse

## Key claim

The rate of neutrino-antineutrino pair emission from hot nuclei in collapsing stellar cores is calculated assuming that only allowed charge-neutral Gamow-Teller (GT₀) transitions contribute, and the results show that for temperatures above 1 MeV the emission is dominated by spin-orbit transitions in the f, g, and h shells rather than the p-shell transitions important at lower temperatures. The authors note that for temperatures above 2.0 MeV, the contribution of first-forbidden high-energy transitions is not negligible and should be taken into account, meaning the presented results are incomplete at the highest temperatures.

## Method

The authors employ the quasiparticle random phase approximation extended to finite temperatures within the thermo field dynamics formalism (TQRPA) to calculate GT₀ transition matrix elements for thermally excited nuclei. The thermal Hamiltonian is diagonalized in two steps: first, a finite-temperature BCS treatment of the single-particle and pairing terms, then inclusion of particle-hole correlations via thermal phonon operators. The total decay rate and energy emission rate are obtained by summing Boltzmann-weighted partial decay rates from the thermal vacuum to tilde one-phonon states for the sample nuclei ⁵⁶Fe and ⁸²Ge at temperatures between 0.5 and 2.5 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The partial decay rate formula includes a factor λ₀ ≈ 1.72 × 10⁻⁴ s⁻¹ MeV⁻⁵, multiplied by the fifth power of the transition energy and the GT₀ strength.
- Calculations are performed for temperatures in the range 0.5 ≤ T ≤ 2.5 MeV, where 0.86 MeV ≈ 10¹⁰ K = 10 GK.
- The critical temperature for pairing correlations is T_cr ≈ 0.8 MeV, above which pairing vanishes.
- At low temperatures (T = 0.5 MeV), the GT₀ transition strength is concentrated in a narrow resonance between 2 and 3 MeV, corresponding to the spin-orbit splitting of the 2p shell.
- The mean energy of emitted neutrino pairs at low T is less than 9 MeV, rising rapidly until T ≈ 1.0 MeV, after which it becomes nearly independent of temperature.
- Using the Fermi gas model, the average nuclear excitation energy for iron-group nuclei (A ≈ 50-60) at T ≈ 2 MeV is approximately 30 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Bahcall et al. (1974), who considered neutrino-pair emission at low temperatures using experimental M1 gamma-decay rates, and by Crawford et al. (1976), who parameterized rates for temperatures up to T₁₀ = 0.6 using statistical level densities. The results are close to those obtained by Fuller and Meyer (1991) using the independent single-particle shell model, and the authors note that their calculated energy emission rate for ⁵⁶Fe agrees with that work. The approach enables future extension to higher temperatures by including first-forbidden transitions, which the authors identify as the subject of subsequent studies.
