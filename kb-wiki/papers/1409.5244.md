# Applications of Neural Networks in Hadron Physics

> The Bayesian approach for the feed-forward neural networks is reviewed. Its potential for usage in hadron physics is discussed. As an example of the application the study of the the two-photon exchange effect is presented. We focus on the model comparison, the estimation of the systematic uncertainties due to the choice of the model, and the over-fitting. As an illustration the predictions of the cross sections ratio $d σ(e^+ p\to e^+ p)/d σ(e^- p\to e^- p)$ are given together with the estimate of the uncertainty due to the parametrization choice.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Bayesian framework for feed-forward neural networks can quantitatively estimate systematic uncertainties due to model choice in hadron physics, specifically for the two-photon exchange correction and proton form factors, but the analysis relies on the assumption that the TPE correction to polarization transfer data is negligible, which is a model-assumption that is difficult to quantitatively account for.

## Method

The authors performed a combined Bayesian analysis of three types of elastic lepton-proton scattering data: unpolarized cross sections (27 independent data sets), polarization transfer measurements of the G_E/G_M ratio, and the R_+/- cross section ratio for positron versus electron scattering. They constructed multi-layer perceptron neural networks with two inputs (Q^2 and epsilon) and three outputs (G_E, G_M, and the TPE correction Delta-tilde-C_2gamma), with the hidden layer divided into a form-factor sector connected only to the Q^2 input and a TPE sector. For each of 102 different network architectures (up to 14 hidden units total), approximately 2300 learning trials were performed using the Levenberg-Marquardt algorithm, totaling about 226,000 networks, and models were ranked by the Bayesian evidence computed in the Hessian approximation.

## Key numbers

* The model with the highest evidence contained 6 hidden units: 2 in the form-factor sector and 4 in the TPE sector.
* The optimal model had ln(evidence) = -547, while an overfitted model minimizing the error function had ln(evidence) = -646.
* Predictions for R_+/- were shown at three kinematic points: (Q^2 = 4 GeV^2, epsilon = 0.10), (Q^2 = 1.43 GeV^2, epsilon = 0.50), and (Q^2 = 0.23 GeV^2, epsilon = 0.95), with the latter two corresponding to measurements by Gramolin et al. (2011).
* The maximum number of hidden units considered was 14, and 102 different MLP schemes were evaluated.

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian neural network approach previously applied by the same authors to nucleon form factors (Graczyk 2010, 2011) and addresses the long-standing discrepancy between Rosenbluth separation and polarization transfer measurements of the proton form factor ratio G_E/G_M, which Arrington (2007) and others attributed to two-photon exchange corrections. The method enables model-independent extraction of the TPE correction directly from scattering data and provides a quantitative way to estimate systematic uncertainties due to parametrization choice, which is relevant for ongoing measurements of the R_+/- ratio by Gramolin (2011) and Bennett (2012).
