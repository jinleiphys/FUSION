# Dispersion Effects in Nucleon Polarisabilities

> We present a formalism to extract the dynamical nucleon polarisabilities defined via a multipole expansion of the structure amplitudes in nucleon Compton scattering. In contradistinction to the static polarisabilities, dynamical polarisabilities gauge the response of the internal degrees of freedom of a composed object to an external, real photon field of arbitrary energy. Being energy dependent, they therefore contain additional information about dispersive effects induced by internal relaxation mechanisms, baryonic resonances and meson production thresholds of the nucleon. We give explicit formulae to extract the dynamical electric and magnetic dipole as well as quadrupole polarisabilities from low energy nucleon Compton scattering up to the one pion production threshold and discuss the connection to the definition of static nucleon polarisabilities. As a concrete example, we examine the results of leading order Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory for the four leading spin independent iso-scalar polarisabilities of the nucleon. Finally, we consider the possible r{ô}le of energy dependent effects in low energy extractions of the iso-scalar dipole polarisabilities from Compton scattering on the deuteron.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the energy dependence of nucleon polarisabilities (dynamical polarisabilities) is not merely a correction to be subsumed into dispersion integrals, but contains intrinsic physical information about internal relaxation mechanisms, baryonic resonances, and meson production thresholds; the formalism is presented as valid for extracting these dynamical polarisabilities from Compton scattering amplitudes up to the one pion production threshold.

## Method

The authors define dynamical electric and magnetic polarisabilities via a multipole expansion of the pole-subtracted Compton structure amplitudes \(\bar{A}_1\) and \(\bar{A}_2\) in the centre-of-mass frame, truncating at angular momentum \(l=3\) to extract the dipole and quadrupole terms. They derive explicit matching formulae that eliminate dependence on octupole polarisabilities, allowing extraction of \(\alpha_{E1}(\omega)\), \(\beta_{M1}(\omega)\), \(\alpha_{E2}(\omega)\), and \(\beta_{M2}(\omega)\) from any theoretical model of nucleon Compton scattering. As a concrete example, they apply the formalism to leading order Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HB\(\chi\)PT) at \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) to compute the iso-scalar dynamical polarisabilities.

## Key numbers

*   Static proton electric polarisability from global analysis: \(\bar{\alpha}_p = (11.9 \pm 0.5 \mp 0.5) \times 10^{-4}\;\mathrm{fm}^3\).
*   Static proton magnetic polarisability from global analysis: \(\bar{\beta}_p = (1.5 \pm 0.6 \pm 0.2) \times 10^{-4}\;\mathrm{fm}^3\).
*   Static neutron electric polarisability range: \(\bar{\alpha}_n = [0 \dots 16.6] \times 10^{-4}\;\mathrm{fm}^3\).
*   Static neutron magnetic polarisability range: \(\bar{\beta}_n = [1.2 \dots 14] \times 10^{-4}\;\mathrm{fm}^3\).
*   The formalism is designed for photon energies up to the one pion production threshold (approximately \(m_\pi \approx 140\;\mathrm{MeV}\)).
*   The leading order HB\(\chi\)PT calculation is performed at \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) and yields the four leading spin-independent iso-scalar dynamical polarisabilities.

## Context

This work extends the concept of static nucleon polarisabilities (defined at zero photon energy) to energy-dependent dynamical polarisabilities, building on the multipole expansion formalism of Ritus (Ritus, Ritus2, Ritus3) and the static definitions of Babusci et al. (babusci). It provides a framework to analyse dispersive effects in Compton scattering that are typically handled via subtracted dispersion relations (as in the work of L'vov (lvov) and Drechsel (drechsel)), enabling future studies of the temporal response of the nucleon's internal degrees of freedom and the role of the pion cloud, baryonic resonances, and production thresholds.
