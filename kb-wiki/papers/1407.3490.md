# Electromagnetic reactions on light nuclei

> Electromagnetic reactions on light nuclei are fundamental to advance our understanding of nuclear structure and dynamics. The perturbative nature of the electromagnetic probes allows to clearly connect measured cross sections with the calculated structure properties of nuclear targets. We present an overview on recent theoretical ab-initio calculations of electron-scattering and photonuclear reactions involving light nuclei. We encompass both the conventional approach and the novel theoretical framework provided by chiral effective field theories. Because both strong and electromagnetic interactions are involved in the processes under study, comparison with available experimental data provides stringent constraints on both many-body nuclear Hamiltonians and electromagnetic currents. We discuss what we have learned from studies on electromagnetic observables of light nuclei, starting from the deuteron and reaching up to nuclear systems with mass number A=16.

# Electromagnetic reactions on light nuclei

## Key claim

This review argues that ab-initio calculations of electromagnetic reactions on light nuclei (A ≤ 16), using both conventional phenomenological potentials and chiral effective field theory frameworks, provide stringent constraints on nuclear Hamiltonians and electromagnetic currents when compared with experimental data, but the transverse components of meson-exchange currents remain model-dependent because the continuity equation does not uniquely constrain them.

## Method

The authors review non-relativistic ab-initio calculations of electron scattering and photonuclear reactions on light nuclei, where nuclear wave functions are obtained by solving the Schrödinger equation with Hamiltonians that include nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) potentials. Electromagnetic currents are expanded in many-body operators, with one-body impulse approximation terms supplemented by two-body meson-exchange currents constructed to satisfy the continuity equation with the nuclear potentials, and the calculations are performed using methods such as Green's function Monte Carlo, hyperspherical harmonics, Faddeev-Yakubovsky, no-core shell model, and coupled-cluster techniques.

## Key numbers

- The fine-structure constant α ~ 1/137 characterizes the perturbative nature of electromagnetic probes, allowing Born approximation to be safely used for light nuclei
- The pion mass m_π ~ 140 MeV sets the energy threshold below which the review's processes occur (ω < m_π)
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale Λ_χ ~ 1 GeV characterizes the convergence of the chiral effective field theory expansion in powers of Q/Λ_χ
- The nucleon axial coupling constant g_A ≃ 1.27 and pion decay amplitude F_π ≃ 186 MeV define the πNN coupling in the one-pion exchange potential
- In Green's function Monte Carlo calculations of A=3-8 nuclei ground-state energies, the 3N potential contribution is up to ~8% of that due to NN potentials, but due to cancellations between kinetic and NN terms, 3N forces can provide up to ~30% of the total calculated energy
- In the 2013 Lovato et al. calculation of ¹²C, meson-exchange current contributions to the transverse sum rule increase the impulse approximation results by up to ~50%
- The average nucleon velocity in A=8 nuclei is of order ~0.2, motivating the non-relativistic description
- The isoscalar and isovector magnetic form factors at Q²=0 are normalized as G^S_M(0)=0.880 μ_N and G^V_M(0)=4.706 μ_N in units of the nuclear magneton

## Context

This review extends the comprehensive 1998 review by Carlson and Schiavilla on electromagnetic reactions in light nuclei, covering developments in the subsequent 15 years including chiral effective field theory formulations of nuclear forces and currents. The work enables future validation of theoretical frameworks for weak interaction processes, such as neutrino-nucleus scattering, where the authors note that understanding electromagnetic reactions is a necessary prerequisite, citing the observed anomaly in quasi-elastic neutrino scattering off ¹²C reported by Aguilar (2008) as a motivating example. The review discusses both the conventional approach (using potentials like Argonne-v18 with Urbana IX or Illinois-7 three-nucleon forces) and chiral EFT approaches (with potentials up to N3LO from groups including Epelbaum, Machleidt, and collaborators, and currents from the JLab-Pisa and Bochum-Bonn groups).
