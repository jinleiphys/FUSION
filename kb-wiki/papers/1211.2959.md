# Universality in tightly bound 3-boson systems

> The effects of two distinct operations of the elements of the symmetry groups of a Hamiltonian on a quantum state might be equivalent in some specific zones of coordinate space. Making use of the matrix representations of the groups, the equivalence leads to a set of homogeneous linear equations imposing on the wave functions. When the matrix of the equations is non-degenerate, the wave functions will appear as nodal surfaces in these zones. Therefore, the equivalence leads to the existence of inherent nodal structure in the quantum states. In this paper, trapped 3-boson systems with different types of interactions are studied. The structures of the tightly bound eigenstates have been analyzed systematically. The emphasis is placed to demonstrate the universality arising from the common inherent nodal structures.

## Key claim

The paper claims that tightly bound 3-boson systems exhibit a universal classification scheme based on the accessibility of specific geometric configurations (regular triangles, isosceles triangles, and collinear geometries) that arises from symmetry constraints, not from the details of the interaction. The hedge is that this universality holds for the tightly bound regime (as opposed to the weakly bound Efimov regime) and is demonstrated for three arbitrarily chosen interaction types, but the authors note that when the hard-core potential V_C is used (which contains no minimum), the pursuit of favorable geometry is "less anxious" and some features become less pronounced.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the Hamiltonian of three identical bosons in an isotropic harmonic trap using a basis of symmetrized harmonic oscillator functions constructed from Jacobi coordinates, with a variational parameter gamma to optimize convergence. Three different two-body interactions are used: V_A (short-ranged, previously used for alpha particles), V_B (Van der Waals type for atoms), and V_C (a hard-core potential). The eigenstates are analyzed by computing spectra, root-mean-square radii, weights of Q-components (projection of total orbital angular momentum L on the body-frame axis), one-body densities, and shape-densities in the hyper-radius and deformation-angle coordinates.

## Key numbers

- For the 0⁺ state with interaction V_A, the converged energy is 2.06557 ħω (with N_max=20 basis functions), showing convergence to five significant figures.
- The weight W₃ for the 3⁻ state is 0.984 (V_A), 0.991 (V_B), and 0.972 (V_C), demonstrating near-identical Q-component dominance across interactions.
- The one-body density of 4⁻ peaks at θ₃ = 63° (V_A), 63° (V_B), and 66° (V_C); the 3⁺ density peaks at 50° (V_A), 49° (V_B), and 49° (V_C).
- The top angles of the isosceles triangle for the RT-inaccessible states are 94° (3⁺), 101° (1⁻), and 105° (2⁻) when using V_A.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of inherent nodal structures in 3-boson systems without a trap (Bao et al., earlier papers) to trapped systems, and adds analysis of isosceles triangle and collinear geometry constraints beyond the regular triangle considered earlier. The paper contrasts this "tightly bound" universality with the well-known Efimov universality in weakly bound 3-body systems (Efimov, 1970). The results enable a universal classification scheme for 3-boson states based purely on symmetry, independent of the specific nuclear, atomic, or molecular system, and the authors briefly extend the analysis to fermion systems to show the same symmetry principles apply.
