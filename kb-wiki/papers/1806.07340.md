# Radiative instability of quantum electrodynamics in chiral matter

> Modification of the photon dispersion relation in chiral matter enables $1\to 2$ scattering. As a result, the single fermion and photon states are unstable to photon radiation and pair production respectively. In particular, a fast fermion moving through chiral matter can spontaneously radiate a photon, while a photon can spontaneously radiate a fast fermion and anti-fermion pair. The corresponding spectra are derived in the ultra-relativistic approximation. It is shown that the polarization of the produced and decayed photons is determined by the sign of the chiral conductivity. Impact of a flat thin domain wall on the spectra is computed.

## Key claim

In chiral matter, the modified photon dispersion relation enables \(1\to 2\) scattering processes that are forbidden in vacuum, making single-fermion and single-photon states unstable to spontaneous radiation and decay; the effect is derived in the ultra-relativistic approximation and depends on the sign of the chiral conductivity, with the caveat that the infrared instability of the electromagnetic field can be ignored only when \(\omega\gg k_\bot\gg |\sigma_\chi|\).

## Method

The author solves the modified Maxwell equations in chiral matter (with Lagrangian including the Chern-Simons term \(-\frac{c_A}{4}\theta\tilde F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu}\)) to obtain the photon wave function in the high-energy approximation, and solves the Dirac equation for the fermion wave function. Using these wave functions, the scattering matrix element for \(f(p)\to f(p')+\gamma(k)\) and for \(\gamma(k)\to \bar f(p)+f(p')\) is computed in the helicity basis, and the spectra are derived by integrating over phase space for the cases of a single infinite chiral domain and for two semi-infinite domains separated by a thin flat domain wall.

## Key numbers

- Photon dispersion relation in chiral matter: \(k^2 = -\lambda\sigma_\chi|\mathbf{k}|\), where \(\lambda\) is photon helicity and \(\sigma_\chi\) is the chiral conductivity.
- For a single domain with \(\sigma_\chi>0\), the photon radiation rate for right-polarized photons is \(\frac{dW_+}{dx} = \frac{\alpha Q^2}{2\e x}\left\{ \sigma_\chi \e \left(\frac{x^2}{2}-x+1\right)-m^2x\right\}\theta(x_0-x)\), where \(x=\omega/\e\) and \(x_0 = 1/(1+m^2/(\lambda\sigma_\chi\e))\).
- Total energy radiated per unit time by a fermion: \(\frac{\Delta \e}{T} = \frac{1}{3}\alpha Q^2\sigma_\chi \e\), neglecting terms of order \(m^2/|\sigma_\chi|\e\).
- For pair production in a single domain with \(\sigma_\chi>0\), the decay rate for left-polarized photons is \(\frac{dW_-}{dx} = \frac{\alpha Q^2}{4\omega}\left\{ \left(x^2+(1-x)^2\right)\sigma_\chi\omega+2m^2 \right\}\theta(x_2-x)\theta(x-x_1)\), where \(x_{1,2}=\frac{1}{2}\left(1\mp\sqrt{1-4m^2/|\sigma_\chi|\omega}\right)\).
- The condition for photon decay requires \(\omega > 4m^2/|\sigma_\chi|\).

## Context

This work extends the study of macroscopic effects of the chiral anomaly in QCD, specifically the emergence of topological \(CP\)-odd domains in hot nuclear matter (Kharzeev 2007). The modified photon dispersion relation in chiral matter was previously derived by Carroll (1989), Lehnert (2004), and others. The calculation method is borrowed from Schildknecht (2005) for transition radiation. The results enable further investigation of the long-time dynamics of radiative instability in quark-gluon plasma, where the chiral conductivity is about two orders of magnitude lower than the plasma temperature (Fukushima 2012), and may impact quark-gluon plasma phenomenology when enhanced by the strong coupling constant.
