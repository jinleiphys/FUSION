# Similarity between the kinematic viscosity of quark-gluon plasma and liquids at the viscosity minimum

> Recently, it has been found that the kinematic viscosity of liquids at the minimum, $ν_m$, can be expressed in terms of fundamental physical constants, giving $ν_m$ on the order of $10^{-7}~{\rm m^2/s}$. Here, we show that the kinematic viscosity of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) has a similar value and support this finding by experimental data and theoretical estimations. The similarity is striking, given that the dynamic viscosity and the density of QGP are about 16 orders of magnitude larger than in liquids and that the two systems have disparate interactions and fundamental theories. We discuss the implications of this result for understanding the QGP including the similarity of flow and particle dynamics at the viscosity minimum, the associated dynamical crossover and universality of shear diffusivity.

## Key claim
The kinematic viscosity of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is approximately \(10^{-7}~\mathrm{m^2/s}\), strikingly similar to the minimum kinematic viscosity of ordinary liquids, despite the dynamic viscosity and density of QGP being about 16 orders of magnitude larger and the two systems having fundamentally different interactions and theoretical descriptions.

## Method
The authors estimated the kinematic viscosity of QGP using experimental data for dynamic viscosity (\(\eta \approx 5\times 10^{11}~\mathrm{Pa\cdot s}\)) and energy density (\(E/V \approx 1~\mathrm{GeV/fm^3}\)) from the literature. They computed the kinematic viscosity as \(\nu = \eta/(\epsilon + p)\) using relativistic hydrodynamics, where \(\epsilon + p\) is the momentum susceptibility approximated by \(sT\) for small baryon chemical potential. They also derived \(\nu\) from the shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio \(\eta/s \approx (3/4\pi)(\hbar/k_B)\) and the QGP temperature \(T \approx 2\times 10^{12}~\mathrm{K}\), and supported the result with theoretical arguments based on the uncertainty principle and the Planckian relaxation time.

- Kinematic viscosity of QGP: \(\nu_{\mathrm{QGP}}^{\mathrm{exp}} \approx 10^{-7}~\mathrm{m^2/s}\) (from Eq. \ref{nuqgp}, using \(\eta = 5\times 10^{11}~\mathrm{Pa\cdot s}\), \(T = 2\times 10^{12}~\mathrm{K}\), and \(\eta/s \approx 3/4\pi \cdot \hbar/k_B\)).
- Experimental kinematic viscosity minima for liquids: \(\nu_m^{\mathrm{exp}} = (0.5-2)\times 10^{-7}~\mathrm{m^2/s}\) (Eq. \ref{nu10}, for noble, molecular, and network liquids at various pressures).

## Context
This work extends the finding of Trachenko et al. (2020) that the minimum kinematic viscosity of liquids can be expressed in terms of fundamental constants, giving \(\nu_m \approx 10^{-7}~\mathrm{m^2/s}\). The authors show that QGP, despite being a relativistic system with vastly different interactions, has the same kinematic viscosity value. This suggests a universality of shear diffusivity across relativistic and non-relativistic systems, as previously discussed by Hartnoll (2014). The result also connects to the dynamical crossover at the Frenkel line in liquids (Trachenko, 2015), implying that the QGP currently measured experimentally may be near a similar crossover between liquid-like and gas-like dynamics. Future higher-energy experiments could test whether the QGP moves into a gas-like regime with increasing viscosity.
