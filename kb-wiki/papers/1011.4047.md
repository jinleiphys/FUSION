# Regularization of Multi-Reference Energy Density Functional Calculations

> We report the first application of a recently proposed regularization procedure for multi-reference energy density functionals, which removes spurious divergent or non-continuous contributions to the binding energy, to a general configuration mixing. As an example, we present a calculation that corresponds to the particle-number and angular momentum projection of axially symmetric time-reversal invariant quasiparticle vacua of different quadrupole deformation for the nucleus O18. The SIII parameterization of the Skyrme energy functional is used.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first application of a regularization procedure for multi-reference energy density functional (MR EDF) calculations to a general configuration mixing, demonstrating that the regularization removes spurious divergent or non-continuous contributions to the binding energy that arise from violations of the Pauli principle in the functional.

## Method

The authors perform particle-number and angular momentum projection of axially symmetric time-reversal invariant quasiparticle vacua of different quadrupole deformation for the nucleus O-18, using the SIII parameterization of the Skyrme energy functional. They compare non-regularized MR EDF calculations, which define the energy kernel in analogy to the generalized Wick theorem (GWT), with regularized calculations that subtract a correction derived from the standard Wick theorem (SWT) to remove unphysical contributions from self-interaction and self-pairing terms.

## Key numbers

- The regularization correction varies from several hundred keV up to about 1 MeV depending on deformation, which is small compared to the total binding energy but sometimes accounts for a substantial percentage of the energy gain from particle-number restoration.
- The non-regularized particle-number projected deformation energy curve for O-18 shows discontinuities that coincide with deformations where single-particle levels cross the Fermi energy; these steps are removed by regularization.
- For the pairing functional, a "volume"-type pairing functional of strength 300 MeV fm^-3 is used.
- The regularized energy curve becomes independent of the number of discretization points L for L greater than 5, whereas the non-regularized curve requires L=199 discretization points and still does not converge at all deformations.
- In the GCM mixing of axial quasiparticle vacua for O-18, no more than about 8 sufficiently independent intrinsic configurations can be found for J=0, and even fewer for higher J values.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of pathologies in MR EDF calculations first identified by Anguiano et al. (2001) and Dobaczewski et al. (2007), who showed that particle-number projection can produce divergences when single-particle levels are half-filled. The regularization procedure, previously proposed by the authors in earlier papers (Bender et al. 2009, 2010), is here applied for the first time to a general configuration mixing combining particle-number restoration, angular-momentum projection, and generator coordinate method (GCM) mixing. The results enable more reliable spectroscopic calculations using MR EDF methods, though the regularization is limited to functionals depending only on integer powers of density matrices, which excludes most currently used functionals of acceptable predictive power.
