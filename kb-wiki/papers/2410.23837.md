# Mode analysis of Nambu-Goldstone modes in U(1) charged first-order relativistic viscous hydrodynamics

> We conduct a mode analysis of a general $U(1)$-charged first-order relativistic hydrodynamics within the framework of effective field theory for dissipative fluids in flat Minkowski spacetime. We derive the most general quadratic action for hydrodynamic modes, including stochastic noise, and analyze the corresponding dispersion relations in a consistent gradient expansion. We argue that spontaneous breaking of spacetime symmetry arises in the presence of a local thermal state specified by a local timelike four-vector. We demonstrate that hydrodynamical perturbations can be identified as Nambu-Goldstone (NG) modes, analogous to their embedding in global $U(1)$-invariant theories. We find that frame-invariant combinations of hydrodynamic transport coefficients determine the first-order dispersion relations in the low-energy limit, making the mode analysis manifestly independent of the choice of hydrodynamic frame. Assuming local Kubo-Martin-Schwinger (KMS) symmetry and unitarity of the underlying UV theory, we show that first-order hydrodynamics is stable if the enthalpy density is positive.

## Key claim

The paper argues that first-order relativistic viscous hydrodynamics can be stable and causal when analyzed with consistent order counting in a manifestly covariant effective field theory framework, and that previously reported instabilities arise from mismatches in order counting between constitutive relations and the perturbative treatment of dynamical modes when solving constraint equations.

## Method

The author constructs the most general quadratic action for hydrodynamic modes in U(1)-charged first-order relativistic hydrodynamics using the Schwinger-Keldysh effective field theory formalism for dissipative fluids in flat Minkowski spacetime. The generating functional is constrained by unitarity and the local Kubo-Martin-Schwinger (KMS) condition, which yields fluctuation-dissipation relations linking transport coefficients to noise coefficients. Hydrodynamic perturbations are identified as Nambu-Goldstone modes associated with spontaneous breaking of spacetime symmetry, and the quadratic action is derived by eliminating redundant degrees of freedom using conservation laws.

## Key numbers

- The effective theory is organized as an expansion in epsilon = (rho + P)^{-1} v nabla, with viscosity coefficients v = epsilon, lambda, zeta, etc.
- The local KMS condition reduces the number of independent parameters from thirty to eleven (three thermodynamic variables, seventeen first-order dissipative coefficients, and ten zeroth-order noise coefficients reduced by FDRs)
- The unitarity condition requires eta >= 0, kappa x^2 + 2 tau x y + chi y^2 >= 0, and epsilon x^2 + zeta y^2 + nu_3 z^2 + 2 lambda x y + 2 nu_1 y z + 2 nu_2 z x >= 0 for arbitrary real x, y, z
- First-order hydrodynamics is stable if the enthalpy density is positive, assuming local KMS symmetry and unitarity of the underlying UV theory

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to dissipative hydrodynamics developed by Crossley, Glorioso, and Liu (2015) to the specific case of U(1)-charged fluids, providing a mode analysis that is manifestly independent of hydrodynamic frame choice. It addresses longstanding concerns about stability and causality in first-order hydrodynamics raised by Hiscock and Lindblom (1983, 1985), which motivated the development of Muller-Israel-Stewart theory (Israel 1976, 1979). The paper enables a unified perspective on stability by showing that frame-invariant combinations of transport coefficients determine first-order dispersion relations, and it provides a systematic framework for incorporating noise and fluctuation-dissipation relations through the KMS condition at the action level.
