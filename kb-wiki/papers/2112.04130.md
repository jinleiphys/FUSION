## Key claim

The radiative decay widths of the $\Xi_c(2923)^0$ state, when interpreted as an $S$-wave $D\Lambda-D\Sigma$ hadronic molecule, are predicted to be 1.23-11.66 KeV for $\Xi_c(2923)^0\to\Xi_c^0\gamma$ and 0.30-3.71 KeV for $\Xi_c(2923)^0\to\Xi_c^{'0}\gamma$, which are orders of magnitude different from predictions assuming it is a conventional charmed baryon; these differences can be used to test the internal structure of the state once experimental measurements become available.

## Method

The authors model $\Xi_c(2923)^0$ as an $S$-wave $D\Lambda-D\Sigma$ molecular bound state, using effective Lagrangians to compute the radiative decay into $\Xi_c^0\gamma$ and $\Xi_c^{'0}\gamma$ via hadronic loop diagrams. The coupling constants of the molecular state to its constituents are determined by the Weinberg compositeness condition, and the model parameter $\Lambda$ (a size parameter for the correlation function) is fixed at 1.0 GeV based on prior work. The amplitudes include contributions from $D$, $D^{*}$, $\Sigma$, and $\Lambda$ exchanges as well as a contact term required to maintain gauge invariance.

## Key numbers

* Radiative decay width $\Gamma(\Xi_c(2923)^0\to\Xi_c^0\gamma)$ = 1.23-11.66 KeV, for the $D\Lambda-D\Sigma$ molecular interpretation with $\chi_{D\Sigma}$ ranging from 0.0 to 1.0.
* Radiative decay width $\Gamma(\Xi_c(2923)^0\to\Xi_c^{'0}\gamma)$ = 0.30-3.71 KeV, for the same molecular interpretation and parameter range.
* For comparison, conventional charmed baryon interpretations give $\Gamma(\Xi_c(2923)^0\to{}^{2}\Xi_c^{'}+\gamma)$ = 472.0 KeV (Wang et al., 2020) and 451.0 KeV (Bijker et al., 2020), and $\Gamma(\Xi_c(2923)^0\to{}^{2}\Xi_c+\gamma)$ = 0.0 KeV (Wang et al., 2020) and 1.1 KeV (Bijker et al., 2020).
* The coupling constants satisfy $(0.0885 g_{\Xi_c^{*0}D\Sigma})^2 = 1 - (0.0891 g_{\Xi_c^{*0}D\Lambda})^2$ from the compositeness condition.

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of $\Xi_c(2923)^0$ previously proposed by Zhu et al. (2020) to its radiative decays, providing a testable prediction that contrasts sharply with the conventional charmed baryon interpretations of Wang et al. (2020) and Bijker et al. (2020). The predicted radiative widths differ by orders of magnitude between the molecular and conventional pictures, especially for the $\Xi_c(2923)^0\to\Xi_c^{'0}\gamma$ channel, enabling future experiments to discriminate between these competing models of the state's internal structure.
