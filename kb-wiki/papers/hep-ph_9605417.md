# A Dynamical Model for Nuclear Structure Functions

> We construct a dynamical model for the parton distributions in a nucleus by perturbative evolution of input distributions from a low starting scale. These input distributions are obtained by modifications of the corresponding free nucleon ones; the modifications being determined by standard nuclear physics considerations. The model gives good agreement with existing data. Its extension to the spin dependent case enables an estimation of nuclear modifications to asymmetries observed in recent doubly polarised deep inelastic scattering experiments. Although the structure functions themselves are very different from the free nucleon ones, their ratio is insensitive to these changes.

# A Dynamical Model for Nuclear Structure Functions

## Key claim

The authors construct a dynamical model for nuclear parton distributions that reproduces the EMC effect and its nuclear-mass dependence, but they find that while the individual spin-dependent and spin-independent structure functions undergo substantial nuclear modifications, the measured asymmetry ratio is largely insensitive to these changes, suggesting that neutron structure functions can be extracted from nuclear targets without large nuclear corrections.

## Method

The authors start with the GRV free-nucleon parton distributions at a low input scale Q² = μ² ≈ 0.23 GeV² and modify them using two nuclear effects: (1) nucleon swelling, which geometrically redistributes partons while conserving number and momentum, constrained by the uncertainty principle; and (2) nuclear binding, which depletes the sea quark (mesonic) component. These modified input distributions are then evolved to higher Q² using leading-order DGLAP equations, and a second binding effect from parton-nucleon overlap is applied at the interaction scale to account for additional small-x shadowing.

## Key numbers

- Input scale: Q² = μ² ≈ 0.23 GeV²
- Swelling parameter for heavy nuclei: δ_vol = 0.15 (the only free parameter)
- Binding energy parameters from the Weizsäcker mass formula: a_vol = 15.67 MeV, a_sur = 17.23 MeV
- Second binding depletion factor: 2β = 0.037
- Shadowing onset: x₀ ≈ 0.1, defined by x₀ = 1/(2M_N d_N)
- Free proton valence input at μ²: xu_v^N = 1.377 x^{0.549} (1-x)^{3.027} P_{N,u}
- Free proton gluon input at μ²: xg_N = 35.8 x^{2.3} (1-x)^{4.0}
- Helium swelling: δ_He = 0.15/2 according to equation (5)
- Lithium swelling: δ_Li = 0.033

## Context

This work extends the dynamical parton model of Glück, Reya, and Vogt (GRV, 1995) from free nucleons to bound nucleons in nuclei, addressing the EMC effect first discovered by the European Muon Collaboration (1983). The approach differs from earlier rescaling models by Jaffe (1984) and Close, Roberts, and Ross (1985) by implementing swelling as a geometric redistribution of partons at the input scale rather than a Q² rescaling, and by treating binding as a depletion of the mesonic sea rather than an x-rescaling. The model enables predictions for nuclear structure functions and their ratios across a wide range of nuclei (A = 4 to A = 118) and Q² values, and its extension to spin-dependent distributions allows estimation of nuclear corrections in polarized deep inelastic scattering experiments on deuterium and ³He targets.
