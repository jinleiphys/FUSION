# Electron Screening in 7Be + p --> 8B + photon reaction

> We evaluate the effect of screening by bound electron in (7Be,e) + p --> (8B,e) + photon transition in the framework of the adiabatic representation of the three particle problem. Comparison with two approaches (united nucleus and static) is presented. We discuss possible applications of this effect both for Solar Neutrinos and low energy fusion experiments.

# Electron Screening in 7Be + p → 8B + γ Reaction

## Key claim

The bound electron in the (7Be,e) + p → (8B,e) + γ reaction enhances the fusion rate compared to the bare-nucleus reaction, but the enhancement is reduced to about 10% at the Gamow window energies (17–20 keV) relevant to the solar core, despite being a factor of 15 at lower energies.

## Method

The authors solved the three-particle Coulomb problem (two nuclei plus one bound electron) in the adiabatic (Born-Oppenheimer) representation, using only the ground-state two-center electron eigenfunction (1Sσ term) from tabulated values. They computed the internuclear wave function at the origin by solving the Schrödinger equation with an effective potential consisting of the Coulomb repulsion plus the electron energy eigenvalue U(R), and compared the resulting enhancement factor γ(E) with two simpler approximations: the united nucleus approach (upper estimate) and the static electron approach (lower estimate).

## Key numbers

- The enhancement factor γ(E) = σ_sc / σ_ba was computed for kinetic energies 0.1 keV ≤ E ≤ 100 keV.
- At mean kinetic energy in the Sun core at temperature T = 15 × 10⁶ K, the screening effect is a factor of 15.
- At the Gamow window around 17–20 keV, the screening effect is reduced to 10%.
- The united nucleus approach and static approach agree well with the exact numerical solution at kinetic energies above 2 keV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of electron screening in solar fusion (Gruzinov, Shoppa, Salpeter, Mitler, Carraro, Brown, Langanke) by providing the first quantum mechanical calculation of bound-electron screening for the specific 7Be(p,γ)8B reaction. The authors note that the Debye-Hückel approximation (Dar and Shaviv, 1996) is inadequate for low-density stars like the Sun, and that bound-electron screening could help address the "second Solar Neutrino Problem" (Bahcall), the deficit of 7Be neutrinos relative to 8B neutrinos in the Standard Solar Model. The results enable improved stellar evolution codes by showing that the simple united nucleus prescription can be used as a reliable approximation.
