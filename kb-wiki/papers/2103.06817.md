# $P_c(4312)^+$, $P_c(4380)^+$, and $P_c(4457)^+$ as double triangle cusps

> Understanding the nature of the hidden charm pentaquark(like) signals in the LHCb data for $Λ_b^0\to J/ψp K^-$ is a central problem of hadron spectroscopy. We propose a scenario completely different from previous ones such as hadron molecules and compact pentaquarks. We identify relevant double triangle mechanisms with leading or lower-order singularities. The associated anomalous threshold cusps at the $Σ_c^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$ thresholds are significantly more singular than the ordinary ones. Then we demonstrate that the double triangle amplitudes reproduce the peak structures of $P_c(4312)^+$, $P_c(4380)^+$, and $P_c(4457)^+$ in the LHCb data, through an interference with other common mechanisms. Only the $P_c(4440)^+$ peak is due to a resonance with width and strength significantly smaller than previously estimated. $P_c^+$ signals are expected in other processes and the proposed model (partly) explains the current data such as: the GlueX $J/ψ$ photoproduction data with no $P_c^+$ signals; the LHCb $Λ_b^0\to J/ψp π^-$ data with a possible signal only from $P_c(4440)^+$. The double triangle singularity is now a possible option to interpret resonancelike structures near thresholds in general.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the peak structures of $P_c(4312)^+$, $P_c(4380)^+$, and $P_c(4457)^+$ in the LHCb data for $\Lambda_b^0\to J/\psi p K^-$ are caused by double triangle kinematic cusps rather than by hadron molecules or compact pentaquarks, with only the $P_c(4440)^+$ peak requiring a genuine resonance interpretation but with width and strength significantly smaller than previously estimated.

## Method

The author constructs a model for $\Lambda_b^0\to J/\psi p K^-$ that includes double triangle diagrams with $\Sigma_c^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$ intermediate states, one-loop diagrams with $\Lambda_c^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$ states, a Breit-Wigner resonance for $P_c(4440)^+$, and direct decay mechanisms. The double triangle amplitudes are evaluated using a single-channel contact interaction model for the $Y_c\bar{D}^{(*)}$ scattering with perturbative transition to $J/\psi p$, and the full model with 26 adjustable parameters is fitted to the LHCb $\cos\theta_{P_c}$-weighted data.

## Key numbers

* The $P_c(4440)^+$ mass from the fit is $4443.1\pm 1.4$ MeV, with width $2.7\pm 2.4$ MeV, significantly narrower than the LHCb analysis value of $20.6\pm 4.9^{+8.7}_{-10.1}$ MeV.
* The $P_c(4440)^+$ contribution is only approximately 1/22 of the LHCb's estimate, where the LHCb reported ${\cal R}\equiv {\cal B}(\Lambda_b^0\to P_c^+K^-){\cal B}(P_c^+\to J/\psi p)/{\cal B}(\Lambda_b^0\to J/\psi pK^-)=1.11\pm 0.33^{+0.22}_{-0.10}$\%.
* The ratio of coupling strengths between the double triangle and one-loop amplitudes is $R=7.2-3.2$ for cutoff $\Lambda=0.8-2$ GeV.
* The scattering length for attractive $\Sigma_c^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$ interactions is fixed at $a\sim 0.5$ fm, while the repulsive $\Lambda_c^+\bar{D}^{*0}$ interaction scattering length varies from $a\sim -0.4$ fm to $-0.05$ fm depending on cutoff.

## Context

This work proposes a completely new interpretation of the $P_c^+$ peaks that contradicts previous hadron-molecule models (such as those by Du et al. (2019) and Xiao et al. (2019)) and compact pentaquark pictures, which assigned a pole to each peak. The double triangle cusp mechanism explains the absence of $P_c^+$ signals in GlueX $J/\psi$ photoproduction data and the possible appearance of only $P_c(4440)^+$ in $\Lambda_b^0\to J/\psi p \pi^-$ data. The work opens the possibility that double triangle singularities should be considered as a general option for interpreting resonancelike structures near thresholds in hadron spectroscopy.
