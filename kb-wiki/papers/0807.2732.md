# Constraints on the spectra of 17,19C

> Diverse means are used to investigate the spectra of the radioactive, exotic ions, 17,19C. First, estimates have been made using a shell model for the systems. Information from those shell model studies were then used in evaluating cross sections of the scattering of 70A MeV 17,19C ions from hydrogen. Complementing those studies, a multichannel algebraic scattering (MCAS) theory for n+16,18C coupled-channel problems has been used to identify structures of the compound systems. The results show that the shell model structure assumed for these ions is reasonable with little need of effective charges. The conditions that two excited states exist within a few hundred keV of the ground state places some restriction upon the structure models. Other positive parity states are expected in the low-lying spectra of the two nuclei.

## Key claim

The shell model structure assumed for 17,19C is reasonable with little need of effective charges, but the condition that two excited states exist within a few hundred keV of the ground state places restrictions on the structure models; the spin-parity assignments of the low-lying states remain uncertain, particularly whether the ground state of 17C is 3/2+ or 5/2+ and whether 19C qualifies as a one-neutron halo nucleus.

## Method

The authors performed shell model calculations using the code OXBASH with WBP interactions within the spsdpf model space to generate spectra for 17,19C. They then used a g-folding model with the Melbourne effective NN interactions to evaluate elastic and inelastic scattering cross sections for 70A MeV 17,19C ions from hydrogen targets. Complementing these studies, they applied the multichannel algebraic scattering (MCAS) method for n+16,18C coupled-channel problems, incorporating Pauli blocking via an orthogonalizing pseudo-potential (OPP) scheme, to identify structures of the compound systems.

## Key numbers

- The first excited state in 17C from shell model calculations is at 32 keV (spin-parity 5/2+) using WBP interaction, or 218 keV using MK3W interaction
- Experimental excited states in 17C observed at 210 keV (tentative 1/2+) and 331 keV (tentative 5/2+), with higher states at 2.20, 3.05, and 6.13 MeV
- Experimental spectrum of 19C has ground state with Jpi=1/2+, with excited states at 72 and 197 keV (spins 3/2 and 5/2), and a higher state at 1.46 MeV (5/2+)
- Single-neutron separation energy of 19C is approximately 0.53 MeV
- Shell model matter rms radii: 17C = 2.500 fm (WBP), 19C = 2.556 fm (WBP); experimental values from Ozawa: 17C = 2.72 +/- 0.04 fm, 19C = 3.23 +/- 0.08 fm (FB) or 3.13 +/- 0.07 fm (OL)
- MCAS calculations used potential geometry with R = 2.9 fm and a = 0.8 fm; deformation parameters beta2 = 0.3 for 17C and beta2 = 0.4 for 19C
- Reaction cross sections from g-folding with oscillator wave functions: 423 mb for 17C and 461 mb for 19C; using Woods-Saxon (halo) wave functions for 19C gave 584 mb
- Resonance neutron widths from MCAS for 19C: 0.35 MeV (3/2+_2), 2.6 MeV (5/2+_2), 0.47 MeV (9/2+), and 0.71 MeV (7/2+)

## Context

This work extends prior shell model studies by Elekes (2005) and coupled-channel calculations by Ridikas (1997, 1998) for 17,19C, while addressing the Pauli principle violation present in the Ridikas approach. The MCAS method had previously been applied to mass-7 isobars and 15F by Canton (2006), and the g-folding approach had been used to characterize helium and lithium isotopes as halo or skin nuclei by Amos (2000) and Lagoyannis (2001). The results enable future experimental measurements of reaction cross sections for 19C scattering from hydrogen to distinguish between halo and non-halo structure models, and provide constraints for further theoretical studies of neutron-rich carbon isotopes near the drip line.
