# From local correlations to regional systematics

> Local correlations of $2^+_1$ excitation energies and B(E2, $2^+_1\rightarrow \text{ g.s.}$) values require linear $N_pN_n$ systematics in a logarithmic scale, as confirmed by an experiment survey. Based on local correlations of $őĪ$-decay energies, neutron separation energies, and proton separation energies, one can decouple them into their proton and neutron contributions separately. These contributions exhibit smooth regional systematics beyond the $N_pN_n$ scheme.

# From local correlations to regional systematics

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that local correlations of nuclear observables (E2, B(E2), QőĪ, Sn, Sp) impose a linear relationship on NpNn systematics in logarithmic scale for E2 and B(E2), and enable decoupling of mass-related observables into separate proton and neutron contributions that exhibit smooth regional systematics, but the logarithmic linearity is only strictly required when the observable also follows the NpNn scheme, and the decoupling scheme is presented as a practical tool rather than a universal law.

## Context

This work extends the NpNn scheme originally developed by Casten (1980s) and the local correlation formalism proposed by Bao et al. (2015), which generalized the NpNn scheme through a functional continuity condition. The paper confirms that E2 and B(E2) data from the ENSDF and Raman compilations follow logarithmic linearity in NpNn, consistent with earlier schematic Hartree-Fock and collective-model derivations from the 1970s. The decoupling of QőĪ, Sn, and Sp builds on the Garvey-Kelson relations and the linear systematics of separation energies proposed by Vogt (2000s). The results enable a new approach to studying regional evolution of nuclear observables that do not follow the NpNn scheme directly, and identify a new critical point in B(E2) evolution near NpNn‚Čą45 that warrants further theoretical investigation.
