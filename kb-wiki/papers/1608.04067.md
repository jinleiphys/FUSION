# Impacts of B-factory measurements on determination of fragmentation functions from electron-positron annihilation data

> Fragmentation functions are determined for the pion and kaon by global analyses of charged-hadron production data in electron-positron annihilation. Accurate measurements were reported by the Belle and BaBar collaborations for the fragmentation functions at the center-of-mass energies of 10.52 GeV and 10.54 GeV, respectively, at the KEK and SLAC B factories, whereas other available $e^+e^-$ measurements were mostly done at higher energies, mainly at the $Z$ mass of 91.2 GeV. There is a possibility that gluon fragmentation functions, as well as quark fragmentation functions, are accurately determined by scaling violation. We report our global analysis of the fragmentation functions especially to show impacts of the B-factory measurements on the fragmentation function determination. Our results indicate that the fragmentation functions are determined more accurately not only by the scaling violation but also by high-statistical nature of the Belle and BaBar data. However, there are some tensions between the Belle and BaBar data in comparison with previous measurements. We also explain how the flavor dependence of quark fragmentation functions and the gluon function are separated by using measurements at different $Q^2$ values. In particular, the electric and weak changes are different depending on the quark type, so that a light-quark flavor separation also became possible in principle due to the precise data at both $\sqrt{s}\simeq 10.5$ GeV and 91.2 GeV.

## Key claim

The B-factory measurements from Belle and BaBar at 10.52 GeV and 10.54 GeV allow more accurate determination of fragmentation functions for pions and kaons through scaling violation and high statistics, but there are some tensions between the Belle and BaBar data in comparison with previous measurements.

## Method

The authors performed global analyses of charged-hadron production data in electron-positron annihilation, including new Belle and BaBar data alongside previous measurements from TASSO, TPC, HRS, TOPAZ, SLD, ALEPH, OPAL, and DELPHI. Fragmentation functions were parametrized in a simple polynomial form at an initial scale Q0^2 = 1 GeV^2 for light quarks and gluon, with separate parametrizations for charm and bottom quarks at their mass thresholds, and the DGLAP evolution equations were solved at next-to-leading order using the modified minimal subtraction scheme.

## Key numbers

- Belle data taken at center-of-mass energy sqrt(s) = 10.52 GeV with 78 pion data points and 78 kaon data points
- BaBar data taken at center-of-mass energy sqrt(s) = 10.54 GeV with 36 pion data points and 36 kaon data points
- Initial scale Q0^2 = 1 GeV^2 for gluon and light quark (u, d, s) fragmentation functions
- Belle normalization correction factor c = 0.64616 plus or minus 0.00003 due to initial-state radiation cut
- Kinematical cuts applied: Q^2 greater than 1 GeV^2, z greater than 0.1 for data at sqrt(s) less than M_Z, z greater than 0.15 for BaBar kaon data, and z greater than 0.05 for data at sqrt(s) = M_Z
- Total of 378 pion data points and 359 kaon data points used in the analysis

## Context

This work extends the previous global analysis of Hirai, Kumano, Nagai, and Sudoh (2007) which determined fragmentation functions without B-factory data and found large uncertainty bands especially in disfavored-quark and gluon functions. The new Belle and BaBar measurements at 10.5 GeV provide a much lower energy scale compared to the Z mass of 91.2 GeV where most previous data were taken, enabling clearer scaling violation effects that allow more accurate determination of gluon fragmentation functions, which are important for analyzing hadron production at RHIC and LHC.
