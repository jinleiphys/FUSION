# Hydrodynamic Waves in an Anomalous Charged Fluid

> We study the collective excitations in a relativistic fluid with an anomalous $U(1)$ current. In $3+1$ dimensions at zero chemical potential, in addition to ordinary sound modes we find two propagating modes in presence of an external magnetic field. The first one which is a transverse degenerate mode, propagates with a velocity proportional to the coefficient of gravitational anomaly; this is in fact the Chiral Alfvén wave recently found in \cite{Yamamoto:2015ria}. Another one is a wave of density perturbation, namely a chiral magnetic wave (CMW). The velocity dependence of CMW on the chiral anomaly coefficient is well known. We compute the dependence of CMW's velocity on the coefficient of gravitational anomaly as well. We also show that the dissipation splits the degeneracy of CAW. At finite chiral charge density we show that in general there may exist five chiral hydrodynamic waves. Of these five waves, one is the CMW while the other four are mixed Modified Sound-Alfvén waves. It turns out that in propagation transverse to the magnetic field no anomaly effect appear while in parallel to the magnetic field we find sound waves become dispersive due to anomaly.

## Key claim

In a relativistic fluid with an anomalous U(1) current at zero chemical potential, an external magnetic field enables two new propagating modes beyond ordinary sound: a Chiral Alfvén wave whose velocity is proportional to the gravitational anomaly coefficient, and a Chiral Magnetic Wave whose velocity depends on both the chiral anomaly coefficient and the gravitational anomaly coefficient.

## Method

The authors linearize the hydrodynamic equations for a relativistic fluid with anomalous transport (including both chiral and gravitational anomaly contributions) around equilibrium at zero chemical potential, using the Landau-Lifshitz frame. They compute the dispersion relations and mode amplitudes by solving the eigenvalue problem for the matrix of linearized equations in Fourier space, first without dissipation and then including dissipative effects (shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and conductivity). The analysis is restricted to wavelengths of the same order as the magnetic length scale, with the magnetic field treated as first order in derivatives.

## Key numbers

- Chiral Alfvén wave velocity: \( v_{CAW} = -\frac{\mathcal{D}}{2}\frac{T^2}{\bar{w}} \boldsymbol{B}\cdot\hat{\boldsymbol{k}} \), where \(\mathcal{D}=1/12\) is the gravitational anomaly coefficient
- Chiral Magnetic Wave velocity: \( v_{CMW} = \left(\frac{\mathcal{C}}{\chi}-\frac{\mathcal{D}}{2}\frac{T^2}{\bar{w}}\right)\boldsymbol{B}\cdot\hat{\boldsymbol{k}} \), where \(\mathcal{C}=1/(4\pi^2)\) is the chiral anomaly coefficient
- At zero chemical potential, the CME transport coefficient \(\xi_B\) vanishes, but its fluctuation \(\partial\xi_B/\partial n = \mathcal{C}/\chi - (\mathcal{D}/2)(T^2/\bar{w})\) is non-zero
- The vortical transport coefficient at zero chemical potential is \(\xi = \mathcal{D}T^2\)

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral hydrodynamic waves beyond the original Chiral Magnetic Wave found by Kharzeev and Yee (2010) for mixed-chirality plasmas, and independently reproduces the Chiral Alfvén wave recently found by Yamamoto (2015). The authors compute the gravitational anomaly contribution to the CMW velocity, which was not included in prior work. The results enable experimental searches for these collective modes in heavy-ion collision plasmas and in the high-temperature plasma above the electroweak phase transition where hypermagnetic fields couple differently to left- and right-handed electrons, as discussed by Giovannini (1997).
