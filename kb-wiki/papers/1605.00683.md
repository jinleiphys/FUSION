# Nuclear response theory for spin-isospin excitations in a relativistic quasiparticle-phonon coupling framework

> A new theoretical approach to spin-isospin excitations in open-shell nuclei is presented. The developed method is based on the relativistic meson-exchange nuclear Lagrangian of Quantum Hadrodynamics and extends the response theory for superfluid nuclear systems beyond relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation in the proton-neutron channel (pn-RQRPA). The coupling between quasiparticle degrees of freedom and collective vibrations (phonons) introduces a time-dependent effective interaction, in addition to the exchange of pion and $ρ$-meson taken into account without retardation. The time-dependent contributions are treated in the resonant time-blocking approximation, in analogy to previously developed relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation (RQTBA) in the neutral (non-isospin-flip) channel. The new method is called proton-neutron RQTBA (pn-RQTBA) and applied to Gamow-Teller resonance in a chain of neutron-rich Nickel isotopes $^{68-78}$Ni. A strong fragmentation of the resonance along with quenching of the strength, as compared to pn-RQRPA, is obtained. Based on the calculated strength distribution, beta-decay half-lives of the considered isotopes are computed and compared to pn-RQRPA half-lives and to experimental data. It is shown that a considerable improvement of the half-life description is obtained in pn-RQTBA because of the spreading effects, which bring the lifetimes to a very good quantitative agreement with data.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new theoretical approach, called proton-neutron Relativistic Quasiparticle Time Blocking Approximation (pn-RQTBA), which extends the description of spin-isospin excitations to open-shell nuclei by including quasiparticle-phonon coupling beyond the proton-neutron Relativistic Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (pn-RQRPA). The method improves agreement with experimental beta-decay half-lives in neutron-rich Nickel isotopes, but the authors note that full self-consistency would require explicit inclusion of the Fock term and restoration of the subtraction procedure, which is beyond the scope of this work.

## Method

The method combines the neutral-channel Relativistic Quasiparticle Time Blocking Approximation (RQTBA) with the proton-neutron Relativistic Time Blocking Approximation (pn-RTBA), using a relativistic meson-exchange Lagrangian from Quantum Hadrodynamics. The static part of the self-energy is computed within Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theory, while the energy-dependent part is obtained from the Quasiparticle-Vibration Coupling (QPVC) model, with the Bethe-Salpeter equation solved in the resonant time-blocking approximation. The effective interaction includes static pion and rho-meson exchange plus a Landau-Migdal term, with the dynamical part arising from coupling to collective phonons, though only forward-going diagrams with isoscalar phonons are implemented.

## Key numbers

- The Landau-Migdal parameter g' is set to 0.6, about twice the bare value of 1/3, to reproduce the Gamow-Teller resonance position in 208Pb.
- The method is applied to Gamow-Teller resonance in a chain of neutron-rich Nickel isotopes 68-78Ni.
- The pn-RQTBA produces a strong fragmentation of the resonance and quenching of the strength compared to pn-RQRPA, leading to considerably improved beta-decay half-lives that show very good quantitative agreement with experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the Relativistic Time Blocking Approximation (RTBA) for spin-isospin modes, previously limited to magic nuclei due to the absence of pairing correlations (Marketin, Litvinova 2014), by incorporating the quasiparticle formalism from the neutral-channel RQTBA (Litvinova 2008). It addresses the known problem that (Q)RPA approaches systematically overestimate beta-decay half-lives and fail to describe the spreading width of resonances, as seen in earlier non-relativistic particle-vibration coupling approaches using Skyrme forces (Niu 2012, Niu 2014, Niu 2015). The new method enables more accurate predictions of beta-decay half-lives for neutron-rich nuclei relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis, particularly for isotopes that remain experimentally inaccessible at facilities like FRIB and FAIR.
