# Nuclear collective dynamics in the lattice Hamiltonian Vlasov method

> The lattice Hamiltonian method is developed for solving the Vlasov equation with nuclear mean-field based on the Skyrme pseudopotential up to next-to-next-to-next-to leading order. The ground states of nuclei are obtained through varying the total energy with respect to the density distribution of nucleons. Owing to the self-consistent treatment of initial nuclear ground state and the exact energy conservation in the lattice Hamiltonian method, the present framework of solving the Vlasov equation exhibits very stable nuclear ground state evolution. As a first application of the new lattice Hamiltonian Vlasov method, we explore the iso-scalar giant monopole and iso-vector giant dipole modes of finite nuclei. The obtained results are shown to be comparable to that from random-phase approximation and consistent with the experimental data, indicating the capability of the present method in dealing with the long-time near-equilibrium nuclear dynamics.

## Key claim

The lattice Hamiltonian Vlasov (LHV) method, when combined with a self-consistently obtained initial nuclear ground state and the N3LO Skyrme pseudopotential, yields stable ground-state evolution and collective-mode results comparable to random-phase approximation (RPA) and experimental data, but only for low-energy, near-equilibrium dynamics where nucleon-nucleon collisions are negligible.

## Method

The authors solve the Vlasov equation using the lattice Hamiltonian method, where the total Hamiltonian is discretized on a spatial lattice and the phase-space distribution is represented by test particles with a triangle form factor. The nuclear mean-field potential is derived from the N3LO Skyrme pseudopotential (including central, momentum-dependent, gradient, and density-dependent terms, but omitting spin-orbit and tensor components), and the initial ground state is obtained by varying the total energy with respect to the radial density distribution (Thomas-Fermi initialization). The equations of motion for test particles are then integrated using Hamilton's equations derived from the lattice Hamiltonian, ensuring exact energy conservation.

## Key numbers

- Saturation densities for the three N3LO Skyrme pseudopotentials: SP6s gives 0.1614 fm^-3, SP6m gives 0.1630 fm^-3, and SP6h gives 0.1647 fm^-3.
- Binding energies per nucleon at saturation: -16.04 MeV (SP6s), -15.94 MeV (SP6m), and -15.61 MeV (SP6h).
- Incompressibility coefficients: 240.9 MeV (SP6s), 233.4 MeV (SP6m), and 240.8 MeV (SP6h).
- Symmetry energy at saturation density: 28.84 MeV (SP6s), 31.93 MeV (SP6m), and 34.97 MeV (SP6h).
- Lattice spacing used in calculations: 0.5 fm in each direction.
- The form factor range parameter n is set to 4, which is large enough to conserve total momentum to high accuracy.

## Context

This work extends the lattice Hamiltonian Vlasov method (originally developed by Lenk and Pandharipande (1989)) by incorporating the N3LO Skyrme pseudopotential, which was previously shown by Wang et al. (2018) to reproduce the empirical nucleon optical potential up to 1 GeV kinetic energy. The method enables future studies of heavy-ion collisions using the same nuclear effective interaction as used in finite-nuclei structure calculations, and the authors note that the present framework (without nucleon-nucleon collisions) is applicable to collective motions where Pauli blocking inhibits individual collisions. The results for isoscalar giant monopole and isovector giant dipole modes of 208Pb are compared with RPA calculations and experimental data.
