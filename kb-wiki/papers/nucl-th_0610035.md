# Event-by-event fluctuations of transverse-momentum and multiparticle clusters in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We analyze the event-by-event fluctuations of mean transverse-momentum measured recently by the PHENIX and STAR Collaborations at RHIC. We argue that the observed scaling of strength of dynamical fluctuations with the inverse number of particles can be naturally explained by formation of multiparticle clusters.

# Event-by-event fluctuations of transverse-momentum and multiparticle clusters in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The observed scaling of dynamical fluctuations of mean transverse-momentum with the inverse number of particles can be naturally explained by the formation of multiparticle clusters, but the explanation in terms of quenched jets is not decisive, any mechanism leading to clusters (such as thermal clusters) would also explain the data, and the nature of the clusters remains an open issue.

## Method
The authors analyzed PHENIX and STAR experimental data on event-by-event fluctuations of mean transverse-momentum at RHIC, comparing the measured variance of mean pT to the variance from mixed events (where particle correlations are removed by construction). They derived the dynamical fluctuation variance σ²_dyn as the difference between the experimental and mixed-event variances, and showed that it scales as 1/⟨n⟩. They then constructed a cluster model where particles are grouped into N_cl clusters of average size r, with correlations only within clusters, and demonstrated that this model reproduces the observed scaling.

## Key numbers
- For PHENIX data at √s_NN = 130 GeV, the mean transverse-momentum ⟨M⟩ is approximately constant at 523 MeV for centralities 0-5% through 20-30%, with σ_p ≈ 290 MeV.
- The dynamical fluctuation σ_dyn multiplied by √⟨n⟩ is approximately constant across centralities: 60.3 ± 1.6 MeV (0-5%), 59.2 ± 1.5 MeV (0-10%), 59.8 ± 1.2 MeV (10-20%), and 57.7 ± 1.1 MeV (20-30%).
- The mixed-event variance σ_M^mix is well approximated by σ_p √(1/⟨n⟩ + σ_n²/⟨n⟩³), working at the 1-2% level.
- Average multiplicities ⟨n⟩ range from 59.6 (0-5% centrality) to 25.0 (20-30% centrality).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of event-by-event pT fluctuations measured by PHENIX (Adcox 2002) and STAR (Adams 2003, 2005) at RHIC, and builds on the authors' earlier analysis (Broniowski 2005). The cluster explanation provides an alternative to the jet-based interpretation proposed by Adler (2003) and Liu (2003). The paper shows that while quenched jets are consistent with the cluster picture, the scaling alone cannot distinguish between jets and other clustering mechanisms such as thermal clusters (Kisiel 2005) or mechanisms of Randrup. The results enable future work to focus on microscopic estimates of the covariance per pair and cluster size to determine the physical origin of the clusters.
