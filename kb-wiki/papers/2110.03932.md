# Coulomb and nuclear excitations of $^{70}$Zn and $^{68}$Ni at intermediate energy

> The reduced transition probabilities $B(E2; 0^+_{g.s.}\rightarrow2_1^+,2^+_2)$ in $^{70}$Zn and the full $B(E2; 0^+_{g.s.}\rightarrow2^+)$ strength up to S$_n$=7.79 MeV in $^{68}$Ni have been determined at the LISE/GANIL facility using the Coulomb-excitation technique at intermediate beam energy on a $^{208}$Pb target. The $γ$ rays emitted in-flight were detected with an array of 46 BaF$_2$ crystals. The angles of the deflected nuclei were determined in order to disentangle and extract the Coulomb and nuclear contributions to the excitation of the 2$^+$ states. The measured $B(E2; 0^+_{g.s.}\rightarrow2_1^+)$ of 1432(124) e$^2$fm$^4$ for $^{70}$Zn falls in the lower part of the published values which clustered either around 1600 or above 2000 e$^2$fm$^4$, while the $B(E2; 0^+_{g.s.}\rightarrow2^+_2)$ of 53(7) e$^2$fm$^4$ agrees very well with the two published values. The relatively low $B(E2; 0^+_{g.s.}\rightarrow2_1^+)$ of 301(38) e$^2$fm$^4$ for $^{68}$Ni agrees with previous studies and confirms a local magicity at $Z=28, N=40$. Combining the results of the low-energy spectra of $^{68}$Ni and $^{70}$Zn and their shell-model interpretations, it is interesting to notice that four different shapes (spherical, oblate, prolate and triaxial) are present. Finally, a summed $E2$ strength of only about 150 e$^2$fm$^4$ has been found experimentally at high excitation energy, likely due to proton excitations across the $Z=28$ gap. The experimental distribution of this high-energy $E2$ excitation agrees with SM calculations, but its strength is about two times weaker.

## Key claim

The measured B(E2; 0+_g.s. -> 2+_1) of 1432(124) e2fm4 for 70Zn falls in the lower part of published values, confirming that values above 2000 e2fm4 from earlier electron and proton inelastic scattering are too large, while the summed E2 strength at high excitation energy in 68Ni agrees with shell-model calculations in its distribution but is about two times weaker in magnitude.

## Method

The reduced transition probabilities were determined using Coulomb excitation at intermediate beam energy on a 208Pb target at the LISE/GANIL facility, with gamma rays detected by an array of 46 BaF2 crystals. The angles of deflected nuclei were measured with a Double Sided Silicon-Strip annular detector to disentangle Coulomb and nuclear contributions to the excitation of 2+ states, and the experimental angular distributions were fitted using both the DWEIKO and ECIS codes to extract beta_C and beta_N parameters.

## Key numbers

- 70Zn: B(E2; 0+_g.s. -> 2+_1) = 1432(124) e2fm4 (mean of DWEIKO and ECIS fits)
- 70Zn: B(E2; 0+_g.s. -> 2+_2) = 53(7) e2fm4 (after subtracting double-step excitation of 0.7 mb)
- 68Ni: B(E2; 0+_g.s. -> 2+_1) = 301(38) e2fm4 (mean of 292(35) from DWEIKO and 312(40) from ECIS)
- 68Ni: summed E2 strength at high excitation energy up to Sn = 7.79 MeV is about 150 e2fm4
- 70Zn: integrated cross section for 2+_1 state between 1 degree and 3.3 degrees is 228(20) mb
- 68Ni: integrated cross section for 2+_1 state between 1 degree and 3.4 degrees is 55(7) mb
- 70Zn: photo-peak efficiency of 25(1)% at 885 keV
- 68Ni: 31(2)% of incoming beam was in the 5- isomeric state; 2.45(2)% in the 0+_2 isomeric state

## Context

This work resolves discrepancies in the B(E2) values for 70Zn, where earlier measurements by Stelson (1962), Sorlin (2002), Kenn (2002), and Celikovic (2013) clustered around 1600 e2fm4 while Neuhausen (1976) and Singh (1998) reported values above 2000 e2fm4. The low B(E2) for 68Ni confirms the local magicity at Z=28, N=40 previously indicated by Sorlin (2002) and Bree (2008), and the search for high-energy E2 strength tests predictions by Langanke (2003) of proton excitations across the Z=28 gap. The results enable further shell-model studies of shape coexistence, with four different shapes (spherical, oblate, prolate, and triaxial) identified in the low-energy spectra of 68Ni and 70Zn.
