# Gamow-Teller strength in 54Fe and 56Fe

> Through a sequence of large scale shell model calculations, total Gamow-Teller strengths ($S_+$ and $S_-$) in $^{54}$Fe and $^{56}$Fe are obtained. They reproduce the experimental values once the $στ$ operator is quenched by the standard factor of $0.77$. Comparisons are made with recent Shell Model Monte Carlo calculations. Results are shown to depend critically on the interaction. From an analysis of the GT+ and GT$-$ strength functions it is concluded that experimental evidence is consistent with the $3(N-Z)$ sum rule.

# Gamow-Teller strength in 54Fe and 56Fe

## Key claim

Large-scale shell model calculations reproduce experimental Gamow-Teller strengths in 54Fe and 56Fe once the sigma-tau operator is quenched by the standard factor of 0.77, and the authors conclude that little strength is actually "missing" (unobserved) because the remaining strength necessary to satisfy the 3(N-Z) sum rule is likely present in satellite structures beyond the resonance region.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell model diagonalizations in the full 0-hbar-omega pf-shell space using the code ANTOINE, with truncation levels t up to 5 (where t is the maximum number of particles promoted from the 1f7/2 orbit to higher orbits). They used two interactions: KB3 (a minimally modified version of the Kuo-Brown G-matrix) and BR (MSOBEP interaction), and compared their results with Shell Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) calculations and experimental data from (p,n) and (n,p) charge-exchange reactions.

## Key numbers

- 54Fe S+ (KB3 extrapolated, quenched by 0.77): 3.56 Gamow-Teller units, compared to experimental values of 3.1±0.6 and 3.5±0.7
- 54Fe S- (KB3 extrapolated, quenched by 0.77): 7.11 Gamow-Teller units, compared to experimental values of 7.5±1.2, 7.8±1.9, and 7.5±0.7
- 56Fe S+ (KB3 extrapolated, quenched by 0.77): 2.7 Gamow-Teller units, compared to experimental values of 2.3±0.6 and 2.9±0.3
- 56Fe S- (KB3 extrapolated, quenched by 0.77): 9.8 Gamow-Teller units, compared to experimental value of 9.9±2.4
- 54Fe S+ (BR interaction, t=5 calculation): 5.5 Gamow-Teller units (unquenched)
- 54Fe S+ (KB3 interaction, t=5 calculation): approximately 6.0 Gamow-Teller units (unquenched, extrapolated)
- Maximum m-scheme dimension for parent states: 1.4 × 10^7; for daughter states after sigma-tau operator: 4.1 × 10^7
- Energy gain from t=4 to t=5 in 54Fe: 270 keV

## Context

This work extends previous shell model calculations by Bloom and Fuller (1985), Muto (1992), and Auerbach et al. (1994) by using larger truncation spaces and comparing two different interactions (KB3 and BR). It directly compares with Shell Model Monte Carlo calculations by Alhassid et al. (1994) and Dean et al. (1995), finding that SMMC results with the BR interaction initially underestimated S+ values but later converged to agreement with finer Monte Carlo steps. The analysis of strength functions follows the approach of Caurier, Poves, and Zuker (1994) for 48Ca, demonstrating that the standard quenching factor of 0.77 accounts for strength within the 0-hbar-omega model space while intruder states carry the remaining strength needed to satisfy the 3(N-Z) sum rule.
