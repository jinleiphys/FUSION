# PHENIX results on three-particle Bose-Einstein correlations in $\sqrt{S_{NN}} = 200$ GeV Au+Au collisions

> Bose-Einstein correlations of identical hadrons reveal information about hadron creation from the strongly interacting matter formed in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. The measurement of three-particle correlations may in particular shed light on hadron creation mechanisms beyond thermal/chaotic emission. In this paper we show the status of PHENIX measurements of three pion correlations as a function of momentum differences within the triplets. We analyze the shape of the correlation functions through the assumption of Lévy sources and a proper treatment of the Coulomb interaction within the triplets. We measure the three-particle correlation strength ($λ_3$), which, together with the two-particle correlation strength $λ_2$, encodes information about hadron creation mechanisms. From a consistent analysis of two- and three-particle correlation strength we establish a new experimental measure of thermalization and coherence in the source.

## Key claim
The paper reports preliminary PHENIX measurements of three-pion Bose-Einstein correlations in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV per nucleon, finding that the core-halo independent parameter κ₃ deviates from unity near m_T ≈ 450 MeV, which may indicate partial coherence in the source, but the authors caution that the results are preliminary and a definitive conclusion requires further analysis.

## Method
Three-particle correlation functions were measured for π⁻π⁻π⁻ and π⁺π⁺π⁺ triplets from 7.3 billion minimum-bias Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV recorded by the PHENIX detector in 2010. The correlation functions were analyzed assuming Lévy-shaped source distributions with the Lévy scale R and Lévy index α fixed to values from preliminary two-particle correlation fits in the same transverse momentum bins, and Coulomb interactions were treated using the Generalized Riverside method. The three-particle correlation strength λ₃ was extracted from fits, and a new parameter κ₃ = (λ₃ − 3λ₂) / (2√(λ₂³)) was constructed to test for partial coherence independently of the core fraction in the Core-Halo model.

## Key numbers
- 7.3 billion minimum-bias Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV were used for the analysis.
- Three-particle correlation functions were measured in 31 m_T bins ranging from 228 to 871 MeV.
- The relative systematic error from the Generalized Riverside Coulomb approximation is less than 2%.
- λ₃ falls within the range [0, 5] as predicted by the Core-Halo model.
- κ₃ is observed to be different from unity at m_T ≈ 450 MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous PHENIX two-pion correlation measurements (Kincses 2016) by adding three-particle correlations to probe hadron creation mechanisms beyond thermal/chaotic emission. The analysis tests the Core-Halo model (Bolz 1992, Csörgő 1994, Csanád 2005) with a thermalized core, and the new κ₃ parameter provides a consistency check between two- and three-particle correlation strengths that is independent of the core fraction. The results enable future investigations of partial coherence in the particle-emitting source, though the authors note that a full systematic analysis is still in progress.
