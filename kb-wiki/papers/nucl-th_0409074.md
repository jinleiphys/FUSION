# Disentangling spatial and flow anisotropy

> Two generalisations of the blast-wave model to non-central nuclear collisions are constructed, and elliptic flow as well as azimuthal dependence of correlation radii are calculated. Particular attention is paid to how different azimuthal dependences of transverse flow direction can cause qualitatively different anisotropic fireballs to give same v_2 as a function of the transverse momentum. The simultaneous dependence of v_2 and the oscillation of correlation radii on both spatial and flow anisotropy is studied in great detail.

## Key claim

Two different blast-wave model generalizations for non-central nuclear collisions can produce identical elliptic flow \(v_2\) as a function of transverse momentum, even when one fireball is elongated in-plane and the other out-of-plane, demonstrating that \(v_2\) alone cannot distinguish spatial from flow anisotropy.

## Method

Two generalizations of the blast-wave model were constructed for non-central collisions, differing in the azimuthal dependence of the transverse flow velocity: Model 1 has flow perpendicular to the surface of constant density, while Model 2 has radially directed flow. Elliptic flow \(v_2\) and azimuthally sensitive HBT correlation radii were calculated analytically for both models using the Cooper-Frye formalism with a boost-invariant Bjorken expansion, ellipsoidal transverse density profile, and Boltzmann distribution at freeze-out temperature \(T\).

## Key numbers

- \(v_2\) calculated for pions and protons at \(\pt = 200\ \text{MeV}/c\) and \(\pt = 500\ \text{MeV}/c\), with \(T = 100\ \text{MeV}\) and \(\rho_0 = 0.88\), showing contour plots of \(v_2\) as function of spatial anisotropy \(a\) and flow anisotropy \(\rho_2\)
- Normalized oscillation amplitudes \(R_{o,2}^2/R_{o,0}^2\) and \(R_{s,2}^2/R_{s,0}^2\) computed at \(K_t = 300\ \text{MeV}/c\) and \(K_t = 900\ \text{MeV}/c\) for both models, with parameters \(T = 0.1\ \text{GeV}\), \(\rho_0 = 0.88\), \(R = 9.41\ \text{fm}\), \(\tau_0 = 9\ \text{fm}/c\), \(\Delta\tau = 1\ \text{fm}/c\)
- Comparison with STAR data at \(\sqrt{s}=200\ A\text{GeV}\) for 20-30% centrality Au+Au collisions used parameters \(T = 0.12\ \text{GeV}\), \(\rho_0 = 0.99\), \(\rho_2 = 0.035\), \(R = 9.41\ \text{fm}\), \(\tau_0 = 5.02\ \text{fm}/c\), \(\Delta\tau = 2.90\ \text{fm}/c\), with spatial anisotropy \(a = 0.946\) (Model 1) or \(a = 1.057\) (Model 2)

## Context

This work extends the blast-wave model of Csorgo and Lorstad (1995) and the generalization by Retiere and Lisa (2004) to systematically study the entanglement of spatial and flow anisotropy in non-central collisions. It analytically demonstrates a conjecture by the STAR Collaboration (2003) that \(v_2\) alone cannot distinguish in-plane from out-of-plane elongation, and confirms the earlier hydrodynamic finding by Kolb, Huovinen, and Ruuskanen (2001) that azimuthal HBT radii are primarily sensitive to spatial anisotropy at low transverse momentum. The results enable experimental disentanglement of spatial and flow anisotropy by combining \(v_2\) measurements for multiple identified particle species with azimuthally sensitive HBT data, provided the correct flow model is identified.
