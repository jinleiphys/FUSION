# Non-exponential decay in quantum field theory and in quantum mechanics: the case of two (or more) decay channels

> We study the deviations from the exponential decay law, both in quantum field theory (QFT) and quantum mechanics (QM), for an unstable particle which can decay in (at least) two decay channels. After a review of general properties of non-exponential decay in QFT and QM, we evaluate in both cases the decay probability that the unstable particle decays in a given channel in the time interval between $t$ and $t+dt.$ An important quantity is the ratio of the probability of decay into the first and the second channel: this ratio is constant in the Breit-Wigner limit (in which the decay law is exponential) and equals the quantity $Γ_{1}/Γ_{2}$, where $Γ_{1}$ and $Γ_{2}$ are the respective tree-level decay widths. However, in the full treatment (both for QFT and QM) it is an oscillating function around the mean value $Γ_{1}/Γ_{2}$ and the deviations from this mean value can be sizable. Technically, we study the decay properties in QFT in the context of a superrenormalizable Lagrangian with scalar particles and in QM in the context of Lee Hamiltonians, which deliver formally analogous expressions to the QFT case.

## Key claim

The ratio of decay probabilities into two channels, which is constant and equal to the tree-level width ratio Γ₁/Γ₂ in the exponential (Breit-Wigner) limit, becomes an oscillating function around that mean value in the full treatment, with deviations that can be sizable; this holds both in quantum field theory and in quantum mechanics, and the paper presents the first calculation of the partial decay probability into each channel as a function of time.

## Method

The author studies a superrenormalizable Lagrangian with three scalar fields (S, φ₁, φ₂) where S decays into φ₁φ₁ and φ₂φ₂, and evaluates the survival probability p(t) and decay probability density h(t) from the Fourier transform of the spectral function d_S(x) obtained from the resummed one-loop propagator. In parallel, the same quantities are derived using Lee Hamiltonians in quantum mechanics, which yield formally analogous expressions. For the two-channel case, the partial decay probability into each channel is computed, and a conjectured form of the solution in QFT is tested against exact results from the QM Lee Hamiltonian approach.

## Key numbers

- Tree-level decay widths for the numerical example: Γ₁ = 0.13 [2m₂], Γ₂ = 0.089 [2m₂], total Γ = 0.22 [2m₂], giving tree-level lifetime τ_BW = 4.63 [2m₂]⁻¹.
- Renormalized mass M = 1.40 [2m₂] from input M₀ = 1.5 [2m₂] with cutoff Λ = 2 [2m₂].
- The time of maximal deviation from exponential decay τ_M = 1.18 [2m₂]⁻¹, yielding τ_M/τ_BW = 0.26, meaning non-exponential behavior lasts about 50% of the tree-level lifetime.
- The spectral function d_S(x) scales as x⁻³ for large x, ensuring finiteness of the mean energy ⟨x⟩.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of non-exponential decay in quantum field theory (Zeno et al., 2010; Giacosa, 2010) and in quantum mechanics using Lee Hamiltonians (Facchi et al., 2001) from single-channel decays to the case of two (or more) decay channels. The paper provides a general formalism that can be applied to any unstable particle with multiple decay modes, and the numerical results show that small deviations of the spectral function from the Breit-Wigner form produce large temporal deviations, enabling future experimental searches for non-exponential decay signatures in systems with multiple decay channels.
