# High strangeness dibaryons in the extended quark delocalization, color screening model

> Dibaryon candidates with strangeness S=-2,-3,-4,-5,-6 are studied in terms of the extended quark delocalization and color screening model. The results show that there are only a few promising low lying dibaryon states: The H and di-Omega may be marginally strong interaction stable but model uncertainties are too large to allow any definitive statement. The SIJ=-3,1/2,2 N-Omega state is 62 MeV lower than the N-Omega threshold and 24 MeV lower than the Lambda-Xi-pi threshold. It might appear as a narrow dibaryon resonance and be detectable in the RHIC detector through the reconstruction of the vertex mass of the Lambda-Xi two body decay. The effects of explicit K and eta meson exchange have been studied and found to be negligible in this model. The mechanisms of effective intermediate range attraction, sigma meson exchange and kinetic energy reduction due to quark delocalization are discussed.

## Key claim

The extended quark delocalization and color screening model (QDCSM) finds that only a few high-strangeness dibaryon states are promising candidates, but model uncertainties are too large to definitively claim strong-interaction stability for the H particle or the di-Omega; the N-Omega state with strangeness -3, isospin 1/2, and spin 2 is predicted to be a narrow dibaryon resonance rather than a strong-interaction-stable bound state.

## Method

The authors used the extended quark delocalization and color screening model (QDCSM) with a Hamiltonian including confinement, one-gluon exchange, and pion exchange with a short-range cutoff, solving the six-quark system via the resonating group method (RGM) with Gaussian basis functions. They performed single-channel and coupled-channel calculations for dibaryon candidates with strangeness S = -2, -3, -4, -5, and -6, and separately studied the effects of adding explicit K and eta meson exchange terms.

## Key numbers

- The SIJ = -3, 1/2, 2 N-Omega state has a coupled-channel mass of 2549.1 MeV, which is 62 MeV below the N-Omega threshold and 24 MeV below the Lambda-Xi-pi threshold.
- The H particle (S = -2, I = 0, J = 0) coupled-channel mass is 2225.5 MeV, which is 6 MeV below the experimental Lambda-Lambda threshold and 1.7 MeV below the model threshold.
- The di-Omega (S = -6, I = 0, J = 0) mass is 3298.2 MeV, about 47 MeV below the experimental Omega-Omega threshold but no more than 2 MeV below the model threshold using the model Omega mass of 1650.1 MeV.
- The SIJ = -5, 1/2, 0 Xi*-Omega state mass is 3145.0 MeV, which is 59 MeV below the experimental Xi*-Omega threshold but only about 2 MeV below the model threshold.
- Adding K and eta meson exchange changes the masses of bound states by no more than 10 MeV; the H particle mass shifts from 2225.48 MeV to 2230.28 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the QDCSM previously applied to the d* dibaryon (Ping et al., 1990s) and the di-Omega (Pang et al., 2003) to higher strangeness systems S = -2 through -5, providing a reference spectrum for strange dibaryons. The results contrast with the chiral quark model (Zhang et al., 1990s) which predicts stronger binding in high-strangeness channels due to universal sigma meson exchange, a mechanism the authors argue is not justified for strange systems. The predicted narrow N-Omega resonance could be tested at RHIC through reconstruction of the Lambda-Xi vertex mass, and the findings enable experimental searches for dibaryons in relativistic heavy ion collisions.
