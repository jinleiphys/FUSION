# Shape coexistence close to $N=50$ in the neutron-rich isotope $^{80}$Ge investigated by IBM-2

> The properties of the low-lying states, especially the relevant shape coexistence in $^{80}$Ge close to one of most neutron-rich doubly magic nuclei at $N=50$ and $Z=28$ have been investigated within the framework of the proton-neutron interacting model (IBM-2). Based on the fact that the relative energy of the $d$ neutron boson is different from proton bosons', the calculated energy levels of low-lying states, $E2$ transition strengths can reproduce the experimental data very well. Particularly, the first excited state 0$^+_2$ is reproduced quite nicely, which is intimately related with the shape coexistence phenomenon. And the $ρ^2(E0, 0^+_2\rightarrow0^+_1)$ transition strength has been predicted. The experimental data and theoretical results indicate that both collective spherical and $γ$-soft vibration structures coexist in $^{80}$Ge. Key words: $^{80}$Ge, low-lying states, $E0$ transition strengths, shape coexistence, IBM-2.

## Key claim
The paper claims that shape coexistence in \(^{80}\)Ge can be described by the proton-neutron interacting boson model (IBM-2) without explicit configuration mixing, provided that the relative energy of the \(d\) neutron boson is taken to be different from that of the proton boson. The authors hedge that the calculated \(0^+_2\) state is "almost equal" to the experimental value, but the theoretical prediction of the \(2^+_2\) state is lower than the experimental value.

## Method
The authors used the IBM-2 Hamiltonian with separate \(d\)-boson energies for protons (\(\varepsilon_{d\pi}\)) and neutrons (\(\varepsilon_{d\nu}\)), a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction between proton and neutron bosons, a dipole proton-proton interaction, and a Majorana interaction. The model parameters were fitted to reproduce the experimental low-lying energy levels and \(B(E2)\) transition strengths of \(^{80}\)Ge, using the doubly magic nucleus \(^{78}\)Ni as an inert core. The Hamiltonian was numerically diagonalized with the NPBOS code, and the resulting wave functions were used to compute \(E2\) and \(E0\) transition strengths.

## Key numbers
- The experimental energy of the first excited \(0^+_2\) state is 639(1) keV; the calculated value is almost equal to this, and it lies below the \(2^+_1\) state.
- The experimental energy ratio \(R_{4/2} = E(4^+_1)/E(2^+_1)\) is 2.64 for \(^{80}\)Ge; the calculated value is 2.68.
- The experimental \(B(E2, 2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1)\) is 200(26) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\); the calculated value is 200.0 \(e^2\)fm\(^4\).
- The experimental \(B(E2, 2^+_2 \rightarrow 0^+_1)\) is 23(7) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\); the calculated value is 21.3 \(e^2\)fm\(^4\).
- The calculated \(\rho^2(E0, 0^+_2 \rightarrow 0^+_1)\) transition strength is 0.001 (dimensionless).
- The fitted IBM-2 parameters include: \(\varepsilon_{d\pi} = 0.315\) MeV, \(\varepsilon_{d\nu} = 1.080\) MeV, \(\kappa = -0.150\) MeV, \(\chi_\pi = -1.200\), \(\chi_\nu = 0.900\), \(\omega_{\pi\pi} = 0.063\) MeV, and \(\lambda_1 = \lambda_3 = 0.800\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous IBM-1 calculations for Ge isotopes by Hsieh (1987) and Duval (1984), which did not distinguish proton and neutron bosons, and by Nomura (2017), whose IBM-1 calculation gave higher energies for the \(0^+_2\) and \(2^+_2\) states in \(^{80}\)Ge. The study also builds on the experimental observation of an \(E0\) transition in \(^{80}\)Ge by Gottardo (2011), which pointed to an intruder \(0^+_2\) state at 639 keV. The results enable future theoretical and experimental work to further probe the \(0^+_2\) state and its \(E2\) and \(E0\) decays, which are currently scarce.
