# Exclusive $Υ$ photoproduction in hadronic collisions at CERN LHC energies

> The exclusive $Υ$ photoproduction in proton-proton and proton - nucleus collisions at LHC energies is investigated using the color dipole formalism and considering different models for the $Υ$ wave function and forward dipole - target scattering amplitude. Our goal is to update the color dipole predictions and estimate the theoretical uncertainty present in these predictions. We present predictions for the kinematical ranges probed by the ALICE, CMS and LHCb Collaborations.

## Key claim

The color dipole formalism predictions for exclusive Upsilon photoproduction at LHC energies carry a large theoretical uncertainty, with different models for the Upsilon wave function and dipole-proton scattering amplitude yielding cross sections that differ by factors up to 3.5, meaning that future measurements can constrain both the nonlinear QCD dynamics and the vector meson wave function models.

## Method

The authors used the color dipole formalism to compute exclusive Upsilon photoproduction cross sections in gamma p, pp, and pPb collisions. They considered two models for the Upsilon wave function (Boosted Gaussian and Gauss-LC) and three models for the forward dipole-proton scattering amplitude (bCGC, GBW, and GBW-KSX), and also compared two treatments of the t-dependence: a full integration over t and an exponential approximation with a slope parameter. The rapidity distributions were obtained by folding the gamma p cross section with the equivalent photon fluxes of protons and nuclei.

## Key numbers

* For pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV, total cross sections range from 91.0 pb (bCGC full, Gauss-LC) to 363.0 pb (GBW KSX, Boosted Gaussian).
* For pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV, total cross sections range from 167.0 pb (bCGC full, Gauss-LC) to 740.0 pb (GBW KSX, Boosted Gaussian).
* For pPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5 TeV, total cross sections range from 31.3 nb (bCGC full, Gauss-LC) to 105.5 nb (GBW KSX, Boosted Gaussian).
* In the LHCb kinematical range (2 <= Y <= 4.5) for pp at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV, cross sections range from 21.0 pb (bCGC full, Gauss-LC) to 91.0 pb (GBW KSX, Boosted Gaussian).
* The GBW and bCGC predictions differ by a factor of 2.7 at Y = 0 and 3.5 at Y = 4 for pp at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV.
* The Boosted Gaussian predictions are approximately 12% larger than the Gauss-LC ones at Y = 0 for pp collisions.

## Context

This work updates and compares previous color dipole predictions for exclusive Upsilon photoproduction, which had been studied by several groups including Vicmag (2010), Motyka and Watt (2008), and Cox and Forshaw (2008) using different assumptions. The results extend the approach previously applied to J/Psi and rho production by Bruno (2013) and Glauber (2012), respectively. The paper provides predictions for the kinematical ranges of the ALICE, CMS, and LHCb Collaborations, and shows that the bCGC predictions are similar to NLO calculations by Martin (2010), while GBW predictions resemble LO fits.
