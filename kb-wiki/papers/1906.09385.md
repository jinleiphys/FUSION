# Local spin polarization in high energy heavy ion collisions

> We revisit the azimuthal angle dependence of the local spin polarization of hyperons in heavy-ion collisions at 200 GeV in the framework of the (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic model CLVisc. Two different initial conditions are considered in our simulation: the optical Glauber initial condition without initial orbital angular momentum and the AMPT initial condition with an initial orbital angular momentum. We find that the azimuthal angle dependence of the hyperon polarization strongly depends on the choice of the so-called "spin chemical potential" $Ω_{μν}$. With $Ω_{μν}$ chosen to be proportional to the temperature vorticity, our simulation shows qualitatively coincidental results with the recent measurements at RHIC for both the longitudinal and transverse polarization. We argue that such a coincidence may be related to the fact that the temperature vorticity is approximately conserved in the hot quark-gluon matter.

## Key claim

The azimuthal angle dependence of hyperon polarization in heavy-ion collisions at 200 GeV strongly depends on the choice of the spin chemical potential; only when the spin chemical potential is taken proportional to the temperature vorticity does the simulation show qualitatively coincidental results with RHIC measurements for both longitudinal and transverse polarization, which may be related to the approximate conservation of temperature vorticity in hot quark-gluon matter.

## Method

The authors used the (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic model CLVisc with two initial conditions (optical Glauber without initial orbital angular momentum and AMPT with initial orbital angular momentum) to compute four types of vorticity fields on the freeze-out hyper-surface. They assumed the spin chemical potential could be constructed from the fluid velocity and temperature as one of four dimensionless vorticities: kinematic vorticity divided by T, temperature vorticity divided by T^2, thermal vorticity, or NR vorticity divided by T. The average spin vector for Lambda hyperons was then calculated by replacing the thermal vorticity in the standard formula with each of these spin chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- Longitudinal polarization from T-vorticity shows sign pattern (+, -, +, -) from first to fourth quadrants in the (px, py) plane, consistent with STAR data at 200 GeV in 20-50% centrality Au+Au collisions
- For the AMPT initial condition with pT in [0, 1.2] GeV, the magnitude of longitudinal polarization matches the data; for pT in [0, 3] GeV, the magnitude is one order of magnitude larger than the data
- Transverse polarization Py from T-vorticity matches the data at phi_p = 0, but at phi_p = pi/2 the result is approximately 0.25 while the data approach zero
- Global polarization data from STAR shows a decreasing trend from about 2% at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV to about 0.3% at 200 GeV

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic calculations of hyperon polarization by Becattini (2017), Xia (2018), and Wei (2018) which could not describe the azimuthal angle dependence of STAR data, showing a sign difference between theory and experiment. The authors argue that the spin degree of freedom may not reach equilibrium in the fireball, so the spin chemical potential becomes a free parameter rather than being determined by thermal vorticity. This motivates future development of spin hydrodynamics as developed by Florkowski (2017, 2018) and Hattori (2019), and spin kinetic theory approaches.
