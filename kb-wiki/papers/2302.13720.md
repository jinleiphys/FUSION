# Isolated photon production in $pp$ collisions at forward rapidities and high multiplicity events

> The production of isolated photons in high multiplicity events is investigated considering the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism. The associated cross-section for proton - proton collisions is estimated considering three distinct solutions of the Balitsky - Kovchegov (BK) equation and predictions for the normalized photon yield as a function of the multiplicities of co - produced charged particles are presented. We predict the increasing of the yield with the multiplicity, with the slope being smaller for larger rapidities. As the isolated photon production is not affected by the fragmentation process, a future experimental investigation of this process in current high energy hadronic colliders is ideal to test the treatment of high multiplicity events using the CGC formalism, previously applied only for the production of hadronic final states.

## Key claim

The authors predict that the normalized isolated photon yield in proton-proton collisions increases with event multiplicity, with the slope of this increase being smaller at larger photon rapidities; this prediction is conditional on the assumption that high-multiplicity events can be modeled by scaling the initial saturation scale in the Color Glass Condensate formalism.

## Method

The isolated photon production cross section was calculated using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism, treating the process as photon bremsstrahlung off a fast projectile quark propagating through the low-x color field of the target. Three distinct solutions of the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation were used to describe the dipole-target scattering amplitude: one with a GBW-type initial condition and two with MV-type initial conditions. The normalized photon yield as a function of multiplicity was obtained by scaling the initial saturation scale \(Q_{s,0}^2\) by a factor \(n\) to represent high-multiplicity configurations, and the correlation between normalized photon and charged hadron yields was computed.

## Key numbers

- The three BK solutions used initial saturation scales: \(Q_{s,0}^2 = 0.24\) GeV\(^2\) (GBW), \(Q_{s,0}^2 = 0.157\) GeV\(^2\) with \(\gamma = 1.101\) (MV), and \(Q_{s,0}^2 = 0.1597\) GeV\(^2\) with \(\gamma = 1.118\) (MV).
- Predictions for the normalized photon yield were computed for \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 13\) TeV, integrating photon transverse momentum in the range \(4.0 \le k_T \le 12.0\) GeV.
- The correlation between normalized photon and charged hadron yields was shown for photon rapidities \(y = 2, 4, 6\).
- The factorization scale was varied in the range \(0.25 Q^2 \le \mu^2 \le 4 Q^2\), with \(Q^2 = \max\{\mathbf{l}_T^2, \mathbf{k}_T^2\}\).
- The isolation cone radius was varied between \(R = 0.1\) and \(R = 0.4\), with negligible effect on the normalized yield.

## Context

This work extends the CGC-based treatment of high-multiplicity events, previously applied only to hadronic final states such as \(J/\Psi\), \(D\), \(K_S^0\), and \(\Lambda\) mesons (Ma 2018, Levin 2019, Kopeliovich 2019, Gotsman 2020, Siddikov 2020, Stebel 2021, Salazar 2021, Lima 2022), to the production of isolated photons. Because isolated photons are not affected by fragmentation or hadronization, the predictions provide a clean test of the CGC formalism's description of initial-state effects in high-multiplicity events, potentially disentangling initial- and final-state contributions that complicate interpretations of hadron data. The results motivate experimental analysis at the LHC, particularly at forward rapidities where non-linear effects are expected to be strongest.
