# Anti-bubble effect of temperature \& deformation: a systematic study for nuclei across all mass regions between A $=$ 20$-$300

> Temperature dependent relativistic mean-field (RMF) plus BCS approach has been used for the first time to investigate the anti-bubble effect of the temperature and deformation in the light, medium-heavy and superheavy nuclei. Influence of temperature is studied on density distribution, charge form-factor, single particle (s.p.) energies, occupancy, deformation and the depletion fraction (DF). At T $=$ 0, the quenching effect of deformation is predominant. DF is found usually less in oblate deformation than in prolate. DF decreases with increasing prolate deformation even though the 2s-orbit is empty which shows the role of deformation in central depletion apart from the unoccupancy in s-orbit as is usually believed. As T increases, the occupancy of s-orbit increases, shell structure melts, the deformation vanishes and the weakening of central depletion is solely due to the temperature. The bubble effect is eliminated at T $\approx$ 3$-$5 MeV as indicated by DF and the charge form factor. The temperature effect is found less prominent in superheavy bubble nuclei where the role of shell effects is indicated.

## Key claim
Temperature and deformation both act to quench the bubble structure in nuclei, with the bubble effect eliminated at a critical temperature T ≈ 3–5 MeV for light and medium-mass nuclei; however, in superheavy bubble nuclei the temperature effect is less prominent and shell effects play a subtle role.

## Method
The authors used a temperature-dependent relativistic mean-field (RMF) plus state-dependent Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) approach, solving the Dirac equations for nucleons and Klein-Gordon equations for mesons by expansion on an axially deformed harmonic-oscillator basis. They computed charge density, depletion fraction (DF), charge form factor, single-particle energies, occupancy, and deformation as functions of temperature for nuclei across mass regions A = 20–300, using TMA, NL3*, and DD-ME2 parameter sets. Quadrupole constrained calculations were performed to obtain potential energy surfaces and ground-state deformations.

## Key numbers
- The bubble effect is eliminated at a critical temperature T_c ≈ 3–5 MeV for light and medium-mass nuclei.
- For ³⁴Si, the critical temperature T_c = 4 MeV, in agreement with Ref. [TAN].
- For ²²O and ²²Si, the critical temperature for washing out the bubble effect is around 3 MeV.
- In ⁴⁶Ar, DF decreases from 0.39 at T = 0 to 0.08 at T = 3 MeV and to 0.008 at T = 5 MeV.
- For ¹⁸⁴Ce, DF has not vanished at T = 5 MeV, indicating T_c > 5 MeV.
- At T = 0, DF in spherical ⁴⁶Ar is 0.5, while in deformed ⁴⁴S DF ≈ 0.37 at β = 0.27.
- For Si isotopes, spherical ³⁴,³⁶,⁴⁸Si show the highest DF (0.25–0.3).
- Deformation β values at T = 0 for deformed nuclei: ²⁴Ne β = 0.22 (RMF), ⁴⁰Mg β = 0.45, ⁴⁴S β = 0.27, ³²Ar β = 0.21.

## Context
This work extends the study of temperature effects on bubble nuclei beyond the spherical light nuclei ³⁴Si and ²²O examined by Ref. [TAN] using Skyrme Hartree-Fock, providing the first systematic investigation across all mass regions including deformed and superheavy nuclei. It builds on prior work showing that pairing correlations and dynamical quadrupole shape effects (Wu et al.) quench but do not eliminate the bubble effect, while here temperature is shown to completely erase it at a critical value. The results enable experimental identification of bubble nuclei via charge form factor measurements at facilities such as SCRIT and RIBF, and call for further theoretical studies using other models.
