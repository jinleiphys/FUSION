# Nuclear energy density functional from chiral pion-nucleon dynamics: Isovector spin-orbit terms

> We extend a recent calculation of the nuclear energy density functional in the systematic framework of chiral perturbation theory by computing the isovector spin-orbit terms: $(\vec \nabla ρ_p- \vec \nabla ρ_n)\cdot(\vec J_p-\vec J_n) G_{so}(k_f)+ (\vec J_p-\vec J_n)^2 G_J(k_f)$. The calculation includes the one-pion exchange Fock diagram and the iterated one-pion exchange Hartree and Fock diagrams. From these few leading order contributions in the small momentum expansion one obtains already a good equation of state of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter. We find that the parameterfree results for the (density-dependent) strength functions $G_{so}(k_f)$ and $G_J(k_f)$ agree fairly well with that of phenomenological Skyrme forces for densities $ρ> ρ_0/10$. At very low densities a strong variation of the strength functions $G_{so}(k_f)$ and $G_J(k_f)$ with density sets in. This has to do with chiral singularities $m_π^{-1}$ and the presence of two competing small mass scales $k_f$ and $m_π$. The novel density dependencies of $G_{so}(k_f)$ and $G_J(k_f)$ as predicted by our parameterfree (leading order) calculation should be examined in nuclear structure calculations.

# Nuclear energy density functional from chiral pion-nucleon dynamics: Isovector spin-orbit terms

## Key claim

The parameter-free, leading-order chiral perturbation theory calculation of the isovector spin-orbit strength functions \(G_{so}(k_f)\) and \(G_J(k_f)\) agrees fairly well with phenomenological Skyrme forces for densities \(\rho > \rho_0/10\), but at very low densities a strong variation sets in due to chiral singularities \(m_\pi^{-1}\) and the competition between the two small mass scales \(k_f\) and \(m_\pi\).

## Method

The calculation extends a previous chiral perturbation theory treatment of the nuclear energy density functional to the isovector spin-orbit sector, using the density-matrix expansion of Negele and Vautherin to treat inhomogeneous systems. The one-pion exchange Fock diagram and the iterated one-pion exchange Hartree and Fock diagrams are evaluated analytically in momentum space, with the medium insertion expanded to quadratic order in proton-neutron differences. The resulting strength functions \(G_{so}(k_f)\) and \(G_J(k_f)\) are expressed in terms of the dimensionless variable \(u = k_f/m_\pi\) and computed using physical constants \(M = 939\) MeV, \(m_\pi = 135\) MeV, \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV, and \(g_A = 1.3\).

## Key numbers

- The zero-density limit of the isovector spin-orbit strength is \(G_{so}(0) = -33.8\) MeV fm\(^5\), arising from the iterated one-pion exchange diagrams.
- The zero-density limit of the squared isovector spin-orbit strength is \(G_J(0) = -108.0\) MeV fm\(^5\), to which the one-pion exchange Fock diagram contributes the most singular \(m_\pi^{-2}\) term.
- The saturation density of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter used for comparison is \(\rho_0 = 0.174\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The empirical saturation point reproduced in prior work is \(\rho_0 \simeq 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\) with binding energy per particle \(\bar E(k_{f0}) \simeq -15.3\) MeV and compressibility \(K \simeq 255\) MeV.
- The asymmetry energy predicted in prior work is \(A(k_{f0}) = 33.8\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory calculation of the nuclear energy density functional by Kaiser (2003) from the isoscalar case to the isovector spin-orbit terms, using the same density-matrix expansion framework of Negele and Vautherin (1972). The results are compared to phenomenological Skyrme forces such as Sly, MSk, and SkP, which treat the isovector spin-orbit strengths as constants \(G_{so} = W_0/4\) and \(G_J = (t_1 - t_2)/32\); the present calculation reveals a nontrivial density dependence that should be tested in nuclear structure calculations. The isoscalar spin-orbit interaction from iterated one-pion exchange was previously found to have the wrong sign compared to experiment, whereas the isovector spin-orbit interaction now gives the correct positive sign, highlighting the need for additional short-range dynamics (e.g., from relativistic mean-field models as in Walecka (1974) and Ring (1996)) to fully describe the isoscalar channel.
