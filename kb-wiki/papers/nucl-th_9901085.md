# The Collective Excitation Spectra of $σ, ω$ and $π$ Mesons in Nuclear Matter

> The recent progress on the study of the collective excitation in relativistic nuclear matter is reviewed. The collective excitation modes are derived by meson propagators in nuclear matter. The mesons we study are $σ, ω, γ$ and $π$ mesons. For pion, we derived not only the relativistic particle - hole, delta - hole excitations but also antiparticle excitations, such as particle - antiparticle, antidelta - particle, delta - antiparticle excitations. By calculating the dispersion relation and the spin - isospin dependent response function, the effects of all these excitation are studied.

## Key claim

The paper reviews a relativistic formalism for deriving collective excitation spectra of σ, ω, and π mesons in nuclear matter, claiming that previous calculations using "Feynman" and "density-dependent" nucleon propagators incorrectly mix particle-hole and particle-antiparticle excitations and violate the Pauli principle. The author asserts that their approach is the first to correctly separate relativistic particle-hole, delta-hole, and antiparticle excitations (such as particle-antiparticle, antidelta-particle, and delta-antiparticle) in a unified framework.

## Method

The collective excitation modes are derived by calculating the poles of meson propagators in nuclear matter, using a decomposition of the nucleon propagator into separate particle, hole, and antiparticle propagators rather than the conventional Feynman-plus-density-dependent decomposition. For the σ and ω mesons, the dimesonic function ε(q) is constructed from the longitudinal and transverse polarization insertions, including σ-ω mixing, and the eigencondition ε(ω,q)=0 determines the collective excitation spectra. For the pion, the polarization insertions for relativistic particle-hole (ph), delta-hole (Δh), and antiparticle excitations (N̄N, Δ̄N, ΔN̄) are derived separately using pseudo-vector πNN and πNΔ couplings, with a form factor correction applied to renormalize divergent antiparticle contributions.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: k_F = 1.42 fm⁻¹
- In the mean field approximation (MFA): effective nucleon mass ratio m̃_N/m_N = 0.56, with coupling constants g_σ = 9.59 and g_ω = 11.7
- In the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA): effective nucleon mass ratio m̃_N/m_N = 0.72, with coupling constants g_σ = 7.93 and g_ω = 8.94
- σ meson mass: m_σ = 550 MeV; ω meson mass: m_ω = 783 MeV; nucleon mass: 938.5 MeV
- Pseudo-vector πNN coupling constant: f_πNN = 0.988; πNΔ coupling constant: f_πNΔ = 2f_πNN
- Landau-Migdal parameter g′ = 0.6 used in the calculation
- Form factor cutoff parameter: Λ_π = 1200 MeV
- For the response function calculation: target density ρ = 0.17 fm⁻³, T_³He = 2.0 GeV, with resonance energy at about 260 MeV
- For fixed momentum q = 2.5k_F, the imaginary part of antiparticle excitations does not vanish for any energy above threshold

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic studies of particle-hole and delta-hole excitations (Oset, 1982; Helgesson, 1995) to a fully relativistic formalism, and corrects earlier relativistic treatments by Chin (1977) that used Feynman and density-dependent propagators, which the author argues produce incorrect polarization insertions that violate the Pauli principle. The formalism enables microscopic calculation of pion dispersion relations and spin-isospin dependent response functions relevant to dilepton production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions (Xia, 1995; Chanfray, 1993) and charge-exchange reactions such as (³He,t) (Dmitriev, 1985; Dmitriev, 1993). The author notes that the vanishing derivative of the pion dispersion relation may lead to enhancement of dilepton production rates, and that antiparticle excitations significantly affect the response function at resonance energies around 260 MeV.
