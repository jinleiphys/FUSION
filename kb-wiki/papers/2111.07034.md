# Near Threshold Heavy Quarkonium Photoproduction at Large Momentum Transfer

> Perturbative QCD is applied to investigate the near threshold heavy quarkonium photoproduction at large momentum transfer. We take into account the contributions from the leading three-quark Fock states of the nucleon. The dominant contribution comes from the three-quark Fock state with one unit quark orbital angular momentum (OAM) whereas that from zero quark OAM is suppressed at the threshold. From our analysis, we also show that there is no direct connection between the near threshold heavy quarkonium photoproduction and the gluonic gravitational form factors of the nucleon. Based on the comparison between our result and recent GlueX data of $J/ψ$ photoproduction, we make predictions for $ψ'$ and $Υ$ (1S,2S) states which can be tested in future experiments.

## Key claim

The authors argue that near threshold heavy quarkonium photoproduction at large momentum transfer has no direct connection to the gluonic gravitational form factors of the nucleon, contrary to claims in prior literature. The dominant contribution at threshold comes from the three-quark Fock state with one unit quark orbital angular momentum (OAM), while the contribution from zero quark OAM is suppressed by a factor of (1-χ) and vanishes at the exact threshold.

## Method

The authors apply perturbative QCD factorization to compute the scattering amplitude for γ + N → V + N' near threshold, where the momentum transfer is large (-t ~ 2 GeV² for J/ψ and ~10 GeV² for Υ). They consider contributions from the leading three-quark Fock states of the nucleon, classified by zero and one unit of quark orbital angular momentum, using nucleon distribution amplitudes at twist-three and twist-four. The heavy quarkonium is treated in non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD), and the amplitude is expanded in the threshold parameter χ = (M_V² + 2M_p M_V)/(W_γp² - M_p²), which approaches 1 at threshold.

## Key numbers

- For J/ψ photoproduction near threshold, the momentum transfer is -t ~ 2 GeV²; for Υ, -t ~ 10 GeV².
- The differential cross section from the twist-three (zero OAM) contribution scales as 1/(-t)⁴ but is suppressed by a factor of (1-χ) at threshold.
- The dominant twist-four (one unit OAM) contribution scales as 1/(-t)⁵ and does not vanish at threshold.
- The color factor for the partonic scattering amplitudes is C_B² = (2/3)².
- The normalization factor N_ψ for J/ψ is given by N_ψ = -4 e_c e g_s² / √(N_c M_V³) ψ_J(0), where ψ_J(0) is the J/ψ wavefunction at the origin.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD treatment of exclusive heavy quarkonium photoproduction (Ryskin 1992, Brodsky 1994) to the near threshold region, where prior work by Kharzeev (1995, 1998) and Hatta (2018, 2019) had argued for a direct connection to the trace anomaly and gluonic gravitational form factors. The authors explicitly compare their results to the gluonic gravitational form factor calculations of Tong (2021) and show no direct connection exists. The paper provides predictions for ψ' and Υ (1S,2S) states that can be tested at JLab-12GeV and future electron-ion colliders, based on comparison with GlueX data for J/ψ photoproduction (Ali 2019).
