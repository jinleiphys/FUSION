# Breit interaction correction to the hyperfine constant of an external s-electron in many-electron atom

> Correction to the hyperfine constant $A$ of an external s-electron in many-electron atom caused by the Breit interaction is calculated analytically: $δA/A =0.68 Zα^2$. Physical mechanism for this correction is polarization of the internal electronic shells (mainly $1s^2$ shell) by the magnetic field of the external electron. This mechanism is similar to the polarization of vacuum considered by Karplus and Klein long time ago. The similarity is the reason why in both cases (Dirac sea polarization and internal atomic shells polarization) the corrections have the same dependence on the nuclear charge and fine structure constant. In conclusion we also discuss $Zα^2$ corrections to the parity violation effects in atoms.

# Breit interaction correction to the hyperfine constant of an external s-electron in many-electron atom

## Key claim

The Breit interaction correction to the hyperfine constant A of an external s-electron in a many-electron atom is δA/A = 0.68 Zα², arising from polarization of internal electronic shells (mainly the 1s² shell) by the magnetic field of the external electron. This correction is the leading Z-dependent part and does not include the Dirac sea polarization contribution calculated by Karplus and Klein (1952), which has opposite sign and must be added to obtain the full physical correction.

## Method

The author performed an analytic calculation of the Breit interaction correction to the hyperfine constant using nonrelativistic Schrödinger wave functions for the 1s, 2s, and external s-electron states in atomic units, with the nuclear Coulomb field treated as unscreened at small distances. Direct and exchange diagrams for both paramagnetic and diamagnetic contributions were evaluated separately using numerical integration of analytic expressions derived from perturbation theory, with the wave function corrections for the hyperfine interaction obtained by solving the inhomogeneous Schrödinger equation in the Coulomb field.

## Key numbers

- Total Breit correction to hyperfine constant: δA_B/A = 0.681 Zα² (direct contribution 0.410 Zα² plus exchange contribution 0.271 Zα²)
- Diamagnetic direct contribution from 1s-shell: 0.207 Zα² (μ₁·μ_N); from 2s-shell: 0.023 Zα² (μ₁·μ_N); total: 0.230 Zα² (μ₁·μ_N)
- Paramagnetic direct contribution from 1s-shell: -0.219 Zα² (μ₁·μ_N); from 2s-shell: -0.021 Zα² (μ₁·μ_N); total: -0.640 Zα² (μ₁·μ_N)
- Exchange diamagnetic contribution: -0.107 Zα² (μ₁·μ_N) (1s: 0.096, 2s: 0.011)
- Exchange paramagnetic contribution (Fig.2b): -0.320 Zα² (μ₁·μ_N)
- Exchange paramagnetic contribution (Fig.2a): +0.156 Zα² (μ₁·μ_N)
- Total exchange magnetic correction: -0.271 Zα² (μ₁·μ_N)
- Coulomb correction (non-magnetic Zα² term): δA_Coulomb/A = -0.558 Zα²
- Combined total Zα² correction from closed shell polarization: δA/A = 0.123 Zα²
- Dirac sea polarization correction (Karplus and Klein, 1952): δA/A = α/2π - Zα²(5/2 - ln 2) ≈ α/2π - 1.81 Zα²

## Context

This work extends the analytic treatment of hyperfine corrections beyond the single-particle Dirac equation results of Bethe and Salpeter (1957) and Sushkov, Flambaam, and Khriplovich (1978). It resolves conflicting numerical results for Cs (Z=55) from Blundell, Johnson, and Sapirstein (1991), Safronova, Johnson, and Derevianko (1999), and Derevianko (2000) by providing an analytic expression that agrees with the most recent computation by Dzuba and Johnson (private communication). The calculation enables separation of the atomic shell polarization effect from the Dirac sea polarization effect of Karplus and Klein (1952), and provides a parametric estimate (Zα²) for the Breit correction to parity nonconservation effects in atoms, which is relevant for interpreting high-precision experiments such as those by Bennett and Wieman (1999).
