# Relativistic quantum kinetic equation of the Vlasov type for systems with internal degrees of freedom

> We present an approach to derive a relativistic kinetic equation of the Vlasov type. Our approach is especially reliable for the description of quantum field systems with many internal degrees of freedom. The method is based on the Heisenberg picture and leads to a kinetic equation which fulfills the conservation laws. We apply the approach to the standard Walecka Lagrangian and an effective chiral Lagrangian.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method for deriving a relativistic quantum kinetic equation of the Vlasov type that is especially reliable for systems with many internal degrees of freedom, but the derivation is restricted to the mean field approximation and does not itself produce the mass shell condition, which must be introduced as an additional external restriction.

## Method

The authors start from the Heisenberg picture equations of motion and use a covariant decomposition of the generators of motion with respect to a time-like direction defined by the unit vector n^μ. They define the one-particle covariant Wigner function and derive its τ-differentiation, leading to a general expression for the Vlasov-like drift integral. The method is then applied to two specific models: the Walecka Lagrangian for hadronic matter and an effective chiral Lagrangian (Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model), where in each case the spinor decomposition of the transport equation is performed and the quasi-classical limit is taken to obtain closed equations for the scalar part of the Wigner function.

## Key numbers

- For the Walecka model, the effective nucleon mass is defined as M* = M - g_s φ, where M is the nucleon mass, g_s is the scalar coupling constant, and φ is the scalar meson mean field.
- In the Walecka model, the mass shell condition is P^2 = (M*)^2, where P = p - g_v ω is the kinetic momentum.
- For the NJL model, the chiral invariant mass is defined as M = √(σ^2 + π^2), and the mass shell condition is p^2 = M^2.
- In the NJL model, the current quark mass m_0 is set to zero in the chiral limit.

## Context

This work extends the Zubarev non-equilibrium statistical operator method (Zubarev, 1974) to the relativistic domain and generalizes earlier relativistic transport equation derivations by Kadanoff and Baym (1962), Martin and Schwinger (1959), and Keldysh (1965). The approach enables future numerical studies of the Vlasov equation in microscopic quark models, particularly for investigating the quark-hadron phase transition where a quark condensate evolves in space and time, as suggested by Meshustin (1995).
