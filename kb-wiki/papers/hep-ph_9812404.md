# MarKov-Yukawa Transversality On Covariant Null Plane: Pion Form Factor, Gauge Invariance And Lorentz Completion

> The Markov-Yukawa Transversality Principle (MYTP) on a 2-body Bethe-Salpeter kernel is formulated on a covariant Null Plane (NP) to reconstruct the 4D BS wave function for 2 fermion quarks in terms of 3D entities that satisfy a 3D BSE. This result is the null-plane counterpart of a 3D-4D interconnection for the 2-body BS wave functions found earlier by imposing MYTP covariantly in the instantaneous rest frame (termed CIA) of the composite. This formulation yields a 3D BSE which is formally identical to its Covariant Instantaneity form, thus fully preserving its spectral results, while ensuring full covariance. More importantly, the reconstructed 4D vertex functions in the covariant null-plane ensure that 4D quark-loops are now free from ill-defined time-like momentum integrations (which had plagued the earlier CIA vertex functions), while a simple prescription of `Lorentz completion' in the new description yields a manifestly Lorentz-invariant result.This is illustrated for the pion and kaon form factors with full QED gauge-invariance, showing a $k^{-2}$ behaviour at large $k^2$, and `correct' slopes at small $k^2$. This method is compared with the Kadychevsky-Karmanov light-front formalism.

## Key claim

The Markov-Yukawa Transversality Principle (MYTP) applied on a covariant null-plane (NP) yields a 3D-4D interconnection for the two-body Bethe-Salpeter wave function that preserves the spectral results of the earlier Covariant Instantaneity Ansatz (CIA) while eliminating the ill-defined time-like momentum integrations that had plagued the CIA vertex functions in loop integrals, provided a simple prescription of "Lorentz completion" is employed to obtain manifestly Lorentz-invariant results.

## Method

The author formulates the MYTP on a covariant null-plane by demanding that the two-body Bethe-Salpeter kernel depends only on the relative momentum transverse to the null-plane, defined via a light-like 4-vector nμ and its dual ñμ. The 4D Bethe-Salpeter wave function for two fermion quarks is reconstructed in terms of 3D entities that satisfy a 3D Bethe-Salpeter equation, using pole integration over the null-plane component qn. The method is applied to calculate the pion and kaon electromagnetic form factors via 4D triangle loop integrals, with QED gauge invariance enforced by substituting each 4-momentum in the vertex function with pμ - eAμ and keeping first-order terms.

## Key numbers

- The pion form factor shows a k⁻² behaviour at large k².
- The squared pion charge radius from experiment is ⟨r²_expt⟩ = 0.43 ± 0.014 fm² (cited from Ref. [28a]).
- The scaled form factor at high k² is k²F(k²) ≈ 0.5 ± 0.1 GeV² (cited from Ref. [28b]).
- The basic constants used in the model are: ω₀ = 158 MeV; m_ud = 265 MeV; m_s = 415 MeV; m_c = 1530 MeV; m_b = 4900 MeV.
- The gaussian parameter β for the 3D wave function is given by β⁴ = [8 m̂₁² m̂₂² M_>² ω₀² α(M_>²)] / [⟨σ⟩ (1 - 8 C₀ m̂₁ m̂₂ α_s(M_>²))], where ⟨σ⟩² = 1 + 24 A₀ (m̂₁ m̂₂ M_>)² / β², with C₀ = 0.27 and A₀ = 0.0283.

## Context

This work extends the Markov-Yukawa Transversality Principle approach previously developed under the Covariant Instantaneity Ansatz (CIA) by Mitra (1998) and others, which had successfully produced hadron spectra but suffered from time-like momentum components in gaussian vertex functions for triangle loop integrals. The covariant null-plane formulation resolves this pathology while preserving the spectral predictions, and is compared with the light-front formalism of Kadychevsky and Karmanov (KK) as reviewed by Carbonell et al. (1998). The method enables gauge-invariant calculation of meson form factors and can be extended to more complicated processes such as 4-point functions.
