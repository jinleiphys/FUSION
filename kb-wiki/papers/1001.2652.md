# Transport calculations of antiproton-nucleus interactions

> The Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model is extended and applied to the antiproton-nucleus interactions in a wide beam momentum range. The model calculations are compared with the experimental data on $\bar p$-absorption cross sections on nuclei with an emphasis on extraction of the real part of an antiproton optical potential. The possibility of the cold compression of a nucleus by an antiproton in-flight is also considered.

# Transport calculations of antiproton-nucleus interactions

## Key claim

The Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model can reproduce antiproton absorption cross section data on nuclei below 1 GeV/c with a scaling factor of the meson-antinucleon coupling constants of ξ ≈ 0.21±0.03, corresponding to a real part of the antiproton optical potential of Re(V_opt) ≈ -(150±30) MeV in the nuclear centre; however, the authors note that BNL and Serpukhov data above 1 GeV/c require a much deeper real part close to the G-parity value of about -660 MeV.

## Method

The GiBUU model solves coupled kinetic equations for hadrons (N, anti-N, Delta, anti-Delta, pions) with two-body collisions, resonance decays, Pauli blocking, and mean-field potentials from a relativistic mean-field model using the NL3 parameter set. Meson-antinucleon coupling constants are scaled from the nucleon values by a factor ξ (0 < ξ ≤ 1), with pure G-parity transformation corresponding to ξ=1. The model computes antiproton absorption cross sections on 12C, 27Al, and 64Cu as a function of beam momentum, and simulates the dynamical compression of 16O by an antiproton initialized with momentum 0.3 GeV/c directed toward the nuclear centre.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit scaling factor of meson-antibaryon coupling constants: ξ = 0.21 ± 0.03, from comparison with KEK data on antiproton absorption cross sections on 12C, 27Al, and 64Cu at p_lab = 470-880 MeV/c.
- Real part of antiproton optical potential in nuclear centre: Re(V_opt) = -(150 ± 30) MeV, extracted from the best fit.
- Imaginary part of antiproton optical potential in nuclear centre: Im(V_opt) = -(105 ± 5) MeV, obtained from Eq. (Im_Vopt) with averaging over nucleon Fermi distribution.
- Antiproton survival probability at time 10 fm/c when nucleon density maximum is reached: P_surv ~ 10^{-3}, for the case of antiproton initialized at nuclear periphery with momentum 0.3 GeV/c and annihilation switched off.
- Nuclear saturation density used: ρ_0 = 0.148 fm^{-3}; compression threshold set at ρ_c = 2ρ_0.
- Probability of antiproton annihilation in compressed zone (ratio σ_compr/σ_ann) is maximal below 0.5 GeV/c and remains ~10^{-5} at beam momenta from 3 to 10 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the GiBUU transport model to antiproton-nucleus interactions, building on prior studies of antiproton optical potentials by Friedman (2007) and Nakamura (1984). The extracted optical potential values are comparable to the phenomenological values of Nakamura (1984) and Friedman (2005), but the authors note that data above 1 GeV/c from BNL and Serpukhov require a much deeper real part. The results enable future measurements at FAIR to resolve the momentum dependence of the antiproton optical potential, and suggest that triggering on high-momentum protons could help identify annihilation events in compressed nuclear zones at beam momenta of 3-10 GeV/c.
