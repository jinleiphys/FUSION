# How many-body correlations and $α$-clustering shape $^6$He

> The Borromean $^6$He nucleus is an exotic system characterized by two `halo' neutrons orbiting around a compact $^4$He (or $α$) core, in which the binary subsystems are unbound. The simultaneous reproduction of its small binding energy and extended matter and point-proton radii has been a challenge for {\em ab initio} theoretical calculations based on traditional bound-state methods. Using soft nucleon-nucleon interactions based on chiral effective field theory potentials, we show that supplementing the model space with $^4$He+$n$+$n$ cluster degrees of freedom largely solves this issue. We analyze the role played by the $α$-clustering and many-body correlations, and study the dependence of the energy spectrum on the resolution scale of the interaction.

## Key claim

By supplementing the model space with $^4$He+$n$+$n$ cluster degrees of freedom within the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC), the authors show that both six-body correlations and alpha-clustering can be simultaneously accounted for to reproduce the ground-state properties of $^6$He, but the inclusion of three-nucleon forces remains crucial for a fully accurate description of the spectrum.

## Method

The authors generalize the NCSMC formalism to treat three-cluster dynamics, writing the $^6$He wave function as a combination of square-integrable no-core shell model (NCSM) eigenstates and continuous microscopic-cluster states describing an alpha core plus two neutrons. They solve the Schrodinger equation in this combined model space using an extension of the microscopic R-matrix method on a Lagrange mesh, and compute matter and point-proton radii from expectation values of the corresponding operators on the ground-state wave function.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of $^6$He: -29.86 MeV for $\Lambda=1.5$ fm$^{-1}$ at $N_{\rm tot}=10$ (converged within 10 keV), compared to experimental value -29.268 MeV
- Ground-state energy of $^6$He: -29.17 MeV for $\Lambda=2.0$ fm$^{-1}$ at $N_{\rm tot}=12$ (NCSMC), compared to NCSM extrapolated value -29.20(11) MeV from Saaf (2014)
- Two-neutron separation energy $S_{2n}=0.94(5)$ MeV (NCSMC, $\Lambda=2.0$ fm$^{-1}$, $N_{\rm max}=10$), compared to experimental 0.975 MeV
- Matter radius $r_m=2.43(2)$ fm (NCSMC, $\Lambda=2.0$ fm$^{-1}$, $N_{\rm max}=10$), compared to experimental 2.32(10) fm
- Point-proton radius $r_{pp}=1.88(2)$ fm (NCSMC, $\Lambda=2.0$ fm$^{-1}$, $N_{\rm max}=10$), compared to experimental 1.938(23) fm
- Width of the $2^+$ resonance: $\Gamma=15$ keV at centroid 0.536 MeV (NCSMC, $\Lambda=1.5$ fm$^{-1}$)

## Context

This work extends previous ab initio studies of $^6$He that either described the interior successfully but could not account for the three-cluster asymptotic behavior (Pieper 2008, Bacca 2012, Saaf 2014), or described the asymptotic configurations but underbound the ground state due to missing many-body correlations (Quaglioni 2013, Romero-Redondo 2014). The present NCSMC approach enables future ab initio studies of $^6$He beta-decay half-life, $^4$He$(2n,\gamma)^6$He radiative capture, and the $^3$H$(^3$H$,2n)^4$He fusion reaction.
