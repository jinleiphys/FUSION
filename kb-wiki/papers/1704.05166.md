# Determination of the density region of the symmetry energy probed by the $π^-/π^+$ ratio

> The studies, from several independent methods, consistently show that $π^-/π^+$ ratio in the $^{132}$Sn+$^{124}$Sn reaction at 300 MeV/nucleon, which is being carried out at Radioactive Isotope Beam Facility (RIBF) in Japan, just probes the symmetry energy around saturation density.

# Determination of the density region of the symmetry energy probed by the π⁻/π⁺ ratio

## Key claim

The π⁻/π⁺ ratio in the ¹³²Sn+¹²⁴Sn reaction at 300 or 200 MeV/nucleon, currently being measured at RIKEN, probes the symmetry energy around saturation density rather than at suprasaturation densities, contrary to the common assumption that this observable always accesses high-density symmetry energy. Only in heavy reaction systems at incident beam energies above 400 MeV/nucleon does the π⁻/π⁺ ratio mainly probe the symmetry energy at suprasaturation densities.

## Method

The authors used a transport model with a Skyrme-type isoscalar mean field and a density-dependent symmetry energy of the form E_sym = 32(ρ/ρ₀)^γ, where γ = 0.5 (soft) and γ = 2 (stiff) were compared. They determined the density region probed by the π⁻/π⁺ ratio using two methods: (1) a "switching method" that changes the symmetry energy parameter γ in different density intervals, and (2) an "adding method" that incrementally adds the symmetry energy in 0.5ρ₀ density steps from zero to maximum density. Pion freeze-out local baryon densities were tracked as a function of time to understand which densities contribute to the final observable.

## Key numbers

- For the ¹³²Sn+¹²⁴Sn reaction at 300 MeV/nucleon, the maximum baryon density reaches about 2ρ₀, but the pion freeze-out average local baryon density is below 1.25ρ₀.
- For the ²⁰⁸Pb+²⁰⁸Pb reaction at 600 MeV/nucleon, the maximum pion freeze-out local baryon density is above 2ρ₀.
- In the ¹³²Sn+¹²⁴Sn reaction at 270 MeV/nucleon, the effect of the slope L (changing from 40 to 80 MeV) on the (π⁻/π⁺)_like ratio is about 3 times larger than the effect of the curvature K_sym (changing from 0 to -400 MeV).
- The π⁻/π⁺ ratio in the ¹³²Sn+¹²⁴Sn reaction probes the symmetry energy mainly in the density regions 0.5ρ₀-ρ₀ and ρ₀-1.5ρ₀, with roughly equal sensitivity in both regions.

## Context

This work extends studies by Li et al. (2005) and others who identified the π⁻/π⁺ ratio as a probe of high-density symmetry energy, but contradicts the assumption that this observable always accesses suprasaturation densities. The paper clarifies that the SπRIT Collaboration experiments at RIKEN (2017) using Sn+Sn reactions at 300 or 200 MeV/nucleon will constrain the symmetry energy around saturation density rather than at high densities. The results enable proper interpretation of ongoing RIBF experiments and guide future experimental designs: heavy systems like ²⁰⁸Pb+²⁰⁸Pb at beam energies above 400 MeV/nucleon are needed to probe suprasaturation-density symmetry energy via the π⁻/π⁺ ratio.
