# Coulomb disintegration of the 208Pb ions at the big hadron collider energy

> The spectrum of virtual photons for nuclei of Pb-208 at big hadron collider energy is analysed. Cross-sections of inelastic Coulomb collisions accompanied with evaporation of one to four neutrons by one of the partners are calculated. By making use of the Glauber's method, executed estimates of neutron multiplicity resulting from the Coulomb desintehration of the nuclei. The predicted neutron multiplicity reaches 30-40 and more for each of the partners. Neutrons are accompanied by emitted fast protons and other charged particles, on the average three-five particles for each partner. The majority of the particles of a kulonovsky origin are characterized with small rapiditty and can thus be registered with ZDCs.

## Key claim

At LHC energies, approximately 20% of inelastic Coulomb collisions between lead ions involve excitation by a hard virtual photon with energy above 1 GeV, leading to the emission of tens of evaporative neutrons and several fast nucleons and mesons; the predicted neutron multiplicity reaches 30–40 or more per partner, but this estimate is considered a lower bound because pre-equilibrium processes and secondary interactions are not fully accounted for.

## Method

The spectrum of virtual photons for colliding 208Pb nuclei at LHC energy (2×1.38 TeV/nucleon) was analyzed using the equivalent-photon method of Weizsäcker-Williams, with finite nuclear size effects included via Fourier transform of the Lienard-Wiechert potential. Cross-sections for Coulomb excitation followed by neutron evaporation (1–4 neutrons) were calculated using a statistical model with GDR parameters. For hard photon absorption (ω ≳ 1 GeV), the Glauber–AGK–Bertocchi–Treleani approach was used to estimate the mean number of struck nucleons and the resulting cascade of evaporative neutrons and fast particles.

## Key numbers

- Lorentz factor in the rest frame of one nucleus: γ_L = 4.32×10^6; corresponding momentum p = 2.1×10^5 GeV.
- GDR energy for 208Pb: ω_0 = 13.5 MeV; width Γ = 4 MeV; peak photoabsorption cross-section 600 mb.
- Total Coulomb excitation cross-section from GDR region (8–30 MeV): 115.89 barn, contributing 60.85% of the total.
- Partial cross-sections for neutron evaporation from GDR: 1n = 91.10 barn, 2n = 23.83 barn, 3n = 2.79 barn, 4n = 0.67 barn.
- Cross-section from Δ-isobar region (150–1000 MeV): 35.04 barn (18.39% of total); from the highest energy bin (10–60 GeV): 0.97 barn (0.51%).
- Mean number of struck nucleons by a 1 TeV photon: n̄ = 5.33 (of which 2.1 are protons); for a 1 GeV photon: n̄ = 3.08 (1.2 protons).
- Estimated total neutron multiplicity per partner: 6–26 (for 1 GeV photon) and 11–42 (for 1 TeV photon), plus up to 8 and 13 fast nucleons respectively.

## Context

This work extends the study of Coulomb dissociation of heavy ions at ultra-relativistic energies, previously examined at lower energies (e.g., at RHIC), to the LHC regime. It uses the equivalent-photon method of Gribov (1970) and the Glauber–AGK–Bertocchi–Treleani formalism (Bertocchi and Treleani, 1977) to estimate neutron multiplicities. The predictions are testable with zero-degree calorimeters (ZDCs) at the LHC and can discriminate between different models of hard photon–nucleus interaction, such as the vector dominance model and the quasideuteron mechanism (Levinger, 1951; Nedorezov, 1980). The paper also notes that recent experimental data from ALICE (2011) agree with the calculated GDR evaporation channels.
