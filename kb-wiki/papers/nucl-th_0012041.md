# Photoproduction of $η$-mesons off light nuclei

# Photoproduction of $\eta$-mesons off light nuclei

## Key claim
The Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) formalism, when applied to coherent $\eta$ photoproduction on deuteron, reveals a resonant peak in the total cross-section that is absent in impulse approximation calculations; however, this peak appears approximately 15 MeV below the experimental peak under all parameter variations, suggesting that an $\eta N$ repulsion at short distances may be needed to shift it to the correct energy.

## Method
The authors employed the exact Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) three-body formalism, modified to include electromagnetic interactions, to describe the reaction $\gamma + d \to \eta + d$. They represented the two-body T-operators in separable form, using an S-wave nucleon-nucleon separable potential and a separable $\eta N$ T-matrix parameterized via the $S_{11}$ resonance, and extended the $\eta N \to \gamma N$ T-matrix off the energy shell using Yamaguchi form-factors. The photoproduction cross-section was obtained from the inverse photoabsorption amplitude via detailed balance, with all rescattering effects included through the coupled AGS equations.

## Key numbers
- The $\eta$-nucleon complex scattering length $a_{\eta N}$ has a real part in the range $0.27\ {\rm fm} \le \Rea \le 0.98\ {\rm fm}$ and an imaginary part in the range $0.19\ {\rm fm} \le \Ima \le 0.37\ {\rm fm}$, with most authors agreeing $\Ima \approx 0.3$ fm.
- The ratio of photoproduction amplitudes for neutron and proton is $A = t_n^{\gamma \eta} / t_p^{\gamma \eta} = -0.84 \pm 0.15$ from multipole analysis.
- The range parameter $\alpha = 3.316$ fm$^{-1}$ was used for the $\eta N$ T-matrix form factor.
- The resonant peak in the total cross-section remains about 15 MeV to the left of the experimental peak under all parameter variations.
- Comparison with experimental data suggests $A > -0.80$, $\Rea > 0.75$ fm, and that the $\eta N$ interaction is likely repulsive at short distances.

## Context
This work extends earlier theoretical studies of $(\gamma,\eta)$ reactions on deuteron that used simple impulse approximation or effective two-body formulations, which gave very different conclusions and cross-sections an order of magnitude lower than experiment (e.g., bib1–bib5). The rigorous AGS treatment shows that rescattering and rearrangement processes produce a resonant enhancement absent in impulse approximation, but the persistent 15 MeV discrepancy with the experimental data of bib6 points to the need for a repulsive short-range $\eta N$ interaction. This enables future few-body calculations to constrain the $\eta N$ interaction parameters and the neutron-to-proton photoproduction amplitude ratio more precisely.
