# On the proliferation of X's, Y's and Z's candidates

> Heavy meson spectroscopy above open flavor thresholds has become a challenge both from the experimental and theoretical points of view. Experimentally, several signals have been interpreted as meson resonances with unusual properties; theoretically, such signals may be identified with meson-meson molecules or compact multiquark structures. We analyze the influence of thresholds on heavy meson spectroscopy comparing different flavor sectors and quantum numbers. The validity of a quark-model picture above open-flavor thresholds would severely restrict the number of channels that may lodge multiquark structures as meson-meson molecules.

## Key claim

The constituent quark model, when applied above open-flavor thresholds, severely restricts the number of channels that can host meson-meson molecules; only a few specific quantum numbers, such as the $(I)J^{PC}=(0)1^{++}$ in charmonium, can produce bound states, and the possibility of such molecules decreases with increasing heavy-quark mass.

## Method

The authors compared experimental thresholds of four-quark systems $Qn\bar{Q}\bar{n}$ (with $Q=s$, $c$, $b$) for several $J^{PC}$ quantum numbers, plotting mass differences relative to $K\bar{K}$, $D\bar{D}$, and $B\bar{B}$ thresholds. They analyzed the ordering of $(Q\bar{n})-(n\bar{Q})$ versus $(Q\bar{Q})-(n\bar{n})$ two-meson thresholds using a Cornell-like potential and discussed the implications for molecular binding based on the reduced-mass dependence of coulombic binding and the absence of long-range forces in charmonium-light systems.

## Key numbers

- For $Q=s$, the $(Q\bar{Q})-(n\bar{n})$ two-meson mass is larger than the $(Q\bar{n})-(n\bar{Q})$ mass for all $J^{PC}$; for $Q=c$ or $b$, the ordering reverses.
- For $J^{PC}=1^{++}$ with $Q=c$, the $(c\bar{n})-(n\bar{c})$ ($D\bar{D}^*$) and $(c\bar{c})-(n\bar{n})$ ($J/\Psi\omega$) thresholds are almost degenerate.
- The $X(3872)$ is the only state firmly established by different collaborations whose properties are hardly accommodated in the quark-antiquark scheme.
- The $Z(3930)$ has been identified as the $\chi_{c2}(2P)$ charmonium state.
- The $Z(4430)$ was seen by Belle but not by BABAR.

## Context

This work extends the historical discussion on the validity of the constituent quark model above flavor thresholds, following the concerns raised by Isgur (1999) about threshold effects and by Close (2003) about the overpopulation of meson states. It contradicts hadronic models based on traditional meson theory of nuclear forces or heavy quark symmetry that predict a twin of the $X(3872)$ in bottomonium. The analysis enables a more restrictive search for genuine multiquark states, suggesting that only the $J^{PC}=2^{++}$ channel in charmonium may host a resonance close above the $D^*\bar{D}^*$ threshold, and that exotic $QQ\bar{n}\bar{n}$ states are more probable in the bottom sector than in the strange sector.
