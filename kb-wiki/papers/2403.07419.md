# Quenching and flow of charm and bottom quarks via semi-leptonic decay of $D$ and $B$ mesons in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC

> Heavy flavor particles provide important probes of the microscopic structure and thermodynamic properties of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) produced in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions. We study the energy loss and flow of charm and bottom quarks inside the QGP via the nuclear modification factor ($R_\mathrm{AA}$) and elliptic flow coefficient ($v_2$) of their decayed leptons in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC. The dynamical evolution of the QGP is performed using the (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics model CLVisc; the evolution of heavy quarks inside the QGP is simulated with our improved Langevin model that takes into account both collisional and radiative energy loss of heavy quarks; the hadronization of heavy quarks is simulated via our hybrid coalescence-fragmentation model; and the semi-leptonic decay of $D$ and $B$ mesons is simulated via PYTHIA. By using the same spatial diffusion coefficient for charm and bottom quarks, we obtain smaller $R_\mathrm{AA}$ and larger $v_2$ of charm decayed leptons than bottom decayed leptons, indicating stronger energy loss of charm quarks than bottom quarks inside the QGP within our current model setup.

## Key claim

By using the same spatial diffusion coefficient for charm and bottom quarks, the authors obtain smaller $R_\mathrm{AA}$ and larger $v_2$ for charm decayed leptons than bottom decayed leptons, indicating stronger energy loss of charm quarks than bottom quarks inside the QGP within their current model setup; however, this hierarchy is not apparent in the current ATLAS data, and the model overestimates the $R_\mathrm{AA}$ of bottom decayed muons.

## Method

The dynamical evolution of the QGP is simulated using the (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics model CLVisc. The evolution of heavy quarks inside the QGP is simulated with an improved Langevin model that includes both collisional and radiative energy loss, using the higher-twist (HT) energy loss formalism for medium-induced gluon radiation. Hadronization of heavy quarks is performed via a hybrid coalescence-fragmentation model, and the semi-leptonic decay of $D$ and $B$ mesons is simulated using PYTHIA. The spatial diffusion coefficient is set to $D_\mathrm{s}(2\pi T)=4$ for both charm and bottom quarks.

## Key numbers

- Spatial diffusion coefficient: $D_\mathrm{s}(2\pi T)=4$ for both charm and bottom quarks, providing a reasonable description of $D$ and $B$ meson quenching and elliptic flow in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC.
- Specific shear viscosity of the QGP: $\eta_\mathrm{v}/s = 0.16$ in the CLVisc hydrodynamics simulation.
- Initial time of hydrodynamics evolution: $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm/$c$; heavy quarks stop interacting when the local temperature drops below $T_\mathrm{c} = 160$ MeV.
- Minimum of $R_\mathrm{AA}$ for heavy flavor electrons appears at intermediate $p_\mathrm{T}$ around 5 GeV; maximum of $v_2$ appears at intermediate $p_\mathrm{T}$ around 2 to 3 GeV.
- Collision energy: $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV for Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Li (2020) and Li (2021) on heavy flavor quenching and flow, using the same Langevin framework with $D_\mathrm{s}(2\pi T)=4$ that described $D$ and $B$ meson observables. It directly addresses recent ATLAS measurements (ATLAS 2020, ATLAS 2021) that separately extract $R_\mathrm{AA}$ and $v_2$ for charm-decayed and bottom-decayed muons. The results enable future improvements by incorporating temperature-, momentum-, and mass-dependent diffusion coefficients, which should help better constrain the collisional versus radiative contributions to heavy quark energy loss and the transport properties of the QGP.
