# Isotope-Resolved Ba and Xe Yields in Actinide Fission and Correlated Heavy--Light Fragment Systematics

> Isotope-resolved post-neutron fission yields in the Ba and Xe chains are calculated and benchmarked against evaluated reference data, with emphasis on element-resolved isotopic chains $Y(N_f)$ at fixed fragment charge $Z$ and on the consistency of heavy--light fragment correlations. Calculations are performed within a four-dimensional (4D) Langevin framework employing Fourier-over-Spheroid shape parametrization. The benchmark covers spontaneous fission of selected Cm and Cf isotopes (including $^{244,246}$Cm and $^{250}$Cf) as well as neutron-induced fission at thermal and 14-MeV energies for representative actinides in the Th--Pu region (including $^{229}$Th, $^{235}$U, $^{239}$Pu, and $^{249}$Cf). The dominant neutron-number maxima are reproduced for a large fraction of the isotopic chains considered, indicating that the mean charge partition and the average neutron content of the main fission channels are described consistently. A systematic residual discrepancy is observed in the isotopic widths: the calculated yields often fall off too rapidly on the distribution tails, producing distributions that are narrower than the evaluated data, most notably for heavy-fragment chains.

## Key claim

The 4D Langevin framework with Fourier-over-Spheroid shape parametrization reproduces the dominant neutron-number maxima for a large fraction of isotopic chains in Ba and Xe yields from actinide fission, but a systematic residual discrepancy remains: the calculated isotopic distributions are narrower than evaluated data, with yields falling off too rapidly on the distribution tails, most notably for heavy-fragment chains.

## Method

The authors employ a four-dimensional Langevin framework with Fourier-over-Spheroid shape parametrization, using collective coordinates of elongation, left-right asymmetry, neck variable, and non-axial deformation. The driving potential is obtained from a microscopic-macroscopic energy functional using the Lublin-Strasbourg Drop model for the macroscopic part and Yukawa-folded single-particle potential for microscopic corrections. Langevin trajectories are propagated from the outer saddle to scission, with pre-scission neutron emission treated via coupled Langevin-plus-Master equations and post-scission fragment cooling described by a statistical evaporation model using the Weisskopf-Ewing formalism.

## Context

This work extends the 4D Langevin framework previously developed by Pomorski and collaborators (Pomorski 2024, Pomorski and Xiao 2025) to isotope-resolved fission yields, benchmarking against the ENDF/B-VIII.0 evaluated nuclear data library. The approach provides a dynamical alternative to semi-empirical models like GEF (Schmidt 2016) and computationally demanding microscopic methods such as the Time-Dependent Generator Coordinate Method (Regnier 2016, Verriere 2021). The results enable systematic refinement of the fluctuation content in the model while preserving the well-reproduced centroid positions, with the identified width deficit pointing toward needed improvements in diffusion strength, scission excitation energy dispersion, and neutron evaporation stochasticity.
