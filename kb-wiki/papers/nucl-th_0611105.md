# HBT shape analysis with q-cumulants

> Taking up and extending earlier suggestions, we show how two- and threedimensional shapes of second-order HBT correlations can be described in a multivariate Edgeworth expansion around gaussian ellipsoids, with expansion coefficients, identified as the cumulants of pair momentum difference q, acting as shape parameters. Off-diagonal terms dominate both the character and magnitude of shapes. Cumulants can be measured directly and so the shape analysis has no need for fitting.

## Key claim

The paper shows that two- and three-dimensional shapes of second-order HBT correlations can be described by a multivariate Edgeworth expansion around gaussian ellipsoids, with the expansion coefficients being the cumulants of the pair momentum difference q that can be measured directly without fitting; however, the method is presented as a promising tool requiring real data testing, and the shapes shown are for illustrative purposes only and do not represent real data.

## Method

The authors derive a multivariate Edgeworth expansion for the normalized probability density f(q) of the pair momentum difference vector q, using a gaussian reference distribution f0(q) with adjustable first and second cumulants. By setting the reference distribution's first and second cumulants equal to those of the measured distribution and exploiting the symmetry C2(-q) = C2(q) which eliminates all odd-order cumulants, the expansion reduces to terms of fourth and sixth order only. The expansion coefficients are the cumulants of q, which can be measured directly from data, and the hermite tensors are known functions of q and the reference distribution parameters.

## Key numbers

- In two dimensions, there are 5 distinct cumulants of fourth order and 7 distinct cumulants of sixth order in the Edgeworth expansion.
- In three dimensions, there are 15 distinct fourth-order cumulants and 28 distinct sixth-order cumulants.
- The cumulant C211 has a weight 12 times larger than diagonal cumulants due to combinatoric prefactors in the three-dimensional expansion.
- For azimuthally symmetric sources, the Pearson correlation coefficients ρos and ρsl are zero, simplifying the inverse covariance matrix.

## Context

This work extends earlier suggestions for Edgeworth expansions in HBT analysis by Hegyi (1993) and Csorgo (2000) to a fully multivariate form including cross terms, and builds on the direct measurement approach for gaussian radii proposed by Wieand (1996). The method provides an alternative to imaging techniques (Brown 1997, 1998, 2005) and cartesian harmonics (Danielewicz 2005) for quantifying non-gaussian shapes of HBT correlations, and enables direct measurement of higher-order shape parameters without fitting procedures.
