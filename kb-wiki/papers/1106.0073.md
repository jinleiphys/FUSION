# Noise, sign problems, and statistics

> We show how sign problems in simulations of many-body systems can manifest themselves in the form of heavy-tailed correlator distributions, similar to what is seen in electron propagation through disordered media. We propose an alternative statistical approach for extracting ground state energies in such systems, illustrating the method with a toy model and with lattice data for unitary fermions.

## Key claim

Sign problems in many-body simulations manifest as heavy-tailed (specifically, approximately log-normal) distributions of correlator values, and a cumulant expansion of the log of the correlator provides a more efficient estimator for ground-state energies than the conventional sample-mean approach, trading systematic error for statistical error as the truncation order increases.

## Method

The authors analyze the distribution of N-body correlators \(C_N(\tau,\phi)\) for unitary fermions on a lattice, finding that \(\ln C_N\) is approximately Gaussian (i.e., \(C_N\) is log-normal) with variance growing linearly in Euclidean time \(\tau\). They derive a mean-field prediction for the mean and variance of \(\ln C_N\) using a saddle-point expansion about a constant auxiliary field \(\phi_0\). They then test a cumulant-based estimator \(\ln\langle X\rangle = \sum_{n=1}^\infty \kappa_n/n!\) (where \(\kappa_n\) are cumulants of \(Y=\ln X\)) on a toy model of products of random numbers and on lattice data for 50 trapped unitary fermions, truncating the sum at various orders \(n_{\text{max}}\).

## Key numbers

* For the toy model with \(g=1/2\) and \(\tau=1000\), the conventional estimator gives \(\mathcal{E}=0.014932\) with statistical error 0.002485 (exact answer is 0); the cumulant expansion truncated at \(n_{\text{max}}=3\) gives \(\mathcal{E}=-0.000412\) with statistical error 0.001618 and systematic error \(-0.000324\).
* For unitary fermions, the mean-field prediction for the variance growth rate is \(\partial\sigma^2/\partial\tau = (40/9\pi)E_0(N)\), where \(E_0(N)=3NE_F/5\) is the free Fermi gas energy; the mean growth rate is \(\partial\bar{y}/\partial\tau = -E_0(N)\).
* The toy model simulation used 250 blocks of 50,000 configurations each (total \(1.25\times10^7\) configurations) at \(\tau=1000\) and \(g=1/2\).
* The unitary fermion simulation used \(10^6\) background field configurations for 50 fermions in a harmonic trap, with fits performed over time intervals \(\tau=45-60\) (for \(n=2,3\)) and \(\tau=13-60\) (for \(n=4,5,6\)).

## Context

This work extends the "Lepage analysis" of noise in QCD correlators (Lepage, 1989) to show that the noise arises from heavy-tailed distributions rather than simple Gaussian fluctuations, analogous to electron propagation in disordered media near the Anderson localization transition (Smolyarenko and Altshuler, 1997). The proposed cumulant method enables extraction of ground-state energies from noisy lattice simulations of unitary fermions (Endres et al., 2011) and may be applicable to other systems with sign problems, such as QCD at nonzero chemical potential (Gibbs, 1986; Splittorff and Verbaarschot, 2006).
