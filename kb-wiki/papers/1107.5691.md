# Role of isospin degree of freedom on N/Z dependence of participant-spectator matter

> We study the N/Z dependence of participant-spectator matter for Ca+Ca reactions at 50 and 250MeV/nucleon. We also study the role of symmetry energy and momentum-dependent interactions on the N/Z dependence of participant-spectator matter.

# Role of isospin degree of freedom on N/Z dependence of participant-spectator matter

## Key claim
The N/Z dependence of participant-spectator matter in Ca+Ca reactions arises from an interplay between mass effects (which increase participant matter with N/Z) and the repulsive symmetry energy (which decreases participant matter with N/Z), with the net effect being a slight change at 50 MeV/nucleon and almost no dependence at 250 MeV/nucleon.

## Method
The study uses the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate symmetric Ca+Ca collisions with N/Z ratios from 1.0 to 2.0 in steps of 0.2, at incident energies of 50 and 250 MeV/nucleon and impact parameters b/b_max = 0.2–0.4. The participant and spectator matter fractions are extracted after following the reactions uniformly up to 800 fm/c, and the roles of symmetry energy and momentum-dependent interactions (MDI) are isolated by setting the symmetry energy strength to zero and by using a stiff momentum-dependent (SMD) equation of state.

## Key numbers
- N/Z ratios studied: 1.0, 1.2, 1.4, 1.6, 1.8, and 2.0, corresponding to reactions of ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁴Ca+⁴⁴Ca, ⁴⁸Ca+⁴⁸Ca, ⁵²Ca+⁵²Ca, ⁵⁶Ca+⁵⁶Ca, and ⁶⁰Ca+⁶⁰Ca.
- Incident energies: 50 and 250 MeV/nucleon.
- Participant-spectator matter changes slightly with N/Z at 50 MeV/nucleon, but is almost independent of N/Z at 250 MeV/nucleon.
- Setting symmetry energy to zero causes participant matter to increase slightly for higher N/Z ratios, with negligible effect on lower N/Z ratios.
- With SMD (momentum-dependent) interactions, participant matter decreases and spectator matter increases compared to the case without MDI.

## Context
This work extends studies of isospin effects in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the role of symmetry energy in collective flow and its disappearance at balance energy, as investigated by Pak (1997) and Gautam (2011). The study uses the IQMD model, which is an extension of the quantum molecular dynamics model and treats different charge states of nucleons, deltas, and pions explicitly, inherited from the Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (VUU) model. The results enable further understanding of how the symmetry energy and momentum-dependent interactions influence the partitioning of nuclear matter into participant and spectator components in isospin-asymmetric reactions.
