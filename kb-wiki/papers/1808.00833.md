# $p_t$- dependence of the flow coefficients for pp collisions in the color string scenario. Monte-Carlo simulations

> In the color string picture with fusion and percolation the dependence of the flow coefficients $v_n$ on the transverse momentum is studied for pp collisions the LHC energy respectively. Monte-Carlo simulations are used to locate simple strings and their fused clusters. The results favorably agree with the CMS data in the region $0.2 \le p_t\le 3.$ GeV/c appropriate for the string scenario.

## Key claim

The color string scenario with fusion and percolation can reproduce the experimentally observed transverse-momentum dependence of flow coefficients in high-multiplicity pp collisions at LHC energies, but only when events with roughly triple the average multiplicity (about 50 strings instead of the average 18) are selected and a thermal (exponential) rather than Schwinger (Gaussian) transverse-momentum distribution is used for the string decay.

## Method

Monte-Carlo simulations were performed for pp collisions at 5–13 TeV, representing each nucleon as a disk of radius 0.8 fm with Gaussian matter density. Strings were thrown onto the interaction area, their fusion into clusters was tracked, and the inclusive particle production was computed using a quenching formula (Eq. 4) with a coefficient κ = 0.48 adjusted to reproduce the integrated v₂ in mid-central Pb-Pb collisions. Event-by-event flow coefficients vₙ(pₜ) were calculated from the azimuthal distribution of produced particles and then averaged over at least 1000 simulated events.

## Key numbers

- Average number of strings in minimum-bias pp collisions at 5–13 TeV: approximately 18.
- For high-multiplicity events, the maximal number of strings is 50.
- Quenching coefficient κ = 0.48, adjusted to reproduce the integrated v₂ in mid-central Pb-Pb collisions at 5–13 TeV.
- Power-law exponents αₙ for vₙ(pₜ) ∝ pₜ^{αₙ} in minimum-bias events: α₂ = 0.59, α₃ = 0.70, α₄ = 0.77, α₅ = 0.83.
- Power-law exponents αₙ for vₙ(pₜ) in triple-multiplicity events: α₂ = 0.65, α₃ = 0.79, α₄ = 0.80, α₅ = 0.82.
- Integrated v₂ for minimum-bias events (temperature distribution): 0.02884.
- Integrated v₂ for triple-multiplicity events (temperature distribution): 0.02308.
- Integrated v₂ for triple-multiplicity events (Schwinger distribution): 0.02240.
- The agreement with CMS data for v₂(pₜ) is shown in the range 0.2 ≤ pₜ ≤ 3.0 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the authors’ previous Monte-Carlo study of flow coefficients and ridge correlations in pp collisions (Braun and Pajares, 2017) by now resolving the pₜ-dependence of vₙ. It builds on the color string fusion and percolation framework developed by Braun, Pajares, and collaborators, which has been successfully applied to multiparticle production in the soft region. The results agree with CMS data for v₂(pₜ) in high-multiplicity pp collisions (CMS Collaboration, 2017) and show that the string scenario can reproduce the flow behavior observed in AA collisions when the multiplicity is sufficiently high. The approach enables future studies of how the quenching mechanism affects the scaling exponents αₙ, which experimentally follow αₙ = n/3 in AA collisions (ALICE Collaboration).
