# Meson assisted dibaryons

> We discuss a new type of L=0 positive-parity dibaryons, pion-B-B', where the dominant binding mechanism is provided by resonating p-wave pion-baryon interactions. Recent calculations of such pion assisted dibaryons are reviewed with special emphasis placed on the non-strange I(JP)=1(2+) N-Delta dibaryon D_{12}(2150) studied recently at JLab, and on the 0(3+) Delta-Delta dibaryon D_{03}(2380) discovered recently by the WASA-at-COSY Collaboration. We review recent searches by the HADES Collaboration at GSI and by the E15 and E27 Experiments at J-PARC for a strangeness S=-1 I(JP)=1/2(0-) K-pp dibaryon and perhaps also for a strange I(JP)=3/2(2+) N-Sigma(1385) pion assisted dibaryon Y_{3/2(2+)}(2270). Charm C=+1 dibaryons, predicted with these same I(JP) values, are also briefly reviewed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a new class of "pion assisted dibaryons" (πBB') with L=0 positive parity can be understood through hadronic models where the dominant binding mechanism comes from resonating p-wave pion-baryon interactions, rather than from quark-model color-magnetic interactions. The key hedge is that while the D_{03}(2380) ΔΔ dibaryon is the only dibaryon established unambiguously so far, the interpretation of other candidates (such as the strange Y_{3/2(2+)}(2270) and charmed dibaryons) remains model-dependent and requires further experimental confirmation.

## Method

The author reviews Faddeev equation calculations with relativistic kinematics for the πNN three-body system, where the πN subsystem is dominated by the P_{33} Δ(1232) resonance channel and the NN subsystem is dominated by the ³S₁ and ¹S₀ channels. These coupled Faddeev equations give rise to effective NΔ and ΔΔ Lippmann-Schwinger equations for three-body S-matrix poles, with energy-dependent kernels incorporating spectator-hadron propagators. For the ΔΔ dibaryon D_{03}, a πNΔ' three-body model was solved where Δ' is a stable Δ(1232) and the NΔ' interaction is dominated by the D_{12} dibaryon.

## Key numbers

- D_{12}(2150) NΔ dibaryon: mass ≈ 2.15 GeV, width Γ ≈ 0.12 GeV, derived from Argand diagram of the ¹D₂ partial wave in pp elastic scattering
- D_{03}(2380) ΔΔ dibaryon: mass 2383 MeV, width 82 MeV (from Table 2, first row, large form factor), reproducing the WASA-at-COSY reported width Γ ≈ 80 MeV
- D_{12}(2150) from preliminary CLAS data: mass 2115 ± 10 MeV, width 125 ± 25 MeV
- D_{30} ΔΔ dibaryon: mass 2412 MeV, width 49 MeV (from Table 2, first row, large form factor), located ≈30 MeV above D_{03}
- Strange dibaryon Y_{3/2(2+)}(2270): predicted near the πΣN threshold at √s ≈ 2270 MeV
- Charmed dibaryon C_{3/2(2+)}(3370): predicted near threshold at √s_th ≈ 3363 MeV, with Σ_c(2455)N threshold ≈27 MeV above the πΛ_c(2286)N threshold

## Context

This work extends the 1964 Dyson-Xuong SU(6)-based classification of non-strange dibaryons (Dyson 1964) by providing a hadronic, rather than quark-based, mechanism for their binding. It contradicts earlier quark-model predictions that placed the D_{03} dibaryon at several hundreds of MeV above the Δ-Δ threshold (PPL 2015), and instead supports a molecular configuration composed of two Δ baryons. The paper enables searches for strange dibaryons (such as the Y_{3/2(2+)}(2270) at HADES and J-PARC experiments E15 and E27) and charmed dibaryons (such as the C_{3/2(2+)}(3370) at J-PARC), extending the pion-assisted dibaryon phenomenology from the non-strange sector to strangeness S=-1 and charm C=+1 sectors.
