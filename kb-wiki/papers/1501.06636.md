# Extended-soft-core Baryon-Baryon Model Esc08 II. Hyperon-Nucleon Interactions

> The YN results are presented from a new version of the Extended-soft-core (ESC) potential model for Baryon-baryon (BB) scattering. The potentials consist of local- and non-local-potentials due to (i) One-boson-exchange (OBE), (ii) Pomeron and Odderon exchanges, (iii) Two pseudoscalar-exchange (PS-PS), and (iv) Meson-Pair-exchange (MPE). The OBE- and MPE-vertices are regulated by gaussian form factors, producing moderate size potentials in the origin. In addition to the mentioned ingredients of the ESC-model, also the possible short range repulsion due to the quark Pauli-principle in the BB-channels is included in the analysis. The NN, YN, and the S=-2 YY scattering is treated in a unified way using flavor SU(3) symmetry. With the ESC08-model a simultaneous fit is performed for the combined NN and YN data, using single sets of parameters. The achievement is a fit with (i) $χ^2/NN_{data}= 1.081$, and (ii) $χ^2/YN_{data}=1.08$.

## Key claim

The ESC08 model achieves a simultaneous fit to combined NN and YN scattering data with χ²/NN_data = 1.081 and χ²/YN_data = 1.08, but this success depends on including a phenomenological short-range repulsion from the quark Pauli principle in specific channels, particularly the SU(3) irreps {10} and {8_s}, which the abstract mentions only as "included in the analysis."

## Method

The authors constructed baryon-baryon potentials from one-boson-exchange (OBE), Pomeron and Odderon exchanges, two-pseudoscalar exchange (PS-PS), and meson-pair-exchange (MPE), regulated by gaussian form factors. They performed a simultaneous fit to NN and YN scattering data using broken SU(3) flavor symmetry, with coupling constants determined from the NN analysis via SU(3) relations and without medium-strong flavor-symmetry-breaking of the coupling constants. The short-range repulsion from Pauli-forbidden six-quark cluster configurations was incorporated by enhancing the effective Pomeron coupling in a channel-dependent way.

## Key numbers

* χ²/NN_data = 1.081 for the simultaneous fit to combined NN and YN data
* χ²/YN_data = 1.08 for the same simultaneous fit
* The inelastic Σ⁻p capture ratio at rest was fitted to r_R = 0.468 ± 0.010, matching the precise experimental datum
* The fit used 35 YN data plus 3 Σ⁺p cross-sections from KEK experiment E289, plus 11 elastic and inelastic Λp cross-sections and 3 elastic Σ⁻p cross-sections at higher energy

## Context

This work extends the ESC04 model (Rijken 2004) by adding Odderon exchange, axial-vector mesons of both J^PC = 1^{++} and 1^{+-} types, and completing 1/M corrections for meson-pair-exchange potentials. It addresses the longstanding problem that meson-exchange models could not produce a positive Σ-nucleus well depth U_Σ, which the inclusion of quark Pauli repulsion now makes possible. The model also reproduces the small spin-orbit splittings in Λ-hypernuclei indicated by experiment (Hiyama 2000, Hashimoto and Tamura 2006), and enables G-matrix calculations for hypernuclear systems that can be compared with observed data such as the ⁶_ΛΛHe double hypernucleus.
