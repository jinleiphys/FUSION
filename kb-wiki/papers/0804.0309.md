# Two photons into π^0π^0

> We perform a theoretical study based on dispersion relations of the reaction γγ\to π^0π^0 emphasizing the low energy region. We discuss how the f_0(980) signal emerges in γγ\to ππwithin the dispersive approach and how this fixes to a large extent the phase of the isoscalar S-wave γγ\to ππamplitude above the K\bar{K} threshold. This allows us to make sharper predictions for the cross section at lower energies and our results could then be used to distinguish between different ππisoscalar S-wave parameterizations with the advent of new precise data on γγ\toπ^0π^0. We compare our dispersive approach with an updated calculation employing Unitary Chiral Perturbation Theory (U\chiPT). We also pay special attention to the role played by the σresonance in γγ\toππand calculate its coupling and width to gammaγ, for which we obtain Γ(σ\toγγ)=(1.68\pm 0.15) KeV.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the dispersive approach to γγ → π⁰π⁰ can be sharpened by using an improved Omnès function that resolves the discontinuity problem near the K̄K threshold, and that the f₀(980) signal in γγ → ππ fixes the phase of the isoscalar S-wave amplitude above the K̄K threshold to the case where it rises with energy (z = +1), removing the previous ambiguity.

## Method

The authors perform a twice-subtracted dispersion relation for the I=0 S-wave γγ → ππ amplitude using a modified Omnès function Ω₀(s) that includes a first-order polynomial zero at s₁ (where the phase φ₀(s₁) = π), ensuring continuous behavior under changes in the ππ phase shift parameterization near the K̄K threshold. They determine the subtraction constants by matching to one-loop χPT at low energies and constrain the remaining constant F₀(s₁) − L₀(s₁) by requiring the σ(γγ → π⁰π⁰) at the f₀(980) region to be ≤ 40 nb. They compare this dispersive approach with an updated Unitary Chiral Perturbation Theory (UχPT) calculation that includes vector and axial-vector resonance exchanges.

## Key numbers

- Γ(σ → γγ) = (1.68 ± 0.15) KeV, calculated from the σ resonance coupling to two photons
- The uncertainty in the dispersive approach of Pennington (2006) at √s = 0.5, 0.55, 0.6, and 0.65 GeV is 20%, 45%, 92%, and 200% respectively, which is largely reduced by the extended method
- The Adler zero for F_N(s) is located at s_A = m_π² in one-loop χPT and moves to s_A = 1.175 m_π² in two-loop χPT
- The f₀(980) γγ width from Belle (2007) is 205⁺⁹⁵₋₈₃(stat)⁺¹⁴⁷₋₁₁₇(sys) eV
- The S-wave contribution to σ(γγ → π⁰π⁰) at s₁ ≈ 1 GeV is constrained to be ≤ 40 nb
- The relative correction to φ₀(s) from inelasticity is approximately 10% for 1.1 ≲ √s ≲ 1.5 GeV and approximately 20% for 1 ≲ √s ≲ 1.1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the dispersive approach of Pennington (2006) and Pennington, Morgan, and collaborators (1993, 1994) by introducing a modified Omnès function that resolves the discontinuity problem identified by Oller and Roca (2007) in the scalar form factor calculations. The approach enables sharper predictions for the γγ → π⁰π⁰ cross section below 0.8 GeV, which can distinguish between different ππ isoscalar S-wave parameterizations from CGL (2001), PY (2003), and AO (2007). The results are compared with the UχPT calculation of Oller and Oset (1998), which simultaneously described multiple γγ → meson-meson reactions from threshold up to √s ≲ 1.5 GeV.
