# Two Gluon Emission from MHV: Two Particle Correlations and the Deviation from Poisson

> We derive the momentum distribution for two soft and collinear gluons emitted from an off-shell quark, modeled by a $qγ\rightarrow qgg$ scattering process and computed using the maximal helicity violating (MHV) technique. We find the explicit non-Abelian corrections to the independent emission Poisson distribution in QED and demonstrate the efficiency of the MHV technique in perturbative QCD calculations. The gluonic two particle correlation function is highly suggestive of that measured in high-multiplicity $pp$ and $pA$ collisions, with a tight correlation in angle but broad correlation in rapidity. Further, the autocorrelation generated by the non-Abelian correlation can provide a significant non-hydrodynamical source of $v_2$ in these high multiplicity $pp$ and $pA$ collisions. Finally, these two gluon correlations can be used to calibrate Monte Carlo shower codes for a more accurate description of QCD processes in $pp$ collisions.

## Key claim

The paper derives the explicit non-Abelian corrections to the independent emission Poisson distribution for two soft and collinear gluons emitted from an off-shell quark, showing that the deviation from Poisson is governed by a correlation function that depends on the color factor \(\delta_N = N^2/(N^2-1)\) and the kinematic trace \(\mathrm{tr}(\slashed{P}\slashed{k}_1\slashed{p}\slashed{k}_2)\).

## Method

The authors model the process as \(q\gamma \rightarrow qgg\) scattering and compute the two-gluon emission amplitude using the maximal helicity violating (MHV) technique in the spinor helicity formalism. They isolate the dominant MHV configuration where both gluons have the same helicity opposite to the photon, factorize the amplitude into a hard scattering kernel and an eikonal factor, and then square the eikonal factor by decomposing it into symmetric (Abelian-like) and antisymmetric (non-Abelian) color-kinematic components. The cross section is obtained by averaging over initial colors and summing over final colors, yielding the two-gluon emission probability as a product of single-gluon probabilities multiplied by a correlation factor.

## Key numbers

* The color factor governing the non-Abelian correlation is \(\delta_N = \frac{1}{2}\frac{C_A}{C_F} = \frac{N^2}{N^2-1}\), where \(C_A = N\) and \(C_F = (N^2-1)/(2N)\) for SU(N).
* The single gluon emission probability is \(d\mathcal{W}(k) = \frac{d^3k}{(2\pi)^3}\frac{1}{2E_k} 8\pi \alpha_s \frac{C_F P\cdot p}{(P\cdot k)(p\cdot k)}\).
* The two-gluon correlation function is \(\mathcal{I}(1,2) = \delta_N \frac{\mathrm{tr}(\slashed{P}\slashed{k}_1\slashed{p}\slashed{k}_2)}{4(k_1\cdot k_2)(P\cdot p)}\).
* The trace of the antisymmetric color factor squared is \(\mathrm{tr}|[T_{a_1},T_{a_2}]|^2 = C_A^2 C_F\).
* The trace of the symmetric color factor squared is \(\mathrm{tr}|\{T_{a_1},T_{a_2}\}|^2 = 4C_A C_F^2 - C_A^2 C_F\).

## Context

This work extends the standard QED-based Poisson approximation for multiple gluon emission used in pQCD energy loss models (Gyulassy 2001, Majumder 2010, Armesto 2011) by computing the explicit non-Abelian corrections for two gluons radiated from an off-shell quark. It provides a benchmark for calibrating vacuum Monte Carlo shower codes (Lonnblad 1992, Gleisberg 2008, Sjostrand 2014, Bellm 2015) and offers a non-hydrodynamical source of azimuthal anisotropy \(v_2\) in high-multiplicity \(pp\) and \(pA\) collisions, challenging the nearly perfect fluid interpretation from hydrodynamics models (Teaney 2003, Song 2010, Gale 2012, Bernhard 2016). The two-gluon correlation function is suggestive of the tight angular but broad rapidity correlations observed experimentally at RHIC and LHC (Wong 2007, Wenger 2008, Abelev 2009, Abelev 2012, Aad 2012, Khachatryan 2010, CMS 2012, Aad 2014, Khachatryan 2015).
