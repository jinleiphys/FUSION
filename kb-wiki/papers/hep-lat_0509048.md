# Nuclear Physics and Lattice QCD

> Lattice QCD is progressing toward being able to impact our understanding of nuclei and nuclear processes. I discuss areas of nuclear physics that are becoming possible to explore with lattice QCD, the techniques that are currently available and the status of numerical explorations.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations, when matched onto effective field theories (EFTs) developed over the last decade, will for the first time allow rigorous determination of nuclear properties directly from QCD, but the Maiani-Testa theorem prevents direct computation of two-body S-matrix elements except at kinematic thresholds, requiring Lüscher's finite-volume method to extract scattering amplitudes.

## Method

The paper reviews the program of matching lattice QCD calculations of two-nucleon, nucleon-hyperon, and hyperon-hyperon systems onto pionless or pionful EFTs, using Lüscher's formula to relate finite-volume energy levels to infinite-volume scattering amplitudes. For electroweak processes such as \(np\rightarrow d\gamma\), background-field techniques are employed to extract the coefficients of gauge-invariant operators not related to the NN scattering amplitude. The NPLQCD collaboration performed exploratory calculations on \(20^3\times 64\) staggered MILC lattices with physical dimension \(\sim 2.5\) fm and lattice spacing \(\sim 0.125\) fm, using domain-wall fermions, analyzing \(I=2\) \(\pi\pi\) scattering and \(\Lambda\Lambda\) correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- NN scattering lengths at physical pion mass: \(^1S_0\) channel \(\sim -24\) fm, \(^3S_1\) channel \(\sim +5.4\) fm (the channel containing the deuteron bound by \(\sim 2.2\) MeV)
- For \(a/L\rightarrow 0\), the lowest two-nucleon energy level is \(E_0 = +4\pi a/(M L^3)[1 - c_1 a/L + c_2 (a/L)^2 + \ldots] + \mathcal{O}(L^{-6})\) with \(c_1 = -2.837297\), \(c_2 = +6.375183\)
- For \(a/L\rightarrow\infty\), the lowest energy level is \(\tilde E_0 = (4\pi^2/(M L^2))[d_1 + d_2 L p\cot\delta_0 + \ldots]\) with \(d_1 = -0.095901\), \(d_2 = +0.0253716\)
- Pion masses used in NPLQCD calculations: \(m_\pi \sim 290, 350, 480\) MeV on lattices of physical dimension \(\sim 2.5\) fm
- NPLQCD awarded \(\sim 40\) Gflop-yrs (\(\sim 8\%\) of JLab resources) for computations
- Nonleptonic hyperon decay amplitudes at LO in SU(2) \(\chi\)PT: \(\Lambda\rightarrow p\pi^-\) S-wave 1.42 (input), P-wave 0.56 (theory) vs 0.52\(\pm\)0.02 (expt); \(\Sigma^-\rightarrow n\pi^-\) S-wave 1.88 (input), P-wave \(-0.50\rightarrow -0.14\) (theory) vs \(-0.06\pm 0.01\) (expt); \(\Sigma^+\rightarrow n\pi^+\) S-wave 0.0 (theory) vs \(0.06\pm 0.01\) (expt), P-wave \(+0.42\rightarrow +0.08\) (theory) vs \(1.81\pm 0.01\) (expt)

## Context

This work extends the program of matching lattice QCD to nuclear EFTs pioneered by Weinberg (1990s) and builds on the finite-volume formalism of Lüscher (1986, 1990). It directly addresses the Maiani-Testa theorem (Maiani, 1975) which prevents direct extraction of scattering amplitudes from Euclidean correlators. The paper discusses how lattice QCD can impact understanding of dense nuclear matter (following Kaplan and Nelson, 1986 on kaon condensation and Bethe and Brown, 1993), neutrinoless \(\beta\beta\)-decay matrix elements, and the quark-mass dependence of NN scattering (Beane et al., 2002; Epelbaum et al., 2002). The NPLQCD collaboration's results on \(I=2\) \(\pi\pi\) scattering (Beane et al., 2005) serve as a warm-up for future NN, NY, and YY calculations.
