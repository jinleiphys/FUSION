# Melting the Color Glass Condensate at the LHC

> The charged particle multiplicity in central AA collisions and the production of heavy flavors in pA collisions at the LHC is predicted in the CGC framework.

## Key claim

The Color Glass Condensate framework, with parameters tuned to reproduce RHIC limiting fragmentation data, predicts that at the LHC the charged particle multiplicity in central AA collisions will be 1000-1400 per unit pseudorapidity at midrapidity, and that the nuclear modification factor for heavy flavor in pA collisions will be below unity already at midrapidity, decreasing further toward the proton fragmentation region.

## Method

The authors used the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation with fixed coupling to evolve dipole scattering amplitudes from an initial condition at x0 ≈ 10^-2, using either the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model or the Golec-Biernat-Wusthoff (GBW) model as initial conditions. They tuned the free parameters and the value of α_s to reproduce the limiting fragmentation curves measured at RHIC from √s = 20 GeV to √s = 200 GeV. For heavy quark production in pA collisions, they computed cross-sections beyond k⊥-factorization (which breaks down for quark pairs) by including 3- and 4-point correlations in the nucleus, then integrated over the antiquark and convolved with fragmentation functions to obtain open heavy flavor cross-sections.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle multiplicity per unit pseudorapidity at midrapidity in central AA collisions at LHC: dN_ch/dη|_(η=0) = 1000-1400, with the lower endpoint corresponding to GBW initial conditions and the upper endpoint to MV initial conditions.
- The BK evolution initial condition was set at x0 ≈ 10^-2.
- The produced particles were assumed to have mass m ∼ 200 MeV when converting rapidity distributions to pseudorapidity distributions.
- The LHC AA collision energy used was √s = 5.5 TeV (the text states "5.5 GeV" but context indicates this is a typo for 5.5 TeV).

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate framework, previously applied to RHIC energies, to make predictions for the LHC. The authors build on the McLerran-Venugopalan model and the Golec-Biernat-Wusthoff model as initial conditions for BK evolution, and they reference prior work on limiting fragmentation (Zakopane, 2005) and heavy quark production (Heavyquarks1, Heavyquarks2, Heavyquarks3). The predictions enable experimental tests of the CGC framework at LHC energies, particularly through measurements of charged particle multiplicity in AA collisions and nuclear modification factors for D mesons and J/ψ in pA collisions.
