# Consistent effective description of nucleonic resonances in an unitary relativistic field-theoretic way

> High energy strong interaction physics is successfully described by the local renormalizable gauge theory called Quantum-Chromo-Dynamics (QCD) with quarks and gluons as ``elementary'' degrees of freedom, while intermediate energy strong interaction physics shows up to be determined by a non-local, non--renormalizable effective field theory (EFT) of ``effective'' degrees of freedom like mesons, ground state baryons and resonances. Within the picture of an effective field theory of strong interaction at intermediate energies I present a ``toy-model'' in which fermionic and bosonic resonances are considered to be ``particles'', i.e. they consistently are described by (anti-)commuting effective field-operators (containing dynamics of infinitely many quark-gluon or meson-nucleon diagrams) which are comfortably treated by Wick's Theorem in a covariant framework and obey unitarity. Non-trivial implications to couplings of non-local interactions are shown.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a consistent, unitary effective field theory description of nucleonic resonances requires introducing two distinct field operators (left and right eigen-fields) per resonance degree of freedom, rather than the usual single complex-mass propagator, because the standard approach violates Hermiticity and unitarity.

## Method

The author constructs a "Unitary Effective Resonance Model" toy model in which a fermionic resonance is described by a pair of fields \(\Psi_R(x)\) and \(\Psi_L(x)\) with complex conjugate masses \(M = m_* - i\Gamma/2\) and \(M^*\), respectively. Canonical quantization yields non-vanishing equal-time anticommutation relations between the right and left fields, and the Feynman propagators take the form \((\slsh{p} - M)^{-1}\) and \((\slsh{p} - M^*)^{-1}\). The model is extended to include a nucleon field, a resonance field, and a meson field, with a vertex matrix containing all interaction structures, and consistency conditions are derived relating pseudoscalar and pseudovector coupling constants for resonances such as the Roper \(P_{11}\) and the \(S_{11}(1535)\).

## Key numbers

- Resonance complex mass: \(M = m_* - i\Gamma/2\) (with \(m_*\) the real part and \(\Gamma\) the width), and its complex conjugate \(M^*\).
- For the Roper \(P_{11}\) resonance, pseudovector couplings satisfy \(f_{\pi NP^R_{11}}/m_\pi = (f_{\pi NP^L_{11}}/m_\pi) \cdot (M_{P_{11}} + m_N)/(M^*_{P_{11}} + m_N)\).
- For the \(S_{11}(1535)\) resonance, pseudovector couplings satisfy \(f_{\pi NS^R_{11}}/m_\pi = (f_{\pi NS^L_{11}}/m_\pi) \cdot (M_{S_{11}} - m_N)/(M^*_{S_{11}} - m_N)\).
- Pseudoscalar couplings are constrained to be equal: \(g_{\pi NP^R_{11}} = g_{\pi NP^L_{11}}\) and \(g_{\pi NS^R_{11}} = g_{\pi NS^L_{11}}\).

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of resonances in intermediate-energy strong interaction physics, where resonances are commonly described by propagators with a complex mass \((\slsh{p} - M)^{-1}\) that violates unitarity. The toy model is presented as a step toward a consistent effective field theory that bridges high-energy QCD and intermediate-energy descriptions, following approaches such as Chiral Perturbation Theory (Gasser and Leutwyler, 1980s) and the QCD Sum Rule method (Shifman, Ioffe, Nambu, 1970s). The framework enables future work on momentum-dependent self-energies, non-linear dispersion relations, and microscopic calculation of vertex functions, while raising the open question of how to avoid double counting between explicit resonance fields and meson-nucleon-generated resonances.
