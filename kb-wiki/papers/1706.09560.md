# New successive variational method of tensor-optimized antisymmetrized molecular dynamics for nuclear many-body systems

> We recently proposed a new variational theory of "tensor-optimized antisymmetrized molecular dynamics" (TOAMD), which treats the strong interaction explicitly for finite nuclei [T. Myo et al., Prog. Theor. Exp. Phys. 2015, 073D02 (2015)]. In TOAMD, the correlation functions for the tensor force and the short-range repulsion and their multiple products are successively operated to the AMD state. The correlated Hamiltonian is expanded into many-body operators by using the cluster expansion and all the resulting operators are taken into account in the calculation without any truncation. We show detailed results for TOAMD with the nucleon-nucleon interaction AV8$^\prime$ for $s$-shell nuclei. The binding energy and the Hamiltonian components are successively converged to exact values of the few-body calculations. We also apply TOAMD to the Malfliet-Tjon central potential having a strong short-range repulsion. TOAMD can treat the short-range correlation and provided accurate energies of $s$-shell nuclei, reproducing the results of few-body calculations. It turns out that the numerical accuracy of TOAMD with double products of the correlation functions is beyond the variational Monte Carlo method with Jastrow's product-type correlation functions.

# New successive variational method of tensor-optimized antisymmetrized molecular dynamics for nuclear many-body systems

## Key claim
The tensor-optimized antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (TOAMD) method, using successive power-series expansion of correlation functions for the tensor force and short-range repulsion, can reproduce few-body benchmark results for s-shell nuclei with the AV8' bare NN interaction and the Malfliet-Tjon central potential, provided that all many-body operators arising from the cluster expansion are included without truncation. The numerical accuracy of double TOAMD (including double products of correlation functions) exceeds that of the variational Monte Carlo method with Jastrow-type product correlation functions for the Malfliet-Tjon potential.

## Method
The TOAMD wave function is constructed by multiplying an antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) Slater determinant of Gaussian wave packets with two-body correlation functions: a tensor-type function F_D (containing the tensor operator S_12) and a central-type function F_S for short-range repulsion. The wave function is expanded as a power series in these correlation functions (single TOAMD: 1+F_S+F_D; double TOAMD: 1+F_S+F_D+F_S F_S+F_S F_D+F_D F_S+F_D F_D), with each correlation function at each order treated as independent variational functions expressed as sums of Gaussians. The correlated Hamiltonian and norm operators are expanded into many-body operators via cluster expansion, and all resulting operators (up to ten-body for double TOAMD with the two-body interaction) are included in the matrix elements to preserve the variational principle. The total energy is obtained by solving the eigenvalue problem for the Hamiltonian and norm matrices constructed with the AMD basis.

## Key numbers
- For ^3H with the Malfliet-Tjon V potential: double TOAMD gives -8.24 MeV total energy, compared to few-body calculation -8.25 MeV and VMC -8.22(2) MeV
- For ^4He with the Malfliet-Tjon V potential: double TOAMD gives -31.28 MeV total energy, compared to few-body calculation -31.36 MeV and VMC -31.19(5) MeV
- For ^3H with the AV8' potential: double TOAMD gives -7.68 MeV total energy, compared to GFMC -7.76 MeV (80 keV difference)
- For ^4He with the AV8' potential: double TOAMD gives -24.74 MeV total energy, compared to GFMC -25.93 MeV (1.2 MeV difference)
- AMD alone gives +11.37 MeV for ^3H and +56.42 MeV for ^4He with AV8' (unbound)
- Single TOAMD (1+F_S+F_D) gives -4.98 MeV for ^3H and -14.74 MeV for ^4He with AV8'
- Range parameter nu optimized in double TOAMD: 0.095 fm^{-2} for ^3H and 0.22 fm^{-2} for ^4He with AV8'
- Range parameter nu optimized for MT-V: 0.11 fm^{-2} for ^3H and 0.25 fm^{-2} for ^4He
- Number of Gaussian functions used for correlation functions: N_G = 7

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous TOAMD formulation (Myo et al., 2015) by providing detailed analysis of s-shell nuclei with the AV8' bare NN interaction and the Malfliet-Tjon central potential. The method is conceptually similar to the tensor-optimized shell model (TOSM) developed by the same group (Myo et al., 2005, 2007, 2009) but uses AMD wave functions to describe clustering correlations. The results are compared with Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) benchmark calculations (Kamada et al., 2001) and variational Monte Carlo (VMC) with Jastrow correlation functions (Carlson, 1981). The approach enables systematic improvement by adding higher-order products of correlation functions and can be extended to heavier nuclei and nuclear matter, as recently formulated for relativistic nuclear matter by Toki and Hu (2017).
