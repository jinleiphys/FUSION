# Effect of two loop correction in the formation of QGP droplet

> The effect of two loop correction in the formation of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) droplet is studied with the introduction of the two loop correction factor in the mean field potential. Due to the correction factor it shows stability in the droplet formation of QGP indicating at different parametrization factors of the QGP fluid. The correction factor in the potential also shows gluon parameter factor shifts to a larger value from its earlier value of gluon factor of one loop correction in obtaining the stable droplets. The results show decreasing in the observable QGP droplets and droplet sizes are found to be $1.5-2.0$ fm radii with the two loop correction. It indicates that there is parameter like Reynold's number which can control the dynamics of QGP droplet formation and the stability of droplet in the case of droplet formation with the two loop correction factor.

## Key claim

The inclusion of a two-loop correction factor in the mean field potential for quark-gluon plasma (QGP) droplet formation produces stable droplets only within a specific range of the gluon flow parameter, between \(48\gamma_q\) and \(52\gamma_q\), and reduces droplet sizes compared to the one-loop correction case; the abstract does not emphasize that stability is lost when the gluon parameter exceeds \(52\gamma_q\).

## Method

The author constructs a thermodynamic partition function incorporating a two-loop corrected thermal mass and mean field potential, using coefficients \(a_1\) and \(a_2\) that depend on the number of light quark flavors. The density of states is derived via a generalized Thomas-Fermi model, and the free energy is computed for quarks (u, d, s) and gluons, including an interfacial energy term to replace the MIT bag model. The surface tension is then extracted by minimizing the free energy difference between the QGP and hadron phases, excluding mixed-phase finite-size effects.

## Key numbers

- Stable QGP droplet radii are found to be \(1.5-2.0\) fm with the two-loop correction.
- The gluon flow parameter for stable droplets lies in the range \(48 \gamma_q \le \gamma_g \le 52 \gamma_q\), where \(\gamma_q = 1/14\).
- The surface tension is calculated as \(\sigma = 0.173 \, T_c^3\), compared to a lattice result of \(\sigma = 0.2 \, T_c^3\).
- The QCD parameter \(\Lambda\) is taken as \(0.15\) GeV.
- Quark masses used: \(m_u = m_d = 0\) MeV, \(m_s = 0.15\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by the same author and collaborators (Singh, 2015; Ramanathan, 2006) that used one-loop corrections in the mean field potential, showing that two-loop corrections reduce droplet sizes and tighten the range of gluon parameters that yield stability. The results enable future studies of QGP droplet dynamics by suggesting that a parameter analogous to the Reynolds number may control droplet formation and stability, and the surface tension result (\(\sigma = 0.173 T_c^3\)) provides a comparison point for lattice QCD calculations (Kajantie, 2003; Iwasaki, 2004; Michael, 1992).
