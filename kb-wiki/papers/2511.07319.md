# Phases and properties of color superconductors

> Cold and dense matter is expected to be in a color-superconducting state. Here we review two calculations, relevant for fundamental properties and applications of color superconductivity, respectively: the weak-coupling QCD calculation of the fermionic energy gap together with the magnetic screening masses of the gauge bosons, and the calculation of bulk viscosity from a non-leptonic electroweak process. These calculations are supplemented by a discussion of color superconductors with mismatched Fermi momenta, and they are embedded in the context of the state of the art by giving an overview of previous and ongoing work and future directions.

## Key claim

Cold dense QCD matter is expected to form a color-superconducting state, but the rigorous weak-coupling QCD calculations of the fermionic energy gap and magnetic screening masses apply only at asymptotically large densities where the quark chemical potential is much larger than all quark masses and the coupling is weak.

## Method

The author reviews the 2-particle-irreducible (Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis) formalism applied to QCD at high density, using Nambu-Gorkov propagators to incorporate Cooper pairing. The gap equation is derived from the quark self-energy diagram in the hard-dense-loop approximation for the gluon propagator, and the bulk viscosity is calculated from a non-leptonic electroweak process in the 2SC phase using the gapped quark propagators.

## Key numbers

- The effective gluon mass squared in a dense QCD plasma is \( m_g^2 = N_f \frac{g^2 \mu^2}{6\pi^2} \), where \( N_f \) is the number of quark flavors and \( \mu \) is the quark chemical potential.
- In the CFL phase, 8 quasiparticles are gapped with gap \( \Delta \), while 1 quasiparticle is gapped with gap \( 2\Delta \).
- For a pointlike interaction, the zero-temperature gap behaves as \( \Delta_0 = 2\delta \cdot 2^{-1/3} \exp\left(-\frac{c}{g^2}\right) \), where \( c \equiv 6M^2\pi^2/\mu^2 \) and \( M \) is the scalar mediator mass.
- The CFL symmetry breaking pattern is \( [SU(3)_c] \times SU(3)_L \times SU(3)_R \times U(1)_B \to SU(3)_{c+L+R} \times \mathbb{Z}_2 \), with the residual group locking color and flavor transformations.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on color superconductivity initiated by Barrois (1977), Frautschi (1978), and Bailin (1983), and builds on the CFL phase proposed by Alford (1997, 1998). The calculations provide rigorous QCD results applicable at asymptotically large densities, and the bulk viscosity calculation in the 2SC phase enables future studies of transport properties in neutron star interiors, where color-superconducting phases may exist at moderate densities.
