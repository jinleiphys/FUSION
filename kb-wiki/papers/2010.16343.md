# Study of ($p,n$)IAS and ($^3$He,$t$)IAS charge-exchange reactions with the $G$-matrix folding method

> Differential cross sections of ($p,n$) and ($^3$He,$t$) charge-exchange reactions leading to the excitation of the isobaric analog state (IAS) of the target nucleus are calculated with the distorted wave Born approximation. The $G$-matrix double-folding method is employed to determine the nucleus-nucleus optical potential within the framework of the Lane model. $G$-matrices are obtained from a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculation using the Argonne Av18 nucleon-nucleon potential. Target densities have been taken from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations which predict values for the neutron skin thickness of heavy nuclei compatible with current existing data. Calculations are compared with experimental data of the reactions ($p,n$)IAS on $^{14}$C at $E_{lab}=135$ MeV and $^{48}$Ca at $E_{lab}=134$ MeV and $E_{lab}=160$ MeV, and ($^3$He,$t$)IAS on $^{58}$Ni, $^{90}$Zr and $^{208}$Pb at $E_{lab}=420$ MeV. Experimental results are well described without the necessity of any rescaling of the strength of the optical potential. A clear improvement in the description of the differential cross sections for the ($^3$He,$t$)IAS reactions on $^{58}$Ni and $^{90}$Zr targets is found when the neutron excess density is used to determine the transition densities. Our results show that the density and isospin dependences of the $G$-matrices play a non-negligible role in the description of the experimental data.

## Key claim

The differential cross sections of (p,n)IAS and (³He,t)IAS charge-exchange reactions can be well described by the G-matrix double-folding method without any rescaling of the optical potential strength, provided that the density and isospin dependences of the G-matrices are properly included; the improvement is particularly clear for (³He,t)IAS reactions on ⁵⁸Ni and ⁹⁰Zr when the neutron excess density is used for the transition densities.

## Method

The authors employed the distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) together with the G-matrix double-folding method to determine the nucleus-nucleus optical potential within the Lane model framework. G-matrices were obtained from a Brueckner–Hartree–Fock calculation using the Argonne Av18 nucleon-nucleon potential, and target densities were taken from Skyrme–Hartree–Fock calculations. The differential cross sections were computed for (p,n)IAS reactions on ¹⁴C at 135 MeV and ⁴⁸Ca at 134 and 160 MeV, and for (³He,t)IAS reactions on ⁵⁸Ni, ⁹⁰Zr, and ²⁰⁸Pb at 420 MeV, and compared with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- (p,n)IAS on ¹⁴C at E_lab = 135 MeV: differential cross section described without rescaling of the optical potential.
- (p,n)IAS on ⁴⁸Ca at E_lab = 134 MeV and E_lab = 160 MeV: differential cross section described without rescaling.
- (³He,t)IAS on ⁵⁸Ni, ⁹⁰Zr, and ²⁰⁸Pb at E_lab = 420 MeV: differential cross section described without rescaling.
- Neutron skin thickness from SHF densities: ΔR_np(⁹⁰Zr) = 0.07 fm and ΔR_np(²⁰⁸Pb) = 0.16 fm, compatible with the values 0.09 ± 0.03 fm and 0.16 ± 0.04 fm from Loc (2014).
- Analyzing power for (p,n)IAS on ⁴⁸Ca at E_lab = 134 MeV: first folding-method calculation reported.

## Context

This work extends the G-matrix folding approach, previously applied to nucleon-nucleus scattering by Arellano and Love (2007) and to nucleus-nucleus scattering by Toyokawa (2015), to charge-exchange reactions leading to the isobaric analog state. It improves upon earlier phenomenological analyses using the Franey and Love interaction (Love 1981, Franey 1985) and the Koning and Delaroche potential (2003), and upon the CDM3Y-based folding calculations of Khoa (2014) and Loc (2014, 2017). The results enable parameter-free predictions for charge-exchange reactions on unstable neutron-rich nuclei, which will be testable at the Facility for Rare Isotope Beams (FRIB).
