# Four-particle condensate in strongly coupled fermion systems

> Four-particle correlations in fermion systems at finite temperatures are investigated with special attention to the formation of a condensate. Instead of the instability of the normal state with respect to the onset of pairing described by the Gorkov equation, a new equation is obtained which describes the onset of quartetting. Within a model calculation for symmetric nuclear matter, we find that below a critical density, the four-particle condensation (alpha-like quartetting) is favored over deuteron condensation (triplet pairing). This pairing-quartetting competition is expected to be a general feature of interacting fermion systems, such as the excition-biexciton system in excited semiconductors. Possible experimental consequences are pointed out.

## Key claim

In symmetric nuclear matter at low densities, the transition to four-particle (alpha-like) condensation occurs at a higher critical temperature than the transition to deuteron (triplet) pairing, meaning the normal state is removed by quartetting before pairing can set in; however, at sufficiently high densities, quartet condensation always loses against weak-coupling Cooper pairing.

## Method

The authors derive a four-particle wave equation from the four-particle Green function in ladder Hartree-Fock approximation, analogous to the Gorkov equation for pairing. They solve this equation for a model system using a separable Gaussian interaction fitted to reproduce the free deuteron binding energy (-2.225 MeV) and the free alpha-particle binding energy (-28.29 MeV). The four-particle wave function is treated variationally as a product of two in-medium deuteron relative-motion wave functions and a Gaussian center-of-mass wave function.

## Key numbers

* Free deuteron binding energy: -2.225 MeV in the neutron-proton triplet channel
* Free alpha-particle binding energy: -28.29 MeV for the four-particle ground state
* Critical density for the crossover from quartetting to pairing: n1^0 = 0.03 fm^{-3} (corresponding to mu*_0 approximately 10 MeV)
* Estimated increase in critical temperature due to alpha-particle condensation in nuclei: about 0.2 MeV, with an estimated accuracy of 30 to 40%
* Estimated critical density n1^0 = 0.03 fm^{-3} for the onset of quartetting, also reliable within 30 to 40%
* Interaction range parameter: b = 1.54 fm^{-1}
* Triplet interaction strength: lambda_t = 1213.8 MeV fm^3
* Singlet interaction strength: lambda_s = 536 MeV fm^3

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum condensates in strongly coupled fermion systems beyond the standard BCS pairing framework, specifically addressing the competition between deuteron condensation (triplet pairing) and alpha-particle condensation (quartetting) in symmetric nuclear matter. Prior work on triplet pairing in nuclear matter by Baldo, Lombardo, and Schuck (1995) and others had found critical temperatures up to about 5 MeV at one-third nuclear matter density, but the present paper shows that four-particle correlations dominate at lower densities. The results are relevant for clustering phenomena in stellar collapse, the outer crust of neutron stars, alpha decay in heavy nuclei, and heavy-ion collisions, and the authors note analogous competition between exciton and biexciton condensates in semiconductors.
