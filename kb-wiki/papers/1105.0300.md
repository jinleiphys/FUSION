# Dependence of elliptic flow on transverse momentum in 200 GeV Au-Au and 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions

> We investigate the dependence of elliptic flows $v_2$ on transverse momentum $P_T$ for charged hadrons produced in nucleus-nucleus collisions at high energy by using a multi-source ideal gas model which includes the interaction contribution of the emission sources. Our calculated results are approximately in agreement with the experimental data over a wider $P_T$ range from the STAR and ALICE Collaborations. It is found that the expansion factor increases linearly with the impact parameter from most central (0-5%) to mid-peripheral (35-40%) collisions.

## Key claim

The multi-source ideal gas model, which includes source deformation and movement parameters, can reproduce the measured elliptic flow $v_2$ as a function of transverse momentum $P_T$ for charged hadrons in both 200 GeV Au-Au and 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions across a wide $P_T$ range, with the expansion factor $k$ increasing linearly with impact parameter from most central (0-5%) to mid-peripheral (35-40%) collisions.

## Method

The authors used a multi-source ideal gas model where each nucleon-nucleon collision forms an emission source described by a Gaussian momentum distribution with width $\sigma = \sqrt{TM}$. Source interactions are parameterized through deformation coefficients $A_x$ and $A_y$ and movement coefficients $B_x$ and $B_y$, with $A_x$ expressed as a function of momentum: $A_x(P_x, P_y) = 1 + k \exp(-\sqrt{(u_1 P_x)^2 + (u_2 P_y)^2}/\sigma)$. The elliptic flow $v_2(P_T)$ was calculated via Monte Carlo simulation and fitted to experimental data from the STAR Collaboration (200 GeV Au-Au) and ALICE Collaboration (2.76 TeV Pb-Pb) across multiple centrality bins.

## Key numbers

- For Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, the squared expansion factor follows $k^2 = (3.161 \pm 0.002)c$, where $c$ is the centrality percent.
- For Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV, the squared expansion factor follows $k^2 = (3.492 \pm 0.012)c$.
- The parameters $u_1$ and $u_2$ were taken as 0.944 and 0.59-0.61 respectively for Au-Au collisions, independent of centrality.
- For Pb-Pb collisions, $u_1 = 0.988$ and $u_2 = 0.625-0.640$.
- The elliptic flow $v_2$ saturates or decreases in the region $P_T > 2$ GeV/$c$ for Au-Au collisions.
- The elliptic flow of charged hadrons at LHC is nearly identical to that at RHIC up to $P_T = 3$ GeV/$c$, independent of collision energy and centrality.

## Context

This work extends the multi-source ideal gas model previously developed by the same group (Li, Liu, and collaborators) from describing (pseudo)rapidity and multiplicity distributions to the description of elliptic flow. The results are compared to experimental data from the STAR Collaboration (Adare, 2010) and ALICE Collaboration (Aamodt, 2010), and the linear dependence of $k^2$ on centrality supports the idea that the increase from central to peripheral collisions reflects the change in initial eccentricity of the fireball (Bhalerao, 2005; Broniowski, 2007). The model provides a simpler alternative to viscous hydrodynamic calculations (Bozek, 2011; Song, 2011) and can be used to study the relationship between the expansion factor and nuclear geometry via Glauber calculations (Miller, 2007).
