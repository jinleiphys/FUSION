# Collective flow in small systems

> The large density of matter in the interaction region of the proton-nucleus or deuteron-nucleus collisions enables the collective expansion of the fireball. Predictions of the hydrodynamic model for the asymmetric transverse flow are presented and compared to experimental data.

## Key claim
The paper argues that collective flow (quantified by harmonic coefficients \(v_n\)) can arise in small collision systems such as p-Pb and d-Au, but the claim is hedged by noting that the hydrodynamic model predictions depend sensitively on assumptions about initial conditions and that alternative saturation-based models can produce qualitatively similar angular correlations.

## Method
The authors performed 3+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations (including shear and bulk viscosity) of the fireball expansion in p-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV and d-Au collisions at RHIC energies. Initial conditions were generated using a Glauber Monte Carlo model with event-by-event fluctuations in participant nucleon positions and energy deposition, and hadron emission was modeled at a freeze-out temperature of 150 MeV with statistical hadronization and resonance decays.

## Key numbers
- Multiplicity in p-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV reaches values comparable to peripheral Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV.
- The number of participant nucleons in the most violent p-Pb collisions goes beyond 20 (from Glauber Monte Carlo).
- Freeze-out temperature used in the hydrodynamic model: 150 MeV.
- The elliptic and triangular flow coefficients from the hydrodynamic model are compared to CMS data for charged particles in p-Pb collisions (Fig. 3 left panel) and to ALICE data for identified particles (Fig. 3 right panel).
- The average transverse momentum of identified particles in p-Pb collisions is compared to ALICE data and to the HIJING model (Fig. 4).

## Context
This work extends earlier hydrodynamic predictions for small systems (Bozek 2011) and contrasts with saturation-based approaches (Dusling 2012) that yield similar angular correlations. The results enable further experimental tests of collectivity in p-Pb, d-Au, and potentially tritium-Au, \(^3\)He-Au, and \(^{12}\)C-nucleus collisions (Sickles 2013, Nagle 2013, Broniowski 2013), and they suggest that observed flow in small systems has a common origin with peripheral heavy-ion collisions.
