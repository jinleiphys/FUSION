# Mass and isospin dependence of symmetry energy coefficients of finite nuclei

> Mass and isospin dependence of symmetry energy coefficients $a_{sym}$ of finite nuclei is investigated with the measured nuclear masses incorporating the liquid drop mass formula. The enhanced $a_{sym}$ for nearly symmetric nuclei are observed. To describe the mass and isospin dependence of $a_{sym}$, a modified formula based on the conventional surface-symmetry term is proposed and the corresponding rms deviation of nuclear masses is checked.

## Key claim

The symmetry energy coefficient \(a_{\rm sym}\) of finite nuclei depends not only on mass number but also on isospin asymmetry, with enhanced values observed for nearly symmetric nuclei; the abstract glosses over the fact that this isospin dependence is extracted via a partial derivative method from measured masses and that the proposed modified formula reduces the rms mass deviation by only about 6% compared to the conventional surface-symmetry form.

## Method

The authors extracted symmetry energy coefficients from over 2000 measured nuclear masses (Audi database) using the liquid drop mass formula. They computed the partial derivative of the experimental binding energy per particle with respect to isospin asymmetry \(I=(N-Z)/A\) by finite differences between neighboring isotopes (\(\Delta Z=1\)), then iteratively solved for \(a_{\rm sym}^{(0)}\) and \(a_{\rm sym}^{(1)}\) terms. They proposed a modified formula \(a_{\rm sym}=c_{\rm sym}[1-\kappa/A^{1/3}+(2-|I|)/(2+|I|A)]\) that adds an isospin-dependent correction to the conventional surface-symmetry term, and fitted its parameters to the extracted coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The extracted \(a_{\rm sym}^{(0)}\) term for heavy nuclei is comparable to the results of Danielewicz et al., but for \(A<120\) the extracted values are generally higher than the Danielewicz et al. results.
- Using the conventional form \(a_{\rm sym}=c_{\rm sym}[1+\kappa A^{-1/3}]^{-1}\) (Danielewicz form) gives an rms deviation of 2.71 MeV for 2149 nuclear masses with \(N,Z\ge8\).
- Using the proposed modified formula with \(c_{\rm sym}=29.38\) MeV and \(\kappa=1.52\) gives an rms deviation of 2.55 MeV for the same 2149 masses, a reduction of about 6%.
- The optimal \(c_{\rm sym}\) changes from 26.09 MeV (without isospin dependence) to 29.38 MeV (with isospin dependence), which is close to the symmetry energy coefficient of nuclear matter at saturation density from Skyrme energy density functional calculations.
- The Wigner energy from the proposed formula is comparable to the Satula et al. result \(E_W \approx 47|I|\) and the Myers-Swiatecki form with \(C_0=10\) MeV, \(W=42\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of symmetry energy coefficients in finite nuclei by Danielewicz et al. (2006) and Samaddar et al. (2008), who observed mass dependence but did not fully capture the isospin dependence. The authors show that the conventional surface-symmetry term alone cannot reproduce the enhanced \(a_{\rm sym}\) for nearly symmetric nuclei, and their modified formula incorporating a Wigner-type correction reduces the rms mass deviation. This enables improved nuclear mass predictions and connects finite-nucleus symmetry energy to the nuclear matter value at saturation density, relevant for astrophysical applications such as neutron star structure.
