# Effect of Energy Loss On Azimuthal Correlation of charm and correlated charm decay in collision of lead nuclei at sqrt{s} = 2.76 A TeV

> We present the effect of energy loss of charm/anti-charm produced in a relativistic heavy ion collision as they traverse the resulting quark gluon plasma on the azimuthal correlation of c\bar{c} and D\bar{D} pairs and their correlated decay into leptons. We employ an empirical model of energy loss by charm quark energy loss and find that the consequences are easily discernible as different cuts on their momenta are applied. We also notice a modest increase in the invariant mass spectrum of dileptons from correlated decay as mentioned above due to energy loss.

# Effect of Energy Loss on Azimuthal Correlation of Charm and Correlated Charm Decay in Collision of Lead Nuclei at sqrt{s} = 2.76 A TeV

## Key claim

The azimuthal correlation of charm–anti-charm pairs and D–anti-D pairs in lead–lead collisions at LHC energies is altered by energy loss in the quark–gluon plasma in a manner that depends on transverse momentum cuts, but the effect is modest and the two different energy loss mechanisms (Bethe–Heitler type and Landau–Pomeranchuk–Migdal type) do not give substantially different outcomes.

## Method

The authors compute charm-pair production at next-to-leading order (NLO) in perturbative QCD using the MNR code with CTEQ5M parton distribution functions, then apply an empirical energy-loss model where charm quarks lose momentum per collision according to (Δp)_i = α (p_i)^β, with mean free path λ = 1 fm. The final charm momenta are obtained via a Monte Carlo sampling of the number of collisions from a Poisson distribution over the path length L, followed by Peterson fragmentation to D mesons and semileptonic decay to electrons. Calculations are performed for central lead–lead collisions (b = 0 fm) at sqrt(s) = 2.76 A TeV with T_AA = 292 fm^{-2}, charm mass m_c = 1.5 GeV, and factorization/renormalization scale 2 sqrt(m_c^2 + p_T^2).

## Key numbers

- T_AA = 292 fm^{-2} used for lead–lead collisions at b = 0 fm (central collisions)
- Charm quark mass m_c = 1.5 GeV
- Peterson fragmentation parameter ε_p = 0.13
- For Bethe–Heitler type energy loss: β = 1.0, α = 0.12
- For LPM type energy loss: β = 0.5, α = 0.25 GeV^{1/2}
- Mean free path λ = 1 fm
- For p_T < 2 GeV or p_T < 6 GeV, the back-to-back correlation (Δφ = π) rises by up to a factor of 10 for Δφ = 0
- For p_T > 2 GeV or p_T > 6 GeV, the Δφ = π correlation reduces by more than a factor of 10
- Di-electron invariant mass distribution shows an increase of almost 12% for M_{e+e-} < 1.0 GeV due to energy loss

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of heavy quark energy loss in the quark–gluon plasma, particularly the empirical model of Younus et al. (2012) and the concept that large drag on charm quarks leads to enhancement of D mesons and single electrons at low momentum, distinct from the Cronin effect. The authors build on NLO pQCD calculations of charm correlations and note that while energy loss alone does not change the direction of heavy quarks, the combination of energy loss with transverse momentum cuts reveals structure that could distinguish LO from NLO production mechanisms. The results enable future studies incorporating elliptic flow and expanding medium dynamics, and the authors note that more recent parton distribution functions (CTEQ6M, CTEQ6.6) should be used in follow-up work.
