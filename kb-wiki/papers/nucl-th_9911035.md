# A Sketch of Two and Three Bodies

> 11 pages (stone-age) LaTeX with 7 figures, uses packages epsfig (8 .eps files) and feynmp (necessary Metapost files included). Talk held at the International Workshop on Hadron Physics ``Effective Theories of Low Energy QCD'' in Coimbra, Portugal, 10th -- 15th September 1999; to be published in the Proceedings

## Key claim

Effective Field Theory (EFT) methods can be systematically extended to two- and three-nucleon systems, with the surprising result that pion exchange is perturbative at leading order and only contact interactions need to be summed non-perturbatively to produce nuclear binding, provided the fine-tuning of large scattering lengths is accommodated through a power counting scheme (Kaplan-Savage-Wise, 1998).

## Method

The author constructs an EFT with non-relativistic nucleons and pions as the relevant degrees of freedom below the Delta production scale, using a chirally invariant Lagrangian with contact interactions and pion couplings. Power counting is established by scaling momenta by a factor Q and kinetic energies by Q²/M, with the fine-tuning of large scattering lengths incorporated via the Power Divergence Subtraction scheme (Kaplan, Savage, Wise, 1998) to renormalize the leading-order contact interaction C₀. For two-body systems, the deuteron propagator is summed geometrically from contact interactions, while pion exchange and higher-derivative terms enter as perturbations at next-to-leading order (NLO). For three-body nd scattering in the quartet channel, the leading-order infinite series of "pinball" diagrams is solved numerically via a Faddeev-type integral equation, with NLO corrections from pionic vertex corrections, deuteron propagator modifications, and three-particle intermediate state corrections.

## Key numbers

- Scattering lengths in the ¹S₀ and ³S₁ channels: 1/a(¹S₀) = -8.3 MeV, 1/a(³S₁) = 36 MeV
- Deuteron binding energy: B = 2.225 MeV, corresponding to binding momentum γ = sqrt(M B) ≈ 46 MeV
- Pion decay constant: fπ = 130 MeV
- Estimated breakdown scale for theory with pions: Λπ ≈ 300 to 800 MeV; for pion-less theory: Λ(no π) ≈ 140 MeV
- Quartet S-wave nd scattering length at LO: a(⁴S₃/₂, LO) = (5.1 ± 1.5) fm
- Quartet S-wave nd scattering length at NLO with pions: a(⁴S₃/₂, NLO, π) = (6.8 ± 0.7) fm
- Quartet S-wave nd scattering length at NLO without pions: a(⁴S₃/₂, NLO, no π) = (6.7 ± 0.7) fm
- Quartet S-wave nd scattering length at NNLO without pions: a(⁴S₃/₂, NNLO, no π) = (6.33 ± 0.1) fm
- Experimental quartet S-wave nd scattering length: a(⁴S₃/₂, exp) = (6.35 ± 0.02) fm (Dilg et al.)
- Expansion parameter estimated as approximately 1/3 in most applications, giving NLO accuracy ~10% and NNLO accuracy ~4%

## Context

This work extends the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (1998) power counting scheme for two-nucleon systems to three-body nd scattering, building on Weinberg's (1990) original suggestion for applying EFT to few-nucleon systems. The author reports calculations with collaborators Chen, Springer, and Savage (1999) for deuteron Compton scattering, and with Bedaque and Gabbiani (1999) for three-body nd scattering. The approach contrasts with the alternative method presented by Meißner at this workshop following Weinberg's original suggestion, and complements the more formal investigation of the two-nucleon EFT presented by Birse. The results demonstrate that the EFT converges systematically, with NLO calculations with and without explicit pions agreeing within error bars, and enable parameter-free predictions for nd scattering observables that can be systematically improved to higher orders.
