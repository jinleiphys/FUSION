# Comment on "Evidence for the Existence of Supersymmetry in Atomic Nuclei"

> Metz et al PRL (83), 1542 (1999) have presented evidence for supersymmetry in atomic nuclei but their model Hamiltonian fails to distinguish between irreducible representations that occur with multiplicities greater than one. This failure raises serious questions as to the validity of the evidence.

# Comment on "Evidence for the Existence of Supersymmetry in Atomic Nuclei"

## Key claim
The model Hamiltonian used by Metz et al. (1999) to claim evidence for supersymmetry in the nuclear quartet $^{194}Pt$, $^{195}Pt$, $^{195}Au$, $^{196}Au$ cannot distinguish between irreducible representations that occur with multiplicities greater than one, specifically for the $[6,1]$ and $[5,1]$ irreps of $U_{\nu+\pi}^{B+F}(6)$, which raises serious questions about the validity of the claimed evidence.

## Method
Wybourne examined the group chain decomposition of the 705942-dimensional irreducible representation $[5]\times[2]$ of the supersymmetry product group $U(6/12)\times U(6/4)$ used by Metz et al. He computed the Kronecker products of the two $U(6)$ irreps involved in the contraction to $U_{\nu+\pi}^{B+F}(6)$, specifically $[1]\times[6] = [7] + [6,1]$ and $[1]\times[5,1] = [61] + [5,2] + [5,1,1]$, demonstrating that two distinct $[6,1]$ irreps arise that are ambiguously labeled as $[6,1]\times\langle0\rangle\times[1]$ and cannot be distinguished by the Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers
- The irreducible representation of the supersymmetry product group $U(6/12)\times U(6/4)$ is 705942-dimensional.
- The Hamiltonian involves six free parameters: $A$, $B$, $B^\prime$, $C$, $D$, and $E$.
- Multiplicity 2 occurs for states based upon the $[6,1]$ and $[5,1]$ irreps of $U_{\nu+\pi}^{B+F}(6)$.

## Context
This comment directly challenges the analysis of Metz et al. (1999) and also calls into question earlier analyses by Van Isacker et al. (1985) and Robard et al. (1993) that used similar group-theoretic methods. The work identifies a specific technical flaw in the Hamiltonian construction that must be resolved before supersymmetry claims can be validated; it suggests that distinguishing the multiplicity-2 states would require modification of the energy eigenvalue expression, possibly by including a term involving the product of the Casimir operators for the two $U(6)$ groups.
