# Relativistic three-particle scattering equations

> We derive a set of relativistic three-particle scattering equations in the three-particle c.m. frame employing a relativistic three-particle propagator suggested long ago by Ahmadzadeh and Tjon in the c.m. frame of a two-particle subsystem. We make the coordinate transformation of this propagator from the c.m. frame of the two-particle subsystem to the three-particle c.m. frame. We also point out that some numerical applications of the Ahmadzadeh and Tjon propagator to the three-nucleon problem use unnecessary nonrelativistic approximations which do not simplify the computational task, but violate constraints of relativistic unitarity and/or covariance.

## Key claim

The authors derive the correct relativistic three-particle scattering equations using the Ahmadzadeh-Tjon (AT) propagator transformed to the three-particle center-of-momentum frame, and they argue that previous numerical applications of the AT propagator to the three-nucleon problem introduced unnecessary nonrelativistic approximations that violate constraints of relativistic unitarity and/or covariance without simplifying the computational task.

## Method

The authors start from the AT three-particle propagator originally defined in the two-particle c.m. frame and perform a coordinate transformation of the integration variable from the two-particle relative momentum \(\vec{\cal P}\) to the three-particle c.m. momentum \(\vec p\), using the Jacobian of this transformation. They then rewrite the three-dimensional reduction of the scattering equation in the three-particle c.m. frame, obtaining explicit expressions for the Born term and the propagator that preserve the correct pole structure for three-particle intermediate state propagation.

## Key numbers

- The Jacobian of the transformation from two-particle c.m. momentum \(\vec{\cal P}\) to three-particle c.m. momentum \(\vec p\) is derived as \(J = \frac{1}{2} \frac{\omega_{\vec p}+\omega_{\vec p+\vec q}}{\omega_{\vec p}\omega_{\vec p+\vec q}} \omega_{\vec {\cal P}}\), where \(\omega_{\vec p} = (|\vec p|^2+m^2)^{1/2}\) for equal-mass particles of mass \(m\).
- The correct propagator denominator in the three-particle c.m. frame contains two poles: one at \(\sqrt s = \omega_{\vec p}+\omega_{\vec q}+\omega_{\vec p+\vec q}\) (three-particle propagation) and another at \(\sqrt s = \omega_{\vec q}-\omega_{\vec p}-\omega_{\vec p+\vec q}\) (particle-antiparticle propagation).
- Previous works by Jackson and Tjon, Hammel et al., Rupp and Tjon, and Sammarruca et al. used the approximation \(J=1\) (setting the Jacobian to unity), while Garcilazo et al. used a different approximation with the factor \([(\omega_{\vec p}+\omega_{\vec p+\vec q})^2-|\vec q|^2]^{1/2}\) in the numerator instead of the correct Jacobian.

## Context

This work extends the original relativistic three-particle propagator formalism of Ahmadzadeh and Tjon (1970) by providing the correct transformation to the three-particle c.m. frame, which had been missing from the literature. The authors identify specific nonrelativistic approximations made in numerical calculations by Jackson and Tjon, Hammel et al., Rupp and Tjon, Sammarruca et al., and Garcilazo et al. that violate relativistic unitarity and/or covariance, and they provide the exact equations that should be used instead. This enables future relativistic three-nucleon calculations to maintain proper unitarity and covariance without additional computational difficulty.
