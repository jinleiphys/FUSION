# Compositeness of S-wave weakly-bound states from next-to-leading order Weinberg's relations

> We discuss a model-independent estimator of the likelihood of the compositeness of a shallow S-wave bound or virtual state. The approach is based on an extension of Weinberg's relations in Phys. Rev. 137, B672 (1965) and it relies only on the proximity of the energy of the state to the two-hadron threshold to which it significantly couples. The scheme only makes use of the experimental scattering length and the effective range low energy parameters, and it is shown to be fully consistent for predominantly molecular hadrons. As explicit applications, we analyse the case of the deuteron, the $^1{\rm S}_0$ nucleon-nucleon virtual state and the exotic $D^{\ast}_{s0}(2317)^\pm$, and find strong support to the molecular interpretation in all cases. Results are less conclusive for the $D^{\ast}_{s0}(2317)^\pm$, since the binding energy of this state is significantly higher than that of the deuteron, and the approach employed here is at the limit of its applicability. We also qualitatively address the case of the recently discovered $T_{cc}^+$ state, within the isospin limit to avoid the complexity of the very close thresholds $D^0D^{\ast +}$ and $D^+D^{\ast 0}$, which could mask the ingredients of the approach proposed in this work.

## Key claim

The authors propose a model-independent estimator for the compositeness (molecular probability) of shallow S-wave bound or virtual states, based on next-to-leading order (NLO) extensions of Weinberg's relations. The estimator is only reliable when the binding momentum \(\gamma_b\) is much smaller than the next momentum scale \(\beta\) (the weak-binding limit), and the approach is at the limit of its applicability for states with binding energies significantly higher than that of the deuteron, such as the \(D^{\ast}_{s0}(2317)^\pm\).

## Method

The authors extend Weinberg's leading-order (LO) relations for the scattering length \(a\) and effective range \(r\) by introducing a common NLO correction term \(\delta r\) (expected to be of order \(1/\beta\)) that appears in both \(a_{\rm NLO}\) and \(r_{\rm NLO}\). They then construct a two-dimensional likelihood function \(\mathcal{L}(Z, \delta r)\) that compares the experimental (or phenomenological) values of \(a\), \(r\), and the binding momentum \(\gamma_b\) to their NLO expressions, using uncertainties that account for both experimental errors and the expected accuracy of the NLO approximation \((\gamma_b/\beta)^2\). The method is applied to the deuteron, the \(^1{\rm S}_0\) nucleon-nucleon virtual state, the \(D^{\ast}_{s0}(2317)^\pm\) exotic state, and qualitatively to the \(T_{cc}^+\) state (within the isospin limit to avoid coupled-channel complexities).

## Key numbers

- Deuteron: binding energy \(E_b^{\rm exp} = -2.224575(9)\) MeV, binding momentum \(\gamma_b^{\rm exp} = 0.2316068(5)\) fm\(^{-1}\), scattering length \(a_{\rm exp} = -5.42(1)\) fm, effective range \(r_{\rm exp} = 1.75(1)\) fm; the NLO accuracy is \((\gamma_b^2/m_\pi^2) \sim 10\%\), and values of \(Z\) greater than \(\gamma_b^{\rm exp}/m_\pi \sim 0.3\) are very implausible.
- \(^1{\rm S}_0\) nucleon-nucleon virtual state: scattering length \(a_{\rm exp} = 23.735(16)\) fm, effective range \(r_{\rm exp} = 2.68(3)\) fm, virtual binding momentum \(\gamma_v^{\rm exp} = 0.03999(5)\) fm\(^{-1}\) corresponding to \(E_v = -0.0663(4)\) MeV; the NLO accuracy \((\gamma_v^2/m_\pi^2) \sim 0.3\%\), and minimum \(\mathcal{L}\) values are found for \(Z < 0.1\).
- \(D^{\ast}_{s0}(2317)^\pm\): binding energy \(E_b^{\rm exp} = -45(4)\) MeV, binding momentum \(\gamma_b^{\rm exp} = 0.95(4)\) fm\(^{-1}\), scattering length \(a_{\rm exp} = -1.3(5)\) fm, effective range \(r_{\rm exp} = -0.1(3)\) fm; the NLO accuracy is \((\gamma_b^2/\beta^2) \sim 40\%\) with \(\beta \sim 300\) MeV, and results favor \(DK\) molecular probabilities of at least 50\%.
- \(T_{cc}^+\) (isospin-limit model): binding momentum \(\gamma_b^\text{ph} = 40.2(1.7)\) MeV, scattering length \(a_\text{ph} = -5.38(30)\) fm, effective range \(r_\text{ph} = 0.95(32)\) fm; the NLO accuracy \((\gamma_b^\text{ph}/\beta)^2 \simeq 8\%\) with \(\beta \simeq m_\pi\).

## Context

This work extends Weinberg's original compositeness relations (Weinberg, 1965) by incorporating NLO corrections in a model-independent way, addressing the well-known ambiguity that a naive evaluation of \(X = 1/\sqrt{1+2r/a}\) can give unphysical probabilities (e.g., \(X = 1.68 > 1\) for the deuteron). The approach is consistent with and builds upon previous efforts to refine compositeness criteria, including those by Matuschek et al. (2020), Esposito et al. (2021), Li et al. (2021), and Song et al. (2022). For the \(T_{cc}^+\) state, the authors note that coupled-channel effects (as discussed by Baru et al., 2021) can generate large negative effective ranges that would otherwise be misinterpreted as evidence for a compact component, and they adopt a simplified isospin-limit treatment based on the model of Albaladejo et al. (2021). The method enables a more quantitative assessment of molecular probabilities for shallow states, but the authors caution that for states with larger binding energies (like the \(D^{\ast}_{s0}(2317)^\pm\)), additional short-distance dynamics beyond the first two ERE parameters are needed for precise conclusions.
