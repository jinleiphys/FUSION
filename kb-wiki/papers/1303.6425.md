# Controlling complex Langevin dynamics at finite density

> At nonzero chemical potential the numerical sign problem in lattice field theory limits the use of standard algorithms based on importance sampling. Complex Langevin dynamics provides a possible solution, but it has to be applied with care. In this review, we first summarise our current understanding of the approach, combining analytical and numerical insight. In the second part we study SL(N,C) gauge cooling, which was introduced recently as a tool to control complex Langevin dynamics in nonabelian gauge theories. We present new results in Polyakov chain models and in QCD with heavy quarks and compare various adaptive cooling implementations.

## Key claim

Complex Langevin dynamics can solve the sign problem in lattice field theory at nonzero chemical potential, but only when the distribution in the complexified configuration space is sufficiently localised; gauge cooling provides a tool to enforce this localisation in nonabelian gauge theories, though the formal justification depends on the projection onto gauge orbits being unaffected by the cooling procedure.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical foundations of complex Langevin dynamics, contrasting it with real Langevin dynamics and emphasising that the Fokker-Planck Hamiltonian is no longer semi-positive definite for complex actions. They then study the distribution in the complexified configuration space using scatter plots and histograms, and introduce gauge cooling as a technique to control the distance from the unitary submanifold by applying complexified gauge transformations that minimise a unitarity norm. New numerical results are presented for Polyakov chain models (SU(2) and SU(3)) and for QCD with heavy quarks, comparing various adaptive cooling implementations.

## Key numbers

- SU(3) spin model critical coupling at μ=0: β_c = 0.133–0.137 (depending on h)
- XY model critical coupling at μ=0: β_c = 0.45421
- XY model: at β=0.7 (ordered phase) the action density is smooth in μ^2; at β=0.3 (disordered phase) a clear nonanalyticity appears, indicating failure of complex Langevin
- XY model: the width of the distribution in the imaginary direction, ⟨(Δφ_I)^2⟩, jumps discontinuously at small β when μ is increased from zero, while at large β it increases smoothly

## Context

This work extends the understanding of complex Langevin dynamics developed by Parisi (1984) and Klauder (1983), and addresses problems identified in earlier studies by Ambjorn (1985, 1986) and Berges (2006, 2007). The gauge cooling technique was introduced by Seiler (2012) and is here applied to Polyakov chain models and heavy-quark QCD, building on effective models constructed by Fromm (2011, 2012). The results enable further application of complex Langevin dynamics to full QCD at finite density, particularly by providing criteria for correctness based on the localisation of the distribution in the complexified configuration space.
