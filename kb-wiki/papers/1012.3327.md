# A new method of description of three-particle Coulombic systems

> We present a method for treatment of three charged particles. The proposed method has universal character and is applicable both for bound and continuum states. A finite rank approximation is used for Coulomb potential in three-body system Hamiltonian, that results in a system of one-dimensional coupled integral equations. Preliminary numerical results for three-body atomic and molecular systems like $H^{-}$, He, $ppμ$ and other are presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents a finite-rank approximation applied to the angular part of the Coulomb potential in hyperspherical coordinates, which reduces the three-body Schrodinger equation to a system of one-dimensional coupled integral equations; the method is claimed to be universal for both bound and continuum states, but the numerical results shown are only for bound states and are described as preliminary.

## Method

The authors express the three-body Hamiltonian in hyperspherical coordinates and replace the angular part of the Coulomb potential with a finite-rank operator constructed from a set of auxiliary hyperspherical functions. This substitution transforms the Schrodinger equation into a system of coupled one-dimensional integral equations for unknown functions \(C_i(\rho)\), with kernels involving the free Green function expanded in hyperspherical harmonics. The resulting integral equations are solved numerically by discretizing the hyperradius \(\rho\) with 100 mesh points and truncating the hypermomentum sum at \(K_{max} = 6, 10,\) and 14.

## Key numbers

- For \(H^{-}\), the precise binding energy is 14.34 eV; the finite-rank approximation with N=6 and \(K_{max}=14\) gives 15.6 eV.
- For \(He\), the precise binding energy is 79.0 eV; the finite-rank approximation with N=6 and \(K_{max}=14\) gives 85 eV.
- For \(H_{2}^{+}\), the precise binding energy is 16.25 eV; the finite-rank approximation with N=6 and \(K_{max}=14\) gives 15.1 eV.
- For \(pp\mu\), the precise binding energy is 2782 eV; the finite-rank approximation with N=6 and \(K_{max}=14\) gives 2332 eV.
- For \(dd\mu\), the precise binding energy is 2988 eV; the finite-rank approximation with N=6 and \(K_{max}=14\) gives 2654 eV.

## Context

This work extends the method of intermediate Hamiltonians originally introduced by Weinstein (1937) and later applied by Bazley and Fox (1961) to compute lower bounds for eigenvalues, but here the finite-rank approximation is applied in the angular subspace of hyperspherical variables rather than in the full Hilbert space. The approach was tested earlier on some three-body Coulombic systems by Belyaev and collaborators (2006). The method is intended to enable treatment of three charged particles in the continuum, though the present results are limited to bound states.
