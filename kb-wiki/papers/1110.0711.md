# Debye mass and heavy quark potential in a PNJL quark plasma

> We calculate the Debye mass for the screening of the heavy quark potential in a plasma of massless quarks coupled to the temporal gluon background governed by the Polyakov loop potential within the PNJL model in RPA approximation. We give a physical motivation for a recent phenomenological fit of lattice data by applying the calculated Debye mass with its suppression in the confined phase due to the Polyakov-loop to a description of the temperature dependence of the singlet free energy for QCD with a heavy quark pair at infinite separation. We compare the result to lattice data.

# Debye mass and heavy quark potential in a PNJL quark plasma

## Key claim
The Polyakov-loop suppressed Debye mass calculated in the PNJL model qualitatively reproduces the temperature dependence of the singlet free energy for a heavy quark pair at infinite separation seen in lattice QCD, but the calculated Debye mass is smaller than the fit from Riek and Rapp (2010) by about a factor of two, and the resulting free energy underestimates lattice data at low temperatures.

## Method
The authors compute the longitudinal polarization function Π₀₀ in the static long-wavelength limit using the random phase approximation (RPA) bubble resummation, with massless quark propagators coupled to a homogeneous temporal gluon background field via the Polyakov loop variable Φ(T). The resulting Debye mass m_D(T) is inserted into a phenomenological expression for the color singlet free energy that includes separate Debye masses for the Coulomb and string sectors, with the string tension fixed to σ = 0.198 GeV². The temperature-dependent Polyakov loop Φ(T) is taken from the nonlocal PNJL model of Blaschke (2007).

## Key numbers
- The Debye mass integral I(Φ) yields I(1)=1 in the deconfinement limit (Φ=1) and I(0)=1/9 in the confinement limit (Φ=0).
- The critical temperature is T_c ≈ 200 MeV.
- The string sector mass parameter is m_G² = 0.631 GeV², giving string tension σ = 0.198 GeV².
- The Coulombic Debye mass from fitting lattice data for high temperatures is m_D = 0.12 T, compared to the perturbative value of about 3.143 T for two flavors with α_s = 0.471.
- Charmonium dissociation occurs at T ≈ 200 MeV and bottomonium dissociation at T ≈ 250 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the PNJL model approach of Ratti (2005) and Hansen (2006) to heavy quark screening, providing a microscopic motivation for the phenomenological fit of the heavy quark free energy by Riek and Rapp (2010). The results enable further studies of quarkonium dissociation in a medium that incorporates confinement effects via the Polyakov loop, and the authors suggest extensions to include chiral symmetry effects through the local NJL interaction as in Hansen (2006).
