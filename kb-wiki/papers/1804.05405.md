# Monte Carlo Glauber model with meson cloud: predictions for 5.44 TeV Xe+Xe collisions

> We study, within the Monte-Carlo Glauber model, centrality dependence of the midrapidity charged multiplicity density $dN_{ch}/dη$ and of the anisotropy coefficients $\varepsilon_{2,3}$ in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV and in Xe+Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.44$ TeV. Calculations are performed for versions with and without nucleon meson cloud. The fraction of the binary collisions, $α$, has been fitted to the data on $dN_{ch}/dη$ in Pb+Pb collisions. We obtain $α\approx 0.09(0.13)$ with (without) meson cloud. The effect of meson cloud on the $dN_{ch}/dη$ is relatively small. For Xe+Xe collisions for $0$-$5$\% centrality bin we obtain $dN_{ch}/dη\approx 1149$ and $1134$ with and without meson cloud, respectively. We obtain $\varepsilon_2(\mbox{Xe})/\varepsilon_2(\mbox{Pb})\sim 1.45$ for most central collisions, and $\varepsilon_2(\mbox{Xe})/\varepsilon_2(\mbox{Pb})$ close to unity at $c\gtrsim 20$\%. We find a noticeable increase of the eccentricity in Xe+Xe collisions at small centralities due to the prolate shape of the Xe nucleus. The triangularity in Xe+Xe collisions is bigger than in Pb+Pb collisions at $c\lesssim 70$\%. We obtain $\varepsilon_3(\mbox{Xe})/\varepsilon_3(\mbox{Pb})\sim 1.3$ at $c\lesssim 1$\%.

## Key claim

The Monte Carlo Glauber model with a meson cloud component predicts that the effect of the meson cloud on the charged multiplicity density in Xe+Xe collisions at 5.44 TeV is relatively small (about 5% in the intermediate centrality region), and that the fraction of binary collisions required to fit Pb+Pb data is lower (alpha ≈ 0.09) when the meson cloud is included compared to the version without it (alpha ≈ 0.13).

## Method

The author performed Monte Carlo Glauber simulations for Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV and Xe+Xe collisions at 5.44 TeV, using a nucleon wave function that includes a meson-baryon Fock component with a total weight of 0.4. The parameters of the Gamma distribution for multiplicity fluctuations were fitted to pp data, and the fraction of binary collisions alpha was fitted to ALICE data on the centrality dependence of dNch/dη in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, for both the version with and without the meson cloud. The initial anisotropy coefficients ε2 and ε3 were then computed from the spatial entropy distribution using a Gaussian source smearing with widths of 0.7 and 0.4 fm, and the flow coefficients v2 and v3 were estimated using the linear response approximation with response coefficients from the hydrodynamic analysis of Olli (2017).

## Key numbers

- Fraction of binary collisions: α ≈ 0.09 with meson cloud, α ≈ 0.13 without meson cloud, fitted to Pb+Pb data at √s = 5.02 TeV
- For Xe+Xe collisions at √s = 5.44 TeV, 0-5% centrality bin: dNch/dη ≈ 1149 with meson cloud, 1134 without meson cloud
- Ratio ε2(Xe)/ε2(Pb) ≈ 1.45 for most central collisions (c ≲ 1%)
- Ratio ε3(Xe)/ε3(Pb) ≈ 1.3 at c ≲ 1%
- For Xe+Xe 0-5% central collisions, dNch/dη is smaller than in Pb+Pb by a factor of about 1.7, corresponding to a decrease in initial QGP temperature by a factor of about 1.2
- The prolate shape of the Xe nucleus increases the eccentricity by about 12% for most central collisions
- The hard-core repulsion reduces ε2 and ε3 by about 10% for most central collisions

## Context

This work extends the author's previous Monte Carlo Glauber model with meson cloud (Zakharov, 2016, 2017) to make predictions for Xe+Xe collisions at 5.44 TeV, complementing earlier studies by Olli (2017) using the TRENTO model and by Eskola (2017) using the EKRT model. The predictions for the anisotropy coefficients and flow coefficients can be tested against upcoming LHC data on Xe+Xe collisions, and the author notes that dynamical quantum effects from giant resonances in nuclei, which are ignored in the Woods-Saxon distributions used here, may need to be addressed in future work.
