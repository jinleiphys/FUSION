# Interface effects of strange quark matter

> The interface effects play important roles for the properties of strange quark matter (SQM) and the related physical processes. We show several examples on the implications of interface effects for both stable and unstable SQM. Based on an equivparticle model and adopting mean-field approximation (MFA), the surface tension and curvature term of SQM can be obtained, which are increasing monotonically with the density of SQM at zero external pressure. For a parameter set constrained according to the 2$M_\odot$ strange star, we find the surface tension is $\sim$2.4 MeV/fm${}^2$, while it is larger for other cases.

## Key claim
The interface effects of strange quark matter (SQM) are crucial for both stable and unstable SQM properties, but the surface tension value depends strongly on the chosen model parameters; for a parameter set constrained by the 2 solar mass strange star observation, the surface tension is approximately 2.4 MeV/fm², while other parameter choices yield larger values.

## Method
The authors adopt an equivparticle model with density-dependent quark masses and mean-field approximation, solving the Dirac equation for quarks and the Klein-Gordon equation for the photon field self-consistently for spherically symmetric strangelets. The quark mass scaling includes a linear confinement term (inversely cubic in baryon density) and a cubic term representing one-gluon-exchange or perturbative interactions. The surface tension and curvature term are extracted by fitting the energy per baryon of strangelets to a liquid-drop type formula.

## Key numbers
- For the parameter set (C=0.7, √D=129 MeV) constrained by the 2 solar mass strange star PSR J0348+0432, the surface tension is σ ≈ 2.4 MeV/fm² and the curvature term is λ ≈ 5.12 MeV/fm.
- The bulk baryon number density n₀ for this parameter set is 0.099 fm⁻³, with minimum energy per baryon E₀/n₀ = 918.94 MeV.
- For other parameter sets, surface tension values range from 2.4 MeV/fm² (C=0.7, √D=129 MeV) to 6.3 MeV/fm² (C=-0.5, √D=180 MeV), with corresponding curvature terms from 5.12 MeV/fm to 15.2 MeV/fm.
- The surface tension and curvature term increase monotonically with the saturation density n₀, approximated by σ ≈ 14.3 n₀ + 1.3 and λ ≈ 36.6 n₀ + 1.3 (units as in Table 1).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of SQM interface effects using the MIT bag model (Oertel, 2008), Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Lugones, 2013; Lugones, 2017), and quasiparticle model (Wen, 2010), which reported surface tensions ranging from 5-30 MeV/fm² up to 145-165 MeV/fm². The present equivparticle model with linear confinement predicts smaller surface tensions (2.4-6.3 MeV/fm²) and provides a systematic parameter dependence. The results enable more precise modeling of strangelet stability, hybrid star structure, and tidal deformabilities relevant to binary neutron star merger events like GW170817.
