# Quark-Hadron Phase-Transition in an Extended NJL Model with Scalar-Vector Interaction

> The quark-hadron phase transition at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential is investigated in an extended NJL model with scalar-vector eight-point interaction by comparing the pressure of symmetric nuclear matter with that of the quark matter. As a result, the extended NJL phase diagram is obtained in the temperature-baryon chemical potential plane and the effects of the scalar-vector coupling constant G_{sv}^q on the chiral phase transition are summarized. It is shown that a quarkyonic-like phase in which the chiral symmetry is restored but the elementary excitation modes are nucleonic appears just before deconfinement in this model.

## Key claim

The quark-hadron phase transition occurs after the chiral phase transition in nuclear matter, and a quarkyonic-like phase (chirally symmetric but with nucleonic excitation modes) appears just before deconfinement in this extended NJL model, but only for specific values of the scalar-vector coupling constant \(G_{sv}^q\).

## Method

The authors compared the pressure of symmetric nuclear matter (described by an extended NJL model with scalar-vector eight-point interaction, treating nucleons as fundamental fermions with \(N_c=1\)) with that of quark matter (described by the same model with \(N_c=3\) and \(G_v^q=0\)) at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential. The realized phase was determined by finding the crossing point of the two pressure curves under the chemical equilibrium condition \(\mu_N = 3\mu_q\). The gap equation for the dynamical quark mass was solved self-consistently, and the chiral phase transition was identified from the pressure as a function of quark chemical potential.

## Key numbers

* Dynamical quark mass in vacuum: \(m_q = 313\) MeV, determined from the pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV.
* For \(G_{sv}^q\Lambda_q^8 = -68.4\) at \(T=0\): chiral phase transition occurs at \(\mu_q \approx 326\) MeV; the first-order chiral transition spans from \(\rho_q \sim 0.38\rho_N^0\) to \(\rho_q \sim 5.41\rho_N^0\).
* For \(G_{sv}^q\Lambda_q^8 = -68.4\) at \(T=0\): quark-hadron phase transition occurs at \(\mu_B \approx 1236\) MeV, with coexistence from \(\rho_B \sim 2.64\rho_N^0\) to \(\rho_B \sim 3.63\rho_N^0\).
* For \(G_{sv}^q\Lambda_q^8 = -68.4\) at \(T=20\) MeV: quark-hadron phase transition occurs at \(\mu_B \approx 1190\) MeV, with coexistence from \(\rho_B \sim 2.49\rho_N^0\) to \(\rho_B \sim 3.33\rho_N^0\).
* For \(G_{sv}^q\Lambda_q^8 = -68.4\) at \(T=40\) MeV: the first-order quark-hadron phase transition disappears; the chiral phase transition becomes second order at \(\mu_q \approx 318\) MeV.
* Nuclear matter parameters: normal nuclear density \(\rho_N^0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\), nucleon mass at normal density \(m_N(\rho_N^0) = 0.6 m_N(\rho_N=0)\), saturation energy \(W_N(\rho_N^0) = -15\) MeV, incompressibility \(K \approx 260\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature results of the same authors (Lee, Tsue, da Providencia, Providencia, and Yamamura, 2012) to finite temperature and chemical potential, using an extended NJL model that incorporates scalar-vector eight-point interactions to reproduce nuclear saturation properties (following the approach of K. B. K. M. et al. and Buballa (1996)). The model predicts a quarkyonic-like phase that appears before deconfinement, which contrasts with standard NJL treatments that lack such a phase. The results enable future studies of the phase diagram with diquark correlations and more realistic quark matter equations of state for neutron star applications.
