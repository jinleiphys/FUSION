# Late-time asymptotic solutions, attractor, and focusing behavior of spin hydrodynamics

> We have investigated the late-time asymptotic solutions, attractor, and focusing behavior of minimal causal spin hydrodynamics in Bjorken expansion. Using the method of dominant balance, we derive the late-time asymptotic solutions of the evolution equation for spin density and identify the specific conditions necessary for the spin density to exhibit a power-law decay. We then analyze both the late-time and early-time attractors for the decay rate of spin density. Additionally, we report the focusing behavior in spin hydrodynamics, which has not been found in conventional relativistic hydrodynamics in Bjorken expansion. Our findings suggest that spin density can be treated as a conventional hydrodynamic variable at late times under certain conditions.

## Key claim

Under the specific condition that the parameter \(\Delta_2 \leq -1\) (which corresponds to a sufficiently weak enhancement of the spin source term response at late times), the spin density in minimal causal spin hydrodynamics exhibits a power-law decay rather than an exponential decay, allowing it to be treated as a conventional hydrodynamic variable at late times; otherwise, the spin density decays exponentially and becomes negligible.

## Method

The authors studied the evolution equation for spin density in Bjorken-expanding spin hydrodynamics, parameterizing the inverse Knudsen number \(w = \tau/\tau_\phi\) and the combination \(\tau_\phi \gamma / \chi\) as power laws in proper time with exponents \(\Delta_1\) and \(\Delta_2\). They applied the method of dominant balance to derive the late-time asymptotic solutions of the spin density equation, and analyzed both late-time and early-time attractors for the relative decay rate of spin density.

## Key numbers

* For \(\Delta_2 = -1\), the power-law decaying solution behaves as \(S_{(2)} \sim w^{-(1+8\alpha)/\Delta_1}\) as \(w \rightarrow +\infty\), where \(\alpha = \tau_\phi \gamma / \chi\) at the reference scale.
* For \(\Delta_2 < -1\), the power-law decaying solution behaves as \(S_{(2)} \sim w^{-1/\Delta_1}\) as \(w \rightarrow +\infty\).
* The energy density in the Bjorken background decays as \(e = e_0 (\tau_0/\tau)^{4/3}\), with speed of sound \(c_s = 1/\sqrt{3}\).
* The domain where spin density can be treated as a hydrodynamic variable is \(\Delta_1 > 0\) and \(\Delta_2 \leq -1\).

## Context

This work extends the study of hydrodynamic attractors, previously established in conventional relativistic hydrodynamics by Heller (2015) and others, to the novel context of spin hydrodynamics. It addresses the open question of whether spin density can survive at late times, given that linear mode analyses around homogeneous equilibrium (e.g., Hattori 2019, Hongo 2021) suggested spin corresponds to non-hydrodynamic modes that decay rapidly. The findings enable the use of attractor methods to circumvent the problem of unknown initial spin density in heavy-ion collision simulations.
