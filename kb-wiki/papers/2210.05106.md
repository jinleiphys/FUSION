# Photoproduction of $e^{+}e^{-}$ in peripheral isobar collisions

> We investigate the photoproduction of di-electrons in peripheral collisions of $_{44}^{96}\textrm{Ru}+_{44}^{96}\textrm{Ru}$ and $_{40}^{96}\textrm{Zr}+_{40}^{96}\textrm{Zr}$ at 200 GeV. With the charge and mass density distributions given by the calculation of the density functional theory, we calculate the spectra of transverse momentum, invariant mass and azimuthal angle for di-electrons at 40-80\% centrality. The ratios of these spectra in Ru+Ru collisions over to Zr+Zr collisions are shown to be smaller than $(44/40)^{4}$ (the ratio of $Z^{4}$ for Ru and Zr) at low transverse momentum. The deviation arises from the different mass and charge density distributions in Ru and Zr. So the photoproduction of di-leptons in isobar collisions may provide a new way to probe the nuclear structure.

## Key claim

The ratios of dielectron spectra (transverse momentum, invariant mass, azimuthal angle) in Ru+Ru collisions over Zr+Zr collisions at 40-80% centrality are smaller than (44/40)^4 (the ratio of Z^4 for Ru and Zr) at low transverse momentum, with the deviation arising from different mass and charge density distributions in the two isobar nuclei.

## Method

The authors used a QED model in the classical field approximation with a wave-packet description of nuclei to calculate the Born-level cross section for photoproduction of e+e- pairs. Nuclear charge and mass density distributions were obtained from density functional theory (DFT) calculations and parameterized with Woods-Saxon distributions, with the charge density used for the nuclear form factor in the cross section and the mass density used to determine centrality via an optical Glauber model. The high-dimensional integration in the cross section was implemented using the ZMCintegral package, with kinematic cuts following STAR experiments (electron/positron transverse momentum > 200 MeV, pair rapidity in [-1,1]).

## Key numbers

- The Woods-Saxon radius parameter for charge density is 5.083 fm for Ru and 4.977 fm for Zr; the skin depth for charge density is 0.477 fm for Ru and 0.492 fm for Zr.
- The Woods-Saxon radius parameter for mass density is 5.093 fm for Ru and 5.022 fm for Zr; the skin depth for mass density is 0.488 fm for Ru and 0.538 fm for Zr.
- At 40-60% centrality, the integrated excess yield ratio Ru/Zr is 1.441, compared to (44/40)^4 ≈ 1.4641.
- At 70-80% centrality, the integrated excess yield ratio Ru/Zr is 1.457.
- The differential cross section spectra for P_T^ee peak at approximately 45 MeV.
- The invariant mass spectra peak at approximately 0.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical methods for lepton pair photoproduction developed by Wang et al. (2021, 2022) to isobar collisions of Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr at RHIC. It builds on the recognition from Xu et al. (2017) and Li et al. (2018) that nuclear density distributions differ between isobars, which affected the baseline for chiral magnetic effect searches in STAR's isobar data (2021). The results suggest that photoproduction of lepton pairs in isobar collisions can serve as a new probe of nuclear structure, complementing studies of multiplicity distributions.
