# T-dependence of the axion mass when the U_A(1) and chiral symmetry breaking are tied

> Modulo the scale of spontaneous breaking of Peccei-Quinn symmetry, the axion mass $m_a(T)$ is given by the QCD topological susceptibility $χ(T)$ at all temperatures $T$. From an approach tying the $U_A(1)$ and chiral symmetry breaking and getting good $T$-dependence of $η$ and $η$ mesons, we get $χ(T)$ for an effective Dyson-Schwinger model of nonperturbative QCD. Comparison with lattice results for $χ(T)$, and thus also for $m_a(T)$, shows good agreement for temperatures ranging from zero up to the double of the chiral restoration temperature $T_c$.

# T-dependence of the axion mass when the U_A(1) and chiral symmetry breaking are tied

## Key claim

The temperature dependence of the axion mass m_a(T) is determined by the QCD topological susceptibility χ(T), which the authors compute from an effective Dyson-Schwinger model that ties U_A(1) and chiral symmetry breaking; the model agrees with lattice results for temperatures from zero up to approximately 2.3 T_c, but the dynamical model can only access temperatures below 2.4 T_c, unlike lattice calculations which extend further.

## Method

The authors compute the QCD topological susceptibility χ(T) using the leading term of the light-quark-sector relation χ(T) = -1 / (1/(m_u ⟨ūu(T)⟩) + 1/(m_d ⟨dd(T)⟩) + 1/(m_s ⟨ss(T)⟩)), where the quark condensates ⟨qq⟩ are obtained from a Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) model of nonperturbative QCD that has been previously applied to the T-dependence of η and η' mesons. The axion mass is then obtained from m_a²(T) f_a² = χ(T), which holds at all temperatures modulo negligible corrections of order M_π²/f_a².

## Key numbers

- The model results for χ(T)^{1/4} (and thus √(m_a(T) f_a)) are compared with lattice data up to T ≈ 2.3 T_c
- The dynamical model can only access temperatures below 2.4 T_c
- The experimental bound on the Θ parameter is |Θ| < 10^{-10} (Baker 2006)
- The correction to the relation m_a²(T) f_a² = χ(T) is of order M_π²/f_a², where M_π is the pion mass

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous DSE-based approach (Horvatic 2018) that successfully described the T-dependence of η and η' meson masses by tying U_A(1) and chiral symmetry breaking. The results are qualitatively similar to those from the NJL model (Lu 2018) and show smooth crossover behavior consistent with lattice results (Petreczky 2016, Borsanyi 2016), in contrast to using massless quark condensates which would predict a sharp second-order transition at T_c. The approach enables predictions for axion dark matter production in the early universe, as the axion mass temperature dependence governs misalignment production mechanisms.
