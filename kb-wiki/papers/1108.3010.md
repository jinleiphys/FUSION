# Search for Narrow Nucleon Resonance in $γp\to ηp$

> Results of a partial wave analysis of new high-statistics data on $γp\to pη$ from MAMI are presented. A fit using known broad resonances and only standard background amplitudes can not describe the relatively narrow peaking structure in the cross section in the mass region of 1660-1750 MeV which follows a minimum. An improved description of the data can be reached by either assuming the existence of a narrow resonance at a mass of about 1700 MeV with small photo-coupling or by a threshold effect. In the latter case the observed structure is explained by a strong (resonant or non-resonant) $γp\toωp$ coupling in the $S_{11}$ partial wave. When the beam asymmetry data, published by part of the GRAAL collaboration, are included in the fit, the solution with a narrow $P_{11}$ state is slightly preferred. In that fit, mass and width of the hypothetical resonance are determined to $M\sim$1694 MeV and $Γ\sim 40$ MeV, respectively, and the photo-coupling to $\sqrt{{\rm Br}_{ηN}} A_{1/2}^p \sim 2.6\cdot 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$. High precision measurements of the target asymmetry and $F$-observable are mandatory to establish the possible existence of such a narrow state and to provide the necessary information to define which partial wave is responsible for the structure observed in the data.

## Key claim

A partial wave analysis of high-statistics MAMI data on $\gamma p\to p\eta$ finds that fits using only known broad resonances and standard background amplitudes cannot describe a narrow peaking structure in the cross section near 1700 MeV; the structure can be explained either by a narrow resonance at about 1694-1719 MeV with small photo-coupling or by a threshold effect from the $\omega p$ channel, with the narrow $P_{11}$ resonance solution slightly preferred when beam asymmetry data from part of the GRAAL collaboration are included.

## Method

The authors performed a partial wave analysis (PWA) using the Bonn-Gatchina coupled-channel framework, incorporating the new MAMI data on $\gamma p\to \eta p$ into a large database of pion- and photo-induced reactions. They re-optimized parameters of two existing solutions (BG2010-01 and BG2010-02) and then tested five scenarios: no narrow resonance, two $P_{11}$ narrow states (positive and negative interference), a $P_{13}$ narrow state, an $S_{11}$ narrow state, and an $S_{11}$ wave with an $\omega p$ channel contribution. The fits were evaluated using $\chi^2$ per data point for the total dataset, the 1660-1750 MeV mass region, and beam asymmetry data.

## Key numbers

- The narrow $P_{11}(-)$ resonance has mass $M\sim 1694$ MeV, width $\Gamma\sim 41$ MeV, and photo-coupling $\sqrt{{\rm Br}_{\eta N}} A_{1/2}^p \sim 2.6\cdot 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$.
- The narrow $P_{11}(+)$ resonance has mass $M\sim 1719$ MeV, width $\Gamma\sim 44$ MeV, and photo-coupling $\sqrt{{\rm Br}_{\eta N}} A_{1/2}^p \sim 2.75\cdot 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$.
- The narrow $P_{13}$ state has mass $M\sim 1728$ MeV, width $\Gamma\sim 72$ MeV, and photo-couplings $\sqrt{{\rm Br}_{\eta N}} A_{1/2}^p \sim 2.45\cdot 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ and $\sqrt{{\rm Br}_{\eta N}} A_{3/2}^p \sim 4.5\cdot 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$.
- The narrow $S_{11}$ state has mass $M\sim 1696$ MeV, width $\Gamma\sim 31$ MeV, and photo-coupling $\sqrt{{\rm Br}_{\eta N}} A_{1/2}^p \sim 0.77\cdot 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$.
- Without any narrow resonance, the fit yields $\chi^2_{\rm sel}/N_{\rm dat}=1.46$ in the 1660-1750 MeV mass region; the $P_{11}(+)$ solution improves this to $0.90$, and the $P_{11}(-)$ solution to $0.92$.
- The $\omega p$ channel solution in $S_{11}$ gives $\chi^2_{\rm sel}/N_{\rm dat}=0.94$ in the same mass region.

## Context

This work extends the search for a narrow nucleon resonance predicted by the chiral quark soliton model (chiQSM) as part of an SU(3) anti-decuplet, following earlier predictions by Diakonov et al. (1997) and Arndt et al. (2003) that such a state would have suppressed couplings to $\pi N$ and $\gamma p$. It directly addresses the MAMI data of the Crystal Ball Collaboration (2011) and the beam asymmetry data from part of the GRAAL collaboration (Kuznetsov et al., 2007, 2009), and contrasts with the SAID GE09 fit interpretation that found no evidence for a narrow bump. The results enable future high-precision measurements of target asymmetry and the $F$-observable to distinguish between the $S_{11}$ threshold effect, $P_{11}$, $P_{13}$, and $S_{11}$ resonance interpretations.
