# On isospin-breaking corrections to the energy level displacement of the ground state of kaonic hydrogen

> In the model of low-energy bar-K N interactions near threshold (EPJA 21, 11 (2004); 25, 79 (2005)) we calculate isospin-breaking corrections to the energy level displacement of the ground state of kaonic hydrogen, investigated by Meissner, Raha and Rusetsky (EPJC 35, 349 (2004)) within the non-relativistic effective Lagrangian approach based on ChPT by Gasser and Leutwyler. Our results agree well with those by Meißner et al.. In addition we calculate the dispersive corrections, caused by the transition K^-p -> bar-K^0n ->K^-p with the bar-K^0n pair on-mass shell. We show also how hypothesis on the dominant role of the bar-K^0n-cusp for the S-wave amplitude of low-energy K^-p scattering near threshold, used by Meissner et al., can be realized in our approach. The result agrees fully with that by Meissner et al..

## Key claim

The authors calculate isospin-breaking corrections to the energy level displacement of the ground state of kaonic hydrogen within their own model of low-energy \(\bar{K}N\) interactions, confirming the smallness of these corrections (of order \(O(\alpha)\)) as asserted by Meissner, Raha and Rusetsky (2004), while also computing additional dispersive corrections from the on-mass-shell \(\bar{K}^0n\) intermediate state that were not addressed in that prior work.

## Method

The calculation uses a quantum field theoretic, relativistic covariant model of low-energy \(\bar{K}N\) interactions near threshold, formulated with effective chiral Lagrangians and treating strange baryon resonances (\(\Lambda(1405)\), \(\Sigma(1750)\)) and exotic scalar mesons (\(a_0(980)\), \(f_0(980)\)) as elementary particle fields. The energy level displacement is obtained from the S-wave \(K^-p\) scattering amplitude at threshold via a Deser-type formula, with isospin-breaking corrections computed to leading order in the quark mass difference \((m_d - m_u)\) and the dispersive corrections evaluated from the two-step transition \(K^-p \to \bar{K}^0n \to K^-p\) with the \(\bar{K}^0n\) pair on-mass shell.

## Key numbers

* The S-wave scattering lengths of \(\bar{K}N\) scattering used are \(a^0_0 = (-1.221 \pm 0.072) + i\,(0.537 \pm 0.064)\,\text{fm}\) and \(a^1_0 = (+0.258 \pm 0.024) + i\,(0.001 \pm 0.000)\,\text{fm}\).
* The dispersive corrections from the on-mass-shell \(\bar{K}^0n\) intermediate state are \(\delta^{\text{Disp}}_S = (4.8 \pm 0.4)\%\) for the shift and \(\delta^{\text{Disp}}_W = (8.0 \pm 1.0)\%\) for the width.
* The isospin-breaking corrections from QCD are \(\delta^{\text{ISB}}_S = 0.04\%\) and \(\delta^{\text{ISB}}_W = -0.04\%\).
* The relative momentum of the \(\bar{K}^0n\) pair below threshold is \(q_0 = 58.4\,\text{MeV}\), computed from \(m_{\bar{K}^0} - m_{K^-} = 3.97\,\text{MeV}\) and \(m_n - m_p = 1.29\,\text{MeV}\).
* The S-wave amplitude of \(K^-p\) scattering at threshold from the authors' earlier work is \(f^{K^-p}_0(0) = (-0.482 \pm 0.034) + i\,(0.269 \pm 0.032)\,\text{fm}\), giving an energy level displacement of \((-203 \pm 15) + i\,(113 \pm 14)\,\text{eV}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier model of low-energy \(\bar{K}N\) interactions (Ivanov et al., 2004; 2005) by incorporating isospin-breaking corrections, directly comparing with the non-relativistic effective Lagrangian approach of Meissner, Raha and Rusetsky (2004) based on chiral perturbation theory by Gasser and Leutwyler. The results confirm the smallness of the QCD isospin-breaking corrections claimed by Meissner et al., while additionally computing dispersive corrections from the on-shell \(\bar{K}^0n\) intermediate state that were not treated in that prior work; these dispersive corrections are specific to the atomic bound-state calculation and do not affect the threshold scattering amplitude. The analysis also shows how the hypothesis of \(\bar{K}^0n\)-cusp dominance for the S-wave \(K^-p\) amplitude, originally proposed by Dalitz and Tuan (1960), can be realized within the authors' approach, and the paper calls for more precise experimental data from the DEAR and KEK collaborations to test this hypothesis.
