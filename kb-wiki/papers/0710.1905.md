# Nonextensive hydrodynamics for relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The nonextensive one-dimensional version of a hydrodynamical model for multiparticle production processes is proposed and discussed. It is based on nonextensive statistics assumed in the form proposed by Tsallis and characterized by a nonextensivity parameter $q$. In this formulation the parameter $q$ characterizes some specific form of local equilibrium which is characteristic for the nonextensive thermodynamics and which replaces the usual local thermal equilibrium assumption of the usual hydrodynamical models. We argue that there is correspondence between the perfect nonextensive hydrodynamics and the usual dissipative hydrodynamics. It leads to simple expression for dissipative entropy current and allows for predictions for the ratio of bulk and shear viscosities to entropy density, $ζ/s$ and $η/s$, to be made.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a one-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamical model based on Tsallis nonextensive statistics, where the nonextensivity parameter \(q\) replaces the usual local thermal equilibrium assumption with a specific form of stationary state. The authors argue that this "perfect nonextensive hydrodynamics" corresponds to usual dissipative hydrodynamics, allowing predictions for the ratio of bulk and shear viscosities to entropy density, \(\zeta/s\) and \(\eta/s\), to be made, but only for the case where \(q\) remains constant throughout the collision process.

## Method

The authors formulate a covariant \((1+1)\)-dimensional hydrodynamical model starting from a nonextensive version of the Boltzmann equation with a \(q\)-generalized molecular chaos hypothesis. They derive the energy-momentum tensor and entropy current from the Tsallis distribution function, assuming a fixed nonextensivity parameter \(q\) and a baryon-free pion gas equation of state. The model is solved for longitudinal expansion using Gaussian initial conditions for the energy density, with the total energy fixed to match RHIC conditions, and the parameter \(q\) is varied to study its effect on the initial conditions and subsequent evolution.

## Key numbers

*   For single-particle distributions in longitudinal phase-space (\(dN/dy\)), the nonextensivity parameter is \(q_{\rm L} - 1 \sim 0.1 \div 0.2\).
*   For transverse momentum distributions (\(dN/dp_{\rm T}\)), the nonextensivity parameter is \(q_{\rm T} - 1 \sim 0.01 \div 0.05\).
*   The initial energy density at \(\eta=0\) for the fixed-width initial condition (\(\sigma=1.25\)) is \(\varepsilon^{\rm (in)} = 27.8\) GeV/fm\(^3\) for \(q=1.00\), and the corresponding initial temperature is \(T_{\rm in} = 0.702\) GeV for the Boltzmann-Gibbs pion gas.
*   For the fixed-maximum-energy-density initial condition (\(\varepsilon^{\rm (in)}=28.7\) GeV/fm\(^3\)), the width \(\sigma\) is 1.25 for \(q=1.00\), and the initial temperature is \(T_{\rm in} = 0.702\) GeV for the Boltzmann-Gibbs pion gas.
*   The total energy allocated to the fluid is fixed at \(E_{\rm tot} = 26.1\) TeV for all calculations.

## Context

This work extends previous applications of Tsallis statistics to multiparticle production, which had been limited to fitting distribution functions (e.g., by Wilk and collaborators) or deriving them from a modified Boltzmann equation (e.g., by Lavagno). It contrasts with all existing hydrodynamical models for heavy-ion collisions, which are based on Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics, and aims to provide a framework that incorporates intrinsic fluctuations and long-range correlations without the full complexity of dissipative hydrodynamics. The proposed "nonextensive/dissipative correspondence" (NexDC) offers a simpler perfect-fluid approach that can mimic dissipative effects, potentially enabling predictions for transport coefficients like \(\zeta/s\) and \(\eta/s\) that are currently of interest due to AdS/CFT calculations (Kovtun, 2005).
