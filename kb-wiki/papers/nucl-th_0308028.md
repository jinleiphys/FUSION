# Multiparton Tomography of Hot and Cold Nuclear Matter

> Multiple parton interactions in relativistic heavy ion reactions result in transverse momentum diffusion and medium induced non-Abelian energy loss of the hard probes traversing cold and hot nuclear matter. A systematic study of the interplay of nuclear effects on the $p_T \geq 2$ GeV inclusive hadron spectra demonstrates that the competition between nuclear shadowing, multiple scattering and jet quenching leads to distinctly different enhancement/suppression of moderate and high-$p_T$ hadron production in $d+Au$ and $Au+Au$ collisions at RHIC. The associated increase of di-jet acoplanarity, measured via the broadening of the back-to-back di-hadron correlation function, provides an additional experimental tool to test the difference in the dynamical properties of the media created in such reactions.

# Multiparton Tomography of Hot and Cold Nuclear Matter

## Key claim
The interplay of nuclear shadowing, multiple scattering, and jet quenching leads to distinctly different enhancement or suppression of moderate and high-$p_T$ hadron production in $d+Au$ versus $Au+Au$ collisions at RHIC, but the quantitative predictions depend on the assumed medium expansion scenario (static versus 1+1D Bjorken) and on the effective gluon density and transport coefficients extracted from existing Cronin data.

## Method
The author incorporates initial-state parton broadening, nuclear shadowing, and medium-induced radiative energy loss into a lowest-order pQCD formalism for inclusive hadron production at $p_T \geq 2$ GeV. The mean transverse momentum broadening $\langle \Delta \mathbf{k}_T^2 \rangle$ and mean energy loss $\langle \Delta E \rangle$ are computed using the GLV approach, with expressions given for both static and 1+1D Bjorken-expanding media. The nuclear modification ratio $R_{AB}(p_T)$ is calculated and compared to PHENIX and STAR data for $Au+Au$ and $d+Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ AGeV, and di-jet acoplanarity is studied via the broadening of the away-side di-hadron correlation function.

## Key numbers
- Effective gluon rapidity density used in calculations: $dN^g/dy = 1150$, consistent with inclusive hadron suppression pattern in $Au+Au$.
- Cold nuclear matter transport coefficient for gluons constrained from existing Cronin data: $({\mu^2}/{\lambda}_g)_{eff} = 2 \times 0.14$ GeV$^2$/fm, with a typical range of $2 \times 0.1$ GeV$^2$/fm to $2 \times 0.15$ GeV$^2$/fm.
- Initial energy density of the deconfined QCD phase at RHIC inferred from jet tomography: $\sim 20$ GeV/fm$^3$, more than 100 times the $1/7$ GeV/fm$^3$ density of cold nuclear matter.
- The centrality dependence of the quenching magnitude is set by $(\langle L \rangle / A_\perp) dN^g/dy \propto N_{part}^{2/3}$.

## Context
This work extends the GLV approach of Gyulassy (2000) and the broadening formalism of Qiu (2003) to systematically compare cold (d+Au) and hot (Au+Au) nuclear matter effects at RHIC. It builds on prior calculations by Vitev (2002) and Arleo (2002) for jet tomography, and uses the Cronin effect data from low-energy p+A measurements to constrain the cold matter transport coefficient. The results enable experimental tests of the dynamical properties of the created medium via di-hadron correlations, and rule out the scenario where high-$p_T$ hadron suppression originates solely from initial-state wavefunction modification (which would give $R_{dAu} \approx \sqrt{R_{AuAu}}$).
