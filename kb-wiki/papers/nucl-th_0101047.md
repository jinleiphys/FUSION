# Current Status of Quark Gluon Plasma Signals

> Compelling evidence for the creation of a new form of matter has been claimed to be found in Pb+Pb collisions at SPS. We discuss the uniqueness of often proposed experimental signatures for quark matter formation in relativistic heavy ion collisions. It is demonstrated that so far none of the proposed signals like Jψmeson production/suppression, strangeness enhancement, dileptons, and directed flow unambigiously show that a phase of deconfined matter has been formed in SPS Pb+Pb collisions. We emphasize the need for systematic future measurements to search for simultaneous irregularities in the excitation functions of several observables in order to come close to pinning the properties of hot, dense QCD matter from data.

# Current Status of Quark Gluon Plasma Signals

## Key claim

The paper argues that none of the proposed experimental signatures for quark-gluon plasma formation in SPS Pb+Pb collisions, including J/ψ meson production/suppression, strangeness enhancement, dileptons, and directed flow, unambiguously demonstrate that a phase of deconfined matter has been formed, and that systematic future measurements searching for simultaneous irregularities in excitation functions of multiple observables are needed to identify the properties of hot, dense QCD matter.

## Method

The authors review experimental data from SPS Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions and compare them to predictions from microscopic transport models (UrQMD), macroscopic three-fluid hydrodynamics with a first-order phase transition, a chiral SU(3) model for particle ratios, and a quark molecular dynamics (qMD) model. They evaluate J/ψ suppression using nonperturbative QCD cross sections with charmonium wave functions from a non-relativistic model, including formation length effects and comoving meson interactions via UrQMD, and compare calculated J/ψ to Drell-Yan ratios to NA50 data.

## Key numbers

- σ_abs(J/ψ–N) = 3.5 ± 0.8 mb from SLAC data at E_inc ~ 20 GeV
- Calculated charmonium-nucleon cross sections: J/ψ = 3.62 mb, ψ' = 20.0 mb, χ_c10 = 6.82 mb, χ_c11 = 15.9 mb
- Formation length of J/ψ at SPS midrapidity: l_f ≈ 3 fm; for ψ': l_f ≈ 6 fm
- Best-fit parameters for ideal gas model to Pb+Pb SPS particle ratios: T = 168 MeV, μ_q = 88.67 MeV
- Best-fit parameters for chiral SU(3) model: T = 144 MeV, μ_q ≈ 95 MeV (temperature lowered by more than 20 MeV relative to ideal gas)
- Directed flow excitation function shows local minimum at 8 AGeV and maximum around 40 AGeV in three-fluid hydrodynamics
- Elliptic flow v_2 at √s_NN = 130 GeV: average value of 6% at midrapidity and p_t ≈ 600 MeV; UrQMD underpredicts this by a factor of 3
- Ratio of hadrons formed by quarks from different initial hadrons versus same initial hadron in qMD for S+Au at 200 GeV/N: r = 0.574 ± 0.008
- Diffusion lengths from qMD: 2.2 fm for quarks from same initial hadron, 4.8 fm for quarks from different initial hadrons

## Context

This review extends the critical assessment of QGP signatures following earlier claims of deconfined matter formation at SPS, and directly challenges interpretations from Kharzeev (1996) on J/ψ suppression and from Rafelski and Müller (1982) on strangeness enhancement as unambiguous QGP signals. The paper enables future work by identifying the need for excitation function measurements across multiple observables, particularly at beam energies between AGS (10 AGeV) and SPS (160 AGeV), and by demonstrating that hadronic models with rescattering (UrQMD) and multi-mesonic reactions (Greiner, 2000) can reproduce many observables without invoking a deconfined phase. The results set the stage for RHIC and LHC programs to search for simultaneous irregularities in flow, particle ratios, and heavy quarkonia.
