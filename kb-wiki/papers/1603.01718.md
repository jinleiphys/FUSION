# Extracting the resonance parameters from experimental data on scattering of charged particles

> A new parametrization of the multi-channel S-matrix is used to fit scattering data and then to locate the resonances as its poles. The S-matrix is written in terms of the corresponding "in" and "out" Jost matrices which are expanded in the Taylor series of the collision energy E around an appropriately chosen energy E0. In order to do this, the Jost matrices are written in a semi-analytic form where all the factors (involving the channel momenta and Sommerfeld parameters) responsible for their "bad behaviour" (i.e. responsible for the multi-valuedness of the Jost matrices and for branching of the Riemann surface of the energy) are given explicitly. The remaining unknown factors in the Jost matrices are analytic and single-valued functions of the variable E and are defined on a simple energy plane. The expansion is done for these analytic functions and the expansion coefficients are used as the fitting parameters. The method is tested on a two-channel model, using a set of artificially generated data points with typical error bars and a typical random noise in the positions of the points.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new parametrization of the multi-channel S-matrix for extracting resonance parameters from scattering data of charged particles, where the Jost matrices are written in a semi-analytic form that explicitly factors out the multi-valued behavior (channel momenta and Sommerfeld parameters), leaving only analytic, single-valued functions to be expanded in Taylor series. The method is tested on a two-channel model with artificially generated data, and the authors note that while the approach is non-relativistic and cannot be directly used in high-energy physics, it could be applied to atomic and low-energy nuclear physics problems.

## Method

The authors express the N-channel Jost matrix in a semi-analytic form where all factors responsible for the multi-valuedness on the Riemann surface of the energy (channel momenta and Sommerfeld parameters) are given explicitly, leaving unknown matrices A(E) and B(E) that are analytic and single-valued functions of energy. These analytic functions are expanded in Taylor series around a chosen energy E0, and the expansion coefficients (real matrices when E0 is on the real axis) serve as fitting parameters. The optimal values are found by minimizing a chi-squared function using the MINUIT code, after which the resonance parameters are obtained by finding the roots of det f(in)(E)=0 on the non-physical sheet of the Riemann surface where all channel momenta have negative imaginary parts.

## Key numbers

- For the two-channel model potential, six resonances were identified with exact energies ranging from Er = 6.278042551 to Er = 7.548492959 and total widths from Gamma = 0.036866729 to Gamma = 27.69926473 (units where reduced masses mu1=mu2=1, hbar c=1, threshold energies E1=0 and E2=0.1)
- Artificial data points were generated with Gaussian noise having standard deviations Delta = 0.01, 0.05, and 0.10
- For low noise (Delta=0.01), M=5 terms in the Taylor series were used; for higher noise, M=3 terms were used
- The expansion center was taken as E0=8, with 30 artificial data points per elastic channel in the energy interval 6 < E < 11
- For the narrowest resonance (resonance 1, exact Er=6.278042552, Gamma=0.036866729), the fitted values at Delta=0.10 were Er=6.278669302 and Gamma=0.036236713
- For resonance 2 (exact Er=8.038507867, Gamma=2.563111275), the fitted values at Delta=0.10 were Er=7.968634195 and Gamma=1.662113407
- For resonance 3 (exact Er=8.861433400, Gamma=7.883809114), the fitted values at Delta=0.10 were Er=9.259323135 and Gamma=2.226793463

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier method for neutral particles (Vaandrager and Rakityansky, 2015) to the more general case of charged particles with Coulomb interactions, building on the rigorous semi-analytic expression for the N-channel Jost matrix derived in their previous work (Rakityansky and Elander, 2014). The approach generalizes the traditional effective-range expansion, which is limited to low energies and difficult to apply to multi-channel processes, by expanding the Jost matrix rather than the phase-shift and allowing expansion around any complex energy. The method enables extraction of resonance parameters (energy, total width, and partial widths) from data in one or two channels while predicting cross sections in other channels where measurements may be difficult or impossible.
