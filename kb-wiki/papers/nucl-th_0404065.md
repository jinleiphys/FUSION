# Dissipation and elliptic flow at RHIC

> We compare elliptic flow evolution from ideal hydrodynamics and covariant parton transport theory, and show that, for conditions expected at RHIC, dissipation significantly reduces elliptic flow even for extreme parton cross sections and/or densities sigma_gg * dN/dη(b=0) ~ 45 mb * 1000. The difference between transport and hydrodynamic elliptic flow is established rather early during the evolution of the system, but the buildup of elliptic flow is surprisingly insensitive to the choice of the initial (formation or thermalization) time in both models.

## Key claim

Dissipation significantly reduces elliptic flow even for extreme parton cross sections and/or densities (σ_gg × dN/dη(b=0) ∼ 45 mb × 1000), contradicting the common interpretation that such large opacities reach the ideal hydrodynamic limit; the difference between transport and hydrodynamic elliptic flow is established rather early during the evolution, but the buildup of elliptic flow is surprisingly insensitive to the choice of initial (formation or thermalization) time in both models.

## Method

The authors compared elliptic flow evolution from ideal (Euler) hydrodynamics and covariant parton transport theory (Boltzmann equation with elastic 2→2 interactions) for an ideal gas of massless gluons with 16 degrees of freedom, using identical initial conditions (longitudinally boost-invariant Bjorken tube in local kinetic equilibrium at temperature T₀, transverse gluon density proportional to binary collision distribution for Woods-Saxon nuclei at impact parameter b=8 fm). The transport solutions were obtained via the MPC algorithm with parton subdivision, using isotropic elastic parton cross sections of 3, 8, and 20 mb (equivalent via transport opacity scaling to Debye-screened leading-order pQCD gg→gg cross sections of approximately 7, 19, and 47 mb), while hydrodynamic solutions conserved particle number explicitly with equation of state ε = 3p and freezeout at gluon density n_g = 0.365 fm⁻³ (corresponding to T=120 MeV).

## Key numbers

- For the largest ∼45 mb parton cross section studied, dissipation reduces v₂ by 30–40% compared to ideal hydrodynamics from identical initial conditions (T₀=700 MeV, τ₀=0.1 fm/c)
- The dissipative reduction of elliptic flow is larger by a factor 1.5–2 than earlier studies found for the final transverse energy dE_T/dy
- By τ = 3 fm/c, most (∼80%) of the final anisotropy is already present in transport, while in hydrodynamics at low p⊥ (0.5 GeV) anisotropy still increases by a factor of 1.5 before freezeout at τ=5.3 fm/c
- Initial gluon density normalized to dN_g/dη(b=0)=1000 to match observed dN_ch/dη ∼ 600, with dN_g/dη ≈ 250 for b=8 fm
- For τ₀=0.6 fm/c and T₀=385 MeV (rescaled according to 1D Bjorken expansion T∼τ⁻¹/³), elliptic flow v₂(p⊥) is largely insensitive to the initialization time in both models

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Bin et al. and others that analyzed transverse energy and found ∼20% dissipative corrections, showing that differential elliptic flow is more sensitive to dissipation. It contradicts the popular interpretation that ∼45 mb elastic parton cross sections are large enough to reach the ideal hydrodynamic limit at p_T<2 GeV, noting that previous comparisons used different initial conditions and thermodynamic properties. The results indicate that the large elliptic flow seen at RHIC can be interpreted either as a harder nuclear equation of state with strongly dissipative evolution, or a softer equation of state with negligible dissipation, and call for future investigation with a more realistic nuclear equation of state including the hadronization phase transition.
