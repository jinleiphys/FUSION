# Rapidity Decorrelation from Hydrodynamic Fluctuations and Initial Fluctuations

> Rapidity decorrelation in high energy heavy-ion collisions is one of the hot topics in understanding longitudinal dynamics of the quark gluon plasma (QGP). In this study we employ an integrated dynamical model with full three dimensional relativistic hydrodynamics and perform event-by-event numerical simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC energy. We analyze factorization ratios to understand rapidity decorrelation from hydrodynamic fluctuations and initial longitudinal fluctuations. We show that factorization breaking happens due to both hydrodynamic fluctuations and initial longitudinal fluctuations. We conclude hydrodynamic fluctuations and initial longitudinal fluctuations are both important in understanding rapidity decorrelation.

## Key claim
The paper shows that both hydrodynamic fluctuations (thermal noise in the QGP evolution) and initial longitudinal fluctuations (event-by-event variation of the entropy density along the beam direction) contribute to rapidity decorrelation, and that including both brings theoretical results closer to experimental CMS data than either alone.

## Method
The authors used an integrated dynamical model combining a Monte-Carlo Glauber model extended longitudinally with PYTHIA for initial conditions, full three-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamics (with and without a fluctuating noise term in the shear-stress tensor), and a hadron cascade model (JAM) via the Cooper-Frye formula. They performed event-by-event simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV and computed the factorization ratio \(r_2(\eta^a_p,\eta^b_p)\) to quantify rapidity decorrelation.

## Key numbers
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio set to \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\).
- Relaxation time set to \(\tau_\pi = 3/4\pi T\).
- Switching temperature for particlization: \(T_{\mathrm{sw}} = 155\) MeV.
- Initial time \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm, Gaussian widths \(\sigma_\perp = 0.1\) fm and \(\sigma_\eta = 0.3\), overall factor \(K = 4.8\) to reproduce centrality dependence of charged particle multiplicity.
- 4000 hydrodynamic events generated, each followed by 100 hadronic cascade simulations.
- Results shown for Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV in centrality classes 0-5%, 20-30%, and 40-50%.

## Context
This work extends previous studies by the same group (Sakai, 2019) that found \(r_2 \sim 1\) when initial longitudinal fluctuations were omitted, demonstrating that such fluctuations are necessary to see any decorrelation. It also contrasts with purely viscous hydrodynamics without noise, showing that adding hydrodynamic fluctuations (via the fluctuation-dissipation relation) further reduces \(r_2\) and brings results closer to CMS experimental data (CMS, 2015). The model enables future systematic studies of longitudinal dynamics in heavy-ion collisions, including the interplay of initial-state and dynamical fluctuations.
