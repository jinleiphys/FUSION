# Propagation of vector-meson spectral-functions in a BUU type transport model: Application to di-electron production

> The time evolution of vector-meson spectral-functions is studied within a kinetic theory approach. We implement this formalism in a BUU type transport model. Applications focus on rho and omega mesons being important pieces for the interpretation of the di-electron invariant mass spectrum measured by the HADES collaboration for the reaction C + C at 2 AGeV bombarding energy. Since the evolution of the spectral functions is driven by the local density, the in-medium modifications are tiny for small collision systems within this approach.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for small collision systems like C + C at 2 AGeV, the in-medium modifications of vector-meson spectral functions are tiny because the evolution of the spectral functions is driven by the local density, which remains low in such systems.

## Method

The authors implement off-shell transport of rho and omega mesons within a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) type transport model (the BRoBUU code), solving the Kadanoff-Baym equations via a test-particle method where the energy of each test particle is treated as an independent variable. The self-energies of the vector mesons are parameterized schematically with a density-dependent mass shift and collision broadening, and the di-electron production is calculated from direct vector-meson decays, pion annihilation, bremsstrahlung, and Dalitz decays of mesons and baryon resonances.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 2 AGeV for the C + C reaction studied.
- Mass shift parameters used: Δm_ω = -50 MeV for the omega meson; Δm_ρ = -100 MeV for the rho meson (considered an upper limit).
- The rho meson production cross section from the global fit overestimates the measured value at 0.33 GeV excess energy by a factor of 2.3; the authors reduce rho production by this factor.
- The ratio σ_np→RN / σ_pp→RN is set to a = 1.34 for most resonances, except for N(1535) where a = 5.
- The momentum dependent soft potential parameters: A = -0.120 GeV, B = 0.151 GeV, τ = 1.23, C = -0.0645 GeV, Λ = 2.167 GeV.
- The spread of rho meson masses in the high density stage is about 300 MeV due to the imaginary part of the self-energy.

## Context

This work extends earlier off-shell transport approaches applied to gamma-A and p-A reactions (Eff_gammaA, Eff_off-shell) and to pA and AA collisions (Bratkovskaya, Brat_Cass) by implementing the formalism in a BUU model for heavy-ion collisions. The analysis supersedes an earlier attempt by the same group (our_first_attempt) and provides a framework for interpreting HADES data on di-electron invariant mass spectra from C + C collisions at 1 and 2 AGeV. The results enable future comparisons with data from the HADES collaboration (HADES_PRL) and highlight the need for larger collision systems to observe significant in-medium effects.
