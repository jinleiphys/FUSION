# Universal properties and structure of halo nuclei

> The universal properties and structure of halo nuclei composed of two neutrons (2n) and a core are investigated within an effective quantum mechanics framework. We construct an effective interaction potential that exploits the separation of scales in halo nuclei and treat the nucleus as an effective three-body system. The uncertainty from higher orders in the expansion is quantified through theoretical error bands. First, we investigate the possibility to observe excited Efimov states in 2n halo nuclei. Based on the experimental data, 20C is the only halo nucleus candidate to possibly have an Efimov excited state, with an energy less than 7 keV below the scattering threshold. Second, we study the structure of 20C and other 2n halo nuclei. In particular, we calculate their matter form factors, radii, and two-neutron opening angles.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether any two-neutron halo nuclei can host an excited Efimov state, finding that only 20C is a candidate, and only if the neutron-core bound state energy is less than 165 keV; the excited state would then lie less than 7 keV below the scattering threshold, a value so small that the leading-order theoretical error bands are comparable to the binding energy itself.

## Method

The authors treat the halo nucleus as an effective three-body system (core plus two neutrons) using zero-range contact interactions in a momentum-expansion framework, solving the coupled Faddeev integral equations for the spectator functions. They use a Gaussian regulator with cutoff Λ, tune the two-body couplings to reproduce the neutron-neutron and neutron-core scattering lengths (or two-body energies), and fix the cutoff by reproducing the experimental three-body ground-state energy. The leading-order uncertainty is estimated as r₀/a ≈ √(2μE)/m_π, where m_π = 140 MeV sets the natural high-momentum scale.

## Key numbers

- The only halo nucleus candidate for an excited Efimov state is 20C, with the excited state energy less than 7 keV below the scattering threshold.
- The neutron-core bound state energy in 20C is (162 ± 112) keV (Audi 1995); an excited Efimov state exists only when this energy is below 165 keV.
- The three-body ground state energy of 20C is 3506.0 keV (TUNL database).
- The neutron-neutron scattering length is a_nn = (-18.7 ± 0.6) fm (Gonzales 1999), corresponding to a virtual two-body energy of 116.04 keV.
- The discrete scaling factor λ₀ for three identical bosons (equal masses) is approximately 22.7; for a very large core mass (A ≫ 1) it approaches 15.7.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of Efimov states in halo nuclei by Amorim (1996) and Mazumdar (2000), finding qualitative agreement for 20C but quantitative differences: the present calculation gives a smaller excited-state energy and only one excited state, whereas Mazumdar found a second excited state for E_nc < 100 keV. The results also differ from Amorim (1996) for core masses other than A=1, though a more recent study by Yamashita (2007) shows better agreement with the present work. The framework enables predictions of matter form factors, radii, and two-neutron opening angles for 2n halo nuclei, and can be systematically improved by including effective-range corrections at next-to-leading order.
