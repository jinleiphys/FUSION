# Heavy Mesons in Nuclear Matter and Nuclei

> Heavy mesons in nuclear matter and nuclei are analyzed within different frameworks, paying a special attention to unitarized coupled-channel approaches. Possible experimental signatures of the properties of these mesons in matter are addressed, in particular in connection with the future FAIR facility at GSI.

## Key claim

The paper reviews theoretical approaches to strange and open-charm mesons in nuclear matter and nuclei, emphasizing that unitarized coupled-channel methods yield a much less attractive antikaon-nucleus potential (around -50 MeV at saturation density) than earlier relativistic mean-field calculations, and that the in-medium modifications of open-charm mesons remain uncertain with different models predicting widely different mass shifts.

## Method

The authors review unitarized coupled-channel approaches based on chiral effective Lagrangians for strange mesons and heavy-quark spin symmetry (HQSS) for open-charm mesons. For antikaons, the self-energy and spectral function are computed from the in-medium Kbar-nucleon interaction in s- and p-waves within a chiral unitary approach at finite temperature. For open-charm mesons, self-energies and spectral functions for D and D* mesons are obtained self-consistently from an s-wave interaction between pseudoscalar and vector meson multiplets with 1/2+ and 3/2+ baryons, incorporating Pauli blocking and open-charm meson self-energies in intermediate propagators.

## Key numbers

- Antikaon-nucleus potential: attraction of order -50 MeV at normal nuclear matter density rho_0, from unitarized chiral approaches (Ramos 1999, Tolos 2001, Tolos 2002)
- Kbar* spectral function width at normal nuclear matter density: five times bigger than in free space
- D-meson mass shift at rho_0: -50 MeV from QCD sum-rule approach (Arata 2008)
- D+-nucleus potential at rho_0: approximately -140 MeV from quark-meson coupling (QMC) model (Tsushima 1999)
- D-meson spatial diffusion coefficient D_x: studied around the phase transition from RHIC to FAIR energies (Tolos 2013)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of strange mesons in matter (Schaffner 1996, Friedman 2007) by showing that unitarized coupled-channel approaches produce significantly less attractive antikaon potentials than relativistic mean-field models. For open-charm mesons, it contrasts the QMC model predictions (Tsushima 1999) and QCD sum-rule results (Arata 2008, Kaempfer 2009) with newer HQSS-based coupled-channel calculations. The results enable predictions for experimental signatures at the future FAIR facility, including Kbar* transparency ratios in photonuclear reactions, D-mesic nuclei formation, and D-meson transport coefficients in heavy-ion collisions from RHIC to FAIR energies.
