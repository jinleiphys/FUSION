# Superfluid two-stream instability in a microscopic model

> The superflow in a superfluid is bounded from above by Landau's critical velocity. Within a microscopic bosonic model, I show that below this critical velocity there is a dynamical instability that manifests itself in an imaginary sound velocity and that is reminiscent of the two-stream instability in electromagnetic plasmas. I compute the onset of this instability and its full angular structure in a relativistic, uniform superfluid for all temperatures. At weak coupling, the instability only operates in a very small region in the phase diagram of temperature and superflow. Varying the coupling of the model suggests that the effect is more prominent at strong coupling and thus could be important for superfluids in compact stars and in the laboratory.

## Key claim

Below Landau's critical velocity, a dynamical instability exists in a relativistic superfluid that manifests as an imaginary sound velocity, analogous to the two-stream instability in electromagnetic plasmas; this instability operates only in a very small region of the temperature-superflow phase diagram at weak coupling but becomes more prominent at strong coupling.

## Method

The author uses a microscopic bosonic field theory with a complex scalar field and φ⁴ interaction, employing the self-consistent two-particle irreducible (2PI) formalism in the Hartree approximation. Sound modes are computed from linearized hydrodynamic equations for a relativistic, uniform superfluid with a background superflow, solving a quartic polynomial for the sound speed that depends on the angle between the superflow and sound wave direction. The calculation is performed in the normal-fluid rest frame for all temperatures, with thermodynamic quantities derived from the self-consistently determined pressure.

## Key numbers

- The critical velocity at zero temperature in the ultra-relativistic weak-coupling limit is v = 1/√3, where the speed of first sound becomes negative for θ = π and the speed of second sound becomes complex for θ = π/2
- At T = 0.4 Tc (with Tc the critical temperature at zero superflow), the instability sets in slightly below Landau's critical velocity vc(T), with the sound speed diverging at v/vc(T) ≃ 0.9984
- For T = 0.009 Tc, the instability occurs only at non-trivial angles rather than in the upstream direction, unlike at intermediate temperatures
- The coupling constant used for most results is λ = 0.05 with boson mass m = 0 (ultra-relativistic limit)

## Context

This work extends the general relativistic two-fluid discussion of the two-stream instability by Samuelsson (2009) by providing a microscopic field-theoretical calculation, and builds on the hydrodynamic framework developed by Alford (2013) for the same bosonic model. The instability is analogous to the Farley-Buneman instability in electromagnetic plasmas and was suggested to be relevant for superfluids in compact stars by Andersson (2004) in the context of pulsar glitches. The results enable future studies of the instability's time evolution and damping by relaxing the assumptions of uniformity and dissipationless flow, and may apply to superfluid helium, cold atomic gases, and neutron star interiors.
