# Timing evolution of accreting strange stars

> It has been suggested that the QPO phenomenon in LMXB's could be explained when the central compact object is a strange star. In this work we investigate within a standard model for disk accretion whether the observed clustering of spin frequencies in a narrow band is in accordance with this hypothesis. We show that frequency clustering occurs for accreting strange stars when typical values of the parameters of magnetic field initial strength and decay time, accretion rate are chosen. In contrast to hybrid star accretion no mass clustering effect is found.

## Key claim
The observed clustering of spin frequencies in low-mass X-ray binaries (LMXBs) can be reproduced by accreting strange stars, but only when the parameters of magnetic field initial strength, decay time, and accretion rate are chosen within a narrow range; in contrast to hybrid star accretion, no mass clustering effect is found.

## Method
The authors model the spin evolution of an accreting strange star as a sequence of stationary states in the angular velocity–baryon number plane, governed by an external torque from accreting plasma and magnetic braking. They solve a first-order differential equation for the angular velocity using an exponential decay model for the magnetic field, with decay times between 10^7 and 10^9 yr and initial fields from 0.2 to 4.0 TG. Three equations of state for strange quark matter are used: two density-dependent confinement models (SS1 and SS2) and the MIT bag model (B60_150).

## Key numbers
- Frequency clustering occurs in the interval 220 Hz < ν < 380 Hz, corresponding to recent observations.
- For the SS1 equation of state, clustering requires B(0) = 2.5 TG, τ_B = 10^8 yr, and accretion rate Ṁ = 10^{-9} M_⊙/yr.
- For the B60_150 bag model, clustering requires B(0) = 3 TG, τ_B = 10^8 yr, and accretion rate Ṁ = 10^{-8} M_⊙/yr.
- The ground state energy per baryon for SS1 is 888 MeV, for SS2 is 926 MeV, and for B60_150 is 836 MeV.
- The ground state mass density for SS1 is 12.3 × 10^{14} g/cm^3, for SS2 is 14.1 × 10^{14} g/cm^3, and for B60_150 is 4.6 × 10^{14} g/cm^3.
- The ground state baryon number density for SS1 is 0.779 fm^{-3}, for SS2 is 0.858 fm^{-3}, and for B60_150 is 0.295 fm^{-3}.

## Context
This work extends the hypothesis that the quasi-periodic oscillation (QPO) phenomenon in LMXBs could be explained by strange stars, as previously suggested by Dey et al. (1999) and Li et al. (1999). It contrasts with hybrid star accretion models by Poghosyan et al. (2001), which show mass clustering due to deconfinement phase transitions; the absence of such clustering in strange stars provides a potential observational discriminant. The results enable future studies to constrain the equation of state of superdense matter by comparing observed spin frequencies and masses of LMXBs with the predicted waiting-time distributions.
