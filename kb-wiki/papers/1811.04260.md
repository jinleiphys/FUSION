# Investigating the hidden-charm and hidden-bottom pentaquark resonances in scattering process

> In the framework of quark delocalization color screening model, both the hidden-charm and hidden-bottom pentaquark resonances are studied in the hadron-hadron scattering process. A few narrow pentaquark resonances with hidden-charm above $4.2$ GeV, and some narrow pentaquark resonances with hidden-bottom above $11$ GeV are found from corresponding scattering processes. Besides, the states $NŒ∑_{c}$, $NJ/œà$, $NŒ∑_{b}$ and $NŒ•$ with $IJ^{P}=\frac{1}{2}\frac{1}{2}^{-}$, as well as $NJ/œà$ and $NŒ•$ with $IJ^{P}=\frac{1}{2}\frac{3}{2}^{-}$ are all possible to be bound by channel-coupling calculation. All these heavy pentaquarks are worth searching in the future experiments.

# Investigating the hidden-charm and hidden-bottom pentaquark resonances in scattering process

## Key claim
Using the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM) with the resonating group method (RGM), narrow pentaquark resonances with hidden-charm above 4.2 GeV and hidden-bottom above 11 GeV are found from hadron-hadron scattering phase shifts, but the calculated decay widths are lower limits because only hidden-charm (or hidden-bottom) channels are considered and D-wave decays through tensor interaction are neglected.

## Method
The authors employed the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM) with the same parameters as their previous bound-state calculation (Huang and Ping, 2018). They calculated S-wave baryon-meson scattering phase shifts using the resonating group method (RGM), performing both two-channel coupling (one bound state with one open channel) and four-channel coupling (three bound states with one open channel) for systems with quantum numbers IJ^P = 1/2 1/2^- and IJ^P = 1/2 3/2^-.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous bound-state calculation of hidden-charm molecular pentaquarks (Huang and Ping, 2018) by studying the same systems in hadron-hadron scattering to identify resonance states with finite widths. The results are qualitatively similar to predictions from a coupled-channel unitary approach by Wu et al. (2012) for hidden-charm N* states and by Zou et al. (2015) for hidden-bottom N* resonances around 11 GeV. The study enables experimental searches for these heavy pentaquarks at facilities such as Jefferson Lab (photo-production of J/œà at threshold), PANDA/FAIR, and the proposed Electron-Ion Collider (EIC).
