# The influence of intruder states in even-even Po isotopes

> We study the role of intruder states and shape coexistence in the even-even $^{190-206}$Po isotopes, through an interacting boson model with configuration mixing calculation. We analyzed the results in the light of known systematics on various observable in the Pb region, paying special attention to the unperturbed energy systematics and quadrupole deformation. We find that shape coexistence in the Po isotopes behaves in very much the same way as in the Pt isotopes, i.e., it is somehow hidden, contrary to the situation in the Pb and the Hg isotopes.

# The influence of intruder states in even-even Po isotopes

## Key claim

Shape coexistence in the Po isotopes is "hidden" or "concealed" due to strong mixing between regular and intruder configurations, similar to the Pt isotopes but contrary to the situation in Pb and Hg isotopes. The intruder configuration becomes dominant in the ground-state wavefunction around midshell (N=104), but the strong mixing makes it difficult to distinguish the two configurations from experimental data alone.

## Method

The authors performed Interacting Boson Model with Configuration Mixing (IBM-CM) calculations for the even-even 190-206Po isotopes, using a model space that mixes [N] and [N+2] boson configurations corresponding to regular and 2p-2h intruder excitations across the Z=82 shell gap. They fitted the Hamiltonian parameters to experimental excitation energies and B(E2) transition rates, then compared the unperturbed (mixing turned off) energy systematics and extracted quadrupole deformation parameters β for both regular and intruder configurations across the Po, Hg, and Pt isotopic series.

## Key numbers

- The energy needed to excite two particles across the Z=82 shell gap (Δ^(N+2)) is 2400 keV for Po isotopes, compared to 3480 keV for Hg and 2800 keV for Pt.
- The 2_1^+ state energy in Po remains roughly constant at 600-650 keV in the mass range A=196-208, with a sudden drop to approximately 300 keV at A=196.
- The study covers the even-even 190-206Po isotopes, with the midshell point at N=104.
- Quadrupole deformation β was extracted using the formula β = 4π√(B(E2: I_i→I_f)) / (3 ⟨I_i K 2 0| I_f 0⟩ Z e R_0^2) with R_0 = 1.2 A^(1/3) fm.

## Context

This work extends previous IBM-CM studies of Pt isotopes (Garcia-Ramos, 2009) and Hg isotopes (Garcia-Ramos, 2014) to the Po isotopic chain, using the same theoretical framework. The results show that Po behaves similarly to Pt, where configuration mixing is concealed, in contrast to Hg and Pb where intruder states are self-evident. The findings enable a systematic understanding of shape coexistence across the entire Pb region and help explain why the characteristic parabolic energy systematics of intruder states are not directly observable in experimental data for Po and Pt isotopes.
