# Nucleon-nucleon correlations in the extreme oxygen isotopes

> There has been an upsurge of interest in two-nucleon decays thanks to the studies of nucleon-nucleon correlations. In our previous work, based on a novel time-dependent three-body approach, we demonstrated that the energy and angular correlations of the emitted nucleons can shed light on the structure of nucleonic pairs formed inside the nucleus. In this work, we apply the new framework to study the decay dynamics and properties of some extreme proton-rich and neutron-rich oxygen isotopes, including two-proton ($2p$) decays of $^{11,12}$O and two-neutron ($2n$) decay of $^{26}$O. Here we show that the low-$\ell$ components of $^{11,12}$O wave functions, which are affected by continuum and configuration-interaction effects, strongly impact decay dynamics and asymptotic correlations. In the calculated wave functions of $^{11,12}$O, diproton and cigarlike structures merge together during the tunneling process and the resulting energy- and angular correlations are very consistent with the experimental data. The asymptotic correlations of the $2n$ decay of $^{26}$O dramatically change as the two-neutron decay energy approaches the zero-energy threshold. The small reported value of $Q_{2n}$ suggests that the $2n$ decay of this nucleus can be understood in terms of the universal phase-space limit.

# Nucleon-nucleon correlations in the extreme oxygen isotopes

## Key claim

The low-ℓ components of the wave functions of ¹¹O and ¹²O, which are affected by continuum and configuration-interaction effects, strongly impact decay dynamics and asymptotic correlations; for ²⁶O, the asymptotic correlations of the two-neutron decay dramatically change as the two-neutron decay energy approaches the zero-energy threshold, and the small reported Q₂ₙ value suggests the decay can be understood in terms of the universal phase-space limit.

## Method

The authors employ a time-dependent three-body approach in which the parent nucleus is described as a core plus two valence nucleons, using Jacobi coordinates and hyperspherical harmonics. The initial wave function is obtained via the Gamow coupled-channel (GCC) method using a Berggren basis in the complex-momentum plane, then decomposed into real-momentum scattering states and propagated in time using a Chebyshev polynomial expansion of the time evolution operator. The pairwise interactions include a Woods-Saxon potential for core-nucleon interactions and the finite-range Minnesota force for the valence nucleon-nucleon interaction, augmented by a two-body Coulomb force for protons.

## Key numbers

- The S = 1 component of the ground-state wave function of ¹²O has a squared amplitude of 27%.
- The weight of the Jacobi-T configuration (K,ℓ_x,ℓ_y,S) = (0,0,0,0) in ¹²O is 34% in the initial state and 76% in the final state.
- The experimental Q₂ₙ value for ²⁶O is 18 ± 5 keV (Kondo 2016).
- For ²⁶O, deviations from the phase-space limit become noticeable at Q₂ₙ = 100 keV.
- The model space uses max(ℓ_x, ℓ_y) ≤ 7 and maximal hyperspherical quantum number K_max = 20.
- The hyperradial part uses Berggren basis for K < 10 channels and harmonic oscillator basis with oscillator length 1.75 fm and N_max = 40 for higher-angular-momentum channels.
- The time evolution is limited to interactions inside a sphere of radius 500 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous time-dependent three-body study of ⁶Be (Wang 2021) to heavier dripline oxygen isotopes, and builds on their earlier spectroscopic and structural studies of ¹¹,¹²,²⁶O (Wang 2017, Wang 2019). The calculated correlations for ¹²O are compared with experimental data from Webb (2019), and the results for ²⁶O are discussed in relation to the universal s-wave phase-space limit described by Grigorenko (2018) and the threshold clustering effects discussed by Okolowicz (2020). The work enables a comprehensive description of extreme oxygen isotopes by revealing their open-quantum-system nature and connecting inner nuclear structure to asymptotic decay correlations.
