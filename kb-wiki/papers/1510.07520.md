# Heavy-flavor dynamics in relativistic p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{S_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV

> We investigate the heavy flavor dynamics in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) medium created in p-Pb collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC). In the (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics model describing QGP medium, the dynamics of heavy quarks are studied in an improved Langevin framework incorporating both collisional and radiative energy loss. The hadronization of the heavy quarks is given by a hybrid model of fragmentation and recombination. We find that the in-medium evolution of charm quarks raises the D-meson $R_{pPb}$ at low transverse momenta while it suppresses the D-meson $R_{pPb}$ at intermediate momenta. In addition, the elliptic flow of D-meson is calculated. For a diffusion coefficient which reproduces central $R_{AA}$ data at the LHC, we find a much smaller D-meson $v_2$ compared to the light hadrons. This observation indicates an incomplete coupling between heavy quarks with the medium, due to the reduced medium size compared to AA collisions.

## Key claim

The in-medium evolution of charm quarks in p-Pb collisions raises the D-meson nuclear modification factor $R_{pPb}$ at low transverse momenta while suppressing it at intermediate momenta; however, the accuracy of current data does not allow unambiguous distinction between scenarios of only cold nuclear matter effects versus combined cold and hot nuclear matter effects when p-Pb results are viewed independently of Pb-Pb results.

## Method

The authors model the quark-gluon plasma medium using (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics (vHLLE) with shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $\eta/s=0.08$, and simulate heavy quark propagation through an improved Langevin framework that includes both collisional energy loss and radiative energy loss via a recoil force term. The initial heavy quark momentum distribution is calculated from leading-order pQCD with CTEQ and EPS09 parametrizations for parton distribution functions and nuclear shadowing, while hadronization of heavy quarks into D-mesons uses a hybrid model of fragmentation and recombination.

## Key numbers

- Spatial diffusion coefficient used: $D(2\pi T) = 5.0$, tuned to reproduce D-meson $R_{AA}$ in 0-7.5% central Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: $\eta/s = 0.08$
- Center-of-mass energy: $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV for p-Pb collisions
- $p_T$ and $\eta$ cuts for elliptic flow calculations: 0.3 GeV $< p_T <$ 3.0 GeV, $|\eta| < 2.4$
- For comparison, a diffusion coefficient $D(2\pi T) = 0.5$ gives the best description of D-meson $v_2$ in Pb-Pb collisions at 30-50% centrality, but is strongly disfavored by lattice QCD

## Context

This work extends heavy flavor studies from Pb-Pb collisions to p-Pb collisions at the LHC, using the same theoretical framework previously applied to heavy-ion collisions by Cao et al. (2013). The reduced medium size in p-Pb collisions allows the authors to probe the transition between cold nuclear matter effects (nuclear shadowing via EPS09 parametrization) and hot nuclear matter effects (in-medium energy loss). The results enable future experimental measurements of centrality-dependent D-meson $R_{pPb}$ to disentangle these two contributions, while the small D-meson elliptic flow for the diffusion coefficient that reproduces central $R_{AA}$ data indicates incomplete thermalization of heavy quarks in the smaller p-Pb system.
