# Pion Gravitational Form Factors in Holographic QCD

> We present the first calculation of the momentum-transfer dependence of the pion gravitational form factors (GFFs) within a top-down holographic QCD framework. These form factors encode essential information about the internal stress distribution of hadrons and may serve as a tool to explore the non-perturbatice dyanmics responsible for quark and gluon confinement in QCD. In particular, we evaluate the forward-limit value of the GFFs, known as the D-term, within the framework of the Sakai-Sugimoto model. Our analysis also reveals glueball dominance, wherein the pion's gravitational interaction is mediated by an infinite tower of glueball excitations.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first calculation of the momentum-transfer dependence of the pion gravitational form factors (GFFs) using a top-down holographic QCD framework (the Sakai-Sugimoto model), revealing that the pion's gravitational interaction is mediated by an infinite tower of glueball excitations. The forward-limit D-term is found to be \(D(0) = -1\) in the chiral limit, consistent with the soft-pion theorem, but this result reflects loop-level contributions in the large-\(N_c\) limit and may be modified by quark mass effects.

## Method
The authors solve the linearized Einstein equations in the 11-dimensional supergravity background of the Sakai-Sugimoto model, treating the pion field as the sole dynamical source of gravitational backreaction via the D8-brane action. The boundary energy-momentum tensor (EMT) matrix elements are extracted using holographic renormalization and the Gubser-Klebanov-Polyakov-Witten (GKP-Witten) prescription, with the metric perturbations decomposed into transverse-traceless (T\(_4\)) and scalar (S\(_4\)) glueball modes. The infinite sums over glueball excitations in the Green's functions are evaluated by expanding as a power series in \(-\vec{\Delta}^2\) and solving recurrence relations derived from the glueball eigenvalue equations.

## Key numbers
- The D-term in the forward limit is \(D(0) = -1\), obtained in the chiral limit within the large-\(N_c\) framework.
- The model parameters are \(\kappa = 0.00745\) and \(M_{KK} = 949\,\mathrm{MeV}\), fixed from the \(\rho\) meson mass \(776\,\mathrm{MeV}\) and the pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92.4\,\mathrm{MeV}\).
- The form factor \(A(t)\) shows good convergence for momentum transfers \(t \lesssim 0.8\,\mathrm{GeV}^2\), while \(D(t)\) begins to deviate from convergence at \(t \gtrsim 0.5\,\mathrm{GeV}^2\).
- The expansion for the Green's functions is truncated at \(k=10\) (with results also shown for \(k=8,9\)).

## Context
This work extends the top-down holographic approach previously applied to nucleon GFFs by Fujita et al. (2022) to the spin-0 pion case, and provides the first top-down calculation of the momentum-transfer dependence of pion GFFs. The results qualitatively agree with recent lattice QCD findings by Hackett et al. (2023), particularly in reproducing the distinct \(t\)-dependence of \(A(t)\) and \(D(t)\), a feature absent in bottom-up holographic models such as Abidin and Carlson (2008). The glueball dominance mechanism identified here enables future studies of GFFs for other hadrons (nucleons, vector mesons) and investigations beyond the chiral limit, including quark mass effects and connections to the trace anomaly.
