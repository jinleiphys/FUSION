# Polyakov loop correlator in perturbation theory

> We study the Polyakov loop correlator in the weak coupling expansion and show how the perturbative series re-exponentiates into singlet and adjoint contributions. We calculate the order $g^7$ correction to the Polyakov loop correlator in the short distance limit. We show how the singlet and adjoint free energies arising from the re-exponentiation formula of the Polyakov loop correlator are related to the gauge invariant singlet and octet free energies that can be defined in pNRQCD, namely we find that the two definitions agree at leading order in the multipole expansion, but differ at first order in the quark-antiquark distance.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the perturbative series for the Polyakov loop correlator re-exponentiates into singlet and adjoint contributions, and that the singlet and adjoint free energies defined via this re-exponentiation agree with the gauge-invariant singlet and octet free energies defined in pNRQCD only at leading order in the multipole expansion, differing at first order in the quark-antiquark distance.

## Method

The authors use the replica trick for Wilson lines to derive an exponentiated expression for the Polyakov loop correlator, splitting it into singlet and adjoint free energies via Fierz projection. They calculate the order \(g^7\) correction to the normalized Polyakov loop correlator in the short-distance limit using Coulomb gauge perturbation theory, and compare the resulting singlet and adjoint free energies with those defined in potential nonrelativistic QCD (pNRQCD) at finite temperature.

## Key numbers

* The order \(g^7\) correction to the Polyakov loop correlator is calculated in the short-distance limit, extending previous NLO and NNLO calculations (Brambilla 2010).
* The singlet and adjoint free energies mix under renormalization with constants \(Z_S = (N^2-1)Z_A = \frac{N^2-1}{N^2}(1-z_1^2)\), where \(z_1\) is a renormalization parameter and \(Z_S, Z_A = \mathcal{O}(\alpha_\mathrm{s})\).
* The renormalized singlet free energy satisfies \(\exp[-F_S^{(R)}/T] - \exp[-F_A^{(R)}/T] = z_1^2 (\exp[-F_S/T] - \exp[-F_A/T])\), showing multiplicative renormalizability.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative calculation of the Polyakov loop correlator beyond the NNLO results of Brambilla (2010) to NNNLO (order \(g^7\)), and connects the perturbative singlet/adjoint free energies to the gauge-invariant definitions in pNRQCD. The results enable improved comparisons with lattice QCD calculations of the static quark-antiquark free energy (Kaczmarek 2002, Borsanyi 2015) and provide a foundation for modeling in-medium quarkonium properties, as reviewed by Mocsy (2013). The approach also generalizes to diquark and baryonic Polyakov loop correlators, where mixing between octet channels occurs.
