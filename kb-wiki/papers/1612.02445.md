# Gluon orbital angular momentum at small-$x$

> We present a general analysis of the orbital angular momentum (OAM) distribution of gluons $L_g(x)$ inside the nucleon with particular emphasis on the small-$x$ region. We derive a novel operator representation of $L_g(x)$ in terms of Wilson lines and argue that it is approximately proportional to the gluon helicity distribution $L_g(x) \approx -2ΔG(x)$ at small-$x$. We also compute longitudinal single spin asymmetry in exclusive diffractive dijet production in lepton-nucleon scattering in the next-to-eikonal approximation and show that the asymmetry is a direct probe of the gluon helicity/OAM distribution as well as the QCD odderon exchange.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the gluon orbital angular momentum distribution \(L_g(x)\) is approximately proportional to the gluon helicity distribution at small \(x\) via the relation \(L_g(x) \approx -2\Delta G(x)\), with the hedge that this holds only if the function \(g(x,|q_\perp|)\) is negligible compared to \(f(x,|q_\perp|)\), which they argue is plausible but not rigorously proven.

## Method

The authors derive a gauge-invariant operator representation of the gluon OAM distribution \(L_g(x)\) in terms of lightlike Wilson lines and covariant derivatives, valid at small \(x\). They then compute the longitudinal single spin asymmetry in exclusive diffractive dijet production in lepton-nucleon scattering using the next-to-eikonal approximation, showing that the asymmetry vanishes at leading eikonal order and first appears at next-to-eikonal order, where it involves the same OAM operator. The asymmetry is also shown to be proportional to the QCD odderon amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The most recent NLO global QCD analysis finds \(\int_{0.05}^1 dx \Delta G(x) \approx 0.2^{+0.06}_{-0.07}\) for the gluon helicity in the moderate \(x\) region.
- The relation \(L_g(x) \approx -2\Delta G(x)\) is proposed for \(x \ll 1\), linking the two distributions at small \(x\).
- The DGLAP splitting functions for OAM and helicity evolution are given explicitly, including \(\hat{P}_{qq}(z)=C_F \left(\frac{z(1+z^2)}{(1-z)_+} + \frac{3}{2}\delta(1-z)\right)\) with \(C_F=4/3\), and \(\Delta P_{gg}(z)=6 \left(\frac{1}{(1-z)_+}-2z+1\right) + \frac{\beta_0}{2}\delta(z-1)\) with \(\beta_0=11-\frac{2n_f}{3}\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for gluon OAM distributions developed by Hatta (2011, 2012) and Ji (2012), and provides a concrete experimental observable for measuring \(L_g(x)\) at small \(x\) via diffractive dijet production. It complements a related proposal by Ji (2016) focusing on the moderate-\(x\) region, and together these represent a major step toward completing the Jaffe-Manohar spin sum rule (1989). The results also connect to the QCD odderon exchange and enable future measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider to constrain the small-\(x\) behavior of both gluon helicity and OAM.
