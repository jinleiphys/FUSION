# A note on the Lipkin model in arbitrary fermion number

> A possible form of the Lipkin model obeying the su(6)-algebra is presented. It is a natural generalization from the idea for the su(4)-algebra recently proposed by the present authors. All the relation appearing in the present form can be expressed in terms of the spherical tensors in the su(2)-algebras. For specifying the linearly independent basis completely, twenty parameters are introduced. It is concluded that, in these parameters, the ten denote the quantum numbers coming from the eigenvalues of some hermitian operators. The five in these ten determine the minimum weight state.

## Key claim
The paper presents a generalization of the Lipkin model to an su(6)-algebra, extending the authors' prior work on the su(4)-algebra. However, the construction yields a linearly independent basis that is not orthogonal; the authors state that an orthogonal set must be constructed by an appropriate method such as the Schmidt method, and the parameters introduced include both quantum numbers and free parameters that must be distinguished.

## Method
The authors construct the su(6)-Lipkin model by expressing all generators in terms of spherical tensors of su(2)-subalgebras. They introduce three su(2)-subalgebras and one su(3)-subalgebra, with three vector operators for the total su(2)-algebra. The linearly independent basis is built by operating excited-state generating operators on a minimum weight state, using tensor operators constructed from the vector operators and lowering operators, with angular momentum coupling rules applied to combine the quantum numbers.

## Key numbers
- The su(6) case (n=6, m=3) requires twenty parameters to specify the linearly independent basis completely.
- Ten of the twenty parameters denote quantum numbers from eigenvalues of hermitian operators; five of these ten determine the minimum weight state.
- The minimum weight state is expressed as |ρ, ρ¹, σ¹, σ², σ³⟩, where the eigenvalues of -R̃, -R̃₀, -S̃₀(1), -S̃₀(2), and -S̃₀(3) are denoted as -ρ, -ρ¹, -σ¹, -σ², and -σ³, respectively.
- The eigenvalue R of the operator R̃ is given by R = 3(μ - λ) + (3/2)(l¹ + l² + 2l³) - ρ.

## Context
This work is a continuation of two prior papers by the same authors (Tsue, Providencia, da Providencia, and Yamamura, 2015-2016), referred to as (I) and (II), which treated the Lipkin model with arbitrary single-particle levels and fermion number. The paper extends the su(4)-algebra construction from (II) to the su(6)-algebra case. The Lipkin model is a classical model in nuclear many-body theories, and the authors note that there remain unsolved problems in its algebraic structure. This work enables the construction of orthogonal bases for the su(6)-Lipkin model with arbitrary fermion numbers, which can be used for further studies of nuclear structure and many-body dynamics.
