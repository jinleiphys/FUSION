# The comparison of the state-of-the-art nucleon-nucleon potentials from phase shift to nuclear matter

> The nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) potential is the residual interaction of the strong interaction in the low-energy region and is also the fundamental input to the study of atomic nuclei. Based on the non-perturbative properties of the quantum chromodynamics (QCD), $NN$ potential is not yet directly accessible from QCD theory. Therefore, various models of $NN$ interactions have been constructed based on Yukawa's meson exchange pictures since the 1930s, including one-boson-exchange models, coordinate operator models and chiral effective field models. Analysis of extensive $NN$ scattering data has shown that the two-body nuclear force exhibits a short-range repulsion and intermediate-range attraction, and decays rapidly with increasing distance. A series of charge-dependent high-precision $NN$ interactions have been further developed in the past thirty years, such as the AV18 potential, CD-Bonn potential, pvCD-Bonn potentials, and the chiral effective nuclear potentials with momentum expansion up to the fifth order. In this work, the phase shifts at different channels, the cross sections, the entanglement entropy in spin space, and the equations of state of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter from these high-precision $NN$ interactions are calculated and systematically compared. It can be found that they have significant differences in the cases with high angular momentum, high laboratory energy, and high-density regions.

## Key claim

While the abstract states that the paper systematically compares phase shifts, cross sections, entanglement entropy, and equations of state from high-precision nucleon-nucleon potentials, the actual work reveals that significant differences between potentials emerge specifically in channels with high angular momentum (F-waves), at laboratory energies above 200 MeV, and at high nuclear densities, with chiral forces showing particular sensitivity to cutoff values and power-counting schemes.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation using the matrix inversion method of Haftel and Tabakin (1970) for 17 high-precision nucleon-nucleon potentials, including AV18, CD-Bonn, pvCD-Bonn (three variants), and three families of chiral N4LO potentials from the EMN, EKM, and RKE groups. They computed phase shifts for partial waves with total angular momentum j ≤ 3 and laboratory energies up to 300 MeV for np, nn, and pp scattering, then used these to calculate differential cross sections, entanglement entropy in spin space, and equations of state for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter.

## Key numbers

- Phase shifts for the np 1S0 channel at 1 MeV: 62.07 ± 0.07 degrees (average over 17 potentials); at 300 MeV: -4.62 ± 0.84 degrees
- Phase shifts for the np 3S1 channel at 5 MeV: 118.18 ± 0.09 degrees; at 300 MeV: 6.66 ± 1.30 degrees
- Phase shifts for the nn 1S0 channel at 1 MeV: 57.40 ± 0.09 degrees; at 300 MeV: -6.57 ± 0.73 degrees
- Phase shifts for the pp 1S0 channel at 5 MeV: 54.91 degrees (with deviation reported in text)
- The N4LO 450(EMN) potential showed significant deviation in the 1S0 channel at energies above 200 MeV, attributed to its smaller cutoff value Λ
- For F-wave channels (3F2, 3F3), only at energies E ≤ 100 MeV were phase shifts from all 17 potentials relatively close; at higher energies, chiral force predictions deviated substantially from PWA93 partial wave analysis

## Context

This work extends the systematic comparison of high-precision nucleon-nucleon potentials initiated by the Nijmegen group (Stoks et al., 1994) and includes the AV18 potential (Wiringa et al., 1995), CD-Bonn potential (Machleidt, 2001), and chiral effective field theory potentials up to fifth order from the EMN group (Entem et al., 2017), EKM group (Epelbaum et al., 2015), and RKE group (Reinert et al., 2018). The study enables future work on ab initio calculations of finite nuclei and infinite nuclear matter using methods such as the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach, and provides guidance on which potentials are reliable for specific angular momentum channels and energy regimes.
