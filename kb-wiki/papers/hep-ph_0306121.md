# Impact parameter dependent quark distribution of the pion

> We present first results for the impact parameter dependent quark distribution in the pion obtained in transverse lattice gauge theory, and discuss recent predictions from other models.

# Impact parameter dependent quark distribution of the pion

## Key claim

The paper presents first results for the impact parameter dependent valence quark distribution in the pion from transverse lattice gauge theory, finding that the x-b dependence does not factorize: at large lightcone momentum fraction x, the quark distribution is dominated by small impact parameters b < 2/3 fm, while at small x the opposite is true.

## Method

The authors applied Discrete Light Cone Quantization (DLCQ) to a transverse lattice formulation of gauge theory at leading order in the 1/N_c expansion, using a coarse transverse lattice of spacing a ~ 2/3 fm. The pion wavefunctions were decomposed into Fock states of a quark-antiquark pair joined by a string of flux-carrying link fields, with couplings fixed by fitting to masses M_π, M_ρ, the decay constant f_π, and string tension √σ. The impact parameter dependent distribution was then obtained by Fourier transforming the hadron states from transverse momentum space to impact parameter space.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing a ~ 2/3 fm, held fixed throughout the calculation
- Calculated charge radius r_π = √(6 dF(-t)/dt)|_{t=0} ≈ 0.59 fm, slightly less than the experimental value 0.663 fm
- Resolution scale of the transverse lattice: 0.5 GeV, set roughly by the lattice spacing a
- Maximum quark-antiquark separation with n=5 truncation: 3a ~ 2 fm
- Fitted distribution at b=0: 0.18(1-4.3√x+11x)x^{-0.16}(1-x)^{0.37}
- Fitted distribution at b=a: 0.11(1-1.9√x+5.5x)x^{-0.78}(1-x)^{2.0}
- Fitted distribution at b=2a: 0.04(1-4.2√x+6.2x)x^{-0.76}(1-x)^{1.94}
- Fitted distribution at b=3a: 0.005(1-4.0√x+8.2x)x^{-0.66}(1-x)^{1.2}
- Integrated valence quark distribution V(x) = (1-x)^{0.33}/x^{0.7} {0.33 -1.1√x + 2x}

## Context

This work extends transverse lattice calculations of pion structure (van de Sande and Dalley, 2002) to impact parameter dependent distributions, which interpolate between traditional structure functions and form factors. The results are compared with predictions from double distribution constructions (Mukherjee et al., 2002), chiral NJL models with Pauli-Villars regulator (Broniowski and Ruiz Arriola, 2003), and a simple two-body formula with sharp fall-off in transverse space. The paper enables experimental discrimination between models through measurement of the small-b dependence, which could clarify discrepancies in the integrated valence distribution V(x) seen in pion-nucleon Drell-Yan scattering data (Conway et al., 1989) between models such as Dyson-Schwinger equations and the Instanton Liquid approach.
