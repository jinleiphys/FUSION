# Mini-Proceedings of the "Fourth International Workshop on the Extension Project for the J-PARC Hadron Experimental Facility (HEF-ex 2024)"

> The mini proceedings of the "Fourth International Workshop on the Extension Project for the J-PARC Hadron Experimental Facility (HEF-ex 2024) [https://kds.kek.jp/event/46965]" held at J-PARC, February 19-21, 2024, are presented. The workshop was devoted to discussing the physics case that connects both the present and the future Hadron Experimental Facility at J-PARC, covering a wide range of topics in flavor, hadron, and nuclear physics related to both experimental and theoretical activities being conducted at the facility.

## Key claim

This mini-proceedings volume summarizes the workshop discussions on the physics case connecting the present and future Hadron Experimental Facility at J-PARC, covering flavor, hadron, and nuclear physics; the contributions are workshop summaries and proposals rather than presenting a single unified experimental result, with the overall claim being that combined experimental programs at J-PARC HEF and JLab CLAS12 are complementary for understanding non-perturbative QCD and strangeness nuclear physics.

## Method

The workshop gathered experimental and theoretical nuclear and hadron physicists to discuss ongoing and proposed measurements at J-PARC HEF and JLab CLAS12, covering strangeness nuclear physics, hadron spectroscopy, hyperon-nucleon interactions, kaonic atoms, antiproton-induced reactions, lattice QCD calculations of hadron interactions, and flavor probes of cosmology. Individual contributions describe specific experimental setups (CLAS12 spectrometer at JLab, SIDDHARTA-2 at DAΦNE, PANDA at FAIR, J-PARC beamlines K1.8, K1.1, K10, and HIHR) and theoretical approaches (HAL QCD lattice method, combined lattice-femtoscopic analysis, baryogenesis models).

## Key numbers

- CLAS12 streaming readout data rate estimated at 50 GB/s with expected data reduction factor of 10 or higher
- PANDA at FAIR will use antiproton beams with momentum range 1.5 GeV/c to 15 GeV/c, peak luminosity 2×10^32 cm^-2 s^-1, and beam resolution δp/p = 2×10^-4 (high-luminosity mode) or δp/p = 4×10^-5 (high-resolution mode at 2×10^31 cm^-2 s^-1)
- PANDA interaction rate of 2×10^7 s^-1 and momentum resolution Δp/p ≈ 1%
- N-φ interaction in J=3/2 channel from lattice QCD at m_π = 146.4 MeV: scattering length a_0^(3/2) = -1.43(23)_stat.(+36/-06)_syst. fm, effective range r_eff^(3/2) = 2.36(10)_stat.(+02/-48)_syst. fm
- N-φ interaction in J=1/2 channel from combined lattice+femtoscopic analysis: Re a_0^(1/2) = 1.54(+0.53/-0.53)_stat.(+0.09/-0.16)_syst. fm, Im a_0^(1/2) = 0.00(+0.00/-0.35)_stat.(+0.00/-0.16)_syst. fm; binding energy estimated in range 12.8-56.1 MeV
- SIDDHARTA-2 kaonic deuterium data taking: integrated luminosity of about 500 pb^-1 collected in Run1 and Run2 (2023), aiming for total 800 pb^-1
- Baryon-to-photon ratio from CMB and BBN: η = (6.12 ± 0.04) × 10^-10

## Context

This workshop proceedings extends the physics case presented in the J-PARC HEF Extension Third White Paper (Aoki et al., 2021) and the review by Ohnishi, Sakuma, and Takahashi (2020), connecting J-PARC hadron physics with complementary programs at JLab CLAS12 (Arrington et al., 2022), DAΦNE SIDDHARTA-2 (Curceanu et al., 2019), and FAIR PANDA (Barucca et al., 2021). The results enable future combined analyses of lattice QCD and experimental femtoscopic data for hadron interactions (as demonstrated by Chizzali et al., 2024 for N-φ), precision measurements of kaonic atoms across the periodic table via the proposed EXKALIBUR program (Curceanu et al., 2023), and tests of baryogenesis mechanisms through flavor experiments such as KOTO, COMET, and Mu2e.
