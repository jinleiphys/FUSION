# Linear Boltzmann Transport for Jet Propagation in the Quark-Gluon Plasma: Elastic Processes and Medium Recoil

> A Linear Boltzmann Transport model within perturbative QCD is developed for the study of parton propagation inside the quark-gluon plasma. Both leading partons and thermal recoil partons are tracked so that one can also study jet-induced medium excitations. In this study, we implement the complete set of elastic parton scattering processes and investigate elastic parton energy loss, transverse momentum broadening and their nontrivial energy and length dependence. We further investigate medium modifications of the jet shape and fragmentation functions of reconstructed jets. Contributions from thermal recoil partons are found to have significant influences on jet shape, fragmentation functions and angular distribution of reconstructed jets.

## Key claim

The Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model, which includes the complete set of elastic 2→2 parton scattering processes with full matrix elements, can describe parton energy loss and jet-induced medium excitations, but the study is limited to a uniform static QGP medium and only elastic processes; inelastic processes and dynamical medium expansion are deferred to future work.

## Method

The authors implement a Monte Carlo simulation of the linear Boltzmann equation for parton propagation in a QGP medium, using the complete set of leading-order pQCD 2→2 elastic scattering matrix elements with a Debye screening mass regulator. Scattering rates are precomputed as functions of parton energy and medium temperature, and the probability of multiple scatterings within discrete time intervals is sampled from a Poisson distribution. Both the leading (more energetic) parton and the thermal recoil parton from each scattering are tracked, with "negative" partons recorded to account for back-reaction from the Boltzmann equation.

## Key numbers

- Total scattering rate for a gluon in a QGP at T=200 MeV is approximately 1.53/fm, and for a quark approximately 0.68/fm, independent of incident energy for E >> T.
- The jet transport parameter q̂ for a gluon is approximately 42C_A ζ(3) α_s² T³ / (π μ_D²) × ⟨q⊥²⟩, with C_A=3; for a quark C_F=4/3.
- The Debye screening mass is μ_D² = g² T² (N_c + N_f/2)/3, with N_c=3 and N_f=3 active quark flavors.
- The strong coupling constant is fixed at α_s = 0.3 throughout the study.
- Elastic energy loss per unit length is studied for partons with initial energies from 10 to 200 GeV in a uniform medium at temperatures T=200, 300, and 400 MeV over a length L=8 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier versions of the LBT model (Li 2010, Wang 2010, 2013, 2014) by including the complete set of 2→2 elastic scattering processes with full matrix elements instead of the small-angle approximation. It enables future studies of jet transport and medium modification in realistic heavy-ion collisions when inelastic processes and dynamical medium expansion are incorporated. The model complements hydrodynamical approaches (Chaudhuri 2005, Betz 2008, Neufeld 2009, Qin 2009) and other parton transport models (Bouras 2012) for studying jet-induced medium excitations such as Mach cones and diffusion wakes.
