# Feasability of constraining the curvature parameter of the symmetry energy using elliptic flow data

> A QMD type transport model supplemented by a phase-space coalescence model fitted to FOPI experimental multiplicities of free nucleons and light clusters has been used to study the density dependence of the symmetry energy above the saturation point by a comparison with experimental elliptic flow ratios measured by the FOPI-LAND and ASYEOS collaborations in $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon impact energy. A previous calculation has proven that neutron-to-proton and neutron-to-charged particles elliptic flow ratios probe on average different densities allowing in principle the extraction of both the slope $L$ and curvature $K_{sym}$ parameters of the symmetry energy. Consequently a Gogny interaction inspired potential has been modified to allow independent changes of $L$ and $K_{sym}$. Comparing theoretical predictions with experimental data for neutron-to-proton and neutron-to-charged particles elliptic flow ratios the following constraints have been extracted: $L$=85$\pm$22(exp)$\pm$20(th)$\pm$12(sys) MeV and $K_{sym}$=96$\pm$315(exp)$\pm$170(th)$\pm$166(sys) MeV. Residual model dependence is accounted for in the magnitude of the quoted theoretical error. Systematical uncertainties are generated by the inability of the transport model to reproduce experimental light-cluster-to-proton multiplicity ratios. A value for $L$, free of systematical theoretical uncertainties, can be extracted from the neutron-to-proton elliptic flow ratio alone: $L$=84$\pm$30(exp)$\pm$19(th) MeV. It has also been demonstrated that elliptic flow ratios reach a maximum sensitivity on the $K_{sym}$ parameter in heavy-ion collisions of about 250 MeV/nucleon impact energy, allowing a reduction of the experimental component of uncertainty to about 150 MeV for this parameter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that both the slope parameter \(L\) and curvature parameter \(K_{sym}\) of the symmetry energy can be constrained simultaneously from elliptic flow data, but the extracted value for \(K_{sym}\) carries very large uncertainties (experimental, theoretical, and systematic) that make the constraint weak; the strongest sensitivity to \(K_{sym}\) occurs at impact energies around 250 MeV/nucleon, not at the 400 MeV/nucleon energy used in the main analysis.

## Method

A quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) transport model with a modified Gogny-inspired momentum-dependent interaction (MDI2) was used to simulate \({}^{197}\)Au+\({}^{197}\)Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon. The model included a phase-space coalescence algorithm fitted to FOPI experimental multiplicities of free nucleons and light clusters. Theoretical predictions for neutron-to-proton and neutron-to-charged particles elliptic flow ratios were compared with experimental data from the FOPI-LAND and ASYEOS collaborations to extract constraints on \(L\) and \(K_{sym}\).

## Key numbers

* \(L = 85 \pm 22\ \text{(exp)} \pm 20\ \text{(th)} \pm 12\ \text{(sys)}\) MeV from combined analysis of neutron-to-proton and neutron-to-charged particles elliptic flow ratios
* \(K_{sym} = 96 \pm 315\ \text{(exp)} \pm 170\ \text{(th)} \pm 166\ \text{(sys)}\) MeV from the same combined analysis
* \(L = 84 \pm 30\ \text{(exp)} \pm 19\ \text{(th)}\) MeV extracted from neutron-to-proton elliptic flow ratio alone, free of systematic theoretical uncertainties
* Maximum sensitivity of elliptic flow ratios to \(K_{sym}\) occurs at heavy-ion collision impact energy of about 250 MeV/nucleon, allowing reduction of the experimental uncertainty component to about 150 MeV for this parameter

## Context

This work extends previous studies that extracted constraints only for the slope parameter \(L\) of the symmetry energy using elliptic flow data from FOPI-LAND and ASYEOS collaborations (Russotto 2011, Cozma 2013, Wang 2014). The earlier studies used parametrizations allowing adjustment of only a single stiffness parameter, whereas this work introduces a modified interaction (MDI2) that permits independent variation of both \(L\) and \(K_{sym}\), following an approach similar to Xu (2014). The results enable future experimental measurements at around 250 MeV/nucleon impact energy to provide significantly tighter constraints on the curvature parameter \(K_{sym}\), which is crucial for understanding neutron star properties and the hyperon puzzle.
