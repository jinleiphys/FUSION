# The three-quark static potential in perturbation theory

> We study the three-quark static potential in perturbation theory in QCD. A complete next-to-leading order calculation is performed in the singlet, octets and decuplet channels and the potential exponentiation is demonstrated. The mixing of the octet representations is calculated. At next-to-next-to-leading order, the subset of diagrams producing three-body forces is identified in Coulomb gauge and its contribution to the potential calculated. Combining it with the contribution of the two-body forces, which may be extracted from the quark-antiquark static potential, we obtain the complete next-to-next-to-leading order three-quark static potential in the colour-singlet channel.

## Key claim
The paper provides the first complete next-to-leading order (NLO) calculation of the three-quark static potential in QCD for all colour channels (singlet, octets, decuplet), and identifies the first genuine three-body contribution at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in the colour-singlet channel. The abstract glosses over the fact that the octet representations mix already at leading order, requiring a \(2\times2\) matrix treatment, and that the NNLO result for the singlet combines the new three-body term with two-body contributions extracted from the quark-antiquark static potential.

## Method
The static potential is computed by matching the large-time behaviour of the three-quark static Wilson loop in QCD to the corresponding Green's function in potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD). Calculations are performed in Coulomb gauge, with amplitudes evaluated order by order in the strong coupling \(\alpha_s\): at LO (\(g^2\)), NLO (\(g^4\)), and NNLO (\(g^6\)). Colour factors for the singlet, symmetric and antisymmetric octets, and decuplet representations are computed explicitly, and the mixing of the two octet representations is handled via a \(2\times2\) potential matrix. At NNLO, diagrams producing three-body forces are identified and evaluated separately from two-body contributions.

## Key numbers
- LO colour-singlet potential: \(V_s^{(0)} = -\frac{2}{3}\alpha_s\left(\frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}_1|} + \frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}_2|} + \frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}_3|}\right)\), where \(\mathbf{r}_1 = \mathbf{x}_1 - \mathbf{x}_2\), \(\mathbf{r}_2 = \mathbf{x}_1 - \mathbf{x}_3\), \(\mathbf{r}_3 = \mathbf{x}_2 - \mathbf{x}_3\).
- LO colour-decuplet potential: \(V_d^{(0)} = \frac{1}{3}\alpha_s\left(\frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}_1|} + \frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}_2|} + \frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}_3|}\right)\).
- LO colour-octet potential is a \(2\times2\) matrix: \(V_O^{(0)} = \alpha_s\left[\frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}_1|}\begin{pmatrix}-\frac{2}{3}&0\\0&\frac{1}{3}\end{pmatrix} + \frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}_2|}\begin{pmatrix}\frac{1}{12}&-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}\\-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}&-\frac{5}{12}\end{pmatrix} + \frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}_3|}\begin{pmatrix}\frac{1}{12}&\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}\\\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}&-\frac{5}{12}\end{pmatrix}\right]\).
- Colour factors for the singlet: \(f^{(0)}_1(S) = f^{(0)}_2(S) = f^{(0)}_3(S) = -\frac{2}{3}\).
- Colour factors for the decuplet: \(f^{(0)}_1(\Delta) = f^{(0)}_2(\Delta) = f^{(0)}_3(\Delta) = \frac{1}{3}\).

## Context
This work extends the perturbative treatment of the static quark-antiquark potential (known up to two loops from Peter (1997) and Schroder (1998), and with infrared divergences resummed at NLL by Brambilla (2009)) to the three-quark system. It provides the first NLO calculation for all colour channels and the first NNLO result for the colour-singlet channel, enabling comparisons with lattice data at short distances (e.g., from Takahashi (2002, 2004) and Hubner (2007)) and tests of the transition from perturbative to non-perturbative QCD in baryonic systems. The octet mixing identified here must be accounted for in lattice simulations using non-isosceles geometries.
