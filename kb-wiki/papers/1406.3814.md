# Phase change near $N=70$ in the wave function of the $I^π=11/2^-$ isomers along the cadmium-isotope chain

> The electromagnetic features of the $11/2^-$ isomers in the $^{111-127}$Cd isotopes are reproduced by numerically optimized shell-model wave-functions. A sudden phase change of the wave functions at $N = 70$ is identified and further confirmed by the evolution of B(E2, $7/2^-_1\rightarrow 11/2^-_1$) values. This phase change gives rise to different linear relations for the $Q$ and $μ$ values with $N<70$ and $N>70$, as needed to reproduce the experimental data. The particle-hole transformation properties for $h_{11/2}$ neutrons in a well-isolated subshell involving degenerate $s_{1/2}$, $d_{3/2}$, $d_{5/2}$ and $h_{11/2}$ orbits is suggested as a possible explanation for this phase change.

## Key claim

A sudden phase change in the hexadecapole component of the shell-model wave functions for the \(11/2^-\) isomers in cadmium isotopes occurs at \(N=70\), explaining the change in linear behavior of quadrupole and magnetic moments; this phase change is attributed to the particle-hole transformation properties of \(h_{11/2}\) neutrons in a nearly degenerate subshell involving the \(s_{1/2}\), \(d_{3/2}\), \(d_{5/2}\) and \(h_{11/2}\) orbits.

## Method

The authors constructed shell-model wave functions for the \(11/2^-\) isomers in \(^{111-127}\)Cd using a proton configuration space of two proton holes in the \(Z=50\) core (the \((g_{9/2})^{-2}\) hole states) coupled to a single unpaired \(h_{11/2}\) neutron in a seniority state. They considered trial wave functions including \(\tilde{S}\), \(\tilde{D}\), and \(\tilde{G}\) configurations (denoted \(SDG\)), with amplitudes fitted to reproduce experimental magnetic moments \(\mu\) and quadrupole moments \(Q\) using a linear parameterization of the \(C_D\) amplitude and a neutron effective charge \(e_\nu\) as a fitting parameter. The relative phase of the \(C_G\) amplitude was then determined separately for isotopes with \(N<70\) and \(N>70\) by comparing calculated \(Q\) values and B(E2, \(7/2^-_1 \rightarrow 11/2^-_1\)) values to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Neutron effective charge from the \(SDG\) fit: \(e_\nu = 1.496 \pm 0.221\) e.
- Root-mean-square deviation (RMSD) of the \(SDG\) fit to quadrupole moments: 0.009 eb.
- Proton effective charge determined from the \(2^+_1\) state of \(^{112}\)Cd: \(e_\pi = -2.844\) e.
- Schmidt magnetic moment of the \(h_{11/2}\) neutron orbit: \(\mu_{sp}(h_{11/2}) = -1.339 \mu_N\).
- Single-particle quadrupole moment of the \(h_{11/2}\) orbit: \(Q_{sp}(h_{11/2}) = -0.055 r^2_0 \times e_\nu\), with \(r^2_0 = \frac{\hbar}{M\omega} = 1.012 A^{1/3}\).
- Proton matrix elements used: \(\langle D_\pi || \hat{Q} || D_\pi \rangle = -4.475 e \times r^2_0\), \(\langle G_\pi || \hat{Q} || G_\pi \rangle = -2.134 e \times r^2_0\), \(\langle S_\pi || \hat{Q} || D_\pi \rangle = -14.265 e \times r^2_0\), \(\langle D_\pi || \hat{Q} || G_\pi \rangle = -12.164 e \times r^2_0\); \(\langle D_\pi || \hat{\mu} || D_\pi \rangle = +3.698 \mu_N\), \(\langle G_\pi || \hat{\mu} || G_\pi \rangle = +6.751 \mu_N\).

## Context

This work extends the study of \(11/2^-\) isomers in Cd isotopes by Stuchbery et al. (2013) (ref. [cd11]), who noted a change in linear behavior of magnetic moments at \(N=70\) and suggested a simple seniority-based description. The present paper provides a shell-model explanation for that change, identifying a phase change in the hexadecapole (\(G_\pi\)) component of the wave function. A density functional calculation (ref. [dif-70]) had previously suggested different mechanisms for \(Q\) linearity on either side of \(N=70\); this work confirms that picture with a microscopic wave-function analysis. The results support the assumption of a nearly degenerate subshell of \(s_{1/2}\), \(d_{3/2}\), \(d_{5/2}\) and \(h_{11/2}\) orbits in the Cd region, as proposed by Stuchbery et al. (2013), and enable future studies of electromagnetic properties in neighboring isotopic chains.
