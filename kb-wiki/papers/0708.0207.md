# Restless pions: orbifold boundary conditions and noise suppression in lattice QCD

> The study of one or more baryons in lattice QCD is severely hindered by the exponential decay in time of the signal-to-noise ratio. The rate at which the signal-to-noise decreases is a function of the the pion mass. More precisely, it depends on the minimum allowed pion energy in the box, which, for periodic boundary conditions, is equal to its mass. We propose a set of boundary conditions, given by a "parity orbifold'' construction, which eliminates the zero momentum pion modes, raising the minimum pion energy without altering the QCD ground state, and thereby improving the signal-to-noise ratio of (multi)-baryon correlation functions at long Euclidean times. We discuss variations of these "restless pions" boundary conditions and focus on their impact on the study of nuclear forces.

## Key claim

The paper proposes "parity orbifold" boundary conditions that eliminate zero-momentum pion modes in lattice QCD, thereby raising the minimum pion energy and improving the signal-to-noise ratio of multi-baryon correlation functions at long Euclidean times, but only when implemented in the valence sector because the boundary action is not gamma5-Hermitian and thus unsuitable for dynamical sea quarks.

## Method

The authors construct a parity orbifold by extending the spatial lattice from [0,L] to [-L,L] and identifying points z and -z through a relation between quark and gluon fields, effectively turning the circle into a line segment. For the one-dimensional S^1/Z_2 orbifold, they impose A_mu(t,x,y,z)=A_mu(t,x,y,-z) for mu not equal to 3, A_3(t,x,y,z)=-A_3(t,x,y,-z), and q(t,x,y,z)=P_z q(t,x,y,-z) where P_z=i gamma5 gamma3 is the z-parity operator. For the three-dimensional T^3/Z_2 orbifold, they impose A_0(t,r)=A_0(t,-r), A_i(t,r)=-A_i(t,-r) for i=1,2,3, and q(t,r)=P q(t,-r) with P=gamma0. The method is implemented only on valence propagators in the background of dynamical gauge configurations with periodic boundary conditions, following the "partially twisted boundary conditions" approach.

## Key numbers

- Signal-to-noise ratio for two-nucleon correlators with periodic boundary conditions decays as sqrt(N) e^{-(2M-3m_pi)t}, where M is the nucleon mass and m_pi is the pion mass
- For the S^1/Z_2 orbifold, the minimum pion energy becomes sqrt((pi/L)^2 + m_pi^2), raising the signal-to-noise decay exponent from (2M-3m_pi) to (2M-3*sqrt((pi/L)^2+m_pi^2))
- For the T^3/Z_2 orbifold, the minimum pion energy becomes sqrt(3(pi/L)^2 + m_pi^2), further improving the signal-to-noise decay exponent to (2M-3*sqrt(3(pi/L)^2+m_pi^2))
- The example calculation uses a pion mass of 350 MeV and a box size of L=2.5 fm, matching parameters from the NPLQCD collaboration calculation of Ref. [nplqcd_NN]
- Energy shifts found in that calculation for pion masses above 350 MeV are of order 10-20 MeV

## Context

This work addresses the severe signal-to-noise problem in lattice QCD studies of baryons and multi-baryon systems, which was identified by Lepage (1989) and has hindered calculations of nucleon-nucleon forces by the NPLQCD collaboration and hyperon-nucleon interactions. The method extends earlier work on twisted boundary conditions by Bedaque (2004) and hybrid boundary conditions used for pentaquark studies by Ishii (2004) and charmonium studies by Iida (2006). The parity orbifold construction draws from similar techniques used in extra-dimensional Standard Model extensions, domain-wall fermions by Kaplan (1992), and the Schrodinger functional formalism. The approach enables improved extraction of nuclear forces from lattice QCD and could benefit K->pi pi amplitude calculations using the Lellouch-Luscher method, though only for the I=2 channel.
