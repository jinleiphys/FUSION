# X(3872) and Its Iso-Triplet Partners

> Decays of X(3872) and its partners as hidden-charm axial-vector tetra-quark mesons are studied. As the result, it is seen that the iso-triplet partners of X(3872) can be broad, and therefore, higher statistics would be needed to find them.

## Key claim

The iso-triplet partners of X(3872) can be considerably broad, with widths potentially reaching hundreds of MeV, which explains why they have not been observed despite searches; higher statistics will be needed to find them, but the negative search results do not exclude their existence.

## Method

The author assigns X(3872) and its partners to hidden-charm axial-vector tetra-quark mesons of the type {[cn](c̄n̄) ± (cn)[c̄n̄]} with I=0 and I=1, using a quark model classification with color anti-triplet and triplet configurations. Decay rates are calculated using vector meson dominance (VMD) and the ωρ⁰ mixing mechanism, comparing the isospin-conserving decay of the iso-triplet partner X⁰_I(+) → ρ⁰J/ψ → π⁺π⁻J/ψ with the isospin-non-conserving decay of X(3872) → ωJ/ψ → ρ⁰J/ψ → π⁺π⁻J/ψ.

## Key numbers

- Mass of X(3872): m_X = 3871.56 ± 0.22 MeV; width: Γ_X < 1.2 MeV (90% CL)
- Ratio of branching fractions: Br(X(3872)→π⁺π⁻π⁰J/ψ) / Br(X(3872)→π⁺π⁻J/ψ) = 0.8 ± 0.3
- Ratio of decay rates: Γ(X(3872)→D⁰D̄*⁰ + c.c.) / Γ(X(3872)→π⁺π⁻J/ψ) = 9.5 ± 3.1
- Mass of X(3875) (identified with X(3872)): m = 3872.6^{+0.6+0.4}_{-0.4-0.5} MeV; width: Γ = 3.9^{+2.8+0.2}_{-1.4-1.1} MeV
- Calculated ratio: Γ(X⁰_I(+)→ρ⁰J/ψ→π⁺π⁻J/ψ) / Γ(X(3872)→π⁺π⁻J/ψ) ∼ 200
- Estimated width for X⁰_I(+)→ρ⁰J/ψ→π⁺π⁻J/ψ: Γ ∼ (20 – 200) MeV

## Context

This work extends the tetra-quark interpretation of X(3872) previously developed by the author (Terasaki, 2007) and contrasts with the charmonium assignment and the isospin-violating tetra-quark model of Maiani (2005). The analysis builds on the ωρ⁰ mixing mechanism for isospin non-conservation in X(3872) decays (Terasaki, 2010) and applies it to predict broad iso-triplet partners. The results motivate future experimental searches with higher statistics, as the broad X⁰_I(+) would appear as a wide enhancement in the π⁺π⁻J/ψ mass distribution behind the narrow X(3872) peak, and existing searches (Babar, 2007) for charged partners have insufficient statistical accuracy to observe such broad states.
