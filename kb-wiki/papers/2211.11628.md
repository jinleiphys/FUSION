# On chiral spin symmetry and the QCD phase diagram

> Recently, an approximate $SU(4)$ chiral spin-flavour symmetry was observed in multiplet patterns of QCD meson correlation functions, in a temperature range above the chiral crossover. This symmetry is larger than the chiral symmetry of massless QCD, and can only arise effectively when colour-electric quark-gluon interactions dynamically dominate the quantum effective action. At temperatures about three times the crossover temperature, these patterns disappear again, indicating the screening of colour-electric interactions, and the expected chiral symmetry is recovered. In this contribution we collect independent evidence for such an intermediate temperature range, based on screening masses and the pion spectral function. Both kinds of observables behave non-perturbatively in this window, with resonance-like peaks for the pion and its first excitation disappearing gradually with temperature. Using symmetry arguments and the known behaviour of screening masses at small densities, we discuss how this chiral spin symmetric band continues into the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim
The paper argues that QCD at physical quark masses exhibits three distinct temperature regimes with different effective symmetries, separated by two crossovers: a low-temperature chirally broken regime, an intermediate regime (roughly $T_\mathrm{pc} \lesssim T \lesssim 3T_\mathrm{pc}$) with an approximate $SU(4)$ chiral spin symmetry that is larger than ordinary chiral symmetry, and a high-temperature regime where ordinary chiral symmetry is recovered. The existence of this intermediate band is inferred from multiple independent observables, but the boundaries are approximate and the upper crossover is identified by a bending in screening masses rather than a sharp transition.

## Method
The authors analyze lattice QCD results for spatial and temporal meson correlation functions, screening masses, and the pion spectral function, using data from multiple prior lattice studies with different fermion discretizations (domain wall fermions, HISQ fermions, and $O(a)$-improved Wilson fermions). For the spectral function, they apply a novel method based on the Kallen-Lehmann representation adapted to finite temperature, fitting spatial correlators with two exponentials to extract resonance parameters for the pion and its first excitation. They then use symmetry arguments and Taylor expansion of screening masses at small baryon chemical potential to extrapolate the chiral spin symmetric band into the QCD phase diagram.

## Key numbers
- The chiral spin symmetric band is observed in the temperature range $T_\mathrm{pc} \lesssim T \lesssim 3T_\mathrm{pc}$, where $T_\mathrm{pc} \approx 150$ MeV is the chiral crossover temperature.
- Screening masses show a sudden change in temperature dependence between $T \sim 500$–$700$ MeV, marking the upper boundary of the chiral spin symmetric band.
- At high temperatures ($T \sim 1$–$160$ GeV), screening masses follow a perturbative parametrization $m_{PS}/(2\pi T) = 1 + p_2 \hat{g}^2(T) + p_3 \hat{g}^3(T) + p_4 \hat{g}^4(T)$ over three orders of magnitude in temperature, with the spin splitting described by a single $\hat{g}^4$ term.
- At $T=220$ MeV, the spectral function extracted from spatial correlators shows resonance-like peaks for the pion and its first excitation, which widen and disappear with increasing temperature.

## Context
This work extends the earlier observation of approximate $SU(4)$ chiral spin symmetry in meson correlation functions by Rohrhofer et al. (2019) and provides independent evidence from screening masses and spectral functions. The screening mass analysis builds on dimensional reduction studies by Hart et al. (2000) showing that colour-electric fields dominate the dynamics, and on high-precision lattice calculations by Dalla Brida et al. (2021) covering $T \sim 1$–$160$ GeV. The paper contradicts the expectation that perturbation theory should work immediately above the chiral crossover, and instead identifies a non-perturbative "stringy fluid" regime. The discussion of the phase diagram at finite density connects to concepts of baryon parity doublet matter, quarkyonic matter (McLerran 2007), and prior work on screening masses at small chemical potential by Hart et al. (2000) and Pushkina et al. (2004). The results enable future lattice studies to map the upper crossover temperature $T_s(\mu)$ as a function of baryon chemical potential using methods like imaginary chemical potential or Taylor expansion.
