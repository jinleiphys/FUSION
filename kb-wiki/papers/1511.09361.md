# Ground state of the two-dimensional attractive Fermi gas: essential properties from few- to many-body

> We calculate the ground-state properties of unpolarized two-dimensional attractive fermions in the range from few to many particles. Using first-principles lattice Monte Carlo methods, we determine the ground-state energy, Tan's contact, momentum distribution, and single-particle correlation function. We investigate those properties for systems of $N=4,8,...,40$ particles and for a wide range of attractive couplings. As the attractive coupling is increased, the thermodynamic limit is reached at progressively lower $N$ due to the dominance of the two-body sector. At large momenta $k$, the momentum distribution displays the expected $k^{-4}$ behavior, but its onset shifts from $k \simeq 1.8 k^{}_F$ at weak coupling towards higher $k$ at strong coupling.

## Key claim
The ground-state energy, Tan's contact, momentum distribution, and single-particle correlation function of two-dimensional attractive fermions show a smooth crossover from few- to many-body behavior, with the thermodynamic limit reached at progressively lower particle numbers as the attractive coupling increases due to the dominance of the two-body sector.

## Method
The authors used first-principles lattice Monte Carlo methods with a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to simulate unpolarized two-dimensional fermions with short-range attractive interactions on spatial lattices of side 24 to 40 points. They computed ground-state properties for systems of N=4,8,12,...,40 particles across a wide range of attractive couplings, extrapolating to infinite volume and infinite imaginary time (beta to infinity) to obtain ground-state results.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state energy calculated for N=4,8,12,...,40 particles at couplings eta = -1.5, -1.0, -0.5, 0.0, 0.5, 1.0, 2.0, 3.0, and infinity (free system), extrapolated to infinite volume
- Momentum distribution for N=36 particles shows expected k^{-4} behavior at large momenta, with onset shifting from k approximately 1.8 k_F at weak coupling toward higher k at strong coupling
- Fit parameter a for the single-particle correlation function ranges from a=0.45(3) at eta=-2.0 to a=0.0(1) at eta=3.0, with mean residuals between 0.001 and 0.03
- Spatial lattice sizes used: N_x = 24, 28, 32, 36, 40 points; temporal lattice spacing tau such that tau/ell^2 = 0.01 to 0.05
- At least 500 decorrelated Monte Carlo samples taken, with statistical uncertainty of order 5% or less

## Context
This work extends prior diffusion Monte Carlo calculations of the ground-state energy and contact in the thermodynamic limit by Bertaina (2011) and the ab initio study by Shi, Chiesa, and Zhang (2013) to the few-body regime and the crossover to many-body physics. The results enable systematic comparison with experimental ultracold atomic gas measurements in two dimensions, particularly for understanding how few-body shell effects heal to the thermodynamic limit, and provide benchmarks for mean-field approaches to the BEC-BCS crossover in two dimensions.
