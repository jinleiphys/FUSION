# Accelerating longitudinal expansion of resistive relativistic-magneto-hydrodynamics in heavy ion collisions

> We study the evolution of the longitudinal expansion of an ideal fluid with finite electrical conductivity, which is subject to the EM fields. In the framework of resistive relativistic-magneto-hydrodynamic, we find an exact analytical solution for the EM fields and for the acceleration of the fluid.

# Accelerating longitudinal expansion of resistive relativistic-magneto-hydrodynamics in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The authors claim that in resistive relativistic magneto-hydrodynamics (RRMHD) with finite electrical conductivity, the longitudinal expansion of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) subject to electromagnetic fields is accelerated, meaning the Bjorken flow is generalized and the solutions are not Lorentz invariant. However, this acceleration depends on the choice of an arbitrary function \(c(\eta)\) and the acceleration parameter \(\lambda\) decreases with proper time, becoming negligible when electrical conductivity is high.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled conservation equations of energy-momentum and Maxwell equations in Milne coordinates within the RRMHD framework, assuming an ideal ultra-relativistic fluid with finite electrical conductivity \(\sigma\) and an equation of state \(P = \kappa\epsilon\) with \(\kappa = 1/3\). They consider a (1+1)-dimensional longitudinal expansion with transverse electromagnetic fields (electric field along \(x\), magnetic field along \(y\)), adopt an ansatz relating the fields to a function \(h(\tau,\eta)\), and reduce the partial differential equations to ordinary differential equations to obtain analytical solutions for the fluid rapidity, four-velocity, electromagnetic fields, and energy density.

## Key numbers

- Electrical conductivity \(\sigma = 0.023\) fm\(^{-1}\) is used as a representative value in the figures.
- The acceleration parameter \(\lambda\) is shown for proper times \(\tau_0 = 0.5\) fm, \(\tau = 1\) fm, and \(\tau_1 = 3\) fm.
- The initial magnetic field at mid-rapidity in the lab frame is taken as \(B_L^y(\tau_0, 0) = 0.0018\) GeV\(^2/e\) from prior work (Gursoy 2018).
- The parameter \(\alpha = 0.1\) is used as a representative value for the function \(c(\eta) = c_0 \cosh(\alpha\eta)\).
- For \(\sigma > 0.053\) fm\(^{-1}\) the rapidity profile of energy density is not flat; for \(\alpha \le 0.06\) the energy density ratio becomes divergent at high rapidity.
- In the infinite conductivity limit (\(\sigma \rightarrow \infty\)), the Bjorken model is recovered with \(\epsilon \propto \tau^{-4/3}\).

## Context

This work extends the Bjorken model (Bjorken 1983) by incorporating finite electrical conductivity and electromagnetic fields into the longitudinal expansion of the QGP, generalizing earlier ideal RMHD treatments (Roy 2015, She 2019). It builds on studies of accelerating hydrodynamic descriptions (Csanad 2018) and on lattice QCD calculations of finite electrical conductivity (Ding 2011, Amato 2013). The results enable future comparisons with experimental observables such as rapidity spectra and the chiral magnetic effect, and suggest that acceleration decreases with increasing center-of-mass energy, consistent with Csanad (2018).
