# Color entanglement for azimuthal asymmetries in the Drell-Yan process

> In the resummation of collinear gluons emitted together with active partons from the hadrons in the Drell-Yan process (DY) effects of color entanglement become important when the transverse directions are taken into account. It is then no longer possible to write the cross section as the convolution of two soft correlators and a hard part. We show that the color entanglement introduces additional color factors that must be taken into account in the extraction of transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMD PDFs) from azimuthal asymmetries. Examples where such effects matter are the extraction of the double Sivers and double Boer-Mulders asymmetries. Furthermore, we will argue why this color entanglement is a basic ingredient already in the tree-level description of azimuthal asymmetries.

## Key claim

Color entanglement from gauge links in the Drell-Yan process introduces additional color factors beyond the naive 1/Nc factor, which must be accounted for when extracting transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMD PDFs) from azimuthal asymmetries; this entanglement is already present at tree-level when transverse momentum is measured, not only in resummation.

## Method

The authors start from the tree-level Drell-Yan cross section expression and include gauge links (Wilson lines) that connect the parton correlators to the hard scattering. They expand both the TMD correlators and the cross section in azimuthal moments (symmetric traceless tensors of rank m in transverse momentum), then compute the color factors that arise when weighting the cross section with products of transverse momenta from each hadron. The color factors are derived by evaluating traces of symmetrized color charges (T^a) that appear when derivatives act on the gauge connections, producing gluonic pole correlators.

## Key numbers

* For the mixed weighting ⟨p1T^α p2T^β σ_DY⟩, the term with two gluonic pole correlators (Φ_G from each hadron) acquires a color factor of -1/(Nc^2-1) * 1/Nc, compared to the naive 1/Nc.
* For weighting with p1T^α1 p1T^α2 p2T^β, a term with Φ_GG and Φ_G has color factor - (Nc^2+2)/[(Nc^2-2)(Nc^2-1)] * 1/Nc.
* For weighting with p1T^α1 p1T^α2 p2T^β1 p2T^β2, a term with two Φ_GG correlators has color factor + (3Nc^4-8Nc^2-4)/[(Nc^2-2)^2(Nc^2-1)] * 1/Nc.
* The gluonic pole coefficients for simple staple gauge links U_± are C_G^[±] = ±1.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of color entanglement in hadroproduction processes first identified by Rogers (2010) and argued to be important at tree-level by Buffing (2011). It provides explicit color factors for the Drell-Yan process, building on the general formalism for TMD correlators with gauge links developed by Buffing (2012). The results show that the double Sivers asymmetry (requiring polarized beams) and the double Boer-Mulders asymmetry (accessible with unpolarized beams at the LHC) are the first azimuthal asymmetries where these additional color factors appear, implying suppression and sign changes compared to naive parton model calculations. The paper does not prove factorization and notes that soft factor inclusion in the TMD definition (Collins, 2011) and next-to-leading order effects remain to be addressed.
