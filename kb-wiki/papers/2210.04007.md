# Bremsstrahlung photon from a hadronizing quark-gluon plasma

> Assuming that quark and antiquark numbers are separately conserved during hadronization, we calculate Bremsstrahlung photon from a hadronizing quark-gluon plasma. The quark and antiquark numbers are obtained from the hadron numbers in the statistical model and the transition amplitudes for the hadronization from the constraint that all quarks and antiquarks must be consumed in the hadronization. Then Bremsstrahlung photon from the hadronization is obtained in the soft photon approximation, and we find that its contribution to the direct photon increases in low-energy heavy-ion collisions and in peripheral collisions where the lifetime of a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is relatively short.

## Key claim

Assuming separate conservation of quark and antiquark numbers during hadronization, the Bremsstrahlung photon contribution to direct photon production increases in low-energy heavy-ion collisions and in peripheral collisions where the QGP lifetime is relatively short, but this contribution is only visible when the thermal photon background is small.

## Method

The author calculates Bremsstrahlung photon emission from hadronization using the soft photon approximation, where the incoming and outgoing momenta of quarks and antiquarks are the only necessary input. Quark and antiquark number densities at the critical temperature \(T_c = 0.16\) GeV are obtained from the statistical model, and constant transition amplitudes for meson, baryon, and antibaryon formation are determined from the constraint that all quarks and antiquarks are consumed during hadronization. The photon spectrum is then computed by integrating over thermal Fermi-Dirac distributions of quarks and antiquarks with effective constituent quark masses.

## Key numbers

*   At \(T_c = 0.16\) GeV and \(\mu_B = 0\), the statistical model gives meson number density \(n_m = 0.35\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) and baryon/antibaryon densities \(n_b = n_{\bar{b}} = 0.032\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
*   At \(T_c = 0.16\) GeV and \(\mu_B = 200\) MeV, the statistical model gives \(n_m = 0.35\ \text{fm}^{-3}\), \(n_b = 0.056\ \text{fm}^{-3}\), and \(n_{\bar{b}} = 0.0046\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
*   The effective (anti)quark mass is taken as 340 MeV for \(\mu_B = 0\) and 310 MeV for \(\mu_B = 200\) MeV.
*   The Bremsstrahlung photon spectrum from a hadronizing QGP at \(\mu_B = 0\) is about 10% larger at low momentum than that at \(\mu_B = 200\) MeV.
*   The QGP volume at \(T_c\) is estimated as 1090 \(\text{fm}^3\) for 0-10% central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A GeV and 1380 \(\text{fm}^3\) for 0-20% central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}} = 200\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of direct photon production in heavy-ion collisions by addressing a previously neglected source: Bremsstrahlung photons from the hadronization process itself, which has barely been studied (vanHees 2014, vanHees 2011, Rapp 2013, Garcia-Montero 2019, Fujii 2022). The calculation uses the soft photon approximation (Low 1958, Koch 1990, Peskin 1995) and the statistical model (Andronic 2021) to determine quark densities. The results are compared with experimental data from the WA98 and PHENIX Collaborations, and the author suggests that this contribution may help explain the "direct photon puzzle" of large elliptic flows, as it is produced at the late hadronization stage. The work enables future studies of hadronization as a signal for QGP formation, particularly at collision energies near the phase boundary.
