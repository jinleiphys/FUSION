# Disperon QED

> We present disperon QED, a method to deal with data input in loop processes in Monte Carlo codes. It relies on dispersion relations, automated tools such as OpenLoops, effective field theory methods and a threshold subtraction. We motivate this method and apply it to the process $ee\toππ$ in McMule to deal with hadronic vacuum polarisation insertions in two-loop contributions as well as the vector form factor of the pion within the form-factor scalar QED approximation. The generality of this method for more complicated processes is emphasised.

## Key claim
Disperon QED transforms a generic numerical hadronic function (such as hadronic vacuum polarisation or the pion vector form factor) into a massive vector boson propagator via a dispersion relation, enabling automated loop calculations with standard Monte Carlo tools like OpenLoops and McMule, provided a threshold subtraction and an effective-field-theory treatment for large dispersive masses are employed.

## Method
The method replaces a hadronic function F(k²) in a loop integral by its once-subtracted dispersion relation, converting the k² dependence into a propagator 1/(k² − s₁) for a new massive particle called a disperon, whose mass √s₁ is integrated over. The loop diagrams are generated and evaluated with OpenLoops in a theory (disperon QED) that includes the disperon as an additional particle with the same couplings as the photon. For large disperon masses, numerical stability is improved by switching to a custom effective field theory (DET) that expands in inverse powers of the disperon mass, and a threshold subtraction handles singularities near the pair-production threshold.

## Key numbers
* The hadronic vacuum polarisation dispersion integral runs from threshold s_thr = 4m_π² to infinity.
* The first resonance (ρ/ω) lies at approximately (7–8) × 4m_π², justifying a linear approximation for Π_h near threshold.
* For the resummed vacuum polarisation near the J/ψ region in ee→μμγ, the difference between resummed and non-resummed NLO computations is 60%.
* The perturbative one-loop vacuum polarisation for a lepton of mass m behaves as −α/(9πk²)(5 + 3 log(−m²/k²)) + O(k⁻⁴) at large k².

## Context
This work extends the dispersive approach used by Colangelo (2022) for the forward-backward asymmetry in ee→ππ and by Ignatov (2022) using generalised vector-meson dominance, and generalises it to a fully automated, process-independent framework. The method enables the inclusion of hadronic vacuum polarisation and form-factor effects in loop calculations for processes such as ee→ππ and ee→ππγ at NLO and NNLO, going beyond the form-factor-times-scalar-QED (F×sQED) approximation that was shown by Budassi (2024) to give poor agreement with experimental data for the forward-backward asymmetry. Future applications include radiative processes with more external legs and two-photon exchange in lepton-proton scattering.
