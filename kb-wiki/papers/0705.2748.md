# Angular anisotropy of the fusion-fission and quasifission fragments

> The anisotropy in the angular distribution of the fusion-fission and quasifission fragments for the $^{16}$O+$^{238}$U, $^{19}$F+$^{208}$Pb and $^{32}$S+$^{208}$Pb reactions is studied by analyzing the angular momentum distributions of the dinuclear system and compound nucleus which are formed after capture and complete fusion, respectively. The orientation angles of axial symmetry axes of colliding nuclei to the beam direction are taken into account for the calculation of the variance of the projection of the total spin onto the fission axis. It is shown that the deviation of the experimental angular anisotropy from the statistical model picture is connected with the contribution of the quasifission fragments which is dominant in the $^{32}$S+$^{208}$Pb reaction. Enhancement of anisotropy at low energies in the $^{16}$O+$^{238}$U reaction is connected with quasifission of the dinuclear system having low temperature and effective moment of inertia.

## Key claim

The deviation of experimental angular anisotropy from standard statistical model predictions is attributed to the contribution of quasifission fragments, not solely to the failure of the statistical model at high angular momenta; the enhancement of anisotropy at low energies in the 16O+238U reaction is specifically connected with quasifission of the dinuclear system having low temperature and effective moment of inertia.

## Method

The authors calculated capture, fusion, and quasifission excitation functions using the dinuclear system (DNS) concept, averaging over orientation angles of the symmetry axes of deformed target and projectile nuclei. They determined mean square values of orbital angular momentum for fusion-fission and quasifission separately, then computed the angular anisotropy A using the Halpern-Strutinski formula with effective moments of inertia from the rotating finite range model (RFRM) for compound nuclei and from their DNS model for quasifission. The competition between complete fusion and quasifission was treated statistically via branching ratios of widths for overcoming the intrinsic fusion barrier and the quasifission barrier.

## Key numbers

- For the 16O+238U reaction at low energies (Elab less than 100 MeV), the anisotropy A from quasifission is very close to experimental data, while fusion-fission fragments show less anisotropy due to small <l^2> values and large effective moment of inertia.
- The dissipation of initial orbital angular momentum during capture reduces the angular momentum by about 25-30 percent from the initial value l0.
- The effective temperature at the saddle point is calculated as T_sad = [(E_cm + Q_gg - B_f(l) - E_n)/(A_CN/8)]^(1/2).
- The quasifission contribution increases with beam energy because the intrinsic fusion barrier B*_fus increases and the quasifission barrier B_qf decreases with increasing orbital angular momentum l.
- For the 16O+238U reaction in the energy interval 90 less than Elab less than 130 MeV, the contribution of fusion-fission fragments dominates over quasifission.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of angular anisotropy in heavy-ion reactions by explicitly separating fusion-fission and quasifission contributions using the DNS model, building on prior experimental studies by Hinde (1995) and Back (1985) who observed deviations from standard statistical model predictions. The method enables quantitative comparison of anisotropy from both processes, addressing the long-standing discrepancy between measured fission fragment anisotropies and transition state model calculations, and provides a framework for interpreting fragment angular distributions in reactions used for superheavy element synthesis.
