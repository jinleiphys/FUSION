# Deconfinement Phase Transition in Neutron Stars and δ-Meson Field

> The Maxwell and Glendenning construction scenarios of deconfinement phase transition in neutron star matter are investigated. The hadronic phase is described within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory, if also the scalar-isovector δ-meson field is taken into account. The strange quark phase is described in the frame of MIT bag model, including the effect of perturbative one-gluon exchange interactions. The influence of the δ-meson field on the deconfinement phase transition boundary characteristics is discussed.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the scalar-isovector δ-meson field in the relativistic mean-field (RMF) description of hadronic matter alters the threshold characteristics of the deconfinement phase transition in neutron star matter, but the specific effect depends on whether the Maxwell construction (MC) or Glendenning construction (GC) scenario is used.

## Method

The hadronic phase was modeled using RMF theory with σ, ω, ρ, and δ meson fields, with coupling constants from a prior parameter set (Alaverdyan, 2012). The quark phase was described using the MIT bag model with one-gluon exchange interactions, with quark masses mu=5 MeV, md=7 MeV, ms=150 MeV, bag constant B=100 MeV/fm³, and strong coupling αs=0.5. The deconfinement phase transition was computed under both the Maxwell construction (first-order transition with a density jump) and the Glendenning construction (mixed phase with continuous pressure and density), applying global electrical neutrality for the mixed phase.

## Key numbers

- GC with δ-meson: lower threshold baryon density nN = 0.241 fm⁻³, upper threshold nQ = 1.448 fm⁻³; lower threshold pressure PN = 16.9 MeV/fm³, upper threshold PQ = 474.4 MeV/fm³; lower threshold energy density εN = 235.0 MeV/fm³, upper threshold εQ = 1889.3 MeV/fm³.
- GC without δ-meson: nN = 0.260 fm⁻³, nQ = 1.436 fm⁻³; PN = 18.0 MeV/fm³, PQ = 471.3 MeV/fm³; εN = 253.8 MeV/fm³, εQ = 1870.8 MeV/fm³.
- MC with δ-meson: nN = 0.475 fm⁻³, nQ = 0.650 fm⁻³; coexistence pressure P0 = 93.3 MeV/fm³; εN = 503.3 MeV/fm³, εQ = 723.5 MeV/fm³.
- MC without δ-meson: nN = 0.551 fm⁻³, nQ = 0.717 fm⁻³; P0 = 121.3 MeV/fm³; εN = 593.4 MeV/fm³, εQ = 810.0 MeV/fm³.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the hadron-quark deconfinement transition in neutron stars (Glendenning, 1992; Heiselberg, 1993) by incorporating the δ-meson field into the RMF description of hadronic matter, using parameter sets from Alaverdyan (2012). The results show that the δ-meson field softens the lower threshold and stiffens the upper threshold in the GC scenario, while lowering the coexistence pressure in the MC scenario; these changes can affect the structural properties of neutron stars containing quark matter.
