# Global polarization of $Λ$ hyperons and its sensitivity to equations of state in low-energy heavy-ion collisions

> Significant global polarization of $Λ$ hyperons along the direction of the orbital angular momentum has been measured in non-central heavy-ion collisions where the equation of state (EOS) of the produced dense matter is expected to change from intermediate to low colliding energies. We study the sensitivity of the global $Λ$ polarization to EOS in heavy-ion collisions within the SMASH transport model. Among the three different EOS we considered, only the hadron resonance gas (HRG) describes the experimental data well at low colliding energies even when it is below the $Λ$ production threshold in nucleon-nucleon collisions. The polarization induced by thermal vorticity as a function of centrality, rapidity, and transverse momentum at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3$ GeV in Au+Au collisions is shown to agree well with the experimental data. Our study also indicates a possible peak in the global $Λ$ polarization around $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.4$ GeV in Au+Au collisions. Furthermore, we find that the rapidity and transverse momentum-dependent helicity polarization induced by thermal vorticity vanishes due to space-reversal symmetry.

## Key claim

The global polarization of Λ hyperons in low-energy heavy-ion collisions is sensitive to the equation of state (EOS) of the produced dense matter, and only the hadron resonance gas (HRG) EOS describes experimental data well at low colliding energies, even below the Λ production threshold in nucleon-nucleon collisions, suggesting that the polarization mechanism may still be thermal vorticity.

## Method

The authors used the SMASH (Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons) transport model, a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck-type model solving the relativistic hadronic Boltzmann equation, to simulate Au+Au collisions at beam energies from √sNN = 2.3 to 7.7 GeV. They extracted the thermal vorticity field via a coarse-graining method on spatial cells of 1 fm size, then computed the Λ hyperon polarization using the modified Cooper-Frye formula with three different equations of state: the baryon-free HotQCD EOS, the finite-baryon-density NEOS-BQS EOS, and the purely hadronic HRG EOS.

## Key numbers

- At √sNN = 7.7 GeV, all three EOS yield consistent global polarization values that agree with experimental data from STAR and HADES.
- At √sNN = 3 GeV in Au+Au collisions (20-50% centrality), the HRG EOS reproduces experimental data well for polarization as a function of centrality, rapidity, and transverse momentum.
- The simulations indicate a possible peak in global Λ polarization around √sNN = 2.4 GeV in Au+Au collisions when using the HRG EOS.
- The global polarization at √sNN = 2.3 GeV is slightly smaller than at √sNN = 2.4 GeV with the HRG EOS.
- The energy-weighted maximum thermal vorticity component ⟨ϖzx⟩max at midrapidity (η = 0) and impact parameter b = 7 fm shows a peak around √sNN = 2.4 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of global Λ polarization in heavy-ion collisions, which had found good agreement between model calculations and experimental data at medium and high collision energies (STAR 2017, STAR 2021, HADES 2022) but poor agreement at low energies using kinetic theory models (Guo 2021). The study also builds on the suggestion from Ivanov (2020) that global polarization in low-energy collisions may depend on the EOS, and on earlier transport model studies by Deng (2020, 2021) that found results subject to large uncertainties. The finding that the HRG EOS describes the data well enables future work to develop a more self-consistent framework incorporating transport models under varying equations of state, and suggests that thermal vorticity remains the underlying mechanism even at very low collision energies.
