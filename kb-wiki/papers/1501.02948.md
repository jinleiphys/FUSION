# Corrected Analytical Solution of the Generalized Woods-Saxon Potential for Arbitrary $\ell$ States

> The bound state solution of the radial Schrödinger equation with the generalized Woods-Saxon potential is carefully examined by using the Pekeris approximation for arbitrary $\ell$ states. The energy eigenvalues and the corresponding eigenfunctions are analytically obtained for different $n$ and $\ell$ quantum numbers. The obtained closed forms are applied to calculate the single particle energy levels of neutron orbiting around $^{56}$Fe nucleus in order to check consistency between the analytical and Gamow code results. The analytical results are in good agreement with the results obtained by Gamow code for $\ell=0$.

## Key claim
The authors derive a corrected analytical eigenvalue equation for the generalized Woods-Saxon potential with arbitrary orbital angular momentum \(\ell\), showing that previous analytical methods (such as the Nikiforov-Uvarov method) fail to incorporate the correct boundary conditions near \(r=R\). The corrected results agree well with numerical Gamow code calculations for \(\ell=0\), but the Pekeris approximation is valid only for small values of the parameter \(\alpha = R/a\).

## Method
The radial Schrödinger equation with the generalized Woods-Saxon potential (including a surface term) is solved by applying the Pekeris approximation to the centrifugal barrier term, expanding it in exponentials up to second order. The resulting differential equation is transformed using \(z = 1/(1+e^{(r-R)/a})\) and solved in terms of hypergeometric functions. The correct boundary conditions at \(r \to 0\) (\(z \to 1\)) and \(r \to \infty\) (\(z \to 0\)) are imposed using the analytic continuation formula for the hypergeometric function, yielding a transcendental eigenvalue equation involving gamma functions and an argument condition.

## Key numbers
- For neutron orbiting \(^{56}\)Fe with \(V_0 = 47.78\) MeV, \(R = 4.9162\) fm, \(a = 0.6\) fm, and \(W_0 = 0\): the corrected analytical eigenvalue equation gives bound-state energies that agree with Gamow code results for \(\ell=0\) (specific numerical energies are not listed in the truncated text, but the agreement is stated).
- For \(n_r = 3, W_0 = 0\), the equation yields \(E_{n_r\ell} = 62.9775\) MeV, which is noted as physically unacceptable (unbound state, mathematical artifact).
- The approximation \(1+e^{R/a} \approx e^{R/a}\) is used, introducing small errors in the eigenvalues.

## Context
This work corrects prior analytical treatments of the generalized Woods-Saxon potential, specifically those by Berkdemir (2008) and Badalov et al., which used the Nikiforov-Uvarov method and produced results inconsistent with numerical Gamow code calculations (as noted by an editor). The corrected eigenvalue equation resolves discrepancies for \(\ell=0\) and extends the solution to arbitrary \(\ell\) states via the Pekeris approximation. This enables more reliable analytical calculations of single-particle energy levels in nuclear structure, though resonant (quasi-bound) states require further treatment via the Complex Scaling Method.
