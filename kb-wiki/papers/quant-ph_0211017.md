# Phase space localization of antisymmetric functions

> Upper and lower bounds are written down for the minimum information entropy in phase space of an antisymmetric wave function in any number of dimensions. Similar bounds are given when the wave function is restricted to belong to any of the proper subspaces of the Fourier transform operator.

# Phase space localization of antisymmetric functions

## Key claim

The paper derives upper and lower bounds for the minimum phase-space information entropy of antisymmetric wave functions in any number of dimensions, and extends these bounds to wave functions restricted to any of the four proper subspaces of the Fourier transform operator (eigenvalues ±1, ±i). The bounds are not proven to be sharp; rather, the author provides "best guess" minimizing sequences based on regularized distributions of Dirac deltas, with strong numerical evidence in one dimension but no proof of optimality.

## Method

The author constructs antisymmetric wave functions as regularized arrays of narrow Gaussian functions located at equally spaced points, with amplitudes modulated by a wide Gaussian envelope, taking the limit as the width parameter a → 0⁺. For the Fourier transform subspaces, the method projects single distributions φ(x, r, α, β) (composed of periodic series of Dirac deltas) onto the λ = -1 and λ = +i subspaces, then regularizes with Gaussian functions at both small and large scales to compute the phase-space entropy. The entropy is shown to be additive over the regularization functions and the mixing entropy of the delta series.

## Key numbers

- For the one-dimensional antisymmetric case, the best guess yields S = 2(1 - log 2) ≈ 0.613706, compared to the first excited harmonic oscillator state which gives S = -1 + log 2 + 2γ ≈ 0.847579 (where γ is Euler's constant)
- The general d-dimensional antisymmetric bound satisfies (d+1)(1 - log 2) ≥ C_d ≥ d(1 - log 2)
- For the λ = -1 Fourier subspace, the best guess entropy is S = 2 + √2 log(√2 - 1) ≈ 0.753550
- For the λ = +i Fourier subspace, the best guess entropy is S = 2 - (2/√3) log(√3 + 1) ≈ 0.839465
- The lowest harmonic oscillator states give S = 1.15934 for λ = -1 and S = 1.38155 for λ = +i

## Context

This work extends the entropic uncertainty principle of Hirschman (1957) and Bialynicki-Birula (1975), which gives the sharp lower bound S(ψ) ≥ d(1 - log 2) for unrestricted wave functions, to the antisymmetric case and to Fourier transform eigen-subspaces. The author's previous work (Salcedo, 1998) conjectured the one-dimensional antisymmetric bound based on numerical evidence and proposed the minimizing sequence ψ₀. The present paper generalizes this approach to arbitrary dimensions and to the λ = -1 and λ = +i subspaces, providing explicit candidate minimizers that significantly outperform the naive harmonic oscillator eigenstates. The results also yield bounds on the norm of the Fourier transform operator restricted to these subspaces.
