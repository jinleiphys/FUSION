# Chiral transition in a Non-Abelian Quasi-Particle Model with three quark flavours

> We combine the recently introduced Non-Abelian Quasi-Particle Model (NAQPM) for gluons with an ideal Fermi gas of three quark species with the aim to describe the equation of state (energy density vs. temperature) of $2+1$ - flavour Lattice-QCD at zero chemical potential. Allowing temperature dependent masses for the fermions, we show that above a critical temperature $T_c$ the quark mass has to drop rapidly in order to obtain energy density values compatible with the Lattice-QCD results. Within this framework, thus, the restoration of chiral symmetry in the system is observed. Furthermore, we demonstrate that the gluon variance -- which is a fundamental quantity of the NAQPM -- is strongly correlated to the fermion mass and decreases by orders of magnitude through the transition. The high temperature phenomenological characteristics of the gluon appear consistent to properties of the perturbative QCD gluon. The model indicates that color deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration are interrelated and classical configurations of the QCD dynamics play an important role to the criticality of the system.

## Key claim

The Extended Non-Abelian Quasi-Particle Model (ENAQPM) can describe the 2+1-flavor Lattice-QCD energy density data only if the pure non-abelian gluon parameters from the earlier NAQPM are abandoned above 230 MeV; the model reveals that chiral symmetry restoration and color deconfinement are interrelated through a strong correlation between the quark mass and the gluon mass variance, with the latter decreasing by orders of magnitude through the transition.

## Method

The authors combined the Non-Abelian Quasi-Particle Model (NAQPM) for gluons, which uses quasi-Gaussian mass distributions for transverse and longitudinal gluon modes with temperature-dependent most probable masses and a common variance, with an ideal Fermi gas of three quark flavors with temperature-dependent masses. They performed a sequential Monte-Carlo fitting procedure to match the total energy density of the model to 2+1-flavor Lattice-QCD data from Karsch (2014) in the temperature range 155-400 MeV, first fitting the gluon variance at high temperatures with fixed small quark mass, then fitting the quark mass at lower temperatures with fixed gluon variance, and finally exploring power-law relations between the two parameters.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the transition is approximately 155 MeV, estimated from specific heat analysis of the Lattice-QCD data.
- The quark mass at the critical temperature T_c is approximately 490 MeV for the three-flavor ENAQPM.
- The gluon variance sigma at 230 MeV reaches a value of approximately 3 x 10^6 (in units consistent with the model) when fitting the high-temperature branch.
- The Boltzmann limit for the ENAQPM is approximately 19.4, higher than the standard value of approximately 15.6 for eight massless non-interacting gluons.
- The peak of the trace anomaly is determined at approximately 204 MeV from the Lattice-QCD data.
- The fermionic contribution saturates to the massless free fermion limit of approximately 10.36 (3 x 3 x 7pi^2/60) above 230 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Non-Abelian Quasi-Particle Model previously introduced by Politis (2016) for pure SU(3) Yang-Mills theory, which successfully reproduced the SU(3)-Lattice equation of state from Boyd (1996) and Borsanyi (2012). The present model adds three quark flavors to describe the full QCD equation of state from Karsch (2014) and Borsanyi (2014) for 2+1 flavors at zero chemical potential. The results enable a unified description of the hadronic and quark-gluon plasma regimes through a single parameter relation connecting the fermion mass and gluon variance, suggesting that classical non-abelian plane-wave solutions play an important role in the critical behavior of the system.
