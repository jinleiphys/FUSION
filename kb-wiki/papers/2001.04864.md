# Open string QED meson description of the X17 particle and dark matter

> As a quark and an antiquark cannot be isolated, the intrinsic motion of a composite $q \bar q$ system in its lowest-energy states lies predominantly in 1+1 dimensions, as in an open string with the quark and the antiquark at its two ends. Accordingly, we study the lowest-energy states of an open string $q\bar q$ system in QCD and QED in 1+1 dimensions. We show that $π^0, η$, and $η'$ can be adequately described as open string $q\bar q$ QCD mesons. By extrapolating into the $q\bar q$ QED sector in which a quark and an antiquark interact with the QED interaction, we find an open string isoscalar $I(J^π)$=$0(0^-)$ QED meson state at 17.9$\pm$1.5 MeV and an isovector $(I(J^π)$=$1(0^-), I_3$=0) QED meson state at 36.4$\pm$3.8 MeV. The predicted masses of the isoscalar and isovector QED mesons are close to the masses of the hypothetical X17 and E38 particles observed recently, making them good candidates for these particles. The decay products of QED mesons may show up as excess $e^+e^-$ and $γγ$ pairs in the anomalous soft photon phenomenon associated with hadron productions in high-energy hadron-proton collisions and $e^+$-$e^-$ annihilations. Measurements of the invariant masses of excess $e^+e^-$ and $γγ$ pairs will provide tests for the existence of the open string $q\bar q$ QED mesons. An assembly of gravitating QED mesons are expected to emit electron-positron pairs and/or gamma rays and their decay energies and lifetimes will be modified by their gravitational binding energies. Consequently, a self-gravitating isoscalar QED meson assembly whose mass $M$ and radius $R$ satisfy $(M/M_\odot)/(R/R_\odot) \gtrsim 4.71 \times 10^5$ will not produce electron-positron pairs nor gamma rays and may be a good candidate for the primordial dark matter.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the X17 particle (observed at ~17 MeV) and the E38 particle (observed at ~38 MeV) can be described as open-string QED mesons, bound states of a light quark and antiquark interacting via QED in 1+1 dimensions, provided the truncation from 3+1 to 1+1 dimensions is approximately valid and the flux tube structure is encoded in the coupling constant.

## Method

The author truncates QCD and QED gauge field theories from 3+1 to 1+1 dimensions by idealizing the quark-antiquark flux tube as a structureless one-dimensional open string, with the transverse flux tube radius stored in the effective 1+1 coupling constant. Using non-Abelian bosonization of the U(3) gauge group (SU(3) for QCD and U(1) for QED), the Hamiltonian density is expressed in terms of boson fields, and the squared masses of bound states are obtained from the quadratic expansion of the potential energy. The physical flavor eigenstates (π⁰, η, η′) are constructed via orthogonal transformations of the flavor components, and the mass formula includes both a confining interaction term and a quark-mass/condensate term.

## Key numbers

* Predicted mass of the isoscalar I(J^π)=0(0⁻) QED meson: 17.9 ± 1.5 MeV.
* Predicted mass of the isovector I(J^π)=1(0⁻), I₃=0 QED meson: 36.4 ± 3.8 MeV.
* Estimated QCD meson mass scale from coupling constants and flux tube radius: ~431 MeV.
* Estimated QED meson mass scale from coupling constants and flux tube radius: ~47 MeV.
* Condition for a self-gravitating isoscalar QED meson assembly to not produce e⁺e⁻ pairs or gamma rays: (M/M_⊙)/(R/R_⊙) ≳ 4.71 × 10⁵.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier proposal (Wong 2010) that quark-antiquark QED bound states (QED mesons) could explain anomalous soft photon phenomena. It provides a more detailed theoretical framework using non-Abelian bosonization and three-flavor QCD to describe π⁰, η, and η′ as open-string QCD mesons, lending support for extrapolation to the QED sector. The predicted masses are close to those of the hypothetical X17 particle observed in ⁸Be and ⁴He decays (Krasznahorkay 2016, 2019) and the E38 boson seen in γγ invariant mass spectra in pC, dC, dCu reactions (Abrashyan 2012, 2019). The work enables experimental tests via measurements of e⁺e⁻ and γγ invariant masses in high-energy collisions and suggests that gravitating assemblies of QED mesons could be dark matter candidates.
