# Transport model study of the $m_T$-scaling for $Λ$, K, and $π$ HBT-correlations

> Based on the microscopic transport model UrQMD in which hadronic and string degrees of freedom are employed, the HBT parameters in the longitudinal co-moving system are investigated for charged pion and kaon, and $Λ$ sources in heavy ion collisions (HICs) at SPS and RHIC energies. In the Cascade mode, $R_O$ and the $R_L$ at high SPS and RHIC energies do not follow the $m_T$-scaling, however, after considering a soft equation of state with momentum dependence (SM-EoS) for formed baryons and a density-dependent Skyrme-like potential for ``pre-formed'' particles, the HBT radii of pions and kaons and even those of $Λ$s with large transverse momenta follow the $m_T$-scaling function $R=3/\sqrt{m_T}$ fairly well.

## Key claim

The HBT radii of pions, kaons, and Λ baryons with large transverse momenta follow the mT-scaling function R = 3/√mT fairly well, but only after including a density-dependent Skyrme-like potential for "pre-formed" particles from string fragmentation in addition to a soft equation of state with momentum dependence for formed baryons; in the cascade mode without these potentials, the scaling fails at high SPS and RHIC energies.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD microscopic transport model with hadronic and string degrees of freedom to simulate central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS beam energies of 20 A GeV and 158 A GeV, and central Au+Au collisions at RHIC √sNN = 200 GeV. They calculated HBT correlation functions in the longitudinally co-moving system using the CRAB v3.0β afterburner, fitting the correlators with three-dimensional Gaussian forms under the Pratt convention, and extracted the HBT radii RL, RO, and RS as functions of transverse mass mT for π−, K+, and Λ pairs.

## Key numbers

- The mT-scaling function used for comparison is R = 3/√mT (with R in fm and mT in appropriate units).
- At √sNN = 200 GeV, without "pre-formed" particle potentials, the RL values of Λs are about 1.2 fm above the scaling line.
- At √sNN = 200 GeV, the ratio RO/RS for pion pairs at 250 < kT < 350 MeV/c increases from ~1.0 to ~1.5 by t ~ 25 fm/c in cascade mode, but stays at ~1 ± 0.05 when "pre-formed" hadron potentials are included.
- The one-dimensional invariant correlation function for pions and Λs at √sNN = 200 GeV was binned with Δqinv = 5 MeV/c for pions and kaons, and Δqinv = 10 MeV/c for Λs, with an upper limit of qinv = 120 MeV/c.
- The Coulomb correction factor for kaon-kaon pairs alters the correlator up to qinv ~ 60 MeV/c, and reduces the λ factor by ~0.3.

## Context

This work extends previous HBT studies that identified the "t-puzzle" (excessively large RO/RS ratios in hydrodynamic and transport cascade calculations compared to data) and the lack of an obvious peak/valley in HBT quantities across beam energies from SIS to RHIC as predicted by Rischke (1996). The authors build on their earlier work (Li 2007) where the "pre-formed" hadron potential was introduced to solve the pion HBT time-puzzle, and here demonstrate that the same mechanism also restores mT-scaling for kaons and Λ baryons. The results enable further transport-model analysis of three-dimensional source imaging methods (Chung 2007, Danielewicz 2006) and motivate investigation of nuclear potential modifications in final-state interactions for baryon-baryon correlations.
