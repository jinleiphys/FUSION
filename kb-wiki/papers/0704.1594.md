# Cooper pairs in atomic nuclei

> We consider the development of Cooper pairs in a self-consistent Hartree Fock mean field for the even Sm isotopes. Results are presented at the level of a BCS treatment, a number-projected BCS treatment and an exact treatment using the Richardson ansatz. While projected BCS captures much of the pairing correlation energy that is absent from BCS, it still misses a sizable correlation energy, typically of order $1 MeV$. Furthermore, because it does not average over the properties of the fermion pairs, the exact Richardson solution permits a more meaningful definition of the Cooper wave function and of the fraction of pairs that are collective.

## Key claim

While number-projected BCS (PBCS) captures much of the pairing correlation energy that is absent from ordinary BCS, it still misses a sizable correlation energy (typically of order 1 MeV) compared to the exact Richardson solution; furthermore, the exact Richardson solution permits a more meaningful definition of the Cooper pair wave function and of the fraction of pairs that are collective, because it does not average over the properties of individual fermion pairs.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent deformed Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations using the Skyrme SLy4 force for the even Sm isotopes (144-158), with pairing treated via a constant-strength pairing force. They then used the HF mean field from these calculations to compare three treatments of pairing: standard BCS, number-projected BCS (PBCS), and the exact Richardson ansatz. The Richardson equations were solved numerically by starting at strong coupling (G = 1 MeV) with complex single-particle energies to avoid singularities, then evolving to the physical G value and letting the imaginary parts go to zero.

## Key numbers

* Pairing correlation energies for 154Sm: exact = -3.678 MeV, PBCS = -2.859 MeV, BCS = -0.390 MeV.
* Pairing correlation energy for 144Sm: exact = -2.960 MeV, PBCS = -2.677 MeV, BCS = 0.0 MeV (normal solution).
* The pairing strengths fitted to 154Sm were G_n = 0.106 MeV (neutrons) and G_p = 0.117 MeV (protons), with experimental gaps Delta_n = 0.98 MeV and Delta_p = 0.94 MeV.
* The mean level spacing between Hartree-Fock single-particle levels in 154Sm is roughly 0.5 MeV.
* For 154Sm, the BEC limit (chemical potential crossing the lowest single-particle energy) occurs at G = 0.788 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of Cooper pairing in finite nuclei by comparing mean-field treatments (BCS and PBCS) with the exact Richardson solution, following the approach of Ortiz (2005) who showed that the Richardson solution gives a precise definition of the Cooper pair wave function in superconducting media. The results are consistent with studies of ultrasmall superconducting grains by von Delft (2001) and others, showing that the quantum phase transition predicted by BCS and PBCS disappears when pairing fluctuations are fully included. The work enables more precise treatment of pair correlations in realistic nuclear systems, with implications for microscopic calculations of nuclear masses as pursued by Pearson (2006) and collaborators.
