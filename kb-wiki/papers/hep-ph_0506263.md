# Path-integral approach in a chiral quark-diquark model to the nucleon structure and interactions

> We study the structure of the nuclear force by using a path-integral hadronization approach in a chiral quark-diquark model. After the construction of the chiral quark-diquark model, we hadronize it to obtain a meson-baryon Lagrangian. The effective meson-baryon Lagrangian incorporates chiral symmetry and the composite description of the mesons and baryons. Using the effective meson-baryon Lagrangian we investigate the structure of the nuclear force in the simple case of neglecting the axial-vector diquark. It is shown that the meson-baryon Lagrangian contains two kinds of the nuclear force; the meson-exchange interaction and a quark-diquark loop interaction. It is also shown that the quark-diquark loop interaction consists of the scalar and vector interactions. The properties of these interactions are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the short-range nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction can be derived from a quark-diquark loop diagram in a chiral quark-diquark model, but the authors explicitly state that the current approach (neglecting the axial-vector diquark) is "not satisfactory" because the short-range part of the nuclear force must be repulsive, whereas their scalar-only calculation does not produce this repulsion.

## Method

The authors start from a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) Lagrangian in the chiral limit, introduce scalar diquark fields, and perform a path-integral hadronization to obtain an effective meson-baryon Lagrangian expressed as a trace-log formula. They then expand the trace-log to extract the NN interaction amplitude from a one-loop quark-diquark diagram, and evaluate the scalar and vector components of this amplitude in the local potential approximation by expanding around zero total momentum (P=0). Numerical calculations are performed using Pauli-Villars regularization with a cutoff mass of 0.63 GeV, with parameters fixed to generate the constituent quark mass and pion decay constant through the NJL gap equation.

## Key numbers

* Constituent quark mass: m_q = 0.39 GeV (used in both parameter sets)
* Scalar diquark mass: M_S = 0.60 GeV (used in both parameter sets)
* Pauli-Villars cutoff mass: Λ_PV = 0.63 GeV (used in both parameter sets)
* Set I: nucleon mass M_N = 0.94 GeV, nucleon charge radius ⟨r²⟩^{1/2} = 0.77 fm, quark-diquark coupling G̃ = 271.0 GeV^{-1}
* Set II: nucleon mass M_N = 0.85 GeV, nucleon charge radius ⟨r²⟩^{1/2} = 0.54 fm, quark-diquark coupling G̃ = 445.9 GeV^{-1}
* For set I (nucleon size 0.77 fm): scalar interaction mass parameter m_S ≈ 650 MeV, vector interaction mass parameter m_V ≈ 800 MeV
* For set II (nucleon size ~0.5 fm): scalar interaction mass parameter m_S ≈ 800 MeV, vector interaction mass parameter m_V ≈ 1000 MeV
* Empirical comparison values: scalar coupling g_S ~ 10, vector coupling g_V ~ 13 (from Machleidt)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous path-integral hadronization approach (Nagata 2003) by providing detailed numerical analysis of the short-range NN interaction from quark-diquark loops. The approach contrasts with conventional boson-exchange models (Lacombe, Machleidt) and quark-exchange models (Oka, Takeuchi, Fujiwara 2001) by incorporating both chiral symmetry and the internal structure of hadrons within a single framework. The results enable future inclusion of the axial-vector diquark, which the authors state is necessary to obtain the correct short-range repulsion, and suggest that the scalar and vector interaction ranges from the quark-diquark loop naturally produce mass parameters close to those of the sigma and omega mesons.
