# Restoration of chiral symmetry in light-front finite temperature field theory

> We investigate the properties of $qq$ and $q\bar q$ states in hot and dense quark matter in the framework of light-front finite temperature field theory. Presently we use the Nambu Jona-Lasinio model of QCD and derive the gap equation at finite temperature and density. We study pionic and scalar diquark dynamics in quark matter and calculate the masses and the Mott dissociation as a function of the temperature $T$ and the chemical potential $μ$. For the scalar diquark we determine the critical temperature of color superconductivity.

# Restoration of chiral symmetry in light-front finite temperature field theory

## Key claim
The Nambu Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, when quantized on the light-front, reproduces the well-known features of chiral symmetry restoration, pion dissociation, and color superconductivity at finite temperature and chemical potential, but the diquark coupling constant must be treated as a free parameter to obtain a reasonably high diquark mass at zero temperature and density.

## Method
The authors derived the in-medium gap equation for the NJL model using light-front quantization at finite temperature and chemical potential, employing the Hartree approximation for the quark self-energy. They then solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the T-matrix in the pseudoscalar (pion) and scalar diquark channels, using the Lepage-Brodsky cut-off scheme to regulate divergences. The critical temperature for color superconductivity was determined via the Thouless criterion, which equates the diquark mass to twice the chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- The pion mass and diquark mass were calculated as functions of temperature T for different values of chemical potential μ, with the continuum threshold given by 2m (where m is the in-medium quark mass).
- The critical temperature of color superconductivity was determined by solving the condition m_d(T, μ) = 2μ, where m_d is the diquark mass.
- The phase diagram shows the chiral phase transition line (solid line), the transition between deconfined and color superconducting phases (dotted line), and the Mott dissociation line of the pion (dashed line) in the T-μ plane.

## Context
This work extends the instant-form NJL model studies reviewed by Klevansky (1992) to the light-front quantization formalism introduced by Dirac (1949). The approach follows previous light-front finite-temperature work by Beyer (2001) and uses the Bethe-Salpeter T-matrix method of Ishii (1995). The results enable exploration of QCD at finite temperatures and particularly at larger densities (μ > T), where lattice QCD faces severe technical problems, and are relevant for understanding the early universe and neutron star interiors.
