# Conventional BCS, Unconventional BCS, and Non-BCS Hidden Dineutron Phases in Neutron Matter

> The nature of pairing correlations in neutron matter is re-examined. Working within the conventional approximation in which the $nn$ pairing interaction is provided by a realistic bare $nn$ potential fitted to scattering data, it is demonstrated that the standard BCS theory fails in regions of neutron number density where the pairing constant $λ$, depending crucially on density, has a non-BCS negative sign. We are led to propose a non-BCS scenario for pairing phenomena in neutron matter that involves the formation of a hidden dineutron state. In low-density neutron matter where the pairing constant has the standard BCS sign, two phases organized by pairing correlations are possible and compete energetically: a conventional BCS phase and a dineutron phase. In dense neutron matter, where $λ$ changes sign, only the dineutron phase survives and exists until the critical density for termination of pairing correlations is reached at approximately twice the neutron density in heavy atomic nuclei.

## Key claim

The standard BCS theory fails in neutron matter at densities where the pairing constant λ has a non-BCS negative sign, leading the authors to propose a non-BCS scenario involving a hidden dineutron state; however, this conclusion depends on the choice of the bare nn interaction potential, as the sign of λ varies with both density and the specific potential model used.

## Method

The authors analyze the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the Cooper channel scattering amplitude in neutron matter, using a decomposition of the pairing interaction into a separable part and a remainder to isolate the Cooper singularity. They then derive a dispersion equation whose solutions determine the existence and nature of pairing instabilities, characterized by the pairing constant λ and a stiffness coefficient κ. The analysis is performed for realistic bare nn potentials (Reid soft-core and Argonne V18) fitted to scattering data, and extended to finite temperature using the Thouless criterion.

## Key numbers

- The critical density for termination of pairing correlations is approximately twice the neutron density in heavy atomic nuclei, corresponding to a Fermi momentum pFt ≃ 1.74 fm⁻¹ (for the RSC potential).
- The BCS gap Δ₀ reaches a maximum close to 3 MeV at pF ≈ 0.85 fm⁻¹ for the RSC potential.
- The pairing constant λ changes sign at pF ≃ 0.8 fm⁻¹ for the Argonne V14 and V18 potentials.
- The dineutron state exists until the density reaches ρt ≃ 2ρ₀ ≃ 0.16 fm⁻³.
- The neutron-neutron scattering resonance lies at 0.067 MeV.
- In the quadrant (λ>0, κ<0), the non-BCS critical temperature T* (λ→0) = √(|κ|/γ) has no exponential smallness, unlike the BCS critical temperature Tc ∝ e^{-1/λ}.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of pairing in neutron matter beyond standard BCS theory, which has been the dominant framework since Migdal first proposed neutron star superfluidity. The authors build on prior studies of the BCS gap equation for realistic nn potentials (Khodel et al., 1997; KKC) and draw an analogy to the pseudogap phase observed in high-Tc superconductors via ARPES (Timusk, 1999; Shen, 2005). The paper introduces a competition between conventional BCS pairing and a dineutron phase analogous to the Shafroth-Butler-Blatt-Alexandrov (SBBA) bipolaronic scenario, enabling future work on the phase diagram of neutron matter and its implications for neutron star cooling.
