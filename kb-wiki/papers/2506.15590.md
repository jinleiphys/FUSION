# Extended multiconfigurational dynamical symmetry

> An extended multiconfigurational dynamical symmetry (EMUSY) within the symplectic symmetry approach to clustering (SSAC) is proposed for the general case of multicluster nuclear systems. A characteristic property of the EMUSY is that it includes more general symplectic, i.e. number non-preserving, transformations which contain the standard number-preserving (unitary) multiconfigurational dynamical symmetry transformations as a special limiting case. In this way the EMUSY becomes able to connect various possible clusterizations of different multicluster type, as well as various many-particle configurations between the shell, collective and cluster models of nuclear structure. The theory is briefly illustrated using the nuclear system $^{24}$Mg as an example.

# Extended multiconfigurational dynamical symmetry (EMUSY)

## Key claim

The paper proposes an extended multiconfigurational dynamical symmetry (EMUSY) within the symplectic symmetry approach to clustering (SSAC) that, unlike the original MUSY, can connect clusterizations of different multicluster type (not just binary clusterizations) by including number-non-preserving symplectic transformations, with the standard number-preserving unitary MUSY transformations appearing as a special limiting case.

## Method

The author embeds the group chain $Sp(6(A-1),R) \supset Sp(6(k-1),R)_R \otimes Sp(6(A-k),R)_C \supset Sp(6,R)_R \otimes Sp(6,R)_C \otimes O(A-k)$ to define the dynamical symmetry, then constructs tensor operators from the symplectic generators $G^R_2$ and $F^C_2$ (and their multilinear products) that shift oscillator quanta between the relative-motion ($R$) and cluster-internal ($C$) subsystems. These tensor operators, belonging to the enveloping algebra of $Sp(6,R)_R \otimes Sp(6,R)_C$, explicitly connect different cluster configurations while preserving the underlying $SU(3)$ dynamical symmetry. The method is illustrated for $^{24}$Mg by showing how tensor operators transform between six different cluster channels.

## Key numbers

- For $^{24}$Mg, the leading $SU(3)$ multiplet is $(8,4)$ with total oscillator quanta $E = 28$.
- The $^{20}$Ne + $\alpha$ channel has $E_R = 8$ oscillator quanta and multiplet structure $(8,0) \otimes (8,0)$.
- The $^{16}$O + $^{8}$Be channel has $E_R = 12$ and multiplet structure $(12,0) \otimes (4,0)$.
- The $^{12}$C + $\alpha + \alpha + \alpha$ channel has $E_R = 20$ and multiplet structure $(20,0) \otimes (0,4)$.
- The $^{12}$C + $^{12}$C channel has $E_R = 12$ and multiplet structure $(12,0) \otimes (0,8)$.
- The $6\alpha$ channel has $E_R = 28$ and multiplet structure $(8,4) \otimes (0,0)$.
- The tensor operator connecting $^{16}$O + $^{8}$Be to $^{20}$Ne + $\alpha$ shifts 4 oscillator quanta from $R$- to $C$-subsystem while preserving $E=28$.
- The tensor operator connecting $^{12}$C + $\alpha + \alpha + \alpha$ to $^{12}$C + $^{12}$C shifts 8 oscillator quanta from $R$- to $C$-subsystem.

## Context

This work extends the multichannel dynamical symmetry (MUSY) introduced by Cseh (1994) and later termed multiconfigurational dynamical symmetry, which previously connected only binary clusterizations via number-preserving unitary transformations (Talmi-Moshinsky transformations). The EMUSY provides a rigorous mathematical foundation within the SSAC framework previously developed for two-cluster (Ganev, 2014) and multicluster (Ganev, 2020) systems, and relates to the earlier work of Wildermuth and Kanellopoulos (1958) on cluster-shell equivalence, Elliott (1958) on collective-shell relations, and the Bayman-Bohr theorem (1958). The theory enables explicit construction of connecting transformations between arbitrary clusterizations of multicluster nuclear systems and unifies the shell, collective, and cluster model descriptions within a single symplectic algebraic framework.
