# Cluster structures of the $0^+$ states of $^{13}_Λ{\rm C}$ with the 3$α+Λ$ four-body orthogonality condition model

> We study structures of the $0^+$ states of $^{13}_Λ$C within the framework of $α+α+α+Λ$ four-body orthogonality condition model with the Gaussian expansion method. We obtain five $0^+$ states of $^{13}_Λ$C and compare them with the low-lying four $0^+$ states of $^{12}$C. We find that the $0_1^+$, $0_2^+$ and $0_4^+$ states correspond to the $0_1^+$, $0_2^+$ and $0_4^+$ states of $^{12}$C, respectively. We also find that both the $0_3^+$ and $0_5^+$ states have analogous nature to the $0_3^+$ state of $^{12}$C, where the additional $Λ$ particle couples with $^8$Be cluster and one $α$ cluster, in the $0_3^+$ state of $^{12}$C, respectively. The former is then shown to have an elongated $^9_Λ$Be$(0_1^+) + α$ cluster structure with a higher nodal behaviour for their relative wave function, and the latter have a dilute three-body gas consisting of $2α+^5_Λ$He clusters.

## Key claim
The paper claims that five \(0^+\) states of \(^{13}_\Lambda\)C can be identified within the \(3\alpha+\Lambda\) four-body orthogonality condition model, but the correspondence to the four \(0^+\) states of \(^{12}\)C is not one-to-one: the \(0_3^+\) and \(0_5^+\) states of \(^{13}_\Lambda\)C both have analogous natures to the single \(0_3^+\) state of \(^{12}\)C, with the additional \(\Lambda\) particle coupling differently in each case.

## Method
The authors solved the four-body Schrödinger equation for the \(\alpha+\alpha+\alpha+\Lambda\) system using the orthogonality condition model (OCM) with the Gaussian expansion method. They employed a modified Hasegawa-Nagata effective nucleon-nucleon potential for the \(\alpha\)-\(\alpha\) interaction and a modified YNG-NF \(\Lambda\)N interaction, tuning the odd-state component to reproduce the binding energies of \(^5_\Lambda\)He, \(^9_\Lambda\)Be, and the ground state of \(^{13}_\Lambda\)C. The Pauli principle between \(\alpha\) clusters was enforced via a Pauli exclusion operator, and a phenomenological three-body repulsive force between the three \(\alpha\) clusters was introduced to reproduce the binding energy of the ground state of \(^{12}\)C.

## Key numbers
- The calculated binding energy of the \(^{13}_\Lambda\)C ground state (\(0_1^+\)) is \(E = -19.15\) MeV relative to the \(3\alpha+\Lambda\) threshold, with a \(\Lambda\) separation energy \(B_\Lambda = 11.86\) MeV (experimental: \(E = -18.65 \pm 0.05\) MeV, \(B_\Lambda = 11.38 \pm 0.05\) MeV).
- The \(0_2^+\), \(0_3^+\), \(0_4^+\), and \(0_5^+\) states of \(^{13}_\Lambda\)C have binding energies \(E = -7.49\) MeV, \(-5.0\) MeV, \(-3.5\) MeV, and \(-1.2\) MeV, respectively, measured from the \(3\alpha+\Lambda\) threshold.
- The root-mean-square (rms) radii of the \(^{13}_\Lambda\)C states are: \(0_1^+\): 2.3 fm, \(0_2^+\): 3.1 fm, \(0_3^+\): 5.4 fm, \(0_4^+\): 4.1 fm, \(0_5^+\): 5.6 fm; for comparison, the \(^{12}\)C rms radii are: \(0_1^+\): 2.4 fm, \(0_2^+\): 4.2 fm, \(0_3^+\): 5.6 fm, \(0_4^+\): 5.4 fm.
- The \(0_2^+\) (Hoyle) state of \(^{12}\)C is calculated at 0.8 MeV above the \(3\alpha\) threshold (experimental: 0.38 MeV), and the \(0_3^+\) and \(0_4^+\) states of \(^{12}\)C are at 1.8 MeV and 4.9 MeV above the \(3\alpha\) threshold, respectively.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of cluster structures in \(^{12}\)C and \(^{13}_\Lambda\)C, particularly the Hoyle state and its analogs in hypernuclei. It builds on the OCM calculations of Hiyama et al. (1997) for \(^{13}_\Lambda\)C and the Hyper-THSR wave function calculations of Funaki et al. (2018), which found four \(0^+\) states in \(^{13}_\Lambda\)C. The present calculation obtains five \(0^+\) states and provides a more detailed assignment of their cluster structures, including the identification of the \(0_3^+\) state as an elongated \(^9_\Lambda\)Be\((0_1^+)+\alpha\) structure and the \(0_5^+\) state as a dilute \(2\alpha+^5_\Lambda\)He gas. This enables future experimental searches for these predicted states and further theoretical studies of \(\Lambda\) hypernuclear clustering near the \(3\alpha+\Lambda\) threshold.
