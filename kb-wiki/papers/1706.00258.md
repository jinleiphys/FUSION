# Toward a systematic strategy for defining power counting in the construction of the energy density functional theory

> We propose a new scheme for constructing an effective-field-theory-based interaction to be used in the energy-density-functional (EDF) theory with specific assumptions for defining a power counting. This procedure is developed through the evaluation of the equation of state (EOS) of symmetric and pure neutron matter going beyond the mean-field scheme and using a functional defined up to next-to-leading order (NLO), that we will call NLO EDF. A Skyrme-like interaction is constructed based on the condition of renormalizibility and on a power counting on $k_F/Λ_{hi}$, where $k_F$ is the Fermi momentum and $Λ_{hi}$ is the breakdown scale of our expansion. To absorb the divergences present in beyond mean-field diagrams, counter interactions are introduced for the NLO EDF and determined through renormalization conditions. In particular, three scenarios are explored and all of them lead to satisfactory results. These counter interactions contain also parameters which do not contribute to the EOS of matter and may eventually be determined through future adjustments to properties of some selected finite nuclei. Our work serves as a simple starting point for constructing a well-defined power counting within the EDF framework.

## Key claim

The authors propose a systematic scheme for constructing an effective-field-theory-based Skyrme-like interaction for energy density functional (EDF) theory, where the power counting is defined by an expansion in \(k_F/\Lambda_{hi}\) and counter terms are introduced to absorb divergences from beyond-mean-field diagrams, but the specific form of the NLO effective interaction remains undetermined and the three explored scenarios all yield satisfactory results only for the equation of state of infinite matter, with additional parameters that require future fitting to finite nuclei.

## Method

Starting from a \(t_0-t_3\) Skyrme-like interaction as the leading-order (LO) effective interaction, the authors compute the second-order (NLO) contributions to the equation of state for symmetric and pure neutron matter using many-body perturbation theory with a sharp cutoff \(\Lambda\) on the outgoing relative momentum. They identify three types of divergent terms in the second-order EOS (proportional to \(k_F^3\), \(k_F^{3+3\alpha}\), and \(k_F^{3+6\alpha}\)) and introduce corresponding counter terms \(V_{\rm NLO}^{(a)}\), \(V_{\rm NLO}^{(b)}\), and \(V_{\rm NLO}^{(c)}\) to absorb these divergences, exploring three scenarios: using only \(V_{\rm NLO}^{(c)}\) (scenario c), using \(V_{\rm NLO}^{(b)}\) plus \(V_{\rm NLO}^{(c)}\) (scenario bc), and using all three counter terms (scenario abc). The LO parameters are fitted to the SLy5 mean-field EOS, and the NLO parameters are determined by renormalization conditions to produce cutoff-independent results.

## Key numbers

- LO fit to SLy5 mean-field EOS yields \(\alpha = 0.4\), \(t_0 = -1686\) MeV fm\(^3\), \(t_3 = 12096\) MeV fm\(^{3+3\alpha}\), \(x_0 = 0.2751\), \(x_3 = 0.2530\), with \(\chi^2 = 77\).
- LO saturation density \(\rho_s = 0.162\) fm\(^{-3}\) (same as SLy5), saturation energy \(E(\rho_s)/A = -16.31\) MeV (SLy5: \(-16.18\) MeV), incompressibility \(K_\infty = 254.64\) MeV (SLy5: 232.67 MeV).
- For scenario abc: saturation density \(\rho_s = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), saturation energy \(E(\rho_s)/A = -15.93\) MeV, incompressibility \(K_\infty = 236.32\) MeV.
- For scenario bc: saturation density \(\rho_s = 0.16 \pm 0.003\) fm\(^{-3}\), saturation energy \(E(\rho_s)/A = -15.98 \pm 0.1\) MeV, incompressibility \(K_\infty = 234.3 \pm 3.5\) MeV.
- For scenario c: saturation density \(\rho_s = 0.16 \pm 0.003\) fm\(^{-3}\), saturation energy \(E(\rho_s)/A = -15.97 \pm 0.1\) MeV, incompressibility \(K_\infty = 233.2 \pm 3.7\) MeV.
- The breakdown scale \(\Lambda_{hi}\) is required to be larger than 2.3 fm\(^{-1}\) for neutron matter and 1.8 fm\(^{-1}\) for symmetric matter at the maximum density \(\rho = 0.4\) fm\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Bira (2016) on renormalizability of Skyrme interactions at second order, where no counter terms were added and specific \(\alpha\) values were required; here the authors release that restriction and introduce counter terms to absorb divergences, following the renormalization-group approach used in pionless EFT for dilute neutron matter by Lee (2009), Hammer (2004), and others. The proposed scheme enables a systematic power counting for EDF beyond mean field, with the counter terms containing extra parameters (such as \(v_a, v_b, v_c\)) that do not affect infinite matter EOS but may be determined by future fits to finite nuclei, providing a starting point for constructing well-defined EFT-based nuclear functionals.
