# Transverse momentum and energy correlations in the equilibrium system from high-energy nuclear collisions

> The so-called $Φ$ parameter, which measures the transverse momentum or energy correlations (fluctuations) in high-energy collisions independently of the particle multiplicity, is computed for the equilibrium ideal gas. As expected $Φ$ vanishes for the particles obeying Boltzmann statistics but is finite for the quantum particles, positive for bosons and negative for fermions. $Φ_{p_{\perp}}$, which is found for the pions gas, significantly exceeds the value of $Φ_{p_{\perp}}$ measured by the NA49 experiment. The discrepancy is discussed.

# Transverse momentum and energy correlations in the equilibrium system from high-energy nuclear collisions

## Key claim

The Φ parameter, which measures transverse momentum or energy correlations independently of particle multiplicity, is non-zero for an ideal quantum gas in thermodynamic equilibrium, positive for bosons and negative for fermions, but the computed value for a pion gas at freeze-out temperatures (6.5–15 MeV) drastically exceeds the experimentally measured value from NA49 central Pb–Pb collisions (0.7 ± 0.5 MeV), indicating that the equilibrium calculation alone cannot explain the observed correlations.

## Method

The author computes the correlation measure Φ for transverse momentum (Φ_{p⊥}) and energy (Φ_E) using the grand canonical partition function of an ideal quantum gas, evaluating the single-particle variance and the event-averaged multiparticle variance for bosons, fermions, and mixed systems. Analytical results are derived for massless particles with vanishing chemical potential, and numerical calculations are performed for massive pions (m = 140 MeV) as functions of temperature T and chemical potential μ. The results are compared to the NA49 experimental measurement of Φ_{p⊥} in central Pb–Pb collisions at 158 A·GeV.

## Key numbers

- For massless bosons with vanishing chemical potential: Φ_E ≅ 0.40 T and Φ_{p⊥} ≅ 0.29 T.
- For massless fermions with vanishing chemical potential: Φ_E ≅ −0.07 T and Φ_{p⊥} ≅ −0.05 T.
- For a baryonless two-flavor quark-gluon plasma (g_f = 24, g_b = 16): Φ_E ≅ 0.17 T.
- NA49 experimental result for central Pb–Pb collisions: Φ_{p⊥} = 0.7 ± 0.5 MeV (Roland 1997).
- Computed Φ_{p⊥} for a pion gas at T = 180 MeV and μ = 0: 15 MeV.
- Computed Φ_{p⊥} for a pion gas at T = 120 MeV and μ = 0: 6.5 MeV.
- To reduce Φ_{p⊥} to 0.7 MeV via negative chemical potential requires μ = −245 MeV.
- Φ_{p⊥} from p–p collisions at 158 A·GeV: 4.2 ± 0.5 MeV (Roland 1997, Adler 1998).

## Context

This work extends the earlier introduction of the Φ measure by Gazdzicki (1992) and addresses the observation by Gazdzicki (1997) that equilibrium systems should retain residual correlations. The calculation shows that the equilibrium value of Φ_{p⊥} for a pion gas is close to the p–p value (4.2 MeV) but far exceeds the Pb–Pb value (0.7 MeV), implying that even in p–p collisions the origin of correlations is poorly understood. The author discusses three possible resolutions, negative chemical potential, resonance decay contributions, and Coulomb effects, but finds none individually sufficient, leaving a complete solution for future studies. The random walk model of Leonidov (1997) is ruled out by prior data (Gazdzicki 1997) for producing stronger correlations in A–A than N–N, contrary to experiment.
