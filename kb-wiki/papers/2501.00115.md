# Bayesian analysis of hybrid neutron star EOS constraints within an instantaneous nonlocal chiral quark matter model

> We present a physics-informed Bayesian analysis of equation of state constraints using observational data for masses, radii and tidal deformability of pulsars and a generic class of hybrid neutron star equation of state with color superconducting quark matter on the basis of a recently developed nonlocal chiral quark model. The nuclear matter phase is described within a relativistic density functional model of the DD2 class and the phase transition is obtained by a Maxwell construction. We find the region in the two-dimensional parameter space spanned by the vector meson coupling and the scalar diquark coupling, where three conditions are fulfilled: (1) the Maxwell construction can be performed, \mbox{(2) the maximum} mass of the hybrid neutron star is not smaller than \mbox{2.0 M$_\odot$} and (3) the onset density of the phase transition is not below the nuclear saturation density $n_0=0.15$ fm$^{-3}$. The result of this study shows that the favorable neutron star equation of state has low onset masses for the occurrence of a color superconducting quark matter core between 0.5-0.7 $M_\odot$ and maximum masses in the range 2.15-2.22 $M_\odot$. In the typical mass range of 1.2-2.0 $M_\odot$, the radii of these stars are between 11.9 and 12.4 km, almost independent of the mass. In principle, hybrid stars would allow for larger maximum masses than provided by the hadronic reference equation of state.

## Key claim

The paper finds that a hybrid neutron star equation of state with a color superconducting quark matter core is viable when the vector meson coupling and scalar diquark coupling are tuned such that the Maxwell construction is possible, the maximum mass exceeds 2.0 solar masses, and the phase transition onset density is not below nuclear saturation density; the abstract's claim of "favorable" parameters is hedged by the requirement that all three conditions must be simultaneously satisfied in the two-dimensional parameter space.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian analysis of hybrid neutron star equations of state using a two-phase formalism: the hadronic phase was described by the relativistic density functional DD2 model, and the quark matter phase by an instantaneous nonlocal chiral quark model with color superconductivity (2SC phase). The phase transition between nuclear and quark matter was constructed via a Maxwell construction, and the quark matter equation of state was parametrized in a constant-speed-of-sound (CSS) form with coefficients fitted as quadratic functions of the vector meson coupling (eta_V) and scalar diquark coupling (eta_D). Observational constraints from pulsar masses, radii, and tidal deformability were applied to identify the allowed region in the (eta_V, eta_D) parameter plane.

## Key numbers

* Maximum masses of hybrid neutron stars lie in the range 2.15-2.22 solar masses.
* Onset masses for the color superconducting quark matter core are between 0.5 and 0.7 solar masses.
* For neutron stars in the mass range 1.2-2.0 solar masses, radii are between 11.9 and 12.4 km, nearly independent of mass.
* The nuclear saturation density is taken as n_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}, which sets the lower bound for the phase transition onset density.
* The maximum mass constraint requires hybrid stars to have at least 2.0 solar masses.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Klahn et al. (2013) and Baym et al. (2019) that mapped the allowed parameter region for vector meson and diquark couplings in hybrid neutron star models, but now uses a confining nonlocal chiral quark model (3DFF) rather than the standard Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. The results enable future comparisons with multi-messenger observations from pulsar timing and gravitational wave events like GW170817, and can be used to test whether a strong first-order phase transition is present in neutron star interiors, a question recently addressed by Brandes et al. (2023) who found evidence against such a transition.
