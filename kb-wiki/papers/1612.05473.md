# $η$-$η'$ Mixing in Large-$N_c$ Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We present a calculation of the $η$-$η'$ mixing in the framework of large-$N_c$ chiral perturbation theory. A general expression for the $η$-$η'$ mixing at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) is derived, including higher-derivative terms up to fourth order in the four momentum, kinetic and mass terms. In addition, the axial-vector decay constants of the $η$-$η'$ system are determined at NNLO. The numerical analysis of the results is performed successively at LO, NLO, and NNLO. We investigate the influence of one-loop corrections, OZI-rule-violating parameters, and $\mathcal{O}(N_c p^6)$ contact terms.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general expression for η-η' mixing at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in large-Nc chiral perturbation theory, showing that a single mixing angle is insufficient to describe the mixing and that a two-angle scheme is required when higher-order corrections are included.

## Method

The authors construct the effective Lagrangian for U(3)L × U(3)R chiral perturbation theory organized by a simultaneous expansion in momenta, quark masses, and 1/Nc, with terms up to O(Nc p6). They compute the one-loop self-energies for the η8 and η1 fields, then perform successive field redefinitions to eliminate higher-derivative terms and diagonalize the kinetic matrix, yielding a diagonal Lagrangian for the physical η and η' states. The axial-vector decay constants are also computed at NNLO using the same framework.

## Key numbers

- Leading-order octet mass squared: M̊8² = (2/3)B( m̂ + 2ms ) = (1/3)(4M̊K² − M̊π²)
- Leading-order singlet mass squared: M̊1² = (2/3)B(2m̂ + ms) = (1/3)(2M̊K² + M̊π²)
- U(1)A anomaly contribution to η1 mass squared: M0² = 6τ/F², where τ = O(Nc0)
- Leading-order mixing mass squared: M̊81² = −(2√2/3)B(ms − m̂) = −(2√2/3)(M̊K² − M̊π²)
- Leading-order kaon mass squared: M̊K² = B(m̂ + ms)
- Leading-order pion mass squared: M̊π² = 2B m̂

## Context

This work extends the large-Nc chiral perturbation theory framework developed by Leutwyler (1996), Herrera-Siklody et al. (1996, 1998), and Kaiser (2000) to NNLO for the η-η' system. It improves upon the two-angle mixing scheme of Feldmann (1998) by providing a systematic EFT calculation that includes OZI-rule-violating parameters and O(Nc p6) contact terms. The results enable future determinations of the low-energy constants from lattice QCD or experimental data on η and η' decays, and provide a foundation for calculating transition form factors and other observables at NNLO.
