# Spin-dependent part of $p\bar{p}$ interaction cross section and Nijmegen potential

> Low energy $p\bar{p}$ interaction is considered taking into account the polarization of both particles. The corresponding cross sections are calculated using the Nijmegen nucleon-antinucleon optical potential. Then they are applied to the analysis of the polarization buildup which is due to the interaction of stored antiprotons with polarized protons of a hydrogen target. It is shown that, at realistic parameters of a storage ring and a target, the filtering mechanism may provide a noticeable polarization in a time comparable with the beam lifetime.

## Key claim

Using the Nijmegen nucleon-antinucleon optical potential, the spin-dependent part of the p-bar-p interaction cross section is calculated and applied to the spin-filtering mechanism for stored antiprotons; the authors state that today it is impossible to predict the beam polarization with high accuracy because different models give essentially different predictions, but that the filtering mechanism may provide a noticeable polarization in a time comparable with the beam lifetime at realistic storage ring and target parameters.

## Method

The authors calculate p-bar-p scattering cross sections in the center-of-mass frame using the Nijmegen optical potential, which includes a Coulomb term and a strong interaction term with an imaginary part describing annihilation into mesons. The cross sections are decomposed into spin-independent (sigma_0) and spin-dependent (sigma_1, sigma_2) components via partial-wave expansions for elastic scattering, charge exchange (p-bar-p to n-bar-n), and annihilation channels, including Coulomb-strong interference contributions. These cross sections are then used in a kinetic equation to model the polarization buildup of a stored antiproton beam interacting with a polarized hydrogen target, yielding the beam polarization and lifetime as functions of the target areal density, beam revolving frequency, and acceptance angle.

## Key numbers

- The beam lifetime due to interaction with the target is tau_b = 1/(n f sigma_0), where n is the target areal density and f is the beam revolving frequency.
- The figure of merit FOM(t) = P_B^2(t) N(t) is maximal at t_0 = 2 tau_b, when the number of antiprotons is N(t_0) approximately 0.14 N(0).
- For a target polarization P_T = 1 and acceptance angle theta_acc^(l) = 10 mrad, the polarization degree P_perp (for zeta_T dot v = 0) becomes independent of antiproton energy at T_lab about 100 MeV; P_parallel (for |zeta_T dot v| = 1) amounts to 40% at energy about 200 MeV.
- The polarization time t_0 is shown as a function of T_lab (MeV) for n = 10^14 cm^{-2} and f = 10^6 c^{-1}, with acceptance angles of 10 mrad, 20 mrad, and 30 mrad (values read from figure, not given as explicit numbers in text).
- Using the Paris potential, previous work (Dmitriev et al., 2007) predicted positive P_perp with maximum 8% at energy about 60 MeV and negative P_parallel raising up to 12%.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the spin-dependent p-bar-p cross section using the Paris potential (Dmitriev et al., 2007) and the Julich potential (PAX Collaboration, 2009), where the Coulomb-strong interference contribution was omitted. The results indicate that while all models predict a sizable polarization, the quantitative disagreement is large, so only experimental measurement of the spin-dependent cross section can prove the applicability of potential models. The work enables further design of spin-filtering experiments for polarized antiproton beams, as proposed by the PAX Collaboration.
