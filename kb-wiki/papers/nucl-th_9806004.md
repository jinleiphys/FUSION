# Freeze out in hydrodynamical models

> We study the effects of strict conservation laws and the problem of negative contributions to final momentum distribution during the freeze out through 3-dimensional hypersurfaces with space-like normal. We study some suggested solutions for this problem, and demonstrate it on one example.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the standard Cooper-Frye freeze-out procedure for hypersurfaces with space-like normal vectors produces unphysical negative contributions to the final momentum distribution, and that a "cut Jüttner distribution" (a thermal distribution multiplied by a step function enforcing only forward-moving particles) can formally satisfy conservation laws, but the resulting post-freeze-out distribution is not a thermal equilibrium distribution and its parameters (especially temperature) become unphysical.

## Method
The authors derive the baryon current, energy-momentum tensor, and entropy current for a cut Jüttner distribution in the Rest Frame of the Gas (RFG), then impose conservation laws (baryon number, energy, and momentum) across a freeze-out surface element with space-like normal. They solve the resulting equations analytically in the massless limit and numerically for massive particles, using a bag-model equation of state for the pre-freeze-out quark-gluon plasma (QGP) side. The post-freeze-out Eckart flow velocity and proper density are computed from the cut distribution.

## Key numbers
- For a pre-freeze-out flow velocity of \(v_0 = 0.2c\) in case (a) (bag constant \(\Lambda_B = 225\) MeV, \(n_0 = 1.2\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(T_0 = 60\) MeV), the post-freeze-out flow velocity increases to \(v_{\text{flow}} = 0.4c\) and the post-freeze-out parameter velocity (determining the \(p_t\) spectrum) increases to \(v = 0.6c\), corresponding to a 60% increase in the slope parameter \(T_{\text{slope}}\).
- In the massless limit, the continuity equation yields a third-order equation for the post-freeze-out velocity parameter \(v\): \(Q_1^{-2} (v+1)^3 + v - 1 = 0\), where \(Q_1^{-1} = \tilde{n}(\mu,T)/(4 n_0 \gamma_0 v_0)\).
- The energy equation in the massless limit gives the same third-order equation with coefficient \(Q_2^{-1} = 3 T \tilde{n}(\mu,T)/(4 e_0 \gamma_0 v_0)\), and consistency requires \(T = (1/3)(e_0/n_0)\).
- The post-freeze-out baryon density \(n\) is reduced compared to the pre-freeze-out density \(\hat{n}\); for example, with \(v_0 = 0.5\), \(T_0 = 50\) MeV, and \(\Lambda_B = 80\) MeV, the density decreases monotonically as pre-freeze-out density increases from 0.1 to 1.2 fm\(^{-3}\).
- The post-freeze-out temperature parameter of the cut Jüttner distribution becomes about an order of magnitude higher than the pre-freeze-out temperature (e.g., pre-FO \(T_0 = 60\) MeV yields post-FO parameter \(T \sim 600\) MeV), but the authors note this has no physical significance as the distribution is not thermal.

## Context
This work extends the earlier suggestion by Bugaev (1996) of using a cut Jüttner distribution for freeze-out with space-like normals, and generalizes it to massive particles and full conservation laws. It contradicts the standard Cooper-Frye (1974) approach by showing that the naive application ignores flow velocity changes and produces negative contributions. The paper enables future work on kinetic models (e.g., Monte Carlo simulations by Bass and Belkacem (1995, 1997)) to derive more realistic post-freeze-out distributions, and highlights that the sharp-surface approximation may be realistic only for rapid hadronization from supercooled QGP (Csernai and Mishustin, 1995).
