# Holography Inspired Stringy Hadrons

> Holography inspired stringy hadrons (HISH) is a set of models that describe hadrons: mesons, baryons and glueballs as strings in four dimensional space time. The models are based on a "map" from stringy hadrons of holographic confining backgrounds. In this note we review the "derivation" of the models. We start with a brief reminder of the passage from the AdS5xS5 string theory to certain flavored confining holographic models. We then describe the string configurations in holographic backgrounds that correspond to a Wilson line,a meson,a baryon and a glueball. The key ingredients of the four dimensional picture of hadrons are the "string endpoint mass" and the "baryonic string vertex". We determine the classical trajectories of the HISH. We review the current understanding of the quantization of the hadronic strings. We end with a summary of the comparison of the outcome of the HISH models with the PDG data about mesons and baryons. We extract the values of the tension, masses and intercepts from best fits, write down certain predictions for higher excited hadrons and present attempts to identify glueballs

# Holography Inspired Stringy Hadrons

## Key claim
The Holography Inspired Stringy Hadrons (HISH) models describe mesons, baryons, and glueballs as strings in four-dimensional spacetime, mapped from holographic confining backgrounds; however, the baryon data do not support the Y-shaped string model or a central baryonic vertex mass, instead favoring a single string with two endpoint masses (quark-diquark model) with slopes very similar to meson slopes, contrary to the Y-shaped prediction of a 2/3 ratio.

## Method
The authors fit Regge trajectories (J, M²) and radial trajectories (n, M²) for baryons using a single string model with two endpoint masses, treating baryons as a quark and diquark connected by a string. They perform chi-squared minimization fits to PDG data for light, strange, and charmed baryons, allowing slope, endpoint masses, and intercepts to vary, and also test fixed-slope fits at 0.95 GeV⁻². For glueballs, they attempt to assign f₀ and f₂ resonances into meson and glueball trajectories by sorting states into linear trajectories and comparing decay modes.

## Key numbers
- For the N baryon (J, M²) trajectory: slope α = 0.944–0.959 GeV⁻², total endpoint mass 2m = 0–170 MeV, even intercept a_e = (−0.32)–(−0.23), odd intercept a_o = (−0.75)–(−0.65)
- For the Δ baryon (J, M²) trajectory: slope α = 0.898–0.969 GeV⁻², total endpoint mass 2m = 0–450 MeV, even intercept a_e = 0.14–0.54, odd intercept a_o = (−0.84)–(−0.42)
- Even-odd splitting in M²: 0.5 GeV² for the N, 1.1 GeV² for the Δ
- For the Λ baryon: slope α = 0.946–0.955 GeV⁻², total endpoint mass 2m = 0–125 MeV, intercept a = (−0.68)–(−0.61)
- For the Σ baryon (fixed slope α = 0.95 GeV⁻²): total endpoint mass 2m = 530–690 MeV
- For the Ξ baryon (fixed slope α = 0.95 GeV⁻²): total endpoint mass 2m = 750–930 MeV
- For the Λ_c baryon (fixed slope α = 0.95 GeV⁻²): total endpoint mass 2m = 1760 MeV
- For the Ξ_c baryon (fixed slope α = 0.95 GeV⁻²): total endpoint mass 2m = 2060 MeV
- For the N radial trajectory (15 states): slope α = 0.815–0.878 GeV⁻², total endpoint mass 2m = 0–425 MeV
- For the Δ radial trajectory (3 states): slope α = 0.920–0.936 GeV⁻², total endpoint mass 2m = 0–175 MeV

## Context
This work extends the HISH framework previously developed for mesons to baryons and glueballs, contradicting the Y-shaped string model prediction of α_b = (2/3)α_m from Sharov (1998) and instead finding baryon and meson slopes are nearly equal. The analysis enables future identification of glueball candidates among f₀ and f₂ resonances by comparing Regge trajectory assignments with decay modes, and tests the holographic prediction that diquark endpoint mass approximately equals single quark mass, as discussed by Selem (2006). The results also open questions about the structure of strange baryons, particularly the discrepancy between the nearly massless Λ and the massive Σ fits.
