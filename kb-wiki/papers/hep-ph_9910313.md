# On the QCD Ground State at High Density

> We investigate the possible ground states of QCD at asymptotic densities, where the theory is expected to exhibit color superconductivity. We characterize the color-flavor structure of possible diquark condensates, and find those that are energy extrema by solving the weak-coupling Dyson-Schwinger equations, including Landau damping and the Meissner effect. We show that, as previously anticipated, in the two flavor case the vacuum breaks SU(3) color to SU(2) and in the three flavor case the vacua with color-flavor locking (CFL) have the lowest energy. We identify a number of relatively flat directions in the potential along which the pattern of gauge symmetry breaking changes and parity is violated. We discuss possible phenomenological consequences of our results.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that at asymptotically high densities, the color-flavor locked (CFL) vacuum has the lowest energy among the possible diquark condensate configurations, but identifies several relatively flat directions in the potential where the pattern of gauge symmetry breaking changes and parity can be violated, with the Meissner effect providing a small correction that becomes more important at lower densities.

## Method

The authors characterize the color-flavor structure of possible diquark condensates using an ansatz parametrized by three eigenvalues (a,b,c) and solve the weak-coupling Dyson-Schwinger (gap) equations including Landau damping and the Meissner effect. They then compute the vacuum energies of the resulting extremal configurations using the CJT effective potential, evaluating both quark loop and gluon loop contributions to determine the true ground state.

## Key numbers

- The gap at asymptotic densities scales as Δ ∼ μ g⁻⁵ exp(-3π²/(√2 g)) in the weak coupling limit (small g, large μ)
- The Debye screening mass is m_D² = N_f g² μ² / (2π²)
- For μ = 400 MeV and μ = 10¹⁰ MeV, numerical gap solutions are presented for the three ansätze (111), (110), and (100)
- The quark binding energy scales as E_q ∼ -a μ² Δ₀², where a is an integer eigenvalue (a=1 for 8 eigenvalues and a=4 for 1 eigenvalue in the CFL case; a=1 for 4 eigenvalues and a=2 for 2 eigenvalues in the (110) case; a=1 for 4 eigenvalues in the (100) case)
- The gluon loop contribution to vacuum energy scales as E_g ∼ m_D² Δ₀² ln(μ/k_*) ∼ g μ² Δ₀², parametrically smaller than the quark contribution
- The LR coupling contribution from gluon loops scales as E_g^{LR} ∼ m_D² Δ₀² ∼ g² μ² Δ₀², further suppressed by a power of the coupling constant

## Context

This work extends the analysis of color superconductivity by Schäfer and Wilczek (1999), who used local four-fermion interactions and concluded that the CFL vacuum had the lowest energy. The present paper includes gluons explicitly, introducing long-range color-magnetic fluctuations (Landau damping) and Meissner screening into the gap equation and vacuum energy calculations. The results enable future investigation of disoriented condensate domains in heavy ion collisions, where the flat directions in the potential could lead to event-by-event variation in color symmetry breaking patterns and observable departures from thermal distributions.
