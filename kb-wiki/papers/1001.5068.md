# The Mach cone signal and energy deposition scenarios in linearized hydrodynamics

> Particle correlation measurements associated with a hard or semi-hard trigger in heavy-ion collisions may reflect Mach cone shockwaves excited in the bulk medium by partonic energy loss. This is of great interest because, when compared with theory, such measurements can provide information on the transport properties of the medium. Specifically, the formation of Mach cone shockwaves is sensitive to the viscosity and speed of sound, as well as the detailed nature of the jet medium interaction. However, modeling the physics of shockwave excitation to obtain a meaningful comparison with the measured correlations is very challenging since the correlations arise from an interplay of perturbative as well as non-perturbative phenomena at different momentum scales. In this work we take a step in that direction by presenting a systematic study of the dependence of azimuthal particle correlations on the spatio-temporal structure of energy deposition into the medium. Our results indicate that detailed modeling of the evolution of an initially produced hard parton and the interaction of this evolving state with the medium is crucial, as both magnitude and shape of the shockwave signal show a strong dependence on the assumptions being made.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the magnitude and shape of the Mach cone shockwave signal in azimuthal particle correlations depend strongly on the assumed time-dependence of energy deposition into the medium, even when the total integrated energy deposited is held fixed; this implies that detailed modeling of the evolution of a hard parton and its interaction with the medium is crucial for meaningful comparison with experimental data.

## Method

The authors solve linearized hydrodynamic equations for a static, infinite QGP medium at constant temperature, coupling a source term of the form \(J^\nu = (dE/dt)(U^\nu - \lambda \partial^\nu)\delta(\mathbf{x} - \mathbf{u}t)\) to the medium. They compute the perturbed energy density and momentum density in Fourier space, then convert the medium response into an azimuthal hadron spectrum via a Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription at fixed time. Three different energy deposition scenarios are compared: a constant ("Flat") collisional loss for a single on-shell parton; a "Crescendo" scenario where radiative gluons increase energy deposition over time; and a "Decreasing" scenario from the YaJEM Monte Carlo shower code where a highly virtual parton shower deposits energy rapidly then declines.

## Key numbers

- Total integrated energy deposition \(\Delta E_{tot} = 20\) GeV is held fixed across all three scenarios for comparison.
- Medium temperature \(T_0 = 250\) MeV and speed of sound \(c_s^2 = 1/3\) are used.
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s\) is varied between 0.1 and 0.2.
- Local excitation parameter \(\lambda\) is varied between 0 and 2 fm.
- Source parton propagates from \(t = 0\) to \(t = 5\) fm/c, with freeze-out at \(t = 7\) fm/c.
- For the "Crescendo" scenario, the radiative gluon spectrum uses \(\hat{q} = 2 \alpha_s C_2 m_D^2 T \ln(2\sqrt{E_p T}/m_D)\) and the collisional loss formula is \(\left(\frac{dE}{dt}\right)_C = \frac{\alpha_s C_2 m_D^2}{2} \ln \frac{2 \sqrt{E_p T}}{m_D}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier proof-of-concept studies of Mach cone shockwaves in heavy-ion collisions (Solana, 2005; Cones1, Cones2, Cones3) and builds on more rigorous formulations of energy-momentum deposition using Hard Thermal Loop and AdS/CFT methods (Neufeld, 2008; Betz, 2008; Noronha, 2008). The paper systematically investigates how different assumptions about the time-dependence of energy deposition, ranging from constant collisional loss to radiative "crescendo" to shower-based "decreasing" scenarios, affect the azimuthal correlation signal. The results enable future work to constrain the spatio-temporal structure of jet-medium interactions by comparing such model calculations with experimental di-hadron correlation data from RHIC (PHENIX, STAR).
