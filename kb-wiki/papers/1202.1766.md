# Investigation of alpha-induced reactions on 130Ba and 132Ba and their importance for the synthesis of heavy p nuclei

> Captures of alpha particles on the proton-richest Barium isotope, 130Ba, have been studied in order to provide cross section data for the modeling of the astrophysical gamma process. The cross sections of the 130Ba(alpha,gamma)134Ce and 130Ba(alpha,n)133Ce reactions have been measured with the activation technique in the center-of mass energy range between 11.6 and 16 MeV, close above the astrophysically relevant energies. As a side result, the cross section of the 132Ba(alpha,n)135Ce reaction has also been measured. The results are compared with the prediction of statistical model calculations, using different input parameters such as alpha+nucleus optical potentials. It is found that the (alpha,n) data can be reproduced employing the standard alpha+nucleus optical potential widely used in astrophysical applications. Assuming its validity also in the astrophysically relevant energy window, we present new stellar reaction rates for 130Ba(alpha,gamma)134Ce and 132Ba(alpha,gamma)136Ce and their inverse reactions calculated with the SMARAGD statistical model code. The highly increased 136Ce(gamma,alpha)132Ba rate implies that the p nucleus 130Ba cannot directly receive contributions from the Ce isotopic chain. Further measurements are required to better constrain this result.

## Key claim

The measured cross sections for 130Ba(alpha,gamma)134Ce are overpredicted by standard statistical model codes by factors of 2-3, but the accompanying 130Ba(alpha,n)133Ce data are well reproduced, implying that the alpha+nucleus optical potential is correct and the discrepancy in the capture channel arises from uncertainties in the neutron or gamma widths at the measured energies; however, at the astrophysically relevant lower energies (5-8 MeV) the alpha width alone determines the capture rate, so the standard potential may still be valid there.

## Method

Alpha-induced reactions on enriched 130Ba targets (11.8% 130Ba, 0.7% 132Ba) were measured using the activation technique at the ATOMKI cyclotron at 11 beam energies in the center-of-mass range 11.6-16 MeV. The 130Ba(alpha,gamma)134Ce cross section was determined via the 604.7 keV gamma ray from the decay of 134La (half-life 6.67 min) after several days of cooling, while the 130Ba(alpha,n)133Ce cross section (separated into ground-state and isomeric-state contributions) and the 132Ba(alpha,n)135Ce cross section were measured from gamma spectra taken shortly after irradiation. Target thickness was determined by three independent methods (weighing, alpha energy loss, Rutherford backscattering) with 8% uncertainty, and detector efficiency was calibrated with multiple radioactive sources at three counting geometries with corrections for true coincidence summing.

## Key numbers

- 130Ba(alpha,gamma)134Ce cross section at E_cm = 11.613 MeV: 65.5 +/- 9.8 microbarn
- 130Ba(alpha,gamma)134Ce cross section at E_cm = 15.509 MeV: 1937 +/- 142 microbarn
- 130Ba(alpha,n)133Ce total cross section at E_cm = 12.596 MeV: 1594 +/- 206 microbarn
- 130Ba(alpha,n)133Ce total cross section at E_cm = 15.998 MeV: 82144 +/- 9892 microbarn
- 132Ba(alpha,n)135Ce cross section at E_cm = 12.051 MeV: 815 +/- 159 microbarn
- 132Ba(alpha,n)135Ce cross section at E_cm = 15.998 MeV: 111000 +/- 20180 microbarn
- The astrophysical Gamow window for 130Ba(alpha,gamma)134Ce at 2 GK is between 5.3 and 8.1 MeV (Rauscher 2010)
- The NON-SMOKER code predicts cross sections in the Gamow window between 10^-15 and 10^-8 barns

## Context

This work provides the first experimental cross sections for alpha-induced reactions on 130Ba, a p nucleus identified as a high-priority target for measurement in the sensitivity study of Rauscher (2006). The data test statistical model calculations (NON-SMOKER, SMARAGD, TALYS) that are the standard input for gamma-process network calculations in core-collapse supernovae. The finding that the 130Ba(alpha,n) data agree with the standard alpha+nucleus optical potential (McFadden and Satchler) while the (alpha,gamma) data are overpredicted motivates further measurements at lower energies to constrain the energy dependence of the alpha width, and the new stellar rates for 130Ba(alpha,gamma)134Ce and 132Ba(alpha,gamma)136Ce (and their inverses) imply that 130Ba cannot receive direct contributions from the Ce isotopic chain via photodisintegration.
