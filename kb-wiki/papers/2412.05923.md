# Implications of latest NICER data for the neutron star equation of state

> As an update to our previously performed Bayesian inference analyses of the neutron star matter equation-of-state and related quantities, the additional impact of the recently published NICER data of PSR J0437-4751 is examined. Including the mass and radius distributions of this pulsar in our data base results in modest shifts from previously inferred median posterior values of radii $R$ and central densities $n_c$ for representative $1.4\,M_\odot$ and $2.1\,M_\odot$ neutron stars: radii are reduced by about $0.2-0.3$ km to values of $R_{1.4} = 12.1\pm 0.5$ km and $R_{2.1} = 11.9^{+0.5}_{-0.6}$ km (at the 68\% level), and central densities increase slightly to values of $n_c(1.4\,M_\odot)/n_0 = 2.8\pm 0.3$ and $n_c(2.1\,M_\odot)/n_0 = 3.8_{-0.7}^{+0.6}$ (in units of equilibrium nuclear matter density, $n_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$), i.e., they still fall below five times nuclear saturation density at the 68\% level. As a further significant result, the evidence established by analyzing Bayes factors for a negative trace anomaly measure, $Δ= 1/3-P/\varepsilon < 0$, inside heavy neutron stars is raised to strong.

## Key claim
Including the new NICER mass and radius data for PSR J0437-4751 shifts previously inferred neutron star radii downward by about 0.2-0.3 km and central densities upward by less than 8%, with the evidence for a negative trace anomaly measure inside heavy neutron stars rising from moderate to strong.

## Method
The authors performed Bayesian inference using a six-segment piecewise linear parametrization of the squared speed of sound as a function of energy density, with chiral effective field theory constraints implemented as a likelihood (not a prior) up to 1.3 times nuclear saturation density and perturbative QCD constraints applied at asymptotic densities. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations and the tidal deformability differential equation for each equation-of-state sample, then computed posterior distributions by adding the new NICER data for PSR J0437-4751 to a standard data set including previous NICER results, Shapiro delay masses, and gravitational wave tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers
- Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: R_1.4 = 12.1 ± 0.5 km (68% level), reduced from 12.3 km without J0437 data
- Radius of a 2.1 solar mass neutron star: R_2.1 = 11.9^{+0.5}_{-0.6} km (68% level), reduced from 12.2 km without J0437 data
- Central baryon density of a 1.4 solar mass star: n_c(1.4 M_sun)/n_0 = 2.8 ± 0.3 (in units of n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3})
- Central baryon density of a 2.1 solar mass star: n_c(2.1 M_sun)/n_0 = 3.8_{-0.7}^{+0.6} (68% level)
- Bayes factor for negative trace anomaly measure: B = 5.6 without black widow pulsar, B = 10.9 with black widow pulsar PSR J0952-0607
- Maximum supported mass: M_max = 2.30_{-0.15}^{+0.12} M_sun with inclusion of black widow mass data
- PSR J0437-4751 mass and radius from NICER: M = 1.418 ± 0.037 M_sun, R = 11.36^{+0.95}_{-0.63} km

## Context
This work updates the Bayesian inference analyses of Brandes and Weise (2023) by incorporating the new NICER data for PSR J0437-4751 from Choudhury et al. (2024), along with updated radius information for PSR J0740+6620 from Salmi et al. (2022, 2024). The results extend previous analyses by Rutherford et al. (2024) using a more flexible sound-speed parametrization and treating chiral EFT as a likelihood rather than a prior. The findings enable improved constraints on the equation of state of cold dense matter and provide a benchmark for testing models such as the QHC21 equation of state from Kojo et al. (2022), while the strong evidence for a negative trace anomaly measure suggests that the conformal limit is not reached within neutron star cores.
