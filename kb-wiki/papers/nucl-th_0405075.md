# Calculations of K+, K- and phi Production in Near-Threshold Proton-Nucleus Collisions

> K+, K- and ϕmeson production in proton-nucleus (pA) collisions has been calculated within a BUU transport model. It is shown that the nucleon-hyperon strangeness transfer channel is essential. The role of three-body reactions has been investigated within the medium. The targetmass dependence of $ϕ$ production is predicted to give important information on the in-medium properties of all three mesons.

# Calculations of K+, K- and phi Production in Near-Threshold Proton-Nucleus Collisions

## Key claim

The nucleon-hyperon (NY) strangeness transfer channel NY → NNK⁻ is essential for describing K⁻ production in proton-nucleus collisions, and its contribution has been largely underestimated in the past; the target-mass dependence of phi meson production is predicted to provide information on the in-medium properties of K⁺, K⁻, and phi mesons, but only if the reaction mechanism is sufficiently known to allow the potential to be fixed by comparison with data.

## Method

The authors employed a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model to simulate K⁺, K⁻, and phi meson production in proton-nucleus collisions near threshold. They incorporated nucleon-hyperon strangeness transfer channels and investigated three-body reactions using Feynman diagrams with propagators, separating on-shell and off-shell contributions to determine whether genuine three-body processes or sequential two-step processes dominate phi production.

## Key numbers

- An attractive antikaon potential of -120 n/n₀ MeV, combined with the NY channels, increases the K⁻ cross section; disregarding the NY → K⁻ channels reduces the cross section by about 40% in pAu collisions at 2.5 GeV beam energy.
- For phi production, the threshold energy reduces from 2.6 GeV for a proton colliding with a single nucleon to 1.8 GeV for a proton interacting with two target nucleons.
- The vacuum mean proper lifetime of phi mesons is about 50 fm/c, which decreases to an order of magnitude smaller value in normal nuclear matter due to the attractive K⁻ potential.
- The in-medium cross section used for rescattering of intermediate pions and rho mesons was 25 mb.
- Four parameter sets for the K⁻ potential of the form V_{K⁻} = -(v + w*exp(-s p_K/MeV)) MeV were used: (i) (0,0,0), (ii) (70,0,0), (iii) (55, 130, 0.0025), and (iv) (150,0,0).
- The relative mass change of the phi meson was assumed proportional to α n/n₀ with α = 0.033.

## Context

This work extends prior transport model studies of K⁺ production by Cassing (2000) and Tsushima (1998, 1999), which extracted a weak repulsive K⁺ potential proportional to nuclear density. The authors build on earlier estimates of the experimentally unknown NY → NNK⁻ cross section by Ko (1999) and Cassing (2001), and on their own previous work (Barz, 2003) investigating the NY channel in pA collisions. The study enables future precision measurements of K⁺K⁻ pairs with invariant mass corresponding to the phi meson, which could probe in-medium effects of all three mesons, and complements heavy-ion studies by Kotte (2003) that emphasized the tight coupling of K⁻ and phi production rates.
