# Neutron Star masses from the Field Correlator Method Equation of State

> We analyse the hadron-quark phase transition in neutron stars by confronting the hadronic Equation of State (EoS) obtained according to the microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock many body theory, with the quark matter EoS derived within the Field Correlator Method. In particular, the latter EoS is only parametrized in terms of the gluon condensate and the large distance quark-antiquark potential, so that the comparison of the results of this analysis with the most recent measurements of heavy neutron star masses provides some physical constraints on these two parameters.

# Neutron Star masses from the Field Correlator Method Equation of State

## Key claim
The maximum mass of neutron stars computed using the Field Correlator Method (FCM) for quark matter can reach values compatible with the observed 2.01 solar mass pulsar PSR J0348+0432, but only when the FCM parameters V1 and G2 are sufficiently large; at those high parameter values, the quark core disappears and the star becomes purely hadronic, with a small window for stable hybrid stars existing only for the relativistic DBHF hadronic EoS with V1 between approximately 0.1 and 0.12 GeV.

## Method
The authors constructed hybrid neutron star equations of state by combining hadronic matter described by the nonrelativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) theory (with Argonne v18 potential plus Urbana three-body force) and its relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) counterpart (with Bonn A potential), with quark matter described by the Field Correlator Method (FCM) parametrized by the gluon condensate G2 and the large-distance quark-antiquark potential V1. The hadron-quark phase transition was treated using the Maxwell construction assuming first-order transition, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to obtain mass-radius relations for various combinations of the FCM parameters.

## Key numbers
- Observed neutron star mass PSR J0348+0432: M = 2.01 ± 0.04 solar masses
- Observed neutron star mass PSR J1614-2230: M = 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses
- Gluon condensate at zero temperature and chemical potential: G2 = 0.012 ± 0.006 GeV^4
- Nuclear matter saturation density: ρ0 ≈ 0.17 fm^{-3}
- Nuclear matter saturation energy: E/A ≈ -16 MeV
- For the DBHF hadronic EoS with V1 > 0.2 GeV, the maximum mass reaches 2.31 solar masses
- For the BHF hadronic EoS with V1 > 0.2 GeV, the maximum mass does not grow beyond 2.03 solar masses
- The V1 parameter range from finite-temperature FCM analysis is 0.8 to 0.9 GeV, while the range found in this neutron star analysis is 0.1 to 0.2 GeV
- Stable hybrid stars with quark matter core exist for DBHF EoS with V1 between approximately 0.1 and 0.12 GeV

## Context
This work extends previous studies of neutron star structure using the BHF and DBHF hadronic equations of state (Burgio et al., various years) by incorporating the FCM description of quark matter, which unlike the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model includes confinement ab initio. The analysis confronts the hybrid EoS predictions with the high-precision mass measurements of PSR J0348+0432 and PSR J1614-2230, providing constraints on the FCM parameters V1 and G2. The results suggest that at finite density and zero temperature, the quark-antiquark potential V1 may need to be replaced by a quark-quark effective interaction with strength approximately V1/4, resolving the discrepancy between the V1 values extracted from finite-temperature lattice comparisons (0.8-0.9 GeV) and those required by neutron star observations (0.1-0.2 GeV).
