# Glassy phase in the Hamiltonian Mean Field model

> We study the relaxation dynamics of a Hamiltonian system of N fully-coupled XY spins. The thermodynamics of the system predicts a ferromagnetic and a paramagnetic phase. Starting from out-of-equilibrium initial conditions, the dynamics at constant energy drives the system into quasi-stationary states (QSS) characterized by dynamical frustration. We introduce the spin polarization as a new order parameter which allows to interpret the dynamically generated QSS regime as a glassy phase of the model.

## Key claim

The quasi-stationary states (QSS) of the Hamiltonian Mean Field (HMF) model, which arise from out-of-equilibrium initial conditions and have a lifetime that diverges with system size, can be interpreted as a thermodynamically distinct glassy phase characterized by a new order parameter (the spin polarization) even though neither disorder nor frustration are a priori present in the interaction.

## Method

The authors performed molecular dynamics simulations of the HMF model (a system of N fully-coupled classical XY spins) in the microcanonical ensemble at constant energy. They introduced a new order parameter, the average polarization p, defined as the average over all rotators of the modulus of the time-averaged spin vector, and compared it to the standard magnetization M. Simulations were run for various system sizes N and energy densities U, with time averages taken over intervals shorter than the QSS lifetime to probe the metastable regime.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature of the equilibrium phase transition is T_c = 0.5, corresponding to a critical energy per particle U_c = E_c/N = 0.75.
- For N = 1000 at U = 0.5 in the QSS regime, the average polarization p = 0.67 and the average magnetization M = 0.63.
- For N = 1000 at U = 0.69 in the QSS regime, p = 0.24 and M = 0.20.
- For U = 0.69, as system size N increases, the magnetization M vanishes as N^{-1/6}, while the polarization p remains constant at 0.24 ± 0.02, independent of N within error.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the HMF model by Antoni and Ruffo (1995) and Latora, Rapisarda, and Ruffo (1998, 1999), which had already identified quasi-stationary states with anomalous diffusion, non-Gaussian velocity distributions, and vanishing Lyapunov exponents. The discovery of aging behavior in the QSS regime by Montemurro, Tamarit, and Anteneodo (2003) and by the present authors (2003) motivated the analogy with spin-glass systems. The introduction of the polarization order parameter enables a direct parallel to the Edwards-Anderson order parameter used in the Sherrington-Kirkpatrick model, and opens the possibility of searching for similar glassy phases in other long-range Hamiltonian systems.
