# Probable observation of the nuclear Cooper pair mean square radius in superfluid nuclei

> The phenomenon of low-temperature superconductivity is intimately associated with the condensation of weakly bound, very extended, strongly overlapping Cooper pairs, and systematic experimental studies of the associated mean square radius (coherence length) have been made. While the extension of BCS theory to the atomic nucleus has been successful beyond expectation, to our knowledge, no measurement of the nuclear coherence length (expected to be much larger than nuclear dimensions) has been reported in the literature. Recent studies of Cooper pair transfer across a Josephson-like junction, transiently established in a heavy ion collision between superfluid nuclei, have likely changed the situation, providing the experimental input for a quantitative estimate of the nuclear coherence length, as well as the basis for a nuclear analogue of the (ac) Josephson effect.

# Probable observation of the nuclear Cooper pair mean square radius in superfluid nuclei

## Key claim
The authors argue that the nuclear coherence length (Cooper pair mean square radius), expected to be much larger than nuclear dimensions, can be inferred from two-neutron transfer reactions between superfluid nuclei, specifically the reaction \(^{116}\text{Sn}+^{60}\text{Ni}\to^{114}\text{Sn}+^{62}\text{Ni}\), which acts as a transient nuclear Josephson-like junction. The claim is hedged as "probable observation" because the extraction relies on interpreting the critical distance of closest approach in the heavy-ion collision as the nuclear analogue of the critical momentum \(q_c = 1/\xi\) that defines the coherence length in condensed-matter superconductors.

## Method
The authors analyzed absolute differential cross sections for one-neutron and two-neutron transfer reactions between the superfluid nuclei \(^{116}\text{Sn}\) and \(^{60}\text{Ni}\) at twelve bombarding energies between 140.60 MeV and 167.95 MeV center-of-mass energy, measured at \(\theta_{cm}=140^\circ\). The one-neutron transfer (a Giaever-like single-quasiparticle process) was calculated using distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) with BCS occupation amplitudes for Sn and \((d,p)\)-data for Ni, summing incoherent contributions from eleven quasiparticle states in \(^{61}\text{Ni}\). The two-neutron transfer (a Josephson-like Cooper pair process) was calculated in second-order DWBA including simultaneous, non-orthogonality, and successive transfer terms, with the successive mechanism dominating. The ratio of two-neutron to one-neutron cross sections, scaled by \((\pi/4)^2\), was plotted as a function of the distance of closest approach \(D_0\) to extract the nuclear coherence length.

## Key numbers
- The two-neutron transfer reaction \(^{116}\text{Sn}+^{60}\text{Ni}\to^{114}\text{Sn}+^{62}\text{Ni}\) has a \(Q\)-value of 1.307 MeV; the one-neutron transfer to \(^{115}\text{Sn}+^{61}\text{Ni}\) has \(Q_{1n} = -1.74\) MeV.
- Bombarding energies ranged from \(E_{cm} = 140.60\) MeV to 167.95 MeV, with the Coulomb barrier at \(E_B = 157.60\) MeV.
- The distance of closest approach at the Coulomb barrier is \(D_0(E_B) = 13.2\) fm; the analysis covers \(D_0\) from 13.12 fm (1 MeV above barrier) to 14.39 fm (12.58 MeV below barrier).
- The abnormal density of the reacting nuclei is estimated as \(n_s' \approx 10^{-2}\) fm\(^{-3}\), compared to nuclear saturation density 0.17 fm\(^{-3}\).
- The junction energy is estimated as \(E_J \approx 0.5\) MeV, close to the Josephson weak-link estimate \(\Delta/2 \approx 0.7\) MeV for \(\Delta \approx 1.4\) MeV.
- The gauge angle fluctuation is \(\delta\phi \approx 0.5\) rad, giving \(|\langle e^{i2\delta\phi}\rangle|^2 \approx 1\).
- Less than 24% of the two-neutron transfer cross section goes to excited states, based on gamma-coincidence experiments.

## Context
This work extends the BCS theory of superconductivity, originally applied to nuclei by Bohr, Mottelson, and Pines (1958) to explain moments of inertia and odd-even mass differences, by attempting to measure the nuclear coherence length, a quantity previously unobserved despite decades of nuclear pairing studies. The experiment builds on two-neutron transfer measurements by Montanari et al. (2014, 2016) and theoretical developments in pairing rotations and vibrations (Bohr 1976, Bes 1966, Brink 2005, Broglia 2000). The analysis uses the concept of a transient Josephson junction between superfluid nuclei, analogous to the condensed-matter Josephson effect (Josephson 1962, Anderson 1964), and the Pippard coherence length (Pippard 1953). If confirmed, this work enables quantitative study of nuclear Cooper pair structure and a nuclear analogue of the alternating-current Josephson effect, where the bias voltage determines the frequency of Cooper pair tunneling rather than the current amplitude.
