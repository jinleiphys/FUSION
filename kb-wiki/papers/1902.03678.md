# Longitudinal spin asymmetries in polarized deuteron DIS with spectator tagging

> Polarized electron-deuteron DIS with spectator proton tagging offers a way of measuring the neutron spin structure functions with maximal theoretical control of nuclear effects. We calculate the nuclear structure factors in the longitudinal double-spin asymmetries using methods of light-front nuclear structure. A unique feature of the spin-1 system is that spin asymmetries can be formed either relative to the cross section in all three spin states ($λ_d = \pm 1, 0$) or in the two maximum-spin states only ($\pm 1$, involving tensor polarization). We find that the two-state deuteron spin asymmetry at small spectator proton momenta permits accurate extraction of the neutron structure function $g_{1n}$. Such measurements could be performed at a future electron-ion collider (EIC) with polarized deuteron beams and suitable forward detectors.

## Key claim

The two-state deuteron spin asymmetry (using only the $\pm 1$ spin states) at small spectator proton momenta permits accurate extraction of the neutron spin structure function $g_{1n}$, and this asymmetry is theoretically more complex due to tensor polarization but practically more convenient for neutron structure extraction than the three-state asymmetry.

## Method

The authors calculated the nuclear structure factors in longitudinal double-spin asymmetries for polarized electron-deuteron deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) with spectator proton tagging, using methods of light-front nuclear structure. The deuteron structure was described by a light-front wave function in nucleon degrees of freedom, incorporating relativistic spin structure via Melosh rotations and constructed approximately using the non-relativistic AV18 deuteron wave function (S and D waves). The spin asymmetries were computed for both the three-state (all $\lambda_d = \pm 1, 0$) and two-state ($\lambda_d = \pm 1$ only) cases as functions of the proton recoil momentum variables $\alpha_p$ and $|\bm{p}_{pT}|$.

## Key numbers

- Both $D_d^{(3)}$ and $D_d^{(2)}$ are unity at $\alpha_p = 1$ and $|\bm{p}_{pT}| = 0$, where only the S-wave is present.
- $D_d^{(3)}$ and $D_d^{(2)}$ remain close to unity for $|\bm{p}_{pT}| \lesssim 150$ MeV, where the S-wave dominates.
- Both structure factors pass through zero at $|\bm{p}_{pT}| \approx 300$ MeV, where the combination $(U - W/\sqrt{2})$ vanishes.
- The two-state factor $D_d^{(2)}$ satisfies the bounds $-1 \leq D_d^{(2)} \leq 1$; no such bound is found for $D_d^{(3)}$, which can attain absolute values larger than unity.
- At small center-of-mass momenta $|\bm{k}| \ll m$, D-wave corrections affect $D_d^{(2)}$ at quadratic order in $W/U$, but $D_d^{(3)}$ at linear order.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of spectator tagging in polarized deuteron DIS, including the work of Frankfurt and Strikman (1983) and more recent feasibility studies for a future electron-ion collider (EIC) by Boer et al. (2011), Guzey et al. (2014), and Cosyn et al. (2016). The results enable precise extraction of the neutron spin structure function $g_{1n}$ from future EIC measurements with polarized deuteron beams and forward proton detectors, and the formalism can be extended to transverse deuteron polarization, azimuthal asymmetries, tensor-polarized tagged structure functions, and hard exclusive processes on the neutron.
