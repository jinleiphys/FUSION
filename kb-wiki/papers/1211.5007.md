# Coupled-channel analysis of the possible $D^{(*)}D^{(*)}$, $\bar{B}^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}$ and $D^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}$ molecular states

> We perform a coupled-channel study of the possible deuteron-like molecules with two heavy flavor quarks, including the systems of $D^{(*)}D^{(*)}$ with double charm, $\bar{B}^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}$ with double bottom and $D^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}$ with both charm and bottom, within the one-boson-exchange model. In our study, we take into account the S-D mixing which plays an important role in the formation of the loosely bound deuteron, and particularly, the coupled-channel effect in the flavor space. According to our calculation, the states $D^{(*)}D^{(*)}[I(J^P)=0(1^+)]$ and $(D^{(*)}D^{(*)})_s[J^P=1^+]$ with double charm, the states $\bar{B}^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}[I(J^P)=0(1^+),0(2^+),1(0^+),1(1^+),1(2^+)]$, $(\bar{B}^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)})_s[J^P=0^+,1^+,2^+]$ and $(\bar{B}^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)})_{ss}[J^P=0^+,1^+,2^+]$ with double bottom, and the states $D^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}[I(J^P)=0(0^+),0(1^+)]$ and $(D^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)})_s[J^P=0^+,1^+]$ with both charm and bottom are good molecule candidates. However, the existence of the states $D^{(*)}D^{(*)}[I(J^P)=0(2^+)]$ with double charm and $D^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}[I(J^P)=1(1^+)]$ with both charm and bottom is ruled out.

## Key claim

The one-boson-exchange model with S-D mixing and coupled-channel effects in flavor space predicts that several doubly heavy molecular states with specific isospin and spin-parity quantum numbers are good molecule candidates, while others are ruled out; however, the authors note that the cutoff parameter is a free parameter that cannot be determined exactly due to lack of experimental data, making definite conclusions difficult.

## Method

The authors applied the one-boson-exchange (OBE) model to derive effective potentials for the D(*)D(*), B(*)B(*), and D(*)B(*) systems, using Lagrangians based on heavy quark symmetry and SU(3)-flavor symmetry. They solved the coupled-channel Schrodinger equation including S-D mixing and coupled-channel effects in flavor space, introducing a monopole form factor at each vertex with a cutoff parameter in the range 0.9 to 2.5 GeV, and considered exchanges of pi, eta, sigma, rho, omega, and phi mesons.

## Key numbers

- D(*)D(*)[I(J^P)=0(1^+)]: binding energy 1.24 MeV relative to DD* threshold, root-mean-square radius 3.11 fm, with cutoff 1.05 GeV using OPE potential alone; dominant channel [DD*]-(3S1) with probability 96.39%
- D(*)D(*)[I(J^P)=0(1^+)]: binding energy 42.82 MeV with OBE potential at cutoff 1.10 GeV
- D(*)D(*)[I(J^P)=1(0^+)]: binding energy 2.64 MeV relative to DD threshold, mass 3730.17 MeV, root-mean-square radius 1.38 fm, with cutoff 2.64 GeV using OBE potential
- D(*)D(*)[I(J^P)=1(1^+)]: binding energy 0.27 MeV relative to DD* threshold, mass 3875.58 MeV, root-mean-square radius 5.81 fm, with cutoff 2.48 GeV using OBE potential
- D(*)D(*)[I(J^P)=1(2^+)]: binding energy 2.95 MeV, mass 4014.29 MeV, root-mean-square radius 1.61 fm, with cutoff 2.48 GeV using OBE potential (excluding DD channels)
- D(*)D(*)[I(J^P)=0(2^+)]: no binding solution found even with cutoff up to 3.0 GeV

## Context

This work extends the study of heavy flavor molecular states with hidden charm (such as X(3872), Y(3930), Y(4140)) to systems with two heavy flavor quarks, following the earlier molecular state concept proposed by Voloshin and Okun (1976) and the deuteron-like two meson bound state studies by Tornqvist (1994). The results provide predictions for future experimental searches for doubly charmed, doubly bottom, and charm-bottom molecular states, and the authors note that their OBE potentials with form factors can generate spurious deeply bound states as discussed by Cordon and Gamermann (2009).
