# Chiral symmetry restoration and eigenvalue density of Dirac operator

> We reinvestigate constraints on the eigenvalue density of the Dirac operator in the chiral symmetric phase of 2 flavor QCD at finite temperature, employing the overlap Dirac operator with the exact chiral symmetry at finite lattice spacings to avoid possible ultra-violet(UV) divergences. Studying multi-point correlation functions in various channels in the thermodynamical limit, we obtain stronger constraints than those found in the previous studies that not only the eigenvalue density at the origin but also its first and second derivatives vanish in the chiral limit of 2 flavor QCD. In addition we show that the axial U(1) anomaly becomes invisible in susceptibilities of scalar and pseudo scalar mesons.

# Chiral symmetry restoration and eigenvalue density of Dirac operator

## Key claim
Using the overlap Dirac operator with exact chiral symmetry at finite lattice spacings, the authors derive stronger constraints on the Dirac eigenvalue density in the chiral symmetric phase of 2-flavor QCD than previously known: not only the eigenvalue density at the origin vanishes, but also its first and second derivatives vanish in the chiral limit, provided the non-singlet chiral symmetry is restored at or above the critical temperature.

## Method
The authors employ spectral decomposition of correlation functions for overlap fermions satisfying the Ginsparg-Wilson relation, and analyze multi-point correlation functions in various channels using chiral Ward-Takahashi identities in the thermodynamical limit. They assume analytic expansion of the eigenvalue density around zero and analyticity of vacuum expectation values in m^2 in the chiral symmetric phase, then derive constraints order by order in N (the degree of the operators) from non-singlet chiral WT identities.

## Key numbers
- The eigenvalue density at the origin satisfies ⟨ρ₀^A⟩_m = O(m⁴) (vanishes as m⁴ in the chiral limit)
- The first derivative of the eigenvalue density satisfies ⟨ρ₁^A⟩_m = O(m²)
- The second derivative satisfies ⟨ρ₂^A⟩_m = O(m²)
- The average number of zero modes satisfies lim_{V→∞} ⟨N_{R+L}^A⟩_m / V = 0 at small but non-zero m
- The topological susceptibility satisfies lim_{V→∞} ⟨Q(A)²⟩_m / V = O(m⁶)
- The final eigenvalue density in the chiral limit behaves as ⟨ρ^A(λ)⟩_0 = ⟨ρ₃^A⟩_0 λ³/3! + O(λ⁴)

## Context
This work extends earlier analytical constraints from Cohen (1997) on the connection between U(1)_A restoration and the Dirac eigenvalue gap near the origin. It addresses conflicting numerical results from lattice simulations using different quark actions, such as those reported by Ohno (2011), Bazavov (2012), Kovacs (2011), and Cossu (2012). The results suggest that the chiral phase transition for 2-flavor QCD is likely first order, contrary to the naive expectation of O(4) universality class behavior, and enable stronger analytical control over the fate of U(1)_A symmetry near T_c.
