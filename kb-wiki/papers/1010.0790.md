# Numerical Study of the Two Color Attoworld

> We consider QCD at very low temperatures and non-zero quark chemical potential from lattice Monte Carlo simulations of the two-color theory in a very small spatial volume (the attoscale). In this regime the quark number rises in discrete levels in qualitative agreement with what is found analytically at one loop on S3xS1 with radius R_S3 << 1/Λ_QCD. The detailed level degeneracy, however, cannot be accounted for using weak coupling arguments. At each rise in the quark number there is a corresponding spike in the Polyakov line, also in agreement with the perturbative results. In addition the quark number susceptibility shows a similar behaviour to the Polyakov line and appears to be a good indicator of a confinement-deconfinement type of transition.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in two-color QCD on a very small spatial volume (the "attoscale"), the quark number rises in discrete steps as chemical potential increases, and the Polyakov line shows spikes at each step, qualitatively matching one-loop perturbative results on $S^3 \times S^1$; however, the detailed level degeneracies observed in the lattice simulations cannot be explained by weak coupling arguments, indicating that non-perturbative effects remain important even in this small-volume regime.

## Method

The authors performed lattice Monte Carlo simulations of SU(2) gauge theory (Two Color QCD) with $N_f=2$ Wilson fermions on a $3^3 \times 64$ lattice at very low temperature, using the hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm. They scanned the quark chemical potential $\mu$ at fixed gauge couplings $\beta=24$ and $\beta=6$ with hopping parameter $\kappa=0.124$, measuring the quark number density $n_q$, Polyakov line ${\mathscr P}$, quark number susceptibility $\chi_q$, and the quark-antiquark condensate. They compared these lattice results with one-loop perturbative calculations on $S^3 \times S^1$ for $N_c=2$ and $N_c=\infty$.

## Key numbers

- Lattice size: $L_s^3 \times L_t = 3^3 \times 64$, with $L_t \gg L_s$ giving very low temperature
- Gauge couplings: $\beta = 24$ (weak coupling) and $\beta = 6$ (stronger coupling), with hopping parameter $\kappa = 0.124$
- For free quarks on the $3^3 \times 64$ lattice, the plateau occupancies ${\cal G}$ are 1, 7, 19, and 27, corresponding to plane-wave degeneracies 1, 6, 12, and 8
- For the interacting theory at $\beta=24$, the observed level degeneracies (multiplied by $2N_f$) are 8, 8, 6, 16, 8, and 8
- The Polyakov line reaches maximum values ${\mathscr P}_{max} \simeq 0.4$ at the level transitions
- Onset value of chemical potential for the interacting theory is $\mu \sim 1.2$ (in lattice units)
- The perturbative $S^3 \times S^1$ calculations use $1/(TR) = 50$ and $mR = 0$

## Context

This work extends the one-loop perturbative analysis of QCD on $S^3 \times S^1$ by Hands, Hollowood, and Myers (2010) into the non-perturbative regime using lattice simulations of the two-color theory, which avoids the sign problem present in real QCD at non-zero chemical potential. The results confirm the qualitative picture that deconfining behavior is associated with partially filled energy levels, but reveal that the detailed level structure differs significantly from the perturbative predictions, possibly due to the different manifold (torus vs. hypersphere) or stronger coupling effects. The findings enable future studies of how non-perturbative dynamics modify the attoscale phase structure and suggest that quark number susceptibility may serve as a useful indicator of confinement-deconfinement transitions in systems with chemical potential.
