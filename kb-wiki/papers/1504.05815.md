# Nonchiral enhancement of scalar glueball decay in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model

> We estimate the consequences of finite masses of pseudoscalar mesons on the decay rates of scalar glueballs in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, a top-down holographic model of low-energy QCD, by extrapolating from the calculable vertex of glueball fields and the eta' meson which follows from the Witten-Veneziano mechanism for giving mass to the latter. Evaluating the effect on the recently calculated decay rates of glueballs in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, we find a strong enhancement of the decay of scalar glueballs into kaons and eta mesons, in fairly close agreement with experimental data on the glueball candidate f0(1710).

# Nonchiral enhancement of scalar glueball decay in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model

## Key claim

By extrapolating the exactly calculable coupling of scalar glueballs to the mass term of the isosinglet pseudoscalar meson in the originally chiral Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, the authors find a significantly enhanced decay of scalar glueballs into kaons and eta mesons compared to flavor-symmetric ratios. This enhancement is sufficient to bring the holographic predictions into fairly close agreement with experimental data on the glueball candidate f0(1710), but only under the assumption that the coupling of the glueball field to the mass term for all pseudoscalar mesons is universal.

## Method

The authors start from the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, a top-down holographic model of low-energy QCD, in which pseudoscalar mesons are massless in the chiral limit. They compute the coupling of the scalar glueball field to the nonlocal mass term for the eta-prime meson that arises from the Witten-Veneziano mechanism for the U(1)_A anomaly, obtaining an exact expression for the coupling constant d0. They then assume that the coupling of the glueball field to the mass terms for all pseudoscalar mesons (pions, kaons, eta) is universal and equal to this exactly calculable coupling, and they incorporate this into the previously computed chiral decay rates of the dilatonic glueball mode G_D with mass 1487 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The mass of the lowest holographic glueball mode (exotic polarization) is 855 MeV; the next-lowest, predominantly dilatonic mode has mass 1487 MeV.
- The Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model parameters are fixed by matching f_pi = 92.4 MeV and m_rho = 776 MeV, giving M_KK = 949 MeV and 't Hooft coupling lambda = 16.63; an alternative fit gives lambda = 12.55.
- The coupling constant d0 for the glueball-eta-prime-eta-prime vertex is approximately 17.915 / (lambda^{1/2} N_c M_KK).
- The enhancement parameter alpha for the dilatonic mode G_D is alpha = 8.480 (dimensionless), independent of lambda and M_KK to leading order.
- For f0(1710), the predicted ratio (4/3) * Gamma(pi pi) / Gamma(K anti-K) is 0.463, compared to the experimental value 0.55 (+0.15 / -0.23) from the Particle Data Group.
- For f0(1710), the predicted ratio 4 * Gamma(eta eta) / Gamma(K anti-K) is 1.12, compared to the experimental value 1.92 +/- 0.60.
- The predicted total width over mass for f0(1710) in the massive case is 0.083 to 0.106, compared to the experimental value 0.078(4).
- The predicted partial width over mass for f0(1710) decay into two kaons in the massive case is 0.029 to 0.038, compared to experimental values of 0.041(2) and 0.047(17) from two different analyses.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of glueball decay rates in the chiral Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model (Brunner and Rebhan, 2015), which found that the dilatonic glueball mode with mass 1487 MeV matched the f0(1500) mass but failed to reproduce its decay pattern, while extrapolation to the f0(1710) mass gave close agreement for two-pion decay but predicted flavor-symmetric ratios for kaon and eta production. The present paper introduces finite pseudoscalar meson masses via a mechanism analogous to the Witten-Veneziano mass term, realizing the "chiral suppression" mechanism proposed by Sexton, Vacca, and Teper (1995) and Chanowitz (2005), which posits that scalar glueballs couple more strongly to more massive pseudoscalar mesons. The results favor a glueball interpretation of f0(1710) over f0(1500) and enable future systematic studies of the correlation between branching ratios and the G to eta eta-prime decay channel, for which only upper limits exist experimentally.
