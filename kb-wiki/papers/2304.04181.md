# Spin alignment of vector mesons by glasma fields

> We explain how spin alignment of vector mesons can be induced by background fields, such as electromagnetic fields or soft gluon fields. Our study is based on the quantum kinetic theory of spinning quarks and antiquarks and incorporates the relaxation of the dynamically generated spin polarization. The spin density matrix of vector mesons is obtained by quark coalescence via the Wigner function and kinetic equation. Our approach predicts a local spin correlation that is distinct from the non-local expressions previously obtained in phenomenological derivations. We estimate the magnitude of such local correlations in the glasma model of the preequilibrium phase of relativistic heavy ion collisions. It is found that the resulting spin alignment could be greatly enhanced and may be comparable to the experimental measurement in order of magnitude. We further propose new phenomenological scenarios to qualitatively explain the transverse-momentum and centrality dependence of spin alignment in a self-consistent framework.

## Key claim

The paper explains how spin alignment of vector mesons can be induced by background fields (electromagnetic fields or soft gluon fields) and predicts that the resulting spin alignment from glasma fields could be greatly enhanced and may be comparable to experimental measurements in order of magnitude, but this result depends on the assumption that the dynamical spin polarization from early-time color fields survives spin relaxation effects.

## Method

The authors use quantum kinetic theory (QKT) for spin-1/2 fermions with color degrees of freedom, deriving axial kinetic equations (AKE) for both color-singlet and color-octet components of the Wigner function. They obtain the spin density matrix of vector mesons through quark coalescence via the Wigner function and kinetic equation, incorporating relaxation-time approximations for collisions. The spin alignment is estimated by computing spin correlations from color fields in the glasma phase of the preequilibrium stage of relativistic heavy ion collisions, using an Abelianized approximation for color fields.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium value of the spin density matrix component ρ₀₀ is 1/3, with deviations from this value characterizing spin alignment
- At LHC energies, ρ₀₀ < 1/3 for global spin alignment is observed for both φ and K*⁰ mesons at small transverse momenta
- At RHIC energies, ρ₀₀ > 1/3 for φ mesons and ρ₀₀ ≈ 1/3 for K*⁰ mesons were found
- The spin-polarization pseudovector of quarks from background electromagnetic fields is given by expressions involving eBˣ·ʸ(0)∂_εₚ f_V^(0)(εₚ) and pˣ eEʸ(0)∂_εₚ f_V^(0)(εₚ) divided by 4mεₚ times thermal distribution integrals
- The color-octet axial current density from color fields is proportional to ℏg/εₚ times combinations of color magnetic and electric fields at initial time

## Context

This work extends previous studies on spin alignment of vector mesons by the same authors (Kumar, Müller, Yang 2022) and others (Sheng 2019-2022, Xia 2020) by deriving a new expression for the ρ₀₀ component from the vector-meson kinetic equation in the quark-coalescence scenario, which includes contributions from both color-singlet and color-octet components of axial-charge current densities. The approach contrasts with phenomenological derivations that gave non-local spin correlations, and it enables future calculations of transverse-momentum and centrality dependence of spin alignment within a self-consistent framework. The work also addresses the puzzling experimental observations from STAR (2019, 2022) and ALICE (2019, 2022) collaborations showing different ρ₀₀ values at RHIC and LHC energies.
