# Pion exchange interaction in the $╬│p \to p e^+e^-$ reaction

> The $Žü^0-Žē$ interference has been studied in the dilepton invariant mass distribution spectra in the photonuclear reaction, but that is not done for the gamma-nucleon reaction. Recent past, the $e^+e^-$ invariant mass distribution spectrum in the $╬│p$ reaction, i.e., $╬│p \to pe^+e^-$ reaction, was measured at Jefferson Laboratory to look for the $Žü^0 -Žē$ interference in the multi-GeV region. To study the mechanism of this reaction, the differential cross section of the $e^+e^-$ invariant mass distribution is calculated in the quoted energy region. The reaction is assumed to proceed as $╬│p \to Vp$; $V \to e^+e^-$, where $V$ denotes a vector meson, i.e., either $Žü^0$ or $Žē$ meson. The photoproduction of the vector meson is described by the Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) model which consists of diagonal and off-diagonal processes. The diagonal process is described as $╬│\to V; ~Vp \to Vp$. The low energy $Žē$ meson photoproduction data is well described by the off-diagonal process which is illustrated as $╬│\to Žü^0; ~Žü^0 p \to Žēp$. The reaction $Žü^0p \to Žēp$ proceeds due to one pion exchange interaction. The differential cross sections of the $╬│p \to pe^+e^-$ reaction due to the above processes of VMD model are compared, and the significance of the pion exchange interaction is investigated in the energy region of $╬│$ beam available at Jefferson Laboratory.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the differential cross section of the dielectron invariant mass distribution in the $\gamma p \to p e^+e^-$ reaction using both diagonal and off-diagonal processes of the Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) model, and finds that the off-diagonal process, which proceeds via one pion exchange interaction, is significant for beam energies from above 1.5 GeV up to approximately 5 GeV, but becomes negligible at around 10 GeV.

## Method

The reaction is modeled as $\gamma p \to Vp$ followed by $V \to e^+e^-$, where $V$ is either a $\rho^0$ or $\omega$ meson. The photoproduction amplitude is described by the VMD model: the diagonal process ($\gamma \to V$; $Vp \to Vp$) is used for both $\rho^0$ and $\omega$ mesons, while the off-diagonal process ($\gamma \to \rho^0$; $\rho^0 p \to \omega p$) is used for $\omega$ meson production via one pion exchange. The differential cross section $\frac{d\sigma}{dm}$ for the $e^+e^-$ invariant mass distribution is computed using the vector meson propagator and the forward photoproduction amplitudes, with the diagonal and off-diagonal contributions added incoherently due to opposite parity exchange.

## Key numbers

- Pole masses used: $m_{\rho^0}=775.26$ MeV and $m_{\omega}=782.65$ MeV.
- Photon-to-vector-meson coupling constants: $\gamma_{\gamma \rho}=2.48$ and $\gamma_{\gamma \omega}=8.53$.
- For the diagonal process differential cross section at $q^2=0$: $C=9.3$ $\mu$b/GeV$^2$ and $D=1.4$ GeV in the parameterization $\frac{d\sigma^D}{dq^2} = C(1 + D/E_\gamma)$.
- At $E_\gamma=1.5$ GeV, the cross section is increased by a factor of approximately 2 due to $\rho^0-\omega$ interference in the diagonal process.
- At $E_\gamma=3$ GeV, the total differential cross section $\frac{d\sigma}{dm}$ is increased by approximately 60% due to the off-diagonal process.
- At $E_\gamma=5$ GeV, the enhancement from the off-diagonal process is approximately 25%.
- At $E_\gamma=10$ GeV, the off-diagonal process contributes only 6.7% to the total differential cross section.
- For a Bremsstrahlung beam with energy range 1.2 to 5.4 GeV (matching the JLab measurement), the total cross section is increased by approximately 72% due to the off-diagonal process.
- For a Bremsstrahlung beam with energy range 1.2 to 10 GeV, the total cross section is increased by approximately 43% due to the off-diagonal process.

## Context

This work extends studies of $\rho^0-\omega$ interference in dilepton production from photonuclear reactions (Alvensleben et al., Biggs et al.) to the $\gamma p$ reaction, motivated by recent measurements at Jefferson Laboratory (JLab) using a Bremsstrahlung photon beam of energy 1.2 to 5.4 GeV (preliminary results reported by Djalali et al.). The calculation uses the VMD model framework of Bauer et al. and the off-diagonal process for $\omega$ photoproduction via one pion exchange as developed by Friman et al., which was previously shown to describe low-energy $\omega$ photoproduction data. The results enable interpretation of the JLab data and clarify the energy regime where the pion exchange interaction must be included in the reaction mechanism.
