# Dissociation of 1 p quarkonium states in a hot QCD medium

> We extend the analysis of a very recent work (Phys. Rev. {\bf C 80}, 025210 (2009)) to study the dissociation phenomenon of 1p states of the charmonium and bottomonium spectra ($χ_c$ and $χ_b$) in a hot QCD medium. This study employed a medium modified heavy quark potential which is obtained by incorporating both perturbative and non-perturbative medium effects encoded in the dielectric function to the full Cornell potential. The medium modified potential has a quite different form (a long range Coulomb tail in addition to the usual Yukawa term) compared to the usual picture of Debye screening. We further study the flavor dependence of their binding energies and dissociation temperatures by employing the perturbative, non-perturbative, and the lattice parametrized form of the Debye masses. These results are consistent with the predictions of the current theoretical works.

# Dissociation of 1p quarkonium states in a hot QCD medium

## Key claim

The dissociation temperatures of the 1p states χ_c and χ_b are computed using a medium-modified Cornell potential that includes both perturbative and non-perturbative effects, but the results depend strongly on which form of the Debye mass is used; the non-perturbative Debye mass yields unrealistically small dissociation temperatures, suggesting that the current non-perturbative corrections may be incomplete.

## Method

The authors extend their earlier work (Phys. Rev. C 80, 025210, 2009) by computing binding energies for the 1p states χ_c and χ_b via a variational treatment of relativistic two-fermion bound states in QED, adapted to QCD by replacing the fine-structure constant with an effective screened charge (2σ/m_D²). The binding energy of χ_c (χ_b) is obtained by adding a spin-dependent correction term to the binding energy of ψ′ (Υ′), which itself comes from solving the Schrödinger equation with the medium-modified potential. Dissociation temperatures are determined from the condition |E(χ_c,χ_b)| ≈ T, using leading-order, lattice-parametrized, and non-perturbative forms of the Debye mass for pure gluonic, 2-flavor, and 3-flavor QCD.

## Key numbers

- String tension σ = 0.184 GeV²
- Critical temperatures: T_c = 270 MeV (pure gluonic), 203 MeV (2-flavor), 197 MeV (3-flavor QCD)
- χ_c dissociation temperature (upper bound, leading-order Debye mass): 1.10 T_c (pure QCD), 1.31 T_c (N_f=2), 1.26 T_c (N_f=3)
- χ_b dissociation temperature (upper bound, leading-order Debye mass): 1.38 T_c (pure QCD), 1.64 T_c (N_f=2), 1.57 T_c (N_f=3)
- χ_c dissociation temperature (upper bound, lattice-parametrized Debye mass): 0.77 T_c (pure QCD), 0.93 T_c (N_f=2), 0.90 T_c (N_f=3)
- χ_b dissociation temperature (upper bound, lattice-parametrized Debye mass): 0.98 T_c (pure QCD), 1.17 T_c (N_f=2), 1.14 T_c (N_f=3)
- With T_c = 192 MeV and lattice-parametrized Debye mass: χ_c dissociates at ≤ 0.9 T_c, χ_b at 1.2 T_c
- Lower bound on dissociation temperatures (using 3T instead of T) are approximately 30% lower than the upper bound values

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Phys. Rev. C 80, 025210, 2009) of ground and first excited quarkonium states to the 1p states χ_c and χ_b, which are important because approximately 30% of observed J/ψ yield comes from χ_c decays. The approach uses a medium-modified Cornell potential that differs from the usual Debye-screened Coulomb form by including a long-range Coulomb tail, motivated by the crossover nature of the QCD phase transition. The results are compared with spectral function calculations from potential models (Mocsy and Petreczky, 2007) and lattice studies (Datta et al., 2004; Aarts et al., 2007; Gert et al., 2007), showing good agreement when using the lattice-parametrized Debye mass but significant disagreement with the non-perturbative form, leaving open the question of how to properly incorporate non-perturbative effects into the Debye mass.
