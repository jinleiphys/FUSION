# Study of the $\bar K N$ system and coupled channels in a finite volume

> We investigate the $\bar KN$ and coupled channels system in a finite volume and study the properties of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance. We calculate the energy levels in a finite volume and solve the inverse problem of determining the resonance position in the infinite volume. We devise the best strategy of analysis to obtain the two poles of the $Λ(1405)$ in the infinite volume case, with sufficient precision to distinguish them.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that by fitting synthetic lattice QCD data (with a typical ±10 MeV error) to a parameterized chiral potential in a finite volume, one can reconstruct the two poles of the Λ(1405) resonance in infinite volume with sufficient precision to distinguish them, but the precision depends on the choice of box geometry and frame.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation with a chiral potential in a finite cubic box by replacing the continuous loop integral with a discrete sum over quantized momenta. They generate synthetic energy levels for the Kbar N and coupled channels system in symmetric boxes, asymmetric boxes, and moving frames, then fit a parameterized energy-dependent potential to these levels. Using the fitted potential, they analytically continue the T-matrix to the unphysical sheet to extract the pole positions of the Λ(1405) in infinite volume.

## Key numbers

- Pole 1 from symmetric box: energy region 1385-1433 MeV, half width 93-137 MeV
- Pole 2 from symmetric box: energy region 1416-1427 MeV, half width 11-20 MeV
- Pole 1 from asymmetric box: (1383-1407) - i(57-69) MeV
- Pole 2 from asymmetric box: (1425-1434) - i(25-35) MeV
- Pole 1 from moving frame: (1388-1418) - i(59-77) MeV
- Pole 2 from moving frame: (1412-1427) - i(16-34) MeV
- Reference chiral model poles (Oset and Ramos, 1998): 1390 - i66 MeV and 1426 - i16 MeV
- Synthetic data assigned a typical error of ±10 MeV

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume formalism of Luscher (1991) and the unitary chiral perturbation theory (UchiPT) models of Oset and Ramos (1998) to the Kbar N system with coupled channels. The method enables lattice QCD practitioners to extract the two-pole structure of the Λ(1405) from finite-volume energy level calculations, with asymmetric boxes and moving frames providing better precision than symmetric static boxes.
