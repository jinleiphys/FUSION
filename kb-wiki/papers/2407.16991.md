# Pauli nonlocality and the nucleon effective mass

> A study of the nucleon mean-field potential in nuclear matter (NM) is done within an extended Hartree-Fock (HF) formalism, using the CDM3Y6 density dependent version of the M3Y interaction which is associated with the nuclear incompressibility $K\simeq 252$ MeV. The momentum dependence of nucleon optical potential (OP) in NM at the saturation density $ρ_0$ is shown to be due mainly to its exchange term up to $k\approx 2$ fm$^{-1}$, so that the Pauli nonlocality is expected to be the main origin of the nucleon effective mass at low momenta. Because nucleons in neutron-rich NM at $ρ\approx ρ_0$ are either weakly bound or unbound by the in-medium nucleon-nucleon interaction, the determination of the effective mass of nucleon scattered on targets with neutron excess at low energies should be of interest for the mean-field studies of neutron star matter. For this purpose, the folding model is used to calculate the nonlocal nucleon OP for the optical model analysis of elastic nucleon scattering on $^{40,48}$Ca, $^{90}$Zr, and $^{208}$Pb targets at energies $E<50$ MeV, to probe the model reliability and validate the WKB local approximation to obtain the local folded nucleon OP. The nucleon effective mass $m^*$ is then carefully deduced from the momentum dependence of the local folded nucleon OP which is resulted from the Pauli nonlocality of the exchange term. The neutron-proton effective mass splitting determined at $ρ\approxρ_0$ from the central strength of the real folded nucleon OP for $^{48}$Ca, $^{90}$Zr, and $^{208}$Pb targets has been found to depend linearly on the neutron-proton asymmetry parameter as $m^*_{n-p}\approx (0.167\pm 0.018)δ$, in a good agreement with the recent empirical constraints.

## Key claim

The momentum dependence of the nucleon optical potential in nuclear matter at saturation density is due mainly to the exchange term up to approximately 2 fm\(^{-1}\), so that the Pauli nonlocality (antisymmetrization) is the main origin of the nucleon effective mass at low momenta; however, this holds only for energies below 50 MeV, because at higher energies the agreement with empirical data worsens and higher-order nucleon-nucleon correlations also contribute.

## Method

The authors performed an extended Hartree-Fock calculation of nuclear matter using the CDM3Y6 density-dependent version of the M3Y interaction, including the rearrangement term via the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem. They then used a folding model to compute both nonlocal and local (via WKB approximation) nucleon optical potentials for elastic scattering on \(^{40,48}\)Ca, \(^{90}\)Zr, and \(^{208}\)Pb targets at energies below 50 MeV, solving the integro-differential optical model equation with the calculable \(R\)-matrix method. The nucleon effective mass was deduced from the momentum dependence of the local folded optical potential, which arises from the Pauli nonlocality of the exchange term.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear incompressibility \(K \simeq 252\) MeV for the CDM3Y6 interaction.
- The momentum dependence of the nucleon optical potential in nuclear matter at saturation density \(\rho_0\) is dominated by the exchange term up to \(k \approx 2\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- The nucleon effective mass in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density from the extended HF calculation is \(m^*/m \approx 0.737\).
- The neutron-proton effective mass splitting determined at \(\rho \approx \rho_0\) from the folded optical potential for \(^{48}\)Ca, \(^{90}\)Zr, and \(^{208}\)Pb targets follows \(m^*_{n-p} \approx (0.167 \pm 0.018)\delta\), where \(\delta\) is the neutron-proton asymmetry parameter.
- The extended HF calculation of asymmetric nuclear matter using CDM3Y6 gives \(m^*_{n-p}(\rho_0,\delta) \approx (0.20 \pm 0.02)\delta\) at the Fermi momentum.
- The empirical constraint from the literature is \(m^*_{n-p}(\rho_0,\delta) \approx (0.27 \pm 0.25)\delta\).
- The imaginary part of the folded neutron optical potential required a renormalization factor of approximately 0.8 to describe neutron scattering data.

## Context

This work extends the Hartree-Fock studies of nuclear matter using the CDM3Yn interactions (Khoa 1993, 1995, 1997) and the nonlocal folding model for nucleon optical potentials (Loan 2020) to determine the nucleon effective mass from finite-nuclei scattering data. The results for the neutron-proton effective mass splitting agree with empirical constraints from Li (2013) and are complementary to mean-field calculations of asymmetric nuclear matter. The approach enables the determination of the radial- and isospin dependence of the nucleon effective mass near saturation density, which is relevant for studies of the nuclear symmetry energy, neutron star matter, and the equation of state.
