# New near-threshold mesons

> We show that under a number of rather plausible assumptions QCD spectrum may contain a number of mesons which have not been predicted or observed. Such states will have the quantum numbers of two existing mesons and masses very close to the dissociation threshold into the two mesons. Moreover, at least one of the two mesonic constituents itself must be very close to its dissociation threshold. In particular, one might expect the existence of loosely bound systems of $D$ and $\D$; similarly, $K$ and $\ff$, $\bar{K}$ and $\ff$, $K$ and $å$ and $\bar{K}$ and $å$ can be bound. The mechanism for binding in these cases is the S-wave kaon exchange. The nearness of one of the constituents to its decay threshold into a kaon plus a remainder, implies that the range of the kaon exchange force becomes abnormally long--significantly longer than $1/m_K$ which greatly aids the binding.

# New near-threshold mesons

## Key claim

Under plausible assumptions about the QCD spectrum, there may exist mesons with the quantum numbers of two existing mesons and masses very close to the dissociation threshold into those two mesons, provided that at least one of the two mesonic constituents is itself very close to its own dissociation threshold. The mechanism for binding is S-wave kaon exchange, whose range becomes abnormally long when one constituent is near its kaon decay threshold.

## Method

The authors derive an attractive Yukawa-like potential from one-kaon exchange between two mesons, where the energy transfer epsilon is the small binding energy of the kaon to one of the constituents. They solve the nonrelativistic Schrodinger equation with this potential for the systems |K f0(980)>, |K a0(980)>, |anti-K f0(980)>, |anti-K a0(980)>, and |D D*_sJ(2317)>. They also consider cutoff versions of the potential at short distances (R = 0.3, 0.5, 0.7 fm) to account for the finite spatial extent of the mesons.

## Key numbers

- The range parameter kappa_1 for the |K f0> and |K a0> systems lies between 100 and 140 MeV.
- The range parameter kappa_2 for the |D D*_sJ> system lies between 200 and 220 MeV.
- For the |K f0> system with coupling alpha = -0.6 and kappa = 100 MeV, the binding energy is 17.3 MeV and the root-mean-square radius is 17.1 fm.
- For the |K f0> system with coupling alpha = -0.6 and kappa = 140 MeV, the binding energy is 8.3 MeV and the root-mean-square radius is 26.7 fm.
- For the |D D*_sJ> system with coupling alpha = -0.6 and kappa = 200 MeV, the binding energy is 90.0 MeV and the root-mean-square radius is 5.0 fm.
- For the |D D*_sJ> system with coupling alpha = -0.6 and kappa = 220 MeV, the binding energy is 82.8 MeV and the root-mean-square radius is 5.3 fm.
- With a cutoff at R = 0.5 fm, the |D D*_sJ> system with alpha = -0.6 and kappa = 200 MeV has binding energy 27.9 MeV and root-mean-square radius 8.5 fm.
- With a cutoff at R = 0.5 fm, the |D D*_sJ> system with alpha = -0.6 and kappa = 220 MeV has binding energy 23.3 MeV and root-mean-square radius 8.9 fm.
- The coupling constant g^2_{K anti-K f0}/(4 pi) is 0.6 GeV^2, giving a dimensionless coupling alpha_{K anti-K f0} of approximately -0.6.
- The reduced mass for case (1) (K with f0 or a0) is approximately 330 MeV; for case (2) (D with D*_sJ) it is approximately 1030 MeV.
- The D*_sJ(2317) state lies about 40 MeV below the KD threshold; the f0(980) and a0(980) lie about 10-20 MeV below the K anti-K threshold.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of near-threshold states such as the D*_sJ(2317) discovered at BABAR, CLEO, and Belle, and the f0(980) and a0(980) mesons near the K anti-K threshold. The authors argue that these states, regardless of whether they are interpreted as quarkonium, tetraquark, or molecular states, can serve as constituents for further loosely bound molecular systems via kaon exchange. The mechanism parallels earlier work by Tornqvist on possible D D* bound states, but with S-wave rather than P-wave couplings, avoiding the compensation between range and strength. The predicted |D D*_sJ> state would be manifestly exotic with strangeness S=1 and charm C=2.
