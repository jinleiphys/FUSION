# Matching pion-nucleon Roy-Steiner equations to chiral perturbation theory

> We match the results for the subthreshold parameters of pion-nucleon scattering obtained from a solution of Roy-Steiner equations to chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order, to extract the pertinent low-energy constants including a comprehensive analysis of systematic uncertainties and correlations. We study the convergence of the chiral series by investigating the chiral expansion of threshold parameters up to the same order and discuss the role of the Δ(1232) resonance in this context. Results for the low-energy constants are also presented in the counting scheme usually applied in chiral nuclear effective field theory, where they serve as crucial input to determine the long-range part of the nucleon-nucleon potential as well as three-nucleon forces.

## Key claim

The authors match subthreshold parameters of pion-nucleon scattering from a Roy-Steiner equation solution to chiral perturbation theory at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO), extracting low-energy constants (LECs) with a full covariance matrix; however, the convergence of the chiral series for threshold parameters is slow, and the LECs determined from the subthreshold region do not necessarily ensure convergence in the full low-energy domain, especially without explicit inclusion of the Δ(1232) resonance.

## Method

The authors use the 13 subthreshold parameters (d_mn±, b_mn±) from a previous Roy-Steiner solution (Hoferichter 2015) as input, equating them to their chiral expansion in chiral perturbation theory up to N3LO. This reduces the LEC determination to an algebraic inversion problem, using the subthreshold expansion where the amplitude (after Born-term subtraction) is a polynomial in ν and t. The uncertainty propagation uses a 13×13 covariance matrix from the Roy-Steiner analysis, which includes variations in the matching point, partial-wave truncation, the πN coupling constant, and scattering length constraints from pionic atoms.

## Key numbers

- d00+ = -1.36(3) Mπ-1, d00- = 1.41(1) Mπ-2 (subthreshold parameters from Roy-Steiner solution)
- c1 = -1.11(3) GeV-1, c2 = 3.13(3) GeV-1, c3 = -5.61(6) GeV-1, c4 = 4.26(4) GeV-1 at N3LO in standard counting
- d1+d2 = 7.42(8) GeV-2, d3 = -10.46(10) GeV-2, d5 = 0.59(5) GeV-2 at N3LO in standard counting
- e14 = 0.89(4) GeV-3, e15 = -0.97(6) GeV-3, e16 = -2.61(3) GeV-3, e17 = 0.01(6) GeV-3, e18 = -4.20(5) GeV-3 at N3LO in standard counting
- S-wave scattering lengths from pionic atoms: a0++ = -0.9(1.4) × 10-3 Mπ-1, a0+- = 85.4(9) × 10-3 Mπ-1 (Baru 2010, Baru 2011)
- Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy gives d18 = -0.44(24) GeV-2

## Context

This work extends the Roy-Steiner equation program for πN scattering (Ditsche 2012, Hoferichter 2012, Hoferichter 2015) by matching to chiral perturbation theory, analogous to how Roy equations were used for ππ LECs (Colangelo 2001). It improves upon earlier matching attempts (Buettiker 1999) by using subthreshold parameters from the Roy-Steiner solution alone, without additional input from the physical region. The extracted LECs serve as crucial input for nuclear chiral effective field theory, determining the long-range part of the nucleon-nucleon potential and three-nucleon forces (Weinberg 1990, Weinberg 1991, Weinberg 1992, Epelbaum 2008, Machleidt 2011), and the results enable uncertainty quantification in future ChEFT calculations (Epelbaum 2014, Furnstahl 2015).
