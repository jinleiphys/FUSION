# The Energy of n Identical Bosons in a Finite Volume at O(L^{-7})

## Key claim

The ground-state energy shift of n identical bosons with short-range interactions in a periodic cubic volume of side length L is computed at order L^{-7} in the large-volume expansion, extending the previous O(L^{-6}) result. This enables a refined determination of the three-pion (pi+ pi+ pi+) interaction from lattice QCD calculations, but the result is scheme-dependent and requires renormalization through the three-body coefficient eta_3(mu).

## Method

The calculation uses standard Schrodinger perturbation theory with a Hamiltonian that includes two-body interactions parameterized by the scattering length a and effective range r, a momentum-independent three-body interaction eta_3(mu), and leading relativistic corrections proportional to M^{-3}. Divergences from loop diagrams are regulated using dimensional regularization and renormalized order-by-order in the expansion, preserving the power counting. The method is equivalent to pionless effective field theory (EFT) describing low-energy nucleon-nucleon interactions, modified for systems with natural scattering lengths.

## Key numbers

- Geometric constants entering the energy shift: I = -8.9136329, J = 16.532316, K = 8.4019240, L = 6.9458079
- Three-body geometric constants: T_0 = -4116.2338, T_1 = 450.6392
- Scheme-dependent constant in MS scheme: S_MS = -185.12506
- Auxiliary sum S_1 = 92.42215, defined as a triple sum over nonzero integer vectors
- The leading relativistic contribution to the energy shift appears at O(L^{-7}) and is proportional to (6 pi a^3)/(M^3 L^7) (n+3) I
- The three-body contribution includes a term (64 pi a^4)/M (3 sqrt(3) - 4 pi) log(mu L) that depends on the renormalization scale mu

## Context

This work extends the O(L^{-6}) calculation of Beane et al. (2007) for n identical bosons in a finite volume, which built upon the classic works of Huang (1957), Wu (1959), and others. The result for n=3 agrees with the independent computation by Tan (2007). The O(L^{-7}) correction will allow refinement of extractions of the three-pion interaction from lattice QCD calculations, such as those performed by Beane et al. (2007) at pion masses of approximately 290 and 350 MeV in a roughly (2.5 fm)^3 volume, where the three-body parameter was found to be consistent with naive dimensional analysis expectations of order 1/(m_pi f_pi^4).
