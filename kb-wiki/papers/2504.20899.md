# Timelike form factor for the anomalous process $γ^\ast π\rightarrow ππ$

> The form factor $F_{3π}(s,t,u)$ for the anomalous process $γ^\ast π\rightarrow ππ$ is calculated in the isospin limit for several values of the light current-quark mass (i.e., the pion mass) using Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations. Beyond a quark interaction kernel representing gluon-mediated interactions, leading beyond-rainbow-ladder effects at low energies are incorporated by back-coupling pions as explicit degrees of freedom. Building upon an earlier calculation of the quark-photon vertex that captures the branch cut associated with the two-pion threshold and the rho meson resonance, the form factor $F_{3π}(s,t,u)$ is determined for timelike Mandelstam s. In particular, predictions are made for the kinematics relevant for the Primakoff reaction studied with COMPASS/AMBER at CERN.

## Key claim
The form factor \(F_{3\pi}(s,t,u)\) for \(\gamma^\ast \pi \rightarrow \pi \pi\) is calculated in the timelike region using Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations with beyond-rainbow-ladder effects, but the \(\rho\)-meson decay channel into two pions is not fully self-consistent in the \(\gamma \pi \to q\bar{q}\) vertex, requiring a correction procedure that limits reliability to Mandelstam \(s\) below \(m_\rho^2\).

## Method
The quark propagator, pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, and dressed quark-photon vertex are solved from their respective Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations using a Maris-Tandy effective interaction with parameters \(\Lambda = 0.78\) GeV and \(\eta = 1.65 \pm 0.05\). Beyond-rainbow-ladder effects are incorporated by back-coupling pions as explicit degrees of freedom in the quark DSE and interaction kernel. The \(\gamma^\ast \pi \rightarrow \pi \pi\) amplitude is obtained from the \(\gamma^\ast \pi \to q\bar{q}\) vertex via an inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation, with the full form factor given by the sum of three permutations \(F_{3\pi}(s,t,u) = f(s,t,u) + f(t,u,s) + f(u,s,t)\). Correction factors extracted from the timelike electromagnetic pion form factor are applied to account for the missing two-pion exchange in the resummed ladders.

## Key numbers
- Anomalous form factor at the soft point for physical pion mass: \(F_{3\pi}^\text{sp} = 10.14\ \text{GeV}^{-3}\) at \(m_\pi = 139\) MeV and \(f_\pi = 91.9\) MeV.
- Extrapolated chiral limit value: \(F_{3\pi}^\text{anomaly} \approx 10.66\ \text{GeV}^{-3}\).
- Chiral anomaly value: \(F_{3\pi}^\text{anomaly} = e/(4\pi^2 f_\pi^3) = 9.76(3)\ \text{GeV}^{-3}\) using \(f_\pi = 92.28(10)\) MeV.
- Computed \(\rho\)-meson mass: \(m_\rho = 748(5)\) MeV; experimental value: \(775.3(3)\) MeV.
- Computed \(\rho\)-meson decay constant: \(f_\rho = 159(2)\) MeV; experimental value: \(156(1)\) MeV.
- Total change from chiral limit to physical point: normalised \(F_{3\pi}\) increases by \(2.8\% \pm 0.5\% \pm 0.2\% \pm 0.2\%\).

## Context
This work extends previous DSE/BSE calculations of the \(\gamma^\ast \pi \rightarrow \pi \pi\) form factor in the spacelike regime (Alkofer 1995, Cotanch 2003, Bistrovic 1999, Xing 2024) into the timelike region relevant for the COMPASS/AMBER Primakoff reaction at CERN. It builds on the authors' earlier calculation of the quark-photon vertex that captures the two-pion threshold branch cut and \(\rho\)-meson resonance (Miramontes 2019), and on their timelike pion form factor work (Miramontes 2021). The results enable comparison with ongoing COMPASS data analysis (Friedrich 2023, Ecker 2023) and with dispersive predictions (Niehus 2021, Hoferichter 2025), and contribute to the data-driven evaluation of the muon anomalous magnetic moment \((g-2)_\mu\).
