# Looking for a hidden-charm pentaquark state with strangeness $S=-1$ from $Ξ^-_b$ decay into $J/ψK^- Λ$

> Assuming that the recently observed hidden-charm pentaquark state, $P_c(4450)$, is of molecular nature as predicted in the unitary approach, we propose to study the decay of $Ξ^-_b\rightarrow J/ψK^- Λ$ to search for the strangeness counterpart of the $P_c(4450)$. There are three ingredients in the decay mechanism: the weak decay mechanism, the hadronization mechanism, and the finite state interactions in the meson-baryon system of strangeness $S=-2$ and isospin $I=1/2$ and of the $J/ψΛ$. All these have been tested extensively. As a result, we provide a genuine prediction of the differential cross section where a strangeness hidden-charm pentaquark state, the counterpart of the $P_c(4450)$, can be clearly seen. The decay rate is estimated to be of similar magnitude as the $Λ_b^0\rightarrow J/ψK^- p$ observed by the LHCb collaboration.

## Key claim

Assuming the hidden-charm pentaquark state \(P_c(4450)\) observed by LHCb is of molecular nature, the authors predict that a strangeness \(S=-1\) counterpart should be observable in \(\Xi^-_b \rightarrow J/\psi K^- \Lambda\) decay, with the caveat that the signal strength depends strongly on the coupling of the hidden-charm state to \(J/\psi\Lambda\), which is taken from prior unitary model calculations without fine-tuning.

## Method

The decay mechanism is decomposed into three steps: the weak decay of the \(b\) quark in \(\Xi^-_b\) (quark content \(bds\)) into a \(c\) quark via a \(W^-\) boson, followed by hadronization of the \(s\) quark and the spectator \(ds\) pair into meson-baryon channels (\(\bar{K}\Lambda\), \(\bar{K}\Sigma\), \(\eta\Xi\)); and final-state interactions in the strangeness \(S=-2\), isospin \(I=1/2\) coupled-channel system described by a chiral unitary model, plus a Breit-Wigner parameterization for the \(J/\psi\Lambda\) interaction representing the predicted pentaquark state. The invariant mass distributions are computed from the full amplitude including tree-level and rescattering contributions.

## Key numbers

- The predicted strange pentaquark mass is estimated as \(M_R = 4650\) MeV, obtained by adding \(\Delta M = 200\) MeV (rough average of \(\Lambda N\) and \(\Sigma N\) mass differences) to the \(P_c(4450)\) mass.
- The width of the predicted state is taken as \(\Gamma_R = 10\) MeV, based on prior unitary model pole positions of \(\sqrt{s}=4368-2.8i\) and \(\sqrt{s}=4547-6.4i\) MeV for the strangeness \(-1\), isospin 0 channel.
- The coupling \(g_{J/\psi\Lambda}\) is taken in the range \(0.5 \sim 0.6\) from Wu et al. (2010).
- The decay rate of \(\Xi^-_b \rightarrow J/\psi K^- \Lambda\) is estimated to be of similar magnitude as \(\Lambda_b^0 \rightarrow J/\psi K^- p\) observed by LHCb, with a relative branching ratio \(\Gamma(\Xi^-_b \rightarrow P_\Lambda K^-)/\Gamma(\Lambda_b^0 \rightarrow P_p K^-) = 0.408\) for spin 3/2 and 0.343 for spin 5/2 from Cheng et al. (2015).

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of the \(P_c(4450)\) state, as proposed in the unitary approach of Wu et al. (2010) and applied to \(\Lambda_b^0 \rightarrow J/\psi K^- p\) by Roca et al. (2015), to the strangeness sector. It predicts a strange hidden-charm pentaquark that can be searched for in \(\Xi^-_b\) decays at LHCb, complementing photoproduction studies by Wang et al. (2015) and Kubarovsky et al. (2015) and other decay-mode analyses by Cheng et al. (2015) and Li et al. (2015). Observation or exclusion of this state would test the unitary model's predictions and help clarify the nature of the \(P_c\) states.
