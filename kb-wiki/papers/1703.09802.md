# Absorptive corrections for vector mesons. Matching to complex mass scheme and longitudinal corrections

> Unstable spin-1 particles are properly described by including absorptive corrections to the electromagnetic vertex and propagator, without breaking the electromagnetic gauge invariance. We show that the modified propagator can be set into a complex mass form, provided the mass and the width parameters, which are properly defined at the pole position, are replaced by energy dependent functions fulfilling the same requirements at the pole. We exemplify the case for the $K^*(892)$ vector meson, where the mass function deviates around 2 MeV from the $Kπ$ threshold to the pole position. The absorptive correction depends on the mass of the particles in the loop. For vector mesons, whose main decay is into two pseudoscalar mesons ($PP'$), the flavor symmetry breaking induces a correction to the longitudinal part of the propagator. Considering the $τ^- \to K_Sπ^-ν_τ$ decay, we illustrate these corrections by obtaining the modified vector and scalar form factors. The $K_Sπ^-$ spectrum is described considering the $K^*(892)$ and $K^{'*}(1410)$ vectors and one scalar particle. Nonetheless, for this case, the correction to the scalar form factor is found to be negligible.

## Key claim

The absorptive-corrected propagator for unstable vector mesons can be cast into a complex mass form only if the mass and width parameters are replaced by energy-dependent functions that match the pole-position values; for the K*(892) meson, the mass function deviates by around 2 MeV from the Kπ threshold to the pole position.

## Method

The authors start from the one-loop absorptive (imaginary-part) corrections to the vector-meson propagator and vertex, computed for a boson loop of two pseudoscalar mesons (PP'). They rewrite the propagator in terms of energy-dependent functions γ(q²) and β(q²) and show that it can be factorized into a complex-mass-like form times a global normalization factor. They then apply this formalism to the τ⁻ → K_Sπ⁻ν_τ decay, constructing the vector and scalar form factors from the modified propagators of the K*(892) and K'*(1410) resonances plus one scalar resonance, and fit the Belle Kπ spectrum data.

## Key numbers

- For the K*(892), the mass at threshold (m) is 893.93 MeV, while the experimental pole mass (M₀) is 891.66 MeV; the difference is approximately 2 MeV.
- The fitted K*(892) mass from the τ⁻ → K_Sπ⁻ν_τ spectrum is 0.8953 ± 0.0002 GeV (both with and without scalar phase).
- The fitted K*(892) width is 0.0475 ± 0.0005 GeV (both cases).
- The fitted K'*(1410) mass is 1.41 ± 0.04 GeV; its width is 0.42 ± 0.03 GeV (without phase) or 0.43 ± 0.03 GeV (with phase).
- The fitted scalar (K₀*) mass is 0.75 ± 0.02 GeV (without phase) or 0.69 ± 0.01 GeV (with phase); its width is 0.15 ± 0.04 GeV (without phase) or 0.06 ± 0.02 GeV (with phase).
- The χ²/dof values for the fits are 92/99 (without scalar phase) and 89/99 (with scalar phase).
- The longitudinal correction parameter β₀ at the K*(892) pole is -0.03; the transverse parameter γ₀ is 0.06.

## Context

This work extends the boson-loop absorptive correction scheme of Baur, Argyres, Beuthe, and collaborators (1990s) and the complex mass scheme of the 2000s, showing their equivalence when energy-dependent mass and width functions are used. It applies the formalism to the τ → Kπν decay, which has been studied experimentally by Belle (2007) and theoretically by Jamin, Moussallam, Boito, Escribano, and others (2001-2014) to investigate a possible scalar substructure near 0.7 GeV. The authors find that for this particular decay the longitudinal correction to the scalar form factor is negligible, but they suggest that systems with stronger flavor symmetry breaking (e.g., D* mesons) or scalar-dominated processes may be more sensitive to these corrections.
