# Lattice Nucleon Isovector Unpolarized Parton Distribution in the Physical-Continuum Limit

> We present the first lattice-QCD calculation of the nucleon isovector unpolarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) at the physical-continuum limit using Large-Momentum Effective Theory (LaMET). The lattice results are calculated using ensembles with multiple sea pion masses with the lightest one around 135~MeV, 3 lattice spacings $a\in[0.06,0.12]$~fm, and multiple volumes with $M_πL$ ranging 3.3 to 5.5. We perform a simultaneous chiral-continuum extrapolation to obtain RI/MOM renormalized nucleon matrix elements with various Wilson-link displacements in the continuum limit at physical pion mass. Then, we apply one-loop perturbative matching to the quasi-PDFs to obtain the lightcone PDFs. We find the lattice-spacing dependence to be much larger than the dependence on pion mass and lattice volume for these LaMET matrix elements. Our physical-continuum limit unpolarized isovector nucleon PDFs are found to be consistent with global-PDF results.

## Key claim

This work presents the first lattice-QCD calculation of the nucleon isovector unpolarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) that simultaneously extrapolates to the physical pion mass and the continuum limit, using Large-Momentum Effective Theory (LaMET). The authors find that the lattice-spacing dependence is much larger than the dependence on pion mass and lattice volume for these matrix elements, and the resulting PDFs are consistent with global-PDF results within two standard deviations.

## Method

The calculation uses clover valence fermions on 2+1+1 flavor HISQ ensembles generated by the MILC Collaboration, with three lattice spacings (0.06, 0.09, 0.12 fm), multiple pion masses (135 to 318 MeV), and multiple volumes (M_pi L from 3.3 to 5.5). Bare nonlocal matrix elements with Wilson-line displacements are computed at nucleon boost momenta of approximately 2.2 and 2.6 GeV, then renormalized nonperturbatively using the RI/MOM scheme. A simultaneous chiral-continuum extrapolation is performed using linear and quadratic ansatze in lattice spacing and pion mass, with Akaike information criterion averaging over fit forms, followed by one-loop perturbative matching to obtain lightcone PDFs.

## Key numbers

* Lightest sea pion mass: approximately 135 MeV
* Lattice spacings: a in [0.06, 0.12] fm (three values)
* M_pi L range: 3.3 to 5.5
* Nucleon boost momenta used for final results: approximately 2.2 and 2.6 GeV
* Nucleon isovector moments <x^n>: approximately 0.2, 0.06, and 0.04 for n=1, 2, 3 respectively
* RI/MOM renormalization scale: mu_R = 3.8 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous lattice PDF calculations at physical pion mass by Lin et al. (2018) and Alexandrou et al. (2018), which used single lattice spacings and did not account for continuum extrapolation or twist-4 systematics. It also builds on finite-volume studies by Lin et al. (2019) and chiral perturbation theory work by Liu et al. (2020) showing finite-volume effects are negligible for current precision. The results enable future lattice PDF calculations with fully controlled systematics, particularly for reducing twist-4 uncertainties and improving determination of small-x and antiquark PDFs.
