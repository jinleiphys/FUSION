# Towards a quantitative understanding of high $p_T$ flow harmonics

> In this proceedings I briefly review the recent progress achieved on the calculation of $v_n$ at high $p_T$ via the coupling of a jet energy loss model with full event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics. It is shown that that this framework can simultaneously describe experimental data for $R_{AA}$, $v_2$, and $v_3$ at high $p_T$. High $p_T$ $v_2$ is found to be approximately linearly correlated with the soft $v_2$ on an event-by-event basis, which opens up a new way to correlate soft and hard observables in heavy ion collisions.

# Towards a quantitative understanding of high \( p_T \) flow harmonics

## Key claim
The coupling of event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics with a jet energy loss model can simultaneously describe experimental data for \( R_{AA} \), \( v_2 \), and \( v_3 \) at high \( p_T \) in heavy ion collisions, with high \( p_T \) \( v_2 \) found to be approximately linearly correlated with the soft \( v_2 \) on an event-by-event basis. This correlation arises because both soft and hard elliptic flows are generated by the same fluctuating initial spatial eccentricity \( \varepsilon_2 \), though the description is limited to the specific energy loss model and hydrodynamic parameters used.

## Method
The v-USPhydro code was used to model the expanding QGP fluid in PbPb collisions at \( \sqrt{s} = 2.76 \) TeV, solving viscous hydrodynamic equations event-by-event with MCGlauber and MCKLN initial conditions for the \( 20-30\% \) centrality class. The BBMG jet-energy loss model was then applied on the event-by-event hydrodynamic background, where the parton energy loss per unit length \( dE/dL \) depends on the local temperature field, path length, and flow factor, using the pQCD-case parameters (\( a=0, z=1, c=3, q=0 \)) giving a linear path length dependence \( dE/dL \sim L \). High \( p_T \) azimuthal coefficients were computed via a soft-hard 2-particle correlator that correctly accounts for how the measurement is performed.

## Key numbers
- Shear viscosity over entropy density ratio: \( \eta/s = 0.08 \) for MCGlauber initial conditions and \( \eta/s = 0.11 \) for MCKLN initial conditions in mid-central \( 20-30\% \) centrality PbPb collisions at \( \sqrt{s} = 2.76 \) TeV.
- The energy loss rate parameters correspond to the pQCD-case: \( a=0, z=1, c=3, q=0 \), giving \( dE/dL \sim L \) (linear path length dependence).
- Results are compared to experimental data for \( \pi^0 \) \( R_{AA}(p_T) \), \( v_2^{exp}(p_T) \), and \( v_3^{exp}(p_T) \) in mid-central \( \sqrt{s} = 2.76 \) TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Noronha-Hostler (2016) and Betz (2016) by demonstrating that realistic event-by-event hydrodynamic modeling, rather than unrealistic event-averaged backgrounds, is essential for solving the high \( p_T \) \( R_{AA} \otimes v_2 \) puzzle. It validates the idea from Gyulassy (2000) that jet energy loss determines the azimuthal anisotropy of the QGP at high \( p_T \). The framework enables new observables involving correlations between soft and hard flow harmonics, such as 4-particle cumulants of high \( p_T \) elliptic flow, and can be extended to test different energy loss models (e.g., linear vs. quadratic path length dependence) using new LHC data at \( \sqrt{s} = 5.02 \) TeV.
