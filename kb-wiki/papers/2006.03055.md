# Collinear expansion for color singlet cross sections

> We demonstrate how to efficiently expand cross sections for color-singlet production at hadron colliders around the kinematic limit of all final state radiation being collinear to one of the incoming hadrons. This expansion is systematically improvable and applicable to a large class of physical observables. We demonstrate the viability of this technique by obtaining the first two terms in the collinear expansion of the rapidity distribution of the gluon fusion Higgs boson production cross section at next-to-next-to leading order (NNLO) in QCD perturbation theory. Furthermore, we illustrate how this technique is used to extract universal building blocks of scattering cross section like the N-jettiness and transverse momentum beam function at NNLO.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates a systematic, improvable collinear expansion technique for color-singlet production cross sections at hadron colliders, valid in the kinematic limit where all final-state radiation is collinear to one incoming hadron. The authors show that the first two terms of this expansion suffice to reproduce the NNLO rapidity distribution for gluon-fusion Higgs production, but the method is presented as a general framework rather than a complete all-order solution.

## Method

The authors expand the hadronic cross section around the strict collinear limit by separating the partonic coefficient function into contributions from collinear and non-collinear modes using generalized scaling arguments. They then extract the bare beam function matching kernels from the collinear limit of the partonic cross section, apply UV renormalization via the MSbar scheme, subtract IR divergences with PDF counterterms, and remove EFT UV divergences with beam-function counterterms. The method is implemented using reverse unitarity and integration-by-parts (IBP) identities to evaluate phase-space integrals, and is applied to both the N-jettiness (Tau_N) and transverse-momentum (q_T) beam functions at NNLO.

## Key numbers

* The first two terms in the collinear expansion of the gluon fusion Higgs boson production cross section at NNLO in QCD perturbation theory are obtained for the rapidity distribution.
* The N-jettiness beam function matching kernel is computed at one loop through O(eps^4) and at two loops through O(eps^2), as required for three-loop beam function calculations.
* The transverse-momentum beam function is computed using the exponential regulator of Li (2016) with a factor exp[2 tau e^{-gamma_E} k_i^0] inserted into each real emission phase space.
* The renormalized NNLO results for the Tau_N beam function agree with Gaunt (2014) and Gaunt (2014), and higher-order epsilon terms agree with Baranowski (2020).

## Context

This work extends the collinear expansion method previously applied to inclusive cross sections, now adapted for differential observables like rapidity distributions and beam functions. It provides an alternative to direct SCET matrix-element calculations of beam functions (Stewart 2010, Gaunt 2014) and to the splitting-function approach of Ritzmann (2014). The technique enables extraction of universal building blocks such as N-jettiness and transverse-momentum beam functions at NNLO, and is used in companion papers (Ebert 2020) to compute these beam functions at N^3LO.
