# Systematics of one-quasiparticle configurations in neutron-rich Sr, Zr, and Mo odd isotopes with the Gogny energy density functional

> The systematics of one-quasiparticle configurations in neutron-rich Sr, Zr, and Mo odd isotopes is studied within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov plus Equal Filling Approximation method preserving both axial and time reversal symmetries. Calculations based on the Gogny energy density functional with both the standard D1S parametrization and the new D1M incarnation of this functional are included in our analysis. The nuclear deformation and shape coexistence inherent to this mass region are shown to play a relevant role in the understanding of the spectroscopic features of the ground and low-lying one-quasineutron states.

## Key claim

The Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov plus Equal Filling Approximation (HFB-EFA) method, using the Gogny energy density functional with both D1S and D1M parametrizations, can qualitatively reproduce the systematics of ground and low-lying one-quasineutron states in neutron-rich Sr, Zr, and Mo odd isotopes, though the authors caution that one cannot expect _a priori_ to reproduce spectroscopic properties in detail from global EDFs.

## Method

The authors performed constrained HFB calculations to generate potential energy curves (PECs) for even-even Sr, Zr, and Mo isotopes with neutron numbers N=52-68, using an axially symmetric harmonic oscillator basis with 13 major shells. For each odd-N nucleus, they carried out up to 288 separate HFB-EFA calculations (8 K-values from 1/2 to 15/2, 3 false vacua for prolate/oblate/spherical shapes, and 12 quasiparticle starting states) to identify the lowest-energy one-quasineutron configurations within a 1 MeV energy window. Both the standard D1S and the newer D1M parametrizations of the Gogny force were employed, with the kinetic energy of center-of-mass motion subtracted and exchange Coulomb energy treated in the Slater approximation.

## Key numbers

- The D1M parametrization achieves an energy root mean square deviation for experimentally known 2149 masses of only 0.8 MeV, which is much better than D1S
- The spin-orbit strength of D1M is 12% smaller than that of D1S
- For each nucleus, up to 8 × 3 × 12 = 288 HFB-EFA calculations were performed in the worst case (three false vacua)
- In 93Sr, the 5/2+ experimental ground state appears at 0.1 MeV in the calculations
- In 95Sr and 97Sr, the 1/2+ experimental ground states are found as oblate excited configurations at E=0.25 MeV and E=1.0 MeV, respectively
- The 5/2- band head at 0.45 MeV in 101Zr and the ground state in 103Zr correspond to the same [532]5/2- single-particle configuration

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shape coexistence and shape transitions in the A~100 mass region (Skalski 1995, Xu 2002) and compares with experimental data from Buchinger (1993), Mach (1995), Urban (2001), Campbell (2002), Hua (2004), and Goodin (2009). The study tests whether the D1M parametrization (Goriely 2009) can supersede the older D1S parametrization (Berger 1991) for odd-mass nuclei, following earlier comparisons for even-even nuclei that showed D1M essentially keeps the same nuclear structure predictive power. The results enable future comparisons with experimental spectroscopic data for neutron-rich nuclei relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis modeling.
