# Correlations among symmetry energy elements in Skyrme models

> Motivated by the interrelationships found between the various symmetry energy elements of the energy density functionals (EDF) based on the Skyrme forces, possible correlations among them are explored. A total of 237 Skyrme EDFs are used for this purpose. As some of these EDFs yield values of a few nuclear observables far off from the present acceptable range, studies are done also with a subset of 162 EDFs that comply with a conservative set of constraints on the values of nuclear matter incompressibility coefficient, effective mass of the nucleon and the isovector splitting of effective nucleon masses to see the enhancement of the correlation strength, if any. The curvature parameter $K^0_{sym}$ and the skewness parameter $Q^0_{sym}$ of the symmetry energy are found to be very well correlated with the linear combination of the symmetry energy coefficient and its density derivative $L_0$ . The isovector splitting of the effective nucleon mass, however, displays a somewhat meaningful correlation with a linear combination of the symmetry energy, its slope and its curvature parameter.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that within the Skyrme energy density functional framework, the curvature parameter \(K_{sym}^0\) of the symmetry energy is robustly correlated with the linear combination \((3C_2^0 - L_0)\), where \(C_2^0\) is the symmetry energy coefficient and \(L_0\) is its slope, but this correlation is model-dependent and its strength depends on the specific set of Skyrme functionals used and on whether empirical constraints on nuclear matter properties are applied.

## Method

The authors used 237 Skyrme energy density functionals (EDFs) compiled by Dutra et al. (2012) and derived analytical expressions for the symmetry energy parameters \(C_2^0\), \(L_0\), \(K_{sym}^0\), \(Q_{sym}^0\), and the isovector splitting of effective nucleon mass \(\Delta m_0^*\) within the Skyrme formalism. They performed linear regression analyses to quantify correlations among these parameters, first using all 237 EDFs and then a restricted subset of 162 EDFs that satisfy conservative constraints on the nuclear matter incompressibility coefficient (\(K_0 = 230 \pm 30\) MeV), the effective mass of symmetric nuclear matter (\(m_0^*/m = 0.85 \pm 0.15\)), and the isovector splitting of effective masses (\(|\Delta m_0^*| < 1\)).

## Key numbers

- Correlation coefficient between \(K_{sym}^0\) and \((3C_2^0 - L_0)\) for all 237 Skyrme EDFs: \(r = -0.926\); for the selected set of 162 EDFs: \(r = -0.931\).
- Best-fit line for \(K_{sym}^0\) vs. \((3C_2^0 - L_0)\) for all EDFs: \(K_{sym}^0 = (-5.51 \pm 0.15)(3C_2^0 - L_0) + (106.84 \pm 3.37)\) MeV.
- Correlation coefficient between \(K_{sym}^0\) and \(L_0\) alone for all EDFs: \(r = 0.808\).
- Correlation coefficient between \(Q_{sym}^0\) and \((3C_2^0 - L_0)\) for the selected set: \(r = 0.864\); for all EDFs: \(r = 0.6\).
- Correlation coefficient between \(Q_{sym}^0\) and a linear combination of \(K_{sym}^0\) and \((3C_2^0 - L_0)\) for the selected set: \(r = 0.965\).
- Correlation coefficient between \(\Delta m_0^*\) and a linear combination of \(K_{sym}^0\) and \((3C_2^0 - L_0)\) for the selected set: \(r = 0.790\); for all EDFs: \(r = 0.286\).
- Estimated value of \(K_{sym}^0\) using fiducial values \(C_2^0 = 32\) MeV and \(L_0 = 59\) MeV: \(-97.0 \pm 6.5\) MeV (all EDFs) and \(-96.9 \pm 5.8\) MeV (selected set).
- Estimated value of \(Q_{sym}^0\) from the selected set: \(299.4 \pm 20.2\) MeV.
- Estimated value of \(\Delta m_0^*\) from the selected set: \(-0.034 \pm 0.081\).

## Context

This work extends the analytical and numerical exploration of correlations among symmetry energy parameters previously studied by Danielewicz (2009), Ducoin (2011), and Tews (2017), who found mixed results for the correlation between \(K_{sym}^0\) and \(L_0\) depending on model selection. The authors build on their own earlier nearly model-independent finding (Mondal, 2017) that \(K_{sym}^0\) is analytically tied to \((3C_2^0 - L_0)\), and they provide explicit Skyrme-formalism expressions that explain the observed correlations. The results enable better estimation of poorly constrained higher-order symmetry energy parameters \(K_{sym}^0\) and \(Q_{sym}^0\) from the relatively better-known quantities \(C_2^0\) and \(L_0\), with implications for neutron star structure and the properties of neutron-rich nuclei.
