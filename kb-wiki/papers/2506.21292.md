# Speed of sound of QCD matter at chiral crossover

> Based on a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach to thermodynamics of QCD motivated by cluster decomposition we present a unified equation of state of hot strongly interacting matter and analyze its properties in a wide range of temperatures. The hadrons are treated as color singlet multiquark clusters in medium with a background gluon field in the Polyakov gauge. The confining aspect of QCD is accounted for by the Polyakov loop mechanism and by a large vacuum quark mass motivated by a confining density functional approach. We demonstrate that an abrupt switching between hadronic and partonic degrees of freedom, which is one of striking manifestations of dynamical restoration of chiral symmetry, is accompanied by a smooth behavior of entropy density at chiral crossover. Individual contributions of different components of strongly interacting matter to its speed of sound are analyzed for the first time. It is shown that restoration of chiral symmetry drives speed of sound of hadron gas to negative values, manifesting its mechanical instability and being in a strike disagreements with the lattice QCD data. Accounting for the partonic excitations naturally resolves this contradiction.

## Key claim

The paper claims that an abrupt switching between hadronic and partonic degrees of freedom at the chiral crossover temperature is accompanied by a smooth entropy density, but that restoration of chiral symmetry drives the speed of sound of the hadron gas to negative values, indicating mechanical instability; this contradiction with lattice QCD data is resolved only by including partonic excitations immediately after the pseudocritical temperature.

## Method

The authors apply a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach to QCD thermodynamics, treating hadrons as color-singlet multiquark clusters in a Polyakov-gluon background, with confinement modeled by the Polyakov loop mechanism and large vacuum quark masses from a confining density functional. Quark masses are switched abruptly from vacuum values (627 MeV for u/d, 770 MeV for s) to current masses (5.6 MeV for u/d, 124 MeV for s) at the pseudocritical temperature \(T_c = 156.6\) MeV. The thermodynamic potential includes contributions from quarks, gluons, 58 mesons, 60 baryons, 60 antibaryons, deuterons, sexaquarks, and colored multiquark clusters (diquarks, tetraquarks, pentaquarks), with phase shifts parameterized to model Mott dissociation above \(T_c\).

## Key numbers

* Pseudocritical temperature of chiral crossover: \(T_c = 156.6\) MeV, fixed from lattice QCD data (HotQCD 2018).
* Vacuum quark masses: \(M_{u,d}|_{T=0} = 627\) MeV, \(M_s|_{T=0} = 770\) MeV; current masses: \(m_{u,d} = 5.6\) MeV, \(m_s = 124\) MeV (Particle Data Group 2022).
* Binding energy per quark bond: \(B = 471\) MeV, determined by fitting vacuum masses of nucleons, pseudoscalar and vector mesons.
* Perturbative momentum cutoff: \(\Lambda_{\rm pert} = 222\) MeV; perturbative coupling parameter: \(T_* = 94\) MeV.
* Parameters for hadron mass and width: \(\alpha_1 = 11.4\), \(\alpha_2 = 1.9\), adjusted to lattice entropy density data.
* Model includes hadrons with masses below 2.6 GeV (58 mesons, 60 baryons, 60 antibaryons).

## Context

This work extends the unified hadron-parton equation of state developed by Blaschke (2023) using cluster decomposition (Baym 1961, 1962) and the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach (Ropke 2012). It provides the first analysis of individual component contributions to the speed of sound in QCD matter, contrasting with lattice QCD results from Borsanyi (2013, 2021) and HotQCD (2014, 2018). The finding that chiral restoration drives hadron gas mechanical instability, resolved by partonic degrees of freedom, enables future modeling of the QCD phase diagram at finite baryon densities, including possible first-order phase transitions and critical endpoints, using the confining density functional approach of Ivanytskyi (2022).
