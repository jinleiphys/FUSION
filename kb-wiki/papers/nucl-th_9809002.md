# Few nucleon dynamics in a nuclear medium

> Few body methods are used in many particle physics to describe correlations, bound states, and reactions in strongly correlated quantum systems. Although this has already been recognized earlier, rigorous attempts to treat three-body collisions have only been done recently. In this talk I shall give examples and areas where few-body methods have been and might be of use in the future.

## Key claim

Few-body methods can be systematically generalized to treat three-body collisions in nuclear matter, going beyond the quasi-particle approximation and the impulse or Born approximations that have been used previously; however, the validity of this approach still needs to be checked against experimental results from heavy ion collisions or ionic plasmas.

## Method

The author follows the Green function formalism and uses a cluster expansion to truncate the many-body hierarchy, treating two-body correlations via the Bethe-Goldstone or Feynman-Galitski equations in ladder approximation. For three-body correlations, the in-medium Faddeev-type equations are rewritten using the AGS approach and solved for the nucleon-deuteron system, including Hartree-Fock self-energy shifts and Pauli blocking. The resulting in-medium transition matrices are then used in a quantum kinetic Boltzmann equation to compute break-up cross sections and deuteron fluctuation lifetimes.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter density of n0 = 0.17 fm^-3 is used as a reference value for the one-particle distribution function.
- The deuteron binding energy is calculated as a function of nuclear density at T = 10 MeV; the Mott density is defined by the condition E_d = 0.
- The triton binding energy is shown as a function of nuclear density for different temperatures, with the Nd continua also indicated.
- In-medium break-up cross sections are computed at T = 10 MeV for densities below the deuteron Mott density; the cross section is significantly enhanced compared to the isolated case, with the threshold shifted to smaller energies.
- The deuteron fluctuation lifetime is calculated at nuclear density n = 0.007 fm^-3 and T = 10 MeV; the inverse lifetime (width) at P=0 is compared with the deuteron binding energy.
- The approximate duration of a heavy ion collision is given as about 200 fm.

## Context

This work extends the quantum statistical approach of Kadanoﬀ (1962) and the cluster approximation of Roepke (1983) to include three-body collisions via in-medium Faddeev/AGS equations, building on earlier treatments by Beyer (1996, 1997, 1998). It enables improved modeling of light cluster formation (deuterons, tritons, helium-3, alpha particles) in the final stage of heavy ion collisions, and is also relevant for the equation of state of neutron stars and for ionic plasmas where three-particle reactions determine ionization rates.
