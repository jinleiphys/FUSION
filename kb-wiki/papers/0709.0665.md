# Multiplicity fluctuations in relativistic nuclear collisions

> Multiplicity distributions of hadrons produced in central nucleus-nucleus collisions are studied within the hadron-resonance gas model in the large volume limit. In the canonical ensemble conservation of three charges (baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness) is enforced. In addition, in the micro-canonical ensemble energy conservation is included. An analytical method is used to account for resonance decays. Multiplicity distributions and scaled variances for negatively charged hadrons are presented along the chemical freeze-out line of central Pb+Pb (Au+Au) collisions from SIS to LHC energies. Predictions obtained within different statistical ensembles are compared with preliminary NA49 experimental results on central Pb+Pb collisions in the SPS energy range. The measured fluctuations are significantly narrower than a Poisson reference distribution, and clearly favor expectations for the micro-canonical ensemble.

# Multiplicity fluctuations in relativistic nuclear collisions

## Key claim

The measured multiplicity distributions of negatively charged hadrons in central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies are significantly narrower than a Poisson reference distribution and clearly favor the micro-canonical ensemble (MCE) over the canonical (CE) or grand-canonical (GCE) ensembles, but this conclusion depends on the assumption that the system volume is large and finite-volume corrections can be neglected.

## Method

The authors compute multiplicity distributions and scaled variances for negatively charged hadrons within the hadron-resonance gas model in the large volume limit, using analytical methods to account for resonance decays. They enforce conservation of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness in the canonical ensemble, and additionally energy conservation in the micro-canonical ensemble, then compare predictions from all three ensembles with preliminary NA49 experimental data on central Pb+Pb collisions at five beam energies (20A, 30A, 40A, 80A, and 158A GeV).

## Key numbers

- Scaled variance for an ultra-relativistic gas of neutral Boltzmann particles in the micro-canonical ensemble converges to ω_MCE = 0.25 in the large volume limit, while the grand-canonical ensemble gives ω_GCE = 1 (Poisson distribution).
- The baryon chemical potential as a function of collision energy is parameterized as μ_B(√s_NN) = 1.308 GeV · (1 + 0.273 √s_NN)^{-1}, where √s_NN is in GeV.
- The strangeness saturation factor is parameterized as γ_S = 1 − 0.396 exp(−1.23 T/μ_B).
- The system is assumed to have charge-to-baryon ratio Q/B = 0.4 and net strangeness S = 0.
- The THERMUS particle table includes all known hadrons and resonances up to a mass of about 2.5 GeV.
- Multiplicity distributions are presented for central Pb+Pb collisions at 20A, 30A, 40A, 80A, and 158A GeV in the NA49 acceptance.

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model approach of Fermi (1950), Landau, and Hagedorn by focusing on multiplicity fluctuations rather than mean multiplicities, which were the subject of earlier studies by Becattini and collaborators. The paper builds on the recent finding by Begun et al. (2004) that scaled variances differ between statistical ensembles even in the thermodynamic limit, unlike mean multiplicities which become equivalent. The results enable future searches for critical point signals in fluctuations, as proposed by Stephanov et al., and provide a baseline for distinguishing statistical hadronization effects from possible deconfinement-related anomalies near the onset of deconfinement.
