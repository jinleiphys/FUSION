# Femtoscopic correlations in multiparticle production and beta-decay

> The basics of formalism of femtoscopic and spectroscopic correlations are given, the orthogonal character of these correlations is stressed. The similarity and difference of femtoscopic correlations in multiparticle production and beta-decay is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that femtoscopic (momentum) correlations in multiparticle production and spectroscopic (space-time) correlations in astronomy are orthogonal and fundamentally different, despite a common misconception that they are analogous; the similarity between femtoscopic correlations in multiparticle production and beta-decay is discussed, but the author stresses that the actual energy release in beta-decay is very small so the integration over neutrino three-momentum does not lead to the diagonalization of the spatial density matrix, unlike the hypothetical large-energy-release case.

## Method

The author presents the Kopylov-Podgoretsky (KP) formalism of independent one-particle classical emitters to derive the two-particle correlation function for identical bosons, incorporating quantum statistics (QS) symmetrization and final state interaction (FSI) effects including Coulomb and strong interactions. The formalism is extended to spectroscopic QS correlations for photons from distant sources, showing the symmetry between emitter-detector interchange, and applied to beta-decay by treating the electron-nucleus Coulomb wave function and the Fermi function.

## Key numbers

* For a ρ-meson and a pion emitted from a small space-time region, the classical approach to emitter decay is a valid approximation at Q < 0.1 GeV/c but overestimates the tail of the two-pion correlation function by about 15 percent (see figure 2 in Lednický 1992).
* The equal-time approximation for pions leads to a slight overestimation (typically < 5%) of the strong FSI effect.
* For two-nucleon systems, the scattering lengths f^S(0) are large (up to ~20 fm).
* For two-meson or meson-baryon systems, the scattering amplitudes are usually quite small (< 0.2 fm).
* The s-wave K̅K scattering length is dominated by the imaginary part of ~1 fm generated by near-threshold resonances f0(980) and a0(980).
* Neglect of the FSI contribution in the analysis of the two-K_S^0 correlation function in Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV would lead to a noticeable (~25%) overestimation of the correlation radius (Stark et al.).

## Context

This work extends the foundational formalism of correlation femtoscopy developed by Kopylov and Podgoretsky (1970s) and clarifies the orthogonal character of momentum versus space-time correlations, correcting misconceptions in earlier reviews such as those by Shuryak (1973) and the review by Boal, Gelbke, and Jennings (1992). The paper enables proper application of femtoscopic correlations to study space-time characteristics of particle production in heavy ion collisions, and draws an analogy to beta-decay where the Fermi function (Fermi 1934) plays a role similar to the squared wave function in femtoscopic FSI correlations.
