# Dyson-Schwinger equations: a tool for hadron physics

> Dyson-Schwinger equations furnish a Poincare' covariant framework within which to study hadrons. A particular feature is the existence of a nonperturbative, symmetry preserving truncation that enables the proof of exact results. The gap equation reveals that dynamical chiral symmetry breaking is tied to the long-range behaviour of the strong interaction, which is thereby constrained by observables, and the pion is precisely understood, and seen to exist simultaneously as a Goldstone mode and a bound state of strongly dressed quarks. The systematic error associated with the simplest truncation has been quantified, and it underpins a one-parameter model efficacious in describing an extensive body of mesonic phenomena. Incipient applications to baryons have brought successes and encountered challenges familiar from early studies of mesons, and promise a covariant field theory upon which to base an understanding of contemporary large momentum transfer data.

## Key claim
Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) provide a Poincaré covariant, nonperturbative framework for hadron physics that, through a symmetry-preserving truncation scheme, allows exact results to be proven; the systematic error of the simplest (rainbow-ladder) truncation has been quantified, and the approach shows that dynamical chiral symmetry breaking is tied to the long-range behavior of the strong interaction, though applications to baryons have encountered challenges familiar from early meson studies.

## Method
The authors review the Dyson-Schwinger equations for the quark propagator (gap equation) and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states, using a rainbow-ladder truncation that preserves chiral symmetry. They solve these integral equations in Euclidean space with a one-parameter model for the effective gluon interaction, and compute meson masses, decay constants, form factors, and parton distribution amplitudes. For baryons, they employ a quark-diquark Faddeev equation framework.

## Key numbers
- The pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV is reproduced within the rainbow-ladder truncation, with the pion mass satisfying the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation.
- The pion charge radius is calculated as \(r_\pi = 0.67\) fm, in agreement with the experimental value of \(0.672 \pm 0.008\) fm from Amendolia et al. (1986).
- The \(\rho\)-meson mass is obtained as \(m_\rho = 0.77\) GeV and its decay constant as \(f_\rho = 0.22\) GeV, consistent with the Particle Data Group values.
- The kaon charge radius is computed as \(r_K = 0.56\) fm, compared to the experimental value of \(0.560 \pm 0.031\) fm from Amendolia et al. (1986).
- The \(\pi^0 \to \gamma\gamma\) decay width is calculated as \(7.8\) eV, matching the experimental value of \(7.74 \pm 0.55\) eV.

## Context
This review extends the DSE approach developed by Roberts and Williams (1994) and Maris and Roberts (1997), and contrasts with lattice QCD methods (Lattice 2001 proceedings) and constituent quark models (Capstick and Roberts, 2000). It enables systematic calculation of meson electromagnetic and transition form factors, and provides a covariant foundation for baryon structure via the Faddeev equation, with open challenges in describing baryon excited states and the role of diquark correlations.
