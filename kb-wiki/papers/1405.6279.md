# Electric dipole response of $^6$He: Halo-neutron and core excitations

> Electric dipole ($E1$) response of $^{6}$He is studied with a fully microscopic six-body calculation. The wave functions for the ground and excited states are expressed as a superposition of explicitly correlated Gaussians (CG). Final state interactions of three-body decay channels are explicitly taken into account. The ground state properties and the low-energy $E1$ strength are obtained consistently with observations. Two main peaks as well as several small peaks are found in the $E1$ strength function. The peak at the high-energy region indicates a typical macroscopic picture of the giant dipole resonance, the out-of-phase proton-neutron motion. The transition densities of the lower-lying peaks exhibit in-phase proton-neutron motion in the internal region, out-of-phase motion near the surface region, and spatially extended neutron oscillation, indicating a soft-dipole mode (SDM) and its vibrationally excited mode.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the low-lying electric dipole (E1) strength in ⁶He, observed as a broad peak just a few MeV above the α+n+n threshold, corresponds to a soft-dipole mode (SDM) and its vibrationally excited mode, while the higher-energy peak around 33 MeV corresponds to the giant dipole resonance (GDR). This conclusion is hedged by the fact that the SDM discussed does not necessarily mean a narrow resonance, and no such resonant state was found in the low-energy region.

## Method

A fully microscopic six-body calculation was performed using the stochastic variational method with explicitly correlated Gaussian (CG) basis functions. The ground state wave function was obtained variationally, and the E1 excited states were constructed from three classes of basis configurations: single-particle excitation, α+n+n three-body decay channel, and t+d+n three-body decay channel, with final state interactions explicitly included. The Hamiltonian used the central Minnesota (MN) potential with the parameter u adjusted to reproduce the experimental two-neutron separation energy (u=1.05).

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of ⁶He: −30.98 MeV (with u=1.05), compared to experimental −29.27 MeV.
- Two-neutron separation energy S₂ₙ: 1.01 MeV (with u=1.05), reproducing the experimental value of 0.972 MeV.
- Matter rms radius rₘ of ⁶He: 2.41 fm (with u=1.05), consistent with the averaged empirical value of 2.37(10) fm.
- Proton rms radius rₚ of ⁶He: 1.83 fm (with u=1.05), slightly smaller than experimental values 1.938(23) fm and 1.912(18) fm.
- Neutron rms radius rₙ of ⁶He: 2.65 fm (with u=1.05).
- The Full model space exhausts 99.9% of the non-energy-weighted E1 sum rule, which is calculated to be 7.21 e²fm².
- The E1 strength function shows a low-lying peak centered around 3 MeV and a higher peak at 33 MeV.
- The rms distance of the two protons rₚₚ in ⁶He is 2.49 fm (with u=1.05), 5% larger than the 2.37 fm in ⁴He, indicating core swelling.

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic six-body calculations by Bacca et al. (2002, 2004) using the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonics (EIHH) method, which also found two peaks for ⁶He and speculated on the existence of the SDM and GDR. It addresses theoretical uncertainties in macroscopic α+n+n three-body models (e.g., Suzuki 1991, Danilin 1997, Myo 2001, Baye 2009, Hagino 2009, Pinilla 2011) where the α particle is treated as a point particle and different phase-shift equivalent α-n potentials give different E1 strength. The fully microscopic approach naturally accounts for core distortion and polarization, which is known to play an important role in binding the halo neutrons (Arai 1999). The results enable a detailed understanding of the SDM and GDR excitation mechanisms in halo nuclei and can be compared with Coulomb breakup experiments (Aumann 1999).
