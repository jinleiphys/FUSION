# Appendix to: Energy Density Functional Approach to Superfluid Nuclei, nucl-th/0210047

> This report is not meant to be submitted for publication at the present time and it is simply a supplement to our work nucl-th/0210047. We have collected together all our results for one--nucleon and two--nucleon separation energies for several isotope and isotone chains and compare them to the values extracted from the 1995 Audi and Wapstra table of recommended masses. Where it was possible we have compared our results to the results of Fayans et al. and Goriely et al. We also present results for the charge radii and compare them to experiment and results of Goriely et al.

## Key claim
This appendix presents one-nucleon and two-nucleon separation energies and charge radii for 212 nuclei, computed using the authors' energy density functional for superfluid nuclei with Fayans' FaNDF$^0$ normal part and a bare pairing coupling constant of g = -200 MeV fm$^{-3}$, but only for spherical nuclei and with a single universal pairing strength for both protons and neutrons, unlike the separate pairing strengths used by Goriely et al. or the renormalized strengths used by Fayans et al.

## Method
The authors used the energy density functional for superfluid nuclei described in their companion paper (nucl-th/0210047), employing Fayans' FaNDF$^0$ functional for the normal part and a bare pairing coupling constant g = -200 MeV fm$^{-3}$ for both proton and neutron systems, without changing its value with atomic number. All nuclei were treated as spherical, pairing was allowed in both proton and neutron subsystems with couplings g = g0 + g1 ≠ 0 and g' = g0 - g1 ≡ 0, and the proton charge radius was taken as rp = 0.87 fm. Results were compared to experimental binding energies from the 1995 Audi and Wapstra table, to digitized results from Fayans et al., and to results from Goriely et al. extracted from their website.

## Key numbers
- Bare pairing coupling constant: g = -200 MeV fm$^{-3}$, used for both proton and neutron systems.
- Proton charge radius used in calculations: rp = 0.87 fm (compared to rp = 0.80 fm used by Goriely et al.).
- Fayans et al. renormalized pairing strength by a factor of 1.05 for tin isotopes and by a factor of 1.35 for calcium isotopes.
- Results presented for 212 nuclei total, across isotope chains of Ca, Ni, Zr, Sn, Pb and isotone chains at N=50, N=82, N=126.

## Context
This work extends the energy density functional approach to superfluid nuclei described in the authors' companion paper (nucl-th/0210047) by providing a comprehensive comparison of separation energies and charge radii against experimental data from Audi and Wapstra (1995) and against calculations by Fayans et al. and Goriely et al. The authors note that many nickel and zirconium isotopes are deformed, as discussed by Goriely et al., and that treating them as spherical likely explains some discrepancies. The results enable future work to incorporate deformation effects and to refine the treatment of Coulomb exchange, correlation energies, and finite nucleon form factors, as emphasized by Fayans in private discussions.
