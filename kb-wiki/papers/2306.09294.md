# Non-Gaussian transverse momentum fluctuations from impact parameter fluctuations

> The transverse momentum per particle, $[p_t]$, fluctuates event by event in ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions, for a given multiplicity. These fluctuations are small and approximately Gaussian, but a non-zero skewness has been predicted on the basis of hydrodynamic calculations, and seen experimentally. We argue that the mechanism driving the skewness is that, if the system thermalizes, the mean transverse momentum increases with impact parameter for a fixed collision multiplicity. We postulate that fluctuations are Gaussian at fixed impact parameter, and that non-Gaussianities solely result from impact parameter fluctuations. Using recent data on the variance of $[p_t]$ fluctuations, we make quantitative predictions for their skewness and kurtosis as a function of the collision multiplicity. We predict in particular a spectacular increase of the skewness below the knee of the multiplicity distribution, followed by a fast decrease.

## Key claim

The authors argue that non-Gaussian transverse momentum fluctuations in ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions arise solely from impact parameter fluctuations at fixed multiplicity, assuming Gaussian fluctuations at fixed impact parameter; the skewness and kurtosis are predicted to show sharp variations around the "knee" of the multiplicity distribution, with a spectacular increase of skewness below the knee followed by a fast decrease.

## Method

The authors model the joint distribution of charged multiplicity $N_{ch}$ and mean transverse momentum $[p_t]$ at fixed impact parameter as a correlated two-dimensional Gaussian, with parameters fitted to ATLAS data on the variance of $[p_t]$ fluctuations in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV. They then integrate over impact parameter using Bayes' theorem, with the distribution of centrality fraction $c_b$ at fixed $N_{ch}$ derived from a Gaussian model of multiplicity fluctuations at fixed $b$. The centrality dependence of the multiplicity standard deviation $\sigma_{N_{ch}}$ is taken from two independent hydrodynamic model parametrizations (Duke and JETSCAPE) to estimate systematic uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- The knee multiplicity $N_{\rm knee}=3680$, multiplicity standard deviation $\sigma_{N_{ch}}=168$, and slope parameter $\beta=18300$ for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV (ATLAS inner detector acceptance).
- The width of the centrality fraction distribution at fixed multiplicity is $\sigma_{N_{ch}}/\beta \simeq 0.9\%$.
- The Pearson correlation coefficient $r$ between $N_{ch}$ and $[p_t]$ at fixed impact parameter is close to $0.7$.
- The maximum of the skewness occurs at $N_{ch}\simeq N_{\rm knee}-\sigma_{N_{ch}}\simeq 3510$.
- The kurtosis has a minimum at $N_{ch}\simeq N_{\rm knee}-2\sigma_{N_{ch}}\simeq 3340$ and a maximum at $N_{ch}\simeq N_{\rm knee}\simeq 3680$.
- The intensive skewness predicted for ATLAS peaks at a value $\sim 18$ below the knee (for $E_T>3.5$ TeV bin).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Samanta (2023) on the variance of $[p_t]$ fluctuations, which showed a sharp decrease in ultracentral collisions explained by impact parameter fluctuations. It provides quantitative predictions for higher cumulants (skewness and kurtosis) that can be tested against upcoming ATLAS data and preliminary ALICE results (Saha, 2022). The mechanism contrasts with earlier hydrodynamic calculations (Giacalone, 2020) that predicted skewness but overlooked the crucial role of impact parameter fluctuations. The predictions enable experimental verification of whether non-Gaussianities in $[p_t]$ fluctuations originate entirely from impact parameter fluctuations, as assumed here, or require additional sources such as intrinsic non-Gaussianity at fixed impact parameter.
