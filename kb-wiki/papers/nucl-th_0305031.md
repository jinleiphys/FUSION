# Configuration-Space-Faddeev Born Approximations

> Alternative definitions of the Born approximation and the distorted-wave Born approximation within the framework of the configuration-space Faddeev equations are explored. The most natural definition does not correspond to the Born approximation derived from the Schroedinger equation, even though the exact T-matrices for both formalisms are equivalent. The Schroedinger form is optimal, although it is shown that the differences are numerically unimportant. The DWBA corresponding to the Faddeev equations is not channel symmetric, although numerically this is unimportant for the p-d (Coulomb) case. Convergence of the Born approximation partial-wave series is briefly investigated for p-d and n-d scattering below breakup threshold.

# Configuration-Space-Faddeev Born Approximations

## Key claim

The most natural definition of the Born approximation within the configuration-space Faddeev equations does not correspond to the Born approximation derived from the Schroedinger equation, even though the exact T-matrices for both formalisms are equivalent; the Schroedinger form is optimal, although the differences are numerically unimportant. The Faddeev distorted-wave Born approximation is not channel symmetric, but this is numerically unimportant for the p-d (Coulomb) case.

## Method

The authors derived alternative definitions of the Born approximation (BA) and distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) within the configuration-space Faddeev framework, comparing the Faddeev-form T-matrix (t_F^BA = 2(112)) with the Schroedinger-form T-matrix (t_S^BA = 2(112) + 2(121) + 2(123)). They then computed exact and approximate differential cross sections and analyzing powers for p-d and n-d scattering below the deuteron-breakup threshold using the AV18 potential, evaluating fractional differences between exact calculations and those using Born substitution or partial-wave truncation.

## Key numbers

- For 3 MeV p-d scattering, truncating the partial-wave series at J=9/2 gives a fractional error of 2.8[-2] (2.8 x 10^-2) in differential cross section and 1.5[0] (1.5) in analyzing power; truncation at J=15/2 gives 3.9[-4] in cross section and 1.8[-2] in analyzing power.
- For 3 MeV n-d scattering with Born substitution, replacing exact matrix elements with Born amplitudes for J=5/2 and above gives fractional error of 1.4[-1] in cross section and 4.1[0] in analyzing power; for J=9/2 and above, errors drop to 1.2[-4] in cross section and 1.9[-2] in analyzing power.
- For 1 MeV p-d scattering with Born substitution at J=5/2, fractional error is 7.0[-2] in cross section and 8.6[0] in analyzing power; at J=9/2, errors are 1.3[-5] and 1.1[-2] respectively.
- For 3 MeV p-d scattering with Born substitution at J=5/2, fractional error is 8.5[-2] in cross section and 4.2[0] in analyzing power; at J=9/2, errors are 9.7[-5] and 1.3[-2] respectively.
- The channel asymmetry in p-d scattering matrix elements at 3 MeV is typically a few times 10^-3.

## Context

This work extends the configuration-space Faddeev methodology for few-nucleon calculations, building on prior approaches by Noyes and by Kievsky et al. (Kohn variational principle). The paper clarifies pitfalls in defining Born approximations for higher partial waves, which is relevant to efficient numerical calculations with realistic potentials such as AV18. The results enable practitioners to use the Schroedinger-form BA and DWBA for higher partial waves in configuration-space Faddeev calculations, reducing computational effort while maintaining accuracy to the 1% criterion for differential cross sections and analyzing powers.
