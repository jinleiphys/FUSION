# Modeling (anti)deuteron formation at RHIC with a geometric coalescence model

> We study (anti)deuteron formation rates in heavy-ion collisions in the framework of a wave-function based coalescence model. The main feature of our model is that nucleons are emitted from the whole spherically symmetric fireball volume, while antinucleons are emitted only from a spherical shell close to the surface. In this way, the model accounts for nucleon-antinucleon annihilations in the center of the reaction at lower beam energies. Comparison with experimental data on the coalescence parameter in the range $\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 4.7-200$ GeV allows us to extract radii of the respective source geometries. Our results are qualitatively supported by data from the UrQMD transport model which shows a comparable trend in the geometric radii as a function of beam energy. In line with our expectations, we find that at lower energies, the central region of the fireball experiences stronger annihilation than at higher energies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed energy dependence of (anti)deuteron coalescence parameters in heavy-ion collisions can be explained by a geometric coalescence model where antinucleons are emitted only from a spherical shell near the fireball surface due to nucleon-antinucleon annihilation in the central region, while nucleons are emitted from the full volume; however, the extracted source radii show a non-monotonic behavior with a maximum near 27 GeV that may indicate a change in the QCD equation of state or a nutcracker shell-like structure, which the authors note is beyond the scope of the paper.

## Method

The authors apply a wave-function based coalescence model using the Hulthén wave function for the deuteron, with nucleon emission described by a Gaussian source function over the full fireball volume and antinucleon emission described by a Gaussian with a central suppression region cut out by a second Gaussian. They extract the fireball radius r0 and suppression radius r* by fitting the model to experimental coalescence parameter B2 data from NA49 and STAR for deuterons and antideuterons at center-of-mass energies from 4.7 to 200 GeV. In a second method, they constrain the root-mean-squared radius of the nucleon source via the number of charged particles measured by PHENIX, solving simultaneously for r0 and r* from the formation rate and the RMS-radius relation. They also compare with freeze-out geometries extracted directly from UrQMD transport model simulations of Au+Au collisions at 0-10% centrality.

## Key numbers

- Coalescence parameter B2 data are used from experiments spanning sqrt(s_NN) = 4.7-200 GeV (NA49 for deuterons, STAR for deuterons and antideuterons).
- The Hulthén wave function parameters are alpha = 0.23 fm^-1 and beta = 1.61 fm^-1.
- The nucleon mass used in the relation A = (m_N/2) B2 is m_N = 0.94 MeV.
- The constant for the proportionality between (anti)nucleon RMS-radius and cube root of charged particle number is found to be 0.925.
- From UrQMD, the (anti)nucleon fireball radii range from 7 to 12 fm across the energy range.
- The r*/r0 ratio from UrQMD approaches 1 at low energy and drops at higher energies; the charged volume constraint method shows the ratio dropping to zero at 200 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the spatial coalescence model of Mrowczynski (1993), which was developed to explain antideuteron production in Si+Au collisions at AGS where simple momentum-space coalescence failed (Aoki 1992). The paper applies this model to a wider energy range using data from NA49 and STAR, and introduces a charged-particle-volume constraint to address the non-monotonic behavior of extracted fireball radii. The results are compared with UrQMD transport model simulations (Bleicher 1999, Bass 1998) and suggest a critical behavior of the suppression radius around 20 GeV that may indicate a longer system lifetime. This enables future studies of how the QCD equation of state and pion dominance affect light (anti)nuclei production.
