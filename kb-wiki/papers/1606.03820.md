# Consistency between SU(3) and SU(2) chiral perturbation theory for the nucleon mass

> Treating the strange quark mass as a heavy scale compared to the light quark mass, we perform a matching of the nucleon mass in the SU(3) sector to the two-flavor case in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory. The validity of the $19$ low-energy constants appearing in the octet baryon masses up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order~\cite{Ren:2014vea} is supported by comparing the effective parameters (the combinations of the $19$ couplings) with the corresponding low-energy constants in the SU(2) sector~\cite{Alvarez-Ruso:2013fza}. In addition, it is shown that the dependence of the effective parameters and the pion-nucleon sigma term on the strange quark mass is relatively weak around its physical value, thus providing support to the assumption made in Ref.~\cite{Alvarez-Ruso:2013fza}.

## Key claim

By treating the strange quark mass as a heavy scale and expanding the SU(3) nucleon mass in powers of \(m_q/m_s\), the authors show that the 19 low-energy constants (LECs) from the SU(3) octet baryon mass fit are largely consistent with the SU(2) LECs, provided the strange quark mass is near its physical value; the agreement is good for the leading-order parameters \(m_0^\mathrm{eff}\) and \(c_1^\mathrm{eff}\) but shows discrepancies at next-to-next-to-leading order for \(\beta^\mathrm{eff}\).

## Method

The authors match the SU(3) covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (EOMS scheme) expression for the nucleon mass, computed up to \(\mathcal{O}(p^4)\) with 19 LECs, to the SU(2) form by expanding kaon- and eta-loop contributions as polynomials in \(m_\pi/m_{s\bar{s}}\) up to fourth order. They then compare the resulting effective SU(2) parameters (\(m_0^\mathrm{eff}\), \(c_1^\mathrm{eff}\), \(\alpha^\mathrm{eff}\), \(\beta^\mathrm{eff}\)) with the SU(2) LECs (\(M_0\), \(c_1\), \(\alpha^\mathrm{SU(2)}\), \(\beta^\mathrm{SU(2)}\)) previously determined from \(n_f=2+1\) lattice QCD data, and study the dependence of these parameters on the strange quark mass.

## Key numbers

*   \(m_0^\mathrm{eff} = 875(10)\) MeV, compared to the SU(2) chiral-limit nucleon mass \(M_0 = 870(3)\) MeV.
*   \(c_1^\mathrm{eff} = -1.07(4)\) GeV\(^{-1}\), compared to the SU(2) LEC \(c_1 = -1.15(3)\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
*   \(\alpha^\mathrm{eff} = 4.81(9)\) GeV\(^{-3}\), compared to the SU(2) LEC \(\alpha^\mathrm{SU(2)} = 6.27(1.98)\) GeV\(^{-3}\).
*   \(\beta^\mathrm{eff} = -4.02(20)\) GeV\(^{-3}\), compared to the SU(2) LEC \(\beta^\mathrm{SU(2)} = -7.62(93)\) GeV\(^{-3}\).
*   The pion-nucleon sigma term obtained from the SU(2) equivalent expansion is \(\sigma_{\pi N} = 57(6)\) MeV at the physical point, consistent with the SU(2) value \(58(3)\) MeV and the SU(3) value \(57(2)\) MeV.
*   The relative deviation \(|R|\) of the effective parameters \(m_0^\mathrm{eff}\), \(c_1^\mathrm{eff}\), \(\alpha^\mathrm{eff}\), and \(\beta^\mathrm{eff}\) is less than 5% for strange quark masses in the range \(0.55\ \mathrm{GeV} < m_{s\bar{s}} < 0.80\ \mathrm{GeV}\) (the range of lattice QCD simulations).

## Context

This work extends the SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory analysis of Ren (2014) by testing the consistency of its 19 LECs against the SU(2) analysis of Alvarez-Ruso (2013), which assumed a weak dependence of SU(2) LECs on the strange quark mass. The matching procedure, similar in spirit to earlier matching studies by Frink (2004) and Mai (2009), provides a nontrivial check on the SU(3) LECs and supports the use of SU(2) baryon chiral perturbation theory for analyzing \(n_f=2+1\) lattice QCD data when the strange quark mass is near its physical value. The results enable future global analyses that incorporate the latest lattice QCD simulations at the physical point to resolve the current tension in the pion-nucleon sigma term.
