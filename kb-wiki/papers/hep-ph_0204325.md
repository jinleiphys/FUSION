# Electric Polarizability of Mesons in Semirelativistic Quark Models

> The electric polarizability of mesons, in particular, that of the charged pion, is studied in the framework of a semirelativistic description of hadrons as bound states of valence quarks in terms of a Hamiltonian composed of the relativistic kinetic energy as well as a phenomenological potential describing the strong interactions between the quarks. The quark-core contribution to the electric polarizability obtainable in quark models of this kind turns out to be in the semirelativistic approaches even smaller than in the nonrelativistic limit.

## Key claim

The valence-quark contribution to the electric polarizability of the charged pion, computed in a semirelativistic quark model with relativistic kinetic energy, is even smaller than the already small nonrelativistic quark-core result, contradicting the expectation that relativistic kinematics would increase the quark-core contribution.

## Method

The authors model the pion as a quark-antiquark bound state using a Hamiltonian with relativistic kinetic energies (the spinless Salpeter equation) and a static "funnel" potential consisting of a Coulomb term from one-gluon exchange plus a linearly rising confining term. They compute the electric polarizability from the energy shift induced by an external electric field using a variational technique with Laguerre basis states, and compare the semirelativistic results to the nonrelativistic Schrödinger equation solutions for the same potential parameters.

## Key numbers

- For the semirelativistic treatment: \(\langle r^2 \rangle = 11.7\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) and \(\langle[z,H_0]\,z\rangle = 1.66\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\).
- For the nonrelativistic treatment: \(\langle r^2 \rangle = 17.8\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) and \(\langle[z,H_0]\,z\rangle = 2.98\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\).
- The ratio of the semirelativistic to nonrelativistic polarizability is \(\kappa / \kappa_{\text{NR}} = 0.77 < 1\).
- The experimental weighted average of the charged-pion electric polarizability from three experiments is \(\kappa_{\pi^\pm}^{\text{exp}} = (4.3 \pm 1.2) \times 10^{-4}\ \text{fm}^3\).
- The nonrelativistic quark model prediction is \(0.054 \times 10^{-4}\ \text{fm}^3\), a factor of 80 below the experimental average.

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic quark potential model study of Leeb (year not given in text) which found a quark-core polarizability two orders of magnitude below experiment, and contrasts with quantum-field-theoretic approaches such as chiral perturbation theory (ChPT, two-loop result \(2.4 \pm 0.5 \times 10^{-4}\ \text{fm}^3\)) and the relativistic Bethe-Salpeter quark model of Koll (2001) which give values within a factor 2.3 of the experimental central value. The results suggest that the valence-quark core contributes only a minor fraction of the total pion polarizability, supporting the picture from Weiner (1985) that hadrons consist of a quark core surrounded by virtual meson clouds, and motivating continued experimental study via processes such as the Primakoff effect at the COMPASS experiment.
