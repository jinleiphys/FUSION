# Is the quark gluon plasma produced in RHIC collisions strongly coupled?

> Recent hexadecapole (v4) and elliptic (v2) flow measurements are used to constrain estimates for the degree of local equilibrium, mean free path $λ$, and the viscosity to entropy density ratio (eta/s) of the plasma produced in Au+Au collisions at RootS = 200 GeV. The eccentricity-scaled flow coefficients v2/e2 and v4/e4 indicate that the plasma achieves a degree of local equilibrium within 5 - 10% of the value expected for a fluid with eta/s equal to the conjectured lower bound of 1/4pi. Estimates for $λ$ and eta/s as a function of collision centrality and particle transverse momentum pT, points to transverse expansion dynamics compatible with a strongly coupled low viscosity plasma.

## Key claim

The eccentricity-scaled flow coefficients v2/ε2 and v4/ε4 indicate that the quark gluon plasma produced in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV achieves a degree of local equilibrium within 5–10% of the value expected for a fluid with η/s equal to the conjectured lower bound of 1/4π, but this estimate carries a hedge: the extracted η/s shows a strong quadratic dependence on particle transverse momentum pT, which the authors attribute to viscous corrections rather than an intrinsic property of the plasma.

## Method

The authors performed simultaneous fits of the eccentricity-scaled elliptic (v2/ε2) and hexadecapole (v4/ε4) flow coefficients for charged hadrons as a function of participant number Npart, using a modified Knudsen-number parametrization (Eq. 3) that relates scaling violations to the inverse Knudsen number K⁻¹ = β Npart^(1/3). The fits extracted the hydrodynamic limits v2h/ε2 and v4h/ε4 and the parameter β, from which the Knudsen number K, mean free path λ, and viscosity-to-entropy ratio η/s were derived using the relation η/s ≈ T λ cs, with T = 220 ± 20 MeV and cs = 0.47 ± 0.03 c from lattice QCD. The analysis used v2 and v4 data from approximately 3.4 × 10⁹ minimum-bias Au+Au events collected by PHENIX in 2007, with eccentricities ε2 and ε4 computed from the factorized Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (fKLN) model.

## Key numbers

- The extracted value of 4π(η/s) = 1.3 ± 0.3 for the lowest pT hadrons, corresponding to η/s ≈ 0.10 ± 0.02.
- The mean free path λ = 0.25–0.3 fm for the plasma.
- The degree of local equilibrium is within 5–10% of the value for a system with η/s = 1/4π.
- The temperature assumed for the plasma when flow develops is T = 220 ± 20 MeV.
- The speed of sound used is cs = 0.47 ± 0.03 c.
- The constant K0 in the fit is 0.7 ± 0.03.
- The hydrodynamic limit v2h/ε2 from fits to simulated data is within 5% of the calculated value of 0.23.
- The v2 measurements agree across different event planes to better than 5% for mid-central collisions; v4 agrees to better than 10% for mid-central collisions, degrading to ~10% and 20% for very central and peripheral collisions respectively.
- The resolution factor for the combined BBC reaction plane averages 0.33 over centrality, with a maximum ≈ 0.42 in mid-central collisions; the MPC and RXNio improve this by about 35% and 100% respectively.

## Context

This work extends prior hydrodynamic and transport calculations that model the quark gluon plasma produced at RHIC, specifically the hydrodynamic calculations of Song and Heinz (2008) and the transport calculations of Xu (2007), which showed good agreement with v2 data. The analysis builds on the Knudsen-number parametrization of scaling violations introduced by Bhalerao (2005) and Drescher (2007), and uses the finding by Teaney (2003) that viscous corrections to ideal hydrodynamics grow as (pT/T)² K. The extracted η/s value is in good agreement with prior extractions by Shuryak (2008), Romatschke (2007), Xu (2007), Drescher (2007), Lacey (2006), and Adare (2006). The results enable further constraints on transport coefficients of the QGP and support the interpretation of a strongly coupled plasma with short mean free path.
