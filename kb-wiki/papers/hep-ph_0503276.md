# Okubo-Zweig-Iizuka rule violation in photoproduction

> We investigate OZI rule violation in omega and phi-meson photoproduction off nucleons. Data on the total cross sections indicate a large phi/omega ratio of about 0.8 at the maximal available photon energy that is in good agreement with expectations from QCD. On the other hand, data at large four momentum transfer exhibit a ratio of about 0.07, showing that the perturbative QCD regime is not approached at |t| > 2 GeV^2 and photon energies E_γ< 4 GeV. The anomanously large phi/omega ratio at low energies, that is close to the reaction threshold, remains to be explained within nonperturbative QCD.

## Key claim

The OZI rule is strongly violated in vector meson photoproduction, with the phi/omega ratio reaching about 0.8 at high photon energies (consistent with flavor-blind QCD expectations) but dropping to about 0.07 at large four-momentum transfer squared (|t| > 2 GeV²) and low photon energies (E_γ < 4 GeV), indicating that the perturbative QCD regime is not approached in that kinematic region.

## Method

The authors compiled existing experimental data on total and differential cross sections for omega and phi-meson photoproduction off protons, covering photon energies from threshold to 200 GeV and four-momentum transfer squared up to about 4 GeV². They compared these data to calculations using a pomeron exchange model (with parameters β₀ and μ₀ fitted to the data), meson exchange models (π, σ, η exchange), and nucleon exchange mechanisms, extracting the phi/omega cross-section ratio as a function of both photon energy and momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- phi/omega ratio at maximal available photon energy: R_φ/ω ≈ 0.8 ± 0.2
- phi/omega ratio at large |t| > 2 GeV² and E_γ < 4 GeV: R_φ/ω ≈ 0.07 ± 0.01
- phi/omega ratio at low |t|: R_φ/ω ≈ 0.15
- phi/omega ratio from SU(3) mixing (ideal mixing angle deviation Δθ_V = 3.7°): R_φ/ω = 4.2 × 10⁻³
- phi/omega ratio from pp and πp reactions: R_φ/ω ≈ (13.4 ± 3.2) × 10⁻³
- phi/omega ratio from phi→ρπ and ω→ρπ decay analysis: R_φ/ω ≈ (12.5 ± 3.4) × 10⁻³
- Pomeron coupling constants: β₀ = 2.35 GeV⁻¹ for light quarks, β₀ = 1.9 GeV⁻¹ for strange quarks, β₀ = 0.45 GeV⁻¹ for charm quarks
- Pomeron trajectory parameters: α₀ = 1.008, α′ = 0.25 GeV⁻²
- phi/omega ratio at threshold: R_φ/ω > 0.03
- Ratio of φπγ to ωπγ coupling constants squared: ≈ (3.5 ± 0.2) × 10⁻³

## Context

This work extends systematic analyses of OZI rule violation by Sibirtsev et al. (2004) from pp and πp reactions into the photoproduction channel, and compares with pomeron exchange models by Donnachie and Landshoff (1984, 1992). The finding that the phi/omega ratio at large |t| is small (0.07) contradicts expectations from two-gluon exchange models and indicates that perturbative QCD is not yet reached at E_γ < 4 GeV and |t| > 2 GeV². The anomalously large ratio near threshold (greater than 0.03) remains unexplained within nonperturbative QCD and motivates new precise measurements from ELSA, JLab, and SPring-8, as well as development of new QCD methods at low energies.
