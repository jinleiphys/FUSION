# Testing a hadronic rescattering model for RHIC collisions using the subdivision method

> Recently it has been shown that calculations based on a hadronic rescattering model agree rather well with experimental results from the first RHIC run. Because of the large particle densities intrinsically present at the early time steps of Monte Carlo calculations attempting to model RHIC collisions undesirable artifacts resulting in non-causality may be present. These effects may compromise the results of such calculations. The subdivision method, which can remove such artifacts, has been used to test the present rescattering model calculations. It is shown that no appreciable changes are seen in the present calculations in using the subdivision method, thus strengthing the confidence in the results of this rescattering model for RHIC.

## Key claim

The hadronic rescattering model for RHIC Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=130 GeV is shown to be free of non-causal numerical artifacts, because applying the subdivision method with a factor of l=5 produces no appreciable changes in the calculated pion observables compared to the standard l=1 calculation, within statistical errors.

## Method

A semi-classical Monte Carlo calculation simulates hadronization, rescattering, and freeze-out for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=130 GeV using isospin-averaged s-wave and p-wave cross sections from Prakash et al. (1993). The subdivision method is applied by simultaneously increasing the particle density by a factor l=5 and decreasing the scattering cross sections by the same factor, and the resulting pion transverse momentum distributions, elliptic flow, and HBT parameters are compared between l=1 and l=5 calculations for a non-central impact parameter of 8 fm.

## Key numbers

- Hadronization model parameters used: initial temperature parameter T=300 MeV, rapidity width sigma_y=2.4, and hadronization proper time tau_had=1 fm/c.
- For central collisions (b=0 fm), freeze-out yields approximately 5000 pions, 500 kaons, and 650 nucleons per event.
- The l=5 subdivision sample required 420 CPU-hours on a 1 GHz PC processor, generating 124 events (compared to 1640 events for l=1).
- Time step used in the rescattering calculation: dt=0.1 fm/c; calculation carried out to 100 fm/c, with most rescattering finishing by about 30 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the hadronic rescattering model previously applied to SPS Pb+Pb collisions by Humanic (1998) to RHIC energies, where it had already shown agreement with experimental data for radial flow, elliptic flow, and HBT measurements from the first RHIC run. The subdivision method, developed by Zhang (1998) and Molnar (2000) to remove non-causal artifacts in high-density Monte Carlo scattering calculations, is used here to validate the existing rescattering code. The results enable confidence in using this model for further RHIC studies without requiring computationally expensive subdivision corrections.
