# Gapless superfluidity in neutron stars: Thermal properties

> The interior of mature neutron stars is expected to contain superfluid neutrons and superconducting protons. The influence of temperature and currents on superfluid properties is studied within the self-consistent time-dependent nuclear energy-density functional theory. We find that this theory predicts the existence of a regime in which nucleons are superfluid (the order parameter remains finite) even though the energy spectrum of quasiparticle excitations exhibits no gap. We show that the disappearance of the gap leads to a specific heat that is not exponentially suppressed at low temperatures as in the BCS regime but can be comparable to that in the normal phase. Introducing some dimensionless effective superfluid velocity, we show that the behavior of the specific heat is essentially universal and we derive general approximate analytical formulas for applications to neutron-star cooling simulations.

## Key claim
The paper claims that within self-consistent time-dependent nuclear energy-density functional theory, neutron and proton superfluids in neutron stars can exist in a gapless regime where the order parameter remains finite but the quasiparticle energy spectrum has no gap, leading to a specific heat that is not exponentially suppressed at low temperatures. This regime occurs for effective superfluid velocities between Landau's critical velocity and a higher critical velocity, and the behavior of the specific heat is shown to be essentially universal when expressed in terms of a dimensionless effective superfluid velocity.

## Method
The authors solved the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) equations exactly for homogeneous superfluid mixtures with stationary flows in neutron star outer core matter (npeμ matter in beta equilibrium), using the Brussels-Montreal functional BSk24. They derived analytical expressions for the quasiparticle energy spectrum, gap equation, and particle number conservation in terms of dimensionless ratios, then calculated the critical temperature, Landau critical velocity, and the critical velocity for complete destruction of superfluidity. The density of quasiparticle states and specific heat were computed across the transition from gapped to gapless superfluidity.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of critical temperature to zero-temperature pairing gap follows the universal BCS scaling: k_B T_cq^(0) / Δ_q^(0) = exp(γ)/π ≈ 0.56693, where γ ≈ 0.57722 is the Euler-Mascheroni constant.
- The critical effective superfluid velocity for complete destruction of superfluidity at zero temperature is V_cq^(0) ≈ (e/2) × (Δ_q^(0) / (ħ k_Fq)) ≈ 1.35914 V_Lq, where V_Lq is Landau's critical velocity and e ≈ 2.71828.
- In the weak-coupling approximation, Landau's critical velocity reduces to V_Lq ≈ Δ_q^(0) / (ħ k_Fq).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of neutron-proton superfluid mixtures using time-dependent nuclear energy-density functional theory (Chamel and Allard, 2019, 2020, 2021) by explicitly calculating critical temperatures and velocities and identifying the gapless superfluid regime. The gapless phase has been known in terrestrial superconductors (Parmenter, 1962) and superfluids (Vollhardt, 1978) but had not been studied in neutron stars. Prior work on superfluid currents in neutron stars by Gusakov (2013) and Leinson (2017, 2018) used Landau's theory of Fermi liquids, while the present work provides a self-consistent microscopic framework. The results enable more realistic neutron-star cooling simulations by providing analytical formulas for the specific heat in the gapless regime.
