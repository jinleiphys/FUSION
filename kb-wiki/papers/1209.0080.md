# Nuclear Symmetry Energy from QCD sum rules

> We calculate the nucleon self-energies in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter using QCD sum rules. Taking the difference of these for the neutron and proton enables us to express the potential part of the nuclear symmetry energy in terms of local operators. We find that the scalar (vector) self-energy part gives a negative (positive) contribution to the nuclear symmetry energy which is consistent with the results from relativistic mean-field theories. Moreover, we find that an important contribution to the negative contribution of the scalar self-energy comes from the twist-4 matrix elements, whose leading density dependence can be extracted from deep inelastic scattering experiments. This suggests that the twist-4 contribution partly mimics the exchange of the $δ$ meson and that it constitutes an essential part in the origin of the nuclear symmetry energy from QCD. Our result also extends an early success of QCD sum rule method in understanding the symmetric nuclear matter in terms of QCD variables to the asymmetric nuclear matter case.

## Key claim

The nuclear symmetry energy can be expressed in terms of QCD local operators, with the scalar self-energy giving a negative contribution and the vector self-energy giving a positive contribution; the twist-4 matrix elements provide an important negative contribution to the scalar part, partly mimicking the exchange of the δ meson, but the results rely on the linear density approximation and the assumption that the continuum threshold remains at the vacuum value of 1.5 GeV.

## Method

The authors applied QCD sum rules to calculate the nucleon self-energies in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, using the Ioffe interpolating current and performing the operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension-six operators. They followed the formalism of Cohen, Furnstahl, and Griegel (1991), writing energy dispersion relations at fixed three-momentum and applying Borel transformations to isolate the quasi-nucleon pole contributions. The in-medium condensates were estimated using the linear density approximation, with isospin asymmetry effects incorporated through proton matrix elements and parton distribution functions from Gluck, Reya, and Vogt (1991).

## Key numbers

- The isospin triplet quark condensate ratio: ⟨[q̄q]₁⟩_p / ⟨[q̄q]₀⟩_p ≈ 0.43, derived from baryon octet masses (m_Ξ⁰ = 1315 MeV, m_Ξ⁻ = 1321 MeV, m_Σ⁺ = 1190 MeV, m_Σ⁻ = 1197 MeV) with m_s = 150 MeV and m_q = 5 MeV.
- The isospin singlet quark condensate: ⟨[q̄q]₀⟩_p = σ_N/(2m_q) ≈ 4.5, using σ_N = 45 MeV and m_q = 5 MeV.
- Vacuum quark condensate: ⟨q̄q⟩_vac = -(263 MeV)³, from the Gellmann-Oakes-Renner relation with m_π = 138 MeV and f_π = 98 MeV.
- Twist-3 matrix element: M_N² e₂(μ²) ≈ 0.3 GeV², taken from Belyaev and Ioffe (1982).
- Twist-2 parton distribution moments: Aᵤ₂(μ²) ≈ 0.74 and Aᵈ₂(μ²) ≈ 0.36 at μ² = 0.25 GeV² (leading order), giving the isospin ratio ℛ_{A₂} ≈ 0.35.
- Continuum threshold: ω₀ = 1.5 GeV, taken as the same as the vacuum value.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach for symmetric nuclear matter developed by Cohen, Furnstahl, and Griegel (1991) and Jin, Nielsen, and collaborators (1992-1994) to the asymmetric case, providing a QCD-based foundation for the nuclear symmetry energy that complements relativistic mean-field theories. The results enable future studies of the density dependence of the symmetry energy at high densities, particularly through the twist-4 matrix elements whose behavior can be constrained by deep inelastic scattering experiments, and connect to the broader effort to understand neutron-rich matter relevant to neutron stars and rare isotope physics.
