# Is the X(3872) a bound state ?

> All existing experimental evidence of the bound state nature of the $X(3872)$ relies on considering its decay products with a finite experimental spectral mass resolution which is typically $Δm \ge 2 $MeV and much larger than its alleged binding energy, $B_X=0.00(18)$MeV. On the other hand, we have found recently that there is a neat cancellation in the $1^{++}$ channel for the invariant $D \bar D^*$ mass around the threshold between the continuum and bound state contribution. This is very much alike a similar cancellation in the proton-neutron continuum with the deuteron in the $1^{++}$ channel. Based on comparative fits of experimental cross section deuteron and $X(3872)$ prompt production in pp collisions data with a finite $p_T$ to a common Tsallis distribution we find a strong argument questioning the bound state nature of the state but also explaining the large observed production rate likely consistent with a half-bound state.

## Key claim

The paper questions whether the X(3872) is a true bound state, arguing that the experimental mass resolution (Δm ≥ 2 MeV) is much larger than its alleged binding energy (B_X = 0.00(18) MeV), and that a cancellation between the bound state and continuum contributions in the 1⁺⁺ channel makes the bound state nature ambiguous; the authors suggest the state may instead be a half-bound state at the unitarity limit.

## Method

The authors compare the prompt production cross sections of the X(3872), deuteron, and ψ(2S) in pp collisions by fitting their transverse momentum (p_T) distributions to a common Tsallis distribution. They then analyze the effect of finite experimental mass resolution (Δm = 2σ, with σ = 5-6 MeV) on the observed occupation number by folding the density of states from the 1⁺⁺ channel (including both bound state and continuum contributions) with the Tsallis distribution, using effective range parameters to determine the phase shifts in the D\bar{D}* channel.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of X(3872): B_X = 0.00(18) MeV (from PDG)
- Experimental mass resolution: Δm ≥ 2 MeV (typically 1-2 MeV)
- Deuteron binding energy: B_d = 2.2 MeV
- Best-fit Tsallis parameters from combined fit: q = 1.122 ± 0.001, T = 7.017 ± 0.07 MeV
- X(3872) to deuteron production ratio: N_X·B_X / N_d = 0.046 (+0.016/-0.013)
- ψ(2S) to deuteron production ratio: N_ψ / N_d = 1.09 (+0.16/-0.17)
- Average p_T for deuteron: ⟨p_T⟩_d = 1.102 ± 0.007 GeV
- Average p_T for X(3872): ⟨p_T⟩_X = 2.249 ± 0.015 GeV
- Average p_T for ψ(2S): ⟨p_T⟩_ψ = 2.142 ± 0.014 GeV
- Branching fraction B(X → J/ψπ⁺π⁻) = 4.5 (+2.3/-1.2)% from BESIII analysis
- Ratio of occupation number with vs. without finite resolution: N_{Δm}/N_X ∼ 0.5-0.6 for reasonable B_X and σ values

## Context

This work extends the Dashen-Kane cancellation mechanism (Dashen and Kane, 1974), originally applied to the deuteron in the np 1⁺⁺ channel, to the X(3872) in the D\bar{D}* 1⁺⁺ channel. The authors build on their previous studies of this cancellation at finite temperature (Ortega and Arriola, 2017) and compare with the analysis of Kang and Oller (2016) on bound vs. virtual state character. The results challenge the standard molecular interpretation of the X(3872) and suggest that improved experimental resolution of its decay products is needed to determine its binding character definitively.
