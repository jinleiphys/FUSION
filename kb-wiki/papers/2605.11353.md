# Ab initio calculation of symmetry-breaking observables

> Symmetry-violating observables such as the nuclear anapole and Schiff moments provide sensitive probes of the fundamental symmetries of nature and physics beyond the Standard Model. Their interpretation has been hindered, however, by the lack of ab initio nuclear structure calculations in the medium-mass and heavy nuclei of interest to experimentalists. To provide them, we introduce a new version of the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) designed to target parity-violating operators. By generalizing the IMSRG flow equations to evolve the weak symmetry-breaking Hamiltonian - and the anapole or Schiff operators - alongside the strong nuclear Hamiltonian, we construct a systematically improvable framework for computing these parity-violating moments. We benchmark the method against the no-core shell model in light nuclei and obtain the first ab initio predictions of the anapole moment in $^{29}$Si and the Schiff moments in $^{129}$Xe. These heavier systems are of direct experimental interest.

## Key claim

The authors introduce a new extension of the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) that can evolve parity-violating operators alongside the strong nuclear Hamiltonian, enabling the first ab initio predictions of the anapole moment in 29Si and the Schiff moment in 129Xe, though the results are preliminary with only one interaction and no variation of model parameters.

## Method

The authors generalize the IMSRG flow equations to evolve a weak symmetry-breaking Hamiltonian and the anapole or Schiff operators together with the strong nuclear Hamiltonian, using a power-counting parameter to track the small parity-violating contributions. They apply the method within the valence-space formulation of the IMSRG (VS-IMSRG) at the IMSRG(2) approximation level, where operators are normal ordered with respect to a reference state and truncated at the two-body level, then diagonalize the effective Hamiltonian using the large-scale shell-model code Kshell. The method is benchmarked against no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations in light nuclei using the chiral interaction NN+3N(lnl).

## Key numbers

- Anapole moment parameter kappa_A for 29Si: 7.7 x 10^-2, which is several times larger than the single-particle estimate of 1.2(8) x 10^-2
- Schiff moment coefficients for 129Xe: S = -0.021 g gbar_pi^(0) - 0.010 g gbar_pi^(1) - 0.044 g gbar_pi^(2) (in units of e fm^3)
- For 2H, the PV-IMSRG gives kappa_A = 0.0024 compared to NCSM value of 0.0025
- For 15N, the PV-IMSRG gives kappa_A = 0.094 compared to NCSM value of 0.088
- For 15N Schiff coefficient a_0: PV-IMSRG gives -0.0062 e fm^3, NCSM gives -0.0066 e fm^3
- For 15N Schiff coefficient a_1: PV-IMSRG gives 0.0067 e fm^3, NCSM gives 0.0092 e fm^3
- For 15N Schiff coefficient a_2: PV-IMSRG gives -0.0170 e fm^3, NCSM gives -0.0141 e fm^3

## Context

This work extends the IMSRG method, previously applied only to symmetry-conserving observables such as ground-state energies, charge radii, and magnetic moments (Hergert 2016, Simonis 2017), to parity-violating observables for the first time. It provides an ab initio alternative to phenomenological models based on mean-field theory and approximations to P-violating nucleon-nucleon forces (Dmitriev 1994, Flambaum 1997, Haxton 2002) that have been used for anapole and Schiff moment calculations. The method enables future work on heavier isotopes such as 199Hg, which has the best experimental limit on an atomic electric dipole moment, and opens the door to quantifying uncertainties associated with model-space and flow-equation truncation.
