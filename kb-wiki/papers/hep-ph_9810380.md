# Flavor Asymmetry of the Sea Quarks in the Baryon Octet

> We show that the chiral $SU(n)\otimes SU(n)$ flavor symmetry on the null-plane severely restricts the sea quarks in the baryon octet. It predicts large asymmetry for the light sea quarks $(u,d,s)$, and universality and abundance for the heavy sea quarks. Further it is shown that existence of the heavy sea quarks constrained by the same symmetry reduces the theoretical value of the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule substantially.

## Key claim

The chiral SU(n) ⊗ SU(n) flavor symmetry on the null-plane, combined with fixed-mass sum rules derived from current anti-commutation relations, severely restricts the sea quark content of the baryon octet; the predictions for light sea quark asymmetry and heavy sea quark universality depend on the regularization of divergent sum rules and assume the soft pomeron with intercept α_P(0) < 1 at small t.

## Method

The author derives sum rules from current anti-commutation relations on the null-plane, regularizing divergent integrals by analytically continuing from the non-forward direction using the soft pomeron model. The matrix elements for the baryon octet are decomposed into F and D terms using SU(3) flavor symmetry, and these are extended to SU(n) with n ≥ 4 to constrain heavy sea quark distributions. The same symmetry is applied to spin-dependent matrix elements to constrain the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule.

## Key numbers

- Modified Gottfried sum rule value: (1/3π) P∫ dα/α A₃^p(α,0) = 0.26
- Mean hypercharge sum rule value: (√3/3π) P∫ dα/α A₈^p(α,0) = 2.12
- F-term: F̃ = 0.89
- D-term: D̃ = -0.50
- Pomeron intercept parameter: α_P(0) = 1.0808
- Pomeron normalization constant: a = 1.2
- Proton light sea quark mean I₃: ⟨I₃⟩ = -0.055
- Proton light sea quark mean hypercharge: ⟨Y⟩ = 0.56
- Proton light sea quark mean charge: ⟨Q⟩ = 0.23
- For the proton: ⟨λ_c - λ_d^p⟩ = 0.5 and ⟨λ_c - λ_s^p⟩ = 1.4
- Experimental values used: F = 0.46 ± 0.01 and D = 0.79 ± 0.01

## Context

This work extends the author's previous derivations of the modified Gottfried sum rule (Koretune 1984, 1993, 1995) by applying chiral SU(n) ⊗ SU(n) symmetry on the null-plane to the full baryon octet. It provides a framework that predicts large flavor asymmetry for light sea quarks (u, d, s) and universal abundance for heavy sea quarks (c, b, t), contradicting the assumption that only light quarks contribute significantly to the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule. The results enable future tests of intrinsic charm in nucleons and suggest that heavy sea quarks can resolve the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule discrepancy without requiring large gluon polarization as proposed by Mankiewicz (1990).
