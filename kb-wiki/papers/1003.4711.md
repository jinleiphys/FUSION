# Microscopic Study of the triple-$α$ Reaction

> We present a microscopic dynamical study of the reactions involving three $^{4}$He clusters. We show that the much discussed triple-$α$ linear chain configuration of $^{12}$C is formed with a certain lifetime and subsequently decays into a triangular configuration of $^{12}$C and then to a configuration near the ground state. Time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory coupled with a density constraint is used to study the properties of these configurations. We find a sequence of dynamical transitions analogous to the suggested astrophysical mechanism for the formation of $^{12}$C nuclei.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the triple-α linear chain configuration of ¹²C is formed dynamically in TDHF simulations and subsequently decays to a triangular configuration and then to a more compact shape, but only for specific initial alignments of the ⁸Be nucleus (β=0°) and at energies above a threshold of 1.3 MeV; the abstract's "certain lifetime" is qualified by strong dependence on impact parameter, energy, and neutron number.

## Method
The authors performed fully three-dimensional Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations using the Skyrme SLy4 interaction, without symmetry assumptions, for head-on collisions of ⁴He+⁸Be and triple collisions of three ⁴He nuclei. They coupled TDHF with a density constraint method to extract potential energy curves as a function of ion-ion separation distance R, and tracked single-particle parity signatures during the time evolution to relate dynamical mode changes to the parity structure of the linear chain state.

## Key numbers
- The ⁴He+⁸Be head-on collision at E_c.m. = 2 MeV forms a linear chain configuration that oscillates for times less than 2,500 fm/c before bending to a triangular shape.
- The bending motion in the triangular configuration continues for approximately 1,000 fm/c before relaxing to a more compact shape.
- The potential barrier height for the combined system ranges from 1.24 to 1.44 MeV depending on the ⁸Be alignment angle β, with the lowest barrier at β=0°.
- The lowest energy at which the linear chain configuration forms in ⁴He+⁸Be is 1.3 MeV, where the lifetime increases to about 2,875 fm/c.
- For the triple ⁴He collision at E_c.m. = 2 MeV, the system spends about 6,000 fm/c in the linear chain configuration before switching to bending and compact modes.
- Using ⁹Be instead of ⁸Be reduces the lifetime of the linear chain state by approximately 30%.

## Context
This work extends static Hartree-Fock studies of cluster configurations in neutron-rich C isotopes by Maruhn et al. (2006, 2010) and provides the first dynamical TDHF observation of mode changes from linear chain to triangular to compact configurations, which had not been possible in earlier TDHF calculations due to axial symmetry assumptions (Umar et al., 1985). The results enable future microscopic studies of the triple-α reaction mechanism and the role of linear chain states in nuclear astrophysics, and suggest that the parity signature of the linear chain state (two positive parity, one negative parity single-particle states) can be used to identify such configurations experimentally.
