# Friedel formula and Krein's theorem in complex potential scattering theory

> In this work, the generalization of Friedel formula and Krein's theorem in complex potential scattering theory is presented. The consequence of various symmetry constraints on dynamical system are discussed. In addition, Muskhelishvili-Omnès representation of Krein's theorem is also given and discussed.

Here is the wiki page body based on the provided paper.

## Key claim
The Friedel formula and Krein's theorem can be generalized to complex potential scattering theory, maintaining similar mathematical forms to those in real potential theory, but only as a consequence of balanced gain and loss in dual (absorbing and emissive) systems. The absorptive part of the Green's function is no longer related to the density of states of a single system and is generally a complex function.

## Method
The authors derive the generalized Friedel formula for one-dimensional complex potentials using the biorthogonal basis of dual systems (one absorbing, one emissive) and the associated Moller and S-matrix operators. They then extend the derivation to include specific symmetry constraints, such as spatial inversion (P) and parity-time (PT) symmetry, showing how the formula simplifies in each case. Finally, they present the Muskhelishvili-Omnes representation of Krein's theorem, connecting the integrated Green's function to a dispersion integral over the complex phaseshifts.

## Key numbers
- The generalized Friedel formula for complex local potentials is given by: `(1/(4i)) Tr[ S̃†(k) dS(k)/dE - S(k) dS̃†(k)/dE ] = - Disc_E [ ∫ dx ⟨x| Ĝ(E) - Ĝ₀(E) |x⟩ ]`.
- Under PT symmetry, the Friedel formula reduces to: `(1/(2i)) d/dE ln(det[S(k)]) = d/dE [δ₊(k) + δ₋(k)]`, where δ₊ and δ₋ are real phaseshifts.
- For P-symmetric systems, the integrated Green's function is related to complex phaseshifts Δ₊ and Δ₋ by: `∫ dx ⟨x| Ĝ(E) - Ĝ₀(E) |x⟩ = - (1/π) [∫_{-∞}^{-E_L} + ∫₀^∞] dε (Δ₊(√(2mε)) + Δ₋(√(2mε))) / (ε - E)²`.

## Context
This work extends the classic Friedel formula (Friedel, 1954) and Krein's theorem (Krein, 1962), which are fundamental to real potential scattering theory in solid-state and nuclear physics, into the domain of non-Hermitian systems. The generalization is motivated by recent experimental and theoretical advances in PT-symmetric systems, such as those in optics (El-Ganainy, 2018) and atomic gases (Hang, 2014). The results enable the study of concepts like generalized time delay for particles scattering off complex barriers and provide a framework for applying dispersion relations (e.g., the Muskhelishvili-Omnes representation) to non-Hermitian scattering problems.
