# Proton and neutron electromagnetic radii and magnetic moments from lattice QCD

> We present results for the electromagnetic form factors of the proton and neutron computed on the $(2 + 1)$-flavor Coordinated Lattice Simulations (CLS) ensembles including both quark-connected and -disconnected contributions. The $Q^2$-, pion-mass, lattice-spacing, and finite-volume dependence of our form factor data is fitted simultaneously to the expressions resulting from covariant chiral perturbation theory including vector mesons amended by models for lattice artefacts. From these fits, we determine the electric and magnetic radii and the magnetic moments of the proton and neutron, as well as the Zemach radius of the proton. To assess the influence of systematic effects, we average over various cuts in the pion mass and the momentum transfer, as well as over different models for the lattice-spacing and finite-volume dependence, using weights derived from the Akaike Information Criterion (AIC).

## Key claim

The authors extract proton and neutron electromagnetic radii and magnetic moments from lattice QCD, including both quark-connected and -disconnected contributions, and find that their result for the proton electric radius is much closer to the PDG value (dominated by muonic hydrogen spectroscopy) and the PRad experiment than to the A1 experiment, while their proton magnetic radius agrees well with the A1 experiment and is in tension with other collected world data.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations on (2+1)-flavor CLS ensembles including both quark-connected and -disconnected contributions. They fitted the Q²-, pion-mass, lattice-spacing, and finite-volume dependence of the form factor data simultaneously to expressions from covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (BχPT) including vector mesons (ρ, ω, φ) and models for lattice artefacts, then averaged over various cuts in pion mass and momentum transfer using Akaike Information Criterion (AIC) weights.

## Key numbers

- Proton electric radius squared: 0.672(14)(18) fm² (statistical and systematic errors)
- Proton magnetic radius squared: 0.658(12)(8) fm²
- Neutron electric radius squared: -0.115(13)(7) fm²
- Neutron magnetic radius squared: 0.667(11)(16) fm²
- Proton magnetic moment: 2.739(63)(18) (in nuclear magnetons)
- Neutron magnetic moment: -1.893(39)(58) (in nuclear magnetons)
- Proton Zemach radius: 1.013(10)(12) fm
- Isovector electric radius squared: 0.785(22)(26) fm²
- Isovector magnetic radius squared: 0.663(11)(8) fm²
- Isovector magnetic moment: 4.62(10)(7) (in nuclear magnetons)
- Isoscalar electric radius squared: 0.554(18)(13) fm²
- Isoscalar magnetic radius squared: 0.657(30)(31) fm²
- Isoscalar magnetic moment: 2.47(11)(10) (in nuclear magnetons)
- Physical pion mass used: 134.8 MeV
- Maximum Q² for fit validity: 0.6 GeV²

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of nucleon electromagnetic form factors by simultaneously including quark-disconnected contributions and controlling all systematic uncertainties (excited-state contamination, discretization, finite-volume effects). It is the first lattice calculation to do so, unlike ETMC19 (Alexandrou 2019) which included disconnected contributions but did not perform continuum and infinite-volume extrapolation. The results contribute to the "proton radius puzzle" and the ongoing debate about the magnetic radius, and enable comparisons with experimental data from A1 (Bernauer 2014), PRad (Xiong 2019), and dispersive analyses (Lin 2021, Lin 2022).
