# Wigner functions and quantum kinetic theory of polarized photons

> We derive the Wigner functions of polarized photons in the Coulomb gauge with the $\hbar$ expansion applied to quantum field theory, and identify side-jump effects for massless photons. We also discuss the photonic chiral vortical effect for the Chern-Simons current and zilch vortical effect for the zilch current in local thermal equilibrium as a consistency check for our formalism. The results are found to be in agreement with those obtained from different approaches. Moreover, using the real-time formalism, we construct the quantum kinetic theory (QKT) for polarized photons. By further adopting a specific power counting scheme for the distribution functions, we provide a more succinct form of an effective QKT. This photonic QKT involves quantum corrections associated with self-energy gradients in the collision term, which are analogous to the side-jump corrections pertinent to spin-orbit interactions in the chiral kinetic theory for massless fermions. The same theoretical framework can also be directly applied to weakly coupled gluons in the absence of background color fields.

## Key claim

The authors derive Wigner functions for polarized photons in the Coulomb gauge using an $\hbar$ expansion of quantum field theory, identifying side-jump effects for massless photons and constructing a quantum kinetic theory (QKT) with collision terms that include quantum corrections from self-energy gradients, analogous to those in chiral kinetic theory for massless fermions.

## Method

The authors employ the spinor-helicity formalism to express polarization vectors of right/left-handed photons in covariant form, then quantize the gauge fields and compute the lesser and greater propagators via Wigner transformation. They expand the integrand to $\mathcal{O}(\hbar)$ and derive explicit forms of the Wigner functions, subsequently using the real-time formalism with a specific power counting scheme to construct the effective QKT for polarized photons.

## Key numbers

- The Wigner functions are derived up to $\mathcal{O}(\hbar)$ in the Coulomb gauge, with the Berry connections $a^{\mu}_{\pm}(q) \equiv {\rm i}c_{\rm R}^{(\pm)\dagger} \partial_{q_{\mu}} c_{\rm R}^{(\pm)}$ appearing in the frame-dependent distribution function ${f}_{\rm R}(q,X)\equiv \big(1+\hbar[a_{+}^{\alpha}(q)-a_-^{\alpha}(q)] \partial_{\alpha}\big) \check{f}_{\rm R}(q,X)$.
- The full lesser propagator for photons is given by $G^{<}_{\mu\nu}=2\pi\delta(q^2)\text{sgn}(q\cdot n)\big[ (P^{(n)}_{\mu\nu} {f}_{\rm V} - \frac{\hbar q_{\perp(\mu}S^{(n)}_{\nu)\alpha}\partial^{\alpha}}{2(q\cdot n)^2}{f}_{\rm A}) -{\rm i}(S_{\mu\nu}^{(n)}{f}_{\rm A}+ \frac{\hbar q_{\perp[\mu}\partial_{\perp\nu]}}{2(q\cdot n)^2}{f}_{\rm V}) \big]$.
- The phase-space CS current density is $\hat{\mathcal{K}}^{\mu}(q,X)= 2 q^{\mu}{f}_{\rm A}^{\gamma}+\hbar S_{\gamma}^{\mu\nu}\partial_{\nu}{f}_{\rm V}^{\gamma}$, where the spin tensor term is twice larger than the corresponding term for massless fermions.

## Context

This work extends the Wigner-function approach previously applied to Weyl and Dirac fermions (Hidaka 2016, Hattori 2019) to polarized photons, providing a first-principle derivation from QED that complements the approach of Huang (2020) which used Maxwell's equations and gauge constraints. The formalism enables future study of entangled spin transport of quarks and gluons in quark-gluon plasmas, and can be directly applied to weakly coupled gluons in the absence of background color fields.
