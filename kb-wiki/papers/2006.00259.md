# $g$-factor and static quadrupole moment for the wobbling mode in $^{133}$La

> The $g$-factor and static quadrupole moment for the wobbling mode in the nuclide $^{133}$La are investigated as functions of the spin $I$by employing the particle rotor model. The model can reproduce the available experimental data of $g$-factor and static quadrupole moment. The properties of the $g$-factor and static quadrupole moment as functions of $I$ are interpreted by analyzing the angular momentum geometry of the collective rotor, proton-particle, and total nuclear system. It is demonstrated that the experimental value of the $g$-factor at the bandhead of the yrast band leads to the conclusion that the rotor angular momentum is $R\simeq 2$. Furthermore, the variation of the $g$-factor with the spin $I$ yields the information that the angular momenta of the proton-particle and total nuclear system are oriented parallel to each other. The negative values of the static quadrupole moment over the entire spin region are caused by an alignment of the total angular momentum mainly along the short axis. Static quadrupole moment differences between the wobbling and yrast band originate from a wobbling excitation with respect to the short axis.

## Key claim

The particle rotor model reproduces the available experimental data for the g-factor and static quadrupole moment in 133La, but the wobbling mode in this nucleus corresponds to an intermediate coupling scheme between transverse and longitudinal wobbling, where the moments of inertia for the short and medium axes are nearly equal, making the simple classification inappropriate.

## Method

The authors employed the particle rotor model (PRM) with triaxial deformation parameters β=0.16 and γ=26°, using spin-dependent moments of inertia for the short axis (Js = [9.125 + 0.657(I-j)] ħ²/MeV) and fixed values for the medium and long axes (Jm=15.33 ħ²/MeV, Jl=2.92 ħ²/MeV). The g-factor was calculated using the generalized Landé formula with gR=Z/A=0.43 for the rotor and gp=1.21 for the h11/2 valence proton, while the static quadrupole moment was computed from expectation values of the squared total angular momentum components along the three principal axes.

## Key numbers

- Experimental g-factor at the bandhead of the yrast band: g = 1.16 ± 0.07, with the PRM reproducing g = 1.16
- Experimental static quadrupole moment at the bandhead: |Q| = 1.71 ± 0.34 e·b, with the PRM giving Q(11/2) = -1.29 e·b
- Rotor angular momentum at the bandhead: R ≃ 2, determined from the g-factor at I=11/2
- Moments of inertia: Jm = 15.33 ħ²/MeV, Jl = 2.92 ħ²/MeV, and Js = [9.125 + 0.657(I-j)] ħ²/MeV
- Triaxial deformation parameters: β = 0.16 and γ = 26°
- Monte Carlo shell-model calculations gave Q = -1.25 e·b for the 11/2- state

## Context

This work extends the particle rotor model analysis of wobbling bands in 133La previously reported by Biswas (2019), which explained the unexpected increase of wobbling energy with spin through nearly equal moments of inertia for the short and medium axes. The study is motivated by recent measurements of the g-factor and static quadrupole moment by Laskar (2020), and it contrasts with the transverse wobbling observed in the isotones 135Pr (Matta 2015, Sensharma 2019) and 131Cs, which show decreasing wobbling energy with spin. The results enable future experimental measurements of g-factors and quadrupole moments at higher spins to test the theoretical predictions for the wobbling motion about the short axis.
