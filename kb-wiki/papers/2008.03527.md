# Axial and pseudoscalar form factors from charged current quasielastic neutrino-nucleon scattering

> We study the scattering of neutrinos on polarized and unpolarized free nucleons, and also the polarization of recoil particles in these scatters. In contrast to electromagnetic processes, the parity-violating weak interaction gives rise to large spin asymmetries at leading order. Future polarization measurements could provide independent access to the proton axial structure and allow the first extraction of the pseudoscalar form factor from neutrino data without the conventional partially conserved axial current (PCAC) ansatz and assumptions about the pion-pole dominance. The pseudoscalar form factor can be accessed with precise measurements with muon (anti)neutrinos of a few hundreds $\mathrm{MeV}$ of energy or with tau (anti)neutrinos. The axial form factor can be extracted from scattering measurements using accelerator neutrinos of all energies.

# Axial and pseudoscalar form factors from charged current quasielastic neutrino-nucleon scattering

## Key claim
Future polarization measurements in neutrino-nucleon charged current quasielastic scattering could provide independent access to the proton axial structure and allow the first extraction of the pseudoscalar form factor from neutrino data without relying on the partially conserved axial current (PCAC) ansatz and assumptions about pion-pole dominance, though axial contributions to asymmetries are slightly kinematically enhanced compared to pseudoscalar ones.

## Method
The author calculates single-spin asymmetries for target nucleon, recoiling nucleon, and recoiling lepton polarizations in charged current quasielastic (anti)neutrino-nucleon scattering, expressing these observables in terms of nucleon form factors through structure-dependent parameters A, B, and C generalized from the unpolarized cross section formalism. The sensitivity of each asymmetry to the axial form factor F_A and pseudoscalar form factor F_P is evaluated at various beam energies by varying each form factor by 20% from expected values and comparing the resulting changes.

## Key numbers
- The axial form factor is currently known to 10-20% and is the main source of error in microscopic description of neutrino interactions at the nucleon level
- The pseudoscalar form factor can be accessed with muon (anti)neutrinos of a few hundred MeV energy (ideally 150-250 MeV) or with tau (anti)neutrinos
- The target transverse single-spin asymmetry T_t in antineutrino-proton scattering can reach up to 85% and shows sensitivity to F_A at the 10-20% level at beam energies of 300 MeV and 500 MeV
- The longitudinal recoil lepton asymmetry L_l is typically above 80% by absolute value and is predicted with percent or subpercent level precision
- The pseudoscalar form factor contribution in electron (anti)neutrino scattering is suppressed by factors m_e^2/E_nu^2, m_e^2/M^2, and m_e^2/(M E_nu)
- At Q^2 ~ 0.88 m_mu^2, the pseudoscalar form factor can be extracted from muon capture on the proton

## Context
This work extends prior studies of polarization observables in neutrino-nucleon scattering by groups such as Bilenky (2013), Graczyk (2019), and others, and is motivated by the need for improved nucleon form factor inputs for experiments at DUNE, Hyper-K, and ESSnuSB. The pseudoscalar form factor has previously been related to the axial form factor via the PCAC ansatz and pion-pole dominance, an approximation only expected valid at low momentum transfers; recent lattice QCD calculations (Bali 2019, Alexandrou 2020) show disagreement on whether PCAC holds at Q^2 ~ 1 GeV^2. The proposed polarization measurements on hydrogen and deuterium targets, as outlined in the Snowmass 2021 Letter of Intent "Neutrino Scattering Measurements on Hydrogen and Deuterium," could provide model-independent extractions of both form factors simultaneously.
