# Semiclassical shell-structure moment of inertia within the phase-space approach

> The moment of inertia for nuclear collective rotations was derived within the semiclassical approach based on the cranking model and the Strutinsky shell-correction method by using the non-perturbative periodic-orbit theory in the phase space variables. This moment of inertia for adiabatic (statistical-equilibrium) rotations can be approximated by the generalized rigid-body moment of inertia accounting for the shell corrections of the particle density. A semiclassical phase-space trace formula allows to express quite accurately the shell components of the moment of inertia in terms of the free-energy shell corrections for integrable and partially chaotic Fermi systems, in good agreement with the quantum calculations.

## Key claim

The moment of inertia for nuclear collective rotations can be approximated by the generalized rigid-body moment of inertia, with its shell components expressed in terms of the free-energy shell corrections via a semiclassical phase-space trace formula; this relation holds for integrable and partially chaotic Fermi systems, but the derivation relies on a nearly-local approximation and a statistical averaging over many quantum states, and the semiclassical results agree well with quantum calculations only when the Thomas-Fermi approximation is used for the phase-space average factor.

## Method

The authors start from the cranking model and the Strutinsky shell-correction method, using the non-perturbative Gutzwiller periodic-orbit theory in phase-space variables. They split the semiclassical Green's function into a direct-path (CT0) contribution, which yields the smooth Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) part, and a reflected-trajectory (CT1) contribution, which gives the shell correction. By transforming the spatial coordinates to phase-space variables and applying the stationary phase method, they derive a trace formula linking the shell component of the moment of inertia to the free-energy shell correction via a phase-space average of \(r_\perp^2/\varepsilon\). The method is applied to a spheroidal cavity mean-field potential and compared with full quantum calculations.

## Key numbers

- The semiclassical condition requires \(S/\hbar \sim k_F R \sim N^{1/3} \gg 1\), where \(k_F\) is the Fermi momentum and \(R\) is the mean nuclear radius.
- The rotation excitation energy \(\hbar\omega\) must be smaller than the distance between major shells \(\hbar\Omega\), with \(\hbar\Omega \approx \varepsilon_F / N^{1/3}\).
- For the spheroidal cavity potential, the Thomas-Fermi average \(\langle r_\perp^2 / \varepsilon \rangle_{\rm TF} = (a^2 + b^2) / (3\lambda)\), where \(a\) and \(b\) are the semi-axes and \(\lambda\) is the chemical potential.
- The free-energy shell correction decreases exponentially with temperature as \(\delta F \sim \exp(-\pi t_{\rm PO} T / \hbar)\), where \(t_{\rm PO}\) is the period of the periodic orbit.
- Quantum and semiclassical shell corrections are compared for deformations \(\eta = b/a = 1.2\) and \(\eta = 2.0\) (Figs. 2-5), with the latter showing enhanced shell structure due to bifurcations of 3D orbits from parent equatorial orbits.

## Context

This work extends the non-perturbative periodic-orbit theory approach previously applied to the harmonic-oscillator mean field (Magner et al., 2010) to a spheroidal cavity potential, and generalizes the semiclassical description of the moment of inertia shell corrections to integrable and partially chaotic Fermi systems. It contrasts with the perturbative approach of Creagh (1990) used by Dossing et al. (2004), which did not include contributions from equatorial orbits or 3D periodic orbits. The derived relation \(\delta\Theta \approx m \langle r_\perp^2 / \varepsilon \rangle \delta F\) enables the calculation of moment-of-inertia shell corrections for larger rotational frequencies and deformations (\(\eta \sim 1.5-2.0\)) where bifurcations dominate, and can be applied to other mean-field potentials such as the deformed harmonic oscillator.
