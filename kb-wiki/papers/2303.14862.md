# New derivation of Time-Independent Perturbation Theory

> We propose a new derivation of Time-Independent Perturbation Theory (PT) that has a fundamental advantage over the usual derivations presented in textbooks on Quantum Mechanics (QM): it is simpler and much shorter. As such, it can provide an easier and quicker way for students to learn PT, than afforded by current methods. In spite of that, our approach does not require the potentials to be energy independent or the inverse free Green function $G_0^{-1}(E)$ to be a linear function of energy $E$, as is the case in QM, and can be applied directly to various extensions of QM including Relativistic QM, the Bethe-Salpeter equation, and all kinds of quasipotential approaches in Quantum Field Theory.

## Key claim

The authors derive time-independent perturbation theory using a novel algebraic trick that reduces the problem to an equation with a separable kernel, yielding exact expressions for the perturbed energy and wavefunction that are simpler and much shorter to obtain than standard textbook derivations. The derivation does not require potentials to be energy independent or the inverse free Green function to be a linear function of energy, making it applicable beyond standard quantum mechanics to relativistic quantum mechanics, the Bethe-Salpeter equation, and quasipotential approaches in quantum field theory.

## Method

The authors start from the bound state equations for the perturbed and unperturbed systems written in terms of the inverse free Green function \(G_0^{-1}(E)\) and potentials \(K_0\) and \(K_1\). They use the pole structure of the unperturbed interacting Green function \(G_u(E)\), which has a pole at the unperturbed bound state energy \({\cal E}_n\) with residue \(|\phi_n\rangle\langle\phi_n|\), to rewrite the perturbed bound state equation as an equation with a separable kernel. This separable form is solved algebraically for the scalar combination \(\langle\phi_n|K_1|\psi_n\rangle\), yielding an exact implicit equation for the perturbed energy \(E_n\) and an exact expression for the perturbed wavefunction \(|\psi_n\rangle\) in terms of the background part of \(G_u\) and the perturbation \(K_1\).

## Key numbers

* The perturbed energy is given by the exact implicit equation \(E_n = {\cal E}_n + \langle\phi_n| K_1(E_n)[1-G_u^b(E_n) K_1(E_n)]^{-1}|\phi_n\rangle\), where \({\cal E}_n\) is the unperturbed energy and \(G_u^b\) is the background part of the unperturbed Green function.
* The perturbed wavefunction is given by \(|\psi_n\rangle = [1-G_u^b(E_n) K_1(E_n)]^{-1}|\phi_n\rangle\), which in standard quantum mechanics implies the normalization \(\langle\phi_n|\psi_n\rangle = 1\).
* For the standard quantum mechanics case with energy-independent \(K_0\), the first few terms of the perturbation series for the energy are \(E_n = {\cal E}_n + \langle\phi_n|K_1|\phi_n\rangle + \sum_{m\neq n} |\langle\phi_m|K_1|\phi_n\rangle|^2/(E_n - {\cal E}_m) + \dots\).
* For the standard quantum mechanics case, the first few terms of the wavefunction series are \(|\psi_n\rangle = |\phi_n\rangle + \sum_{m\neq n} \langle\phi_m|K_1|\phi_n\rangle/(E_n - {\cal E}_m) |\phi_m\rangle + \sum_{k\neq n}\sum_{m\neq n} \langle\phi_m|K_1|\phi_k\rangle\langle\phi_k|K_1|\phi_n\rangle/[(E_n - {\cal E}_m)(E_n - {\cal E}_k)] |\phi_m\rangle + \dots\).

## Context

This work extends the standard textbook derivations of time-independent perturbation theory found in works such as Griffiths (2018) and Sakurai (2020), which rely on energy-independent potentials and the completeness of eigenstates of the unperturbed Hamiltonian. The new derivation is simpler and shorter than the authors' own previous approach (Kvinikhidze, 2003) and, unlike standard methods, applies directly to systems with energy-dependent potentials and nonlinear inverse free Green functions, such as those arising in the Bethe-Salpeter equation (Salpeter, 1951; Gell-Mann, 1951) and quasipotential approaches (Logunov, 1963; Blankenbecler, 1965; Gross, 1969). The results enable perturbation theory for relativistic systems where the perturbed and unperturbed systems may even have different free Green functions.
