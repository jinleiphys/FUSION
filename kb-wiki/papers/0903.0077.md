# Thermodynamics of clusterized matter

> Thermodynamics of clusterized matter is studied in the framework of statistical models with non-interacting cluster degrees of freedom. At variance with the analytical Fisher model, exact Metropolis simulation results indicate that the transition from homogeneous to clusterized matter lies along the $ρ=ρ_0$ axis at all temperatures and the limiting point of the phase diagram is not a critical point even if the surface energy vanishes at this point. Sensitivity of the inferred phase diagram to the employed statistical framework in the case of finite systems is discussed by considering the grand-canonical and constant-pressure canonical ensembles. A Wigner-Seitz formalism in which the fragment charge is neutralized by an uniform electron distribution allows to build the phase diagram of neutron star matter.

## Key claim

At variance with the analytical Fisher model, exact Metropolis simulation results indicate that the transition from homogeneous to clusterized matter lies along the ρ=ρ0 axis at all temperatures and the limiting point of the phase diagram is not a critical point even if the surface energy vanishes at this point.

## Method

The authors solved the cluster model exactly via Metropolis Monte-Carlo simulation in the grand-canonical ensemble for a one-component infinite neutral clusterized system, using the same free energy functional as the analytical Fisher model but without the saddle-point approximation that breaks the independence of different cluster sizes. They extracted the phase diagram from the bimodal structure of probability distributions of total particle number, total energy, and largest cluster size, and calculated critical exponents from thermodynamic fluctuations and cluster distribution moments.

## Key numbers

- The limiting point of the phase diagram occurs at Tlim = 16 MeV and αlim = -2, where the fragment size distribution is an exact power law
- The critical density predicted by the analytical Fisher model is ρc ≈ 0.07488 fm⁻³, while the exact simulation places the limiting point at ρ = ρ0
- The phase diagram is independent of system size for any A0 > 100 particles
- Critical exponents extracted from the Metropolis simulation: α = 0.34, γ = 2.8, β = 0.25, δ = 5.2
- The analytical Fisher model gives α = 0.2, γ = 1.2, β = 0.33, δ = 5
- The liquid-gas universality class expects α = 0.1, γ = 1.2, β = 0.33, δ = 5
- The topological exponent τ0 = 2.2 was used to recover the fractal dimension of the liquid-gas universality class
- Model parameters: av = 16 MeV, as = 16 MeV, a0 = 1/16 MeV⁻¹, Tc = 16 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Fisher cluster model (Fisher 1967) by solving it exactly via Metropolis simulation rather than using the analytical saddle-point approximation. The results contradict the standard interpretation of nuclear multifragmentation as a critical phenomenon, which has been pursued experimentally by Campi (1988), Gilkes (1994), and others over two decades. The finding that the limiting point is not a critical point implies that either the cluster model is inappropriate for nuclear fragmentation or that multifragmentation cannot be interpreted as critical. The Wigner-Seitz formalism presented enables building the phase diagram of neutron star matter, relevant for the inner crust of neutron stars as studied by Ravenhall (1983), Lattimer (1985), and others.
