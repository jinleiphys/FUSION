# First Demonstration that Quark-Gluon Plasma has a Nonzero Resolution Length

> We report on our investigation in arXiv:2509.08881 of how recent jet substructure measurements constrain the resolution length $L_{\rm res}$ of the quark-gluon plasma formed in heavy-ion collisions. $L_{\rm res}$ is defined such that high-energy partons within a jet shower are resolved by the medium if and only if they are separated by a distance greater than $L_{\rm res}$. Using the Hybrid Model, we reproduce ALICE data on the scaled Soft Drop angle $θ_g$ for $R=0.2$ charged-particle jets and ATLAS data on the Hard Group angle $ΔR_{12}$ for $R=1$ jets reclustered from skinny $R = 0.2$ inclusive subjets. We find that the narrowing of the $θ_g$-distribution in PbPb collisions observed by ALICE and the suppression of $R=1$ jets with multiple skinny subjets in PbPb collisions observed by ATLAS rule out $L_{\rm res} = \infty$, where each entire parton shower loses energy to the plasma coherently as if it were a single colored object. We then compare Hybrid Model calculations to ATLAS measurements of $R_{\rm AA}$ of $R=1$ jets reclustered from $R=0.2$ subjets, as a function of the Soft Drop angle $dR_{12}$ obtained by grooming all charged-particle tracks associated with each $R=1$ jet. We demonstrate, for the first time, that the ATLAS data is inconsistent with $L_{\rm res} = 0$, where the plasma resolves every splitting in a parton shower. Our results agree best with the data when QGP possesses a finite, nonzero $L_{\rm res}\sim (1-2)/(πT)$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates for the first time that experimental data from ATLAS on jet suppression as a function of the Soft Drop angle \(dR_{12}\) disfavor both the fully coherent (\(L_{\rm res} = \infty\)) and fully incoherent (\(L_{\rm res} = 0\)) limits of energy loss, and are best described by a finite, nonzero QGP resolution length \(L_{\rm res} \sim (1-2)/(\pi T)\), with \(L_{\rm res}=2/(\pi T)\) providing a somewhat better description than \(1/(\pi T)\).

## Method

The authors used the Hybrid Model, which combines perturbative DGLAP parton showers (implemented via PYTHIA8) with a holographic energy-loss formula for each parton traversing the QGP, including a parameter \(L_{\rm res}\) that controls whether two partons separated by less than that distance lose energy coherently as a single object. They compared Hybrid Model calculations for four values of \(L_{\rm res}\) (0, \(1/(\pi T)\), \(2/(\pi T)\), and \(\infty\)) to three sets of experimental data: ALICE measurements of the scaled Soft Drop angle \(\theta_g\) for \(R=0.2\) charged-particle jets, ATLAS Hard Group measurements of \(\Delta R_{12}\) for \(R=1\) jets reclustered from \(R=0.2\) subjets, and ATLAS measurements of \(R_{\rm AA}\) for \(R=1\) jets as a function of \(dR_{12}\) obtained by applying Soft Drop grooming to charged-particle tracks associated with each jet.

## Key numbers

- \(L_{\rm res} = \infty\) is ruled out by ALICE data on the ratio of PbPb to pp differential cross sections as a function of \(\theta_g\) for \(R=0.2\) charged-particle jets with \(60 < p_T^{\rm ch-jet} < 80\) GeV and \(|\eta^{\rm ch-jet}| < 0.7\), using Soft Drop parameters \(z_{\rm cut}=0.2\) and \(\beta=0\).
- \(L_{\rm res} = \infty\) is ruled out by ATLAS data on \(R_{\rm AA}\) as a function of \(\Delta R_{12}\) for \(R=1.0\) jets with \(200 < p_T < 251\) GeV, reconstructed from \(R=0.2\) skinny subjets with \(p_T > 35\) GeV.
- \(L_{\rm res} = 0\) is disfavored by ATLAS data on \(R_{\rm AA}\) as a function of \(dR_{12}\) for \(R=1.0\) jets with \(200 < p_T < 251\) GeV and \(|\eta| < 1.3\), using Soft Drop parameters \(z_{\rm cut}=0.15\) and \(\beta=0\).
- The best agreement with ATLAS \(dR_{12}\) data is obtained for finite nonzero \(L_{\rm res} \sim (1-2)/(\pi T)\), where \(T\) is the plasma temperature.
- Hybrid Model calculations with \(L_{\rm res}=0\) including elastic \(2 \rightarrow 2\) scatterings still overpredict the suppression of jets with narrow substructure in the region \(dR_{12}\sim 0.05{-}0.2\).

## Context

This work extends the Hybrid Model framework developed by Hulcher (2017), Casalderrey-Solana (2019), and Kudinoor (2025) to constrain the QGP resolution length using multiple jet substructure observables. It builds upon ALICE Soft Drop measurements (ALICE Collaboration, 2021) and ATLAS Hard Group measurements (ATLAS Collaboration, 2023), and introduces the new ATLAS \(dR_{12}\) observable (ATLAS Collaboration, 2025) that bridges the angular gap between these two measurements. The results enable future work to incorporate elastic scatterings into the Hybrid Model with finite nonzero \(L_{\rm res}\), which will be crucial for further constraining the resolution length with higher-statistics data.
