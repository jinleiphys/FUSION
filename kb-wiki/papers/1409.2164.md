# Chemical properties of super-hadronic matter created in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Preliminary charge balance functions from the STAR Collaboration at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) are compared to a model where quarks are produced in two waves. If a chemically equilibrated quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is created the strength and diffusive spread of the first wave should be governed by the chemical composition of the QGP, while the second wave should be determined by the increased number of quarks required to make the observed final-state hadrons. A simple model parameterizes the chemistry of the super-hadronic matter and the two correlation lengths for the two waves. Calculations are compared to preliminary data from the STAR Collaboration. The chemistry of the super-hadronic matter appears to be within 20\% of expectations from lattice gauge theory.

# Chemical properties of super-hadronic matter created in relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The chemistry of super-hadronic matter created in relativistic heavy ion collisions appears to be within 20% of expectations from lattice gauge theory for a chemically equilibrated quark-gluon plasma, based on comparison of preliminary STAR charge balance functions to a two-wave model of quark production. The analysis is limited to the 20% level due to the schematic nature of the model.

## Method
The authors constructed a simple model parameterizing the chemistry of super-hadronic matter and two correlation lengths for two waves of charge production: an initial wave from QGP formation and a second wave from hadronization. They compared calculations of charge balance functions for pion pairs, kaon pairs, and proton-antiproton pairs to preliminary STAR data using a Markov Chain Monte Carlo trace with a model emulator, incorporating thermal smearing via a blast wave model and accounting for STAR acceptance and efficiency.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of strange quarks to up or down quarks in the QGP, $\chi_{ss}/\chi_{uu}$, was found to be approximately 20% below lattice gauge theory expectations (lattice range for $T=250$ to 350 MeV).
- The sum $(\chi_{uu}+\chi_{dd}+\chi_{ss})/s$ (quarks to entropy in QGP) was found to be consistent with lattice calculations, with the lattice expectation near 0.18.
- The spread of balancing charges created in initial thermalization of QGP, $\sigma_{\rm(qgp)}$, was approximately 1 unit of spatial rapidity.
- The spread of balancing charges created during or after hadronization, $\sigma_{\rm(had)}$, was approximately one-fourth of $\sigma_{\rm(qgp)}$.
- Hadron multiplicities were generated assuming chemical equilibrium at a temperature of 165 MeV, with baryon yields reduced by 30% to account for annihilations.
- The collective velocity used in the blast wave model was $u_\perp=0.732c$, with a dissolution temperature of 102 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the charge balance function formalism developed by Pratt (2012) and applies it to preliminary STAR data to extract the chemical properties of the QGP. The results are compared to lattice gauge theory calculations of charge fluctuations by Borsanyi (2011) and Ratti (2011), which show that the ratio of quarks to entropy stays roughly constant above the transition temperature. The two-wave nature of quark production was predicted by Bass (2000) and observed by STAR (Adams 2003). This study motivates improved microscopic modeling and additional data from RHIC and the LHC, particularly from STAR, ALICE, CMS, and ATLAS.
