# Ab initio calculation of the spectrum and structure of $^{16}$O

> We present ab initio lattice calculations of the low-energy even-parity states of $^{16}$O using chiral nuclear effective field theory. We find good agreement with the empirical energy spectrum, and with the electromagnetic properties and transition rates. For the ground state, we find that the nucleons are arranged in a tetrahedral configuration of alpha clusters. For the first excited spin-0 state, we find that the predominant structure is a square configuration of alpha clusters, with rotational excitations that include the first spin-2 state.

## Key claim

Using Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) with chiral nuclear interactions up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), the authors provide the first *ab initio* evidence that the ground state of \(^{16}\)O has a tetrahedral alpha-cluster structure and the first excited \(0^+\) state has a square alpha-cluster structure, with the caveat that the final results include an effective four-nucleon interaction tuned to the binding energy of \(^{24}\)Mg.

## Method

The authors performed lattice Monte Carlo simulations of \(^{16}\)O on a periodic cubic lattice with spatial spacing \(a = 1.97\) fm and length \(L = 11.82\) fm, using Euclidean time projection to extract low-energy states. They employed chiral nuclear effective field theory interactions up to NNLO, including two-nucleon forces, three-nucleon forces, and electromagnetic contributions, with higher-order corrections treated in perturbation theory. The structure was probed by measuring expectation values of four-nucleon density operators for tetrahedral and square configurations, starting from initial trial states with explicit alpha-cluster geometries.

## Key numbers

- Ground state \(0^+_1\) energy at LO: \(-147.3(5)\) MeV; final result with effective 4N interaction: \(-131.3(5)\) MeV (experimental: \(-127.62\) MeV)
- Excited \(0^+_2\) state energy at LO: \(-145(2)\) MeV; final result: \(-123(2)\) MeV (experimental: \(-121.57\) MeV)
- \(2^+_1\) state energy at LO: \(-145(2)\) MeV; final result: \(-123(2)\) MeV (experimental: \(-120.70\) MeV)
- Ground state charge radius at LO: \(2.3(1)\) fm (experimental: \(2.710(15)\) fm)
- Rescaled \(B(E2, 2^+_1 \to 0^+_2)\): \(46(8)\) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\) (experimental: \(65(7)\) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\))
- Rescaled \(B(E2, 2^+_1 \to 0^+_1)\): \(6.2(1.6)\) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\) (experimental: \(7.4(2)\) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\))
- Rescaled \(M(E0, 0^+_2 \to 0^+_1)\): \(3.0(1.4)\) \(e\) fm\(^2\) (experimental: \(3.6(2)\) \(e\) fm\(^2\))

## Context

This work extends previous NLEFT calculations of \(^{12}\)C (Epelbaum 2011, 2012) and nuclei up to \(A=28\) (Lahde 2013) by providing the first *ab initio* structural analysis of \(^{16}\)O. It confirms the alpha-cluster picture proposed by Wheeler (1937) and developed in phenomenological models by Dennison (1954), Iachello, Robson (1979), and others, but now from first-principles lattice QCD-based methods. The results enable future studies of the odd-parity spectrum of \(^{16}\)O, inclusion of N3LO corrections, and calculations with reduced lattice spacing to eliminate the need for the effective four-nucleon interaction.
