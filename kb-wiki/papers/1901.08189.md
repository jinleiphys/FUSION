# Strangeness Enhancement in p+p, p+Pb and Pb+Pb Collisions at LHC Energies

> We investigate whether the quark gluon plasma (QGP) is created in small colliding systems from analyses of various hadron yields and their ratios in proton-proton (p+p), proton-lead (p+Pb) and lead-lead (Pb+Pb) collisions at LHC energies. Recently, the ALICE Collaboration reports enhancement of yield ratio of multi-strange hadrons to charged pions as a function of multiplicity at mid-rapidity. Motivated by these results, we develop the dynamical core-corona initialization framework and find that our results describe tendencies of the ALICE data especially for multi-strange hadrons. These results indicate that the QGP is partly formed in high multiplicity events in small colliding systems.

## Key claim
The authors claim that their dynamical core-corona initialization framework reproduces the ALICE data trends for multi-strange hadron enhancement, indicating that the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is "partly formed" in high-multiplicity events in small colliding systems, rather than being fully formed as in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method
The authors developed a dynamical core-corona initialization framework that combines a Monte Carlo event generator (PYTHIA 8.230) with (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics. Initially produced partons from PYTHIA are converted into QGP fluids at a rate that depends on the local parton density: partons in dense regions become fluids (core), while those in dilute regions remain as partons and undergo string fragmentation (corona). The fluidization rate is parametrized as \(dp_i^\mu/dt = -a_0 (\rho_i / p_{T,i}^2) p_i^\mu\), where \(\rho_i\) is the surrounding parton density and \(p_{T,i}\) is the transverse momentum. Final hadron yields are obtained by summing contributions from the core (via Cooper-Frye freeze-out with resonance decay corrections) and the corona (via PYTHIA string fragmentation).

## Key numbers
- Collision systems and energies: p+p at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 7\) TeV, p+Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 5.02\) TeV, Pb+Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 2.76\) TeV, and Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 200\) GeV.
- Fluid initialization starts at \(\tau_{00} = 0.1\) fm (parton formation time) and continues until \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm (initial fluid time).
- Yield ratios of cascades (\(\Xi^- + \bar{\Xi}^+\)), lambdas (\(\Lambda + \bar{\Lambda}\)), and phi mesons (\(\phi\)) to charged pions increase up to \(\langle dN_{\rm{ch}}/d\eta \rangle \sim 100\) and then saturate in high-multiplicity events.
- For protons (\(p + \bar{p}\)), a deviation between the model and experimental data appears above \(\langle dN_{\rm{ch}}/d\eta \rangle \sim 50\).

## Context
This work extends the core-corona picture, previously applied by Werner, Aichelin, Becattini, Pierog, and Bozek, to a dynamical initialization model originally proposed by Okai (2015). It directly addresses the ALICE Collaboration's surprising observation of multi-strange hadron enhancement in small systems (p+p and p+Pb), which challenges the conventional assumption that QGP forms only in heavy-ion collisions. The results enable future studies of hadronic rescattering effects, particularly for proton and antiproton yields, and support the interpretation that QGP is partially generated in high-multiplicity small colliding systems.
