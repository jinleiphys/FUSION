# Nuclear Dependence in Direct Photon Production

> We calculate the nuclear dependence of direct photon production in hadron-nucleus collisions. In terms of a multiple scattering picture, we factorize the cross section for direct photon production into calculable short-distance partonic parts times multiparton correlation functions in nuclei. We present the hadron-nucleus cross section as $A^α$ times the hadron-nucleon cross section. Using information on the multiparton correlation functions extracted from photon-nucleus experiments, we compute the value of $α$ as a function of transverse momentum of the direct photon. We also compare our results with recent data from Fermilab experiment E706.

## Key claim

The nuclear dependence of direct photon production in hadron-nucleus collisions can be described by a higher-twist formalism in perturbative QCD, where double scattering contributions factorize into short-distance partonic parts convoluted with multiparton correlation functions in nuclei; the authors compute the nuclear dependence parameter α as a function of transverse momentum and find consistency with Fermilab E706 data, but note that the separation between single and double scattering is not unique and that double scattering contributions could be negative in certain phase space regions.

## Method

The authors apply the generalized factorization theorem of Luo, Qiu and Sterman (1995) to direct photon production in hadron-nucleus collisions, expressing the double scattering cross section as a convolution of short-distance partonic hard parts with twist-four multiparton correlation functions (T_q and T_g) in nuclei. They use the leading pole approximation to integrate over two of three independent momentum fractions by contour integration, then evaluate the partonic hard parts for the three types of subprocesses (annihilation, Compton with gluon from target, and Compton with quark from target). The nuclear dependence parameter α is defined via the ratio of the hadron-nucleus cross section to the hadron-nucleon cross section, and the multiparton correlation functions are extracted from photon-nucleus experiments.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear dependence parameter α is defined such that the hadron-nucleus cross section equals A^α times the hadron-nucleon cross section, with α between 1 and 4/3 depending on the relative sizes of single and double scattering contributions.
- The double scattering contribution is proportional to A^{4/3}, signaling a dependence on nuclear size.
- The partonic hard parts for the three subprocesses are: H_{q\bar{q}} = (2/27)(-u/(x's+u) + (x's+u)/(-u)); H_g = (1/36)(x's/(x's+u) + (x's+u)/(x's)); H_q = (1/16)(x's/(-u) + (-u)/(x's)).
- The single scattering cross section uses matrix elements |M_{q\bar{q}→γg}|^2 = e_q^2 (4/9) * 2(û/^t + ^t/û) and |M_{qg→γq}|^2 = e_q^2 (1/6) * 2(-^t/^s + ^s/(-^t)).

## Context

This work extends the higher-twist formalism developed by Luo, Qiu and Sterman (1995) for describing the Cronin effect in hadron-nucleus collisions, applying it specifically to direct photon production where the photon's lack of strong interaction eliminates final-state multiple scattering and provides a clean probe of initial-state multiple scattering. The results are compared with data from Fermilab experiment E706 (Marek, 1995), and the multiparton correlation functions used are the same as those extracted from two-jet photoproduction on nuclear targets by Luo, Qiu and Sterman (1995). This enables independent tests of initial-state versus final-state multiple scattering by comparing direct photon production with jet or single particle production in photon-nucleus and hadron-nucleus collisions.
