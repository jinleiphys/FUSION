# An analytic approach to the RTA Boltzmann attractor

> We reformulate the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation undergoing Bjorken flow in terms of a novel partial differential equation for the generating function of the moments of the distribution function. This is used to obtain an approximate analytic description of this system's far-from-equilibrium attractor via a series expansion at early times. This expansion possesses a finite radius of convergence and can be analytically continued to late times. We find that this procedure reproduces the known values of shear viscosity and other transport coefficients to high accuracy. We also provide a simple approximate analytic expression that describes the attractor in the entire domain of interest for studies of quark-gluon plasma dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first solid analytic evidence for the far-from-equilibrium attractor of the RTA Boltzmann equation under Bjorken flow, showing that the entire distribution function (not just the pressure anisotropy) follows this attractor via a series expansion with a finite radius of convergence that can be analytically continued to late times.

## Method

The authors reformulate the RTA Boltzmann equation for Bjorken flow into a novel partial differential equation for the generating function of the Blaizot-Yan moments of the distribution function. They solve this PDE perturbatively at early times (small w = τ/τ_R) by expanding the moments in power series in w, obtaining recursion relations that determine all coefficients. At late times, they use the same generating function approach to compute the gradient expansion (asymptotic series in 1/w) to very high order, enabling Borel-Padé analysis of the singularity structure.

## Key numbers

- The initial value of the first dimensionless moment at w=0 is m_0^(1) = -1/2, corresponding to the attractor solution.
- The exact generating function at w=0 is h_0(x) = 1/√(1+x), giving m_0^(n) = (-1)^n (2n-1)! / (2^(2n-1) n! (n-1)!) for n≥1.
- The early-time expansion coefficients of the pressure anisotropy grow as m_k^(1) ~ exp(-a k) with a ≈ 1.55, giving a finite radius of convergence.
- The analytically continued Padé approximant of order (70,71) reproduces the asymptotic hydrodynamic behavior at w=100 as P𝒜_(70,71) ~ 1.60004/w + 0.27348/w^2, matching the known shear viscosity η/s = γ/5 (leading coefficient 8/5 = 1.6) to three decimal places.
- A simple approximate analytic formula for the attractor valid for 0 ≤ w ≤ 5 is given by 𝒜 = (1500 - 12w + 30w^2) / (1000 + 434w + 95w^2 + 9w^3), with relative error about 2%.
- For the conformal case (Δ=1), the Borel-Padé analysis reveals a leading cut on the real axis at ζ_1 = 3/2, with additional branch points at complex-conjugate locations.

## Context

This work extends the study of far-from-equilibrium attractors in RTA kinetic theory initiated by Heller and Spaliński (2015) and the moment hierarchy approach of Blaizot and Yan (2017). It provides analytic confirmation of the numerical observation by Strickland (2018) that the entire distribution function follows an attractor. The generating function technique enables calculation of hundreds of gradient expansion coefficients in minutes, far exceeding previous capabilities, and reveals additional branch point structures beyond the cuts identified by Heller and Spaliński (2016, 2018). This approach may be extended to other kinetic systems such as λφ^4 theory and high-temperature QCD plasmas.
