# Two parity violating asymmetries from $n p \to d γ$ in pionless effective field theories

> We consider parity-violating observables from the processes $\vec n p\to d γ$ and $ np \to d \overset {\circlearrowleft}γ$. We perform calculations using pionless effective field theory both with and without explicit dibaryon fields. After combining these results with ones we have already obtained on parity-violating asymmetries in $\vec NN$ scattering, experimental input would in principle allow the extraction of all five parameters occurring at leading order in the parity-violating Lagrangian.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the parity-violating photon asymmetry \(A_\gamma\) in polarized neutron capture \(\vec n p \to d \gamma\) and the circular photon polarization \(P_\gamma\) in unpolarized capture \(np \to d \overset{\circlearrowleft}{\gamma}\) using pionless effective field theory, both with and without explicit dibaryon fields, and shows that combining these results with prior calculations of parity-violating asymmetries in \(\vec NN\) scattering would in principle allow extraction of all five leading-order parity-violating parameters, but only if experimental input becomes available.

## Method

The authors use pionless effective field theory (EFT(\(\not \! \pi\))) with nucleons, photons, and the deuteron as degrees of freedom, constructing the leading-order parity-conserving and parity-violating Lagrangians from contact interactions. They compute the parity-conserving amplitudes \(X\), \(Y\) and parity-violating amplitudes \(W\), \(V\) for the \(np \to d \gamma\) process at threshold, using both a non-dibaryon formalism (where effective ranges are treated as natural) and a dibaryon formalism (where effective ranges are resummed via dynamical dibaryon fields). The photon asymmetry \(A_\gamma\) arises from interference between the parity-conserving amplitude \(Y\) and the parity-violating amplitude \(W\), while the circular polarization \(P_\gamma\) arises from interference between \(Y\) and the parity-violating amplitude \(V\).

## Key numbers

* Photon asymmetry \(A_\gamma\) in the non-dibaryon formalism: \(A_\gamma = \frac{32}{3} \frac{M}{\kappa_1 (1 - \gamma a^{(^1S_0)})} \frac{\mathcal{C}^{(^3S_1-^3P_1)}}{\mathcal{C}_0^{(^3S_1)}}\), where \(\gamma = \sqrt{MB}\) is the deuteron momentum, \(M\) is the nucleon mass, \(\kappa_1\) is the isovector nucleon magnetic moment, and \(a^{(^1S_0)}\) is the \(^1S_0\) scattering length.
* Photon asymmetry \(A_\gamma\) in the dibaryon formalism: \(A_\gamma = 2 M^2 \sqrt{\frac{r^{(^3S_1)}}{\pi}} \frac{1 - \frac{\gamma a^{(^3S_1)}}{3}}{\kappa_1 (1 - \gamma a^{(^1S_0)})} g^{(^3S_1-^3P_1)}\), where \(r^{(^3S_1)}\) is the \(^3S_1\) effective range and \(a^{(^3S_1)}\) is the \(^3S_1\) scattering length.
* Circular polarization \(P_\gamma\) in the non-dibaryon formalism: \(P_\gamma = -16 \frac{M}{\kappa_1 (1 - \gamma a^{(^1S_0)})} \left[ \left(1 - \frac{2}{3} \gamma a^{(^1S_0)}\right) \frac{\mathcal{C}^{(^3S_1-^1P_1)}}{\mathcal{C}_0^{(^3S_1)}} + \frac{\gamma a^{(^1S_0)}}{3} \frac{\mathcal{C}^{(^1S_0-^3P_0)}_{(\Delta I=0)} - 2 \mathcal{C}^{(^1S_0-^3P_0)}_{(\Delta I=2)}}{\mathcal{C}_0^{(^1S_0)}} \right]\).
* Circular polarization \(P_\gamma\) in the dibaryon formalism: \(P_\gamma = -2 \sqrt{\frac{r^{(^3S_1)}}{\pi}} \frac{M^2}{\kappa_1 (1 - \gamma a^{(^1S_0)})} \left[ \left(1 - \frac{2}{3} \gamma a^{(^1S_0)}\right) g^{(^3S_1-^1P_1)} + \frac{\gamma a^{(^1S_0)}}{3} \sqrt{\frac{r^{(^1S_0)}}{r^{(^3S_1)}}} \left( g^{(^1S_0-^3P_0)}_{(\Delta I=0)} - 2 g^{(^1S_0-^3P_0)}_{(\Delta I=2)} \right) \right]\).
* The parity-conserving amplitude \(Y\) in the non-dibaryon formalism: \(Y = -\frac{2}{M} \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{\gamma^3}} \kappa_1 (1 - \gamma a^{(^1S_0)})\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of parity-violating asymmetries in \(\vec NN\) scattering (Phillips, 2008) to photon-involving processes, providing the two additional observables needed to constrain the five leading-order parity-violating parameters in pionless effective field theory. The results are presented in both the non-dibaryon and dibaryon formalisms to facilitate comparison with other calculations, such as the contemporary work on \(P_\gamma\) by Shin, Ando, and Hyun (2009). The calculations enable future extraction of the parity-violating low-energy constants from upcoming experiments at facilities like HIGS and JLab, and provide a foundation for extending the treatment to three-body systems such as \(\vec n d \to t \gamma\).
