# The nuclear symmetry energy and the neutron skin thickness in nuclei

> We investigate possible correlations between the stiffness of the symmetry energy at saturation density, the so-called $L$ parameter, and the neutron skin thickness of ${^{48}}$Ca and ${^{208}}$Pb, for which the recent measurements from the CREX and PREX I+II experiments at the Thomas Jefferson Laboratory became available. We choose an ensemble of nucleonic equations of state (EoS) derived within microscopic (BHF, Variational, AFDMC) and phenomenological (Skyrme, RMF, DD-RMF) approaches. They are all compatible with the laboratory nuclear collisions data and with current observations of neutron stars (NS) mass and the tidal polarizability of a 1.4 $M_{\odot}$ NS, as deduced from the GW170817 event. We find some degree of correlation between the $L$ parameter and the neutron skin thickness whereas a much weaker correlation does exist with the tidal polarizability and the symmetry energy at saturation density. However, some EoS which are able to explain the CREX experimental data, are not compatible with the PREX I+II data, and viceversa. We confirm the results previously obtained with a different set of EoS models, and find a possible tension between the experimental data and the current understanding of the nuclear EoS.

# The nuclear symmetry energy and the neutron skin thickness in nuclei

## Key claim

The paper finds that while some correlation exists between the slope of the symmetry energy (L parameter) and the neutron skin thickness of ⁴⁸Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb, no single equation of state (EoS) among the ensemble studied can simultaneously reproduce both the CREX and PREX I+II experimental data, indicating a possible tension between these measurements and current understanding of the nuclear EoS.

## Method

The authors assembled an ensemble of nucleonic equations of state derived from microscopic methods (Brueckner-Hartree-Fock, variational, Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo) and phenomenological approaches (Skyrme, relativistic mean-field, density-dependent relativistic mean-field), all filtered to be compatible with nuclear saturation properties, maximum neutron star mass observations, and the tidal polarizability constraint from GW170817. They calculated the neutron skin thickness of ⁴⁸Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb using a semi-infinite nuclear matter approach based on the formalism of Steiner (2005) and Centelles (2009), then examined correlations between the skin thickness, the L parameter, the symmetry energy at saturation S₀, and the tidal deformability Λ₁.₄.

## Key numbers

- PREX I+II measured neutron skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb: δR = 0.283 ± 0.071 fm
- CREX measured neutron skin thickness of ⁴⁸Ca: δR = 0.121 ± 0.035 fm
- Correlation coefficient between δR and L parameter: r = 0.75 for both ⁴⁸Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb
- Correlation coefficient between Λ₁.₄ and L: r = 0.55
- Experimental range for L parameter: 30 MeV < L < 87 MeV
- Experimental range for symmetry energy at saturation S₀: 28.5–34.9 MeV
- Tidal polarizability constraint from GW170817 for a 1.4 M⊙ NS: Λ₁.₄ = 190⁺³⁹⁰₋₁₂₀
- Maximum neutron star mass constraint: M > 2.14⁺⁰.¹⁰₋₀.₀₉ M⊙

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Burgio et al., Universe 2020) by incorporating the newer CREX and PREX-II experimental data and applying stricter observational constraints from neutron star masses and the GW170817 tidal deformability measurement. The correlation between neutron skin thickness and the L parameter was first shown by Brown and Typel (2001) and confirmed by later authors including Steiner (2005), Centelles (2009), and Horowitz (2001). The paper confirms that the same tension between CREX and PREX data found by Lattimer (2023) persists across this broader EoS ensemble, suggesting that future experiments at next-generation radioactive ion beam facilities or improved re-analysis of current data will be needed to resolve the discrepancy.
