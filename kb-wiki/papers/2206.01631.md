# Axion effects in the stability of hybrid stars

> We investigate the effects of including strong charge-parity (CP) violating effects through axion fields in the microscopic equation of state of massive hybrid neutron stars. We assume that their cores contain deconfined quark matter and include the effects of axions via an effective 't Hooft determinant interaction. The hadronic crusts are described using different approaches in order to make our results more general. We find that the presence of axions stabilizes massive hybrid neutron stars against gravitational collapse by weakening the deconfinement phase transition and bringing it to lower densities. This enables to reproduce hybrid neutron stars in agreement with modern astrophysical constraints.

## Key claim
The presence of a non-zero axion field condensate (with ratio \(a/f_a = \pi\)) stabilizes massive hybrid neutron stars against gravitational collapse by weakening the first-order deconfinement phase transition and shifting it to lower energy densities, an effect that cannot be achieved by repulsive vector interactions alone.

## Method
The authors use a three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model at zero temperature including an effective 't Hooft determinant interaction to incorporate axion effects, with the axion field taken at its vacuum expectation value. They solve the gap equations for scalar and pseudoscalar quark condensates to obtain the equation of state for quark matter, then combine this with two different hadronic crust models (NL3\(_{\omega\rho}\) and CMF\(_{\omega\rho,\omega^4}\)) to construct complete hybrid star equations of state. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are solved to obtain mass-radius relations, with electric charge neutrality and \(\beta\)-equilibrium imposed throughout.

## Key numbers
- Maximum hybrid star mass \(M > 2\) M\(_{\odot}\) is achieved for axion field ratio \(a/f_a = \pi\) with vector coupling \(G_V = 0.2\,G_s\) and \(G_V = 0.4\,G_s\) using the NL3\(_{\omega\rho}\) crust.
- The nuclear saturation density is taken as \(n_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The NJL model parameters used: cutoff \(\Lambda = 631.4\) MeV, scalar coupling \(G_s = 1.835 / \Lambda^2\), 't Hooft coupling \(K = 9.29 / \Lambda^5\), current quark masses \(m_0^{u,d} = 5.5\) MeV and \(m_0^{s} = 135.7\) MeV.
- The speed of sound shows a bump in the quark matter phase around \(n = 3.6\) to \(4.4\,n_0\) when the strange quark starts to populate.

## Context
This work extends studies of axion effects in dense matter (Bandyopadhyay 2019) and of vector interactions in hybrid stars (Zacchi 2015) by showing that axion condensates can stabilize hybrid configurations that otherwise would be unstable. It connects to observational constraints from LIGO/Virgo gravitational wave data (LIGO Scientific Collaboration 2018) and NICER X-ray observations (Miller 2019, 2021; Riley 2019, 2021), and complements studies of bosonic dark matter in neutron stars (Karkevandi 2021). The results enable future predictions for tidal deformability measurements that could distinguish between hadronic and hybrid stars with axion fields.
