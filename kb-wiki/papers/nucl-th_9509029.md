# Soft two-meson-exchange nucleon-nucleon potentials. I. Planar and crossed-box diagrams

> Pion-meson-exchange nucleon-nucleon potentials are derived for two nucleons in the intermediate states. The mesons we include are (i) pseudoscalar mesons: $π, η, η'$; (ii) vector mesons: $ρ, ω, ϕ$; (iii) scalar mesons: $a_{0}(980), \varepsilon(760), f_{0}(975)$; and (iv) the $J=0$ contribution from the Pomeron. Strong dynamical pair suppression is assumed, and at the nucleon-nucleon-meson vertices Gaussian form factors are incorporated into the relativistic two-body framework using a dispersion representation for the pion- and meson-exchange amplitudes. The Fourier transformations are performed using factorization techniques for the energy denominators. The potentials are first calculated in the adiabatic approximation to all planar and crossed three-dimensional momentum-space $π$-meson diagrams. Next, we calculate the $1/M$ corrections.

## Key claim

The paper derives two-meson-exchange nucleon-nucleon potentials for cases where at least one exchanged meson is a pion, using Gaussian form factors and assuming strong dynamical pair suppression; the leading-order adiabatic approximation yields zero potential for pion-scalar exchanges involving isoscalar scalar mesons ($\varepsilon$, $f_0$) and for pion-Pomeron exchange, making the next-to-leading-order $1/M$ corrections essential for those channels.

## Method

The authors start from relativistic two-body equations and derive fourth-order two-meson-exchange kernels from Feynman diagrams by integrating over relative energies, obtaining "old-fashioned" perturbation graphs. They evaluate planar-box and crossed-box diagrams (BW graphs) and iterated TMO graphs for two nucleons in intermediate states, using pseudovector coupling for pseudoscalar mesons and including Gaussian form factors at nucleon-nucleon-meson vertices. The Fourier transforms to coordinate space are performed using a factorization technique with $\lambda$-integral representations that express the potentials in terms of the basic coordinate-space function $\phi^0_C(r)$ from the Nijmegen OBE model.

## Key numbers

* Mesons included with masses below approximately 1 GeV: pseudoscalar $\pi$, $\eta$, $\eta'$; vector $\rho$, $\omega$, $\phi$; scalar $a_0(980)$, $\varepsilon(760)$, $f_0(975)$; plus $J=0$ Pomeron contribution.
* The leading-order $\pi\varepsilon$ and $\pi f_0$ potentials are identically zero; the leading-order $\pi$-Pomeron potential is also zero.
* For $\pi$-vector and $\pi$-pseudoscalar potentials, large cancellations occur between planar and crossed-box contributions when the meson is isoscalar ($I=0$).
* The pseudovector coupling constant $f_{PV}$ is of order $1/M$, satisfying $f_{PV}/m_\pi = g_{PS}/2M$.
* The $1/M$ corrections arise from three sources: (i) nonadiabatic corrections to energy denominators, (ii) the $\omega/M$ term in the pseudovector vertex, and (iii) cancellation between the $1/M^2$ off-shell term in pseudovector vertices and the $1/M$ term of the two-nucleon intermediate state in TMO diagrams.

## Context

This work extends the Nijmegen soft-core one-boson-exchange potential (Nagels et al., 1978) by including two-meson-exchange contributions, building on earlier two-pion-exchange calculations by Rijken (1991). The $\pi\omega$ potential had previously been derived in a dispersion-relation approach (Riska, 1976) which could not incorporate Gaussian form factors, while $\pi\omega$, $\pi\rho$, and $\pi\sigma$ exchanges had been evaluated only in momentum space using noncovariant perturbation theory (Holinde, 1981). The resulting potentials are incorporated into a new extended soft-core (ESC) nucleon-nucleon model, enabling improved theoretical description of low-energy nucleon-nucleon data and providing coordinate-space interactions useful for Jastrow variational calculations and studies distinguishing meson-exchange from quark-exchange regions.
