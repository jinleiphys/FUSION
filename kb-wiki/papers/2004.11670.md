# First-principles modelling of the magnetic structure of the lightest nuclear systems using effective field theory without pions

> The strong interaction, i.e., quantum chromodynamics at the low energy nuclear regime, is notoriously known to be challenging for predictive modeling. Here, we use the simplest possible nuclear effective field theory (EFT), and show that in the case of the magnetic structure of nuclear systems with $A=2$ and $A=3$ nucleons, it is highly precise as well as predictive. The theoretical framework is the pionless EFT (\pilesseft), of point nucleons with contact interactions, expanded consistently up to next-to-leading order (NLO) in perturbation theory, i.e., including only eleven low-energy parameters, and augmented by a novel Bayesian analysis of theoretical uncertainties. The theory accurately predicts the shell structure reflected in the values of the magnetic moments and reactions of these nuclei within $\approx 1\%$ calculated theoretical uncertainty. We show that this perfect prediction originates in implicit a-posteriori properties of the calculation, particularly an unexpectedly small expansion parameter, as well as a vanishing contribution from the two-body isoscalar current.

## Key claim

The paper claims that pionless effective field theory (pi/EFT), expanded consistently up to next-to-leading order (NLO) with only eleven low-energy parameters, can predict the magnetic moments and M1 reactions of A=2 and A=3 nuclear systems within approximately 1% theoretical uncertainty, but this high precision originates from an unexpectedly small expansion parameter and a vanishing two-body isoscalar current contribution, which are implicit a-posteriori properties rather than guaranteed features of the theory.

## Method

The authors used pionless EFT with point nucleons and contact interactions, employing a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce dynamical dibaryon fields for the spin-triplet and spin-singlet channels. They calculated magnetic moments of the deuteron, triton, and helium-3, as well as the thermal neutron-proton radiative capture cross-section, by evaluating one-body and two-body electromagnetic current matrix elements up to NLO in perturbation theory, including Coulomb interactions for helium-3. A Bayesian analysis of theoretical uncertainties was performed using a log-normal prior for the expansion parameter derived from information theory, combined with Student's t-distribution to estimate truncation errors.

## Context

This work extends previous pionless EFT calculations of A=2 magnetic observables by Chen (1999), Rupak (1999), and Ando (2006) to consistently include A=3 systems with full renormalization group invariance and Coulomb interactions, which earlier studies by Kirscher (2017) and Vanasse (2017) could not achieve due to approximations. The results enable systematic uncertainty quantification for weak interaction processes in A=3 nuclei relevant to big-bang nucleosynthesis and stellar evolution, and suggest that the unexpectedly small expansion parameter may reflect properties of the renormalization group flow from QCD through EFTs with larger breakdown scales applicable to heavier nuclei.
