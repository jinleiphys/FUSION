# Ab initio Rayleigh-Schrödinger perturbation calculation including three-body force

> We first derive the Rayleigh-Schrödinger many-body perturbation theory up to third order (RSPT3) for Hamiltonians with three-body interaction. The structure of closed-shell nuclei in a wide mass range from 4He to 48Ca has been investigated by the RSPT3 with explicit NN+3N Hamiltonian. The RSPT3 calculations are performed within Hartree-Fock bases. The perturbative contribution of antisymmetrized Goldstone diagrams (diagrammatic expansion for RSPT) with normal-ordered interaction has been analyzed. We demonstrate that the normal-ordered two-body level (NO2B) approximation which neglects the residual three-body term can catch the main effect of full three-body force in RSPT calculation. We also present rigorous benchmarks for RSPT3 with non-perturbative coupled cluster and in-medium similarity renormalization group using the same chiral NNLOsat NO2B interaction. The three methods are in good agreement with each other for binding energies and radii. However, the RSPT3 provides an alternative that is computationally inexpensive but comparable in accuracy to state-of-the-art non-perturbative many-body methods.

## Key claim

The authors derive Rayleigh-Schrödinger many-body perturbation theory up to third order (RSPT3) for Hamiltonians that include three-body forces, and demonstrate that the normal-ordered two-body level (NO2B) approximation, which neglects the residual three-body term, captures the main effect of the full three-body force in RSPT calculations for closed-shell nuclei from 4He to 48Ca.

## Method

The authors formulated RSPT3 for Hamiltonians containing explicit two-body (NN) and three-body (3N) interactions, working within Hartree-Fock (HF) bases constructed from harmonic oscillator single-particle states. They transformed the perturbation correction equations into an angular-momentum-coupled representation to reduce computational cost, and analyzed the perturbative contributions of antisymmetrized Goldstone diagrams using normal-ordered interactions. The method was benchmarked against non-perturbative coupled cluster (CC) and in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) calculations using the same chiral NNLOsat NO2B interaction.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous two-body-level RSPT development (Hu et al., 2016) to include explicit three-body forces, and tests the NO2B approximation previously validated in CC and IT-NCSM calculations (Roth et al., 2007; Binder et al., 2013). The HF-RSPT3 results agree at the 2% level with non-perturbative IM-SRG and CC calculations using the same NNLOsat interaction, but at a fraction of the computational cost. This enables future application of full three-body forces in medium-mass and heavy nuclei where non-perturbative methods become computationally prohibitive.
