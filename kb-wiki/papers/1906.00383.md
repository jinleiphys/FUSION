# Magnetic field in nuclear collisions at ultra high energies

> The magnetic field created in proton-proton and nucleus-nucleus collisions at ultra high energies are studied with models of point-like charges and hard sphere for distribution of the constituents for vacuum conditions. The various beam ions are considered from light to heavy nuclei at energies corresponded to the nominal energies of proton beam within the projects of further accelerator facilities high-energy Large Hadron Collider (HE-LHC) and Future Circular Collider (FCC). The magnetic field strength immediately after collisions reaches the value tens of GeV$^{2}$ while the approach with point-like charges some overestimate the amplitude of the field in comparison with more realistic hard sphere model. The absolute value of magnetic field rapidly decrease with time and increases with growth of atomic number. The amplitude for $eB$ is estimated at level $100$ GeV$^{2}$ in order to magnitude for quark-quark collisions at energies corresponded to the nominal energies of proton beams. These estimations are close to the range for onset of $W$ boson condensation.

## Key claim
The magnetic field strength in proton-proton and nucleus-nucleus collisions at ultra-high energies (HE-LHC and FCC) is estimated using point-like charge and hard-sphere models under vacuum conditions, with the point-like approach overestimating the field amplitude compared to the more realistic hard-sphere model; the field reaches tens of GeV² immediately after collision, but these estimates do not account for modifications due to matter produced in the final state.

## Method
The magnetic field at the collision center was calculated using the Biot-Savart law for point-like charges (Eq. 2.0) and the Liénard-Wiechert potentials for the hard-sphere model (Eqs. 2.2a, 2.2b), with contributions from spectator and participant nucleons. The hard-sphere model assumed a uniform constituent density within a spherical nucleus of radius R = r₀A¹/³, and the analytic approximation (Eq. 2.3) was used for the proper time range τ ∈ [R/sinh(y₀), R]. Symmetric collisions of various nuclei (p, O, Ar, Ca, Kr, Xe, Pb) were considered at nominal proton-proton center-of-mass energies of √s_pp = 27 TeV (HE-LHC) and 100 TeV (FCC).

## Key numbers
- For point-like charges at √s_pp = 27 TeV: eB₀ ~ 20 GeV² for p+p, and ~ (13–19) GeV² for other nuclei (semi-central collisions, x=1).
- For point-like charges at √s_pp = 100 TeV: eB₀ ~ 78 GeV² for p+p, and ~ (49–71) GeV² for other nuclei (semi-central collisions, x=1).
- Characteristic time t₀ for point-like charges: ~ 0.4×10⁻⁴ fm/c for p+p and (2–7)×10⁻⁴ fm/c for other nuclei at √s_pp = 27 TeV; ~ 0.1×10⁻⁴ fm/c for p+p and (0.6–1.8)×10⁻⁴ fm/c for other nuclei at √s_pp = 100 TeV.
- For hard-sphere model: eB ~ 10 GeV² at √s_pp = 27 TeV and eB ~ 30 GeV² at √s_pp = 100 TeV (order of magnitude, depending on beam type and centrality).
- For quark-quark collisions (d-quark, Z_q=1/3) with point-like charges: eB₀ ~ 0.6×10² GeV² (60 GeV²) at √s_pp = 27 TeV and ~ 2.3×10² GeV² (230 GeV²) at √s_pp = 100 TeV; t₀ ~ 1.4×10⁻⁵ fm/c (27 TeV) and ~ 0.4×10⁻⁵ fm/c (100 TeV).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of magnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions at √s_NN < 7 TeV (reviewed in AHEP-2013-490495-2013 and RPP-79-076302-2016) to the ultra-high energies of the proposed HE-LHC and FCC-hh facilities. The hard-sphere model results agree reasonably with UrQMD (IJMPA-24-5925-2009), HIJING (PRD-85-044907-2012), and HSD (PRC-83-054911-2011) calculations at lower energies. The estimated field strengths approach the range for W boson condensation (PLB-257-201-1991) and may enable studies of the chiral magnetic effect in p+p collisions, polarization phenomena, and non-linear QED effects.
