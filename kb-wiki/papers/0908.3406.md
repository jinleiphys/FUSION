# Mesons and nucleons from holographic QCD in a unified approach

> We investigate masses and coupling constants of mesons and nucleons within a hard wall model of holographic QCD in a unified approach. We first examine an appropriate form of fermionic solutions by restricting the mass coupling for the five dimensional bulk fermions and bosons. We then derive approximated analytic solutions for the nucleons and the corresponding masses in a small mass coupling region. In order to treat meson and nucleon properties on the same footing, we introduce the same infrared (IR) cut in such a way that the meson-nucleon coupling constants, i.e., g_{pi NN} and g_{rho NN} are uniquely determined. The first order approximation with respect to a dimensionless expansion parameter, which is valid in the small mass coupling region, explicitly shows difficulties to avoid the IR scale problem of the hard wall model. We discuss possible ways of circumventing these problems.

## Key claim

The paper shows that within a hard-wall holographic QCD model, it is not possible to simultaneously reproduce the spectra of the rho meson and the nucleon when using the same infrared cutoff, and that the meson-nucleon coupling constants (g_piNN and g_rhoNN) are systematically underestimated by about 50% even after global fitting, revealing an inherent limitation of the hard-wall approach.

## Method

The authors construct a 5D AdS/QCD hard-wall model with bulk gauge fields for mesons and bulk Dirac spinors for nucleons, introducing a Yukawa coupling g between the scalar field X and the nucleon fields. They decouple the equations of motion for the nucleon wavefunctions into a Sturm-Liouville form, derive approximate analytic solutions in the small mass coupling region using an expansion in dimensionless parameters, and compute the mass spectra and coupling constants by imposing the same infrared cutoff z_m for both mesonic and baryonic sectors.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon ground state mass reproduced at 940 MeV with z_m = (285 MeV)^{-1}, sigma = (227 MeV)^3, and g = -9.6
- Roper resonance N(1440) mass calculated as 1826 MeV (experimental: 1440 MeV), about 27% overestimate
- N(1535) negative parity state mass calculated as 1856 MeV (experimental: 1535 MeV), about 21% overestimate
- Rho meson mass calculated as 685 MeV (experimental: 776 MeV), about 12% underestimate
- g_piNN coupling constant calculated as 6.12 (experimental: 13.1), about 53% underestimate
- g_rhoNN coupling constant calculated as 1.34 (experimental: 2.4), about 44% underestimate
- With m_5 = 0 (vanishing 5D nucleon mass) and parameters from Erlich (2005), nucleon ground state mass = 1009 MeV, rho meson mass = 776 MeV

## Context

This work extends the hard-wall AdS/QCD model of Erlich (2005) for mesons and Hong (2006) for nucleons by treating both sectors with a common infrared cutoff, addressing the inconsistency noted by Maru (2009) where different IR cutoffs were used for mesons and baryons. The results show that the hard-wall model faces an "IR scale problem" where the confinement scale cannot simultaneously fit meson and baryon spectra, motivating future work with alternative approaches such as soft-wall models or introducing anomalous dimensions for the 5D fermion mass.
