# The Weak $ΛΛK^0$ Interaction

> We calculate the leading contribution to the weak $ΛΛK$ coupling in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, including the SU(3) breaking terms arising at one-loop. This coupling gives the leading one-boson exchange amplitude for the process $ΛΛ\toΛn$ and is an important component in understanding the decay of $ΛΛ$-hypernuclei. We find the S-wave and P-wave amplitudes to be stable under one-loop corrections, but the associated errors become large. We discuss the theoretical errors and potential effects of higher order terms.

# The Weak ΛΛK⁰ Interaction

## Key claim

The leading one-loop SU(3) breaking corrections to the weak ΛΛK⁰ coupling in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory yield S-wave and P-wave amplitudes that are stable under loop corrections, but the associated theoretical uncertainties become so large (nearly 100% for P-waves and more than 200% for S-waves) that the ΛΛK⁰ coupling may be one of the quantities not well predicted by a loop-level calculation in chiral perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors compute the weak Δs=1 ΛΛK⁰ vertex using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, including octet and decuplet baryons and the pseudo-Goldstone meson octet. They evaluate tree-level and one-loop diagrams (including SU(3) breaking terms of the form m_s log m_s) for both S-wave and P-wave amplitudes, with parameters fitted to semileptonic baryon decays, strong decuplet decays, and S-wave nonleptonic hyperon decays. The calculation is performed both with zero decuplet-octet mass splitting and with Δm = 200 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Tree-level S-wave amplitude: A^(S)_0(ΛΛK⁰) = 0.55 ± 0.19 (dimensionless)
- Tree-level P-wave amplitude: A^(P)_0(ΛΛK⁰)/Λ_χ = -5.2 ± 1.0 (dimensionless)
- One-loop S-wave amplitude with Δm=0: A^(S)_0 + A^(S)_1 = 0.43 ± 0.89 (dimensionless)
- One-loop P-wave amplitude with Δm=0: A^(P)_0 + A^(P)_1 = -4.9 ± 1.9 (dimensionless)
- One-loop S-wave amplitude with Δm=200 MeV: A^(S)_0 + A^(S)_1 = 0.28 ± 0.82 (dimensionless)
- One-loop P-wave amplitude with Δm=200 MeV: A^(P)_0 + A^(P)_1 = -4.8 ± 4.1 (dimensionless)
- Fitted axial couplings (loop-level, Δm=0): D = 0.54 ± 0.03, F = 0.33 ± 0.03, |C| = 1.11 ± 0.05, H = -1.75 ± 0.46
- Fitted weak couplings (loop-level, Δm=0): h_D = -0.42 ± 0.21, h_F = 0.94 ± 0.44, h_C = 0.30 ± 6.75

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of the weak KNN couplings (Savage and Springer, 1996) to the ΛΛK⁰ vertex, which is needed to understand the decay of double-Λ hypernuclei via the process ΛΛ → ΛN. The calculation uses the same heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory framework applied to nonleptonic hyperon decays by Bijnens, Sonoda, and Wise (1985) and by Jenkins (1992). The results are intended as input for nuclear structure codes (e.g., Parreno, Ramos, and Bennhold, 1997) that model hypernuclear decay. The large uncertainties found here contrast with the better-behaved KNN couplings and suggest that the ΛΛK⁰ vertex may be a case where chiral perturbation theory predictions are not reliable, unlike the situation for nonleptonic hyperon S-waves or the KNN couplings. Future experiments at BNL (AGS-E885, E906) and KEK (E373) on double-Λ hypernuclei could provide measurements of this coupling.
