# Calculation of fermion loops for $η^\prime$ and nucleon scalar and electromagnetic form factors

> The exact evaluation of the disconnected diagram contributions to the flavor-singlet pseudoscalar meson mass, the nucleon sigma term and the nucleon electromagnetic form factors, is carried out utilizing GPGPU technology with the NVIDIA CUDA platform. The disconnected loops are also computed using stochastic methods with several noise reduction techniques. Various dilution schemes as well as the truncated solver method are studied. We make a comparison of these stochastic techniques to the exact results and show that the number of noise vectors depends on the operator insertion in the fermionic loop.

## Key claim

The paper benchmarks stochastic techniques for evaluating disconnected fermion loops in lattice QCD against exact all-to-all propagator calculations, finding that the truncated solver method is the most efficient approach, but only for a specific small lattice volume (16³ × 32) and heavy pion mass (746 MeV) where exact evaluation is feasible.

## Method

The authors computed all-to-all propagators exactly on 165 gauge configurations generated by the SESAM Collaboration with N_f=2 Wilson fermions at β=5.6 and κ=0.157 on a 16³ × 32 lattice, using NVIDIA CUDA GPUs via the QUDA library. They compared this exact result against stochastic estimates using complex Z₂ noise with color, spin, even-odd, and cubic dilution schemes, as well as the truncated solver method, for fermion loops with Γ = 1, γ₅, and γ_μ insertions relevant to the η′ mass, nucleon sigma term, and nucleon electromagnetic form factors.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: am_π = 0.3452(29), corresponding to m_π = 746 MeV after setting the inverse lattice spacing a⁻¹ = 2.16(3) GeV using the Sommer parameter r₀ = 0.5 fm
- η′ mass extracted: am_η′ = 0.54(10), or m_η′ = 1.17(22) GeV
- For the η′ disconnected correlator D(t) at t/a = 3, stochastic error remained almost unchanged after about 600-800 inversions for all four dilution schemes
- The truncated solver method reproduced the exact η′ result with about 215 high-precision inversions (50 high-precision vectors plus ~2500 low-precision vectors), compared to about 600 inversions for other stochastic methods
- For the electromagnetic form factor loops with γ_μ insertion, spin dilution required about 25% of the exact evaluation cost without full convergence, while the truncated solver method achieved convergence with ~10³ high-precision noise vectors (about 2% of the low-precision vectors used)

## Context

This work extends the stochastic evaluation techniques for disconnected diagrams previously applied to the η′ mass, nucleon σ-term, and electromagnetic form factors by groups such as Jansen (2008), Bali (2009), Doi (2009), Babich (2010), Takeda (2010), and Feng (2011). By computing exact all-to-all propagators on a small lattice, the authors provide a benchmark showing that the truncated solver method of Bali (2009) is the most efficient stochastic approach, though the optimal number of noise vectors depends on the operator insertion (γ₅ vs. γ_μ vs. scalar). The extracted η′ mass of 1.17(22) GeV is slightly higher than the SESAM Collaboration estimate and reasonably consistent with the N_f=2 twisted mass fermion result of Jansen (2008) extrapolated to 0.865(65)(65) GeV at the physical point, given the heavier quark mass used here.
