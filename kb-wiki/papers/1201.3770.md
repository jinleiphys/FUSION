# Kaonic atoms and in-medium $K^-N$ amplitudes

> Recent work on the connection between in-medium subthreshold $K^-N$ amplitudes and kaonic atom potentials is updated by using a next to leading order chirally motivated coupled channel separable interaction model that reproduces $\bar KN$ observables at low energies, including the very recent SIDDHARTA results for the atomic $K^-$-hydrogen $1s$ level shift and width. The corresponding $K^-$-nucleus potential is evaluated self-consistently within a single-nucleon approach and is critically reviewed with respect to empirical features of phenomenological optical potentials. The need to supplement the single-nucleon based approach with multi-nucleon interactions is demonstrated by showing that additional empirical absorptive and dispersive terms, beyond the reach of chirally motivated $K^-$-nucleus potentials, are required in order to achieve good agreement with the bulk of the data on kaonic atoms.

# Kaonic atoms and in-medium $K^-N$ amplitudes

## Key claim
The single-nucleon chirally motivated coupled channel model NLO30, which reproduces low-energy $\bar K N$ observables including SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen data, fails to describe the bulk of kaonic atom data unless supplemented by additional phenomenological multi-nucleon terms; the steep decrease of the imaginary part of the in-medium $K^-N$ amplitude with density is identified as the main deficiency of the single-nucleon approach.

## Method
The authors constructed $K^-$-nucleus optical potentials using in-medium $K^-N$ scattering amplitudes from the NLO30 chirally motivated separable interaction model, with subthreshold energy shifts evaluated self-consistently via density-dependent averages of the Mandelstam variable $\sqrt{s}$ and relative momentum $p$. They solved the Klein-Gordon equation for kaonic atoms across the periodic table and compared calculated level shifts and widths to the full data set of 65 points, then added phenomenological $b\rho + B\rho^2/\rho_0$ terms to the potential to achieve acceptable fits.

## Key numbers
- The NLO30 model alone yields $\chi^2$ per point of 10–12 for the full kaonic atom data set of 65 points.
- Adding adjustable $b\rho + B\rho^2/\rho_0$ terms reduces $\chi^2$ per point to 2.0–2.3.
- At nuclear matter density $\rho_0 = 0.17\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$, the real part of the "with SE" amplitude at threshold corresponds to a potential depth of 40–50 MeV.
- The downward energy shift at 50% of nuclear matter density is approximately 40 MeV.
- The momentum scale parameter of the NLO30 model is $\alpha_{\bar K N} = 700\ \mathrm{MeV}/c$, and $p(\rho_0)$ values reach up to 275 MeV/c for phenomenological potential depths of 180 MeV.
- The average nucleon binding energy used is $B_N \approx 8.5$ MeV.

## Context
This work updates earlier studies by Friedman and Gal (2007) and Cieplý, Friedman, Gal, Gazda, and Mareš (2011, 2011a) that first demonstrated the need for multi-nucleon terms in $K^-$-nucleus potentials derived from chiral models. It extends the approach of Waas, Kaiser, and Weise (1996) and Ramos and Oset (2000) by using the NLO30 model that incorporates SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen data from Bazzi et al. (2011). The finding that phenomenological $\rho^2$ or $\rho^3$ terms are required parallels the situation in pionic atom studies, where single-nucleon contributions are supplemented by $\rho^2$ terms representing $\pi NN$ processes as introduced by Ericson and Ericson (1966). The results are relevant to understanding $K^-$ mesons in multistrange self-bound matter and compact stars.
