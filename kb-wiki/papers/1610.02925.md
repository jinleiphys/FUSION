# Vector boson production in proton-lead and lead-lead collisions at the LHC and its impact on nCTEQ15 PDFs

> We provide a comprehensive comparison of W/Z vector boson production data in proton-lead and lead-lead collisions at the LHC with predictions obtained using the nCTEQ15 PDFs. We identify the measurements which have the largest potential impact on the PDFs, and estimate the effect of including these data using a Monte Carlo reweighting method. We find this data set can provide information about both the nuclear corrections and the heavy flavor (strange) PDF components. As the proton flavor determination is dependent on nuclear corrections (from heavy target DIS, for example), this information can also help improve the proton PDFs.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that LHC W/Z boson production data in proton-lead and lead-lead collisions can simultaneously constrain nuclear PDF corrections and the strange quark component of proton PDFs, but the extraction of nuclear effects is "intimately intertwined" with the strange quark distribution, requiring careful disentanglement of these two effects.

## Method

The authors performed next-to-leading order calculations using the FEWZ program with nCTEQ15 nuclear PDFs and CT10 proton PDFs, comparing predictions to rapidity distributions from ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, and LHCb for W and Z boson production in pPb collisions at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV and PbPb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV. They used a Monte Carlo reweighting method with 10,000 replicas generated from the nCTEQ15 Hessian error PDFs to estimate the impact of including the pPb data, applying a modified Giele-Keller weight formula with a tolerance criterion of T approximately 13 corresponding to 68% confidence level.

## Key numbers

- pPb collisions at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV and PbPb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV were analyzed
- The rapidity shift for pPb collisions is delta y = -0.465, calculated as (1/2)ln(82/208)
- 10,000 replicas (N_rep=10^4) were used for the reweighting procedure, reproducing central PDFs to within approximately 0.1% or better
- The nCTEQ15 tolerance criterion T=35 at 90% c.l. was rescaled to T approximately 13 at 68% c.l.
- The shift of 2-flavor results compared to 5-flavor results can be as large as approximately 30%, reflecting strange and charm quark contributions
- For the 2-flavor case (u,d only), all nuclear results (nCTEQ15, CT10+EPS09, CTEQ6.1+EPS09) coalesce and become distinct from the non-nuclear CT10 result

## Context

This work extends the nCTEQ15 global analysis (Kovarik 2015) by evaluating the impact of LHC heavy ion W/Z data that were not included in the original fit. It builds on prior reweighting studies using EPS09 (Eskola 2009) and DSSZ (de Florian 2011) nPDFs by Armesto (2015) and Ru (2016), and addresses the long-standing issue that nuclear target data from neutrino-DIS have been essential for identifying individual parton flavors (Ball 2014, Harland-Lang 2014, Dulat 2015), meaning heavy flavor uncertainties are tied to nuclear uncertainties. The results enable future global fits that simultaneously constrain nuclear corrections and the strange quark PDF, particularly in the small-x region where current nPDFs rely on extrapolation.
