# Microscopic derivation of the Bohr-Mottelson collective Hamiltonian and its application to quadrupole shape dynamics

> We discuss the nature of the low-frequency quadrupole vibrations from small-amplitude to large-amplitude regimes. We consider full five-dimensional quadrupole dynamics including three-dimensional rotations restoring the broken symmetries as well as axially symmetric and asymmetric shape fluctuations. Assuming that the time-evolution of the self-consistent mean field is determined by five pairs of collective coordinates and collective momenta, we microscopically derive the collective Hamiltonian of Bohr and Mottelson, which describes low-frequency quadrupole dynamics. We show that the five-dimensional collective Schrödinger equation is capable of describing large-amplitude quadrupole shape dynamics seen as shape coexistence/mixing phenomena. We summarize the modern concepts of microscopic theory of large-amplitude collective motion, which is underlying the microscopic derivation of the Bohr-Mottelson collective Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the low-frequency quadrupole collective Hamiltonian of Bohr and Mottelson can be derived microscopically from the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) theory, provided the time evolution of the self-consistent mean field is described by five pairs of collective coordinates and momenta; the derivation is valid under the adiabatic assumption that the time scales of single-particle and collective motions are well separated, and the resulting collective inertial masses are not constant but functions of the deformation variables.

## Method

The authors start from the TDHFB theory for superfluid nuclei and introduce five collective coordinates (the quadrupole deformation parameters β and γ plus three Euler angles) and their conjugate momenta as dynamical variables specifying the time-dependent mean field. They then apply the adiabatic self-consistent coordinate (ASCC) method and the local QRPA method to derive the classical kinetic energy terms for vibrations and rotations, from which the collective inertial masses (Dββ, Dβγ, Dγγ and the moments of inertia Jk) are obtained as functions of β and γ. Finally, the classical Hamiltonian is quantized using the Pauli prescription for curvilinear coordinates to produce the five-dimensional collective Schrödinger equation.

## Key numbers

- The first excited 2+ states in even-even nuclei have excitation energies very low compared to the energy gap 2Δ that characterizes superfluidity, with a rule-of-thumb ratio E(2+)/2Δ less than 0.1 for regular rotors.
- The rotational moments of inertia evaluated from E(2+) are about half of the rigid-body value, providing evidence that nuclear ground states are in a superfluid phase.
- The paper discusses superdeformed nuclei with prolate shapes having an axis ratio of about 2:1.

## Context

This review extends the microscopic foundation of the Bohr-Mottelson collective model that began with Bohr and Mottelson (1952) and was summarized in their textbook (Bohr and Mottelson, 1975) and Nobel lectures (1976). It builds on the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) introduced by Baranger (1960) and Marumori (1960) and on the subsequent development of large-amplitude collective motion theory starting in the mid-1970s (Abe, 1983). The work enables the unified description of shape coexistence and mixing phenomena across the nuclear chart, and the techniques are applicable to other collective motions such as octupole vibrations and spontaneous fission.
