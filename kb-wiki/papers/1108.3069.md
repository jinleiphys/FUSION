# Nonperturbative renormalization of the neutrinoless double-beta operator in p-shell nuclei

> We use Lee-Suzuki mappings and related techniques to construct effective two-body p-shell interactions and neutrinoless double-beta operators that exactly reproduce the results of large no-core-shell-model calculations of double-beta decay in nuclei with mass number A=6. We then apply the effective operators to the decay of nuclei with A=7, 8, and 10, again comparing with no-core calculations in much larger spaces. The results with the effective two-body operators are generally good. In some cases, however, they differ non-negligibly from the full no-core results, suggesting that three-body corrections to the decay operator in heavier nuclei may be important. An application of our procedure and related ideas to fp-shell nuclei such as 76Ge should be feasible within coupled-cluster theory.

## Key claim

The authors use Lee-Suzuki mappings to construct effective two-body p-shell neutrinoless double-beta operators that exactly reproduce large no-core-shell-model results for A=6, but when applied to A=7, 8, and 10 the effective two-body operators sometimes differ non-negligibly from full no-core results, suggesting that three-body corrections to the decay operator in heavier nuclei may be important.

## Method

The authors performed no-core shell-model (NCSM) calculations for nuclei with mass numbers A=6 through 10 using the CD-Bonn potential and the N3LO chiral effective-field-theory interaction, with model spaces allowing between 6 and 10 ℏω of excitation energy. They applied Lee-Suzuki techniques to map the full-space results onto a small p-shell model space (with an inert 0s-shell core), constructing effective two-body Hamiltonians and decay operators that exactly reproduce the A=6 results. They then tested these effective operators by comparing p-shell calculations of the double-beta decay matrix-element distribution C(r) and its integral for A=7, 8, and 10 against the full NCSM results.

## Key numbers

- For the SRG-evolved N3LO potential with Nmax=6: full matrix element for A=7 is 1.76, bare p-shell gives 1.49, effective operator gives 1.90
- For the same calculation: full matrix element for A=8 is 0.48, bare gives 0.18, effective gives 0.59
- For the same calculation: full matrix element for A=10 is 0.79, bare gives 0.91, effective gives 1.23
- For the CD-Bonn interaction with Nmax=8 (A=8) and Nmax=6 (A=10): full matrix element for A=8 is -0.41, bare gives -0.48, effective gives -0.03
- For the CD-Bonn interaction: full matrix element for A=10 is -0.67, bare gives -0.80, effective gives -0.68
- For the SRG-evolved N3LO potential with Nmax=2: full matrix element for A=7 is -1.06, bare gives -0.90, effective gives -0.92

## Context

This work extends the program of Lisetskiy et al. (2008, 2009) who applied similar nonperturbative renormalization to charge-conserving electromagnetic transition operators (which are one-body at leading order) to the case of the two-body neutrinoless double-beta decay operator. The results suggest that a similar procedure applied to fp-shell nuclei such as 76Ge should be feasible within coupled-cluster theory, as reported by Jansen et al. (2011) for coupled-cluster calculations in 6He. The authors note that the oscillatory nature of the matrix-element distribution C(r) in light nuclei may not be as problematic in heavier nuclei where both QRPA and shell-model calculations show C(r) nearly vanishes beyond about 3 fm.
