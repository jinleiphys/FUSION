# Light vector correlator in medium: Wilson coefficients up to dimension 6 operators

> As an improvement of the QCD sum rule method to study modifications of light vector mesons in nuclear matter and/or at finite temperature, we calculate the Wilson coefficients of all independent gluonic non-scalar operators up to dimension 6 in the operator product expansion (OPE) of the vector channel for light quarks. To obtain the gluon part of the light quark OPE from the heavy quark one, we also compute the heavy quark expansion of the relevant quark condensates. Together with the results for the quark operators that are already available in the literature, this completes the OPE of the vector channel in a hot or dense medium for operators up to dimension 6.

# Light vector correlator in medium: Wilson coefficients up to dimension 6 operators

## Key claim
The authors calculate for the first time the Wilson coefficients of all independent gluonic non-scalar operators up to dimension 6 in the operator product expansion (OPE) of the vector channel for light quarks, completing the OPE for the vector channel in hot or dense medium. The results are obtained at leading order in alpha_s, and the authors note that the dimension 4 spin-2 gluon operator Wilson coefficient (including its m^2 correction) had never been correctly given in the literature before.

## Method
The authors start from the heavy quark OPE of the vector correlator, which contains only gluon condensate contributions, and take the small quark mass limit. They then subtract the gluonic contributions that become part of the quark condensates in the light quark limit, obtained via a heavy quark expansion (HQE) of the relevant quark condensates. The HQE is performed in D dimensions using dimensional regularization with the MS-bar scheme to ensure consistency in the spin decomposition of operators.

## Key numbers
- The scalar part of the final gluon condensate OPE gives: Pi^{scalar} = (1/(pi^2 Q^4))*(1/48 + (1/36)*(m^2/Q^2))*<G^2> + (1/(pi^2 Q^6))*((1/324) + (1/54)*Logf)*<j^2>
- The spin-2 dimension 4 contribution: I^{4,2}_{mu nu} = ((1/(8 pi^2)) - (47/(48 pi^2))*(m^2/Q^2) + (-1/(6 pi^2) + (5/(6 pi^2))*(m^2/Q^2))*Logf)*G2_{mu nu}
- The spin-2 dimension 6 contribution for I^{6,2}_{mu nu} includes terms with coefficients: (-1/(60 pi^2) + (1/(96 pi^2))*Logf) for X_{mu nu}, (-361/(2880 pi^2) + (3/(32 pi^2))*Logf) for Y_{mu nu}, and (19/(320 pi^2) + (1/(32 pi^2))*Logf) for Z_{mu nu}
- The spin-4 dimension 6 contribution: I^{6,4}_{mu nu kappa lambda} = (-133/(180 pi^2) + (11/(30 pi^2))*Logf)*G4_{mu nu kappa lambda}
- The heavy quark expansion for the scalar quark condensate gives: <qbar q> = -<G^2>/(48 pi^2 m) - <G^3>/(1440 pi^2 m^3) - <j^2>/(120 pi^2 m^3)

## Context
This work extends the QCD sum rule approach for studying light vector mesons (rho, omega, phi) in nuclear matter and at finite temperature, building on the heavy quark OPE results of Kim (2000) and the scalar OPE work of Generalis (1983). The authors note that previous attempts for gluonic non-scalar operators were made by Leupold (1998) but were incomplete. The results enable future QCD sum rule analyses of vector meson modifications in medium, particularly for the phi meson where finite density effects from quark condensates are suppressed, and for studying dispersion relations of vector mesons at non-zero momentum, which is relevant for interpreting experiments such as the E16 experiment at J-PARC.
