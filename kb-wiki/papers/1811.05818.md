# On the origin of forward-backward multiplicity correlations in $pp$ collisions at ultrarelativistic energies

> We study multiplicity correlations of hadrons in forward and backward hemispheres in $pp$ inelastic interactions at energies 200GeV $\leq \sqrt{s} \leq$ 13TeV within the microscopic quark-gluon string model. The model correctly describes (i) the almost linear dependence of average multiplicity in one hemisphere on the particle multiplicity in other hemisphere in the center-of-mass frame; (ii) the increase of the slope parameter $b_{corr}$ with rising collision energy; (iii) the quick falloff of the correlation strength with increase of the midrapidity gap; (iv) saturation of the forward-backward correlations at very high multiplicities. Investigation of the sub-processes on partonic level reveals that these features can be attributed to short-range partonic correlations within a single string and superposition of several sub-processes containing different numbers of soft and hard Pomerons with different mean multiplicities. If the number of Pomerons in the event is fixed, no forward-backward correlations are observed. Predictions are made for the top LHC energy $\sqrt{s} = 13$TeV.

## Key claim

The paper argues that forward-backward multiplicity correlations in $pp$ collisions arise not from intrinsic correlations within a single particle-production source, but from the superposition of sub-processes (different numbers of soft and hard Pomerons) that have different mean multiplicities; when the number of Pomerons is fixed, no forward-backward correlations are observed.

## Method

The authors used the Monte Carlo version of the quark-gluon string model (QGSM) to simulate inelastic $pp$ collisions at center-of-mass energies from 200 GeV to 13 TeV. They analyzed the dependence of the average charged-particle multiplicity in the backward hemisphere on the multiplicity in the forward hemisphere, both for the full event sample and for sub-samples with fixed numbers of soft and hard Pomerons. The model's predictions were compared to experimental data from the UA5 and ALICE collaborations.

## Key numbers

*   The slope parameter $b_{corr}$ increases from $0.46 \pm 0.01$ at $\sqrt{s} = 200$ GeV to $0.59 \pm 0.02$ at $\sqrt{s} = 900$ GeV for non-single diffractive (NSD) $pp$ collisions.
*   At $\sqrt{s} = 7$ TeV, the model reproduces the ALICE data for $\langle n_B(n_F) \rangle$ under kinematic cuts $0.3 \leq p_T \leq 1.5$ GeV/$c$ and $0 \leq \eta \leq 0.8$.
*   The correlation parameter $b_{corr}$ is studied as a function of midrapidity gap $\eta_{gap}$ (from 0 to 1.2) for pseudorapidity bin widths $\delta \eta = 0.2, 0.4, 0.6, 0.8$ at $\sqrt{s} = 900$ GeV, 2.76 TeV, 7 TeV, and 13 TeV.
*   In events with two soft Pomerons, the average multiplicity shifts from 40 for 1SP+1HP events to 100 for 1SP+7HP events at $\sqrt{s} = 7$ TeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier QGSM studies of forward-backward correlations at lower energies (e.g., at $p_{lab} = 32$ GeV/$c$ by Bravina et al. (1989)) to the LHC energy range. It confirms the interpretation that the correlations are due to event mixing, as previously argued in the dual parton model and by Amelin (1994). The results enable a better understanding of the origin of long-range correlations in $pp$ collisions, which is relevant for the ongoing debate about collective effects like the ridge in small systems.
