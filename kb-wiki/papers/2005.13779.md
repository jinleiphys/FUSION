# Moments of nucleon isovector structure functions in $2+1+1$-flavor QCD

> We present results on the isovector momentum fraction, $\langle x \rangle_{u-d}$, helicity moment, $\langle x \rangle_{Δu-Δd}$, and the transversity moment, $\langle x \rangle_{δu-δd}$, of the nucleon obtained using nine ensembles of gauge configurations generated by the MILC collaboration using $2+1+1$-flavors of dynamical highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ). The correlation functions are calculated using the Wilson-Clover action and the renormalization of the three operators is carried out nonperturbatively on the lattice in the RI${}^\prime$-MOM scheme. The data have been collected at lattice spacings $a \approx 0.15,\ 0.12,\ 0.09,$ and 0.06 fm and $M_π\approx 310,\ 220$ and 135 MeV, which are used to obtain the physical values using a simultaneous chiral-continuum-finite-volume fit. The final results, in the $\overline{MS}$ scheme at 2 GeV, are $\langle x \rangle_{u-d} = 0.173(14)(07)$, $\langle x \rangle_{Δu-Δd} = 0.213(15)(22)$ and $\langle x \rangle_{δu-δd} = 0.208(19)(24)$, where the first error is the overall analysis uncertainty and the second is an additional systematic uncertainty due to possible residual excited-state contributions. These results are consistent with other recent lattice calculations and phenomenological global fit values.

# Moments of nucleon isovector structure functions in 2+1+1-flavor QCD

## Key claim
The authors present lattice QCD results for the isovector momentum fraction, helicity moment, and transversity moment of the nucleon that are consistent with other recent lattice calculations and phenomenological global fit values, with the caveat that an additional systematic uncertainty due to possible residual excited-state contributions is assigned as a second error in each result.

## Method
The calculation uses nine ensembles of gauge configurations generated by the MILC Collaboration with 2+1+1 flavors of dynamical highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ), with correlation functions computed using the Wilson-Clover action. Renormalization of the three one-derivative operators (vector, axial-vector, and tensor) is carried out nonperturbatively on the lattice in the RI'-MOM scheme. The data are collected at four lattice spacings (a ≈ 0.15, 0.12, 0.09, and 0.06 fm) and three pion masses (Mπ ≈ 310, 220, and 135 MeV), and the physical values are obtained using a simultaneous chiral-continuum-finite-volume fit.

## Key numbers
- ⟨x⟩_{u-d} = 0.173(14)(07) in the MSbar scheme at 2 GeV, where the first error is the overall analysis uncertainty and the second is systematic due to possible residual excited-state contributions
- ⟨x⟩_{Δu-Δd} = 0.213(15)(22) in the MSbar scheme at 2 GeV
- ⟨x⟩_{δu-δd} = 0.208(19)(24) in the MSbar scheme at 2 GeV
- Lattice spacings used: a ≈ 0.1510(20) fm, 0.1207(11) fm, 0.0888(08) fm, and 0.0582(04) fm (the finest ensemble a06m135 has a = 0.0570(01) fm)
- Pion masses used: Mπ ≈ 320.6(4.3) MeV, 310.2(2.8) MeV, 227.9(1.9) MeV, 138.1(1.0) MeV, and 135.6(1.4) MeV
- The a06m135 ensemble at physical pion mass yields ⟨x⟩_{u-d} = 0.155(14) from the {4,3*} fit strategy

## Context
This work extends the lattice QCD calculations of nucleon structure moments to include a comprehensive simultaneous chiral-continuum-finite-volume fit using nine ensembles, building on the methodology developed by the PNDME Collaboration in Bhattacharya (2015), Bhattacharya (2016), and Gupta (2018). The results are compared with other recent lattice calculations and phenomenological global fits, showing a consistent picture emerging for these quantities. The precision achieved for the first moments is now comparable to that for nucleon charges (zeroth moments), enabling future comparisons with data from the planned Electron-Ion Collider and the Large Hadron Collider.
