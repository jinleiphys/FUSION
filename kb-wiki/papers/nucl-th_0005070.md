# Dynamic and Quasistatic Trajectories in Quasifission Reactions and Particle Emission

> We show that the quasifission paths predicted by the one-body dissipation dynamics, in the slowest phase of a binary reaction, follow a quasistatic path, which represents a sequence of states of thermal equilibrium at a fixed value of the deformation coordinate. This establishes the use of the statistical particle-evaporation model in the case of dynamical time-evolving systems. Pre- and post-scission multiplicities of neutrons and total multiplicities of protons and alpha particles in fission reactions of 63Cu+92Mo, 60Ni+100Mo, 63Cu+100Mo at 10 MeV/u and 20Ne+144,148,154Sm at 20 MeV/u are reproduced reasonably well with statistical model calculations performed along dynamic trajectories whose slow stage (from the most compact configuration up to the point where the neck starts to develop) lasts some 35x10^{-21} s.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that quasifission trajectories in the slowest (reseparation) phase of heavy-ion reactions follow a quasistatic path representing a sequence of states of thermal equilibrium, which justifies applying the statistical particle-evaporation model to dynamical time-evolving systems. The authors show this holds specifically for trajectories whose slow stage lasts approximately 35 × 10⁻²¹ s, from the most compact configuration up to the point where the neck starts to develop.

## Method

The authors compared dynamic trajectories from the one-body dissipation model (implemented in the HICOL code) with quasistatic paths calculated using the extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) model with SkM* Skyrme interaction and Yukawa-plus-exponential (YPE) forces. They characterized nuclear shapes using two collective variables: the elongation coordinate D_mm (distance between centers of mass of the two halves of the nucleus) and the neck radius R_neck. Monte Carlo simulations of light-particle evaporation chains were performed along single "effective" dynamic trajectories, with pre-scission and post-scission multiplicities calculated for neutrons, protons, and alpha particles.

## Key numbers

- The slow stage of the effective dynamic trajectories lasts some 35 × 10⁻²¹ s, consistent with the fission time scale of (35 ± 15) × 10⁻²¹ s deduced from systematic studies of pre-scission neutron multiplicities
- For the system ⁶⁰Ni+¹⁰⁰Mo at 600 MeV beam energy and J = 86 ℏ, the mononucleus lives about 30 × 10⁻²¹ s, the scission stage lasts about 5 × 10⁻²¹ s, and the first stage (when neck fills in) takes about 0.2 × 10⁻²¹ s
- The duration of the reseparation stage decreases from 45 × 10⁻²¹ s to 15 × 10⁻²¹ s when J increases from 85 ℏ to 100 ℏ
- The YPE deformation energy reaches its maximum (about 27.8 MeV) at D_mm ∼ 13.6 fm, while the SkM* energy reaches its maximum (about 25.1 MeV) at D_mm ∼ 15.6 fm
- For ⁶⁰Ni+¹⁰⁰Mo at 10 MeV/u, the fusion-fission J-window at the beginning of the equilibrium emission stage is estimated to be 78–103 ℏ, with trajectories above 83 ℏ belonging to quasifission

## Context

This work extends the quasistatic path concept from Strutinsky (1962) and the one-body dissipation model of Blocki et al. to medium-mass systems (A ≈ 160) where quasifission and fusion-fission are difficult to disentangle. The study addresses the need identified by Gontchar and others to incorporate quasifission trajectories into fission-evaporation routines, and tests the compatibility of the neutron clock with the charged particle clock using data from Gonin et al. (1994) and Lou et al. (1995). The results enable a unified description of fusion-fission and quasifission reactions accompanied by particle emission, and show that the majority of fission-evaporation reactions in this mass region start on quasifission trajectories that later end up on trajectories with a non-zero fission barrier after losing angular momentum.
