# Centrality bin size dependence of multiplicity correlation in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_NN}$=200 GeV}

> We have studied the centrality bin size dependence of charged particle forward-backward multiplicity correlation strength in 5%, 0-5%, and 0-10% most central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_NN}}$=200 GeV with a parton and hadron cascade model, PACIAE based on PYTHIA. The real (total), statistical, and NBD (Negative Binomial Distribution) correlation strengths are calculated by the real events, the mixed events, and fitting the charged particle multiplicity distribution to the NBD, respectively. It is turned out that the correlation strength increases with increasing centrality bin size monotonously. If the discrepancy between real (total) and statistical correlation strengths is identified as dynamical one, the dynamical correlation may just be a few percent of the total (real) correlation.

## Key claim
The charged particle forward-backward multiplicity correlation strength in central Au+Au collisions increases monotonically with increasing centrality bin size. However, the dynamical component of this correlation, defined as the difference between the real (total) and statistical correlation strengths, may constitute only a few percent of the total correlation.

## Method
The authors used the PACIAE parton and hadron cascade model (based on PYTHIA) to simulate Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV. They generated 6000 real events for three centrality selections (5%, 0-5%, and 0-10% most central) and constructed mixed events by randomly sampling particles from the real event pool while preserving the multiplicity of each event. The forward-backward multiplicity correlation strength was calculated for real events (total correlation), mixed events (statistical correlation), and by fitting the charged particle multiplicity distribution to a Negative Binomial Distribution (NBD correlation).

## Key numbers
- In 0-10% most central Au+Au collisions, the real correlation strength is approximately 0.63, the statistical correlation strength is approximately 0.59, and the NBD correlation strength is approximately 0.52.
- The total charged particle multiplicity in 0-6% most central collisions from PACIAE is 4819 for |η| < 4.7, 4983 for |η| < 5.4, and 5100 for total, compared to PHOBOS data of 4810 ± 240, 4960 ± 250, and 5060 ± 250 respectively.
- The impact parameter mapping for 0-10% most central collisions corresponds to b_i < 4.46 fm, for 0-5% to b_i < 3.20 fm, and for 5% to b_i = 3.20 fm.

## Context
This work extends the statistical model of Fu (2008) which calculated the correlation strength in 0-10% central collisions using PHENIX multiplicity data and obtained b ≈ 0.44, compared to the STAR datum of b ≈ 0.60. The present study uses the PACIAE transport model to reproduce the STAR data feature of a flat correlation strength across Δη and demonstrates that the correlation strength depends on centrality bin size. The results enable future investigations of the correlation strength in mid-central and peripheral collisions, where the STAR data show an exponential dependence on Δη.
