# Color Glass Condensate and Glasma

> We review the Color Glass Condensate effective theory, that describes the gluon content of a high energy hadron or nucleus, in the saturation regime. The emphasis is put on applications to high energy heavy ion collisions. After describing initial state factorization, we discuss the Glasma phase, that precedes the formation of an equilibrated quark-gluon plasma. We end this review with a presentation of recent developments in the study of the isotropization and thermalization of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory provides a weak-coupling framework for describing the gluon content of high-energy hadrons and nuclei in the saturation regime, where the gluon occupation number becomes of order \(1/\alpha_s\) and non-perturbative effects arise from large parton densities rather than from a large coupling constant.

## Method

The paper reviews the CGC effective theory, which exploits Lorentz time dilation to treat fast partons in a hadron's wavefunction as static color sources, and uses the eikonal approximation to compute scattering amplitudes via Wilson lines. The framework is applied to Deep Inelastic Scattering (dipole scattering at leading and next-to-leading order), proton-nucleus collisions, and nucleus-nucleus collisions, where the classical field description leads to the Glasma phase. The review also covers the factorization of large logarithms in loop calculations and the role of Yang-Mills instabilities in the isotropization and thermalization of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key numbers

- The Lorentz gamma factor is \(\gamma \sim 100\) at RHIC and \(\gamma \sim 1000\) in heavy ion collisions at the LHC.
- The saturation momentum \(Q_s\) is defined as the coupling constant \(\alpha_s\) times the gluon density per unit area, and processes involving momenta smaller than \(Q_s\) may be affected by gluon saturation.
- The critical temperature for the deconfinement transition to a Quark-Gluon Plasma is expected to be reached in collisions at RHIC and LHC, based on lattice simulations (Karsch 2002).
- The one-loop corrections to dipole scattering are proportional to \(\alpha_s \log(p^+)\), where \(p^+\) is the longitudinal momentum of the dipole, and the rapidity \(Y = \log(p^+)\) can become large such that \(\alpha_s Y\) may not be small.

## Context

This review extends the work on gluon saturation and the CGC framework developed by Gribov, Levin, and Ryskin (1983), Mueller (1990), and Blaizot and Mueller (1993), and builds on earlier reviews by Iancu and Venugopalan (2003), Lappi (2008), and Weigert (2005). It provides a consistent framework for describing heavy ion collisions from the pre-collision initial state through the Glasma phase to the onset of hydrodynamics, and discusses recent developments in understanding isotropization and thermalization of the quark-gluon plasma via instabilities in classical Yang-Mills solutions.
