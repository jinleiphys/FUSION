# Debye screening and Meissner effect in a three-flavor color superconductor

> I compute the gluon self-energy in a color superconductor with three flavors of massless quarks, where condensation of Cooper pairs breaks the color and flavor SU(3)_c x U(3)_V x U(3)_A symmetry of QCD to the diagonal subgroup SU(3)_{c+V}. At zero temperature, all eight electric gluons obtain a Debye screening mass, and all eight magnetic gluons a Meissner mass. The Debye as well as the Meissner masses are found to be equal for the different gluon colors. These masses determine the coefficients of the kinetic terms in the effective theory for the low-energy degrees of freedom. Their values agree with those obtained by Son and Stephanov.

## Key claim

In a three-flavor color superconductor with color-flavor locking, all eight electric gluons acquire a Debye screening mass and all eight magnetic gluons acquire a Meissner mass, with the masses equal across gluon colors; the calculation resolves an ambiguity in the literature by confirming the results of Son and Stephanov (2000) and disagreeing with the superluminous velocity implied by Rho, Shuryak, Wirzba, and Zahed (1999).

## Method

The author computed the gluon self-energy at one-loop order in perturbation theory using the Nambu-Gor'kov formalism for QCD in the color-flavor locked ground state, including both diagonal and off-diagonal components of the quasiparticle propagator. The trace over color and flavor was performed simultaneously using color-flavor space projectors introduced by Shovkovy and Wijewardhana (1999), and the self-energy was evaluated in the static limit (p0 = 0, p → 0) at zero temperature to extract the Debye and Meissner masses from the time-like and space-like components respectively.

## Key numbers

- Debye mass squared: m_D^2 = m_g^2 × (21 − 8 ln 2)/18 ≈ 0.859 m_g^2, where m_g^2 = (N_f/3) × (g^2 μ^2)/(2π^2) for N_f = 3 flavors
- Meissner mass squared: m_M^2 = m_g^2 × (21 − 8 ln 2)/54
- Velocity of pseudoscalar mesons: v_π = m_M/m_D = 1/√3
- The Debye mass in the three-flavor superconductor is reduced by a factor of 3.5 compared to its value in a normal cold medium (where m_D^2 = 3 m_g^2)

## Context

This work extends the author's previous calculation for the two-flavor color superconductor (Rischke, 2000) to the three-flavor color-flavor locked phase, and resolves a discrepancy in the literature: Son and Stephanov (2000) obtained m_D^2 = m_g^2 (21−8 ln 2)/18 and m_M^2 = m_g^2 (21−8 ln 2)/54 using an effective theory near the Fermi surface, while Rho, Shuryak, Wirzba, and Zahed (1999) found m_D^2 = m_g^2/2 and m_M^2 = 5 m_g^2/6 from the full theory (implying superluminous v_π = √(5/3)). The results also agree with Zarembo (2000) and agree with Beane, Bedaque, and Savage (2000) up to a factor of 2. The computed masses determine the decay constants and velocities in the effective nonlinear sigma model for the Goldstone bosons (pions, kaons, eta, eta-prime, and the U(1)_V Goldstone mode) in the color-flavor locked phase.
