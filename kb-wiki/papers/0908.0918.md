# An Explanation for Heavy Quark Energy Loss Puzzle by Flow Effects

> The heavy quark energy loss puzzle is explained by collective flow effects in a dynamic medium. The dead cone and LPM effect are found to be changed comparing to the static medium case. Instead of only one dead cone in the static medium, the collective flow induces two dead cones from two different kinds of processes. One is from the projectile emitting gluon process, the same as that in the static medium. The other is from the gluon emission off the exchanged gluon process, decreasing with increasing flow velocity $v_z$ along jet direction, which lead to the increase of heavy quark energy loss. The differences of the effective average energy loss among charm, bottom and light quarks are very little from a full 3D ideal hydrodynamic simulation for 0-10$%$ central Au-Au collisions at RHIC energy. This would yield similar high $p_{T}$ suppressions between light and heavy quarks for central Au-Au collisions.

## Key claim

Collective flow in a dynamic QCD medium, rather than the static scattering centers assumed in prior work, resolves the heavy quark energy loss puzzle by inducing a second, flow-dependent dead cone that shrinks with increasing flow velocity, thereby increasing heavy quark energy loss. The authors claim that for 0-10% central Au-Au collisions at RHIC energy, the effective average energy loss among charm, bottom, and light quarks becomes nearly equal, but this equality holds only in a specific flow velocity range (0.065 < v_z < 0.13) and does not apply for less central collisions.

## Method

The authors computed the first-order opacity contribution to medium-induced radiative energy loss for heavy quarks using a color-screened potential model that incorporates collective flow via a Lorentz boost of the scattering center rest frame. They derived the modified gluon radiation probability including flow-dependent dead cones and LPM effect, then numerically evaluated the effective average energy loss for charm, bottom, and light quarks by averaging over the medium profile from a full 3D ideal hydrodynamic simulation of Au-Au collisions at RHIC energy.

## Key numbers

- For 0-10% central Au-Au collisions at RHIC energy, the average flow velocity along the jet direction is ⟨v_z⟩ = 0.08, which lies in the region where heavy and light quark energy losses are nearly equal.
- For 40-60% central Au-Au collisions at RHIC energy, the average flow velocity is ⟨v_z⟩ = 0.016, which lies in the region where light quark energy loss remains larger than heavy quark energy loss.
- The dead cone from process II (gluon emission off the exchanged gluon) is given by θ₂ = √(m_g² + x²M² - (2v_z x M²)/(1-v_z)) / (xE), which reduces with increasing v_z; at ω/E = 0.2 the ratio θ₂/θ₁ decreases to zero rapidly as v_z increases.
- The region where heavy and light quark energy losses are nearly the same is 0.065 < v_z < 0.13, for parameters E/μ = 20 and L/l_g = 5.

## Context

This work extends the static medium treatment of heavy quark energy loss by Djordjevic (2003) and the dynamical medium study by Heinz (2006), which improved agreement with data but still could not explain the similar suppression of non-photonic electrons and light hadrons observed by the STAR and PHENIX collaborations at RHIC. The authors' previous work (Cheng 2008) introduced a flow-dependent potential model for light quarks; here they apply it to heavy quarks and show that flow effects can reconcile theory with the experimental puzzle. The results enable future comparisons between jet quenching theory and experimental data for heavy flavor suppression at RHIC and LHC energies.
