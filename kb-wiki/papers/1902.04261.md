# Effect of residual many-body forces due to the evolution in the in-medium similarity renormalization group method

> In the past few years in-medium similarity renormalization group methods have been introduced and developed. In these methods the Hamiltonian is evolved using a unitary transformation in order to decouple a reference state from the rest of the Hilbert space. The evolution by itself will generate, even if we start from a two-body interaction, many-body forces which are usually neglected. In this work we estimate the effect of these residual many-body forces by comparing results obtained with the Hybrid Multi-determinant method, which keeps the Hamiltonian within the two-body sector, with the corresponding ones obtained with the in-medium similarity renormalization group. Although percentage-wise the effect of neglecting these induced many-body forces is not too large, they can be appreciable depending on the nucleus, the shell model space and the harmonic oscillator frequency.

## Key claim

The paper estimates the effect of neglecting induced many-body forces in the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) method by comparing IM-SRG(2) results with the Hybrid Multi-Determinant (HMD) method, which keeps the Hamiltonian strictly within the two-body sector. Although the neglected many-body forces contribute only a few percent to the total energy, their effect can be appreciable and depends on the nucleus, the shell model space size, and the harmonic oscillator frequency.

## Method

The author compared ground-state energies for ⁴He and ¹⁶O obtained from two methods: (1) the IM-SRG(2) method using the Brillouin generator with two different reference states (naive Fermi filling and spherical Hartree-Fock), and (2) the Hybrid Multi-Determinant (HMD) method combined with energy-variance extrapolation (EVE) techniques, which does not generate induced many-body forces. Both methods used a chiral N3LO interaction (Entem and Machleidt) renormalized via the Lee-Suzuki method in a harmonic oscillator basis with varying oscillator frequencies and single-particle space sizes defined by the parameter N_lab.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴He at ℏω=24 MeV and N_lab=5: HMD gives -24.127 MeV, IM-SRG with Fermi filling gives -24.261 MeV, IM-SRG with HF reference gives -24.888 MeV
- For ⁴He at ℏω=38 MeV and N_lab=5: HMD gives -23.544 MeV, IM-SRG with Fermi filling gives -22.264 MeV, IM-SRG with HF reference gives -24.907 MeV
- For ¹⁶O at ℏω=14 MeV and N_lab=5: HMD gives -133.397 MeV, IM-SRG with Fermi filling gives -135.467 MeV, IM-SRG with HF reference gives -130.081 MeV
- For ¹⁶O at ℏω=24 MeV and N_lab=4: HMD gives -97.855 MeV, IM-SRG with Fermi filling gives -93.243 MeV, IM-SRG with HF reference gives -95.664 MeV
- For ¹⁶O at ℏω=32 MeV and N_lab=3: HMD gives -62.823 MeV, IM-SRG with Fermi filling gives -58.461 MeV, IM-SRG with HF reference gives -61.097 MeV
- The experimental ground-state energy is -28.295 MeV for ⁴He and -127.619 MeV for ¹⁶O (reference [37])
- The largest Hilbert space size for ¹⁶O with N_lab=5 is about 10²² for J_z^π=0⁺

## Context

This work extends the analysis of induced many-body forces in IM-SRG previously noted in section 5 of Hergert et al. (2016), where a discrepancy between IM-SRG binding energy for ⁴He and the Fadeev-Jakubowski method was found to be sizable. The paper provides a systematic comparison using the HMD method with EVE extrapolation as a benchmark that avoids induced many-body forces entirely. The results suggest that the neglected many-body forces become more important at higher harmonic oscillator frequencies and larger single-particle spaces, and that these discrepancies may be reduced using the more involved multi-reference IM-SRG approach as done in recent work by Hergert et al. (2017).
