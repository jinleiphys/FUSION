# On Gribov's supercriticality picture of quark confinement

> Some years ago Gribov developed the so-called supercritical light quark confinement scenario. Based on physical arguments he conjectured a drastic change in the analytical properties of the quark propagator when the back-reaction of Goldstone bosons (pions) is considered. We investigate this scenario and provide numerical solutions for the quark propagator in the complex plane with and without the pion back-reaction. We find no evidence for the scenario Gribov advocated. As an aside we present a novel method to solve the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation in the complex plane and discuss new characteristics of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in our truncation scheme.

## Key claim

The authors find no evidence for Gribov's conjecture that the back-reaction of Goldstone bosons (pions) on the quark propagator causes a drastic change in its analytic structure sufficient to produce a supercritical confinement mechanism, contrary to Gribov's physical arguments.

## Method

The authors solve the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) in the complex momentum plane using a truncation scheme that includes both non-perturbative gluon exchange (with a Curtis-Pennington vertex or a simpler 1BC-vertex) and one-pion exchange, where the pion-quark vertex is approximated by the leading γ5 term proportional to B(p²)/fπ. They compare the quark mass function, wave function, and Schwinger function with and without pion back-reaction, and determine the pole locations of the quark propagator from fits to the Schwinger function.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass function at zero momentum M(0) with 1BC-vertex, chiral limit, without pion: 269 MeV; with pion: 252 MeV
- Quark mass function at zero momentum M(0) with CP-vertex, chiral limit, without pion: 322 MeV; with pion: 299 MeV
- Chiral condensate (−⟨Ψ̄Ψ⟩_{2 GeV}^{MS})^{1/3} with 1BC-vertex, chiral limit, without pion: 216 MeV; with pion: 208 MeV
- Chiral condensate with CP-vertex, chiral limit, without pion: 289 MeV; with pion: 276 MeV
- Pole location of quark propagator with 1BC-vertex, chiral limit, without pion: 295 ± i 176 MeV; with pion: 279 ± i 160 MeV
- Pole location with CP-vertex, chiral limit, without pion: 513 ± i 0 MeV; with pion: 478 ± i 0 MeV
- Pion mass used: 0 MeV (chiral limit) and 138 MeV (physical); pion decay constant: 90 MeV (chiral limit) and 93 MeV (physical)

## Context

This work tests Gribov's supercritical confinement scenario (Gribov, 1999), which proposed that pion back-reaction changes the quark propagator's analytic structure to produce confinement. The authors use a truncation scheme developed in Fischer (2007) that improves upon Gribov's approximations by employing momentum-dependent running coupling and a vertex satisfying the full Ward-Takahashi identity. The results contradict Gribov's conjecture and instead show that pion back-reaction only modestly reduces dynamical chiral symmetry breaking without qualitatively altering the propagator's analytic structure, enabling future work on the role of hadronic contributions in Dyson-Schwinger approaches to QCD.
