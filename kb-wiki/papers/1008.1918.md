# Radiative electron capture in the first forbidden unique decay of 81Kr

> The photon spectrum accompanying the orbital K-electron capture in the first forbidden unique decay of 81Kr was measured. The total radiation intensity for the photon energies larger than 50 keV was found to be 1.47(6) x 10^{-4} per K-capture. Both the shape of the spectrum and its intensity relative to the ordinary, non-radiative capture rate, are compared to theoretical predictions. The best agreement is found for the recently developed model which employs the length gauge for the electromagnetic field.

## Key claim

The measured total intensity and spectral shape of radiative K-electron capture in the first-forbidden unique decay of 81Kr agree best with the model of Pachucki et al. (2010) that uses the length gauge for the electromagnetic field, while the older Zon and Rapoport model fails to reproduce either the shape or the intensity.

## Method

The REC spectrum was measured by detecting gamma rays in coincidence with Br-KX rays from the K-capture component of the 81Kr ground-state-to-ground-state transition, using two Ge detectors (GMX and LOAX) in a close face-to-face geometry. The source was produced by implanting 81Rb ions at CERN-ISOLDE, which decayed to 81Kr, and measurements were performed in two series totaling 38.6 days and 78 days of coincidence runs, with background subtraction from separate 71-day coincidence runs. The raw coincidence spectra were corrected for Compton scattering using a peeling-off method based on Monte-Carlo simulated detector responses, then normalized by the full-energy-peak efficiency and the total number of Br-KX rays to yield the absolute REC probability per non-radiative K-capture.

## Key numbers

- Total REC intensity for photon energies above 50 keV: 1.47(6) × 10⁻⁴ per K-capture (weighted average of two measurements: 1.2(1) × 10⁻⁴ from the first series and 1.64(8) × 10⁻⁴ from the second series)
- Decay energy Q_EC of 81Kr: 280.8 ± 0.5 keV, giving a photon end-point energy q_K = 267.3 keV for K-capture
- Number of 81Kr atoms in the final source: 4.1(2) × 10¹⁵
- Total coincidence events in the REC spectrum: 16800 counts, corresponding to about 9 coincident events per hour
- GMX detector efficiency at 100 keV: about 14%
- Half-life of 81Kr: 2.3 × 10⁵ years

## Context

This work extends the study of radiative electron capture in first-forbidden unique (1u) transitions to a third case, following earlier measurements on 41Ca by 41Ca (1998) and 204Tl by Kurcewicz (2004), which showed opposite discrepancies with the Zon and Rapoport model. The results strongly support the new theoretical approach by Pachucki et al. (2010) using the length gauge, which successfully reproduces all three 1u cases without adjustable parameters, and enables future tests on second-forbidden non-unique transitions such as 59Ni and 137La.
