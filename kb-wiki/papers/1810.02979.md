# On Mass correction to Chiral Vortical Effect and Chiral Separation Effect

> We study coefficients of axial chiral vortical effect and chiral separation effect at finite temperature and vector chemical potential in massive theories. We present two independent methods of calculating the coefficients: one from field theory and the other using the mass term in axial anomaly equation. An ambiguity in the integration constant similar to hydrodynamic approach to axial chiral vortical effect exists in the latter, but can be fixed naturally in the presence of mass. We obtain perfect agreement between the methods. The results of axial chiral vortical effect and chiral separation effect indicate that the presence of mass generically suppresses the two coefficients, with less suppression at larger chemical potential. For phenomenologically relevant case of quark gluon plasma with three quark flavor, we find the correction is negligible.

## Key claim

The paper shows that fermion mass generically suppresses the coefficients of the axial chiral vortical effect (ACVE) and chiral separation effect (CSE) at finite temperature and vector chemical potential, but for the phenomenologically relevant case of quark-gluon plasma with three quark flavors, the mass correction is negligible (within 1%).

## Method

The authors compute the ACVE and CSE coefficients in massive QED using two independent methods: an intuitive derivation from the axial anomaly equation with a mass term contribution, and a field-theoretic Kubo formula approach using retarded current-current and current-stress-energy correlators in the imaginary-time formalism. The Kubo formula is derived in a restricted setting where constant chemical potential and temperature prevent the generation of a nonvanishing pseudoscalar condensate, allowing axial charge to be treated as conserved. The results from both methods are shown to agree perfectly.

## Key numbers

* ACVE coefficient at zero temperature: sigma_V = (1/(2 pi^2)) * mu * sqrt(mu^2 - m^2)
* CSE coefficient at zero temperature: sigma_B = (1/(2 pi^2)) * sqrt(mu^2 - m^2)
* For QCD with three quark flavors (m_u = m_d approx 0, m_s = 100 MeV), mass correction to both sigma_B and sigma_V is within 1% in the parameter range 50 MeV < mu < 200 MeV and 200 MeV < T < 400 MeV
* Small mass expansion of ACVE coefficient at mu=0: sigma_V = (1/(2 pi^2)) * [ (pi^2 T^2)/3 + m^2 * (1/2 + 2 * (d/ds)(Li_s(-1) + Li_s(-1))|_{s=0} ) + O(m^4) ]
* Small mass expansion of CSE coefficient: sigma_B = (e/(2 pi^2)) * [ mu + (m^2/(2T)) * (d/ds)(Li_s(-e^{mu/T}) - Li_s(-e^{-mu/T}))|_{s=-1} + O(m^4) ]

## Context

This work extends the massless chiral transport coefficients derived in the hydrodynamic approach of Son and Surowka (2009) and Neiman and Oz (2010), and the field-theoretic Kubo formulas of Amado, Landsteiner, and Megias (2011). It also connects to earlier work on mass corrections to CSE by Metlitski and Zhitnitsky (2005) and Gorbar, Miransky, and Shovkovy (2013). The results provide microscopic input for anomalous hydrodynamics in more realistic massive theories and enable phenomenological applications to heavy-ion collisions, where the chiral limit approximation for ACVE and CSE coefficients is justified by the smallness of the mass correction.
