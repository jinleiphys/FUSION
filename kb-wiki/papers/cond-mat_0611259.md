# Acoustic attenuation rate in the Fermi-Bose model with a finite-range fermion-fermion interaction

> We study the acoustic attenuation rate in the Fermi-Bose model describing a mixtures of bosonic and fermionic atom gases. We demonstrate the dramatic change of the acoustic attenuation rate as the fermionic component is evolved through the BEC-BCS crossover, in the context of a mean-field model applied to a finite-range fermion-fermion interaction at zero temperature, such as discussed previously by M.M. Parish et al. [Phys. Rev. B 71, 064513 (2005)] and B. Mihaila et al. [Phys. Rev. Lett. 95, 090402 (2005)]. The shape of the acoustic attenuation rate as a function of the boson energy represents a signature for superfluidity in the fermionic component.

## Key claim

The shape of the acoustic attenuation rate as a function of boson energy provides a signature for superfluidity in the fermionic component, but this claim is conditional on the mean-field model with a finite-range fermion-fermion interaction at zero temperature, and on the assumption that the BEC phonon velocity is less than the Anderson mode velocity to avoid collective mode excitation.

## Method

The authors study a Fermi-Bose model where the bosonic component is a dilute BEC with a Bogoliubov dispersion relation, and the fermionic component is described by a BCS mean-field ground state with a finite-range Gaussian fermion-fermion interaction. They compute the acoustic attenuation rate in first-order perturbation theory as the sum of contributions from the normal and anomalous fermion densities, using the Comte-Nozieres schematic model for the pairing gap and then a more realistic Gaussian potential. The BEC-BCS crossover is parameterized by the dimensionless quantity eta = (k_F a_F)^{-1}, and the attenuation rate is evaluated as a function of phonon energy for different values of the pairing gap and scattering length.

## Key numbers

- The boson coherence length xi_B is typically of order 0.1 to 1 micrometer, and the phonon velocity c is approximately 0.01 micrometer MHz in condensates with density of 100 per cubic micrometer.
- For the realistic finite-range interaction, the BEC-BCS crossover occurs for eta = (k_F a_F)^{-1} between 0.15 and 0.80, at constant density corresponding to k_F times the interaction range approximately equal to 0.37.
- The bosonic dispersion parameters used are c = 0.02 micrometer MHz and m_B xi_B = 1.6 (atomic mass units times micrometers).
- For the schematic model, the chemical potential is fixed at mu/epsilon_F = 1 and xi_B k_F = 5, with pairing-gap values Delta_0/epsilon_F = 4 (large gap) and 0.1 (small gap).

## Context

This work extends the mean-field treatment of the BEC-BCS crossover in Fermi-Bose mixtures previously discussed by Parish et al. (2005) and Mihaila et al. (2005), using a finite-range fermion-fermion interaction rather than a contact interaction. The model is similar to the one proposed by Timmermans et al. for Feshbach resonance physics and by Bardeen et al. for excitonic systems. The results enable experimental detection of fermionic superfluidity through acoustic attenuation measurements in ultracold atom mixtures, complementing Bragg, rf, and spin-noise spectroscopy techniques that probe spin-spin correlation functions as discussed by Buchler et al. and others.
