# Effect of nuclear medium on the spatial distribution of pions

> We calculate the valence quark generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of the lightest pseudoscalar meson, pion, in an isospin asymmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature by employing a light-cone quark model. The medium modifications in the unpolarized GPDs have been incorporated by taking inputs from the chiral SU($3$) quark mean field model. The electromagnetic form factors (EMFFs) and charge radii have been calculated for both the vacuum and in-medium cases. These results are found to be in agreement with the available experimental data and other model predictions.

## Key claim

The authors calculate valence quark generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of the pion in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature, finding that the baryonic density of the medium significantly modifies the distributions while isospin asymmetry has a smaller effect, with results stated to agree with available experimental data and other model predictions.

## Method

The unpolarized chiral-even GPD \(H(x,0,t)\) of the pion was computed using a light-cone quark model (LCQM) with a Brodsky-Huang-Lepage prescription for the momentum space wave function. Medium modifications were incorporated by using effective quark masses and energies obtained from the chiral SU(3) quark mean field (CQMF) model, which includes scalar (\(\sigma\), \(\zeta\), \(\delta\)) and vector (\(\omega\), \(\rho\)) meson fields to account for baryon density and isospin asymmetry. The GPD was evaluated at zero skewness (\(\xi=0\)) via the overlap of light-cone wave functions, and the electromagnetic form factors (EMFFs) and charge radii were derived from its first moment.

## Key numbers

* Vacuum pion charge radius: the model reproduces the experimental value, with the in-medium charge radius increasing with baryon density up to \(2\rho_0\) (where \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is nuclear saturation density).
* At baryon density \(\rho_B = 2\rho_0\) and isospin asymmetry \(\eta = (\rho_n - \rho_p)/2\rho_B\), the unpolarized GPD at \(t=0\) becomes flatter and broader, with increased amplitude in the regions \(0 < x < 0.25\) and \(0.75 < x < 1\) compared to vacuum.
* For \(-t = 0.5\) GeV\(^2\), the in-medium GPD peak shifts to larger \(x\) values faster up to \(\rho_B = 1.5\rho_0\), and negative distributions appear at low \(x\) for higher densities.
* The EMFFs drop more rapidly as a function of \(-t\) (GeV\(^2\)) with increasing \(\rho_B\), indicating stronger interactions in denser medium.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of in-medium pion structure using the QMC model (Arifi 2024), NJL model (Hutauruk 2018), and BSE (de Melo 2014) in symmetric nuclear matter, now incorporating isospin asymmetry via the CQMF model. The authors previously computed in-medium PDFs, TMDs, and DAs of the pion (Puhan 2024, Kaur 2024), and here they provide the first calculation of unpolarized pion GPDs in asymmetric nuclear matter. The results enable future comparisons with lattice simulations (Chen 2019, Ding 2024) and experimental extractions from DVCS and DVMP at facilities like the EIC.
