# QCD sum rules for J/psi in the nuclear medium: calculation of the Wilson coefficients of gluon operators up to dimension 6

> We calculate the Wilson coefficients of all dimension 6 gluon operators with non zero spin in the correlation function between two heavy vector currents. For the twist-4 part, we first identify the three independent gluon operators, and then proceed with the calculation of the Wilson coefficients using the fixed point gauge. Together with the previous calculation of the Wilson coefficients for the dimension 6 scalar gluon operators by Nikolaev and Radyushkin, our result completes the list of all the Wilson coefficients of dimension 6 gluon operators in the correlation function between heavy vector currents. We apply our results to investigate the mass of $J/ψ$ in nuclear matter using QCD sum rules. Using an upper bound estimate on the matrix elements of the dimension 6 gluon operators to linear order in density, we find that the density dependent contribution from dimension 6 operators is less than 40% of the dimension 4 operators with opposite sign. The final result gives about -4 MeV mass shift for the charmonium at rest in nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper completes the calculation of all Wilson coefficients for dimension 6 gluon operators in the correlation function between two heavy vector currents, and applies these results to estimate the mass shift of J/ψ in nuclear matter. The authors find that the density-dependent contribution from dimension 6 operators is less than 40% of the dimension 4 contribution with opposite sign, yielding a final mass shift of about -4 MeV for charmonium at rest, but note that this result relies on an upper bound estimate for the nucleon matrix elements of dimension 6 operators since no data or lattice results exist for these quantities.

## Method

The authors calculate the leading-order Wilson coefficients for all dimension 6 gluon operators (scalar, twist-2, and twist-4) in the operator product expansion of the two-point correlation function of heavy vector currents, using the fixed point gauge (Fock-Schwinger gauge) for the quark propagator in an external gluon field. They identify three independent twist-4 gluon operators at dimension 6 and compute their Wilson coefficients, then combine these with previously calculated scalar dimension 6 coefficients by Nikolaev and Radyushkin (1983) and dimension 4 contributions. The nucleon matrix elements of the dimension 6 operators are estimated using ground state saturation hypothesis and linear density approximation, and the moment sum rule method is applied at the normalization point Q² = 4m_c² to extract the J/ψ mass shift in nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- The density-dependent contribution from dimension 6 operators is less than 40% of the dimension 4 contribution with opposite sign.
- The final mass shift for J/ψ at rest in nuclear matter is about -4 MeV.
- The charm quark mass used is m_c = 1.24 GeV.
- The strong coupling at the normalization point is α_s(8m_c²) = 0.21.
- Nuclear matter density is taken as ρ_0 = 0.17/fm³.
- The nucleon mass is m_N = 0.93 GeV, with the chiral limit nucleon mass m_N⁰ = 750 MeV.
- The vacuum gluon condensate is ⟨(α_s/π)G²⟩_0 = (0.35 GeV)⁴.
- The vacuum four-quark condensate gives ⟨(α_s/π)G^a_{μν}G^a_{μα;αν}⟩_0 = (0.23 GeV)⁶.
- The vacuum three-gluon plus four-quark operator gives ⟨(α_s/π)G^a_{μν}G^a_{μν;αα}⟩_0 = (0.60 GeV)⁶.
- The twist-2 gluon moments are A^G_2(8m_c²) ≈ 0.9 and A^G_4(8m_c²) ≈ 0.02.
- The vacuum quark condensate is ⟨q̄q⟩_0 = (-0.23 GeV)³.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach for heavy quark systems in nuclear matter previously applied by Klingl et al. (1999) and Hayashigaki (1999), which truncated the operator product expansion at dimension 4 and found a J/ψ mass shift of about -7 MeV. The calculation completes the list of dimension 6 gluon operator Wilson coefficients, complementing the scalar operator calculations by Nikolaev and Radyushkin (1983) and the twist-4 gluon structure function analysis by Braun and Balla (1999). The results enable systematic study of J/ψ properties in nuclear matter and provide a basis for investigating the momentum dependence of charmonium in medium, which is relevant for understanding J/ψ suppression in relativistic heavy ion collisions as a possible quark-gluon plasma signal proposed by Matsui and Satz (1986).
