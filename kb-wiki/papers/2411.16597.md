# Gluons in the $η'$ and in nucleon resonances

> We discuss the role of gluon dynamics in $η'$ physics and in nucleon resonances where excitations of gluonic potentials may also be important. Interesting phenomenology includes a possible narrow near threshold resonance in $η'$ photoproduction and whether the parity doublets observed in the higher mass nucleon resonance spectrum might be hinting at a possible second minimum in the confinement potential corresponding to supercritical confinement.

## Key claim

Gluon topology, which generates the large mass of the η' meson, may also be excited in nucleon resonances, potentially producing a narrow near-threshold resonance in η' photoproduction and explaining parity doublets in the higher-mass nucleon resonance spectrum through a second minimum in the confinement potential.

## Method

The paper reviews the role of non-perturbative gluon topology in QCD, focusing on its manifestation in the Witten-Veneziano mass formula for the η' and η mesons, and extends this to η' photoproduction data from ELSA, GRAAL, JLab, and MAMI. The author analyzes coupled-channels analyses of η' photoproduction cross sections and beam asymmetry measurements, and compares the observed parity doublets in excited nucleon resonances (N* and Δ*) with predictions from quark models and lattice QCD.

## Key numbers

- η' mass = 958 MeV, η mass = 547 MeV
- Singlet gluonic mass term: m̃_η0² = 0.73 GeV², giving η and η' masses correct to within 10% accuracy with mixing angle -20 degrees
- Yang-Mills topological susceptibility: χ^(1/4)(0)|_YM = 185.3 ± 5.6 MeV, close to the phenomenological value 180 MeV
- η'-nucleus optical potential at nuclear matter density: V(ρ=ρ₀) = -40 ± 6 ± 15 MeV, W(ρ=ρ₀) = -13 ± 3 ± 3 MeV
- Possible narrow resonance in η' photoproduction: mass 1900 ± 1 MeV (Bonn D₁₃(1900)) or 1902.6 ± 1 MeV (Mainz S₁₁(1900)), width < 3 MeV (Bonn) or 2.1 ± 0.5 MeV (Mainz)
- η' photoproduction threshold: M_η'p = 1896 MeV
- N*(1895) resonance: mass 1907 ± 10 MeV, width 100^(+40)_(-10) MeV, decay mode to η'p of 10-40%
- First parity doublet excited resonance appears ≈ 700 MeV above the proton mass

## Context

This work extends the understanding of gluon topology from its established role in the η' mass (Witten 1979, Veneziano 1979) to nucleon resonance phenomenology. The discussion of parity doublets builds on observations by Burkert (2022) and Anisovich (2011), and the possible second minimum in the confinement potential draws on Gribov's supercritical confinement scenario (Gribov 1986, 1999). The analysis enables future experimental searches for narrow η'-nucleon resonances at facilities like ELSA, GSI, and in Japan, and poses theoretical challenges for understanding confinement in Minkowski versus Euclidean space.
