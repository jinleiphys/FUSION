# Astrophysical Neutrino Reactions and Muon Capture in Deuterium

> We discuss the importance of precise study of muon capture in deuterium for correct understanding of some fundamental astrophysical processes

## Key claim

Precise measurement of muon capture in deuterium can determine the low-energy constant \(\hat d^R\) (or \(c_D\)) that appears in the space component of the weak axial meson-exchange currents, thereby enabling model-independent predictions for solar neutrino reactions on deuterium and the proton-proton fusion reaction, without the complications of three-nucleon systems.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework for weak nuclear reactions in few-nucleon systems, comparing three approaches: the Tree Approximation Approach (TAA) using one-boson-exchange currents and phenomenological potentials; hybrid calculations that combine chiral perturbation theory (\(\chi\)PT) currents with wave functions from TAA potentials; and fully consistent \(\chi\)PT calculations with chiral potentials up to N\(^3\)LO. They present new \(\chi\)PT calculations for the doublet muon capture rate in deuterium, \(\Lambda_{1/2}\), using the Entem-Machleidt N\(^3\)LO potential, the constant \(c_D = -0.2\) from Gazit et al. (2009), and the resulting \(\hat d^R = 2.400\), and compare these results with those of the Pisa group (MEAL).

## Key numbers

* Total solar neutrino flux at Earth surface: \(\approx 6.4 \times 10^{10} / \text{cm}^2 \text{s}\).
* Measured total flux of active-flavor neutrinos at SNO: \(\approx 5 \times 10^6 / \text{cm}^2 \text{s}\); electron neutrino flux: \(\approx 1.7 \times 10^6 / \text{cm}^2 \text{s}\).
* Neutrino oscillation parameters from combined solar and KamLAND data: \(\theta_{12} = 34.4^{+1.3}_{-1.2}\) degrees, \(\Delta m^2_{12} = 7.59^{+0.19}_{-0.21} \times 10^{-5} \text{ eV}^2\).
* Neutron lifetime (world average): \(879.9 \pm 0.9\) s.
* Muon capture on proton (singlet rate, MuCap): \(\Lambda_s = 725.0 \pm 17.4 \text{ s}^{-1}\).
* Induced pseudoscalar form factor from MuCap: \(g_P^{\text{exp}}(q^2 = 0.88 m_\mu^2) = 7.3 \pm 1.1\); \(\chi\)PT prediction: \(g_P^{\text{th}} = 8.2 \pm 0.2\).
* Triton half-life: \((fT_{1/2})_t = (1129.6 \pm 3)\) s.
* Muon capture on \(^3\)He (statistical rate): \(\Lambda_0 = 1496 \pm 4 \text{ s}^{-1}\).
* Previous measurements of doublet muon capture in deuterium: \(\Lambda_{1/2} = 470 \pm 29 \text{ s}^{-1}\) (M) and \(\Lambda_{1/2} = 409 \pm 40 \text{ s}^{-1}\) (CEAL).
* TAA calculations for \(\Lambda_{1/2}\): \(416 \pm 7 \text{ s}^{-1}\) (ATCS), \(397.8 - 399.6 \text{ s}^{-1}\) (TKK), \(416 \pm 6 \text{ s}^{-1}\) (RTMS); total MEC effect estimated as \(30.6 - 33.1 \text{ s}^{-1}\) (TKK).
* Hybrid calculation for \(\Lambda_{1/2}\) (APKM): \(386 \text{ s}^{-1}\).
* Hybrid calculation with AV18 + Urbana IX (Marcucci and Piarulli): \(\Lambda_{1/2} = 393.1 \pm 8 \text{ s}^{-1}\), \(\Lambda_0 = 1488 \pm 9 \text{ s}^{-1}\).
* Consistent \(\chi\)PT extraction of \(c_D\) and \(c_E\) (Gazit et al., 2009): \(-0.3 \le c_D \le -0.1\), \(-0.220 \le c_E \le -0.189\).
* Pisa group (MEAL) \(\chi\)PT results: \(\Lambda_{1/2} = (389.7 - 394.3) \text{ s}^{-1}\), \(\Lambda_0 = (1471 - 1497) \text{ s}^{-1}\).
* Authors' \(\chi\)PT calculation for \(\Lambda_{1/2}\) (RT): total \(414.1 \text{ s}^{-1}\), with partial wave contributions \(^1S_0 = 258.7 \text{ s}^{-1}\), \(^3P_0 = 19.6 \text{ s}^{-1}\), \(^3P_1 = 59.5 \text{ s}^{-1}\), \(^3P_2 = 69.7 \text{ s}^{-1}\), \(^1D_2 = 6.2 \text{ s}^{-1}\), \(^3F_2 = 0.4 \text{ s}^{-1}\); MEC effect \(\Delta\text{MEC} = 15.4 \text{ s}^{-1}\).
* MEAL \(\chi\)PT results (divided by 1.024 for radiative corrections): total \(384.3 \text{ s}^{-1}\), with \(^1S_0 = 244.6 \text{ s}^{-1}\), \(^3P_0 = 19.4 \text{ s}^{-1}\), \(^3P_1 = 45.3 \text{ s}^{-1}\), \(^3P_2 = 69.8 \text{ s}^{-1}\), \(^1D_2 = 4.3 \text{ s}^{-1}\), \(^3F_2 = 0.9 \text{ s}^{-1}\).

## Context

This work reviews and extends calculations of weak nuclear reactions in few-nucleon systems, focusing on muon capture in deuterium as a gateway to understanding solar neutrino reactions (neutral-current and charged-current on deuterium at SNO) and the proton-proton fusion reaction that powers the Sun. It contrasts the Tree Approximation Approach (TAA) of earlier authors (e.g., Adam et al., 1991; Truhlik et al., 2000) with modern chiral effective field theory (\(\chi\)PT) calculations by groups such as Park et al. (2003), Marcucci et al. (2011), and Gazit et al. (2009). The authors highlight that the MuSun experiment, planned to measure \(\Lambda_{1/2}\) with \(\sim 1.5\%\) accuracy, will resolve discrepancies between current calculations and enable consistent determination of the low-energy constant \(\hat d^R\) from a two-nucleon system, avoiding the complexity of three-nucleon forces. This constant also enters the radiative pion capture reaction \(\pi^- + d \rightarrow \gamma + 2n\), which is the best source of the neutron-neutron scattering length \(a_{nn}\).
