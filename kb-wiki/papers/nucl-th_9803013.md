# A model of short-range correlations in the charge response

> The validity of a model treating the short-range correlations up to the first order is studied by calculating the charge response of an infinite system and comparing the obtained results with those of a Fermi Hypernetted Chain calculation.

# A model of short-range correlations in the charge response

## Key claim

A model that truncates the Correlated Basis Function expansion to include only terms with a single Jastrow-type correlation line (first order in the correlation function h(r)) reproduces the Fermi Hypernetted Chain charge response functions extremely well for infinite nuclear matter, provided both two-point and three-point diagrams are included to preserve proper normalization of the many-body wave function.

## Method

The authors calculated the one-particle one-hole (1p-1h) charge response of infinite nuclear matter using a model that retains only diagrams containing a single Jastrow correlation line h(r)=f^2(r)-1, truncating the CBF expansion at first order in the correlation. They compared these results to full Fermi Hypernetted Chain (FHNC) calculations using the same scalar part of a state-dependent correlation fixed to minimize nuclear binding energy with the Urbana V14 nucleon-nucleon potential, for three values of momentum transfer at a Fermi momentum of 1.09 fm^{-1}.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum used: 1.09 fm^{-1}, chosen to describe quasi-elastic responses of ^{12}C
- Differences between the model and FHNC results are very small, shown multiplied by a factor of 10^5 in the comparison panel
- Three values of momentum transfer were used for the comparison of proton structure functions (exact values not specified in the truncated text)

## Context

This work extends the first-order correlation model previously applied to ground state charge density and momentum distributions of doubly closed shell nuclei by Amaro et al. (1995) to the calculation of excited state charge responses. It directly compares with FHNC calculations for nuclear matter by Fantoni et al. (1987). The results enable future application of this simplified model to two-nucleon emission calculations and to responses induced by other electromagnetic operators, while also suggesting that calculations including only two-point diagrams (such as those by Giusti et al. (1991) and Ryckebusch et al. (1995)) may overestimate correlation effects in 1p-1h responses.
