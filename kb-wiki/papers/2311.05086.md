# $ΔS=0$ Hadronic Parity Violation in Next-to-Leading Order QCD: Anomalous Dimension Matrices and Their Implications

> We construct the effective Hamiltonian for hadronic parity violation in strangeness-nonchanging ($ΔS=0$) processes in next-to-leading order (NLO) in QCD, for all isosectors, and at a renormalization scale of 2 GeV, thus extending our earlier leading-order (LO) analysis. Hadronic parity violation, studied in the context of the low-energy interactions of nucleons and nuclei, exposes the complex interplay of weak and strong interactions in these systems, and thus supports our extension to NLO. Here we exploit the flavor-blind nature of QCD interactions to construct the needed anomalous dimension matrices from those computed in flavor physics, which we then use to refine our effective Hamiltonian and finally our predicted parity-violating meson-nucleon coupling constants, to find improved agreement with few-body experiments.

## Key claim

The authors construct the effective Hamiltonian for hadronic parity violation in strangeness-nonchanging processes at next-to-leading order in QCD for all isosectors, finding improved agreement with few-body experimental data compared to their earlier leading-order analysis, though they note that a pure isospin-2 operator cannot be formed unless the Cabibbo angle is set to zero, meaning the existence of more than one generation of quarks precludes writing the effective Hamiltonian into components of purely definite isospin.

## Method

The authors exploit the flavor-blind nature of QCD interactions to adapt anomalous dimension matrices computed in flavor physics for strangeness-changing processes to the strangeness-conserving case, working in an explicit parity-violating basis of vector-times-axial-vector operators rather than a chiral basis. They construct the complete set of eight four-quark operators from Z0 exchange, compute the current-current and penguin corrections at two-loop order in the 't Hooft-Veltman scheme, and evolve the Wilson coefficients from the W mass scale to 2 GeV using renormalization group techniques at next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers

- The anomalous dimension matrices are computed at next-to-leading order, with the expansion γ(α_s) = (α_s/4π)γ^(0) + (α_s/4π)^2 γ^(1)
- The renormalization scale for the effective Hamiltonian is set to 2 GeV
- The number of dynamical quarks f varies as the energy scale changes, with β_0 = (33 - 2f)/3
- The evolution matrix at NLO takes the form U_f(μ, μ_W) = (1 + α_s(μ)/(4π) J) U_f^(0)(μ, μ_W) (1 - α_s(μ_W)/(4π) J)

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier leading-order analysis (Gardner 2022) of hadronic parity violation to next-to-leading order in QCD, building on prior NLO studies in flavor physics by Buras (1989, 1993) and Buchalla (1996). Previous work on the anomalous dimension matrix for hadronic parity violation had focused on the isovector sector in LO QCD (Dai 1991, Kaplan 1993) and in NLO QCD (Tiburzi), as well as the isotensor sector (Tiburzi), but this paper provides the complete analysis for all isosectors simultaneously. The refined effective Hamiltonian enables improved comparison with recent few-body experimental measurements of parity violation in n + p → d + γ (NPDGamma 2018) and n + ³He → p + ³H (n3He 2020), and supports future systematic assessment of low-energy couplings in chiral effective theory.
