# A deformed QRPA formalism for single and two-neutrino double beta decay

> We use a deformed QRPA formalism to describe simultaneously the energy distributions of the single beta Gamow-Teller strength and the two-neutrino double beta decay matrix elements. Calculations are performed in a series of double beta decay partners with A = 48, 76, 82, 96, 100, 116, 128, 130, 136 and 150, using deformed Woods-Saxon potentials and deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock mean fields. The formalism includes a quasiparticle deformed basis and residual spin-isospin forces in the particle-hole and particle-particle channels. We discuss the sensitivity of the parent and daughter Gamow-Teller strength distributions in single beta decay, as well as the sensitivity of the double beta decay matrix elements to the deformed mean field and to the residual interactions. Nuclear deformation is found to be a mechanism of suppression of the two-neutrino double beta decay. The double beta decay matrix elements are found to have maximum values for about equal deformations of parent and daughter nuclei. They decrease rapidly when differences in deformations increase. We remark the importance of a proper simultaneous description of both double beta decay and single Gamow-Teller strength distributions. Finally, we conclude that for further progress in the field it would be useful to improve and complete the experimental information on the studied Gamow-Teller strengths and nuclear deformations.

# A deformed QRPA formalism for single and two-neutrino double beta decay

## Key claim

Nuclear deformation is identified as a mechanism that suppresses two-neutrino double beta decay matrix elements, with the suppression arising primarily from differences in deformation between parent and daughter nuclei rather than from deformation itself. The matrix elements reach maximum values when parent and daughter have equal deformations and decrease rapidly as the deformation difference increases.

## Method

The authors employ a deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pnQRPA) formalism with an axially symmetric deformed basis, using either a phenomenological Woods-Saxon potential or a selfconsistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock (Sk3 force) mean field. Single-particle wave functions are expanded in eleven major shells of an axially symmetric harmonic oscillator, pairing correlations are treated in the BCS approximation with gap parameters from experimental odd-even mass differences, and residual spin-isospin forces are included in both the particle-hole and particle-particle channels. The formalism computes Gamow-Teller strength distributions for single beta decay branches and the two-neutrino double beta decay matrix elements through second-order perturbation theory, including overlap factors between intermediate nuclear states reached from initial and final nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Calculations performed for 10 double beta decay partner systems: A = 48, 76, 82, 96, 100, 116, 128, 130, 136, and 150.
- For the Woods-Saxon potential, the particle-hole coupling strength from Ref. Homma is given as χ^{ph}_{GT} = 5.2 / A^{0.7} MeV and the particle-particle strength as κ^{pp}_{GT} = 0.58 / A^{0.7} MeV.
- For the Skyrme HF calculations with Sk3, a particle-hole strength of χ^{ph}_{GT} = 0.1 MeV and particle-particle strength of κ^{pp}_{GT} = 6/A MeV reproduce both the measured GT resonances and the two-neutrino double beta decay matrix elements.
- The worst deviation in Q_{ββ} values between Sk3 calculations and experiment is 2.25 MeV for A=96, where Q_{ββ(exp)} = 3.35 MeV and Q_{ββ(Sk3)} = 5.59 MeV.
- For A=128, the Sk3 force gives Q_{ββ} = -0.10 MeV, which is negative and thus unphysical for the decay.
- The Ikeda sum rule values for A=150 are 3(N-Z) = 90 for ¹⁵⁰Nd and 78 for ¹⁵⁰Sm.
- The GT resonance in ⁴⁸Ca is reported at 10 MeV from experiment.
- Experimental matrix elements M^{2ν}_{GT} are extracted using g_A = 1.25 or g_A = 1.0 from the adopted experimental half-lives of Ref. Barabash.

## Context

This work extends earlier deformed QRPA calculations by Sarriguren et al. and by the same group on ⁷⁶Ge (DoubleGe) to all measured two-neutrino double beta decay emitters, simultaneously treating single beta Gamow-Teller strength distributions and double beta decay matrix elements. It builds on the spherical pnQRPA framework of Halbleib and Sorensen and the subsequent inclusion of particle-particle interactions by the Tuebingen group, and on the deformed Nilsson-based QRPA of Krumlinde and Moller. The study contradicts the implicit assumption in many spherical QRPA treatments that deformation effects can be ignored for nearly spherical nuclei, and it enables more reliable extraction of nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double beta decay searches by demonstrating that deformation differences between parent and daughter must be accurately known. The authors call for improved experimental data on Gamow-Teller strengths and nuclear deformations to further constrain the calculations.
