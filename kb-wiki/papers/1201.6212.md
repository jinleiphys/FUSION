# Quantum fermions and quantum field theory from classical statistics

> An Ising-type classical statistical ensemble can describe the quantum physics of fermions if one chooses a particular law for the time evolution of the probability distribution. It accounts for the time evolution of a quantum field theory for Dirac particles in an external electromagnetic field. This yields in the non-relativistic one-particle limit the Schrödinger equation for a quantum particle in a potential. Interference or tunneling arise from classical probabilities.

## Key claim

An Ising-type classical statistical ensemble with a specific linear evolution law for the probability distribution can reproduce the full quantum field theory of Dirac fermions in an external electromagnetic field, including the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation for a particle in a potential, without introducing any non-classical concepts beyond positive probabilities and their time evolution.

## Method

The author constructs a generalized Ising model with discrete occupation numbers (bits) on a lattice, defines a real "classical wave function" as the signed square root of the probability distribution, and specifies its time evolution via a rotation matrix that preserves normalization. The evolution law is derived from a Grassmann functional integral action for massless Majorana fermions, which is then extended to include mass terms and electromagnetic coupling, yielding a Grassmann evolution generator that acts on the wave function. The dynamics for one-particle states is extracted by restricting to eigenstates of the conserved particle number operator.

## Key numbers

- For Majorana fermions, the model uses \(N_s = 4\) Ising spin species per lattice site; for Dirac fermions, \(N_s = 8\).
- The lattice spacing is \(2\Delta\) with \(\epsilon = \Delta\) for the hypercubic bcc lattice; the continuum limit is \(\Delta \to 0\).
- The action for massless Majorana fermions in the continuum limit is \(S = \int_{t,x} \big\{ \psi_\gamma \partial_t \psi_\gamma - \psi_\gamma (T_k)_{\gamma\delta} \partial_k \psi_\delta \big\}\).
- The Dirac matrices \(\gamma^\mu\) are real \(4 \times 4\) matrices obeying \(\{\gamma^\mu, \gamma^\nu\} = 2\eta^{\mu\nu}\) with metric signature \((-1,1,1,1)\).

## Context

This work extends the author's prior series of papers (Wetterich, 2010-2012) on deriving quantum mechanics from classical statistics, specifically building on the "classical wave function" concept and Grassmann formulation developed in those works. It provides a unified framework where quantum features such as interference, tunneling, and fermionic statistics emerge from a classical probability evolution, and it enables the description of decoherence and "zwitter"-particles interpolating between quantum and classical behavior within a single conceptual scheme.
