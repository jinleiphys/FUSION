# Neutrino-nucleon scattering rate in the relativistic random phase approximation

## Key claim
The in-medium modification to the neutrino-nucleon scattering rate is calculated using the relativistic random phase approximation (RPA) within a hadronic meson exchange model, but the reduction factor depends on the nuclear interaction parametrization: parameter sets yielding a lower effective nucleon mass at saturation produce stronger reduction factors than previously reported.

## Method
The neutrino-nucleon scattering rate is computed from the imaginary part of the lepton current contracted with the nucleon structure function, which is proportional to the polarization tensor. The mean-field polarization is replaced by the solution of the Dyson equation to incorporate RPA correlations, using the NL3 and TM1 parametrizations of the nuclear interaction (a relativistic field model with sigma, omega, and rho meson exchange including non-linear meson-meson interactions). The calculation is performed in asymmetric nuclear matter with a proton fraction determined by beta equilibrium, with neutrinos trapped inside the star (finite neutrino chemical potential).

## Key numbers
- The total neutrino-neutron scattering cross section is reduced by RPA correlations at high density by a factor of 20% to 25% for the NL3 and TM1 parameter sets.
- The effective nucleon mass at saturation is 0.63 for TM1 and 0.6 for NL3, compared to 0.8 in the prior parametrization used in Mornas and Pandharipande (2002).
- The transverse contribution provides about 60% of the total cross section result.
- The calculation uses a typical lepton fraction of Y_L = 0.4.
- The transverse response function R_2 is shown at twice saturation density, temperature T = 30 MeV, and fixed momentum transfer k = 100 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the results of Mornas and Pandharipande (2002) by applying the relativistic RPA formalism to the more commonly used NL3 and TM1 parametrizations of nuclear interactions, which are fitted to properties of nuclei and bulk nuclear matter. The longitudinal contribution to the cross section is reduced approximately by a factor of two by RPA correlations, while the transverse contribution is only moderately modified. These calculations are relevant for neutrino transport in supernova collapse simulations and protoneutron star cooling, where the neutrino mean free path is determined mostly by interactions with baryons in dense and hot nuclear matter.
