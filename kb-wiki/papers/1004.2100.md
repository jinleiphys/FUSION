# Exploring the Nucleon Structure from First Principles of QCD

> Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) is generally assumed to be the fundamental theory underlying nuclear physics. In recent years there is progress towards investigating the nucleon structure from first principles of QCD. Although this structure is best revealed in Deep Inelastic Scattering, a consistent analysis has to be performed in a fully non-perturbative scheme. The only known method for this purpose are lattice simulations. We first sketch the ideas of Monte Carlo simulations in lattice gauge theory. Then we comment in particular on the issues of chiral symmetry and operator mixing. Finally we present our results for the Bjorken variable of a single quark, and for the second Nachtmann moment of the nucleon structure functions.

# Exploring the Nucleon Structure from First Principles of QCD

## Key claim
Lattice QCD with Ginsparg-Wilson fermions can determine nucleon structure functions non-perturbatively, but the results for the Bjorken variable ⟨x⟩ depend on the photon momentum and have statistical errors of 5–9, so the method is demonstrated as feasible rather than yielding a single definitive value.

## Method
The QCDSF Collaboration performed lattice QCD simulations with two flavors of degenerate overlap valence quarks at masses 29 MeV and 73 MeV (corresponding to pion masses 280 MeV and 440 MeV). They computed the product of electromagnetic currents between quark states of low momentum p, decomposed it via the operator product expansion (OPE) truncated at O(|p|³), and solved for the Wilson coefficients using singular value decomposition. They then extracted the Bjorken variable ⟨x⟩ from the spin-2 part of the hadronic tensor and the second Nachtmann moment of the nucleon structure functions by integrating the hadronic tensor over the solid angle.

## Key numbers
- Quark masses used: 29 MeV and 73 MeV, corresponding to pion masses of 280 MeV and 440 MeV.
- Three photon momenta probed: |q⁽¹⁾| = 2.2 GeV, |q⁽²⁾| = 3.3 GeV, |q⁽³⁾| = 4.4 GeV.
- Bjorken variable ⟨x⟩ for one unpolarised quark at m_q = 73 MeV: 0.44(9) at q⁽¹⁾, 0.73(5) at q⁽²⁾, 0.60(5) at q⁽³⁾.
- The OPE truncation included 1360 operators, which reduced to 67 equivalence classes for photon momenta q ∝ (1,1,1,1).

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD beyond the hadron spectrum (which can now be computed to a few percent precision) into the non-perturbative study of nucleon structure functions, which are phenomenologically probed in Deep Inelastic Scattering. The use of Ginsparg-Wilson fermions (overlap Dirac operator) preserves a lattice-modified chiral symmetry and prevents uncontrolled operator mixing, overcoming the additive mass renormalisation problem of Wilson fermions. The results demonstrate that explicit determination of structure functions directly from QCD is feasible, enabling future calculations at lighter quark masses and larger volumes to reach the physical pion mass.
