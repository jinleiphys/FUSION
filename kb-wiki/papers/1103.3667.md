# Transverse Beam Spin Asymmetries at Backward Angles in Elastic Electron-Proton and Quasi-elastic Electron-Deuteron Scattering

> We have measured the beam-normal single-spin asymmetries in elastic scattering of transversely polarized electrons from the proton, and performed the first measurement in quasi-elastic scattering on the deuteron, at backward angles (lab scattering angle of 108 degrees) for Q2 = 0.22 GeV^2/c^2 and 0.63 GeV^2/c^2 at beam energies of 362 MeV and 687 MeV, respectively. The asymmetry arises due to the imaginary part of the interference of the two-photon exchange amplitude with that of single photon exchange. Results for the proton are consistent with a model calculation which includes inelastic intermediate hadronic (piN) states. An estimate of the beam-normal single-spin asymmetry for the scattering from the neutron is made using a quasi-static deuterium approximation, and is also in agreement with theory.

## Key claim

The beam-normal single-spin asymmetry (BNSSA) in elastic electron-proton scattering at backward angles is consistent with a model calculation that includes inelastic intermediate hadronic (piN) states, and the first measurement of the BNSSA in quasi-elastic electron-deuteron scattering provides an estimate for the neutron asymmetry that also agrees with theory, but only at the lower energy of 362 MeV where the uncertainties are small enough to draw a conclusion.

## Method

The G0 Collaboration measured the BNSSA in elastic electron-proton and quasi-elastic electron-deuteron scattering at a fixed laboratory scattering angle of 108 degrees using a toroidal-field magnetic spectrometer with eight-fold azimuthal symmetry. Transversely polarized electrons at beam energies of 362 MeV and 687 MeV (corresponding to Q2 = 0.22 GeV2/c2 and 0.63 GeV2/c2, respectively) were scattered from liquid hydrogen and deuterium targets, and the asymmetry was extracted by fitting the octant-dependent measured asymmetry as a sinusoidal function of the azimuthal scattering angle phi. The neutron BNSSA was then estimated from the deuterium data using a quasi-static approximation with the cross-section-weighted average of proton and neutron asymmetries.

## Key numbers

*   Proton BNSSA at 362 MeV and Q2 = 0.22 GeV2/c2: Bnp = -176.5 +/- 9.4 ppm.
*   Deuteron BNSSA at 362 MeV and Q2 = 0.22 GeV2/c2: Bnd = -108.6 +/- 7.2 ppm.
*   Proton BNSSA at 687 MeV and Q2 = 0.63 GeV2/c2: Bnp = -21.0 +/- 24 ppm.
*   Deuteron BNSSA at 687 MeV and Q2 = 0.63 GeV2/c2: Bnd = -55.7 +/- 78 ppm.
*   Extracted neutron BNSSA at 362 MeV: Bnn = 86.6 +/- 41 ppm, compared to a theoretical prediction of 72 ppm.
*   Beam polarization magnitude was 85.8% with uncertainties of +/-2.0% at 362 MeV and +/-1.4% at 687 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the world dataset for BNSSA in elastic electron-proton scattering at backward angles, complementing previous measurements by the PVA4 collaboration (preliminary) and the SAMPLE collaboration (2000). The results provide the first estimate of the BNSSA for the neutron using quasi-elastic deuteron scattering, and the agreement with theory at 362 MeV demonstrates the necessity of including piN intermediate state contributions in two-photon exchange calculations, which are important for understanding radiative corrections in precision electron scattering experiments.
