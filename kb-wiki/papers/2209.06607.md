# Constrained instanton approximation of Skyrmions with massive pions

> We present the idea of using the holonomy along a line of a constrained instanton solution (an approximate solution of the Euclidean equations of motion subject to a constraint) of an $\rm SU(2)$ Yang-Mills-Higgs theory to approximate the Skyrmion solution in a chiral model with massive pions. The fact that the gauge field acquires a nonzero mass due to the Higgs mechanism implies that a constrained instanton decays exponentially far from its center, and so does the Skyrmion configuration that it generates via the Atiyah-Manton construction. This is precisely the desired behavior at large distances for the true Skyrmion solutions when the pion mass is included.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the holonomy of a constrained instanton (an approximate solution of the SU(2) Yang-Mills-Higgs equations subject to a constraint that fixes the instanton size) can generate Skyrmion configurations for the chiral model with massive pions, where the standard Atiyah-Manton construction using pure Yang-Mills instantons fails because it produces configurations with infinite energy.

## Method

The authors use the Atiyah-Manton construction, which obtains a Skyrme field as the holonomy of a gauge field along the Euclidean time direction, but replace the pure Yang-Mills instanton with a constrained instanton from an SU(2) Yang-Mills-Higgs theory in the spontaneously broken phase. They consider two approaches: a perturbative construction of the constrained instanton profile up to order mu^4 following Affleck (1981) and Nielsen (1999), and a non-perturbative ansatz proposed by Wang (1994) that satisfies the correct boundary conditions at both short and large distances. The gauge field acquires a mass m through the Higgs mechanism, causing exponential decay of the instanton configuration far from its center, which matches the required asymptotic behavior of Skyrmions with massive pions.

## Key numbers

- For the massless case (m=0), the instanton-generated Skyrmion has energy E = 1.243 × 12π^2, which is 1% above the true Skyrmion solution energy of 1.232 × 12π^2
- For m=0.5, the constrained instanton approximation gives E = 1.438 × 12π^2 (perturbative) and E = 1.435 × 12π^2 (non-perturbative), compared to the true solution energy of 1.433 × 12π^2
- For m=3, the constrained instanton approximation gives E = 2.250 × 12π^2 (perturbative) and E = 2.224 × 12π^2 (non-perturbative), compared to the true solution energy of 2.170 × 12π^2
- The optimal scale parameter for m=0.5 is λ^2 = 1.47
- For the massless case, the optimal scale parameter is λ^2 = 2.11

## Context

This work extends the Atiyah-Manton construction (Atiyah and Manton, 1989), which successfully approximates massless pion Skyrmions using pure Yang-Mills instantons, to the massive pion case where the standard instanton approximation fails due to insufficiently fast polynomial decay. The constrained instanton approach builds on perturbative methods developed by Affleck (1981) and Nielsen (1999) for Yang-Mills-Higgs theories, and uses a non-perturbative ansatz from Wang (1994). The paper suggests that this method could be extended to higher baryon number Skyrmions and Skyrmion crystals using a modified 't Hooft ansatz, and could enable studies of the effect of nonzero pion mass on Skyrmion-Skyrmion interactions.
