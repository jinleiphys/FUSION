# Color singlet suppression of quark-gluon plasma formation

## Key claim

Thermal nucleation of quark-gluon plasma droplets in superheated hadronic matter is suppressed by many orders of magnitude when the requirements of color singletness and fixed momentum are included, making such nucleation unlikely in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions if the phase transition is first order and reasonably described by the MIT bag model.

## Method

The authors calculated the nucleation rate of quark-gluon plasma droplets within the MIT bag model using homogeneous nucleation theory, incorporating the color singlet and fixed-momentum grand canonical partition function derived by Elze and Greiner (1986). The grand potential was computed as a function of droplet radius for two flavors of massless quarks and gluons, including volume and curvature energy terms but no surface tension, with the free energy barrier height taken directly from the grand potential to estimate the nucleation rate via the exponential factor exp(-ΔF/T).

## Key numbers

- The color singlet and momentum constraints suppress the nucleation rate by 4–5 orders of magnitude at extreme superheating, and much more at moderate superheating, for a bag constant B^{1/4} = 200 MeV and zero quark chemical potential.
- Typical volumes and time scales relevant for ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions are about 100 fm³·fm/c, while the nucleation rate prefactor is approximately 0.2 fm⁻³ (10⁻²³ s)⁻¹ T₁₀₀⁴, where T₁₀₀ is temperature in units of 100 MeV.
- For superheating beyond 20 MeV at μ=0, critical bubbles have RT ≈ 0.9, where the saddle-point approximation used in the partition function is expected to be good to about 30% for RT=1 and a few percent for RT≈2.
- The effect of hadron pressure on the scaling of B^{1/4} is less than 2% for temperatures near the phase transition (T ≈ 0.7 B^{1/4}) at μ=0.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of quark-gluon plasma droplet nucleation by Kapusta, Vischer, and Venugopalan (1995) that neglected color singlet and fixed-momentum constraints, and uses the partition function formalism of Elze and Greiner (1986). The results contradict the common assumption that moderate superheating is sufficient for droplet nucleation, and imply that other mechanisms beyond thermal nucleation (such as nucleation due to impurities) are needed to form quark-gluon plasma in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions if the phase transition is first order. The authors note that the ingredients of their calculation have been available in the literature for about a decade but no one had previously checked these dramatic consequences explicitly.
