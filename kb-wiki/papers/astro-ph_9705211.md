# Quantum Nucleation of Two-Flavor Quark Matter in Neutron Stars

> Rates for nucleation of two-flavor quark matter in a neutron star core, originally composed of nuclear matter in $β$ equilibrium, are calculated at zero temperature by a quantum tunneling analysis incorporating the electrostatic energy. We find that a nucleated droplet would develop into bulk matter due to electron screening effects.

## Key claim

The paper calculates that a droplet of two-flavor (up and down) quark matter nucleated in a neutron star core at zero temperature will develop into bulk matter rather than remain finite, but only because electron screening effects prevent the electrostatic energy from dominating the droplet potential for radii up to about 10 fm.

## Method

The authors model a spherical droplet of two-flavor quark matter forming via quantum tunneling in β-equilibrium nuclear matter, using a semiclassical Lagrangian with an effective droplet mass and a potential that includes bulk, surface, and electrostatic terms. The electrostatic energy is computed within the linear Thomas-Fermi (TF) screening approximation for electrons, with the electron distribution determined by solving the Poisson equation for the excess droplet charge. The nucleation time τ is obtained via the WKB approximation from the underbarrier action and the oscillation frequency of the zeroth bound state.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Bag constant: B = 120 MeV fm⁻³; QCD fine structure constant: αₛ = 0.4
- Transition pressure P₀ ≅ 445 MeV fm⁻³ for the two-flavor quark matter phase boundary
- TF screening length for electrons: ∼ 6 fm (since μₑ ∼ 300 MeV); proton TF screening length: ≳ 10 fm
- Surface tension σ estimated as ∼ 10 MeV fm⁻² from a Fermi-gas model (with μᵤ ∼ 500 MeV, μ_d ∼ 600 MeV, mᵤ ∼ m_d ∼ 10 MeV)
- Nucleation time τ calculated for σ = 5, 10, 15, and 20 MeV fm⁻² shows exponential pressure dependence; oscillation time scale ν₀⁻¹ ∼ 10⁻²³ sec; barrier height ∼ 100 MeV
- For R ≲ 10 fm, eφ/μₑ,init ≲ 0.1, validating the linear TF approximation
- Including muons raises P₀ to ≈ 465 MeV fm⁻³ and shortens the TF screening length by ∼ 1 fm

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Glendenning (1992) and Heiselberg et al. (1993), who proposed that a mixed phase of quark and nuclear matter could exhibit spatial structure due to surface and Coulomb effects, by examining whether such a mixed phase can actually nucleate dynamically. The authors find that electron screening causes any nucleated droplet to expand into bulk matter, making the mixed phase unlikely; the resulting bulk quark matter could convert to three-flavor quark matter via weak interactions in ∼ 10⁻⁹ sec, releasing ∼ 100 MeV per baryon as thermal energy (∼ 10⁵² ergs), which might be a possible origin of γ-ray bursts.
