# Electric-field driven nonequilibrium phase transitions in AdS/CFT

> We study phase transitions and critical phenomena in nonequilibrium steady states controlled by an electric field. We employ the D3/D7 model in the presence of a charge density and electric field at finite temperatures. The system undergoes the first-order and the second-order phase transitions under the variation of the electric field in the presence of dissipation. We numerically find that the critical exponents which we define for the nonequilibrium phase transition in this model take the mean-field values.

## Key claim

The authors numerically find that the static critical exponents (β, δ, γ) for the electric-field-driven nonequilibrium phase transition in the D3/D7 model take the mean-field values, but this result is obtained only for the specific definitions of the order parameter (chiral condensate or conductivity) and for the particular paths on the three-dimensional phase diagram that are examined.

## Method

The authors employ the D3/D7 model in the AdS/CFT correspondence, which is dual to N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory with N=2 hypermultiplets, and study the D7-brane dynamics via the Dirac-Born-Infeld action in an AdS-Schwarzschild black hole background. They numerically solve the equation of motion for the brane embedding angle θ(u) with fixed temperature T, charge density ρ, and electric field E, imposing boundary conditions at the effective horizon, and extract the current density J, chiral condensate, and conductivity from the asymptotic solutions near the AdS boundary.

## Key numbers

- The critical exponent β is approximately 0.495 (conductivity as order parameter, κ=T fixed) and 0.503 (chiral condensate as order parameter, κ=T fixed), both consistent with the Landau theory value β=1/2.
- The critical exponent δ is approximately 3 (1/δ ≈ 0.337 for conductivity, 1/δ ≈ 0.343 for chiral condensate), consistent with the Landau theory value δ=3.
- The critical exponent γ in the first-order phase transition region is approximately 1.020 (conductivity susceptibility, κ=T) and 0.986 (chiral condensate susceptibility, κ=ρ), consistent with the Landau theory value γ=1.
- The critical line approaches E_crit/m^2 ≈ 0.57 in the limit T→0, where E_crit is the value where the D7-brane barely touches the effective horizon.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of current-driven nonequilibrium phase transitions in the D3/D7 model by Nakamura (2012) and Matsumoto (2018) to the electric-field-driven case, and is motivated by experimental observations of electric-field-driven transitions in the organic conductor θ-(BEDT-TTF)_2CsCo(SCN)_4 by Sawano (2005). The results enable future investigation of whether the mean-field critical exponents found here are universal for nonequilibrium phase transitions in strongly coupled systems, and whether the conductivity can serve as an experimentally accessible order parameter for such transitions.
