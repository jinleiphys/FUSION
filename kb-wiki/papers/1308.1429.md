# Subtraction method and stability condition in the extended RPA theories

> The extended RPA theories are analyzed from the point of view of the problem of stability of their solutions. Three kinds of such theories are considered: the second RPA and two versions of the quasiparticle-phonon coupling model within the time-blocking approximation: the model including 1p1h*phonon configurations and the two-phonon model. It is shown that stability is ensured by making use of the subtraction method proposed previously to solve double counting problem in these theories. This enables one to generalize the famous Thouless theorem proved in the case of the RPA. These results are illustrated by an example of schematic model.

## Key claim

The stability of solutions in extended RPA theories (second RPA, TBA1, and TBA2) is guaranteed only when the subtraction method (setting the parameter κ = 1) is used to eliminate double counting; without subtraction (κ = 0), the positive semidefiniteness of the stability matrix and thus the reality of excitation energies are not ensured.

## Method

The paper analyzes three extended RPA models: the second RPA (SRPA) and two versions of the quasiparticle-phonon coupling model within the time-blocking approximation (TBA1, including 1p1h⊗phonon configurations, and TBA2, the two-phonon model). The eigenvalue equations for these models are recast into a linear form in an extended configuration space that includes both 1p1h and complex (2p2h) configurations. The stability matrix in the extended space is constructed, and its positive semidefiniteness is examined as a function of the subtraction parameter κ, with the condition κ ≥ 1 required for stability when the RPA stability matrix is positive semidefinite and the complex-configuration stability matrix is positive definite.

## Key numbers

- In SRPA calculations of giant resonances in ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, and ⁹⁰Zr, downward shifts of centroids relative to RPA values can be up to 10 MeV or more when the configuration space is sufficiently large.
- The condition for stability in the extended RPA is that the subtraction parameter κ must satisfy κ ≥ 1; the subtraction method corresponds to κ = 1.
- The complex-configuration energies Ω_c in the TBA2 are defined as ω_n + ω_{n'}, where both phonon energies ω_n and ω_{n'} are positive.

## Context

This work extends the Thouless theorem (Thouless, 1960, 1961), which established the stability condition for the self-consistent RPA, to the case of extended RPA theories. The subtraction method was previously proposed to solve the double counting problem in the quasiparticle time-blocking approximation (QTBA) and its relativistic generalization (RTBA, RQTBA), but its role in ensuring stability had not been analyzed. The results enable the application of extended RPA theories with guaranteed real excitation energies and non-negative transition probabilities, addressing stability issues reported in SRPA calculations by Papakonstantinou and Roth (2009, 2010) and others.
