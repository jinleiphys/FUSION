# Two-body double pole and three-body bound states: physical and unphysical quark masses

> We solve the Faddeev bound-state equations for three particles with simple two-body nonlocal, separable potentials that yield a scattering length twice as large as a positive effective range, as indicated by some lattice QCD simulations. Neglecting shape parameters, the two-body bound state is a double pole. For bosons we obtain a correlation between three- and two-body energies. For nucleons, this correlation depends additionally on the ratio of effective ranges in the two two-body $S$-wave channels. When this ratio takes the value suggested by lattice QCD, our three-body energy agrees well with a direct lattice determination. When this ratio takes the experimental value, we find a three-body bound state with energy close to that of the physical triton. We suggest that results could be improved systematically with distorted-wave perturbation theory around a separable potential whose form factor is an inverse square root of momentum squared.

# Two-body double pole and three-body bound states: physical and unphysical quark masses

## Key claim
When the two-body scattering length is exactly twice the positive effective range (as suggested by some lattice QCD simulations), the two-body bound state becomes a double pole, and the leading-order three-body binding energy for spinless bosons becomes a universal number independent of potential details; for nucleons, the three-body energy depends only on the ratio of effective ranges in the two S-wave channels, and when this ratio takes the lattice QCD value the predicted three-body energy agrees with direct lattice determinations, while at the experimental ratio the result is close to the physical triton energy.

## Method
The authors solve the Faddeev bound-state equations for three particles using simple two-body nonlocal, separable potentials with a form factor that is an inverse square root of momentum squared (square-root potential), which yields the effective-range expansion truncated at the effective range. They also consider Yamaguchi and Gaussian separable potentials to gauge sensitivity to higher effective-range parameters. For three nucleons in the triton channel (total isospin 1/2, total spin 1/2), they solve coupled integral equations for two profile functions corresponding to spin-singlet and spin-triplet two-nucleon channels, each with its own scattering length and effective range.

## Key numbers
- For spinless bosons with the square-root potential at a2=2r2, the ratio of three-body to two-body binding energy per particle is varsigma3 = 3.73; for the Yamaguchi potential it is 3.80; for the Gaussian potential it is 3.54.
- At unphysical pion mass m_pi = 806 MeV, the lattice QCD two-nucleon ERE parameters are: a21 = 1.82 fm, r21 = 0.906 fm; a20 = 2.33 fm, r20 = 1.130 fm.
- The two-nucleon binding energies predicted from these ERE parameters are: B21 = 25.3 MeV (square-root), 19.5 MeV (Yamaguchi), 18.4 MeV (Gaussian); B20 = 12.7 MeV (square-root), 11.1 MeV (Yamaguchi), 10.7 MeV (Gaussian). The direct LQCD values are B21 = 19.5 MeV and B20 = 15.9 MeV.
- The three-body binding energy from the Gaussian potential at the lattice QCD parameters agrees well with the direct LQCD determination; central values for all three potentials are extremely close and within the LQCD range.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of two-nucleon systems at unphysical quark masses from lattice QCD calculations by NPLQCD (Beane 2006, NPLQCD 2012, NPLQCD 2013, Wagman 2017), which found a2/r2 approximately 2 in both S-wave channels. It contrasts with the standard Pionless EFT power counting (van Kolck 1997, Kaplan 1998, Bedaque 1998, Bedaque 1999), which requires a three-body force at leading order for renormalization; here no three-body force is needed because the double-pole potential has sufficiently soft ultraviolet behavior. The approach is related to the separable potential method of Yamaguchi (1954) and Sitenko and Kharchenko (1963), and the square-root form factor was previously used by Gourdin (1957) and Bander (1964). The results suggest that distorted-wave perturbation theory around the square-root potential could systematically improve predictions, and that the same framework applied at physical pion mass yields a triton binding energy close to experiment.
