# Lattice QCD, Chiral Perturbation Theory and Hadron Phenomenology

> These are the proceedings of the workshop on ``Lattice QCD, Chiral Perturbation Theory and Hadron Phenomenology'' held at the European Centre for Theoretical Studies in Nuclear Physics and Related Areas from October 2 to 6, 2006. The workshop concentrated on bringing together researchers working in lattice QCD and chiral perturbation theory with the aim of improving our understanding how hadron properties can be calculated and analyzed from first principles. Included are a short contribution per talk.

## Key claim
This is a workshop proceedings volume, not a research paper presenting a single new result. The workshop aimed to bring together practitioners of lattice QCD and chiral perturbation theory to improve mutual understanding of how hadron properties can be calculated from first principles, with the explicit recognition that the same theorists generally do not pursue both aspects due to the demanding and distinct technologies involved.

## Method
The workshop was held at the European Centre for Theoretical Studies in Nuclear Physics and Related Areas (ECT*) in Trento, Italy from October 2 to 6, 2006, with 36 participants. 35 talks were presented covering lattice QCD simulations, chiral perturbation theory applications, and hadron phenomenology, with ample time for discussions and a special discussion session titled "Lattice meets chiral perturbation theory" convened by Hartmut Wittig. The proceedings contain a short contribution per talk rather than full-length papers.

## Key numbers
- Pseudo-scalar masses below 300 MeV were obtained on volumes with spatial size larger than 2 fm at lattice spacing values similar or smaller than 0.1 fm (Scorzato/ETM Collaboration).
- Pion masses down to approximately 300 MeV were achieved using N_f=2 nonperturbatively O(a) improved Wilson fermions (Schierholz/QCDSF Collaboration).
- The ratio f_{K+}/f_{pi+} was found to be 1.24 [1.22 using r_0=0.467 fm] compared to the experimental value 1.223 (Schierholz/QCDSF Collaboration).
- The pion decay constant f_{pi+} was determined as 76(4)(2) MeV [81(4)(2) MeV using r_0=0.467 fm] (Schierholz/QCDSF Collaboration).
- Excited meson and baryon masses were computed from a quenched calculation with Chirally Improved quarks at pion masses down to 350 MeV (Lang/BGR Collaboration).
- For pion masses above 400 MeV, numerical results from 2+1 flavor dynamical domain wall fermion simulations were not consistent with NLO chiral perturbation theory; consistency was found for pion masses below 400 MeV (Lin/RBC and UKQCD Collaborations).
- For the rho meson chiral limit mass, the analysis found 650 MeV <= M_rho^0 <= 800 MeV (Meissner, analyzing CP-PACS data).
- In baryon chiral perturbation theory, the power counting was shown to be no longer valid for pion masses M > 550 MeV (Schindler et al.).

## Context
This workshop proceedings documents the state of the field in 2006, when the first full QCD calculations in the chiral regime were becoming available. It extends the ongoing dialogue between lattice practitioners using various fermion formulations (twisted mass, domain wall, staggered, Wilson, overlap) and chiral perturbation theorists developing extrapolation tools. The meeting enabled cross-community discussion of issues such as finite volume effects, lattice artifacts, and the range of validity of chiral expansions, setting the stage for future combined analyses of lattice data with chiral effective field theory.
