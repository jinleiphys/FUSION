# Microscopic calculation of the interaction cross section for stable and unstable nuclei based on the nonrelativistic nucleon-nucleon t-matrix

> Fully quantal calculations of the total reaction cross sections \sigR and interaction cross sections \sigI, induced by stable and unstable He, Li, C and O isotopes on $^{12}$C target at $E_{\rm lab}\approx 0.8$ and 1 GeV/nucleon have been performed, for the first time, in the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) using the microscopic \emph{complex} optical potential and inelastic form factors given by the folding model. Realistic nuclear densities for the projectiles and $^{12}$C target as well as the complex $t$-matrix parameterization of free nucleon-nucleon interaction by Franey and Love were used as inputs of the folding calculation. Our \emph{parameter-free} folding + DWIA approach has been shown to give a very good account (within 1--2%) of the experimental \sigI measured at these energies for the stable, strongly bound isotopes. With the antisymmetrization of the dinuclear system properly taken into account, this microscopic approach is shown to be more accurate than the simple optical limit of Glauber model that was widely used to infer the nuclear radii from the measured \sigI. Therefore, the results obtained for the nuclear radii of neutron-rich isotopes under study can be of interest for further nuclear structure studies.

# Microscopic calculation of the interaction cross section for stable and unstable nuclei based on the nonrelativistic nucleon-nucleon t-matrix

## Key claim
The parameter-free folding plus distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) approach, using the complex t-matrix parameterization of the free nucleon-nucleon interaction by Franey and Love (1985), reproduces experimental interaction cross sections for stable, strongly bound isotopes within 1-2%, but the accuracy for neutron-rich halo nuclei is limited by the neglect of dynamic few-body correlations between core and valence nucleons, which the authors identify as the main limitation of the approach.

## Method
The authors performed fully quantal calculations of total reaction cross sections and interaction cross sections for stable and unstable He, Li, C, and O isotopes on a 12C target at bombarding energies around 0.8 and 1 GeV/nucleon using the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA). They constructed microscopic complex optical potentials and inelastic form factors via a double-folding model using realistic nuclear densities for projectiles and target, together with the nonrelativistic t-matrix parameterization of the free nucleon-nucleon interaction by Franey and Love (1985), with antisymmetrization of the dinuclear system treated through the Fock exchange term. The interaction cross section was obtained by subtracting from the total reaction cross section the DWIA-calculated cross sections for inelastic scattering to the first excited 2+ and 3- states of the 12C target.

## Key numbers
- For stable 4He on 12C at 790 MeV/nucleon: calculated interaction cross section = 504 mb versus experimental 503 ± 5 mb (0.2% difference) using a harmonic oscillator density with RMS radius 1.461 fm
- For stable 12C on 12C at 950 MeV/nucleon: calculated interaction cross section = 844 mb versus experimental 853 ± 6 mb (1.1% difference) using a Fermi distribution density with RMS radius 2.332 fm
- For stable 16O on 12C at 970 MeV/nucleon: calculated interaction cross section = 981 mb versus experimental 982 ± 6 mb (0.1% difference) using a Fermi distribution density with RMS radius 2.618 fm
- For 6He on 12C at 790 MeV/nucleon: calculated interaction cross section = 722 mb versus experimental 722 ± 6 mb (0.0% difference) using an independent particle model density with RMS radius 2.460 fm
- For 11Li on 12C at 790 MeV/nucleon: calculated interaction cross section = 1061 mb versus experimental 1060 ± 10 mb (0.1% difference) using a harmonic oscillator plus halo density with RMS radius 3.227 fm
- For 8He on 12C at 790 MeV/nucleon: calculated interaction cross section = 812 mb versus experimental 817 ± 6 mb (0.6% difference) using a COSMA density with RMS radius 2.526 fm
- The exchange term contribution to the total reaction cross section for 6He+12C at 790 MeV/nucleon is about 3%, corresponding to a difference of up to 7% in extracted nuclear RMS radii

## Context
This work extends the folding model approach, previously applied successfully at low energies using density-dependent M3Y interactions (Khoa 1997, 2000), to high energies around 1 GeV/nucleon where the impulse approximation becomes valid. It provides a more accurate alternative to the optical limit of the Glauber model (Glauber 1970, Ozawa 2001) by properly treating finite-range effects, the Fock exchange term, and quantal scattering solutions rather than eikonal approximation. The results enable more reliable extraction of nuclear radii for neutron-rich isotopes from measured interaction cross sections, though the approach remains limited for halo nuclei where explicit treatment of few-body correlations (as in Ogawa 1992, Al-Khalili 1996, Tostevin 1998) is needed.
