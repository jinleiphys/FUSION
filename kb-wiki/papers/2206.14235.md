# Isospin-symmetry implications for nuclear two-body distributions and short-range correlations

> We study the implications of isospin symmetry in nuclear systems on two-body distributions and derive relations between different densities or momentum distributions of a given nucleus or of different nuclei in the same isospin multiplet. A connection between neutrinoless double beta decay transition of mirror nuclei and two-body densities is also obtained. The relations are numerically verified by means of quantum Monte Carlo calculations. We discuss the relevance of these results to the analysis of nuclear short-range correlations and also show that isospin symmetry allows us to extract information about the spectator nucleons when a short-range correlated pair is formed.

## Key claim

Isospin symmetry imposes exact linear relations among the two-body densities (and momentum distributions) of a given nucleus or of different nuclei in the same isospin multiplet, provided isospin-breaking terms such as the Coulomb force are small. The relations are derived analytically from a general wave-function expansion and are numerically verified by quantum Monte Carlo calculations, but they hold only for specific total isospin values (T=0 and T=1/2 for internal relations) and otherwise require knowledge of densities from one member of the multiplet.

## Method

The authors expand a nuclear wave function with well-defined total isospin T and projection T_z using a complete orthonormal basis of two-body functions with definite pair isospin t and projection t_z. By expressing the expansion coefficients in terms of Clebsch-Gordan coefficients and functions of the A-2 spectator nucleons, they derive analytic relations between different two-body densities ρ_{t,t_z}(r), two-body momentum distributions, and neutrinoless double-beta decay transition densities. These relations are numerically tested using variational Monte Carlo (VMC) calculations for light nuclei (⁶He, ⁶Li*, ⁶Be, ⁷Li, ⁷He, ⁷Li*, ⁷Be*, ⁷B, ¹⁰Be, ¹⁰C) with the Argonne v18 two-body interaction and Urbana X three-body interaction.

## Key numbers

- For T=0 nuclei: ρ_{1,1}(r) = ρ_{1,0}(r) = ρ_{1,-1}(r) (the pp, nn, and t=1 pn densities are equal)
- For T=1/2 nuclei: 2 ρ_{1,0}(r) = ρ_{1,1}(r) + ρ_{1,-1}(r) (the t=1 pn density is the average of pp and nn densities)
- For T=1 mirror nuclei: ρ_F(r) = ρ_{1,-1}(r) + ρ_{1,1}(r) - 2ρ_{1,0}(r) (Fermi 0νββ transition density expressed via two-body densities of the initial-state nucleus)
- For T=1 mirror nuclei: ρ_GT(r) = -3[ρ_{1,-1}^{s=0}(r) + ρ_{1,1}^{s=0}(r) - 2ρ_{1,0}^{s=0}(r)] + [ρ_{1,-1}^{s=1}(r) + ρ_{1,1}^{s=1}(r) - 2ρ_{1,0}^{s=1}(r)] (Gamow-Teller 0νββ transition density via spin-projected two-body densities)
- For T=1 multiplet: ρ_{1,1}(1,0) = ρ_{1,-1}(1,0) = ρ_{1,0}(1,1) and ρ_{1,0}(1,0) = ρ_{1,1}(1,1) - ρ_{1,0}(1,1) + ρ_{1,-1}(1,1)
- For T=3/2 multiplet: three explicit linear relations connecting ρ_{1,t_z}(3/2,1/2) to ρ_{1,t_z}(3/2,3/2) with coefficients 1/3 and 2/3

## Context

This work extends the understanding of isospin symmetry in nuclear systems beyond energy-level comparisons to two-body distribution functions, which are key for modeling electroweak interactions and short-range correlations. The derived relations enable sanity checks for ab-initio calculations and connect to the generalized contact formalism (GCF) developed by Weiss et al. (2015, 2016) for describing short-range correlated pairs. The results allow extraction of nuclear contact values for excited states from ground-state data within the same isospin multiplet, and provide a direct link between 0νββ decay transition densities of mirror nuclei and measurable two-body densities, relevant for ongoing experimental searches and theoretical studies by Cirigliano et al. (2022), Pastore et al. (2017), and Wang et al. (2019).
