# Spectra, elliptic flow and azimuthally sensitive HBT radii from Buda-Lund model for sqrt(s(NN)) = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions

> We present calculations of elliptic flow and azimuthal dependence of correlation radii in the ellipsoidally symmetric generalization of the Buda-Lund hydrodynamic model of hadron production in high-energy nuclear collisions. We compare them to data from RHIC by simultaneous fits to azimuthally integrated invariant spectra of pions, kaons and protons-antiprotons measured by PHENIX in Au+Au reactions at center of mass energy of 200 AGeV. STAR data were used for azimuthally sensitive two-particle correlation function radii and for the transverse momentum dependence of the elliptic flow parameter v2. We have found that the transverse flow is faster in the reaction plane then out of plane, which results in a reaction zone that gets slightly more elongated in-plane than out of plane. The model parameters extracted from the fits are shown and discussed.

## Key claim

The authors claim that in non-central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, the transverse flow is faster in the reaction plane than out of plane, leading to a reaction zone that is slightly more elongated in-plane than out of plane at freeze-out, which contradicts previous analyses that found out-of-plane elongation.

## Method

The authors performed simultaneous fits of the ellipsoidally symmetric generalization of the Buda-Lund hydrodynamic model to azimuthally integrated invariant spectra of pions, kaons, and protons/antiprotons from PHENIX, the transverse momentum dependence of the elliptic flow parameter v2 from STAR, and azimuthally sensitive HBT radii from STAR, using the CERN Minuit fitting package. The model employs a double saddle-point approximation in the integration of the emission function, with a binary source formalism that generates oscillating pre-factors in the two-pion correlation function.

## Key numbers

- Central temperature T0 = 174 ± 6 MeV for 20-30% centrality Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Transverse geometrical size in reaction plane Rx = 9.5 ± 0.5 fm
- Transverse geometrical size out of plane Ry = 7.0 ± 0.2 fm
- Expansion strength in reaction plane Hx = 0.158 ± 0.002
- Expansion strength out of plane Hy = 0.118 ± 0.002
- Mean freeze-out proper time tau0 = 5.4 ± 0.1 fm/c
- Duration of particle production Delta tau = 2.5 ± 0.2 fm/c
- Space-time rapidity width Delta eta = 2.5 ± 0.3
- Baryochemical potential mu_B = 27 ± 16 MeV
- Derived parameters: rho0 = 1.16 ± 0.05 and rho2 = 0.29 ± 0.02
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for full fit: 269.4/152 = 1.77

## Context

This work extends the Buda-Lund hydrodynamic model from central to non-central ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions, building on earlier axially symmetric versions by Csorgo (1999) and Csanad (2004). The results contradict previous analyses using the Blast Wave model by Retiere (2004) and Tomasik (2004), as well as hydrodynamic simulations by Heinz (2002) and Frodermann (2007), which found the fireball at freeze-out to be elongated out of the reaction plane; instead, this analysis finds in-plane elongation for the first time at RHIC energies. The work enables further investigation of the non-monotonous dependence of final state anisotropy on collision energy observed by Lisa (2009).
