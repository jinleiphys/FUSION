# Partial-wave analysis to determine the spin of $Ξ(1690)^-$ and $Ξ(1820)^-$ produced in $\overline{p}p$ annihilation

> Recently [arXiv:2201.03852v2] the PANDA collaboration studied the feasibility of determining the spin and parity of the $Ξ(1690)^{-}$and $Ξ(1820)^{-}$ resonances in the $ΛK^-$ system produced in $\bar{p}p$ collisions via the reaction channel $\overline{p} p \rightarrow \barΞ^{+} ΛK^{-}$. This contribution aims to study these reactions using a model-independent irreducible tensor formalism developed earlier. This study leads us to identify the partial-wave amplitude, which would be zero if $Ξ(1690)^{-}$ or $Ξ(1820)^{-}$ had spin-1/2, provided these resonances are produced at threshold (s-wave production).

# Partial-wave analysis to determine the spin of Ξ(1690)⁻ and Ξ(1820)⁻ produced in \(\overline{p}p\) annihilation

## Key claim
The spin of Ξ(1690)⁻ or Ξ(1820)⁻ can be determined by measuring a specific observable in the reaction \(\overline{p}p \rightarrow \bar{\Xi}^{+} \Lambda K^{-}\): if the integral of that observable with spherical harmonics \(Y_{2m}(\hat{\mathbf{q}}) Y_{2m'}(\hat{\mathbf{p}}_f)\) is zero, the resonance has spin-1/2; if nonzero, it has spin-3/2. This conclusion holds only under the condition that the resonances are produced at threshold (s-wave production, \(l_2 = 0\)).

## Method
The author applies a model-independent irreducible tensor formalism to the reaction \(\overline{p}p \rightarrow \bar{\Xi}^{+} \Lambda K^{-}\), relating its partial-wave amplitudes to those of the two-body process \(\overline{p}p \rightarrow \bar{\Xi}^{+} \Xi^{*-}\) via a transformation of angular momenta (Equation 13, based on Venkataraya (year not specified)). By analyzing the allowed angular momentum quantum numbers for spin-1/2 versus spin-3/2 \(\Xi^{*}\) resonances, the author identifies which partial-wave amplitudes can contribute when the \(\Xi^{*}\) is produced in an s-wave (\(l_2 = 0\)).

## Key numbers
- For \(\Xi^{*}\) with spin-1/2, the relative angular momentum between \(\Lambda\) and \(K^{-}\) can be \(l' = 0, 1\); for spin-3/2, \(l' = 1, 2\).
- For \(\overline{p}p \rightarrow \bar{\Xi}^{+} \Xi^{*-}\), the channel spin \(s\) ranges from 0 to 3 if both \(\bar{\Xi}^{+}\) and \(\Xi^{*-}\) have spin-3/2, and from 0 to 1 if both have spin-1/2.
- The partial-wave amplitude with \(l_2 = 0\) cannot contribute to \(l = l_f = 1\) if \(\Xi^{*}\) has spin-1/2, but can contribute only if \(\Xi^{*}\) has spin-3/2.

## Context
This work extends the feasibility study by the PANDA collaboration (2022, arXiv:2201.03852v2) on determining the spin and parity of Ξ(1690)⁻ and Ξ(1820)⁻ in the \(\Lambda K^{-}\) system. The author uses an irreducible tensor formalism developed earlier (Grishina et al., year not specified; Pachattu, year not specified) and a transformation from Venkataraya (year not specified) to relate the two-body and three-body reaction amplitudes. The result enables a model-independent experimental test of the spin of these resonances at PANDA by measuring angular distributions of the final-state particles.
