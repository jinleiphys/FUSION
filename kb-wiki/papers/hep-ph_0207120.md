# Large-$p_T$ Inclusive $π^0$ Production in Heavy-Ion Collisions at RHIC and LHC

> We present results for the large-$p_T$ inclusive $π^0$ production in p-p and A-A collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. We include the full next-to-leading order radiative corrections, $O(α_s^3)$, and nuclear effects such as parton energy loss and nuclear shadowing. We find the next-to-leading order corrections and the parton energy loss effect to be large and $p_T$ dependent, while the nuclear shadowing effects are small ($< 10%$). We calculate the ratio of prompt photons to neutral pions produced in heavy ion collisions and show that at RHIC energies this ratio increases with $p_T$ approaching one at $p_T \sim 8$ GeV, due to the large suppression of $π^0$ production. We show that at the LHC, this ratio has a steep $p_T$ dependence and approaches 25% effect at $p_T \sim 40$ GeV. We discuss theoretical uncertainties inherent in our calculation, such as choice of the renormalization, factorization and fragmentation scales and the K-factors which signify the size of higher-order corrections.

## Key claim

The authors present next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD calculations for inclusive π⁰ production in p-p and A-A collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, finding that the observed suppression of pion production in heavy-ion collisions is primarily due to medium-induced parton energy loss rather than nuclear shadowing, with the fractional (BH-type) energy loss of 6.1% per collision providing the best description of PHENIX data at √s = 200 GeV.

## Method

The authors used the NLO (O(αₛ³)) perturbative QCD code of Aurenche et al. for π⁰ and prompt photon production in p-p collisions, employing MRS99 parton distribution functions and BKK pion fragmentation functions. Nuclear effects were incorporated by using EKS98 nuclear shadowing parametrization and by modifying the BKK fragmentation functions according to the Huang-Sarcevic-Wang model of medium-induced parton energy loss, with the average number of scatterings determined by a Poisson distribution over a path length ΔL = Rₐ with mean free path λₐ = 1 fm.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear K-factor (ratio of NLO to LO π⁰ cross section) varies between 1.6 and 2.2 for pT between 4 GeV and 20 GeV at √s = 130 GeV, independent of the form of parton energy loss
- Fractional energy loss ΔE/E = 6.1% gives the best description of PHENIX π⁰ data at √s = 200 GeV
- Nuclear shadowing effects are small, less than 10% at RHIC energies
- At RHIC √s = 130 GeV, the γ/π⁰ ratio with BH energy loss approaches 1 at pT ∼ 8 GeV
- At LHC √s = 5.5 TeV, the γ/π⁰ ratio with BH energy loss approaches 25% at pT ∼ 40 GeV
- At RHIC √s = 130 GeV, about 75% of prompt photon contribution at pT = 3 GeV comes from direct processes unaffected by energy loss
- At LHC √s = 5.5 TeV, bremsstrahlung processes contribute about 60% of prompt photon production at pT ∼ 4 GeV
- Theoretical uncertainty from scale variation (Q = pT to Q = 2pT) is about 40% for the absolute cross section, but only a few percent for the AA/pp ratio with BH energy loss

## Context

This work extends NLO perturbative QCD calculations of inclusive hadron production (previously applied to p-p collisions by Aurenche et al.) to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, incorporating nuclear shadowing via the EKS98 parametrization (Eskola, Kolhinen, Salgado, 1999) and parton energy loss via the Huang-Sarcevic-Wang model (1998). The calculations enable discrimination between initial-state effects (such as the color glass condensate) and final-state effects (parton energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma) by comparing the suppression patterns of π⁰ and prompt photons, and provide predictions for the γ/π⁰ ratio that can be tested against upcoming PHENIX and LHC data.
