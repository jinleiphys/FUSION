# Heavy quark photoproduction in proton-proton collisions

> We calculate the photoproduction of heavy quarks in proton-proton collisions at RHIC, Tevatron and LHC energies. The integrated cross section and the rapidity distributions for open charm and bottom production are computed employing sound high energy QCD formalisms. For the linear pQCD approaches we consider both the usual collinear factorization and the k_T-factorization formalisms, whereas for the nonlinear QCD (saturation) calculations one considers the Golec-Biernat-Wuesthoff and the Iancu-Itakura-Munier parameterizations for the dipole cross section within the color dipole picture.

# Heavy Quark Photoproduction in Proton-Proton Collisions

## Key claim

The photoproduction of heavy quarks in proton-proton collisions at RHIC, Tevatron, and LHC energies can probe gluon distributions at smaller Bjorken-x values than those accessible at DESY-HERA, but the cross section predictions depend strongly on whether linear perturbative QCD or nonlinear saturation formalisms are used, with saturation models (SAT-MOD and CGC) producing systematically lower cross sections than collinear or k_T-factorization approaches.

## Method

The authors compute the integrated cross section and rapidity distributions for open charm and bottom photoproduction in proton-proton collisions by convolving the Weizsacker-Williams photon spectrum from one proton with the photon-proton cross section for heavy quark pair production. For the photon-proton cross section, they employ four distinct QCD formalisms: collinear factorization at leading order using GRV98(LO) parton distributions; k_T-factorization using unintegrated gluon distributions derived from the derivative of collinear gluon densities; the Golec-Biernat-Wuesthoff saturation model (SAT-MOD) within the color dipole picture; and the Iancu-Itakura-Munier (CGC) parameterization for the dipole cross section.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass set to m_c = 1.5 GeV; bottom quark mass set to m_b = 4.5 GeV
- For the SAT-MOD saturation model: sigma_0 = 29.12 mb, lambda = 0.277, x_0 = 0.41 x 10^{-4}
- For the CGC model: R_p = 0.641 fm, lambda = 0.253, x_0 = 0.267 x 10^{-4}, N_0 = 0.7, gamma_sat = 0.63, kappa = 9.9
- At LHC energy (sqrt(S_NN) = 14 TeV), the integrated cross section for charm ranges from approximately 3.5-17 x 10^{10} events/year and for bottom from 5.5-24 x 10^{8} events/year, assuming LHC luminosity of 10^{34} cm^{-2}s^{-1}
- At Tevatron (sqrt(S_NN) = 1.96 TeV), charm rates are 2-6 x 10^{9} events/year and bottom rates 2-6 x 10^{7} events/year, assuming luminosity 2 x 10^{32} cm^{-2}s^{-1}
- At RHIC (sqrt(S_NN) = 500 GeV), charm rates are 3.7-7.8 x 10^{6} events/year and bottom rates 3.7-4.3 x 10^{4} events/year, assuming luminosity 10^{31} cm^{-2}s^{-1}
- The resolved photon contribution is estimated to be approximately 15-20% of the total charm and bottom photoproduction cross sections at LHC energy
- The photoproduction cross section is approximately 0.1% of the corresponding hadroproduction cross section

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Klein and Nystrand (2004) on quarkonium photoproduction in proton-proton collisions to the case of open heavy quark production, motivated by the fact that the photon reaches energies higher than those accessible at DESY-HERA. The authors build on their previous studies of heavy quark photoproduction on nucleon and nuclear targets (Goncalves and Machado, 2003) and ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions. The results enable experimental searches at RHIC, Tevatron, and LHC that could distinguish between linear pQCD dynamics and nonlinear saturation effects, and provide constraints on the gluon distribution at small x. The calculations do not include the resolved photon contribution, which the authors estimate at 15-20% based on results from Klein and Vogt for ultraperipheral collisions and from Timneanu and Motyka using k_T-factorization.
