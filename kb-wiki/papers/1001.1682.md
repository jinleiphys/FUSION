# A Quarkyonic Phase in Dense Two Color Matter?

> We present results from simulations of Two Color QCD with two Wilson quark flavors in the presence of a quark chemical potential mu at two different lattice spacings. The equation of state, conformal anomaly, superfluid order parameter and Polyakov line are all discussed. Our results suggest that the transition from hadronic to quark matter, and that from confined to deconfined matter occur at distinct values of mu, consistent with the existence of a quarkyonic phase in this model.

## Key claim

The paper presents lattice simulation evidence that in Two Color QCD (QC₂D) with two quark flavors, the transition from hadronic to quark matter and the transition from confined to deconfined matter occur at distinct values of the quark chemical potential μ, consistent with the existence of a quarkyonic phase. The authors hedge that the deconfining transition at μ_D ≈ 850 MeV is sensitive to lattice temperature and may be a crossover rather than a sharp phase transition, and that the continuum limit has not yet been established.

## Method

The authors performed lattice Monte Carlo simulations of SU(2) gauge theory (Two Color QCD) with two flavors of Wilson quarks at finite quark chemical potential μ, using a hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm with a diquark source term to maintain ergodicity. Data were collected on two lattice volumes with different lattice spacings: an 8³×16 lattice at β=1.7 (a=0.230(5) fm) and a 12³×24 lattice at β=1.9 (a=0.186(8) fm), with physical temperatures of approximately 54 MeV and 44 MeV respectively. They measured the equation of state (quark density, pressure, energy density), the conformal anomaly (trace of the stress-energy tensor), the superfluid order parameter ⟨qq⟩, and the Polyakov line as functions of μ.

## Key numbers

- Onset chemical potential for baryon density: μ_o ≈ 360 MeV (μ_o a = 0.34), corresponding to half the pion mass
- BEC/BCS crossover at μ_Q ≈ 530 MeV, with quark density n_q ≈ 4–5 fm⁻³ (roughly 10 times nuclear density)
- Deconfinement transition at μ_D ≈ 850 MeV, with quark density n_q ≈ 16–32 fm⁻³ (35–70 times nuclear density)
- Lattice spacing on the finer lattice: a = 0.186(8) fm, with m_π a = 0.68(1) and m_π/m_ρ = 0.80(1)
- Lattice spacing on the coarser lattice: a = 0.230(5) fm, with m_π a = 0.79(1) and m_π/m_ρ = 0.779(4)
- Beta-function estimates along the line of constant physics: -0.85(17) for gluons, 0.042(9) for quarks

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of Two Color QCD by Hands et al. (2006) on the 8³×16 lattice, which had identified only a deconfined quark matter phase because the BEC/BCS crossover and deconfinement were indistinguishable at the coarser lattice spacing. The results are compared to chiral perturbation theory predictions by Kogut et al. (2000) and to the free quark gas (Stefan-Boltzmann) limit, and are interpreted in light of the large-N_c quarkyonic matter scenario proposed by McLerran and collaborators (2007). The study enables future investigations of the continuum limit and the nature of the deconfinement transition in dense matter, and raises questions about whether the quarkyonic phase is specific to SU(2) or generalizes to QCD with N_c=3.
