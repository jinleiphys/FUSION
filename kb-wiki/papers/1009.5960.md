# Meson-baryon interaction in the meson exchange picture

> This is the contribution to the proceedings of the MENU 2010 conference. The recent work of Ref. [7] is summarized. Elastic piN scattering and the reaction pi^+ p --> K^+ Sigma^+ are described simultaneously in a unitary coupled-channels approach which respects analyticity. SU(3) flavor symmetry is used to relate the t- and u- channel exchanges that drive the meson-baryon interaction in the different channels. Angular distributions, polarizations, and spin-rotation parameters are compared with available experimental data. The pole structure of the amplitudes is extracted from the analytic continuation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a simultaneous description of elastic πN scattering and the reaction π⁺ p → K⁺ Σ⁺ can be achieved in a unitary coupled-channels approach with SU(3) flavor symmetry, but the model's validity is limited to energies below 2 GeV, and above that energy the K⁺ Σ⁺ data were not fitted.

## Method

The author uses a dynamical coupled-channels framework based on the Jülich model, solving an integral equation in the JLS-basis that fulfills two-body unitarity and analyticity. The pseudo-potential V is constructed from effective Lagrangians with t- and u-channel meson and baryon exchanges, extended to include ΛK and ΣK channels, with SU(3) flavor symmetry relating vertices across channels. Bare s-channel resonance states are introduced in each I=3/2 partial wave (S31, P31, D33, D35, F35, F37, with two in P33) and coupled to πN, KΣ, πΔ, and ρN channels, with a total of 40 fit parameters.

## Key numbers

- The model describes data at 32 different energies from the KΣ threshold up to z = 2.3 GeV.
- For the Δ(1905)F35 resonance: pole position Re z₀ = 1764 MeV, -2 Im z₀ = 218 MeV, residue |r| = 1.4 MeV, branching ratio (Γ^{1/2}_{πN} Γ^{1/2}_{KΣ})/Γ_{tot} = 1.23%.
- For the Δ(1910)P31 resonance: pole position Re z₀ = 1721 MeV, -2 Im z₀ = 323 MeV, residue |r| = 5.5 MeV, branching ratio = 2.98%.
- For the Δ(1920)P33 resonance: pole position Re z₀ = 1884 MeV, -2 Im z₀ = 229 MeV, residue |r| = 5.9 MeV, branching ratio = 5.07%.
- For the Δ(1930)D35 resonance: pole position Re z₀ = 1865 MeV, -2 Im z₀ = 147 MeV, residue |r| = 1.6 MeV, branching ratio = 2.14%.
- For the Δ(1950)F37 resonance: pole position Re z₀ = 1873 MeV, -2 Im z₀ = 206 MeV, residue |r| = 2.7 MeV, branching ratio = 2.54%.

## Context

This work extends the Jülich dynamical coupled-channels model (Schutz 1998, Krehl 1999, Gasparyan 2003, Doring 2009) by adding KΣ and ΛK channels to the existing channel space (Nπ, Nη, Nσ, Δπ, Nρ). It enables baryon spectroscopy by providing a realistic, structured background from meson exchange, allowing a minimal set of s-channel resonances to describe combined πN and K⁺ Σ⁺ data. The analysis provides evidence for the three-star Δ(1920)P33 resonance, which couples weakly to πN but more strongly to KΣ, addressing the "missing resonance problem" where states not seen in πN scattering may be observed in other channels.
