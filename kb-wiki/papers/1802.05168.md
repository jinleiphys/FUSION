# The relativistic chiral Lagrangian for decuplet and octet baryons at next-to-leading order

> A complete and minimal relativistic Lagrangian is constructed at next-to-leading order for SU(3) chiral perturbation theory in the presence of baryon octet and baryon decuplet states. The Lagrangian has 13 terms for the pure decuplet sector, 6 terms for the transition sector from baryon octet to decuplet and (as already known from the literature) 16 terms for the pure octet sector. The minimal field content of 25 of these terms is meson-baryon four-point interactions. 3 terms give rise to the mass splitting for baryon octet and decuplet states, respectively. 2 terms give rise to overall mass shifts. 4 terms provide anomalous magnetic moments and a decuplet-to-octet magnetic transition moment. 1 term leads to an axial vector transition moment. It is shown that meson-baryon three-point coupling constants come in at leading order whereas no additional one appears in the minimal Lagrangian at next-to-leading order. Those low-energy constants that give rise to mass splitting and magnetic moments, respectively, are determined. Predictions are provided for radiative decays of decuplet to octet baryons.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a complete and minimal relativistic next-to-leading-order (NLO) Lagrangian for SU(3) chiral perturbation theory including both baryon octet and baryon decuplet states, but the abstract's claim of "predictions" for radiative decays is qualified by the fact that several low-energy constants remain undetermined from existing data, so the predictions serve only as rough guidelines.

## Method

The authors systematically construct all independent NLO interaction terms (order p^2) for the octet-decuplet transition sector and the pure decuplet sector, using the building blocks χ±, f±^μν, u^μ u^ν, and D^μ u^ν + D^ν u^μ. They eliminate redundant terms using the leading-order equations of motion, parity and charge conjugation constraints, and the fact that only one partial wave (p-wave) exists for meson-baryon three-point couplings at this order. The resulting low-energy constants for mass splitting and magnetic moments are then determined from known baryon masses and magnetic moments, and used to compute radiative decay widths for decuplet-to-octet transitions.

## Key numbers

- The NLO Lagrangian contains 13 terms for the pure decuplet sector, 6 terms for the octet-to-decuplet transition sector, and 16 terms for the pure octet sector (the latter already known from literature)
- 25 of these terms are meson-baryon four-point interactions; 3 terms give mass splitting for octet and decuplet; 2 terms give overall mass shifts; 4 terms provide anomalous magnetic moments and a decuplet-to-octet magnetic transition moment; 1 term gives an axial vector transition moment
- The Δ-nucleon mass difference is approximately 300 MeV, setting a hard scale for chiral perturbation theory
- The pion decay constant is F_π = 92.4 MeV; the axial couplings are D = 0.80, F = 0.46, giving g_A = F + D = 1.26
- The decuplet-octet axial coupling h_A is determined from Σ* decay as h_A = 2.3 ± 0.1, from Ξ* decay as h_A = 2.00 ± 0.06, and from Δ decay as h_A = 2.88; large-N_c estimates give h_A = 2.4 ± 0.5
- The decuplet axial coupling H_A is estimated from large-N_c as H_A = 9/5 g_A ≈ 2.27 or H_A = 9F - 3D ≈ 1.74

## Context

This work extends the systematic construction of chiral Lagrangians from the baryon-octet sector (Fettes 1998, Frink 2006) to include the full decuplet at NLO, filling a gap in the literature. It provides the necessary theoretical framework for analyzing upcoming experimental data on hyperon radiative decays from facilities like FAIR, where HADES and PANDA collaborations plan joint measurements. The results enable NLO predictions for decuplet-to-octet radiative transitions, which have only been observed for some decuplet members so far, and provide a basis for future NNLO extensions.
