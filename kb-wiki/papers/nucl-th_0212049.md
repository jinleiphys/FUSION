# Nuclear energy density functional from chiral pion-nucleon dynamics

> We calculate the nuclear energy density functional relevant for N=Z even-even nuclei in the systematic framework of chiral perturbation theory. The calculation includes the one-pion exchange Fock diagram and the iterated one-pion exchange Hartree and Fock diagrams. From these few leading order contributions in the small momentum expansion one obtains already a very good equation of state of isospin symmetric nuclear matter. We find that in the region below nuclear matter saturation density the effective nucleon mass $\widetilde M^*(ρ)$ deviates by at most 15% from its free space value $M$, with $0.89M<\widetilde M^*(ρ)<M$ for $ρ< 0.11 {\rm fm}^{-3}$ and $\widetilde M^*(ρ)>M$ for higher densities. The parameterfree strength of the $(\vec\nabla ρ)^2$-term, $F_\nabla(k_f)$, is at saturation density comparable to that of phenomenological Skyrme forces. The magnitude of $F_J(k_f)$ accompanying the squared spin-orbit density $\vec J ^2$ comes out somewhat larger. The strength of the nuclear spin-orbit interaction, $F_{so}(k_f)$, as given by iterated one-pion exchange is about half as large as the corresponding empirical value, however, with the wrong negative sign. The novel density dependencies of $\widetilde M^*(ρ)$ and $F_{\nabla,so,J}(k_f)$ as predicted by our parameterfree calculation should be examined in nuclear structure calculations (after introducing an additional short range spin-orbit contribution constant in density).

## Key claim

The paper claims that the nuclear energy density functional for N=Z even-even nuclei can be calculated from chiral perturbation theory using only one-pion exchange and iterated one-pion exchange diagrams, yielding parameter-free predictions for density-dependent strength functions. However, the spin-orbit interaction strength from iterated one-pion exchange comes out with the wrong negative sign and only about half the empirical magnitude, requiring an additional short-range spin-orbit contribution constant in density.

## Method

The authors used the density-matrix expansion of Negele and Vautherin (1972) to derive the nuclear energy density functional from chiral perturbation theory, computing contributions from the two-loop one-pion exchange Fock diagram and the three-loop iterated one-pion exchange Hartree and Fock diagrams. The strength functions \(F_\tau(k_f)\), \(F_\nabla(k_f)\), \(F_{so}(k_f)\), and \(F_J(k_f)\) were derived analytically from these diagrams using the medium insertion formalism, with input parameters limited to the nucleon axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A = 1.3\), nucleon mass \(M = 939\) MeV, pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV, and neutral pion mass \(m_\pi = 135\) MeV. The calculation is complete up to seventh order in small momenta for NN-interactions induced by pion exchange.

## Key numbers

- Effective nucleon mass \(\widetilde M^*(\rho)\) deviates by at most 15% from free space value \(M\) below saturation density, with \(0.89M < \widetilde M^*(\rho) < M\) for \(\rho < 0.11\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) and \(\widetilde M^*(\rho) > M\) for higher densities
- At saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.174\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), the effective nucleon mass \(\widetilde M^*(\rho_0) = 1.15M\)
- The strength function \(F_\nabla(k_f)\) at saturation density is \(F_\nabla(k_{f0}) = 80.1\ \mathrm{MeV}\ \mathrm{fm}^5\), comparable to phenomenological Skyrme forces
- The spin-orbit strength from iterated one-pion exchange Hartree diagram at saturation density is \(F_{so}(k_{f0}) = -83.7\ \mathrm{MeV}\ \mathrm{fm}^5\), with wrong negative sign and about half the empirical magnitude
- The nuclear matter equation of state fit gives \(\bar E(k_f) = 111.63\ \mathrm{MeV}\ \mathrm{fm}^2 \cdot \rho^{2/3} - 752.82\ \mathrm{MeV}\ \mathrm{fm}^3 \cdot \rho + 832.74\ \mathrm{MeV}\ \mathrm{fm}^4 \cdot \rho^{4/3}\)

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory treatment of nuclear matter by Kaiser, Fritsch, and Weise (2002) to inhomogeneous systems, building on their earlier calculation of the nuclear matter equation of state and single-particle potential. The density-dependent strength functions provide a microscopic foundation for the parameters of phenomenological Skyrme forces such as SIII (Beiner et al., 1975), Sly (Chabanat et al., 1998), and MSk (Pearson et al., 2000), and the effective nucleon mass result \(\widetilde M^*(\rho_0) = 1.15M\) is comparable to the value \(1.05M\) from a large-scale fit by Pearson et al. (2000). The calculation enables future nuclear structure studies using these parameter-free density dependencies, though an additional short-range spin-orbit contribution must be introduced to correct the sign and magnitude of the spin-orbit interaction.
