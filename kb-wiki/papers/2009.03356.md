# The Origin of the Giant Dipole Resonance

> The Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR), which is conventionally described as due to collective motion, is instead shown to be the result of a sudden increase in level density at the 2hω shell closure. The energy of the GDR closely follows the shell model harmonic oscillator energy model where hω = 39A^-1/3, for heavy nuclei. A better fit covering the entire mass range is given by hω = 47:55(0:13)(A^-1/3 - A^-2/3). The GDR is shown to be composed of a lower energy peak, E1, corresponding to the population of levels with oblate deformation and a higher energy peak, E2 corresponding to the population of levels with prolate deformation. The peak energy separation is proportional to the β_2 deformation and given by E2-E1 = 11:03(0:22)|β_2|. The total photonuclear cross section, sigma = sigma1 + sigma2, populating the GDR is proportional to the level density at the GDR and is given by sigma = 0:483(0:006)A^4/3 where sigma1 = sigma2. The widths of the two GDR peaks are consistent with Nilsson model predictions and found to be Gamma1 = 7:41(0:15)A^-1/6 MeV and Gamma2 = 11:13(0:16)A^-1/6 respectively. The Standard Lorentzian model parameters are fitt to high accuracy as a function of mass and deformation and can be applied reliably to all nuclei. It is shown that the energies of pygmy and spin flip resonances correspond to the E = hω harmonic oscillator energy and that the giant quadrupole (GQR), giant monopole (GMR), and giant octupole (GOR) resonances coincide with the E = 2-4 hω harmonic oscillator energies where the level density suddenly increases at the shell gaps.

## Key claim

The Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) is not a collective enhancement of photon strength due to the counter-motion of protons and neutrons, but instead results from a sudden increase in level density at the 2ħω harmonic oscillator shell gap. The paper claims that the GDR can be fully described by a Lorentzian distribution whose parameters are defined by the harmonic oscillator and Nilsson deformed shell models, with no collective motion required.

## Method

The author performed least-squares fits to 148 experimental measurements of GDR Brink-Axel photon strength parameters for nuclei with A=2-239, taken from the IAEA Reference Input Parameter Library (RIPL-3) and a recommended set by Kawano (2019). The mean GDR energy, total cross section, and average width were fit as functions of mass number A and deformation β₂, and the results were compared with harmonic oscillator energies from Moszkowski (1957) and Blomqvist and Molinari (1968), as well as with Nilsson model predictions for deformed nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Mean GDR energy fit: Ē = 2(47.34 ± 0.27)(A⁻¹/³ − A⁻²/³) MeV, with R² = 1.00 for A=2-239
- Total GDR cross section: σ_T = (0.483 ± 0.005)A⁴/³ mb, with R² = 0.98
- Average GDR width: (Γ̄, Γ) = (1.110 ± 0.0015)Ē¹/² MeV
- Peak energy separation for even-even nuclei: ΔE = (11.1 ± 0.3)|β₂| MeV, with R² = 0.98
- Peak energy separation for odd-A nuclei: ΔE = (12.3 ± 0.6)|β₂| MeV, with R² = 0.95
- Width ratio: Γ₂/Γ₁ = 1.50 ± 0.07
- Cross section ratio: σ₂/σ₁ = Γ₂/Γ₁ = 1.50 ± 0.07
- Individual peak cross sections: σ₁ = (0.193 ± 0.010)A⁴/³ mb, σ₂ = (0.290 ± 0.015)A⁴/³ mb
- Individual peak energies: E₁ = Ē − (6.68 ± 0.16)|β₂|, E₂ = Ē + (4.45 ± 0.11)|β₂|
- Individual peak widths: Γ₁ = (0.84 ± 0.03)Ē¹/² MeV, Γ₂ = (1.26 ± 0.04)Ē¹/² MeV

## Context

This work directly contradicts the long-standing collective model of the GDR proposed by Goldhaber and Teller (1948), which described the resonance as protons and neutrons oscillating against each other. The paper extends the harmonic oscillator energy systematics of Moszkowski (1957) and Blomqvist and Molinari (1968) to show that GDR energies follow the same functional form as nuclear radii. It also connects the GDR to the Nilsson deformed shell model, showing that the two GDR peaks in deformed nuclei correspond to transitions to oblate and prolate deformed states respectively. The work enables a universal parameterization of GDR photon strength for all nuclei near stability, and predicts that all other giant resonances (GQR, GMR, ISGDR, GOR) occur at 2-4ħω harmonic oscillator energies where level density increases at shell gaps.
