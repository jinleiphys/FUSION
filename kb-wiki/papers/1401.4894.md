# Azimuthally sensitive femtoscopy in event-by-event hydrodynamics

> We analyze the pion femtoscopy correlations in noncentral Au-Au and Pb-Pb collisions. The azimuthally sensitive Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) method is used to extract the interferometry radii depending on the azimuthal angle with respect to the second and third-order event plane. The results are in semiquantitative agreement with the STAR collaboration data on the HBT radii with respect to the second-order reaction plane, with the preliminary PHENIX collaboration data on the HBT radii with respect to the third-order reaction plane in Au-Au collisions at 200GeV, and with the preliminary ALICE collaboration data for the HBT radii with respect to the second-order event plane for Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76TeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that event-by-event 3+1-D viscous hydrodynamic simulations can semiquantitatively reproduce the azimuthal dependence of pion HBT radii with respect to both the second-order and third-order event planes in noncentral Au-Au and Pb-Pb collisions, but the agreement is qualified as semiquantitative and the third-order radii are found to be determined primarily by flow deformation rather than by the final spatial triangularity of the freeze-out surface.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1-D viscous hydrodynamic simulations with a constant shear viscosity to entropy ratio of 0.08 and a bulk viscosity of 0.04 in the hadronic phase, using Glauber Monte Carlo initial conditions with Gaussian smearing of width 0.4 fm. For each centrality class, 100 to 400 independent hydrodynamic runs were evolved to a freeze-out temperature of 150 MeV (or 140 MeV when specified), and pions were emitted statistically using the THERMINATOR code. The two-pion correlation functions were constructed in bins of azimuthal angle and transverse momentum, and the HBT radii were extracted using the Bertsch-Pratt parametrization, with the angular dependence decomposed into zeroth and second-order (or third-order) Fourier harmonics relative to the event plane.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy ratio used: eta/s = 0.08; bulk viscosity to entropy ratio in hadronic phase: zeta/s = 0.04
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 150 MeV (or 140 MeV when specified)
- Gaussian smearing width for initial entropy density: 0.4 fm
- Number of independent hydrodynamic runs per centrality class: 100 to 400
- Number of THERMINATOR events per freeze-out hypersurface: from 100 for central Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV up to 1500 for semiperipheral Au-Au at 200 GeV
- For the smooth initial condition test, the initial triangularity was set to epsilon_3 = 0.12, with mean squared radius <r^2> = 17.5 fm^2
- Deviations of calculated angle-averaged HBT radii from STAR data: 10 to 15 percent
- For the event-averaged emission function, the extracted radii were within 3 percent of the correct event-by-event average

## Context

This work extends prior hydrodynamic calculations of azimuthally sensitive HBT radii with respect to the second-order event plane by Frodermann (2007) and Kisiel (2008) to include event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions and viscous evolution, and it is the first to compute third-order HBT radii relative to the third-order event plane in a full 3+1-D viscous hydrodynamic model. The results are compared to STAR data (Adams 2003) for second-order radii in Au-Au at 200 GeV, preliminary PHENIX data (Niida 2013) for third-order radii in Au-Au at 200 GeV, and preliminary ALICE data (Gramling 2012) for second-order radii in Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV. The study enables future experimental and theoretical work to disentangle the contributions of flow deformation versus final-state spatial triangularity to the observed femtoscopy signals.
