# Jet-Medium Interactions at NLO in a Weakly-Coupled Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We present an extension to next-to-leading order in the strong coupling constant $g$ of the AMY effective kinetic approach to the energy loss of high momentum particles in the quark-gluon plasma. At leading order, the transport of jet-like particles is determined by elastic scattering with the thermal constituents, and by inelastic collinear splittings induced by the medium. We reorganize this description into collinear splittings, high-momentum-transfer scatterings, drag and diffusion, and particle conversions (momentum-preserving identity-changing processes). We show that this reorganized description remains valid to NLO in $g$, and compute the appropriate modifications of the drag, diffusion, particle conversion, and inelastic splitting coefficients. In addition, a new kinematic regime opens at NLO for wider-angle collinear bremsstrahlung. These semi-collinear emissions smoothly interpolate between the leading order high-momentum-transfer scatterings and collinear splittings. To organize the calculation, we introduce a set of Wilson line operators on the light-cone which determine the diffusion and identity changing coefficients, and we show how to evaluate these operators at NLO.

## Key claim

The paper extends the AMY effective kinetic approach for jet energy loss in a quark-gluon plasma to next-to-leading order (NLO) in the strong coupling constant \(g\), showing that the reorganization of the description into collinear splittings, high-momentum-transfer scatterings, drag and diffusion, and particle conversions remains valid at NLO, provided the appropriate modifications to the coefficients are computed and a new kinematic regime of semi-collinear emissions is included.

## Method

The authors reorganize the leading-order Boltzmann equation for hard partons, which separates elastic (\(2\leftrightarrow 2\)) and inelastic (\(1\leftrightarrow 2\)) processes, into four distinct contributions: large-angle scatterings (with momentum transfer \(Q \gg gT\)), collinear splittings, diffusion (drag and transverse/longitudinal momentum broadening from soft gluon exchanges with \(Q \sim gT\)), and conversion processes (identity-changing soft fermion exchanges). They compute the NLO corrections to the drag, diffusion, particle conversion, and inelastic splitting coefficients using Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) effective theory and light-cone Wilson line operators, and they identify and calculate the semi-collinear bremsstrahlung regime that first appears at NLO and interpolates between leading-order high-momentum-transfer scatterings and collinear splittings.

## Key numbers

- The soft momentum scale is of order \(gT\), where \(T\) is the local medium temperature and \(g\) is the strong coupling constant.
- Hard partons are defined by energy \(E\) satisfying \(\exp(-E/T) \ll 1\), with virtuality \(|p_0 - p| \lesssim g^2 T\).
- The expansion parameter for subleading corrections is a single power of \(g\), since soft statistical functions \(\nbe(\omega \sim gT) \sim 1/g\) spoil the vacuum expansion in \(\alpha_s = g^2/4\pi\).
- The collinear emission angle at leading order is of order \(g\) for hierarchical splittings, or as small as \(g \sqrt{T/E}\) for democratic splittings.
- Soft gluon momenta in collinear processes are characterized by \(k^+, k_\perp \sim gT\) and \(k^- \lesssim g^2 T\).

## Context

This work extends the AMY/McGill/MARTINI approach (Arnold, Moore, Yaffe, 2002; Jeon, Moore, 2003; Qin, Ruppert, Gale, Jeon, Moore, 2007; Schenke, Gale, Jeon, 2009) for jet-medium interactions in a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma to NLO, building on earlier NLO calculations for hard real and virtual photon production by Ghiglieri, Moore, and Teaney (2013, 2014). It enables more precise numerical treatments of jet modification in heavy-ion collisions by providing NLO-accurate drag, diffusion, conversion, and splitting coefficients, and introduces a matching procedure that removes dependence on the infrared cutoff scale \(\mu\) separating soft and hard processes.
