# Probing the pion gluon distribution at small-$x$ in photon-induced interactions at LHC

> In this paper we propose the analysis of the heavy quark photoproduction associated with a leading neutron in hadronic collisions at the LHC as an alternative to probe the pion gluon distribution in a kinematical range not covered by previous experiments. We perform an exploratory study of the charm and bottom photoproduction associated with a leading neutron in proton-proton ($pp$) and proton-lead ($pPb$) collisions, and present calculations for the rapidity distributions and cross-sections. Our predictions indicate that experimental studies of these processes are feasible, and that a future measurement of this final state will be useful to constrain the pion gluon distribution at small values of the Bjorken $x$ variable and to improve our understanding of the pion structure.

# Probing the pion gluon distribution at small-$x$ in photon-induced interactions at LHC

## Key claim
The authors propose that measuring heavy quark (charm and bottom) photoproduction accompanied by a leading neutron in ultraperipheral $pp$ and $pPb$ collisions at the LHC can constrain the pion gluon distribution at small Bjorken-$x$, a kinematic region not covered by previous pion-nucleus scattering or HERA leading-neutron experiments. The feasibility depends on the ability to tag the forward neutron using a Zero Degree Calorimeter and on the assumption that the Sullivan process factorizes cleanly from absorptive effects.

## Method
The authors model the process as photon emission from one hadron (proton or lead nucleus) scattering off the virtual pion cloud of the target proton via the Sullivan process, with the photon-gluon fusion subprocess $\gamma g \rightarrow Q\bar{Q}$ dominating heavy quark production. They compute rapidity distributions and total cross-sections at leading order for charm ($m_c=1.5$ GeV) and bottom ($m_b=4.5$ GeV) in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV and $pPb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=8.1$ TeV, using three different pion gluon distribution parameterizations (GRV, JAM21, xFitter) from the LHAPDF library, with a fixed absorption factor $\mathcal{K}=0.8$ and hard scale $\mu=2m_Q$.

## Key numbers
- Charm photoproduction cross-section in $pPb$ collisions (full rapidity range $|Y|<7$): $3.02\times 10^{2}$ $\mu$b (GRV), $2.24\times 10^{2}$ $\mu$b (JAM21), $1.42\times 10^{2}$ $\mu$b (xFitter)
- Charm photoproduction cross-section in $pp$ collisions (full rapidity range $|Y|<7$): $3.47\times 10^{2}$ nb (GRV), $2.14\times 10^{2}$ nb (JAM21), $1.34\times 10^{2}$ nb (xFitter)
- Bottom photoproduction cross-section in $pPb$ collisions (full rapidity range $|Y|<7$): $2.29\times 10^{0}$ $\mu$b (GRV), $2.11\times 10^{0}$ $\mu$b (JAM21), $1.54\times 10^{0}$ $\mu$b (xFitter)
- Bottom photoproduction cross-section in $pp$ collisions (full rapidity range $|Y|<7$): $4.32\times 10^{0}$ nb (GRV), $3.47\times 10^{0}$ nb (JAM21), $2.50\times 10^{0}$ nb (xFitter)
- Charm cross-section in $pp$ collisions at midrapidity ($|Y|<2$): $1.19\times 10^{2}$ nb (GRV), $8.36\times 10^{1}$ nb (JAM21), $4.91\times 10^{1}$ nb (xFitter)
- Bottom cross-section in $pp$ collisions at midrapidity ($|Y|<2$): $1.61\times 10^{0}$ nb (GRV), $1.50\times 10^{0}$ nb (JAM21), $1.05\times 10^{0}$ nb (xFitter)
- Maximum photon-proton center-of-mass energy at LHC: $W_{\gamma p}^{\text{max}} \approx 8.2$ TeV for $pp$ at $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV, $W_{\gamma p}^{\text{max}} \approx 1.4$ TeV for $pPb$ at $\sqrt{s}=8.1$ TeV
- Absorption factor used: $\mathcal{K}=0.8$, constrained from HERA leading neutron electroproduction data

## Context
This work extends the Sullivan process approach previously applied to exclusive processes with a leading neutron (Goncalves 2016, Carvalho 2017) to inclusive heavy quark photoproduction, complementing recent studies of inclusive heavy quark photoproduction in UPCs as probes of proton and nuclear gluon distributions (Gimeno-Estivill 2025, Goncalves 2025, Cacciari 2025). The proposed process accesses pion structure at smaller $x$ than achievable in pion-nucleus scattering at CERN and Fermilab (NA3 1983, NA10 1985, E615 1989) or leading neutron electroproduction at HERA (ZEUS 2002, H1 2010). Future measurements at the LHC could provide constraints complementary to those expected from the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and the proposed Electron-ion collider in China (EicC), which will also use the Sullivan process with tagged leading neutrons.
