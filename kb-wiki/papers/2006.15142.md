# Large-scale shell-model calculations of nuclear Schiff moments of $^{129}$Xe and $^{199}$Hg

> The theoretical uncertainty in the nuclear Schiff moment is an obstacle to set constraints on $ CP $ violation beyond the standard model from experimental upper bounds on atomic electric dipole moments. We perform large-scale shell-model calculations of the $^{129}$Xe and $^{199}$Hg nuclei with realistic effective interactions. To estimate the Schiff moments caused by the $ P $, $ T $-odd $ πNN $ interaction perturbatively, we employ the one-particle one-hole approximation to the intermediate states. The Schiff moments of $^{129}$Xe and $^{199}$Hg are reduced due to the configuration mixing by $ \sim 10 \% $ from the evaluation of the independent particle model. On the other hand, the reduction is more significant in mean-field based calculations and shell-model calculations with a drastic truncation. In order to resolve the discrepancy in the Schiff moment of $^{199}$Hg among several nuclear models, we survey low-energy nuclear structure. The large-scale shell-model calculations reveal that the Schiff moment of $^{199}$Hg is considerably quenched in the second $ \frac{ 1 }{ 2 }^- $ state.

## Key claim

The large-scale shell-model calculations show that the Schiff moments of 129Xe and 199Hg are reduced by only about 10% from the independent particle model (IPM) evaluation due to configuration mixing, which is a much more moderate reduction than predicted by mean-field based calculations or drastically truncated shell-model approaches.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations for 129Xe and 199Hg using realistic effective interactions (SN100PN and SNV for 129Xe; Kuo-Herling for 199Hg) within valence spaces between magic numbers 50-82 for protons and 82-126 for neutrons. The Schiff moment induced by the P,T-odd πNN interaction was treated perturbatively using the one-particle one-hole approximation for the intermediate states, with the energy denominators approximated by single-particle energies from the spherical Nilsson potential.

## Key numbers

- The Schiff moment coefficients of 129Xe are reduced by factors of 0.8-0.9 from IPM results; the final LSSM results with SNV interaction are a0 = -3.8 × 10^-2 e fm^3, a1 = -4.1 × 10^-2 e fm^3, and a2 = -8.1 × 10^-2 e fm^3
- The Schiff moment coefficients of 199Hg are quenched by 4-12% from IPM evaluations; the final LSSM results are a0 = 8.0 × 10^-2 e fm^3, a1 = 7.8 × 10^-2 e fm^3, and a2 = 14.7 × 10^-2 e fm^3
- The second 1/2- state in 199Hg has Schiff moment coefficients more than one order of magnitude smaller than the ground state: a0 = -0.05 × 10^-2 e fm^3, a1 = 0.4 × 10^-2 e fm^3, and a2 = 1.3 × 10^-2 e fm^3
- The nuclear spin matrix elements of 199Hg are ⟨σνz⟩ = -0.322 and ⟨σπz⟩ = -0.006
- The spectroscopic factor for the neutron 2p1/2 orbital in the 199Hg ground state is S = 0.93, while in the second 1/2- state it is S = 0.05

## Context

This work extends earlier IPM calculations by Flambaum (1985-1986) and Dmitriev (2003-2005), and addresses discrepancies among mean-field based approaches including RPA, QRPA, and HFB calculations by Ban (2010). The results contradict the strong quenching (roughly one order of magnitude) predicted by RPA calculations by Dmitriev (2005) and pair-truncated shell model calculations by Yoshinaga (2013) and Teruya (2017). The finding that the second 1/2- state in 199Hg has a strongly quenched Schiff moment suggests that mixing between low-lying states could explain the model dependence, and enables future studies that include core-valence interactions explicitly.
