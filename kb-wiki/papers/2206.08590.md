# Inelastic nuclear scattering from neutrinos and dark matter

> Neutrinos with energy of order 10~MeV, such as from pion decay-at-rest sources, are an invaluable tool for studying low-energy neutrino interactions with nuclei -- previously enabling the first measurement of coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering. Beyond elastic scattering, neutrinos and dark matter in this energy range also excite nuclei to its low-lying nuclear states, providing an additional physics channel. Here, we consider neutral-current inelastic neutrino-nucleus and dark matter(DM)-nucleus scattering off $^{40}$Ar, $^{133}$Cs, and $^{127}$I nuclei that are relevant to a number of low-threshold neutrino experiments at pion decay-at-rest facilities. We carry out large scale nuclear shell model calculations of the inelastic cross sections considering the full set of electroweak multipole operators. Our results demonstrate that Gamow-Teller transitions provide the dominant contribution to the cross section and that the long-wavelength limit provides a reasonable approximation to the total cross section for neutrino sources. We show that future experiments will be sensitive to this channel and thus these results provide additional neutrino and DM scattering channels to explore at pion decay-at-rest facilities.

## Key claim

The paper claims that neutral-current inelastic neutrino-nucleus and dark-matter-nucleus scattering off ⁴⁰Ar, ¹³³Cs, and ¹²⁷I can be calculated using large-scale nuclear shell model calculations, with Gamow-Teller transitions providing the dominant contribution; however, the full multipole analysis is limited to the first 15 excited states for ⁴⁰Ar, so the total cross section estimate relies on the Gamow-Teller approximation, which is accurate only in the long-wavelength limit.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale nuclear shell model calculations using the BIGSTICK code, computing inelastic cross sections via two approaches: a full electroweak multipole operator analysis (using the SevenOperators Mathematica package) and a Gamow-Teller transition analysis in the long-wavelength limit using the Lanczos strength function method. For ⁴⁰Ar, they used the SDPF-NR interaction with a truncation limiting proton excitations to the pf shell to 4 and restricting neutrons to the pf orbits; for ¹³³Cs and ¹²⁷I, they used the jj55pna interaction in the 0g7/2, 1d5/2, 0h11/2, 1d3/2, 2s1/2 orbits. The cross sections were evaluated for neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering and dark-matter-nucleus scattering mediated by a dark photon.

## Key numbers

* For ⁴⁰Ar, the total inelastic neutrino-nucleus cross section from the Gamow-Teller analysis is around an order of magnitude lower than the free nucleon approximation of Bednyakov (2018) at neutrino energies above 10 MeV.
* The elastic CEνNS cross section for ¹³³Cs and ¹²⁷I is virtually the same due to their similar neutron number, while ¹²⁷I has a slightly higher inelastic cross section than ¹³³Cs.
* For ⁴⁰Ar, the multipole calculation includes transitions to the first 15 excited states, which is insufficient above Eν ≈ 10 MeV to capture all accessible transitions.
* The Gamow-Teller analysis for ¹³³Cs included transitions to 300 states, and for ¹²⁷I to 100 states, for an incident neutrino energy of 30 MeV.
* The dark photon production rate at the Spallation Neutron Source is ε² × 0.23 × 10²⁰ per day, mostly from π⁰ decay.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of inelastic neutrino-nucleus scattering, which had focused on charged-current processes or used approximations such as the continuum Random Phase Approximation (VanDessel 2019, 2020), the deformed shell model with only the first excited state (Sahu 2020), or the free nucleon approximation (Bednyakov 2018). The paper provides the first comprehensive calculation of neutral-current inelastic neutrino-nucleus scattering using the nuclear shell model with full electroweak multipole operators, and extends this formalism to inelastic dark-matter-nucleus scattering. These results enable future experiments at pion decay-at-rest facilities (COHERENT, CCM) to use inelastic scattering as an additional channel for neutrino and dark matter detection, and provide necessary background estimates for dark matter searches.
