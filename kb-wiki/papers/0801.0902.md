# An analytic study of the tricritical line in the U(Nf)xU(Nf) sigma model

> The tricritical line of the first-order chiral phase transition is investigated in the U(Nf)xU(Nf) sigma model by means of the ring improved one-loop finite temperature effective potential. To locate the tricritical line in the space of the coupling constants, we expand the effective potential up to third order in the high temperature expansion, and up to sixth order in the order parameter expansion. In this approximation, the tricritical line can be evaluated to the lowest order of the coupling constants and it follows an analytic relation between the tree-level masses of the scalar bosons. The validity of the high temperature expansion, the order parameter expansion, and the ring improved perturbation is critically examined. This result does not alter if one includes the massless fermions with the small Yukawa couplings.

## Key claim

The tricritical line of the first-order chiral phase transition in the U(Nf)×U(Nf) sigma model can be located analytically using a ring-improved one-loop finite-temperature effective potential, but only under the conditions that the high-temperature expansion, the order-parameter expansion, and the ring-improved perturbation theory are all valid; this restricts the analysis to the parameter region where the quartic couplings are small and the number of flavors Nf is large.

## Method

The author expanded the ring-improved one-loop finite-temperature effective potential to third order in the high-temperature expansion (up to O(M^3/T^3)) and to sixth order in the order-parameter expansion (up to O(phi^6/T^6)). The tricritical line was then identified by solving the conditions for a first-order phase transition (vanishing first derivative and equal minima) in the limit where the order parameter phi_c/T_c goes to zero, which forces the coefficients c_2 and c_4 in the expanded potential to vanish simultaneously. The resulting analytic relation between the tree-level masses of the scalar bosons was derived to lowest order in the coupling constants.

## Key numbers

* The tricritical line satisfies the analytic relation m_{h,cep}(phi_0) = 2^{1/4} * ((N_f^2 - 1)/(N_f^2 - 2))^{1/4} * m_xi(phi_0), where m_h and m_xi are the tree-level masses of the h and xi fields.
* The critical temperature at the tricritical line is T_2 = -m_Phi^2 / (b/12 - b_s^2/(16 pi^2)), where b_s = sum_i n_i a_i.
* The validity of the ring-improved perturbation theory at the tricritical line requires beta_{lambda_2/N_f} ~ 0.4 (not much smaller than 1) for large N_f, indicating the analysis is marginal.
* The high-temperature expansion is valid when the scalar boson effective masses satisfy M_{s,i}/T_c -> b_s/(4 pi) lesssim 1 at the tricritical line.

## Context

This work extends analytic studies of tricritical points in the U(Nf)×U(Nf) sigma model, which Pisarski and Wilczek (1983) discussed as a low-energy effective theory of QCD, showing that for Nf >= 3 the chiral symmetry restoration at finite temperature should be first-order. The technique of expanding the effective potential to sixth order in the order parameter was previously applied to study the tricritical point of QCD at finite temperature and density by Barducci et al. (1989) using the two-loop Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis effective potential, but that calculation required numerical evaluation; the present work provides a purely analytic derivation of a mass relation on the tricritical line. The result enables future comparisons with numerical simulations and non-perturbative studies of the tricritical line, and could be extended to phenomenological issues such as the QCD chiral phase transition or electroweak phase transition, though the infrared singularity of transverse gauge bosons remains a problem.
