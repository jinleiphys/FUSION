# The Generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn Integral and the Spin Structure of the Nucleon

> The spin structure functions g1 and g2 have been calculated in the resonance region and for small and intermediate momentum transfer. The calculation is based on a gauge-invariant and unitary model for one-pion photo- and electroproduction. The predictions of the model agree with the asymmetries and the spin sturcture functions recently measured at SLAC, and the first moments of the calculated spin structure functions fullfil the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn and Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rules within an error of typically 5-10 %.

## Key claim

The authors' Unitary Isobar Model (UIM) reproduces the measured spin asymmetries and structure functions from SLAC and satisfies the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn and Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rules to within 5-10%, but only when contributions from eta and multipion channels are included; the one-pion channel alone gives a zero-crossing of the generalized GDH integral at Q^2 = 0.75 (GeV/c)^2, which shifts to Q^2 = 0.45 (GeV/c)^2 when eta and multipion channels are added.

## Method

The authors used the Unitary Isobar Model (UIM), a gauge-invariant and unitary effective-Lagrangian model for one-pion photo- and electroproduction, to calculate the spin structure functions g1 and g2 in the resonance region up to center-of-mass energy W_max = 2 GeV and momentum transfer Q^2 up to 2 (GeV/c)^2. They modeled eta production via the S11(1535) resonance and approximated multipion contributions by scaling the helicity difference of the one-pion and eta channels according to the ratio of multipion to total transverse cross sections, using a monopole form factor F(Q^2) = 1/(1 + 1.05 Q^2) for the multipion Q^2 dependence.

## Key numbers

- The GDH sum rule prediction for the proton at Q^2 = 0 is I_1(0) = -0.804 (dimensionless), while the model integrated up to W_max = 2 GeV gives I_1 = -0.746, a difference of about 7%.
- The BC sum rule prediction at Q^2 = 0 is I_2(0) = 1.252, while the model gives I_2 = 1.338, a difference of about 7%.
- The zero-crossing of the generalized GDH integral I_1(Q^2) shifts from Q^2 = 0.75 (GeV/c)^2 (one-pion only) to Q^2 = 0.45 (GeV/c)^2 (including eta and multipion channels).
- The multipion monopole form factor parameter is alpha_npi = 1.05 (c/GeV)^2, providing the best fit to total inelastic cross section data for Q^2 < 1 (GeV/c)^2.
- The ratio R = sigma_L/sigma_T in the resonance region is approximately 0.06, but for single-pion production at W > 1.8 GeV and Q^2 > 0.3 (GeV/c)^2, R^(1pi) > 0.5.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological analyses of Burkert and Ioffe (1992) and Ioffe (1997) by providing a unitary, gauge-invariant model that explicitly includes eta and multipion channels, addressing the rapid evolution of the GDH integral from negative values at Q^2 = 0 to positive values at Q^2 > 1 (GeV/c)^2. The model enables comparison with upcoming Jefferson Lab experiments (Burkert et al., 1991) that will measure all four virtual photon cross sections in the resonance region, and it highlights the need for accurate multipion channel descriptions to determine the spin structure functions model-independently.
