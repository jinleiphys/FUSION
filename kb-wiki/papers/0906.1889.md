# RQM description of the charge form factor of the pion and its asymptotic behavior

> The pion charge and scalar form factors, $F_1(Q^2)$ and $F_0(Q^2)$, are first calculated in different forms of relativistic quantum mechanics. This is done using the solution of a mass operator that contains both confinement and one-gluon-exchange interactions. Results of calculations, based on a one-body current, are compared to experiment for the first one. As it could be expected, those point-form, and instant and front-form ones in a parallel momentum configuration fail to reproduce experiment. The other results corresponding to a perpendicular momentum configuration (instant form in the Breit frame and front form with $q^+=0$) do much better. The comparison of charge and scalar form factors shows that the spin-1/2 nature of the constituents plays an important role. Taking into account that only the last set of results represents a reasonable basis for improving the description of the charge form factor, this one is then discussed with regard to the asymptotic QCD-power-law behavior $Q^{-2}$. The contribution of two-body currents in achieving the right power law is considered while the scalar form factor, $F_0(Q^2)$, is shown to have the right power-law behavior in any case. The low-$Q^2$ behavior of the charge form factor and the pion-decay constant are also discussed.}

## Key claim

The paper shows that only the standard instant-form (Breit frame) and front-form (with $q^+=0$) approaches provide a reasonable basis for describing the pion charge form factor, while point-form and non-standard momentum configurations fail to reproduce experiment; the correct asymptotic QCD power-law behavior $Q^{-2}$ for the charge form factor requires two-body currents, whereas the scalar form factor already exhibits the right power-law behavior with a one-body current alone.

## Method

The authors solved a quadratic mass operator containing both confinement (string tension $\sigma_{st}=0.2$ GeV$^2$) and one-gluon-exchange interactions for the pion, using constituent quark mass $m_q=0.2$ GeV and pion mass $M_\pi=0.14$ GeV. They calculated the charge form factor $F_1(Q^2)$ and scalar form factor $F_0(Q^2)$ using a one-body current in different forms of relativistic quantum mechanics (instant form, front form, and Dirac-inspired point form) and compared results to experimental data. For the standard instant and front forms, they then examined the contribution of two-body currents to achieve the asymptotic QCD power-law behavior.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: $M_\pi = 0.14$ GeV; constituent quark mass: $m_q = 0.2$ GeV
- String tension: $\sigma_{st} = 0.2$ GeV$^2$; parameter $V_D = 0.5$ GeV; parameter $\Delta = -0.487$ GeV$^2$
- Effective strong coupling: $(8/3)\;g_{eff}^2/(4\pi) = 0.5$ for the low-energy regime
- Pion-decay constant: $f_\pi \simeq 93$ MeV (in the author's conventions)
- Asymptotic QCD prediction: $F_1(Q^2)_{Q^2 \rightarrow \infty} = 16\pi f_\pi^2 \alpha_s / Q^2$
- Wave function asymptotic behavior: $\phi_0(k) \propto k^{-7/2}$ up to log corrections; for coupling $(8/3)\;g_{eff}^2/(4\pi)=0.5$, $\phi_0(k) \propto k^{-3}$; critical regime at $(8/3)\;g_{eff}^2/(4\pi) = 2/\pi$
- Charge form factor measured up to $Q^2 \simeq 10$ (GeV/c)$^2$ (citing Bebek 1978, Amendolia 1986, Volmer 2000, Horn 2006, Tadevosyan 2007, Huber 2007)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of form factors in relativistic quantum mechanics for scalar constituents (Amghar 2002, Desplanques 2004) to the physically relevant case of spin-1/2 quarks in the pion. It addresses the failure of earlier RQM calculations (Cardarelli 1994, 1995) that required ad hoc quark form factors to match experiment, and it systematically compares different forms of relativistic quantum mechanics (Dirac 1949) and momentum configurations. The analysis of asymptotic behavior connects to QCD predictions by Brodsky (1975), Farrar (1979), and Lepage (1979), and the two-body current treatment follows earlier work on restoring asymptotic behavior in point-form approaches (Desplanques 2003). The results enable future work on quantitative aspects of two-body currents and their role in achieving the correct QCD power-law behavior for the pion charge form factor.
