# Relating the pion decay constant to the chiral restoration temperature

> We review the relationship between the pion decay constant $f_π$, the chiral symmetry restoration temperature $T_c$ and the phenomenology of low energy chiral symmetry breaking in view of the recent confirmation of the existence of a sigma meson with a mass of 600-700 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the chiral symmetry restoration temperature \(T_c\) is approximately \(2f_\pi \approx 180\) MeV, a relationship that holds when the sigma meson mass satisfies the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) relation \(m_\sigma = 2m_q\) and when all leading-order terms in the cutoff expansion are consistently kept, contrary to other studies that find \(T_c = \sqrt{2}f_\pi\) or \(T_c = f_\pi\).

## Method

The authors analyze the NJL four-fermion model at finite temperature using the temperature-dependent gap equation with a three-dimensional cutoff \(\Lambda\), and combine it with the quark-loop expression for the pion decay constant \(f_\pi\). They then extend the analysis to a chiral quark model (CQM) that dynamically generates the linear sigma model (LSM) Lagrangian, using dimensional regularization to avoid explicit cutoffs, and derive self-consistent relations for the quark mass, sigma mass, and meson-quark coupling. Finally, they draw an analogy to the BCS theory of superconductivity, comparing the BCS ratio \(2\Delta_0/T_c \approx 3.52\) to the Goldberger-Treiman ratio \(m_q/f_\pi = 2\pi/\sqrt{3} \approx 3.63\).

## Key numbers

- \(T_c = 2f_\pi \approx 180\) MeV for the chiral-limit pion decay constant \(f_\pi \approx 90\) MeV (Equation 19).
- Keeping the \(\mathcal{O}(m_q^4/\Lambda^2)\) term reduces \(T_c\) from 180 MeV to 172 MeV.
- The chiral-limit sigma meson mass is \(m_\sigma = 2m_q = (4\pi/\sqrt{3}) f_\pi \approx 650\) MeV (Equation 29b), consistent with the observed enhancement in the \(\pi^0\pi^0\) invariant mass around 650 MeV.
- The chiral-limit constituent quark mass is \(m_q = (2\pi/\sqrt{3}) f_\pi \approx 325\) MeV (Equation 29a), close to \(M_N/3 \sim 300\) MeV.
- The meson-quark coupling constant is \(g = 2\pi/\sqrt{3} \approx 3.6276\) (Equation 27b), consistent with the empirical \(\pi NN\) pseudoscalar coupling \(g_{\pi NN} \approx 13.08\) via \(g = g_{\pi NN}/3g_A \approx 3.55\) for \(g_A \approx 1.2573\).
- The BCS ratio \(2\Delta_0/T_c = 2\pi e^{-\gamma_E} \approx 3.52\) is compared to the relativistic Goldberger-Treiman ratio \(m_q/f_\pi = 2\pi/\sqrt{3} \approx 3.63\).

## Context

This paper extends the earlier work of Bailin, Cleymans, and Scadron (1985) and Cleymans, Kocic, and Scadron (1989), which proposed \(T_c = 2f_\pi\), and contrasts with subsequent studies by Contreras and Loewe (1990) and Klevansky (1992) that found \(T_c = \sqrt{2}f_\pi\) or \(T_c = f_\pi\) using the same NJL and LSM models. The authors argue that the recent experimental confirmation of a sigma meson with mass 600-700 MeV (KEK-E179 Collaboration, 1993) supports the NJL relation \(m_\sigma = 2m_q\) and thus the \(T_c = 2f_\pi\) result. This work enables a consistent description of low-energy chiral symmetry breaking and the chiral phase transition, linking the pion decay constant, the sigma mass, and the restoration temperature within a unified NJL-LSM framework.
