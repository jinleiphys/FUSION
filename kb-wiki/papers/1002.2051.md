# Surface-peaked effective mass in the nuclear energy density functional and its influence on single-particle spectra

> Calculations for infinite nuclear matter with realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions suggest that the isoscalar effective mass of a nucleon at the saturation density, m*/m, equals 0.8 +/- 0.1. This result is at variance with empirical data on the level density in finite nuclei, which are consistent with m*/m ~ 1. Ma and Wambach suggested that these two contradicting results may be reconciled within a single theoretical framework by assuming a radial-dependent effective mass, peaked at the nuclear surface. The aim of this exploratory work is to investigate this idea within the density functional theory by using a Skyrme-type local functional enriched with new terms, $τ(\mathbf{\nabla}ρ)^2$ and $τ\frac{dρ}{dr}$, where $τ$ and $ρ$ denote the kinetic and particle densities, respectively. We show that each of these terms can give rise to a surface peak in the effective mass, but of a limited height. We investigate the influence of the radial profile of the effective mass on the spin-orbit splittings and centroids. In particular, we demonstrate that the $τ\frac{dρ}{dr}$ term quenches the 1f5/2-1f7/2 splitting in 40Ca, which is strongly overestimated within conventional Skyrme parametrizations.

## Key claim

The paper shows that adding specific gradient-dependent terms to a Skyrme-type energy density functional can produce a surface-peaked isoscalar effective mass, but only of limited height; the term proportional to \(\tau\frac{d\rho}{dr}\) (variant B) reduces the overestimated \(1f_{5/2}-1f_{7/2}\) spin-orbit splitting in \(^{40}\)Ca, offering a possible path to reconcile infinite-matter and finite-nucleus effective masses without radically quenching the spin-orbit strength.

## Method

The authors start from the SkXc Skyrme parametrization and add either a \(\tau(\nabla\rho)^2\) term (variant A) or a \(\tau\frac{d\rho}{dr}\) term (variant B) to the local energy density functional. For each value of the new coupling constant, they readjust \(C^\tau_0\) to enforce a volume-averaged effective mass of unity, and in non-perturbative calculations also refit \(C^{\rho\prime}_0\), \(C^{\rho\prime\prime}_0\), and \(C^{\rho\Delta\rho}_0\) to masses and radii of \(^{16}\)O, \(^{40}\)Ca, \(^{56}\)Ni, and \(^{100}\)Sn. Self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculations are performed for these doubly-magic \(N=Z\) spherical nuclei, and the neutron spin-orbit splittings and centroids are computed as functions of the new coupling constants.

## Key numbers

* In \(^{40}\)Ca, the largest achievable surface peak in variant A gives \(m^*/m \approx 0.9\) in the interior and \(\approx 1.10\) at the surface; in variant B, the interior value is \(\approx 0.8\) and the surface peak reaches \(\approx 1.25\).
* The limiting coupling constants for \(^{40}\)Ca are \(C_0^{\tau(\nabla\rho)^2} = -280\) MeV fm\(^7\) (largest peak) and \(C_0^{\tau d\rho/dr} = 70\) MeV fm\(^6\) (largest peak).
* The SkXc functional misses the experimental \(1f_{5/2}-1f_{7/2}\) splitting in \(^{40}\)Ca (5.240 MeV) by more than 2 MeV; at \(C_0^{\tau d\rho/dr}=70\) MeV fm\(^6\) in perturbative calculations, the discrepancy drops below 1 MeV.
* In variant B perturbative calculations, the \(n=1\) spin-orbit splittings (e.g., \(1d\), \(1f\), \(1g\)) change by as much as 2 MeV across the full range of \(C_0^{\tau d\rho/dr}\).
* In non-perturbative calculations for variant B, masses of \(^{40}\)Ca, \(^{56}\)Ni, and \(^{100}\)Sn vary by less than \(\pm 1\%\), and the incompressibility modulus changes by less than \(\pm 5\) MeV (\(\pm 2\%\)) across the range.

## Context

This work extends the idea of Ma and Wambach (1983), who used a phenomenological one-body Hamiltonian with a surface-peaked effective mass to unify infinite-matter and finite-nucleus level densities. It contrasts with the approach of Farine et al. (2001), who added density-dependent terms to the Skyrme interaction rather than gradient-dependent terms to the functional. The results suggest that the surface-peaked effective mass from variant B may reduce the need for drastic quenching of the spin-orbit strength and strengthening of the tensor term, as previously required by Zalewski et al. (2008, 2009) to reproduce the \(1f\) splitting in \(^{40}\)Ca, and could enable a single functional to simultaneously describe single-particle properties and nuclear masses.
