# Five-body resonances of 8He using the complex scaling method

> The 0+ states of 8He are studied in a five-body 4He+n+n+n+n cluster model. Many-body resonances are treated on the correct boundary condition as Gamow states using the complex scaling method. The 0+_2 state of 8He is predicted as a five-body resonance in the excitation energy of 6.3 MeV with a width of 3.2 MeV, which mainly has a (p_{3/2})^2(p_{1/2})^2 configuration. In this state, number of the 0+ neuron pair shows almost two, which is different from the ground state having a large amount of the 2+ pair component. The monopole transition of 8He from the ground state into the five-body unbound states is also evaluated. It is found that the 7He+n component mostly exhausts the strength, while the 0+_2 contribution is negligible. The final states are dominated by 6He+n+n, not 4He+n+n+n+n. The results indicate the sequential breakup process of 8He to 7He+n to 6He+n+n by the monopole excitation.

## Key claim

The paper predicts a five-body resonance of 8He (the 0+_2 state) at an excitation energy of 6.3 MeV with a width of 3.2 MeV, but the abstract's claim that this state is "predicted" is qualified by the fact that the monopole transition strength into this state is negligible, making it difficult to observe via that channel; the state is identified through the complex scaling method applied to a five-body cluster model, not through direct experimental observation.

## Method

The authors employed a cluster orbital shell model (COSM) for the 4He+4n five-body system, treating 4He as an inert core and expanding the valence neutron wave functions in Gaussian basis functions. Many-body resonances were treated as Gamow states using the complex scaling method (CSM), which rotates coordinates by a complex angle to transform resonant boundary conditions into damping behavior, allowing diagonalization with L2 basis functions. The monopole strength function was calculated using the extended completeness relation (ECR) within CSM, decomposing contributions from bound, resonant, and continuum states including 7He+n, 6He+2n, 5He+3n, and 4He+4n channels.

## Key numbers

- The 0+_2 state of 8He is predicted at an excitation energy of 6.29 MeV with a decay width of 3.19 MeV.
- The ground state binding energy of 8He is obtained as 3.22 MeV from the 4He+4n threshold, compared to the experimental value of 3.11 MeV.
- For 6He, the 2+ state decay width is 0.132 MeV, agreeing with the experimental value of 0.113(20) MeV.
- The 7He ground state is located 0.40 MeV above the 6He ground state, agreeing with recent experiments of 0.44 MeV and 0.36 MeV.
- The matter radius of 8He is 2.52 fm (experimental: 2.49(4) fm); the charge radius is 1.92 fm (experimental: 1.929(26) fm).
- The (p3/2)^4 configuration dominates the 8He ground state with 86.0% mixing; the (p3/2)^2(p1/2)^2 configuration dominates the 0+_2 state with 96.9% mixing.
- The monopole strength into the 0+_2 state is negligible, with transition matrix elements of 1.78-i0.38 fm^4 for isoscalar and -0.003+i0.018 fm^4 for isovector transitions.
- The integrated monopole strength into 6He+n+n final states is 93.9% for isoscalar and 69.0% for isovector transitions; into 4He+4n final states it is 6.1% and 31.0%, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous COSM+CSM studies of 5-7He (Myo 2007, Myo 2009) to the five-body 8He system, successfully describing the 0+_2 resonance as a five-body Gamow state. The results contrast with ab initio Green's function Monte Carlo calculations (Pieper 2004) which used bound-state approximations and did not include continuum effects for unbound excited states. The prediction of a dominant sequential breakup process (8He to 7He+n to 6He+n+n) via monopole excitation, rather than direct five-body breakup, provides a framework for interpreting experimental data from the 6He(t,p)8He reaction reported by Golovkov (2009) and Coulomb breakup measurements by Iwata (2000).
