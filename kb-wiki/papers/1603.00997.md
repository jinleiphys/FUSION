# Axial U(1) symmetry in the chiral symmetric phase of 2-flavor QCD at finite temperature

> We discuss the fate of the axial U(1) symmetry in 2-flavor QCD at finite temperature, where the non-singlet chiral symmetry is restored. We first summarize the previous theoretical investigation on the relation between the eigenvalue density of the Dirac operator and the axial U(1) symmetry. We show that the eigenvalue density near the origin behaves as $λ^γ$ with $γ> 2$ in the chirally symmetric phase, where $λ$ is an eigenvalue. This implies that the axial U(1) symmetry is partially restored, so that the low energy symmetry of the theory becomes SU(2)$\otimes$ SU(2)$\otimes$ Z$_4$. Secondly, we report recent numerical investigations on this issue by lattice QCD simulations with lattice chiral fermions such as Overlap or improved domain-wall fermions. Our preliminary results indicate that the eigenvalue density seems to have a gap at the origin just above $T_c$, the temperature of the chiral symmetry restoration, which implies the axial U(1) symmetry is effectively restored at high temperature. We also point out an importance of the exact lattice chiral symmetry to obtain correct results on this issue.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in the chirally symmetric phase of 2-flavor QCD at finite temperature, the axial U(1) symmetry is partially restored such that the low-energy symmetry becomes SU(2)⊗SU(2)⊗Z₄, rather than fully restored to SU(2)⊗SU(2)⊗U(1)ₐ, based on the analytic result that the eigenvalue density near the origin behaves as λ^γ with γ > 2.

## Method

The authors perform an analytic analysis using the overlap fermion formulation with exact Ginsparg-Wilson lattice chiral symmetry, deriving constraints on the eigenvalue density of the Dirac operator from Ward-Takahashi identities under the assumption that non-singlet chiral symmetry is restored. They then present preliminary lattice QCD numerical results using overlap and improved domain-wall fermions on configurations with spatial size L ≈ 3 fm at T ≈ 190 MeV ≈ 1.05 T_c, comparing eigenvalue densities and U(1)ₐ susceptibilities from three different approaches: original (same fermion for sea and valence), partially quenched (overlap valence on domain-wall sea), and reweighted (full overlap).

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature: T_c ≈ 180 MeV, with simulations performed at T ≈ 190 MeV ≈ 1.05 T_c
- Spatial lattice size: L ≈ 3 fm
- Bare quark mass: m a = 0.0025 in lattice units, corresponding to m ≈ 6 MeV at T ≈ 190 MeV
- The analytic result constrains the eigenvalue density to behave as ρ(λ) ∝ λ^γ with γ > 2 in the chirally symmetric phase, specifically ⟨ρ(λ)⟩_m = ⟨ρ₃⟩_m λ³/3! + O(λ⁴) in the chiral limit

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Cohen (1996) who noted that U(1)ₐ symmetry is restored if the eigenvalue density has a gap at the origin, and of Aoki (2012) who previously derived constraints on the eigenvalue density. The paper addresses the ongoing controversy between lattice groups: JLQCD (2013) and TWQCD (2013) found U(1)ₐ restoration, while HotQCD (2012, 2014) and LLNL/RBC (2013) found violation. The results suggest that exact lattice chiral symmetry via the Ginsparg-Wilson relation is essential, and that partially quenched calculations can produce artificial enhancement of small eigenvalues. The work enables future studies of the chiral phase transition universality class, which the conformal bootstrap method (Nakayama, 2014) confirms may be second-order for either the Z₄ or U(1)ₐ case.
