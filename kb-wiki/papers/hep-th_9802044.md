# On the Local Finite Density Relativistic Quantum Field Theories

> It is found that, in addition to the conventional ones, a local approach to the relativistic quantum field theories at both zero and finite density consistent with the violation of Bell like inequalities should contain, and provide solutions to at least three additional problems, namely, 1) the statistical gauge invariance 2) the dark components of the local observables and 3) the fermion statistical block effects, base upon an asymptotic non-thermo ensemble. An application to models are presented to show the relevance of the discussions.

# On the Local Finite Density Relativistic Quantum Field Theories

## Key claim

A local approach to relativistic quantum field theory at finite density, consistent with Bell inequality violations, requires solving three additional problems beyond conventional treatments: statistical gauge invariance, dark components of local observables, and fermion statistical blocking effects, all based on an asymptotic non-thermo ensemble (ANTE) rather than the standard grand canonical ensemble (GCE). The claim is conditional on the assumption that the principle of locality in relativistic spacetime has not been properly addressed in existing finite-density field theories.

## Method

The author introduces a Lorentz 4-vector local field μ^α(x), called the primary statistical gauge field, to define local measurements of fermion number density via functional derivatives of the partition functional. The asymptotic grand canonical ensemble (AGCE) is defined as the GCE of the corresponding free system in the remote past when interactions are adiabatically switched off. The author then generalizes this to the asymptotic non-thermo ensemble (ANTE), where the initial ensemble of quantum states at zero temperature and density has negative energy states filled up to E = -ε and positive energy states filled up to E = ε, rather than the conventional Dirac sea filling. The formalism is applied to half-bosonized Nambu-Jona-Lasinio and chiral Gross-Neveu models in 3+1 and 2+1 dimensions, with effective potentials evaluated using modified integration contours in the complex p0 plane.

## Key numbers

- For a massless system in AGCE, the local fermion number density is given by ρ̄ = N_g μ^3 / 3π^2, where N_g is the total internal degrees of freedom besides spin.
- The second derivative of the effective potential with respect to μ at μ=0 is negative (∂²V_eff/∂μ²|μ=0 < 0) for σ ≠ 0 and spacetime dimension D ≥ 2, indicating instability of the σ ≠ 0 state with ρ̄=0.
- In the ANTE treatment, the effective potential for the σ ≠ 0 case yields two sets of minima: one with μ = ±μ_vac and ε = 0, and another with μ = 0 and ε = ±ε_vac, where ε_vac < σ_vac in all studied cases.
- The cluster decomposition approximation gives ρ_ΔΩ(σ̄,μ) = √(α/π) ∫_{-μ-σ̄}^{μ-σ̄} dσ' e^{-ασ'²} ρ_∞(σ̄+σ',μ), where ρ_∞(σ,μ) = g (μ²-σ²)^(3/2)/3π² and α is proportional to the spacetime resolution volume ΔΩ.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous developments on 8-component fermion field formalism (Ying, 1998) and dark components of local observables (Ying, 1997). It addresses problems encountered in lattice gauge theory studies at finite density, specifically the early onset of baryon number before chiral symmetry restoration noted by Barbour, Morrison, Klepfish, Kogut, and Lombardo (1998). The paper enables a framework for understanding dark components of local observables as analogous to Anderson localization in condensed matter, with self-generated random potentials from transient quantum fluctuations, and suggests that vacuum states of strong interactions may have diquark condensation phases relevant to the early universe, astronomical objects, and heavy ion collisions.
