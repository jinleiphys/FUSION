# Core meets corona: a two-component source to explain Lambda and anti-Lambda global polarization in semi-central heavy-ion collisions

> We compute the Lambda and anti-Lambda global polarization in semi-central heavy-ion collisions modeling the source as consisting of a high-density core and a less dense corona. We show that when more Lambdas than anti-Lambdas are produced in the corona, and this is combined with a smaller number of Lambdas coming from the core, as compared to those coming from the corona, an amplification effect for the anti-Lambda with respect to that of Lambda polarization can occur. This amplification becomes more important for lower collision energies and quantitatively accounts for the Lambda and anti-Lambda polarizations measured by the STAR beam energy scan.

## Key claim

The paper shows that modeling the source of Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons in semi-central heavy-ion collisions as a two-component system (a high-density core and a less dense corona) can explain the experimentally observed larger polarization of anti-Lambda relative to Lambda at lower collision energies, provided that more Lambdas than anti-Lambdas are produced in the corona and the number of Lambdas from the core is smaller than from the corona.

## Method

The authors model the collision source as a high-density core (QGP region) and a less dense corona (cold nuclear matter region). They compute the number of Lambdas from the core using a participant density model with a critical density threshold of 3.3 fm^-2 for QGP formation, scaling with the square of the number of participants. The number of Lambdas from the corona is computed using the energy-dependent Lambda production cross-section in p+p collisions integrated over the overlap region where participant density falls below the critical threshold. The intrinsic polarization from the core is obtained from spin relaxation times for s and anti-s quarks computed in previous work (Ayala, newtau), while the corona polarization is set to zero as a working approximation.

## Key numbers

- Critical density for QGP production: n_c = 3.3 fm^-2
- Proportionality factor for strange quark production in QGP: c = 0.0025 (intermediate value from range 0.001 to 0.005)
- Fit for w = N_anti-Lambda_REC / N_Lambda_REC in p+p collisions: w(sqrt(s_NN)) = A ln(sqrt(s_NN)) + B, with A = 0.237 +/- 0.007 and B = -0.40 +/- 0.03
- Fit for Lambda production cross-section in p+p: sigma_NN^Lambda(sqrt(s_NN)) = C ln(sqrt(s_NN)) + D, with C = 1.67 +/- 0.05 mb and D = -1.60 +/- 0.08 mb
- For sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV, the ratio N_QGP/N_REC becomes less than 1 for impact parameter b > 6 fm
- Formation time range used for Lambda and anti-Lambda in QGP: 1.5 fm < t < 4.5 fm
- Impact parameter used for comparison with STAR data: b = 8 fm (average for 20-50% centrality)

## Context

This work extends the two-component source model introduced by Ayala (Ayala2) to explain global polarization differences between Lambda and anti-Lambda measured by the STAR Beam Energy Scan (STAR-Nature, STAR2). It contrasts with explanations based on magnetic field effects (Hai-Bo, Liao3, Liao4) and different freeze-out conditions (Vitiuk). The calculation uses spin relaxation times from Ayala (newtau) and enables a quantitative description of the energy dependence of both Lambda and anti-Lambda polarization across the BES energy range.
