# Characterizing flow fluctuations with moments

> We present a complete set of multiparticle correlation observables for ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. These include moments of the distribution of the anisotropic flow in a single harmonic, and also mixed moments, which contain the information on correlations between event planes of different harmonics. We explain how all these moments can be measured using just two symmetric subevents separated by a rapidity gap. This presents a multi-pronged probe of the physics of flow fluctuations. For instance, it allows to test the hypothesis that event-plane correlations are generated by non-linear hydrodynamic response. We illustrate the method with simulations of events in A MultiPhase Transport (AMPT) model.

# Characterizing flow fluctuations with moments

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the full distribution of anisotropic flow coefficients $V_n$ in heavy-ion collisions can be characterized by a complete set of multiparticle correlation moments, measurable using only two symmetric subevents separated by a rapidity gap, and that these moments can test whether event-plane correlations arise specifically from nonlinear hydrodynamic response rather than from other sources.

## Method

The authors define moments of the form $\langle \prod_n (V_n)^{k_n} (V_n^*)^{l_n} \rangle$ and show they can be measured by replacing $V_n$ with flow vectors $Q_n$ measured in two symmetric subevents $A$ and $B$ separated by a pseudorapidity gap, with all $Q_{nA}$ factors on one side and all $Q_{nB}^*$ factors on the other. They validate the method using simulations from the AMPT model for Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV, comparing scaled moments and correlation measures to ATLAS data where available. They derive specific relations between mixed moments that test the hypothesis that $V_4$ and $V_5$ contain nonlinear components proportional to $V_2^2$ and $V_2 V_3$ respectively.

## Key numbers

- Scaled moments $m_n^{(k)} \equiv \langle v_n^{2k} \rangle / (\langle v_n^{2(k-1)} \rangle \langle v_n^2 \rangle)$ for $k=2,3,4$ are computed for $n=2$ and $n=3$ as a function of centrality in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV; for triangular flow $m_3^{(k)} \simeq k$ for all centralities, consistent with Gaussian fluctuations
- The correlation between $v_2^2$ and $v_3^2$ is small and negative ($\langle v_2^2 v_3^2 \rangle - \langle v_2^2 \rangle \langle v_3^2 \rangle < 0$) in AMPT calculations
- Equation (corrfluct1): $\langle V_4 (V_2^*)^2 v_2^2 \rangle / (\langle V_4 (V_2^*)^2 \rangle \langle v_2^2 \rangle) = \langle v_2^6 \rangle / (\langle v_2^4 \rangle \langle v_2^2 \rangle)$ is verified by AMPT for all centralities
- Equation (corrfluct2): $\langle V_4 (V_2^*)^2 v_2^4 \rangle / (\langle V_4 (V_2^*)^2 \rangle \langle v_2^4 \rangle) = \langle v_2^8 \rangle / (\langle v_2^4 \rangle^2)$ is verified by AMPT
- The subevent pseudorapidity gap used is 0.8 units, with subevent A covering $0.4 < \eta < 4.8$ and subevent B symmetric around midrapidity

## Context

This work extends the study of flow fluctuations beyond single-harmonic distributions (Aad 2013) and specific angular correlations between different harmonics (Aad 2014) by providing a complete moment-based framework. It builds on the cumulant method of Borghini (2001) and the subevent technique of Bhalerao (2013), and directly tests the nonlinear hydrodynamic response model of Teaney (2012, 2013) where $V_4$ and $V_5$ are decomposed into linear and nonlinear parts. The method enables future experimental tests of whether event-plane correlations are indeed driven by nonlinear response, using detectors with limited acceptance, and can be compared directly to theoretical calculations without unfolding procedures.
