# Proton Mass Decomposition from the QCD Energy Momentum Tensor

> We report results on the proton mass decomposition and also on related quark and glue momentum fractions. The results are based on overlap valence fermions on four ensembles of $N_f = 2+1$ DWF configurations with three lattice spacings and three volumes, and several pion masses including the physical pion mass. With fully non-perturbative renormalization (and universal normalization on both quark and gluon), we find that the quark energy and glue field energy contribute 33(4)(4)\% and 37(5)(4)\% respectively in the $\overline{MS}$ scheme at $μ= 2$ GeV. A quarter of the trace anomaly gives a 23(1)(1)\% contribution to the proton mass based on the sum rule, given 9(2)(1)\% contribution from the $u, d,$ and $s$ quark scalar condensates. The $u,d,s$ and glue momentum fractions in the $\overline{MS}$ scheme are in good agreement with global analyses at $μ= 2$ GeV.

## Key claim
The paper reports a lattice QCD decomposition of the proton mass into quark condensate, quark energy, glue field energy, and trace anomaly contributions, with the caveat that the quark and glue energy contributions are scheme- and scale-dependent (reported at $\overline{MS}$ 2 GeV) while the quark condensate and trace anomaly terms are renormalization group invariant.

## Method
The authors used overlap valence fermions on four ensembles of $N_f = 2+1$ domain-wall fermion gauge configurations from the RBC/UKQCD collaboration, covering three lattice spacings (0.083, 0.111, and 0.143 fm), three volumes, and several pion masses including the physical pion mass. They calculated the proton mass from two-point functions, the quark and gluon momentum fractions from three-point functions with non-perturbative renormalization in the RI/MOM scheme matched to $\overline{MS}$, and combined these with previously calculated quark scalar condensate contributions to obtain the full mass decomposition via the QCD energy-momentum tensor sum rules.

## Key numbers
- Quark energy contribution to proton mass: 33(4)(4)% in $\overline{MS}$ at $\mu = 2$ GeV
- Glue field energy contribution: 37(5)(4)% in $\overline{MS}$ at $\mu = 2$ GeV
- One quarter of the trace anomaly contribution: 23(1)(1)% based on the sum rule
- Quark scalar condensate contribution from $u$, $d$, and $s$ quarks: 9(2)(1)%
- Predicted proton mass at physical point: 0.960(13) GeV from chiral extrapolation
- Renormalized momentum fractions at $\overline{MS}$ 2 GeV: $\langle x \rangle_u = 0.307(30)(18)$, $\langle x \rangle_d = 0.160(27)(40)$, $\langle x \rangle_s = 0.051(26)(5)$, $\langle x \rangle_g = 0.482(69)(48)$
- Iso-vector momentum fraction $\langle x \rangle_{u-d} = 0.151(28)(29)$, consistent with CT14 global fit value 0.158(6)

## Context
This work extends the proton mass decomposition framework originally proposed by Ji (1994) and previously applied to mesons by Yang et al. (2014), providing the first fully non-perturbative lattice QCD calculation with controlled continuum and chiral extrapolations. The results for individual quark and gluon momentum fractions agree with phenomenological global fits such as CT14 (Dulat et al., 2015) and others summarized by Lin (2017), validating the lattice methodology. This enables future studies of the internal energy structure of nucleons and other hadrons from first principles, with the quark energy, gluon energy, and anomaly contributions found to be fairly insensitive to the pion mass up to 400 MeV.
