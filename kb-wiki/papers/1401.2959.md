# A new event generator for the elastic scattering of charged leptons on protons

> This paper describes a new multipurpose event generator, ESEPP, which has been developed for the Monte Carlo simulation of unpolarized elastic scattering of charged leptons on protons. The generator takes into account the lowest-order QED radiative corrections to the Rosenbluth cross section including first-order bremsstrahlung without using the soft-photon or ultrarelativistic approximations. ESEPP can be useful for several significant ongoing and planned experiments.

## Key claim
ESEPP is a Monte Carlo event generator for unpolarized elastic lepton–proton scattering that includes first-order QED radiative corrections (bremsstrahlung and virtual corrections) without relying on the soft-photon or ultrarelativistic approximations, making it suitable for experiments where lepton mass effects or hard photon emission are non-negligible. The generator outputs full kinematic parameters of all final-state particles, enabling realistic detector simulations.

## Method
The generator computes the Born (Rosenbluth) cross section using proton form factor parametrizations (dipole, Kelly, or Puckett) and adds the lowest-order QED radiative corrections: vacuum polarization, lepton and proton vertex corrections, two-photon exchange (box and crossed-box) amplitudes, and first-order bremsstrahlung from initial/final leptons and protons. Bremsstrahlung is treated exactly, without soft-photon or ultrarelativistic approximations, using the full kinematic equations for the process ℓ± p → ℓ± p γ. Event generation proceeds by sampling the lepton scattering angle, photon energy and angles, then solving the four-momentum conservation equations to obtain the scattered lepton and recoil proton momenta.

## Key numbers
- The dipole parametrization uses Λ² = 0.71 GeV² for the form factor G_D(q²) = (1 – q²/Λ²)⁻².
- For an illustrative example with E_ℓ = 1 GeV, –q² = 1 GeV², and ΔE = 0.1 GeV, the radiative correction factor (1 + δ⁻) for e⁻p scattering is 0.9794 (Mo and Tsai) or 0.9725 (Maximon and Tjon); the ratio (1 + δ⁺)/(1 + δ⁻) is 1.0203 (Mo and Tsai) or 1.0283 (Maximon and Tjon).
- Using the first-order formula (1 + δ) instead of exponentiation exp(δ) introduces a relative error in the extracted Born cross section of 0.02% (Mo and Tsai) or 0.04% (Maximon and Tjon) for e⁻p scattering at these kinematics.
- The conversion factor 1 GeV⁻² ≈ 389 microbarn is used for cross sections.

## Context
This work extends the standard radiative correction prescriptions of Mo and Tsai (1961, 1969) and Maximon and Tjon (2000) by providing a full event-level generator rather than analytic correction formulas. ESEPP is designed for ongoing and planned experiments including the Novosibirsk TPE experiment (VEPP-3), OLYMPUS at DESY, CLAS at JLab, the PRad experiment at JLab (E12-11-106), and the MUSE muon–proton scattering experiment at PSI. The generator also supports searches for a hypothetical light gauge boson A' via e±p → e±p γ background simulation.
