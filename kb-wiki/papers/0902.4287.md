# Direct photons at forward rapidities in high-energy pp collisions

> We investigate direct photon production in pp collisions at the energies of RHIC, CDF and LHC, at different rapidities employing various color-dipole models. The cross section peaks at forward rapidities due to the abelian dynamics of photon radiation. This opens new opportunities for measurement of direct photons at forward rapidities, where the background from radiative hadronic decays is strongly suppressed. Our model calculations show that photon production is sensitive to the gluon saturation effects, and strongly depends on the value of the anomalous dimension.

## Key claim

Direct photon production in pp collisions is enhanced at forward rapidities due to the abelian dynamics of photon radiation, and this enhancement makes forward-rapidity measurements a sensitive probe of gluon saturation effects, provided the anomalous dimension is larger than the critical value of 0.37.

## Method

The authors calculate direct photon production in pp collisions using the color dipole formalism, treating photon radiation as electromagnetic bremsstrahlung by a quark interacting with the target. The hadronic cross section is obtained by convolving the partonic cross section with the proton structure function F2p at a hard scale Q = pT. Several parametrizations of the dipole-proton cross section are employed: the Golec-Biernat-Wusthoff (GBW) model, a DGLAP-evolved version (GBW-DGLAP), the color glass condensate (CGC) model, the impact-parameter-dependent b-CGC model, and a non-saturated model (No Sat) with a fixed anomalous dimension.

## Key numbers

* At RHIC energy sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, the GBW model with charm quark uses parameters sigma0 = 23.9 mb, x0 = 1.11e-4, lambda = 0.287, with effective quark mass mq = 0.14 GeV.
* At Tevatron energy sqrt(s) = 1.8 TeV, the CGC model parameters are sigma0 = 35.7 mb, x0 = 2.7e-7, lambda = 0.177, with gamma_s = 0.63 and N0 = 0.7.
* The b-CGC model uses parameters N0 = 0.558, x0 = 1.84e-6, lambda = 0.119, gamma_s = 0.46, and B_CGC = 7.5 GeV^{-2}.
* The critical anomalous dimension for saturation effects is gamma_cr = 0.37.
* At LHC energy sqrt(s) = 14 TeV, the differential cross section as a function of rapidity is shown for fixed pT = 1, 3, and 5 GeV, with a pronounced forward peak in all saturated models.

## Context

This work extends the color dipole approach previously applied to Drell-Yan and direct photon production at midrapidity (Kopeliovich et al., 2001; Rezaeian et al., 2008) to forward rapidities, where the signal-to-background ratio for direct photons is significantly improved due to suppression of hadronic decay photons. The calculations use dipole cross sections fitted to DIS data, including the GBW model (Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff, 1998), the CGC model (Iancu, Itakura, Munier, 2004), and the b-CGC model (Watt and Kowalski, 2008). The results enable experimental searches for gluon saturation at LHC forward detectors and zero-degree calorimeters, accessing the smallest possible Bjorken x values in the target.
