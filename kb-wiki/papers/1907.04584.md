# Masses of ground and isomeric states of $^{101}$In and configuration-dependent shell evolution in odd-$A$ indium isotopes

> We report first precision mass measurements of the $1/2^-$ isomeric and $9/2^+$ ground states of $^{101}$In. The determined isomeric excitation energy continues a smooth trend of odd-$A$ indium isotopes up to the immediate vicinity of $N=50$ magic number. This trend can be confirmed by dedicated shell model calculations only if the neutron configuration mixing is considered. We find that the single particle energies are different for different states of the same isotope. The presented configuration-dependent shell evolution, type II shell evolution, in odd-$A$ nuclei is discussed for the first time. Our results will facilitate future studies of single-particle neutron states.

## Key claim

The first precision mass measurements of the 1/2- isomeric and 9/2+ ground states of 101In are reported, and the determined isomeric excitation energy continues a smooth trend of odd-A indium isotopes up to the immediate vicinity of the N=50 magic number. This trend can be confirmed by dedicated shell model calculations only if neutron configuration mixing is considered, and the authors find that single particle energies are different for different states of the same isotope, presenting configuration-dependent (type II) shell evolution in odd-A nuclei for the first time.

## Method

Isochronous mass spectrometry (IMS) at the CSRe storage ring at HIRFL in Lanzhou was used to measure revolution times of stored ions from projectile fragmentation of a 400.88 MeV/u 112Sn35+ beam on a beryllium target. The CSRe was tuned to an isochronous ion-optical mode with transition energy gamma_t = 1.302, and a time-of-flight detector with 19 microgram/cm2 carbon foil and 50 ps time resolution recorded timing signals sampled at 40 GHz. Mass calibration used 17 reference nuclides with well-known masses from AME'16 fitted with a third-order polynomial, and a systematic error of 6 keV was added to statistical uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Mass excess of 101In ground state (9/2+): -68550(13)(6) keV
- Mass excess of 101mIn isomeric state (1/2-): -67891(48)(6) keV
- Isomeric excitation energy Ex(1/2-): 659(50) keV
- Mass resolving power: m/delta m ~ 3.2 x 10^5 (FWHM)
- Minimum standard deviation of revolution time peaks: sigma_T ~ 0.5 ps
- Normalized chi_n for mass calibration: 1.363
- Transition energy of CSRe: gamma_t = 1.302
- Magnetic rigidity setting: B*rho = 5.3374 Tm
- Primary beam energy: 400.88 MeV/u
- Recording time per injection: 200 microseconds (~300 revolutions)
- Seven counts attributed to the isomer at mean revolution time ~5 sigma_T (~2.8 ps) larger than the ground state peak

## Context

This work extends the systematics of 1/2- isomer excitation energies in odd-A indium isotopes to the immediate vicinity of the doubly-magic nucleus 100Sn, where previous experiments using fusion-evaporation reactions (e.g., the 50Cr(58Ni,3p4n)101In reaction) had not observed the isomer. The results enable future studies of single-particle neutron states and provide a new example of type II shell evolution, previously confirmed in neutron-rich even-even 96Zr (Krebs et al., 2016) and odd-odd 70Co, now suggested in neutron-deficient odd-A indium isotopes. The shell model calculations using the V_MU plus spin-orbit force (M3Y) with the KSHELL code show that strong neutron configuration mixing in the nu 2d5/2 and nu 1g7/2 orbitals is essential to reproduce the experimental trend, and that the effective single particle energies of these orbitals differ between the 9/2+ ground state and 1/2- isomer within the same nucleus.
