# Deuteron electric quadrupole and octupole polarizabilities

> The direct transition-matrix approach to determination of the electric polarizabilities of quantum bound systems developed in my recent work is applied to study the electric multipole polarizabilities of a two-particle bound complex with a central interaction between the particles. Expressions for the electric quadrupole and octupole polarizabilities of the deuteron are derived and their values in the case of the S-wave separable interaction potential are calculated.

# Deuteron electric quadrupole and octupole polarizabilities

## Key claim

The paper derives expressions for the electric quadrupole and octupole polarizabilities of the deuteron using a transition-matrix approach, but the numerical results are calculated only for a central S-wave separable interaction potential (Yukawa formfactor) and its zero-range limit, which do not include the tensor n-p interaction or higher partial waves that are known to affect the dipole polarizability.

## Method

The author applies a direct transition-matrix (t-matrix) approach to derive a general formula for the electric multipole polarizability of a two-body bound complex with central interaction, expressed in terms of the bound-state wave function and a single partial component of the transition matrix at the negative binding energy. For the deuteron, the calculation is performed using an S-wave separable potential with Yukawa formfactor, with parameters fitted to the deuteron binding energy and either the triplet p-n scattering length or the asymptotic S-wave normalization of the deuteron wave function. The resulting expressions for dipole, quadrupole, and octupole polarizabilities are evaluated numerically.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: b(²H) = 2.224575(9) MeV (Ref. 17)
- Triplet p-n scattering length: ³a_pn = 5.424(3) fm (Ref. 18)
- Asymptotic S-wave normalization: A_S(²H) = 0.8845(8) fm^{-1/2} (Ref. 19)
- With Yukawa potential fitted to b(²H) and A_S(²H): α_E1(²H) = 0.6292 fm³, α_E2(²H) = 5.9462 fm⁵, α_E3(²H) = 156.26 fm⁷
- With Yukawa potential fitted to b(²H) and ³a_pn: α_E1(²H) = 0.6259 fm³, α_E2(²H) = 5.9129 fm⁵, α_E3(²H) = 155.38 fm⁷
- With zero-range interaction: α_E1(²H) = 0.3776 fm³, α_E2(²H) = 3.5247 fm⁵, α_E3(²H) = 92.529 fm⁷
- Experimental dipole polarizability from d-²⁰⁸Pb scattering: α_E(²H) = 0.70 ± 0.05 fm³ (Rodning et al., 1982)
- Experimental dipole polarizability from photoabsorption sum rule: α_E(²H) = 0.61 ± 0.04 fm³ (Friar et al., 1983)
- Dipole polarizability with separable tensor potential: α_E1(²H) = 0.6311 fm³ (Kharchenko, 1997)
- Dipole polarizability with realistic potentials: α_E1(²H) = 0.6328(17) fm³ (Friar and Payne, 1997)
- Deuteron quadrupole moment: Q = 0.2859(3) fm² (Reid and Vaida, 1972; Bishop and Cheung, 1979)

## Context

This work extends the transition-matrix approach previously developed by Kharchenko (2012) for electric polarizabilities of quantum bound systems, applying it specifically to higher multipole orders (quadrupole and octupole) of the deuteron. The method is presented as an alternative to the traditional spectral expansion of the Green's operator used by Friar and Fallieros (1984) and others, offering mathematical simplicity by avoiding calculation of continuum wave functions. The results enable future studies of anisotropic quadrupole polarization of the deuteron when tensor interactions are included, and extension to three- and four-body nuclear systems via Faddeev and Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations.
