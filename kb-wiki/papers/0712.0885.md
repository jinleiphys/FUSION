# Lightest scalar and tensor resonances in $γγ\toππ$ after the Belle experiment

> New high statistics Belle data on the $γγ\toπ^+π^-$ reaction cross section measured in the range of pion-pair invariant masses $\sqrt{s}$ between 0.8 GeV and 1.5 GeV are analyzed to clarify the current situation around the $σ(600)$, $f_0(980)$, and $f_2(1270)$ resonances in $γγ$ collisions. The present analysis shows that the direct coupling constants of the $σ(600)$ and $f_0(980)$ resonances to $γγ$ are small, and the $σ(600)\toγγ$ and $f_0(980)\toγγ$ decays are four-quark transitions caused by the $π^+π^-$ and $K^+K^-$ loop mechanisms, respectively. The chiral shielding of the $σ(600)$ resonance takes place in the reactions $γγ\toππ$ as well as in $ππ$ scattering. Some results of a simultaneous description of the $γγ\toπ^+π^-$ and $γγ\toπ^0π^0$ Belle data are also presented. In particular, the following tentative estimate of the $f_2(1270)\toγγ$ decay width is obtained: $Γ_{f_2\toγγ}(m^2_{f_2})\approx 3.68$ keV.

## Key claim

The direct coupling constants of the σ(600) and f0(980) resonances to γγ are small, and their decays to γγ are dominated by four-quark transitions via π+π− and K+K− loop mechanisms, respectively, rather than by direct couplings; the chiral shielding of the σ(600) resonance occurs in γγ→ππ as well as in ππ scattering.

## Method

The authors analyzed Belle data on γγ→π+π− cross sections for pion-pair invariant masses √s between 0.8 GeV and 1.5 GeV, together with Crystal Ball data on γγ→π0π0 for 2mπ < √s < 1.6 GeV. They constructed S-wave γγ→ππ amplitudes that satisfy unitarity and incorporate Born contributions modified by strong final-state interactions, using a parametrization of the ππ scattering amplitudes with mixed σ(600) and f0(980) resonances and nonresonant background from Achasov and Kiselev (2001). They added terms with direct coupling constants of the σ(600) and f0(980) to γγ and performed simultaneous fits to extract these constants.

## Key numbers

- The visible height of the f0(980) peak in the Belle γγ→π+π− cross section is about 15 nb over a smooth background of order 100 nb, with a visible width of about 30–35 MeV.
- The direct decay width of σ(600)→γγ from the fit is Γ(0)σ→γγ(m2σ) = 0.005 keV.
- The direct decay width of f0(980)→γγ from the fit is Γ(0)f0→γγ(m2f0) = 0.00007 keV, with mf0 = 0.972 GeV.
- The σ(600)→γγ decay width via the π+π− loop mechanism is about 1–2 keV for 0.4 < √s < 0.5 GeV.
- The f0(980)→γγ decay width via the K+K− loop mechanism, averaged by the f0(980) resonance mass distribution in the ππ channel, is about 0.15–0.2 keV.
- The tentative estimate of the f2(1270)→γγ decay width is Γf2→γγ(m2f2) ≈ 3.68 keV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of preliminary Belle data by Achasov and Shestakov (2006) on the f0(980) region, confirming the four-quark nature of the σ(600) and f0(980) resonances as predicted by the four-quark model of Achasov, Devyanin, and Shestakov (1982). The results contradict interpretations that neglect the rapidly changing K+K− loop production mechanism for f0(980), such as those in the Belle collaboration's own consistency checks (2007). The analysis enables future studies of light scalar meson production mechanisms in γγ collisions and clarifies the role of chiral shielding in these reactions.
