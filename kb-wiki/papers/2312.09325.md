# The effects of pseudorapidity-dependent observables on (3+1)D Bayesian Inference of relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> This proceeding highlights the effects of pseudorapidity-dependent charged hadron observables $dN^\mathrm{ch}/dη$ and $v_2^{\rm ch}(η)$ in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV on constraining the initial-state nuclear stopping for the beam remnants and the effective QGP specific shear viscosity in a recent Bayesian inference analysis using an event-by-event (3+1)D hydrodynamics + hadronic transport theoretical framework.

## Key claim

Including pseudorapidity-dependent charged hadron observables from PHOBOS in a (3+1)D Bayesian inference analysis significantly constrains the initial-state rapidity loss of beam remnants and the QGP specific shear viscosity at finite baryon chemical potential, even though these PHOBOS measurements carry large relative uncertainties.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian inference analysis using an event-by-event (3+1)D hydrodynamics + hadronic transport framework (3d-Glauber + MUSIC + UrQMD) to simulate Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV. They trained Gaussian Process emulators on 1,000 parameter sets with 1,000 minimum bias events each, then compared posterior distributions obtained from midrapidity STAR data alone against those obtained after stepwise inclusion of PHOBOS pseudorapidity-dependent charged hadron yield dNch/dη and elliptic flow v2ch(η) measurements.

## Key numbers

- Beam rapidity of colliding nucleons is y_beam = 5.36 at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- The most probable value of α_rem (fraction of beam remnant rapidity loss relative to colliding partons) is around 0.4–0.5 when all experimental observables are included
- PHOBOS v2ch(η) measurements constrain the QGP specific shear viscosity at μ_B ~ 0.2 GeV
- STAR midrapidity measurements reduce the 90% confidence region for QGP specific shear viscosity at μ_B = 0 by approximately half compared to the prior

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian analysis framework of Shen (2023) by quantifying the specific impact of PHOBOS pseudorapidity-dependent measurements on posterior constraints for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV. The results complement midrapidity measurements from the STAR Collaboration (STAR 2008, STAR 2017, STAR 2016) and are consistent with earlier model studies based on charged hadron dNch/dη in minimum bias p+p collisions (Shen 2022). The findings enable future combination of rapidity-dependent anisotropic flow measurements with collision energy dependence from the STAR Beam Energy Scan phase II program to obtain robust constraints on QGP transport properties at finite net baryon density.
