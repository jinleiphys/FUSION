# 1/Nc expansion and the spin-flavor structure of the quark interaction in the constituent quark model

> We study the hierarchy of the coefficients in the 1/Nc expansion for the negative parity L=1 excited baryons from the perspective of the constituent quark model. This is related to the problem of determining the spin-flavor structure of the quark interaction. The most general two-body scalar interaction between quarks contains the spin-flavor structures $t_1^a t_2^a, \vec s_1\cdot \vec s_2$ and $ \vec s_1\cdot \vec s_2 t_1^a t_2^a$. We show that in the limit of a zero range interaction all these structures are matched onto the same hadronic mass operator $S_c^2$, which gives a possible explanation for the dominance of this operator in the 1/Nc expansion for the L=1 states and implies that in this limit it is impossible to distinguish between these different spin-flavor structures. Modeling a finite range interaction through the exchange of a vector and pseudoscalar meson, we propose a test for the spin-flavor dependence of the quark forces. For the scalar part of the quark interaction we find that both pion exchange and gluon exchange are compatible with data.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the limit of a zero-range (contact) scalar interaction, all three possible spin-flavor structures of the two-body quark interaction (isospin-isospin, spin-spin, and spin-isospin) are matched onto the same hadronic mass operator \(S_c^2\), making it impossible to distinguish between them; however, a finite-range interaction introduces sensitivity to the spin-flavor structure through the sign of the ratio \(\tilde c_1/\tilde c_2\).

## Method

The authors match the most general two-body scalar quark Hamiltonian, containing operators \(t_i^a t_j^a\), \(\vec s_i \cdot \vec s_j\), and \(\vec s_i t_i^a \cdot \vec s_j t_j^a\), onto the \(1/N_c\) expansion mass operator for negative parity \(L=1\) baryons using \(S_3\) permutation symmetry. They derive expressions for the coefficients \(\tilde c_1\) and \(\tilde c_2\) in terms of orbital reduced matrix elements \(R_S\) and \(R_{MS}\), and show that for a contact interaction the relation \(R_S = -R_{MS}\) forces \(\tilde c_1 = 0\). For finite-range interactions, they model the spatial dependence as a contact term plus a Yukawa term from meson exchange, and compute the ratio \(\tilde c_1/\tilde c_2\) in the Isgur-Karl harmonic oscillator model.

## Key numbers

- Experimental determination from mixing angles \((\theta_{N1}, \theta_{N3}) = (0.39 \pm 0.11, 2.82 \pm 0.11)\) gives \(\tilde c_1 = 3.9 \pm 11.0\) MeV and \(\tilde c_2 = 129 \pm 18\) MeV.
- The ratio \(\tilde c_1/\tilde c_2 = 0.03 \pm 0.09\) from the same data.
- In the Isgur-Karl model with parameters \(m = 420\) MeV and \(\alpha = 467\) MeV, the allowed range for the exchanged boson mass is \(\mu = [0, 383]\) MeV for the OGE\(_\mu\) model.
- The ratio of reduced matrix elements \(r = (R_S + R_{MS})/(R_S - R_{MS})\) approaches \(r_\infty = -2/3\) for infinitely heavy exchanged mass.

## Context

This work extends the \(1/N_c\) expansion analysis of negative parity \(L=1\) baryons, previously developed by Carlson, Carone, Goity, and others (Carlson 1998), and by Schat, Goity, and Scoccola (Schat 2001, Goity 2002). It provides a dynamical explanation for the observed dominance of the \(S_c^2\) operator, complementing the earlier pion-exchange argument of Carlson (1998). The results enable future tests of the spin-flavor structure of quark interactions (one-gluon exchange versus Goldstone boson exchange) through precise measurements of the mixing angles and the sign of \(\tilde c_1/\tilde c_2\), and suggest that an effective gluon mass may be constrained by baryon spectroscopy.
