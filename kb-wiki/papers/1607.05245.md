# Nonlinear dynamics from the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the Friedmann-Lemaître-Robertson-Walker spacetime

> The dissipative dynamics of an expanding massless gas with constant cross section in a spatially flat Friedmann-Lemaître-Robertson-Walker (FLRW) universe is studied. The mathematical problem of solving the full nonlinear relativistic Boltzmann equation is recast into an infinite set of nonlinear ordinary differential equations for the moments of the one-particle distribution function. Momentum-space resolution is determined by the number of non-hydrodynamic modes included in the moment hierarchy, i.e., by the truncation order. We show that in the FLRW spacetime the non-hydrodynamic modes decouple completely from the hydrodynamic degrees of freedom. This results in the system flowing as an ideal fluid while at the same time producing entropy. The solutions to the nonlinear Boltzmann equation exhibit transient tails of the distribution function with nontrivial momentum dependence. The evolution of this tail is not correctly captured by the relaxation time approximation nor by the linearized Boltzmann equation. However, the latter probes additional high-momentum details unresolved by the relaxation time approximation. While the expansion of the FLRW spacetime is slow enough for the system to move towards (and not away from) local thermal equilibrium, it is not sufficiently slow for the system to actually ever reach complete local equilibrium. Equilibration is fastest in the relaxation time approximation, followed, in turn, by kinetic evolution with a linearized and a fully nonlinear Boltzmann collision term.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in a spatially flat FLRW universe, the non-hydrodynamic moments of the distribution function decouple completely from the hydrodynamic degrees of freedom, so the system flows as an ideal fluid while still producing entropy; however, the expansion is not slow enough for the system to ever actually reach complete local equilibrium, and equilibration is fastest in the relaxation time approximation, followed by the linearized and then the fully nonlinear Boltzmann collision term.

## Method

The authors recast the full nonlinear relativistic Boltzmann equation for a massless gas with constant cross section in a spatially flat FLRW spacetime into an infinite set of nonlinear ordinary differential equations for the energy moments of the one-particle distribution function. They then solve this moment hierarchy numerically for various initial conditions by truncation at a chosen order, and reconstruct the distribution function using a Laguerre polynomial expansion. The solutions are compared with those obtained from the linearized Boltzmann equation and the relaxation time approximation.

## Key numbers

- The normalized moments \(M_0(\hat{t})\) and \(M_1(\hat{t})\) are fixed to 1 for all times due to conservation laws and matching conditions.
- The dimensionless time variable is defined as \(\hat{t} = t / \ell_0\) where \(\ell_0 = 1/(\sigma_T n_0)\) is the mean free path at \(t=0\).
- The decay rate for the \(n\)-th moment is \(\omega_n = (n-1)/(n+1)\).
- The equilibrium distribution function is \(f_k^{\mathrm{eq}} = \lambda e^{-k/T_0}\), which is time-independent when expressed in terms of the covariant momentum magnitude \(k\).
- The temperature redshifts as \(T(t) = T_0 / a(t)\).
- The equilibrium energy moments are \(\rho_n^{\mathrm{eq}}(t) = \frac{(n+2)!}{2\pi^2} \lambda T^{n+3}(t)\).

## Context

This work extends the analytical approach of Bazow (2015), which first recast the relativistic Boltzmann equation in FLRW spacetime into moment equations and found an exact solution for a specific far-from-equilibrium initial condition. The present paper provides semi-analytical numerical solutions for arbitrary initial conditions and uses them to test the domain of applicability of the relaxation time approximation and the linearized Boltzmann equation, showing that both miss the transient high-momentum tails captured by the full nonlinear treatment. The moment equations are shown to be identical in form to those derived by Bobylev, Krook, and Wu (BKW) for a non-relativistic, non-expanding gas, despite the different underlying symmetries.
