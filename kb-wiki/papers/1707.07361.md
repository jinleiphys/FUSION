# Sudakov suppression of jets in QCD media

> We compute modifications to the jet spectrum in the presence of a dense medium. We show that in the large-$N_c$ approximation and at leading logarithmic accuracy the jet nuclear modification factor factorizes into a quenching factor associated to the total jet color charge and a Sudakov suppression factor which accounts for the energy loss of jet substructure fluctuations. This factor, called the jet collimator, implements the fact that subjets, that are not resolved by the medium, lose energy coherently as a single color charge, whereas resolved large angle fluctuations suffer more quenching. For comparison, we show that neglecting color coherence results in a stronger suppression of the jet nuclear modification factor.

## Key claim

The jet nuclear modification factor in a dense QCD medium factorizes into a quenching factor for the total jet color charge and a Sudakov suppression factor (the "collimator") that accounts for energy loss of resolved jet substructure fluctuations, but only at leading logarithmic accuracy and in the large-\(N_c\) approximation; neglecting color coherence results in a stronger suppression.

## Method

The authors compute the first-order (NLO) correction to the jet quenching factor from a quark-gluon pair formed promptly in the medium, using the large-\(N_c\) limit and soft-gluon approximation for medium-induced radiation. They identify the logarithmic phase space where the formation time of the splitting is shorter than the decoherence time, which is itself shorter than the medium length, and resum these contributions to all orders via a non-linear evolution equation for the collimator function, assuming small energy losses and fixed or running coupling.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic decoherence angle is \(\theta_c \sim (\hat q L^3)^{-1/2}\); for the numerical example, \(\theta_c \simeq 0.13\) with \(\hat q = 1\) GeV\(^2\)/fm and \(L = 3\) fm.
- The characteristic gluon frequency is \(\omega_c \equiv \hat q L^2/2\); for the numerical example, \(\omega_c \simeq 22.5\) GeV.
- The vacuum spectrum spectral index used is \(n = 6\).
- The Sudakov suppression factor for quarks at \(\pT > \omega_c\) exponentiates to \(\C_q(\pT,R) \simeq \exp\left[-2 \bar\alpha \ln(R/\theta_c) \left( \ln(\pT/\omega_c) + \frac{2}{3} \ln(R/\theta_c) \right) \right]\).
- The Sudakov suppression factor for quarks at \(\pT < \omega_c\) exponentiates to \(\C_q(\pT,R) \simeq \exp\left[-\frac{3 \bar\alpha}{4} \ln^2 \left( R^{4/3} \pT / \hat q^{1/3} \right) \right]\).

## Context

This work extends the single-parton energy loss formalism of Baier (2001) and the color coherence studies of Mehtar-Tani (2011, 2017) to the inclusive jet spectrum, showing that substructure fluctuations produce an additional Sudakov suppression beyond the quenching of the total color charge. It enables resummed predictions for the jet nuclear modification factor that can discriminate between coherent and incoherent energy loss models, and opens the way to refined studies of jet resummations in heavy-ion collisions, complementing Monte Carlo approaches such as those of Milhano (2015) and Casalderrey-Solana (2016).
