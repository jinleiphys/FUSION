# Frontiers in Nuclear Physics

> These are the proceedings of the symposium on ``Frontiers in Nuclear Physics'' held at the Physikzentrum Bad Honnef from June 18 to 20, 2009. The workshop concentrated on recent advances in the understanding of the nuclear forces, few-nucleon systems and related topics. Included are a short contribution per talk.

## Key claim
This is a symposium proceedings volume summarizing 17 talks presented at a workshop honoring Walter Glöckle, not a single research paper with a unified claim. The volume reports recent advances in nuclear forces, few-nucleon systems, and related topics as presented at the meeting, with each talk contributing its own specific findings.

## Method
The symposium was held at the Physikzentrum Bad Honnef from June 18-20, 2009, with 35 participants. Seventeen speakers presented results in half-hour presentations, and the proceedings contain a short contribution per talk with a description of contents and relevant references. The organizers chose this format over full-fledged proceedings to achieve speedy publication and avoid duplication of results already available on the archives.

## Key numbers
- Triton binding energy: 8.482 MeV (experimental); calculations with AV18+URIX give 8.479 MeV, AV18+TM' give 8.440 MeV, N3LO-Idaho+N2LO give 8.474 MeV (Kievsky et al., Table 1)
- 4He binding energy: 28.30 MeV (experimental); calculations with AV18+URIX give 28.48 MeV, AV18+TM' give 28.31 MeV, N3LO-Idaho+N2LO give 28.37 MeV (Kievsky et al., Table 1)
- Doublet n-d scattering length: 0.645 +/- 0.003 +/- 0.007 fm (experimental); calculations with AV18+URIX give 0.578 fm, AV18+TM' give 0.623 fm, N3LO-Idaho+N2LO give 0.675 fm (Kievsky et al., Table 1)
- Leading chiral four-nucleon force contributions to 4He binding energy: individual diagram classes contribute of order 1 MeV, with net contribution mostly below 500 keV for 4He; expected natural contribution of 200-300 keV per nucleon (Nogga)
- Lattice effective field theory calculations for 8, 12, and 16 neutrons in a periodic cube for density range from 2% to 10% of normal nuclear density (Lee)
- Photon energies of 60 and 120 MeV used in deuteron photodisintegration calculations with two-pion exchange currents (Golak)
- 14C beta decay lifetime: approximately 5730 +/- 30 years, with Gamow-Teller transition matrix element M_GT approximately 2 x 10^-3 (Kaiser)

## Context
This symposium proceedings honors Walter Glöckle's contributions to few-nucleon systems, nuclear forces, and relativistic quantum mechanics. The talks cover work extending chiral effective field theory approaches developed by Weinberg (1990) and Epelbaum (2006) to three-nucleon forces, four-nucleon forces, hyperon-nucleon interactions, and lattice simulations. The results enable improved ab-initio calculations of light nuclei and hypernuclei, and open new avenues for extracting neutron polarizabilities from Compton scattering on 3He targets, building on prior work by the Bochum-Cracow group and Phillips (2007).
