# Further signatures to support the tetraquark mixing framework for the two light-meson nonets

> In this work, we investigate additional signatures to support the tetraquark mixing framework that has been recently proposed as a possible structure for the two nonets, namely $a_0 (980)$, $K_0^* (800)$, $f_0 (500)$, $f_0 (980)$ in the light nonet, $a_0 (1450)$, $K_0^* (1430)$, $f_0 (1370)$, $f_0 (1500)$ in the heavy nonet. First, we advocate that the two nonets form the flavor nonet approximately satisfying the Gell-Mann--Okubo mass relation. Then we reexamine the mass ordering generated from the tetraquark nonets and show that this mass ordering is satisfied by the two nonets although the ordering in the heavy nonet is marginal. The marginal mass ordering however can be regarded as another signature for tetraquarks because it can be explained partially by the hyperfine masses calculated from the tetraquark mixing framework. The tetraquark mixing parameters are found to be independent of isospins giving additional support for the formation of the flavor nonets. In addition, we discuss the other approaches like two-quark pictures or meson-meson bound states, and their possible limitations in explaining the two nonets. As a peculiar signature distinguished from other approaches, we investigate the fall-apart coupling strengths into two vector mesons from our tetraquarks. Coupling strengths into the two-vector modes are found to enhance strongly in the heavy nonet while they are suppressed in the light nonet. The coupling ratios, which depend on the isospin channel, are found to be huge around $\sim 15$. This trend in the two-vector modes, which is opposite to that in the two-pseudoscalar fall-apart modes, can provide another testing ground for the tetraquark mixing framework. Some experimental evidences related to the phenomena are discussed particularly from the resonances belonging to the heavy nonet.

## Key claim

The two light-meson nonets (light: a0(980), K0*(800), f0(500), f0(980); heavy: a0(1450), K0*(1430), f0(1370), f0(1500)) can be described as tetraquark states formed by mixing of spin-0 and spin-1 diquark-antidiquark configurations, though the mass ordering in the heavy nonet is marginal and the tetraquark identification requires additional supporting signatures beyond those previously established.

## Method

The authors constructed two tetraquark types in the J^P=0+ channel: one from a spin-0 diquark with color-flavor structure (3̅_c, 3̅_f) and another from a spin-1 diquark with (6_c, 3̅_f). They diagonalized the 2×2 hyperfine mass matrix from the color-spin interaction to obtain mixed eigenstates identified with the physical resonances. They then calculated fall-apart coupling strengths into two vector mesons by recombining quarks and antiquarks in the wave functions, evaluating color, spin, and flavor recombination factors.

## Key numbers

- The Gell-Mann–Okubo mass relation for the light octet holds within 14 percent: M²[a0(980)] + 3M²[f0(500)] ≈ 4M²[K0*(800)]
- The Gell-Mann–Okubo mass relation for the heavy octet holds within 6 percent: M²[a0(1450)] + 3M²[f0(1370)] ≈ 4M²[K0*(1430)]
- Hyperfine mass splittings in the light nonet: ⟨V_CS⟩_{I=1} − ⟨V_CS⟩_{I=1/2} = 104.2 MeV; ⟨V_CS⟩_{I=1/2} − ⟨V_CS⟩_{I=0}(8_f) = 74.8 MeV
- Hyperfine mass splittings in the heavy nonet: ⟨V_CS⟩_{I=1} − ⟨V_CS⟩_{I=1/2} = 10.1 MeV; ⟨V_CS⟩_{I=1/2} − ⟨V_CS⟩_{I=0}(8_f) = 2.3 MeV
- Mixing parameters are approximately α ≈ √(2/3) = 0.8165 and β ≈ √(1/3) = 0.5774 across all isospin channels
- Coupling ratios into two-vector modes are found to be huge around ∼15

## Context

This work extends the tetraquark mixing framework previously proposed by Kim et al. (2016, 2017) for the two scalar meson nonets listed in the Particle Data Group review. The framework contrasts with the two-quark picture with orbital angular momentum ℓ=1, which can only produce one configuration in the J^P=0+ channel and cannot accommodate both nonets, and with meson-meson molecular pictures proposed for f0(980) and a0(980) by Janssen et al. (1994) and Branz et al. (2007, 2008). The predicted enhancement of fall-apart couplings into two vector mesons for the heavy nonet (opposite to the two-pseudoscalar fall-apart modes) provides a testable signature to distinguish the tetraquark mixing framework from alternative approaches.
