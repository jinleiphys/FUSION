# Variational Calculations of Nuclei with Low-Momentum Potentials

> Variational calculations of the deuteron and the triton illustrate that simple wave function ansätze become more effective after evolving the nucleon-nucleon potential to lower momentum (``$V_{low k}$''). This is consistent with many-body wave functions becoming much less correlated at lower cutoffs, as shown by two-particle wave functions and pair-distribution functions in nuclear matter. These results motivate a program to explore variational many-body calculations of binding energies and other low-energy nuclear properties using low-momentum potentials.

## Key claim

Variational calculations with simple wave function ansätze become dramatically more effective after evolving the nucleon-nucleon potential to lower momentum (cutoffs around 2 fm⁻¹ or less), because the many-body wave functions become much less correlated; the paper explicitly shows that for cutoffs above about 5 fm⁻¹ the simplest deuteron ansatz does not even bind, while at low cutoffs it reaches within a few keV of the exact energy.

## Method

The authors construct low-momentum potentials V_low_k from the Argonne v_18 potential using Lee-Suzuki transformations, which preserve two-body bound-state properties and phase shifts for relative momenta up to the cutoff. They perform variational calculations for the deuteron using two different momentum-space trial wave function ansätze (a Salpeter-inspired form and a sum-of-poles form) and for the triton using a truncated harmonic-oscillator basis in Jacobi coordinates, minimizing the energy with respect to variational parameters. For nuclear matter, they compute in-medium pair wave functions and pair-distribution functions within the Brueckner approximation at empirical saturation density, including the dominant effects of the corresponding three-body force as a density-dependent two-body correction.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron: the Salpeter ansatz yields a variational energy within less than 3 keV of the exact deuteron binding energy (E_d ≈ -2.2246 MeV) at cutoff Λ ≈ 1.5 fm⁻¹; for cutoffs above Λ ≈ 5 fm⁻¹ the same ansatz does not even produce a bound state.
- Deuteron: the sum-of-poles ansatz with n=2 gives a variational estimate about 5 keV above E_d at Λ ≈ 1.4 fm⁻¹ and is unbound above 6 fm⁻¹.
- Triton: for V_low_k with Λ = 1.8 fm⁻¹, the harmonic-oscillator basis with N_max = 6 gives a result 620 keV above the converged Faddeev result, dropping to 160 keV for N_max = 20; by comparison, the Reid soft-core interaction required N_max ≥ 12 to get a negative energy and N_max = 28 still gave E_t = -6.7 MeV (extrapolated to -7.3 ± 0.2 MeV).
- Nuclear matter pair-distribution function: at Λ = 1.9 fm⁻¹, g(r=0) decreases by only 0.1 when the three-body force is included, while at Λ = 3.0 fm⁻¹ the corresponding change is a factor of two larger.

## Context

This work extends the program of using renormalization-group-evolved low-momentum potentials (V_low_k) developed by Bogner, Furnstahl, and collaborators, which had previously shown perturbative convergence in nuclear matter. It directly contrasts with conventional variational Monte Carlo (VMC) calculations using the Argonne v_18 potential, which achieve accuracy only at the five percent level for light nuclei and require the more expensive Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) to evolve wave functions. The results motivate a program to reexamine variational many-body calculations using low-momentum interactions, potentially enabling simpler and more efficient calculations of binding energies and other low-energy nuclear properties, and opening the door to applying techniques from the Coulomb many-body problem to nuclear systems.
