# Intermediate-mass dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions at 200A GeV

> Through the analysis of HELIOS-3 data obtained at the CERN SPS, we demonstrate the importance of secondary processes for dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions in the intermediate invariant mass region. We find that while the dilepton spectra between 1 to 2.5 GeV from proton-induced reactions can be attributed to the decay of primary vector mesons, charmed hadrons, and initial Drell-Yan processes; the strong enhancement seen in the heavy-ion data as compared to the background comes mainly from the secondary processes which are germane to heavy-ion collisions. Furthermore, we find $πa_1\to l{\bar l}$ to be the most important process in this mass region, as was found by thermal rate calculations. We emphasize the constraints on the elementary cross sections by the experimental data from $e^+e^-$ annihilation.

## Key claim

The strong enhancement of dilepton yields in the intermediate invariant mass region (1–2.5 GeV) observed in central S+W collisions at the CERN SPS can be explained by secondary hadronic processes, most importantly πa₁ → l\bar{l}, without invoking quark-gluon plasma formation, though the authors note that the evaluation of the background itself is not entirely free of complications.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic transport model with initial conditions from RQMD string fragmentation to simulate the dynamical evolution of central S+W collisions at 200A GeV. Elementary cross sections for secondary processes (ππ, πρ, πω, πa₁, K\bar{K}, K\bar{K}* → l\bar{l}) were calculated using vector meson dominance and effective hadronic Lagrangians, with electromagnetic form factors constrained by e⁺e⁻ → hadrons data from experiments such as DM2. The dimuon spectra were integrated over the reaction volume and time, including HELIOS-3 acceptances, mass resolution, and normalization.

## Key numbers

- The intermediate-mass region studied is between 1 and 2.5 GeV (invariant mass of the dilepton pair).
- The πa₁ → l\bar{l} process is found to be the most important secondary process in this mass region, consistent with thermal rate calculations by Song (1994) and Kim (1996).
- The ρ(770) meson dominates the pion electromagnetic form factor, while the φ(1020) dominates the kaon form factor.
- Higher resonances such as ρ(1450), ω(1420), φ(1680), and ρ(1700) play important roles in the electromagnetic form factors for various processes.
- The natural width of higher resonances such as ρ(1450) and ρ(1700) is typically 200–300 MeV.
- Below 1.1 GeV, and especially from 0.4 to 0.6 GeV, the dropping vector meson mass scenario (with m_V* = m_V − 2/3 g_σ ⟨σ⟩) improves agreement with experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the transport model approach previously applied by Li and collaborators (1997) to low-mass dilepton and photon production, now addressing the intermediate-mass region where HELIOS-3 data show a strong enhancement over proton-induced reactions. The authors verify quantitatively the earlier thermal rate estimates of Song (1994), Haglin (1995), and Kim (1996), who identified πa₁ → l\bar{l} as the dominant hadronic process. The study enables future use of intermediate-mass dilepton spectra as a probe of the phase transition and QGP formation at higher energies such as RHIC, by providing a baseline for hadronic contributions that must be subtracted before QGP signals can be identified.
