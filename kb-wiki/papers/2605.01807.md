# Relativistic BDNK MHD Evolution in a Boost-Invariant Medium and Its Impact on Dilepton Production

> In this work, we explore a Bemfica--Disconzi--Noronha--Kovtun (BDNK)-type formulation of relativistic magnetohydrodynamics, providing a causal and stable first-order description of dissipative fluids. We derive coupled evolution equations for the temperature and magnetic field in a boost-invariant Bjorken background, restricting to $(0+1)$D dynamics while retaining all relevant first-order gradients. By varying the transport coefficients, we disentangle the interplay and mutual backreaction between the thermal and electromagnetic sectors. We find that, for comparable transport coefficients, the magnetic field responds more strongly to changes in the temperature evolution, while its feedback on the temperature remains subleading. We further analyze the number density evolution, which is sensitive to both temperature gradients and magnetic-field dynamics. We also investigate implications for dilepton production, where the magnetic field modifies the emission rate via the relaxation time in a kinetic-theory framework. The coupled evolution leads to a suppression of the low-mass dilepton spectrum, primarily driven by enhanced cooling in the presence of positive coupling between temperature gradients and magnetic-field evolution, as compared to scenarios without such feedback.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in a causal first-order BDNK magnetohydrodynamic framework with boost-invariant Bjorken expansion, the magnetic field responds more strongly to changes in temperature evolution than temperature responds to magnetic field feedback, and this coupled dynamics leads to suppression of the low-mass dilepton spectrum primarily through enhanced cooling when positive coupling between temperature gradients and magnetic field evolution is present.

## Method

The authors derive coupled evolution equations for temperature, magnetic field, and number density from the BDNK first-order gradient expansion of conserved currents in a (0+1)-dimensional boost-invariant Bjorken background with Milne coordinates, assuming the magnetic field is oriented along the y-direction. They numerically solve the resulting nonlinear ordinary differential equations while retaining only terms linear in time derivatives, systematically varying the 16 non-zero transport coefficients (parametrized with temperature scaling such as ε_i = ε_0i T^3, β_∥i = β_∥0i T, ν_i = ν_0i T^2) within the range (-1,1) to isolate feedback mechanisms between thermal and electromagnetic sectors. The dilepton production rate is then computed using a kinetic-theory framework where the magnetic field modifies the relaxation time.

## Key numbers

- Initial conditions: T_in = 600 MeV and B_in = 20 m_π^2 for the baseline evolution
- In the ideal limit (all first-order coefficients zero), temperature follows T(τ) ~ τ^(-1/3) and magnetic field follows B(τ) ~ τ^(-1), with deviations from ideal scaling at the level of O(10^(-7))
- The matter energy density is given by ε_m = 74.1 × (N_c^2)/(2π^2) × T^4 with N_c = 1, and entropy density s = 99.4 × (N_c^2)/(2π^2) × T^3
- Shear viscosity is set to η_⟂ = s/(4π), but shear effects are neglected by setting b = 0 in the temperature evolution

## Context

This work extends the BDNK first-order causal formulation of relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (Armas 2022, Hoult 2024) to study coupled temperature-magnetic field dynamics in a Bjorken expansion background, building on prior second-order MHD treatments (Panda 2020, Panda 2021, Denicol 2018, Denicol 2019). The results enable phenomenological assessment of how causal first-order MHD dynamics affect dilepton observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly the low-mass dilepton spectrum suppression driven by enhanced cooling from temperature-magnetic field coupling.
