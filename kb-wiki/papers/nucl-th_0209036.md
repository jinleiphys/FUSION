# Photon Exchange in Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> The strong electromagnetic fields in peripheral heavy ion collisions give rise to photon-photon and photon-nucleus interactions. I present a general survey of the photon-photon and photon-hadron physics accessible in these collisions. Among these processes I discuss the nuclear fragmentation through the excitation of giant resonances, the Coulomb dissociation method for application in nuclear astrophysics, and the production of particles.

## Key claim

Peripheral heavy ion collisions (PHIC) provide access to photon-photon and photon-hadron physics through the strong electromagnetic fields generated by the colliding nuclei, but the equivalent photon approximation (EPA) is only valid for processes involving single virtual photon exchange and fails for multiple virtual photon exchange processes.

## Method

The author presents a theoretical survey of electromagnetic processes in peripheral heavy ion collisions using the Weizsacker-Williams method (equivalent photon approximation), where the electromagnetic field of a fast charged particle is replaced by a flux of equivalent photons. The formalism is applied to calculate cross sections for Coulomb excitation and fragmentation, multiphonon giant resonances, atomic ionization, Bremsstrahlung, Delbruck scattering, and particle production via photon-photon fusion. For ultrarelativistic energies, the electromagnetic field is shown to approximate a delta-function interaction in the transverse plane, simplifying calculations of multiple photon exchange processes.

## Key numbers

- For relativistic Coulomb excitation of the 2-phonon (DGDR) state, the cross section is approximately 0.1 * S^2 / (A_P^(1/3) + A_T^(1/3))^2 mb, where S = 5.45 x 10^(-4) * Z_P^2 * Z_T * N_T / A_T^(2/3) mb
- The theoretical prediction for mutual Coulomb excitation cross section at RHIC is about 3 b, agreeing well with experimental results
- The DGDR contributes to only about 10% of the total fragmentation cross section induced by Coulomb excitation in PHIC
- For atomic ionization with naked projectiles, the cross section is sigma_I = zeta(3) * Z_P^2 * r_e^2 / (Z_T * alpha)^2 * [1.8*pi + 9.8*ln(2*gamma/(Z_T*alpha))]
- For Bremsstrahlung in a collider, the differential cross section is d_sigma_gamma/d_omega = 16*Z^6*alpha^3/(3*omega*A^2*m_N^2) * ln(gamma/(omega*R))
- The total cross section for Delbruck scattering (omega >> m_e) in colliders is sigma = 2.54*Z^4*alpha^4*r_e^2*ln^3(gamma/(m_e*R))
- For free e+e- pair production to leading order in ln(gamma): sigma = (28/(27*pi)) * Z_P^2 * Z_T^2 * r_e^2 * ln^3(gamma/4)
- For pair production with electron capture: sigma = 3.3*pi*Z^8*alpha^6*r_e^2/(exp(2*pi*Z*alpha)-1) * [ln(0.681*gamma/2) - 5/3]
- The cross section for para-positronium production at RHIC is about 18 mb

## Context

This review extends the earlier comprehensive reviews of peripheral heavy ion collisions by Bertulani and Baur (1988) and by Baur, Hencken, and Trautmann (2002), covering both intermediate energy fixed-target experiments (E_Lab approximately 100 MeV/nucleon) and ultrarelativistic collider energies at RHIC and LHC. The work surveys the theoretical framework for Coulomb dissociation as a tool for nuclear astrophysics, following the approach of Bertulani and Baur (1986) for reactions like 7Be(p,gamma)8B, and discusses multiphonon giant resonances studied experimentally at GSI. The paper enables further experimental investigations of photon-induced processes at relativistic heavy ion colliders, including meson production via photon-photon fusion and tests of QED in strong electromagnetic fields.
