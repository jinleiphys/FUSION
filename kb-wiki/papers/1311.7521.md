# Nuclear masses near N = Z from Nilsson-Strutinsky calculations with pairing corrections beyond BCS from an isospin-conserving pairing force

> A model with nucleons in a charge-independent potential well interacting by an isovector pairing force is considered. For a 24-dimensional valence space, the Hartree-Bogolyubov (HB) plus random phase approximation (RPA) to the lowest eigenvalue of the Hamiltonian is shown to be accurate except near values of the pairing force coupling constant G where the HB solution shifts from a zero to a non-zero pair gap. In the limit G -> infinity the HB + RPA is asymptotically exact. The inaccuracy of the HB + RPA in the critical regions of G can be remedied by interpolation. The resulting algoritm is used to calculate pairing corrections in the framework of a Nilsson-Strutinsky calculation of nuclear masses near N = Z for A = 24-100, where N and Z are the numbers of neutrons and protons, and A = N + Z. The dimension of the valence space is 2A in these calculations. Adjusting five liquid drop parameters and a power law expression for the constant G as a function of A allows us to reproduce the measured binding energies of 112 doubly even nuclei in this range with a root mean square deviation of 0.95 MeV. Several combinations of the masses for different N, Z, and isospin T are considered and the calculations found to be in good agreement with the data. It is demonstrated by examples how fluctuations as a function of A of the constant X in an expansion of the symmetry energy of the form T(T+X)/(2 theta) can be understood from the shell structure.

## Key claim

The Hartree-Bogolyubov plus random phase approximation (HB+RPA) provides an accurate approximation to the exact lowest eigenvalue of the isovector pairing Hamiltonian except near values of the pairing coupling constant G where the HB solution transitions from zero to nonzero pair gap; this inaccuracy can be remedied by interpolation, and the resulting algorithm allows calculation of nuclear masses near N=Z with a root mean square deviation of 0.95 MeV from measured binding energies of 112 doubly even nuclei.

## Method

The authors consider a Hamiltonian with nucleons in a charge-independent Nilsson potential well interacting via an isovector pairing force, using a valence space of dimension 2A (all single-nucleon states below the T=0 Fermi level plus equally many above). They calculate the lowest eigenvalue using HB+RPA with interpolation across critical regions of the pairing coupling constant G, then incorporate this into a Nilsson-Strutinsky calculation with a deformed liquid drop energy of the Duflo-Zuker form, fitting five liquid drop parameters and a power-law expression for G as a function of A to measured binding energies.

## Key numbers

- Root mean square deviation of calculated binding energies from measured values for 112 doubly even nuclei in the range A=24-100: 0.95 MeV
- Fitted liquid drop parameters: a_v = 15.19 MeV, a_vt = 110.8 MeV, a_s = 16.35 MeV, a_st = 135.3 MeV, a_c = 0.6615 MeV
- Best-fit pairing force coupling constant: G = 8.6 A^{-4/5} MeV
- The empirical trend line for the T=0 even-even-odd-odd binding energy difference is 2Delta = 24 A^{-1/2} MeV

## Context

This work extends the exact diagonalization calculations of Bentley and Frauendorf (2013) to larger valence spaces (dimension 2A versus 24 or 28), resolving the previous underestimation of the isorotational moment of inertia theta. The model builds on Neergaard's (2002, 2003, 2009) microscopic theory of superfluid isorotation and the Duflo-Zuker (1995) semiempirical mass formula. The results enable understanding of fluctuations in the Wigner X parameter from shell structure and provide a framework for calculating nuclear masses near the N=Z line with improved accuracy.
