# Hoyle-analogue state in 13C studied with Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics

> The cluster states in $^{13}{\rm C}$ are investigated by antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. By investigating the spectroscopic factors, the cluster configurations of the excited states are discussed. It is found that the $1/2^+_2$ state is dominantly composed of the $^{12}{\rm C}(0^+_2)\otimes s_{1/2}$ configuration and can be regarded as a Hoyle analogue state. On the other hand, the p-wave states ($3/2^-$ and $1/2^-$) do not have such structure, because of the coupling with other configurations. The isoscalar monopole and dipole transition strengths from the ground to the excited states are also studied. It is shown that the excited $1/2^-$ states have strong isoscalar monopole transition strengths consistent with the observation. On the other hand, the excited $1/2^+$ states unexpectedly have weak isoscalar dipole transitions except for the $1/2^+_1$ state. It is discussed that the suppression of the dipole transition is attributed to the property of the dipole operator.

## Key claim

The $1/2^+_2$ state in $^{13}$C is identified as a Hoyle-analogue state dominated by the $^{12}{\rm C}(0^+_2)\otimes s_{1/2}$ configuration, but its isoscalar dipole transition strength is unexpectedly small due to the property of the dipole operator, contrary to the expectation that such cluster states would have enhanced dipole transitions.

## Method

The authors performed antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations with the Gogny D1S nucleon-nucleon interaction, applying constraints on harmonic oscillator quanta ($N$, $\lambda$, $\mu$) up to $N=18$ ($9\hbar\omega$ excitation) to generate basis wave functions. After energy variation and parity projection, the basis states were projected onto angular-momentum eigenstates and superposed using the generator coordinate method (GCM) to obtain excitation spectra and eigen wave functions. Spectroscopic factors ($S$-factors) in the $^{12}{\rm C}+n$ and $^{9}{\rm Be}+\alpha$ channels were calculated using the Laplace expansion method to identify cluster configurations.

## Key numbers

- The $1/2^+_2$ state is located at 15.4 MeV excitation energy and has an $S$-factor of 0.64 in the $^{12}{\rm C}(0^+_2)\otimes s_{1/2}$ channel, identifying it as the Hoyle-analogue state.
- The ground state ($1/2^-_1$) has a matter rms radius of 2.52 fm, while the Hoyle-analogue $1/2^+_2$ state has a radius of 2.76 fm, compared to the Hoyle state in $^{12}{\rm C}$ at 2.94 fm.
- The $1/2^+_1$ state has an isoscalar dipole transition strength $M(IS1) = 0.95$ W.u. from the ground state, while the Hoyle-analogue $1/2^+_2$ state has unexpectedly small $M(IS1)$.
- The $1/2^-_2$ state at $E_x = 13.8$ MeV has $S$-factors of 0.11 and 0.09 in the $^{9}{\rm Be}(3/2^-_1)\otimes l=2$ and $^{9}{\rm Be}(5/2^-_1)\otimes l=2$ channels, respectively.
- The ground state has $S$-factors of 0.81 in the $^{12}{\rm C}(0^+_1)\otimes p_{1/2}$ channel and 0.94 in the $^{12}{\rm C}(2^+_1)\otimes p_{3/2}$ channel.
- The $1/2^+_2$ state has a reduced width of 0.96 MeV in the $^{12}{\rm C}(0^+_2)\otimes s_{1/2}$ channel.
- The four excited $1/2^-$ states collectively exhaust 24% of the energy-weighted sum rule for isoscalar monopole transitions.

## Context

This work extends the search for Hoyle-analogue states to $^{13}{\rm C}$, following theoretical studies of such states in $^{11}{\rm B}$ by Enyo and Suhara (2007) and in $^{13}{\rm C}$ using the orthogonality condition model by Yamada et al. (2015). The results contradict the expectation that $1/2^-$ and $3/2^-$ states would host Hoyle-analogue configurations with $p$-wave neutrons, instead finding that strong $\alpha$-$n$ $p$-wave interactions fragment these configurations. The identification of the $1/2^+_2$ state as a Hoyle analogue with suppressed isoscalar dipole transition provides a specific prediction for experimental verification using the decay patterns via $^{12}{\rm C}(0^+_2)+n$ and $^{9}{\rm Be}+\alpha$ channels, as discussed by Kawabata et al. (2010-2015).
