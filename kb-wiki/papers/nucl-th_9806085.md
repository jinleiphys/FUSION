# Microscopic calculation of transition intensities for vibrational bands and high-K isomers

> We investigate the effect of the Coriolis coupling and the residual interactions upon the inter-band transition rates for the vibrational bands and the decay of two-quasiparticle high-K isomers.

# Microscopic calculation of transition intensities for vibrational bands and high-K isomers

## Key claim
The authors show that a cranking-model-based method can calculate intrinsic parameters in the generalized intensity relation for inter-band electromagnetic transitions, but the agreement with experiment is conditional: for E1 amplitudes the absolute value of the intrinsic parameter Q_t is difficult to reproduce, and for high-K isomer decays the calculated hindrance factors are systematically too small unless residual spin-spin correlations are included.

## Method
The authors use the cranked random phase approximation (RPA) to describe vibrational bands and two-quasiparticle high-K isomers in even-even nuclei. They compute electromagnetic transition amplitudes using a formula that relates the cranking-model ω_rot-expansion to the I-expansion of the unified model, extracting the intrinsic parameters Q_t and q from microscopic calculations. For high-K isomers, they include Coriolis coupling up to fourth order (E2) and fifth order (M1), and introduce a spin-spin residual interaction (V_0 = 100 keV) diagonalized within a space of two-quasiparticle states up to 5 MeV.

## Key numbers
- For E2 transitions of the γ-vibration band in ¹⁶⁶Er, the agreement with experiment is described as "almost perfect" (no specific numerical deviation given).
- For the K^π = 6⁺ two-quasiparticle isomers in Hf isotopes, inclusion of the spin-spin residual interaction increases hindrance factors by 1–2 orders of magnitude for E2 transitions and by 2–3 orders of magnitude for M1 transitions.
- The effective charge used is e_π = 2e and the effective g-factor is g_s^eff = 0.7 g_s^free.
- The spin-spin interaction strength adopted is V_0 = 100 keV.

## Context
This work extends the cranking-model approach of Nakatsukasa and Shimizu (1996) to calculate transition matrix elements without full angular momentum projection. It applies the method to γ-vibrational bands and octupole vibrational bands, comparing with experimental data from Bohr and Mottelson (1975) for ¹⁶⁶Er and from Ward (1999) for ²³²Th. For high-K isomers, it addresses the Coriolis mixing mechanism studied earlier by the γ-tunneling model of Narimatsu, Shimizu, and Shizuma (1996). The results enable microscopic calculation of hindrance factors for K-forbidden transitions and show that residual correlations beyond the mean field are essential for quantitative agreement with experiment.
