# Efficient operators for studying higher partial waves

> An extended multi-hadron operator is developed to extract the spectra of irreducible representations in the finite volume. The irreducible representations of the cubic group are projected using a coordinate-space operator. The correlation function of this operator is computationally efficient to extract lattice spectra. In particular, this new formulation only requires propagator inversions from two distinct locations, at fixed physical separation. We perform a proof-of-principle study on a $24^3 \times 48$ lattice volume with $m_π\approx 900$~MeV by isolating the spectra of $A^+_1$, $E^+$ and $T^+_2$ of the $ππ$ system with isospin-2 in the rest frame.

## Key claim
The paper introduces a computationally efficient coordinate-space operator that projects two-pion correlation functions onto irreducible representations of the cubic group, requiring propagator inversions from only two distinct source locations at fixed physical separation. The method is demonstrated as a proof-of-principle study on a single lattice ensemble with heavy pion mass, and the authors note that the results are preliminary and that extracting phase shifts for higher partial waves pushes the limits of statistical precision.

## Method
The authors construct a two-pion operator with fixed spatial separation delta between the pions, then sum over the 48 rotations of the cubic group O_h weighted by the characters of each irreducible representation to project the correlation function onto the desired channel. The correlation function is computed using quark propagators from two source positions at x +/- delta/2, with the 48 rotations summed at the sink only, requiring just two propagator inversions per measurement. The energy levels are extracted from plateaus of the effective mass function log(G(t)/G(t+1)) for the A1+, E+, and T2+ representations of the isospin-2 pipi system in the rest frame on a 24^3 x 48 lattice with m_pi ~ 900 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Lattice volume: 24^3 x 48, lattice spacing a = 0.071 fm, pion mass m_pi ~ 900 MeV
- On-shell momenta for the three irreducible representations: p_{A1}L/(2pi) = 0.2455(36), p_E L/(2pi) = 1.047(13), p_{T2}L/(2pi) = 1.457(23)
- Scattering length parameters from effective range expansion: a_2 = -0.571 +/- 0.155 GeV^{-5}, a_4 = 0.114 +/- 0.100 GeV^{-9}
- S-wave phase shift at p = 0.177 GeV: delta_0 = -0.190 +/- 0.017
- Results collected from 376 configurations using 16 different source locations

## Context
This work extends the approach of constructing two-body operators with fixed spatial separation, similar to the "cube" source method of Berkowitz (2015), but improves computational efficiency by requiring only two source inversions per measurement. The method enables extraction of spectra for individual irreducible representations of the cubic group, which is essential for studying higher partial wave states in lattice QCD via Luscher's formalism (Luscher 1990, 1986). The technique can be extended to boosted frames and to variational analyses by varying the separation delta, building on prior work by Dudek (2010, 2012) and Beane (2011) on the isospin-2 pipi system.
