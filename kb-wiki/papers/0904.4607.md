# Final state polarization of protons in $pp \to pp ω$

> Model independent formulae are derived for the polarizations and spin correlations of protons in the final state of $pp \to ppω$, taking into consideration all the six threshold partial wave amplitudes $f_1, ..., f_6$ covering $Ss, Sp$ and $Ps$ channels. It is shown that these measurements of the final state spin observables, employing only an unpolarized beam and an unpolarized target, may be utilized to complement measurements, at the double differential level, suggested earlier [Phys. Rev. \textbf{C78}, 01210(R)(2009)] so that all the six partial wave amplitudes may be determined empirically.

# Final state polarization of protons in $pp \to pp\omega$

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates theoretically that all six threshold partial wave amplitudes ($f_1$ through $f_6$) for the reaction $pp \to pp\omega$ can be determined empirically without discrete ambiguities by measuring final-state proton spin observables (polarizations and spin correlations) using only an unpolarized beam and unpolarized target, complementing earlier measurements that required polarized beams.

## Method
The authors derive model-independent formulae for the polarizations $P_\alpha(i)$ of each final-state proton and their spin correlations $C_{\alpha,\beta}$ in terms of the six partial wave amplitudes $f_1,\ldots,f_6$ that cover the $Ss$, $Sp$, and $Ps$ channels. These new observables are expressed as bilinear combinations of the amplitudes (specifically $\gamma$, $\eta_1$, $\eta_2$, $\eta_3$) and are shown to determine the moduli and relative phases of $f_{23}$, $f_{23}'$, $f_{45}$, $f_{45}'$, and $f_6$ with respect to $f_1$, whose phase is set to zero. The analysis is carried out at the double differential level, using the same formalism as earlier work by the same group.

## Key numbers
- The $\omega$ meson production probes $NN$ interaction at short distances estimated at 0.21 fm for $\omega$ (compared to 0.53 fm for $\pi$ and 0.18 fm for $\varphi$).
- The $\omega \to \pi^0\gamma$ decay branching ratio is 8.92%, which is smaller than the dominant $\omega \to 3\pi$ mode but allows measurement of both vector and tensor polarizations.
- The unpolarized double differential cross-section is given by $\frac{d^2\sigma_0}{dW d\Omega_f d\Omega} = \frac{1}{768\pi^3}[a + 0.9\alpha_2\cos^2\theta + 9\zeta_2\cos^2\theta_f]$.
- The modulus of $f_1$ is determined directly from the beam-to-meson spin transfer: $|f_1|^2 = \beta_3$.
- The modulus of $f_{23}$ is given by $|f_{23}|^2 = \alpha_7$.
- The modulus of $f_{23}'$ is $|f_{23}'|^2 = \alpha_7 + 0.3\alpha_2$.
- The modulus of $f_{45}'$ satisfies $|f_{45}'|^2 = \zeta_0 + \zeta_2 = \zeta_5 - \zeta_4$.

## Context
This work extends the model-independent approach developed by Ramachandran et al. (2009) for $pp \to pp\omega$, which previously identified the six partial wave amplitudes and showed that measurements with a polarized beam could determine only the $Ss$ and $Sp$ amplitudes ($f_1,f_2,f_3$) while only partially constraining the $Ps$ amplitudes ($f_4,f_5,f_6$). The present paper shows that adding final-state proton polarization measurements from an unpolarized experiment resolves this ambiguity, enabling complete empirical determination of all six amplitudes. This is relevant for experiments at facilities such as WASA at COSY, which can detect $\omega \to \pi^0\gamma$ decays. The analysis supersedes an older approach by Ramachandran et al. that made simplifying assumptions and used only single-differential unpolarized cross-section data.
