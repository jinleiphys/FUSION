# Electroexcitation of nucleon resonances of the [70,1-] multiplet in a light-front relativistic quark model

> We utilize a light-front relativistic quark model to predict the 3q core contribution to the electroexcitation of nucleon resonances of the [70,1-] multiplet on the proton and neutron at Q2 < 5GeV2. The investigation is stimulated in large degree by expected progress in the studies of the electroexcitation of nucleon resonances in the third resonance region in the CLAS experiment. For the resonances N(1520)3/2-, N(1535)1/2-, and N(1675)5/2-, experimental data on electroexcitation amplitudes on the proton are available in a wide range of Q2. This allowed us to quantify the expected meson-baryon contributions to these amplitudes as a function of Q2.

## Key claim

The light-front relativistic quark model (LF RQM) predicts the three-quark (3q) core contribution to the electroexcitation of nucleon resonances in the [70,1-] multiplet, but for the resonances N(1520)3/2-, N(1535)1/2-, and N(1675)5/2- the experimental data reveal significant additional meson-baryon contributions that dominate at low Q2 and can be quantified by subtracting the LF RQM predictions from the measured amplitudes.

## Method

The authors computed the gamma* N -> N* transition helicity amplitudes using a light-front relativistic quark model formulated in the infinite momentum frame, where the matrix elements of the electromagnetic current are expressed through the wave functions of the nucleon and resonance in the vertices N(N*) <-> 3q, related to the center-of-mass wave functions via Melosh rotations of quark spin matrices. The spatial wave functions used two forms (exponential in M0^2 and exponential in squared quark momenta) with oscillator parameters and running quark masses previously determined by fitting nucleon electromagnetic form factors. For the N(1535)1/2- and N(1650)1/2- pair and the N(1520)3/2- and N(1700)3/2- pair, the authors incorporated mixing between the SU(6)xO(3) basis states using mixing angles revised from piN decay widths and compared with those from mass descriptions.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon wave function includes a 3q component with weight 0.95 and a piN component with weight 0.313, as determined from fitting nucleon electromagnetic form factors up to Q2 = 16 GeV2.
- Oscillator parameters: alpha1 = 0.37 GeV for the first spatial wave function form, alpha2 = 0.41 GeV for the second form.
- Running quark masses: m_q^(1)(Q2) = 0.22 GeV / (1 + Q2/56 GeV2) for the first wave function, m_q^(2)(Q2) = 0.22 GeV / (1 + Q2/18 GeV2) for the second.
- At Q2 = 5 GeV2, the running quark mass increases the transition helicity amplitudes by 25-35% for the first wave function form and 10-15% for the second.
- Revised mixing angles from piN decay widths: theta_S = -16.6 +/- 5 degrees, theta_D = 11.5 +/- 4 degrees, compared to earlier values of theta_S = -31.9 degrees and theta_D = 10.4 degrees.
- Weight factors c_N* for the 3q contribution: for N(1535)1/2-, c_N* = 0.84 (theta_S = -16.6 degrees) and 0.94 (theta_S = -32 degrees); for N(1520)3/2-, c_N* = 0.92 (theta_D = 6.3 degrees) and 0.94 (theta_D = 11.5 degrees).
- At Q2 = 0, the transverse helicity amplitudes for N(1520)3/2- on the proton: A1/2 = -20 +/- 5, A3/2 = 140 +/- 10 (in units of 10-3 GeV-1/2) from RPP estimates; the inferred meson-baryon contributions are -17 +/- 5 and -174 +/- 10 respectively.
- At Q2 = 0, the transverse helicity amplitudes for N(1535)1/2- on the proton: A1/2 = 115 +/- 15 (10-3 GeV-1/2) from RPP; the inferred meson-baryon contribution is -54 +/- 15.
- At Q2 = 0, the transverse helicity amplitudes for N(1675)5/2- on the proton: A1/2 = 19 +/- 8, A3/2 = 20 +/- 5 (10-3 GeV-1/2) from RPP; the inferred meson-baryon contributions are 16 +/- 8 and 15 +/- 5 respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous LF RQM studies by Aznauryan and collaborators (2012) on the electroexcitation of N(1520)3/2- and N(1535)1/2- to a comprehensive investigation of all resonances assigned to the [70,1-] multiplet on both proton and neutron targets. The predictions are motivated by upcoming CLAS experiment data on the third resonance region, including two-channel analyses of ep -> ep pi0 and ep -> e n pi+ processes, as well as deuterium target measurements. The inferred meson-baryon contributions are compared with the meson-cloud contributions from the EBAC/Argonne-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels analyses, and the results support the interpretation that meson-baryon effects dominate at low Q2 while the 3q core dominates above Q2 ~ 2-3 GeV2.
