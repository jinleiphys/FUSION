# Proving the CEP with compact stars?

> We present a model for hybrid compact stars composed of a quark core and a hadronic mantle with an abrupt first order phase transition at the interface and in accordance with the latest astrophysical measurements of two 2 M_sun pulsars. We demonstrate the possibility of a disconnected mass-radius sequence (third family) of high-mass pulsars as a distinct feature due to a large jump $Δε$ in the energy density of the first order phase transition setting in at $ε_{\rm crit}\approx 500$ MeV/fm$^3$ and fulfilling $Δε/ε_{\rm crit} > 0.6$. We conclude that the measurement of so called \textit{twin} compact stars at high mass (~ 2 M_sun) would support the existence of a first order phase transition in symmetric matter at zero temperature entailing the existence of a critical end point (CEP) in the QCD phase diagram.

# Proving the CEP with compact stars?

## Key claim
The existence of a disconnected third family of high-mass compact stars (twin stars at approximately 2 M☉) in the mass-radius diagram would support a strong first-order phase transition in symmetric matter at zero temperature, which would in turn imply the existence of a critical end point (CEP) in the QCD phase diagram. The claim is conditional: the third family appears only when the energy density jump Δε/εcrit exceeds 0.6, and the model assumes an abrupt first-order phase transition at the hadron-quark interface.

## Method
The authors construct a hybrid equation of state (EoS) combining the hadronic DD2 EoS for the mantle with a quark matter EoS parametrized by a constant squared speed of sound c²QM. The transition is modeled as a sharp first-order phase transition occurring at a critical energy density εcrit with a discontinuity Δε. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for nonrotating compact star configurations and search for stable sequences that satisfy the mass constraints from two precisely measured 2 M☉ pulsars.

## Key numbers
- Critical energy density for phase transition onset: εcrit ≈ 500 MeV/fm³ (with a specific model value of 485 MeV/fm³)
- Energy density jump condition: Δε/εcrit > 0.6 (specific model uses Δε = 0.67 εcrit)
- Squared speed of sound in quark matter: c²QM = 0.94
- Baryon density at quark matter onset: ncrit = 2.9 n₀, where n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Pressure at phase transition: P(εcrit) = 100 MeV/fm³
- Measured pulsar masses: 1.97 ± 0.04 M☉ for PSR J1614-2230 and 2.01 ± 0.04 M☉ for PSR J0348+0432
- Twin star radii: hadronic twin radius ≈ 13 km; hybrid twin radius ≈ 10.5 – 12 km

## Context
This work extends the concept of a third family of compact stars, originally discussed by Gerlach (1968) and Kampfer (1981), and builds on the hybrid star EoS parametrization of Alford (2013) and Zdunik (2012). It directly addresses the stringent mass constraints from the pulsar measurements of Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013). The result enables a potential observational test for the existence of a CEP in the QCD phase diagram, complementing lattice QCD results from Kaczmarek (2011) that show a crossover at zero baryon density. A microphysical study by Blaschke (2013) provides further support for the scenario.
