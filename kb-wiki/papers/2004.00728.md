# Radiative decay of $Ξ_b(6227)$ in a hadronic molecule picture

> The $Ξ_b(6227)$ baryon with the quantum number $J^P=1/2^{-}$ is considered as a molecular state composed of a $Σ_b$ baryon and $\bar{K}$ meson. The partial decay widths of the $Σ_b\bar{K}$ molecular state into $Ξ_bγ$ and $Ξ_b^{'}γ$ final states through hadronic loops are evaluated with the help of the effective Lagrangians. The partial widths for the $Ξ_b(6227)\toγΞ_b$ and $Ξ_b(6227)\toγΞ^{'}_b$ are evaluated to be about 1.50-1.02 KeV and 17.56-24.91 KeV, respectively, which may be accessible for the LHCb. Based on our results we argue that an experimental determination of the radiative decay width of $Ξ_b(6227)$ is important for the understanding of its intrinsic properties.

# Radiative decay of $\Xi_b(6227)$ in a hadronic molecule picture

## Key claim
The $\Xi_b(6227)$ baryon, assuming $J^P = 1/2^-$ and interpreted as a pure $S$-wave $\Sigma_b \bar{K}$ hadronic molecule, yields radiative decay widths into $\Xi_b \gamma$ and $\Xi_b' \gamma$ that are in the keV range and may be detectable at LHCb; however, the authors note that the strong decay widths alone cannot distinguish between a molecular and a conventional three-quark interpretation, making radiative decays a crucial test.

## Method
The authors compute the partial decay widths of the $\Sigma_b \bar{K}$ molecular state into $\Xi_b \gamma$ and $\Xi_b' \gamma$ via hadronic loop diagrams (triangle diagrams and a contact diagram) using effective Lagrangians. The coupling constant $g_{\Xi_b(6227)\Sigma_b\bar{K}}$ is determined from the compositeness condition, with the cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ varied between 0.9 and 1.1 GeV. The amplitudes include photon emission from the kaon meson, the $\Sigma_b$ baryon, and intermediate $K^*$ and $\Lambda_b^0$ exchanges, with gauge invariance enforced by including a contact term.

## Key numbers
- Mass of $\Xi_b(6227)^-$: $6226.9 \pm 2.0 (\text{stat}) \pm 0.3 (\text{syst}) \pm 0.2 (\Lambda_b^0)$ MeV, total width $\Gamma = 18.1 \pm 5.4 (\text{stat}) \pm 1.8 (\text{syst})$ MeV (from LHCb, 2018).
- Partial width $\Gamma(\Xi_b(6227) \to \gamma \Xi_b) = 1.38$–$1.14$ keV for $\Lambda = 0.9$–$1.1$ GeV.
- Partial width $\Gamma(\Xi_b(6227) \to \gamma \Xi_b') = 19.47$–$23.15$ keV for $\Lambda = 0.9$–$1.1$ GeV.
- Coupling constant $g_{\Xi_b(6227)\Sigma_b\bar{K}}$ varies from 2.68 GeV to 2.47 GeV over the same $\Lambda$ range.
- Coupling constants fixed from experiment: $g_{K^{*+}K^{+}\gamma} = 0.580$ GeV$^{-1}$, $g_{K^{*0}K^{0}\gamma} = -0.904$ GeV$^{-1}$; $g_2 = 0.252 \pm 0.013$ from $\Sigma_b \to \Lambda_b^0 \pi$; $g = 6.6$ for $K^*\Sigma_b\Xi_b^{(')}$ vertices.

## Context
This work extends the molecular interpretation of $\Xi_b(6227)$ previously studied by Huang (2018) for strong decays, and by Yu (2018) and Nieves (2019) who also considered a $\bar{K}\Sigma_b$ dynamically generated resonance. Alternative conventional three-quark interpretations were proposed by Chen (2018), Wang (2018), Aliev (2018), Chua (2018), and Cui (2019). The authors argue that since strong decay widths alone cannot distinguish between these pictures, radiative decay measurements at LHCb or Belle II could resolve the internal structure. No prior radiative decay calculations for $\Xi_b(6227)$ existed.
