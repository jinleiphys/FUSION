# Nucleon Scattering from Very Light Nuclei: Intermediate Energy Expansions for Transition Potentials and Breakup Processes

> Optical potentials for elastic p-d scattering and the coupled processes p+$^3$He $\rightarrow$ p+$^3$He and p+$^3$He $\rightarrow$ d+d are derived in the Faddeev-Yakubovsky framework with special emphasis on leading order terms, which are expected to be valid at intermediate energies. In addition, equations for the fragmentations $^3$He(p,ppp)n and $^3$He(p,pp)d are derived within the same framework. Again leading order terms for intermediate energies are considered.

## Key claim

The authors derive rigorous optical potentials and transition potentials for nucleon scattering from deuteron and $^3$He targets within the Faddeev-Yakubovsky framework, with the expectation that leading order terms in an iterative expansion will be valid at intermediate energies (above about 100 MeV), but they explicitly note that numerical studies are still needed to verify the quantitative validity of this truncation.

## Method

The authors start from the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations for three- and four-nucleon systems and decompose the resolvent operators into bound-state and continuum contributions. For p-d scattering, they separate the deuteron channel propagation from the three-body continuum and derive an optical potential that can be expanded iteratively. For p-$^3$He scattering, they introduce Yakubovsky components, sum pair forces within the three-nucleon subcluster to infinite order using a three-body T-operator, and separate the $^3$He and deuteron-deuteron (d-d) channels to obtain coupled integral equations for the transition amplitudes between the two-body fragmentation channels.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron binding energy is denoted $E_d$ and appears in the propagator $g_0({\bf q}) = 1/(E+i\varepsilon - E_d - \frac{3}{4m}q^2)$ for the p-d system
- The $^3$He binding energy is denoted $E_{He}$ and appears in the single particle propagator $g_0 = 1/(E+i\varepsilon - E_{He} - \frac{2}{3m}{\bf u}^2)$ for the p-$^3$He channel
- The d-d channel propagator is $g^{dd}_0 = 1/(E - 2E_d + i\varepsilon - \frac{1}{2m}{\bf v}^2)$ where ${\bf v}$ is the relative momentum of the two deuterons
- The overlap between the $^3$He state and its Faddeev component is $\langle \phi|\phi^F \rangle = 1/3$ for a normalized state
- The overlap between the d-d state and its Faddeev-like component is $\langle \phi_{dd}|\phi_{dd}^F \rangle = 1/2$

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev-Yakubovsky formalism, previously applied to the $\alpha$-particle bound state by Kamada and Gloeckle, to scattering processes involving four nucleons. It provides a rigorous alternative to the spectator expansion approach (Watson, KMT) and the full-folding model used by Brieva, Rook, and others for proton-nucleus scattering from $^{16}$O to $^{208}$Pb. The derived coupled equations for p-$^3$He and d-d channels enable future numerical studies using realistic NN forces without the inconsistency of using different forces for structure and scattering, and can serve as a testing ground for analyzing experimental breakup data on $^3$He(p,ppp)n and $^3$He(p,pp)d reactions.
