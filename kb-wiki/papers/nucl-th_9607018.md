# General introduction to Compton scattering

> Real and virtual Compton scattering has been and will be an important tool to study the structure of hadronic systems. We summarize the status of real Compton scattering and give an outlook at the new theoretical and experimental developments in the field of virtual Compton scattering.

# General Introduction to Compton Scattering

## Key claim

Real and virtual Compton scattering provide complementary tools for studying hadronic structure, but the full potential of virtual Compton scattering (VCS) to extract generalized polarizabilities depends on overcoming large Bethe-Heitler backgrounds and model-dependent theoretical inputs, with only 10–20% effects expected at energies around 1 GeV.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework for real Compton scattering (RCS) and virtual Compton scattering (VCS) off nucleons, covering low-energy theorems, dispersion relations, and multipole expansions. It compares predictions from constituent quark models, effective Lagrangian models, the linear sigma model, and heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory for generalized polarizabilities, and discusses experimental strategies for extracting these quantities from electron scattering data with Bethe-Heitler background subtraction.

## Key numbers

- Baldin sum rule: α + β = (14.2 ± 0.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm³, obtained from the integral of the total photoabsorption cross section over ω.
- Electric polarizability of the proton from dispersion analysis: α = 12 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (sum of real axis contribution 5 and half-circle contribution 7, in same units).
- Magnetic polarizability of the proton from dispersion analysis: β = 2 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (real axis 8, half-circle -6, in same units).
- Thomson cross section limit: dσ/dΩ = ½(1 + cos²Θ) r₀², with r₀² = (e²/m)² ≈ 24 nb/sr.
- Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule: I(0) = −e²κ²/(4πm²), relating the anomalous magnetic moment to the spin-dependent photoabsorption cross section difference.
- Expected VCS differential cross sections at MAMI energies: ~0.1 pb/(MeV sr²) for the polarizability-sensitive region.
- Expected VCS differential cross sections at CEBAF energies: ~1 pb/(GeV sr²) at backward and non-coplanar angles.
- High-energy scaling prediction: dσT/dt ~ nb/GeV² for s ~ 10 GeV², with cross section scaling as s⁻⁶ at constant t.

## Context

This review summarizes the status of Compton scattering as of 1996, extending the classical work on low-energy theorems by Gell-Mann, Goldberger (1954) and Low (1954), and the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule (1966). It provides the theoretical foundation for VCS experiments at MAMI (A1 collaboration, results reported by Andivahis (1995) and Roche (1996)) and CEBAF (proposal by Anderson (1993), scheduled for 1997), and for higher-energy studies at ELFE. The paper enables future work on generalized polarizabilities as a function of Q², connecting low-energy nucleon structure to parton wave functions at high Q².
