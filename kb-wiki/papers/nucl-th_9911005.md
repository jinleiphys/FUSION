# Antiproton annihilation on light nuclei at very low energies

> The recent experimental data obtained by the OBELIX group on $\bar{p}$D and $\bar{p}^4$He total annihilation cross sections are analyzed. The combined analysis of these data with existing antiprotonic atom data allows, for the first time, the imaginary parts of the S-wave scattering lengths for the two nuclei to be extracted. The obtained values are: $Im a^{sc}_0 = [- 0.62 \pm 0.02 ({stat}) \pm 0.04 ({sys})] fm$ for $\bar{p}$D and $Im a^{sc}_0 = [- 0.36\pm 0.03({stat})^{+0.19}_{-0.11}({sys})] fm$ for $\bar{p}^4$He. This analysis indicates an unexpected behaviour of the imaginary part of the $\bar{p}$-nucleus S-wave scattering length as a function of the atomic weight A: $|Im a^{sc}_0|$ ($\bar{p}$p) > $|Im a^{sc}_0|$ ($\bar{p}$D) > $|Im a^{sc}_0|$ ($\bar{p}^4$He).

## Key claim

The combined analysis of antiproton-deuteron and antiproton-helium-4 in-flight annihilation data with antiprotonic atom data allows, for the first time, the imaginary parts of the S-wave scattering lengths for these two nuclei to be extracted; the results indicate an unexpected decreasing trend of the absolute value of the imaginary part of the antiproton-nucleus S-wave scattering length as a function of atomic weight A, contrary to a naive geometrical expectation that it should increase with nucleus size.

## Method

The authors performed a combined analysis of total annihilation cross section data from the OBELIX group for antiproton-deuteron and antiproton-helium-4 at incident momenta below 200 MeV/c, together with shift and width measurements of antiprotonic atom levels (1S for deuterium, 2P for both systems). Using the scattering length approximation for systems with Coulomb attraction, they fixed the P-wave scattering parameters from atomic data and the real part of the S-wave scattering length from atomic data, then performed a fit to the annihilation cross section data with only the imaginary part of the S-wave scattering length as a free parameter.

## Key numbers

* Imaginary part of the antiproton-deuteron S-wave scattering length: Im a0^sc = [-0.62 ± 0.02 (stat) ± 0.05 (sys)] fm, obtained from a combined fit of in-flight annihilation data and atomic data with a chi-squared of 8.3 per point.
* Imaginary part of the antiproton-helium-4 S-wave scattering length: Im a0^sc = [-0.36 ± 0.03 (stat) +0.19/-0.11 (sys)] fm, obtained from a combined fit with a chi-squared of 1.8 per point.
* Imaginary part of the antiproton-proton S-wave scattering length: Im a0^sc = -[0.69 ± 0.01 (stat) ± 0.03 (sys)] fm, extracted from in-flight annihilation data and in agreement with antiprotonic atom data giving Im a0^sc = -(0.694 ± 0.027) fm.
* Imaginary part of the antiproton-proton P-wave scattering volume: Im a1^sc = -[0.75 ± 0.05 (stat) ± 0.04 (sys)] fm^3 from in-flight data, in agreement with a direct atomic measurement giving Im a1^sc = -(0.77 ± 0.06) fm^3.
* Antiproton-deuteron 1S atomic level shift and width: ΔE1S + i(Γ1S/2) = [-(1.05 ± 0.25) + i(0.55 ± 0.37)] keV.
* Antiproton-helium-4 2P atomic level width: Γ2P = (45 ± 5) × 10^3 meV, corresponding to Im a1^sc = -(4.4 ± 0.5) fm^3.

## Context

This work extends the combined analysis approach previously applied to the antiproton-proton system by Carbonell et al. (1997) to the antiproton-deuteron and antiproton-helium-4 systems, using data from Zenoni et al. (1999) and Augsburger et al. (1999). The results contradict a naive geometrical model where the antiproton-deuteron scattering length would be approximately the sum of the antiproton-proton and antiproton-neutron scattering lengths (the latter being Im a0 = -[0.83 ± 0.07] fm from Mutchler et al.). The findings enable future measurements at the CERN AD facility to confirm this unexpected decreasing trend of the scattering length with atomic weight and to extend the study to heavier nuclei.
