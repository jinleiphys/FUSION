# Light scalars in neutron star mergers

> Due to their unique set of multimessenger signals, neutron star mergers have emerged as a novel environment for studies of new physics beyond the Standard Model (SM). As a case study, we consider the simplest extension of the SM scalar sector involving a light CP-even scalar singlet $S$ mixing with the SM Higgs boson. These $S$ particles can be produced abundantly in neutron star mergers via the nucleon bremsstrahlung process. We show that the $S$ particles may either be trapped in or stream freely out of the merger remnant, depending on the $S$ mass, its mixing with the SM Higgs boson, and the temperature and baryon density in the merger. In the free-streaming region, the scalar $S$ will provide an extra channel to cool down the merger remnant, with cooling timescales as small as ${\cal O}$(ms). On the other hand, in the trapped region, the Bose gas of $S$ particles could contribute a larger thermal conductivity than the trapped neutrinos in some parts of the parameter space, thus leading to faster thermal equilibration than expected. Therefore, future observations of the early postmerger phase of a neutron star merger could effectively probe a unique range of the $S$ parameter space, largely complementary to the existing and future laboratory and supernova limits. In view of these results, we hope the merger simulation community will be motivated to implement the effects of light CP-even scalars into their simulations in both the free-streaming and trapped regimes.

## Key claim

Light CP-even scalar particles produced in neutron star mergers via nucleon bremsstrahlung can either be trapped or free-stream out of the merger remnant depending on the scalar mass, its mixing with the SM Higgs boson, and the local temperature and baryon density; in the trapped regime, the scalar Bose gas can contribute a larger thermal conductivity than trapped neutrinos in some parts of parameter space, while in the free-streaming regime, scalars provide an extra cooling channel with millisecond timescales.

## Method

The authors calculated the mean free path (MFP) of a light CP-even scalar singlet S in hot, dense nuclear matter by evaluating the absorption rate from inverse nucleon bremsstrahlung processes (N+N'+S → N+N') using a one-pion-exchange approximation for the strong interaction matrix elements, combined with the scalar decay rates into SM particles. The nuclear matter was modeled with the IUF relativistic mean field equation of state, assuming degenerate Fermi gases of neutrons, protons, electrons, muons, and neutrinos at fixed lepton fractions. The scalar production rate and emissivity were computed from the same bremsstrahlung process, and the average scalar energy was used to evaluate the MFP at given thermodynamic conditions.

## Key numbers

- Cooling timescales as small as O(1 ms) to O(10 ms) are possible for free-streaming scalars in parameter regions allowed by current laboratory constraints.
- The scalar MFP can range from hundreds of kilometers to below one meter depending on thermodynamic conditions, for a particular choice of mixing angle sinθ.
- The supernova SN1987A limits exclude mixing angles sinθ from roughly 5.9×10⁻⁷ to 7.0×10⁻⁶ with scalar mass up to 148 MeV, assuming a neutrino luminosity of 3×10⁵³ erg/sec.
- At nuclear saturation density n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, the Dirac effective mass m* ≈ 560 MeV, dropping to m* ≈ 185 MeV at n_B = 5n₀.
- The average energy of produced scalars in the low-mass limit is ⟨E_S⟩ ≈ 3T, independent of density in strongly degenerate nuclear matter.
- The nucleon-pion coupling f ≈ 1 and α_π ≈ 15 (using vacuum nucleon and pion masses).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of axions and axion-like particles in neutron star mergers (Harris 2020, Dietrich 2019) to the case of CP-even scalars, which have qualitatively different interactions with nucleons and pions and are not ruled out by SN1987A constraints in the trapped regime. The authors find that unlike QCD axions, light CP-even scalars can be trapped in merger remnants in parameter space still allowed by laboratory and supernova limits, potentially affecting thermal equilibration. The paper calls for the merger simulation community to implement these scalar effects in numerical simulations, as future multimessenger observations of the early postmerger phase could probe parameter space complementary to existing and future laboratory experiments such as DUNE, FASER 2, and SBN.
