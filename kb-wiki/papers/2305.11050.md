# Production of $p$-nuclei from $r$-process seeds: the $νr$-process

> We present a new nucleosynthesis process that may take place on neutron-rich ejecta experiencing an intensive neutrino flux. The nucleosynthesis proceeds similarly to the standard $r$-process, a sequence of neutron-captures and beta-decays, however with charged-current neutrino absorption reactions on nuclei operating much faster than beta-decays. Once neutron capture reactions freeze-out the produced $r$-process neutron-rich nuclei undergo a fast conversion of neutrons into protons and are pushed even beyond the $β$-stability line producing the neutron-deficient $p$-nuclei. This scenario, which we denote as the $νr$-process, provides an alternative channel for the production of $p$-nuclei and the short-lived nucleus $^{92}$Nb. We discuss the necessary conditions posed on the astrophysical site for the $νr$-process to be realized in nature. While these conditions are not fulfilled by current neutrino-hydrodynamic models of $r$-process sites, future models, including more complex physics and a larger variety of outflow conditions, may achieve the necessary conditions in some regions of the ejecta.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a new nucleosynthesis process, the νr-process, that can produce p-nuclei and the short-lived nucleus ⁹²Nb from r-process seeds under intense neutrino irradiation, but only if the astrophysical conditions (neutrino flux, expansion timescale, and electron fraction) reach a specific regime not yet realized in current neutrino-hydrodynamic models of r-process sites.

## Method
The authors performed nucleosynthesis calculations using a nuclear reaction network with reaction rates based on the finite-range droplet mass model, including charged-current and neutral-current neutrino-nucleus reactions with light particle spallation. They assumed adiabatic expansions with parametric trajectories varying expansion timescale (δ_ρ = 0.5–8 ms), initial neutrino number density (n_νe,0 = 0.25–2 × 10³³ cm⁻³), neutrino density ratio (R_n = 0.5–4), and neutrino decay timescale (δ_ν = 1–6 × δ_ρ), while fixing initial electron fraction Y_e,0 = 0.4, neutrino temperatures T_νe = 5 MeV and T_ν̄e = 1.25 T_νe.

## Key numbers
- Initial conditions: T_0 = 10 GK, ρ_0 = 4.6 × 10⁶ g cm⁻³, entropy s = 84 k_B per nucleon
- At T = 5 GK, Y_e reaches ≈ 0.467 in all three cases I–III
- Neutron-to-seed ratios n_s at 5 GK: 13.8 (case I), 59.2 (case II), 177 (case III)
- The ν_e-n rate λ_νe n ≈ 1 ms⁻¹ for n_νe = 10³³ cm⁻³ and T_νe = 5 MeV
- CC ν_e-A rates for A = 100–200 near stability are ∼1–10 times larger than λ_νe n
- Significant ⁹²Mo production (≳ 10⁻⁴, > 2.5% of heavy-element mass) occurs when τ_exp λ_νe n ≳ 0.1
- Expected ratio of ∼90/1 between r-process and νr-process material if both follow solar proportions
- ⁹²Nb/⁹²Mo production ratio close to unity, ∼10³ larger than the ∼O(10⁻³) ratio from the ν-process in CCSNe
- Estimated decay time of ∼250 Myr since the last νr-process event to match early solar system ⁹²Nb/⁹²Mo ratio

## Context
This work extends the study of p-nuclei production beyond the γ-process (Arnould 2003, Pignatari 2016), νp-process (Frohlich 2006, Pruet 2006, Wanajo 2006), and rp-process (Schatz 1998), which all operate in proton-rich conditions. The νr-process instead operates in neutron-rich conditions with Y_e ≈ 0.4–0.5 under intense neutrino fluxes, providing an alternative channel for producing p-nuclei and ⁹²Nb that cannot be explained by the γ-process underproduction of ⁹²,⁹⁴Mo and ⁹⁶,⁹⁸Ru (Lugaro 2016). The process may operate in magnetically-driven ejecta from magnetorotational supernovae (Obergaulinger and Reichert 2023), collapsar engines (Siegel 2019), or around magnetized binary neutron star merger remnants (Shibata 2021), and a recent study (Prasanna 2024) has reported suitable conditions may exist in winds from rotating highly magnetized proto-neutron stars.
