# Unified formalism for electromagnetic and gravitational probes: densities

> The use of light front coordinates allows a fully relativistic description of a hadron's spatial densities to be obtained. These densities must be two-dimensional and transverse to a chosen spatial direction. We explore their relationship to the three-dimensional, non-relativistic densities, with a focus on densities associated with the energy momentum tensor. The two-dimensional non-relativistic densities can be obtained from the light front densities through a non-relativistic limit, and can subsequently be transformed into three-dimensional non-relativistic densities through an inverse Abel transform. However, this operation is not invertible, and moreover the application of the inverse Abel transform to the light front densities does not produce a physically meaningful result. We additionally find that the Abel transforms of so-called Breit-frame densities generally differ significantly from the true light front densities. Numerical examples are provided to illustrate the various differences between the light front, Breit frame, and non-relativistic treatment of densities.

## Key claim

Light front coordinates provide the only fully relativistic description of hadron spatial densities, which are necessarily two-dimensional and transverse to a chosen spatial direction; the inverse Abel transform applied to light front densities does not produce a physically meaningful three-dimensional result, and the Abel transforms of Breit-frame densities generally differ significantly from true light front densities.

## Method

The authors derived the relationships between light front, Breit frame, and non-relativistic densities associated with the energy momentum tensor (EMT) for both spin-zero and spin-half hadrons. They examined the Abel transform as a proposed connection between two-dimensional and three-dimensional densities, applied non-relativistic reduction by restoring factors of c and taking the c → ∞ limit, and provided numerical examples using a simple hadronic model to illustrate differences between the three treatments of densities.

## Key numbers

- The two-dimensional non-relativistic density obtained from a spherically symmetric three-dimensional density via the Abel transform is given by ρ_NR^(2D)(b_⊥) = 2 ∫_b^∞ dr (r/√(r²-b_⊥²)) ρ_NR^(3D)(r)
- The inverse Abel transform is ρ_NR^(3D)(r) = -(1/π) ∫_r^∞ db_⊥ (dρ_NR^(2D)(b_⊥)/db_⊥)(1/√(b_⊥²-r²))
- For spin-half particles, the 2D Breit frame and light front comoving stress tensors are related by P⁺ S_LF^ij(b_⊥) = M T_BF,½^ij(b_⊥), but this equality does not hold for spin-zero particles
- The Breit frame mass density for spin-zero hadrons includes a factor 1/√(1-t/4M²) multiplying the form factors, while for spin-half hadrons this factor is absent

## Context

This work extends the foundational understanding of hadron spatial densities established by Burkardt (2002), Miller (2007, 2009, 2018), and Freese (2021), who showed that only two-dimensional Fourier transforms at fixed light front time yield meaningful relativistic densities. The paper directly contradicts recent proposals by Panteleeva (2021) and Kim (2021) that the Abel transform can connect light front and Breit frame formalisms, demonstrating that this connection works only as a formal coincidence for spin-half particles and lacks physical meaning. The findings enable a clearer distinction between relativistic and non-relativistic treatments of EMT densities, particularly for addressing the proton mass puzzle and spin puzzle.
