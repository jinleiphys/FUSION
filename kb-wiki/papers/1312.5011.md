# Update on the 2+1+1 flavor QCD equation of state with HISQ

> We present recent results on the QCD equation of state with 2+1+1 flavors of highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ). We focus on three sets of ensembles with temporal extent 6, 8 and 10, that reach up to temperatures of 967, 725 and 580 MeV, respectively. The strange and charm quark masses are tuned to the physical values and the light quarks mass is set to one fifth of the strange. This corresponds to a Goldstone pion of about 300 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper presents an updated calculation of the 2+1+1 flavor QCD equation of state using the HISQ action, but the results are limited to a single line of constant physics with light quark mass set to one fifth of the strange quark mass (corresponding to a pion of about 300 MeV), and no continuum extrapolation is performed because Nτ=12 ensembles would be needed for that purpose.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with 2+1+1 flavors of highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ) along a line of constant physics with m_l = m_s/5, using finite-temperature ensembles with temporal extents Nτ = 6, 8, and 10 and aspect ratio of four. The trace anomaly (interaction measure) was computed by taking differences between finite- and zero-temperature observables at the same gauge coupling and quark masses, with the β-functions determined from fits to the lattice spacing and quark mass data. The strange and charm quark masses were tuned to physical values using the π, K, η_c, and J/ψ masses, and the lattice scale was set using r1 ≈ 0.31 fm.

## Key numbers

- Maximum temperatures reached: 967 MeV (Nτ=6), 725 MeV (Nτ=8), and 580 MeV (Nτ=10)
- Lattice spacings down to 0.034 fm at β=7.285
- Chiral crossover temperature Tc = 154(9) MeV (from prior work Bazavov (2011))
- Charm contribution to trace anomaly reaches maximum around 500 MeV, accounting for 40% of the total value at that temperature
- At 200 MeV, charm contribution is less than 10% of the total trace anomaly
- The dM_c/dε_N term on Nτ=6 lattices at T=272 and 369 MeV is about 2% of the total result

## Context

This work extends the 2+1+1 flavor equation of state study initiated in Bazavov (2012) by adding new ensembles and increased statistics, building on prior 2+1 flavor calculations by Bazavov (2009) and Borsanyi (2013). The inclusion of dynamical charm quarks is motivated by perturbative calculations by Laine (2006) and quenched charm lattice studies by Cheng (2007) showing non-negligible charm contributions at temperatures as low as 2-3 Tc, which are relevant for LHC heavy-ion experiments. The results enable future comparisons with 2+1 flavor calculations to quantify the effect of sea charm quarks, but Nτ=12 ensembles will be needed for a reliable continuum extrapolation.
