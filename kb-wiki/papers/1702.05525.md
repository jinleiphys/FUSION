# NRQCD Confronts LHCb Data on Quarkonium Production within Jets

> We analyze the recent LHCb measurement of the distribution of the fraction of the transverse momentum, $z(J/ψ)$, carried by the $J/ψ$ within a jet. LHCb data is compared to analytic calculations using the fragmenting jet function (FJF) formalism for studying $J/ψ$ in jets. Logarithms in the FJFs are resummed using DGLAP evolution. We also convolve hard QCD partonic cross sections, showered with PYTHIA, with leading order Non Relativistic Quantum Chromodynamics (NRQCD) fragmentation functions and obtain consistent results. Both approaches use Madgraph to calculate the hard process that creates the jet initiating parton. These calculations give reasonable agreement with the $z(J/ψ)$ distribution that was shown to be poorly described by default PYTHIA simulations in the LHCb paper. We compare our predictions for the $J/ψ$ distribution using various extractions of nonperturbative NRQCD long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) in the literature. NRQCD calculations agree with LHCb data better than default PYTHIA regardless of which fit to the LDMEs is used. LDMEs from fits that focus exclusively on high transverse momentum data from colliders are in good agreement with the LHCb measurement.

# NRQCD Confronts LHCb Data on Quarkonium Production within Jets

## Key claim

NRQCD calculations using the fragmenting jet function (FJF) formalism and a Gluon Fragmentation Improved PYTHIA (GFIP) approach both yield $z(J/\psi)$ distributions that agree with LHCb data significantly better than default PYTHIA simulations, provided the LDMEs are taken from fits that focus exclusively on high transverse momentum collider data rather than from global fits to the world's data.

## Method

The authors generate hard partonic events in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV using MadGraph, then apply two independent methods: (1) GFIP, where PYTHIA showers the events down to a scale of $\sim 2 m_c$, and the resulting parton $z$ distributions are convolved manually with leading-order NRQCD fragmentation functions; (2) FJF, where analytic fragmenting jet functions with DGLAP-resummed logarithms are combined with MadGraph hard events. Both methods incorporate the five dominant NRQCD production mechanisms ($^3S_1^{[1]}$ from charm quarks, and $^3S_1^{[1]}$, $^3S_1^{[8]}$, $^1S_0^{[8]}$, $^3P_J^{[8]}$ from gluons) weighted by three different sets of long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) from the literature.

## Key numbers

- LHCb jet selection: pseudorapidity $2.5 < \eta < 4.0$, jet radius $R=0.5$, jet $p_T > 20$ GeV; muon requirements: $2.0 < \eta < 4.5$, $p > 5$ GeV, $p_T > 0.5$ GeV
- LDME set from Butenschoen and Kniehl (2011, 2012): $\langle\mathcal{O}^{J/\psi}(^3S_1^{[1]})\rangle = 1.32 \pm 0.20$ GeV$^3$, $\langle\mathcal{O}^{J/\psi}(^3S_1^{[8]})\rangle = (0.224 \pm 0.59) \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$, $\langle\mathcal{O}^{J/\psi}(^1S_0^{[8]})\rangle = (4.97 \pm 0.44) \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$, $\langle\mathcal{O}^{J/\psi}(^3P_0^{[8]})\rangle/m_c^2 = (-0.72 \pm 0.88) \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$
- LDME set from Chao et al. (2012): $\langle\mathcal{O}^{J/\psi}(^3S_1^{[1]})\rangle = 1.16 \pm 0.20$ GeV$^3$, $\langle\mathcal{O}^{J/\psi}(^3S_1^{[8]})\rangle = (0.30 \pm 0.12) \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$, $\langle\mathcal{O}^{J/\psi}(^1S_0^{[8]})\rangle = (8.9 \pm 0.98) \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$, $\langle\mathcal{O}^{J/\psi}(^3P_0^{[8]})\rangle/m_c^2 = (0.56 \pm 0.21) \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$
- LDME set from Bodwin et al. (2014): $\langle\mathcal{O}^{J/\psi}(^3S_1^{[1]})\rangle = 1.32 \pm 0.20$ GeV$^3$, $\langle\mathcal{O}^{J/\psi}(^3S_1^{[8]})\rangle = (1.1 \pm 1.0) \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$, $\langle\mathcal{O}^{J/\psi}(^1S_0^{[8]})\rangle = (9.9 \pm 2.2) \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$, $\langle\mathcal{O}^{J/\psi}(^3P_0^{[8]})\rangle/m_c^2 = (0.49 \pm 0.44) \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$
- Comparison with LHCb data is restricted to the range $0.1 < z < 0.9$ to avoid large corrections near the endpoints

## Context

This work extends the FJF formalism previously applied to $e^+e^-$ collisions by Bain et al. (2016) to the LHCb hadron collider environment, where a similar discrepancy between PYTHIA and analytic NLL' calculations had been observed for $J/\psi$ $z$ distributions. The results contradict the default PYTHIA simulation shown in the original LHCb paper (Aaij et al., 2017) and support the NRQCD factorization picture where gluon fragmentation with Altarelli-Parisi evolution dominates. The analysis enables future combined fits of LHCb jet data with high-$p_T$ collider data used in the LDME extractions of Chao et al. (2012) and Bodwin et al. (2014), and motivates experimental measurements of $J/\psi$ polarization as a function of $z(J/\psi)$ and methods to discriminate charm-initiated from gluon-initiated jets.
