# Screening Masses of Scalar and Pseudo-scalar Excitations in Quark-gluon Plasma

> The quark-gluon plasma (QGP) excitations, corresponding to the scalar and pseudoscalar meson quantum numbers, for different temperatures are calculated. Analysis is performed in the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) Approximation and leads to a better understanding of the excitations of QGP in the deconfined phase and is also of relevance for lattice studies.

## Key claim
The screening masses of scalar and pseudoscalar excitations in the quark-gluon plasma, calculated within the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) approximation, approach the non-interacting limit from below as temperature increases, contrary to earlier perturbative results which gave positive corrections above the free limit; the agreement with lattice data is fair for temperatures well above T_c but worsens near the critical temperature.

## Method
The authors computed the spatial correlation function for scalar and pseudoscalar mesonic channels in the HTL approximation, using the dressed quark propagator with a thermal gap mass m_q = gT/√6. They regularized the divergent integrals by subtracting the free spectral function (with an auxiliary mass m = √2 m_q) from the HTL spectral function, then fitted the difference in momentum space to a sum of Yukawa-like functions. After Fourier transforming to coordinate space, they fitted the full spatial correlation function to extract the screening mass as the lightest mass parameter in the exponential decay.

## Key numbers
- Screening mass in the pseudoscalar channel at T/T_c = 1: m_scr^PS / (2πT) = 0.758
- Screening mass in the pseudoscalar channel at T/T_c = 4: m_scr^PS / (2πT) = 0.895
- Screening mass in the scalar channel at T/T_c = 1: m_scr^S / (2πT) = 0.704
- Screening mass in the scalar channel at T/T_c = 4: m_scr^S / (2πT) = 0.847
- Screening mass in the pseudoscalar channel at T/T_c = 10: m_scr^PS / (2πT) = 1.019
- Screening mass in the scalar channel at T/T_c = 10: m_scr^S / (2πT) = 0.998
- Free screening mass (non-interacting quarks with mass m = √2 m_q) at T/T_c = 1: m_scr^free / (2πT) = 1.101
- Free screening mass at T/T_c = 10: m_scr^free / (2πT) = 1.033
- Thermal gap mass ratio m_q/T at T/T_c = 1: m/(2πT) = 0.229 (where m = √2 m_q)
- Transition temperature used: T_c = 202 MeV (for N_f = 2)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous HTL calculation for the pseudoscalar channel (Czerski and Alberico, earlier work) to the scalar channel, with improved numerical precision and a new double-fitting procedure that yields screening masses below the free limit 2πT. This contrasts with other perturbative computations (Laine and Vepsalainen, 2004) which found positive corrections above 2πT, and aligns with lattice results from Cheng et al. (2011) and Mukherjee (2012) that lie below the free limit. The results enable more reliable comparisons between HTL-based analytic calculations and lattice QCD data for mesonic screening masses in the deconfined phase.
