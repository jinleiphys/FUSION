# Pions near condensation under compact star conditions

> The behavior of pions is studied in systems where their normal leptonic decay is forbidden. When thermal fluctuations are present, a low decay rate is generated, and as a consequence of lepton recombination, the amount of pions remains almost unaltered. Compact stars conditions are favorable for the formation of such intermediate state of charged pions: near condensation and almost stable, leading to a continuum source of anti-neutrinos. In particular, protoneutron stars could be an scenario where this state of matter is relevant.

## Key claim

Charged pions can exist in a metastable state (long-lived but not permanent) inside compact stars when the lepton chemical potential exceeds a critical value, because Pauli blocking of the final-state lepton suppresses the normal weak decay; thermal fluctuations then generate a low decay rate, and lepton recombination keeps the pion population nearly constant.

## Method

The authors compute the charged-pion decay rate from the imaginary part of the pion self-energy at finite temperature and chemical potential using the Matsubara formalism, with the weak-interaction Lagrangian as the effective vertex. They also compute the lepton recombination rate from the lepton self-energy, and find the region of chemical potential and temperature where the two rates are comparable (within a factor of 1.5), indicating simultaneous thermal equilibrium. The neutrino emissivity from the metastable-pion decay is then calculated and compared with the standard URCA emissivity.

## Key numbers

* The critical lepton chemical potential for muons, using the vacuum pion mass \(m_\pi = 139.5\) MeV and muon mass \(m_\mu = 105.6\) MeV, is \(\mu_c = 109.74\) MeV.
* The Fermi constant used is \(G_F = 1.17 \times 10^{-5}\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
* For temperatures \(T > 1\) MeV, the lepton chemical potential lies in the metastability region (\(\mu_\ell > \mu_c\)).
* At \(T \sim 1\) MeV and for a low pion mass (115 MeV), the neutrino emissivity from pions decaying into muons can be of the same order of magnitude as the URCA emissivity.
* The emissivity from metastable pions scales as \(\epsilon \sim T^2 e^{-(E_c - \mu_\pi)/T}\), in contrast to the usual pion-gas emissivity \(\epsilon \sim T^{3/2} e^{-(m_\pi - \mu_\pi)/T}\).
* The temperature range where emissivity is maximal is approximately \(T \approx 1\)–\(10\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of pion behavior in dense lepton systems, where previous work by Ohnishi (2008) and Heiselberg (1999) had considered the role of repulsive versus attractive pion-nucleon interactions and hyperon effects on the electron chemical potential. The metastable-pion scenario provides a new, continuous source of anti-neutrinos in protoneutron stars, with an emissivity that can rival the URCA process (Yakovlev, 2000) under certain conditions, and differs qualitatively from the emissivity of a simple pion gas (Jaikumar, 2002). The results suggest that particle-population estimates in compact-star models, which usually ignore pions because of their fast decay, should be revisited.
