# Shell Model States in the Continuum

> We suggest a method for calculating scattering phase shifts and energies and widths of resonances which utilizes only eigenenergies obtained in variational calculations with oscillator basis and their dependence on oscillator basis spacing $\hbarΩ$. We make use of simple expressions for the $S$-matrix at eigenstates of a finite (truncated) Hamiltonian matrix in the oscillator basis obtained in the HORSE ($J$-matrix) formalism of quantum scattering theory. The validity of the suggested approach is verified in calculations with model Woods--Saxon potentials and applied to calculations of $nα$ resonances and non-resonant scattering using the no-core shell model.

## Key claim
The authors propose that scattering phase shifts and resonance parameters can be extracted solely from eigenenergies obtained in variational calculations with a harmonic oscillator basis, by exploiting the dependence of those eigenenergies on the oscillator spacing \(\hbar\Omega\) and basis truncation, without requiring explicit calculation of eigenvectors or additional reaction-theory frameworks.

## Method
The method (SS-HORSE) uses the HORSE (\(J\)-matrix) formalism to relate the \(S\)-matrix at an eigenenergy \(E_{\nu}\) of the truncated Hamiltonian to universal functions of the oscillator basis parameters \(\mathbb N\) and \(\hbar\Omega\). By varying \(\hbar\Omega\) and the basis size, a set of eigenenergies is generated; the phase shift at each eigenenergy is obtained from a simple analytic expression involving only the basis parameters and the channel orbital momentum \(\ell\). A low-energy expansion or a pole-plus-background parametrization of the \(S\)-matrix is then fitted to these discrete phase-shift points to extract the resonant energy \(E_r\) and width \(\Gamma\).

## Key numbers
- The scaling variable \(s = \hbar\Omega/(\mathbb N + 7/2)\) (in energy units) controls the low-energy behavior of the phase shift via \(\tan\delta_{\ell}(E_{\nu}) = j_{\ell}(2\sqrt{E_{\nu}/s}) / n_{\ell}(2\sqrt{E_{\nu}/s})\).
- For bound states, the extrapolation formula \(\varkappa_{\nu} - k_b = -|A_{\ell}|^2 \exp(-4\varkappa_{\nu}\hbar/\sqrt{2ms})\) relates the eigenmomentum \(\varkappa_{\nu}\) to the converged bound-state momentum \(k_b\) and asymptotic normalization constant \(A_{\ell}\).
- The condition for the low-energy approximation to hold is \(E \ll \frac{1}{8}\hbar\Omega(\mathbb N+2-\ell)^2\); a further simplification to \(-2\sqrt{E/s} + \pi\ell/2\) requires \(E \gg \hbar\Omega/[4(\mathbb N+7/2)]\).

## Context
This work extends the HORSE (\(J\)-matrix) formalism, previously used for direct scattering calculations, to a simplified single-state version that avoids summing over many eigenstates. It contrasts with the Gamow shell model (NCGSM) and the no-core shell model / resonating group method (NCSM/RGM), which require Berggren basis states or additional RGM calculations. The scaling property derived here for continuum states parallels the scaling for bound states proposed by Coon et al. (2012) and More et al. (2014), but uses a different scaling parameter \(s\) and is derived from the \(S\)-matrix rather than from energy convergence patterns. The method enables extraction of resonance parameters and non-resonant phase shifts directly from standard NCSM calculations, as demonstrated for \(n\alpha\) scattering with the JISP16 \(NN\) interaction.
