# Modern NN Force Predictions for the Total ND Cross Section up to 300 MeV

> For several modern nucleon-nucleon potentials state-of-the-art Faddeev calculations are carried out for the $nd$ total cross section between 10 and 300 MeV projectile energy and compared to new high precision measurements. The agreement between theory and data is rather good, with exception at the higher energies where a 10% discrepancy builds up. In addition the convergence of the multiple scattering series incorporated in the Faddeev scheme is studied numerically with the result, that rescattering corrections remain important. Based on this multiple scattering series the high energy limit of the total $nd$ cross section is also investigated analytically. In contrast to the naive expectation that the total $nd$ cross section is the sum of the $np$ and $nn$ total cross sections we find additional effects resulting from the rescattering processes, which have different signs and different behavior as function of the energy. A shadowing effect in the high energy limit only occurs for energies higher than 300 MeV. The expressions in the high energy limit have qualitatively a similar behavior as the exactly calculated expressions, but can be expected to be valid quantitatively only at much higher energies.

## Key claim

For several modern nucleon-nucleon potentials, state-of-the-art Faddeev calculations of the total neutron-deuteron (nd) cross section agree well with new high-precision measurements at lower energies, but a discrepancy of about 10% builds up at the highest energies near 300 MeV. The authors find that the naive expectation that the total nd cross section equals the sum of the np and nn total cross sections is incorrect due to rescattering processes, and that a shadowing effect in the high energy limit only occurs for energies above 300 MeV.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations for three-nucleon scattering in momentum space using a partial-wave decomposition, employing the CD-Bonn, AV18, Nijm I, and Nijm II nucleon-nucleon potentials. They computed the total nd cross section via the optical theorem from the forward elastic scattering amplitude, and studied the convergence of the multiple scattering series by evaluating the first, second, and third order terms in the NN t-matrix expansion. Additionally, they derived analytic high-energy limits for the first and second order terms and compared these asymptotic expressions to the exact Faddeev results.

## Key numbers

- The theoretical calculation begins to underestimate the data around 100 MeV by about 4%, and this discrepancy increases to about 11% at 300 MeV.
- The four phase-equivalent potentials (CD-Bonn, AV18, Nijm I, Nijm II) agree with each other within an accuracy of 1% for the total nd cross section.
- The Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon force enhances the total nd cross section by about 4% in the energy regime between 100 and 300 MeV.
- A simple estimate of relativistic kinematic effects gives an increase of about 3% at 100 MeV and about 7% at 250 MeV.
- The high energy limit of the first plus second order terms differs from the exact Faddeev result by 8% at 300 MeV, indicating the asymptotic limit is not yet quantitatively valid at that energy.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of modern NN potentials to the total nd cross section using new high-precision data from Abfalterer et al. (1998). The study builds on previous Faddeev calculations for three-nucleon scattering and provides a detailed analysis of the multiple scattering series, complementing earlier Glauber theory studies of shadowing effects in three-nucleon systems. The results enable future investigations into the role of three-nucleon forces and relativistic effects at higher energies, which the authors identify as necessary to resolve the growing discrepancy between theory and experiment above 100 MeV.
