# Radial Oscillations in Neutron Stars with Delta Baryons

> We investigate the effect of $Δ$ baryons on the radial oscillations of neutron and hyperon stars, employing a density-dependent relativistic mean-field model. The spin-$3/2$ baryons are described by the Rarita-Schwinger Lagrangian density. The baryon-meson coupling constants for the spin-3/2 decuplet and the spin-1/2 baryonic octet are calculated using a unified approach relying on the fact that the Yukawa couplings present in the Lagrangian density of the mean-field models must be invariant under the SU(3) and SU(6) group transformations. We calculate the 20 lowest eigenfrequencies and corresponding oscillation functions of $Δ$-inclusive nuclear (N+$Δ$) and hyperonic matter (N+H+$Δ$) by solving the Sturm-Liouville boundary value problem and also verifying its validity. We see that the lowest mode frequencies for N+$Δ$ and N+H EoSs are higher as compared to the pure nucleonic matter because of the deltas and hyperons present. Furthermore, the separation between consecutive modes increases with the addition of hyperons and $Δ$s.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including Δ baryons in neutron star matter, using a unified SU(3)/SU(6) coupling scheme, raises the lowest radial oscillation mode frequencies compared to pure nucleonic matter, but the effect is conditional on excluding Δ⁺ and Δ⁺⁺ baryons to avoid unphysical behavior where the nucleon effective mass drops to zero at low densities.

## Method

The authors employed a density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DD-RMF) model with the DD-ME2 parameter set, describing spin-3/2 Δ baryons via the Rarita-Schwinger Lagrangian and spin-1/2 baryons via the Dirac Lagrangian. Baryon-meson coupling constants for both the octet and decuplet were determined using SU(3) and SU(6) symmetry arguments with a single free parameter α_v = 1.0. They solved the Sturm-Liouville eigenvalue problem for radial oscillations to compute the 20 lowest eigenfrequencies and corresponding oscillation functions for four compositions: pure nucleonic (N), Δ-inclusive nuclear (N+Δ), hyperonic (N+H), and Δ-inclusive hyperonic (N+H+Δ) matter.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass for pure nucleonic matter: 2.46 M⊙ at radius 12.05 km
- Maximum mass for N+Δ matter: 2.24 M⊙ at radius 11.87 km
- Maximum mass for N+H matter: 1.87 M⊙ at radius 12.09 km
- Maximum mass for N+H+Δ matter: 1.90 M⊙ at radius 11.90 km
- Radius at canonical mass decreases from 13.29 km (pure nucleonic) to 12.82 km (N+Δ)
- Fundamental f-mode frequency (n=0) at maximum mass: 0.571 kHz (N), 1.578 kHz (N+Δ), 1.977 kHz (N+H), 1.865 kHz (N+H+Δ)
- First p-mode frequency (n=1) at maximum mass: 5.173 kHz (N), 5.891 kHz (N+Δ), 6.582 kHz (N+H), 6.241 kHz (N+H+Δ)
- Δ baryon mass: 1232 MeV; Δ baryon potential depth: U_Δ ≈ -98 MeV
- Hyperon potentials: U_Λ = -28 MeV, U_Σ = 30 MeV, U_Ξ = -4 MeV

## Context

This work extends prior studies of radial oscillations in neutron stars with exotic matter, such as dark matter and deconfined quark matter (e.g., Kokkotas and collaborators, 2022), but claims to be the first investigation of radial oscillations specifically including Δ baryons. The study uses the DD-ME2 parameter set from the density-dependent RMF framework of Typel and Wolter (1999) and follows the unified coupling scheme of Lopes (2021) for determining baryon-meson couplings. The results enable future comparisons with gravitational wave observations from post-merger remnants, where radial oscillations in the 1-4 kHz range could be detected, as suggested by Chirenti (2019).
