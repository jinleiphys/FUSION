# Light-Front Model of Transition Form-Factors in Heavy Meson Decay

> Electroweak transition form factors of heavy meson decays are important ingredients in the extraction of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix elements from experimental data. In this work, within a light-front framework, we calculate electroweak transition form factor for the semileptonic decay of $D$ mesons into a pion or a kaon. The model results underestimate in both cases the new data of CLEO for the larger momentum transfers accessible in the experiment. We discuss possible reasons for that in order to improve the model.

# Light-Front Model of Transition Form-Factors in Heavy Meson Decay

## Key claim
The light-front covariant model (LFCM) with a high-power regulator (n=10) fitted to pseudoscalar decay constants underestimates the CLEO data for D→π and D→K semileptonic decay transition form factors at large momentum transfers, while the slope near q²=0 appears reasonable. The authors suggest that a softer vertex function with a smaller power (n≈2) would be required for a better fit.

## Method
The authors used a covariant Bethe-Salpeter vertex model with a Wick rotation of the light-front energy in the Mandelstam formula to calculate the electroweak transition form factors f₊(q²) and f₋(q²) for D→πℓν and D→Kℓν decays. The vertex parameters (regulator scale λ_M and constituent quark masses) were fitted to the experimental pseudoscalar decay constants f_π=94 MeV, f_K=113 MeV, and f_D=144.5 MeV, using a regulator power n=10. The plus component of the weak vector current was employed in a reference frame with q⁺<0 and transverse momenta set to zero.

## Key numbers
- f_π = 94 MeV, f_K = 113 MeV, f_D = 144.5 MeV used as input to fit vertex parameters (from PDG)
- Pion charge radius from model: 0.296–0.392 fm (parameter sets P3–P5), compared to experimental 0.672 fm
- Kaon charge radius from model: 0.344–0.417 fm (parameter sets K1–K5), compared to experimental 0.56 fm
- D⁺ charge radius from model: 0.341–0.346 fm (parameter sets D1–D5)
- f₊(0) for D→K: model values range from 0.729 (K1,D1) to 1.100 (K5,D5); CLEO experimental value 0.739±0.009
- f₊(0) for D→π: model values range from 0.491 (P3,D3) to 0.936 (P5,D5); CLEO experimental value 0.665±0.019
- Regulator scale λ_M for pion: 2666–3564 MeV; for kaon: 2478–3056 MeV; for D⁺: 2138–2163 MeV
- Constituent quark masses: light quarks 203–220 MeV, strange quark 327–344 MeV, charm quark 1442–1459 MeV

## Context
This work extends the light-front covariant model (LFCM) previously applied to elastic electromagnetic form factors of pions, kaons, and D mesons (de Melo and Frederico, 2008) to the weak transition form factors relevant for CKM matrix element extraction. The results are compared to lattice QCD calculations (MILC Collaboration) and dispersion relation calculations (Bruno, 2008), as well as experimental data from CLEO (2009), Belle, BaBar, and BES. The model's difficulty in simultaneously fitting D→π and D→K form factors at zero momentum transfer, combined with the underestimation at large q², indicates that a softer vertex function (power n≈2 rather than n=10) and larger meson charge radii are needed, pointing toward improvements in the Bethe-Salpeter vertex parametrization for future work.
