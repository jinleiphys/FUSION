# Computing the Effective Hamiltonian of Low-Energy Vacuum Gauge Fields

> A standard approach to investigate the non-perturbative QCD dynamics is through vacuum models which emphasize the role played by specific gauge field fluctuations, such as instantons, monopoles or vortexes. The effective Hamiltonian describing the dynamics of the low-energy degrees of freedom in such approaches is usually postulated phenomenologically, or obtained through uncontrolled approximations. In a recent paper, we have shown how lattice field theory simulations can be used to rigorously compute the effective Hamiltonian of arbitrary vacuum models by stochastically performing the path integral over all the vacuum field fluctuations which are not explicitly taken into account. In this work, we present the first illustrative application of such an approach to a gauge theory and we use it to compute the instanton size distribution in SU(2) gluon-dynamics in a fully model independent and parameter-free way.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Vacuum Manifold Projection (VMP) method can compute the instanton size distribution in SU(2) gluon-dynamics directly from lattice simulations without any arbitrary parameters or uncontrolled approximations, in contrast to previous approaches that relied on cooling, eigenvalue filtering, or variational estimates.

## Method

The authors generated three ensembles of Landau gauge-fixed SU(2) lattice configurations using the Wilson action at different lattice spacings (β=2.3 on 16⁴, β=2.4 on 24⁴, β=2.5 on 32⁴). For each configuration, they projected the gauge field onto a manifold spanned by singular-gauge instanton and anti-instanton configurations by solving a system of nonlinear equations that enforce orthogonality between the fluctuation field and the tangent vectors of the manifold. The frequency histogram of the instanton size ρ obtained from solving these equations for many configurations yields the probability distribution, from which the effective Hamiltonian (specifically the single-instanton size distribution n(ρ)) is obtained as the negative logarithm.

## Key numbers

- The instanton size distribution n(ρ) peaks at approximately ρ ≈ 0.2 fm, independent of lattice spacing within statistical errors
- The VMP results agree quantitatively with the one-loop perturbative formula n_one-loop(ρ) ∝ ρ^(11/3 N_c - 5) in the range 0.1 fm ≲ ρ ≲ 0.2 fm
- Three lattice ensembles were used: 100 configurations on 16⁴ at β=2.3 (a ≈ 0.17 fm), 100 configurations on 24⁴ at β=2.4 (a ≈ 0.12 fm), and 25 configurations on 32⁴ at β=2.5 (a ≈ 0.09 fm)
- The projection point instanton size was held fixed at ρ̄ = 5a for all calculations
- A validation test using 1000 configurations of 20 instantons and 20 anti-instantons in a (2.7 fm)⁴ box with a Gaussian size distribution (ρ₀ = 0.3 fm, σ = 0.13 fm) showed the VMP method accurately reconstructs the input distribution for ρ larger than about one lattice spacing

## Context

This work extends the VMP method previously tested on a quantum-mechanical toy model (Millo, Faccioli, and Scorzato, 2010) to a gauge theory for the first time. It provides a parameter-free alternative to cooling algorithms (Michael and Spencer) and eigenvalue filtering (Gattringer) for extracting instanton properties from lattice configurations, and to variational estimates of the instanton size distribution (Diakonov and Petrov, 1984; Shuryak, 1982). The approach enables rigorous computation of the effective Hamiltonian for vacuum models, allowing future work to compute many-body interactions between instantons and to test whether failures of instanton models are due to the choice of degrees of freedom or to uncontrolled approximations in the effective Hamiltonian.
