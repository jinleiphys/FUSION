# Fermionic Molecular Dynamics

> A quantum molecular model for fermions is investigated which works with antisymmetrized many-body states composed of localized single-particle wave packets. The application to the description of atomic nuclei and collisions between them shows that the model is capable to address a rich variety of observed phenomena. Among them are shell effects, cluster structure and intrinsic deformation in ground states of nuclei as well as fusion, incomplete fusion, dissipative binary collisions and multifragmentation in reactions depending on impact parameter and beam energy. Thermodynamic properties studied with long time simulations proof that the model obeys Fermi-Dirac statistics and time averaging is equivalent to ensemble averaging. A first order liquid-gas phase transition is observed at a boiling temperature of $T \approx 5 MeV$ for finite nuclei of mass $16...40$.

# Fermionic Molecular Dynamics

## Key claim

Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD) combines antisymmetrized many-body wave functions composed of localized Gaussian wave packets with a deterministic molecular dynamics approach, thereby capturing both quantum shell effects and classical dissipative fluctuations that time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) fails to reproduce. The model's success depends on having a sufficiently rich trial state and an appropriate effective Hamiltonian, with the caveat that the single Slater determinant used in this work cannot account for short-range correlations from the repulsive core, requiring instead an effective interaction in the spirit of a Brueckner G-matrix treatment.

## Method

The many-body trial state is a Slater determinant of single-particle Gaussian wave packets, each parameterized by mean position, mean momentum, a complex width parameter, and spin orientation angles, with fixed isospin (proton or neutron). Equations of motion are derived from the time-dependent variational principle, yielding Hamiltonian dynamics on a symplectic manifold where the skew-symmetric matrix of the Lagrange function determines the generalized Poisson brackets. The antisymmetrization ensures the Pauli principle is a priori satisfied, and the wave packets guarantee the uncertainty relation cannot be violated.

## Key numbers

- Boiling temperature for the liquid-gas phase transition in finite nuclei of mass 16 to 40: T ≈ 5 MeV
- Heavy-ion collisions calculated at beam energy 6 A MeV show typical fluctuations of dissipative collisions absent in TDHF
- Heavy-ion collisions calculated at beam energy 32 A MeV (relative velocity about the Fermi velocity) lead to multifragmentation rather than fusion or binary dissipative collisions
- Each nucleon is represented by 10 real parameters (position, momentum, complex width, two spin angles, fixed isospin)

## Context

This work extends the particle exchange picture (BBN78, Ran79, Fel87) and addresses the failure of TDHF (DDK85, MaC76, KDM77, DFF78) to produce fluctuations in dissipative collisions, which arises because the mean field in TDHF suppresses fluctuations by averaging over all macro-channels. The model contrasts with Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) approaches (AiS86, Aic91) that treat the Pauli principle only through numerical phase-space occupation constraints or through a "Pauli potential," whereas FMD incorporates antisymmetrization exactly. The approach enables microscopic studies of nuclear ground-state properties (shell effects, cluster structure, intrinsic deformation), heavy-ion reaction mechanisms from fusion through multifragmentation, and thermodynamic properties including the liquid-gas phase transition in finite nuclei.
