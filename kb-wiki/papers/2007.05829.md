# $Î›^*$ matter and its stability

> We performed calculations of nuclear systems composed solely of $Î›^*$ hyperons, aiming at exploring the possibility of existence of absolutely stable $Î›^*$ matter. We considered $Î›^*$ interaction strengths compatible with the $Î›^*Î›^*$ binding energy $B_{Î›^*Î›^*}$ given by the $\bar{K}N$ interaction model by Yamazaki and Akaishi [1]. We found that the binding energy per $Î›^*$ saturates at values well below 100 MeV for mass number $A\geq120$. The $Î›^*$ matter is thus highly unstable against strong interaction decay.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that even with very strong attractive interactions fitted to a $\Lambda^*\Lambda^*$ binding energy of 40 MeV, the binding energy per $\Lambda^*$ in many-body systems saturates at values well below 100 MeV for mass numbers $A \geq 120$, making absolutely stable $\Lambda^*$ matter impossible. This contradicts the earlier conjecture by Yamazaki and Akaishi (2017) that such matter could be stable.

## Method

The authors performed calculations of $\Lambda^*$ few-body systems using the Stochastic Variational Method (SVM) with meson-exchange potentials of the Dover-Gal and Machleidt forms, and many-body systems using the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) approach with linear HS and nonlinear NL-SH models. The coupling constants were scaled to reproduce the $\Lambda^*\Lambda^*$ binding energy $B_{\Lambda^*\Lambda^*} = 40$ MeV from the Yamazaki-Akaishi $\bar{K}N$ interaction model, and the coupled Klein-Gordon and Dirac equations were solved self-consistently for closed-shell $\Lambda^*$ nuclei.

## Key numbers

- $B_{\Lambda^*\Lambda^*} = 40$ MeV: the binding energy of the two-$\Lambda^*$ system used to fit the interaction strengths, taken from the Yamazaki-Akaishi (2007) $\bar{K}N$ model.
- $B/A \approx 130$ MeV for $A=6$ in SVM calculations with the Machleidt potential when spin-spin interaction is omitted.
- rms radius of $\Lambda^*$ nuclei hardly exceeds 0.8 fm even with spin-spin interaction included.
- Binding energy per $\Lambda^*$ saturates for $A \geq 120$ at values well below 100 MeV in all RMF calculations.
- Central density of $\Lambda^*$ nuclei reaches about twice nuclear matter density.
- Conversion width $\Gamma_{\Lambda^*\Lambda^*} = 100$ MeV at threshold, suppressed by about 28% in $A=8$ systems and by less than 1% in $A=168$ systems.
- Saturation binding energies are far below approximately 290 MeV, the energy needed to reduce the $\Lambda(1405)$ mass below the $\Lambda(1116)$ mass.

## Context

This work extends the study of $\Lambda^*$ nuclei initiated by HrtĂˇnkovĂˇ et al. (2018) and directly tests the conjecture by Yamazaki and Akaishi (2017) about absolutely stable $\Lambda^*$ matter. The authors note that the $\bar{K}N$ potentials used by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2007, 2017) fail to reproduce $K^-$ single-nucleon absorption fractions from bubble chamber experiments (Friedman and Gal, 2017). The results enable future studies of $\Lambda^*$ absorption and decay in dense baryonic matter, showing that $\Lambda^*\Lambda^*$ pairs will inevitably decay via strong interactions.
