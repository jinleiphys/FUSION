# Robust ab initio prediction of nuclear electric quadrupole observables by scaling to the charge radius

> Meaningful predictions for electric quadrupole (E2) observables from ab initio nuclear theory are necessary, if the ab initio description of collective correlations is to be confronted with experiment, as well as to provide predictive power for unknown E2 observables. However, converged results for E2 observables are notoriously challenging to obtain in ab initio no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) approaches. Matrix elements of the E2 operator are sensitive to the large-distance tails of the nuclear wave function, which converge slowly in an oscillator basis expansion. Similar convergence challenges beset ab initio prediction of the nuclear charge radius. We demonstrate that the convergence patterns of the E2 and radius observables are strongly correlated, and that meaningful predictions for the absolute scale of E2 observables may be made by calibrating to the experimentally-known ground-state charge radius. We illustrate by providing robust ab initio predictions for several E2 transition strengths and quadrupole moments in p-shell nuclei, in cases where experimental results are available for comparison.

## Key claim

The convergence patterns of electric quadrupole (E2) observables and charge radius observables in no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations are strongly correlated, so that meaningful predictions for the absolute scale of E2 observables can be made by calibrating to the experimentally-known ground-state charge radius, rather than requiring fully converged calculations.

## Method

The authors performed NCCI (no-core shell model) calculations for p-shell nuclei using the Daejeon16 internucleon interaction, carried out with the code MFDn. They computed dimensionless ratios of E2 observables to the appropriate power of the point-proton radius (B(E2)/(e²r_p⁴) and Q/r_p²), and demonstrated that these ratios converge rapidly with increasing basis size Nmax, unlike the individual observables. The converged ratios were then calibrated to experimental charge radii to obtain absolute E2 predictions.

## Key numbers

- For ⁷Li: B(E2;1/2⁻₁→3/2⁻₁)/[e²r_p(3/2⁻₁)⁴] ≈ 0.50 yields B(E2) ≈ 14 e²fm⁴, compared to experimental 16.6(10) e²fm⁴ (ratio 0.58(6))
- For ¹⁰Be: B(E2;2⁺₁→0⁺₁)/[e²r_p(0⁺₁)⁴] ≈ 0.35 yields B(E2) ≈ 9 e²fm⁴, consistent with experimental 9.2(3) e²fm⁴
- For ¹⁰Be: B(E2;2⁺₂→0⁺₁)/[e²r_p(0⁺₁)⁴] ≈ 0.005–0.006 yields B(E2) ≈ 0.12–0.14 e²fm⁴, consistent with experimental 0.11(2) e²fm⁴
- Ground-state charge radii used: ⁷Li r_p = 2.31(5) fm; ¹⁰Be r_p = 2.22(2) fm (from Angeli (2013))
- NCCI predictions for Q/r_p² across p-shell nuclei (A=7–11) are consistent with experiment to within approximately 10%
- For ⁷Li, the predicted Q/r_p² is roughly 10% smaller in magnitude than the experimental value, outside uncertainties by a factor of approximately 2

## Context

This work extends the approach of using ratios of E2 observables to cancel truncation errors, previously applied to ratios of E2 matrix elements within rotational bands (Caprio et al., 2013; Maris et al., 2015) and to calibrating E2 observables to a single measured quadrupole moment (Calci et al., 2016). The key advance is demonstrating that the charge radius, which is precisely known for many nuclei and not subject to angular momentum selection rules, can serve as the calibration observable. This enables robust ab initio predictions for E2 transition strengths and quadrupole moments where direct convergence is otherwise computationally prohibitive. The approach could be combined with methods to improve convergence such as the no-core shell model with continuum (Navratil et al., 2016) or symmetry-adapted calculations (Dytrych et al., 2008).
