# Six-$α$ cluster Bose-Einstein condensation and supersolid $^{12}$C($0_2^+)$+$^{12}$C($0_2^+)$ molecular structure in $^{24}$Mg

> We show for the first time that the low-spin ($J \le 4^+$) six-$α$ condensate candidate states in $^{24}$Mg, recently reported by Fujikawa et al. [Phys. Lett. B 848, 138384 (2024)], are well described by the superfluid $α$-cluster model (SCM). This is achieved by a rigorous treatment of the Nambu-Goldstone (NG) zero mode as the order parameter of condensation in the finite six-$α$ system. We find that a roton rotational band with a large moment of inertia is built on the first excited NG $0^+$ state, analogous to the roton bands observed in three-, four-, and five-$α$ condensates in $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, and $^{20}$Ne, respectively. Remarkably, our calculated roton band reproduces the well-known molecular resonance with a $^{12}$C($0_2^+$)+$^{12}$C($0_2^+$) structure ($16^+$) observed at $E_{\rm c.m.} = 32.5$ MeV in inelastic $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C scattering. This result provides a unified description of both the low-spin six-$α$ condensate states and the high-spin $^{12}$C($0_2^+$)+$^{12}$C($0_2^+$) molecular resonance. Analysis of the wave functions reveals a large overlap between the SCM states and a geometrical $^{12}$C($0_2^+$)+$^{12}$C($0_2^+$) configuration. This dual nature -the coexistence of superfluidity and crystallinity- identifies these states as a signature of a supersolid.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the low-spin six-alpha condensate candidate states in 24Mg recently reported by Fujikawa et al. (2024) are well described by the superfluid cluster model (SCM), and that the same model also reproduces the high-spin 16+ molecular resonance with a 12C(0_2+)+12C(0_2+) structure observed at E_c.m. = 32.5 MeV, provided the moment of inertia increases as spin transitions from low-spin to high-spin states.

## Method

The authors apply the superfluid cluster model (SCM), a field-theoretical approach that treats the Nambu-Goldstone zero mode as the order parameter of condensation in the finite six-alpha system. They solve three coupled equations: the Gross-Pitaevskii equation for the order parameter, the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equations for collective excitations, and the NG zero-mode equation. The parameters Omega and V_r are determined to reproduce the size of the condensate vacuum (R_0 = 6.4 fm) and the excitation energy of the 2+ state observed at 22.5 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The condensate vacuum size is set to R_0 = 6.4 fm, scaled from sizes of 4.2 fm for 12C, 5.6 fm for 16O, and 6.0 fm for 20Ne.
- The determined parameters are Omega = 1.3305 MeV/hbar and V_r = 558 MeV.
- The rotational constant for low-spin states (J = 0+ to 6+) is k = 78 keV, corresponding to a moment of inertia J.
- For high-spin states (J = 12+ to 16+), the rotational constant decreases to k = 42 keV, roughly half the low-spin value.
- The 16+ molecular resonance is observed at E_c.m. = 32.5 MeV with a width Gamma_c.m. = 4.7 MeV.
- The B(E2) value for the transition from the 2+ BdG state to the first excited NG 0+ state is 8365 e^2 fm^4 (70% condensation rate) or 13609 e^2 fm^4 (80% condensation rate).
- Intraband B(E2) values between roton band states range from 1528 e^2 fm^4 (4+ to 2+) to 4986 e^2 fm^4 (18+ to 16+).
- The B(E2) value for the transition from the 2+ BdG state to the vacuum 0+ state is 1179 e^2 fm^4 (70% condensation rate).

## Context

This work extends the superfluid cluster model previously applied to three-, four-, and five-alpha condensates in 12C, 16O, and 20Ne by Ohkubo et al. (2025) to the six-alpha system in 24Mg. It unifies the description of low-spin six-alpha condensate candidate states reported by Fujikawa et al. (2024) with the high-spin 16+ molecular resonance observed by Wuosmaa et al. (1992) and confirmed by Szilner et al. (1997), which was previously analyzed using a coupled-channel method by Hirabayashi et al. (1995) as having a 12C(0_2+)+12C(0_2+) structure rather than a six-alpha linear chain. The dual nature of superfluidity and crystallinity identifies these states as a signature of a supersolid, analogous to findings in lighter alpha-conjugate nuclei.
