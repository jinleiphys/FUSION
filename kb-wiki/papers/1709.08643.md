# Dihadron fragmentation functions in the quark-jet model: Transversely polarized quarks

> Within the most recent extension of the quark-jet hadronization framework, we explore the transverse-polarization-dependent dihadron fragmentation functions (DiFFs) $H_1^\sphericalangle$ and $H_1^\perp$ of a quark into $π^+π^-$ pairs. Monte Carlo (MC) simulations are employed to model polarized quark hadronization and calculate the corresponding number densities. These, in turn, are used to extract the Fourier cosine moments of the DiFFs $H_1^\sphericalangle$ and $H_1^\perp$. A notable finding is that there are previously unnoticed apparent discrepancies between the definitions of the so-called interference DiFF (IFF) $H_1^\sphericalangle$, entering the cross sections for two-hadron semi-inclusive electroproduction, and those involved in the production of two pairs of hadrons from back-to-back jets in electron-positron annihilation. This manuscript completes the studies of all four leading twist DiFFs for unpolarized hadron pairs within the quark-jet framework, following our previous work on the helicity-dependent DiFF $G_1^\perp$.

## Key claim

The paper identifies previously unnoticed apparent discrepancies between the definitions of the interference dihadron fragmentation function (IFF) \(H_1^\sphericalangle\) used in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) and that used in electron-positron annihilation, specifically that the SIDIS definition includes an admixture of the first Fourier cosine moment of \(H_1^\perp\) which is absent in the \(e^+e^-\) definition, potentially impacting transversity parton distribution function extractions.

## Method

The authors employed Monte Carlo simulations of the quark-jet hadronization framework, using Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model splitting functions modified by a \((1-z)^4\) ansatz to mimic QCD evolution effects, to model the fragmentation of a transversely polarized \(u\) quark into \(\pi^+\pi^-\) pairs. They extracted the zeroth and first Fourier cosine moments of the transverse-polarization-dependent dihadron fragmentation functions \(H_1^\sphericalangle\) and \(H_1^\perp\) from the simulated number densities, and computed the combinations entering SIDIS and \(e^+e^-\) cross sections. The results were validated against explicit integral expressions for the two-hadron emission process with \(N_L=2\).

## Key numbers

- The zeroth moment \(\tilde{H}_1^{\sphericalangle,[0]}(z)\) and the first moment \(\tilde{H}_1^{\sphericalangle,[1]}(z)\) have comparable magnitudes for \(N_L=2\), while the first moment \(\tilde{H}_1^{\perp,[1]}(z)\) is much smaller than the zeroth moments of both \(\tilde{H}_1^\sphericalangle\) and \(\tilde{H}_1^\perp\).
- For \(N_L=6\), the analyzing power \(\tilde{H}_1^{\sphericalangle,[0]}(z)/D_1(z)\) and \(\tilde{H}_1^{\sphericalangle, SIDIS}(z)/D_1(z)\) show notable differences that grow with \(z\).
- The analyzing power for \(\pi^+\pi^+\) pairs has the same sign and larger magnitude than that for \(\pi^+\pi^-\) pairs, while \(\pi^-\pi^0\) pairs have opposite sign, for \(N_L=6\) with cut \(z_{1,2}\geq 0.1\).
- The simulations used \(10^{12}\) Monte Carlo events, with 100 discretization points for \(z\) in \([0,1]\) and 200 points for azimuthal angles in \([0,2\pi)\).

## Context

This work completes the study of all four leading-twist dihadron fragmentation functions for unpolarized hadron pairs within the quark-jet framework, following the authors' previous work on the helicity-dependent DiFF \(G_1^\perp\) (Matevosyan 2017). It extends the self-consistent treatment of quark polarization developed in Bentz (2016) and applied to single-hadron Collins functions in Matevosyan (2016). The results enable systematic comparisons with phenomenological extractions of transversity PDFs from HERMES (Airapetian 2008) and COMPASS (Adolph 2012, 2014) data, and with BELLE measurements (Vossen 2011, Seidl 2017), potentially guiding improved parametrizations of the interference fragmentation functions.
