# Two-pion-exchange effects in the $pp\to ppπ^0$ reaction

> We study the pp \to ppπ^0 reaction near threshold based on heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory. We show that the two-pion-exchange diagrams give much larger contribution than the one-pion-exchange diagram which is of lower chiral order in Weinberg's counting scheme. We also discuss the relation of our results to the momentum counting scheme.

## Key claim

The two-pion-exchange (TPE) loop diagrams give much larger contributions to the near-threshold \(pp\to pp\pi^0\) reaction than the one-pion-exchange diagram, even though the one-pion-exchange diagram is of lower chiral order in Weinberg's counting scheme; however, the "sub-leading" parts of the TPE amplitudes (the remainder after subtracting the "leading" momentum-counting-scheme terms) are found to be at least as large as the one-pion rescattering amplitude.

## Method

The nuclear transition operators for the TPE diagrams were derived using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB\(\chi\)PT) with Weinberg's chiral counting scheme (expansion parameter \(\epsilon \simeq m_\pi/m_N \simeq 0.15\)). The initial and final state \(NN\) wave functions were calculated using either phenomenological high-precision NN potentials (Nijmegen, Bonn) or low-momentum NN potentials (\(V_{low-k}\)), with a Gaussian momentum regulator \(\exp(-p^2/\Lambda_G^2)\) applied to suppress high-momentum components. The TPE amplitudes were evaluated in a distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) at a lab energy of \(T_{lab}=281\) MeV, and the "leading" \(t_1\) terms (which sum to zero in the momentum counting scheme) were subtracted to improve numerical convergence.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter in Weinberg's chiral counting is \(\epsilon \simeq m_\pi / m_N \simeq 0.15\); in the momentum counting scheme (MCS) it is \(\tilde{\epsilon} \simeq (m_\pi/m_N)^{1/2} \simeq 0.39\).
- The characteristic momentum scale of the \(NN \to NN\pi\) reaction at threshold is \(|\vec{p}| \sim \sqrt{m_\pi m_N} \simeq 360\) MeV/c.
- For the Nijmegen potential with \(\Lambda_G = 600\) MeV/c, the summed TPE amplitude \(J\) (Eq. 19) is \(-0.74\) (dimensionless), compared to the one-pion rescattering amplitude of \(0.20\).
- For the Nijmegen potential with \(\Lambda_G = 800\) MeV/c, the summed TPE amplitude is \(-0.74\) and the one-pion rescattering amplitude is \(0.22\).
- For \(V_{low-k}\) generated from the CD-Bonn potential with cutoff \(\Lambda_{low-k} = 4\) fm\(^{-1}\) (CD-4), the summed TPE amplitude is \(-0.65\) and the one-pion rescattering amplitude is \(0.23\).
- For \(V_{low-k}\) with cutoff \(\Lambda_{low-k} = 5\) fm\(^{-1}\) (CD-5), the summed TPE amplitude is \(-0.46\) and the one-pion rescattering amplitude is \(0.22\).
- In plane-wave approximation, the summed TPE amplitude is \(-0.37\) and the one-pion rescattering amplitude is \(0.080\).
- The "leading" MCS terms \(t_1\) for the four TPE diagram types (I, II, III, IV) are proportional to 0, -1, -1/2, and 3/2 respectively, summing to zero.
- The ratios \(R_K = T_K / T_{Resc}\) (plane-wave matrix elements relative to rescattering) are: Type I: \(-0.70\), Type II: \(-6.54\), Type III: \(-6.60\), Type IV: \(9.19\), Sum: \(-4.65\).
- After subtracting the \(t_1\) terms, the ratios \(R_K^{\star}\) become: Type I: \(-0.70\), Type II: \(-0.82\), Type III: \(-3.73\), Type IV: \(0.60\), Sum: \(-4.65\).

## Context

This work extends earlier HB\(\chi\)PT studies of the \(pp\to pp\pi^0\) reaction (e.g., by Hanhart and Kaiser (2002) using the momentum counting scheme, and by the author's group in Refs. [dkms99, kkms06, ksmk07a]) by demonstrating that the "sub-leading" parts of the TPE diagrams, which were not considered by Hanhart and Kaiser, give contributions comparable to or larger than the one-pion rescattering amplitude. The results contradict the expectation from the momentum counting scheme that these sub-leading parts should be suppressed by a factor of \(\tilde{\epsilon} \simeq 0.4\); in particular, the "cross-box" diagram (Type III) shows an amplitude more than a factor \(\tilde{\epsilon}^{-1}\) larger than expected. The findings enable future work to include these TPE contributions in a quantitative description of all \(NN\to NN\pi\) reaction channels, and motivate a re-examination of the momentum counting scheme's treatment of the heavy nucleon propagator versus the propagator advocated for MCS.
