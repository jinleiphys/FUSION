# Nuclear chiral and magnetic rotation in covariant density functional theory

> Excitations of chiral rotation observed in triaxial nuclei and magnetic and/or antimagnetic rotations seen in near-spherical nuclei have attracted a lot of attention. Unlike conventional rotation in well-deformed or superdeformed nuclei, here the rotational axis is not necessary coinciding with any principal axis of the nuclear density distribution. Thus, tilted axis cranking is mandatory to describe these excitations self-consistently in the framework of covariant density functional theory (CDFT). We will briefly introduce the formalism of tilted axis cranking CDFT and its application for magnetic and antimagnetic rotation phenomena. Configuration-fixed CDFT and its predictions for nuclear chiral configurations and for favorable triaxial deformation parameters are also presented, and the discoveries of the multiple chiral doublets (M\c{hi}D) in 133Ce and 103Rh are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how covariant density functional theory (CDFT), implemented via tilted axis cranking, can self-consistently describe magnetic rotation, antimagnetic rotation, and nuclear chirality in nuclei where the rotational axis does not coincide with any principal axis of the nuclear density distribution. The authors emphasize that these exotic rotational modes require a three-dimensional or two-dimensional cranking approach, and that the point-coupling version of CDFT reduces computational cost enough to allow applications from light nuclei such as \(^{60}\)Ni up to heavy nuclei such as \(^{198,199}\)Pb.

## Method

The authors present the formalism of tilted axis cranking (TAC) within covariant density functional theory, using both meson-exchange and point-coupling effective interactions. For magnetic and antimagnetic rotation, a two-dimensional cranking CDFT is employed, while for chiral rotation a three-dimensional cranking solution is required. Configuration-fixed CDFT calculations are used to predict favorable triaxial deformation parameters and chiral configurations, and the results are compared with experimental data on multiple chiral doublets (M\(\chi\)D) in \(^{133}\)Ce and \(^{103}\)Rh.

## Key numbers

* More than 195 magnetic dipole bands had been observed in 85 nuclei as of 2013.
* The first experimental evidence for multiple chiral doublets (M\(\chi\)D) was reported in \(^{133}\)Ce in 2013, where negative parity bands 5 and 6 with a \(\pi(1h_{11/2})^2\otimes \nu(1h_{11/2})^{-1}\) configuration and positive parity bands 2 and 3 with a \(\pi[(1g_{7/2})^{-1}(1h_{11/2})^1] \otimes \nu(1h_{11/2})^{-1}\) configuration form two pairs of chiral doublet bands.
* In the chiral rotor model of Frauendorf and Meng (1997), the two lowest bands are nearly degenerate within the spin interval \(13 < I < 18\) for a proton \(h_{11/2}\) particle and neutron \(h_{11/2}\) hole coupled to a triaxial rotor with \(\gamma = 30^\circ\).
* The TAC CDFT with point-coupling interaction has been applied to magnetic rotation in \(^{60}\)Ni, \(^{58}\)Fe, and \(^{198,199}\)Pb, and to antimagnetic rotation in \(^{105}\)Cd.

## Context

This work extends the tilted axis cranking approach originally introduced by Frauendorf (1993) using a quadrupole-quadrupole Hamiltonian, and later developed in nonrelativistic density functional theories by Olbratowski et al. (2004, 2006). The covariant DFT framework described here incorporates nuclear magnetism and time-odd fields self-consistently, building on earlier principal-axis cranking CDFT by Koepf and Ring (1989, 1990) and König and Ring (1993). The review enables future microscopic studies of chiral doublet bands and multiple chiral doublets in triaxial nuclei, and provides a foundation for further experimental searches for magnetic and antimagnetic rotation across the nuclear chart.
