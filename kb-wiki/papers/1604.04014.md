# Spectroscopy of 46Ar by the (t,p) two-neutron transfer reaction

> States in the $N=28$ nucleus $^{46}$Ar have been studied by a two-neutron transfer reaction at REX-ISOLDE (CERN). A beam of radioactive $^{44}$ at an energy of 2.16~AMeV and a tritium loaded titanium target were used to populate $^{46}$ by the t($^{44}$,p) two-neutron transfer reaction. Protons emitted from the target were identified in the T-REX silicon detector array. The excitation energies of states in $^{46}$ have been reconstructed from the measured angles and energies of recoil protons. Angular distributions for three final states were measured and based on the shape of the differential cross section an excited state at 3695~keV has been identified as $J^π= 0^+$. The angular differential cross section for the population of different states are compared to calculations using a reaction model employing both sequential and direct transfer of two neutrons. Results are compared to shell model calculations using state-of-the-art effective interactions.

## Key claim

The paper identifies a previously unknown excited state in 46Ar at 3695(4) keV as a 0+ state based on the characteristic L=0 shape of its angular differential cross section from the (t,p) two-neutron transfer reaction, but the assignment is conditional on the agreement with DWBA calculations and the observed gamma decay pattern (decay only to the 2+ state, not directly to the ground state).

## Method

A radioactive beam of 44Ar at 2.16 AMeV from REX-ISOLDE impinged on a tritium-loaded titanium target (36 μg/cm2 effective tritium thickness) to populate 46Ar via the t(44Ar,p) two-neutron transfer reaction in inverse kinematics. Recoil protons were identified in the T-REX silicon detector array using the ΔE-E method, and excitation energies were reconstructed from measured proton angles and kinetic energies; gamma rays were detected in coincidence with the MINIBALL germanium array. Angular distributions for three final states were measured and compared to DWBA calculations using FRESCO, employing both sequential two-step (t,d)(d,p) and direct one-step pair transfer, with spectroscopic amplitudes from shell model calculations using SDPF-U, SDPF-MU, and EPQQM effective interactions.

## Key numbers

- Excited state at 3695(4) keV identified as Jπ = 0+ (from sum of gamma-ray transition energies; also measured as 3660(60) keV from proton reconstruction)
- First excited 2+ state at 1554 keV (known)
- Additional states placed at 4255(4) keV and 5144(4) keV from gamma-ray coincidences
- Beam energy: 2.16 AMeV
- Average beam intensity: 2 × 105/s of 44Ar
- Target thickness: 0.5 mg/cm2 titanium foil with tritium-to-titanium atomic ratio 1.3, effective tritium thickness 36 μg/cm2
- The N=28 shell gap in 47Ar is reduced by 330(90) keV compared to 49Ca (from prior work cited as Gaudefroy (2006))
- B(E2; 2+1 → 0+gs) values from prior Coulomb excitation: 39(8) e2fm4 (Scheit (1996)), 44(6) e2fm4 (Gade (2003)), 54(5) e2fm4 (Calinescu (2014)); lifetime measurement gave 114+67-32 e2fm4 (Mengoni (2010))

## Context

This work extends studies of the N=28 shell closure in neutron-rich nuclei, specifically addressing conflicting results on collectivity in 46Ar from B(E2) measurements and the nature of excited 0+ states predicted by shell model calculations (Nowacki (2009), Utsuno (2012)) and generator coordinate method calculations (Rodriguez (2002)). The (t,p) reaction provides unambiguous identification of 0+ states via L=0 angular distributions, complementing prior in-beam experiments (Riley (2005), Dombradi (2003)) that had tentatively assigned a 0+ state at 2710 keV based on gamma-ray coincidences alone. The results enable testing of state-of-the-art effective interactions (SDPF-U, SDPF-MU, EPQQM) and reaction models for two-neutron transfer, and clarify the structure of low-lying 0+ states that may indicate shape coexistence or shell erosion in the region.
