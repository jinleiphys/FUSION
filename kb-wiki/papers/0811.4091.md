# Microscopic linear response calculations based on the Skyrme functional plus the pairing contribution

> A self-consistent Quasiparticle-Random-Phase-Approximation (QRPA) model which employs the canonical Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) basis and an energy-density functional with a Skyrme mean field part and a density-dependent pairing, is used to study the monopole collective excitations of spherical even-even nuclei. The influence of the spurious state on the strength function of the isoscalar monopole excitations is clearly assessed. We compare the effect of different kinds of pairing forces (volume pairing, surface pairing and mixed pairing) on the monopole excitation strength function. The energy of the Isoscalar Giant Monopole Resonance (ISGMR), which is related to the nuclear incompressibility $K_{\infty}$, is calculated for tin isotopes and the results are discussed.

# Microscopic linear response calculations based on the Skyrme functional plus the pairing contribution

## Key claim

The fully self-consistent HFB+QRPA model, using a Skyrme mean field and density-dependent pairing, brings the theoretical ISGMR centroid energies in tin isotopes significantly closer to experiment than HF+RPA or HF-BCS+QRPA, but only the combination of the SKM* force with a surface pairing force reproduces the experimental data from $^{112}$Sn to $^{120}$Sn, implying that the nuclear incompressibility $K_{\infty}$ extracted from tin is about 10% smaller than the value obtained from $^{208}$Pb.

## Method

The authors solve the HFB equations in coordinate space inside a spherical box, using a Skyrme force (SLy5 or SKM*) in the particle-hole channel and a zero-range density-dependent pairing force in the particle-particle channel, with the pairing strength fitted to the experimental neutron pairing gap of $^{120}$Sn. The QRPA equations are then solved in the canonical HFB basis, with the spurious state from particle-number symmetry breaking projected out, and the isoscalar monopole strength function and its centroid energy are computed for a chain of tin isotopes.

## Key numbers

- The pairing strength $v_0$ for the Skyrme force SLy5 is -170.92, -537.05, and -272.99 MeV fm$^3$ for volume, surface, and mixed pairing, respectively; for SKM* it is -142.01, -490.48, and -233.22 MeV fm$^3$ for the same three pairing types.
- The ISGMR centroid energies from HFB+QRPA using SKM* and surface pairing, without pairing-rearrangement terms, are 16.32, 16.13, and 16.0 MeV for $^{112}$Sn, $^{116}$Sn, and $^{124}$Sn, respectively; including pairing-rearrangement changes these to 16.34, 16.11, and 16.02 MeV (a negligible effect of about 0.1%).
- The force SLy5 has $K_{\infty} = 233.8$ MeV, while SKM* has $K_{\infty}$ around 215 MeV; the results imply that $K_{\infty}$ from tin data is about 10% smaller than the 230-240 MeV range obtained from $^{208}$Pb.
- The cutoff on occupation probability is set at $v^2_{cut} = 10^{-9}$ and the excitation energy cutoff at $E_{cut} = 200$ MeV, giving stability at the level of 300 keV for the centroid energy.

## Context

This work extends the fully self-consistent HFB+QRPA approach introduced by Terasaki (2005) to the study of the isoscalar monopole resonance in tin isotopes, addressing the puzzle raised by Piekarewicz (2007) of why tin appears "so soft" compared to $^{208}$Pb. Previous non-relativistic RPA calculations (Peru 2005, Sil 2006, Sagawa 2007) and relativistic RPA/QRPA calculations (Vretenar 2003, Piekarewicz 2007) had overestimated the ISGMR centroid energies in tin. The present results show that pairing, especially surface pairing, lowers the centroid energies and partially resolves the discrepancy, but the remaining 10% variation in $K_{\infty}$ between different nuclei points to the need for improved understanding of the density dependence of nuclear effective functionals, particularly the surface and asymmetry contributions to nuclear incompressibility.
