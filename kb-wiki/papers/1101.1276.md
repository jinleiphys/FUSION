# Tagging of eta' decay products to analyze chiral restoration

> In case of chiral U_A(1) symmetry restoration the mass of the eta' boson (the ninth, would-be Goldstone boson) is decreased, thus its production cross section is heavily enhanced. The eta' decays (through one of its decay channels) into five pions. These pions will not be correlated in terms of Bose-Einsten correlations, thus the production enhancement changes the strength of two-pion correlation functions at low momentum. Preliminary results strongly support the mass decrease of the eta' boson. In this paper we propose a method to select pions coming from eta' decays. We investigate the efficiency of the proposed kinematical cut in several collision systems and energies with several simulators. We prove that our method can be used in all investigeted collision systems.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a kinematic selection method to tag pions originating from eta' decays, which could be used to test whether the eta' mass decreases due to partial U_A(1) chiral symmetry restoration in hot QCD matter; the method is shown to work in simulations but its efficiency depends strongly on detector acceptance and collision system.

## Method

The authors simulate p+p collisions at 200 GeV and 14 TeV using Pythia 8.135 and Hijing 1.411, and Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV using Hijing. They apply invariant mass cuts on pion pairs (0.075–0.171 GeV^2/c^4) and quadruplets (0.43–0.69 GeV^2/c^4) from the decay chain eta' → eta + pi+ + pi- → (pi+ + pi- + pi0) + pi+ + pi-. They test two tagging strategies: a single-particle method (starting from one pion and finding three others to form a quadruplet) and a pair method (starting from a same-sign pion pair and finding an opposite-sign pair). Detector geometries of STAR, PHENIX, ALICE, and CMS are applied as acceptance cuts.

## Key numbers

- The invariant mass interval for pion pairs from eta' decays is 0.075–0.171 GeV^2/c^4 (based on simulations, derived from the theoretical range 0.078–0.168 GeV^2/c^4).
- The invariant mass interval for quadruplets from eta' decays is 0.43–0.69 GeV^2/c^4 (derived from the theoretical range 0.43–0.69 GeV^2/c^4).
- The eta' has a vacuum mass of 958 MeV and a mean lifetime of 1000 fm/c.
- The overall probability of the decay chain eta' → eta + pi+ + pi- → (pi+ + pi- + pi0) + pi+ + pi- is 10%.
- The average momentum of the five pions from eta' decay is 138 MeV.
- 1,000,000 p+p events at sqrt(s)=200 GeV were generated with both Pythia and Hijing; 10,000 p+p events at sqrt(s)=14 TeV; 100 Au+Au events at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV with Hijing.

## Context

This work extends the core-halo model of Bose-Einstein correlations (Csorgo, 1999) and earlier suggestions that a decreased eta' mass would enhance eta' production and reduce the lambda parameter in two-pion correlation functions (Vance, 1998; Csanad, 2005; Csorgo, 2009; Vertesi, 2009). The proposed tagging method is similar to one investigated for e+e- collisions by Kulka (1990). If applied experimentally, the method could help confirm whether the observed decrease in the lambda parameter at low momentum is indeed caused by enhanced eta' production, providing evidence for partial chiral symmetry restoration.
