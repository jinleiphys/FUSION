# $pp \to pK^{+}Λ$ reaction in an effective Lagrangian model

> We investigate the $pp \to pK^{+}Λ$ reaction within an effective Lagrangian model where the contributions to the amplitudes are taken into account within the tree level. The initial interaction between the two nucleons is modeled by the exchange of $π$, $ρ$, $ω$ and $σ$ mesons and the $ΛK^{+}$ production proceeds via the excitation of the $N^*$(1650), $N^*$(1710), $N^*$(1720) baryonic resonances. The parameters of the model at the nucleon-nucleon-meson vertices are determined by fitting the elastic nucleon-nucleon scattering with an effective interaction based on the exchange of these four mesons, while those at the resonance vertices are calculated from the known decay widths of the resonances as well as the vector meson dominance model. Available experimental data is described well by this approach. The one-pion-exchange diagram dominates the production process at both higher and lower beam energies. The $ρ$ and $ω$ meson exchanges make negligible contributions. However, the $σ$-exchange processes contribute substantially to the total cross sections at lower beam energies. The excitation of the $N^*$(1710) and $N^*$(1650) resonances dominate this reaction at beam momenta above and below 3 GeV/c respectively. The interference among the amplitudes of various resonance excitation processes is significant. For beam energies very close to the $K^{+}$ production threshold the hyperon-proton final state interaction effects are quite important. The data is selective about the model used to describe the low energy scattering of the two final state baryons.

## Key claim

The paper claims that an effective Lagrangian model with tree-level amplitudes, including the exchange of π, ρ, ω, and σ mesons and excitation of the N*(1650), N*(1710), and N*(1720) resonances, describes available experimental data for the pp → pK⁺Λ reaction well, but the abstract glosses over that the σ-exchange contributes substantially only at lower beam energies and that the final state interaction effects are important only for beam energies very close to the K⁺ production threshold.

## Method

The initial nucleon-nucleon interaction was modeled by exchange of π, ρ, ω, and σ mesons, with coupling constants determined by fitting elastic nucleon-nucleon scattering T-matrices in the beam energy range 400 MeV to 4.0 GeV. The K⁺Λ production proceeded via excitation of the N*(1650), N*(1710), and N*(1720) baryonic resonances, with resonance coupling constants calculated from known decay widths and the vector meson dominance model. The amplitudes included interference terms between resonance excitations, and final state interaction effects were incorporated using the Watson-Migdal theory for near-threshold energies.

## Key numbers

- The N*(1710) resonance dominates the reaction at beam momenta above 3 GeV/c, while the N*(1650) resonance dominates below 3 GeV/c.
- The one-pion-exchange diagram dominates the production process at both higher and lower beam energies.
- The σ-exchange processes contribute substantially to total cross sections at lower beam energies, while ρ and ω meson exchanges make negligible contributions.
- The N*(1650), N*(1710), and N*(1720) resonances have decay branching ratios into the K⁺Λ channel of 3-11%, 5-25%, and 1-15% respectively.
- The cut-off parameter λ for all N*N-meson vertices was fixed to 0.850 GeV.
- The coupling constant g²/4π for the N*(1710)ΛK⁺ vertex is 2.9761, for the N*(1720)ΛK⁺ vertex is 0.0817, and for the N*(1650)ΛK⁺ vertex is 0.0485.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous effective Lagrangian studies of π⁰ and π⁺ production in proton-proton collisions (Shyam 1998, Engel 1996) to the strangeness production channel. It differs from earlier resonance models by Tsushima (1997) and Sibirtsev (1998) by including interference terms between resonance amplitudes and using pseudovector coupling for the NNπ vertex, and from single-boson exchange approaches by Ferrario (1960) and Laget (1991) by explicitly including resonance excitations. The model enables systematic studies of associated strangeness production at COSY, particularly for near-threshold beam momenta where the new data probe hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-strange meson interactions.
