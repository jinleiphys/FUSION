# Prospects for spin physics in semi-inclusive processes

> I discuss inclusive and semi-inclusive lepton-hadron scattering emphasizing the importance of polarization in order to study various single or double spin asymmetries and the importance of particle identification and angular resolution in the dectection of final state particles to study azimuthal asymmetries. The observables obtained in this way enable a detailed study of quark and gluon correlations in hadrons.

## Key claim

Semi-inclusive lepton-hadron scattering, particularly with polarized beams and targets, provides access to quark and gluon correlation functions beyond the leading twist, including transverse-momentum-dependent distribution and fragmentation functions, but the interpretation is limited to the current fragmentation region where the rapidity gap between target remnant and produced hadron is large.

## Method

The author performs a field-theoretic analysis of the soft parts in deep inelastic scattering, parametrizing the quark correlation functions \(\Phi_{ij}(p,P,S)\) for distributions and \(\Delta_{ij}(k,P_h,S_h)\) for fragmentation in terms of Dirac projections. The analysis uses lightcone coordinates and an expansion in \(1/Q\) to classify twist-two (leading) and twist-three (subleading) functions, then computes one-particle inclusive lepton-hadron cross sections via one-photon exchange, folding the soft parts with the hard parton-level scattering.

## Key numbers

- The Bjorken sum rule relates \(\int dx (g_1^p - g_1^n)(x)\) to the nucleon axial charge, a number also obtained from neutron \(\beta\)-decay.
- The cross section for unpolarized semi-inclusive scattering at leading order is \(\frac{d\sigma_{OO}}{d\xbj\,dy\,dz_h} = \frac{2\pi \alpha^2 s}{Q^4} \sum_{a,\bar a} e_a^2 \left[1 + (1-y)^2\right] \xbj f_1^a(\xbj) D_1^a(z_h)\).
- The double-spin asymmetry cross section for longitudinally polarized lepton and target is \(\frac{d\sigma_{LL}}{d\xbj\,dy\,dz_h} = \frac{2\pi \alpha^2 s}{Q^4} \lambda_e \lambda \sum_{a,\bar a} e_a^2 y(2-y) \xbj g_1^a(\xbj) D_1^a(z_h)\).
- The subleading single-spin asymmetry for a transversely polarized target appears at order \(1/Q\) with a \(\sin(\phi_S)\) modulation and involves the twist-three function \(H(z)\).
- The leading-twist azimuthal asymmetry for a longitudinally polarized target with a transversely polarized lepton yields a \(\cos(\phi_h - \phi_S)\) modulation proportional to \(g_{1T}^{(1)}(\xbj) D_1(z_h)\).
- The function \(g_{1T}^{(1)}(x)\) is estimated from SLAC E143 data on \(g_2\) via the relation \(g_{1T}^{(1)}(x) = -\int_x^1 dy\, g_2(y)\).

## Context

This work extends the framework of Soper (1977), Jaffe (1983), and Collins and Soper (1982) for parton distribution and fragmentation functions to include transverse-momentum-dependent and spin-dependent correlations at leading and subleading twist. It systematizes the classification of twist-two and twist-three functions for both distributions and fragmentation, including T-odd fragmentation functions such as \(H_1^\perp\) first discussed by Collins (1993). The results enable experimental searches for azimuthal asymmetries in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering at facilities such as ELFE, and provide a theoretical basis for extracting quark-gluon correlations from measurements of spin asymmetries and transverse momentum distributions.
