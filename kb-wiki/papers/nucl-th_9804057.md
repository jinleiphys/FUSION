# Three-body halos. V. Computations of continuum spectra for Borromean nuclei

> We solve the coordinate space Faddeev equations in the continuum. We employ hyperspherical coordinates and provide analytical expressions allowing easy computation of the effective potentials at distances much larger than the ranges of the interactions where only s-waves in the different Jacobi coordinates couple. Realistic computations are carried out for the Borromean halo nuclei 6He (n+n+α) for Jπ= 0+-, 1+-, 2+- and 11Li (n+n+9Li) for (1/2)+-, (3/2)+-, (5/2)+-. Ground state properties, strength functions, Coulomb dissociation cross sections, phase shifts, complex S-matrix poles are computed and compared to available experimental data. We find enhancements of the strength functions at low energies and a number of low-lying S-matrix poles.

# Three-body halos. V. Computations of continuum spectra for Borromean nuclei

## Key claim

The authors solve the coordinate space Faddeev equations in the continuum for Borromean halo nuclei, providing analytical expressions for effective potentials at large distances where only s-waves in different Jacobi coordinates couple, and find that the method yields reliable continuum spectra when the two-body interactions are sufficiently well known, though the results for 11Li remain more uncertain than for 6He due to incomplete knowledge of the binary subsystems.

## Method

The authors employ hyperspherical coordinates to solve the Faddeev equations for three-body systems, expanding the total wave function in generalized angular functions and solving coupled radial differential equations. At large distances, where the effective potentials become analytically tractable, they derive asymptotic expressions using square well potentials that reproduce the correct scattering lengths and effective ranges. They compute ground state properties, strength functions, Coulomb dissociation cross sections, phase shifts, and complex S-matrix poles for 6He (n+n+alpha) and 11Li (n+n+9Li) across multiple spin-parity channels.

## Key numbers

- For 6He: the neutron-alpha p-wave resonances used as input have energies and widths of E(p3/2)=0.77 MeV, Gamma(p3/2)=0.64 MeV, E(p1/2)=1.97 MeV, and Gamma(p1/2)=5.22 MeV
- For 6He: the neutron-alpha s-wave potential corresponds to a scattering length of -2.13 fm and an effective range of 1.38 fm
- The neutron-alpha interaction potentials are parametrized with Gaussian shapes: V_nc^(l=0)=48.00 exp(-r^2/2.33^2) MeV, V_nc^(l=1)=-47.40 exp(-r^2/2.30^2) -25.49 l*s_n exp(-r^2/1.72^2) MeV, V_nc^(l=2)=-21.93 exp(-r^2/2.03^2) -25.49 l*s_n exp(-r^2/1.72^2) MeV
- The neutron-neutron s-wave potential used is -31 MeV exp(-r^2/(1.8 fm)^2) from Johansen (1990)
- Computations are carried out for 6He for J^pi = 0^pm, 1^pm, 2^pm and for 11Li for J^pi = 1/2^pm, 3/2^pm, 5/2^pm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of three-body halo systems (Fedorov 1993, 1994, 1995; Jensen 1997) by generalizing the hyperspherical Faddeev method from bound-state calculations to continuum spectra. The method addresses the technical difficulty of computing effective potentials at large distances, which had previously limited reliable computations of Borromean continuum spectra as noted by Friar (1995). The results enable more accurate interpretation of fragmentation reaction data for halo nuclei from experiments such as those by Bergmann (1993), Korsheninnikov (1997), and Zinser (1997), and provide a framework for computing observables like Coulomb dissociation cross sections that are essential for understanding Borromean nuclear structure.
