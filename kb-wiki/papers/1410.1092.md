# A Stringy (Holographic) Pomeron with Extrinsic Curvature

> We model the soft pomeron in QCD using a scalar Polyakov string with extrinsic curvature in the bottom-up approach of holographic QCD. The overall dipole-dipole scattering amplitude in the soft pomeron kinematics is shown to be sensitive to the extrinsic curvature of the string for finite momentum transfer. The characteristics of the diffractive peak in the differential elastic $pp$ scattering are affected by a small extrinsic curvature of the string.

## Key claim

The soft pomeron in QCD can be modeled as a closed string exchange with extrinsic curvature in a bottom-up holographic approach, and the diffractive peak in elastic proton-proton scattering shows sensitivity to a small extrinsic curvature parameter, though the slope parameter B(t) remains unchanged.

## Method

The authors model dipole-dipole scattering using a scalar Polyakov string action with an extrinsic curvature term in a 5-dimensional AdS space with a wall (truncated AdS5). They compute the closed string exchange propagator on a cylinder topology with twisted boundary conditions, regularize the infinite products using zeta function regularization, and analytically continue the Euclidean angle to rapidity. The resulting scattering amplitude is then compared to elastic pp differential cross section data at three center-of-mass energies.

## Context

This work extends the holographic pomeron approach of Stoffers, Zahed, and collaborators (2012-2013) by adding extrinsic curvature to the effective string action, as originally proposed by Polyakov (1986) for smooth QCD flux tubes. The analysis builds on the dipole-dipole scattering formalism developed by Basar et al. (2012) and uses the same bottom-up AdS5 geometry with a wall that captures UV conformality and IR confinement. The results enable empirical estimation of the extrinsic curvature of QCD strings from pp scattering data, complementing lattice measurements of the string tension and Luscher term.
