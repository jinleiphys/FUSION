# Theory of deuteron stripping. From surface integrals to generalized $R$-matrix approach

> There are two main reasons for absence of the practical theory of stripping to resonance states which could be used by experimental groups: numerical problem of the convergence of the DWBA matrix element when the full transition operator is included and it is unclear what spectroscopic information can be extracted from the analysis of transfer reactions populating the resonance states. The purpose of this paper is to address both questions. The theory of the deuteron stripping is developed, which is based on the post continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) formalism going beyond of the DWBA and surface integral formulation of the reaction theory [A. S. Kadyrov et al., Ann. Phys. {\bf 324}, 1516 (2009)]. First, the formalism is developed for the DWBA and then extended to the CDCC formalism, which is ultimate goal of this work. The CDCC wave function takes into account not only the initial elastic $d+ A$ channel but also its coupling to the deuteron breakup channel $p + n + A$ missing in the DWBA. Stripping to both bound states and resonances are included. The convergence problem for stripping to resonance states is solved in the post CDCC formalism. The reaction amplitude is parametrized in terms of the reduced width amplitudes (ANCs), inverse level matrix, boundary condition and channel radius, that is the same parameters which are used in the conventional $R$-matrix method. For stripping to resonance states many-level, one and two-channel cases are considered. The theory provides a consistent tool to analyze both binary resonant reactions and deuteron stripping in terms of the same parameters.

## Key claim

The paper develops a theory of deuteron stripping to resonance states that solves the convergence problem of the DWBA matrix element and parametrizes the reaction amplitude using the same reduced width amplitudes (ANCs), inverse level matrix, boundary condition, and channel radius employed in the conventional \(R\)-matrix method, but only for narrow resonances where the imaginary part of the resonance momentum is much smaller than the real part.

## Method

The author starts from the post-form DWBA amplitude for stripping to bound states and splits the configuration space over the relative coordinate \(r_{nA}\) into internal and external regions at a channel radius \(R_{nA}\). Using Green's theorem, the dominant external post-form matrix element is transformed into a surface integral encircling the internal volume plus a small external prior-form contribution. The formalism is then extended to the post continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) framework, which includes coupling to the deuteron breakup channel \(p+n+A\) missing in DWBA, and applied to stripping into resonance states by writing the resonance wave function in standard \(R\)-matrix form with separation into internal and external parts.

## Key numbers

- The relation between ANC and partial resonance width is given by \([C_{A\,j\,l}^{F}]^{2}= (-1)^{l}e^{2\,\phi_{j\,l}(k_{xA(0)j\,l})}\,\frac{\mu_{xA}}{k_{xA(0)j\,l}}\,\Gamma_{xA\,j\,l}\), where \(k_{xA(0)j\,l}\) is the real part of the resonance relative momentum and \(\phi_{j\,l}\) is the non-resonant scattering phase shift.
- The condition for narrow resonance is \(k_{xA(I)j\,l} \ll k_{xA(0)j\,l}\), where \(k_{xA(I)j\,l}\) is the imaginary part of the resonance momentum.
- The reduced-width amplitude is expressed as \(\gamma_{nA\,j_{nA}\,l_{nA}} = \sqrt{R_{nA}/(2\mu_{nA})}\,i^{l_{nA}+1}\kappa_{nA}C_{A\,j_{nA}\,l_{nA}}^{F}h_{l_{nA}}^{(1)}(i\kappa_{nA}R_{nA})\), where \(\kappa_{nA} = \sqrt{2\mu_{nA}\varepsilon_{nA}}\) is the bound state wave number.

## Context

This work extends the surface-integral formulation of reaction theory by Kadyrov et al. (2009) from binary reactions to deuteron stripping, addressing the long-standing problem of extracting spectroscopic information from transfer reactions populating resonance states. It builds on earlier attempts from the 1970s by Huby, Vincent, Bunakov, and others who used methods such as convergence factors, contour integration, and complex scaling to handle divergent radial integrals, but goes beyond DWBA by using the CDCC formalism. The theory enables simultaneous analysis of binary resonant reactions and deuteron stripping using the same \(R\)-matrix parameters, and can be applied to \((n,\gamma)\) resonant capture and Trojan Horse reactions as noted by Lacognata.
