# Dynamical generation of $J^P=3/2-$ resonances and the $螞(1520)$ resonance

> The lowest order chiral Lagrangian is used to study s-wave interactions of the baryon decuplet with the octet of pseudoscalar mesons. The coupled channel Bethe Salpeter equation is used to obtain dynamically generated $\tfrac{3}{2}^-$ resonances in the partial wave amplitudes which provide a reasonable description to a number of 3 and 4-star resonances like $螖(1700)$, $螞(1520)$, $危(1670)$, $危(1940)$, $螢(1820)$, $惟(2250)$ etc. The phenomenological introduction of d-wave channels in the coupled channel scheme along with the existing s-wave channels is shown to provide a much improved description of the $螞(1520)$ resonance. The prediction of the absolute strength of the cross section in the reactions $K^-p\to螞蟺^0蟺^0$ and $K^-p\to螞蟺^+蟺^-$ provided by this scheme shows a good agreement with existing data.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the $\Lambda(1520)$ resonance can be dynamically generated as a quasibound $\pi\Sigma^*$ state through s-wave interactions of the baryon decuplet with pseudoscalar mesons, but only after phenomenologically including d-wave $\bar{K}N$ and $\pi\Sigma$ channels does the model reproduce the resonance's correct mass and width.

## Method
The authors use the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian (Weinberg-Tomozawa term) to compute s-wave scattering amplitudes for the baryon decuplet with the pseudoscalar meson octet. They solve the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation to obtain unitarized partial-wave amplitudes, searching for poles in the complex energy plane. For the $\Lambda(1520)$, they additionally introduce phenomenological d-wave transition potentials for $\bar{K}N$ and $\pi\Sigma$ channels, with parameters fitted to experimental partial-wave amplitudes from $\bar{K}N\to\bar{K}N$ and $\bar{K}N\to\pi\Sigma$ scattering data.

## Key numbers
- The $\Lambda(1520)$ resonance width obtained from the fit is $\Gamma_{\Lambda(1520)} = 15.6$ MeV.
- The coupling constants of the $\Lambda(1520)$ to the different channels are: $g_1 = 0.91$ (to $\pi\Sigma^*$), $g_2 = -0.29$ (to $K\Xi^*$), $g_3 = -0.54$ (to $\bar{K}N$), and $g_4 = -0.45$ (to $\pi\Sigma$).
- The subtraction constants obtained from the fit are $a_0 = -1.8$ for s-wave channels and $a_2 = -8.1$ for d-wave channels.
- The fitted parameters for the d-wave transition potentials are $\gamma_{13} = 0.98$ and $\gamma_{14} = 1.1$ in units of $10^{-7}$ MeV$^{-3}$, and $\gamma_{33} = -1.7$, $\gamma_{44} = -0.7$, $\gamma_{34} = -1.1$ in units of $10^{-12}$ MeV$^{-5}$.
- The predicted branching ratio of $\Lambda(1520)$ into $\pi\pi\Lambda$ (via $\pi\Sigma^*$) is 0.14, while branching ratios for $\bar{K}N$ and $\pi\Sigma$ are 0.45 and 0.43 respectively.

## Context
This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to $J^P = 1/2^-$ resonances (Kaiser, 1995; Ramos, 1997) to the $J^P = 3/2^-$ sector by studying s-wave interactions of the baryon decuplet with mesons. It improves upon the earlier treatment by Sarkar (2004) which only included $\pi\Sigma^*$ and $K\Xi^*$ channels and produced a $\Lambda(1520)$ with too high mass and too large width (about 130 MeV). The inclusion of d-wave $\bar{K}N$ and $\pi\Sigma$ channels enables the model to reproduce the correct resonance properties and predict cross sections for $K^-p\to\Lambda\pi^0\pi^0$ and $K^-p\to\Lambda\pi^+\pi^-$ reactions, which agree with experimental data from Prakhov (2004) and Mast (1973).
