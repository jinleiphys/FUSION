# Nuclides as a liquid phase of $SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R$ chiral perturbation theory I: emergence of pion-less SU(2) $χ$ PT

> The Standard Model of particle physics, augmented with neutrino mixing, is at least very nearly the complete theory of interactions of known particles at energies accessible to Nature on Earth. Candidate effective theories of nuclear structure must therefore reflect SM symmetries, especially the chiral global $SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R$ symmetry of two-massless-quark QCD. For ground-state nuclei, SU(2) chiral perturbation theory (XPT) enables perturbation in inverse powers of $Λ_{XSB}\simeq 1 GeV$, with analytic operators renormalized to all loop orders. We show that pion-less "Static Chiral Nucleon Liquids" (SXNL) emerge as a liquid phase of SU(2) XPT of protons, neutrons and 3 Nambu-Goldstone boson pions. Far-IR pions decouple from SXNL, simplifying the derivation of saturated nuclear matter and microscopic liquid drops (ground-state nuclides). We trace to the global symmetries of two-massless-quark QCD the power of pion-less SU(2) XPT to capture experimental ground-state properties of certain nuclides with even parity, spin zero, even proton number Z, and neutron number N. We derive the SXNL effective SU(2) XPT Lagrangian, including all order $Λ_{XSB},Λ^0_{XSB}$ operators. These include: all 4-nucleon operators that survive Fierz rearrangement in the non-relativistic limit, and effective Lorentz-vector iso-vector neutral "$ρ$-exchange" operators. SXNL motivate nuclear matter as non-topological solitons at zero pressure: the Nuclear Liquid Drop Model and Bethe-Weizsacker Semi-Empirical Mass Formula emerge in an explicit Thomas-Fermi construction provided in the companion paper. For chosen nuclides, nuclear Density Functional and Skyrme models are justified to order $Λ_{χSB}^0$. We conjecture that inclusion of higher order operators will result in accurate "natural" Skyrme, No-Core-Shell, and neutron star models.

## Key claim

The authors show that pion-less SU(2) chiral perturbation theory emerges as a semi-classical "liquid phase" of full SU(2) chiral perturbation theory, but only for static ground-state nuclides with even parity, spin zero, even proton number Z, and even neutron number N, where far-infrared Nambu-Goldstone boson pions decouple from the nucleon liquid.

## Method

The authors start from the SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R chiral perturbation theory Lagrangian for protons, neutrons, and three pions, truncated to order Λ_χSB and (Λ_χSB)^0 in the chiral limit. They take expectation values of the pion equations of motion in the nuclear ground state, showing that all nucleon current expectation values vanish except for the neutral vector charge density ⟨J^3_0⟩, and that static (∂_0π = 0) infrared pions have no nucleon source, decoupling completely. The resulting effective Lagrangian for the Static Chiral Nucleon Liquid contains only free nucleon kinetic terms and four-nucleon contact interactions that survive Fierz rearrangement in the non-relativistic limit.

## Key numbers

- Λ_χSB ≃ 1 GeV: the chiral symmetry breaking scale used for power counting in the perturbation expansion
- f_π = 93 MeV: the pion decay constant appearing in the chiral Lagrangian
- m_π ≃ 140 MeV: the pion mass, compared to up quark mass m_up ≃ 6 MeV and down quark mass m_down ≃ 12 MeV
- For the Walecka model comparison: g_σ²/m_σ² = 284.3 GeV⁻² and g_ω²/m_ω² = 208.8 GeV⁻², fit to nuclear matter density k_Fermi ≃ 1.42/fm and binding energy E_binding/nucleon ≃ 16 MeV

## Context

This work extends Lynn's (1993) original proposal of an SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R chiral liquid phase, which included explicit chiral symmetry breaking terms from non-zero quark masses but did not derive pion-less SU(2) χPT. The paper contrasts with non-topological soliton models such as the Walecka model (Chin and Walecka, 1974; Serot and Walecka, 1986), which suffer from loss of predictive power due to uncontrollable quantum loop corrections. The results justify the empirical success of pion-less chiral perturbation theory, the Nuclear Liquid Drop Model, and the Bethe-Weizsäcker Semi-Empirical Mass Formula, and enable rigorous derivation of nuclear Density Functional and Skyrme models to order Λ_χSB⁰, with conjectured extension to No-Core-Shell and neutron star models upon inclusion of higher-order operators.
