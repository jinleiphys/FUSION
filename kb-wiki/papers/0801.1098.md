# Block Diagonalization using SRG Flow Equations

> By choosing appropriate generators for the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) flow equations, different patterns of decoupling in a Hamiltonian can be achieved. Sharp and smooth block-diagonal forms of phase-shift equivalent nucleon-nucleon potentials in momentum space are generated as examples and compared to analogous low-momentum interactions ("v_lowk").

## Key claim

By choosing a block-diagonal flow operator in the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) equations, one can generate sharp and smooth block-diagonal forms of nucleon-nucleon potentials that are phase-shift equivalent to the original potential, with the decoupling scale set by the cutoff Lambda rather than the flow parameter lambda.

## Method

The authors evolve an initial nucleon-nucleon potential using SRG flow equations with a block-diagonal flow operator G_s = P H_s P + Q H_s Q, where P and Q are projection operators defined by a cutoff Lambda on relative momenta. They implement both sharp step-function cutoffs and smooth exponential regulators f_Lambda(k) = exp[-(k^2/Lambda^2)^n] with n=4 and n=8, evolving the N^3LO potential from Entem and Machleidt (2003) and the Argonne v_18 potential from Wiringa, Stoks, and Schiavilla (1995) to various values of the flow parameter lambda = 1/s^(1/4).

## Key numbers

- Sharp block-diagonal SRG evolution with Lambda = 2 fm^(-1) and lambda = 0.5 fm^(-1) produces potentials in striking agreement with V_lowk at the same cutoff
- Smooth block-diagonal evolution with n=4 and Lambda = 2.0 fm^(-1) works well down to lambda = 1.5 fm^(-1); for smaller lambda the potential becomes distorted
- By lambda = 1 fm^(-1) in the sharp block-diagonal case, the unevolved and evolved phase shift curves for the ^3S_1 partial wave are indistinguishable to the width of the line up to about 300 MeV
- Phase shift errors at E_lab = 100 MeV show a shoulder in Lambda_cut with slope matching the power 2n fixed by the smooth regulator, with the shoulder saturating at Lambda_cut approximately equal to Lambda

## Context

This work extends the SRG approach of Bogner, Furnstahl, and Perry (2007) and the V_lowk method of Bogner, Kuo, and Schwenk (2003) by showing that block-diagonal potentials can be generated directly through SRG flow equations rather than through T-matrix invariance. The method enables the generation of phase-shift equivalent potentials with tailored decoupling patterns, including bizarre choices where the decoupled region consists of multiple rectangles, and opens the possibility of generating V_lowk three-body forces through a tractable SRG framework, though full decoupling in few-body space remains an open question.
