# Octet Baryon Charge Radii, Chiral Symmetry and Decuplet Intermediate States

> We compute the octet baryon charge radii to O(1/Heavy^3) in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. We examine the effect of including the decuplet of spin-3/2 baryons explicitly. We find that it does no t improve the level of agreement between the HBchiPT and experimental values for the Sigma^- charge radius.

## Key claim
The authors compute octet baryon charge radii to order 1/Heavy^3 in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBchiPT) and find that explicitly including the decuplet of spin-3/2 baryons does not improve the agreement between theory and experimental values for the Sigma^- charge radius.

## Method
The authors perform a heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculation of the octet baryon charge radii, working to O(1/Heavy^3). They include loop diagrams with both intermediate octet and decuplet baryon states, evaluating the contributions from diagrams at O(1/Heavy^2) and O(1/Heavy^3) with the decuplet treated as an explicit degree of freedom.

## Key numbers
- The calculation is performed to O(1/Heavy^3) in the heavy baryon expansion, where "Heavy" denotes the baryon mass scale.
- The Sigma^- charge radius is the specific observable where the inclusion of decuplet intermediate states does not improve agreement with experiment.

## Context
This work extends previous heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations of baryon properties by including decuplet intermediate states explicitly. The finding that decuplet contributions do not improve the Sigma^- charge radius result contrasts with expectations that such states might resolve discrepancies between HBchiPT and experiment. The calculation enables future work to explore whether other observables or higher-order terms might benefit from explicit decuplet inclusion, or whether alternative approaches beyond HBchiPT are needed for baryon charge radii.
