# Self-consistent calculations of the strength function and radiative neutron capture cross section for stable and unstable tin isotopes

> The E1 strength function for 15 stable and unstable Sn even-even isotopes from A=100 till A=176 are calculated using the self-consistent microscopic theory which, in addition to the standard (Q)RPA approach, takes into account the single-particle continuum and the phonon coupling. Our analysis shows two distinct regions for which the integral characteristics of both the giant and pygmy resonances behave rather differently. For neutron-rich nuclei, starting from $^{132}$Sn, we obtain a giant E1 resonance which significantly deviates from the widely-used systematics extrapolated from experimental data in the $β$-stability valley. We show that the inclusion of the phonon coupling is necessary for a proper description of the low-energy pygmy resonances and the corresponding transition densities for $A<$132 nuclei, while in the $A>132$ region the influence of phonon coupling is significantly smaller. The radiative neutron capture cross sections leading to the stable $^{124}$Sn and unstable $^{132}$Sn and $^{150}$Sn nuclei are calculated with both the (Q)RPA and the beyond-(Q)RPA strength functions and shown to be sensitive to both the predicted low-lying strength and the phonon coupling contribution. The comparison with the widely-used phenomenological Generalized Lorentzian approach shows considerable differences both for the strength function and the radiative neutron capture cross section. In particular, for the neutron-rich $^{150}$Sn, the reaction cross section is found to be increased by a factor greater than 20. We conclude that the present approach may provide a complete and coherent description of the $γ$-ray strength function for astrophysics applications. In particular, such calculations are highly recommended for a reliable estimate of the electromagnetic properties of exotic nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a self-consistent microscopic theory combining (Q)RPA with phonon coupling and a discretized single-particle continuum can describe the E1 strength function for both stable and unstable tin isotopes, but the success is conditional: the phonon coupling is necessary for a proper description of pygmy resonances in nuclei with A less than 132, while for A greater than 132 its influence is significantly smaller, and the SLy4 Skyrme force may require modifications for a simultaneous description of both the giant and pygmy dipole resonances.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using the SLy4 Skyrme force to obtain ground states, then computed the E1 strength function for 15 even-even tin isotopes from A=100 to A=176 using both the (Q)RPA and the quasi-particle time blocking approximation (QTBA), which includes phonon coupling. The single-particle continuum was treated via a discretization procedure, and 14-16 low-lying phonons of multipolarity L=2-6 were included in the QTBA calculations. Radiative neutron capture cross sections for 124Sn, 132Sn, and 150Sn were calculated using the TALYS reaction code with both the (Q)RPA and QTBA strength functions.

## Key numbers

- For 132Sn, the calculated GDR mean energy is 14.3 MeV and width is 2.9 MeV using the SLy4 force, compared to experimental values of 16.1 plus or minus 0.7 MeV and 4.7 plus or minus 2.1 MeV
- For the A=100-132 region, phonon coupling increases the GDR width by as much as 2 MeV compared to (Q)RPA predictions
- The integrated PDR strength for 132Sn in the 0-10 MeV interval is 4% of the EWSR with phonon coupling included and 2% without, compared to an experimental value of 4 plus or minus 3%
- For the neutron-rich 150Sn, the radiative neutron capture cross section is increased by a factor greater than 20 when using the QTBA strength function compared to the phenomenological Generalized Lorentzian approach
- The smearing parameter used in all calculations is Delta = 200 keV
- A quasi-particle energy cutoff of 100 MeV was used

## Context

This work extends self-consistent calculations of dipole strength from stable nuclei to a long chain of tin isotopes, including both stable and unstable species. It builds on previous self-consistent (Q)RPA calculations by Goriely (2002, 2004) and the relativistic RQRPAZ approach by Ring and collaborators (2009), as well as the non-self-consistent Quasi-particle-Phonon Model of Ponomarev and collaborators. The results enable improved predictions of radiative neutron capture cross sections for r-process nucleosynthesis calculations, particularly for neutron-rich exotic nuclei where phenomenological systematics based on beta-stability valley data fail. The paper also provides a new empirical formula for the GDR mean energy that depends on both A and neutron excess.
