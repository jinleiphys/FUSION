# A simple description of the states $0^+$ and $2^+$ in $^{168}Er$

> A sixth-order quadrupole boson Hamiltonian is used to describe 26 states $0^+$ and 67 states $2^+$ which have been recently identified in $^{168}Er$. Two closed expressions are alternatively used for energy levels. One corresponds to a semi-classical approach while the other one represents the exact eigenvalue of the model Hamiltonian. The semi-classical expression involves four parameters, while the exact eigenvalue is determined by five parameters. In each of the two descriptions a least square fit procedure is adopted. Both expressions provide a surprisingly good agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

A sixth-order quadrupole boson Hamiltonian, treated either semiclassically or via exact eigenvalues, can parameterize the excitation energies of 26 \(0^+\) and 67 \(2^+\) states in \(^{168}\text{Er}\) with surprisingly good agreement, despite the authors' acknowledgment that single-particle degrees of freedom likely dominate the physics of many of these states.

## Method

The authors used a sixth-order quadrupole boson Hamiltonian and derived two closed-form expressions for the excitation energies. The first expression came from a semiclassical treatment, averaging the Hamiltonian on a coherent state and quantizing the resulting classical Hamilton function, yielding a four-parameter formula. The second expression used the exact eigenvalues of the same Hamiltonian in a basis labeled by boson number \(N\) and seniority \(v\), yielding a five-parameter formula. In both approaches, a least-squares fit was performed to the experimental data for \(^{168}\text{Er}\) from a \((p,t)\) reaction.

## Key numbers

- The semiclassical fit gave parameters: \(A = 394.2\ \text{keV}\), \(B = -10.4\ \text{keV}\), \(F = 0.3865\ \text{keV}\), \(\mathcal{C} = -280\ \text{keV}\).
- The exact-eigenvalue fit gave parameters: \(A = 83.2319\ \text{keV}\), \(B = -10.2454\ \text{keV}\), \(C = 24.00\ \text{keV}\), \(F = 0.3865\ \text{keV}\), \(\gamma = 299.8638\ \text{keV}\).
- The data set comprised 26 \(0^+\) states and 67 \(2^+\) states in \(^{168}\text{Er}\), populated via a \((p,t)\) reaction.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological approach of Raduta (2005, 2006), which organized intrinsic states into horizontal bands using a SU(2) group in a fictitious space, to a realistic parameterization of experimental data. It contrasts with microscopic models such as the projected shell model (PSM) and the quasiparticle phonon model (QPM) used by Bucur (2006) to describe the same data, which the authors note have inherent drawbacks regarding fermion-space truncation and Pauli-principle violation. The simple analytical formulae enable a compact description of many states and suggest that anharmonicities can simulate single-particle behavior, but the authors note that electromagnetic transition data are needed for a complete description.
