# Calculations of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance within the self-consistent RPA and its extensions

> The strength distributions of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance have been calculated in $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca, $^{90}$Zr, $^{112-124}$Sn, $^{144}$Sm, and $^{208}$Pb nuclei within the self-consistent random phase approximation and its extensions which include pairing correlations and quasiparticle-phonon coupling. The results are compared with the available experimental data. The problem of the nuclear matter incompressibility is discussed.

## Key claim

The self-consistent random phase approximation (RPA) and its extensions (QRPA and QTBA) can reproduce the mean energies of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) across a range of nuclei, but the value of the nuclear matter incompressibility modulus \(K_{\infty}\) remains ambiguous at \(210 \pm 30\) MeV because different Skyrme force parametrizations (T5 with \(K_{\infty}=202\) MeV and T6 with \(K_{\infty}=236\) MeV) each describe some nuclei well but not all simultaneously.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) plus Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) calculations using Skyrme energy functionals, then computed ISGMR strength distributions via three models: the RPA, the quasiparticle RPA (QRPA), and the quasiparticle time blocking approximation (QTBA) which includes quasiparticle-phonon coupling (QPC) to generate the spreading width. The calculations used two Skyrme force parametrizations (T5 and T6) with nucleon effective mass \(m^* = m\), and the single-particle continuum was exactly included on the RPA level.

## Key numbers

- T5 Skyrme force: \(K_{\infty}=202\) MeV; T6 Skyrme force: \(K_{\infty}=236\) MeV.
- For \(^{112}\)Sn, QTBA with T5 gives peak energy \(E_{\text{GMR}} = 15.8\) MeV and width \(\Gamma = 3.7\) MeV, compared to experimental \(E_{\text{GMR}} = 16.1^{+0.1}_{-0.1}\) MeV and \(\Gamma = 4.0^{+0.4}_{-0.4}\) MeV.
- For \(^{124}\)Sn, QTBA with T5 gives \(E_{\text{GMR}} = 15.1\) MeV and \(\Gamma = 3.8\) MeV, compared to experimental \(E_{\text{GMR}} = 14.8^{+0.2}_{-0.2}\) MeV and \(\Gamma = 4.5^{+0.5}_{-0.5}\) MeV.
- For \(^{208}\)Pb, QTBA with T6 gives \(m_1/m_0 = 14.1\) MeV and \(\Gamma = 3.2\) MeV, compared to experimental \(m_1/m_0 = 13.96^{+0.20}_{-0.20}\) MeV (TAMU) and \(\Gamma = 2.88^{+0.20}_{-0.20}\) MeV (TAMU).
- For \(^{208}\)Pb, QTBA with T5 gives \(m_1/m_0 = 12.8\) MeV, which is 1.2 MeV lower than the TAMU experimental value.
- For \(^{16}\)O, RPA with T5 gives \(\sqrt{m_1/m_{-1}} = 21.3\) MeV, compared to experimental \(19.63^{+0.38}_{-0.38}\) MeV.
- Smearing parameter \(\Delta = 500\) keV used in all calculations.

## Context

This work extends self-consistent RPA calculations of the ISGMR (e.g., Blaizot (1980), Blaizot et al. (1995)) by including pairing correlations and quasiparticle-phonon coupling via the QTBA, which is necessary to reproduce the total width of the resonance. The results confirm that the non-relativistic estimate \(K_{\infty} = 210 \pm 30\) MeV remains ambiguous, as the T5 force (\(K_{\infty}=202\) MeV) describes tin isotopes well but underestimates \(^{208}\)Pb energies, while the T6 force (\(K_{\infty}=236\) MeV) matches \(^{208}\)Pb but overestimates tin isotopes by 1.2-1.9 MeV. The findings enable future work to refine the incompressibility modulus by requiring simultaneous description of ISGMR energies and widths across a broader set of nuclei.
