# Upper limit set by causality on the tidal deformability of a neutron star

> A principal goal of gravitational-wave astronomy is to constrain the neutron star equation of state (EOS) by measuring the tidal deformability of neutron stars. The tidally induced departure of the waveform from that of point-particle (or spinless binary black hole (BBH)) increases with the stiffness of the EOS. We show that causality (the requirement that the speed of sound is less than the speed of light for a perfect fluid satisfying a one-parameter equation of state) places an upper bound on tidal deformability as a function of mass. Like the upper mass limit, the limit on deformabity is obtained by using an EOS with $v_{sound} = c$ for high densities and matching to a low density (candidate) EOS at a matching density of order nuclear saturation density. We use these results and those of [B.D. Lackey et al., Phys. Rev. D 89, 043009 (2014)] to estimate the resulting upper limit on the gravitational-wave phase shift of a black hole-neutron star (BHNS) binary relative to a BBH. Even for assumptions weak enough to allow an maximum mass of $4\ M_\odot$ (a match at nuclear saturation density to an unusually stiff low-density candidate EOS), the upper limit on dimensionless tidal deformability is stringent. It leads to a still more stringent estimated upper limit on the maximum tidally induced phase shift prior to merger. We comment in an appendix on the relation between causality, the condition $v_{sound} < c$, and the condition $dp/dε< 1$ for the effective EOS governing the equilibrium star.

## Key claim

Causality (requiring sound speed less than light speed) places an upper bound on the tidal deformability of neutron stars as a function of mass, but this bound is remarkably close to the values predicted by candidate equations of state, meaning the constraint is stringent even when using unusually stiff low-density EOS assumptions that allow maximum masses up to 4 solar masses.

## Method

The authors construct a "matched causal EOS" by taking the stiff MS1 candidate EOS below a matching density (typically nuclear saturation density) and using the maximally stiff causal EOS \(p - p_{\mathrm{match}} = \epsilon - \epsilon_{\mathrm{match}}\) above that density. They numerically integrate the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain sequences of static spherical neutron stars, then compute the tidal Love number \(k_2\) and tidal deformability \(\lambda\) using the Hinderer method of solving the perturbed Einstein equations in the Regge-Wheeler gauge. To estimate gravitational-wave phase shifts, they apply the numerical fit from Lackey et al. (2014) that extends post-Newtonian results to late inspiral, evaluating the phase shift at the cutoff frequency where tidal disruption damps the amplitude by a factor of \(e\).

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: \(\epsilon_{\mathrm{nuc}} = 2.7 \times 10^{14}\ \mathrm{g/cm^3}\)
- Maximum mass from matched causal EOS at \(\epsilon_{\mathrm{match}} = \epsilon_{\mathrm{nuc}}\): \(M_{\mathrm{max}} = 4.1\ M_{\odot}\)
- Maximum radius from matched causal EOS at \(\epsilon_{\mathrm{match}} = \epsilon_{\mathrm{nuc}}\): \(R_{\mathrm{max}} > 18\ \mathrm{km}\)
- Maximum dimensionful tidal deformability at \(\epsilon_{\mathrm{match}} = \epsilon_{\mathrm{nuc}}\): \(\lambda < 1.5 \times 10^{37}\ \mathrm{g\ cm^2\ s^2}\)
- Dimensionless tidal deformability limit for a \(1.4\ M_{\odot}\) star at \(\epsilon_{\mathrm{match}} = \epsilon_{\mathrm{nuc}}\): \(\Lambda \leq 2300\)
- For comparison, \(1.4\ M_{\odot}\) stars from candidate EOSs give \(\Lambda\) values of 300 (SLy), 490 (MPA1), 900 (H4), and 1400 (MS1)
- Power-law fits for \(\epsilon_{\mathrm{match}} \lesssim \epsilon_{\mathrm{nuc}}\): \(M_{\mathrm{max}} = (4.1\ M_{\odot}) (\epsilon_{\mathrm{match}}/\epsilon_{\mathrm{nuc}})^{-0.4999}\), \(R_{\mathrm{max}} = (17\ \mathrm{km}) (\epsilon_{\mathrm{match}}/\epsilon_{\mathrm{nuc}})^{-0.4990}\), \(\lambda_{\mathrm{max}} = (1.3 \times 10^{37}\ \mathrm{g\ cm^2\ s^2})(\epsilon_{\mathrm{match}}/\epsilon_{\mathrm{nuc}})^{-2.4996}\)
- For a \(1.4\ M_{\odot}\) star: \(\Lambda_{1.4} = 2400 (\epsilon_{\mathrm{match}}/\epsilon_{\mathrm{nuc}})^{-1.8}\) for \(0.33\epsilon_{\mathrm{nuc}} < \epsilon_{\mathrm{match}} < 1.2\epsilon_{\mathrm{nuc}}\)
- The estimated maximum gravitational-wave phase shift from the matched causal EOS differs from the stiffest candidate EOS by less than 14%

## Context

This work extends the approach used by Rhoades and Ruffini (1974) and Lattimer (2012) for causal upper limits on neutron-star mass and radius to the case of tidal deformability. The authors match to the MS1 EOS (Muller and Serot, 1996) as a conservative stiff low-density candidate, and apply the numerical waveform fitting results of Lackey et al. (2014) to estimate phase shifts in black hole-neutron star binaries. The result enables gravitational-wave astronomers to interpret measured tidal deformabilities as direct constraints on the equation of state, since the causal bound is close enough to candidate EOS values to be observationally relevant.
