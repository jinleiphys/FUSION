# Composite nature of exotic states from data analysis

> We introduce two basic concepts: the compositeness for resonance and the Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson (CDD) pole. Applying them to hadron states $X(3872)$, $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$, and $T_{cc}$, we obtain their compositeness values and achieve deeper insights into their inner structure.

# Composite nature of exotic states from data analysis

## Key claim
The compositeness of exotic hadron states can be extracted from experimental data using a parameterization that includes a Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson (CDD) pole, which works more broadly than the effective range expansion (ERE) method. The CDD pole's location relative to threshold determines whether the state is more molecular (CDD pole far from threshold) or more elementary (CDD pole close to threshold), but the compositeness values obtained for specific states span wide ranges and depend on the chosen scenario.

## Method
The scattering amplitude is constructed using the N/D method with inclusion of a CDD pole, expressed as t(s) = [γ²/(s − M_CDD²) + G(s)]⁻¹, where G(s) is the two-point loop function in dimensional regularization. For resonances, the compositeness X is defined as X = |γ² dG(s_R)/ds_R|, evaluated on the unphysical Riemann sheet where the resonance appears, provided the resonance mass exceeds the lightest hadron-pair threshold. The signal shape is parameterized by |d(E)|², derived from the amplitude by removing the zero at the CDD pole, and fitted to experimental invariant mass spectra.

## Key numbers
- For X(3872): the compositeness can range from 0 to 1 depending on the scenario (bound state, virtual state, or higher-order virtual-state pole in the limit of vanishing D*0 width).
- For Z_b(10610): compositeness ranges from 0.39 (when M_CDD = m_th) to 1.0 (pure molecule).
- For Z_b(10650): compositeness ranges from 0.36 (when M_CDD = m_th) to 1.0 (pure molecule).
- For T_cc: compositeness is predicted to be 0.23^{+0.40}_{-0.09}, indicating a large portion of elementary (tetraquark) degree of freedom.

## Context
This work extends the compositeness concept from Weinberg's original definition for bound states (Weinberg, 1962; Weinberg, 1965) to the resonance case using the generalization by Guo and Oller (2015). It applies the CDD-pole parameterization, which is more general than the effective range expansion, to the exotic hadrons X(3872), Z_b(10610), Z_b(10650), and T_cc, using experimental data from Belle (2015), BESIII (2023), and LHCb (2021). The approach enables quantification of molecular versus elementary components in these states, with the T_cc result suggesting a significant tetraquark contribution based on the Morgan rule (Morgan, 1992).
