# Single-particle dissipation in TDHF studied from a phase-space perspective

> We study dissipation and relaxation processes within the time-dependent Hartree-Fock approach using the Wigner distribution function. On the technical side we present a geometrically unrestricted framework which allows us to calculate the full six-dimensional Wigner distribution function. With the removal of geometrical constraints, we are now able to extend our previous phase-space analysis of heavy-ion collisions in the reaction plane to unrestricted mean-field simulations of nuclear matter on a three-dimensional Cartesian lattice. From the physical point of view we provide a quantitative analysis on the stopping power in TDHF. This is linked to the effect of transparency. For the medium-heavy $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca system we examine the impact of different parametrizations of the Skyrme force, energy-dependence, and the significance of extra time-odd terms in the Skyrme functional.

## Key claim

The paper provides a quantitative analysis of stopping power and dissipation in TDHF for the medium-heavy ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca system, showing that the occurrence of transparency is clearly reflected in the global asymmetry of the Wigner momentum distribution, but the two distributions in phase space never truly combine to form a single distribution, indicating that two-body collisions will be necessary to achieve true equilibrium.

## Method

The authors solved the TDHF equations on a three-dimensional Cartesian lattice with 1 fm mesh spacing, using a Taylor-series expansion of the time-evolution operator up to sixth order with a time step of 0.2 fm/c. They computed the full six-dimensional Wigner distribution function from the density matrix via partial Fourier transform, using Open MP parallelization and fast Fourier transforms. For central collisions of ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca at impact parameter b=0, they varied the Skyrme parametrization (SLy4, SLy6, SkMs, SkI3, SkI4), the center-of-mass energy, and the inclusion of extra time-odd terms in the Skyrme functional, analyzing global observables including the quadrupole deformation in momentum space (β_k, γ_k), the occupied phase-space volume V_k, and the coordinate-space quadrupole moment Q²⁰_r.

## Key numbers

- The numerical grid was set up with 36×24² grid points for the ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca calculations.
- The typical mesh spacing was 1 fm, with a time step of t=0.2 fm/c.
- Storing the Wigner function reduced to the reaction plane at one time step consumed approximately 140 Mb of disk space.
- Center-of-mass energies were varied from E_c.m.=2 MeV/nucleon up to E_c.m.=3 MeV/nucleon, corresponding to 160 MeV to 240 MeV total.
- Effective masses m*/m for the Skyrme forces ranged from 0.79 (SkM*) down to 0.57 (SkI3), with corresponding maximal β_k values from 0.0116 down to 0.0095.
- For the lowest energy case E_c.m.=160 MeV, the calculation showed two fully separated fragments in the exit channel; for the highest energy E_c.m.=240 MeV, the system merged.

## Context

This work extends previous phase-space analyses by Loebl (2011) that were restricted to one and two dimensions, now enabling full three-dimensional Wigner function analysis without geometric constraints. The study builds on earlier TDHF calculations by Simenel (2011) demonstrating applicability up to very high energies, and on the work of Maruhn (1985) which found splitting behavior depending on the Skyrme parametrization. The results enable future systematic investigations of impact parameter and energy dependence for fusion and deep-inelastic reactions, as well as heavier systems, though the authors note such studies are beyond current computational feasibility.
