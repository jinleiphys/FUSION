# Schiff moments of deformed nuclei

> Stimulated by recent suggestion of Cosmic Axion Spin Precession Experiment with Eu contained compound we develop a new method for accurate calculation of Schiff moments of even-odd deformed nuclei. The method is essentially based on experimental data on magnetic moments and E1,E3-amplitudes in the given even-odd nucleus and in adjacent even-even nuclei. Unfortunately such sets of data are not known yet for most of interesting nuclei. Fortunately the full set of data is available for $^{153}$Eu. Hence, we perform the calculation for $^{153}$Eu and find value of the Schiff moment. The value is about 30 times larger than a typical Schiff moment of a spherical heavy nucleus. The enhancement of the Schiff moment in $^{153}$Eu is related to the low energy octupole mode. On the other hand the value of Schiff moment we find is 30 times smaller than that obtained in the assumption of static octupole deformation.

## Key claim

The Schiff moment of \(^{153}\)Eu is about 30 times larger than that of a typical spherical heavy nucleus, but also about 30 times smaller than the value obtained under the assumption of static octupole deformation; the enhancement arises from the low-energy octupole vibrational mode rather than from static octupole deformation.

## Method

The author develops a method that combines Nilsson-model wave functions for the quadrupole-deformed even-odd nucleus with an admixture of the collective octupole vibrational mode, whose mixing coefficient \(\alpha\) is extracted from experimental E1 transition amplitudes in \(^{153}\)Eu and the adjacent even-even nucleus \(^{152}\)Sm. The octupole deformation parameter \(\beta_3\) is determined by reanalyzing inelastic electron scattering data on \(^{152}\)Sm from Bertozzi (1972). The T,P-odd mixing matrix element between the parity doublet states is then computed using the T,P-odd interaction Hamiltonian, and the Schiff moment is evaluated from the resulting electric dipole moment and the intrinsic octupole contribution.

## Key numbers

- The mixing coefficient between Nilsson states and the octupole vibration is \(\alpha \approx 0.20\), corresponding to a 4% admixture weight.
- The quadrupole deformation of \(^{153}\)Eu is \(\beta_2 \approx 0.29\), determined from the ground-state quadrupole moment \(Q = 2.412\) barn.
- The octupole deformation parameter extracted from electron scattering data is \(\beta_3 = 0.10\).
- The Schiff moment in the laboratory frame is \(S = -2.97 \times 10^{-7} \, \eta \, [e \cdot \text{fm}^3]\), with \(\eta\) the dimensionless T,P-odd coupling constant.
- The Schiff moment of \(^{153}\)Eu is about 30 times larger than a typical Schiff moment of a spherical heavy nucleus, and about 30 times smaller than the estimate assuming static octupole deformation.

## Context

This work extends the method of Sushkov (1984) for calculating Schiff moments in deformed nuclei by incorporating experimental data on magnetic moments and E1,E3 amplitudes, and it directly contradicts the static octupole deformation model proposed by Flambaum (2020) for \(^{153}\)Eu. The result enables accurate predictions for the Cosmic Axion Spin Precession Experiment (CASPEr) using EuCl\(_3\cdot\)6H\(_2\)O crystals, as suggested by A. Sushkov (2023), and provides a template for calculating Schiff moments in other deformed nuclei such as \(^{237}\)Np using \(^{238}\)U as a reference.
