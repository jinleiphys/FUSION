# Re-formation of Many-Quark Model with the su(4)-Algebraic Structure in the Schwinger Boson Realization

> The energy eigenstates for the su(4)-algebraic model for many-quark system obtained by the present authors in the Schwinger boson space are reconsidered in the original fermion space. Through this task, the structures of the single-quark and the quark-triplet are clarified. Variations of the su(4)-generators which have been adopted by the present authors are discussed.

## Key claim

The energy eigenstates previously derived in the Schwinger boson space for the su(4)-algebraic many-quark model are transcribed into the original fermion space, clarifying that the single-quark operators are the quark operators themselves and revealing the explicit color-singlet structure of the quark-triplet creation operators, though the eigenstates are not color-singlets unless symmetrized over the three color permutations.

## Method

The authors take the minimum weight states and state-generating operators (the su(4)-generators) that were defined in the Schwinger boson representation and find their counterparts in the original fermion space using the explicit bilinear forms of the quark creation and annihilation operators. They then rewrite the eigenstates using multi-commutator techniques to express the action of the operator (S^4(1))^{2ρ} on single-quark and quark-triplet creation operators, yielding a fermionic form that depends on the colors 1 and 3. Finally, they symmetrize over the three color permutations to restore color-singlet behavior on average.

## Key numbers

* The degeneracy of each color state is (2j_s+1), where j_s is a half-integer.
* The minimum weight state is characterized by four quantum numbers: λ, ρ, σ₀, and σ, with the relation σ ≥ σ₀.
* The eigenvalues of the quark-number operators for the minimum weight state are n for color 1 and n₀ for colors 2 and 3, with n ≥ n₀.
* The relation between (σ₀, σ) and (n₀, n) is σ₀ = (1/2)(2j_s+1) - (1/2)(n₀+n) and σ = (1/2)(2j_s+1) - n₀.
* The color-symmetrized state yields average quark numbers ⟨N₁⟩ = ⟨N₂⟩ = ⟨N₃⟩ = N/3.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Tsue et al., 2012, referred to as (I)) which treated the su(4)-algebraic Bonn model (Petry et al., 1983) in the Schwinger boson space but did not explicitly consider the color-symmetric nature of the model. The transcription into the original fermion space clarifies the structure of the single-quark and quark-triplet operators, enabling the identification of the quark-triplet with the "nucleon" in the spherical j-j coupling shell model. The general formulation also allows for variations of the su(4)-generators applicable to hadron physics, nuclear physics, and atomic physics (e.g., trapped three-color atom gases as studied by Errea et al.).
