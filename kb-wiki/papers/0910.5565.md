# The Study of Mass Distribution of products in 7.0 AMeV U238+U238 Collisions

> Within the Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics (ImQMD) Model incorporating the statistical decay Model, the reactions of U238+U238 at the energy of 7.0 AMeV have been studied. The charge, mass and excitation energy distributions of primary fragments are investigated within the ImQMD model and de-excitation processes of those primary fragments are described by the statistical decay model. The mass distribution of the final products in U238+U238 collisions is obtained and compared with the recent experimental data.

## Key claim

The Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics (ImQMD) model combined with a statistical decay model (HIVAP) can reproduce the main features of the experimental mass distribution of products in 7.0 AMeV U238+U238 collisions, although the calculated yields in the mass region 170-200 are overestimated and the yields of transuranium nuclei are underestimated compared to the data at 7.10 AMeV.

## Method

The reaction was simulated in two steps: first, the dynamical formation and re-separation of the composite system was described by the ImQMD model with isospin-dependent nucleon-nucleon scattering cross sections and Pauli blocking, using initial deformed U238 nuclei (epsilon=0.24) with random orientations. Second, the de-excitation of primary fragments (mass number > 50) was treated with the HIVAP statistical evaporation code incorporating a three-Gaussian empirical model for fission fragment mass distributions, with parameters fitted to available experimental fission data for actinide and transactinide nuclei. The matching time between the two models was set to 3000 fm/c, and 500 events per impact parameter were calculated with a maximum impact parameter of 14 fm.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy: 7.0 AMeV for the U238+U238 reaction
- Maximum impact parameter for inelastic scattering: 14 fm
- Matching time between ImQMD and statistical decay model: 3000 fm/c
- Average lifetime of the composite system in central collisions reaches over ~1000 fm/c at incident energies 1000-1300 MeV (from prior work cited in the paper)
- Average excitation energy of primary fragments near uranium (90 ≤ Z ≤ 94) for impact parameters b < 4 fm: about 120 MeV, with maximum reaching 200 MeV
- Scattering angle cut applied for comparison with experiment: 56° ≤ θ ≤ 84° and 96° ≤ θ ≤ 124° in the center-of-mass frame
- Experimental data compared: mass distributions at 6.09, 6.49, 6.91, 7.10, and 7.35 AMeV from Golabek et al. (2008)

## Context

This work extends prior studies of strongly damped reactions in very heavy systems such as U238+U238, which were investigated experimentally by Hildenbrand et al. (1977), Schadel et al. (1978), and Freiesleben et al. (1979). It builds on the ImQMD model developed by Wang et al. (2005) and Tian et al. (2008) for studying transient composite systems, and on the macroscopic Langevin-type approach of Zagrebaev and Greiner (2007). The results enable comparison with recent experimental data from GANIL (Golabek et al., 2008; Heinz et al., 2006-2007) and provide a microscopic framework for understanding the origin of products in different mass regions, including the potential production of neutron-rich superheavy nuclei.
