# Neutrino Electromagnetic Form Factors Effect on the Neutrino Cross Section in Dense Matter

> The sensitivity of the differential cross section of the interaction between neutrino-electron with dense matter to the possibly nonzero neutrino electromagnetic properties has been investigated. Here, the relativistic mean field model inspired by effective field theory has been used to describe non strange dense matter, both with and without the neutrino trapping. We have found that the cross section becomes more sensitive to the constituent distribution of the matter, once electromagnetic properties of the neutrino are taken into account. The effects of electromagnetic properties of neutrino on the cross section become more significant for the neutrino magnetic moment mu_nu > 10^{-10} mu_B and for the neutrino charge radius R > 10^{-5} MeV^{-1}.

## Key claim
The differential cross section for neutrino-electron scattering in dense matter becomes significantly sensitive to the constituent distribution (protons, neutrons, electrons, muons) when neutrino electromagnetic properties are included, but only if the neutrino magnetic moment exceeds approximately \(10^{-10} \mu_B\) or the neutrino charge radius exceeds approximately \(10^{-5} \text{ MeV}^{-1}\); below these thresholds the effect is negligible.

## Method
The authors used a relativistic mean field (RMF) model inspired by effective field theory (E-RMF), specifically the G2* parameter set, to describe non-strange dense matter at zero temperature. They calculated the neutrino-matter differential cross section by including weak, electromagnetic, and interference contributions from neutrinos with nonzero electromagnetic form factors (magnetic moment and charge radius), using the polarization tensor formalism for nucleons and leptons. The calculation was performed for two scenarios: matter without trapped neutrinos and matter with trapped neutrinos at a fixed electronic-leptonic fraction \(Y_{l_e}=0.3\).

## Key numbers
- The threshold for significant electromagnetic effects is \(\mu_{\nu} > 10^{-10} \mu_B\) (Bohr magneton) and \(R > 10^{-5} \text{ MeV}^{-1}\) for the neutrino charge radius.
- The laboratory bound on the neutrino magnetic moment is \(\mu_{\nu} < 1.0 \times 10^{-10} \mu_B\) at 90% confidence level; a stronger astrophysical bound is \(\mu_{\nu} \leq 3.0 \times 10^{-12} \mu_B\) from red giant studies.
- The charge radius of \(\nu_e\) is bounded by the LAMF experiment as \(R^2 = (0.9 \pm 2.7) \times 10^{-32} \text{ cm}^2 = (22.5 \pm 67.5) \times 10^{-12} \text{ MeV}^{-2}\).
- The weak coupling constant used is \(G_F = 1.023 \times 10^{-5} / M^2\), where \(M\) is the nucleon mass.
- Calculations were performed at baryon densities \(\rho_B = 2.5 \rho_0\) and \(\rho_B = 5.0 \rho_0\), with neutrino energy \(E_{\nu} = 5\) MeV and fixed momentum transfer \(q_1 = 2.5\) MeV for some figures.
- The muon production threshold in the E-RMF model occurs at \(\rho \approx 3 \rho_0\) for the trapped neutrino case, compared to \(\rho \approx 2 \rho_0\) in the standard RMF model of Chiapparini (2003).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of neutrino-electron interactions in an electron gas (Caroline et al., 2005) to dense stellar matter including nucleons and muons. It contrasts with standard weak-interaction-only calculations (e.g., Reddy et al., 1998; Horowitz, 2002) by incorporating neutrino electromagnetic form factors, and it uses the E-RMF model (Furnstahl, 1996) rather than the standard RMF model used by Chiapparini (2003) for neutrino-trapped matter. The results suggest that for supernova and protoneutron star simulations, neutrino electromagnetic properties could become relevant at high densities, especially in neutrino-rich matter, and that future work should include strange matter, RPA correlations, and finite temperature effects.
