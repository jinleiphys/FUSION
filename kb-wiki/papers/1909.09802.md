# Strangeness and hypernuclear production in fragmentation reactions induced by antikaons

> Formation mechanism of hyperfragments with strangeness s=-1 and s=-2 in collisions of antikaons on nuclei has been investigated within a microscopic transport model. Dynamics of pseudoscalar mesons and hyperons is modeled within the transport model, in which all possible reaction channels for creating hyperons such as the elastic scattering, resonance production and decay, strangeness exchange reaction and direct strangeness production in meson-baryon and baryon-baryon collisions have been included. A coalescence approach is developed for constructing hyperfragments in phase space and the decay process is described with a statistical approach. It is found that the $Ξ^{-}$ production is correlated to the K$^{+}$ formation and the hyperons $Λ$ and $Σ$ are created within a broad rapidity region. The production cross sections of nucleonic fragments and hyperfragments weakly depends on the incident momentum. The yields of $Λ-$ hyperfragments are the six order of magnitude of $Ξ^{-}-$ hyperfragments.

# Strangeness and hypernuclear production in fragmentation reactions induced by antikaons

## Key claim
The production cross sections of nucleonic fragments and hyperfragments weakly depend on the incident momentum, but the yields of Lambda-hyperfragments are roughly six orders of magnitude larger than those of Xi-minus-hyperfragments, with the latter forming only above the threshold momentum of 1.04 GeV/c for Xi-minus production.

## Method
The Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model was used to simulate antikaon-induced reactions on various target nuclei. Hyperons (Lambda, Sigma, Xi) and mesons (pi, eta, K, anti-K, rho, omega) were propagated with Hamilton's equations under mean-field potentials derived from the relativistic mean-field model with NL3 parameters, using a light-quark counting rule for hyperon self-energies (two-thirds of nucleon values for Lambda and Sigma, one-third for Xi). Primary hyperfragments were constructed via a coalescence approach in phase space with a relative momentum cutoff of 200 MeV/c and a relative distance cutoff of 5 fm for hyperon capture, and de-excitation was described using the GEMINI code with Hauser-Feshbach formalism for hyperon decay.

## Key numbers
- Optical potentials at saturation density: -32 MeV for Lambda and Sigma, -16 MeV for Xi
- Coalescence parameters: relative momentum cutoff P0 = 200 MeV/c, relative distance cutoff R0 = 3 fm for nucleonic fragments and R0 = 5 fm for hyperfragments
- Threshold momentum for K+ and Xi- production: p_th = 1.04 GeV/c
- Yields of Lambda-hyperfragments are approximately six orders of magnitude larger than yields of Xi-minus-hyperfragments
- Lambda-hypernuclide production cross sections at the level of millibarns (mb)
- Double strangeness hypernuclear formation cross sections at the level of nanobarns (nb)
- Hypernuclear production is reduced by three orders of magnitude compared to nucleonic fragments

## Context
This work extends prior transport model studies of hypernucleus formation (Bo15) and statistical approaches (An11, Bo07) by applying the LQMD model specifically to antikaon-induced reactions. The results are relevant for experimental programs at FAIR (PANDA, FOPI/CBM, Super-FRS/NUSTAR), J-PARC, HIAF, and NICA, which aim to study neutron-rich hypernuclei, multiple strangeness nuclei, and high-density hadronic matter with strangeness. The finding that antikaon-induced reactions favor production of target-like hyperfragments, particularly heavy hypernuclides, complements heavy-ion collision approaches and provides guidance for detector design at these facilities.
