# Study of $ Theta^+(udud \bar s) $ in lattice QCD with exact chiral symmetry

> We investigate the mass spectrum of the pentaquark baryon ($ udud \bar s $) in quenched lattice QCD with exact chiral symmetry. Using 3 different interpolating operators for $ Θ$, we measure the $ 3 \times 3 $ correlation matrix and obtain the eigenvalues $ A^{\pm} (t) $ with $ \pm $ parity, for 100 gauge configurations generated with Wilson gauge action at $ β= 6.1 $ on the $ 20^3 \times 40 $ lattice. For the lowest-lying $ J^P = 1/2^- $ state, its mass is almost identical to that of the KN S-wave, while for the lowest-lying $ J^P = 1/2^+ $ state, its mass is smaller than that of the KN P-wave, especially for the regime $ m_u < m_s $. By chiral extrapolation (linear in $m_π^2$) to $ m_π= 135 $ MeV, we obtain the masses of the lowest-lying states: $ m(1/2^-) = 1424(57) $ MeV and $ m(1/2^+) = 1562(121) $ MeV, in agreement with the masses of $ m_K + m_N \simeq 1430 $ MeV and $ Θ^+(1540) $ respectively.

## Key claim

Using a 3×3 correlation matrix with three different interpolating operators for the pentaquark Θ⁺(udud\bar{s}) in quenched lattice QCD with exact chiral symmetry, the authors find that the lowest-lying J^P = 1/2⁺ state has a mass consistent with the experimentally observed Θ⁺(1540) after chiral extrapolation, but only when using the smallest four quark masses where diquark correlations become evident; the lowest-lying J^P = 1/2⁻ state is identified as the KN s-wave scattering state rather than a genuine pentaquark resonance.

## Method

The authors generated 100 gauge configurations with Wilson gauge action at β = 6.1 on a 20³ × 40 lattice, using optimal domain-wall fermions with N_s = 128. They computed a 3×3 correlation matrix from three interpolating operators (O₁, O₂, O₃) for the Θ⁺(udud\bar{s}) system, diagonalized it to extract eigenvalues A^±(t) for positive and negative parity states, and performed single exponential fits to obtain masses. Chiral extrapolation to the physical pion mass (m_π = 135 MeV) was performed using linear fits in m_π² with the smallest four quark masses (m_u a = 0.03, 0.035, 0.04, 0.045), and the resulting masses were compared to KN scattering states computed from disconnected correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing a⁻¹ = 2.237(76) GeV, determined from the pion decay constant f_π = 132 MeV
- Strange quark bare mass m_s a = 0.08, giving M_V = 1029(10) MeV (consistent with φ(1020))
- Chiral extrapolation to m_π = 135 MeV gives m(1/2⁻) = 1424(57) MeV and m(1/2⁺) = 1562(121) MeV
- The KN s-wave scattering state mass from chiral extrapolation is m_KN(1/2⁻) = 1433(72) MeV, agreeing with m_K + m_N ≈ 1430 MeV
- For m_u a = 0.03 (smallest quark mass), the extracted masses in lattice units are: m(1/2⁺)_lowest = 1.034(80) a⁻¹, m(1/2⁺)_first excited = 1.505(137) a⁻¹, m(1/2⁻)_lowest = 0.8045(23) a⁻¹, m(1/2⁻)_first excited = 1.190(388) a⁻¹
- The KN* s-wave mass at the physical pion limit is m_K + m_N* = 2137(142) MeV, ruling out identification with the lowest J^P = 1/2⁺ state

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice QCD studies of the pentaquark Θ⁺, which had produced conflicting results as summarized in the paper's Table 1. Previous studies by Csikor et al. (2003) using O₁+αO₂ operators found m(1/2⁻) = 1539(50) MeV and m(1/2⁺) = 2710(79) MeV, while Sasaki (2003) using O₃ found m(1/2⁻) = 1840(80) MeV and m(1/2⁺) = 2940(130) MeV. Mathur et al. (2004) using overlap fermions with O₁ found m(1/2⁻) ∼ 1450 MeV and m(1/2⁺) ∼ 1650 MeV. The present work is the first lattice QCD study using a 3×3 correlation matrix for Θ⁺, and it enables future investigations into whether the positive parity state survives in full (unquenched) QCD simulations and whether the diquark correlations observed here persist with dynamical fermions.
