# An Effective Lagrangian with Broken Scale and Chiral Symmetry Applied to Nuclear Matter and Finite Nuclei

## Key claim

An effective chiral Lagrangian that incorporates both broken scale invariance (via a glueball field) and broken chiral symmetry can give a sensible description of nuclear matter and finite nuclei in the mean field approximation, provided the omega meson mass term couples to the glueball field rather than the sigma field; however, the spin-orbit splittings are only about 65% of experimental values, and the compression modulus remains somewhat high.

## Method

The authors constructed a chiral Lagrangian generalizing the linear sigma model, adding a glueball field to account for the QCD trace anomaly, and including omega and rho vector mesons. They solved the coupled field equations in the mean field approximation for nuclear matter and for the closed-shell nuclei oxygen-16, calcium-40, and lead-208, using an iterative Green's function technique. The form of the scale-invariant omega mass term was varied between coupling to the sigma field squared, the glueball field squared, or a linear combination, and the parameters were constrained by fitting to nuclear matter saturation properties.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: 0.148 fm^-3 (k_F = 1.3 fm^-1), chosen instead of 0.16 fm^-3 to avoid excessive central densities in lead.
- Binding energy per nucleon in nuclear matter: 16 MeV.
- Compression modulus K for preferred parameter set I: approximately 390 MeV.
- Preferred vacuum value of the sigma field sigma_0: 110 MeV (compared to the naive value of 93 MeV from the linear sigma model).
- Spin-orbit splittings in finite nuclei: on average 65% of experimental values.
- Preferred value of the parameter delta (related to the QCD beta function): in the neighborhood of 4/33 (approximately 0.121).
- Vacuum energy density |epsilon_vac| for preferred parameter set: consistent with QCD sum rule estimates around (240 MeV)^4.
- Symmetry energy: 35 MeV, used to fix the rho meson coupling.

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to incorporate broken scale invariance into nuclear physics effective Lagrangians, particularly the approach of Heide, Rudaz, and Ellis (1992) which modified the scale-breaking potential to include sigma field contributions. It directly addresses the criticism by Furnstahl and Serot (1993) that chiral models are inherently unable to describe finite nuclei; the authors show that including the glueball field and choosing the correct omega mass coupling resolves many of those difficulties. The results enable future investigations of additional scale-invariant terms such as (omega_mu omega^mu)^2, and provide a simplified "frozen glueball" approximation that reproduces the full model accurately. The work contrasts with the standard Walecka model extended with nonlinear sigma^3 and sigma^4 terms, which gives better agreement for single-particle energies but lacks the QCD-motivated scale and chiral symmetry structure.
