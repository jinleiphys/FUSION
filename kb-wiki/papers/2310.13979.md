# Many-channel microscopic theory of resonance states and scattering processes in $^{9}$Be and $^{9}$B

> We present a many-channel microscopic model that extends the three-cluster model previously formulated in \cite{2009NPA...V37}. This extended model incorporates multiple three-cluster configurations, which are subsequently reduced to a comprehensive set of binary channels. These channels dictate the dynamics of various nuclear processes and the resonance structure of a compound nucleus across a broad energy spectrum. The application of this model focuses on investigating the nature of high-energy resonance states in $^{9}$Be and $^{9}$B, as well as the astrophysical $S$-factors for the reactions $^{7}$Li$(d,n)αα$ and $^{7}$Be$(d,p)αα$, particularly pertinent to the cosmological lithium problem. Parameterization of resonance states is performed across a wide range of total angular momenta and includes states of both positive and negative parity. Dominant decay channels are identified for each resonance state. Detailed analysis of astrophysical $S$ factors resulting from deuteron interactions with $^{7}$Li and $^{7}$Be is conducted within an energy range from zero to 2 MeV. Four exit channels in $^{9}$Be ($^{8}$Be($0^{+}$)$+n$, $^{8}$Be($2^{+}$)$+n$, $^{5}$He($3/2^{-}$)$+α$, $^{5}$He($1/2^{-}$)$+α$) and four in $^{9}$B ($^{8}$Be($0^{+}$)$+p$, $^{8}$Be($2^{+}$)$+p$, $^{5}$Li($3/2^{-}$)$+α$, $^{5}$Li($1/2^{-}$)$+α$) are considered. A clear hierarchy of reactions is established for the energy range 0$\leq E<$1.0 MeV. Notably, reactions $^{7}$Li$+d=^{8}$Be($0^{+}$)$+n$ and $^{7}$Be$+d=^{8}$Be($0^{+}$)$+p$ substantially dominate over all other reactions within this energy range. The model satisfactory describes the experimental astrophysical $S$ factors for these reactions.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previously formulated three-cluster model (Lashko et al., 2009) to incorporate multiple three-cluster configurations, which are then reduced to binary channels, in order to study high-energy resonance states in \(^{9}\)Be and \(^{9}\)B and the astrophysical \(S\)-factors for the reactions \(^{7}\)Li\((d,n)\alpha\alpha\) and \(^{7}\)Be\((d,p)\alpha\alpha\); the model "satisfactory describes" experimental astrophysical \(S\)-factors, but the authors note that the cosmological lithium problem remains unresolved, with the reduction in \(^{7}\)Li abundance from a resonance at 0.36(5) MeV in \(^{9}\)B estimated to be only 1.4% to 8.1% (Rijal et al., 2019).

## Method

The authors use an algebraic version of the resonating group method (AMGOB) with Gaussian and oscillator bases to solve the three-cluster Schrödinger equation for \(^{9}\)Be and \(^{9}\)B. They include two three-cluster configurations for each nucleus: \(\alpha+\alpha+n\) and \(\alpha+^{3}\)H\(+d\) for \(^{9}\)Be, and \(\alpha+\alpha+p\) and \(\alpha+^{3}\)He\(+d\) for \(^{9}\)B, which are reduced to four binary channels each. The Minnesota potential with the IV version of spin-orbital interaction is employed, and the wave functions are expanded in a finite set of square-integrable Gaussian functions for two-cluster subsystems and oscillator functions for the relative motion of the third cluster.

## Key numbers

- The model considers four exit channels in \(^{9}\)Be: \(^{8}\)Be(\(0^{+}\))\(+n\), \(^{8}\)Be(\(2^{+}\))\(+n\), \(^{5}\)He(\(3/2^{-}\))\(+\alpha\), \(^{5}\)He(\(1/2^{-}\))\(+\alpha\); and four in \(^{9}\)B: \(^{8}\)Be(\(0^{+}\))\(+p\), \(^{8}\)Be(\(2^{+}\))\(+p\), \(^{5}\)Li(\(3/2^{-}\))\(+\alpha\), \(^{5}\)Li(\(1/2^{-}\))\(+\alpha\).
- A clear hierarchy of reactions is established for the energy range \(0 \leq E < 1.0\) MeV, where reactions \(^{7}\)Li\(+d=^{8}\)Be(\(0^{+}\))\(+n\) and \(^{7}\)Be\(+d=^{8}\)Be(\(0^{+}\))\(+p\) "substantially dominate over all other reactions".
- The experimental resonance at \(E_{c.m.} = 0.36(5)\) MeV in \(^{9}\)B (Rijal et al., 2019) is attributed to a \(5/2^{+}\) compound-nuclear state, an isospin mirror to the 16.671 MeV (\(5/2^{+}\)) state in \(^{9}\)Be; candidates in \(^{9}\)B were reported at 16.80(10) MeV (PhysRevC.84.014308) and 16.71 MeV (2012IJMPE..2150004C).
- The predicted primordial abundance of \(^{7}\)Li is about three times higher than the observed value of \(^{7}\)Li/H = \(1.58 \pm 0.31 \times 10^{-10}\) (2010AxA...522A..26S), compared to the BBN prediction of \(4.89_{-0.39}^{+0.41} \times 10^{-10}\) (2013arXiv1307.6955C, 2014JCAP...10..050C).
- The \(^{7}\)Be\((d,p)^{8}\)Be reaction rate at BBN energies is a factor of 10 smaller than earlier measurements suggested (2004NuPhA.745..155T), based on data from Angulo et al. (2005).

## Context

This work extends the three-cluster model of Lashko et al. (2009) by incorporating multiple three-cluster configurations to study resonance states near the \(^{7}\)Li\(+d\) and \(^{7}\)Be\(+d\) thresholds in \(^{9}\)Be and \(^{9}\)B, respectively. It addresses the cosmological lithium problem, where standard BBN overpredicts primordial \(^{7}\)Li abundance by a factor of about three compared to observations (Coc et al., 2010; Cyburt et al., 2013, 2014). The model enables theoretical interpretation of narrow resonances observed experimentally (Lamia et al., 2018; Rijal et al., 2019) and provides a framework for calculating astrophysical \(S\)-factors that can be compared with data from Angulo et al. (2005) and Rijal et al. (2019), potentially guiding future experiments to resolve the \(^{7}\)Li discrepancy.
