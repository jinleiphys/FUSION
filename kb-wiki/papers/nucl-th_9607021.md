# Chiral Symmetry and the Nucleon's Vector Strangeness Form Factors

> The nucleon's strange-quark vector current form factors are studied from the perspective of chiral symmetry. It is argued that chiral perturbation theory cannot yield a prediction for the strangeness radius and magnetic moment. Arrival at definite predictions requires the introduction of additional, model-dependent assumptions which go beyond the framework of chiral perturbation theory. A variety of such model predictions is surveyed, and the credibility of each is evaluated. The most plausible prediction appears in a model where the unknown chiral counterterms are identified with $t$-channel vector meson exchange amplitudes. The corresponding prediction for the mean square Dirac strangeness radius is $\langle r_s^2\rangle = 0.24$ fm$^2$, which would be observable in up-coming semileptonic determinations of the nucleon's strangeness form factors.

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) cannot yield a prediction for the nucleon's strangeness radius and magnetic moment because the coefficients of the relevant flavor-singlet operators in the chiral Lagrangian cannot be determined from existing measurements using chiral symmetry alone; arriving at definite predictions requires introducing additional, model-dependent assumptions that go beyond the framework of CHPT.

## Method

The authors analyze the nucleon's strange-quark vector current form factors using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, computing the leading non-analytic loop contributions to the strangeness magnetic moment and Dirac radius. They then introduce three model-dependent approaches to estimate the unknown chiral counterterms: (a) a resonance saturation model where counterterms are identified with t-channel vector meson exchange amplitudes; (b) a kaon cloud dominance model; and (c) constituent chiral quark models where strangeness arises from the content of constituent U- and D-quarks.

## Key numbers

- The mean square Dirac strangeness radius predicted by the resonance saturation (vector meson exchange) model is ⟨r_s²⟩ = 0.24 fm².
- The pion decay constant used is f_π ≈ 93 MeV.
- The scale of chiral symmetry breaking is Λ_χ ≈ 4πf ≈ 1 GeV.
- The fitted electromagnetic counterterms are b_+ ≈ 1.4, b_- ≈ 0.9, c_+ ≈ -1.9, c_- ≈ 0.9.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of baryon octet magnetic moments by Jenkins and Manohar (1993) and the study of the nucleon's isovector charge radius by Cohen (1995) to the flavor-singlet channel, showing that CHPT cannot predict strangeness matrix elements. The paper surveys model predictions from Jaffe (1989), Koepf (1992), Forkel (1994), Cohen (1993), and others, and identifies the resonance saturation model as the most plausible, though not airtight. The results are relevant for upcoming parity-violating electron scattering experiments at MIT-Bates, CEBAF, and Mainz that aim to measure the strange quark vector form factors.
