# HIJING can describe the anisotropy-scaled charge-dependent correlations at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

> The experimentally measured charge-depdendent correlations in heavy ion collisions have been suggested as a signature of the chiral magenetic effect (CME). Early model studies could not reproduce the measurement. For example, the Hijing model yielded far smaller magnitude for the charge-dependent correlation than observed in data. This led to the conclusion that the CME had to be invoked to explain the observed correlations in heavy ion collisions. In this paper we show that this conclusion of the CME interpretation is premature. We show that the reason that Hijing predicts a far smaller correlation than data is because the elliptic anisotrpy ($v_{2}$) parameter in Hijing is too small. When properly scaled, the Hijing model can reproduce in entirety the measured correlations. We also employ the AMPT model, which has a large enough $v_2$, to demonstrate that the measured data can be easily accommodated by models without invoking the CME.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the conclusion that the chiral magnetic effect (CME) must be invoked to explain charge-dependent correlations in heavy ion collisions is premature; the HIJING model can reproduce the measured correlations in entirety when its results are scaled by the elliptic anisotropy parameter \(v_2\), which is too small in HIJING compared to data.

## Method

The authors used the HIJING (v1.411) and AMPT (v2.26t5d6, string melting version) models to simulate charge-dependent azimuthal correlations in p+Au, d+Au, and Au+Au collisions at RHIC. They computed the \(\Delta\gamma = \gamma_{\rm OS} - \gamma_{\rm SS}\) correlator, where \(\gamma_{\alpha\beta} = \langle\cos(\phi_\alpha+\phi_\beta-2\psi)\rangle\), and compared model results to experimental data from the STAR collaboration. They introduced a scaled correlator \(\Delta\gamma_{\rm scaled} = (dN_{ch}/d\eta) \cdot \Delta\gamma / v_2\) to remove the dependence on elliptic flow and isolate intrinsic particle correlations.

## Key numbers

- HIJING predictions for \(\Delta\gamma\) in Au+Au collisions are much smaller than data; the \(v_2\) values in HIJING for Au+Au are also much smaller than data.
- After scaling by \(v_2\), the scaled correlator \(\Delta\gamma_{\rm scaled}\) from HIJING shows "not much difference" compared to data across p+Au, d+Au, and Au+Au systems.
- AMPT results for the scaled correlator in Au+Au are also similar to data, but AMPT underpredicts the scaled correlator in small systems (p+Au, d+Au) by "a factor of several" lower than data.
- The parton-parton scattering cross section in AMPT is set to \(\sigma = 3\) mb, with strong coupling \(\alpha_s = 0.33\) and Debye screening mass \(\mu_D = 2.265\)/fm.

## Context

This work directly challenges the earlier conclusion from STAR (Abelev 2009, Abelev 2010) that no model, including HIJING, could reproduce the measured charge-dependent correlations, which was taken as evidence for the CME. The authors show that the discrepancy was due to HIJING's insufficient elliptic flow, not a missing CME signal. The study extends prior background studies by Wang (2009), Bzdak (2009), and Schlichting (2010) and reinforces that background correlations from resonance decays, clusters, and minijets may account for all observed correlations. The paper enables future work to focus on data-driven background subtraction methods rather than relying on model comparisons for CME claims.
