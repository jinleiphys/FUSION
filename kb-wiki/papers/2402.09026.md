# Doubly heavy dibaryons as seen by string theory

> We propose the stringy description of the system consisting of two heavy and four light quarks in the case of two light flavors of equal mass. As an application, we consider the three low-lying Born-Oppenheimer potentials as a function of the heavy quark separation. Our analysis shows that the ground state potential is described in terms of both hadro-quarkonia and hadronic molecules. A connected string configuration makes the dominant contribution to the potential of an excited state at small separations, and for separations larger than $0.1\,\text{fm}$, it exhibits the diquark-diquark-diquark structure $[Qq][Qq][qq]$. For better understanding the quark organization inside the system, we introduce several critical separations related to the processes of string reconnection, breaking and junction annihilation. We also discuss the simplest string configurations including the five-string junctions and their implications for the system, in particular the emergence of composite quark objects different from diquarks and the process of junction fusion.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the three low-lying Born-Oppenheimer potentials for a doubly heavy hexaquark system (QQqqqq) can be described by string theory configurations, with the ground state potential involving both hadro-quarkonia and hadronic molecules, while an excited state exhibits a diquark-diquark-diquark structure [Qq][Qq][qq] for heavy quark separations larger than 0.1 fm, though this description is conditional on the specific parameter choices and the soft-wall model used.

## Method

The author constructs five-dimensional string configurations dual to the QQqqqq system using the soft-wall AdS/QCD model with metric ds² = e^(σ r²) (R²/r²)(dt² + (dx^i)² + dr²), incorporating Nambu-Goto strings, baryon vertices with action S_vert = τ_v ∫ dt e^(-2σ r²)/r, and light quarks modeled as point particles with action S_q = T₀R ∫ dt e^(½σ r²)/r. The Born-Oppenheimer potentials are computed by extremizing the total action for various string configurations (disconnected and connected hexaquark configurations) as functions of the heavy quark separation ℓ, with the three low-lying potentials determined from a mixing analysis of the diagonal and off-diagonal elements of a correlation matrix.

## Key numbers

- The critical separation where the excited state potential transitions to the diquark-diquark-diquark structure is ℓ > 0.1 fm
- The light quark mass is m_{u/d} = 46.6 MeV, determined by fitting the string breaking distance for the Q\bar Q system to lattice data from Bulava (2019)
- The string tension is σ = e σ g, where g = R²/(2πα′) and σ is the deformation parameter
- The baryon vertex position parameter v_s = 1/12 at the critical value k = k_v = -¼ e^(¼)
- The parameter v (related to vertex position) ranges in [0, v_s] for the collapsed-vertex configuration, and the parameter \bar v ranges in [v_s, q_s] for the separated-vertex configuration, where q_s is the light quark position parameter

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies on doubly heavy quark systems (Andreev 2020, 2021, 2022, 2023) by applying the Born-Oppenheimer approximation and gauge/string duality to the QQqqqq hexaquark system, which has not been comprehensively discussed in the existing literature. The approach builds on lattice QCD methods for Born-Oppenheimer potentials in the presence of light quarks (Foster and Michael 1999) and the soft-wall AdS/QCD model that successfully describes the 3Q heavy quark potential (Andreev 2021). The results enable future lattice QCD calculations of doubly heavy hexaquark systems and provide a framework for understanding the organization of quarks within exotic hadrons, including the emergence of composite quark objects different from diquarks and the process of junction fusion.
