# Emergence of a new $SU(4)$ symmetry in the baryon spectrum

> Recently a large degeneracy of $J=1$ mesons, that is larger than the $SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R \times U(1)_A$ symmetry of the QCD Lagrangian, has been discovered upon truncation of the near-zero modes from the valence quark propagators. It has been found that this degeneracy represents the $SU(4)$ group that includes the chiral rotations as well as the mixing of left- and right-handed quarks. This symmetry group turns out to be a symmetry of confinement in QCD. Consequently, one expects that the same symmetry should persist upon the near-zero mode removal in other hadron sectors as well. It has been shown that indeed the $J=2$ mesons follow the same symmetry pattern upon the low-lying mode elimination. Here we demonstrate the $SU(4)$ symmetry of baryons once the near-zero modes are removed from the quark propagators. We also show a degeneracy of states belonging to different irreducible representations of $SU(4)$. This implies a larger symmetry, that includes $SU(4)$ as a subgroup.

## Key claim

Upon truncation of the near-zero modes from the valence quark propagators in lattice QCD, the baryon spectrum exhibits an $SU(4)$ symmetry that is larger than the $SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R \times U(1)_A$ symmetry of the QCD Lagrangian; furthermore, degeneracy of states belonging to different irreducible representations of $SU(4)$ implies an even larger symmetry that contains $SU(4)$ as a subgroup.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using $N_F = 2$ Overlap fermion gauge configurations on a $16^3 \times 32$ lattice with spacing $a \sim 0.12$ fm and pion mass $M_\pi = 289(2)$ MeV, using 87 gauge field configurations. They constructed correlation matrices from nucleon and delta interpolators with different Dirac structures and smearing widths, then removed quasi-zero modes from the inverse Overlap Dirac operator by subtracting contributions from the lowest $k$ eigenmodes (with $k = 2, 4, 10, 16, 20, 30$). Masses were extracted via the variational method solving a generalized eigenvalue problem.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: $M_\pi = 289(2)$ MeV (lattice with $a \sim 0.12$ fm, $16^3 \times 32$ volume)
- Lattice spacing: $a \sim 0.12$ fm
- Number of gauge configurations used: 87
- Onset of degeneracy observed between removal of 10-16 quasi-zero modes
- At truncation $k = 30$, the $N(1/2,1/2^+)$ ground state mass is $am = 0.905 \pm 0.014$ and the $N(1/2,1/2^-)$ ground state mass is $am = 0.896 \pm 0.013$ (in lattice units), showing near degeneracy
- At truncation $k = 30$, the $\Delta(3/2,1/2^+)$ ground state mass is $am = 0.889 \pm 0.023$ and the $\Delta(3/2,1/2^-)$ ground state mass is $am = 0.882 \pm 0.020$ (in lattice units)

## Context

This work extends the discovery of $SU(4)$ symmetry in $J=1$ mesons (Denissenya, Glozman, Pak 2014) and $J=2$ mesons (Denissenya, Glozman, Pak 2015) to the baryon sector, confirming that the symmetry is a general feature of confinement in QCD. The symmetry had been identified as the symmetry group of the confining QCD string (Glozman 2014) and shown to be hidden in the Coulombic interaction part of the QCD Hamiltonian in Coulomb gauge (Glozman 2015). The results enable further investigation of a higher symmetry containing $SU(4)$ as a subgroup, which the authors state will be discussed in a future publication, and suggest that the remaining $N-\Delta$ splitting after mode removal arises from rotational energy differences rather than color-magnetic interactions.
