# Strong Decays of Charmed Baryons in Heavy Hadron Chiral Perturbation Theory: An Update

> We first give a brief overview of the charmed baryon spectroscopy and discuss their possible structure and spin-parity assignments in the quark model. With the new Belle measurement of the widths of $Œ£_c(2455)$ and $Œ£_c(2520)$ and the recent CDF measurement of the strong decays of $Œõ_c(2595)$ and $Œõ_c(2625)$, we give updated coupling constants in heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory. We find $g_2=0.565^{+0.011}_{-0.024}$ for $P$-wave transitions between $s$-wave and $s$-wave baryons, and $h_2$, one of the couplings responsible for $S$-wave transitions between $s$-wave and $p$-wave baryons, is extracted from $Œõ_c(2595)^+\toŒõ_c^+œÄœÄ$ to be $0.63\pm0.07$. With the help from the quark model, two of the couplings $h_{10}$ and $h_{11}$ responsible for $D$-wave transitions between $s$-wave and $p$-wave baryons are determined from $Œ£_c(2880)$ decays. ## Key claim

Using new Belle and CDF measurements of charmed baryon widths, the authors update the coupling constants in heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory (HHChPT), finding that the coupling \(h_2\) extracted from \(\Lambda_c(2595)^+\to\Lambda_c^+\pi\pi\) is substantially enhanced to \(0.63\pm0.07\) compared to the old value of order 0.437, but this value creates a tension by predicting \(\Xi_c(2790)^0\to\Xi'_c\pi\) and \(\Xi_c(2815)^+\to\Xi_c^*\pi\) rates slightly above current experimental limits, which may be resolved by SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors fit the coupling constants of the HHChPT Lagrangian to the experimentally measured strong decay widths of \(s\)-wave and \(p\)-wave charmed baryons, using new Belle data for \(\Sigma_c(2455)\) and \(\Sigma_c(2520)\) widths and CDF data for \(\Lambda_c(2595)\) and \(\Lambda_c(2625)\) decays. For \(D\)-wave transitions between \(s\)-wave and \(p\)-wave baryons, the couplings \(h_{10}\) and \(h_{11}\) are determined from \(\Sigma_c(2880)\) decays with additional input from the quark model. The analysis also employs the quark model to assign spin-parity quantum numbers to observed states and to classify them into SU(3) flavor multiplets.

## Key numbers

*   Coupling constant \(g_2 = 0.565^{+0.011}_{-0.024}\) for \(P\)-wave transitions between \(s\)-wave and \(s\)-wave baryons.
*   Coupling constant \(h_2 = 0.63 \pm 0.07\) for \(S\)-wave transitions between \(s\)-wave and \(p\)-wave baryons, extracted from \(\Lambda_c(2595)^+\to\Lambda_c^+\pi\pi\).
*   Width of \(\Sigma_c(2455)^{++}\) is \(1.94^{+0.08}_{-0.16}\) MeV; width of \(\Sigma_c(2455)^{0}\) is \(1.87^{+0.09}_{-0.17}\) MeV (Belle average).
*   Width of \(\Sigma_c(2520)^{++}\) is \(14.8^{+0.3}_{-0.4}\) MeV; width of \(\Sigma_c(2520)^{0}\) is \(15.3^{+0.3}_{-0.4}\) MeV (Belle average).
*   Width of \(\Lambda_c(2595)^+\) is \(2.59 \pm 0.56\) MeV; width of \(\Lambda_c(2625)^+\) is \(< 0.97\) MeV (CDF measurement).
*   Ratio \(R \equiv \Gamma(\Lambda_c(2880)\to\Sigma_c^*\pi^\pm) / \Gamma(\Lambda_c(2880)\to\Sigma_c\pi^\pm) = (24.1 \pm 6.4^{+1.1}_{-4.5})\%\) from Belle.
*   Width of \(\Xi_c(2645)^+\) is \(2.6 \pm 0.5\) MeV.
*   Width of \(\Lambda_c(2880)^+\) is \(5.8 \pm 1.1\) MeV.

## Context

This work is an update of the earlier HHChPT analysis by Cheng and Chua (2005), incorporating new experimental data from Belle and CDF. It extends the theoretical framework of heavy quark symmetry and chiral symmetry applied to charmed baryon decays, as originally formulated by Yan (1992) for \(s\)-wave baryons and by Cho (1994) and Pirjol (1996) for \(p\)-wave baryons. The updated couplings enable more precise predictions for strong decays of excited charmed baryons, including \(\Xi_c(2790)\), \(\Xi_c(2815)\), and \(\Sigma_c(2880)\), and provide a benchmark for testing SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking in future measurements.
