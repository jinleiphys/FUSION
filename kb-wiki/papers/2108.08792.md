# Quantum information approach to high energy interactions

> High energy hadron interactions are commonly described by using a probabilistic parton model that ignores quantum entanglement present in the light-cone wave functions. Here we argue that since a high energy interaction samples an instant snapshot of the hadron wave function, the phases of different Fock state wave functions cannot be measured - therefore the light-cone density matrix has to be traced over these unobservable phases. Performing this trace with the corresponding $U(1)$ Haar integration measure leads to "Haar scrambling" of the density matrix, and to the emergence of entanglement entropy. This entanglement entropy is determined by the Fock state probability distribution, and is thus directly related to the parton structure functions. As proposed earlier, at large rapidity $η$ the hadron state becomes maximally entangled, and the entanglement entropy is $S_E \sim η$ according to QCD evolution equations. When the phases of Fock state components are controlled, for example in spin asymmetry measurements, the Haar average cannot be performed, and the probabilistic parton description breaks down.

## Key claim

The non-zero entropy of partons in high-energy interactions is the entanglement entropy between the parton number and the phase of the light-cone wave function, arising because the phase cannot be measured in an instant snapshot; this justifies the probabilistic parton model only when the Haar average over unobservable phases is valid, and breaks down when phases are controlled, as in spin asymmetry measurements.

## Method

The author argues that a high-energy interaction (e.g., deep inelastic scattering) samples an instant snapshot of the hadron wave function, making the phases of different Fock state components unmeasurable. The light-cone density matrix of the pure hadron state is therefore traced over these unobservable phases using the U(1) Haar integration measure, yielding a mixed-state density matrix diagonal in parton number. This "Haar scrambled" density matrix is identified with the standard parton model density matrix, and its von Neumann entropy is the entanglement entropy between parton number and phase.

## Key numbers

* The entanglement entropy at high energies is predicted to scale as \(S_E \sim \ln E \sim y\), where \(y\) is the hadron rapidity, based on the power-law growth of parton number \(N \sim E^\alpha\) from QCD evolution.
* For a maximally entangled state with equal parton probabilities \(p_n = 1/N\), the entanglement entropy is \(S_E = \ln N\).
* The linear growth of entanglement entropy with rapidity was found to hold starting from rapidity \(y \simeq 6\) in a prior study by Kharzeev (2017).

## Context

This work extends the quantum information interpretation of the parton model proposed by Kharzeev (2017), which linked entanglement entropy to parton structure functions via QCD evolution equations. It also builds on the light-cone quantization framework of Dirac (1949) and the light-cone wave function formalism reviewed by Brodsky (1997). The paper enables future studies of entanglement entropy under Lorentz boost using specific bound-state models (e.g., the covariant oscillator of Feynman (1971) and Kim (1990)), and suggests that the emergence of entanglement entropy on the light cone may be a general geometric phenomenon even beyond the parton model, such as at strong coupling.
