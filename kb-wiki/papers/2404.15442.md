# Electromagnetic Form Factors for Nucleons in Short-Range Correlations and the EMC effect

> The relationship between medium modifications of nucleon electromagnetic form factors and nucleon structure functions is examined using a model motivated by Light-Front Holographic QCD (LFHQCD). These modifications are closely connected with the influence of short-ranged correlations. The size of the modifications to nucleon form factors is shown to be about the same as the modifications to the structure functions. Thus, small limits on form factors modifications do not rule out an explanation of the EMC effect motivated by the influence of short range correlations, as claimed by a recent paper.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that medium modifications of nucleon electromagnetic form factors are about the same size as the modifications to nucleon structure functions (the EMC effect), contradicting a recent claim by Xing (2023) that SRC-based explanations of the EMC effect would require unphysically large form factor suppressions. The authors show that small experimental limits on form factor modifications do not rule out an SRC-driven EMC effect.

## Method

The authors use a two-component (blob-like configuration and point-like configuration) model of the nucleon, combined with Light-Front Holographic QCD (LFHQCD) form factors from Sufian (2017), to compute medium-modified elastic electromagnetic form factors. The medium modifications are parameterized by two free parameters, delta r_p and delta r_n, which are determined by fitting to measured EMC ratios from Kim (2022). The nuclear potential acting on the blob-like configuration is related to the nucleon virtuality, connecting the form factor modifications to the same dynamics that produce the EMC effect.

## Key numbers

- For A > 4 nuclei, delta r_p values range from 0.044 (Be-9) to 0.078 (Pb-208), with uncertainties of 0.003-0.005
- For A > 4 nuclei, delta r_n values range from 0.035 (Be-9) to 0.064 (Fe-56), with uncertainties of 0.003
- The ratio of medium-modified to free proton mean square radius is given by R_tilde_p^2 / R_p^2 = 1 + (3/20) delta r_p, a tiny effect
- At large Q^2, the ratio of medium-modified to free F_1^p approaches (1 - delta r_p), which is no more than a few percent
- The nuclear potential |U_N| extracted from delta r values ranges from 80 MeV to 156 MeV for A > 4
- The ratio of medium-modified to free F_1^p at Q^2 = 5 M_rho^2 = 3 GeV^2 is shown as a function of virtuality

## Context

This work extends the two-component nucleon model of Frankfurt (1985) and the LFHQCD approach of Kim (2022) to compute medium-modified elastic form factors across all Q^2. It directly contradicts the claims of Xing (2023), who argued that SRC-based EMC models require huge form factor suppressions. The paper enables future experimental measurements of medium-modified form factors at kinematics where nucleon virtuality is large, as current measurements only exist for small virtuality values. The authors note that both mean-field and SRC effects contribute to the average virtuality in roughly a 1:2 ratio, consistent with earlier work by Ciofi degli Atti (2007).
