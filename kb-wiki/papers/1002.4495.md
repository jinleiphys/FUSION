# Triaxial superdeformation in $^{40}$Ar

> Superdeformed (SD) states in $^{40}$Ar have been studied using the deformed-basis antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. Low energy states were calculated by the parity and angular momentum projection (AMP) and the generator coordinate method (GCM). Basis wave functions were obtained by the energy variation with a constraint on the quadrupole deformation parameter $β$, while other quantities such as triaxiality $γ$ were optimized by the energy variation. By the GCM calculation, an SD band was obtained just above the ground state (GS) band. The SD band involves a $K^π= 2^+$ side band due to the triaxiality. The calculated electric quadrupole transition strengths of the SD band reproduce the experimental values appropriately. Triaxiality is significant for understanding low-lying states.

## Key claim

The paper claims that superdeformed (SD) states in $^{40}$Ar form triaxial shapes, with a triaxiality parameter $\gamma \sim 10^\circ$, and that this triaxiality gives rise to a $K^\pi = 2^+$ side band (SD2) alongside the main $K^\pi = 0^+$ SD band (SD0). The claim is qualified by the observation that the calculated energy gap between the $J^\pi = 0^+_\mathrm{SD0}$ and $J^\pi = 2^+_\mathrm{SD2}$ states is slightly overestimated, which may indicate that the triaxiality of the SD states is underestimated in the calculation.

## Method

The authors used deformed-basis antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with the Gogny D1S effective interaction. Basis wave functions were obtained by energy variation with a constraint on the quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta$, while the triaxiality $\gamma$ and other parameters were optimized freely. Parity and angular momentum projection (AMP) and the generator coordinate method (GCM) were then applied to these basis wave functions to calculate low-lying states and rotational bands.

## Key numbers

* The energy gap between the ground state (GS) and SD minima in the positive-parity projected energy curves is approximately 6.7 MeV, which decreases to 2.5 MeV after angular momentum projection to $J^\pi = 0^+$ states.
* The SD minimum occurs at quadrupole deformation parameters $(\beta, \gamma) = (0.478, 11.2^\circ)$.
* The SD minimum has $2\hbar\omega$ excited configurations for both protons and neutrons, giving a total of $4\hbar\omega$ excitation.
* The calculated $B(E2)$ value for the $4^+_\mathrm{SD0} \rightarrow 2^+_\mathrm{SD0}$ intraband transition is 78.54 W.u., compared to the experimental value of $47 \pm 19$ W.u.
* The calculated $B(E2)$ value for the $6^+_\mathrm{SD0} \rightarrow 4^+_\mathrm{SD0}$ intraband transition is 84.64 W.u., compared to the experimental value of $70 \pm 30$ W.u.
* The calculated $B(E2)$ value for the $2^+_\mathrm{SD2} \rightarrow 0^+_\mathrm{SD0}$ interband transition is 0.92 W.u., compared to the experimental value of $1.2 \pm 0.3$ W.u.
* The triaxiality $\gamma$ of the SD states in $^{40}$Ar is approximately $10^\circ$, which is intermediate between $\gamma \sim 0^\circ$ for SD states in $^{36}$Ar and $\gamma \sim 25^\circ$ for SD states in $^{44}$Ti.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of superdeformation in the $A \sim 40$ mass region, following experimental identification of an SD band in $^{40}$Ar by Ideguchi et al. (2010), which were the lightest SD states ever observed in $N \neq Z$ nuclei. The calculations build on previous AMD studies of triaxial SD states in $^{40}$Ca (Taniguchi et al., 2005) and $^{44}$Ti (Kimura et al., 2005), and on Hartree-Fock calculations by Inakura et al. (2002) that suggested systematic triaxial deformation of SD states. The results enable future experimental searches for intraband transitions of the predicted $K^\pi = 2^+$ SD2 band and interband transitions between the SD0 and SD2 bands to confirm the triaxial deformation of the SD states in $^{40}$Ar.
