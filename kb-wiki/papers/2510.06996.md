# Transport-based initial conditions for heavy-ion collisions at finite densities

> We employ the SMASH transport model to provide event-by-event initial conditions for the energy-momentum tensor and conserved charge currents in hydrodynamic simulations of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We study the fluctuations and dynamical evolution of three conserved charge currents (net baryon, net electric charges, and net strangeness) with a 4D lattice-QCD-based equation of state, NEOS-4D, in the hydrodynamic phase. Out-of-equilibrium corrections at the particlization are generalized to finite densities to ensure the conservation of energy, momentum, and the three types of charges. These theoretical developments are integrated within the X-SCAPE code as a unified framework for studying the nuclear matter properties in the Beam Energy Scan program.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that SMASH transport model initial conditions, combined with a 4D lattice-QCD-based equation of state (NEOS-4D) and generalized out-of-equilibrium corrections at particlization, provide a viable multi-stage framework for studying heavy-ion collisions at finite baryon densities; however, the approach assumes fast chemical equilibration at the initialization time and does not yet include charge diffusion currents in the hydrodynamic phase.

## Method
The authors used the SMASH hadronic transport model (version 3.1) in quantum molecular dynamics mode to generate event-by-event initial conditions for the energy-momentum tensor and three conserved charge currents (net baryon, net electric charge, net strangeness) on a constant proper-time hypersurface. These source terms were smeared onto a (3+1)D hydrodynamic grid using either a covariant or Gaussian smearing kernel, then evolved with the MUSIC viscous hydrodynamic code using the NEOS-4D equation of state and DNMR theory for shear and bulk viscosity. At a switching energy density of 0.35 GeV/fm^3, the Cooper-Frye particlization procedure was applied with generalized Grad's 14-moment out-of-equilibrium corrections for three conserved charges, and the resulting hadrons were fed back into SMASH for the afterburner phase.

## Key numbers
- Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, 19.6 GeV, and 7.7 GeV were simulated, with impact parameters b = 0 fm or sampled in the range b = 6-8 fm.
- The initialization proper time tau_0 is 0.5 fm/c at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, 1.23 fm/c at 19.6 GeV, and 3.2 fm/c at 7.7 GeV (based on nucleon mass 0.938 GeV and gold radius 6.37 fm).
- The smearing kernel width parameters were set to sigma = sigma_perp = 0.5 fm and sigma_eta = 0.5.
- Hydrodynamic grid spacings were Delta_eta_s = 0.1 and Delta_x = Delta_y = 0.2 fm, yielding relative uncertainty per step of O(10^{-4}) for net baryon and net electric charge conservation.
- The specific shear viscosity parameters were eta_0 = 0.08, b = 2, mu_{B,0} = 0.6 GeV, and a = 0.7; the bulk viscosity peak value was zeta_0 = 0.1 at T_peak = 0.17 GeV - 0.15 GeV^{-1} mu_B^2.
- The switching energy density for particlization was e_sw = 0.35 GeV/fm^3.

## Context
This work extends hybrid approaches that use hadronic transport for initial conditions, such as the SMASH-vHLLE-Hybrid (Schafer 2021) and UrQMD-based setups (Petersen 2008, Karpenko 2015, Du 2018), by incorporating three conserved charge currents (B, Q, S) with a 4D equation of state (NEOS-4D, Monnai 2024) and generalized out-of-equilibrium corrections at particlization. The framework is integrated into the X-SCAPE code and enables systematic phenomenological studies of the QCD phase diagram at finite densities relevant to the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, though future work is needed to include charge diffusion currents and dynamical chemical equilibration.
