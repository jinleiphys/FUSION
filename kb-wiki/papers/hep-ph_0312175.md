# The \bar q q Bound States and Instanton Molecule at T >~ T_c

> The main objective of this work is to explore the evolution in the structure of the quark-anti-quark bound states in going down in the chirally restored phase from the so-called "zero binding points" $T_{zb}$ to the full (unquenched) QCD critical temperature $T_c$ at which the Nambu-Goldstone and Wigner-Weyl modes meet. In doing this, we adopt the idea recently introduced by Shuryak and Zahed for charmed $\bar c c$, light-quark $\bar q q$ mesons $π, σ, ρ, A_1$ and gluons that at $T_{zb}$, the quark-anti-quark scattering length goes through $\infty$ at which conformal invariance is restored, thereby transforming the matter into a near perfect fluid behaving hydrodynamically, as found at RHIC. We show that the binding of these states is accomplished by the combination of (i) the color Coulomb interaction, (ii) the relativistic effects, and (iii) the interaction induced by the instanton-anti-instanton molecules. The spin-spin forces turned out to be small. While near $T_{zb}$ all mesons are large-size nonrelativistic objects bound by Coulomb attraction, near $T_c$ they get much more tightly bound, with many-body collective interactions becoming important and making the $σ$ and $π$ masses approach zero (in the chiral limit). The wave function at the origin grows strongly with binding, and the near-local four-Fermi interactions induced by the instanton molecules play an increasingly more important role as the temperature moves downward toward $T_c$.

## Key claim

The paper explores how quark-anti-quark bound states evolve between the "zero binding points" \(T_{zb}\) (where scattering length diverges and conformal invariance is restored) and the full QCD critical temperature \(T_c\) (unquenched, approximately 170 MeV), showing that binding is accomplished by a combination of color Coulomb interaction, relativistic effects, and instanton-anti-instanton molecule interactions, with spin-spin forces being negligible.

## Method

The authors solve a Klein-Gordon equation for the quark-anti-quark system with a modified Coulomb potential that accounts for the distributed charge (electric field zero at the origin due to asymptotic freedom), incorporate relativistic current-current interactions that effectively double the attraction, and add the effective four-Fermi interactions induced by instanton-anti-instanton molecules. They treat the system in the chirally restored phase above \(T_c\) using quasiparticle masses from lattice data, expressing meson binding in units of the temperature-dependent quark mass \(m_q(T)\).

## Key numbers

- Quark quasiparticle mass from quenched lattice calculations: \(m_q/T = 3.9 \pm 0.2\), giving \(m_q \sim 1.6\) GeV at \(T = 1.5 T_c = 405\) MeV (quenched, \(T_c = 270\) MeV)
- Gluon quasiparticle mass: \(m_g/T = 3.4 \pm 0.3\), giving \(m_g \sim 1.4\) GeV at the same temperature
- Unquenched critical temperature: \(T_c \approx 170\) MeV
- Shear viscosity to entropy ratio of the matter above \(T_c\): \(\eta/s \sim 0.1\), comparable to the infinite coupling limit \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\)
- The maximal value of the average running coupling from lattice fits: \(\max(A) \approx 1/2\), occurring at \(T = 1.4 T_c\)
- The hyperfine splitting estimate for \(\sigma\) and \(\pi\) mesons: \(\langle H_{hfs}\rangle \sim -m_q/128\) (negligible for \(\alpha_s \sim 0.5\))
- At \(T \rightarrow T_c\) (unquenched), the binding of pion and sigma mesons goes to \(-2 m_q(T)\), making them massless

## Context

This work extends the idea introduced by Shuryak and Zahed (2003) that at zero-binding points \(T_{zb}\), the quark-anti-quark scattering length diverges and conformal invariance is restored, producing a near-perfect fluid as observed at RHIC. It builds on earlier work by Brown et al. (1991, 1993) on hadronic states surviving above \(T_c\), lattice calculations by Datta et al. (2002) and Asakawa and Hatsuda (2003) showing charmonium remains bound above \(T_c\), and the instanton molecule framework of Ilgenfritz and Shuryak (1994) and the review by Schafer and Shuryak (1998). The paper enables a smooth connection between the chirally broken state below \(T_c\) and the chirally restored mesonic states above \(T_c\), showing that the same instanton molecule interactions that break chiral symmetry below \(T_c\) continue to operate above it, with the \(\sigma\) and \(\pi\) masses approaching zero at \(T_c\) in the chiral limit.
