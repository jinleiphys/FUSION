# Nonperturbative dynamics of scalar field theories through the Feynman-Schwinger representation

> In this paper we present a summary of results obtained for scalar field theories using the Feynman-Schwinger (FSR) approach. Specifically, scalar QED and chi^2phi theories are considered. The motivation behind the applications discussed in this paper is to use the FSR method as a rigorous tool for testing the quality of commonly used approximations in field theory. Exact calculations in a quenched theory are presented for one-, two-, and three-body bound states. Results obtained indicate that some of the commonly used approximations, such as Bethe-Salpeter ladder summation for bound states and the rainbow summation for one body problems, produce significantly different results from those obtained from the FSR approach. We find that more accurate results can be obtained using other, simpler, approximation schemes.

## Key claim

The Feynman-Schwinger representation (FSR) provides exact nonperturbative results for scalar field theories in the quenched approximation, and these exact results show that commonly used approximations such as Bethe-Salpeter ladder summation and rainbow Dyson-Schwinger summation produce significantly different results from the exact ones, while simpler approximation schemes can yield more accurate results.

## Method

The authors transform the field-theoretic path integral into a quantum mechanical path integral over particle trajectories (the Feynman-Schwinger representation), explicitly integrating out the gauge or scalar exchange fields. For scalar QED and chi^2 phi theories in the quenched approximation (det S = 1, neglecting matter loops), they compute the two-body Green's function via Monte-Carlo integration over discretized particle trajectories, extracting bound state masses from the spectral decomposition of the Green's function in the large-time limit.

## Key numbers

- For scalar QED in 0+1 dimensions with m = mu = 1 GeV, the exact FSR dressed mass M increases linearly with e^2 for all coupling strengths, while both the rainbow Dyson-Schwinger equation and bubble summation produce complex masses beyond a critical coupling.
- For scalar QED in 3+1 dimensions with mu/m = 0.15, Lambda_1/mu = 3, and Lambda_2/mu = 5, the two-body bound state mass obtained by summing only generalized ladder exchanges with dressed one-body masses (M_2^exch) equals the full result including all interactions (M_2^tot) to within numerical errors.
- The exact self energy in scalar QED is nearly linear in e^2, implying that the fourth-order mass correction (and all higher orders) is zero or very small.

## Context

This work extends the FSR approach developed by Simonov and collaborators (Simonov, 1988-2002) for QCD to scalar field theories, providing exact nonperturbative benchmarks for testing approximations. The results contradict the reliability of rainbow Dyson-Schwinger and ladder Bethe-Salpeter approximations for bound state calculations, and instead support the effectiveness of quasipotential equations (Gross, 1969; Gross, 1982) over the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation. The finding that vertex corrections and self-energy contributions cancel in scalar QED enables future work to compute bound states using only generalized ladder diagrams with dressed masses and bare vertices.
