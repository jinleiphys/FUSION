# Improved QCD Sum-Rule Approach to Heavy-Meson Decay Constants

> A visible improvement of the QCD sum-rule framework, achieved only recently by a slightly more sophisticated consideration of the hadron excitations and continuum, is applied to the extraction of the decay constants of heavy-light pseudoscalar mesons from two-point correlators of heavy-light pseudoscalar currents. This analysis is carried out entirely in terms of the running heavy-quark mass, for which the perturbative expansion exhibits a passable convergence. Our main concern is to gain control over both statistical and systematic uncertainties of the extracted decay constants, the former being induced by the QCD parameter values requested for input, the latter arising from the intrinsically limited accuracy of the QCD sum-rule techniques.

# Improved QCD Sum-Rule Approach to Heavy-Meson Decay Constants

## Key claim
The paper claims that allowing the effective continuum threshold in QCD sum rules to depend on the Borel parameter τ, rather than treating it as constant, significantly improves the accuracy of extracted heavy-meson decay constants. However, the method's success is conditional on the choice of heavy-quark mass scheme: the running MS-bar mass yields a convergent perturbative expansion, while the pole-mass scheme produces no sign of convergence and underestimates decay constants by almost 50%.

## Method
The authors compute two-point correlators of heavy-light pseudoscalar currents to three-loop order in the operator product expansion (OPE), expressed entirely in terms of the running MS-bar heavy-quark mass. They introduce a τ-dependent effective continuum threshold s_eff(τ) and determine its functional form variationally by requiring minimal deviation of a dual mass M_dual from the known ground-state meson mass over the admissible τ range. The decay constant f_P is then extracted from the Borel-transformed sum rule using this optimized threshold.

## Key numbers
- For the B-meson decay constant f_B, the pole-mass OPE yields a value almost 50% smaller than the value found within the MS-bar scheme.
- The choice of b-quark mass m_b(m_b) = (4.163 ± 0.016) GeV leads to decay-constant predictions hardly compatible with lattice findings.
- Fitting the sum-rule result for f_B to the average of lattice findings yields m_b(m_b) = (4.245 ± 0.025) GeV.

## Context
This work extends the QCD sum-rule framework originally proposed by Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979) and builds on the authors' own prior developments in Lucha, Melikhov, and Simula (2011) and Lucha, Melikhov, and Simula (quantum-mechanics analogy). The improved method brings sum-rule results for charmed-meson decay constants into perfect agreement with lattice QCD and experiment, and enables a precise extraction of the b-quark mass by matching to lattice averages. The results contradict a recent accurate determination of m_b by the same authors' earlier work, which the paper notes as a puzzle requiring resolution.
