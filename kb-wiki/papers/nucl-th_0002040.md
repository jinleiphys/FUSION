# Dileptons from transport and hydrodynamical models

> Transport and hydrodynamical models used to describe the expansion stage of a heavy-ion collision at the CERN SPS give different dilepton spectrum even if they are tuned to reproduce the observed hadron spectra. To understand the origin of this difference we compare the dilepton emission from transport and hydrodynamical models using similar initial states in both models. We find that the requirement of pion number conservation in a hydrodynamical model does not change the dilepton emission. Also the mass distribution from the transport model indicates faster cooling and longer lifetime of the fireball.

## Key claim

Even when transport and hydrodynamical models are tuned to reproduce the same observed hadron spectra, they yield different dilepton spectra; the requirement of pion number conservation in a hydrodynamical model does not change the dilepton emission, and the transport model indicates faster cooling and longer fireball lifetime.

## Method

The authors compared dilepton emission from three models: a relativistic BUU transport model, a standard 2+1 dimensional non-boost invariant hydrodynamical model with zero pion chemical potential, and a modified version of that hydrodynamical model where conserved baryon number was replaced by a conserved pion number. All models used the same initial state (a spherical fireball of radius 8 fm with a Woods-Saxon density profile, maximum energy density 0.5 GeV/fm³, maximum initial temperature 218 MeV, containing 560 pions and 260 rhos) and only considered dilepton production via ππ annihilation with free-particle cross sections and widths.

## Key numbers

- Maximum initial temperature of the fireball: T = 218 MeV.
- Maximum initial energy density: ε = 0.5 GeV/fm³.
- Initial pion number density: n_π = 0.38 fm⁻³; initial rho number density: n_ρ = 0.20 fm⁻³.
- Initial particle content: 560 pions and 260 rhos.
- Decoupling temperature in both hydrodynamical models: T_dec = 120 MeV.
- Average pion chemical potential on the decoupling surface in the pion-number-conserving hydro model: ⟨μ_π⟩ = 75 MeV.
- Dilepton yields around invariant mass 500 MeV differ by roughly a factor of two between transport and hydrodynamical models when tuned to reproduce hadron data.

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons by Huovinen (1999) and Koch (1996) that showed a factor-of-two discrepancy in dilepton yields around 500 MeV between transport and hydrodynamical models tuned to the same hadron data at CERN SPS energies. The study isolates the effect of expansion dynamics from that of pion chemical potential, showing that pion number conservation is not the main cause of the difference. The results enable future investigations into the temperature evolution in transport descriptions and the space-time origin of dilepton emission, and highlight the need to check how distributions change when all models are required to produce similar pion spectra.
