# P-wave $c\bar{c}$ meson contributions in exotic hadrons

> The nature of the $X(3872)$ and other exotic hadrons has been a subject of extensive investigation. While various theoretical models have been proposed, experimental evidence suggests that the $X(3872)$ may be a mixture state of a hadronic molecule and a $c\bar{c}$ core. In this work, we perform a systematic study of hidden-charm tetraquark candidates $X(3860)$, $X(3872)$, and $Z(3930)$ using a coupled-channel model that incorporates both $c\bar{c}$ states and $D^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$ hadronic molecular components. The model parameters are fixed to reproduce the masses of the $X(3872)$ and $Z(3930)$, and the resulting framework is used to predict the mass and structure of the $0^{++}$ state associated with the $X(3860)$. Our results support the mixture interpretation of these exotic hadrons, exhibiting strong attractions from the transition potential between $c\bar{c}$ and $D^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$ components. The molecular component dominates in the $X(3872)$, while the $c\bar{c}$ component plays a more prominent role in the $X(3860)$ and $Z(3930)$.

## Key claim

The paper supports a mixture interpretation for the hidden-charm tetraquark candidates, where the X(3872) is predominantly a hadronic molecule (80-85% molecular component) while the X(3860) and Z(3930) are mostly compact c-cbar states (90-95% c-cbar component), but this conclusion depends on fitting model parameters to the observed masses of the X(3872) and Z(3930) and using bare masses from the Godfrey-Isgur quark model.

## Method

The authors solve a coupled-channel Schrodinger equation using the Gaussian expansion method, where the Hamiltonian includes one-boson-exchange potentials for hadron-hadron interactions and a transition potential coupling bare chi_cJ(2P) states to D(*)-Dbar(*) molecular channels. The one-boson-exchange potentials are derived from heavy meson chiral Lagrangians including pseudoscalar (pi, eta, K) and vector (rho, omega, K*, phi) meson exchanges, with cutoff parameters scaled by a free parameter alpha. The transition potential is modeled with a coupling strength g_c-cbar and a range parameter Lambda_q, which are fixed to reproduce the masses of the X(3872) and Z(3930) for three values of alpha (0.7, 1.0, 1.3).

## Key numbers

* Bare chi_cJ(2P) masses from the Godfrey-Isgur model: 3916 MeV for J=0, 3953 MeV for J=1, and 3979 MeV for J=2
* Fitted coupling strength g_c-cbar = 0.0448 (alpha=0.7), 0.0427 (alpha=1.0), 0.0409 (alpha=1.3)
* Fitted range parameter Lambda_q = 2260 MeV (alpha=0.7), 3089 MeV (alpha=1.0), 4647 MeV (alpha=1.3)
* Predicted 0++ bound state at approximately 3860 MeV, matching the X(3860)
* X(3872) is 80-85% D0-Dbar*0 molecular component
* X(3860) and Z(3930) are 90-95% c-cbar component

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel mixture model of Miyake (2025) to systematically study the X(3860), X(3872), and Z(3930) as spin partners, building on the idea that the X(3872) may be a mixture of a hadronic molecule and a chi_c1(2P) core. The results support the mixture interpretation proposed in earlier work by Takizawa (2012) and use bare charmonium masses from the Godfrey-Isgur quark model (Godfrey, 1985; Barnes, 2005). The framework enables future extension to handle resonance states simultaneously, which could provide a more comprehensive explanation of a wider range of exotic hadrons.
