# Effects of $N(2000)\, 5/2^+$, $N(2060)\,5/2^-$, $N(2120)\,3/2^-$, and $N(2190)\,7/2^-$ on $K^*Λ$ photoproduction

> We reinvestigate $K^*Λ(1116)$ photoproduction off the nucleon target, based on an effective Lagrangian approach. We include higher nucleon resonances such as $N(2000)\, 5/2^+$, $N(2060)\,5/2^-$, $N(2120)\,3/2^-$, and $N(2190)\,7/2^-$, of which the data are taken from the 2012 edition of Review of Paritcle Physics, in addition to the $t$-channel diagrams ($K$, $K^*$, and $κ$), the $s$-channel nucleon, and $u$-channel hyperon($Λ$, $Σ$, and $Σ^*$) contributions. We find that the $N(2120)\,3/2^-$ and $N(2190)\,7/2^-$ resonances are essential in describing the new CLAS data for charged $K^*$ photoproduction. On the other hand, they rarely affect for neutral $K^*$ photoproduction.

## Key claim

The nucleon resonances \(N(2120)\,3/2^-\) and \(N(2190)\,7/2^-\) are essential for describing the new CLAS data for charged \(K^{*+}\Lambda\) photoproduction, but they have almost no effect on neutral \(K^{*0}\Lambda\) photoproduction; the strong coupling constant of the \(N(2120)\,3/2^-\) had to be reduced from the value estimated from its decay width to fit the data.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach at tree level, including \(t\)-channel \(K\), \(K^*\), and \(\kappa\) exchanges, \(s\)-channel nucleon and four nucleon resonances (\(N(2000)\,5/2^+\), \(N(2060)\,5/2^-\), \(N(2120)\,3/2^-\), \(N(2190)\,7/2^-\)), \(u\)-channel \(\Lambda\), \(\Sigma\), and \(\Sigma^*\) exchanges, and a contact term for gauge invariance. Coupling constants for the resonances were determined from the Breit-Wigner masses, decay widths, and helicity amplitudes listed in the 2012 Review of Particle Physics, with the strong coupling of \(N(2120)\,3/2^-\) adjusted phenomenologically to match the total cross section data.

## Key numbers

*   The total cross section for \(\gamma p \to K^{*+}\Lambda\) with the \(N(2120)\,3/2^-\) alone (using its unadjusted coupling) was approximately \(1.9\ \mu\)b, which overestimated the data; the coupling constant \(g_1\) was therefore changed from \(+1.29\) to \(+0.30\).
*   The cut-off parameters used were \(\Lambda_{K^*,N,\Lambda,\Sigma,\Sigma^*} = 0.9\) GeV, \(\Lambda_{K,\kappa} = 1.1\) GeV, and \(\Lambda_R = 1.0\) GeV.
*   The photon coupling constants for the \(K^*K\) vertex were \(g^\mathrm{charged}_{\gamma K^*K} = 0.254\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\) and \(g^\mathrm{neutral}_{\gamma K^*K} = -0.388\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\).
*   The Breit-Wigner masses and widths used were: \(N(2000)\,5/2^+\) (2090 MeV, 460 MeV), \(N(2060)\,5/2^-\) (2060 MeV, 375 MeV), \(N(2120)\,3/2^-\) (2150 MeV, 330 MeV), and \(N(2190)\,7/2^-\) (2180 MeV, 335 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis by Kim et al. (2011) which used the old \(D_{13}(2080)\) resonance from the 2010 PDG, now replaced by the \(N(2120)\,3/2^-\) and other updated resonances from the 2012 PDG based on the multichannel partial wave analysis by Anisovich et al. (2013). The new CLAS data (Wei et al., 2013) showed a significantly larger total cross section than previous preliminary data, and the present model reproduces it well for the charged reaction. The results enable future comparisons with upcoming data for the neutral reaction \(\gamma n \to K^{*0}\Lambda\) and provide predictions for single and double polarization observables to be tested in experiments.
