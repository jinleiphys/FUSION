# Roles of High-lying Excited States on Neutrino Reactions and the Gamow Teller strength for $^{40}$Ar

> Neutrino reactions on $^{40}$Ar via charged and neutral currents for detecting solar and core collapsing supernovae (SNe) neutrinos and the Gamow Teller strength are calculated by considering the high-lying excited states up to a few tens of MeV region. The nucleus was originally exploited to identify the solar neutrino emitted from $^{8}$B produced in the pp-chains on the Sun. With the higher energy neutrinos emitted from the core collapsing SNe, contributions from higher multi-pole transitions including the spin dipole resonances (SDR) as well as the Gamow Teller (GT) and Fermi transitions are shown to be important ingredients for understanding reactions induced by the SNe as well as solar neutrinos. In this work, we focused on the role of high-lying excited states which are located beyond a few low-lying states known in the experiment. Expected large difference between the cross sections of $ν_e$ and ${\bar ν_e}$ reactions on $^{40}$Ar, which difference has been anticipated in previous calculations because of the large Q value in the ${\bar ν}_e$ reaction, is significantly diminished. The reduction leads only to about 2 times difference between them. Our calculations are carried out by the Quasi-particle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA), which takes account of the neutron-proton pairing as well as proton-proton and neutron-neutron pairing correlations. They were successfully applied in the description of the nuclear beta decay and relevant neutrino reaction data on $^{12}$C and $^{56}$Fe, and the GT data on the $^{138}$La and $^{180}$Ta.

## Key claim

The expected large difference (about 12 times) between the cross sections of $\nu_e$ and $\bar{\nu}_e$ reactions on $^{40}$Ar, previously anticipated because of the large Q value in the $\bar{\nu}_e$ reaction, is significantly diminished to only about 2 times difference when high-lying excited states up to a few tens of MeV are included in the calculation.

## Method

The authors used the Quasi-particle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) which includes neutron-proton pairing as well as proton-proton and neutron-neutron pairing correlations, with the Brueckner ${\cal G}$ matrix derived from the Bonn CD nucleon-nucleon potential. They calculated charged-current and neutral-current neutrino and antineutrino reactions on $^{40}$Ar by including multipole transitions up to $J^{\pi} = 4^{\pm}$ with explicit momentum dependence, considering excited states up to a few tens of MeV rather than only the few low-lying states known from experiment. For the particle emission decay mode following excitation, they employed the statistical Hauser-Feshbach theory approach as done by Nakazato et al. (2005).

## Key numbers

- Q-value for $^{40}$Ar($\nu_e, e^-$)$^{40}$K$^{*}$ reaction: 1.50 MeV; Q-value for $^{40}$Ar($\bar{\nu}_e, e^+$)$^{40}$Cl$^{*}$ reaction: 7.48 MeV (about 5 times larger)
- Cross sections for $^{40}$Ar($\nu_e, e^-$)$^{40}$K$^{*}$ are about 3.5 times smaller than previous RPA calculations by Rubin et al. (2008)
- Cross sections for $^{40}$Ar($\bar{\nu}_e, e^+$)$^{40}$Cl$^{*}$ are about twice larger than those of Rubin et al. (2003, 2008)
- At $E_{\nu}$ = 80 MeV, the difference between $\nu_e$ and $\bar{\nu}_e$ cross sections is reduced to about 2 times (from the previously expected 12 times)
- Running sum of GT strength up to 7.5 MeV from $^{40}$K ground state: $\Sigma B(GT_{-}) = 4$ to 5 from experiment; QRPA gives $\Sigma B(GT_{-}) = 3.6$ (after multiplying by $(g_V/g_A)^2$)
- GT strength localized at 3.6, 3.7, 8.6, and 8.9 MeV from $^{40}$Ar ground state (corresponding to 2.1, 2.2, 7.1, and 7.4 MeV from $^{40}$K ground state)
- Pairing gaps used: $\Delta_{nn} = 1.768$ MeV, $\Delta_{pp} = 1.776$ MeV, $\delta_{np} = 0.684$ MeV for $^{40}$Ar
- Coulomb distortion increases both $\nu_e$ and $\bar{\nu}_e$ reactions by about 15% maximally
- Cross sections by charged current are 4 to 5 times larger than those by neutral current reactions

## Context

This work extends previous RPA calculations by Kolbe and Langanke (2003) and Rubin et al. (2003, 2008) for neutrino reactions on $^{40}$Ar, and shell model calculations by Suzuki et al. (2006), by explicitly including high-lying excited states up to a few tens of MeV. The QRPA approach had been successfully applied to neutrino reactions on $^{12}$C, $^{56}$Fe, and $^{56}$Ni, and to GT data on $^{138}$La and $^{180}$Ta by the same authors. The results provide a reference for detecting supernova neutrinos in liquid argon time projection chamber detectors such as ICARUS, and suggest that the $\nu_e$ and $\bar{\nu}_e$ reactions on $^{40}$Ar cannot be as easily distinguished as previously thought. The authors note that more experimental data for higher excited states through ($^{3}$He, t) or (p,n) reactions are needed for further conclusions.
