# Re-analysis of the Gamow-Teller distributions for N=Z nuclei, 24Mg, 28Si, and 32S

> The Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions of sd shell N=Z nuclei ($^{24}$Mg, $^{28}$Si, and $^{32}$S) are investigated within the framework of proton-neutron quasi particle random phase approximation (pn QRPA) using a deformed basis. The nuclear properties of these special nuclei were investigated by the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model. The RMF framework with density-dependent interactions (DD-PC1 and DD-ME2) was employed to compute ground-state deformation parameters (beta 2) using potential energy curves. The $β_2$ values computed from the RMF and the finite range droplet model (FRDM) were employed as a free parameter in the pn QRPA calculation to investigate the resulting GT strength distributions.The present model-based analyses compare well with the observed data.

# Re-analysis of the Gamow-Teller distributions for N=Z nuclei, 24Mg, 28Si, and 32S

## Key claim
The Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions for the sd-shell N=Z nuclei 24Mg, 28Si, and 32S, calculated using the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) with deformation parameters from the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model, agree well with experimental data, but the agreement is only marginally sensitive to the specific choice of deformation parameter (beta2) from different models. The RMF-based deformation parameters produce better predictions than the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) parameters at low excitation energies, while FRDM parameters perform marginally better at high excitation energies.

## Method
The authors employed the pn-QRPA model with a deformed Nilsson basis to compute GT strength distributions for 24Mg, 28Si, and 32S. Ground-state quadrupole deformation parameters (beta2) were calculated using the RMF model with two density-dependent interactions (DD-ME2 and DD-PC1) via potential energy curves, and also taken from the FRDM. These three sets of beta2 values were used as free parameters in the pn-QRPA calculation, which included pairing correlations via BCS approximation and separable particle-particle and particle-hole GT interactions with constants from Homma (Ref. Homma). The calculated GT strengths were compared with experimental data from Anderson (Ref. Anderson), Zegers (Ref. Zegers), and Grewe (Ref. Grewe).

## Key numbers
- 24Mg: FRDM beta2 = 0.393, DD-ME2 beta2 = 0.508, DD-PC1 beta2 = 0.525
- 28Si: FRDM beta2 = -0.363, DD-ME2 beta2 = -0.377, DD-PC1 beta2 = -0.376
- 32S: FRDM beta2 = 0.221, DD-ME2 beta2 = 0.263, DD-PC1 beta2 = 0.243
- Calculated GT strengths exhaust almost 100% of the Ikeda sum rule up to Ex = 35 MeV
- For N=Z nuclei, equal GT strength was calculated in the tau+ and tau- channels
- Experimental data for 24Mg were available up to Ex = 12 MeV (Anderson, Ref. Anderson) and up to 6.88 MeV (Zegers, Ref. Zegers)
- Experimental data for 32S were available up to Ex = 7.01 MeV (Grewe, Ref. Grewe)
- The oscillator constant was chosen as hbar-omega = 45 A^{-1/3} - 25 A^{-2/3} MeV

## Context
This work extends previous studies of GT strength distributions in sd-shell N=Z nuclei, particularly the deformed QRPA (DQRPA) calculations by Eunja (Ref. Eunja) which used a Brueckner G-matrix based on the CD Bonn potential with Woods-Saxon single-particle energies. The present study differs by using RMF-computed deformation parameters rather than FRDM values alone, and by employing the pn-QRPA framework with a separable interaction. The results enable improved predictions of beta-decay rates for astrophysical applications, particularly for nuclei near the iron region relevant to weak-interaction rates as discussed by Bethe (Ref. Bethe). The work also addresses the importance of self-consistency in QRPA approaches, as emphasized by Engel (Ref. Engel3), Paar (Ref. Paar), and Borzov (Ref. Borzov, Borzov1).
