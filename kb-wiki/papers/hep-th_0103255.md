# Stability of Massive Cosmological Gravitons

> We analyze the physics of massive spin 2 fields in (A)dS backgrounds and exhibit that: The theory is stable only for masses m^2 >= 2Λ/3, where the conserved energy associated with the background timelike Killing vector is positive, while the instability for m^2<2Λ/3 is traceable to the helicity 0 energy. The stable, unitary, partially massless theory at m^2=2Λ/3 describes 4 propagating degrees of freedom, corresponding to helicities (+/-2,+/-1) but contains no 0 helicity excitation.

## Key claim

Massive spin-2 fields in (anti-)de Sitter backgrounds are stable only when the squared mass satisfies \(m^2 \ge 2\Lambda/3\); the instability for \(m^2 < 2\Lambda/3\) is traced specifically to the helicity-0 mode, and at the critical line \(m^2 = 2\Lambda/3\) the theory becomes partially massless, describing only four propagating degrees of freedom (helicities \(\pm2,\pm1\)) with no helicity-0 excitation.

## Method

The authors perform a 3+1 Hamiltonian decomposition of the cosmological Einstein action plus a Fierz-Pauli mass term, expanded to quadratic order about a de Sitter (dS) background in synchronous gauge. They then apply the orthogonal decomposition of symmetric 2-tensors into transverse-traceless, transverse-vector, and scalar (helicity-0) parts, and analyze the constraint structure and field equations for each helicity sector separately. The conserved energy associated with the background timelike Killing vector is constructed and its positivity is examined using a triangle-inequality method adapted from Abbott and Deser (1982).

## Key numbers

* Stability condition: \(m^2 \ge 2\Lambda/3\) (equivalently \(m^2 \ge 2M^2\) where \(M = \sqrt{\Lambda/3}\)).
* At the critical line \(m^2 = 2\Lambda/3\) (i.e., \(\nu^2 = 0\)), the partially massless theory propagates 4 degrees of freedom: helicities \((\pm2,\pm1)\).
* The effective mass appearing in the helicity-\(\pm2\) and \(\pm1\) actions is \(m^2 - 9M^2/4\).
* The helicity-0 action in the stable region \(m^2 > 2\Lambda/3\) takes the same form as the other helicities, with the same effective mass \(m^2 - 9M^2/4\).
* The dS background scale factor is \(f(t) = e^{Mt}\) with \(M = \sqrt{\Lambda/3}\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of massive spin-2 fields in cosmological backgrounds initiated by Higuchi (1987) and Deser and Waldron (2001), who identified the sign change of the helicity-0 norm across the line \(m^2 = 2\Lambda/3\). It provides a concrete Hamiltonian proof of stability and unitarity in the allowed region, and explicitly demonstrates that the partially massless theory at \(m^2 = 2\Lambda/3\) (first identified by Deser and Nepomechie (1983)) removes the helicity-0 mode via a new scalar gauge invariance. The results enable further study of causality and stability for higher-spin fields in curved backgrounds, and the Hamiltonian formalism developed here can be extended to spin-3/2 and higher spins.
