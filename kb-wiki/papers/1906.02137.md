# Renormalizing random-phase approximation by using exact pairing

> A fully self-consistent renormalized random-phase approximation is constructed based on the self-consistent Hartree-Fock mean field plus exact pairing solutions (EP). This approach exactly conserves the particle number and restores the energy-weighted sum rule, which is violated in the conventional renormalized particle-hole random-phase approximation for a given multipolarity. The numerical calculations are carried out for several light, medium, and heavy-mass nuclei such as $^{22}$O, $^{60}$Ni, and $^{90}$Zr by using an effective MSk3 interaction. To study the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR), the calculations are also performed for the two light and neutron-rich $^{24,28}$O isotopes, whose PDRs are known to be dominant. The results obtained show that the inclusion of ground-state correlations beyond the random-phase approximation (RPA) by means of the occupation numbers obtained from the EP affects the RPA solutions within the whole mass range, although this effect decreases with increasing the mass number. At the same time, the anti-pairing effect is observed via a significant reduction of pairing in neutron-rich nuclei. The enhancement of PDR is found in most of neutron-rich nuclei under consideration within our method.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a fully self-consistent renormalized random-phase approximation (SC-HFEPRPA) that uses exact pairing (EP) occupation numbers to renormalize the residual interaction, claiming this approach restores the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) and exactly conserves particle number without extending the RPA configuration space beyond particle-hole excitations, though the effect of ground-state correlations decreases with increasing mass number.

## Method

The authors start from a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock mean field (using the MSk3 interaction) and add a constant monopole pairing Hamiltonian, which is diagonalized exactly in a truncated single-particle space around the Fermi surface to obtain exact occupation numbers \(f_j^{EP}\). These occupation numbers replace the Hartree-Fock values (1 for holes, 0 for particles) in the ground-state correlation factor \(D_{ph} = f_h - f_p\), which renormalizes the particle-hole residual interaction matrix elements in the RPA equations. The resulting nonlinear RPA equations are solved iteratively until self-consistency between the mean field, exact pairing, and RPA occupation numbers is achieved, with the pairing strength \(G\) adjusted so that the EWSR is fulfilled and the exact pairing gap matches experimental odd-even mass differences.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{22}\)O, the isoscalar dipole energy-weighted sum (EWSS) obtained with the normalization parameter \(\eta \neq 1\) is \(0.677391 \times 10^5\) e\(^2\)fm\(^6\)MeV, which is only about 0.9% larger than the value with \(\eta = 1\) (\(0.671324 \times 10^5\) e\(^2\)fm\(^6\)MeV).
- The spurious state energy in \(^{22}\)O shifts from 3.75 MeV (cut-off energy \(E_c = 60\) MeV) down to 3.12 MeV when \(E_c\) is increased to 130 MeV.
- The spurious state energy in \(^{90}\)Zr reduces from 2.24 MeV (\(E_c = 60\) MeV) to 1.34 MeV (\(E_c = 150\) MeV).
- The difference between the calculated average binding energy \(BE/A\) and experimental data is lower than 0.5% for all nuclei considered.
- The self-consistent iteration convergence criterion is set to \(|\epsilon_j(n) - \epsilon_j(n-1)| \leq 10^{-4}\) MeV for single-particle energies and \(|D_{ph}(n) - D_{ph}(n-1)| \leq 10^{-3}\) for the GSC factor.

## Context

This work extends the renormalized RPA (RRPA) approach of Catara (1996) and Hirsch (1992), which suffered from sum-rule violation because the ground-state correlation factor \(D_{ph}\) reduced the residual interaction matrix elements. By incorporating exact pairing solutions from Volya (2001) and Zelevinsky (2003), the method resolves three issues simultaneously: the inconsistency of using Hartree-Fock occupation numbers in the conventional RRPA, the inclusion of pairing without violating particle-number conservation (unlike BCS or HFB used in QRPA), and the restoration of the EWSR without adding particle-particle and hole-hole configurations. The approach enables studies of pygmy dipole resonances in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes (\(^{24,28}\)O) and can be applied to any nuclei where monopole pairing generates finite ground-state correlations, including magic nuclei.
