# Critical Points in Nuclei and Interacting Boson Model Intrinsic States

> We consider properties of critical points in the interacting boson model, corresponding to flat-bottomed potentials as encountered in a second-order phase transition between spherical and deformed $γ$-unstable nuclei. We show that intrinsic states with an effective $β$-deformation reproduce the dynamics of the underlying non-rigid shapes. The effective deformation can be determined from the the global minimum of the energy surface after projection onto the appropriate symmetry. States of fixed $N$ and good O(5) symmetry projected from these intrinsic states provide good analytic estimates to the exact eigenstates, energies and quadrupole transition rates at the critical point.

## Key claim

The paper shows that at the critical point of a second-order shape phase transition between spherical and deformed γ-unstable nuclei, where the intrinsic energy surface is flat and has no stable minimum deformation, a finite boson system nevertheless possesses an effective β-deformation that can be determined by minimizing the energy surface after projection onto O(5) symmetry.

## Method

The authors construct analytic wave functions with fixed boson number N and good O(5) symmetry by projecting from intrinsic states with an effective β-deformation parameter y = β². They apply this τ-projection method to the interacting boson model (IBM) Hamiltonian at the critical point of the U(5)-O(6) phase transition, where the parameters satisfy ε = (N-1)A. The effective deformation is determined by finding the global minimum of the O(5)-projected energy surface E_{ξ=1,τ=0}(y), and the resulting states are used to compute analytic estimates for energies and quadrupole transition rates.

## Key numbers

- The effective β-deformation parameter for N=5 is y = 0.314, determined as the global minimum of the O(5)-projected energy surface E_{ξ=1,τ=0}(y) for the critical Hamiltonian H_cri.
- Band-mixing parameters η_τ between the ground band and first excited band for N=5 are 0.12% (τ=0), 3.53% (τ=1), 4.14% (τ=2), and 3.05% (τ=3).
- For the critical point, the energy ratio R = (E_{ξ=1,τ=2} - E_{ξ=1,τ=0})/(E_{ξ=1,τ=1} - E_{ξ=1,τ=0}) becomes a function of x = Ny only in the large-N limit, with curves for different N asymptoting to one universal curve.
- The τ-projected calculation with N=5 gives E(L⁺_{1,2}) = 2.19 (normalized to E(2⁺_{1,1})=1), compared to the exact IBM value of 2.195 and the E(5) geometric model value of 2.20.
- The τ-projected B(E2; 0⁺_{2,0} → 2⁺_{1,1}) = 0.43 (in units where B(E2; 2⁺_{1,1} → 0⁺_{1,0})=1), compared to the experimental value for ¹³⁴Ba of 0.42(12).

## Context

This work extends the geometric E(5) critical point introduced by Iachello (2000) for infinite square-well potentials into the finite-N framework of the interacting boson model (IBM) developed by Arima and Iachello. The authors build on the observation by Casten and collaborators (2000) that the critical IBM Hamiltonian with N=5 can numerically replicate the E(5) predictions for ¹³⁴Ba. The τ-projection method provides analytic estimates without invoking large-N approximations, and the approach of variation after projection follows the spirit of Otsuka (1987), who showed that a γ-unstable O(6) state can be generated from a rigid triaxial intrinsic state with an effective γ-deformation of 30°. This work enables analytic calculations at the E(5) critical point for finite boson systems and establishes a relationship between the IBM and geometric model through the scaling x = Ny.
