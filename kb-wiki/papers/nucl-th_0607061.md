# The spectral function of the omega meson in nuclear matter from a coupled-channel resonance model

> We calculate the spectral function of the omega meson in nuclear matter at zero temperature by means of the low-density theorem. The omega N forward scattering amplitude is calculated within a unitary coupled-channel effective Lagrangian model that has been applied successfully to the combined analysis of pion- and photon-induced reactions. While the peak of the omega spectral distribution is shifted only slightly, we find a considerable broadening of the omega meson due to resonance-hole excitations. For omega mesons at rest with respect to the surrounding nuclear medium, we find an additional width of about 60 MeV at saturation density.

## Key claim

The omega meson in nuclear matter at saturation density experiences a considerable broadening (additional width of about 60 MeV for mesons at rest) but only a slight upward shift of the peak position by about 10 MeV, contrary to some earlier predictions of a large downward mass shift.

## Method

The omega meson in-medium self energy was calculated using the low-density theorem, where the self energy is proportional to the omega-nucleon forward scattering amplitude times the nuclear density. The omega-nucleon scattering amplitude was obtained from a unitary coupled-channel K-matrix model that simultaneously describes all available data on pion- and photon-induced reactions on the nucleon up to center-of-mass energies of 2 GeV, including final states such as pi N, 2pi N, eta N, omega N, K Lambda, and K Sigma. The model includes all resonance states with quantum numbers J^P = 1/2^±, 3/2^±, and 5/2^± up to masses of 2 GeV, allowing calculation of the self energy also for finite omega momenta.

## Key numbers

- Additional width of the omega meson at rest: about 60 MeV at saturation density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Peak position shift: upward by about 10 MeV at saturation density
- Omega-nucleon scattering length: a_omegaN = (-0.17 + i 0.31) fm, with contributions a_omegaN^{1/2-} = (-0.27 + i 0.16) fm and a_omegaN^{3/2-} = (+0.11 + i 0.15) fm
- Saturation density: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Resonance-hole branch from S11(1535) excitation appears at invariant mass sqrt(q^2) ≈ 550 MeV for zero momentum, moving to sqrt(q^2) ≈ 500 MeV at momentum 300 MeV
- Cusp structure from elastic channel opening appears at masses of 782 MeV (|q|=0), 750 MeV (|q|=300 MeV), and 649 MeV (|q|=600 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel approach of Lutz et al. (2001), who found similar resonance-hole effects but restricted to angular momentum L=0 between nucleon and vector meson, by including all partial waves up to J=5/2 and allowing for finite omega momenta. The results contrast with the model of Klingl et al. (1997, 1998), which predicted a drastic downward shift of the omega pole mass using chiral SU(3) dynamics with only the Delta(1232) resonance. The calculated spectral functions enable more realistic modeling of nuclear reactions such as gamma A and p A experiments, where produced omega mesons can have large momenta, and provide input for interpreting dilepton measurements from experiments like CB/TAPS and CLAS.
