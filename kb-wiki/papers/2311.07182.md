# Quantal effect on the opening angle distribution between the fission fragment's spins

> Background: Several approaches are currently trying to understand the generation of angular momentum in the fission fragments. The microscopic TDDFT and statistical FREYA lead to different predictions concerning the opening angle distribution formed between the two spins in particular at 0 and 180 degrees. Purpose: This letter aims to investigate how the geometry and the quantum nature of spins impact the distribution of opening angles to understand what leads to different model predictions. Method: Various assumptions of K distribution (K=0, isotropic, isotropic with total K=0, and from TDFFT) are investigated in a quantum approach. These distributions are then compared to the classical limit using the Clebsch-Gordan coefficients in the limit of $\hbar$ approaches zero. Results: It is shown that in all the schematic scenario the quantal distribution of opening angle lead to the expected behavior in the classical limit. The model shows that the quantal nature of the spins prevents the population of opening angles close to 0 and 180 degrees. The difference in opening angle in the 2D and isotropic 3D distribution is discussed and it is shown that the realistic TDFFT opening angle distribution presents an intermediate behavior between the two cases. Conclusions: The last comparison reveals two key differences between the two models' predictions: the quantal spins' nature in TDDFT and the assumption of zero K values in FREYA.

# Quantal effect on the opening angle distribution between the fission fragment's spins

## Key claim

The quantal nature of spins in fission fragments prevents the population of opening angles close to 0 and 180 degrees, and the difference between TDDFT and FREYA predictions arises from two factors: the quantum treatment of spins in TDDFT and the assumption of zero K values in FREYA.

## Method

Various schematic assumptions of K distributions (K=0 for 2D geometry, isotropic 3D, isotropic 3D with total K=0, and distributions from TDDFT calculations) were investigated in a quantum framework using Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. These quantal distributions were compared to the classical limit by taking the limit of ℏ approaching zero in the Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, and to classical Monte Carlo sampling of spin orientations.

## Key numbers

- For the 2D case with spin cutoff parameters σ_H = 7 ℏ and σ_L = 10 ℏ, the quantal distribution shows a peak at 25 degrees with depletion at 0 and 180 degrees
- Obtaining an opening angle of 5 degrees in the 2D case requires a spin cutoff S_c = 262 ℏ, and for a 1-degree angle, S_c = 6565 ℏ
- The classical limit of the 2D distribution yields a uniform distribution P(φ_HL) = sum_{S_L,S_H} c_{S_L,S_H}^2 / π
- The fully isotropic 3D distribution in the classical limit follows P(φ_HL) = sin(φ_HL)/2
- For the realistic TDDFT calculation of ²⁵²Cf spontaneous fission, the angle θ_F between spins and fission axis follows P(θ_H) ∝ exp(-|θ_H - 90| / 0.28)

## Context

This work extends the discussion by Randrup (2022) on the impact of geometry (2D versus 3D) on opening angle distributions, moving from a classical framework to a quantum one. It addresses discrepancies between the microscopic TDDFT approach (Bulgac 2022, Scamps 2023) and the statistical FREYA model (Randrup 2021, Vogt 2021), where FREYA predicts a flat distribution while TDDFT shows vanishing probabilities at 0 and 180 degrees. The analysis enables understanding of why the TDDFT distribution shows an intermediate behavior between the 2D and isotropic 3D cases, and clarifies the role of quantal spin effects versus geometric assumptions in model predictions.
