# Effect of giant resonances on fluctuations of electromagnetic fields in heavy ion collisions

> We perform quantum calculations of fluctuations of the electromagnetic fields in $AA$ collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. Calculations are performed with the help of the fluctuation-dissipation theorem accounting for the giant dipole and quadrupole resonances. We find that in the quantum picture the field fluctuations are much smaller than that predicted by the classical Monte-Carlo simulation with the Woods-Saxon nuclear density used in previous analyses.

## Key claim

Quantum calculations of electromagnetic field fluctuations in AA collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, accounting for giant dipole and quadrupole resonances via the fluctuation-dissipation theorem, yield field fluctuations much smaller than those predicted by classical Monte-Carlo simulations with the Woods-Saxon nuclear density; the reduction is partly from smaller fluctuations of the dipole and quadrupole moments and partly from dynamical quantum effects in the electromagnetic fields.

## Method

The author uses the fluctuation-dissipation theorem (FDT) from Landau and Lifshitz (1960) to compute the correlators of fluctuating electromagnetic fields in the rest frame of each nucleus, then Lorentz-transforms to the center-of-mass frame of the AA collision. The nuclear response is included via dipole and quadrupole polarizability tensors parametrized by Lorentzian forms fitted to photoabsorption cross-section data for the giant dipole resonance (GDR) and to inelastic scattering and Compton scattering data for the isoscalar and isovector giant quadrupole resonances (IS-GQR and IV-GQR) in 208Pb. The field correlators at the center of the fireball are evaluated by integrating over frequency along the positive imaginary axis.

## Key numbers

- For Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=0.2 TeV (RHIC), the ratio sqrt(<delta B_x^2>)/<B_y> at t~0.1 fm is smaller in the quantum picture than in the classical Monte-Carlo calculation by a factor of ~3; at t >= 0.5 fm the quantum result is smaller by a factor of ~3-8.
- For Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV (LHC), the ratio sqrt(<delta B_x^2>)/<B_y> at t~0.01 fm is smaller in the quantum picture than in the classical calculation by a factor of ~3; at t >= 0.05 fm the quantum result is smaller by a factor of ~6-30.
- The quantum dipole moment squared <0|d^2|0> is smaller than the classical Monte-Carlo prediction with Woods-Saxon density by a factor of ~5.
- The quantum quadrupole moment squared <0|(Q_ij/6)^2|0> is smaller than the classical Monte-Carlo prediction by a factor of ~8.
- GDR parameters for 208Pb: omega_d ~ 13.3 MeV, Gamma_d ~ 3.72 MeV, c_d ~ 56.79 GeV^{-3}.
- IS-GQR parameters for 208Pb: omega_q^IS ~ 10.89 MeV, Gamma_q^IS ~ 3 MeV, c_q^IS ~ 3984 GeV^{-5}.
- IV-GQR parameters for 208Pb: omega_q^IV ~ 23 MeV, Gamma_q^IV ~ 3.9 MeV, c_q^IV ~ 1524 GeV^{-5}.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous quantum calculation (Zakharov 2017) that addressed only the dipole mode, by now including the quadrupole modes (IS-GQR and IV-GQR). It contradicts the classical Monte-Carlo approach used in earlier analyses by Skokov et al. (2009), Deng and Huang (2012), Liao (2014), and Roy and Pu (2015), which treated field fluctuations using Lienard-Wiechert potentials of protons with Woods-Saxon nuclear distributions. The results also do not support the qualitative analysis by Tuchin (2013) arguing that quantum diffusion of protons may be very important. The finding that the classical treatment overestimates fluctuations of dipole and quadrupole moments may be important for event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations of AA collisions that currently ignore collective effects in nuclear distributions.
