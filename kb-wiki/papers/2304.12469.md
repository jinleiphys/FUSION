# Polarised parton distribution functions and proton spin

> Supposing there exists an effective charge which defines an evolution scheme for both unpolarised and polarised parton distribution functions (DFs) that is all-orders exact and using Ansätze for hadron-scale proton polarised valence quark DFs, constrained by flavour-separated axial charges and insights from perturbative quantum chromodynamics, predictions are delivered for all proton polarised DFs at the scale $ζ_{\rm C}^2 = 3\,$GeV$^2$. The pointwise behaviour of the predicted DFs and, consequently, their moments, compare favourably with results inferred from data. Notably, flavour-separated singlet polarised DFs are small. On the other hand, the polarised gluon DF, $ΔG(x;ζ_{\rm C})$, is large and positive. Using our result, we predict $\int_{0.05}^1\,dx\,ΔG(x;ζ_{\rm C}) = 0.214(4)$ and that experimental measurements of the proton flavour-singlet axial charge should return $a_0^{\rm E}(ζ_{\rm C}) = 0.35(2)$.

## Key claim

Supposing an effective charge exists that makes leading-order DGLAP evolution all-orders exact, and using Ansätze for hadron-scale polarised valence quark distributions constrained by flavour-separated axial charges, the authors predict all proton polarised parton distribution functions at ζ²_C = 3 GeV²; the polarised gluon distribution is large and positive, while flavour-separated singlet polarised distributions are small.

## Method

The authors construct hadron-scale polarised valence quark distributions using four distinct mapping functions applied to previously calculated unpolarised valence quark distributions from a quark+diquark Faddeev equation, with each mapping's parameter fixed by requiring the first moment to reproduce the flavour-separated axial charges g_A^u and g_A^d. They then evolve these hadron-scale distributions to ζ_C = √3 GeV using an all-orders evolution scheme based on an effective charge α̂, which integrates leading-order DGLAP equations exactly, including thresholds for heavier quark flavours.

## Key numbers

- Predicted gluon polarised first moment: ∫_{0.05}^1 dx ΔG(x;ζ_C) = 0.214(4)
- Predicted proton flavour-singlet axial charge: a_0^E(ζ_C) = 0.35(2)
- Proton axial charge from Faddeev equation: g_A = 1.25(3)
- u quark fraction of axial charge: g_A^u/g_A = 0.76(1); d quark fraction: g_A^d/g_A = -0.24(1)
- Singlet axial charge at hadron scale: a_0 = 0.65(2)
- Hadron scale: ζ_ℋ = 0.331(2) GeV
- Predicted ratio at x=1: Δd/Δu = -0.14(3); Δu/u = 0.63(8); Δd/d = -0.38(7); A_1^p = 0.58(8); A_1^n = 0.15(5); d/u = 0.23(6); F_2^n/F_2^p = 0.45(5)
- Evolved to ζ=10 GeV: ∫_{0.05}^1 dx ΔG(x) = 0.199(3)
- Predicted strange sea integral: ∫_{0.004}^{0.3} dx Δs_S(x;ζ_C) = 0.0072(1)
- Mean value of ΔG/G on COMPASS measurement domain: 0.167(3)

## Context

This work extends the continuum Schwinger function methods (CSMs) approach to polarised parton distributions, building on the unpolarised DF calculations of Chang (2022) and the all-orders evolution scheme developed by Cui, Raya, and collaborators (2019-2022). The predictions for x→1 ratios update earlier Faddeev equation estimates by Roberts (2013) and are compared with results from the DSSV14 global fit and experimental data from HERMES (2004), COMPASS (2010), CLAS EG1 (2006-2017), and Jefferson Lab experiments E06-014 and E99-117. The results enable future comparisons with data from CLAS RGC (E12-06-110) and provide internally consistent predictions for all polarised DFs that can be tested by upcoming high-x measurements.
