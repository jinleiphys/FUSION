# $^4{\rm He}$ vs. $^4{\rm Li}$ and production of light nuclei in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We propose to measure the yields of $^4{\rm He}$ and $^4{\rm Li}$ in relativistic heavy-ion collisions to clarify a mechanism of light nuclei production. Since the masses of $^4{\rm He}$ and $^4{\rm Li}$ are almost equal, the yield of $^4{\rm Li}$ predicted by the thermal model is 5 times bigger than that of $^4{\rm He}$ which reflects the different numbers of internal degrees of freedom of the two nuclides. Their internal structure is, however, very different: the alpha particle is well bound and compact while $^4{\rm Li}$ is weakly bound and loose. Within the coalescence model the ratio of yields of $^4{\rm Li}$ to $^4{\rm He}$ is shown to be significantly smaller than that in the thermal model and the ratio decreases fast from central to peripheral collisions of relativistic heavy-ion collisions because the coalescence rate strongly depends on the nucleon source radius. Since the nuclide $^4{\rm Li}$ is unstable and it decays into $^3{\rm He}$ and $p$ after roughly $30~{\rm fm}/c$, the yield of $^4{\rm Li}$ can be experimentally obtained through a measurement of the $^3{\rm He}\!-\!p$ correlation function.

# $^4{\rm He}$ vs. $^4{\rm Li}$ and production of light nuclei in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The ratio of yields of $^4{\rm Li}$ to $^4{\rm He}$ in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is predicted to be significantly smaller in the coalescence model than the factor of 5 predicted by the thermal model, and this ratio decreases strongly from central to peripheral collisions because the coalescence rate depends on the nucleon source radius. The measurement of this ratio, accessible via the $^3{\rm He}$-proton correlation function, can distinguish between the two production mechanisms.

## Method
The authors compute the coalescence formation rates for $^4{\rm He}$ and $^4{\rm Li}$ using the quantum-mechanical coalescence model, where the rate depends on the nucleon source radius $R_s$ and the internal wave functions of the nuclei. For $^4{\rm He}$, a Gaussian wave function is used with RMS radius $R_\alpha = 1.68$ fm; for $^4{\rm Li}$, a cluster wave function with a $^3{\rm He}$-proton structure is assumed, with the $^3{\rm He}$ cluster radius $R_c = 1.97$ fm and the unknown $^4{\rm Li}$ radius $R_{\rm Li}$ treated as a parameter. The ratio of yields is then compared to the thermal model prediction of 5, which arises purely from the ratio of internal degrees of freedom (five spin states of $^4{\rm Li}$ versus one for $^4{\rm He}$).

## Key numbers
- Thermal model predicts the yield of $^4{\rm Li}$ is 5 times bigger than that of $^4{\rm He}$, reflecting five spin states of $^4{\rm Li}$ versus one for $^4{\rm He}$.
- For $R_{\rm Li} = 3$ fm and central collisions ($R_s \approx 7$ fm), the coalescence model ratio $W_{\rm Li}^{l=2}/W_{\rm He} = 1.2$; for $l=1$, the ratio is larger by a factor of $5/2$.
- For peripheral collisions with $R_s \approx 4$ fm (40-60% centrality), the ratio $W_{\rm Li}^{l=2}/W_{\rm He}$ drops to 0.7.
- The RMS radius of $^4{\rm He}$ is $R_\alpha = 1.68$ fm; the RMS radius of $^3{\rm He}$ is $R_c = 1.97$ fm (Angeli 2013).
- $^4{\rm Li}$ decays into $^3{\rm He} + p$ after roughly $30~{\rm fm}/c$ with a width of 6 MeV.
- The source radius $R_s$ for Pb-Pb collisions at LHC varies between 3 and 7 fm from peripheral to central collisions (Adam 2015).

## Context
This work extends the suggestion by Mrowczynski (2016) to compare $^4{\rm He}$ and $^4{\rm Li}$ yields as a test of production mechanisms. It directly contrasts the thermal model (Andronic 2010, Cleymans 2011), which successfully describes light nuclei yields at LHC with a temperature of 156 MeV, with the coalescence model (Sato 1981, Gyulassy 1982, Mrowczynski 1987). The thermal and coalescence models were previously noted to give similar yields for many light nuclei (Baltz 1993, Zhu 2015, Mrowczynski 2016), but this work identifies a quantitative distinction through the centrality dependence of the $^4{\rm Li}$/$^4{\rm He}$ ratio. The proposed measurement of $^4{\rm Li}$ via the $^3{\rm He}$-proton correlation function was previously demonstrated in low-energy $^{40}{\rm Ar}$-induced reactions on $^{197}{\rm Au}$ at 60 MeV per nucleon (Pochodzalla 1987).
