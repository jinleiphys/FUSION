# Interrelation between the isoscalar octupole phonon and the proton-neutron mixed-symmetry quadrupole phonon in near spherical nuclei

> The interrelation between the octupole phonon and the low-lying proton-neutron mixed-symmetry quadrupole phonon in near-spherical nuclei is investigated. The one-phonon states decay by collective E3 and E2 transitions to the ground state and by relatively strong E1 and M1 transitions to the isoscalar 2+ state. We apply the proton-neutron version of the Interacting Boson Model including quadrupole and octupole bosons (sdf-IBM-2). Two F-spin symmetric dynamical symmetry limits of the model, namely the vibrational and the γ-unstable ones, are considered. We derive analytical formulae for excitation energies as well as B(E1), B(M1), B(E2) and B(E3) values for a number of transitions between low-lying states.

## Key claim

The paper investigates the interrelation between the isoscalar octupole phonon and the proton-neutron mixed-symmetry quadrupole phonon in near-spherical nuclei, proposing that the coupling of these two phonons generates mixed-symmetry two-phonon states with complex decay patterns that can be predicted analytically within the proton-neutron version of the Interacting Boson Model including quadrupole and octupole bosons (sdf-IBM-2), but only within two specific F-spin symmetric dynamical symmetry limits (vibrational and gamma-unstable).

## Method

The authors apply the sdf-IBM-2, which includes monopole (s), quadrupole (d), and octupole (f) bosons with proton-neutron degrees of freedom, and develop the formalism in two dynamical symmetry limits: the U(5) (vibrational) and SO(6) (gamma-unstable) limits. They derive analytical formulae for excitation energies and transition strengths (B(E1), B(M1), B(E2), B(E3)) using group reduction chains and Casimir operators, constructing explicit wave functions for the lowest states including the symmetric and mixed-symmetry one-phonon and two-phonon states. The E1 transition operator includes a quadrupole-octupole coupled two-body part rather than a one-body operator, to account for enhanced low-lying E1 transitions observed experimentally.

## Key numbers

- The U(13) algebra generated by bilinear combinations of proton and neutron boson operators contains 338 generators (2 x 13^2 = 338).
- The Majorana operator M_13 relates to the U(13) quadratic Casimir operator as C_2[U(13)] = N(N+12) - 2 M_13.
- The Majorana operator M_6 relates to the U(6) quadratic Casimir operator as C_2[U(6)] = n(n+5) - 2 M_6.
- The Majorana operator M_7 relates to the U(7) quadratic Casimir operator as C_2[U(7)] = m(m+6) - 2 M_7.
- The magnetic dipole operator includes g-factors for proton and neutron d-boson angular momenta (g_pi, g_nu) and for f-boson angular momenta (g'_pi, g'_nu), with the f-boson contributions set to zero (g'_rho = 0) in the calculations.
- The E1 transition operator contains a two-body part with strength parameter beta/(2N) coupling the quadrupole and octupole operators.

## Context

This work extends the standard sd-IBM-2 (Arima and Otsuka, 1977) and sdf-IBM-1 frameworks by incorporating both the octupole degree of freedom and the proton-neutron degree of freedom simultaneously, enabling the study of mixed-symmetry states involving octupole phonons. The paper builds on experimental observations of gamma transitions between the 3- octupole phonon state and the 2+_ms mixed-symmetry state in near-spherical nuclei (Van de Walle, 1995; Franchoo, private communication), and on the identification of mixed-symmetry two-phonon states such as the 1+ scissors mode and 3+ state in 94Mo (Moller, 1994; Pietralla, 1999). The analytical results enable future experimental searches for negative-parity mixed-symmetry states, particularly the (2+_ms ⊗ 3-) two-phonon quintuplet, and provide a testing ground for the quadrupole-octupole coupled origin of enhanced E1 transitions in vibrational nuclei.
