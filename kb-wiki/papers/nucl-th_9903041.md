# Relativistic Hartree approach including both positive- and negative-energy bound states

> We develop a relativistic model to describe the bound states of positive energy and negative energy in finite nuclei at the same time. Instead of searching for the negative-energy solution of the nucleon's Dirac equation, we solve the Dirac equations for the nucleon and the anti-nucleon simultaneously. The single-particle energies of negative-energy nucleons are obtained through changing the sign of the single-particle energies of positive-energy anti-nucleons. The contributions of the Dirac sea to the source terms of the meson fields are evaluated by means of the derivative expansion up to the leading derivative order for the one-meson loop and one-nucleon loop. After refitting the parameters of the model to the properties of spherical nuclei, the results of positive-energy sector are similar to that calculated within the commonly used relativistic mean field theory under the no-sea approximation. However, the bound levels of negative-energy nucleons vary drastically when the vacuum contributions are taken into account. It implies that the negative-energy spectra deserve a sensitive probe to the effective interactions in addition to the positive-energy spectra.

## Key claim

The authors develop a relativistic Hartree model that simultaneously describes bound states of both positive-energy nucleons and negative-energy nucleons (anti-nucleons) in finite nuclei, using a derivative expansion up to leading order for vacuum contributions. After refitting parameters to spherical nuclei, the positive-energy results match standard relativistic mean-field theory under the no-sea approximation, but the negative-energy bound levels change drastically when vacuum contributions are included, suggesting that negative-energy spectra could serve as a sensitive probe of effective interactions.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equations for nucleons and anti-nucleons simultaneously, obtaining negative-energy nucleon single-particle energies by changing the sign of positive-energy anti-nucleon energies. They evaluate Dirac sea contributions to the meson field source terms using a derivative expansion up to leading derivative order for both the one-meson loop and one-nucleon loop, then refit the model parameters to properties of spherical nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The scalar potential S is approximately -350 MeV and the vector potential V is approximately 300 MeV in nuclear matter from standard RMF calculations (Ser86), giving a nucleon central potential U_cen ~ -50 MeV and an anti-nucleon central potential U_cen ~ -650 MeV
- The critical condition for nucleon-anti-nucleon pair creation at zero momentum is V - S = 2M_N, where M_N is the free nucleon mass
- The leading derivative correction to the effective potential is of the same order of magnitude as the effective potential itself, while the next-order derivative correction is two or three orders of magnitude smaller

## Context

This work extends relativistic mean-field theory (RMF) beyond the no-sea approximation used in standard parameter sets such as NL1 (Rein 1986) and TM1 (Sugahara 1994). It builds on earlier investigations of vacuum corrections by Horowitz (1984), Perry (1986), Wasson (1988), Fox (1989), and Furnstahl (1989) using local density approximations and derivative expansions. The model enables future study of whether stable or meta-stable heavy anti-nuclei can exist, and provides a framework for calculating anti-nucleon bound states that could be tested against collective anti-matter production mechanisms proposed by Greiner (1995).
