# First Measurement of Differential Charged Current Quasielastic-like $ν_μ$-Argon Scattering Cross Sections with the MicroBooNE Detector

> We report on the first measurement of flux-integrated single differential cross sections for charged-current (CC) muon neutrino ($ν_μ$) scattering on argon with a muon and a proton in the final state, $^{40}$Ar($ν_μ$,$μ$p)X. The measurement was carried out using the Booster Neutrino Beam at Fermi National Accelerator Laboratory and the MicroBooNE liquid argon time projection chamber detector with an exposure of 4.59 $\times$ 10$^{19}$ protons on target. Events are selected to enhance the contribution of CC quasielastic (CCQE) interactions. The data are reported in terms of a total cross section as well as single differential cross sections in final state muon and proton kinematics. We measure the integrated per-nucleus CCQE-like cross section (i.e. for interactions leading to a muon, one proton and no pions above detection threshold) of (4.93 $\pm$ 0.76stat $\pm$ 1.29sys) $\times$ 10$^{-38}$cm$^2$, in good agreement with theoretical calculations. The single differential cross sections are also in overall good agreement with theoretical predictions, except at very forward muon scattering angles that correspond to low momentum-transfer events.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first measurement of flux-integrated single differential cross sections for charged-current muon neutrino scattering on argon with a muon and exactly one proton in the final state (CC1p0pi events). The overall agreement with theoretical predictions is good, except at very forward muon scattering angles corresponding to low momentum-transfer events, where discrepancies are observed.

## Method

The MicroBooNE liquid argon time projection chamber (LArTPC) detector, with an active mass of 85 tons, was exposed to the Booster Neutrino Beam at Fermilab for an integrated flux of 4.59 x 10^19 protons on target. Events were selected requiring a reconstructed muon-proton pair with a common vertex, coplanarity condition (|Delta phi - 180 degrees| < 35 degrees), small missing transverse momentum (p_T < 350 MeV/c), and minimal residual activity near the vertex, yielding 410 candidate events with an estimated purity of approximately 84%. Cross sections were extracted using a bin-by-bin efficiency correction method that accounts for bin migration effects, with the efficiency defined as the ratio of reconstructed to true generated CC1p0pi events in each kinematic bin.

## Key numbers

* Integrated per-nucleus CCQE-like cross section: (4.93 +/- 0.76_stat +/- 1.29_sys) x 10^-38 cm^2 for the full measured phase space (-0.65 < cos(theta_mu) < 0.95)
* Integrated cross section for the restricted phase space (-0.65 < cos(theta_mu) < 0.8): (4.05 +/- 1.40) x 10^-38 cm^2
* Event selection purity: approximately 84%, with 81% of measured events originating from an underlying CCQE interaction as defined by the GENIE event generator
* Detection efficiency for CC1p0pi CCQE-like events: approximately 20%
* Minimum proton momentum threshold: 300 MeV/c; minimum muon momentum threshold: 100 MeV/c
* Phase space limits: 0.1 < p_mu < 1.5 GeV/c, 0.3 < p_p < 1.0 GeV/c, -0.65 < cos(theta_mu) < 0.95, cos(theta_p) > 0.15

## Context

This measurement provides the first exclusive CCQE-like differential cross sections on argon, extending prior work on carbon and other nuclear targets by experiments such as MINERvA (Fiorentini 2013, Betancourt 2017) and T2K (Abe 2018). The results serve as a benchmark for theoretical models of neutrino-argon interactions that are critical for future LArTPC-based oscillation experiments, particularly the Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment (DUNE) and the Short Baseline Neutrino (SBN) program. The observed discrepancy at forward muon scattering angles indicates that current event generators (GENIE, NuWro, NEUT, GiBUU) may require improved modeling of low momentum-transfer interactions and nuclear effects such as meson exchange currents and final state interactions.
