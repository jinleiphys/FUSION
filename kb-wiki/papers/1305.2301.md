# Core-excitation three-cluster model description of ^8He and ^10He

> We introduce a new model applying to the core-nucleus and two-neutron system. The Faddeev equations of 6He-n-n and 8He-n-n systems for 8He and 10He are solved, respectively. The potential of the subsystem in the model has been determined to make a coupling both of the ground state and the excited one inside the core nucleus. By a similar mechanism the three-nucleon system is solved with the three-body force originating from an isobar excitation of the nucleon. Inputting only the information of subsystem energy levels and widths we get the coupling constants of rank 1 Yamaguchi potential between the core nucleus and neutron. We calculate the Faddeev three-cluster equations to obtain the low-lying energy levels of 8He and 10He. The 1- state of 10He, which has not been detected yet in experiments, is located in the energy level between the 0+ and 2+ states.

## Key claim

The authors introduce a three-cluster Faddeev model for the core-nucleus + two-neutron systems \(^8\)He and \(^{10}\)He that explicitly couples the ground state and the first excited state of the core nucleus (\(^6\)He or \(^8\)He), and they predict that the \(1^-\) state of \(^{10}\)He, not yet detected experimentally, lies between the \(0^+\) and \(2^+\) states. The model’s accuracy is qualified by the statement that the predicted decay widths are generally larger than experimental values and that the \(1^-\) state “may not be a cluster state” and could be a “simple spurious state.”

## Method

The authors solve the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) form of the Faddeev equations for the \(^6\)He-n-n and \(^8\)He-n-n three-body systems, using a rank-1 separable Yamaguchi potential for the core-neutron interaction. The potential is constructed to couple two Hilbert spaces representing the core in its ground state (\(0^+\)) and its first excited state (\(2^+\)); the coupling constants are determined solely from the experimental resonance energies and widths of the \(^7\)He and \(^9\)He subsystems. The resulting three-body integral equations are solved by an eigenvalue method with contour deformation in the complex momentum plane.

## Key numbers

*   \(^8\)He ground state (\(0^+\)): predicted energy \(-1.35\) MeV from the \(^6\)He + n + n threshold, compared to the experimental value \(-2.14\) MeV.
*   \(^8\)He first excited state (\(2^+\)): predicted resonance energy \(E^{(r)} = 2.01\) MeV and width \(\Gamma = 2.12\) MeV, compared to experimental \(E^{(r)} = 1.06 \pm 0.5\) MeV and \(\Gamma = 0.6 \pm 0.2\) MeV.
*   \(^{10}\)He ground state (\(0^+\)): predicted resonance energy \(E^{(r)} = 0.803\) MeV and width \(\Gamma = 0.665\) MeV, compared to experimental \(E^{(r)} = 1.069\) MeV and \(\Gamma = 0.3 \pm 0.2\) MeV.
*   \(^{10}\)He \(1^-\) state (predicted, not yet observed): predicted resonance energy \(E^{(r)} = 1.25\) MeV and width \(\Gamma = 0.21\) MeV.
*   \(^{10}\)He \(2^+\) state: predicted resonance energy \(E^{(r)} = 3.97\) MeV and width \(\Gamma = 4.71\) MeV, compared to experimental \(E^{(r)} = 4.31 \pm 0.20\) MeV and \(\Gamma = 0.6 \pm 0.3\) MeV.
*   Energy shift \(\delta m\) between ground and first excited state of the core: 1.8 MeV for \(^6\)He and 3.1 MeV for \(^8\)He.

## Context

This work extends the three-cluster Faddeev approach previously applied to \(^6\)He (Eskan 1992) and to the \(\alpha+\alpha+\Lambda\) system (Oryu 2000, Cravo 2002) by incorporating core excitation, an idea analogous to the delta-isobar excitation in the three-nucleon system (Pena 1990). The model is contrasted with ab initio calculations (Kamada 2001, Pastore 2013) and with other effective approaches such as the cluster orbital shell model (COSM) and complex scaling method (CSM) used by Aoyama (1997, 2002) for \(^9\)He and \(^{10}\)He. The results enable predictions for the unobserved \(1^-\) state of \(^{10}\)He and suggest that further experimental measurement is needed to clarify whether this state is a genuine cluster state.
