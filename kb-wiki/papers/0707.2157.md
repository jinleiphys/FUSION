# $pp \to ppÏ€^0$ near threshold in pionless effective theory

> The total cross section of the $pp\to ppÏ€^0$ reaction near threshold is calculated in pionless effective field theory with a di-baryon and external pions. The amplitudes for a leading one-body and subleading contact neutral pion production vertex are obtained including the initial and final state interactions. After estimating a low-energy constant in the contact vertex, we compare our results for the total cross section with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The total cross section of $pp\to pp\pi^0$ near threshold can be reproduced in pionless effective field theory with a di-baryon and external pions, using only one unknown low-energy constant in the contact vertex, provided that the sign of that constant is chosen appropriately; the calculation includes both initial and final state interactions but does not resolve the poor convergence of the chiral series seen in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory.

## Method

The author constructed a pionless effective Lagrangian with a di-baryon field for the $^1S_0$ channel and a contact interaction for the $^3P_0$ channel, coupled to external pions. Low-energy constants for the $NN$ interactions were fixed using the $pp$ scattering length $a_C = -7.8063$ fm and effective range $r_0 = 2.794$ fm for the $^1S_0$ channel, and the $^3P_0$ phase shift $\delta_p(p_{th}) \simeq -7.5^\circ$ at threshold momentum $p_{th} \simeq \sqrt{m_\pi m_N}$. The one-body and contact pion production amplitudes were computed including strong initial state interaction and strong plus Coulomb final state interactions, with the contact LEC $\tilde{d}_\pi^{(2)}$ estimated from a one-pion-exchange diagram in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

* $a_C = -7.8063 \pm 0.0026$ fm: scattering length for $^1S_0$ $pp$ channel, used to fix the di-baryon LECs.
* $r_0 = 2.794 \pm 0.014$ fm: effective range for $^1S_0$ $pp$ channel.
* $\delta_p(p_{th}) \simeq -7.5^\circ$: $^3P_0$ phase shift at threshold momentum $p_{th} \simeq \sqrt{m_\pi m_N}$, used to fix the $P$-wave LEC $C_2^0$.
* $\tilde{d}_\pi^{(2)} \simeq \pm 0.140$ fm$^{5/2}$: estimated value of the contact LEC from a one-pion-exchange diagram in HB$\chi$PT.
* $\tilde{d}_\pi^{(2)\text{fitted}} = -0.12$ fm$^{5/2}$ and $+0.55$ fm$^{5/2}$: two values obtained by fitting to experimental cross section data.
* The one-body amplitude alone contributes approximately half of the experimental cross section in the pionless theory, compared to about 1/5 in previous distorted-wave Born approximation calculations.

## Context

This work extends the application of pionless effective field theory with a di-baryon, previously used for $np\to d\gamma$ and neutron-neutron fusion, to the $pp\to pp\pi^0$ reaction near threshold. It contrasts with heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations at next-to-next-to-leading order (e.g., by Hanhart et al. (2001) and Kolybasov et al. (2007)), which suffer from poor convergence of the chiral series and comparable contributions from formally different orders. The pionless approach provides a minimal formalism that captures the known energy dependence from final-state interactions and phase space (Meyer et al. (1992)), and enables systematic inclusion of short-range effects through a single fitted contact term, though it does not clarify the convergence issues in the chiral approach.
