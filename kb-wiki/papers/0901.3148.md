# Heavy-Light Fermion Mixtures at Unitarity

> We investigate fermion pairing in the unitary regime for a mass ratio corresponding to a 6Li-40K mixture using Quantum Monte Carlo methods. The ground-state energy and the average light and heavy particle excitation spectrum for the unpolarized superfluid state are nearly independent of the mass ratio. In the majority light system, the polarized superfluid is close to the energy of a phase separated mixture of nearly fully polarized normal and unpolarized superfluid. For a majority of heavy particles, we find an energy minimum for a normal state with a ratio of ~ 3:1 heavy to light particles. A slight increase in attraction to kF*a ~ 2.5 yields a ground state energy of nearly zero for this ratio. A cold unpolarized system in a harmonic trap at unitarity should phase separate into three regions, with a shell of unpolarized superfluid in the middle.

## Key claim

The ground-state energy and average quasi-particle excitation spectrum of the unpolarized superfluid state are nearly independent of the mass ratio, but the finite-polarization phase diagram differs significantly from the equal-mass case, with a normal-state energy minimum near a 3:1 heavy-to-light ratio that evolves rapidly with mass ratio and interaction strength.

## Method

Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) calculations were performed for a homogeneous heavy-light fermion mixture at unitarity with mass ratio r = 6.5 (corresponding to a 6Li-40K mixture), using a modified Poschl-Teller potential with effective range r0/12. The superfluid and normal phase trial wave functions provided fixed-node upper bounds to the energy, with the superfluid wave function variationally re-optimized. Calculations used from 60 to 90 particles in periodic boundary conditions, examining both unpolarized and polarized systems.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of the unpolarized superfluid: ξ(r=6.5) = 0.390(5), compared to ξ(r=1) = 0.41(1) for equal masses
- Average pairing gap at r=6.5: η(r=6.5) = 0.38(4), compared to η(r=1) = 0.50(5) for equal masses
- Binding energy of a single heavy particle in a sea of light particles: B_h = 0.99(5) E_F^{m_r}
- Binding energy of a single light particle in a sea of heavy particles: B_l = 0.97(5) E_F^{m_r}
- Effective masses: m_l*/m_l = 1.3 and m_h*/m_h = 1.0
- Critical concentration for majority light species: x'_c = 0.02(2)
- Critical concentration for majority heavy species: x_c = 0.49(5)
- Energy reaches nearly zero at k_F a ≈ 2.5 for the 3:1 heavy-to-light ratio

## Context

This work extends previous QMC studies of equal-mass unitary fermions (Carlson 2003, 2005; Astrakharchik 2004) to the unequal-mass case, specifically addressing the experimentally accessible 6Li-40K mixture. The results enable predictions for trapped-system phase separation into three distinct regions at zero total polarization, and provide benchmarks for mean-field BCS theory predictions of unequal-mass pairing. The findings also connect to studies of Efimov states (Braaten 2006; Nishida 2008a) and suggest that collapse may occur at mass ratios smaller than the 3-body prediction of 13.6.
