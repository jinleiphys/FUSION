# Light clusters in warm stellar matter: calibrating the cluster couplings

> The abundances of light clusters within a formalism that considers in-medium effects are calculated using several relativistic mean-field models, with both density-dependent and density-independent couplings. Clusters are introduced as new quasiparticles, with a modified coupling to the scalar meson field. A comparison with experimental data from heavy ion collisions allows settling the model dependence of the results and the determination of the couplings of the light clusters to the meson fields. We find that extra experimental constraints at higher density are needed to convincingly pin down the density associated to the melting of clusters in the dense nuclear medium. The role of neutron rich clusters, such as $^6$He, in asymmetric matter is discussed.

# Light clusters in warm stellar matter: calibrating the cluster couplings

## Key claim

The scalar cluster-meson coupling fraction \(x_s\) that best reproduces experimental equilibrium constants from heavy ion collisions is model-dependent, with values of \(x_s = 0.91 \pm 0.02\) for FSU2R, \(x_s = 0.93 \pm 0.02\) for DDME2, and \(x_s = 0.83 \pm 0.03\) for SFHo, rather than a single universal value. Extra experimental constraints at higher density are needed to convincingly pin down the density associated to the melting of clusters in the dense nuclear medium.

## Method

The authors applied a generalized relativistic mean-field (gRMF) formalism to low-density clusterized matter, treating light clusters (\(^2\)H, \(^3\)H, \(^3\)He, \(^4\)He, and \(^6\)He) as quasiparticles with a modified coupling to the scalar \(\sigma\)-meson field. They compared six RMF models (FSU2R, NL3\(\omega\rho\), TM1\(\omega\rho\), SFHo, DD2, and DDME2) with both density-dependent and density-independent couplings, and fitted the scalar cluster-meson coupling fraction \(x_s\) for each model to reproduce the equilibrium constants measured by the INDRA collaboration.

## Key numbers

- For FSU2R, the best-fit scalar cluster-meson coupling fraction is \(x_s = 0.91 \pm 0.02\).
- For DDME2, the best-fit scalar cluster-meson coupling fraction is \(x_s = 0.93 \pm 0.02\).
- For SFHo, the best-fit scalar cluster-meson coupling fraction is \(x_s = 0.83 \pm 0.03\).
- For FSU2R and DDME2 with \(x_s = 0.85\), the dissolution density range for all clusters is approximately \(4 - 6 \times 10^{-2}\) fm\(^{-3}\) at \(T = 5\) MeV and \(y_p = 0.2\).
- SFHo predicts dissolution densities approximately 30% larger than FSU2R and DDME2 when using the best-fit \(x_s\) values.
- The saturation density \(n_0\) for the models ranges from 0.145 fm\(^{-3}\) (TM1\(\omega\rho\)) to 0.158 fm\(^{-3}\) (SFHo).
- The symmetry energy at saturation \(E_{\rm sym}\) ranges from 30.7 MeV (FSU2R) to 32.7 MeV (DD2).

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Pais et al. (2018, 2019) that introduced the gRMF formalism within the FSU model, which was too soft to describe neutron stars with two solar masses. The present paper generalizes those studies to other RMF models currently used in astrophysical simulations, including TM1, NL3, SFHo, FSU2R, DD2, and DDME2. The comparison with INDRA experimental data (2020) and earlier NIMROD data (Qin et al., 2012) allows calibration of the cluster-meson couplings, enabling more reliable predictions of light cluster abundances in core-collapse supernovae, proto-neutron stars, and neutron star mergers. The results also inform the ongoing discussion about the role of neutron-rich clusters such as \(^6\)He in asymmetric matter, which was previously raised by Yudin et al. (2019) regarding exotic nuclei in stellar matter.
