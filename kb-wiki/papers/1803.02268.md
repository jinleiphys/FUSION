# Potential Energy Between Static Quarks Is Time Dependent in The Classical Yang-Mills Theory

> Lattice QCD predicts that the potential energy between static quarks is independent of time. However, in this paper we show that the gauge invariant color singlet potential energy between static quarks in the classical Yang-Mills theory depends on time even if the quarks are at rest. This is a consequence of the time dependent fundamental color charge $q^a(t)$ of the quark in the classical Yang-Mills theory. We find that the gauge invariant color singlet time dependent potential energy between static quarks does not violate the conservation of energy in the Yang-Mills theory.

## Key claim

The gauge invariant color singlet potential energy between static quarks in the classical Yang-Mills theory depends on time even when the quarks are at rest, contrary to the time-independent prediction from lattice QCD; this time dependence does not violate conservation of energy because the rate of work done on static quarks by the chromo-electric field is non-zero, unlike in Maxwell theory.

## Method

The author derives the chromo-electric field produced by a static quark from the time-dependent color potential \(A_0^c(t,r)\) obtained from the general solution for the color potential of a quark with time-dependent fundamental color charge \(q^a(t)\) in the classical Yang-Mills theory. The gauge invariant color singlet potential energy between two static quarks is then computed from the chromo-electric field energy \(V(t) = \frac{1}{8\pi} \int d^3r \, \vec{E}^c(t,r) \cdot \vec{E}^c(t,r)\), and energy conservation is verified using the Yang-Mills equation and the gauge invariant Noether's theorem (Poynting theorem) for the Yang-Mills theory.

## Key numbers

* The chromo-electric field for a static quark with constant color charge reduces to the Coulomb-like form \(\vec{E}^c(r) = \hat{r} \, q^c / r^2\) (equation fncs), showing that constant color charges yield an abelian-like theory.
* For constant color charges, the potential energy between two static color sources separated by distance \(r\) becomes the Coulomb potential \(V(r) = g^2 / r\) (equation pte).
* The boundary surface term \(\frac{1}{2} \int dS \, \vec{n} \cdot [A_0^c(t,r) \vec{\nabla} A_0^c(t,r)]\) does not vanish at infinite distance because the chromo-electric field energy density does not fall off faster than \(1/r^3\), implying the color potential does not fall off faster than \(1/r^{1/2}\) (section IV).

## Context

This work extends the author's prior derivations of the time-dependent fundamental color charge \(q^a(t)\) of a quark in the classical Yang-Mills theory (Nayak, 2004-2007) and the general form of the color potential produced by such a charge (Nayak and collaborators). It contradicts the lattice QCD prediction that the color singlet potential energy between static quarks is time-independent (Lattice QCD collaborations, various years). The result enables further study of confinement and hadron formation using the exact time-dependent color potential, which lattice QCD cannot provide, and opens the question of how the time dependence of the potential energy relates to the non-abelian nature of the SU(3) gauge group.
