# Neutral-current neutrino-nucleus cross-sections for A~50-65 nuclei

> We study neutral current neutrino-nucleus reaction cross-sections for Mn, Fe, Co and Ni isotopes. An earlier study for a few selected nuclei has shown that in the supernova environment the cross sections are increased for low energy neutrinos due to finite-temperature effects. Our work supports this finding for a much larger set of nuclei. Furthermore we extend previous work to higher neutrino energies considering allowed and forbidden multipole contributions to the cross sections. The allowed contributions are derived from large-scale shell model calculations of the Gamow-Teller strength, while the other multipole contributions are calculated within the Random Phase Approximation. We present the cross sections as functions of initial and final neutrino energies and for a range of supernova-relevant temperatures. These cross sections will allow improved estimates of inelastic neutrino reactions on nuclei in supernova simulations.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that finite-temperature effects significantly increase neutral-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections at low neutrino energies (below about 10-15 MeV) for a much larger set of nuclei than previously studied, but notes that this effect is noticeably milder for odd-A and odd-odd nuclei compared to even-even nuclei, with exceptions for nuclei like 53Mn and 55Co that have closed neutron shells.

## Method

The authors used a hybrid model combining large-scale shell model calculations (using the Antoine code with a modified KB3 residual interaction) for the Gamow-Teller (GT) contributions to the cross section, and Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations for all higher multipole contributions. For finite-temperature effects, they applied Brink's hypothesis for down-scattering contributions and explicitly calculated GT transitions between thermally populated low-lying states for up-scattering contributions, using experimental excitation energies where available.

## Key numbers

- Thermal effects can increase low-energy cross sections for even-even nuclei (and closed neutron shell nuclei 53Mn and 55Co) by up to two orders of magnitude as temperature rises from 0.86 MeV to 1.72 MeV
- For neutrinos with energy E_nu = 20 MeV, up to 18% of the cross section comes from forbidden transitions (24% in 60Mn)
- At E_nu = 30-50 MeV, allowed and forbidden transitions contribute about equally to the cross section
- At E_nu = 100 MeV, forbidden multipoles dominate, with GT_0 contributing about 15-20%
- The GT_0 centroid for Delta I=0 transitions lies at 8-12 MeV excitation energy, while Delta I=1 transitions lie at 10-15 MeV
- Temperatures studied: T = 0.86 MeV (10^10 K), 1.29 MeV (1.5x10^10 K), and 1.72 MeV (2x10^10 K)
- Cross sections calculated for neutrino energies from 0 to 100 MeV and temperatures from 0.4 to 2.0 MeV
- Study covers 40 isotopes: 50-60Mn, 52-61Fe, 54-63Co, and 56-64Ni

## Context

This work extends the earlier finite-temperature study by Sampaio et al. (2002), which examined only a few selected nuclei (56,59Fe and 56,59Co), to a much larger set of 40 isotopes relevant for supernova simulations. It also extends previous work by Toivanen et al. (2001) on iron isotopes at zero temperature to finite temperatures and higher neutrino energies including forbidden multipole contributions. The results enable improved estimates of inelastic neutrino reactions on nuclei in supernova simulations, which have been previously ignored in such simulations despite the pioneering work by Bruenn and Haxton showing their potential importance for neutrino equilibration during infall.
