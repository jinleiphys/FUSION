# $\bar{K}NN$ quasi-bound state and the $\bar{K}N$ interaction: coupled-channel Faddeev calculations of the $\bar{K}NN - πΣN$ system

> Coupled-channel three-body calculations of an $I=1/2$, $J^π=0^-$ $\bar{K}NN$ quasi-bound state in the $\bar{K}NN - πΣN$ system were performed and the dependence of the resulting three-body energy on the two-body $\bar{K}N - πΣ$ interaction was investigated. Earlier results of binding energy $B_{K^-pp} \sim 50 -70$ MeV and width $Γ_{K^-pp} \sim 100$ MeV are confirmed [N.V. Shevchenko {\it et al.}, Phys. Rev. Lett. {\bf 98}, 082301 (2007)]. It is shown that a suitably constructed energy-independent complex $\bar{K}N$ potential gives a considerably shallower and narrower three-body quasi-bound state than the full coupled-channel calculation. Comparison with other calculations is made.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that a single $I=1/2$, $J^\pi=0^-$ $K^-pp$ quasi-bound state exists with binding energy $B \sim 50-70$ MeV and width $\Gamma \sim 100$ MeV, but the exact pole position depends strongly on the two-body $\bar{K}N - \pi \Sigma$ input parameters, particularly the $K^-p$ scattering length and the mass and width of the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-channel Faddeev calculations in the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) form for the $\bar{K}NN - \pi \Sigma N$ system, extending the formalism to include additional particle channels beyond the usual Faddeev partitions. They constructed separable two-body potentials for the $\bar{K}N - \pi \Sigma$ coupled channel, $NN$, $\Sigma N$, and $\pi N$ interactions, fitted to experimental data including the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance properties, $K^-p$ scattering length, and threshold branching ratios, then solved the homogeneous AGS equations to find the three-body pole energy.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy $B_{K^-pp} \sim 50-70$ MeV and width $\Gamma_{K^-pp} \sim 100$ MeV for the $I=1/2$, $J^\pi=0^-$ quasi-bound state, confirmed from earlier work Shevchenko et al. (2007)
- With the "best set" of $\bar{K}N - \pi \Sigma$ parameters ($\beta=3.5$ fm$^{-1}$, $a_{K^-p}^{\rm KEK} = -0.78 + i\,0.49$ fm, $E_\Lambda^{\rm PDG} = 1406.5 - i\,25$ MeV), the three-body pole energy is approximately $-55 - i\,51$ MeV, varying by less than 1 MeV in binding energy across three different $\Sigma N$ interaction sets
- Using a one-channel real $\bar{K}N$ potential fitted to the real parts of $a_{K^-p}^{\rm KEK}$ and $E_\Lambda^{\rm PDG}$ with $\beta=3.5$ fm$^{-1}$ gives a real bound state at $-43.8$ MeV below the $K^-pp$ threshold, considerably shallower than the coupled-channel result
- Varying the $\Lambda(1405)$ mass from 1400 to 1420 MeV and half-width from 20 to 30 MeV changes the three-body pole from $-62.1 - i\,46.9$ MeV to $-42.8 - i\,50.8$ MeV, showing strong sensitivity to the resonance parameters

## Context

This work extends the authors' preliminary report Shevchenko et al. (2007) by providing a detailed analysis of the dependence of the three-body results on two-body input parameters. It contradicts the earlier single-channel calculation by Yamazaki and Akaishi (2002) which predicted a deeper and narrower state (binding energy 48 MeV, width 61 MeV) using an effective $\bar{K}$ optical potential, and also differs from the FINUDA experimental claim of a $K^-pp$ state with binding energy 115 MeV and width 67 MeV. The results enable future comparisons with other coupled-channel approaches and provide a benchmark for understanding the role of the $\bar{K}N - \pi \Sigma$ coupling in kaonic nuclei, with implications for studies of kaon condensation in dense nuclear matter.
