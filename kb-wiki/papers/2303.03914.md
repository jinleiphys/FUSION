# Jet polarisation in an anisotropic medium

> We study the evolution of an energetic jet which propagates in an anisotropic quark-gluon plasma, as created in the intermediate stages of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. We argue that the partons of the jet should acquire a non-zero average polarisation proportional to the medium anisotropy. We first observe that the medium anisotropy introduces a difference between the rates for transverse momentum broadening along the two directions perpendicular to the jet axis. In turn, this difference leads to a polarisation-dependent bias in the BDMPS-Z rates for medium-induced gluon branching. Accordingly, the daughter gluons in a branching process can carry net polarisation even if their parent gluon was unpolarised. Using these splitting rates, we construct kinetic equations which describe the production and transmission of polarisation via multiple branching in an anisotropic medium. The solutions to these equations show that polarisation is efficiently produced via quasi-democratic branchings, but then it is rapidly washed out by the subsequent branchings, due to the inability of soft gluons to keep trace of the polarisation of their parents. Based on that, we conclude that a net polarisation for the jet should survive in the final state if and only if the medium anisotropy is sizeable as the jet escapes the medium.

## Key claim

The authors argue that jet partons propagating through an anisotropic quark-gluon plasma should acquire a non-zero average polarisation proportional to the medium anisotropy, but they conclude that net polarisation survives in the final state only if the medium anisotropy remains sizeable as the jet escapes the medium, because polarisation produced via quasi-democratic branchings is rapidly washed out by subsequent branchings.

## Method

The authors extend the BDMPS-Z formalism for medium-induced gluon branching to an anisotropic medium and to gluons with definite polarisation states, deriving polarisation-dependent splitting rates that incorporate the difference between the two transverse jet quenching parameters \(\widehat{q}_y\) and \(\widehat{q}_z\). They then construct coupled kinetic equations for the unpolarised and polarised gluon distributions and solve them using both numerical methods and analytic Green's function techniques, studying the evolution of jet energy and polarisation via multiple branchings for the case where the leading gluon is initially unpolarised.

## Key numbers

- The transverse momentum broadening rates satisfy \(\langle p_y^2\rangle = \widehat{q}_y \Delta t\) and \(\langle p_z^2\rangle = \widehat{q}_z \Delta t\), with \(\widehat{q}_y \neq \widehat{q}_z\) in an anisotropic medium.
- The Debye mass in thermal equilibrium is given by \(m_D^2 = (2N_c + N_f) g^2 T^2 / 6\).
- The thermal equilibrium collision kernel is \(\tilde\gamma(k_\perp) = (1/\sqrt{2}) \, T m_D^2 / [k_\perp^2 (k_\perp^2 + m_D^2)]\).
- The polarised distribution for sufficiently low energies is proportional to the unpolarised distribution, with a proportionality coefficient that would vanish for an isotropic medium.

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS-Z formalism (Baier 1996, Zakharov 1996) for medium-induced gluon radiation, originally developed for isotropic plasmas and unpolarised partons, to anisotropic media and polarised gluons. It builds on prior studies of momentum broadening in anisotropic plasmas (Ipp 2020, Carrington 2021) and on the bottom-up thermalisation scenario (Baier 2000) where anisotropy persists due to longitudinal expansion. The results enable future measurements of jet polarisation to be correlated with jet energy loss, potentially providing a probe of medium anisotropy at different times as jets escape the medium.
