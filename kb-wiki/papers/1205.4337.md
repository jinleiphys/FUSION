# Inclusive neutrino scattering off deuteron from threshold to GeV energies

> Background: Neutrino-nucleus quasi-elastic scattering is crucial to interpret the neutrino oscillation results in long baseline neutrino experiments. There are rather large uncertainties in the cross section, due to insufficient knowledge on the role of two-body weak currents. Purpose: Determine the role of two-body weak currents in neutrino-deuteron quasi-elastic scattering up to GeV energies. Methods: Calculate cross sections for inclusive neutrino scattering off deuteron induced by neutral and charge-changing weak currents, from threshold up to GeV energies, using the Argonne $v_{18}$ potential and consistent nuclear electroweak currents with one- and two-body terms. Results: Two-body contributions are found to be small, and increase the cross sections obtained with one-body currents by less than 10% over the whole range of energies. Total cross sections obtained by describing the final two-nucleon states with plane waves differ negligibly, for neutrino energies $\gtrsim 500$ MeV, from those in which interaction effects in these states are fully accounted for. The sensitivity of the calculated cross sections to different models for the two-nucleon potential and/or two-body terms in the weak current is found to be weak. Comparing cross sections to those obtained in a naive model in which the deuteron is taken to consist of a free proton and neutron at rest, nuclear structure effects are illustrated to be non-negligible. Conclusion: Contributions of two-body currents in neutrino-deuteron quasi-elastic scattering up to GeV are found to be smaller than 10%. Finally, it should be stressed that the results reported in this work do not include pion production channels.

## Key claim
Two-body weak currents increase the inclusive neutrino-deuteron quasi-elastic cross sections by less than 10% across the entire energy range from threshold to 1 GeV, but the results do not include pion production channels.

## Method
The authors calculated inclusive neutral-current and charge-changing neutrino scattering cross sections on the deuteron using the Argonne v18 two-nucleon potential with consistent one- and two-body nuclear electroweak currents. Matrix elements of the weak current between the deuteron and two-nucleon scattering states were evaluated by direct numerical integration over the relative momentum of the final nucleons, avoiding multipole expansions. The two-body axial current was constrained by fitting the Gamow-Teller matrix element of tritium beta-decay, and two different sets of cutoff masses for the meson-exchange currents were tested.

## Key numbers
- Two-body current contributions increase cross sections by less than 10% over the whole energy range from threshold to 1 GeV.
- For neutrino energies greater than or equal to 500 MeV, total cross sections computed with plane-wave final states differ negligibly from those with full final-state interactions.
- The axial form factor cutoff mass used is Lambda_A = 1 GeV, with the nucleon axial-vector coupling constant g_A = 1.2694.
- The Gamow-Teller matrix element for tritium beta-decay is 0.955 plus or minus 0.002, used to calibrate the N-to-Delta axial coupling constant g_A*.
- The Fermi constant used is G_F = 1.1803 times 10 to the minus 5 GeV to the minus 2, and cos(theta_C) = 0.97425 for charge-changing processes.

## Context
This work extends the calculations of Nakamura et al. (2001, 2002) for neutrino-deuteron scattering from intermediate energies (below about 150 MeV) up to 1 GeV, using the same theoretical framework but with refined current models and a different computational approach. The results provide a benchmark for future studies of electroweak inclusive response in light nuclei and support plans by Garvey et al. (2012) to determine neutrino fluxes in accelerator-based experiments from deuteron cross-section measurements. The paper does not include pion production channels, which become relevant at the higher end of the energy range studied.
