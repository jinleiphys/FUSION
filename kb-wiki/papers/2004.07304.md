# $^{12}$C(e,e'pN) Measurements of Short Range Correlations in the Tensor-to-Scalar Interaction Transition Region

> High-momentum configurations of nucleon pairs at short-distance are probed using measurements of the $^{12}$C$(e,e'p)$ and $^{12}$C$(e,e'pN)$ reactions (where $N$ is either $n$ or $p$), at high-$Q^2$ and $x_B>1.1$. The data span a missing-momentum range of 300--1000 MeV/c and are predominantly sensitive to the transition region of the strong nuclear interaction from a Tensor to Scalar interaction. The data are well reproduced by theoretical calculations using the Generalized Contact Formalism with both chiral and phenomenological nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interaction models. This agreement suggests that the measured high missing-momentum protons up to $1000$ MeV/c predominantly belong to short-ranged correlated (SRC) pairs. The measured $^{12}$C$(e,e'pN)$ / $^{12}$C$(e,e'p)$ and $^{12}$C$(e,e'pp)$ / $^{12}$C$(e,e'pn)$ cross-section ratios are consistent with a decrease in the fraction of proton-neutron SRC pairs and increase in the fraction of proton-proton SRC pairs with increasing missing momentum. This confirms the transition from an isospin-dependent tensor $NN$ interaction at $\sim 400$ MeV/c to an isospin-independent scalar interaction at high-momentum around $\sim 800$ MeV/c as predicted by theoretical calculation.

## Key claim

The data confirm a transition from an isospin-dependent tensor nucleon-nucleon interaction at approximately 400 MeV/c to an isospin-independent scalar interaction at high momentum around 800 MeV/c, as predicted by theoretical calculations, but the agreement with the Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF) calculations using the N2LO interaction model is poorer than with the AV18 model in the 300-400 MeV/c missing-momentum region, which is most sensitive to details of the dip in the pp wave function.

## Method

The CLAS Collaboration measured the 12C(e,e'p) and 12C(e,e'pN) reactions (N = n or p) at Jefferson Lab using 5.01 GeV electrons, selecting events with Q^2 > 1.5 GeV^2/c^2 and x_B > 1.1 over a missing-momentum range of 300-1000 MeV/c. The measured cross-section ratios were corrected for detector acceptance, efficiency, resolution, nuclear transparency, and single-charge exchange (SCX) effects, then compared to GCF calculations using both the AV18 and N2LO nucleon-nucleon interaction models.

## Key numbers

- Missing-momentum range probed: 300-1000 MeV/c
- Q^2 > 1.5 GeV^2/c^2 and x_B > 1.1 for event selection
- Neutron detection efficiency: 4-5% for momenta above 400 MeV/c, 2-3% between 200 and 400 MeV/c
- Recoil nucleon momentum selection: 350-1000 MeV/c
- The 12C(e,e'pp)/12C(e,e'p) and 12C(e,e'pn)/12C(e,e'p) data include a 5% uncertainty from transparency corrections
- At the 95% confidence level, the data exclude contributions from sources other than 2N-SRCs above 11%
- The mean of the [12C(e,e'pp) + 12C(e,e'pn)]/12C(e,e'p) cross-section ratio exceeds 100%, consistent with a correlated normalization uncertainty of approximately 12%

## Context

This work extends previous SRC measurements in 4He by Korover et al. (2014) that had large uncertainties and in 12C by Schmidt et al. (2020) that only measured the (e,e'pp)/(e,e'p) ratio and was subject to model-dependent SCX corrections of up to 400%. By simultaneously measuring both (e,e'pn) and (e,e'pp) channels, the present study provides the first direct experimental test of SCX corrections and establishes 2N-SRC dominance for high-momentum protons up to 1000 MeV/c. The results enable future studies of the tensor-to-scalar transition in other nuclei and provide constraints for many-body nuclear theory calculations.
