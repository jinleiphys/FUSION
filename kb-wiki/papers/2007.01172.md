# Study of a possibility of observation of hidden-bottom pentaquark resonances in bottomonium photoproduction on protons and nuclei near threshold

> We study the $Υ(1S)$ meson photoproduction on protons and nuclei at the near-threshold center-of-mass energies below 11.4 GeV (or at the corresponding photon laboratory energies $E_γ$ below 68.8 GeV). We calculate the absolute excitation functions for the non-resonant and resonant photoproduction of $Υ(1S)$ mesons off protons at incident photon laboratory energies of 63--68 GeV by accounting for direct ($γp \to {Υ(1S)}p$) and two-step ($γp \to P^+_b(11080,11125,11130) \to {Υ(1S)}p$) $Υ(1S)$ production channels within different scenarios for the non-resonant total cross section of elementary reaction $γp \to {Υ(1S)}p$ and for branching ratios of the decays $P^+_b(11080,11125,11130) \to {Υ(1S)}p$. We also calculate an analogous functions for photoproduction of $Υ(1S)$ mesons on $^{12}$C and $^{208}$Pb target nuclei in the near-threshold center-of-mass beam energy region of 9.0--11.4 GeV by considering respective incoherent direct ($γN \to {Υ(1S)}N$) and two-step ($γp \to P^+_b(11080,11125,11130) \to {Υ(1S)}p$, $γn \to P^0_b(11080,11125,11130) \to {Υ(1S)}n$) $Υ(1S)$ production processes within a nuclear spectral function approach. We show that a detailed scan of the $Υ(1S)$ total photoproduction cross section on a proton and nuclear targets in the near-threshold energy region in future high-precision experiments at the proposed high-luminosity electron-ion colliders EIC and EicC in the U.S. and China should give a definite result for or against the existence of the non-strange hidden-bottom pentaquark states $P_{bi}^+$ and $P_{bi}^0$ ($i=$1, 2, 3) as well as clarify their decay rates.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that a detailed scan of the total photoproduction cross section of Υ(1S) mesons on protons and nuclear targets in the near-threshold energy region (center-of-mass energies below 11.4 GeV) at future high-luminosity electron-ion colliders EIC and EicC could provide a definite result for or against the existence of the predicted non-strange hidden-bottom pentaquark states P⁺_b(11080,11125,11130) and P⁰_b(11080,11125,11130), provided that the non-resonant background cross section is sufficiently well constrained by the chosen parametrization.

## Method
The author calculates absolute excitation functions for non-resonant and resonant Υ(1S) photoproduction off protons at incident photon laboratory energies of 63–68 GeV, considering direct (γp → Υ(1S)p) and two-step (γp → P⁺_b → Υ(1S)p) production channels. For nuclear targets (¹²C and ²⁰⁸Pb), the calculation uses a nuclear spectral function approach to account for Fermi motion and binding effects, considering both direct (γN → Υ(1S)N) and two-step (γp → P⁺_b → Υ(1S)p, γn → P⁰_b → Υ(1S)n) processes. The non-resonant cross section is estimated using two different parametrizations based on scaling from J/ψ photoproduction data and a phenomenological fit, while the resonant contribution is modeled with Breit-Wigner spectral functions for the pentaquark states with assumed total widths of 9.8, 20.6, and 6.4 MeV and branching ratios of 1–10% for decays to Υ(1S)p and Υ(1S)n.

## Key numbers
- Threshold center-of-mass energy for Υ(1S) photoproduction on a free nucleon: √s_th = 10.4 GeV (m_Υ(1S) + m_N).
- Predicted pentaquark masses: M_b1 = 11080 MeV, M_b2 = 11125 MeV, M_b3 = 11130 MeV.
- Corresponding resonant photon laboratory energies for production on free protons: E_γ^R1 = 64.952 GeV, E_γ^R2 = 65.484 GeV, E_γ^R3 = 65.544 GeV.
- Assumed total decay widths for the pentaquark states: Γ_b1 = 9.8 MeV, Γ_b2 = 20.6 MeV, Γ_b3 = 6.4 MeV (taken from the hidden-charm partners P_c⁺(4312), P_c⁺(4440), P_c⁺(4457)).
- Ratio of Υ(1S) to J/ψ photoproduction cross sections at high energies: σ(γp → Υ(1S)p)/σ(γp → J/ψp) ≈ 5 × 10⁻³.
- Υ(1S)–nucleon absorption cross section assumed: σ_Υ(1S)N = 3.5 mb.
- P_b–nucleon inelastic cross section assumed: σ_P_bN = 33.5 mb.
- P_b–nucleon effective absorption cross section assumed: σ^eff_P_bN = 37 mb.
- Calculated branching ratios for P_b⁺ → γp decays: Br[P⁺_b1 → γp] = 1.73 × 10⁻⁴ × Br[P⁺_b1 → Υ(1S)p], Br[P⁺_b2 → γp] = 1.67 × 10⁻⁴ × Br[P⁺_b2 → Υ(1S)p], Br[P⁺_b3 → γp] = 1.67 × 10⁻⁴ × Br[P⁺_b3 → Υ(1S)p].

## Context
This work extends the study of hidden-charm pentaquark resonances in J/ψ photoproduction (Paryev, 2019) to the bottom sector, using the predicted hidden-bottom pentaquark states from the hadronic molecular approach of Ref. [20] (2020). The hidden-bottom pentaquarks P⁺_b(11080,11125,11130) are the bottom-sector analogues of the LHCb-discovered hidden-charm pentaquarks P_c⁺(4312,4440,4457) (LHCb Collaboration, 2019). The calculations provide predictions for future experiments at the proposed EIC and EicC colliders, where scanning the Υ(1S) photoproduction cross section near threshold could confirm or rule out the existence of these predicted exotic states and determine their decay rates.
