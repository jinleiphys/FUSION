# Dynamics of \bar{K} and multi-\bar{K} nuclei

> We report on self-consistent calculations of single-K^- nuclear states and multi-Kbar nuclear states in 12C, 16O, 40Ca and 208Pb within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach. Gradient terms motivated by the p-wave resonance Sigma(1385) are found to play a secondary role for single-K^- nuclear systems where the mean-field concept is acceptable. Significant contributions from the Kbar N -> pi Lambda conversion mode, and from the nonmesonic Kbar NN -> YN conversion modes which are assumed to follow a rho^2 density dependence, are evaluated for the deep binding-energy range of over 100 MeV where the decay channel Kbar N -> pi Sigma is closed. Altogether we obtain K^- total decay widths of 50-100 MeV for binding energies exceeding 100 MeV in single-K^- nuclei. Multi-Kbar nuclear calculations indicate that the binding energy per Kbar meson saturates upon increasing the number of Kbar mesons embedded in the nuclear medium. The nuclear and Kbar densities increase only moderately and are close to saturation, with no indication of any kaon-condensation precursor.

## Key claim

Self-consistent RMF calculations show that for single-K⁻ nuclei with binding energies exceeding 100 MeV, the K⁻ total decay widths are 50–100 MeV, but these widths depend sensitively on the assumed branching ratios for conversion modes and on whether a ρ or ρ² density dependence is used for two-nucleon absorption, with uncertainties reaching approximately 50 MeV in the deep-binding region.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations for single-K⁻ and multi-anti-K nuclear states in ¹²C, ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, and ²⁰⁸Pb, using both linear (HS) and nonlinear (NL-SH) parameterizations of the nucleonic Lagrangian. They incorporated gradient terms motivated by the p-wave Σ(1385) resonance, evaluated the πΛ conversion mode alongside the dominant πΣ mode, and considered both ρ and ρ² density dependencies for the nonmesonic two-nucleon absorption modes K⁻NN → YN. The coupled Dirac equations for nucleons and Klein-Gordon equations for meson fields and antikaons were solved iteratively.

## Key numbers

- K⁻ total decay widths of 50–100 MeV for binding energies exceeding 100 MeV in single-K⁻ nuclei
- The p-wave interaction increases the K⁻ binding energy by approximately 13 MeV in ¹²C and approximately 6 MeV in ⁴⁰Ca, with decay width enhancements of about 6 MeV and about 3 MeV respectively
- Including the πΛ decay mode contributes approximately 20 MeV to the width in ¹²C and 15 MeV in ⁴⁰Ca for binding energies between 100 and 160 MeV
- The ρ² density dependence for two-nucleon absorption increases widths by less than 10 MeV for B_K⁻ ≲ 100 MeV, but by about 20 MeV in C and about 30 MeV in Ca for B_K⁻ ≳ 150 MeV
- The ρ-meson mean field reduces K⁻ binding energy by less than 5 MeV in ²⁰⁸Pb
- The p-wave strength parameter c₀ at √s = 1330 MeV is 0.186 fm³

## Context

This work extends previous RMF calculations by Gazda et al. (2005) by including ρ and φ meson mean fields, p-wave gradient terms from the Σ(1385) resonance, and a more rigorous treatment of absorptive processes including the πΛ conversion channel and ρ² density dependence for two-nucleon absorption. The results contradict the suggestion by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002) and Kishimoto (1999) that deeply bound K⁻ nuclear states could be sufficiently narrow for unique experimental identification, instead supporting the lower limit of Γ_K⁻ ≃ 50 MeV found in earlier work by Friedman et al. (2006). The multi-anti-K calculations show saturation of binding energy per anti-K meson with no indication of kaon-condensation precursors, in contrast to scenarios proposed by Kaplan and Nelson (1986) and subsequent works on kaon condensation in dense matter.
