# Open-Shell Nuclei and Excited States from Multi-Reference Normal-Ordered Hamiltonians

> We discuss the approximate inclusion of three-nucleon interactions into ab initio nuclear structure calculations using a multi-reference formulation of normal ordering and Wick's theorem. Following the successful application of single-reference normal ordering for the study of ground states of closed-shell nuclei, e.g., in coupled-cluster theory, multi-reference normal ordering opens a path to open-shell nuclei and excited states. Based on different multi-determinantal reference states we benchmark the truncation of the normal-ordered Hamiltonian at the two-body level in no-core shell-model calculations for p-shell nuclei, including 6-Li, 12-C, and 10-B. We find that this multi-reference normal-ordered two-body approximation is able to capture the effects of the 3N interaction with sufficient accuracy, both, for ground-state and excitation energies, at the computational cost of a two-body Hamiltonian. It is robust with respect to the choice of reference states and has a multitude of applications in ab initio nuclear structure calculations of open-shell nuclei and their excitations as well as in nuclear reaction studies.

## Key claim

The multi-reference normal-ordered two-body (MR-NO2B) approximation captures the effects of three-nucleon (3N) interactions for both ground-state and excitation energies of open-shell nuclei with sufficient accuracy, at the computational cost of a two-body Hamiltonian, provided the reference state is constructed from the ground state of the nucleus.

## Method

The authors start with chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians containing NN interactions at N3LO and 3N interactions at N3LO, evolved via similarity renormalization group (SRG) transformation in three-body space. They apply multi-reference Wick's theorem to rewrite the 3N interaction in normal-ordered form with respect to a multi-determinantal reference state (the ground state from small-space NCSM calculations), then truncate at the two-body level to obtain the MR-NO2B Hamiltonian. They benchmark this approximation by comparing importance-truncated no-core shell model (IT-NCSM) calculations using the MR-NO2B Hamiltonian against IT-NCSM calculations including explicit 3N interactions for p-shell nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For closed-shell nuclei 4-He and 16-O, the largest relative deviations between MR-NO2B and full 3N calculations are at the level of 1% for ground-state energies.
- For 12-C, the total CPU time for IT-NCSM calculations at Nmax=10 reduces by a factor of 10 and the maximum memory footprint by a factor of 20 when using MR-NO2B compared to explicit 3N calculations.
- For 10-B, absolute energies obtained in the MR-NO2B approximation deviate by up to 1 MeV from the full 3N results, with IT-NCSM threshold extrapolation uncertainties up to 0.3 MeV.
- For 10-B with the NN+3N-full Hamiltonian, excitation energy deviations reach 0.5 MeV and show stronger dependence on the reference state parameter Nmaxref.
- For 6-Li, 12-C, and 10-B, the MR-NO2B approximation always gives the correct level ordering of states.

## Context

This work extends the single-reference normal-ordered two-body approximation previously benchmarked for closed-shell nuclei in Roth (2011) and in coupled-cluster calculations by Binder (2014) and PhysRevC.87.021303. The multi-reference formulation opens the path to open-shell nuclei and excited states, which were inaccessible with the single-reference approach. The MR-NO2B approximation enables ab initio calculations for nuclei in the lower half of the sd-shell within the IT-NCSM at manageable computational cost, and facilitates continuum and reaction calculations in the NCSM/RGM and NCSMC frameworks that are too demanding with explicit 3N terms.
