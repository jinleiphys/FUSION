# Application of the extended pairing model to heavy isotopes

> Relative binding energies (RBEs) within three isotopic chains ($^{100-130}$Sn, $^{152-181}$Yb, and $^{181-202 }$Pb) have been studied using the exactly solvable extended pairing model (EPM) \cite{FPan04}. The unique pairing strength $G$, which reproduces the experimental RBEs, has been determined. Within EPM, $\log(G)$ is a smooth function of the model space dimension $\dim(A)$, as expected for an effective coupling strength. In particular, for the Pb and Sn isotopes $G$ can be described by a two parameter expression that is inversely proportional to the dimensionality of the model space, $G=α\dim(A)^{-β}$ with $β\approx 1$. # Application of the Extended Pairing Model to Heavy Isotopes

## Key claim
The extended pairing model (EPM) can reproduce experimental relative binding energies (RBEs) for three heavy isotopic chains by allowing the pairing strength G to vary as a smooth function of the model space dimension dim(A), rather than as a direct function of mass number A. The authors find that for Pb and Sn isotopes, G follows an inverse power law G = α dim(A)^{-β} with β ≈ 1, but this result is explicitly stated as requiring further studies and is not claimed to be universal.

## Method
The authors used the exactly solvable extended pairing model (EPM), which includes many-pair interaction terms beyond the standard Nilsson plus pairing Hamiltonian, to calculate relative binding energies for three isotopic chains: ¹⁰⁰⁻¹³⁰Sn, ¹⁵²⁻¹⁸¹Yb, and ¹⁸¹⁻²⁰²Pb. Single-particle energies were obtained from the Nilsson deformed shell model with parameters from Moller and Nix, and experimental binding energies were taken from Audi G. For each nucleus, the pairing strength G was determined by requiring exact agreement between the theoretical and experimental RBE, using the core nuclei ¹⁰⁰Sn, ¹⁵²Yb, and ¹⁶⁴Pb (with ²⁰⁸Pb as the zero-binding reference for Pb isotopes). For odd neutron numbers, Pauli blocking was applied to the Fermi level of the last unpaired fermion.

## Key numbers
- For Pb isotopes (A = 181–202), the pairing strength G(A) was fit to G(A) = 366.7702 × dim(A)^{-0.9972}, with the exponent β ≈ 1.
- For Pb isotopes, separate quadratic fits to log(G(A)) as a function of A were obtained: log(G(A)) = 382.3502 − 4.1375 A + 0.0111 A² for even A, and log(G(A)) = 391.6113 − 4.2374 A + 0.0114 A² for odd A.
- The study covered three isotopic chains: ¹⁰⁰⁻¹³⁰Sn (31 nuclei), ¹⁵²⁻¹⁸¹Yb (30 nuclei), and ¹⁸¹⁻²⁰²Pb (22 nuclei).

## Context
This work extends the exactly solvable extended pairing model introduced by Pan (2004) to heavy isotopes, providing an alternative to conventional liquid-drop model calculations and two-body pairing shell model corrections for binding energies. The finding that G scales inversely with model space dimension for Pb and Sn isotopes suggests a systematic behavior of effective pairing strengths in heavy nuclei, though the authors note this requires further investigation. The approach enables calculation of relative binding energies within a pure shell-model framework without resorting to more complex ab-initio methods, and the smooth behavior of log(G) with model space dimension may guide future effective interaction studies.
