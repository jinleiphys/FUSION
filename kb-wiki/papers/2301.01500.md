# The Langevin approach for fission of heavy and super-heavy nuclei

> In this contribution, we present the main relations of the Langevin approach to the description of fission or fusion-fission reactions. The results of Langevin calculations are shown for the mass distributions of fission fragments of super-heavy elements and used for the investigation of memory effects in nuclear fission.

# The Langevin approach for fission of heavy and super-heavy nuclei

## Key claim
The Langevin approach with a four-dimensional two-center shell model shape parametrization can reproduce the three-to-four peak structure of fission fragment mass distributions for super-heavy nuclei from Z=108 to Z=122, but the appearance and relative strength of the strongly asymmetric peak near A_F=208 depends sensitively on the proton and neutron numbers of the compound nucleus. The memory effects in nuclear fission produce a fission width that is generally smaller than the constant-temperature or Bohr-Wheeler approximations, with the dependence on relaxation time being opposite in low versus high damping regimes.

## Method
The authors solved four-dimensional Langevin equations for collective shape variables (elongation, two fragment deformations, and mass asymmetry) using the two-center shell model parametrization, with potential energy calculated via the macroscopic-microscopic method and collective inertia from the Werner-Wheeler approximation. For the memory effects study, they used a one-dimensional two-parabolic Kramers potential with a retarded friction kernel of exponential form, solving non-Markovian Langevin equations for the fission width as a function of damping parameter, relaxation time, and excitation energy.

## Key numbers
- Fission fragment mass distributions were calculated for super-heavy nuclei from **^{276}Hs** to **^{308}122** at excitation energies **E* = 10, 20, and 30 MeV**
- The strongly asymmetric peak appears at fragment mass number close to **A_F = 208**
- For the memory effects study, the barrier height was fixed at **V_b = 6 MeV** and the harmonic oscillator frequency at **ħω_0 = 1.0 MeV**
- Two values of relaxation time were used: **τ = 5 × 10^{-22} sec** and **τ = 10^{-21} sec**
- Excitation energies for the memory study were **E* = 10, 25, and 60 MeV**
- The ratio Γ_f(E)/Γ_eff is close to **1 at E* = 60 MeV** and close to **0.1 at E* = 10 MeV**
- In **^{290-296}Lv** and **^{290-296}Og**, the three peaks in fission fragment mass distribution are approximately of the same magnitude at **E* = 10 MeV**

## Context
This work extends previous Langevin calculations of fission fragment mass distributions for super-heavy elements, with similar strongly asymmetric peaks near A_F=208 recently found by Pasha (2022) using a Fourier shape parametrization. The memory effects investigation addresses conflicting claims in the literature about whether memory effects significantly influence fission dynamics, building on the Kramers (1940) fission width formalism and the non-Markovian Langevin approach of Abe and collaborators. The results enable more reliable predictions for planning experiments on super-heavy element fission at facilities such as the Flerov Laboratory in Dubna.
