# Unpolarized fragmentation function for the pion and kaon via the nonlocal chiral-quark model

> In this talk we present our recent studies for the unpolarized fragmentation functions for the pion and kaon, employing the nonlocal chiral quark model, which manifests the nonlocal interaction between the quarks and pseudoscalar mesons, in the light-cone frame. It turns out that the nonlocal interaction produces considerable differences in comparison to typical local-interaction models.

## Key claim

The nonlocal interaction between quarks and pseudoscalar mesons, as implemented in the nonlocal chiral quark model (NLChQM), produces a bump in the unpolarized fragmentation function at \(z \approx 0.5\), whereas local-interaction models (pseudoscalar scheme and Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model) place the bump at larger \(z \sim 0.7\); the authors expect this shift may yield results more comparable to empirical data after DGLAP evolution to higher \(Q^2\).

## Method

The authors compute the elementary unpolarized fragmentation function for a quark fragmenting into a pion or kaon in the light-cone frame, using the nonlocal chiral quark model (NLChQM) motivated by the instanton vacuum. The effective chiral action is expanded to linear order in the pseudoscalar meson field, giving a nonlocal quark–pseudoscalar-meson vertex with a momentum-dependent effective quark mass of a simple \(n\)-pole form (\(n=2\), \(\Lambda = 600\) MeV). The fragmentation function is then evaluated analytically from the resulting trace over spin spaces, yielding a closed-form expression that reduces to the local-interaction result when the momentum-dependent mass is taken constant.

## Key numbers

- The momentum-dependent effective quark mass is parameterized with \(n=2\) and \(\Lambda = 600\) MeV.
- The elementary fragmentation functions are computed at a renormalization scale \(\mu = 0.6\) GeV.
- The bump in the favored fragmentation function for \(u \to \pi^+\) appears at \(z \approx 0.5\), whereas in the pseudoscalar-scheme and Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model results the bump appears at \(z \sim 0.7\).
- The kaon fragmentation functions (\(u \to K^+\), \(\bar{s} \to K^+\)) are much smaller than the pion fragmentation function at this scale.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of fragmentation functions using local effective models, specifically the pseudoscalar and pseudovector coupling schemes of Bacchetta et al. (2002) and Amrath et al. (2005), and the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model of Ito et al. (2009). The nonlocal interaction, derived from the instanton vacuum, produces a shift of the fragmentation-function bump to lower \(z\) values, which the authors argue may lead to more realistic results after DGLAP evolution to higher \(Q^2\) when compared to empirical parametrizations such as those of Hirai et al. (2007). The work enables future studies including meson-cloud effects, parton distribution functions via the Drell–Levi–Yan relation, and full DGLAP evolution.
