# On the excitation of the $2^+_1$ state in $^{12}$C in the $(e,e'γ)$ reaction

> The excitation of the carbon $2^+$ state at 4.439 MeV by $70-150$ MeV electron impact and its subsequent decay to the ground state by photon emission is described within the distorted-wave Born approximation. The transition densities are obtained from the nuclear quasiparticle phonon model. The photon angular distributions are compared with earlier results and with experiment, including the influence of bremsstrahlung. Predictions for spin asymmetries in the case of polarized electron impact are also made.

## Key claim

The distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) description of the excitation of the carbon \(2^+\) state at 4.439 MeV by 70–150 MeV electrons and its subsequent photon decay reproduces the measured relative photon angular distribution, but the absolute cross section depends strongly on the choice of nuclear transition densities, with the quasiparticle phonon model (QPM) densities giving cross sections a factor of 3.5–3.7 lower than those from the Ravenhall et al. (1987) Fourier-Bessel fit to experiment.

## Method

The triply differential cross section for the \((e,e'\gamma)\) reaction was calculated using the distorted-wave Born approximation for the electron scattering, with the nuclear transition densities (charge density \(\varrho_2\) and current densities \(J_{21}\) and \(J_{23}\)) obtained from the quasiparticle phonon model (QPM) in the one-phonon approximation. The bremsstrahlung contribution was added coherently within the plane-wave Born approximation (PWBA), and the finite resolution of the photon detector was accounted for by averaging over the photon energy window. The Sherman function for polarized electrons was computed from the transition amplitudes without bremsstrahlung.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energy of the \(2^+_1\) state in \(^{12}\)C: 4.439 MeV.
- Experimental \(B(E2, g.s. \to 2^+_1)\) value: 39.7 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) (Raman et al.).
- \(B(E2, g.s. \to 2^+_1)\) from the Ravenhall et al. (1987) charge transition density: 42.41 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) (7% above the experimental value).
- Ground-state decay width of the \(2^+_1\) state: \(\Gamma_n = (1.08 \pm 0.06) \times 10^{-2}\) eV (Ajzenberg-Selove, 1980).
- At 66.9 MeV and \(\vartheta_f = 80^\circ\), the Ravenhall et al. (1987) cross section is a factor of 3.5 higher than the QPM cross section.
- At 66.9 MeV and \(\vartheta_f = 170^\circ\), the Ravenhall et al. (1987) cross section is a factor of 3.7 higher than the QPM cross section.
- Detector resolution values used: \(\Delta \omega / \omega = 3\%\) (133 keV) and \(\Delta \omega / \omega = 0.5\%\) (22.2 keV).
- Maximum Sherman function \(S\) at \(\vartheta_f = 140^\circ\): 0.031 for 70 MeV and 0.066 for 150 MeV (using a step size of \(\Delta \theta_k = 1^\circ\)).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of the \((e,e'\gamma)\) reaction on \(^{12}\)C beyond the earlier combined DWBA/PWBA approach of Ravenhall et al. (1987) by using a full DWBA prescription for both electric and magnetic transitions and by including bremsstrahlung coherently. It also provides predictions for spin asymmetries with polarized electrons, which were not considered in the earlier experimental work of Papanicolas et al. (1985). The results enable future experiments at the S-DALINAC facility to test nuclear structure models by comparing the Sherman function, which is shown to be more sensitive to the choice of transition densities than the unpolarized cross section.
