# Microscopic description of quadrupole-hexadecapole coupling in radium, thorium, uranium and plutonium isotopes with the Gogny energy density functional

> The emergence and stability of static hexadecapole deformations as well as the impact in the development of dynamic deformation due to collective motion considering quadrupole-hexadecapole coupling are studied for a selected set of radium, thorium, uranium and plutonium isotopes, using the Gogny Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov and Generator Coordinate Method frameworks. Sizable hexadecapole deformations are found to play a significant role in the ground and excited states of nuclei in the neighborhood of $^{238}$U. For each of the studied isotopic chains, it is shown that a region with small negative hexadecapole deformation, just below the neutron magic number $N =184$, remains stable once zero-point quadrupole-hexadecapole fluctuations are taken into account. A transition is predicted, with increasing mass number, from a regime in which the quadrupole and hexadecapole degrees of freedom are interwoven to a regime in which they are decoupled, accompanied by an enhanced shape coexistence in the more neutron-rich sectors of the isotopic chains. It is also shown, that quadrupole-hexadecapole configuration mixing brings a nontrivial additional correlation energy gain comparable to the quadrupole correlation energy itself.

## Key claim

The quadrupole and hexadecapole degrees of freedom are strongly coupled in the lighter actinide isotopes studied, requiring full two-dimensional Generator Coordinate Method calculations, but a transition to a decoupled regime occurs in neutron-rich isotopes near neutron number 166-168, where one-dimensional calculations become sufficient.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny-D1S energy density functional with constraints on the axially symmetric quadrupole (Q20) and hexadecapole (Q40) operators for isotopes 232-268 of radium, thorium, uranium, and plutonium. These HFB states were then used as a basis in two-dimensional Generator Coordinate Method (2D-GCM) calculations with (β2, β4) as generating coordinates, solving the Griffin-Hill-Wheeler equation to account for zero-point quantum fluctuations. One-dimensional GCM calculations along the principal axes of the parabolic energy surfaces were also performed for comparison.

## Key numbers

- Static hexadecapole deformations of β4 = 0.09, 0.13, 0.16, and 0.17 were obtained for 238Ra, 238Th, 238U, and 238Pu, respectively, at the HFB level
- Dynamical hexadecapole deformations from 2D-GCM calculations gave β4 = 0.10, 0.13, 0.16, and 0.18 for 238Ra, 238Th, 238U, and 238Pu, respectively
- A region of small negative hexadecapole deformations with -0.034 ≤ β4 ≤ -0.014 was confirmed dynamically for neutron-rich isotopes (250-260Ra, 252-262Th, 254-264U, 256-264Pu) just below the neutron magic number N=184
- 2D-GCM correlation energies ranged from 1.42 MeV to 1.72 MeV across all studied nuclei
- For 238U, the principal axes of the parabolic energy surface were parameterized as β4 = 2.50β2 - 0.52 (direction A) and β4 = -0.40β2 + 0.29 (direction B)
- HFB ground state quadrupole deformations varied within 0.24 ≤ β2 ≤ 0.28 for isotopes up to A=248, decreasing to -0.12 ≤ β2 ≤ 0.0 for 266,268Ra, 266,268Th, 266,268U, and 268Pu

## Context

This work extends a previous large-scale HFB survey of hexadecapole deformations by the same group (Rodriguez-Guzman and Robledo, 2022) that identified regions favoring static β4 deformations and studied dynamical (β2,β4) coupling in Sm and Gd isotopes. The present study applies the same methodology to actinide chains (Ra, Th, U, Pu) motivated by recent hydrodynamic simulations of heavy ion collisions at RHIC (b4-RIHC, 2023) that demonstrated the importance of hexadecapole deformation in 238U collisions. The results enable future inclusion of hexadecapole correlation energies in fitting protocols for the Gogny energy density functional and provide a foundation for extending calculations to include triaxial hexadecapole degrees of freedom to study Kπ=4+ vibrational bands.
