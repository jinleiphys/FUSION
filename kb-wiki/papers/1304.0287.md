# Shear viscosity of quark matter at finite temperature in magnetic fields

> We have applied the Green-Kubo formula to investigate the shear viscosity in the SU(2) light-flavor quark matter at finite temperature under the external strong magnetic field e|B| ~ m^2_pi. For this purpose, we employ the temperature-modified instanton model and the Schwinger method to induce the magnetic field. The quark spectral function with the finite width motivated by the instanton model is adopted to compute the shear viscosity. We find that shear viscosity increases as temperature increases even beyond the transition temperature T_0=170 MeV if temperature-dependent (TDP) model parameters is used. On the other hand, with temperature-independent ones the shear viscosity starts to drop when temperature goes beyond T_0. Although the magnetic field reduces the shear viscosity in terms of the magnetic catalysis, its effects are almost negligible in the chiral-restored phase even for very strong magnetic field, e|B| ~ 10^20 gauss. We also compute the ratio of the shear viscosity and entropy density eta/s. Our numerical results are well compatible with other theoretical results for a wide temperature regions. We obtain the parameterization of the temperature-dependent ratio from our numerical result as eta/s=0.27-0.87/t+1.19/t^2-0.28/t^3 with t = T/T_0 for T=(100 ~ 350) MeV and e|B|=0.

# Shear viscosity of quark matter at finite temperature in magnetic fields

## Key claim
Shear viscosity of SU(2) light-flavor quark matter increases with temperature even beyond the chiral transition temperature T₀=170 MeV when temperature-dependent model parameters (average instanton size and inter-instanton distance) are used, but decreases beyond T₀ when temperature-independent parameters are employed. The magnetic field reduces shear viscosity via magnetic catalysis, but this effect becomes almost negligible in the chiral-restored phase even for very strong fields up to e|B| ≈ 10²⁰ gauss.

## Method
The authors applied the Green-Kubo formula to compute shear viscosity from a quark spectral function with a finite width Λ ∼ 1/ρ̄, motivated by the dilute instanton liquid model (LIM) for the SU(2) light-flavor sector. The temperature dependence of the average instanton size ρ̄(T) and inter-instanton distance R̄(T) was derived from the Harrington-Shepard caloron solution. External magnetic fields were introduced via the Schwinger method with minimal gauge substitution, and the thermodynamic potential in the leading 1/N_c approximation was used to compute entropy density and determine the chiral phase transition.

## Key numbers
- Chiral transition temperature: T₀ ≈ 166 MeV for chiral limit (second-order transition) and T₀ ≈ 170 MeV for finite current-quark mass m=5 MeV (crossover transition)
- Shear viscosity at T ≈ T₀: η ≈ 0.02 GeV³ for all cases considered
- Ratio η/s at T ≈ T₀: η/s ≈ 0.29 for T=(100–350) MeV and e|B|=0
- Parameterization of η/s for e|B|=0: η/s = 0.27 − 0.87/t + 1.19/t² − 0.28/t³ with t = T/T₀ for T=(100–350) MeV
- Magnetic field effects on shear viscosity are less than 10% for e|B| ≲ 100 × m²π ≈ 10²⁰ gauss
- Average instanton size at zero temperature: ρ̄(0) ≈ 1/3 fm; average inter-instanton distance: R̄ ≈ 1 fm
- Chiral condensate reproduced: ⟨q̄q⟩ ≈ −(239 MeV)³ from the model with n=2

## Context
This work extends previous shear viscosity calculations using effective QCD models such as the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model (Fukutome 2007, Iwasaki 2007, Sasaki 2008) by incorporating temperature-dependent instanton parameters and external magnetic fields. The results are compatible with other theoretical estimates from NJL models, lattice QCD (Meyer 2007), and chiral perturbation theory (Chen 2006). The study enables more realistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations of quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions by providing temperature-dependent shear viscosity values and quantifying magnetic field effects relevant to peripheral collisions at RHIC and LHC.
