# Particle number fluctuations in nuclear collisions within excluded volume hadron gas model

> The multiplicity fluctuations are studied in the van der Waals excluded volume hadron-resonance gas model. The calculations are done in the grand canonical ensemble within the Boltzmann statistics approximation. The scaled variances for positive, negative and all charged hadrons are calculated along the chemical freeze-out line of nucleus-nucleus collisions at different collision energies. The multiplicity fluctuations are found to be suppressed in the van der Waals gas. The numerical calculations are presented for two values of hard-core hadron radius, $r=0.3$ fm and 0.5 fm, as well as for the upper limit of the excluded volume suppression effects.

## Key claim

Multiplicity fluctuations in the van der Waals excluded volume hadron gas are suppressed compared to the ideal hadron gas, and this suppression cannot be removed by rescaling the total volume of the system, in contrast to the behavior of average multiplicities.

## Method

The authors calculated the scaled variance (omega) for positive, negative, and all charged hadrons in the grand canonical ensemble using Boltzmann statistics, within a multi-component van der Waals excluded volume hadron-resonance gas model. Both primordial (before resonance decay) and final state (after resonance decay) fluctuations were computed along the chemical freeze-out line for central nucleus-nucleus collisions, using the THERMUS package to solve the transcendental equation for pressure and determine hadron yields and fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- For hard-core radius r = 0.5 fm at high collision energies (LHC, sqrt(s_NN) = 5500 GeV), the primordial scaled variance for positively charged hadrons is omega_+ = 0.845, and the final state value is omega_+ = 0.828.
- For r = 0.5 fm at the same energy, the primordial scaled variance for all charged hadrons is omega_ch = 0.691, and the final state value is omega_ch = 1.056.
- For r = 0.3 fm at sqrt(s_NN) = 5500 GeV, the final state scaled variance for positively charged hadrons is omega_+ = 0.978.
- The upper limit of VDW suppression (n_tot approximately 1/v) gives final state omega_+ = 0.617 and omega_ch = 0.634 at high collision energies.
- At low collision energy (sqrt(s_NN) = 2.32 GeV) with r = 0.5 fm, the primordial scaled variance for positively charged hadrons is omega_+ = 0.915, and for all charged hadrons omega_ch = 0.904.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of multiplicity fluctuations in the ideal hadron-resonance gas model (res_CE, res_MCE) by incorporating repulsive interactions via the van der Waals excluded volume procedure, following the approach of VdW (vdw, vdw1, crit). The results show that excluded volume effects suppress fluctuations in a way qualitatively different from the suppression of particle yields, and the calculations provide predictions for central A+A collisions from SIS to LHC energies that can be compared with experimental data from NA49 and other collaborations.
