# Nucleon transverse quark spin densities

> We present a calculation of the Mellin moments of the nucleon transverse quark spin densities extracted from the unpolarized and transversity generalized form factors. We use three $N_F=2+1+1$ ensembles of twisted mass fermions with quark masses tuned to their physical values and lattice spacings $a\sim 0.08$~fm, $a\sim 0.07$~fm and $a\sim 0.06$~fm and extrapolate the form factors to the continuum limit. Besides isovector densities we also include results for the tensor charge for each quark flavor using the ensemble with $a\sim 0.08$~fm for which we include the disconnected contributions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a lattice QCD calculation of the Mellin moments of nucleon transverse quark spin densities using ensembles with quark masses tuned to their physical values, avoiding chiral extrapolations that may introduce unquantified systematic errors. The continuum extrapolation is performed at fixed physical pion mass, but the results for individual quark flavors (including disconnected contributions) are only given for the coarsest ensemble with lattice spacing \(a\sim 0.08\) fm, not yet extrapolated to the continuum limit.

## Method

The authors compute nucleon matrix elements of local operators on three \(N_F=2+1+1\) twisted mass clover-improved fermion ensembles with physical pion masses and lattice spacings \(a\sim 0.08\) fm, \(0.07\) fm, and \(0.06\) fm. They extract the lowest two Mellin moments of the unpolarized and transversity generalized form factors (GFFs) from two-point and three-point correlation functions, performing excited-state analyses using two-state and three-state fits and the summation method. The GFFs are extrapolated to the continuum limit linearly in \(a^2\) at fixed \(Q^2\), and then Fourier transformed to impact parameter space to obtain the transverse spin densities.

## Key numbers

* Isovector tensor charge \(g_T^{u-d} = 0.924(54)\) after continuum extrapolation from three physical-mass ensembles with lattice spacings \(a\sim 0.08\) fm, \(0.07\) fm, and \(0.06\) fm.
* For the cB211.072.64 ensemble (\(a=0.07961(13)\) fm, \(m_\pi=140.2(2)\) MeV): \(g_T^{u-d}=0.936(25)\), \(g_T^u=0.729(22)\), \(g_T^d=-0.207(75)\), \(g_T^s=-0.00268(58)\), \(g_T^c=-0.00024(16)\) in the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme at 2 GeV.
* Anomalous tensor magnetic moment \(\kappa_T \equiv \bar{B}_{T10}(0) = 1.051(94)\) from the continuum-extrapolated GFFs.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of nucleon tensor charges by using ensembles directly at the physical pion mass, avoiding the chiral extrapolations required in earlier studies by PNDME (Gupta 2018), \(\chi\)QCD (Horkel 2020), NME (Park 2021), and CLS-Mainz (Harris 2019). The continuum-extrapolated isovector tensor charge provides input for phenomenological analyses such as those by the JAM collaboration (Gamberg 2022), and the determination of \(\kappa_T\) confirms that the Boer-Mulders function \(h_1^\perp\) is negative and sizeable, consistent with the transverse momentum dependent PDF study of Musch (2011). The results enable improved determinations of transverse spin asymmetries and the Sivers effect in future experiments at Jefferson Lab and the Electron Ion Collider.
