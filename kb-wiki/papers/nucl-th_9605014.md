# Transverse momentum dependence of transverse flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The strength of transverse flow is examined as a function of transverse momentum $p_t$ using a simple, transversely moving thermal model and a more realistic, relativistic transport model (ART). It is shown that the $p_t$ dependence reveals useful information about the collective flow that is complementary to that obtained from the standard in-plane transverse momentum analysis. Interesting features of using the $p_t$ dependence to study the equation of state of the superdense hadronic matter formed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions are demonstrated.

# Transverse momentum dependence of transverse flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The ratio \( R(p_t) \equiv (dN^+/dp_t)/(dN^-/dp_t) \) of particles emitted in the same versus opposite direction of sideward flow, measured as a function of transverse momentum near projectile rapidity, is a sensitive probe of the equation of state of superdense hadronic matter, but only at high \( p_t \) where particles originate from the most violent stage of the reaction; at low \( p_t \) the ratio is dominated by cold spectator matter and approaches unity.

## Method

The authors first derived analytic expressions for the transverse momentum spectra and the ratio \( R(p_t) \) using a simple transversely moving thermal model with a single temperature \( T \) and transverse flow velocity \( \beta \). They then performed dynamical simulations with the relativistic transport model ART 1.0, which includes baryons (N, Δ(1232), N*(1440), N*(1535), Λ, Σ) and mesons (π, ρ, ω, η, K) with elastic and inelastic collisions, for semicentral Au+Au collisions at a beam momentum of 10.8 GeV/c per nucleon. The ratio \( R(p_t) \) was computed for nucleons in a rapidity bin \( |y-2.95| \leq 0.35 \) near the projectile rapidity of 3.1, comparing results from a pure cascade mode and a soft equation of state.

## Key numbers

- For semicentral Au+Au at \( P_{beam}/A = 10.8 \) GeV/c, the average in-plane transverse momentum per nucleon \( \langle p_x \rangle \) is about 0.1 GeV/c at projectile rapidity, giving a transverse flow velocity \( \beta \approx 0.05 \).
- Effective temperatures extracted from high-\( p_t \) spectra are about 150 MeV for central collisions and 105 MeV for peripheral collisions.
- For central collisions, the ratio \( R(p_t) \) at high \( p_t \) differs by about a factor of 1.5 between the pure cascade and the soft equation of state.
- In peripheral collisions, \( R(p_t) \approx 1 \) for transverse momenta \( p_t \leq 0.2 \) GeV/c due to isotropic Fermi motion of spectator nucleons.
- The spectra show exponential shapes for \( m_t - m \geq 0.2 \) GeV (or \( p_t \geq 0.64 \) GeV/c).

## Context

This work extends the standard in-plane transverse momentum analysis developed by Danielewicz and collaborators (1985) and the azimuthal anisotropy measurements using calorimeters at the AGS (E877 collaboration, 1995). It builds on the suggestion by the E877 collaboration (1995) to study the ratio \( R(p_t) \) as a flow observable, and complements earlier studies of "squeeze-out" at BEVALAC/SIS energies by the KAOS and TAPS collaborations (1994-1995). The approach enables extraction of flow information using particle identification at a single rapidity, which is experimentally easier than full in-plane momentum analysis at AGS energies, and provides a new tool to discriminate between different equations of state for superdense hadronic matter.
