# The Gilbert Damping Factor of Heavy Quark Spin Polarization in the Magnetic Field

> We employ the linear response theory to calculate the polarization rate of heavy quark spin in the presence of a strong magnetic field and the hot QCD matter, both of which are simultaneously generated in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The hot QCD medium is simplified as a fermionic system consisting of only quarks. The spin of heavy quarks can be polarized as a result of combined contributions from spin-spin interactions between quarks and spin-magnetic field interactions. This spin dynamics is modeled as consisting of a polarization term and a dissipation term, which is described by the Landau-Lifshitz-Gilbert (LLG) equation and widely studied in condensed matter physics, analogous to the momentum evolution in the Langevin equation. In this study, we calculate the Gilbert damping factor that characterizes the spin polarization rate of heavy quarks, considering a Coulomb potential between two fermions in the medium. The dependence of the heavy quark spin polarization rate on the strength of the magnetic field, the heavy quark mass, temperature, and baryon chemical potential is studied in detail. This analysis contributes to a better understanding of quark spin dynamics in the hot QCD medium and the magnetic field.

## Key claim

The Gilbert damping factor for heavy quark spin polarization in a hot QCD medium with a strong magnetic field is derived using linear response theory, with the key condition that the calculation assumes the hot QCD medium is simplified as a fermionic system consisting of only quarks and that the spin dynamics is modeled analogously to the Landau-Lifshitz-Gilbert equation from condensed matter physics.

## Method

The authors employed linear response theory to calculate the transverse magnetic susceptibility of the fermionic system, relating the imaginary part of the spin wave frequency to the Gilbert damping factor. They derived the damping factor by comparing two expressions for the transverse susceptibility, one obtained from the spin wave equation and the other from the Heisenberg equation of motion for the spin operator, incorporating spin-orbit coupling and Zeeman energy. The calculation was extended to include two-body scatterings with a color-screened Coulomb potential between quarks, using partial wave analysis and the optical theorem to connect the damping factor to scattering cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The color-screened Coulomb potential between quarks is given by \( V(r) = -\frac{\alpha_c}{r} \exp(-m_D r) \), with the Debye mass \( m_D = T \sqrt{4\pi} \) (the full expression is truncated in the text).
- The Gilbert damping factor \( \alpha \) is expressed as \( \alpha = \frac{\omega_2}{\omega_1} \), where \( \omega_1 = \gamma B_{ext} \) is the precession frequency and \( \omega_2 \) is the imaginary part of the spin wave frequency.
- The total cross section for partial wave scattering is given by \( \sigma_l = \frac{4\pi}{k^2} (2l+1) \sin^2 \delta_l \approx \frac{4\pi}{k^2} (2l+1) \delta_l^2 \) for small phase shifts \( \delta_l \).
- The spin-orbit coupling strength is \( \xi = \frac{1}{2 m^2 c^2 r} \frac{dU}{dr} \), where \( U \) is the external potential.

## Context

This work extends the application of the Landau-Lifshitz-Gilbert equation, previously used in condensed matter physics (Kambersky, 1976; PhysRevB.90.014420), to heavy quark spin dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. It builds on prior studies of quark spin polarization in magnetic fields (Liu, 2024) and J/psi spin alignment measurements by ALICE (2022), providing a theoretical framework to calculate the Gilbert damping factor that governs the spin polarization rate of heavy quarks. This enables more realistic modeling of charm quark spin evolution in the hot QCD medium, which can be incorporated into transport models for quarkonium spin alignment calculations.
