# Two-Pion Exchange in proton-proton Scattering

> We present calculations of the two-pion-exchange contribution to proton-proton scattering at 90 degrees using form factors appropriate for representing the distribution of the constituent partons of the nucleon. Talk given at MENU2001, George Washington University, July 26-31, 2001

# Two-Pion Exchange in proton-proton Scattering

## Key claim
Two-pion exchange provides a significant contribution to proton-proton scattering at 90 degrees in the laboratory momentum range from 0 to 12 GeV/c, but the authors caution that good quantitative agreement with data cannot be expected due to strong inelasticity of the NN interaction at these energies, since distortion effects need to be considered.

## Method
The authors calculated the box and crossed two-pion exchange Feynman diagrams with nucleons as intermediate states, using both pseudo-scalar and pseudo-vector pion-nucleon couplings. Form factors derived from an exponential parton density were applied at each pion-nucleon vertex, using a dipole form with an off-shell range parameter Lambda representing the extension of the proton's constituent quark distribution. The calculations were performed for proton-proton scattering at a center-of-mass angle of 90 degrees.

## Key numbers
- The spin-correlation observable C_NN has a constant value of about 0.07 in the region from 4 GeV/c to 8 GeV/c laboratory momentum at 90 degrees
- The off-shell range parameter Lambda was taken as 1.4 GeV/c for a dipole form, based on values from Coon and Scadron (1990) who found Lambda between 0.8 and 1.0 GeV/c for a monopole form, and from Liu, Dong and Draper (1995) who found Lambda = 0.747 GeV/c for monopole and Lambda = 1.32 GeV/c for dipole
- Simple quark exchange gives C_NN = 1/3, which differs from the observed value of about 0.07

## Context
This work extends earlier two-pion exchange studies of the NN interaction to higher energies, building on the formalism of Gibbs and Loiseau (1994). The calculations address data from multiple experimental groups (Court et al., 1986; Bhatia et al., 1982; Crosbie et al., 1981) and theoretical studies by Brodsky, Carlson and Lipkin (1979) and Farrar et al. (1979). The results show that pseudo-scalar and pseudo-vector couplings bracket the experimental data, consistent with the mixed-coupling findings of Gross and Surya (1993), Goudsmit et al. (1994), and Kondratyuk and Scholten (1999). The work enables future inclusion of distortion effects and inelasticity to achieve quantitative comparison with data.
