# Resonant states of open quantum systems

> We first show that quantum resonant states observe particle number conservation and hence are consistent with the probabilistic interpretation of quantum mechanics. We then present for a class of quantum open systems, a resonant-state expansion of the sum of the retarded and advanced Green's functions. The expansion is given purely in terms of all discrete eigenstates and does not contain any background integrals. Using the expansion, we argue that the Fano asymmetry of resonance peaks is interpreted as interference between discrete eigenstates. We microscopically derive the Fano parameters for several cases.

## Key claim

Quantum resonant states, despite their spatially divergent wave functions and complex eigenvalues, conserve particle number when the integration region is expanded at the same speed as the leaking particles; this conservation holds using the standard definition of probability, contrary to common misconceptions.

## Method

The author solves the one-dimensional time-independent Schrödinger equation under the Siegert boundary condition (only outgoing waves exist) for a square-well potential and a tight-binding model with a finite scatterer attached to an infinite lead. For the tight-binding system, the sum of the retarded and advanced Green's functions is expanded purely in terms of all discrete eigenstates (bound, resonant, anti-resonant, and anti-bound states) by deforming integration contours in the complex wave-number plane and showing that background integrals cancel or vanish.

- For the square-well potential with \(2mV_0 a^2/\hbar^2 = 1\), discrete solutions are obtained in the complex \(K\) plane and complex \(E\) plane (Fig. 2); resonant states lie in the fourth quadrant of the \(K\) plane (Re \(K_n > 0\), Im \(K_n < 0\)) and on the lower-half \(E\) plane.
- The particle-number conservation for a resonant state is shown by the cancellation: \(\text{Im} E_n = (\hbar^2/m) \text{Re} K_n \text{Im} K_n\), making the exponent in Eq. (2-255) vanish.

## Context

This work extends the static view of resonant states (Siegert (1939), Peierls (1959), Landau (1977)) by proving particle-number conservation without complex-rotation methods (Aguilar (1971), Balslev (1971), Simon (1972)). It also provides a resonant-state expansion of the Green's function sum that lacks background integrals, contrasting with prior expansions (Berggren (1970), García-Calderón (1976), Romo (1980)) that required such integrals. This enables a microscopic derivation of Fano asymmetry parameters (Fano (1961)) from interference between discrete eigenstates, including a type of asymmetry possibly not considered in Fano's original argument.
