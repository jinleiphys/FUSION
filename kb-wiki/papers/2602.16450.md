# All-path-length and sub-eikonal corrections to momentum broadening in the opacity expansion approach

> We present a detailed study of momentum broadening for high-energy partons traversing the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP), extending the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) formalism to include both all-path-length (APL) and sub-eikonal corrections. Traditional GLV calculations rely on the large separation distance and large formation time approximations, which are valid for large systems, but whose applicability in small systems such as pp and p/dA may fail. We derive analytic expressions for the momentum broadening distributions to first order in the opacity expansion, and perform a numerical investigation to quantify their impact. Our results show that the APL result reduces the low-momentum broadening and the sub-eikonal correction enhances the high-momentum broadening. The combined APL and sub eikonal correction show that the sub-eikonal correction mitigates the effect of the APL correction.

## Key claim

The authors extend the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) formalism for momentum broadening to include both all-path-length (APL) corrections and sub-eikonal corrections, finding that the APL correction reduces low-momentum broadening while the sub-eikonal correction enhances high-momentum broadening, and that the sub-eikonal correction partially mitigates the effect of the APL correction when both are applied simultaneously.

## Method

The authors compute the momentum broadening distribution for a high-energy parton traversing a Quark-Gluon Plasma to first order in the opacity expansion, using the Gyulassy-Wang Debye-screened potential. They systematically relax two approximations of the standard GLV formalism: the large separation distance approximation (by retaining terms proportional to \(e^{-\mu_\perp \Delta z}\)) and the eikonal approximation (by keeping sub-leading terms in the \(1/P^+\) expansion, introducing an all-order factor \(\gamma = \sqrt{1 - 2q_\perp^2 / P^{+2}}\)). The single-scattering and double-scattering matrix elements are computed with modified kinematics for each correction scheme, and the results are combined ensuring unitarity is preserved.

## Key numbers

* The sub-eikonal calculation remains under perturbative control in the extended kinematic range \(\mu_D \leq q_\perp \leq \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} P^{+}\), significantly enlarging the domain of applicability compared to the conventional eikonal treatment.
* The source amplitude scales as \(J(p) \sim 1/P^{+4}\) in the standard GLV framework, and the sub-eikonal corrections are valid up to \(\mathcal{O}(1/P^{+3})\) as the full source amplitude takes the form \(J(p) \sim 1/(P^{+} + q_\perp)^4\).
* The APL correction introduces a factor \((1 - \frac{1}{2} e^{-\mu_\perp \Delta z})^2\) to the broadening distribution, which reduces to the original GLV result when \(e^{-\mu_\perp \Delta z} \rightarrow 0\).
* The sub-eikonal correction introduces a factor \(4/(1+\gamma)^2\) to the broadening distribution, where \(\gamma = \sqrt{1 - 2q_\perp^2 / P^{+2}}\).

## Context

This work extends the GLV formalism (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev, 1999-2002) for momentum broadening, which relies on large separation distance and large formation time approximations valid for large collision systems like nucleus-nucleus (AA) collisions. The APL correction was first explored in the radiative energy-loss case by Kolbe (2015), where it was found to be negative and dominant at large energies. This work enables more accurate modeling of jet quenching in small collision systems such as pp, p/dA, and intermediate systems like oxygen-oxygen and neon-neon collisions at the LHC, where recent results from ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, and STAR have shown QGP-like signatures. The analysis by van der Schee (2025) and Faraday (2024-2025) further motivates the need for these corrections by demonstrating that nonzero elastic contributions are required to describe suppression patterns across all collision systems.
