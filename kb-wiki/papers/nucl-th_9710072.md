# Analytical Extension of the Frazer-Fulco Unitarity Relations for Isovector Nucleon Form Factors to the Complex t-Plane

> The unitarity relations for isovector nucleon form factors in the two-pion approximation for the intermediate states are analytically extended in a model independent way from the real half-axis t > 4 $μ^2$ ($μ$ is the pion mass) to the whole complex t-plane. As a result, the explicit integral representations for the nucleon form factors are constructed in terms of the pion form factor and imaginary parts of the t-channel p-wave piN-scattering amplitudes at t < 0. New method based on the explicit integral representations is developed for numerical evaluation of the two-pion contribution to the isovector nucleon form factors. The results of different methods are compared and their efficiency is discussed.

# Analytical Extension of the Frazer-Fulco Unitarity Relations for Isovector Nucleon Form Factors to the Complex t-Plane

## Key claim
The unitarity relations for isovector nucleon form factors in the two-pion approximation can be solved to give explicit one-dimensional integral representations valid in the whole complex t-plane, eliminating the need for analytical continuation of the t-channel p-wave piN-scattering amplitudes from t < 0 to t > 4 mu^2. The method yields well-defined predictions for F_{2v}(t) but the unitarity relations do not allow accurate predictions for F_{1v}(t) due to large theoretical errors.

## Method
The author analytically extends the Frazer-Fulco unitarity relations from the real half-axis t > 4 mu^2 to the complex t-plane using dispersion relations and the function K_pi(t), defined as an integral over the pion form factor. The resulting integral representations (Eqs. 2.8 and 2.10) express the nucleon form factors in terms of the pion form factor and the imaginary parts of the t-channel p-wave piN-scattering amplitudes at t < a, where a = 4 mu^2 - mu^4/m^2. A numerical algorithm is developed using weighted sums of subtraction points to suppress the unknown high-energy part of the integrals, with errors minimized via Lagrange multipliers.

## Key numbers
- The two-pion approximation is valid with high accuracy at the rho-meson peak and above, as the branching ratio B(rho -> 4pi) < 0.002.
- The truncated partial wave expansion converges in the region -40 mu^2 < t < 0, setting the lower bound b = -40 mu^2.
- The relative errors for Re(Gamma_{iR}(t)) are taken as Delta_j = 0.05.
- The upper limit for analytical continuation in the Hoehler-Pietarinen method is Lambda^2 approximately 1 GeV^2.
- The form factor F_{2v}(0) estimates from different methods are in reasonable agreement; F_{1v}(0) estimates differ by a factor of two between the differential approximation scheme and the Hoehler-Pietarinen analysis.
- The asymptotic behavior gives Gamma_1(t) = O(1/t) and Gamma_2(t) = O(1/t^{3/2}) as t goes to infinity.

## Context
This work extends the Frazer-Fulco unitarity relations (Frazer and Fulco, 1960) for isovector nucleon form factors, which were previously applied only on the real axis above the two-pion threshold. It addresses the discrepancy between two earlier approaches: the differential approximation scheme by Efremov, Meshcheryakov and Shirkov (1960s) used by Lendel et al., and the discrepancy function method by Hoehler and Pietarinen (1970s). The new method avoids the need for a cut-off parameter Lambda and does not require analytical continuation of the amplitudes to the region t > 4 mu^2, enabling more reliable evaluation of the two-pion contribution to nucleon form factors.
