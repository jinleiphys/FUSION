# Bremsstrahlung of 350--450 MeV protons as a tool to study $NN$ interaction off-shell

> The $pp\to ppγ$ bremsstrahlung cross section is calculated within the method of coordinate space representation. It is shown that in the beam energy range of 350--450~MeV a deep attractive NN-potential with forbidden states (Moscow potential) and realistic meson exchange potentials (MEP) give rise to the cross sections that differ essentially in shape: the cross sections nearly coincide in the minima but differ by a factor of 5 approximately in the maxima. Therefore, the $pp\to ppγ$ reaction at energies $\sim$350--450~Mev can be used to study $NN$ interaction off-shell and to discriminate experimentally between MEP and Moscow potential.

## Key claim

The $pp\to pp\gamma$ bremsstrahlung cross section at beam energies of 350–450 MeV can discriminate between phase-equivalent $NN$ potentials that differ off-shell, such as the deep attractive Moscow potential (MP) and realistic meson exchange potentials (MEP), because the cross sections differ by a factor of approximately 5 in the maxima while nearly coinciding in the minima; this difference is not seen at lower energies (e.g., 280 MeV) where all potentials give similar results.

## Method

The authors calculated the coplanar $pp\to pp\gamma$ cross section using the coordinate space representation formalism, which avoids approximations common in momentum-space approaches. They compared predictions for the Paris potential, the hard-core Hamada–Johnston potential, the Moscow potential, and a supersymmetry partner of MP that is exactly phase-equivalent to MP but lacks the additional node in the wave function at short range. Meson exchange currents were not included in the calculations.

## Key numbers

- At beam energy $\epsilon = 280$ MeV, the $pp\to pp\gamma$ differential cross sections calculated with MP, Paris, and Hamada–Johnston potentials are very similar and describe experimental data well.
- At beam energies of 350–450 MeV, the maximal cross section values for MP and Paris or Hamada–Johnston potentials differ by a factor of several times (approximately a factor of 5 in the maxima).
- For photon angles $30^\circ < \Theta_\gamma < 90^\circ$, the cross section is the same for all potentials at 350–450 MeV.
- The calculations at $\epsilon = 400$ MeV correspond to the kinematical conditions of an experiment started in Osaka (Nomachi, 1997), where the angle between final protons is $26^\circ + 26^\circ = 52^\circ$.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of $pp\to pp\gamma$ that had been restricted to beam energies not exceeding 280 MeV (e.g., experiments by the authors cited as expt3) and had concluded that the difference in cross sections between various $NN$ potentials was too small to be measured (Jäde et al., cited as vonGeramb). The paper shows that at higher energies (350–450 MeV), the off-shell differences between the Moscow potential and meson exchange potentials (such as Paris and Hamada–Johnston) become experimentally distinguishable, enabling a test of $NN$ interaction models that incorporate quark degrees of freedom. The results also indicate that the difference between MP and MEP predictions is larger than the meson exchange contribution even at higher energies (550 MeV) and larger proton angles (de Jong, cited as deJong).
