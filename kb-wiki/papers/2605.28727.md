# Impact of hyperon mixing on neutron star structure based on Skyrme-type equations of state: Systematic analysis of $ΛNN$ and $ΛΛN$ three-body forces with Bayesisan inference

> We study hyperonic density-dependent three-body effects in cold neutron-star matter using a Skyrme energy-density-functional framework. In beta-equilibrated $npeμΛ$ matter, the effective $ΛNN$ and $ΛΛN$ terms are varied separately in the $(β,A_3)$ and $(γ,C_3)$ planes, and each tabulated equation of state is used in Tolman--Oppenheimer--Volkoff calculations. The calculated $P$--$\varepsilon$ branches are classified by monotonicity and extremum structure. The $ΛΛN$ term does not affect the $Λ$-onset condition, but modifies the finite-$Λ$ post-onset EOS: increasing $C_3$ generally stiffens the post-onset branch and raises $M_{\max}$ in mechanically admissible regions, whereas increasing $γ$ reduces this enhancement at fixed $C_3$. In contrast, the $ΛNN$ term shifts the $Λ$-onset density and modifies the post-onset EOS simultaneously, producing organized branch-limited and Maxwell-candidate regions for some reference interactions. Representative two-extrema cases are examined with Maxwell constructions. We also perform an exploratory Bayesian analysis using neutron-star mass--radius information alone and apply XGBoost--SHAP surrogate diagnostics to summarize parameter sensitivities. Within the adopted likelihood and prior ranges, the posterior weight tends to favor sizable hyperonic three-body repulsion, and the SHAP analysis identifies $A_3$ and $C_3$ as important controls of $M_\text{max}$ and $R_{2.0}$. These results show that maximum-mass recovery in hyperonic neutron stars is not a single mechanism: $M_\text{max}$ maps must be interpreted together with onset behavior, branch admissibility, and extremum-count diagnostics. *shortened due to the arXiv's word limit.

# Impact of hyperon mixing on neutron star structure based on Skyrme-type equations of state: Systematic analysis of ΛNN and ΛΛN three-body forces with Bayesian inference

## Key claim

The ΛΛN density-dependent term does not affect the Λ-onset condition but modifies the finite-Λ post-onset equation of state, while the ΛNN term shifts the Λ-onset density and modifies the post-onset EOS simultaneously; maximum-mass recovery in hyperonic neutron stars is not a single mechanism and must be interpreted together with onset behavior, branch admissibility, and extremum-count diagnostics rather than attributed solely to high-density stiffening.

## Method

The authors constructed cold beta-equilibrated npeμΛ matter within a Skyrme energy-density-functional framework and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for each tabulated EOS. The ΛΛN density-dependent term was scanned in the (γ, C₃) plane while the ΛNN density-dependent term was scanned separately in the (β, A₃) plane, with calculated EOS curves classified in the P-ε plane according to monotonicity and extremum structure. An exploratory Bayesian analysis using neutron-star mass-radius information was performed with XGBoost-SHAP surrogate diagnostics to summarize parameter sensitivity of selected observables.

## Key numbers

- The SΛΛ1' reference interaction was used as the primary ΛΛ-sector reference, corresponding to the weaker bond energy assigned to the ¹³_ΛΛB ground state
- SLy230b and BSk24 were used as the two nucleonic Skyrme EDF baselines for the sector-by-sector hyperonic scans
- The SLL4' interaction was used as the primary NΛ reference, with A₀ = -326.0 MeV·fm³, A₁ = 20.50 MeV·fm⁵, A₂ = 20.75 MeV·fm⁵, and A₃ = 705.0 MeV·fm^(3β+3) at β = 1
- The SΛΛ1' baseline has λ₀ = -37.9 MeV·fm³ and λ₁ = 14.1 MeV·fm⁵ with λ₂ = λ₃ = 0
- The XGBoost-SHAP analysis identified A₃ and C₃ as important controls of M_max and R₂.₀, while R₂.₀ exhibited broader multi-parameter sensitivity than M_max

## Context

This work extends recent Skyrme-type studies that constrained ΛΛ and ΛΛN interactions using combinations of hypernuclear and astrophysical information (Sun 2026, Tanimura 2026), and complements Bayesian analyses of hyperonic neutron-star matter in relativistic mean-field models (Malik 2022, Huang 2025). The systematic separation of onset-active (ΛNN) and onset-inactive (ΛΛN) density-dependent terms provides a controlled baseline for interpreting combined hypernuclear and neutron-star constraints, and enables future extensions including systematic coexistence constructions with Gibbs mixed phases and simultaneous variations of both hyperonic sectors.
