# Jet quenching for hadron-tagged jets in $pA$ collisions

> We calculate the medium modification factor $I_{pA}$ for 5.02 TeV $p$+Pb collisions. We use the Monte-Carlo Glauber model to determine the parameters of the quark-gluon plasma fireball in $pA$ jet events. Our calculations show that the jet quenching effect for $I_{pA}$ turns out to be rather small. We have found that the theoretical $I_{pA}$ as a function of the underlying event charged multiplicity density, within errors, agrees with data from ALICE for 5.02 TeV $p$+Pb collisions. However, the experimental errors are too large to draw a firm conclusion on the possible presence of jet quenching.

## Key claim

The medium modification factor $I_{pA}$ for hadron-tagged jets in 5.02 TeV $p$+Pb collisions shows a small jet quenching effect, and while the theoretical $I_{pA}$ as a function of underlying event charged multiplicity density agrees with ALICE data within errors, the experimental uncertainties are too large to draw a firm conclusion on the presence of jet quenching.

## Method

The author calculates the medium modification factor $I_{pA}$ using the light-cone path integral formalism for induced gluon emission, with medium-modified fragmentation functions expressed through the induced gluon spectrum in the independent gluon emission approximation. The parameters of the quark-gluon plasma fireball in $pA$ jet events are determined using a Monte-Carlo Glauber model that accounts for the core-corona structure, where the QGP fireball is defined as regions with local ideal gas temperature above a threshold $T_{min}$ (taken as 160 or 200 MeV). The running QCD coupling $\alpha_s(Q,T)$ uses a parametrization with a plateau around $Q = \kappa T$, where $\kappa = 2.55$ was fitted to LHC data on $R_{AA}$ for Pb+Pb and Xe+Xe collisions.

## Key numbers

* The minimum bias midrapidity charged multiplicity density in 5.02 TeV $p$+Pb collisions from the MC Glauber model is $dN_{ch}^{mb}(p\mathrm{Pb})/d\eta \approx 18.3$, which agrees with the ALICE measurement of $dN_{ch}^{mb}(p\mathrm{Pb})/d\eta \approx 17.8$.
* The underlying event charged multiplicity density in 5.02 TeV $p$+Pb jet events from the MC Glauber model is $dN_{ch}^{ue}(p\mathrm{Pb})/d\eta \approx 33.5$, which agrees with the ALICE value of $dN_{ch}^{ue}(p\mathrm{Pb})/d\eta = 34.19 \pm 1.36$.
* The initial QGP temperature for the ideal gas model is $T_0 \sim 210-220$ MeV at $dN_{ch}^f/d\eta \sim 10$ and $T_0 \sim 330-350$ MeV at $dN_{ch}^f/d\eta \sim 60$.
* The theoretical $I_{pA}$ decreases by about 8% from $dN_{ch}^{ue}/d\eta \sim 10$ to $dN_{ch}^{ue}/d\eta \sim 60$.
* The theoretical normalization of $I_{pA}/\langle I_{pp}\rangle$ is smaller by about 3-4% than the ALICE data, where $\langle I_{pp}\rangle \approx 0.94$ from prior work.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous calculations of $I_{pp}$ (Zakharov, 2021) for $pp$ collisions to $p$+Pb collisions, using the same light-cone path integral approach and running coupling parametrization that was fitted to $R_{AA}$ data for Pb+Pb and Xe+Xe collisions (Zakharov, 2020). The analysis addresses the core-corona structure of the QGP fireball in $pA$ collisions, a concept previously discussed for $AA$ collisions (corona model). The results enable comparison with ALICE data (ALICE, 2023) and suggest that higher-precision measurements are needed to confirm jet quenching in small systems, which would complement evidence from the ridge effect and strange particle production in $pp/pA$ collisions.
