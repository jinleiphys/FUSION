# Pseudoscalar Goldstone Bosons Scattering off Charmed Baryons with Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We have systematically calculated the pseudoscalar Goldstone boson and charmed baryon scattering lengths to the third order with heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. The scattering lengths can reveal the interaction of $K Λ_c$, $K Σ_c$, $πΩ_c$ etc. For each channel, we take into account of the interaction between the external particle and every possible charmed baryon in the triplet, sextet, and excited sextet. We use dimensional regularization and modified minimal subtraction to get rid of the divergences in the loop-diagram corrections. We notice that the convergence of chiral expansion for most of the channels becomes better after we let the low energy constants absorb the analytic contributions of the loop-diagram corrections and keep the nonanalytic terms only.

## Key claim
The paper systematically calculates scattering lengths for pseudoscalar Goldstone bosons scattering off charmed baryons to third order in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, but the convergence of the chiral expansion improves for most channels only when the low-energy constants absorb the analytic loop contributions and only the nonanalytic terms are kept.

## Method
The authors used heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) to compute scattering lengths to O(ε³) for 49 independent channels involving charmed baryons in the triplet, sextet, and excited sextet representations. They employed dimensional regularization with modified minimal subtraction to handle loop-diagram divergences, and determined low-energy constants using quark model symmetry, heavy quark spin symmetry, and SU(4) flavor approximation, with two approximations for the O(ε³) constants: setting them to zero (Approximation I) or letting them absorb all analytic loop corrections (Approximation II).

## Key numbers
- Mass differences between charmed baryon multiplets: δ₁ = M_{B₆*} − M_{B₆} = 67 MeV, δ₂ = M_{B₆} − M_{B_{\bar3}} = 127 MeV, δ₃ = M_{B₆*} − M_{B_{\bar3}} = 194 MeV
- Low-energy constants at O(ε²) from SU(4) flavor approximation: c₀ = −0.79 GeV, c₁ = −0.98 GeV, c₂ = −2.07 GeV − 2α′/(4πf), c₃ = α′/(4πf), c₄ = −0.84 GeV, with α′ in the range [−1,1]
- Axial coupling constants: |g₂| = 0.60, |g₄| = 1.0 from fitting Σ_c and Σ_c* decay widths; |g₁| = √(8/3)|g₂| from quark model symmetry
- Example scattering length for πΣ_c with isospin 2 at O(ε²): a^{(2)}_{πΣ_c} expressed in terms of m_π, f_π, m_{Σ_c}, and LECs
- For φB_{\bar3} channels, the difference between scattering lengths with and without mass differences δ_i is at most 10% of the smaller scattering length in seven out of ten channels
- In 41 out of 49 channels, the difference between Approximation I and Approximation II is at most 20% of the smaller scattering length

## Context
This work extends heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations of meson-baryon scattering to charmed baryons, following similar approaches used for light baryons and charmed mesons by authors such as Baru (2004) and Meissner (2012). The results provide systematic predictions for scattering lengths that can be used for chiral extrapolation of lattice QCD simulations of pion-heavy baryon strong interactions, and may help identify channels with attractive interactions that could form loosely bound molecular states, building on experimental observations of new charmed hadrons from Belle, BABAR, and CDF collaborations.
