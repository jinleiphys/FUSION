# Electromagnetic Couplings of Nucleon Resonances

> An effective Lagrangian calculation of pion photoproduction including all nucleon resonances up to $\sqrt s = 1.7$ GeV is presented. We compare our results to recent calculations and show the influence of different width parametrizations and offshell cutoffs on the photoproduction multipoles. We determine the electromagnetic couplings of the resonances from a new fit to the multipole data.

# Electromagnetic Couplings of Nucleon Resonances

## Key claim
The extracted electromagnetic couplings of nucleon resonances depend heavily on the choice of offshell parameters for spin-3/2 resonances and on width parametrizations, and this problem can only be resolved in a complete calculation that includes rescattering effects. The authors demonstrate that offshell contributions from the N(1520) resonance to the E0+ multipole are of the same order of magnitude as known rescattering effects, meaning that determination of the N(1535) couplings via the E0+ multipole is influenced by the presence of the N(1520).

## Method
An effective Lagrangian calculation of pion photoproduction was performed including all nucleon resonances up to sqrt(s) = 1.7 GeV: Born terms, vector mesons (rho and omega), spin-1/2 resonances (N(1440), N(1535), Delta(1620), N(1650)), and spin-3/2 resonances (Delta(1232), N(1520), Delta(1700)). The electromagnetic couplings were determined by fitting the photoproduction multipole data over the whole energy range, using either resonance masses or pole positions from the Particle Data Group, while systematically varying width parametrizations (two different cutoff functions FG and FM), offshell parameters zi for spin-3/2 resonances, and cutoff factors at the R N pi vertices and u-channel diagrams.

## Context
This work extends the tree-level approach of Garcilazo et al. (1993) by systematically investigating how different width parametrizations and offshell contributions affect extracted electromagnetic couplings, which previous calculations had not examined. The authors note that while unitary calculations including pi N rescattering exist for the Delta(1232) region (Lee, 1991; Davidson et al., 1991) and for the E0+ multipole (Sauer et al., 1994), no combined model extended such unitary calculations up to the third resonance region including all resonances. The results show that offshell parameters for spin-3/2 resonances can compensate for missing rescattering effects, and that a full K-matrix calculation including rescattering is needed to resolve the ambiguities. This work enables future investigations by providing an estimate of the size of different effects and identifying which multipole channels are most sensitive to rescattering.
