# Nucleon and $Δ$ elastic and transition form factors

> We compute nucleon and Delta elastic and transition form factors, and compare predictions made using a framework built upon a Faddeev equation kernel and interaction vertices that possess QCD-like momentum dependence with results obtained using a vector-vector contact-interaction. The comparison emphasises that experiment is sensitive to the momentum dependence of the running couplings and masses in the strong interaction sector of the Standard Model and highlights that the key to describing hadron properties is a veracious expression of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in the bound-state problem. Amongst the results we describe, the following are of particular interest: $G_E^p(Q^2)/G_M^p(Q^2)$ possesses a zero at $Q^2=9.5GeV^2$; any change in the interaction which shifts a zero in the proton ratio to larger $Q^2$ relocates a zero in $G_E^n(Q^2)/G_M^n(Q^2)$ to smaller $Q^2$; and there is likely a value of momentum transfer above which $G_E^n>G_E^p$. Regarding the $Δ(1232)$-baryon, we find that, inter alia: the electric monopole form factor exhibits a zero; the electric quadrupole form factor is negative, large in magnitude, and sensitive to the nature and strength of correlations in the $Δ(1232)$ Faddeev amplitude; and the magnetic octupole form factor is negative so long as rest-frame P- and D-wave correlations are included. In connection with the N-to-Delta transition, the momentum-dependence of the magnetic transition form factor, $G_M^\ast$, matches that of $G_M^n$ once the momentum transfer is high enough to pierce the meson-cloud; and the electric quadrupole ratio is a keen measure of diquark and orbital angular momentum correlations.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a unified QCD-based framework using momentum-dependent interactions can simultaneously describe nucleon and Δ(1232) elastic and transition form factors, while a contact-interaction (CI) framework produces form factors that are too hard. The key hedge is that the Faddeev equation kernels omit resonant contributions (meson-cloud effects), so the computed dressed-quark-core masses are larger than empirical values (m_N=1.18 GeV vs. 0.94 GeV, m_Δ=1.33 GeV vs. 1.23 GeV), and meson-cloud corrections must be added separately.

## Method
The authors solve Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equations for the nucleon and Δ(1232) using a kernel with QCD-like momentum dependence, where two of three dressed quarks are correlated as scalar (0+) and axial-vector (1+) diquarks. They compute electromagnetic currents for elastic and transition form factors using the same propagators and vertices for all baryons, with diquark masses fitted to produce nucleon and Δ masses offset to allow for meson-cloud contributions. Results are compared with those from a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector-vector contact interaction (CI framework).

## Key numbers
- Proton electric-to-magnetic form factor ratio G_E^p(Q^2)/G_M^p(Q^2) has a zero at Q^2 = 9.5 GeV^2
- Dressed-quark-core masses: m_N = 1.18 GeV, m_Δ = 1.33 GeV (empirical: 0.94 GeV and 1.23 GeV)
- Scalar diquark mass m_{[ud]_{0+}} = 0.796 GeV; axial-vector diquark mass m_{(uu)_{1+}} = 0.893 GeV
- Diquark radius r_qq = 0.8 fm used for all diquark form factors
- Proton charge radius (dressed-quark core): r_E^2 = (0.61 fm)^2 (empirical: (0.88 fm)^2)
- Proton magnetic moment (core): μ_p = 2.50 (empirical: 2.79)
- Neutron magnetic moment (core): μ_n = -1.83 (empirical: -1.91)
- Neutron charge radius squared (core): r_E^2 = -(0.28 fm)^2 (empirical: -(0.34 fm)^2)
- Δ(1232) width: 0.12 GeV; mass 30% heavier than nucleon

## Context
This work extends the approach of Cloet (2008) on nucleon electromagnetic form factors to a unified treatment of nucleon and Δ(1232) elastic and transition form factors. It contrasts results from a QCD-kindred framework with those from a contact-interaction framework developed by Wilson (2011), Segovia (2013), and others, showing that experiment is sensitive to the momentum dependence of running couplings and masses in QCD. The predictions are intended to motivate measurements of elastic and transition form factors at high photon virtualities using modern electron-beam facilities like CLAS at Jefferson Lab, where electrocouplings of low-lying N* states have been extracted from meson electroproduction data.
