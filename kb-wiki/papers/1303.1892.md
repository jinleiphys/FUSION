# Relativistic hydrodynamics from Boltzmann equation with modified collision term

> Generalizing the collision term in the relativistic Boltzmann equation to include nonlocal effects, and using Grad's 14-moment approximation for the single-particle distribution function, we derive evolution equations for the relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics and compare them with the corresponding equations obtained in the standard Israel-Stewart and related approaches. Significance of this generalization on hydrodynamic evolution is demonstrated in the framework of one-dimensional scaling expansion.

# Relativistic hydrodynamics from Boltzmann equation with modified collision term

## Key claim

By generalizing the collision term in the relativistic Boltzmann equation to include nonlocal effects (where collisions are allowed to occur at slightly separated spacetime points within a fluid element), the authors derive modified second-order dissipative hydrodynamic equations that differ from the standard Israel-Stewart formulation; the nonlocal corrections produce appreciable deviations in the pressure anisotropy evolution even when the scalar coefficients parameterizing the nonlocality are small (approximately 10% corrections to Navier-Stokes terms and 20% to Israel-Stewart terms).

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic Boltzmann equation and Taylor-expand the collision term to second order in the spacetime separation between incoming and outgoing collisions, introducing momentum-dependent coefficients A^mu and B^{mu nu} parameterized by scalar functions a, b1, b2. Using Grad's 14-moment approximation for the single-particle distribution function in an orthogonal basis, they take the second moment of the modified Boltzmann equation to derive evolution equations for the bulk viscous pressure Pi, the particle diffusion current n^mu, and the shear stress tensor pi^{mu nu}. The resulting equations are solved numerically for a one-dimensional Bjorken scaling expansion of a massless Boltzmann gas at vanishing net baryon density.

## Key numbers

- Initial conditions: proper time tau_0 = 0.9 fm/c, initial temperature T_0 = 360 MeV, specific shear viscosity eta/s = 0.16, initial shear pressure pi_0 = 4 eta/(3 tau_0)
- Temperature decreases monotonically to the crossover temperature T_c approximately 170 MeV at time tau approximately 10 fm/c
- Nonlocal corrections are approximately 10% to Navier-Stokes terms and approximately 20% to Israel-Stewart terms
- The scalar coefficients b1 and b2 vary smoothly to zero at large times, indicating reduced but still significant nonlocal effects at late times

## Context

This work extends the standard Israel-Stewart (Israel 1979) second-order dissipative hydrodynamics by relaxing the assumption of locality in the collision term of the Boltzmann equation, which all previous formulations had strictly assumed. The approach builds on earlier work by Jaiswal (2012) and contrasts with the first-order relativistic Navier-Stokes theory, which suffers from acausality and instability. The results demonstrate that nonlocal effects should be incorporated in transport calculations for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and the method presented is able to generate all possible second-order terms allowed by symmetry.
