# Phase transitions in finite systems = topological peculiarities of the microcanonical entropy surface

> It is discussed how phase transitions of first order (with phase separation and surface tension), continuous transitions and (multi)-critical points can be defined and classified for finite systems from the topology of the energy surface $e^{S(E,N)}$ of the mechanical N-body phase space or more precisely of the curvature determinant $D(E,N)=\partial^2S/\partial E^2*\partial^2S/\partial N^2-(\partial^2S/\partial E\partial N)^2$ without taking the thermodynamic limit. The first calculation of the entire entropy surface $S(E,N)$ for a q=3-states Potts lattice gas on a 50*50 square lattice is shown. There are two lines, where $S(E,N)$ has a maximum curvature $\sim 0$. One is the border between the regions in \{$E,N$\} with $D(E,N)>0$ and with $D(E,N)<0$, the other line is critical starting as a valley in $D(E,N)$ running from the continuous transition in the ordinary q=3-Potts model, converting at $P_m$ into a flat ridge/plateau (maximum) deep inside the convex intruder of $S(E,N)$ which characterizes the first order liquid-gas transition. The multi-critical point $P_m$ is their crossing.

## Key claim

Phase transitions in finite systems can be defined and classified from the topology of the microcanonical entropy surface \(S(E,N)\) and its curvature determinant \(D(E,N)\) without taking the thermodynamic limit, with the caveat that the results are demonstrated with only limited precision due to computational constraints.

## Method

The authors performed microcanonical Monte Carlo simulations of a \(q=3\)-states Potts lattice gas on a \(50\times 50\) square lattice, covering the entire \(\{E,N\}\) space with a mesh of about 1000 knots at spacings \(\Delta\epsilon=0.04\) and \(\Delta n=0.02\). At each knot, approximately \(2\times 10^8\) events were used to construct histograms for the probability of the system being in a narrow region of phase space, from which local derivatives \(\beta = (\partial S/\partial E)_N\) and \(\beta\mu = -(\partial S/\partial N)_E\) were extracted and interpolated to obtain the full entropy surface and its curvatures.

## Key numbers

- The multicritical point \(P_m\) was located at \(\beta_m = 1.48 \pm 0.03\), \(\beta\mu_m = -2.67 \pm 0.02\), or equivalently \(\epsilon_m \sim -1\), \(n_m \sim 0.7\).
- The continuous phase transition of the ordinary \(q=3\)-Potts model occurs at \(n=1\) and \(\epsilon = -1.57\) (near point \(C\)).
- The simulation used a \(50\times 50\) square lattice (\(L^2 = 2500\) sites) with a mesh spacing of \(\Delta\epsilon = 0.04\) and \(\Delta n = 0.02\).
- Approximately \(2\times 10^8\) events were performed at each of the roughly 1000 mesh knots.

## Context

This work extends the microcanonical thermostatics approach previously developed by Gross and collaborators for atomic clusters and nuclei (Gross 1997, Gross 1998a, Gross 1998b) to the \(q=3\) Potts lattice gas model. It directly contradicts the conventional canonical treatment of phase transitions, which requires the thermodynamic limit and loses information about the convex intruder in the entropy surface; the authors note that about half of the phase space is lost in the canonical ensemble. The results enable the study of phase transitions in small and non-extensive systems, and the method can be applied to determine surface tension quantitatively from the microcanonical entropy.
