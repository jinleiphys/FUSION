# Deformation and its influence on $K$ isomerism in neutron-rich Hf nuclei

> We investigate the influence of deformation on the possible occurrence of long-lived $K$ isomers in Hf isotopes around N=116, using configuration-constrained calculations of potential-energy surfaces. Despite having reduced shape elongation, the multi-quasiparticle states in $^{186,188}$Hf remain moderately robust against triaxial distortion, supporting the long expected occurrence of exceptionally long-lived isomers. The calculations are compared with available experimental data.

# Deformation and its influence on $K$ isomerism in neutron-rich Hf nuclei

## Key claim

Despite having reduced quadrupole deformation (smaller shape elongation) compared to the well-known doubly K-magic nucleus 178Hf, the multi-quasiparticle states in 186,188Hf remain moderately robust against triaxial (gamma) distortion, supporting the possible occurrence of exceptionally long-lived isomers at N≈116, but the authors note that uncertainties in residual interactions, single-particle energies, and pairing strengths prevent a definitive conclusion.

## Method

Configuration-constrained potential-energy-surface (PES) calculations were performed using a deformed Woods-Saxon potential with the universal parameter set and Lipkin-Nogami pairing treatment. For each multi-quasiparticle configuration, the total energy was minimized on a (β2, γ, β4) or (β2, β4, β6) deformation lattice by blocking the relevant Nilsson orbits and combining a macroscopic liquid-drop term with a microscopic Strutinsky shell correction.

## Key numbers

- The calculated excitation energy of the unperturbed π² 8⁻ state in 178Hf is 1.35 MeV, reproducing the experimental value of 1.35 MeV.
- The calculated 8⁻ state in 184Hf has an excitation energy of 1.23 MeV, in good agreement with the measured value of 1.26 MeV.
- The 8⁻ state in 190Hf is calculated at an excitation energy of 1.86 MeV, with a prolate shape distinct from the oblate ground state.
- The 4-qp 18⁺ state in 188Hf has a calculated excitation energy close to that of the 16⁻ state in the same nucleus.
- An enhancement of about 10% in pairing strength (relative to the average-gap method) for both protons and neutrons can increase the calculated excitation energy by approximately 400 keV for the ν²₁₀⁻ state.
- The ground states of 188,190Hf have |β₂| < 0.2, with remarkably flat ground-state potential wells against gamma distortion.

## Context

This work extends prior predictions of exceptionally long-lived K isomers in neutron-rich Hf isotopes (Walker 2001, Jain 1995, Xu 2000) and directly addresses the open question of how reduced deformation affects isomer stability. The calculations are compared with experimental data from storage-ring mass measurements (Reed 2010) that observed isomers up to 186Hf (N=114), and with earlier blocked-Nilsson+LN calculations that used fixed deformations. The results suggest that the doubly K-magic character predicted for 188Hf (N=116) remains plausible but requires confirmation through improved treatment of residual spin-spin interactions, single-particle level accuracy, and pairing strengths, as well as future spectroscopic measurements.
