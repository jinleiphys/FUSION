# Low-energy enhancement of the magnetic dipole radiation in odd-mass lanthanides

> We compute the magnetic dipole (M1) $γ$-ray strength functions ($γ$SF) for the odd-mass lanthanides $^{143-151}$Nd and $^{147-153}$Sm using the shell-model Monte Carlo method in combination with the static-path approximation and the maximum-entropy method. In particular, we quantify the statistical uncertainties in the calculated M1 $γ$SFs and show that they are under control for the excitation energies relevant to the experiments despite a Monte Carlo sign problem that originates in the projection onto an odd number of neutrons. We identify a low-energy enhancement (LEE) in the M1 $γ$SFs of these odd-mass lanthanides, which was recently observed experimentally in some of them. We also find a scissors mode resonance (SR) in the strongly deformed isotopes. We observe that the decrease in the LEE strength with neutron number along an isotopic chain is compensated for by an increase in the SR strength in the deformed nuclei. We compare our results with recent experiments.

# Low-energy enhancement of the magnetic dipole radiation in odd-mass lanthanides

## Key claim

The authors identify a low-energy enhancement (LEE) in the M1 gamma-ray strength functions of odd-mass neodymium and samarium isotopes using shell-model Monte Carlo combined with the static-path approximation and maximum-entropy method, but note that for the samarium isotopes the calculated LEE strength at E_gamma near zero is significantly smaller than the strength observed in recent Oslo-method experiments, while the neodymium calculations show overall agreement with experiment.

## Method

The authors computed M1 strength functions for odd-mass 143-151Nd and 147-153Sm using the shell-model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method to calculate imaginary-time response functions, then performed numerical analytic continuation via the maximum-entropy method (MEM) with the static-path approximation (SPA) as a prior strength function. The model space included 40 proton single-particle states (0g7/2, 1d5/2, 1d3/2, 2s1/2, 0h11/2, 1f7/2) and 66 neutron single-particle states (0h11/2, 0h9/2, 1f7/2, 1f5/2, 2p3/2, 2p1/2, i13/2, 1g9/2) with a residual interaction consisting of monopole pairing and multipole terms (lambda = 2, 3, 4). The deexcitation M1 gamma-ray strength function f_M1 was related to the M1 strength function S_M1 through a ratio of level densities, assuming spin-independence and using the spin cutoff model.

## Key numbers

- The M1 strength functions were calculated for inverse temperatures from beta approximately 1.5 MeV^-1 (near neutron separation energy) down to beta approximately 6-7 MeV^-1 (near ground state)
- Lorentzian functions of finite width epsilon = 0.2 MeV were used to produce smooth strength functions from the SPA delta-functions
- Time slices of Delta beta = 1/32 MeV^-1 and 1/64 MeV^-1 were used, with linear extrapolation to Delta beta -> 0
- The scissors mode resonance appears at E_gamma approximately 2-3 MeV in strongly deformed isotopes
- The spin-flip mode dominates the M1 gammaSF at E_gamma approximately 6-9 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous SMMC calculations of M1 gamma-ray strength functions in even-mass samarium isotopes (Fanto 2024) and even-mass neodymium isotopes (Mercenne 2024) to odd-mass systems, where projection onto an odd number of neutrons introduces a Monte Carlo sign problem. The calculations are compared with recent Oslo-method experiments that observed a LEE in odd-mass samarium isotopes (Simon 2016, Naqvi 2019) and in even- and odd-mass neodymium isotopes (Guttormsen 2022). The results enable improved predictions of (n,gamma) cross sections for r-process nucleosynthesis, where a LEE in heavy neutron-rich nuclei could enhance capture rates by more than an order of magnitude (Larsen 2010).
