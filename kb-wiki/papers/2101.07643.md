# Polaron Problems in Ultracold Atoms: Role of a Fermi Sea across Different Spatial Dimensions and Quantum Fluctuations of a Bose Medium

> The notion of a polaron, originally introduced in the context of electrons in ionic lattices, helps us to understand how a quantum impurity behaves when being immersed in and interacting with a many-body background. We discuss the impact of the impurities on the medium particles by considering feedback effects from polarons that can be realized in ultracold quantum gas experiments. In particular, we exemplify the modifications of the medium in the presence of either Fermi or Bose polarons. Regarding Fermi polarons we present a corresponding many-body diagrammatic approach operating at finite temperatures and discuss how mediated two- and three-body interactions are implemented within this framework. Utilizing this approach, we analyze the behavior of the spectral function of Fermi polarons at finite temperature by varying impurity-medium interactions as well as spatial dimensions from three to one. Interestingly, we reveal that the spectral function of the medium atoms could be a useful quantity for analyzing the transition/crossover from attractive polarons to molecules in three-dimensions. As for the Bose polaron, we showcase the depletion of the background Bose-Einstein condensate in the vicinity of the impurity atom. Such spatial modulations would be important for future investigations regarding the quantification of interpolaron correlations in Bose polaron problems.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the spectral function of the medium atoms in Fermi polaron systems can serve as a useful observable for analyzing the transition from attractive polarons to molecules in three dimensions, and that the degree of quantum depletion of a Bose-Einstein condensate around a Bose polaron decreases for repulsive and increases for attractive impurity-medium interactions, in contrast to homogeneous setups where depletion increases independently of the sign of the interaction.

## Method

The authors present a many-body diagrammatic approach using three variants of the T-matrix approximation (TMA, extended TMA, and self-consistent TMA) to compute the finite-temperature spectral function of Fermi polarons in three, two, and one spatial dimensions, incorporating mediated two- and three-body interactions among polarons. For Bose polarons, they employ a real-space Bogoliubov approach to calculate the quantum depletion of the background Bose-Einstein condensate in the vicinity of an impurity atom.

## Key numbers

- At unitarity with temperature \(T=0.3T_{\rm F}\) and impurity concentration \(x=0.1\), the chemical potential of the majority component in the ETMA is \(\mu_{\up}/E_{\rm F}=1-0.526x\), where \(E_{\rm F}=p_{\rm F}^2/(2m_\up)\) is the Fermi energy and \(p_{\rm F}\) is the Fermi momentum.
- The Fermi temperature is defined as \(T_{\rm F}=p_{\rm F}^2/(2m_\up)\).
- The impurity concentration is defined as \(x=N_\down/N_\up\).
- In 3D, the coupling constant \(g_{\rm 3D}\) and scattering length \(a\) are related by \(\frac{m_{\rm r}}{2\pi a}=\frac{1}{g_{\rm 3D}}+\frac{m_{\rm r}\Lambda}{\pi^2}\) with reduced mass \(m_{\rm r}^{-1}=m_{\up}^{-1}+m_{\down}^{-1}\).
- In 2D, the scattering length is \(a_{\rm 2D}=\frac{1}{\Lambda}e^{-\frac{\pi}{m_{\rm r}g_{\rm 2D}}}\).
- In 1D, the scattering length is \(a_{\rm 1D}=\frac{1}{m_{\rm r}g_{\rm 1D}}\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of polaron problems in ultracold atoms by focusing on feedback effects from the medium, building upon prior variational treatments by Chevy (2006) and Combescot (2007) as well as diagrammatic Monte-Carlo simulations by Prokof'ev (2008). It connects to recent experimental observations of medium-induced interactions in Fermi polaron systems by DeSalvo (2019) and Edri (2020), and enables future investigations into interpolaron correlations in both Fermi and Bose polaron problems across different spatial dimensions.
