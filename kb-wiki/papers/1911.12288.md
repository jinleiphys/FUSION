# Evading the model sign problem in the PNJL model with repulsive vector-type interaction via path optimization

> We discuss the sign problem in the Polyakov loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with repulsive vector-type interaction by using the path optimization method. In this model, both of the Polyakov loop and the vector-type interaction cause the model sign problem, and several prescriptions have been utilized even in the mean field treatment. In the path optimization method, integration variables are complexified and the integration path (manifold) is optimized to evade the sign problem, or equivalently to enhance the average phase factor. Within the homogeneous field ansatz, the path is optimized by using the feedforward neural network. We find that the assumptions adopted in previous works, $\mathrm{Re}\,A_8 \simeq 0$ and $\mathrm{Re}\,ω\simeq 0$, can be justified from the Monte-Carlo configurations sampled on the optimized path. We also derive the Euler-Lagrange equation for the optimal path to satisfy. The two optimized paths, the solution of the Euler-Lagrange equation and the variationally optimized path, agree with each other in the region with large statistical weight.

## Key claim

The path optimization method can weaken the model sign problem in the PNJL model with repulsive vector-type interaction, and the Monte-Carlo configurations on the optimized path justify the assumptions of Re A8 ≈ 0 and Re ω ≈ 0 that were previously adopted in mean-field treatments.

## Method

The authors complexified the integration variables (A3, A8, and ω) and optimized the integration path using a feedforward neural network to enhance the average phase factor. They also derived the Euler-Lagrange equation that the optimal path should satisfy, and compared the variationally optimized path with the solution of the Euler-Lagrange equation in the one-site Hubbard model. Within the homogeneous field ansatz, they performed Monte-Carlo calculations on the optimized path for the PNJL model with repulsive vector-type interaction at finite quark chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Average phase factor (APF) with optimization decreases to around 0.6 at μ ≈ 0.35 GeV, where both the vector field and the Polyakov loop grow rapidly
- Without optimization, the APF decays quickly around the transition chemical potential μ = 0.35 GeV
- In the one-site Hubbard model at μ = 0, APF on the optimized path is approximately 4.12 × 10⁻²
- Calculations performed at temperature T = 0.1 GeV and volume factor k = 64
- The transition chemical potential where APF without optimization starts to decay is around μ ≈ 0.3 GeV

## Context

This work extends the path optimization method previously developed by Kashiwa et al. (2019) to the PNJL model with repulsive vector-type interaction, where both the Polyakov loop and the vector interaction cause model sign problems. The approach addresses limitations of the complex Langevin method, which can fail when the drift term distribution has a power-law tail due to zeros of the fermion determinant, and the Lefschetz thimble method, which suffers from residual and global sign problems. The results enable more reliable Monte-Carlo calculations in effective QCD models at finite baryon density, and the subset resummation technique proposed here may help overcome the upper bound on APF from multimodal sign problems.
