# Spectator partons contribution in the rapidity width of lamda in p+p collisions at various SPS and LHC energies

> Pseudorapidity distributions of all primary charged particles produced in p+p collisions at various Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies using UrQMD-3.4 and PYTHIA8.2-generated events are estimated and compared with the existing results of UA5 and ALICE collaborations. With both the sets of generated data, the variation of rapidity widths with the rest masses of different mesons and baryons of p+p collisions at various Super Proton Synchrotron (SPS) and LHC energies are presented. An increase in the width of the rapidity distribution of lambda, similar to heavy-ion data, could be seen in p+p collisions as well from SPS to the LHC energies (up to {sqrt_s} =13 TeV) when the entire rapidity space is considered, indicating its universal feature both in p+p and A+A collisions. The genesis of the increased width of rapidity distribution of Lambda in p+p collision is found to be in the production of light flavored quarks and diquarks from the spectator partons of the colliding hadrons resulting {lambda}/{anti_lambda}>1 at the extreme rapidities of the studied p+p collisions. At the LHC energies for central p+p collision, when there is no spectator partons, the rapidity width of {lambda} is found to take the same Gaussian shape as that of lambda and the widths of both the distributions become the same confirming no non-trivial contribution to the rapidity width of {lambda} as seen in heavy-ion and non-central p+p collisions.

# Spectator partons contribution in the rapidity width of lambda in p+p collisions at various SPS and LHC energies

## Key claim
The increase in the rapidity width of Lambda observed in p+p collisions from SPS to LHC energies (up to sqrt(s)=13 TeV) is caused by the production of light flavored quarks and diquarks from spectator partons of the colliding hadrons, not from spectator hadrons as in heavy-ion collisions. This effect disappears in central p+p collisions where no spectator partons remain, confirming that the jump in rapidity width is a non-trivial, non-kinematic contribution.

## Method
The authors generated 30 to 80 million inelastic p+p events at each studied energy using UrQMD-3.4 and PYTHIA8.2 (with both 4C and Monash tunings) Monte Carlo event generators. They parameterized the rapidity distributions of pions, kaons, protons/antiprotons, phi mesons, Lambda/anti-Lambda, Xi-, and Omega- using double Gaussian functions to extract the rapidity widths, and compared pseudorapidity distributions of primary charged particles with UA5 and ALICE experimental data.

## Key numbers
- Lambda rapidity width at sqrt(s)=13 TeV with UrQMD-3.4: 4.690 +/- 0.009 (double Gaussian excluding extreme peaks), 5.180 +/- 0.003 (triple Gaussian including all three peaks)
- Lambda rapidity width at sqrt(s)=13 TeV with PYTHIA8.2 Monash: 4.489 +/- 0.011 (double Gaussian), 5.085 +/- 0.006 (triple Gaussian)
- Lambda rapidity width at sqrt(s)=20 GeV (Elab) with UrQMD-3.4: 0.728 +/- 0.001
- Lambda rapidity width at sqrt(s)=158 GeV (Elab) with UrQMD-3.4: 1.273 +/- 0.001
- Anti-Lambda rapidity width at sqrt(s)=900 GeV with UrQMD-3.4: 3.487 +/- 0.036 (double Gaussian), 4.55 +/- 0.019 (triple Gaussian)
- Pion rapidity width at sqrt(s)=13 TeV with UrQMD-3.4: 4.267 +/- 0.001
- Pion rapidity width at sqrt(s)=13 TeV with PYTHIA8.2 Monash: 4.291 +/- 0.002

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Deysir and Nursir that found a jump in the rapidity width of Lambda in heavy-ion collisions from AGS to LHC energies, attributed to net baryon density distribution effects and associated production. The ALICE collaboration (2013) reported that the anti-Lambda/Lambda ratio approaches unity at mid-rapidity in p+p collisions at 7 TeV. The present study demonstrates that the same jump in rapidity width occurs in p+p collisions, but originates from spectator partons rather than spectator hadrons as in A+A collisions. This enables future exploration of longitudinal dynamics in small collision systems and suggests that the effect is a universal feature of both p+p and A+A collisions from SPS to LHC energies.
