# Numerical solution of Q^2 evolution equation for the transversity distribution Delta_T q

> We investigate numerical solution of the Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli- Parisi (DGLAP) Q^2 evolution equation for the transversity distribution Delta_T q or the structure function h_1. The leading-order (LO) and next-to- leading-order (NLO) evolution equations are studied. The renormalization scheme is MS or overline{MS} in the NLO case. Dividing the variables x and Q^2 into small steps, we solve the integrodifferential equations by the Euler method in the variable Q^2 and by the Simpson method in the variable x. Numerical results indicate that accuracy is better than 1% in the region 10^{-5}<x<0.8 if more than fifty Q^2 steps and more than five hundred x steps are taken. We provide a FORTRAN program for the Q^2 evolution and devolution of the transversity distribution Delta_T q or h_1. Using the program, we show the LO and NLO evolution results of the valence-quark distribution Delta_T u_v + Delta_T d_v, the singlet distribution sum_i (Delta_T q_i + Delta_T qbar_i), and the flavor asymmetric distribution Delta_T ubar - Delta_T dbar.They are also compared with the longitudinal evolution results.

## Key claim

The authors provide a numerical solution of the DGLAP Q² evolution equation for the transversity distribution Δ_T q (or structure function h₁) at both leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) in the MS or overline{MS} scheme, claiming accuracy better than 1% in the region 10⁻⁵ < x < 0.8 provided more than fifty Q² steps and more than five hundred x steps are taken.

## Method

The integrodifferential DGLAP equation is solved by dividing the variables x and Q² into small steps, using the Euler (brute-force) method for the Q² (t = ln Q²) variable and the Simpson method for the x (or z) integration. The authors implement both linear-step and logarithmic-step options for x and z, and provide a FORTRAN 77 program (H1EVOL) that handles evolution and devolution of up to eight transversity distributions simultaneously, with initial distributions supplied either as function subroutines or as data files.

## Key numbers

* Accuracy better than 1% is achieved in the region 10⁻⁵ < x < 0.8 when more than fifty Q² steps and more than five hundred x steps are taken.
* The program H1EVOL contains 1203 lines of FORTRAN 77 code.
* Typical running time is approximately forty seconds on an AlphaServer 2100 4/200 and two and a half minutes on a SUN-IPX.
* The maximum number of input distributions that can be evolved simultaneously is eight (NFI ≤ 8).
* The number of x steps NX and number of t steps NT are each limited to ≤ 3000.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous numerical studies of DGLAP evolution for unpolarized and longitudinally polarized structure functions (using Laguerre-polynomial and brute-force methods) to the transversity case, which is simpler because the gluon does not couple due to the chiral-odd nature of Δ_T q. The NLO evolution analysis builds on the anomalous dimensions and splitting functions computed by Kumano and Miyama (1997), Vogelsang (1997), and Hayashigaki, Kanazawa, and Koike (1997). The provided FORTRAN program enables researchers involved in the RHIC-Spin project or semi-inclusive lepton scattering experiments to evolve transversity distributions for comparison with future measurements of the double transverse-spin asymmetry A_TT in Drell-Yan processes.
