# Hadronic structure on the light-front V. Diquarks, Nucleons and multiquark Fock components

> This work is a continuation in our series of papers, that addresses quark models of hadronic structure on the light front, motivated by the QCD vacuum structure and lattice results. In this paper we focus on the importance of diquark correlations, which we describe by a quasi-local four-fermion effective 't Hooft interaction induced by instantons. The same interaction is also shown to generate extra quark-antiquark pair of the "sea". Its higher order iteration can be included via "pion-mediation": both taken together yield a quantitative description of the observed flavor asymmetry of antiquarks sea. Finally we discuss the final step needed to bridge the gap between hadronic spectroscopy and parton observables, by forward DGLAP evolution towards the chiral upper scale of $\sim 1\, {\rm GeV}^2$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that instanton-induced 't Hooft interactions, rather than perturbative Coulomb forces, dominate diquark correlations in baryons, and that these same interactions generate the flavor asymmetry of the antiquark sea when combined with pion-mediated processes, providing a bridge between hadronic spectroscopy and parton observables via DGLAP evolution down to a chiral scale of approximately 1 GeV^2.

## Method

The authors use a nonrelativistic variational approach with Gaussian and exponential trial wave functions to compare Coulomb and 't Hooft interactions in diquarks, then extend to light-front Hamiltonians with longitudinal wave equations for diquarks and baryons. For three-quark baryons, they employ a two-parameter Gaussian ansatz in Jacobi coordinates, minimizing the total energy including kinetic, confining, Coulomb, and 't Hooft terms, with the 't Hooft interaction regulated by a Gaussian smearing of the instanton size.

## Key numbers

- Mass difference between vector and scalar ud diquarks: M(1+) - M(0+) ≈ 0.21 GeV, derived from experimental masses of cud and bud baryons
- Lattice estimate of vector-scalar diquark mass difference: m(1+) - m(0+) ≈ 200 MeV
- Lattice diquark size: r0 ≈ 0.6 fm
- 't Hooft coupling needed for diquark binding of ~ -0.2 GeV and size ~0.6 fm: G_qq ≈ 20 GeV^{-2}
- For G_qq = 20 GeV^{-2}, diquark r.m.s. size = 0.56 fm and ground state binding energy E0 - E0(G=0) = -0.187 GeV
- For G_qq = 30 GeV^{-2}, diquark r.m.s. size = 0.50 fm and ground state binding energy E0 - E0(G=0) = -0.318 GeV
- In three-quark baryon variational calculation with strange quark mass m = 0.55 GeV: kinetic energy ≈ 0.76 GeV, confining energy ≈ 0.93 GeV, Coulomb contribution ≈ -0.17 GeV, 't Hooft contribution ≈ -0.28 GeV
- Ground state eigenvalues for longitudinal wave functions: 12.78 (qq pair), 14.23 (sq pair), -8.10 (cq pair), -0.23 (c-ud diquark case)

## Context

This work is the fifth in a series by Shuryak and Zahed (2021-2022) developing light-front quark models of hadronic structure motivated by QCD vacuum structure and lattice results. It extends earlier instanton-based studies of diquark correlations in nucleons by Schafer (1993) and color superconductivity work by Rapp, Alford, and Schafer (1997-2000), while addressing the flavor asymmetry of the antiquark sea that cannot be generated by flavor-blind gluons. The paper enables a systematic connection between spectroscopic quark models and parton distribution functions through chiral dynamics and DGLAP evolution, with the Karliner-Rosner conjecture (2014, 2017) for heavy diquark binding providing a phenomenological benchmark.
