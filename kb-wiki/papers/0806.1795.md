# Nuclear pairing at finite temperature and angular momentum

> An approach is proposed to nuclear pairing at finite temperature and angular momentum, which includes the effects of the quasiparticle-number fluctuation and dynamic coupling to pair vibrations within the self-consistent quasiparticle random-phase approximation. The numerical calculations of pairing gaps, total energies, and heat capacities are carried out within a doubly folded multilevel model as well as several realistic nuclei. The results obtained show that, in the region of moderate and strong couplings, the sharp transition between the superconducting and normal phases is smoothed out, causing a thermal pairing gap, which does not collapse at a critical temperature predicted by the conventional Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer's (BCS) theory, but has a tail extended to high temperatures. The theory also predicts the appearance of a thermally assisted pairing in hot rotating nuclei.

## Key claim

The proposed FTBCS1+SCQRPA theory predicts that, in the region of moderate and strong couplings, the sharp superfluid-normal phase transition is smoothed out, producing a thermal pairing gap that does not collapse at the critical temperature predicted by conventional BCS theory but instead has a tail extending to high temperatures; the theory also predicts the appearance of thermally assisted pairing in hot rotating nuclei.

## Method

The authors extended their self-consistent quasiparticle random-phase approximation (SCQRPA) to finite temperature and finite angular momentum, incorporating both quasiparticle-number fluctuation (QNF) and dynamic coupling to pair vibrations. They solved the pairing Hamiltonian for a system of particles interacting via a pairing force with parameter G, rotating with angular velocity γ and fixed angular momentum projection M, using a variational procedure in the grand canonical ensemble. The resulting equations for the pairing gap, particle number, and total angular momentum include QNF effects, while the quasiparticle occupation numbers are obtained from integral equations involving mass operators derived from double-time quasiparticle Green's functions.

## Key numbers

- Pairing gaps for neutrons in ⁵⁶Fe were calculated and compared with finite-temperature quantum Monte Carlo results from Ref. 8 (boxes and crosses with error bars connected by dash-dotted lines)
- Calculations were performed for the Ω doubly degenerate equidistant model with N=10 particles, as well as for realistic nuclei: ²⁰O, ⁴⁴Ca, ⁵⁶Fe, and ¹²⁰Sn
- For ²⁰O and ⁴⁴Ca, at M higher than the critical value M_c (where the FTBCS gap at T=0 disappears), thermally assisted pairing correlations appear at a given T₁ > 0 and remain finite at T > T₁
- At T=0, the FTBCS and FTBCS1 gaps collapse at M = M_c; with increasing T, FTBCS1 gaps collapse at M > M_c and remain finite even at very high T

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SCQRPA approach (Dang and Hung, 2005) to finite temperature and angular momentum, building on earlier studies of fluctuation effects on nuclear pairing by Moretto (1991), Goodman (1981), and the static path approximation (SPA). The modified BCS (MBCS) theory (Dang and Arima, 1997) first showed that quasiparticle-number fluctuation is the microscopic source of the non-collapsing pairing gap. The present approach improves agreement with exact results for total energies and heat capacities compared to FTBCS1 alone, though for heavy nuclei like ¹²⁰Sn the SCQRPA corrections are negligible relative to FTBCS1 results. The theory enables more accurate predictions of pairing properties in finite nuclei at finite temperature and rotation, particularly for moderate and strong coupling regimes.
