# Dispersive approaches for the HVP and HLbL contributions to $(g-2)_μ$

> Calculations based on the analytic properties of the required matrix elements allow for a wide range of applications constraining the hadronic contributions to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon $a_μ=(g-2)_μ/2$, both hadronic vacuum polarization (HVP) and hadronic light-by-light (HLbL) scattering. Here, we discuss such recent applications, including analyticity constraints on hadronic cross sections, radiative corrections, and isospin-breaking effects.

# Dispersive approaches for the HVP and HLbL contributions to $(g-2)_\mu$

## Key claim
Dispersive techniques, which exploit analytic properties of matrix elements, can constrain hadronic vacuum polarization (HVP) and hadronic light-by-light (HLbL) contributions to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, but the abstract glosses over the fact that for HLbL the dispersive approach is substantially more complicated because it requires reconstructing a hadronic four-point function rather than a two-point function, and that matching the Fermilab precision goal for HLbL requires improving the current precision by a factor of two.

## Method
For HVP, the leading-order contribution is related via a dispersion integral to the total cross section for $e^+e^- \to \text{hadrons}$, with the pion electromagnetic form factor decomposed into an Omnès factor from elastic $\pi\pi$ scattering, an isospin-breaking $3\pi$ cut parameterized by $\omega(782)$ resonance parameters, and a conformal polynomial for inelastic effects such as $4\pi$ intermediate states. For HLbL, the hadronic four-point function $\Pi_{\mu\nu\lambda\sigma} = \langle 0 | T\{j_\mu j_\nu j_\lambda j_\sigma\} | 0 \rangle$ is reconstructed dispersively, with the dominant intermediate states already evaluated and ongoing work on subleading contributions including $\eta$, $\eta'$ poles, axial-vector and tensor contributions, and matching to short-distance constraints.

## Key numbers
- The tension between CMD-3 and the previous experimental average for the $2\pi$ contribution to HVP in the low-energy region evaluates to $\gtrsim 5\sigma$.
- The narrow-resonance estimate for the phase of the $\rho$–$\omega$ mixing parameter $\delta_\epsilon$ is $3.5(1.0)^\circ$.
- The precision of the HLbL contribution needs to be improved by a factor of 2 compared to the reference value from Aoyama (2020) to match the projected final result from the Fermilab experiment.

## Context
This work extends the dispersive framework for HVP that dates back to Bouchiat (1961) and Brodsky (1967), and builds on the HVP evaluation of Aoyama (2020) which used direct integration of cross-section data with constraints from Colangelo (2018) and Hoferichter (2019). For HLbL, it extends the dispersive approach of Colangelo (2014) and Colangelo (2014). The dispersive analysis enables direct comparison to lattice-QCD calculations from Borsanyi (2020) and the RBC/UKQCD collaboration (2018), and suggests that isospin-breaking corrections are unlikely to explain the tension between lattice QCD and phenomenology.
