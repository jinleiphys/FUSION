# The QCD phase diagram and statistics friendly distributions

> The preliminary STAR data for proton cumulants for central collisions at \sqrt{s}=7.7 GeV are consistent with a two-component proton multiplicity distribution. We show that this two-component distribution is statistics friendly in that factorial cumulants of surprisingly high orders may be extracted with a relatively small number of events. As a consequence the two-component model can be tested and verified right now with the presently available STAR data from the first phase of the RHIC beam energy scan.

## Key claim
The preliminary STAR data for proton cumulants in central collisions at sqrt(s)=7.7 GeV are consistent with a two-component proton multiplicity distribution, and this distribution is "statistics friendly" in that factorial cumulants up to eighth order may be extracted with the approximately 150k events already available from the first phase of the RHIC beam energy scan, allowing the model to be tested immediately.

## Method
The authors constructed a two-component proton multiplicity distribution consisting of a dominant binomial distribution (N_max=350, p≈0.1144) with mean ~40 and a small Poissonian component (mean ~25.35) with strength α≈0.0033, which reproduces the first four proton cumulants measured by STAR. They then performed Monte Carlo sampling of 150k events from this distribution to extract the expected statistical errors on factorial cumulants up to ninth order, comparing the results to those from single binomial or negative binomial distributions.

## Key numbers
- The two-component distribution has parameters: α ≈ 0.0033, binomial component with N_max=350 and p≈0.1144, Poissonian component with mean ⟨N_small⟩=25.3525
- For efficiency corrected data with 150k events, the predicted factorial cumulants and their relative statistical errors are: C5 = -2645 (1±0.14), C6 = 40900 (1±0.18), C7 = -615135 (1±0.26), C8 = 8520220 (1±0.42)
- For efficiency uncorrected data with 150k events: C5 = -307 (1±0.31), C6 = 3085 (1±0.41), C7 = -30155 (1±0.61), C8 = 271492 (1±1.06)
- The factorial cumulants alternate in sign and increase in magnitude by a factor of approximately 17 from order to order

## Context
This work extends the two-component model proposed by Bzdak (2018) to explain the non-monotonic behavior of proton cumulants observed by STAR at sqrt(s)=7.7 GeV, which shows a large positive ratio of fourth-order to second-order cumulant. The authors demonstrate that the two-component hypothesis can be tested immediately with existing STAR data from BES-I, rather than waiting for higher statistics from BES-II. If confirmed, this would provide evidence for a system with two "phases" near a first-order phase transition, which could be further explored by measuring flow and other observables as discussed by Bzdak (2018).
