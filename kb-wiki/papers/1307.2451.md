# Chiral Transition Within Effective Quark Models under Strong Magnetic Fields

> In the recently years it has been argued that spectators in heavy ion collisions are responsible for creating a strong magnetic field that could play an important role in the QCD phase transition. In this work we use the SU(2) Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in order to study the chiral transition in quark matter subject to a strong magnetic field. We show some results involving the breaking of chiral symmetry and its restoration at finite temperature and density.

## Key claim

Using the SU(2) Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in mean-field approximation, the crossover pseudo-temperature at zero chemical potential always increases with magnetic field strength, which contrasts with recent lattice QCD results suggesting it may decrease.

## Method

The authors evaluate the Landau free energy for the SU(2) NJL model with three colors, including vacuum, magnetic, and medium contributions, using a momentum cutoff of 590 MeV and coupling constant G = 2.44/Λ². The free energy is minimized via the gap equation to obtain the constituent quark mass, from which the pressure, net quark number density, entropy density, and energy density are derived. Phase boundaries are determined by analyzing the free energy as a function of temperature and chemical potential for different magnetic field values.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff Λ = 590 MeV used for the vacuum integral in the NJL model.
- Coupling constant fixed at G = 2.44/Λ².
- Magnetic field values are given in units of m_π²/e (no explicit numerical values for B are provided in the text).
- The critical end point shifts toward higher T and lower μ as B increases (no explicit numerical values given).
- The crossover pseudo-temperature at μ = 0 always increases with B (no explicit numerical values given).

## Context

This work extends prior NJL model studies of the QCD phase diagram by incorporating strong magnetic fields, following the approach of Debora (year not specified) for the Landau free energy formulation. The results contradict recent lattice QCD calculations that suggest the crossover pseudo-temperature may decrease with magnetic field, leaving open the question of whether magnetic fields increase or decrease the chiral crossover temperature. The study enables further investigation of magnetic field effects on the QCD phase diagram, particularly the oscillatory behavior of baryon density and constituent quark mass due to Landau level filling.
