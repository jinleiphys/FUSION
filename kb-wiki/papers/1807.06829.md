# Role of system size on freezeout conditions extracted from transverse momentum spectra of hadrons

> The data on hadron transverse momentum spectra in different centrality classes of p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}_{NN} = 5.02$ TeV has been analysed to extract the freezeout hypersurface within a simultaneous chemical and kinetic freezeout scenario. The freezeout hypersurface has been extracted for three different freezeout schemes that differ in the way strangeness is treated: i. unified freezeout for all hadrons in complete thermal equilibrium (1FO), ii. unified freezeout for all hadrons with an additional parameter $γ_S$ which accounts for possible out-of-equilibrium production of strangeness (1FO$+γ_S$), and iii. separate freezeout for hadrons with and without strangeness content (2FO). Unlike in heavy ion collisions where 2FO performs best in describing the mean hadron yields as well as the transverse momentum spectra, in p+Pb we find that 1FO$+γ_S$ with one less parameter than 2FO performs better. This confirms expectations from previous analysis on the system size dependence in the freezeout scheme with mean hadron yields: while heavy ion collisions that are dominated by constituent interactions prefer 2FO, smaller collision systems like proton + nucleus and proton + proton collisions with lesser constituent interaction prefer a unified freezeout scheme with varying degree of strangeness equilibration.

## Key claim

In p+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}_{NN} = 5.02\) TeV, the freezeout scheme with a unified freezeout for all hadrons plus a strangeness suppression factor \(\gamma_S\) (1FO+\(\gamma_S\)) provides a better description of transverse momentum spectra than a scheme with separate freezeout surfaces for strange and non-strange hadrons (2FO), contrary to the pattern observed in larger Pb+Pb collision systems where 2FO is preferred.

## Method

The authors analyzed ALICE data on transverse momentum spectra of \(\pi^{+}+\pi^{-}\), K\(^{+}\)+K\(^{-}\), p+\(\bar{\mathrm{p}}\), \(\phi\), \(\Lambda\)+\(\bar{\Lambda}\), \(\Xi\)+\(\bar{\Xi}\) and \(\Omega\)+\(\bar{\Omega}\) in seven centrality classes of p+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}_{NN} = 5.02\) TeV. They used the THERMINATOR event generator with the Krakow model (constant proper-time freezeout hypersurface) to fit the spectra within three freezeout schemes: 1FO (unified freezeout, three parameters T, \(\rho_{max}\), \(\tau_f\)), 1FO+\(\gamma_S\) (adding a strangeness non-equilibrium parameter \(\gamma_S\)), and 2FO (separate freezeout parameters for strange and non-strange hadrons, six parameters total). The goodness of fit was evaluated via \(\chi^2/ndf\) for data points up to \(p_T = 2.5\) GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- For the 0-5% centrality class, the total \(\chi^2/ndf\) values were: 1FO = 622/131, 1FO+\(\gamma_S\) = 464/130, 2FO = 432/128.
- For the 60-80% centrality class, the total \(\chi^2/ndf\) values were: 1FO = 2179/131, 1FO+\(\gamma_S\) = 866/130, 2FO = 1544/128.
- The extracted temperature T in the 1FO+\(\gamma_S\) scheme ranges from 158 MeV (0-5% central) to 153 MeV (80-100% peripheral), with an average error of 2 MeV.
- The extracted \(\gamma_S\) parameter in the 1FO+\(\gamma_S\) scheme ranges from 0.94 (0-5% central) to 0.74 (80-100% peripheral), with an average error of 0.02.
- The freezeout proper time \(\tau_f\) in the 1FO+\(\gamma_S\) scheme ranges from 2.7 fm (0-5% central) to 1.9 fm (80-100% peripheral), with approximately 15% error.
- The maximum transverse size \(\rho_{max}\) in the 1FO+\(\gamma_S\) scheme ranges from 3.8 fm (0-5% central) to 1.7 fm (80-100% peripheral), with approximately 15% error.

## Context

This work extends the system-size dependence study of freezeout schemes previously performed on hadron yields by Chatterjee (2016) to the analysis of transverse momentum spectra. The earlier yield analysis found that 2FO (separate freezeout for strange and non-strange hadrons) is preferred in Pb+Pb collisions, while 1FO+\(\gamma_S\) is preferred in p+Pb and p+p collisions. The present spectra analysis confirms this pattern for p+Pb, and connects to the prior Pb+Pb spectra analysis by Chatterjee (2014) where 2FO was found to describe the data better than 1FO. The results support a physical picture where large systems with more constituent interactions favor flavor-dependent freezeout, while small systems with rapid expansion favor a unified freezeout with varying strangeness equilibration.
