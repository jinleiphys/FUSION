# Counting hot/cold spots in quark-gluon plasma

> We study how local fluctuations in the initial states of relativistic heavy-ion collisions manifest themselves in the correlations between different orders of harmonic moments of the density profiles, particularly those involving only odd harmonics which purely arise from initial state fluctuations. We find the strengths of those correlations are sensitive to the number of hot and cold spots in the initial states. Hydrodynamic evolution of the fireball translates initial state geometric anisotropies as well as their correlations into final state momentum anisotropies and correlations. We conclude that the measurement of the correlations between different harmonic moments of final state azimuthal distribution can be employed to quantify the inhomogeneity of the initial density profiles such as the population of hot and cold spots that are produced in high energy nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the strength of correlations between different odd harmonic moments of the final-state azimuthal distribution is sensitive to the number of hot and cold spots in the initial state of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and that measuring these correlations can quantify the inhomogeneity of the initial density profile. This claim is hedged by the assumption of linear hydrodynamic response and the use of a simplified model where initial fluctuations are represented as discrete hot and cold spots of fixed radius.

## Method

The authors model initial-state fluctuations by adding a small number of circular hot and cold spots (radius 0.25 fm) on top of an event-averaged optical Glauber profile for Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV. They compute the initial spatial eccentricities ε_n and event-plane angles Φ_n, then define correlation functions C^(k) = ⟨cos[n₁Φ_{n₁} + n₂Φ_{n₂} + ... + n_kΦ_{n_k}]⟩ for combinations of odd harmonics with zero sum. These correlations are evaluated as a function of the number of hot spots for two spatial distributions (hard sphere and Glauber model) and compared to results from full Monte-Carlo Glauber simulations.

## Key numbers

- Hot spot radius R_h = 0.25 fm.
- Relative probabilities of hot and cold spots: P_{s_i}(1) = 35% and P_{s_i}(-1) = 65%, chosen to match C^(3)(2,3,-5) ≈ 0.1 from Monte-Carlo Glauber modeling at b = 0 fm with about one hundred hot spots.
- For Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, the Woods-Saxon nuclear radius parameter R_ws = 6.62 fm and diffusion parameter d = 0.546 fm; inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section σ_NN = 64 mb; parameter α = 0.11 for charged hadron multiplicity.
- For anisotropic hot spot distributions, impact parameter b = 12 fm.
- Correlation functions C^(4) increase from central to peripheral collisions, indicating a decrease from about one hundred hot spots in central collisions to just a few in very peripheral collisions.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of initial-state fluctuations and their hydrodynamic translation to final-state flow harmonics, particularly the observation of odd harmonic flows at RHIC and the LHC (Alver 2010, Petersen 2010, Qin 2010). It builds on the cumulant expansion approach of Teaney and Yan (2010) and the correlation studies of Staig (2010) and Bhalerao (2011). The paper enables experimental extraction of the number of hot and cold spots in the quark-gluon plasma through measurement of four-harmonic correlations, which would provide a new constraint on initial-state models such as the Color Glass Condensate.
