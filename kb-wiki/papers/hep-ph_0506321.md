# Freeze-out of strange hadrons at RHIC

> The production and freeze-out conditions of strange particles, produced in Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies, are studied within microscopic transport model. The system of final particles can be represented as a core, containing the particles which are still interacting with each other, and a halo, in which the particles have already decoupled from the system. In microscopic calculations hadrons are continuously emitted from the whole reaction volume. At RHIC, however, significant fractions of both mesons and baryons are emitted from the surface region within the first two fm/c. Different species decouple at different times. Strange mesons (kaons and $ϕ$) are frozen at earlier times and, therefore, can probe earlier stages of the reaction.

## Key claim
The quark-gluon string model (QGSM) predicts that there is no sharp freeze-out of particles at RHIC; instead, hadrons are emitted continuously from the entire reaction volume, with different species decoupling at different times. The abstract's claim that "strange mesons are frozen at earlier times" is qualified by the finding that significant fractions of both mesons and baryons are emitted from the surface region within the first two fm/c, and that the freeze-out order (pions, kaons, lambdas, nucleons) holds for energies from AGS to RHIC.

## Method
The authors used the quark-gluon string model (QGSM) to simulate Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 130\) AGeV with impact parameter \(b = 8\) fm. They analyzed the phase-space distributions of final-state hadrons (pions, kaons, \(\phi\), lambdas, and nucleons) over their last elastic and inelastic collision points, integrating over variables to study three-dimensional distributions in time, transverse radius, and transverse momentum. The freeze-out conditions were determined by tracking the last collision time for each particle species.

## Key numbers
- Average freeze-out time for mesons (kaons and pions): \(\langle t^{\rm mes} \rangle \approx 6\) fm/\(c\)
- Average freeze-out time for baryons (nucleons and lambdas): \(\langle t^{\rm bar} \rangle \approx 13\) fm/\(c\)
- Width of \(dN/dt\) distribution for mesons: \(\Delta t^{\rm mes}\approx 8\) fm/\(c\)
- Width of \(dN/dt\) distribution for baryons: \(\Delta t^{\rm bar}\approx 14\) fm/\(c\)
- Bulk production of mesons takes place within 8–10 fm/\(c\)
- Collision energy: \(\sqrt{s} = 130\) AGeV for Au+Au collisions with \(b = 8\) fm

## Context
This work extends earlier freeze-out studies at AGS and SPS energies (Bravina et al., 2004) and contrasts with the assumption of a sharp freeze-out surface in ideal hydrodynamic models (Landau, 1953). The results justify the implementation of continuous freeze-out in 3+1 dimensional hydrodynamic models (Soff et al., 2004), which improved description of two-particle interferometry data at RHIC. The findings also enable better understanding of elliptic flow evolution, where mesons emitted from the surface within the first few fm/c carry the strongest flow while baryons develop hydro-like flow through later rescatterings.
