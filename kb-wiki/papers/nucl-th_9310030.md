# Quasi-Two-Body Decays of Nonstrange Baryons

> We examine the decays of nonstrange baryons to the final states $Δπ$, $Nρ$, $Nη$, $Nη^\prime$, $Nω$, $N1/2^+(1440)π$, and $\Delta3/2^+(1600)π$, in a relativized pair-creation($^3P_0$) model which has been developed in a previous study of the $Nπ$ decays of the same baryon states. As it is our goal to provide a guide for the possible discovery of new baryon states at CEBAF and elsewhere, we examine the decays of resonances which have already been seen in the partial-wave analyses, along with those of states which are predicted by the quark model but which remain undiscovered. The level of agreement between our calculation and the available widths from the partial-wave analyses is encouraging.

## Key claim

The relativized pair-creation (³P₀) model, previously tested on Nπ decays, provides a useful guide for discovering new baryon states at CEBAF by predicting decay amplitudes to quasi-two-body final states, with the caveat that the level of agreement with available partial-wave analysis widths is "encouraging" but not perfect, and the model systematically underestimates couplings to N(1440)π and Δ(1600)π for lighter baryons.

## Method

The authors computed strong decay amplitudes for nonstrange baryon resonances decaying to Δπ, Nρ, Nη, Nη′, Nω, N(1440)π, and Δ(1600)π using a relativized ³P₀ pair-creation model with wave functions from a Schrödinger-like equation solved in a harmonic oscillator basis. For broad daughter resonances (ρ, Δ, N(1440)), they replaced the Dirac δ-function in the decay width formula with a Breit-Wigner energy denominator to account for the finite width of the unstable daughter, integrating over the momentum of the decay products. The model parameters were fixed from prior work: pair-creation strength γ = 2.6, baryon gaussian parameter α = 0.5 GeV, meson gaussian parameter β = 0.4 GeV, and a phenomenological constant κ = 0.35 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Pair-creation strength γ = 2.6 (dimensionless model parameter)
- Gaussian parameter for baryon wave functions α = 0.5 GeV
- Gaussian parameter for meson wave functions β = 0.4 GeV
- For the N(1520)D₁₃ state, the Nρ decay amplitude at the central mass is 2.5 MeV¹/², but increases to 9.0 MeV¹/² when the mass is increased by 150 MeV, corresponding to a factor of more than 12 in the decay width
- The predicted state [N½⁺]₄(1880) has sizeable Δπ amplitudes, making it a candidate for discovery in the γp → Δ⁺⁺π⁻ experiment proposed in CEBAF proposal 93-033
- The largest partial width into Δ(1600)π or N(1440)π channels for any state in the N ≤ 2 bands is about 16 MeV
- The predicted state [N½⁻]₅(2070) has a Δπ amplitude in excess of 13 MeV¹/²

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of Nπ decays (Capstick and Roberts, 1993) and the baryon spectrum model of Capstick and Isgur (1986) to additional decay channels relevant to the CEBAF N* program. It builds on earlier calculations by Koniuk and Isgur (1980) for Δπ and Nη decays, Koniuk (1982) for Nρ and Nω decays in the narrow-width approximation, and Stancu and Stassart (1988-1993) using a flux-tube-breaking model for Nρ and Nω channels. The results are compared primarily to the partial-wave analysis of the Nππ final state by Manley and Saleski (1992). The paper enables experimental searches for "missing" baryon resonances (predicted by quark models but unseen in Nπ analyses) by identifying which quasi-two-body channels and mass ranges offer the best discovery potential at CEBAF's CLAS detector.
