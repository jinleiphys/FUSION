# Deep inelastic scattering from A=3 nuclei and the neutron structure function

> We present a comprehensive analysis of deep inelastic scattering from He-3 and H-3, focusing in particular on the extraction of the free neutron structure function, F_2^n. Nuclear corrections are shown to cancel to within 1-2% for the isospin-weighted ratio of He-3 to H-3 structure functions, which leads to more than an order of magnitude improvement in the current uncertainty on the neutron to proton ratio F_2^n/F_2^p at large x. Theoretical uncertainties originating from the nuclear wave function, including possible non-nucleonic components, are evaluated. Measurement of the He-3 and H-3 structure functions will, in addition, determine the magnitude of the EMC effect in all A < 4 nuclei.

# Deep Inelastic Scattering from A=3 Nuclei and the Neutron Structure Function

## Key claim

Nuclear corrections cancel to within 1-2% for the isospin-weighted ratio of ³He to ³H structure functions, enabling more than an order of magnitude improvement in the current uncertainty on the neutron to proton ratio F₂ⁿ/F₂ᵖ at large x, provided that the measurement is performed and that the theoretical uncertainties from nuclear wave functions and possible non-nucleonic components are as small as evaluated.

## Method

The authors calculate deep inelastic structure functions for ³He and ³H within the nuclear impulse approximation, using nucleon spectral functions derived from realistic three-body wave functions. Two independent approaches are employed to compute the three-nucleon spectral functions: solving the homogeneous Faddeev equation with separable potentials (PEST, Reid Soft Core, and Yamaguchi) and using a variational technique with the Reid Soft Core interaction. The ratio of EMC-type ratios for the two mirror nuclei is then inverted to extract the free neutron to proton structure function ratio F₂ⁿ/F₂ᵖ.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear corrections cancel to within 1-2% for the isospin-weighted ratio of ³He to ³H structure functions for x < 0.8
- The Faddeev calculation yields tri-nucleon binding energies of -7.266 MeV (PEST) and -8.047 MeV (Yamaguchi), compared to the experimental ³H binding energy of -8.482 MeV
- The variation in the A=3 EMC effect can be up to 5% at large x, while the deviation from unity of the ratio R is typically less than 1%
- Beyond x ~ 0.5, nuclear corrections in deuterium can result in uncertainties of up to ~50% in F₂ⁿ/F₂ᵖ

## Context

This work extends the method proposed at a workshop (WKSHP) for extracting the neutron structure function from A=3 mirror nuclei, providing a detailed theoretical analysis of nuclear corrections that were previously only presented as preliminary results (Afnan). The approach addresses the long-standing problem that the EMC effect in A=2 and A=3 systems remains unknown 20 years after the original EMC observation (EMC), despite extensive studies for A > 4 (GST). The method offers an alternative to techniques such as semi-inclusive DIS from deuterium with spectator proton detection (BONUS, SPEC), parity-violating electron scattering (PARITY), and weak boson production at colliders (W, HERACC), and would not be subject to low rates or reliance on factorization assumptions. A measurement at Jefferson Lab with 12 GeV electron energy is discussed as a future experimental opportunity.
