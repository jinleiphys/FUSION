# Connecting structure functions on the lattice with phenomenology

> We examine the extraction of moments of parton distributions from lattice data, focusing in particular on the chiral extrapolation as a function of the quark mass. Inclusion of the correct chiral behavior of the spin-averaged isovector distribution resolves a long-standing discrepancy between the lattice moments and experiment. We extract the x-dependence of the valence u-d distribution from the lowest few lattice moments, and discuss the implications for the quark mass dependence of meson masses lying on the rho Regge trajectory. The role of chiral symmetry in spin-dependent distributions, and in particular the lattice axial vector charge, g_A, is also highlighted.

## Key claim

Inclusion of the correct chiral behavior (nonanalytic terms in the quark mass) in the extrapolation of lattice QCD moments of parton distributions resolves a long-standing discrepancy where linearly extrapolated lattice moments were typically ~50% larger than experimental values; the resolution depends on the mass scale parameter μ, which cannot be determined from current data and is fitted in the range ~400 to 700 MeV.

## Method

The authors fit lattice QCD data for the lowest moments (n=1,2,3) of the spin-averaged isovector parton distribution u-d using an extrapolation formula that combines the leading nonanalytic chiral behavior (m_π² log m_π) from chiral perturbation theory with a term that enforces the heavy-quark limit (moments → 1/3^n). They then reconstruct the x-dependence of the valence u_v-d_v distribution from the fitted moments using a standard parameterization with Regge-theory constraints, and extend the analysis to predict the quark-mass dependence of meson masses on the ρ Regge trajectory and to examine the axial vector charge g_A.

## Key numbers

- The leading nonanalytic coefficient c_LNA = -(1 + 3 g_A²)/(4π f_π)², with g_A the axial vector charge and f_π the pion decay constant.
- The mass scale μ in the chiral extrapolation is fitted in the range ~400 to 700 MeV, with a central value of 550 MeV.
- The exponent β governing small-x behavior of u_v-d_v from global fits is approximately 0.48.
- The ρ Regge trajectory is predicted at m_π = 0.785 GeV (strange quark mass) and compared with the physical pion mass trajectory.
- Lattice data for g_A, when extrapolated linearly, are ~10-15% lower than the experimental value; the chiral extrapolation using Eq. (xtrap_gA) produces a larger discrepancy.

## Context

This work extends the chiral extrapolation method of Thomas et al. (2000) and Detmold et al. (2001) for lattice moments of parton distributions, resolving a discrepancy noted in earlier quenched and dynamical lattice calculations by the QCDSF and MIT groups. It enables the reconstruction of valence quark distributions from limited lattice moments and predicts the quark-mass dependence of meson masses on the ρ Regge trajectory, which can be tested by future lattice simulations of excited hadrons (e.g., UKQCD Collaboration). The analysis also highlights unresolved issues in the lattice calculation of the axial vector charge g_A, where finite volume effects (noted in domain wall fermion simulations) may be significant.
