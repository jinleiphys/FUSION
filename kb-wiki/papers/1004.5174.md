# Near Threshold Enhancement of p pbar System and p pbar Elastic Scattering

> The observed enhancement of $p\bar p$-production near the threshold in radiative decays of $J/ψ$ and $e^+e^-$-annihilations can be explained with final state interactions among the produced $N\bar N$ system, where the enhancement is essentially determined by $N\bar N$ elastic scattering amplitudes. We propose to use an effective theory for interactions in a $N\bar N$ system near its threshold. The effective theory is similar to the well-known one for interactions in a $NN$ system but with distinctions. It is interesting to note that in the effective theory some corrections to scattering amplitudes at tree-level can systematically be summed into a simple form. These corrections are from rescattering processes. With these corrected amplitudes we are able to describe the enhancement near the threshold in radiative decays of $J/ψ$ and $e^+e^-$-annihilations, and the $p\bar p$ elastic scattering near the threshold.

# Near Threshold Enhancement of p pbar System and p pbar Elastic Scattering

## Key claim

The observed enhancement of p pbar production near threshold in J/psi radiative decays and e+e- annihilations can be explained by final state interactions in the N Nbar system, where the enhancement is essentially determined by N Nbar elastic scattering amplitudes. The authors propose an effective field theory for N Nbar interactions near threshold that can simultaneously describe the enhancement and p pbar elastic scattering, but only when certain higher-order rescattering corrections are summed beyond tree-level; tree-level results alone can only fit data in a very small energy region (E ≤ 20 MeV) with poorly determined coupling constants.

## Method

The authors construct a nonrelativistic effective field theory for N Nbar interactions near threshold, analogous to the effective theory for NN systems but with complex coupling constants to account for annihilation into pions. They use a minimal subtraction scheme (rather than power divergence subtraction) because N Nbar scattering lengths are small (around 1 fm or smaller). The tree-level amplitudes from contact interactions and one-pion exchange are computed, and certain higher-order corrections from multiple rescattering processes (where intermediate N Nbar pairs are on-shell) are summed into a geometric series. A combined fit is performed to BES data for J/psi → gamma p pbar, Babar data for e+e- → p pbar, and PDG data for p pbar elastic scattering cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Scattering lengths from the fit (in fm): a_{[0000,0]} = -1.80(19) + 0.32(17)i, a_{[0000,1]} = 2.61(81) + 0.84(41)i, a_{[1001,0]} = -0.48(46) - 0.25(89)i, a_{[1001,1]} = -1.07(41) + 0.19(29)i
- Coupling constants from the fit (in GeV^{-2}): c_0 = 123(15) - 22(11)i, c_1 = -177(55) - 57(27)i, d_0 = 32(31) + 17(60)i, d_1 = 73(28) - 13(19)i
- Form factor combinations: A_{p pbar} + A_{n nbar} = 0.23(37), A_{p pbar} - A_{n nbar} = 2.22(72); G_M^{(p)} + G_M^{(n)} = 1.18(47), G_M^{(p)} - G_M^{(n)} = -0.11(20)
- chi^2/d.o.f. = 50/63 for the combined fit
- With summed rescattering corrections, BES and Babar data can be fitted up to E ≤ 150 MeV; p pbar elastic scattering cross section data up to E ≤ 70 MeV
- Tree-level results can only fit data in the region E < ~20 MeV
- The BES resonance mass is around 1861 MeV with width Gamma < 38 MeV
- Pion decay constant F_pi ≈ 93 MeV; axial coupling g_A ≈ 1.25

## Context

This work extends the approach of Chen, Dong, and Ma (2009) which used a rescattering mechanism with fixed amplitudes from multi-pion exchange to explain the enhancement in J/psi → gamma p pbar and e+e- → p pbar, but could not simultaneously describe p pbar elastic scattering. The present paper provides an effective field theory framework that unifies both phenomena. The effective theory is analogous to the NN effective theory of Weinberg (1990), van Kolck (1994), and the EFT approach of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998), but adapted for N Nbar systems where scattering lengths are much smaller than in NN systems, allowing use of minimal subtraction rather than power divergence subtraction. The results are comparable with LEAR experiment scattering length determinations and with potential model results from the literature. The work enables future studies of complete one-loop corrections including dispersive parts from pion exchanges, and raises the question of whether a resonance or structure exists below the p pbar threshold.
