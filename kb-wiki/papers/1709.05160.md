# Static response, collective frequencies and ground state thermodynamical properties of spin saturated two-component cold atoms and neutron matter

> The thermodynamical ground-state properties and static response in both cold atoms at or close to unitarity and neutron matter are determined using a recently proposed Density Functional Theory (DFT) based on the s-wave scattering length $a_s$, effective range $r_e$, and unitary gas limit. In cold atoms, when the effective range may be neglected, we show that the pressure, chemical potential, compressibility modulus and sound velocity obtained with the DFT are compatible with experimental observations or exact theoretical estimates. The static response in homogeneous infinite systems is also obtained and a possible influence of the effective range on the response is analyzed. The neutron matter differs from unitary gas due to the non infinite scattering length and to a significant influence of effective range which affects all thermodynamical quantities as well as the static response. In particular, we show for neutron matter that the latter response recently obtained in Auxiliary-Field Diffusion Monte-Carlo (AFDMC) can be qualitatively reproduced when the p-wave contribution is added to the functional. Our study indicates that the close similarity between the exact AFDMC static response and the free gas response might stems from the compensation of the $a_s$ effect by the effective range and p-wave contributions. We finally consider the dynamical response of both atoms or neutron droplets in anisotropic traps. Assuming the hydrodynamical regime and a polytropic equation of state, a reasonable description of the radial and axial collective frequencies in cold atoms is obtained. Following a similar strategy, we estimate the equivalent collective frequencies of neutron drops in anisotropic traps.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a recently proposed Density Functional Theory (DFT) based on s-wave scattering length, effective range, and the unitary gas limit can reproduce ground-state thermodynamics of cold atoms and neutron matter, and that the close similarity between the exact AFDMC static response of neutron matter and the free gas response may stem from compensation of the scattering length effect by the effective range and p-wave contributions, rather than from weak interactions.

## Method

The authors use a five-parameter density functional (Eq. 1) that generalizes the Bertsch parameter for finite s-wave scattering length \(a_s\) and effective range \(r_e\), with parameters fixed by imposing asymptotic limits in the low-density regime and unitary limit. They compute ground-state thermodynamical quantities (pressure, chemical potential, compressibility, sound velocity) as derivatives of the energy with respect to density, and calculate the static response function \(\chi(q)\) using the Lindhard function with a density-dependent coefficient \(G(\rho)\) derived from the second derivative of the potential energy density. For neutron matter, they add the leading-order p-wave contribution \(E_p/E_{\rm FG} = (1/\pi)(a_p k_F)^3\) to the functional.

## Key numbers

- Bertsch parameter \(\xi_0 = 0.376\) at the unitary limit (from Ref. Ku12, For12)
- Parameters at unitarity: \(\eta_e = 0.127\), \(\delta_e = -0.055\) (from Ref. Ku12, For12)
- For cold atoms with \(r_e=0\), the functional parameters include \(U_0 = 0.62400\), \(U_1 = 1.76432\), \(R_0 = 0.12700\), \(R_1 = 1.54722\), \(R_2 = 0.43307\)
- The maximal value of \(\xi\) at unitarity with effective range is \(\xi_0 - \eta_e^2/\delta_e = 0.66\), almost twice \(\xi_0\)
- For neutron matter, \(a_s = -18.9\) fm and \(r_e = 2.716\) fm; values \(-(a_s k_F) = 10, 20, 30\) correspond respectively to \(\rho = 0.005, 0.040, 0.135\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- For AFDMC comparison, \(a_s = -19.295\) fm and \(r_e = 2.716\) fm (AV8 interaction), with p-wave scattering volume \(a_p^3 = 0.0916\) fm
- Static response calculations performed at densities \(\rho = 0.04\) fm\(^{-3}\) and \(\rho = 0.1\) fm\(^{-3}\)

## Context

This work extends the DFT framework developed by Lacroix (2016, 2017) that connects cold atoms and neutron systems, and directly compares to ab-initio AFDMC static response calculations by Buraczynski (2016, 2017) for neutron matter. The authors show that empirical Skyrme EDF (using Sly4, SkM*, SkP, SIII parameter sets) gives static responses with significant differences from the exact AFDMC results, while their functional provides much better agreement. The study enables future work on dynamical response of neutron drops in anisotropic traps and suggests that the static response is highly sensitive to effective range properties, motivating better constraints on \(\eta_e\) and \(\delta_e\) parameters.
