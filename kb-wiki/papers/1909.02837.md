# Study of QCD dynamics using small systems

> The multiplicity, finite system size and collision energy dependencies of heat capacity ($C_V$), conformal symmetry breaking measure (CSBM) and speed of sound ($c_s$) have been investigated using ALICE data for $p+p$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 7 TeV. The aim of this study is to ascertain the possibility of formation of a thermalized medium in such collisions. We find that there is a threshold in charged particle multiplicity beyond which $C_V$, CSBM and $c_s$ attain plateau. The presence of such threshold in multiplicity is further reflected in the variation of these quantities with center-of-mass energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$). In order to have a grasp on experimentally obtained results, variation of $\langle p_T \rangle$ with multiplicity has also been studied. The experimental results have been contrasted with PYTHIA8 and it is found that PYTHIA8 is inadequate to explain the features reflected in these quantities, thereby indicating the possibility of thermalization in such small system. It is also observed that the finite size effects alone cannot explain the non-extensive nature of particle spectra in $p+p$ collisions.

# Study of QCD dynamics using small systems

## Key claim

The authors find a threshold in charged-particle multiplicity in proton-proton collisions at 7 TeV beyond which heat capacity, conformal symmetry breaking measure, and speed of sound attain a plateau, suggesting possible thermalization, but only if the observed deviations from PYTHIA8 simulations (which lack thermalization) are interpreted as evidence of medium formation rather than other non-thermal mechanisms.

## Method

The authors extract Tsallis non-extensive distribution parameters (temperature T and non-extensive parameter q) by fitting the transverse momentum spectra of pions, kaons, K*0, and protons from ALICE data and from PYTHIA8 simulations of proton-proton collisions at 7 TeV. Using these fitted parameters, they compute thermodynamic quantities, heat capacity, conformal symmetry breaking measure (trace anomaly), and speed of sound, via integrals over the Tsallis distribution functions. They compare the multiplicity dependence of these quantities between experimental data and PYTHIA8, which incorporates multi-partonic interactions and color reconnection but no thermalization.

## Key numbers

- Charged-particle pseudorapidity densities range from 2.26 ± 0.12 to 21.3 ± 0.6 across ten multiplicity classes in proton-proton collisions at 7 TeV.
- For pions, the extracted temperature T ranges from 0.068 ± 0.001 GeV (lowest multiplicity) to 0.093 ± 0.001 GeV (highest multiplicity) from ALICE data.
- For pions, the non-extensive parameter q ranges from 1.143 ± 0.001 (lowest multiplicity) to 1.163 ± 0.001 (highest multiplicity) from ALICE data.
- For protons, the extracted temperature T ranges from 0.017 ± 0.001 GeV (lowest multiplicity) to 0.183 ± 0.006 GeV (highest multiplicity) from ALICE data.
- The saturation in C_V/T^3 and C_V/⟨n_i⟩ occurs for charged-particle pseudorapidity density greater than approximately 8 for pions and kaons.

## Context

This work extends the study of QCD thermodynamics to small systems formed in proton-proton collisions, building on earlier investigations of heat capacity in heavy-ion collisions by Basu (2016) and Li (2007). It contrasts with the traditional use of proton-proton collisions as a baseline for heavy-ion studies, motivated by observations of long-range ridge correlations in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions that mimic collective behavior. The results challenge PYTHIA8's ability to reproduce thermodynamic observables, suggesting that finite system size alone cannot explain the non-extensive nature of particle spectra, and open the question of whether thermalization occurs in small systems at LHC energies.
