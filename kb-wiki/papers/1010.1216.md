# Meson screening masses from lattice QCD with two light and the strange quark

> We present results for screening masses of mesons built from light and strange quarks in the temperature range of approximately between 140 MeV to 800 MeV. The lattice computations were performed with 2+1 dynamical light and strange flavors of improved (p4) staggered fermions along a line of constant physics defined by a pion mass of about 220 MeV and a kaon mass of 500 MeV. The lattices had temporal extents Nt = 4, 6 and 8 and aspect ratios of Ns / Nt \geq 4. At least up to a temperature of 140 MeV the pseudo-scalar screening mass remains almost equal to the corresponding zero temperature pseudo-scalar (pole) mass. At temperatures around 3Tc (Tc being the transition temperature) the continuum extrapolated pseudo-scalar screening mass approaches very close to the free continuum result of 2 πT from below. On the other hand, at high temperatures the vector screening mass turns out to be larger than the free continuum value of 2 πT. The pseudo-scalar and the vector screening masses do not become degenerate even for a temperature as high as 4Tc. Using these mesonic spatial correlation functions we have also investigated the restoration of chiral symmetry and the effective restoration of the axial symmetry. We have found that the vector and the axial-vector screening correlators become degenerate, indicating chiral symmetry restoration, at a temperature which is consistent with the QCD transition temperature obtained in previous studies. On the other hand, the pseudo-scalar and the scalar screening correlators become degenerate only at temperatures larger than 1.3Tc, indicating that the effective restoration of the axial symmetry takes place at a temperature larger than the QCD transition temperature.

# Meson screening masses from lattice QCD with two light and the strange quark

## Key claim

The pseudo-scalar screening mass remains almost equal to the zero-temperature pseudo-scalar (pole) mass at least up to 140 MeV, and at temperatures around 3Tc the continuum-extrapolated pseudo-scalar screening mass approaches the free continuum result of 2πT from below, while the vector screening mass remains larger than 2πT at high temperatures; the pseudo-scalar and vector screening masses do not become degenerate even at 4Tc, and effective axial symmetry restoration (degeneracy of pseudo-scalar and scalar screening correlators) occurs only at temperatures above 1.3Tc, which is higher than the QCD transition temperature where chiral symmetry restoration (vector/axial-vector degeneracy) is observed.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with 2+1 dynamical flavors of improved (p4) staggered fermions along a line of constant physics defined by a pion mass of about 220 MeV and a kaon mass of 500 MeV, using lattices with temporal extents Nt = 4, 6, and 8 and aspect ratios Ns/Nt ≥ 4. They computed spatial meson correlation functions from point-to-point and wall sources, extracting screening masses for pseudo-scalar, scalar, vector, and axial-vector channels built from light (u/d) and strange quarks, and analyzed degeneracies between channels to study chiral and axial symmetry restoration.

- At temperatures around 3Tc, the continuum-extrapolated pseudo-scalar screening mass approaches the free continuum value of 2πT from below.
- The vector screening mass at high temperatures is larger than the free continuum value of 2πT.

- Vector and axial-vector screening correlators become degenerate at a temperature consistent with the QCD transition temperature Tc.
- Pseudo-scalar and scalar screening correlators become degenerate only at temperatures larger than 1.3Tc.
- The temperature range studied extends from approximately 140 MeV to 800 MeV.
- Zero-temperature pion mass is about 220 MeV; zero-temperature kaon mass is about 500 MeV.
- Bare strange quark mass is 10 times the bare light quark mass (ms = 10 ml).

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of meson screening masses in quenched and full QCD (reviewed in Karsch and references therein) by using 2+1 flavor dynamical simulations with improved p4 staggered fermions, smaller lattice spacings, and multiple temporal extents to address discretization effects. It builds on earlier studies with 4 flavors using standard staggered actions (detar) and on quenched studies with improved Wilson fermions (Wissel) and overlap fermions (overlap). The results enable tests of dimensionally reduced perturbation theory (Appelquist, Mikko) and resummed perturbation theory (Alberico), and clarify the temperature scales for chiral versus axial symmetry restoration in QCD.
