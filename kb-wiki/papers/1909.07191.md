# Improvement of heavy flavor productions in a multi-phase transport model updated with modern nPDFs

> Recently we have updated a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model with modern parton distribution functions of nuclei (nPDFs). Here we study open charm production in the updated AMPT model and compare to the experimental data from $pp$ and $AA$ collisions over a wide range of collision energies. Besides the update of nPDFs, we have removed the transverse momentum cutoff on initial heavy quark productions and also included the resultant heavy flavor cross section into the total minijet cross section in the initial condition as described by the HIJING model. We show that the AMPT model with these updates provides a much better description of the yields and transverse momentum spectra of various open charm hadrons in comparison with the experimental data. This lays the foundation for further heavy flavor studies within the transport model approach.

## Key claim

The updated AMPT model, which removes the transverse momentum cutoff on initial heavy quark productions and includes heavy flavor cross sections in the total minijet cross section, provides a much better description of open charm hadron yields and transverse momentum spectra compared to the old AMPT model, though the agreement with experimental data is not perfect and shows systematic discrepancies at high transverse momentum and in heavy-ion collisions at LHC energies.

## Method

The authors modified the AMPT model by removing the $p_0$ transverse momentum cutoff for heavy quark production cross sections and including the heavy flavor cross section into the total minijet cross section in the HIJING initial condition, as described by Eq. (6). They used the string melting version of AMPT with modern CTEQ6.1M parton distribution functions and EPS09s nuclear shadowing functions, and employed a spatial quark coalescence model for hadronization with a heavy flavor baryon-to-meson ratio parameter $r_{BM}=1.0$ and vector-to-pseudo-scalar meson ratios of 1.0 for $D^*/D$ and $B^*/B$.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass set to $m_c = 1.3$ GeV/$c^2$ throughout the study
- $K$-factor $K = 2.5$ used for both light and heavy flavor productions
- Heavy flavor $r_{BM}$ parameter set to 1.0 (compared to 0.53 for light flavors) to reproduce $\Lambda_c$ measurements in $pp$ collisions at LHC
- Parton elastic scattering cross section set to $\sigma_p = 3$ mb and hadron cascade cutoff time to 30 fm/$c$
- In central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV, the integrated midrapidity $D^0$ yield at $p_T \geq 1.0$ GeV/$c$ from AMPT gives 3.3 compared to the ALICE data value of 6.0
- Nuclear shadowing leads to approximately 50% suppression of the rapidity density of charm quarks for central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV
- The updated AMPT model gives charm quark yields at mid-rapidity in central $AA$ collisions that are higher than the old AMPT by a factor of approximately 5

## Context

This work extends the recent updates to the AMPT model by Zhang et al. (2019) that introduced modern nPDFs (CTEQ6.1M and EPS09s) and an improved quark coalescence process by He et al. (2017). The study enables systematic comparisons of model predictions with measured heavy flavor $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ to determine QGP transport coefficients such as the spatial diffusion constant $D_s$, drag coefficient $\eta_D$, and momentum transport coefficients $\kappa_L$, $\kappa_T$, $\hat{q}$. The authors note that the current AMPT model uses the $g+g \rightarrow g+g$ cross section for scatterings between all parton flavors, and improvements should treat parton scatterings between different flavors differently to better describe charm $p_T$ spectra data.
