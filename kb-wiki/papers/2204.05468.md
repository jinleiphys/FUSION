# Centre Vortices in the Presence of Dynamical Fermions

> The behaviour of centre vortices in the presence of dynamical fermions is studied for the first time in the context of the static quark potential and the Landau-gauge gluon propagator. These results indicate that in the presence of dynamical fermions, centre vortices are able to better encapsulate the long-range behaviour of QCD, compared to previous studies on pure Yang-Mills lattices. This work provides strong evidence that centre vortices are responsible for the long-range linear potential and the enhanced infrared gluon propagator, which are indicative of confinement in QCD.

# Centre Vortices in the Presence of Dynamical Fermions

## Key claim
Centre vortices can quantitatively capture the full string tension of the static quark potential when dynamical fermions are present, in contrast to pure Yang-Mills studies where vortices only reproduce approximately 62% of the string tension. This improvement is observed even at an unphysically large pion mass of 701 MeV, suggesting dynamical fermions fundamentally alter the vortex structure beyond simple screening effects.

## Method
The authors identify centre vortices on three ensembles of 200 lattice configurations each: two (2+1) flavour dynamical ensembles from the PACS-CS collaboration and one pure-gauge ensemble with matched lattice spacing. They construct vortex-only (VO) fields by projecting links onto the SU(3) centre elements and vortex-removed (VR) fields by dividing out the centre phase, then compute the static quark potential via variational analysis of Wilson loops and the Landau-gauge gluon propagator in momentum space.

## Key numbers
- Vortex-only string tension on pure-gauge ensembles sits at approximately 62% of the original string tension
- Dynamical fermion ensembles studied at pion masses of m_pi = 701 MeV and m_pi = 156 MeV
- Gluon propagator renormalisation function Z(q^2) set to 1 at a renormalisation scale of mu = 5.5 GeV
- Lattice volume for the dynamical ensembles is 32^3 x 64

## Context
This work extends previous pure Yang-Mills centre vortex studies (Langfeld 2003, Bowman 2010, Biddle 2018) which showed that vortices qualitatively reproduce confinement properties but consistently underrepresent the string tension. The results contradict the expectation that dynamical fermions would only introduce screening effects; instead, they dramatically improve the vortex model's quantitative agreement with full QCD. This enables future investigations into the microscopic mechanism by which dynamical fermions modify the vortex vacuum structure, and raises questions about the origin of the residual infrared strength in vortex-removed gluon propagators observed in pure-gauge studies (Biddle 2018).
