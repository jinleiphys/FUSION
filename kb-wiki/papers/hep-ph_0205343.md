# Gluon Saturation and S-Matrix Unitarity

> The impact parameter dependent gluon distribution of the proton xG(x,Q^2,b) is investigated in a loop-loop correlation model that respects the S-matrix unitarity condition in impact parameter space. We find low-x saturation of G(x,Q^2,b) as a manifestation of S-matrix unitarity. The integrated gluon distribution xG(x,Q^2) does not saturate because of the growth of the effective proton radius with decreasing x.

## Key claim

The impact parameter dependent gluon distribution of the proton saturates at low x as a direct consequence of S-matrix unitarity in impact parameter space, but the integrated gluon distribution does not saturate because the effective proton radius grows with decreasing x.

## Method

The authors compute the impact parameter dependent gluon distribution xG(x,Q^2,b) from the profile function for longitudinally polarized virtual photon-proton scattering in a loop-loop correlation model (LLCM). The LLCM combines perturbative two-gluon exchange with non-perturbative interactions from the stochastic vacuum model, using a matrix cumulant expansion and Gaussian approximation of functional integrals to enforce S-matrix unitarity. The loop-loop correlation function S_DD is expressed in terms of cosine functions of energy-dependent perturbative and non-perturbative components, with the cosine terms describing multiple gluonic interactions that ensure unitarity.

## Key numbers

- Saturation of xG(x,Q^2,b=0) occurs at x less than approximately 10^{-10} for Q^2 greater than or approximately equal to 1 GeV^2
- The perturbative energy-dependence exponent is epsilon^pert = 0.73 and the non-perturbative exponent is epsilon^nprt = 0.125
- The black disc limit for the profile function is reached at center-of-mass energies sqrt(s) greater than or approximately equal to 10^8 GeV for Q^2 = 1 GeV^2
- The saturation value of the impact parameter dependent gluon distribution is approximately Q^2/(pi^2 alpha_s)

## Context

This work extends the Berger-Nachtmann approach (Berger 1999) and the stochastic vacuum model of Dosch (1987) to describe gluon saturation as a consequence of S-matrix unitarity, in contrast to approaches where saturation arises from nonlinear evolution equations. The model provides a unified description of pp, gamma* p, and gamma gamma reactions and is consistent with lattice computations of the gluon field strength correlator (Bali 1998). The predicted saturation scale at x less than 10^{-10} is far below the x-regions accessible at HERA (x greater than 10^{-6}) and THERA (x greater than 10^{-7}), enabling future tests at higher-energy colliders.
