# Cluster Production in Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> Cluster production in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collision may prove an interesting tool to extract physical quantities of the fireball.

## Key claim

Cluster production in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions can be used to extract fireball properties such as freeze-out temperature, but only if the formation process is understood and the coalescence mechanism properly accounts for flow and source geometry effects. The abstract's promise of a "tool" is conditional on resolving contradictions between thermal models and data, which the authors address by incorporating a Wigner function coalescence approach with a gaussian source parametrization.

## Method

The authors assume an emission function with gaussian source parametrization and local thermal equilibrium distributions for nucleons in the hot central reaction region. Using a Wigner function coalescence mechanism, they derive a formula for invariant momentum spectra of clusters of A nucleons, incorporating flow-induced correlations between momentum and spatial coordinates that modify the effective source volume. They then apply this model to compute coalescence factors B_A and extract freeze-out temperatures from cluster yield ratios, comparing results from a gaussian versus box profile for the transverse fireball geometry.

## Key numbers

- For central Pb+Pb collisions at the SPS, with coalescence corrections exva{cx{2}} ≈ exva{cx{3}} ≈ 0.7, the coalescence factors are B_2 ≈ 8 × 10^{-4} GeV^2 and B_3 ≈ 4 × 10^{-7} GeV^2.
- From the ratio S_{p/d} in the thermal model, the freeze-out temperature is 120.7 ± 1.8 MeV; from S_{p-bar/d-bar} it is 117.1 ± 3.1 MeV; from S_{d/t} it is 135.8 ± 9.3 MeV; from S_{d-bar/t} it is 139.5 ± 4.9 MeV.
- In the authors' model, the freeze-out temperature from S_{p-bar/d-bar} is 144 MeV and from S_{d-bar/t} is 146 MeV, showing consistency across different particle ratios.
- The effective temperature T_* is given by T_* = T + (M/A) η_f^2, where M/A = m (the nucleon mass), making T_* independent of cluster mass in the gaussian model.

## Context

This work extends coalescence models for cluster production in heavy ion collisions by incorporating flow effects and a Wigner function formalism, building on earlier emission function parametrizations and HBT analysis. The authors resolve a contradiction in the thermal model used by NA52 (1997) for minimum bias Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon, where different particle ratios gave substantially different freeze-out temperatures. The approach enables extraction of freeze-out temperature and chemical potentials from cluster yield ratios, with a more realistic box profile for transverse fireball geometry under development in a follow-up study.
