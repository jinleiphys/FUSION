# A novel invariant mass method to isolate resonance backgrounds from the chiral magnetic effect

> The Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) refers to charge separation along a strong magnetic field, due to topological charge fluctuations in QCD. Charge correlation ($Δγ$) signals consistent with CME have been first observed almost a decade ago. It has also been known since then that the $Δγ$ is contaminated by a major background from resonance decays coupled with elliptic flow. In this contribution, we propose differential $Δγ$ measurements as function of the pair invariant mass ($m_{\rm inv}$). The $Δγ$ in the high $m_{\rm inv}$ region is essentially free of resonance backgrounds. In the low $m_{\rm inv}$ region, the $Δγ$ backgrounds show resonance peaks. The CME signal, presumably smooth in $m_{\rm inv}$, may thus be extracted from a two-component model fit. We demonstrate the feasibility and effectiveness of this novel method by using the AMPT and toy-model Monte-Carlo simulations. We also discuss an application of the method in data analysis.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that differential measurements of the charge correlation observable Δγ as a function of pair invariant mass (m_inv) can isolate the chiral magnetic effect (CME) signal from resonance decay backgrounds, with the caveat that the CME signal is assumed to be a smooth function of m_inv while the background shows resonance peaks.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model and toy-model Monte-Carlo simulations of Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV to demonstrate that Δγ(m_inv) in the high m_inv region (above 2 GeV/c²) is essentially free of resonance backgrounds. They then applied a two-component model fit to extract a possible CME signal from low m_inv data, and further developed an event-shape engineering technique using event-by-event q₂ classification to determine the background shape without assuming its exact functional form.

## Key numbers

- AMPT simulation of Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV in impact parameter range 6.8 < b < 8.2 fm shows Δγ at m_inv > 2 GeV/c² is consistent with zero (no CME in AMPT)
- A cut of m_inv > 2 GeV/c² corresponds to pion pair mean p_T of approximately 1.2 GeV/c
- The two-component model fit was demonstrated with a 20% input CME signal in toy-model simulations
- STAR data analysis used 20-50% Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV with event-shape engineering

## Context

This work extends the understanding that the Δγ correlator, first observed almost a decade ago as a CME signal, is contaminated by resonance decay backgrounds coupled with elliptic flow as noted by Voloshin (2004) and later quantified by Wang (2016). The invariant mass method enables extraction of the CME signal from low m_inv data through two-component model fitting, and the event-shape engineering technique (developed by Schukraft in 2012) provides a data-driven way to determine the background shape without assuming its functional form.
