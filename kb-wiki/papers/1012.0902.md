# Radiative corrections in fermion bags bound by Higgs boson exchange

> Radiative corrections for several heavy fermions bound together via the Higgs boson exchange are studied. The fermion bags considered include 12, or fewer, fermions occupying the lowest S_{1/2} shell. It is shown that for `moderately heavy' fermions with masses 0.4< m c^2< 1 TeV the radiative corrections are small, 10^{-2}, and have an attractive nature. Therefore they do not put existence of the fermion bag in doubt. This proves that these fermion bags can exist in nature.

# Radiative corrections in fermion bags bound by Higgs boson exchange

## Key claim

For fermion bags consisting of up to 12 fermions in the lowest S_{1/2} shell, radiative corrections are small (∼10⁻²) and attractive for fermion masses between 0.4 and 1 TeV, meaning these corrections do not destabilize the bag. The paper shows that previous concerns about radiative corrections destabilizing the vacuum for fermion masses above 2 TeV do not apply to the mass range considered here.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic mean-field approximation to construct self-consistent equations for the Higgs field ξ(r) and fermion wave functions ψ(r) in a spherically symmetric bag. They then compute one-loop radiative corrections by evaluating the renormalized Higgs vertex correction (fermion self-energy) and the renormalized Higgs polarization operator (fermion loops), expanding in powers of k²/m² and m_H²/m² under the assumptions that the bag radius R satisfies mR ≫ 1 and m_H ≪ m. The method is adapted from techniques used to calculate the Lamb shift in atomic physics.

## Key numbers

- Fermion mass range considered: 0.4 ≲ m c² ≲ 1 TeV
- Radiative correction magnitude: ∼10⁻² of the binding energy
- Lower threshold for bag formation: m_th ≈ 320 GeV (for N=12, m_H = 100 GeV)
- Bag radius for m = 0.35 TeV: R ≈ 10 TeV⁻¹, giving mR ≈ 3.5
- Bag radius for m = 0.6 TeV: R ≈ 5 TeV⁻¹, giving mR ≈ 3
- For large m, bag radius reaches m-independent value R₀ = (N/2π)^{1/2} v⁻¹ ≈ 1.6√N TeV⁻¹
- Higgs vacuum expectation value: v = 246 GeV
- Radiative correction estimate: δϵ_rc/ϵ_B ≈ (γ − ν/20) / (2πN), where γ is a logarithmic coefficient and ν is the number of heavy fermions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Kuchiev 2008, Kuchiev 2010) which showed that fermion bags can form for fermions substantially heavier than the top quark, with a lower mass boundary of 320–410 GeV depending on Higgs mass. It addresses earlier concerns (Dimopoulos 1990, Bagger 1991, Farhi 1998) that radiative corrections from strong fermion-Higgs coupling might destabilize the vacuum for fermion masses above 2 TeV. The paper shows similarities with the atomic Lamb shift calculation, where the vertex correction gives repulsion and vacuum polarization gives attraction, but notes that in fermion bags the large number of heavy fermions (up to ν=11 for the complete fourth generation plus top quark) enhances the attractive polarization contribution. The results enable the possibility that fourth-generation fermions with masses 300–500 GeV, suggested to explain CP violation in B decays (B 2009), could form observable fermion bags.
