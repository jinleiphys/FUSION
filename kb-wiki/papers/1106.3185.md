# Static and Dynamic Chain Structures in the Mean-Field Theory

> We give a brief overview of recent work examining the presence of $α$-clusters in light nuclei within the Skyrme-force Hartree-Fock model. Of special significance are investigations into $α$-chain structures in carbon isotopes and $^{16}$O. Their stability and possible role in fusion reactions are examined in static and time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations. We find a new type of shape transition in collisions and a centrifugal stabilization of the $4α$ chain state in a limited range of angular momenta. No stabilization is found for the $3α$ chain.

## Key claim
The paper finds that a 4α linear chain state in ¹⁶O can be stabilized by centrifugal forces in a limited range of angular momenta, but no such stabilization is found for the 3α chain in ¹²C. The 3α chain appears only as a transient, metastable intermediate in collisions, not as a statically stable configuration.

## Method
Static stability was studied via cranked Hartree-Fock calculations with three Skyrme forces (SLy6, SkI3, SkI4) on a Cartesian grid with 0.8 fm spacing, using a twisted 4α initial wave function. Dynamical formation and decay of chain structures were studied using fully three-dimensional time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations for ⁴He+⁸Be, triple ⁴He, and ⁸Be+⁸Be collisions, with no symmetry assumptions and using the SLy4 interaction.

## Key numbers
- For the 4α linear chain in ¹⁶O with the SkI3 interaction, stabilization occurs between rotational frequencies of 1.8 and 2.2 MeV/ℏ, corresponding to angular momenta of about 13 and 18 ℏ.
- For SkI4, stabilization occurs between 1.9 and 2.2 MeV/ℏ (angular momenta 14–18 ℏ); for SLy6, between 2.0 and 2.1 MeV/ℏ (angular momenta 16–18 ℏ).
- The rigid-body moment of inertia coefficient ℏ²/2Θ for the stabilized 4α chain is 0.065 MeV for SkI3 and SkI4, and 0.06 MeV for SLy6.
- In ⁴He+⁸Be collisions at E_c.m. = 2 MeV and β = 0°, the linear chain survival time is about 2500 fm/c; at the lowest energy for chain formation (1.3 MeV) the lifetime increases to about 2875 fm/c.
- The potential barrier for the ⁴He+⁸Be system ranges from 1.24 to 1.44 MeV depending on the ⁸Be alignment angle β.
- Using ⁹Be instead of ⁸Be reduces the linear chain lifetime by approximately 30%.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of α-chain structures in carbon isotopes (Itagaki 2004, Maruhn 2006) and the 4α chain in ¹⁶O suggested by Chevallier (1967) and Suzuki (1976). It goes beyond conventional small-vibration analyses (Horiuchi 1977) by using a large model space and fully dynamical TDHF calculations. The results enable future studies of the role of α-chain configurations as intermediate states in fusion reactions and in the astrophysical formation of ¹²C, and demonstrate that centrifugal stabilization of the 4α chain is a unique feature of ¹⁶O not found in 3α systems.
