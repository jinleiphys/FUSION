# Shear Viscosity of Quark-Gluon Plasma in Weak Magnetic Field in Perturbative QCD: Leading Log

> We compute the shear viscosity of two-flavor QCD plasma in an external magnetic field in perturbative QCD at leading log order, assuming that the magnetic field is weak or soft: $eB\sim g^4\log(1/g)T^2$. We show that the shear viscosity takes a form $η=\barη(\bar B)T^3/(g^4\log(1/g))$ with a dimensionless function $\barη(\bar B)$ in terms of a dimensionless variable $\bar B=(eB)/(g^4\log(1/g)T^2)$. The variable $\bar B$ corresponds to the relative strength of the effect of cyclotron motions compared to the QCD collisions: $\bar B\sim l_{mfp}/l_{cyclo}$. We provide a full numerical result for the scaled shear viscosity $\barη(\bar B)$.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity of two-flavor QCD plasma in a weak external magnetic field at leading-log order takes the form $\eta = \bar\eta(\bar B) T^3 / (g^4 \log(1/g))$, where the dimensionless variable $\bar B = (eB)/(g^4 \log(1/g) T^2)$ measures the relative strength of cyclotron motion compared to QCD collisions; this result holds under the assumption that the magnetic field is parametrically weak ($eB \sim g^4 \log(1/g) T^2$) and that electrodynamics is non-dynamical (the formal limit $e \to 0$ with $eB$ fixed).

## Method

The authors solve the linearized Boltzmann equation for quarks, antiquarks, and gluons in the presence of a homogeneous static external magnetic field, using the leading-log QCD collision term from t-channel soft gluon and soft fermion exchanges. The magnetic field enters only through the Lorentz-force advective term, while the collision term remains unchanged because the cyclotron time scale is comparable to the mean-free path. The disturbance $\delta f$ is expanded in the gradient $\partial_\perp v_z$, and the shear viscosity is extracted from the resulting energy-momentum tensor component $T^{\perp z} = -\eta \, \partial_\perp v_z$.

## Key numbers

* The shear viscosity scaling is $\eta = \bar\eta(\bar B) \, T^3 / (g^4 \log(1/g))$, where $\bar B = (eB) / (g^4 \log(1/g) T^2)$.
* The magnetic field is assumed weak: $eB \sim g^4 \log(1/g) T^2 \ll T^2$.
* The relaxation time for transverse velocity is $\tau_R = (\epsilon + p) / (\sigma B^2)$, where $\sigma$ is the conductivity.
* The cyclotron length scale is $l_{cyclo} \sim p/(eB) \sim (g^4 \log(1/g) T)^{-1}$, and the mean-free path is $l_{mfp} \sim (g^4 \log(1/g) T)^{-1}$, so $\bar B \sim l_{mfp} / l_{cyclo}$.
* The screening mass (Debye mass) is $m_D \sim gT$, with corrections from the magnetic field suppressed by $\sqrt{eB}/T \sim g^2$.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD computation of shear viscosity without magnetic field by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2000) to the case of a weak external magnetic field, where the cyclotron motion and QCD collisions compete on equal footing. It provides the first systematic leading-log result for shear viscosity in a weak magnetic field, complementing studies of bulk viscosity in strong magnetic fields by Hattori et al. (2017) and shear viscosity in the AdS/CFT strong-coupling regime by Finazzo et al. (2016). The result enables future matching to the five shear viscosities of magnetohydrodynamics in the dynamical-electromagnetism case, and provides a benchmark for transport in the perturbative regime of QCD under magnetic fields relevant to off-central heavy-ion collisions.
