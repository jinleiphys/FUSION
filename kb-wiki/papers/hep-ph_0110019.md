# eta and eta' in a coupled Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter approach. II. The gamma^* gamma transition form factors

> The applications of the consistently coupled Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter approach to the eta-eta' complex are extended to the two-photon transition form factors of eta and eta' for spacelike transferred momenta. We compare our predictions with experiment and some other theoretical approaches.

## Key claim

The paper extends the coupled Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter (SD-BS) approach, previously applied to on-shell η and η' decays, to compute the γ*γ transition form factors for spacelike virtualities, but the predictions are pure predictions with no additional model fitting and show systematic overshoot in the intermediate momentum range 0.5–2 GeV².

## Method

The authors compute the γ*γ → η, η' transition amplitudes by numerically evaluating the triangle diagram with dressed quark propagators and bound-state vertices from a previously fixed SD-BS model, using the Ball-Chiu vertex for the quark-photon interaction. The amplitudes for η and η' are obtained by mixing the nonstrange (uū + dđ) and strange (sš) components in the NS–S basis with a mixing angle φ = 42°, and the calculation discards second and higher derivatives in the momentum expansions.

## Key numbers

- Mixing angle φ = 42° (equivalently θ = −12.7°) is used for the NS–S state mixing, consistent with the FKS scheme and theory (φ = 42.4°) and with data.
- Results are presented for spacelike momenta in the range 0 < Q² < 8 GeV².
- The BL Ansatz curves use f_η_NS = 1.07 f_π, f_η_S = 1.34 f_π, and φ = 39.3° for the "FKS scheme and phenomenology" case.
- The "FKS scheme and theory" uses f_η_NS = f_π, f_η_S = 1.41 f_π, and φ = 42.4°.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SD-BS study of the η–η' complex and its on-shell γγ decays (Kekez and Klabucar, 2000) to the off-shell case, going beyond the chiral-limit approximation used earlier for the pion γ*γ form factor (Kekez and Klabucar, 1999). The results are compared with the Brodsky–Lepage Ansatz and with experimental data from CELLO (Behrend et al., 1991), CLEO (Gronberg et al., 1998), and L3 (Acciarri et al., 1998); the overshoot at intermediate Q² is attributed to the absence of vector meson poles in the Ball-Chiu vertex, which Maris and Tandy (2000) showed can be incorporated via a dressed quark-photon vertex.
