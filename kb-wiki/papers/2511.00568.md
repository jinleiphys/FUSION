# Empirical Reconstruction of the JSNS$^2$ KDAR $ν_μ$-$^{12}$C Missing-Energy Spectrum with a Two-Ex-Gaussian and Generalized-Tail Model

> Recent analyses of the JSNS$^2$ monoenergetic $ν_μ$ scattering on $^{12}$C at 235.5~MeV have compared the measured missing-energy spectrum with several nuclear models, including \textsc{NuWro}, \textsc{GiBUU}, and RMF+Achilles. While these models reproduce the overall peak position, their respective $χ^2$ values of $35.5$, $176.8$, and $58.1$ indicate that none can simultaneously describe the spectral width and the high-energy tail, reflecting limitations in the treatment of binding energy, two-particle--two-hole (2p-2h) excitations, and final-state interactions (FSI). To address these discrepancies, we introduce an empirical yet physically motivated representation of the spectrum based on two exponentially modified Gaussian (ex-Gaussian) components for p- and s-shell knockout and a generalized power-exponential continuum term describing multinucleon and FSI-induced strength. The fit reproduces the JSNS$^2$ data within the fitted energy range with $χ^2=8.0$ for 6 degrees of freedom. yielding parameters that quantify asymmetric broadening of the s-shell while preserving a narrow quasielastic p-shell response. This compact model demonstrates that a minimal empirical framework can capture key features of the nuclear response and provides a useful reference for phenomenological comparisons and future studies of quasielastic and 2p-2h dynamics in the few-hundred-MeV regime.

## Key claim

The authors introduce an empirical model using two exponentially modified Gaussian (ex-Gaussian) components for p- and s-shell knockout plus a generalized power-exponential continuum term that reproduces the JSNS$^2$ missing-energy spectrum with a chi-squared of 8.0 for 6 degrees of freedom, but they explicitly state this is a data-driven representation that does not replace microscopic theory or transport calculations.

## Method

The authors fit the JSNS$^2$ binned visible-energy spectrum (with the full covariance matrix from the data release) using a model composed of two left-skewed ex-Gaussian functions for the p- and s-shell bound-state contributions and a generalized power-exponential term for the continuum from multinucleon and final-state interaction strength. All fits were performed directly in the visible-energy variable E_vis (the axis on which the JSNS$^2$ distribution is binned and uncertainties are quoted), with the fit range restricted to 20 MeV < E_vis < 122 MeV to avoid threshold distortions at very low visible energy.

## Key numbers

* The empirical fit reproduces the JSNS$^2$ data with chi-squared = 8.0 for 6 degrees of freedom (chi-squared per degree of freedom approximately 1.3).
* The three reference models compared to the data yield chi-squared values of 35.5 (NuWro v21), 176.8 (GiBUU 2021p1), and 58.1 (RMF+Achilles), each for 16 degrees of freedom.
* The monoenergetic neutrino energy from kaon decay at rest is E_nu approximately 235.5 MeV, with the precise value 235.532 MeV used in the data release.
* The continuum tail parameters are optimized to E_0 approximately 20 MeV, E_t approximately 78 MeV, lambda approximately 21 MeV, and beta approximately 1.24.
* The p-shell amplitude A_P is 1.00 and the s-shell amplitude A_S is 2.01 in the best-fit model.

## Context

This work extends the JSNS$^2$ collaboration's original analysis (JSNS2, 2024) which compared the measured missing-energy spectrum to NuWro, GiBUU, and RMF+Achilles models and found that none simultaneously describe the spectral width and high-energy tail. The empirical two-ex-Gaussian-plus-tail model provides a quantitatively faithful reference for phenomenological comparisons and future studies of quasielastic and 2p-2h dynamics in the few-hundred-MeV regime, complementing generator development by distilling empirical structure from data to guide microscopic refinements.
