# $Ω$-deuteron Interaction in Folding Model

> A simple single folding model for $Ω$-deuteron with maximal spin $\left(I\right)J^{P}=\left(0\right)5/2^{+}$ is investigated. $Ω$ is assumed to orbit an unperturbed deuteron in a $Ω$-deuteron potential based on a separable $Ω$-nucleon potential from lattice QCD. We show that the effective central folding potential of $Ωd$ in the $^{5}S_{2}$ channel has a simple Wood-Saxon form, and approximate the upper bound for the binding energy of $Ω$ particle on a deuteron. In order to investigate how changes in the wave functions affect the results, we consider four analytical forms for $S$-state deuteron wave functions, i.e., two widely used Hulthén forms, as well as the modified Reid93 and Argonne v18 forms. Our calculations of binding energy from simple two-body approximation are compared with the results reported for the three-body problem; it is confirmed that the $Ωd$ system is deeply bound. Although the single folding model reduces the three-body problem to a two-body problem, this simplification is inadequate.

# Ω-deuteron Interaction in Folding Model

## Key claim
The single folding model reduces the three-body Ω-deuteron problem to a two-body problem, but this simplification is inadequate for accurate binding energy calculations; the resulting energies are only upper bounds because the real potential must be more attractive than the folding potential.

## Method
The authors constructed an effective Ω-deuteron potential by folding a separable Ω-nucleon potential from lattice QCD (HAL QCD Collaboration) with four different S-state deuteron wave functions: two Hulthén forms, and modified analytical forms from Reid93 and Argonne v18 potentials. The resulting central folding potential in the ⁵S₂ channel was fitted to a Woods-Saxon form, and the Schrödinger equation was solved in infinite volume to extract binding energies for the maximal spin state (I)Jᴾ = (0)5/2⁺.

## Key numbers
- ΩN scattering length: a₀^ΩN = 5.30 fm; effective range: r_eff^ΩN = 1.26 fm; ΩN binding energy: B^ΩN = 1.54 MeV (from HAL QCD, pion mass m_π = 146 MeV)
- Three-body Ωd binding energy from Ref. Garcilazo (2019): 20.9 MeV (22.0 MeV using HAL QCD masses)
- Two-body binding energies for P₁ ΩN model: Hulthén-1 = 15.7 MeV (16.4 MeV with HAL QCD masses); Hulthén-2 = 19.2 MeV (20.0 MeV); Reid93 = 6.7 MeV (7.0 MeV); Argonne v18 = 6.8 MeV (7.2 MeV)
- Woods-Saxon fit parameters for Hulthén-1: V₀ = 538 MeV, R = 0.98 fm, c = -0.19 fm; for Hulthén-2: V₀ = 675 MeV, R = 0.94 fm, c = -0.31 fm; for Reid93: V₀ = 143 MeV, R = 0.89 fm, c = 1.39 fm; for Argonne v18: V₀ = 148 MeV, R = 0.90 fm, c = 1.34 fm

## Context
This work extends three-body Faddeev calculations of the Ωd system by Garcilazo (2018, 2019) using HAL QCD ΩN potentials, and follows earlier two-body Λ-deuteron models by Congleton (1992). The folding model approach using the Watanabe ansatz provides a computationally simpler alternative to full three-body calculations, though the authors acknowledge its inadequacy for precise results. The binding energies from Hulthén wave functions (15.7-19.2 MeV) approach the three-body result (20.9 MeV) more closely than those from Reid93 or Argonne v18 potentials (6.7-6.8 MeV), which the authors attribute to differences in the short-range behavior of the wave functions. The work enables future three-body scattering calculations of Ω-deuteron scattering length and effective range.
