# Cross sections for inelastic $K$+$ϕ$ scattering

> In the first Born approximation we study the reactions $Kϕ\toπK$, $ρK$, $πK^*$, and $ρK^*$ with quark-antiquark annihilation and creation. Transition amplitudes are derived with the development in spherical harmonics of the relative-motion wave functions of the two initial mesons and of the two final mesons so that parity is conserved and the total angular momentum of the final mesons equals the one of the initial mesons. Unpolarized cross sections are calculated from the transition amplitudes that also contain mesonic quark-antiquark relative-motion wave functions and transition potentials for quark-antiquark annihilation and creation. Notable temperature dependence of the cross sections is shown. While the cross sections for $Kϕ\toρK$, $Kϕ\toπK^*$, and $Kϕ\toρK^*$ may be of the millibarn scale, the cross section for $Kϕ\toπK$ is very small.

## Key claim

Using the first Born approximation with quark-antiquark annihilation and creation, the authors calculate unpolarized cross sections for four inelastic K+phi scattering channels and find that the cross sections for Kphi to rho K, pi K*, and rho K* may be of the millibarn scale, while the cross section for Kphi to pi K is very small; all cross sections show notable temperature dependence.

## Method

The authors derive transition amplitudes for the reactions Kphi to pi K, rho K, pi K*, and rho K* using quark-antiquark annihilation and creation as the underlying mechanism, with the relative-motion wave functions of the two initial and two final mesons expanded in spherical harmonics to ensure parity conservation and equality of total angular momentum. The unpolarized cross sections are computed from these transition amplitudes, which incorporate mesonic quark-antiquark relative-motion wave functions obtained from solving the Schrodinger equation with a temperature-dependent quark potential, and transition potentials for quark-antiquark annihilation and creation derived from perturbative QCD.

## Key numbers

- The cross sections for Kphi to rho K, Kphi to pi K*, and Kphi to rho K* may be of the millibarn scale (no specific numerical cross section values given in the truncated text).
- The critical temperature used in the temperature-dependent quark potential is T_c = 0.175 GeV.
- Constituent quark masses used: 0.32 GeV for up and down quarks, 0.5 GeV for the strange quark.
- The phi meson mass temperature dependence is parametrized as m_phi = 0.931 [1 - (T/(1.12 T_c))^5.46]^1.32 (in GeV).
- The potential parameters include xi_1 = 0.525 GeV, xi_2 = 1.5[0.75+0.25(T/T_c)^10]^6 GeV, xi_3 = 0.6 GeV, and alpha' = 1.04 GeV^-2.
- The summations over orbital angular momentum quantum numbers L_i and L_f in the transition amplitudes are from 0 to 3.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous quark-level studies of inelastic pi+phi and rho+phi scattering (Xu, 2015) to the kaon+phi system, which had not been studied at the quark level before. The mechanism of quark-antiquark annihilation and creation, previously applied to many meson-meson reactions by Xu, Wang, and collaborators, completely differs from the quark interchange mechanism that governed the earlier pi+phi and rho+phi studies. The results enable more complete modeling of phi meson interactions in hadronic matter formed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where phi yield measurements from STAR and ALICE collaborations have shown enhancement relative to proton-proton collisions.
