# Heavy-quark spin symmetry for charmed and strange baryon resonances

> We study charmed and strange baryon resonances that are generated dynamically by a unitary baryon-meson coupled-channels model which incorporates heavy-quark spin symmetry. This is accomplished by extending the SU(3) Weinberg-Tomozawa chiral Lagrangian to SU(8) spin-flavor symmetry plus a suitable symmetry breaking. The model generates resonances with negative parity from the s-wave interaction of pseudoscalar and vector mesons with 1/2+ and 3/2+ baryons in all the isospin, spin, and strange sectors with one, two, and three charm units. Some of our results can be identified with experimental data from several facilities, such as the CLEO, Belle, or BaBar Collaborations, as well as with other theoretical models, whereas others do not have a straightforward identification and require the compilation of more data and also a refinement of the model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that many charmed and strange baryon resonances with negative parity can be dynamically generated as meson-baryon molecules in a unitary coupled-channels model that incorporates heavy-quark spin symmetry (HQSS), but the authors acknowledge that some of their predicted states do not have a straightforward identification with experimental data and require further refinement of the model and more data.

## Method

The authors extend the SU(3) Weinberg-Tomozawa chiral Lagrangian to SU(8) spin-flavor symmetry, treating pseudoscalar and vector mesons on equal footing alongside spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 baryons. They solve the on-shell Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels for s-wave interactions, using a subtraction point regularization scheme with physical hadron masses and meson decay constants. The symmetry is broken adiabatically along the chain SU(8) -> SU(6) -> SU(3) -> SU(2), and baryon resonances are identified as poles of the scattering amplitude on the complex-energy plane.

## Key numbers

*   The model generates four Lambda_c resonances; the Lambda_c(2595) is identified with a pole at 2618.8 - i 0.6 MeV, though its experimental width of 3.6^{+2.0}_{-1.3} MeV is not reproduced due to the omission of the three-body decay channel Lambda_c pi pi.
*   A second Lambda_c resonance with J=1/2 is found at 2617.3 MeV with a width of 89.8 MeV, coupling strongly to Sigma_c pi.
*   A Lambda_c resonance with J=3/2 is found at 2666.6 - i 26.7 MeV and identified with the experimental Lambda_c(2625), which has a width less than 0.97 MeV.
*   Three spin-1/2 Sigma_c resonances are predicted with masses 2571.5, 2622.7, and 2643.4 MeV; the first has a width of 0.8 MeV.
*   Two spin-3/2 Sigma_c resonances are found: a bound state at 2568.4 MeV and a state at 2692.9 - i 33.5 MeV.
*   The experimental Xi_c(2790) (J^P=1/2^-) is assigned to a pole at 2804.8 - i 13.5 MeV; the experimental Xi_c(2815) (J^P=3/2^-) is possibly identified with a pole at 2845.2 - i 22.0 MeV.
*   Three Omega_c bound states with J=1/2 are predicted at masses 2810.9, 2884.5, and 2941.6 MeV; two spin-3/2 bound states are found at 2814.3 and 2980.0 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the SU(8) spin-flavor symmetric unitarized coupled-channels model developed by the same group (SU(8), Gamermann) to systematically study charmed baryon resonances with HQSS. It contrasts with earlier t-channel vector-meson exchange (TVME) models (TVME, JimenezTejero (2009), Hofmann (2006)) and the Julich meson-exchange model, which are not fully consistent with HQSS; for example, the TVME model generates Lambda_c(2595) mostly as an N D bound state, whereas this work finds a dominant N D* component. The results enable future experimental searches at facilities like PANDA at GSI and provide predictions for states such as Sigma_c and Omega_c resonances that have no current experimental data.
