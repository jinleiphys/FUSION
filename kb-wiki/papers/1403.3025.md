# Charmonium Spectral Functions and Transport Properties of Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We study vacuum masses of charmonia and the charm-quark diffusion coefficient in the quark-gluon plasma based on the spectral representation for meson correlators. To calculate the correlators, we solve the quark gap equation and the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation in the rainbow-ladder approximation. It is found that the ground-state masses of charmonia in the pseudoscalar, scalar, and vector channels can be well described. For $1.5\,T_c<T<3.0\,T_c$, the value of the diffusion coefficient $D$ is comparable with that obtained by lattice QCD and experiments: $3.4<2πTD<5.9$. Relating the diffusion coefficient with the ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density $η/s$ of the quark-gluon plasma, we obtain values in the range $0.09<η/s<0.16$.

# Charmonium Spectral Functions and Transport Properties of Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger equation approach, using the rainbow-ladder approximation and a novel spectral representation for meson correlators, yields a charm-quark diffusion coefficient and shear viscosity-to-entropy density ratio for the quark-gluon plasma that are consistent with lattice QCD and experimental results, but only within the temperature range 1.5 T_c < T < 3.0 T_c and under the assumption of a linear interpolation for the drag coefficient between the strongly-coupled (AdS/CFT) and perturbative QCD limits.

## Method

The authors solved the quark gap equation and the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation in the rainbow-ladder approximation using a one-loop renormalization-group-improved interaction model, with the gluon Debye mass introduced for temperatures above T_c. They computed the imaginary-time charmonium correlation functions, applied a discrete transform to handle divergences in the spectral representation, and extracted spectral functions using the maximum entropy method. The charm-quark diffusion coefficient was obtained from the Kubo formula using the vector spectral function, and the shear viscosity-to-entropy density ratio was estimated via the perturbative relation η/s ≈ (1/6) T D.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state charmonium masses at T=0: η_c = 2.980 GeV, J/ψ = 3.113 GeV, χ_c0 = 3.476 GeV, χ_c1 = 3.227 GeV; compared to PDG values (η_c = 2.980 GeV, J/ψ = 3.097 GeV, χ_c0 = 3.415 GeV, χ_c1 = 3.510 GeV)
- Euclidean constituent charm quark mass m^E_c = 1.396 GeV (with current mass m^ζ_c = 0.79 GeV at scale ζ = 19 GeV)
- For 1.5 T_c < T < 3.0 T_c: 3.4 < 2π T D < 5.9
- For 1.5 T_c < T < 3.0 T_c: 0.09 < η/s < 0.16
- J/ψ dissociation temperature estimated at approximately 2 T_c
- At high temperature (T ≈ 3 T_c): thermal quark mass m_T = 0.8 T, strong coupling g_s ≈ 1.6, α_s ≈ 0.2, drag coefficient η_D ≈ 0.9 T^2 / M_0

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach previously applied to light hadrons and the electrical conductivity of the QGP (Qin 2013) to charmonium spectral functions and heavy-quark transport. It provides a continuum nonperturbative alternative to lattice QCD studies of charm-quark diffusion (Banerjee 2011, Ding 2012) and the T-matrix approach (Cabrera 2006). The results enable future extension to nonzero chemical potential and provide a framework for comparing heavy-quark energy loss mechanisms between RHIC and LHC conditions.
