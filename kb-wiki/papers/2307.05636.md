# Impact of the $^6$Li asymptotic normalization constant onto $α$-induced reactions of astrophysical interest

> Indirect methods have become the predominant approach in experimental nuclear astrophysics for studying several low-energy nuclear reactions occurring in stars, as direct measurements of many of these relevant reactions are rendered infeasible due to their low reaction probability. Such indirect methods, however, require theoretical input that in turn can have significant poorly-quantified uncertainties, which can then be propagated to the reaction rates and have a large effect on our quantitative understanding of stellar evolution and nucleosynthesis processes. We present two such examples involving $α$-induced reactions, $^{13}$C($α,n)^{16}$O and $^{12}$C$(α,γ)^{16}$O, for which the low-energy cross sections have been constrained with $(^6$Li$,d)$ transfer data. In this Letter, we discuss how a first-principle calculation of $^6$Li leads to a 21% reduction of the $^{12}$C$(α,γ)^{16}$O cross sections with respect to a previous estimation. This calculation further resolves the discrepancy between recent measurements of the $^{13}$C$(α,n)^{16}$O reaction and points to the need for improved theoretical formulations of nuclear reactions.

# Impact of the $^6$Li asymptotic normalization constant onto $\alpha$-induced reactions of astrophysical interest

## Key claim

A first-principle (six-body) calculation of the $^6$Li asymptotic normalization constant (ANC) yields $(\mathcal{C}_{\alpha-d})^2 = 6.864 \pm 0.210$ fm$^{-1}$, which is 22% larger than the previously adopted value of $5.3 \pm 0.5$ fm$^{-1}$ from Blokhintsev et al. (1993). This revised ANC resolves the discrepancy between the LUNA and JUNA R-matrix evaluations of the $^{13}$C$(\alpha,n)^{16}$O reaction and reduces the $^{12}$C$(\alpha,\gamma)^{16}$O S-factor at 300 keV by 21% relative to the evaluation of deBoer et al. (2017).

## Method

The authors reanalyzed existing $(^6$Li$,d)$ transfer reaction data for $^{13}$C$(\alpha,n)^{16}$O (from Avila et al., 2015) and $^{12}$C$(\alpha,\gamma)^{16}$O (from Brune et al., 1999 and Avila et al., 2015) by replacing the previously used $\alpha$-$d$ ANC of Blokhintsev et al. (1993) with a new first-principle prediction from the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) framework using chiral effective field theory interactions (Hebborn et al., 2023). For $^{12}$C$(\alpha,\gamma)^{16}$O, a reduced R-matrix calculation was performed starting from the best fit of deBoer et al. (2017), rescaling the ANCs of the bound states in $^{16}$O without refitting the remaining parameters.

## Key numbers

- The first-principle $^6$Li ANC squared is $(\mathcal{C}_{\alpha-d})^2 = 6.864 \pm 0.210$ fm$^{-1}$, compared to the previously adopted $5.3 \pm 0.5$ fm$^{-1}$ from Blokhintsev et al. (1993), a 22% increase.
- The re-evaluated Coulomb-modified ANC for the $1/2^+$ threshold state in $^{17}$O is $(\tilde{\mathcal{C}}^{1/2+}_{\alpha-^{13}\rm C})^2 = 2.8 \pm 0.5$ fm$^{-1}$, consistent with the JUNA collaboration value.
- The $^{12}$C$(\alpha,\gamma)^{16}$O S-factor at 300 keV is reduced by 21% relative to the deBoer et al. (2017) evaluation when using the new ANCs.
- For the $0^+$ state in $^{16}$O at 6.05 MeV excitation, the ANC squared changes from $2.43(30) \times 10^6$ fm$^{-1}$ (past work) to $1.88(16) \times 10^6$ fm$^{-1}$ (this work).
- For the $1^-$ state in $^{16}$O at 7.12 MeV excitation, the ANC squared changes from $4.39(59) \times 10^{28}$ fm$^{-1}$ (past work, Avila et al.) to $3.39(34) \times 10^{28}$ fm$^{-1}$ (this work).
- For the $2^+$ state in $^{16}$O at 6.92 MeV excitation, the ANC squared changes from $1.48(16) \times 10^{10}$ fm$^{-1}$ (past work, Avila et al.) to $1.14(7) \times 10^{10}$ fm$^{-1}$ (this work).

## Context

This work extends the indirect method of constraining $\alpha$-induced reaction rates using $(^6$Li$,d)$ transfer data, which was previously limited by poorly quantified uncertainties in the $^6$Li ANC. The new first-principle calculation of $^6$Li (Hebborn et al., 2023) resolves the discrepancy between the LUNA (2022) and JUNA (2022) R-matrix evaluations of $^{13}$C$(\alpha,n)^{16}$O, favoring the JUNA analysis which yields a smaller thermonuclear reaction rate. The 21% reduction in the $^{12}$C$(\alpha,\gamma)^{16}$O S-factor at stellar energies will increase the $^{12}$C to $^{16}$O abundance ratio in helium burning, impacting models of stellar evolution and supernova nucleosynthesis. The authors call for improved theoretical formulations including first-principle calculations of $^7$Li and $^{11}$B ANCs, and for generalization of transfer reaction formalisms to unbound states.
