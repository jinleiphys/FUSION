# Theoretical Analysis of the $p \bar{p}\rightarrow π^0 e^+ e^-$ Process within a Regge Framework

> We study the annihilation process $p\bar{p} \to π^0 e^+ e^-$ within a Regge framework, as a means to provide constraints on timelike nucleon form factors. We present results for the $e^+e^-$ angular distributions and the differential cross sections in kinematics which will be accessible by PANDA@FAIR. To check the consistency of the model, we first test the approach on the process of real photon production, $\bar{p} p \to π^0 γ$, where data in the energy range of 2.911 GeV$\leq\sqrt{s} \leq 3.686$ GeV exist. We find that a Regge pole model is able to well reproduce the available data. The analysis is then extended to a timelike virtual photon in the final state.

# Theoretical Analysis of the \(p \bar{p} \rightarrow \pi^0 e^+ e^-\) Process within a Regge Framework

## Key claim

A Regge pole model based on nucleon and \(\Delta\)-trajectory exchange can describe the \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow \pi^0 \gamma\) data well, whereas the Born diagram model fails by 4–5 orders of magnitude; the same Regge framework is then extended to predict cross sections for \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow \pi^0 e^+ e^-\) in kinematics accessible by PANDA@FAIR, but only in the forward and backward angular regions where \(s \gg |t|\) or \(s \gg |u|\) holds.

## Method

The authors first test their Regge pole model on the real-photon process \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow \pi^0 \gamma\) using existing data from Fermilab (Armstrong 1997) at center-of-mass energies \(2.911\ \text{GeV} \leq \sqrt{s} \leq 3.686\ \text{GeV}\). They replace the Feynman propagators of the Born diagram (single nucleon exchange in \(u\)- and \(t\)-channels) by Regge propagators for the nucleon trajectory (\(\alpha_N(u) = 1/2 + \alpha'_N (u - m_N^2)\) with \(\alpha'_N = 0.97\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\)) and the \(\Delta(1232)\) trajectory (\(\alpha_\Delta(u) = 3/2 + \alpha'_\Delta (u - m_\Delta^2)\) with \(\alpha'_\Delta = 0.9\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\)), while preserving the Feynman vertex structure. A reduction factor \(\mathcal{F} \approx 0.5\) is fitted to the \(\Delta\)-pole residue to match the data. For the virtual-photon process \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow \pi^0 e^+ e^-\), the same Regge amplitudes are used with electromagnetic form factors parametrized by a vector meson dominance (VMD) model following Iachello (2004), and the differential cross section is expressed in terms of the lepton-pair angular distribution via a density matrix formalism.

## Key numbers

- The Born diagram model for \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow \pi^0 \gamma\) yields cross sections \(d\sigma/d\cos\theta_\pi\) that are 4 to 5 orders of magnitude larger than the data, depending on \(\sqrt{s}\).
- The nucleon Regge trajectory slope is \(\alpha'_N = 0.97\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\); the \(\Delta\)-trajectory slope is \(\alpha'_\Delta = 0.9\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\).
- The \(\gamma N\Delta\) coupling strength at the real-photon point is \(g_M(0) = 3.02\); the \(\pi N\Delta\) coupling constant is \(h_A \simeq 2.85\).
- The reduction factor for the \(\Delta\) pole residue is \(\mathcal{F} \approx 0.5\), obtained by fitting to the \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow \pi^0 \gamma\) data.
- The cross section for \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow \pi^0 e^+ e^-\) from the Born model is 1 to 4 orders of magnitude larger than the Regge model results, depending on \(s\), \(q^2\), and \(\theta_\pi\).
- The density matrix \(\rho_{11}\) is about 1 order of magnitude larger than \(\rho_{00}\), \(\rho_{10}\), and \(\rho_{1-1}\) for \(s = 10\ \text{GeV}^2\) and \(q^2 = 0.5\ \text{GeV}^2\) in the \(t\)-channel dominated region.

## Context

This work extends the Regge pole approach previously applied to pion electroproduction and photoproduction (Guidal 1997, Vanderhaeghen 1997) to the annihilation channel \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow \pi^0 e^+ e^-\), providing a framework to extract timelike nucleon form factors in the unphysical region \(0 < q^2 < 4m_N^2\). It contrasts with the Born diagram model of Adamuscin (2007) and Gakh (2012), which the authors show fails for the real-photon case. The results are intended to support the PANDA@FAIR experiment (Lutz 2009) in constraining the relative phases of the electric and magnetic form factors \(G_E\) and \(G_M\), which are inaccessible in \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow e^+ e^-\) alone.
