# Isoscalar Giant Monopole, Dipole, and Quadrupole Resonances in $^{90,92}$Zr and $^{92}$Mo

> The isoscalar giant monopole, dipole, and quadrupole strength distributions have been deduced in $^{90, 92}$Zr, and $^{92}$Mo from "background-free" spectra of inelastic $α$-particle scattering at a beam energy of 385 MeV at extremely forward angles, including 0$^{\circ}$. These strength distributions were extracted by a multipole-decomposition analysis based on the expected angular distributions of the respective multipoles. All these strength distributions for the three nuclei practically coincide with each other, affirming that giant resonances, being collective phenomena, are not influenced by nuclear shell structure near $A\sim$90, contrary to the claim in a recent measurement.

## Key claim

The isoscalar giant monopole, dipole, and quadrupole strength distributions in 90,92Zr and 92Mo are practically identical, affirming that giant resonances in this mass region are not influenced by nuclear shell structure, contrary to the claim in a recent measurement by the Texas A and M group (2013, 2015).

## Method

Inelastic alpha-particle scattering at 385 MeV was measured at the RCNP ring cyclotron using the Grand Raiden magnetic spectrometer at extremely forward angles including 0 degrees. The instrumental background was eliminated using vertical-position spectra from the double-focusing mode. Strength distributions for L=0, 1, and 2 multipoles were extracted via multipole-decomposition analysis (MDA) using DWBA calculations with a hybrid optical-model potential, with uncertainties determined via Markov-Chain Monte-Carlo sampling.

## Key numbers

- ISGMR peak energy for 90Zr: 16.76 ± 0.12 MeV (Lorentzian fit)
- ISGMR peak energy for 92Zr: 16.25 ± 0.10 MeV (Lorentzian fit)
- ISGMR peak energy for 92Mo: 16.85 ± 0.10 MeV (Lorentzian fit)
- ISGMR EWSR fraction for 90Zr: 74.7 ± 9% over Ex range 10–22 MeV
- ISGMR EWSR fraction for 92Zr: 78.9 ± 7% over Ex range 10–22 MeV
- ISGMR EWSR fraction for 92Mo: 64.2 ± 6% over Ex range 10–22 MeV
- Finite nuclear incompressibility KA for 90Zr: 191.4 ± 6.1 MeV (scaling model)
- Finite nuclear incompressibility KA for 92Zr: 184.5 ± 4.9 MeV (scaling model)
- Finite nuclear incompressibility KA for 92Mo: 199.8 ± 5.0 MeV (scaling model)
- ISGDR high-energy component peak for 90Zr: 27.76 (+0.98, -0.78) MeV
- ISGDR high-energy component peak for 92Zr: 27.53 (+1.04, -0.86) MeV
- ISGDR high-energy component peak for 92Mo: 28.16 (+0.94, -0.82) MeV
- Beam energy: 385 MeV
- Excitation energy range measured: 9.5–32.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the long-standing program of compressional-mode studies at RCNP and directly contradicts the recent Texas A and M results (2013, 2015) which reported dramatic variations in ISGMR strength between 90Zr, 92Zr, and 92Mo, with 92Zr and 92Mo showing KA values respectively ~27 MeV and ~56 MeV higher than 90Zr. The present results show that all three nuclei have nearly identical strength distributions, consistent with the standard hydrodynamical picture that giant resonances as collective phenomena should be insensitive to shell structure for A ≥ 90. This work also resolves the apparent discrepancy with the "soft" Sn and Cd isotopic chains studied earlier by Li (2007, 2010) and Darshana (2012), where smooth variations were observed.
