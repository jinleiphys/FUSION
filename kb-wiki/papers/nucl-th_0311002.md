# Modelling nucleon-nucleon scattering above 1 GeV

> Motivated by the recent measurement of proton-proton spin-correlation parameters up to 2.5 GeV laboratory energy, we investigate models for nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering above 1 GeV. Signatures for a gradual failure of the traditional meson model with increasing energy can be clearly identified. Since spin effects are large up to tens of GeV, perturbative QCD cannot be invoked to fix the problems. We discuss various theoretical scenarios and come to the conclusion that we do not have a clear phenomenological understanding of the spin-dependence of the NN interaction above 1 GeV.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while total and differential cross sections for nucleon-nucleon scattering above 1 GeV can be reasonably described by extending a relativistic meson-exchange model with an optical potential and Regge-inspired modifications, the spin-dependence of the interaction above 1 GeV is not quantitatively understood, and no combination of parameter variations within the model can simultaneously fit all spin observables across the energy range up to 2.5 GeV.

## Method

The authors start from a relativistic one-boson-exchange (OBE) model including π, η, σ, ρ, and ω mesons, solved via the Thompson three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, with the Δ(1232) isobar included to account for inelasticity from pion production up to about 1 GeV. Above 1 GeV, they add a smooth optical potential with a Gaussian form factor to generate additional inelasticity, and apply a factor s₀/s to the ρ and ω exchange amplitudes to correct the rising elastic cross section, in the spirit of Regge theory. They then compare predictions of differential cross sections, analyzing powers, and spin correlation coefficients (A_NN, A_SS, A_SL, A_LL) against EDDA data from 400 MeV to 2.5 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The Δ(1232) isobar has a physical mass M_Δ = 1232 MeV; the Roper resonance N*(1440) contributes less than 1 mb to the inelastic cross section even at 2 GeV, and less than 0.1 mb at 775 MeV.
- The optical potential is switched on for T_lab > T_lab^(0) = 0.8 GeV, with a Gaussian range parameter a and strength parameters V_0 and W_0 chosen to give constant total cross sections in the 10–100 GeV range.
- The factor s₀/s is applied to ρ and ω exchanges for s > s₀, where s₀ = 2M(2M + T_lab^(0)) with T_lab^(0) = 0.8 GeV.
- At 400 MeV, the model shows good agreement with data for all observables; at 800 MeV and above, the analyzing power A_N is predicted persistently too large, and spin correlation parameters show only qualitative trend agreement up to 2.5 GeV.
- A_N measured up to 28 GeV laboratory energy shows no decline, contradicting the pQCD prediction A_N = 0; A_NN at 90° measured up to 12 GeV gives about 0.6, disagreeing with the pQCD prediction of 1/3.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic meson-exchange approach of Machleidt (1989) and others, which successfully describes NN scattering up to about 1 GeV, into the poorly understood 1–10 GeV regime where chiral perturbation theory (Weinberg, 1990) is limited to T_lab < 0.5 GeV and perturbative QCD is not applicable. The paper directly confronts the model with precise spin-correlation data from the EDDA Collaboration (2001–2003) and SATURNE (Allgower et al., 1998–2001), and concludes that no quantitative understanding of spin-dependence exists above 1 GeV, motivating future work with lattice QCD or refined Regge phenomenology.
