# A fully dynamical simulation of central nuclear collisions

> We present a fully dynamical simulation of central nuclear collisions around mid-rapidity at LHC energies. Unlike previous treatments, we simulate all phases of the collision, including the equilibration of the system. For the simulation, we use numerical relativity solutions to AdS/CFT for the pre-equilibrium stage, viscous hydrodynamics for the plasma equilibrium stage and kinetic theory for the low density hadronic stage. Our pre-equilibrium stage provides initial conditions for hydrodynamics, resulting in sizable radial flow. The resulting light particle spectra reproduce the measurements from the ALICE experiment at all transverse momenta.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first fully dynamical simulation of central nuclear collisions that includes the equilibration phase, but the results are approximately insensitive to the free parameters of the pre-equilibrium model (initialization time, bulk parameter, and switching time to hydrodynamics) provided the switching occurs later than about 0.5 fm/c.

## Method

The simulation uses numerical relativity solutions to the AdS/CFT correspondence for the pre-equilibrium stage, matching onto viscous hydrodynamics (vh2 code) with a lattice-QCD-inspired equation of state and a shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of 1/(4π), and finally onto a hadron cascade for resonances up to 2.2 GeV. The initial conditions are generated from colliding gravitational shockwaves in AdS, with the nuclear thickness function for 208Pb using radius R=6.62 fm and diffuseness a=0.546 fm, and the normalization ε0 is fixed by matching the experimental multiplicity.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear radius R = 6.62 fm and diffuseness a = 0.546 fm for 208Pb nucleus (from the thickness function)
- AdS/CFT initialization time τ_init range: 0.07 fm to 0.17 fm
- AdS/hydro switching time τ_hydro must be greater than 0.35 fm/c; results become independent of τ_hydro for τ_hydro > 0.5 fm/c
- Switching temperature from hydrodynamics to hadron cascade T_sw = 0.17 GeV
- Pressure discontinuity at the center between AdS and QCD equation of state never exceeded 15%
- Bulk parameter σ range that works well: 7.5 fm^{-1} to 14 fm^{-1}

## Context

This work extends previous treatments that had to guess initial conditions for hydrodynamics at some starting time τ_hydro, by including the far-from-equilibrium evolution obtained from colliding shock waves in AdS via the AdS/CFT correspondence (Romatschke 2013). The approach contrasts with free-streaming (FS) models and zero-flow (ZF) models, which never lead to thermalization and produce results that depend on the choice of τ_hydro. The simulation enables more constrained extraction of QCD properties such as viscosity, and the authors plan to extend the work to non-central collisions and event-by-event simulations using developments in black-hole evolution schemes (Bantilan 2012).
