# Nearly Perfect Fluidity in the Dilute Fermi Gas: An Update

> In this contribution we summarize recent results on the transport properties of strongly correlated dilute Fermi gases. We discuss the hydrodynamic equations in the normal phase and present new results on the structure of second order terms in the gradient expansion. We also discuss predictions from kinetic theory, and show how these predictions can be tested using experimental data on elliptic flow. We summarize current constraints on the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $η/s$.

## Key claim

The dilute Fermi gas at unitarity, like the quark gluon plasma, behaves as a nearly perfect liquid with a shear viscosity to entropy density ratio close to the conjectured quantum lower bound, but the actual constraint from experiment and theory is only that eta/s is less than approximately 0.5 hbar/k_B, not that it exactly equals the AdS/CFT bound of hbar/(4 pi k_B).

## Method

The author reviews the hydrodynamic description of the normal phase (T > T_c ~ 0.14 T_F) of a dilute Fermi gas, presenting new results on the structure of second-order terms in the gradient expansion of the stress tensor, constrained by Galilean invariance and conformal symmetry. Predictions from kinetic theory are also discussed, using the Boltzmann equation in a curved background metric to compute the frequency-dependent shear viscosity eta(omega) and showing how these predictions can be tested using experimental data on elliptic flow.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the normal-to-superfluid transition is T_c ~ 0.14 T_F, where T_F = k_F^2/(2m) is the Fermi temperature.
- At unitarity (infinite scattering length), the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio satisfies eta/s < 0.5 hbar/k_B.
- The conjectured quantum lower bound from AdS/CFT is eta/s = hbar/(4 pi k_B).
- In kinetic theory, the zero-frequency shear viscosity at unitarity (a -> infinity) is eta(0) = (15 (mT)^(3/2))/(32 sqrt(pi)).
- In the weak coupling limit (a -> 0), eta(0) = (15 (mT)^(3/2))/(32 sqrt(pi)) * 1/(3 m T a^2).
- The frequency-dependent shear viscosity follows eta(omega) = eta(0)/(1 + omega^2 tau^2).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of transport in strongly correlated Fermi gases by Dusling (2007), Romatschke (2007), and Turlapov (2007), and connects to the broader program of nearly perfect fluids in both cold atomic gases and the quark gluon plasma. The results enable quantitative tests of kinetic theory predictions against elliptic flow measurements in trapped Fermi gases, and provide a framework for extracting transport coefficients from experiment. The second-order hydrodynamic terms build on the conformal constraint that the bulk viscosity of the unitary Fermi gas is zero, as shown by Son (2005).
