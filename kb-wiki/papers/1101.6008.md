# Heavy-flavour spectra in high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions

> The propagation of the heavy quarks produced in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC and LHC is studied within the framework of Langevin dynamics in the background of an expanding deconfined medium described by ideal and viscous hydrodynamics. The transport coefficients entering into the relativistic Langevin equation are evaluated by matching the hard-thermal-loop result for soft collisions with a perturbative QCD calculation for hard scatterings. The heavy-quark spectra thus obtained are employed to compute the differential cross sections, the nuclear modification factors R_AA and the elliptic flow coefficients v_2 of electrons from heavy-flavour decay.

## Key claim

The paper presents a fully consistent weakly coupled perturbative QCD calculation of heavy-quark transport coefficients and uses them in a Langevin simulation to test how closely the experimental data on heavy-flavour suppression and elliptic flow at RHIC and LHC can be reproduced without invoking more "exotic" strongly coupled scenarios, finding that the results depend significantly on the choice of the renormalization scale for the running coupling.

## Method

The authors generate initial heavy-quark pairs using the POWHEG event generator at NLO pQCD, then propagate charm and bottom quarks through an expanding deconfined medium using a relativistic Langevin equation. The transport coefficients (drag and momentum-diffusion coefficients) are evaluated by matching the hard-thermal-loop (HTL) result for soft collisions with a perturbative QCD calculation for hard scatterings, using a cutoff at the Debye mass scale. The background medium evolution is described by both ideal and viscous (η/s=0.08) hydrodynamics, and the heavy quarks are hadronized through fragmentation and decayed to electrons for comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Total c-cbar production cross section in pp collisions at RHIC (√s=200 GeV): 0.254 mb with CTEQ6M PDF
- Total b-bbar production cross section in pp collisions at RHIC: 1.77×10⁻³ mb with CTEQ6M PDF
- Total c-cbar production cross section in pp collisions at LHC (√s=5.5 TeV): 3.015 mb with CTEQ6M PDF
- Total b-bbar production cross section in pp collisions at LHC: 0.187 mb with CTEQ6M PDF
- Ratio of PHENIX data to POWHEG prediction for electron cross section in pp at RHIC: 1.12 (best fit constant)
- Initial central temperature T₀ for ideal hydro at RHIC: 357 MeV (τ₀=0.6 fm)
- Initial central temperature T₀ for ideal hydro at LHC: 1000 MeV (τ₀=0.1 fm)
- Average squared transverse momentum ⟨k_T²⟩_AA for charm in Au-Au at RHIC: 1.3-1.5 GeV²/c² depending on impact parameter
- Average squared transverse momentum ⟨k_T²⟩_AA for bottom in Pb-Pb at LHC: 2.5-3.3 GeV²/c² depending on impact parameter
- Langevin time step in fluid rest frame: Δt̄=0.02 fm
- Quark masses used: m_c=1.5 GeV, m_b=4.8 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous Langevin-based studies of heavy-quark propagation (Svetitsky, 1988; Rapp et al., 2003; Moore and Teaney, 2005) by providing a fully microscopic pQCD calculation of transport coefficients without free parameters, in contrast to approaches assuming resonant states in the plasma (Rapp, 2009) or using AdS/CFT correspondence (Hirano and Nara, 2004). The results enable systematic comparison with PHENIX data at RHIC and provide predictions for ALICE at LHC (√s=5.5 TeV), allowing assessment of how much room remains for non-perturbative effects beyond the weakly coupled picture.
