# Towards a Unified Description of the Baryon Spectrum and the Baryon-Baryon Interaction within a Potential Model Scheme

> We study the low energy part of the nucleon and $Δ$ spectra by solving the Schrödinger equation for the three-quark system in the hyperspherical harmonic approach. The quark-quark hamiltonian considered includes, besides the usual one-gluon exchange, pion and sigma exchanges generated by the chiral symmetry breaking. This quark-quark potential reproduces, in a Resonating Group Method calculation, the nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts and the deuteron properties. The baryonic spectrum obtained is quite reasonable and the resulting wave function is consistent with the ansatz used in the two baryon system.

# Towards a Unified Description of the Baryon Spectrum and the Baryon-Baryon Interaction within a Potential Model Scheme

## Key claim
The same quark-quark potential that reproduces nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts and deuteron properties in a Resonating Group Method calculation also yields a reasonable low-energy nucleon and Δ baryon spectrum when the three-quark Schrödinger equation is solved in the hyperspherical harmonic approach, though some small discrepancy remains in the relative energy position of the Roper resonance N*(1440) and the first negative parity state N⁻(1535).

## Method
The authors constructed a quark-quark Hamiltonian containing a linear confining potential, one-gluon exchange, and pion and sigma exchanges from chiral symmetry breaking. They solved the three-quark Schrödinger equation using the hyperspherical harmonic approach to obtain the low-energy nucleon and Δ spectra, using the same potential parameters that had previously been fitted to reproduce NN scattering phase shifts and deuteron properties in a Resonating Group Method calculation. The consistency of the baryon wave function with the harmonic oscillator ansatz used in the two-baryon system was tested by comparing the adiabatic NN potential obtained from the Schrödinger solution with that from the gaussian ansatz at different oscillator constants.

## Key numbers
- Constituent quark mass m_q = 313 MeV; harmonic oscillator constant b = 0.518 fm; effective quark-gluon coupling α_s = 0.485; chiral coupling α_ch = 0.027; confinement strength a_c = 91.488 MeV·fm⁻¹; pion mass m_π = 0.7 fm⁻¹; sigma mass m_σ = 3.42 fm⁻¹; cutoff Λ = 4.2 fm⁻¹.
- The baryon spectrum was calculated up to 0.7 GeV excitation energy for nucleon and Δ states.
- The Roper resonance N*(1440) and the first negative parity state N⁻(1535) are the two states whose relative energy position shows discrepancy, as common in two-body potential models.

## Context
This work extends the quark potential model approach of Rujula, Georgi and Glashow (1975) and YAO (unspecified year) for the baryon spectrum by incorporating pion and sigma exchanges from chiral symmetry breaking, following the earlier NN interaction studies of Fernandez and collaborators (FER, FER2). The model aims to unify the description of the baryon spectrum and baryon-baryon interaction within a single potential framework, addressing the controversy over the value of the harmonic oscillator constant b by showing that the value giving the best NN fit also yields a consistent baryon wave function. This enables future work to refine the unified description and extend it to other baryon systems.
