# Induced pseudoscalar form factor of the nucleon at two-loop order in chiral perturbation theory

> We calculate the imaginary part of the induced pseudoscalar form factor of the nucleon $G_P(t)$ in the framework of two-loop heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. The effect of the calculated three-pion continuum on the pseudoscalar constant $g_P = (m_μ/2M) G_P(t=-0.877m_μ^2)$ measurable in ordinary muon capture $μ^-p\to ν_μn$ turns out to be negligibly small. Possible contributions from counterterms at two-loop order are numerically smaller than the uncertainty of the dominant pion-pole term proportional to the pion-nucleon coupling constant $g_{πN}= 13.2\pm 0.2$. We conclude that a sufficiently accurate representation of the induced pseudoscalar form factor of the nucleon at low momentum transfers $t$ is given by the sum of the pion-pole term and the Adler-Dothan-Wolfenstein term: $G_P(t) = 4g_{πN} M f_π/ (m_π^2 -t)- 2g_A M^2 < r_A^2 >/3$, with $<r_A^2> = (0.44 \pm 0.02)$ fm$^2$ the axial mean square radius of the nucleon.

# Induced pseudoscalar form factor of the nucleon at two-loop order in chiral perturbation theory

## Key claim
Two-loop corrections to the induced pseudoscalar form factor G_P(t) and the pseudoscalar coupling constant g_P are numerically insignificant; the dominant pion-pole term plus the Adler-Dothan-Wolfenstein term provide a sufficiently accurate representation at low momentum transfers, with the major theoretical uncertainty coming from the pion-nucleon coupling constant g_πN = 13.2 ± 0.2.

## Method
The imaginary part of the induced pseudoscalar form factor G_P(t) was calculated using two-loop heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, applying Cutkosky cutting rules to ten topologically distinct two-loop diagrams generated by leading-order vertices of the effective chiral Lagrangian. The spectral function Im G_P(t) was obtained as an integral over the Lorentz-invariant three-pion phase space, with the three-pion center-of-mass frame used to perform the three-body phase space integrals. The complete two-loop representation of G_P(t) was then constructed via a twice-subtracted dispersion relation.

## Key numbers
- Pseudoscalar coupling constant: g_P = 8.3 ± 0.2, using g_πN = 13.2 ± 0.2 and ⟨r_A²⟩ = (0.44 ± 0.02) fm²
- Contribution from the dispersion integral to g_P: δg_P ≈ −1·10⁻⁵ at the subtraction point t₀ = 0; magnitude stays below 1% (0 > δg_P > −1·10⁻²) when varying subtraction point in the range −24 m_π² < t₀ < 9 m_π²
- Pion-nucleon coupling constant: g_πN = 13.2 ± 0.2
- Axial mean square radius: ⟨r_A²⟩ = (0.44 ± 0.02) fm²
- Axial dipole mass: M_A = (1.03 ± 0.02) GeV
- Pion decay constant: f_π = 92.4 MeV
- Charged pion mass: m_π = 139.57 MeV
- Muon mass: m_μ = 105.66 MeV
- Average nucleon mass: M = 938.92 MeV
- Squared momentum transfer at muon capture: t_μ = −0.877 m_μ² = −0.502 m_π²
- In the chiral limit (m_π = 0): Im G_P(t) ≈ −3t/M²

## Context
This work extends the one-loop chiral perturbation theory calculation of the induced pseudoscalar form factor by Adler, Dothan, and Wolfenstein (1970s) and the small scale expansion including Δ(1232)-isobars by Hemmert, Holstein, and Kambor (1998). The two-loop corrections are found to be numerically negligible compared to the uncertainty in g_πN, confirming that the one-loop representation is adequate for interpreting ordinary muon capture experiments. The results enable a clean theoretical prediction for g_P = 8.3 ± 0.2, which can be compared with experimental values such as the Saclay result g_P = 8.7 ± 1.9 and the reanalyzed value g_P = 10.6 ± 2.7 from Gorringe and Fearing (2002).
