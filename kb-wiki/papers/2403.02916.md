# Microscopic parametrization of the near threshold oscillations of the nucleon time-like effective electromagnetic form factors

> We present an analysis of the recent near threshold BESIII data for the nucleon time-like effective form factors. The damped oscillation emerging from the subtraction of the dipole formula is treated in non-perturbative-QCD, making use of the light cone distribution amplitudes expansion. Non-perturbative effects are accounted for by considering Q2-dependent coefficients in such expansions, whose free parameters are determined by fitting to the proton and neutron data. Possible implications and future analysis have been discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the damped oscillations observed in nucleon time-like effective electromagnetic form factors near threshold can be described by a microscopic model based on the three-quark structure of nucleons, using light cone distribution amplitudes with Q^2-dependent coefficients, without invoking final state interactions. The model reproduces the neutron data well but fails to capture the oscillatory behavior for the proton, instead yielding a result compatible with the regular background.

## Method

The authors expand the twist-3 nucleon light cone distribution amplitude (LCDA) in a basis of Appell polynomials, with the expansion coefficients taken as Q^2-dependent functions parametrized as truncated Laurent series in powers of Q^{-2}. They then compute the effective form factor using the Chernyak-Zhitnitsky asymptotic formula with these Q^2-dependent LCDAs, and perform a simultaneous fit to the BESIII proton and neutron cross section data to determine the 13 free parameters of the expansion coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The fit uses 56 data points (48 from proton cross section and 18 from neutron cross section) with 13 free parameters, yielding a normalized chi-squared of chi^2/n_DoF = 76.93/(56-13) ≈ 1.79
- The first coefficient B_0 is fixed to 1; B_1 and B_2 have expansions with one free parameter each (b_0 and b_1); B_3, B_4, and B_5 have expansions with two free parameters each (b_0, b_1, b_2)
- For B_1: b_0^(1) = -31 ± 1, b_1^(1) = (-144 ± 11) GeV^2
- For B_2: b_0^(2) = -15.3 ± 0.1, b_1^(2) = (36 ± 1) GeV^2
- For B_3: b_0^(3) = 7.2 ± 0.3, b_1^(3) = (-80 ± 3) GeV^2, b_2^(3) = (251 ± 7) GeV^4
- For B_4: b_0^(4) = 56 ± 2, b_1^(4) = (-2.95 ± 0.03) × 10^3 GeV^2, b_2^(4) = (6.7 ± 0.1) × 10^3 GeV^4
- For B_5: b_0^(5) = 0.6 ± 0.7, b_1^(5) = (80 ± 6) GeV^2, b_2^(5) = (-135 ± 21) GeV^4
- The maximum of the nucleon distribution amplitude phi_N occurs at light-cone momentum fractions x_1 ≈ 0.49, x_2 ≈ 0.24, x_3 ≈ 0.27 at sqrt(-Q^2) = 2.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Chernyak-Zhitnitsky model (1984) and the Brodsky-Lepage formalism (1980) for nucleon form factors by introducing Q^2-dependent non-perturbative coefficients in the LCDA expansion, enabling application near the production threshold. It contrasts with approaches that attribute the oscillations to final state interactions, such as the work of Yang et al. (2023), by testing whether a purely internal structure description can suffice. The model enables future inclusion of higher-twist contributions and separation of electric and magnetic form factors, and provides a unified parametrization for both proton and neutron across the full kinematic range, unlike previous works that required different models for different regions.
