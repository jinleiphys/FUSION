# Study of three-neutron bound and continuum states

> The three-neutron ($3n$) system is studied by numerical calculations with the Faddeev three-body formalism for a realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential. A response function for the transition from ${}^3\mathrm{H}$ to $3n$ continuum states by an isospin excitation operator is calculated, from which no evidence of $3n$ resonance state is found. Different methods to extrapolate the $3n$ energy from bound state energies with an extra attractive effect to the NN potential are examined. While extrapolations with attractive effects by enhanced NN potentials or three-body potentials result the non-existence of $3n$ resonance states, one by external trapping potentials leads to a positive $3n$ energy, which may be considered as a resonance state. It is found that this contradiction is due to a general defect of the trapping method.

## Key claim

The paper finds no evidence of a three-neutron resonance state in calculations using the Faddeev three-body formalism with a realistic nucleon-nucleon potential, contradicting recent claims from quantum Monte Carlo and no-core Gamow shell model calculations; the author argues that the positive energy result from the trapping potential extrapolation method is unreliable due to a general defect where the trapping potential destroys the centrifugal barrier.

## Method

The author solves Faddeev integral equations in coordinate space for the three-neutron system using the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential with partial waves up to j ≤ 4, calculating the response function for the transition from ³H to 3n continuum states via an isospin excitation operator. Three extrapolation methods are examined: enhancing the ³P₂-³F₂ partial wave of the nn potential by a factor (1-α), introducing a Gaussian three-body potential, and adding an external Woods-Saxon trapping potential; the response function peaks are fitted with a Lorentzian form to extract resonance parameters.

## Key numbers

- The 3n (3/2⁻) bound state exists for α < -2.98 when enhancing the ³P₂-³F₂ nn potential component, and a nn(³P₂-³F₂) bound state exists for α < -3.39
- For the enhanced potential method, extracted resonance energy E_r stays at about 6.5 MeV with width Γ ≈ 30 MeV at α = -1.6, with numerical errors of about 0.4 MeV
- For the three-body potential method, the maximum extracted E_r is about 4 MeV with Γ ≈ 8 MeV at W₁ = -40 MeV, with numerical errors of 0.1 MeV at W₁ = -20 MeV
- The three-body potential requires W₁ < -80 MeV to produce a 3n bound state, contrasting with W₁ = -2.55 MeV needed to reproduce the ³H binding energy
- Extrapolation using the trapping potential with Woods-Saxon radius R_WS = 4.5, 6.0, and 7.5 fm yields a positive 3n energy of about 3 MeV at zero trapping strength

## Context

This work extends previous Faddeev-type calculations by Hemmer (2002), Lazauskas (2005), and Deltuva (2018) that found no 3n resonance, and directly contradicts recent quantum Monte Carlo calculations by Gandolfi (2017) and no-core Gamow shell model calculations by Li (2019) that suggested 3n resonance states. The paper enables future studies by demonstrating that the trapping potential extrapolation method has a general defect when applied to P-wave systems, as the longer-range trapping potential destroys the centrifugal barrier that would otherwise support a resonance.
