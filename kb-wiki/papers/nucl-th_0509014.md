# Equation of state for asymmetric nuclear matter with infinite-order summation of ring diagrams

> The particle-particle hole-hole ring-diagram summation method is employed to obtain the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter over a wide range of asymmetry fraction. Compared with Brueckner Hartree-Fock and model-space Brueckner Hartree-Fock calculations, this approach gives a softer equation of state, increased symmetry energy and a lower value for the incompressibility modulus which agrees quite well with the values used in the hydrodynamical model for the supernovae explosion.

# Equation of state for asymmetric nuclear matter with infinite-order summation of ring diagrams

## Key claim

The particle-particle hole-hole ring-diagram summation method produces a softer equation of state for asymmetric nuclear matter than Brueckner Hartree-Fock (BHF) or model-space BHF approaches, with the incompressibility modulus at saturation for symmetric nuclear matter (112 MeV) agreeing well with values used in hydrodynamical supernova explosion models, but only when the ring-diagram correlations are included to all orders.

## Method

The authors extended the particle-particle hole-hole (pphh) ring-diagram summation method, previously applied to symmetric nuclear matter, to asymmetric nuclear matter with varying proton-neutron ratios. They employed a model-space G-matrix approach with the Paris nucleon-nucleon interaction, using a model-space momentum boundary of 3.0 fm⁻¹, and solved the RPA-type secular equation in the relative and center-of-mass frame with angle-averaged Pauli operators and occupation factors to obtain the ground-state energy shift.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon for symmetric nuclear matter (α=0.0) at saturation: -15.93 MeV, with saturation Fermi momentum 1.43 fm⁻¹
- Symmetry energy at saturation density (ρ₀=0.17 fm⁻³): 28 MeV, compared to 25.42 MeV from MBHF calculation
- Incompressibility modulus for symmetric nuclear matter: 112 MeV (κ₀(0)=112 MeV), with parabolic dependence parameter a=1.21
- Incompressibility modulus decreases with asymmetry: 100.20 MeV at α=0.2, 92.74 MeV at α=0.4, 67.35 MeV at α=0.6, and 22.95 MeV at α=0.8
- Binding energy at α=0.8 and k_F=1.20 fm⁻¹: -3.83 MeV (ring) versus +2.51 MeV (MBHF), showing saturation exists in ring calculation but not in MBHF
- For pure neutron matter (α=1.0), no saturation occurs; binding energy at k_F=1.35 fm⁻¹ is +14.22 MeV

## Context

This work extends the ring-diagram summation method of Yang, Heyer and Kuo (1986) from symmetric to asymmetric nuclear matter, continuing the model-space BHF calculations of Song, Wang and Kuo (1992). The ring-diagram approach addresses the long-standing Coester band problem of BHF calculations, which fail to simultaneously reproduce the empirical binding energy (-16±1 MeV) and saturation Fermi momentum (1.35±0.05 fm⁻¹). The softer equation of state and lower incompressibility obtained are relevant for supernova explosion models requiring a soft EOS, as in the prompt explosion model of Kahana (1989), and for neutron star structure calculations. The results also test the validity of the empirical parabolic approximation for asymmetric matter properties used in earlier work by Cooperstein and Wambach (1984) and Wiringa, Fiks and Fabrocini (1988).
