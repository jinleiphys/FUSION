# Proper derivation of subspace mapping from whole space mapping in boson expansion theory

> The norm operator method, which was recently proposed as a new formulation of the boson expansion theory (BET), is used to show that the subspace mapping is properly derived from the whole space mapping. This derivation requires the appropriate renormalization of the contribution of phonons that are not adopted as boson excitations in the subspace mapping. This was impossible with conventional BETs (which ignore these contributions), and is only made possible for the first time by the norm operator method, which treats these contributions appropriately. We also correct the confusion in the claims of conventional BETs. Namely, contrary to conventional claims, we show that when the phonon excitations not adopted as boson excitations make no contribution at all, the subspace mapping is obtained simply by discarding those excitations. Furthermore, we demonstrate that the Park operator, which had been considered effective only in the whole space mapping, is also effective in the subspace mapping. These findings provide a clear criterion for verifying the applicability of the boson expansion theory to large-amplitude collective motions and offer a new perspective on a microscopic foundation of the interacting boson model (IBM).

## Key claim

The paper claims that the subspace mapping in boson expansion theory (BET) can be properly derived from the whole space mapping only when the contribution of phonons not adopted as boson excitations is appropriately renormalized, which was impossible in conventional BETs that ignored these contributions (Non-Adopted-Mode-Discretion, NAMD). The author shows that when these non-adopted phonon excitations make no contribution at all, the subspace mapping is obtained simply by discarding those excitations, contrary to conventional claims.

## Method

The author applies the norm operator method (Taniguchi 2023) to re-examine the relationship between the whole fermion space mapping (WFSM) and the fermion subspace mapping (FSSM) in boson expansion theory. The analysis compares the Hermitian-type mapping (Marumori, Yamamura, and Tokunaga 1964; Kishimoto and Tamura 1983) and non-Hermitian-type mapping (Dyson boson expansion; Takada 2001) for both WFSM and FSSM, focusing on how the projection operators onto physical subspaces are related. The norm operator method is used to derive the FSSM physical subspace from the WFSM physical subspace by renormalizing the contributions of non-adopted phonon excitation modes, which conventional BETs had discarded via NAMD.

## Key numbers

- The Hermitian-type mapping operator for WFSM is given by \(U_H^{(W)}=\sum_{N=0}^\infty\sum_{\alpha_1\beta_1<\cdots<\alpha_N\beta_N}\vert \alpha_1\beta_1\cdots\alpha_N\beta_N)_{\mathcal A}\langle\alpha_1\beta_1\cdots\alpha_N\beta_N\vert\), establishing a one-to-one correspondence between fermion and boson basis states.
- The Dyson boson expansion for quasi-particle pair operators in WFSM is given by \((a_\beta a_\alpha)_D^{(W)}=b_{\alpha\beta}\), \((a_\alpha^\dagger a_\beta^\dagger)_D^{(W)}=b_{\alpha\beta}^\dagger-\sum_{\gamma\delta}b_{\alpha\gamma}^\dagger b_{\beta\gamma}^\dagger b_{\gamma\delta}\), and \((a_\beta^\dagger a_\alpha)_D^{(W)}=\sum_\gamma b_{\beta\gamma}^\dagger b_{\alpha\gamma}\).
- The Hermitian-type mapping for phonon operators in FSSM (KT-3) is \((X_t')_H^{(S)}=b_t^\dagger -\frac 14\sum_{t_1t'_1t'_2}Y(t't'_1t'_2t_1)b_{t_1}^\dagger b_{t'_1}b_{t'_2}\), where \(Y(\mu_1\mu_2\mu_3\mu_4)=\sum_q\Gamma_q^{\mu_1\mu_2}\Gamma_q^{\mu_3\mu_4}=\sum_{\alpha\beta}\Gamma_{\alpha\beta}^{\mu_1\mu_2}\Gamma_{\alpha\beta}^{\mu_3\mu_4}\).
- The non-Hermitian-type mapping for phonon operators in FSSM (Takada) is \((X_t^\dagger)_D^{(S)}=b_t^\dagger-\frac 12\sum_{t_1t_2}\sum_{t'_1}Y(tt_1t_2t'_1)b_{t_1}^\dagger b_{t_2}^\dagger b_{t'_1}\), with \((X_{t'})_D^{(S)}=b_{t'}\) and \((B_q)_D^{(S)}=\sum_t\sum_{t'}\Gamma_q^{t't}b_t^\dagger b_{t'}\).

## Context

This work extends the norm operator method (Taniguchi 2023) to resolve a long-standing confusion in boson expansion theory regarding the relationship between whole space and subspace mappings. It corrects claims in conventional BET formulations by Kishimoto and Tamura (1983) and Takada (2001), which assumed NAMD as a "good approximation" without rigorous justification, and by Park (1987) regarding the applicability of the Park operator in subspace mappings. The findings provide a clear criterion for verifying the applicability of BET to large-amplitude collective motions in atomic nuclei and offer a new perspective on a microscopic foundation of the interacting boson model (IBM).
