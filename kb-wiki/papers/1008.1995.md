# Production, elliptic flow and energy loss of heavy quarks in the quark-gluon plasma

> Production, elliptic flow and the nuclear modification factor of charm and bottom quarks are studied in central and non-central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC using the partonic transport model Boltzmann Approach of MultiParton Scatterings (BAMPS). Employing an initial heavy quark yield obtained with PYTHIA the full space-time evolution of charm and bottom quarks in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is carried out with BAMPS, taking also secondary production in the QGP into account. Only elastic collisions of heavy quarks with particles from the medium cannot describe the experimentally observed elliptic flow and nuclear modification factor. However, using an improved Debye screening and the running coupling yields a result which is much closer to data.

# Production, elliptic flow and energy loss of heavy quarks in the quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim
Elastic collisions of heavy quarks with the medium using a constant coupling and standard Debye screening cannot reproduce the experimentally observed elliptic flow and nuclear modification factor; however, using an improved Debye screening with κ ≈ 0.2 and a running coupling brings the results much closer to data, requiring only a K factor of 4 rather than the previously needed factor of 40–50.

## Method
The partonic transport model BAMPS (Boltzmann Approach of MultiParton Scatterings) was used to simulate the full space-time evolution of charm and bottom quarks in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) for central and non-central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. Initial heavy quark yields were obtained with PYTHIA, and secondary production in the QGP was included via processes such as gg → Q Qbar and g Q → g Q, with cross sections calculated using leading-order pQCD with an improved Debye screening and running coupling.

## Key numbers
- Charm quark production in the QGP at RHIC lies between 0.3 and 3.4 charm pairs per Au+Au collision, depending on the initial gluon distribution model, charm mass, and whether a K=2 factor is employed.
- At LHC, between 11 and 55 charm pairs are produced in the QGP, a sizeable fraction of the initial yield and of the same order for some scenarios.
- The improved cross section with running coupling and corrected Debye screening (κ ≈ 0.2) requires only a K factor of 4 to reproduce the order of magnitude of the elliptic flow data, compared to the previously needed K factor of 40–50.
- The Debye mass is calculated as m_D² = 4π (1 + N_f/6) α_s(t) T² with running coupling, and the screening prefactor κ is fixed to approximately 0.2 by comparing dE/dx to the hard thermal loop approach.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of heavy quark energy loss in the QGP, particularly addressing the discrepancy between the "dead cone effect" prediction (Dokshitzer, 2001) and experimental observations from PHENIX and STAR showing heavy quark elliptic flow and suppression comparable to light quarks (Abelev, 2006; Adare, 2006; Adare, 2010). The approach follows the methodology of Gossiaux (2008) and Peshier (2008) in using an effective running coupling from e+e- annihilation and τ lepton decays, and a corrected Debye screening. The results enable future inclusion of next-to-leading order contributions and 2↔3 interactions for gluons already implemented in BAMPS, as well as planned incorporation of hadronization and decay to electrons for direct comparison with heavy flavor electron data.
