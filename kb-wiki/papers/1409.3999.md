# Short-range correlations in isospin symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter: a microscopic perspective

> Short-range correlations in nuclear and neutron matter are examined through the properties of the correlated wave function obtained by solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation. Tensor correlations are explored through the dominant tensor-driven transition and central correlations through the singlet and triplet S waves. Predictions from a popular meson-theoretic nucleon-nucleon potential employed in the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach are compared with those from two- and three-body high-quality chiral interactions in Brueckner G-matrix calculations. Short-range correlations in symmetric matter are remarkably stronger than in neutron matter. It is found that short-range correlations are very model dependent and have a large impact on the symmetry energy above normal density.

## Key claim

Short-range correlations (SRC) in symmetric nuclear matter are remarkably stronger than in neutron matter, but the degree of this difference is very model dependent, with chiral interactions yielding more localized momentum distributions than meson-theoretic potentials.

## Method

The correlated wave function was obtained by solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation for nuclear matter, and the defect function (the difference between correlated and uncorrelated wave functions) was computed to quantify SRC. Tensor correlations were explored through the dominant tensor-driven transition in the coupled 3S1-3D1 channel, while central correlations were examined through the singlet 1S0 and triplet 3S1 states. Calculations were performed using the Bonn B meson-theoretic potential within the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach, and compared with results from high-quality chiral two-body forces at N3LO combined with consistent three-body forces at N2LO in Brueckner G-matrix calculations.

## Key numbers

- In symmetric nuclear matter at kF=1.4 fm-1, the wound integral contributions are: 0.060 for 3S1-3D1, 0.022 for 3S1-3S1, and 0.019 for 1S0 (Bonn B + DBHF)
- The total wound integral in symmetric matter (0.130) is approximately one to two orders of magnitude larger than in neutron matter (0.0133) at the same total density of 0.185 fm-3 (Bonn B + DBHF)
- With chiral NN + 3BF, the wound integral is 0.099 in symmetric matter versus 0.003 in neutron matter at the same density
- Nearly 12% out of 13% of the total wound integral strength in symmetric matter comes from J=1 states, particularly 3S1-3D1
- The chiral 3BF increases the probability amplitude for the 3S1-3D1 transition near normal density by about 30% around 1.5-2 fm-1

## Context

This work extends the study of short-range correlations from earlier Brueckner nuclear matter calculations (Haftel and Tabakin, 1970) to modern chiral interactions, and connects to experimental measurements from CLAS (Egiyan et al., 2006) and Hall A (Korover et al., 2014) showing strong np pair correlations. The results enable better understanding of the symmetry energy above normal density, which is critical for neutron star structure, and complement self-consistent Green's function studies of tensor correlations (Carbone et al., 2014; Rios et al., 2014) and momentum distributions in light nuclei (Wiringa et al., 2014).
