# Total Gluon Helicity from Lattice without Effective Theory Matching

> We propose two approaches for extracting the total gluon helicity contribution to proton spin from lattice QCD, one from local operator matrix elements in a fixed gauge accessible on lattice with feasible renormalization, and the other from gauge-invariant nonlocal gluon correlators. Neither of these approaches requires a matching procedure when converted to the MS scheme. Our proposal resolves a long-standing inconsistency in the literature regarding lattice calculations of the total gluon helicity, and has the potential to greatly facilitate these calculations.

## Key claim

The authors propose two lattice QCD approaches for extracting the total gluon helicity contribution to proton spin that, unlike previous methods, do not require a perturbative matching procedure when converting results to the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme, thereby resolving an inconsistency in the literature regarding the factorization of the total gluon helicity.

## Method

The first approach uses the nonforward matrix element of the topological current $K^\mu$ in Coulomb gauge, which at large proton momentum $P_z$ differs from the lightcone gauge result only by higher-twist contributions; renormalization is performed using the RI/MOM scheme with off-shell forward matrix elements in Landau gauge. The second approach uses gauge-invariant nonlocal gluon quasi-light-front correlations (quasi-LF correlators) that can be directly connected to the total gluon helicity without perturbative matching.

## Key numbers

- Quark spin contributes approximately 30% of the proton spin, as established by the European Muon Collaboration experiment and subsequent analyses (deFlorian 2009, Nocera 2014, Ethier 2017).
- The matching coefficients for the LaMET factorization of the total gluon helicity at next-to-leading order are: ${\cal C}_{gg}=1+{a_s C_A}\frac 7 3\ln \frac{P_z^2}{\mu^2}+fin.$ and ${\cal C}_{gq}={a_s C_F}\frac 4 3\ln \frac{P_z^2}{\mu^2}+fin.$, where $a_s=\alpha_s/(4\pi)$.
- The matching coefficients for the gluon helicity distribution at next-to-leading order in the physical region $0<x<y<1$ are: $C_{gg}=\delta\left(1-\frac{x}{y}\right)+4a_sC_A\left\{\frac{(2 x^2-3 x y+2 y^2)}{(x-y)\, y}\left(\ln \frac{\mu ^2}{4y^2P_z^2}-\ln\frac{ x (y-x)}{y^2}\right)+fin.\right\}$ and $C_{gq}=\delta\left(1-\frac{x}{y}\right)+4a_sC_F\left\{\frac{(x-2y)}{y}\left(\ln \frac{\mu ^2}{4y^2P_z^2}-\ln\frac{x (y-x)}{y^2}\right)+fin.\right\}$.

## Context

This work extends the LaMET framework for gluon helicity calculations (Ji 2013, Ji 2014) and addresses a long-standing inconsistency between the factorization of the total gluon helicity from the matrix element of $\vec{E}\times\vec{A}$ in fixed gauge and the factorization of the gluon helicity distribution from quasi-LF correlations. The proposal resolves practical difficulties in existing lattice calculations, such as those by Yang (2016) which used Coulomb gauge with poor convergence of lattice perturbation theory for renormalization, and enables feasible nonperturbative renormalization using the RI/MOM scheme (Martinelli 1994).
