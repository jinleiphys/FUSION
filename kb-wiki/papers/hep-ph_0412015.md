# Chromomagnetic Instability and the LOFF state in a two flavor Color Superconductor

> We explore the relation between the chromomagnetic instability of a homogeneous two flavor color superconductor and the LOFF state. We perturb the free energy of the 2SC by generating a small net momentum for the quark pair. We find that the imaginary Meissner mass of a particular gluon implies that the LOFF state is energetically favored.

## Key claim

The chromomagnetic instability (negative squared Meissner mass) of the eighth gluon in a homogeneous two-flavor color superconductor (2SC) is equivalent to a negative momentum susceptibility, which implies that a LOFF (Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell) state with a small net Cooper-pair momentum is energetically favored over the homogeneous 2SC phase. This equivalence holds for all temperatures in the super phase, and the threshold mismatch value for the instability coincides with the known LOFF threshold along the second-order transition line.

## Method

The authors use an NJL (Nambu–Jona-Lasinio) effective Lagrangian for two massless quark flavors (u and d) coupled to external color and electromagnetic gauge fields, with a diquark condensate as the order parameter. They compute the Meissner screening masses for the gluons corresponding to the broken generators of SU(3)_c × U(1)_em via the gluon polarization tensor at one-loop order, and define a momentum susceptibility κ as the coefficient of q² in the free-energy expansion for a small net momentum 2q of the Cooper pair. The calculation is performed analytically near the second-order transition temperature (Δ ≪ k_B T) using an expansion of the quark propagator to second order in the gap Δ, with the approximation δ′ = 0 (color neutrality neglected) and δ = (μ_d − μ_u)/2.

## Key numbers

* The squared Meissner mass for gluons 4,5,6,7 is m²|₄,₅,₆,₇ = −(g² μ² Δ²)/(96 π⁴ (k_B T)²) Re ψ″(½ + iδ/(2π k_B T)).
* The squared Meissner mass for the eighth gluon is m²|₈ = −(g² μ² Δ²)/(72 π⁴ (k_B T)²) (1 + e²/(3g²)) Re ψ″(½ + iδ/(2π k_B T)).
* The momentum susceptibility is κ = −(μ² Δ²)/(6 π⁴ (k_B T)²) Re ψ″(½ + iδ/(2π k_B T)).
* The function −Re ψ″(½ + iδ/(2π k_B T)) changes sign at δ/(2π k_B T) ≃ 0.3041, which is the critical value for the chromomagnetic instability.
* The threshold mismatch along the transition line is δ/Δ₀ = 0.6082, where Δ₀ is the zero-temperature gap in the absence of mismatch.
* In the absence of mismatch (δ = 0), the Meissner masses become positive: m²|₄,₅,₆,₇ = 7ζ(3) g² μ² Δ²/(48 π⁴ (k_B T)²) and m²|₈ = 7ζ(3) g² μ² Δ²/(36 π⁴ (k_B T)²) (1 + e²/(3g²)), with ζ(3) ≈ 1.20206.

## Context

This work extends the discovery by Huang and Shovkovy (2004) of negative squared chromomagnetic masses in the gapless 2SC (g2SC) phase by explicitly linking that instability to the LOFF pairing mechanism. The authors show that the condition for the imaginary Meissner mass of the eighth gluon is identical to the condition for a negative momentum susceptibility, which signals a lower free energy for the LOFF state, analogous to the nonrelativistic superfluid analysis of Wu and Yip (2003). The threshold value δ/Δ₀ = 0.6082 matches the known LOFF threshold from electronic superconductors (Tanaka, 1998), and the results enable future work on whether the LOFF state represents the true equilibrium phase at moderate baryon density, including calculations of Meissner masses at the LOFF minimum and the effects of charge neutrality.
