# Non-linear Supersymmetry for non-Hermitian, non-diagonalizable Hamiltonians: I. General properties

> We study complex potentials and related non-diagonalizable Hamiltonians with special emphasis on formal definitions of associated functions and Jordan cells. The nonlinear SUSY for complex potentials is considered and the theorems characterizing its structure are presented. We present the class of potentials invariant under SUSY transformations for non-diagonalizable Hamiltonians and formulate several results concerning the properties of associated functions . We comment on the applicability of these results for softly non-Hermitian PT-symmetric Hamiltonians. The role of SUSY (Darboux) transformations in increasing/decreasing of Jordancells in SUSY partner Hamiltonians is thoroughly analyzed and summarized in the Index Theorem. The properties of non-diagonalizable Hamiltonians as well as the Index Theorem are illustrated in the solvable examples of non-Hermitian reflectionless Hamiltonians . The rigorous proofs are relegated to the Part II of this paper. At last, some peculiarities in resolution of identity for discrete and continuous spectra with a zero-energy bound state at threshold are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper extends non-linear supersymmetry (SUSY) methods to non-Hermitian, non-diagonalizable Hamiltonians with complex potentials, proving that the structure of Jordan cells in SUSY partner Hamiltonians is governed by an Index Theorem, but the rigorous proofs and the full classification of normalizable associated functions are relegated to a companion paper (Part II), and the Index Theorem is proven only for "softly non-Hermitian" potentials where the imaginary part vanishes asymptotically relative to the real part.

## Method

The authors define biorthogonal bases of eigenfunctions and associated functions for non-diagonalizable Schrödinger Hamiltonians with complex potentials, establishing the existence of such bases and their compatibility with PT-symmetry when unbroken. They then construct non-linear SUSY algebras using N-th order differential intertwining operators (supercharges) with transposition symmetry, and prove two theorems: one characterizing the polynomial closure of the SUSY algebra via matrix representations on zero-mode subspaces, and a "strip-off" theorem that determines when intertwining operators can be factorized into products of elementary Darboux operators and powers of (λ − h). The Index Theorem relating Jordan cell dimensions of SUSY partner Hamiltonians is stated and illustrated with solvable examples of non-Hermitian reflectionless Hamiltonians derived from the free particle via SUSY transformations.

## Key numbers

- The supercharge components q±N are N-th order differential operators with leading coefficient w±N = const ≡ (∓1)N.
- The strip-off theorem reduces the intertwining operator order from N to M = N − 2 Σm l=1 δkl = Σn j=1 kj, where δkl are sizes of smallest cells in paired Jordan cells.
- The biorthogonal basis normalization condition yields γn,0² = 1 for PT-symmetric systems with unbroken PT-symmetry.
- The example of level confluence shows that coalescence of two levels (each of algebraic multiplicity 1) produces a Jordan cell of second order, and coalescence of three levels produces a Jordan cell of third order.

## Context

This work extends the non-linear SUSY formalism previously developed for Hermitian Hamiltonians (Andrianov, Cannata, Sokolov, 2000s) to complex, non-Hermitian potentials, including PT-symmetric systems studied by Bender (1998), Bender and Boettcher (1998), and Mostafazadeh (2002). It builds on the theory of non-diagonalizable Hamiltonians with Jordan cells, which appear at level crossing in atomic and molecular spectra (Solov'ev, 1980s) and in optics (Berry, 2004). The results enable the quantum design of non-Hermitian systems with prescribed Jordan cell structures via Darboux-Crum transformations, and provide a framework for understanding the spectral properties of PT-symmetric Hamiltonians with real spectra. The paper explicitly notes that the Index Theorem and related lemmas are proven only for softly non-Hermitian potentials, with extension to semihard non-Hermiticity promised for future work.
