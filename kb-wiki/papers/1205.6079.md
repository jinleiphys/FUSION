# Non-extensive statistics, relativistic kinetic theory and fluid dynamics

> Experimental particle spectra can be successfully described by power-law tailed energy distributions characteristic to canonical equilibrium distributions associated to Rényi's or Tsallis' entropy formula - over a wide range of energies, colliding system sizes, and produced hadron sorts. In order to derive its evolution one needs a corresponding dynamical description of the system which results in such final state observables. The equations of relativistic fluid dynamics are obtained from a non-extensive Boltzmann equation consistent with Tsallis' non-extensive $q$-entropy formula. The transport coefficients like shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and heat conductivity are evaluate based on a linearized collision integral.

## Key claim

The paper derives relativistic fluid dynamics from a non-extensive Boltzmann equation consistent with Tsallis' $q$-entropy formula, but the derivation relies on a specific postulate for the $q$-generalized Stosszahlansatz (molecular chaos assumption) and a linearized collision integral, meaning the resulting transport coefficients are only valid for small deviations from local $q$-equilibrium.

## Method

The authors start from a non-extensive relativistic Boltzmann equation (NEBE) where the distribution function is raised to the power $q$ in the streaming term, and the collision integral uses a $q$-generalized Stosszahlansatz. They apply the first-order Chapman-Enskog method to linearize the NEBE around local $q$-equilibrium, obtaining expressions for the shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and heat conductivity in terms of $q$-generalized thermodynamic integrals. The transport coefficients are derived by solving the linearized integro-differential equation using an ansatz for the deviation function $\phi_k$ with unknown coefficients $\varphi_i$.

## Key numbers

- The $q$-parameter is restricted to the range $q \in [0, 2]$ for the Tsallis entropy formula.
- The $q$-generalized entropy four-current is defined as $S^{\mu} = -\int dK\ k^{\mu} [(f_k)^q \ln_q(f_k) - f_k]$, where $dK = g d^3\mathbf{k}/((2\pi)^3 k^0)$.
- The equilibrium distribution function is $f_{0k} = \exp_q(\alpha_0 - \beta_0 k^{\mu} u_{\mu})$, where $\beta_0 = 1/T$ and $\alpha_0 = \mu/T$.
- The bulk viscous pressure $\Pi$ is related to the expansion scalar $\theta$ through coefficients $\varphi_0, \varphi_1, \varphi_2$ expressed in terms of $\mathcal{K}_{q(i,j)}$ thermodynamic integrals, with denominator $D_{\Pi}$ involving combinations of $\mathcal{K}_{q(2,1)}$, $\mathcal{K}_{q(3,1)}$, $\mathcal{K}_{q(4,1)}$, $\mathcal{K}_{q(1,0)}$, $\mathcal{K}_{q(2,0)}$, $\mathcal{K}_{q(3,0)}$, and $\mathcal{K}_{q(4,0)}$.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic perfect $q$-hydrodynamics developed by Osada and Wilk (2008) by providing the first full derivation of dissipative fluid dynamics from a non-extensive Boltzmann equation, including explicit expressions for transport coefficients. It builds on the kinetic theory framework of Lavagno (2002) and the thermodynamic integrals formalism of Biró (2011), and enables future calculations of causal relativistic fluid dynamics using Grad's method of moments within the non-extensive framework.
