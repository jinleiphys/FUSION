# Microscopic Analysis of Order Parameters in Nuclear Quantum Phase Transitions

> Microscopic signatures of nuclear ground-state shape phase transitions in Nd isotopes are studied using excitation spectra and collective wave functions obtained by diagonalization of a five-dimensional Hamiltonian for quadrupole vibrational and rotational degrees of freedom, with parameters determined by constrained self-consistent relativistic mean-field calculations for triaxial shapes. As a function of the physical control parameter -- the number of nucleons, energy gaps between the ground state and the excited vibrational states with zero angular momentum, isomer shifts, and monopole transition strengths, exhibit sharp discontinuities at neutron number N=90, characteristic of a first-order quantum phase transition.

## Key claim
The paper claims that observables related to order parameters for a first-order quantum phase transition between spherical and axially deformed shapes in Nd isotopes exhibit sharp discontinuities at neutron number N=90, and that these discontinuities are not significantly smoothed out by the finiteness of the nuclear system, contrary to what is typically seen in Ising-type boson models.

## Method
Constrained self-consistent relativistic mean-field calculations with a point-coupling functional (PC-F1) and a density-independent delta-force for pairing in the BCS approximation were performed to generate triaxial quadrupole binding energy maps in the beta-gamma plane for even-even Nd isotopes (N=84-96). The parameters for a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian for quadrupole vibrational and rotational degrees of freedom (mass parameters, moments of inertia, zero-point energy corrections) were determined from these mean-field solutions, and the Hamiltonian was diagonalized to obtain excitation energies and collective wave functions for computing observables.

## Key numbers
- The energy gap between the ground state and the first excited 0+ state exhibits a pronounced dip at neutron number N=90 in Nd isotopes.
- The isomer shift between the first 2+ state and the ground state shows a pronounced discontinuity between N=88 and N=92.
- The isomer shift between the 0+2 state and the ground state changes sign between N=90 and N=92, with a steep rise for N less than or equal to 90.
- The monopole transition strength rho^2(E0; 0+2 to 0+1) exhibits a markedly sharp increase toward N=90 and remains large in well-deformed nuclei 152,154,156Nd.

## Context
This work extends the microscopic description of nuclear shape quantum phase transitions from previous studies by the same group (Nikšić 2007, Li 2009) that used either generator coordinate method with axial symmetry or five-dimensional Hamiltonian calculations. It directly computes observables related to order parameters rather than relying on deformation parameters, which are not observables, and compares to the phenomenological analysis of Iachello and Zamfir (2004) on QPT in mesoscopic systems. The results enable a fully consistent microscopic description of shape QPT in the rare-earth region around N=90 using universal energy density functionals.
