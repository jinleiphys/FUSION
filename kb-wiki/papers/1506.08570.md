# Elastic nucleon form factors

> The relativized hypercentral Constituent Quark Model is used for the calculation of the elastic electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon. The results are compared with the recent measurements at Jlab.

# Elastic nucleon form factors

## Key claim
The relativized hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM), when augmented with constituent quark form factors whose free parameters are fitted to the ratio R, the proton magnetic form factor, and the neutron electric and magnetic form factors, reproduces the Jefferson Laboratory data showing that the ratio μ_p G_E^p/G_M^p deviates strongly from 1 and decreases almost linearly for Q^2 ≥ 1 (GeV/c)^2, with a possible zero at Q^2 ≈ 8 (GeV/c)^2.

## Method
The authors solve a semirelativistic Hamiltonian with relativistic kinetic energy, a linear-plus-Coulomb hypercentral potential, and hyperfine interaction using variational techniques in the nucleon rest frame. The eigenfunctions are interpreted as mass operator eigenfunctions in a Bakamjian-Thomas point-form dynamics, and nucleon states in the Breit frame are obtained via canonical boosts. The electromagnetic current is written in impulse approximation as a sum of one-body quark currents, and constituent quark form factors are introduced with parameters fitted to experimental data for the ratio R, G_M^p, G_E^n, and G_M^n.

## Key numbers
- The hypercentral potential parameters are τ = 4.59 and α = 1.61 fm^{-2}.
- The ratio μ_p G_E^p/G_M^p deviates strongly from 1 for Q^2 ≥ 1 (GeV/c)^2, with a possible zero at Q^2 ≈ 8 (GeV/c)^2.
- Nonrelativistic calculations predict R = μ_p G_E^p/G_M^p = 1; introducing hyperfine interaction gives R = 0.99.
- Perturbative QCD predicts F_{1p} ∝ 1/Q^4 and F_{2p} ∝ 1/Q^6 at high Q^2, so Q^2 F_{2p}/F_{1p} should reach a constant value.
- Ralston et al. showed that with nonzero orbital angular momentum, F_{2p}/F_{1p} goes as 1/Q.

## Context
This work extends the hypercentral Constituent Quark Model previously used for photocouplings, electromagnetic transition amplitudes, and elastic nucleon form factors (De Sanctis et al., earlier work) by introducing a semirelativistic version with relativistic kinetic energy and constituent quark form factors. The results are compared with Jefferson Laboratory polarization transfer measurements (Milbrath, 1999; Punjabi, 2005) that revealed the deviation of the proton form factor ratio from unity, contradicting earlier expectations. The calculations enable tests of perturbative QCD scaling predictions for the Dirac and Pauli form factors at higher Q^2 and help discriminate among models of nucleon structure regarding the possible zero in the electric form factor.
