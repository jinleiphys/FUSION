# Nuclear energy density functional from chiral pion-nucleon dynamics revisited

> We use a recently improved density-matrix expansion to calculate the nuclear energy density functional in the framework of in-medium chiral perturbation theory. Our calculation treats systematically the effects from $1π$-exchange, iterated $1π$-exchange, and irreducible $2π$-exchange with intermediate $Δ$-isobar excitations, including Pauli-blocking corrections up to three-loop order. We find that the effective nucleon mass $M^*(ρ)$ entering the energy density functional is identical to the one of Fermi-liquid theory when employing the improved density-matrix expansion. The strength $F_\nabla(ρ)$ of the $(\vec\nabla ρ)^2$ surface-term as provided by the pion-exchange dynamics is in good agreement with that of phenomenological Skyrme forces in the density region $ρ_0/2 <ρ<ρ_0$. The spin-orbit coupling strength $F_{so}(ρ)$ receives contributions from iterated $1π$-exchange (of the ``wrong sign'') and from three-nucleon interactions mediated by $2π$-exchange with virtual $Δ$-excitation (of the ``correct sign''). In the region around $ρ_0/2 \simeq 0.08 $fm$^{-3}$ where the spin-orbit interaction in nuclei gains most of its weight these two components tend to cancel, thus leaving all room for the short-range spin-orbit interaction. The strength function $F_J(ρ)$ multiplying the square of the spin-orbit density comes out much larger than in phenomenological Skyrme forces and it has a pronounced density dependence.

# Nuclear energy density functional from chiral pion-nucleon dynamics revisited

## Key claim
The improved density-matrix expansion of Gebremariam, Duguet and Bogner (2009) yields an effective nucleon mass $M^*(\rho)$ in the energy density functional that is identical to the Fermi-liquid theory Landau mass, a consistency that did not hold with the original Negele-Vautherin density-matrix expansion; however, the spin-orbit coupling strength $F_{so}(\rho)$ from iterated $1\pi$-exchange (wrong sign) and $2\pi$-exchange with $\Delta$-excitation (correct sign) tend to cancel near $\rho_0/2$, leaving the short-range spin-orbit interaction as the dominant source.

## Method
The authors used the improved phase-space-averaged density-matrix expansion of Gebremariam, Duguet and Bogner (2009) to compute the nuclear energy density functional from in-medium chiral perturbation theory. They evaluated contributions from $1\pi$-exchange, iterated $1\pi$-exchange, and irreducible $2\pi$-exchange with intermediate $\Delta$-isobar excitations, including Pauli-blocking corrections up to three-loop order, for isospin-symmetric nuclear matter ($N=Z$). The density-dependent strength functions $F_\tau(\rho)$, $F_\nabla(\rho)$, $F_{so}(\rho)$, and $F_J(\rho)$ were derived analytically from diagrammatic calculations using the Fourier-transformed medium insertion.

## Key numbers
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation density: $M^*(\rho_0) = 0.874M$ at $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$, compatible with the phenomenological Skyrme force range $0.7 < M^*(\rho_0)/M < 1$.
- The strength function $F_\nabla(\rho)$ from pion-exchange dynamics agrees well with phenomenological Skyrme forces in the density region $\rho_0/2 < \rho < \rho_0$, where $\rho_0/2 \simeq 0.08$ fm$^{-3}$.
- The spin-orbit coupling strength $F_{so}(\rho)$ receives contributions from iterated $1\pi$-exchange (wrong sign) and from three-nucleon interactions mediated by $2\pi$-exchange with virtual $\Delta$-excitation (correct sign), which tend to cancel near $\rho_0/2 \simeq 0.08$ fm$^{-3}$.
- The strength function $F_J(\rho)$ multiplying the square of the spin-orbit density comes out much larger than in phenomenological Skyrme forces and has a pronounced density dependence.
- Physical input parameters: $g_A = 1.3$, $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV, $m_\pi = 135$ MeV, $M = 939$ MeV, $\Delta = 293$ MeV (delta-nucleon mass splitting).

## Context
This work extends the earlier calculations of the nuclear energy density functional from chiral pion-nucleon dynamics by Kaiser and Weise (2008, 2009) that used the original Negele-Vautherin density-matrix expansion (1972). The improved density-matrix expansion of Gebremariam, Duguet and Bogner (2009) corrects a 50% error in the Fock energy from tensor forces that plagued the Negele-Vautherin expansion, reducing it to a few percent accuracy. The results enable more reliable predictions for nuclear structure calculations of medium-mass and heavy nuclei using chiral effective field theory, and they show that the long-range pion-exchange dynamics alone cannot account for the empirical spin-orbit interaction, requiring short-range contributions.
