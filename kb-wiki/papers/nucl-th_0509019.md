# Insensitivity of the elastic proton-nucleus reaction to the neutron radius of 208Pb

> The sensitivity--or rather insensitivity--of the elastic proton-nucleus reaction to the neutron radius of 208Pb is investigated using a non-relativistic impulse-approximation approach. The energy region (Tlab=500 MeV and Tlab=800 MeV) is selected so that the impulse approximation may be safely assumed. Therefore, only free nucleon-nucleon scattering data are used as input for the optical potential. Further, the optical potential includes proton and neutron ground-state densities that are generated from accurately-calibrated models. Even so, these models yield a wide range of values (from 0.13 fm to 0.28 fm) for the poorly known neutron skin thickness in 208Pb. An excellent description of the experimental cross section is obtained with all neutron densities. We have invoked analytic insights developed within the eikonal approximation to understand the insensitivity of the differential cross section to the various neutron densities. As the diffractive oscillations of the cross sections are controlled by the matter radius of the nucleus, the large spread in the neutron skin among the various models gets diluted into a mere 1.5% difference in the matter radius. This renders ineffective the elastic reaction as a precision tool for the measurement of neutron radii.

## Key claim

The elastic proton-nucleus differential cross section for 208Pb at Tlab = 500 MeV and 800 MeV is insensitive to the neutron skin thickness because the large spread in neutron skin values (0.13 fm to 0.28 fm) among different nuclear-structure models translates into only a 1.5% difference in the matter radius, which controls the diffractive oscillations.

## Method

The authors used a non-relativistic impulse-approximation Kerman-McManus-Thaler (KMT) optical potential with free nucleon-nucleon t-matrices as input, incorporating proton and neutron ground-state densities from four relativistic mean-field models (NL3, FSUGold, FSUGold4, FSUGold5) that yield neutron skins from 0.13 fm to 0.28 fm while keeping the charge radius fixed at its experimental value. They solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in momentum space for the elastic scattering amplitude, and also employed the eikonal approximation to gain analytic insight into the geometric origin of the cross section features.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness range across models: 0.13 fm to 0.28 fm for 208Pb
- Charge radius of 208Pb: 5.5013(7) fm (experimental, 0.01% accuracy)
- The 0.15 fm spread in neutron skin translates into a 1.5% difference in the matter radius
- Binding energy per nucleon for 208Pb: experimental 7.87 MeV; NL3 gives 7.88 MeV; FSUGold gives 7.89 MeV; FSUGold4 gives 8.10 MeV; FSUGold5 gives 8.42 MeV
- Charge radius predictions: NL3 gives 5.51 fm; FSUGold, FSUGold4, FSUGold5 all give 5.52 fm (experimental 5.50 fm)
- Calculations performed at Tlab = 500 MeV and Tlab = 800 MeV
- Cross section drops by about 8 orders of magnitude at momentum transfers q > 3.5 fm

## Context

This work extends the analysis of proton-nucleus elastic scattering for neutron radius extraction, following studies by Karataglidis et al. (2002) who suggested a neutron skin of about 0.17 fm using g-folding potentials, Clark et al. (2003) who reported 0.083-0.111 fm from a global Dirac-phenomenological analysis, and Dupuis et al. (2005) who found 0.13-0.17 fm. The paper contradicts the notion that elastic proton scattering can serve as a precision tool for neutron radii, instead supporting the Parity Radius Experiment (PREX) at Jefferson Laboratory as the preferred method for a model-independent measurement of the neutron radius of 208Pb to within 0.05 fm. The findings enable a clearer focus on parity-violating electron scattering and on giant resonance studies, such as those by Piekarewicz (2004) and Todd-Rutel and Piekarewicz (2005) who constrained the neutron skin to less than 0.21 fm.
