# Working group on $ππ$ and $π$N interactions - Summary

> This is the summary of the working group on $ππ$ and $π$N interactions of the Chiral Dynamics Workshop in Mainz, September 1-5, 1997. Each talk is represented by an extended one page abstract. Some additional remarks by the convenors are added

## Key claim

This paper summarizes the proceedings of the working group on ππ and πN interactions at the 1997 Chiral Dynamics Workshop in Mainz; while the abstract states that each talk is represented by an extended one-page abstract, the convenors note that considerable progress has been made since the 1994 MIT workshop but that several theoretical challenges remain, including the need for systematic treatment of electromagnetic corrections and refinement of partial wave analyses.

## Method

The working group convened theoretical and experimental contributions on ππ and πN interactions, with presentations covering two-loop chiral perturbation theory calculations for ππ scattering, Roy equation analyses, meson exchange models, hadronic atom lifetime calculations, and experimental measurements from Kℓ4 decays (E865 at BNL), pion-induced pion production (CHAOS at TRIUMF), and the DIRAC experiment at CERN. For πN, heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations to order q⁴ were presented, along with renormalization of the one-loop generating functional.

- Electromagnetic correction to π⁰π⁰ → π⁰π⁰ scattering length: a₀(00;00) = 0.034, compared to 0.038 in the isospin limit (5% decrease)

- Pionium lifetime correction from strong interaction pole displacement: δ_S = -0.26%

## Context

This working group summary extends the discussions from the 1994 MIT workshop on chiral dynamics, reporting progress in two-loop chiral perturbation theory calculations for ππ scattering (Knecht et al., 1995; Bijnens et al., 1996) and heavy baryon ChPT for πN scattering (Ecker, 1994; Müller and Meißner, 1997). The results enable precise comparisons with forthcoming data from the DIRAC experiment at CERN (pionium lifetime), Kℓ4 experiments E865 at Brookhaven and KLOE at DAΦNE, and improved determinations of πN low-energy constants and the σ-term. Open questions include the systematic treatment of electromagnetic corrections to ππ and πN processes and the refinement of partial wave analyses to provide precise input for the chiral perturbation theory community.
