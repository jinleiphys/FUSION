# Determining the NJL Coupling and AMM in Magnetized QCD Matter via Machine Learning

> In this study, we investigate the phase structure of magnetized QCD matter by determining the field-dependent parameters of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model through a physics-informed machine learning framework. Specifically, we focus on extracting the optimal functional forms for the running coupling constant $G(eB)$ and the quark anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) ratio $v_2(eB)$, utilizing lattice QCD-computed quark condensate data as the ``ground truth". By embedding the NJL gap equation as a differentiable physics-constrained module, our neural network pipeline identifies continuous parameter functions that accurately reproduce the inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) effect. Our results demonstrate that the magnetic field smoothly suppresses both $G$ and $v_2$. This approach not only bridges the gap between effective models and lattice data but also provides new microscopic insights into the response of the QCD vacuum to strong magnetic fields.

# Determining the NJL Coupling and AMM in Magnetized QCD Matter via Machine Learning

## Key claim
The magnetic field smoothly suppresses both the NJL coupling constant $G(eB)$ and the quark anomalous magnetic moment ratio $v_2(eB)$, enabling the NJL model to reproduce the inverse magnetic catalysis effect observed in lattice QCD, but the reliability of the extracted parameters may decrease when $|eB| > \Lambda^2 \approx 0.4058$ GeV$^2$, where the field strength exceeds the ultraviolet cutoff scale.

## Method
The authors embed the NJL gap equation as a differentiable physics-constrained module within a neural network pipeline, using lattice QCD-computed quark condensate data as ground truth. Two fully connected neural networks with six hidden layers each parameterize $G(eB)$ and $v_2(eB)$ as continuous functions of the magnetic field, with feature-expanded inputs including polynomial, logarithmic, and trigonometric terms. The networks are trained via a multi-objective loss function that combines data-fidelity terms (normalized critical temperature boundary loss and constituent quark mass loss within $\pm 5\%$ tolerance) with physics-informed regularizers (monotonicity of the dynamical quark mass and magnetic catalysis/inverse magnetic catalysis behavior of the condensate at different temperatures).

## Key numbers
- The coupling constant $G(eB)$ decreases from $4.805 \pm 0.012$ GeV$^{-2}$ at $eB = 0$ to $3.550 \pm 0.031$ GeV$^{-2}$ at $eB = 0.6$ GeV$^2$, with relative uncertainty $< 0.9\%$.
- The AMM ratio $v_2(eB)$ decreases from $0.771 \pm 0.057$ GeV$^{-3}$ at $eB = 0$ to $0.734 \pm 0.059$ GeV$^{-3}$ at $eB = 0.6$ GeV$^2$, with approximately $8\%$ relative uncertainty.
- The normalized pseudo-critical temperature $T_c(eB)/T_c(eB=0)$ from the framework agrees with lattice QCD data from Bali (2012) to within a maximum discrepancy of less than $1\%$.
- The vacuum NJL parameters are fixed as $G = 4.79877$ GeV$^{-2}$, $\Lambda = 0.63704$ GeV, and $m_u = m_d = 5$ MeV.
- The quark AMM is estimated to be of order $\kappa \approx 0.75$ GeV$^{-3} \times \sigma^2$.

## Context
This work extends prior efforts to incorporate magnetic-field-dependent couplings into the NJL model, following the approach of Farias (2014), Ferreira (2014), and others who showed that promoting $G$ to $G(eB)$ can reproduce inverse magnetic catalysis. It also builds on studies of the quark anomalous magnetic moment by Chang (2010), Lin (2022), and Kawaguchi (2022), who proposed a form proportional to $\sigma^2$. The machine learning framework provides a general paradigm for bridging lattice QCD data with effective models, enabling future extensions to the Polyakov-loop NJL model, (2+1)-flavor systems, and finite baryon density studies to locate the critical end point under extreme magnetic conditions.
