# Lattice study on kaon pion scattering length in the $I=3/2$ channel

> Using the tadpole improved Wilson quark action on small, coarse and anisotropic lattices, $Kπ$ scattering length in the $I=3/2$ channel is calculated within quenched approximation. The results are extrapolated towards the chiral and physical kaon mass region. Finite volume and finite lattice spacing errors are also analyzed and a result in the infinite volume and continuum limit is obtained. Our result is compared with the results obtained using Roy equations, Chiral Perturbation Theory, dispersion relations and the experimental data.

## Key claim

Using quenched lattice QCD with tadpole improved Wilson fermions on small, coarse, anisotropic lattices, the authors obtain a kaon-pion scattering length in the I=3/2 channel that is consistent with one-loop Chiral Perturbation Theory and early dispersion relation results, but smaller in magnitude than the range of experimental values.

## Method

The authors compute Kπ correlation functions on quenched gauge configurations with lattice sizes 6³×40, 8³×40, and 10³×50 at five gauge couplings (β=1.9, 2.2, 2.4, 2.6, 3.0), corresponding to spatial lattice spacings between roughly 0.1 fm and 0.4 fm and physical volumes from 0.7 fm to 4.0 fm. Energy shifts of the two-meson system are extracted from ratios of correlation functions and converted to scattering lengths via Lüscher's finite-volume formula. The results are extrapolated first in the up/down quark mass to the chiral limit, then in the strange quark mass to the physical kaon mass, followed by infinite-volume and continuum-limit extrapolations.

## Key numbers

* The quantity F = a₀^(3/2) m_ρ² / μ_{Kπ} after all extrapolations is (-1.87 ± 0.55).
* The resulting Kπ scattering length in the I=3/2 channel is a₀^(3/2) m_π = (-0.048 ± 0.014).
* This is compared with the one-loop chiral perturbation theory result of (-0.05 ± 0.02) and the tree-level/early dispersion relation result of -0.07.
* Experimental results for a₀^(3/2) m_π range between -0.13 and -0.05, with a recent dispersion relation analysis giving -0.129 ± 0.006 (Zheng 2004).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous lattice studies of ππ scattering in the I=2 channel (Chuan 2002) and KN scattering in the I=1 channel (Chuan 2004) to the Kπ system. The result is compared with predictions from Chiral Perturbation Theory (Meissner 1991), Roy equations (Buettiker 2003), dispersion relations (Lang 1977, Zheng 2004), and experimental data (Matison 1974, Johannesson 1973, Shaw 1980). The calculation demonstrates that quenched lattice QCD on coarse anisotropic lattices can produce scattering lengths consistent with chiral predictions, and it enables future unquenched calculations with finer lattices to reduce systematic uncertainties.
