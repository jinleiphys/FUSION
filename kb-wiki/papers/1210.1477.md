# Hard four-jet production in pA collisions

> In a suitably chosen back-to-back kinematics, four-jet production in hadronic collisions is known to be dominated by contributions from two independent partonic scattering processes, thus giving experimental access to the structure of generalized two-parton distributions 2GPDs. Here, we show that a combined measurement of the double hard four-jet cross section in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions will allow one to disentangle different sources of two-parton correlations in the proton, that cannot be disentangled with 4-jet measurements in proton-proton collisions alone. To this end, we analyze in detail the structure of 2GPDs in the nucleus (A), we calculate in the independent nucleon approximation all contributions to the double hard four-jet cross section in pA, and we determine corrections arising from the nuclear dependence of single parton distribution functions. We then outline an experimental strategy for determining the longitudinal two-parton correlations in the proton.

## Key claim

A combined measurement of double hard four-jet production in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions can disentangle different sources of two-parton correlations in the proton that cannot be separated using proton-proton data alone, provided the analysis is performed in a back-to-back kinematic regime where the dijet momentum imbalances are much smaller than the individual jet transverse momenta.

## Method

The authors classify all contributions to the nuclear generalized two-parton distribution (₂GPD) in the independent nucleon approximation, distinguishing between cases where both partons originate from the same nucleon (single-nucleon terms) or from two different nucleons (double-nucleon terms). They calculate the double hard four-jet cross section in proton-nucleus collisions by inserting these nuclear ₂GPDs into the factorized cross section formula, and determine corrections arising from the nuclear dependence of single parton distribution functions. The analysis separates contributions from 4→4 processes (two independent 2→2 scatterings) and 3→4 processes (where one parton splits perturbatively into two), and shows how their relative weights change between pp and pA collisions.

## Key numbers

- The effective transverse area S measured at the Tevatron is typically of order 15 mb, a factor ~2 smaller than expectations based on uncorrelated two-parton distributions.
- For a lead nucleus with Wood-Saxon density profile and S = 15 mb, the product S × W(A) is approximately 2.1.
- The double-nucleon scattering contribution to the four-jet cross section scales as O(A^{4/3}), making it A^{1/3}-enhanced compared to single-nucleon contributions that scale linearly with A.
- In pp collisions, the geometrical enhancement of 3→4 relative to 4→4 processes is approximately a factor of 5 (from a ratio of 7/3 from form factor integrals times a combinatorial factor of 2), and this enhancement is absent in pA collisions for sufficiently large A.
- For proton-deuteron collisions with x_i = O(0.01), the nuclear modification factor R_{pD}^{4jet} is approximately 1 + 1.07 K.

## Context

This work extends earlier discussions of double hard four-jet production in proton-nucleus collisions by Strikman and Treleani (2002), incorporating recent pQCD studies by Blok, Dokshitzer, Frankfurt, and Strikman (2010-2012) that clarified the role of 3→4 processes. The analysis enables an experimental strategy for determining longitudinal two-parton correlations in the proton using the upcoming pA run at the LHC, by measuring the nuclear modification factor R_{pA}^{4jet} and extracting the normalized longitudinal correlation function K(x₁',x₂'). The approach contrasts with models that attribute the small effective area S to purely transverse correlations (e.g., hot-spot models of the proton).
