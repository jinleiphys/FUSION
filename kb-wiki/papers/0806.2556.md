# Trionic and quartetting phases in one-dimensional multicomponent ultracold fermions

> We investigate the possible formation of a molecular condensate, which might be, for instance, the analogue of the alpha condensate of nuclear physics, in the context of multicomponent cold atoms fermionic systems. A simple paradigmatic model of N-component fermions with contact interactions loaded into a one-dimensional optical lattice is studied by means of low-energy and numerical approaches. For attractive interaction, a quasi-long-range molecular superfluid phase, formed from bound-states made of N fermions, emerges at low density. We show that trionic and quartetting phases, respectively for N=3,4, extend in a large domain of the phase diagram and are robust against small symmetry-breaking perturbations.

## Key claim
For one-dimensional multicomponent ultracold fermions with attractive contact interactions, a quasi-long-range molecular superfluid phase formed from bound states of N fermions emerges at low density; the trionic (N=3) and quartetting (N=4) phases extend over a large domain of the phase diagram and are robust against small SU(N) symmetry-breaking perturbations.

## Method
The authors studied the one-dimensional N-component fermionic Hubbard model with attractive s-wave contact interactions using a combination of low-energy field theory (Luttinger liquid formalism with spin-charge separation) and density-matrix renormalization group (DMRG) numerical calculations. For N=3 and N=4, they computed equal-time density correlations and molecular (trion/quartet) correlations as a function of distance, extracting the Luttinger parameter K from the power-law decay of the molecular correlations. They also investigated the stability of the molecular superfluid phase by introducing symmetry-breaking perturbations: anisotropic interactions for N=3 (breaking SU(3) to U(1)^3) and an SO(5)-symmetric model for N=4 (breaking SU(4) to SO(5)).

## Key numbers
- For the SU(3) model at density n=1/3 and interaction U/t=-4 with system size L=153, the Luttinger parameter extracted from trion correlations is K=2.7, which exceeds the threshold K > N/√3 ≈ 1.73 for a dominant trionic phase.
- For the SU(4) model at density n=0.25 and U/t=-4 with L=128, the quartet correlations dominate over atomic-density-wave correlations.
- For the anisotropic three-component model (breaking SU(3) symmetry) at density n=1/3, the Luttinger parameter is K=2.65, still above the threshold K > √3 for the trionic phase.
- For the SO(5) model (breaking SU(4) symmetry) with U/t=-4 and V/t=-2 at density n=0.5, the Luttinger parameter is K=2.7, exceeding the threshold K > 2 for the quartetting phase.

## Context
This work extends prior theoretical proposals of trion and quartet formation in ultracold fermionic atoms (Miyake, Rapp, Capponi, Wu 2005) and connects to the concept of alpha-particle condensation in nuclear physics (Schuck). The authors build on their own earlier work (Capponi et al., Physical Review A) and use the Bethe ansatz results for the continuum SU(N) fermionic model with delta interaction. The findings enable experimental exploration of these molecular superfluid phases in spinor ultracold fermionic atoms such as ^6Li (three-component) and spin-3/2 fermions (four-component), and open the door to studying the effects of harmonic trapping potentials via DMRG for quantitative comparisons with future experiments.
