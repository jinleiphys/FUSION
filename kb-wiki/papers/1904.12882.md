# Mapping the Kinematical Regimes of Semi-Inclusive Deep Inelastic Scattering

> We construct a language for identifying kinematical regions of transversely differential semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering cross sections with particular underlying partonic pictures, especially in regions of moderate to low $Q$ where sensitivity to kinematical effects outside the usual very high energy limit becomes non-trivial. The partonic pictures map to power law expansions whose leading contributions ultimately lead to well-known QCD factorization theorems. We propose methods for estimating the consistency of any particular region of overall hadronic kinematics with the kinematics of a given underlying partonic picture. The basic setup of kinematics of semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering is also reviewed in some detail.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a systematic language for identifying which underlying partonic picture (e.g., collinear factorization, TMD factorization, target fragmentation) is appropriate for a given kinematical region of semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering, particularly at moderate to low Q (1-3 GeV) where the boundaries between regimes become ambiguous and require model-dependent assumptions about parton virtuality and non-perturbative effects.

## Method

The authors enumerate a sequence of kinematical tests that probe the consistency of any given hadronic configuration (specified by x_Bj, Q, z_h, and transverse momentum) with a particular partonic picture, using power-counting expansions in m/Q and comparisons of light-cone momentum fractions. They define exact partonic kinematics in both the Breit (photon) frame and the hadron frame, derive transformation laws between frames, and establish relationships between the experimentally accessible variables (x_Bj, z_h, P_BT) and the theoretically natural partonic variables (x_N, z_N, q_T). The method is implemented as a web interface at Ref. [sidistool].

## Key numbers

- Moderate-to-low Q region is defined as Q roughly between 1 GeV and 3 GeV, with Bjorken-x not too far below the valence region
- The average final state hadron multiplicity in such measurements is typically less than about 3 in the valence region of Bjorken-x
- The relationship between z_N and z_h is given by z_N = z_h (1 + O(m^4/Q^4)) in the current fragmentation region, where m represents generic small mass scales
- The hadron frame photon transverse momentum q_T,H is approximately equal to -P_BT/z_h when neglecting m/Q-suppressed corrections

## Context

This work extends the kinematical framework of Collins (2011) and the Trento conventions of Bacchetta (2004) by providing explicit transformation laws between photon and hadron frames that include hadron mass effects, and by proposing quantitative tests for regime identification. It enables systematic comparison of different theoretical approaches (collinear factorization, TMD factorization, fracture functions) in the moderate-Q region relevant to the Jefferson Lab 12 GeV program and future Electron-Ion Collider experiments. The paper reviews and translates past results from Meng, Olness, and Soper (1991), Mulders and Tangerman (1996), and Bacchetta (2006) into an updated language, while noting that Monte Carlo simulations can help but involve physical assumptions whose impact needs systematic understanding.
