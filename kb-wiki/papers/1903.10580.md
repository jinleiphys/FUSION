# Elastic scattering, inelastic excitation, and 1n pick-up transfer cross sections for $^{10}$B+$^{120}$Sn at energies near the Coulomb barrier

> The $^{10}$B+$^{120}$Sn reaction has been systematically studied at laboratory energies around the Coulomb barrier: E$_{\rm LAB}=$31.5, 33.5, 35.0, and 37.5 MeV. Cross sections for the elastic scattering and some reaction processes have been measured: excitation to the $1^+$ state of $^{10}$B; excitation to the $2^+$ and $3^-$ states of $^{120}$Sn; and the one-neutron pick-up transfer $^{120}$Sn($^{10}$B,$^{11}$B)$^{119}$Sn. Coupled reaction channel (CRC) calculations have been performed in the context of the double-folding São Paulo potential. The theoretical calculations result in a good overall description of the experimental angular distributions. The effect on the theoretical elastic-scattering angular distributions of couplings to the inelastic and transfer states (through the CRC calculations) and to the continuum states (through continuum-discretized coupled-channels calculations) has been investigated.

## Key claim
The paper claims that CRC calculations using the double-folding Sao Paulo Potential provide a good overall description of the experimental angular distributions for the 10B+120Sn reaction at four energies near the Coulomb barrier, but the theoretical calculations fail to reproduce the elastic scattering data in the Fresnel angular region at 33.5 and 37.5 MeV, and this discrepancy is not resolved by including couplings to continuum states via CDCC calculations.

## Method
The authors measured elastic scattering, inelastic excitation to the 1+ state of 10B and the 2+ and 3- states of 120Sn, and one-neutron pick-up transfer 120Sn(10B,11B)119Sn at laboratory energies of 31.5, 33.5, 35.0, and 37.5 MeV using the SATURN silicon detector array. They performed CRC calculations with the double-folding Sao Paulo Potential using the FRESCO code, with all parameter values fixed from the 37.5 MeV analysis, making the calculations for the other energies parameter-free predictions. They also performed CDCC calculations treating 10B as a 6Li+alpha cluster to test the effect of breakup couplings on elastic scattering.

## Key numbers
- Laboratory energies studied: 31.5, 33.5, 35.0, and 37.5 MeV
- First excited state of 10B: 1+ state at 0.718 MeV excitation energy
- First excited state of 120Sn: 2+ state at 1.171 MeV excitation energy
- Second excited state of 120Sn: 3- state at 2.400 MeV excitation energy
- 10B breakup threshold into 6Li + 4He: Q = -4.461 MeV
- Imaginary potential normalization factor for CRC calculations: N_I = 0.25, significantly smaller than the value N_I = 0.78 used for tightly-bound nuclei
- Q-value for one-neutron pick-up transfer ground state: Q_g.s. = 2.350 MeV
- 119Sn states grouped into two sets: first group includes g.s., 23.8 keV, and 89.5 keV states; second group includes 787.0, 920.5, 921.4, 1089, and 1354 keV states

## Context
This work is part of the E-125 experimental campaign at the University of Sao Paulo, which systematically studies scattering of weakly-bound stable nuclei (6Li, 7Li, 9Be, 10B, 11B) on the same 120Sn target, extending previous work on 7Li+120Sn by Zagatto (2017) and initial 10B+120Sn results at 37.5 MeV by Gasques (2018). The results enable comparison with reactions involving exotic halo nuclei such as 6He, 11Li, and 11Be, and the unexplained Fresnel region discrepancy mirrors a similar effect recently reported for 9Be+120Sn by Arazi (2018), suggesting the need for measurements at higher energies to understand this behavior.
