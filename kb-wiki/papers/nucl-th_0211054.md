# pi and rho loop corrections to omega photoproduction in the resonance region

> One-loop corrections due to the intermediate $πN$ and $ρN$ states are studied in $ω$ photoproduction near threshold. Our results show that the coupled-channel effects should be taken into account in extracting reliable nucleon resonance parameters from the forthcoming vector meson photoproduction data in the resonance region.

## Key claim
One-loop corrections from intermediate πN and ρN states produce nontrivial interference effects in ω photoproduction near threshold, but the results should be regarded as only a qualitative indication of coupled-channel effects due to uncertainties and approximations in the amplitudes.

## Method
The authors calculated one-loop corrections to ω photoproduction by including intermediate πN and ρN channels as the leading-order terms in a perturbative expansion of a full coupled-channel formalism. Nonresonant amplitudes for the γN → πN, πN → ωN, γN → ρN, and ρN → ωN transitions were constructed from tree diagrams of effective Lagrangians, with the γN → πN amplitude obtained by subtracting resonance contributions from empirical SAID multipole amplitudes. The ρ meson width was included in the ρN propagator, and form factors were fitted to available experimental data.

## Key numbers
- Differential cross sections and single photon asymmetry Σ_x were calculated at photon energies E_γ = 1.125 GeV, 1.23 GeV, 1.45 GeV, and 1.68 GeV for γp → ωp.
- The ρ meson propagator includes a width term Γ(ω(q',E))/2 with a step function θ[ω²(q',E) - 4M²_π] for the two-pion decay threshold.

## Context
This work extends the tree-diagram model of Oh, Titov, and Lee (2001) for ω photoproduction by adding hadronic final-state interactions, following the dynamical formulation of Sato and Lee (1996). It revisits coupled-channel effects first explored in vector meson photoproduction by Söding and Schilling (1968). The results indicate that extracting reliable nucleon resonance parameters from forthcoming vector meson photoproduction data at facilities such as SAPHIR, CLAS, GRAAL, and LEPS will require accounting for coupled-channel effects, particularly in polarization observables.
