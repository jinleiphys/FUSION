# Enhancement factor for two-neutron transfer reactions with a schematic coupled-channels model

> Probabilities for two-neutron transfer reactions, $P_{\rm 2n}$, are often discussed in comparison with the square of the corresponding probabilities for one-neutron transfer process, $(P_{\rm 1n})^2$, implicitly assuming that $(P_{\rm 1n})^2$ provides the probability of two-neutron transfer reactions in the absence of the pairing correlation. We use a schematic coupled-channels model, in which the transfers are treated as effective inelastic channels, and demonstrate that this model leads to $P_{\rm 2n}=(P_{\rm 1n})^2/4$, rather than $P_{\rm 2n}=(P_{\rm 1n})^2$, in the pure sequential limit. We argue that a simple model with spin-up and spin-down neutrons in a single-particle orbital also leads to the same conclusion.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the reference probability for two-neutron transfer in the absence of pairing correlations should be \(P_{\rm 2n} = (P_{\rm 1n})^2/4\) rather than the commonly assumed \(P_{\rm 2n} = (P_{\rm 1n})^2\), based on a schematic coupled-channels model and a microscopic spin model.

## Method

The authors used a schematic coupled-channels model treating transfer channels as effective inelastic excitations, with a 3x3 coupling matrix for sequential two-neutron transfer (0n, 1n, 2n channels) and zero direct 0n-2n coupling. They applied this model to the \(^{40}\)Ca+\(^{96}\)Zr reaction, fitting the coupling form factor parameters to reproduce experimental one-neutron transfer data, then solving the coupled-channels equations with the code CCFULL. They also derived the same 1/4 factor analytically using time-dependent perturbation theory and a microscopic model with spin-up and spin-down neutrons in a single-particle orbital.

## Key numbers

- The ratio \(P_{\rm 2n}/(P_{\rm 1n})^2 = 1/4\) is obtained in the pure sequential limit from the coupled-channels model and perturbation theory.
- For the \(^{40}\)Ca+\(^{96}\)Zr reaction, the coupling form factor parameters used are \(\beta = 9\) MeV fm, \(R = 1.15 \times (40^{1/3} + 96^{1/3})\) fm, and \(a = 1.3\) fm.
- The nuclear potential parameters are: real part \(V_0 = 140\) MeV, \(r_0 = 1.1\) fm, \(a_0 = 0.65\) fm; imaginary part \(W_0 = 30\) MeV, \(r_W = 1.15\) fm, \(a_W = 0.1\) fm.
- The transfer probabilities are plotted for scattering angle \(\theta_{c.m.} = 140\) degrees as a function of the distance of closest approach \(D\).
- The TDHF+BCS result from Scamps and Lacroix (2013) gives \(P_{\rm 2n}/(P_{\rm 1n})^2 \sim (N_v-1)/(2N_v) \cdot (N_f-1)/N_f\), which reduces to 1/4 for \(N_v=2\) when neglecting the \(N_f\) dependence.

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) + BCS result of Scamps and Lacroix (2013), which showed that the ratio \(P_{\rm 2n}/(P_{\rm 1n})^2\) is never larger than 1/2 in the no-correlation limit. The paper contradicts the common assumption used in experimental analyses (von Oertzen, 1991) that \((P_{\rm 1n})^2\) provides the reference probability for two-neutron transfer without pairing. The result enables a more physically meaningful definition of the enhancement factor due to pairing correlations, and the authors advocate using \((P_{\rm 1n})^2/4\) as the reference probability for core+two-neutron systems.
