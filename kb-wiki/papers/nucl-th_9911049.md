# Photodisintegration of Three-Body Nuclei with Realistic 2N and 3N Forces

> Total photonuclear absorption cross sections of $^3$H and $^3$He are studied using realistic NN and NNN forces. Final state interactions are fully included. Two NN potential models, the AV14 and the r-space Bonn-A potentials, are considered. For the NNN forces the Urbana-VIII and Tucson-Melbourne models are employed. We find the cross section to be sensitive to nuclear dynamics. Of particular interest in this work is the effect which NNN forces have on the cross section. The addition of NNN forces not only lowers the peak height but increases the cross section beyond 70 MeV by roughly 15%. Cross sections are computed using the Lorentz integral transform method.

# Photodisintegration of Three-Body Nuclei with Realistic 2N and 3N Forces

## Key claim

The total photonuclear absorption cross sections of ³H and ³He show clear sensitivity to nuclear dynamics, and the addition of three-nucleon forces (3NF) not only lowers the peak height but increases the cross section beyond 70 MeV by roughly 15%. The T=3/2 channel in the tail region (70-110 MeV) separates all models containing a 3NF from those with purely NN forces, with a 5-10% effect, indicating this channel is more sensitive to 3NF than to NN forces.

## Method

The total photoabsorption cross sections for ³H and ³He were computed using the unretarded dipole operator (including exchange currents via Siegert's theorem) and the Lorentz integral transform method, which avoids explicit calculation of final-state continuum wave functions. The ground state and transformed wave functions were expanded in a set of correlated hyperspherical harmonics (CHH) with state-dependent correlation operators. Two NN potentials (Argonne AV14 and r-space Bonn-A) and two 3NF models (Urbana-VIII and Tucson-Melbourne) were employed, with the TM cut-off mass adjusted to reproduce the correct binding energy.

## Key numbers

- ³H binding energies: 7.69 MeV (AV14), 8.15 MeV (Bonn-A), 8.49 MeV (AV14+UrbVIII), 8.48 MeV (AV14+TM), 8.47 MeV (Bonn-A+TM)
- Point proton radii for ³H: 1.66 fm (AV14), 1.61 fm (Bonn-A), 1.60 fm (AV14+UrbVIII), 1.60 fm (AV14+TM), 1.59 fm (Bonn-A+TM)
- D-state probabilities: 8.94% (AV14), 6.88% (Bonn-A), 9.67% (AV14+UrbVIII), 9.67% (AV14+TM), 7.25% (Bonn-A+TM)
- Inverse energy weighted sum rule σ₋₁ for AV14: 2.636 mb (integration) and 2.645 mb (ground state), agreement better than 0.5%
- TRK sum rule for AV14: 70.7 MeV-mb (direct integration to 2000 MeV) and 71.6 MeV-mb (double commutator); integration to 300 MeV exhausts about 90% of the sum rule
- Ground state point proton radii for semi-realistic potentials: 1.61 fm (MT), 1.59 fm (TN)
- Addition of 3NF leads to roughly a 10% decrease in peak height
- T=3/2 channel shows 5-10% effect separating 3NF models from NN-only models in the 70-110 MeV energy range
- TM cut-off masses: Λ = 4.67μ (with AV14) and Λ = 4.07μ (with Bonn-A)

## Context

This work extends earlier trinucleon photodisintegration calculations by Sandhas et al. (1990s) that covered 0-40 MeV for exclusive two-body breakup, by computing inclusive (two-body plus three-body breakup) cross sections over a larger energy range. It also extends previous calculations using semi-realistic potentials such as the Malfliet-Tjon (MT) and Trento (TN) models, showing that realistic potentials produce significantly larger high-energy tails. The results enable future investigations of exclusive three-body breakup reactions, where the Lorentz integral transform method can be modified to calculate exclusive cross sections, and suggest that particular kinematical setups in nnp or npp channels may be sensitive to the presence of three-body forces, as proposed by O'Connell (1990s).
