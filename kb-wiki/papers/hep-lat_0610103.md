# Flavor Twisted Boundary Conditions, Pion Momentum, and the Pion Electromagnetic Form Factor

> We investigate the utility of partially twisted boundary conditions in lattice calculations of meson observables. For dynamical simulations, we show that the pion dispersion relation is modified by volume effects. In the isospin limit, we demonstrate that the pion electromagnetic form factor can be computed on the lattice at continuous values of the momentum transfer. Furthermore, the finite volume effects are under theoretical control for extraction of the pion charge radius.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that partially twisted boundary conditions allow lattice QCD calculations of the pion electromagnetic form factor at continuous values of momentum transfer, but the pion dispersion relation receives finite-volume modifications that renormalize the induced momentum, and these volume effects must be controlled to extract the pion charge radius reliably.

## Method

The authors used partially quenched chiral perturbation theory (PQChPT) in a finite volume to compute one-loop self-energy diagrams for pions and one-loop corrections to the isospin transition matrix element that yields the pion form factor. They evaluated finite-volume sums using Poisson resummation to obtain exponentially convergent expressions, then numerically assessed the magnitude of volume corrections for typical lattice parameters.

## Key numbers

* The lowest non-vanishing Fourier momentum on current dynamical lattices is approximately 500 MeV.
* For a box size of 2.5 fm and valence-sea pion mass of 0.3 GeV, the induced momentum from twisting is shifted by approximately 2% due to finite-volume effects.
* The maximal isospin splitting between charged and neutral pions is approximately 10% at the physical pion mass in a small box, but for pion masses of 0.3 GeV in a 2.5 fm box with a single twist angle less than pi, the isospin splitting is less than 1%.
* The finite-volume shift of the pion charge radius with partially twisted boundary conditions is on the order of a few percent on current dynamical lattices.

## Context

This work extends the study of twisted boundary conditions for hadron observables, following earlier developments by Bedaque (2004), de Divitiis (2004), Sachrajda (2004), and Tiburzi (2005, 2006). It contrasts with the study by Bunton (2006) which found larger volume corrections for the pion charge radius using periodic boundary conditions with the smallest available Fourier momentum. The results enable lattice calculations of the pion charge radius at arbitrarily small momentum transfer while keeping finite-volume effects under theoretical control, which is necessary as pion masses are lowered toward the physical point.
