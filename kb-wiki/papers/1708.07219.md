# Role of small-norm components in extended random-phase approximation

> The role of the small-norm amplitudes in extended RPA theories such as the particle-particle and hole-hole components of one-body amplitudes and the two-body amplitudes other than two particle - two holes components are investigated for the one-dimensional Hubbard model using an extended RPA derived from the time-dependent density-matrix theory. It is found that these amplitudes cannot be neglected in strongly interacting regions where the effects of ground-state correlations are significant.

# Role of small-norm components in extended random-phase approximation

## Key claim

The particle-particle, hole-hole, and non-2p-2h two-body amplitudes (the "small-norm components") cannot be neglected in extended RPA calculations for strongly interacting systems where ground-state correlations are significant, specifically for the one-dimensional Hubbard model at half filling when the interaction strength exceeds approximately U/t > 1.

## Method

The author derives an extended RPA (ERPA) from the small-amplitude limit of the time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM), using a ground state obtained via an adiabatic method that gradually increases the residual interaction strength. The ERPA equation couples one-body amplitudes (including p-p and h-h components) and two-body amplitudes (including 2p-2p, 2h-2h, and ph-ph components beyond the usual 2p-2h and 2h-2p configurations), with norm matrices that become non-zero for these additional configurations when single-particle states are fractionally occupied. Calculations are performed for the six-site one-dimensional Hubbard model at half filling and compared with exact diagonalization approach (EDA) results.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy from TDDM agrees well with EDA across the range U/t = 0 to U/t = 4 (Fig. 2, no explicit numerical values given in text for individual points)
- At U/t = 4, occupation probabilities deviate more than 10% from Hartree-Fock values (n = 1 or 0)
- For the spin mode with momentum transfer q = pi/3, SRPA collapses at U/t = 3.9
- For the spin mode with q = pi, RPA and SRPA collapse beyond U/t = 2.3; rRPA-like approach extends collapse point only slightly to U/t = 2.6
- At U/t = 4 for the q = pi/3 spin mode, the ratio of transition strength using only p-h and h-p components to full transition strength is S_ph/S_full = 0.54
- The three-body correlation matrix used in ERPA at U/t = 3.5 and 4 is reduced by 16% from the value given by the perturbative approximation (Eqs. 14-15)
- For U/t less than or equal to 3, excitation energies in ERPA are little affected by a 16% reduction of the three-body correlation matrix

## Context

This work extends prior extended RPA approaches including the renormalized RPA (rRPA) of Rowe (1968), the self-consistent RPA (SCRPA) of Dukelsky and Schuck (1990), and the extended second RPA (ESRPA) of Takayanagi (1994), by explicitly including small-norm amplitudes that are usually neglected. The author shows that fractional occupation alone (as in rRPA) is insufficient and that coupling to two-body amplitudes beyond 2p-2h configurations is essential. The work enables more complete treatment of ground-state correlations in nuclear structure calculations, though the author notes that truncation of two-body amplitudes remains necessary in realistic applications due to the rapid increase in the number of matrix elements with system size.
