# Centrality dependence of charged-hadron pseudorapidity distributions in PbPb collisions at LHC energies in the RDM

> The centrality dependence of charged-hadron production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at LHC energies is investigated in a nonequilibrium-statistical relativistic diffusion model (RDM) with three sources. Theoretical pseudorapidity distributions are compared with preliminary PbPb ALICE data at LHC energy of sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV for seven centralities, and a previous RDM prediction for this energy is confronted with the data. Refined predictions for 5.52 TeV PbPb based on RDM parameter extrapolations are presented.

## Key claim
The relativistic diffusion model (RDM) with three sources can reproduce the centrality dependence of charged-hadron pseudorapidity distributions in PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV, but only when the limiting fragmentation scaling hypothesis is used as an additional constraint to compensate for the lack of data in the fragmentation region beyond pseudorapidity 5.1.

## Method
The authors solved a linear Fokker-Planck equation in rapidity space for three sources (two fragmentation sources from valence quark-gluon collisions and one midrapidity source from gluon-gluon collisions), then transformed the results to pseudorapidity space using a Jacobian that accounts for the mass and transverse momentum distributions of pions, kaons, and protons. They performed chi-squared minimizations of the RDM parameters against preliminary ALICE data for seven centrality classes at 2.76 TeV, supplementing the missing fragmentation-region data by shifting PHOBOS data from 0.2 TeV AuAu collisions by the difference in beam rapidity (2.63 units) according to the limiting fragmentation hypothesis.

## Key numbers
- At 2.76 TeV PbPb for 0-5% central collisions: total charged hadrons N_ch^tot = 17327, fraction in central source n_gg = 0.56, midrapidity density dN/dη|_(η≈0) = 1601 ± 60 (ALICE data)
- At 2.76 TeV PbPb: beam rapidity y_beam = ±7.99, time parameter τ_int/τ_y = 0.87, fragmentation source FWHM Γ_1,2 = 4.99, central source FWHM Γ_gg = 6.24
- At 5.52 TeV PbPb (extrapolated): total charged hadrons N_ch^tot = 22792, fraction in central source n_gg = 0.61, midrapidity density dN/dη|_(η≈0) = 1940
- At 2.76 TeV central PbPb: Jacobian at midrapidity J_(y=0) = 0.856, effective transverse momentum p_(T,eff) = 0.21 GeV/c for 0-5% centrality and 0.19 GeV/c for 50-60% centrality
- At 2.76 TeV central PbPb: particle abundances 83% pions, 13% kaons, 4% protons; mean transverse momenta 0.51, 0.86, and 1.37 GeV/c respectively

## Context
This work extends the RDM three-sources model previously applied to AuAu collisions at RHIC energies (0.13 and 0.2 TeV) by Wolschin and collaborators (2004-2011) to LHC energies, and corrects an earlier RDM prediction for 2.76 TeV PbPb (Wolschin, 2011) that placed the fragmentation peak too far from midrapidity. The results enable refined predictions for the maximum LHC energy of 5.52 TeV, expected from runs in 2014/15, and show that the midrapidity source becomes dominant at LHC energies (56% of particles) compared to RHIC energies where it remained below 30%.
