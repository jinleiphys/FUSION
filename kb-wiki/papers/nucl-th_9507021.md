# Relativistic Calculations for Photonuclear Reactions (II). Nonrelativistic reductions and nuclear medium effects

> The relativistic amplitude for the direct knock-out contribution to $\left( γ, p \right)$ reactions on nuclei is reduced to a nonrelativistic form using an effective Pauli reduction scheme. The reduction is carried out to second order in the inverse nucleon mass. It is found that the interaction Hamiltonian appearing in the nonrelativistic amplitude has significant dependence, starting at second order, on the vector and scalar mean nuclear potentials. These strong medium modifications are absent in traditional nonrelativistic calculations. Detailed comparisons show that these modifications are crucial to understanding the differences between relativistic and nonrelativistic models. These differences are also examined through reduction of the relativistic amplitude via the Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation. Similar medium modifications are obtained in this case as well. We discuss the implications of these medium modifications for the consistency of existing nonrelativistic calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the significant differences between relativistic and nonrelativistic calculations for (γ,p) reactions arise primarily from nuclear medium modifications in the interaction Hamiltonian at second order in the inverse nucleon mass, rather than from higher-order kinematic corrections alone. These medium modifications, which depend on the vector and scalar mean nuclear potentials, are absent in traditional nonrelativistic calculations even when higher-order relativistic corrections are included.

## Method

The authors performed an effective Pauli reduction of the relativistic distorted wave S-matrix for the direct knock-out contribution to (γ,p) reactions, expanding the effective interaction Hamiltonian in powers of 1/(E+M) to second order. They compared four types of calculations: full relativistic, first-order nonrelativistic, second-order nonrelativistic without nuclear potentials (medium-uncorrected), and second-order nonrelativistic with nuclear potentials included (medium-corrected). Additionally, they applied a Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation to a model amplitude with real vector and scalar potentials to verify the medium modifications obtained through the Pauli reduction.

## Key numbers

- For the ¹⁶O(γ,p)¹⁵N reaction at E_γ = 100 MeV: first-order nonrelativistic calculations at forward angles are almost an order of magnitude larger than relativistic calculations, while at backward angles they are roughly two orders of magnitude smaller than relativistic calculations.
- For the ¹⁶O(γ,p)¹⁵N reaction at E_γ = 312 MeV: first-order nonrelativistic calculations lie above relativistic calculations for angles smaller than 40°, while for other scattering angles they lie below by as much as an order of magnitude.
- The anomalous magnetic moment term uses κ = 1.79 for the proton.
- The expansion is carried out to second order in the inverse nucleon mass, with the first-order terms reproducing the usual nonrelativistic amplitude in the limit E_B, E_C → M.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic calculations for (γ,p) reactions by Lotz and Sherif (1988) and McDermott et al. (1988), which showed better agreement with experimental data than nonrelativistic distorted wave Born approximation calculations. The paper clarifies the origin of differences between relativistic and nonrelativistic models by showing that nuclear medium effects in the interaction Hamiltonian, absent in traditional nonrelativistic calculations following the Foldy-Wouthuysen approach of McVoy and Van Hove, are crucial for bringing nonrelativistic results into agreement with relativistic calculations. This enables future work to incorporate these medium modifications into nonrelativistic calculations for improved consistency.
