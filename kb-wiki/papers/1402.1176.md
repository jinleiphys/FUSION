# Ward-Green-Takahashi identities and the axial-vector vertex

> The colour-singlet axial-vector vertex plays a pivotal role in understanding dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and numerous hadronic weak interactions, yet scant model-independent information is available. We therefore use longitudinal and transverse Ward-Green-Takahashi (WGT) identities, together with kinematic constraints, in order to ameliorate this situation and expose novel features of the axial vertex: amongst them, Ward-like identities for elements in the transverse piece of the vertex, which complement and shed new light on identities determined previously for components in its longitudinal part. Such algebraic results are verified via solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the axial vertex obtained using two materially different kernels for the relevant Dyson-Schwinger equations. The solutions also provide insights that suggest a practical Ansatz for the axial-vector vertex.

## Key claim

The paper uses longitudinal and transverse Ward-Green-Takahashi (WGT) identities, together with kinematic constraints, to derive novel model-independent constraints on the colour-singlet axial-vector vertex, including Ward-like identities for its transverse components; these algebraic results are verified via solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation using two different Dyson-Schwinger equation kernels, but the practical Ansatz proposed for the vertex is validated only in the neighbourhood of the chiral limit and on spacelike momenta.

## Method

The authors first derived algebraic constraints on the axial-vector vertex by combining the longitudinal WGT identity with two transverse WGT identities, eliminating the unfamiliar higher-order vertices through tensor contractions. They then solved the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the axial-vector vertex using two distinct symmetry-preserving kernels: the rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation and a DCSB-improved (DB) kernel, with a current-quark mass that produces a pion mass of 0.14 GeV. The solutions were used to verify the algebraic identities and to study the momentum dependence of the transverse vertex functions.

## Key numbers

- The current-quark mass was chosen to produce a pion mass of \(m_\pi = 0.14\) GeV.
- The ultraviolet momentum scale above which the dominant transverse function \(\tau_1\) evolves according to the analytic expression is \(t^2 = y \gtrsim 1.5\) GeV\(^2\).
- The QCD scale used for reference is \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD} \approx 0.2\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the model-independent analysis of the vector vertex (Ball 1980, Curtis 1990, Bashir 2011, Qin 2013) to the axial-vector vertex, for which prior information was far less robust; previous computations (Zong 2001, Eichmann 2011, Chang 2012) were performed in leading-order truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations. The results enable construction of a practical Ansatz for the axial-vector vertex that depends only on the dressed-quark propagator, analogous to the Ball-Chiu Ansatz for the vector vertex (Ball 1980), which can be used in hadron phenomenology without solving the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation.
