# An EFT approach to Color decoherence in jet quenching

> We use the EFT developed in \cite{Mehtar-Tani:2025xxd}, to understand the interference driven phenomenon of color decoherence in inclusive jet production in a dense nuclear medium such as Nuclei or Quark Gluon Plasma. Using the factorization formula in \cite{Mehtar-Tani:2024smp,Mehtar-Tani:2025xxd}, expressed as a series of multi-sub-jet operators, we define and calculate the contribution of the two sub-jet effective operator. This explicitly reveals the emergent angular scale $θ_c$ that controls color decoherence as well an intricate renormalization group structure for the factorized functions. We show that for a jet of radius R in a medium of size L characterized by a jet quenching parameter $\hat q$, both the LPM effect and color decoherence are controlled by a single dimensionless parameter $\sqrt{\hat q L}LR$ and therefore are equally important for phenomenology. This paper shows how interference driven emergent effects can be included in a factorized framework for computing jet observables in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that both the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect and color decoherence in inclusive jet production are controlled by a single dimensionless parameter \(\sqrt{\hat q L}LR\), where \(\hat q\) is the jet quenching parameter, \(L\) is the medium size, and \(R\) is the jet radius; this implies that multi-sub-jet operators contribute at the same order in power counting as the single sub-jet operator and must be included for precision phenomenology, contrary to previous assumptions that these effects could be treated separately.

## Method

The author uses the open quantum system effective field theory (EFT) framework developed by Mehtar-Tani (2025) to compute the matching coefficients for one- and two-sub-jet operators in the threshold limit (\(z \rightarrow 1\)) of inclusive jet production. The factorization formula from Mehtar-Tani (2024, 2025) is expressed as a series of multi-sub-jet operators, and the two-sub-jet effective operator is explicitly defined and calculated at leading order, including both vacuum and single-medium-interaction contributions. The renormalization group equations for the factorized functions are derived, and the collinear singularity in the two-sub-jet matching coefficient is removed by subtracting the overlap with the single-sub-jet configuration.

## Key numbers

* The critical angle controlling color decoherence is \(\theta_c = 1/(Q_{\text{med}}L)\), where \(Q_{\text{med}}^2 = \hat q L\); for a medium of size \(\sim 5\) fm and \(Q_{\text{med}} \sim 1-2\) GeV, this corresponds to \(\theta_c \sim 0.01 - 0.02 \ll R\).
* The dimensionless parameter controlling both LPM effect and color decoherence is \(\sqrt{\hat q L}LR\).
* The one-loop vacuum result for the single-sub-jet collinear soft operator \(\mathcal{S}^{(1)}_{q,0}\) contains terms proportional to \(\alpha_s C_F/(2\pi \omega_J')\) with coefficients including \(-\ell^2/2 + \pi^2/12\) and logarithmic plus-distributions in \((1-\tilde z)\).
* The two-sub-jet matching coefficient at leading-log accuracy is \(\mathcal{C}_{q \rightarrow q+g}(z', \omega'_J, \theta_{12}, \mu) = -\omega'_J \delta(1-z') (\alpha_s/\pi)(1/\theta_{12})(\ell + 3/2)\), where \(\ell = \ln(4\mu^2/(\omega_J R)^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the EFT framework for jet quenching developed by Mehtar-Tani (2024, 2025), which previously restricted explicit calculations to a dilute medium for a single sub-jet. The paper generalizes the approach to multi-sub-jet operators, explicitly computing the two-sub-jet case and showing how the critical angle \(\theta_c\) emerges in the EFT in the same way as in full QCD calculations for a dipole by Mehtar-Tani (2012). This enables future systematic calculations of jet substructure observables in heavy ion collisions, including non-global logarithms and renormalization group evolution of factorized functions, and provides a foundation for extending the framework to higher multiplicity sub-jet operators.
