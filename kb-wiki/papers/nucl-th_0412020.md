# Polarization transfer in $^4$He$(\vec{e},e^\prime\vec{p})$$^3$H: is the ratio $G_{Ep}/G_{Mp}$ modified in medium ?

> Polarization observables in the $^4$He$(\vec{e},e^\prime\vec{p})$$^3$H reaction are calculated using accurate three- and four-nucleon bound-state wave functions, a realistic model for the nuclear electromagnetic current operator, and a treatment of final-state-interactions with an optical potential. In contrast to earlier studies, no significant discrepancies are found between theory and experiment both for the ratio of transverse to longitudinal polarization transfers and for the induced polarization, when free-nucleon electromagnetic form factors are used in the current operator. The present results challenge the current interpretation of the experimental data in terms of medium-modified form factors.

## Key claim

When free-nucleon electromagnetic form factors are used in the current operator, and when final-state interactions (including charge-exchange processes) and two-body currents are properly accounted for, no significant discrepancies remain between theory and experiment for the polarization transfer ratio and induced polarization in $^4$He$(\vec{e},e^\prime\vec{p})$$^3$H, challenging the interpretation that medium-modified form factors are needed.

## Method

The authors calculated polarization observables using variational hyperspherical-harmonics wave functions for $^3$H, $^3$He, and $^4$He derived from the Argonne $v_{18}$ two-nucleon and Urbana-IX three-nucleon interactions (AV18/UIX). Final-state interactions between the knocked-out proton and the residual $^3$H system were described via an optical potential fitted to $p$+$^3$H elastic and charge-exchange cross section data up to 600 MeV lab energy, including both isospin-independent and isospin-dependent (charge-exchange) central and spin-orbit terms. The nuclear electromagnetic current included one-body operators from the Jeschonnek-Donnelly expansion and two-body operators from the AV18 interaction, $\Delta$-excitation, and $\rho\pi\gamma$ and $\omega\pi\gamma$ transition mechanisms, with matrix elements evaluated by Monte Carlo integration.

## Key numbers

- Proton lab kinetic energies for the four $Q^2$ points: 0.29, 0.55, 0.88, and 1.42 GeV, corresponding to $Q^2$ = 0.5, 1.0, 1.6, and 2.6 (GeV/c)$^2$, respectively
- At $Q^2$ = 0.5 (GeV/c)$^2$, the transverse response $R_{TT^\prime}^l$ = 0.183 fm$^3$ (no charge-exchange) and 0.174 fm$^3$ (with charge-exchange); the interference response $R_{LT^\prime}^t$ = 0.251 fm$^3$ (no charge-exchange) and 0.227 fm$^3$ (with charge-exchange)
- Two-body currents increase the transverse response $R_T$ by about 8% and the interference response $R_{LT^\prime}^t$ by about 4%, suppressing the $P_x^\prime/P_z^\prime$ ratio by about 4% relative to one-body currents alone
- The hydrogen polarization transfer ratio at $Q^2$ = 1.6 (GeV/c)$^2$ was measured as $-0.395 \pm 0.013$, while the Brash fit gives $-0.415$
- The optical potential was fitted to $p$+$^3$H elastic data in the lab energy range 160-600 MeV and charge-exchange data at 57 and 156 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Udias et al. (1999), Lava et al. (2004), and Laget (2004), which found discrepancies with experiment and suggested medium-modified form factors might be required. Those studies neglected correlation effects in bound-state wave functions, many-body currents, and/or charge-exchange processes in final-state interactions. The present results corroborate conclusions from Coulomb sum rule analyses in few-nucleon systems by Schiavilla et al. (1989), which found no missing longitudinal strength when free-proton form factors are used. The work enables a re-evaluation of whether medium-modified nucleon form factors, as predicted by the quark-meson coupling model of Lu et al. (1999), are actually required to explain polarization transfer data in nuclei.
