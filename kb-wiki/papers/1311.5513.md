# More on thermal probes of a strongly coupled anisotropic plasma

> We extend the analysis of 1211.2199, where the photon production rate of an anisotropic strongly coupled plasma with Nf<<Nc massless quarks was considered. We allow here for non-vanishing quark masses and study how these affect the spectral densities and conductivities. We also compute another important probe of the plasma, the dilepton production rate. We consider generic angles between the anisotropic direction and the photon and dilepton wave vectors, as well as arbitrary quark masses and arbitrary values of the anisotropy parameter. Generically, the anisotropy increases the production rate of both photons and dileptons, compared with an isotropic plasma at the same temperature.

## Key claim

The paper extends the analysis of photon production in a strongly coupled anisotropic plasma to include non-vanishing quark masses and also computes the dilepton production rate, finding that anisotropy generically increases both production rates compared to an isotropic plasma at the same temperature, except for a small range of anisotropies when the quark mass and frequency are sufficiently large.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence with a type IIB supergravity black brane solution that has an anisotropic horizon, introducing flavor via probe D7-branes with non-equatorial embeddings corresponding to massive quarks. They solve the equations of motion for gauge field perturbations on the D7-brane worldvolume to compute retarded correlators of the electromagnetic current, from which they extract spectral densities and conductivities for photons and dileptons at generic angles between the anisotropic direction and the wave vectors.

## Key numbers

* For large anisotropy, the entropy density scales as \(s \simeq 3.21\, N_c^2 T^3 (a/T)^{1/3}\), with the transition between isotropic and anisotropic behavior at approximately \(a/T \simeq 3.7\).
* The ratio \(\alpha = (4E + P_\perp - P_L)/(3Ts)\) is well approximated by \(\alpha \simeq 1 - 0.0036 (a/T)^2 - 0.000072 (a/T)^4\) for \(0 < a/T \lesssim 20\).
* The isotropic spectral density \(\chi_{\text{iso}}\) is computed for \(T = 0.33\) and various embeddings with \(\psi_H = 0, 0.53, 0.75, 0.941, 0.98\).
* The conductivity \(\tilde{\sigma}_{(1)}\) for polarization \(\epsilon_{(1)}\) is plotted as a function of \(a/T\) for \(\psi_H = 0, 0.53, 0.75, 0.941, 0.98\), showing a decrease with increasing quark mass.

## Context

This work extends the massless quark analysis of Jahnke et al. (2013) (arXiv:1211.2199) to include massive quarks and dilepton production, building on the anisotropic black brane geometry of Mateos and Trancanelli (2011). The results complement prior holographic studies of photon production in anisotropic plasmas by Caron-Huot et al. (2006), Mateos and Patino (2007), and Wu et al. (2013), and enable more realistic comparisons with heavy-ion collision data from RHIC and LHC, where the quark-gluon plasma exhibits both strong coupling and initial-state spatial anisotropy.
