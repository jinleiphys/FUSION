# Color-Flavor Unlocking and Phase Diagram with Self-Consistently Determined Strange Quark Masses

> The phase diagram of strongly interacting matter at non-zero temperature and baryon chemical potential is calculated within a 3-flavor NJL-type quark model with realistic quark masses. The model exhibits spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking as well as diquark condensation in the two-flavor color-superconducting phase and in the color-flavor locked phase. We investigate the color-flavor unlocking phase transition, taking into account self-consistently calculated effective quark masses. We find that it is mainly triggered by a first order phase transition with respect to the strange quark mass. It takes place at much higher values of the chemical potential than the transition to the hadronic phase such that we find a relatively large region in the phase diagram where the two-flavor color-superconductor seems to be the most favored state.

# Color-Flavor Unlocking and Phase Diagram with Self-Consistently Determined Strange Quark Masses

## Key claim

The color-flavor unlocking transition from the two-flavor color-superconducting (2SC) phase to the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase is mainly triggered by a first-order phase transition with respect to the strange quark mass, and occurs at much higher chemical potentials than the transition to the hadronic phase, leaving a relatively large region where the 2SC phase is the most favored state, but this conclusion depends on the self-consistent treatment of effective quark masses, which had been neglected in prior fixed-mass calculations.

## Method

The authors use a three-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL)-type quark model with a four-fermion interaction having the quantum numbers of single-gluon exchange, Fierz-transformed to yield quark-antiquark and diquark coupling channels. They compute the mean-field thermodynamic potential at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential, solving coupled gap equations for the quark-antiquark condensates (giving effective constituent masses for up/down and strange quarks) and the diquark condensates (giving gaps Δ₂ for the 2SC phase and Δ₅ for the CFL phase) simultaneously and self-consistently.

## Key numbers

- At zero temperature, the first-order transition from the hadronic phase to the 2SC phase occurs at μ₁ = 342.8 MeV; just above this transition, Δ₂ = 111.5 MeV and M_u drops from 362.5 MeV to 55.3 MeV.
- At zero temperature, the first-order transition from the 2SC phase to the CFL phase occurs at μ₂ = 508.5 MeV; at this point Δ₅ jumps from zero to 109.7 MeV, M_s drops from 557.4 MeV to 204.3 MeV, and Δ₂ drops from 135.6 MeV to 111.2 MeV.
- The baryon density in the 2SC phase ranges from 0.42 fm⁻³ (2.5 times nuclear matter density) just above μ₁ to 1.15 fm⁻³ (6.7 times nuclear matter density) just below μ₂; at the CFL transition it jumps to 1.63 fm⁻³ (9.6 times nuclear matter density).
- The critical temperature for the 2SC-to-quark-gluon plasma transition at μ = 343 MeV is T_c = 62.0 MeV, consistent with the BCS relation T_c ≈ 0.57 Δ₂(T=0) which gives 63.5 MeV.
- The tricritical point for the color-flavor unlocking transition is located at μ ≈ 518 MeV and T ≈ 59 MeV; above this point the transition becomes second order.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Alford, Berges, and Rajagopal (1999), who studied color-flavor unlocking with fixed strange quark masses, by incorporating the self-consistent interdependence between effective quark masses and diquark condensates, an effect neglected in that prior study and also in the instanton-mediated calculation of Rapp et al. (2000). The results enable more realistic predictions for the QCD phase diagram at densities relevant to neutron star interiors, and provide a foundation for future studies that include flavor-mixing interactions or additional induced condensates such as color-sextet diquark pairing.
