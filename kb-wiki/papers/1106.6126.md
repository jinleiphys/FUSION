# Initial fluctuations and dihadron and $γ$-hadron correlations in high-energy heavy ion collisions

> Jets, jet-medium interaction and hydrodynamic evolution of fluctuations in initial parton density all lead to the final anisotropic dihadron azimuthal correlations in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. We remove the harmonic flow background and study the net correlations from different sources with different initial conditions within the AMPT model. We also study $γ$-hadron correlations which are only influenced by jet-medium interactions.

## Key claim

After subtracting the harmonic flow background (v_n for n=2-6), the net dihadron correlations reflect contributions from both hot spots in the initial parton density and jet-medium interactions, but the paper hedges that the relative importance of these two sources is still under debate and depends on the initial conditions used in the AMPT model.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model to simulate b=0 fm Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV with a partonic interaction cross section of 10 mb. They subtracted the harmonic flow background from dihadron correlations using the ZYAM normalization scheme and flow coefficients v_n for n=2-6, then compared correlations from four different initial conditions: full HIJING initial conditions, randomized jet shower parton azimuthal angles (isolating "hot spots"), no jet production (isolating "soft hot spots"), and fully smoothed initial conditions. They also computed γ-hadron correlations, which are unaffected by harmonic flow and only sensitive to jet-medium interactions.

## Key numbers

- Eccentricity ε₂ = 0.03 and triangularity ε₃ = 0.24 for the initial transverse parton distribution in a typical b=0 fm Au+Au event at 200 GeV
- Harmonic flow coefficients v_n (n=1-6) are nonzero even for b=0 fm Au+Au 200 GeV collisions
- The double-peak structure in the net dihadron correlation (after flow subtraction) has an opening angle Δφ ≈ 1 radian
- Hot spots contribute to ridge structure up to Δη ≈ 2.5 in longitudinal pseudorapidity

## Context

This work extends the debate between Alver (2010), who attributed double-peak and ridge structures in dihadron correlations to triangular flow, and Takahashi (2009), who attributed them to hot spots from initial fluctuations. The paper uses the AMPT model to separate these contributions and proposes γ-hadron correlations as a cleaner probe of jet-medium interactions, following earlier work on jet energy loss by Wang (1991) and medium response by Casalderrey-Solana (2006). The results enable future experimental separation of initial-state fluctuation effects from jet-medium interaction effects in heavy-ion collisions.
