# Thermal Bogoliubov transformation in nuclear structure theory

> Thermal Bogoliubov transformation is an essential ingredient of the thermo field dynamics -- the real time formalism in quantum field and many-body theories at finite temperatures developed by H. Umezawa and coworkers. The approach to study properties of hot nuclei which is based on the extension of the well-known Quasiparticle-Phonon Model to finite temperatures employing the TFD formalism is presented. A distinctive feature of the QPM-TFD combination is a possibility to go beyond the standard approximations like the thermal Hartree-Fock or the thermal RPA ones.

## Key claim

The paper presents a formalism that extends the Quasiparticle-Phonon Model (QPM) to finite temperatures using Thermo Field Dynamics (TFD), claiming it can go beyond standard approximations like thermal Hartree-Fock or thermal RPA. The hedge is that the approach is demonstrated only for a simplified nuclear Hamiltonian (no distinction between protons and neutrons, separable multipole-multipole interaction) and the phonon amplitudes cannot yet be determined unambiguously at the TQRPA stage.

## Method

The authors apply the thermal Bogoliubov transformation to a nuclear Hamiltonian consisting of a mean field, BCS pairing, and a separable multipole-multipole particle-hole interaction. They first solve finite-temperature BCS equations using TFD to obtain thermal quasiparticle operators, then diagonalize the thermal Hamiltonian within the Thermal Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (TQRPA) to define thermal phonon operators. Finally, they include the quasiparticle-phonon coupling term \( \mathcal{H}_{\mathrm{qph}} \) and derive a secular equation for the energies of states that mix one-phonon and two-phonon configurations.

## Key numbers

- The thermal BCS gap parameter \(\Delta\) and chemical potential \(\lambda\) are solutions of equations involving the Fermi-Dirac occupation factors \(y_j^2 = [1 + \exp(\varepsilon_j/T)]^{-1}\), where \(\varepsilon_j = \sqrt{(E_j-\lambda)^2 + \Delta^2}\).
- The thermal phonon energies \(\omega_{\lambda i}\) satisfy the TQRPA secular equation: \(\frac{2\lambda + 1}{\kappa^{(\lambda)}_0} = \sum_{j_1j_2} (f^{(\lambda)}_{j_1j_2})^2 \left[ \frac{(u^{(+)}_{j_1j_2})^2 \varepsilon_{j_1j_2}^{(+)}(1 - y^2_{j_1} - y^2_{j_2})}{(\varepsilon_{j_1j_2}^{(+)})^2 - \omega^2} - \frac{(v^{(-)}_{j_1j_2})^2 \varepsilon_{j_1j_2}^{(-)}(y^2_{j_1} - y^2_{j_2})}{(\varepsilon_{j_1j_2}^{(-)})^2 - \omega^2} \right]\).
- The Bose-Einstein occupation factors for thermal phonons are \(Y_{\lambda i}^2 = [\exp(\omega_{\lambda i}/T) - 1]^{-1}\), with \(X_{\lambda i} = [1 + Y_{\lambda i}^2]^{1/2}\).
- The transition strengths satisfy the detailed-balancing relation \(\widetilde\Phi^2_{\lambda i} = \exp(-\omega_{\lambda i}/T) \Phi^2_{\lambda i}\).

## Context

This work extends the Quasiparticle-Phonon Model of Soloviev (1992) to finite temperatures using the TFD formalism of Umezawa and coworkers. It builds on earlier finite-temperature treatments by Tanabe (1988), Kosov and Vdovin (1994), and Kosov, Vdovin, and Wambach (1997), but introduces a new procedure for determining phonon amplitudes via the thermal state condition rather than the less evident method used in the authors' own earlier work (2009). The formalism enables the calculation of fragmentation and spreading widths of nuclear excitations in hot nuclei, as well as transition strength distributions over the nuclear spectrum at finite temperature.
