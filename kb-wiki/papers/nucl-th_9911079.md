# SU(3) realization of the rigid asymmetric rotor within the IBM

> It is shown that the spectrum of the asymmetric rotor can be realized quantum mechanically in terms of a system of interacting bosons. This is achieved in the SU(3) limit of the interacting boson model by considering higher-order interactions between the bosons. The spectrum corresponds to that of a rigid asymmetric rotor in the limit of infinite boson number.

## Key claim
The paper shows that a rigid asymmetric rotor spectrum can be realized within the SU(3) dynamical symmetry limit of the interacting boson model (IBM-1), but only when higher-order (up to four-body) interactions are included in the Hamiltonian and in the limit of infinite boson number \(N \to \infty\); for finite boson numbers the spectrum only approximates the rigid rotor.

## Method
The authors construct the most general SU(3) dynamical symmetry Hamiltonian from second-, third-, and fourth-order invariant operators of the SU(3) \(\supset\) SO(3) integrity basis, including terms \(\Omega\) and \(\Lambda\) that are cubic and quartic in the angular momentum and quadrupole operators. They show that by tuning the coefficients of the second-order Casimir \(C_2\) and its square \(C_2^2\), any given SU(3) irreducible representation \((\lambda_0,\mu_0)\) can be brought lowest in energy. The resulting Hamiltonian is rewritten in a form analogous to the rigid rotor Hamiltonian, and the connection is established via the group-theoretical contraction of the su(3) algebra to the rigid rotor algebra t\(_5\) \(\oplus\) so(3) for large \(\lambda,\mu\).

## Key numbers
- The example calculation uses \(N=15\) bosons, with Hamiltonian parameters \(a = -72\) keV, \(b = 0.1\) keV, \(d = 0.1\) keV, \(f = 25\) keV, \(c = e = g = h = 0\), and \(H_0 = 12960\) keV.
- The two lowest SU(3) irreps compared are \((10,10)\) and \((14,8)\).
- The asymmetric rotor spectrum shown corresponds to the case of highest asymmetry, \(\gamma = 30^\circ\).
- The relation between SU(3) labels and shape variables is given by \(\kappa \beta \cos\gamma = \frac13 (2\lambda + \mu + 3)\) and \(\kappa \beta \sin\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} (\mu + 1)\).

## Context
This work extends the IBM-1 beyond its standard one- and two-body Hamiltonians, which can only produce axially symmetric shapes (Ginocchio and Kirson, 1980; Dieperink and Scholten, 1980) or \(\gamma\)-unstable spectra (Wilets and Jean, 1956). It provides the first SU(3) realization of the rigid asymmetric rotor of Davydov and Filippov (1958) within the IBM-1, complementing earlier shell-model SU(3) rotor constructions (Leschber and Draayer, 1986; Castaños and Draayer, 1988). The result enables the description of rigid triaxial nuclei using algebraic IBM-1 Hamiltonians and opens the possibility of studying such spectra with general quadrupole operators beyond the SU(3) limit.
