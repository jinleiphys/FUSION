# Baryonic contributions to e+e- yields in a hydrodynamic model of Pb+Au collisions at the SPS

> We analyze e+e- yields from matter containing baryons in addition to mesons using a hydrodynamic approach to describe Pb+Au collisions at 158 A GeV/c. We use two distinctly different e+e- production rates to provide contrast. Although the presence of baryons leads to significant enhancement of e+e- emission relative to that from mesons-only matter, the calculated results fall below the data in the range 400 < M/MeV < 600. The calculated results are, however, only 1.3-1.5 standard deviations below the data, which may not be statistically significant.

## Key claim

The calculated dielectron yields from a hydrodynamic model of Pb+Au collisions at 158 A GeV/c, including baryonic contributions, fall below the preliminary CERES data in the invariant mass range 400 < M/MeV < 600, but the discrepancy is only 1.3-1.5 standard deviations and may not be statistically significant.

## Method

The authors used a one-fluid hydrodynamic model constrained to reproduce measured hadron spectra, with an equation of state that includes a phase transition to quark-gluon plasma at T_c = 200 MeV and hadronic particles up to 2 GeV. They employed two distinctly different dielectron production rate calculations: the Steele-Yamagishi-Zahed (SYZ) rates based on chiral reduction formulas and virial expansion, and the Rapp-Urban-Buballa-Wambach (RUBW) rates based on a many-body approach with in-medium rho-meson spectral functions. The thermal yields were folded with the kinematic cuts and detector resolution of the CERES experiment and combined with a calculated freeze-out decay background.

## Key numbers

- The average charged-particle multiplicity within the CERES acceptance is ⟨dN_ch/dη⟩ ≅ 330 for the calculation, compared to ⟨dN_ch/dη⟩ = 350 for the nearly central collision data used.
- The SYZ rates with baryons show enhancements of order 2-3 relative to baryon-free matter, restricted to M < 500 MeV.
- The RUBW rates produce enhancements by a factor of about three relative to mesons-only matter in the mass range 300-600 MeV.
- At M = 570 MeV, the SYZ rates (with and without baryons) give results 1.5 standard deviations below the data; the RUBW rates give results 1.3 standard deviations below the data.
- The quark-gluon plasma contribution is about an order of magnitude below the hadronic contributions.
- The freeze-out criterion used is energy density ε_f = 0.15 GeV/fm^3, corresponding to an average freeze-out temperature T_f = 140 MeV.

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic modeling of dielectron production in heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies, following earlier work by Huovinen (1998) on hadron spectra. The calculations directly confront the low-mass dielectron excess observed by the CERES collaboration (Lenkeit, 1998; Ravinovich, 1998) and test two contrasting theoretical rate calculations: the SYZ approach (Steele, Yamagishi, and Zahed, 1997) and the RUBW many-body approach (Rapp, Urban, Buballa, and Wambach, 1998). The results suggest that thermal dielectron production may be sufficient to explain the observed enhancement within statistical uncertainties, and motivate further experimental and theoretical work to reduce uncertainties.
