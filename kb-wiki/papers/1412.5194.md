# Dirac semimetals $A_3Bi$ ($A$=Na,K,Rb) as $Z_2$ Weyl semimetals

> We demonstrate that the physical reason for the nontrivial topological properties of Dirac semimetals $A_3 Bi$ (A=Na,K,Rb) is connected with a discrete symmetry of the low-energy effective Hamiltonian. By making use of this discrete symmetry, we argue that all electron states can be split into two separate sectors of the theory. Each sector describes a Weyl semimetal with a pair of Weyl nodes and broken time-reversal symmetry. The latter symmetry is not broken in the complete theory because the time-reversal transformation interchanges states from different sectors. Our findings are supported by explicit calculations of the Berry curvature. In each sector, the field lines of the curvature reveal a pair of monopoles of the Berry flux at the positions of Weyl nodes. The $Z_2$ Weyl semimetal nature is also confirmed by the existence of pairs of surface Fermi arcs, which originate from different sectors of the theory.

## Key claim

The Dirac semimetals A₃Bi (A=Na,K,Rb) are actually Z₂ Weyl semimetals, because a discrete "ud parity" symmetry allows splitting all electron states into two sectors, each describing a Weyl semimetal with broken time-reversal symmetry, while the full theory remains time-reversal invariant.

## Method

The authors analyzed the low-energy effective Hamiltonian for A₃Bi compounds derived in prior work (Fang, 2012), identifying a discrete symmetry (ud parity) that commutes with the Hamiltonian. They constructed eigenstates of this symmetry, computed the non-Abelian Berry curvature for each sector, and solved boundary-condition matching equations to obtain surface Fermi arc states for a semi-infinite slab geometry.

## Key numbers

- For Na₃Bi, the effective model parameters extracted from ab initio calculations (Fang, 2012) include: M₀ = -0.08686 eV, M₁ = -10.6424 eV·Å², M₂ = -10.3610 eV·Å², A = 2.4598 eV·Å, C₀ = -0.06382 eV, C₁ = 8.7536 eV·Å², C₂ = -8.4008 eV·Å²; lattice constants a = b = 5.448 Å, c = 9.655 Å.
- The two Dirac points are located at k₀± = (0, 0, ±√m) where m ≡ M₀/M₁.
- Surface Fermi arcs were computed for α = 1 eV·Å³ at energies E = 0, ±50, ±100 meV.
- Berry curvature monopoles remain well resolved even for α as large as 250 eV·Å³.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Dirac semimetals A₃Bi (A=Na,K,Rb) and Cd₃As₂, which were identified as 3D Dirac semimetals protected by crystal symmetry in first-principles studies by Fang (2012) and Wang & Weng (2012). The paper provides a symmetry-based explanation for the nontrivial surface Fermi arcs previously observed in these materials, and it enables predictions of quantum oscillations with thickness-dependent periods, analogous to those in true Weyl semimetals (Vishwanath, 2011). The Z₂ Weyl semimetal structure is analogous to the Z₂ topological order in topological insulators (Kane, 2005; Hasan & Zhang, 2010).
