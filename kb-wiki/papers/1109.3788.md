# Event-by-event study of neutron observables in spontaneous and thermal fission

> The event-by-event fission model FREYA is extended to spontaneous fission of actinides and a variety of neutron observables are studied for spontaneous fission and fission induced by thermal neutrons with a view towards possible applications for detection of special nuclear materials.

## Key claim

The event-by-event fission model FREYA, previously applied to neutron-induced fission, is extended to spontaneous fission of actinides and used to study neutron observables for both spontaneous and thermal-neutron-induced fission, with the caveat that the model's adjustable parameter \(x\) (which governs the excitation energy partition between light and heavy fragments) is not tuned to reproduce the neutron multiplicity as a function of fragment mass but rather is set to a single value for each system, leading to imperfect agreement with data in some mass regions.

## Method

FREYA simulates complete fission events by first sampling fragment masses from experimental mass yields fitted with five Gaussians (two asymmetric modes and one symmetric mode), then assigning charges via a Gaussian with dispersion \(\sigma_Z = 0.5\). The total kinetic energy (TKE) for each fragment mass is taken from experimental data, with a thermal fluctuation added; the total excitation energy (TXE) follows from energy conservation and is partitioned between the two fragments assuming thermal equilibrium, then adjusted by a parameter \(x\) (greater than unity) to favor the light fragment. Neutron evaporation from the fully accelerated fragments is treated sequentially, with the spectral shape determined by the maximum temperature in the daughter nucleus.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{239}\)Pu(n\(_{\text{th}}\),f), the average total neutron multiplicity \(\overline{\nu} \approx 2.88\); for \(^{240}\)Pu(sf), \(\overline{\nu} \approx 2.15\).
* For \(^{235}\)U(n\(_{\text{th}}\),f), \(\overline{\nu} \approx 2.47\); for \(^{238}\)U(sf), \(\overline{\nu} \approx 2.0\).
* For \(^{252}\)Cf(sf), \(\overline{\nu} \approx 3.75\); for \(^{244}\)Cm(sf), \(\overline{\nu} \approx 2.72\).
* The maximum temperature in the daughter nucleus for \(^{252}\)Cf(sf) peaks at \(T \approx 1\) MeV, with a tail extending to \(T \approx 2\) MeV.
* The charge dispersion parameter is \(\sigma_Z = 0.5\) (from Reisdorf (1976)).
* The \(^{240}\)Pu(sf) experiment collected about 12,000 spontaneous fission events, a factor of about 200 below the number of thermal-neutron-induced events.
* The \(^{252}\)Cf(sf) fragment yield analysis used \(2.5 \times 10^8\) events; the \(^{244}\)Cm(sf) measurement had \(5.4 \times 10^5\) \(^{252}\)Cf fissions and 71,000 \(^{244}\)Cm fissions.
* The \(^{238}\)U(sf) yield data were based on 2,800 fission events.

## Context

This work extends the FREYA model (previously described by Vogt and Randrup (2009) and Vogt, Randrup, and others) from neutron-induced fission to spontaneous fission of actinides, enabling event-by-event studies of neutron correlations for both fission types. The results are compared to experimental data for fragment mass yields (e.g., Schillebeeckx (2007) for \(^{240}\)Pu(sf), Hambsch (2005) for \(^{252}\)Cf(sf), Ivanov (2006) for \(^{238}\)U(sf)) and neutron multiplicity distributions (e.g., Zucker (1995), Boldeman (1985), Vorobiev (2004)). The study enables future applications for special nuclear material detection by exploiting correlations between neutron observables, and the authors note that a future publication will examine prompt fission photon production.
