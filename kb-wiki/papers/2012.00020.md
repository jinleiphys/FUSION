# Single-particle digitization strategy for quantum computation of a $ϕ^4$ scalar field theory

> Motivated by the parton picture of high energy quantum chromodynamics, we develop a single-particle digitization strategy for the efficient quantum simulation of relativistic scattering processes in a $d+1$ dimensional scalar $ϕ^4$ field theory. We work out quantum algorithms for initial state preparation, time evolution and final state measurements. We outline a non-perturbative renormalization strategy in this single-particle framework.

## Key claim

The authors develop a single-particle digitization strategy for quantum simulation of relativistic scattering in scalar $\phi^4$ field theory that requires resources only logarithmic in volume (but linear in particle number), in contrast to field-operator-based approaches that scale linearly with volume; this efficiency is conditional on low particle number densities typical of high-energy scattering, and the approach introduces an approximation error of order $O(\mathfrak{n}/M)$ from truncating the number of single-particle registers at finite $M$.

## Method

The authors discretize the $\phi^4$ Hamiltonian on a spatial lattice of size $N_s^d$ and decompose the many-particle Hilbert space into single-particle sectors $\mathcal{H} = \bigotimes_{l=0}^\infty \mathcal{H}^{l}$, where each sector is spanned by empty states $|\Omega\rangle^{(l)}$ and occupied momentum eigenstates $|\mathbf{q}\rangle^{(l)}$ represented by $\log_2\mathcal{V}+1$ qubits per particle. They construct Fock operators as $a_{\mathbf{q}} \equiv \lim_{M\rightarrow\infty} \frac{1}{\sqrt{M}} \sum_{l=0}^{M-1} a^{(l)}_{\mathbf{q}}$ with $(a^{(l)\dagger}_{\mathbf{q}})^2=0$, truncating at finite $M$ such that the bosonic commutation algebra is realized up to $O(\mathfrak{n}/M)$ corrections. The algorithm for computing scattering cross-sections comprises initial state preparation of wavepackets in separated phase-space regions, Suzuki-Trotter time evolution, final state measurement, and a non-perturbative renormalization strategy.

## Key numbers

* The single-particle Hilbert space dimension grows logarithmically with volume $\mathcal{V}$ and linearly with the truncation parameter $M$, using $N \equiv \log_2\mathcal{V}+1$ qubits per single-particle register.
* The bosonic commutation algebra $[a_{\mathbf{q}},a^\dagger_{\mathbf{q}'}] = \delta_{\mathbf{q},\mathbf{q}'}$ is realized with error $O(\mathfrak{n}/M)$, where $\mathfrak{n}$ is the typical occupancy.
* The lattice Hamiltonian uses dimensionless bare mass $m = \overline{m} a_s$ and coupling $\lambda = \overline{\lambda} a_s^{4-d}$ on a lattice of size $N_s^d$ with spacing $a_s$.
* The Ioffe time for DIS is $\tau_0 \sim 1/(2 M_p x_{\rm Bj})$ and the interaction time is $\tau_I \sim 1/Q$, with $Q^2 \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2$.

## Context

This work extends the single-particle digitization approach previously introduced by the same authors (Mueller et al., 2020) for deeply inelastic scattering, and builds on the foundational quantum simulation framework for relativistic scattering developed by Jordan, Lee and Preskill (2011, 2012). The strategy is motivated by the parton picture of high-energy QCD and the virial expansion of scattering amplitudes (Dashen, 1969; Dashen, 1974), and enables quantum computation of scattering cross-sections in regimes where particle number density is small, with potential extensions to hybrid quantum-classical treatments of dense systems such as those encountered in small-$x$ QCD (Gelis, 2010) and effective field theories for jet physics (Bauer, 2000).
