# Difficulties in probing density dependent symmetry potential with the HBT interferometry

> Based on the updated UrQMD transport model, the effect of the symmetry potential energy on the two-nucleon HBT correlation is investigated with the help of the coalescence program for constructing clusters, and the CRAB analyzing program of the two-particle HBT correlation. An obvious non-linear dependence of the neutron-proton (or neutron-neutron) HBT correlation function ($C_{np,nn}$) at small relative momenta on the stiffness factor $γ$ of the symmetry potential energy is found: when $γ\lesssim 0.8$, the $C_{np,nn}$ increases rapidly with increasing $γ$, while it starts to saturate if $γ\gtrsim 0.8$. It is also found that both the symmetry potential energy at low densities and the conditions of constructing clusters at the late stage of the whole process influence the two-nucleon HBT correlation with the same power.

## Key claim

The neutron-proton and neutron-neutron HBT correlation functions at small relative momenta show a non-linear dependence on the stiffness factor γ of the symmetry potential energy, with rapid increase for γ ≲ 0.8 and saturation for γ ≳ 0.8, meaning the correlation function cannot distinguish the stiffness of the symmetry energy when γ > 0.8.

## Method

The authors used the updated UrQMD transport model to simulate central collisions of ⁵²Ca+⁴⁸Ca at 100A MeV and ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au at beam energies from 100A to 1000A MeV. A coalescence model (with default parameters P₀ = 0.3 GeV/c and R₀ = 3.5 fm) was used to construct clusters from the transport output, and the CRAB v3.0β program was employed to analyze the two-nucleon HBT correlation functions. The stiffness factor γ of the symmetry potential energy (parametrized as E_sym = S₀u^γ) was varied over a wide range, and different freeze-out conditions (stop times, coalescence parameters) were tested.

## Key numbers

- For ⁵²Ca+⁴⁸Ca central collisions at E_b = 100A MeV, the neutron-proton correlation function C_np saturates at a value of 3.15 ± 0.08 for γ ≳ 0.8.
- The non-linear dependence of C_np on γ shows rapid increase when γ ≲ 0.8, with saturation beginning at γ ≈ 0.8.
- For Au+Au central collisions, the effect of density-dependent symmetry energy on C_np becomes visible at beam energies from 200A MeV up to 1000A MeV.
- At E_b = 1000A MeV for Au+Au collisions, the neutron-neutron correlation function shows an even more obvious non-linear dependence on γ than the neutron-proton case.
- Default coalescence parameters used: P₀ = 0.3 GeV/c and R₀ = 3.5 fm.
- The transport calculations were stopped at 150 fm/c (with additional tests at 250 fm/c for Au+Au at 100A MeV).

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Chen (2003) and Chen (2004) on using two-nucleon HBT correlations as probes of the symmetry energy, but reveals a previously unnoticed non-linear saturation effect that limits the sensitivity for γ > 0.8. The authors note that the free n/p ratio decreases monotonically with increasing γ (consistent with earlier work), while the HBT correlation function does not. The paper also builds on the finding by Kowalski (2006) of a very soft symmetry energy (γ ≈ 0.3) at very low densities (0.01-0.05 times normal density), and addresses the need for more careful examination of experimental observables raised by Li (2008). The results indicate that before using HBT correlations to constrain the symmetry energy, the uncertainty in nucleon multiplicities from freeze-out conditions must be largely reduced, as both the symmetry potential at low densities and cluster construction conditions influence the correlation with equal power.
