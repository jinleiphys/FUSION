# Nature of $X(3872)$ from its radiative decay

> We study the radiative decay of $X(3872)$ based on the assumption that $X(3872)$ is regarded as a $c\overline{c}$ charmonium with quantum number $J^{PC}=1^{++}$ ($J,\,P,\,C$ represent the spin, parity and charge conjugation, respectively). The form factors of $X(3872)$ transitions to $J/ψγ$ and $ψ'γ$ ($ψ'$ denotes $ψ(2S)$ throughout the paper) are calculated in the framework of the covariant light-front quark model. The phenomenological wave function of a meson depends on the parameter $β$, whose inverse essentially describes the confinement scale. In the present work, the parameters $β$ for the vector $J/ψ$ and $ψ'$ mesons will be determined through their decay constants, which are obtained from the experimental values of their partial decay widths to the electron-positron pair. For $X(3872)$, we determined the value of $β$ by the decay width of $X(3872)\rightarrow ψ'γ$. Then, we examined the width of $X(3872)\to J/ψγ$ in a manner of parameter-free prediction and compared it with the experimental value. As a result, an inconsistency or contradiction occurs between the widths of $X(3872)\to J/ψγ$ and $X(3872)\to ψ'γ$. We thus conclude that $X(3872)$ cannot be a pure $c\overline c$ resonance and that other components are necessary in its wave function.

# Nature of $X(3872)$ from its radiative decay

## Key claim
Assuming $X(3872)$ is a pure $c\bar{c}$ charmonium with $J^{PC}=1^{++}$, the predicted partial decay width for $X(3872)\to J/\psi\gamma$ is $(9.1^{+1.7}_{-1.5})\times10^{-1}$ MeV, which is too large compared to the experimental value of $(3.2\pm1.6)\times10^{-3}$ MeV, leading to the conclusion that $X(3872)$ cannot be a pure $c\bar{c}$ resonance and other components are necessary in its wave function.

## Method
The authors calculated the form factors for $X(3872)\to J/\psi\gamma$ and $X(3872)\to \psi'\gamma$ transitions in the covariant light-front quark model (CLFQM). The parameter $\beta$ for $J/\psi$ and $\psi'$ was fixed using their decay constants extracted from experimental $e^+e^-$ partial widths, while $\beta_{X(3872)}$ was determined by fitting to the experimental width of $X(3872)\to \psi'\gamma$. The width of $X(3872)\to J/\psi\gamma$ was then predicted as a parameter-free result and compared to the experimental value.

## Key numbers
- $\beta_{J/\psi} = 0.631$ GeV, fixed from $f_{J/\psi} = 415.49$ MeV (extracted from $\Gamma(J/\psi\to e^+e^-)$ using Br = $5.971\%$ and $\Gamma(J/\psi) = 92.6$ keV)
- $\beta_{\psi'} = 0.487$ GeV, fixed from $f_{\psi'} = 294.35$ MeV (extracted from $\Gamma(\psi'\to e^+e^-)$ using Br = $7.93\times10^{-3}$ and $\Gamma(\psi(2S)) = 294$ keV)
- $\beta_{X(3872)} = 0.56^{+0.04}_{-0.03}$ GeV, fixed from $\Gamma_{\text{exp}}(X(3872)\to \psi'\gamma) = (1.8\pm0.8)\times10^{-2}$ MeV
- Predicted $\Gamma(X(3872)\to J/\psi\gamma) = (9.1^{+1.7}_{-1.5})\times10^{-1}$ MeV, compared to experimental value $(3.2\pm1.6)\times10^{-3}$ MeV
- PDG average total width $\Gamma_{X(3872)} = (1.19\pm0.21)$ MeV (Breit-Wigner); pole width taken as 0.4 MeV

## Context
This work extends the study of $X(3872)$ structure beyond line-shape analyses (Kang 2016), which could not distinguish between bound state and virtual state of $\bar{D}D^*$. The results contradict the pure charmonium interpretation and align with BESIII (2020) findings that disfavor a pure charmonium state. The calculation enables future work comparing tetraquark models (Dubnicka 2011), $D\bar{D}^*$ molecular assumptions (Aceti 2012, Guo 2014), and coupled-channel $D\bar{D}^* + c\bar{c}$ approaches (Badalian 2012) for understanding $X(3872)$ composition.
