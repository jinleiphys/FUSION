# Note on spin-orbit interactions in nuclei and hypernuclei

> A detailed comparison is made between the spin-orbit interactions in $Λ$ hypernuclei and ordinary nuclei. We argue that there are three major contributions to the spin-orbit interaction: 1) a short-range component involving scalar and vector mean fields; 2) a ''wrong-sign'' spin-orbit term generated by the pion exchange tensor force in second order; and 3) a three-body term induced by two-pion exchange with excitation of virtual $Δ(1232)$-isobars (a la Fujita-Miyazawa). For nucleons in nuclei the long-range pieces related to the pion-exchange dynamics tend to cancel, leaving room dominantly for spin-orbit mechanisms of short-range origin (parametrized e.g. in terms of relativistic scalar and vector mean fields terms). In contrast, the absence of an analogous $2π$-exchange three-body contribution for $Λ$ hyperons in hypernuclei leads to an almost complete cancellation between the short-range (relativistic mean-field) component and the ''wrong-sign'' spin-orbit interaction generated by second order $π$-exchange with an intermediate $Σ$ hyperon. These different balancing mechanisms between short- and long-range components are able to explain simultaneously the very strong spin-orbit interaction in ordinary nuclei and the remarkably weak spin-orbit splitting in $Λ$ hypernuclei.

# Note on spin-orbit interactions in nuclei and hypernuclei

## Key claim

The paper argues that the spin-orbit interaction in nuclei and hypernuclei arises from a balance of three major contributions, short-range scalar/vector mean fields, a "wrong-sign" term from second-order pion exchange, and a three-body term from two-pion exchange with virtual Δ(1232) excitation, and that the absence of the three-body term for Λ hyperons leads to an almost complete cancellation between the short-range and wrong-sign components, explaining both the strong spin-orbit splitting in ordinary nuclei and the remarkably weak splitting in Λ hypernuclei.

## Method

The authors compute the spin-orbit coupling strength function F_so(ρ) for nucleons in nuclear matter using the density matrix expansion of Negele and Vautherin, evaluating analytic expressions for the Hartree and Fock diagrams of iterated one-pion exchange and for the Fujita-Miyazawa three-nucleon interaction with intermediate Δ(1232) excitation. For Λ hypernuclei, they calculate the spin-orbit self-energy of a Λ hyperon interacting with weakly inhomogeneous nuclear matter, including the two-pion exchange process with an intermediate Σ hyperon and comparing it to short-range contributions scaled from the nucleonic case.

## Key numbers

- At nuclear matter saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, the "wrong-sign" spin-orbit coupling strength from iterated one-pion exchange (including Fock and Pauli blocking) is F_so(ρ₀)^(ππ) = −47.4 MeV·fm⁵.
- The Fujita-Miyazawa three-body contribution at ρ₀ gives F_so(ρ₀)^(Δ) = 47.5 MeV·fm⁵, canceling the wrong-sign term.
- At half saturation density ρ₀/2 = 0.08 fm⁻³, the balance is −58.1 MeV·fm⁵ (iterated π-exchange) against +48.3 MeV·fm⁵ (three-body), illustrating uncertainties.
- The empirical spin-orbit coupling strength from Skyrme phenomenology is F_so(ρ) ≡ 3W₀/4 ≈ 90 MeV·fm⁵.
- For Λ hypernuclei, the measured p₃/₂−p₁/₂ spin-orbit splitting in ¹³ΛC is (152 ± 65) keV, compared to about 6 MeV in ordinary nuclei.
- The Λ-nuclear spin-orbit coupling strength from two-pion exchange with intermediate Σ hyperon is U_Λls(ρ₀) ≈ −15 MeV·fm² at ρ₀.
- The ΣΛ mass splitting is M_Σ − M_Λ = 77.5 MeV.
- The Δ-nucleon mass splitting is Δ = 293 MeV.
- The pion decay constant is f_π = 92.4 MeV and the nucleon axial vector constant g_A ≈ 1.3.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic scalar-vector mean field models of Walecka (1974) and the Fujita-Miyazawa three-nucleon interaction (1957) by incorporating chiral effective field theory calculations of two-pion exchange contributions. It directly addresses the long-standing puzzle of weak spin-orbit splitting in Λ hypernuclei, previously approached via tensor coupling of the ω meson to the Λ in SU(6) quark models. The cancellation mechanism proposed here builds on the earlier work of Kaiser (2006) on Λ-nuclear spin-orbit coupling and is shown to be consistent with recent finite hypernucleus calculations by Finelli, Kaiser, Vretenar, and Weise (2007). The results enable a unified chiral effective field theory description of spin-orbit interactions across both nuclear and hypernuclear systems.
