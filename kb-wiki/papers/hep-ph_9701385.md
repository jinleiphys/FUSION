# Symmetry Tests of the Electroweak Interaction from Muon Capture on 3He

> Precision measurements in muon capture on 3He to the triton channel provide for interesting tests of the charged electroweak interaction, whether within the Standard Model or beyond it. Based on the statistical capture rate and the triton asymmetry, both the object of recent and on-going experiments using polarised 3He targets, examples of such tests are presented, and shown to lead to stringent complementary constraints for which further dedicated experimental as well as theoretical efforts are required.

# Symmetry Tests of the Electroweak Interaction from Muon Capture on 3He

## Key claim

Precision measurements of the statistical muon capture rate and the triton asymmetry in muon capture on 3He to the triton channel can provide stringent tests of the charged electroweak interaction both within and beyond the Standard Model, but further dedicated experimental and theoretical efforts are required to realize the full potential of these tests, particularly because the present theoretical uncertainties on nuclear form factors, especially the axial form factor FA, dominate the error budget.

## Method

The analysis uses the effective (V−A) amplitude for the charged current interaction, parametrizing the hadronic current in terms of vector, axial, induced pseudoscalar, and second-class form factors (FV, FM, FS, FA, FP, FT) at the relevant momentum transfer q²₀ = −0.954 m²μ. The statistical capture rate λstat and triton asymmetry Av are computed from this amplitude, and the experimental value of λstat (1496 ± 4 s⁻¹) is combined with hypothetical precision values for Av to extract constraints on the form factors and on beyond-Standard-Model couplings, including left-right symmetric model parameters and scalar/tensor interactions.

## Key numbers

- Experimental statistical capture rate: λexp_stat = 1496 ± 3 (stat.) ± 3 (syst.) s⁻¹ = 1496 ± 4 s⁻¹ (0.3% precision)
- Theoretical prediction for λstat: λtheor_stat = 1497 ± 21 s⁻¹ (larger uncertainty) or 1497 ± 12 s⁻¹ (smaller uncertainty)
- Theoretical prediction for triton asymmetry: Atheor_v = 0.524 ± 0.006 (both uncertainty ranges)
- Vector form factors at q²₀: FV = 0.834 ± 0.011, FM = −13.969 ± 0.052
- Axial form factor at q²₀: FA = −1.052 ± 0.005 (or ± 0.010)
- Normalized induced pseudoscalar form factor from λstat: Fnorm_P ≡ (mμ/2M) FP(q²₀) = −20.80 ± 1.57 (or ± 2.74), compared to PCAC prediction of −20.72 ± 0.10 (or ± 0.20)
- Second-class form factor constraints from λstat: FS = −0.062 ± 1.18 (or ± 2.02), FT = 0.075 ± 1.43 (or ± 2.45)
- e−μ universality test from λstat: |hV−−/fV−−|² = 0.9996 ± 0.0083 (or ± 0.0142)
- Left-right symmetric model mixing angle constraint: (gR/gL) Re(eiω vud) tanζ = −0.00016 ± 0.00295 (or ± 0.00506)
- Scalar coupling constraint: (hS++ + hS+−)/(hV−− + hV−+) GS = −0.0012 ± 0.0221 (or ± 0.0380)
- Tensor coupling constraint: hT+− GT = −0.00008 ± 0.00143 (or ± 0.00245)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of muon capture on 3He by Congleton and collaborators (1992, 1996) and by Ackerstaff et al. (1997), who provided the high-precision experimental capture rate. It complements earlier studies of second-class currents by Holstein (1984) and of left-right symmetric models by Deutsch and collaborators (1995). The paper demonstrates that a 1% measurement of the triton asymmetry, currently in progress by Cummings et al. (1995), would enable a purely experimental determination of FA and FP independent of tritium beta-decay, and would improve constraints on second-class form factors by about a factor of two. The analysis also shows that muon capture on 3He provides complementary constraints to those from pion decay and beta-decay for testing e−μ universality and searching for scalar and tensor interactions.
