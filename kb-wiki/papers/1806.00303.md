# Momentum dependence of pion-induced $ϕ$ meson production on nuclei near threshold

> We study the near-threshold pion-induced production of $ϕ$ mesons off nuclei in the kinematical conditions of the HADES experiment, recently performed at GSI. The calculations have been performed within a collision model based on the nuclear spectral function. The model accounts for both the primary $π^-$ meson--proton ${π^-}p \to ϕn$ and the secondary pion--nucleon $πN \to ϕN$ $ϕ$ production processes as well as the effects of the nuclear $ϕ$ and nucleon mean-field potentials. We find that the primary reaction channel ${π^-}p \to ϕn$ dominates in the $ϕ$ production off $^{12}$C and $^{184}$W target nuclei in the HADES acceptance window at incident pion momentum of 1.7 GeV/c. We calculate the momentum dependence of the absolute and relative (transparency ratio) $ϕ$ meson yields from the above direct channel. The calculations have been performed for this initial pion momentum allowing for different options for the $ϕN$ absorption cross section $σ_{ϕN}$ and different scenarios for the in-medium mass shifts of the $ϕ$ meson and secondary neutron, produced together with $ϕ$ in this channel. We demonstrate that the transparency ratio for the $ϕ$ mesons has, contrary to the absolute cross sections, an insignificant sensitivity to the $ϕ$ meson and secondary neutron in-medium mass shifts at $ϕ$ momenta studied in the HADES experiment. On the other hand, we show that there are measurable changes in the transparency ratio due to the $ϕN$ absorption cross section, which means that such relative observable can be useful to help determine this cross section from the data taken in the HADES experiment.

## Key claim

The primary reaction channel π⁻p → φn dominates φ meson production off ¹²C and ¹⁸⁴W target nuclei in the HADES acceptance window at an incident pion momentum of 1.7 GeV/c, but this conclusion depends on the assumption that secondary pion-nucleon processes (πN → φN) and two-step production mechanisms contribute only negligibly under these kinematic conditions.

## Method

The calculations were performed within a collision model based on the nuclear spectral function, accounting for both primary π⁻p → φn and secondary πN → φN production processes, as well as the effects of nuclear φ and nucleon mean-field potentials. The model incorporates the distortion of the incident pion in nuclear matter, φ meson final-state absorption described by an effective in-medium cross section σ_φN, and uses harmonic oscillator (for ¹²C) and Woods-Saxon (for ¹⁸⁴W) nuclear density distributions. The inclusive differential cross sections were integrated over the HADES acceptance window (polar angles 10° to 45°) for φ momenta in the range 0.6–1.1 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- The free threshold momentum for the π⁻p → φn process is 1.559 GeV/c; the incident pion momentum studied is 1.7 GeV/c.
- The free total cross section σ_{π⁻p→φn} at the incident momentum of 1.7 GeV/c (center-of-mass energy √s = 2.025 GeV, excess energy 66 MeV) is 31 μb using parametrization (19), and 28.5 μb using parametrization (20).
- The average nucleon density ratio ⟨ρ_N⟩/ρ₀ is 0.55 for ¹²C and 0.76 for ¹⁸⁴W.
- The φ mass shift adopted is U_φ = -20 MeV (2% reduction at saturation density ρ₀); the neutron scalar potential U_N ≈ 25 MeV for momenta around 0.8 GeV/c.
- The average Fermi momentum is 216 MeV/c for ¹²C and 241 MeV/c for ¹⁸⁴W.
- The φN absorption cross section σ_φN was varied over values 10, 20, 30, and 40 mb.
- The total cross section for free π⁻N interaction at 1.7 GeV/c is taken as σ_{π⁻N}^{tot} = 35 mb.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of φ meson production in nuclei from proton-nucleus reactions (ANKE at COSY, 2010–2015) and photon-nucleus reactions (LEPS at SPring-8, CLAS at JLab) to the pion-induced channel, using the spectral function approach previously applied by Paryev (2014–2017) to meson production. The calculations are designed to interpret data from the HADES experiment at GSI (2018) on π⁻-induced φ production on ¹²C and ¹⁸⁴W targets. The results enable extraction of the in-medium φN absorption cross section from the measured transparency ratio, which is shown to be insensitive to φ and neutron in-medium mass shifts but sensitive to σ_φN.
