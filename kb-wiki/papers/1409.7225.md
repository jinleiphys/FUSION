# $^1S_0$ pairing for neutrons in dense neutron matter induced by a soft pion

> The possibility of neutron pairing in the $^1S_0$ channel is studied for dense neutron matter in a vicinity of the $π^0$ condensation point. The $^1S_0$ pairing gap $Δ$ is shown to occur in a model with a pairing force induced by the exchange of a soft neutral pionic mode. The soft pion induced potential $V_π(r)$ is characterized by an attenuating oscillatory behavior in coordinate space, while in momentum space all $S$-wave matrix elements $V_π(p,p')$ are positive. The solution of the gap equation reveals strong momentum dependence.

# $^1S_0$ pairing for neutrons in dense neutron matter induced by a soft pion

## Key claim

The $^1S_0$ neutron pairing gap can reappear in dense neutron matter at densities above the conventional vanishing point, but only within approximately 5% of the critical density for $\pi^0$ condensation, and only in a model where the pairing interaction is dominated by the exchange of a soft neutral pion mode while all regular contributions to the interaction are omitted.

## Method

The authors solve the BCS gap equation in the $^1S_0$ channel for neutron matter using a pairing potential $V_\pi(q)$ derived from the static limit of the in-medium pion propagator near the $\pi^0$ condensation critical point. The potential is projected onto the spin-singlet state and takes a Lorentzian-like form in momentum space with a dimensionless proximity parameter $\eta$ that measures the distance from the condensation point. The single-particle spectrum is taken as free with bare neutron mass, and the chemical potential is determined self-consistently from the particle number conservation condition.

## Key numbers

* The $^1S_0$ pairing instability occurs at $\eta_\Delta \simeq 0.38$, corresponding to $(n_c-n)/n_c \simeq 2\%$ from the critical density.
* At $\eta = 0.20$ (closest to the critical point studied), the gap function at the Fermi surface is $\Delta_{\rm F} = 0.23$ in units of $p_{\rm F}^2/2m$, and at zero momentum $\Delta_0 = -5.76$ in the same units.
* The correlation length $\xi$ at $\eta = 0.20$ is $\xi\,p_{\rm F} = 5.87$, exceeding the average interparticle distance $r_s = (9\pi/4)^{1/3}/p_{\rm F}$.
* The critical density for $\pi^0$ condensation is taken as $n_c \simeq 0.2$ fm$^{-3}$, with critical momentum $q_c = p_{\rm F}$ in the model.
* Adding the Argonne $v_{18}$ $NN$ potential to $V_\pi$ changes the gap values by 5-30%, with the largest relative effect at $\eta = 0.35$ where $\Delta_{\rm F}$ increases from 0.005 to 0.013 (in units of $p_{\rm F}^2/2m$).

## Context

This work extends the study of neutron pairing beyond the well-established density range $n \le 0.18$ fm$^{-3}$ where $^1S_0$ pairing is known from BCS calculations with bare $NN$ potentials (Dean and Hjorth-Jensen, 2003). It builds on earlier investigations showing that soft pion exchange amplifies spin-triplet $P$-wave pairing (Khodel et al., 2004) and can trigger topological phase transitions in neutron matter (Voskresensky, 2000; Pankratov et al., 2012, 2013). The results suggest that $^1S_0$ superfluidity may exist in neutron star cores near the $\pi^0$ condensation point, providing a possible astrophysical signal for pion condensation. The study is limited to the Landau state with a single Fermi surface; extension to states with non-Fermi-liquid topology is left for future work.
