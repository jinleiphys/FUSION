# Complex Heavy-Quark Potential at Finite Temperature from Lattice QCD

> We calculate for the first time the complex potential between a heavy quark and antiquark at finite temperature across the deconfinement transition in lattice QCD. The real and imaginary part of the potential at each separation distance $r$ is obtained from the spectral function of the thermal Wilson loop. We confirm the existence of an imaginary part above the critical temperature $T_C$, which grows as a function of $r$ and underscores the importance of collisions with the gluonic environment for the melting of heavy quarkonia in the quark-gluon-plasma.

# Complex Heavy-Quark Potential at Finite Temperature from Lattice QCD

## Key claim

The paper presents the first nonperturbative extraction of a complex heavy-quark potential from lattice QCD across the deconfinement transition, but the results are obtained in quenched (pure gauge) QCD on a relatively small spatial lattice of about 2 fm, and the imaginary part above T_C is extracted from the width of the lowest-lying peak in the spectral function using the maximum entropy method, which introduces systematic uncertainties from the choice of prior amplitude.

## Method

The authors compute the Euclidean thermal Wilson loop on an anisotropic 20^3 x N_tau lattice with anisotropy xi = a_sigma / a_tau = 4 and spatial lattice spacing a_sigma = 0.097 fm, at three temperatures corresponding to N_tau = 36, 24, 12 (T/T_C = 0.78, 1.17, 2.33). They reconstruct the spectral function rho_square(r, omega) from the Euclidean Wilson loop data using the maximum entropy method (MEM) with an extended search space, then extract the real part of the potential from the peak position and the imaginary part from the peak width of the lowest-lying peak in the spectral function.

## Key numbers

- Spatial lattice spacing a_sigma = 0.097 fm (beta = 6.1) with anisotropy xi = 4
- Temperatures studied: T/T_C = 0.78, 1.17, 2.33 with T_C approximately 290 MeV
- Spatial lattice size L approximately 2 fm, accommodating the J/psi scale of r_J/psi approximately 0.5 fm
- MEM frequency interval I_omega approximately [-21 GeV, 42 GeV] at N_tau = 24 with N_omega = 1500 points
- At T = 2.33 T_C, a crosscheck with finer lattice (a_sigma = 0.039 fm, 20^3 x 32) yields consistent results for both real and imaginary parts
- Prior amplitude variation explored over interval 10^-2 >= m_0 >= 10^-6

## Context

This work extends perturbative calculations of the complex heavy-quark potential using hard-thermal-loop resummation (Laine 2006, Beraudo 2007) and heavy-quark effective field theory (Brambilla 2008) to the nonperturbative regime around T_C, which was previously inaccessible. The results show that the real part of the potential around T_C is temperature-insensitive and differs from the color-singlet free energies in Coulomb gauge, suggesting that the imaginary part (collisional broadening) may play a more important role in quarkonium melting than Debye screening. This enables first-principles input for nonrelativistic real-time simulations of heavy quarkonia in the quark-gluon plasma, going beyond both models and perturbation theory.
