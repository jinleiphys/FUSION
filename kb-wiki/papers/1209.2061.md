# Chiral symmetry restoration, eigenvalue density of Dirac operator and axial U(1) anomaly at finite temperature

> We reconsider constraints on the eigenvalue density of the Dirac operator in the chiral symmetric phase of 2 flavor QCD at finite temperature. To avoid possible ultra-violet(UV) divergences, we work on a lattice, employing the overlap Dirac operator, which ensures the exact "chiral" symmetry at finite lattice spacings. Studying multi-point correlation functions in various channels and taking their thermodynamical limit (and then taking the chiral limit), we obtain stronger constraints than those found in the previous studies: both the eigenvalue density at the origin and its first and second derivatives vanish in the chiral limit of 2 flavor QCD. In addition we show that the axial U(1) anomaly becomes invisible in susceptibilities of scalar and pseudo scalar mesons, suggesting that the 2nd order chiral phase transition with the O(4) scaling is not realized in 2 flavor QCD. Possible lattice artifacts when non-chiral lattice Dirac operator is employed are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The authors derive stronger constraints than previous studies on the Dirac operator eigenvalue density in the chiral symmetric phase of 2-flavor QCD at finite temperature, showing that not only the eigenvalue density at the origin but also its first and second derivatives vanish in the chiral limit, which suggests that the second-order chiral phase transition with O(4) scaling is not realized.

## Method

The authors work on a lattice using the overlap Dirac operator, which preserves exact chiral symmetry through the Ginsparg-Wilson relation, to avoid ultraviolet divergences. They study multi-point correlation functions of scalar and pseudo-scalar operators in various channels, take the thermodynamical (infinite volume) limit, and then take the chiral limit to derive constraints on the eigenvalue density expansion coefficients. The analysis includes explicit evaluation of Ward-Takahashi identities for the SU(2)L x SU(2)R and U(1)A symmetries at different operator orders.

## Key numbers

- The eigenvalue density near zero is constrained to behave as rho(lambda) ~ |lambda|^alpha with alpha > 2, stronger than the previous constraint of alpha > 1 from Cohen (1996).
- For integer alpha, the authors argue the strongest constraint is alpha >= 3, noting that 2-flavor massless free quarks have alpha = 3 with both SU(2)L x SU(2)R and U(1)A symmetries unbroken.
- The zero-mode contribution to correlation functions such as the Lee-Hatsuda expression is shown to be at least O(1/V), vanishing in the large volume limit V -> infinity.

## Context

This work extends the analytic analysis of Cohen (1996), who derived the alpha > 1 constraint from scalar one-point and pseudoscalar two-point functions, and addresses the claim of Lee and Hatsuda (1996) that zero-mode contributions could keep disconnected scalar channel contributions non-zero. The authors show that zero-mode contributions are O(1/V) and thus vanish in the thermodynamic limit. The results contradict the O(4) scaling scenario for the second-order chiral phase transition in 2-flavor QCD proposed by Pisarski and Wilczek (1984), and provide a framework for interpreting conflicting lattice results from simulations using different quark actions, including those of the HotQCD Collaboration (2012) and Cossu et al. (2011).
