# Anomaly-induced charges in nucleons

> We show a novel charge structure of baryons in electromagnetic field due to the chiral anomaly. A key connection is to treat baryons as solitons of mesons. We use Skyrmions to calculate the charge distributions in a single nucleon and find an additional charge. We also perform calculations of charge distribution for classical multi-baryons with B=2, 3,...,8 and 17; they show amusing charge distributions.

## Key claim

Nucleons in external electromagnetic fields acquire an additional, generally non-integer electric charge due to the chiral anomaly, even for neutrons; this charge is a leading-order effect in the large-\(N_c\) expansion and cannot be cancelled by other electromagnetic corrections.

## Method

The authors use the Skyrme model with a Wess-Zumino-Witten (WZW) term to describe baryons as topological solitons of mesons. They treat the electromagnetic field as a perturbation, compute the anomalous current from the WZW term for hedgehog Skyrmion configurations, and then quantize the \(S^3\) moduli parameters to obtain matrix elements for nucleon states. For multi-baryons with baryon numbers \(B=2,3,\dots,8,17\), they use the rational-map ansatz to construct classical Skyrmion solutions and evaluate the classical anomalous charge distribution.

## Key numbers

- The numerical constant \(c_0 = \int dr \{r^2 f' + \sin(2f)\}\) takes values \((-5.32, -12.3, -10.2, -7.32)\) for pion masses \(m_\pi = (0, m_\pi^{\rm phys}/2, m_\pi^{\rm phys}, 2m_\pi^{\rm phys})\), where \(m_\pi^{\rm phys} \equiv 0.263\) in units of \(e_s F_\pi\).
- The induced anomalous charge for a nucleon is \(Q_{\rm anm} = \frac{4e^2 N_c}{27\pi} I_3 S_3 \frac{c_0 B_3}{(e_s F_\pi)^2}\).
- Under a terrestrial magnetic field \(B \sim 1\) G, the induced charge is about \(10^{-20} e\).
- On the surface of a magnetar with \(B \sim 10^{15}\) G, the induced charge is about \(10^{-5} e\).
- In heavy ion collisions with \(B \sim 10^{17}\) G, the induced charge is about \(10^{-3} e\).
- The leading multipole is a quadrupole: \(Q_{33} = -2Q_{11} = -2Q_{22} \sim e \times 10^{-19} I_3 S_3 \, [{\rm fm}^2\,{\rm G}^{-1}] \times B_3\) [G].

## Context

This work extends the Witten effect (Witten 1979, Wilczek 1987), where monopoles acquire electric charge via an anomalous CP-odd term, and the chiral magnetic effect (Kharzeev 2008, Fukushima 2008, Eto 2010, Voloshin 2009) in heavy-ion collisions. It provides a concrete Skyrme-model calculation of anomaly-induced charges in baryons, predicting that neutrons carry a small electric charge in strong magnetic fields, which could be relevant for neutron stars and heavy-ion collisions. The results call for confirmation via lattice QCD or holographic QCD approaches.
