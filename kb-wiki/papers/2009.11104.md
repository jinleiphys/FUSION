# Density-Dependent Neutron-Neutron Interaction from Subleading Chiral Three-Neutron Forces

> Three-nucleon forces are an essential ingredient for an accurate description of nuclear few- and many-body systems. However, implementing them directly in many-body calculations is technically very challenging. Thus, there is a need for an efficient approximation method. By closing one nucleon line to a loop, it is possible to derive effective in-medium nucleon-nucleon interactions that represent the underlying three-nucleon forces, as constructed in Chiral Effective Field Theory. Since three-neutron forces are equally as important for the computation of the equation of state for pure neutron matter, this work applies the aforementioned approach to the subleading chiral three-neutron forces, in particular the short-range terms and relativistic corrections. It is shown in this work that, while many contributions to the in-medium neutron-neutron interaction are - apart from a constant factor - identical to the terms in isospin-symmetric matter, some differ drastically. Moreover, previously vanishing terms yield now non-zero contributions. As a result of this work, density-dependent in-medium neutron-neutron potentials are now available for the implementation in nuclear many-body calculations, either in closed analytical form, or requiring at most one numerical integration.

## Key claim

This work derives effective in-medium neutron-neutron (nn) potentials from subleading chiral three-neutron (3n) forces, specifically the short-range terms and relativistic corrections, by closing one neutron line to a loop. While many contributions are identical up to a constant isospin factor to those in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, some differ drastically, and previously vanishing terms yield non-zero contributions in pure neutron matter.

## Method

The author starts from subleading chiral three-nucleon forces taken from the literature, adapts them to pure neutron matter by substituting isospin operators (e.g., \(\vec{\tau}_i \cdot \vec{\tau}_j \rightarrow 1\)), and then closes one neutron line to a Fermi-sphere loop integral to obtain an effective density-dependent nn interaction. Four distinct topologies are considered: self-closings, short-range vertex corrections, pionic vertex corrections, and double exchanges. The calculations are performed in the center-of-mass frame under the heavy baryon limit, assuming zero temperature, spin-saturated neutron matter, and elastic on-shell scattering.

## Key numbers

* Neutron mass: \(M = 940\ \text{MeV}\)
* Neutral pion mass: \(m_\pi = 135\ \text{MeV}\)
* Axial-vector coupling constant: \(g_A = 1.3\)
* Weak pion-decay constant: \(f_\pi = 92.2\ \text{MeV}\)
* Neutron density relation: \(\rho_n = k_n^3 / 3\pi^2\), where \(k_n\) is the neutron Fermi momentum
* The one-pion-exchange nn potential at tree level is given by \(V_{1\pi} = - (g_A/2f_\pi)^2 \times (m_\pi^2 + q^2)^{-1}\, \vec{\sigma}_1 \cdot \vec{q}\, \vec{\sigma}_2 \cdot \vec{q}\)
* The two contributions to the one-pion-exchange-contact topology are opposite in sign and identical otherwise, thus adding to zero (no net contribution)
* For the two-pion-exchange-contact topology, the pion-loop function at zero momentum-transfer is \(A(0) = 1/(4m_\pi)\)

## Context

This work extends the method of constructing density-dependent in-medium nucleon-nucleon interactions from subleading chiral three-nucleon forces, previously applied to isospin-symmetric spin-saturated nuclear matter by the same group (e.g., the author cites prior work on short-range terms, intermediate-range terms, and long-range terms), to the case of pure neutron matter. The resulting potentials are now available for implementation in nuclear many-body calculations, enabling improved studies of neutron star properties, the isospin-asymmetry energy, and its slope as a function of nuclear density.
