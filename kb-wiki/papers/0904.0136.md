# The hidden charm decay of Y(4140) by the rescattering mechanism

> Assuming that Y(4140) is the second radial excitation of the P-wave charmonium $χ_{cJ}^{\prime\prime}$ ($J=0, 1$), the hidden charm decay mode of Y(4140) is calculated in terms of the rescattering mechanism. Our numerical results show that the upper limit of the branching ratio of the hidden charm decay $Y(4140)\to J/ψϕ$ is on the order of $10^{-4}\sim 10^{-3}$ for both of the charmonium assumptions for Y(4140), which disfavors the large hidden charm decay pattern indicated by the CDF experiment. It seems to reveal that the pure second radial excitation of the P-wave charmonium $χ_{cJ}^{\prime\prime}$ ($J=0, 1$) is problematic.

## Key claim

Assuming Y(4140) is the second radial excitation of the P-wave charmonium χ″cJ (J=0,1), the upper limit of the branching ratio for the hidden charm decay Y(4140)→J/ψφ is on the order of 10⁻⁴ to 10⁻³, which disfavors the large hidden charm decay pattern indicated by the CDF experiment and suggests that the pure charmonium interpretation is problematic.

## Method

The hidden charm decay Y(4140)→J/ψφ was calculated using the rescattering mechanism, where for the χ″c0 (Jᴾ=0⁺) assumption the decay proceeds via Dₛ⁺Dₛ⁻ rescattering, and for the χ″c1 (Jᴾ=1⁺) assumption via Dₛ⁺Dₛ*⁻+h.c. rescattering. Effective Lagrangians based on chiral symmetry and heavy quark symmetry were employed, with coupling constants taken from prior literature. The coupling constant gY was extracted by fitting the central value of the total width of Y(4140) (11.7 MeV), approximating the open charm decay as the dominant mode, and a monopole form factor with a phenomenological parameter α (range 0.8–2.2) was used to account for off-shell effects.

## Key numbers

- Mass of Y(4140): M = 4143.0 ± 2.9 (stat) ± 1.2 (syst) MeV/c² (from CDF experiment)
- Decay width of Y(4140): Γ = 11.7 +8.3 −5.0 (stat) ± 3.7 (syst) MeV/c² (from CDF experiment)
- Coupling constant gY for χ″c0 assumption: 2.79 GeV
- Coupling constant gY for χ″c1 assumption: 2.65 GeV
- Branching ratio for Y(4140)→J/ψφ assuming χ″c0: ranges from 1.3×10⁻⁴ (α=0.8) to 4.3×10⁻³ (α=2.2)
- Branching ratio for Y(4140)→J/ψφ assuming χ″c1: ranges from 1.4×10⁻⁴ (α=0.8) to 4.1×10⁻³ (α=2.2)
- ΛQCD = 220 MeV used in form factor definition

## Context

This work extends the author's prior analysis (Liu and Zhu, 2009) which considered various interpretations of Y(4140) and concluded it is probably a Dₛ*D̅ₛ* molecular state. The calculation tests the alternative charmonium interpretation (second radial excitation of P-wave charmonium) against the CDF observation of Y(4140) in J/ψφ. The small predicted branching ratio disfavors the pure charmonium assignment and supports molecular interpretations, such as those studied by Branz, Gutsche, and Lyubovitskij (2009) who calculated strong decays of Y(4140) and Y(3940) as molecular states. Further experimental measurement of Y(4140) decay modes is needed to clarify its nature.
