# The hidden charm decay of Y(4140) by the rescattering mechanism

> Assuming that Y(4140) is the second radial excitation of the P-wave charmonium $ПҮ_{cJ}^{\prime\prime}$ ($J=0, 1$), the hidden charm decay mode of Y(4140) is calculated in terms of the rescattering mechanism. Our numerical results show that the upper limit of the branching ratio of the hidden charm decay $Y(4140)\to J/ПҲП•$ is on the order of $10^{-4}\sim 10^{-3}$ for both of the charmonium assumptions for Y(4140), which disfavors the large hidden charm decay pattern indicated by the CDF experiment. It seems to reveal that the pure second radial excitation of the P-wave charmonium $ПҮ_{cJ}^{\prime\prime}$ ($J=0, 1$) is problematic.

Effective Lagrangians based on chiral symmetry and heavy quark symmetry were employed, with coupling constants taken from prior literature. The coupling constant gY was extracted by fitting the central value of the total width of Y(4140) (11.7 MeV), approximating the open charm decay as the dominant mode, and a monopole form factor with a phenomenological parameter Оұ (range 0.8вҖ“2.2) was used to account for off-shell effects.

## Context

This work extends the author's prior analysis (Liu and Zhu, 2009) which considered various interpretations of Y(4140) and concluded it is probably a DвӮӣ*DМ…вӮӣ* molecular state. The calculation tests the alternative charmonium interpretation (second radial excitation of P-wave charmonium) against the CDF observation of Y(4140) in J/ПҲПҶ. The small predicted branching ratio disfavors the pure charmonium assignment and supports molecular interpretations, such as those studied by Branz, Gutsche, and Lyubovitskij (2009) who calculated strong decays of Y(4140) and Y(3940) as molecular states. Further experimental measurement of Y(4140) decay modes is needed to clarify its nature.
