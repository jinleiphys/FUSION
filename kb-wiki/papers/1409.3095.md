# Linearized fluid/gravity correspondence: from shear viscosity to all order hydrodynamics

> In ref. \cite{1406.7222}, we reported a construction of all order linearized fluid dynamics with strongly coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ super-Yang-Mills theory as underlying microscopic description. The linearized fluid/gravity correspondence makes it possible to resum all order derivative terms in the fluid stress tensor. Dissipative effects are fully encoded by the shear term and a new one, emerging starting from third order in hydrodynamic derivative expansion. In this work, we provide all computational details omitted in \cite{1406.7222} and present additional results. We derive closed-form linear holographic RG flow-type equations for momenta-dependent transport coefficient functions. Generalized Navier-Stokes equations are shown to emerge from the constraint components of the bulk Einstein equations. We perturbatively solve the RG equations for the viscosity functions, up to third order in derivative expansion, and up to this order compute spectrum of small fluctuations. Finally, we solve the RG equations numerically, thus accounting for all order derivative terms in the boundary stress tensor.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a linearized all-order resummation of derivative terms in the fluid stress tensor for strongly coupled N=4 super-Yang-Mills theory via the fluid/gravity correspondence, showing that dissipative effects are fully encoded by the shear viscosity function and a new viscosity function that emerges starting at third order in the hydrodynamic derivative expansion.

## Method

The authors linearize the fluid/gravity correspondence by expanding the fluid velocity and temperature parameters to first order in a small perturbation parameter, then solve the bulk Einstein equations in asymptotically AdS5 spacetime for the metric corrections. The dynamical components of the Einstein equations are converted into holographic renormalization group flow-type equations for momentum-dependent transport coefficient functions, while the constraint components yield generalized Navier-Stokes equations. The viscosity functions are obtained both perturbatively up to third order in derivative expansion and numerically to all orders.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: eta_0 / s = 1/(4 pi), universal for Einstein gravities in asymptotically AdS spacetimes
- Perturbative expansion of shear viscosity function: eta(omega,q^2) = 2 + (2 - ln 2) i omega - (1/4) q^2 - (1/24)[6 pi - pi^2 + 12(2 - 3 ln 2 + ln^2 2)] omega^2 + ... (in units where pi T = 1)
- Perturbative expansion of new viscosity function: zeta(omega,q^2) = (1/12)(5 - pi - 2 ln 2) + ... (in units where pi T = 1)
- First term in eta corresponds to the universal ratio eta_0/s = 1/(4 pi); second term accounts for relaxation time

## Context

This work extends the nonlinear fluid/gravity correspondence of Bhattacharyya et al. (2008) to all orders in derivatives by linearizing the problem, and corrects the earlier attempt by Baier et al. (2009) which incorrectly argued that the new viscosity function zeta vanishes. The paper provides complete computational details omitted in the authors' prior report Bu and Lublinsky (2014), and enables computation of dispersion relations for sound and shear modes to all orders, as well as numerical extraction of transport coefficient functions at arbitrarily large momenta.
