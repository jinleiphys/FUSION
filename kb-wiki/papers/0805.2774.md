# Shell States of Neutron Rich Matter

> The equation of state (EOS) for nuclear and neutron rich matter is investigated in a Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model. New shell states are found that minimize the free energy per baryon, calculated in a spherical Wigner-Seitz (WS) approximation, over a significant range of baryon densities. These shell states, that have both inside and outside surfaces, minimize the Coulomb energy of large proton number configurations at the expense of a larger surface energy. This is related to a possible depression in the central density of super heavy nuclei. As the baryon density increases, we find the system changes from normal nuclei, to shell states, and then to uniform matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that new "shell states", spherical density distributions with both inside and outside surfaces, minimize the free energy per baryon over a significant range of baryon densities in neutron-rich matter, but only within the spherical Wigner-Seitz approximation and at a low temperature of T = 1 MeV, with the caveat that non-spherical "pasta" configurations might be even more stable.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent relativistic mean field (RMF) Hartree calculations using the NL3 effective interaction, solving the Dirac equation for nucleons and Klein-Gordon equations for meson fields inside a spherical Wigner-Seitz (WS) cell. They searched over the WS cell radius to minimize the free energy per baryon (including exact lattice Coulomb energy for a BCC lattice) for given baryon density, proton fraction, and temperature T = 1 MeV, treating electrons as a uniform Fermi gas. They also performed stability analyses of uniform matter using both a Vlasov formalism and finite-temperature RPA to determine the critical density for the onset of non-uniformity.

- Transition density from normal nucleus state to shell state at Y_p = 0.4: 0.049 fm^{-3}
- Transition density from shell state to uniform matter at Y_p = 0.4: 0.090 fm^{-3}
- Transition density from nucleus state to shell state at Y_p = 0.5: 0.053 fm^{-3}
- Transition density from shell state to uniform matter at Y_p = 0.5: 0.090 fm^{-3}
- Transition density from nucleus state to shell state at Y_p = 0.3: 0.043 fm^{-3}
- Transition density from shell state to uniform matter at Y_p = 0.3: 0.084 fm^{-3}
- Transition density from nucleus state to shell state at Y_p = 0.2: 0.044 fm^{-3}
- Transition density from shell state to uniform matter at Y_p = 0.2: 0.071 fm^{-3}
- For a schematic model at rho_B = 0.071 fm^{-3} and Y_p = 0.5: the nucleus state has radius 12.52 fm with A=584, Z=292; the shell state has radius 20.08 fm with A=2408, Z=1204
- At rho_B = 0.080 fm^{-3}, Y_p = 0.4, and R_c = 23.5 fm: there are 4892 nucleons inside one WS cell, requiring 427 neutron levels and 419 proton levels

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of nonuniform matter at sub-nuclear density by Negele and Vautherin (using Skyrme Hartree-Fock) and by Lattimer and Swesty (using a compressible liquid-drop model) and by H. Shen et al. (using Thomas-Fermi approximations to RMF), none of which accounted for shell structure or explored the full range of density distributions possible in the spherical WS approximation. The new shell states may be related to the appearance of central density depressions in super heavy nuclei and could significantly change transport properties such as shear viscosity and shear modulus of neutron-rich matter; they may also be connected to the non-spherical "pasta" phases relevant for neutron star crust structure and supernova dynamics.
