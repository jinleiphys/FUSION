# Pairing and the Cooling of Neutron Stars

> In this review, I present a brief summary of the impact of nucleon pairing at supra-nuclear densities on the cooling of neutron stars. I also describe how the recent observation of the cooling of the neutron star in the supernova remnant Cassiopeia A may provide us with the first direct evidence for the occurrence of such pairing. It also implies a size of the neutron 3P-F2 energy gap of the order of 0.1 MeV.

## Key claim

The observation of the rapid cooling of the neutron star in Cassiopeia A may provide the first direct evidence for nucleon pairing at supra-nuclear densities, and implies a neutron 3P-F2 energy gap of the order of 0.1 MeV, but only if the observed cooling is interpreted as the onset of a pairing phase transition occurring around age 330 years.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical framework of neutron star cooling, including the specific heat, photon emission from the envelope, and neutrino emission processes (modified Urca, bremsstrahlung, Cooper pair breaking and formation, and direct Urca cycles). The effects of nucleon pairing are incorporated through control functions that suppress specific heat and neutrino emissivity when the temperature falls below the critical temperature Tc, and the author compares minimal cooling models (excluding all fast neutrino processes) against observational data for isolated cooling neutron stars, with particular focus on the Cassiopeia A neutron star.

## Key numbers

- Neutron star radii are of order 10 km and masses between 1 and 2 solar masses.
- The specific heat of the core can be approximated as C_V ≈ 10^39 × T_9 erg K^{-1}, where T_9 = T/10^9 K.
- The photon luminosity follows L_γ ≈ 10^35 × T_9^2 erg s^{-1}, with effective temperature T_e ≈ 10^6 × (T/10^8 K)^{1/2} K.
- Modified Urca neutrino emissivity is ~2×10^21 × R × T_9^8 erg cm^{-3} s^{-1} (neutron branch) and ~10^21 × R × T_9^8 erg cm^{-3} s^{-1} (proton branch).
- Direct Urca emissivity is ~10^27 × R × T_9^6 erg cm^{-3} s^{-1} for nucleons, hyperons, and quarks.
- Cooper pair formation emissivity is ~5×10^21 × R × T_9^7 erg cm^{-3} s^{-1} for neutrons and ~5×10^19 × R × T_9^7 erg cm^{-3} s^{-1} for protons.
- The critical temperature Tc and zero-temperature energy gap are related by k_B T_c ≈ 0.57 × Δ̄(k_F; T=0).
- The Cassiopeia A neutron star cooled by 4% between 2000 and 2009 (20% decrease in flux), with thermal luminosity L_γ ≈ 10^34 erg s^{-1} and age 330 years.
- The observed cooling slope is s = -d log T_e / d log t ≈ 1.2.
- The deduced maximum critical temperature for the neutron 3P-F2 gap is T_cn^max ≈ 0.5×10^9 K.

## Context

This review extends the "Minimal Cooling" paradigm developed by Page (2004) and Page (2009), which excludes all fast neutrino emission processes and considers only modified Urca, bremsstrahlung, and Cooper pair formation processes. The work contradicts earlier theoretical predictions of the neutron 3P-F2 gap that span three orders of magnitude, as shown by calculations from Hoffberg (1970), Takatsuka (1972), Amundsen (1985), Baldo (1998), Schwenk and Friman (2004), and Zuo (2008). The interpretation of the Cassiopeia A cooling observation, proposed simultaneously by Page (2011) and Shternin (2011), enables future measurements of the neutron pairing gap by fitting theoretical cooling models to observational data, and may also constrain proton superconductivity and neutron star mass.
