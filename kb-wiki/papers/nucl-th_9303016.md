# Nuclear effects in Deep Inelastic Scattering of polarized electrons off polarized 3He and the neutron spin structure functions

> It is shown that the nuclear effects playing a relevant role in Deep Inelastic Scattering of polarized electrons by polarized $^3$He are mainly those arising from the effective proton and neutron polarizations generated by the $S'$ and $D$ waves in $^3$He. A simple and reliable equation relating the neutron, $g_1^n$, and $^3$He, $g_1^3$, spin structure functions is proposed. It is shown that the measurement of the first moment of the $^3$He structure function can provide a significant check of the Bjorken Sum Rule.

# Nuclear effects in Deep Inelastic Scattering of polarized electrons off polarized 3He and the neutron spin structure functions

## Key claim

The nuclear effects that significantly affect Deep Inelastic Scattering of polarized electrons by polarized 3He are primarily those arising from the effective proton and neutron polarizations generated by the S' and D wave components of the 3He wave function, while Fermi motion and binding corrections contribute at most a few percent. The authors propose that the neutron spin structure function g1^n can be reliably extracted from the measured 3He asymmetry using a simple equation that accounts for these effective polarizations, with total theoretical errors well below previously assumed values.

## Method

The authors used a spin-dependent spectral function of 3He, obtained from realistic three-body wave function calculations, to compute the nuclear spin structure function g1^3 and the longitudinal asymmetry A_|| within a convolution approach that simultaneously accounts for Fermi motion and binding corrections. They compared results from three models of increasing complexity: (1) no nuclear effects (pure S wave, no Fermi motion or binding), (2) effective nucleon polarizations from S' and D waves only, and (3) the full convolution approach including Fermi motion and binding. The nucleon spin structure functions used in the convolution were taken from the Carlitz-Kaur model extended by Schafer (1988) to include spin dilution from gluon polarization.

## Key numbers

- Effective neutron polarization in 3He: p_n = 0.86 ± 0.02
- Effective proton polarization in 3He: p_p = -0.028 ± 0.004
- Wave function parameters: Δ = 0.07 ± 0.01 and Δ' = 0.014 ± 0.002, corresponding to the S' and D wave probabilities
- Fermi motion and binding corrections to the extracted neutron structure function are at most 4% for x ≤ 0.7
- The quantity Λ_n/M ≈ 0.72 × 10^(-3) and Λ_p/M ≈ 0.25 × 10^(-3), where Λ_N represents the difference in average removal and recoil energies between spin-up and spin-down states
- First moment of the 3He structure function: Γ_3 = -0.180 + 0.804 Γ_p; using the EMC result Γ_p = 0.126 gives Γ_3 = -0.079 ± 0.003
- The value of the proton polarization used in the earlier work of Woloshyn (1985) was p_p = -0.023, compared to the authors' value p_p = -0.030

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Woloshyn (1985) that considered only Fermi motion effects, by using a spin-dependent spectral function that simultaneously accounts for Fermi motion and binding corrections. The authors also compare two different convolution prescriptions: one from Blankleider and Woloshyn (1984) and one from Schultze and Sauer (unpublished), showing they converge to the same result in the Bjorken limit. The paper enables the extraction of the neutron spin structure function g1^n from planned experiments at SLAC (E-142), CERN (SMC), and HERMES, and shows that the first moment of the 3He structure function can provide a significant check of the Bjorken Sum Rule.
