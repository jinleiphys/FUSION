# Chi_2 production in polarized pp collisions at RHIC: measuring ΔG and testing the color octet model

> We consider the production and decay of the $χ_2$ charmonium state in polarized and unpolarized $pp$ collisions at RHIC in the framework of an effective theory based on the QCD multipole expansion. We find that the angular distribution in the decay of the produced charmonium, $χ_2 \to J/ψ+ γ$, in the unpolarized case allows us to distinguish clearly between the color singlet and color octet production mechanisms. Once the production mechanism is known, the angular distribution in the polarized case can be used to measure the polarized gluon distribution in the proton, $ΔG(x)$.

## Key claim

The angular distribution of the photon in the decay \(\chi_2 \to J/\psi + \gamma\) from unpolarized \(pp\) collisions can distinguish between color singlet and color octet production mechanisms; once the mechanism is known, the same angular distribution in polarized collisions can measure the polarized gluon distribution \(\Delta G(x)\), provided the \(\chi_2\) is produced at low transverse momentum and the gluon-gluon fusion channel dominates.

## Method

The authors used an effective theory based on the QCD multipole expansion to compute helicity amplitudes for \(\chi_2\) production via gluon-gluon fusion in both color singlet (direct) and color octet (two-step, via a \(1^{--}\) color octet \(c\bar c\) state decaying by electric dipole gluon emission) mechanisms. They derived the angular distribution of the decay \(\chi_2 \to J/\psi + \gamma\) for each helicity state of the \(\chi_2\) and combined these with the helicity selection rules for the two production mechanisms to obtain expressions for the unpolarized cross section and the spin asymmetry in polarized \(pp\) collisions.

## Key numbers

* The angular distribution for \(\chi_2\) decay when produced in helicity \(\pm 2\) (color singlet mechanism) is proportional to \(\frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{2} \cos^2\theta\).
* The angular distribution for \(\chi_2\) decay when produced in helicity \(\pm 1\) (color octet mechanism) is proportional to \(\frac{3}{4} - \frac{1}{4} \cos^2\theta\).
* The angular distribution for \(\chi_2\) decay when produced in helicity \(0\) is proportional to \(\frac{5}{6} - \frac{1}{2} \cos^2\theta\).
* The spin asymmetry formula (equation labeled "ang") depends on the ratio of the color octet to color singlet amplitudes \(\mathcal{A}^8 / \mathcal{A}^1\) and the ratio of polarized to unpolarized gluon distributions \(\Delta g(x, M_\chi^2) / g(x, M_\chi^2)\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of charmonium production in polarized collisions by Ioffe (1977), Cortes and Pire (1988), and others, and directly addresses the failure of the color singlet model to explain Tevatron data from CDF and D0 (1992-1997). The proposed method enables a clean experimental separation of production mechanisms at RHIC, which is a prerequisite for extracting \(\Delta G(x)\) from spin asymmetries and for reducing uncertainties in charmonium production in nuclear collisions.
