# Kaon production in heavy ion reactions at intermediate energies

> The article reviews the physics related to kaon and antikaon production in heavy ion reactions at intermediate energies. Chiral dynamics predicts substantial modifications of the kaon properties in a dense nuclear environment. The status of the theoretical predictions as well as experimental evidences for medium effects such as repulsive/attractive mass shifts for $K^+/K^-$ are reviewed. In the vicinity of the thresholds, and even more pronounced below threshold, the production of strangeness is a highly collective process. Starting from elementary reaction channels the phenomenology of $K^+$ and $K^-$ production, i.e. freeze-out densities, time scales etc. as derived from experiment and theoretical transport calculations is presented. Below threshold kaon production shows a high sensitivity on the nuclear compression reached in heavy ion reactions. This allows to put constraints on the nuclear equation-of-state which are finally discussed.

## Key claim

Chiral dynamics predicts substantial modifications of kaon and antikaon properties in dense nuclear matter, with the effective chiral Lagrangian approach predicting a repulsive/attractive mass shift for K+/K- respectively, though the magnitude of these shifts depends on the uncertain kaon-nucleon sigma term ΣKN which can range from 2mπ up to 450 MeV, with a tendency around 400 MeV.

## Method

The paper reviews the application of chiral SU(3)L × SU(3)R Lagrangian to kaon properties in nuclear matter, starting from the Kaplan-Nelson approach (1986) and extending through coupled-channel treatments by multiple authors. The effective chiral Lagrangian is reduced to a kaon-nucleon Lagrangian containing a vector Weinberg-Tomozawa term (repulsive for K+, attractive for K-) and a scalar Kaplan-Nelson term (attractive for both), which is then applied in mean field approximation to derive the in-medium Klein-Gordon equation and quasi-particle dispersion relations for kaons in nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Kaon-nucleon sigma term ΣKN ≈ 400 MeV from kaon-nucleon scattering, with lattice QCD predicting 300-450 MeV (Brown and Rho, 1996; Dong, Lagae, Liu, 1996; Borasoy, Lewis, Ouimet, 2002)
- Heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory gives ΣKN = 380 ± 40 MeV (I=1) (Borasoy, 1999)
- Chiral quark model calculations give ΣKN = 386 MeV (Inoue, Lyubovitskij, Gutsche, Faessler, 2004)
- Weak pion decay constant fπ ≈ 93 MeV
- Up and down quark masses mu ≈ md ≡ mq ≈ 5.5 MeV; strange quark mass ms ≈ 150 MeV
- Chiral symmetry breaking scale Λχ ≈ 4πfπ ~ 1 GeV
- Scalar quark condensate ⟨q̄q⟩ ≈ ((230 ± 25) MeV)³

## Context

This review extends the foundational work of Kaplan and Nelson (1986) who first applied the chiral Lagrangian to kaon properties in nuclear matter, and builds upon subsequent developments by Brown and Rho (1996), Weise and collaborators (Kaiser, Siegel, Weise, 1995; Waas, Kaiser, Weise, 1996), and Lutz and collaborators (Lutz, Steiner, Weise, 1994; Lutz, 1998; Lutz, Korpa, 2002). The work enables constraints on the nuclear equation-of-state from sub-threshold kaon production in heavy ion collisions, and connects to neutron star physics through the work of Brown, Lee, Rho, Thorsson (1994) and Thorsson, Prakash, Lattimer (1994).
