# Precision evaluation of the $η$- and $η'$-pole contributions to hadronic light-by-light scattering in the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon

> Next to the $π^0$ pole, $η$ and $η'$ intermediate states give rise to the leading singularities of the hadronic light-by-light tensor, resulting in sizable contributions to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon $a_μ$. The strength of the poles is determined by the respective transition form factors (TFFs) to two (virtual) photons. We present a calculation of these TFFs that implements a number of low- and high-energy constraints, including the $η^{(\prime)}\toγγ$ decay widths, $η^{(\prime)}\toπ^+π^-γ$ spectra, chiral symmetry for the $η^{(\prime)}\to 2(π^+π^-)$ amplitudes, vector-meson couplings, and asymptotic limits. Crucially, we investigate the role of the leading left-hand singularity generated by the exchange of the $a_2$ tensor meson, yielding, for the first time, an estimate of the associated factorization-breaking corrections. Our final results, $a_μ^{η\text{-pole}}=14.7(9)\times 10^{-11}$ and $a_μ^{η'\text{-pole}}=13.5(7)\times 10^{-11}$, conclude a dedicated effort to evaluate the pseudoscalar-pole contributions to hadronic light-by-light scattering using dispersion relations, amounting to a combined $a_μ^{\text{PS-poles}}=91.2^{+2.9}_{-2.4}\times 10^{-11}$.

## Key claim

The authors present the first full dispersive evaluation of the η- and η'-pole contributions to hadronic light-by-light scattering in the muon g-2, including for the first time an estimate of factorization-breaking corrections from a2 tensor meson exchange, and find results consistent with previous analyses but significantly more precise.

## Method

The η and η' transition form factors (TFFs) are decomposed into isovector, isoscalar, effective-pole, and asymptotic components. The isovector piece is reconstructed dispersively from η(′) → π+π-γ decay spectra, using a hidden-local-symmetry ansatz for the η(′) → 2(π+π-) amplitudes supplemented by a2(1320) exchange, with unitarization via an inhomogeneous Omnès problem solved through complex path deformations. The isoscalar component is parameterized by ω and φ vector-meson dominance, the effective-pole term restores normalization and fits high-energy space-like data, and the asymptotic piece implements perturbative QCD constraints including finite-mass corrections.

## Key numbers

- aμη-pole = 14.7(9) × 10-11 (total uncertainty in parentheses)
- aμη'-pole = 13.5(7) × 10-11
- Combined pseudoscalar-pole contribution aμPS-poles = 91.2+2.9-2.4 × 10-11
- η slope parameter bη = 1.833(41) GeV-2 (total uncertainty)
- η' slope parameter bη' = 1.493(32) GeV-2 (total uncertainty)
- Effective-pole mass for η: Meff between (1.3-2.2) GeV
- Effective-pole mass for η': Meff around 1.4 GeV
- Asymptotic threshold parameter sm = 1.4(4) GeV2 for η and sm = 1.5(3) GeV2 for η'

## Context

This work completes a dedicated dispersive analysis of all three light pseudoscalar-pole contributions (π0, η, η') to hadronic light-by-light scattering, extending the previous dispersive treatment of the π0 TFF by Hoferichter et al. (2018). The results supersede earlier evaluations based on Canterbury approximants (Masjuan 2017) and provide systematically improvable uncertainties that are now smaller than those from the π0 singly-virtual TFF tension between Belle (2012) and BaBar (2009). The analysis enables a complete dispersive evaluation of hadronic light-by-light scattering and achieves precision below the Fermilab experimental goal of 0.14 ppm.
