# Electron and neutrino scattering in the $Δ$-resonance region and beyond

> We present a unified relativistic approach to inclusive electron scattering based on the relativistic Fermi gas model and on a phenomenological extension of it which accounts for the superscaling behaviour of $(e,e')$ data. We present results in the $Δ$ resonance region and in the highly inelastic domain and show some application to neutrino scattering.

## Key claim

The paper presents a unified relativistic approach to inclusive electron scattering that uses the Relativistic Fermi Gas model and a phenomenological extension based on superscaling; the key hedge is that the model's success depends on the assumption that the superscaling function extracted from quasielastic data can be applied universally across all kinematical domains, including the Delta resonance region and highly inelastic region, with the caveat that additional contributions from two-nucleon emission and meson-exchange currents are needed to fully match experimental data.

## Method

The authors extended the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) model from the quasielastic peak to the inelastic region by introducing a scaling variable psi* for each resonance mass m* and convolving the stable-resonance hadronic tensor with a Breit-Wigner distribution to account for the Delta decay width. For the highly inelastic region, they parameterized the single-nucleon inelastic tensor using phenomenological fits of deep inelastic scattering structure functions (the Bodek et al. fit). They then replaced the RFG scaling function with a phenomenological quasielastic scaling function f_QE extracted from experimental data (Jourdan 1996) to create the Extended Relativistic Fermi Gas (ERFG) model, and applied the resulting SuSA (SUperScaling Approximation) approach to predict neutrino-nucleus cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The RFG scaling function is f_RFG(psi*) = (3/4) * Theta(1 - psi*^2) * (1 - psi*^2), defining a parabolic shape between psi* = -1 and psi* = 1
- For the Delta resonance, the mass m_Delta = 1232 MeV is used, with the scaling variable psi* defined via rho(m*) = 1 + (1/(4*tau)) * (m*^2/m_N^2 - 1)
- The calculation for carbon-12 at incident energy E_inc = 4.045 GeV and scattering angles theta_e = 15, 30, 45, and 74 degrees shows the ERFG model underestimates the quasielastic peak height while better reproducing the tails
- An energy shift omega_shift = 20 MeV is applied to reproduce the quasielastic peak position
- For neutrino scattering at E_nu = 1 GeV and theta = 45 degrees, the SuSA model predicts significantly lower cross sections than the RFG model in both the quasielastic and Delta regions

## Context

This work extends the superscaling analysis of Donnelly (1998) from the quasielastic peak to the Delta resonance region and beyond, building on earlier RFG calculations by Alberico (1988) for quasielastic scattering and by Barbaro (2003) for inelastic processes. The approach enables model-independent predictions for neutrino-nucleus cross sections needed for oscillation experiments such as MiniBooNE and K2K/T2K, and complements more fundamental approaches based on relativistic mean field theory (Caballero 2005, Amaro 2006) and the coherent density fluctuation model (Antonov 2007).
