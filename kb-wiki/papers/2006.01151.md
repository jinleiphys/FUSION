# Dynamical Evidence For a Fifth Force Explanation of the ATOMKI Nuclear Anomalies

> Recent anomalies in $^8$Be and $^4$He nuclear decays can be explained by postulating a fifth force mediated by a new boson $X$. The distributions of both transitions are consistent with the same $X$ mass, 17 MeV, providing kinematic evidence for a single new particle explanation. In this work, we examine whether the new results also provide dynamical evidence for a new particle explanation, that is, whether the observed decay rates of both anomalies can be described by a single hypothesis for the $X$ boson's interactions. We consider the observed $^8$Be and $^4$He excited nuclei, as well as a $^{12}$C excited nucleus; together these span the possible $J^P$ quantum numbers up to spin 1. For each transition, we determine whether scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, or axial vector $X$ particles can mediate the decay, and we construct the leading operators in a nuclear physics effective field theory that describes them. Assuming parity conservation, the scalar case is excluded and the pseudoscalar case is highly disfavored. Remarkably, however, the protophobic vector gauge boson, first proposed to explain only the $^8$Be anomaly, also explains the $^4$He anomaly within experimental uncertainties. We predict signal rates for other closely related nuclear measurements, which, if confirmed, will provide overwhelming evidence that a fifth force has been discovered.

## Key claim

The paper tests whether the observed decay rates of the $^8$Be and $^4$He anomalies can be described by a single hypothesis for the $X$ boson's interactions, finding that the protophobic vector gauge boson previously proposed for the $^8$Be anomaly also explains the $^4$He anomaly within experimental uncertainties, but only if the $^4$He anomaly is attributed to the $0^+$ excited state rather than the $0^-$ state.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective field theory (EFT) for low-energy nuclear transitions, treating each nuclear energy level as a separate point-like quantum field with operators built from combinations of fields and powers of the nuclear scale. They analyzed the spin-parity conservation laws for the $^8$Be(18.15) $1^+$ state, the $^4$He(20.21) $0^+$ and $^4$He(21.01) $0^-$ states, and the $^{12}$C(17.23) $1^-$ state, determining which scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, or axial vector $X$ particles could mediate each decay. They then calculated the decay rates for the pseudoscalar, axial vector, and vector cases using the EFT operators.

## Key numbers

* $m_X = 17.01 \pm 0.16$ MeV from the refined $^8$Be(18.15) analysis (Krasznahorkay 2018)
* $m_X = 16.98 \pm 0.16$ (stat) $\pm 0.20$ (sys) MeV from the $^4$He(20.49) analysis (Krasznahorkay 2019)
* $\Gamma (^8\text{Be(18.15)} \to {}^8\text{Be} \, X) = (1.2 \pm 0.2) \times 10^{-5}$ eV, assuming $B(X \to e^+ e^-) = 1$
* $\Gamma (^4\text{He}(20.49) \to {}^4\text{He} \, X) = (4.0 \pm 1.2) \times 10^{-5}$ eV, where the E0 decay width $\Gamma( ^4\text{He}(20.21) \to {}^4\text{He} \, e^+ e^-)_{\text{E0}} = (3.3\pm1.0)\times 10^{-4}$ eV (Walcher 1970)
* The $^8$Be(18.15) state has $J^P = 1^+$, total decay width 138 keV, and photon branching fraction $1.4 \times 10^{-5}$
* The $^4$He(20.21) state has $J^P = 0^+$, total decay width 500 keV, and E0 branching fraction $6.6 \times 10^{-10}$
* The $^4$He(21.01) state has $J^P = 0^-$, total decay width 840 keV, and zero photon branching fraction
* The $^{12}$C(17.23) state has $J^P = 1^-$, total decay width 1150 keV, and photon branching fraction $3.8 \times 10^{-5}$

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Feng, Tait, and collaborators (2016) who first proposed the protophobic vector gauge boson explanation for the $^8$Be anomaly observed by Krasznahorkay (2015). The new $^4$He anomaly reported by Krasznahorkay (2019) provides a second signal that allows a non-trivial check beyond kinematic mass consistency. The paper enables future experimental tests by predicting signal rates for other nuclear measurements, including decays of $^{12}$C(17.23) and other closely related transitions, which if confirmed would provide overwhelming evidence for a fifth force.
