# Engineering Weyl nodes in Dirac semimetals by a magnetic field

> We study the phase diagram of a Dirac semimetal in a magnetic field at a nonzero charge density. It is shown that there exists a critical value of the chemical potential at which a first-order phase transition takes place. At subcritical values of the chemical potential the ground state is a gapped state with a dynamically generated Dirac mass and a broken chiral symmetry. The supercritical phase is the normal (gapless) phase with a nontrivial chiral structure: it is a Weyl semimetal with a pair of Weyl nodes for each of the original Dirac points. The nodes are separated by a dynamically induced chiral shift. The direction of the chiral shift coincides with that of the magnetic field and its magnitude is determined by the quasiparticle charge density, the strength of the magnetic field, and the strength of the interaction. The rearrangement of the Fermi surface accompanying this phase transition is described.

## Key claim

A Dirac semimetal in a magnetic field at nonzero charge density necessarily develops a dynamically generated chiral shift parameter **b**, transforming it into a Weyl semimetal with a pair of Weyl nodes for each original Dirac point; this occurs in the normal (gapless) phase when the chemical potential exceeds a critical value, at which a first-order phase transition takes place.

## Method

The authors study a model of a Dirac semimetal with a contact four-fermion interaction in an external magnetic field, using the Schwinger-Dyson (gap) equation for the fermion propagator in the mean-field approximation. They solve the gap equations perturbatively for the normal phase and nonperturbatively for the phase with a dynamically generated Dirac mass, comparing the free energies of the two phases to locate the phase transition.

## Key numbers

- The critical chemical potential for the first-order phase transition is approximately \(\mu_{\rm cr} \simeq m/\sqrt{2}\), where \(m\) is the dynamically generated Dirac mass in the gapped phase.
- The perturbative result for the dynamically generated chiral shift is \(\mathbf{b} = - (g e \mathbf{B} \mu_0) / (8 \pi^2 v_F^2 c)\), where \(g\) is the coupling constant, \(e\) is the elementary charge, \(\mathbf{B}\) is the magnetic field, \(\mu_0\) is the tree-level chemical potential, \(v_F\) is the Fermi velocity, and \(c\) is the speed of light.
- The dynamical Dirac mass in the weak-coupling limit is \(m \simeq (v_F / \sqrt{\pi} l) \exp\left(-8 \pi^2 v_F l^2 / g + (\Lambda l)^2 / 2\right)\), where \(l = \sqrt{c / |eB|}\) is the magnetic length and \(\Lambda\) is the ultraviolet momentum cutoff.
- The perturbative axial current density is \(\langle \mathbf{j}_5 \rangle_0 = - (e \mathbf{B} \mu_0) / (2 \pi^2 v_F c)\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic phenomenon of Fermi surface rearrangement in magnetized matter, previously studied in the context of magnetars and heavy ion collisions by Gorbar (2009) and Gorbar (2011), to condensed matter systems of Dirac and Weyl semimetals. The dynamical generation of the chiral shift parameter is a three-dimensional analog of the Haldane mass in graphene, which plays a role in the quantum Hall effect as studied by Haldane and by Gorbar (2008). The results enable the prediction that applying a magnetic field to a Dirac semimetal with nonzero charge density can be used to engineer a Weyl semimetal, and the authors note a recent experimental observation of such a transition.
