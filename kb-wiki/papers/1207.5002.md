# Self-force via energy-momentum and angular momentum balance equations

> The radiation reaction for a point-like charge coupled to a massive scalar field is considered. The retarded Green's function associated with the Klein-Gordon wave equation has support not only on the future light cone of the emission point (direct part), but extends inside the light cone as well (tail part). Dirac's scheme of decomposition of the retarded electromagnetic field into the "mean of the advanced and retarded field" and the "radiation" field is adapted to theories where Green's function consists of the direct and the tail parts. The Harish-Chandra equation of motion of radiating scalar charge under the influence of an external force is obtained. This equation includes effect of particle's own field. The self force produces a time-changing inertial mass.

## Key claim

The paper adapts Teitelboim's approach of deriving the self-force via energy-momentum and angular momentum balance equations to the case of a point-like scalar charge coupled to a massive scalar field, where the retarded Green's function has support inside the light cone (tail part). The resulting equation of motion is the Harish-Chandra equation, which includes a time-changing inertial mass due to the particle's own field.

## Method

The author considers a point-like scalar charge coupled to a massive scalar field in flat spacetime, governed by the Klein-Gordon wave equation with a mass parameter k₀. The retarded Green's function for the massive scalar field is decomposed into a direct part (support on the future light cone) and a tail part (support inside the light cone). Following Teitelboim's approach, the stress-energy tensor of the scalar field is split into bound and radiative components; the bound part is absorbed into the particle's inertial mass while the radiative part yields the radiation reaction. The energy-momentum and angular momentum balance equations are then used to derive the equation of motion for the radiating scalar charge.

## Key numbers

- The radiated energy-momentum carried by the massless scalar field is equal to one-half of the Larmor rate of radiation: p_dir,R^ν(τ) = (g²/3) ∫_{-∞}^{τ} ds a²(s) u^ν(s)
- The radiated angular momentum for the massless scalar field is M_dir,R^{μν}(τ) = (g²/3) ∫_{-∞}^{τ} ds { a²(s)[z^μ(s)u^ν(s)-z^ν(s)u^μ(s)] + u^μ(s)a^ν(s)-u^ν(s)a^μ(s) }
- The Yukawa potential for a static scalar charge is φ_Yu(x) = g exp(-k₀r)/r, where k₀ is the mass parameter with dimension of reciprocal length
- The Yukawa force between two scalar charges g₁ and g₂ separated by distance r₁₂ is F₁₂ = -g₁g₂ exp(-k₀r₁₂)/r₁₂² (1+k₀r) n₁₂

## Context

This work extends Teitelboim's (1970) approach for deriving the electromagnetic self-force in flat spacetime via energy-momentum balance to the case of a massive scalar field with tail contributions. The paper builds on the Detweiler and Whiting (2002) decomposition scheme for curved spacetime Green's functions, adapting it to flat spacetime with a massive field. The result recovers the Harish-Chandra (1957) equation of motion for a radiating scalar charge, and the analysis of the massive scalar field case provides deeper understanding of the Detweiler-Whiting decomposition scheme.
