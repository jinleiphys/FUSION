# Effect of hyperons on phase coexistence in strange matter

> The study of liquid gas phase transition in fragmentation of nuclei in heavy ion collisions has been extended to the strangeness sector using the statistical model for multifragmentation. Helmholtz's free energy, specific heat and few other thermodynamic observables have been analyzed in order to examine the occurence of phase transition in the strange matter. The bimodal behaviour of the largest cluster formed in fragmentation also strongly indicates coexistence of both the phases. The presence of hyperons strengthens the signals and also shifts the transition temperature to lower values.

# Effect of hyperons on phase coexistence in strange matter

## Key claim
The presence of hyperons strengthens the signals of liquid-gas phase coexistence in finite strange nuclear systems and shifts the transition temperature to lower values, but the effect is observed only within a finite-system context where thermodynamic signatures are suppressed compared to the infinite-matter limit.

## Method
The authors used a three-component canonical thermodynamical model (neutron, proton, and hyperon) to simulate multifragmentation of hypernuclei with baryon number A0=128 and charge Z0=50. They computed the Helmholtz free energy, entropy, specific heat, largest cluster probability distribution, caloric curves, and pressure-volume isotherms for systems with varying hyperon content (H0=0, 2, 4, 8), both with and without Coulomb interaction.

## Key numbers
- Transition temperature for the system with H0=8 hyperons is lower than for the non-strange system (H0=0), with bimodal probability distribution of the largest cluster shown at T=6.065 MeV for all strangeness contents.
- The liquid-drop formula uses surface tension sigma(T)=sigma0{(Tc^2-T^2)/(Tc^2+T^2)}^(5/4) with sigma0=18.0 MeV and Tc=18.0 MeV.
- The entropy shows a discontinuity in the temperature range of 5.5 to 6 MeV, with the effect more pronounced for the strange system.
- The transition temperature decreases monotonically with increasing total strangeness content of the fragmenting system (shown in Fig. 3 for H0 values from 0 to 8).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of liquid-gas phase transition in normal nuclei (Siemens, Chomaz) and hypernuclei (Mallik 2012, Dasgupta hypernuclei 1 and 2, Botvina 1-3) by analyzing thermodynamic potentials and largest cluster distributions rather than just mass distributions. The three-component canonical model was previously developed by Dasgupta and collaborators. The results enable future studies of strangeness effects in neutron star matter and heavy-ion collisions, and confirm that Coulomb interaction suppresses phase transition signals similarly for both strange and non-strange systems, as previously shown for normal nuclei (Qian, Mallik 2013).
