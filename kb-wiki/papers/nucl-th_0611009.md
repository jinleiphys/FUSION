# Interaction of Regular and Chaotic States

> Modelling the chaotic states in terms of the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble of random matrices (GOE), we investigate the interaction of the GOE with regular bound states. The eigenvalues of the latter may or may not be embedded in the GOE spectrum. We derive a generalized form of the Pastur equation for the average Green's function. We use that equation to study the average and the variance of the shift of the regular states, their spreading width, and the deformation of the GOE spectrum non-perturbatively. We compare our results with various perturbative approaches.

## Key claim

The paper derives a generalized Pastur equation for the average Green's function of a system where a small number of regular bound states interact with a large chaotic background modeled by the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE), and shows that perturbative approaches are reliable for non-overlapping spectra when the distance from the semicircle edge exceeds the semicircle radius, but can badly underestimate the shift when the regular state lies just below the semicircle.

## Method

The authors model the chaotic states using the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble of random matrices (GOE) with dimension N, and couple these to a small number M of regular states in P-space via an interaction V. They derive a generalized Pastur equation for the ensemble-averaged Green's function in the limit N >> 1, which reduces to a saddle-point equation of order M+2. For the one-dimensional P-space case (M=1), they solve this equation numerically and compare the results with perturbative expansions carried out to fourth order in the coupling.

## Key numbers

- The GOE spectrum follows Wigner's semicircle law with radius 2λ, where the average density of states is ρ(E) = (N/(πλ))√(1 - (E/(2λ))²) for |E| ≤ 2λ
- For non-overlapping spectra with |x₀| >> 1 (where x₀ = E₀/(2λ)), the average shift of the regular state is -⟨δα⟩ ≈ -Γ↓λ/(2E₀), where Γ↓ = 2πV²ρ(0) is the spreading width
- The variance of the shift for non-overlapping spectra is √var(δα) ≈ (1/√N)(Γ↓λ/(2|E₀|))
- For overlapping spectra, the average shift acquires an imaginary part: -⟨δα⟩ = -Γ↓E₀/(4λ) + i(Γ↓/2)(ρ(x₀)/ρ(0))
- The perturbative shift underestimates the exact result by about 4-6% for states far from the semicircle, but badly underestimates it for states just below the semicircle edge
- Fluctuations reach saturation at approximately N = 10⁴ states in Q-space
- The dimensionless coupling γ = V²/λ² = Γ↓/(2λ) is restricted to physically reasonable values γ ≤ 1/2

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the doorway state mechanism reviewed by Bohigas and Mahaux (early 1970s) and Feshbach (1992), as well as the series of papers by Feshbach and collaborators (1996) and Molinari and collaborators (2004) on interactions between complex states and low-lying shell-model states. The paper provides a comprehensive non-perturbative treatment within random-matrix theory that goes beyond these earlier perturbative approaches, enabling quantitative assessment of when perturbation theory is reliable for systems where a regular state couples to a chaotic background, such as in spherical nuclei near neutron threshold or quantum dots coupled to electron reservoirs.
