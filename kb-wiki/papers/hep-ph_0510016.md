# $η_c$ - glueball Mixing and Resonance X(1835)

> The mixing of $η_c$ and the lowest mass pseudoscalar glueball is estimated within the framework of the instanton liquid model. It is demonstrated that the mixing is large and may explain the difference between the observed mass of the glueball candidate X(1835) and the theoretical prediction of QCD sum rule analysis.

## Key claim

The mixing between the \(\eta_c\) meson and the lowest pseudoscalar glueball, induced by instantons, is large enough to lower the glueball mass from the QCD sum rule prediction of about 2.05–2.2 GeV down to the observed mass of the X(1835) resonance, but the authors note that fine-tuning of parameters is beyond the accuracy of their vacuum-dominance approximation and that additional mixing effects (e.g., with \(\eta_c(2S)\) and \(\eta^\prime\)) have been omitted.

## Method

The mixing matrix element \(\Delta M^2_{\eta_c G_P}/2\) was computed using the instanton liquid model of the QCD vacuum, with the effective instanton-induced gluon-gluon interaction. The calculation employed the vacuum dominance approximation and used the instanton density \(n(\rho)=n_0\delta(\rho-\rho_c)\) with parameters \(n_0\approx 0.5\ \text{fm}^{-4}\) and \(\rho_c\approx 1/3\ \text{fm}\), together with charmonium wavefunction and coupling values from prior work (Schafer 1996, Diakonov 2002). The resulting mixing was then applied to a two-state mixing scheme between bare \(\eta_c\) and bare glueball states to obtain physical masses and the mixing angle.

## Key numbers

- Mixing matrix element: \(\Delta M^2_{\eta_c G_P} \simeq 1.54\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- Bare masses assumed: \(M_{G_P}^0 = 2\ \text{GeV}\), \(M_{\eta_c}^0 = 2.9\ \text{GeV}\).
- Physical masses after mixing: \(M_{G_P} \simeq 1.87\ \text{GeV}\), \(M_{\eta_c} \simeq 2.98\ \text{GeV}\).
- Mixing angle: \(\theta \simeq 17^\circ\).
- Glueball–gluon coupling used: \(f_{G_P} = \langle 0 | \alpha_s G_{\mu\nu}^b \widetilde{G}_{\mu\nu}^b | G_P \rangle \simeq 2.95\ \text{GeV}^3\), consistent with the QCD sum rule result \(2.9 \pm 1.4\ \text{GeV}^3\) from Forkel (2004).
- Charm quark mass: \(m_c = 1.25\ \text{GeV}\); charmonium wavefunction at origin: \(|\Psi(0)| = 0.19\ \text{GeV}^{3/2}\); strong coupling at instanton scale: \(\alpha_s(\rho_c) = 0.52\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier proposal (Kochelev and Min 2005) that the X(1835) resonance observed by the BES Collaboration is the lowest pseudoscalar glueball, by addressing the discrepancy between its mass and the quenched lattice QCD prediction of 2.1–2.5 GeV and QCD sum rule predictions of \(2.05 \pm 0.19\) GeV (Narison 1996) and \(2.2 \pm 0.2\) GeV (Forkel 2004). The instanton-induced \(\eta_c\)-glueball mixing provides a mechanism to lower the glueball mass toward the experimental value, and the large mixing angle (17°) implies that this mixing must be included in future calculations of \(\eta_c\) and glueball decay properties, such as the observed large decay rates to \(p\bar p\) and \(\eta^\prime \pi^+ \pi^-\) final states.
