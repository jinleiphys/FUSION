# Implications of the Super-K atmospheric data for the mixing angles $θ_{13}$ and $θ_{23}$

> A three-neutrino analysis of oscillation data is performed using the recent, more finely binned Super-K oscillation data, together with the CHOOZ, K2K, and MINOS data. The solar parameters, $Δ_{21}$ and $θ_{12}$, are fixed from a recent analysis, and $Δ_{32}$, $θ_{13}$, and $θ_{23}$ are varied. We utilize the full three-neutrino oscillation probability and an exact treatment of the Earth's MSW effect with a castle-wall density. By including terms linear in $θ_{13}$ and $\varepsilon := θ_{23} - π/4$, we find asymmetric errors for these parameters $θ_{13}=-0.07^{+0.18}_{-0.11}$ and $\varepsilon=0.03^{+0.09}_{-0.15}$. For $θ_{13}$, we see that the lower bound is primarily set by the CHOOZ experiment while the upper bound is determined by the low energy $e$-like events in the Super-K atmospheric data. We find that the parameters $θ_{13}$ and $\varepsilon$ are correlated -- the preferred negative value of $θ_{13}$ permits the preferred value of $θ_{23}$ to be in the second octant, and the true value of $θ_{13}$ affects the allowed region for $θ_{23}$.

## Key claim

Using a full three-neutrino oscillation analysis with exact treatment of the Earth's MSW effect, the authors find that the Super-K atmospheric data produce an asymmetric constraint on θ₁₃, with the upper bound set by low-energy e-like events in Super-K rather than by CHOOZ, and that the preferred negative value of θ₁₃ permits θ₂₃ to lie in the second octant.

## Method

The authors performed a three-neutrino analysis of oscillation data using the more finely binned Super-K atmospheric data together with CHOOZ, K2K, and MINOS data. Solar parameters Δ₂₁ and θ₁₂ were fixed from a recent analysis, while Δ₃₂, θ₁₃, and θ₂₃ were varied using the full three-neutrino oscillation probability and an exact treatment of the Earth's MSW effect with a castle-wall density profile (mantle density 4.5 gm/cm³, core density 11.5 gm/cm³, core radius 3486 km). They compared results from the full calculation with those from the sub-dominant approximation that expands in the ratio α = Δ₁₂/Δ₃₂.

## Key numbers

- θ₁₃ = −0.07⁺⁰·¹⁸₋₀.₁₁ (90% confidence level, three-parameter fit with Δχ² = 6.25)
- ε = θ₂₃ − π/4 = 0.03⁺⁰·⁰⁹₋₀.₁₅ (90% confidence level)
- Δ₃₂ = 0.25⁺⁰·⁰²₋₀.₀₃ eV² (90% confidence level)
- The total difference in Δχ² for θ₁₃ = +0.15 versus θ₁₃ = −0.15 is approximately 7.0, with half of this change (3.5) arising from a single angular bin: e-like events with zenith angle −0.8 < cos ϑ < −0.6 and momentum less than 250 MeV
- The MSW resonant energy in the mantle is on the order of 100 MeV, derived from E_R = Δ₂₁ cos 2θ₁₂ / (2V cos²θ₁₃) with V ≈ 1.7 × 10⁻¹³ eV

## Context

This work extends previous three-neutrino analyses by Schwetz (2008) and others by using the full three-neutrino oscillation probability rather than approximate expansions, and by incorporating the more finely binned Super-K data from Abe (2008). The authors show that terms linear in θ₁₃, previously identified by Latimer (2005) as significant at very long baselines, produce an asymmetry in the allowed region for θ₁₃ that is absent in the sub-dominant approximation. The results demonstrate that future reactor experiments (Daya Bay, Double CHOOZ, RENO) measuring θ₁₃² will determine the magnitude but not the sign of θ₁₃, while long-baseline experiments like T2K will contain effects linear in θ₁₃ that can impact the allowed region for θ₂₃.
