# Hard exclusive electroproduction of a pion in the backward region

> We study the scaling regime of pion electroproduction in the backward region, eN->e'N'pi. We compute the leading-twist amplitude in the kinematical region, where it factorises into a short-distance matrix element and long-distance dominated nucleon Distribution Amplitudes and nucleon to pion Transition Distribution Amplitudes. Using the chiral limit of the latter, we obtain a first estimate of the cross section, which may be experimentally studied at JLab or Hermes.

## Key claim

The authors compute the leading-twist amplitude for hard exclusive pion electroproduction in the backward region, where the process factorizes into a short-distance matrix element and long-distance dominated nucleon Distribution Amplitudes and nucleon-to-pion Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs). Using the chiral limit to constrain the TDAs when the final-state pion is soft, they obtain a first estimate of the cross section, but note that this estimate is reliable only in a restricted kinematical domain (large skewedness parameter xi) and that a realistic model for the TDAs at smaller xi is needed before discussing present data.

## Method

The authors derived the soft-pion limit of the proton-to-pion TDAs at vanishing transverse momentum transfer (Delta_T=0) using current algebra and soft-meson theorems, expressing the three leading-twist TDAs (V1, A1, T1) in terms of the corresponding proton Distribution Amplitudes (DAs). They then calculated the hard-scattering amplitude at leading order in alpha_s for the process gamma* P -> P' pi^0, restricting the analysis to the Efremov-Radyushkin-Brodsky-Lepage (ERBL) region where all three quarks struck by the virtual photon have positive light-cone momentum fractions. Using the Chernyak-Zhitnitsky (CZ) parametrization for both the DAs and the limiting TDAs, they evaluated the unpolarized cross section for backward scattering (theta*_pi = pi) at large xi.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant f_pi = 131 MeV is used in the TDA definitions.
- The normalization constant f_N is estimated through QCD sum rules to be of order 5.2 x 10^{-3} GeV^2, based on Chernyak and Zhitnitsky (1984).
- For xi = 0.8 and alpha_s = 0.4, the differential cross section d sigma / d Omega*_pi at theta*_pi = pi is shown as a function of Q^2, with the analytic result scaling as approximately 4.5 x 10^7 (1+xi) / (Q^6 xi) times kinematic factors.
- The cross section using the asymptotic DA choice is three orders of magnitude smaller than the CZ-based result.

## Context

This work extends the concept of Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) to Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs) for nucleon-to-meson transitions, following the framework introduced by the same authors in earlier papers (Lansberg, Pire, Szymanowski, 2005) for backward pion electroproduction and by Frankfurt et al. (1999) for related processes. The soft-pion limit constraints derived here are comparable to those obtained by Braun et al. (2006) for proton-pion distribution amplitudes. The results provide a first estimate for backward pion electroproduction that could be tested at JLab with 12 GeV CEBAF or at Hermes, and the analysis can be extended to related reactions such as ep -> e' n pi+, ep -> e' Delta^0 pi+, and similar processes with neutron targets.
