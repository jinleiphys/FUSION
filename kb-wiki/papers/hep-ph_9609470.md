# Charmonium Suppression in Nuclear Collisions

> Measurements of $ψ$ and $ψ'$ production from experiment NA50 at the CERN SPS are compared to predictions based on a hadronic model of charmonium suppression by Vogt and myself. Data on centrality dependence and total cross sections are in good accord with our predictions. Uncertainties in theoretical quanities such as NA50's $L$ parameter are discussed.

## Key claim

The abrupt decrease in J/psi production observed by NA50 in Pb+Pb collisions is not a threshold effect signaling deconfinement, but rather reflects the saturation of the nuclear path length L as collisions become increasingly central, combined with additional suppression from comover scattering that was already required to describe S+U data.

## Method

The authors compare NA50 data on J/psi and psi' production in Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon to predictions from a hadronic model that includes both nucleon absorption (with cross section sigma_psiN fixed from pA data) and comover scattering (with parameters fixed from S+U data). The survival probability for nucleon absorption is computed using the standard exponential formula with nuclear density profiles, while comover suppression is modeled using a formation time of 2 fm, freezeout time scaling with nuclear radius, and an initial comover density proportional to transverse energy ET. The calculations use the color evaporation model for J/psi production and Drell-Yan for the continuum, with K-factors fixed from pp data.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon absorption cross section sigma_psiN = 4.8 mb, fixed from pA data in Ref. [gstv]
- Comover formation time tau_0 = 2 fm, relative velocity v_rel = 0.6, initial density n_0 = 0.8 fm^{-3} for central S+U
- Nuclear radius R_Pb = 6.6 fm, R_S = 3.6 fm; the logarithm in the comover survival probability increases by approximately 60% in Pb+Pb relative to S+U
- Total cross section (sigma/AB)_psi = 0.95 nb for S+U at 200 GeV and 0.54 nb for Pb+Pb at 158 GeV in the NA50 acceptance (0.4 > x_F > 0, -0.5 < cos(theta) < 0.5); experimental values are 1.03 +/- 0.04 +/- 0.10 nb for S+U and 0.44 +/- 0.005 +/- 0.032 nb for Pb+Pb
- Drell-Yan cross section (sigma/AB)_DY = 37.2 pb calculated versus NA50 finding of 32.8 +/- 0.9 +/- 2.3 pb
- For the psi'/psi ratio, B*sigma_psi'^NN / B*sigma_psi^NN = 0.02 from pp data; the singlet model gives sigma_psi' ~ 2.1 * sigma_psi for interactions with nucleons or comovers

## Context

This work extends the hadronic comover model developed by Gavin and Vogt (1995) and Gavin et al. (1995) to the new NA50 Pb+Pb data, demonstrating that no new physics beyond nucleon absorption plus comover scattering is required. The paper contradicts the interpretation by Blaizot and Ollitrault (1996) that the NA50 data show a threshold effect signaling quark-gluon plasma formation. The results enable future work on cascade calculations that can avoid the simplifying assumptions (such as n_0 proportional to E_T) used in this analytic model, and suggest that SPS inverse-kinematics experiments and AGS pA studies near the psi threshold can help distinguish between competing models.
