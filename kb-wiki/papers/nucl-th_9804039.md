# Hamiltonian Flow Equations for the Lipkin Model

> We derive Hamiltonian flow equations giving the evolution of the Lipkin Hamiltonian to a diagonal form using continuous unitary transformations. To close the system of flow equations, we present two different schemes. First we linearize an operator with three pairs of creation and destruction operators by reducing it to the $z$ component of the quasi spin. We obtain the well known RPA-result in the limit of large particle number. In the second scheme we introduce a new operator which improves the resulting spectrum considerably especially for few particles.

# Hamiltonian Flow Equations for the Lipkin Model

## Key claim
The Hamiltonian flow equation method, using continuous unitary transformations, can approximately diagonalize the Lipkin model, but the simple linearization scheme only reproduces the RPA result in the limit of large particle number; a second scheme adding a J_z^3 operator is necessary to improve the spectrum for few particles (N = 4, 6, 8, ... 20).

## Method
The authors start with the Lipkin Hamiltonian expressed in quasi-spin operators J_z, J_+, and J_- and introduce a fictitious flow parameter ℓ. They define an anti-hermitian generator η = [H_D, H] and derive first-order differential flow equations dH/dℓ = [η, H]. In the first scheme, they linearize the operator J_z^3 that appears on the right-hand side by replacing it with 3⟨J_z⟩² J_z, closing the system for the couplings ξ(ℓ) and V(ℓ). In the second scheme, they add a γ(ℓ) J_z³ term to the Hamiltonian from the start, compute the commutators including this new term, and linearize the resulting operator products to obtain a closed system of three coupled differential equations for ξ(ℓ), V(ℓ), and γ(ℓ).

## Key numbers
- The splitting Δ between ground state and first excited state in the linearized scheme is Δ = ξ(∞) = ξ₀ √(1 − ε̄²), where ε̄ = √(N(N−1)+1) V₀/ξ₀.
- In the large-N limit, the linearized scheme gives the RPA result Δ_RPA = ξ₀ √(1 − ε²) with ε = N V₀/ξ₀.
- The improved scheme with the J_z³ term yields a splitting Δ = ξ(∞) + γ(∞) (3N²/4 − 3N/2 + 1), with ξ(∞) and γ(∞) given by expressions involving the particle number N and the coupling ε.
- Results are shown for interaction strengths ε = 0.4, 0.6, and 0.8 for particle numbers N = 4, 6, and 8.
- The Hartree-Fock ground state remains stable for ε = N V₀/ξ₀ < 1.

## Context
This work extends the Hamiltonian flow equation method developed by Wegner (1994) and by Lenz and Wegner (1996) for condensed matter problems to the Lipkin model, a standard test case in nuclear physics introduced by Lipkin, Meshkov, and Glick (1965). The Lipkin model has recently been studied in the context of finite temperatures by Tsukamoto et al. and as a test of self-consistent RPA-type approximations by Dukelsky et al. The authors show that the flow equation method can interpolate between perturbation theory for small particle numbers and the RPA result for large N, and they note that Mielke has since constructed an η-operator that preserves the simple structure of the Lipkin Hamiltonian without approximations, applicable even beyond the stability of the Hartree-Fock state (ε > 1). The method opens up new perspectives for systematic approximations in many-body nuclear physics problems.
