# Ground and excited states of doubly open-shell nuclei from ab initio valence-space Hamiltonians

> We present ab initio predictions for ground and excited states of doubly open-shell fluorine and neon isotopes based on chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions. We use the in-medium similarity renormalization group, in both flow-equation and Magnus formulations, to derive mass-dependent sd valence-space Hamiltonians. The experimental ground-state energies are reproduced through neutron number N=14, beyond which a new targeted normal-ordering procedure improves agreement with data and large-scale multi-reference calculations. For spectroscopy, we focus on neutron-rich 23-26F and 24-26Ne isotopes near N=14,16 magic numbers. In all cases we find an agreement with experiment competitive with established phenomenology. Moreover, yrast states are well described in 20Ne and 24Mg, providing an ab initio description of deformation in the medium-mass region.

## Key claim
Ab initio valence-space Hamiltonians derived via the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) from chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions can reproduce ground and excited states of doubly open-shell fluorine and neon isotopes, but only when three-nucleon forces are included and a new targeted normal-ordering procedure is used to account for residual three-nucleon forces between valence nucleons beyond neutron number N=14.

## Method
The authors start from chiral N3LO nucleon-nucleon and N2LO three-nucleon interactions, evolve them via free-space SRG to low-momentum scales (lambda_SRG = 1.88-2.11 fm^-1), then use the IM-SRG in both flow-equation and Magnus formulations to decouple an sd valence-space Hamiltonian above an 16O core. The resulting A-dependent valence-space Hamiltonians are diagonalized using the NushellX and Oslo shell-model codes to obtain ground-state energies and natural-parity spectra for fluorine and neon isotopes.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state energies reproduced through neutron number N=14; beyond N=14 the targeted normal-ordering procedure provides 12 MeV additional repulsion at N=20
- Largest deviations from experiment are 600 keV in 29F and 1.3 MeV in 30Ne (0.5% effect)
- Root-mean-squared deviation from 144 experimental levels: 446 keV in fluorine, 388 keV in neon, 791 keV in magnesium for IM-SRG interactions
- For comparison, USDB phenomenological interaction gives 200 keV in fluorine, 268 keV in neon, 106 keV in magnesium
- Spectra show hw dependence at the 100 keV level or less, approaching 400 keV in a few cases
- Magnus and flow-equation formulations agree at the 10 keV level for both core and valence-space decoupling

## Context
This work extends ab initio valence-space methods beyond semi-magic isotopic chains to doubly open-shell nuclei, building on prior IM-SRG valence-space developments by Tsukiyama et al. (2012) and Bogner et al. (2014). It improves upon many-body perturbation theory approaches by Hjorth-Jensen et al. (1995) and Simonis et al. (2015) by using a nonperturbative decoupling that includes three-nucleon forces, and enables future ab initio predictions for electroweak processes such as neutrinoless double-beta decay through the Magnus formulation for effective operators.
