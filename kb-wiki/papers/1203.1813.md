# Signature of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance in neutron spectra from the $K^-+d$ reaction

> Neutron spectra from the reaction $K^-+d\rightarrow π+Σ+n$ were calculated in the energy range $E^{cm}_{K^-}= 0-50\ MeV$ using coupled channel Faddeev equations for the description of the $\bar KNN-πΣN$ three-body system. The aim was to trace the signature of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance in the spectra. We found, that while in the direct spectra kinematic effects mask completely the peak corresponding to the resonance, the deviation spectrum method [1] is able to eliminate kinematics and differentiate between different models of $Λ(1405)$. Four different phenomenological $\bar{K}N-πΣ$ interactions were used in order to study the effect of their pole positions on the neutron spectra.

## Key claim

The inclusive neutron spectra from the reaction \(K^-+d\rightarrow \pi+\Sigma+n\) show no direct peak from the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance due to kinematic masking, but the deviation spectrum method can eliminate these kinematic effects and differentiate between models of \(\Lambda(1405)\) that have different pole structures, provided the resonance is not too close to the \(\bar{K}N\) threshold with a large width.

## Method

The author solved coupled-channel AGS-Faddeev equations for the \(\bar{K}NN-\pi\Sigma N\) three-body system using \(s\)-wave separable isospin-conserving interactions with Yamaguchi-type form factors, restricting the total angular momentum to \(L=0\). Four different phenomenological \(\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma\) interactions (KEK 1-pole, KEK 2-pole, SIDDHARTA 1-pole, SIDDHARTA 2-pole) were used as input, and the inclusive neutron spectra \(P(E_n)\) were calculated for incident kaon energies \(E^{cm}_{K^-}=1, 20, 50\ \mathrm{MeV}\). The deviation spectrum \(P_{DEV}(E_n)=P(E_n)/P^{Born}(E_n)\) was then constructed using a Born-approximation non-resonant background to remove kinematic effects.

## Key numbers

- The calculation covers incident kaon energies in the range \(E^{cm}_{K^-}=0-50\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (corresponding to \(P^{LAB}_K \sim 0-250\ \mathrm{MeV}/c\)).
- Pole positions for the KEK 1-pole potential: \(-23.6-35.6i\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (relative to \(\bar{K}N\) threshold, corresponding to \(1411.0-35.6i\ \mathrm{MeV}\)).
- Pole positions for the SIDDHARTA 1-pole potential: \(-6.4-46.8i\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (relative to \(\bar{K}N\) threshold, corresponding to \(1428.1-46.8i\ \mathrm{MeV}\)).
- Pole positions for the KEK 2-pole potential: first pole at \(-22.2-36.3i\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (\(1412.4-36.3i\ \mathrm{MeV}\)), second pole at \(-58.8-102.5i\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (\(1375.8-102.5i\ \mathrm{MeV}\)).
- Pole positions for the SIDDHARTA 2-pole potential: first pole at \(-14.8-57.2i\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (\(1419.8-57.2i\ \mathrm{MeV}\)), second pole at \(-56.6-101.8i\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (\(1380.0-101.8i\ \mathrm{MeV}\)).
- \(K^-d\) scattering lengths for KEK 1-pole with averaged masses: \(-1.49+0.97i\ \mathrm{fm}\); with physical masses: \(-1.52+0.98i\ \mathrm{fm}\).
- \(K^-d\) scattering lengths for SIDDHARTA 1-pole with averaged masses: \(-1.47+1.22i\ \mathrm{fm}\); with physical masses: \(-1.50+1.23i\ \mathrm{fm}\).
- The width of the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance must be reduced to \(10-15\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for a direct peak to appear in the inclusive neutron spectra, whereas all realistic models give widths of \(\sim 50-100\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier Faddeev calculations for the \(\bar{K}NN\) three-body system, which previously focused on three-body quasi-bound states (Nina1, Sato) and \(K^-d\) scattering lengths (Nina3, Nina4, Fayard), to the break-up reaction \(K^-+d\rightarrow \pi+\Sigma+n\). It contrasts with the single-plus-double-scattering approximation used by Oset (2010) and Haidenbauer (2011), which the author argues is questionable at low kaon energies. The deviation spectrum method was introduced by Esmaili, Akaishi, and Yamazaki (2009) for extracting resonance information from kinematic-dominated spectra. The only available experimental data for comparison are from Tan (1970) from a bubble chamber experiment. The results enable a strategy where experimental deviation spectra could be used to tune \(\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma\) interaction potentials via exact three-body calculations, analogous to how three-body experiments historically constrained nucleon-nucleon potentials.
