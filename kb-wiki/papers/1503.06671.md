# On the Extraction of Cross Sections for pi0 and eta Photoproduction off Neutrons from Deuteron Data

> We discuss the procedure of extracting the photoproduction cross section for neutral pseudoscalar mesons off neutrons from deuteron data. The main statement is that the final-state interaction (FSI) corrections for the proton and neutron target are in general not equal, but for pi0 production there are special cases were they have to be identical and there are large regions in the parameter space of incident photon energy and pion polar angle, θ^*, where they happen to be quite similar. The corrections for both target nucleons are practically identical for $π_0$ production in the energy range of the Delta(1232)3/2+ resonance due to the specific isospin structure of this excitation. Also above the $Δ$-isobar range large differences between proton and neutron correction factors are only predicted for extreme forward angles ($θ^*$ < 20 deg), but the results are similar for larger angles. Numerical results for the gp-->pi0p and gn-->pi0n correction factors are discussed. Also the model description for the available data on the differential gd-->pi0pn cross sections are given.

## Key claim

The final-state interaction (FSI) corrections for extracting photoproduction cross sections off protons and neutrons from deuteron data are in general not equal, but for pi0 production in the Delta(1232)3/2+ resonance region they become identical due to the specific isospin structure of that excitation, and at higher energies large differences between proton and neutron correction factors are only predicted for extreme forward angles (theta* < 20 degrees).

## Method

The authors constructed a model for the gamma d -> pi0 pn reaction amplitude including impulse-approximation (IA) terms and NN-final-state interaction (FSI) diagrams, using elementary gamma N -> pi N amplitudes from the GW pion photoproduction multipoles and s-wave pn-scattering amplitudes with scattering lengths and effective radii. They calculated correction factors R_p and R_n defined as the ratio of the cross section from the leading IA diagram alone to the full cross section, for cases where the fast final-state nucleon is a proton (case 1) or a neutron (case 2), and compared these factors at photon energies of 340, 630, and 787 MeV.

## Key numbers

- At E_gamma = 340 MeV (Delta(1232)3/2+ region), the correction factors R_p and R_n are equal to good accuracy, with values reaching approximately 6 at theta* = 0 degrees.
- At E_gamma = 630 and 787 MeV, R_p differs from R_n at forward angles (theta* < 20 degrees), while at larger angles both factors approach 1.
- The NN-FSI term uses scattering lengths a_0 = 5.4 fm, a_1 = -24 fm, effective radii r_0 = 1.7 fm, r_1 = 2.7 fm, and off-shell parameters beta_0 = beta_1 = 1.2 fm^{-1}.
- The off-shell correction factor for the gamma N -> pi N amplitude uses Lambda = 1 fm^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of FSI corrections for extracting neutron cross sections from deuteron data, following earlier approaches by Fix (2004), Levchuk (2008), and Darwish (2008). The authors show that the common assumption of equal FSI corrections for proton and neutron targets is only valid in the Delta(1232) region, and that at higher energies the corrections differ significantly at forward angles. This enables more accurate extraction of free neutron photoproduction cross sections from deuteron measurements, which is essential for testing isospin symmetry in baryon resonance studies.
