# Probing factorization violation with vector angularities

> Factorization underlies all predictions at the Large Hadron Collider, but has only been rigorously proven in a few cases. One of these cases is the Drell-Yan process, $pp \to Z/γ+ X$, in the limit of small boson transverse momentum. We introduce a one-parameter family of observables, that we call vector angularities, of which the transverse momentum is a special case. This enables the study of factorization violation, with a smooth transition to the limit for which factorization has been established. Like the angularity event shapes, vector angularities are a sum of transverse momenta weighted by rapidity, but crucially this is a vector sum rather than a sum of the magnitude of transverse momenta. We study these observables in Pythia, using the effect of multi-parton interactions (MPI) as a proxy factorization violation, finding a negligible effect in the case where factorization is established but sizable effects away from it. We also present a factorization formula for the cross section, that does not include factorization violating contributions from Glauber gluons, and thus offers a baseline for studying factorization violation experimentally using vector angularities. Our predictions at next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy (NLL$'$) are in good in agreement with Pythia (not including MPI), and can be extended to higher order.

## Key claim

The paper introduces vector angularities, a one-parameter family of observables for Drell-Yan processes, where the transverse momentum of the boson is a special case (a=0). The authors use multi-parton interactions (MPI) in Pythia as a proxy for factorization violation, finding negligible effects at a=0 where factorization is proven, but sizable effects away from this limit.

## Method

The authors define vector angularities as the vector sum of transverse momenta weighted by rapidity, with parameter a controlling the rapidity weighting. They simulate proton-proton collisions at 13 TeV in Pythia for Drell-Yan production of Z/gamma bosons decaying to electron-positron pairs, comparing results with and without MPI and hadronization. They also present a factorization formula in Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) assuming no factorization-violating Glauber contributions, and perform resummation at NLL' accuracy in Fourier space using a b-star prescription to avoid the Landau pole.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy: 13 TeV for Pythia simulations
- Transverse momentum cut for electron/positron selection: k_perp >= 2.5 GeV
- Number of events generated: 100,000
- Parameter a range explored: -0.25 to 1
- Minimum scale for b-star prescription: mu_0 = 0.8 GeV
- Nonperturbative model parameter for comparison: Lambda = 0.35 GeV
- Born cross section formula includes electromagnetic coupling alpha_em, number of colors N_c=3, Z boson mass m_Z and width Gamma_Z

## Context

This work extends the concept of angularity event shapes, previously studied in e+e- collisions by Berger (2003) and in Drell-Yan by Stewart (2009), to a vector sum that enables probing factorization violation. The connection between Glauber gluons and MPI was established by Gaunt (2014), and the use of MPI as a proxy for factorization violation follows the approach of Kang (2018). The paper enables experimental studies of factorization violation at the LHC using vector angularities, with the baseline factorization formula that can be improved to higher orders, noting that for a=0 (transverse momentum) results have been obtained at N4LL accuracy by Neumann (2022), Moos (2023), and Camarda (2023).
