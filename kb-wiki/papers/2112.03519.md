# Systematic study for the surface properties of neutron star

> [Background] In our earlier work {\bf [Phys. Rev. C 104, 055804 (2021)]}, we studied the surface properties of a neutron star, assuming it as a huge finite nucleus containing protons, neutrons, electrons, and muons. For the first time, we reported these results of a neutron star for a few representative masses. In the present paper, we give a detailed study of these quantities to draw definite conclusions. [Method]To carry forward our earlier idea, the energy density functional of the momentum space of neutron star matter is converted to the coordinate space in a local density approximation. This functional is again used to derive the neutron star surface properties within the coherent density fluctuation model using the weight function obtained from the density profile of the neutron star using the recently developed G3 and widely used NL3 and IU-FSU parameter sets in the context of relativistic mean-field formalism. [Results] The systematic surface properties of the neutron star, such as incompressibility, symmetry energy, slope parameter, and curvature coefficient, is calculated. The volume and surface components of the total symmetry energy are decomposed with the help of the $κ$ factor obtained from the volume to surface ratio of the symmetry energies in the liquid drop limit of Danielewicz. The magnitude of the computed surface quantities increases with the neutron star's mass. [Conclusion] The incompressibility $K^{\rm star}$, symmetry energy $S^{\rm star}$, slope parameter $L_{\rm sym}^{\rm star}$ and curvature coefficient $K_{\rm sym}^{\rm star}$ of the neutron stars with different mass are analyzed and found to be model dependent. NL3 is the stiffest equation of state endue us with the higher magnitude of surface quantities as compared to the G3 and IU-FSU forces.

## Key claim

The paper systematically calculates the surface properties of neutron stars (incompressibility, symmetry energy, slope parameter, and curvature coefficient) as functions of stellar mass, finding that these quantities increase with mass and are model-dependent, with the NL3 parameter set yielding the largest values due to its stiffer equation of state.

## Method

The authors convert the relativistic mean-field energy density functional from momentum space to coordinate space using a local density approximation, then apply the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) to derive neutron star surface properties. The weight function in the CDFM is obtained from the density profile of the neutron star, which is computed by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for three parameter sets: NL3, G3, and IU-FSU. The volume and surface components of the total symmetry energy are decomposed using the kappa factor from Danielewicz's liquid drop prescription.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter incompressibility K at saturation: 271.38 MeV (NL3), 243.96 MeV (G3), 231.31 MeV (IU-FSU)
- Nuclear matter symmetry energy J0 at saturation: 37.43 MeV (NL3), 31.84 MeV (G3), 32.71 MeV (IU-FSU)
- Maximum neutron star masses: 2.85 solar masses (NL3), 2.004 solar masses (G3), 1.940 solar masses (IU-FSU)
- Neutron star incompressibility K_star for maximum mass: 44.956 MeV (NL3), compared to nuclear matter K_infinity = 271.38 MeV for the same set
- Symmetry energy S_star at maximum mass: 146.002 MeV (NL3), 66.813 MeV (G3), 60.758 MeV (IU-FSU)
- For the 1.4 solar mass G3 set: total symmetry energy is 8.904 MeV, with the surface component contributing approximately 9% of the total

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Kumar et al., Phys. Rev. C 104, 055804, 2021) which first reported neutron star surface properties for a few representative masses, now providing a systematic analysis across a range of masses from 0.8 solar masses to the maximum mass. The approach builds on the coherent density fluctuation model previously applied to finite nuclei by Antonov (1980) and Gaidarov (2020), and uses the liquid drop prescription of Danielewicz (2003, 2004, 2007) to decompose symmetry energy into volume and surface components. The results enable future connections between neutron star surface properties and observables such as neutron-skin thickness, dipole and quadrupole polarizability, and constraints from gravitational wave events like GW170817.
