# New relativistic effective interaction for finite nuclei, infinite nuclear matter and neutron stars

> We carry out the study for finite nuclei, infinite nuclear matter and neutron star properties with the newly developed relativistic force named as the Institute Of Physics Bhubaneswar-I(IOPB-I). Using this force, we calculate the binding energies, charge radii and neutron skin thickness for some selected nuclei. From the ground state properties of superheavy element i.e. Z=120, it is noticed that considerable shell gaps appear at neutron numbers N=172, 184 and 198, showing the magicity of these numbers. The low density behavior of the equation of state for pure neutron matter is compatible with other microscopic models. Along with the nuclear symmetry energy, its slope and curvature parameters at the saturation density are consistent with those extracted from various experimental data. We calculate the neutron star properties with the equation of state composed of nucleons and leptons in $\it beta-equilibrium$ which are in good agreement with the X-ray observations by Steiner and Nättilä. We find that the maximum mass of the neutron star to be 2.15$M_{\odot}$ and stellar radius 11.936 km . Moreover, the radius and tidal deformability of a {\it canonical} neutron star mass 1.4$M_\odot$ come out to be 13.242 km and 3.910$\times$10$^{36}$ g cm$^2$ s$^2$ respectively within this parameter set.

## Key claim

The newly developed IOPB-I relativistic effective interaction yields a maximum neutron star mass of 2.15 solar masses and a canonical neutron star radius of 13.2 km, with the symmetry energy and its slope and curvature at saturation density consistent with experimental constraints, though the parameter set omits the delta meson and uses a zero value for the eta_1 coupling.

## Method

The authors constructed a new parameter set (IOPB-I) within the effective field theory motivated relativistic mean field (E-RMF) formalism using the simulated annealing method for global chi-squared minimization. They fitted eight parameters to experimental binding energies and charge radii of eight spherical nuclei (O-16, Ca-40, Ca-48, Ni-68, Zr-90, Sn-100, Sn-132, Pb-208) while constraining the effective nucleon mass ratio, nuclear matter incompressibility, and symmetry energy coefficient to empirical ranges. The resulting interaction was then applied to calculate finite nuclei properties, infinite nuclear matter equation of state, and neutron star observables including mass, radius, and tidal deformability.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass: 2.15 solar masses for the IOPB-I parametrization
- Stellar radius at maximum mass: 11.936 km
- Canonical neutron star (1.4 solar masses) radius: 13.2 km (13.242 km in abstract)
- Canonical neutron star tidal deformability: 3.9 x 10^36 g cm^2 s^2 (3.910 x 10^36 g cm^2 s^2 in abstract)
- Chirp mass from GW170817: 1.188 (+0.004/-0.002) solar masses at 90% credible intervals
- Total mass from GW170817 for low-spin priors: 2.74 (+0.04/-0.01) solar masses
- Shell gaps appear at neutron numbers N=172, 184, and 198 for superheavy element Z=120
- Effective nucleon mass ratio M*/M constrained between 0.50 and 0.90 during fitting
- Nuclear matter incompressibility K constrained between 220 and 260 MeV during fitting
- Symmetry energy coefficient J constrained between 28 and 36 MeV during fitting

## Context

This work extends the E-RMF parameter sets such as G1, G2, and G3 from Furnstahl et al. (1997) and the NL3 set from Lalazissis et al. (1997), while incorporating the isoscalar-isovector cross-coupling (Lambda_omega) that modifies the density dependence of symmetry energy without affecting symmetric nuclear matter saturation properties. The results are compared with the FSUGarnet set from Chen and Piekarewicz (2015) and the G3 set, and the neutron star predictions are consistent with X-ray observations by Steiner and Nattila as well as the gravitational wave event GW170817. The new parameter set enables further studies of finite nuclei properties near drip-lines, superheavy element magicity, and neutron star structure including tidal deformability constraints from binary neutron star mergers.
