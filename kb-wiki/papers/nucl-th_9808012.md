# The analysis of particle multiplicities in Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV/c within hadron gas models

> The preliminary data on hadron multiplicities measured in central Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV/c are analyzed. The ideal hadron gas model fails to give a reasonable explanation to the Pb+Pb data sets. We study the possible effects of pion enhancement due to different hard-core repulsion for pions and other hadrons and strangeness suppression because of incomplete chemical equilibrium. Each of these two modifications improves the results. The combined effect of these two mechanisms leads to an extremely good agreement with the data. An interpretation of the obtained results in terms of the possible quark-gluon plasma formation at the early stage of the collision is also discussed.

# The analysis of particle multiplicities in Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV/c within hadron gas models

## Key claim

The ideal hadron gas model fails to explain the Pb+Pb data, but the combined effect of pion enhancement (via different hard-core repulsion radii for pions versus other hadrons) and strangeness suppression (via a chemical non-equilibrium parameter gamma_s < 1) yields extremely good agreement with the measured hadron multiplicities. The authors interpret the resulting strangeness-to-entropy ratio as consistent with quark-gluon plasma formation at the early stage of the collision.

## Method

The authors fit hadron multiplicities in full 4pi geometry and antiparticle/particle ratios in the central rapidity region from central Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV/c using four variants of the hadron gas model: ideal gas, Van der Waals excluded-volume model with a smaller hard-core radius for pions (r_pi = 0, r > 0 for others), strangeness suppression via a gamma_s factor, and a combined model (SS+VDW) incorporating both modifications. The fits minimize chi-squared with three thermodynamic parameters (temperature T, baryonic chemical potential mu_B, volume V) plus one additional parameter per modification (r for VDW, gamma_s for SS).

## Key numbers

- Temperature T = 165 MeV and baryonic chemical potential mu_B = 235 MeV are found to be the same across all four model variants.
- The ideal gas model yields chi^2/dof = 108/6; the VDW model (r = 0.46 fm) gives chi^2/dof = 95.4/5; the SS model (gamma_s = 0.55) gives chi^2/dof = 12.5/5; the combined SS+VDW model (r = 0.45 fm, gamma_s = 0.62) gives chi^2/dof = 3.58/4.
- In the SS+VDW model, the predicted negative hadron multiplicity h^- = 674 agrees with the data value 680 +/- 50; the phi meson multiplicity is 5.42 versus data 5.4 +/- 0.7; K_s^0 is 65.3 versus data 68 +/- 10.
- The strangeness-to-entropy ratio R_s in the SS+VDW model is 0.026, close to the QGP estimate range of 0.022 to 0.025.
- The SS+VDW model predicts total pion multiplicity of 1850, K^+ = 83.1, K^- = 47.6, Lambda = 38.9, and anti-Lambda = 4.43.

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to fit particle ratios in nucleus-nucleus collisions at AGS and SPS energies, where Cleymans, Redlich, Satz, and Suhonen (1993-1994) found that the ideal hadron gas model could not fit all hadron ratios simultaneously. The authors build on the excluded-volume procedure of Rischke, Gorenstein, Stoecker, and Greiner (1991) and the strangeness suppression scheme of Rafelski (1991), as well as the specific VDW model with r_pi = 0 introduced by Ritchie, Gorenstein, and Miller (1997) and Yen, Gorenstein, Greiner, and Yang (1997). The combined SS+VDW fit enables the authors to argue that the resulting strangeness-to-entropy ratio supports the interpretation of quark-gluon plasma formation at the early stage of the collision, consistent with the conclusion of Gazdzicki and Gorenstein (1998).
