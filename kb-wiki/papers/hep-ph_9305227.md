# Thermal quark production in pure glue and quark gluon plasmas

> We calculate production rates for massless $(u,d)$ and massive $(s,c,b)$ quarks in pure glue and quark gluon plasmas to leading order in the strong coupling constant $g$. The leading contribution comes from gluon decay into $q\bar q$ pairs, using a thermal gluon propagator with finite thermal mass and damping rate. The rate behaves as $α_S^2(\ln 1/α_S)^2 T^4$ when $m, α_S \rightarrow 0$ and depends linearly on the transverse gluon damping rate for all values of the quark mass $m$. The light quark ($u$, $d$, $s$) chemical equilibration time is approximately 10-100 $T^{-1}$ for $g=$2-3, so that quarks are likely to remain far from chemical equilibrium in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The leading-order thermal quark production rate in pure glue and quark-gluon plasmas is dominated by the decay of hard transverse gluons with an anomalously large damping rate, yielding a rate proportional to \(\alpha_S^2 (\ln 1/\alpha_S)^2 T^4\) for massless quarks; this result depends linearly on the transverse gluon damping rate, which itself depends on the unknown non-perturbative magnetic mass, introducing an order-of-magnitude uncertainty for large coupling.

## Method

The authors calculated the quark production rate to leading order in the strong coupling constant \(g\) using thermal field theory with the Braaten-Pisarski resummation program. They evaluated the cut diagram for gluon decay into \(q\bar q\) pairs using a resummed gluon propagator that includes both the hard thermal gluon mass and the anomalously large gluon damping rate, integrating over hard gluon momenta of order \(T\). The calculation was performed separately for massless (\(u,d\)) and massive (\(s,c,b\)) quarks, and for both pure glue plasmas (\(N_f=0\)) and quark-gluon plasmas.

## Key numbers

* Massless quark production rate: \(R_T^{\mbox{q}} = \frac{4\zeta(3)}{3\pi^3} \alpha_S^2 \left( \ln\frac{1}{\alpha_S} \right)^2 T^4 + \mathcal{O}\left(\alpha_S^2 \ln\frac{1}{\alpha_S} T^4\right)\)
* Light quark (\(u,d,s\)) chemical equilibration time: approximately 10–100 \(T^{-1}\) for \(g = 2\)–3
* Massless quark chemical equilibration time formula: \(\tau_{\mbox{q}} = \frac{27\pi}{16 \alpha_S^2 (\ln{1/\alpha_S})^2} T^{-1}\)
* Gluon equilibration time: \(\tau_{\mbox{g}} = \frac{1}{3 \alpha_S\ln{1/\alpha_S}} T^{-1}\)
* Plasma frequency: \(\omega_0^2 = \left( N + \frac{N_f}{2} \right) \frac{g^2 T^2}{9}\) for \(SU(N)\) gauge theory
* Transverse gluon damping rate: \(\gamma_T = \frac{\alpha_S N T}{2} \ln\frac{1}{\alpha_S} + \mathcal{O}(\alpha_S T)\)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Geiger (1992) and Shuryak (1992) showing that hot matter in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions may thermalize quickly but remain largely a gluon plasma with few quarks. The calculation provides the first thermal field theory treatment of quark production rates relevant for proposed signals of quark-gluon plasma, such as enhanced strangeness production (Rafelski and Muller, 1982) and thermal charm production (Shor, 1988). The results enable estimates of chemical equilibration times for quarks in heavy-ion collisions and suggest that measuring thermal charm or bottom production could provide a direct measurement of the gluon damping rate and the non-perturbative magnetic mass scale.
