# A New Parameterization for the Lagrangian Density of Relativistic Mean Field Theory

> A new parameterization for an effective non-linear Lagrangian density of relativistic mean field (RMF) theory is proposed, which is able to provide an excellent description not only for the properties of stable nuclei but also for those far from the valley of beta-stability. In addition recently measured superdeformed mimima in the Hg-region are reproduced with high accuracy.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a new parameter set (NL3) for the relativistic mean field Lagrangian that improves upon existing parameterizations (NL1 and NL-SH) by simultaneously providing an excellent description of stable nuclei, nuclei far from beta-stability, and superdeformed minima in the Hg-region, while also reproducing the isoscalar giant monopole resonance energies of Pb and Zr for the first time in RMF theory.

## Method
The authors performed a multi-parameter fit to the ground-state properties (binding energies, charge radii, neutron radii) of ten spherical nuclei, including the doubly magic nucleus 132Sn and the heavy isotope 214Pb, using the standard RMF Lagrangian with nonlinear sigma self-interactions. Nuclear matter properties (saturation density, incompressibility, asymmetry energy) were also included as constraints in the fit, with the nucleon mass fixed to 939 MeV and the rho meson mass fixed to 763 MeV. Pairing correlations for open-shell nuclei were treated in the BCS formalism using gap parameters determined from odd-even mass differences.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear matter saturation density: 0.1483 fm^-3
- Nuclear matter incompressibility K = 271.8 MeV
- Asymmetry energy J = 37.4 MeV
- Effective mass m*/m = 0.6
- Superdeformed minimum in 194Hg found at 5.997 MeV above the ground state, compared to the experimental value of 6 MeV
- Binding energies and charge radii fitted within 0.1% and 0.2% accuracy respectively; neutron radii fitted within 2% experimental error
- Nuclear matter input constraints: E/A = -16.0 MeV (5% error), rho = 0.153 fm^-3 (10% error), K = 250 MeV (10% error), J = 33 MeV (10% error)

## Context
This work extends the RMF parameterizations NL1 (Reinhard 1986) and NL-SH (Sharma 1993), which had deficiencies: NL1 had a large asymmetry energy (44 MeV) and underestimated monopole resonance energies by about 2 MeV, while NL-SH overestimated them by about 2 MeV and failed to reproduce superdeformed minima in Hg isotopes. The new NL3 set enables improved descriptions of exotic nuclei far from stability, superdeformed bands, and collective excitations, and is expected to be used alongside existing parameterizations in future RMF studies.
