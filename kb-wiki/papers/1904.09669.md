# Nuclear matter properties at finite temperatures from effective interactions

> We study if commonly used nucleon-nucleon effective interactions, obtained from fitting the properties of cold nuclear matter and of finite nuclei, can properly describe the hot dense nuclear matter produced in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions. We use two representative effective interactions, i.e., an improved isospin- and momentum-dependent interaction with its isovector part calibrated by the results from the \emph{ab initio} non-perturbative self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) approach with chiral forces, and a Skyme-type interaction fitted to the equation of state of cold nuclear matter from chiral effective many-body perturbation theory and the binding energy of finite nuclei. In the mean-field approximation, we evaluate the equation of state and the single-nucleon potential for nuclear matter at finite temperatures and compare them to those from the SCGF approach. We find that the improved isospin- and momentum-dependent interaction reproduces reasonably well the SCGF results due to its weaker momentum dependence of the mean-field potential than in the Skyrme-type interaction. Our study thus indicates that effective interactions with the correct momentum dependence of the mean-filed potential can properly describe the properties of hot dense nuclear matter and are thus suitable for use in transport models to study heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that effective interactions with a weaker momentum dependence of the mean-field potential, specifically the improved isospin- and momentum-dependent interaction (ImMDI-GF), can reproduce the finite-temperature properties of nuclear matter from microscopic self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) calculations, while the Skyrme-type interaction (Skχm*) with quadratic momentum dependence shows increasing deviations at higher temperatures and momenta.

## Method

The authors used two effective interactions in the non-relativistic mean-field approximation: the ImMDI-GF interaction, with its isovector part calibrated to reproduce zero-temperature pure neutron matter properties from the SCGF approach, and the Skχm* Skyrme interaction fitted to cold nuclear matter from chiral effective many-body perturbation theory and finite nuclei binding energies. They calculated the equation of state, nucleon occupation probabilities, entropy, heat capacity, and single-nucleon potentials for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter at finite temperatures, comparing these results to those from the non-perturbative SCGF approach using three different chiral interactions (2.0/2.0(EM), 2.0/2.5(EM), and NNLOsat).

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ_sat = 0.16 fm^{-3} for ImMDI-GF and 0.1651 fm^{-3} for Skχm*
- Energy per nucleon at saturation E_0(ρ_sat) = -16 MeV for ImMDI-GF and -16.07 MeV for Skχm*
- Incompressibility K_0 = 230 MeV for ImMDI-GF and 230.4 MeV for Skχm*
- Symmetry energy at saturation E_sym(ρ_sat) = 30 MeV for ImMDI-GF and 30.94 MeV for Skχm*
- Slope parameter of symmetry energy L = 40 MeV for ImMDI-GF and 45.6 MeV for Skχm*
- Isoscalar effective mass m_s* = 0.70 m for ImMDI-GF and 0.750 m for Skχm*
- Isovector effective mass m_v* = 0.59 m for ImMDI-GF and 0.694 m for Skχm*
- Mean-field potential at saturation density and infinite nucleon momentum U_0^∞ = 75 MeV for ImMDI-GF
- Nucleon occupation probability and entropy comparisons were made at temperatures of 10, 30, and 50 MeV at ρ_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the comparison of effective interactions to finite temperatures, building on previous zero-temperature calibrations of the ImMDI model (Xu 2015) and the Skχm* interaction (Zhang 2018). The study enables validation of effective interactions for use in transport models of intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions, where hot dense nuclear matter is produced. The results contradict the assumption that effective interactions fitted only to cold nuclear matter and finite nuclei can automatically describe finite-temperature properties, showing that the momentum dependence of the mean-field potential is critical for reproducing microscopic SCGF results (Carbone 2014, 2018) and chiral effective many-body perturbation theory calculations (Wellenhofer 2015, 2016).
