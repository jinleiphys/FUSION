# Exclusive $J/ψ$, $ψ(2s)$, and $e^{+}e^{-}$ pair production in Au$+$Au ultra-peripheral collisions at RHIC

> Measurements of exclusive $J/ψ$, $ψ(2s)$, and electron-positron ($e^{+}e^{-}$) pair photoproduction in Au$+$Au ultra-peripheral collisions are reported by the STAR experiment at $\sqrt{s_{_\mathrm{NN}}}=200~\rm{GeV}$. We report several first measurements at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider, which are i) $J/ψ$ photoproduction with large momentum transfer up to $2.2~\rm{(GeV/c)^{2}}$, ii) coherent $J/ψ$ photoproduction associated with neutron emissions from nuclear breakup, iii) the rapidity dependence of incoherent $J/ψ$ photoproduction, iv) the $ψ(2s)$ photoproduction cross section at mid-rapidity, and v) $e^{+}e^{-}$ pair photoproduction up to high invariant mass of 6 $\rm{GeV/c^2}$. For measurement ii), the coherent $J/ψ$ total cross section of $γ^{\ast} + \rm{Au} \rightarrow J/ψ+ \rm{Au}$ as a function of the center-of-mass energy $W_{\rm{γ* N}}$ has been obtained without photon energy ambiguities. The data are quantitatively compared with the Monte Carlo models STARlight, Sartre, BeAGLE, and theoretical calculations of gluon saturation with color glass condensate, nuclear shadowing with leading twist approximation, Quantum Electrodynamics, and the Next-to-Leading Order perturbative QCD. At the photon-nucleon center-of-mass energy of 25.0 GeV, the coherent and incoherent $J/ψ$ cross sections of Au nuclei are found to be $71\pm10\%$ and $36\pm7\%$, respectively, of that of free protons. These data provide an important experimental constraint for nuclear parton distribution functions and a unique opportunity to advance the understanding of the nuclear modification effect at the top RHIC energy.

# Exclusive J/ψ, ψ(2s), and e+e- pair production in Au+Au ultra-peripheral collisions at RHIC

## Key claim

The STAR experiment reports that at a photon-nucleon center-of-mass energy of 25.0 GeV, the coherent and incoherent J/ψ cross sections of Au nuclei are 71±10% and 36±7%, respectively, of that of free protons, but these values depend on the specific neutron emission class used to resolve the photon energy ambiguity that has affected previous UPC measurements.

## Method

The STAR experiment analyzed Au+Au ultra-peripheral collision data at √sNN = 200 GeV collected during the 2016 run, corresponding to an integrated luminosity of 13.5 nb−1. Exclusive J/ψ and ψ(2s) events were selected via the e+e− decay channel using the Time Projection Chamber, Barrel Electromagnetic Calorimeter, Time Of Flight system, and Zero Degree Calorimeters, with template fits to invariant mass and transverse momentum distributions used to separate coherent and incoherent production components. Neutron tagging via ZDC hits was employed to categorize events into 0n0n, 0nXn, and XnXn classes, enabling the resolution of the photon energy ambiguity through simultaneous fitting of the three independent rapidity-dependent cross section equations.

## Key numbers

- Coherent J/ψ cross section of Au nuclei at WγN = 25.0 GeV is 71±10% of that of free protons
- Incoherent J/ψ cross section of Au nuclei at WγN = 25.0 GeV is 36±7% of that of free protons
- J/ψ photoproduction measured with momentum transfer up to 2.2 (GeV/c)2
- e+e− pair photoproduction measured up to invariant mass of 6 GeV/c2
- Integrated luminosity of 13.5 nb−1 with approximately 24×106 UPC J/ψ-triggered events
- Approximately 3.9×104 e+e− pairs selected, with 7.9×103 in the J/ψ mass range of 3.0-3.2 GeV/c2
- WγN range of 15-41 GeV within J/ψ rapidity range |y|<1.0
- Overlap rate for ZDC migrations: f1 = 3.8% for single hit, f2 = 0.4% for double hit
- Trigger efficiency scale factors: 1.25 for TOF requirement, 1.04 for BBC veto

## Context

This work extends previous J/ψ photoproduction measurements at RHIC in deuteron systems (STAR 2021) and at the LHC (ALICE, CMS, LHCb) to the kinematic region at xparton ≈ 0.01, which is at the transition between high-x and low-x parton distributions. The data are compared with multiple theoretical models including STARlight, Sartre, Color Glass Condensate calculations (Mantysaari 2022), Leading Twist Approximation nuclear shadowing (Guzey 2013, Strikman 2005), BeAGLE, and NLO pQCD with EPPS21 nuclear PDFs (Eskola 2022). The neutron tagging technique, proposed by Guzey (2013) and Kryshen (2023), enables access to a wider kinematic phase space without photon energy ambiguities, providing new constraints for nuclear parton distribution functions and understanding of nuclear modification effects at RHIC energies.
