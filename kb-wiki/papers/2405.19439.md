# Unveiling baryon charge carriers through charge stopping in isobar collisions

> Utilizing a comprehensive (3+1)D relativistic hydrodynamic framework with multiple conserved charge currents and charge-dependent Lattice-QCD-based equation of state, we study the baryon and electric charge number deposition at mid rapidity in isobar Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at the center of mass energy $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=200$ GeV. Comparing our predictions with upcoming experimental data from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider will shed light on the existence of baryon junctions.

# Unveiling baryon charge carriers through charge stopping in isobar collisions

## Key claim
The baryon-to-electric-charge ratio in isobar Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at 200 GeV can distinguish whether baryon and electric charges share the same initial-state stopping mechanism; if experimental measurements prefer unequal stopping parameters, it would suggest different carriers for baryon and electric charges in nucleons, specifically supporting the picture where gluonic string junctions carry baryon charge while electric charge remains with valence quarks.

## Method
The authors used the (3+1)D relativistic hydrodynamic framework iebe-music with a 3D-Glauber initial state model extended to independently parameterize baryon and electric charge stopping via a probability distribution inspired by string junction stopping. They evolved energy-momentum and conserved charge currents (net baryon, net electric charge, net strangeness) using a four-dimensional lattice-QCD-based equation of state, with particlization at constant energy density 0.35 GeV/fm³ and final-state hadronic transport via UrQMD. The model was first constrained by net-proton measurements in Au+Au collisions across multiple energies, then applied to isobar collisions with nuclear structure parameters including neutron skin effects.

## Key numbers
- Center-of-mass energy: √s_NN = 200 GeV for isobar collisions
- Switching energy density for particlization: e_sw = 0.35 GeV/fm³
- Initial hydrodynamic starting time: τ = 0.5 fm
- Baryon stopping parameter λ_B = 0.2 in the probability distribution gave good agreement with STAR net-proton data; λ_B = 0 underestimated measurements for √s_NN ≥ 10 GeV
- Slope parameter α for net-proton yield vs rapidity loss: α ~ 0.6 from simulations with λ_B = 0.2, matching STAR measurements across all centrality bins
- Normalization factor for isobar ratio: ΔZ/A = 4/96 for Ru (Z=44) and Zr (Z=40) with A=96
- Ratio r ≈ 1 for centralities up to 50% when λ_Q = λ_B = 0.2; ratio increases significantly when λ_Q = 0 (electric charge stays at string ends)
- Neutron skin parameters: Ru half-density radii R_p = 5.09 fm, R_n = 5.105 fm; Zr R_p = 5.02 fm, R_n = 5.12 fm

## Context
This work extends the baryon transport studies of Shen (2022) and Pihan (2023) by implementing independent electric charge stopping in the 3D-Glauber model, building on the multiple-conserved-charge framework of Monnai (2021). It directly addresses the baryon junction hypothesis of Kharzeev (1996) by proposing a measurable observable in isobar collisions at RHIC, complementing the isobar collision program of the STAR Collaboration (2021). The results also demonstrate that electric charge transport measurements can constrain neutron skin structure, providing an alternative to parity-violating electron scattering approaches like the PREX Collaboration (2021).
