# Asymmetric shockwave collisions in $\text{AdS}_{5}$

> Collisions of asymmetric planar shocks in maximally supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory are studied via their dual gravitational formulation in asymptotically anti-de Sitter spacetime. The post-collision hydrodynamic flow is found to be very well described by appropriate means of the results of symmetric shock collisions. This study extends, to asymmetric collisions, previous work of Chesler, Kilbertus, and van der Schee examining the special case of symmetric collisions. Given the universal description of hydrodynamic flow produced by asymmetric planar collisions one can model, quantitatively, non-planar, non-central collisions of highly Lorentz contracted projectiles without the need for computing, holographically, collisions of finite size projectiles with very large aspect ratios. This paper also contains a pedagogical description of the computational methods and software used to compute shockwave collisions using pseudo-spectral methods, supplementing the earlier overview of Chesler and Yaffe.

## Key claim

The post-collision hydrodynamic flow from asymmetric planar shock collisions in strongly coupled maximally supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory is very well described by appropriate combinations (geometric means and harmonic means) of the results from symmetric shock collisions, extending the "universal" flow description to asymmetric cases without requiring full 5D numerical relativity calculations.

## Method

The authors solve the vacuum Einstein equations in asymptotically anti-de Sitter spacetime using a characteristic formulation with infalling Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates, implementing a nested set of linear radial differential equations solved via pseudo-spectral methods. They collide planar shocks with Gaussian longitudinal energy density profiles having different widths \(w_+\) and \(w_-\) but equal transverse energy density scale \(\mu\) in the center-of-momentum frame, and extract the boundary stress-energy tensor to determine the hydrodynamic flow of the dual field theory.

## Key numbers

- The hydrodynamization time remains at \(\mu \, t_{\rm hydro} \approx 2\) even for highly asymmetric collisions, over a range of incoming shock widths from \(0.35/\mu\) down to \(0.075/\mu\)
- For symmetric collisions at \(\tau_{\rm init} = 3.5/\mu\), the amplitude fit is \(A(\mu w) \approx 0.14 + 0.15 \, \mu w - 0.025 \, (\mu w)^2\) and the width fit is \(\sigma(\mu w) \approx 0.96 - 0.49 \, \mu w + 0.13 \, (\mu w)^2\)
- The mean rapidity shift for asymmetric collisions is \(\bar\xi(\mu w_+, \mu w_-) \approx \Xi \frac{w_+-w_-}{w_++w_-}\) with \(\Xi \approx 7 \times 10^{-2}\), constant for \(\tau > 2\)

## Context

This work extends the previous study of symmetric planar shock collisions by Chesler, Kilbertus, and van der Schee (2015) to asymmetric collisions, and supplements the computational methodology overview of Chesler and Yaffe (2013). The results enable modeling of non-planar, non-central collisions of highly Lorentz contracted projectiles by decomposing the transverse plane into independent "pixels," using planar shock results to construct hydrodynamic initial data without requiring full 5D numerical relativity calculations.
