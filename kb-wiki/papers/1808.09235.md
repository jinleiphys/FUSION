# Study of charged particle multiplicity, average transverse momentum and azimuthal anisotropy in Xe+Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.44 TeV using AMPT model

> We have studied the average charged particle density ($<$dN$_{ch}$/d$η$$>$), transverse momentum ($p_{\mathrm{T}}$) spectra, $<$$p_{\mathrm{T}}$$>$ and azimuthal anisotropies of inclusive charged particles produced in Xe+Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.44 TeV using A Multiphase Transport Model (AMPT), which includes the deformation of Xe$^{129}$ nucleus. Calculations have been performed with the string melting version of AMPT model and compared with the recent measurements from the ALICE experiment. The model results over predict the measured $<$dN$_{ch}$/d$η$$>$ for central collisions, agree with the data for mid-central collisions and under predict the measurements for peripheral collisions. The centrality dependence of $<$$p_{\mathrm{T}}$$>$ of charged particles measured in ALICE is not reproduced by the model results. The calculated elliptic flow ($v_{2}$) from AMPT model overpredicts the ALICE measurements in central collisions but are consistent with the data in mid central collisions. We find that the model shows a mild centrality dependence of triangular flow and overestimates the ALICE measurements. Within the model framework, we have also studied various collision configurations of Xe nuclei such as body-body, tip-tip, side-side and random. We find a strong dependence of the above observable on the collision configurations.

## Key claim
The AMPT model with string melting, including the deformation of the Xe-129 nucleus, overpredicts the ALICE data for charged particle multiplicity and elliptic flow in central Xe+Xe collisions at 5.44 TeV, agrees with data for mid-central collisions, and underpredicts the data for peripheral collisions; the model fails to reproduce the centrality dependence of mean transverse momentum measured by ALICE.

## Method
The authors implemented a deformed Woods-Saxon nucleon distribution (with radius R0 = 5.4 fm, diffuseness d = 0.59 fm, deformation parameters beta2 = 0.162 and beta4 = -0.003) in the AMPT model version 2.25t7 with string melting, using a partonic cross section of 3 mb. They simulated 50000 minimum-bias events for each of five collision configurations (random, spherical, tip-tip, body-body, side-side) and compared the results to ALICE measurements using the same centrality selection criteria based on multiplicity in the forward pseudorapidity regions.

## Key numbers
- Charged particle density per participant pair in 0-5% central collisions: AMPT overpredicts ALICE data; in peripheral collisions it underpredicts, while mid-central collisions show good agreement.
- Elliptic flow v2 in 0-5% central collisions: AMPT with random configuration is 15% higher than with spherical Xe nucleus, consistent with hydrodynamics predictions.
- Elliptic flow v2 in side-side configuration is more than 50% larger compared to other configurations in central and mid-central collisions.
- Triangular flow v3 shows a mild centrality dependence in AMPT and overestimates the ALICE measurements across all centralities.
- Mean transverse momentum <pT> from AMPT shows no strong centrality dependence and underpredicts ALICE measurements for all centrality classes except the most peripheral.

## Context
This work extends the study of deformed nuclei collisions beyond hydrodynamic models (such as the hydrodynamical model predictions for Xe+Xe by the authors cited as "hydro") by using a transport-based AMPT framework. It provides a systematic comparison of different collision configurations (body-body, tip-tip, side-side) that can help constrain initial condition models for heavy-ion collisions. The results enable future studies of the chiral magnetic effect in body-tip type collisions where magnetic field is finite but elliptic flow is minimal, and suggest that experimental triggering of specific collision configurations could access different initial state conditions.
