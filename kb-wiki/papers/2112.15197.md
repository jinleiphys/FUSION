# Collision-induced radiative quenching and other disintegration modes of the $2S$ state of muonic hydrogen and deuterium atoms

> The formation and various disintegration modes of $2s$ states for muonic hydrogen and deuterium atoms at kinetic energies both above and below the $2s-2p$ threshold are studied. The cross sections of the collision-induced radiative quenching, elastic scattering, and Coulomb deexcitation of $(μ^- p)_{2s}$ and $(μ^- d)_{2s}$ atoms in collisions with ordinary hydrogen and deuterium atoms at collision energies below the $2s-2p$ threshold are calculated in the framework of the close-coupling approach. The basis set includes all open and closed channels corresponding to exotic-atom states with principal quantum number $n$ from 1 up to 30. Results of these numerical quantum-mechanical calculations of cross sections are used as input data for detailed cascade calculations. The kinetics of atomic cascade for $μ^- p$ and $μ^-d$ atoms is studied in the wide range of the relative target densities, $φ= 10^{-8} -1$ applying the improved version of the cascade model. It is shown that the collision-induced radiative quenching gives a significant contribution to the total disintegration of the $2s$ state of muonic atoms at energies below the $2s-2p$ threshold. The initial population of the $2s$ state for both muonic hydrogen and deuterium atoms, absolute intensities and probabilities of the different disintegration modes as well as their lifetimes are calculated for the fractions with kinetic energies above and below the $2s-2p$ threshold. The obtained results are compared with the known experimental data.

## Key claim

The collision-induced radiative quenching of the $2s$ state of muonic hydrogen and deuterium atoms gives a significant contribution to the total disintegration of the $2s$ state at kinetic energies below the $2s-2p$ threshold, contrary to previous semiclassical treatments that underestimated this process and never included it in cascade calculations.

## Method

Cross sections for elastic scattering, Coulomb deexcitation, Stark transitions, and collision-induced radiative quenching of $(\mu^- p)_{2s}$ and $(\mu^- d)_{2s}$ atoms colliding with ordinary hydrogen and deuterium atoms were calculated using the close-coupling approach with a basis set including all open and closed channels for principal quantum numbers $n=1$ to $30$, incorporating the radiative width $\Gamma_{2p}=80$ $\mu$eV of the $2p$ state. These cross sections were then used as input for detailed atomic cascade calculations over a relative target density range $\varphi = 10^{-8} - 1$ (in units of liquid hydrogen density) at a target temperature of $T=300$ K, tracking the fate of $10^8$ muonic atoms per density value.

## Key numbers

- Radiative width of the $2p$ state used in calculations: $\Gamma_{2p}=80$ $\mu$eV for both muonic hydrogen and muonic deuterium
- Total arrival population $\varepsilon^{\rm tot}_{2s}$ for $(\mu^- p)_{2s}$: about 2% at densities $\varphi=10^{-9}-10^{-7}$, growing to 88% at liquid hydrogen density ($\varphi=1$)
- Total arrival population $\varepsilon^{\rm tot}_{2s}$ for $(\mu^- d)_{2s}$: about 2% at densities $\varphi=10^{-9}-10^{-7}$, growing to 57% at liquid hydrogen density
- At $\varphi=1$, the intensity of collision-induced radiative quenching for $(\mu^- p)_{2s}$ is 48.98% and for $(\mu^- d)_{2s}$ is 30.59% (per formed muonic atom)
- At $\varphi=1$, the intensity of Coulomb deexcitation $2s\to1s$ for $(\mu^- p)_{2s}$ is 4.93% and for $(\mu^- d)_{2s}$ is 1.51%
- At $\varphi=1$, the intensity of Stark $2s\to2p$ transition for $(\mu^- p)_{2s}$ is 32.38% and for $(\mu^- d)_{2s}$ is 24.33%
- Rate of radiative transition $\lambda^{\rm rad}(2p\to1s)=0.12$ ps$^{-1}$
- Muon decay rate: $\lambda_{\mu}=4.54\times 10^{5}$ ps$^{-1}$
- Lamb shift energy: $E_L=202.0$ meV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous close-coupling calculations of cross sections for excited muonic atoms (Popov and Pomerantsev, 2019; 2020) by including the radiative width of the $2p$ state, which was previously neglected. It contradicts the semiclassical results of Mueller (1975) and Cohen and Bardsley (1981), who calculated much smaller cross sections for collision-induced radiative quenching using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation and complex potential models. The results enable improved modeling of the atomic cascade in muonic hydrogen and deuterium, particularly for interpreting experimental data on $K\alpha$ x-ray yields and $2s$ state lifetimes from experiments at Paul Scherrer Institute (e.g., Pohl et al., 2006; 2016).
