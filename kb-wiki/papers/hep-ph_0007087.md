# A simple criterion for effects beyond hard thermal loops

> Thermal amplitudes with ultrasoft momenta, which are not accessible by standard methods of perturbation theory, have recently attracted a lot of interest. However, the comparison of external momenta with the ultrasoft scale $g^2T\ln(1/g)$ is a too crude criterion, since amplitudes with hard external momenta can also be non perturbative, if these momenta are close enough to the light-cone. In this letter, I give a more refined criterion to decide if an amplitude is non perturbative, that applies to all situations. In physical terms, this condition states that non-perturbative effects appear if the particles running in loops have to travel distances larger than their mean free path.

## Key claim

The paper derives a more refined criterion for when collisions (effects beyond hard thermal loops) become important in thermal amplitudes, replacing the crude condition that external momenta must be ultrasoft (Q ≲ g²T ln(1/g)). The new condition states that non-perturbative effects appear if the particles running in loops have to travel distances larger than their mean free path, which can happen even for hard external momenta if they are close to the light-cone.

## Method

The author analyzes a generic finite-temperature loop diagram with external momentum Q, modeling propagators with a mass M (typically the asymptotic mass M∞ ∼ gT) and a collisional width Γ ∼ g²T ln(1/g). By examining the product of an advanced and a retarded propagator adjacent to the external line, the condition for collisions to be important is derived by comparing the real and imaginary parts of an effective mass M²eff. The analysis is extended to vertex (ladder) corrections, which are shown to become of order unity under the same condition.

## Key numbers

- The ultrasoft scale is g²T ln(1/g), which is the typical collisional width Γ and the inverse mean free path λmean⁻¹
- The asymptotic mass M∞ is of order gT
- For almost static fields (q₀ ≪ q), the condition reduces to q ≲ 2Γ ∼ g²T ln(1/g)
- For the opposite limit (q ≪ q₀), the condition becomes q₀ ≲ 2Γ ∼ g²T ln(1/g)
- For hard momenta on the light-cone (Q² = 0), λcoh⁻¹(Q) ≈ M²/2ω, so the condition can be satisfied even for arbitrarily hard external momenta when M ∼ gT
- The Debye mass μ for longitudinal gauge bosons is of order gT; the magnetic mass for transverse gauge bosons is of order g²T

## Context

This work extends the hard thermal loop (HTL) framework developed by Braaten and Pisarski (1990) and Frenkel and Taylor (1990), which resums 1-loop corrections for soft external momenta of order gT. The paper generalizes the ultrasoft criterion from Blaizot and Iancu (1997) and shows that collisions are important not only for ultrasoft momenta but also for hard momenta near the light-cone, as previously encountered in photon production calculations by Aurenche et al. (1998). The criterion unifies understanding of non-perturbative effects in problems including shear viscosity (Jeon, 1995), photon production with the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect, and the damping rate of fast fermions (Blaizot and Iancu, 1997).
