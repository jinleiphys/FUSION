# Flow equation of functional renormalization group for three-body scattering problems

> Functional renormalization group (FRG) is applied to the three-body scattering problem in the two-component fermionic system with an attractive contact interaction. We establish a new and correct flow equation on the basis of FRG and show that our flow equation is consistent with integral equations obtained from the Dyson-Schwinger equation. In particular, the relation of our flow equation and the Skornyakov and Ter-Martirosyan equation for the atom-dimer scattering is made clear.

# Flow equation of functional renormalization group for three-body scattering problems

## Key claim

The paper establishes a correct flow equation for three-body scattering problems within the functional renormalization group (FRG) framework, showing that previous FRG studies of few-body physics missed contributions from diagrams that appear to be particle-hole loops but do not vanish due to non-localities of effective vertices. The new flow equation is proven consistent with the Dyson-Schwinger equation and equivalent to the Skornyakov and Ter-Martirosyan integral equation for atom-dimer scattering.

## Method

The author considers a two-component fermionic system with an attractive contact interaction in the vacuum (temperature T=0, particle number density ρ=0). Starting from the Wetterich equation for the 1PI effective action, the flow equation for the six-point 1PI vertex is derived diagrammatically. To close the hierarchy, all possible eight-point 1PI vertex diagrams that contribute to the three-body sector are identified via perturbative expansion and resummation, yielding nine feedback terms. The resulting closed flow equation for the atom-dimer scattering vertex λ_k is expressed in matrix notation and shown to be equivalent to the k-derivative of the formal solution of the Dyson-Schwinger equation.

## Key numbers

- The s-wave scattering length a_S appears in the renormalization condition: Γ^S(P^0=-2iμ, P=0) = 4π a_S / m
- For a_S > 0, the dimer binding energy is 1/(m a_S^2), and the chemical potential is set to μ = -1/(2m a_S^2) to realize ρ=0 and T=0
- The fermion-four-point vertex at the bound-state pole is identified as √(8π/m^2 a_S)
- The dimer propagator at k=0 is given by D(P) = -(1 + √(1 + m a_S^2 (iP^0 + P^2/(4m)))) / [2(iP^0 + P^2/(4m))]

## Context

This work extends FRG treatments of few-body physics that previously produced flow equations inconsistent with Dyson-Schwinger equations, as seen in studies of three-body and atom-dimer scattering (PhysRevC.77.047001, PhysRevC.78.034001), trion formation (PhysRevA.79.013603), dimer-dimer scattering (PhysRevA.81.043628, PhysRevA.83.023621), and Efimov physics (PhysRevA.79.042705, floerchinger2011efimov, schmidt2012efimov). The paper makes explicit the connection between FRG flow equations and the Skornyakov and Ter-Martirosyan equation (skornyakov1957three), enabling future work on four-body scattering within FRG and unified descriptions of few-body and many-body physics. The formalism is also applied to the model with an auxiliary dimer field via Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation, showing that feedback from higher-point vertices persists even with bosonic degrees of freedom.
