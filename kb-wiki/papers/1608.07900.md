# $X(4140)$, $X(4270)$, $X(4500)$ and $X(4700)$ and their $cs\bar{c}\bar{s}$ tetraquark partners

> In the simple color-magnetic interaction model, we investigate possible ground $cs\bar{c}\bar{s}$ tetraquark states in the diquark-antidiquark basis. We use several methods to estimate the mass spectrum and discuss possible assignment for the $X$ states observed in the $J/ψϕ$ channel. We find that assigning the Belle $X(4350)$ as a $0^{++}$ tetraquark is consistent with the tetraquark interpretation for the $X(4140)$ and $X(4270)$ while the interpretation of the $X(4500)$ and $X(4700)$ needs orbital or radial excitation. There probably exist several tetraquarks around 4.3 GeV that decay into $J/ψϕ$ or $η_cϕ$.

## Key claim
Using a simple color-magnetic interaction (CMI) model in the diquark-antidiquark basis, the authors find that the X(4140), X(4274), and X(4350) are consistent with ground-state S-wave cs\bar{c}\bar{s} tetraquark assignments, but the X(4500) and X(4700) require orbital or radial excitation (D-wave, P-wave, or 2S excitation) and are not good candidates for S-wave tetraquarks. The claim is hedged by noting that the CMI model is oversimplified and that the effective quark masses produce overestimated masses (upper limits) or underestimated masses (lower limits) depending on the reference threshold used.

## Method
The authors adopt a chromomagnetic Hamiltonian H = sum_i m_i - sum_{i<j} C_{ij} \tilde{\lambda}_i · \tilde{\lambda}_j σ_i · σ_j to estimate the mass spectrum of S-wave cs\bar{c}\bar{s} tetraquark states in the diquark-antidiquark basis. They extract effective coupling constants C_{ij} from mass splittings of known baryons and mesons (N/Δ, Σ/Σ*, D/D*, D_s/D_s*, η_c/J/ψ, Σ_c/Σ_c*, Ξ'_c/Ξ_c*) and effective quark masses m_q, m_s, m_c from the nucleon, Ω, and Σ_c* masses. They then compute CMI matrix eigenvalues for J^PC = 2^{++}, 1^{++}, 1^{+-}, and 0^{++} states and estimate tetraquark masses using three methods: (1) direct substitution of effective quark masses, (2) using the D_s D_s* threshold as a reference, and (3) using the J/ψφ threshold as a reference, plus a consistency check by fixing the lowest 1^{++} state to the X(4140) mass.

## Key numbers
- Extracted coupling constants (in MeV): C_qq = 18.4, C_qs = 12.4, C_{c\bar{q}} = 6.8, C_{c\bar{s}} = 6.8, C_{c\bar{c}} = 5.3, C_{cq} = 4.0, C_{sc} = 4.6, C_{ss} = 6.4.
- Effective quark masses (in MeV): m_q = 361.8, m_s = 540.4, m_c = 1724.8.
- In the effective quark mass method, the two 1^{++} tetraquark masses are 4589.5 MeV and 4442.6 MeV, about 300 MeV above the X(4140) (4146.5 MeV) and X(4274) (4274.4 MeV).
- Using the D_s D_s* threshold with color mixing (Part_1), the two 1^{++} masses are 4215.8 MeV and 4068.9 MeV, about 70 MeV below the experimental values.
- Using the J/ψφ threshold, the lowest 1^{++} mass is 3970.0 MeV, about 160 MeV below the X(4140).
- Assigning the X(4140) as the lowest 1^{++} state (mass 4146.5 MeV), the predicted partner masses (in MeV) are: second 1^{++} at 4293.4, highest 0^{++} at 4357.7 (consistent with Belle X(4350)), and two 1^{+-} states at 4303.3 and 4222.2.
- The mass splitting between the two 1^{++} tetraquarks is 146.9 MeV when the X(4140) is fixed as the lowest state.

## Context
This work extends the tetraquark interpretation of X states observed in the J/ψφ channel, following the LHCb confirmation (Aaij 2016) of X(4140) and X(4274) with J^PC = 1^{++} and the discovery of X(4500) and X(4700) with J^PC = 0^{++}. It contrasts with molecular interpretations (Liu 2008, Liu 2009, Mahajan 2009) and charmonium assignments (Ortega 2016), and builds on earlier diquark-antidiquark calculations by Stancu (2009) and Maiani (2016). The results enable searches for additional narrow cs\bar{c}\bar{s} tetraquark states below the J/ψφ threshold (4120 MeV) in J/ψπππ or radiative decay channels, and motivate further study of the X(4500) and X(4700) as D-wave or radially excited tetraquarks.
