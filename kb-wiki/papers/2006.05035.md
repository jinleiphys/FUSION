# Pair invariant mass to isolate background in the search for the chiral magnetic effect in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}$= 200 GeV

> Quark interactions with topological gluon configurations can induce local chirality imbalance and parity violation in quantum chromodynamics, which can lead to the chiral magnetic effect (CME) -- an electric charge separation along the strong magnetic field in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The CME-sensitive azimuthal correlator observable ($Δγ$) is contaminated by background arising, in part, from resonance decays coupled with elliptic anisotropy ($v_{2}$). We report here differential measurements of the correlator as a function of the pair invariant mass ($m_{\rm inv}$) in 20-50\% centrality Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}$= 200 GeV by the STAR experiment at RHIC. Strong resonance background contributions to $Δγ$ are observed. At large $m_{\rm inv}$ where this background is significantly reduced, the $Δγ$ value is found to be significantly smaller. An event-shape-engineering technique is deployed to determine the $v_{2}$ background shape as a function of $m_{\rm inv}$. We extract a $v_2$-independent and $m_{\rm inv}$-averaged signal $Δγ_{\rm sig}$ = (0.03 $\pm$ 0.06 $\pm$ 0.08) $\times10^{-4}$, or $(2\pm4\pm5)\%$ of the inclusive $Δγ(m_{\rm inv}>0.4$ GeV/$c^2$)$ =(1.58 \pm 0.02 \pm 0.02) \times10^{-4}$, within pion $p_{T}$ = 0.2 - 0.8~\gevc and averaged over pseudorapidity ranges of $-1 < η< -0.05$ and $0.05 < η< 1$. This represents an upper limit of $0.23\times10^{-4}$, or $15\%$ of the inclusive result, at $95\%$ confidence level for the $m_{\rm inv}$-integrated CME contribution.

## Key claim

The STAR Collaboration reports that after using the pair invariant mass to isolate and subtract resonance-decay backgrounds, the remaining CME-sensitive signal in the azimuthal correlator is consistent with zero, placing an upper limit of 0.23 x 10^{-4} (15% of the inclusive result) at 95% confidence level for the m_inv-integrated CME contribution in 20-50% centrality Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.

## Method

The STAR experiment measured the CME-sensitive azimuthal correlator Delta-gamma as a function of the pair invariant mass (m_inv) for charged pion pairs with transverse momentum 0.2-0.8 GeV/c in two pseudorapidity ranges (-1 < eta < -0.05 and 0.05 < eta < 1) in 20-50% centrality Au+Au collisions. An event-shape-engineering technique was deployed to determine the elliptic flow (v2) background shape as a function of m_inv, allowing extraction of a v2-independent signal component.

## Key numbers

- Inclusive Delta-gamma for m_inv > 0.4 GeV/c^2: (1.58 +/- 0.02 +/- 0.02) x 10^{-4}
- Extracted v2-independent signal Delta-gamma_sig: (0.03 +/- 0.06 +/- 0.08) x 10^{-4}, or (2 +/- 4 +/- 5)% of the inclusive value
- Upper limit on m_inv-integrated CME contribution at 95% confidence level: 0.23 x 10^{-4}, or 15% of the inclusive result
- Analysis performed for pion pT = 0.2-0.8 GeV/c in 20-50% centrality Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous STAR measurements of the CME-sensitive correlator by introducing the pair invariant mass as a tool to isolate resonance decay backgrounds, which were identified as a major contamination in earlier analyses. The results contradict expectations from a purely CME-driven signal and instead show that the observed Delta-gamma is dominated by background from resonance decays coupled with elliptic anisotropy. This measurement enables future searches for the CME to focus on high-m_inv regions where background is reduced, and provides a benchmark for theoretical calculations of the CME signal magnitude in heavy-ion collisions.
