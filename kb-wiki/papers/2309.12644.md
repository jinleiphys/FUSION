# Maximum gravitational mass $M_{\rm TOV}=2.25^{+0.08}_{-0.07}M_\odot$ inferred at about $3\%$ precision with multimessenger data of neutron stars

> The maximal gravitational mass of nonrotating neutron stars ($M_{\rm TOV}$) is one of the key parameters of compact objects and only loose bounds can be set based on the first principle. With reliable measurements of the masses and/or radii of the neutron stars, $M_{\rm TOV}$ can be robustly inferred from either the mass distribution of these objects or the reconstruction of the equation of state (EoS) of the very dense matter. For the first time we take the advantages of both two approaches to have a precise inference of $M_{\rm TOV}=2.25^{+0.08}_{-0.07}~M_\odot$ (68.3\% credibility), with the updated neutron star mass measurement sample, the mass-tidal deformability data of GW170817, the mass-radius data of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, as well as the theoretical information from the chiral effective theory ($χ$EFT) and perturbative quantum chromodynamics (pQCD) at low and very high energy densities, respectively. This narrow credible range is benefited from the suppression of the high $M_{\rm TOV}$ by the pQCD constraint and the exclusion of the low $M_{\rm TOV}$ by the mass function. Three different EoS reconstruction methods are adopted separately, and the resulting $M_{\rm TOV}$ and $R_{\rm TOV}$ are found to be almost identical, where $R_{\rm TOV}=11.90^{+0.63}_{-0.60}$ km is the radius of the most massive non-rotating NS. This precisely evaluated $M_{\rm TOV}$ suggests that the EoS of neutron star matter is just moderately stiff and the $\sim 2.5-3M_\odot$ compact objects detected by the second generation gravitational wave detectors are most likely the lightest black holes.

## Key claim

The maximum gravitational mass of nonrotating neutron stars is inferred to be \(M_{\rm TOV}=2.25^{+0.08}_{-0.07}M_\odot\) at 68.3% credibility, a result that is robust across three different equation-of-state reconstruction methods and depends on the choice of the density at which perturbative QCD constraints are applied.

## Method

The authors combined a hierarchical Bayesian analysis of the mass distribution of 136 neutron stars (updated to April 2023) with a Bayesian reconstruction of the dense-matter equation of state using multimessenger data: the mass-tidal deformability data of GW170817, the mass-radius data of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620 from NICER, and theoretical constraints from chiral effective theory at low densities and perturbative QCD at high densities. Three independent equation-of-state parameterization methods were used: a single-layer feed-forward neural network, a flexible piecewise linear sound-speed model, and a Gaussian process regression. The posterior distribution of the maximum mass cutoff from the mass-function analysis was incorporated as an additional likelihood in the equation-of-state inference.

## Key numbers

- \(M_{\rm TOV} = 2.25^{+0.08}_{-0.07} M_\odot\) (68.3% credibility), inferred from the combined analysis of the neutron star mass distribution, multimessenger data, and theoretical constraints.
- \(R_{\rm TOV} = 11.90^{+0.63}_{-0.60}\) km (68.3% credibility), the radius of the most massive nonrotating neutron star.
- At 95% confidence level, \(M_{\rm TOV} \leq 2.4 M_\odot\).
- The maximum mass cutoff from the mass-function modeling alone is \(M_{\rm max} = 2.33^{+0.30}_{-0.10} M_\odot\) (68.3% credibility) and \(2.33^{+0.49}_{-0.15} M_\odot\) (90% credibility).
- The most massive neutron star with an accurate measurement is PSR J0740+6620 with a mass of \(2.08 \pm 0.07 M_\odot\).

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian equation-of-state inference approach of Han (2022) and the mass-function cutoff modeling of Shao (2020) by combining both methods for the first time, yielding a precision of about 3% on \(M_{\rm TOV}\). The result is consistent with the earlier supramassive neutron star model inference of \(M_{\rm TOV}=2.17\pm0.09 M_\odot\) from Fan (2020) but avoids the strong assumptions about the GRB 170817A central engine. The narrow credible range is enabled by the suppression of high \(M_{\rm TOV}\) from perturbative QCD constraints, as shown by Gorda (2022) and Han (2022), and the exclusion of low \(M_{\rm TOV}\) by the mass-function cutoff. The result suggests that compact objects of \(2.5-3 M_\odot\) detected by LIGO/Virgo are most likely light black holes, implying the absence of a mass gap between neutron stars and black holes.
