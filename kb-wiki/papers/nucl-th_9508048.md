# The Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction in the Chromo-Dielectric Soliton Model: Dynamics

> The present work is an extension of a previous study of the nucleon-nucleon interaction based on the chromo-dielectric soliton model. The former approach was static, leading to an adiabatic potential. Here we perform a dynamical study in the framework of the Generator Coordinate Method. In practice, we derive an approximate Hill-Wheeler differential equation and obtain a local nucleon-nucleon potential as a function of a mean generator coordinate. This coordinate is related to an effective separation distance between the two nucleons by a Fujiwara transformation. This latter relationship is especially useful in studying the quark substructure of light nuclei. We investigate the explicit contribution of the one-gluon exchange part of the six-quark Hamiltonian to the nucleon-nucleon potential, and we find that the dynamics are responsible for a significant part of the short-range N-N repulsion.

## Key claim

The dynamics of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, treated via the Generator Coordinate Method, are responsible for a significant part of the short-range repulsion; the one-gluon exchange reinforces this repulsive core considerably, but a repulsive core exists even without it, contradicting earlier claims that the color-magnetic part of the one-gluon exchange alone produces the core.

## Method

The authors employed the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) to derive an approximate Hill-Wheeler differential equation for the six-quark system in the chromo-dielectric soliton model, obtaining a local nucleon-nucleon potential as a function of a mean generator coordinate. They then applied a Fujiwara transformation to relate this coordinate to an effective internucleon separation distance, allowing the potential to be expressed in terms of a constant reduced mass. The calculations were performed for three cases: with full one-gluon exchange (OGE), with only the color-magnetic part of OGE, and without any OGE contributions.

## Key numbers

* The asymptotic effective mass (reduced mass) for two well-separated nucleons is 763.6 MeV when one-gluon exchange is included (cases a and b), and 502.3 MeV when it is not (case c).
* The asymptotic value of the potential V(infinity) is 2468 MeV without gluons and 2240 MeV with gluons included.
* The non-adiabatic contribution V_1(beta) becomes important only for deformation parameter beta less than approximately -2 fm.

## Context

This work extends a previous static (Born-Oppenheimer) study of the N-N interaction in the chromo-dielectric soliton model by Koepf, Wilets, Pepin, and Stancu (1994), which produced an adiabatic potential with a soft core. It follows the dynamical approach of Schuh, Pirner, and Wilets (1986) who showed that dynamics contribute significantly to repulsion in the non-topological soliton model, but here one-gluon exchange effects are included explicitly for the first time in such calculations. The Fujiwara transformation relating the deformation parameter to the effective N-N separation enables future applications to studies of quark substructure in light nuclei, as already begun by Koepf and Wilets (1995).
