# Comment on "Evaluation of kinetic freeze-out properties in different relativistic heavy-ion collision systems at √sNN = 200 GeV"

## Key claim
The original paper erroneously treats the kinetic freeze-out temperature (T₀) as identical to the critical temperature (T_c) of the QCD phase transition, and claims that T_c varies across collision systems, a claim that contradicts established lattice QCD calculations showing T_c is a universal property of QCD matter, not dependent on collision system size.

## Method
The comment identifies that the original paper mixes experimental data (Cu+Cu, Au+Au) with AMPT model simulation data (Zr+Zr, Ru+Ru, U+U) while presenting the simulated data as experimental results. It further critiques the fitting methodology where independent fits to π⁺, K⁺, and p spectra are performed while claiming identical T₀ and β_T across species, yet allowing the non-extensive parameter n to vary, a physically inconsistent approach. The comment also notes that AMPT is a transport model that lacks first-principles QGP dynamics and poorly describes baryon production at RHIC energies.

## Key numbers
- Lattice QCD calculations show T_c is universal at approximately 156 MeV for μ_B = 0, independent of collision system size
- Nuclear deformation can alter the initial state geometry by up to 20% in Zr+Zr, Ru+Ru, and U+U systems, yet the original paper fails to specify which deformation scenario was adopted
- The claimed χ²/ndf ≈ 1 for proton spectra showing substantial deviations from fitted curves is only plausible if uncertainties have been substantially overestimated

## Context
This comment extends critiques of methodology in heavy-ion collision freeze-out analyses, specifically targeting the conflation of kinetic freeze-out temperature with the QCD critical temperature established by lattice QCD calculations (HotQCD 2014). The comment builds on prior work showing that AMPT simulations (Lin 2001) cannot replicate the QCD phase transition and that baryon spectra from AMPT are unreliable for constraining freeze-out parameters (Nandi 2019). The comment also references established hydrodynamic principles showing mass-dependent freeze-out (Schnedermann 1993) and proper simultaneous multi-species fitting methodology (Andronic 2017). The comment enables future work by clarifying that any claims of system-dependent critical temperature must be reconciled with universal lattice QCD results, and that proper error propagation and simultaneous fitting procedures must be employed.
