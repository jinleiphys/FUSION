# Twist-3 contribution in the Drell-Yan process with tensor-polarized deuteron

> The tensor-polarized structures of the deuteron can be probed through the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process, where the proton is unpolarized and the deuteron is tensor polarized. This measurement will be conducted at Fermilab in the near future. In this reaction, the twist-3 contribution is not negligible compared to the twist-2 contribution due to the limited invariant mass of the dilepton pair. We calculate the twist-3 contribution for the Drell-Yan cross section with a tensor-polarized deuteron target, preserving the U(1)-gauge invariance of the hadronic tensor. The cross sections and weighted cross sections are expressed in terms of the tensor-polarized parton distribution functions (PDFs), thus one can extract the PDFs $f_{1\scriptscriptstyle{LL}}$, $f_{\scriptscriptstyle{LT}}$, and $f^{(1)}_{\scriptscriptstyle{1LT}}$ from the experimental measurements of Drell-Yan process. Our study should be helpful to solve the puzzle in the tensor-polarized structures of the deuteron.

# Twist-3 contribution in the Drell-Yan process with tensor-polarized deuteron

## Key claim

The twist-3 contribution to the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan cross section is not negligible compared to the twist-2 contribution when the dilepton invariant mass is limited to Q ~ 4-6 GeV, as planned for the upcoming Fermilab measurement; the authors calculate this twist-3 contribution while preserving U(1)-gauge invariance, enabling extraction of the tensor-polarized PDFs f_{1LL}, f_{LT}, and f^{(1)}_{1LT} from experimental data.

## Method

The authors compute the hadronic tensor for the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process in the collinear factorization framework at twist-3, using the Feynman gauge method developed for proton-proton Drell-Yan. They include contributions from intrinsic, kinematical, and dynamical (quark-gluon-quark) correlation functions for spin-1 hadrons, and enforce U(1)-gauge invariance by combining the quark-quark and quark-gluon-quark contributions via the QCD equation of motion. The cross section and weighted cross sections are expressed in terms of the tensor-polarized PDFs f_{1LL}, f_{LT}, and f^{(1)}_{1LT}.

## Key numbers

- The typical invariant mass of the dilepton pair at Fermilab is Q ~ 4-6 GeV, where the twist-3 correction is estimated to be approximately one-third of the leading-twist cross section under the assumption |S_{LL}| ~ |S_{LT}|.
- The twist-3 cross section is suppressed by a factor of M/Q relative to the leading-twist contribution, where M is the deuteron mass.
- A rough numerical estimate of the higher-twist correction yields approximately 0.1 pb for the Drell-Yan process at Fermilab, using the assumption 2x f^q_{LT}(x) - f^{(1)q}_{1LT}(x) = f^q_{1LL}(x) and the HERMES extraction of f_{1LL}.
- The polar and azimuthal angles for the numerical estimate are fixed at theta = pi/4 and hat-phi = 0, with spin parameter |S_{LT}| = 1.

## Context

This work extends the leading-twist calculations for the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process by Hino and Kumano (1998) and Kumano (2016) to include twist-3 contributions, which are necessary because the planned Fermilab measurement (120 GeV unpolarized proton beam on tensor-polarized deuteron) operates at low Q where higher-twist effects are significant. The method follows the twist-3 collinear factorization approach developed for proton-proton Drell-Yan by Qiu and Sterman (1991), Ma et al. (2011-2014), and Chen et al. (2016). The results enable extraction of the twist-3 PDFs f_{LT} and f^{(1)}_{1LT} from future Fermilab data, and allow testing of the Wandzura-Wilczek-type relation between f_{LT} and f_{1LL} proposed by Kumano (2021), which is analogous to the WW relation between g_1 and g_2 in the proton.
