# 133In: A Rosetta Stone for decays of r-process nuclei

> The $β$ decays from both the ground state and a long-lived isomer of $^{133}$In were studied at the ISOLDE Decay Station (IDS). With a hybrid detection system sensitive to $β$, $γ$, and neutron spectroscopy, the comparative partial half-lives (logft) have been measured for all their dominant $β$-decay channels for the first time, including a low-energy Gamow-Teller transition and several First-Forbidden (FF) transitions. Uniquely for such a heavy neutron-rich nucleus, their $β$ decays selectively populate only a few isolated neutron unbound states in $^{133}$Sn. Precise energy and branching-ratio measurements of those resonances allow us to benchmark $β$-decay theories at an unprecedented level in this region of the nuclear chart. The results show good agreement with the newly developed large-scale shell model (LSSM) calculations. The experimental findings establish an archetype for the $β$ decay of neutron-rich nuclei southeast of $^{132}$Sn and will serve as a guide for future theoretical development aiming to describe accurately the key $β$ decays in the rapid-neutron capture (r-) process.

## Key claim

The beta decays of both the ground state and a long-lived isomer of 133In were measured to provide a benchmark for beta-decay theories in neutron-rich nuclei near the r-process path, with the results showing good agreement with newly developed large-scale shell model (LSSM) calculations but revealing that several global models fail to reproduce the competition between Gamow-Teller and First-Forbidden transitions.

## Method

The experiment was performed at the ISOLDE Decay Station (IDS) using a hybrid detection system sensitive to beta, gamma, and neutron spectroscopy with neutron time-of-flight (TOF) technique combined with high-resolution gamma-ray spectroscopy. Selective laser ionization separated decays from the 9/2+ ground state (133gIn) and the 1/2- isomer (133mIn) unambiguously. The beta decay populated neutron-unbound states in 133Sn, which decayed to 132Sn via neutron emission, and the neutron TOF spectra were fitted with response functions to extract excitation energies and decay probabilities.

## Key numbers

- logft value for the Gamow-Teller transition from the 9/2+ ground state to the 7/2+ state at 5.93(9) MeV: 4.7(1)
- logft value for the First-Forbidden transition from the 9/2+ ground state to the 11/2- state at 3.564(1) MeV: 5.7(1)
- logft values from the 1/2- isomer to the 3/2+ state at 3.62(2) MeV: 5.4(1), and to the 1/2+ state at 3.79(2) MeV: 5.8(1)
- Experimental GT strength for the 7/2+ state: 20(4) x 10^-6 s^-1
- Partial half-lives for 133gIn: t(GT) = 260(40) ms and t(FF) = 435(60) ms
- Partial half-lives for 133mIn: t(GT) = 1130(500) ms and t(FF) = 195(10) ms
- LSSM calculated half-lives: 145 ms for ground state and 169 ms for isomer, compared to literature values of 162 and 167 ms

## Context

This work extends spectroscopic studies of 133Sn previously conducted by groups such as Hoff, Vaquero, and others, which had scarce knowledge of states above the neutron separation energy due to weak production rates or inefficient neutron detection. The measurements establish an archetype for beta decay of neutron-rich nuclei southeast of 132Sn and provide validation points for global models used in r-process calculations, including Moller03 (FRDM+QRPA), Borzov16 (DF+CQRPA), Marketin16 (RHB+pn-RQRPA), Ney20 (EFA-pnFAM), and Sarriguren22 (HF+BCS+QRPA), with only Borzov16 achieving good agreement with the experimental data. The results will serve as an anchor point for future theoretical development, including ab-initio approaches, in describing beta decays of r-process nuclei.
