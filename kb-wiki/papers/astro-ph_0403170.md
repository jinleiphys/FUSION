# Cooling of Neutron Stars. Hadronic Model

> We study the cooling of isolated neutron stars. The main cooling regulators are introduced: EoS, thermal transport, heat capacity, neutrino and photon emissivity, superfluid nucleon gaps. Neutrino emissivity includes main processes. A strong impact of medium effects on the cooling rates is demonstrated. With taking into account of medium effects in reaction rates and in nucleon superfluid gaps modern experimental data can be well explained.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a "Nuclear medium cooling scenario," which incorporates medium effects (pion softening, nucleon-nucleon correlations) in all reaction rates and superfluid gaps, can explain the full range of observed neutron star cooling data (slow, intermediate, and rapid cooling) without requiring the direct Urca process to turn on at a mass near 1.4 solar masses, as assumed in competing scenarios.

## Method

The authors constructed a fully general relativistic evolutionary code for neutron star thermal evolution, using the Argonne V18+δv+UIX* equation of state (EoS) parameterized by Heiselberg and Hjorth-Jensen (HHJ, 1999) and a nonlinear Walecka model (NLW) EoS for comparison. They implemented medium-modified emissivities for the modified Urca (MMU), nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung (MNB), neutron and proton pair breaking and formation (nPBF, pPBF), pion condensation (PU), and direct Urca (DU) processes, incorporating density-dependent correlation factors Γ(n) and effective pion gaps ω*(n) from Figure 1, and varied superfluid gaps for 1S0 neutron, 1S0 proton, and 3P2 neutron pairing to fit the observed surface temperature data.

## Key numbers

- The HHJ EoS yields a direct Urca threshold density n_c^DU ≈ 5.19 n_0, corresponding to a neutron star mass M_c^DU ≈ 1.839 M_⊙.
- The NLW model EoS yields a direct Urca threshold density n_c^DU ≈ 2.62 n_0, corresponding to M_c^DU ≈ 1.29 M_⊙.
- For the HHJ EoS, the pion condensation threshold density n_c^PU = 3 n_0 corresponds to a neutron star mass M_c^PU ≈ 1.32 M_⊙.
- The emissivity of the medium-modified Urca process relative to the standard Urca process is given by ε_ν[MMU]/ε_ν[MU] ∼ 10^3 (n/n_0)^(10/3) Γ^6(n)/ω*^8(n).
- The emissivity of the iPBF processes scales as ε_ν[iPBF] ∼ 10^29 m*_iPBF [p_Fi(n)/p_Fn(n_0)] [Δ_ii/MeV]^7 [T/Δ_ii]^(1/2) ξ_ii^2 erg cm^(-3) sec^(-1) for T ≪ Δ_ii.
- The electron specific heat is c_e ∼ 6 × 10^19 (n_e/n_0)^(2/3) T_9 erg cm^(-3) K^(-1).
- The Goldstone boson (phonon) contribution to heat capacity from the 3P_2 superfluid phase is C_G ≈ 6 × 10^14 T_9^3 erg cm^(-3) K^(-1).

## Context

This work extends the "Nuclear medium cooling scenario" developed by Voskresensky (2000) and implemented in the cooling code by Schaab et al. (1997), which demonstrated that medium effects in reaction rates and superfluid gaps could explain cooling data. It directly contradicts the "Standard+exotics DU" scenario of Yakovlev et al. (2003, 2004) and Kaminker et al. (2001), which relies on the direct Urca process turning on at M > 1.358 M_⊙ and requires a suppressed 3P_2 neutron gap and enhanced 1S0 proton gap; the authors criticize this scenario for using an EoS with an unrealistically low DU threshold and for ignoring medium effects in pPBF, MU, and NB processes. The work also contrasts with the "Standard+exotics PU" scenario of Tsuruta et al. (2002, 2004), which fails to fit the hottest object PSR 1055-52. The results enable a unified description of all observed cooling data without invoking exotic processes at masses near 1.4 M_⊙, and motivate further microscopic calculations of medium-modified gaps and reaction rates.
