# Seniority in quantum many-body systems. I. Identical particles in a single shell

> A discussion of the seniority quantum number in many-body systems is presented. The analysis is carried out for bosons and fermions simultaneously but is restricted to identical particles occupying a single shell. The emphasis of the paper is on the possibility of {\em partial} conservation of seniority which turns out to be a peculiar property of spin-9/2 fermions but prevalent in systems of interacting bosons of any spin. Partial conservation of seniority is at the basis of the existence of seniority isomers, frequently observed in semi-magic nuclei, and also gives rise to peculiar selection rules in one-nucleon transfer reactions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while complete seniority conservation in identical-particle systems requires specific conditions on two-body interaction matrix elements, a *partial* conservation of seniority, where some eigenstates retain good seniority even when the interaction generally mixes seniorities, occurs as a peculiar property of spin-9/2 fermions and is prevalent in systems of interacting bosons of any spin.

## Method

The authors analyze the seniority quantum number for systems of identical bosons or fermions occupying a single shell, using group-theoretical classifications based on the algebras U(2j+1) ⊃ SO(2j+1) for bosons and U(2j+1) ⊃ Sp(2j+1) for fermions. They derive conditions for seniority conservation from three-particle and four-particle systems using coefficients of fractional parentage (CFPs) and Racah coefficients, then examine diophantine equations that determine which angular momenta j and interaction components λ allow seniority conservation. The four-particle case is analyzed to identify interactions where some eigenstates have good seniority even when the full interaction mixes seniorities.

## Key numbers

- For fermions, the diophantine equation for a single interaction component λ to conserve seniority is satisfied for (j, λ) = (5/2, 4), (7/2, 4), and (7/2, 6), confirming that for fermions with j ≤ 7/2 any interaction is diagonal in seniority.
- For bosons, the diophantine equation is satisfied for j = 2 with λ = 4, and also for j = 5 with λ = 4, meaning a V̂₄ interaction between h bosons conserves seniority.
- The number of independent seniority v = 3 states is ⌊j/3⌋ for bosons and ⌊(2j−3)/6⌋ for fermions, so no condition on matrix elements follows for j = 1 and 2 (bosons) or j = 3/2, 5/2, and 7/2 (fermions).
- For fermions with j = 9/2, the condition for complete seniority conservation is 65ν₂ − 315ν₄ + 403ν₆ − 153ν₈ = 0.
- For bosons with j = 3, the condition is 11ν₂ − 18ν₄ + 7ν₆ = 0; for j = 4: 65ν₂ − 30ν₄ − 91ν₆ + 56ν₈ = 0; for j = 5: 3230ν₂ − 2717ν₆ − 3978ν₈ + 3465ν₁₀ = 0.

## Context

This work extends the known conditions for seniority conservation in fermionic systems (established by Racah (1943), de-Shalit and Talmi (1963), and Talmi (1993)) to bosonic systems and to the concept of partial seniority conservation. The analysis builds on the dynamical symmetry framework of Frank and Van Isacker (1994) and Iachello (2006), and specifically addresses the example of partial seniority conservation in the j = 9/2 shell noted by Escuderos and Zamick (2006). The results enable understanding of seniority isomers in semi-magic nuclei and selection rules in one-nucleon transfer reactions, and provide a foundation for extending the analysis to multiple shells or different particle types.
