# Could the static properties of nuclei be deduced from the dynamics of a single quark?

> We show that the static properties of a nucleus could arise from a single quark moving in a mean field generated by all other constituents of this nucleus. The resulting model provides a way for determining the nuclear sizes characteristic of the liquid drop model, and reasonably accurate values of magnetic moments of different nuclei with the aid of two parameters $α_s$ and $σ$ appearing in the Cornell potential intended for use in quarkonium physics.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the static properties of nuclei (sizes and magnetic moments) could arise from the dynamics of a single quark moving in a mean field generated by all other constituents of the nucleus, using the Cornell potential with parameters borrowed from quarkonium physics. The hedge is that this is a semiclassical approximation to an effective low-energy QCD theory, and the agreement with experiment is "for the most part within ~20%," with notable exceptions for certain nuclei.

## Method

The authors integrate out all degrees of freedom of a many-quark system except for a single quark Q, then apply a semiclassical approximation where the quark's wave function is a solution to the Dirac equation in spherically symmetric static Lorentz vector and scalar potentials. They impose the pseudospin symmetry condition (U_S = -U_V + constant) and take both potentials to be half the Cornell potential V_C(r) = -α_s/r + σr. They solve the resulting Dirac equations numerically for the radial amplitudes, obtaining energy levels and cavity radii, and then compute magnetic moments using formulas adapted from Ginocchio (1997) with the radial integration limits replaced by the cavity radius r_sc.

## Key numbers

- The Cornell potential parameters used are α_s = 0.7 and σ = 0.14 GeV^2 for quarkonium calculations, with m_c = 1.45 GeV and m_b = 4.92 GeV; agreement with established c-cbar and b-bbar masses is within ~1%.
- For nuclei, the parameters are α_s = 0.7, σ = 0.1 GeV^2, and constituent quark mass m = 0.33 GeV.
- The calculated nuclear radius follows R = R_0 A^{1/3} with R_0 ≈ 1 fm, consistent with the liquid drop model; for example, ^13C (A=13) gives r_sc = 2.84 fm and R_0 = 1.21 fm, while ^207Pb (A=207) gives r_sc = 6.69 fm and R_0 = 1.13 fm.
- Magnetic moments of neutron-odd nuclei with j=1/2 and j=3/2, and proton-odd nuclei with j=1/2, agree with calculated u-quark magnetic moments to within ~20% for most nuclei, using anomalous magnetic moments μ_A = 0.15 μ_B and 0.20 μ_B for the u quark.
- Exceptions include ^113Sn and ^119Te, where the discrepancy is 50-90%.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier approach (Kosyakov, Popov, Vronskii, 2015) that showed nuclear force saturation and the liquid-drop nuclear scale could be derived from a single-quark mean-field model. It contrasts with earlier bag-model approaches (Bleuler, 1983; Petry, 1985) that predicted R ~ N^{1/4} (contrary to experiment) and gave poor magnetic moments (Arima, 1984; Talmi, 1993). The paper also builds on the pseudospin symmetry concept developed by Ginocchio (1997, 1999) and Liang (2015) for nuclear shell models. The results enable a unified effective-theory description of both free hadrons (quarkonia) and nuclei using the same Cornell potential parameters, though the authors note that light nuclei and the single-proton case remain problematic.
