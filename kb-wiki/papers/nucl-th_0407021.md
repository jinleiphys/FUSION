# Electroexcitation of the P33(1232), P11(1440), D13(1520), S11(1535) at Q^2=0.4 and 0.65(GeV/c)^2

> Using two approaches: dispersion relations and isobar model, we have analyzed recent high precision CLAS data on cross sections of π^0, π^+, and ηelectroproduction on protons, and the longitudinally polarized electron beam asymmetry for p(\vec{e},e'p)π^0 and p(\vec{e},e'n)π^+. The contributions of the resonances P33(1232), P11(1440), D13(1520), S11(1535) to πelectroproduction and S11(1535) to ηelectroproduction are found. The results obtained in the two approaches are in good agreement with each other. There is also good agreement between amplitudes of the γ^* N \to S11(1535) transition found in πand ηelectroproduction. For the first time accurate results are obtained for the longitudinal amplitudes of the P11(1440), D13(1520) and S11(1535) electroexcitation on protons.

## Key claim

Using dispersion relations and an isobar model to analyze high-precision CLAS data, the authors extract electroexcitation amplitudes for four nucleon resonances at Q² = 0.4 and 0.65 (GeV/c)², finding good agreement between the two approaches and obtaining the first accurate longitudinal amplitudes for the P₁₁(1440), D₁₃(1520), and S₁₁(1535) resonances.

## Method

The authors analyzed CLAS data on π⁰, π⁺, and η electroproduction cross sections on protons, together with longitudinally polarized electron beam asymmetry data for p(e̅,e'p)π⁰ and p(e̅,e'n)π⁺, using two independent theoretical frameworks: fixed-t dispersion relations and a unitary isobar model with Regge poles. Both approaches parametrize resonance contributions in Breit-Wigner form and include nonresonant backgrounds from Born terms and t-channel exchanges, with the isobar model incorporating K-matrix unitarization of multipole amplitudes below the two-pion production threshold.

## Key numbers

- At Q² = 0.4 (GeV/c)², the P₃₃(1232) magnetic dipole amplitude Im M_{1+}^{3/2} = 4.93 ± 0.01 μb^{1/2} (IM) and 4.97 ± 0.01 μb^{1/2} (DR) at W = 1.229 GeV
- The ratio Im E_{1+}^{3/2} / Im M_{1+}^{3/2} for the P₃₃(1232) is −2.4 ± 0.2% (IM) and −2.9 ± 0.2% (DR) at Q² = 0.4 (GeV/c)²
- The ratio Im S_{1+}^{3/2} / Im M_{1+}^{3/2} for the P₃₃(1232) is −5.0 ± 0.2% (IM) and −5.9 ± 0.2% (DR) at Q² = 0.4 (GeV/c)²
- The S₁₁(1535) transverse helicity amplitude A_{1/2} from π electroproduction is (95 ± 2) × 10⁻³ GeV^{-1/2} (IM) and (88 ± 4) × 10⁻³ GeV^{-1/2} (DR) at Q² = 0.4 (GeV/c)²
- The S₁₁(1535) longitudinal helicity amplitude S_{1/2} from π electroproduction is (−25 ± 2) × 10⁻³ GeV^{-1/2} (IM) and (−18 ± 2) × 10⁻³ GeV^{-1/2} (DR) at Q² = 0.4 (GeV/c)²
- The P₁₁(1440) longitudinal helicity amplitude S_{1/2} is (44 ± 2) × 10⁻³ GeV^{-1/2} (IM) and (41 ± 2) × 10⁻³ GeV^{-1/2} (DR) at Q² = 0.4 (GeV/c)²
- The D₁₃(1520) transverse helicity amplitudes at Q² = 0.4 (GeV/c)² are A_{1/2} = (−66 ± 3) × 10⁻³ GeV^{-1/2} and A_{3/2} = (71 ± 4) × 10⁻³ GeV^{-1/2} (IM)
- The neutron electric form factor required for simultaneous description of π⁰ and π⁺ cross sections is G_en(Q²) = 0.06 at Q² = 0.4 (GeV/c)² and 0.05 at Q² = 0.65 (GeV/c)²

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of π and η photoproduction using dispersion relations and isobar models (Aznauryan 2003, 2004) to the electroproduction regime at finite Q², using new high-precision CLAS data with full angular coverage. The results provide accurate transition form factors that test quark model predictions, including light-front calculations (Capstick 1998) and hybrid state models, and enable comparison with lattice QCD calculations of nucleon resonance structure. The finding of a strong longitudinal response for the Roper resonance rules out its interpretation as a q³G hybrid state (Li 1992), and the extracted amplitudes serve as benchmarks for future theoretical work.
