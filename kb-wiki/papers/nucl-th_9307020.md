# Thermal phenomenology of hadrons from 200 AGeV S+S collisions

> We develop a complete and consistent description for the hadron spectra from heavy ion collisions in terms of a few collective variables, in particular temperature, longitudinal and transverse flow. To achieve a meaningful comparison with presently available data, we also include the resonance decays into our picture. To disentangle the influences of transverse flow and resonance decays in the $m_T$-spectra, we analyse in detail the shape of the $m_T$-spectra.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a consistent thermal description of all measured hadron spectra from 200 AGeV S+S collisions can be achieved using a uniform temperature and longitudinal flow, but that the existence of transverse flow cannot be established from the data alone and requires additional theoretical arguments from hydrodynamics.

## Method

The authors fit a thermal model with resonance decays to the transverse mass spectra of pions, protons, K0_s, Lambda, and anti-Lambda measured by the NA35 Collaboration in central S+S collisions at 200 AGeV. They then extend the model to include longitudinal flow by integrating over a uniform distribution of thermal sources in boost angle, fitting the rapidity distributions to extract the maximal fluid rapidity. Finally, they test the effect of adding transverse flow with a parabolic velocity profile to the transverse mass spectra.

## Key numbers

- The full width at half maximum of the experimental pion rapidity distribution is Gamma_fwhm_exp = 3.3 +/- 0.1, compared to the stationary thermal source prediction of Gamma_fwhm_th = 1.76
- The maximal fluid rapidity extracted from the pion rapidity distribution is eta_max = 1.7, with a fit to all produced particles giving eta_max = 1.7 +/- 0.3 at T = 200 MeV
- The temperature extracted from fits to all five particle species (pions, protons, K0_s, Lambda, anti-Lambda) is T = 200 MeV within statistical uncertainties
- For the transverse flow test, a parabolic velocity profile with beta_s = 0.5 c was used
- The baryon chemical potential was fixed at mu_b = 200 MeV throughout the analysis

## Context

This work extends the thermal model approach previously applied to BNL data by the same authors (Schnedermann, Sollfrank, Heinz, 1993) and builds on the boost-invariant longitudinal expansion model of Bjorken (1983). The paper modifies the Bjorken scenario by restricting the boost angle to a finite interval as proposed by Sinyukov (1990). The resonance decay treatment follows the method of Sollfrank et al. (1991). The analysis enables a consistent hydrodynamical description of the collision zone, which the authors develop in subsequent papers to extract information about the final state of the hadronic system and to prove the existence of transverse flow by theoretical means.
