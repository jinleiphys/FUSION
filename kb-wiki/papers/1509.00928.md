# Relativistic few-body methods

> I discuss the role of relativistic quantum mechanics in few-body physics, various formulations of relativistic few-body quantum mechanics and how they are related.

## Key claim

Relativistic quantum mechanics provides a framework for modeling few-body systems that is directly expressed in terms of observable particle degrees of freedom, but the interactions necessarily become complicated when reformulated from an underlying quantum field theory such as QCD.

## Method

The paper reviews four distinct Hilbert space representations of relativistic quantum theories of particles: local field theory (Wightman functions), Euclidean field theory (Euclidean Green functions), direct interaction representations (particle Hilbert space with explicit Poincaré group generators), and Schwinger-Dyson/quasipotential representations (time-ordered Green functions). It discusses how these representations are related through analytic continuations and unitary transformations, and illustrates their application to few-nucleon scattering calculations using direct interaction methods.

## Key numbers

- p-d breakup cross section calculations at 500 MeV using a Malfliet Tjon interaction, comparing relativistic and non-relativistic calculations constrained to give the same two-body center-of-mass cross sections
- Elastic p-d scattering cross section at 135 MeV and 250 MeV, comparing relativistic calculations with CD Bonn two-nucleon and TM99 three-nucleon forces against non-relativistic calculations
- At 250 MeV, the combined effect of relativistic effects and three-nucleon forces at back angles is almost an order of magnitude too small to explain the discrepancy with data from PhysRevC.66.044002 and PhysRevC.76.014004

## Context

This review extends the line of work on Dirac's forms of dynamics (Dirac 1949) and S-matrix preserving unitary transformations between representations (Sokolov 1977). It connects covariant field theory representations (Wightman functions, Euclidean Green functions) to explicit particle representations suitable for few-body calculations. The paper enables future work on few-nucleon problems at the few-GeV scale, where a transition from hadronic degrees of freedom to QCD degrees of freedom is expected, and identifies missing physics in standard meson-exchange nuclear forces at 250 MeV below pion production threshold.
