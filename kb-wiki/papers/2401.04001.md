# Systematic shell-model study of $^{99-129}$Cd isotopes and isomers in neutron-rich $^{127-131}$In isotopes

> Systematic shell-model calculations are presented for odd-mass Cd isotopes with $N=51-81$ utilizing a combination of a $G$-matrix interaction and a semiempirical one. The excited energy spectra and electromagnetic transition probabilities are compared with the recently available experimental data. We have found that the observed quadrupole moments in the $11/2^-_1$ states that linearly change with the neutron number are well accounted for by the dominance of prolate shapes throughout the Cd isotope chain. We have also described the properties of several isomeric states in neutron-rich $^{127-131}$In isotopes that were recently observed in Jyväskylä.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the observed linear change of quadrupole moments in the \(11/2^-_1\) states with neutron number across the Cd isotope chain is well accounted for by the dominance of prolate shapes, but this conclusion is hedged by the observation that the energy surfaces become shallow in the \(\gamma\) direction for mid-shell isotopes, meaning the shape may not be rigidly prolate in those cases.

## Method
Large-scale shell-model calculations were performed for odd-mass \(^{99-129}\)Cd and \(^{127-131}\)In isotopes using a combined interaction: a \(G\)-matrix-based interaction for the proton-neutron part and the SNBG1 interaction for the neutron-neutron part, within a model space of two proton orbitals (\(1p_{1/2}\), \(0g_{9/2}\)) and five neutron orbitals (\(1d_{5/2}\), \(2s_{1/2}\), \(1d_{3/2}\), \(0g_{7/2}\), \(0h_{11/2}\)) above a \(^{88}\)Sr inert core. The shell-model code KSHELL was used to diagonalize the Hamiltonian matrices without truncation, with the largest \(M\)-scheme dimension reaching \(0.9 \times 10^9\) for \(^{113,115}\)Cd. Additionally, \(Q\)-constrained Hartree-Fock calculations with variation after parity projection were carried out to probe nuclear shapes.

## Key numbers
- The largest \(M\)-scheme dimension in the shell-model calculations is \(0.9 \times 10^9\) for \(^{113,115}\)Cd.
- For \(^{99}\)Cd, the calculated \(B(E2; 17/2^+_1 \rightarrow 13/2^+_1)\) value is \(65.8\;e^2\text{fm}^4\), compared to the experimental value of \(68(3)\;e^2\text{fm}^4\).
- For \(^{101}\)Cd, the calculated \(B(E2; 19/2^+_1 \rightarrow 15/2^+_1)\) value is \(27.9\;e^2\text{fm}^4\), compared to the experimental value of \(4.5(6)\;e^2\text{fm}^4\).
- For \(^{105}\)Cd, the calculated \(B(E2; 21/2^+_1 \rightarrow 17/2^+_1)\) value is \(2.9\;e^2\text{fm}^4\), compared to the experimental value of \(2.35(26)\;e^2\text{fm}^4\).
- For \(^{107}\)Cd, the calculated \(B(E2; 7/2^+_1 \rightarrow 5/2^+_1)\) value is \(797.4\;e^2\text{fm}^4\), compared to the experimental value of \(120.7(91)\;e^2\text{fm}^4\).
- For \(^{111}\)Cd, the calculated \(B(E2; 3/2^+_1 \rightarrow 1/2^+_1)\) value is \(530.4\;e^2\text{fm}^4\), compared to the experimental value of \(370.7(444)\;e^2\text{fm}^4\).
- For \(^{129}\)Cd, the calculated \(3/2^+\) state lies 244 keV above the \(11/2^-\) ground state.
- The effective charges used for \(E2\) transitions are \((e_\pi, e_\nu) = (1.6e, 0.8e)\).

## Context
This work extends systematic shell-model studies of Cd isotopes beyond earlier calculations that were limited to neutron-deficient regions, such as the \(G\)-matrix interaction fitted by Boelaert (2010) for even-mass Cd with \(50 \leq N \leq 58\). The study also builds on experimental measurements of quadrupole moments by Yordanov (2020) and isomeric states in neutron-rich In isotopes observed at Jyvaskyla. The results enable predictions for unobserved excited states in \(^{129}\)Cd and support the interpretation of the \(11/2^-_1\) band as an unpaired neutron in \(h_{11/2}\) coupled to the ground band of even-mass Cd isotopes, similar to the \(9/2^+_1\) bands in neutron-rich Cr, Fe, and Ni isotopes studied by Togashi (2015).
