# Interaction of accelerator neutrinos with energies up to 55 MeV with ${}^{127}$I nuclei

> The interaction of neutrinos with an energy of up to 55~MeV from the Spallation Neutron Source (SNS) accelerator with a perspective ${}^{127}$I detector at the Oak Ridge National Laboratory (United States) has been studied. The resonance structure of the charge-exchange strength function $S(E)$ has been calculated taking into account high-lying resonances, and the effect of this structure on the cross section $σ(E)$ for the accelerator neutrino capture by the ${}^{127}$I nucleus has been examined. The influence of the Gamow-Teller resonance GTR-1 and the second new higher resonance GTR-2 on the energy dependence of the cross section $σ(E)$ has been analyzed. The effect of the high-lying analog resonance AR-2 has also been taken into account for the first time. It has been found that the contributions of GTR-1 to the calculated cross section $σ(E)$ are from 60% to $\approx$80%, and GTR-2 about 12%, and AR-2 $\le$ 10%, respectively. The contribution of high-lying resonances to the $(ν, n)$ and $(ν, 2n)$ neutrino capture cross sections with neutron emission and the formation of the ${}^{126}$I and ${}^{125}$I isotopes, respectively, has been analyzed. The comparison of the cross sections for the interaction of accelerator neutrinos calculated by different methods with experimental data has shown coincidence at energies below the neutron separation threshold $E_x < S_{1n}$ and strong discrepancy at higher energies, which is difficult to explain. A new measurement of cross sections at energies $E_x > S_{1n}$ is needed.

## Key claim

The paper calculates that the dominant contribution to the neutrino capture cross section on ${}^{127}$I comes from the Gamow-Teller resonance GTR-1 (60% to approximately 80%), while the newly identified higher resonances GTR-2 and AR-2 contribute only about 12% and less than or equal to 10%, respectively; however, the calculated cross sections for events with neutron emission disagree strongly with the COHERENT experimental data, a discrepancy the authors find difficult to explain.

## Method

The authors calculated the charge-exchange strength function $S(E)$ for ${}^{127}$I up to 60 MeV using the microscopic theory of finite Fermi systems (TFFS) with a Landau-Migdal nucleon-nucleon interaction, using parameters $f_0^\prime = 1.351$ and $g_0^\prime = 1.214$. They then computed the neutrino capture cross section $\sigma(E_\nu)$ by integrating the strength function with the Fermi function, using a linear extrapolation of tabulated Fermi function values to cover the high electron energies from accelerator neutrinos. The spectrum-averaged cross sections were obtained by folding $\sigma(E_\nu)$ with the analytic electron neutrino spectrum from pion decay at the Spallation Neutron Source.

## Key numbers

* The Gamow-Teller resonance GTR-1 is located at $E_{\text{GTR1}} = 14.8$ MeV, and the new higher resonances GTR-2a and GTR-2b are at $E_{\text{GTR2a}} = 30.8$ MeV and $E_{\text{GTR2b}} = 34.3$ MeV.
* The analog resonance AR-1 is at $E_{\text{AR1}} = 12.68$ MeV, and the new higher analog resonance AR-2 is at $E_{\text{AR2}} = 28.7$ MeV.
* The calculated spectrum-averaged cross section for neutrino capture without neutron emission is $\langle \sigma(0n) \rangle = 1.94 \times 10^{-40}$ cm$^2$, in agreement with the COHERENT experimental value of $5.2_{-3.1}^{+3.4} \times 10^{-40}$ cm$^2$ and the LAMPF value of $2.84 \pm 0.91(\text{stat.}) \pm 0.25(\text{syst.}) \times 10^{-40}$ cm$^2$.
* The calculated cross section for one-neutron emission is $\langle \sigma(1n) \rangle = 16.07 \times 10^{-40}$ cm$^2$, and for two-neutron emission is $\langle \sigma(2n) \rangle = 2.88 \times 10^{-40}$ cm$^2$, which are consistent with the MARLEY Monte Carlo results but differ from the COHERENT measurement of $\langle \sigma(\ge 1n) \rangle = 2.2_{-2.2}^{+3.5} \times 10^{-40}$ cm$^2$.
* The neutron separation energies are $S_{1n} = 7.246$ MeV and $S_{2n} = 17.264$ MeV, and the electron capture threshold is $Q_{\text{EC}} = 662.3$ keV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous microscopic calculations of the ${}^{127}$I charge-exchange strength function (Lutostansky and Shulgina, 1991; Lutostansky et al., 2020) to higher excitation energies up to 60 MeV, motivated by the harder spectrum of accelerator neutrinos from the Spallation Neutron Source compared to solar neutrinos. The calculations are compared to the recent COHERENT measurement (2023) and the earlier LAMPF measurement (2003), and the authors find good agreement for transitions to bound states but a strong discrepancy for transitions above the neutron separation threshold, a problem also noted by the COHERENT collaboration for lead targets. The paper calls for a new measurement of the strength function using the ${}^{127}$I(${}^{3}$He, $t$)${}^{127}$Xe reaction at higher energies to resolve the discrepancy.
