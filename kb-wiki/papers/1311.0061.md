# Renormalization Group Invariance in the Subtractive Renormalization Approach to the NN Interactions

> In this work, we apply the subtracted kernel method (SKM) to the chiral nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interaction in the $^3P_0$ channel up to next-to-next-to-leading-order ($NNLO$). We demonstrate, by explicit numerical calculations, that the SKM procedure is renormalization group invariant under the change of the subtraction scale provided the driving-term of the subtracted scattering equation is evolved through a non-relativistic Callan-Symanzik (NRCS) flow-equation.

## Key claim
The subtracted kernel method (SKM) applied to the chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction in the \(^3P_0\) channel up to next-to-next-to-leading-order is renormalization group invariant under changes of the subtraction scale, but only if the driving term of the subtracted scattering equation is evolved through a non-relativistic Callan-Symanzik flow equation.

## Method
The authors applied the subtracted kernel method (SKM) to the chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction in the \(^3P_0\) channel up to next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO). They solved the subtracted Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the K-matrix with four subtractions, fitting the renormalized strength of the contact interaction at a reference subtraction scale \(\bar{\mu}=0.7\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) to low-energy scattering data from the Nijmegen-II potential. They then evolved the driving term through the non-relativistic Callan-Symanzik (NRCS) flow equation from the reference scale to \(\mu=0.1\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) and verified that the calculated phase-shifts remained invariant.

## Key numbers
- Reference subtraction scale: \(\bar{\mu}=0.7\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) used as the boundary condition for the NRCS flow equation.
- Evolved subtraction scale: \(\mu=0.1\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) reached by evolving the driving term through the NRCS equation.
- Phase-shift invariance: relative differences smaller than \(10^{-12}\) due to numerical errors when comparing phase-shifts at the reference scale and after evolution.
- Fitting data range: low-energy momenta \(k \le 0.1\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) used to fix the renormalized strength \(C_1(\mu)\).
- Pion mass: \(m_\pi = 138\ \mathrm{MeV}\); axial coupling constant: \(g_a = 1.25\); pion decay constant: \(f_\pi = 93\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Momentum grid: 200 gaussian integration points used for discretization in relative momentum space.

## Context
This work extends the subtracted kernel method (SKM) previously developed by the same group (Szpigel and Timóteo, various years) to the chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction at next-to-next-to-leading-order, specifically in the \(^3P_0\) channel. It demonstrates that the SKM procedure is renormalization group invariant when the driving term is evolved through the NRCS flow equation, addressing a key requirement for consistency in chiral effective field theory approaches. The method provides an alternative to the standard cutoff regularization scheme (Epelbaum, 2009; Machleidt, 2011) and enables calculations where the subtraction scale becomes a non-physical parameter, with the reference scale remaining as the only relevant scale parameter.
