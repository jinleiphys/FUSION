# Correlations of Baryon and Charge Stopping in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Baryon numbers are carried by valence quarks in the standard QCD picture of the baryon structure, while some theory proposed an alternative baryon number carrier, a non-perturbative Y-shaped configuration of the gluon field, called the baryon junction in the 1970s. However, neither of the theories has been verified experimentally. It was recently suggested to search for the baryon junction by investigating the correlation of net-charge and net-baryon yields at midrapidity in heavy-ion collisions. This paper presents studies of such correlations in collisions of various heavy ions from Oxygen to Uranium with the UrQMD Monte Carlo model. The UrQMD model implements valence quark transport as the primary means of charge and baryon stopping at midrapidity. Detailed study are also carried out for isobaric $_{40}^{96}\mathrm{Zr}$ + $_{40}^{96}\mathrm{Zr}$ and $_{44}^{96}\mathrm{Ru}$ + $_{44}^{96}\mathrm{Ru}$ collisions. We found a universal trend of the charge stopping with respect to the baryon stopping, and that the charge stopping is always more than the baryon stopping. This study provides a model baseline in valence quark transport for what is expected in net-charge and net-baryon yields at midrapidity of relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Using the UrQMD model, which implements valence quark transport as the primary baryon stopping mechanism, the authors find a universal trend where charge stopping is always greater than baryon stopping at midrapidity; this provides a baseline for distinguishing valence quark transport from the alternative baryon junction mechanism, but the model itself does not rule out either theory.

## Method

The authors simulated collisions of seven nuclear species (O+O, Al+Al, Cu+Cu, Zr+Zr, Ru+Ru, Au+Au, U+U) at center-of-mass energy per nucleon-nucleon pair of 200 GeV using the UrQMD event generator, which implements baryon stopping via excitation and fragmentation of strings between diquarks and valence quarks. Net-charge and net-baryon yields were calculated at midrapidity (|y|<1.0) for centrality classes from 0-10% to 90-100%, and the correlation between them was quantified by fitting a linear slope. For isobaric Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions, the difference in net-charge between the two systems was compared to the net-baryon yield scaled by the difference in charge-to-mass ratio.

## Key numbers

* The slope a relating net-charge density to net-baryon density increases from 0.401 ± 0.001 in U+U collisions to 0.677 ± 0.001 in O+O collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV.
* A power-law fit to the scaled data (dQ/dy divided by Z/A versus dB/dy) yields parameters a = 1.2302 ± 0.0007 and n = 0.9413 ± 0.0003, collapsing data from O+O to U+U onto a universal curve.
* The ratio of (B × ΔZ/A) over ΔQ for isobaric Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at |y|<1.0 is below unity across all centralities, with values ranging from approximately 0.6 in peripheral (60-80%) to approximately 0.9 in central (0-20%) collisions.
* The net-charge difference between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions is close to dB/dy × ΔZ/A at midrapidity but exceeds it at y=2-3, with the transition occurring at y=2-3.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical proposal by Brandenburg et al. (2022) to search for the baryon junction mechanism by measuring correlations of net-charge and net-baryon yields at midrapidity. The UrQMD results provide a model baseline for valence quark transport that can be compared to experimental data from the STAR Collaboration, which has collected large samples of Ru+Ru, Zr+Zr, O+O, and Au+Au collisions at RHIC. The study enables experimental discrimination between the standard valence quark picture and the alternative baryon junction mechanism proposed by Artru (1974) and Rossi and Veneziano (1977), where the latter predicts that stopped baryons are composed of sea quarks and thus net-charge should not scale with the initial nuclear charge.
