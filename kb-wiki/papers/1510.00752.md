# A consistent four-body CDCC model of low-energy reactions: Application to 9Be + 208Pb

> We investigate the $^9$Be + $^{208}$Pb elastic scattering, breakup and fusion at energies around the Coulomb barrier. The three processes are described simultaneously, with identical conditions of calculations. The $^{9}$Be nucleus is defined in an $α+ α$ + n three-body model, using the hyperspherical coordinate method. We first analyze spectroscopic properties of $^9$Be, and show that the model provides a fairly good description of the low-lying states. The scattering with $^{208}$Pb is then studied with the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) method, where the $α+α$ + n continuum is approximated by a discrete number of pseudostates. Optical potentials for the $α$+ $^{208}$Pb and n+ $^{208}$Pb systems are taken from the literature. We present elastic-scattering and fusion cross sections at different energies.

# A consistent four-body CDCC model of low-energy reactions: Application to 9Be + 208Pb

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a four-body Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) model, treating 9Be as an α+α+n three-body system, can simultaneously describe elastic scattering, breakup, and fusion for 9Be + 208Pb at near-barrier energies without renormalization of the optical potentials. However, the agreement with fusion data is achieved only after removing the neutron capture contribution from the calculated cross section, and the model overestimates fusion at sub-barrier energies.

## Method
The authors constructed 9Be wave functions using a three-body α+α+n model with the hyperspherical coordinate method, employing real α+α and α+n interactions from the literature and adding a phenomenological three-body force to reproduce the experimental ground-state binding energy. They then applied the CDCC method to 9Be + 208Pb scattering, discretizing the three-body continuum into pseudostates and using optical potentials for α+208Pb and n+208Pb from the literature. The coupled-channel equations were solved with R-matrix theory, and fusion cross sections were computed from the scattering matrices, with a separate calculation removing the neutron-target imaginary potential to match experimental fusion definitions.

## Key numbers
- 9Be ground-state binding energy: −1.57 MeV experimentally; the bare three-body model gives −3.12 MeV, requiring a phenomenological three-body force to correct.
- 9Be ground-state r.m.s. radius: calculated 2.36 fm, compared to experimental 2.45 ± 0.01 fm.
- 9Be ground-state quadrupole moment: calculated 4.96 e·fm², compared to experimental 5.29 ± 0.04 e·fm².
- 9Be ground-state magnetic moment: calculated −1.33 μ_N, compared to experimental −1.18 μ_N.
- Cutoff energy for pseudostates in the CDCC calculation: 12.5 MeV.
- Elastic scattering data shown at 9Be laboratory energies of 38 MeV and higher (exact second energy not specified in truncated text).
- Above the Coulomb barrier (E_cm ≈ 40 MeV), the full CDCC calculation with neutron capture removed shows excellent agreement with experimental fusion data; the calculation without this removal overestimates data by about a factor of two.

## Context
This work extends previous CDCC treatments of two-body projectiles (such as deuteron or 7Li) to a full four-body treatment of the Borromean nucleus 9Be, which has no bound two-body subsystems. It builds on prior three-body hyperspherical calculations for 9Be structure and on earlier CDCC studies of 9Be + 208Pb by other groups (e.g., Diaz-Torres et al., 2003; Jha et al., 2014). The results confirm that continuum couplings are more important at low energies, as suggested by Descouvemont and Diaz-Torres (2012), and that the influence of breakup channels increases at sub-barrier energies. The approach enables consistent simultaneous description of elastic scattering, breakup, and fusion without renormalizing optical potentials, though the sub-barrier fusion overestimation remains an open question for further investigation.
