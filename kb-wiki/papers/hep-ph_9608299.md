# Dilepton/Photon Production in Heavy Ion Collisions and the QCD Phase Transition

> We calculate electromagnetic production from highly excited hadronic matter created in heavy ion collisions. The rates include the usual lowest order processes in quark-gluon plasma plus the usual reactions in the hadronic phase, related with rho,a_1 mesons. The space-time integration is done using a hydrodynamical model. We have found that dilepton mass spectrum agrees with results of other previous works, but *disagrees* with the CERES dilepton data. In order to explain these data, some "unconventional" production mechanism need to be incorporated: we discuss especially the notion of modified rho,a_1 masses, which indeed may explain the data. Other suggestions (e.g. the longer-lived fireball) to increase the production of low mass dileptons seem to be insufficient for the task. The results for direct photon production are below the current WA80 experimental bounds, for all variants considered.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that conventional hadronic and quark-gluon plasma dilepton production rates, integrated over a hydrodynamical space-time evolution, cannot explain the CERES data on low-mass dilepton excess in S-Au and Pb-Au collisions at SPS energies, but that a scenario with temperature-dependent dropping masses of the rho and a1 mesons (with both masses reduced to about half their vacuum values at the critical temperature) can fit the data without changing the production mechanism or space-time evolution.

## Method

The authors compute dilepton and photon production rates using standard lowest-order QCD processes for the quark-gluon plasma and hadronic reactions involving rho and a1 mesons, with rates taken from the literature (specifically SYZ_96). They integrate these rates over a space-time evolution obtained from numerical solutions of relativistic (non-viscous) hydrodynamics for central S-Au at 200 A GeV and Pb-Au at 160 A GeV, using an equation of state with a phase transition at T_c = 160 MeV and a freeze-out temperature of T_f = 140 MeV. For the modified-mass scenarios, they implement temperature-dependent rho and a1 masses that decrease toward T_c, using a parametrized function fitted to different chiral-restoration paths in the m_rho-m_a1 plane.

- Freeze-out temperature T_f = 140 MeV used for the breakup surface
- Vacuum rho meson mass m_rho0 = 770 MeV, width Gamma_rho0 = 149 MeV
- Vacuum a1 meson mass m_a10 = 1210 MeV, width Gamma_a10 = 400 MeV
- Pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV; a1 decay constant f_a = 190 MeV
- Bag constant B = 0.32 GeV/fm^3 used in the QGP equation of state
- Speed of sound in hadronic phase c_s^2 = 0.19

- Bjorken-like scenario: initial energy density epsilon_i = 9.0 GeV/fm^3, initial temperature T_i = 0.27 GeV, initial longitudinal half-size z_0 = 1.1 fm

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of dilepton production from heavy-ion collisions by several groups (Li-Ko-Brown using RQMD-based cascades; Srivastava et al. using Bjorken-scaling hydrodynamics) which all obtained similar conventional mass spectra that disagreed with the CERES data. The authors test the "dropping m_rho" hypothesis previously proposed by Hofmann (1994) and Li, Ko, and Brown (1995), and link it to chiral symmetry restoration via Weinberg-type sum rules from Kapusta and Shuryak (1994). They also test the long-lived fireball scenario from their own previous work (Hung and Shuryak, 1995) and find it insufficient to explain the data with standard masses. The results enable future experimental searches for modified a1 contributions through dilepton p_t distributions at invariant masses around 300 MeV.
