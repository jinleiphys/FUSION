# The (magnetized) effective QCD phase diagram

> I present the highlights of a recent study of the effective QCD phase diagram on the temperature T and quark chemical potential mu plane, where the strong interactions are modeled using the linear sigma model coupled to quarks. The phase transition line is found from the effective potential at finite T and mu taking into account the plasma screening effects. We find the location of the critical end point (CEP) to be (mu^CEP/T_c,T^CEP/T_c) \sim (1.2,0.8), where T_c is the (pseudo)critical temperature for the crossover phase transition at vanishing mu. This location lies within the region found by lattice inspired calculations. Since the linear sigma model does not exhibit confinement, I argue that the location is due to the proper treatment of the plasma screening effects and not to the size of the confining scale. I also comment on the extension of this study to determine the dependence of the CEP's location on the strength of an external magnetic field.

## Key claim

The critical end point (CEP) of the effective QCD phase diagram can be located in the region found by lattice-inspired calculations using the linear sigma model with quarks, even though this model does not include confinement; the author argues that this location is due to the proper treatment of plasma screening effects rather than the size of the confining scale.

## Method

The author computed the effective potential for the linear sigma model coupled to quarks at finite temperature T and quark chemical potential μ, including ring diagram contributions to account for plasma screening effects beyond the mean field approximation. The boson self-energy was computed in the high-temperature limit with finite chemical potential, and the fermion contribution to the effective potential was obtained by solving a differential equation with boundary conditions at vanishing fermion mass. The model parameters (λ = 0.2, g = 0.71) were fixed by requiring that the thermal boson masses vanish at the phase transition for μ = 0, using a vacuum sigma mass m_σ = 300 MeV and a critical temperature T_c ≈ 170 MeV.

## Key numbers

- CEP location: (μ^CEP/T_c, T^CEP/T_c) ∼ (1.2, 0.8), where T_c is the (pseudo)critical temperature for the crossover phase transition at vanishing μ
- Model parameters used: λ = 0.2, g = 0.71, with m_σ^vac = 300 MeV
- T_c ≈ 170 MeV (chosen for two-flavor massless quarks in the chiral limit, slightly larger than N_f = 2+1 lattice results)
- The relation between couplings from the vanishing curvature condition at T_c: sqrt(λ/2 + N_f N_c g^2/6) = 0.77

## Context

This work extends studies of the QCD phase diagram using effective models that describe chiral symmetry restoration, such as the linear sigma model and Nambu–Jona-Lasinio type models (NJL, pNJL, and related approaches). Previous work by Roberts and collaborators argued that the CEP location depends on the confining length scale, with models lacking confinement producing a CEP closer to the μ axis. The author shows that including plasma screening effects (ring diagrams) in the linear sigma model yields a CEP location consistent with mathematical extensions of lattice techniques, which place the CEP in the region (μ^CEP/T_c, T^CEP/T_c) ∼ (1.0–1.4, 0.9–0.95) as reported by Sharma. The author also previews ongoing work extending this analysis to include external magnetic fields, where preliminary results indicate the CEP moves to lower μ and T with increasing field strength, eventually reaching the T-axis, consistent with recent lattice results by Endrodi and collaborators showing inverse magnetic catalysis and a first-order transition at asymptotically large magnetic fields.
