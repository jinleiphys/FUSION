# Role of low-$l$ component in deformed wave functions near the continuum threshold

> The structure of deformed single-particle wave functions in the vicinity of zero energy limit is studied using a schematic model with a quadrupole deformed finite square-well potential. For this purpose, we expand the single-particle wave functions in multipoles and seek for the bound state and the Gamow resonance solutions. We find that, for the $K^π=0^{+}$ states, where $K$ is the $z$-component of the orbital angular momentum, the probability of each multipole components in the deformed wave function is connected between the negative energy and the positive energy regions asymptotically, although it has a discontinuity around the threshold. This implies that the $K^π=0^{+}$ resonant level exists physically unless the $l=0$ component is inherently large when extrapolated to the well bound region. The dependence of the multipole components on deformation is also discussed.

# Role of low-\(l\) component in deformed wave functions near the continuum threshold

## Key claim

For \(K^{\pi}=0^{+}\) states in a deformed potential, the \(s\)-wave component becomes dominant only exactly at the zero-binding threshold, and the resonant level in the continuum exists as a physical state unless the \(l=0\) component is inherently large when extrapolated from the well-bound region. The paper shows that the intuitive picture of a resonant level as a smooth extension of a bound level into the continuum is valid, except for a narrow region around zero energy where the \(s\)-wave component exhibits singular behavior.

## Method

The authors employed a schematic model with a quadrupole-deformed (\(Y_{20}\)) finite square-well potential, expanded to first order in the deformation parameter \(\beta_2\), without spin-orbit force. Single-particle wave functions were expanded in multipoles and solved analytically using coupled-channels equations, with bound states and Gamow resonance states treated on the same footing via the Zel'dovich regularization for normalization. The potential depth \(V_0\) was varied at fixed deformation \(\beta_2 = 0.5\), and the deformation parameter was varied at fixed potential depth \(V_0 = 45.0\) MeV and radius \(R_0 = 5.0\) fm.

## Key numbers

- For \(K^{\pi}=0^{+}\) states at \(\beta_2 = 0.5\), the resonance width follows \(\Gamma \propto \Re(E)^{l+1/2} \times \Re(N_l)|_{l=0}\) in the small positive energy region 0.1 MeV \(< \Re(E) <\) 1.0 MeV.
- No physical solution was found between potential depths \(V_0 = 41.62\) MeV and \(V_0 = 41.68\) MeV for the \(K^{\pi}=0^{+}\) state.
- For \(K^{\pi}=0^{-}\) states, no singular behavior was observed around zero energy; the width increases gradually in the small positive energy region.
- For the deformation dependence study, the potential depth was fixed at \(V_0 = 45.0\) MeV and radius at \(R_0 = 5.0\) fm, with deformation varied from \(\beta_2 = 0.0\) to 0.5.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of single-particle levels near the continuum threshold, particularly the finding by Misu (1997) and Hamamoto (2004) that the \(s\)-wave component dominates Nilsson levels with \(\Omega^{\pi}=1/2^{+}\) as binding energy approaches zero. It also builds on the Gamow state approach of Romo (1968) and the eigenphase sum method of Hagino (2004). The results clarify that the ACCC method used by Cattapan (2000) for deformed resonant levels requires caution near zero energy due to the discontinuity in the slope \(\mathrm{d}E/\mathrm{d}V_0\). The paper enables more reliable interpretation of deformed single-particle resonances in drip-line nuclei, where the Fermi level lies close to zero.
