# Role of nucleonic Fermi surface depletion in neutron star cooling

## Key claim
The Fermi surface depletion of nucleons in beta-stable nuclear matter, measured by the quasiparticle strength Z factor, significantly reduces neutrino emissivity, heat capacity, and especially neutron 3PF2 superfluidity at high baryonic density, thereby slowing the cooling rates of young neutron stars. However, the effect is conditional on the density regime: the reduction is most pronounced at high densities, and the weak superfluidity resulting from the depletion cannot explain the observed rapid cooling of the Cas A neutron star remnant via Cooper pair breaking and formation.

## Method
The authors calculated neutron and proton Z factors at the Fermi surface within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach using the AV18 two-body force supplemented by a microscopic three-body force, truncating the self-energy to fourth order in the G-matrix expansion. They then incorporated these Z factors into calculations of the neutron 3PF2 pairing gap via coupled gap equations, neutrino emissivities for direct Urca (DU), modified Urca (MU), nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung, and Cooper pair breaking and formation (PBF) processes, and nucleon heat capacity, all for beta-stable npeμ matter. Cooling curves for a canonical neutron star were computed using the publicly available NSCool code with the APR equation of state.

## Key numbers
- Neutron 3PF2 pairing gap peak value drops to 0.04 MeV when Z factor is included, compared to about one order of magnitude larger with Z=1.
- The superfluidity domain shrinks to 0.1–0.4 fm⁻³ in total baryonic density with Z factor included.
- Neutrino emissivity for the DU process is reduced by a factor of Z_F,n × Z_F,p, resulting in more than ~50% reduction.
- MU neutrino emissivity for the neutron branch is reduced by >50%, and for the proton branch by >70%.
- At high density ρ = 0.8 fm⁻³, the MU neutrino emissivity is reduced by one order of magnitude.
- The Z factor retards neutron star cooling for the first 3 × 10³ years but accelerates cooling thereafter due to the weak superfluidity.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of Z factor effects on 3PF2 superfluidity in pure neutron matter (Dong et al. 2013) to asymmetric and beta-stable nuclear matter, and generalizes the analysis to neutrino emission and heat capacity. It contrasts with the conclusion of Frankfurt et al. (2008), who argued that short-range correlations enhance DU neutrino emissivity and lower its threshold; the present work finds the opposite effect because the kinematic threshold for DU is unchanged and only thermal excitations near the Fermi surface participate. The results are relevant for interpreting observed cooling of the Cas A neutron star remnant (Heinke and Ho 2010; Posselt et al. 2013; Page et al. 2011; Shternin et al. 2011) and enable more accurate theoretical modeling of neutron star thermal evolution by incorporating Fermi surface depletion effects.
