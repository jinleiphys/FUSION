# The LPM effect in sequential bremsstrahlung 2: factorization

> The splitting processes of bremsstrahlung and pair production in a medium are coherent over large distances in the very high energy limit, which leads to a suppression known as the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect. In this paper, we continue analysis of the case when the coherence lengths of two consecutive splitting processes overlap (which is important for understanding corrections to standard treatments of the LPM effect in QCD), avoiding soft-gluon approximations. In particular, this paper analyzes the subtle problem of how to precisely separate overlapping double splitting (e.g.\ overlapping double bremsstrahlung) from the case of consecutive, independent bremsstrahlung (which is the case that would be implemented in a Monte Carlo simulation based solely on single splitting rates). As an example of the method, we consider the rate of real double gluon bremsstrahlung from an initial gluon with various simplifying assumptions (thick media; $\hat q$ approximation; large $N_c$; and neglect for the moment of processes involving 4-gluon vertices) and explicitly compute the correction $Δ\,dΓ/dx\,dy$ due to overlapping formation times.

## Key claim

The paper computes the correction Δ dΓ/dx dy to the double gluon bremsstrahlung rate due to overlapping formation times, defined as the difference between the full double-splitting rate and the rate obtained from an idealized Monte Carlo that treats consecutive splittings as independent; this correction is finite even though the individual rates diverge in an infinite medium, and its sign determines whether overlapping formation times suppress or enhance the double bremsstrahlung rate.

## Method

The authors evaluate the "sequential" interference diagrams for double gluon bremsstrahlung g → ggg in a static, uniform, infinite medium using the multiple-scattering (harmonic oscillator or q̂) approximation, large-Nc limit of QCD, and thick-medium assumption; they subtract the idealized Monte Carlo result (two consecutive independent single splittings) from the full double-splitting rate to isolate the correction Δ dΓ/dx dy, with the subtraction carefully defined to cancel contributions from well-separated splittings and leave only effects from overlapping formation times.

## Key numbers

* The correction Δ dΓ/dx dy is given in units of C_A² α_s² (q̂_A/E)^{1/2}, where C_A = N_c is the adjoint Casimir, α_s is the strong coupling, q̂_A is the medium transport coefficient for gluons, and E is the initial gluon energy.
* In the limit y ≪ x ≪ 1, the numerical result approaches Δ dΓ/dx dy ≈ -0.895 (C_A² α_s²)/(π² x y^{3/2}) √(q̂_A/E).
* The correction changes sign depending on the momentum fractions x and y: positive in some regions of the phase space (y < x < z = 1−x−y) and negative in others, with the zero line shown in the results figure.
* The result is plotted for the region y < x < z, with all other orderings equivalent by permutation of the three identical final-state gluons.

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed by two of the authors (Arnold and Iqbal, 2015) for double bremsstrahlung with overlapping formation times, which previously computed only the "crossed" interference contributions; the present paper adds the "sequential" contributions and performs the subtraction needed to define the correction to the idealized Monte Carlo picture. The analysis avoids soft-gluon approximations and generalizes earlier leading-logarithm treatments of overlapping formation times in the limit y ≪ x ≪ 1 by Blaizot, Iancu, and Wu (2013-2014). The results enable future implementation of overlapping-formation-time corrections in Monte Carlo shower simulations (such as JEWEL or MARTINI) and provide a foundation for including 4-gluon vertex contributions and virtual corrections in subsequent work.
