# First Measurement of the Ar$(e,e^\prime)X$ Cross Section at Jefferson Lab

> The success of the ambitious programs of both long- and short-baseline neutrino-oscillation experiments employing liquid-argon time-projection chambers will greatly rely on the precision with which the weak response of the argon nucleus can be estimated. In the E12-14-012 experiment at Jefferson Lab Hall A, we have studied the properties of the argon nucleus by scattering a high-quality electron beam off a high-pressure gaseous argon target. Here, we present the measured $^{40}$Ar$(e,e^{\prime})$ double differential cross section at incident electron energy $E=2.222$~GeV and scattering angle $θ= 15.541^\circ$. The data cover a broad range of energy transfers, where quasielastic scattering and delta production are the dominant reaction mechanisms. The result for argon is compared to our previously reported cross sections for titanium and carbon, obtained in the same kinematical setup.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first measurement of the argon $(e,e^\prime)$ double differential cross section at beam energy 2.222 GeV and scattering angle 15.54 degrees, covering quasielastic and delta-production regions, with the caveat that the data are compared to previously reported titanium and carbon cross sections from the same experiment to support the strategy of using titanium as a proxy for argon in neutrino oscillation studies.

## Method

The experiment used a 2.222 GeV continuous-wave electron beam from CEBAF at Jefferson Lab, scattered off a high-pressure gaseous argon target with thickness 1.455 g/cm^2 contained in a 25 cm long cell. Scattered electrons were detected in the Left High-Resolution Spectrometer (LHRS) at 15.54 degrees, equipped with superconducting magnets and a detector package for tracking, timing, and particle identification. The cross section was extracted using two independent methods: an acceptance-correction method and a yield-ratio method, with both methods showing good agreement.

## Key numbers

- Incident electron energy: E = 2.222 GeV, known with accuracy better than 0.1%
- Scattering angle: theta = 15.54 degrees
- Argon target thickness: 1.455 +/- 0.005 g/cm^2
- Total uncertainties remain below ~4.0%, with statistical uncertainties of 1.7-2.9% and systematic uncertainties of 1.8-3.0%
- Fermi momentum for argon from scaling analysis: k_F = 245 MeV
- Fermi momentum for titanium from scaling analysis: k_F = 240 MeV
- Fermi momentum for carbon from scaling analysis: k_F = 220 MeV (from Moniz et al. 1971)
- Background remaining after dummy cell subtraction: ~0.2%

## Context

This work extends the limited electron-scattering data on argon, which previously consisted of only one dataset from Frascati National Laboratory (Anghinolfi et al. 1995) at 700 MeV and 32 degrees, which showed normalization inconsistencies. The new precise measurement enables validation and improvement of theoretical models of nuclear response to electroweak interactions, such as the relativistic Green's function approach (Meucci et al. 2003), and is vital for reducing systematic uncertainties in neutrino oscillation experiments like DUNE and the Short-Baseline Neutrino program that use liquid-argon time-projection chambers.
