# Microscopic coloured quark-dynamics in the soft non-perturbative regime -- Description of hadron formation in relativistic S+Au collisions at CERN

> The quark-molecular-dynamics model is used to study microscopically the dynamics of the coloured quark phase and the subsequent hadron formation in relativistic S+Au collisions at the CERN-SPS. Particle spectra and hadron ratios are compared to both data and the results of hadronic transport calculations. The non-equilibrium dynamics of hadronization and the loss of correlation among quarks are studied.

## Key claim

The quark-molecular-dynamics (qMD) model, when coupled to the UrQMD hadronic transport model to generate initial conditions, can reproduce global observables (rapidity spectra, transverse momentum spectra, hadron ratios) for S+Au collisions at 200 GeV per nucleon at the CERN-SPS, but the missing hadronic rescattering in the qMD approach distorts the relative numbers of protons and hyperons compared to data.

## Method

The qMD model treats quarks and antiquarks as classical particles carrying fixed colour charges (red, green, blue or their anticolours) and four flavours (u, d, s, c) with current masses, interacting via a two-body Cornell potential (Coulomb plus linear confining term) with colour matrix elements derived from the diagonal gluons of the maximally Abelian gauge. The initial quark distributions are generated by running the UrQMD transport model until a critical energy density of 1 GeV/fm³ is reached (at 1.5 fm/c after the beginning of the interaction), at which point all hadrons are dissolved into their constituent quarks and newly produced quark-antiquark pairs. The classical time evolution of the quark system yields colourless clusters of three quarks with different colours or a quark-antiquark pair with opposite colours, which are then mapped onto hadronic resonance states according to energy-momentum conservation and spin/isospin quantum numbers; these resonances decay into stable hadrons without further hadronic rescattering.

## Key numbers

- Critical energy density for hadron dissolution: 1 GeV/fm³, in line with lattice calculations (Okamoto, 1999).
- Current quark masses used: m_u = m_d = 10 MeV, m_s = 150 MeV, m_c = 1.5 GeV.
- Estimated number of thermal gluons at full overlap: 88, assuming Bjorken-like expansion and T = 200 MeV, carrying approximately 10% of the entropy of the system.
- On average, only 0.1 quark-antiquark pairs per event are produced by string breakup in a S+Au collision.
- Mean diffusion length for quarks recombining from the same initial hadron: 2.2 fm (decay length for rehadronization).
- Mean decay length for quarks forming new, different hadrons via rearrangement: 4.8 fm.
- Ratio r of mixed hadrons (formed by quarks from different initial hadrons) to total hadrons: r = 0.574 ± 0.008 for S+Au at 200 GeV per nucleon.

## Context

This work extends earlier exploratory attempts using one-fluid hydrodynamics (Rischke, 1996) and three-fluid hydrodynamics (Brachmann, 1997; Brachmann, 1999) by providing the first microscopic simulation of the quark phase and hadronization in the non-perturbative regime, addressing the inapplicability of perturbative QCD at SPS energies noted by Harris (1996) and Bass (1999). The approach enables detailed study of quark rearrangement dynamics and loss of correlation during hadronization, and suggests that balance functions (Bass, 2000) could be used to observe the degree of deconfinement, though better statistics would be required.
