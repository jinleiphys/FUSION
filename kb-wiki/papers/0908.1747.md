# Jet Medium interactions at Quark Matter 09

> The suppression of the yield of high transverse momentum $p_{T}$ hadrons in heavy-ion collisions, referred to as "jet-quenching", has now developed into a comprehensive science. Jets are now used as probes of a variety of properties of the dense medium through which they propagate. Major theoretical improvements include jet modification in a 3-D fluid dynamical medium, the first set of in-medium Monte-Carlo implementations, an understanding of multi-hadron observables and energy flow within perturbative QCD, along with improvements in the AdS/CFT description of energy loss. On the experimental side, high statistics data are allowing for the first discriminatory test of various theoretical models and approximations while the new measurements of full jet reconstruction pose a challenge to theory.

## Key claim

Jet quenching has developed into a comprehensive science where jets are used as probes of the dense medium produced in heavy-ion collisions, but the abstract glosses over the fact that while pQCD-based approaches successfully explain single inclusive hadron suppression, they face major challenges in describing heavy flavor suppression and conical emission, leading to the development of alternative strong-coupling approaches.

## Method

The paper is a conference summary of the Quark Matter 2009 meeting, reviewing theoretical and experimental advances in jet-medium interactions. It compares three pQCD-based energy loss formalisms (AMY, HT, ASW) reduced to one-parameter calculations using an identical dynamical medium, and discusses new AdS/CFT approaches, Monte-Carlo implementations (Q-PYTHIA, JEWEL, YaJEM, MARTINI), and experimental data on single hadron suppression, photon-triggered yields, and full jet reconstruction from STAR and PHENIX.

## Key numbers

- Central collisions show a factor of 5 suppression of high $p_T$ hadrons up to $p_T \simeq 20$ GeV (measured in $\pi^0$)
- The transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ from the ASW scheme is a factor of 5 larger than values from HT and AMY schemes
- PHENIX reconstructed jets in Cu-Cu collisions up to 40 GeV
- STAR reconstructed jets in both p-p and Au-Au up to 50 GeV for two different cone radii
- New corrections from $J/\psi$ decay led to an approximate 15% upward shift of the $R_{AA}$ of non-photonic electrons
- The AdS/CFT approach yields a cubic length dependence of energy loss: $\Delta E \sim L^3$

## Context

This conference summary extends the work of the TECHQM collaboration and reviews the status of jet quenching since the first RHIC data at Quark Matter 2001. It compares the three major pQCD formalisms (AMY from Arnold, Moore and Yaffe; HT from higher twist expansions; ASW from Armesto, Salgado and Wiedemann) and their ability to explain data from STAR and PHENIX. The paper enables future work on full jet reconstruction, medium-modified fragmentation functions, and the resolution of the $\hat{q}$ discrepancy as a stated goal of the Theory and Experimental Collaboration for Hot QCD Matter (TECHQM).
