# Correlation of the hyperon potential stiffness with hyperon constituents in neutron stars and heavy-ion collisions

> The breaking of SU(6) symmetry to a more general flavor SU(3) symmetry could serve as a potential explanation for the "hyperon puzzle" of neutron stars by adjusting the hyperon potentials. Specifically, when the soft relativistic mean-field (RMF) $Λ$ hyperon potentials fall within the domains of chiral SU(3) interactions NLO13 with two-body forces, the maximum mass of neutron stars is expected to be lower than 2.0 $M_\odot$, whereas it can exceed $2.0M_\odot$ if the RMF $Λ$ hyperon potentials are sufficiently stiff to be consistent with those from chiral SU(3) interactions NLO13 with three-body forces. In our investigation involving these two types of $Λ$ hyperon potentials, we explore how the hyperon yields and flows are affected in heavy-ion collisions. We find that the inclusion of hyperon potentials results in better agreement of the $Λ$ directed flows with data but without clear differentiation in the stiffness of the hyperon potentials. Similarly negligent is the disparity in the rapidity distributions of the $Λ$ collective flows predicted by the stiff and soft hyperon potentials. In contrast, the $Λ$ collective flows beyond the central rapidity region turn out to be sensitive to the stiffness of the RMF equation of state (EOS) with the preference of a soft RMF EOS to a stiff one. Notably, the transverse momentum distributions of $Λ$ hyperon production are sensitive to both the stiffness of the RMF EOS and $Λ$ hyperon potential at high transverse momenta.

# Correlation of the hyperon potential stiffness with hyperon constituents in neutron stars and heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The breaking of SU(6) symmetry to flavor SU(3) symmetry can explain the "hyperon puzzle" of neutron stars, but only if the relativistic mean-field (RMF) Lambda hyperon potentials are sufficiently stiff to be consistent with chiral SU(3) interactions NLO13 including three-body forces; soft potentials consistent with only two-body forces produce maximum neutron star masses below 2.0 solar masses. In heavy-ion collisions, the transverse momentum distributions of Lambda hyperon production are sensitive to both the stiffness of the RMF equation of state and the Lambda hyperon potential at high transverse momenta, while directed and elliptic flows are sensitive primarily to the RMF equation of state stiffness rather than the hyperon potential stiffness.

## Method

The authors constructed stiff and soft Lambda hyperon potentials within the RMF approach by varying the ratio parameter z in the flavor SU(3) symmetry, with stiff potentials matching chiral SU(3) three-body force results and soft potentials matching two-body force results. They incorporated these potentials into a relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) model to simulate near-central (b=1-3 fm) Au-197 + Au-197 collisions at nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energy sqrt(s_NN)=3 GeV, and compared the calculated proton and Lambda hyperon collective flows and yields with STAR collaboration experimental data. The neutron star mass-radius trajectories were obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations using the same RMF parametrizations.

## Key numbers

- The maximum mass of neutron stars with pure nuclear matter equation of state is 2.37 solar masses for GM1, 2.12 solar masses for SOT1, and 2.03 solar masses for SOT2.
- With stiff hyperon potentials (z=0.4 in GM1, z=0.1 in SOT1, z=0 in SOT2), the neutron star maximum masses are 2.00, 2.02, and 1.97 solar masses respectively, consistent with NICER observations for J0740+6620.
- With soft hyperon potentials (z=0.8 in GM1, z=0.6 in SOT1, z=0.5 in SOT2), the neutron star maximum masses are notably lower than 2.0 solar masses and do not meet NICER observations for J0740+6620.
- The saturation properties of the GM1 parametrization include saturation density 0.153 fm^-3, binding energy -16.3 MeV, incompressibility 300 MeV, symmetry energy 32.5 MeV, and slope of symmetry energy 94.0 MeV.
- The incompressibilities of SOT1 and SOT2 are 260 MeV and 240 MeV respectively, with symmetry energies of 31.6 MeV and slopes of 87.8 MeV and 87.1 MeV.
- The Lambda hyperon potential depths at saturation density are U_Lambda(rho_0) = -30 MeV, U_Sigma(rho_0) = +30 MeV, and U_Xi(rho_0) = -14 MeV.
- Hyperons in neutron stars arise at densities exceeding 0.3 fm^-3, and hyperons are primarily produced at densities approximately 2.25 times the saturation density in heavy-ion collisions.

## Context

This work extends the flavor SU(3) symmetry approach to hyperon potentials previously applied to neutron stars (Wei and collaborators, 2021-2022) by connecting it directly to heavy-ion collision observables. It builds on the Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) model (Feng and collaborators, 2023) and earlier relativistic QMD studies that treated Lambda hyperon potentials non-relativistically. The results contradict the expectation that hyperon collective flows would clearly differentiate between stiff and soft hyperon potentials, instead showing that transverse momentum distributions at high p_T provide the discriminating signal. This enables future experimental verification at facilities such as STAR at RHIC, linking neutron star maximum mass constraints with laboratory measurements of hyperon production.
