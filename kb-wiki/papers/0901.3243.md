# Low density instabilities in asymmetric nuclear matter within QMC with $δ$-meson

> In the present work we include the isovector-scalar $δ$-meson in the quark-meson coupling model (QMC) and study the properties of asymmetric nuclear within QMC without and with the $δ$-meson. Recent constraints set by isospin diffusion on the slope parameter of the nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density are used to adjust the model parameters. The thermodynamical spinodal surfaces are obtained and the instability region at subsaturation densities within QMC and QMC$δ$ models are compared with mean-field relativistic models. The distillation effect in the QMC model is discussed.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the isovector-scalar δ-meson in the quark-meson coupling model (QMC) modifies the symmetry energy and instability region at subsaturation densities, but the effect on the QMC model is much smaller than in the non-linear Walecka model (NL3), with the QMCδ model showing a symmetry energy slope parameter L = 102.077 MeV that falls within the empirical constraint L = 88 ± 25 MeV from isospin diffusion data.

## Method

The authors extended the quark-meson coupling model by including the δ-meson (isovector-scalar) field, treating nucleons as non-overlapping MIT bags interacting through scalar (σ, δ) and vector (ω, ρ) mean fields. Model parameters were fitted to reproduce saturation properties of nuclear matter (binding energy -15.7 MeV at ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³, symmetry energy 33.7 MeV) and the symmetry energy slope constraint L = 88 ± 25 MeV. The thermodynamical spinodal surfaces were computed from the free energy curvature matrix, and the instability directions (proton-neutron density fluctuation ratios) were compared across QMC, QMCδ, NL3, NL3δ, TW, and Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) models.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³ for QMC and QMCδ models
- Binding energy per nucleon B/A = -15.7 MeV at saturation for QMC and QMCδ
- Symmetry energy at saturation: 33.7 MeV (QMC), 34.2 MeV (QMCδ)
- Symmetry energy slope L = 93.5 MeV (QMC), 102.1 MeV (QMCδ)
- Incompressibility K = 291 MeV for both QMC and QMCδ
- Effective nucleon mass ratio M*/M = 0.77 at saturation for QMC and QMCδ
- Proton and neutron effective masses at saturation in QMCδ: M_p* = 727.718 MeV, M_n* = 729.007 MeV
- Density where pressure has minimum (ρ_s): 0.098 fm⁻³ for both QMC and QMCδ
- K_asy = -570.8 MeV (QMC), -577.6 MeV (QMCδ), compared to experimental constraint K_asy = -550 ± 100 MeV
- Crossing density for proton and neutron bag radii: ~0.175 fm⁻³ for y_p=0.3, ~0.11 fm⁻³ for y_p=0.1, ~0.1 fm⁻³ for neutron matter
- Quark-meson coupling constants: g_σ^q = 5.981, g_ω = 8.954, g_ρ = 8.615 (QMC without δ), g_ρN = 12.599 and g_δ^q = 12.6 (QMCδ)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of asymmetric nuclear matter instabilities within the QMC model (Panda et al., 2005) and relativistic mean-field hadron models (Providência et al., 2004; 2006) by adding the δ-meson. The results are compared with the non-linear Walecka model NL3 (Lalazissis et al., 1997), NL3δ, the density-dependent relativistic model TW (Typel and Wolter, 1999), and microscopic BHF calculations using the Argonne V18 potential (Vidaña and Polls, 2008). The distillation effect (isospin fractionation) in QMC is intermediate between TW and NL3, and the inclusion of the δ-meson enhances the distillation. The study enables future work on finite-temperature instabilities within QMC and identification of how the density dependence of the symmetry energy determines the spinodal shape at large isospin asymmetries.
