# Short-range correlations in semi-exclusive electron scattering experiments

> One-nucleon emission electron scattering experiments are studied with a model that considers short--range correlations up to the first order in the number of correlation lines. The proper normalization of the many-body wave functions requires the evaluation of two- and three-point diagrams, the last ones usually neglected in the literature. When all these diagrams are included the effects of the short-range correlations are rather small. The results of our calculations are compared with experimental data taken on $^{16}$O.

## Key claim

Short-range correlation effects in one-nucleon emission electron scattering are extremely small when all diagrams up to first order in the number of correlation lines are included, and cannot explain the spectroscopic factors needed to reproduce experimental data, contrary to earlier claims that these correlations produce noticeable effects.

## Method

The authors model the $^{16}$O(e,e'p)$^{15}$N reaction using a Jastrow-type scalar correlation function and a cluster expansion truncated at first order in the correlation function $h_{ij}=f^2(r_{ij})-1$, retaining one-point, two-point, and three-point Mayer-like diagrams. The transition matrix elements are evaluated by multipole expansion of the one-body charge and current operators, with the nuclear ground state and final states described by Slater determinants built from Woods-Saxon single-particle wave functions, using inputs from Fermi Hypernetted Chain (FHNC) calculations with the S3 and V8' nucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Key numbers

- The calculations are performed at fixed energy transfer $\omega = 128$ MeV and fixed emitted proton momentum $|\mathbf{p}| = 444$ MeV/c, for protons knocked out from the $1p_{1/2}$ level in $^{16}$O.
- The Woods-Saxon potential depth for protons and neutrons is $V_0 = -53.0$ MeV (V8 input) or $V_0 = -52.5$ MeV (S3 input), with radius $R = 3.45$ fm (V8) or $3.2$ fm (S3) and diffuseness $a = 0.7$ fm (V8) or $0.53$ fm (S3).
- The spin-orbit strength is $V_{ls} = 0.0$ MeV for V8 and $V_{ls} = -7.0$ MeV (protons) or $-6.54$ MeV (neutrons) for S3.
- The normalized differences between correlated and uncorrelated cross sections (divided by the maximum uncorrelated cross section) are shown for both perpendicular and parallel kinematics; the two-point diagram effect is positive for S3 and negative for V8, but the full calculation including three-point diagrams yields effects that are "rather small" and "extremely small."

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous model for inclusive responses (Co' et al., 2000) to semi-exclusive (e,e'p) reactions, using the same FHNC ground-state inputs from Arias de Saavedra et al. (1996) and Fabrocini et al. (2000). It contradicts earlier calculations by Giusti et al. (1994), Ryckebusch et al. (1995), and Jaminon et al. (2000) that found noticeable correlation effects using simplified treatments, and it addresses the inconsistency in those calculations where single-particle wave functions and correlations were not linked by a unique nuclear Hamiltonian and proper wave-function normalization was not preserved. The results enable future work to focus on other sources of the spectroscopic factor, such as relativistic effects or in-medium modifications of nucleon properties.
