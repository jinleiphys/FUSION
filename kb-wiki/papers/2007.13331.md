# Trojan horse method as surrogate indirect approach to study resonant reactions in nuclear astrophysics

> We review the Trojan horse method and its applications to resonant reactions in nuclear astrophysics.

## Key claim
The Trojan horse method (THM) can extract astrophysical factors for resonant rearrangement and radiative-capture reactions at energies too low for direct measurement, but the standard plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) fails when the subreaction energy \(E_{xA}\) is significantly smaller than the binding energy \(\varepsilon_{sx}\) of the Trojan horse particle, because the transferred particle \(x\) remains off-the-energy-shell and Coulomb rescattering cannot be neglected.

## Method
The authors develop a few-body formalism for the two-step THM resonant reaction \(a + A \to s + F^* \to s + b + B\), where the first step is a transfer reaction populating an isolated resonance \(F^*\) in the \(x+A\) system. They split the prior-form distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) amplitude into internal (\(r_{xA} \leq R_{ch}\)) and external (\(r_{xA} > R_{ch}\)) parts, then transform the internal volume integral into a surface integral using the \(R\)-matrix formalism, expressing the amplitude in terms of the observable resonance width, boundary condition, and channel radius. The external part is evaluated using the Coulomb asymptotic form of the resonant wave function.

## Key numbers
- The quasi-free (QF) condition \(p_{sx}=0\) yields \(E_{xA} = p_{xA(0)}^2/(2\mu_{xA}) - \varepsilon_{sx}\), where \(\varepsilon_{sx}\) is the binding energy of the Trojan horse particle \(a=(sx)\).
- For charged particles \(s\) and \(x\), the bound-state wave function in momentum space has a branching-point singularity: \(\varphi_a(\mathbf{p}_{sx}) \propto [p_{sx}^2 + \kappa_{sx}^2]^{\eta_{sx}-1}\), with Coulomb parameter \(\eta_{ij} = Z_i Z_j e^2 \mu_{ij} / \kappa_{ij}\).
- The external prior-form DWBA amplitude is expressed as \(\mathcal{M}_{\text{ext}}^{\text{DW(prior)}} = e^{i\delta^p_{s_{xA}l_{xA}J_F}(k_{0(xA)})} \sqrt{\mu_{xA} \Gamma_{(xA)} / k_{0(xA)}} \times \langle \Psi_{\mathbf{k}_{sF}}^{C(-)} O^*(k_{0(xA)}, r_{xA})/r_{xA} | V_{sA}^C + V_{xA}^C - U_{aA}^C | \varphi_a \Psi_{\mathbf{k}_{aA}}^{C(+)} \rangle |_{r_{xA} > R_{ch}}\).

## Context
This review extends the THM formalism beyond the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) originally suggested by Baur (1986) and developed experimentally by Spitaleri and collaborators. It addresses the shortcomings of PWIA when \(E_{xA} \ll \varepsilon_{sx}\) and when Coulomb interactions between charged particles are significant. The surface-integral approach, combined with \(R\)-matrix theory, provides a consistent framework for analyzing resonant reactions such as \({}^{12}\text{C}+{}^{12}\text{C}\) fusion, \({}^{13}\text{C}(\alpha,n){}^{16}\text{O}\) (the neutron source in low-mass AGB stars), and \({}^{12}\text{C}(\alpha,\gamma){}^{16}\text{O}\) via the indirect reaction \({}^{12}\text{C}({}^{6}\text{Li},d\gamma){}^{16}\text{O}\).
