# Novel Rosenbluth Extraction Framework for Compton Form Factors from Deeply Virtual Exclusive Experiments

> We use a generalization of the Rosenbluth separation method for a model independent simultaneous extraction of the Compton Form Factors ${\cal H}$ and ${\cal E}$, from virtual Compton scattering data on an unpolarized target. A precise evaluation of ${\cal H}$ and ${\cal E}$, enabled by the proposed method, is the first step towards pinning down the the distribution of angular momentum inside the proton.

## Key claim

The authors propose a generalization of the Rosenbluth separation method to simultaneously extract the Compton Form Factors (CFFs) ℋ and ℰ from unpolarized deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) data, arguing that previous extractions were hindered by an overly complicated harmonic expansion formalism that obscured the underlying electric, magnetic, and axial current structure of the cross section.

## Method

The authors re-express the Bethe-Heitler (BH) and BH-DVCS interference cross sections in terms of generalized electric, magnetic, and axial form factors, analogous to the Rosenbluth separation used in elastic electron-proton scattering. They extract the interference contribution σ_𝒰𝒰^ℐ from total cross section data by subtracting the exactly calculable BH term and evaluating the pure DVCS term from the intercept at φ=90° and φ=270°. A linear fit of the reduced interference cross section plotted against the ratio of kinematic coefficients A^ℐ/B^ℐ yields the CFFs ℋ and ℰ simultaneously from unpolarized data.

## Key numbers

- Data from kinematic bin: x_Bj = 0.343, t = -0.172 GeV², Q² = 1.82 GeV² (Defurne 2015)
- CFFs extracted at fixed Q² = 2 GeV², ε₁ = 6 GeV, x_Bj = 0.36
- Additional kinematic bins from Georges (2018): x_Bj = 0.36, t = -0.35 GeV²; x_Bj = 0.45, t = -0.47 GeV²; x_Bj = 0.60, t = -1.06 GeV²
- The coefficient C^ℐ of the axial vector CFF ℋ̃ is negligibly small compared to other coefficients
- Twist-three coefficients A^(3)ℐ, B^(3)ℐ, and C^(3)ℐ are small and comparable in size to the axial vector coefficient C^ℐ

## Context

This work extends the Rosenbluth separation technique, originally developed for elastic electron-proton scattering (Rosenbluth 1950), to DVCS by building on the helicity amplitudes formalism of Diehl (2005) and the coincidence scattering description of Arens (1996) and Vanderhaeghen (1998). The authors' previous work (Kriesten 2019, 2020) introduced the framework of generalized electric, magnetic, and axial form factors. The results are consistent with prior extractions of Re ℋ from Moutarde (2009) and Guidal (2008), and of Im ℋ from Dupre (2017), while providing the first extraction of Re ℰ and Im ℰ from data. The Q² dependence of the extracted CFFs is consistent with leading-order perturbative QCD evolution predictions from Ji (1996) and Golec-Biernat (1998), contrary to claims in Defurne (2017) about significant twist-three effects. This method enables future precise determinations of all CFFs by refining kinematic coverage for linear Rosenbluth plots.
