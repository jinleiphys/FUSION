# Gauge Field Fluctuations and First-Order Phase Transition in Color Superconductivity

> We study the gauge field fluctuations in dense quark matter and determine the temperature of the induced first-order phase transition to the color-superconducting phase in weak coupling. We find that the local approximation of the coupling between the gauge potential and the order parameter, employed in the Ginzburg-Landau theory, has to be modified by restoring the full momentum dependence of the polarization function of gluons in the superconducting phase.

## Key claim

The gauge field fluctuations in dense quark matter induce a first-order phase transition to the color-superconducting phase at a temperature \(T_c^*\) above the pairing instability temperature \(T_c\), but only when the full momentum dependence of the gluon polarization function is restored; the local coupling approximation used in prior Ginzburg-Landau treatments breaks down for color superconductivity, unlike for metallic superconductors.

## Method

The authors used the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) formalism to compute the effective potential for a homogeneous color-flavor locked (CFL) superconductor, treating the full gluon and quark propagators as variational parameters. They separated the free energy into a condensation term (giving the Ginzburg-Landau expansion in the gap \(\Delta\)) and a fluctuation term \(\Gamma_{fluc}\) that retains the momentum-dependent magnetic gluon mass \(m_A^2(k)\) rather than approximating it by a constant. The transition temperature \(T_c^*\) and the gap at the transition were then determined by solving the simultaneous equations \(\gamma(t^*,\Delta)=0\) and \(\partial\gamma/\partial\Delta^2=0\) for the free energy density \(\gamma\).

## Key numbers

* The relative shift of the transition temperature in weak coupling is \(\frac{T_c^*-T_c}{T_c} = \frac{\pi^2}{12\sqrt{2}}\, g \simeq 0.58\, g\) (Eq. 2).
* At the transition, the gap satisfies \(\left(\frac{\Delta}{k_BT_c}\right)^2 = \frac{\pi^2}{63\zeta(3)}g^2\) (Eq. 12).
* For an extrapolation to \(\mu = 500\) MeV with \(g = 3.1\) (one-loop running, \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}=200\) MeV), the estimate gives \((T_c^*-T_c)/T_c \simeq 1.8\).
* The critical temperature from the pairing instability is given by \(\ln\frac{k_BT_c}{\mu} = -\frac{3\pi^2}{\sqrt{2}g} + \ln\frac{2048\sqrt{2}\pi^3}{9\sqrt{3}g^5} + \gamma - \frac{\pi^2+4}{8} + O(g)\) (Eq. 1).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of gauge-field fluctuations in color superconductivity beyond the local-coupling approximation used by Bailin and Love (1984) and by Halperin, Lubensky, and Ma (1974) for metallic superconductors. It corrects the overestimate of \(T_c^*\) in those earlier treatments and also goes beyond the truncated-momentum approach of Baym et al. (2003), finding a stronger first-order transition than they reported. The result enables quantitative predictions for the phase transition at moderate baryon densities relevant to neutron-star interiors, and it shows that the fluctuation-induced first-order transition is not limited to three-flavor pairing.
