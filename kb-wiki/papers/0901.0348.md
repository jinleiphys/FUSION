# Induced P-wave superfluidity within full energy-momentum dependent Eliashberg approximation in asymmetric dilute Fermi gases

> We consider a very asymmetric system of Fermions with an interaction characterized by a positive scattering length only. The minority atoms pair and form a BEC of dimers, while the surplus fermions interact only indirectly through the exchange of Bogoliubov sound modes. This interaction has a finite range, the retardation effects are significant and the surplus fermions will form a P-wave superfluid. We compute the P-wave pairing gap in the BCS and Eliashberg with only energy dependence approximations, and demonstrate their inadequacy in comparison with a full treatment of the momentum and energy dependence of the induced interaction. The pairing gap computed with a full momentum and energy dependence is significantly larger in magnitude and that makes it more likely that this new exotic paired phase could be put in evidence in atomic trap experiments.

## Key claim

The induced P-wave pairing gap in asymmetric dilute Fermi gases, computed with full momentum and energy dependence of the induced interaction, is significantly larger than the gap obtained from either the BCS approximation or the simple Eliashberg approximation that includes only energy dependence, making this exotic paired phase more likely to be observable in atomic trap experiments than previously thought.

## Method

The authors solve the Dyson equation for the full fermion propagator with a self-energy that includes wavefunction renormalization, Hartree-Fock potential, and pairing field, retaining both momentum and energy dependence. They perform a Wick rotation to imaginary frequencies and use Fast Fourier Transform techniques to solve the self-consistent equations iteratively, comparing results to the simpler BCS and energy-only Eliashberg approximations. The induced interaction between surplus fermions is mediated by Bogoliubov sound modes of the BEC of dimers formed by minority atoms, with the form derived by Bardeen, Baym, and Pines (1967).

- The pairing gap is computed for ratios of dimer to surplus fermion densities \(y = n_b/n_f = 2, 1, 0.5\), shown in Fig. 1.
- The dimensionless strength of the induced interaction is \(\lambda = \frac{mk_fn_b U_{fb}^2}{2\pi^2\varepsilon_{F}} \sim \frac{n_b}{n_f}(k_f a)^2\).
- The ratio of sound speed to Fermi velocity is \(s = \frac{2 n_b U_{bb}}{\varepsilon_f} \sim \frac{c^2}{v_f^2} \sim \frac{n_b}{n_f}(k_f a)\).
- The Debye wave vector is defined as \(k_D = \pi n_b^{1/3}\), and the cutoff for exchanged energies is \(|\omega| \le \omega_D = c\hbar k_D\).
- The pairing gap \(\Delta(p)\) is given in units of the Fermi energy of surplus fermions \(\varepsilon_f = \hbar^2k_f^2/(2m)\), where \(n_f = k_f^3/(6\pi^2)\).
- The simple Eliashberg approximation underestimates the pairing gap by "sometimes close to an order of magnitude" compared to the full calculation.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Bulgac, Forbes, and Schwenk (2008) and Bulgac, Bedaque, and Forbes (2006), who first proposed P-wave superfluidity in asymmetric Fermi gases on the BEC side of a Feshbach resonance. It contradicts the assumption that BCS or simple Eliashberg approximations are adequate for these systems, showing that both momentum and energy dependence of the induced interaction are crucial. The results enable experimental searches for this coexisting bosonic-fermionic superfluid phase in atomic traps, particularly near the Feshbach resonance where the pairing gap becomes comparable to the Fermi energy of surplus fermions.
