# Non-equilibrium Chiral Magnetic/Vortical Effects in Viscous Fluids

> We utilize the chiral kinetic theory in a relaxation-time approximation to investigate the nonlinear anomalous responses of chiral fluids with viscous effects. Unlike the cases in equilibrium, it is found that the chiral magnetic effect and chiral vortical effect are modified by the shear and bulk strengths. Particularly, the shear strength could result in charged Hall currents for chiral magnetic and chiral vortical effects, which propagate perpendicular to applied magnetic fields and vorticity. These quantum corrections stemming from side jumps and anomalies are dissipative and pertinent to interactions. Although the non-equilibrium effects upon charge currents are dissipative, the second law of thermodynamics is still satisfied.

## Key claim

The chiral magnetic effect (CME) and chiral vortical effect (CVE) conductivities are modified by shear and bulk viscous strengths in non-equilibrium chiral fluids, with the shear strength producing charged Hall currents that propagate perpendicular to applied magnetic fields and vorticity; these corrections are dissipative but the second law of thermodynamics remains satisfied.

## Method

The authors used chiral kinetic theory (CKT) within a relaxation-time approximation (RTA) to perturbatively solve for the non-equilibrium distribution function of Weyl fermions, expanding in both the Planck constant (hbar) and derivatives. They computed the quantum corrections to the charge current by incorporating viscous corrections from shear and bulk strengths, and applied hydrodynamic equations of motion (EOM) derived from charge and energy-momentum conservation with the chiral anomaly to obtain the final transport coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The viscous correction to the CME conductivity tensor in the high-temperature limit (mu-bar much less than 1) is: delta-sigma_B^{munu} = hbar tau_R mu (5 P^{munu} / (18 pi^2) theta - pi^{munu} / (14 pi^2))
- The viscous correction to the CVE conductivity tensor in the high-temperature limit is: delta-sigma_omega^{munu} = -hbar tau_R T^2 [P^{munu} / 108 theta (1 + 111 mu-bar^2 / (7 pi^2)) - 5 pi^{munu} / 27 (1 - 6 mu-bar^2 / (7 pi^2))]
- In the low-temperature limit (mu-bar much greater than 1), the CME correction becomes: delta-sigma_B^{munu} = hbar tau_R mu (5 P^{munu} / (18 pi^2) theta - pi^{munu} / (6 mu-bar^2))
- In the low-temperature limit, the CVE correction becomes: delta-sigma_omega^{munu} = -hbar tau_R mu^2 (P^{munu} / (12 pi^2) theta - 2 pi^{munu} / (9 pi^2))
- The coefficient I_1 appearing in the CVE correction is defined as I_1 = mu-bar^2 + pi^2 / 3

## Context

This work extends the inviscid (ideal fluid) analysis of non-equilibrium anomalous transport by Hidaka and Yang (2017) to include viscous effects, and complements studies of chiral kinetic theory with collisions by Chen et al. (2014, 2015) and Hidaka et al. (2016). The results enable quantitative predictions for charge separation signals in heavy-ion collisions and for hydrodynamic transport in Weyl semimetals, where viscous corrections to CME and CVE conductivities may become observable. The approach is valid for weakly coupled systems with small gradients (tau_R times derivative much less than 1) and weak background fields.
