# Three-body interactions from the finite-volume QCD spectrum

> We perform a fit of the finite-volume QCD spectrum of three pions at maximal isospin to constrain the three-body force. We use the unitarity-based relativistic three-particle quantization condition, with the GWUQCD spectrum obtained at 315 MeV and 220 MeV pion mass in two-flavor QCD. For the heavier pion mass we find that the data is consistent with a constant contact term close to zero, whereas for the lighter mass we see a statistically significant energy dependence in tension with the prediction of leading order ChPT. Our results also suggest that with enough three-body energy levels, the two-body amplitude could be constrained.

## Key claim

The authors perform a fit of the finite-volume QCD spectrum of three pions at maximal isospin to constrain the three-body force, finding that for the heavier pion mass (315 MeV) the data is consistent with a constant contact term close to zero, whereas for the lighter mass (220 MeV) they observe a statistically significant energy dependence in tension with the prediction of leading order Chiral Perturbation Theory.

## Method

The authors use the unitarity-based relativistic three-particle quantization condition (FVU formalism) to relate the finite-volume spectrum from lattice QCD to infinite-volume scattering amplitudes. They implement the modified Inverse Amplitude Method (mIAM) to parametrize the two-body pi-pi scattering input, and explore various parametrizations of the three-body contact term C0, fitting these to 30 energy levels below 5m_pi extracted from six GWUQCD ensembles at two pion masses (315 MeV and 220 MeV) with different box geometries and boosts.

## Key numbers

- Pion masses used: m_pi approximately 315 MeV (ensembles E1, E2, E3) and 220 MeV (ensembles E4, E5, E6)
- Lattice spacing: 0.1210(2)(24) fm for ensembles E1-E3, 0.1215(3)(24) fm for ensembles E4-E6
- Number of Monte Carlo configurations: 300 for ensembles E1-E3, 400 for ensembles E4-E6
- Total of 30 energy levels below 5m_pi across all six ensembles used in the analysis
- For ensemble E3 (elongated volume, eta=2.00), 16 energy levels were extracted below the inelastic threshold, compared to 3 in the cubic volume at the same pion mass
- Previous fit to NPLQCD data gave c0 = (0.3 plus or minus 2.3) times 10 to the minus 6 MeV to the minus 2

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of three-pion systems using the FVU formalism, including the first determination of the three-body force from NPLQCD data by Mai (2018) which found the three-body force consistent with zero, and later work by Blanton (2019) using the RFT formalism which found a non-zero three-body force. The study addresses a discrepancy where FVU predictions with vanishing three-body term showed chi-squared per degree of freedom of approximately 2.68 when compared to GWUQCD data from Culver (2019). The results suggest that with enough three-body energy levels, the two-body amplitude could be constrained from three-body data alone.
