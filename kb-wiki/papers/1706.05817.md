# Proxy-SU(3) symmetry in heavy nuclei: Foundations

> An approximate SU(3) symmetry appears in heavy deformed even-even nuclei, by omitting the intruder Nilsson orbital of highest total angular momentum and replacing the rest of the intruder orbitals by the orbitals which have escaped to the next lower major shell. The approximation is based on the fact that there is a one-to-one correspondence between the orbitals of the two sets, based on pairs of orbitals having identical quantum numbers of orbital angular momentum, spin, and total angular momentum. The accuracy of the approximation is tested through calculations in the framework of the Nilsson model in the asymptotic limit of large deformations, focusing attention on the changes in selection rules and in avoided crossings caused by the opposite parity of the proxies with respect to the substituted orbitals.

## Key claim

The proxy-SU(3) approximation, which replaces intruder Nilsson orbitals (except the one with highest total angular momentum) by orbitals from the next lower major shell, produces Nilsson diagrams for the 126-184 shell that are very similar to those of its proxy-SU(3) sdgi counterpart, provided the extra non-vanishing matrix elements introduced by the approximation are much smaller than the diagonal matrix elements (by at least one order of magnitude) after multiplication by the small spin-orbit and angular-momentum-squared coefficients.

## Method

The authors tested the proxy-SU(3) approximation by calculating matrix elements of the spin-orbit (l·s) and orbital angular-momentum-squared (l²) operators, and of the full Nilsson Hamiltonian, for both the original 126-184 neutron shell and its proxy-SU(3) sdgi counterpart. They compared the diagonal and non-diagonal matrix elements in tabular form and plotted the resulting Nilsson diagrams as functions of the deformation parameter ε, using the Nilsson model in the asymptotic limit of large deformations with parameters from Bohr and Mottelson (1975).

## Context

This work extends the proxy-SU(3) scheme introduced in earlier papers by Bonatsos and collaborators (2015, 2016) and described in Bonatsos et al. (2017). It provides the Nilsson-model justification for the approximation, which was previously applied to medium-mass and heavy deformed nuclei. The results enable future applications of the proxy-SU(3) scheme for calculating nuclear properties, as will be discussed in Martinou et al. (2017) and Sarantopoulou et al. (2017). The same approach has also been shown to work for the 28-50, 50-82, and 82-126 shells compared to their pf, sdg, and pfh proxy-SU(3) counterparts.
