# Relativistic fluid dynamics in a 'hydro' frame

> In this letter, we investigate how field redefinition influences the spectrum of linearized perturbations in relativistic fluid dynamics. We show that the hydrodynamic modes do not get affected under local field redefinition, whereas the non-hydrodynamic modes do. These non-hydrodynamic modes can be removed through a suitable all-order field redefinition. This process leads to a new frame containing only hydrodynamic modes, which we refer to as the hydro frame. Additionally, we demonstrate that the resulting stress-energy tensor may constitute an infinite series in momentum space, with the radius of convergence associated with the removed non-hydrodynamic mode, highlighting its role in the hydrodynamic expansion's validity.

## Key claim

The authors show that non-hydrodynamic modes in relativistic fluid dynamics can be removed through an all-order field redefinition, producing a "hydro frame" containing only hydrodynamic modes; however, the resulting stress-energy tensor typically becomes an infinite series whose radius of convergence is determined by the removed non-hydrodynamic mode, meaning the physical significance of the original non-hydrodynamic modes depends on whether they can be fully eliminated by such a transformation.

## Method

The authors analyze how field redefinitions of fluid variables (velocity and temperature) transform the linearized perturbation spectrum around a rotationally and translationally invariant equilibrium background. They derive the general form of the frame transformation matrix S relating perturbations in two different frames, showing that the dispersion polynomial in the new frame equals the original polynomial multiplied by a factor Det[1+S] that generates additional non-hydrodynamic modes. They then invert this procedure, constructing an all-order field redefinition that eliminates all non-hydrodynamic modes from the spectrum while preserving the hydrodynamic frequencies and eigenvectors, and apply this algorithm to the BDNK theory of relativistic fluids.

## Key numbers

- The shear channel non-hydrodynamic mode frequency is given by w_sh = -i/theta + i lambda k^2, where theta and lambda are transport coefficients
- The sound channel non-hydrodynamic modes are w_snd^(1) = -i/theta + (i/3)[4 lambda - theta chi/(theta - chi)] k^2 and w_snd^(2) = -i/chi + (i/3)(theta chi/(theta - chi)) k^2
- The sound channel hydrodynamic modes are omega_snd^(1) = k/sqrt(3) - (2i lambda/3) k^2 - (2 lambda^2/(3 sqrt(3))) k^3 and omega_snd^(2) = -k/sqrt(3) - (2i lambda/3) k^2 + (2 lambda^2/(3 sqrt(3))) k^3
- The shear channel hydrodynamic mode is omega_sh = -i lambda k^2

## Context

This work extends the analysis of field redefinitions in relativistic hydrodynamics, building on the BDNK formalism (Bemfica 2017, Kovtun 2019) which leaves the fluid frame unfixed to achieve causality and stability at first order. The authors provide a systematic method to distinguish physical non-hydrodynamic modes from frame artefacts, complementing their companion study (Bhattacharyya 2024) on how non-hydrodynamic modes control the validity of hydrodynamic expansions. This enables a clearer understanding of which fluid models are physically meaningful under arbitrary field redefinitions, addressing questions raised by earlier work on causality and stability in relativistic hydrodynamics (Hiscock 1985, Geroch 1990).
