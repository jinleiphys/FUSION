# Towards modeling cluster structure of $^8$Be with chiral interaction

> How the nuclear force behaves in cluster states, in particular those consisting of the $α$ clusters, has been investigated so far, but not yet elucidated. Today the chiral effective field theory is established and it would shed new light on the microscopic understanding of the cluster states. We aim to address a possible source of the attraction in the cluster states of $^8\mathrm{Be}$ in view of the pion exchange. Namely, we investigate whether the two-pion-exchange interaction acts as a dominant attraction in the $α+α$ system as predicted by a previous work. We describe theoretically the cluster structure of $^8\mathrm{Be}$ by the Brink model, for which the effective interaction is designed from the realistic nuclear force derived through the chiral effective field theory. The two-body matrix elements of the chiral interaction with the local-Gaussian bases are formulated within the approximation of the spin-isospin saturation forming an $α$ particle. Introducing a global prefactor to the chiral interaction phenomenologically, the ground and low-lying excited states of $^8\mathrm{Be}$, the scattering phase shift of the $α$-$α$ system as well, are satisfactorily depicted. The attraction in the cluster states is found to be stemming from the two-pion-exchange contributions dominantly, along with nonnegligible short-range terms. The present work can be the foundation towards constructing realistic cluster models, by which the cluster states will be revealed microscopically in the next step.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether the two-pion-exchange interaction acts as the dominant source of attraction in the alpha-alpha system of 8Be, using a phenomenological prefactor applied globally to the chiral interaction rather than a fully microscopic derivation, and finds that the two-pion-exchange contributions are indeed dominant but short-range contact terms also provide nonnegligible attraction.

## Method

The cluster structure of 8Be was described using the Brink model with spin-isospin-saturated alpha clusters, where the effective nuclear interaction was constructed by taking the chiral effective field theory interaction at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) and multiplying it by a global prefactor c0. The two-body matrix elements of the chiral interaction were formulated within local-Gaussian bases under the approximation of spin-isospin saturation, and the generator coordinate method (GCM) was used to superpose Slater determinants with different alpha-alpha distances to obtain the ground and excited states and scattering phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- The prefactor c0 was fixed to 1.37, 1.79, and 2.15 for cutoffs Lambda = 450, 500, and 600 MeV, respectively, by matching the calculated relative energy of the 8Be ground state to the experimental value.
- The calculated ground-state energy of 4He was -33.0397 MeV (Lambda=450 MeV), -34.3761 MeV (Lambda=500 MeV), and -58.6637 MeV (Lambda=600 MeV), compared to the experimental value of -28.2957 MeV.
- The calculated 0+ energy of 8Be relative to the alpha-alpha threshold was 0.135 MeV (Lambda=450 MeV), 0.146 MeV (Lambda=500 MeV), and 0.050 MeV (Lambda=600 MeV), consistent with the measured value.
- The root-mean-square matter radius of 8Be(0+) from the GCM was 2.71 fm (Lambda=450 MeV), 2.76 fm (Lambda=500 MeV), and 2.79 fm (Lambda=600 MeV).
- The size parameter nu = 0.26 fm^{-2} was chosen, corresponding to harmonic-oscillator energy hbar-omega approximately 22 MeV, giving a 4He matter radius of 1.47 fm compared to the experimental 1.45 fm.

## Context

This work extends the assumption of Ref. (1962) that two-pion-exchange interaction is the source of attraction in alpha-cluster states of 8Be, which had not been confirmed previously. It provides a foundation for constructing realistic cluster models such as the Brueckner generator coordinate method, where the two-body matrix elements of the chiral interaction formulated here can be applied. The results are qualitatively comparable to those using phenomenological Volkov-No.2 and Tohsaki-F1 interactions, and the phase shifts are consistent with those obtained using the Hasegawa-Nagata potential.
