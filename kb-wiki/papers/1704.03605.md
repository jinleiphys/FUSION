# Two-photon exchange corrections to $γ^*NΔ$ form factors for $Q^2\leq 4 $ (Gev/$c$)$^2$

> We evaluate the corrections of the two-photon exchange (TPE) process on the $γ^* NΔ$ transition form factors. The contributions of the TPE process to the $eN\rightarrow eΔ(1232)\rightarrow eNπ$ are calculated in a hadronic model with the inclusion of only the elastic nucleon intermediate states, to estimate its effects on the multipoles $M^{(3/2)}_{1^+}, E^{(3/2)}_{1^+}, S^{(3/2)}_{1^+}$ at the $Δ$ peak. We find that TPE effects on $G^*_M$ is very small. $G^*_E,$ and $ G^*_C$ are also little affected at small $Q^2 $. For $G^*_E$, the TPE effects reach about $3 - 8\%$ near $Q^2\sim 4$ GeV$^2$, depending on the model, MAID or SAID, used to emulate the data. For $G^*_C$, the TPE effects decrease rapidly with increasing $ε$ while growing with increasing $Q^2$ to reach $\sim 6 - 15\%$ with $Q^2 \sim 4$ GeV$^2$ at $ε= 0.2$. Sizeable TPE corrections to $G^*_E$ and $G^*_C$ found here points to the need of including TPE effects in the multipole analysis in the region of high $Q^2$ and small $ε$. The TPE corrections to $R_{EM}$ and $R_{SM}$ obtained in our hadronic calculation are compared with those obtained in a partonic calculation for moderate momentum transfer of $2<Q^2<4$ GeV$^2$.

# Two-photon exchange corrections to γ*NΔ form factors for Q² ≤ 4 (GeV/c)²

## Key claim
Two-photon exchange (TPE) corrections to the γ*NΔ transition form factors G*_E and G*_C become sizeable (3-15%) at high Q² (~4 GeV²) and small ε, but only when the experimental data are emulated using either the MAID or SAID phenomenological model; the corrections depend strongly on which model is used and on the kinematic variable ε.

## Method
The authors performed a hadronic calculation of TPE corrections to the process eN → eΔ(1232) → eNπ, including only elastic nucleon intermediate states in the TPE box and cross-box diagrams plus a contact term required by gauge invariance. They emulated experimental pion electroproduction cross sections using the MAID and SAID models, then subtracted the OPE-TPE interference term iteratively to extract "genuine" OPE multipoles M_{1+}^{(3/2)}, E_{1+}^{(3/2)}, S_{1+}^{(3/2)} at the Δ peak via a χ² minimization procedure. The form factors G*_M, G*_E, G*_C were obtained from these multipoles for 0 < Q² < 4 GeV².

## Key numbers
- TPE effects on G*_E reach about 3% (using MAID) and 8% (using SAID) near Q² ~ 4 GeV², with mild ε dependence.
- TPE effects on G*_C reach about 15% (MAID) and 6% (SAID) at Q² ~ 4 GeV² and ε = 0.2.
- TPE corrections to G*_C decrease from ~7.5% (SAID) and ~15% (MAID) near ε = 0 to about 2% as ε approaches 0.9, at Q² = 2.8 GeV².
- TPE corrections to δR_SM reach about -1.4% (ε = 0.2) and -0.7% (ε = 0.5) at Q² = 4 GeV².
- TPE corrections to δR_EM reach about -0.1% (MAID) and -0.2% (SAID) at Q² = 4 GeV².
- At small Q² = 0.127 GeV², TPE corrections to both G*_E and G*_C are less than 1% for all ε.

## Context
This work extends the hadronic approach to TPE corrections previously applied to elastic ep scattering by Blunden (2003) to the inelastic N→Δ transition. It contrasts with the partonic calculation by Pascalutsa (2006), which used NΔ generalized parton distributions and found much smaller TPE corrections (0.2-0.6%) for 2 < Q² < 4 GeV². The authors find that their hadronic results for δR_SM are substantially more negative than the partonic results, while δR_EM results are comparable only at ε = 0.5 with MAID. The study motivates new precision measurements of ep → eΔ → epπ⁰ at 2 < Q² < 4 GeV² at multiple ε values to detect the predicted ε-dependence, and calls for future inclusion of higher resonances (e.g., Δ intermediate states) in the TPE calculation.
