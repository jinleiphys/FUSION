# Proton mass decomposition: naturalness and interpretations

> I discuss the scope and naturalness of the proton mass decomposition (or sum rule) published in PRL74, 1071 (1995) and answer a few criticisms that appeared recently in the literature, focusing particularly on its interpretation and the quantum anomalous energy contribution. I comment on the so-called frame-independent or invariant-mass decomposition from the trace of the energy-momentum tensor. I stress the importance of measuring the quantum anomalous energy through experiments. Finally, I point out a large discrepancy in the scalar radius of the nucleon extracted from vector-meson productions and lattice QCD calculations.

## Key claim

The proton mass decomposition originally published in PRL74, 1071 (1995) is natural and physically meaningful, with the quantum anomalous energy contribution being an unambiguous physical quantity that can be calculated on the lattice and determined experimentally, contrary to recent criticisms that have questioned its interpretation.

## Method

The author derives the proton mass decomposition by expressing the QCD Hamiltonian as the integral of the energy-momentum tensor's 00-component, then separating the tensor into traceless quark and gluon parts plus a trace part. The traceless parts yield contributions proportional to momentum fractions measured in deep inelastic scattering, while the trace part yields the quantum anomalous energy contribution from scale symmetry breaking, with the full decomposition containing four terms when quark masses are included.

## Key numbers

- Quark kinetic/kinetic energy contribution: \(M_q(\mu) = \frac{3}{4}M \langle x_q \rangle(\mu)\), where \(\langle x_q \rangle\) is the quark momentum fraction
- Gluon energy contribution: \(M_g(\mu) = \frac{3}{4}M \langle x_g \rangle(\mu)\), where \(\langle x_g \rangle\) is the gluon momentum fraction
- Quantum anomalous energy contribution: \(M_a = \frac{1}{4}M\), scale-independent in the chiral limit
- State-of-art verification of the sum rule is at the 10% level, with largest error from the anomalous energy calculation
- Heavy charm quark contribution to proton mass: \(\langle P|m_c \bar cc|P\rangle\) is on the order of 100 MeV, with anomaly cancellation leaving approximately 35 MeV total
- Pion mass decomposition: quantum anomalous energy contributes \(m_\pi/8\), gluon energy contributes approximately \(3m_\pi/8\), and quark kinetic energy is approximately 0

## Context

This paper defends the original decomposition by Ji (1995) against recent criticisms, particularly from Lorce (2017) who advocated a fluid-model interpretation and from Metz (2020) who used dimensional regularization. The work extends the understanding of proton mass structure by clarifying that the trace anomaly relation \(\langle P|T^\alpha_\alpha|P\rangle = 2M^2\) (Shifman, 1978) is not a natural frame-independent mass decomposition but rather expresses scale symmetry breaking effects. The paper enables experimental programs at Jefferson Lab 12 GeV and the future Electron-Ion Collider to measure the quantum anomalous energy through processes like threshold \(J/\psi\) production and deeply virtual Compton scattering, while also highlighting a large discrepancy in the scalar radius of the nucleon extracted from vector-meson productions versus lattice QCD calculations.
