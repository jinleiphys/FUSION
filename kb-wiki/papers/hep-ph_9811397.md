# Quark Gluon Plasma as a Strongly Coupled Color-Coulombic Plasma

> We show that the extensively studied equation of state (EOS) of strongly coupled QED plasma fits the recent lattice EOS data of gluon plasma remarkably well, with appropriate modifications to take account of color degrees of freedom and running coupling constant. Hence we conclude that the quark gluon plasma near the critical temperature is a strongly coupled color-Coulombic plasma.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the equation of state (EOS) of strongly coupled QED plasma, with modifications for color degrees of freedom and a running coupling constant, fits recent lattice EOS data for gluon plasma remarkably well, leading to the conclusion that quark gluon plasma near the critical temperature is a strongly coupled color-Coulombic plasma. The hedge is that the fit requires adjusting a single parameter (t0 or βc) to match the lattice data, and the value of βc obtained is an order of magnitude smaller than that from lattice results.

## Method
The author adapted the nonrelativistic strongly coupled QED plasma EOS to a relativistic color-Coulombic plasma by replacing the fine structure constant α with 4αs/3 in the plasma parameter Γ, and using the running coupling constant αs(T) from lattice calculations. The energy density was expressed as ε = (3 + uex(Γ)) n T, where uex(Γ) interpolates between the Abe cluster-expansion result and the one-component plasma (OCP) computer simulation result. The pressure was then obtained via thermodynamic integration, and the model was fitted to lattice data for gluon plasma by adjusting the single parameter t0 (or βc) in the running coupling constant.

## Key numbers
- The plasma parameter Γ near the critical temperature is estimated as Γ ≈ 2/3, using αs ≈ 0.5, rav ≈ 1 fm, and Tc ≈ 200 MeV.
- The best fit to lattice data was obtained with t0 = 0.5763 or βc = 0.57.
- The constant af for gluons was taken as af = 16π²/90.
- For Λ = 200 MeV, the model yields Tc = 347 MeV when using the running coupling form from Rischke (1995) and Tc = 110 MeV when using the form from Biran (1995).
- The gluon plasma remains strongly coupled even at T = 5 Tc, as shown by the plotted Γ(t) and αs(t).

## Context
This work extends prior attempts to explain lattice EOS data for gluon plasma, including the quasi-particle model of Peshier (1996) and the author's earlier work using the Cornell potential and Mayer's cluster expansion (Bannur 1998), both of which had limited success near Tc. The paper directly uses the lattice data of Boyd (1996) and the running coupling constant from that same lattice calculation. It enables future recalculation of QGP signatures such as J/ψ suppression, screening length, and collective effects, which may be dramatically modified in a strongly coupled plasma.
