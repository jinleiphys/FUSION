# Landau hydrodynamic solution for non-central heavy ion collisions

> The manuscript presents generalization of the Landau hydrodynamic solution for multiparticle production applied for non-central relativistic heavy ion collisions. Obtained solution enables to calculate anisotropy parameter $v_2$ for different rapidities with respect to the initial conditions for hydrodynamics.

## Key claim
The paper presents an approximate analytic generalization of Landau's hydrodynamic solution to non-central heavy ion collisions, enabling calculation of the elliptic flow parameter \(v_2\) as a function of rapidity. The solution is explicitly approximate and relies on the same assumptions as the original Landau model, including separate treatment of longitudinal and transverse expansion and an ideal relativistic gas equation of state.

## Method
The author solves the relativistic hydrodynamic equations of energy-momentum conservation in two stages: first, the 1+1-dimensional longitudinal expansion along the beam axis is solved exactly using rapidity variables; second, the transverse expansion at a fixed azimuthal angle \(\phi\) is solved approximately by assuming the acceleration term is proportional to \(r/t^2\), neglecting the quadratic velocity gradient, and approximating the radial energy density gradient as \(-e/R(\phi)\) with a modified edge-to-center energy density ratio \(f(R(\phi))\). The longitudinal and transverse solutions are matched at the time when the conic-flight stage begins, and the entropy per rapidity is computed; the elliptic flow \(v_2(\eta)\) is then obtained from the azimuthal distribution of produced particles, with the normalization canceling out.

## Key numbers
- The initial nuclear density uses parameters: \(R_A = 1.12A^{1/3} - 0.86A^{-1/3}\) fm, diffuseness \(d = 0.54\) fm, and central density \(n_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The model is compared to data for Au-Au collisions at impact parameter \(b = 6\) fm and Cu-Cu at \(b = 3\) fm, at beam energies \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 64\) and \(200\) GeV.
- The constants used in the toy model are \(C = 1.5 R_A\) and \(a = R_A\) for all four curves shown in the figure.

## Context
This work extends the classic Landau hydrodynamic solution (Landau, 1953, 1956) for multiparticle production in relativistic heavy ion collisions to non-central geometries, where azimuthal asymmetries become important. It directly addresses the experimentally observed universal behavior of elliptic flow as a function of pseudorapidity at RHIC (reported in rapidity distribution data from RHIC experiments). The analytic solution enables comparison with a wider range of experimental data than the original Landau model, and the author notes that more sophisticated initial condition models (e.g., based on participant nucleons and binary collisions) are in development for quantitative fits.
