# A study of nuclear structure of light nuclei at the Electron-Ion Collider

> Understanding the substructure of atomic nuclei, particularly the clustering of nucleons inside them, is essential for comprehending nuclear dynamics. Various cluster configurations can emerge depending on excitation energy, the number and types of core clusters, and the presence of excess neutrons. Despite the prevalence of tightly bound cluster formations in low-lying states, understanding the correlation between clusters and their formation mechanisms remains incomplete. This exploring study investigates nuclear clustering at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) using simulations based on the modified BeAGLE model. By simulating collisions involving $e$+$^{9}$Be, $e$+$^{12}$C, and $e$+$^{16}$O nuclei, we find that the average energy of particles $\langle E \rangle$ and the system size ratios of particles at forward rapidity exhibit sensitivity to alpha clustering and its various configurations. These findings offer valuable insights into the dynamics of nuclear clustering and its implications for future studies at the EIC.

## Key claim

Using simulations with the modified BeAGLE model, the average energy of particles at forward rapidity and system size ratios show sensitivity to alpha clustering and its configurations in e+9Be, e+12C, and e+16O collisions, but the authors note that the maximum sensitivity is approximately 10% and that whether such differences can be experimentally measured requires detailed detector studies beyond the scope of this work.

## Method

The authors employed version 1.03 of the BeAGLE Monte Carlo event generator, a hybrid model combining DPMJet, PYTHIA6, PyQM, FLUKA, and LHAPDF5, to simulate electron-nucleus collisions. They modified the GLISSANDO module within BeAGLE to implement alpha-clustered configurations for 9Be (2alpha+neutron), 12C (3alpha), and 16O (4alpha) with cluster centers separated by specific distances and an alpha-cluster RMS radius of 1.1 fm, and compared results to the standard Woods-Saxon density distribution. The mean energy of particles in the forward rapidity region (4.5 < eta < 5.9) was studied as a function of collision centrality defined by impact parameter cuts.

## Key numbers

- Alpha-cluster size set to 1.1 fm for all systems (9Be, 12C, 16O)
- Distance between cluster centers: 3.6 fm for 9Be, 2.8 fm for 12C, and 3.2 fm for 16O
- Expulsion distance between nucleon pair centers: 0.9 fm
- The three reaction stages (hard scattering, INC, evaporation) dominate at mean pseudorapidity values of 1.8, 4.9, and 6.9 respectively for e+12C at 18x122 GeV
- Forward rapidity acceptance used: 4.5 < eta < 5.9
- Maximum sensitivity of mean energy ratios to nuclear structure differences is approximately 10%

## Context

This work extends previous studies of alpha clustering in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions (Broniowski 2013, Zhang 2017, Li 2020) to electron-nucleus collisions at the future Electron-Ion Collider. It builds on the Ikeda diagram framework (Ikeda 1968) and antisymmetrized molecular dynamics calculations (Kanada-Enyo 2006, Kravvaris 2017) that suggest cluster structures may be present even in nuclear ground states. The study enables future experimental tests of nuclear clustering at the EIC using forward-rapidity observables, though the authors identify that defining centrality in e+A collisions and achieving experimental sensitivity to the approximately 10% effect remain open challenges.
