# The electromagnetic signature of jets

> Recent RHIC data have suggested an interesting scenario where jets, after being formed in the very first instants of the nuclear collision, interact strongly and are absorbed by the hot and dense matter subsequently created. In this respect, electromagnetic signals constitute another class of penetrating radiation. We first propose the study of large mass dileptons induced by the passage of high-energy quark jets through quark-gluon plasma. We find that the yield due to the jet-plasma interaction gets progressively larger as the collision energy increases: it is slightly less than the Drell-Yan contribution at RHIC energies, and it is a factor of 10 larger than Drell-Yan at the LHC. In a related investigation, we then propose to study dilepton-tagged jets. We estimate quantitatively the various background sources, and identify windows where those are sub-dominant.

# The electromagnetic signature of jets

## Key claim

Jet-plasma interactions produce a new source of virtual photons that can compete with or exceed Drell-Yan production, but the magnitude depends strongly on collision energy: the yield is slightly less than Drell-Yan at RHIC energies and a factor of 10 larger than Drell-Yan at LHC energies, assuming a thermally and chemically equilibrated plasma with an isentropic expansion.

## Method

The authors decompose the parton phase space distribution into a thermal plasma component (approximated by an exponential distribution at temperature T) and a jet component calculated from perturbative QCD with CTEQ5L parton distributions and EKS98 shadowing corrections. They compute lepton pair production from quark-antiquark annihilation where one parton is thermal and the other is a hard QCD jet, and compare this to Drell-Yan background and thermal radiation for central Pb+Pb collisions at both RHIC and LHC energies, assuming an isentropic expansion with initial proper times of 0.15 and 0.5 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC energies (sqrt(s_nn) = 200 GeV), the jet-plasma dilepton yield is slightly less than the Drell-Yan contribution.
- At LHC energies (sqrt(s_nn) = 5.5 TeV), the jet-plasma dilepton yield is a factor of 10 larger than Drell-Yan.
- The new real photon source from jet-plasma interactions is dominant up to p_T approximately 6 GeV/c.
- Initial proper times considered: tau_0 = 0.15 fm/c (rapid thermalization limit) and 0.5 fm/c (more conservative).

## Context

This work extends the study of jet quenching observed in RHIC data by proposing electromagnetic signatures as complementary penetrating probes. The authors build on prior calculations of real photon production from jet-plasma interactions (Fries, Muller, Srivastava) and on the general framework of jet quenching (Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, Schiff; Wang, Gyulassy). The results enable experimental searches for dilepton-tagged jets, where the authors identify windows in invariant mass and transverse momentum where the electromagnetic signal dominates over background from correlated heavy quark decays, offering advantages over photon-tagged jets for studying in-medium energy modification.
