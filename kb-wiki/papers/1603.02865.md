# Dyson-Schwinger Approach to Color-Superconductivity: Effects of Selfconsistent Gluon Dressing

> The phase diagram of dense QCD at nonvanishing temperatures and large quark chemical potentials is studied with Dyson-Schwinger equations for 2+1 quark flavors, focusing on color-superconducting phases with 2SC and CFL-like pairing. The truncation scheme of our previous investigations is extended to include the dressing of gluons with selfconsistently determined quarks, i.e., taking into account the dynamical masses and superconducting gaps of the quarks in the gluon polarization. As a consequence the gluon screening is reduced, leading to an enhancement of the critical temperatures of the color-superconducting phases by about a factor of 2 as compared to the case where the gluons are dressed with bare quarks. We also calculate the Debye and Meissner masses of the gluons and show that they are consistent with weak-coupling results.

## Key claim

The critical temperatures of color-superconducting phases (2SC and CFL-like) are enhanced by about a factor of 2 when gluons are dressed with selfconsistently determined quarks (including dynamical masses and superconducting gaps) compared to the case where gluons are dressed with bare quarks, but the precise phase boundaries are complicated by large spinodal regions where multiple phases coexist.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations for the quark propagator and gluon propagator in Landau gauge at finite temperature and chemical potential for 2+1 quark flavors, extending their previous truncation scheme (Müller 2013) by calculating the gluon polarization loop with fully dressed Nambu-Gorkov quark propagators instead of using hard-thermal-loop/hard-dense-loop (HTL-HDL) approximation with bare quarks. The quark-gluon vertex is modeled with an Abelian part plus a Ball-Chiu-type longitudinal term constructed from the anomalous self-energies to preserve renormalizability and consistency with weak-coupling results for Debye and Meissner masses. The Yang-Mills part of the gluon propagator is taken from fits to lattice data (Fischer 2010), and the regularization of the polarization loop follows a scheme similar to Brown and Pennington (1988).

## Key numbers

* Critical temperatures of 2SC solutions reach about 40–50 MeV with selfconsistent gluon dressing, compared to about 20 MeV in the HTL-HDL truncation (Müller 2013)
* Pion decay constant in vacuum: fπ = 95 MeV with the improved truncation, versus 127 MeV in the HTL-HDL approximation (empirical value is 92.4 MeV)
* Chiral critical endpoint for ms = 30 MeV: around (T, μ) = (100, 160) MeV; for ms = 54 MeV: around (T, μ) = (120, 120) MeV
* Diquark condensates at T = 10 MeV show CFL pairing above thresholds of about μ = 500 MeV (ms = 30 MeV) or μ = 600 MeV (ms = 54 MeV)
* Vertex parameter d1 = 14 GeV², about 50% higher than the value in Müller (2013)
* Renormalization point for gluon polarization: |ν| = 7.65 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger study of color superconductivity by Müller (2013) from HTL-HDL approximation to a selfconsistent treatment where the gluon polarization includes dynamical quark masses and superconducting gaps, following the approach of Fischer (2012) who showed that including dynamical quark masses shifts the chiral critical endpoint. The improved truncation resolves the previous inconsistency where vacuum and in-medium observables could not be simultaneously matched (fπ was overestimated by 40% when fitting to the chiral critical temperature). The results enable more reliable predictions for the QCD phase diagram at high chemical potentials, though the large spinodal regions and the lack of a thermodynamically consistent effective action leave the precise phase boundaries uncertain.
