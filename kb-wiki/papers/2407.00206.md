# Lattice QCD Calculation of $x$-dependent Meson Distribution Amplitudes at Physical Pion Mass with Threshold Logarithm Resummation

> We present a lattice quantum chromodynamics (QCD) calculation of the $x$-dependent pion and kaon distribution amplitudes (DA) in the framework of large momentum effective theory. This calculation is performed on a fine lattice of $a=0.076$ fm at physical pion mass, with the pion boosted to $1.8$ GeV and kaon boosted to $2.3$ GeV. We renormalize the matrix elements in the hybrid scheme and match to $\overline{\rm MS }$ with a subtraction of the leading renormalon in the Wilson-line mass. The perturbative matching is improved by resumming the large logarithms related to the small quark and gluon momenta in the soft-gluon limit. After resummation, we demonstrate that we are able to calculate a range of $x\in[x_0,1-x_0]$ with $x_0=0.25$ for pion and $x_0=0.2$ for kaon with theoretical systematic errors under control. The kaon DA is shown to be slighted skewed, and narrower than pion DA. Although the $x$-dependence cannot be direct calculated beyond these ranges, we estimate higher moments of the pion and kaon DAs by complementing our calculation with short-distance factorization.

## Key claim

The authors present a lattice QCD calculation of the $x$-dependent pion and kaon distribution amplitudes at physical pion mass, claiming that after threshold logarithm resummation the theoretical systematic errors are under control for $x\in[0.25,0.75]$ for the pion and $x\in[0.2,0.8]$ for the kaon, but the $x$-dependence cannot be directly calculated beyond these ranges.

## Method

The calculation uses a 2+1 flavor Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) gauge ensemble with lattice spacing $a=0.076$ fm and physical pion mass, boosting the pion to $1.8$ GeV and the kaon to $2.3$ GeV. Bare quasi-distribution amplitude matrix elements are renormalized in the hybrid scheme with leading-renormalon resummation and matched to the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme, then Fourier transformed to $x$-space and matched to light-cone distribution amplitudes using next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic threshold resummation combined with next-to-leading order perturbative matching.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacing: $a = 0.076$ fm
* Pion boosted momentum: $1.8$ GeV
* Kaon boosted momentum: $2.3$ GeV
* Pion mass: $140(1)$ MeV; kaon mass: $498(1)$ MeV
* Accessible $x$ range for pion: $x \in [0.25, 0.75]$ (i.e., $x_0 = 0.25$)
* Accessible $x$ range for kaon: $x \in [0.2, 0.8]$ (i.e., $x_0 = 0.2$)
* Kaon decay constant extracted at $n_z=1$: $153.3(4)$ MeV; at $n_z=2$: $152.7(4)$ MeV (FLAG average for 2+1 flavor QCD is $155.7(7)$ MeV)
* Linear divergence counterterm $\delta m \approx 0.6$ GeV for kaon and $\delta m \approx 0.57$ GeV for pion
* First Mellin moment of kaon: $\langle \xi \rangle_K(\mu) \approx 0.0013(60)$, corresponding to first Gegenbauer coefficient $a_1 = 0.002(10)$

## Context

This work extends previous LaMET calculations of meson distribution amplitudes by including threshold resummation at NNLL order, which was recently developed for pion DAs on a domain-wall ensemble by Baker (2024). It builds on earlier continuum extrapolations at physical pion mass without leading-renormalon resummation by LatticeParton (2022) and the first implementation of LRR with NLO matching and RGR by Holligan (2023). The results enable improved first-principles constraints on the $x$-dependence of pion and kaon distribution amplitudes, which are important inputs for hard exclusive processes and CP-violating heavy meson decays such as $B\to\pi K$ and $D\to K\bar{K}$.
