# Mode-by-mode fluid dynamics for relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We propose to study the fluid dynamic propagation of fluctuations in relativistic heavy ion collisions differentially with respect to their azimuthal, radial and longitudinal wavelength. To this end, we introduce a background-fluctuation splitting and a Bessel-Fourier decomposition of the fluctuating modes. We demonstrate how the fluid dynamic evolution of realistic events can be build up from the propagation of individual modes. We describe the main elements of this mode-by-mode fluid dynamics, and we discuss its use in the fluid dynamic analysis of heavy ion collisions. As a first illustration, we quantify to what extent only fluctuations of sufficiently large radial wave length contribute to harmonic flow coefficients. We find that fluctuations of short wave length are suppressed not only due to larger dissipative effects, but also due to a geometrical averaging over the freeze-out hyper surface. In this way, our study further substantiates the picture that harmonic flow coefficients give access to a coarse-grained version of the initial conditions for heavy ion collisions, only.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that harmonic flow coefficients in relativistic heavy ion collisions can be built up from the linear superposition of individually propagated fluctuating modes, and that only fluctuations of sufficiently large radial wavelength contribute to these coefficients due to both dissipative suppression and geometrical averaging over the freeze-out hypersurface.

## Method

The authors introduce a background-fluctuation splitting of the enthalpy density and other fluid dynamic fields, then perform a Bessel-Fourier decomposition of the fluctuating modes in azimuthal, radial, and longitudinal directions. They propagate each mode individually as linearized perturbations on top of a smooth, azimuthally symmetric background field solved with relativistic viscous fluid dynamics, and reconstruct experimental observables such as particle spectra and harmonic flow coefficients by summing the contributions of individually propagated modes weighted by their initial correlations.

## Key numbers

- The radial wave vectors are set by the zeroes of Bessel functions as \(k_l^{(m)} = z_l^{(m)}/R\) with \(R = 8\) fm for the results presented.
- The evolution is initialized at \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/c with a shear viscosity to entropy ratio \(\eta/s = 0.08 \, \hbar/k_B\) and relaxation time \(\tau_\text{Shear} = 0.23 \, \hbar/(k_B T)\).
- The freeze-out temperature is taken as \(T_\text{fo} = 120\) MeV for particle spectra calculations.
- The sum for harmonic flow coefficients converges quickly for small radial wave numbers \(l \leq l_{\text{max}} \approx 5\).
- A simple rescaling of one fluctuating mode by \(\tilde{w}_1^{(2)} \to 0.7 \, \tilde{w}_1^{(2)}\) improved agreement between simulation and data for \(v_2\) over a much increased \(p_T\)-range.

## Context

This work extends the event-by-event fluid dynamic approach to heavy ion collisions (Alver and Roland, 2010; Schenke, Jeon, and Gale, 2012) by introducing an orthonormal mode decomposition that allows propagating fluctuations differentially by wavelength, rather than using cumulant-based characterizations of initial conditions (Teaney and Yan, 2011; Qiu and Heinz, 2011). The mode-by-mode framework enables efficient "backward engineering" of initial conditions from experimental data and provides a systematic way to test which features of the initial state survive the hydrodynamic evolution, building on earlier perturbative studies of fluctuations around simple backgrounds (Staig and Shuryak, 2011; Floerchinger and Wiedemann, 2011).
