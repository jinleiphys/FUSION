# Energy-momentum tensor in QCD: nucleon mass decomposition and mechanical equilibrium

> We review and examine in detail recent developments regarding the question of the nucleon mass decomposition. We discuss in particular the virial theorem in quantum field theory and its implications for the nucleon mass decomposition and mechanical equilibrium. We reconsider the renormalization of the QCD energy-momentum tensor in minimal-subtraction-type schemes and the physical interpretation of its components, as well as the role played by the trace anomaly and Poincaré symmetry. We also study the concept of "quantum anomalous energy" proposed in some works as a new contribution to the nucleon mass. Examining the various arguments, we conclude that the quantum anomalous energy is not a genuine contribution to the mass sum rule, as a consequence of translation symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the "quantum anomalous energy" is not a genuine contribution to the nucleon mass sum rule, because it conflates the constraint of mechanical equilibrium (the virial theorem) with the actual mass decomposition, and because translation symmetry prevents it from being a separate physical term.

## Method

The authors derive the quantum field theory version of the virial theorem from the dilatation current and the divergence of the energy-momentum tensor (EMT), showing that for a closed system in a stationary state the integrated stress tensor vanishes. They then analyze the forward matrix elements of the total and partial EMTs using Poincaré symmetry and gravitational form factors, separating the mass decomposition into a proper energy term (involving \(A_a(0)+\bar C_a(0)\)) and a mechanical equilibrium condition (the sum rule \(\sum_a \bar C_a(0)=0\)). They also examine the renormalization of the EMT in minimal-subtraction-type schemes and the operator structure of the proposed four-term decomposition.

## Key numbers

* The forward matrix element of the total EMT for a momentum eigenstate is \(\langle p|T^{\mu\nu}(x)|p\rangle = 2p^\mu p^\nu\), ensuring the total four-momentum is \(p^\mu\).
* The sum rule for the gravitational form factors is \(\sum_a A_a(0) = 1\) and \(\sum_a \bar C_a(0) = 0\), where the latter expresses the virial theorem for a closed system.
* In the rest frame, the partial EMT expectation value gives \(\langle p_\text{rest}|\int d^3x\,T^{\mu\nu}_a(x)|p_\text{rest}\rangle / \langle p_\text{rest}|p_\text{rest}\rangle = M\) times a diagonal matrix with entries \(A_a(0)+\bar C_a(0)\) for the time-time component and \(-\bar C_a(0)\) for the spatial components.
* The mass decomposition is \(M = \sum_a [A_a(0) + \bar C_a(0)] M\), which in the rest frame reduces to the sum of quark and gluon proper energies \(U_q + U_g\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Lorcé (2017) on the nucleon mass decomposition and the role of mechanical equilibrium, and directly addresses claims by Ji (2021) that a four-term decomposition including "quantum anomalous energy" is needed. The paper clarifies that the virial theorem in QFT is equivalent to the von Laue condition for mechanical equilibrium, previously discussed by Polyakov (2002, 2018). It enables a clean separation of the mass sum rule into a two-term (quark and gluon) or three-term (quark rest mass, quark kinetic/potential, gluon) decomposition, without introducing spurious contributions from the trace anomaly.
