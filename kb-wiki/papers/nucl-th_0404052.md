# Jet quenching at intermediate RHIC energies

> The final state energy loss of fast partons penetrating a longitudinally expanding quark-gluon plasma of effective gluon rapidity density $dN^{g}/dy=650-800$ is evaluated and incorporated together with the multiple initial state Cronin scattering in the lowest order perturbative QCD hadron production formalism. Predictions for the neutral pion attenuation in central $Au+Au$ collisions at the intermediate RHIC energy of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=62$ GeV relative to the binary collision scaled $p+p$ result are given. The quenching is found to be a factor of 2-3 with a moderate transverse momentum dependence and the attenuation of the away-side di-hadron correlation function is estimated to be 3-5 fold.

## Key claim

The paper predicts a factor of 2-3 suppression of neutral pion production in central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 62 GeV, but this result is conditional on an effective gluon rapidity density of dN^g/dy = 650-800 and the interplay between initial-state Cronin enhancement and final-state parton energy loss.

## Method

The author evaluates medium-induced non-Abelian gluon bremsstrahlung using the reaction operator approach to third order in opacity for a 1+1D Bjorken-expanding quark-gluon plasma, with the soft parton rapidity density as the controlling parameter. The energy loss is incorporated into a lowest-order perturbative QCD hadron production formalism that also includes multiple initial-state Cronin scattering via Gaussian kT smearing, nuclear parton distribution functions, and leading-twist shadowing. The nuclear modification factor RAA is computed by comparing central Au+Au yields to binary-collision-scaled p+p results.

## Key numbers

- Effective gluon rapidity density used: dN^g/dy = 650-800 for central Au+Au at √sNN = 62 GeV
- Predicted neutral pion attenuation factor: 2-3 relative to binary-collision-scaled p+p
- Predicted attenuation of away-side di-hadron correlation function: 3-5 fold
- Cronin enhancement maximum: RAA(max) = 1.8-2 at pT = 3-3.5 GeV at √sNN = 62 GeV
- Estimated initial energy density: ε0 = 6-8 GeV/fm^3, temperature T0 = 300-325 MeV, with initial equilibration time τ0 = 0.8-1 fm
- Mean energy per radiated gluon: ⟨ΔE⟩/⟨Ng⟩ ≈ 0.8-1.9 GeV
- For comparison at √sNN = 200 GeV: dN^g/dy = 1150, RAA ∼ 0.2-0.25

## Context

This work extends the jet quenching formalism previously applied at SPS energies (√sNN = 17 GeV) and top RHIC energy (√sNN = 200 GeV) to the intermediate RHIC energy of 62 GeV, filling a critical gap in mapping out the energy dependence of parton energy loss. The predictions are designed for direct comparison with upcoming PHENIX data and test the linearity of the relationship between soft parton rapidity density and jet attenuation, building on prior calculations by Vitev (2002) and Gyulassy (2003, 2004). A strong deviation from the predicted factor of 2-3 suppression would indicate non-linear dependence of non-Abelian energy loss on dN^g/dy, while confirmation would validate jet tomography as a tool for probing QGP properties across collision energies.
