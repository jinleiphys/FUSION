# Phase Structure of the Interacting Vector Boson Model

> The two-fluid Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM) with the U(6) as a dynamical group possesses a rich algebraic structure of physical interesting subgroups that define its distinct exactly solvable dynamical limits. The classical images corresponding to different dynamical symmetries are obtained by means of the coherent state method. The phase structure of the IVBM is investigated and the following basic phase shapes, connected to a specific geometric configurations of the ground state, are determined: spherical, $U_{p}(3)\otimes U_{n}(3)$, $γ-$unstable, O(6), and axially deformed shape, $SU(3)\otimes U_{T}(2)$. The ground state quantum phase transitions between different phase shapes, corresponding to the different dynamical symmetries and mixed symmetry case, are investigated.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the two-fluid Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM) exhibits three distinct ground-state phase shapes corresponding to its dynamical symmetries, and that quantum phase transitions between spherical and deformed shapes are of second order, with no first-order transitions appearing due to the absence of a cubic term in the Landau potential.

## Method

The classical limits of Hamiltonians corresponding to different dynamical symmetry chains of the U(6) group were obtained using the coherent state method, specifically projective coherent states constructed from two vector boson creation operators. The energy functional was minimized with respect to the deformation parameters \(r_1\), \(r_2\), and the relative angle \(\theta\) to determine equilibrium shapes. A generalized Hamiltonian with two control parameters \(\eta\) and \(g\) was constructed to interpolate between the dynamical symmetry limits, and the phase diagram was mapped by analyzing the order parameter \(\rho_0 = r_2/r_1\) as a function of these control parameters.

## Key numbers

- Critical value \(\eta'_c = 0.2\) for the second-order phase transition along the \(U_p(3) \otimes U_n(3)\) to \(O(6)\) leg (spherical to \(\gamma\)-unstable deformed shape).
- Critical value \(\eta''_c = 0.33\) for the second-order phase transition along the \(U_p(3) \otimes U_n(3)\) to \(SU(3) \otimes U_T(2)\) leg (spherical to axially deformed shape).
- Equilibrium deformation parameter \(|\rho_0| = 1\) for the deformed phase shapes in the \(O(6)\), \(\overline{SU_{\pm}(3)}\), and \(SU(3) \otimes U_T(2)\) limits.
- The critical line equation \(\eta = 1/(5 - 2g)\) describes the continuous boundary between spherical and deformed phases, reducing to \(\eta' = 0.2\) at \(g=0\) and \(\eta'' = 0.33\) at \(g=1\).

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum phase transitions in algebraic nuclear models, previously applied to the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) as reviewed in IBMQPT, to the two-fluid Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM) introduced in IVBM and pepan. The phase structure identified here parallels the spherical, \(\gamma\)-unstable, and axially deformed shapes found in IBM-1 and IBM-2, with the additional feature of a two-dimensional phase diagram controlled by two parameters. The results enable future studies of mixed-symmetry states and intermediate geometries, and the relation between the IVBM shape parameters and the standard Bohr collective model variable \(\gamma_{eff}\) provides a bridge to geometric collective models.
