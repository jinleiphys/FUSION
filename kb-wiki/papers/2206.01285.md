# An Improved Method for Coupling Hydrodynamics with Astrophysical Reaction Networks

> Reacting astrophysical flows can be challenging to model because of the difficulty in accurately coupling hydrodynamics and reactions. This can be particularly acute during explosive burning or at high temperatures where nuclear statistical equilibrium is established. We develop a new approach based on the ideas of spectral deferred corrections (SDC) coupling of explicit hydrodynamics and stiff reaction sources as an alternative to operator splitting that is simpler than the more comprehensive SDC approach we demonstrated previously. We apply the new method to a double detonation problem with a moderately-sized astrophysical nuclear reaction network and explore the timestep size and reaction network tolerances to show that the simplified-SDC approach provides improved coupling with decreased computational expense compared to traditional Strang operator splitting. This is all done in the framework of the Castro hydrodynamics code, and all algorithm implementations are freely available.

## Key claim

The simplified spectral deferred corrections (SDC) method provides improved coupling between hydrodynamics and stiff reaction sources with decreased computational expense compared to traditional Strang operator splitting, but is restricted to second-order accuracy in time overall, unlike the more comprehensive SDC approach demonstrated previously by the same authors.

## Method

The authors implement a simplified SDC approach within the Castro hydrodynamics code that iteratively couples explicit CTU+PPM hydrodynamics with implicit reaction integration using the VODE ODE solver. Each timestep iteration uses an iteratively-lagged advection term and reactive source term to update the system, with the advection term computed using the CTU method including reactive sources in the interface state prediction, followed by integration of the combined advection and reaction system over the full timestep. The method uses two temporal nodes (t^n and t^{n+1}) with kmax = 2 iterations sufficient for second-order accuracy, and reuses the same numerical kernels for advection and reactions as the Strang splitting approach.

## Key numbers

- Two SDC iterations (kmax = 2) are sufficient for second-order accuracy; further iterations continue to decrease splitting error but do not increase formal order of accuracy
- The advective CFL condition is unity (full corner coupling), compared to method-of-lines approaches
- The energy limiter parameter fe = 0.5 limits the timestep such that reactions can only increase internal energy by 50%
- The method was applied to a double detonation problem with a moderately-sized astrophysical nuclear reaction network

## Context

This work extends the spectral deferred corrections approach previously developed by Zingale et al. (2019) in Castro for coupling hydrodynamics and nuclear reaction networks, but simplifies it to reuse existing CTU+PPM hydrodynamics kernels rather than requiring a method-of-lines formulation. The approach contrasts with traditional Strang splitting (Strang, 1968) and addresses issues with operator splitting where reactions do not directly incorporate hydrodynamics effects and vice versa, which can be particularly acute during explosive burning or at high temperatures where nuclear statistical equilibrium is established. The method enables tighter coupling without the timestep cutting approaches used by Couch et al. (2015) and Rivas et al. (2022), and avoids the complications described by McCorquodale and Colella for filling ghost cells in method-of-lines integration with adaptive mesh refinement.
