# Elastic and quasi-elastic electron scattering on the N = 14, 20, and 28 isotonic chains

> We present theoretical predictions for electron scattering on the N = 14, 20, and 28 isotonic chains from proton-deficient to proton-rich nuclei. The calculations are performed within the framework of the distorted-wave Born approximation and the proton and neutron density distributions are evaluated adopting a Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) approach with a density dependent meson-exchange interaction. We present results for the elastic and quasi-elastic cross sections and for the parity-violating asymmetry parameter. Owing to the correlations between the evolution of the electric charge form factors along each chain with the underlying proton shell structure of the isotones, elastic electron scattering experiments on isotones can provide useful informations about the occupation and filling of the single-particle levels of protons.

## Key claim
The paper presents theoretical predictions for elastic and quasi-elastic electron scattering on N = 14, 20, and 28 isotonic chains, showing that the evolution of electric charge form factors along each chain correlates with the underlying proton shell structure. The authors hedge that while elastic electron scattering on isotones can provide information about proton single-particle level occupation, the exclusive (e,e'p) reaction would be the best tool for this study but is very difficult to measure.

## Method
The calculations use the distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) for elastic scattering and the relativistic Green's function (RGF) model for inclusive quasi-elastic scattering. Proton and neutron density distributions are obtained from a Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) approach with a density-dependent meson-exchange interaction (DDME1 parameterization), with pairing correlations described by the finite-range Gogny interaction. The RHB equations are solved self-consistently together with Klein-Gordon equations for meson fields and the Poisson equation for the photon field.

## Key numbers
- Elastic electron scattering cross sections calculated at electron energy ε = 250 MeV for calcium isotopes (40,42,44,48Ca) and at ε = 200 MeV for 50,52,54Cr and ε = 250 MeV for 48Ti
- Elastic cross sections presented at ε = 850 MeV for all isotonic chains as functions of scattering angle θ
- Parity-violating asymmetry parameter A_pv calculated at ε = 850 MeV for all isotonic chains
- For 42Si, experimental 2+1 state at 770 ± 19 keV is cited as evidence of N = 28 shell closure disappearance
- Proton occupation number of 2s1/2 shell: 0.330 for 46Ar, 0.153 for 44S
- Fermi constant G_F ≈ 1.16639 × 10^-11 MeV^-2 and Weinberg angle sin^2Θ_W ≈ 0.23 used in A_pv calculations

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study on oxygen and calcium isotopic chains (Meucci et al., 2013) to isotonic chains, providing complementary information about proton shell structure evolution. The predictions are relevant for future electron scattering facilities including ELISe at FAIR (Germany) and SCRIT at RIKEN (Japan), and relate to the PREX experiment on 208Pb and the approved CREX experiment on 48Ca for measuring neutron distributions via parity-violating electron scattering. The study also connects to theoretical work on "bubble" nuclei, particularly 34Si, where RMF models predict central density depletion (Todd, Chu, Wang 2013) but experimental observation remains elusive.
