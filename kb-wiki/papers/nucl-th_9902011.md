# Shell correction energy for bubble nuclei

> The positioning of a bubble inside a many fermion system does not affect the volume, surface or curvature terms in the liquid drop expansion of the total energy. Besides possible Coulomb effects, the only other contribution to the ground state energy of such a system arises from shell effects. We show that the potential energy surface is a rather shallow function of the displacement of the bubble from the center and in most cases the preferential position of a bubble is off center. Systems with bubbles are expected to have bands of extremely low lying collective states, corresponding to various bubble displacements.

# Shell correction energy for bubble nuclei

## Key claim
The potential energy surface for bubble displacement in a many-fermion system is a rather shallow function, and in most cases the preferential position of a bubble is off-center rather than at the center, contrary to tacit symmetry-based assumptions in prior literature.

## Method
The authors computed the single-particle spectrum for an infinite square-well potential with Dirichlet boundary conditions using a 3D conformal mapping method, treating spinless fermions with no electric charge. They applied the Strutinsky shell-correction method to extract the shell correction energy as a function of bubble displacement for systems with up to 8,000 particles, analyzing the unfolded spectrum via the Weyl formula for the average cumulative number of states.

## Key numbers
- For a bubble of radius a = R/2 (where R = R₀N¹/³), the shell correction energy was computed for up to N = 8,000 spinless fermions, with energy measured in units of ħ²/2mr₀².
- For a bubble of radius a = R/5, the shell correction energy was computed for up to N = 1,000 spinless fermions.
- The probability to encounter a nearest-neighbor energy spacing s greater than s₀ = 3 is 8.5 × 10⁻⁴; for s₀ = 4 it is 3.5 × 10⁻⁶; for s₀ = 5 it is 3 × 10⁻⁹, based on the Wigner surmise.
- The positional entropy of a bubble is given by S_pos(d) = 2 ln d + const, where d is the displacement vector of the bubble center relative to the sphere center.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of bubble formation in nuclei by Wheeler and Pomorski, and in charged alkali metal clusters by Dietrich, by addressing the previously unexamined question of bubble positioning. The authors show that the shell correction energy landscape is oscillatory and that off-center configurations can be energetically favored, analogous to the Jahn-Teller effect in molecules. The results enable predictions of extremely low-lying collective states in bubble systems and suggest applications to neutron star crusts (as discussed by Chris), metallic clusters with bubbles, and systems such as a metallic ball inside a superconducting microwave resonator (as discussed by Caio).
