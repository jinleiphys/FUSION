# Reply to "Comment on Sequential Single-pion Production Explaining the dibaryon $d^*(2380)$ peak''

> In a comment [arXiv:2106.00494] to our paper on ``Sequential single-pion production explaining the dibaryon $d^*(2380)$ peak'' the authors provide arguments that apparently invalidate our claims and present what they call ``proofs'' of the dibaryon explanation of the $np\to π^0π^0d$ fusion reaction. In this reply we refute the arguments of the comment and show that no existing experiment proves the dibaryon nature of the peak of the fusion reaction.

## Key claim
The authors refute the claim that the $d^*(2380)$ peak observed in $np\to\pi^0\pi^0d$ and $np\to\pi^+\pi^-d$ reactions is a genuine dibaryon resonance, arguing instead that the peak can be explained by a sequential single-pion production mechanism involving a triangle singularity. They assert that no existing experiment proves the dibaryon nature of the peak, and that all claimed evidence is equally consistent with a kinematic origin.

## Method
The authors respond point-by-point to eight criticisms raised in a comment by proponents of the dibaryon interpretation. They analyze the sequential two-step mechanism: first $np(I=0)\to\pi^-pp$ (or $\pi^+nn$), followed by $pp\to\pi^+d$ (or $nn\to\pi^-d$), where the second step involves a triangle singularity. They use isospin relations to extract $np(I=0)$ cross sections from measured $np\to pp\pi^-$ and $pp\to pp\pi^0$ data, and evaluate the allowed angular momenta in the $pp$ subsystem at different kinematic configurations. They also discuss the relationship between the fusion reaction $pn(I=0)\to\pi^+\pi^-d$ and open-channel reactions $pn\to\pi^+\pi^-np$, as well as the effects on $np$ partial waves and polarization observables in $\gamma d\to pn$.

## Key numbers
- The $np\to\pi^0\pi^0d$ and $np\to\pi^+\pi^-d$ reactions show a narrow peak in cross section around 2380 MeV.
- For $T_p=1200$ MeV, the $pp$ invariant mass at minimum $\pi^-p$ invariant mass is $M(pp)=2239.47$ MeV, with excess energy of 362.9 MeV.
- At maximum $\pi^-p$ invariant mass, $M(pp)=1920.2$ MeV, with excess energy of 43.65 MeV.
- At the peak of the $\pi^-p$ distribution around $M(\pi^-p)=1370$ MeV, $M(pp)\simeq 1951$ MeV, corresponding to about 75 MeV excess energy for the two protons.
- The angular momentum $L$ for the $pp$ system can reach up to $L=6$ at minimum $\pi^-p$ mass, $L=2$ at maximum, and $L=3$ at the peak distribution.
- The $\gamma d\to pn$ reaction has a clear peak due to $\Delta(1232)$ excitation around $E_\gamma=260$ MeV.
- The $pn(I=1)\to\pi^+\pi^-d$ cross section in the sequential mechanism cancels exactly (two amplitudes cancel), while $pn(I=0)$ amplitudes sum, producing an enhancement.

## Context
This reply extends the debate on the nature of the $d^*(2380)$ peak, originally attributed to a dibaryon by the WASA-at-COSY collaboration (2012, 2011). The sequential single-pion production mechanism was first proposed by Bar-Nir et al. and reformulated by Molina, Ikeno, and Oset (2021) using Feynman diagrams showing a triangle singularity. The authors refute claims by the comment authors that the peak constitutes proof of a dibaryon, citing prior work by Wilkin and Miguel showing that the peak in the fusion reaction necessarily produces peaks in related open reactions regardless of the underlying mechanism. The work also references the COMPASS collaboration's demonstration that triangle singularities produce Argand plots resembling ordinary resonances. This enables future quantitative calculations of the first step ($np(I=0)\to pp\pi^-$) to provide a more complete description of the two-step process.
