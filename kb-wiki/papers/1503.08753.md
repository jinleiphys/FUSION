# Core-collapse supernova matter: light clusters, pasta phase and phase transitions

> The pasta phase in core-collapse supernova matter (finite temperatures and fixed proton fractions) is studied within relativistic mean field models. Two different calculations are used for comparison, the Thomas-Fermi (TF) and the Coexisting Phases (CP) approximations. The effects of including light clusters in nuclear matter and the densities at which the transitions between pasta configurations and to uniform matter occur are also investigated. Finally, a comparison with a finite temperature Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculation is drawn.

## Key claim

The pasta phase and light clusters lower the free energy of core-collapse supernova matter, but the effect of light clusters is weak and only noticeable at very low densities (below approximately 0.001 fm⁻³); the specific stable pasta geometries and their melting temperatures depend on the nuclear model parametrization used, particularly on the symmetry energy and its slope.

## Method

The authors compare three approaches: a 3D Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculation (3DHFEOS) with four Skyrme interactions (SkM*, SLy4, NRAPR, SQMC700); relativistic mean field (RMF) Thomas-Fermi (TF) and Coexisting Phases (CP) approximations with three RMF parametrizations (FSU, IUFSU, TW); and a thermodynamical spinodal (TS) calculation. The pasta phase is studied in Wigner-Seitz cells with spherical, cylindrical, or slab geometries, and light clusters (deuterons, tritons, helions, alpha particles) are included as additional degrees of freedom in the RMF Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ₀ for the models ranges from 0.15 fm⁻³ (FSU, TW) to 0.17 fm⁻³ (SQMC700); binding energy per particle B/A ranges from -15.49 MeV (SQMC700) to -16.40 MeV (IUFSU).
- Light clusters are present only for densities ρ ≲ 0.001 fm⁻³ and melt for ρ ≳ 0.001 fm⁻³ (FSU interaction, T = 4 MeV, yp = 0.3).
- For the SQMC700 model at T = 2 MeV and yp = 0.3, pasta shapes appear at onset densities: 0.020 fm⁻³ (droplets), 0.032 fm⁻³ (rods), 0.042 fm⁻³ (cross-rods), 0.052 fm⁻³ (slabs), 0.090 fm⁻³ (tubes), 0.108 fm⁻³ (bubbles), 0.114 fm⁻³ (uniform).
- The difference between TF/CP and 3DHFEOS transition densities to uniform matter is ∼ 0.015 fm⁻³ and decreases with increasing temperature.
- For β-equilibrium matter, IUFSU has pasta up to T ∼ 10 MeV, while other parametrizations show pasta melting at T ∼ 4–6 MeV.
- The difference between the thermodynamical spinodal method and self-consistent calculations for the transition density is ∼ 0.015 fm⁻³ at T = 0 MeV and decreases with increasing T.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the pasta phase in core-collapse supernova matter by Ravenhall (1983), Horowitz (2005), and Maruyama (2005), and builds on earlier Thomas-Fermi calculations by Avancini (2010, 2012) and Grill (2014). It provides a systematic comparison of non-relativistic (Skyrme) and relativistic (RMF) methods, including the effects of light clusters, and shows that the pasta phase structure is relatively insensitive to the choice of Skyrme parametrization within the range of nuclear matter properties constrained by experiment. The results enable more accurate modeling of neutrino transport in supernova simulations and proto-neutron star cooling, as the pasta phase and light clusters affect neutrino opacity.
