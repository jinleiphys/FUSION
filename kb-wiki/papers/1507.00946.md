# Symmetric and Standard Matter-Neutrino Resonances Above Merging Compact Objects

> Matter-neutrino resonances (MNR) can occur in environments where the flux of electron antineutrinos is greater than the flux of electron neutrinos. These resonances may result in dramatic neutrino flavor transformation. Compact object merger disks are an example of an environment where electron antineutrinos outnumber neutrinos. We study MNR resonances in several such disk configurations and find two qualitatively different types of matter-neutrino resonances: a standard MNR and a symmetric MNR. We examine the transformation that occurs in each type of resonance and explore the consequences for nucleosynthesis.

## Key claim

The paper identifies two qualitatively different types of matter-neutrino resonances (MNR) above compact object merger disks, a standard MNR and a symmetric MNR, and shows that whether flavor transformation occurs depends on the configuration of the emission surfaces and the relative flux of mu and tau neutrinos, with sufficiently large mu/tau neutrino fluxes suppressing the standard MNR transition.

## Method

The authors constructed two representative disk models for a black hole-neutron star merger: a single-radius model where all neutrino flavors are emitted from a disk of radius 4.5×10⁶ cm with different temperatures, and a multiple-radius model where each flavor is emitted from a disk with its own radius and temperature. They solved the flavor evolution equations for a test neutrino traveling at 45° from the disk plane, using the full three-flavor neutrino oscillation framework with matter potential, vacuum Hamiltonian, and neutrino self-interaction potential, tracking 800 energy bins between 1 and 101 MeV with mixing parameters from the Particle Data Group.

## Key numbers

- Single-radius model: electron neutrino temperature 6.4 MeV, electron antineutrino temperature 7.1 MeV, mu/tau neutrino temperature 7.4 MeV, all from radius 4.5×10⁶ cm
- Multiple-radius model: electron neutrino temperature 5.9 MeV from radius 5.2×10⁶ cm, electron antineutrino temperature 7.8 MeV from radius 3.9×10⁶ cm, mu/tau neutrino temperature 9.9 MeV from variable radius
- The standard MNR region occurs at approximately 2×10⁷ cm along the trajectory where matter potential and self-interaction potential become equal in magnitude
- The symmetric point in the multiple-radius model occurs at approximately 10⁶ cm where the self-interaction potential crosses zero
- The scale height at the standard MNR region is τ_single ∼ 7×10⁶ cm with α ∼ 1.4, requiring mixing angle θ > 3×10⁻² for transition; the measured θ₁₃ ∼ 0.2 is within this range
- With no mu/tau neutrinos, the flux-weighted electron neutrino survival probability drops to nearly zero after the MNR
- With mu/tau neutrino flux at 85% of the electron antineutrino flux, electron flavor neutrinos remain in electron flavor through the MNR region

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of MNR transitions in collapsar-type disks (Malkus 2012, Malkus 2014) to the environment of compact object mergers, where electron antineutrinos outnumber electron neutrinos unlike in core-collapse supernovae. The paper distinguishes MNR transitions from the synchronized oscillations, bipolar/nutation oscillations, and MSW oscillations previously studied in supernova contexts (Hannestad 2006, Duan 2007, Dasgupta 2008). The results enable predictions for how neutrino flavor transformation affects nucleosynthesis, particularly the r-process, in merger outflows by showing that the capture rates of electron neutrinos and antineutrinos can be significantly altered depending on the disk configuration and mu/tau neutrino content.
