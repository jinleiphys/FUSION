# Relativistic mean-field description of the dynamics of giant resonances

> The relativistic mean-field theory provides a framework in which the nuclear many-body problem is described as a self-consistent system of nucleons and mesons. In the mean-field approximation, the self-consistent time evolution of the nuclear system describes the dynamics of collective motion: nuclear compressibility from monopole resonances, regular and chaotic dynamics of isoscalar and isovector collective vibrations.

# Relativistic mean-field description of the dynamics of giant resonances

## Key claim
The relativistic mean-field (RMF) framework, applied as a time-dependent initial-value problem, reproduces experimental giant resonance energies in spherical nuclei and indicates that the nuclear matter compression modulus lies in the range 250–270 MeV, approximately 20% larger than values from non-relativistic Skyrme or Gogny forces. The abstract omits that this conclusion depends on comparing six specific parameter sets and that only two (NL1 and NL3) produce excitation energies close to experiment.

## Method
The authors solve the time-dependent Dirac equation for A nucleons and the Klein-Gordon equations for meson fields (σ, ω, ρ) and the photon, in the mean-field (no-sea) approximation, starting from a stationary ground state. Initial conditions are set to simulate giant resonances, and the time evolution of the one-body density operator is computed; excitation energies are extracted from Fourier analysis of the time-dependent monopole moment ⟨r²(t)⟩. For nonlinear dynamics analysis, phase-space reconstruction (time delay, embedding dimension, correlation dimension) and information-theoretic functionals (mutual information, von Neumann entropy) are applied to the time series of collective variables.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear matter compression modulus K_nm = 211.7 MeV for the NL1 parameter set, 271.8 MeV for NL3, and 355.0 MeV for NL-SH.
- Experimental GMR excitation energy in ²⁰⁸Pb: 13.7 ± 0.3 MeV; NL3 calculation gives 14.1 MeV.
- NL1 calculation for ²⁰⁸Pb GMR is approximately 1 MeV lower than the average experimental value.
- Empirical curve for GMR energies: E_x ≈ 80 A^(−1/3) MeV.
- Isovector monopole resonance in ²⁰⁸Pb: experimental value 26 ± 3 MeV; NL3 calculation agrees excellently.
- Time delay for phase-space reconstruction: 27 fm/c for isoscalar mode, 13 fm/c for isovector mode.
- Embedding dimension: d_E = 3 for isoscalar mode, d_E = 4 for isovector mode.
- Correlation dimension D_2 saturates at 2 for the isoscalar mode; for the isovector mode it does not saturate but increases to a fractional value between 2 and 3.

## Context
This work extends earlier time-dependent RMF calculations by Vretenar et al. (1997) and Ring et al. (1996) to a systematic study of monopole resonances across multiple spherical nuclei and to the nonlinear dynamics of isoscalar versus isovector modes. It directly contradicts non-relativistic Hartree-Fock plus RPA calculations with Skyrme and Gogny forces (which yield K_nm ≈ 210–220 MeV) by finding a higher compression modulus (250–270 MeV). The results enable future studies of chaotic dynamics in finite nuclear systems and provide a benchmark for constraining the nuclear matter equation of state relevant to supernovae and neutron stars.
