# UrQMD calculations of two-pion HBT correlations in p+p and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies

> Two-pion Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) correlations for p+p and central Pb+Pb collisions at the Large-Hadron-Collider (LHC) energies are investigated with the ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics model combined with a correlation afterburner. The transverse momentum dependence of the Pratt-Bertsch HBT radii $R_{long}$, $R_{out}$, and $R_{side}$ is extracted from a three-dimensional Gaussian fit to the correlator in the longitudinal co-moving system. In the p+p case, the dependence of correlations on the charged particle multiplicity and formation time is explored and the data allows to constrain the formation time in the string fragmentation to $τ_f \leq 0.8$ fm/c. In the Pb+Pb case, it is found that $R_{out}$ is overpredicted by nearly 50%. The LHC results are also compared to data from the STAR experiment at RHIC. For both energies we find that the calculated $R_{out}/R_{side}$ ratio is always larger than data, indicating that the emission in the model is less explosive than observed in the data.

## Key claim

The paper uses the UrQMD model to show that LHC p+p HBT data can constrain the formation time in string fragmentation to τ_f ≤ 0.8 fm/c, but for Pb+Pb collisions the model overpredicts R_out by nearly 50% and the R_out/R_side ratio is always larger than data, indicating the modeled emission is less explosive than observed.

## Method

The ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model in cascade mode (v3.3) was used to simulate p+p collisions at √s = 7 TeV and central Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, with hard partonic interactions treated via PYTHIA. The freeze-out phase-space distributions were fed into the correlation afterburner (CRAB) to construct two-pion HBT correlators in the longitudinally co-moving system, and the Pratt-Bertsch radii R_long, R_out, and R_side were extracted from three-dimensional Gaussian fits. For p+p collisions, the formation time τ_f was varied (0.3, 0.8, and 2.0 fm/c) to study its effect on the HBT radii across different charged-particle multiplicity classes.

## Key numbers

- In p+p collisions at √s = 7 TeV, the formation time in string fragmentation is constrained to τ_f ≤ 0.8 fm/c; values of τ_f ≥ 2 fm/c are ruled out.
- For p+p collisions, the UrQMD average dN_ch/dη is 15% smaller than ALICE data across eight multiplicity classes (e.g., UrQMD 2.52 vs. ALICE 3.2 for N_charged = 1–11; UrQMD 27.57 vs. ALICE 31.1 for N_charged = 58–149).
- In central Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, R_out is overpredicted by nearly 50% compared to ALICE data.
- The calculated R_out/R_side ratio at LHC is markedly larger than data; artificially decreasing the emission duration by a factor of t_scale = 2 or 3 improves agreement.
- The calculated HBT radii for Pb+Pb at LHC are larger than those for Au+Au at RHIC (√s_NN = 200 GeV), with the largest increase in the longitudinal direction.

## Context

This work extends earlier UrQMD studies of bulk properties at LHC energies (Mitrovski 2008, Petersen 2011) and compares directly to ALICE HBT data for p+p (Aamodt 2011) and Pb+Pb (Aamodt 2011) collisions. The overestimation of R_out and R_out/R_side is attributed to insufficient early-stage pressure in the cascade model, consistent with findings by Pratt (2008) and Li (2007). The authors suggest that an improved hybrid approach combining Boltzmann transport with hydrodynamics (Petersen 2008, Steinheimer 2010) may resolve the discrepancy by incorporating a proper equation of state for the QGP phase.
