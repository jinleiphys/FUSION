# Pion production in d+Au collisions at RHIC energy

> We present our results on neutral pion pi0 production in pp and dAu collisions at RHIC energy. Pion spectra are calculated in a next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD-based model. The model includes the transverse component of the initial parton distribution (``intrinsic kT''). We compare our results to the available experimental data from RHIC, and fit the data with high precision. The calculation tuned this way is repeated for the dAu collision, and used to investigate the interplay of shadowing and multiple scattering at RHIC. The centrality dependence of the nuclear modification factor shows a measurable difference between different shadowing parameterizations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD model with intrinsic transverse momentum and nuclear multiscattering can reproduce neutral pion production data in pp and dAu collisions at RHIC, but the centrality dependence of the nuclear modification factor shows a measurable difference between impact-parameter-dependent and impact-parameter-independent shadowing parameterizations, which is largely averaged out in minimum bias data.

## Method

The authors calculate neutral pion production in pp and dAu collisions at RHIC energy using an NLO pQCD-improved parton model that includes a Gaussian intrinsic transverse momentum distribution for initial partons. For dAu collisions, they incorporate nuclear multiscattering via broadening of the parton transverse momentum distribution and nuclear shadowing using the HIJING and EKS parameterizations, with the collision geometry described by the Glauber model. The model parameters are tuned by fitting pp data at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, then applied to dAu collisions to compute the nuclear modification factor R_dAu and its centrality dependence.

## Key numbers

- Intrinsic k_T width in pp collisions: ⟨k_T^2⟩ = 2.5 GeV^2/c^2
- Multiscattering parameter C = 0.4 GeV^2/c^2 (with sample calculation also using C = 1 GeV^2/c^2)
- Maximum number of semi-hard collisions contributing to broadening: ν_A(b)_max = 4
- Nuclear modification factor R_dAu shows Cronin enhancement of approximately 10-15% in minimum bias dAu collisions
- At fixed p_T = 3.7 GeV/c, the centrality dependence of R_dAu differs between b-independent and b-dependent shadowing parameterizations
- Double ratio R* = R_dAu(0-50%)/R_dAu(50-90%) shows approximately 30% difference between b-independent and b-dependent shadowing in the window 2 GeV/c < p_T < 3 GeV/c
- NLO calculation uses scales Q=Q_R=(4/3)p_q and Q_F=(4/3)p_T, with κ=4/3

## Context

This work extends earlier LO analyses of pA data by YZ02 and LO calculations of dAu by Vitev to NLO accuracy, utilizing recent NLO developments for pp collisions by pgNLO. The results are compared to PHENIX experimental data on π0 production in dAu collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, including centrality-dependent measurements from PHENIXdAu07centr. The paper enables future investigations of the interplay between nuclear shadowing, multiscattering, and possible jet energy loss in cold nuclear matter, as noted in subsequent work by EMC07 using the HKM shadowing function.
