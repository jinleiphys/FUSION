# Big Bang Nucleosynthesis constraints on the Energy-Momentum Squared Gravity: The $\mathbb{T}^{2}$ model

> Scale-independent energy-momentum squared gravity (EMSG) allows different gravitational couplings for different types of sources and has been proven to have interesting implications in cosmology. In this paper, the Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) formalism and the latest observational constraints on nuclear abundances are being used to put bounds on this class of modified gravity models. Using the tight constraint from BBN on the correction term in the Friedmann equation in EMSG scenario, we report the allowed deviation from the standard cosmic expansion rate.

## Key claim

The Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) epoch provides stringent constraints on the Energy-Momentum Squared Gravity (EMSG) model parameter \(\alpha\), requiring it to be negative to match observed light-element abundances, which also satisfies the theoretical stability condition against ghost and gradient instabilities.

## Method

The authors incorporated the modified Friedmann equation from EMSG, which includes a correction term proportional to \(\alpha\) times squared energy density and pressure terms, into a BBN calculation code with updated reaction rates from the JINA REACLIB database. They computed the cosmic expansion rate and primordial abundances of D, \(^{3}\)He, \(^{4}\)He, and \(^{7}\)Li as functions of \(\alpha\), using the central neutron lifetime from the Particle Data Group and the baryon-to-photon ratio from Planck CMB observations, then compared the resulting abundances to observational data.

## Key numbers

- The D/H abundance from metal-poor Lyman-\(\alpha\) absorption (D/H = \(2.527 \pm 0.030\)) constrains \(\alpha\) to \(-2.45 \times 10^{-26}\,\mathrm{GeV^{-6}}\) at \(2\sigma\) and \(-4.20 \times 10^{-26}\,\mathrm{GeV^{-6}}\) at \(4\sigma\).
- The \(^{4}\)He mass fraction from metal-poor extra-galactic H II regions (\(Y_p = 0.2448 \pm 0.0033\)) constrains \(\alpha\) to \(-5.25 \times 10^{-27}\,\mathrm{GeV^{-6}}\) at \(2\sigma\) and \(-13.3 \times 10^{-27}\,\mathrm{GeV^{-6}}\) at \(4\sigma\).
- The \(^{3}\)He abundance is consistent with the observational upper limit of \(^{3}\mathrm{He/H} = 1.1 \pm 0.2\) for all \(\alpha\) values considered.
- The \(^{7}\)Li abundance for negative \(\alpha\) is higher than the observational data of \(^{7}\mathrm{Li/H} = 1.58 \pm 0.31\), worsening the standard BBN lithium over-prediction.

## Context

This work extends previous constraints on the EMSG model from CMB and BAO data (Akarsu et al., 2018) by applying the more stringent test of BBN, finding that BBN bounds are much tighter than those combined CMB+BAO bounds. The modified expansion rate in EMSG, which scales as \(T^8\) for the correction term, affects only the initial stage of BBN around \(T \sim 1\,\mathrm{MeV}\) and then converges to the standard rate. The results enable future studies of EMSG in conjunction with inflationary observables and comparisons to other modified gravity frameworks such as braneworld cosmology and loop quantum gravity.
