# Intrinsic Density Matrices of the Nuclear Shell Model

> A new method for calculation of shell model intrinsic density matrices, defined as two-particle density matrices integrated over the centre-of-mass position vector of two last particles and complemented with isospin variables, has been developed. The intrinsic density matrices obtained are completely antisymmetric, translation-invariant, and do not employ a group-theoretical classification of antisymmetric states. They are used for exact realistic density matrix expansion within the framework of the reduced Hamiltonian method. The procedures based on precise arithmetic for calculation of the intrinsic density matrices that involve no numerical diagonalization or orthogonalization have been developed and implemented in the computer code.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new method for calculating intrinsic density matrices of the nuclear shell model that avoids group-theoretical classification of antisymmetric states and requires no numerical diagonalization or orthogonalization, instead relying on precise arithmetic and fractional parentage coefficient expansions. The method is claimed to be simpler and more effective than the translationally invariant shell model, though it is limited to systems where the center-of-mass is in its ground state.

## Method

The authors construct intrinsic density matrices (two-particle density matrices integrated over the center-of-mass position vector of the two last particles, complemented with isospin variables) by expanding shell model wave functions in terms of intrinsic wave functions and center-of-mass ground state functions. They use generalized fractional parentage coefficients (GCFP) to separate two last particles from the A-particle system, then apply Talmi-Moshinsky-Smirnov transformations to convert from single-particle coordinates to Jacobi coordinates, specifically isolating the relative coordinate of the two last particles (ξ₂). The intrinsic density matrix in harmonic oscillator representation is obtained by summing over grandparent states and two-particle quantum numbers, with coefficients derived from the expansion coefficients relating shell model and intrinsic wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The intrinsic density matrix reduces the number of variables from 5A-3 (for the full wave function) to 7+7 orbital, spin and isospin variables
- The center-of-mass Hamiltonian minimal eigenvalue is 3/2, which defines the subspace of intrinsic motion
- The transformation coefficients include factors of (2n_r n_p / A(A-1))^{1/2} for two particles from different shells and (n_r(n_r-1)/A(A-1))^{1/2} for two particles from the same shell
- The antisymmetrization factor for two nucleons from the same shell is (1/2)[1-(-1)^{J''+T''}]

## Context

This work extends the translationally invariant shell model approach of earlier authors (references 1-3 in the paper) by providing a practical computational method that avoids the group-theoretical classification of antisymmetric states. The method builds on the fractional parentage coefficient formalism developed in reference 4 and the Jacobi coordinate transformation techniques of references 7-8. It enables exact realistic density matrix expansion within the Reduced Hamiltonian method framework, allowing calculation of expectation values of symmetric operators (intrinsic Hamiltonian, root-mean-square radius operators) without requiring the complete wave function, which is particularly relevant for describing exotic nuclei with weak binding energies where large-space shell model expansions are necessary.
