# New estimator for symmetry plane correlations in anisotropic flow analyses

> Correlations of symmetry planes are important observables used to quantify anisotropic flow phenomenon and constrain independently the properties of strongly interacting nuclear matter produced in the collisions of heavy ions at the highest energies. In this paper, we point out current problems of measuring correlations between symmetry planes and elaborate on why the available analysis techniques have a large systematic bias. To overcome this problem, we introduce a new approach to approximate multi-harmonic flow fluctuations via a two-dimensional Gaussian distribution. Employing this approximation, we introduce a new estimator, dubbed Gaussian Estimator (GE), to extract pure correlation between symmetry planes. We validate GE by using the realistic event-generator iEBE-VISHNU and demonstrate that it outperforms all existing estimators. Based on event-shape engineering, we propose an experimental procedure to improve GE accuracy even further.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new estimator (Gaussian Estimator, GE) for symmetry plane correlations that corrects for the large systematic bias present in existing methods (scalar product and event plane methods), which arises from neglecting correlated event-by-event fluctuations of different flow amplitudes. The GE approximates multi-harmonic flow fluctuations via a two-dimensional Gaussian distribution and is validated using the iEBE-VISHNU event generator, showing it outperforms all existing estimators, though minor discrepancies remain for some specific combinations of symmetry planes.

## Method

The authors model the joint fluctuations of the product of flow amplitudes and the cosine of the symmetry plane combination (quantities R_x and R_y) as a two-dimensional Gaussian distribution. From this approximation, they derive an analytical expression for the Gaussian Estimator (GE) of symmetry plane correlations, which involves the ratio of the mean of R_x to the square root of the sum of variances of R_x and R_y, multiplied by a factor sqrt(pi/4). They validate the GE against the true symmetry plane correlations computed directly from event-by-event symmetry planes in iEBE-VISHNU simulations of Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, and propose an event-shape engineering procedure to further improve accuracy by removing outlier events with large R values.

## Key numbers

- The Gaussian Estimator formula includes a numerical factor sqrt(pi/4) approximately equal to 0.886.
- For validation, 14,000 events per centrality bin of Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV were generated using iEBE-VISHNU with shear viscosity over entropy density eta/s = 0.08.
- In the event-shape engineering correction, events with R > 2 sigma_r (alpha = 2) are classified as high R class; this class contains approximately 6% of all events in the simulation.
- For the specific case of R = v2 v4 v6, Theta = 2 Psi_2 + 4 Psi_4 - 6 Psi_6 at 40% centrality, the ratio sigma_x/sigma_y computed from all events is around 3, while using only low R class events this ratio reduces to 1.7.

## Context

This work extends previous experimental studies of symmetry plane correlations by ATLAS (Jia et al., 2012; Aad et al., 2014) and theoretical studies by Bhalerao et al. (2011, 2013) and Luzum et al. (2012), which used scalar product or event plane methods that suffer from systematic bias when correlated flow amplitude fluctuations are present. The new Gaussian Estimator enables more reliable extraction of pure symmetry plane correlations from experimental data, providing independent constraints on the properties of the quark-gluon plasma and initial collision geometry, and can be further optimized using event-shape engineering techniques (Schukraft et al., 2012).
