# The excited state of the $α$-particle: a benchmark study

> A benchmark study is performed for the excited state of $^4$He. When the Coulomb interaction is switched off, the $^4$He nucleus exhibits a bound excited state in the vicinity of $p-{}^3$H threshold. As the Coulomb interaction is gradually introduced, the excited state crosses the threshold and eventually becomes a resonant state. Using three numerical methods, we track the evolution of this excited state and determine the resonance energy and width. Comparisons of the theoretical predictions reveal a significant discrepancy with commonly used $R$-matrix values based on the analysis of the experimental data. We explain the origin for this discrepancy. Additionally, the two-level energy spectrum of $^4$He in the absence of the Coulomb force exhibits characteristics linked to Efimov physics, suggesting a reduced sensitivity to interaction details.

## Key claim

When the Coulomb interaction is fully included, the excited state of \(^4\)He becomes a resonance; the authors track its evolution using three numerical methods and find a significant discrepancy with the commonly used \(R\)-matrix values extracted from experimental data, which they explain as originating from the treatment of the Coulomb interaction in the \(R\)-matrix analysis.

## Method

The authors solved the four-nucleon scattering problem using three independent numerical methods: the Faddeev-Yakubovsky (FY) equations in configuration space, the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations in momentum space with a screened and renormalized Coulomb potential, and the Hyperspherical Harmonic (HH) expansion method with the Kohn variational principle. They used the chiral N3LO-EM nucleon-nucleon potential (500 MeV cutoff) and varied the Coulomb interaction strength via a parameter \(\lambda_c\) from 0 (no Coulomb) to 1 (full Coulomb), tracking the \(0^+\) excited state of \(^4\)He from a bound state below the \(p\)-\(^3\)H threshold to a resonance above it.

## Key numbers

* At \(\lambda_c = 0\) (no Coulomb), the \(^4\)He excited state binding energy relative to \(^3\)H is 0.076 MeV (FY), 0.074 MeV (AGS), and 0.07(1) MeV (HH); the \(p\)-\(^3\)H scattering length is 18.0(2) fm (FY), 18.7(6) fm (AGS), and 23(1) fm (HH).
* At \(\lambda_c = 0.25\), the excited state is still bound with a binding energy relative to \(^3\)H of 0.005 MeV (FY); the \(p\)-\(^3\)H scattering length is 266(40) fm (FY) and 326(50) fm (HH).
* At \(\lambda_c = 0.3\), the excited state has crossed the threshold; the \(p\)-\(^3\)H scattering length becomes negative: -363(30) fm (FY) and -400(50) fm (HH).
* At \(\lambda_c = 1.0\) (full Coulomb), the \(p\)-\(^3\)H scattering length is -18.5(2) fm (FY) and -17(1) fm (HH).
* The critical value of \(\lambda_c\) where the bound state crosses the threshold is \(\lambda_0 = 0.28\) (FY interpolation).
* The ground state binding energy of \(^4\)He at \(\lambda_c = 1\) is 25.396(10) MeV; the difference between \(^3\)H and \(^3\)He binding energies at \(\lambda_c = 1\) is 0.726(1) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the benchmark study performed for the \(^4\)He ground state by Kamada (2001) and the scattering benchmark for \(p\)-\(^3\)H and \(n\)-\(^3\)He by Viviani (2017) to the \(0^+\) excited state of \(^4\)He. The authors show that the excited state's behavior is linked to Efimov physics, as the two-level energy spectrum of \(^4\)He without Coulomb exhibits characteristics of universal physics seen in bosonic systems near the unitary limit (Platter 2004, Hammer 2007, Kievsky 2014, Gatto 2019). The results enable precise theoretical predictions for the \(^4\)He resonance that can be compared with future experimental analyses, and they explain why the commonly used \(R\)-matrix values (Tilley 1992) disagree with the theoretical calculations.
