# Quantum corrections to screening at strong coupling

> We compute a certain class of corrections to (specific) screening lengths in strongly coupled nonabelian plasmas using the AdS/CFT correspondence. In this holographic framework, these corrections arise from various higher curvature interactions modifying the leading Einstein gravity action. The changes in the screening lengths are perturbative in inverse powers of the 't Hooft coupling or of the number of colours, as can be made precise in the context where the dual gauge theory is superconformal. We also compare the results of these holographic calculations to lattice results for the analogous screening lengths in QCD. In particular, we apply these results within the program of making quantitative comparisons between the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma and holographic descriptions of conformal field theory.

## Key claim

The paper computes leading perturbative corrections to specific screening lengths (mass gap and screening masses for vector and spin-2 exchange) in strongly coupled nonabelian plasmas using holography, but only for those screening masses corresponding to graviton exchange; corrections to screening masses from dilaton, axion, and other supergravity modes are beyond current technical capability.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence to compute screening masses from spatial correlators of Polyakov loops in the strongly coupled plasma. They work in the AdS soliton background and solve linearized graviton equations of motion with higher curvature corrections (Weyl tensor contractions) added to the Einstein-Hilbert action, using a shooting method to find eigenvalues that satisfy boundary conditions of regularity at the horizon and asymptotic falloff as 1/r^4. The leading higher curvature correction considered is the supersymmetric W^4 term, which gives corrections proportional to the small parameter gamma = zeta(3)/(8 lambda^{3/2}) + sqrt(lambda)/(384 N_c^2).

## Key numbers

* Mass gap (scalar channel) at leading order: M_0 = 2.336 pi T; with first-order correction: M = pi T (2.336 - 139.514 gamma)
* Vector channel screening mass at leading order: M_{v0} = 4.322 pi T
* Spin-2 channel screening mass at leading order: M_{s0} = 3.404 pi T
* Temperature relation with correction: T = (R_0 / (L^2 pi)) (1 + 15 gamma)
* Entropy density: s = (2 pi^4 L^3 T^3 / ell_p^3) (1 + 15 gamma)
* Free energy: w = -(pi^4 L^3 T^4 / (2 ell_p^3)) (1 + 15 gamma)
* For lambda = 6 pi, N_c = 3 (alpha_s = 0.5), gamma is approximately 0.003

## Context

This work extends the holographic screening mass calculations of Bak et al. (2007) and Constable and Myers (1999) by including higher curvature corrections that go beyond the leading Einstein gravity approximation. The results are part of a phenomenological program initiated by Buchel et al. (2008) to make quantitative comparisons between holographic descriptions of conformal field theory and lattice QCD results for screening lengths in the quark-gluon plasma. The holographic screening masses are larger than lattice results, and the authors hope that finite coupling and 1/N_c corrections will improve agreement.
