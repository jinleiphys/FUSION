# The mean free path of protons and neutrons in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter

> We calculate the mean free path of protons and neutrons in symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter, based on microscopic in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections. Those are obtained from calculations of the G-matrix including relativistic "Dirac" effects. The dependence of the mean free path on energy and isospin asymmetry is discussed. We conclude by suggesting possible ways our microscopic predictions may be helpful in conjunction with studies of rare isotopes.

## Key claim
The mean free path of protons and neutrons in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter shows strong sensitivity to isospin asymmetry only at energies near the respective Fermi surfaces; at higher energies the mean free path becomes essentially insensitive to isospin asymmetry.

## Method
In-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections were calculated from a G-matrix including relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) effects, using the Bonn-B potential as the free-space two-nucleon force. The mean free path was then obtained from these cross sections and the neutron and proton densities via the relation λ = 1/(ρ_p σ_pp + ρ_n σ_pn) for protons (and analogously for neutrons), with the Pauli operator set to zero the cross section when the two-body c.m. momentum q_0 is at or below the Fermi level.

## Key numbers
- In symmetric matter at average Fermi momentum k_F = 1.1 fm⁻¹, the pp cross section at kinetic energy T = 26.58 MeV is 23.46 mb and the np cross section is 60.57 mb; at T = 361.68 MeV, σ_pp = 32.63 mb and σ_np = 29.08 mb.
- In symmetric matter at k_F = 1.4 fm⁻¹, the pp cross section at T = 46.76 MeV is 13.70 mb and the np cross section is 17.26 mb; at T = 361.68 MeV, σ_pp = 35.40 mb and σ_np = 32.34 mb.
- In asymmetric matter with average Fermi momentum 1.4 fm⁻¹ and isospin asymmetry α = 0.5, the pp cross section at T = 26.58 MeV is 15.51 mb while the nn cross section is still 0.0000 mb; at T = 361.68 MeV, σ_pp = 36.06 mb, σ_nn = 34.50 mb, and σ_np = 32.21 mb.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous microscopic predictions of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections in isospin-symmetric and asymmetric matter (Sammarruca 2005) to the calculation of the nucleon mean free path. It contrasts with the approach of Jiang and collaborators (2007), who obtained mean free paths from the nucleon optical potential using empirical NN scattering amplitudes and the relativistic mean field model, then extracted in-medium cross sections; here the calculation proceeds in the opposite direction, from microscopic cross sections to mean free path. The results enable improved input for transport models such as the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) equation used in heavy ion collision simulations, and may ultimately help extract nuclear radii from reaction cross section data via the nuclear transparency function within Glauber-type models.
