# Strong Matter: Rethinking Philosophically

> Normal condensed matter is merely of electromagnetic interaction. A novel state of strong-interaction matter is revisited.

## Key claim

The paper argues that 3-flavored (u, d, s quarks) "strangeon" matter, rather than 2-flavored nucleonic matter, could be the true ground state of strong-interaction matter in bulk, but only for systems with baryon number larger than about 10^9; small nuclei remain 2-flavored because the strange quark mass is much larger than the electron mass.

## Method

The author presents a phenomenological and philosophical argument based on the energy scale of strong matter at a few nuclear densities (E_scale ~ 0.5 GeV), comparing it to the chiral symmetry breaking scale (Lambda_chi > 1 GeV) and the strange-up/down quark mass difference (Delta m_uds ~ 0.1 GeV). Using a Coulomb-like analogy with electromagnetic matter, the author estimates the density of strong matter and introduces a "flavor maximization principle" whereby short-range interactions favor maximizing quark flavors to minimize total energy, leading to a 3-flavor state for sufficiently large systems. The paper then reviews observational tests of the resulting "strangeon star" model against pulsar mass measurements and gravitational-wave data.

## Key numbers

- The separation between quarks in strong matter at a few nuclear densities is ~ 0.5 fm, giving an energy scale E_scale ~ 0.5 GeV.
- The mass difference between strange and up/down quarks is Delta m_uds ~ 0.1 GeV.
- The estimated density of strong matter is rho_SM ~ 2 x 10^15 g/cm^3, using alpha_s = 1 and dressed quark mass m_q ~ 0.3 GeV.
- The critical baryon number for 3-flavor symmetry restoration is A > (lambda_e/fm)^3 ~ 10^9, where lambda_e ~ 10^3 fm is the electron Compton wavelength.
- The maximum mass of a strangeon star could be as high as 3 solar masses.
- The dimensionless tidal polarizability for a strangeon star of ~1.4 solar masses is Lambda ~ 400.
- The number density of strangeon dark matter around Earth is n_sdm ~ 0.1/A cm^-3.
- For strangeon dark matter with baryon number A_30 = A/10^30 = 1, the event rate penetrating Earth is ~ 80/A_30 yr^-1, the Moon ~ 6/A_30 yr^-1, and the Sun ~ 10^6/A_30 yr^-1.
- The rest mass of a strangeon nugget with A ~ 10^10 is ~ 10^19 eV.
- The estimated event rate of energetic strangeon air-showers is ~ 0.1 A_10^-1 km^-2 yr^-1 (A_10 = A/10^10).

## Context

This work extends the conjecture of Witten (1984) that strange quark matter in bulk could be the true ground state of strong-interaction matter, and the author's own earlier proposal (Xu 2003) that quarks remain clustered as "strangeons" rather than free. The paper contrasts the strangeon star model with conventional neutron star models and with strange quark star models, noting that the strangeon star passes the tests of massive pulsar observations (PSR J1614-2230, PSR J0348+0432) and the gravitational-wave event GW 170817. The paper also discusses implications for dark matter (as strangeon nuggets from the cosmic QCD phase transition) and for ultrahigh-energy cosmic rays, and suggests that future facilities like FAST and eXTP could provide decisive tests.
