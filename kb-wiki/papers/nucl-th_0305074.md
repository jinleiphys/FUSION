# Meson Exchange Currents in (e,e'p) recoil polarization observables

> A study of the effects of meson-exchange currents and isobar configurations in $A(\vec{e},e'\vec{p})B$ reactions is presented. We use a distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) model where final-state interactions are treated through a phenomenological optical potential. The model includes relativistic corrections in the kinematics and in the electromagnetic one- and two-body currents. The full set of polarized response functions is analyzed, as well as the transferred polarization asymmetry. Results are presented for proton knock-out from closed-shell nuclei, for moderate to high momentum transfer.

## Key claim

Meson-exchange currents (MEC) and isobar configurations produce measurable effects on recoil polarization observables in $A(\vec{e},e'\vec{p})B$ reactions, but these effects are kinematically dependent and often smaller than those from final-state interactions (FSI) and relativistic corrections, particularly at higher momentum transfer.

## Method

The authors extended a distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) model to include semi-relativistic one-body and two-body (MEC) electromagnetic current operators, obtained via Pauli reduction of relativistic operators expanded only in missing momentum over nucleon mass. Final-state interactions were treated through a phenomenological optical potential derived from the Schrodinger-equivalent form of a scalar-vector Dirac optical potential. The full set of eighteen polarized response functions and transferred polarization asymmetries were computed via multipole expansion of the nuclear current operators for proton knockout from closed-shell nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Two kinematic regimes studied: (i) $q=460$ MeV/c, $\omega=100$ MeV and (ii) $q=1$ GeV/c, $\omega=450$ MeV, both near the quasielastic peak
- Up to $\sim 36$ multipoles needed for convergence at $q=1$ GeV/c
- Results presented for proton knockout from $p_{1/2}$ and $p_{3/2}$ shells in $^{16}$O
- For high missing momentum $p\ge 300$ MeV/c, the $A_{TL}$ asymmetry shows an oscillatory structure consistent with dynamical relativistic calculations

## Context

This work extends the DWIA+MEC model developed for unpolarized reactions by Amaro et al. (1999, 2003) to spin observables in $(\vec{e},e'\vec{p})$ processes. It builds on prior studies of recoil polarization by the Pavia group (Boffi et al., 1990, 1991) and the Gent group (Ryckebusch et al., 1999, 2001), but uses a different MEC formalism that avoids effective one-body operators. The semi-relativistic approach provides a computationally tractable way to incorporate relativistic effects into existing non-relativistic descriptions, enabling exploration of the high momentum region where a complete relativistic distorted wave analysis of MEC is still lacking.
