# Core polarization for the electric quadrupole moment of neutron-rich Aluminum isotopes

> The core polarization effect for the electric quadrupole moment of the neutron-rich $^{31}$Al, $^{33}$Al and $^{35}$Al isotopes in the vicinity of the island of inversion are investigated by means of the microscopic particle-vibration coupling model in which the Skyrme Hartee-Fock-Bogoliubov and quasiparticle-random-phase approximation are used to calculate the single-quasiparticle wave functions and the excitation modes. It is found that the polarization charge for the proton $1d_{5/2}$ hole state in $^{33}$Al is quite sensitive to coupling to the neutrons in the $pf$-shell associated with the pairing correlations, and that the polarization charge in $^{35}$Al becomes larger due to the stronger collectivity of the low-lying quadrupole vibrational mode in the neighboring $^{36}$Si nucleus.

## Key claim

The polarization charge for the proton \(1d_{5/2}\) hole state in neutron-rich Aluminum isotopes is sensitive to coupling to neutrons in the \(pf\)-shell via pairing correlations, and the polarization charge in \(^{35}\)Al becomes larger due to the stronger collectivity of the low-lying quadrupole vibrational mode in the neighboring \(^{36}\)Si nucleus, but the results carry a theoretical uncertainty from the scaling factor \(f_{\mathrm{LM}}\) applied to the particle-vibration coupling interaction.

## Method

The author performed microscopic particle-vibration coupling (PVC) calculations on top of the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) and quasiparticle-random-phase approximation (QRPA) in coordinate space, using the SkM* interaction for the mean field and a mixed-type pairing interaction. Odd-\(A\) Aluminum isotopes were described as a proton single-hole state coupled to the neighboring Silicon isotopes, and the Hamiltonian was diagonalized within a subspace of one-quasiparticle and phonon-coupled states. The polarization charge was extracted from the calculated electric quadrupole moment relative to the bare single-hole value.

## Key numbers

*   Quadrupole moment of \(^{31}\)Al: \(13.6\ (13.9)\ e\,\mathrm{fm}^2\) for \(f_{\mathrm{LM}}=0.8\ (0.9)\), corresponding to a polarization charge \(e^{\pi}_{\mathrm{pol}} = 1.03e\ (1.08e)\); the experimental value is \(11.2 \pm 3.2\ e\,\mathrm{fm}^2\) (Nagatomo 2008).
*   Quadrupole moment of \(^{33}\)Al: \(13.0\ (13.4)\ e\,\mathrm{fm}^2\) for \(f_{\mathrm{LM}}=0.8\ (0.9)\), with polarization charge \(e^{\pi}_{\mathrm{pol}} = 0.89e\ (0.96e)\).
*   Quadrupole moment of \(^{35}\)Al: \(14.7\ (15.1)\ e\,\mathrm{fm}^2\) for \(f_{\mathrm{LM}}=0.8\ (0.9)\), with polarization charge \(e^{\pi}_{\mathrm{pol}} = 1.12e\ (1.18e)\).
*   When only RPA modes above 10 MeV were included, the polarization charges were \(0.20e\ (0.22e)\) in \(^{31}\)Al, \(0.21e\ (0.24e)\) in \(^{33}\)Al, and \(0.18e\ (0.20e)\) in \(^{35}\)Al, close to the systematic values of \(0.21e\), \(0.18e\), and \(0.15e\) from Suzuki (2003).
*   Without pairing correlations in \(^{34}\)Si, the \(2_1^+\) state in \(^{34}\)Si had an isoscalar strength of only \(237\ \mathrm{fm}^4\), and the polarization charge in \(^{33}\)Al dropped to \(e^{\pi}_{\mathrm{pol}} = 0.59e\ (0.65e)\).
*   The isoscalar transition strengths for the \(2_1^+\) state were \(626\), \(637\), and \(1137\ \mathrm{fm}^4\) in \(^{32,34,36}\)Si, corresponding to 104, 98, and 162 Weisskopf units, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic particle-vibration coupling approach, previously applied by Suzuki (2003) using only giant resonances on top of HF+RPA, to include low-lying collective modes and pairing correlations via the Skyrme HFB+QRPA framework. The results are directly relevant to recent electric quadrupole moment measurements of \(^{33}\)Al at GANIL (Nagatomo 2009) and connect to the broader discussion of the "island of inversion" at \(N=20\), where the small excitation energy and large \(B(E2)\) in \(^{32}\)Mg (Motobayashi 1995) indicate breaking of the spherical magic number. The finding that neutron pairing correlations are crucial for the collectivity in \(^{34}\)Si parallels the enhancement mechanism of \(B(E2)\) in \(^{32}\)Mg (Yamagami 2004). The calculations enable predictions for the quadrupole moments of \(^{31}\)Al and \(^{35}\)Al, which can be tested by future experiments.
