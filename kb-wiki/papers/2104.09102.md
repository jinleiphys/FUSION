# Dynamics of equilibration and collisions in ultradilute quantum droplets

> Employing time-dependent density-functional theory, we have studied dynamical equilibration and binary head-on collisions of quantum droplets made of a $^{39}$K-$^{39}$K Bose mixture. The phase space of collision outcomes is extensively explored by performing fully three-dimensional calculations with effective single-component QMC based and two-components LHY-corrected mean-field functionals. We exhaustively explored the important effect -- not considered in previous studies -- of the initial population ratio deviating from the optimal mean-field value $N_2/N_1 = \sqrt{a_{11} / a_{22}}$. Both stationary and dynamical calculations with an initial non-optimal concentration ratio display good agreement with experiments. Calculations including three-body losses acting only on the $\left|F, m_{F}\right\rangle=|1,0\rangle$ state show dramatic differences with those obtained with the three-body term acting on the total density.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the experimentally observed critical velocity for droplet separation in head-on collisions of ultradilute quantum droplets can be explained by deviations from the optimal population ratio between the two components, without necessarily invoking three-body losses, contrary to the interpretation of Ferioli et al. (2019).

## Method

The authors performed fully three-dimensional time-dependent density-functional theory calculations for head-on collisions of 39K-39K Bose mixture droplets, using both an effective single-component Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) based functional and a two-component LHY-corrected mean-field functional. They systematically varied the initial atom number ratio between the two hyperfine states, defined as x = (N2/N1) * sqrt(a22/a11), and compared results with and without three-body losses acting only on the |1,0> state.

## Key numbers

- Equilibrium density at B = 56.54 G: rho_0 = 1.217 x 10^15 cm^-3, with healing length xi = 1.479 micrometers
- Equilibrium density at B = 56.23 G: rho_0 = 5.773 x 10^15 cm^-3, with healing length xi = 0.496 micrometers
- Three-body loss coefficients explored: K_111 = 0, 1.1, and 22 in units of hbar/(m xi^2 rho_0^2), corresponding to 0, 5.5 x 10^-28 cm^6/s, and 1.1 x 10^-26 cm^6/s respectively
- Initial droplet atom number for stationary calculations: N = 2.5 x 10^5 atoms, with Gaussian width sigma_r = 3 micrometers
- Initial droplet separation for collision simulations: d approximately 3500 a_11, approximately 9 xi

## Context

This work extends the experimental study of head-on collisions between 39K-39K droplets by Ferioli et al. (2019), who found a discrepancy between experimental critical velocities and theoretical predictions using the standard MF+LHY approach. The authors also build on the stationary droplet measurements of Semeghini et al. (2018), which showed significant differences in three-body loss intensities between components. By using a two-component framework that allows non-optimal population ratios, the authors provide an alternative explanation to the experimental results that does not require invoking three-body losses, and show that the QMC-based functional including effective-range corrections yields more binding than the MF+LHY functional.
