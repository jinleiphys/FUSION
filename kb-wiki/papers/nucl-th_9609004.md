# Antikaon flow in heavy-ion collisions: the effects of absorption and mean fields

> We study antikaon flow in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies based on the relativistic transport model (RVUU 1.0). The production of antikaons from both baryon-baryon and pion-baryon collisions are included. Taking into account only elastic and inelastic collisions of the antikaon with nucleons and neglecting its mean-field potential as in the cascade model, a strong antiflow or anti-correlation of antikaons with respect to nucleons is seen as a result of the strong absorption of antikaons by nucleons. However, the antiflow of antikaons disappears after including also their propagation in the attractive mean-field potential. The experimental measurement of antikaon flow in heavy-ion collision will be very useful in shedding lights on the relative importance of antikaon absorption versus its mean-field potential.

# Antikaon flow in heavy-ion collisions: the effects of absorption and mean fields

## Key claim

The strong antiflow (anti-correlation) of antikaons with respect to nucleons predicted by cascade-type calculations disappears when antikaon propagation in an attractive mean-field potential is included, making the antikaon distribution almost isotropic. The experimental observation of such a disappearance would provide evidence for the presence of an antikaon mean-field potential in medium, but the paper does not claim that this effect has been experimentally confirmed.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic transport model (RVUU 1.0) to simulate Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93 GeV/nucleon and impact parameter b=4 fm, including antikaon production from both baryon-baryon and pion-baryon collisions. They compared two scenarios: a cascade-type calculation with only elastic and inelastic (strange-exchange) collisions of antikaons with baryons, and a calculation that additionally included antikaon scalar and vector mean-field potentials derived from a chiral Lagrangian with Brown-Rho scaling, affecting both production thresholds and propagation via equations of motion.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear matter density ρ₀, the antikaon potential is about -190 MeV for a K⁻ at rest, close to the -200±20 MeV found from kaonic atom data.
- In the cascade-type calculation, the antikaon flow parameter (slope at mid-rapidity) is about -50 MeV.
- After including the mean-field potential, the antikaon flow parameter becomes about 15 MeV.
- For Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93 GeV/nucleon, contributions to antikaon production are about 70% from baryon-baryon collisions and 30% from pion-baryon collisions.
- The isospin-averaged cross section for πN → NK̄K is given by (7/3) times the parameterization σ(π⁻p → nK⁺K⁻) = 0.08(√s - √s₀)² / [0.043 + (√s - √s₀)²] mb, where √s₀ = m_N + 2m_K.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kaon and lambda flow in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies (Li 1995, Brown 1996, Li 1996) to antikaons, where the FOPI collaboration data on kaon and lambda flow in Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93 GeV/nucleon had already provided evidence for mean-field potential effects. The results contradict earlier cascade-model predictions of strong antikaon antiflow from RQMD and ARC calculations at AGS energies, and suggest that experimental measurement of antikaon flow could distinguish between absorption-dominated and mean-field-dominated scenarios, with similar considerations applicable to antiprotons.
