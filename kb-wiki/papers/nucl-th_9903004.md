# Phase equivalent potentials for three-body halos

> We compare the properties of three-body systems obtained with two-body potentials with Pauli forbidden states and with the corresponding phase equivalent two-body potentials. In the first case the forbidden states are explicitly excluded in the calculation. Differences arise due to the off-shell properties of these on-shell equivalent potentials. We use the adiabatic hyperspherical method to formulate a practical prescription to exclude Pauli forbidden states in three-body calculations. Schematic as well as realistic potentials are used. Almost indistinguishable results are obtained.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that three-body calculations using deep two-body potentials with explicit Pauli forbidden states yield almost indistinguishable results from those using phase-equivalent shallow potentials that lack those bound states, provided the forbidden adiabatic eigenvalues are removed from the basis. The hedge is that this equivalence holds exactly only when all Pauli forbidden states occur in a single two-body subsystem; when more than one subsystem has forbidden states, the small-distance spectra are only approximately identical.

## Method
The authors employ the adiabatic hyperspherical expansion method to solve the three-body Schrödinger equation, first computing angular eigenvalues λ_n(ρ) as functions of the hyperradius ρ for fixed two-body potentials. They construct phase-equivalent two-body potentials by adding a repulsive term ΔV that removes a specific bound state while preserving all on-shell scattering properties, then compare the angular eigenvalue spectra and resulting three-body observables for deep potentials (with forbidden states explicitly excluded) versus their phase-equivalent partners. Analytical derivations of the short-distance and large-distance asymptotic behavior of the angular eigenvalues are performed, supplemented by numerical examples using schematic and realistic potentials.

## Key numbers
- The additional repulsive term ΔV_{lsj}(r) diverges as (ħ²/μ)(2l+3)/r² at short distances (Eq. dpo1) and decays as (4ħ²κ²/μ)exp(-2κr) at large distances (Eq. dpo2).
- For a single Pauli forbidden s-wave bound state, the angular momentum quantum number l_x in the three-body equation is effectively increased by 2 units, shifting the eigenvalue spectrum from ν_n = 2(n+1) to ν̃_n = 2(n+2) at small ρ (Eqs. eig1, fct3).
- At large distances, the angular eigenvalues for non-diverging states behave as λ_n = (2(n+1))² – 4 – [16(n+1)²/π](∑_i ã_i)/ρ, where ã_i are the modified scattering lengths (Eq. large).
- A two-body bound state produces an additional angular eigenvalue that diverges parabolically as λ ≈ –(2mρ²/ħ²)E_b at large ρ (Eq. nubound).

## Context
This work extends earlier approximate tests by Garrido (1997) and Riedel (1996) on using phase-equivalent potentials in three-body halo calculations, providing a rigorous analytical derivation of the asymptotic behavior of the adiabatic potentials. The prescription of removing the lowest angular eigenvalues as Pauli forbidden states formalizes and validates the approach previously used in cluster-model studies of two-neutron halos (Zhu 1993, Garrido 1997). The results enable more accurate treatment of the Pauli principle in three-body calculations for nuclear halos and other spatially extended cluster systems, particularly when higher precision is required or when the overlap between clusters is not negligible.
