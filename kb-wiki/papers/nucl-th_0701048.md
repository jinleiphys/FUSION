# Temperature-dependent errors in nuclear lattice simulations

> We study the temperature dependence of discretization errors in nuclear lattice simulations. We find that for systems with strong attractive interactions the predominant error arises from the breaking of Galilean invariance. We propose a local "well-tempered" lattice action which eliminates much of this error. The well-tempered action can be readily implemented in lattice simulations for nuclear systems as well as cold atomic Fermi systems.

## Key claim

The dominant source of temperature-dependent discretization errors in nuclear lattice simulations for systems with strong attractive interactions is the breaking of Galilean invariance on the lattice, which causes the two-particle ground state energy to deviate from the continuum result proportionally to the square of the total momentum; this error can be substantially reduced by using a local "well-tempered" lattice action that tunes the kinetic energy dispersion relation to match the continuum integral over the Brillouin zone.

## Method

The authors analyze finite-cutoff errors in nuclear lattice simulations by studying a one-dimensional model of spin-1/2 fermions with an attractive zero-range interaction, then extend the analysis to three-dimensional zero-range neutron matter. They compute two-particle energies on the lattice using the standard nearest-neighbor hopping Hamiltonian, an O(a^2)-improved action with next-to-nearest neighbor hopping, and a proposed "well-tempered" action that tunes the coefficient of the O(p_x^4) term in the dispersion relation to match the integral of the continuum kinetic energy over the Brillouin zone. The virial expansion is used to compute the second virial coefficient b_2(T) and relate it to the two-particle scattering phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- For the one-dimensional model with continuum coupling C = -0.1000, the continuum ground state energy at zero total momentum is E_0(p_x=0) = -2.348 MeV; the standard lattice action requires an adjusted coupling C(Lambda) = -0.1105 to match this value.
- The lattice spacing is set at a = (50 MeV)^{-1}, giving a cutoff momentum Lambda = pi a^{-1} approximately 157 MeV.
- The neutron mass is taken as m = 939 MeV throughout the calculations.
- For the well-tempered action in one dimension, the tuning parameter s is found to be s = (2/3)pi^2 - 4 approximately 2.5797, and the adjusted coupling is C(Lambda) = -0.1260.
- The second virial coefficient for a free gas of neutrons is b_2^free(T) = -2^{-5/2} approximately -0.177; in the unitary limit (infinite scattering length, zero effective range) b_2(T) = 3 x 2^{-5/2} approximately 0.530.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of finite-cutoff errors first observed in lattice simulations of zero-range neutron matter by Lee (2005), where sizable lattice errors were found at nonzero temperature but little dependence on lattice spacing was seen at zero temperature. The paper explains why retuning the two-body interaction to match the second virial coefficient b_2(T), as done in Lee (2005), could cancel much of the temperature-dependent error, and proposes the well-tempered action as a more systematic alternative. The results enable improved control of systematic errors in future nuclear lattice simulations and in simulations of cold atomic Fermi systems with strong attractive interactions.
