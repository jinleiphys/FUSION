# Thermalization of dense hadronic matter in Au + Au collisions at the energies available at FAIR

> The conditions of local thermodynamic equilibrium of baryons (non-strange, strange) and mesons (strange) are presented for central Au + Au collisions at FAIR energies using the microscopic transport model UrQMD. The net particle density, longitudinal-to-transverse pressure anisotropy and inverse slope parameters of the energy spectra of non-strange and strange hadrons are calculated inside a cell in the central region within rapidity window $|y| < 1.0$ at different time steps after the collision. We observed that the strangeness content is dominated by baryons at all energies, however contribution from mesons become significant at higher energies. The time scale obtained from local pressure (momentum) isotropization and thermalization of energy spectra are nearly equal and found to decrease with increase in laboratory energy. The equilibrium thermodynamic properties of the system are obtained with statistical thermal model. The time evolution of the entropy densities at FAIR energies are found very similar with the ideal hydrodynamic behaviour at top RHIC energy.

## Key claim

The authors claim that local thermodynamic equilibrium of baryons and strange mesons is achieved in central Au+Au collisions at FAIR energies, but the thermalization time scale depends on beam energy and is about 1.5 times larger than the ad hoc nuclear passage time used in hybrid models by Petersen (2008).

## Method

The authors used the microscopic transport model UrQMD (version 3.3p2) in default cascade mode to simulate central Au+Au collisions at impact parameter b = 2 fm for laboratory energies 10A, 20A, 30A, and 40A GeV. They analyzed a central cell of 2x2x2 fm^3 with rapidity cut |y_cm| < 1.0, calculating the time evolution of net particle density, longitudinal-to-transverse pressure ratio (P_L/P_T), and inverse slope parameters from Tsallis distribution fits to energy spectra of protons and lambdas. For each energy and time step from 1 to 15 fm/c, 6x10^4 events were simulated.

## Key numbers

- Peak baryonic matter density reaches about 7-8 times ground state nuclear matter density at 40A GeV
- Pressure isotropization time for non-strange baryons is about 6.5 fm/c at 10A GeV and decreases to about 5 fm/c at 40A GeV
- The P_L/P_T ratio for baryons reaches a constant value of about 0.8 for t >= 9 fm/c at 10A GeV, and about 0.7 for t >= 6 fm/c at 40A GeV
- The inverse slope parameter T_slope from Tsallis fits shows t^{-1/3} scaling starting at t >= 9 fm/c for 10A GeV and t >= 5 fm/c for 40A GeV
- At 30A GeV, the Tsallis inverse slope parameter for protons at t = 6 fm/c is 0.140 GeV, while the Maxwell-Boltzmann temperature is 0.195 GeV
- The non-extensivity parameter (q-1) remains approximately constant at about 0.03 during the thermal regime
- At 10A GeV, chemical and kinetic freeze-out happen nearly simultaneously at t about 17 fm/c; at 30A GeV, chemical freeze-out occurs at t about 13 fm/c and kinetic freeze-out at t about 15 fm/c

## Context

This work extends earlier UrQMD-based studies of thermalization at AGS energies (UrQMD-1) and provides a test of the local equilibrium assumption that underlies hydrodynamic models of heavy ion collisions. The authors compare their thermalization time scale with the ad hoc switching time used in the hybrid UrQMD+hydrodynamics model by Petersen (2008), finding their estimate is about 1.5 times larger. The entropy density evolution at 30A GeV closely resembles the ideal hydrodynamic behavior at top RHIC energy from Kolb (2003), while at 10A GeV the entropy density falls even faster, suggesting more ideal fluid behavior at lower beam energies consistent with calculations by Denicol (2013) showing that baryon-rich hadron gas is closer to the ideal fluid limit.
