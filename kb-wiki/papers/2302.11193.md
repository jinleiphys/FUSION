# Manifestation of pairing modes in nuclear collisions

> We discuss the possible manifestation of pairing dynamics in nuclear collisions beyond the standard quasi-static treatment of pairing correlations. These involve solitonic excitations induced by pairing phase difference of colliding nuclei and pairing dynamic enhancement in the di-nuclear system formed by merging nuclei.

# Manifestation of pairing modes in nuclear collisions

## Key claim

Pairing dynamics in nuclear collisions may manifest through two distinct mechanisms beyond the standard quasi-static BCS treatment: solitonic excitations generated by phase differences between colliding nuclei, and dynamic enhancement of pairing correlations in the di-nuclear system formed after merging. However, the observed exponential growth of pairing correlations after collision is unlikely to represent a true Higgs mode because its timescale is an order of magnitude longer than the expected Higgs mode period and the excitation energies are close to the critical temperature.

## Method

The authors performed time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) simulations of collisions between medium-mass and heavy nuclei at energies near and above the Coulomb barrier. They tracked the time evolution of the spatially averaged neutron pairing gap $\bar{\Delta}_n$ and analyzed the pairing field distributions to identify solitonic excitations and pairing instability growth. The excitation energies of the di-nuclear systems were estimated using a Thomas-Fermi approach.

## Key numbers

- The pairing gap in atomic nuclei does not exceed 3% of Fermi energy; at subnuclear densities characteristic of neutron star crusts it may reach at most about 5%.
- For the reaction $^{96}$Zr + $^{96}$Zr, TDDFT simulations were performed at center-of-mass energy $E_{cm} = 187$ MeV.
- The typical time scale for Higgs mode oscillations is $\hbar/\bar{\Delta}_n \approx 200$ fm/$c$.
- Estimated excitation energies for neck formation during collision: 20 MeV for $^{90}$Zr+$^{90}$Zr, 27 MeV for $^{90}$Zr+$^{132}$Sn, and 34 MeV for $^{40}$Ca+$^{208}$Pb.
- The additional energy barrier from solitonic excitations scales with phase difference $\Delta\phi$ as $\sin^2(\Delta\phi/2)$.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical investigations of pairing dynamics beyond the adiabatic approximation, particularly the finding by Bulgac et al. (2016) that even during slow fission the pairing field fluctuates rapidly. The solitonic excitation regime was first identified by Magierski et al. (2017), while the Josephson effect analogue in nuclear collisions was studied by Potel et al. (2021). The paper calls for systematic assessment of these pairing effects in nuclear dynamics, especially for understanding the quasifission process relevant to superheavy element synthesis as discussed by Hofmann.
