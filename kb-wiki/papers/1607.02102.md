# Looking for bound states and resonances in the $η^\prime K\bar K$ system

> Motivated by the continuous experimental investigations of $X(1835)$ in three-body decay channels like $η^\prime π^+ π^-$, we investigate the $η^\prime K \bar K$ system with the aim of searching for bound states and/or resonances when the dynamics involved in the $K\bar K$ subsystem can form the resonances: $f_0(980)$ in isospin 0 or $a_0(980)$ in isospin 1. For this, we solve the Faddeev equations for the three-body system. The input two-body $t$-matrices are obtained by solving Bethe-Salpeter equations in a coupled channel formalism. As a result, no signal of a three-body bound state or resonance is found.

## Key claim

Solving the Faddeev equations for the $\eta^\prime K\bar K$ system, with the $K\bar K$ subsystem dynamics that generate $f_0(980)$ or $a_0(980)$, yields no signal of a three-body bound state or resonance, contradicting a prior finding by Albaladejo et al. that reported a state at 1835 MeV.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations for the $\eta^\prime K\bar K$ three-body system without assuming any fixed cluster formation for the $K\bar K$ subsystem. The input two-body $t$-matrices for $\eta^\prime K$, $\eta^\prime \bar K$, and $K\bar K$ were obtained by solving Bethe-Salpeter equations in a coupled-channel formalism, using a U(3) chiral Lagrangian at leading order in the large-$N_c$ limit. The three-body equations were reduced to algebraic form by exploiting cancellations between off-shell two-body amplitudes and contact terms, and the resulting $T$-matrix was evaluated as a function of total energy $\sqrt{s}$ and the invariant mass $\sqrt{s_{23}}$ of the $K\bar K$ pair.

## Key numbers

- The three-body threshold cusp appears at $(\sqrt{s}, \sqrt{s_{23}}) = (1960, 992)$ MeV for both total isospin 0 and 1.
- No structure is found at $\sqrt{s_{23}} \approx 980$ MeV, where the $K\bar K$ system forms $f_0(980)$ (isospin 0) or $a_0(980)$ (isospin 1).
- The three-body loop function cut-off was varied in the range 800-1100 MeV, producing only minor changes in the amplitude size.
- The $X(2370)$ state is stated to be too heavy to be explained as an $\eta^\prime K\bar K$ resonance.

## Context

This work extends the line of three-body Faddeev studies applied to systems such as $KK\bar K$, $\phi K\bar K$, and $J/\psi K\bar K$ by the same authors (Martinez Torres et al., 2011-2013). It directly contradicts the finding of Albaladejo et al. (2012), who reported a state at 1835 MeV with 70 MeV width using a different regularization scheme, and agrees with the null result of Liang et al. (2013) obtained via the fixed-center approximation. The result suggests that the $X(1835)$ and $X(2120)$ structures observed by BESIII in $\eta^\prime \pi^+ \pi^-$ and $\eta K\bar K$ spectra cannot be explained as three-pseudoscalar bound states from $\eta^\prime K\bar K$ dynamics, leaving their origin as an open question.
