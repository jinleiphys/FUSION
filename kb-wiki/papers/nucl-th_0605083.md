# Parity non-conservation in nuclear excitation by circularly polarized photon beam

> We show that the asymmetries in the nuclear resonance fluorescence processes with a circular polarized photon beam may be used as a tool for studying the parity non-conservation (PNC) in nuclei. The PNC asymmetry measurements both in exciting the parity doublet states and in exciting the discrete states near the ground states with parity mixing are discussed. We derived the formulae needed for measuring the PNC asymmetries.

## Key claim

The asymmetry in nuclear resonance fluorescence (NRF) processes using a circularly polarized photon beam can serve as a tool for studying parity non-conservation (PNC) in nuclei, and this asymmetry depends on the angle between the absorbed and emitted photons, which can enhance or reduce the PNC effect compared to the traditional asymmetry measured in photon absorption alone.

## Method

The authors derived the PNC asymmetry formulae for NRF processes by considering the absorption of a circularly polarized photon by a nucleus, followed by decay via photon emission, using first-order perturbation theory for parity mixing between opposite-parity states. They calculated the angular dependence of the asymmetry \(A_{RL}(\theta)\) for specific nuclei including \(^{19}\)F, \(^{18}\)F, and \(^{21}\)Ne, and provided general expressions for transitions with various initial and final spins. The analysis treats the electromagnetic transition amplitudes for absorption and decay separately, connecting them via Wigner rotation functions to account for the different quantization axes of the incoming and outgoing photons.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{19}\)F: the 1/2\(^+\) ground state and 1/2\(^-\) first excited state at 109.9 keV form a parity doublet; the PNC asymmetry is \(A_{RL}(\theta) = (1+\cos\theta)\langle A_{RL}\rangle\).
- For \(^{21}\)Ne: the 1/2\(^-\) and 1/2\(^+\) states at 2789 keV and 2795 keV have a level spacing of 5.7 keV, with a mixing of about \(300\times10^{-4}\) expected; the asymmetry is \(A_{RL}(\theta) \simeq (1 + \frac14\cos\theta)\langle A_{RL}\rangle\).
- For \(^{18}\)F: the 0\(^+\) and 0\(^-\) parity doublet states at 1.042 and 1.081 MeV yield an isotropic asymmetry \(A_{RL}(\theta) = \langle A_{RL}\rangle\).
- For \(^{19}\)F at 109.9 keV: the resonance width is \(\Gamma = 7.7\times10^{-7}\) eV; with a LiF target of thickness 0.5 cm and density 2.64 g/cm\(^3\), and a photon intensity of \(10^{13}\) photons/second at 100 eV energy width, the expected yield rate is \(7.4\times10^8\)/second.
- For \(^{20}\)Ne: the amplifier factor \(|R_N/\Delta E|\) for the \((0^+,0)\to(1^-,0)\) transition at 11270 keV with admixture at 11262 keV is \(670\pm7000\) MeV\(^{-1}\).
- For \(^{14}\)N: the \((1^+,0)\to(2^-,1)\) transition at 9509 keV with admixture at 9172 keV has \(|R_N/\Delta E| = 45\pm5\) MeV\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the traditional approach to PNC measurements, which previously relied on measuring the circular polarization of gamma rays emitted from parity-doublet states excited by nuclear reactions (reviewed by Adelberger and Haxton (1985) and Desplanques (1998)). The authors propose an inverse method using a circularly polarized photon beam to excite the states directly via NRF, which provides different isospin structure in the transition matrix elements and thus independent information on parity-violating meson-nucleon coupling constants. This enables future experiments using high-intensity synchrotron radiation sources such as SPring-8, potentially achieving accuracy better than 10-20% in one week of measurement, compared to previous experiments like the Seattle group's measurement on \(^{21}\)Ne which only gave an upper bound of \((0.8 \pm 1.4) \times 10^{-3}\).
