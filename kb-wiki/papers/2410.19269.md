# Towards next-generation optical potentials for nuclear reactions and structure calculations

> Optical-model potentials (OMPs) are critical ingredients for basic and applied nuclear physics. Present-day computational capabilities allow us to generate data-driven nucleon-nucleus OMPs that are non-local and exactly dispersive (as theoretically required to be), include statistically-sound uncertainty quantification, and are trained on both scattering and bound-state data from a wide area of the nuclear chart. Combined together, these features allow for significant improvement in fidelity and extrapolative power of the model. Here, we present preliminary work toward the development and training of such an OMP. The capability of the model to describe data at this first stage is encouraging.

# Towards next-generation optical potentials for nuclear reactions and structure calculations

## Key claim
The authors present preliminary work toward a phenomenological nucleon-nucleus optical-model potential (OMP) that is fully non-local and exactly dispersive, trained on both scattering and bound-state data. The model shows encouraging but preliminary accuracy, with performance for scattering data comparable to the Koning-Delaroche global OMP within the fitted energy range, though extrapolation to higher energies and high-accuracy reproduction of binding energies remain limited.

## Method
The authors construct a phenomenological OMP using Woods-Saxon-based volume and surface terms (complex and energy-dependent) with a real energy-independent Woods-Saxon spin-orbit term, plus a uniform-sphere Coulomb term. Non-locality is expressed using the Perey-Buck form (Perey 1962) and treated exactly with a full non-local solver. The model is trained on a subset of scattering data from Pruitt (2023) above 15 MeV (excluding compound-nucleus contributions) and on bound-state properties (particle numbers, charge radii from Angeli (2013), and binding energies from Wang (2021) and NIST Atomic Spectra Database) for 7 nuclides: ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, ⁵⁸Ni, ⁶⁴Ni, ⁹⁰Zr, ¹²⁴Sn, and ²⁰⁸Pb.

## Key numbers
- Training data includes scattering data above 15 MeV incident laboratory energy, with non-polarized elastic cross sections, total cross sections for neutrons, and reaction cross sections for protons between 15 and 150 MeV.
- Binding energy predictions from the model often deviate by about 10% from experimental data.
- The model was trained on 7 nuclides across the nuclear chart.
- Extrapolation to scattering energies beyond the fitted region (e.g., ⁴⁸Ca+n total cross-section up to 150 MeV, ²⁰⁸Pb+p elastic cross-section at 200 MeV) is described as less accurate.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Pruitt (2023) by incorporating bound-state data alongside scattering data and enforcing exact dispersive relations and non-locality. It contrasts with most dispersive OMPs that focus on one or few nuclides at a time (e.g., Atkinson 2019, Pruitt 2019, Zhao 2023) and with the recent global dispersive OMP of Morillon (2024) trained only on neutron-scattering data. The authors aim to produce a global parametrization applicable across the nuclear chart, including unstable nuclei, with statistically-sound uncertainty quantification from Pruitt (2023).
