# The anisotropic non-equilibrium hydrodynamic attractor

> We determine the dynamical attractors associated with anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) and the DNMR equations for a 0+1d conformal system using kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation. We compare our results to the non-equilibrium attractor obtained from exact solution of the 0+1d conformal Boltzmann equation, Navier-Stokes theory, and second-order Mueller-Israel-Stewart theory. We demonstrate that the aHydro attractor equation resums an infinite number of terms in the inverse Reynolds number. The resulting resummed aHydro attractor possesses a positive longitudinal to transverse pressure ratio and is virtually indistinguishable from the exact attractor. This suggests that kinetic theory involves not only a resummation in gradients (Knudsen number) but also a novel resummation in inverse Reynolds number. We also demonstrate that the DNMR result provides a better approximation to the exact kinetic theory attractor than Mueller-Israel-Stewart theory. Finally, we introduce a new method for obtaining approximate aHydro equations which relies solely on an expansion in inverse Reynolds number, carry out this expansion to third order, and compare these third-order results to the exact kinetic theory solution.

## Key claim

The anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) attractor equation resums an infinite number of terms in the inverse Reynolds number, making its attractor solution virtually indistinguishable from the exact attractor obtained from the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation, provided the system is conformal and 0+1d boost-invariant.

## Method

The authors solved the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation for a 0+1d conformal system under Bjorken flow. They derived the dynamical attractor equations for anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro), Denicol-Niemi-Molnar-Rischke (DNMR) theory, and Mueller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) theory, then compared these to the exact attractor obtained from the integral solution of the Boltzmann equation. The aHydro attractor equation was obtained by combining the first moment of the Boltzmann equation with the second moment, recast in terms of the amplitude φ(w) where w = τT.

## Key numbers

- The aHydro attractor boundary condition at w → 0 gives φ = 3/4, which guarantees positive longitudinal pressure at all points.
- Approximate isotropization (longitudinal to transverse pressure ratio > 0.9) occurs only for w > 15.
- The inverse Reynolds number is related to the shear stress via R_π^{-1} = 3√(3/2) |Π/ε|.
- In the small ξ expansion, the aHydro equation at linear order in Π agrees with previously obtained RTA second-order viscous hydrodynamics results, with β_ππ = 38/21 for DNMR.
- The MIS value β_ππ = 4/3 gives a different attractor, while DNMR with β_ππ = 38/21 provides a better approximation to the exact kinetic theory attractor than MIS.

## Context

This work extends the study of hydrodynamic attractors initiated by Heller and Spalinski (2015) for MIS theory to the aHydro and DNMR frameworks. It shows that aHydro, which was developed by Florkowski and collaborators (2010-2017) for describing the quark-gluon plasma, naturally resums an infinite number of terms in the inverse Reynolds number, unlike standard second-order hydrodynamics. The results suggest that kinetic theory involves a novel resummation in inverse Reynolds number beyond the usual gradient resummation in Knudsen number, and enable future work on extending aHydro to higher orders and to more realistic 3+1d systems.
