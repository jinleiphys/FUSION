# Tensor-force effects on shell-structure evolution in $N = 82$ isotones and $Z = 50$ isotopes in the relativistic Hartree-Fock theory

> The evolution of the energy difference between the neutron states $1i_{13/2}$ and $1h_{9/2}$ in the $N=82$ isotones and that between the proton states $1h_{11/2}$ and $1g_{7/2}$ in the $Z = 50$ isotopes are investigated within the framework of the relativistic Hartree-Fock theory, using the density-dependent effective interactions PKA1 and PKO$i$ ($i = 1 $, $ 2 $, $ 3 $). By identifying the contributions of the tensor force, which is naturally induced via the Fock terms, we find that the tensor force plays crucial roles in the evolution of the shell structure. The strength of the tensor force is also explored. It is found that moderately increasing the coupling strength of pion-nucleon coupling, i.e., $f_π$, will significantly improve the description of the shell-structure evolution. In particular, reducing the density dependence of $f_π$ is shown to be more preferable, in comparison to enlarging $f_π$ with a factor. This is in consistence with the idea of "tensor renormalization persistency" and provides valuable guidance for the development of nuclear energy density functional in the relativistic framework.

## Key claim

The tensor force, naturally induced via Fock terms in relativistic Hartree-Fock theory, plays a crucial role in the shell-structure evolution of N=82 isotones and Z=50 isotopes; moderately increasing the pion-nucleon coupling strength fπ improves the description of this evolution, and reducing the density dependence of fπ is more preferable than simply enlarging fπ by a factor, consistent with the idea of "tensor renormalization persistency."

## Method

The authors used the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) theory with density-dependent effective interactions PKA1, PKO1, PKO2, PKO3, and a newly developed interaction to calculate single-particle energy differences in N=82 isotones (between neutron states 1i13/2 and 1h9/2) and Z=50 isotopes (between proton states 1h11/2 and 1g7/2). They identified the tensor-force contributions in each meson-nucleon coupling (sigma-scalar, omega-vector, rho-vector, rho-tensor, rho-vector-tensor, and pi-pseudovector) using a nonrelativistic reduction method developed in Wang (2018), then evaluated the tensor-force contributions to single-particle energies by excluding them from the full calculation. Pairing correlations were treated using the BCS method with a zero-range density-dependent interaction.

## Key numbers

- For N=82 isotones, the energy difference Δε = ε(ν1i13/2) - ε(ν1h9/2) at Z=58 was 1.58 MeV (PKA1), 2.84 MeV (PKO1), 2.48 MeV (PKO2), and 2.96 MeV (PKO3); the original experimental value at Z=58 was -0.05 MeV, and the pseudodata value (with particle-vibration coupling subtracted) was 0.76 MeV.
- The π-PV coupling strength fπ depends on baryon density as fπ(ρb) = fπ(0) exp(-aπ ξ), where ξ = ρb/ρsat and ρsat is the saturation density; the density-dependence factor aπ in PKO3 is smaller than in PKO1, giving PKO3 a stronger tensor force.
- The pairing strength V0 was uniformly chosen as -500 MeV·fm³, with the active pairing space limited to single-particle states below +10 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of tensor-force effects on shell evolution from the nonrelativistic Skyrme framework (e.g., Colò (2007) with SLy5+T and SIII+T functionals) to the relativistic Hartree-Fock theory, where the tensor force is naturally contained via Fock terms without extra free parameters. The method builds on the tensor-force identification in RHF theory by Wang (2018) and the earlier RHF studies of N=82 isotones by Long (2008). The results provide guidance for developing nuclear energy density functionals in the relativistic framework, particularly regarding the strength and density dependence of the pion-nucleon coupling.
