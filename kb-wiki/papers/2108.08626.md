# Dynamics of particle production in Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV using PYTHIA8 Angantyr model

> We study the dynamics of identified, strange, and multi-strange particle production in Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV using the recently developed Angantyr model, incorporated within PYTHIA8. We show the interplay between multi-parton interactions (MPI) and color reconnection (CR) on the experimentally measured quantities. The charged-particle multiplicity ($N_{\rm ch}$) and mean transverse momentum ($\langle p_{\rm T}\rangle$) distributions are well explained by PYTHIA8 Angantyr with proper tuning, as presented in this paper. Predictions of $p_{\rm T}$ spectra, $\langle p_{\rm T} \rangle$, $p_{\rm T}$ integrated yields of the identified, strange and multi-strange particles are studied. To provide insight into the collective nature of the produced particles, we look into the ratio of particle yields to pions and kaons. PYTHIA8 Angantyr with CR and MPI mimic signs of collectivity and is possibly one of the suitable candidates to study ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

PYTHIA8 Angantyr with multi-parton interactions (MPI) and color reconnection (CR) can reproduce charged-particle multiplicity and mean transverse momentum distributions in Pb–Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV without a thermalized medium, and the resulting flow-like features are attributed to CR rather than hydrodynamic expansion.

## Method

The authors generated approximately 2 million Pb–Pb collision events at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV using PYTHIA8 version 8.235 with the Angantyr heavy-ion extension, which combines multiple nucleon-nucleon collisions via a Glauber-model eikonal approximation with Gribov corrections for diffractive excitation. Events were divided into nine centrality classes based on charged-particle multiplicity in the pseudorapidity range |η| < 0.8, matching ALICE TPC acceptance. The authors compared simulations with MPI on/off and CR on/off against ALICE data for multiplicity distributions, transverse momentum spectra, and particle yields of identified hadrons (π±, K±, p/p̅) and strange baryons (Λ, Ξ, Ω).

## Context

This work extends the PYTHIA8 Angantyr model (Bierlich 2018) to Pb–Pb collisions at LHC energies, building on earlier studies of pp and pA collisions with color reconnection tunes (Ortiz Velasquez 2013). The results show that MPI with CR can reproduce experimental data from the ALICE Collaboration (2010, 2013) without requiring a hydrodynamic QGP phase, suggesting that flow-like patterns observed in heavy-ion collisions may have contributions from initial-state color reconnection mechanisms. The study enables further investigation of higher-order flow harmonics in QGP-free collision systems and provides a baseline for distinguishing genuine collective effects from color reconnection signatures.
