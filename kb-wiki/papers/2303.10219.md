# Three relativistic neutrons in a finite volume

> We generalize the relativistic field-theoretic (RFT) three-particle finite-volume formalism to systems of three identical, massive, spin-$1/2$ fermions, such as three neutrons. This allows, in principle, for the determination of the three-neutron interaction from the finite-volume spectrum of three-neutron states, which can be obtained from lattice QCD calculations.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the relativistic field-theoretic (RFT) three-particle finite-volume formalism from spinless particles to three identical spin-1/2 fermions (such as three neutrons), but the formalism is only valid for center-of-momentum frame energies up to the first inelastic threshold (pion production, 3N → 3N+π), and the complications from spin lead to mixing of spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 three-neutron states due to the lack of Lorentz invariance of spin projections.

## Method

The authors follow the relativistic field theory (RFT) approach, extending the formalism previously developed for identical scalars by Hansen and Sharpe (2014, 2015) to include spin-1/2 degrees of freedom. They introduce two coordinate systems for spin states (lab-frame axis and dimer-frame axis) related by Wigner rotations, and modify the building blocks (K₂, F, G, F₃) to carry spin indices. The derivation proceeds through a skeleton expansion of finite-volume correlation functions, accounting for antisymmetry of fermion fields and the need for momentum-dependent spin transformation operators.

## Key numbers

* The formalism is valid for CMF energies up to the first inelastic threshold at 3N → 3N+π; for physical quark masses this occurs at energies where nucleons are nonrelativistic, but for heavier-than-physical quarks (e.g., mπ ≈ 700–800 MeV) the quantity k²/mN² can take values up to 0.5 near threshold.
* The quantization condition det[1 + K_df,3(E₃*) F₃(E₃, P, L)] = 0 is valid for m < E₃* < 5m, where E₃* is the three-particle CMF energy.
* The two-particle K-matrix is defined via K₂^(ℓ)(E₂,k*)⁻¹ = (1/(16π E₂,k*)) [p cot δ^(ℓ)(p) + |p|(1 – H(k))] evaluated at p = q*₂,k, where q*₂,k = √(E₂,k*²/4 – m²).

## Context

This work extends the RFT three-particle finite-volume formalism of Hansen and Sharpe (2014, 2015) from spinless particles to spin-1/2 fermions, taking the first step toward applying the formalism to nucleon and nuclear physics systems such as the tritium nucleus or the three-nucleon force. It contrasts with the nonrelativistic EFT approach of Hammer, Pang, and Rusetsky (2017) and others, which is simpler for physical quark masses but less suitable for the heavier-than-physical quark masses expected in near-term lattice QCD studies. The generalization enables, in principle, determination of the three-neutron interaction from lattice QCD finite-volume spectra, and provides the foundation for future extensions to generic three-nucleon systems or the Nπ+Nππ system needed for the Roper resonance.
