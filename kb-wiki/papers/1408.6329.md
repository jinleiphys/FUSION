# Bulk and shear viscosities of hot and dense hadron gas

> We estimate bulk and shear viscosity at finite temperature and baryon densities of hadronic matter within hadron resonance gas model. For bulk viscosity we use low energy theorems of QCD for the energy momentum tensor correlators. For shear viscosity coefficient, we estimate the same using molecular kinetic theory to relate the shear viscosity coefficient to average momentum of the hadrons in the hot and dense hadron gas. The bulk viscosity to entropy ratio increases with chemical potential and is related to the reduction of velocity of sound at nonzero chemical potential. The shear viscosity to entropy ratio on the other hand, shows a nontrivial behavior with the ratio decreasing with chemical potential for small temperatures but increasing with chemical potential at high temperatures and is related to decrease of entropy density with chemical potential at high temperature due to finite volume of the hadrons.

## Key claim

The authors estimate bulk and shear viscosities of hadronic matter using a hadron resonance gas model that includes both discrete resonances up to 2 GeV and a continuum Hagedorn spectrum, finding that the bulk viscosity to entropy ratio increases with chemical potential due to reduction of sound velocity, while the shear viscosity to entropy ratio shows a nontrivial dependence on chemical potential that changes sign with temperature.

## Method

The bulk viscosity is computed using the Kubo formula combined with low-energy theorems of QCD that relate the stress-energy tensor correlator to thermodynamic quantities including the velocity of sound, trace anomaly, and in-medium quark condensates. The shear viscosity is estimated using molecular kinetic theory for a relativistic gas of hard-core spheres, where the shear viscosity coefficient is proportional to the average momentum of hadrons divided by the square of the hard-core radius. Thermodynamic quantities are calculated within a hadron resonance gas model that includes all known hadrons with masses below 2 GeV plus a continuum Hagedorn spectrum (either exponential or power-law form) fitted to lattice QCD data at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- For zero chemical potential, the minimum shear viscosity to entropy ratio reaches approximately 0.7, which is an order of magnitude larger than the Kovtun-Son-Starinets bound of 1/(4π)
- The hard-core radius is taken as 0.4 fm for mesons and 0.5 fm for baryons
- The exponential Hagedorn spectrum uses T_H = 0.210 GeV and A = 0.63; the power-law spectrum uses T_H = 0.180 GeV, A = 0.51, and α = 3
- The cutoff mass Λ for the discrete spectrum is 2 GeV
- The nucleon sigma term is 42 MeV and the Λ hyperon sigma term is 263.5 MeV
- The valley structure in η/s as a function of chemical potential occurs at a nucleon number density of approximately 0.07 fm^(-3), about half nuclear matter density

## Context

This work extends the approach of Greiner et al. (2008, 2010) for shear viscosity in hadron resonance gas at zero baryon density to include finite chemical potential effects, relevant for beam energy scan at RHIC, compressed baryonic matter at GSI, and NICA at Dubna. The bulk viscosity calculation generalizes the method of Karsch, Kharzeev, and Tuchin (2008) to finite chemical potential, differing from the Schwinger-Dyson approach of Wang (2012). The results enable comparison with lattice QCD data at finite baryon density and may help identify signatures of liquid-gas phase transition through the valley structure in η/s.
