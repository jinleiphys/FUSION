# Antiproton-proton partial-wave analysis below 925 MeV/c

> A partial-wave analysis of all antiproton-proton scattering data below 925 MeV/c antiproton laboratory momentum is presented. The method used is adapted from the Nijmegen phase-shift analyses of pp and np scattering data. The Nijmegen 1993 antiproton-proton database, consisting of 3646 antiproton-proton scattering data, is presented and discussed. The best fit to this database results in chi^2_min/Ndata = 1.043. The pseudovector coupling constant of the charged pion to nucleons is determined to be (f_c)^2 = 0.0732(11) at the pion pole, where the error is statistical.

## Key claim

The authors present a partial-wave analysis of antiproton-proton scattering data below 925 MeV/c antiproton laboratory momentum, achieving a fit with χ²_min/N_data = 1.043, but note that the analysis is limited by the absence of high-quality data below about 400 MeV/c, unlike the case for nucleon-nucleon scattering.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Schrödinger equation for the coupled antiproton-proton and antineutron-neutron channels, using a theoretically well-founded potential for the long- and intermediate-range interactions (Coulomb potential with relativistic corrections, magnetic-moment interaction, one-pion-exchange potential, and heavy-boson exchanges from the Nijmegen one-boson-exchange potential). The short-range interactions, including coupling to mesonic annihilation channels, are parametrized by an energy-dependent complex boundary condition (P-matrix) specified at a radius of 1.3 fm, using spherical-well potentials that are independent of energy but dependent on spin and isospin.

## Key numbers

- The Nijmegen 1993 antiproton-proton database contains 3646 scattering data in the momentum interval 119–923 MeV/c
- The best fit yields χ²_min/N_data = 1.043
- The pseudovector coupling constant of the charged pion to nucleons is determined to be f_c² = 0.0732(11) at the pion pole (statistical error only)
- The charge-exchange threshold occurs at p_lab = 99.1 MeV/c (T_lab = 5.2 MeV)
- The boundary radius used is b = 1.3 fm, indicating annihilation is a relatively long-range process

## Context

This work extends the Nijmegen group's partial-wave analysis methodology, previously applied to proton-proton and neutron-proton scattering data (Bergervoet et al., 1990; Kloet et al., 1993), to the more complex antiproton-proton system where annihilation into mesons occurs. The analysis uses the tail of the Nijmegen one-boson-exchange soft-core potential (Nagels et al., 1978) and enables future model-independent studies of antiproton-proton scattering, including the determination of the pion-nucleon coupling constant from charge-exchange data.
