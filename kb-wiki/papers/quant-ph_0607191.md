# A family of many-body models which are exactly solvable analytically

> We present a family of many-body models which are exactly solvable analytically. The models are an extended n-body interaction Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model which considers spin-flip terms which are associated with the interaction of an external classical field which coherently manipulates the state of the system in order to, for example, process quantum information. The models also describe a two-mode Bose-Einstein condensate with a Josephson-type interaction which includes n-particle elastic and inelastic collisions. One of the models corresponds to the canonical two-mode Bose-Einstein Hamitonian plus a term which we argue must be considered in the description of the two-mode condensate. Intriguingly, this extra term allows for an exact and analytical solution of the two-particle collision two-mode BEC problem. Our results open up an arena to study many-body system properties analytically.

## Key claim
The paper presents a family of many-body Hamiltonians that are exactly solvable analytically by construction, achieved by applying a general SU(2) rotation to a polynomial in the diagonal operator \(J_z\). The key qualifier is that the exact solution exists only when the physical parameters satisfy a specific relationship between elastic and inelastic collision rates, as given by Eq. (4); if this relation does not hold for a given system, the analytical solution cannot be found using this method.

## Method
The authors construct the family by starting with a Hamiltonian \(H_0^n = \sum_{i=0}^n A_i J_z^i\) and applying the most general SU(2) rotation \(U = e^{i\phi J_z} e^{i\theta J_y}\) to obtain \(H^n = U^\dagger H_0^n U\). The eigenstates are then simply \(U^\dagger |j,m\rangle\) with energies \(\mathcal{E}_m^n = \sum_{i=0}^n A_i m^i\). For the 2-model, they express the Hamiltonian in the Schwinger boson representation to map it onto a two-mode Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) Hamiltonian that includes both elastic and inelastic two-particle collisions, plus single and two-particle spin-flip terms from an external classical field.

## Key numbers
- The 2-model Hamiltonian includes terms with coupling constants \(\delta\omega = A_1 \cos\theta\), \(\lambda = A_1 \sin\theta\), \(\mathcal{U} = A_2 (1 - 3\cos^2\theta)\), \(\mu = 2 A_2 \cos\theta \sin\theta\), and \(\Lambda = A_2 \sin^2\theta\), where \(A_1\) and \(A_2\) are real constants and \(\theta\) is a rotation angle.
- The rate of elastic to inelastic collisions is given by \((\mu + \Lambda)/\mathcal{U} = (\lambda/2) \big( (\lambda + 2\delta\omega) / (A_1^2 - 3\delta\omega^2) \big)\).
- For \(A_2 > 0\), the ground state \(m_0\) is the nearest integer to \(-A_1/(2A_2)\), or \(m_0 = -A_1 N/|A_1|\) when \(|-A_1/(2A_2)| > N\). For \(A_2 < 0\), the minimum is \(m_0 = N\) if \(A_1 < 0\) or \(m_0 = -N\) otherwise.
- The relative population distribution is plotted for \(N = 2001\) atoms, showing macroscopic superpositions involving several coherent states for \(m_0 \leq 1000\).
- The evolution of \(\langle j_z \rangle\) is shown for an initial state \(|N=100, m=100\rangle\) with \(A_1 = 1\), \(A_2 = 0.01\), and \(\theta = 3/2\), exhibiting Rabi-type oscillations with collapse and revivals.

## Context
This work extends the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model of nuclear physics (Lipkin, Meshkov, and Glick, 1965) by including single spin-flip terms and all consistent second-order processes, which yields an exact analytical solution where the original LMG model had none. It also extends the canonical two-mode BEC Josephson Hamiltonian (Cirac et al., 1998) by adding n-particle elastic and inelastic collisions, which are physically present in real condensates but were previously omitted; the extra terms make the model exactly solvable. The results enable analytical study of higher-order many-body interactions, ground-state macroscopic superpositions involving multiple coherent states, and collapse-and-revival dynamics, and suggest generalizations to SU(3) algebras for three-mode or spin-1 BECs.
