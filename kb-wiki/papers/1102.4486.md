# N/Z dependence of balance energy as the probe of symmetry energy in heavy-ion collisions

> We study the N/Z dependence of balance energy (E$_{bal}$) for isotopic series of Ca having N/Z varying from 1.0 to 2.0. We show that the N/Z dependence of E$_{bal}$ is sensitive to symmetry energy and its density dependence at densities higher than saturation density and is insensitive towards the isospin dependence of nucleon-nucleon cross section

## Key claim

The N/Z dependence of the balance energy (E_bal) in heavy-ion collisions is sensitive to the symmetry energy and its density dependence at densities above saturation, but is insensitive to the isospin dependence of the nucleon-nucleon cross section; this sensitivity is demonstrated through simulations of Ca isotopic chains with N/Z from 1.4 to 2.0.

## Method

The authors performed simulations of Ca+Ca collisions (isotopes ⁴⁸Ca, ⁵²Ca, ⁵⁶Ca, ⁶⁰Ca) using the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model at impact parameters b̂ = 0.2–0.4, employing a soft equation of state and an anisotropic standard isospin- and energy-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross section (σ = 0.8 σ_NN^free). They computed the balance energy (E_bal) for each system and then systematically switched off the symmetry energy, made the cross section isospin-independent, and changed the symmetry energy density dependence to ρ² to isolate the contributions of each effect.

## Key numbers

- For the Ca isotopic series with N/Z from 1.4 to 2.0, E_bal decreases linearly with increasing N/Z when the symmetry energy is included (solid circles in Fig. 1a).
- Switching off the symmetry energy changes the slope of E_bal versus N/Z from -30 to -7 (Fig. 1a, open circles).
- With symmetry energy proportional to ρ², the slope of E_bal versus N/Z is reduced to -17, compared to the linear density dependence case (Fig. 1c, open pentagons).
- The isospin-dependent cross section uses σ_np = 3σ_nn (or σ_pp), while the isospin-independent case sets σ_np = σ_nn = σ_pp.

## Context

This work extends studies of collective transverse in-plane flow and its disappearance at balance energy (E_bal) as a probe of the isospin dependence of the nuclear equation of state, following earlier work by Pak (1997) and Gautam (2010). The results demonstrate that the N/Z dependence of E_bal can serve as a specific observable to constrain the symmetry energy and its density dependence at supra-saturation densities, while ruling out significant sensitivity to the isospin dependence of the nucleon-nucleon cross section. This enables future experimental measurements with rare isotope beams to extract the density dependence of the symmetry energy.
