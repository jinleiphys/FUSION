# Color transparency in $π^-$-induced dilepton production on nuclei

> We argue that the observation of the color transparency effect in the semiexclusive $A(π^-,l^+ l^-)$ process is important for determining whether it is possible to extract the generalized parton distributions of the nucleon from the elementary reaction $π^- p \to l^+ l^- n$ at $p_{\rm lab}=15-20$ GeV/c at small $|t|$ and large invariant mass of the dilepton pair $l^+ l^-$. Assuming that the transverse size of the pionic $q \bar q$ pair in the hard interaction point is similar to the one in the reaction $γ^* p\to π^+ n$ studied at JLab we predict large color transparency effects in the discussed kinematic range. We also suggest that the semiexclusive $ρ^0$ production in $π^-$-induced reactions in the same beam momentum region may provide new information on the dynamics of the interaction in the non-vacuum channel, while the $J/ψ$ production can be used to get information on $J/ψN$ total interaction cross section.,

# Color transparency in π⁻-induced dilepton production on nuclei

## Key claim

The observation of color transparency in the semiexclusive A(π⁻, l⁺l⁻) process would serve as a necessary condition for the validity of factorization and the possibility to extract generalized parton distributions (GPDs) from the elementary reaction π⁻ p → l⁺ l⁻ n, assuming that the transverse size of the pionic qq̅ pair at the hard interaction point is similar to that in the γ* p → π⁺ n reaction studied at JLab.

## Method

The authors model the semiexclusive dilepton production on nuclei within a generalized eikonal (Glauber) approximation, computing the nuclear transparency as the ratio of the nuclear cross section to the elementary proton cross section multiplied by the proton number Z. Color transparency effects are incorporated via the quantum diffusion model, where the effective πN cross section evolves from a reduced value at the hard interaction point (proportional to ⟨n²k²t⟩/M²l⁺l⁻) to the full hadronic cross section over the pion coherence length lπ = 2plab/ΔM². The model is benchmarked against JLab data on A(e, e′π⁺) before being applied to π⁻-induced reactions, and extended to vector meson production (ρ⁰, J/ψ) with analogous effective cross section treatments.

## Key numbers

- Beam momentum range for the elementary reaction: plab = 15–20 GeV/c at small |t| and large dilepton invariant mass
- Pion coherence length: lπ = 2plab/ΔM² with default ΔM² = 0.7 GeV² (alternative value 1.4 GeV² also tested)
- Average transverse momentum of a quark in a hadron: ⟨k²t⟩¹/² ≈ 0.35 GeV/c
- For J/ψ production: characteristic kt values of 0.8–1.0 GeV/c; default σJ/ψN = 4 mb
- ρ meson decay width at rest: Γρ = 0.149 GeV; default σρN = 25 mb (also tested with 20 mb)
- For M²l⁺l⁻ = 4 GeV² at plab = 20 GeV/c: longitudinal momentum transfer to the target is 0.107 GeV/c
- Transparency for (π⁻, l⁺l⁻) at plab = 16 GeV/c and M²l⁺l⁻ = 4 GeV²: power-law fit exponent α = 0.904 ± 0.005 (quantum diffusion) vs α = 0.786 ± 0.003 (Glauber)
- Transparency varies by ~5% for ¹²C and ~20% for ¹⁹⁷Au over plab = 15–20 GeV/c at fixed M²l⁺l⁻ = 4 GeV²
- For ρ⁰ production: ρ-decay inside nucleus reduces transparency by more than 20% for ¹⁹⁷Au at plab ≲ 10 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of color transparency previously applied to pion electroproduction at JLab (Clasie 2007) and to pionic quasielastic knockout (Miller 2010), proposing a new observable in the exclusive Drell-Yan process on nuclei. The study is motivated by the planned high-momentum π⁻ beam line at J-PARC (up to 20 GeV/c) and the ongoing debate on factorization for exclusive Drell-Yan (Berger 2001, Goloskokov 2015, Muller 2012, Qiu 2015). The results enable experimental tests of whether squeezing of qq̅ configurations occurs in time-like processes, which would support the extraction of nucleon GPDs from π⁻ p → l⁺ l⁻ n, and also provide predictions for complementary ρ⁰ and J/ψ production channels to study non-vacuum exchange dynamics and the J/ψN interaction cross section.
