# Some aspects of the phase diagram of nuclear matter relevant to compact stars

> Dense matter as it can be found in core-collapse supernovae and neutron stars is expected to exhibit different phase transitions which impact the matter composition and the equation of state, with important consequences on the dynamics of core-collapse supernova explosion and on the structure of neutron stars. In this paper we will address the specific phenomenology of two of such transitions, namely the crust-core solid-liquid transition at sub-saturation density, and the possible strange transition at super-saturation density in the presence of hyperonic degrees of freedom. Concerning the neutron star crust-core phase transition at zero and finite temperature, it will be shown that, as a consequence of the presence of long-range Coulomb interactions, a clusterized phase is expected which is not accessible in the grand-canonical ensemble. A specific quasi-particle model will be introduced and some quantitative results relevant for the supernova dynamics will be shown. The opening of hyperonic degrees of freedom at higher densities corresponding to the neutron stars core also modifies the equation of state. The general characteristics and order of phase transitions in this regime will be analyzed in the framework of a self-consistent mean-field approach.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the first-order liquid-gas phase transition expected in neutral nuclear matter is quenched in stellar matter at sub-saturation densities due to Coulomb frustration, replaced by a continuous transition through a clusterized medium; at supra-saturation densities, a strangeness phase transition involving hyperons may occur, but its existence is not model-independent and depends on the presence of a hyperon-hyperon interaction.

## Method

The authors employ a nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) quasi-particle model for sub-saturation densities, treating clusters as new species with excluded-volume interactions and nucleons in a self-consistent Hartree-Fock approximation with a phenomenological energy functional. For supra-saturation densities, they analyze a neutron-Lambda mixture using the Balberg-Gal energy density functional (1997) at zero and finite temperature, performing Gibbs phase equilibrium constructions to identify phase coexistence regions and critical points.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for the strangeness phase transition in the n-Lambda model: T_c = 14.8 MeV
- Surface symmetry coefficient in the in-medium mass formula: a_{Ssym} = -32 MeV
- Exponents for in-medium surface term density dependence: gamma_1 = gamma_2 = 2
- The clusterized phase exists across the entire sub-saturation density regime, from about rho > 10^10 g/cm^3 (approx 10^-4 rho_0) up to several times rho_0
- The hyperon-rich phase in the coexistence region contains approximately 25% hyperons at zero temperature under mu_n = mu_Lambda condition

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear matter (Siemens, 1983; Ducoin, 2007; Rios, 2008; Typel, 2010) by incorporating Coulomb frustration effects that quench the first-order transition in stellar matter, confirming results from microscopic models (Watanabe, 2003). The NSE approach builds on prior statistical equilibrium models (Mishustin, 1993; Blinnikov, 1996; Souza, 2009) and the Lattimer-Swesty model (1991), while improving upon them by including in-medium modifications of cluster binding energies from Hartree-Fock calculations. The strangeness phase transition analysis extends work by Balberg and Gal (1997) and Vidana et al. (2010), addressing the tension between hyperon softening of the equation of state and the observed two-solar-mass neutron star (Demorest, 2010). The results enable improved modeling of supernova dynamics and neutron star structure by providing a more accurate treatment of cluster distributions and the possibility of hyperon-rich and hyperon-poor domain coexistence.
