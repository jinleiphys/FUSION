# The Isospin Distribution of Fragments in Reactions 96Ru+96Ru, 96Ru+96Zr, 96Zr+96Ru, and 96Zr+96Zr at Beam Energy 400 AMeV

> The isospin distribution of particles and fragments in collisions 96Ru+96Ru, 96Ru+96Zr, 96Zr+96Ru, and 96Zr+96Zr at beam energy 400 AMeV is studied with isospin dependent QMD model. We find that the rapidity distribution of differential neutron-proton counting in neutron rich nucleus-nucleus collisions at intermediate energies is sensitive to the isospin dependent part of nuclear potential. The study of the N/Z ratio of nucleons, light charged particles (LCP) and intermediate mass fragments (IMF) shows that the isospin dependent part of nuclear potential drives IMF to be more isospin symmetric and emitted nucleons to be more neutron rich. From the study of the time evolution of the isospin distribution in emitted nucleons, LCP and IMF we find that neutrons diffuse much faster than protons at beginning and the final isospin distribution is a result of dynamical balance of symmetry potential and Coulomb force under the charge conservation.

## Key claim

The rapidity distribution of the normalized differential neutron-proton counting (N_np) in neutron-rich nucleus-nucleus collisions at intermediate energies is sensitive to the symmetry energy coefficient, but only when the isospin-dependent part of the nuclear potential is switched on; the normalized proton number counting R_Z is not sensitive to the value of C_S. The sensitivity to C_S increases as the system becomes more neutron rich, and the effect is most pronounced at projectile and target rapidity regions.

## Method

The authors performed isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) calculations for four mass-96 systems: 96Ru+96Ru, 96Ru+96Zr, 96Zr+96Ru, and 96Zr+96Zr at a beam energy of 400 AMeV and impact parameter b = 0 fm. They tested four values of the symmetry energy coefficient C_S (0, 27, 35, and 50 MeV) corresponding to a_sym values of 0, 27, 31, and 38 MeV, using a soft equation of state (K = 200 MeV) with linear density dependence of the symmetry potential. Clusters were constructed using a coalescence model with R_0 = 3.5 fm and P_0 = 300 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 400 AMeV for all four reactions
- N/Z ratios of the systems: 96Ru (N/Z = 1.18), 96Zr (N/Z = 1.40), mixed systems (N/Z = 1.28)
- Symmetry energy coefficient values tested: C_S = 0, 27, 35, and 50 MeV, corresponding to a_sym = 0, 27, 31, and 38 MeV
- Coalescence parameters: R_0 = 3.5 fm, P_0 = 300 MeV/c
- Nuclear matter incompressibility: K = 200 MeV (soft EOS)
- Saturation density: rho_0 = 0.168 fm^{-3}
- Fermi energy: epsilon_F^0 = 38 MeV
- Time range studied for isospin evolution: 30 fm/c to 100 fm/c, where 30 fm/c is the time when central density drops to rho_0

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of isospin asymmetry in nuclear multifragmentation, building on the theoretical framework of Barranco and Buchler (1980) and Muller and Serot (1995) who showed that spinodal decomposition in asymmetric systems involves chemical rather than mechanical instability. The authors reference experimental findings by Xu (2000), Tsang (2001), and Verde (2000) that the N/Z ratio of gas exceeds that of liquid in multifragmentation. The study enables future experimental tests of the density dependence of the symmetry energy by measuring the normalized differential neutron-proton counting in neutron-rich collisions, particularly at projectile and target rapidity regions, and suggests that the N/Z ratio of light charged particles at central rapidity is a relatively sensitive observable to the symmetry energy coefficient.
