# The masses of hadrons in the chiral symmetry restored vacuum

> We calculate the masses of the vector and axial-vector mesons as well as the nucleon and the delta resonance in the chiral symmetry restored vacuum. This is accomplished by separating the quark operators appearing in the QCD sum rules for these hadrons into the chiral symmetric and symmetry breaking parts depending on the contributions of the fermion zero modes. We then extract the vacuum expectation values of all the separated parts of the quark operators using the QCD sum rule relations for these hadrons with their vacuum masses and widths. By taking the chiral symmetry breaking parts to be zero while keeping the symmetric operators to their vacuum values, we obtain the chiral symmetric part of the hadron masses. We find that the masses of chiral partners, such as the $(ρ,a_1)$ and $(K^*,K_1)$, become degenerate to values between 500 and 600 MeV in the chiral symmetry restored vacuum, while parity partners $(ω,f_1)$ that are chiral partners only in the limit where the disconnected diagrams are neglected remain non-degenerate with masses $(655,1060)$ MeV, respectively. The masses of the nucleon and the Delta are also found to reduce to about 500 and 600 MeV, respectively, in the chiral symmetric vacuum. This shows that while chiral symmetry breaking is responsible for the mass difference between chiral partner, both the meson and baryon retain non-trivial fraction of their masses in the chiral symmetry restored vacuum.

## Key claim

The authors calculate hadron masses in the chiral symmetry restored vacuum by separating quark operators in QCD sum rules into chiral symmetric and symmetry breaking parts based on fermion zero mode contributions, then setting the breaking parts to zero while keeping symmetric operators at their vacuum values. The hedge is that the extracted masses depend on the assumed width of the \(a_1\) meson (taken as 425 MeV for central values, with variations between 250 and 600 MeV producing shifts of up to 27.5 MeV).

## Method

The authors use Borel-transformed QCD sum rules for vector and axial-vector mesons (\(\rho, a_1, \omega, f_1, K^*, K_1\)) and baryons (nucleon, \(\Delta\)). They separate the dimension-6 four-quark operators appearing in the operator product expansion into chiral symmetric and breaking parts according to their dependence on fermion zero modes, following the Banks-Casher formula. By fitting the sum rules to physical masses and widths using an auxiliary parameter \(\kappa\) that multiplies the vacuum saturation value of the chiral breaking operators, they extract the symmetric and breaking components separately, then set the breaking parts to zero to obtain masses in the chiral restored vacuum.

## Key numbers

* \(\rho\) and \(a_1\) become degenerate at \(572.5 \pm 27.5\) MeV in the chiral symmetry restored vacuum (central value with \(a_1\) width 425 MeV; uncertainty from \(a_1\) width range 250–600 MeV)
* \(K^*\) and \(K_1\) become degenerate at \(545 \mp 5\) MeV in the chiral symmetry restored vacuum (uncertainty from \(a_1\) width variation)
* \(\omega\) mass reduces to \(655 \pm 15\) MeV and \(f_1\) mass reduces to \(1060 \pm 30\) MeV in the chiral symmetry restored vacuum (non-degenerate because disconnected diagrams break chiral partner relation)
* Nucleon mass reduces to about 500 MeV and \(\Delta\) mass reduces to about 600 MeV in the chiral symmetric vacuum
* Disconnected four-quark condensates: \(\langle (\bar{q} \gamma_\mu \gamma_5 \lambda^a q)^2 \rangle_{dis,S} = 1.65 \langle \bar{u}u \rangle^2\) and \(\langle (\bar{q} \gamma_\mu \lambda^a q)^2 \rangle_{dis,S} = -4.73 \langle \bar{u}u \rangle^2\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QCD sum rule analysis of the \(\rho\)-\(a_1\) system (Kim 2020) to additional chiral partners (\(K^*, K_1\)) and parity partners (\(\omega, f_1\)), as well as baryons (nucleon, \(\Delta\)). It contradicts the vacuum saturation hypothesis (Shifman 1979) by showing that chiral symmetric four-quark condensates are as large as symmetry breaking parts, explaining why previous sum rules required a \(\kappa\) factor larger than 1 (Leupold 1998, 2001). The results enable predictions for mass shifts in heavy-ion collision experiments and nuclear matter studies where chiral symmetry is partially restored.
