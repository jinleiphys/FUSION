# Unitarity Constraints on effective interaction in $πN$ scattering

> Positivity constraints are derived on pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes and their even-order derivatives inside the Mandelstam Triangle with the help of dispersion relations. Fairly interesting constraints are obtained on some of the low energy constants, by a combination of the chiral perturbation theory for heavy baryons and existing fitting results from available pion-nucleon phase shifts at intermediate energies.

## Key claim
Positivity constraints derived from dispersion relations, unitarity, and crossing symmetry can bound combinations of low-energy constants (LECs) in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, but the constraints become nontrivial only when some LECs (such as \(c_i\) and \(\bar{d}_j\)) are taken as known inputs from phase-shift fits; the resulting inequalities then force certain fourth-order LECs (\(\bar{e}_{15,16,20,35}\)) to be an order of magnitude larger than their expected natural size.

## Method
The authors used fixed-\(t\) dispersion relations at \(t=0\) for pion-nucleon scattering, combined with the optical theorem to relate the imaginary part of the amplitude to total cross sections, to obtain positivity conditions on the second derivative of the forward scattering amplitude inside the Mandelstam triangle. They evaluated these derivatives using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCPT) at the specific kinematic points \(\omega = \pm M/\sqrt{2}\) (where \(M\) is the pion mass) to avoid the breakdown of the heavy-baryon expansion near the poles. The resulting inequalities were then applied by inserting central values of the lower-order LECs \(c_i\) and \(\bar{d}_j\) from the “Fit 2” results of Fettes et al. (1998) to obtain numerical bounds on the fourth-order LECs \(\bar{e}_{15}, \bar{e}_{16}, \bar{e}_{20}, \bar{e}_{35}\).

## Key numbers
- Input LECs from “Fit 2” of Fettes et al. (1998): \(c_1 = -1.42\), \(c_2 = 3.13\), \(c_3 = -5.85\), \(c_4 = 3.50\), \(\bar{d}_3 = -2.75\), \(\bar{d}_{18} = -0.78\) (all in units of \(\text{GeV}^{-1}\) or \(\text{GeV}^{-2}\) as appropriate).
- With \(g = 1.26\), the inequality at \(\omega = +M/\sqrt{2}\) gives \(0.2(\bar{e}_{15} + \bar{e}_{20} + \bar{e}_{35}) + 0.6\bar{e}_{16} \gtrsim 18.7\).
- The inequality at \(\omega = -M/\sqrt{2}\) gives \(0.2(\bar{e}_{15} + \bar{e}_{20} + \bar{e}_{35}) + 0.6\bar{e}_{16} \gtrsim -11.3\).
- The pion decay constant used is \(F = 92.4\ \text{MeV}\), nucleon mass \(m = 938\ \text{MeV}\), pion mass \(M = 140\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context
This work extends the program of Adams et al. (2006) and Distler et al. (2006), who derived positivity constraints on Wilson coefficients in effective field theories from causality, analyticity, and unitarity, to the specific case of pion-nucleon interactions. It builds on decades-old dispersion-relation techniques for \(\pi N\) scattering (Hohler, 1983; Hamilton, 1963) and on the heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory framework of Fettes et al. (2000). The results enable future work to test the consistency of fitted LECs and to assess the role of higher-dimension operators and isospin-violating effects, though the authors note that the reliability of the input fits and the convergence of HBCPT inside the Mandelstam triangle remain open questions.
