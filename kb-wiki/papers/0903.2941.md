# QCD in the nuclear medium and effects due to Cherenkov gluons

> The equations of in-medium gluodynamics are proposed. Their classical lowest order solution is explicitly shown for a color charge moving with constant speed. For nuclear permittivity larger than 1 it describes emission of Cherenkov gluons resembling results of classical electrodynamics. The values of the real and imaginary parts of the nuclear permittivity are obtained from the fits to experimental data on the double-humped structure around the away-side jet obtained at RHIC. The dispersion of the nuclear permittivity is predicted by comparing the RHIC, SPS and cosmic ray data. This is important for LHC experiments. Cherenkov gluons may be responsible for the asymmetry of dilepton mass spectra near rho-meson, observed in the SPS experiment with excess in the low-mass wing of the resonance. This feature is predicted to be common for all resonances. The "color rainbow" quantum effect might appear according to higher order terms of in-medium QCD if the nuclear permittivity depends on color.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that in-medium QCD equations, analogous to classical electrodynamics with a dielectric permittivity, admit classical solutions describing Cherenkov gluon emission when the nuclear permittivity exceeds 1, and that this mechanism can explain the double-humped structure around away-side jets observed at RHIC, with the caveat that the extracted permittivity values differ between STAR and PHENIX experiments, possibly indicating a need to account for dispersion.

## Method

The author generalizes the Yang-Mills equations to in-medium gluodynamics by introducing a nuclear permittivity ε, replacing the chromoelectric field E_a with εE_a in the vector equations of motion. The classical lowest-order (Abelian) solution is derived for a color charge moving at constant speed, yielding Cherenkov gluon emission when ε > 1, with the angular spectrum given by a Breit-Wigner-like distribution controlled by the real and imaginary parts of ε. The resulting gluon spectrum is then converted into hadron distributions via a Monte Carlo procedure using PYTHIA for initial parton scattering and a phenomenological fragmentation function with a transverse smearing parameter Δ_⊥, and the three parameters (ε₁, ε₂, Δ_⊥) are fitted to STAR and PHENIX data on the away-side jet angular distributions.

## Key numbers

- STAR data fit: ε₁ = 5.4, ε₂ = 0.7, Δ_⊥ = 0.7 GeV, with angular maximum θ_max = 1.04 rad
- PHENIX data fit: ε₁ = 9.0, ε₂ = 2.0, Δ_⊥ = 1.1 GeV, with angular maximum θ_max = 1.27 rad
- The ratio (ε₂/ε₁)² ≤ 0.05 in all fitted cases, indicating a small imaginary part relative to the real part
- The maximum energy for Cherenkov emission in the simulation was chosen as ω_max = 3.5 GeV, consistent with experimental transverse momentum intervals of δp_⊥ ≤ 1 GeV
- The cosmic ray event at primary energy about 10¹⁶ eV showed two ring-like structures, fitted with ε close to 1 (refractive index near 1), in contrast to RHIC values

## Context

This work extends the classical electrodynamic treatment of Cherenkov radiation to QCD by introducing in-medium gluodynamics equations, building on earlier suggestions of Cherenkov gluons by Dremin (2001) and collaborators. The fitted permittivity values from RHIC data (STAR and PHENIX) are compared with earlier cosmic ray events (Apanasenko et al., 1979) and SPS data (Vokal et al., Ghosh et al., Ajitanand et al.), showing an energy dependence (dispersion) of the nuclear permittivity that can be tested at LHC. The paper also connects Cherenkov gluon emission to the asymmetry of dilepton mass spectra near the rho-meson observed at SPS (Damjanovic et al., 2006), and predicts that such asymmetry should be common to all resonances.
