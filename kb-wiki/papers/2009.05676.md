# The luminosity constraint in the era of precision solar physics

> The luminosity constraint is a very precise relationship linking the power released by the Sun as photons and the solar neutrino fluxes. Such a relation, which is a direct consequence of the physical processes controlling the production and the transport of energy in the solar interior, is of great importance for the studies of solar neutrinos and has a special role for the search of neutrinos from the CNO cycle, whose first detection with a 5$σ$ significance has been recently announced by the Borexino collaboration. Here we revise the luminosity constraint, discussing and validating its underlying hypotheses, in the light of latest solar neutrino and luminosity measurements. We generalize the current formulation of the luminosity constraint relation so that it can be easily used in future analysis of solar neutrino data, and we provide a specific application showing the link between CNO and pp neutrino fluxes.

## Key claim

The luminosity constraint, a precise linear relationship between solar photon luminosity and neutrino fluxes, is revised to account for small deviations from nuclear equilibrium of intermediate isotopes (³He and ¹⁴N) and for gravothermal energy contributions, which become relevant at the current 0.04% precision level of solar luminosity measurements.

## Method

The authors re-derive the luminosity constraint from lepton number and energy conservation, then generalize it by relaxing the assumption that only ⁴He is synthesized and that all intermediate isotopes are in local nuclear equilibrium. They compute the time derivatives of ³He and ¹⁴N abundances and the gravothermal energy contribution using standard solar models (SSMs) with two different heavy-element compositions (GS98 high-metallicity and PLJ14 low-metallicity), and incorporate these corrections into a revised constraint equation.

## Context

This work revises the luminosity constraint originally formulated by Bahcall (1996) and discussed in the context of the "luminosity constraint" paper, in light of the 0.04% precision solar luminosity measurement from the IAU (2015) and the recent 5σ detection of CNO neutrinos by Borexino (Agostini 2020). The generalized formulation enables model-independent extraction of pp and pep neutrino fluxes from the luminosity constraint, which is critical for separating CNO neutrino signals from pep backgrounds in current and future solar neutrino experiments.
