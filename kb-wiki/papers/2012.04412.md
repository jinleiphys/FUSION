# The Shape of the Correlation Function

> The correlation function measured in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions is strongly non-Gaussian. Using two different models we study which effects can influence its shape and how much. In particular, we focus on the parametrizations expressed with the help of Lévy-stable distributions. We show that the Lévy index may deviate substantially from 2 due to non-critical effects such as resonance decays, event-by-event fluctuations and functional dependence on $Q_{LI}$ or similar. We also study the corrections including the first-order Lévy expansion.

# The Shape of the Correlation Function

## Key claim
The Lévy index extracted from femtoscopic correlation functions can deviate substantially from the Gaussian value of 2 due to non-critical effects such as resonance decays and the use of one-dimensional projections of the relative momentum, not only due to critical phenomena near the QCD phase diagram's critical endpoint.

## Method
The authors used two independent models, a blast-wave model implemented via the DRAGON Monte Carlo event generator with resonance decays, and the 2+1-D hydrodynamic model iEBE-VISHNU with Glauber MC initial conditions, to generate events for charged pion pairs. Correlation functions were computed using CRAB for the blast-wave model and HoTCoffeeh for the hydrodynamic model, then fitted with one-dimensional and three-dimensional Lévy-stable parametrizations to extract the Lévy index α as a function of transverse momentum KT.

## Key numbers
- Inclusion of resonance decays reduces the Lévy index by 0.2–0.3 across the studied KT range.
- The blast-wave model parameters used: temperature T = 120 MeV, average transverse radius R0 = 7 fm, freeze-out time τfo = 10 fm/c, transverse expansion strength ρ0 = 0.8, with 50,000 events generated.
- The hydrodynamic model used: 1,000 events of 0–10% Au+Au collisions at 200A GeV with freeze-out temperature 120 MeV and η/s = 0.08.
- The first-order Lévy expansion coefficient c1 was found to be closer to 0 for higher KT, but the first-order correction is not negligible, indicating the correlation functions are not exactly Lévy-shaped.

## Context
This work extends studies of non-Gaussian correlation functions in heavy-ion collisions, following experimental results from Adare (2017) that reported Lévy indices well below 2. The authors show that such deviations can arise from non-critical effects, specifically resonance decays and 1D momentum projections, rather than solely from critical endpoint physics, which had been argued to produce α ≈ 0.5. The use of two distinct models (blast-wave and hydrodynamic) demonstrates the robustness of these findings. This enables more careful interpretation of future experimental Lévy index measurements at RHIC and LHC, as corrections from these non-critical effects must be accounted for before attributing small α values to critical phenomena.
