# Constraints on the in-medium nuclear interaction from chiral symmetry and Lattice-QCD

> In this paper we discuss the combined effects on nuclear matter properties of the quark confinement mechanism in nucleon and of the chiral effective potential resulting from the spontaneous breaking of the chiral symmetry in nuclear matter. Based on the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio predictions, it is shown that the chiral potential acquires a specific scalar field cubic dependence, which contributes to the three-body interaction. We also discuss the constraints induced by Lattice-QCD on the model parameters governing the saturation properties. We introduce the term "QCD-connected parameters" for these quantities. We demonstrate that chiral symmetry and Lattice-QCD provide coherent constraints on the in-medium nuclear interaction, suggesting a fundamental origin of the saturation mechanism.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that chiral symmetry and Lattice-QCD provide coherent constraints on the in-medium nuclear interaction, suggesting a fundamental origin of the saturation mechanism, but only when the chiral effective potential is taken from the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model rather than the simpler linear sigma model (LσM); the NJL potential reduces the attractive tadpole diagram and introduces a new parameter \(C_\chi\) that combines with the nucleon response parameter \(C\) to generate saturation.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic chiral confining model for nuclear matter where the nucleon mass depends on the scalar field \(s\) (the fluctuation of the chiral condensate) through two QCD-connected parameters: the scalar coupling \(g_S\) and the dimensionless scalar susceptibility \(C\). The chiral effective potential is derived from the NJL model via bosonization, expanded to third order in \(s\), and compared to the LσM. The in-medium gap equation is solved to second order in density, yielding an effective three-body force whose repulsive or attractive character depends on the combination \(\tilde{C}_3 \simeq (M_N/(g_S F_\pi)) C + (1/2) C_\chi\). Lattice-QCD data on the nucleon scalar charge and chiral susceptibility are used to constrain the model parameters through derivatives of the nucleon mass with respect to the current quark mass.

## Key numbers

* NJL parameter set (no vector interaction, \(G_2=0\)): string tension \(\sigma_g = 0.18\) GeV\(^2\), gluon correlation length \(T_g = 0.286\) fm, cutoff \(\Lambda = 0.604\) GeV, current quark mass \(m = 5.8\) MeV, giving \(F_\pi = 91.9\) MeV, \(M_\pi = 140\) MeV, vacuum constituent quark mass \(M_0 = 356.7\) MeV, effective sigma mass \(M_\sigma = 716.4\) MeV, and \(C_{\chi,\mathrm{NJL}} = 0.488\).
* NJL parameter set with vector interaction (\(G_2 = G_1\)): \(G_1 = 7.705\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\Lambda = 0.740\) GeV, \(m = 3.5\) MeV, giving \(M_0 = 365.3\) MeV and \(C_{\chi,\mathrm{NJL}} = 0.43\).
* The dimensionless nucleon response parameter \(C\) is constrained by a Bayesian analysis with lattice data to \(C \sim 1.4\), close to the value where the scalar susceptibility changes sign (\(C = 1.5\)).
* In the MIT bag model used in the QMC scheme, \(C_\mathrm{MIT} \simeq 0.5\).
* The NL3 model corresponds to an effective \(C_\chi \sim 1.47\) and \(\tilde{C}_3 \sim 0.74\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic chiral approach (Chanfray 2005, 2007; Massot 2008, 2009, 2012) by replacing the linear sigma model (LσM) chiral potential with the NJL model potential, which reduces the attractive tadpole diagram that previously required a large nucleon response parameter \(C > 1\) to achieve saturation. The paper connects to the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model of Guichon (1988) and to the work of Bentz and Thomas (2001), and uses Lattice-QCD data from LTY (2003, 2004), TGLY (2004), and AALTY (2010) to constrain the QCD-connected parameters. The results enable a more fundamental understanding of nuclear saturation and will be extended in a forthcoming paper (NJLFCM) that explicitly calculates the confining interaction from the field correlator method.
