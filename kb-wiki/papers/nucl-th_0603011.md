# First-Order Quantum Phase Transition in a Finite System

> The dynamics at the critical-point of a general first-order quantum phase transition in a finite system is examined, from an algebraic perspective. Suitable Hamiltonians are constructed whose spectra exhibit coexistence of states corresponding to two degenerate minima in the energy surface separated by an arbitrary barrier. Explicit expressions are derived for wave functions and observables at the critical-point.

# First-Order Quantum Phase Transition in a Finite System

## Key claim
The paper constructs a general critical-point Hamiltonian for a first-order quantum phase transition in a finite system (nuclei described by the interacting boson model) that can accommodate an arbitrary barrier between two degenerate minima, not just the extremely low barrier characteristic of the previously studied U(5)-SU(3) critical Hamiltonian. The dynamics at criticality depend on the barrier height, with high-barrier cases producing rigid-rotor-like spectra that differ from the X(5)-like spectra of low-barrier cases.

## Method
The author decomposes the critical Hamiltonian into an intrinsic part and a collective part within the interacting boson model (IBM) framework. The intrinsic part is constructed to have an energy surface with two degenerate minima at β=0 and β=β₀ separated by a barrier of arbitrary height, and yields solvable L-projected deformed eigenstates and spherical eigenstates. The collective part contains rotational kinetic terms (Casimir operators of O(3), O(5), and O(6)) that affect rotational splittings and mixing between coexisting spherical and deformed states, with observables evaluated using variation after projection and two-level mixing of L=0 states.

## Key numbers
- The critical energy surface has the form f(β) = β²(1+β²)⁻²(β-β₀)², with minima at β=0 and β=β₀ = 2a/b > 0
- For the high-barrier case studied (β₀=1.3, barrier height h=0.1), the spectrum with c₃-term gives E(4⁺₁)=3.32, E(6⁺₁)=6.98, E(8⁺₁)=11.95, E(10⁺₁)=18.26 (in units of E(2⁺₁)=1)
- For the c₆-term (X(5)-like), E(4⁺₁)=2.81, E(6⁺₁)=5.43, E(8⁺₁)=8.66, E(10⁺₁)=12.23, E(0⁺₂)=4.56
- B(E2; 4⁺₁→2⁺₁)=1.40 for c₃-term and c₅-term, 1.46 for c₆-term (in units of B(E2; 2⁺₁→0⁺₁)=1)
- B(E2; 0⁺₂→2⁺₁)=0.003 for c₃-term, 0.003 for c₅-term, 0.24 for c₆-term
- For large N, vibrational frequencies about the deformed minimum: ε=εβ=h₂β₀²N, εγ=9(1+β₀²)⁻¹εβ
- The critical U(5)-SU(3) Hamiltonian corresponds to β₀=1/2√2 and barrier height h≈10⁻³

## Context
This work extends studies of shape-phase transitions in the interacting boson model (IBM) by Iachello (2000, 2001) who introduced analytic solutions E(5) and X(5) for second-order and first-order transitions respectively, and by numerous authors (Iachello, Zamfir, Rowe, and others) who studied finite-N attributes of phase transitions using IBM Hamiltonians. The critical U(5)-O(6) Hamiltonian was found to exhibit a γ-independent flat-bottomed energy surface characteristic of a second-order transition, while the critical U(5)-SU(3) Hamiltonian displayed an energy surface with degenerate minima separated by an extremely low barrier, corresponding to a very specific first-order transition. The present work generalizes this to arbitrary barrier heights and provides an algebraic method for constructing critical Hamiltonians that can be applied to other finite systems described by similar algebraic models, such as the proton-neutron IBM for nuclei and the vibron model for molecules.
