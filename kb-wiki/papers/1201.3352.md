# Fluctuations and Correlations of Conserved Charges in the $(2+1)$ Polyakov Quark Meson Model

> We consider the $(2+1)$ flavor Polyakov Quark Meson Model and study the fluctuations (correlations) of conserved charges upto sixth (fourth) order. Comparison is made with lattice data wherever available and overall good qualitative agreement is found, more so for the case of the normalised susceptibilities. The model predictions for the ratio of susceptibilities go to that of an ideal gas of hadrons as in Hadron Resonance Gas Model at low temperatures while at high temperature the values are close to that of an ideal gas of massless quarks. Our study provides a strong basis for the use of PQM as an effective model to understand the topology of the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

The (2+1) flavor Polyakov Quark Meson Model with fermionic vacuum fluctuations included shows good qualitative agreement with lattice QCD data for fluctuations and correlations of conserved charges, particularly for normalized susceptibilities (ratios), though the agreement is better for the Wuppertal-Budapest data than for the HotQCD data, and the model fails to reproduce the low-temperature behavior of the electric charge susceptibility due to the absence of explicit pionic fluctuations.

## Method

The authors computed the thermodynamic potential of the (2+1) flavor Polyakov Quark Meson Model in the mean-field approximation, including the fermionic vacuum term, and used algorithmic differentiation (ADOL-C) to take derivatives of the pressure with respect to chemical potentials up to sixth order for diagonal susceptibilities and fourth order for correlations. The model parameters were fixed from vacuum properties and two parameter sets (ModelHotQCD and ModelWB) were obtained by fitting to lattice data from the respective collaborations. All calculations were performed at zero chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- The model reaches 93% of the Stefan-Boltzmann limit for $\chi_2^B$ by $T \sim 2.4 T_c$ in ModelHotQCD, while the WB data attains 90% of the SB limit by the same temperature.
- For $\chi_4^S$ at $T/T_c \sim 2.4$, the models are 11% higher than the Stefan-Boltzmann limit, while lattice data is 50% higher.
- The ratio $\chi_{11}^{BQ}/\chi_2^{B}$ approaches 1/2 in the low temperature limit.
- The sixth order susceptibilities $\chi_6^B$ and $\chi_6^Q$ are nonzero only in the temperature range from $0.8 T_c$ to $1.1 T_c$.
- The model parameters include: $g = 6.5$, $T_0 = 210$ MeV (ModelHotQCD) and $T_0 = 270$ MeV (ModelWB), with $\kappa = 0.1$ and $\kappa = 0.2$ respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of susceptibilities in the 2-flavor Polyakov Quark Meson Model (PQMsus2f) and the (2+1) flavor PQM model (PQMsus2p1f1, PQMsus2p1f2) by including the fermionic vacuum fluctuations, which were shown in earlier work (PQMVT-2+1) to smooth the phase transition and improve agreement with lattice data. The results are compared with lattice QCD data from the HotQCD collaboration (HotQCDlat2) using p4 action and the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (WBlat) using stout action. The study enables further use of the PQM model to explore the QCD phase diagram topology, particularly the critical end point, and provides a baseline for comparing with experimental event-by-event fluctuation measurements from RHIC and LHC.
