# Boost-Invariant (2+1)-dimensional Anisotropic Hydrodynamics

> We present results of the application of the anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) framework to (2+1)-dimensional boost invariant systems. The necessary aHydro dynamical equations are derived by taking moments of the Boltzmann equation using a momentum-space anisotropic one-particle distribution function. We present a derivation of the necessary equations and then proceed to numerical solutions of the resulting partial differential equations using both realistic smooth Glauber initial conditions and fluctuating Monte-Carlo Glauber initial conditions. For this purpose we have developed two numerical implementations: one which is based on straightforward integration of the resulting partial differential equations supplemented by a two-dimensional weighted Lax-Friedrichs smoothing in the case of fluctuating initial conditions; and another that is based on the application of the Kurganov-Tadmor central scheme. For our final results we compute the collective flow of the matter via the lab-frame energy-momentum tensor eccentricity as a function of the assumed shear viscosity to entropy ratio, proper time, and impact parameter.

## Key claim

The paper extends the anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) framework to (2+1)-dimensional boost-invariant systems using a relaxation rate proportional to the local hard momentum scale, which allows smooth matching to second-order Israel-Stewart viscous hydrodynamics in the limit of small momentum-space anisotropy, unlike prior works that assumed a fixed rate of isotropization.

## Method

The authors derive dynamical equations by taking zeroth and first moments of the Boltzmann equation using a momentum-space anisotropic one-particle distribution function characterized by one anisotropy parameter and a single momentum scale. They solve the resulting partial differential equations numerically using two implementations: one with straightforward integration supplemented by two-dimensional weighted Lax-Friedrichs smoothing for fluctuating initial conditions, and another based on the Kurganov-Tadmor central scheme. The collective flow is computed via the lab-frame energy-momentum tensor eccentricity as a function of shear viscosity to entropy ratio, proper time, and impact parameter, using both smooth Glauber and fluctuating Monte-Carlo Glauber initial conditions.

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic hydrodynamics methodology previously applied to (0+1)-dimensional boost-invariant systems by Florkowski (2010) and Martinez (2010), and to (1+1)-dimensional systems by Martinez (2010) and Ryblewski (2010). It differs from earlier (2+1)-dimensional extensions by Ryblewski (2011) and Florkowski (2011) by using a relaxation rate proportional to the hard momentum scale rather than a fixed rate, enabling smooth matching to second-order viscous hydrodynamics. The framework enables more realistic modeling of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC where large momentum-space anisotropies develop, particularly near the longitudinal and transverse edges of the system and at early times.
