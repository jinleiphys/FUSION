# Effect of BCS pairing on entrainment in neutron superfluid current in neutron star crust

> The relative current density $n^i$ of conduction neutrons in a neutron star crust beyond the neutron drip threshold can be expected to be related to the corresponding particle momentum covector $p_i$ by a linear relation of the form $n^i=K^{ij}p_j$ in terms of a physically well defined mobility tensor $K^{ij}$. This result is describable as an ``entrainment'' whose effect - wherever the crust lattice is isotropic - will simply be to change the ordinary neutron mass m to an effective mass $m_\star$ such that in terms of the relevant number density n of unconfined neutrons we shall have $K^{ij}=n/m_\starγ^{ij}$. In a preceding analysis based on an independent particle treatment using Bloch boundary conditions to obtain the distribution of energy $E_k$ and associated group velocity $v_k^i=\partial E_k/\partial\hbar k_i$ as a function of wavenumber $k_i$, it was shown that the mobility tensor would be given by $K^{ij}\propto\int d^3 k v_k^i v_k^jδ\{E_k-μ\}$, where $μ$ is the Fermi energy. Using the approach due to Bogoliubov, it is shown here that the effect of BCS pairing with an energy gap Δ_F and corresponding quasiparticle energy function \EE_k=\sqrt{(E_k-μ)^2+Δ_F^2}$ will just be to replace the Dirac distributional integrand by a smoother distribution : $K^{ij}\propto \int d^3 k v_k^i v_k^j Δ_F^2 /\EE^3_k$. It is also shown how the pairing condensation gives rise to superfluidity in the technical sense of providing (meta) stability against resistive perturbations for a current that is not too strong (its momentum $p_i$ must be small enough to give $2|p_i v_k^i|<\EE_k^2/|E_k-μ|$ for all modes). It is concluded that the prediction of a very large effective mass enhancement in the middle layers of the crust will not be significantly affected by the pairing mechanism.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including BCS pairing (with energy gap Δ_F) in the description of neutron superfluid currents in the inner crust of a neutron star modifies the mobility tensor by replacing the Dirac delta function on the Fermi surface with a smoother distribution proportional to Δ_F²/ℰ³_k, but this does not significantly affect the previously predicted very large effective mass enhancement in the middle layers of the crust.

## Method

The authors start from a second-quantized independent-particle treatment using Bloch boundary conditions for neutrons in a periodic crystalline lattice, then generalize it using the Bogoliubov transformation to include BCS-type pairing interactions in a mean-field model with a contact two-body interaction. They derive the modified mobility tensor K^{ij} for the superfluid phase by evaluating the expectation value of the neutron current density operator in a stationary state that minimizes energy subject to constraints on total particle number and total current, using Lagrange multipliers μ (chemical potential) and p_i (momentum per neutron).

## Key numbers

- The neutron drip threshold density is about 10¹¹ g/cm³, above which unbound ("conduction") neutrons appear in the crust
- The critical temperature for BCS pairing condensation is of order 10⁹ degrees Kelvin, while the relevant temperature range for isolated neutron stars is well below 10⁸ degrees Kelvin
- The superfluid gap parameter is denoted Δ_F, and the quasiparticle energy is ℰ_k = √((E_k − μ)² + Δ_F²)
- The stability condition for superfluid current is 2|p_i v_k^i| < ℰ_k²/|E_k − μ| for all modes, where p_i is the neutron momentum

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous independent-particle treatment (Carter, Chamel, Haensel, 2004) that predicted a very large effective mass enhancement for free neutrons in the middle layers of the inner crust, contrasting with the moderate effective mass reduction found in deeper core layers by Comer et al. (2003, 2004) using relativistic σ-ω mean field models and by Borumand et al. (1996) using Fermi liquid theory. The paper shows that BCS pairing does not significantly alter the effective mass prediction, while confirming that the pairing condensation provides the metastability (superfluidity) needed for currents to persist without dissipation, which is relevant for understanding pulsar glitch mechanisms.
