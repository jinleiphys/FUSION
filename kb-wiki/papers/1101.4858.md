# Three-body interactions in Fermi systems

> We show that the contributions of three-quasiparticle interactions to normal Fermi systems at low energies and temperatures are suppressed by n_q/n compared to two-body interactions, where n_q is the density of excited or added quasiparticles and n is the ground-state density. For finite Fermi systems, three-quasiparticle contributions are suppressed by the corresponding ratio of particle numbers N_q/N. This is illustrated for polarons in strongly interacting spin-polarized Fermi gases and for valence neutrons in neutron-rich calcium isotopes.

# Three-body interactions in Fermi systems

## Key claim
Three-quasiparticle interactions in normal Fermi systems at low energies and temperatures are suppressed by the ratio of quasiparticle density to ground-state density (n_q/n) compared to two-quasiparticle interactions, even when the underlying three-body forces are strong. This suppression holds for both excitations that conserve particle number and those that add or remove valence particles, explaining why effective two-body interactions in shell-model calculations work so well.

## Method
The authors use Fermi liquid theory to expand the energy of a normal Fermi system in powers of the quasiparticle distribution δn, showing that the expansion parameter is the quasiparticle density n_q/n (or equivalently Δ/μ, the excitation energy over chemical potential) rather than the interaction strength. They analyze the scaling of one-, two-, and three-quasiparticle contributions to the energy for both δN=0 excitations and δN≠0 valence-particle additions, then illustrate the suppression with two examples: polarons in strongly interacting spin-polarized Fermi gases and valence neutrons in neutron-rich calcium isotopes.

## Key numbers
- Polaron binding energy: η = -0.615 (universal constant for 1/a_s=0, where E_p = η ε_F)
- Polaron effective mass: m_p*/m = 1.20(2) from Monte-Carlo calculations
- Average two-quasiparticle interaction for polarons: F^(2) = 6B/5 ≈ 0.17 (with B ≈ 0.14)
- For calcium isotopes fit: μ = -10.8 MeV and ⟨F^(2)⟩ = -2.0 from fit to AME2003 atomic mass evaluation
- Estimated three-quasiparticle contribution to ⁵²Ca (N_q=12): δE ∼ 1.5 MeV (assuming ⟨F^(3)⟩ ∼ 1)
- Estimated three-quasiparticle contribution to ⁵⁸Ca (N_q=18): δE ∼ 5.5 MeV (assuming ⟨F^(3)⟩ ∼ 1)

## Context
This work extends the Fermi liquid theory framework of Landau (1950s) and the induced interaction formalism of Babu and Brown (1970s) to systematically quantify the suppression of three-body quasiparticle interactions. It addresses the apparent contradiction between the importance of three-nucleon forces in few-body systems and light nuclei (established by ab-initio methods such as Green's Function Monte Carlo and No-Core Shell Model) and the success of effective two-body interactions in shell-model calculations for medium-mass and heavier nuclei, as noted by Talmi. The results enable estimates of where residual three-body interactions might become detectable in large-scale shell-model calculations, no-core shell model with a core, coupled-cluster theory, and nuclear lattice simulations, and explain why normal-ordered two-body treatments of three-nucleon forces in low-momentum interactions (Vlowk) are so successful.
