# A Deeply Bound Dibaryon is Incompatible with Neutron Stars and Supernovae

> We study the effect of a dibaryon, S, in the mass range 1860 MeV < m_S < 2054 MeV, which is heavy enough not to disturb the stability of nuclei and light enough to possibly be cosmologically metastable. Such a deeply bound state can act as a baryon sink in regions of high baryon density and temperature. We find that the ambient conditions encountered inside a newly born neutron star are likely to sustain a sufficient population of hyperons to ensure that a population of S dibaryons can equilibrate in less than a few seconds. This would be catastrophic for the stability of neutron stars and the observation of neutrino emission from the proto-neutron star of Supernova 1987A over ~ O(10)s. A deeply bound dibaryon is therefore incompatible with the observed supernova explosion, unless the cross section for S production is severely suppressed.

## Key claim
A deeply bound dibaryon (the "S" sexaquark) with mass between 1860 MeV and 2054 MeV would be produced rapidly in the hot, dense interior of a proto-neutron star via exothermic Lambda-Lambda collisions, converting all baryon number into dibaryons in less than a few seconds. This is incompatible with the observed neutrino burst from Supernova 1987A lasting about 10 seconds, unless the dibaryon production cross section is suppressed by roughly 11 orders of magnitude.

## Method
The authors solved coupled differential equations for the number densities of nucleons (N), Lambda hyperons, and S dibaryons in a proto-neutron star core, assuming constant temperature T=30 MeV and nuclear saturation density n0=0.16 fm^-3. They calculated the thermally averaged cross section for Lambda production via NN -> Lambda N using the weak coupling g_Lambda N pi extracted from the vacuum Lambda lifetime, and the cross section for S production via Lambda Lambda -> S gamma using a wavefunction overlap model (Isgur-Karl and Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone) that depends on the radii of the Lambda and the S dibaryon. The equilibration time t_S for S production was then compared to the observed neutrino cooling timescale of about 10 seconds.

## Key numbers
- S dibaryon mass range considered: 1860 MeV < m_S < 2054 MeV, corresponding to binding energy range 176.9 MeV < B_S < 361 MeV.
- Default cross section for Lambda production used: <sigma_{NN -> Lambda N} v> = 3 x 10^-30 cm^3/s (conservative value assuming 90% cancellation in the matrix element).
- S production equilibrates in time t_S = n0 / (n_Lambda^infinity)^2 <sigma_{Lambda Lambda -> S gamma} v>, which for typical parameters gives t_S ~ (4 x 10^-34 cm^3/s) / <sigma_{Lambda Lambda -> S gamma} v> seconds.
- For <sigma_{Lambda Lambda -> S gamma} v> >= 10^-34 cm^3/s, S production is fatal for the proto-neutron star (t_S << 10 s).
- The coupling g_Lambda N pi^2 ~ 7 x 10^-11, extracted from the vacuum Lambda lifetime tau_Lambda ~ 2.6 x 10^-10 s.
- The minimum S radius from Compton wavelength considerations: r_S >= 0.1 fm x (2054 MeV / m_S) + 0.34x fm, with x=0 or 0.1.
- For m_S < 1950 MeV, the two-pion channel Lambda Lambda -> S pi pi gives t_S << 1 ns, independent of r_S.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical study of the hypothetical H dibaryon (Jaffe 1976) and the more deeply bound S sexaquark (Farrar 2003, 2017) by testing its astrophysical consequences. Prior lattice QCD studies (Beane 2010, 2011, 2012) support a weakly bound dibaryon with binding energy ~O(10) MeV but cannot rule out the more tightly bound sexaquark. The authors show that the existence of such a deeply bound state is incompatible with observations of neutron star stability and the neutrino signal from Supernova 1987A (Hirata 1987, Bionta 1987, Alekseev 1987), unless the S production cross section is severely suppressed by requiring its radius to be extremely small (~0.1 fm). This enables future lattice QCD calculations and experimental searches (e.g., in Upsilon decays, Echenard 2018) to definitively test the sexaquark hypothesis.
