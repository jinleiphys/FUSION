# Hypernuclei and nuclear matter in a chiral SU(3) RMF model

> We develop a chiral SU(3) RMF model for octet baryons, as an extension of the recently developed chiral SU(2) RMF model with logarithmic sigma potential. For Sigma-meson coupling, strong repulsion(SR) and weak repulsion(WR) cases are examined in existing atomic shift data of Sigma^-. In both of these cases, we need an attractive pocket of a few MeV depth around nuclear surface.

## Key claim

The chiral SU(3) RMF model can reproduce separation energies of single Λ hypernuclei and the ΛΛ bond energy in ⁶_ΛΛHe, but for Σ⁻-nucleus potentials the SU_f(3) symmetry relation for g_ρΣ must be broken to fit atomic shift data at N > Z, suggesting that short-range repulsion from quark Pauli blocking is not captured by the chiral SU(3) RMF framework.

## Method

The authors extend a chiral SU(2) RMF model with a logarithmic sigma potential to SU(3) by including mesons with hidden strangeness (ζ and φ) and using a chiral potential with a determinant interaction to handle the U_A(1) anomaly. Hyperon-vector meson couplings are set by SU_f(3) symmetry relations and the OZI rule, while hyperon-scalar meson couplings are treated as free parameters fitted to Λ hypernuclear data (S_Λ and ΔB_ΛΛ) and to Σ⁻ atomic shift data for two cases of vector repulsion strength (Strong Repulsion and Weak Repulsion).

## Key numbers

- Incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter: K ∼ 220 MeV, obtained by fitting bulk properties of normal nuclei and the nuclear matter saturation point.
- Λ potential depth around 30 MeV, represented by a linear combination of g_σΛ and g_ζΛ, reproduces S_Λ data across a wide mass region.
- Total χ² per degree of freedom for Σ⁻ atomic shift data (O, Mg, Al, Si, S, W, Pb) is around 1.3.
- Imaginary part of the Σ⁻ optical potential (conversion width) is found to be −15 to −20 MeV, extracted from fits to Σ⁻ atomic data.

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(2) RMF model of Tsubakihara (2006) to SU(3), incorporating strangeness degrees of freedom. It builds on earlier chiral RMF models by Papazoglou (1998) and Zschiesche (2006) but uses a logarithmic chiral potential derived from the strong coupling limit of lattice QCD. The results enable predictions for Ξ hypernuclei and hyperatoms, and the authors note that consistency checks with quasi-free Σ⁻ spectrum analyses (using DWIA with local optimized Fermi average t-matrix) are needed to judge whether Σ⁻ repulsion should be strong or weak.
