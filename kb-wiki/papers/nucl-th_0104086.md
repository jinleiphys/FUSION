# Mesons electromagnetic production study via a constituent quark approach

> Using a chiral constituent quark approach based on the broken SU(6) \otimes O(3) symmetry, we focus on the spectroscopy of isospin-1/2 nucleonic resonances. A model for the eta photoproduction, embodying all known nucleonic resonances, shows clear need for a yet undiscovered S11 resonance, for which we determine the mass (1.730 GeV) and the total width (180 MeV).

## Key claim

Using a chiral constituent quark model with broken SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry, the author finds that all known nucleonic resonances are insufficient to describe η photoproduction data, requiring a yet undiscovered S11 resonance with mass 1.730 GeV and total width 180 MeV. The claim is conditional on the specific quark-model framework and the assumption that configuration mixing between S11 states follows the Moorhouse selection rule.

## Method

The author constructs a model for γp → ηp using a chiral constituent quark approach based on the low-energy QCD Lagrangian, including s-channel, u-channel, and t-channel contributions. The s-channel explicitly includes 11 known nucleonic resonances below 2 GeV with their SU(6)⊗O(3) assignments, while resonances above 2 GeV are treated as degenerate. Configuration mixing between the two S11 states and the two D13 states is parameterized by mixing angles ΘS and ΘD, and the model is fitted to differential cross-section data from MAMI/Mainz and Graal, plus polarized beam asymmetry from Graal.

## Key numbers

- New S11 resonance mass: 1.729 ± 0.003 GeV (Model II)
- New S11 resonance total width: 183 ± 10 MeV (Model II)
- S11 mixing angle ΘS: -27 ± 1 degrees (Model II), compared to -32 degrees from Isgur-Karl (1977)
- D13 mixing angle ΘD: 5.1 ± 0.2 degrees (Model II), compared to 6 degrees from Isgur-Karl (1977)
- S11(1535) mass: 1.542 GeV (Model II)
- S11(1535) width: 162 MeV (Model II)
- χ² per degree of freedom: 1.6 for Model II (with third S11), versus 3.8 for Model I (without third S11)
- Predicted third S11 from Li and Workman (1996): mass 1.712 GeV, total width 184 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model approach of Li and Saghai (1998) and Zhao, Saghai, and Li (2000) to η photoproduction at higher energies. The need for a third S11 resonance contradicts the assumption that all known resonances suffice, and the extracted parameters are compatible with the Isgur-Karl (1977) quark model predictions for mixing angles. The result enables searches for this resonance in η electroproduction at JLab and in strangeness production channels, and raises the question of whether the S11(1730) has a three-quark structure or a KΣ molecular structure as suggested by Li and Workman (1996).
