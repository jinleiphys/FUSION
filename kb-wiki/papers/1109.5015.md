# Chiral Magnetic Effect and evolution of electromagnetic field

> The energy dependence of observable two particle correlator in search for the local strong parity violation in Au+Au collisions is estimated within a simple phenomenological model. The model reproduces available RHIC data but at LHC predicts that the Chiral Magnetic effect (CME) will be about 20 times weaker than at RHIC, contrary to the first LHC measurements. In the lower energy range this effect should vanish sharply at energy somewhere above the top SPS one with agreement with the preliminary results of the Beam Energy Scan program. To elucidate CME background effects a transport HSD model including magnetic field evolution is put forward and electromagnetic dynamics at the RHIC energy is investigated. It is observed that the electromagnetic field included into the hadronic model does not influence on observables due to mutual compensation of effects of electric and magnetic fields.

# Chiral Magnetic Effect and Evolution of Electromagnetic Field

## Key claim
The chiral magnetic effect (CME) signal in heavy-ion collisions is predicted to be about 20 times weaker at LHC energies than at RHIC, contrary to first LHC measurements, and should vanish sharply at an energy somewhere above the top SPS energy. However, when electromagnetic field evolution is included in a full transport model, the electromagnetic field does not influence observables due to mutual compensation of electric and magnetic field effects, calling into question whether the CME signal can be cleanly extracted.

## Method
The authors use a phenomenological one-dimensional random walk model in topological number space to estimate the energy dependence of the two-particle correlator associated with local parity violation in Au+Au collisions. They also extend the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) transport model to include retarded electromagnetic fields via Liénard-Wiechert potentials, solving the coupled equations of motion for strongly interacting particles in the presence of electric and magnetic fields, and compare observables with and without electromagnetic field effects at RHIC energy.

## Key numbers
- The CME at LHC energy 5.5 TeV is predicted to be weaker by a factor of about 20 compared to RHIC at 200 GeV.
- The critical magnetic field value used is eB_crit = 0.7 m_pi^2, which results in absence of CME above top SPS energy.
- Maximal magnetic field strength at RHIC (200 GeV, b=10 fm) reaches eB_y ~ 5 m_pi^2 (where m_pi^2 ≈ 10^18 gauss).
- Maximal energy density at center of colliding system exceeds 50 GeV/fm^3 at moment of maximal overlap.
- The magnetic field from spectators vanishes at the center for t ≈ 1 fm/c.
- The electric field at the central point is negligible for t ≳ 0.15 fm/c.

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological analysis of Toneev and Voronyuk (2010) of first STAR data on charge separation, and compares with preliminary results from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program (BES11) and first LHC measurements (ALICE11). The HSD transport model with electromagnetic fields builds on prior work by Kharzeev (2004), Kharzeev and Zhitnitsky (2007), and Skokov, Illarionov, and Toneev (2009) on magnetic field creation in heavy-ion collisions. The finding that electromagnetic fields do not affect observables due to compensation challenges the interpretation of charge separation as a clean CME signal and motivates further study of background effects, particularly at LHC and lower RHIC energies where the Beam Energy Scan program can test the predicted sharp disappearance of the effect.
