# Baryon-Strangeness Correlations from Hadron/String- and Quark-Dynamics

> Baryon-strangeness correlations ($C_{BS}$) are studied with a hadron/string transport approach (UrQMD) and a dynamical quark recombination model (quark molecular dynamics, qMD) for various energies from $E_{lab}=4A$ GeV to $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. As expected, we find that the hadron/string dynamics shows correlations similar to a simple hadron gas. In case of the quark molecular dynamics, we find that initially the $C_{BS}$ correlation is that of a weakly interacting QGP but changes in the process of hadronization also to the value for a hadron gas. Therefore, we conclude that the hadronization process itself makes the initial baryon strangeness correlation unobservable. To make an experimental study of this observable more feasible, we also investigate how a restriction to only charged kaons and $Λ$'s (instead of all baryons and all strange particles) influences the theoretical result on $C_{BS}$. We find that a good approximation of the full result can be obtained in this limit in the present simulation.

## Key claim

The baryon-strangeness correlation coefficient $C_{BS}$, initially proposed as a diagnostic for quark-gluon plasma formation, is found to be unobservable as a QGP signal because the hadronization process itself destroys the initial correlations, even when a quark phase is explicitly present in the simulation.

## Method

The authors used two microscopic transport models to simulate central Au+Au/Pb+Pb collisions: the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics model (UrQMD v2.2), a hadron/string transport approach describing rescattering, string excitation and resonance decays; and the quark Molecular Dynamics model (qMD), which propagates quarks through an effective potential and hadronizes via dynamical recombination into white clusters mapped to known hadrons. The correlation coefficient $C_{BS}$ was calculated event-by-event from the baryon number and strangeness in each event within a rapidity window of $y_{\rm max}=0.25$, scanning beam energies from $E_{lab}=4A$ GeV to $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV.

## Key numbers

- For UrQMD at the highest RHIC energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV), $C_{BS} \approx 0.4$, slightly lower than the value for a fully thermalized hadron gas.
- For an ideal weakly coupled QGP, the expected value is $C_{BS}=1$; for a hadron gas, the value is $C_{BS}=0.66$ as evaluated by Koch et al. (2005).
- In the qMD model at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, $C_{BS} \approx 1$ at early times (during the quark phase), but decreases to a final value of $C_{BS} \approx 0.6-0.7$ around 6 fm/c when hadronization starts.
- The energy scan covers the range from $E_{lab}=4A$ GeV to $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Koch et al. (2005) to use $C_{BS}$ as a diagnostic of QGP formation, testing it with two dynamical models. The UrQMD results confirm the hadron-gas-like correlation expected from a purely hadronic description, while the qMD results show that even with an initial QGP phase, hadronization via recombination blurs the signal. This contradicts the expectation that $C_{BS}$ would survive as a clear QGP signature and may explain why no decisive fluctuation signal for QGP has been observed in experimental data. The finding that restricting to only charged kaons and $\Lambda$'s gives a good approximation of the full $C_{BS}$ at high energies makes experimental measurement more feasible.
