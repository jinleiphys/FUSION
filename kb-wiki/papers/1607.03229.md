# A nucleus-dependent valence-space approach to nuclear structure

> We present a nucleus-dependent valence-space approach for calculating ground and excited states of nuclei, which generalizes the shell-model in-medium similarity renormalization group to an ensemble reference with fractionally filled orbitals. Because the ensemble is used only as a reference, and not to represent physical states, no symmetry restoration is required. This allows us to capture 3N forces among valence nucleons with a valence-space Hamiltonian specifically targeted to each nucleus of interest. Predicted ground-state energies from carbon through nickel agree with results of other large-space ab initio methods, generally to the 1\% level. In addition, we show that this new approach is required in order to obtain convergence for nuclei in the upper $p$ and $sd$ shells. Finally, we address the $1^+$/$3^+$ ground-state inversion problem in $^{22}\text{Na}$ and $^{46}\text{V}$. This approach extends the reach of ab initio nuclear structure calculations to essentially all light- and medium-mass nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper presents a nucleus-dependent valence-space approach that generalizes the shell-model in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) to an ensemble reference with fractionally filled orbitals, capturing three-nucleon (3N) forces among valence nucleons without requiring symmetry restoration. The authors claim this approach is required to obtain convergence for nuclei in the upper p and sd shells, and that it extends ab initio nuclear structure calculations to essentially all light- and medium-mass nuclei.

## Method

The authors generalize the IM-SRG framework to use an ensemble (mixed-state) reference defined by a density matrix with coefficients chosen such that the ensemble-averaged particle number matches the nucleus of interest. Normal ordering is defined with respect to this ensemble reference, allowing the normal-ordered two-body approximation to capture the dominant effects of residual 3N forces among valence nucleons. The IM-SRG decoupling is performed using the Magnus formulation with the arctangent variant of the White generator, followed by valence-space diagonalizations using NuShellX@MSU. The interaction is transformed to a Hartree-Fock basis, and the valence space is defined by one major harmonic-oscillator shell for protons and neutrons.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energies from carbon through nickel agree with results of other large-space ab initio methods generally to the 1% level.
- Calculations use a chiral NN force at N3LO with a cutoff of Lambda_NN = 500 MeV, a local 3N force at N2LO with a cutoff of Lambda_3N = 400 MeV (and 500 MeV for some comparisons), SRG softened to lambda_SRG = 1.88 fm^-1, with an oscillator frequency of 24 MeV.
- The model space includes all single-particle states with 2n + l <= e_max = 12.
- For 16O and 40Ca, the error due to the additional valence-space decoupling step amounts to approximately 1%.
- For 46V with Lambda_3N = 500 MeV, the SRG-induced many-body forces lead to unreliable results, so only the Lambda_3N = 400 MeV result is reported.

## Context

This work extends the shell-model IM-SRG approach previously developed by Tsukiyama (2012), Bogner (2014), and Stroberg (2016), which used a closed-shell core as the reference and showed increasing overbinding as valence particles were added. The new ensemble normal ordering (ENO) approach corrects this deficiency by capturing the repulsive 3N force among valence nucleons that was previously neglected. The method enables ab initio calculations for the 1+/3+ ground-state inversion problem in 22Na and 46V, which were previously inaccessible to large-space methods such as importance-truncated no-core shell model (IT-NCSM) or coupled cluster. This approach enables future calculations of ground and excited states for essentially all light- and medium-mass nuclei, including deformed systems.
