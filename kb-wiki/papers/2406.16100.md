# Multipole modes of excitation in tetrahedrally deformed neutron-rich Zr isotopes

> The multipole modes of excitation for tetrahedrally deformed neutron-rich Zr isotopes are investigated using the quasiparticle finite amplitude method based on the covariant density functional theories. By employing the density-dependent point-coupling covariant density functional theory with the parameter set DD-PC1 in the particle-hole channel and a separable pairing interaction of finite range, it is observed that a distinct peak emerges at $ω= 9.0 - 10.0$ MeV in the isoscalar quadrupole $K=0$ strength when $β_{32}$ distortion is considered for $^{110,112}$Zr. This peak is absent when the deformation is limited to axially symmetric octuple or spherical case. It also does not appears in neighboring axially quadrople or octupole deformed nuclei, thus can be viewed as an indicator for the tetrahedral shape.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a distinct peak at ω = 9.0–10.0 MeV in the isoscalar quadrupole K=0 strength function, which appears only when β32 (tetrahedral) deformation is included for 110,112Zr, can serve as an indicator for tetrahedral shape. The hedge is that this peak is unique to tetrahedral deformation and does not appear in neighboring axially quadrupole or octupole deformed nuclei, but the claim is based on theoretical calculations using one specific covariant density functional (DD-PC1) and a separable pairing force.

## Method

The quasiparticle finite amplitude method (QFAM) based on the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) plus quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) model was used to compute multipole transition strengths. The density-dependent point-coupling covariant density functional theory with parameter set DD-PC1 was employed in the particle-hole channel, with a separable pairing interaction of finite range in the particle-particle channel. The RHB equation was solved by expanding Dirac spinors in a simplex-y harmonic oscillator basis with 20 oscillator shells, and the FAM equations were solved iteratively with an imaginary broadening γ = 1.0 MeV and a frequency spacing of 0.5 MeV.

## Key numbers

- A distinct peak emerges at ω = 9.0–10.0 MeV in the isoscalar quadrupole K=0 strength for 110,112Zr when β32 distortion is considered.
- For 110Zr at the tetrahedrally deformed ground state minimum (β32 = 0.18), discrete QRPA eigenfrequencies were found at Ωi = 8.59, 8.75, 9.03, 9.22, and 9.43 MeV with transition strengths of 36, 86, 72, 346, and 80 fm4, respectively.
- For 110Zr at the pear-shaped minimum (β30 = 0.20), QRPA transition strengths for Ωi = 8.55, 8.79, and 9.04 MeV were 58, 76, and 44 fm4, respectively.
- The isoscalar giant monopole resonance peak appears at ω ≈ 17.5 MeV for 110Zr across all deformation shapes.
- The dominant isoscalar quadrupole giant resonance peak is located at ω ≈ 15.0 MeV for 110Zr.
- For 112Zr, the tetrahedral deformation parameter is β32 = 0.15, and the intermediate peak appears at ω ≈ 10.0 MeV.
- For 114Zr, the ground state is pear-shaped with β30 = 0.15.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical investigations of tetrahedral symmetry in nuclei, particularly the prediction by Zhao (2017) that the ground state shapes of 110,112Zr are tetrahedral based on multidimensionally constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations. The study builds on the understanding that collective modes in nuclear response functions can reveal nuclear shapes, as demonstrated for triaxial deformation in 100Mo by Shi (2023) and Washiyama (2024). The results suggest that the unique peak in the isoscalar quadrupole K=0 strength could provide a new experimental signature for identifying tetrahedral deformation, which has remained experimentally unconfirmed despite prior searches in nuclei such as 160Yb and 154,156Gd by Bark (2010) and Jentschel (2010).
