# Updates of the nuclear equation of state for core-collapse supernovae and neutron Stars: effects of 3-body forces, QCD, and magnetic fields

> We summarize several new developments in the nuclear equation of state for supernova simulations and neutron stars. We discuss an updated and improved Notre-Dame-Livermore Equation of State (NDL EoS) for use in supernovae simulations. This Eos contains many updates. Among them are the effects of 3- body nuclear forces at high densities and the possible transition to a QCD chiral and/or super-conducting color phase at densities. We also consider the neutron star equation of state and neutrino transport in the presence of strong magnetic fields. We study a new quantum hadrodynamic (QHD) equation of state for neutron stars (with and without hyperons) in the presence of strong magnetic fields. The parameters are constrained by deduced masses and radii. The calculated adiabatic index for these magnetized neutron stars exhibit rapid changes with density. This may provide a mechanism for star-quakes and flares in magnetars. We also investigate the strong magnetic field effects on the moments of inertia and spin down of neutron stars. The change of the moment of inertia associated with emitted magnetic flares is shown to match well with observed glitches in some magnetars. We also discuss a perturbative calculation of neutrino scattering and absorption in hot and dense hyperonic neutron-star matter in the presence of a strong magnetic field. The absorption cross-sections show a remarkable angular dependence in that the neutrino absorption strength is reduced in a direction parallel to the magnetic field and enhanced in the opposite direction. The pulsar kick velocities associated with this asymmetry comparable to observed pulsar velocities and may affect the early spin down rate of proto-neutron star magnetars with a toroidal field configuration.

## Key claim

The updated Notre-Dame-Livermore Equation of State (NDL EoS) can accommodate neutron star masses of at least 1.97 solar masses, but only if three-body nuclear forces are included at high baryon density; a first-order phase transition to a quark-gluon plasma is consistent with this mass constraint only for a bag constant above 220 MeV.

## Method

The authors constructed a new Skyrme energy-density functional with a modified zero-range three-body interaction, treating the power-law index sigma as a free parameter constrained by the nuclear compressibility (K0 = 240 ± 10 MeV) and skewness coefficient (Q0 = -390 ± 90 MeV) derived from breathing-mode data. For the QCD transition, they used a bag model with two-loop corrections and a strange quark mass of 150 MeV, comparing first-order mixed-phase and crossover transitions. For magnetized neutron stars, they employed a quantum hadrodynamic (QHD) equation of state with and without hyperons, and performed perturbative calculations of neutrino scattering and absorption in strong magnetic fields.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: n0 ≈ 0.16 fm^-3; binding energy per nucleon: E0 = -16 MeV
- Compressibility: K0 = 240 ± 10 MeV (adopted from Colo)
- Skewness coefficient derived from saturation conditions: Q0 = -390 ± 90 MeV
- Skyrme parameters for fiducial NDL EoS: t0 = -1718 MeV fm^3, (3t1+5t2) = -102 MeV fm^5, t3 = 13226 MeV fm^(3σ+3), σ = 0.369
- Symmetry energy at saturation: S0 = 30.5 MeV
- Neutron star mass constraint: maximum mass ≥ 1.97 ± 0.04 M⊙ (from Demorest)
- QCD vacuum energy range from lattice gauge theory: 165 ≤ B^(1/4) ≤ 225 MeV
- For a first-order QCD transition, bag constant B^(1/4) > 220 MeV gives transition temperature Tc = 158 MeV
- Neutrino absorption asymmetry in strong magnetic fields: up to 2% of the entire neutrino momentum

## Context

This work extends the original Livermore equation of state (Bowers and Wilson) and updates the Notre-Dame-Livermore EoS to be consistent with modern experimental nuclear matter constraints and the high-mass neutron star observation of Demorest (2010). It contrasts with the softer LS180 EoS of Lattimer and Swesty (1991) and the Shen EoS (1998), which cannot satisfy the 1.97 solar mass constraint without three-body forces. The magnetic field studies build on prior work by Suh (2001, 2010) and Ryu (2012) on magnetar structure and glitches, and enable future simulations of proto-neutron star spin-down and pulsar kick velocities from asymmetric neutrino emission in toroidal magnetic field configurations.
