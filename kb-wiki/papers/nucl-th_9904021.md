# Heavy Meson Observables

> An accurate and unified description of the non-hadronic electroweak interactions of light- and heavy-mesons is possible when the dressing of quark propagators and the finite size of hadronic bound states is explicitly accounted for.

## Key claim

An accurate and unified description of non-hadronic electroweak interactions of both light- and heavy-mesons is possible when the dressing of quark propagators and the finite size of hadronic bound states are explicitly accounted for, but the heavy-meson binding energy is large enough that significant deviations from heavy-quark symmetry predictions (up to 30% in b-to-c transitions and up to a factor of 2 in c-to-d transitions) remain in current experiments.

## Method

The authors employ a Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) framework for dressed-quark propagators, with heavy quarks (c, b) approximated by a momentum-independent constituent mass function, while light quarks (u, d, s) retain significant momentum dependence. Bound states are described via Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, and a Gaussian Ansatz is used for the heavy-meson amplitude. A chi-squared fit to 42 observables (leptonic decays, semileptonic heavy-to-heavy and heavy-to-light transitions, radiative and strong decays, and the rare B-to-K*-gamma process) is performed using ten parameters plus four quark masses.

## Key numbers

- Current-quark masses: m_u,d = 5.4 MeV, m_s = 119 MeV
- Constituent-quark masses: hat M_c = 1.32 GeV, hat M_b = 4.65 GeV
- Width of Gaussian Ansatz for heavy-meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitude: omega_H = 1.81 GeV
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom: chi^2/d.o.f = 1.75; chi^2/N_obs = 1.17
- Calculated decay constants: f_pi = 0.146 GeV (observed 0.131), f_K = 0.178 GeV (observed 0.160), f_D = 0.213 GeV (observed 0.200 +/- 0.030), f_Ds = 0.234 GeV (observed 0.251 +/- 0.030), f_B = 0.182 GeV (observed 0.170 +/- 0.035)
- Calculated strong decay widths: Gamma_rho-pi-pi = 0.118 GeV (observed 0.151), Gamma_Kstar-(K-pi) = 0.052 GeV (observed 0.051)
- Calculated Isgur-Wise function values: xi(1.1) = 0.84 (observed 0.86 +/- 0.03), xi(1.2) = 0.72 (observed 0.75 +/- 0.05), xi(1.3) = 0.63 (observed 0.66 +/- 0.06), xi(1.4) = 0.56 (observed 0.59 +/- 0.07), xi(1.5) = 0.50 (observed 0.53 +/- 0.08)
- Calculated slope parameter rho^2 = 1.84 (observed 1.53 +/- 0.36)
- Calculated coupling g_B*B-pi = 23.2 (observed 23.0 +/- 5.0), g_D*D-pi = 11.0 (observed 10.0 +/- 1.3)

## Context

This work extends the DSE-based phenomenological approach of earlier work by the same group (Ivanov, Kalinovsky, and Roberts, 1999) to provide a unified description of both light- and heavy-meson observables, reproducing all acknowledged consequences of heavy-quark symmetry while also predicting a linear rise of heavy-meson mass with current-quark mass. The framework yields the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation in the light-quark limit and provides semi-quantitative constraints on the long-range behavior of the QCD interaction, enabling future studies of deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration transitions.
