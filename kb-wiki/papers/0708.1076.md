# Early evolution of transversally thermalized partons

> The idea that the parton system created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions (i) emerges in a state with transverse momenta close to thermodynamic equilibrium and (ii) its evolution at early times is dominated by the 2-dimensional (transverse) hydrodynamics of the ideal fluid is investigated. It is argued that this mechanism may help to solve the problem of early equilibration.

# Wiki Page: Early evolution of transversally thermalized partons

## Key claim
The authors propose that the parton system created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions emerges with transverse momenta already close to thermodynamic equilibrium, and that its early evolution can be described by 2-dimensional (transverse) ideal hydrodynamics. This mechanism may help solve the problem of early equilibration, but the authors hedge that this description is only adequate during a limited initial period before 3-dimensional hydrodynamics takes over.

## Method
The authors construct a parton distribution function as a superposition of clusters whose longitudinal motion satisfies the Bjorken in-out condition (spatial rapidity equals particle rapidity), while transverse momenta in each cluster follow a Boltzmann equilibrium distribution in the local rest frame. From this ansatz, they derive the energy-momentum tensor and entropy current, obtaining hydrodynamic equations for the transverse expansion that are solved numerically using the method of Chojnacki (2006) and Baym (1983). The initial energy density profile is determined from the density of wounded nucleons via Glauber formulae, and parton spectra are evaluated using the Cooper-Frye prescription at a surface of constant temperature.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperature at the center of the system: T_i = 250 MeV, obtained by fitting the slope of the transverse-momentum spectrum to PHENIX pion data at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV for centrality 20-40%.
- Final temperature (transition from 2D to 3D regime): T_f = 180 MeV, determined by fitting the elliptic flow parameter v_2 to PHENIX data.
- Lifetime of the 2D evolution: approximately 4 fm for the 20-40% centrality class.
- Impact parameter for centrality 20-40%: b approximately 7.9 fm.
- Number of internal degrees of freedom: nu_g = 16 for gluons.

## Context
This work extends the idea of early transverse equilibration, previously explored by Heinz and Wong (2004) with different conclusions; Heinz and Wong found that transverse thermalization could not satisfactorily describe elliptic flow and argued for full 3D equilibrium from the start. The present authors attribute the discrepancy to three factors: differences in the density distribution ansatz (their approach yields truly 2D thermodynamics of an ideal fluid versus an effective viscous description in Heinz and Wong), the absence of an extra timescale parameter tau_0 in their formulation, and different choices of reference data for fitting. The paper enables future work on the nature of the 2D-to-3D transition, the internal longitudinal structure of clusters, and more realistic treatments of the early stage of heavy-ion collisions.
