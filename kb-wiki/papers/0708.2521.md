# Trigonometric quark confinement potential of QCD traits

> We make the case that the Coulomb- plus linear quark confinement potential predicted by lattice QCD is an approximation to the exactly solvable trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential that has the property to interpolate between the Coulomb- and the infinite wells. We test the predictive power of this potential in the description of the nucleon (considered as a quark-diquark system) and provide analytic expressions for its mass spectrum and the proton electric form factor. We compare the results obtained in this fashion to data and find quite good agreement. We obtain an effective gluon propagator in closed form as the Fourier transform of the potential under investigation.

## Key claim

The Coulomb-plus-linear quark confinement potential from lattice QCD is an approximation to the exactly solvable trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential, which interpolates between the Coulomb well and the infinite well; the ratio of linear to Coulomb strengths from the Taylor expansion of this potential (1:3) falls within the range of approximately 1/4 to 1/2 reported by lattice QCD analysis.

## Method

The authors propose the trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential as a quark confinement potential for three-quark systems, expressed in terms of cotangent and cosecant squared functions of scaled interquark distances. They solve the three-dimensional Schrodinger equation exactly for this potential in the quark-diquark approximation to nucleon structure, obtaining analytic wave functions expressed in terms of Romanovski polynomials. The proton electric form factor is then calculated analytically from the ground state wave function, and an effective gluon propagator is derived as the Fourier transform of the ground state matrix element of the potential.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of linear to Coulomb strengths from the Taylor expansion of the trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential is 1:3, which fits within the lattice QCD range of approximately 1/4 to 1/2 reported by Takh (2004).
- Fitted potential parameters for the nucleon spectrum: b = 5.85, d = 2.31 fm, reduced mass mu = 1.06 fm^-1.
- All observed nucleon resonances with masses below 2.5 GeV fall into K = 1, 3, 5 multiplets, with K = 2, 4 levels attributed to entirely "missing" resonances.
- The proton electric form factor is given analytically as a closed-form expression involving an arctangent function of the momentum transfer.

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD results of Takh (2004) on Coulomb-plus-linear confinement potentials by providing an exactly solvable trigonometric alternative with dynamical O(4) symmetry. The trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential was previously identified as a shape-invariant potential in supersymmetric quantum mechanics by De and Dutt (1990) and first constructed mathematically by Levai (1989). The exact single-particle basis enables analytic calculation of the proton electric form factor, which follows experimental data quite well, and provides a starting point for more detailed spectroscopic studies including spin dynamics and screening effects.
