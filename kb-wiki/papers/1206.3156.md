# Isospin breaking in octet baryon mass splittings

> Using an SU(3) flavour symmetry breaking expansion in the quark mass, we determine the QCD component of the nucleon, Sigma and Xi mass splittings of the baryon octet due to up-down (and strange) quark mass differences in terms of the kaon mass splitting. Provided the average quark mass is kept constant, the expansion coefficients in our procedure can be determined from computationally cheaper simulations with mass degenerate sea quarks and partially quenched valence quarks. Both the linear and quadratic terms in the SU(3) flavour symmetry breaking expansion are considered; it is found that the quadratic terms only change the result by a few percent, indicating that the expansion is highly convergent.

## Key claim

Using an SU(3) flavour symmetry breaking expansion in the quark mass, the QCD component of the nucleon, Sigma and Xi mass splittings can be determined from computationally cheaper simulations with mass-degenerate sea quarks and partially quenched valence quarks, provided the average quark mass is kept constant. The quadratic terms in the expansion change the result by only a few percent, indicating the expansion is highly convergent, though the authors note that a much larger quark mass range than that from the symmetric point to the physical point is needed to reliably determine the curvature.

## Method

The authors perform lattice QCD simulations with N_f = 2+1 O(a) improved clover fermions at beta = 5.50 on 32^3 x 64 lattices, keeping the average quark mass constant along a path from the SU(3) flavour symmetric point (where all three quarks are mass-degenerate) down to the physical point. To determine the quadratic expansion coefficients more accurately, they extend the numerical results using partially quenched valence quark masses spanning from about one third of the strange quark mass up to the charmed quark mass, while the sea quarks remain at the SU(3) symmetric point. The expansion coefficients for the baryon octet are determined from fits to these data, and the physical quark mass point is found by applying the same SU(3) flavour expansion to the pseudoscalar meson octet using experimental K^0, K^+ and pi^+ masses.

## Key numbers

- Experimental mass splittings: (M_n - M_p)^exp = 1.293333(33) MeV; (M_Sigma- - M_Sigma+)^exp = 8.079(76) MeV; (M_Xi- - M_Xi0)^exp = 6.85(21) MeV
- The SU(3) flavour symmetric point has a pion mass of approximately 411 MeV, with a lattice spacing a ~ 0.079 fm
- The flavour-blind quantity X_N^exp = 1.1610 GeV at the physical point, defined as the average of the six outer-ring octet baryon masses squared
- The initial SU(3) flavour symmetric kappa_0 value used is 0.12090

## Context

This work extends the SU(3) flavour symmetry breaking expansion method developed by Bietenholz (2010, 2011) for extrapolating from the flavour symmetric point to the physical point while keeping the average quark mass constant. It follows the older Gell-Mann and Okubo (1962) approach of studying SU(3) breaking by quark mass differences, as opposed to chiral perturbation theory which expands about the massless point. The results enable lattice determination of the QCD component of isospin splittings using only 2+1 flavour simulations, and the authors note that the Coleman-Glashow relation (Coleman, 1961) should hold at the order considered. Previous lattice determinations of the QCD contribution to these mass splittings include Beane (2006), Blum (2010), and de Divitiis (2011).
