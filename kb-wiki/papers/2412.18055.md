# Low-Energy Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering and New Physics

> The interactions of low-energy neutrinos with nuclei provide a unique window to explore various Standard Model (SM) and Beyond the Standard Model (BSM) processes. In particular, the recent observation of coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CEvNS), predicted over five decades ago, has generated significant interest across disciplines. With its high cross section and suitability for compact detectors, particularly with stopped pion neutrinos, CEvNS offers a powerful probe for light, weakly coupled new physics. Ongoing global experimental efforts now aim to leverage CEvNS to test SM predictions and search for BSM signals, where deviations in event rates or spectra could reveal new physics. We present here an estimate of the number of recoil events obtained from CEvNS using the current and upcoming liquid argon based experiments. Furthermore, the event rate due to the inclusion of neutrino magnetic moment is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper estimates that deviations in CEvNS event rates or spectra from Standard Model predictions could reveal new physics, but the actual analysis only presents expected event rates for current and proposed liquid argon detectors, with the hedge that any observed deviation "could just be coming from the defined parameters like weak mixing angle, neutron radius or from some new source."

## Method

The authors compute CEvNS differential cross sections using the tree-level weak neutral current formalism with a Helm form factor parameterization for argon nuclei, then integrate over the known decay-at-rest neutrino flux from stopped-pion sources (29.8 MeV monoenergetic νμ, plus νe and ν̅μ up to mμ/2). They calculate expected nuclear recoil event rates per day for four liquid argon detector configurations (CENNS10, CENNS750, CCM, and PIP2-BD at F2D2) using Eq. ## Key numbers

- The CEvNS cross section is proportional to the square of the weak nuclear charge Q_W^2 = [(1-4sin^2θ_W)Z - N]^2, with sin^2θ_W = 0.23857 used at low momentum transfers.
- The prompt νμ flux has a monoenergetic energy of 29.8 MeV, while νe and ν̅μ energies extend up to mμ/2 (approximately 52.8 MeV).
- The Helm form factor parameters used: effective nuclear radius R_0^2 = (1.23 A^{1/3} - 0.6)^2 + (7/3)π^2 r_0^2 - 5s^2 with r_0 = 0.52 fm and folding width s = 0.9 fm.
- The neutrino magnetic moment in extended electroweak theory is μ_ν = 3.2 × 10^{-19} (m_ν/eV) μ_B, while direct detection experiments and COHERENT have placed bounds of order 10^{-10} μ_B.
- The kink in the recoil spectrum at roughly 50 keV is due to the endpoint of recoils caused by the prompt νμ flux.

## Context

This work extends the CEvNS program initiated by the COHERENT experiment (Akimov 2017), which first observed CEvNS using a CsI[Na] scintillator at the Spallation Neutron Source, decades after the prediction by Freedman (1973). The paper provides event rate estimates for liquid argon detectors at three stopped-pion sources: ORNL (CENNS10 and proposed CENNS750), LANL (CCM), and Fermilab (proposed PIP2-BD at F2D2). The analysis enables future searches for neutrino magnetic moments, non-standard interactions, and new light mediators, with the low-energy enhancement from magnetic moments providing a potential method to constrain μ_ν beyond current bounds from direct dark matter experiments (Aprile 2022) and COHERENT (Coloma 2022).
