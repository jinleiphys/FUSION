# Vertex corrections and wavefunction renormalization for atoms, nuclei, and other heavy composite particles

> We study QED corrections to operator matrix elements involving heavy composite particles (e.g., heavy-mesons, nuclei, and atoms). We define a new notion of reducible and irreducible graphs which is useful for systems with many discrete excited states. The equivalence of the LSZ reduction formula and old fashioned perturbation theory is explicitly demonstrated. The self energy and vertex corrections are defined (to all orders), and the one-loop corrections are reduced to operator matrix elements which may be evaluated by hadronic, nuclear, or atomic theorists. The gauge dependence of the various pieces are studied in detail at one loop, and cancellation of spurious contributions are demonstrated in a class of covariant gauges; Coulomb gauge is also discussed. The formalism is applied to superallowed beta decay where the one-loop structure is connected to existing literature based on current algebra techniques. We further identify the well known $O(Z^2α^2)$ isospin breaking correction from the intranuclear Coulomb field as arising from two-loop diagrams. We comment on future applications of our results to the radiative corrections necessary in extractions of $|V_{ud}|$, in particular for corrections that required beyond one-loop order.

## Key claim

The paper develops a formalism for QED corrections to operator matrix elements involving heavy composite particles (atoms, nuclei, heavy mesons) that avoids the standard one-particle irreducible (1PI) scheme, which becomes intractable for systems with many discrete excited states; instead, the authors define "external particle irreducible" (EPI) graphs and demonstrate that the resulting perturbation theory is equivalent to Brillouin-Wigner time-independent perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors construct an LSZ reduction formula for bound states of electrically charged constituents using interpolating fields that satisfy perfect overlap with a single bound state and orthogonality to all others. They define EPI graphs using projectors that isolate a specific external particle state, derive Feynman rules for hadronic propagators with these projectors, and compute the one-loop self-energy and vertex corrections in terms of nuclear matrix elements of current and seagull operators. The gauge dependence of these corrections is studied in covariant (Rξ) gauges, and the cancellation of spurious gauge-dependent contributions is demonstrated explicitly at one loop.

## Key numbers

* The wavefunction renormalization at one loop is expressed as Z_A = [1/(1 - Σ₂')] evaluated at p₀ = E_A, where Σ₂' is the derivative of the self-energy with respect to p₀.
* The ξ-dependent part of the one-loop wavefunction renormalization is given by (1/2)Z_{A,ξ}^{(1)} = (-i e²/2)(1-ξ) ∫ dω/(2π) d³q/(2π)³ (1/q⁴) ⟨A|ρ(-q)ρ(q)|A⟩.
* The ξ-dependent part of diagram-(a) for the vertex correction is A_{a,ξ}^{(1)} = i e²(1-ξ) ∫ dω/(2π) d³q/(2π)³ (1/q⁴) ⟨B|ρ(-q) O(k) ρ(q)|A⟩.
* The double commutator of the charge density with a charged current operator yields [ρ(-q),[ρ(q), O(k)]] = Q_O² O(k), where Q_O is the electric charge of the operator.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Sirlin (1977) and Preparata (1968) for radiative corrections in beta decay, providing a systematic diagrammatic approach that connects to existing current algebra techniques. The authors identify the well-known O(Z²α²) isospin breaking correction from the intranuclear Coulomb field as arising from two-loop diagrams, and their results enable future calculations of radiative corrections beyond one-loop order needed for precision extractions of |V_ud| from superallowed beta decay. The formalism also connects directly to the work of Miller (2008, 2009) on isospin breaking in nuclear beta decays where only the Coulomb interaction was retained.
