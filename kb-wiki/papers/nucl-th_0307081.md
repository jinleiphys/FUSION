# Pygmy Dipol Resonances as a Manifestation of the Structure of the Neutron-Rich Nuclei

> Dipole excitations in neutron-rich nuclei below the neutron threshold are investigated. The method is based on Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) and Quasiparticle-Phonon Model (QPM) theory. Of our special interest are the properties of the low-lying 1- Pygmy Resonance and the two-phonon quadrupole-octupole 1- states in Sn-isotopes including exploratory investigations for the experimentally unknown mass regions. In particular we investigate the evolution of the dipole strength function with the neutron excess. The use of HFB mean-field potentials and s.p. energies is found to provide a reliable extrapolation into the region off stability.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR) in neutron-rich tin isotopes is a distinct mode of predominantly isoscalar character, not simply the low-energy tail of the isovector Giant Dipole Resonance, and that its properties (total strength and centroid energy) correlate with neutron excess and neutron skin thickness, but this conclusion is hedged by the use of a semi-microscopic approach where the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mean-field is re-parameterized into a Woods-Saxon potential with empirical adjustments.

## Method

The authors combined Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory to determine ground state properties (single-particle energies, binding energies, charge radii, and neutron skin thickness) with the Quasiparticle-Phonon Model (QPM) to describe excited states. The HFB mean-field was re-parameterized into a Woods-Saxon potential fitted to reproduce binding energies from Audi and Wapstra (1995) and neutron skin data from Krasznahorkay et al. The QPM diagonalized a model Hamiltonian including one-, two-, and three-phonon configurations for 1- states in the neutron-rich tin isotopes 120Sn through 132Sn, using separable multipole and spin-multipole residual interactions.

## Key numbers

- The PDR in the QRPA calculations is located in the energy region 5.9 to 7 MeV for 120-126Sn and up to 7.5 MeV for 128-132Sn.
- The first three 1- QRPA states are separated from higher-lying states by an energy gap of more than 1.3 MeV.
- The first QRPA state contains approximately 99.7% neutron two-quasiparticle component, with proton contributions of only about 0.2%.
- The lowest two-phonon quadrupole-octupole 1- state energy varies from 3.203 MeV in 120Sn to 4.115 MeV in 128Sn (QPM results).
- The two-phonon 1- state structure is predominantly (88 to 93%) of quadrupole-octupole character in 120-130Sn.
- In 122Sn and 130Sn, about 85% of the PDR one-phonon strength is exhausted in the energy intervals up to 8 MeV and 7.5 MeV, respectively.
- The B(E1) transition probability for the 1-1 state to ground state in 124Sn is 6.27 x 10-3 e2fm2 (QPM), compared to experimental 6.08(66) x 10-3 e2fm2.

## Context

This work extends previous QPM studies of the PDR in stable nuclei such as 116,124Sn (Pon1, 2001) and 208Pb (Pb-pdr) to neutron-rich tin isotopes up to the N=82 shell closure, including experimentally unknown mass regions. The method enables a unified description of single- and multiple-phonon states, distinguishing the PDR from two-phonon quadrupole-octupole 1- states, and predicts a correlation between PDR strength and neutron skin thickness that can be tested at future facilities like REX-ISOLDE, MAFF, and the GSI project.
