# Rotational excitations in rare-earth nuclei: a comparative study within three cranking models with different mean fields and the treatments of pairing correlations

> High-spin rotational bands in rare-earth Er ($Z=68$), Tm ($Z=69$) and Yb ($Z=70$) isotopes are investigated by three different nuclear models. These are (i) the cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (CRHB) approach with approximate particle number projection by means of the Lipkin-Nogami (LN) method, (ii) the cranking covariant density functional theory (CDFT) with pairing correlations treated by a shell-model-like approach (SLAP) or the so called particle-number conserving (PNC) method, and (iii) cranked shell model (CSM) based on the Nilsson potential with pairing correlations treated by the PNC method. A detailed comparison between these three models in the description of the ground state rotational bands of even-even Er and Yb isotopes is performed. The similarities and differences between these models in the description of the moments of inertia, the features of band crossings, equilibrium deformations and pairing energies of even-even nuclei under study are discussed. These quantities are considered as a function of rotational frequency and proton and neutron numbers. The changes in the properties of the first band crossings with increasing neutron number in this mass region are investigated. On average, a comparable accuracy of the description of available experimental data is achieved in these models. However, the differences between model predictions become larger above the first band crossings. Because of time-consuming nature of numerical calculations in the CDFT-based models, a systematic study of the rotational properties of both ground state and excited state bands in odd-mass Tm nuclei is carried out only by the PNC-SCM. With few exceptions, the rotational properties of experimental 1-quasiparticle and 3-quasiparticle bands in $^{165,167,169,171}$Tm are reproduced reasonably well.

## Key claim

On average, the three cranking models achieve comparable accuracy in describing experimental data for ground-state rotational bands in even-even Er and Yb isotopes, but the differences between model predictions become larger above the first band crossings; for odd-mass Tm nuclei, only the PNC-CSM (particle-number conserving cranked shell model) is used systematically due to the time-consuming nature of CDFT-based calculations.

## Method

The paper compares three cranking models applied to rare-earth nuclei: (i) the cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov approach with Lipkin-Nogami approximate particle number projection (CRHB+LN), (ii) cranking covariant density functional theory with pairing treated by the shell-model-like approach (cranking CDFT-SLAP), and (iii) the cranked shell model based on the Nilsson potential with pairing treated by the particle-number conserving method (PNC-CSM). For even-even Er and Yb isotopes, all three models are used to compute moments of inertia, band crossing features, equilibrium deformations, and pairing energies as functions of rotational frequency. For odd-mass Tm isotopes (165,167,169,171), only the PNC-CSM is employed to study 1-quasiparticle and 3-quasiparticle bands.

## Key numbers

- The first band crossing in even-even Er and Yb isotopes is studied as a function of neutron number across the N=96-102 range.
- Experimental 1-quasiparticle and 3-quasiparticle bands in 165Tm, 167Tm, 169Tm, and 171Tm are reproduced reasonably well by PNC-CSM, with few exceptions.
- A sharp backbending in the ground state band is observed experimentally in 169Tm (Asgar 2017) and 165Tm (Jensen 2001), while 167Tm shows only a smooth upbending (Burns 2005).
- The MPC space dimension of 1000 for both protons and neutrons is sufficient for rare-earth nuclei in the SLAP diagonalization.
- The scaling factor f for the Gogny pairing force (parameter set D1S) is used for fine tuning of pairing properties to the mass region under study.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of rotational bands in rare-earth nuclei using cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (Andersson 1976), cranked shell model with Nilsson (Bengtsson 1979) and Woods-Saxon (Nazarewicz 1985) potentials, projected shell model (Hara 1995), tilted axis cranking (Frauendorf 2001), and cranked relativistic and non-relativistic density functional theories (Afanasjev 1996, 2000; Terasaki 1995; Egido 1993). The comparative analysis evaluates the weak and strong points of the three approaches, tests whether the Lipkin-Nogami method is a good approximation to the particle-number conserving method, and assesses typical accuracy of experimental data description. The results enable systematic studies of rotational properties across the rare-earth region and provide benchmarks for future CDFT-based calculations of odd-mass and multi-quasiparticle bands.
