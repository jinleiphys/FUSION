# $πN σ$ Term , $\bar{s}s$ in Nucleon, and Scalar Form Factor --- a Lattice Study

> We report on a lattice QCD calculation of the $πN σ$ term, the scalar form factor, and $\langle N|\bar{s}s|N\rangle$. The disconnected insertion part of $\sg$ is found to be $1.8 \pm 0.1$ times larger than the connected insertion contribution. The $q^2$ dependence of $\sg(q^2)$ is about the same as $G_E(q^2)$ of the proton so that $\sg(2m_π^2) - \sg(0) = 6.6 \pm 0.6$ MeV. The ratio $y= ß/\ud = 0.36 \pm 0.03$. Both results favor a $\sg \sim 53$ MeV, slightly larger than our direct calculation of $\sg = 49.7 \pm 2.6$ MeV. We also compute $F_s$ and $D_s$ and find that the agreement with those from the octect baryon mass splittings crucially depends on the inclusion of the large disconnected insertion. Finally, we give our result for the $K N σ$ term.

# πN σ Term, s̄s in Nucleon, and Scalar Form Factor, a Lattice Study

## Key claim

The lattice QCD calculation finds that the scalar form factor is not as soft as previously claimed in chiral perturbation theory (Gasser, Leutwyler, Sainio 1991), yielding Δσ = 6.6 ± 0.6 MeV rather than 15.2 ± 0.4 MeV; combined with a larger strangeness content ratio y = 0.36 ± 0.03, this favors σπN ∼ 53 MeV, slightly above the direct lattice result of 49.7 ± 2.6 MeV.

## Method

The connected insertion (C.I.) and disconnected insertion (D.I.) contributions to the scalar matrix element ⟨N|ūu + d̄d|N⟩ were computed separately on a quenched 16³ × 24 lattice at β = 6.0 using Wilson fermions at three quark masses (κ = 0.148, 0.152, 0.154). The C.I. was evaluated from three-point functions as in prior axial form factor work (Liu, Dong, Draper 1994), while the D.I. was obtained stochastically with Z₂ noise (Dong, Liu 1994) by extracting the slope of a summed ratio over time slices. Chiral extrapolations used a linear-plus-m¹/² form to account for quenched chiral non-analytic behavior.

## Key numbers

- Disconnected insertion part of σπN is 1.8 ± 0.1 times larger than the connected insertion contribution.
- Δσ = σπN(2mπ²) − σπN(0) = 6.6 ± 0.6 MeV.
- Ratio y = ⟨N|s̄s|N⟩ / ⟨N|ūu + d̄d|N⟩ = 0.36 ± 0.03.
- Direct lattice result for σπN = 49.7 ± 2.6 MeV.
- Connected insertion scalar matrix element gS,con = 3.02 ± 0.09 (continuum MS scheme).
- Disconnected insertion scalar matrix element ⟨N|ūu + d̄d|N⟩_dis = 5.41 ± 0.15.
- Strange scalar content ⟨N|s̄s|N⟩ = 1.53 ± 0.07.
- Dipole mass from scalar form factor fit: mD = 0.80 ± 0.04 GeV.
- Root-mean-square radius of the s̄s distribution: ⟨r²⟩_S^{1/2}(s) = 1.06 ± 0.09 fm.
- KN σ term σKN = 362 ± 13 MeV.
- Fs = 1.51 ± 0.12, Ds = −0.88 ± 0.28.

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice attempts to compute σπN via the Feynman-Hellmann theorem (Guagnelli et al. 1988; Gupta et al. 1991), which in the quenched approximation gave only 15–25 MeV because the D.I. was omitted. It directly confronts the chiral perturbation theory resolution of the Cheng (1976) puzzle proposed by Gasser, Leutwyler, and Sainio (1991), which required a very soft scalar form factor (Δσ = 15.2 MeV) and y ∼ 0.2–0.3. The results here indicate that the C.I. contribution, dominated by a harder scalar meson exchange, was missing from that χPT picture. The computed Fs and Ds agree with values from octet baryon mass splittings (Gasiorowicz 1981; Martin 1987) only when the large D.I. is included, demonstrating the importance of sea-quark contributions. Future work requires larger lattices, smaller quark masses, and dynamical fermions to resolve the remaining tension between the direct σπN and the value inferred from Δσ and y.
