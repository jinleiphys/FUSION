# Parity violation in neutron capture on the proton: determining the weak pion-nucleon coupling

> We investigate the parity-violating analyzing power in neutron capture on the proton at thermal energies in the framework of chiral effective field theory. By combining this analysis with a previous analysis of parity violation in proton-proton scattering, we are able to extract the size of the weak pion-nucleon coupling constant. The uncertainty is significant and dominated by the experimental error which is expected to be reduced soon.

## Key claim

By combining chiral effective field theory analyses of parity violation in proton-proton scattering and in radiative neutron capture on the proton, the authors extract the weak pion-nucleon coupling constant \(h_\pi\) with significant uncertainty dominated by experimental error; the extracted value is marginally consistent with bounds from \({}^{18}\)F experiments and lattice QCD, but the analysis suggests that very small values of \(h_\pi \sim 10^{-7}\) are barely consistent with the few-body data.

## Method

The authors calculated the parity-violating analyzing power \(a_\gamma\) in thermal neutron-proton capture \(\vec n p \to d \gamma\) using chiral effective field theory at leading order, including the one-pion-exchange parity-violating potential and the leading parity-violating two-body electromagnetic current. They combined this with a previous chiral EFT analysis of the longitudinal analyzing power in \(\vec p p\) scattering at three energies (13.6, 45, and 221 MeV), which depends on \(h_\pi\) through two-pion-exchange diagrams and on a short-range parity-violating low-energy constant \(C\). The strong interaction was described by the next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N\({}^{3}\)LO) chiral potential with cut-off regularization at \(\Lambda = \{450, 550, 600\}\) MeV.

## Key numbers

* The weak pion-nucleon coupling constant extracted from combined fits is \(h_\pi = (1.1 \pm 1.0) \times 10^{-6}\), with the contact-term LEC \(C = (-6.5 \pm 4.5) \times 10^{-6}\).
* The preliminary experimental value for the analyzing power in \(\vec n p \to d \gamma\) is \(a_\gamma = (-7.14 \pm 4.4) \times 10^{-8}\) (Crawford, preliminary).
* The leading-order contribution to \(a_\gamma\) as a function of \(h_\pi\) is \(a_\gamma = (-0.11 \pm 0.05)\, h_\pi\), with a subleading correction from short-range terms estimated as \((-0.5 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-8}\).
* The total unpolarized cross section for \(np\) capture calculated with the N\({}^{3}\)LO potential plus leading one-pion-exchange currents is \(319 \pm 5\) mb, compared to the experimental value \(334.2 \pm 0.5\) mb.
* The upper bound on \(h_\pi\) from the most conservative estimate using earlier data is \(|h_\pi| \leq 4.5 \times 10^{-6}\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT analysis of parity-violating nuclear forces previously applied to \(\vec p p\) scattering by de Vries et al. (2013, 2014) to include the \(\vec n p \to d \gamma\) capture process, enabling a combined extraction of the weak pion-nucleon coupling. The results are compared to the DDH meson-exchange model of Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1980), to the bound from \({}^{18}\)F gamma-ray emission by Adelberger et al. (1983) giving \(h_\pi \leq 1.3 \times 10^{-7}\), and to lattice QCD calculations yielding \(h_\pi \simeq 10^{-7}\). The expected improvement of the \(a_\gamma\) measurement to \(10^{-8}\) precision will allow a definitive test of whether small values of \(h_\pi\) are consistent with few-body experiments.
