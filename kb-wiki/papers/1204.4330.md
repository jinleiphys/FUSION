# An effective gluon potential and hybrid approach to Yang-Mills thermodynamics

> We derive the partition function for the SU(3) Yang-Mills theory in the presence of a uniform gluon field within the background field method. We show, that the $n$-body gluon contributions in the partition function are characterized solely by the Polyakov loop. We express the effective action through characters of different representations of the color gauge group resulting in a form deduced in the strong-coupling expansion. A striking feature of this potential is that at low temperature gluons are physically disfavored and therefore they do not yield the correct thermodynamics. We suggest a hybrid approach to Yang-Mills thermodynamics, combining the effective gluon potential with glueballs implemented as dilaton fields.

## Key claim

The authors derive an effective gluon thermodynamic potential for SU(3) Yang-Mills theory that is expressed solely through the Polyakov loop, but they find that this potential produces unphysical negative entropy and energy densities at low temperatures where the Polyakov loop vanishes, contradicting the correct thermodynamics expected from lattice calculations.

## Method

The authors use the background field method to compute the one-loop partition function for SU(3) Yang-Mills theory in a constant uniform gluon background field, diagonalizing the inverse propagator and performing the Matsubara summation to express the thermodynamic potential in terms of the Polyakov loop in the adjoint representation. They then rewrite the potential using characters of the fundamental representation, obtaining coefficients for n-body gluon contributions that match the form of the strong-coupling expansion on the lattice. To resolve the low-temperature unphysical behavior, they construct a hybrid model that switches from the gluon potential above the critical temperature to a glueball dilaton field description below it.

## Key numbers

* The critical temperature for the first-order deconfinement transition is set to T_c = 270 MeV, matching SU(3) lattice calculations.
* For massless gluons (M_g = 0), the Polyakov loop expectation value at T_c is ⟨Φ⟩_{T_c} = 0.395, with model parameters a_0 = (0.197 GeV)^3 and c_0 = -(0.180 GeV)^4.
* For massive gluons with M_g = 850 MeV (half the glueball mass), the Polyakov loop at T_c is ⟨Φ⟩_{T_c} = 0.439, with a_0 = (0.125 GeV)^3 and c_0 = -(0.130 GeV)^4.
* The glueball dilaton model uses a vacuum glueball mass M_χ = 1.7 GeV and vacuum energy density B/4 = 0.6 GeV fm^{-3}, yielding B = (0.368 GeV)^4 and χ_0 = 0.16 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous effective Polyakov-loop models (Pisarski, 2000; Meisinger et al., 2002) and quasi-particle approaches (Peshier et al., 1994) by deriving the gluon potential directly from the background field method rather than using truncated expansions. The authors show that their potential corresponds to the strong-coupling expansion used in lattice effective actions (SlQCD, 2004) and recovers the form used in PNJL models (Fukushima, 2004; Ratti et al., 2006). The hybrid approach combining gluons with glueballs as dilaton fields (Schechter, 1980) enables a consistent description of both the deconfined and confined phases, addressing the unphysical low-temperature behavior that plagues pure Polyakov-loop models.
