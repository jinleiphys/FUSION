# Dispersion (asymptotic) theory of charged particle transfer reactions at low energies and nuclear astrophysics: II. the asymptotic normalization coefficients and their nuclear-astrophysical application

> Within the asymptotic theory proposed by authors R. Yarmukhamedov and K.I. Tursunmakhatov [Phys. Rev. C (submitted 2019)] for the peripheral sub- and above-barrier transfer $A$($x$, $y$)$B$ reaction in the three-body ($A$, $a$ and $y$) model ($ x$= $y$ + $a$ and $B$= $A$ + $a$, and $ a$ is a transferred particle), the analysis of the experimental angular distributions of the differential cross sections is performed for the peripheral proton and triton transfer ${\rm{^9Be(^{10}B,\,^9Be)^{10}B}}$, ${\rm{^{16}O(^3He}},\,d{\rm{)^{17}F}}$ and ${\rm{^{19}F(}}p,\,α{\rm{)^{16}O}}$ reactions at above-and sub-barrier projectile energies, respectively. New estimates and their uncertainties are obtained for magnitudes of the asymptotic normalization coefficients (respective the nuclear vertex constants) for ${\rm{^9Be}} \,+p \to\,{\rm{^{10}B}}$, ${\rm{^{16}O}} \,+\,p \to\, {\rm{^{17}F}}$ and ${\rm{^{16}O}} \,+\,t\to{\rm{^{19}F}}$. They are applied for calculations of the astrophysical $S$ factors for the nuclear-astrophysical ${\rm{^9Be(}}p,\,γ{\rm{)^{10}B}} $, ${\rm{^{16}O(}}p,\,γ{\rm{)^{17}F}}$ and ${\rm{^{19}F(}}p,\,α{\rm{)^{16}O)}}$ reactions at thermonuclear energies. New values and their uncertainties are obtained for the astrophysical $S$ factors at stellar energies.

# Dispersion (asymptotic) theory of charged particle transfer reactions at low energies and nuclear astrophysics: II. the asymptotic normalization coefficients and their nuclear-astrophysical application

## Key claim

The asymptotic theory for peripheral sub- and above-barrier transfer reactions provides more reliable asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) for nuclear-astrophysical applications than previous modified distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) analyses, because the three-body Coulomb dynamics in the transfer mechanism are correctly accounted for, which the earlier "post" form of the modified DWBA did not guarantee with sufficient accuracy for astrophysical use.

## Method

The authors analyzed experimental angular distributions of differential cross sections for three peripheral transfer reactions: the proton exchange reaction 9Be(10B,9Be)10B at 100 MeV incident energy, the 16O(3He,d)17F reaction at 29.75 MeV, and the 19F(p,α)16O reaction at six sub-barrier proton energies (250, 327, 350, 387, 450, and 486 keV). The analysis was performed within the asymptotic theory developed by Yarmukhamedov and Tursunmakhatov (2019) using the three-body (A, a, y) model, where the influence of three-body Coulomb dynamics on the peripheral partial amplitudes at large orbital angular momenta is correctly taken into account. The ANCs were extracted by normalizing calculated cross sections to experimental data in the angular region of the main peak, using known ANCs for the virtual decays d+p→3He (C²=4.20±0.32 fm⁻¹) and t+p→α (C²=54.2±4.5 fm⁻¹).

## Key numbers

- Weighted mean ANC for 9Be + p → 10B (ground state, Jπ=3+): C² = 4.35 ± 0.28 fm⁻¹, which is 2.5σ lower than the value from Mukhamedzhanov et al. (2002)
- Weighted mean ANCs for 9Be + p → 10B (first excited state, E*=0.718 MeV): C² = 1.39 ± 0.09 fm⁻¹ (j=1/2) and 3.74 ± 0.32 fm⁻¹ (j=3/2)
- Weighted mean ANC for 9Be + p → 10B (second excited state, E*=1.740 MeV): C² = 3.58 ± 0.34 fm⁻¹ (j=3/2)
- Weighted mean ANCs for 9Be + p → 10B (third excited state, E*=2.154 MeV): C² = 0.25 ± 0.06 fm⁻¹ (j=1/2) and 0.72 ± 0.19 fm⁻¹ (j=3/2)
- Total astrophysical S factor for 9Be(p,γ)10B at zero energy: S(0) = 0.946 ± 0.194 keV·b
- Direct component of astrophysical S factor for 9Be(p,γ)10B at zero energy: S_DC(0) = 0.331 ± 0.021 keV·b, which is 2.5σ lower than the value 0.38 ± 0.02 keV·b from Sattarov et al. (1999) and Wulf et al. (1998)
- Total astrophysical S factor for 9Be(p,γ)10B at 25 keV: S(25 keV) = 0.970 ± 0.200 keV·b
- Direct component of astrophysical S factor for 9Be(p,γ)10B at 25 keV: S_DC(25 keV) = 0.327 ± 0.021 keV·b
- Weighted mean ANC values for 16O + p → 17F (ground and first excited states) are in good agreement within about 1σ with those of Artemov et al. (2009) and Yarmukhamedov and Baye (2011)
- ANC values for 16O + t → 19F obtained from EXP-1978 and EXP-2015 data sets differ by a factor of about 2.2 on average
- Gamma-width for the 0+ resonance in 9Be(p,γ)10B: Γγ = 6.5 eV, differing by about 31% from the value 8.5 eV recommended in Ajzenberg-Selove (1988)

## Context

This work extends the asymptotic theory developed by Yarmukhamedov and Tursunmakhatov (2019) for peripheral transfer reactions and applies it to three nuclear-astrophysically important reactions: 9Be(p,γ)10B, 16O(p,γ)17F, and 19F(p,α)16O. The results correct overestimates in previous ANC determinations from Mukhamedzhanov et al. (2002) and Mukhamedzhanov et al. (1999) that used the "post" form of the modified DWBA, which did not adequately account for three-body Coulomb effects. The new ANCs enable more reliable calculations of astrophysical S factors at stellar energies, particularly resolving discrepancies in the 9Be(p,γ)10B reaction where previous direct capture S factors varied by factors up to 4.2 between different analyses. The work also provides the first determination of the ANC for 16O + t → 19F, though the authors recommend decisive new measurements of the 19F(p,α)16O differential cross sections to resolve the factor-of-2.2 discrepancy between the two existing experimental data sets.
