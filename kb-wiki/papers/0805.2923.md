# K$^+$-nucleon interaction

> The Born approximation to the quark-gluon-exchange mechanism for K$^+$N scattering is used as a starting point to generate a potential for this system. The valence quark wave function of the nucleon is generalized from a single Gaussian to a sum of Gaussians in order to have a more flexible representation than previous work. We obtain a potential derived from a valence density given by lattice calculations. By comparing with a recent amplitude analysis it is found that the strength of the quark-gluon based potential needs to be increased by a factor of order 2-4 relative to the normalization given by more traditional values of the governing parameters. The method is used to estimate the change in effective K$^+$N amplitudes which would result from changes in the valence quark distributions or strength of the interaction which might arise from nuclear medium effects in K$^+$ scattering from nuclei.

## Key claim

The quark-gluon-exchange Born approximation for K⁺N scattering, when iterated via a potential to obtain full phase shifts, requires the strength of the interaction to be increased by a factor of 2–4 relative to traditional parameter values; the exact factor depends on the assumed spatial extent of the valence quark distributions in the nucleon and kaon.

## Method

The authors generalized the Barnes and Swanson (1997) quark-gluon-exchange Born amplitude for K⁺N scattering from a single-Gaussian nucleon wave function to a sum of Gaussians, enabling a fit to lattice-calculated valence quark densities. They treated the Born amplitude as the first term of a potential and solved the resulting non-local Schrödinger equation to obtain s-wave phase shifts, which were compared with the amplitude analysis of Gibbs (2005). The potential strength was scaled by a factor F relative to a standard value of γ₀ = 5.51 (GeV/c²)⁻², and the kaon size parameter β was adjusted to reproduce the experimental s-wave scattering lengths.

## Key numbers

- The reference parameter set (α = 0.40 GeV/c, β = 0.35 GeV/c) gave Born scattering lengths of a₀ = -0.12 fm and a₁ = -0.35 fm, but the potential solution required F = 2.06 and β = 0.50 GeV/c to fit the data.
- For the single-Gaussian fit to the Lissia et al. (1996) lattice data (α = 0.61 GeV/c), the potential needed F = 3.36 and β = 0.93 GeV/c to reproduce the phase shifts.
- For the double-Gaussian fit to the same lattice data (αₐ = 0.58 GeV/c, α_b = 1.32 GeV/c, X = 0.1), the required strength factor was F = 4.1.
- At a laboratory momentum of 0.599 GeV/c, a 15% increase in nucleon rms radius (from 0.566 fm to 0.652 fm) produced an 18% increase in the s-wave total cross section; at 0.941 GeV/c, the same radius change gave a 14% increase.
- At 0.599 GeV/c, increasing the strength factor F from 4.1 to 5.0 (a 22% increase) produced a 10% increase in the s-wave total cross section.

## Context

This work extends the quark-gluon-exchange approach of Barnes and Swanson (1997) by using a more flexible sum-of-Gaussians representation of the nucleon valence quark wave function, fitted to lattice calculations by Lissia et al. (1996) and Alexandrou et al. (1999). The resulting potential enables estimates of how changes in nucleon size or interaction strength, such as those expected from nuclear medium effects in K⁺ scattering from nuclei, would alter the effective K⁺N amplitudes. The authors find that the energy dependence of the cross-section changes with nucleon radius runs opposite to the trend needed to explain the anomalous K⁺-nucleus total cross-section ratios measured by Krauss et al. (1994) and Weiss et al. (1994), suggesting that simple radius changes may not be the full explanation.
