# A new determination of α_s from hadronic τ decays

> We present a new framework for the extraction of the strong coupling from hadronic τdecays through finite-energy sum rules. Our focus is on the small, but still significant non-perturbative effects that, in principle, affect both the central value and the systematic error. We employ a quantitative model in order to accommodate violations of quark-hadron duality, and enforce a consistent treatment of the higher-dimensional contributions of the Operator Product Expansion to our sum rules. Using 1998 OPAL data for the non-strange isovector vector and axial-vector spectral functions, we find the n_f=3 values α_s(m_τ^2)=0.307+-0.019 in fixed-order perturbation theory, and 0.322+-0.026 in contour-improved perturbation theory. For comparison, the original OPAL analysis of the same data led to the values 0.324+-0.014 (fixed-order) and 0.348+-0.021 (contour-improved).

## Key claim

The authors present a new framework for extracting the strong coupling from hadronic tau decays that explicitly models violations of quark-hadron duality, finding that the central values of alpha_s are lower than those from the original OPAL analysis, but the quoted uncertainties are larger because the systematic error from non-perturbative effects is now included rather than assumed negligible.

## Method

The authors employ finite-energy sum rules (FESRs) with a quantitative model for duality-violating (DV) parts of the vector and axial-vector spectral functions, parametrized as an exponentially damped sine function, and enforce a consistent treatment of higher-dimensional Operator Product Expansion (OPE) contributions. Using 1998 OPAL data for the non-strange isovector vector and axial-vector spectral functions, they perform fits that simultaneously determine alpha_s, OPE condensates up to dimension 8, and the four DV parameters in each channel, checking stability with respect to varying the upper integration limit s_0.

## Key numbers

* alpha_s(m_tau^2) = 0.307 +/- 0.019 (n_f=3) in fixed-order perturbation theory (FOPT)
* alpha_s(m_tau^2) = 0.322 +/- 0.026 (n_f=3) in contour-improved perturbation theory (CIPT)
* For comparison, the original OPAL analysis of the same data gave 0.324 +/- 0.014 (FOPT) and 0.348 +/- 0.021 (CIPT)
* The perturbative Adler function coefficients used: c_01 = c_11 = 1, c_21 = 1.63982, c_31 = 6.37101, c_41 = 49.07570 (in the MSbar scheme)
* The next coefficient c_51 is guessed as 283, with an uncertainty of +/- 283 assigned to estimate truncation error

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Maltman and Yavin (2008) who demonstrated self-consistency problems in earlier OPE-based analyses, and builds on the DV model developed by Cata, Golterman, and Peris (2005, 2008). The new framework enables a self-contained FESR analysis that checks stability with respect to varying s_0 without relying on external values for OPE parameters, addressing the long-standing issue that all previous tau-decay determinations of alpha_s assumed quark-hadron duality a priori. The results highlight that the discrepancy between FOPT and CIPT results persists even after properly treating non-perturbative effects, motivating future work to resolve this systematic uncertainty.
