# U(1) Goldberger-Treiman Relation and Its Connection to the Proton Spin

> The U(1) Goldberger-Treiman (GT) relation for the axial charge $g_A^0$ is reexamined. It is stressed that the isosinglet GT relation in terms of the $η_0$ holds irrespective of the quark masses and the axial anomaly. We pointed out that the identification of the $η_0-N$ and $\dk-N$coupling terms with the quark and gluon spin components respectively in a proton is possible but valid only in the chiral-invariant factorization scheme. In general, the two-component U(1) GT relation can be identified in a gauge-invariant way with connected and disconnected insertions. The observation that $(\sqrt{3}f_π/2m_N)\getao$ is related to the connected insertion i.e., the total valence quark contribution to the proton spin enables us to determine the physical coupling constants $\getap$ and $\geta$ from the GT relations for $g_A^0$ and $g_A^8$. We found $\getap=3.4$ and $\geta=4.7\,$.

## Key claim

The U(1) Goldberger-Treiman relation for the axial charge \(g_A^0\) holds irrespective of quark masses and the axial anomaly, but the identification of its two components with quark and gluon spin contributions is valid only in the chiral-invariant factorization scheme; in a gauge-invariant scheme, the two components correspond to connected and disconnected insertions (valence quark and sea quark contributions).

## Method

The author rederives the isosinglet Goldberger-Treiman relation using the \(\eta_0\) pole dominance for the divergence of the flavor-singlet axial current, showing it remains unchanged when quark masses and the anomaly are varied. The two-component expression involving the bare \(\eta_0\)-nucleon coupling and the ghost field coupling is then reinterpreted in terms of connected and disconnected insertions, using lattice QCD results for sea polarization. The physical coupling constants \(g_{\eta'NN}\) and \(g_{\eta NN}\) are extracted by equating the connected insertion to the total valence quark spin \(3F-D\) and solving the Goldberger-Treiman relations for \(g_A^8\) and \(g_A^0\) with \(\eta\)-\(\eta'\) mixing.

## Key numbers

*   \(g_A^0(0) = 0.12 \pm 0.18\) at \(Q^2 = 10.7\ \text{GeV}^2\) from the EMC experiment (pre-1993).
*   \(\eta\)-\(\eta'\) mixing angle \(\theta_3 = -18.5^\circ\).
*   \(g_{\eta'NN} = 3.4\) and \(g_{\eta NN} = 4.7\) extracted from the Goldberger-Treiman relations.
*   \(g_{\eta_0NN} = 4.8\) and \(g_{\eta_8NN} = 3.4\).
*   Sea quark polarization from lattice QCD: \(\Delta u_s = \Delta d_s = \Delta s = -0.12 \pm 0.01\).
*   Combined data yields \(\Delta u = 0.83 \pm 0.02\), \(\Delta d = -0.43 \pm 0.02\), \(\Delta s = -0.09 \pm 0.02\), and \(g_A^0(0) = \Delta\Sigma = 0.31 \pm 0.06\) at \(\langle Q^2 \rangle = 10\ \text{GeV}^2\).
*   Ghost coupling \(\gghost \approx 55\ \text{GeV}^{-3}\).

## Context

This work reexamines the U(1) Goldberger-Treiman relation in light of the small \(g_A^0\) measured by the EMC Collaboration (1990), extending earlier derivations by Schechter et al. (1990) and Bartelski and Tatur (1991). It clarifies the two-component interpretation proposed by Shore and Veneziano (1990), showing that the identification with quark and gluon spin is scheme-dependent, and instead connects the components to connected and disconnected insertions as computed in lattice QCD by Dong et al. (1995) and Fukugita et al. (1995). This enables a determination of the physical \(\eta'\)-nucleon and \(\eta\)-nucleon coupling constants, which are otherwise subject to large phenomenological uncertainties.
