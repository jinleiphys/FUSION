# QCD Tests of the Puzzling Scalar Mesons

> Motivated by several recent data, we test the QCD spectral sum rules (QSSR) predictions based on different proposals (\bar qq, \bar q\bar q qq, and gluonium) for the nature of scalar mesons. In the I=1 and 1/2 channels, the unusual (wrong) splitting between the a_0(980) and κ(900) and the a_0(980) width can be understood from QSSR within a \bar qq assignement. However, none of the \bar qq and \bar q\bar q qq results can explain the large κwidth, which may suggest that it can result from a strong interference with non-resonant backgrounds. In the I=0 channel, QSSR and some low-energy theorems (LET) require the existence of a low mass gluonium σ_B(1 GeV) coupled strongly to Goldstone boson pairs which plays in the U(1)_V channel, a similar role than the η' for the value of the U(1)_A topological charge. The observed σ(600) and f_0(980) mesons result from a maximal mixing between the gluonium σ_B and \bar qq(1 GeV) mesons, a mixing scheme which passes several experimental tests. OZI violating J/ψ--> ϕπ^+π^-, D_s--> 3πdecays and J/ψ--> γS glueball filter processes may indicate that most of the I=0 mesons above 1 GeV have important gluonium in their wave functions. We expect that the f_0(1500), f_0(1710) and f_0(1790) have significant gluonium component in their wave functions, while the f_0(1370) is mostly \bar qq. Tests of these results can be provided by the measurements of the pure gluonium η'ηand 4πspecific U(1)_A decay channels.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed scalar mesons below 1 GeV (σ(600) and f₀(980)) can be explained by a maximal mixing between a low-mass gluonium σ_B (≈1 GeV) and bare \(\bar qq\) mesons, but this mixing scheme is presented as one possible interpretation that passes several experimental tests rather than a definitive conclusion; the author explicitly notes that the large width of the κ(900) cannot be explained by either \(\bar qq\) or four-quark assignments alone.

## Method

The author applies QCD spectral sum rules (QSSR) to two-point correlators of scalar currents and the trace of the energy-momentum tensor, including perturbative QCD series to order α_s³, non-perturbative condensates up to dimension 6, and 1/q² terms mimicking UV renormalon effects. For the I=0 channel, the analysis combines QSSR with low-energy theorems (LET) for the gluonium correlator, using subtracted and unsubtracted sum rules to extract masses and decay constants of two gluonium states (σ_B at ≈1 GeV and G at ≈1.5 GeV). The mixing scheme between gluonium and \(\bar qq\) bare states is then constrained by fitting predicted decay widths to experimental data for γγ, ππ, and KK channels.

## Key numbers

- \(M_{a_0} \simeq 930\) MeV and \(M_{\kappa} \simeq 920\) MeV (each with about 10% error), from QSSR analysis of the I=1 and I=1/2 channels
- \(f_{a_0} \simeq (1.6 \pm 0.5)\) MeV, the decay constant of the a₀(980) in the \(\bar qq\) assignment
- \(\Gamma(a_0 \to \eta\pi) \simeq 84\) MeV, from vertex sum rule prediction, consistent with the experimental range 50–100 MeV
- \(\Gamma(\kappa \to K\pi) \simeq 104\) MeV, about a factor 4 smaller than experimental data (≈310 MeV from BES)
- \(\Gamma a_0(\bar qq) \to \gamma\gamma \simeq (0.3 \sim 2.1)\) keV, compared to \(\Gamma a_0(4q) \to \gamma\gamma \simeq (2 \sim 5) \times 10^{-4}\) keV and the measured value \((0.24 \pm 0.08)\) keV
- \(M_{\sigma_B} \simeq 1\) GeV and \(M_G \simeq (1.5 \pm 0.2)\) GeV, from QSSR of the gluonium correlator
- \(f_{\sigma_B} \simeq (884 \pm 116)\) MeV and \(f_G \simeq (390 \pm 145)\) MeV, the decay constants of the two gluonium states
- \(\psi_s(0) \simeq 2 M^2_{\sigma_B} f_{\sigma_B}^2 \simeq 1.5\) GeV⁴, compared to the LET value of 1.6 GeV⁴, showing near-saturation by the σ_B
- \(g_{\sigma_B \pi^+ \pi^-} \simeq (3.2 \sim 6.8)\) GeV, from NV sum rules
- \(\Gamma(G \to \eta'\eta) \simeq (5 \sim 10)\) MeV, from G-dominance of the η₁η₁ vertex sum rule
- \(\Gamma(\sigma_B \to \gamma\gamma) \simeq (0.03 \sim 0.08)\) keV, \(\Gamma(\sigma'_B \to \gamma\gamma) \simeq (0.01 \sim 0.03)\) keV, \(\Gamma(G \to \gamma\gamma) \simeq (0.3 \sim 0.6)\) keV from box-diagram calculation
- Mixing angle \(\theta_S \simeq (45 \pm 15)^\circ\) from γγ width constraints
- \(g^{exp}_{\sigma \pi^+ \pi^-} \simeq (3.0 \pm 1.5)\) GeV and \(g^{exp}_{f_0 \pi^+ \pi^-} \simeq (1.5 \pm 0.3)\) GeV, corresponding to \(\Gamma^{exp}(\sigma(600) \to \pi\pi) \approx 481\) MeV and \(\Gamma^{exp}(f_0(980) \to \pi\pi) \approx (70 \pm 30)\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the author's previous QSSR analyses of scalar mesons (Narison, 2002; Narison, 2003) and tests the three competing proposals (\(\bar qq\), four-quark \(\bar q\bar q qq\), and gluonium) against recent experimental data from BES, E791, FOCUS, KLOE, SND, CMD2, BELLE, and WA102. The paper contradicts the instanton-based analysis of Forkel (2004) which finds a single scalar gluonium at 1.25 GeV with a decay constant that would produce an unacceptably large J/ψ→Sγ branching ratio. The results enable future lattice QCD calculations with dynamical fermions to test the predicted σ_B parameters via the scalar correlator subtraction constant, and suggest that precise measurements of the a₀ decay constant (which scales linearly with light quark mass in the \(\bar qq\) scheme but is constant in the four-quark scheme) could distinguish between these assignments.
