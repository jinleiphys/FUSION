# Chiral phase transition: effective field theory and holography

> We consider the chiral phase transition relevant for QCD matter at finite temperature but with vanishing baryon density. Presumably, the chiral phase transition is of second order for two-flavor QCD in the chiral limit. Near the transition temperature, we apply the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism and construct a low-energy effective field theory for the system, in which fluctuations and dissipations are systematically captured. The dynamical variables involve the chiral charge densities and order parameter (chiral condensate). Via the holographic Schwinger-Keldysh technique, the effective action is further confirmed within a modified AdS/QCD model. With higher-order terms suitably neglected, the stochastic equations derived from the effective field theory resemble those of model F in the Hohenberg-Halperin classification. Within the effective field theory, we briefly discuss the spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry and Goldstone modes.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a low-energy effective field theory (EFT) for the chiral phase transition in two-flavor QCD at finite temperature and zero baryon density, specifically assuming the transition is of second order in the chiral limit and that the temperature is slightly above the critical value so that flavor symmetry is not spontaneously broken.

## Method

The authors apply the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism to build a hydrodynamic EFT whose dynamical variables are the chiral charge densities and the chiral condensate (order parameter), constrained by unitarity, rotational symmetry, flavor SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R symmetry, chemical shift symmetry, dynamical KMS symmetry, and Onsager relations. They then confirm the effective action via the holographic Schwinger-Keldysh technique within a modified AdS/QCD model, tuning the mass of a bulk scalar field so that the dual operator has the dimension of a chiral condensate near the critical point.

## Key numbers

- The coefficient \(b_0\) in the order-parameter Lagrangian scales as \(b_0 \sim (T - T_c)\) with \(T_c\) the critical temperature.
- The diffusive Lagrangian coefficients satisfy \(a_1 \leq 0\), \(a_2 \leq 0\), \(a_8 \leq 0\), \(a_9 \leq 0\) due to the positivity constraint.
- The Onsager relation imposes \(c_2 = -d_2 = b_2\) among the cubic coupling coefficients.
- The KMS symmetry constraints include \(c_0 = c_1 = c_1^*\), \(c_2 = c_2^*\), \(d_0 = d_1 = d_1^*\), \(d_2 = d_2^*\), and for quartic terms \(c_3 = 2c_4 = 2c_4^*\), \(d_3 = 2d_4 = 2d_4^*\), \(\chi_4 = \chi_4^*\).

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic EFT construction of Glorioso (2020), Bu (2022), and Hongo (2024) from SU(2) to SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R symmetry and adds a non-conserved chiral condensate as a dynamical variable, motivated by the critical slowing-down phenomenon near the phase transition. It generalizes the EFT for a nearly critical U(1) superfluid (Donos 2023, Bu 2024) to the non-Abelian case, and the resulting stochastic equations resemble model F in the Hohenberg-Halperin classification, which the authors interpret as describing a non-Abelian superfluid. The work enables future extensions to U(3)_L × U(3)_R flavor symmetry, finite quark masses, and inclusion of energy-momentum dynamics to determine the actual QCD universality class (model H, as argued by Son (2004)).
