# SU(3)-guided Realistic Nucleon-nucleon Interactions for Large-scale Calculations

> We examine nucleon-nucleon realistic interactions, based on their SU(3) decomposition to SU(3)-symmetric components. We find that many of these interaction components are negligible, which, in turn, allows us to identify a subset of physically relevant components that are sufficient to describe the structure of low-lying states in $^{12}$C and related observables, such as excitation energies, electric quadrupole transitions and rms radii. We find that paring the interaction down to half of the SU(3)-symmetric components or more yields results that practically coincide with the corresponding ab initio calculations with the full interaction. In addition, we show that while various realistic interactions differ in their SU(3) decomposition, their renormalized effective counterparts exhibit a striking similarity and composition that can be linked to dominant nuclear features such as deformation, pairing, clustering, and spin-orbit effect.

## Key claim

The authors show that realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, when decomposed into SU(3)-symmetric components, contain a large number of negligible terms; retaining only the most dominant SU(3) tensors (as few as half or fewer of all components) yields calculated observables for low-lying states in 12C that practically coincide with full ab initio calculations, with the caveat that this agreement is strongest for optimal harmonic oscillator parameters and larger model spaces.

## Method

The authors decompose realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions (JISP16, N3LO, and their renormalized counterparts via Okubo-Lee-Suzuki and Similarity Renormalization Group techniques) into SU(3)-symmetric tensors and compute each tensor's relative strength using a Hilbert-Schmidt norm. They then perform symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) calculations for 12C using only the most dominant SU(3) tensors (selected by ordering from highest to lowest relative strength) and compare excitation energies, B(E2) transition probabilities, and root-mean-square radii to results from the full interaction.

## Key numbers

- For JISP16 interaction in Nmax=10 with ħΩ=15 MeV, 51 largest SU(3) tensors out of 169 total account for about 80% of the total interaction strength.
- With less than 30% of interaction components retained, the deviation from full-interaction results is less than 2% for the 12C ground-state rms radius and less than 7% for the B(E2: 2+1 → 0+1) value.
- For the proton-neutron system, retaining only 26% of the SU(3)-symmetric interaction components yields a root-mean-square error of 0.5–0.9 MeV for positive-parity states and 0.9–1.2 MeV for negative-parity states up through 30 MeV.
- The empirical estimate ħΩ=41/A1/3 gives 18 MeV for 12C, around which the selected interactions show minimal deviation from full-interaction results.

## Context

This work extends the symmetry-adapted no-core shell model framework (Launey 2016, Dytrych 2020) by providing a systematic SU(3)-based reduction of realistic interactions, linking dominant SU(3) tensors to physical operators such as quadrupole-quadrupole (Q·Q), pairing, spin-orbit, and tensor forces. The findings explain why earlier schematic models like Elliott's SU(3) model (Elliott 1958) and the no-core symplectic model (Dreyfuss 2013, Tobin 2014) successfully describe nuclear deformation and clustering, and they enable large-scale ab initio calculations using dramatically reduced interactions without significant loss of accuracy for low-energy observables.
