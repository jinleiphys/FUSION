# Pionic Measures of Parity and CP Violation in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> The collisions of large nuclei at high energies could produce metastable vacua which are odd under parity, P, charge conjugation, C, and/or CP. Using only the three-momenta of charged pions (or kaons), we show how to construct global observables which are odd under P, C, and CP, and which can be measured on an event by event basis. Model dependent estimates of the P-odd observables are on the order of 10^(-3).

# Pionic Measures of Parity and CP Violation in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim

The authors propose that metastable vacua odd under parity (P), charge conjugation (C), and/or CP could be produced in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions, and that global observables constructed solely from charged pion (or kaon) three-momenta can detect these discrete symmetry violations on an event-by-event basis. The estimated magnitude of the P-odd observables is model-dependent and at least on the order of 10^(-3), contingent on the specific parameters of their nonlinear sigma model with an anomaly term.

## Method

The authors construct several global observables from the three-momenta of charged pions (or kaons) that are odd under P, C, and/or CP, using antisymmetric epsilon tensor invariants and introducing either a fixed unit vector or momentum-dependent axes. They then estimate the magnitude of these observables within a specific model where metastable P-odd bubbles arise as stationary points of an effective lagrangian with a mass term, an anomaly term proportional to topological susceptibility, and a Wess-Zumino-Witten term, using the condensate fields phi_u, phi_d, phi_s and the pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Model-dependent estimate of P-odd observables: at least ~10^(-3)
- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 93 MeV
- Quark mass ratios used: m_u/m_d approximately 1/2; m_u/m_s approximately 1/36
- Critical ratio for P-odd bubble appearance: (a/c)_cr approximately 0.24, where a is the anomaly coefficient and c is a constant from the mass term
- Condensate field values at the critical point: phi_u approximately 1.8, phi_d approximately 0.5, phi_s approximately 0.03
- Energy density within a P-odd bubble relative to ordinary vacuum: approximately 25 n^2 MeV/fm^3, where n is the winding number (n = 1, 2, 3...)
- For a bubble of approximately 5 fm radius: approximately 100 n^2 pions produced in its decay
- Pion multiplicity at RHIC energies: approximately 1000 per unit rapidity

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous model (Kharzeev, Pisarski, and Tytgat, 1998) in which metastable vacua related to theta-vacua could arise in hot QCD, and builds on the concept of "handedness" introduced by Nachtmann (1977) and Efremov (1978) for studying spin-dependent effects in jet fragmentation. The proposed observables enable experimental searches for parity and CP violation in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and similar facilities, and are related to earlier ideas about exciting metastable vacua in hadronic reactions (Lee and Wick, 1974) and collective pion field fluctuations in heavy ion collisions (Anselm, 1989; Bjorken, Kowalski, and Taylor, 1993; Rajagopal and Wilczek, 1993).
