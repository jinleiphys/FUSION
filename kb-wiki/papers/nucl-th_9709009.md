# ZPC 1.0.1: a parton cascade for ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions

> A Monte Carlo program solving Boltzmann equation for partons via cascade method is presented. At presented, only gluon-gluon elastic scattering is included. The scattering cross section is regulated by a medium generated screening mass. Three different geometric modes (3 dimension expansion, 1-d expansion, and scattering inside a box) are provided for theoretical study of the parton transport and the applicability of the cascade method. Space cell division is available to save the number of computer operations. This improves the speed of the calculation by a large factor and makes the code best optimized for simulation of parton cascade in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

ZPC 1.0.1 is a Monte Carlo program that solves the Boltzmann equation for partons via the cascade method, but in its current version it includes only gluon-gluon elastic scattering, with the scattering cross section regulated by a medium-generated screening mass. The abstract's claim of solving the Boltzmann equation is hedged by the fact that only elastic gluon-gluon collisions are implemented, and the screening mass is estimated using a simplified model that assumes formulas for equilibrium or near-equilibrium systems can be applied to the far-from-equilibrium case of ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Method

The program uses the cascade method, where two partons scatter when their closest distance in a specified frame is less than the interaction length, defined as sqrt(sigma/pi). The gluon-gluon elastic scattering differential cross section is regularized by a Yukawa-type screening mass, yielding a total cross section sigma = (9 pi alpha_S^2)/(2 mu^2). Three geometric modes are provided: 3-dimensional expansion, 1-dimensional expansion, and scattering inside a box, with space cell division implemented to reduce the number of operations from proportional to N^2 to proportional to C*N*n, where n is the average number of particles per cell.

## Key numbers

- For 3-dimensional expansion with initial density in the local rest frame approximately 50/fm^3 at proper time 0.1 fm/c and transverse radius 5 fm, with parton-parton scattering cross section 3.5 mb, the code takes around 6 minutes to finish 1 event (approximately 7000 binary collisions) on SPARC20, and 30 seconds on alpha Personal Workstation.
- The built-in initial condition for 3-d expansion uses 4000 particles uniformly distributed in pseudo-rapidity range -5 to +5, generated in local thermal equilibrium at temperature 500 MeV, within a transverse disk of radius 5 fm, with longitudinal formation time tau_0 = 0.1 fm.
- The screening mass estimate for RHIC conditions gives mu approximately 4.5 fm^{-1}, using alpha approximately 0.4 and N_G/(2Y) approximately 300.
- Example output for 10 events with screening mass mu = 3 fm^{-1} shows number of collisions between particles ranging from 6856 to 6954 per event.

## Context

This work extends the parton cascade approach developed in parallel with GCP by Yang Pang (1995), with ZPC 1.0.1 focusing specifically on gluon elastic scatterings and including a local interaction list optimization using space cell division. The code is designed to study final state parton interactions in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC energies and beyond, enabling investigation of how elastic scattering affects global observables before hadronization, and providing a framework that can be modified to include inelastic collisions, parton statistics, and hadronization in future versions.
