# All orders factorization and the Coulomb problem

> In the limit of large nuclear charge, $Z\gg 1$, or small lepton velocity, $β\ll 1$, Coulomb corrections to nuclear beta decay and related processes are enhanced as $Zα/β$ and become large or even non-perturbative (with $α$ the QED fine structure constant). We provide a constructive demonstration of factorization to all orders in perturbation theory for these processes and compute the all-orders hard and soft functions appearing in the factorization formula. We clarify the relationship between effective field theory amplitudes and historical treatments of beta decay in terms of a Fermi function.

## Key claim

The paper provides a constructive demonstration that Coulomb corrections to nuclear beta decay and related processes factorize to all orders in perturbation theory, with the factorization formula involving hard and soft functions that can be computed explicitly; the abstract's claim of "constructive demonstration" is qualified by the restriction to the static limit where the nucleus is treated as infinitely heavy and recoil corrections are neglected.

## Method

The authors analyze Feynman diagrams for a charged lepton interacting with a static Coulomb field, separating loop integrals into soft momentum regions (|L| ~ photon mass λ) and hard momentum regions (|L| ~ lepton momentum p). They compute the soft factor by exponentiation of the one-loop result following Yennie (1961) and Weinberg (1965), evaluate the hard factor order by order in perturbation theory through three loops for the Schrodinger-Coulomb case, and extract the all-orders hard function by comparing the perturbative expansion with the known analytic solution of the Coulomb wavefunction. For the relativistic Dirac-Coulomb problem, they introduce a charge form factor regulator to handle ultraviolet divergences and compute the UV contribution separately.

## Key numbers

* One-loop Schrodinger-Coulomb amplitude: M^(1) → (i m/p)(Ze²/4π)[log(2p/λ) - iπ/2] in the limit ε→0, λ→0
* One-loop hard factor for Schrodinger-Coulomb: M_H^(1) = [i Z ᾱ / v] (-4p²/μ² - i0)^(-ε) [-1/(2ε)] with v = p/m
* Two-loop hard factor for Schrodinger-Coulomb: M_H^(2) = [i Z ᾱ / v]² [1/(8ε²) + π²/12 + 5ζ(3)ε + O(ε²)]
* Three-loop hard factor for Schrodinger-Coulomb: M_H^(3) = [i Z ᾱ / v]³ [-1/(48ε³) - π²/(24ε) - 13ζ(3)/6 + O(ε)]
* All-orders Schrodinger-Coulomb hard function: M_H(μ) = Γ(1 - i Zα/v) exp[(Zα/v)(π/2 + i log(2p/μ) - iγ_E)]
* One-loop Dirac-Coulomb hard factor: M_H1^(1) = [i Z ᾱ / v] (-4p²/μ² - i0)^(-ε) [-1/(2ε)], M_H2^(1) = [i Z ᾱ / v] (-4p²/μ² - i0)^(-ε) [1/(2(1-2ε))]
* Two-loop UV contribution from form factor: M_UV^(2) = [Z ᾱ (μ/Λ_UV)^(2ε)]² [-1/(8ε) - 1/2 + O(ε)]

## Context

This work extends the historical treatment of Coulomb corrections in beta decay via Fermi functions (Fermi 1934) and wavefunction methods (Wilkinson 1982, Kotila 2012, Hayen 2017) by reformulating the problem in terms of effective field theory factorization. The authors clarify that traditional position-space wavefunctions at r=0 are UV divergent beyond one loop for the Dirac case, and their approach using dimensional regularization and MS-bar subtraction enables systematic matching to underlying quark-level Lagrangians. The factorization formula enables resummation of logarithms and consistent treatment of higher-order radiative corrections, which is crucial for precision measurements in nuclear beta decays as discussed by Hill (2023).
