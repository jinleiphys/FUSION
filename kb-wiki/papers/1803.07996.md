# The approach to equilibrium of a quarkonium in a quark-gluon plasma

> We derive equations of motion for the reduced density matrix of a heavy quarkonium in contact with a quark-gluon plasma in thermal equilibrium. These equations allow in particular a proper treatment of the regime when the temperature of the plasma is comparable to the binding energy of the quarkonium. These equations are used to study how the quarkonium approaches equilibrium with the plasma, and we discuss the corresponding entropy increase, or free energy decrease, depending on the temperature regime. The effect of collisions can be accounted for by the generalization of the imaginary potential introduced in previous studies, and from which collision rates are derived. An important outcome of the present analysis is that this imaginary potential has a sizeable dependence on the energy of the relevant transitions.

## Key claim

The paper derives equations of motion for the reduced density matrix of a heavy quarkonium in a quark-gluon plasma that allow a proper treatment of the regime where the plasma temperature is comparable to the binding energy, going beyond the high-temperature Langevin-type approximation used in prior work (Blaizot 2017). The key hedge is that the imaginary potential introduced in previous studies has a sizeable dependence on the energy of the relevant transitions, which the abstract mentions but does not quantify.

## Method

The authors start from the full QCD Hamiltonian for a heavy quark-antiquark pair immersed in a thermal quark-gluon plasma, treat the interaction between the heavy quarks and the plasma perturbatively to second order in the gauge coupling, and derive a Markovian master equation for the reduced density matrix after tracing over plasma degrees of freedom. They then decompose the density matrix into color-singlet and color-octet components, integrate out the center-of-mass motion assuming small center-of-mass velocity, and obtain coupled evolution equations for the relative-motion density matrices that include memory integrals and evolution operators accounting for the different potentials in singlet and octet channels.

## Key numbers

* The singlet potential is given by \( V_{\rm s}({\boldsymbol r}) = -C_F \alpha_s / r \) with \( C_F = (N_c^2 - 1)/(2N_c) \) and \( N_c = 3 \); the octet potential is \( V_{\rm o}({\boldsymbol r}) = \alpha_s / (2N_c r) \).
* The color algebra yields factors such as \( N_c = 3 \) for the number of colors, \( T_F = 1/2 \) for the normalization of color matrices, and the structure constants satisfy \( f^{ABC} f^{ABD} = N_c \delta^{CD} \) and \( d^{ABC} d^{ABD} = (N_c^2 - 4)/N_c \delta^{CD} \).
* The operator \( {\cal S}_{{\bf q} \cdot \hat{\bf s}} \equiv 2 \sin({\bf q} \cdot \hat{\bf s} / 2) \) appears in the relative-motion equations, where \( \hat{\bf s} \) is the relative coordinate operator.

## Context

This work extends the open-quantum-systems approach to quarkonium in a quark-gluon plasma, complementing the study presented in Blaizot (2017) which assumed the intrinsic dynamics of heavy quarks is slow compared to the plasma and derived Langevin-type equations valid at high temperature. The present paper addresses the regime where the binding energy is comparable to the temperature, which is relevant for realistic heavy-ion collision conditions. The imaginary potential formalism was introduced by Laine (2006), Beraudo (2007), Brambilla (2008), and Escobedo (2008, 2010), and the Lindblad equation approach was developed by Akamatsu (2014); this work enables more accurate treatment of color-changing transitions (singlet-to-octet and octet-to-singlet) that occur when a quarkonium absorbs or emits a gluon.
