# Viscous Effects on the Mapping of the Initial to Final State in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We investigate the correlation between various aspects of the initial geometry of heavy ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider energies and the final anisotropic flow, using v-USPhydro, a 2+1 event-by-event viscous relativistic hydrodynamical model. We test the extent of which shear and bulk viscosity affect the prediction of the final flow harmonics, $v_n$, from the initial eccentricities, $\varepsilon_n$. We investigate in detail the flow harmonics $v_1$ through $v_5$ where we find that $v_1$, $v_4$, and $v_5$ are dependent on more complicated aspects of the initial geometry that are especially important for the description of peripheral collisions, including a non-linear dependence on eccentricities as well as a dependence on shorter-scale features of the initial density. Furthermore, we compare our results to previous results from NeXSPheRIO, a 3+1 relativistic ideal hydrodynamical model that has a non-zero initial flow contribution, and find that the combined contribution from 3+1 dynamics and non-zero, fluctuating initial flow decreases the predictive ability of the initial eccentricities, in particular for very peripheral collisions, but also disproportionately in central collisions.

## Key claim

The paper tests how well initial-state eccentricities predict final-state anisotropic flow harmonics when shear and bulk viscosity are included, finding that while the linear mapping works well for elliptic flow ($v_2$) and triangular flow ($v_3$), the higher harmonics $v_1$, $v_4$, and $v_5$ require more complicated estimators that include nonlinear combinations of eccentricities and sensitivity to smaller-scale features of the initial density, especially in peripheral collisions.

## Method

The authors used v-USPhydro, a 2+1 event-by-event viscous relativistic hydrodynamical model with Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions at RHIC Au+Au energies, assuming boost invariance and zero initial transverse flow and viscous stress. They computed flow harmonics $v_n$ for $n=1$ through $5$ by running simulations with ideal hydrodynamics, shear viscosity only, bulk viscosity only, and both shear and bulk viscosity together, then quantified the quality of various initial-state estimators using the linear correlation coefficient $Q_n$ between the flow vector $V_n$ and the estimator vector. They compared these results to previous calculations from NeXSPheRIO, a 3+1 ideal hydrodynamical model with fluctuating initial flow from the NeXus event generator.

## Key numbers

- 150 events were generated for each centrality class in both the v-USPhydro and NeXSPheRIO calculations.
- The freeze-out temperature was taken as $T_{FO}=150$ MeV.
- Flow harmonics were calculated over the transverse momentum range $0 \leq p_T \leq 5$ GeV.
- The total number of $\pi^{+}$'s was fixed to 123 in the v-USPhydro calculations.
- For NeXSPheRIO, approximately $6 \times 10^5$ particles per event were used for flow analysis, with particles in the pseudorapidity interval $|\eta| < 1$.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Gardim et al. (2011) that used ideal hydrodynamics with NeXSPheRIO to map initial eccentricities to final flow harmonics, by systematically including shear and bulk viscosity effects in a controlled 2+1 setup. The results show that the combined contribution from 3+1 dynamics and non-zero fluctuating initial flow (as present in NeXSPheRIO) decreases the predictive ability of initial eccentricities, particularly for very peripheral and central collisions, indicating that future work must account for these complications when using flow measurements to constrain initial conditions and transport coefficients.
