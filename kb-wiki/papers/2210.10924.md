# Nuclear Equation of State and Single-nucleon Potential from Gogny-like Energy Density Functionals Encapsulating Effects of Nucleon-nucleon Short-range Correlations

> Nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations (SRCs) induce a high momentum tail (HMT) in the single-nucleon momentum distribution function $n_{ǩ}^J(ρ,δ)$ in cold neutron-rich matter. While there are clear experimental evidences that the SRC/HMT effects are different for neutrons and protons and their strengths depend strongly on the isospin asymmetry of finite nuclei mostly based on electron-nucleus scattering experiments, much less is known experimentally about the SRC/HMT effects in the dense neutron-rich matter. To facilitate further explorations of SRC/HMT effects in dense neutron-rich matter especially with heavy-ion reactions involving high-energy radioactive beams as well as multimessenger observations of neutron stars and their mergers, by incorporating the SRC-induced HMT in $n_{ǩ}^J(ρ,δ)$ into a Gogny-like energy density functional we study SRC/HMT effects on the equation of state (EOS) especially its symmetry energy term and single-nucleon potential in the dense asymmetric nucleonic matter (ANM). Using a parametrization as a surrogate for the momentum-dependent kernel in the Gogny-like energy density functional (EDF) we derive analytical expressions for all components of the ANM EOS and their characteristics (e.g., magnitude, slope and curvature as well as nucleon effective mass) at saturation density $ρ_0$ as well as the momentum-dependent single-nucleon optical potential in neutron-rich matter using parameters characterizing nuclear interactions as well as the size, shape and isospin dependence of the HMT at $ρ_0$. Some consequences of the SRC/HMT effects on properties of neutron stars are also studied.

## Key claim

By incorporating a high-momentum tail (HMT) induced by nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations (SRCs) into a Gogny-like energy density functional, the authors derive analytical expressions for the equation of state (EOS) and single-nucleon potential in asymmetric nucleonic matter, finding that the SRC/HMT enhances the kinetic EOS but reduces the single-nucleon potential in symmetric nuclear matter, while the total symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities is strongly softened; these effects depend on the specific parametrization of the HMT (e.g., HMT-exp vs. HMT-SCGF models) and the momentum-dependent kernel used.

## Method

The authors incorporated a SRC-induced HMT in the single-nucleon momentum distribution function \(n_{\v{k}}^J(\rho,\delta)\) into a Gogny-like energy density functional (EDF) with a parametrized surrogate for the momentum-dependent kernel \(\Omega(\v{k},\v{k}')\) (originally from Yukawa or Gogny finite-range interactions). Using this surrogate, they derived analytical expressions for all components of the asymmetric nucleonic matter (ANM) EOS (kinetic and potential parts) and the momentum-dependent single-nucleon optical potential at saturation density \(\rho_0\), with parameters fixed by empirical constraints on the EOS of symmetric nuclear matter (SNM), symmetry energy, nucleon optical potential, and SRC/HMT properties.

## Key numbers

- The percentage of nucleons in the HMT above the Fermi surface in SNM is about 28% (from experimental data), decreasing to about 1% in pure neutron matter (PNM) (Hen14, Hen15).
- The HMT amplitude parameter \(C_0\) for SNM is \(0.161 \pm 0.015\) in the HMT-exp model, with the HMT cutoff parameter \(\phi_0 \approx 2.38 \pm 0.56\).
- For PNM, the HMT amplitude \(C_{\rm{n}}^{\rm{PNM}} \approx 0.12\) and the cutoff parameter \(\phi_{\rm{n}}^{\rm{PNM}} \approx 1.04 \pm 0.02\), giving \(C_1 \approx -0.25 \pm 0.07\) and \(\phi_1 \approx -0.56 \pm 0.10\).
- In the HMT-SCGF model (self-consistent Green's function with AV18 interaction), the HMT in SNM is 11%–13% at saturation density, with \(\phi_0 \approx 1.49\), \(\phi_1 \approx -0.25\), \(C_0 \approx 0.121\), and \(C_1 \approx -0.01\).
- The saturation density \(\rho_0\) is taken as approximately \(0.16 \, \text{fm}^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of SRC/HMT effects on nuclear matter EOS, which were mostly carried out within nonlinear relativistic mean-field (RMF) models (Cai 2016b, Lou 2022a, Lu 2022) where interactions are momentum independent, by instead using a non-relativistic Gogny-like EDF with explicit momentum dependence. The results enable future applications in heavy-ion reaction simulations with high-energy radioactive beams and multimessenger observations of neutron stars, while also providing parametrized forms of the EOS and single-nucleon potential for use in both nuclear physics and astrophysics.
