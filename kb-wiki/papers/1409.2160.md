# Collective flow in ultrarelativistic $^3$He-Au collisions

> The triangular flow in ultrarelativistic $^3$He-Au collisions at RHIC energies is enhanced due to the triangular arrangement of the nucleon configurations in $^3$He. We study the fireball eccentricities in the Glauber Monte Carlo model and find that since the configurations of the projectile $^3$He are elongated triangles, the created fireball has a large ellipticity and a smaller triangularity. The dependence of the triangularity on centrality is weak, so it cannot be extracted from the centrality dependence of the triangular flow $v_3$, as it is dominated by the centrality dependence of the hydrodynamic response. We propose to look at the centrality dependence of the ratio $v_n\{4\}/v_n\{2\}$, where the uncertainties from the hydrodynamic response cancel, and show that the basic signature of the geometry-driven collective flow is the raise of the ratio $v_3\{4\}/v_3\{2\}$ with the number of participant nucleons for centralities less than 10%.

# Collective flow in ultrarelativistic $^3$He-Au collisions

## Key claim

The triangular flow $v_3$ in $^3$He-Au collisions at RHIC energies is enhanced by the triangular arrangement of nucleons in the $^3$He projectile, but the effect is small because the most probable configurations are elongated isosceles triangles (not equilateral), and the centrality dependence of triangularity is weak; the authors propose that the signature of geometry-driven triangular flow is instead visible in the ratio $v_3\{4\}/v_3\{2\}$, which rises with the number of participant nucleons for centralities below 10%.

## Method

The authors used the Glauber Monte Carlo model (implemented in GLISSANDO) to simulate the initial fireball eccentricities in $^3$He-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, sampling $^3$He wave functions from the Monte Carlo Green's function method. They computed cumulant moments of the eccentricity distributions $\epsilon_n\{2\}$ and $\epsilon_n\{4\}$ for $n=2,3$ as functions of the number of wounded nucleons $N_w$, and used the linear hydrodynamic response relation $v_n = \kappa_n \epsilon_n$ to argue that ratios $v_n\{4\}/v_n\{2\}$ cancel the centrality-dependent response coefficient $\kappa_n$, allowing direct comparison to $\epsilon_n\{4\}/\epsilon_n\{2\}$.

## Key numbers

- The inelastic NN cross section at RHIC energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV is 42 mb.
- The $^3$He nucleon configurations projected on the transverse plane yield a triangularity distribution peaked at $\epsilon_3 \simeq 0.6$ and an ellipticity distribution peaked at $\epsilon_2 \simeq 1$.
- The minimum of the ratio $v_3\{4\}/v_3\{2\}$ occurs at a centrality of approximately 10%, corresponding to $N_w \approx 22$.
- For ultra-central events with centrality below 0.1% ($N_w = 34$), the ratio $v_3\{4\}/v_3\{2\}$ increases, signaling geometry-driven triangular flow.
- The Gaussian smearing width used for source density from wounded nucleon positions is 0.4 fm.

## Context

This work extends studies of collective flow in small collision systems at RHIC and the LHC, following hydrodynamic calculations for p-Pb, d-Au, and $^3$He-Au collisions by Bozek (2011), Nagle (2013), and others. It directly addresses the difficulty identified by Nagle (2013) that the centrality dependence of triangularity in $^3$He-Au is too weak to extract from $v_3$ alone due to the centrality dependence of the hydrodynamic response. The proposed ratio method enables experimental tests at RHIC and could be applied to other deformed projectiles such as d-Au, $^9$Be-Au, or $^{12}$C-Au collisions, as suggested by Broniowski (2013).
