# Gaplessness of the Gaffnian

> We study the Gaffnian trial wavefunction proposed to describe fractional quantum Hall correlations at Bose filling factor $ν=2/3$ and Fermi filling $ν=2/5$. A family of Hamiltonians interpolating between a hard-core interaction for which the physics is known and a projector whose ground state is the Gaffnian is studied in detail. We give evidence for the absence of a gap by using large-scale exact diagonalizations in the spherical geometry. This is in agreement with recent arguments based on the fact that this wavefunction is constructed from a non-unitary conformal field theory. By using the cylinder geometry, we discuss in detail the nature of the underlying minimal model and we show the appearance of heterotic conformal towers in the edge energy spectrum where left and right movers are generated by distinct primary operators.

## Key claim

The Gaffnian trial wavefunction, proposed for fractional quantum Hall states at Bose filling factor ν=2/3 and Fermi filling ν=2/5, is gapless in the thermodynamic limit, consistent with its construction from a non-unitary conformal field theory (CFT) and contradicting the expectation of an incompressible gapped phase.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale exact diagonalizations of bosonic systems on the sphere for up to 16 particles, studying a one-parameter family of Hamiltonians that interpolates between a pure two-body hard-core delta interaction (known to produce a gapped composite fermion state) and the special three-body derivative-delta interaction for which the Gaffnian is the exact zero-energy ground state. They also analyzed the Gaffnian on the cylinder geometry with open boundary conditions, including the thin-torus limit, to classify edge states according to the Virasoro conformal towers of the minimal model M(5,3).

## Key numbers

* The flux-number relation for the Gaffnian on the sphere is N_phi = (3/2)N - 3, corresponding to filling factor ν=2/3 for bosons.
* The Gaffnian CFT M(5,3) contains primary fields with conformal dimensions: Δ_ψ = 3/4 (ψ field), Δ_φ = 1/5 (φ field), Δ_σ = -1/20 (σ field).
* Gap extrapolations to the thermodynamic limit were performed for N=12, 14, and 16 bosons; the pure Gaffnian Hamiltonian (α=1) showed a gap consistent with zero within the precision of the method, while the pure two-body hard-core case (α=0) gave a finite gap.
* The cylinder geometry required 2K+1 = (3/2)N - 2 orbitals for the unique zero-momentum ground state.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the Gaffnian by Simon et al. (2007) and addresses the problem raised by Read (2009) that non-unitary CFTs should produce gapless bulk states, in contrast to unitary CFTs like the Ising model underlying the Moore-Read Pfaffian. The results confirm the critical nature of the Gaffnian, supporting the argument that only unitary CFTs (such as the Read-Rezayi Z_k parafermion series) describe incompressible FQHE phases, while non-unitary minimal models like M(5,3) describe critical points between different FQHE phases. This enables future work to distinguish candidate FQHE states by testing for gaplessness in numerical simulations.
