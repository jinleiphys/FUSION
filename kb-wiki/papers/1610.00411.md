# Excited State Mass spectra and Regge trajectories of Bottom Baryons

> We present the mass spectra of radial and orbital excited states of singly heavy bottom baryons; $Σ_{b}^{+}, Σ_{b}^-, Ξ_b^-, Ξ_b^0, Λ_b^0$ and $Ω_b^-$. The QCD motivated hypercentral quark model is employed for the three body description of baryons and the form of confinement potential is hyper coulomb plus linear. The first order correction to the confinement potential is also incorporated in this work. The semi-electronic decay of $Ω_b$ and $Ξ_b$ are calculated using the spectroscopic parameters of the baryons. The computed results are compared with other theoretical predictions as well as with the available experimental observations. The Regge trajectories are plotted in (n, $M^2$) plane.

## Key claim

The hypercentral constituent quark model (hCQM) with a hyper-Coulomb-plus-linear confinement potential and first-order corrections reproduces the known ground-state masses of singly bottom baryons to within a few MeV, and predicts a dense spectrum of radial and orbital excitations up to approximately 7.7 GeV, though the model's predictions for the first orbital excitations of the Lambda_b (the 1P states) lie about 60-80 MeV above the experimental values reported by LHCb.

## Method

The authors solve the nonrelativistic Schrodinger equation in six-dimensional hyperspherical coordinates for a three-body system of constituent quarks (u, d, s, b) with unequal masses. The confinement potential is taken as a sum of a hyper-Coulomb term and a linear term in the hyperradius x, with a first-order perturbative correction; spin-dependent effects (spin-spin, spin-orbit, and tensor terms) are included. Mass spectra for the ground and excited states (up to n=6 and L=4) are computed for the sextet and anti-triplet bottom baryons (Sigma_b, Xi_b, Lambda_b, Omega_b), and Regge trajectories are plotted in the (n, M^2) plane.

## Key numbers

- Lambda_b(1^2S_{1/2}) ground state: computed 5.621 GeV (model A) and 5.621 GeV (model B); experimental PDG value 5.620 GeV.
- Lambda_b(1^2P_{1/2}) first orbital excitation: computed 5.992 GeV (A) and 6.000 GeV (B); experimental value 5.912 GeV (LHCb).
- Lambda_b(1^2P_{3/2}) first orbital excitation: computed 5.980 GeV (A) and 5.988 GeV (B); experimental value 5.920 GeV (LHCb).
- Sigma_b^+(1^2S_{1/2}) ground state: computed 5.811 GeV (A and B); experimental PDG value 5811.3 +/- 1.7 MeV.
- Sigma_b^-(1^2S_{1/2}) ground state: computed 5.816 GeV (A and B); experimental PDG value 5815.5 +/- 1.7 MeV.
- Xi_b^0(1^2S_{1/2}) ground state: computed 5.793 GeV (A and B); experimental PDG value 5793.1 +/- 2.5 MeV.
- Xi_b^-(1^2S_{1/2}) ground state: computed 5.795 GeV (A and B); experimental PDG value 5794.9 +/- 0.9 MeV.
- Omega_b^-(1^2S_{1/2}) ground state: computed 6.048 GeV (A and B); experimental PDG value 6048.8 +/- 3.2 MeV.
- Constituent quark masses used: m_u = 0.338 GeV, m_d = 0.350 GeV, m_s = 0.500 GeV, m_b = 4.67 GeV; strong coupling alpha_s(mu_0=1 GeV) = 0.6.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hypercentral quark model studies of singly charmed baryons (Thakkar et al., 2015) to the bottom sector, where experimental data on excited states are sparse (only two orbitally excited Lambda_b states known from LHCb). The results are compared with predictions from other approaches including the relativistic quark model of Ebert et al. (2011), the relativistic flux tube model of Chen et al. (2015), and QCD sum rule calculations of Yamaguchi et al. (2015). The computed mass spectra and Regge trajectories provide predictions to guide future experimental searches for excited bottom baryons at LHCb and other facilities.
