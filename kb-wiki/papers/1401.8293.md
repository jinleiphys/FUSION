# Jet (de)coherence in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC

> We study the modifications of jets created in heavy-ion collisions at LHC energies. The inherent hierarchy of scales governing the jet evolution allows to distinguish a leading jet structure, which interacts coherently with the medium as a single color charge, from softer sub-structures that will be sensitive to effects of color decoherence. We argue how this separation comes about and show that this picture is consistent with experimental data on reconstructed jets at the LHC, providing a quantitative description simultaneously of the jet nuclear modification factor, the missing energy in di-jet events and the modification of the fragmentation functions. In particular, we demonstrate that effects due to color decoherence are manifest in the excess of soft particles measured in fragmentation functions in Pb-Pb compared to proton-proton collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a consistent description of multiple jet observables in Pb-Pb collisions emerges from the separation of scales between the jet and the medium, where the leading jet structure interacts coherently as a single color charge while softer sub-structures are sensitive to color decoherence; the authors demonstrate that deviations from the coherent picture are essential for explaining the excess of soft particles in fragmentation functions, which they claim is evidence for partial decoherence.

## Method

The authors model jet modifications using perturbative QCD, solving a kinetic rate equation for the medium-modified parton distribution that describes quasi-democratic branchings of soft gluons leading to turbulent energy flow to large angles. They compute the nuclear modification factor via convolution of the vacuum jet cross-section with the medium-modified quark distribution, and calculate the modified fragmentation function using a jet calculus rule that separates coherent vacuum-like and medium-induced branchings, with an additional incoherent contribution from color decoherence of the first nontrivial splitting. The medium parameters are related via the transport coefficient \(\hat q\) and the mean path length \(L\), with the branching rate derived from the BDMPS one-gluon emission spectrum in the multiple scattering regime.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear modification factor \(R^\text{jet}_{AA}\) shows about 50% suppression in central collisions across a wide range in jet energy, with extracted \(\omega_c = 80\) GeV for 0-10% central Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\text{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV.
* Average transport coefficient \(\hat q = 5.1\) GeV\(^2\)/fm extracted using mean jet path length \(L = 2.5\) fm for 0-10% central collisions.
* Characteristic medium scale \(Q_s = \sqrt{\hat q L} = 3.6\) GeV.
* In-cone energy fraction: 14-19% of energy flows out of a cone of \(\Theta_\text{jet} = 0.3\) (\(x_0=0.12\)), and 9-15% is still missing for \(\Theta_\text{jet} = 0.8\) (\(x_0=0.045\)).
* Angular deviation of sub-leading jet estimated as \(\Delta \Theta_\text{jet} \sim Q_s/p_\perp \sim 0.036\) for a jet \(p_\perp=100\) GeV.
* Minimal angle for induced radiation \(\Theta_c \sim 0.08\) compared to jet reconstruction radius \(\Theta_\text{jet} = 0.3\).
* Vacuum baseline fragmentation function fitted with parameters \(Q_0 = 0.4\) GeV, \(m_h = 1.1\) GeV and \(K = 1.6\) for jets with \(Q = 30\) GeV.
* Medium-modified jet scale varied within \(E \in [100, 125]\) GeV for comparison with CMS data on fragmentation functions.

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS formalism for medium-induced radiation (Baier et al., 1994) and the kinetic rate equation approach (Jeon and Moore, 2003; Blaizot et al., 2013) to provide a unified description of multiple jet observables at the LHC. The authors argue that the coherent jet picture, where the medium does not resolve leading sub-structures, breaks down in the soft sector and requires the inclusion of color decoherence effects previously studied by Mehtar-Tani et al. (2010, 2011) and Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2012). The approach enables the use of jets as a tool to extract information about the quark-gluon plasma and color coherence, with the decoherence correction explaining the excess of soft particles in fragmentation functions measured by CMS (2012).
