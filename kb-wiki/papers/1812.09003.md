# Structure of resonances in a simple quantum-mechanical model

> We study the structure of resonances derived from the solution of an exactly solvable Lippmann-Schwinger equation. Within this framework, we discuss the concept of "resonance form factors", and the description of the resonant amplitudes in terms of effective energy-dependent potentials.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that resonance form factors and associated spatial density functions can be defined via analytic continuation of bound-state form factors to the second Riemann sheet, but these densities are not the analytic continuations of the absolute squares of bound-state wave functions and do not share all their properties (e.g., parity behavior).

## Method

The author solves the Lippmann-Schwinger equation exactly for a one-dimensional quantum-mechanical potential consisting of two delta functions separated by a distance \(2d\), with constant coupling strength \(v\). Bound-state form factors are computed from the off-shell scattering amplitude using the residue at the bound-state pole, and these expressions are analytically continued to the complex momentum plane to define resonance form factors and spatial density functions. The parity-even part of the scattering amplitude is also described using an effective energy-dependent delta-potential, and the resulting form factors are compared to those from the original double-delta potential.

## Key numbers

- Bound-state mean-square radius for the s-wave state: \(\langle\hat{x}^2\rangle_B^{(+)} = d^2 + \frac{1}{2\kappa_B^2} - \frac{4\kappa_B d^3 e^{-2\kappa_B d}}{3(1+(1+2\kappa_B d)e^{-2\kappa_B d})}\)
- Bound-state mean-square radius for the p-wave state: \(\langle\hat{x}^2\rangle_B^{(-)} = d^2 + \frac{1}{2\kappa_B^2} + \frac{4\kappa_B d^3 e^{-2\kappa_B d}}{3(1-(1+2\kappa_B d)e^{-2\kappa_B d})}\)
- Resonance pole positions for \(v=3\), \(\mu=d=1\): \(k_R^+ \approx 1.213 - 0.105i\) and \(k_R^- \approx 2.572 - 0.317i\)
- In the limit of infinite repulsive coupling, the s-wave resonance pole approaches \(k_R^+ = \frac{\pi}{2d}\) and the p-wave resonance pole approaches \(k_R^- = \frac{\pi}{d}\)
- The resonance form factor in the infinite-coupling limit for the s-wave case: \(F_R^{(+)}(Q^2) \rightarrow \frac{4k_R^2}{4k_R^2 - Q^2} \left(\frac{\sin Qd}{Qd}\right)\) with \(k_R \rightarrow \frac{\pi}{2d}\)

## Context

This work extends the study of resonance structure concepts (form factors, compositeness, energy-dependent potentials) from relativistic quantum field theory and hadron physics into a simple exactly-solvable quantum-mechanical model. It builds on the framework of Gamow (1928), Zeldovich (1961), and Berggren (1968) for resonance wave functions, and connects to the compositeness/elementariness decomposition approach developed by Weinberg (1962, 1965), Baru et al. (2003), Hyodo (2013), and others. The results show that effective energy-dependent potentials can reproduce on-shell scattering amplitudes while yielding completely wrong form factors and spatial densities, highlighting the need for off-shell information to determine resonance structure.
