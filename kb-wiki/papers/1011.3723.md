# OPE, Heavy-Quark Mass, and Heavy-Meson Decay Constants from QCD Sum Rules

> We present a sum-rule extraction of the decay constants of heavy-light mesons from the two-point correlator of pseudoscalar currents. To this end, we compare the perturbative expansions for the correlator and the decay constant performed in terms of either the pole mass or the running MS-bar mass of the heavy quark. The perturbative expansion expressed in terms of the pole mass exhibits no sign of convergence whereas reorganizing this very expansion in terms of the MS-bar mass yields a rather clear hierarchy of the perturbative contributions. Accordingly, the decay constants extracted from the pole-mass correlator turn out to be considerably smaller than those extracted from its MS-bar-mass counterpart. Then, making use of the OPE in terms of the MS-bar mass we derive the decay constants of heavy mesons with emphasis on acquiring control over the uncertainties in the decay constants, related both to the input QCD parameters and to the limited accuracy of the method of sum rules. Gaining this control has become possible due to the application of our novel procedure for extracting hadron observables based on dual thresholds which depend on the Borel parameter.

## Key claim

The decay constants of heavy-light mesons extracted from QCD sum rules depend strongly on whether the heavy-quark mass is expressed in the pole-mass scheme or the MS-bar scheme; the pole-mass expansion shows no convergence and yields decay constants almost 50% smaller than the MS-bar scheme, and the use of a Borel-parameter-dependent effective threshold is essential for obtaining reliable results with controlled uncertainties.

## Method

The authors compute the two-point correlator of pseudoscalar heavy-light currents using the operator product expansion (OPE) to three-loop (NNLO) accuracy, first in the pole-mass scheme and then reorganized in the MS-bar mass scheme. They extract decay constants by applying a novel procedure where the effective continuum threshold is taken as a function of the Borel parameter tau, using polynomial Ansatze (linear, quadratic, cubic) and requiring the dual mass to reproduce the known ground-state meson mass. The OPE-related uncertainties are estimated by bootstrapping over variations of all input QCD parameters (quark masses, alpha_s, condensates).

## Key numbers

- For charmed mesons: f_D = (206.2 ± 7.3_(OPE) ± 5.1_(syst)) MeV; f_Ds = (245.3 ± 15.7_(OPE) ± 4.5_(syst)) MeV.
- For beauty mesons: f_B = (193.4 ± 12.3_(OPE) ± 4.3_(syst)) MeV; f_Bs = (232.5 ± 18.6_(OPE) ± 2.4_(syst)) MeV.
- The b-quark MS-bar mass extracted by matching the sum-rule f_B to the average of lattice determinations is m_b(m_b) = (4.245 ± 0.025) GeV.
- The decay constant from the pole-mass OPE is almost 50% smaller than from the MS-bar scheme.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of Borel-parameter-dependent effective thresholds (Lucha, Melikhov, Simula, 2007-2010) and applies it to heavy-light mesons, building on the three-loop OPE computed by Chetyrkin (1997) and the MS-bar reorganization proposed by Jamin and Lange (2002). The results bring QCD sum-rule predictions for D and D_s decay constants into agreement with lattice QCD and experimental data, and enable a precise determination of the b-quark MS-bar mass by requiring consistency between sum-rule and lattice results for f_B.
