# The Quark/Antiquark Asymmetry of the Nucleon Sea

> Although the distributions of sea quarks and antiquarks generated by leading-twist QCD evolution through gluon splitting $g \rightarrow \bar q q$ are necessarily CP symmetric, the distributions of nonvalence quarks and antiquarks which are intrinsic to the nucleon's bound state wavefunction need not be identical. In this paper we investigate the sea quark/antiquark asymmetries in the nucleon wavefunction which are generated by a light-cone model of energetically-favored meson-baryon fluctuations. The model predicts striking quark/antiquark asymmetries in the momentum and helicity distributions for the down and strange contributions to the proton structure function: the intrinsic $d$ and $s$ quarks in the proton sea are predicted to be negatively polarized, whereas the intrinsic $\bar d$ and $\bar s$ antiquarks give zero contributions to the proton spin. Such a picture is supported by experimental phenomena related to the proton spin problem and the violation of the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule. The light-cone meson-baryon fluctuation model also suggests a structured momentum distribution asymmetry for strange quarks and antiquarks which could be relevant to an outstanding conflict between two different determinations of the strange quark sea in the nucleon. The model predicts an excess of intrinsic $d \bar d$ pairs over $u \bar u$ pairs, as supported by the Gottfried sum rule violation. We also predict that the intrinsic charm and anticharm helicity and momentum distributions are not identical.

# The Quark/Antiquark Asymmetry of the Nucleon Sea

## Key claim

Although sea quarks and antiquarks generated by leading-twist QCD evolution through gluon splitting are necessarily CP symmetric, the distributions of nonvalence quarks and antiquarks intrinsic to the nucleon's bound-state wavefunction need not be identical; the light-cone meson-baryon fluctuation model predicts that intrinsic down and strange quarks in the proton sea are negatively polarized while their corresponding antiquarks give zero contribution to the proton spin.

## Method

The authors adopt a light-cone two-level convolution model in which the nucleon is treated as a two-body system of meson and baryon fluctuations, each composite of quarks and gluons. They compute momentum and helicity distributions for intrinsic strange, down, and charm quark-antiquark pairs by modeling the lowest off-shell configurations (e.g., K+ Lambda for strangeness, pi+ n for down pairs) using Gaussian and power-law wavefunctions parameterized by an invariant mass scale. The quark helicity projections are related to spin projections in the target rest frame via Wigner rotation factors.

## Key numbers

- The net strange quark spin contribution from the K+ Lambda configuration is Delta S_s = -1/3 P_{K+Lambda}, where P_{K+Lambda} is the probability of that fluctuation; the antistrange contribution is Delta S_{bar s} = 0.
- The relative probability of the K*+ Lambda configuration compared to K+ Lambda is 3.6% (Gaussian wavefunction) or 8.9% (power-law wavefunction).
- The NMC measurement of the Gottfried sum rule gives S_G = 0.235 +/- 0.026, implying integral_0^1 dx [d_s(x) - u_s(x)] = 0.148 +/- 0.039.
- The probability of intrinsic s bar s pairs is approximately 10% from fitting measured strange sea at x approx 0.2.
- The helicity contribution from intrinsic strange sea quarks with Fock state probability 4-15% is Delta s_s approx -0.01 to -0.05, compared to the empirical global fit value Delta s = -0.10 +/- 0.03.
- Global fit values for quark helicity contributions: Delta u = 0.83 +/- 0.03, Delta d = -0.43 +/- 0.03, Delta s = -0.10 +/- 0.03.

## Context

This work extends the meson-baryon fluctuation approach previously applied in the cloudy bag model (Signal, 1987) and Skyrme solutions to chiral theories (Burkardt, 1992) by providing a boost-invariant light-cone Fock state description. The model offers a unified explanation for several experimental puzzles: the Gottfried sum rule violation observed by NMC (1991), the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule violation and proton spin problem, and the apparent conflict between CTEQ and CCFR determinations of the strange quark sea. The predictions enable future tests through measurements of Lambda and anti-Lambda polarization in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering and through reanalysis of neutrino dimuon data allowing structured asymmetric intrinsic strange sea contributions.
