# Dyson-Schwinger Equations and the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We review applications of Dyson-Schwinger equations at nonzero temperature, T, and chemical potential, mu, touching topics such as: deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration; the behaviour of bulk thermodynamic quantities; the (T,mu)-dependence of hadron properties; and the possibility of diquark condensation.

# Dyson-Schwinger Equations and the Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim
Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) provide a nonperturbative framework for studying QCD at nonzero temperature and chemical potential, but the reliability of predictions depends critically on the truncation scheme used; the rainbow truncation (leading term in a 1/N_c expansion) necessarily yields mean-field critical exponents and may miss mesonic correlation effects that dominate near phase transitions.

## Method
The authors review DSE applications to the quark gap equation (the QCD gap equation) and Bethe-Salpeter equations at nonzero temperature T and chemical potential μ, using Landau gauge and rainbow truncation (Γ_ν = γ_ν) with various models for the dressed-gluon propagator. The dressed-quark propagator is solved as a function of Matsubara frequencies, and the phase boundary is located by computing the (T,μ)-dependent bag constant B(T,μ) and confinement order parameters derived from the Schwinger function. Diquark condensation is studied via a bispinor gap equation in SU(2) and SU(3) color groups.

## Key numbers
- For a model with m_t = 0.69 GeV (fixed from π and ρ properties at T=0), coincident chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement occur at T_c^χ = 0.15 GeV = T_c^{κ_0} with mean-field critical exponents.
- At T=0, the same model gives a coincident first-order transition at μ_c = 0.375 GeV, corresponding to baryon density ρ_B = 2.9 ρ_0 (where ρ_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}).
- The vacuum quark condensate at T=0, μ=0 is ⟨q̄q⟩^0 ≈ -(0.24 GeV)^3, required for good light-meson observables.
- In an algebraic model (η = 1.06 GeV), the T=0 critical chemical potential is μ_c^0 = 0.3 GeV, and for μ=0 the critical temperature is T_c^0 = 0.17 GeV.
- In the algebraic model, the transverse ρ-meson mass M_{ρ-}^2 = η^2/2 is independent of T and μ; the longitudinal ρ-meson mass at nuclear matter density (μ_0 = 260 MeV) is M_{ρ+}(μ_0) ≈ 0.75 M_{ρ+}(0), and M_{φ+}(μ_0) ≈ 0.85 M_{φ+}(0).
- At the T=0 critical chemical potential (≈3ρ_0), M_{ρ+} ≈ 0.65 M_{ρ+}(0) and M_{φ+} ≈ 0.80 M_{φ+}(0).
- The bag constant at T=0, μ=0 is B(0) = (0.104 GeV)^4 = 15 MeV/fm^3.

## Context
This review extends the DSE approach to nonzero T and μ, building on earlier work by Roberts and collaborators (1990s) that established DSEs as a tool for studying confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. The results are compared with lattice QCD simulations (Cucchieri, 1990s) and DSE studies (Alkofer, 1990s) that suggest infrared-suppressed gluon propagators may not support confinement or DCSB. The work enables systematic calculation of hadron property changes in dense and hot matter, with implications for neutron star cores and heavy-ion collisions. Open questions include the role of vertex corrections beyond rainbow truncation near phase boundaries and the possibility of diquark condensation in SU(3) QCD.
