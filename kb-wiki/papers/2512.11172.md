# Study of few $^3$He-induced nuclear fusion reactions using density-dependent double-folding complex potential

> Nuclear fusion reactions at sub-barrier energies play crucial roles in many aspects of primordial nucleosynthesis in stellar objects. One of the primary aspects that plays a pivotal role in understanding the relationship between stellar evolution and nuclear reaction dynamics is the energy dependence of astronuclear observables, such as the fusion cross-section $σ$. This paper presents the results of a few $^3$He-induced nuclear fusion reactions-$^{3}$He($^3$He,2p)$^{4}$He, $^{6}$Li($^3$He,d)$^{7}$Be and $^{10}$B($^3$He,n)$^{12}$N which are investigated adopting the single-step selective resonant tunnelling model (SRTM). As an improvement over earlier works, the authors have used a microscopically derived density-dependent double-folding potential model, invoking the M3Y-Reid NN interactions, for the numerical computation of the astrophysical S-factor, $S(E)$, and the fusion cross-section, $σ$. The results of the calculations have been compared with those found in the literature. The results obtained in the present studies agree fairly with the experimentally observed results found in the literature.

# Study of few ³He-induced nuclear fusion reactions using density-dependent double-folding complex potential

## Key claim
The density-dependent double-folding potential model with M3Y-Reid NN interactions, when applied within the single-step selective resonant tunnelling model (SRTM), yields computed astrophysical S-factors and fusion cross-sections that agree fairly with experimentally observed data for three ³He-induced reactions, but only after introducing two adjustable parameters (εr and εi) to control the real and imaginary components of the nuclear optical potential.

## Method
The authors employed the single-step selective resonant tunnelling model (SRTM) with a microscopically derived density-dependent double-folding potential based on M3Y-Reid nucleon-nucleon interactions and a two-parameter Fermi (Woods-Saxon) density distribution for the colliding nuclei. The complex nucleus-nucleus potential was constructed as U_N(r) = εr U_DF(r) + i εi U_iDF(r), where the imaginary component was derived from the derivative of the real double-folding potential, and the parameters εr and εi were adjusted to match experimental data. The Schrödinger equation was solved for the total potential including Coulomb and centrifugal terms, and the fusion cross-section and astrophysical S-factor were computed from the complex phase shift for the l=0 partial wave.

## Key numbers
- For ³He(³He,2p)⁴He at 31.2 keV: computed S-factor = 5.86 keV-mb (experimental: 6.40 ± 0.39 keV-mb); computed cross-section = 0.245 nb (experimental: 0.246 nb)
- For ³He(³He,2p)⁴He at 45.3 keV: computed S-factor = 4.86 keV-mb (experimental: 5.39 ± 0.09 keV-mb); computed cross-section = 15.248 nb (experimental: 15.300 nb)
- For ⁶Li(³He,d)⁷Be at 1.328 MeV: computed cross-section = 215.11 mb (experimental: 213.50 mb)
- For ⁶Li(³He,d)⁷Be at 1.900 MeV: computed cross-section = 313.40 mb (experimental: 320.80 mb)
- For ¹⁰B(³He,n)¹²N at 3.29 MeV: computed cross-section = 3.50 mb (experimental: 3.52 mb)
- Nuclear size parameter R0 values: 0.80 fm for ³He+³He, 1.71 fm for ³He+⁶Li, 1.36 fm for ³He+¹⁰B
- Fitting parameters for S(E) = S0 + S1 exp(-E/S2): for ³He(³He,2p)⁴He: S0 = 2.325 keV-mb, S1 = 4.6×10¹⁶ μb, S2 = 0.81 μb/keV

## Context
This work extends the SRTM approach previously applied by Li (2002), Li, Wei, and Liu (2008), and Liang, Dong, and Li (2015) using constant square-well potentials, and by Khan et al. (2023) who introduced a phenomenological space-varying complex potential. The key improvement is the adoption of a microscopically derived density-dependent double-folding potential (following Durant et al., 2018) instead of phenomenological potentials, which provides a smoother surface profile and better predictive power. The three reactions studied, ³He(³He,2p)⁴He, ⁶Li(³He,d)⁷Be, and ¹⁰B(³He,n)¹²N, are relevant to primordial nucleosynthesis, the pp-II and pp-III chains in stellar energy production, and the lithium problem in Big Bang nucleosynthesis, respectively. The results enable improved reaction rate inputs for stellar evolution models and nucleosynthesis network calculations.
