# Optimized Perturbation Theory at Finite Temperature--- Two-Loop Analysis---

> We study the optimized perturbation theory (OPT) at finite temperature, which is a self-consistent resummation method. Firstly, we generalize the idea of the OPT to optimize the coupling constant in lambda phi^4 theory, and give a proof of the renormalizability of this generalized OPT. Secondly, the principle of minimal sensitivity and the criterion of the fastest apparent convergence, which are conditions to determine the optimal parameter values, are examined in lambda phi^4 theory. Both conditions exhibit a second-order transition at finite temperature with critical exponent beta = 0.5 in the two-loop approximation.

## Key claim

The optimized perturbation theory (OPT) with both mass and coupling constant optimization is renormalizable at finite temperature, and both the principle of minimal sensitivity (PMS) and the fastest apparent convergence (FAC) condition yield a second-order phase transition with critical exponent beta = 0.5 in the two-loop approximation for lambda phi^4 theory, though this mean-field value indicates the approximation has not captured fluctuations beyond the mean-field level.

## Method

The author generalizes the OPT to optimize both the mass and coupling constant in lambda phi^4 theory by splitting the renormalized mass mu^2 into m^2 - chi and the coupling lambda into g - eta, where chi and eta are treated as O(delta) perturbations. The effective potential is calculated up to two-loop order using the real-time formalism with dimensional regularization and the MS-bar scheme, and the optimal parameters m^2 and g are determined by either the PMS condition (partial derivative of the effective potential with respect to m^2 equals zero) or the FAC condition (the two-loop contribution to the retarded self-energy at zero momentum equals zero).

## Key numbers

- The critical exponent beta = 0.5 was found for both PMS and FAC conditions in the two-loop approximation, matching the Landau mean-field theory prediction.
- At high temperature in the symmetric phase, the PMS condition yields the Debye screening mass m^2(T) = mu^2 + lambda T^2 / 24.
- Initial parameters for numerical calculations were lambda = 10.0 and phi_0 = 10.0, yielding mu^2 = m^2 = -170, kappa^2 = 87.6, and M^2 = 330 for the PMS condition.
- For the FAC condition with the same initial lambda = 10.0 and phi_0 = 10.0, the resulting parameters were mu^2 = m^2 = -166, kappa^2 = 137, and M^2 = 334.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous formulation of OPT at finite temperature (Chiku 1998) to include optimization of the coupling constant, and provides a proof of renormalizability that was missing in earlier approaches such as the delta-expansion studied by Oko and the work by Bedaque and Mishra. The method enables systematic resummation of hard thermal loops in scalar field theory across a wide temperature range, avoiding the tachyon problem that plagues naive perturbation theory, and provides a framework that could be extended to study phase transitions in effective models of QCD beyond the mean-field level.
