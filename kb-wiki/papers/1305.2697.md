# A Number-Conserving Theory for Nuclear Pairing

> A microscopic theory for nuclear pairing is proposed through the generalized density matrix formalism. The analytical equations are as simple as that of the BCS theory, and could be solved within a similar computer time. The current theory conserves the exact particle number, and is valid at arbitrary pairing strength (including those below the BCS critical strength). These are the two main advantages over the conventional BCS theory. The theory is also of interests to other mesoscopic systems.

# A Number-Conserving Theory for Nuclear Pairing

## Key claim
The paper proposes a microscopic theory for nuclear pairing, based on the generalized density matrix (GDM) formalism, that conserves exact particle number and yields non-trivial solutions at arbitrary pairing strength, including values below the BCS critical strength. The theory achieves this while maintaining analytical equations as simple as BCS and solvable within similar computer time.

## Method
The theory uses a pair condensate with definite particle number as the variational ground state, rather than the BCS quasi-particle vacuum. The GDM equations of motion for the one-body density matrix and pair transition amplitude are expanded in bosonic mode operators, and the lowest-order (mean-field) equations are solved. The key equation (Eq. 15) relates the binding energy difference between neighboring even-even nuclei to single-particle energies, occupation numbers, and pair emission amplitudes, and is solved for the pair structure parameters.

## Key numbers
- For ⁴⁸Ca at realistic pairing strength (G=1.0), the GDM occupation numbers are: n(f7/2)=0.9759, n(p3/2)=0.0249, n(f5/2)=0.0122, n(p1/2)=0.0098; exact shell-model values are: n(f7/2)=0.9780, n(p3/2)=0.0222, n(f5/2)=0.0116, n(p1/2)=0.0089.
- For ⁴⁸Ca at G=1.0, the GDM pair emission amplitudes are: s(f7/2)=0.5032, s(p3/2)=0.1571, s(f5/2)=0.1103, s(p1/2)=0.0988; exact values are: s(f7/2)=0.5097, s(p3/2)=0.1472, s(f5/2)=0.1067, s(p1/2)=0.0940.
- The BCS critical pairing strength for ⁴⁸Ca is G_c = 1.345; below this value BCS gives only trivial zero solutions, while GDM reproduces exact results.
- Single-particle energies used for calcium isotopes (from FPD6): epsilon(f7/2) = -8.3876 MeV, epsilon(p3/2) = -6.4952 MeV, epsilon(f5/2) = -1.8966 MeV, epsilon(p1/2) = -4.4783 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the GDM formalism originally introduced by Kerman (1963), Belyaev and Zelevinsky (1970), and others, which previously was limited to conventional BCS treatment of pairing. The theory is applied to calcium isotopes (A=42-58) using the FPD6 interaction, with comparisons to exact shell-model results from the NuShellX code. The method enables future treatment of odd-mass nuclei, spectroscopic factors, and correlations beyond mean field within the same GDM framework, and is also applicable to other mesoscopic systems.
