# Particle Number Fluctuations in Relativistic Bose and Fermi Gases

> Particle number fluctuations are studied in relativistic Bose and Fermi gases. The calculations are done within both the grand canonical and canonical ensemble. The fluctuations in the canonical ensemble are found to be different from those in the grand canonical one. Effects of quantum statistics increase in the grand canonical ensemble for large chemical potential. This is, however, not the case in the canonical ensemble. In the limit of large charge density a strongest difference between the grand canonical and canonical ensemble results is observed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that particle number fluctuations in relativistic Bose and Fermi gases are suppressed in the canonical ensemble (CE) compared to the grand canonical ensemble (GCE), and this suppression survives in the thermodynamic limit \(V\rightarrow\infty\), even though average quantities become equal between ensembles. The strongest differences between GCE and CE results occur at large charge densities, where exact charge conservation becomes more important than quantum Bose or Fermi effects.

## Method

The authors calculated particle number densities and scaled variances \(\omega^\pm = \langle \Delta N_\pm^2 \rangle / \langle N_\pm \rangle\) for positively and negatively charged particles in relativistic ideal Bose and Fermi gases, using both the grand canonical ensemble (GCE) and the canonical ensemble (CE) with exact charge conservation. In the GCE, fluctuations were derived from the standard single-mode occupation number variance \(\langle \Delta n_p^{\pm 2} \rangle = \langle n_p^\pm \rangle (1 + \gamma \langle n_p^\pm \rangle)\) with \(\gamma = +1\) for bosons and \(\gamma = -1\) for fermions. In the CE, the microscopic correlator was modified by imposing the constraint \(\Delta Q = \sum_p (\Delta n_p^+ - \Delta n_p^-) = 0\), leading to a suppression term in the scaled variance formula.

## Key numbers

- For massless particles at \(\mu^* = 0\), the GCE scaled variance for bosons is \(\omega^\pm_{g.c.e.} = \zeta(2)/\zeta(3) \simeq 1.368\), and for fermions \(\omega^\pm_{g.c.e.} = (2/3)\,\zeta(2)/\zeta(3) \simeq 0.912\).
- For Boltzmann statistics in the CE at zero net charge density, \(\omega^{+}_{c.e.} = \omega^{-}_{c.e.} = 0.5\), compared to \(\omega^{+}_{g.c.e.} = \omega^{-}_{g.c.e.} = 1\) in the GCE.
- At \(\mu^* \rightarrow m^*\) in the Bose gas, \(\omega^{+~Bose}_{g.c.e.} \propto (m^* - \mu^*)^{-1/2}\) diverges, while \(\omega^{-~Bose}_{g.c.e.}\) approaches 1 from above as \(\mu^* = m^* \rightarrow \infty\).
- For fermions at \(\mu^* \rightarrow \infty\), \(\omega^{+~Fermi}_{g.c.e.} \simeq 3/\mu^*\) (tending to 0), and \(\omega^{-~Fermi}_{g.c.e.}\) approaches 1 from below.
- The maximum Bose enhancement factor for particle number density at \(\mu^* = m^* \rightarrow \infty\) is \(\max[R_+^{Bose}] = \zeta(3/2) \simeq 2.612\).
- The Bose condensation temperature in the non-relativistic limit follows \(\widetilde{T}_C \simeq 3.313 \, \widetilde{\rho}_Q^{~2/3}\), and in the ultra-relativistic limit \(\widetilde{T}_C \simeq 1.732 \, \widetilde{\rho}_Q^{~1/2}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of particle number fluctuations in relativistic gases, which were previously restricted to the Boltzmann approximation with zero net charge (Begun et al., 2004) or to quantum gases at zero conserved charge (Begun et al., 2004). The authors show that the CE suppression of fluctuations, first noted for the Boltzmann case, persists for Bose and Fermi statistics at non-zero charge density and survives in the thermodynamic limit, unlike the suppression of average multiplicities which vanishes for large systems. This enables more accurate modeling of event-by-event fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where exact charge conservation must be accounted for even in large systems.
