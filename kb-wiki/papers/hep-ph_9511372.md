# QED in strong fields

> We discuss the status of atomic physics in strong field. We focus on the problem of electron-positron lines observed in heavy-ion collisions and on QED effects, calculated in strong Coulomb fields, especially Delbrück-scattering. We discuss the similarities and differences between these effects and channeling respectively beamstrahlung. We investigated the prospects for photon-channeling, calculated channeling from first principles on the basis of the Dirac equation, and determined the rate for electron-positron pair production in the collision of two high-energy particle pulses.

## Key claim

The paper argues that despite superficial similarities, the physics of channeling and beamstrahlung is fundamentally different from overcritical Coulomb fields in heavy-ion collisions, because the Lorentz boost that creates strong electric fields in channeling also introduces a confining magnetic field that prevents the charge separation necessary for strong-field QED effects like spontaneous pair production.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical framework for treating strong electromagnetic fields non-perturbatively by including the Coulomb potential exactly in the Dirac Hamiltonian, then solving the time-dependent Dirac equation via expansion in eigenstates of the two-ion system. For Delbrück scattering, exact propagators in the Coulomb field are used to compute vacuum polarization graphs in all orders of Zα. For channeling, the Dirac equation is solved from first principles for relativistic electrons in crystal axes, and the photon-channeling problem is treated by calculating Delbrück scattering in the axially symmetric field of a channeling axis.

## Key numbers

- The combined atomic charge threshold for overcritical binding (binding energy exceeding twice the electron rest mass) is roughly 173, depending on shielding by other electrons.
- For Delbrück scattering at photon energy ω = 1.5 m_e and scattering angle Θ = 1°, the scattering amplitude M for Z=80 is 1.63(2)×10⁻¹, compared to the Born result of 1.5×10⁻¹, showing corrections for high Z.
- For Delbrück scattering at ω = 1.5 m_e and Θ = 1°, the amplitude for Z=60 is 8.9(1)×10⁻² versus Born value 8.6×10⁻²; for Z=20 it is 9.62(8)×10⁻³ versus 9.6×10⁻³; for Z=10 it is 2.39(3)×10⁻³ versus 2.4×10⁻³.
- Theoretical and experimental results for heavy-ion collision processes agree within uncertainties of typically 10-20 percent, with the exception of δ-electron production in U+Pd collisions at 6.1 MeV/u.
- For photon-channeling, the average rotation angle Φ is very far from π (which would imply complete channeling) even for very small angles of incidence, and no substantial increase with photon energy was observed.

## Context

This talk reviews work by the Frankfurt atomic physics group (Greiner, Reinhardt, Soff, and others) on strong-field QED, extending earlier calculations of Delbrück scattering and overcritical binding in heavy-ion collisions. The paper contrasts the channeling phenomenon with the electron-positron line puzzle from GSI experiments (EPOS and Orange collaborations), which could not be reproduced by improved experiments including APEX at Argonne, and may instead be explained by nuclear pair conversion. The results enable a clearer theoretical distinction between different strong-field regimes and rule out the possibility of observing spontaneous pair production or photon channeling in currently accessible channeling experiments.
