# Fusion of light proton-rich exotic nuclei at near-barrier energies

> We study theoretically fusion of the light proton-rich exotic nuclei $^{17}$F and $^8$B at near-barrier energies in order to investigate the possible role of breakup processes on their fusion cross sections. To this end, coupled channel calculations are performed considering the couplings to the breakup channels of these projectiles. In case of $^{17}$F, the coupling arising out of the inelastic excitation from the ground state to the bound excited state and its couplings to the continuum have also been taken into consideration. It is found that the inelastic excitation/breakup of $^{17}$F affect the fusion cross sections very nominally even for a heavy target like Pb. On the other hand, calculations for fusion of the one-proton halo nucleus $^8$B on a Pb target show a significant suppression of the complete fusion cross section above the Coulomb barrier. This is due to the larger breakup probability of $^8$B as compared to that of $^{17}$F. However, even for $^8$B, there is little change in the complete fusion cross sections as compared to the no-coupling case at sub-barrier energies.

## Key claim

Coupled-channel calculations show that breakup/inelastic excitation of the proton-rich nucleus 17F has a negligible effect on its fusion cross sections even on a heavy Pb target, whereas for the one-proton halo nucleus 8B the complete fusion cross section is significantly suppressed above the Coulomb barrier; however, at sub-barrier energies even 8B shows little change in complete fusion compared to the no-coupling case.

## Method

Coupled-channel calculations were performed using the incoming wave boundary condition method, with the projectile treated as a core plus a single valence proton. The continuum was discretized into 10 bins of 200 keV width up to 2 MeV, and couplings to breakup channels were included for multipolarities 1 and 2, considering s-, p-, d- and f-waves in the continuum. For 17F, the bound excited state at 495 keV above the ground state was also included, with its couplings to the continuum. The target was assumed inert in all cases.

## Key numbers

- For 8B on Pb at 60 MeV centre-of-mass energy, the complete fusion cross section is approximately 69% of the total (complete + incomplete) fusion cross section.
- The one-proton separation energy in 17F is 0.6 MeV, approximately 4.5 times the 0.137 MeV separation energy in 8B.
- The rms proton radius of 17F is 3.7 fm; the rms proton radius of 8B is 4.24 fm.
- The semiclassical E1 breakup cross section of 8B on Pb at 60 MeV laboratory energy is 5.1 barns, close to 4.6 barns for 11Be on the same target at the same energy.
- The E1 dissociation cross section for 17F on Pb at near-barrier energy is 46.46 mb only.
- At sub-barrier energies, the total (complete + incomplete) fusion cross section for 8B shows approximately 20% increase compared to the zero-coupling case.
- For 8B on Pb at 60 MeV, including only dipole transitions increases the complete fusion cross section by about 4% compared to the full (dipole + quadrupole) calculation.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Hagino, Vitturi, Dasso and Lenzi (2000), who studied neutron-halo nuclei 11Be and 6He on Pb, to the proton-rich systems 17F and 8B. The calculations for 17F are compared with the first fusion measurements for that nucleus by Rehm et al. (1998). The results show that the suppression of complete fusion above the barrier for 8B is similar to that seen for the one-neutron halo nucleus 11Be, but the sub-barrier enhancement is much smaller for 8B. The paper suggests experimental measurement of the different fusion components for 8B on a heavy target.
