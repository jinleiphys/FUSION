# The Spectral Density of the Dirac Operator above T_c

> The importance of the spectral density of the Dirac operator in studying spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and anomalous U(1) axial symmetry breaking are reviewed. It is shown that both types of symmetry breaking can be traced to effects of modes near zero virtuality. Above T_c, where chiral symmetry is restored, it is shown on general grounds that (in the massless quark limit), the density of states vanishes at zero virtuality faster than $λ$, where $λ$ is the virtuality-- $ρ(λ) \sim |λ|^α$ is not possible for $α\le 1$. Isospin invariance is used to show that $ρ(λ) \sim m_q^{1-α} |λ|^α$ is also not possible for $α\le 1$. State-of-the-art lattice calculations are reviewed in light of these constraints. In particular, it is argued that violations of these constraints by lattice calculations indicate possible large systematic errors; this raises questions about $U(1)_A$ violating effects seen on the lattice.It is also shown that above $T_c$, the Dirac spectrum has a gap near zero (in the $m_q \to 0$ limit) unless contributions from quark-line-connected and disconnected contributions conspire to cancel.

## Key claim

Above the chiral restoration temperature \(T_c\), the spectral density of the Dirac operator must vanish at zero virtuality faster than linearly in the massless quark limit; a power-law form \(\rho(\lambda) \sim |\lambda|^\alpha\) with \(\alpha \le 1\) is forbidden, and a form \(\rho(\lambda) \sim m_q^{1-\alpha} |\lambda|^\alpha\) with \(\alpha \le 1\) is also ruled out by isospin invariance, raising questions about lattice results that appear to violate these constraints.

## Method

The paper uses analytic reasoning based on the Euclidean functional integral formulation of QCD at finite temperature, applying the Banks-Casher relation and Ward identities to derive constraints on the Dirac spectral density \(\rho(\lambda)\) in the chirally restored phase. It analyzes the behavior of the chiral condensate, the pion susceptibility \(\chi_\pi\), the \(U(1)_A\) susceptibility \(\chi_{U(1)_A}\), and the mixed-flavor susceptibility \(\chi_{ud}\) as functions of quark mass and virtuality, considering both quark-line-connected and disconnected contributions. The constraints are then compared to lattice data from Chandrasekharan and Christ (1997) and Bernard et al. (1997) that extracted \(\rho(\lambda)\) via a partially quenched calculation of \(f(m_\xi) = \int d\lambda \, \rho(\lambda) / (i\lambda + m_\xi)\).

## Key numbers

- The Banks-Casher relation gives \(\langle \overline{q} q \rangle = \pi \rho(0)\) in the limit \(m_q \to 0\).
- For a power-law form \(\rho(\lambda) = c |\lambda|^\alpha\) with \(\alpha \le 1\), the pion susceptibility \(\chi_\pi\) diverges as \(c \, \pi / [m_q^{1-\alpha} \cos(\alpha \pi/2)]\) in the chiral limit, contradicting chiral restoration.
- For the form \(\rho(\lambda) = b \, m_q^{1-\alpha} |\lambda|^\alpha\), the \(U(1)_A\) susceptibility becomes \(\chi_{U(1)_A} = (1-\alpha) \chi_\pi\), but isospin invariance forces a contradiction unless isospin is spontaneously broken.
- Lattice data from Chandrasekharan and Christ show \(f(m_\xi) \sim m_\xi^\alpha\) with \(\alpha < 1\) over several decades, implying \(\rho(\lambda) \sim |\lambda|^\alpha\) with \(\alpha < 1\), which violates the derived constraints.

## Context

This work extends the Banks-Casher (1980) relation connecting the chiral condensate to the Dirac spectral density at zero virtuality, and builds on earlier arguments by Cohen (1996) that all \(U(1)_A\)-violating amplitudes derive from modes near \(\lambda=0\). It directly addresses the question raised by Shuryak (1993) whether \(U(1)_A\) effects vanish above \(T_c\), and the subsequent debate involving Lee and Hatsuda (1996) and Evans, Hsu, and Schwetz (1996) about whether a dilute instanton gas could produce a measure-zero contribution. The paper critiques lattice calculations by Chandrasekharan and Christ (1997) and Bernard et al. (1997) that claim nonzero \(U(1)_A\) violation above \(T_c\), arguing that the spectral densities implied by those calculations violate analyticity constraints from chiral restoration and isospin symmetry, suggesting the results may be dominated by lattice artifacts from staggered fermions. The constraints derived here provide a theoretical test for future lattice studies of the Dirac spectrum above \(T_c\).
