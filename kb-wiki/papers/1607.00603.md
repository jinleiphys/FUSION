# Forward $J/ψ$ production in pA collisions: centrality dependence

> The nuclear suppression of forward $J/ψ$ production in high energy proton-nucleus collisions can be used as a probe of gluon saturation at small $x$. In an earlier work we studied this suppression in minimum bias collisions in the Color Glass Condensate formalism, relying on the optical Glauber model to obtain the dipole cross section of the nucleus from the one of the proton fitted to HERA DIS data. Here we study how the impact parameter dependence of this model can be used to compare our results with recent LHC data on the centrality dependence of this suppression.

# Forward J/ψ production in pA collisions: centrality dependence

## Key claim

The centrality dependence of forward J/ψ suppression in proton-nucleus collisions can be studied within the Color Glass Condensate formalism using the optical Glauber model, but relating the impact parameter dependence of the model to experimental centrality classes is not straightforward and leads to a too strong variation of the nuclear modification factor with centrality, particularly in peripheral collisions.

## Method

The authors compute J/ψ production in the Color Glass Condensate formalism using the Color Evaporation Model for hadronization, where a fixed fraction of charm quark pairs with invariant mass below twice the D-meson mass form J/ψ bound states. The proton dipole cross section is obtained from the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation with running coupling corrections using the MV^e parametrization fitted to HERA DIS data, and the nuclear dipole cross section is obtained via the optical Glauber model using the Woods-Saxon distribution for the nuclear density. The centrality dependence is studied by relating impact parameter to the number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions, using experimental estimates from ALICE to define centrality classes.

## Key numbers

- Average number of binary collisions in the optical Glauber model for the 2-10% centrality class: 14.7, compared to the ALICE estimate of 11.7 ± 1.2 ± 0.9
- Average number of binary collisions in the optical Glauber model for the 60-80% centrality class: 3.7, compared to the ALICE estimate of 4.3 ± 0.3 ± 0.3
- Impact parameter corresponding to the 60-80% centrality class: 6.29 fm
- Impact parameter corresponding to the 80-100% centrality class: 6.91 fm
- The nuclear modification factor Q_pA reaches approximately 1 at N_coll ~ 4 in the fixed impact parameter approximation, significantly above the ALICE data
- The saturation scale of the nucleus becomes as small as that of the proton at impact parameter ~ 6.3 fm, corresponding to N_coll ~ 4.3

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous minimum bias study (Ducloue 2015) of forward J/ψ suppression in the Color Glass Condensate formalism, which showed improved agreement with LHC data compared to an earlier study by Fujii (2013). The results are compared with recent ALICE measurements (Adam 2015) of the centrality dependence of nuclear suppression. The paper identifies that the too strong centrality dependence in the model arises because the effective transverse area of the proton extracted from HERA DIS fits is much smaller than the total inelastic proton-proton cross section, as noted in Lappi (2013). Future progress requires unbiased experimental determinations of N_coll distributions in each centrality class.
