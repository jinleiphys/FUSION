# Pairing and the structure of the pf-shell N ~ Z nuclei

> The influence of the isoscalar and isovector L=0 pairing components of the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction is evaluated for several isobaric chains, in the framework of full pf shell model calculations. We show that the combined effect of both isospin channels of the pairing force is responsible for the appearance of T=1 ground states in N=Z odd-odd nuclei. However, no evidence is found relating them to the Wigner energy. We study the dependence of their contributions to the total energy on the rotational frecuency in the deformed nucleus 48Cr. Both decrease with increasing angular momentum and go to zero at the band termination. Below the backbending their net effect is a reduction of the moment of inertia, more than half of which comes from the proton-neutron channel.

# Pairing and the structure of the pf-shell N ~ Z nuclei

## Key claim
The combined effect of both isoscalar (P10) and isovector (P01) L=0 pairing components of the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction is responsible for the appearance of T=1 ground states in N=Z odd-odd nuclei, but no evidence is found relating them to the Wigner energy. The authors show that the isoscalar pairing contribution to the ground state binding energy may be very important, yet it is not at the origin of the Wigner energy, nor is the isovector term.

## Method
Full pf-shell model calculations were performed using the Lanczos shell model code ANTOINE with the effective interaction KB3. The authors subtracted the isovector (P01) and isoscalar (P10) pairing Hamiltonians from KB3 to create interactions KB3-P01 and KB3-P10, then compared results to the reference KB3 calculation for isobaric chains A=46, 48, and 50. The pairing operators were defined following Dufour and Zuker (1996), using pairing constants G = -0.295 for P01 and G = -0.459 for P10.

## Key numbers
- For A=48, the Wigner energy epsilon_W = 2.82 MeV (KB3), 3.04 MeV (KB3-P01), 2.47 MeV (KB3-P10)
- For A=50, the Wigner energy epsilon_W = 1.99 MeV (KB3), 2.60 MeV (KB3-P01), 2.12 MeV (KB3-P10)
- The isovector monopole gap puts the centroid of T=0 states lower than T=1 states by about 1.3 MeV in A=46
- The total pairing contribution to T=1 is larger by more than 1.5 MeV than the contribution to T=0 in A=46
- In 48Cr, at J=0 the P01 contribution almost doubles the P10 contribution; both go to zero at the aligned J=16 state
- The P10 contribution to the moment of inertia of 48Cr amounts to about one fourth of the P01 contribution
- The squared overlap of the isovector pairing condensate with the physical ground state in 48Cr is 38%

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of isovector pairing among like particles and proton-neutron pairing, building on the algebraic model and Shell Model Monte Carlo calculations of Langanke (1993) and the characterization of multipole pairing by Dufour and Zuker (1996). The results contradict suggestions that isoscalar pairing might explain the Wigner energy, as discussed in works by Satula and Wyss (1997). The findings enable improved understanding of N=Z rotors, explaining discrepancies between Cranked Hartree Fock Bogolyubov calculations using the Gogny force and shell model results for the yrast band of 48Cr, as reported by Caurier et al. (1995). The paper also rules out the existence of isoscalar pairing condensates in the pf-shell region due to spin-orbit splitting of the nuclear mean field.
