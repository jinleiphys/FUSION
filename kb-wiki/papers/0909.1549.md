# Interplay of shear and bulk viscosity in generating flow in heavy-ion collisions

> We perform viscous hydrodynamic calculations in 2+1 dimensions to investigate the influence of bulk viscosity on the viscous suppression of elliptic flow in non-central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energies. Bulk and shear viscous effects on the evolution of radial and elliptic flow are studied with different model assumptions for the transport coefficients. We find that the temperature dependence of the relaxation time for the bulk viscous pressure, especially its critical slowing down near the quark-hadron phase transition at T_c, partially offsets effects from the strong growth of the bulk viscosity itself near T_c, and that even small values of the specific shear viscosity eta/s of the fireball matter can be extracted without large uncertainties from poorly controlled bulk viscous effects.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that bulk viscosity's effect on elliptic flow suppression is partially offset by critical slowing down of the bulk viscous pressure relaxation time near the quark-hadron phase transition, allowing extraction of small specific shear viscosity values without large uncertainties from poorly controlled bulk viscous effects, provided the specific bulk viscosity and its relaxation time are proportional.

## Method
The authors performed (2+1)-dimensional causal relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the VISH2+1 code with boost-invariant longitudinal expansion, solving Israel-Stewart equations for Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies. They employed a lattice QCD-based equation of state (EOS L) with Glauber model initial conditions at proper time 0.6 fm/c, and studied various parametrizations for the temperature dependence of specific bulk viscosity ζ/s and its relaxation time τ_Π, including a "minimal bulk viscosity" parametrization based on the Buchel bound and scaled versions thereof.

## Key numbers
- Specific shear viscosity used: η/s = 1/(4π) ≈ 0.08 (the KSS bound)
- Peak value of minimal specific bulk viscosity at T_c: (ζ/s)(T_c) ≈ 0.04
- Relaxation time parametrization for bulk pressure: τ_Π(T) = max[120 fm/c · (ζ/s)(T), 0.1 fm]
- Constant relaxation time values studied for comparison: τ_Π = 0.5 fm/c and 5 fm/c
- Initial time for hydrodynamic evolution: τ_0 = 0.6 fm/c
- Peak energy density in central collisions: e_0 = 30 GeV/fm³
- Freeze-out temperature: T_dec = 130 MeV
- Impact parameter for non-central collisions: b = 7 fm
- For constant η/s = 1/(4π), bulk viscosity increases viscous suppression of v_2(p_T) by 5–50% depending on initial conditions and relaxation time details
- With proportional ζ/s and τ_Π, the uncertainty range reduces to 10–20%
- Upper limit on effective η/s of QGP: η/s|_QGP < 5 × 1/(4π)

## Context
This work extends earlier (0+1)-dimensional studies of bulk viscosity in boost-invariant expansion without transverse flow by Torrieri, Tomášik and Mishustin (2007), Torrieri (2008), Fries (2008), and Rajagopal (2009), which predicted cavitation effects near the phase transition. The authors improve on these by including realistic transverse density profiles and transverse flow, finding more restrictive stability constraints. The paper enables more reliable extraction of the specific shear viscosity from elliptic flow data by quantifying the contamination from bulk viscous effects, and motivates further theoretical work to constrain the temperature dependence of bulk viscosity and its relaxation time.
