# Thermal D mesons from anisotropic lattice QCD

> We present results for correlators and spectral functions of open charm mesons using 2+1 flavours of clover fermions on anisotropic lattices. The D mesons are found to dissociate close to the deconfinement crossover temperature Tc. Our preliminary results suggest a shift in the thermal D meson mass below Tc. Mesons containing strange quarks exhibit smaller thermal modifications than those containing light quarks.

# Thermal D mesons from anisotropic lattice QCD

## Key claim
The paper presents evidence that D mesons dissociate close to the deconfinement crossover temperature \(T_c\), with preliminary results suggesting a thermal mass shift below \(T_c\). The authors note that the dissociation appears to occur already at \(T = 0.95 T_c\), but this temperature lies within the broad crossover region for their parameters, so the result is conditional on the crossover nature of the transition.

## Method
The study uses 2+1 flavours of anisotropic clover fermions on FASTSUM collaboration ensembles with spatial lattice spacing \(a_s = 0.123\) fm and anisotropy \(\xi = a_s / a_\tau = 3.5\). Temporal correlators and spectral functions for pseudoscalar and vector channels of light–charm (\(D, D^*\)) and strange–charm (\(D_s, D_s^*\)) mesons are computed using the maximum entropy method (MEM) with a Fourier basis, cross-checked against standard SVD basis results. Reconstructed correlators are computed directly from the lowest-temperature correlator at \(T_r = 44\) MeV (\(N_\tau = 128\)) to assess thermal modifications without extracting spectral functions.

## Key numbers
- Spatial lattice spacing: \(a_s = 0.123\) fm
- Anisotropy: \(\xi = a_s / a_\tau = 3.5\)
- Pion mass: \(m_\pi \approx 380\) MeV
- Temperatures studied range from 44 MeV (\(T/T_c = 0.24\)) to 352 MeV (\(T/T_c = 1.90\)), with \(T_c\) determined from the Polyakov loop inflection point
- Reference temperature for reconstructed correlators: \(T_r = 44\) MeV (\(N_\tau = 128\))
- Statistical uncertainties in correlators are of order 1%

## Context
This work extends prior lattice studies of open charm at high temperature by Bazavov (2014) and Mukherjee (2015), which used spatial correlators and cumulants, by presenting the first temporal correlator and spectral function results for open charm mesons. The findings support theoretical predictions that \(D_s\) yields may be enhanced relative to \(D\) yields in heavy ion collisions, consistent with emerging experimental evidence from Adam (2015). The results enable future studies with improved statistics using multiple sources per configuration and cross-checks with alternative methods such as the BR method.
