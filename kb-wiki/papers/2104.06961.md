# Unified quark-hadron EoS and critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram

> We present a recent development towards a unified description of quark-hadron matter in the QCD phase diagram that is based on a cluster decomposition of the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach to quark matter, selfconsistently coupled to Polyakov-loop and mesonic background fields. The Mott dissociation of hadrons under extreme conditions of temperature and density is triggered by chiral symmetry restoration and confining aspects are modeled by the coupling to the background mean fields. First results for the QCD phase diagram with the capability to describe critical endpoints as well as crossover-all-over are presented and an excellent agreement with lattice QCD on the temperature axis is obtained.

# Unified quark-hadron EoS and critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram

## Key claim
The cluster decomposition of the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach, self-consistently coupled to Polyakov-loop and mesonic background fields, can describe both a critical endpoint and a crossover-all-over scenario for the deconfinement transition, depending on model parameters, a capability not possible within two-phase approaches employing Maxwell construction.

## Method
The total thermodynamic potential is decomposed into a QGP part (quark quasiparticles plus Polyakov-loop mean field plus perturbative QCD) and a modified hadron resonance gas (MHRG) part. The MHRG pressure is computed via a Beth-Uhlenbeck formula with a simple step-function phase shift that implements the Mott dissociation of hadrons when their mass reaches the continuum threshold, triggered by chiral symmetry restoration. For the high-temperature case at vanishing chemical potential, the threshold masses are fit to lattice QCD chiral condensate data; for the low-temperature case, an effective density functional determines quasiparticle dispersion relations for quarks and nucleons via self-consistent gap equations.

## Key numbers
- Pseudocritical temperature from lattice QCD: \( T_c = 156.5 \pm 1.5 \) MeV (Bazavov 2018)
- Mott dissociation of hadrons occurs in the temperature region \( 150 < T [\text{MeV}] < 200 \) at vanishing chemical potential
- Critical endpoint for the deconfinement transition in the low-temperature case: \( T_\mathrm c = 43 \) MeV and \( \mu_\mathrm c = 909 \) MeV
- Simple HRG model alone describes lattice QCD data well up to \( T \sim 200 \) MeV, beyond the pseudocritical temperature

## Context
This work extends the cluster expansion approach of the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck formalism (Schmidt 1990, Hufner 1994, Blaschke 2013, Blaschke 2016) to unify hadron and quark matter descriptions. It contrasts with two-phase models using Maxwell construction (Satz 2018) and interpolation methods (Asakawa 1995, Albright 2014, Parotto 2018) that cannot capture microphysical details of bound state dissociation. The approach enables future applications to heavy-ion collision simulations and compact star astrophysics, including neutron star mergers and supernovae, and can be extended to finite isospin asymmetries and parity doubling effects (Marczenko 2020).
