# Tagged jets and jet reconstruction as a probe of the quark-gluon plasma

> The large center-of-mass energies available to the heavy-ion program at the LHC and recent experimental advances at RHIC will enable QCD matter at very high temperatures and energy densities, that is, the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), to be probed in unprecedented ways. Fully-reconstructed inclusive jets and the away-side hadron showers associated with electroweak bosons, that is, tagged jets, are among these exciting new probes. Full jet reconstruction provides an experimental window into the mechanisms of quark and gluon dynamics in the QGP which is not accessible via leading particles and leading particle correlations. Theoretical advances in these exciting new fields of research can help resolve some of the most controversial points in heavy ion physics today such as the significance of the radiative, collisional and dissociative processes in the QGP and the applicability of strong versus weak coupling regimes to describe jet production and propagation. In this proceedings, I will present results on the production and subsequent suppression of high energy jets tagged with Z bosons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies using the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) parton energy loss approach.

## Key claim

Tagged jets with Z bosons can calibrate the initial parton energy in heavy-ion collisions at leading order, but next-to-leading order (NLO) smearing from extra jet production compromises this exact correspondence, requiring careful experimental treatment.

## Method

The author uses the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) parton energy loss formalism to compute medium-induced radiative energy loss for jets associated with Z0/γ* → μ+μ- in Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 4 TeV per nucleon pair. NLO calculations are performed using the MCFM Monte Carlo code developed by Campbell and Ellis, with results compared for different jet reconstruction cone radii R = 0.2 and R = 0.8. The analysis also examines jet RAA as a function of cone size for central Cu+Cu collisions at √s = 200 GeV per nucleon.

## Key numbers

- Tagging Z0/γ* is required to have 7 < pT < 13 GeV in p+p collisions at √s = 4 TeV
- Results are shown for jet reconstruction radii R = 0.2 and R = 0.8 in Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 4 TeV per nucleon pair
- Jet RAA predictions are given for most central Cu+Cu collisions at √s = 200 GeV per nucleon, showing sensitivity of suppression on cone size R

## Context

This work extends the study of jet quenching beyond leading particle suppression measurements at RHIC (Gyulassy 2003, Jacobs 2004) by proposing tagged jets and jet shapes as more differential observables. The author notes that leading particle suppression alone cannot discriminate between partonic energy loss formalisms or extract quantitative jet quenching properties (Bass 2008). The results enable future experimental measurements at LHC and upgraded RHIC to probe the angular distribution of medium-induced radiation and test the applicability of strong versus weak coupling regimes for jet production and propagation.
