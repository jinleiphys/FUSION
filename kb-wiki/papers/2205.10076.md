# The proton structure in and out of muonic hydrogen

> Laser spectroscopy of muonic atoms has been recently used to probe properties of light nuclei with unprecedented precision. We introduce nuclear effects in hydrogen-like atoms, nucleon structure quantities (form factors, structure functions, polarizabilities) and their effects in the Lamb shift and hyperfine splitting (HFS) of muonic hydrogen ($μ$H). Updated theory predictions for the Lamb shift and HFS in $μ$H are presented. We review the challenges of the ongoing effort to measure the ground-state HFS in $μ$H and its impact on our understanding of the nucleon spin structure. To narrow down this search, we present a novel theory prediction obtained by scaling the measured HFS in hydrogen leveraging radiative corrections. We also summarize recent developments in the spectroscopy of simple atomic and molecular systems and emphasize how they allow for precise determinations of fundamental constants, bound-state QED tests and New Physics searches.

## Key claim

The paper presents updated theory predictions for the Lamb shift and hyperfine splitting (HFS) in muonic hydrogen, including a novel theory prediction for the ground-state HFS obtained by scaling the measured HFS in hydrogen using radiative corrections, which narrows the search range for ongoing experiments.

## Method

The authors introduce nuclear effects in hydrogen-like atoms by describing nucleon structure quantities (form factors, structure functions, polarizabilities) and their contributions to the Lamb shift and hyperfine splitting. They compute finite-size effects using elastic form factors (electric and magnetic) and treat polarizability effects through two-photon exchange diagrams involving inelastic structure functions. The novel HFS prediction is derived by scaling the measured hydrogen HFS using radiative corrections to obtain a muonic hydrogen value.

## Key numbers

- The enhancement factor of muonic hydrogen sensitivity to proton structure compared to hydrogen is of order (m_mu/m_e)^3 approximately 10^7
- The proton charge radius from muonic hydrogen was 7 sigma smaller than the CODATA 2010 value
- The CREMA Collaboration aims to measure the ground-state hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen with 1 ppm relative accuracy
- The hyperfine splitting resonance is two orders of magnitude narrower than the 2S-2P line width

## Context

This work extends the proton-radius puzzle investigations initiated by the CREMA Collaboration (Pohl 2010, Antognini 2013), which found a proton charge radius 7 sigma smaller than the CODATA 2010 value from electron-proton scattering and hydrogen spectroscopy. The paper reviews subsequent measurements including muonic deuterium (CREMA 2016), new hydrogen spectroscopy results (Fleurbaey 2018, Grinin 2020, Bezginov 2019, Beyer 2017, Brandt 2021), and the PRad electron-proton scattering experiment (Xiong 2019). The updated theory predictions enable refined determinations of fundamental constants, bound-state QED tests, and New Physics searches, while the novel HFS prediction guides ongoing experimental searches at CREMA, J-PARC (Sato 2014), and RIKEN-RAL (Adamczak 2001).
