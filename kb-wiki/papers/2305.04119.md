# Relativistic Bulk Rheology: From Neutron Star Mergers to Viscous Cosmology

> We develop the first causal and stable theory of a bulk-viscous relativistic pseudoplastic (or dilatant) fluid. This new formalism brings to light the rheological properties of several relativistic physical systems. Neutron star collisions can behave as a relativistic pseudoplastic material with viscous properties dictated by the non-conservation of lepton currents due to weak decay. Two-temperature relativistic plasmas, such as those surrounding supermassive galactic black holes, are predominantly pseudoplastic. Our framework can also be employed to construct novel viscous models for the evolution of the Universe with pseudoplastic or dilatant features.

## Key claim

The authors develop the first causal and stable relativistic theory of bulk-viscous pseudoplastic (or dilatant) fluids, showing that the Israel-Stewart equation can be extended to arbitrarily large bulk stress values (not just near equilibrium) by allowing the relaxation time and bulk viscosity coefficient to become rheological functions of the bulk stress itself.

## Method

The authors start from a general framework where a fluid's macroscopic state is described by four fields (4-velocity, energy density, baryon density, and a non-equilibrium excursion parameter phi) and the equations of motion include a relaxation equation for phi. By inverting the relation between phi and the bulk stress Pi, they rewrite the system exactly into an Israel-Stewart-like form where the transport coefficients tau_Pi and zeta become functions of Pi itself. They then apply this formalism to three physical systems: neutron star matter (where phi is the charge fraction Y), two-temperature plasmas (where phi is the electron pressure), and cosmological fluids (where phi is the energy density of one component in a two-fluid mixture).

## Key numbers

- For the neutron star matter toy model: tau_Pi = C (constant), zeta = [n^(3/2)/2 + 2*Pi]*C, where n is baryon density and Pi is bulk stress
- For the two-temperature plasma model: tau_Pi = C, zeta = (2C/81)*(rho - n + 63*Pi), with equilibrium pressure P_eq = (4/9)*(rho - n)
- For the cosmological two-fluid model: tau_Pi = C, zeta/C = [rho*alpha*(w1-w2)^2/(1+alpha)^2] + [1 + (w1+alpha*w2)/(1+alpha)]*Pi, where w1>w2>0 are constant equation-of-state parameters and alpha>0 is the equilibrium ratio
- The resummed bulk viscosity for the neutron star model at late times is zeta_res = zeta_NS / (1 + (1/2)*tau_Pi*gradient_mu_u^mu), where zeta_NS = P_eq*tau_Pi/2

## Context

This work extends the Israel-Stewart theory of bulk viscosity (Hiscock and Lindblom, 1983; Denicol et al., 2012) to the far-from-equilibrium regime by incorporating pseudoplasticity, which was previously only studied in non-relativistic rheology (Steffe, 1996). The formalism connects to the Hydro+ effective theory framework of Stephanov and Yin (2017) and provides a rigorous foundation for recent numerical studies of bulk viscosity in neutron star mergers (Camelio et al., 2022; Most et al., 2022). The results enable the computation of rheological transport coefficients directly from microphysics for neutron star matter, black hole accretion plasmas, and cosmological models, going beyond the linear response analyses of Gavassino et al. (2020) and Celora et al. (2022).
