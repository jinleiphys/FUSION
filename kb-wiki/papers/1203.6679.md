# Hyperon/meson ratios in rare high-multiplicity $pp$ collisions at energies available at the Large Hadron Collider, and potential signatures for mini-quark-gluon plasma formation

> We use the framework of the HIJING/BB v2.0 model to simulate high-multiplicity (HM) $p+p$ collision events at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) to study observables sensitive to possible collective phenomena, such as strong longitudinal color fields (SLCF) modeled by an enhanced string tension ($κ$). We focus on the hyperon/meson yield ratios at center-of-mass (c.m.) energy $\sqrt{s}$ = 7 TeV, in the transverse momentum region, $1 < p_T < 4 $ GeV/{\it c}. For minimum bias events these ratios are well described assuming an energy dependence $κ= κ(s)= κ_{0} (s/s_{0})^{0.04} {\rm GeV/fm}$ ($κ_{0}$= 1 GeV/fm), giving a value $κ= 2$ GeV/fm at $\sqrt{s}$ = 7 TeV. We compare minimum bias (MB) events to simulated HM events assuming that $κ(MB)=2$ GeV/fm could grow to an extreme value of $κ(HM)=5$ GeV/fm that saturates the strangeness suppression factor. With this assumption the model predicts a very strong enhancement of (multi)strange baryon/meson ratios in HM events. If observed, such an enhancement could be also interpreted as a possible signature for formation in HM $p+p$ collision events of a deconfined but out of local thermal equilibrium {\em mini quark-gluon plasma} (mQGP).

## Key claim

The paper proposes that if high-multiplicity (HM) proton-proton collisions at LHC energies show a very strong enhancement of (multi)strange baryon/meson ratios, this could be interpreted as a possible signature for formation of a deconfined but out of local thermal equilibrium mini quark-gluon plasma (mQGP), but only under the assumption that the effective string tension grows from 2 GeV/fm in minimum bias events to an extreme value of 5 GeV/fm in HM events.

## Method

The authors used the HIJING/BB v2.0 model, which combines collinear-factorized pQCD multiple minijet production with soft longitudinal string excitation and hadronization, to simulate minimum bias and high-multiplicity proton-proton collision events at center-of-mass energy 7 TeV. They replaced the default vacuum string tension (1 GeV/fm) with an energy-dependent effective string tension following a power law kappa(s) = kappa_0 (s/s_0)^0.04 GeV/fm, and for HM events they tested an extreme value of kappa = 5 GeV/fm that saturates the strangeness suppression factor via the Schwinger formula. The analysis focused on hyperon/meson yield ratios in the transverse momentum region 1 < p_T < 4 GeV/c at mid-rapidity.

## Key numbers

- The effective string tension at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV for minimum bias events is kappa = 2 GeV/fm, derived from the energy dependence kappa(s) = kappa_0 (s/s_0)^0.04 GeV/fm with kappa_0 = 1 GeV/fm
- For HM events, an extreme value of kappa = 5 GeV/fm is assumed, which increases the strangeness suppression factor gamma_s from 0.4 (at kappa_0 = 1 GeV/fm) to approximately 0.85
- The predicted enhancement for (p+anti-p)/(pi+ + pi-) in HM events with kappa = 5 GeV/fm is a multiplicative factor of approximately 3 relative to MB results with kappa = 2 GeV/fm
- The predicted enhancement for (Lambda+anti-Lambda)/(pi+ + pi-) is up to a factor of four
- The predicted enhancement for (Omega- + Omega+)/(pi+ + pi-) is up to a factor of twenty
- The predicted enhancement for (Omega- + Omega+)/2 phi is up to a factor of twenty
- At sqrt(s) = 0.9 TeV, the model gives kappa = 1.7 GeV/fm using the new parametrization

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies using the HIJING/BB v2.0 model (Topor Pop 2010) that introduced strong longitudinal color field effects in proton-proton collisions up to LHC energies, but with a weaker energy dependence (exponent 0.04 instead of 0.06) to better describe recent ALICE and CMS data on multi-strange baryon yields. The predictions for HM events contrast with PYTHIA event generator results (Sjostrand 2007, Skands 2010, Field 2010), which strongly underestimate strange particle production, and with the EPOS model (Werner 2010) that also predicts mini-plasma formation in proton-proton collisions. If confirmed by LHC measurements of identified particles over a larger p_T range, these enhancements could provide evidence for out-of-equilibrium mQGP formation distinct from the strongly coupled plasma produced in central nucleus-nucleus collisions.
