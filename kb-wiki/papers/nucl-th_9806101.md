# Realistic Effective Interactions and Large-Scale Nuclear Structure Calculation

> We describe the properties of complex nuclei, such as the Sn isotopes with mass numbers A = 100 - 132, in terms of the free nucleon--nucleon interaction as obtained from meson--exchange theory. This amounts to first calculating an effective interaction in which the free interaction is modified by the presence of the appropriate nuclear medium. The short--range correlations are included within the framework of Brueckner theory yielding the nuclear reaction matrix and the long--range correlations by using the reaction matrix in many--body perturbation theory to obtain an effective interaction. The resulting effective interaction is then employed in calculating the nuclear properties. Particular emphasis is placed on the ability of our calculation to describe systematic trends of the properties of these nuclei. Both successful achievements and problematic features are pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a realistic effective interaction derived from a modern meson-exchange nucleon-nucleon potential, without any parameter adjustment, can reproduce the near-constant 0+–2+ excitation energy spacing across the tin isotopes from A=102 to A=132, but the calculated relative binding energies show clear deviations from experiment, indicating problems with the many-body methods or the potential itself.

## Method

The authors start from the charge-dependent Bonn CD nucleon-nucleon potential and first calculate the Brueckner G-matrix using a harmonic-oscillator basis with oscillator energy ħΩ = 7.87 MeV. They then include long-range correlations by using the G-matrix in a folded-diagram many-body perturbation theory, including all non-folded diagrams through third order in G, to obtain an effective two-body interaction for the valence space consisting of the 2s1/2, 1d5/2, 1d3/2, 0g7/2, and 0h11/2 orbits. This effective interaction is used in large-scale shell-model calculations (Lanczos algorithm, dimensions up to ~2×10^7) for Sn isotopes, treating light isotopes (A<116) as valence neutrons above a Z=50, N=50 core and heavy isotopes (A>116) as valence neutron holes below a Z=50, N=82 core.

## Context

This work extends the application of realistic effective interactions from nuclei with few valence particles (such as oxygen and calcium isotopes, as done by earlier authors) to the much larger system of tin isotopes with many valence neutrons, testing both the shell-model algorithm and the reliability of the effective interaction. The authors explicitly refrain from parametrizing or adjusting the interaction to fit data, unlike many current shell-model calculations. The results enable future work on including effective three-body forces (deferred to a later paper by the same group) and on improving the treatment of intruder orbits and radial wave functions. The overbinding problem is discussed in the context of similar findings by Andreozzi and collaborators.
