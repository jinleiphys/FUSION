# A Global View of the EDM Landscape

> Permanent electric dipole moments (EDMs) are sensitive probes of the symmetry structure of elementary particles, which in turn is closely tied to the baryon asymmetry in the universe. A meaningful interpretation framework for EDM measurements has to be based on effective quantum field theory. We interpret the measurements performed to date in terms of a hadronic-scale Lagrangian, using the SFitter global analysis framework. We find that part of this Lagrangian is constrained very well, while some of the parameters suffer from too few high-precision measurements. Theory uncertainties lead to weaker model constraints, but can be controlled within the global analysis.

## Key claim

The paper performs a global analysis of permanent electric dipole moment (EDM) measurements using the SFitter framework, interpreting all existing data in terms of a hadronic-scale effective Lagrangian; the authors find that while some Lagrangian parameters are tightly constrained, others suffer from too few high-precision measurements, and theory uncertainties weaken model constraints but can be controlled within the global analysis.

## Method

The authors construct a hadronic-scale Lagrangian describing CP-violating interactions of nucleons, pions, and electrons at the GeV scale, with parameters including the electron EDM, semileptonic scalar, tensor, and pseudoscalar couplings, pion-nucleon couplings, and nucleon EDMs. They match these hadronic-scale Wilson coefficients to SMEFT operators at the weak scale, reducing the semileptonic parameters to three independent combinations. Using the SFitter global analysis framework, they perform a profile likelihood analysis of all current EDM measurements to extract correlations and limits on the multi-dimensional parameter space, first without and then with theory uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- The neutron EDM experimentally constrains the QCD theta parameter to such a small value that the fine-tuning problem requires a proper explanation, and the authors exclude it from the global analysis.
- Indirect constraints on EDMs of heavy leptons (muon and tau) are many orders of magnitude weaker than for the electron, with little interplay among the relevant model parameters.

## Context

This work extends previous EDM global analyses by Gaul (2023) and others, using the SFitter framework previously applied to Higgs and new physics analyses by Lafaye (2004, 2007, 2009), Brivio (2022), and Elmer (2023). The hadronic-scale Lagrangian approach follows the framework developed by Pospelov (2005), Mereghetti (2010), Chupp (2014, 2017), and Dekens (2018). The analysis enables future interpretation of new EDM measurements and identification of shortcomings in relating measurements to fundamental parameters, while remaining easy to repeat or adapt in response to new theoretical or experimental inputs.
