# Relationship between dinuclear systems and nuclei in highly deformed states

> Potential energies, moments of inertia, quadrupole and octupole moments of dinuclear systems are compared with the corresponding quantities of strongly deformed nuclei. As dinuclear system we denote two touching nuclei (clusters). It is found that the hyperdeformed states of nuclei are close to those of nearly symmetric dinuclear systems, whereas the superdeformed states are considered as states of asymmetric dinuclear systems. The superdeformed and hyperdeformed states constructed from two touching clusters have large octupole deformations. The experimental measurement of octupole deformation of the highly deformed nuclei can answer whether these nuclei have cluster configurations as described by the dinuclear model.

# Relationship Between Dinuclear Systems and Nuclei in Highly Deformed States

## Key claim
The paper proposes that superdeformed (SD) and hyperdeformed (HD) nuclear states can be described as dinuclear system (DNS) configurations, two touching nuclei, rather than as rotational ellipsoids from mean-field theories. The claim is conditional: the experimental measurement of octupole deformation in highly deformed nuclei can determine whether these nuclei actually have cluster configurations as described by the dinuclear model.

## Method
The authors calculated potential energies, moments of inertia, quadrupole moments, and octupole moments for dinuclear systems using a frozen density approximation with a double folding formalism and a Skyrme-type effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. They compared these calculated quantities to the corresponding experimental values for known SD and HD nuclei across several mass regions (A ≈ 76, 100, 150, 230). The DNS potential energy was minimized as a function of mass asymmetry η = (A₂ − A₁)/A, charge asymmetry, and deformation parameters of the constituent nuclei, with the relative distance fixed at the touching configuration Rₘ ≈ R₁ + R₂.

## Key numbers
- For the HD state in ¹⁵²Dy, the DNS configuration ⁵⁰Ti+¹⁰²Ru yields a moment of inertia ℑʳ = 131 ℏ² MeV⁻¹, close to the experimental value ℑᵉˣᵖ = 130 ℏ² MeV⁻¹; the calculated β₂ = 1.3 agrees with the experimental estimate β₂ ≥ 0.9.
- For the SD state in ¹⁵²Dy, the DNS ²²Ne+¹³⁰Ba gives ℑʳ = 100 ℏ² MeV⁻¹, Q₂ = 20 e·barn, and β₂ = 0.8, compared to experimental values ℑᵉˣᵖ = (85 ± 3) ℏ² MeV⁻¹ and Q₂ᵉˣᵖ = (18 ± 3) e·barn.
- For the SD state in ¹⁵²Dy, the DNS ²⁶Mg+¹²⁶Xe gives ℑʳ = 104 ℏ² MeV⁻¹, Q₂ = 24 e·barn, and β₂ = 0.9.
- For ²³²Th, the DNS configurations ¹⁰⁰Zr+¹³²Sn and ⁸²Ge+¹⁵⁰Ce yield β₂ ≈ 1.5, β₃ ≈ 0.40, ℑʳ ≈ 290 ℏ² MeV⁻¹, and ℑᵉ ≈ 240 ℏ² MeV⁻¹.
- For ²³⁴U, the depth of the third well (HD state) is (3.6 ± 0.3) MeV experimentally; the DNS configurations ¹⁰⁰Zr+¹³⁴Te and ¹⁰⁴Mo+¹³⁰Sn give a pocket depth of about 3.4 MeV.
- For ⁷⁶Kr, the DNS ⁸Be+⁶⁸Ge gives Q₂ = 4.9 e·barn, Q₃ = 2.0 × 10³ e·fm³, and ℑʳ = 29 ℏ² MeV⁻¹ (ℑᵉ = 17.2 ℏ² MeV⁻¹).
- For ²²⁶Ra, the calculated multipole moments from the α-cluster DNS are Q₂ = 776 e·fm² and Q₃ = 2662 e·fm³, compared to experimental Q₂ = 750 e·fm² and Q₃ = 3100 e·fm³; the calculated dipole moment Q₁ = 4 e·fm is an order of magnitude larger than the experimental 0.16 e·fm.
- For ¹⁴⁸Nd, the calculated Q₂ = 486 e·fm² and Q₃ = 1844 e·fm³ compare to experimental Q₂ = 400 e·fm² and Q₃ = 1500 e·fm³; the calculated Q₁ = 3 e·fm exceeds the experimental 0.32 e·fm.

## Context
This work extends the cluster approach to heavy nuclei, building on earlier studies of light α-particle nuclei where SD shapes were linked to di-molecular configurations (Author, 1996; Author, 1997). It contrasts with mean-field calculations using cranked Woods-Saxon and Nilsson approaches (Author, 1993; Author, 1994) which describe SD and HD shapes as rotational ellipsoids. The paper specifically addresses the observation by Author (1995) that the nucleus in the third minimum corresponds to a DNS configuration but with overlapping clusters, whereas the present work uses non-overlapping touching configurations. The results enable experimental tests: measuring octupole deformations in SD and HD nuclei can distinguish between ellipsoidal and cluster-type configurations, and the DNS interpretation can explain the parity splitting observed in octupole-deformed nuclei such as ²²⁶Ra and ¹⁴⁸Nd.
