# Modal expansions and non-perturbative quantum field theory in Minkowski space

> We introduce a spectral approach to non-perturbative field theory within the periodic field formalism. As an example we calculate the real and imaginary parts of the propagator in 1+1 dimensional phi^4 theory, identifying both one-particle and multi-particle contributions. We discuss the computational limits of existing diagonalization algorithms and suggest new quasi-sparse eigenvector methods to handle very large Fock spaces and higher dimensional field theories.

## Key claim
The paper introduces a spectral (diagonalization) approach to non-perturbative quantum field theory within the periodic field formalism, but the method is demonstrated only for 1+1 dimensional phi^4 theory and the authors note that extension to higher dimensions requires new quasi-sparse eigenvector methods because straightforward diagonalization becomes computationally prohibitive.

## Method
The authors approximate the field theory as a finite-dimensional quantum mechanical system by expanding the field in periodic box modes with a momentum cutoff N_max, then restrict the Fock space further using auxiliary cutoffs K_max (kinetic energy estimate) and D_max (maximum number of excited modes). They compute matrix elements of the Hamiltonian in this truncated space using a symbolic differentiation-integration algorithm, diagonalize the resulting matrix, and extract the full propagator by inserting the complete set of eigenstates. The multi-particle contribution to the propagator is isolated by subtracting the single-particle pole contribution, and finite-volume effects are smoothed by averaging over several box sizes L and including a small width to each discrete state.

## Key numbers
- For N_max=9, K_max=19, D_max=7, the Fock space has 2036 dimensions and requires about 100 MB of RAM using dense matrix methods.
- For coupling lambda/4! = 0.50 (above perturbative threshold ~0.25 but below spontaneous symmetry breaking ~2.5), the one-particle state appears near p^2 = (0.93)^2 and the three-particle threshold is at approximately p^2 = (2.9)^2.
- For lambda/4! = 1.00, the magnitude of the multi-particle contribution to the propagator remains small (less than 0.003).
- For N_max=9 and L=2.0pi through 2.8pi, spectral results with K_max,D_max=19,7 and lambda/4! < 1 are within 20% of the K_max,D_max -> infinity limit.
- Errors for finite N_max are expected to scale as N_max^(-2) from dimensional power counting.
- For a typical eigenvector at the critical point (lambda/4! = 2.5), 75% of the probability is captured by the 10 largest Fock-space components, 84% by 20 components, 90% by 40 components, and 94% by 80 components (for the ground state |v0⟩).

## Context
This work extends the modal field theory approach previously applied to Euclidean space using Monte Carlo techniques (Periodic, 1995) and to unquenched fermion systems using matrix Runge-Kutta methods (Thirring, 1998). It is similar in spirit to the diagonalization approach of Brooks and Frautschi (1990) for a 1+1 dimensional Yukawa model, as well as Hamiltonian lattice formulations and Tamm-Dancoff light-cone quantization methods. The spectral method enables calculation of multi-particle contributions to the propagator in Minkowski space, which cannot be obtained using Euclidean Monte Carlo methods. The authors suggest that quasi-sparse eigenvector methods, to be described in a future publication, could handle the much larger Fock spaces required for 2+1 and 3+1 dimensional theories (estimated at 10^12 and 10^18 states respectively).
