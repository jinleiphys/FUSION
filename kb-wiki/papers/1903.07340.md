# Structure, formation and decay of $\bar{K}NN$ system by Faddeev-AGS calculations

> The Faddeev AGS equations are solved for coupled-channels $\bar{K}NN-πΣ{N}$ system with quantum numbers $I=1/2$ and $S=0$. Using separable potentials for $\bar{K}N-πΣ$ interaction, we have calculated the transition probability for the $(Y_{K})_{I=0}+N\rightarrowπΣ{N}$ reaction. The possibility to observe the trace of $K^{-}pp$ quasi-bound state in the $πΣ{N}$ mass spectra was studied. Various types of chiral based and phenomenological potentials are used to describe the $\bar{K}N-πΣ$ interaction. It was shown that not only we can see the signature of the $K^{-}pp$ quasi-bound state in the mass spectra, but also, one can see the trace of branch points in the observables.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the signature of the $K^{-}pp\) quasi-bound state can be observed in the \(\pi\Sigma N\) mass spectra from the \((Y_{K})_{I=0}+N\rightarrow\pi\Sigma N\) reaction, but the visibility and exact position of this signal depend on the choice of the \(\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma\) interaction model (phenomenological vs. chiral-based) and on the pole structure (one-pole vs. two-pole) of the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled-channels Faddeev-AGS equations for the \(\bar{K}NN-\pi\Sigma N\) system with total isospin \(I=1/2\) and total spin \(S=0\), using separable potentials for the \(\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma\) interaction. They employed the Hilbert-Schmidt expansion method to find the \(K^{-}pp\) pole positions and then constructed the break-up amplitude for the \((Y_{K})_{I=0}+N\rightarrow\pi\Sigma N\) reaction to compute the transition probability as a function of the \(\pi\Sigma N\) invariant mass. Four phenomenological potentials (KEK and SIDDHARTA, each with one-pole and two-pole versions) and two chiral-based potentials (energy-dependent and energy-independent) were used for the \(\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma\) interaction, while the \(NN\) interaction was described by either a rank-two separable potential or the PEST potential.

## Key numbers

- For the KEK one-pole phenomenological potential, the \(\bar{K}NN\) pole position is \(2329.0 - i26.0\) MeV.
- For the KEK two-pole phenomenological potential, the \(\bar{K}NN\) pole position is \(2327.6 - i19.5\) MeV.
- For the SIDDHARTA one-pole phenomenological potential, the \(\bar{K}NN\) pole position is \(2320.7 - i31.5\) MeV.
- For the SIDDHARTA two-pole phenomenological potential, the \(\bar{K}NN\) pole position is \(2325.0 - i24.1\) MeV.
- For the energy-independent chiral potential, the \(\bar{K}NN\) pole position is \(2313.4 - i21.9\) MeV.
- For the energy-dependent chiral potential, the \(\bar{K}NN\) pole position is \(2346.5 - i22.0\) MeV.
- The DISTO experiment observed a peak corresponding to a binding energy of about 103 MeV and a width \(\Gamma = 118\) MeV for the \(K^{-}pp\) system.
- The E15 experiment at J-PARC suggests a broad \(K^{-}pp\) quasi-bound state structure at 15 MeV just below the \(\bar{K}NN\) threshold.

## Context

This work extends previous Faddeev calculations by Ohnishi et al. (2013) and Shevchenko et al. (2007, 2012) by systematically comparing the sensitivity of the three-body observables to different phenomenological and chiral-based \(\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma\) potentials. The study is motivated by experimental efforts at J-PARC (E15 and E27 experiments) and the earlier DISTO experiment, which have reported candidate signals for the \(K^{-}pp\) quasi-bound state. The results enable future experimental comparisons to discriminate between different \(\bar{K}N\) interaction models and to confirm the existence and properties of the \(K^{-}pp\) system.
