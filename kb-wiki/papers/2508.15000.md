# Helicity amplitudes of $N(1520)$ and $N(1535)$ including pentaquark components

> We investigate the helicity amplitudes of the $N(1520)$ and $N(1535)$ resonances in the electromagnetic transition process $γ^*p\to N^*$, where the $N(1520)$ and $N(1535)$ are assumed to include both the $L=1$ three-quark and $L=0$ spatial symmetric $q^4\bar q$ pentaquark components. The helicity transition amplitudes $A_{1/2}$, $A_{3/2}$ (for spin 3/2 states) and $S_{1/2}$ are computed within the constituent quark model. The inclusion of the $q^4 \bar q$ pentaquark components via the $γ^*\to q\bar q$ diagram significantly improves the theoretical description of the helicity amplitudes for both $N(1520)$ and $N(1535)$, yielding a closer agreement with experimental data compared to the pure three-quark picture. The work reveals that the $N(1520)$ and $N(1535)$ resonances may contain a considerable pentaquark components.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including a ground-state \(L=0\) spatial symmetric \(q^4\bar q\) pentaquark component, mixed with the conventional \(L=1\) three-quark configuration, significantly improves the theoretical description of the helicity amplitudes for both the \(N(1520)\) and \(N(1535)\) resonances compared to the pure three-quark picture, but the improvement is conditional on the specific choice of pentaquark configuration and the mixing coefficients being fitted to experimental data.

## Method

The authors construct wave functions for the \(N(1520)\) and \(N(1535)\) resonances as a mixture of \(L=1\) three-quark (\(q^3\)) and \(L=0\) spatial symmetric \(q^4\bar q\) pentaquark components, using a constituent quark model with harmonic oscillator basis states. The helicity transition amplitudes \(A_{1/2}\), \(A_{3/2}\) (for spin 3/2 states), and \(S_{1/2}\) for the process \(\gamma^* p \to N^*\) are computed separately for the three-quark part (via impulse approximation) and the pentaquark part (via the \(\gamma^* \to q\bar q\) diagram), then combined with mixing coefficients \(B_{3q}\) and \(B_{5q}\) that are fitted to experimental data over the range \(0 \leq Q^2 \leq 4.5\ \text{GeV}^2\).

## Key numbers

- The pentaquark state with configuration \(\Psi^{csf}_{[211]_{C}[31]_{FS}[22]_{F}[31]_{S}}(q^{4}\bar q)\) and quantum numbers \(I(J^P)=\frac12(\frac12^-)\) has the lowest predicted mass, around 1680 MeV.
- For \(N(1520)\), the fitted mixing coefficients are \(B_{3q}^2: B_{5q}^2 = 0.91: 0.09\).
- For \(N(1535)\), the fitted mixing coefficients are \(B_{3q}^2: B_{5q}^2 = 0.75: 0.25\).
- The constituent quark masses used are \(m_u = m_d = 327\ \text{MeV}\), \(m_s = 498\ \text{MeV}\), \(m_c = 1642\ \text{MeV}\), and \(m_b = 4960\ \text{MeV}\).
- The dynamical quark mass for \(u\) and \(d\) quarks in the \(\gamma^*\to q\bar q\) transition is taken as \(m = 5\ \text{MeV}\).
- The helicity amplitudes are computed and fitted over the range \(0 \leq Q^2 \leq 4.5\ \text{GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group (Xu et al., 2020; MosEpja) that described the masses of \(N(1520)\) and \(N(1535)\) by including ground-state pentaquark components, and that pushed the three-quark contribution to its upper limit but found only \(A_{1/2}\) could be fairly described. The present calculation improves upon pure three-quark models (Capstick, 1994; Pace, 2000) and earlier pentaquark-inclusive studies (An, 2009; Zou, 2010) by systematically computing all helicity amplitudes for both resonances in a unified mixing framework. The results are compared with soft-wall AdS/QCD predictions (PhysRevD.101.034026, PhysRevD.102.094008) and show overall consistency, especially in the low-\(Q^2\) region, enabling future studies of the pentaquark content in other nucleon resonances.
