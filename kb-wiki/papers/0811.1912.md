# Mass Distributions Beyond TDHF

> The mass distributions for giant dipole resonances in 32S and 132Sn decaying through particle emission and for deep-inelastic collisions between 16O nuclei have been investigated by implementing the Balian-Veneroni variational technique based upon a three-dimensional time-dependent Hartree-Fock code with realistic Skyrme interactions. The mass distributions obtained have been shown to be significantly larger than the standard TDHF results.

## Key claim

The Balian-Veneroni (BV) variational technique, implemented with a three-dimensional time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) code using realistic Skyrme interactions, produces mass distributions for giant dipole resonances and deep-inelastic collisions that are significantly larger than those from standard TDHF, and can exceed the theoretical upper limit of TDHF mass fluctuations.

## Method

The authors implemented the Balian-Veneroni variational method, which requires performing a forward TDHF evolution from initial time t0 to final time t1, then applying a transformation involving the operator of interest (here, the nucleon number operator in a bounded spatial region) to the wavefunctions at t1, and evolving the transformed wavefunctions backward to t0. This procedure was repeated for a range of small parameter values epsilon, and the mass fluctuation Delta N_BV^2 was obtained by extrapolating to epsilon=0. The calculations used a fully three-dimensional TDHF code with the SLy6 Skyrme interaction, applied to giant dipole resonances in 32S and 132Sn decaying by particle emission, and to deep-inelastic collisions between 16O nuclei at ECM=128 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For the GDR in 32S: standard TDHF gave Delta N_TDHF^2 = 4.08 (with mean number of nucleons ⟨N⟩ = 26.65, using cut-off radius Rc = 8 fm), while the BV method gave Delta N_BV^2 = 5.52, representing a 16% increase in Delta N and exceeding the TDHF upper limit of (Delta N_TDHF^2)_MAX = 4.46.
- For the GDR in 132Sn: standard TDHF gave Delta N_TDHF^2 = 8.46 (with ⟨N⟩ = 121.02), while the BV method gave Delta N_BV^2 = 11.29, representing a 14% increase in Delta N and exceeding the TDHF upper limit of (Delta N_TDHF^2)_MAX = 10.07.
- For 16O+16O collisions at ECM=128 MeV: at impact parameter b=0 fm, Delta N_TDHF = 1.37 and Delta N_BV = 2.84 (a +107% increase); at b=1 fm, Delta N_TDHF = 1.38 and Delta N_BV = 2.87 (+108%); at b=2 fm, Delta N_TDHF = 1.44 and Delta N_BV = 3.17 (+120%); at b=4 fm, the nuclei fused and only TDHF was calculated, giving Delta N_TDHF = 2.06.
- The GDR in 32S showed dipole oscillation periods of approximately 71 fm/c along the long axis (excitation energy Ex ≈ 17.5 MeV) and approximately 68 fm/c along the other axes (Ey ≈ Ez ≈ 18.3 MeV).
- The GDR in 132Sn showed a dipole oscillation period of approximately 88 fm/c, corresponding to a resonance energy of approximately 14.1 MeV (compared to the experimental value of 16.1(7) MeV from Adrich).

## Context

This work extends the Balian-Veneroni variational approach, originally developed in the 1980s (Balian and Veneroni), from simplified calculations using spherical or axial symmetries and schematic interactions to fully three-dimensional TDHF calculations with realistic Skyrme interactions. It directly addresses the well-known deficiency of standard TDHF, first observed by Davies (1978) for 84Kr+208Pb and 84Kr+209Be reactions, where TDHF underestimated experimental mass fluctuations by about an order of magnitude. The method enables calculations of mass distributions that exceed the unphysical upper limit of TDHF fluctuations identified by Dasso, and the authors plan to apply it to heavier systems for comparison with experimental data.
