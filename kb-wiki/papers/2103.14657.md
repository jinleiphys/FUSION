# From hydro to jet quenching, coalescence and hadron cascade: a coupled approach to solving the $R_{AA}\otimes v_2$ puzzle

> Hydrodynamics and jet quenching are responsible for the elliptic flow $v_2$ and suppression of large transverse momentum ($p_T$) hadrons, respectively, two of the most important phenomena leading to the discovery of a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. A consistent description of the hadron suppression factor $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$, especially at intermediate $p_T$, however, remains a challenge. We solve this long-standing $R_{AA}\otimes v_2$ puzzle by including quark coalescence for hadronization and final state hadron cascade in the coupled linear Boltzmann transport-hydro model that combines concurrent jet transport and hydrodynamic evolution of the bulk medium. We illustrate that quark coalescence and hadron cascade, two keys to solving the puzzle, also lead to a splitting of $v_2$ for pions, kaons and protons in the intermediate $p_T$ region. We demonstrate for the first time that experimental data on $R_{AA}$, $v_2$ and their hadron flavor dependence from low to intermediate and high $p_T$ in high-energy heavy-ion collisions can be understood within this coupled framework.

## Key claim

The authors claim to have solved the long-standing \(R_{AA}\otimes v_2\) puzzle by including quark coalescence for hadronization and final-state hadron cascade in a coupled linear Boltzmann transport-hydro model, but the solution is conditional on the specific implementation of a hybrid hadronization scheme (Hydro-Coal-Frag) with a separation scale \(p_{Ts}=1.5\) GeV/\(c\) and two adjustable parameters (the effective coupling to medium partons \(\alpha_s=0.17\) and a lower cut-off \(p_{T\mathrm{min}}\) that depends on centrality).

## Method

The authors coupled the (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic model CLVisc with the linear Boltzmann transport (LBT) model for jet shower and medium recoil partons, using an energy-momentum source term for the coupling. Hadronization was handled via a hybrid Hydro-Coal-Frag model: hydro freeze-out at low \(p_T\), quark coalescence (including thermal-thermal, thermal-shower, and shower-shower coalescence) at intermediate \(p_T\), and PYTHIA8 string fragmentation at high \(p_T\), followed by hadronic rescatterings and resonance decays using the UrQMD model. The initial conditions were generated with the TRENTo model, and PYTHIA8 with EPPS16 nuclear parton distributions was used to generate initial jet showers.

## Key numbers

- Effective coupling to medium partons set to \(\alpha_s = 0.17\), adjusted to fit \(R_{AA}\) at high \(p_T\) in central Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV.
- Lower cut-off \(p_{T\mathrm{min}} = 7.0\) GeV/\(c\) for 10-20% centrality and \(p_{T\mathrm{min}} = 5.0\) GeV/\(c\) for 40-50% centrality Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV.
- Separation scale for effective constituent quarks set to \(p_{Ts} = 1.5\) GeV/\(c\), corresponding to \(p_{T\mathrm{meson}} < 3\) GeV/\(c\) and \(p_{T\mathrm{baryon}} < 4.5\) GeV/\(c\) for hadron production through hydro freeze-out.
- Specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s = 0.10\), freeze-out temperature \(T_{\mathrm{sw}} = 150\) MeV, initial time \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\).
- Minimum hard scale for jet production \(\hat p_{T0} = 4.0\) GeV/\(c\); \(\sigma_{NN}^{\mathrm{inel}} = 70\) mb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV.
- Without coalescence, CoLBT-hydro underestimates \(v_2\) by up to a factor of 2 in the intermediate \(p_T\) region (3-8 GeV/\(c\)).
- \(v_2\) of protons from coalescence is about a factor of 4 larger than from fragmentation; for pions and kaons, the factors are about 2 and 3, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the CoLBT-hydro model (Chen 2020) by adding quark coalescence and a hadronic afterburner (UrQMD) to address the long-standing puzzle that parton energy loss models adjusted to describe \(R_{AA}\) under-predict \(v_2\) at intermediate \(p_T\) (Molnar 2013, Noronha-Hostler 2016, Cao 2017). Previous attempts using exotic monopole interactions near \(T_c\) (Xu 2014, Shi 2018) or event-by-event fluctuations (Noronha-Hostler 2016) could not address the constituent quark number (NCQ) dependence of \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\). The approach enables a unified description of hadron production from low to high \(p_T\) in heavy-ion collisions, and predicts a splitting between \(v_2\) of kaons and pions in the intermediate \(p_T\) region as a precision test of the coalescence mechanism.
