# Feynman Graphs and Generalized Eikonal Approach to High Energy Knock-Out Processes

> The cross section of hard semi-exclusive $A(e,e'N)(A-1)$ reactions for fixed missing energy and momentum is calculated within the eikonal approximation. Relativistic dynamics and kinematics of high energy processes are unambiguously accounted for by using the analysis of appropriate Feynman diagrams. A significant dependence of the final state interactions on the missing energy is found, which is important for interpretation of forthcoming color transparency experiments. A new, more stringent kinematic restriction on the region where the contribution of short-range nucleon correlations is enhanced in semi-exclusive knock-out processes is derived. It is also demonstrated that the use of light-cone variables leads to a considerable simplification of the description of high-energy knock-out reactions.

## Key claim
The paper calculates the cross section of hard semi-exclusive $A(e,e'N)(A-1)$ reactions within the eikonal approximation, finding that final state interactions depend significantly on missing energy, a dependence that is not captured by conventional Glauber or optical model approaches. This dependence is important for interpreting color transparency experiments, but the analysis is restricted to energies where inelastic diffraction is a small correction (knocked-out nucleon energies $\lesssim 10$ GeV and photon virtualities $Q^2 \sim 1-3$ GeV$^2$).

## Method
The authors derive the eikonal approximation for final state interactions in $A(e,e'N)(A-1)$ reactions by analyzing covariant Feynman diagrams corresponding to multiple rescatterings of the knocked-out nucleon off spectator nucleons in the residual nucleus. They perform a nonrelativistic reduction of the nuclear vertices by taking residues over nucleon propagator poles, then transform the resulting amplitudes into coordinate space to obtain operators for impulse approximation, single rescattering, and double rescattering terms. The method accounts for nucleon Fermi motion and uses light-cone variables to simplify the relativistic kinematics of high-energy knock-out processes.

## Key numbers
- Missing momentum range considered: $|\vec p_m| \lesssim 400$ MeV/c
- Light-cone fraction $\alpha$ range: $\alpha \approx 1 \pm 0.3$
- Knocked-out nucleon energy range: $E_N \ge 1$ GeV (with upper limit $\lesssim 10$ GeV for validity of neglecting inelastic diffraction)
- Photon virtuality range: $Q^2 \sim 1-3$ GeV$^2$ (where color coherent phenomena are expected to be a small correction)
- The factor $\Delta_0$ in the modified profile function is given by $\Delta_0 = (q_0/q) E_m$, where $E_m$ is the missing energy

## Context
This work extends the nonrelativistic Glauber model (Glauber, 1959) and optical model approaches (OV) that were successful at medium energies ($E_N \le 1$ GeV) but become problematic at higher energies where $NN$ interactions become predominantly inelastic. The authors build on the Feynman diagram method for hadron-nucleus collisions developed by Gribov (1969) and Bertocchi (1972), and extend their own previous results for $^2H(e,e')(pn)$ processes (Frankfurt et al., 1995) to nucleon knock-out off $^3He$ and $^4He$ targets. The derived formulae provide a baseline model for searching for color coherent phenomena at higher $Q^2$ and enable the determination of optimal kinematic conditions for investigating short-range nucleon correlations in nuclei.
