# Anisotropic flow from hard partons in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions

> Anisotropies of hadronic distribution in nuclear collisions are used for determination of properties of the nuclear matter. At the LHC it is important to account for the contribution to the flow due to momentum transferred from hard partons to the quark-gluon plasma.

# Anisotropic flow from hard partons in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions

## Key claim

At LHC energies, momentum transferred from hard partons (jets) to the quark-gluon plasma generates anisotropic flow even from smooth initial conditions, breaking the usual proportionality between initial-state spatial anisotropy and final-state flow harmonics. This mechanism can produce elliptic and triangular flow without any initial-state anisotropy, and its contribution must be included in realistic hydrodynamic simulations aiming to extract transport coefficients.

## Method

The authors developed a 3+1D ideal hydrodynamic simulation code using the SHASTA scheme, including a force term that represents the dragging of the fluid by hard partons. They generated multiple dijet pairs per event with positions from the optical Glauber model, momenta from a parametrized pT distribution (B = 14.7 mb/GeV², p0 = 6 GeV, n = 9.5), and energy loss scaling with entropy density. Simulations started with smooth, boost-invariant initial conditions, and final-state hadrons were generated using THERMINATOR2 at a freeze-out temperature of 150 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Hard parton threshold: pT > 3 GeV/c
- Energy loss scaling reference: s0 = 78.2/fm³ (corresponding to energy density 20 GeV/fm³)
- Two energy loss settings tested: dE/dx|0 = 4 GeV/fm and 7 GeV/fm, producing the same momentum anisotropies
- Freeze-out temperature: 150 MeV
- In non-central collisions (30–40% centrality): about 50% addition to v2 compared to smooth initial conditions
- Flow anisotropies from hot spots (energy only, no momentum) are about half of those from hard partons with momentum deposition

## Context

This work extends the study of flow anisotropies beyond the standard hydrodynamic paradigm where vn coefficients follow initial-state spatial anisotropies, as discussed by Gale (2012) and Niemi (2012). It addresses the degeneracy problem highlighted by Luzum (2008), where different initial conditions can yield the same flow anisotropies if transport coefficients are re-tuned. The 3+1D approach distinguishes this work from lower-dimensional studies by Andrade (2014) and Floerchinger (2014), and the inclusion of multiple dijet pairs per event goes beyond the single-jet studies of Tachibana (2014). The mechanism enables future extraction of quark-gluon plasma properties by breaking the link between initial conditions and observed flow, potentially resolving ambiguities in viscosity determinations.
