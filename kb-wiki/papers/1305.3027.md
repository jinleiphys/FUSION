# Probe the 2$s_{1/2}$ and 1$d_{3/2}$ state level inversion with electron-nucleus scattering

> The neutron-rich even-even nuclei $^{26-40}$Mg, $^{28-46}$Si, $^{30-48}$S, and $^{32-56}$Ar are calculated with the RMF model and the phase-shift electron scattering method. Results show that level inversion of the 2$s_{1/2}$ and 1$d_{3/2}$ proton states may occur for the magnesium, silicon, sulphur, and argon isotopes with more neutrons away from the stability line. Calculations show that the variation of the central charge densities for $^{30-48}$S, and $^{32-56}$Ar are very sensitive to the 2$s_{1/2}$ and 1$d_{3/2}$ proton state level inversion, and the level inversion can lead to a large measurable central charge depletion to the charge density distributions for the neutron-rich isotopes. Calculations also show that the charge density differences between the isotopes with and without central charge depletion can reveal not only the level inversion of the 2$s_{1/2}$ and 1$d_{3/2}$ proton states but also the behavior of the proton wave functions of both states. The results can provide references for the possible study of the nuclear level inversion and nuclear bubble phenomenon with electron scattering off short-lived nuclei at RIKEN or/and GSI in the future. In addition, direct nuclear reaction $^{44}$S(n, d)$^{43}$P or $^{44}$S($^{3}$H, $α$)$^{43}$P might also be a possible way to study the 2$s_{1/2}$ and 1$d_{3/2}$ proton state level inversion.

# Probe the 2s₁/₂ and 1d₃/₂ state level inversion with electron-nucleus scattering

## Key claim
Level inversion of the 2s₁/₂ and 1d₃/₂ proton states *may* occur for magnesium, silicon, sulphur, and argon isotopes with more neutrons away from the stability line, as calculated with the RMF model using the NL-SH parameter set. The inversion is predicted to produce a large measurable central charge depletion in neutron-rich sulphur and argon isotopes, but the authors hedge that this remains a theoretical prediction pending experimental verification at facilities like RIKEN or GSI.

## Method
The authors combined the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with the NL-SH parameter set to calculate single-particle energy levels, occupation probabilities, and charge density distributions for the even-even nuclei ²⁶–⁴⁰Mg, ²⁸–⁴⁶Si, ³⁰–⁴⁸S, and ³²–⁵⁶Ar. They then used the phase-shift analysis method for elastic electron-nucleus scattering to compute charge form factors, differential cross sections, and cross-section differences between isotopes, including corrections for recoil and effective momentum transfer.

## Key numbers
- For ²⁶–⁴⁰Mg, the 1d₅/₂ state occupation probabilities are approximately 0.65, corresponding to nearly 4 protons; the 2s₁/₂ and 1d₃/₂ occupation probabilities are less than 0.03, corresponding to less than 0.18 protons.
- For ²⁸–⁴⁶Si, the 1d₅/₂ state occupation probabilities are approximately 0.95, corresponding to nearly 6 protons; the 2s₁/₂ occupation probability is less than 0.09 and the 1d₃/₂ less than 0.06.
- For sulphur isotopes from ³⁰S to ⁴⁸S, the proton occupation number of the 2s₁/₂ state decreases from 1.07 to 0.20, while that of the 1d₃/₂ state increases from 1.06 to 1.91.
- For magnesium, silicon, and sulphur, the possible level inversion occurs for isotopes with neutron number N > 20; for argon, the possible level inversion occurs for isotopes with N ≥ 18.
- The proton charge radius used in the folding procedure is r_p = 0.81 fm, with Q² = 18.29 fm⁻² = 0.71 GeV².

## Context
This work extends theoretical studies of s-d level inversion and nuclear bubble phenomena in exotic nuclei, following earlier investigations by Otsuka (2001), Utsuno (2004), and Poves (2005). The authors propose that electron scattering experiments at the MUSES double storage rings at RIKEN or the electron-ion collider at GSI could test these predictions, building on the SCRIT demonstrative experiment with ¹³³Cs performed in 2009. The paper also suggests that direct nuclear reactions such as ⁴⁴S(n,d)⁴³P or ⁴⁴S(³H,α)⁴³P could provide an alternative experimental probe of the level inversion.
