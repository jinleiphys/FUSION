# Relativistic mean field approximation to the analysis of 16O(e,e'p)15N data at |Q^2|\leq 0.4 (GeV/c)^2

> We use the relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation to analyze data on 16O(e,e'p)15N at |Q^2|\leq 0.4 (GeV/c)^2 that were obtained by different groups and seemed controversial. Results for differential cross-sections, response functions and A_TL asymmetry are discussed and compared to different sets of experimental data for proton knockout from p_{1/2} and p_{3/2} shells in 16O. We compare with a nonrelativistic approach to better identify relativistic effects. The present relativistic approach is found to accommodate most of the discrepancy between data from different groups, smoothing a long standing controversy.

# Relativistic Mean Field Approximation to the Analysis of 16O(e,e'p)15N Data at |Q^2| ≤ 0.4 (GeV/c)^2

## Key claim

The relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) can accommodate most of the discrepancy between previously controversial experimental data sets for 16O(e,e'p)15N at low momentum transfer, but only after tuning the relativistic mean-field parameters to reproduce the experimental binding energies and root-mean-square radii of the p-shell orbitals, rather than using standard Lagrangian parameter sets fitted to heavy nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) calculations using Dirac equation solutions for both bound and scattered nucleons, with scalar-vector potentials derived from relativistic mean-field Lagrangians (NLSH, HS, NL3) and global optical potentials (EDAI-O, EDAD-1,2,3). They compared fully relativistic calculations using CC1 and CC2 current operators with projected calculations that removed negative-energy components, and with nonrelativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) results, analyzing reduced cross-sections, response functions, and the ATL asymmetry for proton knockout from the p1/2 and p3/2 shells in 16O across three experimental data sets.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer range: |Q^2| ≤ 0.4 (GeV/c)^2
- For the Saclay data (Set b): beam energy ε = 580 MeV, outgoing proton kinetic energy TF = 160 MeV, |q| = 570 MeV/c, ω = 170 MeV, |Q^2| = 0.3 (GeV/c)^2
- For the Spaltro et al. data (Set c): (ω, |q|) = (90 MeV, 460 MeV/c), |Q^2| ≈ 0.2 (GeV/c)^2
- For the Leuschner et al. data (Set a): beam energies 304, 456, and 521 MeV, outgoing proton kinetic energy around 90 MeV, missing momentum range -180 < pm < 270 MeV/c
- Coulomb distortion effects on the cross-section for 16O: less than 1.5%
- Spaltro et al. found experimental RTL enhanced by a factor of approximately 2.05 for the 1p3/2 shell and approximately 1.5 for the 1p1/2 shell compared to nonrelativistic DWIA
- EDAD-type optical potentials gave 15-20% smaller reduced cross-sections than EDAI-O, leading to 15-20% larger spectroscopic factors
- A variation of 5 MeV in TF and ω changed the cross-section by up to 7% and the reduced cross-section by less than 2%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RDWIA studies on 208Pb and 40Ca at low |Q^2| (Udias 1993, 1995, 1996) and on 16O at high |Q^2| (Udias 1999, Gao 2000) to resolve a long-standing controversy between Saclay data (Chinitz 1991) and NIKHEF data (Spaltro 1993, Leuschner 1994) for 16O(e,e'p)15N at low momentum transfer. The nonrelativistic DWIA calculations by Spaltro et al. using Schwandt (1982) optical potentials showed large discrepancies with experiment, while the present relativistic approach smooths most of these discrepancies. The results enable more reliable extraction of spectroscopic factors for light nuclei and provide a framework for analyzing forthcoming Jefferson Lab data on 16O responses.
