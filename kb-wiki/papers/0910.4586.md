# Renormalization group study of the four-body problem

> We perform a renormalization group analysis of the non-relativistic four-boson problem by means of a simple model with pointlike three- and four-body interactions. We investigate in particular the unitarity point where the scattering length is infinite and all energies are at the atom threshold. We find that the four-body problem behaves truly universally, independent of any four-body parameter. Our findings confirm the recent conjectures of Platter et al. and von Stecher et al. that the four-body problem is universal, now also from a renormalization group perspective. We calculate the corresponding relations between the four- and three-body bound states, as well as the full bound state spectrum and comment on the influence of effective range corrections.

# Renormalization group study of the four-body problem

## Key claim

The authors perform a renormalization group analysis of the non-relativistic four-boson problem using a simple model with pointlike three- and four-body interactions, finding that the four-body system behaves universally without any four-body parameter. This universality is demonstrated directly at the unitarity point (infinite scattering length and all energies at the atom threshold), where previous quantum mechanical calculations had difficulty accessing the complete spectrum.

## Method

The authors employ a functional renormalization group (FRG) approach with a two-channel model containing elementary boson fields and composite dimer fields, truncating the effective flowing action to include pointlike three- and four-body interactions. They solve the flow equations hierarchically, first treating the two-body sector analytically, then the three-body sector where the Efimov effect appears as an RG limit cycle, and finally the four-body sector where three coupled flow equations for the couplings λ_φ, β, and γ are integrated numerically using a complex extension method to handle periodic divergences. The bound state spectrum is extracted from divergences in the vertex functions as the RG scale is lowered.

## Key numbers

- The Efimov parameter obtained in the pointlike approximation is s₀ ≈ 0.925203, compared to the exact value s₀ ≈ 1.00624.
- The ratio between adjacent trimer binding energies at unitarity follows E_T^(n+1)/E_T^(n) = exp(-2π/s₀).
- For the trimer binding energy E* at a → ∞, the universal relations with threshold degeneracies are a*_- κ* ≈ -1.68 and a*_+ κ* ≈ 0.08, compared to exact results a*_- κ* = -1.56(5) and a*_+ κ* = 0.07076.
- The dimer binding energy in the limit h → ∞ (zero effective range) is E_D = -2/a².
- The decay width of the nth Efimov trimer at unitarity is Γ_T^(n) = 4ε E_T^(n), with ε related to the Braaten-Hammer parameter η* by ε = η*/s₀.

## Context

This work extends the renormalization group analysis of few-body physics, previously applied to the three-body problem, to the four-boson system. It confirms the conjectures of Platter et al. (2004) and von Stecher et al. (2009) that the four-body problem is universal and requires no four-body parameter, now from a complementary RG perspective. The approach enables direct investigation of the unitarity point where previous quantum mechanical calculations by von Stecher, D'Incao, and Greene (2009) could only determine the lowest few bound state levels, and provides a framework for studying effective range corrections and the full bound state spectrum.
