# Photon neutrino-production in a chiral EFT for nuclei and extrapolation to $E_ν\sim$ GeV region

> We carry out a series of studies on pion and photon productions in neutrino/electron/photon--nucleus scatterings. The low energy region is investigated by using a chiral effective field theory for nuclei. The results for the neutral current induced photon production ($γ$-NCP) are then extrapolated to neutrino energy $E_ν\sim$ GeV region. By convoluting the cross sections with MiniBooNE's beam spectrum and detection efficiency, we estimate its $γ$-NCP, event number, and conclude that such photon production can not fully explain its low energy event excess in both neutrino and antineutrino runs.

# Photon neutrino-production in a chiral EFT for nuclei and extrapolation to \(E_\nu\sim\) GeV region

## Key claim
Neutral-current induced photon production (\(\gamma\)-NCP) cannot fully explain MiniBooNE's low-energy event excess in either neutrino or antineutrino runs, based on cross sections convoluted with the MiniBooNE beam spectrum and detection efficiency. The conclusion is hedged by noting that the difference between the calculated \(\gamma\)-NCP events and the MiniBooNE excess is "not large enough to explain all the low energy excess," leaving room for other contributions.

## Method
The interaction kernel for \(\gamma\)-NCP was derived from the same chiral Lagrangian used for pion production, within a quantum hadrodynamics effective field theory (QHD-EFT) that includes nonlinear chiral symmetry and vector meson dominance. Low-energy results (\(E_\nu \leq 0.5\) GeV) were obtained using QHD-EFT with medium modifications to nucleon and \(\Delta\) spectra, then extrapolated to \(E_\nu \sim 1\) GeV using phenomenological form factors. The cross sections were convoluted with the MiniBooNE beam spectrum and detector efficiency to estimate \(\gamma\)-NCP event numbers in three \(E_{QE}\) bins.

## Key numbers
- For the coherent \(\gamma\)-NCP channel on \(^{12}\)C, the estimated event numbers in the MiniBooNE neutrino run are: 1.5 (lower bound) to 2.9 (upper bound) in the \(E_{QE}\) bin [0.2, 0.3] GeV; 6.0 to 9.2 in [0.3, 0.475] GeV; 2.1 to 8.0 in [0.475, 1.25] GeV.
- For the incoherent \(\gamma\)-NCP channel on \(^{12}\)C, the estimated event numbers are: 12.0 to 14.1 in [0.2, 0.3] GeV; 25.5 to 31.1 in [0.3, 0.475] GeV; 12.6 to 23.2 in [0.475, 1.25] GeV.
- For \(\gamma\)-NCP from free protons (hydrogen in CH\(_2\)), the estimated event numbers are: 4.1 to 4.4 in [0.2, 0.3] GeV; 10.6 to 11.6 in [0.3, 0.475] GeV; 4.6 to 6.3 in [0.475, 1.25] GeV.
- The total \(\gamma\)-NCP event estimates across all channels are: 17.6 to 21.4 in [0.2, 0.3] GeV; 42.1 to 51.9 in [0.3, 0.475] GeV; 19.3 to 37.5 in [0.475, 1.25] GeV.
- The MiniBooNE observed event numbers (from oscillation analysis) are: 19.5 in [0.2, 0.3] GeV; 47.3 in [0.3, 0.475] GeV; 19.4 in [0.475, 1.25] GeV.
- The MiniBooNE low-energy excess numbers are: 42.6 \(\pm\) 25.3 in [0.2, 0.3] GeV; 82.2 \(\pm\) 23.3 in [0.3, 0.475] GeV; 21.5 \(\pm\) 34.9 in [0.475, 1.25] GeV.

## Context
This work extends the QHD-EFT framework previously applied to charged-current pion production (Serot et al., 2010-2012) and incoherent electro-production (Zhang, 2012) to neutral-current photon production. It directly addresses the MiniBooNE low-energy excess anomaly (MiniBooNE Collaboration, oscillation summary) by testing whether \(\gamma\)-NCP could account for it, concluding it cannot. The same conclusion was claimed by Alvarez-Ruso et al. (2013) and presented by En Wang at the workshop, though differences in incoherent production calculations between the two approaches remain to be resolved. The work enables future experimental tests using scintillator additions to MiniBooNE (Dharmapalan et al., 2013) and by ArgoNeuT and MicroBooNE experiments, and calls for improved theoretical treatment of the resonance region via dispersion relations.
