# High Statistics Analysis using Anisotropic Clover Lattices: (III) Baryon-Baryon Interactions

> Low-energy baryon-baryon interactions are calculated in a high-statistics lattice QCD study on a single ensemble of anisotropic clover gauge-field configurations at a pion mass of m_π~ 390 MeV, a spatial volume of L^3 ~ (2.5 fm)^3, and a spatial lattice spacing of b~0.123 fm. Lüscher's method is used to extract nucleon-nucleon, hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon scattering phase shifts at one momentum from the one- and two-baryon ground-state energies in the lattice volume. The isospin-3/2 NΣinteractions are found to be highly spin-dependent, and the interaction in the ^3S_1 channel is found to be strong. In contrast, the NΛinteractions are found to be spin-independent, within the uncertainties of the calculation, consistent with the absence of one-pion-exchange. The only channel for which a negative energy-shift is found is ΛΛ, indicating that the ΛΛinteraction is attractive, as anticipated from model-dependent discussions regarding the H-dibaryon. The NN scattering lengths are found to be small, clearly indicating the absence of any fine-tuning in the NN-sector at this pion mass. This is consistent with our previous Lattice QCD calculation of NN interactions. The behavior of the signal-to-noise ratio in the baryon-baryon correlation functions, and in the ratio of correlation functions that yields the ground-state energy splitting is explored. In particular, focus is placed on the window of time slices for which the signal-to-noise ratio does not degrade exponentially, as this provides the opportunity to extract quantitative information about multi-baryon systems.

## Key claim

At a pion mass of approximately 390 MeV, the nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths are found to be small and of natural size, clearly indicating the absence of any fine-tuning in the NN sector at this unphysical pion mass, in contrast to the unnaturally large scattering lengths observed at the physical pion mass.

## Method

Lüscher's finite-volume method was used to extract baryon-baryon scattering phase shifts at one momentum from the ground-state energy shifts of one- and two-baryon systems in a lattice volume of L^3 ~ (2.5 fm)^3. The calculations were performed on a single ensemble of n_f=2+1 flavor anisotropic clover gauge-field configurations generated by the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration, with 364 randomly-distributed measurements on each of 1195 configurations. The energy shifts were determined from ratios of two-baryon to single-baryon correlation functions, and converted to scattering phase shifts using the Lüscher quantization condition.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_pi ~ 390 MeV
- Spatial lattice spacing: b_s = 0.1227 ± 0.0008 fm
- Lattice volume: L^3 × T = 20^3 × 128, corresponding to L^3 ~ (2.5 fm)^3
- Anisotropy: xi = 3.500 ± 0.032
- Nucleon mass: M_N = 1164.1 ± 1.9 ± 1.7 ± 13.1 MeV (statistical, systematic, and combined lattice spacing/anisotropy uncertainties)
- Lambda mass: M_Lambda = 1252.1 ± 1.5 ± 1.5 ± 14.1 MeV
- Sigma mass: M_Sigma = 1280.6 ± 1.7 ± 1.6 ± 14.3 MeV
- Xi mass: M_Xi = 1356.5 ± 1.5 ± 1.5 ± 15.2 MeV
- Pion mass: 390.39 ± 0.67 ± 0.28 ± 4.38 MeV
- Kaon mass: 546.06 ± 0.56 ± 0.19 ± 6.13 MeV
- Single baryon masses extracted with uncertainties below 0.2%

## Context

This work extends the NPLQCD Collaboration's previous lattice QCD calculations of nucleon-nucleon interactions (Beane 2006) and hyperon-nucleon scattering lengths (Beane 2006) to higher statistics on anisotropic clover lattices. It also builds upon the group's earlier high-statistics study of single-hadron correlation functions (Beane 2009) and first calculations of three-baryon systems (Beane 2009). The results enable future investigations of baryon-baryon interactions at lighter pion masses approaching the physical point, where the fine-tuning of the NN scattering lengths is expected to emerge, and provide benchmarks for effective field theory descriptions of nuclear forces.
