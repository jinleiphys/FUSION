# Quasiparticle time blocking approximation within the framework of generalized Green function formalism

> The problem of the microscopic description of excited states of the even-even open-shell atomic nuclei is considered. A model is formulated which allows one to go beyond the quasiparticle random phase approximation. The physical content of the model is determined by the quasiparticle time blocking approximation (QTBA) which enables one to include contributions of the two-quasiparticle and the two-phonon configurations, while excluding (blocking) more complicated intermediate states. In addition, the QTBA ensures consistent treatment of ground state correlations in the Fermi systems with pairing. The model is based on the generalized Green function formalism (GGFF) in which the normal and the anomalous Green functions are treated in a unified way in terms of the components of generalized Green functions in a doubled space. Modification of the GGFF is considered in the case when the many-body nuclear Hamiltonian contains two-, three-, and other many-particle effective forces.

## Key claim

The quasiparticle time blocking approximation (QTBA) enables inclusion of two-quasiparticle and two-phonon configurations while blocking more complicated intermediate states, and ensures consistent treatment of ground state correlations in Fermi systems with pairing; the model is formulated within the generalized Green function formalism (GGFF) to go beyond the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) for describing excited states of even-even open-shell nuclei.

## Method

The author extends the time blocking approximation (TBA) of Tselyaev (1989) to open-shell nuclei by incorporating pairing correlations through the GGFF, where normal and anomalous Green functions are treated in a unified doubled space. The total mass operator and effective interaction amplitude are split into energy-independent mean-field parts and energy-dependent dynamic parts representing complex configurations. The Bethe-Salpeter equation for the non-antisymmetric response function is transformed into a form suitable for including two-quasiparticle and two-phonon configurations, with the blocking of more complicated intermediate states achieved through the structure of the equations.

## Key numbers

- The model extends the approach of Tselyaev (1989), which was developed for doubly magic nuclei and successfully described giant multipole resonances (GMRs) including total resonance widths, to open-shell nuclei with pairing.
- The single-quasiparticle basis functions satisfy the eigenvalue equation with eigenvalues \(E_1 = \eta_1 E_{\lambda_1}\), where \(\eta_1 = \pm 1\) and \(E_{\lambda_1} = |E_1|\), and the conjugate states have eigenvalues \(E_{\bar{1}} = -E_1\).
- The occupation factors satisfy \(u_\lambda = \sqrt{1 - v_\lambda^2}\) with \(v_p = v_{\bar{p}} \leq 1\) for paired states.

## Context

This work generalizes the time blocking approximation of Tselyaev (1989), which was limited to doubly magic nuclei and described GMRs including spreading widths, to open-shell nuclei by adding pairing correlations. It extends the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) by including two-phonon configurations, addressing the known failure of RPA and QRPA to reproduce total widths and fine structure of giant resonances (KTT). The model provides a microscopic framework for describing excited states of open-shell nuclei that can compete with the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) of Soloviev (1992), which was previously the only working approach treating complex configurations and pairing on equal footing.
