# Nuclear parton distribution functions with uncertainties in a general mass variable flavor number scheme

> In this article we obtain a new set of nuclear parton distribution functions (nuclear PDFs) at next-to-leading order and next-to-next-to-leading order accuracy in perturbative QCD. The common nuclear deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) data analyzed in our study are complemented by the available charged-current neutrino DIS data with nuclear targets and data from Drell-Yan cross-section measurements for several nuclear targets. In addition, the most recent DIS data from the Jefferson Lab CLAS and Hall C experiments are also added to our data sample. For these specific datasets, we consider the impact of target mass corrections and higher twist effects which are expected to be important in the region of large $x$ and intermediate-to-low $Q^2$. Our analysis is based on a publicly available open-source tool {\tt APFEL}, which has been modified to be applicable for our analysis of nuclear PDFs. Heavy quark contributions to nuclear DIS are considered within the framework of the {\tt FONLL} general-mass variable-flavor-number scheme. The most recent {\tt CT18} PDFs are used as baseline proton PDFs. The uncertainties of nuclear PDFs are determined using the standard Hessian approach. The results of our global QCD analysis are compared with existing nuclear PDF sets and with the fitted cross-sections, for which our set of nuclear PDFs provides a very good description.

## Key claim
The authors obtain a new set of nuclear parton distribution functions (nuclear PDFs) at NLO and NNLO accuracy, but the extraction relies on the specific choice of the CT18 proton PDF baseline and the FONLL general-mass variable-flavor-number scheme, with target mass corrections and higher twist effects applied only to the Jefferson Lab datasets in the high-x and intermediate-to-low Q² region.

## Method
The analysis uses the open-source APFEL tool modified for nuclear collisions, performing a global QCD fit to neutral-current charged-lepton nuclear DIS data, charged-current neutrino DIS data with nuclear targets, and Drell-Yan cross-section ratios for several nuclear targets. The nuclear PDFs are parameterized via multiplicative nuclear modification factors applied to the CT18 free proton PDFs at the input scale Q₀² = 1.69 GeV², with heavy quark contributions treated in the FONLL general-mass variable-flavor-number scheme and uncertainties estimated using the Hessian approach.

## Key numbers
- Input scale for nuclear PDF parameterization: Q₀² = 1.69 GeV²
- Heavy quark masses fixed at m_c = 1.30 GeV and m_b = 4.75 GeV
- Strong coupling constant set to α_s(M_Z) = 0.118 for both NLO and NNLO fits
- Total of 781 data points from neutral-current charged lepton DIS on nuclear targets used in the fit
- Maximum photon virtuality of nuclear DIS data: Q²_max = 123 GeV²
- Momentum fraction x range covered by nuclear DIS data: 0.005 to 0.95
- Higher twist parameters for NLO analysis: {H₀, H₁, H₂} = {-3.28 GeV², 1.92, -2.07} from CJ15
- Higher twist parameters for NNLO analysis: {H₀, H₁, H₂} = {-1.32 GeV², 1.49, -1.96} from Goharipour (2020)

## Context
This work extends previous nuclear PDF determinations such as KA15 (Khanpour, 2016), which first provided NNLO nuclear PDFs in the zero-mass variable-flavor-number scheme, and nNNPDF1.0 (AbdulKhalek, 2019), which used neural networks in the general-mass scheme. The new KSASG20 set enables improved calculations of hard scattering cross sections in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC and high-energy neutrino interactions with nuclear targets, with the authors noting that future inclusion of LHC proton-lead data on W and Z boson production could further constrain the nuclear gluon PDF.
