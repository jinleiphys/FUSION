# Nuclear Dependence of Structure Functions in Coordinate Space

> The momentum distributions of partons in bound nucleons are known to depend significantly on the size of the nucleus. The Fourier transform of the momentum ($\xbj$) distribution measures the overlap between Fock components of the nucleon wave function which differ by a displacement of one parton along the light cone. The magnitude of the overlap thus determines the average range of mobility of the parton in the nucleon. By comparing the Fourier transforms of structure functions for several nuclei we study the dependence of quark mobility on nuclear size. We find a surprisingly small nuclear dependence ($<2\%$ for He, C and Ca) for displacements $t=z \lsim 2.5$ fm, after which a nuclear suppression due to shadowing sets in. The nuclear effects observed in momentum space for \mbox{$\xbj \lsim 0.4$} can be understood as a reflection of only the large distance shadowing in coordinate space.

## Key claim

The nuclear dependence of parton distributions in coordinate space is surprisingly weak (less than 2% for helium, carbon, and calcium) for light-cone displacements up to about 5 fm, with the nuclear effects observed in momentum space for Bjorken x below 0.4 being attributable solely to the shadowing phenomenon at large distances in coordinate space.

## Method

The authors Fourier transformed the measured momentum-space structure function ratios R_A(x) = F_2^A(x)/(A/2)F_2^D(x) for helium, carbon, and calcium targets using the operator product expansion relation that connects the quark distribution in momentum space to a coordinate-space "mobility" distribution. They used the NMC parametrization of the deuteron structure function at Q^2 = 5 GeV^2 and fitted smooth curves to the experimental data on nuclear ratios from CERN and SLAC, then evaluated the coordinate-space target ratio R_A(y^+) = Q^A(y^+)/(A/2)Q^D(y^+) for light-cone separations y^+.

## Key numbers

- The coordinate-space target ratio R_A(y^+) for calcium is within 2% of unity for y^+ less than or equal to 5 fm (corresponding to y^0 = y^3 less than or equal to 2.5 fm)
- The integral of [F_2^Ca - F_2^D] over x from 0.0035 to 0.80 equals (-1.5 plus or minus 0.4 plus or minus 1.4) times 10^{-3}, normalized by the integral of F_2^D which is approximately 0.15
- For a simple rescaling model Q^A(y^+) proportional to Q^D(xi y^+), the parameter 1-xi must be less than or equal to 2% to match the data for y^+ less than or equal to 3 fm
- The analysis covers x ranges: 0.0035 to 0.88 for helium, 0.00015 to 0.8 for carbon, and 0.0035 to 0.8 for calcium

## Context

This work extends the coordinate-space analysis of parton distributions, which had previously been applied mainly to the deuteron, to nuclear targets. The authors contrast their approach with "nucleon swelling" models (proposed in the context of the EMC effect) that postulated up to 30% increases in effective nucleon radius, finding that such models cannot reproduce the Fourier transform results unless the radius increase is below 2%. The analysis enables a separation of nuclear effects in momentum space into contributions from short-distance (moderate y^+) and long-distance (shadowing) phenomena, showing that the anti-shadowing enhancement and EMC suppression for x below 0.4 can be reproduced using only the shadowing effect at y^+ above 5 fm.
