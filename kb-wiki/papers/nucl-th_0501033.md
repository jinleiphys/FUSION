# On Nucleon Electromagnetic Form Factors: A Pre'cis

> Electron scattering at large Q^2 probes a nucleon's quark core. This core's contribution to electromagnetic form factors may be calculated using Poincare' covariant Faddeev amplitudes combined with a nucleon-photon vertex that automatically fulfills a Ward-Takahashi identity for on-shell nucleons. The calculated behaviour of G_E^p(Q^2)/G_M^p(Q^2) on 2<Q^2(GeV^2)<6 agrees with that inferred from polarisation transfer data, and exhibits a zero at Q^2\approx 6.5 GeV^2. There is some evidence that F_2(Q^2)/F_1(Q^2) \propto [\ln(Q^2/Λ^2)]^2/Q^2 for Q^2>6 GeV^2.

## Key claim

The quark-core contribution to nucleon electromagnetic form factors, calculated using Poincaré covariant Faddeev amplitudes and a Ward-Takahashi-identity-preserving nucleon-photon vertex, reproduces the polarisation-transfer data for the ratio μ_p G_E^p(Q^2)/G_M^p(Q^2) on 2 < Q^2 (GeV^2) < 6 and predicts a zero at Q^2 ≈ 6.5 GeV^2, but the existence of the zero is robust while its location depends on model parameters.

## Method

The nucleon is described as a bound state of a dressed quark and nonpointlike scalar and axial-vector diquark correlations, solved via a Poincaré covariant Faddeev equation. The dressed-quark propagator carries no free parameters, having been fixed in analyses of meson observables; the diquark mass scales were fitted to nucleon and Δ masses (Table 1). The nucleon-photon vertex was formulated to satisfy a Ward-Takahashi identity, introducing three parameters tied to the axial-vector diquark's magnetic dipole moment (μ_{1^+}), electric quadrupole moment (χ_{1^+}), and the strength of axial-vector ↔ scalar diquark transitions (κ_T).

## Key numbers

- The ratio μ_p G_E^p(Q^2)/G_M^p(Q^2) exhibits a zero at Q^2 ≈ 6.5 GeV^2.
- For Q^2 ∈ [2,6] GeV^2, the calculated ratio μ_p G_E^p(Q^2)/G_M^p(Q^2) agrees with polarisation transfer data.
- The ratio varies by less than 10% on 0 < Q^2 < 0.6 GeV^2 if the form factors are approximately dipole, because the proton's electric and magnetic radii are approximately equal.
- The weighted ratio √(Q^2) F_2(Q^2)/F_1(Q^2) is approximately constant for 2 ≲ Q^2 (GeV^2) ≲ 6, but this scaling relation fails for Q^2 ≳ 6 GeV^2.
- The perturbative QCD scaling Q^2/[ln(Q^2/Λ^2)]^2 · F_2(Q^2)/F_1(Q^2) = constant may be valid for Q^2 ≳ 6 GeV^2, with Λ = 0.94 GeV (the nucleon mass).

## Context

This work extends the covariant Faddeev approach of Hoell et al. (2004) by increasing numerical accuracy in the calculation of Faddeev amplitudes and form factors, and expanding the domain of the calculation. It addresses the marked discrepancy between Rosenbluth-separation and polarisation-transfer extractions of the proton form factor ratio for Q^2 ≳ 2 GeV^2, as reported by Jones, Gayou, Arrington, and Qattan (various years). The results enable tests of perturbative QCD scaling predictions by Belitsky (2003) and demonstrate that nonzero quark orbital angular momentum, a necessary outcome of Poincaré covariance, governs the large-Q^2 behaviour of the Pauli/Dirac form factor ratio.
