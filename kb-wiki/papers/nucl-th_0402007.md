# Role of quark-quark correlation in baryon structure and non-leptonic weak transitions of hyperons

> We study the role of quark-quark correlation in the baryon structure and, in particular, the hyperon non-leptonic weak decay, which is sensitive to the correlation between quarks in the spin-0 channel. We rigorously solve non-relativistic three-body problem for SU(3) ground state baryons to take into account the quark-pair correlation explicitly. With the suitable attraction in the spin-0 channel, resulting static baryon properties as well as the parity conserving weak decay amplitudes agree with the experimental values. Special emphasis is placed also on the effect of the SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry breaking on the baryon structure. Although the SU(6) breaking effects on the local behavior of the quark wave functions are considerable due to the spin-0 attraction, the calculated magnetic moments are almost the same as the naive SU(6) expectations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that introducing a short-range attractive correlation between quark pairs in the spin-0 channel, within a non-relativistic constituent quark model, can simultaneously reproduce both static baryon properties and the parity-conserving non-leptonic weak decay amplitudes of hyperons, which the standard constituent quark model fails to describe. The hedge is that this agreement depends on the specific choice of a Gaussian-shaped spin-dependent force and the use of a harmonic oscillator confinement potential, and the authors note that further refinement of the potential could improve the mass spectrum.

## Method

The authors solved the non-relativistic three-body Schrödinger equation for SU(3) ground-state baryons using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM), which employs Gaussian basis functions with range parameters in a geometric progression. They introduced a Hamiltonian with a two-body harmonic oscillator confinement potential and a Gaussian-shaped spin-dependent interaction that acts only on quark pairs with total spin 0, with the strength parameter \(C_{SS}\) and range \(\beta\) adjusted to reproduce the nucleon-delta mass difference and the proton charge radius. The weak decay matrix elements for the parity-conserving amplitudes of \(\Lambda\) and \(\Sigma\) hyperon decays were then calculated using the baryon pole approximation, with the weak Hamiltonian expressed in the non-relativistic limit as a two-body operator involving a delta function and a spin-projection operator \((1 - \vec\sigma_u \cdot \vec\sigma_s)\).

## Key numbers

- The model parameters were set to \(K = 0.007\ \text{GeV}^3\), \(\beta = 0.55\ \text{fm}\), and \(C_{SS}/m_u^2 \simeq 1.10\ \text{GeV}\), with quark masses \(m_u = m_d = 330\ \text{MeV}\) and \(m_s = 500\ \text{MeV}\).
- The calculated nucleon-delta mass splitting is \(m(\Delta) - m(N) \simeq 293\ \text{MeV}\).
- The calculated proton charge radius squared (valence core part) is \(\langle r^2_p \rangle_{\text{core}} = (0.60\ \text{fm})^2\), extracted from the experimental value \((0.86\ \text{fm})^2\) by subtracting vector meson dominance contributions.
- The calculated neutron charge radius squared is \(\langle r^2_n \rangle = -0.05\ \text{fm}^2\), compared to the experimental value \(-0.12\ \text{fm}^2\).
- The quark-pair correlation function at zero separation \(\rho^{(s)}(0)\) for the spin-0 case is about three times as large as that for the spin-1 case in the nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model approach of Isgur and Karl (Harmonic Oscillator model) and the MIT bag model, which both fail to reproduce the absolute magnitude of non-leptonic weak decay amplitudes, giving values about half of experiment. The authors incorporate a short-range spin-0 quark-pair correlation motivated by instanton liquid models and QCD sum rules, as suggested by Bando and others, and show that this correlation can enhance the \(\Delta I = 1/2\) weak decay amplitudes to match data. The study enables future work to apply the same framework to other hyperon decay channels and to refine the potential form beyond the harmonic oscillator confinement.
