# The fermion dynamical symmetry model for the even--even and even--odd nuclei in the Xe--Ba region

> The even--even and even--odd nuclei $^{126}$Xe-$^{132}$Xe and $^{131}$Ba-$^{137}$Ba are shown to have a well-realized $SO_8 \supset SO_6 \supset SO_3$ fermion dynamical symmetry. Their low-lying energy levels can be described by a unified analytical expression with two (three) adjustable parameters for even--odd (even--even) nuclei that is derived from the fermion dynamical symmetry model. Analytical expressions are given for wavefunctions and for $E2$ transition rates that agree well with data. The distinction between the FDSM and IBM $SO_6$ limits is discussed. The experimentally observed suppression of the the energy levels with increasing $SO_5$ quantum number $τ$ can be explained as a perturbation of the pairing interaction on the $SO_6$ symmetry, which leads to an $SO_5$ Pairing effect for $SO_6$ nuclei.

## Key claim

The even–even and even–odd nuclei in the Xe–Ba region (¹²⁶Xe–¹³²Xe and ¹³¹Ba–¹³⁷Ba) can be described by a unified analytical expression derived from the fermion dynamical symmetry model (FDSM) with an SO₈ ⊃ SO₆ ⊃ SO₃ chain, but the agreement is limited to low-lying states with τ ≤ 3; for higher τ values in heavier isotopes the pure SO₆ prediction fails and requires a perturbative treatment of the pairing interaction to produce an SO₅ τ-compression effect.

## Method

The authors constructed an FDSM Hamiltonian using Casimir operators of SO₈, SO₆, and SO₅, imposing the symmetry conditions G₀ = G₂ for even–even nuclei and an additional condition B₁ − B₃ = b₁ + b₃ for even–odd nuclei. Energy eigenvalues were derived analytically for both even–even (three adjustable parameters: g₆, g′₅, g′ᴵ) and even–odd (two additional parameters beyond those from the even–even core: g₅ and gⱼ) systems. Wavefunctions were built using vector coherent state techniques, coupling collective wavefunctions with one-fermion states via SO₆ ⊃ SO₅ ⊃ SO₃ Clebsch–Gordan coefficients. E2 transition rates were computed using an operator that includes both the standard P²(i) term and an additional (D†D̃)² term to allow Δτ = 0, ±2 transitions.

## Key numbers

* For even–even Xe isotopes, the SO₃ parameter g′ᴵ was fixed at 11.9 keV across the isotopic chain.
* For even–odd Xe and Ba isotopes, the parameter gⱼ was taken as 35.3 keV for all nuclei except ¹²⁷Xe.
* The relation g′₅ ≅ −g₆ was found empirically from the fitted parameters, corresponding to the IBM SO₆ limit relation A/4 ≅ B.
* The energy formula for even–even nuclei reproduced spectra with three parameters; for even–odd nuclei, two extra parameters (with gⱼ constant except for ¹²⁷Xe) reproduced about 15 levels each for ¹²⁷–¹³³Xe and ¹³¹–¹³⁵Ba.
* The SO₅ τ-compression effect was modeled by adding a term −B′[τ(τ+3)]² to the energy formula, significantly improving agreement for ¹²⁶Xe.

## Context

This work extends the FDSM framework, previously applied to the Pt region (Pan et al., 1995), to the A=130 (Xe–Ba) region where both protons and neutrons occupy the 6th shell with SO₈ᵖ × SO₈ⁿ symmetry. It provides an alternative to the interacting boson model (IBM) and nuclear supersymmetry (NSUSY) approaches of Iachello (1980) and Jolie et al. (1987), which used U(6/4) or U(6/20) supergroups. The FDSM description is fermionic from the outset, incorporates Pauli effects, and yields spectral formulas essentially identical to NSUSY for even nuclei but differing for odd nuclei in the number of SO₆ and SO₅ Casimir operators and in the treatment of the SO₃ parameters. The work enables systematic comparison of FDSM and IBM predictions for E2 transitions and quadrupole moments, and identifies the SO₅ pairing effect as a mechanism for the observed τ-compression in heavier Xe isotopes.
