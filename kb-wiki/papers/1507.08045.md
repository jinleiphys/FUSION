# Unraveling the pattern of the $XYZ$ mesons

> We present a plausible mechanism for the origin of the $XYZ$ mesons in the heavy meson spectra within a standard quark-model picture. We discuss the conditions required for the existence of four--quark bound states or resonances contributing to the heavy meson spectra, being either compact or molecular. We concentrate on charmonium and bottomonium spectra, where several new states, difficult to understand as simple quark-antiquark pairs, have been reported by different experimental collaborations. The pivotal role played by entangled meson-meson thresholds is emphasized.

## Key claim

The authors propose that four-quark bound states (contributing to the XYZ meson spectra) can emerge only when three conditions are simultaneously met: (i) the two allowed two-meson thresholds are close in energy, (ii) the higher threshold system exhibits an attractive interaction, and (iii) there is a strong coupling between the two threshold channels. This mechanism is restrictive enough to avoid predicting a proliferation of bound states.

## Method

The authors solved the four-body Schrodinger equation using the Hyperspherical Harmonic formalism with the constituent quark cluster model (CQCM) for charmonium and bottomonium systems. They analyzed the convergence of the four-quark energy as a function of hyperangular momentum K for different quantum numbers, comparing the energy to the lowest two-meson threshold. They also varied the energy gap between the two allowed thresholds to study the conditions under which bound states appear.

## Key numbers

- The lowest thresholds for the bottom counterpart of X(3872) (b bbar n nbar with L=0, S=1, I=0, C=+1, P=+1) are B Bbar* at 10611 MeV and Upsilon omega at 10155 MeV, separated by 456 MeV.
- When the threshold energy difference Delta = E[(b nbar)(n bbar)] - E[(b bbar)(n nbar)] was reduced to approximately 50 MeV or less, a bound state emerged; for Delta greater than about 50 MeV, no bound state was found.
- In the charmonium sector, 20 XYZ states have been reported above the D Dbar threshold (15 neutral, 5 charged); in the bottom sector, 2 charged and 1 neutral state have been reported.
- Only 8 of the 23 reported states have been observed independently by two different collaborations with significance greater than 5 sigma: X(3872), X(3915), chi_c2(2P), G(3900), Y(4140), Y(4260), Y(4360), and Z_c+(3900).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Vijande (2007) and Carames (2009) on four-quark states in the charmonium sector, where no compact four-quark bound states were found for any quantum numbers. The authors show that the same mechanism that produces the X(3872) as a molecular state with dominant D Dbar* component can also explain other XYZ states, but only under restrictive conditions. The results provide a pattern to be tested against ongoing experiments at BESIII, LHC, Belle II, and Panda, as well as lattice QCD calculations by Liu (2012).
