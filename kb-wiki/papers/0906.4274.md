# Potential for measurement of the tensor polarizabilities of nuclei in storage rings by the frozen spin method

> The frozen spin method can be effectively used for a high-precision measurement of the tensor electric and magnetic polarizabilities of the deuteron and other nuclei in storage rings. For the deuteron, this method would provide the determination of the deuteron's polarizabilities with absolute precision of order of $10^{-43}$ cm$^3$.

## Key claim
The frozen spin method in storage rings can measure the tensor electric and magnetic polarizabilities of the deuteron with absolute precision of order \(10^{-43}\) cm\(^3\), but only when using tensor-polarized initial beams; with vector-polarized beams the precision is about an order of magnitude worse (\(5\times10^{-42}\) cm\(^3\)).

## Method
The author derived the matrix Hamiltonian for spin-1 nuclei in a storage ring under frozen spin conditions, including the correction terms from tensor electric polarizability \(\alpha_T\) and tensor magnetic polarizability \(\beta_T\). The evolution equations for both vector and tensor polarization of the deuteron beam were solved analytically, and the sensitivity to each polarizability was estimated by comparing the spin precession signals with those expected from a deuteron electric dipole moment (EDM) experiment.

## Key numbers
- For a vector-polarized initial beam, the sensitivity to the tensor electric polarizability is \(\delta\alpha_T \approx 5\times10^{-42}\) cm\(^3\), corresponding to an EDM sensitivity of \(1\times10^{-29} e\cdot\text{cm}\).
- For a tensor-polarized initial beam, the absolute precision improves to \(\delta\alpha_T \sim 10^{-43}\) cm\(^3\) (relative precision \(\sim 10^{-1}\)).
- The tensor magnetic polarizability can be measured with absolute precision \(\delta\beta_T \sim 10^{-43}\) cm\(^3\), giving a relative precision \(\delta\beta_T/\beta_T \sim 10^{-3}\).
- Three theoretical predictions for the deuteron tensor electric polarizability are given: \(\alpha_T = -6.2\times10^{-41}\) cm\(^3\) (CGS), \(-6.8\times10^{-41}\) cm\(^3\) (JL), and \(3.2\times10^{-41}\) cm\(^3\) (FP).
- The theoretical estimate for the deuteron tensor magnetic polarizability is \(\beta_T = 1.95\times10^{-40}\) cm\(^3\) (CGS, JL).

## Context
This work extends the Baryshevsky effect (Baryshevsky and co-workers, 2000s) and earlier calculations by Silenko (PRC, 2008) for measuring tensor polarizabilities in storage rings. The frozen spin method, originally proposed for EDM searches (Storage Ring EDM collaboration), is shown to be applicable to polarizability measurements, with tensor-polarized beams offering a significant advantage in systematic error suppression. The results enable experimental tests of nuclear force theories by comparing measured polarizabilities with the three existing theoretical predictions.
