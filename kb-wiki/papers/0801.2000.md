# Elastic parton scattering and non-statistical event-by-event mean-pt fluctuations in Au + Au collisions at RHIC

> Non-statistical event-by-event mean-pt fluctuations in Au + Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 and 200 GeV are analyzed in AMPT with string-melting, and the results are compared with STAR data. The analysis suggests that in-medium elastic parton scattering may contribute greatly to the mean-p_t fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Furthermore, it is demonstrated that non-statistical event-by-event mean-pt fluctuations can be used to probe the initial partonic dynamics in these collisions. The comparison shows that with an in-medium elastic parton scattering cross section sigma_p=10 mb, AMPT with string-melting can well reproduce sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV data on the centrality dependence of non-statistical event-by-event mean-pt fluctuations. The comparison also shows that the fluctuation data for sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV Au + Au collisions can be well reproduced with sigma_p between 6 and 10 mb.

## Key claim

In-medium elastic parton scattering may contribute greatly to non-statistical event-by-event mean-pt fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, beyond the contributions from jets/minijets alone. The AMPT model with string-melting can reproduce STAR data only when the in-medium elastic parton scattering cross section sigma_p is set to 10 mb for sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV, or between 6 and 10 mb for sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model with string-melting to simulate Au + Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 and 200 GeV. They generated three classes of events: class I with both partonic interactions and hadronic cascade, class II with partonic interactions but no hadronic cascade, and class III with neither partonic interactions nor hadronic cascade (essentially HIJING events). Non-statistical event-by-event mean-pt fluctuations were quantified using two measures, Delta sigma_{pt:n} and <Delta p_{t,i} Delta p_{t,j}>, for charged hadrons with |eta| < 1 and 0.15 < pt < 2 GeV/c, and compared with STAR data as a function of collision centrality.

## Key numbers

* For mid-central Au + Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV (impact parameter 5 < b < 7 fm) with sigma_p = 10 mb and both partonic and hadronic processes (class I): Delta sigma_{pt:n} = 70.3 +/- 1.3 MeV/c and <Delta p_{t,i} Delta p_{t,j}> = 68.5 +/- 1.3 (MeV/c)^2.
* For the same collisions with sigma_p = 10 mb but without hadronic cascade (class II): Delta sigma_{pt:n} = 79.9 +/- 1.4 MeV/c and <Delta p_{t,i} Delta p_{t,j}> = 73.9 +/- 1.3 (MeV/c)^2.
* For the same collisions with neither partonic nor hadronic cascade (class III, HIJING-like): Delta sigma_{pt:n} = 29.7 +/- 1.1 MeV/c and <Delta p_{t,i} Delta p_{t,j}> = 21.1 +/- 0.8 (MeV/c)^2.
* For the same collisions with sigma_p = 3 mb and both partonic and hadronic processes (class IV): Delta sigma_{pt:n} = 31.7 +/- 1.4 MeV/c and <Delta p_{t,i} Delta p_{t,j}> = 29.0 +/- 1.2 (MeV/c)^2.
* The STAR data for sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV are well reproduced with sigma_p = 10 mb; for sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, sigma_p between 6 and 10 mb reproduces the data.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies that found HIJING (which lacks partonic and hadronic cascades) significantly underestimates STAR data on mean-pt fluctuations (STAR, 2002; STAR, 2004). It builds on the AMPT model framework developed by Lin et al. (2005) and Lin and Ko (2002), which had already reproduced elliptic flow and pion interferometry at RHIC. The results suggest that non-statistical event-by-event mean-pt fluctuations can serve as a probe of initial partonic dynamics, complementing other observables like jet quenching and Mach cone structures. The analysis also connects to the cluster model of Broniowski et al. (2004), where fluctuations increase with the number and size of correlated particle clusters.
