# Nuclear Symmetry Energy in Relativistic Mean Field Theory

> The Physical origin of the nuclear symmetry energy is studied within the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory. Based on the nuclear binding energies calculated with and without mean isovector potential for several isobaric chains we conform earlier Skyrme-Hartree-Fock result that the nuclear symmetry energy strength depends on the mean level spacing $ε(A)$ and an effective mean isovector potential strength $κ(A)$. A detaied analysis of isospin dependence of the two components contributing to the nuclear symmetry energy reveals a quadratic dependence due to the mean-isoscalar potential, $\simεT^2$, and, completely unexpectedly, the presence of a strong linear component $\simκT(T+1+ε/κ)$ in the isovector potential. The latter generates a nuclear symmetry energy in RMF theory that is proportional to $E_{sym}\sim T(T+1)$ at variance to the non-relativistic calculation. The origin of the linear term in RMF theory needs to be further explored.

## Key claim

The nuclear symmetry energy in relativistic mean field (RMF) theory follows a \(T(T+1)\) dependence, contrary to the expectation that a Hartree approximation would yield only a quadratic \(T^2\) term; the origin of the unexpectedly large linear component in the isovector potential remains unexplained.

## Method

The authors performed RMF calculations for ten isobaric chains (A=40, 48, 56, 88, 100, 120, 140, 160, 164, 180) using three effective Lagrangians (NL3, TM1, PK1). They separated the total binding energy into contributions from the isoscalar fields (sigma and omega mesons) and the isovector field (rho meson) by switching the rho-meson coupling on and off, then extracted the mean level spacing \(\varepsilon(A)\) and the effective isovector potential strength \(\kappa(A)\) from the energy differences along each chain.

## Key numbers

- The mean level spacing \(\varepsilon(A)\) after rescaling by the effective mass \(m^*/m\) (0.595 for NL3, 0.634 for TM1, 0.6055 for PK1) falls within the empirical bounds \(53/A \leq \varepsilon^{\text{emp}} \leq 66/A\) MeV.
- The nuclear symmetry energy coefficient from a least-square fit to RMF calculations with PK1 is \(a_{\text{sym}}^{\text{(RMF)}} = 133.20/A - 220.27/A^{4/3}\) MeV.
- The volume and surface contributions to the mean level spacing are \(\varepsilon = 130.42/A - 127.83/A^{4/3}\) MeV.
- The volume and surface contributions to the effective isovector potential strength are \(\kappa = 144.77/A - 342.08/A^{4/3}\) MeV.
- The asymptotic symmetry energy coefficient extrapolated to infinite nuclear matter is \(a_{\text{sym}}' \approx 33.6\) MeV, about 10% smaller than the infinite symmetric nuclear matter values (37.4 MeV for NL3, 36.9 MeV for TM1, 37.6 MeV for PK1).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the nuclear symmetry energy previously performed in the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) model by Satula and Wyss (2003, 2005), confirming that the isoscalar field generates a \(\varepsilon T^2\) contribution governed by the mean level density rather than kinetic energy. The key new finding is that RMF theory, despite being a Hartree approximation, produces a strong linear term in the isovector potential that makes the total symmetry energy follow \(T(T+1)\), similar to the empirical Duflo-Zuker mass formula (1995) and the shell-model results of Talmi (1956, 1962) and Zeldes (1996). The discrepancy between the finite-nucleus extrapolation and infinite nuclear matter values requires further study, and the physical mechanism restoring the linear term in RMF remains an open question.
