# Shadowing, Binding and Off-Shell Effects in Nuclear Deep Inelastic Scattering

> We present a unified description of nuclear deep inelastic scattering (DIS) over the whole region $0<x<1$ of the Bjorken variable. Our approach is based on a relativistically covariant formalism which uses analytical properties of quark correlators. In the laboratory frame it naturally incorporates two mechanisms of DIS: (I) scattering from quarks and antiquarks in the target and (II) production of quark-antiquark pairs followed by interactions with the target. We first calculate structure functions of the free nucleon and develop a model for the quark spectral functions. We show that mechanism (II) is responsible for the sea quark content of the nucleon while mechanism (I) governs the valence part of the nucleon structure functions. We find that the coherent interaction of $\bar qq$ pairs with nucleons in the nucleus leads to shadowing at small $x$ and discuss this effect in detail. In the large $x$ region DIS takes place mainly on a single nucleon. There we focus on the derivation of the convolution model. We point out that the off-shell properties of the bound nucleon structure function give rise to sizable nuclear effects.

## Key claim

The paper presents a unified description of nuclear deep inelastic scattering (DIS) across the entire Bjorken x region (0 < x < 1) using a relativistically covariant formalism based on analytical properties of quark correlators, but the authors find that the scaling contribution from mechanism (II) (quark-antiquark pair production) alone is insufficient to reproduce the empirical A-dependence of nuclear shadowing, requiring the inclusion of strongly correlated qqbar pairs propagating partly as vector mesons.

## Method

The authors develop a relativistically covariant formalism that separates DIS into two mechanisms: (I) scattering from quarks and antiquarks already present in the target, and (II) production of quark-antiquark pairs that subsequently interact with the target. They construct a model for quark spectral densities using a separation parameter s0 = 4 GeV² to distinguish low-energy (mechanism I) and high-energy (mechanism II) contributions, with the high-energy part connected to quark-nucleon and antiquark-nucleon forward scattering amplitudes via Pomeron and Reggeon exchange. The nucleon structure functions are fitted to NMC and neutrino data at Q² ≈ 5-10 GeV², and the formalism is extended to nuclear targets to study shadowing at small x and off-shell effects at large x.

## Key numbers

- The separation parameter between low-energy and high-energy parts of the quark spectrum is s0 = 2s̄ = 4 GeV²
- The cutoff parameters for the quark spectral functions are Λ_V² = 1.2 GeV², Λ_P² = 2.5 GeV², and Λ_R² = 4.0 GeV²
- The valence quark distribution requires n_V = 4 to give q^(I)(x→1) ∝ (1-x)³, consistent with quark counting rules
- The Pomeron and Reggeon exponents are n_P = n_R = 4, giving a (1-x)⁵ behavior for sea quark distributions at large x
- The average squared quark four-momentum in the target is ⟨k²⟩ ≈ -0.1 GeV², with average light-cone fraction ȳ ≈ 0.3
- The high-energy quark-nucleon and antiquark-nucleon cross sections are approximately σ_qN ≈ σ_q̄N ≈ (1/3)σ_pp ≈ 13 mb

## Context

This work extends the laboratory-frame approach to nuclear shadowing developed by Piller and Weise (1990) and others (Brodsky and Lu 1990, Nikolaev and Zakharov 1991) by providing a unified covariant treatment that bridges the small-x shadowing region and the large-x EMC-effect region. The authors point out that their results for the scaling contribution to shadowing differ from those of Brodsky and Lu (1990), and they conclude that vector meson propagation is necessary to reproduce the observed A-dependence. The paper enables future work on off-shell corrections to bound nucleon structure functions, which the authors argue are sizable and cannot be ignored in the standard convolution model used by Akulinichev et al. (1985), Dunne and Thomas (1986), and others.
