# On the superfluid properties of the inner crust of neutron stars

> We investigated the superfluid properties of the inner crust of neutron stars, solving the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations in spherical Wigner-Seitz cells. Using realistic two-body interactions in the pairing channel, we studied in detail the Cooper-pair and the pairing-field spatial properties, together with the effect of the proton clusters on the neutron pairing gap. Calculations with effective pairing interactions are also presented, showing significant discrepancies with the results obtained with realistic pairing forces. At variance with recent studies on finite nuclei, the neutron coherence length is found to depend on the strength of the pairing interaction, even inside the nucleus. We also show that the Wigner-Seitz approximation breaks down in the innermost regions of the inner crust, already at baryonic densities $ρ_b \ge 8 \cdot 10^{+13}$ g $\cdot$ cm$^{-3}$.

## Key claim

The Wigner-Seitz cell approximation for modeling the inner crust of neutron stars breaks down at baryonic densities ρb ≥ 8 × 10¹³ g·cm⁻³ (approximately 0.25ρ₀), where proton density profiles become unstable and boundary-condition effects become significant, contrary to the assumption that this approximation remains valid throughout the inner crust.

## Method

The authors solved the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations in spherical Wigner-Seitz cells using the SLy4 Skyrme functional for the mean field and three different pairing interactions: a density-dependent delta interaction, the finite-range Gogny D1 interaction, and low-momentum realistic interactions (Vlowk) derived from the Argonne AV18 potential via renormalization-group evolution. They studied 11 density regions from ρb ≈ 5 × 10¹¹ g·cm⁻³ to ρb ≈ 10¹⁴ g·cm⁻³, comparing the superfluid properties of the inner crust with those of infinite neutron matter to isolate the effect of proton clusters.

## Key numbers

- The Wigner-Seitz approximation breaks down at baryonic densities ρb ≥ 8 × 10¹³ g·cm⁻³ (approximately 0.25ρ₀) and definitely fails at ρb ≈ 10¹⁴ g·cm⁻³ (approximately 0.35ρ₀)
- The maximum neutron pairing gap obtained with Vlowk realistic interactions is 2.6 MeV, compared to 3.2 MeV for effective pairing interactions
- At saturation density, the Gogny D1 pairing gap is approximately 1.2 MeV, while the Vlowk pairing gap is approximately 0.2 MeV
- The presence of proton clusters reduces the neutron pairing gap by at most 10% at the maximum of the curve
- Neutron pairing gaps change by at most 30 keV for infinite neutron matter and 100 keV for Wigner-Seitz cells when the Vlowk cutoff Λ ranges between 1.8 fm⁻¹ and 4.0 fm⁻¹
- The inclusion of the Coulomb term in the Gogny D1 pairing interaction quenches proton pairing gaps by 20% to 30% in cells 1350Sn, 1800Sn, and 1500Zr

## Context

This work extends the seminal Hartree-Fock calculations of Negele (1973) on neutron star crusts by incorporating realistic pairing interactions derived from fundamental nucleon-nucleon forces, rather than relying solely on effective interactions fitted to stable nuclei. The results contradict earlier studies on finite nuclei by Pastore (2008), Vinas (2010), and Pillet (2010) which found that the neutron coherence length has little dependence on pairing interaction strength; the authors show that in the inner crust, where the Fermi energy lies in the continuum, the coherence length does depend on pairing strength even inside the nucleus. This work enables more reliable microscopic calculations of neutron star crust properties by demonstrating that realistic Vlowk pairing interactions should be preferred over effective interactions, and by establishing the density limit beyond which the Wigner-Seitz approximation cannot be trusted.
