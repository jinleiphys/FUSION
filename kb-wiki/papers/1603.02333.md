# Nucleon-deuteron scattering using the adiabatic projection method

> In this paper we discuss the adiabatic projection method, a general framework for scattering and reaction calculations on the lattice. We also introduce several new techniques developed to study nucleus-nucleus scattering and reactions on the lattice. We present technical details of the method for large-scale problems. To estimate the systematic errors of the calculations we consider simple two-particle scattering on the lattice. Then we benchmark the accuracy and efficiency of the numerical methods by applying these to calculate fermion-dimer scattering in lattice effective field theory with and without a long-range Coulomb potential. The fermion-dimer calculations correspond to neutron-deuteron and proton-deuteron scattering in the spin-quartet channel at leading order in the pionless effective field theory.

## Key claim

The adiabatic projection method, when combined with spherical-harmonic projection and radial binning, yields scattering phase shifts for nucleon-deuteron systems in the spin-quartet channel at leading order in pionless effective field theory, with systematic errors from the new techniques shown to be at most a few percent relative error in the phase shift.

## Method

The authors discretize space on a periodic cubic lattice with spacing \(a = 1.97\) fm and time with spacing \(a_t = 1.32\) fm, using an \(\mathcal{O}(a^4)\)-improved free Hamiltonian and a short-range two-fermion potential. They construct initial two-cluster states (fermion plus dimer) on the lattice, project them onto spherical harmonics with angular momentum quantum numbers \(\ell, \ell_z\), and group lattice points into radial rings of width \(a_R\). These states are evolved in Euclidean time using the microscopic transfer matrix to produce dressed cluster states, from which a radial adiabatic transfer matrix is built. Scattering phase shifts are extracted by imposing a spherical hard-wall boundary condition and matching the lattice spectrum to continuum scattering wave functions, with infinite-time extrapolation of the phase shifts.

## Key numbers

* Spatial lattice spacing \(a = 1.97\) fm; temporal lattice spacing \(a_t = 1.32\) fm.
* For the two-particle test system with an attractive Gaussian potential (strength \(-8.0\) MeV, range \(0.02\) MeV\(^{-1}\)), radial bin sizes \(a_R = 0.010\), \(0.125\), and \(0.250\) lattice units produce phase shifts with at most a couple percent relative error for partial waves \(\ell \leq 4\).
* For the fermion-dimer system, the absolute difference between the interacting and trivial adiabatic transfer matrices at Euclidean time step \(L_t = 15\) equals \(18\%\) of the corresponding trivial matrix element at the lattice site where the difference is maximum; physical distances for radial indices \(R, R' = 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11\) are 2.22, 3.20, 4.68, 6.16, 7.14, and 8.62 fm, respectively.
* The fermion-dimer reduced mass is \(\mu = 626\) MeV; the lattice cutoff is \(\pi a^{-1} \simeq 314\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the adiabatic projection method previously applied to alpha-alpha scattering (Elhatisari 2015) and to elastic neutron-deuteron scattering (Pine 2013, Elhatisari 2014, Rokash 2015). The new techniques of spherical-harmonic projection and radial binning, benchmarked here on fermion-dimer systems, make large-scale Monte Carlo calculations of nucleus-nucleus scattering more practical. The results enable future ab initio lattice calculations of scattering and reactions involving heavier nuclei, including systems with long-range Coulomb forces.
