# Possible existence of a dibaryon candidate $NΔ$ ($D_{21}$)

> Inspired by the experimental report by WASA-at-COSY Collaboration, we investigate the possibile existence of the dibaryon candidate $NΔ$ with quantum numbers $IJ^P=21^+$ ($D_{21}$). The dynamical calculation shows that we cannot obtain the bound $D_{21}$ state in the models which can obtain the experimental $d^{*}$. %although the $D_{21}$ state can bound in the range of parameters in quark models. The low-energy scattering phase shifts of the $N-Δ$ scattering give the same conclusion. Besides, the mass calculation by using the Gursey-Radicati mass formula and the analysis of the matrix elements of the color magnetic interaction show that the mass of $D_{21}$ is larger than that of $D_{12}$ ($NΔ$ with $IJ^P=12^+$), which indicate that it is less possible for the $D_{21}$ than the $D_{12}$ to form bound state.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the dibaryon candidate \(N\Delta\) (\(D_{21}\)) with quantum numbers \(IJ^P = 21^+\) cannot form a bound state in the quark models that successfully reproduce the experimental \(d^*\) (\(\Delta\Delta\) \(D_{03}\)) resonance, although a bound state appears in one parameter set (QDCSM2) that gives too low a mass for the \(d^*\).

## Method
The authors used two constituent quark models, the chiral quark model (ChQM) and the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM), with the resonating group method (RGM) to calculate the mass of the \(D_{21}\) state in single-channel and multi-channel calculations (including \(S\)-wave, \(D\)-wave, and hidden-color channels). They also computed low-energy \(N-\Delta\) scattering phase shifts using the Kohn-Hulthen-Kato variational method and extracted scattering lengths and effective ranges. Additionally, they applied the Gursey-Radicati mass formula and analyzed color magnetic interaction (CMI) matrix elements to compare masses of dibaryon states.

## Key numbers
- The mass of the \(D_{21}\) state in the single-channel calculation is 2179.8 MeV in both ChQM and QDCSM1, and 2176.5 MeV in QDCSM2, all above the \(N\Delta\) threshold of 2171 MeV.
- In the most complete coupling (cc3) in ChQM, the mass is 2176.6 MeV, still above threshold.
- The scattering length \(a_0\) is \(-1.9939\) fm in ChQM and \(-2.5159\) fm in QDCSM1 (negative, indicating unbound), while in QDCSM2 it is \(+6.0756\) fm (positive, indicating bound).
- The Gursey-Radicati mass formula gives masses of 2168 MeV for \(D_{12}\) and 2182 MeV for \(D_{21}\), with the \(D_{21}\) mass 14 MeV larger than that of \(D_{12}\).
- The CMI matrix element for \(D_{21}\) is \(80/3 C\), compared to \(16 C\) for \(D_{12}\), indicating a larger mass for \(D_{21}\).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of dibaryon systems, where they found that the \(d^*\) (\(D_{03}\)) is a tightly bound six-quark state (Goldman, Ping2, QDCSM0, QDCSM1, QDCSM2) and that \(N\Delta\) (\(D_{12}\)) could be a resonance near threshold (Ping2). It directly addresses the experimental report by the WASA-at-COSY Collaboration (2018) of a \(D_{21}\) candidate with mass 2140(10) MeV and width 110(10) MeV, and the prediction by Dyson and Xuong (1964) of a \(D_{21}\) state at 2160 MeV. The results contradict the Faddeev equation calculation by Gal and Garcilazo (Gal1, Gal2) which found \(D_{21}\) slightly below threshold, and they show that the spin-isospin symmetry between \(D_{12}\) and \(D_{21}\) is broken by one-gluon exchange. The work enables future searches for non-strange dibaryons to test the gluon exchange mechanism versus Goldstone boson exchange models.
