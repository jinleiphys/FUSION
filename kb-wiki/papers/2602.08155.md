# Shear viscosity of a massless quark-gluon gas in chemical equilibrium including all $2\leftrightarrow 2$ cross sections

> The analytical expressions of the shear viscosity of both one and two particle species with Boltzmann statistics and $2 \rightarrow 2$ elastic scatterings are known from the Chapman-Enskog method and have been shown to be quite accurate. The expression for a multi-species hadronic gas under $2 \rightarrow 2$ elastic scatterings is also known. Here we use the Chapman-Enskog method to derive the shear viscosity of a massless quark-gluon gas of $N_f$ quark flavors in chemical equilibrium subjected to all $2 \rightarrow 2$ parton scatterings including for the first time inelastic scatterings. We then verify the relation in a general single-species limit, where the shear viscosity of the quark-gluon gas should reduce to the result for a single particle species. In addition, we show the explicit analytical result in terms of the seven independent cross sections for the special case of isotropic and energy-independent cross sections. The analytical relations derived here can be useful for determining the shear viscosity of parton transport models with any $2 \rightarrow 2$ scattering cross sections. They can also be coupled with finite temperature QCD cross sections to help study the shear viscosity of the quark gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The authors derive the first analytical expression for the shear viscosity of a massless quark-gluon gas in chemical equilibrium that includes all $2 \leftrightarrow 2$ scatterings (both elastic and inelastic) using the first-order Chapman-Enskog method with Boltzmann statistics, but the result is only a first-order approximation and higher-order terms would change the result by several percent.

## Method

The authors apply the Chapman-Enskog method to a system of massless gluons and $N_f$ flavors of massless quarks in chemical equilibrium, using Boltzmann statistics. They compute the collision matrix elements $C_{ij}^{00}$ for all parton species by evaluating collision brackets that separate into elastic terms ($\tilde{c}_0$, $\tilde{c}_1$, $\tilde{c}_2$) and inelastic terms ($\tilde{\omega}$) for each scattering channel. The shear viscosity is then obtained by inverting the collision matrix and applying $\eta = (T/10) \vec{\gamma}^T \mathbf{C}^{-1} \vec{\gamma}$, yielding a general expression in terms of the matrix elements $C_{ij}^{00}$.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity for a single species of massless partons with isotropic and energy-independent cross section $\sigma$ is $\eta_1^{\rm iso} = 6T/(5\sigma)$.
- For the $N_f=0$ case (gluons only), the general result reduces to $\eta = 160T/C_{gg}^{00} = 160T/\tilde{c}_0[g]$.
- For massless partons, the inner product coefficient $\gamma_{i,0} = \gamma_0 = 40$ for $r=0$.
- In chemical equilibrium, the number fractions are $x_g = 8/(8+6N_f)$ and $x_q = x_{\bar q} = 3(1-\delta_{0N_f})/(8+6N_f)$.
- For isotropic and energy-independent cross sections, the shear viscosity is $\eta^{\rm iso} = (12T/5)(A/B)$ where $A$ and $B$ are polynomials in the seven independent cross sections.

## Context

This work extends the Chapman-Enskog method previously applied to single-species systems (Plumari 2012, MacKay 2022) and multi-species hadronic gases with only elastic scatterings (Wiranata 2013) to include inelastic $2 \leftrightarrow 2$ scatterings for the first time in a quark-gluon gas. The results enable parton transport models (Zhang 1997, Lin 2004, Xu 2007, Kurkela 2022) to compute shear viscosity analytically from any $2 \leftrightarrow 2$ cross sections, and can be coupled with finite-temperature QCD cross sections to study the shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma, complementing the AMY framework (Arnold 2000, 2003) which includes both $2\leftrightarrow2$ and $1\leftrightarrow2$ interactions.
