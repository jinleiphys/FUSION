# Generator coordinate method with proton--neutron pairing fluctuations and magnetic properties of $N=Z$ odd--odd nuclei

> Pairing correlations play an important role in a variety of nuclear phenomena. However, a quantitative understanding of proton--neutron $(pn)$ pairing, especially isoscalar $pn$ pairing $(S=1, T=0)$ remains elusive. To clarify the property of $pn$ pairing, we investigate the roles of $pn$ pairing in the $M1$ transition of $N=Z$ odd--odd nuclei. We develop a theoretical model based on the generator coordinate method (GCM) in which the isoscalar and isovector $pn$-pair amplitudes are used as the generator coordinates. Using the particle and the angular-momentum projections, the $pn$-pair GCM well reproduces the $M1$ transition of odd--odd nuclei for the exactly solvable SO(8) model. We apply the method to $N=Z$ odd--odd nuclei and find that the experimental values of $B(M1)$ are well reproduced. We also study the sensitivity of $B(M1)$ to the strength of the isoscalar pairing interaction.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the generator coordinate method (GCM) with isoscalar and isovector proton-neutron pair amplitudes as generator coordinates can accurately reproduce experimental $M1$ transition strengths in $N=Z$ odd-odd nuclei, but the sensitivity of $B(M1)$ to the isoscalar pairing strength is weak for realistic values of the ratio $g_{\rm pp}=G_{\rm IS}/G_{\rm IV} < 2$, making $B(M1)$ a poor probe of isoscalar pairing in that regime.

## Method

The authors develop a two-dimensional generator coordinate method (GCM) using the isoscalar pair amplitude $P_0$ and isovector pair amplitude $R_0$ as generator coordinates, applied to axially symmetric proton-neutron mixed Hartree-Bogoliubov (HB) states. Particle-number projection (for protons and neutrons) and angular-momentum projection are performed on the HB states before solving the Hill-Wheeler equation. The method is first validated against exact solutions of the SO(8) model for $N=Z=5$ systems, then applied to realistic $sd$-shell nuclei $^{18}$F, $^{22}$Na, and $^{26}$Al using single-particle energies from the Skyrme SkP energy density functional.

## Key numbers

- For $^{18}$F, the experimental energy difference $E(1^+_1)-E(0^+_1) = -1.04$ MeV is reproduced with $g_{\rm pp}=1.51$, $G_{\rm IV}=1$ MeV, and $G_{\rm ph}=20.8/A^{0.7}$ MeV
- The calculated magnetic moment of the $1^+_1$ state in $^{18}$F is $\mu = 0.825 \mu_N$ (orbital part $0.059 \mu_N$, spin part $0.767 \mu_N$)
- For $^{22}$Na, the calculated magnetic moment is $\mu = 0.832 \mu_N$, overestimating the experimental value $0.535 \pm 0.10 \mu_N$
- The energy difference $E(1_1^+)-E(0_1^+)$ for $^{22}$Na is reproduced as $-0.07$ MeV (experimental: $-0.07$ MeV), and for $^{26}$Al as $1.97$ MeV (experimental: $0.83$ MeV)
- In the SO(8) model test with $\Omega=12$ and $N=Z=5$, the $pn$-pair GCM reproduces exact $B(M1)$ values across the full range $x=-1$ to $x=1$ with high precision
- The norm cutoff is set to $0.65$ for realistic calculations, yielding collective dimension 8 for $J=0$ and $J=1$ states

## Context

This work extends the generator coordinate method approach of Engel (1997) and Hinohara (2014) by incorporating both isoscalar and isovector proton-neutron pairing fluctuations simultaneously, applied for the first time to odd-odd $N=Z$ nuclei. The study builds on the three-body model calculations of Tanimura (2014) that demonstrated the importance of $(S=1,T=0)$ interactions in $M1$ transitions, and on the AMD calculations of Kanada-En'yo (2014) that analyzed spatial $pn$ correlations in $^{18}$F. The results enable future investigations of low-energy Gamow-Teller transitions observed by Fujita (2014, 2019) and may help determine whether realistic $N=Z$ nuclei are close to the critical point of isoscalar pair condensation suggested by Yoshida (2014).
