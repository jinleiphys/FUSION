# Two body final states production in electron-positron annihilation and their contributions to $(g-2)_μ$

> In this paper, we study the processes of $e^{+}e^{-}$ annihilation into two body final states, either two pseudoscalar mesons or one meson with a photon. The hadronic vacuum polarization form factors are calculated within the framework of resonance chiral theory in the energy region of $E \lesssim 2$ GeV, with final state interactions taken into account. A joint analysis on the processes of $e^{+}e^{-} \rightarrow π^{+}π^{-}$, $K^{+}K^{-}$, $K_{L}^{0}K_{S}^{0}$, $π^{0}γ$, and $ηγ$ has been performed, and the latest experimental data are included. Based on the vacuum polarization form factors of these processes, we estimate their contributions to the lowest order of anomalous magnetic moment of the muon, $(g-2)_μ$. Combined with other contributions from hadronic vacuum polarization and other interactions from the standard model, the discrepancy between theoretical prediction and experimental measurement is $Δa_μ=(24.1\pm5.4)\times 10^{-10}$, i.e., 4.5$σ$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a combined theoretical analysis of five electron-positron annihilation processes into two-body final states, using resonance chiral theory with final state interactions, yields a discrepancy between the standard model prediction and experimental measurement of the muon anomalous magnetic moment of Δaμ = (24.1 ± 5.4) × 10⁻¹⁰ (4.5σ), but this result depends on including all other standard model contributions and assumes the theoretical framework correctly describes the hadronic vacuum polarization in the energy region up to 2 GeV.

## Method

The authors calculated hadronic vacuum polarization form factors for e⁺e⁻ → π⁺π⁻, K⁺K⁻, K_L⁰K_S⁰, π⁰γ, and ηγ within resonance chiral theory (RChT) in the energy region E ≲ 2 GeV, including final state interactions via the Omnès function method. They performed two joint fits to experimental data: Fit A covering energies up to 1.1 GeV with only ground-state vector resonances (ρ, ω, φ), and Fit B extending to 2.3 GeV by including two heavier vector resonance multiplets (V' and V'') with Breit-Wigner propagators. The unknown RChT coupling constants were constrained by QCD high-energy constraints from operator product expansion matching and by fitting to decay widths of V → Pγ, P → Vγ, and P → γγ alongside the cross-section data.

## Key numbers

- The combined discrepancy between standard model prediction and experimental measurement of the muon anomalous magnetic moment is Δaμ = (24.1 ± 5.4) × 10⁻¹⁰, corresponding to 4.5σ significance
- The experimental average for the muon anomalous magnetic moment is aμ(Exp) = 116592061(41) × 10⁻¹¹ (0.35 ppm) from Fermilab and previous measurements
- The pion decay constant used is F ≈ 92.2 MeV
- Lightest vector resonance masses taken as Mρ = 773.80 MeV, Mω = 782.48 MeV, Mφ = 1019.20 MeV, with widths Γω = 8.67 MeV and Γφ = 3.85 MeV
- The η-η' mixing angle from Fit A is θP = -20.74(16)°, and from Fit B is θP = -20.50(30)°
- The ω-φ mixing angle from Fit A is θV = 38.60(1)°, and from Fit B is θV = 38.62(1)°
- The ρ-ω mixing angle is δ = -1.80(1)° in both fits
- The vector coupling FV = 0.148(1) GeV in Fit A and 0.138(1) GeV in Fit B
- Heavier resonance masses from Fit B: Mρ' = 1.519(1) GeV, Mω' = 1.250(3) GeV, Mφ' = 1.656(3) GeV, Mρ'' = 1.720(1) GeV, Mω'' = 1.725(2) GeV, Mφ'' = 2.160(1) GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis in Qin (2020) by including the e⁺e⁻ → K_L⁰K_S⁰ process for a complete ππ-KKbar coupled channel analysis, and adding e⁺e⁻ → π⁰γ and ηγ processes for the first time in this RChT framework. The approach builds on the resonance chiral theory formalism of Ecker (1988, 1989) and Dai (2013), and contrasts with data-driven methods such as those by Colangelo (2018), Davier (2019), and Keshavarzi (2019) by providing a systematic theoretical tool for the middle-energy region (Mρ ≤ E ≤ 2 GeV). The results enable improved theoretical predictions for the hadronic vacuum polarization contribution to (g-2)μ and help address the tension between data-driven and lattice QCD predictions noted by Ce (2022) and Borsanyi (2020).
