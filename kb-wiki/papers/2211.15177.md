# High energy nucleus-nucleus collision and halo radii in different approaches of Glauber theory

> The complete Glauber calculation of the differential cross sections of $^{12}$C--$^{12}$C and halo nuclei on $^{12}$C scattering was performed using the previously proposed in Refs.~\cite{Shabelski:2021iqk, Shabelski:2022xkw} method of generating function. The results are different as compared with the similar calculations in the optical model and the rigid target approximation. The halo nuclei radii extracted from the scattering data via the complete Glauber analysis come out to be larger than those obtained in the approximate approaches

## Key claim

The complete Glauber calculation yields halo nuclei radii that are systematically larger (by about 10%) than those extracted using the optical model approximation, because screening corrections reduce the calculated cross section and therefore require a larger radius to match experimental data.

## Method

The authors performed complete Glauber calculations of elastic and reaction cross sections for nucleus-nucleus scattering using a generating function method that analytically accounts for all Glauber diagrams without additional approximations. They compared these results with the optical model and rigid target approximations for \(^{12}\)C–\(^{12}\)C scattering and for halo nuclei (\(^{8}\)B, \(^{11}\)Li, \(^{11}\)Be, \(^{14}\)Be) scattering on \(^{12}\)C, using Gaussian parameterizations for nucleon densities and adjusting root-mean-square radii to match experimental interaction cross sections.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{12}\)C–\(^{12}\)C scattering at about 1 GeV per nucleon, the experimental reaction cross section is \(\sigma^r = 853 \pm 6\) mb (Ozawa 2000, Ozawa 2001).
- The extracted \(^{12}\)C root-mean-square radius is 2.19 fm in the optical model, 2.27 fm in the rigid target approximation, and 2.44 fm in the complete Glauber calculation.
- For \(^{8}\)B (core \(^{7}\)Be + p), the core radius is 2.47 fm; the extracted halo radii range from 2.93 to 3.36 fm depending on halo parametrization and experimental cross section.
- For \(^{11}\)Be (core \(^{10}\)Be + n), the core radius is 2.44 fm; the extracted halo radii are 6.21 fm (Gaussian), 5.45 fm (O-type), and 5.46 fm (2S-type).
- For \(^{11}\)Li (core \(^{9}\)Li + 2n), the core radius is 2.47 fm; the extracted halo radii are 5.51 fm (Gaussian), 5.35 fm (O-type), and 5.36 fm (2S-type).
- For \(^{14}\)Be (core \(^{12}\)Be + 2n), the core radius is 2.70 fm; the extracted halo radii are 5.42 fm (Gaussian), 5.53 fm (O-type), and 5.55 fm (2S-type).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous generating function method (Shabelski 2021, Shabelski 2022) for complete Glauber calculations and directly compares it with the optical model (Czyz 1969) and rigid target approximation (Bialas 1977, Alkhazov 1977). The results show that both core and halo radii extracted via complete Glauber analysis are about 10% larger than those from the optical model (Hassan 2015), consistent with earlier suggestions that more complicated halo structure requires larger density radii (Al-Khalili 1996). This enables more accurate extraction of nuclear radii from scattering data and highlights the importance of using the complete Glauber formalism rather than approximate methods.
