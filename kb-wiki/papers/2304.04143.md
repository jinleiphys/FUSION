# Entanglement Structures in Quantum Field Theories II: Distortions of Vacuum Correlations Through the Lens of Local Observers

> When observing a quantum field via detectors with access to only the mixed states of spatially separated, local regions -- a ubiquitous experimental design -- the capacity to access the full extent of distributed entanglement can be limited, shrouded by classical correlations. By performing projective measurements of the field external to two detection patches and classically communicating the results, underlying pure states may be identified for which entanglement quantification is clear. In the Gaussian continuous-variable states of the free scalar field vacuum, this protocol uncovers a disparity between the spacelike entanglement established within the field and that which is locally detectable. This discrepancy is found to grow exponentially with the separation between observation regions. The protocol developed herein offers insight and practical guidance for clarifying the unavoidable distortion of quantum field correlations when viewed from the vantage of a pair of local observers.

## Key claim

By performing projective measurements of the field external to two detection patches and classically communicating the results, underlying pure states can be identified for which entanglement quantification is clear, revealing a disparity between the spacelike entanglement established within the field and that which is locally detectable; this discrepancy grows exponentially with the separation between observation regions.

## Method

The authors analyze the free scalar field vacuum on a one-dimensional lattice using Gaussian continuous-variable quantum information formalism. They compare two scenarios: tracing out the external field volume (representing standard local observation) versus projectively measuring the external volume in either the field or conjugate momentum basis and classically communicating the results to identify underlying pure states in the patch Hilbert space. The entanglement between two disjoint patches is quantified using logarithmic negativity computed from the covariance matrices of the resulting states.

## Key numbers

- The locally accessible entanglement (under volume tracing) vanishes at a finite dimensionless separation of $\tilde{r}_{\Nslash} = 264$ for massless fields with patch size $d = 16$ sites, while the underlying entanglement (under volume measurement) persists at all separations
- The correlation matrix elements in the massive regime decay as $\sim -\sqrt{\frac{m}{2\pi r^3}} e^{-m r}$ for the field correlators and $\sim \frac{e^{-mr}}{\sqrt{2\pi m r}}$ for the inverse correlation matrix elements
- In the massless regime, the inverse correlation matrix elements decay logarithmically as $\sim -\left( \ln(mr)+\gamma-\ln[2]\right)/\pi$, where $\gamma$ is the Euler-Mascheroni constant
- The correlation matrix elements in the massless regime decay polynomially as $\sim \frac{4}{\pi - 4 \pi \hat{r}^2}$

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of mixed-state entanglement between disjoint patches of the free scalar field vacuum by Marcovitch (2008), Calabrese (2012), and Klco (2020, 2021), which found that accessible entanglement vanishes at finite separations despite the known presence of spacelike entanglement at all distances in continuum quantum fields (Reeh 1961, Summers 1985, 1987). The protocol developed here enables rigorous understanding of how classical correlations generated by tracing external degrees of freedom distort the observable quantum entanglement, with implications for interpreting measurements from local detectors in quantum simulation and heavy ion collision experiments.
