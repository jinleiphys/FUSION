# Scattering of soft, cool pions

> Consider the effective lagrangian for pions in the chiral limit, computed to leading order in an expansion about zero temperature. To describe the scattering of pions with small momenta, it is necessary to include not just the usual shift in the pion decay constant, but also a new, nonlocal term, which is precisely analogous to the hard thermal loops of hot gauge theories.

# Scattering of soft, cool pions

## Key claim

For the scattering of pions with momenta much smaller than the temperature (soft) and temperature much smaller than the pion decay constant (cool), the effective chiral lagrangian must include not only the usual temperature-dependent shift in the pion decay constant but also a new, nonlocal term analogous to hard thermal loops in hot gauge theories; this nonlocal term is as important as the original two-derivative term in the chiral lagrangian for soft scattering processes.

## Method

The authors use the background field method, redefining the chiral field as $g \rightarrow g \exp(i\pi/f_\pi)$ and expanding to quadratic order in the pion fluctuations $\pi$. They compute the one-loop effective action for the background field $R_\mu = g^\dagger \partial_\mu g$ by integrating out the hard pion modes (with momenta on the order of the temperature $T$), isolating the hard thermal loop contributions that dominate when the external momenta are soft. The resulting nonlocal term is expressed in coordinate space using a null four-vector $\hat{K}^\mu = (i,\hat{k})$ integrated over all directions, with the structure $\int F (1/(D\cdot\hat{K})^2) F$.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant at zero temperature is $f_\pi = 93$ MeV in QCD.
- The temperature-dependent pion decay constant to leading order is $f_\pi(T) = \left(1 - \frac{N}{24} \frac{T^2}{f_\pi^2}\right) f_\pi$, where $N$ is the number of light quark flavors.
- The hard thermal loop correction to the self energy of $R_\mu$ involves the integral $\frac{T^2}{24}(\delta^{\mu\nu} - \delta\Pi^{\mu\nu}(P))$, where the $T^2/24$ factor sets the scale of the nonlocal term.
- For elastic scattering of two pions of different isospin in the forward direction, the leading amplitude from the nonlocal term is $\frac{2}{9}\delta^{ac}\delta^{bd} (T^2/f_\pi^2) (p^2/f_\pi^2)$, while the tree-level term from ${\cal L}_2$ vanishes for this kinematic configuration.
- The anomalous correction $\delta{\cal L}_5$ has coefficient $-\frac{i N_c T^2}{144 \pi^2 f_\pi^2}$, where $N_c = 3$ is the number of colors in QCD.

## Context

This work extends the standard chiral perturbation theory framework of Weinberg (1979) and Gasser and Leutwyler (1984) to nonzero temperature, specifically to the regime where both temperature and momenta are small compared to $f_\pi$. The nonlocal term is precisely analogous to the hard thermal loops discovered by Pisarski (1989) and Braaten and Pisarski (1990) in hot gauge theories, and the authors use the formalism of Braaten and Pisarski (1992) to write the effective lagrangian in coordinate space. The results enable computation of novel scattering amplitudes for Goldstone bosons at nonzero temperature, with potential applications to spin waves in ferromagnets and antiferromagnets, and suggest that the known hard thermal loops are the first terms in an infinite series of nonlocal effective lagrangians at nonzero temperature.
