# The complex life of hydrodynamic modes

> We study analytic properties of the dispersion relations in classical hydrodynamics by treating them as Puiseux series in complex momentum. The radii of convergence of the series are determined by the critical points of the associated complex spectral curves. For theories that admit a dual gravitational description through holography, the critical points correspond to level-crossings in the quasinormal spectrum of the dual black hole. We illustrate these methods in ${\cal N}=4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory in 3+1 dimensions, in a holographic model with broken translation symmetry in 2+1 dimensions, and in conformal field theory in 1+1 dimensions. We comment on the pole-skipping phenomenon in thermal correlation functions, and show that it is not specific to energy density correlations.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the hydrodynamic derivative expansion for dispersion relations has a finite radius of convergence determined by branch point singularities (level-crossings) in the complex momentum plane, and that the pole-skipping phenomenon in thermal correlation functions occurs within the convergence domain for strongly coupled N=4 SYM theory but outside it for other models, indicating no direct relation between the two phenomena.

## Method

The authors treat hydrodynamic dispersion relations as Puiseux series in complex momentum, analyzing the analytic structure of the associated complex spectral curves derived from the poles of retarded two-point functions. They compute exact and numerical dispersion relations for shear and sound modes in holographic models: the strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory in 3+1 dimensions via holographic duality, a holographic model with broken translation symmetry in 2+1 dimensions (the Davison-Goutéraux model), and conformal field theory in 1+1 dimensions. The analysis involves studying quasinormal mode level-crossings in the dual black hole spectra and examining pole-skipping singularities in retarded correlation functions of the energy-momentum tensor.

## Key numbers

* For the strongly coupled N=4 SYM theory, the radius of convergence for the sound mode dispersion relation is |q_c^sound| = sqrt(2) (in units where q = |q|/(2πT)).
* For the strongly coupled N=4 SYM theory, the radius of convergence for the shear mode dispersion relation is |q_c^shear| ≈ 1.49 (in units where q = |q|/(2πT)).
* The pole-skipping point for both sound and shear modes in N=4 SYM occurs at |q_*| = sqrt(3/2) and |w_*| = 1 (in units where w = ω/(2πT) and q = |q|/(2πT)).
* In the Davison-Goutéraux holographic model with broken translation symmetry, the hydrodynamic series converges with radius |q_c| = 1/2, while the pole-skipping point is at |q_*| = sqrt(2).
* In the Davison-Goutéraux model, the exact dispersion relation for diffusive modes is w(q) = -i/2 (1 - sqrt(1 - 4q^2)), with branch points at q_c = ±1/2.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Withers (2018), who studied convergence of the shear-diffusion mode dispersion relation in a holographic Reissner-Nordstrom-AdS4 model and found a finite radius of convergence from a branch point at purely imaginary momentum. The paper also builds on the pole-skipping phenomenon identified by Grozdanov (2017) and Blake (2017, 2018) for energy density correlations, showing that pole-skipping occurs in all retarded functions of T^{μν} in N=4 SYM theory, not just energy density correlations. The results enable systematic study of hydrodynamic series convergence through complex spectral curves and clarify that pole-skipping singularities are not directly related to the convergence radius of the hydrodynamic derivative expansion.
