# New level density parameter beyond Egidy-Bucurescu's systematics

> Extending beyond the Egidy-Bucurescu systematics, the nuclear level density parameters (LDPs) for the back-shifted Fermi gas model were compiled. Three forms of LDPs were fitted: the liquid-drop model (LDM), the droplet model (DM), and the power-law dependence on mass number A. Additionally, the root-mean-square deviations (RMSDs) of the new LDPs and existing literature values were calculated. The newly fitted global LDM-type parameters outperform the commonly used Toke-Swiatecki parameters in various statistical model calculations. In contrast, neither the global nor the combined DM-type parameters yielded satisfactory results. Among the tested parameter sets, the widely adopted Reisdorf parameters exhibited the best overall performance, as evidenced by the larger number of experimental data points falling within their narrower RMSD confidence intervals. For the power-law A-dependence, the new global parameters performed better than the existing homogeneous ones. The ground-state deformation and isospin correction factors had minimal overall impact on the LDP fits. However, the current results suggest that theoretical calculations for transitional nuclei should account for ground-state deformation effects.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Egidy-Bucurescu systematics of nuclear level density parameters (LDPs) for the back-shifted Fermi gas model by compiling new experimental data and refitting three forms of LDP formulas, but the newly fitted global and combined droplet-model-type parameters did not yield satisfactory results, and the widely used Reisdorf parameters still exhibited the best overall performance.

## Method

The authors compiled a dataset of experimental LDP values for the back-shifted Fermi gas model, including data from the Egidy-Bucurescu systematics (310 nuclides) and newer measurements from the Oslo method and proton evaporation spectra. They performed least-squares fitting of three empirical formula forms: liquid-drop model (LDM) with volume and surface terms, droplet model (DM) with an additional curvature term, and a power-law dependence on mass number A. For each form, they fitted both global parameters (over the full mass range A=18-252) and combined parameters (separately for three mass intervals: 18-100, 100-180, and 180-252), and calculated root-mean-square deviations (RMSDs) to compare with existing literature parameter sets.

## Key numbers

* The newly fitted global LDM-type parameters gave RMSD σ_G = 2.078 MeV^{-1}, outperforming the Toke-Swiatecki parameters with σ_3 = 2.175 MeV^{-1}.
* The newly fitted global DM-type parameters gave RMSD σ_G = 2.31 MeV^{-1}, while the combined DM parameters gave RMSD σ_C = 4.641 MeV^{-1}.
* For the power-law A-dependence, the new global parameters gave RMSD σ_G = 2.175 MeV^{-1}, while parameter set a_33 (from Iljinov 1980) had 126 experimental data points within its ±5% confidence interval versus 121 for the new global parameters.
* The Reisdorf parameters (a_12) had 132 experimental data points within their ±5% confidence interval, the most among the five best DM-type parameter sets.
* The global LDM-type fit without ground-state deformation gave a = 0.057A + 0.265A^{2/3}, while with deformation it gave a = 0.045A + 0.322A^{2/3}B_s.

## Context

This work extends the systematic compilation of LDPs by von Egidy and Bucurescu (2005, 2009) by incorporating newer experimental data from the Oslo method and from proton evaporation spectra in lithium-induced reactions (Voinov 2024). The paper enables improved selection of LDP parameters for statistical model calculations, particularly recommending the Reisdorf parameters (Reisdorf 1981) for DM-type calculations and noting that for nuclides near mass number 208, relatively smaller LDP values should be chosen. The results also indicate that ground-state deformation effects should be accounted for in transitional nuclei (A=60-150 and A=190-220), while isospin correction factors had minimal impact on the fits.
