# First Extraction of Transverse Momentum Dependent Helicity Distributions

> We report on the first global analysis of transverse momentum dependent helicity distributions of the proton. The analysis is performed at next-to-leading order with the evolution factor at next-to-next-to-leading-logarithmic accuracy. Nonzero signals are determined for up and down quarks and their $k_T$-integrated polarization are consistent with analyses in collinear factorization, while the distributions of other flavors are loosely constrained by existing data. With increasing transverse momentum, quarks at large $x$ become less polarized while those at small $x$ become more polarized.

## Key claim

This paper reports the first global extraction of transverse momentum dependent (TMD) helicity distributions of the proton, performed at next-to-leading order with next-to-next-to-leading-logarithmic evolution. The analysis finds nonzero signals for up and down quarks, but the distributions of sea quarks and gluons remain loosely constrained by existing data; the qualitative behavior of polarization with transverse momentum depends on the longitudinal momentum fraction \(x\), with opposite trends at large and small \(x\).

## Method

The authors perform a global fit of longitudinal double-spin asymmetry (DSA) data from semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) measured by HERMES and CLAS, using TMD factorization at NLO and NNLL accuracy. The TMD helicity distributions are parametrized at the saddle point of the evolution equations in \(b\)-space, with the collinear helicity distributions from NNPDFpol1.1 as input, and evolved to the hard scale \(Q\) using the \(\zeta\)-prescription. Unpolarized TMD PDFs and FFs are taken from the SV19 simultaneous fit. A total of 253 data points with \(P_{hT}/(zQ) < 0.5\) and \(Q > 1\ \mathrm{GeV}\) are included, and uncertainties are estimated from 1000 replicas.

## Key numbers

* Total \(\chi^2/N = 0.74\) for 253 data points from HERMES and CLAS.
* HERMES \(e^\pm p \to e^\pm h X\): 84 data points, \(\chi^2/N = 0.72\).
* HERMES \(e^\pm d \to e^\pm h X\): 160 data points, \(\chi^2/N = 0.71\).
* CLAS \(e^- p \to e^- \pi^0 X\): 9 data points, \(\chi^2/N = 1.43\).
* The analysis uses 17 free parameters; for \(\bar{u}\), \(\bar{d}\), \(s\), \(\bar{s}\), and gluons, the parameters \(\alpha_f = \beta_f = \epsilon_f = 0\) are fixed, and \(N_s = N_{\bar s}\).

## Context

This work provides the first extraction of TMD helicity distributions, extending previous collinear helicity analyses such as NNPDFpol1.1 (Nocera, 2014) and the SV19 unpolarized TMD analysis (Scimemi, 2019). The results are consistent with collinear helicity distributions for \(u\) and \(d\) quarks in the data-covered region up to \(x \sim 0.3\), and the observed decrease of polarization with transverse momentum at large \(x\) qualitatively supports the Wigner rotation effect discussed by Ma (1991, 1992). The analysis enables future precision tests at the Electron-Ion Collider and Jefferson Lab, and a concurrent analysis by Bacchetta (2024) appeared during the review process.
