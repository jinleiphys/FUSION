# Anisotropic pressure in dense neutron matter under the presence of a strong magnetic field

> Dense neutron matter with recently developed BSk19 and BSk21 Skyrme effective forces is considered in magnetic fields up to $10^{20}$ G at zero temperature. The breaking of the rotational symmetry by the magnetic field leads to the differentiation between the pressures along and perpendicular to the field direction which becomes significant in the fields $H>H_{th}\sim10^{18}$ G. The longitudinal pressure vanishes in the critical field $10^{18}<H_c\lesssim10^{19}$ G, resulting in the longitudinal instability of neutron matter. For the Skyrme force fitted to the stiffer underlying equation of state (BSk21 vs. BSk19) the threshold $H_{th}$ and critical $H_c$ magnetic fields become larger. The longitudinal and transverse pressures as well as the anisotropic equation of state of neutron matter are determined under the conditions relevant for the cores of magnetars.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in ultrastrong magnetic fields (above about 10^18 G), the pressure anisotropy in dense neutron matter prevents the formation of a fully spin polarized state, contrary to earlier claims that such a state could form at fields above 10^19 G. The longitudinal pressure vanishes at critical fields below 10^19 G, causing a longitudinal instability that limits spin polarization to mild values.

## Method

The authors used a Fermi liquid approach with Skyrme effective interactions (BSk19 and BSk21 parametrizations) to calculate thermodynamic properties of spin polarized neutron matter at zero temperature in magnetic fields up to 10^20 G. They solved self-consistency equations for the single particle energy components and distribution functions, then computed the longitudinal and transverse pressures from the stress tensor to determine the anisotropic equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Threshold magnetic field for significant pressure anisotropy at density 4 times nuclear saturation: H_th ≈ 1.2 × 10^18 G for BSk19 force and H_th ≈ 2.2 × 10^18 G for BSk21 force
- Critical magnetic field where longitudinal pressure vanishes at density 4 times nuclear saturation: H_c ≈ 1.7 × 10^18 G for BSk19 force and H_c ≈ 3.2 × 10^18 G for BSk21 force
- Critical magnetic field at density 6 times nuclear saturation: H_c ≈ 3.4 × 10^18 G for BSk19 force and H_c ≈ 6.3 × 10^18 G for BSk21 force
- Maximum spin polarization at critical field at density 4 times nuclear saturation: Π_c ≈ -0.42 for BSk19 force and Π_c ≈ -0.26 for BSk21 force
- Maximum spin polarization at critical field at density 6 times nuclear saturation: Π_c ≈ -0.13 for BSk19 force and Π_c ≈ -0.20 for BSk21 force
- Magnetic field for full spin polarization at density 4 times nuclear saturation: H ≈ 1.3 × 10^19 G for BSk19 force and H ≈ 4.3 × 10^19 G for BSk21 force
- Neutron magnetic moment: μ_n ≈ -6.031 × 10^-18 MeV/G

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of neutron matter in strong magnetic fields by Perez-Garcia (2008) and by Ferrer, Incera, Keith, Portillo, and Springsteen (2010) by including both interparticle interactions and the pressure anisotropy effect. It contradicts the conclusion of Broderick, Prakash, and Lattimer (2000) that a fully spin polarized state could form in magnetar cores, showing instead that the longitudinal instability prevents complete polarization. The results enable more realistic modeling of magnetar structure using anisotropic equations of state and axisymmetric Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.
