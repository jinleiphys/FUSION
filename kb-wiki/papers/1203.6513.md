# Particle spectra in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV

> Particle production in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV is studied in the (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model. The shapes of the calculated transverse momentum spectra of pions, kaons, protons, Xis, and Omegas are in satisfactory agreement with preliminary data of the ALICE Collaboration, while the particle ratio proton/pion is slightly overpredicted, and the strange barion yields are underpredicted.

## Key claim

The (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model with bulk viscosity can reproduce the shapes of transverse momentum spectra for pions, kaons, protons, Xis, and Omegas in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, but the proton/pion ratio is slightly overpredicted (by 17%) and strange baryon yields are underpredicted (by 30-40%), indicating that a single effective chemical freeze-out temperature cannot describe all measured particle ratios.

## Method

The authors solve (3+1)-dimensional second-order viscous hydrodynamic equations with the Israel-Stewart formalism, using a constant shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of η/s=0.08 and bulk viscosity ζ/s=0.04 or 0.08 in the hadronic phase only. Initial entropy density profiles are constructed from a Glauber model with a mixture of participant nucleon and binary collision densities, with longitudinal parameters adjusted to reproduce charged particle pseudorapidity distributions. Particles are emitted from the freeze-out hypersurface using the Cooper-Frye formula with shear and bulk viscosity corrections to the equilibrium distribution, and spectra are generated via a Monte-Carlo statistical emission code.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: η/s = 0.08 (constant)
- Bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio in hadronic phase: ζ/s = 0.04 and ζ/s = 0.08
- Initial time for hydrodynamic expansion: 0.6 fm/c
- Optimal freeze-out temperature: T_f = 140 MeV (with ζ/s = 0.08)
- Alternative freeze-out temperature tested: T_f = 150 MeV
- Glauber model parameters: nuclear radius R_A = 6.48 fm, skin depth a = 0.535 fm, nucleon-nucleon cross section σ = 62 mb
- Longitudinal profile parameters: η_0 = 2.3, σ_η = 1.4
- Mixing parameter for binary collisions: α = 0.15
- Bulk viscosity corrections increase proton number by 35%
- Ratio p/π overpredicted by 17%
- Ratios Ξ/π and Ω/π underpredicted by 30-40%
- Effective chemical equilibrium temperature from nonequilibrium corrections: approximately 150 MeV

## Context

This work extends (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic calculations from Au-Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV (Bozek, 2011) to Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies, where the longitudinal plateau half-width increases from η_0 = 1.5 to η_0 = 2.3. The results contradict the simple assumption of a common chemical freeze-out temperature from statistical models (Braun-Munzinger, 2001; Andronic, 2005; Cleymans, 2004), consistent with ALICE observations that proton yields are lower than expected. The approach enables predictions for rapidity distributions of identified hadrons and shows that (2+1)-D hydrodynamic models are a good approximation at midrapidity (|y| < 1), while also demonstrating that different equilibration rates for different particle species would be needed to improve agreement with data.
