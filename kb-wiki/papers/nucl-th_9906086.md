# Electron-deuteron scattering in a current-conserving description of relativistic bound states: including meson-exchange-current contributions

> Using a three-dimensional formalism that includes relativistic kinematics, the effects of negative-energy states, approximate boosts of the two-body system, and current conservation we calculate the electromagnetic form factors of the deuteron up to $Q^2=6$ GeV$^2$. This is done both in impulse approximation and with a $ρπγ$ meson-exchange current included. The experimentally-measured quantities $A$, $B$, and $T_{20}$ are calculated over the kinematic range probed in recent Jefferson Laboratory experiments. The meson-exchange current provides significant strength in $A$ at large $Q^2$, but has little impact on $B$ or $T_{20}$.

## Key claim

Using a three-dimensional formalism that includes relativistic kinematics, negative-energy states, approximate boosts, and current conservation, the authors calculate deuteron electromagnetic form factors up to Q^2=6 GeV^2; the meson-exchange current provides significant strength in the structure function A at large Q^2 but has little impact on B or T20, yet the calculation overpredicts the JLab data for A at Q^2 > 4 GeV^2 and disagrees with B data already below Q^2=1 GeV^2.

## Method

The authors construct an NN interaction based on one-boson exchange, place it in a three-dimensional relativistic scattering equation derived from the Bethe-Salpeter equation with a resummation of crossed-ladder graphs to ensure the correct one-body limit, and fit the sigma-meson coupling to NN data. They then calculate the deuteron electromagnetic form factors FC, FQ, and FM using a conserved current that includes both impulse approximation and a rho-pi-gamma meson-exchange current, with the single-nucleon form factors taken from the Mergell et al. parametrization based on vector-meson dominance.

## Key numbers

- The structure function A is calculated up to Q^2 = 6 GeV^2, the range probed by recent Jefferson Laboratory experiments.
- T20 has been measured at Q^2 up to almost 2 GeV^2, B out to about 1.3 GeV^2.
- The sigma-meson coupling is adjusted from g_sigma^2/(4 pi) = 8.08 to g_sigma^2/(4 pi) = 8.55 when negative-energy states are included.
- The rho-pi-gamma coupling is g_{rho pi gamma} = 0.563, extracted from the decay rho -> pi gamma.
- The rho-pi-gamma MEC contribution to A scales as Q^{-20} at large Q.
- The calculation reproduces T20 data out to Q^2 approximately 2 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the three-dimensional relativistic formalism developed by Phillips and Wallace (1996, 1997, 1998) to include meson-exchange currents for electron-deuteron scattering, building on earlier relativistic treatments such as Hummel and Tjon (1990) while eliminating some approximations made there. The results enable comparison with recent Jefferson Laboratory experiments and highlight the need for additional mechanisms beyond the rho-pi-gamma MEC to describe the structure functions A and B, particularly at high Q^2, while the successful description of T20 demonstrates the importance of relativistic effects in that observable.
