# Baryon as a Quantum Hall Droplet and the Cheshire Cat Principle

> We show that the recent proposal to describe the $N_f=1$ baryon in the large number of color limit as a quantum Hall droplet, can be understood as a chiral bag in a 1+2 dimensional strip using the Cheshire cat principle. For a small bag radius, the bag reduces to a vortex line which is the smile of the cat with flowing gapless quarks all spinning in the same direction. The disc enclosed by the smile is described by a topological field theory due to the Callan-Harvey anomaly out-flow. The chiral bag carries naturally unit baryon number and spin $\frac 12 N_c$. The generalization to arbitrary $N_f$ is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the recent proposal to describe the $N_f=1$ baryon in the large $N_c$ limit as a quantum Hall droplet can be understood as a chiral bag in 1+2 dimensions using the Cheshire cat principle, but only for a small bag radius where the bag reduces to a vortex line (the "smile of the cat") with gapless quarks all spinning in the same direction.

## Method

The authors construct a 1+2 dimensional chiral bag model as an annulus of radius $R$ lying in the $xy$-plane, clouded by an $\eta^\prime$ field with a $2\pi$ monodromy. They impose chiral bag boundary conditions on free 2-dimensional quarks of charge $e$ along the radial $x$-direction, then analyze the spectral flow of the Dirac equation in the limit of zero bag radius. The anomaly out-flow from the bag is captured by an emergent U(1) gauge field described by a topological field theory, using the Callan-Harvey mechanism to connect the 1+1 dimensional anomaly to the 1+2 dimensional parity anomaly.

## Key numbers

- The chiral bag carries unit baryon number and spin $\frac{1}{2} N_c$ (from the abstract).
- At the magic angle $\Delta\theta = \pi$, the charge fractionalizes as $\Delta Q = e \Delta\theta / 2\pi$, with half the charge in and half out.
- The gapless mode at the magic angle has energy $E_{-1} \sim k_y$ running in the $y$-direction with fixed helicity.
- The Chern-Simons coupling for the emergent U(1) field is $\kappa = e^2/2\pi$, leading to a statistical phase of $\pi$ for particle exchange.
- For $N_c$ quarks with baryon number $1/N_c$, the flux attachment factor becomes $\kappa = N_c/2\pi$, giving the emergent Abelian Chern-Simons term $\frac{N_c}{4\pi} \int_{1+2} A dA$.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Komargodski (2019) that baryons in large $N_c$ QCD with $N_f=1$ are edge excitations of 1+2 dimensional quantum Hall droplets, which was motivated by the absence of chiral soliton representations since $\pi_3(U(1))=0$. The paper connects this to the Cheshire cat principle developed by the authors and collaborators (Rho, Zahed, and others, 1980s-1990s) and to the Callan-Harvey anomaly out-flow mechanism (Callan and Harvey, 1985). The generalization to arbitrary $N_f$ is discussed, yielding a non-Abelian Chern-Simons action $\frac{N_c}{4\pi} \int_{1+2} \text{Tr}(\mathbb{A} d\mathbb{A} + \frac{2}{3} \mathbb{A}^3)$. The work enables further investigation of the relationship between highly spinning baryons and skyrmions in the spin-isospin tower $J=I=1/2, ..., N_c/2$, and suggests potential applications to dense matter in neutron stars and dark matter candidates.
