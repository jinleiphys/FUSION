# Investigation of 3/2$_2^-$ state of $^{9}$Li nucleus with microscopic structure and reaction models

> The low-lying states of the $^{9}$Li nucleus are investigated with a unified framework of microscopic structure and reaction models. In the structure model, the wave function is fully antisymmetrized and the $^{9}$Li nucleus is described as an $α$ + $t$ + $n$ + $n$ four-body system, and low-lying 1/2$^{-}$, 3/2$^{-}$, 5/2$^{-}$, and 7/2$^{-}$ states are obtained by the stochastic multi-configuration mixing method. Using these wave functions, the quasi-elastic cross section at $E/A$ = 60 MeV and the elastic and inelastic cross sections at $E/A$ = 50 MeV on the $^{12}$C target are calculated in the framework of the microscopic coupled channel (MCC) method. The characteristic inelastic angular distribution is seen in the 3/2$_{2}^{-}$ state, whose $α+t$ cluster structure and valence neutron configurations are discussed in detail. We find the possibility of triaxial deformation and mixing of di-neutron components in the $^{9}$Li nucleus.

## Key claim

The 3/2₂⁻ state of ⁹Li exhibits a characteristic quadrupole-dominated inelastic angular distribution, which the authors interpret as evidence for triaxial deformation and mixing of di-neutron components, but this conclusion is conditional on the bound-state approximation used for states above the neutron threshold.

## Method

The ⁹Li nucleus was described as a fully antisymmetrized α + t + n + n four-body system using the stochastic multi-configuration mixing method, with the Volkov No.2 central potential and G3RS spin-orbit interaction. The resulting wave functions were used in microscopic coupled channel (MCC) calculations with the complex G-matrix interaction CEG07 to compute quasi-elastic cross sections at E/A = 60 MeV and elastic/inelastic cross sections at E/A = 50 MeV on a ¹²C target, with the imaginary part renormalized by a factor N_W = 0.6 fitted to quasi-elastic data.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the ground 3/2₁⁻ state: 41.04 MeV (experimental value 45.34 MeV)
- Neutron threshold opens at E_x = 4.0639 MeV, reproduced in the calculation
- Matter radius of ground state: 2.459 fm (experimental: 2.32(2) fm from Tanihata (1988), 2.44(6) fm from Dobrovolsky (2006))
- B(E2; 1/2₁⁻ → 3/2₁⁻) = 8.778 e²fm⁴ (experimental: 6.8(3) e²fm⁴ from Fallon (2013))
- Proton Q_J moment of ground state: -2.651 e fm² (experimental: -2.53(9) e fm² from Arnold (1988))
- Neutron Q_J moment of ground state: -3.333 fm²; 3/2₂⁻ state: 4.343 fm²
- Renormalization factor N_W = 0.6 fixed from quasi-elastic data at E/A = 60 MeV

## Context

This work extends the α + t + n + n cluster model of Arai (2001) and the stochastic multi-configuration mixing method of Ichikawa (2011) and Mutoh (2011) to unify structure and reaction calculations for ⁹Li. The finding that the 3/2₂⁻ state has K = 3/2 character with prolate deformation and quadrupole-dominated excitation contrasts with the monopole-dominated transitions seen in the 3/2₃⁻ and 3/2₄⁻ states. The results enable future studies of core excitation effects in ¹¹Li reactions, where ⁹Li serves as the core nucleus, and suggest that multi-step coupling from projectile excitation is essential for describing inelastic scattering to this state.
