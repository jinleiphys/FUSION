# Charmonium production in ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions with two-photon processes

> We calculate the production of large-pTcharmonium and narrow resonance state (exotic charmonium) in proton-proton, proton-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus collisions with the semi-coherent two-photon interactions at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC), Large Hadron Collider (LHC), and Future Circular Collider (FCC) energies. Using the large quasi-real photon fluxes, we present the γγ->H differential cross section for charmonium and narrow resonance state production at large transverse momentum in ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions. The numerical results demonstrate that the experimental study of ultra-peripheral col-lisions is feasible at RHIC, LHC, and FCC energies.

## Key claim

The paper calculates that large-transverse-momentum charmonium and narrow resonance states can be produced via semi-coherent two-photon interactions in ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions, but the feasibility is conditional: for proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions the differential cross sections are "not prominent" at RHIC and LHC energies, becoming non-negligible only at Future Circular Collider (FCC) energies, while for nucleus-nucleus collisions the cross sections are enhanced by a factor of Z^4 and become evident at all three collider energies.

## Method

The authors use the equivalent photon approximation, replacing the electromagnetic field of ultrarelativistic protons or nuclei with a flux of quasi-real photons. They adopt a semi-coherent two-photon approach where one photon is hard and incoherently emitted (carrying large transverse momentum) while the other is soft and coherently emitted, then compute the differential cross section dσ/d²p_T dy for charmonium states (J/ψ, ψ(2S), η_c(1S), η_c(2S), χ_c0(1P), χ_c2(1P)) and narrow resonance states (X(3940), X(4140), Z(3930), X(4160), Y_p(3930), X(3915)) in pp, pA, and AA collisions at RHIC, LHC, and FCC energies. The nuclear form factor is taken as a monopole with Λ=0.091 GeV for ¹⁹⁷Au and Λ=0.088 GeV for ²⁰⁸Pb, while the proton form factor is the electric dipole form F_p(q²)=1/[1+q²/(0.71 GeV²)]².

## Key numbers

- For nucleus-nucleus collisions, the two-photon differential cross section scales as Z^4, enhancing it by a factor of Z^4 compared to proton-proton collisions and by Z^2 compared to proton-nucleus collisions.
- The monopole form factor parameter for ¹⁹⁷Au is Λ = 0.091 GeV; for ²⁰⁸Pb it is Λ = 0.088 GeV.
- The proton electric dipole form factor uses the scale 0.71 GeV² in the denominator.
- A single track acceptance condition imposes a lower cutoff on the photon transverse momentum: q_{iT} > 0.2 GeV.
- The differential cross section for charmonium and narrow resonance states is presented as a function of transverse momentum p_T and rapidity y at RHIC, LHC, and FCC energies (specific numerical cross section values are not given in the truncated text; the results are shown in figures).

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of two-photon processes in ultra-peripheral collisions (PHENIX, STAR, ALICE, CMS collaborations) by focusing on the transverse momentum distributions rather than total cross sections or rapidity distributions alone, which the authors note are more experimentally accessible. The semi-coherent approach differs from purely coherent or non-coherent treatments used in earlier work by Baltz (2009), Klein (2009), and others, and the authors cite prior studies of charmonium production via two-photon fusion by Kłusek-Gawenda (2014) and others. The results enable experimental searches for exotic charmonium states like X(3940), X(4140), and Z(3930) in ultra-peripheral collisions at current and future colliders, particularly at FCC where proton-proton and proton-nucleus cross sections become measurable.
