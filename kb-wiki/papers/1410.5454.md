# Baryon susceptibilities, nongaussian moments and the QCD critical point

> We calculate model-independently the impact of the critical point on higher order baryon susceptibilities $χ_n$, showing how they depend on fluctuations of the order parameter. Including all tree level diagrams, we find new contributions to $χ_4$ equally important to the kurtosis of the order parameter fluctuations, and we characterize the kurtosis and other nonguassian moments as functions on the phase diagram. Important features of this analysis are then confirmed by a Gross-Neveu model study with good agreement with other model studies as well as lattice and experimental data. This suggests the universality of the characteristic peak in baryon susceptibilities as a signal of the critical point. We discuss leveraging measurements of different $χ_n$ to extrapolate the location of the critical point.

# Baryon susceptibilities, nongaussian moments and the QCD critical point

## Key claim

The critical point's influence on higher-order baryon susceptibilities includes new contributions to χ₄ that are equally important as the kurtosis of order parameter fluctuations, and the characteristic peak in baryon susceptibilities may be a universal signal of the critical point, though non-monotonic behavior along a single freeze-out line is insufficient to establish proximity to the critical point.

## Method

The authors derive model-independent expressions for baryon susceptibilities χ₂, χ₃, and χ₄ by expanding the effective potential of the order parameter field σ around its minimum and computing tree-level contributions to the μ-derivatives of the partition function. They then confirm the analytical results with a numerical study of the 1+1 dimensional Gross-Neveu (GN) model in the large N limit, evaluating the susceptibilities and their ratios m₁ = Tχ₃/χ₂ and m₂ = T²χ₄/χ₂ as functions on the phase diagram.

## Key numbers

- The kurtosis κ₄ = ⟨δσ⁴⟩ − 3⟨δσ²⟩² = 6(T³/V³)(2(λ₃ξ)² − λ₄)ξ⁸, where ξ is the correlation length, λ₃ and λ₄ are cubic and quartic couplings of the fluctuation effective potential
- The second moment κ₂ = ⟨δσ²⟩ = (T/V)ξ² and third moment κ₃ = ⟨δσ³⟩ = −2λ₃(T²/V²)ξ⁶
- For the GN model with explicit symmetry breaking parameter γ = 0.1, the κ₄ < 0 region is delineated by a dot-dashed line with a cusp at the critical end point (CEP)
- The CEP conditions are g₂ = 5g₆v⁴, g₄ = −(10/3)g₆v², v⁵ = (3/8)(J/g₆) where v = ⟨σ⟩ is the order parameter vev
- The λ₃ = 0 line satisfies g₂ = (7/3)g₆v⁴ + J/v, g₄ = −(10/3)g₆v²
- The temperatures of the peaks are ordered T_max,m₁ > T_max,m₂ > T_CEP

## Context

This work extends the model-independent approach of Stephanov (2008, 2011) by including all tree-level contributions to χ₄ beyond just the σ field kurtosis κ₄, finding new terms of equal importance. The results agree qualitatively with NJL model studies by Asakawa (2009) and with lattice QCD simulations and heavy-ion collision data from RHIC. The analysis enables combining measurements of different susceptibility ratios m₁ and m₂ to extrapolate the location of the QCD critical point, though the authors caution that non-monotonic behavior along a single freeze-out curve is insufficient to establish proximity to the CEP.
