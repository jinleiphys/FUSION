# Phase structure of neutron $^{3}P_{2}$ superfluids in strong magnetic fields in neutron stars

> We discuss the effect of a strong magnetic field on neutron $^{3}P_{2}$ superfluidity. Based on the attraction in the $^{3}P_{2}$ pair of two neutrons, we derive the Ginzburg-Landau equation in the path-integral formalism by adopting the bosonization technique and leave the next-to-leading order in the expansion of the magnetic field $B$. We determine the $(T,B)$ phase diagram with temperature $T$, comprising three phases: the uniaxial nematic (UN) phase for $B=0$, D$_{2}$-biaxial nematic (BN) and D$_{4}$-BN phases in finite $B$ and strong $B$ such as magnetars, respectively, where D$_{2}$ and D$_{4}$ are dihedral groups. We find that, compared with the leading order in the magnetic field known before, the region of the D$_{2}$-BN phase in the $(T,B)$ plane is extended by the effect of the next-to-leading-order terms of the magnetic field. We also present the thermodynamic properties, such as heat capacities and spin susceptibility, and find that the spin susceptibility exhibits anisotropies in the UN, D$_{2}$-BN, and D$_{4}$-BN phases. This information will be useful to understand the internal structures of magnetars.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including next-to-leading-order terms in the magnetic field expansion (up to O(B⁴A²) and O(B²A⁴)) extends the region of the D₂-biaxial nematic phase in the (T,B) phase diagram compared to earlier leading-order results, but this conclusion is restricted to magnetic fields where the expansion parameter γₙ²B²/T² remains less than unity (B ≲ 1.6 × 10¹⁶ G for T near T_c0).

## Method

The authors derive the Ginzburg-Landau free energy for neutron ³P₂ superfluidity from the Bogoliubov-de-Gennes equation using path-integral bosonization, keeping terms up to sixth order in the gap field A and including magnetic field contributions up to O(B⁴A²) and O(B²A⁴). They then minimize the free energy numerically for a nematic order parameter parametrized by two variables A₀ and r, with the magnetic field aligned along the y axis, using parameters T_c0 = 0.2 MeV, n = 0.17 fm⁻³ (p_F = 338 MeV), and Landau parameter F₀ᵃ = -0.75.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature at zero magnetic field is T_c0 = 0.2 MeV.
- The neutron number density used is n = 0.17 fm⁻³, corresponding to Fermi momentum p_F = 338 MeV.
- The Landau parameter is set to F₀ᵃ = -0.75, referenced from ³He liquid at low temperature.
- A magnetic field of B = 10¹⁵ G (10¹¹ T), typical for magnetars, corresponds to γₙB/πT_c0 = 0.02 for T_c0 = 0.2 MeV.
- The upper limit for validity of the magnetic field expansion is B ≲ 1.6 × 10¹⁶ G (1.6 × 10¹² T), i.e., γₙB/(πT_c0) ≲ 0.3, based on the ratio β⁽⁴⁾B²/β⁽²⁾ ≈ γₙ²B²/T² being less than unity.

## Context

This work extends previous Ginzburg-Landau studies of neutron ³P₂ superfluidity in magnetic fields by Masuda (2015) and Mizushima (2016), which considered only leading-order magnetic field terms (O(B²A²)). The inclusion of next-to-leading-order terms (O(B⁴A²) and O(B²A⁴)) is motivated by the strong magnetic fields in magnetars (up to ~10¹⁵ G), where the neutron magnetic moment energy splitting (~10 keV) becomes comparable to the pairing gap scale (O(10²)-O(10³) keV). The results provide phase diagrams and thermodynamic properties (heat capacity, spin susceptibility) that could help interpret observations of magnetar internal structure, building on prior work on ³P₂ pairing by Tabakin (1968), Hoffberg (1970), Tamagaki (1970), and Takatsuka (1971).
