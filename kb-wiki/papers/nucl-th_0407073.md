# Bounds on the bound η^3He system

> We investigate the relation between the η^3He binding energy and width and the (complex) η^3He scattering length. Following our systematic analysis of the eta 3He scattering length we set limits on the η^3He binding. If bound states exist the binding energy (width) should not exceed 5 MeV (10 MeV). In addition, we comment on a recently claimed observation of an η-mesic ^3He quasi-bound state by the TAPS collaboration based on ηphotoproduction data. Although our limits are in reasonable agreement with the values reported by this collaboration, our analysis of these data does not lead to a solid conclusion concerning the existence of an η-mesic bound state. More dedicated experiments are necessary for further clarification.

## Key claim

If an η-mesic bound state of ³He exists, its binding energy should not exceed 5 MeV and its width should not exceed 10 MeV, based on limits derived from the complex η³He scattering length. However, the analysis of the TAPS collaboration’s photoproduction data does not lead to a solid conclusion about the existence of such a bound state, and the sign of the real part of the scattering length, which determines whether binding is possible, cannot be reliably determined from existing production data.

## Method

The authors varied the real and imaginary strength parameters of a complex optical potential proportional to a Gaussian ³He density distribution (root-mean-square radius 1.9 fm) and solved the Schrödinger equation to compute both the complex η³He scattering length and the complex binding energy for each potential. They then compared these calculated scattering lengths to the χ²+1 contours from their earlier systematic analysis of pd→η³He data, and also performed a Breit-Wigner fit to the TAPS collaboration’s γ³He→π⁰pX enhancement data near the η threshold.

## Key numbers

- If a bound state exists, the binding energy B ≤ 5 MeV and width Γ ≤ 10 MeV, derived from the solution to the Mayer et al. data alone.
- From the combined fit to Mayer et al. and Berger et al. data, the scattering length a_{η³He} = (−4.3 ± 0.3) + i(0.5 ± 0.5) fm would suggest B = 4.3 ± 1.2 MeV and Γ = 2.8 ± 2.8 MeV.
- The authors’ Breit-Wigner fit to the TAPS γ³He→π⁰pX enhancement data yields B = 0.43 ± 2.9 MeV and Γ = 27.9 ± 7.2 MeV, with χ²/dof = 0.89.
- The TAPS collaboration reported B = 4.4 ± 4.2 MeV and Γ = 25.6 ± 6.1 MeV from their combined analysis.
- For V_R = 2.1 fm giving 0.25 MeV binding, changing V_I from zero to 0.1 fm changes B to 0.21 MeV and Γ to 0.38 MeV, and changes the scattering length from −17.1 fm to −14.3 + i6.0 fm.
- The accumulation point where equal-value contours converge is at about −2.0 + i0.9 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors’ earlier systematic analysis of the η³He scattering length (Sibirtsev et al., 2004) by connecting those limits to possible binding energies and widths. It contradicts earlier optical model calculations by Haider and Liu (1986, 2002) that excluded η binding in the three-nucleon system, and it comments on the claimed observation of an η-mesic ³He state by the TAPS collaboration (Pfeiffer et al., 2004). The results provide constraints for planning dedicated experiments aimed at direct observation of η-nucleus bound states, but the authors conclude that more dedicated experiments are necessary for further clarification.
