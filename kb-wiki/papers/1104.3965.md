# Uncertainties in nuclear transition matrix elements for neutrinoless $ββ$ decay within the PHFB model

> The nuclear transition matrix elements $M^{(0ν)}$ for the neutrinoless double beta decay of $^{94,96}$Zr, $^{98,100}$Mo, $^{104}$Ru, $^{110}$Pd, $^{128,130}$Te and $^{150}$Nd isotopes in the case of $0^{+}\rightarrow 0^{+}$ transition are calculated using the PHFB wave functions, which are eigenvectors of four different parameterizations of a Hamiltonian with pairing plus multipolar effective two-body interaction. \QCOM{35}{In addition, the consideration of} Employing two (three) different parameterizations of Jastrow-type short range correlations, \QCOM{19}{provides us with} a set of eight (twelve) different nuclear transition matrix elements $M^{(0ν)}$ is built for each decay, whose averages in conjunction with their standard deviations provide an estimate of the model uncertainties.

## Key claim
The nuclear transition matrix elements for neutrinoless double beta decay are calculated within the PHFB model using four different parameterizations of the effective two-body interaction and three different Jastrow-type short-range correlation parametrizations, producing a set of eight to twelve matrix elements per decay whose average and standard deviation provide an estimate of model uncertainties, but the quoted uncertainties reflect only the spread within this specific model framework, not a full quantification of all theoretical errors.

## Method
The PHFB (projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov) model was employed to compute neutrinoless double beta decay matrix elements for the 0+ to 0+ transition in nine candidate nuclei. Four different parameterizations of a pairing-plus-multipolar Hamiltonian were used (PQQ1, PQQHH1, PQQ2, PQQHH2), combining quadrupole-quadrupole and hexadecapole-hexadecapole interactions with two prescriptions for the isoscalar coupling. Short-range correlations were included via three Jastrow-type parametrizations (Miller-Spencer, Argonne V18, CD-Bonn), and finite nucleon size was treated with a dipole form factor (cutoff 850 MeV). The average energy denominator followed Haxton's prescription of 1.12 times the square root of the mass number in MeV.

## Key numbers
- For 94Zr with PQQ1 interaction and point nucleons: M(0nu) = 5.4382 (bare) and 5.8729 (with average energy denominator halved)
- For 100Mo with PQQ1 interaction and finite size plus SRC1: M(0nu) = 6.5036
- For 130Te with PQQ1 interaction and point nucleons: M(0nu) = 5.7440 (bare) and 6.3018 (with halved denominator)
- For 150Nd with PQQHH1 interaction and finite size plus SRC3: M(0nu) = 2.8359
- Average NTME for 100Mo with gA=1.254, Case II: 7.2163 ± 0.4977
- Average NTME for 130Te with gA=1.254, Case II: 4.6633 ± 0.4269
- Extracted upper limit on effective neutrino mass for 130Te with gA=1.254: 0.30 (+0.03/-0.02) eV from experimental half-life limit of 3.0 × 10^24 years
- Predicted half-life for 100Mo with gA=1.254 and <m_nu> = 50 meV: 4.32 (+0.66/-0.54) × 10^25 years
- SRC parametrization parameters: a = 1.1, 1.59, 1.52 fm^-2; b = 0.68, 1.45, 1.88 fm^-2; c = 1.0, 0.92, 0.46 for Miller-Spencer, Argonne V18, and CD-Bonn respectively

## Context
This work extends previous PHFB calculations by Rath, Chandra, Chaturvedi, Raina, and Hirsch (2008, 2009) that studied deformation effects on neutrinoless double beta decay matrix elements, now incorporating multiple Hamiltonian parameterizations and three SRC models to quantify theoretical uncertainties. It complements uncertainty estimates by Rodin et al. (2003) using QRPA/RQRPA with multiple basis sets and interactions, and by Simkovic et al. (2008, 2009) using UCOM and coupled-cluster methods. The results enable comparison with shell-model calculations by Caurier et al. (2008) and interacting boson model by Barea and Iachello (2009), and provide uncertainty bands for extracting effective Majorana neutrino masses from experimental half-life limits.
