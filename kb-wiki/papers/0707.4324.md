# Additivity of effective quadrupole moments and angular momentum alignments in the A~130 nuclei

> The additivity principle of the extreme shell model stipulates that an average value of a one-body operator be equal to the sum of the core contribution and effective contributions of valence (particle or hole) nucleons. For quadrupole moment and angular momentum operators, we test this principle for highly and superdeformed rotational bands in the A~130 nuclei. Calculations are done in the self-consistent cranked non-relativistic Hartree-Fock and relativistic Hartree mean-field approaches. Results indicate that the additivity principle is a valid concept that justifies the use of an extreme single-particle model in an unpaired regime typical of high angular momenta.

## Key claim

The additivity principle for one-body operators (quadrupole moments and angular momentum alignments) is a valid concept for highly and superdeformed rotational bands in the A~130 mass region, but only at high rotational frequencies (specifically at hbar-omega = 0.65 MeV) where pairing is quenched and level crossings are absent in the core configuration.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent cranked non-relativistic Hartree-Fock (CHF) calculations using the SLy4 Skyrme interaction and cranked relativistic mean-field (CRMF) calculations using the NL1 parametrization, both at a rotational frequency of hbar-omega = 0.65 MeV. They constructed 183 independent-particle configurations (CHF) and 105 configurations (CRMF) around the highly deformed core of 131Ce, then used multivariate least-square fits to extract effective single-particle quadrupole moments and angular momentum alignments from the calculated observables.

## Key numbers

* Effective charge quadrupole moments q_eff_20 for neutron orbitals are entirely due to polarization (bare value = 0.0 eb), ranging from -0.35 +/- 0.01 eb (nu [402]5/2^+i, CHF+SLy4) to +0.24 +/- 0.03 eb (nu [532]5/2^-i, CHF+SLy4)
* Effective charge quadrupole moments q_eff_20 for proton orbitals range from -0.64 +/- 0.02 eb (pi [541]3/2^+i, CHF+SLy4) to +0.30 +/- 0.02 eb (pi [532]5/2^+i, CHF+SLy4)
* The analysis was performed at rotational frequency hbar-omega = 0.65 MeV, where the yrast SD band in 132Ce extends to approximately 68 hbar
* The core configuration in 131Ce has neutron occupation [18,19,18,18] and proton occupation [14,14,15,15] in parity-signature blocks

## Context

This work extends the additivity principle studies previously performed for the A~150 mass region (Ref. SDDN.96, using CHF with Skyrme forces around the 152Dy SD core) and for a few configurations near 152Dy in CRMF (Ref. ALR.98). It systematically tests additivity for both quadrupole moments and angular momentum alignments in the A~130 region, where bands exhibit greater gamma-softness than in the A~140-150 region. The results enable the use of an extreme single-particle model for interpreting experimental data from differential lifetime measurements on over 15 different nuclei (Ce, Pr, Nd, Pm, Sm isotopes) reported in Refs. A130-exp1 and A130-exp2.
