# Dilepton production in proton-proton and quasifree proton-neutron reactions at 1.25 GeV

> We investigate the pp --> pp e+e- and quasifree pn --> pn e+e- reactions within an effective Lagrangian model at the laboratory kinetic energy of 1.25 GeV for which experimental data have recently been reported by the HADES Collaboration. The model uses a meson-exchange approximation to describe the initial nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering. Contributions to the reaction amplitudes are included from the NN bremsstrahlung as well as from the excitation, propagation and radiative decay of the Delta(1230) isobar state. It is found that the HADES data on the e+e- invariant mass distribution in the pp --> pp e+e- reaction are reproduced excellently by our model where the Delta isobar term dominates the spectrum. However, the quasifree pn --> pn e+e- cross sections are underpredicted in the invariant mass region of 0.40 - 0.55 GeV/c^2 even after including contributions of the eta Dalitz decay and the subthreshold production and decay of the rho^0 meson via the baryonic resonance N*(1520). In the case of the quasifree pn --> pn e+e- reaction, a strong sensitivity to the pion electromagnetic form factor is observed which helps to bring the calculated cross sections closer to the data in the higher dilepton mass region.

## Key claim

The effective Lagrangian model reproduces the HADES data on the dilepton invariant mass distribution in pp → pp e+e- reactions excellently, but significantly underpredicts the quasifree pn → pn e+e- cross sections in the invariant mass region of 0.40–0.55 GeV/c² even after including eta Dalitz decay and subthreshold rho⁰ meson contributions. The quasifree pn reaction shows a strong sensitivity to the pion electromagnetic form factor, which helps bring calculated cross sections closer to data in the higher dilepton mass region.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian model with a meson-exchange approximation to describe initial nucleon-nucleon scattering, including contributions from NN bremsstrahlung and the excitation, propagation, and radiative decay of the Δ(1230) isobar state. For the quasifree pn reaction, Fermi smearing was applied using the Argonne V18 deuteron wave function to account for the neutron momentum distribution in the deuteron, and additional contributions from η Dalitz decay and subthreshold ρ⁰ meson production via the N*(1520) resonance were included. The pion electromagnetic form factor was incorporated at the charged internal meson line using two parameterizations (FF1 and FF2) while preserving gauge invariance.

## Key numbers

- Beam kinetic energy: 1.25 GeV for both pp and quasifree pn reactions
- Maximum allowed dilepton invariant mass for pp reaction: 0.545 GeV
- NN bremsstrahlung contributions are smaller than Δ isobar contributions by almost an order of magnitude for lower M values and by factors of 3–5 at higher M in the pp reaction
- The difference between theory and data for quasifree pn reaction varies from factors of 2–3 at lower mass values to more than an order of magnitude for M around 0.5 GeV/c²
- Final state interaction effects increase cross sections by no more than 15–20% even at extreme kinematical limits
- The η meson production threshold is 1.258 GeV, above the 1.25 GeV beam energy
- The ρ⁰ meson total width parameter Γ_ρ⁰→ππ = 0.150 GeV, with interaction radius r_C = 2 fm
- The FF2 form factor parameter λ² = 1.9 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the effective Lagrangian model of Shyam and Mosel (2003, 2009) and Schafer et al. (1994) to describe new HADES Collaboration data (2010) on dilepton production in both pp and quasifree pn reactions at 1.25 GeV. The results contradict the model of Kaptari and Kampfer (2006), which overestimates pp dilepton yields and fails to describe the pn data. The findings are important because the dilepton excess observed in C+C collisions at 1–2 GeV/nucleon can be explained by a superposition of elementary pp and pn reactions, and the sensitivity to the pion electromagnetic form factor in the pn channel provides a new constraint for future measurements and transport model calculations.
