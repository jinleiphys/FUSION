# In-medium relativistic kinetic theory and nucleon-meson systems

> Within the $σ-ω$ model of coupled nucleon-meson systems, a generalized relativistic Lenard--Balescu--equation is presented resulting from a relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA). This provides a systematic derivation of relativistic transport equations in the frame of nonequilibrium Green's function technique including medium effects as well as flucuation effects. It contains all possible processes due to one meson exchange and special attention is kept to the off--shell character of the particles. As a new feature of many particle effects, processes are possible which can be interpreted as particle creation and annihilation due to in-medium one meson exchange. In-medium cross sections are obtained from the generalized derivation of collision integrals, which possess complete crossing symmetries.

## Key claim

Within the σ-ω model of coupled nucleon-meson systems, the authors derive a generalized relativistic Lenard-Balescu equation from a relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA), claiming that this provides a systematic derivation of relativistic transport equations including medium and fluctuation effects. The key hedge is that the derivation relies on the condition of weakening of initial correlation (τ_corr ≪ τ_coll) and a gradient expansion that assumes the space-time interaction range is short compared to the system's inhomogeneity scale.

## Method

The authors start from the Yukawa Lagrangian for scalar and vector mesons coupled to fermions and use nonequilibrium Green's function techniques on the Keldysh contour, introducing an infinitesimal meson generating flux to derive self-energies and polarization functions via Schwinger's variational method. They apply a relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA) by neglecting higher vertex corrections, then perform a gradient expansion to obtain kinetic equations for the Wigner distribution function. The collision integrals are derived from the generalized Kadanoff-Baym equations, yielding a set of eight processes including elastic particle-particle scattering, particle-antiparticle scattering, and five in-medium processes interpreted as particle creation and annihilation via virtual meson exchange.

- The quasiparticle energy is given by E_{1/2} = Re Σ₀ʳ ± √[(p_I - Re Σ_Iʳ)² + (κ₀ + Re Σ_I)²] evaluated at ω = E_{1/2}, representing a nonlinear equation showing particle/antiparticle symmetry.
- The in-medium cross section for proton-proton scattering is given by dσ/dΩ = (1/32π²) × {2g_v⁴/[s(m_v² - t)²]} × [(Δ(√s + Δ) + κ̃² - u/2)² + (Δ(√s + Δ) - κ̃² + s/2)² - 2κ̃²(Δ(√s + Δ) - t/2)] + ... where Δ = Re Σ₀ʳ and κ̃ = κ + Re Σ_Iʳ.
- The condition for new in-medium reaction channels to open is √s = -2 Re Σ₀ʳ, meaning the center-of-mass energy must equal negative twice the real part of the vector self-energy.
- The meson quasiparticle energy is E_d² = p² + m² - Re Π, and the static Yukawa potential becomes V(r) = g² e^{-r/r₀}/r with r₀⁻² = m² - Re Π.

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic Lenard-Balescu equation (KKER86) to the relativistic domain within the Walecka σ-ω model (Walecka, 1986), providing a systematic many-particle treatment that includes off-shell effects and particle-antiparticle symmetry. It differs from earlier relativistic transport approaches by Botermans and Malfliet (1990), De Groot et al. (1980), and De Boer (1995) by including pair creation/destruction processes and dynamical screening via the RRPA, and by avoiding mixed coupling terms between scalar and vector mesons that arise from neglecting meson initial correlations. The derived kinetic equation is intended as a starting point for describing hot nuclear matter, replacing the relativistic Vlasov treatment (XYXS90) with a Lenard-Balescu-type equation that includes Pauli blocking, screening, and inelastic medium effects.
