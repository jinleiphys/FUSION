# $π^0$-Photoproduction on the deuteron via $Δ$-excitation using the Lorentz Integral Transform

> The Lorentz Integral Transform method (LIT) is extended to pion photoproduction in the $Δ$-resonance region. The main focus lies on the solution of the conceptual difficulties which arise if energy dependent operators for nucleon resonance excitations are considered. In order to demonstrate the applicability of our approach, we calculate the inclusive cross section for $π^0$-photoproduction off the deuteron within a simple pure resonance model.

## Key claim

The Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method is extended to handle energy-dependent operators for nucleon resonance excitations in pion photoproduction, by expanding the configuration space to include previously projected-out states; the method is demonstrated for inclusive π⁰-photoproduction on the deuteron in the Δ-resonance region using a simple pure resonance model, not yet a realistic description of data.

## Method

The authors construct a Fock space comprising π⁰NN, NN, and NΔ subspaces, with a Hamiltonian containing kinetic terms and interactions restricted to V_NN and V_Δπ (the ΔNπ⁰ vertex). The Lorentz state is solved via resolvent methods, and the inclusive cross section is obtained from the Lorentz transform L(σ) = −(1/σ_I) Im{⟨Ψ| O_{NΔ} 𝒢_Δ O_{ΔN} |Ψ⟩}, where 𝒢_Δ includes the Δ self-energy from a πN loop. Calculations are performed in impulse approximation (IA) with both a static Δ-mass (1232 MeV) and a σ-dependent Δ-mass, and also including the NΔ-interaction from one-pion exchange with total angular momentum J ≤ 5.

## Key numbers

- The M1-NΔ current coupling constant used is G_{M1}^{0 ΔN} = 4.22.
- The static Δ-mass used for comparison is M_Δ = 1232 MeV.
- Including the NΔ-interaction lowers the absolute cross section near the maximum by about 8 %.
- All channels with total angular momentum J ≤ 5 of the NΔ-system were included for convergence.

## Context

This work extends the LIT method, previously applied to inclusive pion photoproduction on the deuteron near threshold using only the Kroll-Ruderman term (Reiß, 2005), to higher photon energies in the Δ(1232)-resonance region. It addresses conceptual difficulties with energy-dependent resonance operators that arise in realistic production operators (Blomqvist, 1997; Drechsel, 1999). The results enable future extension to more realistic descriptions including nonresonant Born contributions and final state interactions, and to meson photoproduction on heavier light nuclei like ³He or ⁴He, where conventional Faddeev-Yakubovsky treatments (Glöckle, 1983) are very complicated.
