# Effect of medium recoil and $p_T$ broadening on single inclusive jet suppression in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> Jet energy loss and single inclusive jet suppression in high-energy heavy-ion collisions are studied within a pQCD parton model that includes both elastic and radiative interactions between jet shower and medium partons as they propagate through the quark-gluon plasma. The collisional energy loss of jets with a given cone-size is found to be relatively small comparing with the radiative energy loss. However the effect of transverse momentum broadening due to elastic scattering is significant in the calculation of radiative energy loss within the higher-twist formalism. The nuclear modification factors for single inclusive jets with different cone-sizes are calculated and compared to experimental data as measured by ALICE and ATLAS experiments in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76$ TeV. Results on jet suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02$ TeV are also presented.

# Effect of medium recoil and $p_T$ broadening on single inclusive jet suppression in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The collisional energy loss of jets with a given cone-size is relatively small compared to radiative energy loss, but the effect of transverse momentum broadening due to elastic scattering significantly increases the radiative jet energy loss within the high-twist formalism, and inclusion of recoil partons reduces the elastic jet energy loss by a factor of about 2. The calculated nuclear suppression factor $R_{AA}$ describes experimental data at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76$ TeV well, though the theoretical results are smaller than data at lower jet $p_T$.

## Method
The authors use a next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD parton model that includes both elastic and radiative interactions between jet shower partons and thermal medium partons in a quark-gluon plasma described by a hydrodynamic evolution. Elastic energy loss is calculated from two-parton scattering rates with a Debye-screened matrix element, accounting for whether recoil thermal partons fall inside or outside the jet cone. Radiative energy loss is computed within the high-twist formalism, modified to include a Gaussian model for transverse momentum broadening of radiated gluons and a cut on soft gluons below the Debye mass. The single inclusive jet cross section in heavy-ion collisions is obtained by shifting the initial jet $p_T$ by the total (elastic plus radiative) jet energy loss, using both leading-order and PYTHIA-determined quark/gluon jet fractions.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperature $T_0 = 468$ MeV at $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76$ TeV, $0-10\%$ centrality with impact parameter $b = 3.2$ fm
- Initial temperature $T_0 = 478.5$ MeV for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02$ TeV
- Debye mass $\mu_D^2 = (1 + n_f/6) g^2 T^2$; soft gluons with energy $zE < \mu_D \sim 1$ GeV are considered lost to the medium
- Radiative jet energy loss with $p_T$ broadening is approximately double that without broadening for a 200 GeV quark jet with cone-size $R=0.4$ in central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76$ TeV
- Jet suppression factor $R_{AA}$ calculated for cone-sizes $R=0.4$ and $R=0.2$ at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76$ TeV and $5.02$ TeV, compared to ATLAS data (cone-size $R=0.4$, $|y|<2.1$) and ALICE data (cone-size $R=0.2$, $|y|<0.5$)

## Context
This work extends the high-twist approach to parton energy loss (Guo and Wang, 2000; Wang and Guo, 2001; Wang, 2009) by incorporating medium recoil and transverse momentum broadening effects that are important for finite-cone-size jet observables. It builds on earlier phenomenological studies of jet suppression (Burke et al., 2013; Vitev et al., 2009; Wang et al., 2013; Qin et al., 2010; Chang et al., 2016) and Monte Carlo simulations of jet-medium interactions (He et al., 2015; Luo et al., 2014; Casalderrey-Solana et al., 2015). The results enable further constraints on the jet transport coefficient $\hat q$ and provide predictions for jet suppression at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02$ TeV that can be tested by future LHC measurements.
