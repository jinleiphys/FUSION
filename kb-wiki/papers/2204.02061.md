# Effect of nucleon effective mass and symmetry energy on the neutrino mean free path in a neutron star

> The Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU energy density functional (KIDS-EDF) models, constructed from the perturbative expansion of the energy density in nuclear matter, have been successfully and widely applied in describing the properties of finite nuclei and infinite nuclear matter. In the present work, we extend the applications of the KIDS-EDF models to investigate the implications of the nucleon effective mass and nuclear symmetry energy for the properties of neutron stars (NSs) and neutrino interaction with the NS constituent matter in the linear response approximation (LRA). At fixed neutrino energy and momentum transfer, we analyze the total differential cross section of neutrino, the neutrino mean free path (NMFP), and the NS mass-radius (M-R) relations. Remarkable results are given by the KIDS0-m*87 and SLy4 models, in which $M_n^* /M \lesssim 1$, and their NMFPs are quite higher in comparison with those obtained from the KIDS0, KIDS-A, and KIDS-B models, which result in $M_n^*/M \gtrsim 1$. For the KIDS0, KIDS-A, and KIDS-B models, we obtain $λ\lesssim R_{\textrm{NS}}$, indicating that these models could predict the slow NS cooling and neutrino trapping in NSs. In contrast, the KIDS0-m*87 and SLy4 models yield $λ\gtrsim R_{\textrm{NS}}$ and thus we expect faster NS cooling and a small possibility of neutrino trapping within NSs. We also calculate the NMFP as a function of the neutrino energy and the nuclear matter density and find that the NMFP decreases as the density and neutrino energy increase, which is consistent with the result obtained in the Brussels-Montreal Skyrme (BSk17 and BSk18) models at saturation density.

## Key claim

The neutrino mean free path (NMFP) in a neutron star depends sensitively on whether the neutron effective mass is less than or greater than the free neutron mass, and on the stiffness of the nuclear symmetry energy; models with \(M_n^*/M \lesssim 1\) (KIDS0-m*87 and SLy4) yield NMFP larger than the neutron star radius, implying faster cooling, while models with \(M_n^*/M \gtrsim 1\) (KIDS0, KIDS-A, KIDS-B) yield NMFP smaller than the radius, implying slower cooling and possible neutrino trapping.

## Method

The authors used five nuclear energy density functionals (KIDS0, KIDS0-m*87, KIDS-A, KIDS-B, and SLy4) to compute the equation of state of beta-equilibrated neutron star matter. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations, and calculated the neutrino differential cross section and mean free path in the linear response approximation for neutral-current scattering off neutrons and protons. The neutron and proton effective masses and particle fractions were determined from the Skyrme-force parameterization of each functional.

## Key numbers

*   The KIDS0, KIDS-A, and KIDS-B models give neutron effective mass \(M_n^*/M \gtrsim 1\) and yield \(\lambda \lesssim R_{\textrm{NS}}\), indicating slow NS cooling and neutrino trapping.
*   The KIDS0-m*87 and SLy4 models give \(M_n^*/M \lesssim 1\) and yield \(\lambda \gtrsim R_{\textrm{NS}}\), indicating faster NS cooling and a small possibility of neutrino trapping.
*   The saturation density is \(\rho_0 \simeq 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\); the symmetry energy at saturation \(J\) is 32.8 MeV for KIDS0 and KIDS0-m*87, 33 MeV for KIDS-A, and 32 MeV for KIDS-B.
*   The slope parameter \(L\) of the symmetry energy is 49.1 MeV for KIDS0 and KIDS0-m*87, 66 MeV for KIDS-A, 58 MeV for KIDS-B, and 45.9 MeV for SLy4.
*   The isoscalar effective mass ratio \(\mu^*_s\) is 0.991 for KIDS0, 0.800 for KIDS0-m*87, 1.004 for KIDS-A, and 0.997 for KIDS-B; the isovector ratio \(\mu^*_v\) is 0.819, 0.700, 0.827, and 0.825 respectively.
*   All KIDS-EDF models predict a maximum neutron star mass larger than 2.0 \(M_\mathrm{sun}\), consistent with observations of PSR J0348+0432, PSR J0740+6620, and PSR J1614-2230.
*   The NMFP decreases as the density and neutrino energy increase, consistent with results from the Brussels-Montreal Skyrme models BSk17 and BSk18 at saturation density.

## Context

This work extends the KIDS-EDF framework, previously applied to finite nuclei and nuclear matter (Choi 2021, Gil 2021), to neutrino interactions in neutron stars. It contrasts the cooling implications of models with neutron effective mass above and below the free mass, and compares the symmetry-energy dependence with the SLy4 model (Skyrme-Lyon) and with ab initio PNM EoS (APR). The results enable future studies linking neutrino transport simulations to neutron star mass-radius constraints from NICER (Miller 2021) and LIGO/Virgo tidal deformability measurements.
