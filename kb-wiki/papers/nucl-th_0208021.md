# The Quark-Mass Dependence of Two-Nucleon Systems

> We explore the quark-mass dependence of two-nucleon systems. Allowed regions for the scattering lengths in the 1S0 and 3S1 channels as functions of the light-quark masses are determined from the current uncertainty in strong-interaction parameters that appear at next-to-leading order in the effective field theory. Where experimental constraints are absent, as is the case for the quark-mass dependent four-nucleon operators, we use naive dimensional analysis. We find it likely that there is no bound state in the 1S0 channel in the chiral limit. However, given the present uncertainties in strong-interaction parameters it is unclear whether the deuteron is bound or unbound in the chiral limit.

## Key claim

The paper finds it likely that there is no bound state in the 1S0 channel in the chiral limit, but given the present uncertainties in strong-interaction parameters it is unclear whether the deuteron is bound or unbound in the chiral limit. The authors stress that the lack of experimental constraints on the quark-mass-dependent four-nucleon operator D2 and the uncertainties in the pion-nucleon low-energy constants conspire to prevent a definitive conclusion.

## Method

The authors use an effective field theory (BBSvK-counting) to compute the quark-mass dependence of two-nucleon scattering lengths and the deuteron binding energy at next-to-leading order. In the 1S0 channel, analytic expressions from the perturbative-pion KSW expansion are used; in the 3S1-3D1 coupled channels, the Schrodinger equation is solved numerically with a spatial square-well regulator, including one-pion exchange at leading order and two-pion exchange and contact interactions at next-to-leading order. Where experimental constraints are absent for the quark-mass-dependent four-nucleon operators, naive dimensional analysis is used to estimate their allowed range, with the parameter eta set to 1/5 and 1/15 to illustrate the uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- Physical 1S0 scattering length: a = -23.714 +/- 0.013 fm
- Physical 1S0 effective range: r = 2.73 +/- 0.03 fm
- Physical 3S1 scattering length: a ~ +5.425 fm
- The low-energy constant d18 is sampled over the range -1.54 GeV^{-2} < d18 < -0.51 GeV^{-2} (from 1-sigma limits of three determinations)
- The low-energy constant d16 is sampled over the range -2.61 GeV^{-2} < d16 < -0.17 GeV^{-2} (from 1-sigma limits of three determinations)
- The parameter l4 is fixed to 4.4 +/- 0.2
- The cutoff radius R = 0.45 fm is used for the square-well regulator
- The chiral limit of the pion-nucleon coupling gA is taken as gA ~ 1.25, fpi ~ 132 MeV (1S0 channel) or 135 MeV (3S1 channel), and MN ~ 939 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous complete next-to-leading order calculation (Beane and Savage, 2002) by incorporating the present uncertainties in strong-interaction parameters, and serves as a sequel to that paper. It also builds on the BBSvK-counting scheme introduced by Beane, Bedaque, Savage and van Kolck (2002). The results are compared with a contemporaneous analysis by Epelbaum, Gloeckle and Meissner (2002), who used Weinberg counting and obtained a chiral-limit deuteron binding energy of 9.6 +/- 1.9^{+1.8}_{-1.0} MeV; the present authors argue that the error analysis in that work is incomplete and that when a complete error analysis is performed, the claims are likely to be greatly diminished. The work is motivated by the need to bridge lattice QCD simulations at unphysical quark masses to nuclear physics, and by previous incomplete estimates of quark-mass dependences used to place bounds on physics beyond the standard model via time-variation of fundamental parameters (Bulgac, Miller and Strikman, 1997; and many others cited in the paper).
