# Warm strange hadronic matter in an effective model with a weak Y-Y interaction

> An effective model is used to study the equation of state of warm strange hadronic matter with nucleons, Lambda-hyperons, Xi-hyperons, sigmastar and phi. In the calculation, a newest weak Y-Y interaction deduced from the recent observation of a He double hypernucleus is adopted. Employing this effective model, the results with strong Y-Y interaction and weak Y-Y interaction are compared.

## Key claim

The stability of warm strange hadronic matter depends critically on whether the Y-Y (hyperon-hyperon) interaction is strong or weak: with the weak Y-Y interaction deduced from the recent observation of a double hypernucleus (ΔBΛΛ = 1.01 MeV), the system is less stable than ordinary nuclear matter except at very small strangeness fractions, whereas with the strong interaction it becomes more stable at large strangeness fractions.

## Method

The authors extended the Furnstahl-Serot-Tang (FST) relativistic mean-field model to include Λ and Ξ hyperons, plus σ* and φ mesons mediating hyperon-hyperon interactions. They solved the mean-field equations at finite temperature to obtain the thermodynamic potential, pressure, energy density, and Helmholtz free energy for strange hadronic matter under chemical equilibrium conditions (Λ+Λ ↔ n+Ξ⁰ and Λ+Λ ↔ p+Ξ⁻). Two parameter sets for the σ*-hyperon coupling constants were used: one corresponding to the strong Y-Y interaction (ΔBΛΛ ≈ 4–5 MeV) and one to the weak Y-Y interaction (ΔBΛΛ = 1.01 MeV), with the latter derived from the Takahashi et al. (2001) observation of ₆ΛΛHe.

## Key numbers

- ΔBΛΛ = 1.01 ± 0.20⁺⁰·¹⁸₋₀·₁₁ MeV: the Λ–Λ interaction energy deduced from the ₆ΛΛHe double hypernucleus observation (Takahashi et al., 2001), used to set the weak Y-Y interaction strength.
- For the weak Y-Y interaction: UΛ^(Ξ) ≈ UΞ^(Ξ) ≈ 10 MeV (hyperon potential depth in a bath of the other hyperon at reference densities), compared to ≈ 40 MeV for the strong Y-Y interaction.
- For the weak Y-Y interaction: gσ*Λ = 5.46 and gσ*Ξ = 11.39 (coupling constants of σ* meson to hyperons).
- For the strong Y-Y interaction: gσ*Λ = 48.31 and gσ*Ξ = 154.62.
- Saturation density of ordinary nuclear matter (fS = 0): 0.148 fm⁻³.
- For the strong Y-Y interaction at T = 6 MeV: the lowest minimum of free energy per baryon occurs at fS ≈ 1.3, with saturation density ≈ 0.56 fm⁻³.
- For the weak Y-Y interaction at T = 6 MeV: the minimum free energy per baryon occurs at fS ≈ 0.1; for fS > 1.25, no negative minimum exists (system unstable).
- For the weak Y-Y interaction: the inflection point in p–ρB isotherms appears around T = 12 MeV at fS = 0.5, and around T = 6 MeV at fS = 1.3.
- Bare baryon masses used: MN = 939 MeV, MΛ = 1116 MeV, MΞ = 1318.1 MeV; meson masses: mω = 783 MeV, mσ* = 975 MeV, mφ = 1020 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of strange hadronic matter at zero temperature (Zhang et al., 2003) and finite temperature (Qian et al., 2004) using the FST model, by incorporating the weak Y-Y interaction deduced from the Takahashi et al. (2001) observation of ₆ΛΛHe. It contrasts with earlier work by Schaffner et al. (1994) that used the Nijmegen model D with strong Y-Y interaction (ΔBΛΛ ≈ 4–5 MeV). The results are consistent with those from an independent modified quark-meson coupling (MQMC) model (Song et al., 2004), indicating the conclusion is not model-dependent. The findings suggest that if the weak Λ–Λ interaction is correct, previous studies of strange hadronic matter and its astrophysical implications (e.g., neutron star cores containing hyperons) should be reexamined, and further precise measurements of double hypernuclei are needed.
