# Synchronization effects on rest frame energy and momentum densities in the proton

> We obtain two-dimensional relativistic densities and currents of energy and momentum in a proton at rest. These densities are obtained at surfaces of fixed light front time, which physically corresponds to using an alternative synchronization convention. Mathematically, this is done using tilted light front coordinates, which consist of light front time and ordinary spatial coordinates. In this coordinate system, all sixteen components of the energy-momentum tensor obtain clear physical interpretations, and the nine Galilean components reproduce results from standard light front coordinates. We find angular modulations in several densities that are absent in the corresponding instant form results, which are explained as optical effects arising from using fixed light front time when motion is present within the target. Additionally, transversely-polarized spin-half targets exhibit an energy dipole moment -- which evaluates to $-1/4$ for all targets if the Belinfante EMT is used, but which is target dependent and vanishes for pointlike fermions if the asymmetric EMT is instead used.

## Key claim

The paper claims that using tilted light front coordinates (which correspond to an alternative synchronization convention where the speed of light is infinite in the \(-z\) direction) yields rest frame energy and momentum densities in the proton that are relativistically exact, but that these densities exhibit angular modulations absent in standard instant form results because they represent optical effects from using fixed light front time when internal motion exists.

## Method

The authors obtain two-dimensional relativistic densities and currents of energy and momentum in a proton at rest by evaluating expectation values of the energy-momentum tensor (EMT) on surfaces of fixed light front time using tilted light front coordinates, which consist of light front time \(\tau = t_{\text{IF}} + z_{\text{IF}}\) and ordinary Cartesian spatial coordinates \((x,y,z)\). They decompose the physical EMT expectation values into intrinsic rest-frame distributions and state-dependent smearing functions, then compute the intrinsic densities from the gravitational form factors \(A(\varDelta^2)\), \(B(\varDelta^2)\), \(D(\varDelta^2)\), and \(J(\varDelta^2)\) using Eq. (6) for spin-half targets. The results are presented for both the Belinfante (symmetric) EMT and the asymmetric EMT that includes an antisymmetric piece describing intrinsic fermion spin.

## Key numbers

- The energy dipole moment for transversely-polarized spin-half targets evaluates to \(-1/4\) for all targets when the Belinfante EMT is used.
- The energy dipole moment vanishes for pointlike fermions when the asymmetric EMT is used instead.
- The total integrated energy density for a spin-zero target equals \(m\), as expected for a system at rest.
- The rest-frame energy radius for a spin-zero target is given by \(\langle \bm{b}_\perp^2 \rangle_{\text{energy}} = 4 \frac{d A(\varDelta^2)}{d \varDelta^2}\big|_{\varDelta^2=0} - \frac{1}{m^2} D(0)\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior application of tilted light front coordinates to electromagnetic currents (Freese 2023) and provides a framework for obtaining relativistically exact EMT densities that are independent of the target's wave packet, unlike Breit frame densities which are leading-order approximations subject to relativistic corrections (Polyakov 2018). The results enable direct connection to debates about the proton mass decomposition (Ji 1994, Lorce 2017) by providing two-dimensional relativistic energy densities in the rest frame, and the formalism clarifies that all sixteen components of the EMT obtain clear physical interpretations in tilted coordinates, whereas components like \(T^{i-}(x)\) and \(T^{--}(x)\) lack such interpretations in standard light front coordinates.
