# Dynamical properties of constrained drops

> In this communication we analyze the behavior of excited drops contained in spherical volumes. We study different properties of the dynamical systems i.e. the maximum Lyapunov exponent MLE, the asymptotic distance in momentum space $d_{\infty}$ andthe normalized variance of the maximum fragment NVM. It is shown that the constrained systems behaves as undergoing a first order phase transition at low densities while as a second order one at high densities. The transition from liquid-like to vapor-like behavior is signaled both by the caloric curves, thermal response functions and the MLE. The relationship between $MLE,d_{\infty}$, and the CC is explored.

# Dynamical properties of constrained drops

## Key claim

Constrained Lennard-Jones drops of 147 particles confined in spherical volumes undergo a phase transition that changes from first-order-like at low densities to second-order-like at high densities, with the maximum Lyapunov exponent (MLE) detecting the transition even when the caloric curve (CC) becomes nearly featureless at the highest density.

## Method

The authors performed classical molecular dynamics simulations of 147 Lennard-Jones particles confined in spherical volumes of radii R = 4σ, 6σ, 8σ, and 15σ, using a cut-and-shifted Lennard-Jones potential with cutoff radius rc = 3σ and a short-range repulsive wall potential. They calculated caloric curves (temperature versus energy), thermal response functions (dE/dT), entropy via integration of 1/T, the maximum Lyapunov exponent using the Benettin rescaling method, the asymptotic distance in momentum space d∞, and the normalized variance of the maximum fragment (NVM). Fragment recognition was performed using both the Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) algorithm in configuration space and the Early Cluster Recognition Algorithm (ECRA) in phase space.

## Key numbers

- System: 147 Lennard-Jones particles; constraining volumes with radii R = 4σ, 6σ, 8σ, and 15σ
- The transition from first-order-like to second-order-like behavior occurs at a threshold density corresponding to R ∼ 8.7σ, which gives ρ ∼ 0.05σ⁻³
- For the dilute case (R = 15σ), the caloric curve displays a loop; for R = 8σ the thermal response function (TRF) is always positive and displays a single maximum; for R = 4σ the CC is essentially straight
- The entropy for R = 15σ shows a convex intruder between energies E₁ and E₂, with a linear function a + bE subtracted where a = 4.3 and b = 1.4
- The MLE displays a pronounced loop for R = 15σ in the energy range 0 ≲ E ≲ 1, a clear loop for R = 8σ and R = 6σ, and a valley for R = 4σ in the same energy region
- The NVM displays a clear maximum in all four cases at energies corresponding to the anomaly in the MLE
- d∞ scales as the square root of the kinetic energy (and thus as the square root of temperature) for both R = 8σ and R = 15σ

## Context

This work extends previous molecular dynamics studies of unconstrained Lennard-Jones drops by Strachan and Dorso (1999) and Chernoff et al. (2001), which found that unconstrained systems produce "rise-plateau" caloric curves with no vapor branch due to collective expansion acting as a heat sink. The authors show that confining the system in a fixed volume restores the vapor branch and allows equilibrium, consistent with statistical models such as those of Gulminelli (2000) and Gross (1997). The results enable using the MLE and d∞ as alternative dynamical probes of phase transitions in finite systems, particularly when temperature is not reliably measurable, and suggest that similar behavior should appear in nuclear systems due to the similar equation of state.
