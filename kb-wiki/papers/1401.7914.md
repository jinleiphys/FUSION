# Fitting N$^{3}$LO pseudopotentials through central plus tensor Landau parameters

> Landau parameters determined from phenomenological finite-range interactions are used to get an estimation of N$^3$LO pseudopotentials parameters. The parameter sets obtained in this way are shown to lead to consistent results concerning saturation properties. The uniqueness of this procedure is discussed, and an estimate of the error induced by the truncation at N$^3$LO is given.

## Key claim

Landau parameters derived from phenomenological finite-range interactions can be used to estimate N³LO pseudopotential parameters, but the solution for the zeroth-order parameters (t₀, x₀) is not unique because it requires the Landau parameters to fulfill specific relations that depend on the truncation order; the quantity δ₃ provides a rough estimate of the uncertainties in these parameters.

## Method

The authors derived the explicit Cartesian-basis form of the N³LO gauge-invariant pseudopotential (sixth order in relative momenta) and expressed its central and tensor coupling constants as linear combinations of Landau parameters up to multipole ℓ=3 for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter. They then calculated the Landau parameters from two phenomenological finite-range interactions (D1MT and M3Y-P2) at saturation density ρ=0.16 fm⁻³ and inverted the relations to obtain the N¹LO, N²LO, and N³LO pseudopotential parameters, including a standard Skyrme density-dependent term with γ=1/3 to partially resolve the overdetermination of the zeroth-order parameters.

## Key numbers

- For D1MT at N³LO: t₀ = -1670 MeV·fm³, x₀ = 0.45, t₃ = 9115 MeV·fm^(3+3γ), x₃ = 0.49
- For M3Y-P2 at N³LO: t₀ = -1748 MeV·fm³, x₀ = 0.387, t₃ = 7827 MeV·fm^(3+3γ), x₃ = 0.635
- For D1MT at N³LO: t₁ = 617 MeV·fm⁵, t₂ = 1031 MeV·fm⁵; for M3Y-P2: t₁ = 1011 MeV·fm⁵, t₂ = 689 MeV·fm⁵
- For D1MT at N³LO: t_e = 670 MeV·fm⁵, t_o = 223 MeV·fm⁵; for M3Y-P2: t_e = 76 MeV·fm⁵, t_o = 27 MeV·fm⁵
- Saturation density from D1MT at N³LO: ρ₀ = 0.163 fm⁻³; from M3Y-P2: ρ₀ = 0.162 fm⁻³
- Energy per particle at saturation from D1MT at N³LO: -15.2 MeV; from M3Y-P2: -16.0 MeV
- Incompressibility from D1MT at N³LO: 216 MeV; from M3Y-P2: 217 MeV
- The quantity δ₃ at ρ=0.16 fm⁻³ is -12 MeV·fm³ for D1MT and -60 MeV·fm³ for M3Y-P2

## Context

This work extends the method initiated by Carlsson et al. (2008) and Raimondi et al. (2011) for expanding the nuclear energy density functional in higher-order derivatives of densities up to N³LO, and follows the authors' own previous derivation of the N²LO pseudopotential in Cartesian basis (Davesne et al., 2013). The approach provides an alternative starting point to the density matrix expansion method used by Carlsson et al. (2010) for fixing N³LO parameters, and could serve as input for a complete minimization procedure as pursued by Kortelainen et al. (2010) and others. The method enables future fitting of N³LO pseudopotentials to finite nuclei properties, with work in progress toward that goal.
