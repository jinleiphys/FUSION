# Working Group Report: Heavy-Ion Physics and Quark-Gluon Plasma

> 20 pages, 6 eps figures, Heavy-ion physics and QGP activity report in "IX Workshop on High Energy Physics Phenomenology (WHEPP-09)" held in Institute of Physics, Bhubaneswar, India, during January 3-14, 2006. To be published in PRAMANA - Journal of Physics (Indian Academy of Science)

## Key claim

The working group report summarizes discussions and progress on several aspects of Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) believed to be created in heavy-ion collisions and in the early universe, but it does not present a single new experimental result; instead it reviews the status of lattice QCD calculations, experimental observations from RHIC and SPS, and theoretical models, noting that the equation of state from lattice QCD shows only a crossover transition at T_c ~ 200 MeV rather than a first-order phase transition.

## Method

The working group structured its activity around review talks followed by discussions, focusing on eight specific topics: charmonium suppression, jet quenching and jet identification, hydrodynamic description of elliptic flow, susceptibilities and speed of sound in QGP, possibility of bound states in QGP, phi-production at RHIC, AdS/CFT applied to QCD/QGP, and neutrino emission from crystalline color superconducting quark matter. Participants presented recent lattice QCD results, experimental data from SPS (NA50, NA60) and RHIC (STAR, PHENIX), and theoretical calculations using models such as the Polyakov-loop Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model and dissipative relativistic hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers

- Crossover temperature from lattice QCD: T_c ~ 200 MeV
- Charmonium states remain bound at least up to T ~ 2 T_C (from lattice QCD)
- Pseudoscalar and vector channels deviate significantly from free behavior at T ~ 3 T_C (from lattice QCD)
- J/psi absorption cross section from p-A data: sigma_absorption(J/psi) = 4.18 ± 0.35 mb (from NA50 at 400 and 450 GeV)
- Onset of anomalous J/psi suppression in In-In collisions at N_part ~ 90 (NA60 at 158 GeV/nucleon)
- Onset of anomalous J/psi suppression in Pb-Pb collisions at N_part ~ 125 (NA50 at 158 GeV/nucleon)
- For J/psi with p_T > 3.5 GeV/c, the central-to-peripheral ratio R_CP becomes almost independent of centrality (NA50 Pb-Pb)
- PNJL model: pressure reaches about 90% of ideal gas value at 2.5 T_c (with T_c = 227 MeV used in the model)
- PNJL model: quark number susceptibility (c_2) reaches about 85% of ideal gas value at high temperature
- PNJL model: speed of sound squared (v_s^2) near T_c has a minimum close to 0.08
- PNJL model: specific heat C_V shows a peak at T = T_c

## Context

This working group report from the IX Workshop on High Energy Physics Phenomenology (WHEPP-09, January 2006) extends the ongoing effort to characterize the QGP produced in heavy-ion collisions, building on lattice QCD calculations by Gavai (2006) and others, experimental results from STAR and PHENIX at RHIC, and SPS experiments NA50 and NA60. The report discusses comparisons of charmonium suppression data with models by Satz (percolation scenario), Rapp (regeneration), Capella (comover absorption), and Kostyuk (statistical coalescence). The work on susceptibilities using the PNJL model extends lattice studies by Alton (2005) and Gavai (2005), while the discussion of dissipative hydrodynamics addresses the breakdown of ideal hydrodynamics seen in centrality-selected data from STAR (2005). The report enables future work on jet identification using flow coefficients, dissipative relativistic hydrodynamics, and bound states in QGP.
