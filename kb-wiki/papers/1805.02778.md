# Multinucleon Short-Range Correlation Model for Nuclear Spectral Functions

> The main goal of the research presented in this dissertation was to develop a theoretical model for relativistic nuclear spectral functions at high missing momenta and removal energies based on the multi-nucleon short-range correlation (SRC) model. The nuclear spectral functions are necessary for the description of high energy nuclear processes currently being studied at different labs such as JLAB, LHC and FNAL. The model followed the effective Feynman diagrammatic approach in order to account for the relativistic effects important in the SRC domain. In addition to the two-nucleon (2N) SRC with center of mass motion contribution, the contribution of the three-nucleon SRCs to the spectral functions was also derived. The latter was modeled based on the assumption that the 3N SRCs are a product of two sequential short range nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions. The nuclear spectral functions models were derived from two theoretical frameworks for evaluating covariant Feynman diagrams: In the first, referred to as the virtual nucleon approximation, the Feynman diagrams were reduced to the time ordered non-covariant diagrams by evaluating the nucleon spectators in the SRC at their positive energy poles, neglecting explicitly the contribution from vacuum diagrams. In the second approach, referred to as the light-front approximation, the boost invariant nuclear spectral function was formulated in the light-front reference frame in which case the vacuum diagrams are kinematically suppressed and the bound nucleon is described by its light-front variables such as momentum fraction, transverse momentum and invariant mass. On the basis of the derived nuclear spectral functions, the corresponding computational models were developed from which the numerical estimates of the SRC spectral functions, the SRC momentum distributions, and the SRC density matrices were obtained.

## Key claim

The dissertation develops a theoretical model for relativistic nuclear spectral functions at high missing momenta and removal energies based on a multi-nucleon short-range correlation (SRC) approach, with the condition that the three-nucleon SRC contribution is modeled as a product of two sequential short-range nucleon-nucleon interactions rather than a simultaneous three-body process.

## Method

The author employed an effective Feynman diagrammatic approach to derive covariant amplitudes for two-nucleon (2N) and three-nucleon (3N) SRC contributions to nuclear spectral functions. The model was implemented within two theoretical frameworks: the virtual nucleon approximation, where spectator nucleons are evaluated at their positive energy poles neglecting vacuum diagrams, and the light-front approximation, where vacuum diagrams are kinematically suppressed and bound nucleons are described by light-front variables. Computational models were then developed from these theoretical frameworks to obtain numerical estimates of SRC spectral functions, momentum distributions, and density matrices.

## Key numbers

- The 2N SRC light-front nuclear spectral function includes a factor \(a_2(A,Z)/(2x_{p/n})^\gamma\) where \(a_2(A,Z)\) is the per-nucleon probability for finding a nucleon in a 2N SRC and \(x_{p/n}\) are the proton/neutron fractions defined in Eq. (2N-fraction)
- The relative momentum cutoff for 2N SRCs is set by requiring \(\mathrm k_1 \ge \mathrm k_{src}\) (Eq. 2NSRC_relmd)
- The center-of-mass momentum of the 2N SRC system satisfies \(k_{CM} \ll M_{NN}\) for the nonrelativistic approximation to hold
- The normalization condition for the 2N SRC density matrix \(\rho^N_{NN}\) yields \(\int \rho^N_{NN}(\beta,\mathbf k_\perp) d\beta/\beta \, d^2\mathbf k_\perp = \int \rho^N_{NN}(\beta,\mathbf k_\perp)\beta \, d\beta/\beta \, d^2\mathbf k_\perp = n^N_{2N}\), where \(n^N_{2N}\) is the contribution to the total norm of the spectral function
- The light-front density matrix for the deuteron \(\rho_d\) satisfies \(\int \rho_d(\beta_1, \mathbf k_{1,\perp}) d\beta_1/\beta_1 \, d^2\mathbf k_1 = 1\)

## Context

This work extends the SRC model of spectral functions previously developed by Ciofi degli Atti and Simula (CiofiSimula) by incorporating relativistic effects important in the SRC domain and deriving both 2N and 3N SRC contributions. The model enables calculations of nuclear spectral functions needed for describing high-energy nuclear processes at facilities such as JLAB, LHC, and FNAL, and provides a framework for studying the relationship between SRCs and the European Muon Collaboration (EMC) effect. The light-front formulation builds on the work of Frankfurt and Strikman (FS81, FSS15) and Weinberg (Weinberg) to suppress vacuum diagram contributions.
