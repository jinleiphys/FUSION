# Transport coefficients in high temperature gauge theories: (II) Beyond leading log

> Results are presented of a full leading-order evaluation of the shear viscosity, flavor diffusion constants, and electrical conductivity in high temperature QCD and QED. The presence of Coulomb logarithms associated with gauge interactions imply that the leading-order results for transport coefficients may themselves be expanded in an infinite series in powers of 1/log(1/g); the utility of this expansion is also examined. A next-to-leading-log approximation is found to approximate the full leading-order result quite well as long as the Debye mass is less than the temperature.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first full leading-order evaluation of shear viscosity, flavor diffusion constants, and electrical conductivity in high-temperature QCD and QED, going beyond the leading-log approximation. The authors find that a next-to-leading-log approximation reproduces the full leading-order result quite well as long as the Debye mass is less than the temperature, but caution that the expansion in powers of 1/log(1/g) is only asymptotic and likely not Borel summable.

## Method

The authors formulate an effective kinetic theory that correctly describes leading-order dynamics of hard excitations (quarks and gluons with momenta of order T) in a weakly coupled gauge plasma. The collision term includes both 2-to-2 particle scattering processes (with thermal self-energies included via hard thermal loop approximation) and effective 1-to-2 particle splitting and joining processes representing nearly-collinear bremsstrahlung and pair production/annihilation in the presence of soft thermal fluctuations (the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect). Transport coefficients are obtained by linearizing the Boltzmann equation about local equilibrium and solving the resulting linear integral equation using a variational approach with a finite basis of functions, then extrapolating to the infinite-basis limit.

## Key numbers

* For QCD with 3 light quark flavors, the leading-order shear viscosity is η ≈ 27.2 T³ / (g⁴ log(1/g)) times a correction factor; the next-to-leading-log approximation gives η ≈ 27.2 T³ / (g⁴ [log(1/g) + 1.20]) for Nc=3, Nf=3
* For the quark number diffusion constant Dq in QCD with 3 flavors, the leading-order result is Dq ≈ 6.0 / (g⁴ T) times a correction factor; the next-to-leading-log approximation gives Dq ≈ 6.0 / (g⁴ T [log(1/g) + 1.20])
* For electrical conductivity in QED (pure electromagnetic plasma with electrons and positrons), the leading-order result is σ ≈ 15.7 T / (e⁴ log(1/e)) times a correction factor; the next-to-leading-log approximation gives σ ≈ 15.7 T / (e⁴ [log(1/e) + 1.20])

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous leading-log calculations (Arnold, Moore, Yaffe 2000) by including all effects suppressed by powers of 1/log(1/g) while only neglecting relative corrections suppressed by powers of g. The leading-log result for shear viscosity in pure Yang-Mills theory was first evaluated by Heiselberg (1994) and closely approximated by earlier work of Baym et al. (1990). The paper enables reliable quantitative predictions for transport coefficients in weakly coupled gauge plasmas relevant to cosmological applications such as electroweak baryogenesis and the origin of primordial magnetic fields, as well as hydrodynamic models of heavy ion collisions. The authors also prove that the expansion in inverse powers of log(1/g) is only asymptotic and not convergent, and give a non-rigorous argument suggesting it is not Borel summable.
