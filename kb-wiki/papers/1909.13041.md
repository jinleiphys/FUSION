# Finite-range separable pairing interaction in Cartesian coordinates

> Within a simple SO(8) algebraic model, the coexistence between isoscalar and isovector pairing modes can be successfully described using a mean-field method plus restoration of broken symmetries. In order to port this methodology to real nuclei, we need to employ realistic density functionals in the pairing channel. In this article, we present an analytical derivation of matrix elements of a separable pairing interaction in Cartesian coordinates and we correct errors of derivations available in the literature. After implementing this interaction in the code hfodd, we study evolution of pairing gaps in the chain of deformed Erbium isotopes, and we compare the results with a standard density-dependent contact pairing interaction.

# Finite-range separable pairing interaction in Cartesian coordinates

## Key claim

The authors derive corrected analytical matrix elements for a finite-range separable pairing interaction in Cartesian coordinates, correcting errors in prior derivations by Nikšić et al. (2010), and demonstrate that this interaction can be implemented in the 3D code hfodd to study pairing gaps in deformed Erbium isotopes, though the charge-symmetric separable interaction cannot simultaneously reproduce experimental neutron and proton pairing gaps in ¹⁷⁰Er.

## Method

The authors analytically derive matrix elements of a separable pairing interaction with a Gaussian form factor (range a = 1.232 fm) in a Cartesian harmonic oscillator basis, using Moshinsky transformations to separate center-of-mass and relative coordinates. They implement the interaction in the code hfodd (v2.91a) and perform Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations for the chain of deformed Erbium isotopes using the SLy4 functional in the particle-hole channel, comparing results with a standard zero-range volume contact pairing interaction.

## Key numbers

- Range of the Gaussian form factor: a = 1.232 fm
- Adjusted separable pairing strength: W̃ = -B̃ = -300 MeV·fm³
- Volume contact pairing strength: V₀ = -195 MeV·fm³ (with equivalent-spectrum cut-off of 60 MeV)
- Convergence of pairing gaps achieved at N₀ = 14 harmonic oscillator shells
- Matrix elements reproduced results of Veselý et al. (2011) up to eV accuracy
- In some Erbium isotopes, the separable interaction yields a vanishing neutron gap, while the volume interaction gives Δ_N ≈ 0.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SO(8) algebraic model study (Romero et al., 2019) that demonstrated coexistence of isoscalar and isovector pairing using variation-after-projection techniques, aiming to port this methodology to realistic nuclear density-functional theory. The derivation corrects errors in the Cartesian-coordinate matrix elements of Nikšić et al. (2010) and provides an alternative to the spherical implementation of Veselý et al. (2011). The results enable future implementation of symmetry-restoration techniques with realistic pairing functionals in deformed nuclei, and the comparison with volume pairing (Kortelainen et al., 2010) shows systematic differences in isotopic trends.
