# The inelastic vs. total nucleon-nucleon cross section at large $N_c$

> In this paper, the implication of the extreme large $N_c$ limit for nucleon-nucleon cross section is examined. Starting from the $N_c$ scaling of the S-matrix elements, a relation between the total inelastic and total cross sections is derived for the regime in which the momenta are much larger than the QCD scale. A conceptual complication arise from the fact that there is a tower of baryons, such as the $Δ$, with mass splittings from the nucleon which go to zero at large $N_c$. Since these baryons are stable at large $N_c$, the meaning of elasticity must be modified. Processes which only transform nucleons to these baryons are considered elastic; processes in which at least one additional meson is produced are considered inelastic. It is shown that in the extreme large $N_c$ limit, the total inelastic cross section is exactly one eighth of the total cross section. In contrast, in the physical world of $N_c=3$ the total cross section for high-energy nucleon-nucleon scattering is dominated by inelastic processes.

# The inelastic vs. total nucleon-nucleon cross section at large $N_c$

## Key claim

In the extreme large $N_c$ limit (where $\log(N_c) \gg 1$) and under Witten kinematics (fixed velocity with momenta much larger than the QCD scale), the total inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section is exactly one eighth of the total cross section. This result holds only in the formal limit where $\log(N_c) \gg 1$ and should not be expected to hold even approximately at $N_c=3$, where the total cross section is dominated by inelastic processes.

## Method

The authors start from the $N_c$ scaling of the logarithm of the elastic S-matrix elements, which are proportional to $N_c$ for both real and imaginary parts of the phase shifts. They treat the scattering using a semi-classical quantum mechanical approach with a complex potential, where the imaginary part parameterizes flux loss into inelastic channels. The inelastic cross section is expressed via a partial wave expansion, and in the large $N_c$ limit the system is shown to behave as a black-disk scatterer with a crossover impact parameter $b_0 \approx \log(N_c)/(2 m_\pi)$, determined by the longest-range (two-pion exchange) part of the absorptive potential.

## Key numbers

- $\sigma_{\rm tot} = \frac{2 \pi}{m_{\pi}^2} \log^2(N_c)$ for the total nucleon-nucleon cross section in the extreme large $N_c$ limit under Witten kinematics.
- $\sigma_{\rm in} = \frac{\pi}{4 m_{\pi}^2} \log^2(N_c)$ for the inelastic cross section in the same limit, yielding $\sigma_{\rm in}/\sigma_{\rm tot} = 1/8$.
- At $N_c=3$, the predicted total cross section from Eq. (resulttot) is approximately $150$ mb, compared to the LHC measurement at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV of approximately $100$ mb.
- At LHC energies, the experimental inelastic-to-total ratio is approximately $3/4$, in complete disagreement with the $1/8$ prediction.
- Corrections to the ratio $1/8$ are expected to be of relative order $1/\log(N_c)$.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the total nucleon-nucleon cross section at large $N_c$ derived in Cohen (2012), which gave the $\sigma_{\rm tot} \propto \log^2(N_c)$ result, and the general analysis of S-matrix scaling in Cohen and Krejcirik (2018). The spin-flavor dependence of inclusive differential cross sections was previously studied by Banerjee et al., and the emergent spin-flavor symmetry at large $N_c$ was established by Gervais and Sakita (1984), Dashen and Manohar (1993), and Dashen, Jenkins, and Manohar (1994, 1995). The result enables analytic understanding of the decomposition of nucleon-nucleon scattering into elastic and inelastic channels in a controlled theoretical limit, though the extreme large $N_c$ regime ($\log(N_c) \gg 1$) is far from the physical $N_c=3$ world.
