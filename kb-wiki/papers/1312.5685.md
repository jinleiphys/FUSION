# Ab Initio Path to Heavy Nuclei

> We present the first ab initio calculations of nuclear ground states up into the domain of heavy nuclei, spanning the range from 16-O to 132-Sn based on two- plus three-nucleon interactions derived within chiral effective field theory. We employ the similarity renormalization group for preparing the Hamiltonian and use coupled-cluster theory to solve the many-body problem for nuclei with closed sub-shells. Through an analysis of theoretical uncertainties resulting from various truncations in this framework, we identify and eliminate the technical hurdles that previously inhibited the step beyond medium-mass nuclei, allowing for reliable validations of nuclear Hamiltonians in the heavy regime. Following this path we show that chiral Hamiltonians qualitatively reproduce the systematics of nuclear ground-state energies up to the neutron-rich Sn isotopes.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first *ab initio* calculations of nuclear ground states reaching into the heavy-nuclei regime (up to 132Sn), but the authors hedge that the chiral Hamiltonians only *qualitatively* reproduce the systematics of ground-state energies, with a constant shift of about 1 MeV per nucleon relative to experiment.

## Method

The authors start from chiral two-nucleon (NN) interactions at N3LO and a local three-nucleon (3N) interaction at N2LO, then soften the Hamiltonian via a similarity renormalization group (SRG) transformation. They solve the many-body problem using coupled-cluster theory with singles and doubles (CCSD) plus a non-iterative triples correction [CR-CC(2,3) or LambdaCCSD(T)], employing a harmonic-oscillator basis truncated in the principal quantum number emax and a normal-ordered two-body approximation for the 3N interaction.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state energies computed for nuclei from 16O to 132Sn, spanning an unprecedented mass range for *ab initio* methods.
* For 56Ni, the deviation between two different SRG model-space ramps (ramp C and ramp J) is about 0.4 MeV per nucleon; for Sn isotopes this deviation grows to around 7 MeV per nucleon.
* The residual truncation error from the SRG model space (ramp J versus auxiliary ramps K and L) is below 50 keV per nucleon even for the heaviest nuclei.
* Overall accuracy of the calculated energies for a given Hamiltonian at fixed SRG flow parameter is estimated at about 2%.
* For the NNN-full Hamiltonian with 3N cutoff 400 MeV/c, the experimental binding-energy pattern is reproduced up to a constant shift of the order of 1 MeV per nucleon.
* Calculated charge radii are systematically smaller than experiment: for 16O by 0.3 fm, for 40Ca by 0.5 fm, for 88Sr by 0.7 fm, and for 120Sn by 1.0 fm.

## Context

This work extends the reach of *ab initio* coupled-cluster calculations from the medium-mass regime (previously accessible via methods like the importance-truncated no-core shell model of Roth (2009) and Roth et al. (2014)) into heavy nuclei, overcoming technical hurdles in the SRG model space that had prevented such calculations. The results enable future studies of consistent chiral Hamiltonians at N3LO including initial and SRG-induced four-nucleon interactions, and provide a benchmark for validating chiral interactions in the heavy regime against experimental binding energies and charge radii.
