# Electroweak matrix elements at large Nc: matching quarks to mesons

> I review some progress made on the problem of calculating electroweak processes of mesons at low energy with the use of an approximation to large-Nc QCD which we call the Minimal Hadronic Approximation. An update of results for the matrix elements of the electroweak penguin operators Q7 and Q8 is also given.

## Key claim

The Minimal Hadronic Approximation (MHA) provides a systematic, well-defined interpolating function between the low-energy chiral expansion and the high-energy operator product expansion (OPE) of QCD Green's functions at large \(N_c\), enabling the matching of short-distance Wilson coefficients to long-distance meson matrix elements in a way that cancels convention dependence (such as \(\gamma_5\) prescriptions and renormalization scale \(\mu\)).

## Method

The MHA approximates the infinite sums over vector and axial-vector resonances that appear in large-\(N_c\) QCD by keeping only a finite number of resonances, whose masses and residues are fixed by matching to the first few terms of both the chiral expansion (low \(Q^2\)) and the OPE (high \(Q^2\)) of the relevant Green's function, such as \(\Pi_{LR}(Q^2)\). This yields a rational approximant that interpolates between the two regimes. The matching conditions produce generalized Weinberg sum rules and a relation (the "dictionary") connecting the four-quark condensate to meson parameters, which is then used to compute electroweak matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- Charged pion mass shift from the MHA: \(\sim 4.88\ \mathrm{MeV}\), compared to the experimental value \(4.5936(5)\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Input values: \(f_\pi^{\chi-\mathrm{limit}} = 87 \pm 3.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(M_V = 748 \pm 29\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (large-\(N_c\) values extracted from ALEPH data).
- Ratio from matching: \(M_V^2 / M_A^2 \simeq 0.50 \pm 0.06\), using \(L_{10}(\mu=M_\rho) = -(5.1 \pm 0.2) \times 10^{-3}\).
- Matrix element \(M_8\) (NDR) from Knecht et al.: \(2.34 \pm 0.73\ \mathrm{GeV}^3\).
- Matrix element \(M_7\) (NDR) from Knecht et al.: \(0.11 \pm 0.03\ \mathrm{GeV}^3\).
- Matrix element \(M_8\) (HV) from Knecht et al.: \(2.52 \pm 0.79\ \mathrm{GeV}^3\).
- Matrix element \(M_7\) (HV) from Knecht et al.: \(0.67 \pm 0.20\ \mathrm{GeV}^3\).

## Context

This work extends the program of matching short-distance QCD to chiral perturbation theory using large-\(N_c\) methods, building on the resonance dominance approach of Ecker et al. and the generalized Weinberg sum rules of Knecht and de Rafael. The MHA provides a practical dictionary that resolves the long-standing puzzle of \(\gamma_5\) convention dependence in analytic calculations of electroweak matrix elements, as demonstrated explicitly for the \(\pi^+\)-\(\pi^0\) mass difference and the penguin operators \(Q_7\) and \(Q_8\). The approach enables next-to-leading-order matching for kaon decays and has been successfully applied to processes such as \(\pi^0 \rightarrow e^+ e^-\) and \(\eta \rightarrow \mu^+ \mu^-\), as well as recent reanalyses of the muon anomalous magnetic moment \(g_\mu-2\).
