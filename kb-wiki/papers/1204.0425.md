# Momentum distributions, spin-dependent observables, and the D_2 parameter for 3He breakup

> Using a recent parametrization of the fully antisymmetric three-nucleon wave function, based on the Paris and CD-Bonn potentials, we analyze the momentum distributions of constituents in 3He, as well as the spin-dependent observables for (3He,d) and (3He,p) breakup reactions. Special attention is paid to the region of small relative momenta of the 3He constituents, where the single parameter, D_2, has determining role for the spin-dependent observables in the d+p channel. This fact results in some useful relations between experimental observables.

## Key claim

The spin-dependent observables for \(^3\)He breakup in the \(d+p\) channel are completely determined at small relative momenta (below \(\sim 150\) MeV/\(c\)) by a single parameter, \(D_2\), which governs the ratio \(w(q)/u(q) \approx q^2 D_2\) and thereby controls both the sign and momentum dependence of the observables in this region.

## Method

The authors used a recent parametrization of the fully antisymmetric three-nucleon wave function for \(^3\)He, based on the Paris and CD-Bonn potentials, restricted to five partial waves in the channel spin coupling scheme. Within the spectator model and a plane-wave Born approximation, they calculated one-nucleon and two-nucleon momentum distributions, as well as spin-dependent observables for \((^3\mathrm{He},d)\) and \((^3\mathrm{He},p)\) breakup reactions, including the tensor polarization \(\rho_{20}\) of the deuteron and polarization transfer coefficients \(\kappa_d\) and \(\kappa_p\). Comparisons with experimental data were performed using the "minimal relativization scheme" based on light-cone (infinite momentum frame) dynamics.

## Key numbers

- \(D_2\) parameter values: \(-0.2387\) fm\(^2\) (Paris potential), \(-0.2487\) fm\(^2\) (CD-Bonn potential), compared to experimental value \(-0.259 \pm 0.014\) fm\(^2\) from Sen (reference in text).
- Partial channel probabilities for Paris potential: \(^1s_0S = 0.5000\), \(^3s_1S = 0.4600\), \(^3s_1D = 0.0282\), \(^3d_1S = 0.0103\), \(^3d_1D = 0.0015\); for CD-Bonn: \(^1s_0S = 0.5000\), \(^3s_1S = 0.4658\), \(^3s_1D = 0.0231\), \(^3d_1S = 0.0102\), \(^3d_1D = 0.0009\).
- Effective number of deuterons in \(^3\)He: 1.39 (Paris), 1.36 (CD-Bonn), compared to 1.38 from variational calculations of Schiavilla (reference in text) with Argonne and Urbana potentials.
- \(D\)-wave probability in the \(d+p\) configuration: 1.53% (Paris), 1.43% (CD-Bonn).
- At small \(q\), \(\rho_{20} \approx -2q^2 D_2\), \(\kappa_d \to 2/3\) when \(q\to 0\), and \(\widetilde\kappa_p \to -1/3\) when \(q\to 0\).
- Good agreement between calculations and empirical momentum distributions for \(k \lesssim 0.25\) GeV/\(c\) from data at \(p_\mathrm{He} = 10.8\) GeV/\(c\) from Ableev (reference in text).

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical analyses of \(^3\)He structure by using a recent parametrization of the three-nucleon wave function from Baru (reference in text), based on the Paris and CD-Bonn potentials, to compute momentum distributions and spin observables. It provides relations between observables such as \(\rho_{20}\), \(\kappa_d\), and \(\widetilde\kappa_p\) that serve as consistency checks for experimental data, particularly in the low-momentum region where the spectator model works well. The results enable future comparisons with data from hadronic probes, including the spin-correlation parameter \(C_{yy}\) measured at RCNP (reference in text) and tensor polarization measurements from Sitnik (reference in text), and can be used to test three-nucleon force models and non-nucleonic degrees of freedom.
