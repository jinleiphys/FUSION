# Dissipative fluid dynamics for the dilute Fermi gas at unitarity: Anisotropic fluid dynamics

> We consider the time evolution of a dilute atomic Fermi gas after release from a trapping potential. A common difficulty with using fluid dynamics to study the expansion of the gas is that the theory is not applicable in the dilute corona, and that a naive treatment of the entire cloud using fluid dynamics leads to unphysical results. We propose to remedy this problem by including certain non-hydrodynamic degrees of freedom, in particular anisotropic components of the pressure tensor, in the theoretical description. We show that, using this method, it is possible to describe the crossover from fluid dynamics to ballistic expansion locally. We illustrate the use of anisotropic fluid dynamics by studying the expansion of the dilute Fermi gas at unitarity using different functional forms of the shear viscosity, including a shear viscosity which is solely a function of temperature, $η\sim (mT)^{3/2}$, as predicted by kinetic theory in the dilute limit.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that including non-hydrodynamic degrees of freedom, specifically anisotropic components of the pressure tensor, can remedy the failure of standard fluid dynamics in the dilute corona of an expanding Fermi gas, enabling a local description of the crossover from fluid dynamics to ballistic expansion.

## Method
The authors extend standard fluid dynamics by introducing an anisotropic distribution function with direction-dependent temperatures \(T_a\) and corresponding anisotropic pressure components \(P_a\). They derive conservation laws and additional relaxation equations for the anisotropic energy densities from moments of the Boltzmann equation with a BGK collision term, then solve these equations numerically using a Lagrangian-remap scheme (PPMLR) adapted from the VH1 code.

## Key numbers
- The shear viscosity in the dilute limit is \(\eta = \text{const} \cdot (mT)^{3/2}\) with \(\text{const} = 15/(32\sqrt{\pi})\) from kinetic theory.
- For a strongly deformed trap with \(\lambda = 0.045\), the mean velocity in fluid dynamics is larger than in free streaming by a factor \(\sqrt{3/2} \simeq 1.22\).
- The dimensionless relaxation time is \(\bar{\tau} = \bar{\eta}/\bar{P}\), where \(\bar{\eta} = \bar{\alpha}_n \bar{n} + \bar{\alpha}_T \bar{T}^{3/2}\).
- For \(\bar{\alpha}_n = 0.1\), anisotropic fluid dynamics and Navier-Stokes theory show good agreement in the aspect ratio evolution; for \(\bar{\alpha}_n = 1000\), the aspect ratio approaches the free streaming limit.

## Context
This work extends the scaling solutions of the Boltzmann equation studied by Menotti (2002) and Pedri (2003) and the global crossover analysis by Dusling (2011). It addresses the failure of Navier-Stokes theory in the dilute corona, where the shear viscosity \(\eta \sim (mT)^{3/2}\) leads to unphysical constant dissipative heating. The method is analogous to anisotropic fluid dynamics approaches used for heavy-ion collisions by Florkowski (2010) and Martinez (2010). It enables model-independent extraction of transport coefficients from trapped Fermi gas expansion experiments.
