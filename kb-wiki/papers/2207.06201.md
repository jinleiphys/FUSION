# Anomalous low-energy $E2$-related behavior in triaxial nuclei

> The anomalous low-energy $E2$-related behavior of a triaxially-deformed nucleus bas been identified and analyzed based on the SU(3) algebraic theory within the framework of the interacting boson model. The results show striking features that include a $B(E2;4_1^+\rightarrow2_1^+)/B(2_1^+\rightarrow0_1^+)<1.0$ transition rate ratio and a $E(4_1^+)/E(2_1^+)>2.0$ excitation energy ratio that can be tracked back to a finite-$N$ effect, which in a large-$N$ limit of the theory yields normal results for a stable $γ$-deformation. This description is shown to be able to explain observed $E2$ anomalous phenomenon in neutron-deficient nuclei such as $^{172}$Pt and $^{168}$Os, and in so doing yields a deeper understanding of the physical features of a soft triaxially-deformed nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the anomalous low-energy E2 behavior observed in neutron-deficient nuclei, specifically a B(E2;4₁⁺→2₁⁺)/B(2₁⁺→0₁⁺) ratio below 1.0 combined with an E(4₁⁺)/E(2₁⁺) ratio above 2.0, arises naturally from a finite-N (finite boson number) effect in the SU(3) realization of a triaxial rotor within the interacting boson model, rather than from any of the conventional collective modes.

## Method

The authors construct a triaxial rotor Hamiltonian within the SU(3) limit of the interacting boson model (IBM) using the consistent-Q Hamiltonian plus higher-order SU(3) Casimir operators, including a cubic term and a squared quadratic Casimir term. They map the IBM Hamiltonian to a triaxial rotor Hamiltonian via an exact algebraic mapping procedure, then compute excitation energies and B(E2) transition rates for yrast states as functions of boson number N. The model is applied to fit experimental data for ¹⁷²Pt and ¹⁶⁸Os using N=8 bosons, with parameters constrained by the mapping from the triaxial rotor.

## Key numbers

- B(E2;4₁⁺→2₁⁺)/B(2₁⁺→0₁⁺) < 1.0 is the anomalous ratio observed, with experimental values of 0.55(19) for ¹⁷²Pt and 0.34(18) for ¹⁶⁸Os
- E(4₁⁺)/E(2₁⁺) > 2.0 is the accompanying energy ratio, with values of 1.070/0.458 ≈ 2.34 for ¹⁷²Pt and 0.857/0.341 ≈ 2.51 for ¹⁶⁸Os
- For the N=9 example with (λ₀,μ₀)=(6,6) and γ=30°, the mapping parameters are t₁=3.0, t₂=0.553, t₃=-0.227
- The model fits for ¹⁷²Pt use ε=0.049 MeV, a₁=-1.21 MeV, a₂=0.27 MeV, a₃=0.27 MeV, t₁=0.135 MeV, t₂=0.063 MeV, t₃=0.0015 MeV
- The model fits for ¹⁶⁸Os use ε=0.022 MeV, a₁=-0.768 MeV, a₂=0.172 MeV, a₃=0.172 MeV, t₁=0.094 MeV, t₂=0.053 MeV, t₃=0.0026 MeV
- The ground-state γ-deformation for both nuclei is ⟨γ_S⟩_g ≈ 35°

## Context

This work extends the SU(3) algebraic approach to nuclear structure, building on the earlier triaxial rotor realizations in the shell model by Leschber, Castanos, and Draayer (1987, 1988) and in the IBM by Smirnov (2000) and Zhang (2014). It directly addresses the anomalous B(E2) ratios observed experimentally in neutron-deficient nuclei by Grahn (2016), Cederwall (2018), Goasduff (2019), and Zhang (2021), which could not be explained by conventional collective modes or large-scale shell model calculations. The mechanism proposed here enables future studies of triaxial deformation in other mass regions, including intermediate-mass and light nuclei such as ²⁰Mg and ²⁰O, where similar anomalous behavior may appear.
