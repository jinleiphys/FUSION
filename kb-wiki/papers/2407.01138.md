# Deuteron-proton backward elastic scattering at GeV energies

> Deuteron-proton elastic scattering is considered at the energies from 880 MeV to 2 GeV at the scattering angles $θ^*\ge 140^\circ$. The multiple-scattering method is used to calculate the reaction amplitude. Four reaction mechanisms are taken into account: one-nucleon-exchange, single-scattering, and double-scattering with a nucleon and a delta-isobar in the intermediate state. The method allows calculating both unpolarized differential cross section and any polarization observables. All the results obtained are compared to the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including a delta-isobar intermediate state in the multiple-scattering model is decisive for describing deuteron-proton backward elastic scattering data at GeV energies, but the agreement is qualitative rather than exact for polarization observables, particularly for the tensor analyzing power \(T_{20}\) between 0.7 and 1.3 GeV where the data show a rise while the model curve rises only slightly.

## Method

The author calculates the deuteron-proton elastic scattering amplitude using a multiple-scattering expansion in powers of the nucleon-nucleon \(t\)-matrix, avoiding partial-wave decomposition. Four reaction mechanisms are included: one-nucleon-exchange (ONE), single scattering (SS), double scattering (DS) with a nucleon intermediate state, and double scattering with a \(\Delta\)-isobar intermediate state. The NN \(t\)-matrix uses the Love-Franey parameterization with a new fit to the SP07 phase-shift analysis, the deuteron wave function is taken from the CD Bonn potential, and the \(NN\to N\Delta\) transition is described by \(\pi\)- and \(\rho\)-exchange potentials.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross sections are calculated at deuteron energies of 880, 1000, 1200, and 1300 MeV for scattering angles \(\theta^*\ge 140^\circ\).
- The \(\Delta\)-isobar width parameters used are \(\Gamma_0 = 0.120\) GeV, \(\gamma = 0.200\) GeV, and the \(\Delta\) mass \(m_\Delta = 1.232\) GeV.
- Coupling constants for the \(NN\to N\Delta\) potential: \(f_\pi = 1.008\), \(f_\pi^* = 2.156\), \(f_\rho = 7.8\), \(f_\rho^* = 1.85 f_\rho\).
- Form factor cutoffs: \(\Lambda_\pi = 0.8\) GeV, \(\Lambda_\rho = 1.8\) GeV.
- The ONE mechanism alone predicts \(T_{20}\) drops to a minimum of \(-\sqrt{2}\) at a deuteron energy of about 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous multiple-scattering model (Ladygina, JAPH and EPJA, 2009-2016) by including the \(\Delta\)-isobar term to address the failure of ONE, SS, and DS mechanisms to describe backward-angle data at GeV energies. It directly confronts experimental data from the Nuclotron at JINR Dubna (Pl, At, Dss, 2010s) and earlier measurements of \(T_{20}\) and \(\varkappa_0\) by Punjabi (1990s) and Saclay. The model enables future calculations of any polarization observable in \(dp\) elastic scattering at backward angles, and the results show that relativistic effects within the ONE framework alone (Kaptari, 1990s) are insufficient to explain the data.
