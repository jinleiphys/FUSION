# Second-order Relativistic Hydrodynamic Equations for Viscous Systems; how does the dissipation affect the internal energy?

> We derive the second-order dissipative relativistic hydrodynamic equations in a generic frame with a continuous parameter from the relativistic Boltzmann equation. We present explicitly the relaxation terms in the energy and particle frames. Our results show that the viscosities are frame-independent but the relaxation times are generically frame-dependent. We confirm that the dissipative part of the energy-momentum tensor in the particle frame satisfies $δT^μ_μ= 0$ obtained for the first-order equation before, in contrast to the Eckart choice $u_μδT^{μν} u_ν= 0$ adopted as a matching condition in the literature. We emphasize that the new constraint $δT^μ_μ= 0$ can be compatible with the phenomenological derivation of hydrodynamics based on the second law of thermodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper derives second-order relativistic hydrodynamic equations from the Boltzmann equation and shows that the usual Eckart matching condition \(u_\mu \delta T^{\mu\nu} u_\nu = 0\) in the particle frame must be replaced by \(\delta T^\mu_\mu = 0\), meaning the dissipative part of the energy-momentum tensor is traceless rather than having zero energy density in the local rest frame; this result holds for both first-order and second-order equations.

## Method

The authors apply the renormalization group (RG) method to the relativistic Boltzmann equation, performing a systematic reduction to obtain hydrodynamic equations as the long-wavelength, low-frequency limit. They introduce a macroscopic-frame vector \(\Ren{a}^\mu_p\) with a continuous parameter \(\theta\) that interpolates between the energy frame (\(\theta=0\)) and the particle frame (\(\theta=\pi/2\)), and construct the second-order solution by incorporating the next slow modes (bilinear forms of momenta) orthogonal to the five zero modes (1, \(p^\mu\)). The resulting relaxation equations for the bulk pressure \(\Pi\), heat flow \(J^\mu\), and shear stress \(\pi^{\mu\nu}\) are presented explicitly for both frames.

## Key numbers

- In the particle frame, the dissipative part of the energy-momentum tensor satisfies \(\delta T^\mu_\mu = 0\), giving \(f_e = 3\) and \(f_n = 0\), so that \(\delta e = 3\Pi\) and \(u_\mu \delta T^{\mu\nu} u_\nu = 3\Pi \neq 0\)
- In the energy frame, the microscopic theory gives \(f_e = 0\) and \(f_n = 0\), so \(\delta e = 0\) and the usual Landau-frame constraints are unchanged
- The thermodynamic force for bulk pressure in the particle frame is \(X_\Pi = -\zeta (3\gamma - 4)^{-2} (\nabla^a u_a - 3T D T^{-1})\), where \(\gamma \equiv 1 + (z^2 - \hat{h}^2 + 5\hat{h} - 1)^{-1}\) is the ratio of specific heats
- The shear viscosity \(\eta\) and bulk viscosity \(\zeta\) are frame-independent, while the relaxation times \(\tau_\Pi\), \(\tau_J\), and \(\tau_\pi\) are generically frame-dependent

## Context

This work extends the first-order hydrodynamic equations derived by Tsumura, Kunihiro, and Ohnishi (TKO, 2007) to second order, confirming that the Eckart constraint in the particle frame is incompatible with the underlying Boltzmann equation even at second order. The result contradicts the standard Israel-Stewart approach and earlier work by Betz et al. (2008) regarding the matching conditions, though it agrees with their observation that relaxation times are frame-dependent. The new constraint \(\delta T^\mu_\mu = 0\) was previously postulated by Marle (1969) and advocated by Stewart in the Grad moment method, but the present work derives it systematically from kinetic theory. The phenomenological derivation shows that finite corrections \(\delta e\) and \(\delta n\) to the internal energy and particle density are compatible with the second law of thermodynamics, contrary to the common assumption that only the pressure acquires dissipative corrections.
