# Two-body current and axial form factor effects in charged-current quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering within the NEUT event generator

> We present a charged-current quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering model based on an unfactorized representation of the spectral function, employing relativistic momentum distributions for bound nucleons and the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation with an energy-dependent relativistic potential to describe the scattered nucleon. The model incorporates two-body meson-exchange currents contributing to one-particle-one-hole final states and tests several axial form factor parametrizations, including recent LQCD and MINERvA fits. It is implemented in the NEUT event generator and benchmarked against T2K and MINERvA $ν_μ$-$^{12}$C CC0$π$ measurements. We find that two-body meson-exchange currents lead to a sizeable increase of the total cross section, arising from an enhancement of the transverse response, which is the dominant component in charged-current neutrino scattering. On the other hand, recent fits of the axial form factor predict larger values than the standard dipole form, yielding a systematic enhancement of the cross section. The LQCD+MINERvA parametrization tends to overestimate the data, while the MINERvA-only fit provides a more moderate increase. Overall, no single configuration consistently provides the best agreement with the different datasets.

## Key claim

The inclusion of two-body meson-exchange currents and alternative axial form factor parametrizations systematically increases the predicted charged-current quasielastic cross section on carbon-12, but no single combination of these ingredients consistently provides the best agreement with both T2K and MINERvA datasets, with the LQCD+MINERvA axial form factor tending to overestimate data.

## Method

The authors extended a previously developed relativistic unfactorized spectral function model, originally validated for electron scattering, to charged-current neutrino-nucleus interactions. The model uses relativistic mean-field momentum distributions for bound nucleons and the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation with an energy-dependent relativistic potential for the outgoing nucleon, and was implemented in the NEUT event generator. Two-body meson-exchange currents contributing to one-particle-one-hole final states were added, and the hadronic tensor was decomposed into vector-vector, vector-axial, and axial-axial components to allow event-by-event reweighting of the axial form factor. The model was benchmarked against T2K and MINERvA muon-neutrino on carbon-12 CC0pi measurements using several axial form factor parametrizations: the standard dipole with axial mass 1.05 GeV, a MINERvA-only fit, and a combined LQCD+MINERvA fit.

## Key numbers

* The standard dipole parametrization uses an axial mass of M_A = 1.05 GeV, extracted from neutrino-deuteron scattering and pion electroproduction data.
* The two-body meson-exchange current calculation uses an effective nucleon mass M_N* = 0.8 M_N and a vector potential E_v = 141 MeV for the intermediate bound-nucleon state.
* The LQCD+MINERvA axial form factor parametrization systematically overestimates the data, while the MINERvA-only fit provides a more moderate increase relative to the dipole.
* For MINERvA data, the dipole parametrization with meson-exchange currents is accepted based on p-value for proton momentum and proton scattering angle, while for muon momentum the dipole with only one-body currents has better chi-squared values.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic unfactorized spectral function model previously developed by Franco-Munoz et al. (2023, 2025) for electromagnetic interactions into the neutrino sector, building upon an initial NEUT implementation at the one-body current level by McKean et al. (2025). The study addresses limitations in lepton-nucleus cross section models commonly used in neutrino event generators, which have been revealed by comparisons with electron scattering data and accelerator-based neutrino measurements. The results enable future applications of the model for muon-antineutrino and electron-(anti)neutrino scattering on various nuclei, including oxygen-16 and argon-40, which would be valuable for experiments such as MicroBooNE, SBND, and DUNE.
