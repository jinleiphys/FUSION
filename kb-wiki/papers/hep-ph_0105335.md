# Fate of Vector Dominance in the Effective Field Theory

> We reveal the full phase structure of the effective field theory for QCD, based on the hidden local symmetry (HLS) through the one-loop renormalization group equation including quadratic divergences. We then show that vector dominance (VD) is not a sacred discipline of the effective field theory but rather an accidental phenomenon peculiar to three-flavored QCD. In particular, the chiral symmetry restoration in HLS model takes place in a wide phase boundary surface, on which the VD is realized nowhere. This suggests that VD may not be valid for chiral symmetry restoration in hot and/or dense QCD.

# Fate of Vector Dominance in the Effective Field Theory

## Key claim

Vector dominance (VD) is not a fundamental requirement of the effective field theory for QCD but rather an accidental phenomenon that happens to hold for three-flavored QCD at zero temperature and density; the chiral symmetry restoration surface in the hidden local symmetry (HLS) model is a wide phase boundary on which VD is realized nowhere, suggesting VD may be invalid for chiral restoration in hot and/or dense QCD.

## Method

The authors performed a one-loop renormalization group equation (RGE) analysis of the HLS model including quadratic divergences, deriving RGEs for the pion decay constant $F_\pi$, the parameter $a = F_\sigma^2/F_\pi^2$, and the HLS gauge coupling $g$. They identified fixed points and fixed lines of the RGEs, mapped the phase diagram in the $(X, a, G)$ parameter space (where $X = C \mu^2/F_\pi^2$ and $G = C g^2$), and determined the phase boundary where chiral symmetry is restored ($F_\pi^2(0)=0$). The RG flow for $N_f=3$ QCD was obtained by Wilsonian matching to the underlying QCD at the $\rho$ mass scale.

## Key numbers

- The fixed point for the $N_f=3$ QCD RG flow is $(X^\ast_4, a^\ast_4, G^\ast_4) \simeq (0.2, 9.3, 0.02)$, far from the VD value $a=2$.
- The parameters at the $\rho$ mass scale for $N_f=3$ QCD are $(X(m_\rho), a(m_\rho), G(m_\rho)) \simeq (0.46, 1.22, 0.38)$, which happen to be near the VD condition $X(m_\rho) = 1/2$.
- The VM fixed point on the phase boundary is $(X^\ast_2, a^\ast_2, G^\ast_2) = (1, 1, 0)$, where $a(0)=1$ and VD is badly violated.
- The fixed point $(X^\ast_3, a^\ast_3, G^\ast_3) = (3/5, 1/3, 0)$ governs RG flows for $a<1$ in the high-energy limit.

## Context

This work extends the HLS approach developed by Bando, Kugo, Uehara, Yamawaki (1985) and Bando, Kugo, Yamawaki (1988), and builds on the vector manifestation (VM) concept introduced by Harada and Yamawaki (2000) for large $N_f$ QCD. The paper contradicts the long-standing assumption, dating to Sakurai (1960), that vector dominance is a universal feature of hadron physics; it shows that VD is accidental for $N_f=3$ QCD and will be violated at chiral restoration. The results enable predictions for dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions and for vector meson properties near the critical temperature and density, where the VM with $a=1$ should replace VD.
