# Transport theory for a two-flavor color superconductor

> QCD with two light quark flavors at high baryonic density and low temperature is a color superconductor. The diquark condensate partially breaks the SU(3) gauge symmetry down to an SU(2) subgroup. We study thermal fluctuations of the superconductor for temperatures below the gap. These are described by a simple transport equation, linked to a quasiparticle behavior of the thermal excitations of the condensate. When solved in the collisionless limit and close to equilibrium, it gives rise to the ``hard superconducting loop'' (HSL) effective theory for the unbroken SU(2) gauge fields with momenta smaller than the gap. This theory describes Debye screening and Landau damping of the gauge fields in the presence of the diquark condensate. We also explain how our effective theory follows to one-loop order from quantum field theory. Our approach provides a convenient starting point for the computation of transport coefficients of the two-flavor color superconductor.

# Transport theory for a two-flavor color superconductor

## Key claim

The authors derive a transport equation for the gapped quasiparticle excitations of a two-flavor color superconductor, valid for temperatures below the gap Δ, and show that in the collisionless limit it yields a "hard superconducting loop" (HSL) effective theory for the unbroken SU(2) gauge fields with momenta k ≪ Δ. The effective theory describes Debye screening and Landau damping of the gauge fields in the presence of the diquark condensate, but only for temperatures within the range Λ'QCD ≪ T < Tc, where Tc ≈ 0.567 Δ0 and Δ0 is the gap at zero temperature.

## Method

The authors introduce an on-shell one-particle phase space density f(x, p, Q) for the thermal quasiparticles, which carry SU(2) color charges because the constituents of the diquark condensate do, and write a transport equation with a covariant drift term, a force term from color electric and magnetic fields, and a collision term. They solve the transport equation in the collisionless limit (C[f] = 0) and close to thermal equilibrium to leading order in the gauge coupling g, obtaining the induced color current and the polarization tensor for the unbroken SU(2) gauge fields. They then match this result to the one-loop gluon self-energy from quantum field theory as computed by Rischke (2000), showing that the particle-particle contributions with poles at k0 = ±(εp − εp−k) reproduce the transport-theory result.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the condensate is Tc ≈ 0.567 Δ0, where Δ0 is the gap at vanishing temperature.
- The Debye mass squared in the limit Δ/T ≫ 1 and T/μ ≪ 1 is m²D = M² √(2π Δ/T) exp(−Δ/T), where M² ≡ g² Nf μ²/(2π²) is the Debye mass of the ultradegenerate normal-phase plasma.
- The plasma frequency is ω²pl = (1/(3ε)) M² I₂(Δ/T, T/μ), where ε is the dielectric susceptibility and I₂ is a dimensionless function.
- The dielectric susceptibility to leading order is ε = 1 + g² μ²/(18π² Δ²) (from Rischke, Son and Stephanov (2000)).
- The confinement scale of the SU(2) theory is Λ'QCD ∼ Δ exp(−(2√2 π)/(11) μ/(g Δ)) (from Rischke, Son and Stephanov (2000)).

## Context

This work extends the infrared effective theory for the two-flavor color superconductor at zero temperature introduced by Rischke, Son and Stephanov (2000) to finite temperatures below the gap. The transport equation is analogous to the hard thermal loop (HTL) kinetic theory for the quark-gluon plasma at high temperature developed by Heinz and others, and to the hard dense loop (HDL) theory at high density and zero temperature by Manuel (1996). The approach provides a starting point for computing transport coefficients such as color conductivity, and the authors note that transport equations for gapless quarks and the η meson, which dominate thermal and electrical conductivities and shear viscosity, will be discussed elsewhere.
