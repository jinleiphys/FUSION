# Nucleon relativistic weak-neutral axial-vector four-current distributions

> Relativistic full weak-neutral axial-vector four-current distributions inside a general spin-$\frac{1}{2}$ hadron are systematically studied for the first time, where the second-class current contribution associated with the induced pseudotensor form factor (FF) is included. We clearly demonstrate that the 3D axial charge distribution, being parity-odd in the Breit frame, is in fact related to the induced pseudotensor FF $G_T^Z(Q^2)$ rather than the axial FF $G_A^Z(Q^2)$. We study the frame-dependence of full axial-vector four-current distributions for a moving hadron, and compare them with their light-front counterparts. We revisit the role played by the Melosh rotation, and understand more easily and intuitively the origins of distortions appearing in light-front distributions (relative to the Breit frame ones) using the conjecture that we propose in this work. In particular, we show that the second-class current contribution, although explicitly included, does not contribute in fact to the mean-square axial and spin radii. We finally illustrate our results in the case of a proton using the weak-neutral axial-vector FFs extracted from experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the 3D axial charge distribution in the Breit frame is related to the induced pseudotensor form factor \(G_T^Z(Q^2)\) rather than the axial form factor \(G_A^Z(Q^2)\), and that the second-class current contribution, although explicitly included, does not contribute to the mean-square axial and spin radii.

## Method

The author uses the quantum phase-space formalism to define relativistic weak-neutral axial-vector four-current distributions inside a general spin-1/2 hadron, evaluating matrix elements of the axial-vector current operator in the Breit frame and elastic frame. The full vertex function including the axial, induced pseudoscalar, and induced pseudotensor form factors is inserted into the Fourier transform definitions, and the resulting distributions are analyzed for their frame dependence and compared with light-front counterparts via the infinite-momentum frame limit.

## Key numbers

- The 3D mean-square spin radius for the proton is \(\langle r_\text{spin}^2 \rangle \approx (2.1054~\text{fm})^2\).
- The quantity \(R_A^2\) defined via the slope of \(G_A^Z(Q^2)\) is \(\approx (0.6510~\text{fm})^2\) for the proton.
- The relativistic contribution \(\frac{1}{4M^2}\left(1 + \frac{2G_P^Z(0)}{G_A^Z(0)}\right)\) plays a dominant role in the mean-square spin radius.

## Context

This work extends the author's recent study (Chen 2024) on weak-neutral axial-vector distributions by including the second-class current contribution associated with the induced pseudotensor form factor, which is often neglected under G-parity invariance of QCD. It clarifies that the 3D axial charge distribution is parity-odd and related to \(G_T^Z(Q^2)\), contradicting the common practice of identifying the axial radius via the slope of \(G_A^Z(Q^2)\) as done in works by Meissner (1986), Bernard (1992), and the A1 Collaboration (1999). The results enable a proper definition of mean-square radii and provide a framework for understanding distortions in light-front distributions via the proposed conjecture on infinite-momentum frame limits.
