# Nuclear density probed by Kaon-Nucleus systems and Kaon-Nucleus interaction

> Effective nuclear densities probed by kaon- and anti-kaon-nucleus systems are studied theoretically both for bound and low energy scattering states. As for the anti-kaon bound states, we investigate kaonic atoms. We find that the effective density depends on the atomic states significantly and we have the possibility to obtain the anti-kaon properties at various nuclear densities by observing the several kaonic atom states. We also find the energy dependence of the probed density by kaon and anti-kaon scattering states. We find that the study of the effective nuclear density will help to find the proper systems to investigate the meson properties at various nuclear densities.

## Key claim

The effective nuclear density probed by kaonic atoms depends significantly on the atomic state quantum numbers, offering the possibility to extract anti-kaon properties at various nuclear densities by observing multiple kaonic atom states, rather than only at the approximately fixed density of 0.6ρ₀ typical for pionic atoms.

## Method

The authors calculated the effective nuclear density for anti-kaon bound states (kaonic atoms) and for kaon and anti-kaon scattering states on ¹²C by defining overlapping probability functions of the meson wavefunction density with the nucleon density distribution. For bound states, the effective density was taken as the nucleon density at the peak of the overlapping probability S_bound(r), which is most sensitive to the binding energy shift. For scattering states, a new overlapping probability S_scat(r) was defined to be most sensitive to the differential cross section at zero degrees, and the meson wavefunctions were obtained by solving the Klein-Gordon equation with two types of anti-kaon optical potentials (chiral unitary and phenomenological) and one kaon potential.

## Key numbers

- The effective nuclear density probed by pionic atoms is known to be around ~0.6ρ₀ for almost every pionic state, as cited from Seki (1983) and Ikeno (2011).
- For anti-kaon scattering on ¹²C, the effective density decreases for higher anti-kaon kinetic energy (shown as a function of kinetic energy in MeV).
- For kaon scattering on ¹²C, the effective density increases for higher kaon kinetic energy, opposite to the anti-kaon case.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of effective nuclear densities previously performed for pionic atoms by Seki (1983) and Ikeno (2011), where the probed density was found to be nearly constant at ~0.6ρ₀, limiting the study of chiral symmetry restoration to that single density. The authors build on their own prior work on kaonic atoms (Yamagata-Sekihara, 2006) and introduce a new formalism for scattering states. This enables the design of future experiments to probe meson properties at multiple nuclear densities, moving beyond the linear density approximation discussed by Jido (2011).
