# Toward QCD on Quantum Computer: Orbifold Lattice Approach

> We propose an orbifold lattice formulation of QCD suitable for quantum simulations. We show explicitly how to encode gauge degrees of freedom into qubits using noncompact variables, and how to write down a simple truncated Hamiltonian in the coordinate basis. We show that SU(3) gauge group variables and quarks in the fundamental representation can be implemented straightforwardly on qubits, for arbitrary truncation of the gauge manifold.

## Key claim

The authors propose an orbifold lattice formulation of QCD for quantum simulation that uses noncompact gauge variables with a flat measure, claiming that SU(3) gauge group variables and fundamental quarks can be implemented straightforwardly on qubits for arbitrary truncation of the gauge manifold, provided that large scalar mass terms are added to stabilize the lattice and decouple unwanted scalar degrees of freedom.

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian lattice gauge theory using complex N×N matrices Z_{j,n} on spatial links, related to unitary link variables U_{j,n} and Hermitian scalars s_{j,n} via Z_{j,n} = (1/g_4d) sqrt(a/2) exp(ag_4d s_{j,n}) U_{j,n}. They truncate the infinite-dimensional Hilbert space by discretizing the coordinate basis for each bosonic degree of freedom into Λ=2^K points within a finite range [-R,R], representing the resulting states on K qubits using binary encoding. The momentum operator is approximated using the shift operator S with periodic boundary conditions, and the Hamiltonian's interaction terms become products of at most four Pauli σ_z operators.

## Key numbers

* The lattice spacing is denoted a, with the bare Yang-Mills coupling constant g_4d appearing in the relation Z_{j,n} = (1/g_4d) sqrt(a/2) W_{j,n} U_{j,n}.
* The coordinate truncation uses Λ = 2^K points per bosonic degree of freedom, with spacing δ_x = 2R/(Λ-1) and range [-R,R].
* For SU(3), the determinant constraint |Im det Z_j|^2 contains 300 terms when expressed in terms of real and imaginary parts of the matrix elements.
* The scalar mass term added to stabilize the lattice requires m ≳ 1/a to compensate for the unbounded-from-below plaquette Hamiltonian.

## Context

This work extends the orbifold lattice approach previously applied to pure Yang-Mills theory by Buser (2020) to include fundamental fermions and a truncation scheme suitable for qubit-based quantum computers. It offers an alternative to the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian formulation, which has been the standard approach for Hamiltonian lattice gauge theory since Kogut (1974). The authors note that unlike earlier orbifold constructions by Kaplan (2002) and Cohen (2003) that aimed at exact supersymmetry on the lattice, the scalar mass terms that break supersymmetry are harmless for QCD applications. This work enables future development of full quantum simulation algorithms for SU(3) lattice gauge theory using the orbifold lattice formulation.
