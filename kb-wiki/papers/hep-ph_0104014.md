# Nonperturbative vacuum and condensates in QCD below thermal phase transition

> Thermodynamic properties of the QCD nonperturbative vacuum with two light quarks are studied. It is shown that at low temperatures T<M_πrelativistic massive pions can be treated within the dilute gas approximation. Analytic temperature dependence of the quark condensate is found in perfect agreement with the numerical calculations obtained at the three-loop level of the chiral perturbation theory with non-zero quark mass. The gluon condensate slightly varies with the increase of the temperature. It is shown that the temperature derivatives of the anomalous and normal (quark massive term) contributions to the trace of the energy-momentum tensor in QCD are equal to each other in the low temperature region.

## Key claim
The paper shows that at temperatures below the pion mass (T < M_π ≈ 140 MeV), the QCD vacuum can be described by a dilute gas of relativistic massive pions, and that this simple gas approximation yields analytic results for the quark condensate that agree with three-loop chiral perturbation theory calculations to within 2.5% at T = M_π, while the gluon condensate shifts only slightly (∼0.1% at T ∼ 200 MeV).

## Method
The author uses the renormalization-group properties of the QCD partition function to derive relations between the pressure of a massive pion gas and the temperature-dependent quark and gluon condensates. The pressure of the relativistic massive pion gas is computed using the ideal gas formula with Bose-Einstein statistics, and the condensates are obtained by applying differential operators (derivatives with respect to quark mass and the operator D̂) to this pressure. The dilute gas approximation is validated by comparing the mean interparticle distance to the ππ mean free path from chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers
- The quark condensate at T = M_π (140 MeV) deviates from the three-loop ChPT numerical result by only 2.5%.
- The gluon condensate shift is Δ⟨G²⟩/⟨G²⟩ ∼ 0.1% at T ∼ 200 MeV, using ⟨G²⟩ = 0.5 GeV⁴.
- The pion decay constant is F_π ≈ 93 MeV.
- The critical temperature for deconfinement with two flavors is T_c(N_f=2) ≈ 173 MeV (from lattice calculations).
- The quark condensate deviation from the chiral limit is ∼25% at T = 140 MeV.
- In the low-temperature limit T ≪ M_π, the quark condensate shift behaves as ΔΣ/Σ ∝ −(3 M_π^{1/2} T^{3/2} / (2^{5/2} π^{3/2} F_π²)) e^{-M_π/T}.
- In the chiral limit M_π ≪ T < T_c, the quark condensate shift is ΔΣ/Σ = −T²/(8F_π²).

## Context
This work extends the study of QCD vacuum properties at finite temperature beyond the chiral limit, where previous work by Gasser and Leutwyler (1987) and others had computed the quark condensate in chiral perturbation theory at three-loop order. The paper provides an analytic alternative to the numerical ChPT results of Leutwyler (1993) for the quark condensate with non-zero quark mass, and confirms the earlier finding by Leutwyler (1993) that the gluon condensate shift arises only at three-loop level in the chiral limit. The derived thermodynamic relation equating the temperature derivatives of the gluonic and quark contributions to the trace anomaly (Eq. 41) is a new QCD theorem valid at low temperatures. This work enables simpler calculations of thermodynamic quantities in the hadronic phase below T_c and clarifies the role of the pion mass being numerically close to the transition temperature.
