# Universality of Three Identical Bosons with Large, Negative Effective Range

> "Resummed-Range Effective Field Theory'' is a consistent nonrelativistic effective field theory of contact interactions with large scattering length $a$ and an effective range $r_0$ large in magnitude but negative. Its leading order is non-perturbative. Its observables are universal, i.e.~they depend only on the dimensionless ratio $ξ:=2r_0/a$, with the overall distance scale set by $|r_0|$. In the two-body sector, the position of the two shallow $S$-wave poles in the complex plane is determined by $ξ$. We investigate three identical bosons at leading order for a two-body system with one bound and one virtual state ($ξ\le0$), or with two virtual states ($0\leξ<1$). Such conditions might, for example, be found in systems of heavy mesons. We find that no three-body interaction is needed to renormalise (and stabilise) Resummed-Range EFT at LO. A well-defined ground state exists for $0.366\ldots\geξ\ge-8.72\ldots$. Three-body excitations appear for even smaller ranges of $ξ$ around the ``quasi-unitarity point'' $ξ=0$ ($|r_0|\ll|a|\to\infty$) and obey discrete scaling relations. We explore in detail the ground state and the lowest three excitations and parametrise their trajectories as function of $ξ$ and of the binding momentum $κ_2^-$ of the shallowest \twoB state from where three-body and two-body binding energies are identical to zero three-body binding. As $|r_0|\ll|a|$ becomes perturbative, this version turns into the ``Short-Range EFT'' which needs a stabilising three-body interaction and exhibits Efimov's Discrete Scale Invariance. By interpreting that EFT as a low-energy version of Resummed-Range EFT, we match spectra to determine Efimov's scale-breaking parameter $Λ_*$ in a renormalisation scheme with a ``hard'' cutoff. Finally, we compare phase shifts for scattering a boson on the two-boson bound state with that of the equivalent Efimov system.

## Key claim

The authors claim that for three identical bosons with large, negative effective range, no three-body interaction is needed to renormalise the leading order of Resummed-Range Effective Field Theory, unlike in Short-Range EFT where a stabilising three-body interaction is required. This universality holds only for a limited range of the dimensionless ratio xi = 2r0/a, specifically between approximately -8.72 and 0.366, with three-body excitations appearing only in even narrower ranges around the quasi-unitarity point xi = 0.

## Method

The authors solve the homogeneous Faddeev equation at leading order in Resummed-Range EFT for three identical bosons, using the two-body scattering amplitude that includes both a non-derivative and a two-derivative contact interaction non-perturbatively. They compute bound-state trajectories as functions of the dimensionless ratio xi = 2r0/a for the cases where the two-body system has either one bound and one virtual state (xi less than or equal to 0) or two virtual states (xi between 0 and 1). The three-body spectrum is obtained by varying a hard momentum cutoff to check renormalisation-group convergence, and the results are compared to the Efimov spectrum of Short-Range EFT by matching the two EFTs in the limit where |r0| is much smaller than |a|.

## Key numbers

* A well-defined three-body ground state exists for 0.366... greater than or equal to xi greater than or equal to -8.72...
* The quasi-unitarity point is defined by xi = 0, corresponding to |r0| much less than |a| approaching infinity.
* The critical value xi = 1 marks the point where two two-body virtual states coalesce into a double pole.
* The overall distance scale of the three-body spectrum is set by |r0|, the magnitude of the effective range.

## Context

This work extends the Resummed-Range EFT formalism developed by Habashi, van Kolck and collaborators (2020, 2022) from the two-body sector to the three-body sector. It contrasts with the well-known Efimov effect in Short-Range EFT (Bedaque, Hammer, van Kolck, 1998-1999), where a three-body interaction is required at leading order and produces a geometric tower of states with discrete scale invariance. The results may apply to systems of heavy mesons such as the Ds*(2317) studied by Matuschek and collaborators (2020), and to certain Xi-N and Xi-Xi two-fermion systems investigated by Haidenbauer and collaborators (2015, 2021). The paper enables future studies of three-body scattering and higher-order corrections from the shape parameter.
