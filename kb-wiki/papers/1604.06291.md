# On γ-rigid regime of the Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian in the presence of a minimal length

> A prolate γ-rigid regime of the Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian within the minimal length formalism, involving an infinite square well like potential in β collective shape variable, is developed and used to describe the spectra of a variety of vibrational-like nuclei. The effect of the minimal length on the energy spectrum and the wave function is duly investigated. Numerical calculations are performed for some nuclei revealing a qualitative agreement with the available experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper derives approximate solutions of the Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian in a prolate gamma-rigid regime (gamma = 0) by incorporating a minimal length from generalized uncertainty principle (GUP) into the kinetic energy operator, using an infinite square well potential in the beta collective variable. The authors claim that the minimal length correction increases the energy spectrum slightly and that the resulting X(3)-ML and X(5)-ML models show qualitative agreement with experimental data for several vibrational-like nuclei, with the caveat that the improvement over the standard X(3) and X(5) models is modest and depends on the specific nucleus and angular momentum.

## Method

The authors start from the Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian for a prolate gamma-rigid nucleus (gamma = 0) and introduce a minimal length by replacing the standard momentum operator with a deformed one, P_i = (1 + alpha p^2) p_i, derived from the commutation relation [X,P] = i hbar (1 + alpha^2 P^2). Working to first order in the minimal length parameter alpha, they obtain a Schrodinger-like equation containing a bi-Laplacian term, which they eliminate via an auxiliary wave function transformation. The resulting radial equation in the beta variable, with an infinite square well potential (V(beta)=0 for beta <= beta_omega, infinite otherwise), reduces to a Bessel equation whose solutions give the energy spectrum E_{s,L} = (hbar^2 / 2B_m) * (k_{s,eta}^2) / (1 - 2 hbar^2 alpha k_{s,eta}^2), where k_{s,eta} = chi_{s,eta} / beta_omega and chi_{s,eta} are zeros of Bessel functions. The models are applied to six nuclei (Nd-150, Os-176, Os-178, Os-180, Dy-156, Gd-154) by fitting the two free parameters alpha and beta_omega.

## Key numbers

* For X(3)-ML with beta_omega = 60 and alpha = 0.5, the normalized ground-state band energies (relative to 2+ state) are: 4+ = 2.455, 6+ = 4.274, 8+ = 6.448, 10+ = 8.980, 12+ = 11.880.
* For X(3)-ML with beta_omega = 60 and alpha = 1.0, the normalized ground-state band energies are: 4+ = 2.465, 6+ = 4.315, 8+ = 6.551, 10+ = 9.194, 12+ = 12.270.
* For X(5)-ML with beta_omega = 60 and alpha = 0.5, the normalized ground-state band energies are: 4+ = 2.914, 6+ = 5.477, 8+ = 8.610, 10+ = 12.290, 12+ = 16.530.
* For X(5)-ML with beta_omega = 60 and alpha = 1.0, the normalized ground-state band energies are: 4+ = 2.925, 6+ = 5.526, 8+ = 8.741, 10+ = 12.570, 12+ = 17.050.
* The fitted parameters for Nd-150 are: X(3)-ML alpha = 0.961, beta_omega = 29.446; X(5)-ML alpha = 0.184, beta_omega = 67.308.
* The fitted parameters for Os-176 are: X(3)-ML alpha = 0.421, beta_omega = 42.517; X(5)-ML alpha = 0.000, beta_omega = 64.670.
* The minimal observable length from the GUP is given by (Delta X)_min = hbar sqrt(alpha) for the one-dimensional case, and (Delta X_i)_min = hbar sqrt(3 alpha + alpha') for the general three-dimensional case.

## Context

This work extends the X(3) model of Bonatsos (2004) and the X(5) model of Iachello (2001) by incorporating a minimal length from generalized uncertainty principle, which arises from string theory and quantum gravity considerations. The models are applied to nuclei with R_{4/2} = E(4_g+)/E(2_g+) >= 2.44, complementing the work of Budaca (2012) on X(3)-beta^{2n} models which target nuclei with R_{4/2} < 2.44. The paper enables future studies of gamma-rigid nuclei using deformed quantum mechanics, though the B(E2) transition probabilities remain unchanged from the standard X(3) and X(5) models.
