# Application of EFT at Thermal Energies

> We have been evaluated some observables of n-d systems by using pionless Effective Field Theory(\EFTNoPion) and insertion of the three-body force up to next-to-next to leading order(N$^2$LO). The evaluated data has been compared with experimental and the three-nucleon calculation of the total cross section with modern realistic two- and three-nucleon forces AV18/UrbIX potential models calculations.

# Application of EFT at Thermal Energies

## Key claim
Pionless Effective Field Theory (EFT($\pi\!\!/$)) with a three-body force inserted up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO) reproduces the neutron-deuteron capture cross section and photon polarization parameter $R_c$ at thermal energies, with systematic errors smaller than the experimental error bar, provided the triton binding energy and nd scattering length in the triton channel are used to fix the three-body parameters.

## Method
The Faddeev equation for neutron-deuteron scattering was solved numerically with a cut-off $\Lambda$, and a three-body force was added to achieve cut-off independent results. The $M_1$ magnetic dipole amplitude for the $nd\rightarrow{^3H}\gamma$ capture process was calculated up to N$^2$LO in pionless EFT, considering only $S$-wave states at thermal incident neutron energies. The triton binding energy and nd scattering length in the triton channel were used to fix the three-body force parameters.

## Key numbers
- $nd\rightarrow{^3H}\gamma$ cross section at thermal energies: $0.503 \pm 0.003$ mb (EFT N$^2$LO result), compared to experimental value $0.503 \pm 0.003$ mb.
- Photon polarization parameter $R_c$: $-0.412 \pm 0.003$ (EFT N$^2$LO result), compared to experimental value $-0.42 \pm 0.03$.
- Triton photodisintegration cross section at 12 MeV: 0.866 mb (including $M_1$ and $E_2$ contributions), compared to 0.882 mb from AV18/UrbIX with three-nucleon forces (Skibinski).
- AV18/UrbIX (IA+MI+MD+$\Delta$) result for $nd\rightarrow{^3H}\gamma$: 0.631 mb (Viviani).
- AV18/UrbIX (gauge invariant + 3N-current) result for $nd\rightarrow{^3H}\gamma$: 0.578 mb (Marcucci).

## Context
This work extends the application of pionless EFT to thermal-energy neutron-deuteron observables relevant to Big-Bang Nucleosynthesis, following earlier calculations by Sadeghi (2007-2009) for astrophysical observables. The results are compared with modern realistic two- and three-nucleon force calculations using the AV18/UrbIX potential models (Viviani, Marcucci, Skibinski) and with experimental data (Jurney). The approach enables systematic, order-by-order improvement of low-energy three-nucleon observables with controlled theoretical uncertainties, and demonstrates that pionless EFT can achieve precision matching or exceeding experimental accuracy at thermal energies.
