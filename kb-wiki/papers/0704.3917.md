# A fully microscopical simulation of nuclear collisions by a new QMD model

> Nucleon-ion and ion-ion collisions at non relativistic bombarding energies can be described by means of Monte Carlo approaches, such as those based on the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model. We have developed a QMD code, to simulate the fast stage of heavy-ion reactions, and we have coupled it to the de-excitation module available in the FLUKA Monte Carlo transport and interaction code. The results presented in this work span the projectile bombarding energy range within 200 - 600 MeV/A, allowing to investigate the capabilities and limits of our non-relativistic QMD approach.

## Key claim

The newly developed QMD code, when coupled to the FLUKA de-excitation module, can reproduce experimental data for nucleon-ion and ion-ion collisions in the 200–600 MeV/A range, but its non-relativistic potentials limit its reliability above a few hundred MeV/A and it systematically underestimates Z=13 fragment production cross-sections while overestimating Z=6 production for Cu and Pb targets.

## Method

The authors developed a Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) code to simulate the fast stage (a few hundred fm/c) of heavy-ion reactions, then coupled it to the PEANUT de-excitation module in the FLUKA Monte Carlo code, which handles evaporation, fission, fragmentation, and Fermi break-up for excited pre-fragments. Simulations were performed for various projectile-target combinations (p+C, Ar+C, Si+C, Si+Cu, Si+Pb) at bombarding energies between 200 and 600 MeV/A, and results were compared to experimental data for neutron, proton, and fragment emission cross-sections.

## Key numbers

- Ar projectiles on C at 560 MeV/A bombarding energy: neutron double-differential production cross-sections were compared to experimental data from Iwata; the model underestimates neutron emission tails from ~800 MeV/A up to the highest energies at forward angles (5–10°), but reproduces tails well at angles ≥40°.
- p + C at 300 MeV/A and 392 MeV/A bombarding energies: proton emission cross-sections show good agreement at forward angles except for the quasi-elastic peak, while tails at larger angles are underestimated.
- Si ions on C at 266 and 344 MeV/A: projectile-like fragment production cross-sections for charges Z=6–13 were compared to experimental data from HIMAC.
- Si ions on Cu at 274, 344, and 545 MeV/A: same fragment production comparisons.
- Si ions on Pb at 274, 364, and 540 MeV/A: same fragment production comparisons; discrepancies between model predictions and experimental data do not exceed ~30–35% in most cases for the heavy target.

## Context

This work extends the QMD approach by coupling it to the FLUKA de-excitation module (PEANUT), building on prior QMD models such as those by Aichelin and the CoMD model developed by Papa et al. The results enable improved calculations for hadrontherapy and space radioprotection dose estimates, and the paper identifies the need for relativistic corrections and inclusion of pion production to extend the model to higher energies. The systematic underestimation of Z=13 fragments and overestimation of Z=6 fragments suggests the model works better for central collisions than for stripping reactions, and the authors note that electromagnetic dissociation may contribute to Z=12 and Z=13 production for heavy targets at high energy, as suggested by the experimentalists.
