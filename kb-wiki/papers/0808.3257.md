# Role of the N*(1440) resonance in the pp->pnpi+ reaction

> New measurement by CELSIUS-WASA Collaboration on the pp->pn pi+ reaction reveals clear evidence for the presence of the Roper resonance N*(1440) which has been ignored in previous theoretical calculations. In this article, based on an effective Lagrangian approach and available knowledge on the Roper resonance, we investigate the role of the Roper resonance for the pp->pn pi+ reaction. It is found that the contribution from the Roper resonance N*(1440) becomes significant for kinetic energy above 1.1 GeV, consistent with the new experimental observation. The t-channel sigma-meson exchange is dominant for the production of the Roper resonance.

## Key claim

The Roper resonance N*(1440) contributes significantly to the pp → pn π+ reaction for proton beam kinetic energies above 1.1 GeV, consistent with the CELSIUS-WASA Collaboration measurement, but only when the t-channel σ-meson exchange mechanism is included for its production.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach with a Lorentz covariant orbital-spin scheme to calculate the total cross section and invariant mass spectra for the pp → pn π+ reaction. They included contributions from the Δ(1232) resonance, the N*(1440) Roper resonance, and the off-shell nucleon pole, with π⁰, π⁺, and σ-meson exchanges, using Monte Carlo phase space integration (CERN program FOWL) and a Jost function formalism for the np final-state interaction in the triplet ³S₁ state.

## Key numbers

- The Roper resonance contribution becomes significant for proton beam kinetic energy above 1.1 GeV, with the σ-meson exchange mechanism dominant for its production.
- The Δ⁺⁺(1232) production by π⁺ exchange dominates for T_P between 0.6 and 1.1 GeV; the Δ⁺(1232) with π⁰ exchange gives a cross section one order of magnitude smaller due to an isospin factor of 1/9.
- At T_P = 1.3 GeV, the interference term between Δ⁺⁺ and N*(1440) resonances contributes at most ±5% of the Δ⁺⁺ contribution alone.
- The Roper resonance appears in the nπ⁺ invariant mass spectrum at M ≈ 1360 MeV/c² with a width of about 150 MeV, matching the CELSIUS-WASA observation.
- The cut-off parameter for the ΔπN vertex form factor was adjusted to Λ^Δ_π = 0.59 GeV (compared to 0.65 GeV in previous work by Kundu and 0.63 GeV by Dmitriev).
- The np ³S₁ FSI parameters used were a_t = 5.424 fm and r_t = 1.759 fm.

## Context

This work extends the earlier covariant calculation by Engel et al. (1997) for the pp → pn π⁺ reaction, which included only the Δ resonance and off-shell nucleon pole without the Roper resonance, and the calculation by Kundu (2004) at T_P = 0.8 GeV. It is motivated by the CELSIUS-WASA Collaboration's observation of a clear Roper resonance peak in the nπ⁺ invariant mass spectrum, consistent with the earlier BES Collaboration result from J/ψ → pπ⁻ n̄. The study enables future investigations of N* resonances at COSY with WASA-at-COSY and HIRFL-CSR facilities, particularly for searching for "missing" Δ*⁺⁺ and N* resonances at higher energies.
