# Effect of nuclear periphery on nucleon transfer in peripheral collisions

> A comparison of experimental heavy residue cross sections from the reactions 86Kr+64Ni,112,124Sn with the model of deep-inelastic transfer (DIT) is carried out. A modified expression for nucleon transfer probabilities is used at non-overlapping projectile-target configurations, introducing a dependence on isospin asymmetry at the nuclear periphery. The experimental yields of neutron-rich nuclei close to the projectile are reproduced better and the trend deviating from the bulk isospin equilibration is explained. For the neutron-rich products further from the projectile, originating from hot quasiprojectiles, the statistical multifragmentation model reproduces the mass distributions better than the model of sequential binary decay. In the reaction with proton-rich target 112Sn the nucleon exchange appears to depend on isospin asymmetry of nuclear periphery only when surface separation is larger than 0.8 fm due to the stronger Coulomb interaction at more compact di-nuclear configuration.

## Key claim

The observed enhancement of neutron-rich yields in peripheral collisions of 86Kr with 64Ni, 112Sn, and 124Sn at 25 AMeV can be explained by a modification of nucleon transfer probabilities that depends on isospin asymmetry at the nuclear periphery, but only when the projectile and target surfaces are separated by more than 0.8 fm for the proton-rich target 112Sn due to stronger Coulomb repulsion.

## Method

The authors compared experimental heavy residue cross sections from the reactions 86Kr+64Ni,112,124Sn at 25 AMeV with the deep-inelastic transfer (DIT) model of Tassan-Got. They introduced a modified expression for nucleon transfer probabilities at non-overlapping projectile-target configurations by scaling the standard probabilities with exponential factors containing the differences of experimental neutron and proton separation energies relative to liquid-drop values, using a free parameter kappa. The modified DIT calculations were combined with two de-excitation codes: the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) and the sequential binary decay model (GEMINI), and the simulated yields were filtered for the angular acceptance of the separator.

## Key numbers

- The optimum value of the free parameter kappa was determined to be 0.53, obtained by best reproducing the experimental mass distributions for the reaction 86Kr+64Ni at 25 AMeV.
- For the reaction 86Kr+112Sn, the nucleon exchange depends on isospin asymmetry of nuclear periphery only when the surface separation is larger than 0.8 fm, due to stronger Coulomb interaction at more compact di-nuclear configurations.
- The experimental data were obtained at a beam energy of 25 AMeV for all three reactions.
- The separator for the 86Kr+64Ni reaction covered polar angles 1.0 - 2.7 degrees; for the 86Kr+112,124Sn reactions the separator was positioned at 4 degrees covering polar angles 2.7 - 5.4 degrees.

## Context

This work extends the DIT model of Tassan-Got (1994) by adding a phenomenological correction for shell structure effects at the nuclear periphery, building on earlier attempts by Souliotis et al. (2003) to implement density-dependent corrections that were not sufficiently consistent. The study explains the enhancement of neutron-rich yields previously observed by Souliotis et al. (2003) in the reaction 86Kr+64Ni, and is consistent with the isoscaling study of Souliotis et al. (2003) that correlated the isoscaling parameter with isospin equilibration. The results enable better predictions of production rates for exotic nuclei with a wide range of N/Z ratios at future rare isotope beam facilities, and suggest that for proton-rich targets the Coulomb repulsion suppresses the sensitivity to peripheral isospin asymmetry at surface separations below 0.8 fm.
