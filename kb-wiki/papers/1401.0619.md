# Kaon femtoscopy in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV central Au+Au collsions at STAR

> Three-dimensional analyses of the pion source revealed a heavy, non-Gaussian tail in the direction of the pair transverse momentum. The interpretation of these pion sources in terms of pure hydrodynamical evolution is, however, complicated by the strong contribution of feed-down from long-lived resonances to the source. On the other hand, kaons provide a much cleaner probe of the expanding fireball. Here we present a recent three-dimensional kaon correlation analysis using Cartesian harmonics decomposition technique. In contrary to pions, the three-dimensional source function of kaons is largely Gaussian. Comparison with thermal simulations and a hydrokinetic model show that resonance decays, as well as non-zero emission duration and/or rescattering in the hadronic phase play an important role. The analysis of the three dimensional extent of the kaon source w.r.t. the pair transverse momentum favors the hydrokinetic model over the exact mT-scaling featured by perfect hydrodynamical models.

# Kaon femtoscopy in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV central Au+Au collisions at STAR

## Key claim

The three-dimensional kaon source function in central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV is largely Gaussian, in contrast to pions which show a non-Gaussian tail; however, the transverse mass scaling expected from perfect hydrodynamical models appears to be broken in the longitudinal direction, favoring the hydrokinetic model over pure hydrodynamic calculations.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration analyzed Bose-Einstein correlations of low-transverse-momentum like-sign kaon pairs at mid-rapidity from 4.6 million (2004) and 16 million (2007) 0-20% central Au+Au events, plus 6.6 million 0-30% central events for the transverse-momentum-dependent analysis. The three-dimensional correlation functions were decomposed using Cartesian harmonics, and a 3D Gaussian trial function was fitted simultaneously to the even independent moments up to l=4, yielding chi-squared per degree of freedom values between 1.1 and 1.7. Source imaging was not possible due to limited kaon statistics.

## Key numbers

- Kaon source shape analysis used 4.6 million 0-20% central events from 2004 and 16 million 0-20% central events from 2007, with pair transverse momentum kT between 0.2 and 0.36 GeV/c
- The kT-dependent analysis used 6.6 million 0-30% central events from 2004, with two kT bins: 0.2-0.36 GeV/c and 0.36-0.48 GeV/c
- Kaon tracks were selected with transverse momentum between 0.1 and 1.0 GeV/c
- The Gaussian fit in the source shape analysis yielded chi-squared/ndf = 1.7; in the kT-dependent analysis, chi-squared/ndf = 1.1 and 1.3 for the two kT bins respectively
- The primary vertex position along the beam line was constrained to |z| < 30 cm

## Context

This work extends prior pion femtoscopy studies at STAR and PHENIX (Adler 2006, Chung 2010) which revealed non-Gaussian tails in the pion source, complicated by resonance feed-down. A one-dimensional kaon source image measurement by PHENIX (Akiyama 2009) reported an even heavier tail than pions but over a broad kT range. The present three-dimensional analysis shows kaons have a largely Gaussian source, and the kT-dependence of the Gaussian radii favors the hydrokinetic model (Shapoval 2013) over the Buda-Lund perfect fluid model (Csanad 2008), suggesting that resonance decays, non-zero emission duration, and rescattering in the hadronic phase all contribute to the observed source characteristics.
