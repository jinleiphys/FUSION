# Description of the Totem experimental data on elastic pp-scattering at sqrt(s)=7 TeV in the framework of unified systematic of elastic scattering data

> An unified systematic of elastic (anti)proton-proton scattering data is proposed based on a simple expression for the process amplitude - f(q)=A[(πdq)/sh(πdq)][i J_1(Rq)/(Rq)+ρJ_0(Rq)]. The parameters R and d are obtained at a fitting of (anti)proton-proton experimental data on differential cross sections from Plab=1 GeV/c up to ISR energies. The fitting gives extra-ordinary good results, χ^2/NoF ~ 1 of below at |t|< 1.75 (GeV/c)^2. An extrapolation of the parameter's energy dependencies to the LHC energies allows excellent description of the Totem data up to the second diffraction maximum. Predictions for other LHC energies are presented also. The amplitude provides one with parameterizations of total and elastic cross sections. Its impact parameter representation corresponds to the 2-dimensional Fermi-function - 1/[1+exp((b-R)/d)], which is very useful for Glauber calculations of nucleus-nucleus cross sections at super high energies. It is shown for the first time that experimental high |t| elastic scattering has a weak energy dependency. This allows to describe high |t| tail of the Totem data.

## Key claim

The authors propose a unified systematic description of elastic (anti)proton-proton scattering data using a simple analytic amplitude, and show that extrapolating the energy dependence of its parameters to LHC energies provides an excellent description of the TOTEM data at sqrt(s)=7 TeV up to the second diffraction maximum, but only for |t| < 1.75 (GeV/c)^2.

## Method

The elastic scattering amplitude is parameterized as f(q)=A[(pi d q)/sinh(pi d q)][i J_1(Rq)/(Rq)+rho J_0(Rq)], where the parameters R, d, rho, and a_1 are obtained by fitting differential cross section data for pp and antiproton-proton scattering from Plab ~ 1 GeV/c up to Tevatron energies, restricted to |t| < 1.75 (GeV/c)^2. The energy dependencies of R and d are then extrapolated to LHC energies to predict the TOTEM data. The amplitude's impact parameter representation corresponds to a 2-dimensional Fermi function, 1/[1+exp((b-R)/d)].

## Key numbers

- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for pp-interactions: chi^2/NoF = 1156/1489 ~ 0.78 at Plab >= 9.9 GeV/c
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for antiproton-proton interactions: chi^2/NoF = 856/675 ~ 1.27 at Plab >= 8 GeV/c
- The fitting region is restricted to |t| < 1.75 (GeV/c)^2
- Parameterization for R_pp: R = 0.07 + 0.05 ln(s) + 0.6/s^(1/4) + 0.2/s^(1/2) (fm)
- Parameterization for d_pp = d_antipp: d = 0.36 - 0.5/sqrt(s) (fm)
- The lower energy boundary for pp-interactions is estimated as Plab = 2 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the Strong Absorption Model (SAM) approach used by Frahn and collaborators for hadron-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus scattering, and builds on the work of Brogueira and Dias de Deus (2005) who used a similar Fermi-function form for elastic pp and antiproton-proton data at lower energies. The proposed parameterization enables Glauber calculations of hadron-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus cross sections at super high energies using the 2-dimensional Fermi function, which is relevant for cosmic ray studies and for use in the Quark-Gluon-String Model (QGSM) and HIJING event generator.
