# Perspectives of photon physics at future colliders

> We review current results on physics with photons at the LHC and discuss realistic perspectives of photon physics at future colliders. In particular, we focus on Standard Model (SM) measurements with photons at the upcoming high-luminosity and a possible high-energy LHC as well as jet measurements at an Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) to be constructed either at BNL or at JLAB and their potential to constrain nuclear parton densities. We also discuss future searches for physics beyond the SM with photons in the high-luminosity phase of the LHC.

## Key claim
The paper argues that photon physics at future colliders (HL/HE LHC and EIC) offers realistic opportunities for Standard Model measurements and BSM searches, but emphasizes that the LHC has so far produced no evidence for BSM particles, making the decision to build a linear collider still pending.

## Method
The author reviews current LHC results on prompt photon production, inclusive and exclusive photoproduction, and BSM searches with photons, then extrapolates to future collider scenarios using existing theoretical calculations (NLO, NNLO, POWHEG, PYTHIA) and experimental projections from the HL-LHC, HE-LHC, and EIC design studies. The analysis focuses on how photon-induced processes can constrain proton and nuclear parton distribution functions (PDFs) and probe dark matter candidates.

## Key numbers
- HL LHC can reach inclusive photon transverse momenta up to 5 TeV and diphoton transverse momenta up to 2 TeV, compared to current reach of about 1 TeV and 700 GeV respectively.
- In ultraperipheral PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV, photonuclear dijet data with 5% precision could reduce nuclear PDF uncertainties at x ~ 10^{-3} by more than a factor of two.
- At the HL LHC, the reduction of nuclear PDF uncertainty could reach a factor of four if jet p_T is measured down to 8 GeV (compared to 20 GeV).
- For exclusive dimuon pairs in PbPb collisions, the HL LHC would reach invariant masses up to 100 GeV.
- At the EIC, the eRHIC option with 21 GeV electron and 100 GeV per nucleon ion beam allows inclusive jet p_T values up to 35 GeV in DIS.
- Monophoton searches at the HL LHC with 3 ab^{-1} integrated luminosity can set limits on dark matter masses up to 300 GeV.
- For dark photon searches, LHCb can probe masses between 214 MeV and 70 GeV via A' -> mu+ mu- decays.
- Light-by-light scattering in PbPb collisions at 5.52 TeV provides unique sensitivity to axion-like particles in the mass range from 7 to 140 GeV.

## Context
This review extends earlier photon-photon interaction studies at linear e+e- colliders (Badelek 2001, Berge 2000) and summarizes current LHC results including Higgs boson discovery (Aad 2012), light-by-light scattering observation (Aaboud 2017), and quark-gluon plasma temperature measurements (Klasen 2013). The work enables future experimental programs at the HL/HE LHC and EIC, particularly for constraining nuclear PDFs (building on EPPS16 and nCTEQ15 analyses) and searching for dark matter candidates including WIMPs, dark photons, and axion-like particles.
