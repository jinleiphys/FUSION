# Non-Fermi liquid behavior of the drag and diffusion coefficients in QED plasma

> We calculate the drag and diffusion coefficients in low temperature QED plasma and go beyond the leading order approximation. The non-Fermi-liquid behavior of these coefficients are clearly revealed. We observe that the subleading contributions due to the exchange of soft transverse photon in both cases are larger than the leading order terms coming from the longitudinal sector. The results are presented in closed form at zero and low temperature.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the subleading (next-to-leading order) contributions to the drag and diffusion coefficients from soft transverse photon exchange in low-temperature QED plasma are larger than the leading-order contributions from longitudinal photon exchange, revealing non-Fermi-liquid behavior; this dominance does not imply breakdown of perturbation theory because the next-to-leading order terms in each sector individually are smaller than their corresponding leading parts.

## Method

The authors calculate the drag coefficient (η) and longitudinal diffusion coefficient (B∥) for a fermion propagating in ultradegenerate QED plasma at low temperature (T ≪ μ) by evaluating the imaginary part of the fermion self-energy weighted by energy or squared momentum transfer, respectively. They use the hard-thermal-loop/hard-density-loop (HTL/HDL) resummed photon propagator in Coulomb gauge for the soft momentum-transfer region and expand the integrand in powers of the small parameter a = T/m_D, retaining terms up to a^{10/3} for the transverse sector and up to a^4 for the longitudinal sector. The zero-temperature limit is obtained by taking T → 0, where the polylogarithmic functions reduce to powers of |E-μ|.

## Key numbers

- The zero-temperature drag coefficient is given by η = (e²|E-μ|²)/(48πE) - (3×2^{1/3}e²m_D²)/(72π^{7/3}E) × (|E-μ|/m_D)^{8/3} + (e²|E-μ|³)/(96m_DE) + ..., where the first two terms are from the transverse sector and the third from the longitudinal sector.
- The zero-temperature longitudinal diffusion coefficient is given by B = (e²|E-μ|³)/(72π) - (2^{1/3}e²m_D³)/(33π^{7/3}) × (|E-μ|/m_D)^{11/3} + (e²|E-μ|⁴)/(128m_D) + ..., with the same sector decomposition.
- The Debye mass is m_D² = (e²/π²)(μ² + π²T²/3), which sets the screening scale for both electric and magnetic interactions.
- The small expansion parameter is a = T/m_D ≪ 1, with the condition T ∼ |E-μ| ≪ eμ ≪ μ for the astrophysically relevant regime.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous leading-order calculation (Sarkar 2010) of drag and diffusion coefficients in ultradegenerate relativistic plasma by including subleading terms, and follows the approach of Gerhold (2005) who first noted the dominance of subleading transverse contributions in fermion self-energy calculations. The results are relevant for determining transport properties (entropy, pressure, specific heat, viscosity) of the quark component of neutron or proto-neutron stars, and for calculations of neutrino emissivity and mean free path in ungapped quark matter (Schafer 2004, Pal 2011). The non-Fermi-liquid behavior originates from the absence of magnetostatic screening, as first identified by Holstein (1973) in the context of specific heat corrections in degenerate electron gas.
