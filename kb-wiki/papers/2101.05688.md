# Properties of the QGP created in heavy-ion collisions

> We review the properties of the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (QGP) at finite temperature $T$ and baryon chemical potential $μ_B$ as created in heavy-ion collisions at ultrarelativistic energies. The description of the strongly interacting (non-perturbative) QGP in equilibrium is based on the effective propagators and couplings from the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM) that is matched to reproduce the equation-of-state of the partonic system above the deconfinement temperature $T_C$ from lattice QCD. Based on a microscopic transport description of heavy-ion collisions we discuss which observables are sensitive to the QGP creation and its properties.

# Properties of the QGP created in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (QGP) created in heavy-ion collisions can be described by the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM) matched to lattice QCD results, but the sensitivity of bulk hadronic observables and collective flow coefficients to the baryon chemical potential dependence of partonic properties and cross sections is very low, because at high energies the QGP region has small baryon chemical potential while at lower energies the QGP fraction is small.

## Method
The authors employed the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach (version 5.0), which solves Kadanoff-Baym equations in first-order gradient expansion, with the partonic phase modeled by the DQPM. The DQPM provides effective propagators and couplings for quarks and gluons with complex self-energies (masses and widths) that depend on temperature T and baryon chemical potential μB, matched to reproduce the lattice QCD equation of state. Partonic interaction cross sections (elastic and inelastic) were calculated from leading-order Feynman diagrams using the DQPM effective couplings and depend on invariant energy √s, T, and μB. The hadronization from partonic to hadronic degrees of freedom is described by covariant transition rates for quark-antiquark fusion or three-quark fusion.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature Tc = 0.158 GeV (used as phase boundary reference)
- For central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A GeV, the T-μB distribution at early times (t < 2 fm/c) peaks at temperature ~0.25 GeV and chemical potential ~0.6 GeV; for 2 fm/c < t < 4 fm/c the peak drops to average temperature ~0.18 GeV and chemical potential ~0.3 GeV; for t > 4 fm/c the distribution stays around μB ≈ 0.25 GeV
- The average μB from PHSD simulations around chemical freeze-out for central Pb+Pb at 158 A GeV is 0.2489 GeV, in agreement with statistical model results
- At high collision energies, approximately 90% of matter at midrapidity is in the QGP phase (for a short time)
- Local critical energy density εc ≈ 0.5 GeV/fm³ extracted from lattice QCD results

## Context
This work extends the PHSD transport approach (previously developed by Cassing, Bratkovskaya, and collaborators over more than a decade) by incorporating explicit μB dependence of parton masses, widths, and cross sections, building on earlier studies where these depended only on T (Ozvenchuk 2012). The results are compared to experimental data from the STAR collaboration at RHIC (Beam Energy Scan program) and to statistical model analyses by Andronic (2009), Cleymans (2005), and Adamczyk (2017). The study shows that while transport coefficients η/s and ζ/s from DQPM agree with lattice QCD results for pure SU(3) gauge theory (Astrakhantsev 2017, 2018), the μB dependence of partonic interactions has only a small visible effect on collective flow coefficients, with antihyperons being the most sensitive probes. This work enables future improvements in T-μB extraction for hadronic-dominated matter and motivates further study of hadronic vector potentials.
