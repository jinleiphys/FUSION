# Probing the sensitivity of anisotropic flow coefficients to the initial nuclear structure in pO and OO collisions at the LHC

> RHIC and LHC have injected $^{16}\rm O$ nuclei in their accelerator complexes with a focus on investigating collectivity and the origin of quark-gluon plasma signatures in small collision systems. The $^{16}\rm O$ nuclei are known to possess clusters of $α$-particles ($^{4}\rm He$) inside the nucleus. This paper attempts to study the clustered-nuclear-geometry dependence of anisotropic flow coefficients such as elliptic flow ($v_2$) and triangular flow ($v_3$), which are sensitive to the nuclear geometry of colliding nuclei. The study is performed in pO and OO collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=9.61$~TeV and 7~TeV respectively, employing a hybrid model encompassing IP-Glasma + MUSIC + iSS + UrQMD. The results of the clustered nuclear geometry are compared with those of the Woods\,--\,Saxon nuclear profile. Both initial and final state anisotropies are estimated. This study is thus one of its first kind, where the study of anisotropic flow coefficients for pO and OO collisions is presented using a hybrid hydrodynamics model. While the effect of $α$-clustering in pO is found to be small, it is significant for each observable studied in OO collisions. It is also observed that the magnitude of this effect has a noteworthy dependence on the size of the \textsuperscript{4}He.

# Probing the sensitivity of anisotropic flow coefficients to the initial nuclear structure in pO and OO collisions at the LHC

## Key claim

The effect of alpha-clustering in oxygen nuclei on anisotropic flow coefficients is small in pO collisions but significant for each observable studied in OO collisions; the magnitude of this effect has a noteworthy dependence on the size of the helium-4 cluster, and the study is presented using a hybrid hydrodynamics model for the first time for these collision systems.

## Method

The authors simulate pO collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=9.61 TeV and OO collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=7 TeV using a hybrid model framework consisting of IP-Glasma for initial conditions, MUSIC for (2+1)D boost-invariant viscous hydrodynamic evolution with eta/s=0.12 and temperature-dependent bulk viscosity, iSS for particlization via the Cooper-Frye formula, and UrQMD for final-state hadronic cascade. Nucleonic positions in oxygen are sampled either from a Woods-Saxon distribution or from an alpha-clustered configuration where four alpha-particles sit at the vertices of a regular tetrahedron with side-length 3.42 fm, and anisotropic flow coefficients v2 and v3 are estimated using the two-particle Q-cumulant method with a pseudorapidity gap |Delta-eta|>1.0.

## Key numbers

- pO collisions simulated at sqrt(s_NN)=9.61 TeV; OO collisions simulated at sqrt(s_NN)=7 TeV
- Tetrahedron side-length set to 3.42 fm, yielding an RMS radius of 2.699 fm for the alpha-clustered oxygen nucleus
- Woods-Saxon parameters for helium-4 clusters: w=0.517 (deformation parameter), a=0.322 fm (skin-depth), r0=0.964 fm (mean radius), RMS radius 1.676 fm
- Minimum nucleon-nucleon separation fixed at 0.4 fm
- Hydrodynamic switching time tau_switch=0.4 fm; switching energy density epsilon_switch=0.18 GeV/fm^3
- Shear viscosity to entropy ratio eta/s=0.12
- Lattice size L=14, lattice spacing a=0.02 fm for IP-Glasma
- 200 hadronic freezeout samples per IP-Glasma+MUSIC event
- In OO collisions, v2 in the 10-20% centrality class for alpha-cluster profile is approximately 30% higher than the corresponding v2 from Woods-Saxon profile
- In OO collisions, triangularity epsilon_3 from alpha-clustered profile is approximately 30% higher than Woods-Saxon in most central collisions
- In pO collisions, epsilon_2 toward peripheral collisions is around 25% higher than most central collisions for both density profiles
- In OO collisions, epsilon_2 rises more than 40% from central to peripheral for Woods-Saxon profile

## Context

This work extends prior studies of alpha-clustered nuclear structure effects in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly building on earlier AMPT model studies of OO collisions by Behera et al. (2023) and Prasad et al. (2024) that showed anisotropic flow fluctuations are sensitive to clustered nuclear geometry. The study also connects to the RHIC geometry scan program (PHENIX 2018, STAR 2022) and to LHC small-system collectivity measurements (ALICE 2024). The results enable future experimental comparisons with LHC pO and OO run data, and may help tune cosmic ray air shower models by resolving puzzles in muon production.
