# Superfluid to normal phase transition and extreme regularity of superdeformed bands

> We derive the exact semiclassical expression for the second inertial parameter $\cal B$ for the superfluid and normal phases. Interpolation between these limiting values shows that the function ${\cal B}(I)$ changes sign at the spin $I_c$, which is critical for a rotational spectrum. The quantity $\cal B$ turns out to be a sensitive measure of the change in static pairing correlations. The superfluid-to-normal transition reveals itself in the specific variation of the ratio ${\cal B}/{\cal A}$ versus spin $I$ with the plateau characteristic of the normal phase. We find this dependence to be universal for normal deformed and superdeformed bands. The long plateau with a small value ${\cal B}/{\cal A}\sim A^{-8/3}$ explains the extreme regularity of superdeformed bands.

## Key claim

The second inertial parameter $\mathcal{B}$ changes sign at a critical spin $I_c$, and the ratio $\mathcal{B}/\mathcal{A}$ exhibits a universal dependence on spin $I$ for both normal deformed and superdeformed bands, with a long plateau at a small value $\mathcal{B}/\mathcal{A} \sim A^{-8/3}$ that explains the extreme regularity of superdeformed bands, though this regularity is achieved only after the superfluid-to-normal phase transition, and the interpolation between limiting values avoids the difficult transition region where pairing fluctuations dominate.

## Method

The author derives exact semiclassical expressions for the second inertial parameter $\mathcal{B}$ in both the superfluid phase (where static pairing gap $\Delta$ dominates over fluctuations $\delta\Delta$) and the normal phase ($\Delta=0$) using a Green's function formulation of the cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogolubov (HFB) method in an axially deformed oscillator potential. An interpolation between these limiting values is used to study the spin dependence of $\mathcal{B}/\mathcal{A}$, avoiding the transition region where quantum fluctuations of the pairing field are strong. The nonuniform pairing induced by rotation is treated via a perturbation expansion of the Gor'kov equations in the cranking term $V = -\omega\ell_x$, with the self-consistent pairing field corrections $\Delta^{(1)}$ and $\Delta^{(2)}$ determined from integral equations.

## Key numbers

- For the superdeformed band $^{194}$Pb(1), the ratio $\mathcal{B}/\mathcal{A} \sim 10^{-5}$.
- For $^{152}$Dy(1), the ratio $\mathcal{B}/\mathcal{A} \sim 10^{-6}$, which is 1000 times smaller than the earlier estimate $\mathcal{B}/\mathcal{A} \sim A^{-4/3}$ for normal deformed nuclei.
- The plateau value for the normal phase is $\mathcal{B}/\mathcal{A} \sim A^{-8/3}$.
- The average level spacing for normal deformed nuclei is $\delta\varepsilon \sim \varepsilon_F/A$, while the pairing gap $\Delta \sim \varepsilon_F/A^{2/3}$, so approximately $A^{1/3}$ levels fall within the interval $\Delta$.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier calculations with Grin' (1970) and Pavlichenkov (1977) on the second inertial parameter, which identified quasiparticle alignment and Coriolis antipairing as the dominant nonadiabatic effects (scaling as $A^{-4/3}$), with centrifugal stretching and vibration-rotation coupling contributing smaller $A^{2/3}$ fractions. The paper addresses the puzzle of superdeformed band regularity, which had been reproduced numerically by Satula (1993), Dobaczewski (1993), and others but lacked a microscopic explanation. The universal scaling $\mathcal{B}/\mathcal{A} \sim A^{-8/3}$ for the normal phase provides this explanation and enables systematic classification of rotational bands by their pairing phase.
