# Construction of the center-of-mass free space for the SU(3) no-core shell model

> We address the removal of states with center-of-mass excitation from the SU(3) no-core shell model [SU(3)-NCSM] space, i.e., construction of the nonspurious subspace. A procedure is formulated based on solution of the null-space problem for the center-of-mass harmonic oscillator lowering operator Bcm, operating at the level of SU(3) irreducible representations. Isolation of the center-of-mass free subspace for the SU(3)-NCSM provides the foundation for exact removal of center-of-mass dynamics in the proposed Sp(3,R) symplectic no-core shell model. We outline the construction process for the matrix representation of Bcm, present the algorithm for obtaining the nonspurious space, and examine the dimensions obtained for center-of-mass free SU(3) subspaces in representative light nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper presents a procedure for constructing the center-of-mass free (nonspurious) subspace within the SU(3) no-core shell model by solving the null-space problem for the center-of-mass harmonic oscillator lowering operator, operating at the level of SU(3) irreducible representations, rather than by the more common approach of adding a Lawson term to the Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors formulate the identification of center-of-mass free states as a null-space problem for the one-body center-of-mass lowering operator B_cm, which is an SU(3) (01) tensor. They construct the matrix representation of the SU(3)-reduced matrix elements of this operator between basis states of the SU(3)-NCSM at excitation Nex and Nex-1, then solve for null vectors to obtain the nonspurious subspace. The procedure is carried out independently within subspaces of definite Nex, SU(3) quantum numbers (lambda mu), and spin quantum numbers (S_proton, S_neutron, S), exploiting the fact that the center-of-mass Hamiltonian commutes with the SU(3) generators and spin operators.

## Key numbers

- For the deuteron (²H), at each Nex value, the null-space solution yields exactly two center-of-mass free reduced states: both with (lambda mu) = (Nex 0), one with S=0 and one with S=1.
- The dimensions of the SU(3)-reduced subspaces for ⁴He, ⁶Li, and ⁸Be vary from 1 to 640 for Nex ≤ 8, 6, and 4 respectively.
- For large Nex, the center-of-mass free fraction of the total space falls as Nex^(-3), based on approximate counting arguments.
- The estimated center-of-mass free fraction from counting arguments is approximately (3A-1 choose 3) / (Nex+3A-1 choose 3), which gives the exact result for the deuteron when counting all M substates.

## Context

This work extends the SU(3) no-core shell model (SU(3)-NCSM) developed by Dytrych et al. (2012) and provides the foundation for the proposed Sp(3,R) symplectic no-core shell model (Sp-NCSM) described by Dytrych et al. (2008). The approach differs from the conventional method of adding a Lawson term to the Hamiltonian, as used by Gloeckner and Lawson (1974), instead explicitly removing spurious states from the model space before diagonalization. The construction enables exact separation of center-of-mass dynamics in the Sp-NCSM, where each application of the translationally-invariant symplectic raising operator does not introduce center-of-mass excitation, so an Sp(3,R) irrep is center-of-mass free as long as it is built from an extremal SU(3) state that is free of such excitation.
