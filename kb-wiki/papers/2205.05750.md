# Combining nonperturbative transverse momentum dependence with TMD evolution

> Central to understanding the nonpertubative, intrinsic partonic nature of hadron structure are the concepts of transverse momentum dependent (TMD) parton distribution and fragmentation functions. A TMD factorization approach to the phenomenology of semi-inclusive processes that includes evolution, higher orders, and matching to larger transverse momentum, is ultimately necessary for reliably connecting with phenomenologically extracted nonperturbative structures, especially when widely different scales are involved. In this paper, we will address some of the difficulties that arise when phenomenological techniques that were originally designed for very high energy applications are extended to studies of hadron structures, and we will solidify the connection between standard high energy TMD implementations and the more intuitive, parton model based approaches to phenomenology that emphasize nonperturbative hadron structure. In the process, we will elaborate on differences between forward and backward TMD evolution, which in the context of this paper we call "bottom-up" and "top-down" approaches, and we will explain the advantages of a bottom-up strategy. We will also emphasize and clarify the role of the integral relations that connect TMD and collinear correlation functions. We will show explicitly how they constrain the nonperturbative "$g$-functions" of standard Collins-Soper-Sterman (CSS) implementations of TMD factorization. This paper is especially targeted toward phenomenologists and model builders who are interested in merging specific nonperturbative models and calculations (including lattice QCD) with TMD factorization at large $Q$. Our main result is a recipe for incorporating nonperturbative models into TMD factorization, and for constraining their parameters in a way that matches to perturbative QCD and evolution.

## Key claim

The paper provides a recipe for incorporating nonperturbative models of hadron structure (including lattice QCD calculations) into TMD factorization at large Q, while clarifying that standard "top-down" high-energy TMD implementations must be adapted to a "bottom-up" approach when the goal is to extract intrinsic, nonperturbative transverse momentum dependence from phenomenology.

## Method

The authors develop a bottom-up TMD evolution strategy that starts from a low-energy scale Q0 and evolves forward to higher Q, contrasting this with the conventional top-down approach that evolves from high to low energy. They demonstrate how integral relations connecting TMD and collinear correlation functions constrain the nonperturbative g-functions in Collins-Soper-Sterman (CSS) implementations, and they show explicit calculations for a fragmentation function example using Gaussian nonperturbative models with parameters M, mD, and mK.

## Key numbers

- Q0 = 2 GeV is used as the starting scale for bottom-up evolution, with example calculations shown at Q = 2, 10, 100, and 1000 GeV
- Nonperturbative model parameters: M = 0.20 GeV, mD = 0.30 GeV, mK = 0.10 GeV (with uncertainties of +-0.10, +-0.15, and +-0.05 GeV respectively)
- The ratio r(a1,a2) is shown for a1 = 2 GeV and a2 = 4 GeV, comparing results at Q = 4 GeV and Q = 100 GeV
- The bmax parameter is varied between 0.10, 0.50, and 1.00 GeV^-1 in the CSS-style bstar prescription comparison

## Context

This work extends the Collins-Soper-Sterman (CSS) TMD factorization framework to better accommodate nonperturbative hadron structure models, addressing difficulties that arise when high-energy phenomenological techniques are applied to lower-energy hadron structure studies. It enables phenomenologists and model builders to merge specific nonperturbative calculations (including lattice QCD results) with perturbative QCD evolution, and clarifies the distinction between forward (bottom-up) and backward (top-down) evolution approaches that have been conflated in prior implementations.
