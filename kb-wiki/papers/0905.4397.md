# Ground state energy of spin polarized quark matter with correlation

> We calculate the ground state energy of cold and dense spin polarized quark matter with corrections due to correlation energy $(E_{corr})$. Expressions for $E_{corr}$ both in the non-relativistic and ultra-relativistic regimes have been derived and compared with the exchange and kinetic term present in the perturbation series. It is observed that the inclusion of correlation energy does not rule out the possibility of the ferromagnetic phase transition at low density within the model proposed by Tatsumi\cite{tatsumi00}. We also derive the spin stiffness constant in the high density limit of such a spin polarized matter.

## Key claim

The inclusion of correlation energy (beyond the Hartree-Fock level) does not rule out the possibility of a ferromagnetic phase transition in cold dense quark matter at low density within the one-gluon-exchange model of Tatsumi (2000); rather, it makes the transition more probable, although the correlation contribution to the total energy is numerically not large.

## Method

The authors calculate the gluon self-energy in spin-polarized quark matter with arbitrary spin polarization, then use it to construct the in-medium one-loop corrected gluon propagator. The correlation energy is obtained by summing the series of ring diagrams (beyond the exchange loop) in the long-wavelength limit, performing a Wick rotation and evaluating the resulting integrals analytically in the non-relativistic and ultra-relativistic limits and numerically for intermediate regimes. The ground state energy is computed as the sum of kinetic, exchange, and correlation energy densities for one-flavor quark matter interacting via one-gluon exchange.

## Key numbers

- The critical density for the ferromagnetic phase transition is found to be around \(n_q^c \sim 0.16\ \mathrm{fm^{-3}}\) (normal nuclear matter density).
- The coupling constant used is \(\alpha_c = g^2/(4\pi) = 2.2\) and the quark mass is \(m_q = 300\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- In the non-relativistic limit, the correlation energy is \(E_{corr}^{nr} = \frac{g^4 \ln g^2}{(2\pi)^6} (1-\ln 2) \frac{1}{3} m_q p_f^3\) and is independent of the spin polarization parameter \(\xi\).
- In the ultra-relativistic limit, the total correlation energy is \(E_{corr}^{ur} = \frac{g^4 \ln g^2}{2048 \pi^6} p_f^4 [(1+\xi)^{4/3}+(1-\xi)^{4/3}+2(1-\xi^2)^{2/3}]\).
- The spin stiffness constant in the high density limit is \(\beta_s = \frac{p_f^4}{3\pi^2} \left[1 - \frac{g^2}{6\pi^2} - \frac{g^4}{384\pi^4} (\ln r_s - 0.286)\right]\).

## Context

This work extends the Hartree-Fock calculations of Tatsumi (2000) and Pal et al. (2009) for spin-polarized quark matter by including correlation energy via ring diagrams, analogous to the Gell-Mann and Brueckner (1957) treatment of the degenerate electron gas. The results enable more complete calculations of Fermi liquid parameters and spin susceptibility in dense quark matter, and provide a foundation for studying multi-flavor systems relevant to neutron star and quark star astrophysics.
