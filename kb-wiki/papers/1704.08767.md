# Inclusive Breakup Theory of Three-Body Halos

> We present a recently developed theory for the inclusive breakup of three-fragment projectiles within a four-body spectator model \cite{CarPLB2017}, for the treatment of the elastic and inclusive non-elastic break up reactions involving weakly bound three-cluster nuclei in $A\,(a,b)\,X$ / $a = x_1 + x_2 + b$ collisions. The four-body theory is an extension of the three-body approaches developed in the 80's by Ichimura, Autern and Vincent (IAV) \cite{IAV1985}, Udagawa and Tamura (UT) \cite{UT1981} and Hussein and McVoy (HM) \cite{HM1985}. We expect that experimentalists shall be encouraged to search for more information about the $x_{1} + x_{2}$ system in the elastic breakup cross section and that also further developments and extensions of the surrogate method will be pursued, based on the inclusive non-elastic breakup part of the $b$ spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper presents a four-body theory for inclusive breakup of three-fragment projectiles (such as Borromean or two-nucleon halo nuclei) that extends the three-body approaches of Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV), Udagawa-Tamura (UT), and Hussein-McVoy (HM) from the 1980s. The authors expect that experimentalists will be encouraged to search for information about the x1 + x2 system in the elastic breakup cross section, and that further developments of the surrogate method will be pursued based on the inclusive non-elastic breakup part of the b spectrum.

## Method

The authors derive the inclusive breakup cross section for the reaction A(a,b)X where a = x1 + x2 + b, starting from a four-body Hamiltonian in the spectator approximation. They separate the inclusive breakup cross section into an elastic breakup term (where both x1 and x2 emerge) and an inclusive non-elastic breakup term (where x1 and/or x2 are absorbed), with the latter containing single-fragment absorption cross sections and a new three-body absorption term W3B. The four-body source function is expressed as a sum of a Hussein-McVoy (HM) term and an Udagawa-Tamura (UT) term, generalizing the three-body Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) formula.

## Key numbers

- The inclusive breakup cross section is given by d2σb/dEbdΩb = d2σEBb/dEbdΩb + d2σINEBb/dEbdΩb, where the elastic breakup term involves an integral over kx1 and kx2 momenta with a delta function δ(E - Eb - E(kx1, kx2))
- The inclusive non-elastic breakup cross section is expressed as d2σINEBb/dEbdΩb = (2/ℏva)ρb(Eb) ⟨ρ̂x1,x2|Wx1 + Wx2 + W3B|ρ̂x1,x2⟩
- The inelastic breakup cross section is written as a sum of three terms: (k_a/E_a)[(E_x1/k_x1)σR^x1 + (E_x2/k_x2)σR^x2 + (E_CM(x1,x2)/(k_x1+k_x2))σR^3B]
- The single fragment inclusive cross sections are σR^x1 = (k_x1/E_x1)⟨ρ̂x1,x2|Wx1|ρ̂x1,x2⟩ and σR^x2 = (k_x2/E_x2)⟨ρ̂x1,x2|Wx2|ρ̂x1,x2⟩
- The double fragment inclusive cross section is σR^3B = ((k_x1+k_x2)/E_CM(x1,x2))⟨ρ̂x1,x2|W3B|ρ̂x1,x2⟩

## Context

This work extends the three-body inclusive breakup theories developed in the 1980s by Ichimura, Austern and Vincent (IAV), Udagawa and Tamura (UT), and Hussein and McVoy (HM) to the four-body case for three-fragment projectiles. The theory can be applied to reactions with weakly bound Borromean and two-nucleon halo nuclei, where fingerprints of Efimov physics and universality could be revealed through long-range correlations between the three fragments. The authors note that the four-body CDCC calculations by Descouvemont and collaborators (2015) for 9Be + 208Pb could not obtain partial or incomplete fusion cross sections, which the present theory addresses, and that the work enables extensions of the surrogate method to reactions like (t,d) and (3He,d) and the Trojan Horse Method to three-fragment projectiles.
