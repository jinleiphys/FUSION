# $γ^\ast N \to N^\ast(1520)$ form factors in the timelike regime

> The covariant spectator quark model, tested before in a variety of electromagnetic baryon excitations, is applied here to the $γ^\ast N \to N^\ast(1520)$ reaction in the timelike regime. The transition form factors are first parametrized in the spacelike region in terms of a valence quark core model together with a parametrization of the meson cloud contribution. The form factor behavior in the timelike region is then predicted, as well as the $N^\ast(1520) \to γN$ decay width and the $N^\ast (1520)$ Dalitz decay, $N^\ast (1520) \to e^+ e^- N$. Our results may help in the interpretation of dielectron production from elementary $pp$ collisions and from the new generation of HADES results using a pion beam. In the $q^2=0$--1 GeV$^2$ range we conclude that the {\it QED approximation} (a $q^2$ independent form factor model) underestimates the electromagnetic coupling of the $N^\ast(1520)$ from 1 up to 2 orders of magnitude. We conclude also that the $N^\ast (1520)$ and the $Δ(1232)$ Dalitz decay widths are comparable.

## Key claim

The covariant spectator quark model predicts that the meson cloud contributions dominate the $\gamma^\ast N \to N^\ast(1520)$ transition form factors in the timelike regime, and that the constant form factor (QED approximation) underestimates the electromagnetic coupling of the $N^\ast(1520)$ by 1 to 2 orders of magnitude in the $q^2 = 0$–1 GeV$^2$ range.

## Method

The authors first parametrize the transition form factors in the spacelike region using a valence quark core model combined with a meson cloud contribution, where the meson cloud is modeled via the pion electromagnetic form factor. They then extend the model to the timelike region by introducing finite mass widths for the $\rho$ and $\omega$ mesons in the quark form factors, and calculate the form factor behavior, the $N^\ast(1520) \to \gamma N$ decay width, and the Dalitz decay $N^\ast(1520) \to e^+ e^- N$.

## Key numbers

- The QED approximation underestimates the electromagnetic coupling of the $N^\ast(1520)$ by 1 up to 2 orders of magnitude in the $q^2 = 0$–1 GeV$^2$ range.
- The $\omega$ meson decay width at the $\omega$ mass point is $\Gamma_\omega (m_\omega^2) \simeq 7.6$ MeV, consistent with the data (total $\omega$ width 8.4 MeV).
- The $\rho$ meson decay width parameter is $\Gamma_{\rho}^0 = 0.149$ GeV.
- The meson cloud parametrization uses cutoff parameters $\Lambda_M^2 = 1.241$ GeV$^2$ and $\Lambda_C^2 = 1.263$ GeV$^2$.
- The $N^\ast(1520)$ and $\Delta(1232)$ Dalitz decay widths are comparable.

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator quark model, previously applied to the $\Delta(1232)$ resonance in the timelike regime (Ramalho and Pena, 2012), to the $N^\ast(1520)$ resonance. It is motivated by HADES experiments using a pion beam that show a large peak in dielectron events attributed to $N^\ast(1520)$ decay, and by the need to improve on the QED approximation commonly used in the literature. The results enable better interpretation of dielectron production from $pp$ collisions and HADES pion-beam data, and may help disentangle the contributions of low-lying resonances in dilepton decay reactions.
