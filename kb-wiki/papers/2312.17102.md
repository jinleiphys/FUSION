# Two-flavor color superconducting quark stars may not exist

> Large uncertainties in the determinations of the equation of state of dense stellar matter allow the intriguing possibility that the bulk quark matter in beta equilibrium might be the true ground state of the matter at zero pressure. And quarks will form Cooper pairs very readily since the dominant interaction between quarks is attractive in some channels. As a result, quark matter will generically exhibit color superconductivity, with the favored pairing pattern at intermediately high densities being two-flavor pairing. In the light of several possible candidates for such self-bound quark stars, including the very low-mass central compact object in supernova remnant HESS J1731-347 reported recently, we carry out one field-theoretic model, the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, of investigation on the stability of beta-stable two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase of quark matter, nevertheless find no physically-allowed parameter space for the existence of 2SC quark stars.

## Key claim

Using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with vector and scalar diquark interactions, the authors find no physically-allowed parameter space where two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) quark matter has an energy per baryon below 930 MeV, meaning 2SC quark stars cannot be absolutely stable under the Bodmer-Witten hypothesis, despite the abstract's suggestion that such objects are an intriguing possibility.

## Method

The authors employed a two-flavor NJL-type model incorporating quark-antiquark scalar and vector interactions plus scalar diquark interactions, solved under mean-field approximation with electric and color charge neutrality conditions. They treated the vacuum bag constant B, the vector coupling ratio R_V = G_V/G_S, and the diquark coupling ratio R_D = G_D/G_S as free parameters, solving the coupled gap equations for the constituent quark mass M, diquark gap Δ, and chemical potentials to compute the equation of state and energy per baryon of 2SC quark matter.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum constituent quark mass is M = 313 MeV at zero chemical potential
- In the charged 2SC phase with R_V=0, R_D=0.75, the diquark gap Δ is around 50 MeV, significantly smaller than the uncharged case (∼100 MeV)
- The electron chemical potential μ_e extends to ∼150 MeV in the charged 2SC phase
- The critical baryon chemical potential where quark matter pressure exceeds neutron star matter pressure is μ_B ∼ 1.1 GeV
- The stability criterion requires energy per baryon E/A < 930 MeV (the value for ⁵⁶Fe)
- For B^{1/4} ≲ 60 MeV, the minimum energy per baryon remains relatively constant
- The momentum cutoff is Λ = 0.6533 GeV and the scalar coupling constant G_S = 5.0163 GeV⁻²

## Context

This work extends the Bodmer-Witten hypothesis (Bodmer 1971, Witten 1984) for self-bound quark matter to the 2SC phase, motivated by the ultra-low-mass compact object in HESS J1731-347 (Doroshenko 2022). Previous studies examined 2SC quark matter properties (Alford 2001, 2008) but did not test absolute stability against ⁵⁶Fe. The authors contrast their NJL results with MIT bag model calculations (Alford 2002, Zhang 2021) and note that Alford (2002) already demonstrated the absence of a 2SC phase in three-flavor bag model quark stars. The work enables future extensions incorporating gluon effects (Rischke 2000, Ipp 2006) and finite temperature (Pisarski 2000, He 2007).
