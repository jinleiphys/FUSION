# Connections and performances of Green's function methods for charged and neutral excitations

> In recent years, Green's function methods have garnered considerable interest due to their ability to target both charged and neutral excitations. Among them, the well-established $GW$ approximation provides accurate ionization potentials and electron affinities and can be extended to neutral excitations using the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) formalism. Here, we investigate the connections between various Green's function methods and evaluate their performance for charged and neutral excitations. Comparisons with other widely-known second-order wave function methods are also reported. Additionally, we calculate the singlet-triplet gap of cycl[3,3,3]azine, a model molecular emitter for thermally activated delayed fluorescence, which has the particularity of having an inverted gap thanks to a substantial contribution from the double excitations. We demonstrate that, within the $GW$ approximation, a second-order BSE kernel with dynamical correction is required to predict this distinctive characteristic.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that, within the GW approximation, a second-order Bethe-Salpeter equation kernel with dynamical correction is required to predict the inverted singlet-triplet gap of cycl[3,3,3]azine, a molecular emitter for thermally activated delayed fluorescence, where the abstract's claim of requiring this specific treatment is conditioned on the substantial contribution from double excitations to the gap.

## Method
The authors systematically compare three Green's function self-energy approximations (GF2, GW, and T-matrix) for charged excitations, and four corresponding BSE kernels (static and dynamical) for neutral excitations, using one-shot schemes with a Hartree-Fock starting point. They employ an upfolding framework that transforms the nonlinear quasiparticle equation into a linear eigenvalue problem, and apply perturbative dynamical corrections to the static BSE kernels. Calculations are performed on atoms and molecules from the GW100 dataset for charged excitations, and on a separate set of molecules for neutral excitations including valence and Rydberg states.

## Key numbers
- Principal ionization potentials are calculated for a subset of atoms and molecules from the GW100 dataset, with comparisons between GF2, GW, and T-matrix self-energies
- The singlet-triplet gap of cycl[3,3,3]azine is computed at various levels of theory, where the inverted gap requires a second-order BSE kernel with dynamical correction within the GW approximation
- Renormalization factors Z_p for quasiparticle solutions are reported, with values between 0 and 1
- Dynamical correction renormalization factors zeta_m are found to be close to unity in most cases, indicating satisfactory convergence of the perturbative series

## Context
This work extends the systematic comparison of Green's function methods for both charged and neutral excitations, building on prior studies of GW approximations by Hybertsen (1985, 1986), Godby (1986-1988), and Blase (1995, 2018, 2020), as well as GF2 developments by Casida (1989, 1991) and Ortiz (2013), and T-matrix work by Romaniello (2012) and Zhang (2017). The dynamical BSE correction follows the approach of Loos and Blase (2020) and the upfolding framework of Bintrim and Berkelbach (2021) and Backhouse and Booth (2020). The paper enables future work on understanding the role of double excitations in molecular emitters for thermally activated delayed fluorescence, and provides a unified framework connecting different Green's function approximations through the upfolding formalism.
