# Valence Neutron-Proton Orientation in Atomic Nuclei

> It is shown that the renormalized nuclear deformations in different mass regions can be globally scaled by two probability partition factors of Boltzmann-like distribution, which are derived from the competing valence $np$ and like-nucleon interactions. The partition factors are simply related to the probabilities of anti-parallel and fully-aligned orientations of the angular momenta of the neutrons and protons in the valence $np$ pairs, responsible for spherical- and deformed-shape phases, respectively. The partition factors derived from the renormalized deformations are also present in the new scaling law for the energies of the first $2^+$ states. A striking concordance between the distributions of the renormalized deformations and of the newly introduced parameter for the energies of the first $2^+$ states over the extended mass region from Ge to Cf is achieved, giving strong support to the existence of two phases: anti-aligned and fully-aligned subsets of $np$ pairs.

## Key claim

The renormalized nuclear deformations across a wide mass region (Ge to Cf) can be globally scaled by a Boltzmann-like distribution with two probability partition factors, which are interpreted as the probabilities of anti-parallel and fully-aligned orientations of neutron and proton angular momenta in valence np pairs. The authors claim this scaling holds around newly defined inflection points P_c that vary by mass region, not as a universal constant.

## Method

The authors compiled experimental quadrupole deformation parameters β and first 2+ excitation energies from the literature for even-even nuclei from Ge to Cf. They introduced a renormalized deformation βA^(1/3) and plotted it against the Casten P factor (P = N_n N_p/(N_n+N_p)), using region-dependent critical P_c values to align the data. A hyperbolic tangent function with a single fitted parameter λ = 0.3135 was used to fit the universal scaling curve, and the same P_c values were applied to a new energy ratio parameter O = (R-1)/(R_s-1) where R is the ratio of the first 2+ energy of a single closed-shell nucleus to that of a given isotope.

## Key numbers

- The fitted parameter λ in the scaling law βA^(1/3) ≈ 1 + tanh[λ(P-P_c)] is λ = 0.3135.
- The renormalized deformation βA^(1/3) ranges from approximately 0 to approximately 2 across the full dataset.
- Critical P_c values for different mass regions: Po-Cf 3.53, Dy-Hg 4.6, Te-Gd (N>82) 3.6, Te-Gd (N<82) 4.45, Kr-Cd (N>50) 4.6, Ge-Zr (N<50) 4.75.
- Saturated energy ratios R_s for the same regions: Po-Cf 32.78, Dy-Hg 16.00, Te-Gd (N>82) 17.16, Te-Gd (N<82) 12.94, Kr-Cd (N>50) 12.70, Ge-Zr (N<50) 10.86.
- For sd shells, the total number of available np parallel interactions is 63, while anti-parallel interactions number 25.

## Context

This work extends the Casten P-factor systematics (Casten 1985, 1987) and the concept of Ising-like chains of np pairs introduced by Danos and Gillet (1966, 1967) for N=Z nuclei to N≠Z cases. It provides a unified scaling of nuclear deformations and first 2+ energies across mass regions from Ge to Cf, which had previously been treated with regional systematics (Raman 1988, Pritychenko 2016). The results enable a global description of shape phase transitions in finite nuclei and suggest the existence of two coexisting phases (anti-aligned and fully-aligned np pairs) with a Boltzmann-like probability distribution.
