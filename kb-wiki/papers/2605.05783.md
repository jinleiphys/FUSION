# Isomer depletion via nuclear excitation by inelastic electron scattering

> Isomer depletion via the process of nuclear excitation by inelastic electron scattering is investigated theoretically. A comprehensive study on low-energy nuclear excitations by inelastic electron scattering is performed to analyze the impact of the nuclear and ion charge, the nuclear transition energy, and the nuclear transition multipolarity on the cross section of the process. We apply the analysis to the case of isomer depletion, in which an excitation from the isomeric state to a nuclear level above the isomeric state can lead to decay to a nuclear level below the isomer itself and hence lead to the release of the energy stored in the isomer. For this purpose, the isomer depletion of $\mathrm{{}^{93m}{Mo}}$, $\mathrm{{}^{152m}{Eu}}$, and $\mathrm{{}^{178m}{Hf}}$, which represent the most important scenarios of isomer depletion, are studied. Our results demonstrate the capability of the process of nuclear excitation by inelastic electron scattering for isomer depletion.

## Key claim

The paper theoretically demonstrates that nuclear excitation by inelastic electron scattering (NEIES) is a viable mechanism for isomer depletion, but the cross sections are strongly dependent on the nuclear transition multipolarity and energy, with the process being most effective for low-multipolarity transitions such as E2 and M1.

## Method

The authors employed the Dirac distorted wave Born approximation to calculate NEIES cross sections for low-energy nuclear excitations, using the RADIAL code with Dirac-Hartree-Fock-Slater method and Fermi charge distribution to compute relativistic continuum electron wave functions. They first analyzed general features of NEIES for hypothetical nuclei with atomic numbers Z=20, 50, and 100 across various transition multipolarities (E2, E3, E4, M1, M2, M3) and transition energies (1-50 keV), then applied the formalism to three specific isomer depletion cases: 93mMo, 152mEu, and 178mHf.

## Key numbers

- For 93mMo (isomer energy 2424.95 keV, half-life 6.85 h), the NEIES cross section for the E2 transition from isomeric state to gateway state is on the order of 10^-33 cm^2 to 10^-32 cm^2 for electron energies up to 500 keV, with a minimum near 80 keV incident electron energy
- For 152mEu (isomer energy 45.5998 keV, half-life 9.3116 h), the NEIES cross section for the M1 transition is on the order of 10^-33 cm^2 to 10^-32 cm^2, using B(M1)=7.9x10^-5 W.u. for the gateway-to-isomer transition
- For 178mHf (isomer energy 2446.09 keV, half-life 31 years), the NEIES cross section for the M2 transition is on the order of 10^-37 cm^2 to 10^-36 cm^2, using B(M2)=0.0142 W.u. for the gateway-to-isomer transition
- For general E2 transitions with 1 W.u. reduced transition probability, NEIES cross sections range from 10^-35 cm^2 to 10^-33 cm^2 for Z=20, 10^-34 cm^2 to 10^-32 cm^2 for Z=50, and 10^-32 cm^2 to 10^-30 cm^2 for Z=100
- The NEIES cross section for M1 transitions (1 W.u.) spans from 10^-30 cm^2 to 10^-28 cm^2 for Z=20, about 5-7 orders of magnitude larger than M2 and 10-14 orders larger than M3 transitions

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of NEIES for low electron energies, which had received limited attention compared to high-energy electron cases (PhysRevC.106.044604, 2022). The analysis contrasts NEIES with other electron-induced nuclear excitation mechanisms such as NEET and NEEC, noting that NEIES has a much weaker dependence on ionic charge states than those processes. The results enable quantitative assessment of NEIES as a tool for controlled energy release from nuclear isomers, particularly for the three representative cases (93mMo, 152mEu, 178mHf) that cover the most important isomer depletion scenarios.
