# Toward a unified equation of state for multi-messenger astronomy

> We present a first step in developing a benchmark equation-of-state (EoS) model for multi-messenger astronomy that unifies the thermodynamics of quark and hadronic degrees of freedom. A Lagrangian approach to the thermodynamic potential of quark-meson-nucleon (QMN) matter was used. In this approach, dynamical chiral-symmetry breaking is described by the scalar mean-field dynamics coupled to quarks and nucleons and their chiral partners, whereby its restoration occurs in the hadronic phase by parity doubling, as well as in the quark phase. Quark confinement was achieved by an auxiliary scalar field that parametrizes a dynamical infrared cutoff in the quark sector, serving as an ultraviolet cutoff for the nucleonic phase space. The gap equations were solved for the isospin-symmetric case, as well as for neutron star (NS) conditions. We also calculated the mass-radius (MR) relation of NSs and their tidal deformability parameter. The obtained EoS is in accordance with nuclear matter properties at saturation density and with the flow constraint from heavy ion collision experiments. For isospin-asymmetric matter, a sequential occurrence of light quark flavors is obtained, allowing for a mixed phase of chirally-symmetric nucleonic matter with deconfined down quarks. The MR relations and TDs for compact stars fulfill the constraints from the latest astrophysical observations for PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0030+0451, and the NS merger GW170817, whereby the tension between the maximum mass and compactness constraints rather uniquely fixes the model parameters. The model predicts the existence of stars with a core of chirally restored but purely hadronic (confined) matter for masses beyond $1.8~M_\odot$. Stars with pure-quark matter cores are found to be unstable against the gravitational collapse. This instability is shifted to even higher densities if repulsive interactions between quarks are included.

# Toward a unified equation of state for multi-messenger astronomy

## Key claim
The hybrid quark-meson-nucleon (QMN) model can simultaneously satisfy constraints from nuclear saturation properties, heavy-ion collision flow data, and astrophysical observations (PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0030+0451, GW170817) only when the tension between maximum mass and compactness rather uniquely fixes the model parameters; the model predicts stars with chirally restored but purely hadronic (confined) cores above 1.8 solar masses, while stars with pure-quark matter cores are unstable against gravitational collapse.

## Method
The authors constructed a Lagrangian-based thermodynamic potential for quark-meson-nucleon (QMN) matter that couples baryonic parity doublet states (positive-parity nucleons and their negative-parity partners) and quark degrees of freedom to scalar (sigma, pi), isoscalar-vector (omega), and isovector-vector (rho) meson fields. Dynamical chiral symmetry breaking is described by scalar mean-field dynamics, with chiral restoration occurring in the hadronic phase via parity doubling and in the quark phase via vanishing quark mass. Quark confinement is implemented through an auxiliary scalar field (b-field) that acts as a dynamical infrared cutoff for quarks and an ultraviolet cutoff for nucleons in the Fermi-Dirac distribution functions. The gap equations were solved for both isospin-symmetric matter and neutron star conditions (beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality), and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were integrated to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities.

## Context
This work extends the hybrid QMN model previously developed by Benic (2015), Marczenko (2017, 2018, 2019) by including isoscalar-vector and isovector-vector couplings to quarks, which were absent in earlier versions that could not satisfy the two-solar-mass constraint. The approach contrasts with two-phase models that assume a priori first-order hadron-quark transitions and with Polyakov loop-based models (such as PNJL and PQM models by Fukushima 2003, Ratti 2005, Schaefer 2007) that lack hadronic degrees of freedom at low temperature and density. The model enables future applications in dynamical simulations of neutron star mergers and supernova collapse, where the sequential chiral and deconfinement phase transitions could produce observable signatures in neutrino emission and gravitational wave signals.
