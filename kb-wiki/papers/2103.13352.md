# Pions and Contacts at N4LO: Some details on the chiral nuclear force

> In this work we have performed a detailed study of chiral nuclear forces at N4LO approximation applied to selected channels of the neutron-proton ($n p$) scattering. The idea is to analyse the different contributions to the nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) potential by separating the part coming from the exchange of pions and the one coming from the contact interactions. We consider two state-of-the-art chiral interactions at N4LO which are constructed using different regularization procedures: the non-local Idaho-Salamanca force and the semi-local interaction from the Bochum group. In order to compare the two types of regularization we consider both interactions with a 500 MeV cutoff and to analyse the cutoff dependence we select the Bochum potential with three different cutoff values: 500, 450 and 400 MeV. Our results show that the balance between pion exchanges and contact interactions depends strongly on the regularization procedure. The non-local angle-independent regularization of both components of the interaction implemented in the Idaho-Salamanca potential make the contact terms to be present at large distances while the local regularization of the pion exchanges in the Bochum potential restricts the contact interactions to small distances. Also, the value of the cutoff affects the strength of the potential but the interplay between pion exchanges and contact terms remains qualitatively the same.

## Key claim

The balance between pion exchanges and contact interactions in N4LO chiral nuclear forces depends strongly on the regularization procedure, with the non-local Idaho-Salamanca potential producing contact terms that extend to large distances while the semi-local Bochum potential restricts contact interactions to short distances; the cutoff value affects the strength of the potential but the qualitative interplay between pions and contacts remains similar when the cutoff is changed.

## Method

The authors separated the N4LO+ chiral potentials into contributions from pion exchanges (summed from LO through N4LO) and contact interactions (summed from LO through N5LO, with N5LO contacts only in F-waves) for selected neutron-proton scattering channels. They performed a two-dimensional Fourier-Bessel transform of the momentum-space interactions to obtain configuration-space potentials, then compared the diagonal and off-diagonal matrix elements and the resulting phase shifts for the non-local Idaho-Salamanca force (smooth cutoff at 500 MeV) and the semi-local Bochum potential (cutoffs of 500, 450, and 400 MeV).

## Key numbers

- The Bochum potential required a momentum cutoff of 80 GeV (400 fm^-1) for the Fourier-Bessel transform to converge and remove oscillations in the configuration-space matrix elements.
- The Idaho-Salamanca momentum-space potential vanishes at relatively small momenta (approximately 0.8 GeV), while the Bochum semi-local potential extends to very high momenta (approximately 8 GeV).
- In the ^3D_1 channel for the Idaho-Salamanca force, pion exchanges alone describe the phase shifts up to 50 MeV, with contact interactions required at higher energies.
- In the ^1F_3 channel, pion exchanges describe the phase shifts up to 100 MeV, with contact interactions becoming important at higher energies.
- The Idaho-Salamanca potential with 500 MeV cutoff shows that for r, r' < 1 fm, the full interaction results from strong attraction from pion exchanges balanced by stronger repulsion from contact interactions.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of N4LO chiral potentials first developed by Entem, Machleidt and Nosik (2015) and Epelbaum, Krebs and Meissner (2015), specifically analyzing the Idaho-Salamanca force (Entem, Machleidt, and Nosik, 2017) and the semi-local Bochum interaction (Epelbaum, Krebs, and Meissner, 2018). The study reveals that the different regularization procedures produce fundamentally different structures in configuration space, which is often overlooked when these interactions are used as inputs for nuclear structure and nuclear matter calculations. The results enable a better understanding of how the choice of regularization affects the decomposition of the nuclear force into its pion-exchange and contact components, which is relevant for future chiral potential development and for interpreting the role of short-range dynamics in higher partial waves such as F-waves.
