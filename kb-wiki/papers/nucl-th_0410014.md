# A Padé-aided analysis of nonperturbative $NN$ scattering in $^1S_0$ channel

> We carried out a Padé approximant analysis on a compact factor of the $T$-matrix for $NN$ scattering to explore the nonperturbative renormalization prescription in a universal manner. The utilities and virtues for this Padé analysis were discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a Padé approximant analysis of the compact factor $\mathcal{G}$ in the $T$-matrix provides a universal parametrization of nonperturbative renormalization prescription dependence for $NN$ scattering, without needing to specify a particular regularization or subtraction scheme. The authors claim that this approach disentangles the implementation of EFT power counting rules from the nonperturbative renormalization procedure, but only after the Padé parameters are fixed through fitting to low-energy phase shift data.

## Method

The authors parametrize the nonperturbative factor $\mathcal{G}(p)$ in the on-shell $T$-matrix relation $T^{-1}(p) = V^{-1}(p) - \mathcal{G}(p)$ using Padé approximants of varying order (constant, linear in $p^2$, and a ratio of linear polynomials in $p^2$). They employ chiral potentials at leading order (Lo), next-to-leading order (Nlo), and next-to-next-to-leading order (Nnlo) from Epelbaum, Glöckle, and Meißner (2000, 2002, 2004), then fit the Padé parameters to reproduce $^1S_0$ channel phase shifts in specified low-energy fitting windows. The phase shifts are predicted from the resulting parametrized $T$-matrix without performing explicit loop integrations or 'endogenous' subtractions.

## Key numbers

- Fitting windows for Padé parameters: Lo at $T_{\text{lab}} \in (0, 3)$ MeV; Nlo at $T_{\text{lab}} \in (0.2, 13)$ MeV; Nnlo at $T_{\text{lab}} \in (3, 23)$ MeV.
- At leading order with the Padé approximant $\text{Re}(\mathcal{G}) \approx (1 + \frac{\nu_1}{\nu_0}p^2)/(\frac{\delta_0}{\nu_0} + \frac{\delta_1}{\nu_0}p^2)$, the predicted $^1S_0$ phase shifts show surprisingly good agreement with partial-wave analysis data, especially at higher energies, despite using only a leading-order potential ($V_{1\pi}$ plus contact term $C_0$).
- At Nnlo, the results show no obvious differences compared to the predictions of Epelbaum, Glöckle, and Meißner (2000, 2002, 2004), indicating that higher-order interactions lessen the nonperturbative renormalization prescription dependence.

## Context

This work extends the compact parametrization of the $T$-matrix proposed by Yang (2004) and the demonstration of 'endogenous' renormalization for contact potentials by Yang and Huang (2005). It provides a prescription-independent framework to test the applicability of chiral effective field theory to nuclear forces, in contrast to most prior approaches (e.g., Epelbaum, Glöckle, and Meißner (2000, 2002, 2004); Beane, Bedaque, Savage, and van Kolck (2002)) where a specific regularization and renormalization scheme must be specified. The analysis enables systematic exploration of nonperturbative prescription dependence and suggests a method for determining EFT couplings in a more prescription-independent manner through combined fitting of couplings and Padé parameters.
