# Pion and kaon form factors using twisted-mass fermions

> We present a calculation of the scalar, vector and tensor pion and kaon form factors using one ensemble of two degenerate light, a strange and a charm quark ($N_f=2+1+1$) of maximally twisted mass fermions with clover improvement. The quark masses are chosen so that they produce a pion mass of about 265 MeV, and a kaon mass of 530 MeV. The lattice spacing of the ensemble is 0.093 fm and the lattice has a spatial extent of 3 fm. We use a rest frame, as well as a boosted frame to obtain the form factors for a wider and denser set of four-vector momentum transfer squared, $Q^2$. To assess and eliminate excited-states contamination, we analyze several values of the source-sink time separation within the range of 1.12 - 2.23 fm (1.12 - 1.67 fm) for the rest (boosted) frame. The $Q^2$ dependence of the form factors is parametrized using a monopole fit, which leads to the extraction of the corresponding radius, and the tensor anomalous magnetic moment for the tensor form factor. The results for these parametrizations are compared for the pion and kaon to assess the level of the SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking.

## Key claim

The paper presents a lattice QCD calculation of scalar, vector, and tensor form factors for the pion and kaon using a single ensemble of twisted-mass fermions at a pion mass of about 265 MeV (not at the physical pion mass), and the results for radii are therefore not directly comparable to experimental values.

## Method

The authors use one ensemble of \(N_f=2+1+1\) maximally twisted mass clover-improved fermions with lattice spacing 0.093 fm and spatial extent 3 fm, producing a pion mass of 265 MeV and kaon mass of 530 MeV. They extract form factors from three-point correlation functions in both a rest frame and a boosted frame (with momentum boost \(\mathbf{p'}=2\pi/L(\pm1,\pm1,\pm1)\)), using source-sink time separations from 1.12 to 2.23 fm (rest frame) and 1.12 to 1.67 fm (boosted frame). Excited-state contamination is assessed via plateau fits and two-state fits, and the \(Q^2\) dependence is parametrized with a monopole (vector meson dominance) ansatz to extract radii and the tensor anomalous magnetic moment.

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD calculations of meson form factors to include scalar and tensor operators alongside the vector, using a modern twisted-mass fermion ensemble with dynamical strange and charm quarks. The results are compared to prior lattice calculations by Gulpers et al. (2013) using \(N_f=2\) Wilson fermions at 280 MeV pion mass and by Kaneko et al. (2010) using \(N_f=2+1\) overlap fermions at 310 MeV pion mass, finding compatible radii. The study enables future comparisons with chiral perturbation theory predictions (Gasser 1990, Bijnens 1998) and provides input for beyond-Standard-Model phenomenology, while also quantifying SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking effects up to 20% in the form factor ratios.
