# A corresponding-state approach to quark-cluster matter

> The state of super-dense matter is essential for us to understand the nature of pulsars, but the non- perturbative quantum chromodynamics (QCD) makes it very difficult for direct calculations of the state of cold matter at realistic baryon number densities inside compact stars. Nevertheless, from an observational point of view, it is conjectured that pulsars could be made up of quark clusters since the strong coupling between quarks might render quarks grouped in clusters. We are trying an effort to find an equation of state of condensed quark-cluster matter in a phenomenological way. Supposing that the quark-clusters could be analogized to inert gases, we apply here the corresponding-state approach to derive the equation of state of quark-cluster matter, as was similarly demonstrated for nuclear and neutron-star matter in 1970s. According to the calculations presented, the quark-cluster stars, which are composed of quark-cluster matter, could then have high maximum mass that is consistent with observations and, in turn, further observations of pulsar mass would also put constraints to the properties of quark-cluster matter. Moreover, the melting heat during solid-liquid phase conversion and the related astrophysical consequences are also briefly discussed.

## Key claim

By analogizing quark-clusters to inert gas atoms and applying the corresponding-state law, the authors derive a phenomenological equation of state for quark-cluster matter that yields maximum stellar masses above 2 solar masses, but only under the assumption that the interaction between quark-clusters can be approximated by a Lennard-Jones potential with parameters constrained by surface density estimates and a quantum parameter \(\Lambda^* < 2\).

## Method

The authors assume quark-clusters interact via a Lennard-Jones 6-12 potential, analogous to inert gas atoms, and apply the law of corresponding states to map reduced thermodynamic quantities (\(P^*, V^*, T^*\)) from experimental data of noble gases to quark-cluster matter. They select four representative parameter sets (A–D) for cluster size \(N_q = 6\) or 18, potential depth \(\varepsilon\) (40–200 MeV), and length scale \(\sigma\) (1.5–2.5 fm), constrained by surface density \(\rho_s\) between 1 and 3 times nuclear matter density \(\rho_0\) and quantum parameter \(\Lambda^* < 2\). Using de Boer's experimental \(P^*\)-\(\Lambda^*\) curves at zero temperature and a fitted \(V^*_0\)-\(\Lambda^*\) relation, they derive pressure-density relations, then integrate to obtain internal energy and the full equation of state, which is input into the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation to compute mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

* Maximum mass of quark-cluster stars can be well above \(2M_\odot\) (exact value depends on parameter set; e.g., for case A with \(N_q=18\), \(\varepsilon=40\) MeV, \(\sigma=2.5\) fm, the derived pressure at \(n/n_0=2.7\) is \(P=1.0 \times 10^{35}\) dyn/cm\(^2\)).
* Surface density \(\rho_s\) for the four cases ranges from \(1.23\rho_0\) (case D) to \(2.72\rho_0\) (case B), where \(\rho_0\) is nuclear matter density.
* Quantum parameter \(\Lambda^*\) values for the four cases: 1.05 (A), 0.72 (B), 1.56 (C), 1.02 (D).
* Fitted relation for reduced volume at zero temperature and pressure: \(V^*_0 = 0.57 + 9.45 \times 10^{-5} (\Lambda^* + 6.35)^{4.44}\).
* Fitted relation for reduced internal energy at zero temperature and pressure: \(U^*_0 = -8.72 + 4.91\Lambda^* - 0.71\Lambda^{*2}\).

## Context

This work extends the corresponding-state approach previously applied to nuclear and neutron-star matter in the 1970s (e.g., Pandharipande and others, 1974; Canuto, 1975) to the hypothetical quark-cluster phase, motivated by the conjecture that strong coupling at densities \(\sim (2-10)\rho_0\) inside compact stars could cause quarks to cluster (Xu, 2003). It builds on earlier Lennard-Jones models for quark matter (Lai and Xu, 2009) and polytropic models for quark-cluster stars (Lai and Xu, 2009; Niu and Xu, 2011). The results enable predictions of mass-radius relations for quark-cluster stars that can be tested against pulsar mass observations (e.g., PSR J1614-2230 at \(2M_\odot\)), and the discussion of melting heat during solid-liquid phase conversion suggests a possible explanation for the plateau of gamma-ray bursts.
