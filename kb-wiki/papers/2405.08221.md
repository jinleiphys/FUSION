# Towards Quarkonium Fragmentation from NRQCD in a Variable-Flavor Number Scheme

> We address quarkonium formation at moderate to large transverse momenta, where the single-parton collinear fragmentation prevails over the short-distance emission, directly from the hard sub-scattering, of the constituent heavy-quark pair. We rely on Non-Relativistic-QCD (NRQCD) Next-to-Leading Order (NLO) calculations for all parton fragmentation channels to quarkonia, taken as proxies for initial-scale inputs. Preliminary sets of Variable-Flavor Number-Scheme (VFNS) fragmentation functions (FFs) are built via a DGLAP scheme that properly accounts for evolution thresholds. Statistical errors are assessed via a Monte Carlo (MC), replica-like approach aimed at catching Missing Higher-Order Uncertainties (MHOUs).

## Key claim

The paper presents preliminary results for new collinear fragmentation functions (FFs) for color-singlet vector and pseudoscalar quarkonia, named NRFF1.0, built from NRQCD NLO calculations for all parton fragmentation channels, but the results are preliminary and only two bottom-to-bottomonium channels are shown as examples.

## Method

The author develops a methodology called Heavy-flavor Non-Relativistic Evolution (HFNRevo) that uses NRQCD NLO calculations for all parton fragmentation channels to quarkonia as initial-scale inputs. The DGLAP evolution is split into two steps: an expanded and decoupled evolution (EDevo) that accounts for evolution thresholds, followed by a standard all-order evolution (AOevo). Statistical uncertainties from Missing Higher-Order Uncertainties (MHOUs) are assessed via a Monte Carlo replica-like approach.

## Key numbers

- Bottom-quark NLO FF to CS Upsilon mesons and eta_b mesons are shown for three values of the factorization scale mu_F (specific numerical values of the FFs are not given in the text, only shown in figures)
- The NRFF1.0 FFs will supersede the ZCW19+ and ZCFW22 sets currently used in phenomenological studies

## Context

This work extends the NRQCD fragmentation approach of Braaten (1993) and Zheng (2021) by building a consistent Variable-Flavor Number Scheme (VFNS) framework with proper DGLAP evolution thresholds. The NRFF1.0 FFs will supersede the ZCW19+ and ZCFW22 sets used in studies by Celiberto (2022, 2023) for vector quarkonia and B_c mesons. The results will guide quarkonium physics at the HL-LHC, EIC, and lepton colliders, and serve as a reference for future AI-based extractions.
