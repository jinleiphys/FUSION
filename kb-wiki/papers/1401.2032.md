# On Chiral Symmetry Breaking, Topology and Confinement

> We start with the relation between the chiral symmetry breaking and gauge field topology. New lattice result further enhance the notion of Zero Mode Zone, a very narrow strip of states with quasizero Dirac eigenvalues. Then we move to the issue of "origin of mass" and Brown-RHo scaling: a number of empirical facts contradicts to the idea that masses of quarks and such hadrons as $ρ,N$ decrease near $T_c$. We argue that while at $T=0$ the main contribution to the effective quark mass is chirally odd $m_{\snchi}$, near $T_c$ it rotates to chirally-even component $m_χ$, because "infinite clusters" of topological solitons gets split into finite ones. Recent progress in understanding of topology require introduction of nonzero holonomy $<A_0>\neq 0$, which splits instantons into $N_c$ (anti)selfdual "instanton-dyons". Qualitative progress, as well as first numerical studios of the dyon ensemble are reported. New connections between chiral symmetry breaking and confinement are recently understood, since instanton-dyons generates holonomy potential with a minimum at confining value, if the ensemble is dense enough.

## Key claim

The paper argues that near the chiral restoration temperature \(T_c\), the effective quark mass does not decrease as predicted by Brown-Rho scaling; instead, the chirally-odd component \(m_{\snchi}\) partially rotates into a chirally-even component \(m_\chi\), so the total mass \(M = \sqrt{m_{\snchi}^2 + m_\chi^2}\) may remain roughly constant, contradicting the expectation that quarks and hadrons become lighter.

## Method

The author reviews lattice results on the density of Dirac eigenvalues, the Zero Mode Zone (ZMZ) from the instanton liquid model, and the splitting of instantons into \(N_c\) instanton-dyons under nonzero holonomy \(\langle A_0 \rangle \neq 0\). He then discusses first numerical simulations of the instanton-dyon ensemble, which calculate Dirac eigenvalue spectra and the conditions for chiral symmetry breaking, and shows that dyon-antidyon repulsion can generate a holonomy potential with a minimum at the confining value.

## Key numbers

- The width of the Zero Mode Zone is parametrically \(\Delta \lambda \sim \rho^2 / R^3 \sim 20\ \mathrm{MeV}\), based on mean instanton size \(\rho \sim 1/3\ \mathrm{fm}\) and inter-instanton distance \(R \sim 1\ \mathrm{fm}\).
- Removing only a tiny fraction \(\sim 10^{-4}\) of all quark states (the near-zero strip) from propagators causes chiral pairs such as \((A_1, \rho)\) and \((N^*(1/2^-), N)\) to become near-degenerate.
- The instanton-induced t' Hooft vertex provides about \(300\ \mathrm{MeV}\) of nucleon binding in the ud scalar diquark channel.
- Lattice studies find no trace of chiral symmetry breaking for \(N_f \sim 8\) or more flavors.

## Context

This work extends the instanton liquid model (Shuryak 1981) and the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis (Brown and Rho 1991) by introducing the chirally-even mass component to resolve contradictions with empirical data: chemical freezeout in heavy ion collisions shows no mass shifts, NA60 dilepton data reveal the \(\rho\) peak hardly shifted, and lattice analyses (Liao and Shuryak 2005; Glozman 2012) show nucleon and rho masses increasing rather than decreasing near \(T_c\). The introduction of instanton-dyons (Lee and others 1998; Diakonov 2009) connects chiral symmetry breaking and confinement through the same topological objects, enabling future quantitative derivation of effective PNJL-type models.
