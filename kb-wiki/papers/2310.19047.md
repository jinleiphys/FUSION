# Transversity PDFs of the proton from lattice QCD with physical quark masses

> We present a lattice QCD calculation of the transversity isovector- and isoscalar-quark parton distribution functions (PDFs) of the proton utilizing a perturbative matching at next-to-leading-order (NLO) accuracy. Additionally, we determine the isovector and isoscalar tensor charges for the proton. In both calculations, the disconnected contributions to the isoscalar matrix elements have been ignored. The calculations are performed using a single ensemble of $N_f = 2 +1$ highly-improved staggered quarks simulated with physical-mass quarks and a lattice spacing of $a = 0.076$ fm. The Wilson-clover action, with physical quark masses and smeared gauge links obtained from one iteration of hypercubic smearing, is used in the valence sector. Using the NLO operator product expansion, we extract the lowest four to six Mellin moments and the PDFs via a neural network from the matrix elements in the pseudo-PDF approach. In addition, we calculate the PDFs in the quasi-PDF approach with hybrid-scheme renormalization and the recently developed leading-renormalon resummation technique, at NLO with the resummation of leading small-$x$ logarithms.

## Key claim

This lattice QCD calculation of the proton transversity parton distribution functions (PDFs) uses physical quark masses and next-to-leading-order (NLO) perturbative matching, but the isoscalar results ignore disconnected contributions and the entire calculation uses a single lattice spacing, leaving several systematic uncertainties unquantified.

## Method

The authors computed two-point and three-point correlation functions on a 64^3 x 64 ensemble of N_f = 2+1 highly-improved staggered quarks with physical pion mass and lattice spacing a = 0.076 fm, using Wilson-clover valence quarks with HYP smearing and momentum smearing for boosted nucleon states. Ground-state matrix elements were extracted from two-state fits to the ratio of three-point to two-point functions, then used in both the pseudo-PDF approach (with short-distance factorization and deep neural network reconstruction) and the quasi-PDF approach (with hybrid-scheme renormalization and leading-renormalon resummation) at NLO with leading-logarithmic resummation.

## Key numbers

- Tensor charge g_T^(u-d) = 1.01(3) in the MSbar scheme at mu = 2 GeV
- Tensor charge g_T^(u+d) = 0.61(2) in the MSbar scheme at mu = 2 GeV
- Tensor charge g_T^u = 0.81(2) and g_T^d = -0.20(1) in the MSbar scheme at mu = 2 GeV
- Ratio Z_T / Z_V = 1.018(18) using three-loop conversion to MSbar at mu = 2 GeV
- Ratio Z_T / Z_V = 1.007(20) using four-loop conversion to MSbar at mu = 2 GeV
- Lattice spacing a = 0.076 fm, with pion mass m_pi = 0.14 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of the unpolarized proton PDF (Gao 2022) to the transversity case, and is among the first physical-pion-mass calculations of transversity PDFs using both pseudo-PDF and quasi-PDF approaches. The results show overall agreement with the global analysis from JAM3D-22 (Gamberg 2022) for the lowest Mellin moments and x-dependent PDFs, with some mild tension in small x regions, while the quasi-PDF results show significant tension with the extraction from Radici and Bacchetta. Future work requires multiple lattice spacings, NNLO corrections, inclusion of disconnected diagrams for the isoscalar case, and higher statistics to enable the deep neural network to play a more significant role.
