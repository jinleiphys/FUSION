# Thermal and Chemical Equilibration in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We investigate the thermalization and the chemical equilibration of a parton plasma created from Au+Au collision at LHC and RHIC energies starting from the early moment when the particle momentum distributions in the central region become for the first time isotropic due to longitudinal cooling. Using the relaxation time approximation for the collision terms in the Boltzmann equations for gluons and for quarks and the real collision terms constructed from the simplest QCD interactions, we show that the collision times have the right behaviour for equilibration. The magnitude of the quark (antiquark) collision time remains bigger than the gluon collision time throughout the lifetime of the plasma so that gluons are equilibrating faster than quarks both chemically and kinetically. That is we have a two-stage equilibration scenario as has been pointed out already by Shuryak sometimes ago. Full kinetic equilibration is however slow and chemical equilibration cannot be completed before the onset of the deconfinement phase transition assumed to be at $T_c=200$ MeV. By comparing the collision entropy density rates of the different processes, we show explicitly that inelastic processes, and \emph{not} elastic processes as is commonly assumed, are dominant in the equilibration of the plasma and that gluon branching leads the other processes in entropy generation. We also show that, within perturbative QCD, processes with higher power in α_s need not be less important for the purpose of equilibration than those with lower power. The state of equilibration of the system has also a role to play. We compare our results with those of the parton cascade model.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in a parton plasma created from Au+Au collisions at LHC and RHIC energies, equilibration proceeds via a two-stage scenario where gluons equilibrate faster than quarks both chemically and kinetically, but full kinetic equilibration is slow and chemical equilibration cannot be completed before the deconfinement phase transition at T_c = 200 MeV. The author claims that inelastic processes, not elastic processes as commonly assumed, dominate the equilibration of the plasma.

## Method

The author solves Boltzmann equations for gluons and quarks using both the relaxation time approximation and real collision terms constructed from tree-level QCD interactions (gg ↔ ggg, gg ↔ gg, gg ↔ qq̅, gq ↔ gq, qq̅ ↔ qq̅, qq ↔ qq). The collision times and target equilibrium temperatures for each species are determined from energy density transfer rates and collision entropy density rates, with infrared regularization using Debye mass and quark medium mass. The evolution starts from isotropic initial conditions obtained from HIJING results for Au+Au collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- Initial gluon collision time at RHIC: 2.18 fm/c; at LHC: 0.73 fm/c
- Initial quark collision time at RHIC: 239.72 fm/c; at LHC: 30.92 fm/c
- Initial temperature at RHIC: 0.50 GeV; at LHC: 0.74 GeV
- Initial proper time at RHIC: 0.70 fm/c; at LHC: 0.50 fm/c
- Deconfinement phase transition temperature assumed: T_c = 200 MeV
- Gluon fugacity at the point when gluon temperature drops to 200 MeV: 0.935 at LHC (at τ ~ 6.25 fm/c); 0.487 at RHIC (at τ ~ 2.85 fm/c)
- Initial gluon energy density at RHIC: 3.20 GeV/fm³; at LHC: 40.00 GeV/fm³
- Initial quark energy density at RHIC: 0.63 GeV/fm³; at LHC: 7.83 GeV/fm³

## Context

This work extends the earlier pure-gluon plasma study by Wong (1996) to include quarks and antiquarks, and supports the two-stage equilibration scenario previously proposed by Shuryak. The results are compared with the parton cascade model (PCM) of Geiger and Müller, which predicted an equilibration time of 2.4 fm/c for Au+Au at 200 GeV/nucleon but found that momentum distributions were not perfect exponentials. The paper enables future studies of how far a quark-gluon plasma can equilibrate before the phase transition, and demonstrates that within perturbative QCD, processes with higher power in α_s need not be less important for equilibration than those with lower power.
