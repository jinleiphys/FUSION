# A phenomenological estimate of isospin breaking in hadronic vacuum polarization

> Puzzles in the determination of the hadronic-vacuum-polarization contribution currently impede a conclusive interpretation of the precision measurement of the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon at the Fermilab experiment. One such puzzle concerns tensions between evaluations in lattice QCD and using $e^+e^-\to\text{hadrons}$ cross-section data. In lattice QCD, the dominant isospin-symmetric part and isospin-breaking (IB) corrections are calculated separately, with very different systematic effects. Identifying these two pieces in a data-driven approach provides an opportunity to compare them individually and trace back the source of the discrepancy. Here, we estimate the IB component of the lattice-QCD calculations from phenomenology, based on a comprehensive study of exclusive contributions that can be enhanced via infrared singularities, threshold effects, or hadronic resonances, including, for the first time, in the $e^+e^-\to3π$ channel. We observe sizable cancellations among different channels, with a sum that even suggests a slightly larger result for the QED correction than obtained in lattice QCD. We conclude that the tensions between lattice QCD and $e^+e^-$ data therefore cannot be explained by the IB contributions in the lattice-QCD calculations.

## Key claim
The authors estimate that the net isospin-breaking (IB) corrections to hadronic vacuum polarization are small, with sizable cancellations among different channels, and conclude that the tensions between lattice QCD and \(e^+e^-\) data cannot be explained by the IB contributions in the lattice-QCD calculations. The hedge is that the sum of IB effects even suggests a slightly larger result for the QED correction than obtained in lattice QCD, meaning the discrepancy would only increase if lattice QCD were to revise its IB estimates.

## Method
The authors performed a phenomenological estimate of isospin-breaking (IB) corrections to hadronic vacuum polarization (HVP) by evaluating exclusive contributions that are enhanced via infrared singularities, threshold effects, or hadronic resonances. They computed contributions from radiative channels (\(\pi^0\gamma\), \(\eta\gamma\), \(\omega(\to\pi^0\gamma)\pi^0\)), final-state radiation (FSR) in \(2\pi\), \(3\pi\), and \(K^+K^-\) channels, \(\rho\)–\(\omega\) mixing in both \(2\pi\) and \(3\pi\) channels, and threshold effects from pion and kaon mass differences, using dispersive analyses, unitarized chiral perturbation theory, and Khuri–Treiman techniques. The results were decomposed into Euclidean-time windows (short-distance, intermediate, long-distance) and separated into QED (\(\mathcal{O}(e^2)\)) and strong IB (\(\mathcal{O}(\delta = m_u - m_d)\)) contributions.

## Key numbers
* Full HVP QED correction (\(\mathcal{O}(e^2)\)): \(0.71(1.75) \times 10^{-10}\) from this work, compared to \(-1.5(6) \times 10^{-10}\) from Borsanyi (2020) and \(-1.0(6.6) \times 10^{-10}\) from RBC (2018).
* Full HVP strong IB correction (\(\mathcal{O}(\delta)\)): \(1.47(1.67) \times 10^{-10}\) from this work, compared to \(1.9(1.2) \times 10^{-10}\) from Borsanyi (2020) and \(10.6(8.0) \times 10^{-10}\) from RBC (2018).
* Intermediate-window QED correction (\(\mathcal{O}(e^2)\)): \(2.34(92) \times 10^{-10}\) from this work, compared to \(-0.09(6) \times 10^{-10}\) from Borsanyi (2020) and \(0.0(2) \times 10^{-10}\) from RBC (2018).
* Intermediate-window strong IB correction (\(\mathcal{O}(\delta)\)): \(0.02(79) \times 10^{-10}\) from this work, compared to \(0.52(4) \times 10^{-10}\) from Borsanyi (2020) and \(0.1(3) \times 10^{-10}\) from RBC (2018).
* Pion mass correction in the \(2\pi\) channel (full HVP): \(-7.67(94) \times 10^{-10}\).
* \(\rho\)–\(\omega\) mixing in the \(2\pi\) channel (full HVP): \(3.79(19) \times 10^{-10}\).
* \(\rho\)–\(\omega\) mixing in the \(3\pi\) channel (full HVP): \(-2.68(70) \times 10^{-10}\).
* FSR in the \(2\pi\) channel (full HVP): \(4.42(4) \times 10^{-10}\).
* FSR in the \(3\pi\) channel (full HVP): \(0.51(1) \times 10^{-10}\).

## Context
This work extends previous phenomenological estimates of isospin-breaking corrections to HVP by Borsanyi (2017), Jegerlehner (2017), and Hoferichter (2022), now including for the first time a comprehensive treatment of FSR and \(\rho\)–\(\omega\) mixing in the \(e^+e^- \to 3\pi\) channel. The results enable a direct comparison of the IB components of lattice-QCD calculations from Borsanyi (2020) and RBC (2018) with data-driven determinations, and the authors conclude that the observed tensions between lattice QCD and \(e^+e^-\) data cannot be attributed to the IB corrections in the lattice-QCD calculations.
