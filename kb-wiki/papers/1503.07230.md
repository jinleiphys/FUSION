# Bosonic and fermionic Weinberg-Joos (j,0)+ (0,j) states of arbitrary spins as Lorentz-tensors or tensor-spinors and second order theory

> We propose a general method for the description of arbitrary single spin-j states transforming according to (j,0)+(0,j) carrier spaces of the Lorentz algebra in terms of Lorentz-tensors for bosons, and tensor-spinors for fermions, and by means of second order Lagrangians. The method allows to avoid the cumbersome matrix calculus and higher \partial^{2j} order wave equations inherent to the Weinberg-Joos approach. We start with reducible Lorentz-tensor (tensor-spinor) representation spaces hosting one sole (j,0)+(0,j) irreducible sector and design there a representation reduction algorithm based on one of the Casimir invariants of the Lorentz algebra. This algorithm allows us to separate neatly the pure spin-j sector of interest from the rest, while preserving the separate Lorentz- and Dirac indexes. However, the Lorentz invariants are momentum independent and do not provide wave equations. Genuine wave equations are obtained by conditioning the Lorentz-tensors under consideration to satisfy the Klein-Gordon equation. In so doing, one always ends up with wave equations and associated Lagrangians that are second order in the momenta. Specifically, a spin-3/2 particle transforming as (3/2,0)+ (0,3/2) is comfortably described by a second order Lagrangian in the basis of the totally antisymmetric Lorentz tensor-spinor of second rank, Ψ_[ μν]. Moreover, the particle is shown to propagate causally within an electromagnetic background. In our study of (3/2,0)+(0,3/2) as part of Ψ_[μν] we reproduce the electromagnetic multipole moments known from the Weinberg-Joos theory. We also find a Compton differential cross section that satisfies unitarity in forward direction. The suggested tensor calculus presents itself very computer friendly with respect to the symbolic software FeynCalc.

## Key claim

The authors propose a general method to describe arbitrary single spin-\(j\) states transforming as \((j,0)\oplus(0,j)\) under the Lorentz algebra using Lorentz-tensors for bosons and tensor-spinors for fermions, with wave equations and Lagrangians that are second order in momenta rather than the higher-order \(\partial^{2j}\) equations inherent to the Weinberg-Joos approach; the method avoids cumbersome matrix calculus and is designed to be computer-friendly for symbolic software like FeynCalc.

## Method

The authors embed the \((j,0)\oplus(0,j)\) irreducible representation space into a reducible Lorentz-tensor or tensor-spinor representation space of minimal dimensionality, then isolate the pure spin-\(j\) sector using a momentum-independent projector based on the Casimir invariant \(F\) of the Lorentz algebra. Wave equations are obtained by imposing the Klein-Gordon equation on the projected states, yielding second-order master equations; for double-spin valued spaces such as \((j_1,1/2)\oplus(1/2,j_1)\), an additional projector based on the squared Pauli-Lubanski vector is employed to separate the two spins. The method is applied explicitly to spin-\(3/2\) as part of the totally antisymmetric Lorentz tensor-spinor of second rank, \(\Psi_{[\mu\nu]}\).

## Key numbers

* The Casimir eigenvalues for the three irreducible sectors in the antisymmetric tensor-spinor of second rank are: \(c_{(1/2,0)} = 3/4\), \(c_{(1/2,1)} = 11/4\), and \(c_{(3/2,0)} = 15/4\).
* The Lorentz projector on the \((3/2,0)\oplus(0,3/2)\) sector is given by \(\left[\mathcal{P}^{(3/2,0)}_F\right]_{[\alpha\beta][\gamma\delta]} = \frac{1}{8}(\sigma_{\alpha\beta}\sigma_{\gamma\delta} + \sigma_{\gamma\delta}\sigma_{\alpha\beta}) - \frac{1}{12}\sigma_{\alpha\beta}\sigma_{\gamma\delta}\).
* The spin-\(3/2\) tensor-spinors satisfy \(\gamma^\alpha\gamma^\beta [w^{(3/2,0)}_\pm]_{[\alpha\beta]} = 0\).
* The Compton differential cross section for the \((3/2,0)\oplus(0,3/2)\) state satisfies unitarity in forward direction.

## Context

This work extends the second-order fermion approaches previously developed for spin-\(1/2\) (Hostler, Morgan, Vaquero) and spin-\(3/2\) (Napsuciale, 2006) to arbitrary spins, filling the gap for spins higher than \(3/2\) where no second-order theory existed. The method contrasts with the linear Rarita-Schwinger framework, which suffers from acausal propagation in electromagnetic backgrounds (Velo, 1970) and violation of unitarity in Compton scattering (Ferrara), and with the higher-order Weinberg-Joos formalism (Weinberg, 1964). The antisymmetric tensor-spinor representation for spin-\(3/2\) was previously employed by Niederle and Nikitin in a linear framework, but without calculation of physical observables or separation of the \((3/2,0)\oplus(0,3/2)\) sector. The present scheme enables efficient tensor calculations of scattering cross-sections using FeynCalc and applies to both bosons and fermions.
