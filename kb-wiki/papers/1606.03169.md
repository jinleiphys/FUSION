# Can realistic interaction be useful for nuclear mean-field approaches?

> Recent applications of the M3Y-type semi-realistic interaction to the nuclear mean-field approaches are presented: (i) Prediction of magic numbers and (ii) isotope shifts of nuclei with magic proton numbers. The results exemplify that realistic interaction, which is derived from the base $2N$ and $3N$ interaction, furnish a new theoretical instrument for advancing nuclear mean-field approaches.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that semi-realistic M3Y-type interactions, which incorporate realistic tensor and spin-orbit forces derived from bare two-nucleon (2N) and three-nucleon (3N) interactions, can successfully describe nuclear mean-field properties such as magic numbers and isotope shifts, provided the spin-orbit channel is augmented by a density-dependent term motivated by chiral effective-field theory (chiEFT) three-nucleon forces.

## Method

Spherical Hartree-Fock (HF) and Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations were performed using the M3Y-P6 semi-realistic interaction and its variant M3Y-P6a, which includes a density-dependent spin-orbit term \(v^{(\mathrm{LS}\rho)}\) to simulate the effect of the three-nucleon spin-orbit interaction. The tensor force in M3Y-P6 is identical to that in the M3Y-Paris interaction, while the spin-orbit part of M3Y-P6a combines the realistic 2N spin-orbit force from M3Y-Paris with a density-dependent term whose strength \(w_1\) was fitted to reproduce the \(n0i_{13/2}\)-\(n0i_{11/2}\) splitting in \(^{208}\)Pb. Magic numbers were identified by the vanishing of proton or neutron pair correlations in spherical HFB calculations, and submagic numbers by condensation energies below thresholds of 0.5 MeV and 0.8 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The \(p1s_{1/2}\)-\(p0d_{3/2}\) energy difference \(\Delta\varepsilon_{13}\) in Ca isotopes shows an inversion from \(^{40}\)Ca to \(^{48}\)Ca; the M3Y-P6 interaction reproduces the \(N\)-dependence, and removing the tensor-force contribution makes the \(N\)-dependence similar to that of the Gogny-D1S interaction (with a constant shift).
- The shell gap between \(n0d_{3/2}\) and \(n1s_{1/2}\) increases by about 3 MeV from \(^{30}\)Si to \(^{24}\)O, with about 2 MeV of that increase coming from the tensor force.
- In \(^{208}\)Pb, the M3Y-P6a interaction gives a single-particle spacing \(\varepsilon(n0i_{11/2})-\varepsilon(n1g_{9/2}) = 0.72\) MeV, close to the experimental value of 0.78 MeV from \(^{209}\)Pb.
- The isotope shift \(\Delta\langle r^2\rangle_p(^{48}\mathrm{Ca}) \approx 0\) (i.e., nearly equal charge radii for \(^{40}\)Ca and \(^{48}\)Ca) is reproduced by M3Y-P6a for the first time in a self-consistent mean-field calculation.
- Submagic numbers identified include \(N=16\) at \(^{24}\)O, \(N=32\) at \(^{52}\)Ca, \(N=40\) at \(^{68}\)Ni, and \(Z=64\) at \(^{146}\)Gd.

## Context

This work extends the M3Y-type semi-realistic interaction program (Nakada, 2003, 2013) and addresses long-standing problems in nuclear mean-field theory: the kink in Pb isotope shifts at \(N=126\) (previously reproduced only in relativistic approaches by Sharma et al. (1994) or with modified Skyrme functionals by Reinhard and Flocard (1995)) and the close charge radii of \(^{40}\)Ca and \(^{48}\)Ca, which had resisted explanation in self-consistent calculations. The results connect to recent chiral EFT studies by Kohno (2012, 2013) on the three-nucleon spin-orbit force and to ab initio coupled-cluster calculations (reported in 2015) that also reproduce the Ca radii. The approach enables systematic predictions of magic numbers across the nuclear chart and suggests that semi-realistic interactions can bridge ab initio and energy-density-functional methods.
