# Narrow bound states of the DNN system

> We report on a recent calculation of the properties of the $DNN$ system, a charmed meson with two nucleons. The system is analogous to the $\bar K NN$ system substituting a strange quark by a charm quark. Two different methods are used to evaluate the binding and width, the Fixed Center approximation to the Faddeev equations and a variational calculation. In both methods we find that the system is bound by about 200 MeV and the width is smaller than 40 MeV, a situation opposite to the one of the $\bar K NN$ system and which makes this state well suited for experimental observation.

## Key claim

The DNN system (a charmed meson with two nucleons) is bound by about 200 MeV with a width smaller than 40 MeV, which is opposite to the behavior of the analogous Kbar NN system and makes this state well suited for experimental observation; however, the binding and width values depend on the specific NN potential used and the total spin channel considered, with the J=0 channel being clearly bound while the J=1 channel is less bound and more uncertain.

## Method

Two independent methods were used: the Fixed Center approximation to the Faddeev equations, where the two nucleons form a cluster and the D meson scatters with them, and a variational calculation using a Hamiltonian with kinetic energy, NN potential, and a complex DN potential derived from coupled-channel calculations. The variational calculation employed three different NN potentials (HN1R, Minnesota force, and a gaussian-fitted version of Argonne v18) and considered a trial wave function with two components for the J=0 channel.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy B = 225 MeV (HN1R potential, J=0), 251 MeV (Minnesota, J=0), 209 MeV (Av18, J=0) from variational calculation
- Total mass of the bound state M_B = 3520 MeV (HN1R, J=0), 3494 MeV (Minnesota, J=0), 3536 MeV (Av18, J=0)
- Width from D absorption by two nucleons Gamma = 26 MeV (HN1R, J=0), 38 MeV (Minnesota, J=0), 22 MeV (Av18, J=0)
- DN potential contribution V(DN) = -575 MeV (HN1R, J=0), -708 MeV (Minnesota, J=0), -540 MeV (Av18, J=0)
- Kinetic energy E_kin = 352 MeV (HN1R, J=0), 438 MeV (Minnesota, J=0), 335 MeV (Av18, J=0)
- FCA calculation shows a clear peak around 3500 MeV with a width of about 20-30 MeV
- Binding energy B = 208 MeV for the J=1 channel (HN1R, unbound relative to Lambda_c* N threshold)

## Context

This work extends studies of the Kbar NN system (Dote 2008, Ikeda 2010, Barnea 2012, Bayar 2011, Oset 2012, Bayar 2012) to the charm sector, where the analogous Lambda_c(2595) resonance has a width of only 2.6 MeV compared to the Lambda(1405) width of about 30 MeV. The variational method was formulated for the Kbar NN system in Dote (2008) and Dote (2008), and the DN interaction uses coupled-channel results from Mizutani (2006). The finding of a narrow, deeply bound state enables experimental searches via antiproton-induced reactions at FAIR, pion-induced reactions at J-PARC, and relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
