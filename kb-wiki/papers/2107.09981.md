# Potential energy surfaces and fission fragment mass yields of even-even superheavy nuclei

> Potential energy surfaces and fission barriers of superheavy nuclei are analyzed in the macroscopic-microscopic model. The Lublin-Strasbourg Drop (LSD) is used to obtain the macroscopic part of the energy, whereas the shell and pairing energy corrections are evaluated using the Yukawa-folded potential. A standard flooding technique has been used to determine the barrier heights. It was shown the Fourier shape parametrization containing only three deformation parameters reproduces well the nuclear shapes of nuclei on their way to fission. In addition, the non-axial degree of freedom is taken into account to describe better the form of nuclei around the ground state and in the saddles region. Apart from the symmetric fission valley, a new very asymmetric fission mode is predicted in most superheavy nuclei. The fission fragment mass distributions of considered nuclei are obtained by solving the 3D Langevin equations.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that, in addition to the dominant symmetric fission channel, a new very asymmetric fission mode producing heavy fragments around mass A=208 (the doubly magic lead region) exists in most superheavy nuclei with A≥280, but this mode is significantly suppressed in spontaneous fission compared to neutron-induced fission.

## Method

The authors used a macroscopic-microscopic (mac-mic) model with the Lublin-Strasbourg Drop (LSD) for the macroscopic energy and Yukawa-folded single-particle potential for shell and pairing corrections, evaluated in a 4D Fourier deformation parameter space (elongation, left-right asymmetry, neck, and non-axiality). Fission barrier heights were determined using a standard flooding technique in the 4D space, and fission fragment mass yields were obtained by solving 3D Langevin equations (excluding the non-axial degree of freedom at large elongations) for 20,000 trajectories per nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Fission barrier heights vary from around 7 MeV for Rf to Ds nuclei, reach 9 MeV for 294Fl, then decrease to around 5 MeV for element Z=120, and grow again to 3.76 MeV for the hypothetical isotope 304126.
- The non-axial degree of freedom reduces the fission barrier of 304120 by approximately 3 MeV.
- The left-right (octupole-like) asymmetry reduces the fission barrier of 304120 by approximately 1 MeV.
- The asymmetric fission component reaches up to 40% of trajectories for Fl isotopes when Langevin trajectories begin around the saddle point (neutron-induced fission case), but is about four times smaller in the spontaneous fission case.
- The Langevin calculation used a time step of Δτ=1 ħ/MeV (approximately 2/3 × 10⁻²¹ s) and typically 20,000 trajectories per nucleus.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous mac-mic model studies of fission fragment mass yields for even-even Ra-Th and actinide nuclei (Kostryukov et al., 2020; 2021) into the superheavy region (Z=104-126). It contrasts with the Born-Oppenheimer+Wigner model used in their earlier work (Kostryukov et al., 2021), which could not handle two competing fission modes. The prediction of a very asymmetric fission mode leading to heavy fragments around A=208 aligns with earlier suggestions by Poenaru et al. (2018) and Warda et al. (2018) regarding cluster emission or super-asymmetric fission. The results enable improved predictions for the synthesis chances of elements with Z≥118 and provide input for astrophysical models.
