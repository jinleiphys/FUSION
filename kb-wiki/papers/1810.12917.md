# Multimessenger Parameter Estimation of GW170817

> We combine gravitational wave (GW) and electromagnetic (EM) data to perform a Bayesian parameter estimation of the binary neutron star (NS) merger GW170817. The EM likelihood is constructed from a fit to a large number of numerical relativity simulations which we combine with a lower bound on the mass of the remnant's accretion disk inferred from the modeling of the EM light curve. In comparison with previous works, our analysis yields a more precise determination of the tidal deformability of the binary, for which the EM data provide a lower bound, and of the mass ratio of the binary, with the EM data favoring a smaller mass asymmetry. The 90\% credible interval for the areal radius of a $1.4\ M_\odot$ NS is found to be $12.2^{+1.0}_{-0.8} \pm 0.2\ {\rm km}$ (statistical and systematic uncertainties).

## Key claim

The paper claims that including electromagnetic (EM) data from the kilonova AT2017gfo in a joint Bayesian analysis with gravitational wave (GW) data from GW170817 yields a more precise determination of the binary tidal deformability and mass ratio than GW data alone, but this result depends on the assumption that the merger remnant formed an accretion disk of at least 0.04 solar masses, which is inferred from EM light curve modeling rather than directly measured.

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian parameter estimation using the relative binning method for the GW likelihood (using the IMRPhenomD_NRTidal waveform model on LIGO data from 23 to 1000 Hz) and constructed an EM likelihood from a fit to 29 numerical relativity simulations of binary neutron star mergers using four nuclear equations of state (DD2, BHBΛφ, LS220, SFHo). The EM likelihood was approximated as the probability that the remnant disk mass exceeds 0.04 solar masses, based on a fitting formula relating disk mass to the tidal deformability parameter Λ̃, with the disk mass assumed to follow a log-normal distribution with standard deviation 0.5. The joint posterior was sampled using the MultiNest nested sampling algorithm.

## Key numbers

- The 90% credible interval for the areal radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is 12.2^{+1.0}_{-0.8} ± 0.2 km (statistical and systematic uncertainties)
- The 90% confidence interval for Λ̃ shifts from (53, 625) with median 297 (GW-only) to (323, 776) with median 487 (GW+EM)
- The EM data excludes values of Λ̃ smaller than about 300, corresponding to a lower limit on the neutron star radius of 11.2 km
- The disk mass fitting formula uses coefficients α = 0.084, β = 0.127, γ = 567.1, and δ = 405.14
- The EM likelihood assumes a minimum disk mass of 0.04 solar masses, based on kilonova models requiring 0.02-0.05 solar masses of ejecta
- The rest-frame chirp mass is found to have median value 1.186 solar masses (using redshift z = 0.0099)

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of Radice (2017) by incorporating numerical relativity results into a full joint Bayesian framework rather than a qualitative estimate, and it improves upon the GW-only analyses of the LIGO/Virgo collaboration (2017) and De (2018) by adding EM constraints that break degeneracies in the GW parameter space. The approach enables tighter constraints on the neutron star equation of state than previously possible, and the authors note that future work should extend the simulations to include first-order phase transitions (the "twin star" hypothesis) and a wider range of mass ratios.
