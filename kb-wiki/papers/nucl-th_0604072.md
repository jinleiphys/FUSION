# Collective-coupling analysis of spectra of mass-7 isobars: ^7He, ^7Li, ^7Be, ^7B

> A nucleon-nucleus interaction model has been applied to ascertain the underlying character of the negative-parity spectra of four isobars of mass seven, from neutron-- to proton--emitter driplines. With one single nuclear potential defined by a simple coupled-channel model, a multichannel algebraic scattering approach (MCAS) has been used to determine the bound and resonant spectra of the four nuclides, of which ^7He and ^7B are particle unstable. Incorporation of Pauli blocking in the model enables a description of all known spin-parity states of the mass-7 isobars. We have also obtained spectra of similar quality by using a large space no-core shell model. Additionally, we have studied ^7Li and ^7Be using a dicluster model. We have found a dicluster-model potential that can reproduce the lowest four states of the two nuclei, as well as the relevant low-energy elastic scattering cross sections. But, with this model, the rest of the energy spectra cannot be obtained.

## Key claim

Using a single nucleon-nucleus interaction potential defined by a simple coupled-channel model, the multichannel algebraic scattering approach (MCAS) can describe all known negative-parity spin-parity states of the four mass-7 isobars (^7He, ^7Li, ^7Be, ^7B) from the neutron to proton driplines, but only when Pauli blocking is incorporated via the Orthogonalizing Pseudo-Potential method; the dicluster model can reproduce only the lowest four states of ^7Li and ^7Be and cannot obtain the rest of the energy spectra.

## Method

The MCAS method was applied to nucleon+mass-6 systems (p+^6He, n+^6Be) using a collective-model prescription with a quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ and a Woods-Saxon form for the interaction potentials, including central, spin-orbit, l², and spin-spin terms. Pauli blocking was treated via the Orthogonalizing Pseudo-Potential method with strength parameters λ_c set to 1000 MeV for forbidden states and zero for allowed states, with intermediate values for "Pauli-hindered" states treated as free parameters. Additionally, a dicluster-model calculation was performed for ^7Li and ^7Be as α+^3H and α+^3He systems respectively, using a single-channel potential without the OPP term.

## Context

This work extends the MCAS method previously developed for stable light nuclei (Amos 2003, Pisent 2005, Svenne 2006) to particle-unstable nuclei at the driplines, following earlier application to ^15F (Canton 2006). The results are compared with no-core shell-model calculations using the G-matrix interactions of Zheng (1995) and with dicluster model studies of ^7Li (Fowler 2005). The approach enables prediction of spectra for neutron-rich ^7He and proton-rich ^7B, which are particle-unstable, and provides a framework for analyzing future low-energy scattering data of ^6He ions from hydrogen to determine positive-parity interactions.
