# Microscopic description of neutron emission rates in compound nuclei

> The neutron emission rates in thermal excited nuclei are conventionally described by statistical models with a phenomenological level density parameter that depends on excitation energies, deformations and mass regions. In the microscopic view of hot nuclei, the neutron emission rates can be determined by the external neutron gas densities without any free parameters. Therefore the microscopic description of thermal neutron emissions is desirable that can impact several understandings such as survival probabilities of superheavy compound nuclei and neutron emissivity in reactors. To describe the neutron emission rates microscopically, the external thermal neutron gases are self-consistently obtained based on the Finite-Temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (FT-HFB) approach. The results are compared with the statistical model to explore the connections between the FT-HFB approach and the statistical model. The Skyrme FT-HFB equation is solved by HFB-AX in deformed coordinate spaces. Based on the FT-HFB approach, the thermal properties and external neutron gas are properly described with the self-consistent gas substraction procedure. Then neutron emission rates can be obtained based on the densities of external neutron gases. The thermal statistical properties of $^{238}$U and $^{258}$U are studied in detail in terms of excitation energies. The thermal neutron emission rates in $^{238, 258}$U and superheavy compound nuclei $_{112}^{278}$Cn and $_{114}^{292}$Fl are calculated, which agree well with the statistical model by adopting an excitation-energy-dependent level density parameter. The coordinate-space FT-HFB approach can provide reliable microscopic descriptions of neutron emission rates in hot nuclei, as well as microscopic constraints on the excitation energy dependence of level density parameters for statistical models.

## Key claim

The paper claims that neutron emission rates in hot compound nuclei can be described microscopically using the Finite-Temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (FT-HFB) approach without any free parameters, and that these rates agree well with the statistical model when an excitation-energy-dependent level density parameter (specifically \(a = E^{*}/T^{2}\)) is adopted from the FT-HFB results, rather than a constant parameter.

## Method

The Skyrme FT-HFB equation was solved using the HFB-AX solver in deformed coordinate spaces with a mesh size of 0.6 fm and B-spline order of 12, for nuclei including \(^{238}\mathrm{U}\), \(^{258}\mathrm{U}\), \(^{278}_{112}\mathrm{Cn}\), and \(^{292}_{114}\mathrm{Fl}\) at temperatures up to \(kT = 2\) MeV. External neutron gas densities were extracted via a self-consistent gas subtraction procedure, where the FT-HFB equation was solved both with and without nuclear interactions to isolate the gas contribution. Neutron emission widths were then calculated from the uniform neutron gas density using the nucleosynthesis formula \(\Gamma_n / \hbar = n_{\mathrm{gas}} \langle \sigma v \rangle\), with the geometrical cross section \(\pi R^2\) and the average velocity derived from Fermi-Dirac statistics.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{238}\mathrm{U}\) at \(kT = 1.0\) MeV: neutron gas density \(n_{\mathrm{gas}} = 2.07 \times 10^{-6}\) fm\(^{-3}\) and FT-HFB neutron emission width \(\Gamma = 3.69 \times 10^{-3}\) MeV.
* For \(^{238}\mathrm{U}\) at \(kT = 2.0\) MeV: \(n_{\mathrm{gas}} = 7.67 \times 10^{-5}\) fm\(^{-3}\) and \(\Gamma = 1.94 \times 10^{-1}\) MeV.
* For \(^{258}\mathrm{U}\) at \(kT = 1.0\) MeV: \(n_{\mathrm{gas}} = 1.67 \times 10^{-5}\) fm\(^{-3}\) and \(\Gamma = 3.16 \times 10^{-2}\) MeV.
* For \(^{258}\mathrm{U}\) at \(kT = 2.0\) MeV: \(n_{\mathrm{gas}} = 2.11 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^{-3}\) and \(\Gamma = 5.70 \times 10^{-1}\) MeV.
* For \(^{278}_{112}\mathrm{Cn}\) at \(kT = 1.0\) MeV: \(n_{\mathrm{gas}} = 5.53 \times 10^{-7}\) fm\(^{-3}\) and \(\Gamma = 1.09 \times 10^{-3}\) MeV.
* For \(^{292}_{114}\mathrm{Fl}\) at \(kT = 1.0\) MeV: \(n_{\mathrm{gas}} = 1.15 \times 10^{-6}\) fm\(^{-3}\) and \(\Gamma = 3.69 \times 10^{-3}\) MeV.
* The statistical model with constant level density parameter \(a = A/13\) MeV\(^{-1}\) gave widths \(\Gamma(a)\) that deviated from FT-HFB results, especially at low temperatures for superheavy nuclei; for \(^{278}_{112}\mathrm{Cn}\) at \(kT = 1.0\) MeV, \(\Gamma(a) = 1.36 \times 10^{-4}\) MeV versus the FT-HFB value of \(1.09 \times 10^{-3}\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous FT-HFB studies of fission barriers and neutron gases (Pei 2009, Pei 2010) to directly compute neutron emission rates, which had rarely been undertaken microscopically. It contrasts with the conventional statistical model of Weisskopf (1937) and Bohr (1936), which relies on a phenomenological level density parameter, and with the spherical FT-Hartree-Fock calculations of Bonche (1985). The results enable microscopic constraints on the excitation-energy dependence of level density parameters for statistical models, which can impact predictions of survival probabilities in superheavy element synthesis and neutron emissivity in reactors.
