# Impact of centre vortex removal on the Landau-gauge quark propagator in dynamical QCD

> The important role of centre vortices in dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and corresponding dynamical mass generation has been demonstrated in pure gauge studies of the Landau-gauge quark propagator. We present initial results of our investigation into the impact of centre vortex removal on the Landau-gauge propagator on dynamical gauge fields. To ensure sensitivity to the topology of the gauge fields, the propagator is computed with overlap fermions. Upon removal of centre vortices we find that dynamical mass generation is suppressed. The quark renormalisation function remains remarkably flat except in the deep infrared where it exhibits significant suppression. These effects become more prominent at lighter quark masses.

# Impact of centre vortex removal on the Landau-gauge quark propagator in dynamical QCD

## Key claim
Centre vortices appear to be the primary mediator of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in dynamical QCD, but the evidence is suggestive rather than conclusive: the authors find that dynamical mass generation is suppressed upon vortex removal, with the suppression becoming more prominent at lighter quark masses, and they interpret this as indicating vanishing dynamical mass generation toward the physical point.

## Method
The overlap Landau-gauge quark propagator was computed on untouched and vortex-removed 32^3 x 64 PACS-CS 2+1 flavour dynamical gauge field ensembles at a pion mass of 156 MeV, using the fat link irrelevant clover (FLIC) overlap fermion action. Centre vortices were identified by fixing to maximal centre gauge and projecting links to Z(3) elements, then vortex-removed links were constructed as the product of the gauge-fixed link and the inverse of its centre-projected link. The mass function M(p) and renormalisation function Z(p) were compared between untouched and vortex-removed backgrounds at seven quark masses.

## Key numbers
- Lattice volume: 32^3 x 64, with 26 configurations at m_pi = 156 MeV
- Seven quark masses studied: m_q = 6, 8, 9, 19, 28, 56, 84 MeV
- At the heaviest mass (m_q = 84 MeV), dynamical mass generation in the mass function is suppressed upon vortex removal, consistent with pure gauge theory
- At the middle mass (m_q = 19 MeV), only a remnant of dynamical mass generation persists upon vortex removal
- The renormalisation function exhibits significant suppression in the deep infrared upon vortex removal, with greater significance at lighter quark masses

## Context
This work extends pure SU(3) gauge theory studies of centre vortices and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking to full dynamical QCD. Prior work by Trewartha (2015) on 20^3 x 40 Luscher-Weisz ensembles in pure gauge theory showed that dynamical mass generation is suppressed upon vortex removal and reproduced on vortex-only backgrounds. A study by Bowman (2010) using AsqTad fermions found weak dependence on centre vortices, but this was contradicted by the Trewartha (2015) overlap fermion study. The present results lend further credibility to centre vortices as the primary mediator of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in dynamical QCD, with results at the dynamical point currently in preparation.
