# Relativistic chiral SU(3) symmetry, large N_c sum rules and meson-baryon scattering

> The relativistic chiral SU(3) Lagrangian is used to describe kaon-nucleon scattering imposing constraints from the pion-nucleon sector and the axial-vector coupling constants of the baryon octet states. We solve the covariant coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation with the interaction kernel truncated at chiral order Q^3 where we include only those terms which are leading in the large N_c limit of QCD. The baryon decuplet states are an important explicit ingredient in our scheme, because together with the baryon octet states they form the large N_c baryon ground states of QCD. Part of our technical developments is a minimal chiral subtraction scheme within dimensional regularization, which leads to a manifest realization of the covariant chiral counting rules. All SU(3) symmetry-breaking effects are well controlled by the combined chiral and large N_c expansion, but still found to play a crucial role in understanding the empirical data. We achieve an excellent description of the data set typically up to laboratory momenta of p_lab about 500 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a relativistic chiral SU(3) Lagrangian, combined with a Bethe-Salpeter equation and a minimal chiral subtraction scheme, can describe kaon-nucleon and antikaon-nucleon scattering data up to laboratory momenta of about 500 MeV, but only when the interaction kernel is truncated at chiral order Q^3 and includes only terms leading in the large N_c limit of QCD, with the baryon decuplet states as an explicit ingredient.

## Method

The authors constructed a relativistic chiral SU(3) Lagrangian including baryon octet, decuplet (J^P = 3/2^+), and nonet d-wave resonance (J^P = 3/2^-) fields, then solved the covariant coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation with the interaction kernel truncated at chiral order Q^3, retaining only terms leading in the large N_c limit. They developed a minimal chiral subtraction scheme within dimensional regularization to maintain manifest chiral counting rules, and adjusted parameters to simultaneously describe kaon-nucleon, antikaon-nucleon, and low-energy pion-nucleon scattering data along with axial-vector coupling constants of the baryon octet.

## Key numbers

- The K^+p scattering length is a_{K^+p} ≈ 0.28–0.34 fm, taken from prior empirical analysis (Dover).
- The K^+n scattering length is a_{K^+n} ≈ 0.1 fm, from a recent analysis (Barnes).
- The pion decay constant is f_π = 92.42 ± 0.33 MeV and the kaon decay constant is f_K ≈ 113.0 ± 1.3 MeV, leading to a naive estimate f ≈ 104 MeV.
- The axial-vector coupling constants are F_{[8]} ≈ 0.45 and D_{[8]} ≈ 0.80, constrained by baryon octet weak decays.
- The decuplet coupling constant is C_{[10]} ≈ 1.6, estimated from baryon decuplet hadronic decay widths.
- The resonance parameters are F_{[9]} ≈ 1.8, D_{[9]} ≈ 0.84, and C_{[9]} ≈ 2.5, with a singlet-octet mixing angle ϑ ≈ 28° for the Λ(1520) resonance.
- The data set is described typically up to laboratory momenta p_lab ≈ 500 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral SU(3) approaches to kaon-nucleon scattering (Kaiser, Ramos, Oller-Meissner) by including s- and p-waves systematically, using a covariant Bethe-Salpeter equation with a subtraction scheme rather than a cutoff scheme as employed previously (Kaiser, Ramos). It enables predictions for poorly known meson-baryon coupling constants, subthreshold antikaon-nucleon scattering amplitudes relevant for nuclear matter studies, and SU(3) reaction amplitudes like πΛ → πΣ, πΣ, K̄N for transport model simulations of heavy-ion reactions.
