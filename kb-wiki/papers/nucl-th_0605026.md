# Nuclear Excitations Described by Randomly Selected Multiple Slater Determinants

> We propose a new stochastic method to describe low-lying excited states of finite nuclei superposing multiple Slater determinants without assuming generator coordinates a priori. We examine accuracy of our method by using simple BKN interaction.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a stochastic method for describing low-lying excited states of finite nuclei by superposing multiple Slater determinants, without requiring the user to choose generator coordinates a priori; the method's accuracy is tested only with the simple BKN interaction, and the authors note that calculations with more realistic effective interactions remain a future subject.

## Method

The authors generate many initial Slater determinants randomly, then evolve each using the imaginary-time method (a standard procedure for solving self-consistent Hartree-Fock equations). They pick up a state every 250 iterations, preferentially selecting configurations that linger near local minima or quasi-minima (shoulder states). Each selected state is projected onto parity and angular momentum, and a configuration mixing calculation is performed by solving the Hill-Wheeler-type equation; to avoid overcompleteness, a candidate state is excluded if its overlap with any already-selected state (including parity and rotation operations) exceeds 0.7.

## Key numbers

- The ground-state energy of \(^{16}\mathrm{O}\) obtained with the method is lower than that of a single Slater determinant by about 2 MeV.
- The overlap threshold for excluding a candidate state to avoid overcompleteness is set at 0.7 (the condition uses the absolute value of the overlap with any already-selected state, including parity and 24 special rotations).
- The energy of \(J^{\pi}=0^{+}\) states in \(^{16}\mathrm{O}\) is shown as a function of the number of basis states; two independent calculations (solid and dotted lines) produce almost identical energies for the lowest four levels, indicating qualitative convergence.

## Context

This work extends the generator coordinate method (GCM) by removing the need to choose generator coordinates with physical intuition, which in standard GCM is often restricted to one dimension and makes it difficult to describe many kinds of low-lying excitations simultaneously. The method is tested only with the simple BKN interaction, and the authors state that calculation with more realistic effective interactions (such as Skyrme or Gogny, which have been used successfully for ground-state properties in prior work by the self-consistent mean-field community) is a future subject. The approach enables systematic selection of multiple Slater determinants corresponding to local minima and soft modes near the ground state, potentially allowing simultaneous description of various low-lying excitations in finite nuclei.
