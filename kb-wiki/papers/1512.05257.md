# DBI scalar field theory for QGP hydrodynamics

> A way to describe the hydrodynamics of the quark-gluon plasma using a DBI action is proposed, based on the model found by Heisenberg for high energy scattering of nucleons. The expanding plasma is described as a shockwave in a DBI model for a real scalar standing in for the pion, and I show that one obtains a fluid description in terms of a relativistic fluid that near the shock is approximately ideal ($η\simeq 0$) and conformal. One can introduce an extra term inside the square root of the DBI action that generates a shear viscosity term in the energy-momentum tensor near the shock, as well as a bulk viscosity, and regulates the behaviour of the energy density at the shock, making it finite. The resulting fluid satisfies the relativistic Navier-Stokes equation with $u^μ, ρ,P,η$ defined in terms of $ϕ$ and its derivatives. One finds a relation between the parameters of the theory and the QGP thermodynamics, $\a/\b^2=η/(sT)$, and by fixing $\a$ and $\b$ from usual (low multiplicity) particle scattering, one finds $T\propto m_π$.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the hydrodynamics of the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) near a shockwave can be described by a Dirac-Born-Infeld (DBI) scalar field action, but only if one uses a specific improved energy-momentum tensor (not the Belinfante tensor) and introduces an extra term inside the square root of the DBI action to generate finite shear and bulk viscosities; the resulting fluid is approximately ideal and conformal only near the shock, not globally.

## Method

The author starts from the DBI action for a real scalar field (standing in for the pion) originally used by Heisenberg for high-energy nucleon scattering, and considers a shockwave solution depending only on the Lorentz invariant \(s = t^2 - x^2\). An improved energy-momentum tensor is constructed by adding a divergence of a superpotential to the Belinfante tensor, chosen so that in the massless limit the tensor becomes traceless on-shell. A viscous term is then introduced inside the square root of the DBI action, proportional to \(\sqrt{-(\partial_\lambda\phi)^2}\,\partial^\mu u_\mu\), which generates shear and bulk viscosities in the energy-momentum tensor. The fluid velocity is defined as \(u^\mu = \partial^\mu\phi / \sqrt{-(\partial_\lambda\phi)^2}\), and the energy density, pressure, and viscosities are extracted from the improved energy-momentum tensor.

## Key numbers

* The DBI parameter \(\beta\) is fixed from chiral perturbation theory as \(\beta = \sqrt{2}/(\sqrt{3}\,m_\pi f_\pi) \simeq 1/(126\ \text{MeV})^2\), using \(m_\pi \simeq 140\ \text{MeV}\) and \(f_\pi \simeq 93\ \text{MeV}\).
* Near the shock, the conformal relation \(3P \simeq \rho\) holds, with both quantities diverging as \(\rho \simeq 1/(\beta^2 \sqrt{1+\beta^2(\partial_\mu\phi)^2}) \rightarrow \infty\).
* The shear viscosity from the viscous term is \(\eta = (\alpha \sqrt{-(\partial_\lambda\phi)^2})/(\beta^2 \sqrt{1+\beta^2[(\partial\phi)^2+m^2\phi^2] + \alpha[\partial^2\phi - (\partial_\mu\phi)(\partial^\mu\partial^\rho\phi)(\partial_\rho\phi)/(\partial_\lambda\phi)^2]})\), which blows up near the shock, but the ratio \(\eta/s = T \alpha/\beta^2\) remains finite.
* The relation \(\alpha/\beta^2 = \eta/(sT)\) connects the action parameters to QGP thermodynamics, and fixing \(\alpha\) from scattering experiments gives \(T \propto m_\pi\); numerically matching the experimental freeze-out temperature suggests \(\alpha \simeq \beta/(16 m_\pi)\).

## Context

This work extends the Heisenberg model (Heisenberg, 1952) for high-energy nucleon scattering, which uses a DBI scalar action and predicts saturation of the Froissart bound, to the description of QGP hydrodynamics. It builds on the observation that the sQGP is well described by nearly ideal hydrodynamics with \(\eta/s \sim 1/(4\pi)\) (Policastro, 2001; Buchel, 2003; Kovtun, 2004), and on the gravity dual analysis of Kang and Nastase (2005) that connected the Heisenberg model to AdS/CFT. The paper enables a first-principles derivation of QGP transport coefficients from an effective field theory, with the potential to predict the freeze-out temperature and the ratio \(\eta/s\) from the pion mass and decay constant.
