# New applications of renormalization group methods in nuclear physics

> We review recent developments in the use of renormalization group (RG) methods in low-energy nuclear physics. These advances include enhanced RG technology, particularly for three-nucleon forces, which greatly extends the reach and accuracy of microscopic calculations. We discuss new results for the nucleonic equation of state with applications to astrophysical systems such as neutron stars, new calculations of the structure and reactions of finite nuclei, and new explorations of correlations in nuclear systems.

## Key claim

Renormalization group methods can systematically decouple low- and high-momentum degrees of freedom in nuclear Hamiltonians, making many-body calculations more perturbative and improving convergence, but only if induced many-body forces (particularly three-nucleon forces) are consistently included during the evolution.

## Method

The authors review two RG approaches for evolving nuclear interactions: the Vlowk method, which lowers a momentum cutoff while preserving phase shifts up to that cutoff, and the similarity renormalization group (SRG), which drives the Hamiltonian toward band-diagonal form via flow equations with generator Gs = Trel. For three-nucleon forces, they describe four strategies: evolution in a discrete harmonic oscillator basis, adding unevolved chiral 3NFs to evolved NN interactions, evolving 3NFs in a continuous plane-wave basis, and using a hyperspherical momentum representation.

## Key numbers

- Typical relative momentum in the Fermi sea of any large nucleus is of order 1 fm^-1 or 200 MeV; 2 fm^-1 is adopted as the dividing line between low and high momentum for nuclei
- Phenomenological potentials describe proton-neutron scattering up to laboratory energies of 300 MeV
- The ground state energy of 3H as a function of SRG flow parameter lambda shows that neglecting induced 3N forces results in significant variation of binding energy, while including all 3N contributions keeps the energy invariant under changes in resolution scale
- At lambda = 1.5 fm^-1, evolved 3N force matrix elements from five different chiral interactions show a remarkably reduced model dependence in the dominant kinematical region

## Context

This review extends earlier work on Vlowk methods summarized in Bogner (2009) and Furnstahl (2012), and addresses the challenge of consistently treating three-nucleon forces that chiral effective field theory (Epelbaum 2008, Hammer 2012) has shown to be essential for accurate nuclear calculations. The RG evolution enables more perturbative many-body calculations (as demonstrated in nuclear matter) and opens the door to techniques such as many-body perturbation theory, while the universality of low-momentum interactions suggests that chiral low-energy coupling constants cD and cE flow to approximately universal values at low resolution.
