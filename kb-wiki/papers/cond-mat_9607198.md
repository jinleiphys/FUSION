# Algebraic approach to renormalization

> In close analogy to the Bloch-Feshbach formalism known from the theory of nuclear dynamics, I develop a mathematical framework that allows one to understand renormalization in terms of purely algebraic operations (projections, dilatations) in Hilbert space. This algebraic approach is put to the test in the study of the low-energy dynamics of interacting quantum gases, and proves to be efficient in deriving such diverse results as the renormalization group equation for an interacting Bose gas, the $β$ function of $ϕ^4$ theory, the screening of fermion-fermion interactions or the BCS instability.

# Algebraic approach to renormalization

## Key claim
The Bloch-Feshbach projection technique in Hilbert space can be used as an efficient algebraic tool to derive renormalization group equations for interacting quantum gases, contrary to the assertion that such projection operator methods are limited to essentially one-dimensional problems. The approach is demonstrated for bosons and fermions in more than one dimension, but relies on a perturbative expansion to second order and the assumption that the effective Hamiltonian can be approximated by its value at the external parameter b=0.

## Method
The author develops a modified Bloch-Feshbach formalism in which a projection operator P selects a subspace of the full Hilbert space (e.g., momentum modes below a cutoff), and the reduced statistical operator is recast into canonical form with an effective Hamiltonian Heff. The effective Hamiltonian is obtained to second order in perturbation theory as Heff(0) = PHP + Σ, where Σ = -PVQ(1/ℒ(0))QVP accounts for the residual influence of eliminated modes. For interacting quantum gases, the cutoff is lowered in infinitesimal steps Λ(s+Δs) = exp(-Δs)Λ(s), and after each step the modification of the two-body interaction is computed from the Σ term, yielding flow equations for the coupling constants.

## Key numbers
- For bosons with point interaction in three dimensions: flow equation ΔU = -(mΛ/2π²) U²·Δs, where m is the mass, Λ the cutoff, and U the coupling constant.
- For φ⁴ theory in three dimensions with Λ ≫ m: flow equation Δg = -(3g²/16π²)Δs, where g is the coupling constant.
- For screening of fermion-fermion interactions: the effective interaction Veff(q) = [1/Vbare(q) + ρ(εF)]⁻¹, where ρ(εF) is the density of states at the Fermi surface.
- For BCS instability: flow equation ΔV = (ρ(εF)/2) V²·Δs, where V is the attractive pairing coupling and ρ(εF) the density of states at the Fermi surface.

## Context
This work extends the connection between the Bloch-Feshbach projection formalism (Bloch 1958, Feshbach 1962) and Wilson's renormalization group (Wilson 1971, Wegner 1972, Polchinski 1984) by demonstrating that algebraic Hilbert space techniques can reproduce results previously obtained via functional integral methods. The paper builds on earlier work by Muller and Rau on φ⁴ theory renormalization with Bloch-Feshbach techniques, and on Shankar's (1994) renormalization group treatment of fermion systems. The approach enables the derivation of renormalization group equations for bosons, φ⁴ theory, fermion screening, and BCS instability within a unified algebraic framework, and opens the possibility of applying projection operator methods to more complex many-body problems.
