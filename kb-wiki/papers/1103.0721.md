# New Parity-Violating Muonic Forces

> The recent discrepancy between proton charge radius measurements extracted from electron-proton versus muon-proton systems is suggestive of a new force that differentiates between lepton species. We identify a class of models with gauged right-handed muon number, which contains new vector and scalar force carriers at the 100 MeV scale or lighter, that is consistent with observations. Such forces would lead to an enhancement by several orders-of-magnitude of the parity-violating asymmetries in the scattering of low-energy muons on nuclei. The relatively large size of such asymmetries, O(10^{-4}), opens up the possibility for new tests of parity violation in neutral currents with existing low-energy muon beams.

# New Parity-Violating Muonic Forces

## Key claim

The discrepancy between proton charge radius measurements from electron-proton versus muon-proton systems (a 5σ difference) could be explained by a new gauge force coupled specifically to right-handed muons, provided the vector and scalar force carriers have masses at or below the 100 MeV scale and the model parameters satisfy a tight cancellation condition to remain consistent with the muon anomalous magnetic moment measurement.

## Method

The authors construct a model with a gauged U(1)_R symmetry that couples exclusively to right-handed muons, introducing a new vector boson V and a scalar Higgs S. They compute the corrections to the proton charge radius extracted from ordinary hydrogen, muonic hydrogen, and electron-proton scattering, and derive the parity-violating asymmetry in low-energy muon-nucleus scattering. The model parameters are constrained by requiring consistency with the observed charge radius pattern, muon g-2, electron g-2, and existing bounds from muonic atom spectroscopy.

## Key numbers

- The difference in squared proton charge radius between e-p and μ-p results is Δr² ≈ 0.06 fm², corresponding to a 5σ discrepancy between atomic hydrogen (r_p = 0.8768(69) fm) and muonic hydrogen (r_p = 0.84184(67) fm), and 4.6σ between electron-proton scattering (r_p = 0.879(8) fm) and muonic hydrogen.
- The parity-violating asymmetry in low-energy muon-nucleus scattering can reach O(10⁻⁴), enhanced by several orders of magnitude compared to the Standard Model prediction of O(10⁻⁷) at p = 29 MeV/c.
- Three benchmark points satisfy all constraints: (A) m_V = 10 MeV, g_R = 0.01, κ = 0.0015; (B) m_V = 50 MeV, g_R = 0.05, κ = 0.0075; (C) m_V = 100 MeV, g_R = 0.07, κ = 0.02.
- The scalar mass m_S is tuned to 102.84 MeV (point A), 90.44 MeV (point B), or 84.97 MeV (point C) to achieve per-mille level cancellation of the vector and scalar contributions to muon g-2.
- The UV cutoff from anomaly considerations is Λ_UV ≤ 700 GeV × (m_V / 10 MeV) × (g_R / e)⁻².
- For muonic helium, the model predicts an effective charge radius shift of Δr²_He = -0.06 fm².
- The one-photon decay rate of the 2s state in μ⁴He from parity mixing is Γ^γ_{2s→1s} ≈ 1.9 × 10³ Hz, with a ratio to the two-photon rate of 0.018.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological approach of Barger et al. (2011) and Itay et al. (2011), who proposed dimension-6 operators to explain the proton radius puzzle, by constructing a renormalizable gauge theory that satisfies constraints from neutrino scattering, QED tests, and collider bounds. The model contrasts with the kinetically mixed vector scenario of Holdom (1986), which predicts the wrong ordering of charge radius shifts (r_p,1 < r_p,3 < r_p,2) and cannot explain the observed pattern. The predicted large parity-violating asymmetries open the possibility for new tests of neutral currents using existing low-energy muon beams at facilities such as PSI, and motivate searches for parity-violating decays in muonic helium Lamb shift experiments.
