# Studies on the $K^*Σ$ bound-state via $K^+p\to K^+ϕ\,p$

> In the present work, we investigate the hidden-strangeness production process in the $S=+1$ channel via $K^+p\to K^+ϕ\,p$, focussing on the exotic \textit{pentaquark} molecular $K^*Σ$ bound state, assigned by $P^+_s(2071,3/2^-)$. For this purpose, we employ the effective Lagrangian approach in the tree-level Born approximation. Using the experimental and theoretical inputs for the exotic state and for the ground-state hadron interactions, the numerical results show a small but obvious peak structure from $P^+_s$ with the signal-to-background ratio $\approx1.7\,\%$, and it is enhanced in the backward-scattering region of the outgoing $K^+$ in the center-of-mass frame. We also find that the contribution from the $K^*(1680,1^-)$ meson plays an important role to reproduce the data. The proton-spin polarizations are taken into account to find a way to reduce the background. The effects of the possible $27$-plet pentaquark $Θ^{++}_{27}$ is discussed as well.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the exotic pentaquark molecular bound state \(P^+_s(2071,3/2^-)\), composed of \(K^*\) and \(\Sigma\), can be observed in the \(K^+p\to K^+\phi\,p\) reaction with a small signal-to-background ratio of approximately 1.7%, and that this signal is enhanced in the backward-scattering region of the outgoing \(K^+\) in the center-of-mass frame. The claim is conditional on the use of a specific phase-angle parameter set \((0,\pi,0,0)\) for the scattering amplitudes, which avoids destructive interference that would otherwise destroy the \(P^+_s\) peak.

## Method

The author employed an effective Lagrangian approach at the tree-level Born approximation to compute the scattering amplitudes for \(K^+p\to K^+\phi\,p\). The model included \(\phi\)-meson exchange diagrams in the \(t\) channel, \(\Lambda^{(*)}\)-hyperon intermediate diagrams in the \(u\) channel, a \(K^*(1680,1^-)\) meson contribution, and a pole diagram for the \(P^+_s\) pentaquark in the \(t\) channel. Phenomenological form factors with a cutoff mass of 480 MeV were used, and coupling constants were taken from the Nijmegen potential model, the hidden-local symmetry approach, and fits to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Signal-to-background ratio for the \(P^+_s\) peak: approximately 1.7% at \(\sqrt{s}=2.65\) GeV in the \(\phi\,p\) invariant-mass distribution.
- Mass and spin-parity of the \(P^+_s\) state: 2071 MeV, \(3/2^-\); full decay width \(\Gamma_{P^+_s}=14\) MeV, taken from Khemchandani (2011).
- Mass and spin-parity of the \(K^*(1680)\) meson: 1680 MeV, \(1^-\); full decay width \(\Gamma_{K^*(1680)}=322\) MeV.
- Cutoff mass for the phenomenological form factors: 480 MeV, determined by fitting total cross section data for \(\sqrt{s}\gtrsim2.7\) GeV.
- Coupling constant \(g_{KK^*(1680)\phi}=1.6\) (fit); \(g_{\phi P^+_sP^+_s}=0.14+0.2i\) from Khemchandani (2011).
- Mass of the \(\Theta^{++}_{27}\) pentaquark: 1.60 GeV; spin-parity \(3/2^+\); full width \(\lesssim43\) MeV from Wu (2003).
- Coupling constant \(g_{KN\Theta^{++}_{27}}\lesssim2.06\) from Wu (2003), treated as a free parameter in the calculations.

## Context

This work extends the search for hidden-flavor pentaquark molecular bound states from the heavy-flavor sector (e.g., \(P^+_c(4457)\) observed at LHCb in 2019) to the light-flavor sector, proposing \(P^+_s(2071,3/2^-)\) as the light-flavor partner of \(P^+_c(4457)\). It builds on theoretical predictions of a \(K^*\Sigma\) molecular bound state from the coupled-channel approach with hidden-local symmetry by Khemchandani (2011) and on the quark delocalization color screening model by Gao (2017). The results provide a guide for future experiments at J-PARC with high-momentum kaon beams to observe this exotic state and to extract information on the \(K^*(1680)\) meson.
