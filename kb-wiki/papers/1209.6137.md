# Domain Growth in Chiral Phase Transitions: Inertial Dynamics

> We investigate the kinetics of phase transitions for chiral symmetry breaking in heavy-ion collisions. We use a Langevin description for order-parameter kinetics in the chiral transition. The Langevin equation of motion includes {\it dissipation} and an {\it inertial term}. We study the ordering dynamics subsequent to a quench from the massless quark phase to the massive quark phase, and discuss the effect of inertia on the growth kinetics.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how an inertial term (second-order time derivative) in the Langevin equation for chiral order-parameter kinetics affects domain growth after a quench, finding that the inertial term produces a faster initial growth law \(L(t) \sim t (\ln t)^{1/2}\) before crossing over to the standard Cahn-Allen \(L(t) \sim t^{1/2}\) behavior at a crossover time \(t_c \sim \gamma^{-1}\), where \(\gamma\) is the dissipation coefficient.

## Method

The authors model chiral symmetry breaking using the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, mapping its thermodynamic potential to an \(M^6\)-Landau potential. They then solve a Langevin equation with both dissipative and inertial terms for the order parameter field \(M(\vec{r},t)\) on a 3-dimensional lattice of size \(256^3\) using Euler-discretization, with initial conditions of small random fluctuations around \(M=0\). The kinetics are studied for two quench scenarios: deep quenches through the second-order line (spinodal decomposition) and shallow quenches through the first-order line (nucleation and growth), for various values of the dissipation coefficient \(\gamma = 0, 0.4, 1.0\) and \(\gamma = 0.25, 0.4, 0.5\) respectively.

## Key numbers

- For the deep quench case (second-order line), the Landau parameters used are \((a/\Lambda^2, b, d\Lambda^2) = (-1.6 \times 10^{-2}, 9 \times 10^{-2}, 7.1 \times 10^{-2})\) with \(\lambda = |a|d/|b|^2 = 0.14\), corresponding to \((\mu,T) = (231.6, 85)\) MeV.
- For the shallow quench case (first-order line), the Landau parameters are \((a/\Lambda^2, b, d\Lambda^2) = (3.5 \times 10^{-3}, -0.1, 0.4)\) with \(\lambda = 0.14\), corresponding to \((\mu,T) = (321.8, 10)\) MeV.
- The tricritical point is located at \(T_{\rm tcp} = 78\) MeV and \(\mu_{\rm tcp} = 282.58\) MeV.
- The estimated length scale \(\xi = \sqrt{K/|a|} \simeq 2.8\) fm and time scale \(t_0 = 1/\sqrt{|a|} \simeq 5.1\) fm for surface tension \(\sigma \simeq 50\) MeV/fm\(^2\); for \(\sigma = 10\) MeV/fm\(^2\), \(\xi \simeq 0.56\) fm.
- The critical bubble radius in the shallow quench case is \(R_c \simeq 8\) in dimensionless units, corresponding to \(R_c \simeq 4.5\) fm for \(\sigma = 10\) MeV/fm\(^2\).
- The noise amplitude used in simulations is \(A_n = 0.5\) corresponding to \(\epsilon = 0.008\) for the deep quench case, and \(\epsilon = 0.6\) for the shallow quench case.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Singh, Puri, Mishra 2010) of far-from-equilibrium kinetics in chiral phase transitions using a TDGL equation without an inertial term, now incorporating inertial dynamics motivated by microscopic derivations from the closed-time-path Green's function formalism (Berges 1999, Greiner 1994, Rischke 1998) and from the NJL model (Florkowski 2011). The study is complementary to prior work by Fraga and Krein (2005) on early-time spinodal decomposition, Bessa et al. (2009) on nucleation kinetics, and Randrup (2009) on spinodal fluctuations in fluid-dynamical models. The results enable quantitative predictions for domain growth kinetics in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, LHC, and future facilities like the beam energy scan at RHIC, compressed baryonic matter at GSI, and NICA at Dubna.
