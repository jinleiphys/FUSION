# Pion and kaon electromagnetic and gravitational form factors

> A unified set of predictions for pion and kaon elastic electromagnetic and gravitational form factors is obtained using a symmetry-preserving truncation of each relevant quantum field equation. A key part of the study is a description of salient aspects of the dressed graviton + quark vertices. The calculations reveal that each meson's mass radius is smaller than its charge radius, matching available empirical inferences; and meson core pressures are commensurate with those in neutron stars. The analysis described herein paves the way for a direct calculation of nucleon gravitational form factors.

## Key claim

Using a symmetry-preserving truncation of quantum field equations, the authors obtain unified predictions for pion and kaon electromagnetic and gravitational form factors, finding that each meson's mass radius is smaller than its charge radius and that meson core pressures are comparable to those in neutron stars, with the caveat that these results depend on a specific truncation of the dressed graviton+quark vertex that includes only the dominant scalar and tensor meson pole contributions.

## Method

The authors employ continuum Schwinger function methods (CSMs) within rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation, solving the probe+meson scattering problem at leading order using a specific gluon+quark interaction kernel defined by a two-parameter Ansatz. They compute dressed quark propagators, meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, and probe+quark vertices (both electromagnetic and gravitational) by solving coupled gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations, with the graviton+quark vertex constructed to satisfy its Ward-Green-Takahashi identity and include contributions from isoscalar-scalar and tensor meson poles. The form factors are extracted from the five-point Schwinger functions using appropriate projection operators.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_pi = 0.135 GeV; pion decay constant: f_pi = 0.095 GeV
- Kaon mass: m_K = 0.495 GeV; kaon decay constant: f_K = 0.116 GeV
- Renormalization-point-invariant current quark masses: hat_m_u = hat_m_d = 0.0055 GeV, hat_m_s = 0.14 GeV (corresponding to m_u^zeta2 = 3.8 MeV and m_s^zeta2 = 97 MeV at zeta = 2 GeV)
- Scalar meson pole masses from the interaction: m_S(u=d) = 0.55 GeV, m_S(s) = 1.07 GeV; tensor meson pole masses: m_T(u=d) = 1.11 GeV, m_T(s) = 1.65 GeV
- Interaction kernel parameters: omega = 0.8 GeV, omega D = (0.94 GeV)^3

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pion and kaon structure using GPD overlap representations with light-front wave functions constrained by valence-quark distribution functions (Zhang 2021, Raya 2021), and a data-driven prediction for the pion mass distribution (Xu 2023). The calculations are performed using the rainbow-ladder truncation scheme (Munczek 1994, Bender 1996) with an interaction kernel developed by Qin (2011) and Binosi (2014), which has been applied to systems ranging from light to heavy quarks. The analysis enables future direct calculations of nucleon gravitational form factors.
