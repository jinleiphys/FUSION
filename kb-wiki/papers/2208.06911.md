# Repulsive vector interaction as a trigger for the non-conformal peak in $V_s^2$

> Considering the NJL model with a repulsive vector channel, parametrized by $G_V$, I show that one may generate a non-monotonic behavior for the speed of sound which peaks at $V_s^2 > 1/3$. This can be achieved by assuming $G_V$ to be density dependent so that the resulting EoS is stiff/repulsive at low densities and soft/non-repulsive at high densities. The interpolation between the two regimes happens through a cross-over which takes place after the first order chiral transition sets in. The model explicitly shows that a non-conformal peak in $V_s^2$ is not in tension with the QCD trace anomaly being positive at all densities, supporting recent claims in this direction. A brief discussion on how the running coupling may affect the mass-radius relation is carried out in the context of simple non-strange quark stars.

# Repulsive vector interaction as a trigger for the non-conformal peak in $V_s^2$

## Key claim

Using the NJL model with a density-dependent repulsive vector coupling $G_V(\mu)$, one can generate a non-monotonic speed of sound that peaks above the conformal value $V_s^2 > 1/3$ while keeping the QCD trace anomaly positive at all densities, provided the coupling runs from a stiff/repulsive regime at low densities to a soft/non-repulsive regime at high densities through a cross-over after the first-order chiral transition.

## Method

The author employs the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with two quark flavors, extended by a repulsive vector channel parametrized by $G_V$. A Woods-Saxon type ansatz is introduced for $G_V(\mu)$ that interpolates between $G_V(0) = G_S/3$ at low chemical potential and $G_V \to 0$ at high chemical potential, with the drop centered at $\mu_0 = 500$ MeV and thickness $\delta = 10$ MeV. The speed of sound is computed from the baryon density via $V_s^2 = n_B/[\mu_B (d n_B/d\mu_B)]$ at zero temperature using standard mean-field evaluation, and the resulting equation of state is applied to non-strange quark stars via the TOV equations under $\beta$-stability and charge neutrality.

## Key numbers

- The speed of sound peak occurs at $V_s^2 = 0.39$ at baryon density $n_B = 3.25 \, n_0 = 0.52 \, \text{fm}^{-3}$.
- The first-order chiral transition occurs at $n_B \simeq 2.5 \, n_0$.
- A cross-over from stiff to soft EoS takes place at $n_B \simeq 6 \, n_0$, corresponding to $\mu = 508.7$ MeV in the quark number susceptibility.
- The cross-over in the EoS occurs at energy density $\epsilon = 1286 \, \text{MeV fm}^{-3}$.
- The maximum mass-radius ratios for non-strange quark stars are 0.18 for $G_V=0$, 0.19 for $G_V=G_S/3$, and 0.15 for $G_V(\mu)$.
- The NJL model parameters used: $m = 5.6$ MeV, $\Lambda = 587.9$ MeV, $G_S \Lambda^2 = 2.44$, with $M(0) = 400$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the recent statistical analysis by Sinar et al. (2021) and the trace anomaly conjecture by Fukushima (2022) by providing a concrete microscopic model that demonstrates a non-conformal peak in $V_s^2$ is compatible with a positive trace anomaly at all densities. The approach contrasts with hybrid EoS models (Theo5, Theo6, Theo7, Theo8) where the peak arises from the hadronic component, and with fixed-$G_V$ NJL treatments (Buballa, 2005; Fukushima, 2008) that produce monotonically increasing $V_s^2$. The results enable further studies of quark matter in neutron stars using running couplings, and suggest that NJL models with fixed $G_V$ produce EoS that disagree with pQCD predictions at asymptotically high densities.
