# Constraining the density dependence of the symmetry energy with nuclear data and astronomical observations in the KIDS framework

> The KIDS framework for the nuclear equation of state (EoS) and energy density functional (EDF) offers the possibility to explore symmetry-energy (SE) parameters such as J (value at saturation density), L (slope), Ksym (curvature) and so on independently of each other and of assumptions about the effective mass. Here we examine the performance of EoSs with different SE parameters in reproducing nuclear properties and astronomical observations in an effort to constrain especially L and Ksym or the droplet-model counterpart Ktau. Assuming a standard EoS for symmetric matter, we explore several points on the hyperplane of (J,L,Ksym or Ktau) values. For each point, the corresponding EDF parameters and a pairing parameter are obtained for applications in spherical even-even nuclei. This is the first application of KIDS EDFs with pairing correlations. The EoSs are tested successively on properties of closed-shell nuclei, along the Sn isotopic chain, and on astronomical observations, in a step-by-step process of elimination and correction. A small regime of best-performing parameters is determined. The results strongly suggest that Ksym is negative and no lower than -200MeV, that Ktau lies between roughly -400 and -300MeV and that L lies between 40 and 65MeV with L<55MeV more likely. Correlations between symmetry-energy parameters are critically discussed. Predictions for the position of the neutron drip line and the neutron skin thickness of selected nuclei are reported. They are only weakly affected by the choice of effective mass values. Parts of the drip line can be sensitive to the SE parameters. The results underscore the role of Ktau and of precise astronomical input. Better constraints are possible with precise fits to nuclear energies and, in the future, more-precise input from astronomy.

## Key claim

The paper constrains the symmetry-energy parameters \(L\) (slope at saturation) and \(K_{\mathrm{sym}}\) (curvature at saturation) or its droplet-model counterpart \(K_{\tau}\) by testing equations of state (EoSs) against both nuclear data and astronomical observations, finding that \(K_{\mathrm{sym}}\) is negative and no lower than \(-200\) MeV, \(K_{\tau}\) lies between roughly \(-400\) and \(-300\) MeV, and \(L\) lies between 40 and 65 MeV with \(L \lessapprox 55\) MeV more likely, though these ranges depend on the assumption of a fixed symmetric-matter EoS and a fixed skewness parameter \(Q_{\mathrm{sym}} = 650\) MeV.

## Method

The authors used the KIDS (Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU) framework, which allows independent variation of symmetry-energy parameters \(J\), \(L\), and \(K_{\mathrm{sym}}\) (or \(K_{\tau}\)) without coupling to the effective mass. They fixed the symmetric-matter EoS (saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), energy per particle \(-16\) MeV, incompressibility \(K_0 = 240\) MeV) and \(Q_{\mathrm{sym}} = 650\) MeV, then explored a hyperplane of \((J, L, K_{\tau})\) values. For each point, they derived a KIDS energy density functional (EDF) and a pairing parameter, and applied it in spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations for even-even nuclei. The EoSs were tested successively on closed-shell nuclei (binding energies and charge radii of \(^{16}\)O, \(^{40,48}\)Ca, \(^{90}\)Zr, \(^{132}\)Sn, \(^{208}\)Pb, and binding energy of \(^{218}\)U), then on the Sn isotopic chain, and finally on neutron-star properties, in a step-by-step elimination process.

## Key numbers

*   The optimal range for \(K_{\tau}\) (droplet-model curvature parameter) is between roughly \(-400\) and \(-300\) MeV, based on average deviation per datum (ADPD) for closed-shell nuclei.
*   The slope parameter \(L\) is constrained to lie between 40 and 65 MeV, with \(L \lessapprox 55\) MeV more likely, from combined fits to nuclear data and astronomical observations.
*   The curvature parameter \(K_{\mathrm{sym}}\) is found to be negative and no lower than \(-200\) MeV.
*   For the KIDS-P4 EoS, the parameters are \((J, L, K_{\mathrm{sym}}/K_{\tau}, Q_{\mathrm{sym}}) = (33, 49, -157/-374, 583)\) MeV (rounded values).
*   The saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter is fixed to \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), the energy per particle at saturation to \(-16\) MeV, and the incompressibility to \(K_0 = 240\) MeV.
*   The skewness parameter of the symmetry energy is fixed to \(Q_{\mathrm{sym}} = 650\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the KIDS framework, previously applied to closed-shell nuclei and neutron stars (Gil et al., 2017; Papakonstantinou, 2018), by incorporating pairing correlations for the first time and testing against open-shell nuclei (Sn isotopes) and astronomical data. It contradicts the strong linear correlation between \((3J - L)\) and \(K_{\mathrm{sym}}\) enforced by traditional Skyrme functionals (Mondal et al., 2017), showing that the KIDS model can explore these parameters independently. The results enable tighter constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry energy, with predictions for the neutron drip line and neutron skin thickness that are weakly affected by the effective mass, and underscore the need for more precise astronomical input and nuclear energy fits.
