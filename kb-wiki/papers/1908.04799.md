# Dynamical Derivation of the Momentum Space Shell Structure for Quarkyonic Matter

> The phase space structure of zero temperature Quarkyonic Matter is a Fermi sphere of Quark Matter, surrounded by a shell of Nucleonic Matter. We construct a quasi particle model of Quarkyonic Matter based on the constituent quark model, where the quark and nucleon masses are related by m_Q = m_N/N_c, and N_c is the number of quark colors. The region of occupied states is for quarks k_Q < k_F/N_c, and for nucleons k_F < k_N < k_F + Δ. We first consider the general problem of Quarkyonic Matter with hard core nucleon interactions. We then specialize to a quasi-particle model where the hard core nucleon interactions are accounted for by an excluded volume. In this model, we show that the nucleonic shell forms past some critical density related to the hard core size, and for large densities becomes a thin shell. We explore the basic features of such a model, and argue this model has the semi-quantitative behaviour needed to describe neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a quasi-particle model of Quarkyonic Matter, where nucleons have hard-core interactions treated via an excluded volume, produces a nucleonic shell in momentum space that forms only above a critical density related to the hard-core size; at high densities this shell becomes thin, and the model yields a sound velocity with a characteristic maximum near the transition density, which is argued to be semi-quantitatively consistent with neutron star phenomenology.

## Method

The authors construct a quasi-particle model based on the constituent quark model, with quark and nucleon masses related by m_Q = m_N/N_c. They treat hard-core nucleon interactions using an excluded volume approach, where nucleons propagate freely in the volume not occupied by their hard cores. The momentum-space structure consists of a quark Fermi sphere (k_Q < k_F/N_c) surrounded by a nucleon shell (k_F < k_N < k_F + Δ). The energy density is minimized at fixed total baryon density to determine the equilibrium between quark and nucleon contributions, and the sound velocity is computed from the resulting equation of state.

## Key numbers

- The hard core density is set to n_0 = 0.6 fm^{-3} in the mean field example (Fig. 1).
- The mean field model uses parameters γ = 0.7 and κ ∼ 1 for the sound velocity plot.
- The excluded volume density is defined as n_0 = 1/v_0, where v_0 = (4/3)π r_0^3 is the hard core volume.
- The non-perturbative infrared scale Λ is introduced as a parameter of order Λ ∼ Λ_QCD to modify the low-density quark density of states.
- The sound velocity at low density is estimated as v_s^2 ∼ 0.03 for n_N^N/n_0 ∼ 0.25 with κγ ∼ 1.

## Context

This work extends the concept of Quarkyonic Matter introduced by McLerran (2007) to include hard-core nucleon interactions, building on earlier mean-field models by McLerran (2018) and subsequent studies by Philipsen (2019) and Han (2019). The excluded volume treatment follows approaches developed by Zalewski (2015), Redlich (2016), and Vovchenko (2015). The paper enables a dynamical derivation of the momentum-space shell structure and provides a framework for understanding the stiff equation of state needed for neutron star descriptions, connecting to analyses by Kojo (2014) and others who argued that sound velocity exceeds 1/3 at a few times nuclear density.
