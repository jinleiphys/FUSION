# Incompressibility of asymmetric nuclear matter

> The incompressibility $K_sat(δ)$ of isospin asymmetric nuclear matter at its saturation density. Our results show that in the expansion of $K_sat(δ)$ in powers of isospin asymmetry $δ$, i.e., $K_sat(δ)$=K_{0}+K_{sat,2}δ^{2}+K_{sat,4}δ^{4}+O(δ^{6})$, the magnitude of the 4th-order K_{sat,4} parameter is generally small. The 2nd-order K_{sat,2} parameter thus essentially characterizes the isospin dependence of the incompressibility of asymmetric nuclear matter at saturation density. Furthermore, the K_{sat,2} can be expressed as K_{sat,2}=K_{sym}-6L-J_{0}/{K_{0}L in terms of the slope parameter $L$ and the curvature parameter $K_{\mathrm{sym}}$ of the symmetry energy and the third-order derivative parameter $J_0$ of the energy of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density, and we find the higher order $J_0$ contribution to K_{sat,2} generally cannot be neglected. Also, we have found a linear correlation between K_{sym} and $L$ as well as between $J_{0}/K_{0}$ and $K_{0}$. Using these correlations together with the empirical constraints on $K_{0}$ and $L$, the nuclear symmetry energy $E_sym(ρ_{0})$ at normal nuclear density, and the nucleon effective mass, we have obtained an estimated value of K_{sat,2}=-370 +- 120 MeV for the 2nd-order parameter in the isospin asymmetry expansion of the incompressibility of asymmetric nuclear matter at its saturation density.

## Key claim

The 2nd-order parameter \(K_{\mathrm{sat,2}}\) essentially characterizes the isospin dependence of the incompressibility of asymmetric nuclear matter at saturation density, and its estimated value is \(-370 \pm 120\) MeV, but this value has only small overlap with the constraint of \(K_{\tau} = -550 \pm 100\) MeV obtained from giant monopole resonance measurements in Sn isotopes, indicating that the extraction from finite-nuclei data remains ambiguous.

## Method

The authors used three theoretical models: the isospin- and momentum-dependent modified Gogny (MDI) interaction, the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) approach with 63 Skyrme interactions, and a phenomenological modified Skyrke-like (MSL) model. They expanded the incompressibility of asymmetric nuclear matter at saturation density in powers of isospin asymmetry \(\delta\) and derived analytic expressions for the 2nd-order parameter \(K_{\mathrm{sat,2}}\) in terms of the slope parameter \(L\) and curvature parameter \(K_{\mathrm{sym}}\) of the symmetry energy and the third-order derivative parameter \(J_0\) of symmetric nuclear matter. They then used empirical constraints on \(K_0\), \(L\), the symmetry energy at normal density, and the nucleon effective mass, together with linear correlations found between \(K_{\mathrm{sym}}\) and \(L\) and between \(J_0/K_0\) and \(K_0\), to estimate \(K_{\mathrm{sat,2}}\).

## Key numbers

* \(K_{\mathrm{sat,2}} = -370 \pm 120\) MeV for the 2nd-order parameter in the isospin asymmetry expansion of the incompressibility of asymmetric nuclear matter at saturation density, estimated using the MSL model with \(K_0 = 240 \pm 20\) MeV, \(E_{\mathrm{sym}}(\rho_0) = 30 \pm 5\) MeV, \(46\) MeV \(\leq L \leq 111\) MeV, and nucleon effective mass \(m_{s,0}^* = 0.8 \pm 0.1 m\).
* The incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density is \(K_0 = 240 \pm 20\) MeV from analyses of nuclear giant monopole resonances.
* The slope parameter \(L\) of the symmetry energy is constrained to \(46\) MeV \(\leq L \leq 111\) MeV from heavy-ion collision data.
* The nuclear symmetry energy at normal nuclear density is \(E_{\mathrm{sym}}(\rho_0) = 30 \pm 5\) MeV from the empirical liquid-drop mass formula.
* The \(K_{\mathrm{sat,2}}\) parameter varies from \(-251\) MeV to \(-487\) MeV within the MSL model using \(\gamma_{\mathrm{sym}} = 4/3\) to \(5/3\), \(K_0 = 240 \pm 20\) MeV, \(25\) MeV \(\leq E_{\mathrm{sym}}(\rho_0) \leq 35\) MeV, and \(46\) MeV \(\leq L \leq 111\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter, particularly the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, using heavy-ion collision data and nuclear structure measurements. The authors derive a theoretical expression for \(K_{\mathrm{sat,2}}\) in terms of \(K_0\), \(J_0\), \(L\), and \(K_{\mathrm{sym}}\), and find linear correlations between \(J_0/K_0\) and \(K_0\) consistent with the early finding by Pearson (1991), and between \(K_{\mathrm{sym}}\) and \(L\) consistent across multiple models. The estimated \(K_{\mathrm{sat,2}} = -370 \pm 120\) MeV has small overlap with the \(K_{\tau} = -550 \pm 100\) MeV obtained by Li (2007) and Garg (2007) from giant monopole resonance measurements in Sn isotopes, but the authors note that recent studies by Sagawa (2007), Piekarewicz (2007, 2009), and Khan (2009) show that no single model can simultaneously describe the Sn isotope GMR data and the GMR data of \(^{90}\)Zr and \(^{208}\)Pb, making accurate determination of \(K_{\mathrm{sat,2}}\) from these data difficult. This work enables future experimental and theoretical efforts to resolve the open challenge of extracting the incompressibility of asymmetric nuclear matter from finite-nuclei measurements.
