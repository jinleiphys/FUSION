# Coexistence of spherical states with deformed and superdeformed bands in doubly magic 40-Ca; A shell model challenge

> Large scale shell model calculations, with dimensions reaching 10**9, are carried out to describe the recently observed deformed (ND) and superdeformed (SD) bands based on the first and second excited 0+ states of 40-Ca at 3.35-MeV and 5.21-MeV respectively. A valence space comprising two major oscillator shells, sd and pf, can accommodate most of the relevant degrees of freedom of this problem. The ND band is dominated by configurations with four particles promoted to the pf-shell (4p-4h in short). The SD band by 8p-8h configurations. The ground state of 40-Ca is strongly correlated, but the closed shell still amounts to 65%. The energies of the bands are very well reproduced by the calculations. The out-band transitions connecting the SD band with other states are very small and depend on the details of the mixing among the different np-nh configurations, in spite of that, the calculation describes them reasonably. For the in-band transition probabilities along the SD band, we predict a fairly constant transition quadrupole moment Q_0(t)~170 e fm**2 up to J=10, that decreases toward the higher spins. We submit also that the J=8 states of the deformed and superdeformed band are maximally mixed.

## Key claim

Large-scale shell model calculations in a valence space comprising the *sd* and *pf* major oscillator shells (with the 0d₅/₂ orbit blocked) can reproduce the coexistence of spherical, normally deformed (ND), and superdeformed (SD) bands in ⁴⁰Ca, but only after adjusting two global monopole terms and scaling cross-shell off-diagonal matrix elements by a factor of 0.8 to keep the closed-shell component of the ground state at 65%.

## Method

The authors performed shell model calculations using the SDPF.SM effective interaction in the r₂pf valence space (1s₁/₂, 0d₃/₂, 0f₇/₂, 1p₃/₂, 0f₅/₂, 1p₁/₂ orbits), with basis dimensions reaching 10⁹. They first carried out fixed np-nh calculations (4p-4h for the ND band, 8p-8h for the SD band), then performed fully mixed calculations in the full r₂pf space, using the ANTOINE shell model code. The monopole corrections δ₁ = −0.27 MeV and δ₂ = −0.130 MeV were fitted to place the two excited 0⁺ states near their experimental energies.

## Key numbers

- The first excited 0⁺ state (ND band head) is at 3.35 MeV experimentally; the calculation gives 3.49 MeV.
- The second excited 0⁺ state (SD band head) is at 5.21 MeV experimentally; the calculation gives 4.80 MeV.
- The ground state of ⁴⁰Ca has 65% closed-shell (0p-0h) component, 29% 2p-2h, and 5% 4p-4h.
- The ND 0⁺ state has 64% 4p-4h, 25% 6p-6h, and 9% 8p-8h; the SD 0⁺ state has 87% 8p-8h, 9% 4p-4h, and 4% 6p-6h.
- For the SD band in the full calculation, the transition quadrupole moment Q₀(t) is 171 e·fm² at J=2, 169 e·fm² at J=4, 167 e·fm² at J=6, 166 e·fm² at J=8, 158 e·fm² at J=10, then drops to 126 e·fm² at J=12, 127 e·fm² at J=14, and 111 e·fm² at J=16.
- For the ND band in the fixed 4p-4h calculation, Q₀(t) is 116 e·fm² at J=2, 113 e·fm² at J=4, 104 e·fm² at J=6, 97 e·fm² at J=8, corresponding to a deformation parameter β ≈ 0.3.
- For the SD band in the fixed 8p-8h calculation, Q₀(t) is 180 e·fm² at J=2, 178 e·fm² at J=4, 176 e·fm² at J=6, 173 e·fm² at J=8, 168 e·fm² at J=10, corresponding to β ≈ 0.6.
- The correlation energy gain in the fixed np-nh calculations is 9.5 MeV for the 4p-4h 0⁺ state (5.5 MeV from T=1 pairing, 0.5 MeV from T=0 pairing, 3.5 MeV from quadrupole correlations) and 18.5 MeV for the 8p-8h 0⁺ state (5.5 MeV T=1 pairing, 0.5 MeV T=0 pairing, 12.5 MeV quadrupole correlations).

## Context

This work extends the large-scale shell model approach previously applied to ³⁶Ar (Caurier et al., 2005) to the more challenging case of ⁴⁰Ca, which exhibits both deformed and superdeformed bands above a spherical ground state. The calculations build on earlier mean-field studies of np-nh states in ⁴⁰Ca using Hartree-Fock with Skyrme forces (Zamick, 1990s) and on the SU(3) and pseudo-SU(3)/quasi-SU(3) algebraic frameworks developed by Rowe, Elliott, and others. The results enable quantitative predictions for in-band and out-band electromagnetic transitions that can be tested against experimental data from Euroball and Gammasphere, and they provide a microscopic understanding of shape coexistence in doubly magic nuclei.
