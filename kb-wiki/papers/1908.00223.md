# Canonical interpretation of the newly observed $Λ_b(6146)^0$ and $Λ_b(6152)^0$ via strong decay behaviors

> Stimulated by the newly observed $Λ_b(6146)^0$ and $Λ_b(6152)^0$ resonances, we investigate the strong decays of the low-lying singly bottom baryons within the quark pair creation model. Considering their masses and decay modes, we tentatively assign these states as the $λ-$ mode $Λ_b(2S)$, $Λ_b(1D)$, $Σ_b(2S)$, and $Σ_b(1P)$ states, and then estimate their strong decay widths. Compared with the experimental total widths and decay modes measured by the LHCb Collaboration, the $Λ_b(6146)^0$ and $Λ_b(6152)^0$ can be reasonably clarified into the $J^P=5/2^+$ and $J^P=3/2^+$ $Λ_b(1D)$ sates respectively, while other canonical assignments are disfavored. Other low-lying bottom states are also presented, where various narrow states may have good potential to be observed in future LHCb experiments.

## Key claim

The newly observed $\Lambda_b(6146)^0$ and $\Lambda_b(6152)^0$ resonances can be reasonably assigned as the $J^P=5/2^+$ and $J^P=3/2^+$ $\Lambda_b(1D)$ doublet states, respectively, based on their strong decay widths and decay modes; other canonical assignments as $\Lambda_b(2S)$, $\Sigma_b(2S)$, or $\Sigma_b(1P)$ states are disfavored, though the authors note that the predicted total widths are somewhat larger than the experimental values but still acceptable given model and experimental uncertainties.

## Method

The authors used the $^3P_0$ quark pair creation model (also known as the quark-pair-creation model) to calculate the Okubo-Zweig-Iizuka-allowed two-body strong decay widths of excited $\Lambda_b$ and $\Sigma_b$ baryons. They employed simple harmonic oscillator wave functions, a spatially constant pair production strength $\gamma$ determined by reproducing the $\Sigma_c(2520)^{++} \to \Lambda_c \pi^+$ width, and relativistic phase space. The masses of the initial states were taken either from the LHCb experimental data (when testing assignments) or from the relativistic quark model predictions of Ebert (2011) for states not identified with the observed resonances.

## Key numbers

* Mass of $\Lambda_b(6146)^0$: $6146.17 \pm 0.33 \pm 0.22 \pm 0.16$ MeV (LHCb measurement).
* Width of $\Lambda_b(6146)^0$: $2.9 \pm 1.3 \pm 0.3$ MeV (LHCb measurement).
* Mass of $\Lambda_b(6152)^0$: $6152.51 \pm 0.26 \pm 0.22 \pm 0.16$ MeV (LHCb measurement).
* Width of $\Lambda_b(6152)^0$: $2.1 \pm 0.8 \pm 0.3$ MeV (LHCb measurement).
* Predicted total width of $\Lambda_b(6146)^0$ as $\Lambda_b(2S)$ state: $28.23$ MeV, excluding that assignment.
* Predicted total width of $\Lambda_b(6152)^0$ as $\Lambda_b(2S)$ state: $30.28$ MeV, excluding that assignment.
* Predicted total width of $\Lambda_b(6146)^0$ as $J^P=3/2^+$ $\Lambda_b(1D)$ state: $6.17$ MeV.
* Predicted total width of $\Lambda_b(6152)^0$ as $J^P=3/2^+$ $\Lambda_b(1D)$ state: $6.60$ MeV.
* Predicted total width of $\Lambda_b(6146)^0$ as $J^P=5/2^+$ $\Lambda_b(1D)$ state: $5.05$ MeV.
* Predicted total width of $\Lambda_b(6152)^0$ as $J^P=5/2^+$ $\Lambda_b(1D)$ state: $5.44$ MeV.
* Partial decay width ratio $\Gamma[\Sigma_b \pi] : \Gamma[\Sigma_b^* \pi]$ for $J^P=3/2^+$ $\Lambda_b(1D)$ state: approximately $6.1$.
* Partial decay width ratio $\Gamma[\Sigma_b \pi] : \Gamma[\Sigma_b^* \pi]$ for $J^P=5/2^+$ $\Lambda_b(1D)$ state: approximately $6 \times 10^{-3}$.
* Predicted total width of $\Lambda_b(2S)$ state at mass 6089 MeV: $12.56$ MeV.
* Predicted total width of $\Sigma_b(2S)$ state at predicted mass 6213 MeV: $42.53$ MeV.
* Predicted total width of $\Sigma_b^*(2S)$ state at predicted mass 6226 MeV: $43.50$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical interpretation of the two $\Lambda_b$ resonances first reported by the LHCb Collaboration (2019) in the $\Lambda_b^0 \pi^+ \pi^-$ invariant mass distribution. The authors systematically test assignments as $\Lambda_b(2S)$, $\Lambda_b(1D)$, $\Sigma_b(2S)$, and $\Sigma_b(1P)$ states, building on prior quark model mass predictions by Ebert (2011) and Capstick (1986). The results favor the $\Lambda_b(1D)$ doublet interpretation, consistent with the LHCb suggestion, and provide specific branching ratio predictions (e.g., the near-vanishing $\Sigma_b \pi$ mode for the $J^P=5/2^+$ state) that can be tested in future LHCb experiments. The study also clarifies that the previously observed $\Sigma_b(6097)$ is best described as a $J^P=3/2^-$ mixing state with a mixing angle of about $17^\circ$, in agreement with other theoretical works such as Chen (2018) and Wang (2018).
