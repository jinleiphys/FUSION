# Full resummation analysis of jet quenching and tests of the QCD Equation of State

> Jet quenching has become a fundamental tool to study the hot QCD matter produced in heavy ion collisions. While important theoretical and experimental advances have been made in the last two decades, the extraction of the medium properties and the comparison with finite temperature QCD is still particularly worrisome. In this work we show that improvements in the calculation of the medium-induced gluon spectrum are required for a correct extraction of the parameters without temperature issues. In particular, we employ an improved numerical implementation of multiple hard scatterings that resums all terms in the opacity expansion beyond the Gaussian approximation. We find significant differences in the extracted medium parameters when comparing with two of the most used approximations in phenomenological analyses to date, the first order opacity expansion and the Gaussian approximation. We also make a first attempt to compare the extracted medium parameters with lattice results.

## Key claim

Improvements in the calculation of the medium-induced gluon spectrum, specifically a full resummation of multiple hard scatterings beyond the Gaussian approximation, are required for a correct extraction of QGP parameters without the temperature-dependent inconsistencies that have plagued previous phenomenological analyses.

## Method

The author implements a numerical resummation of all terms in the opacity expansion for the medium-induced gluon spectrum, going beyond both the first-order opacity expansion (GLV) and the Gaussian (BDMPS-ASW) approximation. The nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}^h\) is computed using this full resummation and fitted to existing data from Cu-Cu, Au-Au, Pb-Pb, and Xe-Xe collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, with the QGP number density \(\rho(T_0)\) as the single free parameter, while all other medium parameters are fixed by lattice QCD and experimental constraints.

## Key numbers

- Deviation factor of \(K\simeq 1.22\) in opacity, or \(K\simeq 1.29\) in the transport parameter \(\hat{q}\), when going from most central Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{nn}}=2.76\) TeV (LHC) to most central Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{nn}}=200\) GeV (RHIC) using the full resummation.
- This factor is consistent with the value \(K\simeq 1.3\) observed in the Jet Collaboration analysis using the \(N=1\) approximation, while larger factors \(K\simeq 2\) have been found using the Gaussian approximation.
- Reference temperature for most central PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{nn}}=2.76\) TeV set to \(T_0=470\) MeV.
- The relation \(\epsilon\tau_0 = (8.85\pm 0.44)\times \left(\sqrt{s_{nn}}\right)^{0.33\pm 0.02}\) used for energy density scaling between \(\sqrt{s_{nn}}=27\) GeV and 2.76 TeV.
- QGP lifetime set with \(\tau_f=(0.87\pm 0.01)\times(dN_{ch}/d\eta)^{1/3}\) fm/c, with kinetic freeze-out at \(T_f=120\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the line of jet quenching phenomenology that has reported inconsistent deviations in extracted medium opacity between RHIC and LHC energies, as documented by the Jet Collaboration (2014) and Andres et al. (2016). The author shows that the full resummation of multiple hard scatterings resolves the temperature-dependent discrepancies that appear when using either the first-order opacity expansion (GLV) or the Gaussian approximation (BDMPS-ASW). The extracted QGP number density is compared with lattice predictions for the QCD Equation of State from the Wuppertal collaboration (2016), showing agreement with the pressure relation, and potentially enabling future precision studies of the QCD Equation of State through jet quenching.
