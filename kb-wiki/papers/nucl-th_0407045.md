# Tensor analyzing powers in forward angle exclusive $π$-meson photoproduction on deuteron

> The target tensor analyzing powers of the process $γd -> π^{-}pp$ had been studied in the plane wave impulse approximation, if both ejected protons are detected in coincidence and in the directions, symmetrical respect to the incoming photon momentum, being in the proton plane. The matrix elements of the studied reaction become essentially simple, if the magnitudes of the proton momenta p1 = p2 = p are equal. That proton kinematics keeps the ejected pion angle near zero, therefore the one body pion production operator does not include the spin-non-flip term. Moreover, the transition to the triplet, spatially antisymmetric final pp- state is strongly suppressed from the symmetrical deuteron S - state as well as matrix elements have a large dependence from the orientation of the deuteron spin. The values of the $T_{22}(\vec p)$ component, calculated with three realistic deuteron wave functions in the proton momentum region p >= 350 MeV/c and proton polar angle theta >= 40 deg., differ one from another noticeably.

# Tensor analyzing powers in forward angle exclusive π-meson photoproduction on deuteron

## Key claim
In the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) with symmetric proton kinematics (equal momenta and symmetric angles relative to the photon direction), the tensor analyzing power components T₂₀ and T₂₂ for the reaction γd → π⁻pp become sensitive discriminators between realistic deuteron wave functions, particularly for proton momenta p ≥ 350 MeV/c and polar angles θ ≥ 40°, where T₂₂ values calculated with Bonn and Paris potential wave functions differ noticeably from each other and from a parametrization with 8% D-state probability.

## Method
The authors calculated the target tensor analyzing powers T₂₀ and T₂₂ for the reaction γd → π⁻pp within the plane wave impulse approximation, using the special kinematic condition where both ejected protons have equal momenta (p₁ = p₂ = p) and are detected symmetrically with respect to the incoming photon momentum in the proton plane. Under this kinematics, the pion is emitted near zero angle, the elementary pion production operator reduces to only the spin-flip term (iσ·ε), and the transition to the triplet spatially antisymmetric final pp-state is strongly suppressed, allowing the tensor analyzing powers to be expressed purely in terms of the S-wave and D-wave components of the deuteron wave function. Calculations were performed using three realistic deuteron wave functions: the Bonn potential model, the Paris potential model, and a parametrization with 8% D-state probability.

## Key numbers
- The T₂₂ component calculated with the Bonn potential deuteron wave function differs essentially from that calculated with the Paris potential wave function starting from proton momentum p = 350 MeV/c.
- Experimental data were obtained at large ejected proton momenta in the range 300 MeV/c < p < 700 MeV/c.
- The detector angular acceptance was 63° < θ < 83° with Δφ = 40°.
- At the specific condition 1 + 3cos(2θ) = 0 (equivalent to Y₂₀(θ) = 0), the expressions simplify to T₂₀ = (2u²(p) + w²(p))/(2√2(u²(p) + 2w²(p))) and T₂₂ = -√3 w(p)(√2 u(p) + w(p))/(2(u²(p) + 2w²(p))).
- One experimental data point with restricted kinematics (|p₁ - p₂| ≤ 50 MeV/c, |θ₁ - θ₂| ≤ 3.5°) gave T₂₀(⟨p⟩ = 400 MeV/c, ⟨θ⟩ = 71.5°) = 0.68 ± 0.65 and T₂₂(⟨p⟩ = 400 MeV/c, ⟨θ⟩ = 71.5°) = -0.16 ± 0.53.

## Context
This work extends earlier experimental studies of the differential cross section and tensor target asymmetries for γd → π⁻pp using the internal polarized deuterium target at the VEPP-3 storage ring (Loginov et al., 2004), where the experimental yield required integration over five of six independent parameters. The authors previously found that including final state interaction improved agreement with data but could not directly extract the nucleon momentum distribution in the deuteron. The present analysis builds on the theoretical framework of Daresbury (Dar) for single spin asymmetries in d(γ,π)NN reactions and addresses the conclusion from that work that measurements of T₂₀ would not discriminate between deuteron wave functions with different D-state percentages. By exploiting the symmetric proton kinematics, the authors show that T₂₂ measurements in the region p ≥ 400 MeV/c with high precision could in principle select the most suitable deuteron wave function among those with different P_D values. The neglect of final state interaction is the main approximation, and differences between measured and predicted T₂₀ values would indicate contributions from pion-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon rescattering as well as meson exchange currents.
