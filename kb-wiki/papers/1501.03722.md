# The hypercentral Constituent Quark Model and its application to baryon properties

> The hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM) for the baryon structure is reviewed and its applications are systematically discussed. The model is based on a simple form of the quark potential, which contains a Coulomb-like interaction and a confinement, both expressed in terms of a collective space coordinate, the hyperradius. The model has only three free parameters, determined in order to describe the baryon spectrum. Once the parameters have been fixed, the model, in its non relativistic version, is used to predict various quantities of physical interest, namely the elastic nucleon form factors, the photocouplings and the helicity amplitudes for the electromagnetic excitation of the baryon resonances. In particular, the $Q^2$ dependence of the helicity amplitude is quite well reproduced, thanks to the Coulomb-like interaction. The model is reformulated in a relativistic version by means of the Point Form hamilton dynamics. While the inclusion of relativity does not alter the results for the helicity amplitudes, a good description of the nucleon elastic form factors is obtained.

## Key claim

The hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM) provides a simple description of baryon structure using only three free parameters, but its success in reproducing the $Q^2$ dependence of helicity amplitudes depends on the inclusion of a Coulomb-like interaction; the relativistic reformulation via Point Form Hamiltonian dynamics does not alter the helicity amplitude results but improves the description of nucleon elastic form factors.

## Method

The model uses a quark potential expressed in terms of the hyperradius (a collective coordinate) containing a Coulomb-like term and a linear confinement term, with three free parameters fitted to the baryon spectrum. In its nonrelativistic version, the model predicts elastic nucleon form factors, photocouplings, and helicity amplitudes for electromagnetic excitation of baryon resonances. The model is then reformulated relativistically using Point Form Hamiltonian dynamics, and the results for helicity amplitudes and elastic form factors are compared.

## Key numbers

- Three free parameters are used in the hCQM, determined to describe the baryon spectrum.
- The Isgur-Karl model uses a harmonic oscillator constant fitted to the proton r.m.s. radius, obtaining $\alpha^2 = 1.23$ fm$^2$.
- In the Isgur-Karl model, the hyperfine interaction parameter $\delta$ is fixed to about 300 MeV, substantially the $N-\Delta$ mass difference.
- The Isgur-Karl model predicts the ratio $R = -G_{E2}/G_{M1} \approx -0.02$ for the $N-\Delta$ transition, which compares favorably with the experimental value.
- The Goldstone Boson Exchange model uses four free parameters (two for smearing of the $\delta$ function, the $\eta'$ coupling constant, and the linear confinement strength C) to describe the 14 lowest N and $\Delta$ states.

## Context

This review systematically discusses the hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM) and compares it with other major constituent quark models including the Isgur-Karl model (Isgur and Karl, 1978), the Capstick-Isgur model (Capstick and Isgur, 1986), the algebraic U(7) model (Bijl, Iachello, and Leviatan), the Goldstone Boson Exchange model (Glozman and Riska), and the Bonn instanton model. The work extends previous calculations of baryon properties by applying the hCQM to elastic nucleon form factors and helicity amplitudes, and it enables future relativistic calculations of helicity amplitudes for all baryon resonances, with preliminary results for the $\Delta$ resonance already available.
