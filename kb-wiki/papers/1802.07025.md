# A geometry for the shell model

> A geometric interpretation is given of matrix elements of a short-range interaction between states that are written in terms of aligned neutron-proton pairs.

## Key claim

A geometric interpretation of short-range neutron-proton (np) interaction matrix elements can be extended from two-nucleon to four-nucleon configurations when the states are written in terms of aligned np pairs, but the description in terms of aligned np pairs is adequate only for low total angular momentum J and fails for higher J values such as J=8 and 10.

## Method

The author derives analytic expressions for matrix elements of the np interaction in a basis of two identical aligned np pairs (np-np basis) using 12j coefficients of the second kind and unitary 9j coefficients. For a surface delta interaction (SDI), classical approximations to the 12j coefficients are worked out in the high-J limit using the classical limit of 3nj coefficients, and a separate low-J approximation is derived for the 12j coefficient under the assumption that the np pair is aligned. The approximations are tested against exact shell-model calculations for the nucleus 128Cd using the realistic jj45pna interaction in the (ν0h11/2)−2(π0g9/2)−2 model space.

## Key numbers

- For 128Cd with two neutron holes in ν0h11/2 and two proton holes in π0g9/2, the aligned-np-pair approximation (using only the np matrix element V10−νπ) is adequate for low J but fails for J=8 and 10.
- The classical approximation for the matrix element of the SDI is given by ⟨J2max;J|V̂SDIνπ|J2max;J⟩ ≈ 2VJmaxνπ − (3a0+a1)/(π sin θ) + (−)ℓν+jν+ℓπ+jπ (a0−a1)/(π tan θ), where a0 and a1 are isoscalar and isovector strengths and θ is the angle between the angular momenta of the two np pairs.
- For the low-J approximation of the 12j coefficient, the expression involves an exponential with terms including R(R+1), J(J+1), and (jν−jπ)2, and is accurate for J=0 but deteriorates rapidly for increasing J unless single-particle angular momenta are very large.
- In the example of 128Cd, the SDI strengths obtained from a fit to the jj45pna interaction are a0=0.5 and a1=0.13.

## Context

This work extends the geometric interpretation of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, which goes back to Schiffer (1971) and was shown to result from the short-range character of the effective interaction by Molinari (1975), from two-nucleon to four-nucleon configurations. The author builds on prior work by Van Isacker (2014) on the geometry of the shell model for four-nucleon configurations and by Kim et al. on the crucial role of the aligned np matrix element. The results enable a clearer distinction between two approximations: (i) using the full interaction in a restricted space of aligned np pairs, which yields a simple geometric interpretation for short-range interactions, and (ii) using only the aligned component of the np interaction in the full shell-model space, which gives a partially solvable problem for the four-nucleon system.
