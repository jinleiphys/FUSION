# Inclusive $(e,e^\prime N)$, $(e,e^\prime NN)$, $(e,e^\prime π)$ ... reactions in nuclei

> We study the inclusive $(e,e^\prime N)$, $(e,e^\prime NN)$, $(e,e^\prime π)$, $(e,e^\prime πN)$ reactions in nuclei using a Monte Carlo simulation method to treat the multichannel problem of the final state. The input consists of reaction probabilities for the different steps evaluated using microscopical many body methods. We obtain a good agreement with experiment in some channels where there is data and make predictions for other channels which are presently under investigation in several electron laboratories. The comparison of the theoretical results with experiment for several kinematical conditions and diverse channels can serve to learn about different physical processes ocurring in the reaction. The potential of this theoretical tool to make prospections for possible experiments, aiming at pinning down certain reaction probabilities, is also emphasized.

## Key claim

The authors present a Monte Carlo simulation framework that can describe inclusive $(e,e^\prime N)$, $(e,e^\prime NN)$, $(e,e^\prime \pi)$, and $(e,e^\prime \pi N)$ reactions across a range of virtual photon energies from 100 MeV to about 600 MeV, but the agreement with experiment is qualified as "fair globally" with "some punctual discrepancies" for certain data points, rather than the uniformly good agreement suggested by the abstract.

## Method

The authors first evaluate reaction probabilities for the primary interaction of a virtual photon with the nucleus using microscopic many-body methods, separating contributions from 1p1h, 2p2h, 3p3h, 1p1h1π, and 2p2h1π excitations via Cutkosky rules applied to the virtual photon self-energy. They then use a Monte Carlo simulation to trace the subsequent propagation of produced pions and nucleons through the nucleus, accounting for pion quasielastic scattering, pion absorption, nucleon-nucleon collisions, and Pauli blocking, with nucleons moving under a real optical potential derived from the local Fermi energy and a complex potential providing collision probabilities.

## Key numbers

- Virtual photon energy range studied: 100 MeV to about 600 MeV, corresponding to typical energies of the Mainz facility

- For $(e,e^\prime pn)$ at $\omega=400$ MeV: two peaks appear in the missing energy spectrum, one at low energies from primary $\gamma^*$ absorption by a pair of nucleons and a broad peak around 250 MeV from pion production followed by reabsorption

## Context

This work extends the Monte Carlo simulation approach previously applied to real photon reactions by Carrasco and Oset (1992) and the microscopic many-body evaluation of the inclusive $(e,e^\prime)$ reaction by Gil, Nieves, and Oset (1997) to predict exclusive final-state channels. It contrasts with the approaches of the Pavia group (Boffi et al., 1993; Pacati and Radici, 1994) and the Gent group (Janssen et al., 1996), which use distorted waves with complex or real optical potentials but do not explicitly treat pion production and reabsorption channels that dominate two-nucleon emission above $E_\gamma=250$ MeV. The framework enables predictions for channels under investigation at electron laboratories and can help disentangle the contributions of different physical mechanisms such as direct photon absorption, pion production, and final-state interactions.
