# The baryon vector current in the combined chiral and 1/Nc expansions

> The baryon vector current is computed at one-loop order in large-Nc baryon chiral perturbation theory, where Nc is the number of colors. Loop graphs with octet and decuplet intermediate states are systematically incorporated into the analysis and the effects of the decuplet-octet mass difference and SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking are accounted for. There are large-Nc cancellations between different one-loop graphs as a consequence of the large-Nc spin-flavor symmetry of QCD baryons. The results are compared against the available experimental data through several fits in order to extract information about the unknown parameters. The large-Nc baryon chiral perturbation theory predictions are in very good agreement both with the expectations from the 1/Nc expansion and with the experimental data. The effect of SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking for the |Delta S|=1 vector current form factors f1(0) results in a reduction by a few percent with respect to the corresponding SU(3) symmetric values.

## Key claim

The leading SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking corrections to the baryon vector current form factors \(f_1(0)\) for \(|\Delta S|=1\) transitions are computed at one-loop order in large-\(N_c\) baryon chiral perturbation theory, with the hedge that the calculation is performed at leading order in the breaking (\(\mathcal{O}(p^2)\)) and that the SU(3) breaking mass splittings in baryon propagators are disregarded because they are of sub-leading order in the chiral expansion.

## Method

The baryon vector current was computed at one-loop order in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory combined with the \(1/N_c\) expansion, using the spin-flavor symmetric baryon multiplet that includes both octet and decuplet intermediate states. Loop diagrams were evaluated with the decuplet-octet hyperfine mass splitting \(\Delta\) accounted for, and the resulting operator products were reduced using the complete basis of spin-flavor generators truncated at the physical value \(N_c=3\). The theoretical expressions were then compared to experimental data through least-squared fits to extract the unknown low-energy constants.

## Key numbers

- The decuplet-octet hyperfine mass splitting is \(\Delta = \frac{3}{N_c} m_2^{0,\mathbf{1}}\), where \(m_2^{0,\mathbf{1}}\) is the leading coefficient of the \(1/N_c\) expansion of the baryon mass operator, of order \(\mathcal{O}(\Lambda_{QCD})\).
- The pion decay constant is taken as \(F_0 = F_\pi = 93\ \text{MeV}/c^2\).
- The axial coupling \(\mathring{g}_A\) is related to the nucleon axial coupling by \(\mathring{g}_A = \frac{6}{5} g_A\), with \(g_A = 1.27\).
- The SU(3) symmetry breaking correction to \(f_1(0)\) for \(|\Delta S|=1\) transitions results in a reduction by a few percent relative to the SU(3) symmetric values.

## Context

This work extends the combined chiral and \(1/N_c\) expansion formalism previously applied to baryon mass splittings (Jenkins 1995), axial-vector couplings (Flores-Mendieta 2006, 2012), and magnetic moments (Flores-Mendieta 2009, Ahuatzin 2010) to the baryon vector current. It contrasts with earlier chiral perturbation theory calculations by Krause (1990), Anderson and Luty (1993), Villadoro (2006), Lacour, Kubis and Meissner (2007), and Geng, Martin-Camalich and Vicente-Vacas (2009), which found varying signs and magnitudes for the SU(3) breaking corrections; the present analysis provides a framework to resolve these discrepancies and enables future calculations that include sub-leading SU(3) breaking effects and a more rigorous combined expansion scheme.
