# Transverse mass scaling of dilepton radiation off a quark-gluon plasma

> The spectrum of dileptons produced by the quark-gluon plasma in an ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collision depends only, to a good approximation, on the transverse mass M_t of the dilepton. This scaling is exact as long as transverse flow is negligible, and the system is in local thermal equilibrium. We implement a state-of-the-art modelization of kinetic and chemical equilibration in the early stages of the evolution to study the modifications of the spectrum. Violations of M_t scaling resulting from these effects are evaluated as a function of the shear viscosity to entropy ratio (eta/s) that controls the equilibration time. We determine the dependence of the spectrum on system size, centrality, rapidity, and collision energy. We show that the quark-gluon plasma produces more dileptons than the Drell-Yan process up to invariant masses of order M = 4 GeV. Due to different kinematics, for a given M_t , the dependence of the dilepton yield on M is opposite for the two processes, so that experiment alone can in principle determine which process dominates.

# Transverse mass scaling of dilepton radiation off a quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim

The spectrum of dileptons produced by the quark-gluon plasma in ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions depends only on the transverse mass M_t to a good approximation, but this scaling is broken by pre-equilibrium dynamics (kinetic and chemical equilibration) in a way that depends on the shear viscosity to entropy ratio eta/s; the breaking is modest, with the yield for a fixed M_t decreasing as the invariant mass M increases, and the effect is smaller when quark suppression is included.

## Method

The authors model dilepton production from a quark-gluon plasma using leading-order perturbative QCD with a Boltzmann distribution for quarks and antiquarks, modified by an anisotropy parameter xi and a quark suppression factor q_s to account for pre-equilibrium dynamics. These parameters are computed as functions of time using QCD kinetic theory, matched to the pressure anisotropy and quark energy fraction, with the shear viscosity to entropy ratio eta/s as the only free parameter controlling the equilibration time scale. The dilepton spectrum is then obtained by integrating the production rate over the space-time history of the plasma, assuming longitudinal boost invariance and neglecting transverse flow, for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV in the 0-5% centrality range.

## Key numbers

- The transverse area A_perp = 104 fm^2 = 2670 GeV^(-2) and tau T^3 = 10.3 fm^(-2) = 0.40 GeV^2 for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV in the 0-5% centrality range.
- The ideal (McLerran-Toimela) spectrum scales as M_t^(-6), with effective temperature T_eff = M_t/6.
- Pre-equilibrium dynamics suppresses dilepton production by at least a factor 10 for M_t > 5 GeV for eta/s values between 0.16 and 0.32.
- The best-fit parameters for the suppression formula with quark suppression are a = 0.61 GeV^(-2), b = 1.6 GeV^(-2), n = 3.1; without quark suppression, a = 0.07 GeV^(-2), b = 2.4 GeV^(-2), n = 1.2.
- The quark-gluon plasma produces more dileptons than the Drell-Yan process up to invariant masses of order M = 4 GeV at LHC energies.

## Context

This work extends the seminal McLerran-Toimela result (McLerran and Toimela, 1984) by incorporating a state-of-the-art treatment of pre-equilibrium dynamics from QCD kinetic theory, building on the authors' previous work (Coquet et al., 2021) which calculated only the mass spectrum integrated over momentum. The study enables extraction of the shear viscosity to entropy ratio eta/s at early times from the effective temperature of the dilepton spectrum, and provides a parametrization that can be used to separate QGP dilepton production from the Drell-Yan background in experimental data. The results are complementary to hydrodynamic calculations by Deng et al. (2010) that include transverse flow effects, which the authors plan to address in future work.
