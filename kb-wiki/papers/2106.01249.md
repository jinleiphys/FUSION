# Phase-space distributions of nuclear short-range correlations

> Nuclear short-range correlations (SRCs) induce high-momentum/high-energy fluctuations in the nuclear medium. In order to assess their impact on nuclear bulk properties, like nuclear radii and kinetic energies, it is instrumental to determine how SRCs are distributed in phase space as this sheds light on the connection between their appearance in coordinate and momentum space. Using the lowest-order correlation operator approximation (LCA) to include SRC, we compute two-dimensional nuclear Wigner quasiprobability distributions $w(r, k)$ to locate those $({r}, {k})$ phase-space regions that are most heavily impacted by SRCs. The SRC-induced high-momentum components find their origin in a radial range that is confined to the nuclear interior. Significant SRCs strength is generated in the full momentum range $0 \leq k \lesssim 5 ~\text{fm}^{-1} $ covered in this work, but below the Fermi momentum those are dwarfed by the mean-field contributions. As an application of $w(r, k)$, we focus on the radial dependence of the kinetic energy $T$ and the momentum dependence of the radius $r_{\text{rms}}$ for the symmetric nuclei $^{12}$C, $^{40}$Ca and the asymmetric nucleus $^{48}$Ca. The kinetic energy almost doubles after including SRCs, with the largest increase occurring in the nuclear interior $r \lesssim 2$ fm. The momentum dependence of the $r_{\text{rms}}$ teaches that the largest contributions stem from $k \lesssim 2 $ fm$^{-1}$, where the SRCs induce a slight reduction of the order of a few percent. The SRCs systematically reduce the $^{48}$Ca neutron skin by an amount that can be 10\%.

# Phase-space distributions of nuclear short-range correlations

## Key claim

Nuclear short-range correlations (SRCs) induce high-momentum fluctuations that are confined to the nuclear interior (r less than about 2 fm), and while SRC strength appears across the full momentum range 0 to 5 fm^-1, the mean-field contributions dominate below the Fermi momentum; the SRCs systematically reduce the neutron skin of 48Ca by up to 10%, but the overall impact on nuclear radii is modest (a few percent) compared to the near-doubling of kinetic energy.

## Method

The authors use the lowest-order correlation operator approximation (LCA) to incorporate short-range correlations into nuclear wave functions, computing two-dimensional Wigner quasiprobability distributions w(r,k) for the symmetric nuclei 12C, 40Ca and the asymmetric nucleus 48Ca. The LCA imposes universal correlation operators on mean-field Slater-determinant states, truncating many-body operators at the two-body level, and the Wigner distributions are obtained by integrating over the solid angles of spatial and momentum coordinates. The method is applied with two choices for the central correlation function (a hard Reid potential version and a softer Argonne VMC version) and two parameterizations of the harmonic oscillator frequency.

## Key numbers

- The kinetic energy almost doubles after including SRCs, with the largest increase occurring in the nuclear interior at r less than 2 fm.
- SRCs induce a slight reduction of the rms radius of the order of a few percent for momenta k less than 2 fm^-1.
- The SRCs systematically reduce the 48Ca neutron skin by an amount that can be 10%.
- For 40Ca, the LCA gives kinetic energy T and rms radius values that deviate at most 10% from ab initio calculations with the AV18 two-body force (T = 32.29 MeV, r_rms = 3.41 fm).
- The LCA prediction for the 48Ca/40Ca a_2 ratio is 4.89/4.99 = 0.98, in close agreement with the experimental value of 0.971 plus or minus 0.012.
- For 48Ca, all LCA model variants predict the proton kinetic energy is about 1 MeV larger than the neutron kinetic energy (kinetic energy inversion).
- The SRC contribution to the kinetic energy at r = 0 has an uncertainty of about 20% on the SRC contribution and 10% on the LCA result depending on the central correlation function choice.
- The variation of the harmonic oscillator frequency across model variants does not exceed 13%.

## Context

This work extends the study of short-range correlations from momentum distributions (as in Feldmeier 2011, Atti 2015, Hen 2016, Lonardoni 2017) to joint position-momentum phase space using Wigner distributions, following earlier Wigner distribution calculations for finite nuclei by Shlomo (1981) and Prakash (1981). The LCA approach shares assumptions with the generalized contact formalism (GCF) of Weiss (2015) and Cruz-Torres (2019) but shifts complexity from wave functions to operators, enabling application across the nuclear mass table. The results enable quantitative assessment of SRC impacts on nuclear bulk properties like radii and kinetic energies, and the finding that SRC-induced high-momentum components originate in the nuclear interior raises questions about how these interior correlations can be probed in scattering experiments where final-state interactions are strong.
