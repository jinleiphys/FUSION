# Spectral Functions and Nuclear Response

> I discuss the relation between the nuclear response and the Green function describing the propagation of a nucleon in the nuclear medium. Within this formalism, the widely used expressions in terms of spectral functions can be derived in a consistent and rigorous fashion. The results of recent applications to the study of the inclusive electron-nucleus cross section in the impulse approximation regime are brielfy analyzed.

## Key claim

The widely used expression of the nuclear response in terms of spectral functions, often advocated using heuristic arguments, can be derived in a rigorous and fully consistent fashion from the Green function formalism. The accuracy of the zero-th order approximation in the effective interaction is expected to be fairly accurate at momentum transfers above about 500 MeV, but the description of the delta peak at lower momentum transfer shows a deficit of strength that may be due to deficiencies in the description of the elementary electron-nucleon cross section above pion production threshold.

## Method

The author derives the nuclear response from the imaginary part of the polarization propagator and relates it to the nucleon Green function via the Lehman representation, obtaining hole and particle spectral functions. The formalism is applied to uniform nuclear matter with equal numbers of protons and neutrons, using a realistic nuclear hamiltonian that describes nucleon-nucleon scattering data up to pion production threshold, and the response is computed in the impulse approximation regime with corrections for final state interactions obtained from the measured nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude within the eikonal approximation.

## Key numbers

- The zero-th order approximations of the response are fairly accurate at momentum transfers |q| greater than or approximately equal to 500 MeV.
- The hole spectral function of nuclear matter is shown for momenta |k| = 1.0, 0.5, and 1.5 fm^{-1}, with quasiparticle strengths reported.
- Inclusive electron scattering cross section off carbon is shown at beam energy 1.3 GeV and scattering angle 37.5 degrees, as a function of electron energy loss.
- Inclusive electron scattering cross section off oxygen is shown at beam energy 1.2 GeV and scattering angle 32 degrees, as a function of electron energy loss.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Benhar, Fabrocini, and Fantoni (1989, 1992) and Fetter and Walecka, providing a rigorous derivation of the spectral function approach to nuclear response. The results are applied to inclusive electron-nucleus scattering data from Sealock (1989) on carbon and Anghinolfi (1996) on oxygen, and the approach is relevant to the analysis of neutrino oscillation experiments. The paper enables further investigation of deficiencies in the description of the elementary electron-nucleon cross section above pion production threshold, as noted in the NUINT07 workshop.
