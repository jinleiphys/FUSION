# Relativistic configuration-interaction density functional theory: Nonaxial effects on nuclear $ββ$ decay

> The relativistic configuration-interaction density functional theory is developed for even-even and odd-odd nuclei and is used to predict the nuclear matrix element of the neutrinoless $ββ$ ($0νββ$) decay in nucleus $^{76}$Ge, amongst the most promising $ββ$-decay candidates. The nonaxial deformation, i.e., triaxiality, which poses severe challenges in evaluating the nuclear matrix element of $^{76}$Ge, is incorporated within a full model space for the first time. The spectroscopic properties of the $ββ$-decay partners $^{76}$Ge and $^{76}$Se, and the nuclear matrix element governing the two-neutrino $ββ$ ($2νββ$) decay in $^{76}$Ge are well reproduced, providing solid examinations for the validity of theoretical calculations. The inclusion of the triaxial degree of freedom enhances the nuclear matrix element of the $0νββ$ decay significantly by a factor around two. The present results indicate that the goals of next-generation experiments searching for the $0νββ$ decay in $^{76}$Ge can be achieved using only a quarter amount of the experimental materials.

## Key claim

The inclusion of triaxial (nonaxial) deformation in the relativistic configuration-interaction density functional theory enhances the neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix element of ⁷⁶Ge by approximately a factor of two, which implies that the half-life sensitivity goal of next-generation experiments could be achieved with only a quarter of the germanium detector mass previously thought necessary.

## Method

The authors developed the Relativistic Configuration-interaction Density functional (ReCD) theory, which combines a relativistic density functional self-consistent solution with shell-model configuration mixing built on top of that solution. They applied this method to calculate the spectroscopic properties, two-neutrino double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements, and neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements of ⁷⁶Ge and its decay partner ⁷⁶Se, both with and without inclusion of triaxial deformation in a full model space.

## Key numbers

- The calculated triaxial deformation γ at the energy minima is 22° for ⁷⁶Ge and 32° for ⁷⁶Se, compared with experimental values of about 27° and 24°, respectively.
- The two-neutrino double-beta decay nuclear matrix element M²ν is enhanced by around 11% when triaxiality is included.
- The neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix element M⁰ν is enhanced by a factor of approximately two when triaxial deformation is included (from 3.16 to 5.92 with configuration mixing).
- The predicted half-life for ⁷⁶Ge with triaxiality is 1.27×10²⁸ yr, compared to 4.47×10²⁸ yr without triaxiality, for an effective neutrino mass ⟨mββ⟩ of about 10 meV.
- The double Fermi transition NMEs with and without triaxiality are 0.837 and 0.170, respectively; the double Gamow-Teller transition NMEs with and without triaxiality are 1.194 and 0.264, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic density functional theory approach to include triaxial deformation in a full model space for the first time, addressing a limitation of previous calculations for ⁷⁶Ge. The results contradict earlier shell-model calculations (Senkov 2016, Kostensalo 2022, Caurier 2012) and interacting boson model calculations (Barea 2013, 2015) that used limited model spaces and could not reproduce the observed triaxiality. The findings enable more precise predictions for the LEGEND and CDEX next-generation experiments searching for neutrinoless double-beta decay in ⁷⁶Ge, suggesting that less detector material is needed to reach the sensitivity required to probe the normal hierarchy region of neutrino mass.
