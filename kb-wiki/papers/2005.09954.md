# Isoscalar monopole and quadrupole modes in Mo isotopes: microscopic analysis

> The recent RCNP $(α, α')$ data on the Isoscalar Giant Monopole Resonance (ISGMR) and Isoscalar Giant Quadrupole Resonance (ISGQR) in $^{92,94,96,98,100}$Mo are analyzed within a fully self-consistent Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) approach with Skyrme interactions, in which pairing correlations and possible axial deformations are taken into account. The Skyrme sets SkM*, SLy6, SVbas and SkP$^δ$, that explore a diversity of nuclear matter properties, are used. We discuss the connection between the line shape of the monopole strength ISGMR and the deformation-induced coupling between the ISGMR and the $K=0$ branch of the ISGQR. The ISGMR centroid energy is best described by the force SkP$^δ$, having a low incompressibility $K_{\infty}$ = 202 MeV. The ISGQR data are better reproduced by SVbas, that has large isoscalar effective mass $m^*/m$ = 0.9. The need of describing simultaneously the ISGMR and ISGQR data is stressed, with the requirement of suitable values of $K_\infty$ and $m^*/m$. Possible extensions of the QRPA to deal with soft systems are also envisaged.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the low incompressibility value of SkP$^\delta$ ($K_\infty$ = 202 MeV) best reproduces the ISGMR centroid energies in the Mo isotopes, while the SVbas force with a large isoscalar effective mass ($m^*/m$ = 0.9) best describes the ISGQR data; however, the authors caution that the deformation-induced coupling between the ISGMR and the $K=0$ branch of the ISGQR prevents a fully quantitative conclusion on the extracted $K_\infty$ value.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) calculations using two independent methods (QRPA-I and QRPA-II) with four Skyrme energy density functionals (SkM*, SLy6, SVbas, and SkP$^\delta$) that span different values of nuclear matter incompressibility $K_\infty$ and isoscalar effective mass $m^*/m$. Pairing correlations were included via either HF-BCS (QRPA-I) or HFB (QRPA-II) treatments, and axial deformations were taken into account by performing calculations both at spherical minima and at deformation parameters $\tilde{\beta}$ extracted from experimental E2 transition data. The calculated monopole (L=0) and quadrupole (L=2) strength distributions were smoothed with a Lorentz weight of $\Delta$ = 2.5 MeV and compared to the RCNP $(\alpha, \alpha')$ experimental data for $^{92,94,96,98,100}$Mo.

## Key numbers

- SkP$^\delta$ has the lowest incompressibility $K_\infty$ = 202 MeV and reproduces ISGMR peak energies with overestimates of 0.0 MeV ($^{92}$Mo), 0.8 MeV ($^{94}$Mo), 0.5 MeV ($^{96}$Mo), 0.2 MeV ($^{98}$Mo), and 0.1 MeV ($^{100}$Mo).
- SVbas has $K_\infty$ = 234 MeV and $m^*/m$ = 0.9, and reproduces ISGQR peak energies with overestimates of only 0.1-0.5 MeV across the isotopic chain.
- SkM* ($K_\infty$ = 217 MeV, $m^*/m$ = 0.79) overestimates ISGMR peak energies by 0.6-1.8 MeV and significantly overestimates ISGQR peak energies.
- SLy6 ($K_\infty$ = 230 MeV, $m^*/m$ = 0.69) overestimates ISGMR peak energies by 1.0-2.2 MeV and significantly overestimates ISGQR peak energies.
- The deformation-induced coupling shifts the ISGMR peak upward by approximately 0.8 MeV in $^{98}$Mo when comparing spherical ($\beta$ = 0) and deformed ($\beta$ = 0.172) calculations with SkM*.
- Experimental deformation parameters $\tilde{\beta}$ extracted from E2 transitions are 0.109, 0.151, 0.172, 0.168, 0.162 for $^{92,94,96,98,100}$Mo, respectively, though these are considered upper limits.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the RCNP $(\alpha, \alpha')$ data on Mo isotopes (presented in a companion paper by the experimental collaboration, cited as "tbp") beyond the simple RPA calculations that did not include pairing or deformation. The study addresses the longstanding problem of why open-shell nuclei appear softer against compression than $^{208}$Pb, previously discussed by Avogadro, Fluffy3, and PhysRevC.82.024322, and builds on earlier self-consistent Skyrme QRPA analysis of deformation-induced ISGMR/ISGQR mixing in PhysRevC.94.064302. The results enable tighter constraints on the nuclear matter incompressibility $K_\infty$ and isoscalar effective mass $m^*/m$ from simultaneous description of monopole and quadrupole resonances, though the authors note that more advanced methods such as multi-reference DFT (MR-DFT) or projection methods (erler2014collective) are needed for truly soft nuclei with flat potential energy curves.
