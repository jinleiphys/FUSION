# Tsallis statistics approach to the transverse momentum distributions in p-p collisions

> Transverse momentum distributions of negatively charged pions produced in p-p interactions at beam momenta 20, 31, 40, 80 and 158 GeV/c are studied using the Tsallis distribution as a parametrization. Results are compared with higher energies data and changes of parameters with energy are determined. Different Tsallis-like distributions are compared.

## Key claim

The Tsallis distribution provides an excellent description of transverse momentum spectra of negatively charged pions in p-p collisions across a wide energy range, but the extracted temperature and volume parameters are sensitive to the low-pT part of the spectrum and depend on the specific form of the Tsallis-like parametrization used.

## Method

The authors fitted the Tsallis distribution (Eq. 2, with g=1 and μ=0) to two-dimensional (pT, y) spectra of negatively charged pions from p-p collisions at five beam momenta (20, 31, 40, 80, 158 GeV/c) measured by the NA61/SHINE collaboration. They extracted the nonextensivity parameter q, temperature T, and volume V (expressed as radius R) as functions of rapidity and center-of-mass energy, then parametrized the energy dependence of these parameters using power-law functions. The results were compared with higher-energy data from ATLAS, ALICE, and UA1 collaborations at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- Beam momenta studied: 20, 31, 40, 80, and 158 GeV/c, corresponding to center-of-mass energies √s = 6.27, 7.74, 8.76, 12.32, and 17.27 GeV
- Energy dependence of q: q(s) = 1.027 (√s)^0.01326
- Energy dependence of T: T(s) = 0.1014 (√s)^(-0.03262) GeV
- Energy dependence of R: R(s) = 2.31 (√s)^0.09 fm
- Temperature parameter T shows rapidity dependence parametrized as T ≈ 0.09 cosh(y) GeV
- Charged particle dN/dy at y=0: dN/dy ≈ 0.1 + 0.56 (√s)^0.24
- Chemical potential energy dependence: μ(s) ≈ 0.39 (√s)^(-0.022) GeV
- HBT radius related to fitted R by factor κ = 3.5: R_HBT ≈ R/3.5

## Context

This work extends the Tsallis statistics analysis of transverse momentum distributions from RHIC and LHC energies (Cleymans 2011, Cleymans 2013) down to the few-GeV range using NA61/SHINE data (Abgrall 2013). The study enables systematic comparison of nonextensive parameters across five orders of magnitude in collision energy and provides a unified parametrization that can be used to predict particle yields at intermediate energies. The authors also compare different Tsallis-like parametrizations, noting that the form using pT^2 (Eq. 1) differs from those using pT in the low-pT region, and relate their fitted radius to HBT measurements from hadron-hadron reactions (Kageya).
