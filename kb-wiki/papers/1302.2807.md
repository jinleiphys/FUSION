# $μ-H$ Lamb shift: dispersing the nucleon-excitation uncertainty with a finite energy sum rule

> We assess the two-photon exchange contribution to the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen with forward dispersion relations. The subtraction constant $\bar T(0,Q^2)$ that is necessary for a dispersive evaluation of the forward doubly-virtual Compton amplitude, through a finite energy sum rule, is related to the fixed J=0 pole generalized to the case of virtual photons. We evaluated this sum rule using excellent virtual photoabsorption data that are available. We find that the "proton polarizability correction" to the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen is $-(40\pm5)μ$eV. We conclude that nucleon structure-dependent uncertainty by itself is unlikely to resolve the large (300$μ$eV) discrepancy between direct measurement of the Lamb shift in $μH$ and expectations based on conventional Hydrogen measurements.

# μ-H Lamb shift: dispersing the nucleon-excitation uncertainty with a finite energy sum rule

## Key claim

The proton polarizability correction to the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen is −(40±5) μeV, and nucleon structure-dependent uncertainty by itself is unlikely to resolve the large (300 μeV) discrepancy between direct measurement of the Lamb shift in μH and expectations based on conventional Hydrogen measurements. This conclusion depends on using a finite energy sum rule to relate the subtraction constant in the forward doubly-virtual Compton amplitude to the fixed J=0 pole generalized to virtual photons.

## Method

The authors used forward dispersion relations to evaluate the two-photon exchange contribution to the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen. They employed a finite energy sum rule (FESR) to relate the subtraction function T₁(0,Q²) that arises in the dispersive evaluation of the forward doubly-virtual Compton amplitude to the fixed J=0 pole contribution, using virtual photoabsorption data parametrized with Regge theory for high energies and Breit-Wigner resonances plus a smooth background for low and intermediate energies. The total hadronic O(α⁵) contribution was split into three parts: elastic (nucleon pole), inelastic (excited intermediate states), and subtraction term contributions.

## Key numbers

- Total hadronic O(α⁵) contribution to the 2P-2S Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen: −(40±5) μeV
- Elastic contribution ΔE^el: −30.1 μeV (this work); −29.5±1.3 μeV (Carlson and Vanderhaeghen)
- Inelastic contribution ΔE^inel: −13.0 μeV (this work); −12.7±0.5 μeV (Carlson and Vanderhaeghen)
- Subtraction term ΔE^subt: 3.3±4.6 μeV
- Individual resonance contributions to ΔE^subt: Δ(1232) = 0.95±0.09 μeV; S₁₁(1535) = −4.02±3.14 μeV; D₁₃(1520) = 0.41±0.09 μeV; S₁₁(1665) = −0.23±0.16 μeV; F₁₅(1680) = −0.32±0.06 μeV; P₁₁(1440) = 0.10±0.02 μeV; F₃₇(1950) = −0.76±0.26 μeV
- Background contribution to ΔE^subt: −29.34±2.93 μeV
- Regge contribution to ΔE^subt: 36.55±1.6 μeV
- Real-photon fixed pole C_∞(0): (−0.72±0.35) μb·GeV
- Pomeron intercept α_P = 1.097, Reggeon intercept α_R = 0.5; c_P(0) = 68.0±0.2 μb, c_R(0) = 99.0±1.2 μb at ν₀ = 1 GeV
- The discrepancy between muonic and electronic Hydrogen measurements: approximately 300 μeV

## Context

This work extends the dispersive approach of Carlson and Vanderhaeghen (2011) for evaluating two-photon exchange contributions to the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift, and addresses the long-standing uncertainty in the subtraction function T₁(0,Q²) that previous analyses by Pachucki (1996) and Carlson and Vanderhaeghen treated with different phenomenological models. The finite energy sum rule method introduced here provides, for the first time, a prediction of the Q²-dependence of the subtraction function directly from existing experimental data, reducing the systematic uncertainty. The result confirms that nucleon structure effects cannot explain the proton radius puzzle, directing future work toward other possible explanations for the discrepancy between the muonic hydrogen measurement by Pohl et al. (2010) and CODATA values based on electronic hydrogen and electron scattering data from Bernauer et al. (2010).
