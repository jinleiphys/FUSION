# Formation time dependence of femtoscopic $ππ$ correlations in p+p collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=7 TeV

> We investigate femtoscopic $ππ$ correlations using the UrQMD approach combined with a correlation afterburner. The dependence of $ππ$ correlations on the charged particle multiplicity and formation time in p+p collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$= 7 TeV is explored and compared to present ALICE data. The data allows to constrain the formation time in the string fragmentation to $τ_f \leq 0.8$ fm/c.

## Key claim
The paper shows that femtoscopic pion-pion correlation data from ALICE at LHC can constrain the formation time in string fragmentation to values of tau_f approximately 0.3-0.8 fm/c, with the abstract's claim of tau_f <= 0.8 fm/c being a simplified upper bound that the full analysis refines to a range.

## Method
The authors used the UrQMD (v3.3p1) event generator to simulate proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=7 TeV, then applied a correlation afterburner to compute three-dimensional two-pion correlation functions using the Pratt-Bertsch parametrization in the longitudinal comoving system. They extracted HBT radii (R_out, R_side, R_long) as functions of transverse pair momentum K_perp and charged particle multiplicity, comparing results for formation times tau_f = 0.3, 0.8, and 2.0 fm/c against ALICE data.

## Key numbers
- Formation time constraint: tau_f = 0.3-0.8 fm/c is favored, with tau_f >= 2 fm/c ruled out by comparison to ALICE data
- Default UrQMD formation time: tau_f = 0.8 fm/c for hard collisions
- Pseudorapidity acceptance: |eta| < 1.2 for particle selection
- K_perp bins: 100 MeV width covering K_perp = 0.1-1.0 GeV
- Fit range for correlation functions: |q_i| < 800 MeV
- Average dN_ch/deta from UrQMD is 15% smaller than ALICE values across multiplicity classes (e.g., for multiplicity class 1-11: UrQMD 2.52 vs ALICE 3.2; for class 58-149: UrQMD 27.57 vs ALICE 31.1)

## Context
This work extends femtoscopic correlation studies from heavy-ion collisions at RHIC to high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at LHC energies, following earlier HBT studies in pp collisions at RHIC and Tevatron (Alexopoulos 1992). The results enable experimental constraints on the formation time in flux tube fragmentation, a parameter that is often treated as a free constant in models like the Lund string model (Andersson 1983). The analysis builds on previous UrQMD studies of Pb+Pb reactions at LHC (Li 2011, Graef 2011) and addresses the open question of whether space-momentum correlations develop in small systems at high multiplicities, as suggested by Werner (2011).
