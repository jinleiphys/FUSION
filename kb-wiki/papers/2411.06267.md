# Divergence and resummation of the moment expansion for an ultrarelativistic gas in Bjorken flow

> In this letter, we demonstrate for the first time that the moment expansion for an ultrarelativistic gas undergoing Bjorken flow diverges. We then show how this series can be resummed using the Borel-Padé method and use this to determine the single-particle distribution function of the gas. Finally, we compare the exact resummed solution of the single-particle distribution function with solutions of the Boltzmann equation in the hydrodynamic limit and verify that the system displays considerable deviations from local equilibrium.

## Key claim

The moment expansion for the single-particle distribution function of an ultrarelativistic gas undergoing Bjorken flow diverges due to factorial growth of the irreducible moments, contrary to the common assumption of convergence; the series can be resummed using the Borel-Padé method, but the resulting exact solution shows that the system displays considerable deviations from local equilibrium even at late times.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation for a massless classical gas under Bjorken flow, expanding the single-particle distribution function in a complete basis of Legendre polynomials (in the momentum-space angle) and associated Laguerre polynomials (in the energy variable). They convert the Boltzmann equation into an infinite set of coupled ordinary differential equations for the rescaled irreducible moments \(\chi_{n,\ell}\), solve these equations numerically for \(n=0,\ldots,40\) and \(\ell=0,\ldots,20\) with equilibrium initial conditions at three initial rescaled proper times (\(\hat{\tau}_0 = 0.05, 0.5, 1\)), and reconstruct the distribution function via the moment expansion. The divergent series is then resummed using the Borel-Padé method, and the resummed multipoles are compared with the 14-moment approximation and the first-order Chapman-Enskog solution.

## Key numbers

* The moment expansion diverges for rescaled proper times \(\hat{\tau}/\hat{\tau}_0 > 8\) in the case of a constant relaxation time (analytically demonstrated in the supplemental material).
* The irreducible moments \(\chi_{n,1}\) increase significantly in magnitude as \(n\) increases, with this behavior becoming more pronounced for smaller initial rescaled proper times \(\hat{\tau}_0 = 0.05, 0.5, 1\).
* The expansion coefficients \(c_{n,1}\) increase with \(n\) for intermediate values of the rescaled proper time, guaranteeing divergence of the multipole expansion at least for those times.
* For the Borel-Padé resummation, the Padé approximant denominator order is set to \(s=2\) for the \(\ell=0\) and \(\ell=2\) multipoles, and \(s=4\) for the \(\ell=1\) multipole, to avoid spurious poles.
* The resummed multipoles are computed at \(\hat\tau = 1, 5, 10\) for \(\hat{\tau}_0 = 0.5\) and shown to display convergent behavior.

## Context

This work extends the investigation of the method of moments, originally developed by Grad (1949) for non-relativistic gases and generalized to relativistic systems by Israel and Stewart (1970s), by testing its convergence for the first time in a Bjorken flow scenario. The authors show that the 14-moment approximation, widely used in heavy-ion collision modeling (e.g., by the JETSCAPE collaboration, 2020), is not in good agreement with the exact resummed solution, and that the first-order Chapman-Enskog solution (Chapman and Cowling, 1970) performs better but still shows deviations. The results challenge the reliability of truncated moment expansions in fluid-dynamical models of heavy-ion collisions and open the question of how to properly derive transient fluid dynamics from the Boltzmann equation without assuming convergence of the moment series.
