# Observing many body effects on lepton pair production from low mass enhancement and flow at RHIC and LHC energies

> The $ρ$ spectral function at finite temperature calculated using the real-time formalism of thermal field theory is used to evaluate the low mass dilepton spectra. The analytic structure of the $ρ$ propagator is studied and contributions to the dilepton yield in the region below the bare $ρ$ peak from the different cuts in the spectral function are discussed. The space-time integrated yield shows significant enhancement in the region below the bare $ρ$ peak in the invariant mass spectra. It is argued that the variation of the inverse slope of the transverse mass ($M_T$) distribution can be used as an efficient tool to predict the presence of two different phases of the matter during the evolution of the system. Sensitivity of the effective temperature obtained from the slopes of the $M_T$ spectra to the medium effects are studied.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed enhancement of dilepton yield in the low invariant mass region (below the bare $\rho$ peak) arises from many-body effects in the thermal medium, specifically from the Landau cut contributions of $\pi-\omega$, $\pi-h_1$, and $\pi-a_1$ loops in the $\rho$ spectral function, and that the inverse slope of the transverse mass ($M_T$) distribution can serve as a tool to distinguish QGP and hadronic phases during the system evolution.

## Method

The authors calculated the $\rho$ meson spectral function at finite temperature using the real-time formalism of thermal field theory, with interaction vertices from chiral perturbation theory and one-loop self-energy diagrams involving $\pi-h$ loops ($h = \pi, \omega, a_1, h_1$). The dilepton emission rate was obtained from the imaginary part of the $\rho$ propagator, including contributions from unitary and Landau cuts, and the space-time evolution of the fireball was modeled using ideal relativistic hydrodynamics with two different equations of state (hadronic resonance gas with bag model for QGP, and lattice QCD) for RHIC and LHC initial conditions.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature $T_i = 320$ MeV with thermalisation time $\tau_i = 0.2$ fm/c for RHIC ($\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV) Au+Au collisions
- Initial temperature $T_i = 686$ MeV with $\tau_i = 0.1$ fm/c for LHC Pb+Pb collisions (0-10% centrality)
- Phase transition temperature $T_c = 175$ MeV; kinetic freeze-out temperature $T_F = 120$ MeV; chemical freeze-out temperature $T_{ch} = 170$ MeV
- Enhancement in dilepton yield observed in the invariant mass region $0.1 \leq M \leq 0.7$ GeV from medium effects
- Coupling constants $F_R = 0.156$ GeV ($\rho$), $0.046$ GeV ($\omega$), $0.079$ GeV ($\phi$) from partial decay widths into $e^+e^-$
- Chemical potentials at $T_F = 120$ MeV: $\mu_\pi = 68$ MeV, $\mu_\omega = 179$ MeV, $\mu_{h_1} = 204$ MeV, $\mu_{a_1} = 204$ MeV, $\mu_\phi = 252$ MeV, $\mu_{\text{proton}} = 258$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of in-medium $\rho$ spectral function calculations by Rapp and collaborators (2000s) and the NA60 experimental results at CERN SPS, which showed an excess of dimuon pairs below the $\rho$ peak in In-In collisions attributed to $\rho$ broadening. The authors provide a unified description of scattering and decay processes in the medium via branch cuts in the complex energy plane, building on their earlier work (Ghosh et al., EPJC). The study enables future extraction of QGP flow properties by selecting appropriate invariant mass and transverse momentum windows, and tests sensitivity to different equations of state (HRG+bag model vs. lattice QCD from MILC Collaboration) for RHIC and LHC energies.
