# Inelasticity resulting from rapidity spectra analysis

> In this work we study the pseudorapidity spectra o charged particles produced in proton+proton and proton+antiproton interactions in a wide energy range using the non-extensive Tsallis approach. We evaluate the inelasticity coefficients of the discussed reactions which remain approximately independent of the collision energy.

## Context

This work extends earlier estimates of inelasticity from cosmic ray experiments (Zatsepin, 1940s; Cocconi, 1958) and from the Bologna-CERN-Frascati Collaboration at ISR (Basile, 1980) by applying a Tsallis non-extensive approach to a wider energy range up to 8000 GeV. The result that inelasticity remains constant at K вүҲ 0.5 contradicts some QCD-inspired models that predicted a decrease with energy (Wlodarczyk, 1995; Beggio, 2020) and others that predicted an increase (Shabelski, 1991). The analysis enables a model-independent extraction of inelasticity from rapidity spectra using only one free parameter (longitudinal temperature), and supports the use of constant inelasticity in cosmic ray cascade simulations and in statistical/hydrodynamical models of multiparticle production (Carruthers, 1983; Shuryak, 1984).
