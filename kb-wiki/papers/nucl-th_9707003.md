# Pion mass and decay constant

> Independent of assumptions about the form of the quark-antiquark scattering kernel we derive the explicit relation between the pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, Gamma_pi, and the quark propagator in the chiral limit; Gamma_pi necessarily involves a non-negligible gamma_5 gamma.P term (P is the pion four-momentum). We also obtain exact expressions for the pion decay constant, f_pi, and mass, both of which depend on Gamma_pi; and demonstrate the equivalence between f_pi and the pion Bethe-Salpeter normalisation constant in the chiral limit. We stress the importance of preserving the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity in any study of the pion itself, and in any study whose goal is a unified understanding of the properties of the pion and other hadronic bound states.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact, model-independent relations between the pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude and the quark propagator in the chiral limit, showing that the pion necessarily contains a non-negligible \(\gamma_5\gamma\cdot P\) term; these relations hold independent of any assumptions about the form of the quark-antiquark scattering kernel, provided the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity is preserved.

## Method

The authors employ the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) and the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for the pion, together with the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity (AV-WTI), to derive exact chiral-limit relations between the Dirac components of the pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude and the dressed-quark propagator functions \(A(p^2)\) and \(B(p^2)\). They then extract the residues of the pion pole in the axial-vector and pseudoscalar vertices, identifying them with the pion decay constant \(f_\pi\) and the vacuum quark condensate, respectively, and obtain the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation without any truncation of the scattering kernel.

## Key numbers

- Constituent-quark mass estimate: \(M_q \simeq 350\) MeV, with the ratio \(m_\pi/2 \approx 0.2 M_q\) illustrating the pion's anomalously low mass.
- Renormalisation-group-invariant current-quark mass for \(u\)- and \(d\)-quarks: \(\hat m \sim 10\) MeV.
- Euclidean constituent-quark mass from model studies: \(M^E_{u,d} \approx 400\) MeV.
- Neglecting the subleading Dirac components \(F_\pi\), \(G_\pi\), and \(H_\pi\) in solving the pion BSE leads to an underestimation of \(m_\pi\) by about 20% (eigenvalue reduction of approximately 40%).

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to the pion, building on the framework reviewed in DSE reviews and adapting the discussion of Jona-Lasinio and collaborators (1973). It provides a model-independent derivation of the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation, \(f_\pi^2 m_\pi^2 = 2 m_{(\mu)} \langle \bar q q \rangle_\mu^0\), which was previously obtained only in specific models such as that of Miransky (1993). The results enable systematic truncations of the quark-antiquark scattering kernel, such as the rainbow-ladder approximation, to be tested and improved, and they clarify why models that violate the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity require fine-tuning to describe the pion as a nearly-massless bound state.
