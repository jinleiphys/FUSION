# Perfect-fluid hydrodynamics with constant acceleration along the stream lines and spin polarization

> A global equilibrium state of a spin polarized fluid that undergoes constant acceleration along the stream lines is described as a solution of recently introduced perfect-fluid hydrodynamic equations with spin 1/2.

## Key claim

A global equilibrium state of a spin-polarized perfect fluid undergoing constant acceleration along stream lines is a solution of recently introduced hydrodynamic equations with spin 1/2, but only if the ratio of acceleration to temperature satisfies a bound that ensures positive energy density.

## Method

The authors consider a one-dimensional hydrodynamic flow with four-velocity parametrized by constants A, B, and g, where the fluid four-acceleration is constant along stream lines. They construct phase-space spin density matrices for spin-1/2 particles using an ansatz with a spin polarization tensor, then derive thermodynamic variables (charge density, energy density, pressure, entropy density) by analytic continuation of previous results. The hydrodynamic equations with spin are solved by requiring that temperature, chemical potential, and the spin-related variable Omega all scale as 1/tau, where tau is the proper time along stream lines.

## Key numbers

- The four-acceleration magnitude is a = g/tau, with a^mu a_mu = -g^2/tau^2
- Temperature scales as T(tau) = T_0 * tau_0/tau
- The spin polarization tensor component omega_{03} = 2 Omega/T is constant, with omega_{03} = 2 Omega_0/T_0
- The condition for positive energy density requires g/(T_0 tau_0) <= pi, equivalently a/pi <= T
- The limiting temperature for the spin-1/2 fluid is T = a/pi, differing from the scalar field Unruh temperature T_U = a/(2pi)

## Context

This work extends the framework of perfect-fluid hydrodynamics with spin introduced by Florkowski et al. (2017) to include accelerating flows, complementing the rotating global equilibrium solutions previously studied. It builds on the analysis of accelerating fluids for real scalar fields by Becattini (2017), where a minimal proper temperature T_U = a/2pi was found. The results enable further investigation of spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the relation between polarization and thermal vorticity, and suggest that an exact treatment of the Dirac field at finite temperature and acceleration would likely recover the scalar field Unruh-like behavior.
