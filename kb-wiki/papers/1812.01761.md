# The 9Li(d,p) reaction, a specific probe of 10Li, paradigm of parity--inverted nuclei around N=6 closed shell

> We show, within the framework of renormalized nuclear field theory and of the induced reaction surrogate formalism, that the highly debated $^{10}$Li structure, observed in a recent $^9$Li(d,p)$^{10}$Li one--neutron transfer experiment is consistent with or better, requires the presence of a virtual $1/2^+$ state of similar single--particle strength than that of the $1/2^-$ resonance at 0.45$\pm$ 0.03 MeV. Based on continuum spectroscopy self-energy techniques, we find that the physical mechanism responsible for parity inversion in $^{10}_3$Li is the same as that at the basis of the similar phenomenon observed in $^{11}_4$Be and to that needed in $^{11}$Li to have an important $s$--wave ground state component. Furthermore, it is also consistent with the (normal) sequence of the $1p_{1/2}$ and $2s_{1/2}$ levels in the $N=7$ isotones $^{12}_5$B and $^{13}_6$C.

## Key claim

The authors show that the highly debated structure of \(^{10}\)Li, observed in a recent \(^{9}\)Li(d,p)\(^{10}\)Li one-neutron transfer experiment, is consistent with or requires the presence of a virtual \(1/2^{+}\) state of similar single-particle strength to the \(1/2^{-}\) resonance at 0.45 ± 0.03 MeV, provided one looks at excitation energies \(E_x \leq 0.2\) MeV and at a different angular region (\(\theta_{cm} > 40^{\circ}\)) than that employed in the experiment (5.5°–16.5°).

## Method

The authors employed renormalized nuclear field theory and the induced reaction surrogate formalism, using continuum spectroscopy self-energy techniques to calculate the dressing of single-particle states through coupling to the quadrupole vibration of the \(^{9}\)Li core. They extrapolated the bare mean-field parameters determined from fitting the \(^{11}\)Be, \(^{12}\)B, and \(^{13}\)C spectra to the case of \(^{10}\)Li, then calculated absolute double differential cross sections \(d^{2}\sigma/dEd\Omega\) for the \(^{9}\)Li(d,p)\(^{10}\)Li reaction and integrated them over the experimental angular and energy ranges.

## Key numbers

- The \(1/2^{-}\) resonance in \(^{10}\)Li is observed at 0.45 ± 0.03 MeV
- The virtual \(\widetilde{1/2^{+}}\) state has a scattering length \(\alpha = -8\) fm, corresponding to an energy \(\epsilon_{\widetilde{1/2}^{+}} = 0.32\) MeV
- The \(\widetilde{1/2^{-}}\) resonance has centroid \(\epsilon_{\widetilde{1/2}^{-}} = 0.50\) MeV and width \(\Gamma_{\widetilde{1/2}^{-}} = 0.35\) MeV
- The \(\widetilde{1/2^{+}}\) state wavefunction is \(98\%\) \(|s_{1/2}\rangle\) and \(2\%\) \(|(d_{5/2} \otimes 2^{+})_{1/2^{+}}\rangle\)
- The \(\widetilde{1/2^{-}}\) state wavefunction is \(94\%\) \(|p_{1/2}\rangle\) and \(7\%\) \(|((p_{1/2},p_{3/2}^{-1})_{2^{+}} \otimes 2^{+})_{0^{+}}, p_{1/2};1/2^{-}\rangle\)
- The scattering lengths of the \(2^{-}\) and \(1^{-}\) states (from the \(s_{1/2}\) doublet) are -19 fm and -5 fm respectively, with energies \(\epsilon_{2^{-}} \approx 0.05\) MeV and \(\epsilon_{1^{-}} \approx 0.8\) MeV
- The positive parity doublet has energies \(\epsilon_{1^{+}} \approx 0.3\) MeV and \(\epsilon_{2^{+}} \approx 0.6\) MeV
- The quadrupole vibration parameters of \(^{9}\)Li are \(\hbar\omega_2 = 3.37\) MeV and \(\beta_2 = 0.72\)
- Theory predicts a resonant \(5/2^{+}\) state with centroid at approximately 3.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework previously applied to \(^{11}\)Be by Barranco (2017) and to \(^{11}\)Li by Barranco (2001) and Potel (2010), demonstrating that the same parity-inversion mechanism operates in \(^{10}\)Li. The results directly address the challenge posed by the Cavallaro (2017) experiment, which questioned whether shell inversion occurs in \(^{10}\)Li, and show that the apparent absence of \(s\)-wave strength was due to the restricted angular window of that measurement. The findings enable future experiments to search for the virtual \(1/2^{+}\) state at backward angles (\(\theta_{cm} > 40^{\circ}\)) and confirm the unified picture of \(N=7\) isotones from \(^{13}\)C through \(^{10}\)Li.
