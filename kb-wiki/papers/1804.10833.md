# Description of Nuclei with Magic Number Z(N) = 6

> Encouraged with the evidence for Z = 6 magic number in neutron-rich carbon isotopes, we have performed relativistic mean-field plus BCS calculations to investigate ground state properties of entire chains of isotopes(isotones) with Z(N) = 6 including even and odd mass nuclei. Our calculations include deformation, binding energy, separation energy, single particle energy, rms radii along with charge and neutron density profile etc., and are found in an excellent match with latest experimental results demonstrating Z = 6 as a strong magic number. N = 6 is also found to own similar kind of strong magic character.

## Key claim

The paper claims that both Z = 6 and N = 6 are strong magic numbers across the entire chains of isotopes and isotones, from the neutron drip-line to the proton drip-line, based on relativistic mean-field plus BCS calculations. The hedge is that the claim rests on theoretical calculations that show "excellent match" with available experimental data, but the experimental data are limited to specific nuclei and the full chain includes many nuclei with no experimental confirmation.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations with axially symmetric deformed shapes using the NL3* and PK1 force parameters, combined with a state-dependent BCS pairing treatment using a zero-range delta force with strength V0 = 350 MeV fm^3. They calculated ground state properties including binding energies, quadrupole deformations, pairing energies, separation energies, single-particle energies, and charge and neutron density distributions for all isotopes with Z = 6 (carbon) and all isotones with N = 6. The spherical RMF framework was used for nuclei identified as spherical to examine single-particle level structures and density profiles.

## Key numbers

- The energy gap between proton 1p3/2 and 1p1/2 single-particle states is always greater than 7 MeV for all Z = 6 isotopes from neutron drip-line to proton drip-line.
- Binding energy of 12C: 89.86 MeV (NL3*), 90.00 MeV (PK1), experimental 92.16 MeV.
- Binding energy of 14C: 106.11 MeV (NL3*), 106.21 MeV (PK1), experimental 105.28 MeV.
- Binding energy of 22C: 122.95 MeV (NL3*), 118.78 MeV (PK1), experimental 119.28 MeV.
- Charge radius of 12C: 2.42 fm (NL3*), 2.39 fm (PK1), experimental 2.47 +/- 0.002 fm.
- Charge radius of 14C: 2.52 fm (NL3*), 2.48 fm (PK1), experimental 2.50 +/- 0.009 fm.
- Quadrupole deformation beta2 for 12C: 0.000 (both NL3* and PK1), indicating spherical shape.
- Quadrupole deformation beta2 for 22C: 0.000 (both NL3* and PK1), indicating spherical shape.
- Pairing energy vanishes for protons in all C isotopes and for neutrons in all N = 6 isotones.

## Context

This work extends the experimental evidence for Z = 6 magicity in neutron-rich carbon isotopes reported by Tran et al. (2017) and the earlier suggestion of N = 6 sub-shell closure by Kanungo et al. (2002). It also builds on previous theoretical studies using the Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula by Samanta et al. and RMF calculations by Jha et al. The calculations identify 12C, 14C, 22C, 8He, and 14O as doubly magic nuclei, consistent with earlier reports by Skaza et al. (2006) for 8He and Brown et al. for 14O. The results provide a systematic theoretical foundation for future experimental investigations of magicity at Z = 6 and N = 6 across the full isotopic and isotonic chains.
