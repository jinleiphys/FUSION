# New Types of Hydrogenlike matter Composed of Electron(s) and Meson(s)

> In the present work, we predict the existence of new types of hydrogenlike matter, including hydrogenlike atoms ($π^+e^-$, $K^+e^-$, $D^+e^-$), hydrogenlike molecular ions ($π^+π^+e^-$, $K^+K^+e^-$, $D^+D^+e^-$) and hydrogenlike molecules ($π^+π^+e^-e^-$, $K^+K^+e^-e^-$, $D^+D^+e^-e^-$). By solving the Schrödinger equation, the binding energy of hydrogenlike atoms is obtained as $E_n=-\frac{1}{2n^2}$. For hydrogenlike molecular ions and molecules, the variational method is employed to calculate the binding energies, i.e., $E_+=-0.587$ and $E_0=-1.139$ for hydrogenlike molecular ions and molecules, respectively. And the bond lengths for hydrogenlike molecular ions and molecules are also calculated, whose values are $2.003$ and $1.414$, respectively. Here all the quantities are in atomic units for convenience. In addition, the strong interaction between the two constituent mesons is considered in our calculations, where we find that its influence on the hydrogenlike molecular ions and molecules can be neglected. Comparisons of hydrogenlike molecular ion and molecule with the systems governed by the strong interaction are made, which suggests the possible existence of doubly heavy triquark, hidden heavy-flavor tetraquarks and doubly heavy tetraquarks. Hopefully, these types of matter would be observed in the future with the improvement of accuracy in the high energy physical experiments.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that hydrogenlike atoms, molecular ions, and molecules formed by replacing the proton(s) with positively charged mesons (π⁺, K⁺, D⁺) should exist as bound states, but only under the condition that the strong interaction between the two mesons can be neglected for the molecular systems, and that for molecular ions only the even-parity spatial wave function yields a bound state.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrödinger equation analytically for the hydrogenlike atom (Me⁻) systems, obtaining the standard hydrogenic energy levels. For the hydrogenlike molecular ion (MMe⁻) and hydrogenlike molecule (MMe⁻e⁻) systems, they employed the Born-Oppenheimer approximation and the variational method with trial wave functions composed of superpositions of hydrogenic 1s orbitals, treating the meson-meson distance R and the effective nuclear charge λ as variational parameters. The strong interaction between the two mesons was then added as an additional potential calculated from effective Lagrangians with vector-meson exchange (ρ⁰, ω, φ) using the Breit approximation and Fourier transformation with an exponential form factor.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of hydrogenlike atoms: E_n = -1/(2n²) in atomic units (e.g., E₁ = -0.5 a.u.)
- Binding energy of hydrogenlike molecular ions (π⁺π⁺e⁻, K⁺K⁺e⁻, D⁺D⁺e⁻): E₊ = -0.587 a.u.
- Binding energy of hydrogenlike molecules (π⁺π⁺e⁻e⁻, K⁺K⁺e⁻e⁻, D⁺D⁺e⁻e⁻): E₀ = -1.139 a.u.
- Bond length for hydrogenlike molecular ions: R = 2.003 a.u.
- Bond length for hydrogenlike molecules: R = 1.414 a.u.
- Variational parameter λ = 1.238 for molecular ions; λ = 1.166 for molecules

## Context

This work extends prior predictions of π⁺π⁺e⁻e⁻ and K⁺K⁺e⁻e⁻ bound states by Rebane (2012) and symmetry studies by Rebane (2012), as well as predictions of hydrogenlike charm-pion or charm-kaon matter by Luo (2023). The authors also draw analogies between these QED-bound systems and QCD-bound systems, suggesting that the repulsion-attraction pattern in hydrogenlike molecular ions mirrors that of doubly heavy triquarks, while the pattern in hydrogenlike molecules mirrors that of hidden heavy-flavor tetraquarks and doubly heavy tetraquarks. The work enables future experimental searches at facilities such as the proposed Super τ-Charm Facility (STCF), with possible production channels including K_L⁰ → π⁺e⁻ν_e for π⁺e⁻ atoms.
