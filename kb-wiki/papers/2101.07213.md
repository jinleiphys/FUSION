# Heavy-hadron molecular spectrum from light-meson exchange saturation

> If known, the spectrum of heavy-hadron molecules will be a key tool to disentangle the nature of the exotic states that are being discovered in experiments. Here we argue that the general features of the molecular spectrum can be deduced from the idea that the short-range interaction between the heavy hadrons is effectively described by scalar and vector meson exchange, i.e. the $σ$, $ω$ and $ρ$ mesons. By means of a contact-range theory where the couplings are saturated by the aforementioned light mesons we are indeed able to postdict the $X(3872)$ (as a $D^* \bar{D}$ molecule) from the three $P_c(4312)$, $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ pentaquarks (as $\bar{D}Σ_c$ and $\bar{D}^* Σ_c$ molecules). We predict a $J^{PC} = 1^{--}$ $D \bar{D}_1$ molecule at $4240-4260\,{\rm MeV}$ which might support the hypothesis that the $Y(4260)$ is at least partly molecular. The extension of these ideas to the light baryons requires minor modifications, after which we recover approximate SU(4)-Wigner symmetry in the two-nucleon system and approximately reproduce the masses of the deuteron and the virtual state.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the short-range interaction between heavy hadrons can be effectively described by scalar and vector meson exchange (sigma, omega, and rho mesons) within a contact-range theory, and that this framework can postdict the X(3872) from the three P_c pentaquarks, but the proportionality constant between the contact-range coupling and the saturation ansatz must be fixed by solving the bound state equation for a known molecular candidate such as the P_c(4312).

## Method

The authors construct a contact-range potential for two-heavy-hadron systems where the couplings are saturated by scalar and vector meson exchange, using a renormalization group equation to combine contributions from mesons with different masses. They determine the vector meson couplings from Sakurai's universality and vector meson dominance, the magnetic-like couplings from the non-relativistic quark model, and the scalar couplings from the linear-sigma model. The proportionality constant is fixed by reproducing the P_c(4312) as a reference molecule, after which the bound state equation is solved for other molecular candidates using a Gaussian regulator with a physical cutoff of 1 GeV.

## Key numbers

* P_c(4312) reproduced with coupling C_Pc^sat = -0.80 fm^2 at cutoff Lambda_H = 1 GeV
* X(3872) predicted at M = 3871.7 MeV (experimental mass 3871.69 MeV) with binding energy B = 4.1 MeV
* J^PC = 1^{--} D D_1 molecule predicted at M = 4240-4260 MeV, with binding energy B = 19-55 MeV depending on the dipolar coupling kappa_E1' = 2.6-3.6
* Deuteron predicted at M = 1876.9 MeV (experimental 1875.6 MeV) with binding energy B = 0.9 MeV
* Singlet nucleon-nucleon virtual state predicted at M = 1877.7 MeV (experimental pole at 1877.8 MeV)
* P_c(4440) predicted at M = 4445.2 MeV (experimental 4440.3 MeV) with binding energy B = 16.9 MeV
* P_c(4457) predicted at M = 4459.8 MeV (experimental 4457.3 MeV) with binding energy B = 2.3 MeV
* Z_c(3900) configuration predicted as a virtual state at M = 3849.6 MeV
* P_cs-like Xi_c D^* bound state predicted at M = 4466.9 MeV for I=0, J=1/2, 3/2
* Scalar meson mass taken as m_S = 475 +/- 75 MeV; vector meson mass m_V = (m_rho + m_omega)/2 with m_rho = 770 MeV and m_omega = 780 MeV

## Context

This work extends the saturation approach of Peng (2020) to systematically predict the heavy-hadron molecular spectrum, building on earlier molecular interpretations of exotic states by Voloshin (1976) and De Rujula (1976). The framework reproduces the P_c pentaquarks observed by LHCb (Aaij 2019) and the X(3872), and predicts a D D_1 molecule that may correspond to the Y(4260) measured by BESIII (Ablikim 2020). The extension to light baryons recovers approximate SU(4)-Wigner symmetry in the two-nucleon system, reproducing the deuteron and the singlet virtual state. The approach differs from effective field theory in that the cutoff is physical rather than auxiliary, chosen to reproduce the known spectrum.
