# Highlights of the Spectroscopy of Hyperons and Cascade Baryons

> We highlight topical issues in hyperon spectroscopy that will be accessible when a secondary beam of neutral kaons is created in Hall D at Jefferson Lab as proposed to the JLab Program Advisory Committee. The new beam will have a flux of $10^4$ $K_L$/s. The reaction products will be analyzed in the GlueX experimental setup. We point out which physical questions need to be answered and suggest a number of novel experiments. In particular we suggest to measure the presently disputed SU(3) structure of the $Λ(1405)$, to search for Pentaquarks mit exotic quantum numbers and to search for baryons belonging to the SU(6) 20-plet representation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a proposed secondary beam of neutral kaons at Jefferson Lab (flux of \(10^4\) \(K_L\)/s) will enable decisive measurements of hyperon spectroscopy, but the SU(3) structure of the \(\Lambda(1405)\) remains ambiguous because existing data allow two equally good partial-wave solutions, one where it is dominantly an SU(3) singlet and one where it is dominantly an SU(3) octet, and only new data from near-threshold \(K^-p\) scattering can resolve the dispute.

## Method

The authors review the current status of \(\Lambda\), \(\Sigma\), \(\Xi\), and \(\Omega\) baryon spectroscopy from quark-model and coupled-channel perspectives, then propose specific experiments using the GlueX detector with a \(K_L\) beam. They detail how to determine the SU(3) nature of the \(\Lambda(1405)\) by measuring the sign of transition amplitudes in the decay sequences \(\Sigma^+(1670)3/2^- \to \pi^+ \Lambda(1405); \Lambda(1405) \to \Sigma^\pm \pi^\mp\) and comparing with \(\Sigma^+(1670)3/2^- \to \pi^+ \Sigma(1385); \Sigma(1385) \to \Sigma^\pm \pi^\mp\). They also outline searches for pentaquarks with exotic quantum numbers and for baryons belonging to the SU(6) 20-plet representation via cascade decays.

## Key numbers

*   Proposed \(K_L\) beam flux: \(10^4\) particles per second.
*   \(\Lambda(1405)\) pole mass from BnGa partial-wave analysis: \((1421 \pm 3) - i(23 \pm 3)\) MeV.
*   Two isoscalar poles from chiral unitary approach: 1379.2 MeV and 1433.7 MeV, plus one isovector pole at 1444.0 MeV.
*   Expected mass window for \(\Lambda\) and \(\Sigma\) partners of the \((56,1^-_3)\) representation: 2000 to 2100 MeV.
*   Expected mass interval for 45 \(\Sigma\) resonances in the third excitation band: 2000 to 2400 MeV.
*   Number of predicted \(\Lambda\) resonances in the second excitation shell: 19; number observed with 3* or 4* rating: 5.
*   Number of predicted \(\Sigma\) resonances in the second excitation shell: 21; number observed with 3* or 4* rating: 3.
*   Number of predicted \(\Xi\) resonances in the second excitation shell: 21; number observed with any known spin-parity: 0.
*   Number of predicted \(\Omega\) resonances in the second excitation shell: 8; number observed with any known spin-parity: 0.

## Context

This work extends the program of baryon spectroscopy from the \(N\) and \(\Delta\) sector, where the first excitation band is complete and Regge trajectories exist up to \(L=6\), into the poorly known hyperon sector where even the first excitation band is incomplete. It directly addresses the long-standing dispute over the \(\Lambda(1405)\) structure between quark models (Isgur 1978, Loring 2001) predicting an SU(3) singlet and chiral coupled-channel approaches (Kaiser 1996, Oller 2000, Jido 2003) predicting two poles with mixed singlet-octet character. The proposed measurements would complement efforts at COMPASS (Ketzer 2019), J-PARC (Sako 2013), and the forthcoming PANDA experiment (Iazzi 2016), and could enable the first identification of baryons from the SU(6) 20-plet representation, which has never been observed.
