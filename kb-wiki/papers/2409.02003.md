# Implications of first neutrino-induced nuclear recoil measurements in direct detection experiments

> PandaX-4T and XENONnT have recently reported the first measurement of nuclear recoils induced by the $^8$B solar neutrino flux, through the coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CE$ν$NS) channel. As long anticipated, this is an important milestone for dark matter searches as well as for neutrino physics. This measurement means that these detectors have reached exposures such that searches for low mass, $\lesssim 10$ GeV dark matter cannot be analyzed using the background-free paradigm going forward. It also opens a new era for these detectors to be used as neutrino observatories. In this paper we assess the sensitivity of these new measurements to new physics in the neutrino sector. We focus on neutrino non-standard interactions (NSI) and show that -- despite the still moderately low statistical significance of the signals -- these data already provide valuable information. We find that limits on NSI from PandaX-4T and XENONnT measurements are comparable to those derived using combined COHERENT CsI and LAr data, as well as those including the latest Ge measurement. Furthermore, they provide sensitivity to pure $τ$ flavor parameters that are not accessible using stopped-pion or reactor sources. With further improvements of statistical uncertainties as well as larger exposures, forthcoming data from these experiments will provide important, novel results for CE$ν$NS-related physics.

## Key claim

The first measurements of solar neutrino-induced nuclear recoils by PandaX-4T and XENONnT, despite having only moderate statistical significance (approximately 2 sigma), already provide competitive constraints on neutrino non-standard interactions (NSI) that are comparable to those from dedicated COHERENT measurements, and uniquely provide sensitivity to pure tau flavor parameters inaccessible to stopped-pion or reactor sources.

## Method

The authors performed a chi-square statistical analysis comparing the observed event rates from PandaX-4T (paired and US2 data sets) and XENONnT to Standard Model predictions modified by neutrino NSI parameters. They employed a two-flavor approximation for neutrino propagation in the Sun, calculating the averaged electron neutrino survival probability including NSI-induced matter potentials, and then computed the expected event rates at the detectors using NSI-modified weak charges in the CEνNS cross section, considering both up-quark and down-quark couplings in a single-parameter approach and selected two-parameter analyses.

## Key numbers

- PandaX-4T US2 sample: best fit 8B event rate of 75 ± 28 events with 1.04 tonne-year exposure and 0.3-3.0 keV recoil energy range
- PandaX-4T paired sample: best fit 8B event rate of 3.5 ± 1.3 events with 1.25 tonne-year exposure and 1.1-3.0 keV recoil energy range
- XENONnT combined analysis: best fit 8B event rate of 10.7 ± 3.95 events with 3.51 tonne-year exposure and 0.5-3.0 keV recoil energy range
- Statistical uncertainties for all data sets are approximately 37%
- XENONnT SM predicted event rate: 11.9 events (with +4.5/-4.2 uncertainty), yielding a Z-score of 0.2 sigma relative to the measured value
- PandaX-4T SM predicted event rates: 2.4 events (paired) and 46.8 events (US2)
- The two-flavor approximation used is reliable to within approximately 10% corrections

## Context

This work extends the analysis of neutrino NSI from CEνNS measurements, which previously relied on COHERENT data using CsI, LAr, and Ge targets (DeRomeri 2022, Liao 2024), to the new solar neutrino detection channel opened by PandaX-4T and XENONnT. The results demonstrate that direct dark matter detectors have reached the "neutrino floor" where solar neutrino backgrounds become relevant for low-mass dark matter searches (below approximately 10 GeV), as anticipated by earlier theoretical work (Monroe 2007, Strigari 2009, Billard 2013). This measurement represents the second pure neutral current detection of solar neutrinos, complementing the SNO deuterium measurement (SNO 2011), and enables future probes of neutrino electromagnetic properties, sterile neutrinos, and light mediator scenarios.
