# The Three-Boson System at Next-To-Next-To-Leading Order

> We discuss effective field theory treatments of the problem of three particles interacting via short-range forces (range R >> a_2, with a_2 the two-body scattering length). We show that forming a once-subtracted scattering equation yields a scattering amplitude whose low-momentum part is renormalization-group invariant up to corrections of O(R^3/a_2^3). Since corrections of O(R/a_2) and O(R^2/a_2^2) can be straightforwardly included in the integral equation's kernel, a unique solution for 1+2 scattering phase shifts and three-body bound-state energies can be obtained up to this accuracy. We use our equation to calculate the correlation between the binding energies of Helium-4 trimers and the atom-dimer scattering length. Our results are in excellent agreement with the recent three-dimensional Faddeev calculations of Roudnev and collaborators that used phenomenological inter-atomic potentials.

## Key claim

The authors show that a once-subtracted scattering equation yields a three-body scattering amplitude whose low-momentum part is renormalization-group invariant up to corrections of order \(R^3/a_2^3\), and that up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in the \(R/a_2\) expansion, no additional three-body counterterm beyond the one needed at leading order is required for consistent renormalization, contrary to claims by Bedaque et al. (2002) and Grießhammer (2005) that an extra three-body datum would be necessary at that order.

## Method

The authors develop an effective field theory (EFT) for three identical bosons interacting via short-range forces, using a Lagrangian with contact interactions rewritten in terms of a particle field and a dimeron field representing the two-body bound state. They formulate a once-subtracted integral equation for the 1+2 scattering K-matrix, using the atom-dimer scattering length \(a_3\) as the three-body input, and then include corrections to the two-body t-matrix at next-to-leading order (NLO) and NNLO by expanding the dimeron propagator to include effective-range contributions up to order \((r/2)^n\) where \(r \sim R\) is the effective range. They analyze the high-momentum behavior of the threshold amplitude and the convergence of the integral equation to demonstrate cutoff independence up to NNLO.

## Key numbers

- The two-body binding energy at leading order is \(B_2 = 1/(m a_2^2)\) (with \(\hbar = 1\)).
- For Helium-4 atoms, the atom-atom scattering length is \(a_2 = (104^{+8}_{-18})\,\text{Å}\) and the range of van der Waals forces is \(R \approx 7\,\text{Å}\), giving an expansion parameter \(R/a_2 \lesssim 10\%\).
- The leading-order two-body t-matrix is \(\tau^{(0)}(E) = \frac{2}{\pi m^2} \frac{\gamma + \sqrt{-mE}}{E + B_2}\) with \(\gamma = \sqrt{m B_2} = 1/a_2\).
- The K-matrix at threshold relates to the atom-dimer scattering length as \(K^{(0)}(0,0;-B_2) = \frac{3 m a_3}{8\gamma}\).
- The high-momentum fall-off of the threshold amplitude is \(K^{(n)}(q,0;-B_2) \sim m \beta^{(n)}(q) / q^{1 + n/2}\) for \(q \gg 1/r\), with \(\beta^{(n)}(q)\) oscillatory and bounded.
- The contribution of high-momentum modes (\(q \gg 1/r\)) to the integral equation is suppressed by three powers of the small parameter \(p r\), scaling as \(\sim (m/\gamma) p^2 r^3\).

## Context

This work extends the EFT treatment of the three-body problem with large two-body scattering length, systematized by Hammer and Mehen (2001) and Bedaque et al. (2002), who showed that \(O(R/a_2)\) corrections come from two-body physics alone. The authors contradict the claim by Bedaque et al. (2002) and Grießhammer (2005) that an additional three-body force is needed at NNLO, showing instead that the same subtraction used at leading order suffices up to \(O(R^3/a_2^3)\). The formalism enables model-independent predictions for three-body observables in systems with short-range forces, such as Helium-4 trimers, neutron-deuteron scattering, and proton-deuteron scattering with Coulomb corrections, and the authors use it to calculate the correlation between Helium-4 trimer binding energies and the atom-dimer scattering length, finding agreement with the Faddeev calculations of Roudnev and collaborators (2004) but not with those of Motovilov et al. (2001).
