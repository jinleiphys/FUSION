# Tyree-body mechanisms in the 3^He(e,e'p) reactions at high missing momentum

> A particular three-body mechanism is responsible for the missing strength which has been reported in $^3$He(e,e$'$p) reactions at missing momentum above 700 MeV/c. It corresponds to the absorption of the virtual photon by a nucleon at rest which subsequently propagates on-shell and emits a meson which is reabsorbed by the pair formed by the two other nucleons. Its amplitude, which is negligible in photon induced reactions as well as in the electro-production of an on-shell meson, becomes maximal in the quasi-free kinematics (X=1). It relates the amplitude of the $^3$He(e,e$'$p)D reaction to the amplitude of $pD$ elastic scattering at backward angles.

# Three-body mechanisms in the $^3$He(e,e$'$p) reactions at high missing momentum

## Key claim
A specific three-body mechanism, the absorption of a virtual photon by a nucleon at rest, which then propagates on-shell and emits a meson reabsorbed by the other two nucleons, accounts for the missing cross section in $^3$He(e,e$'$p) reactions at missing momenta above 700 MeV/c, but only becomes maximal under the quasi-free kinematics condition X=1, where the on-shell nucleon singularity is realized.

## Method
The reaction amplitude is expanded in terms of relevant Feynman diagrams computed in momentum space in the laboratory frame, with relativistic kinematics and no angular approximations in loop integrals. The three-body amplitude is evaluated by factorizing the elementary matrix elements at the on-shell singularity (X=1), using the low-momentum components of the $^3$He Faddeev wave function (Paris potential) and on-shell elementary amplitudes calibrated independently against the corresponding reactions. The nine-fold integral over nucleon momenta is reduced to an analytic form by picking the pole in the intermediate nucleon propagator.

## Key numbers
- Missing momentum above 700 MeV/c: the region where three-body mechanisms dominate and restore agreement with data, where one- and two-body mechanisms miss by one order of magnitude at $p_m \simeq$ 1 GeV/c.
- $Q^2 = 1.55$ GeV$^2$ and $E_e = 4.795$ GeV: the kinematics at which the $^3$He(e,e$'$p)d momentum distribution and angular distributions are computed and compared to data from Mar04 and Fat04.
- $p_m = 1$ GeV/c: the constant missing momentum at which the angular distribution of the deuteron is shown, with the singular part of the nucleon Born term producing a sharp maximum at X=1.
- $p_m = 600$ MeV/c: the missing momentum below which the two-nucleon peak dominates the continuum missing energy spectra in the $^3$He(e,e$'$p)pn reaction.
- Six decades: the range over which the calculation reproduces the experimental cross section without any free parameter.

## Context
This work extends the author's prior evaluations (Laget 2004) of one- and two-body mechanisms in $^3$He electrodisintegration, which missed the experimental data by an order of magnitude at high missing momentum. The three-body mechanism relates the $^3$He(e,e$'$p)D amplitude to backward pD elastic scattering studied at Saturne and Dubna (Laget 1993). The calculation completes a program begun in earlier work (Laget 1985, 1987, 1988) and enables future experiments at JLab using a third spectrometer in Hall A or CLAS in Hall B to map the angular distribution at constant missing momentum and to extend measurements beyond the pion production threshold.
