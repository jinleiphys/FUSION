# Nonuniform-temperature effects on the phase transition in an Ising-like model

> In this study, we investigate the spatially nonuniform-temperature effects on the QCD chiral phase transition in the heavy-ion collisions. Since the QCD effective theory and the Ising model belong to the same universality class, we start our discussion by mimicking the QCD effective potential with an Ising-like effective potential. In contrast to the dynamical slowing down effects which delays the phase transition from quark-gluon-plasma to hadron gas, the spatially nonuniform-temperature effects show a possibility to lift the phase transition temperature. Besides, both the fluctuations and the correlation length are enhanced in the phase transition region. Furthermore, the critical phenomena is strongly suppressed like as the critical slowing down effects. The underlying mechanism is the nonzero-momentum mode fluctuations of the order parameter induced by the nonuniform temperature. Our study provides a method to evaluate the nonuniform-temperature effects, and illustrate its potential influence on analyzing the QCD phase transition signals at RHIC.

## Key claim

The spatially nonuniform temperature distribution in a heavy-ion collision fireball can lift the QCD chiral phase transition temperature above the equilibrium critical temperature \(T_c\), in contrast to the dynamical slowing down effect which delays the transition. The authors show this using a simplified Ising-like effective potential and a Markov approximation that neglects dynamical memory effects.

## Method

The authors model a slender brick cell of the fireball with a tanh-type temperature profile along one spatial dimension, assuming isothermal cross-sections and a uniform baryon chemical potential. They map the QCD effective potential to an Ising-like potential with a linear mapping between temperature and chemical potential and the Ising variables. The stationary order parameter profile is obtained by solving the extremum condition of the probability distribution functional, and the fluctuations and correlation length are computed from the discretized inverse of the fluctuation matrix.

## Key numbers

- Temperature bias \(\delta T = 40\) MeV and width \(w = 1\) fm (or \(w = 0.5\) fm) for the tanh temperature profile, giving a temperature gradient of about 20 MeV/fm (or 40 MeV/fm) in the phase transition region.
- Phase transition temperature is lifted by about 3 MeV (for \(w = 1\) fm) and about 8 MeV (for \(w = 0.5\) fm) above \(T_c = 160\) MeV.
- Correlation length \(\xi\) near the phase transition point is about 1.65 fm to 1.9 fm in the central part of the brick cell, compared to about 1 fm in the periphery.
- With a more realistic temperature profile from hydrodynamics, the phase transition temperature is lifted by about 8 MeV (to about 168 MeV) and the correlation length at the phase transition point is about 1.45 fm.

## Context

This work extends studies of dynamical phase transitions in heavy-ion collisions, contrasting with the critical slowing down effect (Berdnikov 2000, Mukherjee 2015, Jiang 2017) which delays the transition. It provides a method to evaluate nonuniform-temperature effects, which have been overlooked in favor of temporal memory effects. The results suggest that the nonuniform-temperature effect and the dynamical memory effect (Stephanov 2018, Nahrgang 2019, Rajagopal 2020) are two extreme cases (spatial vs. temporal correlation dominance) that should be combined for a better description of the QCD phase transition at RHIC.
