# Violations of parity and charge conjugation in the theta vacuum with imaginary chemical potential

> Charge conjugation (C) and Parity (P) are exact symmetries at theta =pi and Theta = mu/(iT)=pi, where theta is the parameter of the so-called theta-vacuum, mu is the imaginary quark-number chemical potential and T is the temperature. Spontaneous breakings of these discrete symmetries are investigated by the the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model. At zero T, P symmetry is spontaneously broken while C symmetry is conserved. As T increases, P symmetry is restored just after C symmetry is spontaneously broken, so that either P or C symmetry or both the symmetries are spontaneously broken for any T. The chiral-symmetry restoration and the deconfinement transition at theta =Theta =0 are remnants of the P restoration and the C breaking at theta =Theta =pi, respectively.

## Key claim

At theta = pi and Theta = mu/(iT) = pi, parity (P) and charge conjugation (C) are exact symmetries, but they are spontaneously broken at different temperatures: P symmetry is broken at low T and restored at higher T, while C symmetry is conserved at low T and broken at higher T, so that either P or C (or both) is spontaneously broken for any temperature.

## Method

The authors used the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model and its extension, the entanglement-PNJL (EPNJL) model, to analyze spontaneous P and C violations at finite theta and imaginary chemical potential. They computed the thermodynamic potential in the mean-field approximation, solving stationary conditions for the chiral condensate, the eta condensate (P-odd order parameter), and the Polyakov loop, and identified phase transitions by examining order parameters and susceptibilities as functions of temperature.

## Key numbers

- At theta = pi and Theta = 0, P symmetry is spontaneously broken below T_P = 202 MeV and restored above that temperature (second-order transition).
- In the PNJL model at theta = Theta = 0, the pseudocritical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration is T_chi = 216 MeV and for deconfinement is T_d = 173 MeV.
- In the NJL model (T=0 limit), the P restoration temperature is T_P approximately 172 MeV.
- The parameter c = G_2/G is set to 0.2, with G_1 = (1-c)G and G_2 = cG; the current quark mass m_0 = 5.5 MeV; the Polyakov potential parameter T_0 is rescaled to 212 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of parity violation in the NJL model by Fujihara, Inagaki, and Kimura (2006) and by Boer and Boomsma (2008-2009) to finite temperature using the PNJL framework, which also incorporates confinement and the Roberge-Weiss transition. The authors show that the chiral crossover at theta = Theta = 0 is a remnant of the second-order P restoration at theta = pi, and the deconfinement crossover at theta = Theta = 0 is a remnant of the C breaking at theta = Theta = pi, linking the thermodynamics at these two special points.
