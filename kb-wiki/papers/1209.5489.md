# Production and parity determination of $Ξ$ baryons

> To understand the structure of hadrons and their production mechanisms, it is very important to be able to identify their quantum numbers. For investigating the spin and parity quantum numbers of $Ξ$ and $Ω$ baryons, in particular, it is very important to understand strong interactions in the strangeness sector. At present, such quantum numbers are known for only a few of the $Ξ$ baryons, and even the parity of the ground state $Ξ(1318)$ has never been measured. In this article, we present a novel, model-independent way to determine the parity of $Ξ$ baryons solely based on symmetry considerations for the hadronic reaction $\bar{K} N \to K Ξ$.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model-independent method to determine the parity of spin-1/2 Ξ baryons by measuring the spin-transfer coefficient K_yy in the hadronic reaction K̅ N → K Ξ, where K_yy is shown to be constant and equal to the parity of the produced Ξ. The method relies solely on symmetry considerations (Bohr's theorem) rather than on any particular production model.

## Method

The authors analyze the spin structure of the reaction amplitude for K̅ N → K Ξ constrained by rotation and parity-inversion symmetries, deriving distinct forms for positive-parity and negative-parity Ξ baryons. They show that the spin-transfer coefficient K_yy, a double-polarization observable, is constant and directly equals the parity of the produced Ξ. They also demonstrate that combinations of single-polarization observables (target asymmetry T_y and recoil asymmetry P_y) provide an alternative parity determination.

## Key numbers

- The ground state Ξ(1318) has never had its parity measured experimentally; the positive parity quoted by the Particle Data Group is taken from quark-model calculations.
- Only three of eleven reported cascade states (Ξ(1318), Ξ(1530), and Ξ(1820)) have known spin and parity quantum numbers.
- The Ω⁻(1672) baryon's spin-3/2 was only confirmed experimentally in 2006 (BABAR Collaboration), more than 40 years after its discovery.
- The BABAR Collaboration claimed in 2008 that Ξ(1690) has J^P = 1/2⁻.

## Context

This work extends the study of multi-strangeness baryon production, which has been limited compared to Λ and Σ hyperon studies due to very small cross sections for producing S=-2 and S=-3 baryons from non-strangeness initial states. The method enables parity determination at current facilities like J-PARC, which can provide energetic kaon beams, and at future facilities like FAIR (PANDA Collaboration) and JLab (CLAS Collaboration), which have established feasibility for Ξ spectroscopy via photoproduction. The approach can be generalized to higher-spin Ξ baryons and to polarization observables in Ξ photoproduction, as well as to parity determination of Ω baryons.
