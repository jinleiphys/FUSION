# Numerical tests of causal relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics

> We present numerical methods to solve the Israel-Stewart (IS) equations of causal relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics with bulk and shear viscosities. We then test these methods studying the Riemann problem in (1+1)-- and (2+1)-dimensional geometry. The numerical schemes investigated here are applicable to realistic (3+1)--dimensional modeling of a relativistic dissipative fluid.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Israel-Stewart (IS) equations of causal relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics can be solved numerically for the Riemann problem in (1+1)- and (2+1)-dimensional geometries, but the numerical schemes are tested only for a massless Boltzmann gas without conserved charges, using a simplified equation of state where the heat flow and charge diffusion are set to zero.

## Method

The authors discretize the IS equations using numerical methods suitable for shock-capturing and solve the relativistic Riemann problem in (1+1)-dimensional Cartesian and cylindrical coordinates, as well as in (2+1)-dimensional Cartesian coordinates. They assume a massless Boltzmann gas with equation of state e = 3p and no conserved charges, and they evolve the conservation equations for charge, energy, and momentum together with relaxation equations for the bulk viscous pressure and shear stress tensor components. The numerical tests focus on how the dissipative solutions approach the ideal-fluid limit when shock discontinuities are present.

## Key numbers

- The equation of state used is e = 3p and e = (3g/pi^2) T^4, where g is the number of degrees of freedom
- The equilibrium entropy density is s_eq = (4g/pi^2) T^3
- For the massless Boltzmann gas, the thermodynamic integral beta_2 = 3/(4p)
- The Navier-Stokes shear pressure in 1D Cartesian coordinates is pi_NS = -(4/3) eta theta_z, where theta_z is the expansion scalar
- The Navier-Stokes bulk pressure is Pi_NS = -zeta theta, and the shear stress tensor is pi^{mu nu}_{NS} = 2 eta sigma^{mu nu}

## Context

This work extends prior numerical studies of the Israel-Stewart equations by Baier (2006) on sound propagation and by Song (2007) on expansion scenarios, filling the gap of testing algorithms in situations where shock discontinuities occur in the ideal-fluid limit. The paper provides a foundation for future (3+1)-dimensional modeling of relativistic dissipative fluids in heavy-ion collisions, where the matter created at RHIC exhibits nearly perfect fluid behavior as described by RHIC (2005) and the AdS/CFT bound by Kovtun (2004).
