# Precursor of Color Superconductivity in Hot Quark Matter

> We investigate possible precursory phenomena of color superconductivity in quark matter at finite temperature T with use of a simple Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. It is found that the fluctuating pair field exists with a prominent strength even well above the critical temperature T_c. We show that the collective pair field has a complex energy located in the second Riemann sheet, which approaches the origin as T is lowered to T_c. We discuss the possible relevance of the precursor to the observables to be detected in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The fluctuating color Cooper-pair field exists with prominent strength even well above the critical temperature \(T_c\), with a precritical region one to two orders larger in units of \(T_c\) than that in electric superconductors, provided the phase transition is second order (though the collective mode persists even for weak first-order transitions).

## Method

The authors use a two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a scalar coupling \(G_S\) and a diquark coupling \(G_C\) to study fluctuations of the color Cooper-pair field in the normal phase above \(T_c\). They compute the linear response of the system to an external pair field via the anomalous Green function, obtaining the retarded response function \(D^R(\mathbf{k},\omega)\) and its spectral function. The pole of the response function in the complex energy plane is then located to identify the soft collective mode.

## Key numbers

- The scalar coupling constant is set to \(G_S = 5.01\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\) and the three-momentum cutoff to \(\Lambda = 650\ \mathrm{MeV}\), reproducing \(f_\pi = 93\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(\langle \bar{\psi}\psi \rangle = (-250\ \mathrm{MeV})^3\).
- The diquark coupling constant is fixed at \(G_C = 3.11\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\).
- The critical temperature of the chiral transition at \(\mu = 0\) is \(T = 185\ \mathrm{MeV}\); the critical chemical potential of the chiral–CSC transition at \(T = 0\) is \(\mu = 316\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The tricritical point where the chiral transition changes from second to first order is located at approximately \((T,\mu) = (70, 280)\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The spectral function shows a prominent peak surviving at temperatures as high as \(\epsilon \equiv (T-T_c)/T_c \sim 0.2\) above \(T_c\).
- For \(\mu = 400\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(\mu = 500\ \mathrm{MeV}\), the pole of the soft mode approaches the origin as \(T\) is lowered toward \(T_c\); the imaginary part \(\omega_i\) is larger than the real part \(\omega_r\) for both chemical potentials.

## Context

This work extends the study of color superconductivity (CSC) beyond the mean-field critical temperature, using a simple NJL model similar to that employed by Berges and Rajagopal (1999) and by Schwarz, Klevansky, and Papp (1999). It identifies a soft mode for CSC analogous to the soft mode for the chiral transition discussed by Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1994). The results suggest that a pseudogap phase, analogous to that observed in high-\(T_c\) superconductors (Timusk and Statt, 1999), may exist in hot quark matter, and that color-paraconductivity could be observable in heavy-ion collisions with large baryon stopping.
