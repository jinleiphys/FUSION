# Nuclear Structure Towards N=40 60Ca: In-beam gamma-ray Spectroscopy of 58,60Ti

> Excited states in the neutron-rich N=38,36 nuclei \nuc{60}{Ti} and \nuc{58}{Ti} were populated in nucleon-removal reactions from \nuc{61}{V} projectiles at 90~MeV/nucleon. The γ-ray transitions from such states in these Ti isotopes were detected with the advanced γ-ray tracking array GRETINA and were corrected event-by-event for large Doppler shifts (v/c \sim 0.4) using the γ-ray interaction points deduced from online signal decomposition. The new data indicate that a steep decrease in quadrupole collectivity occurs when moving from neutron-rich N=36,38 Fe and Cr toward the Ti and Ca isotones. In fact, \nuc{58,60}{Ti} provide some of the most neutron-rich benchmarks accessible today for calculations attempting to determine the structure of the potentially doubly-magic nucleus \nuc{60}{Ca}.

## Key claim

The first 2+ state of 60Ti at 850(5) keV is located at almost twice the excitation energy of the corresponding 2+ level in the N=38 isotone 62Cr, signaling a steep decrease in collectivity with Z and another sudden structural change near N=40; however, the authors note that the excitation energies of both states in 60Ti are underestimated by the LNPS shell-model interaction (2+ by 150 keV and 4+ by 240 keV), indicating that this nucleus is one of the furthest extrapolation points with no data available previously.

## Method

Excited states in 58Ti and 60Ti were populated via one-proton knockout reactions from a 61V secondary beam at 90 MeV/nucleon on a 9Be target at the NSCL Coupled Cyclotron Facility. Gamma rays were detected with the GRETINA array of 36-fold segmented HPGe detectors, with event-by-event Doppler reconstruction using online signal decomposition and momentum vectors from the S800 spectrograph. Particle identification of reaction residues was performed at the S800 focal plane using energy loss and time-of-flight measurements.

## Key numbers

- 60Ti: 2+1 state at 850(5) keV and 4+1 state at 866(5) keV (doublet analysis)
- 58Ti: 2+1 state at 1047(4) keV, candidate 4+1 state at 991(4) keV, candidate 6+1 state at 619(5) keV
- Inclusive cross section for one-proton knockout from 61V to 60Ti: sigma_inc = 7.9(7) mb
- Population fractions in 60Ti from doublet analysis: 29(12)% to 0+1, 30(11)% to 2+1, 40(10)% to 4+1
- 60Ti 2+1 excitation energy is almost twice that of the N=38 isotone 62Cr

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of quadrupole collectivity in neutron-rich N=36,38 Fe and Cr isotopes toward the Ti and Ca isotones, following prior measurements of 58Ti by Aoi (2008) and Suzuki (2013). The results provide experimental benchmarks for shell-model calculations using the LNPS effective interaction (Lenzi 2010) and GXPF1A (Honma), with the data favoring the larger LNPS model space that includes neutron d5/2 and g9/2 orbitals. The findings are crucial for constraining predictions of the N=40 shell gap in 60Ca, which is closely tied to the location of the neutron drip line in the Ca isotopic chain and has been addressed theoretically by Meng (2001), Hagen (2012, 2013), and Brown (2013).
