# 2N and 3N Tensor Force in the $N=34$ Shell Evolution: An Ab Initio Perspective

> Shell evolution plays a vital role in understanding the nuclear shell structures across the nuclear chart. In this work, we have investigated the $N = 34$ shell structure using the state-of-the-art ab-initio valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization (VS-IMSRG) approach. Notably, we employ nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) interactions derived from chiral effective field theory and make use of the spin-tensor decomposition scheme to examine the contributions of individual interaction components. We discuss the evolution of the shell structures, which have been investigated by considering the roles of various components, including central, spin-orbit, and tensor effects of NN and 3N forces, respectively. The $N=34$ shell gap gradually decreases from $^{54}$Ca as the proton occupancy in the $π{0f_{7/2}}$ orbital increases, and eventually disappears in the $^{62}$Ni as a consequence of the tensor-force driven shell evolution. Our analysis reveals that this disappearance is predominantly governed by the NN tensor force, which accounts for approximately 83$\%$, while the 3N tensor force also contributes about 17$\%$.

## Key claim

The disappearance of the N=34 shell gap from ⁵⁴Ca to ⁶²Ni is predominantly governed by the nucleon-nucleon (NN) tensor force (contributing approximately 83%), while the three-nucleon (3N) tensor force contributes about 17%, as quantified through spin-tensor decomposition of chiral effective field theory interactions within the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) framework.

## Method

The authors employed the VS-IMSRG approach to derive effective shell-model Hamiltonians in the full fp-shell valence space above a ⁴⁰Ca core, starting from chiral NN interactions at N³LO and 3N interactions at N²LO (the 1.8/2.0 (EM) and ΔN²LO_GO(394) interactions). They applied spin-tensor decomposition separately to the bare NN and 3N interactions before the IMSRG evolution, decomposing each into central (λ=0), spin-orbit (λ=1), and tensor (λ=2) components, then evolved each component individually to isolate their contributions to the effective single-particle energies (ESPEs) and the N=34 shell gap.

## Key numbers

- The NN tensor force accounts for approximately 83% of the disappearance of the N=34 shell gap in ⁶²Ni, while the 3N tensor force contributes about 17%.
- The first 2⁺ excitation energy in ⁵⁴Ca calculated with the 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction differs by about 800 keV from the value obtained with the ΔN²LO_GO(394) interaction.
- The NN interaction was softened by free-space SRG evolution with momentum scale λ = 1.8 fm⁻¹; the 3N interaction used momentum cutoff Λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹ for the 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction.
- The harmonic oscillator basis used ℏω = 16 MeV with truncation e_max = 12 and E_3max = 24 for the 3N force.
- The experimental first 2⁺ excitation energy in ⁵⁴Ca is 2.043 MeV, establishing the N=34 subshell closure.

## Context

This work extends the tensor-driven shell evolution mechanism proposed by Otsuka et al. (2001, 2005) by providing a fully ab initio quantification of the separate NN and 3N tensor force contributions, going beyond the phenomenological spin-tensor decomposition of effective NN interactions performed by Smirnova et al. (2012) and the recent VS-IMSRG study by Sahoo et al. (2025) which decomposed the full Hamiltonian rather than the bare interactions. The results enable future studies to systematically separate NN and 3N contributions in other mass regions and to test whether the dominance of NN tensor force over 3N tensor force persists for other shell evolution phenomena.
