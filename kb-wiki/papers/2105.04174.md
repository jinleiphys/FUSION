# Three-$α$ configurations in the $0^{+}$ states of $^{12}\mathrm{C}$

> Geometric configurations of three-$α$ particles in the ground- and first-excited $J^π=0^+$ states of $^{12}$C are discussed within two types of $α$-cluster models which treat the Pauli principle differently. Though there are some quantitative differences especially in the internal region of the wave functions, equilateral triangle configurations are dominant in the ground state, while in the first excited $0^+$ state isosceles triangle configurations dominate, originating from $^8{\rm Be}+α$ configurations.

## Key claim

Equilateral triangle configurations dominate the ground state of 12C, while isosceles triangle configurations (originating from 8Be+alpha configurations) dominate the first excited 0+ state; however, the two cluster models used (orthogonality condition model and shallow potential model) show quantitative differences in the internal region of the wave functions, particularly in the occupation of harmonic oscillator quanta below the Pauli-allowed Q=8 threshold.

## Method

The authors performed precise three-alpha calculations using the stochastic variational method with symmetrized correlated Gaussians, treating the alpha particle as a point particle. Two phase-shift equivalent potential models were employed: the orthogonality condition model (OCM), which imposes orthogonality to Pauli-forbidden states, and the shallow Ali-Bodmer potential (AB), which uses a repulsive short-range component. Two-body density distributions, occupation probabilities of harmonic oscillator quanta, partial wave probabilities, one-body densities in coordinate and momentum space, and spectroscopic amplitudes for 8Be+alpha configurations were computed and compared between the two models.

## Key numbers

- Ground state (0_1+) energy: -7.13 MeV (OCM), -6.67 MeV (AB)
- First excited state (0_2+) energy: 0.78 MeV (OCM), 0.38 MeV (AB)
- Root-mean-square point-alpha radius for 0_1+: 1.73 fm (OCM), 1.84 fm (AB)
- Root-mean-square point-alpha radius for 0_2+: 4.58 fm (OCM), 3.39 fm (AB)
- Partial wave probability P_l=0 for 0_1+: 0.295 (OCM), 0.704 (AB)
- Partial wave probability P_l=0 for 0_2+: 0.693 (OCM), 0.886 (AB)
- Spectroscopic factor for 8Be+alpha in 0_2+: 0.693 (OCM), 0.729 (AB)
- Oscillator energy set to 22 MeV for the harmonic oscillator quanta analysis
- Peak positions of spectroscopic amplitude for 0_2+ located at approximately 5 fm
- Root-mean-square distances of 8Be: 6.36 fm (AB), 5.93 fm (OCM)

## Context

This work extends algebraic cluster model studies by Fortunato (2019) and others on geometric configurations in 12C, using precise wave functions rather than algebraic approximations. The two models compared (OCM and AB) are known from earlier work by Pinilla (2011) and Arai (2018) to give different results for some observables despite being phase-shift equivalent. The results enable future experimental comparisons using decay properties and electromagnetic transitions to distinguish which model better describes the actual alpha-cluster configurations in 12C.
