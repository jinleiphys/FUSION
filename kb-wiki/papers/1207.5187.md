# In-medium hadron properties from lattice QCD

> I review recent results from lattice QCD calculations on the in-medium hadron properties. I discuss the thermal dilepton rates, heavy quarkonium properties as well as the chiral and $U(1)_A$ symmetries at finite temperature.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations of in-medium hadron properties show that the thermal dilepton rate from the light vector channel agrees with hard thermal loop (HTL) approximations for ω/T ≳ 2, but differs by about a factor of two for 1 ≲ ω/T ≲ 2, and that the electrical conductivity σ/T is about an order of magnitude smaller than leading-order pQCD predictions.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD results on Euclidean temporal and spatial correlation functions for light, charm, and bottom quark channels, from which spectral functions are extracted using the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM). For light quarks, continuum-extrapolated vector correlation functions on isotropic quenched lattices at T ≈ 1.45 Tc are fit with a Breit-Wigner plus continuum ansatz. For charmonia, spectral functions are computed on fine isotropic quenched lattices at multiple temperatures (0.73, 1.46, 2.20, 2.93 Tc) using MEM. For bottomonia, Non-Relativistic QCD (NRQCD) on highly anisotropic lattices is used. Chiral and U(1)A symmetry restoration are studied via susceptibilities computed with domain wall fermions and overlap fermions.

## Key numbers

- Thermal dilepton rate from lattice QCD at T ≈ 1.45 Tc agrees with HTL for ω/T ≳ 2; differences of about a factor of two exist for 1 ≲ ω/T ≲ 2.
- Electrical conductivity: 1/3 ≲ (1/Cem) σ/T ≲ 1 at T ≈ 1.45 Tc (Ding 2010); σ/T ≈ 0.33 Cem, nearly temperature-independent in the range 1.16 Tc ≲ T ≲ 2.98 Tc (Francis 2011).
- Soft photon emission rate at T ≈ 1.45 Tc: lim_{ω→0} ω dRγ/d³p = (0.0004 – 0.0013) Tc².
- Charmonium: J/ψ, ηc, χc0, and χc1 all disappear at T ≥ 1.46 Tc (Ding 2012); screening masses in the pseudoscalar channel suggest dissociation at T ≳ 1.5 Tc (Karsch 2012).
- Bottomonium: Υ ground state survives up to 2.09 Tc in NRQCD (Aarts 2011).
- Charm diffusion coefficient: ≈ 1/(π T) for T ∈ (1.46 Tc, 2.93 Tc) (Ding 2012); heavy quark diffusion coefficient ≈ 2/(π T) from heavy quark effective theory (Francis 2011, Banerjee 2011).
- U(1)A symmetry remains broken up to T ≈ 200 MeV (≈ 1.2 Tpc) from domain wall fermion calculations with mπ ≈ 200 MeV (HotQCD 2012); JLQCD using overlap fermions with mπ ≳ 290 MeV and fixed zero topological charge finds U(1)A restoration at 209 MeV (Cossu 2012).

## Context

This review extends earlier lattice QCD reviews (DeTar 2009) by presenting recent results on thermal dilepton rates, heavy quarkonium spectral functions, and chiral/U(1)A symmetry restoration. The light vector channel results (Ding 2010) provide continuum-extrapolated electrical conductivity values that are significantly smaller than leading-order pQCD (Arnold 2003) and earlier unrenormalized lattice estimates (Gupta 2003, Aarts 2007). The charmonium dissociation temperatures (Ding 2012) contradict previous anisotropic-lattice studies that suggested S-wave survival up to 2 Tc, while the bottomonium results (Aarts 2011) extend the temperature range studied. The U(1)A symmetry studies (HotQCD 2012, Cossu 2012) address the open question of whether U(1)A restoration occurs at or above the chiral crossover, which affects the order of the chiral phase transition in two-flavor QCD (Pisarski 1983). Future work requires larger volumes, continuum extrapolations for NRQCD, and simulations at temperatures between Tc and 1.46 Tc to locate precise dissociation temperatures.
