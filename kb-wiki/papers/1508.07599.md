# Critical line of 2+1 flavor QCD: Toward the continuum limit

> We determine the continuum limit of the curvature of the pseudocritical line of QCD with $n_f$=2+1 staggered fermions at nonzero temperature and quark density. We perform Monte Carlo simulations at imaginary baryon chemical potentials, adopting the HISQ/tree action discretization, as implemented in the code by the MILC collaboration. Couplings are adjusted so as to move on a line of constant physics, as determined in Ref.~\cite{Bazavov:2011nk}, with the strange quark mass $m_s$ fixed at its physical value and a light-to-strange mass ratio $m_l/m_s=1/20$. The chemical potential is set at the same value for the three quark species, $μ_l=μ_s\equiv μ$. We attempt an extrapolation to the continuum using the results on lattices with temporal size up to $L_t=12$. Our estimate for the continuum value of the curvature $κ$ at zero baryon density, $κ=0.020(4)$, is compared with recent lattice results and with experimental determinations of the freeze-out curve.

## Key claim

The continuum-extrapolated curvature of the QCD pseudocritical line at zero baryon density is κ = 0.020(4), but this result relies on a constant fit to data from lattices with temporal sizes L_t = 6, 8, 10, and 12, and the extrapolation becomes largely undetermined if the smallest lattice (L_t = 6) is excluded from the fit.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of 2+1 flavor QCD with the HISQ/tree action at imaginary baryon chemical potentials, using the same chemical potential for all three quark species (μ_l = μ_s ≡ μ). They worked on a line of constant physics with physical strange quark mass and light-to-strange mass ratio m_l/m_s = 1/20, on lattices with temporal sizes L_t = 6, 8, 10, and 12 and aspect ratio four. The pseudocritical temperature at each imaginary chemical potential was determined from the peak of the renormalized disconnected susceptibility of the light quark chiral condensate, and the curvature κ was extracted by fitting T_c(μ)/T_c(0) as a linear function of (iμ/(πT_c(μ)))^2 and analytically continuing to real chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- Continuum-extrapolated curvature of the pseudocritical line: κ = 0.020(4)
- Curvature parameter from the r_1 scale on individual lattices: κ = 0.017(3) for L_t = 6, 0.021(2) for L_t = 8, 0.019(3) for L_t = 10, 0.015(5) for L_t = 12
- Curvature parameter from the f_K scale on individual lattices: κ = 0.019(4) for L_t = 6, 0.023(2) for L_t = 8, 0.020(3) for L_t = 10, 0.016(5) for L_t = 12
- Pseudocritical temperature at zero chemical potential: T_c(0) = 0.154(9) GeV, used in the parametrization T_c(μ_B) = T_c(0) - b μ_B^2 with b = 0.128(25) GeV^{-1}
- The critical line is considered reliable up to real baryon chemical potentials μ_B ≲ 0.4 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous determination of the curvature κ = 0.018(4) from Cea (2014) by performing an extrapolation to the continuum limit. The result is compared with recent lattice estimates from Bonati (2015) and Bellwied (2015), and with experimental freeze-out curves from Cleymans (2005) and Alba (2014). The paper addresses the systematic dependence of curvature determinations on lattice setup and probe observable, which had previously caused values to vary by almost a factor of three across different studies.
