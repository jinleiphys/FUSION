# Shear-bulk coupling in nonconformal hydrodynamics

> We compute the temporal evolution of the pressure anisotropy and bulk pressure of a massive gas using second-order viscous hydrodynamics and anisotropic hydrodynamics. We then compare our results with an exact solution of the Boltzmann equation for a massive gas in the relaxation time approximation. We demonstrate that, within second-order viscous hydrodynamics, the inclusion of the full set of kinetic coefficients, particularly the shear-bulk couplings, is necessary to properly describe the time evolution of the bulk pressure. We also compare the results of second-order hydrodynamics with those obtained using the anisotropic hydrodynamics approach. We find that anisotropic hydrodynamics and second-order viscous hydrodynamics including the shear-bulk couplings are both able to reproduce the exact evolution with comparable accuracy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including the full set of kinetic coefficients in second-order viscous hydrodynamics, particularly the shear-bulk couplings (lambda_Pi_pi and lambda_pi_Pi), is necessary to properly describe the time evolution of the bulk pressure in a massive gas; without these couplings, as in Israel-Stewart theory, the bulk pressure evolution does not agree with the exact solution of the Boltzmann equation.

## Method

The authors solved the 0+1d boost-invariant second-order viscous hydrodynamics equations derived from the 14-moment approximation (DNMR formalism) for a massive classical gas, including the shear-bulk coupling terms lambda_Pi_pi and lambda_pi_Pi. They compared these results with exact solutions of the 0+1d massive Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation (RTA), obtained via an iterative numerical method, and with solutions from anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) that includes an explicit bulk degree of freedom. All calculations used a fixed relaxation time tau_eq = 0.5 fm/c, initial time tau_0 = 0.5 fm/c, and initial temperature T_0 = 600 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For m = 30 MeV, tau_0 = 0.5 fm/c, tau_eq = 0.5 fm/c, T_0 = 600 MeV, and xi_0 = 0: the full second-order hydrodynamics including shear-bulk couplings reproduced the exact pressure anisotropy and bulk pressure evolution well, while setting lambda_Pi_pi = lambda_pi_Pi = 0 caused significant disagreement in the bulk pressure.
- For m = 300 MeV and m = 1 GeV, with tau_0 = 0.5 fm/c, tau_eq = 0.5 fm/c, T_0 = 600 MeV, and xi_0 = 0: the full second-order hydrodynamics showed larger deviations from the exact solution compared to the m = 30 MeV case, but still outperformed the version without shear-bulk couplings.
- For xi_0 = 100 (highly oblate initial anisotropy) with m = 30 MeV, tau_0 = 0.5 fm/c, tau_eq = 0.5 fm/c, T_0 = 600 MeV: the shear-bulk couplings had a larger relative effect on the bulk pressure evolution than on the pressure anisotropy.
- For m = 30 MeV, tau_0 = 0.5 fm/c, tau_eq = 0.5 fm/c, T_0 = 600 MeV, and xi_0 = 0: anisotropic hydrodynamics and full second-order viscous hydrodynamics both reproduced the exact solution with comparable accuracy.

## Context

This work extends prior comparisons of second-order and anisotropic hydrodynamics that were limited to conformal or massless systems (Florkowski 2013). It directly addresses the failure of Israel-Stewart theory (Israel 1979) to reproduce exact solutions of the massive 0+1d Boltzmann equation reported by Florkowski (2014). The results enable the use of complete second-order viscous hydrodynamics or anisotropic hydrodynamics for modeling nonconformal fluids such as the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions, where bulk viscous effects and shear-bulk coupling are relevant.
