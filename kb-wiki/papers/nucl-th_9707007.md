# Photodisintegration of the Three-Nucleon Systems and their Polarizabilities

> The total photodisintegration cross sections of three-body nuclei are calculated with semirealistic NN potentials below pion threshold. Full final state interaction with Coulomb force is taken into account via the Lorentz integral transform method. The experimental total cross sections are well described and the sum rule $σ_{-1}(^3$H) agrees with elastic electron scattering data. The calculated ^3He polarizability is 0.15 fm^3.

# Photodisintegration of the Three-Nucleon Systems and their Polarizabilities

## Key claim
The total photodisintegration cross sections of ³H and ³He calculated with semirealistic NN potentials (Malfliet–Tjon I+III and Trento) using the Lorentz integral transform method agree well with experimental data below pion threshold, but the calculated TRK sum rule (σ₀) is about 20% lower than results with realistic potentials due to neglected tensor correlations.

## Method
The authors solved the Schrödinger-like equation for the Lorentz integral transform of the dipole response function using correlated hyperspherical and hyperradial expansions with Kmax = 7, for both MT and TN semirealistic local even central NN potentials including the Coulomb force. The ground-state wave functions were obtained with the same Hamiltonians, yielding binding energies of 8.7 MeV (³H) and 8.0 MeV (³He). The transform was inverted with σI = 20 MeV and 5 MeV, incorporating the n-d low-energy threshold behavior [Eγ − (Eγ)min]³/² into the trial response.

## Key numbers
- Binding energies: 8.7 MeV for ³H, 8.0 MeV for ³He (0.2 MeV overbinding compared to experiment)
- Charge radii (MT potential): rch(³H) = 1.76 fm, rch(³He) = 1.94 fm; (TN potential): rch(³H) = 1.74 fm, rch(³He) = 1.92 fm
- Experimental charge radii: rch(³H) = 1.76 ± 0.04 fm, rch(³He) = 1.976 ± 0.015 fm
- σ₋₁(³H) sum rule from experiment: 2.485 ± 0.135 mb, consistent with calculated values
- ³He electric polarizability αE1: 0.151 fm³ (MT potential) and 0.143 fm³ (TN potential), confirming the experimental value of 0.15 ± 0.02 fm³
- ³H electric polarizability αE1: 0.143 fm³ (MT potential) and 0.135 fm³ (TN potential)
- Sum rule consistency checks: differences of about 2% for σ₋₂, less than 1% for σ₋₁, and 3–4% for σ₀ between direct sum-rule evaluation and integration of calculated cross sections

## Context
This work extends prior calculations of three-nucleon photodisintegration that used separable NN potentials (BaP70, GiL76) or low-energy local potentials with hyperspherical approaches (Fang78), and improves on the realistic-force calculation of Vost81 by using the Lorentz integral transform method to include full final-state interaction with Coulomb force. The results support the authors’ earlier prediction of a pronounced giant dipole resonance in ⁴He (ELO96) and confirm the ³He polarizability value extracted from photoabsorption experiments (Faul81, Tic73, GLK91), which had been at variance with the value from elastic scattering on ²⁰⁸Pb. The work enables future calculations with more complete physics including tensor correlations, higher multipoles, retardation, and subnuclear currents.
