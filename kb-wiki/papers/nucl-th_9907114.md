# $pp\to ppω$ reaction near threshold

> We analyze the total cross section data for $pp \to ppω$ near threshold measured recently at SATURNE. Using an effective range approximation for the on-shell $pp$ S-wave final state interaction we extract from these data the modulus $|Ω| = 0.53$ fm$^4$ of the threshold transition amplitude $Ω$. We present a calculation of various (tree-level) meson exchange diagrams contributing to $Ω$. It is essential that $ω$-emission from the anomalous $ωρπ$-vertex interferes destructively with $ω$-emission from the proton lines. The contribution of scalar $σ$-meson exchange to $Ω$ turns out to be negligibly small. Without introducing off-shell meson-nucleon form factors the experimental value $|Ω|=0.53$ fm$^4$ can be reproduced with an $ωN$-coupling constant of $g_{ωN}=10.7$. The results of the present approach agree qualitatively with the Jülich model. We also perform a combined analysis of the reactions $pp\to ppπ^0, pnπ^+, ppη, ppω$ and $pn\to pnη$ near threshold.

## Key claim

The total cross section data for $pp\to pp\omega$ near threshold can be described using an effective range approximation for the on-shell $pp$ S-wave final state interaction, yielding a threshold transition amplitude modulus $|\Omega| = 0.53$ fm$^4$, but the approach may not be valid up to the highest measured excess energy of $Q=30.1$ MeV.

## Method

The author extracts the threshold amplitude $\Omega$ from five SATURNE data points ($Q \leq 31$ MeV) by assuming the T-matrix is constant near threshold and the energy dependence of the total cross section is given by three-body phase space combined with on-shell $pp$ S-wave final state interaction treated in effective range approximation using empirical scattering length $a_p = 7.81$ fm and effective range $r_p = 2.77$ fm. Tree-level meson exchange diagrams (including $\pi^0$, $\eta$, $\omega$, $\rho^0$, $\sigma$ exchange and the anomalous $\omega\rho\pi$ vertex) are then calculated analytically to reproduce the extracted $|\Omega|$ value.

## Key numbers

- $|\Omega| = 0.53$ fm$^4$: modulus of the threshold transition amplitude extracted from SATURNE data, with fit $\chi^2 = 2.5$
- $g_{\omega N} = 10.7$: $\omega N$-coupling constant that reproduces $|\Omega| = 0.53$ fm$^4$ without off-shell form factors
- $\Omega^{(\pi)} = 0.42$ fm$^4$: contribution from $\pi^0$-exchange diagrams
- $\Omega^{(\eta)} = 0.04$ fm$^4$: contribution from $\eta(547)$-exchange using $g_{\eta N} = 4.64$
- $\Omega^{(\omega)} = 0.28$ fm$^4$: contribution from $\omega$-exchange
- $\Omega^{(\rho)} = 0.74$ fm$^4$: contribution from $\rho^0$-exchange with $g_{\rho N} = 3.04$ and $\kappa_\rho = 6.1$
- $\Omega^{(\sigma)} = -0.02$ fm$^4$: negligibly small contribution from scalar $\sigma$-meson exchange with $m_\sigma = 550$ MeV and $g_{\sigma N} = 8.5$
- $\Omega^{(an)} = -0.93$ fm$^4$: contribution from the anomalous $\omega\rho\pi$-vertex with $G_{\omega\rho\pi} = -1.2$
- Experimental cross sections: $\sigma_{\rm tot}^{\rm exp} = 0.32 \pm 0.08$ $\mu$b at $Q = 3.8$ MeV, $0.70 \pm 0.14$ $\mu$b at $Q = 9.1$ MeV, $1.07 \pm 0.25$ $\mu$b at $Q = 14.4$ MeV, $1.51 \pm 0.30$ $\mu$b at $Q = 19.6$ MeV, $1.77 \pm 0.55$ $\mu$b at $Q = 30.1$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the author's previous approach to meson production in $pp$ collisions (Kaiser 1998) from $\pi^0$, $pn\pi^+$, $pp\eta$, and $p\Lambda K^+$ channels to the $\omega$ channel, and agrees qualitatively with the Julich meson exchange model (Hanhart 1998, 1999). The combined analysis of $pp\to pp\pi^0$, $pn\pi^+$, $pp\eta$, $pp\omega$ and $pn\to pn\eta$ near threshold using a common set of coupling constants ($g_{\omega N}=9.8$, $g_{\eta N}=5.22$, $G_{\omega\rho\pi}=-1.03$) shows remarkable consistency across all channels, though the author notes that dynamical complexities may only appear in more exclusive observables like angular distributions and polarization asymmetries.
