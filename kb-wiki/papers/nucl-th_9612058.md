# Deformation of Nuclei Close to the Two-Neutron Drip Line in Mg Region

> We perform the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations for ground states of even Mg isotopes using the Skyrme force and a density-dependent zero-range pairing force. The HFB equation is solved in a three-dimensional cartesian mesh, and a convergence of deformation is carefully examined with respect to a cut-off radius for a check of the calculations. We discuss systematics of the two-neutron separation energy, deformation and root-mean-square radius. We have found that 36,38,40Mg have appreciable static deformation, where 40Mg is a two-neutron drip-line nucleus in our calculation, and the deformations of the neutron and proton are different in these three nuclei. The deformation property is analyzed on the basis of the single-particle diagram. It is shown that N=28 is not a closed shell in Mg as well as Si.

# Deformation of Nuclei Close to the Two-Neutron Drip Line in Mg Region

## Key claim

The authors find that 36,38,40Mg have appreciable static deformation, with 40Mg being the two-neutron drip-line nucleus in their calculation, but they note that the result depends on the specific Skyrme parametrization used (SIII or SLy4) and that the deformation energy curve for 32Mg still shows a spherical minimum, which may overestimate the N=20 shell effect compared to experimental evidence.

## Method

The authors solve the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations on a three-dimensional cartesian mesh for ground states of even Mg isotopes, using a Skyrme force (SIII or SLy4) for the mean-field channel and a density-dependent zero-range pairing force with strength V0 = 1000 MeV fm^3. The calculations use a box size of 15 fm, 70 single-particle wave functions for neutrons and 35 for protons, and a smooth cut-off of the pairing interaction at 5 MeV above the Fermi level.

## Key numbers

- 40Mg and 20Mg are predicted to be at the two-neutron and two-proton drip lines, respectively.
- The two-proton separation energy of 20Mg calculated with SIII is 3.31 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 2.33 MeV.
- For 40Mg, the number of neutrons outside a sphere of radius 15 fm is 0.430 × 10^(-2) for box size 16 fm and 0.683 × 10^(-2) for box size 18 fm, indicating no neutron halo.
- The asymptotic values of the neutron and proton deformations β_n and β_p for 40Mg are reached when the cutoff radius r_in equals 6.0 fm, which is 5/3 of the root-mean-square radius (3.6 fm).
- The N=28 single-particle gap in 40Mg is about 3 MeV, much smaller than the 5 MeV gap found in 48Ca with the same effective force.
- 40Mg is bound by less than 2.0 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier mean-field studies of Mg isotopes that used the BCS approximation with a constant gap (Ren et al., 1996; Werner et al., 1996), which the authors note breaks down when bound and unbound single-particle states interact via pairing correlations. The results contradict the suggestion by Tanihata et al. (1995) that all drip-line nuclei are spherical. The calculations enable future investigation of whether heavy Mg isotopes are the lightest examples of nuclei with differing neutron and proton deformations, and suggest that dynamical quadrupole collective effects may play an important role in 34Ne, 42Si, and 46Si.
