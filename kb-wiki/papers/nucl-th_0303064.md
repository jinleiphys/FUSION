# Probing Delta structure with pion electromagnetic production

> The Dubna-Mainz-Taipei dynamical model for pion electromagnetic production, which can describe well the existing data from threshold up to 1 GeV photon lab energy, is presented and used to analyze the recent precision data in the $Δ$ region. We find that, within our model, the bare Delta is almost spherical while the physical Delta is oblate. The deformation is almost saturated by the pion cloud effects. We further find that up to Q^2 = 4.0 (GeV/c)^2, the extracted helicity amplitude A_{3/2} and A_{1/2} remain comparable with each other, implying that hadronic helicity is not conserved at this range of Q^2. The ratio E_{1+}/M_{1+} obtained show, starting from a small and negative value at the real photon point, a clear tendency to cross zero, and to become positive with increasing Q^2. This is a possible indication of a very slow approach toward the pQCD region. Finally, we find that the bare helicity amplitude A_{1/2} and S_{1/2}, but not A_{3/2}, starts exhibiting the scaling behavior at about Q^2 \ge 2.5 (GeV/c)^2.

## Key claim
Within the Dubna-Mainz-Taipei dynamical model, the bare Delta is almost spherical while the physical Delta is oblate, with the deformation almost saturated by pion cloud effects; however, the extracted helicity amplitudes A_{3/2} and A_{1/2} remain comparable up to Q^2 = 4.0 (GeV/c)^2, implying that hadronic helicity is not conserved in this range, and the ratio E_{1+}/M_{1+} shows a clear tendency to cross zero and become positive with increasing Q^2, indicating a very slow approach toward the perturbative QCD region.

## Method
The authors used the Dubna-Mainz-Taipei (DMT) dynamical model for pion electromagnetic production, which decomposes the t-matrix into background and resonant contributions, with the background described by Born terms and t-channel vector meson exchanges and the resonant part treated via a Breit-Wigner form for the Delta(1232). The model was fit to existing data from threshold up to 1 GeV photon lab energy, and then applied to analyze recent precision JLab differential cross section data on p(e,e'p)pi^0 at Q^2 = 2.8 and 4.0 (GeV/c)^2, using a global fitting procedure with 751 and 867 data points respectively.

## Key numbers
- Bare magnetic helicity amplitude A_{1/2}^{Delta} = -80 +/- 2 (10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}) and A_{3/2}^{Delta} = -136 +/- 3 (10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}) at Q^2 = 0.
- Dressed magnetic helicity amplitude A_{1/2}^{Delta} = -134 +/- 2 (10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}) and A_{3/2}^{Delta} = -256 +/- 2 (10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}) at Q^2 = 0, in agreement with PDG values of -140 +/- 5 and -258 +/- 6 respectively.
- Bare transition magnetic dipole moment mu_{N->Delta} = 1.922 mu_N and dressed value = 3.516 mu_N at Q^2 = 0, compared to PDG value of 3.512 mu_N.
- Bare transition electric quadrupole moment Q_{N->Delta} = 0.009 fm^2 and dressed value = -0.081 fm^2 at Q^2 = 0, compared to PDG value of -0.072 fm^2.
- Best fit parameters for the form factor F(Q^2) = (1 + beta Q^2) e^{-gamma Q^2} G_D(Q^2) gave gamma = 0.42 (GeV/c)^{-2} and beta = 0.61 (GeV/c)^{-2}.
- Bare G_M^*(0) = 1.65 +/- 0.02 and dressed G_M^*(0) = 3.06 +/- 0.02.
- The bare helicity amplitude A_{1/2} and S_{1/2} start exhibiting pQCD scaling behavior at about Q^2 >= 2.5 (GeV/c)^2.

## Context
This work extends the dynamical model approach of Yang (1985) and Tanabe and Ohta (1985) by constructing the DMT model that describes data from threshold to 1 GeV, and applies it to analyze JLab data from Frolov et al. (1999). It contrasts with the MAID model of Drechsel et al., which uses a K-matrix approximation without off-shell rescattering contributions, leading to different predictions for the Q^2 evolution of R_{EM} and R_{SM}. The results enable further investigation of the transition from non-perturbative to perturbative QCD regimes in the Delta resonance, and suggest that scaling behavior may set in earlier than helicity conservation, consistent with predictions from Brodsky (1981) and Carlson and Stoler.
