# Compton scattering from $^4$He at the TUNL HI$γ$S facility

> Differential cross sections for elastic Compton scattering from $^4$He have been measured with high statistical precision at the High Intensity $γ$-ray Source at laboratory scattering angles of $55^\circ$, $90^\circ$, and $125^\circ$ using a quasi-monoenergetic photon beam with a weighted mean energy value of $81.3$ MeV. The results are compared to previous measurements and similar fore-aft asymmetry in the angular distribution of the differential cross sections is observed. This experimental work is expected to strongly motivate the development of effective-field-theory calculations of Compton scattering from $^4$He to fully interpret the data.

## Key claim

Differential cross sections for elastic Compton scattering from 4He at 81.3 MeV show a strong fore-aft asymmetry with backward peaking, but a full effective-field-theory calculation for 4He is not yet available, so the data cannot yet be used to extract nucleon polarizabilities.

## Method

The experiment used a quasi-monoenergetic, circularly polarized photon beam from the HIγS facility incident on a cryogenic liquid 4He target (thickness 4.17 × 10^23 nuclei/cm², maintained at 3.4 K). Scattered photons were detected by an array of eight NaI(Tl) detectors at laboratory angles of 55°, 90°, and 125°, with cosmic-ray background rejected via anticoincidence shield cuts and time-of-flight selection. The detector response function was obtained from a GEANT4 simulation of the full apparatus, and the final energy spectra were fitted with the simulated lineshape plus background components to extract yields.

## Key numbers

- Weighted mean incident photon energy: 81.3 MeV, with beam energy profile peaked at 85.1 MeV and FWHM of 5.5 MeV (6.5%)
- Differential cross section at 55°: 78.0 ± 1.9 (stat) ± 2.4 (syst) nb/sr
- Differential cross section at 90°: 61.9 ± 1.3 (stat) ± 1.6 (syst) nb/sr
- Differential cross section at 125°: 97.0 ± 1.5 (stat) ± 2.5 (syst) nb/sr
- Target thickness: (4.17 ± 0.04) × 10^23 nuclei/cm²
- Effective solid angles ranged from 63.4 to 66.9 msr
- Total systematic uncertainty per angle: 2.5%–3.5%
- Cross section approximately a factor of 6 to 8 larger than that from the deuteron

## Context

This work extends the previous HIγS measurement on 4He at 61 MeV (Sikora et al., 2017) to a higher energy of 81.3 MeV, providing greater sensitivity to nucleon polarizabilities. The results agree with earlier Lund data at 87 MeV (Fuhrberg et al., 1995) and show a similar fore-aft asymmetry distinct from the 61 MeV data. The high statistical precision of both HIγS measurements is expected to motivate development of EFT calculations for 4He Compton scattering, analogous to existing EFT treatments for 3He (Margaryan et al., 2018; Shukla et al., 2018), enabling future extraction of isoscalar nucleon polarizabilities.
