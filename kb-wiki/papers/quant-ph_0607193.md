# Efimov states and their Fano resonances in a neutron-rich nucleus

> Asymmetric resonances in elastic n+$^{19}$C scattering are attributed to Efimov states of such neutron-rich nuclei, that is, three-body bound states of the n+n+$^{18}$C system when none of the pairs is bound or some of them only weakly bound. By fitting to the general resonance shape described by Fano, we extract resonance position, width, and the "Fano profile index". While Efimov states have been discussed extensively in many areas of physics, there is only one very recent experimental observation in trimers of cesium atoms. The conjunction that we present of the Efimov and Fano phenomena may lead to experimental realization in nuclei.

# Efimov states and their Fano resonances in a neutron-rich nucleus

## Key claim
Asymmetric resonances observed in elastic n+$^{19}$C scattering are attributed to Efimov states of the n+n+$^{18}$C three-body system, but only when the two-body binding energy of n+$^{18}$C is tuned above specific thresholds (approximately 220 keV for the first excited state and 140 keV for the second) so that the Efimov states become unbound and appear as resonances embedded in the continuum.

## Method
The authors analyzed elastic scattering cross sections for n+$^{19}$C calculated in a prior study (Mazumdar et al., 2006) by fitting the resonance profiles to the Fano formula $\sigma = \sigma_0 [(q+\epsilon)^2/(1+\epsilon^2)]$, where $\epsilon = (E-E_r)/(\Gamma/2)$. They extracted the resonance position $E_r$, width $\Gamma$, and Fano profile index $q$ for resonances appearing when the two-body binding energy of n+$^{18}$C was tuned above the thresholds where Efimov states become unbound.

## Key numbers
- For the first excited Efimov state with n-$^{18}$C binding energy of 250 keV: resonance position $E_r$ = 1.63 keV, width $\Gamma$ = 0.25 keV, and Fano profile index $q$ = 4.0.
- The first excited Efimov state becomes unbound at approximately 220 keV binding energy of n+$^{18}$C.
- The second excited Efimov state becomes unbound at approximately 140 keV binding energy of n+$^{18}$C.
- The second excited Efimov state resonance at 150 keV binding energy fits the Fano formula with approximately the same $q$ value as the 250 keV curve.
- In the related system $^{19}$B, three Efimov states were observed for a scattering length of -179 fm.

## Context
This work extends prior calculations of Efimov states in neutron-rich nuclei (Mazumdar et al., 2006) by connecting them to Fano resonance theory (Fano, 1961), which describes asymmetric resonance profiles arising from interference between direct and discrete-state pathways. The study contrasts with the atomic case of doubly-excited helium states, where long-range Coulomb forces readily provide infinite series of such resonances; in nuclear systems, the Efimov effect is necessary to produce the weakly bound states that generate Fano resonances. The authors suggest that analogous resonances may be observable in other neutron-rich nuclei such as $^{19}$B and $^{22}$C, and propose alternative experimental routes including gamma-ray photoexcitation of $^{20}$C and fragmentation of $^{20}$C on heavy targets.
