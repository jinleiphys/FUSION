# New Developments and Applications of Thermal Field Theory

> The lecture provides an introduction to thermal field theory and its applications to the physics of the quark-gluon plasma, possibly created in relativistic heavy ion collisions. In particular the Hard Thermal Loop resummation technique, providing a consistent perturbative description of relativistic, high-temperature plasmas is introduced. Using this method interesting quantities of the quark-gluon plasma (damping rates, energy loss, photon and dilepton production) are discussed. Furthermore recent developments on non-equilibrium field theory, which are relevant for high-energy heavy ion physics, are presented.

## Key claim

The lecture argues that perturbative thermal field theory, specifically the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) resummation technique, can provide a consistent perturbative description of the quark-gluon plasma at high temperatures, but only where the temperature-dependent running coupling constant is small enough (estimated at T = 250 MeV as α_s = 0.3–0.5) and where infrared singularities can be controlled.

## Method

The paper introduces both the Imaginary Time Formalism (ITF), which uses Matsubara frequency sums and imaginary time τ restricted to the interval [0, β), and the Real Time Formalism (RTF), which employs a 2×2 matrix propagator to avoid pinch singularities. The Keldysh representation is then constructed from linear combinations of the RTF components, defining retarded, advanced, and symmetric propagators. The HTL resummation technique is presented as a method to consistently handle infrared divergences in high-temperature gauge theories.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for deconfinement transition: T_c = 150–200 MeV (from lattice calculations)
- Critical baryon density: approximately 10 times nuclear density ρ_0, where ρ_0 = 0.125 GeV/fm³ = 2.2 × 10¹⁷ kg/m³
- Maximum temperature estimates for heavy ion collisions: AGS (BNL) T_max ≈ 150 MeV at √s = 4–5 A·GeV; SPS (CERN) T_max ≈ 190 MeV at √s = 17–20 A·GeV; RHIC (BNL) T_max ≈ 230 MeV at √s = 200 A·GeV; LHC (CERN) T_max ≈ 260 MeV at √s = 5500 A·GeV
- Estimated QGP lifetime in heavy ion collisions: 5–10 fm/c
- Estimated parton number in fireball: approximately 10000
- Maximum fireball volume (U-U collisions): approximately 3000 fm³
- Pre-equilibrium period: approximately 1 fm/c = 3 × 10⁻²⁴ s
- Running coupling estimate at T = 250 MeV: α_s = 0.3–0.5

## Context

This lecture extends the perturbative QCD approach to finite temperature, building on the work of Fetter and Walecka on non-relativistic Green functions at finite temperature and on lattice QCD results for static quantities such as the critical temperature and equation of state. The HTL resummation technique enables calculation of dynamical quantities (damping rates, energy loss, photon and dilepton production) that lattice QCD cannot easily address, and the RTF formulation provides a starting point for non-equilibrium field theory relevant to the early stages of heavy ion collisions.
