# Mass modification of D-meson in hot hadronic matter

> We evaluate the in-medium $D$ and $\bar D$-meson masses in hot hadronic matter induced by interactions with the light hadron sector described in a chiral SU(3) model. The effective Lagrangian approach is generalized to SU(4) to include charmed mesons. We find that the D-mass drops substantially at finite temperatures and densities, which open the channels of the decay of the charmonium states ($Ψ^\prime$, $χ_c$, $J/Ψ$) to $D \bar D$ pairs in the thermal medium. The effects of vacuum polarisations from the baryon sector on the medium modification of the $D$-meson mass relative to those obtained in the mean field approximation are investigated. The results of the present work are compared to calculations based on the QCD sum-rule approach, the quark-meson coupling model, chiral perturbation theory, as well as to studies of quarkonium dissociation using heavy quark potential from lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The D-meson mass drops substantially in hot hadronic matter due to attractive scalar meson exchange interactions, with the magnitude of the drop depending on whether mean-field or relativistic Hartree approximations are used; the effect is larger than predictions from chiral perturbation theory and comparable to QCD sum-rule and quark-meson coupling model results.

## Method

The authors generalized a chiral SU(3) model to SU(4) to include charmed mesons, describing the interactions of D-mesons with light hadrons through scalar meson exchange, vector interactions, omega exchange, and range terms. They solved the mesonic field equations by minimizing the thermodynamical potential in both the mean-field approximation and the relativistic Hartree approximation (which includes vacuum polarization effects from the baryon Dirac sea). The coefficient d1 in the interaction Lagrangian was fixed by fitting to the empirical isospin-averaged KN scattering length of approximately -0.255 fm.

## Key numbers

- The DN sigma term calculated in the chiral model is 7366 MeV, ignoring the charm condensate contribution to the nuclear scalar density.
- The D-decay constant is taken as 135 MeV.
- The isospin-averaged KN scattering length used for fitting is approximately -0.255 fm.
- The coefficient d1 is evaluated as 5.63/mK in the mean field case and 4.33/mK in the relativistic Hartree approximation.
- The D-mass shows a turnover at around 0.4 times nuclear matter density (rho0) in the mean field approximation.
- The QMC model predicts a D-meson mass shift of about 60 MeV at nuclear matter density.

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) model previously used for hot and strange hadronic matter (Kristof 2001) to the charm sector, and compares its predictions to QCD sum-rule calculations (Arata 2000), the quark-meson coupling model (QMC), chiral perturbation theory (Kaplan 1995), and lattice QCD studies of quarkonium dissociation using heavy quark potentials (Digal 2001). The results enable improved modeling of charmonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions, as the substantial D-mass drop opens decay channels for excited charmonium states (Psi', chi_c, J/Psi) to D Dbar pairs in the thermal medium, which could explain the anomalous J/Psi suppression observed by the NA50 collaboration at SPS energies.
