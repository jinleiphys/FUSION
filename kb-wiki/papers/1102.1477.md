# Gas-like state of $α$ clusters around $^{16}$O core in $^{24}$Mg

> We have studied gas-like states of $α$ clusters around an $^{16}$O core in $^{24}$Mg based on a microscopic $α$-cluster model. This study was performed by introducing a Monte Carlo technique for the description of the THSR (Tohsaki Horiuchi Schuck Röpke) wave function, and the coupling effect to other low-lying cluster states was taken into account. A large isoscalar monopole ($E0$) transition strength from the ground to the gas-like state is discussed. The gas-like state of two $α$ clusters in $^{24}$Mg around the $^{16}$O core appears slightly below the 2$α$-threshold e

## Key claim

The paper claims that a gas-like state of two alpha clusters around an 16O core in 24Mg appears slightly below the 2-alpha threshold energy, but this result depends on the spatial extension parameter (sigma) of the THSR wave function and on coupling to normal cluster states; the state survives coupling only when the spatial extension is comparable to that of the second 0+ state of 12C.

## Method

The authors introduced a Monte Carlo technique (the "virtual THSR" wave function) to describe alpha-condensed states, where Gaussian center parameters for two alpha clusters around a 16O core are randomly generated with a Gaussian weight function. They then diagonalized the Hamiltonian including both these gas-like basis states (with sigma = 3, 4, 5, and 6 fm) and 200 different configurations of normal 16O+alpha+alpha cluster states, using the Volkov No.2 effective N-N potential with Majorana exchange parameter M = 0.63. Parity and angular momentum projection to 0+ was performed numerically with 4096 Euler angle mesh points.

## Key numbers

- The lowest state of 16O+2alpha (24Mg) is calculated at -16.24 MeV from the three-body threshold energy, compared with the experimental value of -14.046 MeV
- The 7th 0+ state at -4.267 MeV (from the 16O+alpha+alpha threshold) shows a squared isoscalar monopole (E0) transition strength of 187.5 fm4 from the ground state
- The 7th 0+ state has about 40% squared overlap with the THSR wave function for sigma = 5 fm (0.392) and sigma = 4 fm (0.426)
- The 10th 0+ state at 0.140 MeV (just above threshold) has a squared E0 strength of 73.62 fm4
- For sigma = 3 fm, the converged energy of the gas-like state is approximately -6 MeV below the threshold

## Context

This work extends the study of alpha-cluster gas-like states from light nuclei (12C and 16O) to heavier systems with a core, following experimental hints from Kokalova (2005) and Kokalova (2006) of enhanced 8Be and 12C(0+2) emission from compound states in 52Fe. It builds on the THSR wave function formalism of Tohsaki (2001) and the virtual THSR technique of Itagaki (2007), and it enables experimental searches for large isoscalar monopole transitions as signatures of well-developed alpha cluster states in 24Mg, as proposed by Kawabata (2007).
