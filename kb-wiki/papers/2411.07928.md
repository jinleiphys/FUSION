# Nonlocal interaction and collective excitation in deuteron breakup on ${}^{24}$Mg nucleus

> Deuteron breakup in collision with a ${}^{24}\mathrm{Mg}$ nucleus is studied using rigorous three-body scattering equations, extended to include also the excitation of the nucleus. Predictions based on local and nonlocal nucleon-nucleus optical potentials with rotational quadrupole deformation enabling the excitation of the ${}^{24}\mathrm{Mg}(2^+)$ state are compared. The nonlocality effect is less pronounced than in the deuteron inelastic scattering ${}^{24}\mathrm{Mg}(d,d')$ at the same energies, and manifests itself quite differently for semi-inclusive differential cross sections of elastic and inelastic breakup.

# Nonlocal interaction and collective excitation in deuteron breakup on ${}^{24}$Mg nucleus

## Key claim

The nonlocality effect in deuteron breakup on ${}^{24}$Mg is less pronounced than in deuteron inelastic scattering at the same energies, and manifests itself quite differently for semi-inclusive differential cross sections of elastic and inelastic breakup, though the integrated cross section for inelastic breakup is almost unaffected by nonlocality.

## Method

The authors solved rigorous Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) three-body scattering equations in momentum-space partial wave representation, extended to include collective excitation of the ${}^{24}$Mg nucleus via rotational quadrupole deformation. Local and nonlocal nucleon-nucleus optical potentials from Deltuva (2023) were used as dynamic input, with the nonlocality function having a range of 1 fm; the neutron-proton interaction was described by the CD Bonn potential. The long-range Coulomb force was included using the method of screening and renormalization.

## Key numbers

- Core excitation energy $\Delta m_A = 1.369$ MeV for the first $2^+$ excited state of ${}^{24}$Mg
- At $E_d = 90$ MeV, integrated elastic breakup cross section: local 118(5) mb, nonlocal 132(8) mb
- At $E_d = 90$ MeV, integrated inelastic breakup cross section: local 5.4(3) mb, nonlocal 5.2(2) mb
- At $E_d = 90$ MeV, integrated $(d,d')$ cross section from Deltuva (2023): local 48.9(5) mb, nonlocal 43.5(8) mb
- Proton-nucleus reaction cross section at 34.9 MeV: $\sigma_R = 635 \pm 20$ mb
- Nonlocality effect on integrated cross sections for elastic breakup and $(d,d')$ is slightly above 10%

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev-type calculations of Deltuva (2023) for $d+{}^{24}$Mg scattering, which previously covered elastic and inelastic deuteron scattering, to the deuteron breakup channels including simultaneous core excitation. The study uses the same local and nonlocal nucleon-nucleus optical potentials fitted to $p+{}^{24}$Mg elastic and inelastic scattering data at proton beam energies 30.4, 34.9, 39.9, and 44.9 MeV from Deltuva (2023). The results demonstrate the feasibility of Faddeev-type calculations for deuteron breakup with collective target excitation, enabling future modeling of reactions with other light nuclei and various interaction models.
