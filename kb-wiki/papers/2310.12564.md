# Chiral Analysis of the Nucleon Mass and Sigma Commutator

> Schemes for describing the light quark mass dependence of the nucleon mass calculated in lattice QCD are compared. The three schemes in consideration include a fully relativistic and Lorentz covariant scheme, one that is fully relativistic but not Lorentz covariant, and a semirelativistic scheme utilizing the heavy baryon approximation. Calculations of observables involving pseudoscalar meson loop diagrams generate nonanalytic terms proportional to square roots and logarithms of the quark mass. The three schemes all yield the correct model independent leading and next-to-leading nonanalytic terms of the chiral expansion of the baryon mass. Results for the masses of the other members of the octet are also presented. Here, low-energy coefficients of the analytic terms of the expansion for the nucleon and hyperons are constrained by lattice QCD results and are demonstrated to be independent of the renormalization scheme used. The differences in the leading coefficient of the chiral expansions are found to be consistent with strange quark counting. Using the schemes examined herein, we report results for the pion-nucleon sigma commutator based upon recent lattice results from the CLS Collaboration. We find $σ_{πN}=51.7 \pm 3.2 \pm 1.4$ MeV where the uncertainties are statistical and systematic respectively.

## Key claim

The three chiral extrapolation schemes (covariant, relativistic, and heavy-baryon) yield mutually compatible renormalized residual series coefficients when the regulator cutoff is allowed to vary between schemes, contradicting earlier claims in the literature that the heavy-baryon approximation introduces significant discrepancies.

## Method

The authors compare three finite-range regularization (FRR) schemes for extrapolating lattice QCD nucleon masses to the physical pion mass: a fully relativistic Lorentz-covariant scheme with a 4-dimensional dipole regulator, a fully relativistic scheme with a 3-dimensional dipole regulator, and a semirelativistic heavy-baryon scheme with a 3-dimensional dipole regulator. They fit the renormalized chiral expansion of the octet baryon masses to lattice QCD data from the PACS-CS and CLS collaborations, including pion, kaon, and eta loop contributions with octet and decuplet intermediate baryons, plus tadpole terms parameterized by the SU(2) low-energy constants \(c_1, c_2, c_3\). Finite-volume corrections are applied to the lattice data, and lattice spacing effects are treated at \(\mathcal{O}(a^2)\).

## Key numbers

*   Pion-nucleon sigma commutator: \(\sigma_{\pi N} = 51.7 \pm 3.2 \pm 1.4\) MeV, where the first uncertainty is statistical and the second is systematic, based on CLS Collaboration lattice data.
*   Regulator cutoff correspondence: for the nucleon, the covariant scheme requires \(\Lambda = 1.05\) GeV and the relativistic scheme requires \(\Lambda = 1.01\) GeV to match the heavy-baryon scheme at \(\Lambda_{\text{(HB)}} = 0.8\) GeV.
*   Tadpole coefficient \(\chi C \equiv -2c_1 + c_2/4 + c_3 + \mathcal{A}/4 = -1.3_{-1.1}^{+3.3}\) GeV\(^{-1}\), where \(\mathcal{A} \approx 3.1\) GeV\(^{-1}\) accounts for the \(N\Delta\pi\) next-to-leading nonanalytic contribution.
*   The next-to-leading nonanalytic coefficient from the \(N\Delta\pi\) loop in the limit \(m_\pi \ll \Delta_{\Delta N}\) is approximately \(-3.1\) GeV\(^{-1}\), within the range \(-8.4^{+5.2}_{-4.4}\) GeV\(^{-1}\) quoted by Frink and Meissner (2005) for the tadpole NLNA coefficient.

## Context

This work extends previous chiral extrapolation studies of the nucleon mass from lattice QCD, notably those by the PACS-CS collaboration (2009) and the CLS collaboration (Ottnad 2022), by systematically comparing relativistic and heavy-baryon formulations. It addresses the historical criticism that the heavy-baryon approximation introduces significant errors in analyses of lattice data, showing instead that the three schemes are compatible when the regulator cutoff is adjusted. The result for \(\sigma_{\pi N}\) provides a new determination from the most recent CLS lattice data, and the demonstration that the low-energy coefficients are independent of the renormalization scheme enables more reliable chiral extrapolations of baryon masses from lattice QCD.
