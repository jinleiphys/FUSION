# Singlet S-wave superfluidity of proton in neutron star matter

> The possible $^{1}S_{0}$ protonic superfluidity is investigated in neutron star matter, and the corresponding energy gap as a function of baryonic density is calculated on the basis of BCS gap equation. We have discussed particularly the influence of hyperon degrees of freedom on $^{1}S_{0}$ protonic superfluidity. It is found that the appearance of hyperons leads to a slight decrease of $^{1}S_{0}$ protonic pairing energy gap in most density range of existing $^{1}S_{0}$ protonic superfluidity. However, when the baryonic density $ρ_{B}>$0.377 (or 0.409) fm${^{-3}}$ for TM1 (or TMA) parameter set, $^{1}S_{0}$ protonic pairing energy gap is significantly larger than the corresponding values without hyperons. And the baryonic density range of existing $^{1}S_{0}$ protonic superfluidity is widen due to the appearance of hyperons. In our results, the hyperons not only change the EOS and bulk properties but also change the size and baryon density range of $^{1}S_{0}$ protonic superfluidity in neutron star matter.

## Key claim
The paper claims that including hyperon degrees of freedom in neutron star matter widens the baryonic density range where singlet S-wave protonic superfluidity exists and, above a threshold density (0.377 fm^{-3} for TM1 or 0.409 fm^{-3} for TMA), increases the pairing energy gap relative to matter without hyperons, even though hyperons slightly reduce the peak gap value.

## Method
The authors used the relativistic mean field (RMF) model with TM1 and TMA parameter sets to describe neutron star matter, considering two compositions: nucleons plus leptons only (npeμ) and nucleons plus hyperons (Λ, Ξ0, Ξ−) plus leptons (npHeμ). They solved the BCS gap equation using the Reid soft core (RSC) potential for the proton-proton interaction in the 1S0 channel, computing the pairing energy gap as a function of baryonic density under charge neutrality and β-equilibrium conditions.

## Key numbers
- Peak 1S0 protonic pairing energy gap Δp_max = 1.560 MeV for TM1 in npeμ matter, decreasing to 1.528 MeV in npHeμ matter; for TMA, Δp_max = 1.068 MeV in npeμ matter and 1.052 MeV in npHeμ matter.
- Corresponding peak critical temperatures Tcp_max = 1.030×10^10 K (TM1 npeμ) and 1.008×10^10 K (TM1 npHeμ); for TMA, Tcp_max = 7.049×10^9 K (npeμ) and 6.943×10^9 K (npHeμ).
- The baryonic density range of 1S0 protonic superfluidity widens from 0.0 – 0.649 fm^{-3} (TM1 npeμ) to 0.0 – 0.953 fm^{-3} (TM1 npHeμ); for TMA, from 0.0 – 0.685 fm^{-3} (npeμ) to 0.0 – 0.853 fm^{-3} (npHeμ).
- Above baryonic density ρB > 0.377 fm^{-3} (TM1) or ρB > 0.409 fm^{-3} (TMA), the pairing energy gap in npHeμ matter becomes significantly larger than in npeμ matter.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of 1S0 protonic superfluidity in neutron stars, which were first suggested by Migdal (1960), by incorporating hyperon degrees of freedom within the RMF framework. Previous calculations using the RSC potential (Nishizaki 1991, Wambach 1993) and various RMF parameter sets had not systematically examined how hyperons modify the pairing gap. The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star cooling, since the protonic superfluid gap suppresses neutrino emission processes such as the direct Urca reaction, and the widened density range implies that superfluidity may persist deeper into the stellar core than previously thought.
