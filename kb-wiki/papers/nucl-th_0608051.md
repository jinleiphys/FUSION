# Dynamical coupled-channel model of meson production reactions in the nucleon resonance region

> A dynamical coupled-channel model is presented for investigating the nucleon resonances in the meson production reactions induced by pions and photons. The model is based on an energy-independent Hamiltonian which is derived from a set of Lagrangians by using a unitary transformation method. By applying the projection operator techniques,we derive a set of coupled-channel equations which satisfy the unitarity conditions within the channel space spanned by the considered two-particle meson-baryon states and the three-particle $ππN$ state. We present and explain in detail a numerical method based on a spline-function expansion for solving the resulting coupled-channel equations which contain logarithmically divergent one-particle-exchange driving terms resulted from the $ππN$ unitarity cut. We show that this driving term can generate rapidly varying structure in the reaction amplitudes associated with the unstable particle channels. It also has large effects in determining the two-pion production cross sections. Our results indicate that cautions must be taken to interpret the $N^*$ parameters extracted from using models which do not include $ππN$ cut effects.

## Key claim

The paper presents a dynamical coupled-channel model for analyzing nucleon resonances in meson production reactions, but the authors explicitly caution that resonance parameters extracted from models that do not include the effects of the $\pi\pi N$ unitarity cut must be interpreted with care, as this cut can generate rapidly varying structure in reaction amplitudes and has large effects on two-pion production cross sections.

## Method

The authors derive an energy-independent Hamiltonian from a set of Lagrangians using a unitary transformation method, then apply projection operator techniques to obtain coupled-channel equations that satisfy unitarity within a channel space spanned by two-particle meson-baryon states ($\gamma N$, $\pi N$, $\eta N$, $\pi\Delta$, $\rho N$, $\sigma N$) and the three-particle $\pi\pi N$ state. They solve the resulting equations using a spline-function expansion method to handle the logarithmically divergent one-particle-exchange driving terms that arise from the $\pi\pi N$ unitarity cut, and present results for two-pion photo-production to illustrate the dynamical consequences of this term.

## Key numbers

- The model is expected to be valid up to invariant mass $W = 2$ GeV, below which three-pion production is very weak.
- Two-pion photo-production cross sections become larger than one-pion photo-production when the $\gamma p$ invariant mass exceeds $W \sim 1.4$ GeV.
- The $\gamma p \rightarrow \pi^+\pi^-p$ production cross section is a factor of about 10 larger than the production cross sections for $K^+\Lambda$, $K^+\Sigma^0$, $K^0\Sigma^+$, $\eta p$, and $\omega p$ channels.
- The analysis identified a new $N^*$($\frac{3}{2}^+, 1720$) resonance and the production of the isobar channel $\pi^+ D_{13}(1520)$ in prior work using the JLab-Moscow State University isobar model.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical model developed by Sato and Lee (2001) for the $\Delta$ region to higher mass nucleon resonances by including the $\pi\pi N$ channel explicitly, addressing limitations of earlier approaches such as the VPI-GWU (SAID) model, the Carnegie-Mellon-Berkeley model, and the Kent State University model which treat non-resonant amplitudes phenomenologically. The authors build on the three-dimensional reduction methods of Blankenbecler and Sugar (1966) and the isobar model approach of Aaron, Amado, and Young (1968), and their results confirm the earlier analysis of Aaron and Amado (1976) regarding the importance of $\pi\pi N$ unitarity cut effects. The model enables future extraction of resonance parameters that can be interpreted in terms of hadron structure calculations and compared with Lattice QCD calculations.
