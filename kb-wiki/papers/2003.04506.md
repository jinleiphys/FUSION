# Light-quark flavours in a triangle

> All of the light flavors seem to work well for bulk strong matter at pressure free, which could be manifested diversely in the form of compact star and strange nugget. Observational tests are discussed.

# Light-quark flavours in a triangle

## Key claim
Bulk strong matter at zero pressure can be stable with three light quark flavours (up, down, strange) in a nearly symmetric configuration, not just with two flavours as in ordinary nuclear matter. The actual condition is that this three-flavour symmetry becomes more perfect as the strong coupling constant increases, and the stable state lies in region "b" of the quark-number triangle, close to the symmetric point where all three flavours are equal.

## Method
The author uses a bag-linked model (applicable to both deconfined quark matter and localized strangeon matter) to compute the thermodynamic potentials for three-flavour quark matter, treating quarks as either free (strange quark matter) or localized in bag-like strangeons. The ratio of electron to baryon number density and the isospin asymmetry are calculated as functions of the strong coupling constant \(\alpha_{\rm c}\), for a fixed baryon density of \(n_{\rm b} \simeq 0.3\)/fm\(^3\). A geometric triangle diagram is introduced to classify possible flavour configurations by their up, down, and strange quark number densities.

## Key numbers
- The typical density of strong matter at pressure free is estimated as \(\rho_{\rm SM} \sim 2 \times 10^{15}\) g/cm\(^3\), representative of nuclear density \(\rho_0\), using constituent quark mass \(\tilde{m}_{\rm q} = 0.3\) GeV and strong coupling \(\alpha_{\rm c} = 1\).
- For gravity-free strong matter with baryon density \(n_{\rm b} \simeq 0.3\)/fm\(^3\), the ratio of electron to baryon number density \(\delta_{\rm e} = n_{\rm e}/n_{\rm b}\) ranges from \(10^{-7}\) to \(10^{-5}\).
- The down-quark excess over up-quark, \(\delta_{\rm du} = (n_{\rm d} - n_{\rm u})/n_{\rm b}\), is at a level of 1% to 9% for \(n_{\rm b} = 0.3\)/fm\(^3\).
- The magnetic field on a strangeon nugget surface is estimated as \(B \sim 2 \times 10^{11}\) G, for \(\delta_{\rm e} = 10^{-5}\) and \(n_{\rm b} = 2\rho_0 \simeq 0.3\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The stopping distance for a strangeon nugget in solid earth is \(L \simeq 8 \times 10^4 (N_{\rm b}/10^{12})^{1/3}\) cm \(\cdot\) (1 g cm\(^{-3}/\rho_{\rm m}\)), so nuggets with baryon number \(N_{\rm b} < 10^{24}\) slow significantly in the Earth.

## Context
This work extends the long-standing discussion of strange quark matter initiated by Witten (1984) and the strangeon matter model proposed by Xu (2003), contrasting with the traditional neutron star (Landau's "gigantic nucleus") and hyperon star scenarios. The triangle diagram systematizes the possible flavour configurations of bulk strong matter, and the author suggests that observational tests could come from compact star phenomenology (e.g., distinguishing neutron stars from strange stars via magnetospheric activity) and from searches for strangeon nuggets passing through the Earth, which could be detected via air showers or seismic signals. The paper also notes that the FAST radio telescope (Jiang 2019) may identify signatures via single pulses (Lu 2019).
