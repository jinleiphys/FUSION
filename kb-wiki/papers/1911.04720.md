# Evolution of fluctuations in the initial state of heavy-ion collisions from RHIC to LHC

> Fluctuations in the initial state of heavy-ion collisions are larger at RHIC energy than at LHC energy. This fact can be inferred from recent measurements of the fluctuations of the particle multiplicities and of elliptic flow performed at the two different energies. We show that an analytical description of the initial energy-density field and its fluctuations motivated by the color glass condensate (CGC) effective theory predicts and quantitatively captures the measured energy evolution of these observables. The crucial feature is that fluctuations in the CGC scale like the inverse of the saturation scale of the nuclei.

# Evolution of fluctuations in the initial state of heavy-ion collisions from RHIC to LHC

## Key claim

Initial-state fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions are larger at RHIC energy than at LHC energy, and this energy evolution is quantitatively captured by a Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory model where fluctuations scale as the inverse of the saturation scale of the nuclei, provided that the pre-equilibrium dynamics of the system during the first fm/c of its evolution is taken into account.

## Method

The authors compare experimental data on charged-particle multiplicity fluctuations and elliptic flow fluctuations from STAR (Au+Au at sqrt(s)=200 GeV) and ATLAS (Pb+Pb at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV). They then compute the same observables using an analytical CGC-based model of the initial energy-density field, where the local average energy density is proportional to the product of the saturation scales squared of the two nuclei, and fluctuations are given by a short-range connected 2-point function proportional to the saturation scales. The model is implemented in a Monte Carlo code called magma, and the saturation scale values are fitted from anisotropic flow data and checked against HERA scaling.

## Key numbers

- Relative fluctuations of charged-particle multiplicity in central collisions: sigma[N_ch]/<N_ch>(b=0) = 0.065 at RHIC (Au+Au, sqrt(s)=200 GeV) and 0.044 at LHC (Pb+Pb, sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV), a ratio of approximately 1.5.
- Saturation scale ratio from HERA fits: Q_s[LHC]/Q_s[RHIC] = (sqrt(s_LHC)/sqrt(s_RHIC))^0.14 ≈ 1.5.
- Relative fluctuations of total energy per unit longitudinal length in the CGC model for central collisions: sigma[E]/<E>(b=0) = 0.134 at RHIC and 0.088 at LHC, approximately a factor of 2 larger than the multiplicity fluctuations.
- Infrared cutoff scale used in the model: m = 0.14 GeV (pion mass).

## Context

This work extends the CGC-based initial condition model introduced by Giacalone et al. (2019) and builds on the fluctuation statistics derived by Albacete et al. (2018). It directly compares with experimental multiplicity data from STAR (Adamczyk et al., 2015) and ATLAS (Aaboud et al., 2019), and with elliptic flow cumulant measurements from the same collaborations. The results enable quantitative tests of the CGC framework across collision energies and highlight the need to understand pre-equilibrium dynamics (Schlichting et al., 2019) that modify the relationship between initial energy fluctuations and final entropy fluctuations (Giacalone et al., 2019).
