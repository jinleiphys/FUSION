# Atomic Cascade in Muonic and Hadronic Hydrogen Atoms

> The atomic cascade in $μ^- p$ and $π^- p$ atoms has been studied with the improved version of the extended cascade model in which new quantum mechanical calculations of the differential and integral cross sections of the elastic scattering, Stark transitions and Coulomb de-excitation have been included for the principal quantum number values $n\le 8$ and the relative energies $E \ge 0.01$ eV. The $X$-ray yields and kinetic energy distributions are compared with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The improved extended cascade model, incorporating new quantum-mechanical close-coupling cross sections for elastic scattering, Stark transitions, and Coulomb de-excitation for principal quantum numbers \(n \le 8\) and relative energies \(E \ge 0.01\) eV, can reproduce the observed kinetic energy distributions of \(\pi^- p\) atoms at nuclear absorption, including the high-energy components near 105 eV and 209 eV, without employing any fitting parameters, in contrast to previous semiclassical or adiabatic approaches.

## Method

The atomic cascade in \(\mu^- p\) and \(\pi^- p\) atoms was simulated using an extended standard cascade model (ESCM) that divides the cascade into a classical domain (for \(n \ge 9\), using classical-trajectory Monte Carlo with molecular target structure) and a quantum mechanical domain (for \(n \le 8\), using fully quantum mechanical close-coupling calculations of differential and integral cross sections for all \(nl \mapsto n'l'\) transitions). The external Auger effect was treated semiclassically throughout the cascade, and initial distributions in \(n\), \(l\), and kinetic energy were varied; \(10^6\) events were simulated for each condition.

## Key numbers

- For \(\mu^- p\) at target pressure 10 bar (\(\sim 10^{-2}\) LHD), the Doppler broadening of the \(K_\alpha\) line extends up to \(\pm 1.25\) eV.
- The mean kinetic energy of \(\mu^- p\) at the instant of \(K_\alpha\) radiative transition reaches a maximum above 100 eV at a density of \(\sim 8 \times 10^{-2}\) LHD.
- For \(\mu^- p\), the mean kinetic energy at the instant of radiative transitions increases from 4 eV to 8 eV over the density range \(10^{-7}\) LHD to \(\sim 2 \times 10^{-4}\) LHD; the \(K_\beta\) line mean energy reaches a maximum of \(\sim 20\) eV in the density interval \(10^{-3}\)–\(10^{-2}\) LHD.
- For \(\pi^- p\) in liquid hydrogen, the high-energy components in the kinetic energy distribution at nuclear absorption are located at \(\sim 105\) eV (attributed to \(5 \to 3\) Coulomb transitions) and \(\sim 209\) eV (attributed to \(3 \to 2\) Coulomb transitions).
- The induced absorption parameter for the \(2p\) state in pionic hydrogen was set to 0.045 meV.

## Context

This work extends the extended standard cascade model (ESCM) of Markushin (1990s) and earlier semiclassical treatments by Leon and Bethe (1960s) by incorporating fully quantum mechanical close-coupling cross sections from Jensen, Popov, and Pomerantsev (2006–2007) for collisional processes at low \(n\). The model successfully reproduces experimental X-ray yields for muonic hydrogen from Borie (1980s) and for pionic hydrogen from PSI experiments, and for the first time explains the detailed kinetic energy distribution of \(\pi^- p\) atoms at nuclear absorption measured in neutron time-of-flight experiments (PSI, 1990s). This enables more reliable predictions for future experiments on exotic hydrogen atoms and tests of fundamental interactions.
