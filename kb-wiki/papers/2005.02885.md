# Transport and hydrodynamics in the chiral limit

> We analyze the evolution of hydrodynamic fluctuations for QCD matter below $T_c$ in the chiral limit, where the pions (the Goldstone modes) must be treated as additional non-abelian superfluid degrees of freedom, reflecting the broken $SU_L(2) \times SU_R(2)$ symmetry of the theory. In the presence of a finite pion mass $m_π$, the hydrodynamic theory is ordinary hydrodynamics at long distances, and superfluid-like at short distances. The presence of the superfluid degrees of freedom then gives specific contributions to the bulk viscosity, the shear viscosity, and diffusion coefficients of the ordinary theory at long distances which we compute. This determines, in some cases, the leading dependence of the transport parameters of QCD on the pion mass. We analyze the predictions of this computation, as the system approaches the $O(4)$ critical point.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for QCD matter below the chiral transition temperature, the presence of a finite pion mass means the hydrodynamic theory transitions from superfluid-like behavior at short distances (wavelengths of order the inverse pion mass) to ordinary hydrodynamics at long distances, with the superfluid modes leaving calculable imprints on the ordinary transport coefficients; these corrections determine the leading dependence of QCD transport parameters on the pion mass in some regimes.

## Method

The authors construct a non-abelian superfluid effective theory for the broken SU_L(2) × SU_R(2) symmetry, treating pions as Goldstone modes coupled to the conserved energy-momentum tensor and left/right currents. They extend prior work by including mass terms and treating dissipation non-linearly, deriving constitutive relations from entropy production analysis that yield bulk viscosity, shear viscosity, and diffusion coefficients. The dissipative parameters of the superfluid theory are then matched to the ordinary hydrodynamic description at long distances to compute how the superfluid modes correct the transport coefficients of QCD.

## Key numbers

- The pion velocity is given by v² ≡ f²/χ_A, where f² is the spatial pion decay constant and χ_A is the total axial susceptibility
- The screening mass m is related to the pole mass by m_p² ≡ v² m²
- The total axial charge susceptibility χ_A ≡ χ_0 + χ_1 + f², where χ_0 is the iso-vector susceptibility and χ_A^nrm ≡ χ_0 + χ_1 is the normal component susceptibility
- The chemical equilibration rate Γ_chem ≡ 1/ζ^(2), where ζ^(2) is the dissipative coefficient governing pion damping

## Context

This work extends the non-abelian superfluid equations of motion originally written by Son (1999) and later extended to include dissipation and finite quark mass effects by Son and Stephanov (2002) and Pujol and others (2002). It builds on formal developments by Jain (2016) that clarified the general structure of the equations of motion. The results enable computation of how transport coefficients of QCD depend on the pion mass, with implications for understanding soft pion signals from the chiral phase transition that could be observed in upcoming ALICE ITS detector upgrades.
