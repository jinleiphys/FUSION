# Extension of Glauber-like model for Proton-Proton collisions using anisotropic and inhomogeneous density profile

> Results from proton-proton ($pp$) collisions have routinely been used as a baseline to analyze and understand the production of QCD matter expected to be produced in nuclear collisions. But recent studies of small systems formed in $pp$ collisions at the LHC energies hint at the possibility of producing medium with collective behavior. Therefore, results from $pp$ collisions required more careful investigation to understand whether QCD matter is produced in high multiplicity $pp$ collisions. With this motivation, the Glauber model traditionally used to study the heavy-ion collision dynamics at high energy is applied here to understand the dynamics of $pp$ collisions. We have used anisotropic and inhomogeneous quark/gluon-based proton density profile, a realistic picture obtained from deep inelastic scattering results and this model explains the charged particle multiplicity distribution of $pp$ collisions at LHC energies very well. Collision geometric properties like impact parameter and mean number of binary collisions ($\langle N_{coll} \rangle$), mean number of participants ($\langle N_{part} \rangle$) at different multiplicities are determined for $pp$ collisions. We further used these collision geometric properties to estimate average charged-particle pseudorapidity density ($\langle dN_{ch}/dη\rangle$) and found it to be comparable with the experimental results. Knowing $\langle N_{coll} \rangle$, we have obtained nuclear modification-like factor ($R_{pp}$) in $pp$ collisions which has not been done before to the best of our knowledge.

## Key claim

The authors extend a Glauber-like model to proton-proton collisions by using an anisotropic and inhomogeneous quark/gluon-based proton density profile, and they find that this model can reproduce the charged-particle multiplicity distribution from ALICE data at mid-multiplicity (15 < Nch < 90) with a 5-10% discrepancy, but it fails to describe the extreme low and high multiplicity regions.

## Method

The authors model the proton as three effective quarks connected by gluonic flux tubes, with a density function that includes both quark and gluon components controlled by a parameter κ = 0.5. They compute the overlap function Tpp(b) for colliding protons, then determine the number of binary parton collisions Ncoll(b) and number of participant partons Npart(b) using an effective partonic cross-section σgg = 4.3 ± 0.6 mb with Ng = 10 partons to reproduce the inelastic pp cross-section of 60 mb at √s = 7 TeV. They use a two-component model with negative binomial distribution to estimate charged-particle multiplicity, with free parameters f = 0.85, average multiplicity n̄ = 8, and width parameter k = 0.13.

## Key numbers

- Effective partonic cross-section σgg = 4.3 ± 0.6 mb, used with Ng = 10 partons to reproduce the inelastic pp cross-section of 60 mb at √s = 7 TeV
- Free parameter f = 0.85, average multiplicity n̄ = 8, and width parameter k = 0.13 give best agreement with ALICE data for charged-particle multiplicity distribution
- Model reproduces ALICE data in the mid multiplicity region 15 < Nch < 90 with a 5-10% discrepancy
- Rpp is less than 1 for pT < 1 GeV/c and greater than 1 for pT > 1 GeV/c in pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV

## Context

This work extends the Glauber model, traditionally used for heavy-ion collisions, to proton-proton collisions by replacing nucleons with partons (quarks and gluons) and using an anisotropic proton density profile from Kubiczek (2015) rather than the azimuthally symmetric profiles used in earlier work by d'Enterria (2010). The approach enables estimation of collision geometric properties (impact parameter, Ncoll, Npart) for pp collisions and calculation of a nuclear modification-like factor Rpp, which may help investigate whether collective behavior or medium formation occurs in high-multiplicity pp events at LHC energies.
