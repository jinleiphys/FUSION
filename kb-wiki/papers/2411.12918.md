# Equation of state of isospin asymmetric QCD with small baryon chemical potentials

> We extend our measurement of the equation of state of isospin asymmetric QCD to small baryon and strangeness chemical potentials, using the leading order Taylor expansion coefficients computed directly at non-zero isospin chemical potentials. Extrapolating the fully connected contributions to vanishing pion sources is particularly challenging, which we overcome by using information from isospin chemical potential derivatives evaluated numerically. Using the Taylor coefficients, we present, amongst others, first results for the equation of state along the electric charge chemical potential axis, which is potentially of relevance for the evolution of the early Universe at large lepton flavour asymmetries.

## Key claim
The authors extend lattice QCD measurements of the equation of state to small baryon and strangeness chemical potentials by computing leading-order Taylor expansion coefficients directly at non-zero isospin chemical potentials, but the extrapolation of fully connected contributions to vanishing pion sources (the chiral limit) is particularly challenging and is overcome using numerically evaluated isospin chemical potential derivatives.

## Method
The study uses \(N_f=2+1\) flavors of improved rooted staggered quarks with two levels of stout smearing and physical quark masses, performing simulations at non-zero isospin chemical potential \(\mu_I\). The Taylor expansion coefficients \(\chi^L_2\) and \(\chi^s_2\) (the diagonal second derivatives of pressure with respect to light-quark baryon and strange chemical potentials) are computed at leading order \(O(\mu^2)\), with a pionic source term \(\lambda\) used as a regulator in the Bose-Einstein condensate phase; results are obtained after extrapolating to \(\lambda=0\) using an improvement program involving valence quark improvement and leading-order reweighting.

## Key numbers
- The Taylor expansion is performed at temperatures \(T = 123\) MeV, \(T = 147\) MeV, and \(T = 163\) MeV, with isospin chemical potentials up to \(\mu_I/m_\pi = 1.0\).
- The light-quark baryon susceptibility \(\chi^L_2/m_\pi^2\) is computed at \(\mu_I/m_\pi = 0.77\) and \(T = 132\) MeV, with values ranging from approximately \(-1.0\) to \(10.0\) depending on the improvement scheme.
- The strangeness susceptibility \(\chi^s_2/m_\pi^2\) is computed at the same temperatures, with values ranging from \(0.0\) to \(1.1\) over the isospin chemical potential range.
- The pressure along the electric charge chemical potential axis \(p/T^4\) is presented for \(\mu_Q/m_\pi\) up to \(1.8\), with values ranging from \(0.1\) to \(1.6\) at the three temperatures.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous lattice studies of QCD at pure isospin chemical potential (Brandt 2017, Brandt 2018, Brandt 2019, Brandt 2021, Brandt 2022) by using those simulation points as expansion points for a Taylor series in baryon and strangeness chemical potentials, rather than expanding around zero chemical potential as in the standard approach of Gottlieb (1988). The results enable the equation of state to be mapped in the full three-dimensional parameter space of light-quark chemical potentials, which is relevant for the evolution of the early Universe at large lepton flavour asymmetries (Wygas 2018, Vovchenko 2020) and for heavy-ion collision phenomenology.
