# Possible interrelations among chemical freezeout conditions

> At thermal equilibrium, different chemical freezeout conditions have been proposed so far. They have an ultimate aim of proposing a universal description for the chemical freezeout parameters ($T_{ch}$ and $μ_b$), which are to be extracted from the statistical fitting of different particle ratios measured at various collision energies with calculations from thermal models. A systematic comparison between these conditions is presented. The physical meaning of each of them and their sensitivity to the hadron mass cuts are discussed. Based on availability, some of them are compared with recent lattice calculations. We found that most of these conditions are thermodynamically equivalent, especially at small baryon chemical potential. We propose that further crucial consistency tests should be performed at low energies. The fireball thermodynamics is another way of guessing conditions describing the chemical freezeout parameters extracted from high-energy experiments. We endorse the possibility that the various chemical freezeout conditions should be interpreted as different aspects of one universal condition.

## Key claim

The paper argues that most proposed chemical freezeout conditions are thermodynamically equivalent, especially at small baryon chemical potential, and should be interpreted as different aspects of one universal condition; however, the authors hedge that further crucial consistency tests must be performed at low energies, where the equivalence breaks down.

## Method

The authors systematically compare five chemical freezeout conditions (average energy per particle, baryon plus antibaryon density, normalized entropy density, entropy per particle, and trace anomaly) using the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model in the grand canonical ensemble with full chemical equilibrium. They calculate each condition from the freezeout parameters (T_ch and mu_b) extracted from statistical fits to experimental particle ratios at various collision energies from SIS, AGS, SPS, RHIC, and LHC, and compare the conditions with each other and with lattice QCD calculations where available.

## Key numbers

- The average energy per particle condition epsilon/n = 1 GeV was proposed as a universal freezeout condition; the Hawking-Unruh mechanism predicts 1.09 GeV.
- The baryon plus antibaryon density condition n_b + n_anti-b = 0.12 fm^{-3} was proposed; at very high energies the value should be reduced to 0.08 fm^{-3}.
- The normalized entropy density condition s/T^3 = 7 corresponds to three quark flavors; s/T^3 = 5 corresponds to two quark flavors.
- The entropy per particle condition s/n = 7.18 was used; at high energies s/n approximately equals 7.
- The trace anomaly condition (epsilon-3p)/T^4 = 3.5 was proposed; lattice QCD gives values between 2.09 and 2.76 for temperatures between 150 and 160 MeV.
- At small baryon chemical potential, assuming T = 0.16 GeV and epsilon/n = 1 GeV gives s/n = 7.25, showing thermodynamic equivalence.
- The pseudo-critical temperature from lattice QCD is approximately 155 +/- 9 MeV for three quark flavors.

## Context

This work extends a comparison of three chemical freezeout conditions performed about ten years earlier by Cleymans (2006) to include additional conditions and recent RHIC and LHC results. The paper systematically compares the conditions of Cleymans (1998), Braun-Munzinger (2002), Tawfik (2006A, 2006B), Tiwari (2012), Oliinychenko (2013), and Tawfik (2013trace) for trace anomaly. The findings enable future work to test the proposed universal condition at low collision energies where the thermodynamic equivalence is expected to break down, and suggest that fireball thermodynamics provides another way to guess freezeout conditions.
