# Ab initio no-core solutions for $^6$Li

> We solve for properties of $^6$Li in the ab initio No-Core Full Configuration approach and we separately solve for its ground state and $J^π=2_{2}^{+}$ resonance with the Gamow Shell Model in the Berggren basis. We employ both the JISP16 and chiral NNLO$_{opt}$ realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions and investigate the ground state energy, excitation energies, point proton root-mean-square radius and a suite of electroweak observables. We also extend and test methods to extrapolate the ground state energy, point proton root-mean-square radius, and electric quadrupole moment. We attain improved estimates of these observables in the No-Core Full Configuration approach by using basis spaces up through N$_{max}$=18 that enable more definitive comparisons with experiment. Using the Density Matrix Renormalization Group approach with the JISP16 interaction, we find that we can significantly improve the convergence of the Gamow Shell Model treatment of the $^6$Li ground state and $J^π=2_{2}^{+}$ resonance by adopting a natural orbital single-particle basis.

## Key claim

The paper reports that both the JISP16 and chiral NNLO\(_{\rm opt}\) interactions underbind \(^6\)Li relative to experiment, with the underbinding being more pronounced for NNLO\(_{\rm opt}\) (1.44 MeV) than for JISP16 (0.46 MeV) when using the A5 extrapolation method, and that the extrapolated ground state energies differ by about 200 keV between the two extrapolation methods employed.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio No-Core Full Configuration (NCFC) calculations for \(^6\)Li using the JISP16 and chiral NNLO\(_{\rm opt}\) nucleon-nucleon interactions in harmonic oscillator basis spaces up to \(N_{\rm max}=18\). They also performed separate Gamow Shell Model (GSM) calculations in the Berggren basis for the ground state and \(J^{\pi}=2_{2}^{+}\) resonance, using the Density Matrix Renormalization Group (DMRG) approach with a natural orbital single-particle basis to improve convergence. Extrapolation to the infinite basis limit was performed using two different methods: a 5-parameter fit (A5) for energies and a 3-parameter fit (A3) for radii, and a second method based on the infrared length scale \(L_{\rm eff}\).

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of \(^6\)Li with NNLO\(_{\rm opt}\) extrapolated using \(L_{\rm eff}\) method: \(-30.32(7)\) MeV; with JISP16: \(-31.38(3)\) MeV.
- Ground state energy of \(^6\)Li with NNLO\(_{\rm opt}\) extrapolated using A5 method: underbound by 1.44 MeV relative to experiment; with JISP16: underbound by 0.46 MeV.
- Variational minimum ground state energy at \(N_{\rm max}=18\): \(-30.27\) MeV for NNLO\(_{\rm opt}\) at \(\hbar\Omega=20\) MeV; \(-31.43\) MeV for JISP16 at \(\hbar\Omega=17.5\) MeV.
- Point proton root-mean-square radius extrapolated using \(L_{\rm eff}\) method: 2.32(9) fm for NNLO\(_{\rm opt}\); 2.31(6) fm for JISP16.
- Electric quadrupole moment of the ground state extrapolated using \(L_{\rm eff}\) method: \(-0.034(3)\) \(e\,{\rm fm}^2\) for NNLO\(_{\rm opt}\); \(-0.072(1)\) \(e\,{\rm fm}^2\) for JISP16.
- \(B({\rm E2};(3^+,0))\) transition strength extrapolated using \(L_{\rm eff}\) method: 12(2) \(e^2\,{\rm fm}^4\) for NNLO\(_{\rm opt}\); 9.2(6) \(e^2\,{\rm fm}^4\) for JISP16.
- Binding energy of \(^6\)Li relative to the \(\alpha\)-\(d\) threshold at convergence: 0.73 MeV for NNLO\(_{\rm opt}\); 1.01 MeV for JISP16.

## Context

This work extends earlier NCFC studies of \(^6\)Li by Cockrell et al. (2012) by using larger basis spaces up to \(N_{\rm max}=18\) and by including results for the chiral NNLO\(_{\rm opt}\) interaction for the first time for excited states. The results enable more definitive comparisons with experiment and provide benchmarks for testing extrapolation methods. The GSM-DMRG calculations with natural orbitals demonstrate improved convergence for resonance states, which can be applied to other light nuclei with continuum states.
