# Yano-Koonin-Podgoretskii Parametrisation of the Hanbury Brown-Twiss Correlator

> The Yano-Koonin-Podgoretskii (YKP) parametrisation of Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) two-particle correlation functions opens new strategies for extracting the emission duration and testing the longitudinal expansion in heavy-ion collisions. Based on the recently derived model-independent expressions, we present a detailed parameter study of the YKP parameters for a finite, hydrodynamically expanding source model of heavy-ion collisions. For the class of models studied here, we show that the three YKP radius parameters have an interpretation as longitudinal extension, transverse extension and emission duration of the source in the YKP frame. This frame is specified by the fourth fit parameter, the Yano-Koonin velocity which describes to a good approximation the velocity of the fluid element with highest emissivity and allows to test for the longitudinal expansion of the source. Deviations from this interpretation of the YKP parameters are discussed quantitatively.

## Key claim

The Yano-Koonin-Podgoretskii (YKP) parametrisation of the HBT correlation function allows extraction of the emission duration and a test of longitudinal expansion in heavy-ion collisions, but only for the class of models studied here (finite, hydrodynamically expanding sources without surface-dominated emission) do the three YKP radius parameters have a clean interpretation as longitudinal extension, transverse extension, and emission duration in the YKP frame; deviations from this interpretation are discussed quantitatively.

## Method

The authors performed a detailed parameter study of the YKP parameters using a finite, hydrodynamically expanding source model with a Gaussian emission function that includes longitudinal Bjorken scaling flow and a linear transverse flow profile. They computed the second space-time moments of the emission function analytically and numerically, then used model-independent expressions to calculate the YKP radius parameters \(R_\perp\), \(R_\parallel\), \(R_0\) and the Yano-Koonin velocity \(v\) for pions and kaons. The study systematically varied the transverse flow strength \(\eta_f\) and pair momentum to examine how transverse expansion affects the interpretation of the YKP parameters.

## Key numbers

- Source parameters used for numerical calculations: freeze-out temperature \(T = 140\) MeV, transverse geometric radius \(R = 3\) fm, space-time rapidity width \(\Delta \eta = 1.2\), average freeze-out proper time \(\tau_0 = 3\) fm/\(c\), mean proper emission duration \(\Delta\tau = 1\) fm/\(c\).
- For the class of models studied, in the YK frame the YKP parameters approximately satisfy: \(R_\perp^2 \approx \langle \tilde{y}^2 \rangle\), \(R_\parallel^2 \approx \langle \tilde{z}^2 \rangle\), and \(R_0^2 \approx \langle \tilde{t}^2 \rangle\).
- The Yano-Koonin velocity \(v\) is approximately given by \(v \approx \langle \tilde{z} \tilde{t} \rangle / (\langle \tilde{t}^2 \rangle + \langle \tilde{z}^2 \rangle)\) in the YK frame.
- For sources without transverse collective expansion, the YKP parameters show perfect \(M_\perp\)-scaling, being universal functions of the transverse mass \(M_\perp = \sqrt{m^2 + \mathbf{K}_\perp^2}\) independent of particle rest mass; this scaling is broken by transverse flow.

## Context

This work extends the model-independent expressions for HBT parameters derived by Chapman, Scotto, and Heinz (1995) and by Heiselberg and Vischer (1995) by applying them specifically to the YKP parametrisation. It provides a detailed numerical study that complements the short analytical account given by Heinz, Tomasik, Wiedemann, and Wu (1996). The results enable experimental tests of longitudinal expansion via the pair-momentum dependence of the Yano-Koonin velocity, and suggest that comparing pion and kaon correlations can provide an independent estimate of transverse flow velocity, though such estimates remain model-dependent.
