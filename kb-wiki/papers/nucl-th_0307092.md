# Isospin fractionation in the nucleon emissions and fragment emissions in the intermediate energy heavy ion collisions

> The degree of isospin fractionation is measured by $(N/Z)_{n}$ / $(N/Z)_{N_{imf}}$, where $(N/Z)_{n}$ and $(N/Z)_{N_{imf}}$ are the saturated neutron-proton ratio of nucleon emissions (gas phase) and that of fragment emissions (liquid phase) in heavy ion collision at intermediate energy . The calculated results by using the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics model show that the degree of isospin fractionation is sensitive to the neutron-proton ratio of colliding system but insensitive to the difference between the neutron-proton ratio of target and that of projectile. In particular, the degree of isospin fractionation sensitively depends on the symmetry potential. However its dependences on the isospin dependent in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section and momentum dependent interaction are rather weak. The nucleon emission (gas phase) mainly determines the dynamical behavior of the degree of isospin fractionation in the isospin fractionation process, compared to the effect of fragment emission. In this case, we propose that $(N/Z)_{n}$ / $(N/Z)_{N_{imf}}$ or $(N/Z)_{n}$ can be directly compared with the experimental data so that the information about symmetry potential can be obtained

## Key claim

The degree of isospin fractionation, measured by the ratio of the saturated neutron-proton ratio of nucleon emissions to that of fragment emissions, is sensitive to the symmetry potential and the neutron-proton ratio of the colliding system, but is insensitive to the difference between the neutron-proton ratios of target and projectile, the isospin dependence of the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section, and the momentum dependent interaction.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model, which includes a density-dependent mean field with Skyrme, Coulomb, Yukawa, Pauli, symmetry, and momentum-dependent potentials, as well as an in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section. They simulated collisions of various tin isotopes (e.g., 104Sn, 112Sn, 124Sn) and other systems at beam energies around 50 MeV/nucleon, and calculated the impact-parameter-averaged ratio of the neutron-proton ratio of emitted nucleons to that of emitted intermediate mass fragments (IMFs, defined as fragments with charge from 2 to half the total charge of projectile plus target). The time evolution was followed up to 300 fm/c, which roughly corresponds to the freeze-out time.

## Key numbers

*   For the reaction 124Sn+124Sn at E = 50 MeV/nucleon and impact parameter b = 0.0 fm, the ratio (N/Z)n/(N/Z)N_imf is larger than 1.0 for the neutron-rich system, indicating a neutron-rich gas phase and a neutron-poor liquid phase.
*   The neutron-to-proton ratios of the three colliding systems 104Sn+104Sn, 112Sn+112Sn, and 124Sn+124Sn are 1.01, 1.34, and 1.48, respectively.
*   The curvatures K_sym of the three symmetry potentials U1_sym, U2_sym, and U3_sym are -27 MeV, -69 MeV, and 61 MeV, respectively.
*   The strength parameter c of the symmetry potential is 32 MeV.
*   The in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section is parameterized as sigma_NN = (1 + alpha * rho/rho_0) * sigma_free_NN, with alpha approximately -0.2.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of isospin fractionation by Bao-An Li (early 2000s), V. Baran et al. (early 2000s), and others, who investigated the unequal partitioning of neutrons and protons in low- and high-density regions of nuclear matter. The paper proposes that the ratio (N/Z)n/(N/Z)N_imf, or simply (N/Z)n, can be directly compared with experimental data to extract information about the symmetry potential, which is a key ingredient in the nuclear equation of state for asymmetric matter. This enables future experiments to constrain the density dependence of the symmetry potential by measuring the neutron-proton ratio of emitted nucleons and fragments.
