# Phase diagram of chiral quark matter: Fulde-Ferrell pairing from weak to strong coupling

> We calculate the phase diagram of two-flavor quark matter in the temperature-flavor asymmetry plane in the case where there are three competing phases: the homogeneous Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) phase, the unpaired phase, and a phase with broken spatial symmetry, which is here taken to be the counterpart of the Fulde-Ferrell (FF) phase in condensed matter physics. We show that the system belongs to the universality class of paramagnetic-ferromagnetic -helical systems. We vary the coupling constant of the theory, which is obtained from integrating out the gluonic degrees of freedom. In weak coupling, the FF phase is favored at arbitrary flavor asymmetries for sufficiently low temperatures; at intermediate coupling its occupancy domain is shifted towards larger asymmetries. Strong coupling features a new regime of an inhomogeneous FF state, which we identify with a current-carrying Bose-Einstein condensate of tightly bound up and down quarks. We analyze the occupation numbers and the Cooper-pair wave function and show that when the condensate momentum is orthogonal to the particle momentum the minority component contains a blocking region (breach) around the Fermi sphere in the weak-coupling limit, which engulfs more low-momentum modes as the coupling is increased, and eventually leads to a topological change in strong coupling, where the minority Fermi sphere contains either two occupied strips or an empty sphere. For non-orthogonal momenta, the blocking region is either reduced or extinct, i.e., no topological changes are observed.

# Phase diagram of chiral quark matter: Fulde-Ferrell pairing from weak to strong coupling

## Key claim

The Fulde-Ferrell (FF) phase with broken spatial symmetry is favored over the homogeneous BCS phase and the unpaired phase in two-flavor quark matter across a wide range of coupling strengths, but the region of the phase diagram where it dominates shifts toward larger flavor asymmetries as coupling increases. In the strong-coupling limit, the inhomogeneous FF state becomes a current-carrying Bose-Einstein condensate of tightly bound up and down quarks, with topological changes in the minority Fermi sphere that depend on the relative orientation of the condensate momentum and particle momentum.

## Method

The authors start from the QCD Lagrangian, perform a local transformation on the quark fields to allow for Cooper pairs with non-zero center-of-mass momentum, and integrate out the gluonic degrees of freedom to obtain a four-fermion contact interaction (NJL-type model). They solve the coupled mean-field gap equations, density equations, and total momentum equation simultaneously at fixed baryonic density n_B = 0.55 fm^{-3}, using a three-dimensional momentum cutoff regularization with parameters G_d = 3.11 GeV^{-2} and G_d Λ_NJL^2 = 1.31. The coupling strength is varied by scaling the dimensionless ratio f of the actual coupling to the weak-coupling value G, with values f = 1.0, 2.0, and 2.93.

## Key numbers

- Critical asymmetry at T=0: α_c(1) = 0.18 for weak coupling (f=1.0), α_c(2) = 0.77 for intermediate coupling (f=2.0), α_c(2.93) = 1.0 for strong coupling (f=2.93)
- Critical temperature: T_c(1) = 23.0 MeV for weak coupling, T_c(2) = 144.0 MeV for intermediate coupling, T_c(2.93) = 252.0 MeV for strong coupling
- Fixed baryonic density: n_B = 0.55 fm^{-3}
- Coupling constant parameters: G_d = 3.11 GeV^{-2}, G_d Λ_NJL^2 = 1.31
- The strong-coupling value f = 2.93 is slightly below the zero-temperature critical coupling at which the FF phase becomes extinct

## Context

This work extends the study of inhomogeneous pairing in quark matter, previously explored for the Fulde-Ferrell phase by Alford (2000), Giannakis (2005), Schäfer (2005), Fukushima (2006), Kiriyama (2006), and He (2006), and for Larkin-Ovchinnikov crystalline phases by Bowers (2002), Casalbuoni (2005), Rajagopal (2006), and Mannarelli (2006). It is the first investigation of asymmetric, inhomogeneous quark matter at strong coupling, generalizing the Nozières–Schmitt-Rink theory (1985) for non-relativistic fermions and its relativistic extensions by Abuki (2006), Gubankova (2006), and Chatterjee (2008) to include broken spatial symmetry. The results enable predictions for neutron star cooling behavior and gravitational wave emission from crystalline color superconducting matter in compact stars.
