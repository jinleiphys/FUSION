# Covariant Energy Density Functionals for Neutron Star Matter Equation of State Modeling: Cross-Comparison Analysis Using \texttt{CompactObject}

> This study analyzes and contrasts different phenomenological methods used to model the nuclear equation of state (EOS) for neutron star matter based on covariant energy density functionals (CEDF). Using two complementary methodologies, we seek to capture a comprehensive picture of the potential behaviors of ultra-dense nucleonic matter and identify the most plausible models based on current observational and experimental constraints. Observational data from radio pulsar timing, gravitational wave detection of GW170817, and X-ray timing provide critical benchmarks for testing the models. We have derived the EOS posteriors for various CEDF models within the \texttt{CompactObject} package, utilizing recent observational data on neutron stars, state-of-the-art theoretical constraints from chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) calculations for pure neutron matter at low densities, and pQCD-derived constraints. Our analysis has demonstrated that while all considered CEDF models broadly reproduce current astrophysical and theoretical constraints, subtle yet important differences persist among them, with each framework exhibiting distinct characteristics at supra-nuclear density. This is in particular true for the proton fraction inside neutron stars, but also supported by the models' behavior with respect to the pure neutron matter EOS and the density dependence of the speed of sound. Our study highlights the sensitivity of dense matter predictions to the underlying EOS parameterizations and the priors considered.

## Key claim

While all considered covariant energy density functional (CEDF) models broadly reproduce current astrophysical and theoretical constraints, subtle yet important differences persist among them at supra-nuclear density, particularly in the proton fraction inside neutron stars and the density dependence of the speed of sound, with the results being sensitive to the underlying EOS parameterizations and the priors considered.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian inference analysis using the open-source CompactObject package to constrain the nuclear equation of state (EOS) parameters for four classes of CEDF models: non-linear (NL) models with constant couplings, and density-dependent models DDB, DDH, and GDFMX. They combined likelihoods from nuclear saturation properties, gravitational-wave data from GW170817, NICER X-ray mass-radius measurements of PSR J0437+4715, PSR J0030+0451, and PSR J0740+6620, chiral effective field theory constraints for pure neutron matter at low densities, and perturbative QCD constraints at high density, then compared models via their posterior distributions and Bayes factors.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density prior: n0 = 0.153 ± 0.005 fm^-3
- Binding energy per nucleon prior: epsilon0 = -16.0 ± 0.2 MeV
- Incompressibility prior: K0 = 230 ± 40 MeV
- Symmetry energy prior: J_sym,0 = 32.5 ± 2.5 MeV
- pQCD constraint applied at rho = 1.2 fm^-3 (approximately 7.5 n0)
- chiEFT constraints applied at rho = 0.04, 0.08, 0.12, and 0.16 fm^-3
- PSR J0437+4715 mass: 1.44 ± 0.07 solar masses from radio timing
- PSR J0740+6620 radius: 12.49 (+1.28/-0.88) km with mass 2.073 ± 0.069 solar masses
- Combined astrophysical likelihood peaks centered around log(L_Astro) approximately -9 for all models
- Approximately 145,000 posterior samples released across all models

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of CEDF models (e.g., Malik 2022, 2023; Huang 2024, 2025) by performing a systematic cross-comparison of multiple CEDF families under identical observational constraints and computational frameworks. The study incorporates the most recent NICER measurements of PSR J0437+4715 (Choudhury 2024) and updated mass-radius constraints for PSR J0740+6620 (Salmi 2024), while integrating theoretical limits from chiral effective field theory (Huth 2021) and perturbative QCD (Komoltsev 2023). The released posterior dataset enables future applications in machine learning, meta-analyses, and model validation, with a companion paper planned to extend the analysis to model-agnostic EOS descriptions.
