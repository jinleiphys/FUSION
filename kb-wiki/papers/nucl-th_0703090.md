# The Friedrichs-Model with fermion-boson couplings

> In this work we present an extended version of the Friedrichs Model, which includes fermion-boson couplings. The set of fermion bound states is coupled to a boson field with discrete and continuous components. As a result of the coupling some of the fermion states may become resonant states. This feature suggests the existence of a formal link between the occurrence of Gamow Resonant States in the boson sector, as predicted by the standard Friedrichs Model, with similar effects in the set of solutions of the fermion central potential (Gamow fermion resonances). The structure of the solutions of the model is discussed by using different approximations to the model space. Realistic couplings constants are used to calculate fermion resonances in a heavy mass nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper claims that an extended Friedrichs model including fermion-boson couplings can produce fermion resonant states (Gamow resonances) from the coupling of fermion bound states to a boson field, suggesting a formal link between boson-sector resonances from the standard Friedrichs model and fermion resonances in a central potential, but this result depends on neglecting the background integral term in the diagonalized Hamiltonian, making the resulting Hamiltonian not formally self-adjoint.

## Method

The authors extend the standard Friedrichs model by adding fermion bound states coupled to both the discrete boson state and the continuous boson field, with interaction Hamiltonians for fermion-boson and boson-boson couplings. They diagonalize the boson sector in terms of Gamow vectors and background, then neglect the background to obtain a finite-dimensional matrix eigenvalue problem for the coupled fermion-boson system. The coupling constants \(\Lambda_{kl}\) are computed from the form factors \(f(\omega)\) and \(V(\omega)\) using the residue at the boson resonance pole \(z_R\), with the principal value integral neglected as an approximation.

## Key numbers

- The boson resonance pole is located at \(z_0 = E_R - i\frac{\Gamma}{2}\) with \(E_R > 0\) and \(\Gamma > 0\).
- For a single fermion level, the coupled system yields eigenvalues \(E = \varepsilon + \frac{z_R}{2} \pm \frac12 \sqrt{z_R^2 + \Lambda^2(z_R)}\), where \(\varepsilon\) is the fermion bound state energy and \(z_R\) is the boson resonance energy.
- The coupling constant \(\Lambda(z_R)\) is approximated as \(\Lambda(z_R) \approx -i\pi\lambda f(z_R)V(z_R)\), where \(\lambda\) is the boson-boson coupling constant.
- For two fermion levels with a simplified coupling matrix \(\mathbf{\Lambda} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & a \\ a & 0 \end{pmatrix}\), the eigenvalues satisfy a quartic equation with solutions \(E_{1,2} = \frac12 (z_R + \varepsilon_1 + \varepsilon_2 \pm \sqrt{(z_R + \varepsilon_1 + \varepsilon_2)^2 - 4(\varepsilon_1\varepsilon_2 + \varepsilon_2 z_R - a^2)})\) and similarly for \(E_{3,4}\).

## Context

This work extends the standard Friedrichs model, originally formulated for boson fields (Friedrichs 1948), to include fermion degrees of freedom, building on earlier solvable model approaches to resonance phenomena (Berggren 1968; Berggren and Lind 1993). The model is motivated by nuclear structure calculations near the drip line, where particle-vibration coupling in the continuum is important, and it provides a solvable limit of nucleon-boson interactions in Berggren's basis, as previously discussed by Civitarese, Gadella, and collaborators. The results enable analytic study of how fermion bound states acquire decay widths through coupling to boson resonances, which is relevant for understanding single-particle properties in exotic nuclei.
