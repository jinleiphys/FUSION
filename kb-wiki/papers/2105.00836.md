# On the structure in the $ΛN$ cross section at the $ΣN$ threshold

> The complexity of threshold phenomena is exemplified on a prominent and long-known case - the structure in the $Λp$ cross section (invariant mass spectrum) at the opening of the $ΣN$ channel. The mass splitting between the $Σ$ baryons together with the angular momentum coupling in the $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ partial wave imply that, in principle, up to six channels are involved. Utilizing hyperon-nucleon potentials that provide an excellent description of the available low-energy $Λp$ and $ΣN$ scattering data, the shape of the resulting $Λp$ cross section is discussed and the poles near the $ΣN$ threshold are determined. Evidence for a strangeness $S=-1$ dibaryon is provided, in the form of a deuteron-like (unstable) $ΣN$ bound state. Predictions for level shifts and widths of $Σ^-p$ atomic states are given.

## Key claim

The structure in the Λp cross section at the ΣN threshold is reproduced by modern hyperon-nucleon potentials, and the pole positions near threshold provide evidence for a strangeness S=-1 dibaryon in the form of a deuteron-like (unstable) ΣN bound state, though the pole lies above the ΣN threshold and there is no Breit-Wigner type peak well separated from threshold.

## Method

The authors utilized hyperon-nucleon (YN) potentials from chiral effective field theory (NLO13 and NLO19, with cutoffs 500-650 MeV) and the Nijmegen NSC97f meson-exchange potential, all of which achieve χ² ≈ 12-13 for 24 ΣN data points. They performed a multichannel effective range expansion for the coupled ³S₁-³D₁ partial wave (involving up to six channels due to Σ baryon mass splitting) to determine pole positions in the complex q_ΣN plane, and computed Λp and Λn cross sections and Σ⁻p atomic level shifts and widths.

## Key numbers

- The total χ² for the 24 ΣN data points is approximately 12-13 for the best potentials (NLO13, NLO19 with cutoff 600-650 MeV, and NSC97f).
- For the Q=1 system, the pole energy for NLO13 (cutoff 600 MeV) is 2131.90 - i 1.39 MeV; for NLO19 (cutoff 600 MeV) it is 2131.73 - i 1.11 MeV; for NSC97f it is 2133.04 - i 3.80 MeV.
- The Σ⁺n threshold is at 2128.97 MeV and the Σ⁰p threshold at 2130.87 MeV for Q=1; for Q=0, the Σ⁰n threshold is at 2132.17 MeV and the Σ⁻p threshold at 2135.67 MeV.
- The capture ratio r_R is evaluated at p_lab = 10 MeV/c and contributes a χ² of 0.0-1.1 for the best potentials.
- For Σ⁻p atomic states, the ¹S₀ shift for NLO13 (cutoff 600 MeV) is -146 eV and the width is 1401 eV.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing study of threshold phenomena in the Λp system, first observed by Dahl (1961) and Tan (1969), and later investigated in reactions such as pp → K⁺Λp by ElSamad (2013), Roder (2013), Hauenstein (2017), and Munzer (2018), and in Λp correlation functions by the ALICE Collaboration (2021). The authors contradict earlier studies by Dosch (1978, 1980), Toker (1979, 1981), and Torres (1986) that concluded no ΣN bound state exists, arguing those works used simplified models that ignored the tensor coupling and SU(3) constraints. The results enable future discrimination between dynamical scenarios through differential observables, such as those planned at J-PARC by Miwa (2020).
