# Natural constraints on the gluon-quark vertex

> In principle, the strong-interaction sector of the Standard Model is characterised by a unique renormalisation-group-invariant (RGI) running interaction and a unique form for the dressed--gluon-quark vertex, $Γ_μ$; but, whilst much has been learnt about the former, the latter is still obscure. In order to improve this situation, we use a RGI running-interaction that reconciles both top-down and bottom-up analyses of the gauge sector in quantum chromodynamics (QCD) to compute dressed-quark gap equation solutions with 1,660,000 distinct Ansaetze for $Γ_μ$. Each one of the solutions is then tested for compatibility with three physical criteria and, remarkably, we find that merely 0.55% of the solutions survive the test. Plainly, therefore, even a small selection of observables places extremely tight bounds on the domain of realistic vertex Ansaetze. This analysis and its results should prove useful in constraining insightful contemporary studies of QCD and hadronic phenomena.

## Key claim

Even a small selection of observables places extremely tight bounds on the domain of realistic gluon-quark vertex Ansätze; only 0.55% of 1,660,000 distinct vertex Ansätze tested survive three physical criteria, meaning the hyper-volume of physically acceptable vertices is extremely small within the parameter space.

## Method

The authors used a renormalisation-group-invariant (RGI) running interaction that reconciles top-down and bottom-up analyses of QCD's gauge sector to compute dressed-quark gap equation solutions in the chiral limit. They randomly generated 1,660,000 distinct Ansätze for the gluon-quark vertex, parametrised by four dimensionless coefficients (a₁, a₃, a₄₅, a₈), and tested each solution against three criteria: sufficient dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) strength, negative-definite dressed-quark anomalous chromomagnetic moment (ACM), and pion decay constant within 5% of its chiral-limit value.

## Key numbers

- Only 0.55% of the 540,000 vertex Ansätze in Case D (all four coefficients nonzero) simultaneously satisfied all three physical criteria.
- The chiral-limit pion decay constant used as a criterion was fπ⁰ ≈ 0.088 GeV, with solutions required to be within 5% of this value.
- The acceptable dressed-quark mass at zero momentum, M(0), was constrained to lie in the range (0.25, 0.45) GeV by criterion (i), with Case A yielding M(0) ∈ (0.26, 0.31) GeV and Case C yielding M(0) ∈ (0.25, 0.36) GeV.
- The renormalisation-group-invariant interaction used parameters ς = 0.55 GeV and ω = 0.6 GeV, with Λ_QCD = 0.57 GeV (MOM scheme).
- In Case A (a₁ = a₃ = 0), only 0.4% of 360,000 vertex Ansätze survived all three cuts; in Case B (a₁ = 0), 400,000 Ansätze were tested; in Case C (a₃ = 0), 360,000 Ansätze were tested.
- The acceptable vertex parameter subdomain G₄ is contained within a₁ ∈ [-0.5, 1], a₃ ∈ [-1, 1], a₄₅ ∈ [-2, -0.4], a₈ ∈ [-4, 1].

## Context

This work extends the unification of top-down continuum and lattice analyses of QCD's gauge sector with bottom-up studies of its matter sector, as described in Binosi (2014), by systematically constraining the gluon-quark vertex form. The analysis builds on decades of work on the vertex's role in DCSB, including studies by Ball (1980), Curtis (1990), Bashir (2011), and Qin (2013), and enables future studies of hadron properties to employ realistic vertex Ansätze rather than the bare vertex, which the authors show is incapable of inducing DCSB with the RGI interaction. The results also connect to the possible existence of a zero in the proton's electric form factor, as discussed by Cloet (2013).
