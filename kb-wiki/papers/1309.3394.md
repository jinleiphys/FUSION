# Three-body model calculations for N=Z odd-odd nuclei with T=0 and T=1 pairing correlations

> We study the interplay between the isoscalar (T=0) and isovector (T=1) pairing correlations in N=Z odd-odd nuclei from 14N to 58Cu by using three-body model calculations. The strong spin-triplet T=0 pairing correlation dominates in the ground state of 14N, 18F, 30P, and 58Cu with the spin-parity J^π=1+, which can be well reproduced by the present calculations. The magnetic dipole and Gamow-Teller transitions are found to be strong in 18F and 42Sc as a manifestation of SU(4) symmetry in the spin-isospin space. We also discuss the spin-quadrupole transitions in these nuclei.

# Three-body model calculations for N=Z odd-odd nuclei with T=0 and T=1 pairing correlations

## Key claim

The interplay between isoscalar (T=0) and isovector (T=1) pairing correlations in N=Z odd-odd nuclei can be described by a three-body model with a density-dependent contact interaction, but the quantitative agreement depends on the choice of energy cutoff and the ratio of spin-triplet to spin-singlet pairing strengths (v_t/v_s = 1.9 for a 20 MeV cutoff, versus ~1.6 from shell model estimates).

## Method

The authors employed a core-plus-two-valence-nucleon three-body model, diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in a valence two-particle space with single-particle states obtained from a mean-field potential (Woods-Saxon plus spin-orbit and Coulomb terms). The proton-neutron pairing interaction was taken as a density-dependent contact interaction with projectors onto spin-singlet and spin-triplet channels, with strengths determined from empirical p-n scattering lengths (a^{(s)}_{pn} = -23.749 fm, a^{(t)}_{pn} = 5.424 fm) and a momentum cutoff. The three density-dependence parameters (x_s, x_t, alpha) were fitted to reproduce the ground (1^+), first excited (3^+), and second excited (0^+) states in 18F.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of isoscalar to isovector pairing strengths: v_t/v_s = 1.9 for energy cutoff E_cut = 20 MeV; ratio becomes 1.6 for E_cut = 30 MeV
- Energy differences Delta E = E(0^+_1) - E(1^+_1) in MeV: 18F exp. 1.04, cal. 1.04; 34Cl exp. -0.46, cal. -0.69; 42Sc exp. -0.61, cal. -0.61; 58Cu exp. 0.20, cal. 0.68
- Magnetic dipole transition B(M1) down arrow in mu_N^2: 18F exp. 19.71, cal. 18.19; 42Sc exp. 6.16, cal. 6.81; 14N exp. 0.047, cal. 0.682
- Gamow-Teller transition B(GT) in g_A^2/4pi: 18O to 18F ground state exp. 3.11 +/- 0.03, cal. 2.48; 42Ca to 42Sc (E_x = 0.61 MeV) exp. 2.16 +/- 0.05, cal. 1.80
- Spin-singlet probability P(S=0) in 0^+ state: 18F 82.2%, 30P 94.8%, 34Cl 40.7%, 42Sc 70.5%
- Spin-triplet probability P(S=1) in 1^+ state: 18F 90.1%, 30P 95.8%, 34Cl 64.3%, 42Sc 65.7%

## Context

This work extends three-body model techniques previously applied to Borromean nuclei such as 11Li and 6He (BeEs91, EsBeH97, HS2005) to the study of pairing correlations in N=Z odd-odd nuclei. The calculations address the competition between T=0 and T=1 pairing, a topic previously discussed by Bertsch (2012) and Poves (1998) using shell model approaches. The strong B(M1) and B(GT) transitions found in 18F and 42Sc are interpreted as manifestations of SU(4) symmetry in the spin-isospin space, consistent with earlier analyses by HaBa89 and VoOr93. The results enable future experimental searches for T=0 pairing signatures in radioactive beam facilities and provide a framework for studying spin-isospin transitions across the N=Z line.
