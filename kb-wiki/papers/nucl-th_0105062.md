# Toward a Spin- and Parity-Independent Nucleon-Nucleon Potential

> A supersymmetric inversion method is applied to the singlet $^1S_0$ and $^1P_1$ neutron-proton elastic phase shifts. The resulting central potential has a one-pion-exchange (OPE) long-range behavior and a parity-independent short-range part; it fits inverted data well. Adding a regularized OPE tensor term also allows the reproduction of the triplet $^3P_0$, $^3P_1$ and $^3S_1$ phase shifts as well as of the deuteron binding energy. The potential is thus also spin-independent (except for the OPE part) and contains no spin-orbit term. These important simplifications of the neutron-proton interaction are shown to be possible only if the potential possesses Pauli forbidden bound states, as proposed in the Moscow nucleon-nucleon model.

# Toward a Spin- and Parity-Independent Nucleon-Nucleon Potential

## Key claim
A deep nucleon-nucleon potential with Pauli forbidden bound states can reproduce singlet and triplet neutron-proton phase shifts and the deuteron binding energy using a short-range central potential that is independent of both spin and parity, with no spin-orbit term. This simplification is only possible if the potential possesses Pauli forbidden bound states, as proposed in the Moscow nucleon-nucleon model.

## Method
The author applies a two-step supersymmetric inversion method to neutron-proton elastic scattering phase shifts from the PWA93 database. First, the singlet $^1P_1$ phase shifts are inverted using a rational approximation of the scattering S-matrix with three additional poles, constructing a generalized Bargmann potential. Second, a Pauli forbidden bound state is added phase-equivalently via two successive supersymmetric transformations, with the binding energy and mean radius chosen to simultaneously fit the $^1S_0$ and $^1P_1$ phase shifts. A regularized one-pion-exchange tensor term is then added to reproduce triplet partial waves and the deuteron binding energy.

## Key numbers
- Phase shift data fitted at center-of-mass energies $E_{\mathrm c.m.}=0.05, 0.5, 2.5, 5, 10, 15, \dots, 140$ MeV (below pion-production threshold)
- Neutron-proton reduced mass $\mu = 469.45926$ MeV/$c^2$ with $\hbar c=197.327053$ MeV fm
- Three S-matrix poles at $\kappa_m=\pm1.848+ 1.860 \imath$ and $-1.848 \imath$ fm$^{-1}$ for the $^1P_1$ fit
- Pauli forbidden bound state energy $E_{\mathrm PFS}=-101.9$ MeV with mean radius $r^{\mathrm mean}_{\mathrm PFS}=0.6064$ fm
- Regularization parameters: $V_{\mathrm reg}=-2071$ MeV, $r_{\mathrm reg}=0.8231$ fm, $\Lambda_{\mathrm C}=468.1$ MeV/$\hbar c$
- Root-mean-square deviations of $3.9 \times 10^{-3}\pi$ for $^1S_0$ and $2.3 \times 10^{-3}\pi$ for $^1P_1$ phase shifts
- Deuteron binding energy $E_{\mathrm d}=-2.224575(9)$ MeV reproduced with rms deviation of $6.0 \times 10^{-3}\pi$ for $^3S_1$ phase shifts
- Tensor cutoff parameters: $\Lambda^{\mathrm even}_{\mathrm T}=601.14$ MeV/$\hbar c$ for triplet-even, $\Lambda^{\mathrm odd}_{\mathrm T}=517.7$ MeV/$\hbar c$ for triplet-odd
- Analytical approximation: $V^{\mathrm s.r.}_{\mathrm C}(r)=-3050\, e^{-5.890 r^2}-70.39\, e^{-0.5687 r^2}$ (in MeV, $r$ in fm)

## Context
This work extends the Moscow nucleon-nucleon model of Kukulin (1999) by using supersymmetric inversion to deduce the short-range potential form factor from data rather than assuming an exponential or Gaussian form. It contradicts the standard approach of conventional potentials such as Paris (Lacombe, 1980), Nijmegen (Stoks, 1994), Bonn (Machleidt, 1987), and Argonne (Wiringa, 1995), which require spin-, parity-, and spin-orbit-dependent terms. The result enables the construction of a simpler phenomenological nucleon-nucleon interaction, though higher partial waves ($J \ge 2$) are not reproduced, leaving the question of a satisfactory non-local term open for future work.
