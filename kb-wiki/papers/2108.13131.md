# Hyperon dynamics and production of multi-strangeness hypernuclei in heavy-ion collisions at 3A GeV

> Within the microscopic transport, systematic investigation of the many facets of hyperons and hypernuclei up to strangeness $S = -2$ are carried out for $^{197}$Au + $^{197}$Au and $^{40}$Ca + $^{40}$Ca at the incident energy of $3A$ GeV. The spatial, temporal and density distributions of hyperon production, absorption and freeze-out are thoroughly investigated. The rapidity and kinetic energy spectra of $Ξ$ hyperons and double $Λ$ hypernuclei are analyzed. It is revealed that the chemical balance of hyperon production is established in baryon-baryon channels while the opposite is found in baryon-meson channels. It turns out that the rapidity spectra of $^{4,5}_{ΛΛ}$X are single-peak and more than two orders of magnitude lower than that of $^{3}_Λ$H. Formation of double $Λ$ hypernuclei through $Ξ$ hypernuclei as intermediate states is also discussed in kinematics.

# Hyperon dynamics and production of multi-strangeness hypernuclei in heavy-ion collisions at 3A GeV

## Key claim

The chemical balance of hyperon production is established in baryon-baryon channels but not in baryon-meson channels for heavy-ion collisions at 3A GeV, and the rapidity spectra of double Λ hypernuclei are single-peaked and more than two orders of magnitude lower than that of single Λ hypernuclei, with the yields of double Λ hypernuclei in ⁴⁰Ca + ⁴⁰Ca being two orders of magnitude lower than in ¹⁹⁷Au + ¹⁹⁷Au.

## Method

The Lanzhou Quantum Molecular Dynamics (LQMD) transport model was used to simulate ¹⁹⁷Au + ¹⁹⁷Au and ⁴⁰Ca + ⁴⁰Ca collisions at 3A GeV, including all resonances below 2 GeV and all relevant strangeness production and absorption channels. Hypernuclei formation was treated using the Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) coalescence method with proximity parameters r₀ = 3.5 fm for Λ and r₀ = 5.0 fm for Ξ in coordinate space and p₀ = 200 MeV/c in momentum space. The spatial zones (fireball, mixed, spectator, free) were identified based on baryon density and the fraction of fireball or spectator particles according to a Glauber-model-inspired prescription.

## Key numbers

- Maximum average baryon density ρ = 4ρ₀ reached at 17.5 fm/c in central ¹⁹⁷Au + ¹⁹⁷Au collisions (b < 5 fm), and ρ = 3ρ₀ at 15 fm/c in peripheral collisions (5 fm < b < 10.5 fm)
- Ratio Ξ⁻/(Λ + Σ⁰) = 0.0035 for central collisions and 0.0020 for peripheral collisions in ¹⁹⁷Au + ¹⁹⁷Au at 3A GeV
- Average freeze-out radius of hyperons at about R = 5 fm, common to all three hyperon species
- Cross section for ⁴_ΛΛH production: 2.6×10⁻² mb in ¹⁹⁷Au + ¹⁹⁷Au versus 1.0×10⁻⁴ mb in ⁴⁰Ca + ⁴⁰Ca at 3A GeV
- Cross section for ⁵_ΛΛH production: 5.9×10⁻³ mb in ¹⁹⁷Au + ¹⁹⁷Au versus ∼10⁻⁵ mb in ⁴⁰Ca + ⁴⁰Ca
- Cross section for ⁵_ΛΛHe production: 5.1×10⁻³ mb in ¹⁹⁷Au + ¹⁹⁷Au versus ∼10⁻⁵ mb in ⁴⁰Ca + ⁴⁰Ca
- Cross section for ⁵_ΛΛLi production: 1.4×10⁻³ mb in ¹⁹⁷Au + ¹⁹⁷Au versus ∼10⁻⁶ mb in ⁴⁰Ca + ⁴⁰Ca
- Cross section for ⁶_ΛΛHe production: 2.2×10⁻³ mb in ¹⁹⁷Au + ¹⁹⁷Au versus ∼10⁻⁶ mb in ⁴⁰Ca + ⁴⁰Ca
- Cross section for ⁷_ΛΛHe production: 6.8×10⁻⁴ mb in ¹⁹⁷Au + ¹⁹⁷Au versus ≲10⁻⁶ mb in ⁴⁰Ca + ⁴⁰Ca
- Attractive potential of -32 MeV for Λ and -16 MeV for Ξ at normal nuclear density ρ₀
- Momentum released in conversion of ΞN system to ΛΛ system is below 270 MeV/c
- Production of double Λ hypernuclei enhanced by 2-3 times when r₀ for Λ coalescence is increased from 3.5 fm to 5.0 fm

## Context

This work extends previous transport model studies of hypernucleus production (using models such as DCM, GiBUU, HSD, and UrQMD with coalescence or MST methods) by providing the first systematic kinematic spectra of double Λ hypernuclei up to strangeness S = -2 from the LQMD model. The study addresses the unresolved puzzle of hyperon enhancement at 1-2A GeV noted by Whitten (2009), Chen (2004), and Greiner (2014), and provides theoretical guidance for upcoming experiments at FAIR in Germany, NICA in Russia, J-PARC in Japan, and HIAF in China. The results contradict the assumption that hyperons serve as clean probes of dense nuclear matter, as demonstrated in recent work by Zhang (2021), and show that spectator coalescence dominates hypernucleus formation in lighter systems like ⁴⁰Ca + ⁴⁰Ca while fireball coalescence dominates in heavier systems like ¹⁹⁷Au + ¹⁹⁷Au.
