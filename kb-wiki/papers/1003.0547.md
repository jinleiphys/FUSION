# Solutions of the dispersion equation in the region of overlapping of zero-sound and particle-hole modes

> In this paper the solutions of the zero-sound dispersion equation in the random phase approximation (RPA) are considered. The calculation of the damped zero-sound modes ω_s(k) (complex frequency of excitation) in the nuclear matter is presented. The method is based on the analytical structure of the polarization operators Π(ω,k). The solutions of two dispersion equations with Π(ω,k) and with Re(Π(ω,k)) are compared. It is shown that in the first case we obtain one-valued smooth solutions without "thumb-like" forms. Considering the giant resonances in the nuclei as zero-sound excitations we compare the experimental energy and escape width of the giant dipole resonance (GDR) in the nucleus A with ω_s(k) taken at a definite wave vector k=k_A.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that solving the zero-sound dispersion equation with the full complex polarization operator \(\Pi(\omega,k)\) yields one-valued smooth solutions for the damped zero-sound mode \(\omega_s(k)\) without the "thumb-like" forms that appear when only the real part \(\mathrm{Re}(\Pi(\omega,k))\) is used; the damped solutions reside on the unphysical sheet of the Riemann surface of the logarithm in the polarization operator.

## Method

The zero-sound dispersion equation \(1 = C_0 F \Pi(\omega,k)\) is solved in symmetric nuclear matter using the standard random phase approximation (RPA) for the retarded polarization operator \(\Pi(\omega,k)\), expressed via the Migdal function \(\phi(\omega,k)\). The solutions \(\omega_s(k)\) are obtained for wave vectors \(k\) where the zero-sound mode overlaps with the particle-hole (\(ph\)) continuum, requiring continuation onto the unphysical sheet of the logarithm in \(\phi(\omega,k)\) by adding \(-2\pi i\) to the logarithmic term. The resulting complex frequencies \(\omega_s(k)\) are compared with solutions of the approximate equation using only \(\mathrm{Re}(\Pi(\omega,k))\), and are then applied to giant dipole resonances (GDR) in nuclei via the Steinwedel-Jensen model relation \(k_A = \pi/(2R)\) with \(R = r_0 A^{1/3}\).

## Key numbers

- For the interaction parameter \(F=2\), the threshold wave vector for the onset of damping is \(k_{th} = 0.51\,p_0\) where \(p_0 = 268\) MeV.
- The "thumb-like" solutions of the equation with \(\mathrm{Re}(\Pi(\omega,k))\) disappear at \(k_{max} = 0.74\,p_0\).
- For the nucleus \({}^{12}\mathrm{C}\), the phenomenological GDR energy from Eq. (15) is \(E_{GDR} = 27.2\) MeV with experimental escape width \(\Gamma \approx 3\) MeV; the calculation with \(p_F=200\) MeV, \(F=1.2\), \(m=m_0\) gives \(E_{GDR}=30\) MeV and \(\Gamma=6.8\) MeV.
- For the nucleus \({}^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\), the phenomenological GDR energy is \(E_{GDR} = 13.7\) MeV with experimental escape width \(\Gamma \approx 0.5\)–\(2.0\) MeV; the calculation with \(p_F=200\) MeV, \(F=1.6\), \(m=0.8m_0\) gives \(E_{GDR}=12.7\) MeV and \(\Gamma=0.5\) MeV.
- The phenomenological formula for GDR energies is \(E_{GDR}(A) = 31.2 A^{-1/3} + 20.6 A^{-1/6}\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Landau Fermi-liquid theory and RPA treatment of zero-sound in nuclear matter (Landau and Lifshitz; Pines and Nozieres; Fetter and Walecka) to the regime where the zero-sound mode overlaps with the particle-hole continuum, producing complex frequencies. It contrasts with approaches that use only the real part of the polarization operator (e.g., in pion dispersion studies by other authors), which yield "thumb-like" multi-valued solutions. The method enables a smooth continuation of the zero-sound branch and allows comparison with experimental GDR energies and escape widths in nuclei, building on the Migdal theory of finite Fermi systems and the Steinwedel-Jensen model.
