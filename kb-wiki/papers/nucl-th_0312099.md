# Re-visit the N/Z ratio of free nucleons from collisions of neutron -rich nuclei as a probe of EoS of asymmetric nuclear matter

> The N/Z ratio of free nucleons from collisions of neutron-rich nuclei as a function of their momentum is studied by means of Isospin dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics. We find that this ratio is not only sensitive to the form of the density dependence of the symmetry potential energy but also its strength determined by the symmetry energy coefficient. The uncertainties about the symmetry energy coefficient influence the accuracy of probing the density dependence of the symmetry energy by means of the N/Z ratio of free nucleons of neutron-rich nuclei.

# Re-visit the N/Z ratio of free nucleons from collisions of neutron-rich nuclei as a probe of EoS of asymmetric nuclear matter

## Key claim
The N/Z ratio of free nucleons from collisions of neutron-rich nuclei is sensitive to both the form of the density dependence of the symmetry potential energy AND the strength of that potential (determined by the symmetry energy coefficient). The existing uncertainties in the symmetry energy coefficient (a_sym) significantly reduce the sensitivity of this ratio as a probe of the density dependence of the symmetry energy, meaning that a more precise value of a_sym is urgently needed before the N/Z ratio can reliably constrain the density dependence.

## Method
The authors used the Isospin dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate collisions of neutron-rich nuclei (96Zr+96Zr and 132Sn+132Sn) at beam energies of 50A MeV and 100A MeV. They varied both the strength parameter C_S (corresponding to symmetry energy coefficients a_sym from 27 to 38 MeV) and the density dependence exponent gamma (0.5, 1.0, 1.5) of the symmetry potential energy term F(u)=u^gamma, and calculated the N/Z ratio of emitted free nucleons as a function of their momentum. Clusters were identified using a coalescence model with R_0=3.5 fm and P_0=300 MeV/c.

## Key numbers
- The empirical symmetry energy coefficient is 30 +/- 4 MeV; theoretical predictions range from 27-38 MeV (non-relativistic Hartree-Fock), 35-40 MeV (relativistic mean field), 31 MeV (Brueckner-Hartree-Fock), 28.7 MeV (extended BHF), 26-34 MeV (relativistic BHF), and 28.1 MeV (Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone).
- C_S values of 27, 35, and 50 MeV were used, corresponding to a_sym values of 27, 31, and 38 MeV respectively; a reduced range of C_S=37, 40, 45 MeV (a_sym=32, 34, 36 MeV) was also studied.
- For central collisions of 132Sn+132Sn at 50A MeV with F(u)=u, the N/Z ratio of nucleons with momentum ~300 MeV/c calculated with C_S=50 MeV is about 1.5 times larger than that with C_S=27 MeV.
- The compressibility parameter K_sym changes sign from negative to positive as gamma increases from 0.5 to 1.5, with values ranging from about -400 MeV to +466 MeV predicted by many-body theories.
- A recent constraint from isovector GDR in 208Pb gives a reduced range of 32 MeV <= a_sym <= 36 MeV.

## Context
This work extends prior studies by Li et al. (2002) on the influence of C_S on isospin distributions, and by Li et al. (1997) who proposed the N/Z ratio of free nucleons as a sensitive observable to the density dependence of the symmetry energy. The authors show that earlier studies typically adopted a fixed C_S value and tested sensitivity only to the form of density dependence, neglecting the confounding effect of uncertainties in the symmetry energy coefficient. The paper demonstrates that even with the reduced range of a_sym=32-36 MeV deduced from isovector GDR data (Vretenar et al., 2003), the sensitivity of the N/Z ratio to the density dependence remains influenced by these uncertainties. This motivates the need for more precise determination of a_sym before the N/Z ratio can serve as a clean probe of the density dependence of the symmetry energy in asymmetric nuclear matter.
