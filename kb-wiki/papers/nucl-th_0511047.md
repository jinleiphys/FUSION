# Effect of gauge-field fluctuations on the phase transition between normal and color-superconducting quark matter

> Type-I color superconductors display a first-order phase transition due to thermal gauge-field fluctuations. We numerically evaluated the critical temperature of the first-order phase transition and the corresponding discontinuity of the diquark condensate at the critical point.

# Effect of gauge-field fluctuations on the phase transition between normal and color-superconducting quark matter

## Key claim
Thermal gauge-field fluctuations induce a first-order phase transition in type-I color superconductors, and the fluctuation-induced critical temperature is higher than the mean-field pairing temperature at high but finite densities, approaching the mean-field value only in the limit of infinitely large chemical potential.

## Method
The authors constructed a Ginzburg-Landau free energy density for a homogeneous color-flavor locked (CFL) superconductor that includes a term for gluon fluctuations derived in prior work (Rischke, 2004). They solved numerically the pair of equations requiring the free energy and its derivative with respect to the squared gap to vanish simultaneously, yielding the first-order critical temperature and the discontinuity of the diquark condensate at that temperature as functions of the quark chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of the fluctuation-induced critical temperature to the mean-field pairing temperature, \(T_c^*/T_c\), approaches 1 as \(\mu \rightarrow \infty\).
- The ratio of the gap at the critical temperature to its zero-temperature value, \(\Delta(T_c^*)/\Delta(0)\), approaches 0 as \(\mu \rightarrow \infty\).
- The magnetic penetration depth is defined as \(1 / \lambda^2(T) = \frac{7\zeta(3)}{24\pi^4} \left( \frac{g\mu\Delta(T)}{k_B T_c} \right)^2\).
- The coherence length at zero temperature is \(\xi_0 \equiv 1/(2\pi k_B T_c)\).

## Context
This work extends the analytical study of gauge-field fluctuations in type-I CFL superconductors by Rischke (2004) by providing numerical results for the critical temperature and condensate discontinuity. The results contradict the earlier findings of Matsuura et al. (2004), who obtained a fluctuation-induced critical temperature lower than the mean-field value and a much smaller gap discontinuity; the authors attribute this difference to their treatment of gluon fluctuations contributing negatively to the free energy, which favors diquark condensation. The results enable more precise predictions for the phase structure of dense quark matter relevant to compact star interiors.
