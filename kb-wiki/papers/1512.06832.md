# The EOS of neutron matter and the effect of $Λ$ hyperons to neutron star structure

> The structure of neutron stars is determined by the equation of state of the matter inside the star, which relies on the knowledge of nuclear interactions. While radii of neutron stars mostly depend on the equation of state of neutron matter at nuclear densities, their maximum mass can be drastically affected by the appearance of hyperons at higher densities in the inner core of the star. We summarize recent quantum Monte Carlo results on the calculation of the equation of state of neutron matter at nuclear and higher densities. We report about the development of realistic hyperon-nucleon interactions based on the available experimental data for light- and medium-heavy hypernuclei and on the effect of $Λ$ hyperons to the neutron star structure.

# The EOS of neutron matter and the effect of Λ hyperons to neutron star structure

## Key claim
Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of neutron matter and Λ-neutron matter show that, when hypernuclear interactions are tuned to reproduce experimental hypernuclei separation energies, the presence of Λ hyperons in neutron star cores cannot be conclusively established; the hyperon puzzle may be resolved if the three-body hyperon-nucleon force is sufficiently repulsive, but this depends on the specific parametrization of the ΛNN interaction.

## Method
The authors used auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) to solve the many-body ground state of neutron matter and Λ-neutron matter, employing non-relativistic Hamiltonians with Argonne AV8' two-body nucleon-nucleon potentials and Urbana IX three-body nucleon forces. For hyperonic systems, they added phenomenological ΛN and ΛNN interactions modeled after the Urbana-type potential, with parameters fitted to Λ separation energies of closed-shell hypernuclei across a wide mass range. The equation of state was then used to integrate the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Symmetry energy at saturation: E_sym = 32 MeV and 33.7 MeV for the two bands of EOSs studied
- At about 3ρ₀, different three-neutron force models giving the same symmetry energy at saturation produce an uncertainty in the EOS of about 20 MeV
- From neutron star observations, constraints at 2-σ confidence level: 31.2 MeV < E_sym < 34.3 MeV and 36.6 MeV < L < 55.1 MeV
- Observed neutron star masses: 1.97(4) M_⊙ (PSR J1614-2230) and 2.01(4) M_⊙ (PSR J0348+0432)
- For the ΛNN(II) interaction that best reproduces hypernuclei binding energies, the Λ fraction remains zero at least up to ρ = 0.56 fm⁻³, and the predicted maximum neutron star mass is 2.09(1) M_⊙
- The ΛN+ΛNN(I) interaction gives hyperons appearing at around twice saturation density and a predicted maximum mass less than 1.4 M_⊙

## Context
This work extends quantum Monte Carlo calculations of neutron matter EOS (Gandolfi 2012, Gandolfi 2014) to include Λ hyperons, addressing the hyperon puzzle first raised by Ambartsumyan and Saakyan (1960). The results contradict earlier predictions from some many-body methods (Dapo 2010, Schulze 2011, Vidana 2011, Massot 2012) that found strong softening of the EOS and large maximum mass reductions, while being more consistent with approaches suggesting weaker hyperon effects (Bednarek 2012, Weissenborn 2012, Jiang 2012, Miyatsu 2013, Gupta 2013, Yamamoto 2014). The findings enable future work to constrain hypernuclear interactions through systematic experimental study of hypernuclei over a wide mass range, particularly for highly asymmetric systems and excited hyperon states.
