# Rotating Bose-Einstein condensates: Closing the gap between exact and mean-field solutions

> When a Bose-Einstein condensed cloud of atoms is given some angular momentum, it forms vortices arranged in structures with a discrete rotational symmetry. For these vortex states, the Hilbert space of the exact solution separates into a "primary" space related to the mean-field Gross-Pitaevskii solution and a "complementary" space including the corrections beyond mean-field. Considering a weakly-interacting Bose-Einstein condensate of harmonically-trapped atoms, we demonstrate how this separation can be used to close the conceptual gap between exact solutions for systems with only a few atoms and the thermodynamic limit for which the mean-field is the correct leading-order approximation. Although we illustrate this approach for the case of weak interactions, it is expected to be more generally valid.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for rotating Bose-Einstein condensates, the exact many-body Hilbert space separates into a "primary" space corresponding to the mean-field Gross-Pitaevskii solution and a "complementary" space containing corrections beyond mean-field, and that this separation can close the conceptual gap between exact solutions for few-atom systems and the thermodynamic limit. The authors illustrate this approach for weak interactions but claim it is expected to be more generally valid.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the many-body Hamiltonian for harmonically-trapped bosonic atoms with contact interactions in the lowest Landau level, subject to constraints of fixed particle number N and fixed total angular momentum L. They separate the Hilbert space into a primary subspace containing only Fock states constructed from single-particle states that are macroscopically occupied in the mean-field approximation, and a complementary space containing all other Fock states. For the case L/N = 1 (unit vortex) and L/N = 9/5 (two-vortex state), they construct truncated Hamiltonians by including only the lowest-energy states for each configuration of particles in the complementary space, then diagonalize to obtain approximate energies and wavefunctions.

## Key numbers

- For L/N = 1 with N = 100 atoms, relative errors in interaction energy as small as 10^{-7} to 10^{-10} are obtained for values of N_c (highest occupancy of complementary space) approximately equal to N/2
- For L/N = 9/5, the yrast energy within the primary space is E ≈ (0.1880 N^2 - 0.3149 N) v_0, where v_0 is the interaction energy unit
- Including the m=1 orbital from the complementary space shifts the yrast energy to E ≈ (0.1880 N^2 - 0.3885 N) v_0 for L/N = 9/5
- For L/N = 9/5 with N_c = 18 particles in the complementary space, the energy shift from the primary space is ΔE = -0.1893 N v_0
- The quantity 1 - |⟨Ψ_A^0|Ψ_ex⟩|^2 shows power-law behavior ∝ N^{-γ} for large N at L/N = 9/5
- The full CI matrix dimension for the L = N = 100 case with 0 ≤ m ≤ 6 is 189,509

## Context

This work extends previous studies of rotating Bose-Einstein condensates within the Gross-Pitaevskii approximation by Butts and Rokshar (1999) and beyond mean-field by Wilkin (1998), Mottelson (1999), Bertsch (1999), and Jackson (2001). The method provides a strategy for understanding how finite-size bosonic systems approach the thermodynamic limit where the Gross-Pitaevskii approach is known to be exact, as proven by Lieb (2000, 2009) and Lewin (2009). The approach enables study of the intermediate regime between small systems accessible to exact diagonalization and large systems where mean-field is accurate, and is expected to remain valid for stronger interactions beyond the perturbative regime considered here.
