# Ground--state energies and widths of $^5$He and $^5$Li

> We extract energies and widths of the ground states of $^5$He and $^5$Li from recent single--level R--matrix fits to the spectra of the $^3$H$({\rm d},γ$)$^5$He and the $^3$He$({\rm d},γ$)$^5$Li reactions. The widths obtained differ significantly from the formal R--matrix values but they are close to those measured as full widths at half maxima of the spectra in various experiments. The energies are somewhat lower than those given by usual estimates of the peak positions. The extracted values are close to the S--matrix poles calculated previously from the multi--term analyses of the N-$^4$He elastic scattering data.

## Key claim

The S-matrix pole energies and widths of the ground states of 5He and 5Li, extracted from single-level R-matrix fits to radiative capture spectra, differ significantly from the formal R-matrix values but are close to the full widths at half maximum measured in various experiments; the real parts of the poles are shifted downwards relative to the peak positions in the spectra.

## Method

The authors extracted S-matrix pole positions (complex energies E = E0 - i Gamma/2) by solving the pole equation derived from the single-level R-matrix parameterization used by Balbes et al. (1991) to fit the gamma-ray spectra of the 3H(d,gamma)5He and 3He(d,gamma)5Li reactions at Ed = 8.6 MeV and theta_lab = 90 degrees. For 5He, the equation was solved analytically; for 5Li, Coulomb functions with complex arguments were computed using the code of Thompson and Barnett (1985) and the equation was solved with a Newton-type method. The authors also corrected the input E_res value for 5He from 0.89 MeV to 0.98 MeV to align the peak position with the observed value of 0.89 MeV, producing a second set of results.

## Key numbers

- 5He S-matrix pole energy (uncorrected): E0 = (0.735 +/- 0.02) MeV, width Gamma = (0.57 +/- 0.02) MeV
- 5He S-matrix pole energy (corrected E_res = 0.98 MeV): E0 = (0.80 +/- 0.02) MeV, width Gamma = (0.65 +/- 0.02) MeV
- 5Li S-matrix pole energy (uncorrected): E0 = (1.635 +/- 0.03) MeV, width Gamma = (1.16 +/- 0.03) MeV
- 5Li S-matrix pole energy (corrected): E0 = (1.72 +/- 0.03) MeV, width Gamma = (1.28 +/- 0.03) MeV
- Formal R-matrix widths from Balbes et al. (1991): Gamma_R(5He) = (1.36 +/- 0.19) MeV, Gamma_R(5Li) = (2.44 +/- 0.21) MeV, with channel radius R = 3.6 fm and E_res = 0.89 MeV (5He) and 1.97 MeV (5Li)
- The corrected 5He results agree with Bond and Kirk (1977) (E0 = 0.77 MeV, Gamma = 0.64 MeV) and Kraus and Linck (1974) (E0 = 0.78 MeV, Gamma = 0.72 MeV) from multi-term analyses of elastic n-4He scattering
- The corrected 5Li E0 = 1.72 MeV agrees with Kraus and Linck (1974) (E0 = 1.6 MeV) from elastic p-4He scattering

## Context

This work extends the single-level R-matrix analysis of Balbes et al. (1991) by extracting the physical S-matrix pole parameters, which differ significantly from the formal R-matrix widths but are close to the full widths at half maximum measured in various experiments. The results are in good agreement with the multi-term analyses of elastic N-4He scattering by Bond and Kirk (1977) and Kraus and Linck (1974), and the extracted widths are relevant for astrophysical applications such as those by Gorres et al. (1995), Oberhummer et al. (1995), and Efros et al. (1995). The work also addresses difficulties in obtaining p-wave N-4He phase shifts from realistic non-central NN interactions, as noted by Ustinin and Efros (1989).
