# Tritonlike molecules of three identical baryons

> In nuclear physics, triton and helium-3 nucleus can be understood as three-body hadronic molecules. Analogous to the loosely bound structures for the triton and helium-3 nucleus, whether there is a bound state formed by three hadrons leaves us an open issue. Based on the one-boson exchange model as well as the adoption of the variational approach, we make a comprehensive investigation on the tritonlike systems of three identical baryons $NNN$, $ΛΛΛ$, $ΞΞΞ$ and $ΣΣΣ$. We predict that the three-body molecular states for the systems of three identical hadrons of baryon octet are probably existent as long as their two-body subsystems have bound states. The numerical results of this work may be helpful for the theoretical and experimental researches on the tri-hadron molecules in future.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that three-body molecular bound states of identical baryons from the octet (NNN, ΛΛΛ, ΞΞΞ, ΣΣΣ) are probably existent, but only under the condition that their two-body subsystems themselves have bound states; the prediction is hedged as "probably existent" rather than definitive.

## Method

The authors construct a formalism for three identical fermions using the Born-Oppenheimer potential (BOP) method, where the influence of one fermion on the other two is represented as a BO potential derived from one-boson exchange (OBE) interactions including π, η, σ, ρ, ω, and φ exchange. They solve the three-body Schrödinger equation in a configuration space of three basis states (each omitting one fermion and replacing it with its BO potential), using an orthonormalized basis of Laguerre polynomials, and include distortion corrections from the three-body wave function. The method is first verified on the three-nucleon system (NNN) before being extended to the hyperon systems ΛΛΛ, ΞΞΞ, and ΣΣΣ.

## Key numbers

- The triton (NNN) has a binding energy of 8.40 MeV, and the helium-3 nucleus has a binding energy of 7.80 MeV, cited as known experimental values for the physical three-nucleon systems.
- The two-body binding energy E₂ for the N-N subsystem in the (I₂,S₂) = (0,1) channel (deuteron-like) is used as input to calibrate the scaling parameter λ; the specific numerical value of E₂ is not given in the truncated text.
- For the ΛΛΛ system, only the (I₂,S₂) = (0,0) case is considered; no explicit binding energy numbers for the three-body systems are reported in the truncated text.

## Context

This work extends the one-boson exchange model, previously successful for di-hadron molecules (Li 2012, Chen 2015, Zhao 2014, Zhao 2015, Sun 2011), to tri-hadron systems composed of identical baryons from the octet. It builds on the understanding of the triton and helium-3 as three-body hadronic molecules and uses the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, previously applied to systems with light and heavy particles (Ma 2017, Moroz 2014, Braaten 2014, Zhao 2014). The results are intended to guide future theoretical and experimental searches for tri-hadron molecular states, with other configurations (non-identical baryons) left for future work.
