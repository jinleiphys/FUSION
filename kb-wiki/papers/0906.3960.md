# Particle Ratios as a Probe of the QCD Critical Temperature

> We show how the measured particle ratios can be used to provide non-trivial information about the critical temperature of the QCD phase transition. This is obtained by including the effects of highly massive Hagedorn resonances on statistical models, which are used to describe hadronic yields. The inclusion of Hagedorn states creates a dependence of the thermal fits on the Hagedorn temperature, $T_H$, which is assumed to be equal to $T_c$, and leads to an overall improvement of thermal fits. We find that for Au+Au collisions at RHIC at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV the best square fit measure, $χ^2$, occurs at $T_c \sim 176$ MeV and produces a chemical freeze-out temperature of 172.6 MeV and a baryon chemical potential of 39.7 MeV.

## Key claim

The measured particle ratios in Au+Au collisions at RHIC can be used to determine the QCD critical temperature, provided that the effects of highly massive Hagedorn resonances are included in the statistical model; the best fit favors a lower critical temperature around 176 MeV over a higher one around 196 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed thermal fits of hadron yield ratios using a grand-canonical statistical model that includes an exponentially growing Hagedorn resonance spectrum above 2 GeV, with the Hagedorn temperature assumed equal to the QCD critical temperature. They fitted the model parameters (maximum Hagedorn mass and degeneracy) to lattice QCD thermodynamics at zero baryon chemical potential from two different collaborations (RBC-Bielefeld and BMW), then varied the chemical freeze-out temperature and baryon chemical potential to minimize chi-squared against experimental data from STAR and PHENIX for six particle ratios.

## Key numbers

- Best fit for the BMW collaboration (Tc = 176 MeV) gave chi-squared = 17.8, chemical freeze-out temperature Tch = 172.6 MeV, and baryon chemical potential μb = 39.7 MeV.
- Best fit for the RBC-Bielefeld collaboration (Tc = 196 MeV) gave chi-squared = 20.9, Tch = 165.9 MeV, and μb = 25.3 MeV.
- Fit without Hagedorn states gave chi-squared = 21.2, Tch = 160.4 MeV, and μb = 22.9 MeV.
- For the BMW case, Hagedorn states contributed 48.5% of pions, 41.6% of kaons, 29.1% of protons, and 41.0% of lambdas.
- For the RBC-Bielefeld case, Hagedorn states contributed 11.2% of pions, 10.5% of kaons, 4.7% of protons, and 6.2% of lambdas.

## Context

This work extends statistical model fits to particle ratios, previously done by Andronic et al. (2005) and Manninen et al. (2008), by including Hagedorn resonances to connect the chemical freeze-out temperature to the QCD critical temperature. The results support earlier findings by Noronha-Hostler et al. (2007) that Hagedorn states help drive hadrons into chemical equilibrium near Tc, and they enable future experimental discrimination between lattice QCD predictions from the RBC-Bielefeld collaboration (Tc around 196 MeV) and the BMW collaboration (Tc around 176 MeV).
