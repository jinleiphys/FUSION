# Femtoscopic study of coupled-channel $NΞ$ and $ΛΛ$ interactions

> The momentum correlation functions of S = -2 baryon pairs (p Xi^- and Lambda Lambda) produced in high-energy pp and pA collisions are investigated on the basis of the coupled-channel formalism. The strong interaction is described by the coupled-channel HAL QCD potential obtained by the lattice QCD simulations near physical quark masses, while the hadronic source function is taken to be a static Gaussian form. The coupled-channel effect, the threshold difference, the realistic strong interaction, and the Coulomb interaction are fully taken into account for the first time in the femtoscopic analysis of baryon-baryon correlations. The characteristic features of the experimental data for the p Xi^- and Lambda Lambda pairs at LHC are reproduced quantitatively with a suitable choice of non-femtoscopic parameters and the source size. The agreement between theory and experiment indicates that the N Xi (Lambda Lambda) interaction is moderately (weakly) attractive without having a quasi-bound (bound) state.

## Key claim
The coupled-channel HAL QCD potential, combined with a static Gaussian source function, quantitatively reproduces the experimental LHC data for both \(p\Xi^-\) and \(\Lambda\Lambda\) correlation functions, indicating that the \(N\Xi\) interaction is moderately attractive without a quasi-bound state and the \(\Lambda\Lambda\) interaction is weakly attractive without a bound state.

## Method
The authors solved the coupled-channel Schrödinger equation for the \(p\Xi^-\)-\(n\Xi^0\)-\(\Lambda\Lambda\) system (spin 0 and spin 1) using the local, energy-independent HAL QCD potential from lattice QCD at near-physical quark masses (\(m_\pi = 146\) MeV, \(m_K = 525\) MeV). The Coulomb interaction was included for the charged \(p\Xi^-\) channel, and the correlation functions were computed via the Koonin–Pratt–Lednicky–Lyuboshits–Lyuboshits (KPLLL) formula with a static Gaussian source of size \(R\). The spin-averaged \(p\Xi^-\) correlation function was obtained as a weighted sum of singlet (1/4) and triplet (3/4) contributions.

## Key numbers
- Scattering length for \(p\Xi^-\) (\(J=0\)): \(a_0 = -1.22 (0.13)(^{+0.08}_{-0.00}) - i1.57 (0.35)(^{+0.18}_{-0.23})\) fm (with Coulomb switched off).
- Scattering length for \(\Lambda\Lambda\) (\(J=0\)): \(a_0 = -0.78 (0.22)(^{+0.00}_{-0.13})\) fm (real, no lower channel).
- Scattering length for \(n\Xi^0\) (\(J=0\)): \(a_0 = -2.07 (0.39)(^{+0.28}_{-0.35}) - i0.14 (0.08)(^{+0.00}_{-0.01})\) fm.
- Effective range for \(p\Xi^-\) (\(J=0\)): \(r_{\rm eff} = 3.7 (0.3)(^{+0.1}_{-0.1}) - i2.7 (0.2)(^{+0.1}_{-0.3})\) fm.
- Lattice pion and kaon masses used in the potential: \(m_\pi = 146\) MeV, \(m_K = 525\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the femtoscopic analysis of \(S=-2\) baryon pairs by incorporating, for the first time, the full coupled-channel \(N\Xi\)-\(\Lambda\Lambda\) framework with threshold differences, realistic strong interactions from lattice QCD (HAL QCD collaboration, Sasaki et al. 2020), and Coulomb interactions. It builds on earlier femtoscopic studies of \(\Lambda\Lambda\) correlations (Morita et al. 2015; ALICE 2019) and \(p\Xi^-\) correlations (ALICE 2019, 2020), and on the coupled-channel treatment of the \(S=-1\) \(\bar{K}N\)-\(\pi\Sigma\)-\(\pi\Lambda\) system (Kamiya et al. 2020). The results rule out a bound \(H\) dibaryon below \(\Lambda\Lambda\) and a quasi-bound state below \(N\Xi\), consistent with the existence of \(\Xi\)-hypernuclei (Nakazawa et al. 2015; Hayakawa et al. 2021; Yoshimoto et al. 2021) and with chiral effective field theory calculations (Haidenbauer et al. 2016; Li et al. 2018). This enables quantitative extraction of hyperon–hyperon and hyperon–nucleon interactions from LHC data, with implications for the hyperon puzzle in neutron star matter (Nishizaki et al. 2002; Lattimer 2021).
