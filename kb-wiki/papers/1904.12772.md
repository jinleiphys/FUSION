# Bulk viscosity and contact correlations in attractive Fermi gases

> The bulk viscosity determines dissipation during hydrodynamic expansion. It vanishes in scale invariant fluids, while a nonzero value quantifies the deviation from scale invariance. For the dilute Fermi gas the bulk viscosity is given exactly by the correlation function of the contact density of local pairs. As a consequence, scale invariance is broken purely by pair fluctuations. These fluctuations give rise also to logarithmic terms in the bulk viscosity of the high-temperature nondegenerate gas. For the quantum degenerate regime I report numerical Luttinger-Ward results for the contact correlator and the dynamical bulk viscosity throughout the BEC-BCS crossover. The ratio of bulk to shear viscosity $ζ/η$ is found to exceed the kinetic theory prediction in the quantum degenerate regime. Near the superfluid phase transition the bulk viscosity is enhanced by critical fluctuations and has observable effects on dissipative heating, expansion dynamics and sound attenuation.

# Bulk viscosity and contact correlations in attractive Fermi gases

## Key claim

The bulk viscosity of the dilute Fermi gas is exactly given by the correlation function of the contact density of local pairs, meaning scale invariance is broken purely by pair fluctuations; however, the bulk viscosity vanishes precisely at unitarity (where 1/a=0) due to a prefactor c_d^2 ~ 1/a^2, even though pair fluctuations remain strong there.

## Method

The author rewrites the bulk viscosity as a correlation function of the contact density operator, which is proportional to the local pair density operator Δ†Δ. Analytical results are obtained in the high-temperature limit via virial expansion to second order in fugacity, and numerical results for the quantum degenerate regime are computed using the Luttinger-Ward diagrammatic strong-coupling approach, which treats fermions and the pair field on equal footing and includes vertex corrections representing scattering between pairs.

## Key numbers

- At unitarity in the high-temperature limit, the dynamical bulk viscosity has a logarithmic singularity a²ζ ~ ln(T/ω) z² for small frequencies from the modified Bessel function K₀(βω/2).
- The dc bulk viscosity in the high-temperature limit throughout the BEC-BCS crossover is given by ζ_3D,vir(v) = (2√2/9π) z² λ⁻³ v² [-1 - (1+v²)e^(v²) Ei(-v²)], with v = (λ/a)/√(2π).
- In 2D, the dc bulk viscosity is approximately ζ_2D,vir(εb/T) ≃ 2π z² λ⁻² / [ln²(T/2εb) + π²]².
- The ratio ζ/η exceeds the kinetic theory prediction ζ_kin ≡ η[(P-2E/3)/P]² in the quantum degenerate regime, with ζ > ℏ n near T_c.
- The sum rule S = (2/π)∫₀^∞ dω ζ(ω) in 3D is given by S = (1/36π m a²) (∂C/∂a⁻¹)_s, where C is the contact density.

## Context

This work extends kinetic theory calculations of the bulk viscosity for the nondegenerate high-temperature limit by Dusling (2013) and Chafin (2013), and for the low-temperature superfluid state by Escobedo (2009) and Hou (2013). The Luttinger-Ward approach previously applied to shear viscosity by Enss (2011) is here extended to compute bulk viscosity via the contact correlation function. The results enable experimental observation of bulk viscosity through dissipative heating rate measurements following Fujii (2018), through scaling or breathing dynamics as in Murthy (2019), and through sound attenuation.
