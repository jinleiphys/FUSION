# Description of nuclear systems within the relativistic Hartree-Fock method with zero range self-interactions of the scalar field

> An exact method is suggested to treat the nonlinear self-interactions (NLSI) in the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approach for nuclear systems. We consider here the NLSI constructed from the relativistic scalar nucleon densities and including products of six and eight fermion fields. This type of NLSI corresponds to the zero range limit of the standard cubic and quartic self-interactions of the scalar field. The method to treat the NLSI uses the Fierz transformation, which enables one to express the exchange (Fock) components in terms of the direct (Hartree) ones. The method is applied to nuclear matter and finite nuclei. It is shown that, in the RHF formalism, the NLSI, which are explicitly isovector-independent, generate scalar, vector and tensor nucleon self-energies strongly density-dependent. This strong isovector structure of the self-energies is due to the exchange terms of the RHF method. Calculations are carried out with a parametrization containing five free parameters. The model allows a description of both types of systems compatible with experimental data.

# Description of nuclear systems within the relativistic Hartree-Fock method with zero range self-interactions of the scalar field

## Key claim

The paper presents an exact method to treat nonlinear self-interactions (NLSI) of the scalar field in the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) framework by taking the zero range limit, which allows the Fock exchange terms to be expressed exactly via the direct Hartree terms using the Fierz transformation. The authors show that isovector-independent NLSI generate scalar, vector, and tensor nucleon self-energies with a strong isovector structure and density dependence, but only when the exchange terms of the RHF method are included.

## Method

The authors construct a Lagrangian containing linear meson-exchange terms (sigma, omega, pion, and rho mesons) plus NLSI terms of the form (psi-bar psi)^3 and (psi-bar psi)^4, which correspond to the zero range limit of the standard cubic and quartic self-interactions of the scalar field. They apply the Fierz transformation to express the exchange (Fock) components of these NLSI in terms of the direct (Hartree) components, then derive the contributions to the total energy and to the scalar, vector, and tensor nucleon self-energies. The model is applied to both symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter and to finite doubly-magic nuclei (16O, 40Ca, 48Ca, 90Zr, 208Pb) using a five-parameter fit.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density for symmetric nuclear matter: rho_0 = 0.14 fm^{-3} for the ZRL1 and ZRL2 parameter sets
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: E/A = -15.75 MeV for both ZRL1 and ZRL2 sets
- Compressibility modulus: K = 250 MeV for the ZRL1 set, K = 275 MeV for the ZRL2 set
- Scalar meson mass: m_sigma = 550 MeV for the ZRL1 set, m_sigma = 530 MeV for the ZRL2 set (compared to 500 MeV in the HFSI set of Bernardos et al. (1993))
- Coupling constants for the ZRL1 set: g_s^2/4pi = 3.0621, g_omega^2/4pi = 7.849, b-bar = -0.011533, c-bar = -0.01461
- Coupling constants for the ZRL2 set: g_s^2/4pi = 3.1715, g_omega^2/4pi = 8.4777, b-bar = -0.006718, c-bar = 0.042561
- Pion coupling constant: f_pi^2/4pi = 0.08 (experimental value)
- Rho meson coupling constant: g_rho^2/4pi = 0.55 (experimental value)
- Ratio f_rho/g_rho = 3.7 (from vector dominance model)
- Meson masses: m_pi = 138 MeV, m_omega = 783 MeV, m_rho = 770 MeV (empirical values)
- Nucleon mass: M = 939 MeV

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Hartree-Fock approach developed by Bouyssy et al. (1987) and Bernardos et al. (1993) by providing an exact treatment of nonlinear self-interactions in the zero range limit, whereas Bernardos et al. (1993) used an approximate method based on a scalar meson effective mass. The method enables a simultaneous description of nuclear matter and finite nuclei with five free parameters, achieving results comparable to the earlier HFSI approximation but with solutions valid up to much higher densities (beyond 0.22 fm^{-3} where the HFSI set breaks down). The approach can be extended to treat self-interactions of the omega^4 type and other mixed nonlinearities, as noted by Savushkin et al. (1997), and connects to point-coupling models discussed by Hoch et al. (1994) and Burvenich et al. (2002).
