# Equation-of-State Independent Relations in Rapidly Rotating Hybrid Stars

> We study 22 hadronic and 100 hybrid equations of state (EoSs) that allow for heavy baryons and deconfined quark matter at high densities, aiming to establish quasi-universal relations for both slowly and rapidly rotating neutron stars. All EoSs are consistent with current observational constraints, including NICER and GW170817. Our results confirm that the I-Love-Q and I-C-Q relations remain approximately EoS-insensitive across this broad EoS set, with deviations typically within 10\% for hadronic stars and up to 20\% for those with complex core compositions. These relations are extended to include low-mass neutron stars such as HESS J1731-347, and to stars with general core compositions--nucleonic, hyperonic (full baryon octet), and quark matter. The analysis underscores both the robustness and the limitations of universal relations when applied to compact stars with exotic degrees of freedom and rapid rotation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the I-Love-Q and I-C-Q quasi-universal relations remain approximately equation-of-state independent across a broad set of 22 hadronic and 100 hybrid equations of state, but the universality degrades when exotic core compositions (heavy baryons and deconfined quark matter) are included, with deviations typically within 10% for hadronic stars and up to 20% for hybrid stars.

## Method

The author constructed 22 hadronic equations of state within the Relativistic Mean Field framework (including NL3, DD2, DDHδ, DDME2, DDMEX, GM1, TM1, MTVTC, FSU2, TMA, and D1M* parametrizations) that incorporate the full baryon octet, and an additional 100 hybrid equations of state that allow for hadron-quark deconfinement transitions via Maxwell construction and the replacement interpolation method. Equilibrium configurations for both slowly rotating (spin parameter χ = 10⁻³) and rapidly rotating stars (χ = 0.3, 0.4, 0.6, and Keplerian sequences) were computed, and fits were performed to establish relations between dimensionless moment of inertia (Ī), compactness (C), dimensionless quadrupole moment (Q̄), and tidal deformability (Λ̄).

## Key numbers

- 22 hadronic equations of state and 100 hybrid equations of state were studied
- Deviations in the I-Love-Q and I-C-Q relations are typically within 10% for hadronic stars and up to 20% for hybrid stars with complex core compositions
- The central compact object HESS J1731-347 has mass M = 0.77⁺⁰·²⁰₋₀·₁₇ M⊙ and radius R = 10.4⁺⁰·⁸⁶₋₀·₇₈ km
- PSR J0740+6620 has mass 2.08⁺⁰·⁰⁷₋₀·⁰⁷ M⊙ at 68.3% credibility
- PSR J1748-2446ad spins at 716 Hz with mass around 2 M⊙ and radius within approximately 16 km

## Context

This work extends the quasi-universal I-Love-Q relations originally established by Yagi (2013) and the I-C-Q relations for rapidly rotating stars by Doneva (2013, 2014) and Pappas (2014) to include both heavy baryonic degrees of freedom (full baryon octet) and hadron-quark phase transitions. The study builds on prior work by Chatterjee (2016), Li (2018), and Lenka (2019) who examined how heavy baryons modify neutron star structure, and by Khosravi Largani (2022) and Papigkiotis (2023) who explored universal relations for rapidly rotating hybrid stars with limited equation of state sets. The results enable more robust inference of neutron star properties from gravitational wave and electromagnetic observations even when the core composition is unknown, while also quantifying the systematic uncertainties introduced by exotic matter.
