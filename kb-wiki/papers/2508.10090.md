# Baryon-baryon, meson-meson, and meson-baryon interactions in nonrelativistic QCD

> Van der Waals potentials describing interactions between color-singlet mesons and/or baryons vanish at leading order in potential nonrelativistic quantum chromodynamics (pNRQCD). This result and constraints from Gauss's law are used to prove that weakly-coupled pNRQCD van der Waals potentials in generic non-Abelian gauge theories with only heavy quarks are too weak to form bound states whose color state is a product of color-singlets. Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of four, five, and six quarks with equal masses provide numerical evidence that exotic color configurations are higher energy than products of color-singlet hadrons, suggesting that equal-mass fully-heavy tetraquark, pentaquark, and hexaquark bound states do not exist at next-to-leading order in pNRQCD and at all orders in QCD-like theories in which all quark masses are asymptotically large. Mechanisms for generating hadron-hadron bound states are identified, which necessarily involve large quark-mass hierarchies, relativistic effects arising from the presence of sufficiently light quarks, or nonperturbative effects outside the scope of weakly-coupled pNRQCD.

## Key claim

Van der Waals potentials between color-singlet mesons and/or baryons vanish at leading order in potential nonrelativistic QCD (pNRQCD), and Gauss's law constraints prove that weakly-coupled pNRQCD van der Waals potentials in generic non-Abelian gauge theories with only heavy quarks are too weak to form bound states whose color state is a product of color-singlets, but this result strictly applies only in the asymptotic heavy-mass limit and does not rule out bound states arising from large quark-mass hierarchies, relativistic effects from light quarks, or nonperturbative effects outside weakly-coupled pNRQCD.

## Method

The authors use all-orders analytic arguments based on generator tracelessness and Gauss's law to prove that van der Waals potentials between color-singlet hadrons vanish at leading order and fall at least as fast as 1/R^3 at all orders in the pNRQCD expansion for SU(N), SO(N), and Sp(2N) gauge theories. They then perform Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations of four-, five-, and six-quark systems with equal masses using a complete basis of color tensors and physically motivated trial wavefunctions at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) in pNRQCD to search for exotic color configurations that could form bound states.

## Key numbers

- The color-singlet Q̅Q potential is given by -α/r times d_Adj/(2N), where d_Adj = N^2-1 for SU(N); for SU(3) this gives -4α_V/(3|r|) at tree level
- The color-antisymmetric QQ potential is given by -α/r times d_Adj/(2N(N-1)); for SU(3) this gives -2α_V/(3|r|) at tree level
- The adjoint Q̅Q potential at tree level is α_V/(6|r|), with NNLO corrections δa_2^8 = α_s(α_s/(4π))^2[-9π^2(12-π^2)] + O(α_s^4)
- The color-symmetric QQ potential at tree level is α_V/(6|r|), with NNLO corrections δa_2^6 = α_s(α_s/(4π))^2[-15π^2(12-π^2)] + O(α_s^4)
- The color-antisymmetric QQ potential NNLO corrections are δa_2^3̅ = α_s(α_s/(4π))^2[-(3/2)π^2(12-π^2)] + O(α_s^4)

## Context

This work extends the pNRQCD framework for multihadron systems developed in Assi (2023) and the variational and GFMC studies of tetraquark systems in Assi (2023), which found that tetraquark bound states exist only when the heavy quark/antiquark mass ratio exceeds a critical value consistent with earlier variational studies of one-gluon-exchange potentials by Czarnecki (2017). The results contradict the possibility of equal-mass fully-heavy tetraquark, pentaquark, and hexaquark bound states at NLO in pNRQCD, while identifying mechanisms for generating hadron-hadron bound states that necessarily involve large quark-mass hierarchies, relativistic effects from light quarks, or nonperturbative effects outside weakly-coupled pNRQCD. The work enables future studies of heavy-quark dark sector models and lattice QCD calculations of two-baryon systems with heavy quarks.
