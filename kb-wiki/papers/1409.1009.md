# $Ω_{ccc}$ Production in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> We investigate the production of $Ω_{ccc}$ baryon in high energy nuclear collisions via quark coalescence mechanism. The wave function of $Ω_{ccc}$ is solved from the Schrödinger equation for the bound state of three charm quarks by using the hyperspherical method. The production cross section of $Ω_{ccc}$ per binary collision in a central Pb+Pb collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV reaches 9 nb, which is at least two orders of magnitude larger than that in a p+p collision at the same energy. Therefore, it is most probable to discover $Ω_{ccc}$ in heavy ion collisions at LHC, and the observation will be a clear signature of the quark-gluon plasma formation.

## Key claim

The production cross section of the $\Omega_{ccc}$ baryon per binary collision in central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV reaches 9 nb, which is at least two orders of magnitude larger than in p+p collisions at the same energy, but the authors note that this yield from the sudden coalescence approach without considering dissociation is more like an upper limit of the production.

## Method

The authors solve the non-relativistic three-body Schrödinger equation for the ground state of three charm quarks using the hyperspherical method, with the potential taken as a sum of pair interactions using the Cornell potential with parameters that reproduce $J/\psi$ and $\Upsilon$ masses. They then construct the Wigner function from the obtained wave function and use it as the coalescence probability in a coalescence model, where the charm quark distribution is taken as a kinetically thermalized phase space distribution with local temperature and fluid velocity determined by hydrodynamic equations for the fireball evolution.

## Key numbers

- $\Omega_{ccc}$ mass: 4.7 GeV, with binding energy 900 MeV
- Average radius of $\Omega_{ccc}$: approximately 0.5 fm
- Most probable phase space location: $(r,p) \sim (0.5\ \text{fm}, 1\ \text{GeV})$, giving $r \cdot p \approx 2.5$
- $\Omega_{ccc}$ yield in most central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV: $5 \times 10^{-4}$
- Effective cross section per binary collision for $\Omega_{ccc}$: 9 nb (using $\sigma_{pp}=62$ mb, $\Delta \eta=1.8$, $N_{coll}=2000$)
- Charm quark mass used: $m_c = 1.25$ GeV
- Critical temperature for coalescence: $T_c = 165$ MeV
- Maximum temperature at starting time $\tau_0=0.6$ fm/c: $T_0=484$ MeV
- Charm production cross section: $d\sigma_{c\bar c}/d\eta = 0.7$ mb

## Context

This work extends the coalescence mechanism, previously applied to light hadron production and quarkonium production (Fries 2003, Molnar 2004, Greco 2003), to the production of the triply-charmed $\Omega_{ccc}$ baryon as a probe of quark-gluon plasma formation. The authors note that the statistical coalescence model was previously used to study particles with double, triple and hidden charm (Kostyuk 2003), and that $B_c$ meson production via regeneration was suggested for RHIC and LHC (Schroedter 2000, Liu 2009). The calculation enables experimental searches for $\Omega_{ccc}$ at LHC, particularly through weak decay modes such as $\Omega_{ccc} \to \Omega_{sss} + 3\pi^+$, and the observation would serve as a clear signature of quark-gluon plasma formation.
