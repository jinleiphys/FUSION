# Four-boson bound states from a functional renormalisation group

> We use the functional renormalisation group to study the spectrum of three- and four-body states in bosonic systems around the unitary limit. Our effective action includes all energy-independent contact interactions in the four-atom sector and we introduce a running trimer field to eliminate couplings that involve the atom-atom-dimer channel. The results show qualitatively similar behaviour to those from exact approaches. The truncated action we use leads to overbinding of the two four-body states seen in those treatments. It also generates a third state, although only for a very narrow range of two-body scattering lengths.

# Four-boson bound states from a functional renormalisation group

## Key claim
The functional renormalisation group (FRG) with a truncated action that includes all energy-independent contact interactions and a running trimer field yields three four-body bound states per Efimov cycle, one more than seen in exact solutions, but the third state exists only within a very narrow range of two-body scattering lengths and the two deeper states are overbound relative to exact results.

## Method
The authors extend a previous FRG analysis (Jaramillo Ávila and Birse, 2013) from zero-energy to nonzero energy by adding an external energy flowing along each atom line, shifting the expansion point for the action. They evolve the effective action from a large cutoff scale where only atom fields are dynamical, using ordinary differential equations for all couplings derived from the Wetterich flow equation, and identify bound-state energies by scanning the atom-atom scattering length at fixed external energy to find where the trimer self-energy vanishes or four-body couplings diverge.

## Key numbers
- The momentum scaling factor from the Efimov effect in this truncation is 29.8, compared to the true value of 22.7.
- The trimer binding momentum at the unitary limit is κ₃|a₃| = 1.71, compared to the exact result 1.5077 from Deltuva (2012).
- The trimer becomes unbound at |a₃|/a = +31.1 in this approach, versus the exact value of +21.30 from Deltuva (2011, 2012).
- The tetramer states become bound with respect to the four-atom channel at |a₃|/a = -2.29, -1.14, and -1.003; exact treatments give -2.351 and -1.096 from Deltuva (2012).
- At the unitary limit, the two surviving tetramer binding momenta are κ|a₃| = 3.71 and 1.89, corresponding to κ/κ₃ = 2.18 and 1.11; exact results are 2.147 and 1.0011 from Deltuva (2010).
- The deepest tetramer becomes unbound at the dimer-dimer threshold at |a₃|/a = +11.75, compared to +9.700 from Deltuva (2011, 2012).
- The second tetramer hits the trimer threshold at |a₃|/a = +3.99, compared to +3.140.

## Context
This work extends the FRG approach of Jaramillo Ávila and Birse (2013) from zero-energy to the full spectrum of three- and four-body bound states in bosonic systems near the unitary limit. It builds on earlier RG analyses of the Efimov effect (Bedaque et al., 1999, 2000; Braaten and Hammer, 2006) and exact few-body calculations of four-body states (Hammer and Platter, 2007; von Stecher et al., 2009; Deltuva, 2010, 2011, 2012). The results show that the truncated FRG can reproduce qualitative features of the exact solutions with quantitative agreement at the 20% level or better for many quantities, but the overbinding and extra tetramer state indicate the need for truncations that include more energy dependence through higher-order terms in propagators and energy- or momentum-dependent couplings.
