# The nucleon in the chiral quark model: an alternative computation scheme

> We formulate the quark sea contributions to the energy and various physical observables in terms of Euclidean Green functions and their K-spin partial wave reduction. In this framework it is not necessary to discretize the continuous spectrum by introducing a finite boundary. Using this formulation we perform a new self-consistent computation of the nucleon state in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. Besides this technical advantage such an alternative computation scheme makes it possible to obtain the numerical predictions of the model in an entirely independent way. We use the Pauli-Villars cutoff to define the model. Our results for the nucleon energy, the mesonic profile, and various observables essentially confirm results obtained by other groups using this regularization. The results for $g\sub{A}$, obtained on the basis of quark currents, are close to the experimental value.

## Key claim

The authors present an alternative computational scheme for the nucleon in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model that uses Euclidean Green functions and their K-spin partial wave reduction, avoiding the need to discretize the continuous spectrum by introducing a finite boundary; their results for the nucleon energy, mesonic profile, and various observables "essentially confirm" results obtained by other groups using Pauli-Villars regularization, with the axial coupling constant g_A obtained from quark currents being "close to the experimental value."

## Method

The authors formulate the quark sea contributions to the energy and physical observables in terms of Euclidean Green functions and their K-spin partial wave reduction, using the Pauli-Villars cutoff to regularize the theory. They perform a self-consistent computation of the nucleon state in the NJL model with only pi and sigma fields varied on the chiral circle, applying Pauli-Villars regularization to the quark sea but not to the filled bound state. The method computes the zero point energy and its functional derivative with respect to the meson field using the bosonic Green function, and also formulates the moment of inertia in terms of these Green functions for quantization of collective coordinates.

## Key numbers

- The Pauli-Villars cutoff Lambda is fixed by the relation Lambda = M * sqrt(exp(4 pi^2 / (N_c g^2))), where M = g f_pi is the dynamical quark mass and N_c is the number of colors.
- The bound state energy E_bou is obtained from the eigenvalue equation (-Delta + V^{0+}(x)) psi_0 = omega_0^2 psi_0, where omega_0 is the bound state eigenvalue.
- The symmetry breaking contribution to the energy is E_br = -4 pi m_pi^2 f_pi^2 integral dr r^2 [cos(vartheta(r)) - 1].
- The moment of inertia theta_ab is proportional to the unit matrix, theta_ab = theta delta_ab, and contributes an extra term J(J+1)/(2 theta) to the nucleon energy after collective coordinate quantization.

## Context

This work extends the Euclidean Green function technique previously developed by Baacke (1990, 1992) for computing functional determinants and fluctuation determinants to the NJL model for nucleon structure. It provides an independent computational approach that avoids the level summation method used by groups such as Pobylitsa et al. (1998) for parton distributions, and addresses the momentum sum rule violation found by Diakonov (1996) when using Schwinger proper-time regularization. The technique enables self-consistent computation of mesonic profiles and sea quark contributions to observables like the moment of inertia without reverting to level summation, and can be applied to other problems involving the effective action in the NJL model.
