# Constraints on the Symmetry Energy Using the Mass-Radius Relation of Neutron Stars

> The nuclear symmetry energy is intimately connected with nuclear astrophysics. This contribution focuses on the estimation of the symmetry energy from experiment and how it is related to the structure of neutron stars. The most important connection is between the radii of neutron stars and the pressure of neutron star matter in the vicinity of the nuclear saturation density $n_s$. This pressure is essentially controlled by the nuclear symmetry energy parameters $S_v$ and $L$, the first two coefficients of a Taylor expansion of the symmetry energy around $n_s$. We discuss constraints on these parameters that can be found from nuclear experiments. We demonstrate that these constraints are largely model-independent by deriving them qualitatively from a simple nuclear model. We also summarize how recent theoretical studies of pure neutron matter can reinforce these constraints. To date, several different astrophysical measurements of neutron star radii have been attempted. Attention is focused on photospheric radius expansion bursts and on thermal emissions from quiescent low-mass X-ray binaries. While none of these observations can, at the present time, determine individual neutron star radii to better than 20% accuracy, the body of observations can be used with Bayesian techniques to effectively constrain them to higher precision. These techniques invert the structure equations and obtain estimates of the pressure-density relation of neutron star matter, not only near $n_s$, but up to the highest densities found in neutron star interiors. The estimates we derive for neutron star radii are in concordance with predictions from nuclear experiment and theory.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the pressure of neutron star matter near nuclear saturation density, which controls neutron star radii, can be expressed solely in terms of the symmetry energy parameters \(S_v\) and \(L\) in the quadratic approximation, but this connection relies on the assumption that quartic and higher-order terms in the energy expansion are negligible, which has not been experimentally verified.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for the symmetry energy, dipole polarizability, and neutron skin thickness within a generalized hydrodynamical model that extends the Lipparini and Stringari (1989) approach to allow arbitrary density dependence of the symmetry energy \(S_2(n)\). They then use these expressions to map constraints from nuclear experiments (masses, dipole resonances, neutron skin measurements) onto the \(S_v\)-\(L\) parameter space relevant for neutron star structure, and compare these with astrophysical constraints from Bayesian analyses of neutron star radius observations.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: \(n_s \simeq 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Binding energy of symmetric matter at saturation: \(B \simeq 16\) MeV
- Symmetric matter surface energy parameter: \(E_s \simeq 19\) MeV
- Surface thickness parameter: \(a = 0.523\) fm, giving \(t_{90-10} \simeq 2.3\) fm
- Gradient coefficient: \(Q \simeq 3.65\) MeV fm\(^2\)
- Surface tension: \(\sigma_o \simeq 1.17\) MeV fm\(^{-2}\)
- Symmetric matter surface energy from hydrodynamical model: \(E_s = 4\pi r_o^2 \sigma_o \simeq 19.2\) MeV
- Compressibility parameter: \(K \simeq 240\) MeV
- Coefficients \(C(n_s, 1.4M_\odot)\) for pressure-radius correlation: \(9.52 \pm 0.49\) km (for \(\hat M = 2.0 M_\odot\))
- Coefficients \(C(1.5n_s, 1.4M_\odot)\): \(7.06 \pm 0.24\) km (for \(\hat M = 2.0 M_\odot\))
- Coefficients \(C(2n_s, 1.4M_\odot)\): \(5.68 \pm 0.14\) km (for \(\hat M = 2.0 M_\odot\))
- Measured neutron star masses: PSR J1614+2230 at \(M = 1.97 \pm 0.04 M_\odot\); PSR J0348+0432 at \(M = 2.01 \pm 0.04 M_\odot\)
- Proton fraction at saturation density in beta equilibrium: \(x \simeq 0.04\)
- Correlation ellipse orientation from liquid drop model: \(\alpha_{\rm DM} \simeq 9.8^\circ\) with respect to \(S_s\) axis
- Correlation coefficient from liquid drop model: \(r_{\rm DM} \simeq 0.997\)

## Context

This work extends the pressure-radius correlation found by Lattimer and Prakash (2001) and the re-analysis by Lattimer and Lim (2013) that restricted to equations of state satisfying the \(2.0 M_\odot\) maximum mass constraint. The authors generalize the hydrodynamical model of Lipparini and Stringari (1989) to allow arbitrary density dependence of the symmetry energy, and compare their treatment of Coulomb effects with the approach of Danielewicz (2003), finding that the latter overestimates Coulomb modifications to the total energy. The results enable tighter connections between nuclear experiment constraints on \(S_v\) and \(L\) and astrophysical observations of neutron star radii from photospheric radius expansion bursts and quiescent low-mass X-ray binaries.
