# On the formation of Hubble flow in Little Bangs

> A dynamical appearance of scaling solutions in the relativistic hydrodynamics applied to describe ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions is studied. We consider the boost-invariant cylindrically symmetric systems and the effects of the phase transition are taken into account by using a temperature dependent sound velocity inferred from the lattice simulations of QCD. We find that the transverse flow acquires the scaling form r/t within the short evolution times, 10 - 15 fm, only if the initial transverse flow originating from the pre-equilibrium collective behavior is present at the initial stage of the hydrodynamic evolution. The amount of such pre-equilibrium flow is correlated with the initial pressure gradient; larger gradients require smaller initial flow. The results of the numerical calculations support the phenomenological parameterizations used in the Blast-Wave, Buda-Lund, and Cracow models of the freeze-out process.

## Key claim

The transverse flow in boost-invariant, cylindrically symmetric relativistic heavy-ion collisions acquires the scaling form \(v_r = r/t\) within the short evolution times of 10-15 fm only if an initial transverse flow from pre-equilibrium collective behavior is present at the start of hydrodynamic evolution; the amount of such pre-equilibrium flow is correlated with the initial pressure gradient, with larger gradients requiring smaller initial flow.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic hydrodynamic equations for boost-invariant, cylindrically symmetric systems with zero net baryon density, using the characteristic method of Baym, Friman, Blaizot, Soyeur, and Czyz (1983). They incorporated a temperature-dependent sound velocity inferred from lattice QCD simulations (the "lattice I" compilation of Mahonty and Alam (2003) based on Karsch (2001)) and also used an analytic model for \(c_s(T)\). Initial conditions were specified at a constant time \(t_0 = 1\) fm, with the initial temperature profile derived from the nuclear thickness function and the initial transverse flow parameterized by a Hubble constant \(H\) in the formula \(v_r^0 = H r / \sqrt{1 + H^2 r^2}\).

## Key numbers

- The scaling form \(v_r = r/t\) is approached after about 7 fm when the initial pre-equilibrium flow parameter is \(H = 0.25\) fm\(^{-1}\) and the initial central temperature is \(T_0 = 2 T_c\) (with \(T_c \approx 170\) MeV, giving \(T_0 \approx 340\) MeV).
- For the case with no initial transverse flow (\(H = 0\)) and \(T_0 = 2 T_c\), the calculated velocity profiles do not agree with the ideal Hubble-like profiles \(r/t_i\) at evolution times up to 16 fm.
- The initial central temperature values studied are \(T_0 = 1.5 T_c\), \(2 T_c\), and \(3 T_c\).
- The initial time for hydrodynamic evolution is set to \(t_0 = 1\) fm.
- The Hubble law \(v = H r\) is satisfied in the region \(r \ll t\) after \(t = 7\) fm in all numerically explored cases, regardless of initial conditions, but the Hubble constant typically deviates from the inverse of time.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Baym, Friman, Blaizot, Soyeur, and Czyz (1983), who solved the hydrodynamic equations numerically only for a constant sound velocity squared of \(1/3\), by incorporating a realistic temperature-dependent sound velocity from lattice QCD. The results support the phenomenological parameterizations used in the Blast-Wave, Buda-Lund, and Cracow models of the freeze-out process, and are consistent with the earlier work of Kolb and Rapp (2002) which found that including pre-equilibrium transverse flow improves agreement with data. The paper enables more realistic modeling of the short evolution times (about 10-15 fm) inferred from RHIC data, showing that the scaling Hubble flow can develop dynamically within those times only if pre-equilibrium transverse flow is present.
