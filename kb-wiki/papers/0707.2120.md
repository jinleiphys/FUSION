# Dineutron structure in $^{8}$He

> The ground and excited states of $^{8}$He were investigated with a method of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics(AMD). We adopted effective nuclear interactions which systematically reproduce the binding energies of $^4$He, $^6$He and $^8$He. The ground state of $^8$He has both the $j$-$j$ coupling feature($p_{3/2}$ closure) and the $L$-$S$ coupling feature($^4$He$+2n+2n$) with a slight tail of dineutron at the long distance region. The theoretical results give an indication of the $0^+_2$ state with dineutron gas-like structure. The dineutron structure, $^4$He+$2n$+$2n$, of this state is similar to the $3α$-cluster structure of the $^{12}$C($0^+_2$) state which has been interpreted as an $α$ condensate state. Since the $^8$He($0^+_2$) state has a significant overlap with the dineutron condensate wave function where two dineutrons are moving in $S$ wave around the $α$ core with a dilute density, we suggest that this theoretically predicted $0^+_2$ state is a candidate of the dineutron condensate state.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that the theoretically predicted $0^+_2$ state of $^8$He is a candidate for a dineutron condensate state, analogous to the $\alpha$-condensate interpretation of the $^{12}$C($0^+_2$) state, based on AMD+GCM calculations that show this state has significant overlap with a dineutron condensate wave function where two dineutrons move in $S$ wave around the $\alpha$ core with dilute density.

## Method

The authors applied antisymmetrized molecular dynamics combined with the generator coordinate method (AMD+GCM) to investigate the ground and excited states of $^8$He, using effective nuclear interactions (Volkov No.2 and MV1 central forces with G3RS spin-orbit force) tuned to reproduce subsystem energies of $^4$He, $^6$He and $^8$He. They performed energy variation with constraints on total oscillator quanta and deformation, then superposed spin-parity projected AMD wave functions to obtain the $^8$He states, analyzing the dineutron structure by extracting reduced width amplitudes for $^6$He$^{SU(3)}(0^+)$-$^2n$ cluster motion.

## Key numbers

- The $0^+_2$ state is predicted at excitation energy $E_x = 10.3$ MeV (v58 interaction) and $E_x = 8.5$ MeV (m56 interaction), just above the $2^+_1$ state at $E_x = 9.3$ MeV (v58) and $E_x = 6.5$ MeV (m56)
- The neutron radius of the $0^+_2$ state is $r_n = 2.94$ fm (v58) and $r_n = 3.12$ fm (m56), compared to the ground state $r_n = 2.37$ fm (v58) and $r_n = 2.63$ fm (m56)
- The monopole neutron transition matrix element from ground state to $0^+_2$ state is $M_n(0^+_1\rightarrow 0^+_2)=13.5$ fm$^2$ (m56) and $13.9$ fm$^2$ (v58), more than factor 2 larger than the observed proton matrix element $M_n(0^+_1\rightarrow 0^+_2)=5.4$ fm$^2$ of $^{12}$C
- The $^2n$-cluster reduced width amplitude for $^8$He($0^+_2$) peaks at $r=4-6$ fm, similar to the $\alpha$-cluster motion peak in $^{12}$C($0^+_2$)
- The $S=0$ component in $^6$He($0^+_1$) is 0.87 (m56), in good agreement with three-body model calculations

## Context

This work extends the concept of cluster condensation, previously proposed for $\alpha$ particles in $^{12}$C by Tohsaki (2001) and suggested in dilute nuclear matter by Ropke (1998), to dineutron condensation in neutron-rich nuclei. The theoretical prediction of a $0^+_2$ state in $^8$He with dineutron gas-like structure builds on earlier AMD studies of He isotopes by Itagaki (2000) and Aoyama (2006), and on three-body model calculations of $^6$He by Bertsch (1991), Zhukov (1993), and Aoyama (2001). The results enable experimental searches for the $0^+_2$ state via inelastic scattering, exploiting its large monopole neutron transition strength, and motivate further theoretical investigation of dineutron condensate states in other neutron-rich nuclei.
