# A Statistical Analysis of Hadron Spectrum: Quantum Chaos in Hadrons

> The nearest-neighbor mass-spacing distribution of the meson and baryon spectrum (up to 2.5 GeV) is described by the Wigner surmise corresponding to the statistics of the Gaussian orthogonal ensemble of random matrix theory. This can be viewed as a manifestation of quantum chaos in hadrons.

## Key claim

The nearest-neighbor mass-spacing distribution of the meson and baryon spectrum (up to 2.5 GeV) is described by the Wigner surmise corresponding to the statistics of the Gaussian orthogonal ensemble of random matrix theory, which can be viewed as a manifestation of quantum chaos in hadrons. The claim is hedged by the low statistics of the available hadron data, with the analysis relying on moments of the distribution rather than direct histogram fits to overcome this limitation.

## Method

The author collected experimentally measured masses of mesons and baryons up to 2.5 GeV from the Particle Data Group Summary Tables, excluding poorly known one-star baryons. States were organized into multiplets with definite quantum numbers (isospin, spin, parity, strangeness, baryon number, and charge conjugation for mesons), and the nearest-neighbor mass spacings within each multiplet were computed. The spacings were normalized by the mean spacing for each spectrum (baryon, meson, or combined hadron), and the first ten moments of the resulting distributions were compared to the moments of the Wigner surmise for the Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (GOE) with Dyson index beta = 1.

## Key numbers

- Mean mass spacing for baryons: 289.8 +/- 18.8 MeV
- Mean mass spacing for mesons: 284.3 +/- 35.8 MeV
- Mean mass spacing for combined hadron spectrum: 287.1 +/- 27.3 MeV
- Chi-squared per point for fit of beta = 1 Wigner surmise to first ten moments of baryon spacing distribution (no unfolding): 0.52
- Chi-squared per point for fit of beta = 1 Wigner surmise to first ten moments of meson spacing distribution (no unfolding): 0.66
- Chi-squared per point for fit of beta = 1 Wigner surmise to first ten moments of combined hadron spacing distribution (no unfolding): 0.57
- Chi-squared per point for combined hadron distribution after unfolding by baryon vs meson: 0.54
- Chi-squared per point for combined hadron distribution after unfolding by strangeness (S=0 vs S=-1): 0.33

## Context

This work extends the application of random matrix theory and the Wigner-Dyson statistical analysis, previously successful for complex nuclear spectra (Wigner, 1950s; Dyson, 1960s; Brody, 1981) and lattice gauge theories (Verbaarschot, 1995, 2000; Berg, 2000; Bittner, 2001), to the experimentally measured hadron spectrum. The results provide empirical support for theoretical studies of quantum chaos in lattice QCD and quantum-mechanical Yang-Mills models, and suggest that hadron-exchange models could benefit from random matrix theory methods analogous to the stochastic theory of compound-nucleus reactions. The work enables future studies of hadron width distributions (anticipated to follow the Porter-Thomas distribution) and investigations of how quantum chaos emerges from quark-gluon dynamics in lattice QCD or quark-model frameworks.
