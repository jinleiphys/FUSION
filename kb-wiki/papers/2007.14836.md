# Applications of the chiral potential with the semi-local regularization in momentum space to the disintegration processes

> We apply the chiral potential with the momentum space semi-local regularization to the $^2$H and $^3$He photodisintegration processes and to the (anti)neutrino induced deuteron breakup reactions. Specifically, the differential cross section, the photon analyzing power and the final proton polarization have been calculated for the deuteron photodisintegration at the photon energies 30 MeV and 100 MeV. For the $^3$He photodisintegration predictions for the semi-inclusive and exclusive differential cross sections are presented for the photon energies up to 120 MeV. The total cross section is calculated for the (anti)neutrino disintegrations of the deuteron for the (anti)neutrino energies below 200 MeV. The predictions based on the Argonne V18 potential or on the older chiral force with regularization applied in coordinate space are used for comparison. Using the fifth order chiral nucleon-nucleon potential supplemented with dominant contributions from the sixth order allows us to obtain converged predictions for the regarded reactions and observables. Our results based on the newest semi-local chiral potentials show even smaller cutoff dependence for the considered electroweak observables than the previously reported ones with a coordinate-space regulator. However, some of the studied polarization observables in the deuteron photodisintegration process reveal more sensitivity to the regulator value than the unpolarized cross section. The chiral potential regularized semi-locally in momentum space yields also fast convergence of results with the chiral order. These features make the used potential a high quality tool to study electroweak processes.

## Key claim

The semi-local momentum-space (SMS) regularized chiral potential yields even smaller cutoff dependence for electroweak observables in deuteron and helium-3 disintegration than the older coordinate-space regularized chiral force, though some polarization observables in deuteron photodisintegration show more sensitivity to the regulator value than the unpolarized cross section.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrodinger and Lippmann-Schwinger equations for two-nucleon reactions and the Faddeev formalism for three-nucleon reactions, using the SMS chiral potential up to fifth order (N4LO) with some sixth-order contributions (N4LO+). For electromagnetic reactions, the single-nucleon current was supplemented implicitly via the Siegert theorem; for weak reactions, only the single-nucleon current was used. Calculations were performed in momentum space with partial wave decomposition up to total angular momentum j=4 for two-nucleon systems and J=15/2 for three-nucleon systems.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross section for deuteron photodisintegration at E_gamma = 30 MeV: difference between N2LO and N3LO predictions is approximately 0.06% at the cross section maximum
- Differential cross section for deuteron photodisintegration at E_gamma = 100 MeV: difference between N2LO and higher orders remains below 3%
- Truncation error band width at N4LO+ for deuteron photodisintegration: approximately 0.1% of cross section magnitude at E_gamma = 30 MeV and 2.5% at E_gamma = 100 MeV
- Difference between N4LO SMS and SCS cross sections: 1.07 microbarn per steradian (approximately 3%) at theta_p = 79 degrees for E_gamma = 30 MeV; 0.467 microbarn per steradian (approximately 8%) at theta_p = 52 degrees for E_gamma = 100 MeV
- Photon analyzing power A_X at E_gamma = 100 MeV: cutoff dependence up to 14% at theta_p = 72 degrees
- Proton polarization P_y at E_gamma = 100 MeV: relative difference between regulator values less than 5% at theta_p = 131 degrees
- For neutrino-induced deuteron breakup at E_nu = 100 MeV: relative difference between N4LO and N4LO+ total cross sections is 0.093% for nu_e + 2H -> nu_e + p + n, 0.092% for antineutrino NC, and 0.029% for antineutrino CC; cutoff dependence at N4LO is 0.96%, 0.98%, and 0.90% respectively

## Context

This work extends applications of the SMS chiral potential (Epelbaum et al., 2018) beyond purely strong processes to electroweak reactions, following earlier studies of nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering and nucleon-induced deuteron breakup (Epelbaum, 2020; Volkotrub, 2020). It directly compares with predictions from the older semi-local coordinate-space (SCS) regularized chiral potential (Epelbaum, 2014) and the Argonne V18 potential (Wiringa, 1995). The results show improved cutoff stability over previous chiral force applications to electroweak processes (Skibinski, 2006, 2014; Rozpedzik, 2011), though full consistent two-body currents remain under development (Krebs, 2019, 2020) and will be needed for complete predictions.
