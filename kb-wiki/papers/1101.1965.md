# Analysis Tools for Discovering Strong Parity Violation at Hadron Colliders

> Several arguments suggest parity violation may be observable in high energy strong interactions. We introduce new analysis tools for describing the azimuthal dependence of multi-particle distributions, or "azimuthal flow." Analysis uses the representations of the orthogonal group O(2) and dihedral groups $D_{N}$ necessary to define parity correctly in two dimensions. Classification finds that collective angles used in event-by-event statistics represent inequivalent tensor observables that cannot generally be represented by a single "reaction plane". Many new parity-violating observables exist that have never been measured, while many new parity-conserving observables formerly lumped together are now distinguished. We use the concept of "event shape sorting" to suggest separating right- and left-handed events, and we discuss the effects of transverse and longitudinal spin. The analysis tools are statistically robust, and can be applied equally to low or high multiplicity events at the Tevatron, $RHIC$ or $RHIC\, Spin$, and the $LHC$.

## Key claim

Parity violation in high-energy strong interactions may be observable, but the paper argues that previous analyses have been embedded in an overly restrictive model (the "standard event plane formalism") that unknowingly made parity-violating observables much harder to construct; the authors introduce a model-independent framework using group theory to define proper two-dimensional parity tests, showing that many new parity-violating observables exist that have never been measured.

## Method

The authors classify azimuthal observables using representations of the orthogonal group O(2) and dihedral groups D_N to define parity correctly in two dimensions, showing that collective angles used in event-by-event statistics represent inequivalent tensor observables that cannot be represented by a single reaction plane. They develop event-by-event statistics in terms of conditional probabilities and propose "event shape sorting" to separate right- and left-handed events, demonstrating the procedure with a Monte Carlo simulation of 5000 events with 4-mode distributions.

## Key numbers

- The L_6 Lagrangian operator involves a mass scale M estimated in the TeV range; in the regime of small momentum transfers of O(MeV), the ratio (MeV/TeV)^2 ~ 10^{-12} would suppress its effects
- The Monte Carlo simulation used 5000 events with 4-mode distributions (modes m=0...3), generating 3787 Type-Plus events and 3826 Type-Minus events based on second-moment sorting criteria
- The dihedral group D_N has 2N elements (R_Nk, P_Nk) for k=0...N-1, with det(R_Nk)=1 and det(P_Nk)=-1

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of strong parity violation in QCD, building on earlier suggestions by Morley and Schmidt (1983) and recent activity by Halperin (1998), Kharzeev (1998, 2004, 2007), and others. The paper directly addresses the STAR experiment's 2010 observation of large effects in multi-pion azimuthal flow, noting that STAR measured a parity-conserving statistic despite claims of sensitivity to charge separation predicted by strong CP violation. The analysis tools can be applied at the Tevatron, RHIC, RHIC Spin, and the LHC, and enable direct model-independent tests of parity symmetry rather than relying on indirect deviations from Monte Carlo simulations.
