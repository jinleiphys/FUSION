# Scale and Scheme Independence and Position-Momentum Equivalence of Nuclear Short-Range Correlations

> Ab-initio Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) calculations of nuclei from deuterium to 40Ca, obtained using four different phenomenological and local chiral nuclear potentials, are analyzed using the Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF). We extract spin- and isospin-dependent "nuclear contact terms" for each interaction in both coordinate and momentum space. The extracted contact terms, that count the number of short-range correlated (SRC) pairs with different quantum numbers, are dependent on the nuclear interaction model used in the QMC calculation. However, the ratios of contact terms for a nucleus A to deuterium (for spin-1 pn pairs) or to 4He (for all NN pairs) are independent of the nuclear interaction model and are the same for both short-distance and high-momentum pairs. This implies that the relative abundance of short-range pairs in the nucleus is a long-range (mean-field) quantity that is insensitive to the short-distance nature of the nuclear force. Measurements of exclusive (e,e'NN) pair breakup processes are instead more sensitive to short-range dynamics

## Key claim

The ratios of nuclear contact terms for a nucleus A to deuterium (for spin-1 pn pairs) or to 4He (for all NN pairs) are independent of the nuclear interaction model and are the same for both short-distance and high-momentum pairs, implying that the relative abundance of short-range pairs in the nucleus is a long-range (mean-field) quantity insensitive to the short-distance nature of the nuclear force.

## Method

The authors used the Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF) to analyze ab-initio Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) calculations of two-nucleon densities in nuclei from deuterium to 40Ca. The QMC calculations employed four different nuclear potentials: the phenomenological Argonne V18 (AV18) plus Urbana X (UX), its tensor-less reduction AV4' plus UIX_c, and two local chiral effective field theory interactions at N2LO with coordinate-space cutoffs of 1.0 fm and 1.2 fm. Spin- and isospin-dependent nuclear contact terms were extracted from both coordinate-space and momentum-space two-nucleon distributions for each interaction.

## Key numbers

- Contact term ratios for spin-1 pn pairs in various nuclei relative to deuterium were extracted and found consistent across all four interaction models within uncertainties
- Contact term ratios for all NN pairs relative to 4He were extracted and found consistent across all four interaction models within uncertainties
- The scaling onset for pn momentum distributions occurs at 3.5-4 fm^-1 for phenomenological potentials and at 2-2.5 fm^-1 for chiral interactions
- The normalization of universal two-body functions |phi_NN^alpha(q)|^2 was fixed such that its integral above 1.3 fm^-1 (approximately k_F = 250 MeV/c) equals unity
- The pp-to-pn ratio in 4He calculated with AV4'+UIX_c is inconsistent with calculations using other interactions and with experimental data from Ref. korover14

## Context

This work extends previous GCF studies by Weiss (2016) that validated the factorization using QMC calculations with AV18+UX, and by Lynn (2019) that showed evidence for scale-and-scheme independence of rho_A(r)/rho_d(r) ratios for short distances. The results contradict claims by Arrington (2012, 2019) that there exists a difference between scaling of SRC pairs with small separation and high relative momenta versus pairs with small separation but any relative momenta. The findings enable using soft interactions to calculate contact ratios for heavy nuclei, then multiplying by hard-interaction contacts for light nuclei to obtain absolute contacts for heavy nuclei.
