# Interaction of real and virtual $p\bar{p}$ pairs in $J/ψ\to p\bar{p}γ(ρ,ω)$ decays

> The $p\bar{p}$ invariant mass spectra of the processes $J/ψ\to p\bar{p}ω$, $J/ψ\to p\bar{p}ρ$, and $J/ψ\to p\bar{p}γ$ close to the $p\bar{p}$ threshold are calculated by means of the $N\bar{N}$ optical potential. The potential model for $N\bar{N}$ interaction in the $^{1}S_{0}$ state is proposed. The parameters of the model are obtained by fitting the cross section of $N\bar{N}$ scattering together with the $p\bar{p}$ invariant mass spectra of the $J/ψ$ decays. Good agreement with the available experimental data is achieved. Using our potential and the Green's function approach we also describe the peak in the $η'π^{+}π^{-}$ invariant mass spectrum in the decay $J/ψ\toγη'π^{+}π^{-}$ in the energy region near the $N\bar{N}$ threshold.

## Key claim

The near-threshold peak in the \(p\bar{p}\) invariant mass spectrum of \(J/\psi\to p\bar{p}\gamma\) arises from a significant compensation of the two isospin amplitudes above 10 MeV per nucleon, rather than from a peak in either isospin channel individually; the model predicts no such peak in \(J/\psi\to p\bar{p}\rho\), contrary to earlier chiral model results.

## Method

The authors constructed an optical \(N\bar{N}\) potential for the \(^{1}S_{0}\) partial wave, consisting of a short-range complex potential well plus a long-range pion-exchange tail, with parameters fitted simultaneously to Nijmegen partial-wave \(p\bar{p}\) scattering data and to the \(p\bar{p}\) invariant mass spectra of \(J/\psi\to p\bar{p}\omega\), \(J/\psi\to p\bar{p}\gamma\), and \(\psi(2S)\to p\bar{p}\gamma\). The decay amplitudes were expressed in terms of the regular wave function at the origin \(\psi_R^I(0)\) obtained from the Schrödinger equation, and the contribution of virtual \(N\bar{N}\) pairs was computed via the Green's function of the same equation.

## Key numbers

* The short-range potential parameters for the isoscalar channel: \(U_0 = -28 \pm 4\) MeV, \(W_0 = 76 \pm 5\) MeV, \(a_0 = 1.16 \pm 0.02\) fm.
* The short-range potential parameters for the isovector channel: \(U_1 = 17.2^{+1}_{-1.1}\) MeV, \(W_1 = -7.6^{+0.8}_{-0.9}\) MeV, \(a_1 = 1.44 \pm 0.04\) fm.
* The fit used 143 data points and 11 free parameters, yielding \(\chi^2_{\mathrm{min}}/N_{\mathrm{df}} = 151/132\).
* The ratio of coupling constants for \(J/\psi\to p\bar{p}\gamma\): \(\mathcal{G}_{\gamma1}/\mathcal{G}_{\gamma0} = -0.57 - 0.3\,i\).
* The ratio of coupling constants for \(\psi(2S)\to p\bar{p}\gamma\): \(\mathcal{G}_{\gamma1}/\mathcal{G}_{\gamma0} = -1.21 - 0.05\,i\).
* The quasi-bound state in the isoscalar channel has energy \(E_B = (11 - 20\,i)\) MeV; turning off the imaginary part moves it to \(E_B = -3.4\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous optical potential model for the \(^{3}S_{1}-{}^{3}D_{1}\) partial waves (Dmitriev, 2016) to the \(^{1}S_{0}\) channel, enabling description of decays where the \(p\bar{p}\) pair has spin zero. It contradicts the chiral model results of Kang (2015) which predicted a pronounced peak in \(J/\psi\to p\bar{p}\rho\) near threshold. The Green's function approach allows the model to describe the peak in \(J/\psi\to\gamma\eta'\pi^{+}\pi^{-}\) observed by Ablikim (2016), which had not been previously modeled with an explicit \(N\bar{N}\) interaction.
