# Delta isobars in relativistic mean-field models with $σ$-scaled hadron masses and couplings

> We extend the relativistic mean-field models with hadron masses and meson-baryon coupling constants dependent on the scalar $σ$ field, studied previously to incorporate $Δ(1232)$ baryons. Available empirical information is analyzed to put constraints on the couplings of $Δ$s with meson fields. Conditions for the appearance of $Δ$s are studied. We demonstrate that with inclusion of the $Δ$s our equations of state continue to fulfill majority of known empirical constraints including the pressure-density constraint from heavy-ion collisions, the constraint on the maximum mass of the neutron stars, the direct Urca and the gravitational-baryon mass ratio constraints.

# Delta isobars in relativistic mean-field models with σ-scaled hadron masses and couplings

## Key claim

The authors extend two previously developed relativistic mean-field models (KVORcut03 and MKVOR) to include Δ(1232) isobars and demonstrate that the resulting equations of state can still satisfy the majority of known empirical constraints, including the maximum neutron star mass constraint, provided the Δ potential at saturation density is not too attractive (around -50 MeV). However, in the MKVOR model with Δ baryons, the nucleon effective mass in isospin-symmetric matter drops to zero at densities around (4-6)n₀ if a Δ potential of -(50-100) MeV is used, requiring a modification of the model (MKVOR*) to maintain a valid hadronic description.

## Method

The authors incorporate Δ isobars into relativistic mean-field models where hadron masses and meson-baryon coupling constants scale with the scalar σ field, using the same formalism previously applied to nucleons and hyperons. They analyze empirical information from electron-nucleus scattering, pion-nucleus scattering, photoabsorption, and heavy-ion collision data to constrain the Δ-meson coupling constants, particularly the Δ potential U_Δ(n₀) at saturation density. The equations of state are constructed for cold infinite baryonic matter in beta equilibrium, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation is solved to obtain neutron star configurations.

## Key numbers

- The bare mass of the Δ isobar is m_Δ = 1232 MeV.
- The Δ potential at saturation density is estimated as U_Δ(n₀) = -50 MeV as the most realistic value, with a tested range from -150 MeV to -50 MeV.
- For the KVORcut03 model, the variation of U_Δ(n₀) in the interval -150 MeV to -50 MeV corresponds to x_σΔ values from 1.49 down to 0.94 (for x_ωΔ = 1).
- For the MKVOR model, the same U_Δ variation corresponds to x_σΔ values from 1.34 down to 0.94.
- The pion-baryon sigma-term values used are Σ_πN = 43.3 ± 4.4 MeV and Σ_πΔ = 32 ± 3 MeV, yielding x_σΔ in the range 0.61 to 0.90.
- The maximum neutron star mass from the KVOR model without Δs or hyperons is M_max[KVOR] = 2.01 M_⊙.
- The observed neutron star masses used as constraints are PSR J1614-2230 at 1.97 ± 0.04 M_⊙ and PSR J0348+0432 at 2.01 ± 0.04 M_⊙.

## Context

This work extends the KVORcut03 and MKVOR models previously developed by Maslov et al. (2015) to include Δ isobars, following the same approach used to address the "hyperon puzzle" in neutron star interiors. The study addresses the "Δ puzzle" formulated by Drago et al. (2014, 2015), which suggests that Δ baryons could soften the equation of state similarly to hyperons. The results enable future work on neutron star structure and composition with multiple baryon species, and the authors note that the MKVOR model with Δs requires modification (MKVOR*) because the nucleon effective mass drops to zero at densities of (4-6)n₀ for realistic Δ potentials.
