# Strong and electromagnetic interaction views on deuteron structure

> The purpose of this contribution is to give an outlook of recent results connected with deuteron physics, with electromagnetic and strong interacting probes at intermediate energy. Special attention will be devoted to polarization observables.

# Strong and electromagnetic interaction views on deuteron structure

## Key claim

Despite high-precision data on elastic electron-deuteron scattering up to momentum transfer Q^2 ≈ 6 (GeV/c)^2, the transition region between the nucleon-meson picture and the quark-gluon description of the deuteron has not yet been reached; the tensor polarization data t_20 clearly show that the perturbative QCD limit is not yet attained, and the data follow the trend suggested by the impulse approximation.

## Method

The paper reviews elastic electron-deuteron scattering data from Jefferson Laboratory (cross sections up to Q^2 ≈ 6 (GeV/c)^2 and t_20 up to Q^2 = 1.9 (GeV/c)^2) and analyzes them for evidence of two-photon exchange contributions by comparing Rosenbluth-plot deviations at fixed Q. It also examines proton-deuteron backward elastic scattering and deuteron break-up data from Saturne and Dubna, extracting tensor analyzing power T_20 and polarization transfer κ_0, and compares them to impulse approximation predictions. The authors further extract the isoscalar nucleon electric form factor from deuteron data and use an ω-exchange model to describe T_20 in the reaction d + p → d + X, incorporating four overlapping nucleon resonances.

## Key numbers

- Elastic ed cross section measured up to Q^2 ≈ 6 (GeV/c)^2 at Jefferson Laboratory
- t_20 measured up to Q^2 = 1.9 (GeV/c)^2 at Jefferson Laboratory
- Cross sections appear to scale as (Q^2)^{-10}, as predicted by pQCD
- Two-photon exchange effects may become observable at Q = 8-12 fm^{-1}
- Electric neutron form factor G_En extracted up to Q^2 = 0.7 (GeV/c)^2 from earlier data (Platchkov 1990)
- New proton electric form factor data from Jones (2000) extend up to Q^2 = 3.5 (GeV/c)^2
- Direct measurement of G_En at Q^2 = 0.67 (GeV/c)^2 gives G_En = 0.052 ± 0.011 ± 0.005 (Rohe 1999)
- T_20 data for d + p → d + X show scaling as a function of t with a minimum near |t| ≈ 0.35 GeV^2, for incident momenta 3.7-9 GeV/c
- Starting from Q^2 ≈ 2 (GeV/c)^2, the extracted G_En becomes larger than G_Ep

## Context

This review extends the long-standing program of deuteron structure studies using both electromagnetic and hadronic probes at intermediate energies, building on the early high-Q^2 ed scattering work of Arnold (1975) and the neutron form factor extraction of Platchkov (1990). The paper shows that the new Jefferson Laboratory data, while reaching higher momentum transfer, have not resolved the long-standing questions about the role of meson exchange currents, isobar configurations, and the transition to quark-gluon degrees of freedom. The analysis enables future work on two-photon exchange effects, improved extraction of the neutron electric form factor, and the use of isoscalar probes (deuterons and alpha particles) to study nucleon resonance structure, particularly the Roper resonance.
