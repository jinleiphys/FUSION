# Effect of first forbidden decays on the shape of neutrino spectra

> We examine the effect of First Forbidden (FF) decays on $β$-decay neutrino spectra by performing microscopic nuclear structure calculations. By analyzing the FF decay branches of even-even nuclei we conclude that FF decays may be responsible for part of the missing neutrinos in the so called "Reactor Neutrino Anomaly". Further calculations and more experimental data are needed for a firm conclusion.

# Effect of first forbidden decays on the shape of neutrino spectra

## Key claim

First Forbidden (FF) beta decays may account for part of the missing neutrino flux in the Reactor Neutrino Anomaly, but the correction is likely smaller than previously estimated, with an average effect of only 1-2% rather than the larger values suggested by simplified treatments such as Huber (2011) and Hayes et al. (2013). A firm conclusion requires further calculations and more experimental data.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic nuclear structure calculations using two complementary methods: the shell model (SM) with the NuShellX@MSU code in the jj56 model space, and the proton-neutron Quasi-Particle Random Phase Approximation (pn-QRPA) with a Hamiltonian based on the CD-Bonn interaction. They focused on the even-even fission products 136Te and 140Xe in the Xe region, computing the neutrino spectra for individual FF decay branches with spin-parity changes of 0-, 1-, and 2-, and comparing the full microscopic results to the simplified matrix-element approximations used by Hayes et al. (2013).

## Key numbers

- The observed reactor anti-neutrino flux at distances less than 100 m accounts for only 0.946 ± 0.023 of the theoretical expectation (the Reactor Neutrino Anomaly).
- For 1- FF decays, the change in the number of neutrinos above the inverse beta-decay threshold (Δ) reaches up to 10% for individual branches, with Δ = 5-15% for end-point energies of 4-6 MeV.
- For 2- FF decays, Δ is only 2-3% for end-point energies of 4-6 MeV.
- For 0- FF decays, the change is negligible (Δ near zero) except below end-point energies of 3 MeV.
- In the most extreme case where FF decays are dominated by ΔJπ = 1- transitions, the overall change to the neutrino spectrum could be as large as Δ = 4.5%.
- Averaging over all types of FF transitions, end-point energies, and logft values gives a smaller correction of Δ = 1-2%.
- The finite size effects and weak magnetism corrections for allowed Gamow-Teller decays from Huber (2011) are estimated to be Δ = 2-3%.
- The logft values from shell model and QRPA calculations agree with experimental values to within 0.2 (a factor of 1.5 in transition rates).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Reactor Neutrino Anomaly, first identified by Mention et al. (2011) and Mueller et al. (2011), by including realistic First Forbidden decay contributions that were omitted in the earlier correction study by Huber (2011). It improves upon the simplified treatment of FF decays by Hayes et al. (2013), which used only 4 of the 9 relevant matrix elements and consequently gave qualitatively different results for 1- transitions. The calculations enable more accurate predictions of reactor anti-neutrino spectra, which are essential for interpreting sterile neutrino search experiments.
