# Oblate-prolate shape coexistence at finite angular momentum

> We investigate shape coexistence in a rotating nucleus. We concentrate on the interesting case of 72-Kr which exhibits an interesting interplay between prolate and oblate states as a function of angular momentum. The calculation uses the local harmonic version of the method of self-consistent adiabatic large-amplitude collective motion. We find that the collective behaviour of the system changes with angular momentum and we focus on the role of non-axial shapes.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the self-consistent collective path for shape coexistence in $^{72}$Kr changes qualitatively with angular momentum: at zero spin the path goes through a spherical saddle point, while at finite angular momentum the oblate and prolate minima are connected via non-axial (triaxial) states, and at $I=4$ the collective dynamics essentially disappears due to pairing collapse.

## Method

The authors use the local harmonic approximation (LHA) of self-consistent adiabatic large-amplitude collective motion, applied to a constrained pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian treated in the Hartree-Bogoliubov approximation. The collective coordinate is determined by solving the force equations and local RPA equations self-consistently, with Lagrange multipliers for fixed angular momentum and particle number. The collective Hamiltonian is then quantized and solved to obtain wave functions and excitation energies for $I=0$, $2$, and $4$.

## Key numbers

- The first excited $I=0$ state (prolate band-head) lies at an excitation energy of **0.37 MeV** above the oblate ground state.
- The third $I=0$ state (approximately spherical) lies at **0.96 MeV**.
- For $I=2$, the first excited state is at **0.32 MeV**, the second at **0.61 MeV**, and the third at **1.33 MeV**.
- For $I=4$, the first excited state is at **1.04 MeV** and the second at **1.07 MeV**.
- At $I=4$, the points of zero neutron or proton pairing have an excitation energy of less than **150 keV**.
- The ground state ($0_1$) has deformation $\epsilon_2 = 0.28$ and $\gamma = 60.0^\circ$; the first excited $0_2$ state has $\epsilon_2 = 0.37$ and $\gamma = 0.0^\circ$.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent collective path method of Walet and collaborators (2004) to include constraints on angular momentum, building on earlier constrained mean-field calculations for $^{72}$Kr by Yamagami and Matsuyanagi (2001) and beyond-mean-field studies by Petrovici and Schmid (2000). The results confirm the importance of pairing-collapsed states for the collective path, as suggested in earlier work by Almehed and Walet (2004). The calculations support experimental data from Bouchez et al. (2003) showing oblate-prolate shape coexistence in $^{72}$Kr, though the authors find the prolate band-head lower in energy than experiment suggests. The work enables future studies with multiple collective coordinates, which the authors indicate they intend to pursue.
