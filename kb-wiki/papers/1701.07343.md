# Quark Deconfinement in Rotating Neutron Stars

> In this paper, we use a three flavor non-local Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, an~improved effective model of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) at low energies, to investigate the existence of deconfined quarks in the cores of neutron stars. Particular emphasis is put on the possible existence of quark matter in the cores of rotating neutron stars (pulsars). In contrast to non-rotating neutron stars, whose particle compositions do not change with time (are frozen in), the type and structure of the matter in the cores of rotating neutron stars depends on the spin frequencies of these stars, which opens up a possible new window on the nature of matter deep in the cores of neutron stars. Our study shows that, depending on mass and rotational frequency, up to around 8% of the mass of a massive neutron star may be in the mixed quark-hadron phase, if the phase transition is treated as a Gibbs transition. We also find that the gravitational mass at which quark deconfinement occurs in rotating neutron stars varies quadratically with spin frequency, which can be fitted by a simple formula.

## Key claim

Using a three-flavor non-local Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a Gibbs construction for the mixed phase, up to around 8% of the mass of a massive neutron star may be in the mixed quark-hadron phase, but this result depends on the star's mass and rotational frequency, and the phase transition is treated as a Gibbs transition rather than a Maxwell construction.

## Method

The authors modeled hadronic matter using the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approximation with two parameter sets (GM1 and DD2), and deconfined quark matter using a non-local extension of the SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (n3NJL) model with Gaussian form factors. The quark-hadron mixed phase was treated via the Gibbs condition for phase equilibrium, enforcing global baryon number and electric charge conservation. Rotating neutron star models were constructed in general relativity using the metric for rotating configurations, with the Kepler frequency as the absolute stability limit.

## Key numbers

- Up to around 8% of the mass of a massive neutron star may be in the mixed quark-hadron phase, depending on mass and rotational frequency.
- The maximum masses of non-rotating neutron stars for the equations of state used are between 1.9 and 2.1 solar masses.
- Stellar rotation increases the maximum masses to between 2.2 and 2.4 solar masses, while central densities drop by around 20%.
- The most rapidly rotating neutron star observed to date is pulsar J1748-2446ad, rotating at 716 Hz (rotational period of 1.4 milliseconds).
- The Kepler period for 1.4 solar mass neutron stars is typically around 1 ms.
- The vector coupling constant G_V was varied from 0 to 0.09 G_S to account for uncertainty.
- The current quark mass of up and down quarks was fitted to 6.2 MeV, with a cutoff Lambda = 706.0 MeV, G_S Lambda^2 = 15.04, and H Lambda^5 = -337.71; the strange quark mass was set to 140.7 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of deconfined quark matter in neutron stars using non-local NJL models, which had been carried out for the SU(2) version by Grigorian (2004) and Blaschke (2007). The study specifically addresses rotating neutron stars (pulsars), where the core composition can depend on spin frequency, unlike non-rotating stars whose composition is frozen in. The results enable future comparisons with observational data from missions like NICER, which is dedicated to studying neutron star interiors.
