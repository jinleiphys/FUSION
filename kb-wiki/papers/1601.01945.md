# The light-front vacuum

> We discuss the relation between the trivial light-front vacuum and the non-trivial Heisenberg vacuum.

## Key claim

The light-front vacuum is identical to the free Fock vacuum only when the test functions used to smear the fields vanish at \(p^+=0\); zero-mode contributions can appear in intermediate steps of constructing the mapping from the local Heisenberg algebra to the light-front Fock algebra, but they are not present in the final unitary representation of the dynamics.

## Method

The authors compare the canonical (equal-time) and light-front representations of free scalar fields, analyzing the relation between their annihilation operators and vacuum states. They then examine the light-front Fock algebra generated by fields restricted to the light-front hyperplane, showing that the mapping from the local Heisenberg algebra to a sub-algebra of the light-front Fock algebra preserves all Wightman functions and is unitary, provided the mapped test functions vanish at \(p^+=0\). The analysis is extended to interacting fields using the Haag expansion of Heisenberg fields in normal-ordered products of asymptotic free fields.

## Key numbers

- The light-front momentum component \(P^+ = P^0 + \hat{\mathbf{n}}\cdot \mathbf{P}\) is non-negative, with each single-particle \(p_n^+ \geq 0\).
- The kernel \(F_m(x,\tilde{\mathbf{y}})\) mapping the local Heisenberg algebra to the light-front Fock algebra is given by \(F_m(x,\tilde{\mathbf{y}}) = \frac{1}{(2\pi)^2} \int \frac{d\tilde{\mathbf{p}}}{2} e^{-i \frac{p_\perp^2 + m^2}{p^+} x^+ + i \tilde{\mathbf{p}} \cdot (\tilde{\mathbf{x}} - \tilde{\mathbf{y}})}\).
- The light-front scalar product is \((\tilde{f},\tilde{g}) = \int \frac{d\tilde{\mathbf{p}} \, \theta(p^+)}{p^+} \tilde{f}(-\tilde{\mathbf{p}}) \tilde{g}(\tilde{\mathbf{p}})\), which has a logarithmic singularity at \(p^+=0\) when test functions do not vanish there.
- The integral \(\int_0^a \frac{e^{i c/p^+}}{p^+} dp^+ = \int_{c/a}^\infty \frac{e^{iu}}{u} du = \frac{\pi}{2} - (\text{Ci}(c/a) + i \text{Si}(c/a))\) shows that the \(1/p^+\) singularity is regulated by oscillations when \(x^+ - y^+ \neq 0\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the light-front vacuum problem discussed by Dirac (1949), Chang (1971), and others, and addresses the apparent inconsistency between the trivial light-front vacuum and the non-trivial Heisenberg vacuum in canonical field theory. The authors build on the algebraic approach of Schlieder and Seiler (1975) and the work of Leutwyler (1974) and Coester (1992) on the light-front Fock algebra. The results enable a unitary mapping between the local Heisenberg algebra and a sub-algebra of the light-front Fock algebra, clarifying the role of zero modes in light-front quantization and providing a framework for constructing Poincare-invariant light-front quantum field theories.
