# Elucidating the sign problem through noise distributions

> Due to the presence of light pions in the theory, lattice QCD at finite densities suffers from issues with noise in both grand canonical and canonical formulations. We study two different formulations of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model reduced to 2+1 dimensions at large N, where N is the number of flavors. At finite chemical potential one formulation has a severe sign problem and a fermion correlator which displays a broad probability distribution with small mean. In the other we find no sign problem and a distribution amenable to the cumulant expansion techniques developed in earlier work.

## Key claim

The sign problem in lattice QCD at finite density is not inherent to fermions but arises from interactions that produce light pions; in a large-N NJL model in 2+1 dimensions, two physically equivalent formulations can exhibit either a severe sign problem (A/V formulation) or no sign problem (σ/π formulation with even N), depending on how the auxiliary fields are introduced.

## Method

The authors studied two formulations of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model reduced to 2+1 dimensions at large N (number of flavors). In the σ/π formulation, scalar and pseudoscalar auxiliary fields are introduced as singlets under the SU(2N) flavor symmetry, yielding a real fermion determinant for even N. In the A/V formulation, Fierz rearrangement leads to matrix-valued vector and axial vector auxiliary fields, producing a complex determinant analogous to QCD. They computed probability distributions for fermion correlators in both formulations using cumulant expansions derived from connected Feynman diagrams, analyzing the scaling of cumulants with N, Euclidean time T, and volume V.

## Key numbers

- The constituent fermion mass M in the chiral limit is given by M = f/2 [1 + sqrt(1 + 4m/f)], where f = ⟨σ⟩ is the chiral symmetry breaking minimum at m=0.
- For the A/V formulation, the variance κ₁,₁ near the chiral limit scales as κ₁,₁ ≃ (2π)/(m_π f V N) e^{-m_π T} at late Euclidean time T.
- Cumulants in the A/V formulation scale as κ_{m,n} ∼ e^{-(m-n)MT} e^{-n m_π T} / N^{m+n-1} for m ≥ n.
- In the σ/π formulation, cumulants for the logarithm of the fermion correlator are κ₁ = ln z - M T and κ_{n≥2} = -[(2(n-2))!/(n-2)!] (2M-f) T ζ^{n-1}, with ζ ≃ π / [N V (2M-f)²].

## Context

This work extends the analysis of noise problems in lattice QCD by Splittorff and Verbaarschot (2006, 2007), who linked the sign problem at finite chemical potential to pion condensation, and by Lepage (1989) and Savage (2010), who connected signal-to-noise ratios in baryon correlators to pion masses. It also builds on the cumulant expansion techniques developed by Lee (2011) and Endres (2011) for log-normal distributions in unitary fermion systems. The results suggest that introducing the pion as a fundamental field in QCD could provide a path toward solving the sign problem and enabling first-principles studies of dense matter.
