# Early deconfinement of asymptotically conformal color-superconducting quark matter in neutron stars

> We present a relativistic density functional approach to color superconducting quark matter that mimics quark confinement by a fast growth of the quasiparticle selfenergy in the confining region. The approach is shown to be equivalent to a chiral model of quark matter with medium dependent couplings. While the (pseudo)scalar sector of the model is fitted to the vacuum phenomenology of quantum chromodynamics, the strength of interaction in the vector and diquark channels is varied in order to provide the best agreement with the observational constraints on the mass-radius relation and tidal deformability of neutron stars modelled with our approach. In order to recover the conformal behavior of quark matter at asymptotically high densities we introduce a medium dependence of the vector and diquark couplings motivated by the nonperturbative gluon exchange. Our analysis signals that the onset of deconfinement to color superconducting quark matter is likely to occur in neutron stars with masses below 1.0 $M_\odot$.

## Key claim

The analysis signals that the onset of deconfinement to color-superconducting quark matter is likely to occur in neutron stars with masses below 1.0 solar masses, but only if the vector and diquark couplings are chosen to satisfy the observational constraint on the tidal deformability of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic density functional (RDF) approach for two-flavor quark matter that mimics confinement via a fast growth of the quasiparticle selfenergy, expanded to second order around mean-field solutions to yield an NJL-type model. They fit the scalar and pseudoscalar sector to vacuum QCD observables (pion mass, decay constant, scalar meson mass, chiral condensate), treat the vector and diquark couplings as free parameters, and introduce a density dependence of those couplings motivated by non-perturbative massive gluon exchange to recover conformal behavior at high densities. The quark matter equation of state is matched to the DD2npY-T hadronic equation of state via Maxwell construction for a first-order phase transition, and the resulting hybrid star sequences are compared to observational constraints on mass-radius relations and tidal deformability.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum effective quark mass is m*_0 = 718 MeV.
- The model parameters yield a pseudocritical temperature at zero baryon chemical potential of T_chi = 163 MeV.
- For constant couplings, the tidal deformability of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is Lambda_1.4 = 398 at (eta_V, eta_D) = (0.235, 0.540) and Lambda_1.4 = 326 at (eta_V, eta_D) = (0.265, 0.555).
- For density-dependent couplings with gluon mass M_g = 600 MeV, the tidal deformability is Lambda_1.4 = 475 at (eta_V, eta_D) = (0.330, 0.750) and Lambda_1.4 = 416 at (eta_V, eta_D) = (0.370, 0.770).
- The observational constraint on the tidal deformability is Lambda_1.4 = 190 (+390, -120) from the GW170817 event.
- The measured radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is R_1.4 = 11.7 (+0.86, -0.81) km (Dietrich 2020) and of a 2.0 solar mass neutron star is R_2.0 = 13.7 (+2.6, -1.5) km (Miller 2021).
- The non-perturbative gluon mass is estimated as M_g = 500-1000 MeV from the Shifman-Vainshtein-Zakharov expansion and M_g = 300-700 MeV from Schwinger-Dyson equations; the authors adopt M_g = 600 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the confining RDF approach of Ivanytskyi (2022) by introducing density-dependent vector and diquark couplings that recover the conformal limit at high densities, consistent with the perturbative QCD constraint from Komoltsev (2021). It addresses the "hyperon puzzle" by showing that an early onset of stiff quark matter below 1.0 solar masses can simultaneously satisfy the mass-radius constraints from Miller (2021) and the tidal deformability limit from the GW170817 event (LIGO Scientific Collaboration 2018), supporting the scenario advocated by Blaschke (2020) and Baym (2017). The approach enables systematic Bayesian analyses of hybrid star equations of state and predictions for the special point of intersection of hybrid star sequences.
