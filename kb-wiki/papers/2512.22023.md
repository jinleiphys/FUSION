# Fermionic domain-wall Skyrmions of QCD in a magnetic field

> The ground state of low-energy QCD matter in strong magnetic fields is either a chiral soliton lattice (CSL), a periodic array of neutral pion domain walls (chiral solitons) perpendicular to the magnetic field, or domain-wall Skyrmion phase, in which Skyrmions are induced on top of the CSL. Previously found domain-wall Skyrmions are bosons with the baryon number two. In this paper, we show that the minimum domain-wall Skyrmions are fermions with baryon number one; a bosonic domain-wall Skyrmion can be separated without energy cost into two fermionic domain-wall Skyrmions attached on the opposite sides of a chiral soliton. The phase boundary between the CSL and domain-wall Skyrmion phases is unchanged. In the chiral limit, the CSL reduces to a linearly dependent neutral pion on the direction of the magnetic field, while fermionic domain-wall Skyrmions sit in an equal distance of half a period.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the minimum domain-wall Skyrmions in QCD under a strong magnetic field are fermions with baryon number one, whereas previously found domain-wall Skyrmions were bosons with baryon number two; a bosonic domain-wall Skyrmion can be separated without energy cost into two fermionic domain-wall Skyrmions attached on opposite sides of a chiral soliton.

## Method

The authors construct an effective chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) Lagrangian with Wess-Zumino-Witten (WZW) term for two-flavor QCD in a background magnetic field, then apply the moduli approximation to derive an effective theory over half the period of the chiral soliton lattice (CSL). They integrate the baryon number density and WZW term over a half period to obtain a single-baryon effective theory, and evaluate the spin statistics using the Witten 5-dimensional embedding method. The separation energy between two fermionic domain-wall Skyrmions is analyzed by promoting the lump position modulus to a function of the spatial coordinate along the magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- The half-period effective theory yields a baryon number of exactly 1 per Skyrmion winding number, as shown by the integral \(\int_{0}^{\ell/2} dz\,\mathcal{B} = q(x,y)\) where \(\ell = 2\kappa K(\kappa)/m_\pi\) is the CSL period.
- The spin-statistics calculation gives \(\Gamma_5 = \pi \nu N_C\), confirming fermionic statistics for odd winding number \(\nu\) (with \(N_C = 3\) for QCD).
- The critical chemical potential for the domain-wall Skyrmion phase boundary is \(\mu_c = \pi \mathcal{C}(\kappa)\), unchanged from the full-period theory, where \(\mathcal{C}(\kappa) = \frac{16f_\pi^2}{3m_\pi}\frac{1}{\kappa^3}[(2-\kappa^2)E(\kappa)+2(\kappa^2-1)K(\kappa)]\).
- The separation energy between two fermionic domain-wall Skyrmions is zero for a step-function profile \(d(z) = D\theta(z) + d_0\), as \(d'(z) \tanh^2 m_\pi z \propto \delta(z) \tanh^2 m_\pi z = 0\).
- The ChPT validity bound is \(B_\text{max} = 16\pi^3 f_\pi^3 / \mu_B\), above which ChPT no longer applies.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of domain-wall Skyrmions in QCD under magnetic fields by Eto et al. (2023, 2025) and Amari et al. (2024), which found that full-period domain-wall Skyrmions carry baryon number two with bosonic statistics. The present paper shows that the minimal constituents are fermionic with baryon number one, analogous to the case of domain-wall Skyrmions under rapid rotation studied by Eto et al. (2023). This enables further investigation of the ground state structure of dense QCD matter in strong magnetic fields, relevant for neutron star interiors and heavy-ion collision experiments.
