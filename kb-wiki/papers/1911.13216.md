# Effects of oscillating spacetime metric background on a complex scalar field and formation of topological vortices

> We study the time evolution of a complex scalar field in the symmetry broken phase in the presence of oscillating spacetime metric background. In our (2+1)-dimensional simulations, we show that the spacetime oscillations can excite an initial field configuration, which ultimately leads to the formation of topological vortices in the system. At late times, field configuration achieves a disordered state. A detailed study of the momentum and frequency modes of the field reveals that these field excitations are driven by the phenomenon of parametric resonance. In extremely high frequency regime where frequency of spacetime oscillations is much larger than the field-mass, the formed vortices are not topological in nature. Interestingly in this regime, for a suitable choice of parameters of the simulation, we observe a persistent lattice structure of vortex-antivortex pairs. We discuss applications of our study to the dynamics of interior superfluidity of neutron stars during binary neutron star mergers, in generation of excitation in ultralight axion-like field near a strong gravitational wave source, etc.

## Key claim

Spacetime metric oscillations can excite a complex scalar field in the symmetry-broken phase and lead to the formation of topological vortices via parametric resonance, but only when the oscillation frequency is less than or close to the field mass; for frequencies much larger than the field mass, the resulting vortices are not topological in nature.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the nonlinear Klein-Gordon equation for a complex scalar field in (2+1) dimensions on a 200×200 lattice, using a Minkowski metric with a periodic time-dependent perturbation of amplitude ε and frequency ω. The initial field configuration was set to the vacuum expectation value Φ₀ plus small random transverse fluctuations, and the field was evolved using the second-order Leapfrog method with periodic boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

- Lattice size: 200×200 points with spacing Δx = Δy = 0.01 Λ, giving total size L = 2.0 Λ in each direction; time step Δt = 0.005 Λ.
- For most simulations: ε = 0.4, ω = 100 Λ⁻¹, Φ₀ = 10 Λ⁻¹, λ = 40, giving field mass m_Φ = 89.44 Λ⁻¹.
- For the high-frequency regime: Φ₀ = 0.1 Λ⁻¹, λ = 4, giving m_Φ = 0.28 Λ⁻¹, with ω = 17 Λ⁻¹ or ω = 100 Λ⁻¹.
- The lowest frequency cut-off for parametric resonance is set by system size: ω must be greater than 2/(system size) = 2/(2.0 Λ) = 1 Λ⁻¹.
- For neutron star superfluidity during binary neutron star mergers: required frequency > ~30 kHz for a typical neutron star radius of ~10 km, while maximum merger frequencies reach only ~1 kHz.

## Context

This work extends the mechanism of parametric resonance for topological defect formation previously studied in Φ⁴ theories with oscillating temperature by ResoDef and Kink (no year given), and in Bose-Einstein condensates with oscillating trapping potentials by OscBEC1 and OscBEC2 (no year given). The key difference is that spacetime oscillations couple to field gradients rather than the field magnitude, allowing resonance for a wider range of frequencies not tied to the field mass. The results suggest that parametric resonance from tidal deformations during binary neutron star mergers is unlikely to excite neutron star interior superfluidity, but the mechanism may apply to ultralight axion-like fields near strong gravitational wave sources.
