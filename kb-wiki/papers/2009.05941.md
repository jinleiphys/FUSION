# Nature of $\bm{S}$-wave $\bm{NN}$ interaction and dibaryon production at nucleonic resonance thresholds

> Phase shifts and inelasticity parameters for $NN$ scattering in the partial-wave channels ${}^3S_1$--${}^3D_1$ and ${}^1S_0$ at energies $T_{\rm lab}$ from zero to about 1 GeV are described within a unified $NN$ potential model assuming the formation of isoscalar and isovector dibaryon resonances near the $NN^*(1440)$ threshold. Evidence for these near-threshold resonances is actually found in the recent WASA experiments on single- and double-pion production in $NN$ collisions. There, the excitation of the Roper resonance $N^*(1440)$ exhibits a structure in the energy dependence of the total cross section, which corresponds to the formation of dibaryon states with $I(J^π)=0(1^+)$ and $1(0^+)$ at the $NN^*(1440)$ threshold. These two $S$-wave dibaryon resonances may provide a new insight into the nature of the strong $NN$ interaction at low and intermediate energies.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the strong $NN$ interaction in $S$-wave channels at low and intermediate energies (up to about 1 GeV) is governed by the formation of two dibaryon resonances near the $NN^*(1440)$ threshold, rather than by conventional meson-exchange mechanisms alone. The claim is hedged by the recognition that the inelasticity parameters above the resonance position are not perfectly described, and the dibaryon width in the $^1S_0$ channel is too narrow, indicating that a more detailed treatment of inelastic processes is required.

## Method

The authors constructed a unified $NN$ potential model combining a peripheral one-pion exchange potential (OPEP) with an energy-dependent separable pole term representing coupling to a "bare" dibaryon state in an internal six-quark channel. They also included an orthogonalising potential $V_{\rm orth}$ to account for the repulsive core and the mixing between $NN$ and $NN^*(1440)$ channels. The model parameters were fitted to reproduce the SAID partial-wave analysis data for phase shifts and inelasticity parameters in the coupled $^3S_1$--$^3D_1$ and $^1S_0$ partial-wave channels from zero to about 1.2 GeV laboratory energy.

## Key numbers

- For the $^3S_1$--$^3D_1$ channel, the theoretical dibaryon resonance parameters are $M_{\rm th}(^3SD_1) = 2310$ MeV and $\Gamma_{\rm th}(^3SD_1) = 157$ MeV.
- For the $^1S_0$ channel, the theoretical dibaryon resonance parameters are $M_{\rm th}(^1S_0) = 2330$ MeV and $\Gamma_{\rm th}(^1S_0) = 51$ MeV.
- From a Lorentzian fit to the isoscalar single-pion production cross section measured by WASA-at-COSY, the experimental dibaryon parameters are $m = 2315(10)$ MeV and $\Gamma = 150(30)$ MeV.
- The bare dibaryon mass parameter used in the model is $M_0 = 2275$ MeV with $\Gamma_0 = 80$ MeV for the $^3S_1$--$^3D_1$ channel, and $M_0 = 2300$ MeV with $\Gamma_0 = 40$ MeV for the $^1S_0$ channel.
- The orthogonalising potential strength is $\lam_0 = 165$ MeV with range $r_0 = 0.475$ fm for the triplet channel and $r_0 = 0.48$ fm for the singlet channel.

## Context

This work extends the dibaryon-induced model previously applied to higher partial waves (such as $^1D_2$, $^3P_2$, $^3F_3$, and $^3D_3$--$^3G_3$) by Kukulin et al. (2019) and Yafremov et al. (2019) to the $S$-wave channels, which are most sensitive to short-range dynamics. The predicted dibaryon resonances are consistent with recent WASA-at-COSY experimental data on single- and double-pion production (Clement et al., 2019), where the Roper resonance $N^*(1440)$ excitation shows a structure in the energy dependence of the total cross section. The results enable future studies of the role of quark degrees of freedom in nuclear forces and suggest that similar near-threshold dibaryon resonances may appear at other nucleonic resonance thresholds.
