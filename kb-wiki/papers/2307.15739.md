# Energy Correlators on Tracks: Resummation and Non-Perturbative Effects

> Energy correlators measured inside high-energy jets at hadron colliders have recently been demonstrated to provide a new window into both perturbative and non-perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics. A number of the most interesting features of these correlators, namely their universal scaling behavior and the ability to image the confinement transition, require precise angular resolution, necessitating the use of tracking information in experimental measurements. Theoretically, tracking information can be incorporated into the energy correlators using track functions, which are non-perturbative functions describing the fragmentation of quarks and gluons into charged hadrons. In this paper, we apply our recently developed track function formalism to energy correlators, and study in detail the interplay of track functions with perturbative resummation and non-perturbative power corrections. We provide resummed results for the energy correlators at collinear next-to-leading-logarithmic accuracy and compare with parton shower Monte Carlo simulations. For the two-point correlator the use of tracking has a minimal effect throughout the entire distribution, but it has a significant effect for higher point correlators. Our results are crucial for the theoretical interpretation of recent experimental measurements of the energy-energy correlators.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the two-point energy correlator measured on charged-particle tracks is almost identical to the all-hadron measurement throughout the entire distribution (up to an overall normalization), enabling clean interpretation of confinement transition measurements on tracks, while higher-point correlators show significant modifications from tracking that require perturbative resummation for correct theoretical interpretation.

## Method

The authors apply the track function formalism to projected N-point energy correlators in the collinear limit, deriving a factorization theorem that separates perturbative jet functions from non-perturbative track function moments. They compute one-loop matching coefficients for the jet functions for N=2 through N=6, perform resummation at collinear next-to-leading-logarithmic (NLL) accuracy, and compare with parton shower Monte Carlo simulations using Pythia for e+e- collisions at Q=250 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Track function moments at μ=250 GeV: T_q(1)=0.62, T_q(2)=0.42, T_q(3)=0.29, T_q(4)=0.22, T_q(5)=0.16, T_q(6)=0.13; T_g(1)=0.62, T_g(2)=0.40, T_g(3)=0.26, T_g(4)=0.18, T_g(5)=0.13, T_g(6)=0.090
- The ratio of track-based to all-hadron correlators in the deep non-perturbative region approaches approximately (2/3)^2 ≈ T(1)^2 for all N-point correlators
- One-loop quark jet function constant for the 2-point correlator: J_{q,0}^{[2],(1)} = -C_F (37/12) T_g(1)T_q(1)
- One-loop gluon jet function constant for the 2-point correlator: J_{g,0}^{[2],(1)} = -C_A (449/150) T_g(1)T_g(1) - n_f T_F (7/25) T_q(1)T_q(1)

## Context

This work extends the track function formalism developed by Chang et al. (2013) and the recent advances in understanding non-linear RG evolution by Li et al. (2021), Jaarsma et al. (2022), and Chen et al. (2022) from e+e- event-based observables to jet substructure observables at hadron colliders. It builds on the factorization theorems for energy correlators in the collinear limit derived by Dixon et al. (2019) and Chen et al. (2020), and is directly motivated by recent experimental measurements of energy correlators on tracks at ALICE and STAR. The results enable theoretical interpretation of these measurements and provide a systematically improvable framework for precision studies of both perturbative QCD scaling and the confinement transition using tracking information.
