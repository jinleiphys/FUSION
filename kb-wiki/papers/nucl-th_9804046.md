# Radial flow of kaon mesons in heavy ion reactions

> This work investigates the collective motion of kaons in heavy ion reactions at SIS energies (about 1-2 GeV/nucleon). A radial collective flow of $K^+$ mesons is predicted to exist in central Au + Au collisions, which manifests in a characteristic "shoulder-arm" shape of the transverse mass spectrum of the midrapidity $K^+$ mesons. The $K^+$ radial flow arises from the repulsive $K^+$ mean field in nuclear matter. In spite of a strong reabsorption and rescattering the attractive $K^-$ mean field leads as well to a collective radial flow of $K^-$ mesons. The $K^-$ radial flow, however, is different from that of $K^+$ mesons and can be observed by a characteristic "concave" structure of the transverse mass spectrum of the $K^-$ mesons emitted at midrapidity. The kaon radial flows can therefore serve as a novel tool for the investigation of kaon properties in dense nuclear matter.

# Radial flow of kaon mesons in heavy ion reactions

## Key claim

The paper predicts that both K+ and K- mesons exhibit a collective radial flow in central Au+Au collisions at SIS energies (1-2 GeV/nucleon), but this flow arises from the kaon mean field rather than from an expanding source; the K+ radial flow is driven by the repulsive mean field and produces a "shoulder-arm" shape in the transverse mass spectrum, while the K- radial flow is driven by the attractive mean field and produces a "concave" structure, with the caveat that the K- flow is partially canceled by reabsorption and rescattering.

## Method

The authors used the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) transport approach to simulate Au+Au and Ni+Ni collisions at subthreshold beam energies, including baryons, Delta and N* resonances, pions, and kaons with their production, absorption, elastic scattering, Coulomb interactions, and in-medium mean fields based on chiral perturbation theory. They compared the transverse mass spectra of midrapidity kaons from full simulations to Boltzmann distributions and to simulations without the kaon mean field, and fitted the spectra with a boosted thermal distribution to extract the radial flow velocity and temperature.

## Key numbers

- K+ radial flow velocity beta = 0.1 for Au+Au at 1 GeV/nucleon and impact parameter b = 5 fm, with temperature T = 60 MeV
- K+ radial flow velocity beta = 0.11 for Au+Au at 1 GeV/nucleon and b = 3 fm, with temperature T = 62 MeV
- Average transverse mass of K+ increases from <m_t> = 566 MeV to 594 MeV (b = 5 fm) when the kaon potential is included, corresponding to a radial boost of beta = 0.102
- Average transverse mass of K+ increases from <m_t> = 571 MeV to 600 MeV (b = 3 fm), corresponding to a radial boost of beta = 0.113
- K- radial flow velocity beta = 0.085 for Au+Au at 1.8 GeV/nucleon and b = 5 fm, with temperature T = 83 MeV
- K- radial flow velocity beta = 0.066 for Ni+Ni at 1.93 GeV/nucleon with b < 4 fm, with temperature T = 76 MeV
- Average transverse mass of K- decreases from <m_t> = 596 MeV to 575 MeV for Au+Au (reduction of 21 MeV) and from 585 MeV to 569 MeV for Ni+Ni (reduction of 16 MeV)
- K+ potential at saturation density rho_0 is about +28 MeV (using the potential from Brown (1996) that agrees with impulse approximation)
- K- potential at saturation density rho_0 is about -100 MeV (using Sigma_KN = 350 MeV)
- K+ elastic cross section sigma_KN ~ 10 mb
- K- elastic cross section and absorption cross section sigma_{K-N -> pi Y} ~ 50 mb at laboratory K- momentum of 0.2 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends studies of collective flow in heavy ion reactions from nuclear matter (bounce-off, squeeze-out, radial flow) to secondary particles, specifically kaons. It builds on prior work by Li (1995), Wang (1997), and Ritman (1995) on kaon transverse flow and in-medium kaon properties, and on the chiral perturbation theory framework of Kaplan and Nelson (1986) and Brown (1996) for kaon-nucleon interactions. The predicted kaon radial flow signatures provide a new tool to probe kaon mean fields in dense nuclear matter, complementing studies of kaonic atoms by Batty (1997) and K- production experiments by the KaoS Collaboration (Barth 1997). The results also connect to astrophysical questions about kaon properties in compact stars as discussed by Brown (1994).
