# Parton Equilibration Enforcing Baryon Number Conservation

> Parton equilibration for a thermally equilibrated but chemically non-equilibrated quark gluon plasma is presented. Parton equilibration is studied enforcing baryon numbver conservation. Process like quark flavour interchanging is also considered. The degree of equilibration is studied comparatively for the various reactions / constraints that are being considered.

# Parton Equilibration Enforcing Baryon Number Conservation

## Key claim
For a thermally equilibrated but chemically non-equilibrated quark-gluon plasma with a finite baryon number density, enforcing baryon number conservation and including quark flavour interchanging processes leads to a slower temperature drop and a slower rise of most parton fugacities (except light anti-quarks) compared to cases without such constraints, with the chemical potential remaining negative throughout the evolution.

## Method
The author solves coupled rate equations for gluon, light quark, light anti-quark, and strange quark number densities together with the energy-momentum conservation (Bjorken) equation, using a (1+1)D expanding fireball model. The chemical potential is evolved self-consistently from the baryon number conservation equation, and the rates for gluon multiplication, quark-antiquark pair production, and quark flavour changing processes are computed using full phase-space integrals with matrix elements from Matsui et al. (1986). The system of equations is closed by a truncated expansion of the exponential in the chemical potential terms to avoid a vanishing determinant.

## Key numbers
- For RHIC initial conditions at thermalization time τ_i = 0.25 fm/c: initial temperature T = 0.668 GeV, gluon fugacity λ_g = 0.34, light quark fugacity λ_q = 0.064, strange quark fugacity λ_s = 0.032.
- For LHC initial conditions at τ_i = 0.25 fm/c: initial temperature T = 1.02 GeV, λ_g = 0.43, λ_q = 0.086, λ_s = 0.043.
- The anti-proton to proton ratio at √s_NN = 130 GeV Au-Au collisions (STAR) hovers between 0.6 and 0.8; the anti-Lambda to Lambda ratio hovers between 0.7 and 0.8.
- At √s_NN = 200 GeV Pb-Pb collisions (BRAHMS), the anti-proton to proton ratio is a maximum of about 0.8 at zero rapidity.
- Baryon number density is varied between 0.11/fm³ and 0.21/fm³; light quark to anti-quark fugacity ratio is varied between 1.1 and 1.9.
- Representative plot uses baryon density 0.15/fm³ and fugacity ratio 1.5.

## Context
This work extends the parton equilibration studies of He et al. (2004), who included baryon number conservation but used an expansion of number densities in powers of chemical potential and did not maintain the quark-antiquark distinction throughout. It also builds on earlier chemical equilibration studies by Dutta et al. (1999, 2000) that assumed a chemical potential equal to the system temperature and did not enforce baryon number conservation. The present work explicitly includes finite strange quark mass, maintains quark-antiquark distinction, uses full phase-space calculations for pair production and flavour changing processes (rather than factorised rates), and iterates to obtain the initial chemical potential from a given baryon number density. The results enable comparison of equilibration degree for varying initial conditions relevant to RHIC and LHC heavy-ion collisions.
