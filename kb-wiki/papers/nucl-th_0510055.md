# Autocorrelation of Hadrons in Jets Produced in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Autocorrelation of two pions produced in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate $p_T$ is calculated in the framework of the recombination model. The differences of the pseudo-rapidities and azimuthal angles of the two pions are related to the angle between two shower partons in a jet. It is shown how the autocorrelation distribution reveals the properties of jet cone of the shower partons created by high-$p_T$ partons in hard collisions.

## Key claim

The autocorrelation distribution of two pions at intermediate $p_T$ (3–8 GeV/c) in heavy-ion collisions can be related, via the recombination model, to the width $\sigma$ of a Gaussian angular distribution of shower partons within a jet cone, but only under the simplifying assumption that $\sigma$ is independent of the momentum fraction of the shower parton.

## Method

The authors calculate the two-pion autocorrelation function $A(\eta_-,\phi_-)$ in the framework of the parton recombination model, focusing on thermal-shower ($\cal TS$) recombination for pions produced at midrapidity. They assume the angular distribution of two shower partons in a jet factorizes into a product of identical Gaussian distributions $G_1(\psi) = \exp(-\psi^2/2\sigma^2)$ with width $\sigma$, and then derive the distribution $H(\chi)$ of the angle $\chi$ between the two shower partons. This $\chi$ distribution is then mapped onto the hadronic angular differences $\eta_-$ and $\phi_-$ via a $\delta$-function constraint, and integrated over the average polar angle $\bar\theta$ to obtain the normalized autocorrelation $\tilde A(\eta_-,\phi_-)$.

## Key numbers

* The intermediate $p_T$ region considered is $3 < p_T < 8$ GeV/c for central Au+Au collisions.
* The Gaussian width of the single-parton angular distribution is denoted $\sigma$; the width of the normalized $\chi$ distribution is $\sigma_H = \sqrt{2}\,\sigma$ (for $\sigma \ll \alpha$, with $\alpha = \pi/4$).
* The integration range for the average polar angle $\bar\theta$ is set to $\epsilon = 0.3$ rad.
* The maximum angle $\alpha$ for the shower parton polar angles is set to $\pi/4$.
* The normalized autocorrelation $\tilde A(\eta_-,0)$ and $\tilde A(0,\phi_-)$ are plotted for $\sigma = 0.1, 0.2, 0.3, 0.4$ (radians).

## Context

This work extends the recombination model of Hwa and collaborators (2003–2005), which successfully explained the high $p/\pi$ ratio at intermediate $p_T$, to the study of two-particle correlations without triggers. It contrasts with approaches based on local parton-hadron duality or fragmentation functions, which cannot address the baryon puzzle. The autocorrelation method, pioneered by Trainor and collaborators (2003–2005) for low $p_T$ data from RHIC, is here applied theoretically for the first time at intermediate $p_T$, enabling future experimental extraction of the jet cone width $\sigma$ from autocorrelation data without trigger bias or background subtraction.
