# Effective field theory for radiative corrections to charged-current processes II: Axial-vector coupling

> We discuss the hadronic structure-dependent radiative corrections to the axial-vector coupling that controls single-nucleon weak charged-current processes -- commonly denoted by $g_A$. We match the Standard Model at the GeV scale onto chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order in the one-nucleon sector, in the presence of electromagnetic and weak interactions. As a result, we provide a representation for the corrections to $g_A$ in terms of infrared finite convolutions of simple kernels with the single-nucleon matrix elements of time-ordered products of two and three quark bilinears (vector, axial-vector, and pseudoscalar). We discuss strategies to determine the required non-perturbative input from data, lattice-QCD (+QED), and possibly hadronic models. This work paves the way for a precise comparison of the values of the ratio $g_A/g_V$ extracted from experiment and from lattice-QCD, which constrain physics beyond the Standard Model.

## Key claim

The paper provides a representation for the hadronic structure-dependent radiative corrections to the axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A\) in terms of infrared-finite convolutions of simple kernels with single-nucleon matrix elements of time-ordered products of two and three quark bilinears, but the full correction requires non-perturbative input from lattice QCD (+QED), data, or hadronic models, and the authors demonstrate that three-point correlation functions contribute non-trivially beyond the two-point functions considered in prior work.

## Method

The authors match the Standard Model at the GeV scale onto heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) at next-to-leading order in the one-nucleon sector, including electromagnetic and weak interactions. They first integrate out electroweak-scale physics to obtain the low-energy effective field theory (LEFT), then match LEFT to HBChPT by equating functional derivatives of the effective action with respect to spacetime-dependent spurion fields representing electromagnetic and weak charges. The matching expresses the combination of HBChPT low-energy couplings \(\hat{C}_A\) in terms of two-point and three-point QCD correlation functions of quark bilinears (vector, axial-vector, and pseudoscalar), with the three-point contributions isolated via derivatives with respect to pseudoscalar or axial-vector external sources.

## Key numbers

* The axial-vector coupling expansion is given by \(g_A(\mu_\chi) = g_A^{(0)} C_\beta^r(\mu) [1 + \sum_{n=2}^\infty \Delta^{(n)}_{A,\chi} + \frac{\alpha}{2\pi} \sum_{n=0}^\infty \Delta^{(n)}_{A,\rm em}(\mu,\mu_\chi)]\), where \(g_A^{(0)}\) is the chiral-limit value and \(C_\beta^r(\mu)\) includes a term \(\frac{\alpha}{\pi} \ln(M_Z/\mu)\).
* The leading electromagnetic correction \(\Delta^{(0)}_{A,\rm em}\) contains a term \(Z_\pi [\frac{1+3(g_A^{(0)})^2}{2}(\ln\frac{\mu_\chi^2}{m_\pi^2} -1) - (g_A^{(0)})^2] + \hat{C}_A(\mu,\mu_\chi)\), with \(Z_\pi\) related to the pion-mass splitting \(m_{\pi^\pm}^2 - m_{\pi^0}^2 = 2 e^2 F_\pi^2 Z_\pi\).
* The next-to-leading electromagnetic correction \(\Delta^{(1)}_{A,\rm em} = 4 Z_\pi \pi m_\pi [c_4 - c_3 + \frac{3}{8 m_N} + \frac{9}{16} \frac{(g_A^{(0)})^2}{m_N}]\), where \(c_3\) and \(c_4\) are LECs of dimension GeV\(^{-1}\).
* The combination \(\hat{C}_A = 8\pi^2 [-\frac{X_6}{2} + 2(A_1+A_2+A_3+A_4) + \frac{1}{g_A^{(0)}}(g_1+g_2+g_{13}+\frac{g_{11}}{2})]\) is split into two-point and three-point contributions, with the two-point part involving the same non-perturbative input as the axial-vector \(\gamma W\) box diagram.
* The HBChPT expansion parameter \(\epsilon_\chi = m_\pi/\Lambda_\chi\) with \(\Lambda_\chi = 4\pi F_\pi \approx 1\) GeV, and \(F_\pi \approx 92\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach of Cirigliano (2022) and Cirigliano (2023), which uncovered potentially large structure-dependent radiative corrections to \(g_A\) and provided a precise determination of the vector coupling \(g_V\). It complements the traditional current-algebra approach of Sirlin (1967) and the dispersion-theory framework of Seng (2018), as well as the lattice-QCD evaluations of Chang (2018) and Ma (2023), by providing a systematically improvable EFT framework that includes both two-point and three-point QCD correlation functions. The results enable a precise comparison of the ratio \(g_A/g_V\) extracted from experiment (e.g., neutron beta decay asymmetry measurements by Markisch (2018) and Dubbers (2018)) with lattice-QCD predictions, thereby constraining physics beyond the Standard Model.
