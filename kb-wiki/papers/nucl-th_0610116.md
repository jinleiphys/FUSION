# Understanding nuclear "pasta": current status and future prospects

> In cores of supernovae and crusts of neutron stars, nuclei can adopt interesting shapes, such as rods or slabs, etc., which are referred to as nuclear "pasta." In recent years, we have studied the pasta phases focusing on their dynamical aspects with a quantum molecular dynamic (QMD) approach. In this article, we review these works. We also focus on the treatment of the Coulomb interaction.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the atom-dimer relaxation rate as a function of temperature and scattering length, showing that measurements of this rate can accurately determine the Efimov parameters $\kappa_*$ and $\eta_*$ that govern universal Efimov physics, provided the temperature is known with sufficient accuracy.

## Method

The authors derive an analytic expression for the inelastic atom-dimer cross section using an effective range expansion truncated after the effective range term, valid for collision energies much less than the dimer binding energy. They then compute the thermal average of this cross section using Boltzmann statistics to obtain the dimer relaxation rate constant $\beta$ as a function of temperature and scattering length. The effects of deep dimers are incorporated by making the substitution $\ln a_* \rightarrow \ln a_* - i\eta_*/s_0$ in the universal expressions for the atom-dimer scattering length and effective range.

## Key numbers

* The discrete scaling factor for identical bosons is $e^{2\pi/s_0} \approx 515$ for binding energies and $e^{\pi/s_0} \approx 22.7$ for scattering lengths, where $s_0 \approx 1.00624$.
* The universal atom-dimer scattering length is $a_{AD} = (1.46 + 2.15 \cot[s_0 \ln(a/a_*)]) \; a$, and the effective range is $r_{s,AD} = (1.13 + 0.73 \cot[s_0 \ln(a/a_*) + 0.98])^2 \; a$.
* In the low-temperature limit, the dimer relaxation rate is $\beta = [20.3 \sinh(2\eta_*)]/[\sin^2(s_0 \ln(a/a_*)) + \sinh^2 \eta_*] \; (\hbar a/m)$.
* For $^{133}$Cs atoms with $a_* = 400 \; a_0$, the temperature scale is $T_* = 8145$ nK, and the experimental measurements were carried out near 250 nK.
* The Innsbruck group observed a resonance in inelastic atom-dimer collisions near $a \approx 400 \; a_0$ and a 3-body recombination loss feature at $a \approx -850 \; a_0$ with $\eta_* = 0.06(1)$ at 10 nK.

## Context

This work extends the universal theory of Efimov physics for identical bosons, previously developed by Efimov (1970, 1971, 1979) and refined by Braaten and Hammer (2004, 2003), to include finite-temperature effects on atom-dimer relaxation. The calculations enable accurate determination of the Efimov parameters $\kappa_*$ and $\eta_*$ from the Innsbruck group's experimental observations of resonant inelastic losses in $^{133}$Cs atoms (Grimm 2006, Ferlaino 2008). Once these parameters are measured, they can be used to make universal predictions for other Efimov phenomena, such as the locations of minima and maxima in the 3-body recombination rate.
