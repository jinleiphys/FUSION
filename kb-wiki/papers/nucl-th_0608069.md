# Heavy quarks at RHIC from parton transport theory

> There are several indications that an opaque partonic medium is created in energetic Au+Au collisions \sqrt{s} ~ 100 GeV/nucleon at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). At the extreme densities of ~ 10-100 times normal nuclear density reached even heavy-flavor hadrons are affected significantly. Heavy-quark observables are presented from the parton transport model MPC, focusing on the nuclear suppression pattern, azimuthal anisotropy ("elliptic flow"), and azimuthal correlations. Comparison with Au+Au data at top RHIC energy \sqrt{s} = 200 GeV indicates significant heavy quark rescattering, corresponding to roughly five times higher opacities than estimates based on leading-order perturbative QCD. We propose measurements of charm-anticharm, e.g., D-meson azimuthal correlations as a sensitive, independent probe to corroborate these findings.

## Key claim

Comparison of the parton transport model MPC with Au+Au data at top RHIC energy indicates significant heavy quark rescattering, corresponding to roughly five times higher opacities than estimates based on leading-order perturbative QCD, but the study is limited to elastic 2-to-2 transport and does not include radiative channels.

## Method

The author solved the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation for on-shell parton phase space distributions using the covariant MPC algorithm with parton subdivision, considering elastic 2-to-2 QCD processes for massless gluons, light quarks/antiquarks, and charm and bottom quarks/antiquarks with masses m_c = 1.5 GeV and m_b = 4.75 GeV. Heavy-quark scattering cross sections with gluons were treated as a free parameter, ranging from the leading-order perturbative QCD estimate of sigma ~ 1.3 mb up to a 15 times enhanced value of sigma = 20 mb, while light-parton cross sections were scaled to reproduce observed elliptic flow at RHIC. Initial conditions for Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV used leading-order pQCD minijet distributions for light partons and FONLL calculations for heavy quarks, with charm-anticharm correlations obtained from PYTHIA.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass set to m_c = 1.5 GeV; bottom quark mass set to m_b = 4.75 GeV
- Heavy-quark - gluon scattering cross sections explored: sigma = 1.33 mb (leading-order pQCD estimate), 5.33 mb, and 20 mb (15 times enhanced)
- Light-parton gg -> gg cross section fixed at sigma = 45 mb, about fifteen times the elastic 2-to-2 perturbative QCD estimate
- For the perturbative sigma = 1.3 mb, charm and bottom R_AA at high p_T > 4 GeV is approximately 0.6-0.7
- For the perturbative sigma ~ 1.3 mb, charm and bottom elliptic flow is at most a few percent
- Sizeable heavy-quark elliptic flow v_2 ~ 0.1 at moderate p_T ~ 2-3 GeV requires 5-10 times enhanced cross sections
- Electron v_2 ~ 5-10% from transport requires a 4-8 times increase in heavy-quark scattering rates to sigma ~ 5-10 mb
- The away-side peak in charm-anticharm azimuthal correlations is reduced by about half for the perturbative sigma ~ 1.3 mb

## Context

This work extends earlier transport results for charm quark elliptic flow (Molnar, 2004) by including bottom quarks and studying nuclear suppression and charm-anticharm azimuthal correlations. The results are compared to the Fokker-Planck approach (Moore and Teaney, 2005) and the AMPT transport model (Zhang et al., 2005), with the covariant transport showing differences in the high-p_T behavior of R_AA and the shape of v_2(p_T). The paper proposes charm-anticharm azimuthal correlations as a sensitive independent probe to corroborate the finding that heavy-quark opacities are roughly five times higher than leading-order perturbative QCD estimates, and suggests future extension to hidden heavy-flavor observables such as J/psi suppression.
