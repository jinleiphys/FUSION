# Dissociation of quarkonium in an anisotropic hot QCD medium

> We have investigated the properties of quarkonium states in an anisotropic hot QCD medium by correcting the full Cornell potential, not the Coulomb term alone as usually done in the literature, with a dielectric function from the hard-loop resummed gluon propagator. We have found that in-medium modification in anisotropic medium causes less screening than in isotropic medium. In the short distance limit, potential does not show any medium dependence whereas in the long-distance limit, it reduces to the Coulomb potential with a dynamically screened color charge. In addition, anisotropy in momentum space introduces a characteristic angular dependence in the potential and as a result, quarkonium states in anisotropic medium are more tightly bound than in isotropic medium. In particular, quark pairs aligned in the direction of anisotropy are more bound than perpendicular to the direction of anisotropy.

## Key claim

The authors claim that in a weakly anisotropic hot QCD medium, quarkonium states are more tightly bound than in an isotropic medium, with quark pairs aligned along the direction of anisotropy being more strongly bound than those perpendicular to it. This result depends on correcting the full Cornell potential (not just the Coulomb term) with a dielectric function from the hard-loop resummed gluon propagator, and is derived under the assumption of small anisotropy parameter ξ.

## Method

The authors computed the in-medium modification to the heavy quark potential by taking the Fourier transform of the full Cornell potential (Coulomb plus linear string term) and dividing by the dielectric permittivity obtained from the hard-loop resummed gluon propagator in an anisotropic medium. For the anisotropic case, they expanded the dielectric permittivity to linear order in the anisotropy parameter ξ and derived separate expressions for the potential when the quark-antiquark separation vector is parallel or perpendicular to the direction of anisotropy. They then used first-order quantum mechanical perturbation theory to estimate the shift in energy eigenvalues due to the small anisotropic correction to the spherically-symmetric isotropic potential.

## Key numbers

- The Debye mass is defined as \( m_D^2 = g^2 T^2 (N_c/3 + N_f/6) \), where \( N_c \) and \( N_f \) are the number of colors and flavors, respectively.
- The anisotropy parameter is defined as \( \xi = \langle \mathbf{k}_T^2 \rangle / (2\langle k_L^2 \rangle) - 1 \), and in the weak-anisotropy limit ξ is treated as small (ξ < 1).
- In the small ξ limit, the pole masses are given by \( m_+^2 = (1 + \frac{\xi}{6}(3\cos 2\beta_n - 1)) m_D^2 \) and \( m_-^2 = -\frac{\xi}{3} \cos 2\beta_n m_D^2 \), where β_n is the angle between the momentum k and the anisotropy direction n.
- The isotropic part of the Coulomb term is \( V_1^{(1)}(r, \xi=0,T) = -\frac{\alpha m_D}{\hat{r}} e^{-\hat{r}} - \alpha m_D \), where \( \hat{r} = m_D r \).
- The isotropic part of the linear term is \( V_2^{(1)}(r, \xi=0,T) = \frac{2\sigma}{m_D \hat{r}} e^{-\hat{r}} - \frac{2\sigma}{m_D \hat{r}} + \frac{2\sigma}{m_D} \).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quarkonium dissociation in isotropic hot QCD media (Thakur et al. 2012, earlier work by the same group) by incorporating momentum-space anisotropy that arises in the early stages of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. It builds on the hard-loop resummed gluon propagator formalism for anisotropic plasmas developed by Romatschke and Strickland (2003-2004) and on earlier calculations of the heavy quark potential in anisotropic media by Laine et al. (2007) and Dumitru et al. (2008). The results enable more realistic predictions for quarkonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where the plasma is not perfectly isotropic at the time of quarkonium formation.
