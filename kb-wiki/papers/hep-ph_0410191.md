# Helicity-dependent generalized parton distributions and composite constituent quarks

> An approach, recently proposed to calculate the nucleon generalized parton distributions (GPDs) in a constituent quark model (CQM) scenario, in which the constituent quarks are taken as complex systems, is used to obtain helicity-dependent GPDs. They are obtained from the wave functions of the non relativistic CQM of Isgur and Karl, convoluted with the helicity-dependent GPDs of the constituent quarks themselves. The latter are modelled by using the polarized structure functions of the constituent quark, the double distribution representation of GPDs, and a phenomenological constituent quark form factor. The present approach permits to access a kinematical range corresponding to both the Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi and the Efremov-Radyushkin-Brodsky-Lepage regions, for small values of the momentum transfer and of the skewedness parameter. In this kinematical region, the present calculation represents a prerequisite for the evaluation of cross sections relevant to deeply virtual Compton scattering. In particular, we have calculated the leading twist helicity-dependent GPD \tilde H and, from our expressions, its general relations with the non relativistic definition of the axial form factor and with the leading twist polarized quark density are consistently recovered.

## Key claim

The authors extend a previously proposed approach for unpolarized generalized parton distributions (GPDs) to helicity-dependent GPDs, using a constituent quark model where the constituent quarks themselves are treated as composite systems; the calculation is valid only for small values of the momentum transfer and the skewedness parameter, and the results are accurate to order O(ξ², k²/m², Δ²/m²).

## Method

The helicity-dependent GPD \(\tilde H_q\) is obtained by convoluting the non-relativistic wave functions of the Isgur-Karl constituent quark model with the helicity-dependent GPDs of the constituent quarks themselves, using an impulse approximation formalism. The constituent quark GPDs are modeled using the polarized structure functions of the constituent quark, the double distribution representation of GPDs, and a phenomenological constituent quark form factor, following the Altarelli-Cabibbo-Maiani-Petronzio (ACMP) scheme. The calculation is performed in the Breit frame and accesses both the DGLAP region (x > ξ) and the ERBL region (-ξ < x < ξ) for small values of the momentum transfer and skewedness parameter.

## Key numbers

- The calculation is valid for \(\xi^2 \ll 1\), \(\vec k^2 \ll m^2\), and \(\vec \Delta^2 \ll m^2\), where \(m\) is the constituent quark mass
- The skewedness parameter \(\xi\) satisfies \(0 \le \xi \le \sqrt{-\Delta^2}/\sqrt{4M^2 - \Delta^2}\)
- The forward limit recovers the polarized quark density: \(\tilde H_q(x,0,0) = \Delta q(x)\)
- The x-integral of \(\tilde H_q\) yields the contribution of flavor \(q\) to the axial form factor: \(\int dx \tilde H_q(x,\xi,\Delta^2) = G_A^q(\Delta^2)\)

## Context

This work extends the unpolarized GPD calculation of Scopetta and Vento (2003) to helicity-dependent GPDs, following the same ACMP-based approach that treats constituent quarks as composite systems. It improves upon earlier constituent quark model calculations by Scopetta et al. (1998, 2001) and the Isgur-Karl model calculations of the Valencia group (2002, 2003), which could only access the DGLAP region; the present approach enables exploration of the ERBL region as well. The results enable future calculations of cross sections for deeply virtual Compton scattering, which are needed for planning experiments at facilities such as Jefferson Lab.
