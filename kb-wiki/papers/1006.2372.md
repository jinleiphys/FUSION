# On a theory of neutrino oscillations with entanglement

> We show that, despite appearances, a theoretical approach to neutrino oscillation in which the neutrino and its interaction partners are entangled yields the standard result for the neutrino oscillation wavelength. We also shed some light on the question of why plane-wave approaches to the neutrino oscillation problem can yield the correct oscillation wavelength even though they do not explicitly account for the localization of the neutrino source and the detector.

## Key claim
The paper shows that a theoretical approach to neutrino oscillations in which the neutrino and its recoil partner are treated as an entangled two-body state yields an oscillation wavelength that appears different from the standard expression, but is physically equivalent to it when the correct distance variable is used. The authors also explain why plane-wave approaches can yield the correct oscillation wavelength despite not explicitly accounting for source and detector localization.

## Method
The authors derive the neutrino oscillation wavelength from a plane-wave treatment where the neutrino and recoil from a two-body decay P → ν + R form an entangled state. They compute the two-particle wave function in the parent rest frame, express it in terms of the lab-frame separation D' between neutrino and recoil, and extract the oscillation wavelength from the phase differences between mass eigenstates. They then compare this wavelength to the standard expression for oscillation in the source-to-detector distance L' and show the two are related by the same factor as the distance variables D' and L' themselves.

## Key numbers
- The oscillation wavelength in the recoil-neutrino separation D' is λ'_D,ij ≈ (4πγ m_P / Δm²_ij) × (m_P² - m_R²)/(m_P² + m_R²)
- The standard oscillation wavelength in source-to-detector distance L' is λ'_L,ij = 4πE_0'/Δm²_ij, where E_0' is the energy massless neutrinos would have in the laboratory
- The ratio of the two wavelengths is λ'_D,ij/λ'_L,ij = (1+β_R)/(1+β), where β_R is the recoil speed in the parent rest frame and β is the parent speed in the lab frame
- The ratio of the distance variables is D'/L' = (1+β_R)/(1+β), establishing the physical equivalence

## Context
This work extends the long-standing discussion of neutrino oscillation theory, which has been treated using plane waves (Mann 1976, Bilenky 1978, Lipkin 2005), wave packets (Nussinov 1976, Kayser 1981, Giunti 1991, Kiers 1995), and quantum field theory (Grimus 1996, Beuthe 2001, Akhmedov 2010). The paper specifically addresses a recent entangled plane-wave approach that appeared to yield a different oscillation wavelength, and reconciles it with the standard result. It also clarifies why plane-wave treatments can be justified despite their formal inconsistencies, and notes that the entanglement between neutrino and recoil must be broken by further interactions for oscillations to occur, as previously observed by Cohen (2008).
