# Coherent bubble-sum approximation for coupled-channel resonance scattering

> For coupled-channel resonance scattering we derive a model with a closed form solution for the $T$-matrix that satisfies unitarity and analyticity. The two-channel case is handled explicitly for an arbitrary number of resonances. The method focuses on the expansion of the transition matrix elements, $Γ(s)$, in known analytical functions. The appropriate hadronic form factors and the related energy shifts can be determined from the scattering data. The differences between this method and the $K$-matrix and the Breit-Wigner approximation are illustrated in the case of the $S_{11}$ resonances $S_{11}(1535)$ and $S_{11}(1650)$.

# Coherent bubble-sum approximation for coupled-channel resonance scattering

## Key claim
The paper derives a closed-form solution for the coupled-channel T-matrix that satisfies unitarity and analyticity, but the method is restricted to the kinematical domain where only the designated channels can go on shell; full covariance is only restored by replacing the energy dispersion relation with an s-channel dispersion relation, which yields a generally small difference.

## Method
The method solves the Lippmann-Schwinger equation algebraically for two coupled channels with an arbitrary number of resonances, expanding the transition matrix elements in known analytical functions derived from threshold behavior. The form factors and energy shifts are determined from scattering data by fitting a polynomial expansion in the variable (s - s_th)/s, where each term has a closed-form solution for the dispersion integral. The approach is implemented in the open-source program BUBBLEGUM and compared to K-matrix and Breit-Wigner approximations.

## Key numbers
- Bare masses for the S11 resonances are 1.60 GeV and 1.79 GeV; renormalized masses come to 1.51 GeV and 1.71 GeV.
- Coupling constants for the two S11 resonances: g_{1,piN} = -0.11, g_{2,piN} = 0.175, g_{1,etaN} = -0.10, g_{2,etaN} = -0.11.
- Data fitted between threshold and 1.77 GeV for the S11 resonances at 1.535 GeV and 1.65 GeV.
- Including the first expansion coefficient c1 halves the mean square deviation between data and fit.

## Context
This work extends the bubble-sum dressing of single resonances to coupled channels with channel mixing, which previously appeared only in a few analyses with fixed form factors (e.g., the Pitt-ANL analysis and work by Surya). The method contrasts with the K-matrix approach (Newstead, 1966) and Breit-Wigner approximation (Feshbach, 1992; Feller, 1969), which violate analyticity and produce significant deviations when thresholds are close to resonance values. The approach enables extraction of non-perturbative transition matrix elements between asymptotic states and resonances from scattering data, providing constraints on hadron models.
