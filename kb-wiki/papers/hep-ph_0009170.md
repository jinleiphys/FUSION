# The Little Bang: Searching for quark-gluon matter in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> I review the status of the search for quark-gluon plasma in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The available data provide strong evidence for the "three pillars of the Little Bang model": strong radial expansion of the collision fireball with Hubble-like scaling, thermal hadron emission and primordial hadrosynthesis. It is argued that the initial state of the reaction zone exhibits features which cannot be understood with conventional hadronic dynamics, but are consistent with the formation of deconfined quark-gluon matter.

## Key claim

The paper argues that data from CERN SPS heavy-ion collisions provide "compelling evidence" for a new state of matter, but explicitly distinguishes this from a "discovery of quark-gluon plasma" (QGP), stating that the evidence is consistent with deconfined quark-gluon matter while acknowledging that several crucial issues can only be resolved by experiments at higher energies (RHIC and LHC).

## Method

The author reviews experimental data from 158 A GeV/c Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN SPS, analyzing hadron transverse mass spectra, two-particle Bose-Einstein (HBT) correlations, dilepton invariant mass spectra, and hadronic particle ratios. These observables are compared to predictions from a thermal fireball model with collective expansion, a hadron resonance gas in chemical equilibrium, and lattice QCD calculations of the critical temperature and energy density.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density in central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS: epsilon_Bj = 3.2 +/- 0.3 GeV/fm^3 at tau_0 = 1 fm/c
- Critical energy density for QCD phase transition: epsilon_c ~ 1 GeV/fm^3
- Critical temperature from lattice QCD: T_c ~ 170 MeV +/- 15%
- Freeze-out temperature from combined analysis of spectra and HBT correlations: T_f ~ 100 MeV
- Mean transverse flow velocity at freeze-out: v_perp ~ 0.55 c
- Thermal energy density at freeze-out: epsilon_f ~ 0.05 GeV/fm^3
- Chemical freeze-out temperature from particle ratio fits: T_chem = 168 MeV with mu_B = 266 MeV
- Transverse area growth from initial to freeze-out: factor 4-5
- Fireball volume expansion from tau_0 to tau_f: factor 40
- Initial temperature estimate if thermalized at 1 fm/c: T_0 ~ 210-220 MeV

## Context

This review summarizes the status of the CERN SPS heavy-ion program after 15 years of experiments, particularly results from the last five years with the 158 A GeV/c Pb beam. It extends earlier work on QGP signatures (Rafelski, 1982) and the Bjorken energy density estimate (Bjorken, 1983), and provides the scientific basis for the CERN announcement on February 10, 2000. The paper identifies open questions that require the higher energies of RHIC and LHC to resolve, particularly the need to penetrate deeper into the QGP phase beyond the region at and slightly above T_c accessible at SPS.
