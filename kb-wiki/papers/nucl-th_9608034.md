# Square-well solution to the three-body problem

> The angular part of the Faddeev equations is solved analytically for s-states for two-body square-well potentials. The results are, still analytically, generalized to arbitrary short-range potentials for both small and large distances. We consider systems with three identical bosons, three non-identical particles and two identical spin-1/2 fermions plus a third particle with arbitrary spin. The angular wave functions are in general linear combinations of trigonometric and exponential functions. The Efimov conditions are obtained at large distances. General properties and applications to arbitrary potentials are discussed. Gaussian potentials are used for illustrations. The results are useful for numerical calculations, where for example large distances can be treated analytically and matched to the numerical solutions at smaller distances. The saving is substantial.

## Key claim

The paper provides an exact analytic solution to the angular part of the Faddeev equations for s-states when the two-body interaction is a square-well potential, and then generalizes these results analytically to arbitrary short-range potentials at both small and large distances. The hedge is that the analytic solutions are exact only for square-well potentials; for arbitrary potentials they serve as approximations valid in the limits of small and large hyperradius, with numerical matching required at intermediate distances.

## Method

The authors solve the angular eigenvalue problem of the Faddeev equations in hyperspherical coordinates for three-body systems, first for three identical bosons with spin-independent square-well interactions, then generalize to three non-identical particles and to systems with two identical spin-1/2 fermions plus a third particle. The angular wave functions are expressed as linear combinations of trigonometric and exponential functions in different regions of the hyperangular variable α, determined by whether the two-body potential is finite or vanishes. Matching conditions at region boundaries yield quantization conditions for the angular eigenvalue λ̃, and the large-distance limit is shown to reproduce the Efimov condition.

## Key numbers

- For three identical bosons at short distances (ρ ≤ R₀/√2), the angular eigenvalue is λ̃ₙ = 4n² − ρ²v₀(1 − (2/n)(−1)ⁿ(2/√3) sin(nπ/3)), where n is an integer and v₀ = 2mV₀/ħ².
- At ρ = 0, the eigenvalue reduces to λ̃ₙ = 4n².
- In the large-distance limit (ρ → ∞), the eigenvalue approaches λ̃ → 4n², with the leading correction λ̃ₙ = 4n²(1 − 12a_scat/(πρ√2)), where a_scat is the two-body scattering length.
- For infinite scattering length, the large-distance eigenvalue equation reduces to λ̃ = −1.01251, reproducing the Efimov condition.
- The two-body scattering length for the square-well potential is given by 1/a_scat = √(v₀/2) / [−R₀√(v₀/2) + tan(R₀√(v₀/2))].

## Context

This work extends the coordinate-space Faddeev method developed by Fedorov, Jensen, and collaborators (1993, 1994) by providing analytic angular solutions for square-well potentials, which previously existed only for harmonic oscillator potentials (Barnea, 1992). The results enable substantial computational savings in numerical three-body calculations by allowing analytic treatment of large distances, which must be handled with particular care for loosely bound systems and Efimov states. The method opens the possibility of treating the three-body Coulomb problem in the continuum and is applicable to Borromean systems, molecular systems, and nuclear systems where Efimov states have been sought.
