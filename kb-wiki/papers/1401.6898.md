# Parity violating elastic electron scattering from 27Al and the Qweak measurement

> I calculate the parity violating asymmetry A_{pv} for elastic electron scattering from 27Al in order to compare with the Qweak experiment "background" 27Al measurement. I find that Coulomb distortions, and the quadrupole form factor, reduce A_{pv} near the diffraction minimum. The Qweak data can be used to confirm the neutron radius of 27Al, if nuclear structure uncertainties are indeed small, as I suggest, and one can estimate inelastic and impurity contributions. This could provide an important check of the measurement, analysis, and theory.

## Key claim

The Qweak experiment's parity-violating asymmetry data for elastic electron scattering from 27Al can be used to confirm the neutron radius of 27Al, but only if nuclear structure uncertainties are indeed small and inelastic and impurity contributions can be estimated to the necessary accuracy.

## Method

The author calculates the parity-violating asymmetry A_pv for elastic electron scattering from 27Al using a relativistic mean field model (FSUgold interaction) that treats 27Al as a simple d5/2 proton hole. Coulomb distortions for the spherically symmetric C0 contributions are treated exactly by solving the Dirac equation numerically, while the quadrupole C2 contributions are added in Born approximation with a parameter xi^2 varied between 0.5 and 1.5 to cover nuclear structure uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Proton radius R_p = 2.904 fm, neutron radius R_n = 2.913 fm, charge radius R_ch = 3.013 fm for the FSUgold RMF model of 27Al
- Total weak charge of 27Al: Q_W = -12.8919 (using Q_n = -0.9878 and Q_p = 0.0721 with radiative corrections)
- At the average Qweak momentum transfer (7.8 degrees for 1160 MeV electrons), the log derivative |d ln A_DW(C0)/d ln R_n| is approximately 2, meaning a 4% measurement of A_pv is sensitive to 2% changes in R_n
- The best estimate for A_pv near the diffraction minimum (13.5 degrees) is only one third of the plane-wave Born approximation value A_pv^0

## Context

This work extends the parity-violating electron scattering technique previously applied to 208Pb by the PREX experiment (2012) and planned for 48Ca by the CREX experiment, now to the lighter nucleus 27Al. The Qweak experiment primarily measures the weak charge of the proton from hydrogen, but its aluminum target windows create a background that requires a separate 27Al measurement. The calculation uses a simple relativistic mean field model similar to earlier shell model calculations using harmonic oscillator wave functions (old27Al), and the cross sections agree well with measured 250 MeV elastic scattering data from 27Al (27Alsigma). A successful determination of the 27Al neutron radius would provide a sharp test of nuclear structure theory, since for N≈Z nuclei most models predict R_n≈R_p, unlike the symmetry-energy-sensitive case of 208Pb.
