# Decoding Dark Matter Admixed Neutron Stars: From Static Structure to Rotational Deformation

> In this study, we investigate the impacts of dark matter (DM) on the properties of both static and rotating neutron stars utilizing a self-interacting DM model, motivated by the neutron decay anomaly. DM-admixed NSs are modeled by assuming chemical equilibrium between ordinary matter and the dark sector, treating a single-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) framework. By treating the DM interaction strength ($G$) as a free parameter, we explore its influence on NS properties, considering a broad range of equations of state (EoSs). Using the mass-radius constraints from NICER pulsar measurements, we constrain the DM interaction strength for each EoS via a likelihood analysis. Extending this model to rotating NSs, we analyze how centrifugal forces associated with increasing angular velocity ($Ω$) enhance both mass and radius, causing deformation. We assess the impact of DM on rotational deformation by calculating the eccentricity, highlighting the interplay between DM and rotational forces. Since both DM and rotation simultaneously influence NS properties, we compute the relative changes in mass and radius across varying $G$ and $Ω$ values to quantify their combined effects.

## Key claim

The paper claims that dark matter (DM) admixed neutron stars, modeled under a single-fluid approximation with chemical equilibrium between baryonic and dark sectors, can simultaneously satisfy NICER mass-radius constraints while reducing the maximum mass of neutron stars, but only for a specific range of DM self-interaction strengths that depends strongly on the stiffness of the chosen hadronic equation of state.

## Method

The authors model DM-admixed neutron stars using a relativistic mean-field (RMF) framework for baryonic matter with four different equations of state (Hornick1-4), combined with a self-interacting DM model inspired by the neutron decay anomaly where a dark fermion of mass 938.0 MeV is in chemical equilibrium with neutrons. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for static stars and apply the Hartle-Thorne formalism for slowly rotating stars, treating the DM interaction strength G as a free parameter. A likelihood analysis using Gaussian kernel density estimation is performed against NICER mass-radius data from PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620 to constrain G for each equation of state.

## Key numbers

- DM fermion mass set to m_chi = 938.0 MeV, with scalar boson mass m_phi = 0
- Hornick1 EOS favors G = 7.50^{+5.50}_{-2.50} fm^2 at 68% confidence interval
- Hornick4 EOS favors G = 40.00^{+960.00}_{-29.00} fm^2 at 68% confidence interval
- Maximum DM mass fraction for Hornick1: 32.62^{+1.18}_{-1.13} percent at 68% confidence
- Maximum DM mass fraction for Hornick4: 7.76^{+1.11}_{-0.20} percent at 68% confidence
- Fastest pulsar considered spins at angular velocity Omega = 4498 s^{-1} (PSR J1748-2446ad)
- Keplerian frequency formula used: Omega_k = 0.65 sqrt(M/R^3)

## Context

This work extends the single-fluid DM-admixed neutron star framework previously applied to static stars by Husain (2022) and Swarnim (2023), and builds on the approach of Rutherford (2023) who used NICER data to constrain both baryonic and DM equations of state. The study is the first to systematically combine the neutron decay anomaly DM model with the Hartle-Thorne formalism for rotating stars, enabling simultaneous investigation of DM effects and rotational deformation. The results enable future work to disentangle the competing influences of DM content and rotation on neutron star observables, particularly for millisecond pulsars where both effects are significant.
