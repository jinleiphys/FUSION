# Transport properties of the fluid produced at RHIC

> It is by now well known that the relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, BNL have produced a strongly interacting fluid with remarkable properties, among them the lowest ever observed ratio of the coefficient of shear viscosity to entropy density. Arguments based on ideas from the String Theory, in particular the AdS/CFT correspondence, led to the conjecture --- now known to be violated --- that there is an absolute lower limit $1/4 π$ on the value of this ratio. Causal viscous hydrodynamics calculations together with the RHIC data have put an upper limit on this ratio, a small multiple of $1/4 π$, in the relevant temperature regime. Less well-determined is the ratio of the coefficient of bulk viscosity to entropy density. These transport coefficients have also been studied nonperturbatively in the lattice QCD framework, and perturbatively in the limit of high-temperature QCD. Another interesting transport coefficient is the coefficient of diffusion which is also being studied in this context. I review some of these recent developments and then discuss the opportunities presented by the anticipated LHC data, for the general nuclear physics audience.

## Key claim

The fluid produced at RHIC has the lowest ever observed ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density, with a robust upper limit of approximately 5/(4π) in the relevant temperature region, though this value is an average and depends on uncertainties in initial conditions, equation of state, and other inputs.

## Method

The author reviews the extraction of transport coefficients from relativistic heavy-ion collisions by comparing elliptic flow data with causal viscous hydrodynamics calculations, and surveys theoretical calculations from high-temperature perturbative QCD, lattice QCD simulations, and string theory via the AdS/CFT correspondence. The review also discusses the coefficient of diffusion and its potential extraction from RHIC data using first-order (Fick) and second-order (Kelly) theories of relativistic diffusion.

## Key numbers

- The KSS bound conjectured an absolute lower limit of η/s = 1/(4π) for all substances, now known to be violated for certain conformal field theories.
- Meyer's lattice QCD results for SU(3) gluodynamics give η/s = 0.134(33) at T = 1.65 T_c and η/s = 0.102(56) at T = 1.24 T_c.
- Meyer's lattice results for ζ/s give 0.008(7) at T = 1.65 T_c, 0.065(17) at T = 1.24 T_c, and 0.73(3) at T = 1.02 T_c.
- The upper limit from RHIC elliptic flow data is η/s ≲ 5/(4π) as an average over the relevant temperature region.
- Extracted η/s values from RHIC data depend on initial conditions: Glauber initial conditions yield η/s ≃ 0.08, while colour-glass-condensate initial conditions yield η/s ≃ 0.16.
- At LHC, the initial temperature is expected to be ∼4 T_c, compared to ∼2 T_c at RHIC, with initial energy density 15-60 GeV/fm³ at LHC versus ∼5 GeV/fm³ at RHIC.

## Context

This review summarizes the state of knowledge on transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma following the discovery at RHIC that the produced fluid has an extremely low shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, extending earlier work on ideal hydrodynamics by Huovinen and others. The paper discusses the KSS bound from Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2004) and its subsequent violation found by Buchel (2008) and Brigante (2008), and compares lattice QCD results from Nakamura (2004) and Meyer (2007) with perturbative high-temperature QCD calculations by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2000, 2003, 2006). The review enables future work at LHC by identifying the need for temperature-dependent transport coefficients in hydrodynamic calculations and the potential to extract the diffusion coefficient from RHIC data.
