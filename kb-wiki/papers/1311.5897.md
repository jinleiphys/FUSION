# New fission fragment distributions and r-process origin of the rare-earth elements

> Neutron star (NS) merger ejecta offer a viable site for the production of heavy r-process elements with nuclear mass numbers A > 140. The crucial role of fission recycling is responsible for the robustness of this site against many astrophysical uncertainties, but calculations sensitively depend on nuclear physics. In particular the fission fragment yields determine the creation of 110 < A < 170 nuclei. Here we apply a new scission-point model, called SPY, to derive the fission fragment distribution (FFD) of all relevant neutron-rich, fissioning nuclei. The model predicts a doubly asymmetric FFD in the abundant A ~ 278 mass region that is responsible for the final recycling of the fissioning material. Using ejecta conditions based on relativistic NS merger calculations we show that this specific FFD leads to a production of the A ~ 165 rare-earth peak that is nicely compatible with the abundance patterns in the Sun and metal-poor stars. This new finding further strengthens the case of NS mergers as possible dominant origin of r-nuclei with A > 140.

## Key claim
The SPY scission-point model predicts a doubly asymmetric (four-hump) fission fragment distribution for the abundant A ~ 278 fissioning nuclei in neutron star merger ejecta, and this specific FFD produces the A ~ 165 rare-earth peak in r-process abundances that matches solar and metal-poor star patterns, whereas previous symmetric FFD models underproduced these rare-earth elements.

## Method
The authors applied the SPY scission-point model, a parameter-free statistical approach using microscopic mean-field ingredients with the Gogny effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, to compute fission fragment distributions for all neutron-rich fissioning nuclei relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis. They coupled these FFDs with a nuclear reaction network containing 5000 species from protons up to Z=110, using ejecta conditions from relativistic smoothed particle hydrodynamics simulations of symmetric 1.35–1.35 solar mass neutron star mergers with the DD2 equation of state. The network included neutron-induced, photo-induced, beta-delayed, and spontaneous fission rates based on HFB-14 fission paths and TALYS code calculations.

## Key numbers
- Ejecta mass from the symmetric 1.35–1.35 solar mass NS merger simulation: ~3 × 10^{-3} solar masses
- Neutron densities during irradiation: typically 10^{33-35} cm^{-3}
- Average number of prompt neutrons emitted per fission for A ~ 278 isobars: about 4 neutrons
- Maximum average number of evaporated neutrons for heaviest Z ~ 110 nuclei at the neutron drip line: ~14 neutrons
- The A ~ 278 mass region is the dominant abundance peak in the actinide region during the irradiation phase, and these nuclei fission with a doubly asymmetric four-hump pattern

## Context
This work extends the NS merger r-process scenario of Goriely (2011) and Bauswein (2013) by replacing phenomenological symmetric fission fragment distributions with the SPY model predictions. It contradicts earlier FFD models such as Kodoma (1975) and the 2013 GEF model, which predicted symmetric mass yields for A ~ 278 fissioning nuclei and consequently underproduced the rare-earth peak. The result strengthens the case for NS mergers as the dominant origin of r-nuclei with A > 140 and opens the need for dynamical mean-field calculations (Goutte 2005) to quantitatively confirm the SPY predictions, as well as future experiments to probe the predicted doubly asymmetric fission mode.
