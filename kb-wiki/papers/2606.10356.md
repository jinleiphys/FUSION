# Light and Strange Baryons in Medium

> We solve the Goldstone-boson-exchange (GBE) relativistic constituent quark model of light and strange baryons by using the Faddeev approach. The model reproduces the vacuum mass spectrum of light and strange baryons below $2$ GeV reasonably well. To test the sensitivity of the model to possible medium-induced effects, we vary the masses of the constituent quarks and the exchange bosons, the confinement strength, and the quark-meson coupling constant. In a parametric study, we consider a set of power law scaling relations for these parameters, including some motivated by constituent quark level current algebra relations. We find that the baryon spectrum is most sensitive to the quark-meson coupling constant, and generally observe a decrease in baryon mass with decreasing constituent quark mass. We qualitatively estimate the impact of these mass shifts on ideal-gas baryon yields and yield ratios. Absolute yields can change significantly already for mass shifts of a few $10$~MeV, whereas yield ratios are strongly modified only when the compared baryons have different constituent-quark-mass dependence.

## Key claim

The Goldstone-boson-exchange relativistic constituent quark model, solved via the Faddeev approach, predicts that baryon masses generally decrease with decreasing constituent quark mass when medium-induced parameter variations are applied; however, the model breaks down for certain scaling schemes at relatively small deviations from vacuum (Σ-Σ* < 100 MeV), and the strongest sensitivity arises from the scaling of the quark-meson coupling constant rather than from meson mass or confinement scaling.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Faddeev equations for three-quark bound states using a Goldstone-boson-exchange (GBE) potential that includes linear confinement and pseudoscalar meson exchange (π, K, η, η') hyperfine interactions. They performed a parametric study varying the constituent quark masses (m_u = m_d = 340 MeV, m_s = 500 MeV in vacuum), exchange boson masses, confinement strength, and quark-meson coupling constants according to power-law scaling relations motivated by current algebra and Brown-Rho scaling, with seven different scaling schemes (O, A, B, C, D, E, F) and two choices for confinement scaling (ν_c = 0 or 1).

## Context

This work extends the Goldstone-boson-exchange constituent quark model of Glozman et al. (1996-1998) by introducing medium-motivated scaling relations for model parameters, building on the Faddeev approach developed by Papp et al. (1999, 2000). The scaling schemes are inspired by Brown-Rho scaling (Brown 1991, 1996) and Nambu scaling (Koch 1993), and the study tests how chiral symmetry restoration affects the baryon spectrum. The results enable qualitative estimates of how in-medium baryon mass shifts would modify ideal-gas hadron-resonance gas (HRG) model predictions for particle yields and yield ratios, though the authors caution that a full thermodynamic treatment would require specifying interaction contributions to the net pressure.
