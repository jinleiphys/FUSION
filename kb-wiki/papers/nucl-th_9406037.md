# Nucleon Structure Functions from Relativistic Constituent Quarks

> We investigate deep inelastic lepton scattering from the nucleon within a constituent quark picture, in which the internal structure of constituent quarks is modeled by meson and diquark dressing. In a covariant framework this structure leads to a breakdown of the factorization necessary for a convolution formulation. We perform our analysis in time-ordered perturbation theory in the infinite momentum frame where factorization of subprocesses is automatic. Numerical results are compared with recent data on valence quark distributions in the proton.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a relativistic constituent quark model, formulated in the infinite momentum frame (IMF) using time-ordered perturbation theory, can produce valence quark distributions in the nucleon that agree well with data, but only because the IMF automatically ensures factorization of subprocesses; in a covariant framework, the off-shell structure of constituent quarks breaks factorization and makes a simple convolution approach invalid.

## Method

The authors model deep inelastic scattering (DIS) from the nucleon as a two-step process: first, a constituent quark momentum distribution in the nucleon is determined by a relativistic quark-diquark-nucleon (QDN) vertex function, with scalar (S=0) and pseudovector (S=1) diquark components; second, the internal structure of the constituent quark is described by the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, where dressing by meson (pion) and diquark clouds softens the bare delta-function quark distribution. The analysis is performed in time-ordered perturbation theory in the IMF, which keeps all particles on-mass-shell and allows the quark distribution to be expressed as a convolution of the constituent quark momentum distribution in the nucleon and the structure function of a constituent quark.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass: m_Q ≈ 0.4 GeV; scalar diquark mass: m_D ≈ 0.6 GeV; pseudovector diquark mass: m_D ≈ 0.8 GeV.
- The Q–π coupling constant: g_{Qπ} = m_Q / f_π ≈ 4, from the Goldberger-Treiman relation.
- The cut-off for the QQπ vertex function: Λ ≈ 0.9 GeV, characteristic of a typical chiral symmetry breaking scale.
- The QDN vertex function cut-offs: Λ_0 = 1.5 GeV for scalar diquarks and Λ_1 = 1.2 GeV for pseudovector diquarks.
- The input scale for evolution: Q_0^2 ≈ (0.32 GeV)^2, evolved to Q^2 = 5 GeV^2 for comparison with data.
- The diquark-exchange process contributes 20-30% of the nucleon antiquark distribution at Q^2 ~ 5 GeV^2.
- Neglecting the p_T dependence in the pion-dressed contributions leads to differences of up to 30-40% from the full calculation.

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to link parton distributions to quark models, including bag and soliton models (BAGS), non-relativistic quark models (CONST), and relativistic quark-nucleon vertex function approaches (VFNS). The authors specifically build on the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (NJL, WW) and the vertex function formalism of VFNS and MST. The paper enables future modeling of the nucleon sea and spin structure within the same relativistic constituent quark framework, with a forthcoming publication (MW) to address these extensions.
