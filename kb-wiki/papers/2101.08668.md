# Photoproduction of $螞^*$ and $危^*$ resonances with $J^P=1/2^-$ off the proton

> We study the photoproduction of the $螞(1405)$ and $危(1400)$ hyperon resonances, the latter of which is not a well established state. We evaluate the $s$-, $t$- and $u$-channel diagrams in the Born approximation by employing the effective Lagrangians. A new ingredient is the inclusion of a nucleon resonance $N^*(1895)$ that is dynamically generated with predictions for its coupling to the $K螞(1405)$ and $K危(1400)$ channels. To extend the applicability of the model to energies beyond the threshold region, we consider a Regge model for the $t$-channel $K$- and $K^*$-exchanges. Our results are in good agreement with the CLAS data available on $螞(1405)$, while for $危(1400)$ we predict observables for its production. We also provide polarization observables for both hyperon productions, which can be useful in future experimental investigations. The present study provides new information on the nucleon resonance $N^*(1895)$ which can be an alternative source for generating the hyperon resonances $螞(1405)$ and $危(1400)$.

## Key claim

The photoproduction of the $\Lambda(1405)$ and $\Sigma(1400)$ hyperon resonances can be described by including a dynamically generated nucleon resonance $N^*(1895)$ in the $s$-channel, whose couplings to $K\Lambda(1405)$ and $K\Sigma(1400)$ are taken from prior coupled-channel calculations, and the model reproduces CLAS data for $\Lambda(1405)$ while predicting observables for the less-established $\Sigma(1400)$.

## Method

The authors evaluate $s$-, $t$-, and $u$-channel Born diagrams using effective Lagrangians, with strong couplings for $KNY^*$ and $K^*NY^*$ vertices taken from the chiral unitary model of Khemchandani et al. (2018). A Regge model replaces the Feynman propagators for $t$-channel $K$- and $K^*$-exchanges to extend the model to higher energies. The electromagnetic couplings of $N^*(1895)$, $\Lambda(1405)$, and $\Sigma(1400)$ are determined via the vector meson dominance model using vector-baryon couplings from prior work.

## Key numbers

- The $K^+ \Lambda(1405)$ photoproduction cross sections are in good agreement with CLAS data; the $N^*(1895)$ exchange plays an important role near threshold.
- The radiative decay width of $\Lambda(1405) \to \Lambda \gamma$ obtained by superposing two poles is $26.19 \pm 6.93$ keV, in agreement with the experimental value of $27 \pm 8$ keV from the Particle Data Group (2020).
- For $\Lambda(1405) \to \Sigma \gamma$, the calculated width is $2.50 \pm 1.37$ keV, closer to the PDG value of $10 \pm 4$ keV than to the alternative $23 \pm 7$ keV.
- The $N^*(1895) \to p \gamma$ branching ratio for the second pole ($N_2^*$) is $0.11$-$0.13\%$, near the upper limit of the PDG range $0.01$-$0.06\%$.
- The $N^*(1895)$ has two poles at $1801 - i96$ MeV and $1912 - i54$ MeV, with partial decay widths to $K\Lambda(1405)$ and $K\Sigma(1400)$ ranging from $1.1$ to $12.1$ MeV depending on the pole and final state.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel studies of Khemchandani et al. (2018), which found two poles for $\Lambda(1405)$ and a $1/2^-$ $\Sigma$ state near 1400 MeV, by calculating photoproduction observables for these resonances. It also builds on the analysis of Kim et al. (2017) by including the $N^*(1895)$ exchange, whose strong decays to $KY^*$ were recently determined by Khemchandani et al. (2020). The results provide predictions for $\Sigma(1400)$ photoproduction and polarization observables that can guide future experiments at ELSA and other facilities.
