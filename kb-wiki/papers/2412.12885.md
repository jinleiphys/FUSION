# Baryon Form Factors

> We review the status of baryon form factors with a special focus on the nucleon electromagnetic form factors which are known best. First, we give an introduction into the dispersive analyses and emphasize the role of unitarity and analyticity in the construction of the isoscalar and isovector spectral functions. Second, we present the state of the art in our understanding of nucleon form factors and radii including reliable uncertainty estimates from bootstrap and Bayesian methods. Third, we discuss the physics of the time-like form factors and point out further issues to be addressed in this framework. Finally, we review the status of hyperon form factors and comment on the pion cloud.

## Key claim

This review argues that dispersion relations, combined with unitarity and analyticity, provide the most rigorous framework for extracting nucleon electromagnetic form factors and radii from experimental data, but the reliability of these extractions depends critically on properly including the full two-pion continuum in the isovector spectral function rather than just the rho-meson pole.

## Method

The authors present a comprehensive review of the dispersive analysis of baryon form factors, focusing on the construction of isoscalar and isovector spectral functions from unitarity constraints and experimental data. They describe how the spectral functions are built from continuum contributions (two-pion, three-pion, K-anti-K, rho-pi) plus effective vector meson poles, with parameters constrained by low-energy normalizations, superconvergence relations from perturbative QCD, and the neutron charge radius. The review covers both space-like form factors from elastic electron scattering and time-like form factors from electron-positron annihilation, including the treatment of two-photon exchange corrections and Coulomb final-state interactions.

## Key numbers

* Proton anomalous magnetic moment: kappa_p = 1.793 (in units of nuclear magneton)
* Neutron anomalous magnetic moment: kappa_n = -1.913 (in units of nuclear magneton)
* Dipole mass squared: M_dip^2 = 0.71 GeV^2
* Sigma-minus charge radius: 0.78(10) fm (measured using a Sigma- beam at mean energy 610 GeV)
* Neutron charge radius squared from chiral EFT analysis: <r_n^2> = -0.105^{+0.005}_{-0.006} fm^2
* Isovector electric radius squared sum rule (cutoff at Lambda = 2m): 0.405(36) fm^2
* Isovector magnetic radius squared sum rule (cutoff at Lambda = 2m): 1.81(11) fm^2
* Two-pion threshold for isovector spectral function: t_0 = 4 M_pi^2
* Three-pion threshold for isoscalar spectral function: t_0 = 9 M_pi^2
* Rho resonance mass: sqrt(t) = 0.77 GeV
* Singularity location from nucleon pole projection: t_c = 4 M_pi^2 - M_pi^4/m^2 ≈ 3.98 M_pi^2

## Context

This review updates and extends the dispersive analysis framework previously developed by Hohler (1974) and Belushkin (2006), incorporating recent precise calculations of the two-pion continuum by Hoferichter (2016) using state-of-the-art pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes from dispersion theory. The work enables improved extraction of nucleon radii with reliable uncertainty estimates from bootstrap and Bayesian methods, and provides a foundation for analyzing new time-like form factor data from BESIII (2021) covering center-of-mass energies from 3.51 to 4.6 GeV. The review also discusses a proposed new technique by Lin (2023) to access hyperon charge radii from radiative Dalitz decay of charmonium.
