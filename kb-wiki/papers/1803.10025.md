# New algorithm in the variation after projection calculations for non-yrast nuclear states

> We present a novel and simple algorithm in the variation after projection (VAP) approach for the non-yrast nuclear states. It is for the first time that the yrast state and non-yrast states can be varied on the same footing. The orthogonality among the calculated states is automatically fulfilled by solving the Hill-Wheeler equation. This avoids the complexity of the frequently used Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization, as adopted by the excited VAMPIR method. Thanks to the Cauchy's interlacing theorem in the matrix theory, the sum of the calculated lowest projected energies with the same quantum numbers can be safely minimized. Once such minimization is converged, all the calculated energies and the corresponding states can be obtained, simultaneously. The present VAP calculations are performed with time-odd Hartree-Fock Slater determinants. It is shown that the calculated VAP energies (both yrast and non-yrast) are very close to the corresponding ones from the full shell model calculations. It looks the present algorithm is not limited to the VAP, but should be universal, i.e., one can do the variation with different forms of the many-body wavefunctions to calculate the excited states in different quantum many-body systems.

## Key claim

The paper presents a novel algorithm for variation after projection (VAP) calculations that, for the first time, allows yrast and non-yrast nuclear states to be varied simultaneously on the same footing, with orthogonality automatically fulfilled by solving the Hill-Wheeler equation rather than requiring Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization as in the excited VAMPIR method.

## Method

The authors take time-odd Hartree-Fock Slater determinants (SDs) and project them onto good spin using angular momentum projection operators. Multiple SDs are varied simultaneously by changing complex parameters in the Thouless formula, and the sum of the lowest m projected energies with the same quantum numbers is minimized using an exact trust region method with calculated gradients and Hessian matrices. The minimization relies on Cauchy's interlacing theorem, which guarantees that the sum of the lowest m calculated energies is bounded from below by the sum of the exact shell model energies.

## Key numbers

- For the ground 0+ state in 24Mg with m=1 and n=2 (two SDs), the present VAP gives -87.039 MeV, compared to -86.936 MeV from varying the first SD alone and -87.008 MeV from the FED VAMPIR approach.
- For the lowest two 0+ states in 24Mg with m=2 and n=2, the present VAP gives S2 = -166.474 MeV (E1 = -86.944 MeV, E2 = -79.530 MeV), while the exact shell model gives e1 = -87.104 MeV and e2 = -79.766 MeV.
- The EXCITED VAMPIR method for the same two 0+ states in 24Mg gives S2 = -166.341 MeV (E1 = -86.940 MeV, E2 = -79.400 MeV), with E2 lying 130 keV above the present VAP result.
- For the 10 lowest Jπ=0+ states in even-even sd shell nuclei calculated with 10 SDs, most δEi values range from 0 to 300 keV, with the largest within 600 keV; for 20Ne, 28Ne and 36Ar, δEi = 0 for all calculated states.
- The VAP iteration terminates when |∇Sm(d)| ≤ 0.01 keV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous VAP method for yrast states (Gao 2015, Wang 2017) to non-yrast excited states, providing an alternative to the excited VAMPIR method (Schmid 1989, 2004) which requires successive variation with Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization. The algorithm is demonstrated in the sd shell with the USDB interaction and compared to full shell model calculations using NuShellX. The authors suggest the method is universal and could be applied to other quantum many-body systems, such as excited electronic states in chemical systems (Carlos 2013), and could be extended to heavier nuclei after parallelization of the code.
