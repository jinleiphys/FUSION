# Quantal description of nucleon exchange in stochastic mean-field approach

> Nucleon exchange mechanism is investigated in central collisions of symmetric heavy-ions in the basis of the stochastic mean-field approach. Quantal diffusion coefficients for nucleon exchange are calculated by including non-Markovian effects and shell structure. Variances of fragment mass distributions are calculated in central collisions of ${}^{40}$Ca + ${}^{40}$Ca, ${}^{48}$Ca + ${}^{48}$Ca and ${}^{56}$Ni + ${}^{56}$Ni systems.

## Key claim

The stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach, extended to a fully quantal framework including non-Markovian (memory) effects and shell structure, yields quantal diffusion coefficients for nucleon exchange that are smaller than their semi-classical values and exhibit oscillations; the resulting variances of fragment mass distributions are reduced by nearly a factor of two to three compared to semi-classical calculations, though part of this reduction may be due to the use of a Fermi-gas level density that underestimates the actual density of continuum states.

## Method

The authors extended a time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) code to compute time-dependent unoccupied single-particle wave functions in addition to occupied hole states, using 3000–4000 positive-energy continuum states. They calculated the quantal diffusion coefficient \(D_{AA}(t)\) from the correlation function of the fluctuating nucleon flux across the window between colliding ions, including the full time history (non-Markovian effects). The variance of the fragment mass distribution was then obtained by integrating the diffusion coefficient over time. Calculations were performed for central collisions of symmetric systems at energies slightly below the fusion barrier, using the SLy4 Skyrme interaction.

## Key numbers

* For the central collision of \(^{40}\text{Ca} + ^{40}\text{Ca}\) at \(E_{\text{cm}} = 52.7\) MeV, the quantal variance of the fragment mass distribution is smaller than the semi-classical result by nearly a factor of two.
* For the central collision of \(^{48}\text{Ca} + ^{48}\text{Ca}\) at \(E_{\text{cm}} = 50.7\) MeV, the quantal variance is smaller than the semi-classical result by nearly a factor of three.
* For the central collision of \(^{56}\text{Ni} + ^{56}\text{Ni}\) at \(E_{\text{cm}} = 99.9\) MeV, the quantal variance is smaller than the semi-classical result by nearly a factor of two.
* The quantal diffusion coefficients exhibit oscillations and take negative values during the separation stage of the collision, in contrast to the smooth behavior of semi-classical calculations.
* The Fermi-gas level density constant used for continuum states was \(C = 7.0\) MeV\(^{-3/2}\), based on a normalization volume of \(24 \times 24 \times 49\) fm\(^3\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous semi-classical SMF studies of nucleon exchange in heavy-ion collisions (Ayik 2009, Washiyama 2009, Yilmaz 2011) by incorporating quantal effects, shell structure, and non-Markovian memory effects. The quantal diffusion coefficients are compared with semi-classical results obtained using two different TDHF codes (Kim et al. and Umar et al.). The findings enable more refined descriptions of nucleon exchange and fragment mass distributions in deep-inelastic collisions, but also highlight the need for improved treatment of the level density of continuum states to resolve remaining discrepancies.
