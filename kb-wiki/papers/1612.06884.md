# Determination of plasma screening effects for thermonuclear reactions in laser-generated plasmas

> Due to screening effects, nuclear reactions in astrophysical plasmas may behave differently than in the laboratory. The possibility to determine the magnitude of these screening effects in colliding laser-generated plasmas is investigated theoretically, having as a starting point a proposed experimental setup with two laser beams at the Extreme Light Infrastructure facility. A laser pulse interacting with a solid target produces a plasma through the Target Normal Sheath Acceleration scheme, and this rapidly streaming plasma (ion flow) impacts on a secondary plasma created by the interaction of a second laser pulse on a gas jet target. We model this scenario here and calculate the reaction events for the astrophysically relevant reaction $^{13}$C($^4$He, $n$)$^{16}$O. We find that it should be experimentally possible to determine the plasma screening enhancement factor for fusion reactions by detecting the difference in reaction events between two scenarios of ion flow interacting with the plasma target and a simple gas target. This provides a way to evaluate nuclear reaction cross-sections in stellar environments and can significantly advance the field of nuclear astrophysics.

### Key claim
It should be experimentally possible to determine the plasma screening enhancement factor for the astrophysically relevant reaction 13C(4He, n)16O by detecting the difference in neutron events between two scenarios: a TNSA-accelerated ion flow interacting with a laser-generated plasma target versus a simple gas target, provided the plasma temperature is low enough (around 100 eV) and the helium density is sufficiently high (above 10^20 cm^-3).

### Method
The authors modeled the Target Normal Sheath Acceleration (TNSA) mechanism using a one-dimensional isothermal fluid model (Mora 2003) to obtain the energy spectrum of accelerated 13C ions. They then calculated the neutron yield for the 13C(4He, n)16O reaction by combining the TNSA ion spectrum with the nuclear cross-section (from Xu 2013 and Harissopulos 2005) and the Salpeter plasma screening enhancement factor (Salpeter 1954) for a secondary helium plasma target. The total neutron events per laser pulse were computed by integrating over the incident ion energy, comparing the cases of a plasma target and a gas target.

### Key numbers
- For a 10 PW laser (10^20 W/cm^2, 25 fs) with a 5 μm 13C target and a 4He plasma target at density 5 × 10^20 cm^-3, thickness 100 μm, and temperature 100 eV, the total neutron events per laser pulse are 24.5 × 10^4 for the plasma target versus 21.6 × 10^4 for the gas target.
- For the same TNSA parameters but with a helium density of 10^21 cm^-3, the neutron events are 51.6 × 10^4 for the plasma target and 43.1 × 10^4 for the gas target, a difference of approximately 20%.
- For a 1 PW laser (10^19 W/cm^2, 250 fs) with a 5 μm 13C target and the same helium target (5 × 10^20 cm^-3, 100 μm, 100 eV), the neutron events are 2.75 × 10^4 for the plasma target versus 2.53 × 10^4 for the gas target.
- The screening enhancement factor g_scr for the reaction at a helium density of 5 × 10^20 cm^-3 and temperature 100 eV is significantly larger than unity, but approaches unity for temperatures around 1 keV.

### Context
This work extends the proposal for colliding laser-generated plasma plumes for nuclear physics studies (Mascali 2010, Labaune 2013) and the ELI-NP Technical Design Report (Roth 2015) by specifically addressing the determination of plasma screening effects. It provides a theoretical framework to experimentally test the Salpeter weak-screening formula (Salpeter 1954) for thermonuclear reactions, which has lacked experimental verification in contrast to laboratory electron screening experiments (Adelberger 2011, Assenbaum 1987). The results enable future experiments at ELI-NP to directly measure screening enhancement factors for astrophysically relevant reactions, potentially advancing nuclear astrophysics by providing data on reaction cross-sections in stellar-like plasma conditions.
