# Angular-momentum projection in coupled-cluster theory: structure of $^{34}$Mg

> Single-reference coupled-cluster theory is an accurate and affordable computational method for the nuclear many-body problem. For open-shell nuclei, the reference state typically breaks rotational invariance and angular momentum must be restored as a good quantum number. We perform angular-momentum projection after variation and employ the disentangled coupled-cluster formalism and a Hermitian approach. We compare our results with benchmarks for $^8$Be and $^{20}$Ne using a two-nucleon interaction from chiral effective field theory and for $pf$-shell nuclei within the traditional shell model. We compute the rotational band in the exotic nucleus $^{34}$Mg and find agreement with data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that angular-momentum projection after variation in coupled-cluster theory, using both a non-Hermitian disentangled formalism and a Hermitian approach, can accurately describe rotational bands in open-shell nuclei, but the static correlation energy from projection scales as \(A^{-\eta}\) with \(\eta \approx 1/6\) and thus becomes small for heavy nuclei, meaning size-extensive methods are not required for this contribution.

## Method

The authors start from a deformed, axially symmetric Hartree-Fock reference state that breaks rotational symmetry, then perform coupled-cluster singles and doubles (CCSD) calculations to capture dynamical correlations. Angular-momentum projection is applied after variation using either a non-Hermitian disentangled cluster formalism (truncating the disentangled operator \(W\) at the two-body level) or a Hermitian approach with singles quadratic doubles (SQD) or singles linear doubles (SLD) approximations to the coupled-cluster wavefunction. The projected energy is computed by integrating norm and Hamiltonian kernels over the rotation angle \(\beta\) using the projection operator \(P_J = \frac{1}{2}\int_0^\pi d\beta \sin\beta \, d^J_{00}(\beta) R(\beta)\).

## Key numbers

- For \(^8\)Be and \(^{20}\)Ne, the method is benchmarked against results using a two-nucleon interaction from chiral effective field theory; the rotational band of \(^{34}\)Mg is computed and found to agree with experimental data.
- The static correlation energy \(\delta E\) from angular-momentum projection scales as \(\delta E \propto A^{-\eta}\) with \(\eta \approx 1/6\), estimated from \(\delta E \sim \langle J^2\rangle/\Theta\) where the moment of inertia \(\Theta \sim A^{5/3}\) and \(\langle J^2\rangle \sim A^{3/2}\).
- The coupled-cluster singles and doubles (CCSD) approximation yields about 90% of the correlation energy for closed-shell nuclei; inclusion of triples yields about 98%.

## Context

This work extends the disentangled cluster formalism for angular-momentum projection developed by Qiu et al. (2017, 2018, 2019) and applies it to nuclear structure, building on prior coupled-cluster studies of open-shell nuclei by Novario et al. (2020). It enables ab initio computation of rotational bands in medium-mass exotic nuclei such as \(^{34}\)Mg, complementing shell-model approaches for \(pf\)-shell nuclei and providing a path toward heavier deformed nuclei where symmetry-adapted methods (Dytrych et al., 2013, 2020) or effective theories (Papenbrock et al., 2011, 2020) have been used.
