# Universal Bound States of Two Particles in Mixed Dimensions or Near a Mirror

> Some novel TWO-body effects analogous to the well-known THREE-body Efimov effect are predicted. In the systems considered, particle A is constrained on a TRUNCATED or BENT one-dimensional line or two-dimensional plane, or on one side of a flat mirror in three dimensions (3D). The constraining potential is fine-tuned such that particle A's ground state wave function is a constant in the region in which it is constrained. Particle B moves in 3D and interacts with particle A resonantly. An infinite sequence of giant two-body bound states are found in each case.

## Key claim

An infinite sequence of giant two-body bound states, analogous to the three-body Efimov effect, exists for two particles with short-range interactions when one particle is constrained to a truncated or bent one-dimensional line, a truncated or bent two-dimensional plane, or one side of a flat mirror, provided the constraining potential is fine-tuned so that the isolated particle's ground state wave function is a constant in the allowed region and the two-body scattering length is infinite.

## Method

The paper considers seven distinct scenarios where particle A is constrained to a half-infinite 1D ray (Scenario 1), a V-shaped bent line (Scenario 2), a half-infinite 2D sheet (Scenario 3), a bent 2D plane (Scenario 4), one side of a flat mirror in 3D (Scenario 5), or both particles are constrained to the same side of a mirror with either Neumann (Scenario 6) or Dirichlet (Scenario 7) boundary conditions for particle B. The interaction is replaced by a Bethe-Peierls boundary condition with infinite scattering length, and the Schrödinger equation is solved analytically to find bound state wave functions and energies, with the condition that the wave function satisfies a Neumann boundary condition at the vertex, edge, or mirror surface for particle A.

## Key numbers

* For two identical bosons in the Type-IIN mirror effect (Scenario 6), the scaling factor is \(\lambda = 78.4\) (from \(s_0 = 0.7202\)).
* For the three-body Efimov effect of three identical bosons, the scaling factor is \(22.7\) (provided for comparison).
* For a \(^6\)Li and \(^{133}\)Cs mixture (mass ratio \(m_A/m_B\) with A = \(^6\)Li, B = \(^{133}\)Cs), the scaling factor \(\lambda = 9.8\) for the one-leg vertex effect, one-sheet edge effect, and Type-I mirror effect; \(\lambda = 9.1\) for the Type-IIN mirror effect; and \(\lambda = 10.7\) for the Type-IID mirror effect.
* In Scenario 7 (Type-IID mirror effect), the parameter \(s_0\) vanishes at a critical mass ratio \(u_c \approx 0.195\), and no real solution exists for \(m_A/m_B > u_c\), limiting the effect to mass ratios below this value.
* The binding energies form a geometric sequence \(E = -\hbar^2 \kappa_n^2 / 2m_B\) with \(\kappa_n = \kappa_0 \lambda^{-n}\) (\(n = 0, 1, 2, \dots\)), and the linear sizes of the bound states form a geometric sequence with common ratio \(\lambda = e^{\pi/s_0}\).

## Context

This work extends the Efimov effect (Efimov, 1970; Efimov, 1971) from three-body systems to two-body systems by exploiting mixed-dimensional geometries and fine-tuned confining potentials. It contrasts with prior studies of two particles near a hard wall with Dirichlet boundary conditions (bouncing, 2010), which found no infinite sequence of bound states, and generalizes earlier work on mixed-dimensional few-body systems (MixedD, 2008; MixedD, 2010; MixedD, 2011). The predicted bound states are expected to be free from three-body recombination and potentially much longer lived than Efimov trimers created experimentally (EfimovExperiment, 2006; EfimovExperiment, 2008; EfimovExperiment, 2009; EfimovExperiment, 2010), and the spatial mobility of dimers in Scenarios 3-7 may allow easy transport and manipulation.
