## Key claim

The moment products \(S\sigma\) and \(\kappa\sigma^2\) from net-proton multiplicity distributions measured by STAR in Au+Au collisions show finite-size scaling patterns consistent with a second-order phase transition at a critical end point (CEP), but only when using the specific CEP location (\(T^{\text{cep}} \sim 165\) MeV, \(\mu_B^{\text{cep}} \sim 95\) MeV) and critical exponent (\(\nu \sim 0.66\)) previously extracted from HBT radii measurements; test values placing the CEP at higher \(\mu_B\) break the scaling.

## Method

The authors performed finite-size scaling (FSS) tests on centrality-dependent STAR data for \(S\sigma\) and \(\kappa\sigma^2\) (related to baryon number susceptibility ratios \(\chi_B^{(3)}/\chi_B^{(2)}\) and \(\chi_B^{(4)}/\chi_B^{(2)}\)) across \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7 - 200\) GeV. They used a Monte Carlo Glauber calculation to extract the characteristic transverse size \(\bar{R} = L\) for each centrality, then tested for data collapse onto a single scaling function using Eq. (8) with the previously reported values \(T^{\text{cep}} \sim 165\) MeV, \(\mu_B^{\text{cep}} \sim 95\) MeV, and \(\nu \sim 0.66\), assuming the exponent \((m-n)\delta\beta/\nu \sim 0\) due to finite-time effects.

## Key numbers

- \(T^{\text{cep}} \sim 165\) MeV and \(\mu_B^{\text{cep}} \sim 95\) MeV: the CEP location used for successful scaling, taken from Lacey (2014, 2015).
- \(\nu \sim 0.66\): the critical exponent used for scaling, from Lacey (2014, 2015).
- Systematic uncertainties for the STAR fluctuation measurements: 4% for \(\sigma\), 5% for \(S\), and 12% for \(\kappa\).
- Systematic uncertainties for \(\bar{R}\) from the Glauber model: less than 10%.
- Test values \(\acute{T}^{\text{cep}} \sim 160\) MeV and \(\acute{\mu_B}^{\text{cep}} \sim 205\) MeV (corresponding to \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \sim 20\) GeV) broke the scaling.

## Context

This work extends the finite-size scaling approach previously applied by Lacey (2014, 2015) to HBT radii (\(\Delta R^2\)) in Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions, which had indicated a CEP in the 3D Ising model universality class. Here the same CEP parameters are validated against independent net-proton fluctuation measurements from STAR (Adamczyk 2013), showing that the moment products \(S\sigma\) and \(\kappa\sigma^2\) collapse onto scaling functions consistent with a second-order transition. The results contradict arguments (e.g., Luo 2015, Karsch 2015) that the CEP lies at \(\mu_B > 200\) MeV, as test values in that range fail to produce scaling. The work enables future high-statistics studies with STAR detector upgrades to refine the CEP location and critical exponents.
