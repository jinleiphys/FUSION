# Particle removal reactions with deformed projectiles

> Single-particle removal reactions are becoming an important tool for studying radioactive nuclei. The nuclei far from stability may reveal new regions of large deformation. We discuss the influence of the projectile deformation on the stripping cross sections and longitudinal momentum distributions of the core. The Glauber formalism for describing such reactions is developed and compared with the simpler geometric approach and with existing experimental data. The most significant effects are expected for nuclei with valence spherical orbitals strongly mixed by deformation. The shape of the momentum distribution, in turn, can be used for determining the degree of deformation of the projectile.

## Key claim

The paper argues that projectile deformation significantly affects stripping cross sections and longitudinal momentum distributions of the core, but only for nuclei where valence spherical orbitals are strongly mixed by deformation; for orbitals that are pure spherical states, the deformation dependence is minimal.

## Method

The authors develop a Glauber formalism for stripping reactions that incorporates projectile deformation by fixing the orientation of the deformed core during the collision (adiabatic approximation) and using Nilsson model single-particle wave functions quantized on the intrinsic symmetry axis. They also present a simpler geometric approach based on a "black disc" approximation and the Fourier transform of the valence nucleon wave function restricted to the region overlapping with the target. Numerical calculations are performed for the reaction ⁹Be(²⁵Al,²⁴Mg)X using both approaches, with the core profile function computed as a convolution of a nucleon-target optical potential with the deformed core density.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction ⁹Be(²⁵Al,²⁴Mg)X, the valence proton in ²⁵Al occupies the Nilsson orbital [202 5/2], which is generated by the spherical j=5/2, m_j=5/2 basis function and is not mixed by the core deformed mean field with other spherical orbitals from the sd-shell.
- The authors state that deformation changes the total (angle-integrated) reaction cross sections by less than 1%, consistent with the conclusion of Christley and Tostevin (1998).
- The binding energies considered for tightly bound projectiles are ≥5 MeV, below which the Nilsson Hamiltonian results should be considered preliminary estimates.
- The effective core radius is given by R_c = r_0 A_c^(1/3), and the minimum impact parameter is b_min = R_c + R_T, where R_T is the effective target radius.

## Context

This work extends the Glauber formalism for stripping reactions, previously applied to spherical nuclei by Hansen (1996) and Hencken (1996), to include projectile deformation. It builds on the adiabatic approximation developed by Drozdov (1955) and Inopin (1956) for scattering from deformed targets, and on the combined coupled-channel and Glauber treatment of Abgrall et al. (1979) for proton scattering. The paper enables the interpretation of momentum distributions from stripping reactions on deformed projectiles, such as neutron-rich Mg isotopes in the "island of inversion" region (Wildenthal 1980), where deformation is expected to be large and spectroscopic information is scarce.
