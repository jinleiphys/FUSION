# The puzzle of anomalously large isospin violations in $╬Ę(1405/1475)\to 3ŽĆ$

> The BES-III Collaboration recently report the observation of anomalously large isospin violations in $J/Žł\to ╬│╬Ę(1405/1475) \to ╬│ŽĆ^0 f_0(980)\to ╬│+3ŽĆ$, where the $f_0(980)$ in the $ŽĆŽĆ$ invariant mass spectrum appears to be much narrower ($\sim$ 10 MeV) than the peak width ($\sim$50 MeV) measured in other processes. We show that a mechanism, named as triangle singularity (TS), can produce a narrow enhancement between the charged and neutral $K\bar{K}$ thresholds, i.e., $2m_{K^\pm}\sim 2m_{K^0}$. It can also lead to different invariant mass spectra for $╬Ę(1405/1475)\to a_0(980)ŽĆ$ and $K\bar{K}^*+c.c.$, which can possibly explain the long-standing puzzle about the need for two close states $╬Ę(1405)$ and $╬Ę(1475)$ in $╬ĘŽĆŽĆ$ and $K\bar{K}ŽĆ$, respectively. The TS could be a key to our understanding of the nature of $╬Ę(1405/1475)$ and advance our knowledge about the mixing between $a_0(980)$ and $f_0(980)$.

## Key claim

The anomalously large isospin violations observed by BES-III in $J/\psi\to \gamma\eta(1405/1475) \to \gamma+3\pi$ can be explained by a triangle singularity (TS) mechanism, rather than requiring two separate states $\eta(1405)$ and $\eta(1475)$; the authors show that a single $0^{-+}$ state (denoted $\eta(1440)$) suffices when the TS mechanism is included.

## Method

The authors performed an effective Lagrangian analysis of three decay transitions: $\eta(1440)\to K\bar{K}\pi$, $\eta\pi\pi$, and $3\pi$, using VVP, VPP, and SPP couplings with SU(3) flavor symmetry relations. They fitted the $K\bar{K}\pi$ invariant mass spectrum from DM2, MARK III, and BES-I data to fix the $\eta(1440)$ mass and coupling $g_{\eta(1440)K^*\bar{K}}$, then applied these parameters to predict the $\pi^+\pi^-$ and $\eta\pi^0\pi^0$ invariant mass spectra via the triangle singularity loop integrals, comparing constant-width and energy-dependent-width treatments for the $\eta(1440)$.

## Key numbers

- The $f_0(980)$ peak width observed by BES-III in the $\pi\pi$ invariant mass spectrum is $\sim 10$ MeV, compared to the typical peak width of $\sim 50$ MeV measured in other processes.
- Best fit for the $K\bar{K}\pi$ invariant mass spectrum with a constant width gives $\Gamma_{\eta(1440)}=67$ MeV at $m_{\eta(1440)}=1.42$ GeV; with an energy-dependent width, $\Gamma_{\eta(1440)}(\sqrt{s_0})=166$ MeV at $\sqrt{s_0}=1.42$ GeV and $m_{\eta(1440)}=1.55$ GeV.
- The peak position in $K\bar{K}\pi$ is about $1.44$ GeV, while the TS mechanism shifts the peak in $\eta\pi^0\pi^0$ to about $1.415$ GeV.
- The $a_0(980)$-$f_0(980)$ mixing contribution to $J/\psi \to \gamma\eta(1405/1475) \to \gamma+3\pi$ is estimated at $\sim 10^{-6}$, nearly one order of magnitude smaller than the experimental data of $10^{-5}$.
- Coupling constants used: $g_{aK\bar{K}}=3.33$ GeV, $g_{a\eta\pi}=2.45$ GeV; for $f_0(980)$, KLOE values $g_{fK\bar{K}}=5.92$ GeV and $g_{f\pi\pi}=2.09$ GeV, BES values $g_{fK\bar{K}}=4.18$ GeV and $g_{f\pi\pi}=1.66$ GeV; $g^c_{K^*K\pi}=3.268$ for charged channel, $g^n_{K^*K\pi}=3.208$ for neutral channel.

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing puzzle of whether $\eta(1405)$ and $\eta(1475)$ are two separate states or a single state observed in different decay modes, as listed by the Particle Data Group (2010) and reviewed by Klempt (2007). The triangle singularity mechanism provides a natural explanation for the anomalously large isospin violations recently reported by BES-III (2011) and the different mass peaks seen in $K\bar{K}\pi$ versus $\eta\pi\pi$ channels, without requiring two distinct pseudoscalar states. The results enable future BES-III studies of the isoscalar pseudoscalar spectrum and could clarify the search for the lightest pseudoscalar glueball candidate, which lattice QCD (Bali, 1993) predicts to be heavier than $\eta(1440)$.
