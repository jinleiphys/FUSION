# The Core of $^{25}$F in the Rotational Model

> In a recent experiment, carried out at RIBF/RIKEN, the $^{25}$F$(p,2p)$$^{24}$O reaction was studied at 270 MeV/A in inverse kinematics. Derived spectroscopic factors suggest that the effective core of $^{25}$F significantly differs from a free $^{24}$O nucleus. We interpret these results within the Particle-Rotor Model and show that the experimental level scheme of $^{25}$F can be understood in the rotation-aligned coupling scheme, with its $5/2^+_1$ ground state as the band-head of a decoupled band. The excitation energies of the observed $1/2_1^+$ and $9/2_1^+$ states correlate strongly with the rotational energy of the effective core, seen by the odd proton, and allow us to estimate its $2^+$ energy at $\approx$ 3.2 MeV and a moderate quadrupole deformation, $ε_2 \approx 0.15$. The measured fragmentation of the $πd_{5/2}$ single-particle strength is discussed and some further experiments suggested.

## Key claim

The effective core of \(^{25}\)F is not a free \(^{24}\)O nucleus but rather a slightly deformed rotor with a \(2^+\) energy of approximately 3.2 MeV and quadrupole deformation \(\epsilon_2 \approx 0.15\), as inferred from the rotation-aligned coupling scheme of the Particle-Rotor Model; the abstract's claim that the core "significantly differs" from free \(^{24}\)O is hedged by the model-dependent interpretation that the core is deformed and rotating, not that the core is a different nucleus.

## Method

The authors applied the Particle-Rotor Model (PRM) to the experimental level scheme of \(^{25}\)F, treating the odd proton as a \(d_{5/2}\) Nilsson multiplet coupled to a deformed core. They adjusted the core's \(2^+\) energy and deformation to reproduce the observed energies of the \(1/2_1^+\) and \(9/2_1^+\) states in \(^{25}\)F, and calculated spectroscopic factors for the \(^{25}\)F\((p,2p)^{24}\)O reaction using the PRM formalism with explicit core overlap. The calculations were compared to experimental data from Tang et al. (2020) and shell-model results using the SDPF-MU interaction.

## Key numbers

- The \(2^+\) energy of the effective core in \(^{25}\)F is estimated at \(\approx\) 3.2 MeV.
- The quadrupole deformation of the effective core is \(\epsilon_2 \approx 0.15\).
- The experimental spectroscopic factor for the ground state of \(^{24}\)O is 0.36(13); for the excited state of \(^{24}\)O it is 0.65(25).
- The PRM calculation without core overlap (PRM1) gives spectroscopic factors of 0.85 (ground) and 0.15 (excited); with core overlap (PRM2) gives 0.56 (ground) and 0.44 (excited).
- The core overlap factor used in PRM2 is \(\langle\phi_f|\phi_i\rangle \approx 0.81\).
- The free \(^{24}\)O nucleus has a \(2^+\) energy of 4.7 \(\pm\) 0.1 MeV and a \(B(E2)\) of approximately 1/2 Weisskopf units (from Tshoo et al., 2012).

## Context

This work extends the rotational model interpretation previously applied by Macchiavelli et al. to \(^{29}\)F, and contrasts with the doubly-magic interpretation of \(^{24}\)O supported by studies of neutron decay (Calem et al., 2009) and one-neutron removal (Ritu et al., 2009). The results challenge the tensor interaction in widely used effective interactions, which Tang et al. (2020) found too weak to reproduce the observed spectroscopic factors. The PRM description enables predictions for electromagnetic observables (magnetic and quadrupole moments, \(B(E2)\) values) and suggests further experiments such as proton knockout from \(^{26}\)Ne or \((d,^3He)\) reactions.
