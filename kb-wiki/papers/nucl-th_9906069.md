# Statistical Properties of Charmonium Spectrum and a New Mechanism of J/ψSuppression

> The statistical properties of Charmonium energy spectrum determined by the Bethe-Salpeter equation are investigated. It is found that the regular motion of the $c\bar{c}$ system can be expected at a small value of color screening mass but the chaotic motion at a large one. It is shown that the level mixing due to color screening serves as a new mechanism resulting in the J/$ψ$ suppression. Moreover, this kind of suppression can occur before the color screening mass reaches its critical value for J/$ψ$ dissociation. It implies that a strong J/$ψ$ suppression is possible in the absence of dissociation of J/$ψ$.

# Statistical Properties of Charmonium Spectrum and a New Mechanism of J/ψ Suppression

## Key claim
The authors propose that level mixing induced by color screening, rather than direct dissociation, can cause strong J/ψ suppression at screening masses below the critical value for dissociation (μ_c = 0.90 GeV), with significant suppression already appearing at μ = 0.60 GeV.

## Method
The authors solve the three-dimensional spin-independent reduced Bethe-Salpeter equation with a color-screened quark-antiquark potential in momentum space, using parameters fitted to experimental 1S, 2S, and 3S charmonium states. They then analyze the statistical properties of the resulting energy spectrum using random matrix theory, computing the nearest neighbor level spacing distribution P(s) and spectral rigidity Δ₃(L) for 200 s-wave eigenstates at various color screening masses μ, and examine the mixing of zero-temperature eigenfunctions into finite-temperature states.

## Key numbers
- String tension σ = 0.22 GeV², charm quark mass m_c = 1.474 GeV, effective coupling α_s(c\bar{c}) = 0.47, zero-temperature screening mass μ₀ = 0.06 GeV
- Critical screening mass for J/ψ dissociation: μ_c = 0.90 GeV
- Brody parameter ω = 0.22, 0.51, 0.89 for μ = 0.06, 0.20, 0.60 GeV respectively, indicating transition from regular (ω≈0) to chaotic (ω≈1) motion
- Probability for zero-temperature J/ψ state to appear in finite-temperature J/ψ state: approximately 90% at μ = 0.10 GeV, 70% at μ = 0.20 GeV, and 10% at μ = 0.60 GeV
- Strong suppression critical screening mass lowered from 0.90 GeV (dissociation) to 0.60 GeV (level mixing mechanism)

## Context
This work extends the study of charmonium suppression beyond the standard deconfinement dissociation mechanism predicted by Matsui and Satz (1986) and observed by the NA38 collaboration, and addresses the anomalously strong J/ψ suppression reported by the NA50 collaboration. The authors apply random matrix theory, originally developed for complex nuclear spectra by Wigner (1950s) and later applied to quantum field theory and disordered systems, to the charmonium spectrum for the first time. The results suggest that level mixing due to color screening provides an alternative mechanism for J/ψ suppression that can occur at lower temperatures than dissociation, potentially explaining experimental observations without requiring full quark-gluon plasma formation.
