# Quark-Gluon Plasma Fireball

> Lattice-QCD results provide an opportunity to model, and extrapolate to finite baryon density, the properties of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). Upon fixing the scale of the thermal coupling constant and vacuum energy to the lattice data, the properties of resulting QGP equations of state (EoS) are developed. We show that the physical properties of the dense matter fireball formed in heavy ion collision experiments at CERN-SPS are well described by the QGP-EoS we presented. We also estimate the properties of the fireball formed in early stages of nuclear collision, and argue that QGP formation must be expected down to 40A GeV in central Pb--Pb interactions.

# Quark-Gluon Plasma Fireball

## Key claim

The authors show that the physical properties of the dense matter fireball formed in heavy ion collisions at CERN-SPS are well described by their QGP equations of state, which are calibrated to lattice-QCD results. They argue that QGP formation must be expected down to 40A GeV in central Pb-Pb interactions, but note that when the energy and baryon number deposited in the initial fireball drops below 20% (due to large impact parameter or small nuclei), QGP formation becomes improbable.

## Method

The authors develop semi-phenomenological QGP equations of state by fixing the scale of the thermal coupling constant and vacuum energy density to reproduce lattice-QCD results for pressure and energy density at zero baryon chemical potential. They then extrapolate these equations of state to finite baryon density and compare the resulting QGP properties (temperature, baryo-chemical potential, energy per baryon, entropy per baryon) to the fireball properties extracted from their earlier statistical analysis of hadron abundances and spectra in Pb-Pb collisions at 158A GeV. They also explore initial thermal conditions by considering the chemical equilibration of quarks and gluons in the fireball.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum energy density: B = 0.19 GeV/fm^3, consistent with a lower bound B >= 0.17 GeV/fm^3 from fireball dynamics
- Chemical freeze-out temperature from hadron analysis: T_f = 142 +/- 3 MeV (unconstrained fit)
- Energy per baryon at freeze-out: E_f/B = 7.8 +/- 0.5 GeV (intrinsic energy in flowing frame)
- Entropy per baryon at freeze-out: S_f/B = 42 +/- 3
- Baryo-chemical potential at freeze-out: mu_b = 204 +/- 10 MeV (derived from lambda_q = 1.61 +/- 0.02)
- Initial energy per baryon before expansion: E^0/b = 9.3 GeV (after correcting for collective flow with gamma = 1.19)
- Energy density 3 GeV/fm^3 corresponds to temperature 212 MeV in equilibrated fireball
- Initial temperature of equilibrated glue phase: T ~ 270 MeV; full chemical equilibrium would give T >= 230 MeV
- Collision energy threshold for QGP formation: down to 40A GeV in central Pb-Pb interactions

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior statistical analysis of hadron production in Pb-Pb collisions at CERN-SPS (Letessier and Rafelski, 2000; 1999) by developing QGP equations of state that reproduce lattice-QCD results from Karsch (2000). The approach contrasts with higher-order perturbative expansions (Zhai and Kastening, 1995; Andersen, 1999) which the authors find do not converge well at the temperatures of interest. The paper enables future study of the sudden hadronization mechanism and mechanical instability of the super-cooled fireball, which the authors have explored in related work (Rafelski, 2000).
