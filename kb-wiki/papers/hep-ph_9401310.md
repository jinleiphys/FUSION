# QCD Phenomenology based on a Chiral Effective Lagrangian

> We review the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) approach to the dynamical breaking of chiral symmetry in Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). The subjects treated in Part I include the vacuum structure of QCD, mass spectra and coupling constants of hadrons, flavor mixing in mesons, the violation of the OZI rule in baryons, and the validity of the chiral perturbation in QCD. A subtle interplay between the axial anomaly and the current-quark masses is shown to play important roles, and a realistic evaluation of the strangeness and heavy quark contents of hadrons is given. In Part II, the NJL model is applied to the system at finite temperature ($T$) and density ($ρ$) relevant to the early universe, interior of the neutron stars and the ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. The subjects treated here include the quark condensates in the medium, meson properties at finite $T$ ($ρ$) and their experimental implications. A special attention is paid to fluctuation phenomena near the critical temperature. (to be published in Physics Reports)

## Key claim

The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model provides a unified qualitative description of non-perturbative QCD phenomena at low energies through the interplay of three ingredients: dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, the U_A(1) anomaly, and explicit symmetry breaking from current quark masses; however, the authors emphasize this is an effective theory that is "not equivalent with the whole QCD dynamics" and is intended to develop physical ideas rather than give precise quantitative descriptions at the few-percent level.

## Method

The authors construct a NJL-type four-fermion interaction Lagrangian with U_L(3) ⊗ U_R(3) chiral invariance to describe dynamical breaking of chiral symmetry (DBCS), supplemented by a Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft (KMT) six-fermion determinantal interaction to simulate the U_A(1) anomaly and explicit current-quark mass terms. They solve the model in the mean-field approximation to obtain the gap equation for dynamical quark masses, then compute meson spectra as collective excitations via dispersion equations from the residual interaction, and describe baryons using a constituent quark model with the dynamical masses identified as constituent quark masses.

- Pion decay constant: f_π = 93 MeV
- Quark condensate: ⟨ūu⟩ ≈ ⟨dd̄⟩ ≈ [-(225 ± 25) MeV]^3 at μ² = 1 GeV

- Chiral restoration critical temperature: T_c ~ (150-200) MeV in the chiral limit
- Scalar meson mass from Nambu relation: m_σ ≈ 2M ≈ 700 MeV
- QCD scale parameters: Λ^{(3)}_{MS̄} = (290 ± 30) MeV, Λ^{(4)}_{MS̄} = (220 ± 90) MeV, Λ^{(5)}_{MS̄} = (140 ± 60) MeV

## Context

This review extends the earlier work of Nambu and Jona-Lasinio (1961) on dynamical symmetry breaking and applies it systematically to QCD phenomenology, unifying the description of the vacuum structure, hadron spectra, and the OZI rule. The approach provides an intermediate theory between the fundamental QCD Lagrangian and low-energy chiral Lagrangians written in terms of meson fields, enabling calculations of observables such as the strangeness content of the nucleon and meson properties at finite temperature and density that are difficult to obtain from lattice QCD or other methods. The work enables future studies of soft modes near the chiral transition, quark-number susceptibility at high temperature, and experimental signatures in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions.
