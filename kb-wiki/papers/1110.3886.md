# Beta Decays of Isotones with Neutron Magic Number of N=126 and R-process Nucleosynthesis

> Beta decays of the isotones with N=126 are studied by shell model calculations taking into account both the Gamow-Teller (GT) and first-forbidden (FF) transitions. The FF transitions are found to be important to reduce the half-lives, by nearly twice to several times, from those by the GT contributions only. Possible implications of the short half-lives of the waiting point nuclei on the r-process nucleosynthesis during the supernova explosions are discussed. A slight shift of the third peak of the element abundances in the r-process toward higher mass region is found.

## Key claim

First-forbidden (FF) transitions reduce the beta-decay half-lives of N=126 isotones by factors of 1.4 to 5.7 compared to Gamow-Teller (GT) contributions alone, but the reduction is not as large as predicted in some prior CQRPA calculations (Bor03). The shorter half-lives produce a slight shift of the third r-process abundance peak toward higher mass.

## Method

Shell model calculations were performed for N=126 isotones with proton number Z=64 to 73, using a model space of proton holes in the 0h11/2, 1d3/2, and 2s1/2 orbits outside a 208Pb core and neutron orbits in the 0h9/2, 1f5/2,7/2, 2p1/2,3/2, and 0i13/2 shells. Both GT and FF (0-, 1-, 2-) transition strengths were computed with the quenching factor gA/gA^free = 0.7 for 1+, 1-, and 2- transitions, and an enhancement factor epsilon = 2.0 for the M0^T matrix element in 0- transitions. Half-lives were obtained from the decay rates using the shape factor formalism of Warburton (WB) and Schopper.

## Key numbers

- For Z=64 (Gd): GT half-life = 5.76 ms; GT+FF half-life = 4.02 ms
- For Z=68 (Er): GT half-life = 29.31 ms; GT+FF half-life = 18.11 ms
- For Z=70 (Yb): GT half-life = 102.49 ms; GT+FF half-life = 44.18 ms
- For Z=72 (Hf): GT half-life = 504.79 ms; GT+FF half-life = 129.65 ms
- For Z=73 (Ta): GT half-life = 1584.4 ms; GT+FF half-life = 278.88 ms
- Summed GT strengths: 14.4 (Z=64), 14.6 (Z=66), 11.7 (Z=68), 8.5 (Z=70), 5.6 (Z=72)
- Summed spin-dipole strengths B(SD1): 55.5 fm^2 (Z=64), 49.2 fm^2 (Z=66), 48.4 fm^2 (Z=68), 40.1 fm^2 (Z=70), 35.1 fm^2 (Z=72)
- The approximation mu1 = lambda2 = 1 changes half-lives by only within 0.3%
- For 204Pt (Z=78): experimental half-life = 10.3 +/- 1.4 s; calculated values range from 10.9 s to 31.8 s depending on quenching factors

## Context

This work extends shell model studies of N=126 beta decays beyond prior GT-only calculations by Langanke (Lang) and Martinez-Pinedo (GLM), and provides a comparison with CQRPA results from Borzov (Bor03) and the FRDM database of Moller (Moll). The shorter half-lives have implications for r-process nucleosynthesis in supernova neutrino-driven winds, particularly for the waiting-point nuclei near the third abundance peak. The results call for experimental measurements of masses and Q-values for these nuclei, which are currently in a "blank spot" region with no data.
