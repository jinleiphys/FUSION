# Model Dependence of the Properties of S11 Baryon Resonances

> The properties of baryon resonances are extracted from a complicated process of fitting sophisticated, empirical models to data. The reliability of this process comes from the quality of data and the robustness of the models employed. With the large of amount of data coming from recent experiments, this is an excellent time for a study of the model dependence of this extraction process. A test case is chosen where many theoretical details of the model are required, the S11 partial wave. The properties of the two lowest N* resonances in this partial wave are determined using various models of the resonant and non-resonant amplitudes.

## Key claim

The properties of the two lowest S11 baryon resonances, particularly their masses, widths, and branching fractions, show significant variation depending on whether the extraction uses a CMB model (which enforces analyticity and full multichannel unitarity), a K-matrix model (which enforces unitarity but not analyticity), or a Breit-Wigner model (which enforces neither), with the largest discrepancies appearing for the S11(1650) width and the S11(1535) piN branching fraction.

## Method

The authors fit the T-matrix elements for piN to piN and piN to etaN scattering in the S11 partial wave using three classes of models: the Carnegie-Mellon Berkeley (CMB) model with dressed propagators and full analyticity, a K-matrix model with real symmetric K-matrix, and Breit-Wigner models with both non-relativistic and relativistic energy-dependent widths. For each class, two treatments of non-resonant background were used: a "distant poles" parameterization with three background poles, and a polynomial expansion in the variable x_c = sqrt(s - s_th,c), yielding a total of eight different models fitted to the same partial-wave amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- S11(1535) mass: ranges from 1526 +/- 2 MeV (CMB with polynomial background) to 1558 +/- 1 MeV (Breit-Wigner relativistic with distant poles), compared to PDG range 1520-1555 MeV
- S11(1535) width: ranges from 112 +/- 6 MeV (CMB with polynomial background) to 143 +/- 4 MeV (Breit-Wigner relativistic with distant poles), compared to PDG range 100-200 MeV
- S11(1535) piN branching fraction: ranges from 29 +/- 1% (CMB with distant poles) to 67 +/- 1% (Breit-Wigner relativistic with distant poles), compared to PDG range 35-55%
- S11(1650) mass: ranges from 1637 +/- 2 MeV (Breit-Wigner relativistic with distant poles) to 1688 +/- 2 MeV (CMB with polynomial background), compared to PDG range 1640-1680 MeV
- S11(1650) width: ranges from 109 +/- 5 MeV (both Breit-Wigner models with polynomial background) to 193 +/- 6 MeV (CMB with polynomial background), compared to PDG range 145-190 MeV
- S11(1650) piN branching fraction: ranges from 51 +/- 1% (both Breit-Wigner models with polynomial background) to 80 +/- 1% (CMB with distant poles), compared to PDG range 55-90%
- chi-squared per data point for distant-poles fits: 3.8 (CMB), 3.7 (K-matrix), 1.5 (Breit-Wigner non-relativistic), 1.9 (Breit-Wigner relativistic)
- chi-squared per data point for polynomial-background fits: 5.0 (CMB), 3.9 (K-matrix), 5.0 (both Breit-Wigner models)

## Context

This work extends the CMB model of Cutkosky (1980) and Vrana (2000) and the K-matrix approach of Moorhouse (1979) and Chung (1995) by systematically comparing resonance parameters extracted from the same partial-wave data using models with different levels of theoretical constraint. The study was motivated by the wide variation in S11 resonance parameters reported in the literature, ranging from analyses of piN elastic scattering (VPI group, 1990s; Karlsruhe-Helsinki group, 1980) giving a width of about 120 MeV for S11(1535) to threshold eta photoproduction data (Krusche and Mukhopadhyay, 1990s) giving a width of about 210 MeV. The results show that Breit-Wigner models can produce good chi-squared values but yield resonance parameters that differ substantially from those obtained with the more theoretically constrained CMB model, particularly for the piN branching fraction of S11(1535) and the width of S11(1650). This work enables future studies to assess which resonance parameters are robust enough to compare with quark model predictions (Karl, 1980) or lattice QCD calculations (quenched calculations from 2000-2003) for understanding the microscopic structure of these states.
