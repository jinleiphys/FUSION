# Non-Perturbative Quark-Antiquark Production From a Constant Chromo-Electric Field via the Schwinger Mechanism

> We obtain an exact result for the non-perturbative quark (antiquark) production rate and its p_T distribution from a constant SU(3) chromo-electric field E^a with arbitary color index $a$ by directly evaluating the path integral. Unlike the WKB tunneling result, which depends only on one gauge invariant quantity |E|, the strength of the chromo-electric field, we find that the exact result for the p_T distribution for quark (antiquark) production rate depends on two independent Casimir (gauge) invariants, E^aE^a and [d_{abc}E^aE^bE^c]^2.

## Key claim

The exact non-perturbative quark-antiquark production rate and its transverse momentum distribution from a constant SU(3) chromo-electric field depend on two independent Casimir (gauge) invariants, \(E^aE^a\) and \([d_{abc}E^aE^bE^c]^2\), unlike the WKB tunneling result which depends only on the single gauge-invariant field strength \(|E|\).

## Method

The author directly evaluates the path integral for the one-loop effective action of quarks in a constant SU(3) chromo-electric background field \(E^a\) with arbitrary color index \(a\), using the background field method of QCD. The calculation diagonalizes the matrix \(\Lambda_{ij} = T^a_{ij}E^a\) in the fundamental representation of SU(3) to obtain its three eigenvalues, then evaluates the trace over Dirac matrices and performs the proper-time integral via contour integration, using the expansion of \(1/\sinh x\) and Schwinger's analytic continuation. The resulting probability per unit time and volume is summed over the three eigenvalues and over integer \(n\) to yield the final rate.

## Key numbers

* The production rate per unit time, per unit volume, and per unit transverse momentum is given by \(\frac{dN_{q,\bar q}}{dt d^3x d^2p_T} = -\frac{1}{4\pi^3} \sum_{j=1}^3 |g\lambda_j| \ln[1 - e^{-\frac{\pi (p_T^2+m^2)}{|g\lambda_j|}}]\), where \(m\) is the quark mass.
* The eigenvalues \(\lambda_j\) are \(\lambda_1 = \sqrt{C_1/3} \cos\theta\), \(\lambda_2 = \sqrt{C_1/3} \cos(2\pi/3 - \theta)\), \(\lambda_3 = \sqrt{C_1/3} \cos(2\pi/3 + \theta)\), with \(\cos^2 3\theta = 3C_2/C_1^3\).
* The two Casimir invariants are \(C_1 = E^aE^a\) and \(C_2 = [d_{abc}E^aE^bE^c]^2\), with the constraint \(C_1^3 \ge 3C_2\).
* The WKB tunneling result (Equation 7) is \(\frac{dN_{q,\bar q}}{dt d^3x d^2p_T} = \frac{-|gE|}{4\pi^3} \ln[1 - e^{-\frac{\pi (p_T^2+m^2)}{|gE|}}]\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous derivation of non-perturbative gluon pair production from a constant SU(3) chromo-electric field (Nayak, 2005) to the case of quark-antiquark pair production. It corrects the WKB tunneling method (WKB1, WKB2) which, while valid in QED, fails in QCD due to the non-trivial color generators of SU(3); the exact result reproduces Schwinger's total production rate (Schwinger, 1951; Yildiz, 1979) upon integration over transverse momentum. The result enables more accurate modeling of hadronization at low transverse momentum in high-energy electron-positron and proton-proton collisions, as well as quark-gluon plasma formation from classical chromo fields at RHIC and the LHC.
