# Large scale shell model calculations for odd-odd $^{58-62}$Mn isotopes

> Large scale shell model calculations have been carried out for odd-odd $^{58-62}$Mn isotopes in two different model spaces. First set of calculations have been carried out in full $\it{fp}$ shell valence space with two recently derived $\it{fp}$ shell interactions namely GXPF1A and KB3G treating $^{40}$Ca as core. The second set of calculations have been performed in ${fpg_{9/2}}$ valence space with the $fpg$ interaction treating $^{48}$Ca as core and imposing a truncation by allowing up to a total of six particle excitations from the 0f$_{7/2}$ orbital to the upper $\it{fp}$ orbitals for protons and from the upper $\it{fp}$ orbitals to the 0g$_{9/2}$ orbital for neutron. For low-lying states in $^{58}$Mn, the KB3G and GXPF1A both predicts good results and for $^{60}$Mn, KB3G is much better than GXPF1A. For negative parity and high-spin positive parity states in both isotopes $fpg$ interaction is required. Experimental data on $^{62}$Mn is sparse and therefore it is not possible to make any definite conclusions. More experimental data on negative parity states is needed to ascertain the importance of 0g$_{9/2}$ and higher orbitals in neutron rich Mn isotopes.

# Large scale shell model calculations for odd-odd $^{58-62}$Mn isotopes

## Key claim
Large-scale shell model calculations in two different valence spaces show that for low-lying states in $^{58}$Mn both the KB3G and GXPF1A interactions predict good results, while for $^{60}$Mn the KB3G interaction is much better than GXPF1A; however, for negative parity and high-spin positive parity states in both isotopes the inclusion of the $0g_{9/2}$ orbital via the $fpg$ interaction is required, though the present $fpg$ interaction needs further modification as it predicts compressed negative parity levels that lie too low in energy.

## Method
The authors performed large-scale shell model calculations using the code ANTOINE for odd-odd $^{58-62}$Mn isotopes in two model spaces: a full $fp$ shell valence space (with $^{40}$Ca core) using the GXPF1A and KB3G effective interactions without truncation, and a $fpg_{9/2}$ valence space (with $^{48}$Ca core and eight frozen $0f_{7/2}$ neutrons) using the $fpg$ interaction with a truncation allowing up to six particle excitations from the $0f_{7/2}$ orbital to the upper $fp$ orbitals for protons and from the upper $fp$ orbitals to the $0g_{9/2}$ orbital for neutrons.

## Key numbers
- For $^{58}$Mn: the $fpg$ interaction predicts the ground state as $1^+$ (experimentally determined), while GXPF1A and KB3G predict $4^+$ as ground state with the first excited $1^+$ state at 51 keV and 11 keV respectively.
- For $^{58}$Mn: the first $3^+$ state is predicted at 170 keV by GXPF1A, 295 keV by KB3G, and 448 keV by $fpg$, compared to the experimental value of 170 keV.
- For $^{58}$Mn: the $5_1^+$ level is predicted at 402 keV (GXPF1A), 471 keV (KB3G), and 616 keV ($fpg$), compared to the experimental value of 448 keV.
- For $^{60}$Mn: the ground state spin $1^+$ is correctly reproduced by GXPF1A and KB3G; the $4^+$ state is at 237 keV (GXPF1A) and 223 keV (KB3G) compared to the experimental value of 271 keV.
- For $^{60}$Mn: the $5_1^+$ level is predicted at 671 keV (GXPF1A), 749 keV (KB3G), and 589 keV ($fpg$), compared to the experimental value of 726 keV.
- For $^{60}$Mn: the $7_1^+$ level is predicted at 1973 keV (GXPF1A), 2241 keV (KB3G), and 1914 keV ($fpg$), with an experimental indication at 2049 keV.
- For $^{62}$Mn: all three interactions predict $2^+$ as ground state, while experimentally the ground state spin is uncertain ($1^+$, $3^+$, or $4^+$).
- For $^{62}$Mn: the occupancy of the $g_{9/2}$ orbital for the ground state is 2.09.

## Context
This work extends previous shell model studies of neutron-rich $fp$ shell nuclei, including earlier calculations for $^{58}$Mn and $^{60}$Mn using the GXPF1 interaction by Liddick et al. (2006), and builds on experimental data from fusion-evaporation reactions by Steppenbeck et al. (2010) and multi-nucleon transfer reactions by Valiente-Dobon et al. (2008). The calculations test the suitability of the GXPF1A, KB3G, and $fpg$ interactions for describing odd-odd Mn isotopes approaching $N=40$, and the results indicate that further tuning of the $fpg$ interaction is needed to correctly reproduce negative parity states and ground state spins in more neutron-rich isotopes. The work enables future experimental searches for negative parity levels that would confirm the role of the $0g_{9/2}$ orbital in this region.
