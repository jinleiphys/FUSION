# From NS observations to nuclear matter properties: a machine learning approach

> This study is devoted to the inference problem of extracting the nuclear matter properties directly from a set of mass-radius observations. We employ Bayesian neural networks (BNNs), which is a probabilistic model capable of estimating the uncertainties associated with its predictions. To simulate different noise levels on the $M(R)$ observations, we create three different sets of mock data. Our results show BNNs as an accurate and reliable tool for predicting the nuclear matter properties whenever the true values are not completely outside the training dataset statistics, i.e., if the model is not heavily dependent on its extrapolating capacities. Using real mass-radius pulsar data, the model predicted, for instance, $L_{\text{sym}}=39.80\pm17.52 $ MeV and $K_{\text{sym}}=-101.67\pm62.86 $ MeV ($2σ$ interval). Our study provides a valuable inference framework when new NS data becomes available.

## Key claim
Bayesian neural networks can accurately predict nuclear matter properties from mass-radius observations, but only when the true values are not completely outside the training dataset statistics, meaning the model should not be heavily dependent on its extrapolating capacities.

## Method
The authors trained Bayesian neural networks on a dataset of 25,287 relativistic mean field nuclear models constrained by saturation properties, a 2 solar mass neutron star maximum, and chiral effective field theory pure neutron matter calculations. The input consisted of five simulated mass-radius observations per equation of state, with three different noise levels applied to create separate training datasets (set 0 with no noise, set 1 with sigma_M=0.136 solar masses and sigma_R=0.626 km, set 2 with sigma_M=0.271 solar masses and sigma_R=1.253 km). The BNN architecture used three hidden layers with ten sigmoid-activated neurons each and a 12-neuron linear output layer (six means and six standard deviations).

## Key numbers
- Training dataset: 25,287 nuclear models, with 22,758 used for training and 2,529 for testing
- Input vector: 10 values representing 5 mass-radius observations per equation of state
- For real pulsar data, the model predicted L_sym = 39.80 +/- 17.52 MeV and K_sym = -101.67 +/- 62.86 MeV (2-sigma interval)
- L_sym residual standard deviation: 8.77 MeV (set 0), 9.52 MeV (set 1), 9.69 MeV (set 2)
- K_0 residual standard deviation: 16.44 MeV (set 0), 17.89 MeV (set 1), 18.71 MeV (set 2)
- Q_0 residual standard deviation: 39.56 MeV (set 0), 48.66 MeV (set 1), 52.66 MeV (set 2)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous BNN approach (Carvalho 2023) that mapped neutron star observations to speed of sound and proton fraction, and complements the work of Krastev (2022, 2023) which used deep neural networks to map mass-radius curves to the equation of state and then to nuclear matter properties but lacked robust uncertainty quantification. The method provides an alternative to standard Bayesian inference approaches (Steiner 2010, Malik 2022, 2023) and Gaussian process methods (Essick 2019, Landry 2020) for extracting nuclear matter properties from observations, enabling instantaneous inference without separate Bayesian analyses for each observation set.
