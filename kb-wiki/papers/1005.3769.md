# Large amplitude behavior of the bulk viscosity of dense matter

> We study the bulk viscosity of dense matter, taking into account non-linear effects which arise in the large amplitude "supra-thermal" region where the deviation $μ_Δ$ of the chemical potentials from chemical equilibrium fulfills $μ_Δ>T$. This regime is relevant to unstable modes such as r-modes, which grow in amplitude until saturated by non-linear effects. We study the damping due to direct and modified Urca processes in hadronic matter, and due to nonleptonic weak interactions in strange quark matter. We give general results valid for an arbitrary equation of state of dense matter and find that the viscosity can be strongly enhanced by supra-thermal effects. Our study confirms previous results on quark matter and shows that the non-linear enhancement is even stronger in the case of hadronic matter. Our results can be applied to calculations of the r-mode-induced spin-down of fast-rotating neutron stars, where the spin-down time will depend on the saturation amplitude of the r-mode

## Key claim

The paper shows that the bulk viscosity of dense matter can be strongly enhanced by non-linear "supra-thermal" effects when the chemical potential deviation from equilibrium exceeds the temperature ($\mu_\Delta \gtrsim T$), and that this enhancement is even larger for hadronic matter than for quark matter, contrary to what previous work might have suggested.

## Method

The authors derive a general differential equation for the chemical potential fluctuation under oscillating compression, incorporating both linear and non-linear terms in the weak interaction equilibration rate. They solve this equation numerically for hadronic matter (direct and modified Urca processes) and strange quark matter (non-leptonic weak interactions), and also provide an analytic approximation for the quark matter case using a truncated Fourier ansatz. The results are expressed in terms of a dimensionless function $\mathcal{I}(d,f)$ that depends on two parameters: the driving term $d$ (proportional to oscillation amplitude) and the feedback term $f$ (proportional to equilibration rate divided by frequency).

## Key numbers

- For hadronic matter with modified Urca process, the weak interaction parameter $\tilde{\Gamma} = 4.68 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{MeV}^{(3-\kappa)} \left( \frac{x n}{n_0} \right)^{1/3}$ with $\kappa = 6$, and the non-linearity coefficients are $\chi_1 = 189/(367\pi^2)$, $\chi_2 = 21/(367\pi^4)$, $\chi_3 = 3/(1835\pi^6)$
- For hadronic matter with direct Urca process, $\tilde{\Gamma} = 5.24 \times 10^{-15} \, \text{MeV}^{(3-\kappa)} \left( \frac{x n}{n_0} \right)^{1/3}$ with $\kappa = 4$, and $\chi_1 = 10/(17\pi^2)$, $\chi_2 = 1/(17\pi^4)$, $\chi_3 = 0$
- For strange quark matter with non-leptonic process, $\tilde{\Gamma} = 6.59 \times 10^{-12} \, \text{MeV}^{(3-\kappa)} \left( \frac{\mu_d}{300 \, \text{MeV}} \right)^5$ with $\kappa = 2$, and $\chi_1 = 1/(4\pi^2)$, $\chi_2 = 0$, $\chi_3 = 0$
- The maximum bulk viscosity in both sub-thermal and supra-thermal regimes is $\zeta_{\text{max}} = C^2/(2\omega B)$, independent of the weak interaction process
- In the supra-thermal limit, the viscosity scales as $\zeta \sim (\Delta n_* / \bar{n}_*)^{-2N/(2N+1)}$, where $N$ is the highest power of $\mu_\Delta^2/T^2$ in the rate expansion

## Context

This work extends previous studies of bulk viscosity in dense matter, which had mostly been restricted to the sub-thermal regime ($\mu_\Delta \ll 2\pi T$), by Madsen (1992) for strange quark matter and by many others for various forms of hadronic matter (Sawyer 1989, Haensel 2000, Lindblom 2001, Jones 2001, Alford 2006). The authors provide a general formalism applicable to any equation of state, enabling calculations of r-mode saturation amplitudes in neutron stars. The results can be applied to determine the spin-down time of fast-rotating neutron stars due to r-mode instability, where the saturation amplitude depends on the non-linear enhancement of bulk viscosity.
