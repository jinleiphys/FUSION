# The photon content of the proton in the CT18 global analysis

> Recently, two photon PDF sets based on implementations of the LUX ansatz into the CT18 global analysis were released. In CT18lux, the photon PDF is calculated directly using the LUX master formula for all scales, $μ$. In an alternative realization, CT18qed, the photon PDF is initialized at the starting scale, $μ_0$, using the LUX formulation and evolved to higher scales $μ(>μ_0)$ with a combined QED+QCD kernel at $\mathcal{O}(α),~\mathcal{O}(αα_s)$ and $\mathcal{O}(α^2)$. In the small-$x$ region, the photon PDF uncertainty is mainly induced by the quark and gluon PDFs, through the perturbative DIS structure functions. In comparison, the large-$x$ photon uncertainty comes from various low-energy, nonperturbative contributions, including variations of the inelastic structure functions in the resonance and continuum regions, higher-twist and target-mass corrections, and elastic electromagnetic form factors of the proton. We take the production of doubly-charged Higgs pairs, $(H^{++}H^{--})$, as an example of scenarios beyond the Standard Model to illustrate the phenomenological implications of these photon PDFs at the LHC.

## Key claim

The CT18lux and CT18qed photon PDF sets, both based on the LUX ansatz, agree in the intermediate-\(x\) region (\(10^{-3} \lesssim x \lesssim 0.3\)) but differ at low and large \(x\); the authors recommend CT18qed with \(\mu_0 = 3\) GeV as the nominal set due to its smaller uncertainty at large \(x\), while noting that the low-\(x\) photon uncertainty is driven by quark and gluon PDFs and the large-\(x\) uncertainty by nonperturbative contributions.

## Method

The CT18lux photon PDF is computed directly from the LUX master formula at all scales \(\mu\), using proton structure functions. The CT18qed photon PDF is initialized at a starting scale \(\mu_0\) (either 1.3 GeV or 3 GeV) via the LUX formulation and then evolved to higher scales using combined QED+QCD DGLAP kernels at \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha)\), \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha\alpha_s)\), and \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha^2)\). The uncertainty budget includes variations from quark/gluon PDFs, elastic form factors, resonance structure functions, higher-twist and target-mass corrections, and the matching scale between low-\(Q^2\) and perturbative regions.

## Key numbers

- At \(\mu = 1\) TeV, CT18qed gives an enhanced photon PDF at low \(x\) relative to CT18lux, while at large \(x\) the DGLAP approach gives a significantly smaller photon than the LUX approach.
- For doubly-charged Higgs pair production at a 14 TeV LHC, the NLO QCD \(K\)-factor for the Drell-Yan mechanism ranges from about 1.1 to 1.35 as the Higgs mass increases from 100 GeV to 2 TeV.
- The PDF uncertainty for the Drell-Yan cross section increases from 2% to 15% over the same mass range.
- Single-photon initiated processes contribute about 60% of the LO Drell-Yan cross section at low \(H^{\pm\pm}\) mass, rising to equal size at \(M_{H^{\pm\pm}} = 2\) TeV.
- Double-photon initiated processes increase from 10% to 80% of the LO Drell-Yan cross section as the Higgs mass increases.
- The total PDF uncertainty for the sum of NLO DY, SPI, and DPI contributions increases from 2% to 10% for both CT18lux and CT18qed.

## Context

This work extends the CT18 global analysis (Hou et al., 2019) by incorporating the LUX formalism (Manohar et al., 2016, 2017) to produce two new photon PDF sets, following similar second-generation efforts by NNPDF (Bertone et al., 2017) and MMHT (Harland-Lang et al., 2019). Compared to first-generation photon PDFs such as MRST2004QED (Martin et al., 2004), CT14QEDinc (Schmidt et al., 2015), and NNPDF2.3QED (Ball et al., 2013), the new sets improve precision to the percent level and enable more reliable predictions for photon-initiated processes at the LHC, including beyond-Standard-Model scenarios like doubly-charged Higgs production.
