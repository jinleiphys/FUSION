# Shell-like Structures in an expanding Quark-Antiquark Plasma

> The particle density distribution emerging from the solution of the Vlasov equation for relativistic, non-interacting particles with spherically symmetric initial conditions is shown to exhibit a shell-like structure for late fixed times in the center-of-mass (CM) frame of the system. A similar phenomenon was recently observed employing the test-particle method to solve the Vlasov equation for quarks with Nambu-Jona-Lasinio-type interactions, and was attributed to the attractive forces among the particles. Contrary to this claim, it is demonstrated here that this effect is of purely relativistic origin and is sensitive only to the mass of the particles and their initial phase-space distribution.

## Key claim

The shell-like structure observed in test-particle simulations of an expanding quark-antiquark plasma with Nambu-Jona-Lasinio-type interactions is not due to attractive forces among the particles, as previously claimed by Abada and Aichelin (1995), but is instead a purely relativistic kinematic effect that depends only on the particle mass and the initial phase-space distribution.

## Method

The authors analytically solved the relativistic Vlasov equation for non-interacting particles with spherically symmetric initial conditions, constructing the particle density distribution for a uniformly filled sphere of radius R with a thermal (Fermi) momentum distribution. They then compared this free-streaming solution to numerical solutions of ideal relativistic hydrodynamics for the same initial conditions, using a one-dimensional relativistic Harten-Lax-van Leer-Einfeldt algorithm with a Sod predictor-corrector step for spherical geometry.

## Key numbers

- For a quark mass of 50 MeV/c² and temperature T=160 MeV, the thermal velocity is ⟨v⟩ ≈ 0.9858 c and the spread of the velocity distribution is Δv ≈ 0.0031 c.
- The shell forms at time t = R/c (dimensionless time t̃ = 1) and has a width of 2R.
- For masses below 300 MeV/c², relativistic effects become important and a shell structure emerges in the temporal evolution.
- The estimated change in average momentum due to the self-consistent mass gradient is at most 5% (⟨Δp⟩/⟨p⟩ ≤ 0.05).
- The deceleration of particles due to mass increase from 50 MeV/c² to 300 MeV/c² is approximately 0.09 c.
- The overall density of the shell decreases as ∼1/t³.

## Context

This work directly contradicts the interpretation of Abada and Aichelin (1995), who attributed the shell structure to attractive Nambu-Jona-Lasinio-type interactions and argued that a similar effect occurs in the non-relativistic limit. The authors demonstrate that the shell structure arises solely from relativistic kinematics and does not appear in the non-relativistic limit. The analytic solutions also serve as benchmark tests for numerical algorithms like the test-particle method, and the authors note that similar structures appear in ideal hydrodynamical expansions (Biro, 1995), confirming the kinematic origin. The work enables future studies of whether the phase transition happens before or after shell formation, depending on the initial temperature relative to the critical temperature T_c ∼ 140 MeV.
