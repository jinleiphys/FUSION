# Different effect of the hyperons $Λ$ and $Ξ$ on the nuclear core

> We demonstrate the different effect of strange impurities ($Λ$ and $Ξ$) on the static properties of nuclei within the framework of the relativistic mean-field model. Systematic calculations show that the gluelike role of $Λ$ hyperon is universal for all $Λ$-hypernuclei considered. However, $Ξ^-$ hyperon has the gluelike role only for the protons distribution in nuclei, and for the neutrons distribution $Ξ^-$ hyperon plays a repulsive role. On the other hand, $Ξ^0$ hyperon attracts surrounding neutrons and reveals a repulsive force to the protons. Possible explanations of the above observation are discussed.

## Key claim

Within the relativistic mean-field model, the gluelike role (shrinkage of the nuclear core) that is universal for the Λ hyperon is not universal for the Ξ hyperon: Ξ⁻ attracts protons but repels neutrons, while Ξ⁰ attracts neutrons but repels protons, an effect attributed to their different isospin and the role of the ρ meson, though the magnitude of the effect depends on the uncertain depth of the Ξ-nucleus potential.

## Method

The authors performed systematic relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations for a range of Λ-hypernuclei (from ⁷_ΛLi to ²⁰⁹_ΛPb) and for several Ξ⁻ and Ξ⁰ hypernuclei (O, Ca, Zr, Pb). For the Λ sector, coupling constants were taken from Ref. [ma]; for the Ξ sector, vector couplings were fixed by SU(6) symmetry and scalar couplings were varied to reproduce different assumed depths of the Ξ optical potential (V₀^Ξ = -10, -18, and -28 MeV). The static properties (r.m.s. radii of protons, neutrons, and the hyperon, and binding energy per baryon) were compared with those of the corresponding normal nuclei.

## Key numbers

* For ⁷_ΛLi, the r.m.s. radius of neutrons (r_n) decreases from 2.32 fm in ⁶Li to 2.25 fm, and the r.m.s. radius of protons (r_p) decreases from 2.37 fm to 2.29 fm, demonstrating the Λ gluelike effect.
* For Ξ⁻-hypernuclei, with V₀^Ξ = -18 MeV, adding a Ξ⁻ makes r_n slightly larger and r_p smaller compared to the normal nucleus; for example, in the Zr system the reduction of r_p is faster than that of r_n.
* For Ξ⁰-hypernuclei, with V₀^Ξ = -18 MeV, adding a Ξ⁰ makes r_p slightly larger and r_n smaller, opposite to the Ξ⁻ case.
* The depth of the Ξ potential is uncertain, with values in the literature ranging from about -30 to -10 MeV; the paper uses V₀^Ξ = -10, -18, and -28 MeV to test sensitivity.
* When the ρ-meson coupling is set to zero (g_{ρΞ}=0), both Ξ⁻ and Ξ⁰ reduce r_n and r_p, mimicking the Λ gluelike effect.

## Context

This work extends the study of the gluelike role of hyperons from the well-studied Λ hyperon (e.g., the KEK-PS E419 experiment on ⁷_ΛLi) to the less-understood Ξ hyperon, motivated by experimental evidence that the Ξ-nucleus potential is attractive (Dover and Gal, analyzing old emulsion data; Fukuda et al., KEK E224; E885 at AGS). The results show that the Λ’s universal shrinkage does not hold for Ξ, and that the isospin-dependent coupling to the ρ meson is responsible for the different behavior of Ξ⁻ and Ξ⁰. The findings enable future work on coupled-channel effects in ΛΛ and ΛΞ hypernuclei, where more reliable information on the ΞN interaction is needed.
