# Limits on $CP$-violating hadronic interactions and proton EDM from paramagnetic molecules

> Experiments with paramagnetic ground or metastable excited states of molecules (ThO, HfF$^+$, YbF, YbOH, BaF, PbO, etc.) provide strong constraints on electron electric dipole moment (EDM) and coupling constant $C_{SP}$ of contact semileptonic interaction. We compute new contributions to $C_{SP}$ arising from the nucleon EDMs due to combined electric and magnetic electron-nucleon interaction. This allows us to improve limits from the experiments with paramagnetic molecules on the $CP$-violating parameters, such as the proton EDM, $|d_p|< 1.1\times 10^{-23} e\cdot $cm, the QCD vacuum angle, $|\bar θ|<1.4\times 10^{-8}$, as well as the quark chromo-EDMs and $π$-meson-nucleon couplings. Our results may also be used to search for the axion dark matter which produces oscillating $\barθ$.

## Key claim

The paper derives new contributions to the effective semileptonic coupling constant \(C_{SP}\) from nucleon electric dipole moments (EDMs) via combined electric and magnetic electron-nucleon interactions, improving limits on hadronic \(CP\)-violating parameters from paramagnetic molecule experiments. The key hedge is that the calculation assumes spinless nuclei and uses single-particle nuclear shell model estimates with up to 50% uncertainty in the nuclear matrix elements.

## Method

The authors computed the atomic EDM induced by permanent nucleon EDMs through second-order perturbation theory, combining the electric dipole interaction of nucleon EDMs with the magnetic dipole interaction of nucleon magnetic moments. They evaluated electronic matrix elements using Dirac-Coulomb wave functions for \(s_{1/2}\) and \(p_{1/2}\) states in heavy atoms, summing over continuum intermediate states, and combined these with nuclear spin-flip M1 transition matrix elements from the single-particle shell model. The resulting effective \(C_{SP}\) contribution was then compared with the contact semileptonic interaction to extract limits on nucleon EDMs, the QCD vacuum angle \(\bar\theta\), quark chromo-EDMs, and \(CP\)-odd pion-nucleon couplings.

## Key numbers

- Proton EDM limit: \(|d_p| < 1.1 \times 10^{-23} \, e\cdot\text{cm}\) from paramagnetic molecule experiments
- QCD vacuum angle limit: \(|\bar\theta| < 1.4 \times 10^{-8}\)
- Contact coupling constraint from ACME ThO experiment (90% C.L.): \(|C_{SP}|_{\text{Th}} < 7.3 \times 10^{-10}\)
- For \(^{232}\)Th: electronic matrix element coefficients \(M_p = 244 \, a_B^{-1}\) and \(M_n = 385 \, a_B^{-1}\)
- For \(^{180}\)Hf: electronic matrix element coefficients \(M_p = 121 \, a_B^{-1}\) and \(M_n = 114 \, a_B^{-1}\)
- Relation between nucleon EDMs and pion-nucleon couplings: \(d_n \approx 1.1 \times 10^{-14} \, \bar g^{(0)}_{\pi NN} \, e\cdot\text{cm}\) and \(d_p \approx -1.3 \times 10^{-14} \, \bar g^{(0)}_{\pi NN} \, e\cdot\text{cm}\)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Flambaum, Pospelov, Ritz, and Samsonov (2019) who calculated two-photon and meson-exchange contributions to \(C_{SP}\), obtaining \(|\bar\theta| \lesssim 3 \times 10^{-8}\). The new contributions from discrete nuclear intermediate states are comparable or larger than those earlier results, enabling improved limits on hadronic \(CP\)-violating parameters. The results can also be applied to searches for axion dark matter, which produces oscillating \(\bar\theta\) as discussed by Graham and Rajendran (2013) and Roussy et al. (2020).
