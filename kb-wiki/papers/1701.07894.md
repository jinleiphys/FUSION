# Heavy neutrino potential for neutrinoless double beta decay

> Heavy neutrino potential for neutrinoless double beta decay is studied with focusing on its statistical property. The existence condition for heavy neutrinos is also presented. In particular sterile neutrinos are possible candidate for heavy nuetrinos. The statistics provide a gross view of understanding amplitude of constitutional components of the nuclear matrix element.

# Heavy neutrino potential for neutrinoless double beta decay

## Key claim
The heavy neutrino potential for neutrinoless double beta decay is studied statistically, showing that s-wave contributions dominate roughly twice as strongly in the heavy neutrino case compared to the light ordinary neutrino case. The existence condition for heavy neutrinos requires that the ratio of heavy-to-light nuclear matrix elements differ between two different decaying nuclei, and a sufficient condition for heavy neutrino existence is that the light-neutrino-only half-life relation does not hold.

## Method
The author computes the neutrino potential in the heavy mass limit (m_nu >> closure parameter, m_nu^2 >> q^2) using the closure approximation, evaluating the radial matrix elements <n' l'| H_alpha(sqrt(2) rho) | n l> for harmonic oscillator basis states with n, n' = 0-3 and l, l' = 0-6. The frequency distribution and ranking of these matrix elements are analyzed separately for Fermi, Gamow-Teller, and tensor parts, and compared to the massless (light neutrino) limit results from Iwata (2016).

## Key numbers
- The largest contribution for all three parts (Fermi, Gamow-Teller, tensor) comes from the (n l n' l') = (0 0 0 0) transition, with values 0.261 (Fermi), 0.230 (Gamow-Teller), and 0.125 (tensor)
- The average of nonzero components is 0.0526 for Fermi, 0.0485 for Gamow-Teller, and 0.0063 for the tensor part
- Contributions with l = l' = 0 cover 49.6% of total for Fermi, 52.3% for Gamow-Teller, and 12.8% for tensor parts in heavy neutrino case, compared to 27.1%, 27.1%, and 7.2% respectively in light neutrino case
- The tensor part total sum of 9.458 results from cancellation between +9.943 and -0.485
- The minimum tensor part value is -0.0086 for (n l n' l') = (3 0 3 4) and (3 4 3 0)
- The ten largest contributions cover 49.6% of total for Fermi, 52.3% for Gamow-Teller, and 13.4% for tensor parts
- The l = l' components of the tensor part cover 28.9% of total tensor contributions
- The vector-to-axial coupling ratio (g_V/g_A)^2 = (1/1.27)^2 ~ 0.62 is applied for Gamow-Teller part in NME calculations

## Context
This work extends the statistical analysis of neutrino potentials from Iwata (2016) for light neutrinos to the heavy neutrino case, relevant for sterile neutrino searches motivated by LSND (Dodelson, 2006), GALLEX/SAGE (Bahcall, 1997), and reactor anomaly experiments. The heavy neutrino existence condition builds on the framework of Faessler (2011) and Lisi (2015). The results are intended to inform nuclear matrix element calculations for neutrinoless double beta decay, where precise NMEs are needed to extract neutrino masses from half-life measurements of two different decaying nuclei (e.g., calcium and xenon). The next step is to study the cancellation components of the nuclear matrix elements.
