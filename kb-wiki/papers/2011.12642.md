# Consistent description for cluster dynamics and single-particle correlation

> Cluster dynamics and single-particle correlation are simultaneously treated for the description of the ground state of ${}^{12} \mathrm{C} $. The recent development of the antisymmetrized quasi cluster model (AQCM) makes it possible to generate $jj$-coupling shell-model wave functions from $α$ clusters models. The cluster dynamics and the competition with the $jj$-coupling shell-model structure can be estimated rather easily. In the present study, we further include the effect of single-particle excitation; the mixing of the two-particle-two-hole excited states is considered. The single-particle excitation is not always taken into account in the standard cluster model analyses, and the two-particle-two-hole states are found to strongly contribute to the lowering of the ground state owing to the pairing-like correlations. By extending AQCM, all of the basis states are prepared on the same footing, and they are superposed based on the framework of the generator coordinate method (GCM).

## Key claim

The authors show that including two-particle-two-hole excited states (generated via random shifts of Gaussian center parameters) within the antisymmetrized quasi cluster model (AQCM) framework strongly lowers the ground state energy of 12C due to pairing-like correlations, but the abstract does not specify that the energy lowering is more than 3.5 MeV relative to the AQCM-only result and that the final energy converges to -91.66 MeV, still about 0.5 MeV above the experimental value of -92.2 MeV.

## Method

The authors use AQCM to generate basis states for 12C by varying the inter-cluster distance R (0.5 to 3.0 fm) and the cluster-breaking parameter Λ (0.0, 0.2, 0.4), giving 18 basis states. They then generate 100 two-particle-two-hole states by randomly shifting the Gaussian center parameters of two nucleons (either two protons or two neutrons) in the optimal AQCM basis state (R = 2.1 fm, Λ = 0.2), using an exponential probability distribution with σ = 1 fm. All 118 basis states are superposed via the generator coordinate method (GCM) with angular momentum and parity projection, using the Tohsaki interaction for the central part and the G3RS interaction for the spin-orbit part.

## Key numbers

- Optimal single AQCM basis state energy: -86.68 MeV at R = 2.1 fm and Λ = 0.2
- Energy after superposing 18 AQCM basis states: -88.04 MeV (1.4 MeV lower than the optimal single basis state)
- Final 0+ ground state energy after including 100 two-particle-two-hole states: -91.66 MeV
- Energy lowering from mixing two-particle-two-hole states: more than 3.5 MeV
- Experimental ground state energy of 12C: -92.2 MeV
- 0+–2+ energy spacing with AQCM only: 3.9 MeV
- 0+–2+ energy spacing after adding proton two-particle-two-hole states: 5.1 MeV
- 0+–2+ energy spacing after adding neutron two-particle-two-hole states: 6.7 MeV
- 0+–2+ energy spacing after full K-mixing: 4.9 MeV (experimental value: 4.6 MeV)
- Expectation value of the isospin-squared operator in the ground state: 0.016 (indicating isospin breaking at the order of 10^-3)
- Expectation value of the principal quantum number N with 18 AQCM basis states: 9.15; after including two-particle-two-hole states: 9.13
- Size parameter ν = 0.23 fm^-2
- Spin-orbit interaction strength: V_ls^1 = V_ls^2 = 1800 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous AQCM study of 12C (Itagaki, 2016) by including two-particle-two-hole excitations, which were not considered in standard cluster model analyses. The approach contrasts with antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) studies that focus on one-particle-one-hole states (PhysRevC.93.054307, 2016) and with modern ab initio approaches that include tensor and short-range correlations. The method enables a consistent treatment of both cluster dynamics and single-particle correlations, and the results show that BCS-like pairing effects are crucial for reproducing the 0+–2+ level spacing in 12C, a long-standing problem in microscopic cluster models.
