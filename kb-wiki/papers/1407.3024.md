# Toward extremes of angular momentum: Application of the Pfaffian algorithm in realistic calculations

> In a calculation of rotated matrix elements with angular momentum projection, the generalized Wick's theorem may encounter a practical problem of combinatorial complexity when the configurations have more than four quasi-particles (qps). The problem can be solved by employing the Pfaffian algorithm generally applicable to calculations of matrix elements for Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov states with any number of qps. This breakthrough in many-body techniques enables studies of high-spin states in a shell-model framework. As the first application of the Pfaffian algorithm, the configuration space of the Projected Shell Model is expanded to include 6-qp states for both positive and negative parities. Taking $^{166}$Hf as an example, we show that 6-qp states become the main configuration of the yrast band beyond spin $I \approx 34\hbar$, which explains the observed third back-bending in moment of inertia. Structures of multi-qp high-$K$ isomers in $^{176}$Hf are analyzed as another example.

## Key claim

The Pfaffian algorithm solves the combinatorial complexity problem that previously limited the Projected Shell Model (PSM) to configurations with at most four quasiparticles, enabling the inclusion of 6-quasiparticle states for both positive and negative parities; this expansion explains the observed third back-bending in moment of inertia for $^{166}$Hf at spin $I \approx 34\hbar$, but the calculations are restricted to axially symmetric deformed bases and do not yet incorporate triaxial degrees of freedom.

## Method

The authors extended the Projected Shell Model (PSM) configuration space by implementing the Pfaffian algorithm to compute rotated overlap matrix elements for Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov states with arbitrary numbers of quasiparticles. They constructed multi-quasiparticle states up to 6-qp from a deformed Nilsson-BCS basis with three major harmonic-oscillator shells for neutrons ($N=4,5,6$) and protons ($N=3,4,5$), performed angular-momentum projection, and solved the eigenvalue equation with a Hamiltonian containing quadrupole-quadrupole, monopole-pairing, and quadrupole-pairing separable interactions. The method was applied to $^{166}$Hf and $^{176}$Hf using deformation parameters from the literature.

## Key numbers

- For $^{166}$Hf, the third anomaly in moment of inertia occurs at $\omega^2 \approx 0.25$, corresponding to spin $I \approx 34\hbar$, where 6-qp states become the main configuration of the yrast band.
- The first back-bending in $^{166}$Hf occurs at $\omega^2 \approx 0.10$ (spin $I \approx 12$), and the second anomaly at $\omega^2 \approx 0.15$ (spin $I \approx 24$).
- For $^{176}$Hf, the calculated $B(E2)$ for the allowed transition from the $22^-$ isomer to the $20^-$ state is $3.7 \times 10^{-2}$ W.u., compared to the experimental value $4.8 \times 10^{-3}$ W.u.
- The $K^\pi = 14^-$ isomer in $^{176}$Hf at 2866 keV is well reproduced by the calculation with a 4-qp configuration.
- The calculated band-head energy for the $K^\pi = 15^+$ 4-qp band in $^{176}$Hf is about 700 keV higher than the experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the Projected Shell Model (PSM) framework, previously limited to 3-qp states for odd-mass and 4-qp states for even-even nuclei (Chen 2012, Liu 2011), by implementing the Pfaffian algorithm introduced for nuclear overlap calculations by Robledo (2009, 2011) and Mizusaki (2013). The expanded configuration space enables description of the third back-bending in $^{166}$Hf observed by experiments (Hf166-EPJA2000) and analysis of multi-qp high-$K$ isomers in $^{176}$Hf (Hf176-2010PRC). Future work should extend the method to triaxial deformed bases with three-dimensional angular momentum projection, as triaxiality may affect $K$-forbidden transitions and low-lying states in this mass region (Chen 2013).
