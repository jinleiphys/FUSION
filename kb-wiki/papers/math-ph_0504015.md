# On positive functions with positive Fourier transforms

> Using the basis of Hermite-Fourier functions (i.e. the quantum oscillator eigenstates) and the Sturm theorem, we derive the practical constraints for a function and its Fourier transform to be both positive. We propose a constructive method based on the algebra of Hermite polynomials. Applications are extended to the 2-dimensional case (i.e. Fourier-Bessel transforms and the algebra of Laguerre polynomials) and to adding constraints on derivatives, such as monotonicity or convexity.

# On positive functions with positive Fourier transforms

## Key claim

The paper provides a constructive method for building functions where both the function and its Fourier transform are positive, using a truncated expansion in Hermite-Fourier (or Laguerre-Fourier) basis states combined with the Sturm theorem to enforce positivity conditions. The method is limited to finite expansions and does not guarantee convergence for infinite series, nor does it address periodic functions.

## Method

The authors expand the function ψ(r) in the basis of harmonic oscillator eigenstates (Hermite-Fourier functions), which are eigenstates of the Fourier transform with eigenvalues i^n. By restricting to even-n components (for real transforms) and separating the expansion into subspaces with eigenvalue +1 (n multiples of 4) and -1 (n multiples of 4 plus 2), the Fourier partner φ(s) becomes a linear combination of the same polynomials with sign changes. The Sturm theorem is then applied to the resulting polynomials in the variables ρ=r^2 and σ=s^2 to determine parameter domains where both polynomials have no real positive roots, ensuring positivity of both ψ and φ. The method extends to 2-dimensional Fourier-Bessel transforms by replacing Hermite polynomials with Laguerre polynomials in r^2.

## Key numbers

- The first four normalized Hermite polynomials listed are: H_0=1, H_1=√2 r, H_2=(2r^2-1)/√2, H_3=(2r^3-3r)/√3
- The first four normalized 2-d radial Laguerre states are: v_0=√2 exp(-r^2/2), v_1=√2 exp(-r^2/2)(r^2-1), v_2=√2 exp(-r^2/2)(r^4-4r^2+2)/2, v_3=√2 exp(-r^2/2)(r^6-9r^4+18r^2-6)/6
- For the H_0,H_4,H_8 mixture, the resultant R is proportional to an expression containing terms up to ψ_8^5 with coefficients such as 2100, 1050, 240, 400, 165, 480, 4560, 13320, 1600, 792, 1728, 612, 10080, 2520, and 1260
- For the H_0,H_4,H_8 mixture with convexity constraint, the positivity domain disappears when r_c^2 < approximately 0.4
- For the 2-d case with v_0,v_2,v_4 mixture and third derivative negativity constraint, results are shown for r_t^2=1.4

## Context

This work addresses the practical problem of constructing functions with positive Fourier transforms, which arises in nuclear physics (relating cross sections to nuclear profiles, as in Glauber theory), particle physics (relating color dipole distributions to gluon transverse momentum distributions in QCD, as in Kovchegov's work), probability theory (relating distributions to characteristic functions, as in Levy processes), and crystallography. The paper extends prior sufficient conditions for positivity such as convexity of ψ (Lafforgue) and builds upon the theory of self-Fourier functions (Caola 1991, Cincotti 1991) and Bochner's theorem on positive-definite functions (Gelfand). The method enables systematic exploration of positivity domains for truncated expansions, with potential applications to constructing physically admissible profile functions and dipole distributions.
