# Generalized Universality of Definite Rank Gluon Transverse Momentum Dependent Correlators

> Transverse momentum dependent (TMD) parton correlators describing the partonic structure of hadrons contain gauge links, required by color gauge invariance. The required gauge links enter in the matrix elements that contain the parton fields and depend on the color flow in the hard process. The correlators are expanded in terms of transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions, referred to as TMD PDFs, or in short TMDs. In this paper, we introduce gluon TMDs of definite rank, by making an expansion of the TMD gluon correlator with the help of irreducible tensors built from the transverse momenta. The process dependence is isolated in gauge link dependent gluonic pole factors multiplying the TMDs. It is important to account for the different possibilities in the color structure within the matrix elements, leading to multiple TMDs at a given rank. In this way we are able to write the leading tree level result for a hard process in terms of process dependent gluon correlators which are expressed in a finite set of universal TMDs. We tabulate the gluonic pole factors for various gauge links, among them those that are relevant for 2 to 2 processes.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a systematic classification of gluon transverse momentum dependent (TMD) correlators of definite rank, showing that the process dependence of these correlators can be isolated into gauge-link-dependent gluonic pole factors multiplying a finite set of universal TMDs, provided one accounts for the different color structures within the matrix elements.

## Method

The authors expand the gluon TMD correlator using irreducible tensors built from transverse momenta, ordering the correlators by rank according to their transverse momentum dependence. They classify the gauge link structures into three types based on color trace configurations (single trace, multiple traces with Wilson loops, and two separate traces each containing one gluon field), then compute transverse moments by applying transverse derivatives to the gauge links, which yields collinear multi-parton correlators involving gluonic pole operators \(G_\st^\alpha(\xi)\) and derivative terms. The process dependence is tabulated as gluonic pole factors for various gauge link structures relevant for \(2\rightarrow 2\) processes.

## Key numbers

- The gluonic pole factor for the gauge link structure \([+\square,+^\dagger\square^\dagger]\) in a single-weighted type 1 correlator is \(C_{G,1}^{[+\square,+^\dagger\square^\dagger]} = +3\).
- Single transverse weighting and azimuthal averaging of the gluon correlator yields the transverse moments \(f_{1T}^{\perp g (1)}\), \(g_{1T}^{g (1)}\), and \(h_{1T}^{g (1)}\).
- Double transverse weighting and azimuthal averaging yields \(h_1^{\perp g (2)}\) and \(h_{1L}^{\perp g (2)}\).
- Triple transverse weighting and azimuthal averaging yields \(h_{1T}^{\perp g (3)}\).
- The collinear gluon PDFs surviving \(p_\st\)-integration are \(g(x) = f_1^g(x)\) and \(\Delta g(x) = g_{1L}^g(x)\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of rank-two quark TMD correlators (Buffing 2012) to gluon TMDs, which exhibit a richer color structure due to more complicated gauge link configurations. The formalism builds on earlier treatments of gauge-link-dependent gluon correlators (Mulders 2000, Bomhof 2006, Bomhof 2007) and the identification of gluonic pole (Efremov-Teryaev-Qiu-Sterman) matrix elements (Efremov 1981, Qiu 1991). The results enable the description of leading tree-level hard processes in terms of a finite set of universal gluon TMDs multiplied by process-dependent gluonic pole factors, which is essential for understanding factorization and universality in processes involving transverse momentum dependence.
