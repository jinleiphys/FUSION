# Ab-initio Green's Functions Calculations of Atoms

> The Faddeev random phase approximation (FRPA) method is applied to calculate the ground state and ionization energies of simple atoms. First ionization energies agree with the experiment at the level of ~10 mH or less. Calculations with similar accuracy are expected to provide information required for developing the proposed quasiparticle-DFT method.

## Key claim

The Faddeev random phase approximation (FRPA) method yields first ionization energies for simple atoms that agree with experiment at the level of approximately 10 mH or less, with the caveat that the extrapolated results deviate from experiment by about 5 mH for first ionization energies and increase to 10-15 mH for deeper electron orbits.

## Method

The FRPA method computes the one-body Green's function by solving the Dyson equation with an irreducible self-energy that couples single-particle motion to particle-hole and particle-particle/hole-hole collective excitations via Faddeev equations for the 2p1h/2h1p polarization propagators. Calculations used correlation-consistent cc-pVTZ and cc-pVQZ Gaussian basis sets (cc-pCV(TQ)Z for Mg), with results extrapolated to the basis set limit using the relation E_X = E_infinity + A X^{-3}, where X is the cardinal number of the basis.

## Key numbers

- He ground state binding energy: FRPA gives -2.903 H (deviation +1 mH from experimental -2.904 H)
- Be ground state binding energy: FRPA gives -14.643 H (deviation +24 mH from experimental -14.667 H)
- Ne ground state binding energy: FRPA gives -128.917 H (deviation +11 mH from experimental -128.928 H)
- Mg ground state binding energy: FRPA gives -200.058 H (deviation -15 mH from experimental -200.043 H)
- He 1s ionization energy: FRPA gives 0.900 H (deviation -4 mH from experimental 0.904 H)
- Ne 2p ionization energy: FRPA gives 0.803 H (deviation +10 mH from experimental 0.793 H)
- Ne 1s ionization energy: FRPA gives 1.795 H (deviation +13 mH from experimental 1.782 H)
- Ar 3p ionization energy: FRPA gives 0.578 H (deviation -1 mH from experimental 0.579 H)
- Ar 3s ionization energy: FRPA gives 1.065 H (deviation -10 mH from experimental 1.075 H)
- Ar 2p ionization energy: FRPA gives 9.199 H (deviation +39 mH from experimental 9.160 H)
- FRPA accounts for 96% of the correlation energy in the larger systems studied

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev-RPA approach previously developed by Barbieri (2001) and Barbieri (2007) to atomic systems, going beyond the GW approximation of Hedin (1965) by including Pauli correlations at the 2p1h/2h1p level and pp/hh configurations. The FRPA method completely includes the algebraic diagrammatic construction method at third order [ADC(3)] of Schirmer (1983) and gives similar results for small atomic systems, while the explicit inclusion of RPA phonons holds promise for applications to extended systems. These accurate calculations of quasiparticle properties are expected to provide information required for developing the proposed quasiparticle-DFT method of Van Neck (2006).
