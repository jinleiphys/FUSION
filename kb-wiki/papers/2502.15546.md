# Status of two-baryon scattering in lattice QCD

> In these proceedings, I will review lattice QCD calculations of baryon-baryon scattering, their methods, and their challenges. In the last few years, there has been a new generation of calculations with increased focus on controlling systematic uncertainties. Contrary to the findings of earlier exploratory calculations, it now appears probable that at heavy pion masses there is no nucleon-nucleon bound state.

## Key claim

Contrary to earlier exploratory calculations that claimed deeply bound deuteron and dineutron states at heavy pion masses, it now appears probable that there is no nucleon-nucleon bound state at significantly-heavier-than-physical pion masses, once systematic uncertainties from excited-state contamination and discretization effects are controlled.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD calculations of baryon-baryon scattering using finite-volume spectroscopy combined with Lüscher's quantization condition, where low-lying finite-volume energy levels are computed from two-point correlation functions and then used to constrain the infinite-volume scattering amplitude. The review emphasizes the transition from earlier point-source methods with asymmetric correlation functions to newer variational methods using generalized eigenvalue problems (GEVP) with multiple interpolating operators, including both local hexaquark operators and bilocal baryon-baryon operators constructed via distillation or sparsening techniques.

## Key numbers

- For a two-nucleon system at physical pion mass with \(m_\pi L=4\), the lowest energy gap to elastic excitations is approximately \(4\pi^2/(m_N L^2)\), corresponding to slightly more than \(m_\pi/3\), making Euclidean time evolution only one sixth as effective at filtering excited states compared to the single-nucleon case
- At a heavy SU(3)-symmetric point with \(m_\pi=m_K=m_\eta\approx 800\) MeV, earlier NPLQCD work using point sources found a dineutron (\(NN\) \(^1S_0\)) bound state with binding energy \(B_{nn}\approx 21\) MeV, while newer variational calculations on the same ensembles found no bound state
- At a light SU(3)-symmetric point with \(m_\pi\approx 420\) MeV, the \(H\) dibaryon binding energy in the continuum limit was roughly 5 MeV, while at nonzero lattice spacing this was up to seven times larger
- For the \(^1P_1\) partial wave at the light SU(3)-symmetric point, a chiral EFT-inspired fit with three parameters (\(g_{\pi NN}\) and two contact term coefficients) gave \(g_{\pi NN}\approx 13\) and described the spectrum well with no discretization effects

## Context

This review extends and contradicts earlier exploratory lattice QCD calculations by Yamazaki (2012, 2015), Berkowitz (2015), Wagman (2017), and NPLQCD (2011, 2012, 2020) that reported deeply bound nucleon-nucleon states at heavy pion masses, while being consistent with HAL QCD results by Inoue (2011) that found no NN bound state. The new generation of calculations using variational methods by Horz (2020), Amarasinghe (2021), Detmold (2024), and the BaSc collaboration enables quantitative cross-checks between different collaborations and pushes toward lighter quark masses and the physical point, though new techniques may be needed to overcome the signal-to-noise problem as the physical pion mass is approached.
