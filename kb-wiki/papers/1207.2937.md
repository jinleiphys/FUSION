# Neutron rich nuclei in a new binding energy formula and the astrophysical $r-$process

> Neutron rich nuclei has been studied with a new phenomenological mass formula. Predictions of different mass formulas for the location of the neutron dripline are compared with those from the present calculation. The implications of the new mass formula for r-process nucleosynthesis are discussed. It is found that though the neutron drip line obtained from this formula differs substantially from other formulas, the r-process abundance upto mass 200 are unlikely to be significantly different. The errors inherent in the mass formula are found to play an insignificant role beyond mass A = 80.

# Neutron rich nuclei in a new binding energy formula and the astrophysical r-process

## Key claim
Although the neutron drip line predicted by the new phenomenological mass formula differs substantially from predictions of the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) and the Duflo-Zuker formula, especially beyond Z ~ 64, the resulting r-process abundance pattern up to mass A = 200 is unlikely to be significantly different between the formulas. The inherent errors in the mass formula (r.m.s. error 0.376 MeV) play an insignificant role in the abundance distribution beyond mass A = 80.

## Method
The authors used the phenomenological binding energy formula from Gangopadhyay (Ref. I) to calculate the neutron drip line by determining the last bound isotope for each element where the neutron separation energy becomes zero or negative. They then performed canonical r-process calculations within the waiting point approximation, using the Saha equation to relate abundances of neighboring isotopes, with beta decay lifetimes and beta-delayed neutron emission probabilities taken from Möller et al. Three standard r-process scenarios with different neutron densities and temperatures were combined with relative weights to produce a final abundance pattern, which was normalized to the abundance at A = 130.

## Key numbers
- The root mean square (r.m.s.) error in ground state binding energies for the new formula is 0.376 MeV, evaluated over 2140 nuclei.
- The three r-process components used: Component I with neutron density N_n = 10^21 /cm^3 and temperature T = 1.9 GK; Component II with N_n = 10^25 /cm^3 and T = 1.5 GK; Component III with N_n = 10^27 /cm^3 and T = 1.3 GK.
- The relative weights for the three components were 30, 10, and 1, respectively.
- The r-process time was assumed to be two seconds in all scenarios.
- The r-process path is characterized by neutron separation energies S_n approximately 2-3 MeV away from closed shells.
- The abundance peaks are observed around masses 80, 130, and 195, corresponding to neutron closed shells N = 50, 82, and 126.

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological mass formula developed by Gangopadhyay (Ref. I), which was previously applied to proton-rich nuclei for the rp-process, to the neutron-rich side of the stability valley for r-process nucleosynthesis. The neutron drip line predictions are compared with those from the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) and the Duflo-Zuker formula, showing substantial differences for heavy nuclei beyond Z ~ 64. The results enable future studies to assess whether the choice of mass formula significantly impacts r-process abundance calculations, particularly for masses above A = 200 where the present work does not rule out differences.
