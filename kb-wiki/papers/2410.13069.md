# An end-to-end generative diffusion model for heavy-ion collisions

> We train a generative diffusion model (DM) to simulate ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions from end to end. The model takes initial entropy density profiles as input and produces two-dimensional final particle spectra, successfully reproducing integrated and differential observables. It also captures higher-order fluctuations and correlations. These findings suggest that the generative model has successfully learned the complex relationship between initial conditions and final particle spectra for various shear viscosities, as well as the fluctuations introduced during initial entropy production and hadronization stages, providing an efficient framework for resource-intensive physical goals. The code and trained model are available at https://huggingface.co/Jing-An/DiffHIC/tree/main.

## Key claim
The authors claim that a generative diffusion model (DiffHIC) can successfully learn the complex mapping from initial entropy density profiles to final charged particle spectra in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, but the model's precision decreases systematically as the number of correlated particles increases, and it may struggle in other collision systems without fine-tuning or retraining.

## Method
The authors trained a conditional generative diffusion model using a variance-preserving stochastic differential equation framework, with a noise-prediction network based on a U-net architecture. The model was trained on 12,000 pairs of initial entropy density profiles (generated by the TrENTo model) and final particle spectra (generated by the hybrid MUSIC + iSS + UrQMD simulation chain) for Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, with shear viscosity over entropy density ratios of 0.0, 0.1, and 0.2. The reverse process used a probability flow ordinary differential equation for deterministic sampling, with 4000 total noise steps and a linear noise schedule from beta_1 = 0.5e-4 to beta_T = 0.01.

## Key numbers
- Training dataset: 12,000 pairs of initial entropy density profiles and final particle spectra for each of three shear viscosity values (eta/s = 0.0, 0.1, 0.2)
- Particle spectra are two-dimensional discretized distributions of size 64 x 64 pixels (in p_T and phi_p)
- Transverse momentum range: p_T in [0.0, 2.0] GeV; rapidity range: y in [-0.5, 0.5]
- Total noise steps: T = 4000; noise schedule: beta_1 = 0.5e-4 to beta_T = 0.01
- DiffHIC generates one central collision event in 10^-1 seconds on a GeForce GTX 4090 GPU, compared to approximately 120 minutes (10^4 seconds) on a single CPU for traditional numerical simulation
- Switching energy density for particlization: epsilon_sw = 0.18 GeV/fm^3

## Context
This work extends the application of generative diffusion models, previously used in detector simulations for heavy-ion experiments (Amram 2023, Go 2024, Leigh 2023, Mikuni 2023, Shmakov 2023), to the full end-to-end simulation of heavy-ion collisions from initial conditions to final particle spectra. It addresses the computational bottleneck of traditional hybrid approaches (TrENTO + MUSIC + iSS + UrQMD) that require approximately 120 minutes per central event, enabling rapid generation of large event samples needed for statistics-demanding observables such as higher-order flow cumulants and multi-particle correlations. The model enables future studies of nuclear structure effects and transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma that require millions of simulated events.
