# Covariantizing Phase Space

> We covariantize calculations over the manifold of phase space, establishing Stokes' theorem for differential cross sections and providing new definitions of familiar observable properties like infrared and collinear safety. Through the introduction of explicit coordinates and a metric we show phase space is isomorphic to the product space of a simplex and a hypersphere, and we identify geometric phenomena that occur when its dimensions are large. These results have implications for fixed order subtraction schemes, machine learning in particle physics and high-multiplicity heavy ion collisions.

# Covariantizing Phase Space

## Key claim

The paper establishes a covariant (coordinate-independent) geometric formulation of phase space in perturbative quantum field theory, showing that Stokes' theorem can be applied to differential cross sections and that the conditions for Stokes' theorem to hold may provide a new definition of infrared and collinear (IRC) safety. The authors explicitly state this as a conjecture, noting that a detailed study of whether pathological IRC safe or unsafe observables exist that evade this definition is left to future work.

## Method

The authors introduce explicit global coordinates for N-body massless phase space by expressing momenta in spinor variables, then gauge-fixing the little group redundancy to show phase space is isomorphic to the product space of an (N-1)-simplex (representing lightcone momentum conservation) and a (2N-3)-sphere. They construct a metric on this product manifold and use it to rewrite differential cross sections as surface integrals, enabling application of Stokes' theorem. The vector field describing flow along observable gradients is defined, and the authors classify additive observables as those for which soft emissions produce flow perpendicular to lower-body phase space submanifolds.

## Key numbers

- Phase space dimension is 3N-4, reconstructed as (N-1) from the simplex plus (2N-3) from the sphere.
- Volume of the simplex Delta_{N-1} is 1/(N-1)! and volume of the sphere S^{2N-3} is 2 pi^{N-1}/(N-2)!.
- For the C-parameter in e+e- -> q qbar g at leading order, the differential cross section at C = 3/4 is F(C = 3/4) = 256 pi sqrt(3) / 243.
- For large N with a flat matrix element, the probability of m particles having lightcone momentum p+ less than rho_min Q (with rho_min much less than 1/N) follows a binomial distribution with mean mu = N^2 rho_min and variance sigma^2 = N^2 rho_min (1 - N rho_min).

## Context

This work extends geometric treatments of phase space by Cox (2018) and Henning (2019), who identified phase space as a Stiefel manifold modulo the little group, and by Komiske (2020) who proposed a metric on unordered collections of particle momenta. The authors provide the first explicit metric directly constructed from the phase space manifold itself. The results enable new approaches to fixed-order subtraction schemes (such as N-jettiness subtraction by Boughezal (2015) and Gaunt (2015)), machine learning architectures equivariant under the Lorentz group as in Bogatskiy (2020), and geometric test statistics for high-multiplicity heavy ion collisions. The paper also notes that optimization and machine learning on Stiefel manifolds is well-explored in other fields, particularly pattern recognition by Edelman (1998) and others.
