# Universal hydrodynamic flow in holographic planar shock collisions

> We study the collision of planar shock waves in AdS$_5$ as a function of shock profile. In the dual field theory the shock waves describe planar sheets of energy whose collision results in the formation of a plasma which behaves hydrodynamically at late times. We find that the post-collision stress tensor near the light cone exhibits transient non-universal behavior which depends on both the shock width and the precise functional form of the shock profile. However, over a large range of shock widths, including those which yield qualitative different behavior near the future light cone, and for different shock profiles, we find universal behavior in the subsequent hydrodynamic evolution. Additionally, we compute the rapidity distribution of produced particles and find it to be well described by a Gaussian.

# Universal hydrodynamic flow in holographic planar shock collisions

## Key claim

The post-collision stress tensor near the light cone exhibits transient non-universal behavior depending on both shock width and functional form of the shock profile, but over a large range of shock widths and for different shock profiles, the subsequent hydrodynamic evolution is universal, with the only shock-width dependence appearing in the normalization and rapidity width of the proper energy density.

## Method

The authors numerically solve Einstein's equations in AdS$_5$ for colliding planar gravitational shock waves with different longitudinal profiles (Gaussian and non-Gaussian), using a characteristic formulation of gravitational dynamics. They extract the boundary stress tensor via holographic renormalization, identify the spacetime region where viscous hydrodynamics applies using a residual measure comparing exact and hydrodynamic stress tensors, and study the proper energy density and fluid velocity on a fixed proper-time surface.

## Key numbers

- Hydrodynamization time: $\mu t_{\rm hydro} \approx 2$
- Proper time for initial hydrodynamic data surface: $\mu \tau_{\rm init} = 3.5$
- Shock width range studied: $w = n w_o$ with $n = 1,2,\dots,7$ where $w_o \equiv 0.075/\mu$ (Gaussian profile); also $w = w_o$ and $w = 5 w_o$ for non-Gaussian profile
- Quadratic fits for proper energy amplitude at $\xi=0$: $A(\mu w) = 0.14 + 0.15 (\mu w) - 0.025 (\mu w)^2$
- Quadratic fits for full width at half maximum in rapidity: $\xi_{\rm FWHM}(\mu w) = 2.25 - 1.15 (\mu w) + 0.31 (\mu w)^2$
- Gaussian width parameter for rescaled proper energy: $\sigma = [8 \log 2]^{-1/2} \approx 0.425$
- Power law decay exponent for shock remnants on light cone: $t^{-p}$ with $p \approx 0.9$
- Hydrodynamic residual minimum values at $\mu t = 8$: $0.015$ for $w = 5 w_o$ and $0.013$ for $w = w_o$
- Boundary of hydrodynamic region: $\mu \tau_* = 1.5$ and $\mu \Delta t = 0.58$
- Extrapolated $w=0$ values: $\xi_{\rm FWHM}|_{w=0} \approx 2.25$ and $\hat \epsilon(\xi=0)|_{w=0}/\mu^4 \approx 0.14$

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of planar shock collisions in AdS$_5$, including those by Chesler (2010) and Casalderrey-Solana (2013), which used Gaussian longitudinal profiles and found sensitivity of qualitative features to shock thickness. The authors study collisions at zero background temperature (unlike prior work with low-temperature plasma backgrounds) and demonstrate that despite qualitative differences near the light cone, the hydrodynamic flow is universal across shock widths and profiles. This enables construction of initial hydrodynamic data for axisymmetric heavy-ion collisions by treating transverse dynamics as slowly varying, and allows computation of rapidity distributions of produced particles via Cooper-Frye freeze-out, yielding Gaussian rapidity spectra with variances of 1.9 and 2.1 for $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ energies of 200 GeV and 2.76 TeV respectively.
