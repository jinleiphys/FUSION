# Gamow shell model description of exceptional point in $^7$Li

> We report the first identification of an exceptional point (EP) within the Gamow Shell Model in the Coupled Channels representation (GSM-CC). In the spectrum of $^{7}$Li, an EP is found for the $5/2^-$ doublet, where the two states coalesce in both energy and width, the phase rigidity vanishes, and the S-matrix develops a double pole. These features manifest directly in observables: the elastic cross section acquires a split-peak structure, and the phase shift shows a single $2π$ jump. This work demonstrates that GSM-CC provides a powerful framework to explore EP phenomena in nuclei and their experimental signatures.

## Key claim

Using the Gamow Shell Model in the Coupled Channels representation (GSM-CC), an exceptional point (EP) is identified for the first time in a nuclear spectrum, specifically for the \(5/2^-\) doublet in \(^7\)Li, but the control parameters (proton and neutron spin-orbit strengths) cannot be tuned experimentally to place the system exactly at the EP; only proximity effects are accessible.

## Method

The authors performed GSM-CC calculations for \(^7\)Li modeled as a \(^4\)He core with three valence nucleons, using a channel basis that included \(^4\)He + \(^3\)H, \(^5\)He + \(^2\)H, and \(^6\)Li + n partitions. The Hamiltonian employed a Woods-Saxon one-body potential with spin-orbit term plus an FHT nucleon-nucleon interaction, and the proton and neutron spin-orbit strengths were varied as control parameters to locate the EP. The coupled-channel equations were solved to obtain energies, widths, phase rigidities, S-matrix poles, elastic cross sections, and phase shifts for the \(5/2^-\) doublet.

## Key numbers

* The two \(5/2^-\) states at the experimental fit have energies \(E_1 = -4.269\) MeV and \(E_2 = -3.490\) MeV, with widths \(\Gamma_1 = 729\) keV and \(\Gamma_2 = 51.3\) keV.
* At the exceptional point, the coalesced state has energy \(E_{EP} = -4.023\) MeV and width \(\Gamma_{EP} = 476\) keV.
* The EP occurs at proton spin-orbit strength \(V_{so}(p) = 5.67\) MeV and neutron spin-orbit strength \(V_{so}(n) = 4.97\) MeV.
* Corrective factors for channel-channel couplings involving one-nucleon channels are \(c(3/2^-)=0.9972\), \(c(1/2^-)=1.0037\), \(c(7/2^-)=1.0027\), \(c(5/2^-)=0.96164\); for cluster channels involving triton \(c_t(5/2^-)=1.2693\) and deuteron \(c_d(5/2^-)=1.3108\).

## Context

This work extends the study of exceptional points, previously observed in microwave cavities (PhysRevLett.86.787), optics (PhysRevLett.108.173901), and atomic/molecular physics (PhysRevLett.103.123003), into realistic nuclear structure calculations using the GSM-CC framework developed by Michel et al. (PhysRevC.99.044606, PhysRevC.89.034624, Michel2021). It enables future investigations of EP signatures in other observables such as electromagnetic transition strengths and spectroscopic factors, and suggests that proximity to an EP may explain strong parameter sensitivity in nuclear reaction data.
