# Efficient formulas for efficiency correction of cumulants

> We derive formulas which connect cumulants of particle numbers observed with efficiency losses with the original ones based on the binomial model. These formulas can describe the case with multiple efficiencies in a compact form. Compared with the presently suggested ones based on factorial moments, these formulas would drastically reduce the numerical cost for efficiency corrections when the order of the cumulant and the number of different efficiencies are large. The efficiency correction with realistic $p_T$-dependent efficiency would be carried out with the aid of these formulas.

# Efficient formulas for efficiency correction of cumulants

## Key claim
The paper derives formulas connecting original cumulants of particle numbers to observed cumulants under efficiency losses that are more computationally efficient than existing factorial-moment-based methods, particularly when the number of different efficiencies \(M\) and the cumulant order are large. The formulas are derived under the binomial model assumption that efficiencies for individual particles are uncorrelated.

## Method
The author starts from the cumulant generating function of the observed probability distribution \(\tilde{P}(\vec{n})\), which is related to the original distribution \(P(\vec{N})\) via the binomial model with independent efficiencies \(\epsilon_i\). Using the cumulant expansion and the property that the binomial cumulant generating function \(k_N(\theta)\) is linear in \(N\), the author expresses the observed cumulants in terms of original cumulants, then inverts these relations to obtain the original cumulants of a linear combination \(Q = \sum_i a_i N_i\) as functions of mixed cumulants of observed quantities \(q_{(\cdots)}\). The coefficients involve \(\tilde{a}_i = a_i/\epsilon_i\) and binomial cumulant coefficients \(\xi_m(\epsilon_i)\).

## Key numbers
- The formulas for cumulants of \(Q\) up to fourth order are given explicitly in Eqs. (5)–(8), with coefficients depending on \(\tilde{a}_i\) and \(\xi_m^{(i)}\).
- Binomial cumulant coefficients \(\xi_m(p)\) are provided up to sixth order: \(\xi_1(p) = p\), \(\xi_2(p) = p(1-p)\), \(\xi_3(p) = p(1-p)(1-2p)\), \(\xi_4(p) = p(1-p)(1-6p+6p^2)\), \(\xi_5(p) = p(1-p)(1-2p)(1-12p+12p^2)\), \(\xi_6(p) = p(1-p)(1-30p+150p^2-240p^3+120p^4)\).
- The single-variable case (\(M=1\), \(a_1=1\)) yields explicit relations for \(\llangle n^m \rrangle_{\rm c}\) in terms of \(\langle N^m \rangle_{\rm c}\) up to sixth order, e.g., \(\llangle n^3 \rrangle_{\rm c} = \xi_3 \langle N \rangle_{\rm c} + 3 \xi_2 \xi_1 \langle N^2 \rangle_{\rm c} + \xi_1^3 \langle N^3 \rangle_{\rm c}\).
- For the multi-variable case, the number of cumulants needed in the formulas does not depend on \(M\), unlike the factorial-moment approach which requires \(M^m\) terms for \(m\)-th order cumulants.

## Context
This work extends the binomial model for efficiency correction introduced by Kitazawa (2012) from the net-particle case to general linear combinations with multiple efficiencies. It improves upon the factorial-moment-based formulas of Bzdak (2013) and Luo (2014), which become numerically costly for large numbers of efficiency bins \(M\). The new formulas enable efficient correction for realistic \(p_T\)-dependent efficiencies in heavy-ion collision experiments, and can be extended to higher orders and mixed cumulants. The author notes that the binomial model assumes uncorrelated efficiencies, and recent work suggests that small correlations can cause large discrepancies for higher-order cumulants, so results must be interpreted with caution.
