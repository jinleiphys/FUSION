# Testing nuclear models via neutrino scattering

> Recent progresses on the relativistic modeling of neutrino-nucleus reactions are presented and the results are compared with high precision experimental data in a wide energy range.

# Testing nuclear models via neutrino scattering

## Key claim

The updated SuSAv2 model, which incorporates transverse enhancement and isospin effects from the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model, gives a good description of high-energy neutrino-nucleus data from NOMAD and MINERνA but still underpredicts the MiniBooNE data at lower energies, suggesting that two-body meson exchange currents beyond the impulse approximation are needed to resolve the discrepancy.

## Method

The authors compare predictions from the Superscaling (SuSA) model and its improved version SuSAv2 against charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) neutrino and antineutrino cross section data from MiniBooNE, NOMAD, and MINERνA experiments on carbon-12 targets. They also compute cross sections using spectral function approaches with natural orbitals and harmonic oscillator wave functions including final state interactions, and analyze the phase-space integral for two-particle-two-hole (2p2h) meson exchange current contributions in a relativistic framework.

## Key numbers

- MiniBooNE data at average neutrino energy ⟨Eν⟩ ∼ 0.7 GeV: SuSAv2 cross section is significantly larger than SuSA but still falls below the MiniBooNE data
- NOMAD experiment: incident neutrino energy extends from 3 to 100 GeV; both SuSA and SuSAv2 results are compatible with NOMAD data
- MINERνA experiment: neutrino energy flux extends from 1.5 to 10 GeV, peaked at Eν ∼ 3 GeV; SuSAv2 improves agreement with MINERνA data compared to SuSA
- Scaling is expected at momentum transfers larger than roughly 400 MeV/c
- The RFG scaling function has the parabolic form fRFG(ψ) = (3/4)(1 − ψ²), which differs from the experimental scaling function

## Context

This work extends the superscaling analysis of inclusive electron scattering data performed by Donnelly and Sick (1999) for the quasielastic region, and the subsequent application to neutrino scattering by Amaro et al. (2004). The SuSAv2 model incorporates effects from the Relativistic Mean Field model of Amaro et al. (2011) and the Relativistic Green's Function model of Giusti et al. (2014) and Meucci et al. (2014). The results enable future inclusion of fully relativistic two-body meson exchange current calculations, which are needed to resolve the discrepancy with MiniBooNE data and to improve neutrino cross section predictions for ongoing experiments such as T2K, MINERνA, and ArgoNeuT.
