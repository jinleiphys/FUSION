# f_2, sigma, and 2-pi exchanges in rho meson photoproduction

> The $σ$ meson exchange model for $ρ$ photoproduction at low energies is re-examined and a new model is developed by considering explicit two-pion exchange and the $f_2$ tensor meson exchange. The $f_2$ exchange model, which is motivated by the low energy proton-proton elastic scattering, is constructed by fully taking into account of the tensor structure of the $f_2$ meson interactions. Phenomenological informations together with tensor meson dominance and vector meson dominance assumptions are used to estimate the $f_2$ meson's coupling constants. For $2π$ exchange, the loop terms including intermediate $πN$ and $ωN$ channels are calculated using the coupling constants determined from the study of pion photoproduction. It is found that our model with $f_2$ and $2π$ exchanges can successfully replace the commonly used $σ$ exchange model that suffers from the big uncertainty of the coupling constants. We found that the two models can be distinguished by examining the single and double spin asymmetries.

## Key claim
The commonly used sigma meson exchange model for rho meson photoproduction at low energies suffers from a large uncertainty in the coupling constant g_sigma-rho-gamma, which overestimates the observed rho to pi0 pi0 gamma decay width by two orders of magnitude; a new model replacing sigma exchange with f2 tensor meson exchange and explicit two-pion exchange can successfully reproduce the data and can be distinguished from the sigma model only by examining single and double spin asymmetries.

## Method
The authors constructed two models for gamma p to rho0 p: model (A) includes Pomeron, sigma, pi, eta exchanges plus s- and u-channel nucleon terms; model (B) replaces the sigma exchange with f2 exchange and two-pion exchange (including intermediate pi N and omega N loop terms). The f2 exchange amplitude was built from effective Lagrangians respecting the tensor structure of the f2 meson, with coupling constants fixed using tensor meson dominance and vector meson dominance assumptions together with empirical data from backward pi N scattering analyses.

## Key numbers
- Sigma exchange model parameters: M_sigma = 0.5 GeV, g^2_sigma-NN/4pi = 8.0, g_sigma-rho-gamma = 3.0
- QCD sum rule values for g_sigma-rho-gamma: 3.2 +/- 0.6 or 2.2 +/- 0.4
- Empirically estimated branching ratio BR(rho to sigma gamma) = (1.9 +0.9/-0.8 +/- 0.4) x 10^{-5}, giving |g_sigma-rho-gamma| approximately 0.25
- f2-NN coupling constant G^2_f2-NN/4pi = 2.2, with F_f2-NN = 0
- Differential cross sections and spin asymmetries calculated at photon energy E_gamma = 3.55 GeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous efforts on nonresonant mechanisms in meson photoproduction (Oh and Lee, 2001; Oh and Lee, 2002) and addresses the uncertainty in the sigma exchange model used by Friman and Soyeur (1996) and Oh, Lee, and Tsushima (2000). The f2 exchange approach is motivated by analyses of low-energy proton-proton elastic scattering by Donnachie and Landshoff (1992) and earlier work on Pomeron-f proportionality. The model enables future experimental tests at facilities like CLAS, GRAAL, and LEPS, where measurements of single and double spin asymmetries at small momentum transfer could distinguish between the sigma exchange and f2-plus-two-pion exchange mechanisms.
