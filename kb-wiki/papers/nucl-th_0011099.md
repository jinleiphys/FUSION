# Elastic Form Factors in Point-form Approach

> Using the point-form approach to relativistic quantum mechanics, a covariant framework is presented for the calculation of proton and neutron electromagnetic form factors. Results for charge radii, magnetic moments, and electric as well as magnetic form factors are produced using the wave functions obtained in the so-called Goldstone-boson-exchange constituent quark model. All the predictions are found in remarkable agreement with existing experimental data.

## Key claim

Using the point-form approach to relativistic quantum mechanics, the Goldstone-boson-exchange constituent quark model predictions for nucleon electromagnetic form factors, charge radii, and magnetic moments are in remarkable agreement with experimental data, but only when proper Lorentz boosts of the three-quark wave functions are included; the results assume point-like constituent quarks (no quark form factors) and are obtained in the impulse approximation.

## Method

The three-quark wave functions from the Goldstone-boson-exchange constituent quark model (GBE CQM) were used as eigenstates of the mass operator in the Bakamjian-Thomas construction for point-form relativistic quantum mechanics. The matrix elements of the electromagnetic current operator were computed in the impulse approximation (IA) using velocity states and the Breit frame, with the Sachs form factors extracted via the Wigner-Eckhart theorem for the Poincaré group. Point-like constituent quarks were assumed, meaning the single-quark current used $f_1(Q^2)=1$ and $f_2(Q^2)=0$.

## Key numbers

* Proton charge radius squared: $r^2_p = 0.75$ fm$^2$ (predicted), compared to experimental values $0.774(27)$ fm$^2$ and $0.780(25)$ fm$^2$
* Neutron charge radius squared: $r^2_n = -0.12$ fm$^2$ (predicted), compared to experimental $-0.113(7)$ fm$^2$
* Proton magnetic moment: $\mu_p = 2.64$ nuclear magnetons (predicted), compared to experimental $2.792847337(29)$ n.m.
* Neutron magnetic moment: $\mu_n = -1.67$ n.m. (predicted), compared to experimental $-1.91304270(5)$ n.m.
* Average spectator quark energy in the nucleon wave functions: $\langle \omega_i \rangle = 768$ MeV, which drives the relativistic boost effect where $\frac{\omega_i}{m_N} > \frac{1}{3}$

## Context

This work extends the Goldstone-boson-exchange constituent quark model of Glozman, Plessas, and collaborators (1998), which successfully described baryon spectra, to electromagnetic observables using the point-form relativistic framework of Klink (1998). The results contradict earlier nonrelativistic calculations (NRIA) which gave much smaller charge radii (e.g., $r^2_p = 0.10$ fm$^2$), and show that no constituent quark form factors are needed for the momentum transfers considered, unlike previous relativistic studies by other groups (e.g., Rome, Indiana, and Coester groups). The approach enables future exploration of nucleon electromagnetic transitions to other resonances and tests of the smallness of many-body current contributions.
