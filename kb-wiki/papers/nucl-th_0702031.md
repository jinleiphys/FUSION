# Reduction of the RPA eigenvalue problem and a generalized Cholesky decomposition for real-symmetric matrices

> The particular symmetry of the random-phase-approximation (RPA) matrix has been utilized in the past to reduce the RPA eigenvalue problem into a symmetric-matrix problem of half the dimension. The condition of positive definiteness of at least one of the matrices A+-B has been imposed (where A and B are the submatrices of the RPA matrix) so that, e.g., its square root can be found by Cholesky decomposition. In this work, alternative methods are pointed out to reduce the RPA problem to a real (not symmetric, in general) problem of half the dimension, with the condition of positive definiteness relaxed. One of the methods relies on a generalized Cholesky decomposition, valid for non-singular real symmetric matrices. The algorithm is described and a corresponding routine in C is given.

# Reduction of the RPA eigenvalue problem and a generalized Cholesky decomposition for real-symmetric matrices

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the RPA eigenvalue problem can be reduced to a real, non-symmetric problem of half the dimension without requiring the positive definiteness of either A+B or A-B, relaxing a condition that previous methods (Cholesky-based or orthogonal transformation) had imposed. The generalized Cholesky decomposition presented works for any non-singular real symmetric matrix, but its numerical stability is not guaranteed for indefinite matrices, though RPA matrices are expected to be mostly well behaved.

## Method

The author derives a general reduction framework where the matrix A+B is factorized as CDE, leading to a reduced eigenvalue problem of dimension n x n. Two specific approaches are presented: a "no decomposition" method that directly solves one of the n x n equations from the RPA system, and a generalized Cholesky decomposition F = L D L^T where D is a diagonal matrix with entries +1 or -1 and L is lower-triangular. The algorithm for the generalized Cholesky decomposition is described and implemented in a C routine based on the standard Cholesky algorithm from Press et al. (1997).

## Key numbers

- The generalized Cholesky algorithm executes the inner loop approximately n^3/6 times, with n square roots and about n^2/2 divides, comparable to the usual Cholesky decomposition.
- A 1000 x 1000 symmetric matrix was factorized in about 2 seconds on an Intel Pentium 4 machine.
- A 10000 x 10000 symmetric matrix was factorized in about 40 minutes on the same machine.
- The orthogonal transformation method (Householder reduction plus QL decomposition) requires about 2n^3/3 + 30n^2 operations, more than the generalized Cholesky approach.

## Context

This work extends the reduction methods of Chiba (1970) and Ullah and Rowe (1971), which required positive definiteness of at least one of the matrices A+B or A-B for the Cholesky or orthogonal transformation approaches. The paper addresses situations where this condition fails, such as in relativistic RPA models (Rin2001) where A+B and A-B are indefinite due to Dirac sea states, or near phase transitions where the stability matrix can have negative eigenvalues. The method enables efficient solution of RPA problems in iterative schemes (e.g., renormalized RPA) and for evaluating RPA correlation energies, where many RPA problems with different quantum numbers must be solved.
