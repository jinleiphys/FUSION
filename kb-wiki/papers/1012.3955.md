# Elliptic flow in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76 TeV: hybrid model assessment of the first data

> We analyze the elliptic flow parameter v_2 in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76 TeV and in Au+Au collisions at sqrt{s_{NN}} =200 GeV using a hybrid model in which the evolution of the quark gluon plasma is described by ideal hydrodynamics with a state-of-the-art lattice QCD equation of state, and the subsequent hadronic stage by a hadron cascade model. For initial conditions, we employ Monte-Carlo versions of the Glauber and the Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi models and compare results with each other. We demonstrate that the differential elliptic flow v_2(p_T) hardly changes when the collision energy increases, whereas the integrated v_2 increases due to the enhancement of mean transverse momentum. The amount of increase of both v_2 and mean p_T depends significantly on the model of initialization.

## Key claim

The differential elliptic flow \(v_2(p_T)\) hardly changes when the collision energy increases from \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV (Au+Au at RHIC) to \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV (Pb+Pb at LHC), while the integrated \(v_2\) increases due to the enhancement of mean transverse momentum; however, the amount of increase of both \(v_2\) and mean \(p_T\) depends significantly on the model of initialization (MC-Glauber vs. MC-KLN).

## Method

The authors used a hybrid model where the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) evolution is described by ideal hydrodynamics with a lattice QCD equation of state (s95p-v1.1), and the subsequent hadronic stage is modeled by the hadron cascade JAM. Initial conditions for the transverse plane were generated using Monte-Carlo versions of the Glauber model (MC-Glauber) and the Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi model (MC-KLN), with initial time \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\) and Bjorken scaling in the longitudinal direction. The initial entropy distribution in the MC-Glauber model was a linear combination of participant and binary collision densities, tuned to reproduce the centrality dependence of charged hadron multiplicity from ALICE data.

## Key numbers

- In the MC-Glauber initialization, mean \(p_T\) of charged hadrons increases from RHIC to LHC by 21% in 10-20% centrality and by 19% in 40-50% centrality.
- In the MC-KLN initialization, mean \(p_T\) increases from RHIC to LHC by 25% in 10-20% centrality and by 24% in 40-50% centrality.
- From RHIC to LHC, the \(p_T\)-integrated \(v_2\) (with \(|\eta| < 0.8\)) increases by 24% and 25% in 10-20% and 40-50% centrality, respectively, in the MC-Glauber initialization.
- In the MC-KLN initialization, the increase of \(p_T\)-integrated \(v_2\) reaches 42% and 44% in 10-20% and 40-50% centrality, respectively.
- Eccentricity changes at most \(\pm 6\%\) in 40-50% centrality from RHIC to LHC.
- The switching temperature between hydrodynamics and hadron cascade is \(T_{\mathrm{sw}} = 155\) MeV.
- The initial time of hydrodynamic simulations is fixed to \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\).

## Context

This work is a sequel to the authors' previous prediction (Hirano et al., 2010) of elliptic flow before LHC data were available, and it uses the first ALICE data (Aamodt, 2010) to fix the final particle multiplicity and remove the main uncertainty. The results extend the comparison of hybrid model calculations from RHIC to LHC energies, showing that the MC-Glauber initialization reproduces the centrality dependence of integrated \(v_2\) at both energies, while the MC-KLN initialization overpredicts \(v_2\), indicating a need for viscous corrections. This enables future work to extract the specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s\) of the QGP by comparing viscous hydrodynamics hybrid models with data, and emphasizes the importance of determining initial conditions to understand the bulk and transport properties of the QGP.
