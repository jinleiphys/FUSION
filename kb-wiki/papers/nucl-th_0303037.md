# Neutrino emission in neutron stars

> Neutrino emissivities in a neutron star are computed for the neutrino bremsstrahlung process. In the first part the electro-weak nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung is calculated in free space in terms of a on-shell $T$-matrix using a generalized Low energy theorem. In the second part the emissivities are calculated in terms of the hadronic polarization at the two-loop level. Various medium effects, such as finite particle width, Pauli blocking in the $T$-matrix are considered. Compared to the pioneering work of Friman and Maxwell in terms of (anti-symmetrized) one-pion exchange the resulting emissivity is about a factor 4 smaller at saturation density.

## Key claim

The neutrino emissivity from bremsstrahlung in neutron stars is about a factor 4 smaller at saturation density than the pioneering result of Friman and Maxwell (1979), when the full on-shell T-matrix is used instead of the one-pion exchange approximation, and when medium effects such as finite particle width and Pauli blocking are included.

## Method

The authors computed neutrino pair bremsstrahlung (nn → nn ν ν̄ and np → np ν ν̄) in two stages: first in free space using a soft bremsstrahlung formalism that expresses the amplitude in terms of an on-shell T-matrix via a generalized Low energy theorem, and second in dense matter using a two-loop hadronic polarization approach within quantum transport theory. Medium effects were incorporated by including a finite single-particle width (to account for the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect) and by replacing the free T-matrix with an in-medium G-matrix to account for Pauli blocking.

## Key numbers

- The emissivity from the full T-matrix is about a factor 4 smaller than the Friman and Maxwell (1979) result at saturation density.
- For nn bremsstrahlung in free space at a neutron momentum of approximately 300 MeV/c in the center-of-mass system, the full T-matrix gives a cross section reduction of a factor of 4-5 compared to one-pion exchange.
- The relative relativistic correction R for the nn ν ν̄ process remains below 15 percent, and for the np ν ν̄ process below 5 percent, when using one-pion exchange as the NN interaction.
- For the nn ν ν̄ process using the full T-matrix, the relative relativistic correction R remains below 10 percent.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering cooling calculations of Friman and Maxwell (1979), which used a central Landau interaction plus one-pion exchange in the non-relativistic limit, by employing a model-independent on-shell T-matrix derived from phase shifts. It also improves upon the earlier in-medium treatment of Sedrakian and Dieperink (2000), which included the LPM effect but only in the one-pion exchange approximation. The results enable more accurate predictions for neutron star cooling rates and demonstrate the importance of using realistic NN interactions and medium effects beyond the quasi-particle approximation.
