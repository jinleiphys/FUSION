# Excluded-volume effects for a hadron gas in Yang-Mills theory

> When the multiplicities of particles produced in heavy-ion collisions are fitted to the hadron-resonance-gas model, excluded-volume effects play a significant role. In this work, we study the impact of such effects on the equation of state of pure Yang-Mills theory at low temperatures, comparing the predictions of the statistical model with lattice results. In particular, we present a detailed analysis of the SU(2) and SU(3) Yang-Mills theories: we find that, for both of them, the best fits to the equilibrium thermodynamic quantities are obtained when one assumes that the volume of different glueball states is inversely proportional to their mass. The implications of these findings for QCD are discussed.

## Key claim

The best fits to the equilibrium thermodynamic quantities of both SU(2) and SU(3) Yang-Mills theories are obtained when one assumes that the volume of different glueball states is inversely proportional to their mass, rather than constant or directly proportional to mass.

## Method

The authors performed lattice Monte Carlo simulations of SU(2) Yang-Mills theory on lattices with temporal extents N_t = 6, 7, 8, and 10, using the Wilson action with a combination of heat-bath and over-relaxation updates. They computed the trace of the stress-energy tensor Δ via the integral method, subtracted zero-temperature plaquette expectation values, and extrapolated results to the continuum limit using a 1/N_t^2 fit. The continuum-extrapolated Δ/T^4 data were then fitted to a hadron-resonance-gas model with excluded-volume effects, using glueball spectra from Teper (1998) and Lucini (2004), testing three eigenvolume parameterizations: fixed radius, volume directly proportional to mass, and volume inversely proportional to mass. For SU(3) Yang-Mills theory, the same analysis was applied to lattice data from Borsanyi (2012).

## Key numbers

- For SU(2) Yang-Mills theory, the best-fit radius of the lightest (0^+) glueball under the inverse-proportionality assumption is r_{0^+} = 0.82 fm, with an uncertainty of 0.14 fm and reduced chi-squared χ²_red = 0.39.
- For SU(2) Yang-Mills theory, the fixed-radius assumption yields r_{0^+} = 0.65 fm with uncertainty 0.12 fm and χ²_red = 0.74.
- For SU(2) Yang-Mills theory, the direct-proportionality assumption yields r_{0^+} = 0.47 fm with uncertainty 0.19 fm and χ²_red = 1.87.
- For SU(2) Yang-Mills theory, the point-like particle assumption gives χ²_red = 8.16.
- The deconfinement critical temperature for SU(2) Yang-Mills theory is T_c/√σ = 0.7091(36), corresponding to T_c ≈ 312(2) MeV.
- The Hagedorn temperature for SU(2) Yang-Mills theory is set equal to T_c = 312(2) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of excluded-volume effects in the hadron-resonance-gas model, previously applied to QCD by Rischke (1991), Yen (1997, 1998), and Alba (2016), to pure Yang-Mills theories where the particle content consists only of glueballs. The results confirm the pattern found in Alba (2016) for strange hadrons in QCD, where heavier states have smaller effective radii, and provide a cleaner theoretical testbed free from complications of quark masses and multiple chemical potentials. The findings enable improved modeling of conserved-charge fluctuations and the QCD phase diagram, and suggest that the inverse-mass-volume relation may be a robust feature of hadronic interactions.
