# A new baryonic equation of state at sub-nuclear densities for core-collapse simulations

> We calculate a new equation of state for baryons at sub-nuclear densities meant for the use in core-collapse simulations of massive stars. The abundance of various nuclei is obtained together with the thermodynamic quantities. The formulation is the NSE description and the liquid drop approximation of nuclei. The model free energy to minimize is calculated by relativistic mean field theory for nucleons and the mass formula for nuclei with the atomic number up to ~ 1000. We have also taken into account the pasta phase, thanks to which the transition to uniform nuclear matter in our EOS occurs in the conventional manner: nuclei are not dissociated to nucleons but survive right up to the transition to uniform nuclear matter. We find that the free energy and other thermodynamical quantities are not very different from those given in the Shen's EOS, one of the standard EOS's that adopt the single nucleus approximation. The average mass is systematically different, on the other hand, which may have an important ramification to the rates of electron captures and coherent neutrino scatterings on nuclei in supernova cores. It is also interesting that the root mean square of the mass number is not very different from the average mass number, since the former is important for the evaluation of coherent scattering rates on nuclei but has been unavailable so far. The EOS table is currently under construction, which will include the weak interaction rates.

## Key claim

The authors construct a new baryonic equation of state (EOS) at sub-nuclear densities for core-collapse simulations that includes a distribution of nuclei (up to atomic number ~1000) and the nuclear pasta phase, but they note that the free energy and thermodynamic quantities are "not very different" from the standard Shen EOS, which uses the single nucleus approximation (SNA); the main differences appear in the average mass number and the root-mean-square mass number, which affect weak interaction rates.

## Method

The authors employ nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) with a liquid drop model for nuclei, minimizing a model free energy that combines relativistic mean field theory (RMF) for free nucleons and a mass formula for nuclei. The bulk energy of nuclei uses experimental mass data (Audi 2003) at low densities and RMF at high densities, with linear interpolation between 10^12 g/cm^3 and nuclear saturation density. The pasta phase is treated approximately by explicitly considering only the bubble phase when the nuclear volume fraction exceeds 0.7, with cubic interpolation between normal and bubble states for intermediate volume fractions.

## Key numbers

- The EOS covers baryon density range 10^5 to 10^15 g/cm^3 and temperature range 10^9 to 10^12 K
- The surface tension for symmetric nuclei is sigma_0 = 1.15 MeV/fm^3, with surface symmetry energy S_s = 45.8 MeV
- The lower limit for surface tension of neutron-rich nuclei is set at sigma = 0.272 MeV/fm^3, obtained from the most neutron-rich nucleus (Z, A) = (1, 7)
- The bubble phase is assumed to begin when the nuclear volume fraction u_i exceeds 0.7, with normal phase below 0.3
- The translational energy includes an excluded-volume factor (1 - n_B/n_s) where n_s is the saturation density for symmetric nuclei
- The internal excitation of nuclei uses parameters a(A_i) = (A_i/8)(1 - 0.8 A_i^{-1/3}) MeV^{-1} and c_1 = 0.2 MeV^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the approach of Hempel (2010) by including very heavy nuclei (Z greater than 100) via a mass formula and by incorporating the pasta phase, which was ignored in that prior work. It aims to merge features of the Shen (1998) EOS (RMF for uniform matter) with the Lattimer-Swesty (1991) EOS (pasta phase treatment) while providing nuclear abundance distributions needed for electron capture and coherent neutrino scattering calculations. The EOS table is under construction and will include weak interaction rates.
