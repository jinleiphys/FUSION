# The domain of validity of fluid dynamics and the onset of cavitation in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions

> If the bulk viscosity of QCD matter is large, the effective pressure of the hot and dense matter created in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions can become negative, leading to instabilities in the evolution of the plasma. In the context of heavy ion collisions, this effect is sometimes referred to as cavitation. In this contribution we discuss the onset of cavitation in event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations of ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions at LHC energies. We estimate how large the bulk viscosity of the QGP has to be in the QCD (pseudo) phase transition region in order for the effective pressure of the system to actually become negative.

## Key claim
If the bulk viscosity over entropy density ratio reaches approximately 1 near the QCD phase transition region (around 180 MeV), the effective pressure in hydrodynamic simulations of ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions becomes negative, indicating cavitation and breakdown of the fluid-dynamical description. However, if the bulk viscosity is only about 0.3 in that same temperature region, cavitation does not occur even though the bulk viscosity still significantly affects the system evolution.

## Method
The authors performed event-by-event relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics simulations of central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies using the MUSIC code, with initial conditions from the MC-Glauber model. They solved the time-evolution equations for bulk viscous pressure and shear-stress tensor derived from kinetic theory, using two different parametrizations of the bulk viscosity over entropy density ratio that differ only in the narrow temperature window of 170-190 MeV around the QCD phase transition. The simulations tracked the ratio of bulk viscous pressure to thermodynamic pressure (Π/P₀) in space-time to identify regions where the effective pressure becomes negative.

## Key numbers
- Bulk viscosity over entropy density ratio at T_c=180 MeV for parametrization 1: ζ/s(T_c) ≈ 0.3
- Bulk viscosity over entropy density ratio at T_c=180 MeV for parametrization 2: ζ/s(T_c) ≈ 1
- Temperature domain where the two parametrizations differ: 170 < T < 190 MeV
- Initial time for hydrodynamic simulation: τ₀ = 0.6 fm
- By increasing ζ/s(T_c) in parametrization 1 from 0.3 to 0.6, cavitation starts to occur
- Time steps analyzed for spatial profiles: τ = 2.6 fm, 4.6 fm, and 6.6 fm

## Context
This work extends earlier theoretical studies by Torrieri (2008) and Rajagopal (2009) who proposed that large bulk viscosity could cause cavitation in QCD matter. The authors use realistic event-by-event simulations with the MUSIC code (developed by the same group) to test whether cavitation actually occurs under LHC conditions. The results show that the threshold for cavitation lies between ζ/s ≈ 0.3 and ζ/s ≈ 1 near T_c, which provides a constraint on the bulk viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma and indicates that future hydrodynamic simulations must account for the possibility of negative effective pressures when bulk viscosity is large.
