# An attractive spin-orbit potential from the Skyrme model

> We derive the nucleon-nucleon isoscalar spin-orbit potential from the Skyrme model and find good agreement with the Paris potential. This solves a problem that has been open for more than thirty years and gives a new geometric understanding of the spin-orbit force. Our calculation is based on the dipole approximation to skyrmion dynamics and higher order perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The Skyrme model, using the dipole approximation and higher-order perturbation theory, produces an isoscalar spin-orbit potential that matches the Paris potential at large separations, solving a thirty-year-old problem. However, the calculation is only valid when the perturbation is small compared to energy differences, corresponding to separations greater than approximately 1.25 fm, and the agreement at mid-range separations may be an artefact of the approximations made.

## Method

The authors derived the nucleon-nucleon isoscalar spin-orbit potential from the Skyrme model by treating widely separated skyrmions as relativistic dipoles and constructing an interaction Lagrangian in terms of relative separation, orientation matrices, and angular velocities. They quantised the system using the Laplace-Beltrami operator and applied degenerate perturbation theory to second order in the interaction strength, projecting onto the lowest-energy eigenspace corresponding to two spin-1/2 nucleons. The calculation involved evaluating contributions from both first-order and second-order terms in the perturbation expansion, including sums over intermediate states of nucleons and delta resonances.

## Key numbers

- The spin-orbit potential at leading order for large separations with zero pion mass scales as \(H_{LS}^2 \sim -101\hbar\rho^2/972M X^4\Lambda^2\), giving a negative (attractive) sign.
- The calculation is valid for separations satisfying \(\rho/X^3 < \hbar^2/\Lambda\), which corresponds to \(X > 1.25\) fm.
- The parameters used were \(F_\pi = 108\) MeV, \(e = 3.93\), and \(m_\pi = 149\) MeV, with the dimensionless constant \(C_1 = 1.815\).
- The first-order contribution to the spin-orbit potential is \(H_{LS}^1 = -\frac{\rho^2\hbar e^{-2s}}{3M\Lambda^2 X^4}\boldsymbol{L}\cdot(\boldsymbol{\sigma}_1+\boldsymbol{\sigma}_2)(3+4s+s^2)\), where \(s = m_\pi X/\hbar\).

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to calculate the spin-orbit potential from the Skyrme model, which consistently produced the wrong sign using the product approximation (OSYKS 1988, RD 1988, ASW 1993). The authors argue that the product approximation is unreliable except at large separations, and that their use of the dipole approximation and higher-order perturbation theory is more reliable. The result enables future calculation of the complete nucleon-nucleon potential from the Skyrme model and could be adapted for use in holographic QCD and for modelling halo nuclei.
