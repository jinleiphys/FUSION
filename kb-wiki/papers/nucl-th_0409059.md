# The Skyrme energy functional and low lying 2+ states in Sn, Cd and Te isotopes

> We study the predictive power of Skyrme forces with respect to low lying quadrupole spectra along the chains of Sn, Cd, and Te isotopes. Excitation energies and B(E2) values for the lowest quadrupole states are computed from a collective Schroedinger equation which as deduced through collective path generated by constraint Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) plus self-consistent cranking for the dynamical response. We compare the results from four different Skyrme forces, all treated with two different pairing forces (volume versus density-dependent pairing). The region around the neutron shell closure N=82 is very sensitive to changes in the Skyrme while the mid-shell isotopes in the region N<82 depend mainly on the adjustment of pairing. The neutron rich isotopes are most sensitive and depend on both aspects.

# The Skyrme energy functional and low lying 2+ states in Sn, Cd and Te isotopes

## Key claim

The predictive power of Skyrme forces for low-lying quadrupole spectra in Sn, Cd, and Te isotopes depends critically on the interplay between the mean-field parameterization and the pairing recipe, with the region around the neutron shell closure N=82 being most sensitive to the Skyrme force choice while mid-shell isotopes (N<82) depend mainly on pairing adjustments; however, the neutron-rich isotopes above N=82 are most sensitive and depend on both aspects simultaneously, and the force SkO fails to reproduce experimental E(2+) energies at the doubly magic 132Sn because its fitting strategy squeezed the spectral gap too much.

## Method

The authors computed excitation energies and B(E2) values for the lowest 2+ quadrupole states by constructing a collective Bohr-Hamiltonian from a collective path generated through constrained Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) plus self-consistent cranking for the dynamical response, using the generator coordinate method with Gaussian overlap approximation (GCM-GOA). Four different Skyrme forces (SkM*, SLy6, SkI3, and SkO) were each combined with two pairing recipes (volume delta-interaction and density-dependent delta-interaction), with microscopic calculations restricted to axial symmetry and interpolated into the full five-dimensional quadrupole space.

## Key numbers

- E(2+) in 132Sn: SkI3 gives 4.36 MeV, SLy6 gives 3.76 MeV, SkM* gives 3.94 MeV, SkO gives 2.41 MeV; experimental value is 4.04 MeV
- Spectral gaps in 132Sn: protons range from 6.0 MeV (SkO) to 6.6 MeV (SkI3); neutrons range from 4.0 MeV (SkO) to 6.6 MeV (SkI3)
- Effective masses m*/m for the forces: SkI3 = 0.6, SLy6 = 0.7, SkM* = 0.8, SkO = 0.9
- Pairing strengths for SkM*: DI proton 279.1 fm^-3, DI neutron 259.0 fm^-3; DDDI proton 990.0 fm^-3, DDDI neutron 802.0 fm^-3
- Pairing strengths for SkO: DI proton 253.0 fm^-3, DI neutron 269.0 fm^-3; DDDI proton 1007.4 fm^-3, DDDI neutron 893.7 fm^-3
- The lowest 1-particle-1-hole energies in 132Sn are approximately 1.5 MeV above the computed E(2+) values for all forces
- B(E2) values for N <= 82 differ from experimental data by about a factor of two across all forces

## Context

This work extends the program of connecting microscopic mean-field models to collective nuclear spectra, building on the GCM-GOA framework established by Reinhard (1987) and the self-consistent cranking approach of Goeke (1978). It provides a systematic comparison of four Skyrme parameterizations (SkM*, SLy6, SkI3, SkO) that had previously been fitted primarily to ground-state properties, revealing that low-energy vibrational spectra can discriminate between forces that otherwise give comparable bulk properties. The finding that SkO fails at the doubly magic 132Sn due to its fitting strategy for two-nucleon shell gaps at 208Pb demonstrates the need for internal coherence between mean-field and collective observables. The results enable future tuning of both Skyrme forces and pairing strengths using spectroscopic data, particularly in neutron-rich regions above N=82 where experimental B(E2) values would be most discriminating.
