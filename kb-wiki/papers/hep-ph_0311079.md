# Electromagnetic Form Factor of a Composed Particle in the Light-Front

> The violation of the rotational symmetry constraint of the matrix elements of the plus component of the vector current, in the Drell-Yan frame, is studied using an analytical and covariant model of spin-1 composite particle. The contributions from pair terms or zero modes, if missed cause the violation of the rotational symmetry. We reanalyze the prescription by Grach and Kondratyuk to extract form factors which can eliminate theses contributions in particular models.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the Grach-Kondratyuk prescription for extracting electromagnetic form factors of a spin-1 composite particle eliminates the pair-term (Z-diagram) contributions to the plus component of the current in the Drell-Yan frame, but only for the specific case of a \(\gamma^\mu\) vertex structure; the derivative-coupling part of the vertex does not generate Z-diagrams at all.

## Method
The authors use an analytical, covariant model of a spin-1 composite particle (a two-fermion bound state) with a \(^3S_1\) meson vertex that includes both a \(\gamma^\mu\) term and a derivative coupling. They compute the plus component of the electromagnetic current in the Breit-frame with the Drell-Yan condition (purely transverse momentum transfer, \(q^+=0\)), using the pole dislocation method to isolate Z-diagram contributions from the region \(p^+ < k^+ < p^{\prime+}\). The matrix elements are evaluated in the instant-form polarization basis (cartesian representation), and the Grach-Kondratyuk prescription is applied to extract the form factors \(G_0\), \(G_1\), and \(G_2\).

## Key numbers
- The angular condition for the instant-form spin basis is given by \(J^{+}_{yy} = J^{+}_{zz}\).
- The Grach-Kondratyuk form factors are defined as: \(G_0^{GK} = \frac{1}{3}[J_{xx}^{+} + J_{yy}^{+} (2 - \eta) + \eta J_{zz}^{+}]\), \(G_1^{GK} = [J_{yy} - J_{zz}^{+} - \frac{J_{zx}^{+}}{\eta}]\), \(G_2^{GK} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{3}[J_{xx}^{+} + J_{yy}^{+} (-1 - \eta) + \eta J_{zz}^{+}]\), where \(\eta = q^2 / (4 m_v^2)\).
- For the \(\gamma^\mu\) vertex, the Z-diagram contributions satisfy \(J^{+Z}_{xx}[\gamma\gamma] = -\eta J^{+Z}_{zz}[\gamma\gamma]\) and \(J^{+Z}_{zx}[\gamma\gamma] = -\sqrt{\eta} J^{+Z}_{zz}[\gamma\gamma]\), leading to \(G_0^{GK,Z} = G_1^{GK,Z} = G_2^{GK,Z} = 0\).
- The trace \(Tr[dd]_{yy}^{Bad}\) for the derivative coupling vanishes, and the Z-diagram integrals for the derivative vertex vanish in the limit \(\delta^+ \rightarrow 0\) due to denominators \([6]\) and \([7]\).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Pacheco (1997) and Pacheco (1998) on rotational symmetry breaking in light-front descriptions of spin-1 particles, and generalizes the result of JI (2001) that the Grach-Kondratyuk prescription (Grach and Kondratyuk, 1984) cancels pair-term contributions when using light-cone polarization states. The present paper shows the same cancellation holds for instant-form polarization states in the cartesian representation, and also examines a derivative-coupling vertex. The full calculation for the complete \(^3S_1\) vertex is stated to be in progress, and the results could be applied to realistic studies of the \(\rho\)-meson or deuteron elastic photo-absorption processes.
