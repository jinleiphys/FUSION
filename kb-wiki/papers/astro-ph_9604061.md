# The MSW effect in a fluctuating matter density

> We consider the effect on matter-enhanced neutrino flavor transformation of a randomly fluctuating, delta-correlated matter density. The fluctuations will produce a distribution of neutrino survival probabilities. We find the mean and variance of the distribution for the case of solar neutrinos, and discuss the possibility of placing a limit on solar density fluctuations using neutrino data.

## Key claim

Random, delta-correlated fluctuations in the solar electron density produce a distribution of neutrino survival probabilities whose mean and variance can be computed; the fluctuations tend to suppress the neutrino flavor transition except in the adiabatic region where they can enhance it, and the effect is most noticeable when neutrinos are produced near the MSW resonance region.

## Method

The authors model the fluctuating electron density as white noise (delta-correlated Gaussian noise) added to the Bahcall-Pinsonneault Standard Solar Model density profile, with the fluctuation amplitude parameterized by the root-mean-square value ⟨β⟩_rms and a fixed correlation length τ = 10 km. They solve the Redfield-type evolution equations for the averaged density matrix (to obtain the mean survival probability) and for higher moments (to obtain the variance), using numerical integration for several mixing angles and fluctuation amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Correlation length τ is taken as 10 km, leading to the constraint sin2θ Δm²/E ≪ 3.95 × 10⁻⁵ eV²/MeV for the delta-correlation approximation to hold.
- For sin²2θ = 0.01 and Δm²/E = 1.63 × 10⁻⁵ eV²/MeV (giving a 60% survival probability), a 1% density fluctuation yields σ/⟨P_e⟩ = 0.15.
- The variance σ² saturates at 1/12 (independent of mixing parameters) for large noise amplitudes, corresponding to a uniform probability distribution between 0 and 1.
- For small mixing angles, the standard deviation σ peaks sharply around Δm²/E = 1.6 × 10⁻⁵ eV²/MeV; for sin²2θ = 0.7, a similar peak appears around Δm²/E = 1.6 × 10⁻⁶ eV²/MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Redfield-equation formalism developed by Loreti and Balantekin (1995) for neutrinos in fluctuating matter, previously applied to supernova environments, to the solar neutrino case. It builds on earlier studies of periodic density perturbations by Koonin (1987) and Haxton (1987), and on step-function density changes by Krastev and Smirnov (1989). The results enable placing observational limits on solar density fluctuations using existing radiochemical experiments (GALLEX, SAGE, Homestake) and suggest that future real-time detectors like HELLAZ and Borexino could probe shorter-timescale fluctuations, while Super-Kamiokande and SNO are less suited due to their higher energy thresholds.
