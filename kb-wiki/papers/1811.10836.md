# Weak interference between the 1$^-$ states in the vicinity of $α$-particle threshold of $^{16}$O

> The subthreshold 1$^-_1$ state at an excitation energy $E_x = 7.12$ MeV in $^{16}$O has been believed to enhance the $S$-factor of $^{12}$C($α$,$γ$)$^{16}$O. The enhancement seems to originate from strong interference between 1$^-_1$ and 1$^-_2$ ($E_x\approx 9.6$ MeV) in the vicinity of the $α$-particle threshold. However, weak interference between them and a resulting small $E$1 $S$-factor are exemplified with $R$-matrix theory. Including a higher-order correction of the resonance parameters, the present example appears to reproduce the experimental data consistently. It would therefore be possible that the $E$1 $S$-factor is reduced at low energies.

## Key claim

Using R-matrix theory with a higher-order correction of the resonance parameters, the author demonstrates that interference between the 1\(^-\)_1 state at \(E_x = 7.12\) MeV and the 1\(^-\)_2 state at \(E_x \approx 9.6\) MeV in \(^{16}\)O may be weak rather than strong, potentially reducing the E1 S-factor of \(^{12}\)C(\(\alpha\),\(\gamma\))\(^{16}\)O at low energies.

## Method

The author applied R-matrix theory using formal resonance parameters obtained from an exact expression that includes a higher-order correction term \(Q_{nL}\), which was set to zero in most previous R-matrix analyses. Reduced \(\alpha\)-particle widths for the 1\(^-\)_1 and 1\(^-\)_2 states were estimated from the author's prior potential model calculations (Katsuma 2008, 2010), and the R-matrix calculations were compared with experimental data on the E1 S-factor, \(\beta\)-delayed \(\alpha\)-particle spectrum of \(^{16}\)N, and \(p\)-wave phase shift of \(\alpha\)+\(^{12}\)C elastic scattering.

## Key numbers

- Observed (Breit-Wigner) parameters for 1\(^-\)_1: \(E_{11} = -0.0451\) MeV, \(\gamma_{11} = 0.345\) MeV\(^{1/2}\)
- Observed (Breit-Wigner) parameters for 1\(^-\)_2: \(E_{21} = 2.434\) MeV, \(\gamma_{21} = 0.850\) MeV\(^{1/2}\)
- Channel radius: \(a_c = 4.75\) fm
- ANC of 1\(^-\)_1: \(5.0 \times 10^{28}\) fm\(^{-1}\)
- Experimental \(\alpha\)-particle width of 1\(^-\)_2: \(\Gamma_\alpha^{\rm exp} = 420 \pm 20\) keV; theoretical reproduction in this work: \(\Gamma_\alpha^{\rm th} = 432\) keV
- Multi-level parameter for 1\(^-\)_1: \(d_{11} = -1.0133\); for 1\(^-\)_2: \(d_{21} = 0\)

## Context

This work extends the author's previous potential model predictions (Katsuma 2008, 2010) of a small E1 S-factor for \(^{12}\)C(\(\alpha\),\(\gamma\))\(^{16}\)O, which contradicted the prevailing assumption of strong interference between the two 1\(^-\) states. The R-matrix example with the higher-order correction reproduces experimental data on the \(\beta\)-delayed \(\alpha\)-spectrum of \(^{16}\)N, the \(p\)-wave phase shift, and the \(\alpha\)-decay width of 1\(^-\)_2, while earlier R-matrix calculations (Azu94) with \(Q_{nL}=0\) and narrow reduced widths did not reproduce the experimental 1\(^-\)_2 width. The author suggests this approach enables a hybrid model combining E1 from R-matrix with E2 from the potential model (Katsuma 2008, 2012) for the total S-factor and reaction rates.
