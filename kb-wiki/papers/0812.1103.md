# Relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory with Density Dependent Meson-Nucleon Couplings

> Relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) theory with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings is presented. The integro-differential RHFB equations are solved by expanding the different components of the quasi-particle spinors in the complete set of eigen-solutions of the Dirac equations with Woods-Saxon potentials. Using the finite-range Gogny force D1S as an effective interaction in the pairing channel, systematic RHFB calculations are performed for Sn isotopes and N=82 isotones. It is demonstrated that an appropriate description of both mean field and pairing effects can be obtained within RHFB theory with finite range Gogny pairing forces. Better systematics are also found in the regions from the stable to the neutron-rich side with the inclusion of Fock terms, especially in the presence of $ρ$-tensor couplings.

## Key claim

The paper presents Relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) theory with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings as a natural extension of Density Dependent Relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory, demonstrating that an appropriate description of both mean field and pairing effects can be obtained for Sn isotopes and N=82 isotones, with better systematics from stable to neutron-rich nuclei when Fock terms (especially ρ-tensor couplings) are included.

## Method

The integro-differential RHFB equations are solved by expanding the upper and lower components of the quasi-particle spinors in the complete set of eigen-solutions of the Dirac equation with Woods-Saxon potentials (DWS basis). The finite-range Gogny force D1S is used as the effective interaction in the pairing channel, and systematic calculations are performed for even-even Sn isotopes from 106Sn to 136Sn and N=82 isotones. The results are compared with RHFB calculations using zero-range delta pairing forces and with DDRHF calculations using BCS pairing.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies per nucleon E_B/A for Sn isotopes from N=56 to N=86: values range from -8.4339 MeV (N=56, Gogny) to -8.1934 MeV (N=86, Gogny), with experimental values ranging from -8.4327 MeV to -8.1990 MeV
- Neutron radii r_n for Sn isotopes from N=56 to N=86: values range from 4.456 fm (N=56, Gogny) to 5.050 fm (N=86, Gogny)
- For magic nuclei (N=50, 82), RHFB with Gogny, Delta, and BCS(δ) all give identical binding energies and neutron radii (e.g., N=82: E_B/A = -8.3579 MeV, r_n = 4.935 fm for all three methods)
- DWS basis parameters for general applications: R_max = 20 fm, N_F = 28 positive energy states, N_D = 12 negative energy states; for weakly bound systems: R_max = 24 fm, N_F = 36
- BCS pairing gives slightly larger neutron radii (~0.01 fm) than Bogoliubov pairing for non-magic nuclei

## Context

This work extends Density Dependent Relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory (Long, 2006; Long, 2007) to include pairing correlations via the Bogoliubov transformation, going beyond the BCS approximation used in earlier DDRHF calculations. The approach builds on earlier Relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov (RHB) theory (Kucharek, 1991; Gonzalez, 1996; Meng, 1996; Meng, 1998) which showed that scattering of Cooper pairs into the continuum plays an important role for neutron halos. The use of the Dirac Woods-Saxon basis (Zhou, 2003) enables treatment of weakly bound nuclei near the drip lines where the simple BCS method may break down.
