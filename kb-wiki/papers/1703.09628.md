# Correlation femtoscopy study at NICA and STAR energies within a viscous hydrodynamic plus cascade model

> Correlation femtoscopy allows one to measure the space-time characteristics of particle production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions due to the effects of quantum statistics (QS) and final state interactions (FSI). The main features of the femtoscopy measurements at top RHIC and LHC energies are considered as a manifestation of strong collective flow and are well interpreted within hydrodynamic models employing equation of state (EoS) with a crossover type transition between Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) and hadron gas phases. The femtoscopy at lower energies was intensively studied at AGS and SPS accelerators and is being studied now in the Beam Energy Scan program (BES) at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider in the context of exploration of the QCD phase diagram. In this article we present femtoscopic observables calculated for Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7 - 62.4$ GeV in a viscous hydro + cascade model vHLLE+UrQMD and their dependence on the EoS of thermalized matter.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that femtoscopic observables in Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7 - 62.4\) GeV, calculated with the vHLLE+UrQMD viscous hydrodynamic plus cascade model, show sensitivity to the equation of state (EoS) of thermalized matter, but the model parameters were tuned only to reproduce basic hadronic observables (rapidity distributions, transverse momentum spectra, and elliptic flow) using the crossover-type EoS, not the femtoscopic data themselves.

## Method

The authors used the hybrid vHLLE+UrQMD model, which combines the UrQMD approach for the early and late stages of the collision with a (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic evolution for the hot and dense expanding matter. The initial state for hydrodynamics was generated from UrQMD at a constant longitudinal proper time \(\tau_0\), with energy, momentum, and charges distributed to fluid cells via Gaussian profiles. Two equations of state were compared: a chiral model EoS with a crossover transition (XPT EoS) and a bag model EoS with a first-order phase transition (1PT EoS). The fluid-to-particle transition (particlization) was set at a constant energy density \(\epsilon_{\rm sw}=0.5\) GeV/fm\(^3\), and the resulting hadrons were further processed with the UrQMD cascade. Femtoscopic correlation functions were calculated from the model using the weight procedure for non-interacting identical pions, and Gaussian femtoscopy radii were extracted by fitting the three-dimensional correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- The hydrodynamic starting time \(\tau_0\) ranges from 3.2 fm/c at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) GeV to 0.7 fm/c at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 62.4\) GeV (Table I).
- The shear viscosity over entropy ratio \(\eta/s\) ranges from 0.2 at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) GeV to 0.08 at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 62.4\) GeV (Table I).
- Average pion creation times at the particlization surface with the 1PT EoS: \(\bar{t} = 7.24\) fm/c (RMS 2.84 fm/c) at 7.7 GeV, and \(\bar{t} = 7.00\) fm/c (RMS 2.82 fm/c) at 62.4 GeV (Table II).
- Average pion creation times at the particlization surface with the XPT EoS: \(\bar{t} = 6.16\) fm/c (RMS 2.01 fm/c) at 7.7 GeV, and \(\bar{t} = 6.60\) fm/c (RMS 2.63 fm/c) at 62.4 GeV (Table II).
- Average pion emission times at last interactions with the 1PT EoS: \(\bar{t} = 13.15\) fm/c (RMS 6.56 fm/c) at 7.7 GeV, and \(\bar{t} = 14.11\) fm/c (RMS 8.50 fm/c) at 62.4 GeV (Table II).
- Average pion emission times at last interactions with the XPT EoS: \(\bar{t} = 11.61\) fm/c (RMS 6.26 fm/c) at 7.7 GeV, and \(\bar{t} = 12.72\) fm/c (RMS 7.81 fm/c) at 62.4 GeV (Table II).
- The \(R_{\rm out}\) radius with the 1PT EoS is generally larger than with the XPT EoS by not more than 0.5 fm at any collision energy (Section III.B).
- Single Gaussian fit radii in PRF at 7.7 GeV with the 1PT EoS: \(R_{\rm out} = 11.10 \pm 0.01\) fm, \(R_{\rm side} = 4.19 \pm 0.01\) fm (Table III).
- Single Gaussian fit radii in PRF at 7.7 GeV with the XPT EoS: \(R_{\rm out} = 10.03 \pm 0.01\) fm, \(R_{\rm side} = 4.16 \pm 0.01\) fm (Table III).

## Context

This work extends the application of the vHLLE+UrQMD hybrid model, previously tuned for basic observables at RHIC BES energies (Karpenko 2016), to femtoscopic observables. It directly addresses the question raised by STAR Collaboration measurements (STAR 2015) of a wide maximum in \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}\) near \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \sim 20\) GeV, which could be related to a change in the type of phase transition. The study enables future comparisons with NICA and FAIR data, and demonstrates that the crossover-type EoS (XPT) provides a better description of the femtoscopic data than the first-order transition EoS (1PT), though the model with XPT EoS shows a monotonic increase in \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}\) rather than the observed maximum.
