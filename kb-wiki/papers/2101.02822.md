# Cumulants and factorial cumulants in the 3-dimensional Ising universality class

> The high-order cumulants and factorial cumulants of conserved charges are suggested to study the critical dynamics in heavy ion collisions. In this paper, using parametric representation of the 3-dimensional Ising model, the sign distribution on the phase diagram and temperature dependence of the cumulants and factorial cumulants is studied and compared. In the vicinity of the critical point, the cumulants and factorial cumulants can not be distinguished. Far away from the critical point, sign changes occur in the factorial cumulants comparing with the same order cumulants. The cause of these sign changes is analysed. They may be used to measure the distance to the critical point.

# Cumulants and factorial cumulants in the 3-dimensional Ising universality class

## Key claim

In the 3-dimensional Ising model, cumulants and factorial cumulants of the order parameter are indistinguishable near the critical point, but far from the critical point the factorial cumulants develop sign changes relative to the same-order cumulants; these sign changes may serve as a measure of the distance to the critical point, but only in the ordered (low-temperature) phase where the sign of factorial cumulants is dominated by the first-order cumulant (magnetization).

## Method

The authors used the parametric representation of the 3-dimensional Ising model, parameterizing magnetization M and reduced temperature t by variables R and theta, with the equation of state given by h(theta) = theta(3 - 2 theta^2). They derived analytic expressions for the first six cumulants kappa_n from derivatives of magnetization with respect to magnetic field H, then expressed the first six factorial cumulants C_n as linear combinations of the cumulants. Density plots on the (H, t) phase diagram and temperature dependence at fixed magnetic fields H = 0.01, 0.05, 0.2, and -0.05 were computed and compared.

## Key numbers

- Critical exponents used: beta = 0.3267(10) and delta = 4.786(14) for the 3-dimensional Ising universality class; approximate values beta = 1/3 and delta = 5 were taken for the analytic calculations
- Ratio of maximum to minimum for kappa_4 temperature dependence approximates -28; for kappa_6 approximates -6
- Ratio of minimum to maximum for kappa_5 temperature dependence approximates -10 at positive magnetic fields and -0.1 at negative magnetic fields
- Magnetic field values studied for temperature dependence: H = 0.01, 0.05, 0.2, and -0.05

## Context

This work extends the study of factorial cumulants in the Ising model by Pan, Xu, and Wu (2020) which showed that signs of second to fourth order factorial cumulants can exclude regions near the QCD critical point. It also builds on the parametric representation approach of linear parametric models and on the relation between cumulants and factorial cumulants established in PhysRevC96024910. The results enable using comparisons of cumulant and factorial cumulant signs to estimate the distance to the critical point in heavy-ion collision experiments, complementing the critical fluctuation model of PhysRevC93034915 which found cumulants and factorial cumulants indistinguishable near the critical point.
