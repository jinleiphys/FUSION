# Excited state quantum phase transitions in many-body systems

> Phenomena analogous to ground state quantum phase transitions have recently been noted to occur among states throughout the excitation spectra of certain many-body models. These excited state phase transitions are manifested as simultaneous singularities in the eigenvalue spectrum (including the gap or level density), order parameters, and wave function properties. In this article, the characteristics of excited state quantum phase transitions are investigated. The finite-size scaling behavior is determined at the mean field level. It is found that excited state quantum phase transitions are universal to two-level bosonic and fermionic models with pairing interactions.

## Key claim

Excited state quantum phase transitions (ESQPTs) are universal to two-level bosonic and fermionic models with pairing interactions, occurring as simultaneous singularities in the eigenvalue spectrum, order parameters, and wave function properties throughout the excitation spectrum, not just in the ground state.

## Method

The authors analyze the semiclassical dynamics of two-level pairing models using coherent states to derive a classical Hamiltonian with a quadratic-quartic potential, then apply first-order WKB quantization to determine the action integral and level density. They extend the semiclassical analysis of Refs. Leyvraz (2005) and Cejnar (2006) to characterize the singularity in the eigenvalue spectrum and finite-size scaling behavior at the mean field level, and perform numerical calculations for the full quantum problem in the U(3) vibron model.

## Key numbers

* The ground state quantum phase transition occurs at the critical control parameter value xi_c = 1/5 for the Hamiltonian H = (1-xi)/N * Nhat_b - xi/N^2 * (s^dagger b + b^dagger s) dot (s^dagger b + b^dagger s)
* The top of the barrier in the classical potential is always at energy E = 0, independent of xi
* For the U(3) vibron model with N = 100 particles, eigenvalue spectra are shown for l = 0 states at several xi values including the critical point xi = 0.2
* The semiclassical gap Delta(E) = 2 pi N^{-1} (partial S / partial E)^{-1} vanishes at E = 0, where the classical period tau becomes infinite

## Context

This work extends earlier observations of excited state singularities in the Lipkin model by Heiss (2002, 2005) and Leyvraz (2005), and in the interacting boson model (IBM) by Heinze (2006), Macek (2006), and Cejnar (2006). The paper establishes that ESQPTs occur in a broader class of many-body models than previously identified, specifically all two-level bosonic and fermionic models with pairing interactions. The results enable future investigations into the finite-size scaling behavior and experimental signatures of ESQPTs in nuclear, molecular, and condensed matter systems.
