# Neutron skin and signature of the $N$ = 14 shell gap found from measured proton radii of $^{17-22}$N

> A thick neutron skin emerges from the first determination of root mean square radii of the proton distributions for $^{17-22}$N from charge changing cross section measurements around 900$A$ MeV at GSI. Neutron halo effects are signaled for $^{22}$N from an increase in the proton and matter radii. The radii suggest an unconventional shell gap at $N$ = 14 arising from the attractive proton-neutron tensor interaction, in good agreement with shell model calculations. $Ab$ $initio$, in-medium similarity re-normalization group, calculations with a state-of-the-art chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interaction reproduce well the data approaching the neutron drip-line isotopes but are challenged in explaining the complete isotopic trend of the radii.

## Key claim

The first determination of point proton radii for neutron-rich nitrogen isotopes (¹⁷–²²N) from charge-changing cross sections reveals a thick neutron skin and a dip in proton radii at ²¹N, which the authors interpret as evidence for a shell gap at N = 14 arising from the attractive proton-neutron tensor interaction. The ab initio calculations reproduce the data for the most neutron-rich isotopes but are challenged in explaining the complete isotopic trend of the radii, particularly the observed decrease from ¹⁷N to ²¹N.

## Method

Charge-changing cross sections (σ_cc) were measured for ¹⁴,¹⁵N and ¹⁷–²²N at approximately 900A MeV using the transmission technique at the FRS fragment separator at GSI. Beams were produced by fragmentation of ²²Ne and ⁴⁰Ar on a beryllium target, and the isotopes were identified event-by-event using magnetic rigidity, time-of-flight, and energy loss in a MUSIC detector. Point proton radii were extracted from the measured σ_cc using a Glauber model analysis with a harmonic oscillator point proton density distribution, and matter radii were derived by combining the proton radii with interaction cross section data.

## Key numbers

* Point proton radius of ¹⁴N: 2.43(4) fm (from σ_cc), consistent with the electron scattering value of 2.43(1) fm
* Point proton radius of ²¹N: 2.49(3) fm (R_p^ex,avg), showing a local minimum compared to neighboring isotopes
* Point proton radius of ²²N: 2.53(3) fm (R_p^ex,avg), showing an increase beyond ²¹N
* Matter radius of ²²N: 3.08(12) fm, the largest among the measured isotopes
* Neutron skin thickness (R_n - R_p) increases linearly from ¹⁸N to ²²N, with a small decrease at N = 14 (²¹N)
* One-neutron removal momentum distribution width: 160±32 MeV/c for ²¹N and 77±32 MeV/c for ²²N, indicating a change from l = 2 to l = 0 neutron orbital dominance
* Valence neutron probability in the 2s₁/₂ orbital for ²²N: 50%–100%, consistent with momentum distribution measurements

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shell evolution in neutron-rich nuclei, particularly the N = 14 shell gap that was first indicated in ²²O from excited state measurements (Beaumel and Stanoiu, early 2000s) and later studied in nitrogen isotopes through knockout reactions (Rodriguez and collaborators, 2011; Diaz and collaborators, 2018). The proton radii provide a new observable for shell gaps, complementing earlier charge radius measurements in neon isotopes (Marinova and collaborators, 2011) that showed local minima at N = 8 and 14. The results enable further development of ab initio theories, as the coupled-cluster and VS-IMSRG calculations with the chiral N²LO_sat interaction reproduce the data for the most neutron-rich isotopes but fail to predict the decreasing trend from ¹⁷N to ²¹N, similar to discrepancies observed in neutron-rich calcium isotopes (Garcia Ruiz and collaborators, 2016).
