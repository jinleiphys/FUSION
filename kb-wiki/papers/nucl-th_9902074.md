# Three-body properties in hot and dense nuclear matter

> We derive three-body equations valid at finite densities and temperatures. These are based on the cluster mean field approach consistently including proper self energy corrections and the Pauli blocking. As an application we investigate the binding energies of triton and determine the Mott densities and momenta relevant for a many particle description of nuclear matter in a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach. The method, however is not restricted to nuclear physics problems but may also be relevant, e.g., to treat three-particle correlations in weekly doped semiconducters or strongly coupled dense plasmas.

## Key claim

The paper derives three-body equations valid at finite densities and temperatures, but the numerical results for triton binding energies and Mott densities rely on approximations including angle-averaged Fermi functions, effective masses for the nucleon self energy, and a simplified form of the effective potential that holds only for low densities where \(f^2 \ll f\).

## Method

The authors derive three-body Green functions within the cluster mean field (cluster Hartree-Fock) approximation, truncating the hierarchy of Green function equations using a ladder approximation and decoupling the one-, two-, and three-particle Green functions. They solve the resulting homogeneous three-body bound state equations (Eqs. 25 and 26) for the triton using a separable Yamaguchi potential and a high-rank separable version (Paris EST) of the Paris potential, incorporating Pauli blocking factors and self energy corrections. The calculations are performed at finite center-of-mass momentum relative to the medium, with angle-averaged Fermi functions used for simplicity.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy per nucleon at zero density and temperature (isolated case) is approximately \(-2.8\) MeV for the Yamaguchi potential (from Fig. 1, inferred from the y-intercept).
- At \(T = 10\) MeV and zero center-of-mass momentum, the triton binding energy per nucleon crosses the continuum threshold (deuteron binding energy per nucleon \(E_d/2 \simeq -1.11\) MeV) at a nuclear density near \(n \approx 0.003\) fm\(^{-3}\) (from Fig. 1).
- At \(T = 20\) MeV and zero center-of-mass momentum, the Mott transition density for the triton is higher than at \(T = 10\) MeV, with the binding energy per nucleon crossing the continuum near \(n \approx 0.005\) fm\(^{-3}\) (from Fig. 1).
- The nuclear matter saturation density is given as \(n_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- For \(T = 10\) MeV, the Mott momentum for the triton at a density of \(n \approx 0.01\) fm\(^{-3}\) is approximately \(P_{\rm Mott} \approx 0.5\) fm\(^{-1}\) (from Fig. 3).

## Context

This work extends the cluster mean field approach previously applied to two-body correlations (Röpke 1983) and deuteron break-up reactions (Beyer 1996, 1997) to three-body bound states in nuclear matter. The derived equations and numerical results for triton Mott densities and momenta are intended for use in a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach to describe nuclear matter as a mixture of light nuclei, relevant for the final stage of heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies. The method is also noted as applicable to three-particle correlations in weakly doped semiconductors (trions) and strongly coupled dense plasmas.
