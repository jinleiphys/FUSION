# Meson Emission Model of Psi to N Nbar m Charmonium Strong Decays

> In this paper we consider a sequential "meson emission" mechanism for charmonium decays of the type Psi -> N Nbar m, where Psi is a generic charmonium state, N is a nucleon and m is a light meson. This decay mechanism, which may not be dominant in general, assumes that an NNbar pair is created during charmonium annihilation, and the light meson m is emitted from the outgoing nucleon or antinucleon line. A straightforward generalization of this model can incorporate intermediate N* resonances. We derive Dalitz plot event densities for the cases Psi = eta_c, J/psi, chi_c0, chi_c1} and psi' and m = pi0, f0 and omega (and implicitly, any 0^{-+}, 0^{++} or 1^{--} final light meson). It may be possible to separate the contribution of this decay mechanism to the full decay amplitude through characteristic event densities. For the decay subset Psi -> p pbar pi0 the two model parameters are known, so we are able to predict absolute numerical partial widths for Gamma(Psi -> p pbar pi0). In the specific case J/psi -> p pbar pi0 the predicted partial width and M_{p pi0} event distribution are intriguingly close to experiment. We also consider the possibility of scalar meson and glueball searches in Psi -> p pbar f0. If the meson emission contributions to Psi -> N Nbar m decays can be isolated and quantified, they can be used to estimate meson-nucleon strong couplings {g_NNm}, which are typically poorly known, and are a crucial input in meson exchange models of the NN interaction. The determination of g_NNpi from Jψ-> p pbar pi0 and the (poorly known) g_NNomega and the anomalous "strong magnetic" coupling kappa_{NNomega} from J/psi -> p pbar omega are considered as examples.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the sequential "meson emission" mechanism (charmonium decays to an intermediate nucleon-antinucleon pair, which then radiates a light meson) may not be the dominant decay mechanism in general, but its contribution can be isolated through characteristic Dalitz plot event densities, and for the specific case of J/psi to p pbar pi0 the predicted partial width and event distribution are intriguingly close to experiment.

## Method

The authors derived analytic Dalitz plot event densities for charmonium decays Psi to N Nbar m assuming a sequential meson emission mechanism, where the charmonium state decays to an intermediate N Nbar pair and the light meson is emitted from the outgoing nucleon or antinucleon line. They used known partial widths for Psi to p pbar to estimate the Psi-NN coupling constants and the well-known NNpi coupling (g_NNpi = 13.0) to predict absolute partial widths for Psi to p pbar pi0 decays. For the omega meson case, they included both Dirac (vector) and Pauli (anomalous magnetic) couplings in the NNomega vertex.

## Key numbers

- Predicted partial width for J/psi to p pbar pi0: 97 eV, compared to experimental value of 102 +/- 9 eV
- Extracted g_NNpi from J/psi to p pbar pi0: 13.3 +/- 0.6
- Extracted g_NNpi from psi' to p pbar pi0: 9.9 +/- 0.7
- Predicted partial width for eta_c to p pbar pi0: 1.7 keV
- Predicted partial width for chi_c0 to p pbar pi0: 2.6 keV (experimental: 6.0 +/- 1.3 keV)
- Predicted partial width for chi_c1 to p pbar pi0: 17 eV (experimental: 103 +/- 43 eV)
- Predicted partial width for psi' to p pbar pi0: 75 eV (experimental: 41 +/- 5 eV)
- J/psi to p pbar coupling constant g_J/psi pp = 1.62 x 10^-3

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous crossing-relation results for 2-to-2 processes p pbar to pi0 Psi (Barnes 2006) to the 1-to-3 decay processes Psi to p pbar m, and provides new formulas for f0 and omega final states not previously considered. The paper enables future experimental studies at BES and PANDA to isolate the meson emission mechanism contribution, which could then be used to extract poorly known meson-nucleon coupling constants (such as g_NNomega and kappa_NNomega) that are crucial inputs for meson exchange models of the NN interaction (Machleidt 1987, Nagels 1976, Stoks 1994). The results also suggest that J/psi to p pbar f0 decays could be used to search for light scalar mesons and glueball candidates.
