# Spin in relativistic quantum theory

> We discuss the role of spin in Poincaré invariant formulations of quantum mechanics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in Poincaré-invariant quantum mechanics, spin is not a unique observable; rather, an infinite number of spin operators exist, each defined by a choice of a standard four-momentum and a specific Lorentz transformation relating that standard vector to the physical momentum. The abstract's general "discussion" glosses over the central technical point that different spin definitions (canonical, helicity, light-front) are related by momentum-dependent rotations and that the choice affects how spins are coupled in few-body systems.

## Method

The authors construct spin operators as operator-valued functions of the Poincaré generators by first fixing a standard four-vector \(p_s\) (e.g., the rest momentum \((m,\mathbf{0})\)) and a parameterized set of Lorentz transformations \(\Lambda_s(p)\) that map \(p_s\) to any physical momentum \(p\). They then replace \(p\) by the four-momentum operator \(\underline{P}\) to obtain a Lorentz-transformation-valued operator, and define the \(s\)-spin via the Pauli-Lubanski vector \(W^\mu\) as \(\mathbf{j}_s = (1/m) \Lambda_{0s}^{-1}(\underline{P})^\mu{}_\nu W^\nu\). The commutation relations and transformation properties of the Poincaré generators are used to verify that these operators satisfy \(SU(2)\) commutation relations, commute with the four-momentum, and have a square equal to the spin Casimir operator.

## Key numbers

* The spin Casimir operator is given by \(\mathbf{j}^2 = W^2/M^2\) for massive systems with \(M>0\) (Eq. c.31).
* The canonical boost \(\Lambda_c(p)\) is parameterized by the momentum of a mass \(m\) particle initially at rest, with matrix elements such as \(\Lambda_c(p)^0{}_0 = p^0/m\) and \(\Lambda_c(p)^i{}_0 = p^i/m\) (Eq. b.19).
* The light-front boost in \(SL(2,\mathbb{C})\) representation has matrix elements \(\Lambda_f(p)_{11} = \sqrt{(p^0+p^3)/m}\) and \(\Lambda_f(p)_{21} = (p_1+ip_2)/\sqrt{m(p^0+p^3)}\) (Eq. d.5).

## Context

This work extends the foundational classification of unitary irreducible representations of the Poincaré group by Wigner (1939) and the ray-representation analysis by Bargmann (1954). It provides a general framework for constructing spin operators in relativistic few-body models, showing that any model can be related to a Bakamjian-Thomas model (Bakamjian, 1953) by an \(S\)-matrix-preserving unitary transformation. The paper enables systematic treatment of spin in relativistic three-nucleon problems and clarifies how different spin observables (canonical, helicity, light-front) are related, which is essential for comparing theoretical predictions with experimental observables.
