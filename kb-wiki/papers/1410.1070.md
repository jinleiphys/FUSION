# $π_{e3}$ form factor $f_{-}$ near the mass shell

> The generalized Ward-Takahashi identity (gWTI) in the pion sector for broken isotopic symmetry is derived and used for the model-independent calculation of the longitudinal form factor $f_{-}$ of the $π_{e3}$ vector vertex. The on-shell $f_{-}$ is found to be proportional to the mass difference of the pions and the difference between the vector isospin $T = 1$ and scalar isospin $ T = 2 $ pion radii. A numerical estimate of the form factor yields a value two times higher than the previous estimate from the quark model. Off-shell form factors are known to be ambiguous because of the gauge dependence and the freedom in the parameterization of the fields. The near-mass-shell $f_{-}$ appears to be an exception, allowing for experimental verification of the consequences of the gWTI. We calculate the near-mass-shell $f_{-}$ using the gWTI and dispersion techniques. The results are discussed in the context of the conservation of vector current (CVC) hypothesis.

## Key claim

The longitudinal form factor \(f_{-}\) of the \(\pi_{e3}\) vector vertex is uniquely defined near the mass shell (unlike generic off-shell form factors) and can be expressed model-independently in terms of the pion mass difference and the difference between the vector isospin \(T=1\) and scalar isospin \(T=2\) pion radii, with a numerical estimate yielding a value two times higher than the previous quark-model estimate.

## Method

The author derives a generalized Ward-Takahashi identity (gWTI) for broken isotopic symmetry in the pion sector, then uses it to relate the longitudinal form factor \(f_{-}\) of the weak vector current to the pion mass splitting and the isospin \(T=1\) and \(T=2\) pion radii. Dispersion techniques are applied to extract the \(T=2\) scalar pion radius from experimental \(\pi\pi\) scattering phase shifts, enabling a numerical evaluation of \(f_{-}\).

## Key numbers

* The on-shell form factor \(f_{-}\) is proportional to \((m_{\pi^{+}}^{2} - m_{\pi^{0}}^{2})/6\) times the difference \(\langle r^{2}\rangle_{v}^{T=1} - \langle r^{2}\rangle_{s}^{T=2}\) (Eq. 16).
* The scalar \(T=2\) pion radius \(\langle r^{2}\rangle_{s}^{T=2}\) is computed from the dispersion integral \(\frac{6}{\pi} \int_{4\mu^{2}}^{\infty} \frac{\delta^{T=2}(s')}{s'^{2}} ds'\) (Eq. B5), using phase shift data up to \(\sqrt{s_{\max}} = 2.1\) GeV.
* The numerical estimate of \(f_{-}\) yields a value two times higher than the previous estimate from the quark model (abstract).
* The scalar \(T=0\) pion radius from prior work is cited as \(\langle r^{2}\rangle_{s}^{T=0} = 0.61 \pm 0.04\ \mathrm{fm}^{2}\) (Cola 2001).

## Context

This work extends the generalized Ward-Takahashi identity approach to broken isotopic symmetry in the pion sector, providing a model-independent calculation of \(f_{-}\) that contrasts with earlier quark-model estimates. The result enables experimental tests of the gWTI via \(\pi^{+} \to \pi^{0} e^{+} \nu_{e} \gamma\) decay, and any deviation from the predicted relationship could indicate new physics at or above the electroweak scale. The dispersion treatment of the \(T=2\) pion radius builds on experimental \(\pi\pi\) scattering data from prior analyses (e.g., Cola 2001 for the \(T=0\) channel) and can be refined with semi-phenomenological models (Xiem 2014) and lattice QCD (Bean 2012).
