# Relativistic hybrid stars in light of the NICER PSR J0740+6620 radius measurement

> We explore the implications of the recent radius determination of PSR J0740+6620 by the NICER experiment combined with the neutron skin measurement by the PREX-II experiment and the associated inference of the slope of symmetry energy, for the structure of hybrid stars with a strong first-order phase transition from nucleonic to quark matter. We combine a covariant density-functional nucleonic equation of state (EOS) with a constant-speed-of-sound EOS for quark matter. We show that the radius and tidal deformability ranges obtained from GW170817 can be reconciled with the implication of the PREX-II experiment if there is a phase transition to quark matter in the low-mass compact star. In the high-mass segment, the EoS needs to be stiff to comply with the large-radius inference for PSR J0740+6620 and J0030+0451 with masses $M\simeq 2M_{\odot}$ and $M\simeq 1.4M_{\odot}$. We show that twin stars are not excluded, but the mass and radius ranges (with $M \geq M_\odot$) are restricted to narrow domains $ΔM_{\rm twin} \lesssim 0.05 M_\odot$ and $ΔR_{\rm twin} \sim 1.0$~km. We also show that the existence of twin configurations is compatible with the light companion in the GW190814 event being a hybrid star in the case of values of the sound-speed square $s=0.6$ and $s=1/3$.

# Relativistic hybrid stars in light of the NICER PSR J0740+6620 radius measurement

## Key claim
A strong first-order phase transition from nucleonic to quark matter at low densities can reconcile the large slope of symmetry energy inferred from the PREX-II experiment (L_sym ~ 106 ± 37 MeV) with the relatively small tidal deformability from GW170817, provided the quark-matter equation of state is stiff enough at high densities to match the large radius of PSR J0740+6620. Twin stars are not excluded, but their mass range is restricted to ΔM_twin ≲ 0.05 M⊙ and radius difference ΔR_twin ~ 1.0 km for sound-speed square s = 1.0, with larger radius differences possible for lower sound speeds.

## Method
The authors combined a covariant density-functional nucleonic equation of state (based on the DDME2 parametrization) with a constant-speed-of-sound (CSS) parametrization for quark matter, assuming a sharp first-order phase transition. They constructed six representative nucleonic EOSs by varying the skewness Q_sat (−500, −100, 900 MeV) and the slope of symmetry energy L_sym (65, 105 MeV), then matched these to CSS quark-matter EOSs with sound-speed squares s = 1.0, 0.6, and 1/3, adjusting the transition density and energy-density jump to produce twin configurations consistent with NICER and gravitational-wave constraints.

## Key numbers
- PSR J0740+6620 mass: 2.08^(+0.07)_(−0.07) M⊙ (NANOGrav 2019)
- PSR J0740+6620 radius estimates: 12.39^(+1.30)_(−0.98) km and 13.71^(+2.61)_(−1.50) km (NICER 2021)
- Neutron skin thickness of 208Pb: R_skin = 0.283 ± 0.071 fm (PREX-II)
- Slope of symmetry energy from Reed (2021): L_sym = 106 ± 37 MeV; from Reinhard (2021): L_sym = 54 ± 8 MeV
- Twin-star mass range: ΔM_twin ≲ 0.05 M⊙ for stars with M ≥ 1.0 M⊙
- Twin-star radius difference: ΔR_twin ~ 1.0 km for s = 1.0; up to ~3 km for s = 0.6
- Tidal deformability for canonical-mass star (1.4 M⊙) in softest nucleonic model (Q_sat = −500, L_sym = 65 MeV): Λ_1.4 = 452, radius R_1.4 = 12.58 km
- Tidal deformability for stiffest nucleonic model (Q_sat = 900, L_sym = 105 MeV): Λ_1.4 = 1245, radius R_1.4 = 14.37 km
- Transition densities for twin configurations: ρ_tran/ρ_sat = 1.81 and 2.05 (corresponding to M^N_max/M⊙ = 1.0 and 1.3)

## Context
This work extends the hybrid-star modeling framework of Li, Sedrakian, and Alford (2020) by incorporating the NICER radius measurement of PSR J0740+6620 and the PREX-II neutron skin measurement. It addresses the potential tension identified by Reed (2021) between the large L_sym inferred from PREX-II and the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint, showing that a first-order phase transition to quark matter can resolve this tension. The results enable future searches for twin-star signatures through tidal deformability measurements from gravitational-wave inspiral signals, which the authors identify as more promising than radius measurements given the small ΔR_twin values. The work also connects to the interpretation of the GW190814 secondary component as a possible hybrid star, consistent with the findings of Christian (2021) for certain sound-speed values.
