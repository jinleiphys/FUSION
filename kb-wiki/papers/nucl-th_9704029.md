# The Delta(1232)-nucleon interaction in the 2H(p,n) charge exchange reaction

> The 2H(p,n) charge exchange reaction at T_p=790 MeV is used to study the Delta(1232)-nucleon interaction in the Delta resonance excitation energy region. For the Delta-N potential, a meson exchange model is adopted where pi, rho, omega, and sigma meson exchanges are taken into account. The deuteron disintegration below and above pion threshold is calculated using a coupled channel approach. Various observables, such as the inclusive cross section, the quasifree Delta decay, the coherent pion production, and the two-nucleon breakup are considered. It is shown that these observables are influenced by the dynamical treatment of the Delta degrees of freedom. Of special interest is the coherent pion decay of the Delta resonance which is studied by means of the exclusive reaction 2H(p,n pi+)2H. Both the peak energy and the magnitude of the coherent pion production cross section depend very sensitively on the strength of the Delta-N potential. The coherent pions have a peak energy of 300 MeV and a strongly forward peaked angular distribution.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the $^2$H$(p,n)$ charge exchange reaction at $T_p=790$ MeV can be used to study the $\Delta(1232)$-nucleon interaction, and that both the peak energy and magnitude of the coherent pion production cross section depend "quite sensitively" on the strength of the $\Delta N$ potential, with the caveat that the analysis uses a coupled channel approach with a meson exchange model where the $\Delta$ is treated as a quasi-particle with an intrinsic decay width.

## Method

The authors used a coupled channel approach in configuration space to describe the $\Delta N$ system in the $^2$H$(p,n)$ reaction at $T_p=790$ MeV, solving the coupled equations with the Lanczos method. The $\Delta N$ potential was constructed from a meson exchange model including $\pi$, $\rho$, $\omega$, and $\sigma$ meson exchanges, with coupling constants determined from the Chew-Low relation and the static quark model. The inclusive cross section was decomposed into partial cross sections for the breakup channel (two-nucleon breakup), coherent pion production, and quasifree $\Delta$ decay, with distortion effects treated within the eikonal approximation.

## Key numbers

- The coherent pions have a peak energy of $\omega=300$ MeV and a strongly forward peaked angular distribution.
- The incident proton kinetic energy is $T_p=790$ MeV.
- The $\Delta$ resonance peak position in nuclear targets (with mass number $A \geq 10$) is shifted downward by approximately 70 MeV compared to the proton target.
- The $\Delta$ mass is taken as $M_\Delta = 1232$ MeV.
- The Landau-Migdal parameter for the $\Delta N \rightarrow NN$ transition potential is $g_{N \Delta} = 1/3$.
- The coupling constants follow the relations $f_{\pi / \rho N \Delta} = 2 f_{\pi / \rho NN}$ and $f_{\pi / \rho \Delta \Delta} = \frac{1}{5} f_{\pi / \rho NN}$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $\Delta$ dynamics in nuclei using inclusive and exclusive $(p,n)$ and $(^3He,t)$ charge exchange reactions, particularly the observation of a downward energy shift of the $\Delta$ resonance peak in heavier nuclei (Contardo 1986, Lind 1987, Gaarde 1991, Gaarde 1996, Prout 1996). The deuteron target offers advantages over heavier nuclei because its wave function is well known and Fermi motion can be treated properly, allowing more direct study of the $\Delta N$ interaction. The results enable comparison with experimental data from Prout (1994) and provide a framework for understanding the spin-longitudinal and spin-transverse components of the $\Delta N$ interaction, complementing earlier work on $\gamma d$ and $\pi d$ scattering by Maxwell (1980), Leidemann (1987), Niskanen (1995), Wilhelm (1995, 1996), Schmidt (1996), and Kamalov (1997).
