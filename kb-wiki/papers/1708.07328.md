# Deep inelastic scattering in the dipole picture at next-to-leading order

> We study quantitatively the importance of the recently derived NLO corrections to the DIS structure functions at small x in the dipole formalism. We show that these corrections can be significant and depend on the factorization scheme used to resum large logarithms of energy into renormalization group evolution with the BK equation. This feature is similar to what has recently been observed for single inclusive forward hadron production. Using a factorization scheme consistent with the one recently proposed for the single inclusive cross section, we show that it is possible to obtain meaningful results for the DIS cross sections.

## Key claim

The NLO corrections to DIS structure functions in the dipole formalism can be significant and depend on the factorization scheme used to resum large logarithms into BK evolution; using a scheme consistent with that recently proposed for single inclusive hadron production yields meaningful results, but the NLO corrections can become negative at large Q² if an approximate subtraction scheme is used.

## Method

The authors numerically evaluate the NLO impact factor for DIS structure functions in the dipole picture, using the expressions derived by Beuf (2016, 2017). They separate the NLO corrections into a gluon emission term (σ^{qg}) and a dipole term (σ^{dip}), and implement a factorization procedure where the target momentum fraction X = x_Bj/z_2 is used as the evolution variable, with the lower integration limit set by requiring X(z₂) < x₀. They compare results using this "unsubtracted" scheme with an approximate "x_Bj-subtraction" scheme analogous to the CXY scheme for single inclusive particle production, using both fixed coupling (α_s = 0.2) and running coupling (parent dipole prescription).

## Key numbers

- At x_Bj = 10⁻³ with fixed α_s = 0.2, the total NLO cross section is smaller than the LO one for both F_L and F_T across Q² values shown (up to ~100 GeV²)
- The approximate x_Bj-subtraction scheme makes the NLO cross section negative for Q² ≳ 10 GeV² at x_Bj = 10⁻³
- The NLO/LO ratio for F_L and F_T at x_Bj = 10⁻³ with fixed coupling shows the NLO correction decreases the cross section; with running coupling this reverses near x_Bj ≈ x₀ = 0.01
- The initial condition uses Q_{s,0}² = 0.2 GeV² and Λ_QCD = 0.241 GeV for the MV model
- The running coupling is frozen at value 0.7 at large dipole sizes

## Context

This work extends the dipole picture for DIS from leading order (where fits to HERA data exist, e.g. Albacete (2010), Lappi (2013)) to next-to-leading order, following the recent NLO impact factor calculations by Beuf (2016, 2017). It parallels developments for single inclusive forward hadron production, where similar issues with negative cross sections were resolved by the factorization scheme of Iancu (2016) and Ducloue (2017). The results enable future comparisons with experimental data once combined with NLO BK evolution (Lappi 2016) or collinearly resummed evolution (Iancu 2015).
