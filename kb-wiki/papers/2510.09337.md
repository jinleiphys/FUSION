# Revealing the temperature effect on the nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section in isospin-asymmetric nuclear medium

> The nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) inelastic cross section plays an important role in constraining the nuclear equation of state at high baryon density and in describing the formation and evolution of compact astrophysical objects. In this study, the temperature $T$ dependence of the $Δ^{++}$ and $Δ^{-}$ production cross sections in the isospin-symmetric and -asymmetric nuclear medium is investigated within the self-consistent and relativistic Boltzmann--Uehling--Uhlenbeck (RBUU) framework. Two relativistic mean-field parameterizations are employed: the density-dependent parameterization (called DD-ME$δ$) and the nonlinear-dependent parameterization (called OMEG). Both parameterizations yield similar $T$-dependent baryon effective masses and mass splittings, although the OMEG set exhibits a stronger density dependence, particularly at higher densities ($> 1.5ρ_{0}$). Consequently, at lower densities, the energy, density, temperature, and isospin dependence of both $Δ^{++}$ and $Δ^{-}$ production cross sections are comparable for both sets, whereas at higher densities, the OMEG set predicts a stronger temperature and density sensitivity. Moreover, the $T$ dependence of the $NN$ inelastic cross section is enhanced with increasing density, but is suppressed in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter compared to that in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter. The isospin dependence of the cross section remains nearly $T$-independent at small asymmetries, yet becomes more intricate in highly asymmetric systems. These findings provide valuable testing inputs for improving the thermal treatment of $Δ$-related dynamical processes in transport models and offer insights into the behavior of $Δ$ in astrophysical environments, such as core-collapse supernovae and binary neutron star mergers.

## Key claim

The temperature dependence of the nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section for Δ⁺⁺ and Δ⁻ production is enhanced with increasing density but suppressed in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter compared to isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, with the effect becoming pronounced only at densities above approximately 1.5ρ₀ and depending on the choice of relativistic mean-field parameterization.

## Method

The authors employed the self-consistent relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) framework with an effective Lagrangian including σ, ω, δ, ρ, and π mesons coupling to both nucleons and Δ resonances. Two relativistic mean-field parameterizations were used: the density-dependent DD-MEδ set and the nonlinear-dependent OMEG set, with the in-medium nucleon and Δ effective masses determined self-consistently at finite temperature using Fermi-Dirac distributions. The in-medium NN → NΔ cross sections were then calculated as functions of center-of-mass energy, baryon density, temperature (0–150 MeV), and isospin asymmetry.

## Key numbers

- The temperature dependence of the NN inelastic cross section was studied for temperatures from 0 to 150 MeV at densities of ρ₀, 1.5ρ₀, 2ρ₀, and 3ρ₀.
- At densities below approximately 1.5ρ₀, the ratio R(α=0, T) = σ*(T)/σ*(T=0) increases nearly identically for both DD-MEδ and OMEG parameter sets at a typical kinetic energy E_K = 1 GeV (√s = 2.326 GeV).
- At higher densities (≥ 1.5ρ₀), the OMEG set predicts a stronger temperature sensitivity than the DD-MEδ set, with the OMEG results showing larger increases in the ratio R(α=0, T).
- The ratio R(α=0, T) ≈ R(α=0.3, T) at densities of ρ₀ and 1.5ρ₀, while R(α=0, T) > R(α=0.3, T) at higher densities.
- The OMEG parameter set uses g_σ = 9.22, g_ω = 11.35, g_δ²/4π = 2.488, g_ρ²/4π = 3.39, with symmetry energy E_sym = 32.0 MeV and slope parameter L = 50 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous self-consistent RBUU studies of in-medium NN → NN (Li, 2000; Li, 2003), NN → NΔ (Li, 2016), Nπ → Δ (Li, 2017), and NΔ → NΔ (Nan, 2023; Nan, 2024) cross sections to include temperature dependence, which was previously neglected. The results provide inputs for improving thermal treatment of Δ-related processes in transport models, addressing long-standing discrepancies in pion multiplicity predictions noted by the Transport Model Evaluation Project (2022) and others. The findings are relevant for astrophysical environments such as core-collapse supernovae and binary neutron star mergers, where Δ resonances appear at densities around 2ρ₀ as shown by Cai (2015).
