# Quarkonium spin alignment in a vortical medium

> We use a potential model to investigate the phenomenology of quarkonium in a thermal rotating medium, where vorticity and spin density are not necessarily in equilibrium. We find that the quarkonium spin density matrix, as well as the binding energy and melting temperature, are sensitive to both the vorticity and the lack of equilibrium between vorticity and spin. This means that quarkonium spin alignment is a sensitive probe for vorticity and spin within the hydrodynamic phase. Information unequivocably pointing to spin-orbit non-equilibrium dynamics can be obtained from a combined study of quarkonium relative abundance and spin alignment, as well as experimentally obtainable off-diagonal density matrix elements.

## Key claim
Quarkonium spin alignment, measured through the full spin density matrix including off-diagonal elements, can serve as a sensitive probe of non-equilibrium between vorticity and spin density in the quark-gluon plasma, provided that measurements are performed as a function of the azimuthal reaction plane angle rather than integrated over it.

## Method
The authors solved a Schrodinger equation with a Cornell potential in a rotating reference frame, incorporating a spin-vorticity coupling term and using the circulation theorem to relate vorticity to angular momentum. They then used the resulting energy eigenvalues to compute the spin density matrix elements in the lab frame via a Wigner rotation, and compared these to experimental polarization parameters from the ALICE Collaboration for J/psi and Upsilon states.

## Key numbers
- For J/psi in the Collins-Soper frame, the Gell-Mann parameter tilde{n} = 0.09 +/- 0.10 for 4 < p_T < 6 GeV/c^2 and tilde{n} = 0.02 +/- 0.07 for 6 < p_T < 10 GeV/c^2; in the helicity frame tilde{n} = 0.09 +/- 0.11 for 6 < p_T < 10 GeV/c^2
- Blast wave model estimates of density matrix coefficients: for J/psi, lambda_theta = 0.021, lambda_phi = 0.023, lambda_{theta phi} = 0.012; for Upsilon, lambda_theta = 0.054, lambda_phi = 0.054, lambda_{theta phi} = 0.002
- Melting temperature estimate for charmonium with m=1 polarization increases considerably relative to the non-rotating case, while for m=0 it does not change and for m=-1 it decreases less than the increase for m=1
- The maximum of the function f(tilde{r}) = tilde{r}(1+tilde{r})e^{-tilde{r}} is 0.840 at tilde{r} = 1.92, used to determine the bound state condition

## Context
This work extends the spin hydrodynamics framework developed by the authors in Kayman et al. (2022) by applying a potential model to quarkonium states, building on the Cornell potential approach of Brambilla et al. and the rotating-frame methods of Anan et al. The analysis uses experimental polarization data from the ALICE Collaboration (2022) for J/psi and Upsilon states. The results suggest that measuring polarization parameters as a function of the reaction plane angle, rather than integrated over p_T as in current ALICE measurements, could distinguish between a statistical Cooper-Frye freeze-out scenario and a non-equilibrium coalescence scenario involving vorticity. The finding that melting temperature depends on spin polarization could also connect quarkonium suppression measurements to spin alignment phenomenology.
