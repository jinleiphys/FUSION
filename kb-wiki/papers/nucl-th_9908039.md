# Coherent Nuclear Difractive Production of Mini-Jets - Signature of Color Transparency

> The process $π+ A$ leading to a pair of mini-jets at high relative transverse momentum ($k_t \gsim 2$ GeV) while leaving the nucleus in its ground state was selected as a definitive test of the existence of color transparency by Frankfurt, Miller and Strikman in 1993. The preliminary results of Fermilab experiment E791, led by Ashery and Weiss-Babai, show a strong A-dependence, consistent with the notion of color transparency.

# Coherent Nuclear Diffractive Production of Mini-Jets, Signature of Color Transparency

## Key claim
The process π + A → q q̄ + A (ground state) with relative transverse momentum kₜ ≳ 2 GeV shows a strong A-dependence consistent with color transparency, but the k²ₜ dependence of the cross section has not yet been verified experimentally, so the conclusion remains preliminary.

## Method
The authors analyze the coherent diffractive production of a high-relative-transverse-momentum q q̄ pair from a 500 GeV pion beam incident on nuclear targets, where the final nucleus remains in its ground state. The nuclear amplitude is computed using a two-gluon exchange model in which the small transverse size of the q q̄ pair suppresses further interactions (color transparency), leading to a nuclear amplitude proportional to A times the nucleon amplitude, with a correction term from soft rescattering. The cross section is parametrized as σ(A) ∝ A^α, and the theoretical prediction is compared to preliminary results from Fermilab experiment E791.

## Key numbers
- The relative transverse momentum threshold is kₜ ≳ 2 GeV/c.
- The average squared transverse separation of the q q̄ pair in the pion is ⟨b²⟩ ≈ 0.24 fm².
- For the two targets Pt (A = 195) and C (A = 12), the theoretical exponent α ≈ 1.58 when including the γ correction factor.
- The preliminary experimental result from E791 is α ≈ 1.55 ± 0.05.
- The ratio σ(Pt)/σ(C) = 75 from the measured α = 1.55, compared to a typical diffractive ratio of 7 (α = 2/3), giving a factor of 10 enhancement.
- The Primakoff (electromagnetic) amplitude is estimated to be only −0.06 i times the strong amplitude, confirming it is negligible.

## Context
This work extends the 1993 prediction by Frankfurt, Miller and Strikman that coherent diffractive mini-jet production would provide a definitive test of color transparency. The preliminary E791 results (Ashery and Weiss-Babai, 1999) show α ≈ 1.55, remarkably close to the theoretical value of 1.58, and represent the strongest evidence to date for color transparency. If confirmed, this would validate earlier hints of color transparency from previous experiments (reviewed by Frankfurt, Miller and Strikman) and motivate increased efforts at Jefferson Lab to observe the effect. The existence of point-like configurations (PLCs) implies the existence of blob-like "huskyon" configurations (A. B. et al.), leading to a new view of the nucleus as composed of oscillating, color-singlet composite objects.
