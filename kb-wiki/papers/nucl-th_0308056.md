# Lowest Order Effective Field Theory for the weak $ΛN$ interaction

> The $|ΔS|=1$ $ΛN$ interaction, responsible for the decay of hypernuclei, is studied by means of an Effective Field Theory where the long range physics is described by pion and kaon exchange mechanisms, and its short range counterpart is obtained from the most general non-derivative local four-fermion interaction. We show that, including the Lowest Order Parity Conserving contact terms, allows us to reproduce the total decay rates for $^5_Λ{\rm He}$, $^{11}_Λ{\rm B}$ and $^{12}_Λ{\rm C}$ with a reasonable value of ${\hat χ^2}$, while in order to get a prediction for the Parity Violating (PV) asymmetry compatible with experiments, we have to include the Lowest Order PV contact pieces.

# Lowest Order Effective Field Theory for the weak ΛN interaction

## Key claim
The paper shows that including Lowest Order Parity Conserving (PC) contact terms in an Effective Field Theory (EFT) framework can reproduce the total decay rates for three hypernuclei with a reasonable chi-squared value, but to obtain a prediction for the Parity Violating (PV) asymmetry compatible with experiments, the Lowest Order PV contact pieces must also be included.

## Method
The authors model the |ΔS|=1 ΛN interaction using an EFT where long-range physics is described by one-pion exchange (OPE) and one-kaon exchange (OKE) mechanisms, while short-range contributions are parametrized by the most general non-derivative local four-fermion contact interaction. Standard nuclear structure techniques decouple a ΛN pair from the initial hypernuclei, which then connects through the two-body potential to a NN final pair, with strong interaction effects accounted for by the NSC97f baryon-baryon interaction model. The contact interaction is smeared using a normalized Gaussian form with a range δ ≈ 0.36 fm.

## Key numbers
- Total decay rate Γ(⁵_ΛHe): 0.44 (with LO PV) vs. experimental 0.41 ± 0.14 and 0.50 ± 0.07 (units not specified, presumably in units of the free Λ decay rate or Γ_Λ)
- Γ_n/Γ_p ratio for ⁵_ΛHe: 0.55 (with LO PV) vs. experimental 0.93 ± 0.55 and 0.50 ± 0.10
- Parity-violating asymmetry A(⁵_ΛHe): 0.24 (when helium asymmetry included in fit) vs. experimental 0.24 ± 0.22
- Total decay rate Γ(¹¹_ΛB): 0.88 (with LO PV) vs. experimental 0.95 ± 0.14
- Total decay rate Γ(¹²_ΛC): 0.93 (with LO PV) vs. experimental 1.14 ± 0.2, 0.89 ± 0.15, and 0.83 ± 0.11
- Reduced chi-squared: 0.98 for LO PC fit, 1.50 for LO PC+PV fit, 1.16 when helium asymmetry included
- Contact interaction smearing range δ: 0.36 fm
- Low-energy constant C_IS: 5.83 ± 0.82 (when helium asymmetry included in fit)
- Low-energy constant C_IV: 1.52 ± 0.24 (when helium asymmetry included in fit)

## Context
This work extends previous meson-exchange approaches to the weak ΛN interaction, which traditionally used OPE and OKE but could not simultaneously reproduce total decay rates and the Γ_n/Γ_p ratio. Prior studies by Parreno et al. (2001) and others showed that including kaon exchange substantially enlarges the Γ_n/Γ_p ratio while reducing total decay rates. The present EFT approach provides a systematic framework for incorporating short-range contributions via contact terms, and the results support the validity of the EFT framework for nonmesonic hypernuclear weak decay, with the hope that next-generation high-precision experimental data will better constrain the parameters.
