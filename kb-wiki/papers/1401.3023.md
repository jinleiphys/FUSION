# Probing the neutron skin thickness in collective modes of excitation

> Nuclear collective motion provides valuable constraint on the size of neutron-skin thickness and the properties of nuclear matter symmetry energy. By employing relativistic nuclear energy density functional (RNEDF) and covariance analysis related to $χ^2$ fitting of the model parameters, relevant observables are identified for dipole excitations, which strongly correlate with the neutron-skin thickness $(r_{np})$, symmetry energy at saturation density $(J)$ and slope of the symmetry energy $(L)$. Using the RNEDF framework and experimental data on pygmy dipole strength ($^{68}$Ni, $^{132}$Sn, $^{208}$Pb) and dipole polarizability ($^{208}$Pb), it is shown how the values of $J$, and $L$, and $r_{np}$ are constrained. The isotopic dependence of moments associated to dipole excitations in $^{116-136}$Sn shows that the low-energy dipole strength and polarizability in neutron-rich nuclei display strong sensitivity to the symmetry energy parameter $J$, more pronounced than in isotopes with moderate neutron-to-proton number ratios.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that pygmy dipole strength (PDS) and dipole polarizability are strongly correlated with the neutron-skin thickness and symmetry energy parameters, but notes that this correlation is model-dependent, as it is at variance with a previous study based on the Skyrme functional.

## Method

The authors employed the relativistic nuclear energy density functional (RNEDF) framework, using relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) and relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (RQRPA) to calculate dipole transition strength distributions. They performed covariance analysis based on χ² fitting of model parameters to experimental data for 17 nuclei, obtaining the DDME-min1 parameterization with Pearson correlation coefficients to identify correlations between observables and nuclear matter properties.

## Key numbers

- Dipole polarizability in ²⁰⁸Pb measured as α_D = (20.1 ± 0.6) fm³
- The set of DD-ME effective interactions spans J = 30–38 MeV and correspondingly L = 30–110.8 MeV
- PDS strength in ¹³²Sn increases by factor ≈ 3–4 when J increases from 30 to 38 MeV
- Energy cut-off between low- and high-energy dipole transitions in ²⁰⁸Pb selected at 10 MeV (stable also for 11 and 12 MeV)
- PDS exhausting only a few percent of the energy-weighted sum rule can exhaust more than 20% of the dipole polarizability in neutron-rich nuclei

## Context

This work extends previous studies using relativistic and non-relativistic energy density functionals to constrain neutron-skin thickness from dipole polarizability, as done by Piekarewicz (2011) and Tamii (2011). It builds on covariance analysis introduced by Reinhard (2010) and contrasts with Skyrme-functional results that showed different correlations. The findings enable improved constraints on the symmetry energy parameters J and L from experimental PDS data in ⁶⁸Ni, ¹³²Sn, and ²⁰⁸Pb, and suggest that charge-exchange excitations studied by Krasznahorkay (2013) provide another complementary approach.
