# Flavor content of nucleon form factors in the space- and time-like region

> I discuss a two-component model of nucleon form factors in which the external photon couples both to an intrinsic three-quark structure and to a meson cloud via vector-meson dominance, and present a simultaneous analysis of the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon in the space- and time-like regions as well as their strangeness content.

## Key claim

The two-component model (intrinsic three-quark structure plus vector-meson dominance) can simultaneously describe nucleon electromagnetic form factors in both space-like and time-like regions and their strangeness content, but the agreement with time-like data shows serious discrepancies for intermediate values of momentum transfer, pointing to an inconsistency between space-like and time-like data that was already noted in prior work.

## Method

The author combines the two-component model of Bijker and Iachello (2004) for electromagnetic nucleon form factors with Jaffe's method for determining strangeness content via coupling of the strange current to the isoscalar vector mesons ω and φ. The isoscalar and isovector Dirac and Pauli form factors are parametrized with a dipole intrinsic form factor g(Q²) = (1+γQ²)⁻² and vector-meson pole contributions, with five free parameters (γ, β_ω, α_φ, β_ρ, α_ρ) determined by a least-square fit to electric and magnetic form factors of the proton and neutron. The strange form factors are then obtained from the same fitted parameters using the mixing angle ε = 0.053 rad (3.0°) determined from radiative decays of ω and φ mesons.

## Key numbers

- Strange magnetic moment calculated as μ_s = 0.315 μ_N (nuclear magnetons)
- Mixing angle ε = 0.053 rad (3.0°) used for ω-φ mixing, determined from radiative decays of ω and φ mesons
- Global fit of all measurements at Q² = 0.1 (GeV/c)² gives G_M^s = 0.18 ± 0.27
- Lattice calculation gives μ_s = -0.046 ± 0.019 μ_N (for comparison)
- Strange electric form factor G_E^s is small and negative, suppressed by τ = Q²/4M_N² at small Q²
- Strange magnetic form factor G_M^s is positive, dominated by Pauli form factor contribution

## Context

This work extends the two-component model of Bijker and Iachello (2004) for electromagnetic nucleon form factors by combining it with Jaffe's method (1989) to determine strangeness content via ω and φ meson couplings. The approach provides a simultaneous description of space-like form factors and their strange quark content, with strangeness contributions to charge and magnetization distributions at the few percent level. The results point to an inconsistency between space-like and time-like data already noted in earlier work by Bijker and Iachello (2004), Hammer et al. (1998), and Wan and Iachello (2005), suggesting that future measurements separating electric and magnetic contributions in the time-like region and measuring polarization observables may help resolve this discrepancy.
