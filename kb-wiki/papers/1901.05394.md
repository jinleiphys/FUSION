# Direct flow of heavy mesons as unique probe of the initial Electro-Magnetic fields in Ultra-Relativistic Heavy Ion collisions

> In Ultra-relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions (HICs) very strong initial electro-magnetic (E.M.) fields are created: the order of magnitude of the magnetic field is about $10^{19} \, Gauss$, the most intense field in the Universe, even larger than that of a magnetar. These fields rapidly decrease in time, inducing a drift of particles in the reaction plane. The resulting flow is odd under charge exchange and this allows to distinguish it from the large vorticity of the bulk matter due to the initial angular momentum conservation. Conjointly charm quarks, thanks to their large mass $M_{c}>>Λ_{QCD}$, are produced in hard partonic processes at formation time $τ_f \approx 1\,/\,( 2M_{HQ} )$ which is comparable with the time scale when the E.M. field attains its maximum value. Moreover, with a mass of $M_c \approx 1.3 \,$ GeV there should be no mixing with the chiral magnetic dynamics and the condition $M_c \gg T$ allows charm quarks to have sufficiently large thermalization time, so that they can probe the entire phase-space evolution of the QGP retaining the initial kick given by the E.M. field. We show that such E.M. field entails a transverse motion of charm quarks resulting in a splitting of directed flow $v_1$ of $ D$ and $\bar{D}$ mesons of few percent, i.e. much larger compared to the measured pion one.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the directed flow \(v_1\) of \(D\) and \(\bar{D}\) mesons, split by charge, can reach a few percent due to the initial electromagnetic field in heavy-ion collisions, which is much larger than the measured pion \(v_1\). This signal is proposed as a unique probe of the initial electromagnetic fields, provided that the charm quark mass (\(M_c \approx 1.3\) GeV) ensures no mixing with chiral magnetic dynamics and that the thermalization time is long enough for charm quarks to retain the initial electromagnetic kick.

## Method
The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation for heavy quarks (HQs) in a quark-gluon plasma (QGP), incorporating electromagnetic fields derived from spectator protons using Maxwell equations with a constant electric conductivity \(\sigma_{el} = 0.023 \, \text{fm}^{-1}\) from lattice QCD. The collision integral includes elastic scattering at leading-order pQCD with non-perturbative effects via a Quasi-Particle (QP) prescription tuned to lattice QCD thermodynamics. Initial charm quark momenta are sampled from FONLL \(pp\)-spectra, and the bulk medium includes a vorticity profile to account for angular momentum transfer; hadronization is modeled via a hybrid fragmentation plus coalescence mechanism.

## Key numbers
- Initial magnetic field at RHIC: \(eB_y \approx 5 m_{\pi}^2\) (with \(m_{\pi} \approx 0.14\) GeV), corresponding to about \(10^{19}\) Gauss.
- Initial magnetic field at LHC: \(eB_y \approx 50 m_{\pi}^2\).
- Charm quark mass: \(M_c \approx 1.3\) GeV.
- Formation time of charm quarks: \(\tau_f \approx 1 / (2 M_{HQ})\).
- Electric conductivity of QGP: \(\sigma_{el} = 0.023 \, \text{fm}^{-1}\) (constant, at \(T \approx 2 T_c\)).
- Predicted directed flow \(v_1\) of \(D\) and \(\bar{D}\) mesons at RHIC: \(v_1 \approx 10^{-2}\) (few percent), for collisions with impact parameter \(b = 9 \, \text{fm}\).

## Context
This work extends the hint from Coci et al. (2018) that heavy quarks can probe initial electromagnetic fields, and it contrasts with the light hadron sector where \(v_1\) is much smaller and dominated by bulk vorticity. The study uses a Boltzmann approach previously validated for describing the nuclear suppression factor \(R_{AA}\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\) of \(D\) mesons at RHIC and LHC energies. The results enable future studies with more complex vorticity models (e.g., Chatterjee 2018), temperature-dependent electric conductivity, and event-by-event fluctuations to further isolate the electromagnetic signal.
