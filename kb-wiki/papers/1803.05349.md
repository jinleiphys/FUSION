# Dispersion relation formalism for the two-photon exchange correction to elastic muon-proton scattering: elastic intermediate state

> We evaluate the two-photon exchange correction to the unpolarized cross section in the elastic muon-proton scattering within dispersion relations. One of the six independent invariant amplitudes requires a subtraction. We fix the subtraction function to the model estimate of the full two-photon exchange at one of three MUSE beam energies and make a prediction for the two other energies. Additionally, we present single and double polarization observables accounting for the lepton mass.

## Key claim

The authors evaluate the two-photon exchange (TPE) correction to elastic muon-proton scattering using dispersion relations, but one of the six independent invariant amplitudes (the helicity-flip amplitude F4) requires a subtraction; the subtraction function is fixed to a model estimate of the full TPE at one of the three MUSE beam energies, allowing predictions for the other two energies.

## Method

The authors use fixed-Q² dispersion relations to reconstruct the real parts of TPE invariant amplitudes from their imaginary parts, which are obtained via unitarity relations for the elastic intermediate state. For the amplitude F4, which does not vanish at infinite energy, a once-subtracted dispersion relation is required, while the amplitudes G1, G2, F3, and F5 are reconstructed using unsubtracted dispersion relations. The subtraction function for F4 is fixed to the near-forward TPE model estimate of Tomalak (2015) at one MUSE beam energy, and the formalism is then used to predict the TPE correction at the other two MUSE energies.

## Key numbers

- The critical momentum transfer where the photon polarization parameter epsilon = 1 for all beam energies is Q² = 0.022 GeV² for muon beams, which lies inside the MUSE kinematical region for all three nominal beam momenta.
- The TPE correction to the unpolarized cross section vanishes in the forward limit (Q² → 0) and in the high-energy limit (ν → ∞) at fixed Q².
- The leading model-independent terms in the Q² expansion (Q² ≪ m²) for the TPE amplitudes are: Re G1^(2γ) → (α π ω Q)/(2 k²) (1 + m/(2M)), Re G2^(2γ) → (α π ω Q)/(4 k²) (1 + 2m/M), and Re G4^(2γ) → -(α π M Q)/(4 k²) (1 + m/M + ω²/(M m)).

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation approach previously applied to electron-proton scattering (Tomalak 2014, Tomalak 2017) to the muon-proton case, accounting for the finite lepton mass. It addresses the proton radius puzzle by providing a framework for the MUSE experiment, which will measure muon-proton scattering at low momentum transfer. The elastic intermediate state TPE contribution was previously estimated within hadronic models (Blunden 2003, Tomalak 2014), but those models can violate unitarity; the dispersion relation approach provides a more rigorous treatment, and this work enables predictions for TPE corrections at MUSE energies that can be tested against upcoming experimental data.
