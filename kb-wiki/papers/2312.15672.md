# Saturation of nuclear matter in the relativistic Brueckner Hatree-Fock approach with a leading order covariant chiral nuclear force

> Nuclear saturation is a crucial feature in nuclear physics that plays a fundamental role in understanding various nuclear phenomena, ranging from properties of finite nuclei to those of neutron stars. However, a proper description of nuclear saturation is highly nontrivial in modern nonrelativistic~\textit{ab initio}~studies because of the elusive three-body forces. In this letter, we calculate the equation of state for nuclear matter in the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) framework with the leading order covariant chiral nuclear force. We show that a simultaneous description of the nucleon-nucleon scattering data and the saturation of the symmetric nuclear matter can be achieved. In this regard, the relativistic effects nicely explain the saturation of nuclear matter. As a result, the present study based on the covariant chiral nuclear force shows that in the RBHF framework, one can achieve saturation with a leading order covariant chiral nuclear force with only two-body forces, in contrast to the vast majorities of studies in the non-relativistic framework, where the next-to-next-to-leading order two-body and three-body chiral forces are needed. This study sets the foundation for studying nuclear saturation with the covariant chiral force in the RBHF framework, which allows for a systematic understanding of one of the key features of nuclear physics more microscopically.

## Key claim

The authors show that nuclear matter saturation can be achieved in the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) framework using only a leading order covariant chiral two-body force, without explicit three-body forces, but only for a specific cutoff value of 567 MeV; the results show a strong cutoff dependence that the authors acknowledge needs to be alleviated at higher chiral orders.

## Method

The authors solve the in-medium Dirac equation and the Thompson equation (a relativistic reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation) self-consistently within the RBHF framework, using a leading order covariant chiral nuclear force that consists of contact terms and one-pion-exchange. The low-energy constants of the chiral force are fitted to nucleon-proton scattering phase shifts up to 100 MeV laboratory energy, and the potential is regularized with an exponential cutoff function with cutoff values ranging from 450 MeV to 600 MeV. The binding energy per nucleon of symmetric nuclear matter is then computed as a function of density.

## Key numbers

* Saturation energy per nucleon of symmetric nuclear matter: -15.82 MeV at a cutoff of 567 MeV, compared to the empirical value of -16 ± 1 MeV.
* Saturation density: 0.16 fm⁻³ at a cutoff of 567 MeV, compared to the empirical value of 0.16 ± 0.01 fm⁻³.
* Compression modulus at saturation density: 252 MeV at a cutoff of 567 MeV, compared to the empirical value of 240 ± 20 MeV.
* Cutoff values studied: 450, 550, 567, and 600 MeV, with corresponding chi-squared per data point values of 7.43, 4.66, 4.25, and 3.57, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the line of research showing that relativistic effects can explain nuclear matter saturation (Brockmann 1990) by replacing the phenomenological meson-exchange Bonn potentials with a QCD-based covariant chiral nuclear force. It contrasts with nonrelativistic approaches such as those of Sammarruca (2014, 2021) and Drischler (2017), where saturation requires next-to-next-to-leading order chiral forces including three-body forces. This study sets the foundation for systematically studying nuclear saturation with covariant chiral forces in the RBHF framework, enabling future work with higher-order chiral forces and extensions to pure neutron matter for neutron star applications.
