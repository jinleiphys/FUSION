# Statistical hadronization phenomenology in $K/π$ fluctuations at ultra-relativistic energies

> We discuss the information that can be obtained from an analysis of fluctuations in heavy ion collisions within the context of the statistical model of particle production. We then examine the recently published experimental data on ratio fluctuations, and use it to obtain constraints on the statistical properties (physically relevant ensemble, degree of chemical equilibration, scaling across energies and system sizes) and freeze-out dynamics (amount of reinteraction between chemical and thermal freeze-out) of the system.

## Key claim

The statistical model of particle production, particularly in the grand-canonical ensemble, can describe the scaling of K/π ratio fluctuations across system sizes and energies at RHIC, but no model perfectly matches the data; the introduction of a non-equilibrium parameter γq improves the agreement, though residual discrepancies may be due to imperfections in the mixed-event subtraction procedure used by experimental collaborations.

## Method

The authors derive that the scaled variance of a particle ratio, specifically Ψ = ⟨N₁⟩ σ_dyn^(N₁/N₂), is strictly independent of volume fluctuations in the grand-canonical ensemble. They then compare this theoretical prediction to published experimental data on K/π fluctuations from the STAR collaboration at RHIC (62 and 200 GeV) and the NA49 collaboration at SPS, using statistical model parameters from previous fits (Janik et al., 2009; Becattini et al., 2004) to compute expected values. They also extract the K*⁰/K⁻ ratio from the difference between same-charge and opposite-charge fluctuation measurements to constrain resonance reinteraction between chemical and thermal freeze-out.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC 200 GeV, Ψ for K/π fluctuations shows a flat dependence with system size (centrality), consistent with the grand-canonical statistical model prediction; no "kink" is visible that would indicate a canonical-to-grand-canonical transition.
- The discrepancy between fluctuation-inferred K*⁰/K⁻ and directly measured K*⁰/K⁻ is within 10-15% error bars, indicating that rescattering and regeneration of K* resonances have little net effect on their final abundance.
- Transport model estimates for K* rescattering are approximately 40%, which is larger than the 10-15% margin allowed by the fluctuation comparison.
- The exponent α, defined to parametrize the scaling of fluctuations with yields across SPS energies, shows that none of the simple scalings examined in Koch (2008) are compatible with the full statistical model, due to rapid changes in μ_B and γ_q,s with √s.

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model analysis of particle yields (Becattini, 2004; Braun-Munzinger et al., 2004; Andronic et al., 2006) to event-by-event fluctuations, building on earlier fluctuation studies (Torrieri et al., 2006; Jeon and Koch, 2004). It directly tests the STAR fluctuation data (Abelev et al., 2009) and SPS data (NA49, 2009) against statistical model predictions. The method enables future constraints on freeze-out dynamics by comparing fluctuation-inferred resonance abundances to direct resonance measurements, and motivates improved mixed-event subtraction procedures and uniform-acceptance measurements at lower energies (STAR beam energy scan, PHENIX, NICA).
