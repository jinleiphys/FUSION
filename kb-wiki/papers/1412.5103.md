# Separation of flow from chiral magnetic effect in U+U collisions using spectator asymmetry

> We demonstrate that the prolate shape of the Uranium nucleus generates anti-correlation between spectator asymmetry and initial state ellipticity of the collision zone, providing a way to constrain the initial event shape in U+U collisions. As an application, we show that this can be used to separate the background contribution due to flow from the signals of chiral magnetic effect.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in U+U collisions, the prolate shape of the Uranium nucleus creates an anti-correlation between spectator neutron asymmetry and initial-state ellipticity, which can be exploited to separate the background contribution from elliptic flow from the chiral magnetic effect signal; this separation works only within specific centrality bins and requires careful cuts on spectator numbers.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo Glauber simulations of U+U collisions at 193 GeV and Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, using a two-component Glauber model tuned to reproduce the min-bias multiplicity distribution from the IP-Glasma model. They computed the initial-state ellipticity from participant nucleon positions and the magnetic field correlator \(\gamma^B = e^2 B^2 \cos[2(\Psi_B - \Psi_{RP})]\) as a proxy for the CME signal, using Lienard-Wiechert potentials at the center of the participant zone. The spectator neutron asymmetry \(|L-R|\) was calculated from the difference between left-going and right-going spectator neutrons, and its correlation with ellipticity and \(\gamma^B\) was studied across centrality bins.

## Key numbers

- For the 0-20% centrality bin, \(\varepsilon_2\) decreases monotonically with increasing \(|L-R|\) by about 20%.
- For the 0-20% centrality bin, \(\gamma^B\) grows with increasing \(|L-R|\) by about 50% when using the second-order spectator plane as the reaction plane, and by about 20% when using the participant plane.
- The \(|L-R|\) parameter was varied in the range from 0 to 45 for the correlation studies.
- Centrality selection covered the range 0-40%, over which \(\varepsilon_2\) is expected to be a good proxy for elliptic flow \(v_2\).

## Context

This work extends earlier suggestions by Voloshin (2010) to use deformed Uranium nuclei for CME studies, and by Bzdak (2011) to use event-by-event fluctuations of ellipticity in Au+Au collisions. The proposed method enables experimental separation of flow background from CME signals by comparing correlations of \(\gamma^{ab}\) versus \(v_2\) obtained from varying centrality with those obtained from varying spectator asymmetry within a fixed centrality bin, which could provide a definitive test for the chiral magnetic effect.
