# Strange form factors of the nucleon in a two-component model

## Key claim
The strange magnetic moment of the nucleon is found to be positive (0.315 μ_N) in a two-component model consisting of an intrinsic three-quark structure surrounded by a meson cloud, in contrast with most theoretical studies that favor negative values, but in agreement with parity-violating electron scattering experiments. The model parameters are completely determined by the electromagnetic form factors of the proton and neutron, with no additional free parameters for the strangeness sector.

## Method
The Dirac and Pauli form factors are parametrized according to a two-component model in which the external photon couples both to an intrinsic three-quark structure described by a dipole form factor g(Q²) = (1+γQ²)⁻² and to a meson cloud through intermediate vector mesons ρ, ω, and φ. The strange form factors arise through the coupling of the strange current to the ω and φ mesons, using the formalism of Jaffe with ω-φ mixing angle ε = 0.053 rad, and the five remaining coefficients (γ, β_ω, α_φ, β_ρ, α_ρ) are determined by a least-square fit to the electromagnetic form factors of the proton and neutron.

## Key numbers
- Strange magnetic moment: μ_s = 0.315 μ_N (positive)
- Strangeness charge radius: ⟨r²_s⟩_E = 0.005 fm²
- Strangeness magnetic radius: ⟨r²_s⟩_M = 0.410 fm²
- Spatial extent of intrinsic three-quark structure: ⟨r²⟩^{1/2} = 0.49 fm
- ω-φ mixing angle: ε = 0.053 rad
- At Q² = 0.091 (GeV/c)², experimental G_E^s = -0.038 ± 0.042 ± 0.010 (HAPPEX Collaboration)
- At Q² = 0.1 (GeV/c)², experimental G_M^s = 0.37 ± 0.20 ± 0.26 ± 0.07 (SAMPLE Collaboration)
- Combined fit at Q² ~ 0.1 (GeV/c)²: G_M^s(0.1) = 0.55 ± 0.28 and G_E^s(0.1) = -0.01 ± 0.03 (Aniol05b)
- Lattice calculation comparison: μ_s = -0.046 ± 0.019 μ_N (Leinweber1)
- Intrinsic form factor parameter: γ = 0.512 (GeV/c)⁻²

## Context
This work extends the two-component model of Bijker and Iachello (2004) for electromagnetic nucleon form factors by incorporating the mechanism of Jaffe (1989) to determine strangeness content via ω and φ meson couplings. The model reproduces experimental data from the SAMPLE, PVA4, HAPPEX, and G0 collaborations, finding a positive strange magnetic moment in contrast with most theoretical studies (reviewed by Beck, 2001) that favor negative values, but consistent with recent parity-violating electron scattering results. The approach enables a simultaneous and consistent description of electromagnetic and weak vector form factors of the nucleon, and future parity-violating electron scattering and neutrino scattering experiments could further unravel quark flavor contributions to nucleon structure.
