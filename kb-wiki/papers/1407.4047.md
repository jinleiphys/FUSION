# Transverse Spin and Classical Gluon Fields: Combining Two Perspectives on Hadronic Structure

> In recent decades, the spin and transverse momentum of quarks and gluons were found to play integral roles in the structure of the nucleon. Simultaneously, the onset of gluon saturation in hadrons and nuclei at high energies was predicted to result in a new state of matter dominated by classical gluon fields. Understanding both of these contributions to hadronic structure is essential for current and future collider phenomenology. In this Dissertation, we study the combined effects of transverse spin and gluon saturation using the Glauber-Gribov-Mueller / McLerran-Venugopalan model of a heavy nucleus in the quasi-classical approximation. We investigate the use of a transversely-polarized projectile as a probe of the saturated gluon fields in the nucleus, finding that the transverse spin asymmetry of produced particles couples to the component of the gluon fields which is antisymmetric under both time reversal and charge conjugation. We also analyze the effects of saturation on the transverse spin asymmetry (Sivers function) of quarks within the wave function of the nucleus, finding that gluon saturation preferentially generates the asymmetry through the orbital angular momentum of the nucleons, together with nuclear shadowing.

## Key claim

The dissertation demonstrates that transverse spin asymmetries in high-energy scattering can be used to probe the component of saturated gluon fields that is antisymmetric under both time reversal and charge conjugation, and that gluon saturation preferentially generates the Sivers asymmetry through the orbital angular momentum of nucleons together with nuclear shadowing.

## Method

The author employs the Glauber-Gribov-Mueller / McLerran-Venugopalan model of a heavy nucleus in the quasi-classical approximation to study the combined effects of transverse spin and gluon saturation. The analysis investigates two complementary scenarios: using a transversely-polarized projectile as a probe of saturated gluon fields in the nucleus, and analyzing the effects of saturation on the transverse spin asymmetry (Sivers function) of quarks within the nuclear wave function.

## Key numbers

* The quark spin contribution to the proton spin is estimated at approximately Delta Sigma ≈ 0.25, corresponding to about 25% of the proton spin (from modern determinations cited in the text)
* The gluon polarization Delta G is found to contribute about Delta G ≈ 0.20, corresponding to approximately 40% of the proton spin (from very recent measurements cited in the text)
* Single transverse spin asymmetries of up to 30-40% were measured in collisions of transversely-polarized protons at Fermilab (E581/E704 Collaborations, 1991)
* The European Muon Collaboration (1988) found that only "14 ± 9 ± 21% of the proton spin is carried by the spin of the quarks"

## Context

This work bridges two previously parallel research programs: the spin-and-transverse-momentum paradigm driven by experiments such as those at RHIC (PHENIX, STAR, BRAHMS) and FNAL (E581/E704), and the gluon saturation paradigm driven by theoretical consistency requirements for unitarity at high energies. The analysis extends the McLerran-Venugopalan model to include spin degrees of freedom, enabling future work that connects transverse-momentum-dependent parton distributions to saturation physics and opens the door to using spin asymmetries as probes of the saturation regime in current and future collider experiments.
