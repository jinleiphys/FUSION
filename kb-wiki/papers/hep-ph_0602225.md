# More on the infrared renormalization group limit cycle in QCD

> We present a detailed study of the recently conjectured infrared renormalization group limit cycle in QCD using chiral effective field theory. It was conjectured that small increases in the up and down quark masses can move QCD to the critical trajectory for an infrared limit cycle in the three-nucleon system. At the critical quark masses, the binding energies of the deuteron and its spin-singlet partner are tuned to zero and the triton has infinitely many excited states with an accumulation point at the three-nucleon threshold. We exemplify three parameter sets where this effect occurs at next-to-leading order in the chiral counting. For one of them, we study the structure of the three-nucleon system in detail using both chiral and contact effective field theories. Furthermore, we investigate the matching of the chiral and contact theories in the critical region and calculate the influence of the limit cycle on three-nucleon scattering observables.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that by tuning the up and down quark masses (equivalently the pion mass) to specific critical values, QCD can be moved onto the critical trajectory for an infrared renormalization group limit cycle in the three-nucleon system, where the deuteron and its spin-singlet partner become unbound and the triton develops infinitely many excited states; however, the authors note that it is unlikely that physical QCD corresponds to any of the specific parameter sets they present, and a more detailed investigation is needed to test whether independent variation of the up and down quark masses can reach the critical point.

## Method

The authors used chiral effective field theory at next-to-leading order (NLO) to calculate the pion mass dependence of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, searching for parameter sets where the inverse scattering lengths in the spin-triplet and spin-singlet channels vanish simultaneously at the same critical pion mass. They then solved Faddeev equations with the chiral potential to obtain triton binding energies near the limit cycle, and matched these results to the simpler contact (pionless) effective field theory to calculate additional three-body observables and the structure of excited states.

## Key numbers

- Critical pion masses for three parameter sets: 197.8577 MeV (set a), 186.3276 MeV (set b), 179.0417 MeV (set c), all at NLO in chiral counting
- The universal discrete scaling factor for the Efimov effect: lambda_0^2 approximately 515
- The transcendental number s_0 = 1.0062378 appearing in the three-body force renormalization
- The three-body parameter Lambda_* varies smoothly by about 15% for pion masses between 190 and 210 MeV
- Typical error between chiral and contact EFT calculations: about 10% for the triton ground state and below 1% for the second excited state
- The bounds on dimensionless constants: -3 <= F_pi^2 Lambda_chi^2 bar D_{S,T} <= 3, where Lambda_chi is approximately 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the conjecture by Braaten, Hammer, and Meissner (2003) that QCD could be tuned to an infrared limit cycle by small changes in quark masses, using the quark mass dependence of nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths from Epelbaum, Gloeckle, and Meissner (2002). The study connects the Efimov effect (Efimov, 1971) in three-nucleon systems to renormalization group limit cycles, building on the contact EFT framework of Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1999). The results enable future investigations of whether independent variation of up and down quark masses can reach the critical trajectory, and provide a benchmark for testing the universality of three-body observables near the limit cycle.
