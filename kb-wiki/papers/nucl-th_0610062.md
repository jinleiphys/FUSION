# Strangeness in the proton and N*(1535)

> The newest progress on the study of the strangeness in the proton and in the lowest negative parity nucleon excited state $N^*(1535)$ is reviewed. Implications on the internal quark structure of the proton, $N^*(1535)$ and other baryons are discussed. The diquark cluster picture for the 5-quark components in baryons gives a natural explanation not only to the empirical indications for a positive strangeness magnetic moment $μ_s$ and positive strangeness radius of the proton but also the longstanding mass-reverse problem of $N^*(1535)$, $N^*(1440)$ and $Λ^*(1405)$ resonances as well as the unusual decay pattern of the $N^*(1535)$ resonance. Evidence for possible existence of $N^*(1535)$'s ${1/2}^-$ SU(3) nonet partners in this picture is pointed out, and suggestion is made to search for these $1/2^-$ hyperon excited states under the well known $Σ^*(1385)$, $Λ^*(1520)$ and $Ξ^*(1530)$ peaks in various reactions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the diquark cluster picture for 5-quark components in baryons can simultaneously explain the positive strangeness magnetic moment and positive strangeness radius of the proton, the mass-reverse problem of the \(N^*(1535)\), \(N^*(1440)\) and \(\Lambda^*(1405)\) resonances, and the unusual decay pattern of the \(N^*(1535)\), but notes that a recent combined analysis of parity-violating electron scattering data gives strange form factors consistent with zero, which would imply roughly equal amounts of meson-cloud and \(q^2q^2\bar q\) components in the proton.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental data from BES at BEPC on \(J/\psi \to \bar p p \eta\) and \(J/\psi \to \bar p K^+ \Lambda\) to extract the ratio of effective coupling constants \(g_{N^*(1535)K\Lambda}/g_{N^*(1535)p\eta}\). It then applies a coupled-channel Breit-Wigner formula including the \(N^*K\Lambda\) coupling to recalculate the Breit-Wigner mass of the \(N^*(1535)\). The paper also examines invariant mass spectra from \(J/\psi \to pK^-\bar\Lambda\) and \(J/\psi \to \Lambda\bar\Sigma^+\pi^-\) for evidence of predicted SU(3) partner states.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of effective coupling constants is \(g_{N^*(1535)K\Lambda}/g_{N^*(1535)p\eta} = 1.3 \pm 0.3\).
- The excess of \(\bar d\) over \(\bar u\) in the proton is 0.12.
- The branching ratio for \(J/\psi \to \bar\Sigma^-\Sigma^*(1385)^+\) is \((3.1 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-4}\).
- The branching ratio for \(J/\psi \to \bar\Xi^+\Xi^*(1530)^-\) is \((5.9 \pm 1.5) \times 10^{-4}\).
- The branching ratio for \(J/\psi \to \bar p N^*(1535)^+\) is \((10 \pm 3) \times 10^{-4}\).
- The strangeness spin \(\Delta_s\) has an empirical indication of \((-0.10 \pm 0.06)\).
- The predicted masses for SU(3) partner states are: an additional \(\Lambda^*~1/2^-\) around 1570 MeV, a triplet \(\Sigma^*~1/2^-\) around 1360 MeV, and a doublet \(\Xi^*~1/2^-\) around 1520 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the diquark cluster model previously applied to the \(\theta^+\) pentaquark by Jaffe (2003) to the proton and excited baryons, offering an alternative to the meson-cloud picture of Thomas (1983) and Weise (2005). The analysis contradicts earlier predictions of negative strangeness magnetic moment and radius from Brodsky (1998) and Musolf (1994), and provides a framework to resolve the mass ordering failure of the classical 3-quark constituent quark model for the \(N^*(1535)\), \(N^*(1440)\), and \(\Lambda^*(1405)\). The results motivate searches for predicted \(1/2^-\) hyperon states under known \(\Sigma^*(1385)\), \(\Lambda^*(1520)\), and \(\Xi^*(1530)\) peaks at facilities such as CEBAF and SPring-8, and with BESIII data.
