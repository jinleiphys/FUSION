# Coherence Optimization in Neutron Interferometry through Defocussing

> A zero-area four-blade perfect crystal neutron interferometer (NI) possess a decoherence-free subspace (DFS) for low-frequency mechanical vibrations and thus is easier to site. %has the potential to broaden the application of crystal-based neutron interferometry to a higher number of neutron sources. However, unlike the standard three-blade Mach-Zehnder NI the ideal contrast of this four-blade NI geometry is less than one. By applying a recently introduced quantum information model for dynamical diffraction we show that the contrast for the four-blade DFS NI can be increased by offsetting the focusing condition. The contrast optimization leads to an NI geometry where the distances between the centers of the blades are equidistant. An experiment is proposed to verify the increase in contrast.

## Key claim

By relaxing the focusing condition that demands the two paths inside a four-blade decoherence-free subspace (DFS) neutron interferometer be refocused on the last blade, the contrast can be increased to a maximum at a specific defocusing condition where the distances between the centers of the blades are equal, rather than the standard geometry where the separation between the second and third blade is twice that of the other separations.

## Method

The authors applied a quantum information (QI) model of dynamical diffraction, which discretizes the scattering medium into coarse-grained unitary operators, to simulate the four-blade DFS neutron interferometer geometry. They modeled defocusing by translating the neutron wavefunction of one path by m nodes in the transverse direction, equivalent to displacing the last blade by Δz, and computed the coherence function Γ_m as a function of the defocusing parameter m. The contrast was then optimized over the QI model parameters ξ, θ, ζ, and N (number of layers).

## Key numbers

- Optimal contrast of 0.68 was obtained at N = 44 and θ = π/64 for the standard four-blade DFS neutron interferometer geometry.
- Maximum contrast occurs at a blade displacement Δz equal to the crystal thickness z0, which corresponds to m = N/2 for the optimal values N = 44 and θ = π/64.
- The three-blade Mach-Zehnder neutron interferometer simulation used N = 100 and θ = π/32, showing contrast starting at a maximum when m = 0 and falling steadily to zero as defocusing increases.
- The proposed experiment uses neutrons of wavelength 4.4 Å, with the time τ a neutron takes between the first and second blades appearing in the noise contrast expression.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the four-blade DFS neutron interferometer first demonstrated by Pushin (2011), which had a measured contrast of only 25%, less than half the expected value. The authors build on a recent study by Nsofini (2017) suggesting the maximum achievable contrast for the DFS NI is unavoidably less than one, and show that defocusing can recover higher contrast. The work enables experimental verification using existing neutron interferometry facilities at the National Institute of Standards and Technology, and motivates construction of defocused four-blade DFS NI geometries that trade perfect noise cancellation for higher contrast.
