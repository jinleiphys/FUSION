# On inclusion of the long-range proton-proton Coulomb force in the three-nucleon scattering Faddeev calculations

> We propose a simplified approach to incorporate the long-range proton-proton (pp) Coulomb force in the three-nucleon (3N) scattering calculations, based on exact formulation presented in Eur. Phys. Journal A {\bf{41}}, 369 (2009) and {\bf{41}}, 385 (2009). It permits us to get elastic proton-deuteron (pd) scattering and breakup observables relatively simply by performing standard Faddeev calculations as known for the neutron-deuteron (nd) system. The basic ingredient in that approach is a 3-dimensional screened pp Coulomb t-matrix obtained by numerical solution of the 3-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger (LS) equation. Based on this t-matrix pure Coulomb transition terms contributing to elastic scattering and breakup are calculated without any need for partial wave decomposition. For elastic scattering such a term removes the Rutherford amplitude for point deuteron proton-deuteron (pd) scattering. For breakup it has never been applied in spite of the fact that its contributions could become important in some regions of the breakup phase space. We demonstrate numerically that the pd elastic observables can be determined directly from the resulting 3N amplitudes without any renormalization, simply by increasing the screening radius in order to reach the existing screening limit. However,for pd breakup the renormalization of the contributing on-shell amplitudes is required. We apply our approach in a wide energy range of the incoming proton for pd elastic scattering as well as for pd breakup reaction.

## Key claim

The authors propose a simplified approach to include the long-range proton-proton Coulomb force in three-nucleon Faddeev calculations that avoids the computationally demanding terms involving the 3-dimensional screened Coulomb t-matrix, provided that the partial wave basis is extended to sufficiently high angular momenta where only the Coulomb interaction acts. For elastic proton-deuteron scattering the screening limit exists without renormalization, but for breakup the on-shell amplitudes require renormalization by removing an oscillatory phase factor from the half-shell pp t-matrix.

## Method

The authors start from the exact Faddeev equations in momentum-space partial wave basis, separating states with total two-nucleon angular momentum j below some maximum (where both nuclear and Coulomb forces act) from states with higher j (where only the screened pp Coulomb force acts). They simplify the exact coupled equations by omitting the terms containing the 3-dimensional screened Coulomb t-matrix, reducing the system to a form identical to neutron-deuteron Faddeev calculations. For elastic scattering the transition amplitude is obtained from off-shell Faddeev amplitudes without renormalization, while for breakup the on-shell amplitudes require renormalization of the pp-partition contributions by removing the oscillatory phase factor from the half-shell pp t-matrix.

## Key numbers

- The screening radius R is increased to reach the existing screening limit for elastic pd observables, with the screening function using exponential form V_c^R(r) = alpha/r * exp(-(r/R)^n)
- The on-shell condition for breakup is given by p^2/m + 3q^2/(4m) = E = 3q_max^2/(4m), with p_0 = sqrt(3/4 * (q_max^2 - q^2))
- The oscillatory phase factor for exponential screening is Phi_n^R(p) = -eta[ln(2pR) - gamma/n] + eta*sum_{k=1}^{infinity} (-1)^k/(kn k! (2pR)^{kn}), where eta = m_p*alpha/(2p) is the Sommerfeld parameter and gamma = 0.5772 is the Euler number

## Context

This work extends the exact formulation presented in Eur. Phys. Journal A 41, 369 (2009) and 41, 385 (2009) by the same authors, which used 3-dimensional screened Coulomb t-matrices but was computationally demanding. It contrasts with the variational hyperspherical harmonic approach of Kievsky and collaborators, and with the partial-wave-based screened Coulomb approach of Deltuva and collaborators (2005-2006) which required renormalization for both elastic scattering and breakup. The simplified scheme enables faster calculations suitable for fine-tuning chiral forces using high-precision pd data, and for the first time includes the pure Coulomb term for breakup that was previously omitted in all pd breakup calculations.
