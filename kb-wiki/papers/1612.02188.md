# Microscopic Study of ${}^1{S_0}$ Superfluidity in Dilute Neutron Matter

> Singlet $S$-wave superfluidity of dilute neutron matter is studied within the correlated BCS method, which takes into account both pairing and short-range correlations. First, the equation of state (EOS) of normal neutron matter is calculated within the Correlated Basis Function (CBF) method in lowest cluster order using the ${}^1{S_0}$ and ${}^3P$ components of the Argonne $V_{18}$ potential, assuming trial Jastrow-type correlation functions. The ${}^1{S_0}$ superfluid gap is then calculated with the corresponding component of the Argonne $V_{18}$ potential and the optimally determined correlation functions. The dependence of our results on the chosen forms for the correlation functions is studied, and the role of the $P$-wave channel is investigated. Where comparison is meaningful, the values obtained for the ${}^1{S_0}$ gap within this simplified scheme are consistent with the results of similar and more elaborate microscopic methods.

# Microscopic Study of ${}^1{S_0}$ Superfluidity in Dilute Neutron Matter

## Key claim

The ${}^1{S_0}$ superfluid gap in dilute neutron matter calculated within a simplified correlated BCS scheme using Jastrow-type correlation functions and only the $S$ and $P$ components of the Argonne $V_{18}$ potential yields values consistent with more elaborate microscopic methods, but only where comparison is meaningful and with the caveat that higher-order cluster corrections and three-nucleon forces are expected to be minimal in the density regime considered.

## Method

The authors first compute the equation of state of normal neutron matter using the Correlated Basis Function method in lowest cluster order, with Jastrow-type correlation functions and the ${}^1{S_0}$ and ${}^3P$ components of the Argonne $V_{18}$ potential. They then solve the gap equation for the ${}^1{S_0}$ superfluid gap using the separation method of Khodel, Khodel, and Clark, employing the same optimally determined correlation functions and an effective-mass approximation for single-particle energies.

## Key numbers

- The smallness parameter $\xi$ governing cluster expansion convergence ranges from $5.66 \times 10^{-6}$ at $k_F = 0.1~{\rm fm}^{-1}$ to $0.006$ at $k_F = 1~{\rm fm}^{-1}$ and $0.026$ at $k_F = 1.5~{\rm fm}^{-1}$ for the Benhar correlation case, and from $5.36 \times 10^{-6}$ at $k_F = 0.1~{\rm fm}^{-1}$ to $0.006$ at $k_F = 1~{\rm fm}^{-1}$ and $0.027$ at $k_F = 1.5~{\rm fm}^{-1}$ for the Dave correlation case.
- The gap function $\Delta(k)$ at $k_F = 0.9~{\rm fm}^{-1}$ exhibits a node at $k \simeq 2~{\rm fm}^{-1}$ for all four correlation choices.
- Inclusion of the $P$-wave contribution begins to play a role for $k_F$ values beyond about $0.8~{\rm fm}^{-1}$, and the gap values with $P$-wave effects are slightly smaller than $S$-wave-only values for $k_F \gtrsim 0.7~{\rm fm}^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the correlated BCS approach previously applied by Chen et al. (1993) to neutron matter superfluidity, using the Argonne $V_{18}$ potential rather than earlier potentials and including both $S$ and $P$ partial-wave components. The results supplement the broader literature of ${}^1{S_0}$ gap calculations including those by Khodel et al. (1996), Schwenk et al. (2003), and various Monte Carlo studies by Gandolfi et al. (2008, 2009, 2010), with the present simplified scheme producing gaps compatible with these more elaborate methods. The correlation functions determined here can be used for calculating other observables of low-density neutron matter beyond the pairing gap.
