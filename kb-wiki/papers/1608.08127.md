# Realistic calculations for $c$-coefficients of the isobaric mass multiplet equation in $1p0f$ shell nuclei

> We present calculations for the $c$-coefficients of the isobaric mass multiplet equation for nuclei from $A=42$ to $A=54$ based on input from three realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions. We demonstrate that there is a clear dependence on the short-ranged charge-symmetry breaking (CSB) part of the strong interaction and that there is significant disagreement in the CSB part between the commonly used CD-Bonn, N$^3$LO, and Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon interactions. In addition, we show that all three interactions give a CSB contribution to the $c$-coefficient that is too large when compared to experiment.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the charge-symmetry breaking (CSB) contribution to the $c$-coefficient of the isobaric mass multiplet equation (IMME) for nuclei from $A=42$ to $A=54$ is too large when computed from any of three realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions (CD-Bonn, N$^3$LO, and Argonne V18), and that the three interactions yield significantly different CSB contributions from each other, indicating a deficiency in understanding of isospin-symmetry breaking in the nuclear force.

## Method

Shell-model calculations using the program BIGSTICK were performed for odd-odd $N=Z$ nuclei and their $T=1$ analogs in the $1p0f$ shell with $42 \le A \le 54$, using the full $1p0f$-shell model space except for $A=54$ where up to five particles excited from the $0f_{7/2}$ orbit were permitted. The $c$-coefficients were computed via first-order perturbation theory using eigenstates from the isoscalar GX1A Hamiltonian, with CSB interactions derived from the CD-Bonn, N$^3$LO, and Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon interactions through renormalization using either the $G$-matrix approach or the V$_{low~k}$ method, followed by many-body perturbation theory up to $3^{\rm rd}$ order.

## Key numbers

- The $c$-coefficient for $A=10$ from CD-Bonn in no-core shell model calculations was 535 keV, compared to the experimental value of 362 keV.
- The average difference for diagonal Coulomb two-body matrix elements between harmonic oscillator and energy-density functional calculations for $^{48}$Cr was about 1 keV; the difference for the $0f_{7/2}$ orbital was (13, 10, 8, 8) keV for $J$ = (0, 2, 4, 6) TBME.
- The Coulomb contribution to the $c$-coefficient increases by 10-20% at $3^{\rm rd}$ order in many-body perturbation theory compared to $1^{\rm st}$ and $2^{\rm nd}$ order.
- The harmonic oscillator basis used $\hbar\omega = 10.5$ MeV with an effective Hilbert space of the first twelve oscillator shells; the V$_{low~k}$ cut-off parameter was $\Lambda = 2.1$ fm$^{-1}$.
- The $A$-dependence of Coulomb matrix elements was scaled by $\sqrt{\hbar\omega(A)/10.5}$, where $\hbar\omega(A)$ was determined from spherical EDF calculations for $42 \le A \le 54$.

## Context

This work extends earlier empirical studies of isospin-nonconserving interactions for $0s1d$ and $1p0f$-shell nuclei by Ormand (year not specified), which found that $c$-coefficients are well reproduced if the $T=1$ $pn$ interaction is 2% more attractive than the average of $pp$ and $nn$. It also builds on the discussion of mirror-energy displacement and triple-energy displacement by Zuker (2002) and Gadea (2006), which concluded that the observed $J$-dependence was explained by underlying two-body matrix elements. The overprediction of the $c$-coefficient for CD-Bonn was previously noted by Caurier (2002) in no-core shell model calculations for $A=10$ nuclei. The results enable future investigations to use the $c$-coefficient as a constraint on the CSB part of nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions, and suggest that first-order Coulomb plus 80% of the N$^3$LO CSB part provides a good empirical description of the data.
