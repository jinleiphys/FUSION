# Transition Distribution Amplitudes for gamma* gamma collisions

> We study the exclusive production of pi-pi and rho-pi in hard gamma* gamma scattering in the forward kinematical region where the virtuality of one photon provides us with a hard scale in the process. The newly introduced concept of Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDA) is used to perform a QCD calculation of these reactions thanks to two simple models for TDAs. The sizable cross sections for rho-pi and pi-pi production may be tested at intense electron-positron colliders such as CLEO and B factories (Belle and BaBar).

## Key claim

The paper claims that exclusive $\pi\pi$ and $\rho\pi$ production in hard $\gamma^\star \gamma$ scattering can be calculated using the newly introduced concept of Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs), but the cross section estimates rely on two simple toy models for the TDAs since no non-perturbative calculations of the defining matrix elements exist.

## Method

The authors factorize the amplitude for $\gamma_L^\star \gamma \to A \pi$ (where $A$ is a $\rho$ or $\pi$ meson) into a convolution of a perturbatively calculable hard scattering amplitude, the distribution amplitude of meson $A$, and a photon-to-pion TDA. They construct two models for the vector TDA $V(x,\xi,t)$: Model 1 uses a double distribution ansatz with a linear $\beta$-dependence and a vector form factor normalization, while Model 2 uses a $t$-dependent double distribution from Tiburzi (2005). The differential cross section for $e\gamma \to e\rho\pi$ is then computed as a function of $\xi$ and $Q^2$ for both models.

## Key numbers

- The vector form factor for the pion is $F_V^{\pi^{\pm}} = 0.017 \pm 0.008$, used to normalize the TDA sum rule.
- The $\rho$ meson decay constant is taken as $f_\rho = 0.216$ GeV.
- The asymptotic meson distribution amplitude used is $\phi_\rho(z) = 6z\bar{z}$.
- The scaling law for the amplitude is ${\cal M}^{TDA}_{\gamma^\star \gamma}(Q^2, \xi,t) \sim \alpha_s \sqrt{-t} / Q$.
- The differential cross section is plotted for $s_{e\gamma} = 40$ GeV$^2$, $t = -0.5$ GeV$^2$, $Q^2 = 4$ GeV$^2$ (for $\xi$ dependence), and $\xi = 0.2$ (for $Q^2$ dependence).
- The strong coupling constant is set to $\alpha_s = 1$ in the numerical study.

## Context

This work extends the factorisation theorems for exclusive processes, previously applied to deep-exclusive $\gamma\gamma$ reactions using distribution amplitudes (ERBL) and generalised distribution amplitudes (GDA), to the case of forward hard exclusive scattering in $\gamma^\star\gamma$ collisions. The new TDA framework is contrasted with generalised parton distributions (GPDs), where the forward limit connects to parton distributions from deep inelastic scattering (DIS); no such constraint exists for TDAs. The results enable experimental tests at intense electron-positron colliders such as CLEO and the B factories (Belle and BaBar), and the model-dependent cross section estimates provide a basis for future measurements that could extract the TDA matrix elements.
