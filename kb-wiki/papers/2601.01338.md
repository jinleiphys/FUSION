# Investigating $Ωϕ$ Interaction and Correlation Functions

> In this work, we investigate the interaction between the $Ω$ baryon and the $s\bar{s}$ meson within the framework of the quark delocalization color screening model. The spectra calculations show that no bound state is formed in any of the considered channels, while the scattering indicates that the $Ωϕ$ interaction with $J^{P}=1/2^{-}$ is weakly attractive. As for the $Ωϕ$ interactions with $J^{P}=3/2^{-}$ and $5/2^{-}$, as well as the $Ωη^{\prime}$ interaction with $J^{P}=3/2^{-}$, they are all repulsive. After an investigation on the femtoscopic correlation functions, we find that, due to the spin-averaging effect, the overall $Ωϕ$ correlation function exhibits a weak dependence on the source size, which provides a crucial significance of our model for future experimental examinations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that within the quark delocalization color screening model, no bound state is formed in any considered channel of the Omega-phi or Omega-eta-prime systems, but the Omega-phi interaction with J^P = 1/2^- is weakly attractive, while all other channels are repulsive; the overall Omega-phi correlation function shows weak dependence on source size due to spin-averaging effects, which the authors argue provides significance for future experimental tests in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors employed the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM), a constituent quark model incorporating quark delocalization and color screening, to calculate the interaction between the Omega baryon and s\bar{s} mesons. They performed bound-state calculations and low-energy scattering analyses using the resonating group method (RGM) and generating coordinates method for the Omega-phi and Omega-eta-prime systems in S-wave configurations with different spin-parity quantum numbers. Femtoscopic correlation functions were then computed using the Lednicky-Lyuboshits (LL) model based on the extracted scattering parameters, with spin-averaging applied to account for experimental conditions.

## Key numbers

* Omega-phi system with J^P = 1/2^-: scattering length = -1.29 fm, effective range = 1.53 fm, indicating weakly attractive interaction
* Omega-phi system with J^P = 3/2^-: scattering length = 0.26 fm, effective range = 0.80 fm, indicating repulsive interaction
* Omega-phi system with J^P = 5/2^-: scattering length = 0.91 fm, effective range = 0.70 fm, indicating repulsive interaction
* Omega-eta-prime system with J^P = 3/2^-: scattering length = 0.71 fm, effective range = 1.15 fm, indicating repulsive interaction
* Calculated energy for Omega-phi with J^P = 1/2^- is 2666 MeV, compared to threshold 2662 MeV (4 MeV above threshold)
* Calculated energy for Omega-phi with J^P = 3/2^- is 2668 MeV, compared to threshold 2662 MeV
* Calculated energy for Omega-phi with J^P = 5/2^- is 2669 MeV, compared to threshold 2662 MeV
* Calculated energy for Omega-eta-prime with J^P = 3/2^- is 2560 MeV, compared to threshold 2554 MeV

## Context

This work extends the application of the quark delocalization color screening model, previously used for systems such as N-Omega and Omega-Omega by Huang (2015) and Yan (2024), to the less-studied Omega-meson sector. The study is motivated by experimental femtoscopic measurements by the ALICE Collaboration (2020, 2021) that supported attractive interactions for Omega-proton and phi-proton systems, and by theoretical interest in the Omega(2012) resonance as a possible hadronic molecular state studied by Valderrama (2018), Huang (2018), and others. The results provide theoretical predictions for Omega-phi and Omega-eta-prime correlation functions that can be tested in future relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments, particularly given the weak source-size dependence of the spin-averaged Omega-phi correlation function.
