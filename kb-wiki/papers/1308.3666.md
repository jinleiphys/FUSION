# The Nuclear Pairing Gap -- How Low Can It Go?

> The pairing gap for $^{53}$Ca obtained from new experimental data on the masses of $^{52-54}$Ca has the smallest value yet observed. This is explained in the framework of the nuclear shell model with schematic and realistic Hamiltonians as being due to shell gaps around the low-$ j $ orbital $ 1p_{1/2} $. Minima in the pairing gaps for all nuclei are shown and discussed

## Key claim

The pairing gap for ⁵³Ca, derived from new mass measurements of ⁵²⁻⁵⁴Ca, is the smallest value yet observed in any nucleus, but this low value is conditional on the specific shell structure around the low-j 1p₁/₂ orbital and is only observed for calcium (Z=20) because the required shell gaps quickly disappear for neighboring elements.

## Method

The author used the nuclear shell model in the fp model space (0f₇/₂, 0f₅/₂, 1p₃/₂, 1p₁/₂) with the GX1A (GXPF1A) and KB3G effective Hamiltonians, as well as an ab-initio Hamiltonian including three-nucleon interactions, to calculate binding energies and one-neutron separation energies for calcium isotopes. The odd-even staggering in separation energies was quantified using the Dₙ(N) parameter (twice the pairing gap), and the results were compared to experimental masses from the 2012 mass table and new data for ⁵¹⁻⁵⁴Ca. The author also employed the schematic surface-delta interaction (SDI) and modified surface-delta interaction (MSDI) models with varying shell gaps to understand the underlying mechanisms producing minima in the pairing gaps.

## Key numbers

- The experimental Dₒ value for ⁵³Ca is 0.63(10) MeV, compared to calculated values of 1.170 MeV (MBPT), 0.425 MeV (GX1A), and 0.489 MeV (KB3G)
- The ⁵³Ca Dₒ value of 0.63 MeV ties with that of ²⁰⁷Pb (0.630 MeV), but when scaled by (A)⁻¹/², the scaled minimum is 50% lower in ⁵³Ca compared to ²⁰⁷Pb
- The <(1p₁/₂)²|V|(1p₁/₂)²> effective two-body matrix element is 0.151 MeV for KB3G and 0.053 MeV for GX1A, compared to -0.20 MeV with the MSDI model
- In the SDI model with a completely isolated 1p₁/₂ orbital, Dₒ = -2C = 0.4 MeV
- The MSDI model includes a constant B = 0.2 MeV that adds to the interaction

## Context

This work extends the understanding of nuclear pairing gaps by identifying the specific mechanism of isolated low-j orbitals producing minima in the odd-even staggering parameter Dₒ, building on the framework of Bohr and Mottelson (1975) for the odd-even mass parameter. The results contradict the predictions of global mass models such as the DZ31 model of Duflo and Zuker and the HFB-21 Skyrme interaction, neither of which predicted the dip at N=33 in calcium. The findings enable targeted mass measurements of neutron-rich nuclei such as ²¹,²²C, ⁷⁸⁻⁸⁰Ni, and more neutron-rich calcium isotopes to test for similar minima at N=15, N=51, and N=34 respectively.
