# A Generalized Linear Transport Model for Spatially-Correlated Stochastic Media

> We formulate a new model for transport in stochastic media with long-range spatial correlations where exponential attenuation (controlling the propagation part of the transport) becomes power law. Direct transmission over optical distance $τ(s)$, for fixed physical distance $s$, thus becomes $(1+τ(s)/a)^{-a}$, with standard exponential decay recovered when $a\to\infty$. Atmospheric turbulence phenomenology for fluctuating optical properties rationalizes this switch. Foundational equations for this generalized transport model are stated in integral form for $d=1,2,3$ spatial dimensions. A deterministic numerical solution is developed in $d=1$ using Markov Chain formalism, verified with Monte Carlo, and used to investigate internal radiation fields. Standard two-stream theory, where diffusion is exact, is recovered when $a=\infty$. Differential diffusion equations are not presently known when $a<\infty$, nor is the integro-differential form of the generalized transport equation. Monte Carlo simulations are performed in $d=2$, as a model for transport on random surfaces, to explore scaling behavior of transmittance $T$ when transport optical thickness $τ_\text{t} \gg 1$. Random walk theory correctly predicts $T \propto τ_\text{t}^{-\min\{1,a/2\}}$ in the absence of absorption. Finally, single scattering theory in $d=3$ highlights the model's violation of angular reciprocity when $a<\infty$, a desirable property at least in atmospheric applications. This violation is traced back to a key trait of generalized transport theory, namely, that we must distinguish more carefully between two kinds of propagation: one that ends in a virtual or actual detection, the other in a transition from one position to another in the medium.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a generalized linear transport model where exponential attenuation is replaced by a power-law transmission law $(1+\tau(s)/a)^{-a}$, with standard exponential decay recovered only in the limit $a\to\infty$, and this generalization is motivated specifically by long-range spatial correlations in stochastic media such as the Earth's cloudy atmosphere.

## Method

The authors formulate the generalized transport model in integral form for $d=1,2,3$ spatial dimensions, replacing the standard exponential transmission factor $\exp(-\tau)$ with the power-law form $(1+\tau/a)^{-a}$ in the kernel of the integral radiative transfer equation. A deterministic numerical solution is developed in $d=1$ using Markov Chain formalism and verified with Monte Carlo simulations. Monte Carlo simulations are also performed in $d=2$ to explore scaling behavior of transmittance, and single scattering theory in $d=3$ is used to examine angular reciprocity.

## Key numbers

- Direct transmission over optical distance $\tau(s)$ becomes $(1+\tau(s)/a)^{-a}$, with standard exponential decay recovered when $a\to\infty$
- For $d=2$ transport on random surfaces with transport optical thickness $\tau_\text{t} \gg 1$ and no absorption, transmittance scales as $T \propto \tau_\text{t}^{-\min\{1,a/2\}}$
- The Henyey-Greenstein phase function parameter $g$ is defined as $g = \int_{\Xi_d}\Omvec^\prime\cdot\Omvec p(\Omvec^\prime\cdot\Omvec)\dif\Omvec$
- In $d=3$, the constant $c_d$ in boundary conditions equals $\pi$; in $d=2$, $c_d = 2$; in $d=1$, $c_d = 1$
- The diffusion boundary condition constant $\chi_d$ equals $2/3$ for $d=3$, $\pi/4$ for $d=2$, and $1$ for $d=1$

## Context

This work extends the line of stochastic transport theory developed by Levermore, Pomraning et al. (1986, 1988) for binary mixture models and by Davis and Marshak (2004) and Davis (2006) for cloudy atmosphere applications. It also connects with the work of Davis and Mineev-Weinstein (2011) on power-law statistics in stochastic media. The model enables investigation of transport in media where homogenization fails due to long-range correlations, and provides a framework for understanding observed angular non-reciprocity in atmospheric radiative transfer.
