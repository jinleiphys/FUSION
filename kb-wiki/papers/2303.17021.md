# Theoretical and Experimental Constraints for the Equation of State of Dense and Hot Matter

> This review aims at providing an extensive discussion of modern constraints relevant for dense and hot strongly interacting matter. It includes theoretical first-principle results from lattice and perturbative QCD, as well as chiral effective field theory results. From the experimental side, it includes heavy-ion collision and low-energy nuclear physics results, as well as observations from neutron stars and their mergers. The validity of different constraints, concerning specific conditions and ranges of applicability, is also provided.

## Key claim

This review compiles modern constraints on the equation of state of dense and hot strongly interacting matter from first-principle theoretical calculations (lattice QCD, perturbative QCD, chiral effective field theory) and experimental observations (heavy-ion collisions, low-energy nuclear physics, neutron star astrophysics), while explicitly noting that the validity of each constraint is limited to specific thermodynamic regimes and ranges of applicability.

## Method

The authors survey theoretical constraints from lattice QCD (reliable at vanishing baryon chemical potential for temperatures above approximately 125 MeV, and at finite chemical potential up to mu_B/T ~ 3.5 via expansion methods), perturbative QCD (applicable at temperatures above approximately 300 MeV at zero chemical potential and at baryon densities above approximately 40 times nuclear saturation density at zero temperature), and chiral effective field theory (applicable for baryon densities below approximately 2 times nuclear saturation density and temperatures below approximately 20 MeV). On the experimental side, they compile heavy-ion collision results across a range of center-of-mass beam energies from sqrt(s_NN) = 2 to 200 GeV, low-energy nuclear physics measurements of the symmetry energy around saturation density, and astrophysical observations from neutron stars and their mergers including gravitational wave and X-ray data.

## Key numbers

- Lattice QCD provides the equation of state reliably for temperatures T greater than approximately 125 MeV at vanishing baryon chemical potential
- At finite baryon chemical potential, lattice QCD expansions are valid up to mu_B/T approximately 3.5
- Perturbative QCD is applicable at T greater than approximately 300 MeV at mu_B = 0
- Perturbative QCD is applicable at baryon densities n_B greater than approximately 40 times nuclear saturation density at T = 0
- Chiral effective field theory calculations are applicable for n_B less than approximately 2 times nuclear saturation density
- Heavy-ion collisions probe temperatures in the range T approximately 50 to 650 MeV depending on center-of-mass beam energy
- Heavy-ion collision beam energies range from sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 to 200 GeV at RHIC and sqrt(s_NN) approximately 2 to 11 GeV at lower-energy facilities
- Low-energy heavy-ion collisions reach baryon chemical potentials mu_B approximately 20 to 800 MeV
- The crossover phase transition from hadron gas to quark-gluon plasma occurs at T approximately 135 MeV from lattice QCD

## Context

This review extends and systematizes the discussion of constraints on the QCD equation of state across multiple regimes, building on prior work including lattice QCD calculations by Borsanyi (2021, 2022), perturbative QCD results by Haque (2013, 2014, 2020) and Gorda (2018, 2021), and chiral effective field theory developments by Drischler (2020, 2021) and Tews (2018). The paper enables future work by clearly delineating the regimes where each constraint is valid and where effective models must connect these regimes, particularly for the QCD phase diagram at high baryon density relevant to neutron star interiors and low-energy heavy-ion collisions.
