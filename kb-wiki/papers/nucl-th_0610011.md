# Recent progress in eta-nucleus physics

> This is the summary of the 'International Workshop on eta--Nucleus Physics' held in Juelich, May 8--12, 2006. Each talk is represented by an extended abstract. At the same time the document provides a progress report of the corresponding working group.

## Key claim

This workshop summary and working group progress report consolidates the status of eta-nucleus physics as of 2006, concluding that while there is no conflict between a sizable imaginary part of the eta-A scattering length and recent claims, the size of the imaginary part can only be resolved experimentally from very near-threshold eta-A production data, and a small imaginary part remains a precondition for a (quasi)bound state.

## Method

The document compiles extended abstracts from talks at the International Workshop on eta-Nucleus Physics (May 8-12, 2006, Julich), covering photoproduction of eta mesons off nucleons and nuclei, Crystal Ball data on pion and eta production on complex targets, the dp to He-3 eta reaction at ANKE, and searches for eta-mesic nuclei at COSY-11 and with the ENSTAR detector. Each talk presents experimental measurements and theoretical analyses using standard hadronic physics terminology including scattering lengths, final state interactions, and resonance parameters.

## Key numbers

- eta-N absorption cross section on heavy nuclei measured at Mainz TAPS: approximately 30 mb, corresponding to a mean-free path of about 2 fm (Roebig 1996)
- Neutron/proton cross section ratio for eta photoproduction in the S11(1535) range: approximately 2/3, from Mainz TAPS (Krusche 1995c), Bonn PHOENICS (Hoffmann-Rothe 1997), and Mainz TAPS (Weiss 2003)
- Upper limit for the cross section of p + Al-27 to He-3 + Mg-25_eta: 0.7 nbarn, from ENSTAR data at COSY (Kirillov 2006)
- Incident pion beam momenta for Crystal Ball data: 408 MeV/c and 750 MeV/c, on H2, C, Al, and Cu targets (Starostin 2006)
- Excess energy range for dp to He-3 eta at ANKE: from below threshold up to Q = +12 MeV, with additional data at Q = 20, 40, and 60 MeV (Mersmann 2006)
- Energy resolution for the ANKE dp to He-3 eta excitation function: Delta Q approximately 0.25 MeV (Mersmann 2006)
- Maximum photon beam energy at MAMI for Crystal Ball/TAPS data: 819 MeV (Starostin 2006)

## Context

This workshop summary extends the work of Bhalerao and Liu (1985) on eta-N scattering length predictions (a = 0.27 + i0.22) and the possibility of eta-mesic nuclei for A > 10, as well as subsequent scattering length calculations by many groups (Ueda 1991, Wilkin 1993, Green and Wycech 1996, Garcilazo and Pena 2001) that span the range 0.2-1.0 fm. It enables future experimental programs to resolve the size of the eta-A imaginary part through near-threshold production data, particularly for the eta-He-3 system using ANKE and COSY-11 measurements, and motivates searches for eta-mesic nuclei in heavier systems beyond He-4 where binding is expected to grow with increasing A.
