# Hadronization and Charm-Hadron Ratios in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Understanding the hadronization of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) remains a challenging problem in the study of strong-interaction matter as produced in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions (URHICs). The large mass of heavy quarks renders them excellent tracers of the color neutralization process of the QGP when they convert into various heavy-flavor (HF) hadrons. We develop a 4-momentum conserving recombination model for HF mesons and baryons that recovers the thermal and chemical equilibrium limits and accounts for space-momentum correlations (SMCs) of heavy quarks with partons of the hydrodynamically expanding QGP, thereby resolving a long-standing problem in quark coalescence models. The SMCs enhance the recombination of fast-moving heavy quarks with high-flow thermal quarks in the outer regions of the fireball. We also improve the hadro-chemistry with "missing" charm-baryon states, previously found to describe the large $Λ_c/D^0$ ratio observed in proton-proton collisions. Both SMCs and hadro-chemistry, as part of our HF hydro-Langevin-recombination model for the strongly coupled QGP, importantly figure in the description of recent data for the $Λ_c/D^0$ ratio and $D$-meson elliptic flow in URHICs.

## Key claim

The authors develop a 4-momentum conserving recombination model for heavy-flavor mesons and baryons that recovers thermal and chemical equilibrium limits, resolving a long-standing problem in quark coalescence models by accounting for space-momentum correlations (SMCs) of heavy quarks with partons of the hydrodynamically expanding QGP.

## Method

The authors generalize the resonance recombination model (RRM) from 2-body (meson) to 3-body (baryon) formation via a two-step process: first, two quarks recombine into a diquark, then the diquark recombines with a third quark into a baryon. They implement an event-by-event procedure where each diffusing charm quark entering a hydro cell at hadronization temperature T_H=170 MeV is assigned a recombination probability computed from thermal light-quark phase-space distributions, then sampled to produce hadron momentum distributions. The model incorporates a large set of "missing" charm-baryon states predicted by the relativistic-quark model and consistent with lattice-QCD, and includes Langevin simulations for charm-quark diffusion through the QGP followed by hadronic-phase propagation.

## Key numbers

- Hadronization temperature: T_H = 170 MeV
- Resonance widths: Γ_M ≈ 0.1 GeV for mesons, Γ_dq ≈ 0.2 GeV for diquarks, Γ_B ≈ 0.3 GeV for baryons
- Total charm cross section: dσ_c\bar{c}/dy ≈ 1.0 mb from midrapidity ALICE 5.02 TeV pp data
- Binary nucleon-nucleon collisions for 0-20% Pb-Pb at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV: N_coll ≈ 1370
- Resulting charm quark yield: dN_c/dy ≈ 15.4
- Probability normalization factor: P_0 = 3.6
- Kinetic freezeout temperature: T_kin = 110 MeV
- K-factor of 1.6 applied to QGP diffusion rate
- Upon doubling all resonance widths, D-meson observables change by less than 10%, while Λ_c suffers additional suppression up to ~30% at intermediate p_T ≈ 6 GeV

## Context

This work extends the resonance recombination model (RRM) previously developed for mesons (Ravagli 2007, He 2011) to baryon formation, resolving a long-standing problem in instantaneous coalescence models (ICMs) identified by Molnar (2004) and Fries (2008) regarding space-momentum correlations. The "missing" charm-baryon states were previously shown by He (2019) to account for the large Λ_c/D^0 ratio measured in pp collisions at the LHC. The model enables quantitative predictions for charm-hadron observables including nuclear modification factors R_AA, elliptic flow v_2, and hadron ratios in heavy-ion collisions at both RHIC and LHC energies.
