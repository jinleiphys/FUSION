# Nucleons, Nuclear Matter and Quark Matter: A unified NJL approach

> We use an effective quark model to describe both hadronic matter and deconfined quark matter. By calculating the equations of state and the corresponding neutron star properties, we show that the internal properties of the nucleon have important implications for the properties of these systems.

# Nucleons, Nuclear Matter and Quark Matter: A unified NJL approach

## Key claim

By equating the scalar diquark pairing strength in color superconducting quark matter with the scalar diquark interactions inside the nucleon within a unified Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, the authors show that the internal structure of the nucleon, specifically the balance between diquark correlations and pion cloud contributions, determines whether a phase transition to deconfined quark matter is physically plausible in neutron stars. The claim is conditional: without including pion cloud contributions to the nucleon mass (i.e., attributing all attraction to diquarks), the model produces a phase structure where quark matter overwhelms nuclear matter even at low densities, which is unphysical.

## Method

The authors use an NJL model with quark and diquark interactions to describe both hadronic matter (as a quark-diquark Faddeev approach in the static approximation) and deconfined quark matter (with color superconducting pairing in the scalar diquark channel). The nucleon mass is computed including pion cloud self-energy contributions from N→Nπ→N and N→Δπ→N processes, with the dipole cut-off Λ varied to produce nucleon self-energies Σ_N of 0, -200, -300, and -400 MeV. The effective potential is minimized for fixed chemical potentials in both phases, and phase diagrams are constructed by comparing pressures; globally charge neutral equations of state are built using mixed phase constructions (Gibbs criteria) between nuclear matter and quark matter, then fed into the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to compute neutron star masses and radii.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant reproduced: f_π = 93 MeV; pion mass: m_π = 140 MeV; constituent quark mass at zero density: M_0 = 400 MeV.
- Infra-red cut-off: Λ_IR = 285 MeV.
- Nuclear matter saturation density: ρ_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}; symmetry energy: a_4 = 32 MeV at ρ_0.
- Nucleon self-energies from pion cloud: Σ_N = 0, -200, -300, and -400 MeV, corresponding to dipole cut-offs Λ = (none), 710, 803, and 877 MeV respectively.
- Saturation densities for the four cases: 0.14, 0.19, 0.21, and 0.25 fm^{-3} as Σ_N increases.
- Maximum masses for pure hadronic stars: 2.4, 2.0, 1.8, and 1.7 solar masses as Σ_N increases (and scalar diquark coupling r_s decreases).
- Maximum masses for hybrid stars with quark cores: approximately 1.4 solar masses (r_s = 0.24) and 1.5 solar masses (r_s = 0.22).
- Radii of hybrid stars reduced by 2–4 km when pion cloud contribution increases.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Bentz and Thomas (2001) and the quark-meson coupling model (QMC, 1988) by using a unified NJL framework to connect nucleon structure to nuclear matter and quark matter equations of state. It directly tests the idea that diquark correlations within the nucleon (as in the Faddeev approach of Ishii et al., 1995) are the same interactions that produce color superconducting pairing in dense quark matter (the 2SC phase). The results enable future studies of hybrid neutron stars with quark cores, and suggest that observed neutron star masses near 1.4 solar masses could be explained by plateaus in the mass-radius relation arising from phase transitions, as discussed by Schertler et al. (2000) and Fraga et al. (2001). The paper also notes that including hyperons could shift the transition to higher densities and produce more massive hybrid stars.
