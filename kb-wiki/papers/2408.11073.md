# Semiclassical Features of Wobbling and Chiral Properties in Nuclei

> A semiclassical approach is used to describe the wobbling and chiral motion in even-even and odd-even nuclei The trial function involved in the variational equation for the quantal action is a coherent state for the SU(2 ) group associated to a triaxial rotor for the case of an even-even system and a coherent state for the group SU(2)$\otimes$ SU(2) describing the particle-core system. Application is made for $^{158}$Er and $^{161, 163, 165,167}$Lu, ${135}$Pr. Th parameters involved in the coherent state expression are complex numbers depending on time and play the role of the phase space generalized conjugate coordinates whose equation of motion may be brought to the Hamilton canonical form.Within a harmonic approximation one analytically obtains the wobbling frequencies which are further used to calculate the excitation energies for the states forming a band. A new procedure to quantize the classical orbitals is proposed. In the new picture the electromagnetic transition probabilities are calculated both for the in-band and the intraband transitions. Additionally, for odd systems, some other observable like aligned angular momentum, excitation energies relative to a reference spectrum, dynamic moment of energies are calculated and compared with the corresponding experimental data.In the parameter space one identified several nuclear phases with specific properties. An example is given where the wobbling and chiral motions coexist. The existence of a transversal wobbling is widely commented. A new boson representation for the angular momenta is proposed. The approach was successfully applied to $^{135}$Pr. One concludes that semiclassical description is an efficient tool to account quantitatively for the wobbling and chiral properties in nuclei.

## Key claim

The semiclassical approach, using coherent states as trial wavefunctions for a triaxial rotor (even-even) or a particle-core system (odd-even), can quantitatively describe wobbling and chiral properties in nuclei, but the harmonic approximation for wobbling frequencies and the subsequent quantization procedure rely on specific parameter choices and the assumption that the system's classical energy surface has a well-defined minimum.

## Method

The authors employed a time-dependent variational principle with coherent states of the SU(2) group for even-even nuclei (triaxial rotor) and SU(2)⊗SU(2) for odd-even nuclei (particle-core system). Complex time-dependent parameters in the coherent states were interpreted as canonical phase-space coordinates, and the classical equations of motion were brought to Hamilton canonical form. A harmonic approximation around the minimum of the classical energy surface yielded analytic wobbling frequencies, which were then used to calculate excitation energies and electromagnetic transition probabilities. A new boson representation for angular momenta was introduced, and the resulting Schrodinger equation was solved via the Bargmann representation, leading to a Lame equation.

## Key numbers

* For 158Er, the fitted moments of inertia were I1 = 125 hbar^2 MeV^-1, I2 = 31.4 hbar^2 MeV^-1, I3 = 42 hbar^2 MeV^-1.
* The calculated B(E2; 2+ -> 0+) value for 158Er was 61.287 W.u., compared to the experimental value of 129 +/- 9 W.u.
* The calculated B(E2; 4+ -> 2+) value for 158Er was 170.243 W.u., compared to the experimental value of 186 +/- 6 W.u.
* The calculated B(E2; 6+ -> 4+) value for 158Er was 212.149 W.u., compared to the experimental value of 246 +/- 8 W.u.
* The calculated B(E2; 8+ -> 6+) value for 158Er was 234.334 W.u., compared to the experimental value of 298 +/- 10 W.u.
* The calculated B(E2; 10+ -> 8+) value for 158Er was 248.068 W.u., compared to the experimental value of 250 +/- 4 W.u.
* The calculated B(E2; 12+ -> 10+) value for 158Er was 257.407 W.u., compared to the experimental value of 260 +/- 3 W.u.
* The nuclear deformation beta for 158Er was taken as 0.203, and the gamma deformation was determined to be approximately 55 degrees.
* The maximal deviation between yrast energies from diagonalization, Schrodinger equation, and the wobbling formula was less than 5 keV for 158Er.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical treatment of wobbling motion in triaxial nuclei previously developed by Raduta and collaborators (Rad07, Rad017, RaPoAl) and builds on the classical rotor model of Bohr and Mottelson (BMott) and the microscopic description by Marshalek (Marsh). The approach was applied to 158Er and the odd-even lutetium isotopes 161,163,165,167Lu, as well as 135Pr, and successfully describes the coexistence of wobbling and chiral motion. The method enables quantitative predictions for excitation energies, electromagnetic transition probabilities, and the identification of nuclear phases in the parameter space, including the existence of transversal wobbling.
