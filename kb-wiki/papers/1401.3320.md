# Proton polarisabilities from Compton data using Covariant Chiral EFT

> We present a fit of the spin-independent electromagnetic polarisabilities of the proton to low-energy Compton scattering data in the framework of covariant baryon chiral effective field theory. Using the Baldin sum rule to constrain their sum, we obtain $α=[10.6\pm0.25$(stat)$\pm0.2$(Baldin)$\pm0.4$(theory)$]\times10^{-4}$fm$^3$ and $β=[3.2\mp0.25$(stat)$\pm0.2$(Baldin)$\mp0.4$(theory)$]\times10^{-4}$fm$^3$, in excellent agreement with other chiral extractions of the same quantities.

## Key claim

Using covariant baryon chiral effective field theory (χEFT) at partial next-to-next-to-leading order (e²δ⁴) to fit low-energy Compton scattering data, the extracted spin-independent electromagnetic polarisabilities of the proton are essentially identical to those obtained in heavy-baryon χEFT, despite the two versions predicting quite different spin polarisabilities.

## Method

The authors performed a fit of the spin-independent electromagnetic polarisabilities αE1 and βM1 to low-energy Compton scattering data below 170 MeV using covariant baryon chiral effective field theory with δ-counting, supplemented by a fourth-order Lagrangian term for the proton. The database included the TAPS dataset (with a 4% point-to-point systematic error), data from Federspiel (1991), Zieger (1992), Hallin (1993), MacGibbon (1995), and older experiments, with normalisation uncertainties incorporated into the χ² function. Fits were performed both with and without imposing the Baldin sum rule constraint αE1+βM1=13.8±0.4.

## Key numbers

- Baldin-constrained fit: αE1 = [10.6 ± 0.25(stat) ± 0.2(Baldin) ± 0.4(theory)] × 10⁻⁴ fm³, βM1 = [3.2 ∓ 0.25(stat) ± 0.2(Baldin) ∓ 0.4(theory)] × 10⁻⁴ fm³, with χ² of 112.5 for 136 degrees of freedom
- Unconstrained fit: αE1 = 10.9 ± 0.45(stat) ± 0.4(theory) × 10⁻⁴ fm³, βM1 = 3.6 ± 0.55(stat) ± 0.4(theory) × 10⁻⁴ fm³, with χ² of 111.8 for 135 degrees of freedom
- Baldin-constrained αE1 − βM1 = 7.4 ± 0.5(stat) ± 0.4(theory) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- The covariant δ³ prediction (no fit) gave αE1 = 10.8 ± 0.7 and βM1 = 4.0 ± 0.7 (theory only)

## Context

This work extends the covariant chiral EFT calculation of Lensky and Pascalutsa (2009) from order e²δ³ to partial order e²δ⁴, and directly compares with the heavy-baryon χEFT extraction of McGovern et al. (2012) which found αE1=10.65±0.35(stat) and βM1=3.2∓0.35(stat) with the Baldin constraint. The results confirm the tension between χEFT extractions and the dispersion-relation-based result of Olmos de León et al. (2001) which gave αE1−βM1=10.5±0.9±0.7, and suggest that more unpolarised data around pion-production threshold at backward angles, or polarised scattering measurements, may be needed to resolve the discrepancy.
