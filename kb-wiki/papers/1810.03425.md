# Wobbling motion in $^{165,167}$Lu within a semi-classical framework

> The results obtained for $^{165,167}$Lu with a semi-classical formalism are presented. Properties like excitation energies for the super-deformed bands TSD1, TSD2, TSD3, in $^{165}$Lu, and TSD1 and TSD2 for $^{167}$Lu, inter- and intra-band B(E2) and B(M1), the mixing ratios, transition quadrupole moments are compared either with the corresponding experimental data or with those obtained for $^{163}$Lu. Also alignments, dynamic moments of inertia, relative energy to a reference energy of a rigid symmetric rotor with an effective moment of inertia and the angle between the angular momenta of the core and odd nucleon were quantitatively studied. One concludes that the semi-classical formalism provides a realistic description of all known wobbling features in $^{165, 167}$Lu.

## Key claim

The semi-classical formalism, which couples a triaxial rotor core to a single high-j particle, provides a realistic description of all known wobbling features in \(^{165,167}\)Lu, but the quantitative agreement depends on fitting two free parameters (\({\cal I}_0\) and \(V\)) and assumes constant moments of inertia, which is not supported by microscopic calculations that include pairing and angular-momentum-dependent effects.

## Method

The authors dequantize a particle-rotor Hamiltonian via a time-dependent variational principle with a trial function that is a product of coherent states for the core and odd-particle angular momenta, obtaining classical equations of motion. Linearizing around the energy minimum yields two coupled harmonic wobbling frequencies \(\Omega_1\) and \(\Omega_2\); the spectrum is then built by assigning phonon quanta to the three super-deformed bands TSD1, TSD2, and TSD3. The two parameters, the rigid-body moment-of-inertia scale \({\cal I}_0\) and the single-particle potential strength \(V\), are fixed by a least-squares fit to the experimental excitation energies of the three bands in \(^{165}\)Lu.

## Key numbers

- Fitted parameters for \(^{165}\)Lu: \({\cal I}_0 = 52.51568\) MeV/\(\hbar^2\) and \(V = 0.195749\) MeV.
- The effective moment of inertia extracted from the fitted parameters is \(46.63\ \hbar^2\ \text{MeV}^{-1}\), which is 1.43 times smaller than the spherical rigid-rotor value of \(66.66\ \hbar^2\ \text{MeV}^{-1}\).
- For \(^{165}\)Lu, the super-deformed minimum is taken at deformation \(\beta = 0.38\) and triaxiality \(\gamma = 20^\circ\).
- The alignment reference uses \({\cal I}_1 = 30\ \hbar^2\ \text{MeV}^{-1}\) and \({\cal I}_3 = 40\ \hbar^4\ \text{MeV}^{-3}\).
- The reference energy for the rigid rotor is \(E_{\text{REF}} = 0.0075\, I(I+1)\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical formalism previously applied to \(^{163}\)Lu (Raduta 2017) to the heavier isotopes \(^{165,167}\)Lu, comparing results with experimental data from Schoengasser (2003) and Amro (2003). The approach contrasts with the Holstein-Primakoff boson expansion used by Tanabe (2017) for transverse wobbling; the authors note that their Dyson-boson-based method yields different wobbling frequencies and level energies. The paper enables future extension to transverse wobbling regimes and to systems where the frozen-alignment hypothesis for the odd particle is relaxed.
