# Meson-Meson Scattering in the Quark Model: Spin Dependence and Exotic Channels

> We apply a quark interchange model to spin-dependent and exotic meson-meson scattering. The model includes the complete set of standard quark model forces, including OGE spin-orbit and tensor and scalar confinement spin-orbit. Scattering amplitudes derived assuming SHO and Coulomb plus linear plus hyperfine meson wavefunctions are compared. In I=2 pi pi we find approximate agreement with the S-wave phase shift from threshold to 1.5 GeV, where we predict an extremum that is supported by the data. Near threshold we find rapid energy dependence that may reconcile theoretical estimates of small scattering lengths with experimental indications of larger ones based on extrapolation of measurements at moderate kpi^2. In PsV scattering we find that the quark-quark L*S and T forces map into L*S and T meson-meson interactions, and the P-wave L*S force is large. Finally we consider scattering in J^PC-exotic channels, and note that some of the Deck effect mechanisms suggested as possible nonresonant origins of the pi_1(1400) signal are not viable in this model.

## Key claim

The quark interchange model, including the complete set of standard quark model forces (OGE spin-orbit, tensor, and scalar confinement spin-orbit), can reproduce I=2 ππ S-wave phase shifts from threshold to 1.5 GeV with approximate agreement, and predicts that some Deck effect mechanisms proposed as nonresonant origins of the π₁(1400) signal are not viable in this model.

## Method

The authors derived meson-meson scattering amplitudes at Born order using quark interchange diagrams (transfer and capture diagrams) with Gaussian (SHO) wavefunctions and compared these to results using Coulomb plus linear plus hyperfine wavefunctions. The quark-quark interaction included color Coulomb, linear scalar confinement, OGE spin-spin, OGE spin-orbit, OGE tensor, and linear confinement spin-orbit forces. Scattering amplitudes were evaluated for pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar (I=2 ππ) and pseudoscalar-vector (πρ) systems, with phase shifts extracted from the T-matrix using standard partial wave projection.

## Key numbers

- I=2 ππ S-wave phase shift shows approximate agreement with experimental data from threshold to 1.5 GeV, with a predicted extremum near M_{ππ}=1.5 GeV supported by Durusoy et al. data
- Total I=2 ππ scattering length a₀^{I=2} = -0.109 fm (with standard parameters α_s=0.6, β=0.4 GeV, m=0.33 GeV, b=0.18 GeV²)
- Individual contributions to scattering length: OGE spin-spin -0.085 fm, color Coulomb -0.007 fm, linear confinement -0.017 fm
- D-wave phase shift at M_{ππ}=1.5 GeV: spin-spin -0.8°, Coulomb +0.2°, linear +0.4° (approximately canceling)
- Experimental scattering lengths: Losty et al. -0.13(2) fm, Prukop et al. -0.24(2) and -0.22{+0.03/-0.04} fm
- Theoretical comparisons: LGT -0.053(7) fm, meson exchange -0.052 fm, Roy Eqs. -0.053 fm, PCAC -0.063 fm
- Hyperfine smearing parameter σ = 0.86 GeV required to fit M_ρ - M_π splitting with standard parameters

## Context

This work extends the quark interchange model previously applied to I=2 ππ scattering by Barnes and Swanson (1990s) and to KN, BB, and NN systems, now incorporating the full spin-dependent quark-quark interaction. The results for I=2 ππ are compared to experimental phase shift analyses by Colton, Durusoy, Hoogland, and Losty, and to theoretical predictions from chiral perturbation theory (Weinberg), LGT, and the Jülich meson-exchange model. The PsV scattering results provide a theoretical framework for future experimental measurement of spin-dependent phase shifts, for example through resonance decays like b₁ → πω, and enable comparison with meson-exchange calculations by Janssen et al. and Böckmann et al. for πρ scattering.
