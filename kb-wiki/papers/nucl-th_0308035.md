# Two-neutrino double beta decay within Fully-Renormalized QRPA: Effect of the restoration of the Ikeda sum rule

> The recently proposed Fully-Renormalized QRPA (FR-QRPA), which fullfils the Ikeda sum rule (ISR) exactly, is applied to the two-neutrino double beta decay of $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{100}$Mo, $^{116}$Cd, $^{128}$Te and $^{130}$Xe. The results obtained are compared with those of other approaches, standard QRPA and self-consistent QRPA (SCQRPA). The similarities and the differences among the methods are discussed. The influence of the restoration of the Ikeda sum rule on the $2νββ$-decay amplitude is analyzed.

# Two-neutrino double beta decay within Fully-Renormalized QRPA: Effect of the restoration of the Ikeda sum rule

## Key claim

The Fully-Renormalized QRPA (FR-QRPA), which exactly fulfills the Ikeda sum rule (ISR), yields two-neutrino double beta decay matrix elements that are considerably less sensitive to the particle-particle interaction strength than standard QRPA or self-consistent QRPA (SCQRPA), but only for relatively small model spaces where full convergence can be achieved; the effect of ISR restoration becomes important only for large values of the particle-particle strength beyond the collapse point of standard QRPA.

## Method

The authors apply the FR-QRPA formalism, which includes quasiparticle scattering terms in the phonon operator to restore the Ikeda sum rule, to calculate the $2\nu\beta\beta$-decay matrix elements for six nuclei: $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{100}$Mo, $^{116}$Cd, $^{128}$Te, and $^{130}$Xe. The Hamiltonian uses a separable Gamow-Teller residual interaction in both particle-hole and particle-particle channels, with single-particle energies from a Coulomb-corrected Woods-Saxon potential and pairing gaps determined phenomenologically from odd-even mass differences. The particle-hole strength is fixed to reproduce the experimental Gamow-Teller giant resonance position from (p,n) reactions, while the particle-particle strength is treated as a free parameter. Results are compared with standard QRPA and SCQRPA calculations using the same small model spaces.

## Key numbers

* Pairing gaps for $^{76}$Ge: $\Delta_p = 1.561$ MeV, $\Delta_n = 1.535$ MeV; for $^{76}$Se: $\Delta_p = 1.751$ MeV, $\Delta_n = 1.710$ MeV
* Pairing gaps for $^{82}$Se: $\Delta_p = 1.401$ MeV, $\Delta_n = 1.544$ MeV; for $^{82}$Kr: $\Delta_p = 1.734$ MeV, $\Delta_n = 1.644$ MeV
* Pairing gaps for $^{100}$Mo: $\Delta_p = 1.612$ MeV, $\Delta_n = 1.358$ MeV; for $^{100}$Ru: $\Delta_p = 1.548$ MeV, $\Delta_n = 1.296$ MeV
* Pairing gaps for $^{116}$Cd: $\Delta_p = 1.493$ MeV, $\Delta_n = 1.377$ MeV; for $^{116}$Sn: $\Delta_p = 1.763$ MeV, $\Delta_n = 1.204$ MeV
* Pairing gaps for $^{128}$Te: $\Delta_p = 1.127$ MeV, $\Delta_n = 1.307$ MeV; for $^{128}$Xe: $\Delta_p = 1.177$ MeV, $\Delta_n = 1.266$ MeV
* Pairing gaps for $^{130}$Te: $\Delta_p = 1.299$ MeV, $\Delta_n = 1.243$ MeV; for $^{130}$Xe: $\Delta_p = 1.043$ MeV, $\Delta_n = 1.180$ MeV
* Particle-hole strength $\chi$: 0.21 MeV ($^{76}$Ge), 0.18 MeV ($^{82}$Se), 0.17 MeV ($^{100}$Mo), 0.14 MeV ($^{116}$Cd and $^{128}$Te), 0.12 MeV ($^{130}$Te)
* The Ikeda sum rule $S^{(-)} - S^{(+)} = 3(N-Z)$ is exactly fulfilled within FR-QRPA, while SCQRPA violates it by up to 3% (previous RQRPA violations were up to 17% as cited from Stoica and Klapdor-Kleingrothaus (2001))

## Context

This work extends the FR-QRPA formalism developed by Rodin and Faessler (2002) to numerical calculations of $2\nu\beta\beta$-decay, addressing the long-standing problem that standard RQRPA and SCQRPA violate the model-independent Ikeda sum rule, as documented by Toivanen and Suhonen (1997) and Stoica and Klapdor-Kleingrothaus (2001). The FR-QRPA approach modifies the phonon operator by including scattering terms without introducing additional degrees of freedom, thereby avoiding spurious low-energy solutions that plagued earlier attempts by Raduta et al. (1998). The results enable future work with larger model spaces and more realistic interactions, provided a proper ansatz for the RPA ground state can be developed to overcome the current convergence limitations.
