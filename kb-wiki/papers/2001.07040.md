# How to renormalize integral equations with singular potentials in effective field theory

> We briefly review general concepts of renormalization in quantum field theory and discuss their application to solutions of integral equations with singular potentials in the few-nucleon sector of the low-energy effective field theory of QCD. We also describe a particular subtractive renormalization scheme and consider a specific application to a toy-model with a singular potential serving as its effective field theoretical leading-order approximation.

# How to renormalize integral equations with singular potentials in effective field theory

## Key claim

The authors argue that chiral effective field theory in the few-nucleon sector does not require any new non-perturbative renormalization beyond what is already well-established in quantum field theory; the practical difficulty lies in the technically complicated implementation of standard QFT renormalization, not in conceptual gaps. The paper demonstrates a specific subtractive renormalization scheme for integral equations with singular potentials, but notes that the scheme is chosen for demonstration purposes rather than being optimal for realistic nucleon-nucleon scattering.

## Method

The authors split the effective potential into a singular part (with polynomial dependence on quark masses) and a regular part, then apply BPHZ subtractive renormalization to the singular part by expanding loop integrals around a fixed energy E = -E_mu and subtracting the first N+1 terms. The renormalized amplitude is obtained by solving a modified Lippmann-Schwinger equation where the singular potential is replaced by an effective potential that incorporates all necessary counterterms. The scheme is applied to a toy model with an underlying "fundamental" potential that behaves as -alpha exp(-M r)/r^3 at large distances, with the leading-order EFT consisting of a delta potential plus this long-range singular part.

## Key numbers

- The toy model parameters are alpha = -36 GeV^{-2}, M = 0.1385 GeV, m_1 = 0.75 GeV, and m_2 = 1.15 GeV.
- The coupling constant alpha = -36 GeV^{-2} is approximately -1/(0.167 GeV)^2, chosen so the LO potential is non-perturbative for momenta k ~ M = 0.1385 GeV.
- The low-energy EFT of strong interactions has a breakdown scale of about 500 MeV, relative to which small masses and momenta are defined.
- For the toy model with alpha = -36, the LO phase shifts are shown for subtraction scales mu and mu_1 varied over ranges including mu_1 = 600 MeV, with results sensitive to scale choices for momenta k greater than or approximately equal to M.
- Additional cases considered include alpha = 36 (attractive long-range potential) and alpha = +/- 72 (doubled strength), with mu_1 = 600 MeV shown as a representative hard-scale choice.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous discussion (Epelbaum et al., 2018) of common misconceptions in renormalization of few-body chiral EFT, shifting focus from what should not be done to what should be done. The subtractive scheme generalizes earlier approaches by Weinberg (1990) and Gegelia (1998) to the case of one-pion-exchange potentials, and differs from schemes by Frederico et al. (1999), Timoteo et al. (2005, 2010), and Yang et al. (2007, 2009) which the authors argue either have unclear realization in terms of local counterterms or impose scheme-independence requirements inconsistent with finite-order perturbative calculations. The paper enables practical implementation of proper QFT renormalization for integral equations with singular potentials, though the authors note that for realistic nucleon-nucleon scattering, subtraction schemes with two scales are preferable, as discussed in their earlier work (Epelbaum et al., 2015, 2017).
