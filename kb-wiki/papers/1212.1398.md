# Finite Range Effects in Energies and Recombination Rates of Three Identical Bosons

> We investigate finite-range effects in systems with three identical bosons. We calculate recombination rates and bound state spectra using two different finite-range models that have been used recently to describe the physics of cold atomic gases near Feshbach resonances where the scattering length is large. The models are built on contact potentials which take into account finite range effects; one is a two-channel model and the other is an effective range expansion model implemented through the boundary condition on the three-body wave function when two of the particles are at the same point in space. We compare the results with the results of the ubiquitous single-parameter zero-range model where only the scattering length is taken into account. Both finite range models predict variations of the well-known geometric scaling factor 22.7 that arises in Efimov physics. The threshold value at negative scattering length for creation of a bound trimer moves to higher or lower values depending on the sign of the effective range compared to the location of the threshold for the single-parameter zero-range model. Large effective ranges, corresponding to narrow resonances, are needed for the reduction to have any noticeable effect.

## Key claim

Both finite-range models (effective range expansion and two-channel) predict variations of the well-known geometric scaling factor 22.7 that arises in Efimov physics, but large effective ranges corresponding to narrow resonances are needed for the reduction to have any noticeable effect; the threshold value at negative scattering length for creation of a bound trimer moves to higher or lower values depending on the sign of the effective range compared to the zero-range model.

## Method

The authors used the hyperspherical adiabatic approach to solve the three-body problem for three identical bosons, implementing two different finite-range contact interaction models: an effective range expansion model using a modified Bethe-Peierls boundary condition, and a two-channel model with open and closed channels coupled by a contact potential. Recombination rates for positive scattering length were calculated using the WKB method with hidden crossings, while bound trimer spectra were computed for both positive and negative scattering lengths by solving the hyperradial equation with the adiabatic potentials.

## Key numbers

- The geometric scaling factor 22.7 from the zero-range model is reduced in both finite-range models; for the two-channel model with $R_0 = -10 a_{bg}$ the ratio of minima positions $a_2^*/a_1^*$ decreases significantly from 22.7
- The branchpoint in the single-channel model is located at $\rho_b = (1.8327 + 2.1029i)a$
- The effective range at resonance is given by $R_0 = -\frac{1}{a_{bg}}\frac{\hbar^2}{m_r \Delta\mu \Delta B}$
- The effective range as a function of scattering length follows $R(a) = R_0\left(1 - \frac{a_{bg}}{a}\right)^2$
- The dimer binding energy with effective range corrections is $E_D \approx -\frac{1}{a^2}\left(1 + \frac{R}{a}\right)$ for $|R| \ll a$
- The hyperangular eigenvalue at $\rho/a = 0$ is $\nu = i s_0$ with $s_0 = 1.00624$
- The experimentally found threshold satisfies $a^{(-)} \sim -9.8 r_\textrm{vdW}$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Efimov physics in ultracold atomic gases (Efimov 1970, Nielsen 2001, Braaten 2006) by systematically comparing two finite-range contact models to the standard zero-range approximation, building on earlier finite-range studies by Fedorov and Jensen (2001), Kokkelmans (2002), Jonsell (2004), and Petrov (2004). The results enable quantitative assessment of when zero-range models are reliable for describing three-body recombination and bound state spectra near narrow Feshbach resonances, and connect to recent experimental findings by Berninger (2011) showing that the three-body threshold is determined by the van der Waals length scale.
