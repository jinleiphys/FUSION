# Structure functions at small x from world-lines. I: Unpolarized distributions

> The world-line representation of quantum field theory is a powerful framework for the computation of perturbative multi-leg Feynman amplitudes. In particular, in gauge theories, it provides an efficient way, via point particle Grassmann functional integrals, to compute spinor and color traces in these amplitudes. Further, semi-classical approximations to quantum mechanical world-line trajectories provide useful intuition in a wide range of dynamical problems. We develop here the world-line approach to compute deeply inelastic structure functions in the small x Regge limit of QCD. In particular, in a shockwave approximation valid in this limit, we show how one recovers the well-known dipole model for unpolarized structure functions. In a follow-up work, we will discuss the world-line computation of polarized structure functions at small x.

## Key claim

The authors develop a world-line formalism for computing deeply inelastic scattering structure functions in the small \(x\) Regge limit of QCD, showing that the well-known dipole model for unpolarized structure functions is recovered in a shockwave approximation valid in this limit; the derivation is a first demonstration of the world-line approach's power at small \(x\), with a follow-up work planned for polarized structure functions.

## Method

The authors reformulate the QCD effective action as a one-dimensional functional integral over bosonic (coordinate) and fermionic (Grassmann) world-line trajectories, representing the quark loop in a background gluon field. They then apply this formalism to the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) shockwave background, where the gauge field has infinitesimal support in the \(x^-\) direction, and compute the hadron tensor via the second functional derivative of the effective action with respect to the electromagnetic current. The computation uses world-line propagators for bosonic and fermionic degrees of freedom on a closed loop of proper length \(T\), with periodic and antiperiodic boundary conditions respectively, to recover the dipole model expression for unpolarized structure functions.

## Key numbers

* The shockwave background field solution is given by \(A_{\rm cl}^+(x) = -\frac{1}{\partial^2_\perp} \rho(x_\perp)\delta(x^-)\), with \(A_{\rm cl}^- = A_{\rm cl}^i = 0\), where the delta function represents the infinitesimal support of order \(1/P^+\) in the \(x^-\) direction.
* The free world-line functional integrals evaluate to \(\int \mathcal{D} x \exp\{-\int_0^T d\tau \frac{1}{4} \dot{x}^2\} = (4\pi T)^{-D/2}\) and \(\int \mathcal{D}\psi \exp\{-\int_0^T d\tau \frac{1}{2}\psi\dot{\psi}\} = 4\), where \(D\) is the number of spacetime dimensions.
* The bosonic world-line propagator is \(G_B(\tau, \tau') = |\tau - \tau'| - (\tau - \tau')^2/T\) and the fermionic propagator is \(G_F(\tau, \tau') = {\rm sign}(\tau - \tau')\), both on a circle of circumference \(T\).

## Context

This work extends the world-line formalism, previously applied to perturbative multi-leg amplitudes and soft gluon resummation (Strassler 1992, Bern, Dixon, Dunbar, and Kosower 1994), to the small \(x\) Regge limit of QCD. It provides a first-principles derivation of the dipole model for unpolarized structure functions (Nikolaev and Mueller 1990, Mueller 1989) within the CGC effective field theory framework (McLerran, Venugopalan 1993-1994, Iancu 2003, Gelis 2010). The approach enables future computation of polarized structure functions and the polarized dipole model (planned in paper II), and may allow quantum computation of structure functions in non-perturbative background fields.
