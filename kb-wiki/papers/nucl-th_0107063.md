# A Theoretical Study of $ρ^{0}$-Photoproduction on Nucleons near Threshold

> We investigate the possibility that the process of $ρ^{0}$-meson photoproduction on proton, $γ+p\to p+ρ^{0}$, in the near threshold region $E_γ< 2$ GeV, can be considered in the framework of model with $π$-, $σ$- and N-exchanges. This suggestion is based on a study of the t-dependence of differential cross section, $dσ(γp \to p ρ^{0})/dt$, which has been measured by SAPHIR Collaboration. We find that the suggested model provides a good description of the experimental data with new values of $ρNN$-coupling constants in the region of the time-like $ρ^{0}$-meson momentum. Our results suggest that such model can be considered as a suitable nonresonant background mechanism for the future discussion of possible role of nucleon resonance contributions. Our predictions for $ρ^{0}$-meson photoproduction on neutron target and for beam asymmetry on both proton and neutron targets are presented.

# A Theoretical Study of ρ⁰-Photoproduction on Nucleons near Threshold

## Key claim

The paper suggests that a simple model with π-, σ-, and nucleon-exchange mechanisms can provide a good description of the SAPHIR differential cross section data for γ+p → p+ρ⁰ in the near-threshold region (Eγ < 2 GeV), but the authors explicitly state that the available data are not discriminative enough to prefer this model over other possible approaches, and that two different sets of coupling constants produce comparably good fits.

## Method

The authors construct a model for the nonresonant background of ρ⁰ photoproduction on nucleons using t-channel π and σ exchanges and s- and u-channel nucleon exchanges, with phenomenological form factors for the t-channel exchanges and no form factors for the nucleon-exchange amplitudes to preserve gauge invariance. They fit the three adjustable coupling constants (gρNNV, gρNNT, and gρσγ) to the SAPHIR Collaboration's differential cross section data dσ/dt for γ+p → p+ρ⁰ at three energy intervals between 1.45 and 2.03 GeV, while fixing the cut-off parameters and the σNN coupling constant to values from the literature. They then predict the differential cross sections for three lower energy intervals not used in the fit, and calculate predictions for beam asymmetry on proton and neutron targets and for the cross section ratio on neutron versus proton targets.

## Key numbers

- The fit yields two sets of coupling constants: Set 1 with gρNNV = 0.4, gρNNT = 1.0, and fixed gρσγ = 2.7; Set 2 with gρNNV = 1.0, gρNNT = -1.2, and gρσγ = -3.0.
- The χ²/ndf values are 2.1 for the second set and 2.5 for the first set.
- The standard values from NN-potential are gρNNV ranging from 2.97 to 3.16 and gρNNT ranging from 12.5 to 20.8, showing the fitted values are much smaller.
- The cut-off parameters used are Λπ = 0.7 GeV, Λρπγ = 0.77 GeV, Λσ = 1.0 GeV, and Λρσγ = 0.9 GeV.
- The σ-meson mass is fixed at mσ = 500 MeV, from an allowed interval of 400 to 1200 MeV.
- The coupling constants gπNN²/4π = 14.0 and gρπγ = 0.54 are taken as well-known from the ρ⁰ radiative decay width.

## Context

This work extends the line of nonresonant background models for vector meson photoproduction, specifically contrasting with the simpler (π+σ)-exchange model of Ref. R5 (which produces no π-σ interference and trivial polarization phenomena) and with models including Pomeron exchange such as Ref. R3. The authors argue that their (π+σ+N) model is more suitable near threshold than approaches using Pomeron exchange, which is typically applied at higher energies. The work is motivated by the search for missing nucleon resonances at JLAB, where a realistic nonresonant background model is needed to isolate resonance contributions; the authors note that the missing resonance problem requires extensive polarization data for a unique multipole analysis.
