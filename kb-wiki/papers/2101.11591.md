# QCD Equation of State at Finite Chemical Potentials for Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> We review the equation of state of QCD matter at finite densities. We discuss the construction of the equation of state with net baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness using the results of lattice QCD simulations and hadron resonance gas models. Its application to the hydrodynamic analyses of relativistic nuclear collisions suggests that the interplay of multiple conserved charges is important in the quantitative understanding of the dense nuclear matter created at lower beam energies. Several different models of the QCD equation of state are discussed for comparison.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the construction of a hybrid QCD equation of state at finite chemical potentials for net baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness, arguing that the interplay of multiple conserved charges is important for quantitative hydrodynamic modeling of relativistic nuclear collisions at lower beam energies, though the construction relies on a crossover-type equation of state by design and does not include a critical point.

## Method

The authors construct the NEOS equation of state by connecting a Taylor-expanded lattice QCD pressure (using second- and fourth-order diagonal and off-diagonal susceptibilities at vanishing chemical potentials) with a hadron resonance gas model pressure at lower temperatures, using a smooth hyperbolic connecting function with a temperature- and baryon-chemical-potential-dependent crossover temperature. Three variants are implemented: NEOS B (only net baryon number conserved), NEOS BS (strangeness neutrality with zero electric charge chemical potential), and NEOS BQS (strangeness neutrality with electric charge density fixed to 0.4 times the net baryon density, reflecting the proton-to-nucleon ratio of heavy nuclei). The equation of state is then applied in hydrodynamic simulations to compare with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The connecting temperature is parameterized as \(T_c(\mu_B) = 0.16\ \mathrm{GeV} - 0.4\,(0.139\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1} \mu_B^2 + 0.053\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-3} \mu_B^4)\), motivated by the chemical freeze-out line.
- The connecting width is \(\Delta T_c = 0.1\,T_c(0)\).
- Constant entropy-density-to-net-baryon-density ratios \(s/n_B = 420, 144, 51, 30\) correspond to center-of-mass energies \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200, 62.4, 19.6, 14.5\ \mathrm{GeV}\), respectively.
- The hadron resonance gas model includes all hadrons and resonances with masses smaller than 2 GeV from the Particle Data Group list.
- The proton-to-nucleon ratio for gold (Au) is \(Z/A = 0.401\) and for lead (Pb) is \(Z/A = 0.394\).

## Context

This work extends earlier finite-density hybrid equations of state such as s95p-v1 (Huovinen 2011) and earlier NEOS versions that included only net baryon chemical potential (Monnai 2015), by incorporating electric charge and strangeness as conserved charges alongside net baryon number. It enables baseline hydrodynamic calculations without critical behavior for the Beam Energy Scan programs at RHIC, FAIR, NICA, and J-PARC, against which future experimental deviations could be used to deduce the existence and location of the QCD critical point. The approach is similar to that of Noronha-Hostler (2019) and builds on lattice QCD results from Bazavov (2014, 2017) and Ding (2015).
