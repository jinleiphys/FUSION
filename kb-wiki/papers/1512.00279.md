# Particle production in high-energy collisions beyond the shockwave limit

> We compute next to eikonal (NE) and next to next to eikonal (NNE) corrections to the Lipatov vertex due to a finite target thickness. These arise from electric field insertions into the eikonal Wilson lines. We then derive a $k_T$-factorization formula for single inclusive gluon production at NNE accuracy and find that nuclear effects are absent. We also analyze NNE corrections to two-gluon production where some of the contributions are found to exhibit corrections proportional to $A^{2/3}$.

## Key claim

Next-to-eikonal (NE) and next-to-next-to-eikonal (NNE) corrections to the Lipatov vertex arise from the finite thickness of the target, proportional to powers of the target thickness and hence to A^{1/3}; however, for single inclusive gluon production these sub-eikonal corrections cancel when using a local (in longitudinal coordinate) color charge correlator, while for two-gluon production the NNE corrections do not cancel and are proportional to A^{2/3}.

## Method

The authors compute NE and NNE corrections to the Lipatov vertex by expanding Wilson lines with electric field insertions to linear order in the target color charge density, using the formalism of Altinoluk (2014, 2015). They then derive k_T-factorization formulas for single and double inclusive gluon production, averaging over color charge densities with the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model and a generalization with finite color correlation length lambda^+.

## Key numbers

- The NNE correction to the Lipatov vertex is proportional to (p^2 * z_2^+ / p^+)^2, where z_2^+ is the longitudinal coordinate and p^+ the light-cone momentum of the produced gluon.
- The single inclusive gluon cross section at NNE accuracy contains a correction factor [1 - (1/6)(p^2 * lambda^+ / (2p^+))^2], where lambda^+ is the color correlation length in the target.
- The two-gluon production cross section contains NNE corrections proportional to (ell^+)^2, where ell^+ ~ A^{1/3} is the target thickness, with a factor [1 - (1/12)(p^2/(2p^+) - q^2/(2q^+))^2 * ell^+^2] for Type B diagrams and [1 - (1/12)(p^2/(2p^+) + q^2/(2q^+))^2 * ell^+^2] for Type C diagrams.
- The NNE correction to two-gluon production is proportional to A^{2/3}.

## Context

This work extends the shockwave (eikonal) approximation for particle production in high-energy nuclear collisions, which was formulated by Kovner (1995), Kovchegov (1997), and Blaizot (2004). It goes beyond the eikonal limit by accounting for the finite thickness of the target, relevant for heavy-ion targets where ell^+ ~ A^{1/3}. The results enable studies of two-particle azimuthal correlations at finite energies, where corrections proportional to the target thickness may affect angular correlations when ell^+ * p^2 / p^+ is not very small. The work contrasts with kinematic finite-energy corrections not proportional to target thickness derived by Babansky and Balitsky (2002), and with finite-x evolution corrections to unintegrated gluon distributions derived by Balitsky (2015).
