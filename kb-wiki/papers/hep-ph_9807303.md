# Hadronization with a confining equation of state

> We present a fast hadronization model for the constituent quark plasma (CQP) produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions at SPS. The model is based on rate equations and on a confining equation of state inspired by the string phenomenology. We display the time evolution of the relevant physical quantities during the hadronization process and the final hadron multiplicities. The results indicate that the hadronization of CQP is fast.

## Key claim

The paper presents a hadronization model for a constituent quark plasma (CQP) that uses rate equations and a confining equation of state inspired by string phenomenology, claiming that hadronization is fast. The hedge is that the model requires both a confining equation of state and a dynamic confinement mechanism (relating screening length to color density) to achieve complete color confinement within a short timescale, and that the results depend on specific parameter choices such as an effective diquark color string tension of q_d = 1.5.

## Method

The authors model hadronization of a massive constituent quark plasma (CQP) in thermal but not chemical equilibrium using a set of 41 coupled rate equations plus 2 equations for temperature and volume evolution. The model incorporates a non-ideal equation of state where colored particles (quarks, diquarks, anti-quarks, anti-diquarks) receive an additional free energy contribution from strings proportional to σ_s n_c^{-1/3} Q, with string tension σ_s ≈ 1.0 GeV/fm, and a dynamic confinement mechanism where the screening length ρ(t) scales as (N_c(0)/N_c(t))^{1/3}. The reaction network includes quark-quark fusion to diquarks, quark-anti-quark fusion to mesons, and quark-diquark fusion to baryons, with thermally averaged rates computed from a Coulomb potential cross section with running coupling α(p).

## Key numbers

- Initial volume V_0 ≈ 431 fm^3 and initial internal energy E_0 ≈ 2.13 TeV for a central Pb-Pb collision at 158 GeV/nucleon (√s = 3.4 TeV, η_0 ≈ 1.75, R_0 ≈ 7 fm, τ_0 ≈ 0.8 fm)
- Initial temperature T_0 = 0.18 GeV, derived from initial quark numbers N_u(0) = 544, N_d(0) = 626, plus 400 u-anti-u, 400 d-anti-d, and 168 s-anti-s pairs (strangeness fraction f_s = 0.21)
- Effective string tension σ_s ≈ 1.0 GeV/fm; running coupling α_0 ≈ 1.4; screening length ρ = 0.2 fm
- Hadronization completes at 2 fm/c after the beginning of the process
- Final hadron multiplicities from the Transchemistry model: h^- = 677.0, π^+ = 581.7, π^0 = 617.0, π^- = 613.1, K^+ = 79.58, K^0 = 79.58, anti-K^0 = 39.47, K^- = 39.47, p^+ = 158.7, n^0 = 175.8, Σ^+ = 8.38, Σ^0 = 9.79, Σ^- = 9.79 (for Pb+Pb at SPS)
- Quark masses: m_u = m_d = 0.15 GeV (thermal mass), m_s = 0.212 GeV (from m_s0 = 0.15 GeV plus thermal mass 0.15 GeV)

## Context

This work extends the ALCOR model (Biro, Levai, Zimanyi, 1995) by replacing its algebraic approximation with a full time-dependent rate equation approach and by incorporating a confining equation of state motivated by string phenomenology, which the authors state had not been discussed in the literature previously. The model addresses the problem that purely hadronic interpretations of SPS data (Stachel, 1996) have difficulty explaining the short hadronization time scale (1-2 fm/c), and that slow first-order phase transitions through near-equilibrium states would require up to 50 fm/c due to re-heating (Csernai and Kapusta, 1986). The results enable comparison with experimental data from NA49 and with other models such as RQMD, and suggest that a constituent quark plasma with string-like mean field interactions may be formed in Pb-Pb collisions at SPS.
