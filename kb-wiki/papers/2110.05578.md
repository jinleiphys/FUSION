# Anisotropic flow decorrelation in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC-BES energies with 3D event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics

> In the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, gold nuclei are collided with different collision energies in the range from few to 62.4 GeV. The goals of the program are to explore the onset of QGP creation, locate the critical point of QCD and study dense baryon matter. We report on the first application of Monte Carlo Glauber (GLISSANDO2) and T$_{\rm R}$ENTo $p=0$ initial states extended to 3D for event-by-event viscous fluid dynamic (vHLLE) with hadronic cascade modelling of Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=27$ and 62.4 GeV, which is the upper region of RHIC BES energies. The initial states are extended into both the longitudinal direction and for finite baryon density using simple ansätze. The full energy and baryon charge counting in the initial states is implemented. We show the reproduction of elliptic flow, at both collision energies and with both initial states. We compare it also to the results obtained with UrQMD initial state. Furthermore, we show the results for rapidity decorrelation of elliptic flow $r_2$ at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=27$ and 200 GeV from the same setup of hydrodynamic calculations with the 3D Monte Carlo Glauber and UrQMD initial states. We discuss the features of the initial states responsible for the magnitude of the observed flow decorrelation.

## Key claim
The paper reports the first application of 3D event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic modeling with Monte Carlo Glauber (GLISSANDO2) and T$_{\rm R}$ENTo $p=0$ initial states extended to longitudinal direction and finite baryon density for Au+Au collisions at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies, but the reproduction of elliptic flow data is limited to $p_T<1$ GeV and the model with T$_{\rm R}$ENTo initial state provides the best description only at midrapidity.

## Method
The authors used a hybrid event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic model (vHLLE) with shear viscosity, coupled to Monte Carlo hadron sampling via the Cooper-Frye formula and subsequent hadronic cascade (UrQMD). Three initial state models were employed: UrQMD (with PYTHIA6 string formation), GLISSANDO2 (Monte Carlo Glauber), and T$_{\rm R}$ENTo $p=0$; the latter two were extended to 3D with longitudinal structure and fixed total energy and baryon charge to participants. Elliptic flow was calculated using both the event plane method and 2-particle cumulant method, and longitudinal decorrelation was quantified via the factorization ratio $r_2$ and its decomposition into flow magnitude and flow angle components.

## Key numbers
- Elliptic flow $v_2$ and triangular flow $v_3$ as functions of centrality were calculated for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=27$ and 62.4 GeV, with T$_{\rm R}$ENTo initial state showing best agreement with STAR data for $v_2$ in non-central collisions at 27 GeV and all centralities at 62.4 GeV.
- $p_T$-dependent elliptic flow calculations agree with STAR data only for $p_T<1$ GeV; at larger $p_T$, all initial states under-predict the data.
- The factorization ratio $r_2$ for elliptic flow decorrelation was computed at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=27$ and 200 GeV for 10-40% centrality; GLISSANDO initial state describes the experimental data within uncertainties at both energies, while UrQMD initial state shows much stronger decorrelation than data at 27 GeV.
- At 27 GeV, the flow angle decorrelation $r_2^\Psi$ plays a more important role than the flow magnitude decorrelation $r_2^v$, consistent with observations at LHC energies by Bozek (2017).

## Context
This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies by incorporating 3D initial state fluctuations and finite baryon density, which were absent in earlier calculations. The results are compared to preliminary STAR data on flow decorrelation (Nie, 2019; Nie, 2020) and to prior calculations at LHC energies by Bozek (2017). The finding that longitudinal decorrelation originates primarily from initial-state eccentricity decorrelation enables future studies to constrain initial state models using flow decorrelation measurements at lower collision energies.
