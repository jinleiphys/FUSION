# Chiral expansion of moments of quark distributions

> We present formulae for the chiral extrapolation of spin-dependent and spin-independent moments of quark distributions of octet baryons, including loop corrections and counterterms to leading non-analytic order. This analysis allows for isospin breaking, and may be used for the chiral extrapolation of both (2+1)- and (1+1+1)-flavor lattice QCD results. An example of such an application is given, with the extrapolation formulae applied to recent (2 + 1)-flavor QCDSF/UKQCD Collaboration lattice results for the first spin-independent and first two spin-dependent Mellin moments.

## Key claim

The authors present chiral extrapolation formulae for spin-dependent and spin-independent moments of quark distributions of octet baryons, including loop corrections and counterterms to leading non-analytic order, with the specific extension of allowing for isospin breaking (i.e., \(m_u \ne m_d\)) so that the results may be applied to both (2+1)- and (1+1+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations.

## Method

The authors constructed effective operators within heavy-baryon SU(3) chiral perturbation theory, matching the twist-2 quark bilinear operators onto hadronic analogues that transform as (8,1) ⊕ (1,8) under SU(3)_L × SU(3)_R. They included octet and decuplet baryon intermediate states, loop diagrams, tadpole contributions, and wavefunction renormalization, evaluated using finite-range regularization (FRR) with a regulator \(u(k)\) and a mass scale \(\Lambda\). The loop integrals were expressed in terms of subtracted integrals \(\widetilde{J}(m^2)\), \(\widetilde{J}_1(m^2,\delta)\), \(\widetilde{J}_2(m^2,\delta)\), and \(\widetilde{J}_T(m^2)\) to remove analytic pieces, and the formalism was developed for all spin-independent and spin-dependent Mellin moments of octet baryons.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant in the chiral limit is taken as \(f_{\text{chiral}} = 0.0871\) GeV, from Amoros (2001).
- The zeroth spin-dependent moments are related to meson-baryon couplings by \(\Delta \alpha^{(0)} = 2\left(\frac{2}{3}D+2F\right)\) and \(\Delta \beta^{(0)} = 2\left(-\frac{5}{3}D+F\right)\), where \(D\) and \(F\) are the usual SU(3) axial couplings.
- The decuplet operator insertion for the zeroth spin-dependent moment uses the SU(6) relation \(\Delta\gamma^{(0)} = 2\mathcal{H} = -6D\).
- The transition operator insertion for the zeroth spin-dependent moment is \(\omega^{(0)} = \mathcal{C}\), the same parameter appearing in the strong-interaction Lagrangian.

## Context

This work extends previous chiral extrapolation developments for nucleon PDF moments (Chen 2001, Arndt 2001, Diehl 2006, Dorati 2007, Detmold 2005) by generalizing to all octet baryons and explicitly including isospin breaking. It builds on the SU(3) flavor-symmetry-breaking expansions of Cooke (2012) and the heavy-baryon formalism of Jenkins (1990, 1991). The results enable chiral extrapolation of lattice QCD results for both (2+1)- and (1+1+1)-flavor simulations, and the authors illustrate this by applying the formulae to recent QCDSF/UKQCD Collaboration lattice data for the first spin-independent and first two spin-dependent Mellin moments.
