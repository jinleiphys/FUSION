# Transition from BCS pairing to Bose-Einstein condensation in low-density asymmetric nuclear matter

> We study the isospin-singlet neutron-proton pairing in bulk nuclear matter as a function of density and isospin asymmetry within the BCS formalism. In the high-density, weak-coupling regime the neutron-proton paired state is strongly suppressed by a minor neutron excess. As the system is diluted, the BCS state with large, overlapping Cooper pairs evolves smoothly into a Bose-Einstein condensate of tightly bound neutron-proton pairs (deuterons). In the resulting low-density system a neutron excess is ineffective in quenching the pair correlations because of the large spatial separation of the deuterons and neutrons. As a result, the Bose-Einstein condensation of deuterons is weakly affected by an additional gas of free neutrons even at very large asymmetries.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in low-density asymmetric nuclear matter, the suppression of neutron-proton pairing by a neutron excess becomes ineffective because the large spatial separation of deuterons and free neutrons renders Pauli blocking negligible, allowing Bose-Einstein condensation of deuterons to persist even at very large asymmetries, contrary to the high-density regime where even a minor neutron excess rapidly destroys pairing.

## Method

The authors solve the zero-temperature BCS gap equation for isospin-singlet neutron-proton pairing in bulk nuclear matter, using the Argonne V14 bare interaction in the \(^3SD_1\) channel. They treat the system as a superfluid of neutron-proton Cooper pairs coexisting with a gas of excess neutrons, with the single-particle spectrum taken as kinetic energy only (no mean-field renormalization). The coupled equations for the gap, total density, and neutron excess are solved numerically across a range of densities and asymmetries, and analytical limiting cases are derived for the weak-coupling (high-density) and strong-coupling (low-density) regimes.

## Key numbers

- The Mott transition (where chemical potential passes through zero) occurs at a density of approximately \(\rho \approx \rho_0/100\), where \(\rho_0 \approx 0.17\;\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) is nuclear matter saturation density.
- In pure neutron matter, the chemical potential becomes negative at \(\mu_n = (\pi/2)^2 \mu_D\), corresponding to a density \(\rho \approx 0.0016\;\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), where \(\mu_D = E_D/2 \approx 1.1\;\mathrm{MeV}\) is half the deuteron binding energy.
- In the low-density limit, the gap follows \(\Delta/\mu_D(\rho,\alpha) = \sqrt{\rho(1-\alpha)/\rho_\mu}\) with \(\rho_\mu \equiv (2m\mu_D)^{3/2}/(8\pi) \approx 0.00049\;\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\).
- In the high-density regime, the maximum asymmetry for superfluidity is \(\alpha_{\mathrm{max}} = 3\Delta_0/(4\mu)\), which is a very small value in the weak-coupling limit.
- The gap vanishes in the high-density regime when the width of the blocking window reaches \(2\delta\epsilon = \Delta_0\), the gap value at symmetry.
- In the low-density limit, the chemical potentials asymptotically approach \(\mu_n \to 0\), \(\mu_p \to -E_D\), \(\mu \to -E_D/2\), \(\delta\mu \to E_D/2\), independent of asymmetry.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the BCS-to-BEC crossover in symmetric nuclear matter (Alm et al., 2000; Schuck et al., 2000) to the isospin-asymmetric case, and builds on the blocked gap equation formalism from nuclear structure (Ring and Schuck, 1980). It contrasts with the behavior of spin-polarized electron systems in a magnetic field, where the analogous asymmetry leads to a first-order phase transition rather than the smooth suppression seen here. The results are relevant for low-density nuclear systems such as the surface of nuclei, expanding nuclear matter from heavy ion collisions, and collapsing stars, and enable future studies of the competition between isospin-singlet and isospin-triplet pairing in asymmetric matter.
