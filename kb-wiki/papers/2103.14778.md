# Generation of fragment angular momentum in fission

> A recent analysis of experimental data [J. Wilson $et. al$, Nature $\mathbf 590$, 566 (2021)] found that the angular momenta of nuclear fission fragments are uncorrelated. Based on this finding, the authors concluded that the spins are therefore determined only $after$ scission has occurred. We show here that the nucleon-exchange mechanism, as implemented in the well-established event-by-event fission model $\mathtt{FREYA}$, while agitating collective rotational modes in which the two spins are highly correlated, nevertheless leads to fragment spins that are largely uncorrelated. This fact invalidates the reasoning of those authors. Furthermore, it was reported [J. Wilson $et. al$, Nature $\mathbf 590$, 566 (2021)] that the mass dependence of the average fragment spin has a sawtooth structure. We demonstrate that such a behavior naturally emerges when shell and deformation effects are included in the moments of inertia of the fragments at scission.

## Key claim

The authors show that the nucleon-exchange mechanism, as implemented in the event-by-event fission model FREYA, produces fragment spins that are largely uncorrelated even though the underlying collective rotational modes (wriggling and bending) are highly correlated. This invalidates the reasoning of Wilson et al. (2021) that the absence of spin correlation proves spins are determined only after scission.

## Method

The authors use the FREYA Monte Carlo fission model, which implements the nucleon-exchange transport mechanism by assuming full relaxation of the transverse dinuclear rotational modes (wriggling and bending) while the coaxial modes (twisting and tilting) are not agitated. The fragment spins are sampled from thermal distributions of these normal modes, with the amplitudes determined by the mode-specific moments of inertia and the scission temperature. To reproduce the sawtooth mass dependence of average spin, the authors modify the standard rigid-body moments of inertia using microscopic results on fragment equilibrium shapes, reducing the moment of inertia near the doubly magic nucleus 132Sn and increasing it for deformed fragments.

## Key numbers

- For 235U(n,f), the average spin magnitude of the light fragment is 4.27 hbar and of the heavy fragment is 5.66 hbar, with a spin magnitude correlation coefficient of 0.2%
- For 238U(n,f), the average spin magnitude of the light fragment is 4.43 hbar and of the heavy fragment is 5.80 hbar, with a spin magnitude correlation coefficient of 0.2%
- For 239Pu(n,f), the average spin magnitude of the light fragment is 4.58 hbar and of the heavy fragment is 5.93 hbar, with a spin magnitude correlation coefficient of 0.1%
- For 252Cf(sf), the average spin magnitude of the light fragment is 5.08 hbar and of the heavy fragment is 6.33 hbar, with a spin magnitude correlation coefficient of 0.1%
- The directional correlation amplitude f1 in the distribution of spin opening angles ranges from -8.2% to -8.4% for the four fission cases studied
- The standard FREYA model uses a global reduction factor of 0.5 for the rigid-body moment of inertia; near 132Sn the reduction factor must be 0.2 to match measured spin values

## Context

This work directly challenges the conclusion of Wilson et al. (2021) that uncorrelated fragment spins imply post-scission spin generation, showing that the nucleon-exchange transport model (developed by Randrup and collaborators in the 1970s-1980s) naturally produces uncorrelated spins from correlated pre-scission modes. The paper extends the statistical equilibrium model of Moretto and Schmitt (1981) by implementing only partial relaxation of the six dinuclear rotational modes, consistent with the differing mobility coefficients derived by Randrup (1982). The demonstration that the sawtooth mass dependence of average spin reflects fragment moments of inertia at scission enables future measurements of spin distributions to provide experimental constraints on scission configurations, which would aid development of fission theory.
