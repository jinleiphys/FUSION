# Phi meson production in In-In collisions at $E_{\rm lab}$=158$A$ GeV: evidence for relics of a thermal phase

> Yields and transverse mass distributions of the $ϕ$-mesons reconstructed in the $ϕ\toμ^+μ^-$ channel in In+In collisions at $E_{\rm lab}$=158$A$ GeV are calculated within an integrated Boltzmann+hydrodynamics hybrid approach based on the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model with an intermediate hydrodynamic stage. The analysis is performed for various centralities and a comparison with the corresponding NA60 data in the muon channel is presented. We find that the hybrid model, that embeds an intermediate locally equilibrated phase subsequently mapped into the transport dynamics according to thermal phase-space distributions, gives a good description of the experimental data, both in yield and slope. On the contrary, the pure transport model calculations tend to fail in catching the general properties of the $ϕ$ meson production: not only the yield, but also the slope of the $m_T$ spectra, very poorly compare with the experimental observations.

## Key claim

The hybrid model (Boltzmann+hydrodynamics) gives a good description of the experimental phi meson yield and transverse mass slope, while pure transport model calculations fail in both yield and slope; however, the thermal emission from the hydrodynamic stage itself is almost an order of magnitude smaller than the cascade emission, meaning the thermal phase is essential not for its direct emission but because it imprints "thermal relics" onto the subsequent cascade particles that dominate the final spectra.

## Method

The authors employed an integrated hybrid approach based on the UrQMD transport model with an intermediate (3+1)-dimensional ideal relativistic one-fluid hydrodynamic stage, using a hadron gas equation of state. Phi mesons were reconstructed from the dimuon channel by evaluating phi-to-mu+mu- emission perturbatively in the pre-equilibrium and post-equilibrium cascade stages via the time integration method, and from thermal rates in the hydrodynamic stage using the Boltzmann and pole approximation. The analysis was performed for five centrality classes in In+In collisions at 158A GeV, with the hybrid model results compared to NA60 experimental data and to pure UrQMD cascade calculations.

## Key numbers

- The phi meson vacuum lifetime is approximately 44 fm, while the fireball lifetime is 7-10 fm, explaining why thermal emission is much smaller than cascade emission
- The phi meson vacuum total width is 0.00426 GeV, with a width-to-mass ratio of approximately 0.4%
- The phi meson pole mass is 1.019 GeV
- The thermal emission from the hydrodynamic stage is found to be almost one order of magnitude smaller than the emission from the cascade stage
- The pre-equilibrium emission is two orders of magnitude smaller than the post-equilibrium emission
- The transition from hydrodynamic to cascade occurs when energy density drops below five times the ground state energy density, using transverse slices of thickness 0.2 fm

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of phi meson production at SPS energies by experiments including NA49 (in the kaon channel), CERES (dielectron channel), and NA50 and NA60 (dimuon channel). The hybrid model approach addresses the failure of pure transport calculations (such as those by Alt (2008) for NA49 data) to reproduce phi meson yields and slopes. The results enable further study of whether a phase transition from quark-gluon to hadronic matter occurs, since the hadronic equation of state used here means the thermal dimuon emission should be regarded as an upper limit. The work also connects to earlier findings by Cheng (2003) and Hirano (2007) at RHIC energies regarding early decoupling of phi mesons from hadronic rescattering dynamics.
