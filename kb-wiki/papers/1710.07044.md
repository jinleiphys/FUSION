# Investigating thermalization of a strongly interacting non-Abelian plasma

> Using gauge/gravity duality methods, we study the relaxation towards equilibrium of strongly interacting non-Abelian matter. We adopt boundary sourcing to drive the system out-of-equilibrium, and analyze the equilibration process through local probes (energy density and pressures) and nonlocal probes (lengths of the geodesics between two boundary points and extremal surfaces having a Wilson loop as contour on the boundary). We also investigate the real-time dissociation of the heavy quarkonium in the out-of-equilibrium plasma. Systematic comparison with the results of a geometry dual to viscous hydrodynamics sheds light on the thermalization process.

## Key claim

Using gauge/gravity duality with boundary sourcing (a quench in the 4D metric), the authors find that the thermalization time of a strongly interacting non-Abelian plasma depends on the probe used: local observables (energy density) thermalize faster than nonlocal probes (geodesic lengths and Wilson loop areas), and the thermalization time increases with the size of the nonlocal probe.

## Method

The authors solve the 5D Einstein equations in Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates for a metric dual to a boost-invariant, expanding plasma, imposing a time-dependent quench profile γ(τ) on the boundary metric. They compute local observables (energy density, transverse and longitudinal pressures) from the near-boundary expansion of the metric, and nonlocal observables (geodesic lengths between two boundary points and extremal surface areas for rectangular and circular Wilson loops) by solving the corresponding equations of motion in the bulk. They also study real-time heavy quarkonium dissociation by solving the Nambu-Goto action for a string falling in the time-dependent bulk geometry, with endpoints representing a heavy quark-antiquark pair.

## Key numbers

- For quench model A₂, the pressure anisotropy persists up to t_isotr^A = 6 (in units of the AdS radius); for model B, up to t_isotr^B = 6.74.
- Imposing T_eff = 500 MeV at the end of the quench, the thermalization times are of order 1 fm.
- The quench profile for model A₂ persists up to τ_f^A = 3.25; for model B, up to τ_f^B = 5.
- The asymptotic dissociation time for a string starting at r_max = 12 is t_∞ = (2/(3 r_max)) * _2F_1(1, 5/4, 7/4, -1).
- String calculations use initial separations L and y_L in the range [0.1, 100] and initial velocities v = 0, -0.5, -1.

## Context

This work extends the holographic thermalization studies of Chesler and Yaffe (2008) and the viscous hydrodynamic metric of Janik and Peschanski (2005) by systematically comparing local and nonlocal probes of thermalization for different quench profiles. The authors previously developed the numerical solution algorithm in Bellantuono et al. (2015) and applied it to the same quench models. The quarkonium dissociation analysis extends the holographic approach of Lin and Shuryak (2006) and Iatrakis and Rajagopal (2015) to out-of-equilibrium plasmas. The results enable a more complete understanding of how different observables equilibrate at different rates in strongly coupled plasmas, relevant for interpreting heavy ion collision data.
