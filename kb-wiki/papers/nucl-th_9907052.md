# $K^-$ and $\bar p$ deeply bound atomic states

> The strongly absorptive optical potentials $V_{\rm opt}$ which have been deduced from the strong-interaction level shifts and widths in X-ray spectra of $K^-$ and $\bar p$ atoms produce effective repulsion leading to substantial suppression of the {\it atomic} wave functions within the nucleus. The width of atomic levels then saturates as function of the strength of Im $V_{\rm opt}$. We find that `deeply bound' atomic states, which are inaccessible in the atomic cascade process, are generally narrow, due to this mechanism, over the entire periodic table %reaching values of $Γ_{K^-} \sim 1.7$ MeV and %$Γ_{\bar p} \sim 1.9$ MeV for the $1s$ state in Pb, and should be reasonably well resolved. These predictions are insensitive to $V_{\rm opt}$, provided it was fitted to the observed X-ray spectra. In contrast, the {\it nuclear} states bound by $V_{\rm opt}$ are very broad and their spectrum depends sensitively on details of $V_{\rm opt}$. We discuss production reactions for $K^-$ atomic states using slow $K^-$ mesons from the decay of the $ϕ$(1020) vector meson, and the ($\bar p, p$) reaction for $\bar p$ atomic states. Rough cross-section estimates are given.

# K⁻ and \(\bar p\) Deeply Bound Atomic States

## Key claim

Deeply bound atomic states of kaons and antiprotons, which are inaccessible via the normal atomic cascade process, are predicted to be narrow (widths of order 1–2 MeV) over the entire periodic table because the strongly absorptive optical potentials fitted to X-ray data produce effective repulsion that suppresses atomic wave functions inside the nucleus, causing the widths to saturate; these predictions are insensitive to the details of the optical potential provided it reproduces the observed X-ray spectra.

## Method

The authors solved the Klein-Gordon equation with complex optical potentials of the \(t\rho\) and density-dependent (DD) forms, fitted to experimental strong-interaction level shifts and widths from X-ray spectra of normal kaonic and antiprotonic atoms. They calculated binding energies and widths for atomic states across the periodic table (C, Ni, Zr, Pb) using both \(t\rho\) and DD potentials, and verified saturation of widths by varying the strength of the imaginary part of the potential. For production reactions, they estimated cross sections in the plane-wave approximation for the \((K^-, p)\), \((\phi, K^+)\), and \((\bar p, p)\) reactions.

## Key numbers

- Width of the \(1s\) state in kaonic Pb: \(\Gamma_{K^-} \sim 1.7\) MeV
- Width of the \(1s\) state in antiprotonic Pb: \(\Gamma_{\bar p} \sim 1.9\) MeV
- Forward \((\bar p, p)\) cross section for producing the \(1s\) \(\bar p\) atomic state in \(^{30}\)Si: about 10 \(\mu\)b/sr
- Forward \((\bar p, p)\) cross section for producing the \(2p\) \(\bar p\) atomic state in \(^{30}\)Si: smaller by a factor of about 17 than the \(1s\) cross section
- Forward \((\bar p, p)\) cross section for producing the \(2p\) \(\bar p\) atomic state in Zr or Pb: roughly 0.1 \(\mu\)b/sr
- Momentum transfer for the \((K^-, p)\) reaction at DA\(\Phi\)NE (\(p_L = 127\) MeV/c): \(q(0^0) = 47\) MeV/c
- Momentum space radial wave function \(\tilde R_{1s}(0)\) for kaonic Ni: \(265 - i 49.5\) fm\(^{3/2}\)
- Momentum space radial wave function \(\tilde R_{1s}(0)\) for kaonic Pb: \(268 + i 116\) fm\(^{3/2}\)

## Context

This work extends the concept of narrow deeply bound hadronic atomic states, previously demonstrated for pions using the \((d, ^3\text{He})\) reaction by Yamazaki et al. (1996, 1998) following predictions by Friedman and Soff (1985) and Toki and Yamazaki (1988), to kaons and antiprotons. The authors show that despite the strongly attractive real potentials for these species, the strong absorptive part produces effective repulsion and saturation of widths, a mechanism noted qualitatively by Krell (1971), Koch et al. (1971, 1972) for kaons, and Green et al. (1982, 1983) for antiprotons, but not previously studied systematically and quantitatively for realistic situations. The predictions enable experimental searches using low-momentum-transfer reactions such as \((\bar p, p)\) at LEAR, \((K^-, p)\) at the Frascati \(\Phi\)-factory DA\(\Phi\)NE, or the \((\phi, K^+)\) reaction using \(\phi\) mesons produced in \(\bar p p\) annihilation or photoproduction.
