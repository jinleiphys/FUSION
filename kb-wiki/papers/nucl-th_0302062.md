# Coulomb excitation of 11Be reexamined

> The study of Coulomb excitation of 11Be to the first excited state in intermediate energy collisions with heavy targets is presented. The existing experimental data are reanalised by including the probability of projectile survival into the calculation of Coulomb excitation cross sections. The survival probabilities are calculated using a recently developed global optical model potential tailored in line with the double folding model. The extracted B(E1) values for the transition 1/2+ --> 1/2- in 11Be are found to be slightly larger than those obtained so far using the b_0-recipe in Coulomb excitation theory.

## Key claim

The inclusion of a full projectile survival probability (calculated from a global optical model potential with a halo density) in the Coulomb excitation analysis of 11Be yields B(E1) values that are slightly larger (by about 4%) than those obtained using the traditional b0-recipe cutoff method, but the revised values agree within uncertainties with the previous analyses.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed existing experimental Coulomb excitation cross sections for 11Be on 208Pb and 197Au targets at intermediate energies (45, 59.7, and 64 MeV/u) by multiplying the standard first-order perturbation theory excitation probability P(b) by a survival probability |S(b)|^2 calculated from the optical model. The optical potential was obtained from a double folding model using a particle-rotor model density for the 11Be halo (including core excitation of 10Be) and global Fermi distributions for the target densities, with the imaginary part set to 0.78 times the real nuclear potential. Coulomb recoil was included using the Winther-Alder method, and the integration was performed from the experimental impact parameter cutoff b0 determined by the experimental aperture.

## Key numbers

- For 11Be + 208Pb at 59.7 MeV/u (Fauerbach et al.): the inclusion of survival probability reduced the calculated cross section by 4% compared to pure Coulomb excitation with b0 = 11.5 fm, yielding a revised B(E1) = 0.098(11) e^2fm^2 (compared to the original 0.094(11) e^2fm^2)
- For 11Be + 197Au at 59.7 MeV/u: the reduction of the calculated cross section was 5%, yielding a revised B(E1) = 0.083(08) e^2fm^2 (compared to the original 0.079(08) e^2fm^2)
- For 11Be + 208Pb at 64 MeV/u (Nakamura et al.): no difference was found between the revised B(E1) = 0.099(10) e^2fm^2 and the original value
- For 11Be + 208Pb at 45 MeV/u (Anne et al.): the revised B(E1) = 0.048(06) e^2fm^2
- The adopted B(E1) from Millener et al. (1983) is 0.116 e^2fm^2, which the particle-core model reproduces with a 11Be radius of 3.0 fm
- The nuclear excitation contribution for the lead target was estimated as approximately 10 mb, implying a 3% effect

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Coulomb excitation experiments on 11Be performed at GANIL (Anne et al., 1995), RIKEN (Nakamura et al., 1997), and MSU (Fauerbach et al., 1997), which all used a sharp impact parameter cutoff b0 to exclude nuclear processes. The authors improve upon the b0-recipe by using a smooth survival probability from the global optical model potential NLM3Y developed by Chamon et al. (2002), which was fitted to elastic scattering data for several systems. The revised B(E1) values remain consistent with the lifetime measurements of Millener et al. (1983) and suggest that similar survival probability corrections should be applied to Coulomb dissociation studies of 11Be and other halo nuclei.
