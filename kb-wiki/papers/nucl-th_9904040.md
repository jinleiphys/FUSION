# $ϕ$-meson production in proton-proton collisions

> The production of $ϕ$-mesons in proton-proton collisions is investigated within a relativistic meson-exchange model of hadronic interactions. The experimental prerequisites for extracting the $NNϕ$ coupling strength from this reaction are discussed. In the absence of a sufficient set of data, which would enable an accurate determination of the $NNϕ$ coupling strength, we perform a combined analysis, based on some reasonable assumptions, of the existing data for both $ω$- and $ϕ$-meson production. We find that the recent data from the DISTO collaboration on the angular distribution of the $ϕ$ meson indicate that the $NNϕ$ coupling constant is small. The analysis yields values for $g_{NNϕ}$ that are compatible with the OZI rule.

## Key claim

The DISTO collaboration's nearly isotropic angular distribution of the $\phi$ meson in $pp \rightarrow pp\phi$ at $T_{lab} = 2.85$ GeV indicates that the $NN\phi$ coupling constant is small, and a combined analysis with $\omega$-production data yields values of $g_{NN\phi}$ compatible with the OZI rule, but only if an OZI violation is introduced at the $v\rho\pi$ vertices in the meson-exchange current.

## Method

The authors describe $pp \rightarrow pp\phi$ within a relativistic meson-exchange model, calculating the transition amplitude in Distorted Wave Born Approximation with the $NN$ final state interaction taken explicitly into account using the Bonn B potential. They identify the nucleonic current and the $\phi\rho\pi$ meson-exchange current as the two dominant production mechanisms, with all other exchange currents contributing about two orders of magnitude less to the total cross section. Due to insufficient data for a direct determination of $g_{NN\phi}$, they perform a combined analysis of the DISTO $\phi$-meson angular distribution and SATURNE $\omega$-meson total cross section data, imposing assumptions that the cutoff parameters and tensor-to-vector coupling ratios are the same for $\omega$ and $\phi$ production.

## Key numbers

- The measured ratio $\sigma_\phi / \sigma_\omega = (3.7 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-3}$ from the DISTO collaboration at $T_{lab} = 2.85$ GeV.
- The interpolated $\omega$ total cross section at $T_{lab}=2.85$ GeV is $\sigma_\omega \sim 0.7 \times 10^2$ $\mu$b, yielding $\sigma_\phi \sim 0.26$ $\mu$b.
- The extracted cutoff parameter for the meson-exchange current form factor is $\Lambda_M = 1450$ MeV.
- The extracted $NN\phi$ coupling constants range from $g_{NN\phi} = -0.19$ to $g_{NN\phi} = -0.90$, depending on the combination of $\kappa$ and $\Lambda_N$ values.
- The OZI prediction from SU(3) symmetry gives $g_{NN\phi} = -(0.60 \pm 0.15)$, using $g_{NN\rho} = 2.63 - 3.36$ and $\alpha_V \cong 3.7^\circ$.
- The $\phi\rho\pi$ coupling constant extracted from the measured decay width of $\phi \rightarrow \rho + \pi$ is $g_{\phi\rho\pi} = -1.64$, while the OZI value at $q_\rho^2=0$ is $g_{\phi\rho\pi}(q_\rho^2=0) \cong -0.65$.
- The $\omega\rho\pi$ coupling constant extracted from radiative decay via vector meson dominance is $g_{\omega\rho\pi} = 10$.
- The naive OZI ratio estimate is $R = 4.2 \times 10^{-3}$, while the full model calculation with OZI-imposed couplings yields a ratio roughly $3 \times 10^{-3}$ at the same excess energy.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous meson-exchange model for $\omega$ production (Nakayama, 1998) to $\phi$ production in $pp$ collisions, addressing the controversy over whether large $\phi$ production cross sections observed in $\bar pp$ annihilation at LEAR (Aston, 1990; Crystal Barrel, 1995; Obe, 1995) indicate hidden strangeness in the nucleon (Ellis, 1995; Gutsche, 1997) or can be explained by rescattering effects (Loche, 1994; Buzatu, 1994; Klempt, 1994; Mull, 1994). The analysis enables future tests of the model in other reactions such as $\phi$ and $\omega$ photoproduction, and highlights the need for $\omega$ angular distribution data in the energy region where the model is applicable to better constrain possible OZI violation at the $v\rho\pi$ vertices.
