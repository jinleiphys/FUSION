# Polarized electron-deuteron deep-inelastic scattering with spectator nucleon tagging

> Background: DIS on the polarized deuteron with detection of a proton in the nuclear breakup region (spectator tagging) represents a unique method for extracting the neutron spin structure functions and studying nuclear modifications. The tagged proton momentum controls the nuclear configuration during the DIS process and enables a differential analysis of nuclear effects. Such measurements could be performed with the future electron-ion collider (EIC) and forward proton detectors if deuteron beam polarization could be achieved. Purpose: Develop theoretical framework for polarized deuteron DIS with spectator tagging. Formulate procedures for neutron spin structure extraction. Methods: A covariant spin density matrix formalism is used to describe general deuteron polarization in collider experiments (vector/tensor, pure/mixed). Light-front (LF) quantum mechanics is employed to factorize nuclear and nucleonic structure in the DIS process. A 4-dimensional representation of LF spin structure is used to construct the polarized deuteron LF wave function and efficiently evaluate the spin sums. Free neutron structure is extracted using the impulse approximation and analyticity in the tagged proton momentum (pole extrapolation). Results: General expressions of the polarized tagged DIS observables in collider experiments. Analytic and numerical study of the polarized deuteron LF spectral function and nucleon momentum distributions. Practical procedures for neutron spin structure extraction from the tagged deuteron spin asymmetries. Conclusions: Spectator tagging provides new tools for precise neutron spin structure measurements. D-wave depolarization and nuclear binding effects can be eliminated through the tagged proton momentum dependence. The methods can be extended to tensor-polarized observables, spin-orbit effects, and diffractive processes.

## Key claim

Spectator tagging in polarized electron-deuteron deep-inelastic scattering enables extraction of the free neutron spin structure function \(g_{1n}\) by controlling the nuclear configuration through the tagged proton momentum, with the caveat that the method relies on the impulse approximation and analyticity in the tagged proton momentum (pole extrapolation) to eliminate D-wave depolarization and nuclear binding effects, and that deuteron beam polarization at the EIC is regarded as technically possible but considered a future option.

## Method

The authors develop a covariant spin density matrix formalism to describe general deuteron polarization (vector/tensor, pure/mixed) in collider experiments, and employ light-front (LF) quantum mechanics to factorize nuclear and nucleonic structure in the DIS process. A 4-dimensional representation of LF spin structure is used to construct the polarized deuteron LF wave function and efficiently evaluate spin sums, with free neutron structure extracted using the impulse approximation and analyticity in the tagged proton momentum (pole extrapolation). The general expressions for polarized tagged DIS observables are derived, and the polarized deuteron LF spectral function and nucleon momentum distributions are studied analytically and numerically, leading to practical procedures for neutron spin structure extraction from tagged deuteron spin asymmetries.

## Key numbers

- Tagged proton momenta are detected with \(p_p \lesssim 300\) MeV in the deuteron rest frame, where the deuteron can be described with good accuracy in terms of nucleonic degrees of freedom.
- The EIC design enables electron-deuteron collisions at electron-nucleon CM energies \(\sqrt{s_{eN}} \sim 20\)–100 GeV and luminosities \(\sim 10^{33}\)–\(10^{34}\) cm\(^{-2}\) s\(^{-1}\) per nucleon.
- Forward proton detectors at the EIC can detect protons with transverse momenta from zero to \(\sim\) few 100 MeV with a resolution \(\lesssim 30\) MeV, and longitudinal momenta from \(\sim 0.5\)–1.5 times the nominal spectator momentum.
- The BoNuS fixed-target setup at JLab detects only protons with momenta \(p_p \gtrsim 70\) MeV, as slower protons cannot escape the target.
- FSI in unpolarized tagged DIS at intermediate \(x\) (\(\sim 0.1\)–0.5) were found to be moderate at proton momenta \(\lesssim 100\) MeV in the deuteron rest frame.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for unpolarized spectator-tagged DIS on the deuteron (Frankfurt 1981, Strikman 2017) to the polarized case, building on earlier discussions of polarized deuteron DIS with tagging (Frankfurt 1983). The method enables precision neutron spin structure measurements at the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) by eliminating D-wave depolarization and nuclear binding effects through the tagged proton momentum dependence, and can be extended to tensor-polarized observables, spin-orbit effects, and diffractive processes.
