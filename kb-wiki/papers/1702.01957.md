# The baryon spectroscopy: strong decays and strange suppression

> In this contribution, we present the open-flavor strong decays of light baryons computed within the framework of quark model. The transition amplitudes are computed using a modified $^3P_0$ operator, where a mechanism strange suppression is taken into account. Also we discus the strange suppression within an extension of the quark model.

## Key claim

The open-flavor strong decay widths of light baryons, computed within a quark model using a modified $^3P_0$ operator that includes a strange suppression mechanism, show improved agreement with experimental data for many resonances, but the suppression mechanism is not sufficient to reproduce the measured width of the $\Sigma^*(2030) \rightarrow \Xi K$ decay.

## Method

The two-body strong decay widths of baryon resonances were computed using a $^3P_0$ pair-creation model with harmonic oscillator wave functions, where the effective pair-creation strength $\gamma_0^{\text{eff}}$ was modified to include a factor $m_n/m_i$ (with $i = n$ for $u,d$ or $s$) to account for strange quark suppression. The transition amplitudes were calculated for decays into baryon-pseudoscalar meson pairs, using mass spectra from both the $U(7)$ algebraic model and the hypercentral quark model (hQM). Additionally, the unquenched quark model (UQM) was applied to compute ratios of electro-production cross sections and the strangeness suppression factor in the proton.

- $N(1720) \rightarrow \Lambda K$: width without suppression = 39 MeV (U(7)) or 33 MeV (hQM); with suppression = 14 MeV (U(7)) or 12 MeV (hQM); experimental range = 2-60 MeV
- $N(1900) \rightarrow \Lambda K$: width without suppression = 36 MeV (both models); with suppression = 13 MeV (both models); experimental range = 0-37 MeV
- $N(1900) \rightarrow \Sigma K$: width without suppression = 3 MeV (both models); with suppression = 1 MeV (both models); experimental range = 6-26 MeV
- $\Delta(1910) \rightarrow \Sigma K$: width without suppression = 105 MeV (both models); with suppression = 38 MeV (both models); experimental range = 9-48 MeV
- $\Delta(1920) \rightarrow \Sigma K$: width without suppression = 64 MeV (U(7)) or 61 MeV (hQM); with suppression = 23 MeV (U(7)) or 22 MeV (hQM); experimental range = 3-7 MeV
- $\Delta(1950) \rightarrow \Sigma K$: width without suppression = 14 MeV (U(7)) or 8 MeV (hQM); with suppression = 4 MeV (U(7)) or 3 MeV (hQM); experimental range = 1-2 MeV
- $\Sigma^*(2030) \rightarrow \Xi K$: width without suppression = 208 MeV (U(7)); with suppression = 75 MeV (U(7)); experimental range = 26-46 MeV
- UQM ratio $p \rightarrow \Lambda K^+/p \rightarrow n\pi^+$ = 0.227; experimental = $0.19 \pm 0.01 \pm 0.03$
- UQM ratio $p \rightarrow \Lambda K^+/p \rightarrow p\pi^0$ = 0.454; experimental = $0.50 \pm 0.02 \pm 0.12$
- UQM ratio $p \rightarrow p\pi^0/p \rightarrow n\pi^+$ = 0.500; experimental = $0.43 \pm 0.01 \pm 0.09$
- UQM strangeness suppression factor $s\bar{s}/d\bar{d}$ = 0.265; experimental = $0.22 \pm 0.07$

- UQM strangeness suppression factor $2s\bar{s}/(u\bar{u}+d\bar{d})$ = 0.338; experimental = $0.25 \pm 0.08$ (Mestayer) and $0.29 \pm 0.02$ (Bocquet)

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of baryon strong decays within the $^3P_0$ model framework (Micu, LeYaouanc) and the $U(7)$ algebraic model (Bijker, Iachello, Leviatan) and hypercentral quark model (Giannini, Santopinto) by incorporating a strange suppression mechanism. The unquenched quark model results for electro-production ratios and strangeness suppression are compared with experimental data from Mestayer and Bocquet, and the approach generalizes the unitarized quark model (van Beveren, 1979; Tornqvist, 1995) to describe continuum-coupling effects in baryons. The work enables further tests of the strange suppression mechanism in additional decay channels and calls for more precise experimental data to make definitive conclusions.
