# Exclusive neutrino-production of a charmed vector meson and transversity gluon GPDs

> We calculate at the leading order in $α_s$ the QCD amplitude for exclusive neutrino production of a $D^*$ or $D_s^*$ charmed vector meson on a nucleon. We work in the framework of the collinear QCD approach where generalized parton distributions (GPDs) factorize from perturbatively calculable coefficient functions. We include $O(m_c)$ terms in the coefficient functions and $O(m_D)$ term in the definition of heavy meson distribution amplitudes. The show that the analysis of the angular distribution of the decay $D_{(s)}^*\to D_{(s)} π$ allows to access the transversity gluon GPDs.

# Exclusive neutrino-production of a charmed vector meson and transversity gluon GPDs

## Key claim
The authors show that the angular distribution of the decay \(D_{(s)}^*\to D_{(s)} \pi\) in exclusive neutrino production of a \(D^*\) or \(D_s^*\) charmed vector meson on a nucleon allows access to the transversity gluon generalized parton distributions (GPDs), but they caution that the non-existence of a reasonable model for these GPDs and the experimental challenges of neutrino facilities make it premature to work on the phenomenology of the proposed reaction.

## Method
The authors calculate the QCD amplitude at leading order in \(\alpha_s\) within the collinear QCD factorization framework, where gluon GPDs factorize from perturbatively calculable coefficient functions. They include \(O(m_c)\) terms in the coefficient functions and \(O(m_D)\) terms in the definition of the heavy meson distribution amplitudes, using a simple delta-function parametrization \(\phi_D(z) = \delta(z - m_c/M_D)\) for the \(D^*\) meson distribution amplitude. The scattering amplitude is computed from handbag Feynman diagrams involving the hard subprocess \(W^+(q) g \to D^{*+} g\), and the resulting helicity amplitudes are used to derive expressions for the differential cross section and the decay angular distribution of the \(D^* \to D \pi\) final state.

## Key numbers
- The \(D^*(2010)\) meson mass is 2010 MeV and its main decay channel is \(D^*(2010) \to D(1870) \pi\).
- The \(D_s^*(2112)\) meson mass is 2112 MeV and its main decay channel is \(D_s^*(2112) \to D_s(1968) \gamma\).
- The CKM matrix element \(V_{sc}\) enhances the amplitude for \(D_s^*\) neutrino production, making it dominated by gluon GPD contributions.
- The differential cross section for neutrino production integrated over the leptonic azimuthal angle \(\varphi\) is given by \(\frac{d^4\sigma}{dy\, dQ^2\, dt} = 2\pi \bar\Gamma \{\sigma_{LL} + \varepsilon\sigma_{00}\}\), where \(\varepsilon \approx (1-y)/(1-y+y^2/2)\) and \(\bar\Gamma\) contains the Fermi constant \(G_F\).
- The decay angular distribution yields moments \(\langle \cos 2\varphi_D \rangle\) and \(\langle \sin 2\varphi_D \rangle\) that are linear in the transversity GPDs, with \(\alpha = 2\,\mathcal{Re}[\mathcal{M}_{RR} \mathcal{M}_{RL}^*]/\sigma_{LL}\) and \(\beta = 2\,\mathcal{Im}[\mathcal{M}_{RR} \mathcal{M}_{RL}^*]/\sigma_{LL}\).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of exclusive neutrino production of a charmed scalar \(D^+\) meson (Pire and Szymanowski, 2015), which accessed transversity chiral-odd quark GPDs, to the vector meson case for accessing transversity gluon GPDs. The phenomenology of gluon transversity GPDs has been previously restricted to angular asymmetries in deeply virtual Compton scattering (Belitsky et al., 2000), which are experimentally difficult. The proposed reaction could enable new measurements at planned high-energy neutrino facilities such as NOvA, though the authors note that neutrino experiments have not yet demonstrated the ability to analyze exclusive charmed meson production even for the simpler pseudoscalar case.
