# Density Functional Theory for non-relativistic Fermions in the Unitarity Limit

> We derive an energy density functional for non-relativistic spin one-half fermions in the limit of a divergent two-body scattering length. Using an epsilon expansion around d=4-epsilon spatial dimensions we compute the coefficient of the leading correction beyond the local density approximation (LDA). In the case of N fermionic atoms trapped in a harmonic potential this correction has the form E=E_(LDA)(1+c_s (3N)^(-2/3)), where E_(LDA) is the total energy in LDA approximation. At next-to-leading order in the epsilon expansion we find c_s=1.68, which is significantly larger than the result for non-interacting fermions, c_s=0.5.

## Key claim

The authors derive an energy density functional for non-relativistic spin-1/2 fermions at infinite two-body scattering length (unitarity limit) using an epsilon expansion around d=4-epsilon spatial dimensions, computing the coefficient of the leading correction beyond the local density approximation (LDA). The result is rigorous for infinite systems with smoothly varying density but has limitations for finite systems with sharp surfaces, such as fermions in a harmonic trap, where pairing leads to odd-even effects that a local density functional cannot describe.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective Lagrangian for the unitary Fermi gas that incorporates spontaneous symmetry breaking, Goldstone modes, and constraints from Galilean and conformal symmetry. They determined the coefficients in this Lagrangian using an epsilon expansion around d=4-epsilon spatial dimensions, computing the phonon dispersion relation and static susceptibility at next-to-leading order (NLO) in the epsilon expansion, then matching these results to the low-energy effective theory to fix the parameters c1 and c2 in the energy density functional.

## Key numbers

- The coefficient of the leading correction beyond LDA for N fermions in a harmonic trap is c_s = 1.68 at NLO in the epsilon expansion, compared to c_s = 0.5 for non-interacting fermions.
- The energy density functional at NLO is: E(x) = n(x)V(x) + 1.364 * n(x)^(5/3)/m + 0.022 * (nabla n(x))^2/(m n(x)) + O(nabla^4 n).
- The Bertsch parameter (chemical potential in units of Fermi energy) is xi = epsilon^(3/2)/2 * [1 + (1/8)epsilon log(epsilon) - (1/4)(12C - 5 + 5 log(2))epsilon + O(epsilon^2)], where C ≈ 0.14424.
- For epsilon=1 (d=3), xi ≈ 0.475.
- The two-loop self energy constants are C1 = -9/2 * C ≈ -0.64908 and C2 = -3/2 * C ≈ -0.21636.
- The phonon dispersion relation at NLO is p_0 = sqrt(mu * epsilon_p) * (1 + epsilon/8) * {1 + epsilon_p/(8 mu) * (1 - epsilon/4) + ...}.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to the unitary Fermi gas developed by Son (2005) and the epsilon expansion method introduced by Nussinov (2004) and Nishida (2006). The resulting density functional enables systematic calculations for neutron star crusts, neutron halos in nuclei, and trapped fermionic atoms near Feshbach resonances. The authors note that their result for c_s = 1.68 is consistent with numerical data from Chang (2007) and Stecher (2007) for up to N=30 trapped fermions, which give c_s in the range 0.9-2.5 when using the commonly accepted bulk value xi = 0.40-0.44.
