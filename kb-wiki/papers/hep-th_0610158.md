# Bethe-Salpeter equation with cross-ladder kernel in Minkowski and Euclidean spaces

> Some results obtained by a new method for solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation are presented. The method is valid for any kernel given by irreducible Feynman graphs. The Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, both in Minkowski and in Euclidean spaces, and the binding energy for ladder + cross-ladder kernel are found. We calculate also the corresponding electromagnetic form factor.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates a new method for solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation that works for any kernel given by irreducible Feynman graphs, but the actual results shown are limited to the ladder plus cross-ladder kernel for spinless particles with massive exchange, and the method's validity for the cross-ladder kernel specifically confirms that Wick rotation is possible for this kernel (which had not been proved before).

## Method

The Bethe-Salpeter amplitude is expressed using the Nakanishi integral representation and substituted into the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The resulting equation is then projected onto the light-front plane by integrating over the variable k_- = k_0 - k_z, which eliminates the singularities of the original amplitude and yields an integral equation for the weight function g(γ,z). This equation is solved numerically for the ladder and ladder+cross-ladder kernels, and the resulting weight function is used to reconstruct the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude in both Minkowski and Euclidean spaces.

## Key numbers

- Ground state mass M obtained for exchanged mass μ = 0.15 (in units of constituent mass) with ladder+cross-ladder kernel, compared with Feynman-Schwinger representation results
- For binding energy B = 0.01 and μ = 0.5, coupling constant α = 1.206 from the new method versus α = 1.205 from Euclidean space calculation (accuracy better than 0.1%)
- For binding energy B = 1.0 and μ = 0.5, coupling constant α = 4.549 from the new method versus α = 4.551 from Euclidean space calculation (accuracy better than 0.1%)
- Additional coupling constant values for B = 0.05 (α = 1.607), B = 0.1 (α = 1.930), B = 0.2 (α = 2.416), and B = 0.5 (α = 3.446) from the new method, all with corresponding Euclidean values within 0.1% agreement

## Context

This work extends the method developed in Karmanov et al. (2005) for solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation, which was previously only validated for the ladder kernel by Wick (1954) and for separable kernels by Brayshaw et al. The results for the ladder+cross-ladder kernel agree with earlier Euclidean space calculations by Carbonell et al. (1998) and Mangin-Brinet et al. (2001), as well as with light-front dynamics calculations by Carbonell et al. (2003). The method enables exact calculation of electromagnetic form factors using the Minkowski-space Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, avoiding the static approximation of Zuilhof and Tjon (1980) which introduces errors that increase with momentum transfer.
