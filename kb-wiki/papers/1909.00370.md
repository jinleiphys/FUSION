# Coupled-channel approach in hadron-hadron scattering

> Coupled-channel dynamics for scattering and production processes in partial-wave amplitudes is discussed from a perspective that emphasizes unitarity and analyticity. We elaborate on several methods that have driven to important results in hadron physics, either by themselves or in conjunction with effective field theory. We also develop and compare with the use of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in near-threshold scattering. The final(initial)-state interactions are discussed in detail for the elastic and coupled-channel case. Emphasis has been put in the derivation and discussion of the methods presented, with some applications examined as important examples of their usage.

## Key claim

The paper reviews coupled-channel methods for hadron-hadron scattering that emphasize unitarity and analyticity, but the author explicitly states that the list of methods is "not really exhaustive but rather shows the idiosyncrasy of the author," and that "no particular interest has been put in offering an exhaustive list of references."

## Method

The paper develops the mathematical framework of partial-wave amplitudes with well-defined total angular momentum, spin, and orbital angular momentum (the LSJ basis), deriving unitarity relations for coupled channels. It presents the N/D method for separating unitarity cuts from crossed-channel cuts, the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for near-threshold scattering, and the inverse-amplitude method for perturbative treatment of crossed-channel dynamics. The final-state interaction formalism using the Omnès solution and the Khuri-Treiman formalism for three-body decays are also developed.

## Key numbers

- The two-body unitarity relation for partial-wave amplitudes includes the phase-space factor \(\rho_{ij}(s) = |\mathbf{p}|_i/(8\pi\sqrt{s})\theta(s-s_{\text{th};i})\delta_{ij}\)
- The differential cross section between two-body states is given by \(d\hat{\sigma}/d\Omega = (|\mathbf{p}'_1|/|\mathbf{p}_1|) |\langle f|T|i\rangle|^2/(64\pi^2 s)\)
- The optical theorem gives \(\Im T_{ii} = 2|\mathbf{p}_1|\sqrt{s}\,\hat{\sigma}_i\)
- The two-body differential phase-space factor in the center-of-mass frame is \(dQ = |\mathbf{p}_1|d\Omega/(16\pi^2\sqrt{s})\)

## Context

This review extends the author's previous work on hadron-hadron interactions and the N/D method (Oller, 2018), and discusses methods that have been applied to the lightest resonances in Quantum Chromodynamics. The revival of non-relativistic techniques, particularly the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, is motivated by the discovery of the X(3872) (Choi, 2003) and other heavy-quark hadrons near threshold that require coupled-channel dynamics. The work enables systematic treatment of final-state interactions in production processes and provides the mathematical foundation for the Khuri-Treiman formalism applied to eta-to-three-pion decays.
