# Lattice QCD constraints on the critical point from an improved precision equation of state

> In this Letter we employ lattice simulations to search for the critical point of quantum chromodynamics (QCD). We search for the onset of a first order QCD transition on the phase diagram by following contours of constant entropy density from imaginary to real chemical potentials under conditions of strangeness neutrality. We scan the phase diagram and investigate whether these contours meet to determine the probability that the critical point is located in a certain region on the $T-μ_B$ plane. To achieve this we introduce a new, continuum extrapolated equation of state at zero density with improved precision using lattices with $N_τ=8,10,12,16$ timeslices, and supplement it with new data at imaginary chemical potential. The current precision allows us to exclude, at the $2σ$ level, the existence of a critical point at $μ_B < 450$~MeV.

## Key claim

The authors use lattice QCD simulations to search for the QCD critical point by following contours of constant entropy density from imaginary to real chemical potentials under strangeness neutrality, and find that at the 2σ confidence level the existence of a critical point at baryon chemical potential μ_B < 450 MeV can be excluded, though the analysis does not provide a clear proof that such a critical endpoint exists.

## Method

The authors performed new lattice QCD simulations using the 4stout staggered action with N_f=2+1+1 quarks at physical quark masses on lattices with N_τ=8,10,12,16 timeslices. They computed a continuum extrapolated equation of state at zero baryon density with improved precision, then supplemented it with new data at imaginary chemical potential to construct entropy contours. The contours of constant entropy density were extrapolated from imaginary to real chemical potentials using a rational ansatz, and the derivative (∂T_s/∂T_0)_{μ_B} was evaluated to determine where the entropy becomes multi-valued, indicating a first-order transition.

## Key numbers

- The integration constant for the pressure at T_0=185 MeV is p(T_0)/T_0^4 = 1.371(28), representing approximately a 2× improvement in precision over the previous result from Borsanyi (2013)
- At the 2σ confidence level, the existence of a critical point is excluded below μ_B^{2σ}=450 MeV
- The equation of state was computed in the temperature range T=120-300 MeV
- Lattice simulations used N_τ=8,10,12,16 timeslices for the continuum extrapolation
- The transition at vanishing density is located at around T=155-160 MeV

## Context

This work extends the method proposed by Shah (2024) for locating the critical point via entropy contours, but improves upon it by using a new continuum extrapolated equation of state with significantly reduced uncertainties, by extrapolating entropy contours from imaginary chemical potential rather than from μ_B=0 data, and by enforcing strangeness neutrality. The results are consistent with the conclusions from an analysis of the Polyakov loop in a small physical volume by Borsanyi (2024), where a broadening of the crossover transition was observed at μ_B ≲ 400 MeV. The lower bound on the critical chemical potential enables future experimental searches at the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC to focus on higher baryon densities.
