# The Faint Stellar Object SDSS J1257+3419 is a Dark Matter Dominated System

> A recent study has revealed SDSS J1257+3419 is either a faint and small dwarf galaxy or a faint and widely extended globular cluster. In this Letter, the author suggests this stellar system is a dwarf spheroidal (dSph). Adopting an observational relation between binding energy and mass of old stellar systems, we derive a relation between mass and size of dSphs by assuming that they are dark matter dominated and virialized objects. Letting half-light radius represent size of SDSS J1257+3419, we find that its mass is $\sim 7\times 10^6$ solar mass. This indicates mass-to-light ratio ($M/L$) of SDSS J1257+3419 is about 1000 in the solar unit. This large $M/L$ is expected from a Mateo plot of dSphs. Thus, we insist SDSS J1257+3419 is a dSph.

## Key claim

The author argues that SDSS J1257+3419 is a dark matter dominated dwarf spheroidal (dSph) galaxy, based on deriving a mass-to-light ratio of about 1000 in solar units from a theoretical relation between mass and size, rather than from direct dynamical measurements.

## Method

The author derives a relation between dark matter mass and radius for dSphs by assuming they form as virialized objects obeying the Saito (1979a) binding energy–mass relation, then undergo a galactic wind that removes most gas, and finally re-virialize with dark matter dominating the mass. This relation is applied to SDSS J1257+3419 using its observed half-light radius of 0.038 kpc and absolute magnitude of -4.8 in V-band to estimate its mass and mass-to-light ratio.

## Key numbers

- Mass of SDSS J1257+3419 is estimated as ~7 × 10^6 solar masses.
- Mass-to-light ratio (M/L) of SDSS J1257+3419 is about 1000 in solar units.
- Stellar mass of SDSS J1257+3419 is estimated as 0.71 × 10^4 solar masses, assuming M/L = 1 for the stellar component.
- Absolute magnitude of SDSS J1257+3419 is -4.8 in V-band.
- Half-light radius of SDSS J1257+3419 is 0.038 kpc.
- For comparison, Bootes dSph has M/L of 555 and half-light radius 0.22 kpc; Draco dSph has M/L of 243 and half-light radius 0.12 kpc.
- Tidal radius of SDSS J1257+3419 is estimated as ~1000 pc assuming Milky Way mass of ~10^12 solar masses.

## Context

This work extends the study of very faint dSphs discovered in the Sloan Digital Sky Survey, such as Ursa Major II (Zucker et al. 2006) and Coma Berenices (Belokurov et al. 2007), and follows the earlier suggestion by Kamaya (2007) that SDSS J1257+3419 is a dSph based on its sparse stellar density. The result is consistent with the Mateo plot (Mateo et al. 1993; Gilmore et al. 2007) which shows that fainter dSphs have larger M/L, and the author notes that a recent dynamical study by Simon and Geha (2007) gives a lower M/L of 336 ± 240, attributing the difference to gradual mass-loss effects that require further numerical modeling.
