# Lattice Results on QCD Thermodynamics

> We review recent results on QCD at finite temperature. Main emphasis is put on a discussion of observables which are of immediate interest to experimental searches for the Quark Gluon Plasma, i.e. the phase transition temperature, the equation of state in 2 and 3-flavour QCD, the heavy quark free energy and thermal effects on hadron masses.

## Key claim

This review argues that lattice QCD calculations of thermodynamic observables (transition temperature, equation of state, heavy quark free energy, and thermal hadron masses) have reached a consistent picture across different discretization schemes, but all results for QCD with dynamical quarks have been obtained on lattices with temporal extent Nτ = 4 (lattice spacing a ≈ 0.3 fm), and the systematic error on the transition temperature is estimated to be of similar magnitude as the quoted statistical error.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD simulations using improved staggered fermions (p4-action), improved Wilson (clover) fermions, and domain wall fermions, all on lattices with temporal extent Nτ = 4. The transition temperature is extracted from the chiral extrapolation of Tc/mV and Tc/√σ for 2-flavor, 3-flavor, and (2+1)-flavor QCD. The equation of state is computed from the pressure and energy density using improved gauge and staggered fermion actions, and the heavy quark free energy is obtained from Polyakov loop correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperature in 2-flavor QCD (chiral limit, using mρ to set scale): Tc = (171 ± 4) MeV with clover-improved Wilson fermions; Tc = (173 ± 8) MeV with improved staggered fermions
- Transition temperature in 3-flavor QCD (chiral limit): Tc = (154 ± 8) MeV with improved staggered fermions
- Difference between 2-flavor and 3-flavor transition temperatures: Tc(nf=2) - Tc(nf=3) ≈ 20 MeV
- Quark mass dependence of Tc in 3-flavor QCD: (Tc/√σ)x = (Tc/√σ)0 + 0.04(1) x, where x = mPS/√σ
- Critical energy density at Tc: εc ≈ (6 ± 2) Tc⁴
- Screening of heavy quark free energy: at T ≈ 0.95 Tc, screening sets in at r ≈ 0.4 fm, compared to r ≈ 0.8 fm at low temperature
- Lattice spacing at Nτ = 4: a ≈ 0.3 fm

## Context

This review extends earlier lattice calculations of QCD thermodynamics that used standard Wilson (Bit91) and staggered (Ber97) fermion actions, which showed significant discrepancies in the transition temperature. The newer results with improved actions (clover Wilson, domain wall, and p4 staggered) have largely resolved those differences. The work enables future continuum extrapolations of the equation of state for QCD with light quarks and provides input for experimental searches for the Quark Gluon Plasma at RHIC and LHC. Open questions include the critical behavior of 2-flavor QCD in the chiral limit, the physically realized (2+1)-flavor case with a heavier strange quark, and the entire phase diagram at non-zero baryon number density.
