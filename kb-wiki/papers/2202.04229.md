# Matter radii and skins of $^{6,8}$He from reaction cross section of proton+$^{6,8}$He scattering based on the Love-Franey $t$-matrix model (published in Results in Physics)

> For $^{4,6,8}$He, Tanihata et al. determined matter radii $r_{m}(σ_{\rm I})=1.57(4), 2.48(3), 2.52(3)$~fm from interaction cross sections $σ_{\rm I}$ for $^{4,6,8}$He scattering on Be, C Al targets at 790~MeV/nucleon. Lu et al. measured the atomic isotope shifts (AIS) for $^{4,6,8}$He and determined proton radii $r_{p}({\rm AIS})$ for $^{4,6,8}$He. As for p+$^{4,6,8}$He scattering, reaction cross sections $σ_{\rm R}({\rm exp})$ are available at 700~MeV with high accuracy. Our aim is to determine matter radii $r_{m}$ and skins $r_{\rm skin}$ for $^{6,8}$He from the $σ_{\rm R}({\rm exp})$ and the $r_{p}({\rm AIS})$. {\bf Method:} Our model is the Love-Franey $t$-matrix folding model, since the model is better than the optical limit of Glauber model. Our results for $^{6,8}$He are $r_{m}({\rm exp})=2.48(3), 2.53(2)$~fm and $r_{\rm skin}=$0.78(3), 0.82(2)~fm. For $^{6,8}$He, our results $r_{m}(σ_{\rm R})$ agree with those of Tanihata {\it et al.}. For $^{8}$He, the distance between $^{4}$He and the center of mass of valence four neutrons is 2.367~fm.

## Key claim
The authors determine matter radii and neutron skins for $^{6,8}$He by combining reaction cross section data for proton scattering at 700 MeV with proton radii from atomic isotope shift measurements, using the Love-Franey t-matrix folding model rather than the simpler optical limit of the Glauber model. The results agree with earlier matter radii from Tanihata et al. based on interaction cross sections, but the paper's method requires a fine-tuning factor of 1.1385 for $^{4}$He to reproduce the experimental reaction cross section.

## Method
The authors use the Love-Franey t-matrix folding model to calculate reaction cross sections for p+$^{4,6,8}$He scattering at 700 MeV. They start with proton and neutron densities from D1S-Gogny HFB calculations, then scale these densities to match the known proton radii from atomic isotope shift measurements and the neutron radii deduced from interaction cross sections. For $^{6,8}$He, they adjust the scaling to simultaneously satisfy the experimental reaction cross section (after applying a fine-tuning factor determined from $^{4}$He) and the proton radii from atomic isotope shifts.

## Key numbers
- Matter radii from this work: $r_m(^{6}{\rm He}) = 2.48(3)$ fm, $r_m(^{8}{\rm He}) = 2.53(2)$ fm
- Neutron skin thicknesses: $r_{\rm skin}(^{6}{\rm He}) = 0.78(3)$ fm, $r_{\rm skin}(^{8}{\rm He}) = 0.82(2)$ fm
- Distance between $^{4}$He core and center of mass of valence neutrons in $^{8}$He: $d_{\alpha-4n} = 2.367$ fm
- Distance between $^{4}$He core and center of mass of valence neutrons in $^{6}$He: $d_{\alpha-2n} = 3.798$ fm
- Fine-tuning factor for $^{4}$He: $F = 1.1385$, determined from the ratio of experimental to calculated reaction cross section
- Proton radii from atomic isotope shifts used as input: $r_p(^{4}{\rm He}) = 1.462(6)$ fm, $r_p(^{6}{\rm He}) = 1.934(9)$ fm, $r_p(^{8}{\rm He}) = 1.881(17)$ fm (from Lu et al.)
- Matter radii from Tanihata et al. used for comparison: $r_m(^{4}{\rm He}) = 1.57(4)$ fm, $r_m(^{6}{\rm He}) = 2.48(3)$ fm, $r_m(^{8}{\rm He}) = 2.52(3)$ fm

## Context
This work extends the determination of nuclear matter radii and skins for helium isotopes by combining two independent experimental inputs: reaction cross sections for proton scattering at 700 MeV (Neumaier et al., 2002) and atomic isotope shift measurements of proton radii (Lu et al., 2013). The Love-Franey t-matrix folding model improves upon the optical limit of the Glauber model used by Tanihata et al. (1988) for interaction cross section analysis, and was previously validated for $^{4,6,8}$He+$^{12}$C scattering at 790 MeV per nucleon (Tagami et al., 2020). The results enable more precise characterization of the halo structure in $^{6,8}$He, with the extracted core-valence distances providing direct geometric information about the neutron halo configuration.
