# Stochastic quantization at nonzero chemical potential

> Lattice QCD at finite chemical potential is difficult due to the sign problem. We use stochastic quantization and complex Langevin dynamics to study this issue. First results for QCD in the hopping expansion are encouraging. U(1) and SU(3) one link models are used to gain further insight into why the method appears to be successful.

# Stochastic quantization at nonzero chemical potential

## Key claim
Complex Langevin dynamics may be insensitive to the sign problem at finite chemical potential, provided the complexified gauge links remain bounded and do not run away; the method appears successful in U(1) and SU(3) one-link models and yields encouraging first results for QCD in the hopping expansion, but the authors stop short of claiming general proof of convergence.

## Method
The authors apply complex Langevin dynamics to three models: a U(1) one-link model, an SU(3) one-link model, and QCD in the hopping expansion (Wilson fermions with small hopping parameter κ, retaining terms with κ e^{±μ}). The Langevin update is performed in SL(3,ℂ) rather than SU(3), using a drift term derived from the complex effective action S_eff = S_B − ln det M, with a stepsize ε = 2–5×10⁻⁵ and dynamical stepsize control to eliminate runaway solutions. Observables computed include the Polyakov loop, the conjugate Polyakov loop, the density, and the average phase factor of the determinant.

## Key numbers
- Stepsize ε = 2–5×10⁻⁵ used in numerical integration of the complex Langevin equation.
- QCD hopping expansion results on a 4⁴ lattice at β = 5.6, κ = 0.12, with N_f = 3 flavours; Polyakov loop and density increase substantially from μ = 0.5 to larger μ, interpreted as a transition from a low-density confining phase to a high-density deconfining phase.
- In the U(1) one-link model, the plaquette ⟨cos x⟩ shows smooth connection between complex Langevin (μ² > 0) and real Langevin (μ² < 0) with comparable statistical errors.
- In the U(1) model, the smallest nonzero eigenvalues of the complex Fokker-Planck operator are found numerically to be positive definite for various β and μ values at κ = 1/2.

## Context
This work extends earlier attempts at complex Langevin dynamics for finite-density QCD, which had been hindered by numerical instabilities and convergence issues (Karsch 1985, Ambjorn 1986). The authors were motivated by recent successes of refined Langevin algorithms in nonequilibrium Minkowski quantum field dynamics (Berges 2005, 2006, 2007). The paper provides a detailed analysis of why the method works in simple one-link models via classical flow diagrams and complex Fokker-Planck eigenvalue analysis, and it enables future application of stochastic quantization to full lattice QCD at finite chemical potential without reweighting or Taylor expansion.
