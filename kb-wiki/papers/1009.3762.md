# Quark spectral properties above Tc from Dyson-Schwinger equations

> We report on an analysis of the quark spectral representation at finite temperatures based on the quark propagator determined from its Dyson-Schwinger equation in Landau gauge. In Euclidean space we achieve nice agreement with recent results from quenched lattice QCD. We find different analytical properties of the quark propagator below and above the deconfinement transition. Using a variety of ansaetze for the spectral function we then analyze the possible quasiparticle spectrum, in particular its quark mass and momentum dependence in the high temperature phase. This analysis is completed by an application of the Maximum Entropy Method, in principle allowing for any positive semi-definite spectral function. Our results motivate a more direct determination of the spectral function in the framework of Dyson-Schwinger equations.

## Key claim

The quark spectral function above the deconfinement transition temperature can be meaningfully extracted from Dyson-Schwinger equation solutions using ansatz-based fitting methods, but the quasiparticle interpretation is limited because the decay widths may become comparable to the thermal masses at strong coupling, questioning the existence of well-defined quasiparticles at small momenta.

## Method

The authors solved the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation in Landau gauge using the Matsubara formalism, with temperature-dependent gluon propagators taken from quenched lattice QCD fits and a phenomenological Ball-Chiu inspired quark-gluon vertex whose infrared strength was adjusted to reproduce lattice thermal masses. They then inverted the spectral representation by fitting several ansatze (single-pole, two-pole delta functions, and Gaussian-broadened versions) to the Euclidean Matsubara propagator, and also applied the Maximum Entropy Method as a less restrictive alternative.

- Thermal masses extracted at zero momentum: \(m_T/T = 0.865\) at \(T=1.25\,T_c\), \(0.862\) at \(T=1.5\,T_c\), and \(0.822\) at \(T=2.2\,T_c\) (Table 1).
- Vertex parameter \(d_1\) was set to \(4.6\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) below \(T_c\), and reduced to \(0.5\), \(0.4\), and \(0.25\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) at \(T/T_c = 1.25\), \(1.5\), and \(2.2\) respectively (Table 1).
- The two-pole delta ansatz gave \(\ell_+^2\) values more than one order of magnitude smaller than the single-pole delta ansatz for all temperatures and masses studied.
- For heavy quarks (\(m/T \gtrsim 0.2\)), the two-pole delta ansatz outperformed the single Gaussian ansatz by roughly one order of magnitude in \(\ell_+^2\).
- The Schwinger function \(S^M_+(\tau)\) changed sign at \(T=0.99\,T_c\) for current quark masses \(m(\mu)=0.2\) and \(0.4\ \mathrm{GeV}\) in an interval around \(\tau T \approx 0.8\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of quark spectral functions from lattice QCD by Karsch et al. (2009) into the Dyson-Schwinger equation framework, using the same two-pole ansatz methodology for comparison. It also builds on earlier DSE studies of the chiral and deconfinement transition by Fischer et al. (2009, 2010) and on HTL results by Braaten et al. (1989) and Blaizot et al. (1993). The results motivate a more direct determination of the spectral function within Dyson-Schwinger equations, potentially enabling improved transport calculations for the quark-gluon plasma and more reliable predictions for dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions.
