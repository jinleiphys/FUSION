# Comment on "Nuclear Emissions During Self-Nucleated Acoustic Cavitation"

> In a recent Letter to PRL, Taleyarkhan and coauthors claim to observe DD fusion produced by acoustic cavitation. Among other evidence, they provide a proton recoil spectrum that they interpret as arising from 2.45 MeV DD fusion neutrons. My analysis concludes the spectrum is inconsistent with 2.45 MeV neutrons, cosmic background, or a PuBe source, but it is consistent with a Cf-252 source.

## Key claim

The proton recoil spectrum that Taleyarkhan and coauthors interpret as evidence of 2.45 MeV DD fusion neutrons from acoustic cavitation is statistically inconsistent with DD fusion, cosmic background, or a PuBe source, but is consistent with a Cf-252 source.

## Method

Naranjo used Geant4 simulations to model the liquid scintillator detector's response to electron recoils from calibration gamma sources, fitting the simulated spectra to measured Compton edges to determine the detector's energy scale and resolution. He then simulated proton recoil spectra for four candidate neutron sources (2.45 MeV neutrons with and without shielding, Cf-252, and PuBe) and fit them simultaneously to the cavitation `on' and `off' data extracted from the original Letter's figures, using the chi-lambda goodness-of-fit variable.

## Key numbers

- The observed chi-lambda value for Cf-252 is 432, with a Z-value of -0.45 (within one standard deviation of the mean), indicating statistical consistency.
- The observed chi-lambda values for 2.45 MeV neutrons (653, Z=5.9), 2.45 MeV neutrons with shielding (637, Z=5.5), and PuBe (621, Z=5.9) are each more than five standard deviations beyond the mean, indicating statistical inconsistency.
- With cavitation off, the ratio of tail (channels 11 and above) to peak (channels 10 and below) counts is 291/764 = 0.38; with cavitation on, the ratio becomes 1216/835 = 1.5.

## Context

This comment directly challenges the claim of DD fusion from acoustic cavitation made by Taleyarkhan (2006) in Physical Review Letters. The analysis extends Naranjo's prior work (2005) on accurately modeling DD fusion proton recoil spectra, and it contradicts the original authors' interpretation by showing that their data match a spontaneous fission neutron source (Cf-252) rather than fusion neutrons. The finding raises serious questions about the evidence for sonofusion and enables further scrutiny of the original experimental claims.
