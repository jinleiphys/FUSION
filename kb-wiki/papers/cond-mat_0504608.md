# Wavelet versus Detrended Fluctuation Analysis of multifractal structures

> We perform a comparative study of applicability of the Multifractal Detrended Fluctuation Analysis (MFDFA) and the Wavelet Transform Modulus Maxima (WTMM) method in proper detecting of mono- and multifractal character of data. We quantify the performance of both methods by using different sorts of artificial signals generated according to a few well-known exactly soluble mathematical models: monofractal fractional Brownian motion, bifractal Levy flights, and different sorts of multifractal binomial cascades. Our results show that in majority of situations in which one does not know a priori the fractal properties of a process, choosing MFDFA should be recommended. In particular, WTMM gives biased outcomes for the fractional Brownian motion with different values of Hurst exponent, indicating spurious multifractality. In some cases WTMM can also give different results if one applies different wavelets. We do not exclude using WTMM in real data analysis, but it occurs that while one may apply MFDFA in a more automatic fashion, WTMM has to be applied with care. In the second part of our work, we perform an analogous analysis on empirical data coming from the American and from the German stock market. For this data both methods detect rich multifractality in terms of broad f(alpha), but MFDFA suggests that this multifractality is poorer than in the case of WTMM.

# Wavelet versus Detrended Fluctuation Analysis of multifractal structures

## Key claim
For a practitioner who does not know the fractal properties of a process a priori, Multifractal Detrended Fluctuation Analysis (MFDFA) should be recommended over the Wavelet Transform Modulus Maxima (WTMM) method, because WTMM gives biased outcomes for monofractal fractional Brownian motion that can spuriously indicate multifractality, and its results can depend on the choice of wavelet. However, the authors do not exclude using WTMM in real data analysis, noting that MFDFA can be applied in a more automatic fashion while WTMM must be applied with care.

## Method
The authors generated artificial signals from exactly soluble mathematical models: monofractal fractional Brownian motion (classical Brownian motion with Hurst exponent H=0.5, antipersistent H=0.3, and persistent H=0.75), bifractal Levy flights (alpha_L=1.5), and several types of multifractal binomial cascades (deterministic, log-Poisson, log-gamma, and log-normal). They applied both MFDFA (using polynomial detrending orders P^(1) through P^(4)) and WTMM (using Gaussian derivative wavelets psi^(1) through psi^(4)) to these signals, comparing the resulting singularity spectra f(alpha) and scaling exponents tau(q) against theoretical predictions. They also analyzed empirical data from 30 stocks of the German DAX and 30 stocks of the American DJI indices.

## Key numbers
- For classical Brownian motion (H=0.5) with time series length N=130,000, MFDFA with polynomial P^(2) correctly identified monofractality for q-range -3 to 3, while WTMM with wavelet psi^(3) produced a broad f(alpha) spectrum that could suggest multifractal scaling.
- For fractional Brownian motion with H=0.3 and H=0.75, time series of length N=130,000 were used with 10 independent realizations per process.
- For the Levy process with alpha_L=1.5, time series of length N=250,000 were used with 10 independent realizations.
- For deterministic binomial cascades, signals were generated in k=17 stages with length N=131,072 points, using mass allocation parameters a=0.55, a=0.65, and a=0.75.
- For log-Poisson binomial cascades with gamma=1.4, signals were generated in k=17 stages (N=131,072) with 10 independent realizations.
- For log-gamma binomial cascades with gamma=1 and beta=ln 2, signals were generated in k=17 stages (N=131,072) with 10 independent realizations.
- For log-normal binomial cascades with lambda=1.1, signals were generated in k=17 stages (N=131,072) with 10 independent realizations.
- For classical Brownian motion, time series lengths of N=15,000, N=65,000, and N=130,000 were compared; WTMM showed noticeable N-dependence while MFDFA gave stable results even for short signals.
- For empirical data, 30 stocks from DAX and 30 stocks from DJI were analyzed; both methods detected rich multifractality in terms of broad f(alpha), but MFDFA suggested poorer multifractality than WTMM.

## Context
This work extends the brief comparison of MFDFA and WTMM previously sketched by Kantelhardt et al. (2002), which was not a central focus of that earlier study. The paper provides a more thorough quantitative comparison using multiple artificial signal types and real financial data. The results enable practitioners to choose MFDFA as the more robust default method for detecting multifractality in nonstationary time series, particularly when signal lengths are limited, while cautioning that WTMM requires careful application and longer data samples. The findings are relevant to fields analyzing DNA sequences, heart physiology, neuron spiking, atmospheric science, financial markets, and geophysics.
