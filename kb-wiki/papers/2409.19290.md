# Nucleation of baryons in relativistic hadron-nucleus collisions

> We suggest a new theoretical method to describe the baryon clusterization of nuclei in hadron-nucleus reactions. As an example we have explored the nuclei production in $π^-+C$ and $π^-+W$ collisions at p$_{lab}$=1.7 GeV by using the hybrid approach consisting of the Ultra Relativistic Quantum Dynamics Model (UrQMD) and the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM). The UrQMD describes the production of new baryons, and the propagation toward the subnuclear densities with the fluctuations leading to the formation of excited baryonic clusters. The SMM describes the production of final nuclei and hypernuclei after interaction of baryons inside these clusters. We demonstrate the transverse momenta, rapidity, mass distributions and excitation energies of both primary clusters and final nuclei (including hypernuclei). The results of the UrQMD and UrQMD+SMM model calculations for different clusterization parameters are compared with the available HADES experimental data on baryon production, providing a very promising window for future research on nuclei and hypernuclei formation in these reactions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that baryon clusterization and subsequent nucleus/hypernucleus formation in hadron-nucleus collisions can be described by a hybrid dynamical-statistical approach, where the key condition is that the expanding nuclear matter must reach subnuclear densities (around 0.1-0.3 of normal nuclear density) and moderate temperatures (5-8 MeV) corresponding to the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition coexistence region, rather than being a simple coalescence or single-source multifragmentation process.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra Relativistic Quantum Dynamics Model (UrQMD) in cascade mode (v.3.5) to simulate the dynamical stage of π⁻+C and π⁻+W collisions at p_lab=1.7 GeV, tracking all nucleons and hyperons up to t=20 fm/c after the first hadron interaction. They then applied the Clusterization of Baryons (CB) model to identify hot primary clusters from baryons with relative coordinates corresponding to 0.1-0.3 of normal nuclear density and relative velocities v_c=0.14-0.22c, and finally used the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) to describe the de-excitation of these clusters into final nuclei and hypernuclei.

## Key numbers

- Beam momentum: p_lab = 1.7 GeV for both π⁻+C and π⁻+W collisions
- Cluster identification performed at time t = 20 fm/c after first hadron interaction
- Normal nuclear density: ρ₀ ≈ 0.15 fm⁻³
- Baryon cluster identification uses relative coordinates corresponding to 0.1-0.3 ρ₀
- Relative velocity parameters for clusterization: v_c = 0.14c and v_c = 0.22c
- Cluster excitation energies in the range 4-10 MeV per nucleon
- Cluster temperature range: 5-8 MeV
- Cluster density threshold for selection: 1/6 ρ₀ during matter expansion
- Total cross sections used for normalization: 196.35 mb for π⁻+C, 1327.32 mb for π⁻+W
- Free protons constitute only about 10% of total protons in the carbon case at |y| < 0.3

## Context

This work extends the hybrid UrQMD+SMM approach previously applied to heavy-ion collisions (Botvina 2020, Buyukcizmeci 2023) to hadron-nucleus reactions, specifically pion-induced reactions studied by the HADES experiment (HADES 2023). It provides a unified framework connecting the well-established multifragmentation of large excited nuclear residues (Bondorf 1995) with the nucleation of small clusters in dilute matter, enabling predictions for light nuclei (up to A=3-4) and hypernuclei (including A=5 systems) that can be tested by HADES and other experiments with nearly 4π exclusive registration.
