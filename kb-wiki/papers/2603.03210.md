# Relativistic distorted-wave analysis of the missing-energy spectrum measured with monochromatic $ν_μ$-$^{12}$C interactions at JSNS$^{2}$

> Recently, the JSNS$^2$ collaboration measured for the first time the missing-energy distribution of $^{12}$C using a monochromatic neutrino beam coming from kaon decays at rest. In this work we present the results of an analysis of this spectrum using the relativistic distorted-wave approach. A new parameterization of the spectral function for neutrons in $^{12}$C, which incorporates detailed information from $\left(e,e'p\right)$ experiments with high missing-energy resolution has been used. The role of the recoil of the residual nucleus, final-state interactions, and neutrino event generators are discussed.

## Key claim

The relativistic distorted-wave analysis of the JSNS$^2$ missing-energy spectrum shows that the shape of the cross section can be reproduced only when both the recoil of the residual nucleus and final-state interactions (including neutron emission from proton re-scattering) are properly accounted for, but the experimental data provide only the shape and not the absolute normalization, limiting the strength of the conclusions.

## Method

The authors performed a relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) analysis of the charge-current quasielastic (QE) interaction of monochromatic 235.5 MeV muon neutrinos with carbon-12, using the energy-dependent relativistic mean field (ED-RMF) model and the relativistic optical potential (ROP) to describe the ejected proton. A new parameterization of the neutron spectral function for carbon-12 was constructed using three Dirac delta functions for the p-shell states, a Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution for the s-shell, and an exponential background to account for short-range correlations, based on updated (e,e'p) data. The NuWro intranuclear cascade model was used to simulate inelastic final-state interactions that produce neutron emission, and meson-exchange current (MEC) contributions were calculated separately using a semi-inclusive relativistic Fermi gas model.

## Key numbers

* The incoming neutrino energy from kaon decay at rest is 235.5 MeV.
* The NLSH RMF potential predicts a root-mean-square charge radius of 2.46811 fm for carbon-12, in agreement with the experimental value of 2.4701 ± 0.0022 fm.
* The total integrated QE cross section predicted by the ED-RMF model is 6.53 × 10^{-39} cm^2, while the ROP model predicts 4.77 × 10^{-39} cm^2 (about 27% smaller).
* The MEC contribution to the total integrated cross section is 1.01 × 10^{-40} cm^2, one order of magnitude smaller than the QE background contribution.
* The p-shell contributions are dominated by the 1p_{3/2} state at missing energy 18.724 MeV with occupancy 1.95 neutrons; the s-shell peaks at 37.0 MeV with occupancy 1.74 neutrons.
* The recoil kinetic energy of the residual nucleus yields values up to approximately 5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic distorted-wave formalism previously applied to inclusive (e,e') and (nu,l) data (PhysRevC.100.045501, PhysRevC.101.015503) to the first missing-energy measurement from the JSNS^2 collaboration (mar25), which uses a monochromatic neutrino beam from kaon decay at rest. The new spectral function parameterization improves upon the work of Ankowski et al. (PhysRevC.110.054612) by using a Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution for the s-shell to avoid unphysical contributions below the nucleon emission threshold, and by incorporating three Dirac delta functions for the p-states based on exclusive (e,e'p) data from Van der Steenhoven et al. (1988). The analysis enables future comparisons between theoretical models and KDAR neutrino data, which can reduce experimental and theoretical uncertainties in neutrino-nucleus interactions relevant to oscillation experiments.
