# The optical model of $N\bar{N}$ interaction without cut-off

> We suggest a regular potential model of $N\bar{N}$ interaction without any cut-off. The effect of singular terms of OBE potential, modified by annihilation is shown to be repulsive. The experimental data for S- and P-wave scattering lengths are well reproduced.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a regular potential model of the N-bar N interaction can be constructed without any cut-off radius, because a sufficiently strong short-range annihilation component regularizes the attractive singular terms (such as 1/r^3) that arise from G-conjugation of the one-boson-exchange potential; the singular part of the potential, once modified by annihilation, becomes effectively repulsive and cannot produce spurious bound states.

## Method

The authors analyze the quantum-mechanical scattering properties of singular potentials of the form -(alpha + i omega)/r^s (s >= 2) with a complex coupling constant, showing that an infinitesimal imaginary part selects a unique boundary condition of full absorption at the origin. They then construct an optical model for the N-bar N system using the real part of the Kohno-Weise one-boson-exchange potential without any cut-off, plus an imaginary singular term -i A/r^3 exp(-r/tau) to represent annihilation. The S- and P-wave scattering lengths are computed by solving the Schrodinger equation with this regularized potential.

## Key numbers

*   The imaginary part of the optical potential uses parameters A = 4.7 GeV fm^2 and tau = 0.4 fm.
*   For the singlet S-wave state with isospin 1 (^11S_0), the calculated scattering length is -0.08 - i 1.16 fm.
*   For the triplet S-wave state with isospin 1 (^13S_1), the calculated scattering length is 1.19 - i 0.64 fm.
*   For the triplet P-wave state with isospin 1 and total angular momentum 0 (^13P_0), the calculated scattering length is -7.67 - i 4.74 fm.
*   For the triplet P-wave state with isospin 1 and total angular momentum 1 (^13P_1), the calculated scattering length is 4.75 - i 0.15 fm.
*   For the triplet P-wave state with isospin 1 and total angular momentum 2 (^13P_2), the calculated scattering length is -0.12 - i 0.82 fm.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of singular potentials with complex strength by Voronin (2003) to the specific case of the N-bar N system. It directly addresses the long-standing problem of cut-off dependence in N-bar N models, as highlighted by Carbonell (2001), by showing that the singular terms from G-conjugation of the NN one-boson-exchange potential (as in the Kohno-Weise and Dover-Richard models) can be regularized by annihilation rather than by an ad hoc cut-off. The model reproduces the experimental S- and P-wave scattering lengths and supports the conclusion that near-threshold resonances (the P-wave enhancement observed at LEAR) are determined by the long-range pionic tail of the potential, not by artifacts of the singular short-range part.
