## Key claim

The authors claim that the $\eta$-$\pi^0$ mixing angle and the partial width of $\eta\rightarrow\pi^0\pi^+\pi^-$ can be greatly enhanced in neutron-rich asymmetric nuclear matter, based on a nucleon one-loop calculation in in-medium chiral perturbation theory that treats the nuclear matter as a free Fermi gas at leading order.

## Method

The authors apply in-medium chiral perturbation theory at the nucleon one-loop level, using a Lagrangian of the $\pi N$ system with terms up to second order. The nuclear matter effect is incorporated through the nucleon propagator modified by Pauli blocking, treating the medium as a free Fermi gas; the calculation retains only terms of order $k_f^{(p,n)3}$, assuming small proton and neutron densities $\rho_{p,n} = \frac{1}{3\pi^2}k_f^{(p,n)3}$. The nucleon mass $m_N$ is treated as large, and meson masses and three-momenta are ignored in comparison.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing theoretical study of $\eta\rightarrow 3\pi$ decay, which Sutherland (1966) showed cannot be explained by electromagnetic effects and is instead due to intrinsic isospin-symmetry breaking in QCD (the $u$-$d$ quark mass difference). The authors apply in-medium chiral perturbation theory (Meissner 2002, Kaiser 2002) to explore a novel medium effect: enhancement of the decay in asymmetric nuclear matter, which could be detectable in experiments with heavy nuclei. The authors note that future work should include higher-order meson loop contributions, which give large corrections in free space and are related to the $\sigma$ resonance in the S-wave $\pi^+\pi^-$ channel (Roiesnel 1981, Gasser 1985).
