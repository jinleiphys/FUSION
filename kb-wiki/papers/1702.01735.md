# Higher-order anisotropies in the Blast-Wave Model - disentangling flow and density field anisotropies

> We formulate a generalisation of the blast-wave model which is suitable for the description of higher order azimuthal anisotropies of the hadron production. The model includes anisotropy in the density profile as well as an anisotropy in the transverse expansion velocity field. We then study how these two kinds of anisotropies influence the single-particle distributions and the correlation radii of two-particle correlation functions. Particularly we focus on the third-order anisotropy and consideration is given to the averaging over different orientation of the event plane.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while the anisotropy of single-particle momentum distributions (v_n) alone cannot uniquely determine both spatial and flow anisotropies of the fireball at freeze-out, the combined measurement of v_n and the azimuthal dependence of femtoscopic correlation radii can disentangle these two contributions, provided that averaging over the orientation of the other-order event plane is included.

## Method

The authors extended the standard blast-wave model to include higher-order (up to third) anisotropies in both the spatial density profile (via a Fourier expansion of the transverse radius R(θ)) and the transverse expansion velocity field (via a Fourier expansion of the transverse rapidity ρ). They calculated single-particle spectra and the azimuthal dependence of two-pion correlation radii (R_o, R_s, R_os) in the out-side-longitudinal frame, including an explicit integration over the uncorrelated second-order or third-order event plane orientation to mimic experimental event-averaging. The resulting Fourier amplitudes of the correlation radii were then mapped as contour plots against the spatial (a_n) and flow (ρ_n) anisotropy parameters.

## Key numbers

*   For pions at p_t = 300 MeV, T = 120 MeV, ρ_0 = 0.8, R_0 = 7 fm: the second-order oscillation amplitude of the sideward correlation radius (R_{s,2,2}^2/R_{s,2,0}^2) is mainly set by the spatial anisotropy a_2, with a weak dependence on the flow anisotropy ρ_2.
*   For the same parameters, the third-order scaled amplitude of the outward radius (R_{o,3,3}^2/R_{o,3,0}^2) is the best reflector of third-order spatial anisotropy a_3, while the sideward third-order oscillation depends on both a_3 and ρ_3.
*   The third-order oscillation amplitudes are typically smaller than second-order ones by an order of magnitude; the next higher order (sixth) is suppressed to the level of a few per mille or less.
*   From a qualitative comparison to PHENIX data (pions at p_t = 863 MeV for v_3 and K_t = 877 MeV for correlation radii), the extracted model parameters are a_3 = 0.0091 and ρ_3 = 0.0069, using STAR-derived second-order parameters (T = 98 MeV, ρ_0 = 0.98, ρ_2 = 0.05, τ_0 = 7.8 fm/c, Δτ = 2.59 fm/c, R_0 = 11.4 fm, a_2 = 0.0439).

## Context

This work extends the earlier second-order blast-wave analysis of Tomasik (2004) and the Buda-Lund model study of Lokos (2016) to third-order anisotropies, providing detailed parameter maps (contour plots) that allow simultaneous extraction of flow and spatial anisotropy from data. It goes beyond the toy-model study of Plumberg (2013) by including both second and third order anisotropies and integrating over the order not being investigated, as done in real experiments. The results enable future Bayesian analyses (e.g., Bernhard, 2016) that simultaneously fit identified spectra, v_n, and correlation radii to determine the fireball state at freeze-out.
