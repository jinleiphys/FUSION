# Charmonium production from the hadronic phase

> Charmonium production from the hadron gas formed in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions is studied. Using the $J/ψ$-hadron absorption cross section determined from the nonperturbative quark-exchange model, which has a peak value similar to that used in the comover model for $J/ψ$ suppression and a thermally averaged value consistent from that extracted from $J/ψ$ data in heavy-ion collisions, we find that $J/ψ$ production from the hadron gas is negligible in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energies but is important at LHC energies as a result of the large number of charm mesons produced at higher energy collisions. The number of $J/ψ$ produced from these secondary collisions at LHC may be comparable to that of primary $J/ψ$'s, which are expected to be dissociated in the quark-gluon plasma created in the collisions, leading thus to a possible absence of $J/ψ$ suppression. Similar results are obtained for $ψ^\prime$ production in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

J/ψ production from D D̅ annihilation in the hadron gas formed after the quark-gluon plasma phase transition may be comparable to the number of primary J/ψ at LHC energies, potentially leading to an absence of J/ψ suppression; at RHIC energies the effect is negligible.

## Method

The authors used the HIJING parton model to determine initial conditions (proper time, temperature, charm multiplicity) for Au+Au collisions at RHIC and LHC. They modeled the hadron gas as an isentropically expanding pion gas with transverse acceleration, and computed J/ψ production rates from D D̅ → J/ψ π reactions using a nonperturbative quark-exchange cross section, applying detailed balance to relate production and absorption cross sections. The time-integrated J/ψ yield was obtained by solving the rate equation with thermal averages of the cross sections over the expanding hadron gas.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC, the initial number of c c̅ pairs in central Au+Au collisions is about 3.8; at LHC it is about 136.
- Primary J/ψ number is about 0.095 at RHIC and 3.4 at LHC (assuming a c c̅ to J/ψ ratio of about 40).
- The thermally averaged J/ψ absorption cross section by pions, ⟨σ_{J/ψπ}v⟩, ranges from 0.25 to 1.0 mb over the relevant temperatures.
- At RHIC, the number of J/ψ produced from D D̅ annihilation in the hadron gas is only 0.005, more than an order of magnitude smaller than the primary number.
- At LHC, with transverse acceleration a = 0.1 c²/fm, the number of J/ψ produced from the hadron gas is comparable to the primary number (about 3.4); varying the phase transition lifetime gives values between 2.25 and 6.15.
- For ψ′ at LHC, the number produced from the hadron gas is about 1.15, exceeding the primary ψ′ number of about 0.682.

## Context

This work extends the study of charmonium regeneration in hadronic matter, previously shown to be important for strange hadrons (Phi, 1995), to the J/ψ and ψ′ channels at RHIC and LHC. It contrasts with the comover absorption model (Gavin, 1991; Capella, 1997) and the Debye-screening picture (Matsui and Satz, 1986) by showing that regeneration can offset suppression at LHC. The results depend critically on the J/ψ–hadron absorption cross section; if that cross section is an order of magnitude smaller (as in perturbative QCD calculations by Kh, 1997, or effective Lagrangian calculations by Mat, 1998), the regenerated yield drops to 0.41, much less than the primary yield. The work motivates better experimental and theoretical determination of the J/ψπ → D D̅ cross section to interpret charmonium signals at LHC.
