# Stellar electron capture rates based on finite temperature relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation

> The electron capture process plays an important role in the evolution of the core collapse of a massive star that precedes the supernova explosion. In this study, the electron capture on nuclei in stellar environment is described in the relativistic energy density functional framework, including both the finite temperature and nuclear pairing effects. Relevant nuclear transitions $J^π= 0^\pm, 1^\pm, 2^\pm$ are calculated using the finite temperature proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation with the density-dependent meson-exchange effective interaction DD-ME2. The pairing and temperature effects are investigated in the Gamow-Teller transition strength as well as the electron capture cross sections and rates for ${}^{44}$Ti and ${}^{56}$Fe in stellar environment. It is found that the pairing correlations establish an additional unblocking mechanism similar to the finite temperature effects, that can allow otherwise blocked single-particle transitions. Inclusion of pairing correlations at finite temperature can significantly alter the electron capture cross sections, even up to a factor of two for ${}^{44}$Ti, while for the same nucleus electron capture rates can increase by more than one order of magnitude. We conclude that for the complete description of electron capture on nuclei both pairing and temperature effects must be taken into account.

## Key claim
The paper claims that including both finite temperature and nuclear pairing correlations in the relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation framework is necessary for a complete description of stellar electron capture rates, with the caveat that the isoscalar pairing strength in the residual interaction is treated as a free parameter and its effect is studied by variation rather than being constrained by data.

## Method
The authors used the finite temperature proton-neutron relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation (FT-PNRQRPA) with the density-dependent meson-exchange effective interaction DD-ME2 to calculate nuclear transitions with J^π = 0±, 1±, 2±. Ground-state properties were obtained using finite temperature Hartree-BCS theory with monopole pairing, and the residual interaction included both isovector pairing (from the Gogny interaction) and isoscalar pairing (with a free strength parameter V0^is varied between 0 and 200 MeV). Electron capture cross sections and rates were computed for 44Ti and 56Fe at stellar temperatures up to 1.5 MeV.

## Key numbers
- For 44Ti at zero temperature, the main GT+ peak from FT-PNRRPA (no pairing) is at E = 0.36 MeV with B(GT+) = 3.06, formed mainly by the (π1f7/2, ν1f5/2) transition.
- For 44Ti at zero temperature using FT-PNRQRPA with no isoscalar pairing (V0^is = 0 MeV), the main peak shifts to E = 3.18 MeV with B(GT+) = 2.83, with additional peaks at E = -2.29 MeV (B(GT+) = 1.07) and 6.30 MeV (B(GT+) = 0.43).
- The critical temperature for neutrons in 44Ti is Tc = 1.83 MeV and for protons is Tc = 0.88 MeV, with a neutron pairing gap Δn = 3.38 MeV.
- Inclusion of pairing correlations at finite temperature can alter electron capture cross sections by up to a factor of two for 44Ti, and electron capture rates can increase by more than one order of magnitude for the same nucleus.
- The axial-vector coupling constant was quenched to gA = -1.0 from its free-nucleon value of -1.26.

## Context
This work extends previous finite-temperature RPA calculations for electron capture on pf-shell nuclei (Fuller, Fowler, and Newman (1980-1985); Langanke et al. (2001)) by incorporating pairing correlations within a relativistic energy density functional framework. It builds on the finite-temperature proton-neutron relativistic RPA (FT-PNRRPA) approach previously applied to 54,56Fe and 76,78Ge (PhysRevC.83.045807) and the finite-temperature QRPA formalism developed for Gamow-Teller transitions (Yuksel et al., 2019). The framework enables future calculations of electron capture rates for a wider range of nuclei relevant to presupernova evolution, where both pairing and temperature effects are significant.
