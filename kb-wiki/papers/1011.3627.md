# Superfluid properties of dilute neutron matter

> We report results of fully non-perturbative calculations, based on Auxiliary Field Quantum Monte Carlo (AFQMC) approach, for the dilute neutron matter at the density $ρ=0.003\fm^{-3}$. Fundamental quantities which characterize the superfluid state: the single particle energy gap $Δ(T)$, and the critical temperature $\Tc$ have been determined. The large value of $Δ(0)/\Tc\approx 3.2$ indicates that the system is not a BCS-type superfluid at low temperatures.

## Key claim

The dilute neutron matter at density ρ = 0.003 fm⁻³ (ρ ≈ 0.02 ρ₀) is not a BCS-type superfluid at low temperatures, as indicated by the large ratio Δ(0)/T_c ≈ 3.2, which exceeds the BCS value of 1.76 by nearly a factor of two.

## Method

The authors performed fully non-perturbative Auxiliary Field Quantum Monte Carlo (AFQMC) calculations at non-zero temperatures for dilute neutron matter at a density of ρ = 0.003 fm⁻³ (Fermi momentum k_F = 0.45 fm⁻¹). They extracted the critical temperature T_c from finite-size scaling of the condensate fraction α computed from two-body density matrices, and determined the pairing gap Δ(T) by analytic continuation of the imaginary-time propagator using truncated SVD and maximum entropy methods.

## Key numbers

- Density: ρ = 0.003 fm⁻³ (ρ ≈ 0.02 ρ₀, where ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is saturation density)
- Fermi momentum: k_F = 0.45 fm⁻¹
- Critical temperature: T_c ≈ 0.09 ε_F (0.39 MeV), where ε_F is the Fermi energy
- Zero-temperature pairing gap: Δ(0)/ε_F = 0.29⁺⁰·⁰²₋₀·₀₄
- Ratio Δ(0)/T_c ≈ 3.2, compared to the BCS value of 1.76
- Scattering length: a = -18.8 ± 0.3 fm; effective range: r_eff = 2.75 ± 0.11 fm
- Lattice filling factor: ν = N/(2N_s³) ≈ 5% for the two largest lattices (N_s = 8, 10)
- Resolution limit for gap detection: Δ_min ≈ 0.2 ε_F

## Context

This work extends previous AFQMC investigations by Wlazlowski and Magierski (2010) to finite temperatures, and provides the first fully non-perturbative determination of both the critical temperature and pairing gap for dilute neutron matter at this density. The results agree with the zero-temperature Green Function Monte Carlo calculations of Gezerlis and Carlson (2008), but differ from the Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo results of Gandolfi et al. (2009) which predict larger pairing gaps. The large Δ/T_c ratio suggests a pseudogap phase similar to that observed in unitary Fermi gases, and enables future studies of the superfluid-to-normal phase transition in neutron star crusts.
