# Fermi and Gamow-Teller Strength in Charge Exchange with Radioactive Beams

> At forward angles, and bombarding energies E > 200 MeV, the (p,n) and (n,p) reactions are thought to be directly proportional to the Gamow-Teller transition strengths in the nuclei. Assuming that this relationship also holds for charge exchange induced by high-energy heavy ions, it would be very useful in studies with radioactive beams. Contrary to this expectation, we show that the determination of Gamow-Teller and Fermi matrix elements from heavy-ion charge-exchange at forward angles is very inaccurate.

# Fermi and Gamow-Teller Strength in Charge Exchange with Radioactive Beams

## Key claim

Contrary to the expectation that heavy-ion charge exchange (HICEX) at forward angles could be used to extract Gamow-Teller and Fermi transition strengths in a manner analogous to (p,n) reactions, the authors show that such determinations are very inaccurate. The relationship fails even for a simple single-particle model and mirror nuclei, making it unlikely to hold for more complex systems.

## Method

The authors develop a strong absorption model of heavy-ion charge exchange based on a single-particle approach, using the reaction 13C(13N,13C)13N at 105 MeV/nucleon as a test case. They calculate the differential cross section using a meson-exchange potential (pi, rho, omega) and eikonal scattering waves, then compare the exact calculation to the "low-q" approximation that would relate the cross section directly to Gamow-Teller and Fermi matrix elements. The transition matrix elements are computed for single-particle transitions between 1p1/2 and 1p3/2 states.

## Key numbers

- At forward angles, the ratio between the exact calculation and the low-q approximation amounts to a factor of 5, demonstrating the failure of the simple proportionality.
- For the 1/2- to 1/2- Fermi transition, the matrix element drops from 1.0 at q=0 to 0.65 at q=0.5 fm^-1 and to 0.14 at q=1.0 fm^-1.
- For the 1/2- to 1/2- Gamow-Teller transition, the matrix element drops from 2.66 at q=0 to 1.73 at q=0.5 fm^-1 and to 0.37 at q=1.0 fm^-1.
- For the 1/2- to 3/2- Gamow-Teller transition, the matrix element drops from 0.33 at q=0 to 0.18 at q=0.5 fm^-1 and to 0.02 at q=1.0 fm^-1.
- The low-q approximation would only become valid if the interaction range were increased by a factor of about 10, to approximately 10 fm.
- At theta=0, the deviation between the exact calculation and the I=0-only approximation is about 2 percent.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Osterfeld et al. (1992), who suggested using HICEX as a probe of Gamow-Teller matrix elements, and directly tests the method used by Steiner et al. (1996) to draw conclusions on Gamow-Teller and Fermi matrix elements in 13C and 13N. The paper contradicts the assumption that the well-established proportionality between (p,n) cross sections and beta-decay strengths (Goodman et al., 1980; Tadeucci et al., 1987) carries over to heavy-ion reactions. The results show that strong absorption in heavy-ion collisions destroys the simple relationship, and that even for (p,n) reactions the low-q approximation has been shown to be ill-defined for weak transitions (Austin et al., 1994). This work implies that alternative methods are needed to map beta transition strength functions in drip-line nuclei using radioactive beams.
