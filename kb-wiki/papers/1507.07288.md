# Nuclear matter equation of state from a quark-model nucleon-nucleon interaction

> Starting from a realistic constituent quark model for the nucleon-nucleon interaction, we derive the equation of state (EOS) of nuclear matter within the Bethe-Brueckner-Goldstone approach up to three-hole-line level, without need to introduce three-nucleon forces. To estimate the uncertainty of the calculations both the gap and the continuous choices for the single-particle potential are considered and compared. The resultant EOS is compatible with the phenomenological analysis on the saturation point, the incompressibility, the symmetry energy at low density and its slope at saturation, together with the high-density pressure extracted from flow data on heavy ion collisions. Although the symmetry energy is appreciably larger in the gap choice in the high-density region, the maximum neutron star masses derived from the continuous-choice EOS and the gap-choice EOS are similar and close to two solar masses, which is again compatible with recent observational data. Comparison with other microscopic EOS is presented and discussed.

## Key claim

The quark-model nucleon-nucleon interaction fss2, when used within the Bethe-Brueckner-Goldstone (BBG) approach up to the three-hole-line level, produces an equation of state (EOS) for nuclear matter that is compatible with phenomenological constraints on saturation, incompressibility, symmetry energy, and high-density pressure from heavy-ion collisions, without requiring three-nucleon forces. However, the agreement depends on the choice of single-particle potential (continuous versus gap choice), and the symmetry energy becomes appreciably larger in the gap choice at high density, even though the maximum neutron star masses from both choices are similar and close to two solar masses.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Goldstone equation for the G-matrix using the energy-independent renormalized Gaussian representation of the fss2 quark-model nucleon-nucleon interaction, within the non-relativistic BBG expansion up to the three-hole-line (THL) level. They performed self-consistent Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations for symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) and pure neutron matter (PNM), using both the continuous choice and the gap choice for the single-particle potential, and included the bubble, U-insertion, ring, and higher-order THL diagrams. The EOS for asymmetric nuclear matter was approximated using the parabolic approximation, and neutron star structure was computed from the resulting EOS.

## Key numbers

- Saturation energy per nucleon for SNM with continuous choice: -16.19 MeV at Fermi momentum k_F = 1.3 fm^{-1} (density ρ = 0.148 fm^{-3}); with gap choice: -15.39 MeV at k_F = 1.3 fm^{-1} (ρ = 0.148 fm^{-3})
- Saturation energy per nucleon for SNM with continuous choice: -15.86 MeV at k_F = 1.4 fm^{-1} (ρ = 0.185 fm^{-3}); with gap choice: -15.59 MeV at k_F = 1.4 fm^{-1} (ρ = 0.185 fm^{-3})
- Total THL contribution (E_3) to SNM energy per nucleon at k_F = 1.4 fm^{-1}: +8.35 MeV (continuous choice), +1.28 MeV (gap choice)
- Deuteron binding energy from fss2: 2.2206 MeV (experimental: 2.224644 ± 0.000046 MeV)
- Deuteron root-mean-square radius: 1.961 fm (experimental: 1.971 ± 0.006 fm)
- Deuteron quadrupole moment: 0.270 fm^2 (experimental: 0.2859 ± 0.0003 fm^2)
- Deuteron D-state probability: 5.52%
- χ^2/N_data for fss2 phase shifts relative to Nijmegen PSA: 6.34
- Maximum neutron star masses from both continuous-choice and gap-choice EOS are similar and close to two solar masses

## Context

This work extends the earlier demonstration (Fukukawa et al., 2014) that the fss2 quark-model interaction reproduces the nuclear matter saturation point without three-nucleon forces, by now comparing with additional phenomenological constraints (incompressibility, symmetry energy, high-density pressure from flow data) and applying the EOS to neutron star calculations. It contrasts with meson-exchange models such as the Argonne V18 potential (which requires three-body forces like Urbana or Illinois models) and the variational method of Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall (1998), as well as relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approaches. The results enable future refinements of the quark-model interaction, particularly improvements to the tensor force and the short-range central/LS balance, and provide a microscopic EOS for neutron star structure calculations that is compatible with two-solar-mass observations.
