# A QCD space-time analysis of quarkonium formation and evolution in hadronic collisions

> The production of heavy quarkonium as QQbar bound-states in hadron-hadron collisions is considered within the framework of a space-time description, combining parton-cascade evolution with a coalescence model for bound-state formation. The `hard' production of the initial QQbar, directly or via gluon fragmentation and including both color-singlet and color-octet contributions, is calculated from the PQCD cross-sections. The subsequent development of the QQbar system is described within a space-time generalization of the DGLAP parton-evolution formalism in position- and momentum-space. The actual formation of the bound-states is accomplished through overlap of the QQbar pair and a spectrum of quarkonium wave-functions. This coalescence can only occur after sufficent gluon radiation reduces the QQbar relative velocity to a value commensurate with the non-relativistic kinematics of these bound systems. The presence of gluon participants in the cascade then is both necessary and leads to the natural inclusion of both color-singlet and color-octet mechanisms. The application of this approach to pp (ppbar) collisions from sqrt(s)= 30 GeV - 14 TeV reveals very decent agreement with available data from ISR and Tevatron - without the necessity of introducing fit parameters. Moreover, production probabilities are calculated for a complete spectrum of charmonium and bottonium states, with the relative significance compared to open charm (bottom) production. An analysis of the space-time development is carried through which sheds light on the relevance of gluon radiation and color-structure, suggesting a correponding experimental investigation.

## Key claim

The paper presents a space-time QCD description of quarkonium production in hadronic collisions that combines parton-cascade evolution with a coalescence model for bound-state formation, claiming to achieve decent agreement with ISR and Tevatron data without introducing fit parameters, while the abstract glosses over the fact that the approach relies on model wave-functions for the coalescence probability rather than first-principles non-perturbative matrix elements.

## Method

The author calculates the hard production of initial QQbar pairs (directly or via gluon fragmentation, including both color-singlet and color-octet contributions) from PQCD cross-sections, then describes the subsequent development of the QQbar system using a space-time generalization of the DGLAP parton-evolution formalism in both position- and momentum-space. The formation of bound-states is accomplished through overlap of the QQbar pair with a spectrum of quarkonium wave-functions, where coalescence can only occur after sufficient gluon radiation reduces the QQbar relative velocity to a value commensurate with non-relativistic bound-state kinematics. The total production cross-section is obtained by summing over all contributing channels and multiplying the factorized production, evolution, and coalescence amplitudes with proper flux- and phase-space factors, implemented via Monte Carlo methods in the computer program VNI.

## Key numbers

- The analysis covers pp (ppbar) collisions from sqrt(s) = 30 GeV to 14 TeV, spanning ISR, Tevatron, and LHC beam energies
- Typical heavy quark velocities are v_ccbar = 0.23 and v_bbbar = 0.08
- The hard scale Q^2 is defined as the transverse momentum squared p_perp^2, with the factorization scale mu^2 set at approximately 1-2 GeV marking the boundary between perturbative and non-perturbative regimes
- The partonic cross-sections are calculated to O(alpha_s^3) for both color-singlet and color-octet contributions
- The typical formation time of a QQbar bound-state is t_form ~ 1/(M_Q v) greater than or approximately 1 fm, requiring the emitted gluon to have at least energy omega_g = O(1 GeV)

## Context

This work extends the NRQCD formalism developed by Bodwin, Braaten, and Lepage (1995) and the color-octet mechanism calculations by Cho and Leibovich (1996) that resolved the "CDF anomaly" at the Tevatron, where quarkonium yields were underestimated by more than an order of magnitude by the earlier color-singlet model. The author criticizes the physical justification of the color-octet mechanism as ad-hoc and develops a dynamical cascade description that allows tracing the space-time history from initial QQbar production through propagation and cascading to bound-state formation, in contrast to previous static calculations that made no reference to microscopic time-development. This approach enables future application to heavy-ion collisions and the issue of quarkonium suppression in dense nuclear matter, where space-time information of QQbar evolution and interaction with the nuclear medium is essential.
