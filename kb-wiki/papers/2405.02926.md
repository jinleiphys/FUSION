# Charge-changing cross section and interaction cross sections for 4$\leq$Z$ \leq$9 isotopes

> The root-mean-square proton and neutron radii for $^{7,9-12,14}$\rm Be, $^{10-15,17}$\rm B, $^{12-19}$\rm C, $^{14,15,17-22}$\rm N, $^{16,18-24}$\rm O, and $^{18-21,23-26}$\rm F isotopes are deduced from a systematic analysis of experimental charge-changing and interaction cross sections in the framework of Glauber model. The calculations involve descriptions of nuclei based on Slater determinants using harmonic oscillator single-particle wave functions. The extracted proton and neutron radii have been examined in the light of some important features such as neutron skin thickness/halo-like structure/subshell closure observed in exotic isotopes.

## Key claim

The authors deduce root-mean-square proton and neutron radii for a wide range of light isotopes (Be through F) by analyzing experimental charge-changing and interaction cross sections within the Glauber model, using a newly prescribed correction parameter that is nearly constant within each isotopic chain and energy-independent, rather than the energy-dependent or Z/N-dependent parameters used in prior work.

## Method

The authors employ the Glauber model with a correlation expansion of the S-matrix up to two-body terms, using Slater determinant harmonic oscillator (SDHO) single-particle wave functions for nuclear densities. They first determine a correction parameter epsilon for each isotopic chain by fitting to nuclei with known charge radii, then use this parameter to extract proton radii from experimental charge-changing cross sections on a carbon-12 target, and finally obtain neutron radii by reproducing interaction cross sections with adjusted oscillator constants for neutron densities.

## Key numbers

- For beryllium isotopes, the average correction parameter epsilon_avg = 1.073 ± 0.013, derived from experimental charge-changing cross sections at energies 772-962 MeV/nucleon on a carbon-12 target
- For boron isotopes, epsilon_avg = 1.023 ± 0.005, from data at 925-932 MeV/nucleon
- For carbon isotopes, epsilon_avg = 0.993 ± 0.008, from data at 828-937 MeV/nucleon
- For nitrogen isotopes, epsilon_avg = 1.014 ± 0.016, from data at 776-932 MeV/nucleon
- For oxygen isotopes, epsilon_avg = 1.002 ± 0.004, from data at 857-872 MeV/nucleon
- The maximum deviation between extracted and experimental proton radii for test nuclei is about 2%
- A local minimum in proton radii is observed at neutron number N = 14 for both nitrogen and oxygen isotopes, indicating a subshell closure

## Context

This work extends the approach of Yamaguchi et al. (2011) and Li et al. (2012), who used an energy-dependent correction parameter, and contrasts with the method of Bhagwat and Gambhir (2008), who used a Z/N-dependent parameter. The authors' new prescription yields a nearly constant correction parameter within each isotopic chain, enabling systematic extraction of proton radii for exotic neutron-rich isotopes. The finding of a subshell closure at N = 14 supports prior results by Bagchi et al. (2011) for nitrogen and Kaur et al. (2015) for oxygen, attributed to tensor interactions between protons in the 1p1/2 orbital and neutrons in the 1d5/2 orbital. The extracted radii enable parameter-free calculations of charge-changing cross sections on proton targets for carbon and nitrogen isotopes.
