# Kaon production and propagation at intermediate relativistic energies

> We systematically study $K^+$ observables in nucleus-nucleus collisions at 1-2 A GeV within the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model. We compare our calculations with the KaoS data on the kaon multiplicities and spectra. In addition, the kaon collective flow is computed and compared with the FOPI and KaoS data. We show, that the elliptic kaon flow measured recently by the KaoS Collaboration is best described by using the Brown-Rho parametrization of the kaon potential ($U_K(ρ_0) \simeq 30$ MeV).

## Key claim

The paper systematically tests whether a kaon potential is needed to describe K⁺ observables in nucleus-nucleus collisions at 1-2 A GeV and, if so, how strong it must be. The authors show that the elliptic kaon flow measured by the KaoS Collaboration is best described using the Brown-Rho (BR) parametrization of the kaon potential (U_K(ρ₀) ≃ 30 MeV), but note that the inverse slope parameters of the kaon spectra are best described by the weaker Ko-Li (KL) potential (U_K(ρ₀) ≃ 6 MeV), while the BR potential best reproduces the kaon multiplicities.

## Method

The authors performed Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model calculations for nucleus-nucleus collisions at beam energies of 1-2 A GeV, propagating a large set of baryonic resonances (N* and Δ states) and including strangeness production channels such as NN → NYK, ΔN → NYK, and πB → KY. The kaon single-particle energies were computed using the Brown-Rho (BR) parametrization with an in-medium pion decay constant and the Ko-Li (KL) parametrization with the vacuum pion decay constant, solving Hamiltonian equations of motion that include both the time and spatial components of the kaon vector field. Kaon production was treated perturbatively, and the calculations were compared with KaoS and FOPI data on multiplicities, spectra, and collective flow observables.

## Context

This work extends previous transport studies of strangeness production at SIS energies, including the BUU calculations by Aichelin and Ko (1985) that first proposed kaon yields as a probe of the nuclear equation of state, and the QMD calculations by Fuchs et al. (2001) that favored a soft EOS (K=200 MeV) from kaon yield ratios. The paper specifically addresses the debate between the Tübingen QMD group (Zheng et al., 2004) and the HSD model (Bratkovskaya et al., 1997; Mishustin et al., 2004) regarding the strength of the kaon potential, with the former finding best agreement using the BR parametrization (U_K(ρ₀) ≃ 30 MeV) and the latter favoring the chiral perturbation theory value (U_K(ρ₀) ≃ 20 MeV). The results enable future studies to disentangle the effects of the kaon mean field from the Lorentz force generated by the spatial components of the vector field, and to refine the determination of the nuclear equation of state from kaon observables.
