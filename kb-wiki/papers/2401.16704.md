# Bottomonium suppression in 5.02 and 8.16 TeV p-Pb collisions

> We compute the suppression of Upsilon(1S), Upsilon(2S), and Upsilon(3S) states in p-Pb collisions relative to pp collisions, including nuclear parton distribution function (nPDF) effects, coherent energy loss, momentum broadening, and final-state interactions in the quark-gluon plasma. We employ the EPPS21 nPDFs and calculate the uncertainty resulting from variation over the associated error sets. To compute coherent energy loss and momentum broadening, we follow the approach of Arleo, Peigne, and collaborators. The 3+1D viscous hydrodynamical background evolution of the quark-gluon plasma is generated by anisotropic hydrodynamics. The in-medium suppression of bottomonium in the quark-gluon plasma is computed using a next-to-leading-order open quantum system framework formulated within potential nonrelativistic quantum chromodynamics. We find that inclusion of all these effects provides a reasonable description of experimental data from the ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, and LHCb collaborations for the suppression of Upsilon(1S), Upsilon(2S), and Upsilon(3S) as a function of both transverse momentum and rapidity.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a combination of cold nuclear matter effects (nPDFs, coherent energy loss, and momentum broadening) plus final-state interactions in a short-lived quark-gluon plasma provides a reasonable description of bottomonium suppression data in p-Pb collisions, but the abstract's claim of "reasonable description" is qualified by the finding that final-state QGP effects are a small correction for Upsilon(1S) while being necessary to achieve agreement with data for Upsilon(2S) and Upsilon(3S).

## Method

The authors compute bottomonium suppression in minimum-bias p-Pb collisions at 5.02 and 8.16 TeV using a three-stage model: (1) initial-state nuclear parton distribution function effects using EPPS21 nPDF sets within the Color Evaporation Model; (2) coherent energy loss and transverse momentum broadening following the formalism of Arleo, Peigne, and collaborators; and (3) real-time evolution of bottomonium states in a quark-gluon plasma background generated by 3+1D viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics, solved using a next-to-leading-order open quantum system Lindblad equation formulated within potential nonrelativistic QCD. The transport coefficients kappa and gamma are treated as parameters, with kappa allowed to vary to account for temperature-dependent behavior near the phase transition.

## Key numbers

- The EPPS21 nPDF uncertainty bands deviate from the central cross section by approximately 20%
- The intrinsic transverse momentum squared for the proton is 2.84 GeV^2 at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV and 3 GeV^2 at sqrt(s_NN) = 8.16 TeV
- The transport coefficient hat-kappa = 4 (dimensionless) was used for Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, while larger values around 5 were preferred for Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV
- The bottom quark mass used in the CEM fit is 4.65 +/- 0.09 GeV
- The effective path length for Pb is 10.41 fm, and for the proton is 1.5 fm
- The transport coefficient hat-q_0 = 0.075 GeV^2/fm

## Context

This work extends previous studies of bottomonium suppression in p-Pb collisions by combining state-of-the-art calculations of nPDF effects, coherent energy loss, and QGP-induced suppression using a 3+1D dissipative hydrodynamic background, going beyond earlier approaches such as Du et al. (2018), Dinh et al. (2019), Wen et al. (2022), Kim et al. (2022), and Chen et al. (2023), which used simpler 2+1D hydrodynamics or transport models. The results provide further evidence for the production of a short-lived quark-gluon plasma in p-Pb collisions and enable systematic comparisons with data from ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, and LHCb collaborations across different rapidity intervals.
