# Pseudo-spin as a Relativistic Symmetry

## Key claim
Pseudo-spin symmetry in nuclei could arise from nucleons moving in a relativistic mean field where the attractive scalar potential and repulsive vector potential are nearly equal in magnitude (V_s ~ V_v), but the symmetry is exact only in the unphysical limit where no bound Dirac valence states exist; quasi-degeneracy coexists with bound states only when the potentials are nearly but not exactly equal.

## Method
The author solves the Dirac equation for a nucleon in a spherical relativistic mean field, first analytically for a Coulomb potential with arbitrary scalar and vector components, then numerically for a spherical potential well. The eigenvalue equations for the two members of a pseudo-spin doublet (with opposite signs of the quantum number kappa) are derived and compared, showing they become identical when the difference between scalar and vector potentials (Delta = V_s - V_v) vanishes. The energy splitting between pseudo-spin partners is calculated in the limit of large scalar potential.

## Key numbers
- For the Coulomb potential with small delta (difference between scalar and vector potentials), the binding energy is approximately E_{n,kappa} ~ delta^2 / (2n^2) to leading order, with symmetry breaking appearing only in third order in delta.
- For the spherical potential well, the energy splitting between pseudo-spin partners is given by E_{n_r-1,kappa>0} - E_{n_r,kappa<0} ~ (2*tilde_l + 1) * Delta * E^{(0)}_{n,tilde_l} / ( (Y^{(0)}_{n,tilde_l})^2 * (Delta - E^{(0)}_{n,tilde_l}) ), where Delta = V_s - V_v.
- The potential well calculations use parameters V = 1.7, Delta = 0.3, and R = 33.5 (dimensionless units corresponding to the radius of Pb-208).

## Context
This work extends the long-standing observation of pseudo-spin degeneracy in heavy nuclei, first noted by Hecht and Adler (1969) and Arima, Harvey, and Shimizu (1969), which had previously lacked a fundamental explanation. The author shows that the near equality of scalar and vector potentials follows from relativistic mean field theories (Serot and Walecka, 1986), Skyrme-type interactions (Nikolaus, Hoch, and Madland, 1992), and QCD sum rules (Cohen, Furnstahl, and Griegel, 1991). The result enables further investigation of pseudo-spin symmetry in deformed nuclei and suggests that the symmetry may be enhanced in proton-rich nuclei with N ~ Z, which will be studied at new radioactive beam facilities.
