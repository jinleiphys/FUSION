# Weak decays of H-like 140Pr58+ and He-like 140Pr57+ ions

> The nuclear K-shell electron-capture (EC)and positron (beta+) decay constants, lambda_(EC) and lambda_(beta^+) of H-like 140Pr58+ and He-like 140Pr57+ ions, measured recently in the ESR ion storage ring at GSI, were calculated using standard weak interaction theory. The calculated ratios R = lambda_(EC)/lambda_(beta^+) of the decay constants agree with the experimental values within an accuracy better than 3 %.

## Key claim

Using standard weak interaction theory, the calculated ratios of electron-capture to positron decay constants for H-like 140Pr58+ and He-like 140Pr57+ ions agree with experimental values measured at the GSI storage ring within an accuracy better than 3%, but only when the hyperfine structure of the H-like ion (decay from the F=1/2 ground state) and the electron screening in the He-like ion are properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors calculated the K-shell electron-capture (EC) and positron (β+) decay constants for H-like 140Pr58+ and He-like 140Pr57+ ions using the standard weak interaction Hamiltonian with a Gamow-Teller transition. They employed Dirac wave functions for the bound electrons, a Woods-Saxon nuclear density distribution, and included the hyperfine structure of the H-like ion (decay from the F=1/2 hyperfine ground state only). The nuclear matrix element of the Gamow-Teller transition cancels in the ratios, allowing direct comparison with experimental data from the ESR at GSI.

## Key numbers

- Theoretical ratio R(H),th_EC/β+ = 1.40(4) for H-like 140Pr58+, compared to experimental R(H),exp_EC/β+ = 1.36(9)
- Theoretical ratio R(He),th_EC/β+ = 0.94(3) for He-like 140Pr57+, compared to experimental R(He),exp_EC/β+ = 0.96(8)
- Theoretical ratio R(H/He),th_EC/EC = 1.50(4) for the ratio of EC decay constants between H-like and He-like ions, compared to experimental R(H/He),exp_EC/EC = 1.49(8)
- Q-values: Q_H = (3348 ± 6) keV for H-like EC decay, Q_He = (3351 ± 6) keV for He-like EC decay, Q_β+ = (3396 ± 6) keV for both β+ decays
- Fermi integral f(Q_β+, Z-1) = (2.21 ± 0.03) MeV^5
- Hyperfine splitting ΔE = -1.12 eV for the H-like ion (with corrections dropped at 1% accuracy)
- Lifetime of the F=3/2 hyperfine state τ = 8.5 × 10^{-3} sec

## Context

This work extends earlier measurements of EC/β+ ratios in neutral 140Pr by Biryukov and Shimanskaya (R = 0.74(3)), Evans et al. (R = 0.90(8)), and Campbell et al. (R = 0.73(3)) to highly charged ions in the ESR storage ring at GSI. The calculations confirm the experimental finding by Litvinov et al. that the H-like ion decays 1.49(8) times faster than the He-like ion, and show that this ratio arises from both statistical factors (3/2) and electron screening effects, contrary to the simpler analysis by Patyk et al. and Litvinov et al. The work enables future measurements of EC and β+ decays in Li-like 140Pr56+ ions to further test the dependence on electron structure.
