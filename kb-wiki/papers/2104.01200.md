# Exploring the structure of $^{29}$Ne

> We apply a fully quantum mechanical Coulomb breakup theory under the aegis of post form finite-range distorted wave Born approximation to analyze the elastic Coulomb breakup of $^{29}$Ne on $^{208}$Pb at $244$\,MeV/u. We calculate several reaction observables to quantify its structural parameters. One-neutron removal cross-section is calculated to check the consistency of the ground state configuration of $^{29}$Ne with the available experimental data. A scrutiny of the parallel momentum distribution of the charged fragment reveals a full width at half maximum of $82$\,MeV/c, which is in good agreement with the experimental value and indicates a moderate halo for a nearly spherical $^{29}$Ne in the $^{28}$Ne$(0^+) \otimes 2p_{3/2}ν$ ground state. The energy-angular distributions and average momentum of the charged fragment point to the absence of post-acceleration effects in the breakup process, a desirable result for the elastic breakup.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the ground state of $^{29}$Ne has spin-parity $3/2^{-}$ with a dominant $2p_{3/2}$ neutron configuration, but this assignment is conditional on the parallel momentum distribution (FWHM of 82 MeV/c) being consistent with the experimental value of 98(12) MeV/c only after considering mixing of configurations of the same parity, and the authors note that deformation effects reduce the FWHM, potentially contradicting larger deformation values suggested by Nilsson model predictions.

## Method

The authors applied a post-form finite-range distorted wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) to calculate the elastic Coulomb breakup of $^{29}$Ne on a $^{208}$Pb target at 244 MeV/u. They computed one-neutron removal cross sections, parallel momentum distributions, relative energy spectra, and neutron energy-angular distributions for different possible ground state configurations ($2s_{1/2}$, $2p_{3/2}$, $1d_{3/2}$, $1f_{7/2}$) using a Woods-Saxon potential with adjustable depth to reproduce the one-neutron separation energy of 0.96 MeV. Deformation effects were introduced via an axially symmetric quadrupole deformed Woods-Saxon potential with varying $\beta_2$ values.

## Key numbers

- One-neutron separation energy of $^{29}$Ne: $S_n = 0.96(0.14)$ MeV
- FWHM of parallel momentum distribution for $^{28}$Ne($0^+$) $\otimes 2p_{3/2}\nu$ configuration at $\beta_2 = 0.0$: 82 MeV/c
- FWHM of parallel momentum distribution for $^{28}$Ne($0^+$) $\otimes 2s_{1/2}\nu$ configuration: 52 MeV/c
- FWHM of parallel momentum distribution for $^{28}$Ne($0^+$) $\otimes 1d_{3/2}\nu$ configuration: 155 MeV/c
- FWHM of parallel momentum distribution for $^{28}$Ne($0^+$) $\otimes 1f_{7/2}\nu$ configuration: 207 MeV/c
- Experimental FWHM from Koba (2016): 98(12) MeV/c
- FWHM for mixed $J^{\pi} = 3/2^{-}$ configuration: 93 MeV/c
- FWHM for mixed $J^{\pi} = 1/2^{+}$ configuration: approximately 180 MeV/c
- Quadrupole deformation range consistent with $2p_{3/2}$ configuration: $0.0 \leq \beta_2 \leq 0.78$
- FWHM values for $^{28}$Ne($0^+$) $\otimes 2p_{3/2}\nu$ at different $\beta_2$: 82 MeV/c ($\beta_2=0.0$), 76 MeV/c ($\beta_2=0.1$), 71 MeV/c ($\beta_2=0.2$), 68 MeV/c ($\beta_2=0.3$), 66 MeV/c ($\beta_2=0.4$)
- Woods-Saxon potential depths: 33.8 MeV ($2s_{1/2}$), 54.9 MeV ($2p_{3/2}$), 38.6 MeV ($1d_{3/2}$), 52.9 MeV ($1f_{7/2}$)

## Context

This work extends the experimental study of Koba (2016), which measured one-neutron removal cross sections on C and Pb targets and concluded a $3/2^{-}$ ground state with moderate $p$-wave halo for $^{29}$Ne. The calculations use the FRDWBA formalism previously applied to $^{31}$Ne by Shubhchintak (2014) and $^{37}$Mg by Shubhchintak (2015), and the results confirm the $3/2^{-}$ assignment while providing constraints on the quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta_2$. The study enables future experimental measurements of relative energy spectra to independently distinguish between $s$-wave and $p$-wave ground state configurations of $^{29}$Ne.
