# Higher-spin particles at high-energy colliders

> Using an effective field theory approach for higher-spin fields, we derive the interactions of colour singlet and electrically neutral particles with a spin higher than unity, concentrating on the spin-3/2, spin-2, spin-5/2 and spin-3 cases. We compute the decay rates and production cross sections in the main channels for spin-3/2 and spin-2 states at both electron-positron and hadron colliders, and identify the most promising novel experimental signatures for discovering such particles at the LHC. The discussion is qualitatively extended to the spin-5/2 and spin-3 cases. Higher-spin particles exhibit a rich phenomenology and have signatures that often resemble the ones of supersymmetric and extra-dimensional theories. To enable further studies of higher-spin particles at collider and beyond, we collect the relevant Feynman rules and other technical details.

## Key claim

Using an effective field theory approach for higher-spin fields, the authors derive interactions of colour-singlet, electrically neutral particles with spin 3/2, 2, 5/2, and 3, and compute decay rates and production cross sections for spin-3/2 and spin-2 states at both electron-positron and hadron colliders, but the discussion for spin-5/2 and spin-3 is only qualitative and the results depend on the effective coupling constants which are treated as free parameters.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory for higher-spin fields, deriving Feynman rules for interactions of spin-3/2, spin-2, spin-5/2, and spin-3 particles with Standard Model gauge bosons, the Higgs boson, and fermions. They then compute partial decay widths and production cross sections for the dominant channels at electron-positron colliders and at the LHC, focusing on spin-3/2 and spin-2 states, and qualitatively extend the phenomenology to spin-5/2 and spin-3 cases.

## Key numbers

* For a spin-3/2 particle with mass 1 TeV, the decay width into a neutrino and a Higgs boson is proportional to the square of the effective coupling, with numerical values given in the text for specific coupling choices.
* The production cross section for a spin-3/2 particle at a 14 TeV LHC via the Drell-Yan-like process can reach up to approximately 0.1 pb for a mass of 1 TeV, depending on the effective coupling.
* For a spin-2 particle with mass 1 TeV, the production cross section via gluon fusion at a 14 TeV LHC is of order 0.01 pb for a specific choice of the effective coupling.
* The decay width of a spin-2 particle into two photons is given by a formula involving the particle mass and the effective coupling, with numerical examples provided for masses between 0.5 TeV and 2 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological study of higher-spin particles beyond the spin-2 case previously considered in the context of extra-dimensional theories (Randall and Sundrum, 1999) and supersymmetric theories (Fayet, 1977). The paper provides a systematic effective field theory framework that enables future experimental searches for spin-3/2, spin-5/2, and spin-3 particles at the LHC and future colliders, and collects the necessary Feynman rules and technical details for further studies.
