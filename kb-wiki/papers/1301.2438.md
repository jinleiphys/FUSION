# Unified description of dense matter in neutron stars and magnetars

> We have recently developed a set of equations of state based on the nuclear energy density functional theory providing a unified description of the different regions constituting the interior of neutron stars and magnetars. The nuclear functionals, which were constructed from generalized Skyrme effective nucleon-nucleon interactions, yield not only an excellent fit to essentially all experimental atomic mass data but were also constrained to reproduce the neutron-matter equation of state as obtained from realistic many-body calculations.

## Key claim
The authors have developed a set of unified equations of state for neutron stars and magnetars based on nuclear energy density functionals, but the global structure of a magnetar with a surface magnetic field around 10^15 G remains almost indistinguishable from that of an ordinary neutron star; only for fields approaching 10^16 G do the composition and properties of the outer crust deviate substantially.

## Method
The authors constructed three Brussels-Montreal nuclear energy density functionals (BSk19, BSk20, BSk21) from generalized Skyrme effective nucleon-nucleon interactions, fitting essentially all measured atomic masses via the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method with an rms deviation of 0.58 MeV. They constrained these functionals to reproduce three different representative neutron-matter equations of state from microscopic many-body calculations, then used them to calculate consistently the properties of all regions of a cold non-accreting neutron star from surface to center. For magnetars, they recalculated the outer crust equation of state using the BSk21 functional with the electron motion quantized into Landau levels due to the strong magnetic field.

## Key numbers
- The Brussels-Montreal energy density functionals BSk19, BSk20, and BSk21 fit atomic mass data with an rms deviation of 0.58 MeV.
- The neutron drip transition in the outer crust occurs at a density of approximately 4 x 10^11 g/cm^3 for zero magnetic field.
- Surface magnetic fields of order 10^14 to 10^15 G have been estimated in soft gamma-ray repeaters and anomalous X-ray pulsars.
- Magnetic fields up to approximately 10^17 G could be generated via dynamo effects in hot newly-born neutron stars according to the magnetar model.
- Deviations in the equilibrium nuclide sequence become particularly significant for magnetic fields B ~ 10^16 G.
- For B ~ 10^15 G, only the equation of state in the outer crust is affected by the magnetic field.

## Context
This work extends the Brussels-Montreal unified equations of state (Chamel 2011, Pearson 2011, Pearson 2012) to magnetars by incorporating magnetic field effects in the outer crust (Chamel 2012). The resulting equations of state are consistent with radius constraints from observations of X-ray bursters and low-mass X-ray binaries (Steiner 2010), and only the BSk20 and BSk21 functionals are stiff enough to support neutron stars as massive as PSR J1614-2230. The study enables future calculations of magnetar structure and crust properties under extreme magnetic fields, though effects of magnetic fields on nuclei themselves, neglected here, could become important for B greater than 10^17 G (Pena 2011).
