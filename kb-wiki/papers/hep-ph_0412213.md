# Color superconducting 2SC+s quark matter and gapless modes at finite temperatures

> We investigate the phase diagram of color superconducting quark matter with strange quarks (2SC+s quark matter) in beta equliibrium at zero as well as finite temperatures within a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. The variational method as used here allows us to investigate simultaneous formation of condensates in quark--antiquark as well as in diquark channels. Color and electric charge neutrality conditions are imposed in the calculation of the thermodynamic potential. Medium dependance of strange quark mass plays a sensitve role in maintaining charge neutrality conditions. At zero temperature the system goes from gapless phase to usual BCS phase through an intermediate normal phase as density is increased. The gapless modes show a smooth behaviour with respect to temperature vanishing above a critical temperature which is larger than the BCS transition temperature. We observe a sharp transition from gapless superconducting phase to the BCS phase as density is increased for the color neutral matter at zero temperature. As temperature is increased this however becomes a smooth transition.

## Key claim

The paper investigates the phase diagram of color superconducting 2SC+s quark matter under beta equilibrium, showing that at zero temperature the system transitions from a gapless phase to a BCS phase through an intermediate normal phase as density increases, with the gapless modes vanishing above a critical temperature that exceeds the BCS transition temperature.

## Method

The authors use a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a variational approach that allows simultaneous formation of quark-antiquark condensates (chiral symmetry breaking) and diquark condensates (color superconductivity). Color and electric charge neutrality conditions are imposed through chemical potentials for baryon number, electric charge, and color charges (μ₃ and μ₈), and the thermodynamic potential is minimized to obtain self-consistent gap equations for the constituent quark masses and the superconducting gap. Finite temperature effects are incorporated using thermofield dynamics.

## Key numbers

- The NJL model parameters are chosen with cutoff Λ = 0.6533 GeV and scalar coupling G_s = 5.0163 GeV⁻², fitting f_π = 93 MeV and ⟨ūu⟩¹/³ = -250 MeV
- The current quark mass for strange quarks is taken as m_s = 120 MeV
- At zero temperature and density, constituent quark masses are M₁ = M₂ = 0.313 GeV for u,d quarks and M₃ = 0.541 GeV for strange quarks
- At zero temperature, a first order phase transition occurs at baryon chemical potential μ_B ≈ 960 MeV, where the diquark gap jumps from zero to about 80 MeV
- The gapless phase exists within a window of about 80 MeV in baryon chemical potential
- Strange quark number densities reach about 40% of up quark number density in the gapless phase

## Context

This work extends previous studies of two-flavor color superconducting quark matter (2SC) that showed gapless modes (g2SC) arising from Fermi momentum differences when charge and color neutrality are imposed (Igor and Mishra, 2004). It generalizes the variational approach of Mishra (2004) to include finite temperature effects, which is relevant for proto-neutron star physics. The results enable further investigation of neutron star cooling through neutrino emission from gapless superconducting phases.
