# Quantum-Number Exotic Tetraquarks at Large $N_c$ in QCD(AS)

> It is shown that the large $N_c$ limit of QCD with quarks in the two-index antisymmetric color representation [QCD(AS)] has narrow tetraquark states with exotic flavor quantum numbers. They decay into mesons with a width that is parametrically $O(1/N_c^2)$. Tetraquarks with non-exotic quantum numbers mix at leading order with mesons of the same overall quantum numbers. QCD(AS) at $N_c=3$ corresponds to ordinary QCD; its large $N_c$ limit represents an alternative starting point for a $1/N_c$ expansion to the standard one with quarks in the fundamental color representation.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the large \(N_c\) limit of QCD with quarks in the two-index antisymmetric color representation [QCD(AS)], narrow tetraquark states with exotic flavor quantum numbers necessarily exist, contrary to the long-standing belief that tetraquarks are forbidden at large \(N_c\) in standard QCD. The existence is conditional on using QCD(AS) rather than the standard fundamental-representation large \(N_c\) expansion, and on restricting attention to tetraquarks with exotic quantum numbers that cannot be formed from a single \(\overline{q}q\) pair.

## Method

The authors perform a 't Hooft double-line color-flow analysis adapted to QCD(AS), where quarks as well as gluons are represented by double lines. They construct a tetraquark source operator in which the color indices are entangled such that the source cannot be split into two color singlets for \(N_c > 3\), and then compute the \(N_c\) scaling of the two-point correlation function for this source. By examining the color structure of intermediate states exposed by cutting leading-order planar diagrams, they show that all cuts have a single-color trace structure, implying that the correlation function is saturated by single-hadron states; if the source creates states with exotic quantum numbers, these must be narrow tetraquarks.

## Key numbers

- The two-point correlation function for the tetraquark source scales as \(N_c^4\) at leading order.
- The tetraquark-meson-meson interaction vertex scales as \(1/N_c\).
- The decay width of a tetraquark into two mesons scales as \(O(1/N_c^2)\), making the tetraquark parametrically narrow at large \(N_c\).
- A general hadronic vertex with \(n\) hadrons (tetraquarks, glueballs, hybrids, and mesons) scales as \(\Gamma_n \sim N_c^{2-n}\).
- A diagram containing \(n_T\) tetraquark sources and any number of meson, hybrid, and gluon sources scales as \(D \sim N_c^{2 + n_T}\).

## Context

This work extends the recent critique by Weinberg (2013) showing that standard arguments against tetraquarks at large \(N_c\) are flawed, but does so in the alternative framework of QCD(AS) rather than the standard fundamental-representation large \(N_c\) expansion. The paper resolves the open question left by Weinberg's analysis by providing a specific large \(N_c\) limit in which narrow tetraquarks with exotic quantum numbers are proven to exist. The result enables future study of tetraquark phenomenology in QCD(AS), which at \(N_c=3\) is identical to ordinary QCD, and suggests that the interpretation of scalar mesons such as the \(f_0(980)\) as tetraquarks (as proposed by Jaffe (1976) and many others) may be consistent with large \(N_c\) reasoning in this alternative expansion.
