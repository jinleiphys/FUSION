# Hard scale dependent gluon density, saturation and forward-forward dijet production at the LHC

> We propose a method to introduce Sudakov effects to the unintegrated gluon density promoting it to be hard scale dependent. The advantage of the approach is that it guarantees that the gluon density is positive definite and that the Sudakov effects cancel on integrated level. As a case study we apply the method to calculate angular correlations and $R_{pA}$ ratio for p+p vs. p+Pb collision in the production of forward-forward dijets.

# Hard scale dependent gluon density, saturation and forward-forward dijet production at the LHC

## Key claim
The paper proposes a method to introduce Sudakov effects into the unintegrated gluon density, making it dependent on the hard scale of the process, while guaranteeing that the gluon density remains positive definite and that the Sudakov effects cancel at the integrated level. The method is applied to forward-forward dijet production at the LHC, where the hard scale dependence leads to a suppression of the nuclear modification ratio R_pA in the region where saturation effects are relevant.

## Method
The author constructs a hard scale dependent unintegrated gluon density F(x,k^2,mu^2) by multiplying the standard BK-evolved unintegrated gluon density F(x,k^2) with a Sudakov form factor T_s(mu^2,k^2) for k^2 < mu^2, while keeping F(x,k^2) unchanged for k^2 > mu^2. The construction is normalized so that the integrated gluon density xg(x,mu^2) is preserved. The resulting gluon density is used in the hybrid high-energy factorization formula to compute angular correlations and the R_pA ratio for forward-forward dijet production in p+p collisions at 7 TeV and p+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers
- The unintegrated gluon density is evaluated at x = 10^{-5} for both proton and lead targets, with hard scales mu^2 = 20 GeV^2 and mu^2 = 200 GeV^2 shown in the figures.
- The input gluon density for the BK evolution is given by xg(x,k_0^2=1) = 0.994 (1 + 82.1 x)^{18.6} at the starting scale k_0^2 = 1 GeV^2.
- The kinematic cuts for the dijet cross section are p_{t1}, p_{t2} > 20 GeV and rapidities 4.9 > y_1, y_2 > 3.2, with a jet algorithm radius R = sqrt((Delta y)^2 + (Delta phi)^2) > 0.5.
- The p+p collision energy is 7 TeV, while the p+Pb collision energy is 5.02 TeV.
- The hard scale for the dijet process is defined as mu = (p_{t1} + p_{t2})/2.

## Context
This work extends the approach of vanHameren (2014) which introduced Sudakov effects at the cross-section level, by instead incorporating them directly into the unintegrated gluon density. The method is motivated by the CCFM evolution equation (Ciafaloni 1987, Catani 1989) and the Kimber-Martin-Ryskin formalism (Kimber 2001) for including coherence effects. The momentum-space BK equation used includes kinematic constraints (Kwiecinski 1996) and running coupling corrections, and has been previously applied to F_2 structure function data (Kutak 2012) and forward-central dijet correlations (vanHameren 2014). The results enable predictions for forward-forward dijet production at the LHC, where the gluon density is probed at x approximately 10^{-5}, and demonstrate that the hard scale dependence reduces the R_pA ratio in the saturation-dominated region.
