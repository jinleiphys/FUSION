# Bethe Ansatz approach to the pairing fluctuations in the mesoscopic regime

> We review the exact treatment of the pairing correlation functions in the canonical ensemble. The key for the calculations has been provided by relating the discrete BCS model to known integrable theories corresponding to the so called Gaudin magnets with suitable boundary terms. In the present case the correlation functions can be accessed beyond the formal level, allowing the description of the cross-over from few electrons to the thermodynamic limit. In particular, we summarize the results on the finite size scaling behavior of the canonical pairing clarifying some puzzles emerged in the past. Some recent developments and applications are outlined.

## Key claim

The paper reviews an exact method for computing pairing correlation functions in the canonical ensemble for the discrete BCS model, showing that the crossover from few electrons to the thermodynamic limit can be described analytically, but only because the BCS model is related to twisted Gaudin magnets and the correlation functions are accessed via determinant formulas that become tractable through reduction formulas.

## Method

The authors connect the discrete BCS Hamiltonian to the integrable twisted Gaudin magnet by expressing the BCS Hamiltonian in terms of the Gaudin integrals of motion, which are obtained from the quantum inverse scattering method via a quasiclassical expansion of the transfer matrix. Correlation functions are then computed using Sklyanin's generating function technique, which expresses them as sums over partitions of the Bethe rapidities, and these are further simplified into determinant representations using the Slavnov formula for scalar products and the solution of the inverse problem. Finally, reduction formulas developed by Faribault, Calabrese, and Caux reduce the complexity of the two-point correlation functions to sums over only N determinants.

## Key numbers

* The mesoscopic regime for 6Li at quantum degeneracy can be achieved with δϵ ∼ Δ ∼ 10−12 eV, corresponding to a confinement frequency of ω ∼ 1 kHz.
* The pairing correlation function u_{lm}(E,F) for l ≠ m is given by a formula involving sums over partitions, with the explicit expression shown in Eq. (problem) of the paper.
* The norm of Bethe states is given by det_N G_N, where the Gaudin matrix G has diagonal elements ∑_i 2/(e_a − ε_i)^2 − ∑_{c≠a} 2/(e_a − e_c)^2 and off-diagonal elements 2/(e_a − e_b)^2.

## Context

This work extends the exact solution of the BCS model by Richardson and Sherman (1964) and the later connection to Gaudin magnets by Gaudin (1967), which remained largely unnoticed until the late 1990s when it was rediscovered for metallic grains. The paper reviews the subsequent development of correlation function calculations using the quantum inverse scattering method, building on the work of Sklyanin, Zhou et al., and Faribault, Calabrese, and Caux, and it enables exact studies of pairing fluctuations in mesoscopic systems such as metallic nanograins, nuclei, and confined degenerate Fermi gases.
