# The $\bar{K} N \rightarrow K Îž$ reaction in coupled channel chiral models up to next-to-leading order

> The meson-baryon interaction in s-wave in the strangeness S=-1 sector has been studied, employing a chiral SU(3) Lagrangian up to next-to-leading order (NLO) and implementing unitarization in coupled channels. The parameters of the Lagrangian have been fitted to a large set of experimental data in different two-body channels, paying special attention to the $\bar{K} N \rightarrow K Îž$ reaction, which is particularly sensitive to the NLO terms. With the aim of improving the model in the $KÎž$ production channels, effects of the high spin hyperon resonances $Î£(2030)$ and $Î£(2250)$ have been taken into account phenomenologically.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the $\bar{K} N \rightarrow K \Xi$ reaction is especially sensitive to the next-to-leading order (NLO) terms of the chiral Lagrangian, because the lowest-order Weinberg-Tomozawa term gives zero direct contribution to these channels; however, even with NLO terms included, the unitary coupled-channel model alone cannot fully reproduce the cross section structures near 2 GeV and 2.2 GeV, requiring the phenomenological addition of high-spin hyperon resonances $\Sigma(2030)$ and $\Sigma(2250)$.

## Method

The authors employed a chiral SU(3) Lagrangian up to next-to-leading order (NLO) and implemented unitarization in coupled channels via the Bethe-Salpeter equation, using dimensional regularization with a scale of 1 GeV. They fitted the parameters (pion decay constant, six subtraction constants, and seven NLO low-energy constants) to 161 experimental cross section points for $K^- p$ scattering and related channels, plus branching ratios and the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen shift and width, with a normalized chi-squared procedure that assigns equal weight to different observables. They then added phenomenological resonant contributions from the $\Sigma(2030)$ and $\Sigma(2250)$ hyperons to improve the description of the $K\Xi$ production channels.

## Key numbers

* The NLO fit (including $K\Xi$ data) yields a pion decay constant ratio $f/f_\pi = 1.197 \pm 0.008$, where $f_\pi = 93$ MeV.
* The $K^- p$ scattering length from the NLO fit is $a_{K^- p} = -0.73 + i\,0.85$ fm, compared to the experimental value $-0.66 + i\,0.81$ fm.
* The SIDDHARTA values used are $\Delta E_{1s} = 283 \pm 36$ eV and $\Gamma_{1s} = 541 \pm 92$ eV for kaonic hydrogen.
* The branching ratios used are $\gamma = 2.36 \pm 0.04$, $R_n = 0.664 \pm 0.011$, and $R_c = 0.189 \pm 0.015$.
* The WT (no $K\Xi$) fit predicts $K^- p \rightarrow K\Xi$ cross sections below 0.015 mb, about one order of magnitude smaller than measured values.
* The NLO fit (including $K\Xi$ data) achieves $\chi^2_{\rm d.o.f.} = 0.65$, while the WT fit (including $K\Xi$ data) gives $\chi^2_{\rm d.o.f.} = 2.57$.
* The subtraction constant $a_{\pi\Lambda}$ in the WT fit reaches $-248.6 \times 10^{-3}$, one order of magnitude larger than natural size.

## Context

This work extends previous unitarized chiral perturbation theory studies of the $S=-1$ meson-baryon interaction, such as those by Oset and Ramos (1998) and the two-pole description of the $\Lambda(1405)$, by incorporating the $K\Xi$ production channels as a new constraint. It builds on the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen measurements and the CLAS data on $\Lambda(1405)$ production, and it is motivated by resonance-based models for $\Xi$ photoproduction (Nakayama 2006, Man 2011) and strong production (Sharov 2011, Shyam 2011, Jackson 2014) that identified contributions from $\Sigma(2030)$ and $\Sigma(2250)$. The results enable more precise determination of the NLO low-energy constants and provide a framework for future studies of cascade hyperon production in meson-baryon scattering.
