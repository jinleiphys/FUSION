# pQCD phenomenology of elastic ed scattering

> Electron-deuteron elastic scattering data ($A(Q^2)$ and $B(Q^2)$ structure functions and polarization observables $t_{20}$, $t_{21}$ and $t_{22}$) are fit with a model that respects asymptotic properties of pQCD at high momentum transfer. The data analysis shows that pQCD starts from $Q^2=3.5 \rm (GeV/c)^2$. Predictions for the magnetic structure function $B(Q^2)$ and the polarization observables at high momentum transfer are given.

## Key claim

The paper claims that perturbative QCD (pQCD) behavior in elastic electron-deuteron scattering begins at a momentum transfer of \(Q^2 = 3.5\ \rm (GeV/c)^2\), but the analysis of the \(A(Q^2)\) structure function alone suggests pQCD works from a lower region between 1 and 2 \(\rm (GeV/c)^2\), while for the magnetic form factor \(B(Q^2)\) pQCD should start between 2 and 3 \(\rm (GeV/c)^2\).

## Method

The authors fit a model to experimental data for the elastic electron-deuteron scattering structure functions \(A(Q^2)\) and \(B(Q^2)\) and the tensor polarization observables \(t_{20}\), \(t_{21}\), and \(t_{22}\). The model parameterizes the helicity transition amplitudes \(J_{00}^{+}\), \(J_{10}^{+}\), and \(J_{1-1}^{+}\) using a reduced amplitude formalism with a dipole nucleon form factor, connecting a low-\(Q^2\) region (parameterized by sums of poles) to a high-\(Q^2\) asymptotic region (governed by pQCD power laws with logarithmic corrections) via smooth matching conditions at a fitted transition scale \(Q^2_{\rm QCD}\). The fit uses 200 data points with 10 independent parameters, yielding \(\chi^2 = 399\).

## Key numbers

- The transition scale where pQCD behavior begins is determined to be \(Q^2_{\rm QCD} = 3.5\ \rm (GeV/c)^2\).
- The QCD cutoff parameter \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\) is fixed at 200 MeV.
- The fit uses 200 data points with 10 independent parameters, yielding \(\chi^2 = 399\).
- The deuteron magnetic moment in the fit constraints is \(\mu_d = 0.857406\ M/m_p\) (in deuteron magnetons) and the deuteron quadrupole moment is \(Q_d = 25.84\) in units of \(M^2/e\).
- The asymptotic power behavior of the leading helicity amplitude is \(J_{00}^{+} \sim (\Lambda_{\rm QCD}/Q)^{10}\).
- The dipole form factor parameter \(\mu^2\) is different from the free nucleon value of \(0.71\ \rm (GeV/c)^2\).

## Context

This work extends earlier parameterizations by Kobushkin and Syamtomov (1999) that connected nucleon-meson and pQCD regions, now including logarithmic corrections to the power behavior and incorporating new high-precision data from TJNAF on \(A(Q^2)\) up to \(Q^2 = 6\ \rm (GeV/c)^2\) (Alexa, Abbott, 1999-2000) and polarization observables up to \(Q^2 = 1.7\ \rm (GeV/c)^2\) (Abbott, 2000). The model enables predictions for the magnetic structure function \(B(Q^2)\) and polarization observables at higher momentum transfers where pQCD is expected to dominate, which can be tested in future experiments.
