# Present Constraints on the H-dibaryon at the Physical Point from Lattice QCD

> The current constraints from lattice QCD on the existence of the H-dibaryon are discussed. With only two significant lattice QCD calculations of the H-dibaryon binding energy at approximately the same lattice spacing, the forms of the chiral and continuum extrapolations to the physical point are not determined. In this brief report, we consider the constraints on the H-dibaryon imposed by two simple chiral extrapolations. In both instances, the extrapolation to the physical pion mass allows for a bound H-dibaryon or a near-threshold scattering state. Further lattice QCD calculations are required to clarify this situation.

## Key claim

The paper finds that, using two simple chiral extrapolation forms (quadratic and linear in pion mass), the current lattice QCD results from NPLQCD and HALQCD are not precise enough to determine whether the H-dibaryon is bound at the physical pion mass; both a bound state and a near-threshold scattering state remain allowed.

## Method

The authors take the two most statistically significant lattice QCD calculations of the H-dibaryon binding energy, from NPLQCD (n_f=2+1, m_pi ~ 390 MeV, single lattice spacing b ~ 0.123 fm, four volumes) and HALQCD (n_f=3, m_pi ~ 837 MeV, b ~ 0.121 fm, three volumes), and perform chiral extrapolations to the physical pion mass using two simple functional forms: a quadratic form B_H(m_pi) = B_0 + d_1 m_pi^2 and a linear form B_H(m_pi) = B̃_0 + c_1 m_pi. The extrapolations are performed using both statistical uncertainties alone and statistical plus systematic uncertainties combined in quadrature.

## Key numbers

- NPLQCD binding energy at m_pi ~ 390 MeV: B_H = 13.2 ± 1.8 ± 4.0 MeV (updated from the original 16.6 ± 2.1 ± 4.6 MeV)
- HALQCD binding energy at m_pi ~ 837 MeV: B_H = 37.4 ± 4.4 ± 7.3 MeV
- Quadratic chiral extrapolation to physical pion mass: B_H = 7.4 ± 2.1 ± 5.8 MeV
- Linear chiral extrapolation to physical pion mass: B_H = -0.2 ± 3.3 ± 7.3 MeV
- NPLQCD preliminary result at m_pi ~ 230 MeV (single volume, 4 fm, ~9×10^4 measurements): B_H = -0.6 ± 8.9 ± 10.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends the recent lattice QCD calculations by the NPLQCD collaboration (Beane et al., 2010) and the HALQCD collaboration (Inoue et al., 2010), which both found a bound H-dibaryon at heavier-than-physical quark masses. The paper addresses the long-standing question, first raised by Jaffe (1977) using the MIT bag model, of whether a bound six-quark state (uuddss) exists in nature. The results enable future lattice QCD studies to focus on calculations at lighter pion masses and multiple lattice spacings to resolve the binding status, and they provide context for experimental searches that have found no bound state between 2.136 and 2.231 GeV (Trattner et al., 2006) but have suggested a near-threshold resonance (Yoon et al., 2007).
