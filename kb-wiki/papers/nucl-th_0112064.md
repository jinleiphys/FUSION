# Dissociation of Heavy Quarkonia in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Using a temperature-dependent potential obtained from lattice gauge calculations, we study the stability of heavy quarkonia in the quark-gluon plasma. We find that only the Upsilon(1S) and the eta_b(1S) are bound in the quark-gluon plasma, and have a small binding energy. The quark-gluon plasma may be revealed by an Upsilon(1S) dilepton peak with an invariant mass close to twice the current b quark mass, which is lower than the Upsilon(1S) mass in free space. The quarkonia Upsilon(1S) and eta_b(1S) can dissociate by collision with quarks and gluons in the quark-gluon plasma. We evaluate the dissociation cross section of the Upsilon(1S) due to gluon absorption. The Upsilon(1S) and the eta_b(1S) can also dissociate spontaneously at temperatures above the dissociation temperature 1.11T_c, where T_c is the quark-gluon plasma phase transition temperature. At temperatures slightly above the dissociation temperature these states appear as resonances, which provides another signature for the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

Using a temperature-dependent potential extracted from lattice gauge calculations, the author finds that only the $\Upsilon(1S)$ and $\eta_b(1S)$ are bound in the quark-gluon plasma, but with the important qualification that this result applies specifically to the non-perturbative region of temperatures slightly above $T_c$, where color-singlet and color-octet states are not approximate eigenstates and the effective interaction potential is obtained directly from the Polyakov loop rather than from a perturbative separation procedure.

## Method

The author solves the Schrödinger equation for heavy quarkonium states using a screened Yukawa potential $V_{Q\bar Q}(r,T) = -\frac{4}{3}\alpha_{\rm eff}(T) e^{-\mu(T) r}/r$, with parameters $\alpha_{\rm eff}(T)=0.20$, $\mu(T)=\mu_0 + \mu_T (T/T_c-1)$, $\mu_0=0.323$ GeV, and $\mu_T=3.04$ GeV, fitted to lattice gauge calculations of Karsch et al. (2000). The dissociation cross section for $\Upsilon(1S)+g \to b+\bar b$ is evaluated using a multipole expansion of the gluon field analogous to electromagnetic photodissociation, with the overlap integral computed from numerically obtained bound and continuum wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Dissociation temperature of $\Upsilon(1S)$ and $\eta_b(1S)$: $T_d = 1.11 T_c$, where $T_c$ is the quark-gluon plasma phase transition temperature
- Root-mean-squared radius of $\Upsilon(1S)$ at $T=T_c$: 0.80 fm; at $T=1.10 T_c$ (close to dissociation): 3.3 fm
- Binding energy of $\Upsilon(1S)$ near dissociation: approximately 0.01 GeV (order of magnitude)
- Screening mass parameter at $T=T_c$: $\mu_0 = 0.323$ GeV; temperature coefficient: $\mu_T = 3.04$ GeV
- Effective coupling constant in the quark-gluon plasma: $\alpha_{\rm eff}(T) = 0.20$
- Current $b$ quark mass used: $m_b = 4.2$ GeV; current $c$ quark mass: $m_c = 1.25$ GeV
- At $T=T_d+0.02 T_c$, the $\Upsilon(1S)$ resonance occurs at $\epsilon=0.008$ GeV with half-width separation of about 0.30 GeV on the higher energy side
- At $T=T_d+0.04 T_c$, the resonance occurs at $\epsilon=0.018$ GeV with half-width separation of about 0.43 GeV on the higher energy side

## Context

This work extends the study of heavy quarkonium dissociation in the quark-gluon plasma beyond the perturbative color-singlet treatment of Digal, Petreczky, and Satz (2001), who obtained dissociation temperatures of 2.31$T_c$ for color-singlet $\Upsilon(1S)$ and 1.13$T_c$ for color-singlet $\chi_b$. The author argues that in the non-perturbative region near $T_c$, the color states are strongly mixed and the effective potential from the Polyakov loop (Karsch et al., 2000) should be used directly. The results enable the use of $\Upsilon(1S)$ dilepton peaks at invariant masses close to twice the current $b$ quark mass as a signature for quark-gluon plasma formation, and the resonance behavior above $T_d$ provides an additional plasma diagnostic.
