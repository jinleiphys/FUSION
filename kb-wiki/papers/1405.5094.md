# Pion-assisted charmed dibaryon candidate

> The Lambda_c(2286)-N system is studied in a chiral constituent quark model and the resulting s-wave interaction is used in separable form within three-body models of the pi-Lambda_c-N system with quantum numbers (C,I,JP)=(+1,3/2,2+). Separable interactions are also used for the dominant p-wave pion-baryon channels dominated by the Delta(1232) and Sigma_c(2520) resonances. Faddeev equations with relativistic kinematics are solved on the real axis to search for bound states and in the complex plane to search for three-body resonances. Some of the models considered generate a very narrow bound state, requiring isospin violation for its strong decay. Other models lead to a narrow resonance (Gamma < 0.4 MeV) for resonance mass below the Sigma_c(2455)-N threshold. This would be the lowest-lying C=+1 dibaryon, with mass estimated approximately as 3370+/-15 MeV.

# Pion-assisted charmed dibaryon candidate

## Key claim

The $(I,J^P)=(\frac{3}{2},2^+)$ state of the $\pi\Lambda_c N$ system is a strong candidate for the lowest-lying charmed dibaryon, but the existence of a bound state versus a narrow resonance depends on the specific choice of the $\Lambda_c N$ interaction model and the $\pi\Lambda_c$ form factor parameters; the most physically acceptable models produce a narrow resonance (width less than 0.2 MeV) rather than a true bound state, with mass estimated at approximately 3370±15 MeV.

## Method

The authors solved Faddeev equations with relativistic kinematics for the $\pi\Lambda_c N$ three-body system in the $(C,I,J^P)=(+1,3/2,2^+)$ channel, using separable $p$-wave interactions for the $\pi N$ and $\pi\Lambda_c$ subsystems fitted to the $\Delta(1232)$ and $\Sigma_c(2520)$ resonances, and a rank-2 separable $s$-wave interaction for the $\Lambda_c N$ subsystem derived from a chiral constituent quark model (CCQM) with three values of the charmed quark oscillator parameter $b_c$ (0.2, 0.5, and 0.8 fm). Bound states were searched on the real axis and resonances in the complex plane.

## Key numbers

- $\Lambda_c(2286)$ mass: 2286 MeV (charmed baryon replacing $\Lambda(1116)$)
- $\Sigma_c(2520)$ resonance: mass 2520 MeV, width used to fit $\pi\Lambda_c$ interaction
- $\Sigma_c(2455)N$ threshold lies approximately 30 MeV above the $\pi\Lambda_c N$ threshold
- $\pi\Lambda_c N$ threshold mass: $E_{\rm th} \approx 3363$ MeV
- Bound-state energies for $b_c=0.2$ fm models: from $-14.6$ MeV to $-2.3$ MeV relative to threshold (for $A=0.1$ to $A=0.0$ fm$^2$)
- Resonance energies for $b_c=0.5$ fm models: from $26.3-i0.18$ MeV to $46.2-i1.10$ MeV relative to threshold
- Resonance energies for $b_c=0.8$ fm models: from $54.8-i1.16$ MeV to $68.2-i3.05$ MeV relative to threshold
- Narrow resonance width: $\Gamma < 0.4$ MeV for resonance mass below the $\Sigma_c(2455)N$ threshold (Re $E < 27$ MeV)
- Preferred resonance mass range: $3363 \lesssim \sqrt{s} \lesssim 3383$ MeV (up to about 20 MeV above threshold)
- $\Lambda_c N$ scattering lengths: $a = -1.33$ fm ($b_c=0.2$ fm), $a = -0.79$ fm ($b_c=0.5$ fm), $a = -0.63$ fm ($b_c=0.8$ fm)
- $\pi N$ $p$-wave form factor zero: $r_0^{(\pi N)} = 1.36$ fm
- $\pi\Lambda_c$ $p$-wave excitation energy: 231.5 MeV (compared to 293 MeV for $N\to\Delta$)

## Context

This work extends the formalism previously applied to the $\pi\Lambda N$ system by Gal, Garcilazo, Valcarce, and Fernández-Caramés (2013) to the charmed sector by replacing $\Lambda(1116)$ with $\Lambda_c(2286)$ and $\Sigma(1385)$ with $\Sigma_c(2520)$. The predicted $(I,J^P)=(3/2,2^+)$ $\pi\Lambda_c N$ dibaryon candidate, denoted ${\cal Y}_c$, would be the lowest-lying charmed dibaryon, considerably below the approximately 3500 MeV mass predicted for a $DNN$ bound state with $(I,J^P)=(1/2,0^-)$ by another group (2012). The authors propose production reactions using proton and pion beams at J-PARC, including $pp \to {\cal Y}_c^{+++} + D^-$ and $\pi^\pm d \to {\cal Y}_c^{+++/+} + D^-$, with subsequent decay to $\Sigma_c(2455)N$.
