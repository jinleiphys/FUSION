# Drell-Yan proton-deuteron asymmetry and polarized light-antiquark distributions

> We discuss the Drell-Yan proton-deuteron (p-d) asymmetry R_{pd}, which is defined by the proton-proton and proton-deuteron cross-section ratio Δ_{(T)}σ^{pd}/2Δ_{(T)}σ^{pp}, and its relation to the polarized light-antiquark flavor asymmetry. Using a formalism of the polarized {\it pd} Drell-Yan process, we show that the R_{pd} especially in the large-x_F region is very useful for finding the flavor asymmetry in the longitudinally-polarized and transversity distributions. Our results are particularly important to study the flavor asymmetry in the transversity distributions because they cannot be measured by inclusive deep inelastic scattering and W-production process.

## Key claim

The Drell-Yan proton-deuteron asymmetry \(R_{pd}\) is shown to be a useful observable for detecting flavor asymmetry in polarized light-antiquark distributions, particularly in the large-\(x_F\) region; the paper's analysis relies on model assumptions for the asymmetry and neglects nuclear corrections in the deuteron, with the caveat that no experimental proposal for polarized \(pd\) Drell-Yan yet exists.

## Method

The author defines the polarized \(pd\) Drell-Yan asymmetry \(R_{pd}\) as the ratio \(\Delta_{(T)}\sigma^{pd} / 2\Delta_{(T)}\sigma^{pp}\) for both longitudinally-polarized (\(\Delta\)) and transversity (\(\Delta_T\)) distributions. Using the parton-model formalism from Refs. 8 and 9, the cross-section differences are expressed in terms of quark and antiquark distributions in the proton and deuteron, assuming isospin symmetry and neglecting nuclear effects. Numerical calculations are performed at leading-order (LO) and next-to-leading-order (NLO) using the LSS99 parametrization for longitudinally-polarized distributions, with transversity distributions assumed equal to the longitudinal ones at \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\), and the flavor-asymmetry ratio \(r_{\bar q} = \Delta_{(T)}\bar u / \Delta_{(T)}\bar d\) is varied as 0.7, 1.0, or 1.3.

## Key numbers

* The flavor-symmetric case (\(r_{\bar q} = 1.0\)) yields \(R_{pd} = 1\) in the large-\(x_F\) limit and \(R_{pd} = 0.625\) in the small-\(x_F\) limit.
* For the Pauli-exclusion-principle model, the predicted magnitudes are \(\Delta\bar u = -0.13\) and \(\Delta\bar d = +0.05\).
* Numerical calculations use center-of-mass energy \(\sqrt{s} = 50\) GeV and dimuon mass \(M_{\mu\mu} = 5\) GeV.
* Results are also shown at \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) and 500 GeV (RHIC energies) with the same \(M_{\mu\mu} = 5\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of unpolarized light-antiquark flavor asymmetry, established by NMC (Gottfried-sum-rule violation), NA51, and E866 (Drell-Yan \(pd\) asymmetry), to the polarized case where the asymmetry is unknown. It builds on the formalism for polarized \(pd\) Drell-Yan developed in Refs. 8 and 9, and compares model predictions from the meson-cloud model and Pauli-exclusion-principle model (Refs. 5 and 6). The results are particularly important for transversity distributions, which cannot be accessed via inclusive deep inelastic scattering or \(W\)-production due to their chiral-odd nature, and suggest that future experiments at FNAL, HERA, or RHIC could probe this asymmetry.
