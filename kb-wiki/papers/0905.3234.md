# Glittering Glasmas

> We compute the production of gluons from Glasma color flux tubes. We calculate the probability distribution of gluon multiplicities arising from the distribution of color electric and color magnetic flux tubes found in the Glasma. We show that the result corresponds to the negative binomial probability distribution observed in experiments. The parameter k that characterizes this distribution is proportional to the number of colors N_c^2-1 and to the number of flux tubes. For one gluon color and one flux tube, the multiplicity distribution is close to a Bose-Einstein distribution. We call this decay process "Glitter", a term that is explained below.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the multiplicity distribution of gluons produced from Glasma color flux tubes is a negative binomial distribution, not a Poisson distribution as might be expected for decay of an external source in a weakly interacting theory. The result is derived to leading logarithmic accuracy and leading order in the strong coupling constant, assuming the transverse momenta of produced gluons are all larger than the saturation scale.

## Method

The authors compute the factorial cumulants of the gluon multiplicity distribution by averaging over Gaussian distributions of color charge densities (the McLerran-Venugopalan model) for the two colliding nuclei. They evaluate connected diagrams for multigluon production at leading order in the sources, showing that only "rainbow" diagram topologies contribute when all gluon momenta are above the saturation scale. The infrared divergences in the resulting integrals are regulated at the saturation momentum scale, and the factorial cumulants are shown to match those of a negative binomial distribution.

## Key numbers

- The parameter \(k\) characterizing the negative binomial distribution is given by \(k = \kappa \frac{(N_c^2 - 1) Q_s^2 S_\perp}{2\pi}\), where \(\kappa\) is a constant of order one, \(N_c\) is the number of colors, \(Q_s\) is the saturation momentum, and \(S_\perp\) is the transverse area.
- For one gluon color and one flux tube, the multiplicity distribution is close to a Bose-Einstein distribution (the \(k=1\) case of the negative binomial).
- From PHENIX data on central gold-gold collisions at RHIC, the extracted value of \(k \approx 690\) (extrapolated to zero centrality bin width) combined with \(Q_s \approx 1.1\) GeV gives an estimate \(\kappa \approx 0.2\).
- The UA5 experiment found \(k \approx 2\) to \(4\) for proton-antiproton collisions (number of participants \(\approx 2\)), while PHENIX found \(k \approx 350\) for 0-5% most central gold-gold collisions (number of participants \(\approx 350\)).

## Context

This work extends the Glasma flux tube picture developed in the Color Glass Condensate framework by McLerran, Venugopalan, and others (1994-2006) to explain the observed negative binomial multiplicity distributions in high energy collisions. The result provides a natural interpretation where the transverse area is filled with \(Q_s^2 S_\perp\) independent flux tubes, each emitting gluons in \(N_c^2 - 1\) colors, with a single flux tube producing a Bose-Einstein-like distribution. This contrasts with earlier string model approaches (Andersson 1983, Braun 2000, Dias de Deus 2003) where each string produced particles with a Poisson distribution. The paper enables future systematic studies of bin size effects on the extraction of the parameter \(k\) from various centralities of heavy ion collisions to test the flux tube interpretation.
