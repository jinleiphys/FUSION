# Systematics of 2+ states in semi-magic nuclei

> We propose a simple systematics of low lying 2+ energy levels and electromagnetic transitions in semi-magic isotopic chains Z=28,50,82 and isotonic chains N=28,50,82,126. To this purpose we use a two-level pairing plus quadrupole Hamiltonian, within the spherical Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA). We derive a simple relation connecting the 2+ energy with the pairing gap and quadrupole-quadupole (QQ) interaction strength. It turns out that the systematics of energy levels and B(E2) values predicted by this simple model is fulfilled with a reasonable accuracy by all available experimental data. Both systematics suggest that not only active nucleons but also those filling closed shells play an important role.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a simple systematics for low-lying 2+ states in semi-magic nuclei using a two-level pairing plus quadrupole Hamiltonian within the spherical Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA), finding that the relation between 2+ energy and pairing gap is fulfilled with reasonable accuracy for both proton and neutron roots, except for the Z=28 isotopic chain where the proton-neutron interaction plays an important role for N approximately equal to Z nuclei.

## Method

The authors used a spherical QRPA with a Hamiltonian containing one proton level and one neutron level, with pairing and quadrupole-quadrupole (QQ) two-body interactions. They derived a simple relation connecting the 2+ energy with the pairing gap and QQ interaction strength, and fitted this relation to experimental data for isotopic chains Z=28, 50, 82 and isotonic chains N=28, 50, 82, 126. The pairing gaps were estimated from experimental binding energies, and the analysis included both energy levels and B(E2) transition values.

## Context

This work extends the generalized seniority scheme of Talmi (1971), which predicted constant 2+ energies in semi-magic nuclei, by showing that the 2+ energy actually depends on matrix elements of the effective interaction and that both active nucleons and those filling closed shells play important roles. The analysis reveals that the proton-neutron QQ interaction has vanishing values along most isotopic and isotonic chains, except for Z=28 where N is approximately equal to Z, and that the pairing effects of closed-shell nucleons are essential for explaining low-lying collective states. The results enable a simple parameterization of 2+ energies and B(E2) values across semi-magic nuclei, with the interaction strength following an A^(-0.35) scaling law, contrasting with the much stronger A^(-2.2) dependence derived by Uher and Sorensen (1966).
