# The Role of Center Vortices in QCD

> Center vortices are unambiguously identified after Laplacian Center Gauge fixing and their influence on confinement and chiral symmetry breaking is investigated on a sample of SU(2) configurations at zero and finite temperature.

## Key claim

Center vortices identified via Laplacian Center Gauge fixing reproduce the string tension of full SU(2) gauge theory and produce a non-zero quark condensate at zero temperature, but the Z₂-projected quark condensate does not vanish in the deconfined phase even though the Z₂ string tension does, indicating that the center-projected condensate may not directly reflect the chiral properties of SU(2).

## Method

The authors fix to Laplacian Center Gauge by using the two lowest eigenvectors of the covariant adjoint Laplacian operator to define a unique gauge transformation, avoiding Gribov copy ambiguities present in the Direct Maximal Center gauge. They then project the gauge-fixed SU(2) links onto their closest Z₂ center elements, identify P-vortices as negative plaquettes in the projected theory, and measure the string tension via Creutz ratios and the quark condensate as a function of quark mass on 16³ × Nₜ lattices at β=2.4 for Nₜ=16, 8, and 4.

## Key numbers

- P-vortex density is approximately 11% with Laplacian Center Gauge versus approximately 5.5% with Direct Maximal Center gauge on a 16⁴ lattice at β=2.4.
- On a cooled one-instanton configuration, fewer than 100 P-vortices are found, all located near the instanton center, regardless of the original instanton size.
- The Z₂-projected theory reproduces the SU(2) string tension value from Ref. 10 (no explicit numerical value given in the text).
- The quark condensate in the Z₂-projected theory at zero temperature extrapolates linearly to a non-zero value as m_q → 0, with a functional form ⟨ψ̅ψ⟩_{Z₂} = a + b/m_q + c·m_q; the parameters a, b, and c do not vary much with temperature.

## Context

This work extends the center vortex confinement picture of Greensite (1997) and Del Debbio et al. (1998) by introducing a Gribov-copy-free Laplacian Center Gauge, addressing the ambiguity concerns raised by Kovacs (1999). The Laplacian approach generalizes the Laplacian Abelian Gauge of Vink and Wiese (1992) and allows simultaneous identification of center vortices and monopoles. The results enable future studies of the relationship between center vortices and topological charge, and the method readily generalizes to SU(N) gauge groups.
