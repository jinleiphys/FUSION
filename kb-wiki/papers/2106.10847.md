# Accessing Topological Fluctuations of Gauge Fields with Chiral Magnetic Effect

> Gauge fields provide the fundamental interactions in the Standard Model of particle physics. Gauge field configurations with nontrivial topological windings are known to play crucial roles in many important phenomena, from matter-anti-matter asymmetry of today's universe to spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking in strong interaction. Their presence is however elusive for direct detection in experiments. Here we show that measurements of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) in heavy ion collisions can be used for accessing the topological fluctuations of the non-Abelian gauge fields in the Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). To achieve this, we implemented a key ingredient, the stochastic dynamics of gauge field topological fluctuations, into a state-of-the-art framework for simulating the CME in these collisions. This new framework provides the necessary tool to quantify initial topological fluctuations from any definitive CME signal to be extracted from experimental data. It also reveals a universal scaling relation between initial topological fluctuations and particle multiplicity produced in the corresponding collision events.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that measurements of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) in heavy ion collisions can be used to quantitatively extract the average variance of topological winding number fluctuations of QCD gauge fields, provided that the stochastic dynamics of these fluctuations during the fireball evolution is properly accounted for in the simulation framework.

## Method

The authors extended the anomalous-viscous fluid dynamics (AVFD) framework by implementing both event-by-event initial topological fluctuations from glasma flux tubes and a stochastic relaxation term for the axial current evolution, characterized by a Chern-Simons diffusion rate. They performed event-by-event simulations for AuAu, CuCu, RuRu, and ZrZr collisions at RHIC 200 GeV beam energy, varying a control parameter for initial chirality generation to establish a mapping between initial topological fluctuations and the final CME-induced charge correlation signal H^{SS-OS}.

## Key numbers

- For AuAu collisions at 200 GeV and (20-50)% centrality with alpha_s=0.3, the fitted quadratic relation is H^{SS-OS} = 1.038 x 10^{-9} sigma_w^2, where sigma_w is the standard deviation of the topological winding number.
- The optimal value of sigma_w extracted from STAR data is 119 for this centrality class.
- With stochastic dynamics included, the correlation signal H^{SS-OS} is reduced by a factor of 3.6 compared to a reference calculation without dissipation.
- For alpha_s=0.1, the fitted relation is H^{SS-OS} = 3.578 x 10^{-9} sigma_w^2; for alpha_s=0.5, it is H^{SS-OS} = 7.4 x 10^{-11} sigma_w^2.
- The universal scaling relation across all systems and centralities is sigma_w = (2.56 +/- 0.17) x N_ch^(0.648 +/- 0.013).
- For isobar collisions at (20-50)% centrality, predicted sigma_w values are 36.32 for RuRu and 35.35 for ZrZr, yielding H^{Ru} = 1.41 x 10^{-5} and H^{Zr} = 1.17 x 10^{-5} with a ratio of 1.2.
- The CME signal fraction in the Delta-gamma correlator is predicted as f_CME^{Ru} approx 0.040 and f_CME^{Zr} approx 0.032.

## Context

This work extends the AVFD framework of Shi (2017, 2019) and Jiang (2016) by incorporating the stochastic dynamics of gluon field topological fluctuations, which was previously neglected. It provides a quantitative tool to extract the topological winding number fluctuations from CME measurements, building on experimental analysis methods developed by Zhao (2018), Li (2020), and others. The universal scaling relation between topological fluctuations and charged hadron multiplicity enables predictions for isobar collisions that can be tested against STAR collaboration data, and the framework can ultimately help constrain the Chern-Simons diffusion rate, a key property of gauge fields relevant to baryon asymmetry and chiral symmetry breaking.
