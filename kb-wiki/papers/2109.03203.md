# Determination of $1p$ and $2p$ stripping excitation functions for $^{16}$O+$^{142}$Ce using a Recoil Mass Spectrometer

> We report the first direct measurement of differential transfer cross sections using a Recoil Mass Spectrometer. Absolute differential $1p$ and $2p$-stripping cross sections at $θ_\mathrm{c.m.}=180^\circ$ have been determined for the system $^{16}$O+$^{142}$Ce by detecting the heavier target-like ions at the focal plane of the Heavy Ion Reaction Analyzer. Focal plane spectra have been compared with the results of a semi-microscopic Monte-Carlo simulation to unambiguously identify the transfer channels. Transmission efficiency of the target-like ions through the spectrometer has also been estimated using the simulation. The methodology adopted in this work can be applied to other recoil separators. The measured excitation functions for the reactions $^{142}\mathrm{Ce(}^{16}\mathrm{O,}^{15}\mathrm{N)}^{143}\mathrm{Pr}$ and $^{142}\mathrm{Ce(}^{16}\mathrm{O,}^{14}\mathrm{C)}^{144}\mathrm{Nd}$ have been compared with coupled reaction channel calculations. An excellent matching between measurement and theory has been obtained. For $1p$-stripping, major contribution to the cross section has been found to be the transfer of a proton from $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ to the $2d_{\frac{5}{2}}$ excited state of $^{143}\mathrm{Pr}$, leaving behind $^{15}\mathrm{N}$ in the $1p_{\frac{1}{2}}$ ground state. Transfer of a cluster of two protons from $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ to the $2^{+}$ excited state of $^{144}\mathrm{Nd}$, resulting in $^{14}\mathrm{C}$ in the $0^{+}$ ground state, appears to be the most probable cause for $2p$-stripping. Measured transfer probabilities for $1p$ and $2p$ channels have been compared with Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock calculations. Proton stripping channels are found to be more favourable compared to neutron pick-up channels. However, the theory overpredicts measurement hinting at the need for extended approaches with explicit treatment of pairing correlations in the calculations.

## Key claim
The paper reports the first direct measurement of absolute differential transfer cross sections using a Recoil Mass Spectrometer, overcoming previous assumptions about uniform transmission efficiency. However, the excellent agreement between measurement and coupled reaction channel calculations applies specifically to the 1p-stripping channel, while for 2p-stripping the theory underpredicts the data by an order of magnitude unless the radius parameter of the binding potential is increased.

## Method
Absolute differential cross sections at θ_c.m. = 180° for 1p and 2p stripping were extracted by detecting heavier target-like ions (¹⁴³Pr and ¹⁴⁴Nd) at the focal plane of the Heavy Ion Reaction Analyzer (HIRA) for the system ¹⁶O+¹⁴²Ce at beam energies from 57 to 69 MeV. Focal plane spectra were compared with semi-microscopic Monte-Carlo simulations to unambiguously identify transfer channels, and transmission efficiency of target-like ions through the spectrometer was estimated using the same simulation. The measured excitation functions were compared with finite-range Coupled Reaction Channel (CRC) calculations using the code FRESCO and with Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations using the Skyrme SLy4d energy density functional.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy (E_lab) varied between 57 and 69 MeV for the ¹⁶O beam on a ¹⁴²Ce target of thickness ~121.7 μg/cm²
- 1p-stripping Q-value (Q₀^(-1p)) = -6.303 MeV; 2p-stripping Q-value (Q₀^(-2p)) = -8.54 MeV
- For 1p-stripping, the major contribution comes from proton transfer to the 2d_5/2 excited state of ¹⁴³Pr, with spectroscopic factor 1.275 for the 1p_1/2 ground state of ¹⁵N
- TDHF calculations at b = 0 show fusion occurs for E_c.m. ≥ 56.7 MeV, while binary reactions occur for E_c.m. ≤ 56.6 MeV
- At E_c.m. = 56.6 MeV, TDHF gives average number of transferred protons ~0.86 and neutrons less than 0.06
- TDHF overestimates 1p-stripping probability by about a factor of three compared to measurements
- Systematic uncertainties include: monitor detector angle 0.5° (12.8% effect), transmission efficiency 10% (10% effect), and Ω^eff_HIRA 17.1% (17.1% effect)

## Context
This work extends earlier measurements of few-nucleon transfer probabilities using recoil mass spectrometers at IUAC (Kataria 1997, Baby 1997, Sinha 1997, Varier 1999, Tripathi 1999, Kalkal 2011) which assumed uniform transmission efficiency for all channels and did not extract differential cross sections. The methodology overcomes these limitations following the approach of Biswas (2020, 2021) for measuring differential quasi-elastic scattering cross sections. The results enable direct measurement of multi-nucleon transfer cross sections using other recoil separators, and the comparison with TDHF calculations highlights the need for extended approaches with explicit treatment of pairing correlations.
