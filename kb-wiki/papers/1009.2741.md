# Bulk viscosity of the massive Gross-Neveu model

> A calculation of the bulk viscosity for the massive Gross-Neveu model at zero fermion chemical potential is presented in the large-$N$ limit. This model resembles QCD in many important aspects: it is asymptotically free, has a dynamically generated mass gap, and for zero bare fermion mass it is scale invariant at the classical level (broken through the trace anomaly at the quantum level). For our purposes, the introduction of a bare fermion mass is necessary to break the integrability of the model, and thus to be able to study momentum transport. The main motivation is, by decreasing the bare mass, to analyze whether there is a correlation between the maximum in the trace anomaly and a possible maximum in the bulk viscosity, as recently conjectured. After numerical analysis, I find that there is no direct correlation between these two quantities: the bulk viscosity of the model is a monotonously decreasing function of the temperature. I also comment on the sum rule for the spectral density in the bulk channel, as well as on implications of this analysis for other systems.

## Key claim

The paper finds that there is no direct correlation between the maximum in the trace anomaly and a possible maximum in the bulk viscosity in the massive Gross-Neveu model; the bulk viscosity is a monotonously decreasing function of temperature, contrary to the conjecture that a peak in the trace anomaly near the critical temperature might drive a peak in the bulk viscosity.

## Method

The author calculates the bulk viscosity of the massive Gross-Neveu model in 1+1 dimensions at zero fermion chemical potential in the large-N limit using a kinetic theory approach. A bare fermion mass is introduced to break the integrability of the model (which otherwise would make the bulk viscosity infinite), and the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck equation is solved to linear order in deviations from equilibrium, considering only inelastic 2↔4 and 3↔3 processes since elastic 2→2 collisions cannot relax momentum perturbations in 1+1 dimensions.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the massless case (m=0) is T_c ≈ 0.57 M_0, where M_0 is the vacuum mass gap.
- For m=0⁺ above T_c, the pressure corresponds to an ideal gas of massless fermions: P = π N T²/6, with bag pressure P_b = N M_0²/(4π).
- The bulk viscosity is a monotonously decreasing function of temperature for all values of the bare mass m/M_0 studied (including m/M_0 = 0.01, 0.05, 0.1, 0.2, 0.5, 1.0).

## Context

This work tests a conjecture by Kharzeev (2008) that a maximum in the trace anomaly near the critical temperature might drive a maximum in the bulk viscosity, a conjecture later questioned by Moore (2008), Caron-Huot (2009), and Romatschke (2009). The calculation extends prior work on transport coefficients in the Gross-Neveu model and related systems (Arnold et al. 2006, Jeon 1995, 1996). The result implies that for systems where the trace anomaly peaks but the bulk viscosity does not, the simple correlation between scale symmetry breaking and bulk viscosity does not hold, and the author notes that the sum rule for the spectral density in the bulk channel must be carefully interpreted.
