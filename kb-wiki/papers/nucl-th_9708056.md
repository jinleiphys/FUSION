# Pionic decay of a possible d'-dibaryon and the short-range NN interaction

> We study the pionic decay of a possible dibaryon d' --> N N πin the microscopic quark shell model. The initial d' dibaryon wave function (J^P=0^-, T=0) consists of one 1\hbarωsix-quark shell-model s^5p[51]_X configuration. The most important final six-quark configurations s^6[6]_X, s^4p^2[42]_X and (s^4p^2-s^52s)[6]_X are properly projected onto the NN channel. The final state NN interaction is investigated by means of two phase-equivalent - but off-shell different - potential models. We demonstrate that the decay width Γ_d' depends strongly on the short-range behavior of the NN wave function. In addition, the width Γ_d' is very sensitive to the mass and size of the d' dibaryon. For dibaryon masses slightly above the experimentally suggested value M_d'=2.065 GeV, we obtain a pionic decay width of Γ_d' = 0.18 - 0.32 MeV close to the experimental value Γ_d' = 0.5 MeV.

## Key claim

The pionic decay width of the hypothetical d' dibaryon depends strongly on the short-range behavior of the NN wave function and is very sensitive to the mass and size of the d' dibaryon; for dibaryon masses slightly above the experimentally suggested value of 2.065 GeV, the calculated width of 0.18-0.32 MeV approaches the experimental value of 0.5 MeV, but the result is conditional on the choice of phase-equivalent but off-shell different NN potential models.

## Method

The authors calculated the pionic decay d' → N N π using a microscopic quark shell model, where the initial d' dibaryon (J^P=0^-, T=0) is described by a single 1ħω six-quark s^5p[51]_X configuration. The final six-quark configurations s^6[6]_X, s^4p^2[42]_X, and (s^4p^2-s^52s)[6]_X were projected onto the NN channel, and the final state NN interaction was investigated using two phase-equivalent but off-shell different potential models: the Tabakin potential and the separable model of Ueda et al. The decay matrix elements were computed using fractional parentage coefficient techniques for the six-quark shell model.

## Key numbers

- The experimentally suggested mass of the d' dibaryon is M_d' = 2.065 GeV, with an experimental decay width Γ_d' ≈ 0.5 MeV (uncertain by a factor of two).
- For dibaryon masses slightly above 2.065 GeV, the calculated pionic decay width is Γ_d' = 0.18 - 0.32 MeV.
- The πNN coupling constant used is f_πN^2/(4π) = 0.07491, with the πqq coupling derived as f_πq = (3/5) f_πN.
- The average pion mass used in the calculation is m_π = 138 MeV.
- The d' mass is approximately 50 MeV above the πNN threshold.

## Context

This work extends earlier quark-model studies of the d' dibaryon by the Tubingen group (Wagner 1995, Buchmann 1995, Itonaga) and addresses the pionic decay mechanism first proposed to explain the narrow resonance-like structure observed in pion double charge exchange experiments at T_π ≈ 50 MeV (Bilger 1991, Martemyanov 1991). The calculation improves on the earlier work of Itonaga by including antisymmetrization of the final NN state at the quark level, a complete basis of final six-quark states, and final state interaction for the two-nucleon system. The results demonstrate that the d' decay width can discriminate between different short-range NN potential models (Tabakin versus Ueda et al.) that are phase-equivalent but differ off-shell, and the work suggests that electroexcitation of the d' on the deuteron at large momentum transfers (Schep) could provide the needed vacuum mass data to resolve the remaining uncertainty.
