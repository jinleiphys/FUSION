# Collision of ultra-relativistic proton with strong magnetic field: production of ultra-high energy photons and neutrinos

> Proton-proton interaction and photo-hadronic interaction in cosmic accelerators are the two main channels for the production of cosmic ultra-high energy photons and neutrinos (TeV-PeV). In this Letter, we use FWW approach to obtain the production of cosmic ultra-high energy photons and neutrinos from the collision between UHE proton with magnetic field which could be considered as the virtualphoton in the rest frame of UHE proton. We name this as $pB$ process. The threshold for the occurrence of the $pB$ process is that the combination of the Lorentz factor of proton and the strength of the magnetic field is about $γ_p B \simeq 5\times 10^{18}$Gauss. Beyond this threshold, the rate of energy loss of proton due to the $pB$ process is about three orders higher than that due to the synchrotron radiation of proton in the same magnetic field. The $pB$ process might potentially happen in the atmosphere of white dwarfs, neutron stars or even that of stellar massive black holes.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that ultra-high-energy (UHE) protons colliding with a strong magnetic field can produce pions (and thus photons and neutrinos) via the Fermi-Weizsacker-Williams (FWW) virtual-photon approach, naming this the "pB" process; the key condition is that the product of the proton Lorentz factor and the magnetic field strength must exceed approximately γ_p B ≃ 5×10^18 Gauss for the pB process to dominate over proton synchrotron radiation.

## Method

The authors apply the Fermi-Weizsacker-Williams (FWW) method to treat the static magnetic field as a flux of virtual photons in the proton's rest frame, then compute the energy-loss rate of the proton by integrating the virtual-photon spectrum over the total photo-meson interaction cross section (including Δ-resonance, higher resonances, direct-pion, and multi-pion channels). The interaction rate is calculated as Γ^IT(E_p, B) = γ_p^{-1} ∫ ε_r^{-1} (d²P'/dε'_r dA') σ^IT(ε_r) dε_r, using the spectral Poynting flux of equivalent photons derived from Fourier analysis of the proton's motion in its rest frame. The resulting energy-loss rates are compared to classical synchrotron radiation losses for various magnetic field strengths and proton energies.

## Key numbers

- The threshold condition for the pB process is γ_p B ≃ 5×10^18 Gauss.
- The critical magnetic field of the proton is B_c^p = 1.5×10^20 Gauss.
- The resonant photo-meson cross section is σ_res ≃ 200 μbarn for photon energies between 0.2 GeV and 0.5 GeV in the proton rest frame.
- The proton Thomson cross section is σ_T^p ≈ 0.20 μbarn.
- Beyond the threshold, the energy-loss rate due to the pB process is about three orders of magnitude higher than that due to synchrotron radiation in the same magnetic field.
- For B = 10^11 G, the pB process dominates at γ_p ∼ 10^8.
- The peak virtual-photon flux in the proton rest frame is approximately d²P'/dε'_r dA' ∼ 4.7×10^51 cm^{-2}s^{-1} (γ_p B/B_c^p)^{-1} (ε'_r/m_p c^2)² [K_{1/3}²(ξ) + 2K_{2/3}²(ξ)].

## Context

This work extends the FWW method, previously applied to synchrotron radiation and inverse Compton scattering by Lieu (1993), to the production of pions and their decay products from proton-magnetic-field collisions. It contrasts with earlier quantum-mechanical treatments of direct-pion production from proton synchrotron (Maruyama 2015, 2016) and semi-classical approximations (Tokuhisa 1999, Berezinsky 1995), which considered only the p + B → p + π⁰ channel; the present approach includes all photo-meson channels (Δ-resonance, N-resonance, direct-pion, multi-pion) and also yields π± production for neutrino emission. The results enable estimates of UHE neutrino and photon production in astrophysical environments such as white dwarf atmospheres (B ∼ 10^6-9 G), magnetars (B ∼ 10^14-15 G), and merger remnants of binary black holes or neutron stars, where conventional pp or pγ targets may be absent.
