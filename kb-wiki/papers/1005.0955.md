# On the gluon spectrum in the glasma

> We study the gluon distribution in nucleus-nucleus collisions in the framework of the Color-Glass-Condensate. Approximate analytical solutions are compared to numerical solutions of the non-linear Yang-Mills equations. We find that the full numerical solution can be well approximated by taking the full initial condition of the fields in Coulomb gauge and using a linearized solution for the time evolution. We also compare kt-factorized approximations to the full solution.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a linearized time evolution of the gauge fields after the collision, combined with the full nonlinear initial condition in Coulomb gauge, provides a good approximation to the full numerical solution of the classical Yang-Mills equations for gluon production in nucleus-nucleus collisions, except at very low transverse momenta where the linearized treatment fails to capture infrared screening effects.

## Method

The authors solve the classical Yang-Mills equations for the glasma gauge fields in the Color-Glass-Condensate framework, using the McLerran-Venugopalan model with Gaussian-distributed color charges to generate Wilson lines for the colliding nuclei. They compare three approaches: the full numerical solution of the nonlinear Yang-Mills equations, a linearized approximation in the \(\Omega=VU\) gauge, and a linearized approximation in the two-dimensional Coulomb gauge at \(\tau=0^+\), where the spectrum is computed from the fields at \(\tau=0\) using a reduction formula. The gluon spectrum is evaluated for symmetric nucleus-nucleus collisions and asymmetric proton-nucleus collisions, and the nuclear modification factors \(R_{pA}\) and \(R_{AA}\) are computed.

## Key numbers

- For the saturated strong field regime with \(g^2\mu = 2\ \textrm{GeV}\), \(m=0.1\ \textrm{GeV}\), and \(N_y=20\), the linearized Coulomb gauge result overestimates the full CYM result by less than 15% in the intermediate momentum region \(0.2 < k_T/g^2\mu < 3\), with both peaking at \(k_T/g^2\mu \simeq 1.2\).
- The saturation scale \(Q_s\) is proportional to and numerically of the same order as \(g^2\mu_A\), with relevant values of order \(g^2\mu_A = 1-2\ \textrm{GeV}\) for RHIC and \(2-3\ \textrm{GeV}\) for the LHC.
- In the dilute limit with \(g^2\mu = 0.2\ \textrm{GeV}\), \(m=0.1\ \textrm{GeV}\), and \(N_y=20\), the linearized approximations agree well with the full CYM result.
- For the nuclear modification factor \(R_{pA}\) with \(g^2\mu_A = 2\ \textrm{GeV}\) and \(g^2\mu_p = 0.32\ \textrm{GeV}\), the Cronin enhancement peaks at about two times the saturation scale \(g^2\mu_A\); for \(R_{AA}\) with the same parameters, the peak is at about three times \(g^2\mu_A\).

## Context

This work extends the linearized approximation developed by Blaizot, Lappi, and Mehtar-Tani (2008) for the proton-nucleus case to the fully nonlinear nucleus-nucleus case, comparing it to the full numerical solutions of Krasnitz, Nardi, and Venugopalan (1998-2003) and Lappi (2003). The results show that the Coulomb gauge linearized approximation works well for the differential spectrum at momenta above \(k_T \sim 0.2 g^2\mu\), but fails in the infrared where the full CYM result is finite due to screening effects, as noted by Krasnitz (2000) and Romatschke (2005, 2006). The paper also tests the \(k_T\)-factorized ansatz for AA collisions, which has no formal derivation in the fully nonlinear case, and finds that it deviates from the full solution.
