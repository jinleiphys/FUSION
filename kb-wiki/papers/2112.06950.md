# Probe for bound states of SU(3) fermions and colour deconfinement

> Fermionic artificial matter realized with cold atoms grants access to an unprecedented degree of control on sophisticated many-body effects with an enhanced flexibility of the operating conditions. We consider three-component fermions with attractive interactions to study the formation of complex bound states whose nature goes beyond the standard fermion pairing occurring in quantum materials. Such systems display clear analogies with quark matter. Here, we address the nature of the bound states of a three-component fermionic system in a ring-shaped trap through the persistent current. In this way, we demonstrate that we can distinguish between color superfluid and trionic bound states. By analyzing finite temperature effects, we show how finite temperature can lead to the deconfinement of bound states. For weak interactions the deconfinement occurs because of scattering states. In this regime, the deconfinement depends on the trade-off between interactions and thermal fluctuations temperature. For strong interactions the features of the persistent current result from the properties of a suitable gas of bound states.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the frequency of the persistent current in a ring-shaped gas of three-component fermions can distinguish between color superfluid (CSF) and trionic bound states, and that finite temperature can lead to deconfinement of these bound states. The authors show that for weak interactions deconfinement occurs because of scattering states and depends on the trade-off between interactions and thermal fluctuations, while for strong interactions the persistent current features result from the properties of a gas of bound states.

## Method

The authors model three-component fermions with attractive interactions on an L-site ring-shaped lattice pierced by an effective magnetic flux using the SU(3) Hubbard model. They employ a combination of exact diagonalization, density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) calculations, and Bethe ansatz analysis of the Gaudin-Yang-Sutherland model to study the persistent current and its Fourier components at both zero and finite temperature. The persistent current is computed as the derivative of the free energy with respect to the effective magnetic flux, and time-of-flight imaging is analyzed as an experimental probe.

## Key numbers

- For trion formation with symmetric interactions at large U, the persistent current period reduces to phi_0/3 (one-third of the bare flux quantum), demonstrated for N_p = 9, L = 15 using DMRG
- For CSF states with asymmetric interactions (|U_AB|/t = |U_BC|/t = 0.01 and |U_AC|/t = 3), the paired colours display a halved periodicity phi_0/2 while the unpaired colour shows bare periodicity phi_0
- The exact expression for zero-temperature persistent current in the continuous limit for a system of trions at large U is I(phi) = -6(2pi/L)^2[K_a/3 + phi], where K_a denotes quantum numbers
- For N_p = 3, L = 15, the Fourier weight C_3/|I_max| follows distinct power laws: lambda = -1.25 for weak U/t, lambda = -0.33 for intermediate U/t, and lambda = -0.1 for strong U/t, with temperature T*k_B/t ranging from 0.01 to 0.08
- For CSF configuration with |U_AB|/t = |U_BC|/t = 0.01 and |U_AC|/t = 5, and trionic configuration at |U|/t = 5, time-of-flight results are obtained for N_p = 3, L = 10 using exact diagonalization at temperatures T*k_B/t = {0, 0.01, 0.1}

## Context

This work extends the study of SU(N) fermionic systems, building on prior investigations of three-component fermions by Honerkamp (2004), Cherng (2007), Rapp (2007), Catelani (2008), Capponi (2008), Klingschat (2010), Batchelor (2010), Kuhn (2012), Pohlmann (2013), and Guan (2013). The persistent current approach follows the Leggett theorem (1991) framework and previous work on persistent currents in bosonic systems by Naldesi (2019) and Polo (2020), and in repulsive SU(N) fermions by Chetcuti (2020). The results enable experimental distinction between trion and CSF bound states in cold atom platforms, with potential analogies to quark-gluon plasma formation at high temperature and low baryonic density in quantum chromodynamics, though the authors note their system lacks key QCD features such as string breaking and colour charge screening.
