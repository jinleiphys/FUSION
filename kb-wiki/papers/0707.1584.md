# On the K^+D Interaction at Low Energies

> The Kd reactions are considered in the impulse approximation with NN final-state interactions (NN FSI) taken into account. The realistic parameters for the KN phase shifts are used. The "quasi-elastic" energy region, in which the elementary KN interaction is predominantly elastic, is considered. The theoretical predictions are compared with the data on the K^+d->K^+pn, K^+d->K^0pp, K^+d->K^+d and K^+d total cross sections. The NN FSI effect in the reaction K^+d->K^+pn has been found to be large. The predictions for the Kd cross sections are also given for slow kaons, produced from phi(1020) decays, as the functions of the isoscalar KN scattering length a_0. These predictions can be used to extract the value of a_0 from the data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the NN final-state interaction (FSI) effect in the reaction K‚Āļd ‚Üí K‚Āļpn is large, but this conclusion is hedged by the approximation that only s-wave KN scattering is used in the FSI calculation, meaning the NN FSI is treated only in the triplet ¬≥S‚āĀ state and is absent in the K‚Āļd ‚Üí K‚Āįpp reaction due to the Pauli principle.

## Method

The authors calculate K‚Āļd cross sections in the impulse approximation using pole diagrams for the break-up reactions K‚Āļd ‚Üí K‚Āļpn and K‚Āļd ‚Üí K‚Āįpp, plus a diagram for NN final-state interaction (FSI) in the triplet state. The KN scattering amplitudes include only s- and p-wave contributions from the phase-shift parameters of Gibbs (2005), and the deuteron wave function from the Bonn potential (full model) is used. The elastic K‚Āļd ‚Üí K‚Āļd cross section is computed in a single-scattering approximation with form factors derived from the deuteron wave function.
