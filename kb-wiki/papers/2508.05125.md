# Probing surface vibration of spherical nuclei in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> There has been increasing interest in recent years in using relativistic heavy-ion collisions to probe nuclear structure, such as static nuclear deformation. Here we discuss the role of quantum zero-point fluctuations of the surface vibration of spherical nuclei in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. To this end, we employ an approach to describe the vibration in the space-fixed frame, which has been well established in the field of low-energy heavy-ion fusion reactions. We particularly consider the quadrupole vibration of $^{58}$Ni in $^{58}$Ni+$^{58}$Ni reaction and the octupole vibration of $^{208}$Pb in $^{208}$Pb+$^{208}$Pb reaction. We show that the surface vibration leads to comparable eccentricity parameters to those for static deformation, while they give significantly different distributions of the initial states, suggesting the importance of the proper treatment of the surface vibration in heavy-ion collisions. We perform similar analysis also for triaxial deformation and gamma-soft vibration.

## Key claim

The paper argues that quantum zero-point fluctuations of surface vibrations in spherical nuclei produce eccentricity parameters comparable in magnitude to those from static deformation, but with significantly different distributions (especially in skewness and kurtosis), suggesting that proper treatment of vibrational degrees of freedom is crucial for interpreting relativistic heavy-ion collision data.

## Method

The authors employ a harmonic-oscillator model for surface vibrations in the space-fixed frame, treating all deformation parameters α_{λμ} for a given multipolarity λ as independent Gaussian-distributed variables. They compute the eccentricity parameters ε_n and inverse mean-square area d for the initial-state geometry using a deformed Woods-Saxon density with complete overlap approximation for ⁵⁸Ni+⁵⁸Ni (quadrupole vibration, λ=2) and ²⁰⁸Pb+²⁰⁸Pb (octupole vibration, λ=3) collisions, comparing results with static deformation and with a simplified axial-fluctuation treatment.

## Key numbers

- β₂(⁵⁸Ni) = 0.218, derived from measured B(E2)↑ = 0.0695 e² b² for the transition to the first 2⁺ state at 1.45 MeV, with R₀ = 1.1 × 58^(1/3) fm
- β₃(²⁰⁸Pb) = 0.144, derived from measured B(E3)↑ = 0.608 e² b³ for the transition to the first 3⁻ state at 2.61 MeV, with R₀ = 1.1 × 208^(1/3) fm
- For ⁵⁸Ni |ε₂|: surface vibration mean = 0.112(1), standard deviation = 0.0554(7), skewness = 0.49(3), kurtosis = -0.02(11); static deformation mean = 0.119(1), standard deviation = 0.0500(5), skewness = -0.79(3), kurtosis = -0.62(6)
- For ²⁰⁸Pb |ε₃|: surface vibration mean = 0.0822(8), standard deviation = 0.0416(5), skewness = 0.55(4), kurtosis = 0.15(11); static deformation mean = 0.0821(8), standard deviation = 0.0461(4), skewness = -0.38(3), kurtosis = -1.29(3)
- For triaxial deformation of ⁵⁸Ni with β₂=0.218: mean |ε₂|² = 0.0157 for static γ=π/6, mean |ε₂|² = 0.0151 for flat γ distribution between 0 and π/3

## Context

This work extends the use of relativistic heavy-ion collisions to probe nuclear structure, which has previously focused on static deformation parameters including quadrupole (Giacalone 2020, Jia 2022), hexadecapole (Xu 2024), triaxial (Bally 2022, Zhao 2024), and octupole (Zhang 2025) deformations. It directly builds on a recent study by Xu et al. (2025) that showed octupole surface vibration of ²⁰⁸Pb can resolve a puzzle in ultra-central ²⁰⁸Pb+²⁰⁸Pb anisotropic flow data from ALICE. The formalism is adapted from low-energy heavy-ion fusion reaction theory (Hagino 2012, 2022; Dasgupta 1998), where surface vibrations are well known to affect sub-barrier fusion. The results enable experimental discrimination between static and dynamical deformation through higher-order cumulants of anisotropic flow and event-by-event distributions on the v₂–p̄_T plane.
