# Chiral confining Hartree-Fock Lagrangians based on Nambu-Jona--Lasino model

> We study a relativistic Hartree-Fock Lagrangian model which considers confinement, chiral symmetry breaking, nucleon form factor and short range correlations. The chiral potential originally based on the linear sigma-model is compared to an improved potential generated by the Nambu-Jona--Lasino (NJL) model for quark interaction. Our model is also anchored in fundamental hadronic properties predicted by Lattice-QCD calculations and a few nuclear empirical properties. We explore in a Bayesian approach the role of the saturation density, the energy per particle and the incompressibility modulus for the model selection. We find that most of our models could not reproduce these empirical quantities, unless a phenomenological "missing" energy is added. The properties of this "missing" energy are therefore inferred from our Bayesian analysis and we obtain that it shall be attractive. Finally we analyse the origin of the break down density in relativistic approaches and we relate it to the properties of the scalar potential.

## Key claim

Most of the relativistic Hartree-Fock models based on the NJL chiral potential cannot simultaneously reproduce empirical saturation properties (density, energy per particle, incompressibility) unless a phenomenological attractive "missing" energy is added, whose properties are inferred from a Bayesian analysis.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic Hartree-Fock Lagrangian (RHF-CC) that incorporates confinement, chiral symmetry breaking, nucleon form factors, and short-range correlations, replacing the linear sigma-model chiral potential with a potential derived from the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for quark interactions. They perform a Bayesian analysis to explore the parameter space, constraining the model using Lattice-QCD predictions for the nucleon mass expansion coefficients \(a_2\) and \(a_4\), the pion mass and decay constant, and nuclear empirical parameters (saturation density, energy per particle, incompressibility modulus).

## Key numbers

- Saturation density \(n_{\text{sat}} = 0.155 \pm 0.005\) fm\(^{-3}\) (centroid from Margueron (2018), used as constraint)
- Energy per particle at saturation \(E_{\text{sat}} = -15.8 \pm 0.3\) MeV (centroid from Margueron (2018), used as constraint)
- Incompressibility modulus \(K_{\text{sat}} = 230 \pm 20\) MeV (centroid from Margueron (2018), used as constraint)
- Lattice-QCD coefficient \(a_2 = 1.553 \pm 0.136\) GeV\(^{-1}\) (from Leinweber (2003), used as constraint)
- Lattice-QCD coefficient \(a_4 = -0.509 \pm 0.054\) GeV\(^{-3}\) (from Leinweber (2003), used as constraint)
- Pion decay constant \(F_\pi = 92 \pm 2\) MeV (used as constraint)
- Pion mass \(m_\pi = 139.6 \pm 2\) MeV (used as constraint)
- Constituent quark mass in vacuum \(M_0 < 400\) MeV (imposed as physical prior)
- Example NJL parameters giving \(M_0 = 398\) MeV and \(m_s = 809\) MeV: \(m = 5.9\) MeV, \(\Lambda = 586\) MeV, \(G_1 = 14.21\) GeV\(^{-2}\)

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Hartree-Fock chiral confining model previously developed by Chamseddine (2023) using the linear sigma-model potential, replacing it with a richer NJL-based chiral potential as suggested by Chanfray (2023). The study addresses the known issue that chiral effective theories without confinement lead to nuclear collapse (Boguta (1983), Kerman and Miller (1974)), which the authors' earlier work resolved by including nucleon polarizability via the parameter \(C_{\text{NS}}\). The present results show that even with the NJL potential, most models fail to reproduce empirical saturation properties without an additional attractive "missing energy" term, and the analysis relates the breakdown density in relativistic approaches to the scalar potential properties.
