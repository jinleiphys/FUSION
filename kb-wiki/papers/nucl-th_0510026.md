# From percolation to the Color Glass Condensate

> I review the problem of parton saturation and its implications through three in principal different approaches, but somewhat related: saturation in a geometrical approach, QCD saturation through the Color Glass Condensate and perturbative Pomeron approach with initial conditions. I also make some comments about how that could be related to a Quark Gluon Plasma formation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that three conceptually different approaches to parton saturation, geometrical percolation of color sources, the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory, and perturbative QCD pomeron exchanges with saturation, are related and can all describe the onset of color deconfinement, but the author emphasizes that percolation does not require or imply thermalization, only a critical density of color sources.

## Method

The author reviews and compares three frameworks: (1) a geometrical percolation model where color strings (or partons) are treated as discs of fixed radius in the transverse plane, with a critical density threshold η_c = 1.12–1.175 for cluster formation; (2) the Color Glass Condensate effective theory, where fast partons act as classical color sources that emit soft gluons, with saturation described by a renormalization group equation for the weight functional W_τ[ρ]; and (3) a perturbative QCD approach using BFKL pomerons with triple-pomeron vertices and the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation, solved with initial conditions from the Golec-Biernat saturation model generalized for nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Critical percolation density threshold: η_c = 1.12 to 1.175 (dimensionless, from Monte Carlo and direct connectedness expansion).
- Fixed radius of independent color sources in the string model: r = 0.2 to 0.25 fm, corresponding to a momentum around 1 GeV (from String Fusion Model Code at SPS energies).
- Critical momentum for percolation in central Pb-Pb collisions at SPS: Q_c^2 ≈ 1 GeV^2.
- Critical momentum for percolation in central Au-Au collisions at RHIC: Q_c^2 ≈ 2.5 GeV^2.
- Saturation momentum scaling: Q_s^2(x,A) ∼ A^δ x^{-λ}, with δ ≈ 1/3 and λ ≈ c ᾱ_s in first approximation.
- Estimated saturation momentum at RHIC: Q_s = 1 to 2 GeV.
- For central A-A collisions at very high energies, density η ∝ A^{2/3} (from N ∝ A^{4/3} and S_A ∝ A^{2/3}).
- For the perturbative pomeron approach, inclusive cross-sections at momenta below Q_s are proportional to A (number of participants), while at larger momenta they grow approximately as A^{1.1} (slower than A^{4/3} for number of collisions).
- Color dipole cross-sections at momenta substantially below Q_s differ from AGK-rule results by a universal constant factor < 0.8 to 0.9.

## Context

This review connects three lines of work: the percolation approach to string fusion (Ferreiro et al., 1996; SFMC code), the Color Glass Condensate effective theory (developed by McLerran, Venugopalan, and others), and the perturbative QCD pomeron approach with BK evolution (Balitsky, Kovchegov, Weigert). The author notes that the percolation threshold provides a geometric criterion for color deconfinement that does not require thermalization, unlike the Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) formation scenario. The paper enables future work on how saturated initial states, whether from percolation, CGC, or pomeron approaches, may evolve into the strongly coupled QGP (sQGP) observed at RHIC at temperatures (1–3) T_c, where T_c ∼ 160 MeV.
