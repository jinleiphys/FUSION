# Definition and parametrization of non-perturbative effects in quenched QCD

> The notion of a non-perturbative effect is ambiguous if it requires the subtraction of a perturbative part defined by a diverging series. A common procedure consists in dropping the order of minimal contribution and the higher orders. This allows us to isolate very accurately the one-instanton effect for the double-well potential. For the one plaquette gauge theory, an exact analytical expression can be written for the non-perturbative part. We report recent attempts to extend this approach to the average plaquette of quenched QCD. Our goal is to express the non-perturbative effects in terms of expressions of the form beta^B exp(-A beta) calculable semi-classically. The situation is complicated by zeroes of the partition function in the complex $β$ plane (presumably near 5.75 pm i 0.2). We discuss two methods to describe the intermediate and large order behavior of the perturbative series. One is inspired by mean field theory (logarithmic specific heat) and reproduces accurately the known perturbative series with only two free parameters. A diagrammatic interpretation of this fact is still lacking. The other is based on infra-red renormalons with a factorial growth showing up at order larger than 20 and a possible effective theory interpretation. These extrapolations are compatible with the non-perturbative part of the plaquette being proportional to a^4. We propose an exponential parametrization to the corrections to the universal part of the beta function and find results compatible with the suggestion of a^2 corrections made by C. Allton.

# Definition and parametrization of non-perturbative effects in quenched QCD

## Key claim
The non-perturbative part of the average plaquette in quenched QCD can be expressed in semi-classical form as \(A \beta^B e^{-C\beta}\), but this separation is a computational commodity with no claim of physical meaning unless observables that vanish in perturbation theory can be identified. The situation is complicated by zeroes of the partition function in the complex \(\beta\) plane, presumably near \(5.75 \pm i 0.2\).

## Method
The authors define the non-perturbative part by truncating the asymptotic perturbative series at the order of minimal contribution, then subtract this truncated series from the full numerical result. For the one-plaquette SU(2) model, an exact analytical separation is achieved by decompactifying the group integrals and discarding the tails of integration. For quenched QCD, two models are compared: a mean-field-inspired logarithmic specific heat ansatz with two free parameters, and an infrared renormalon model with factorial growth appearing above order 20. Direct searches for partition function zeroes are performed using reweighting methods on \(4^4\) and \(8^4\) lattices.

## Key numbers
- Zeroes of the partition function on a \(4^4\) lattice found near \(\beta = 5.55 \pm i 0.1\)
- Mean-field fit parameters: \(\Gamma = 0.003\), \(K = 0.0654\), \(\beta_m = 5.787\) using known coefficients \(a_9\) and \(a_{10}\)
- For the renormalon model, minimal perturbative error estimate: \(Min_k |\Delta_k| \simeq 3.5 (\bar{\beta})^{204/121 - 1/2} e^{-(16\pi^2/33)\bar{\beta}}\)
- Lattice scale from integration constant: \(\Lambda_L = \exp(-C)/r_0 \simeq 4.4\ \text{MeV}\) with \(C = 4.528(1)\)
- Exponential parametrization of beta function corrections: \(A = -1.35 \times 10^7\), \(B = 2.82\) in the interval \(5.9 < \beta < 6.4\)
- Prediction at \(\beta = 7.5\): \(\ln(a/r_0) = -3.59\) (compared to -3.63 from Guagnelli et al. 2002); at \(\beta = 8.5\): -4.74 (compared to -4.81)

## Context
This work extends the approach of defining non-perturbative effects via truncation of asymptotic series, previously validated for the double-well potential and one-plaquette gauge theory, to quenched QCD. The mean-field-inspired model reproduces known perturbative coefficients from order 3 to 16, while the renormalon model follows the continuum Borel transform approach of Mueller (1993) and the ITEP group, connected to the lattice via coupling redefinition as in Burgio et al. (1997). The exponential parametrization of the beta function corrections is compatible with the suggestion of \(a^2\) corrections made by Allton. The results enable future semi-classical calculation of the parameters \(A\), \(B\), and \(C\), and a precise connection between the non-perturbative plaquette and the gluon condensate in the continuum.
