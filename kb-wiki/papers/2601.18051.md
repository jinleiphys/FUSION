# Neutrino opacities in magnetic fields for binary neutron star merger simulations

> Neutrino interactions play a central role in transport and flavor evolution in the ejecta of binary neutron star mergers. Simulations suggest that neutron star mergers may produce magnetic fields as strong as $10^{17}$ G, but computational difficulties have hampered the inclusion of magnetic field effects in neutrino interaction rates. In this paper we give approximate interaction rates for neutrinos in the presence of strong magnetic fields, including the effects of Landau quantization and anomalous magnetic moments with errors of order $\sqrt{T/M}$. We also comment on a neutrino production channel from individual neutrons that can produce low-energy $ν\barν$ pairs even at low density.

## Key claim

The paper provides approximate neutrino interaction rates in strong magnetic fields (up to $10^{17}$ G) for binary neutron star merger simulations, with errors of order $\sqrt{T/M}$, by including Landau quantization and anomalous magnetic moments while making simplifying approximations to reduce computational expense.

## Method

The authors derive analytic approximations for charged current (neutron capture on neutrinos and proton capture on antineutrinos) and neutral current (neutrino-nucleon scattering) opacities in magnetized matter below nuclear density, using non-relativistic nucleon dispersions with anomalous magnetic moments and Landau-quantized electron and proton wavefunctions. They approximate the electron Landau level sum by interpolating between the lowest-Landau-level and continuum regimes, and use Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics for non-degenerate nucleons with a Sommerfeld expansion for degenerate neutrons. The resulting expressions reduce to the Burrows-Reddy-Thompson (BRT) results in the zero-field limit.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strengths up to $10^{17}$ G are considered, as suggested by binary neutron star merger simulations
- The approximation errors are of order $\sqrt{T/M}$, where $T$ is temperature and $M$ is the nucleon mass
- The non-degenerate approximation is valid until $\beta\mu \approx -1$, corresponding to number density $\rho \approx (T/10\ \mathrm{MeV})^{3/2} \times 4.7 \times 10^{-2} n_{\mathrm{sat}}$ where $n_{\mathrm{sat}} = 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$
- The neutron-proton mass splitting is $\Delta M \simeq 1.3\ \mathrm{MeV}$
- Neutron gyromagnetic ratio $g_n = -3.8263$, proton gyromagnetic ratio $g_p = 5.5858$
- The electron gyromagnetic ratio is set to $g_e = 2$ (with $g_e-2 \simeq 0.002$ neglected)
- Weak magnetism corrections: $\delta_{WM}^{(n)} = 1 + 1.1 k_\nu/M$ for neutrons, $\delta_{WM}^{(p)} = 1 - 7.1 k_\nu/M$ for protons

## Context

This work extends the foundational neutrino interaction rates of Burrows, Reddy, and Thompson (BRT), which did not include magnetic field effects, to incorporate the strong fields ($10^{17}$ G) found in binary neutron star merger simulations. It builds on prior studies of neutrino interactions in magnetized matter by Duan and Qian (2004, 2005) and the authors' own preliminary exploration (Kumamoto and Welch, 2025), which found substantial effects on low-energy neutrino opacities at field strengths up to $10^{17}$ G. The approximations enable implementation in merger simulations where full numerical calculations are computationally prohibitive, and enable study of neutrino transport effects on ejecta composition and nucleosynthesis.
