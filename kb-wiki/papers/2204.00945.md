# Energy-independent complex single $P$-waves $NN$ potential from Marchenko equation

> We extend our previous results of solving the inverse problem of quantum scattering theory (Marchenko theory, fixed-$l$ inversion). In particular, we apply an isosceles triangular-pulse function set for the Marchenko equation input kernel expansion in a separable form. The separable form allows a reduction of the Marchenko equation to a system of linear equations for the output kernel expansion coefficients. We show that in the general case of a single partial wave, a linear expression of the input kernel is obtained in terms of the Fourier series coefficients of $q^{1-m}(1-S(q))$ functions in the finite range of the momentum $0\leq q\leqπ/h$ [$S(q)$ is the scattering matrix, $l$ is the angular orbital momentum, $m=0,1,\dots,2l$]. Thus, we show that the partial $S$--matrix on the finite interval determines a potential function with $h$-step accuracy. The calculated partial potentials describe a partial $S$--matrix with the required accuracy. The partial $S$--matrix is unitary below the threshold of inelasticity and non--unitary (absorptive) above the threshold. We developed a procedure and applied it to partial-wave analysis (PWA) data of $NN$ elastic scattering up to 3 GeV. We show that energy-independent complex partial potentials describe these data for single $P$-waves.

# Energy-independent complex single $P$-waves $NN$ potential from Marchenko equation

## Key claim

The paper claims that energy-independent complex local partial potentials can describe elastic $NN$ scattering partial-wave analysis data for single $P$-waves up to 3 GeV, contrary to the common assumption that such potentials require strong energy dependence above the inelastic threshold. The claim is hedged by the condition that the $S$-matrix must be defined with a specific analytic continuation for negative momenta (Eq. OP_Smatrix) to handle non-unitary (absorptive) behavior above threshold.

## Method

The author extends the algebraic Marchenko inversion method by expanding the input kernel of the Marchenko integral equation in a separable series using an isosceles triangular-pulse function set (Eq. tr_set). This reduces the Marchenko equation to a system of linear equations for the output kernel expansion coefficients (Eq. f11n). The input kernel expansion coefficients are obtained linearly from Fourier series coefficients of $q^{1-m}(1-S(q))$ functions on the finite momentum interval $0 \leq q \leq \pi/h$, where $h$ is the step size determining the spatial grid. The method is applied to single $P$-wave $NN$ partial-wave analysis data (SW16 single-energy solutions) up to $E_{\text{lab}} \approx 3$ GeV, with phase shifts and inelasticity parameters smoothed by asymptotic expansions for $q > 3$ fm$^{-1}$.

## Key numbers

- The method uses a step size $h = 0.02$ fm, corresponding to a maximum momentum $q_{\text{max}} \approx 157.08$ fm$^{-1}$.
- The $NN$ scattering data are analyzed up to $E_{\text{lab}} \approx 3$ GeV.
- The test reconstruction uses a potential $V(r) = -3 \exp(-3r/2)$ in units of fm$^{-2}$, equivalent to $-124.5$ MeV, with angular momentum $l=1$.
- The test reconstruction uses $h = 0.04$ and a potential range $R = 4$ fm.
- The asymptotic smoothing for phase shifts uses $\delta(q) \sim \sum_{k=3}^{8} A_k/q^k$ for $q > 3$ fm$^{-1}$.
- The asymptotic smoothing for inelasticity parameters uses $\rho(q) \sim \sum_{k=1}^{9} A_k/q^k$ for $q > 3$ fm$^{-1}$.
- Barriers in the real parts of $P$-wave potentials are reconstructed at approximately 0.2-1.3 fm.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous algebraic Marchenko inversion method applied to the $^{1}S_{0}$ partial wave (Khokhlov, 2021) and generalizes earlier Marchenko and Gelfand-Levitan inversions by Geramb and Kohlhoff (1994) and Khokhlov (2006, 2007), which used rational fraction (Pade) expansions of the $S$-matrix and were limited to energies below the inelastic threshold or suffered from convergence issues with high-order approximants. The results contradict the claim by Fernandez-Soler and Ruiz Arriola (2017) that $NN$ optical potentials require strong energy dependence up to 3 GeV; instead, the reconstructed $P$-wave potentials show repulsive barriers rather than a soft repulsive core. The method enables construction of energy-independent complex partial $NN$ potentials for use in microscopic optical model calculations of nuclear reactions at energies above the pion production threshold.
