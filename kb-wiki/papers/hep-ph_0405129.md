# Manifestation of the $a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)$ mixing in the reaction $蟺^-p\to畏蟺^0n$ on a polarized target

> It is argued that the single-spin asymmetry in the reaction $蟺^-p\to a_0^0(980)n\to(畏蟺^0)_S n$ is extremely sensitive to the mixing of the $a^0_0(980)$ and $f_0(980)$ resonances. It is shown that at low momentum transfers (namely, in any one of the intervals $0\leq-t\leq0.025,...,0.1$ GeV$^2$) the normalized asymmetry, which can take the values from -1 to 1, must undergo a jump in magnitude close to 1 in the $畏蟺^0$ invariant mass region between 0.965 GeV and 1.01 GeV. The strong asymmetry jump is the straightforward manifestation of the $a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)$ mixing. For observing the jump, any very high quality $畏蟺^0$ mass resolution is not required. The energy dependence of the polarization effect is expected to be rather weak. Therefore, it can be investigated at any high energy, for example, in the range from eight to 100 GeV.

# Manifestation of the $a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)$ mixing in the reaction $\pi^-p\to\eta\pi^0n$ on a polarized target

## Key claim

The single-spin asymmetry in the reaction $\pi^-p\to a_0^0(980)n\to(\eta\pi^0)_S n$ at low momentum transfers (any interval $0\leq-t\leq0.025,...,0.1$ GeV$^2$) must undergo a jump in magnitude close to 1 in the $\eta\pi^0$ invariant mass region between 0.965 GeV and 1.01 GeV, provided the $a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)$ mixing exists. The asymmetry jump is predicted to be robust against finite mass resolution and weak energy dependence, but the overall sign of the asymmetry is not fixed by the model.

## Method

The authors constructed Regge pole amplitudes for $\rho_2$, $b_1$, and $\pi$ exchange in the reaction $\pi^-p\to a_0^0(980)n\to(\eta\pi^0)_S n$, where the $\pi$ exchange amplitude is forbidden by $G$-parity conservation and arises only through $a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)$ mixing. The mixing amplitude $\Pi_{a_0f_0}(m)$ was computed from one-loop diagrams involving $K^+K^-$ and $K^0\bar K^0$ intermediate states, exploiting the $K^+$-$K^0$ mass difference. The $\rho_2$ and $b_1$ exchange parameters were fitted to existing unpolarized BNL data at 18.3 GeV, and the normalized asymmetry $A(t,m)=I(t,m)/[d^2\sigma/dt dm]$ was calculated as a function of $\eta\pi^0$ invariant mass $m$ for several $-t$ intervals.

## Key numbers

- The asymmetry jump occurs in the $\eta\pi^0$ invariant mass region between **0.965 GeV** and **1.01 GeV**, with magnitude close to **1** (smoothed value approximately **0.95** for $0\leq-t\leq0.025$ GeV$^2$ and approximately **0.75** for $0\leq-t\leq0.2$ GeV$^2$).
- The total cross section for $\pi^-p\to a^0_0(980)n\to(\eta\pi^0)_S n$ at 18.3 GeV is approximately **200 nb** (from BNL data, integrated over $m$ from 0.8 to 1.2 GeV and all $t$).
- The $\pi$ exchange contribution to the total cross section is approximately **10.9 nb**, making up about **5.5%** of the total; at its maximum near $t\approx-0.0149$ GeV$^2$, $d\sigma^\pi/dt\approx139$ nb/GeV$^2$, accounting for approximately **14.7%** of $(d\sigma/dt)|_{t\approx0}$.
- The $K^+K^-$ and $K^0\bar K^0$ thresholds are separated by **8 MeV**; between them, $|\Pi_{a_0f_0}(m)|\approx0.1265\,|g_{a_0K^+K^-}g_{f_0K^+K^-}/16\pi|$.
- The $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ resonance parameters used: $m_{f_0}\approx0.980$ GeV, $m_{a_0}\approx0.9847$ GeV, $\Gamma_{f_0\pi\pi}(m_{f_0})\approx98$ MeV, $\Gamma_{a_0\eta\pi^0}(m_{f_0})\approx166$ MeV; visible (effective) widths at half maxima approximately **42 MeV** and **68 MeV**, respectively.
- The energy range for investigating the effect is from **8 to 100 GeV**.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier proposal (Achasov and Shestakov, 2004, Physical Review Letters) for using polarization phenomena to detect $a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)$ mixing, providing detailed quantitative estimates and model comparisons. The mixing itself was first predicted theoretically as a threshold phenomenon induced by the $K^+$-$K^0$ mass difference (Achasov, Devyanin, and Shestakov, 1979). Previous attempts to observe the mixing in unpolarized experiments, such as central production in $pp\to p_s(\eta\pi^0)p_f$ (Dzierba, 1995), have not yielded unambiguous identification. The present work enables a definitive experimental test using polarized targets at facilities including KEK, BNL, IHEP, CERN (COMPASS), and others, where the asymmetry jump provides a clear signature that does not require high $\eta\pi^0$ mass resolution.
