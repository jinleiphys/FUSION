# Baryon states with open charm in the extended local hidden gauge approach

> In this paper we examine the interaction of $D N$ and $D^* N$ states, together with their coupled channels, by using an extension of the local hidden gauge formalism from the light meson sector, which is based on heavy quark spin symmetry. The scheme is based on the use of the impulse approximation at the quark level, with the heavy quarks acting as spectators, which occurs for the dominant terms where there is the exchange of a light meson. The pion exchange and the Weinberg-Tomozawa interactions are generalized and with this dynamics we look for states generated from the interaction, with a unitary coupled channels approach that mixes the pseudoscalar-baryon and vector-baryon states. We find two states with nearly zero width which are associated to the $Λ_c(2595)$ and $Λ_c(2625)$. The lower state, with $J^P = 1/2^-$, couples to $D N$ and $D^* N$, and the second one, with $J^P = 3/2^-$, to $D^* N$. In addition to these two $Λ_c$ states, we find four more states with $I=0$, one of them nearly degenerate in two states of $J=1/2,\ 3/2$. Furthermore we find three states in $I=1$, two of them degenerate in $J=1/2, 3/2$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $\Lambda_c(2595)$ and $\Lambda_c(2625)$ states can be dynamically generated from the interaction of $DN$ and $D^*N$ states using an extension of the local hidden gauge formalism, with the caveat that the $\Lambda_c(2595)$ state couples to both $DN$ and $D^*N$ channels while the $\Lambda_c(2625)$ state is mostly tied to the $D^*N$ channel, and that the model requires tuning of cut-off parameters to reproduce the experimental masses.

## Method

The authors extended the local hidden gauge formalism from the light meson sector to the charm sector using heavy quark spin symmetry and the impulse approximation at the quark level, where heavy quarks act as spectators. They constructed transition potentials for pseudoscalar-baryon ($PB$) and vector-baryon ($VB$) channels using SU(3) symmetry with $C_{ij}$ coefficients, and included pion exchange for mixing $PB$ and $VB$ states. The scattering matrix was obtained via the Bethe-Salpeter equation with cut-off regularization, and box diagrams with intermediate $DN$ and $D^*N$ states were evaluated to break the degeneracy between $J^P=1/2^-$ and $J^P=3/2^-$ states.

## Key numbers

- The $\Lambda_c(2595)$ state with $J^P = 1/2^-$ and the $\Lambda_c(2625)$ state with $J^P = 3/2^-$ are reproduced with nearly zero width, with the $J^P = 3/2^-$ state tuned to 2628 MeV using cut-off parameters.
- For $I=0$ and $J=3/2$, a second state is found at approximately 2990 MeV with an uncertainty of $\pm 20$ MeV and a width $\Gamma < 2$ MeV, depending on cut-off choices (e.g., 2969.64 MeV with $q_{max}^B = 600$ MeV, $q_{max}^V = 771$ MeV; 3004.21 MeV with $q_{max}^B = 1000$ MeV, $q_{max}^V = 715$ MeV).
- The pion decay constant used is $f_\pi = 93$ MeV, and the vector meson mass $m_V \approx 780$ MeV is used for the coupling $g = m_V / 2 f_\pi$.
- The ratio of $D^*$ to $K^*$ propagators near $DN$ threshold is approximately $1/4$, used to suppress heavy vector exchange transitions.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Liang et al. (2013) from the beauty sector (where $\Lambda_b(5912)$ and $\Lambda_b(5920)$ were obtained) to the charm sector, using the same local hidden gauge formalism with heavy quark spin symmetry. It contrasts with the SU(8) spin-flavor scheme of Garcia-Recio et al. (2008) and Romanets et al. (2012), which also obtained $\Lambda_c(2595)$ and $\Lambda_c(2625)$ but without explicit pion-exchange dynamics. The results enable predictions for additional $I=0$ and $I=1$ baryon states with open charm, including four more $I=0$ states and three $I=1$ states, which can be tested in future experiments.
