# Squeezed back-to-back correlations of bosons with nonzero widths in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We derive the formulas for calculating the squeezed back-to-back correlation (SBBC) between a boson and antiboson with nonzero width produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The SBBCs of $D^0$ and $ϕ$ mesons with finite in-medium widths are studied. We find that the finite width can change the pattern of the SBBC function of $D^0{\bar D}^0$ with respect to mass. However, the SBBC function of $ϕϕ$ is insensitive to the width. In the high-particle-momentum region, the SBBC function of $ϕϕ$ increases with particle momentum rapidly and can exceed that of $D^0{\bar D}^0$ whether the width is nonzero or not.

# Squeezed back-to-back correlations of bosons with nonzero widths in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The finite in-medium width can significantly change the pattern of the squeezed back-to-back correlation (SBBC) function of D0D0 pairs with respect to mass, but the SBBC function of φφ pairs is insensitive to the width. In the high-particle-momentum region, the SBBC function of φφ increases with particle momentum rapidly and can exceed that of D0D0 whether the width is nonzero or not, though the diagonalization of the in-medium Hamiltonian is an approximation because the time-decayed term was removed.

## Method
The authors derived formulas for the SBBC function between a boson and antiboson with nonzero in-medium width by performing a Bogoliubov transformation between the annihilation and creation operators of quasiparticles in the medium and the corresponding free particles, extending previous zero-width treatments. They then calculated the SBBC functions for D0 and φ mesons using the derived expressions, incorporating the Bose-Einstein distribution of quasiparticles and a time suppression factor of the form (1+4ωk2Δt2)-1 for an exponential decay time-profile function.

## Key numbers
- D0 meson vacuum mass m0 = 1864.86 MeV/c2 and vacuum width Γ0 = 0 MeV/c2 (PDG: 1.60×10-9 MeV/c2, corresponding to mean life 4.10×10-15 s)
- φ meson vacuum mass m0 = 1019.46 MeV/c2 and vacuum width Γ0 = 4.26 MeV/c2
- SBBC functions calculated at particle momentum |k| = 1000 MeV/c with Δt = 2 fm/c
- In-medium mass shift Δm = -10 MeV/c2 used for momentum-dependent calculations
- In-medium widths Γ = Γ0 and 10 MeV/c2 used for momentum-dependent calculations
- At very high momenta, the SBBC function behavior is approximately determined by 16k4/[4m02Δm2+m02(Γ-Γ0)2]
- Average width of D0 in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC is approximately 30 MeV/c2, corresponding to characteristic size cτ ~ 6.6 fm

## Context
This work extends the formalism of squeezed back-to-back correlations originally developed by Asa and Csorgo (1996) and Asa, Csorgo, and Gyulassy (1999), and later applied by Padula (2006), Dudek and Padula (2010), and Zhang (2015, 2016) for bosons with zero width. The authors generalize the approach to include nonzero in-medium widths, which is necessary because the in-medium mass modification of bosons includes both a mass shift and an increase of width. The results enable more realistic calculations of SBBC for D and φ mesons, which are of interest because charm and strange quarks are believed to experience the entire evolution of the quark-gluon plasma created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The work also notes that the diagonalization approximation is valid for D0 because its cτ (~6.6 fm) is smaller than the typical source size, while for φ mesons the cτ is comparable to the source size, leaving open the need for further refinement.
