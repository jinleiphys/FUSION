# Jet substructure measurements in heavy-ion collisions

> Jet substructure in heavy-ion collisions is a rapidly evolving area with lots of intriguing new measurements. This contribution presents a selection of recent jet-substructure measurements from experiments at the LHC, in particular, soft-drop groomed radii of jets and reclustered large-radius jets from ATLAS, jet axis difference and generalized jet angularities from ALICE, as well as dijet shapes and b-jet shapes from the CMS experiment.

# Jet substructure measurements in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
While jet substructure measurements show strong dependence of jet suppression on internal jet properties and a general narrowing of the jet core in heavy-ion collisions, there is currently no clear evidence for point-like scattering centers in the quark-gluon plasma. The measurements favor decoherence-based energy loss mechanisms over models with Moliere scattering.

## Method
The paper reviews recent jet substructure measurements from ATLAS, CMS, and ALICE at the LHC using Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV. Measurements include soft-drop groomed jet radii and reclustered large-radius jets (ATLAS), jet axis differences and generalized jet angularities (ALICE), and dijet shapes and b-jet shapes (CMS), all compared to theoretical models such as the Hybrid model and JETSCAPE.

## Key numbers
- CMS measured nuclear modification factor (R_AA) for large-radius jets in 0-10% central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, showing substantial suppression at high momenta from small to large jet radii
- ATLAS measured R_AA as a function of jet pT for four intervals of groomed jet radius r_g, finding jets with wider opening angles lose significantly more energy than jets with smaller r_g
- ATLAS measured R_AA for R=1 reclustered jets as a function of sqrt(d_1,2) in four centrality intervals, showing significant difference between single-subjet and multi-subjet large-radius jets
- ALICE measured groomed angularities of charged-particle R=0.2 jets with 40 < pT < 60 GeV/c in Pb-Pb and pp collisions, observing a shift toward lower angularities indicating narrowing of groomed jets
- CMS measured Pb-Pb to pp ratio for leading jet shapes in 0-10% centrality class for different x_j values, showing stronger energy redistribution for balanced dijets
- CMS measured ratio of b-jet to inclusive-jet shapes for 0-10% centrality Pb-Pb collisions, finding enhancement relative to pp indicating stronger medium response to heavier quarks

## Context
This review extends the program of jet quenching measurements first established by PHENIX (2001) by moving beyond inclusive jet suppression to detailed substructure observables that probe energy loss mechanisms. The measurements test specific theoretical predictions including the Hybrid model of Casalderrey-Solana (2014) which includes decoherence effects, and the JETSCAPE framework of Putschke (2019). The results enable discrimination between coherent and incoherent energy loss scenarios and constrain the medium resolution length, with future Run 3 data expected to provide access to energy-energy correlators, photon-tagged systems, and extended heavy-flavor measurements.
