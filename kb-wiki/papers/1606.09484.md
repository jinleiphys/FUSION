# Polyakov loop and heavy quark entropy in strong magnetic fields from holographic black hole engineering

> We investigate the temperature and magnetic field dependence of the Polyakov loop and heavy quark entropy in a bottom-up Einstein-Maxwell-dilaton (EMD) holographic model for the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (QGP) that quantitatively matches lattice data for the $(2+1)$-flavor QCD equation of state at finite magnetic field and physical quark masses. We compare the holographic EMD model results for the Polyakov loop at zero and nonzero magnetic fields and the heavy quark entropy at vanishing magnetic field with the latest lattice data available for these observables and find good agreement for temperatures $T\gtrsim 150$ MeV and magnetic fields $eB\lesssim 1$ GeV$^2$. Predictions for the behavior of the heavy quark entropy at nonzero magnetic fields are made that could be readily tested on the lattice.

## Key claim

The holographic Einstein-Maxwell-dilaton (EMD) model quantitatively reproduces lattice QCD results for the Polyakov loop and heavy quark entropy only for temperatures \(T\gtrsim 150\) MeV and magnetic fields \(eB\lesssim 1\) GeV\(^2\), and the heavy quark entropy at nonzero magnetic field is predicted to decrease with increasing \(B\) in a narrow temperature region between 150 and 300 MeV.

## Method

The authors construct a bottom-up anisotropic EMD holographic model with a dilaton potential \(V(\phi)\) and Maxwell-dilaton coupling \(f(\phi)\) dynamically fixed by matching the lattice QCD equation of state and magnetic susceptibility at zero magnetic field from Borsanyi (2013) and Bonati (2013). They solve the coupled equations of motion for the EMD fields with a constant magnetic field \(\vec{B}\) pointing in the \(z\)-direction, then compute the regularized free energy of a static heavy quark via the on-shell Nambu-Goto action for a straight string extending from the boundary to the black hole horizon, using the string-frame metric \(g_{\mu\nu}^{(s)} = e^{\sqrt{2/3}\,\phi} g_{\mu\nu}\).

## Key numbers

* The holographic model matches lattice data for the renormalized Polyakov loop at zero and nonzero magnetic fields for \(T\gtrsim 150\) MeV and \(eB\lesssim 1\) GeV\(^2\), using an effective t'Hooft coupling \(\sqrt{\lambda_t} = 1450\).
* The heavy quark entropy ratio (relative to its value at \(T=200\) MeV, \(B=0\)) agrees with lattice data from Bazavov (2016) for \(T\gtrsim 150\) MeV at \(B=0\), with no free parameters.
* The model parameters are: \(G_5 = 0.46\), \(\Lambda = 1058.83\) MeV, and the dilaton potential \(V(\phi) = -12\cosh(0.63\phi) + 0.65\phi^2 - 0.05\phi^4 + 0.003\phi^6\).
* The Maxwell-dilaton coupling is \(f(\phi) = 0.95\,\text{sech}(0.22\phi^2 - 0.15\phi - 0.32)\).

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic EMD model of Rougemont (2015) and Finazzo (2016), which previously matched the lattice QCD equation of state at finite magnetic field from Bali (2014), to the Polyakov loop and heavy quark entropy. It provides the first holographic predictions for the heavy quark entropy at nonzero magnetic field, which can be tested on the lattice, and demonstrates that the scalar field \(\phi\) must be interpreted as the dilaton (string-frame coupling) to achieve quantitative agreement with QCD data, as opposed to using the Einstein frame metric directly.
