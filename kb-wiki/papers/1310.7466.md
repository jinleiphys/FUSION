# Transport coefficients of the QGP

> The FASTSUM collaboration presents a study on the temperature dependence of the electrical conductivity $σ$ in the quark-gluon plasma, using the methods of lattice QCD. Correlators of the exactly conserved vector current are measured at different temperatures across the deconfinement transition, using ensembles of $2+1$ flavours of dynamical fermions on anisotropic lattices. We then employ bayesian methods (MEM) to extract the relevant spectral functions, which are found to be consistent with $σ/T$ rising as a function of $T$. The robustness of the results is verified by a detailed analysis of the systematics involved in the bayesian reconstruction of the spectral functions.

## Key claim
The FASTSUM collaboration presents the first lattice QCD calculation of the temperature dependence of the electrical conductivity \(\sigma/T\) across the deconfinement transition, finding that \(\sigma/T\) rises with temperature. The result is qualified by a detailed analysis of systematic uncertainties in the Bayesian reconstruction of spectral functions, with stability achieved only when the default model parameter \(b \gtrsim 0.4\).

## Method
The authors used clover-improved Wilson fermions with \(2+1\) flavours on anisotropic lattices (spatial spacing \(a_s = 0.1227(8)\) fm, temporal spacing \(a_\tau = 0.03506(23)\) fm, anisotropy \(\xi = 3.5\)) to measure Euclidean correlators of the exactly conserved vector current at eight temperatures across the deconfinement transition. They employed the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) with a Shannon-Jaynes entropy default model \(m(\omega) = m_0 (b + \omega) \omega\) to perform the analytic continuation and extract spectral functions, from which the electrical conductivity was obtained via Kubo's formula \(\sigma/T = (1/6T) \lim_{\omega \to 0} \rho^{\text{em}}(\omega)/\omega\). Disconnected diagrams were neglected, and the up and down quark contributions were combined with the charge factor \(C_{\text{em}} = 5/9 e^2\).

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing ratio: spatial \(a_s = 0.1227(8)\) fm, temporal \(a_\tau = 0.03506(23)\) fm, anisotropy \(\xi = a_s/a_\tau = 3.5\).
- Light quark mass tuned to give \(M_\pi/M_\rho = 0.446(3)\).
- Eight temperatures studied: \(T = 117, 141, 156, 176, 201, 235, 281, 352\) MeV, corresponding to \(T/T_c = 0.63, 0.76, 0.84, 0.95, 1.09, 1.27, 1.52, 1.90\).
- Critical temperature \(T_c\) estimated from the renormalized Polyakov loop inflection point.
- MEM default model parameter \(b\) must satisfy \(b \gtrsim 0.4\) for stability of the conductivity result.

## Context
This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of the electrical conductivity by Aarts et al. (2007) and others using \(N_f=0\) (quenched) and \(N_f=2\) dynamical fermions, and also compares with Dyson-Schwinger equation results. The use of anisotropic lattices with clover-improved Wilson fermions provides higher temporal resolution than staggered fermion formulations, which suffer from opposite parity partner contamination. The temperature-dependent results enable future extraction of the charge diffusion constant \(D\) when combined with electric charge susceptibility calculations, and are relevant for modeling electromagnetic field evolution in heavy-ion collision experiments.
