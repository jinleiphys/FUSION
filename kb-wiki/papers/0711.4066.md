# Quantum reflection and dwell times of metastable states

> The concept of phase and dwell times used in tunneling is extended to quantum collisions to derive a relation between the phase and dwell time delays in scattering. This relation can be used to remove the near threshold s-wave singularities in the Wigner-Eisenbud delay times and amounts to an extension of the concept of quantum reflection to strong interactions. Dwell time delay emerges as the quantity which gives the correct behaviour of the density of states of a metastable state at all energies. This fact is demonstrated by investigating some recently found metastable states of mesic-nuclei.

# Quantum reflection and dwell times of metastable states

## Key claim

The dwell time delay, rather than the standard Wigner-Eisenbud phase time delay, gives the correct behavior of the density of states for metastable states at all energies, because the self-interference term in the phase time delay produces a near-threshold s-wave singularity that can be removed by identifying it with quantum reflection in scattering.

## Method

The author derives a relation between phase time delay and dwell time delay in elastic scattering by extending a known relation from one-dimensional tunneling problems, identifying the reflection amplitude in tunneling with quantum reflection in three-dimensional s-wave collisions. The dwell time delay is then evaluated for eta-meson elastic scattering on helium-3 and helium-4 nuclei using t-matrices from few-body calculations within the finite rank approximation, for several models of the elementary eta-nucleon interaction.

## Key numbers

- For the eta-He-3 system with the largest eta-nucleon scattering length of a_etaN = (0.88, 0.41) fm, a resonance appears just above threshold at about 0.5 MeV excitation energy, with a clear Lorentzian distribution in the dwell time delay.
- For a_etaN = (0.28, 0.19) fm in the eta-He-3 system, a broad peak appears around -5 MeV, in agreement with experimental findings of eta-mesic helium.
- For the eta-He-4 system with a_etaN = (0.28, 0.19) fm, a bump appears at a negative energy of -2 MeV.
- For the eta-He-4 system with the larger scattering length model, the real part of the eta-He-4 scattering length is Re[a_eta4He] = -3.94 fm, indicating a repulsive interaction and producing a negative time delay (time advancement).

## Context

This work extends the relation between phase and dwell times from tunneling problems (Winful, 2003; Hauge and Stovneng, 1989) to quantum collisions, and applies the concept of quantum reflection (previously observed in ultracold atoms and molecules) to strong interactions in nuclear physics. The method enables the location of s-wave metastable states near threshold, which is demonstrated for eta-mesic nuclear states that have been the subject of recent experimental claims (Pfeiffer et al., 2004) and theoretical models (Fix and Arenhovel, 1997; Bhalerao and Liu, 1985; Green and Wycech, 1995). The dwell time delay approach removes the threshold singularity that makes the standard Wigner time delay (Wigner, 1955) unreliable for locating resonances in s-wave scattering near threshold.
