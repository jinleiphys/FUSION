# Weak charge and weak radius of ${}^{12}$C

> We present a feasibility study of a simultaneous sub-percent extraction of the weak charge and the weak radius of the ${}^{12}$C nucleus using parity-violating electron scattering, based on a largely model-independent assessment of the uncertainties. The corresponding measurement is considered to be carried out at the future MESA facility in Mainz with $E_{\rm beam} = 155$ MeV. We find that a combination of a $0.3\%$ precise measurement of the parity-violating asymmetry at forward angles with a $10\%$ measurement at backward angles will allow to determine the weak charge and the weak radius of ${}^{12}$C with $0.4\%$ and $0.5\%$ precision, respectively. These values could be improved to $0.3\%$ and $0.2\%$ for a $3\%$ backward measurement. This experimental program will have impact on precision low-energy tests in the electroweak sector and nuclear structure.

## Key claim

A simultaneous sub-percent extraction of both the weak charge and the weak radius of 12C is feasible using parity-violating electron scattering at the MESA facility, but only if a forward-angle measurement at 0.3% precision is combined with a backward-angle measurement at 3-10% precision; a single forward measurement alone cannot separate the weak mixing angle from nuclear-structure uncertainties even with theory input.

## Method

The authors performed distorted-wave calculations of the parity-violating asymmetry A_PV for elastic electron scattering off 12C at a beam energy of 155 MeV, solving the Dirac equation with Coulomb and axial-vector potentials using the ELSEPA package. They parametrized the weak charge distribution via a symmetrized Fermi distribution and introduced a "weak-skin" parameter λ = (R_wk - R_ch)/R_ch to separate the dependence on the weak radius from higher-moment model dependence. A chi-squared fit of combined forward (29°) and backward (145°) measurements was performed with three free parameters: sin²θ_W, λ, and a model parameter ζ, using five nuclear-structure models (four relativistic and one non-relativistic Skyrme-type energy density functional) to quantify the model uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: E_beam = 155 MeV (fixed for the proposed measurement at MESA)
- Charge radius of 12C: R_ch = 2.4702(22) fm (from world data on elastic electron-carbon scattering)
- Central value of the weak-skin parameter from nuclear models: λ_0 = -0.90%
- Range of predicted weak-skin parameters from models: |λ_SF| ≲ 2%
- Forward-angle measurement precision assumed: 0.3% in A_PV
- For a 10% backward measurement: δ sin²θ_W / sin²θ_W = ±0.39%; weak radius determined to 0.5% precision
- For a 3% backward measurement: δ sin²θ_W / sin²θ_W = ±0.32%; weak radius determined to 0.2% precision relative to R_ch (i.e., R_wk within 0.19% of R_ch)
- Backward-angle asymmetry variation from varying λ over the model range: ±24% at θ = 145°
- Coefficients from distorted-wave calculation at forward angle (θ=29°): p_0 = +0.04005, p_1 = -0.50612, p_2 = -0.06969
- Coefficients from distorted-wave calculation at backward angle (θ=145°): p_0 = +0.09586, p_1 = -29.5132, p_2 = -3.86420

## Context

This work extends the methodology of parity-violating electron scattering (PVES) previously applied to heavy nuclei like 208Pb in the PREX experiment (Abrahamyan 2012) and to the proton in the Qweak experiment (Qweak Collaboration) to the light, symmetric nucleus 12C. The proposed program at the MESA facility in Mainz (Becker 2018) would complement the P2 experiment's plan for a 0.3% determination of the weak charge of 12C by adding a backward-angle measurement to disentangle nuclear-structure effects. The results will impact precision low-energy tests of the electroweak sector and, through the determination of the weak skin of symmetric nuclei, help quantify isospin symmetry-breaking effects relevant to superallowed nuclear beta decays and the extraction of V_ud (Hardy 2014).
