# The microscopic theory of fission

> Fission-fragment properties have been calculated for thermal neutron-induced fission on a $^{239}\textrm{Pu}$ target, using constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with a finite-range effective interaction. A quantitative criterion based on the interaction energy between the nascent fragments is introduced to define the scission configurations. The validity of this criterion is benchmarked against experimental measurements of the kinetic energies and of multiplicities of neutrons emitted by the fragments.

## Key claim

The authors introduce a quantitative criterion for defining scission configurations in fission based on the interaction energy between nascent fragments, rather than relying on the more ambiguous neck-size parameter. The criterion identifies scission as the point where the energy available in the system (measured from the top of the second fission barrier) equals the attractive interaction energy between the fragments.

## Method

Constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the D1S finite-range effective interaction were performed for thermal neutron-induced fission of 239Pu, using a one-center axially deformed harmonic-oscillator basis with up to 27 shells. The scission criterion was implemented by progressively decreasing the neck-size constraint QN in small increments (typically ΔQN = 0.05) and calculating the interaction energy between fragments after applying a unitary transformation to reduce the tails of single-particle wave functions extending into the complementary fragment. Fragment properties were extracted by integrating densities to the left and right of the neck position along the symmetry axis.

## Key numbers

- The neck-size constraint operator uses a Gaussian width parameter aN = 1 fm²
- The neck-size constraint QN was decreased in increments of ΔQN = 0.05 when approaching scission
- 30 iterations of the tail-reduction procedure were sufficient to reach a decrease in tail size of less than 0.5% between subsequent iterations
- For symmetric fission (Q30 = 0), the nucleus reached an extreme elongation of Q20 = 595 b at scission
- The calculated total kinetic energies (TKE) agree with three experimental measurements to better than 15% across all fragment masses
- The experimental TKE values near symmetric fission vary widely from approximately 153 to 169 MeV
- The calculated neutron multiplicity near A = 120 is ν ≈ 4.1, while the most recent data from Batenkov et al. (2004) show ν ≈ 4.4 near A = 117
- The average kinetic energy of emitted neutrons was taken as Kn = 2 MeV in the neutron multiplicity calculation

## Context

This work extends the microscopic fission program developed at Bruyères-le-Châtel over three decades (Berger et al., 1984; Goutte et al., 2005; Dubray et al., 2008) by introducing a quantitative scission criterion based on interaction energy rather than neck size. The calculations reproduce the experimentally observed decrease in TKE near symmetric fission and the dip in neutron multiplicity around the nearly-spherical A = 130 fragments. The approach enables future extension to more points along the scission line and full dynamical treatment of fission using the methods developed by Berger et al. (1984) and Goutte et al. (2005).
