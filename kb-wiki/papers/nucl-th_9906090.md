# What does `rho-exchange' in piN scattering mean?

> We present an alternative method for calculating amplitudes for correlated pi pi exchange in the ``sigma'' and rho channel in piN scattering. Starting from a fixed mass meson exchange potential, we introduce the width of the exchanged particles by integrating over a mass spectral function. The spectral functions are constructed from the pseudoempirical N\bar{N} -> pi pi data. Using this approach we develop a prescription for resolving ambiguities of the correlated pi pi exchange in the rho channel that occur when different dispersion theoretical formulations of rho exchange are used to construct piN potentials.

## Key claim

The paper presents an alternative method for calculating correlated ππ exchange amplitudes in πN scattering that resolves ambiguities in the ρ channel that arise when different dispersion theoretical formulations are used; the method yields on-shell amplitudes that agree with dispersion relations over the Γ amplitudes of Höhler and Pietarinen, but the key hedge is that the off-shell behavior differs between approaches and must be chosen compatibly with the scattering formalism used.

## Method

The authors start from a fixed-mass meson exchange potential and introduce the width of exchanged particles by integrating over a mass spectral function, constructed from pseudoempirical N̄N → ππ data via the Frazer-Fulco f amplitudes. For the σ channel, they relate the spectral function to the dressed σ propagator extracted from the N̄N → ππ T-matrix, then compare with dispersion-theoretic results. For the ρ channel, they test two assignments of spectral functions, one assigning separate spectral functions to the A and B amplitudes, another assigning spectral functions to the vector and tensor couplings separately, and show both yield identical amplitudes, unlike the ambiguous dispersion relation approaches.

## Key numbers

- The σ-channel on-shell amplitudes from the spectral function method and from dispersion theory agree exactly, as the factors (−2p₂_μp⁴_μ) and (t − 2m_π²) become equal on-shell.
- The ρ-channel coupling constants g_{ρππ}g_v/4π, g_{ρππ}g_t/4π, and the ratio κ = g_t/g_v differ substantially depending on which dispersion relation formulation is used (solid, dashed, or dotted curves in Fig. 1), illustrating the ambiguity.
- The spectral function method yields amplitudes in the ρ channel that match the dispersion relation over the Γ amplitudes (eq. 4.6 in the paper), which corresponds to the solid curves in Fig. 1.
- The relation p_t² = t/4 − m_N² is used, with m_N the nucleon mass and m_π the pion mass.

## Context

This work extends prior meson-exchange models of the two-nucleon interaction (Cottingham 1973, Brown and Jackson 1976, Durso 1980, Machleidt 1987) and earlier πN and KN scattering calculations (Schütz 1994, Hoffmann 1995) that incorporated correlated two-pion exchange but encountered ambiguities in the ρ channel. The paper resolves the ambiguity identified by Schütz (1995) among three dispersion relation formulations, those using f amplitudes directly, those using Γ amplitudes (Höhler and Pietarinen 1975), and those using g and h amplitudes (Richard, private communication), by showing that the spectral function method selects the Γ-amplitude formulation as the correct one. This enables unambiguous construction of πN potentials for use in scattering equations and other hadronic reactions.
