# Direct quarkonium production in DIS from a joint CGC and NRQCD framework

> We compute the differential cross section for direct quarkonium production in high-energy electron-nucleus collisions at small $x$. Our computation is performed within the nonrelativistic QCD factorization formalism that separates the calculation into short distance coefficients and long distance matrix elements that depend on the color and spin of the state. We obtain the short distance coefficients of the production of the heavy quark pair within the framework of the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory, which resums coherent multiple interactions of the heavy quark pair with the nucleus to all orders. Our results are expressed as the convolution of perturbatively calculable perturbative functions with multi-point light-like Wilson line correlators. In the correlation limit, we establish the correspondence between our CGC formulation with calculations employing the transverse momentum dependent (TMD) framework. We extend this correspondence by resumming kinematic power corrections within the improved TMD framework, which interpolates between the TMD formalism and $k_\perp$ factorization formalism. We present a detailed numerical analysis, focusing on $J/ψ$ production in the kinematics accessible at the future Electron-Ion Collider, highlighting the importance of genuine higher-order saturation contributions when the electron collides with a large nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first computation of direct quarkonium production in deep inelastic scattering at small x within a combined Color Glass Condensate and nonrelativistic QCD framework, claiming that genuine higher-order saturation contributions become important when the electron collides with a large nucleus at kinematics accessible at the future Electron-Ion Collider.

## Method

The authors compute the short-distance coefficients for heavy quark pair production within the CGC effective field theory, which resums coherent multiple interactions of the heavy quark pair with the nucleus to all orders. These coefficients are then combined with NRQCD long-distance matrix elements to obtain the differential cross section for direct quarkonium production, including both color octet and singlet contributions as well as S-wave and P-wave states. The results are expressed as convolutions of perturbatively calculable functions with multi-point light-like Wilson line correlators, and the correspondence with transverse momentum dependent factorization is established in the correlation limit.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale Q_s^2 is compared to the hard scales Q^2 and M_Q^2 (the quarkonium mass), with the condition that TMD factorization holds when Q_s^2 is sufficiently smaller than both Q^2 and M_Q^2
- The effective virtuality is defined as overline{Q}_xi^2 = (1/2 + xi)(1/2 - xi)Q^2 + m_Q^2, where xi = k^+/q^+ and m_Q is the heavy quark mass
- The analysis focuses on J/psi production with transverse momentum p_perp less than or approximately 5 GeV in the semi-hard regime
- The differential cross section decomposition includes terms with cos(phi_eH) and cos(2 phi_eH) azimuthal modulations, where phi_eH is the relative azimuthal angle between the electron and produced quarkonium

## Context

This work extends the CGC + NRQCD framework previously developed for proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions at RHIC and the LHC by Kang (2013) and subsequent phenomenological studies by Ma (2014-2018) and Salazar (2021), now applying it to electron-nucleus collisions for the first time. The computation enables predictions for direct quarkonium production at the future Electron-Ion Collider, where the saturation scale is expected to be comparable to the J/psi mass, and establishes connections between the CGC formalism and TMD factorization approaches previously used by Bacchetta (2018) and Boer (2021-2023) for quarkonium production.
