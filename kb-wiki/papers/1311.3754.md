# Determination of the axial nucleon form factor from the MiniBooNE data

> Both neutrino and antineutrino charged-current quasi-elastic scattering on a carbon target are studied to investigate the nuclear effect on the determination of the axial form factor F_A(Q^2). A method for extraction of F_A(Q^2) from the flux-integrated $dσ/dQ^2$ cross section of (anti)neutrino scattering on nuclei is presented. Data from the MiniBooNE experiment are analyzed in the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation, Fermi gas model, and in the Fermi gas model with enhancements in the transverse cross section. We found that the values of the axial form factor, extracted in the impulse approximation and predicted by the dipole approximation with the axial mass M_A~1.37 GeV are in good agreement. On the other hand, the Q^2-dependence of F_A extracted in the approach with the transverse enhancement is found to differ significantly from the dipole approximation.

## Key claim

The authors present a method to extract the axial nucleon form factor F_A(Q^2) directly from flux-integrated differential cross sections of neutrino and antineutrino charged-current quasielastic scattering on nuclei, rather than assuming a dipole parametrization. They find that the extracted F_A(Q^2) agrees with the dipole approximation with axial mass M_A approximately 1.37 GeV when using the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation or Fermi gas model, but the Q^2-dependence of F_A extracted using the transverse enhancement model differs significantly from the dipole approximation.

## Method

The authors calculated flux-integrated vector, axial, and vector-axial cross sections for neutrino and antineutrino CCQE scattering on carbon using the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA), the relativistic Fermi gas model (RFGM), and the RFGM with transverse enhancement (RFGM+TE). They then solved for F_A(Q^2) as a function of Q^2 by inserting the MiniBooNE measured flux-integrated dσ/dQ^2 cross sections into the expression relating these cross sections to the vector, axial, and vector-axial components, using the booster neutrino beam line fluxes for both neutrino and antineutrino modes.

## Key numbers

- Axial mass M_A = 1.37 GeV used in RDWIA calculations, yielding good agreement between extracted F_A(Q^2) and dipole parametrization
- Axial mass M_A = 1.36 GeV used in RFGM calculations, yielding similar extracted F_A(Q^2) values to the RDWIA result
- Axial mass M_A = 1.014 GeV used in RFGM+TE model, for which the extracted F_A(Q^2) shape could not be well described by the dipole ansatz
- The MiniBooNE "shape-only" fit yielded M_A = 1.35 ± 0.17 GeV and κ = 1.007 ± 0.012 within the Fermi gas model with dipole parametrization
- At Q^2 ≤ 0.05 (GeV/c)^2, the RFGM results were higher than RDWIA by approximately 10% for ⟨σ^V⟩, 12% for ⟨σ^A⟩, and 8% for ⟨σ^{VA}⟩
- The meson-dominance ansatz used masses m_{a_1} = 1.230 GeV and m_{a'_1} = 1.647 GeV with Γ_{a_1} = 0.425 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous extractions of F_A(Q^2) from neutrino-deuterium scattering by Budd et al. to nuclear targets, addressing the discrepancy between the world-averaged axial mass of M_A = 1.026 ± 0.021 GeV from Bernard et al. and the larger value M_A = 1.35 ± 0.17 GeV reported by the MiniBooNE Collaboration. The results enable model-independent tests of the dipole parametrization assumption for the axial form factor, and show that the transverse enhancement model of Bodek et al., which reproduces MiniBooNE total cross sections with M_A = 1.014 GeV, requires a non-dipole parametrization of F_A(Q^2) for a self-consistent description of the data.
