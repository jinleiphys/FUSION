# RPA Correlations and Nuclear Densities in Relativistic Mean Field Approach

> The relativistic mean field approach (RMF) is well known for describing accurately binding energies and nucleon distributions in atomic nuclei throughout the nuclear chart. The random phase approximation (RPA) built on top of the RMF is also a good framework for the study of nuclear excitations. Here, we examine the consequences of long range correlations brought about by the RPA on the neutron and proton densities as given by the RMF approach.

## Key claim
RPA long-range correlations produce modest but non-negligible corrections to the RMF ground-state densities of magic nuclei, with the net effect being small due to strong cancellations between particle and hole contributions; the corrections are of order 0.4%–1% on root-mean-square radii.

## Method
The authors solve the relativistic mean field (RMF) equations with the density-dependent DD-ME2 Lagrangian for the spherical magic nuclei 16O, 40Ca, and 48Ca. They then build the complete single-particle basis including both positive-energy (Fermi) and negative-energy (Dirac) states, diagonalize the relativistic RPA (RRPA) secular matrix for natural-parity multipoles up to J=8 (16O) or J=10 (40,48Ca), and use the resulting Y-amplitudes to compute the one-body density matrix of the correlated RPA ground state via Eqs. (7)–(8). The center-of-mass spurious 1− state is identified and removed.

## Key numbers
- 16O: RMF mass rms radius = 2.594 fm; RMF+RPA mass rms radius = 2.614 fm (change ~0.8%)
- 16O: RMF charge rms radius = 2.729 fm; RMF+RPA charge rms radius = 2.747 fm (change ~0.7%)
- 40Ca: RMF mass rms radius = 3.346 fm; RMF+RPA mass rms radius = 3.385 fm (change ~1.2%)
- 40Ca: RMF charge rms radius = 3.466 fm; RMF+RPA charge rms radius = 3.501 fm (change ~1.0%)
- 48Ca: RMF neutron rms radius = 3.576 fm; RMF+RPA neutron rms radius = 3.592 fm (change ~0.4%)
- 48Ca: RMF proton rms radius = 3.389 fm; RMF+RPA proton rms radius = 3.420 fm (change ~0.9%)
- 48Ca: RMF mass rms radius = 3.499 fm; RMF+RPA mass rms radius = 3.521 fm (change ~0.6%)
- 48Ca: RMF charge rms radius = 3.482 fm; RMF+RPA charge rms radius = 3.511 fm (change ~0.8%)
- The spurious 1− state energy is less than 500 keV from zero.
- The single-particle spectrum is truncated at E_cut = 120 MeV; box radius is 12 fm for 16O and 15 fm for Ca nuclei.

## Context
This work extends earlier non-relativistic HF-RPA studies of density corrections using the Gogny interaction (Gogny, 1979; Dupuis et al., 2006) to the relativistic RMF-RRPA framework with the DD-ME2 Lagrangian (Lalazissis et al., 2005). It demonstrates that the Dirac sea states contribute comparably to the Fermi states in the density corrections, a feature absent in non-relativistic approaches. The results enable future studies of unnatural-parity (magnetic) transitions once the effective Lagrangian is extended to include Hartree-Fock exchange and pion/rho tensor couplings (Long et al., 2006).
