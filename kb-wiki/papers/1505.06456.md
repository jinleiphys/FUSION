# Testing different formulations of leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics

> A recently obtained set of the equations for leading-order (3+1)D anisotropic hydrodynamics is tested against exact solutions of the Boltzmann equation with the collisional kernel treated in the relaxation time approximation. In order to perform the detailed comparisons, the new anisotropic hydrodynamics equations are reduced to the boost-invariant and transversally homogeneous case. The agreement with the exact solutions found using the new anisotropic hydrodynamics equations is similar to that found using previous, less general, formulations of anisotropic hydrodynamics. In addition, we find that, when compared to a state-of-the-art second-order viscous hydrodynamics framework, leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics better reproduces the exact solution for the pressure anisotropy and gives comparable results for the bulk pressure evolution. Finally, we compare the transport coefficients obtained using linearized anisotropic hydrodynamics with results obtained using second-order viscous hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The new (3+1)D leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics formulation, when reduced to the boost-invariant and transversally homogeneous case, shows agreement with exact solutions of the Boltzmann equation that is similar to previous, less general formulations; however, the choice of which moment equation to use for the bulk viscous pressure evolution affects the accuracy, with the zeroth moment being better for bulk pressure and the trace of the second moment being better for the pressure anisotropy ratio.

## Method

The authors reduced the general (3+1)D anisotropic hydrodynamics equations derived by Tinti (2014) to the (0+1)D Bjorken flow case (boost-invariant, transversally homogeneous) and solved them numerically for a massive gas with the Boltzmann collision kernel treated in the relaxation time approximation. They compared three different prescriptions for the bulk viscous pressure evolution equation: the trace of the second moment, the U-projection of the second moment, and the zeroth moment of the Boltzmann equation. The numerical solutions were compared against exact solutions of the Boltzmann equation and against second-order viscous hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers

- The expansion scalar in the (0+1)D case is θ = 1/τ, where τ is the longitudinal proper time.
- The shear stress tensor components in the (0+1)D case are σ⊥ = 1/(3τ) and σ∥ = -2/(3τ).
- The equilibrium energy density is given by εeq = 4πÑ T⁴ m̂eq² [3K₂(m̂eq) + m̂eq K₁(m̂eq)], where m̂eq = m/T.
- The equilibrium pressure is Peq = 4πÑ T⁴ m̂eq² K₂(m̂eq).
- The equilibrium number density is neq = (4πÑ) T³ m̂eq² K₂(m̂eq).

## Context

This work extends the leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics framework from earlier formulations restricted to (0+1)D and (1+1)D cases (Florkowski 2010, Martinez 2010, Ryblewski 2010) to a general (3+1)D formulation by Tinti (2014). It tests this new formulation against exact solutions of the Boltzmann equation, following the methodology of previous comparison studies (Florkowski 2013, Denicol 2014, Jaiswal 2014). The results enable phenomenological applications of the more general anisotropic hydrodynamics framework to relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, while identifying which moment equation is optimal for describing bulk viscous effects.
