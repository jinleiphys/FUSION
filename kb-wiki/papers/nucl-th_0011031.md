# Experimental Signatures of Anomaly Induced DCC Formation

> We discuss characteristic experimental signatures related to the formation of domains of disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) triggered by the axial anomaly in relativistic heavy ion collisions. We predict that the enhancement of the fraction of neutral pions compared to all pions depends on the angle of emission with respect to the scattering plane and is concentrated at small transverse momentum and small rapidity in the center-of-mass frame. The anisotropy with respect to the reaction plane is also observable in the inclusive photon distribution.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the enhancement of the neutral-to-charged pion ratio due to anomaly-induced DCC formation depends on the azimuthal angle of emission relative to the nuclear scattering plane, with the effect concentrated at small transverse momentum and small rapidity, and that this anisotropy persists in the inclusive photon distribution.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of 1000 events in the linear sigma model framework, using an anomaly kick parameter a_n = 0.1 (representative of Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV/A at RHIC). They calculated the ratio n_pi0/n_pi for low-momentum pions (|k| < 250 MeV) as a function of azimuthal angle phi, for both an infinitely extended system and a finite system with transverse extension R_0 = 5 fm, at three different times (tau = 1, 6, and 11 fm). They also repeated the simulation with R_0 = 5 fm for three different values of the kick amplitude a_n (0.05, 0.1, and 0.2) to study the Z-dependence, and calculated the azimuthal dependence of the inclusive photon distribution from decaying neutral pions.

## Key numbers

- The anomaly kick parameter a_n was taken as 0.1, which is the expected order for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV/A at RHIC.
- The finite system simulation used R_0 = 5 fm for the transverse extension of the system.
- Low-momentum pions were defined as those with |k| < 250 MeV.
- The peak value of the ratio R = n_pi0/n_pi as a function of a_n was fit to c + b (a_n)^alpha, yielding alpha approximately 1.8.
- Since a_n scales as Z^2, the simulation indicates the Z-dependence of the anomaly effect is roughly consistent with Z^4 dependence.
- The inclusive photon distribution was calculated for photons with 0 < k_T < 500 MeV, with most anisotropy due to photons with k_T < 250 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Asakawa, Minakata, Muller 1998; Minakata, Muller 1996) which demonstrated that the axial anomaly may enhance DCC domain formation in non-central nuclear collisions. The new result is the prediction of a specific azimuthal anisotropy of the pi0/pi ratio relative to the scattering plane, which provides a clear signature distinguishable from the elliptical flow observed in Pb+Pb collisions at CERN-SPS (WA98, NA49 collaborations) and Au+Au collisions at RHIC (STAR collaboration). The effect enables experimental searches for DCC formation using event-by-event determination of the collision plane via collective flow patterns, and could be observed through inclusive photon distributions without requiring full pi0 reconstruction.
