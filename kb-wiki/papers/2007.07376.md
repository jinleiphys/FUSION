# Resolution of hyper-triton chemical freeze-out puzzle in high energy nuclear collisions

> The recently developed hadron resonance gas model with multicomponent hard-core repulsion is used to address and resolve the long standing problem to describe the light nuclear cluster multiplicities including the hyper-triton measured by the STAR Collaboration, known as the hyper-triton chemical freeze-out puzzle. An unprecedentedly accurate description is obtained for the hadronic and other light nuclear cluster data measured by STAR at the collision energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =200$ GeV and by ALICE at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =2.76$ TeV. This success is achieved by applying the new strategy of analyzing the light nuclear cluster data and by using the value for the hard-core radius of the (anti-)$Λ$ hyperons found in earlier work. One of the most striking results of the present work is that for the most probable scenario of chemical freeze-out for the STAR energy the obtained parameters allow to simultaneously reproduce the values of the experimental ratios $S_3$ and $\bar{S}_3$ which were not included in the fit.

## Key claim
The paper claims to resolve the hyper-triton chemical freeze-out puzzle by using a hadron resonance gas model with multicomponent hard-core repulsion, where the key innovation is treating the hyper-triton's excluded volume as the sum of its constituent hadron volumes (two nucleons plus one Lambda hyperon) rather than using a simple baryon-number scaling, which had previously failed to reproduce the problematic hyper-triton ratios measured by STAR.

## Method
The authors employ the induced surface tension (IST) equation of state for a multicomponent hadron resonance gas model, fitting hadron yield ratios and light nuclear cluster yields measured by STAR at √sNN = 200 GeV and ALICE at √sNN = 2.76 TeV. They introduce two generalized approaches for the hyper-triton: the ISTΛ EoS, where the hyper-triton hard-core radius, eigensurface, and eigenvolume are sums of those of two baryons and one Lambda hyperon, and the BMRΛ EoS, which uses an effective hard-core radius derived from the excluded volume of the hyper-triton with pions in a pion-dominated medium. The fit uses fixed hadronic hard-core radii from prior work (Rπ = 0.15 fm, RK = 0.395 fm, RΛ = 0.085 fm, Rb = 0.365 fm, Rm = 0.42 fm) and considers both single and separate chemical freeze-out scenarios for hadrons and light nuclear clusters.

## Key numbers
- For STAR data at √sNN = 200 GeV, the separate CFO scenario with ISTΛ EoS yields a fit where the experimental ratios S3 and S̄3 (not included in the fit) are simultaneously reproduced.
- For ALICE data at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, the single CFO scenario gives CFO temperatures Th|BMRΛ = 150.29 ± 1.92 MeV and Th|ISTΛ = 150.39 ± 1.90 MeV, differing by only 0.1 MeV.
- The hard-core radius of (anti-)Λ hyperons used is RΛ = 0.085 fm, taken from earlier work (Sagun 2014, HRGM_IST1, HRGM_IST2, HRGM_IST3).
- For the separate CFO scenario at STAR energy, the hyper-triton CFO temperature was found to be TA = 167.3 ± 3.93 MeV with χ²A ≈ 9.6 using the older prescription, and TA = 240.3 ± 21.38 MeV with χ²A ≈ 4.92 using the BMR EoS (from prior work HRGM_IST6).

## Context
This work extends the induced surface tension hadron resonance gas model developed by Bugaev, Sagun, and collaborators (2015-2020) which had previously resolved the anti-Lambda hyperon puzzle by introducing a separate hard-core radius for Lambda hyperons. It directly addresses the hyper-triton chemical freeze-out puzzle, where simpler thermal models (Andronic 2017, Braun-Munzinger 2018) and coalescence models had failed to reproduce the STAR measurements of ³ΛH/³He and ³ΛH̄/³Hē ratios. The new generalized treatment of hyper-triton excluded volumes enables accurate description of all light nuclear cluster data simultaneously, and the authors argue this provides a tool to determine individual hard-core radii of short-lived hadrons like Lambda hyperons through their binding in loosely-bound nuclear clusters.
