# Comparing phenomenological estimates of dilepton decays of pseudoscalar mesons with lattice QCD

> Dilepton decays of pseudoscalar mesons have been drawing particular interest, thanks to their sensitivity to both the QCD dynamics at low energy and also signals beyond the Standard Model. In this context, we shortly review our recent study on an improved Standard-Model prediction for the rare decay $π^0\to e^+e^-$, and compare it with the first determination on the lattice that predicted also the $π^0\to γγ$ decay width as a byproduct. In addition, we discuss our recent work on $K_L\to\ell^+\ell^-$ decays and its connection to lattice QCD. We comment on the current uncertainty estimates and discuss how they could be improved profiting from future experiments and progress in lattice QCD.

# Comparing phenomenological estimates of dilepton decays of pseudoscalar mesons with lattice QCD

## Key claim
The dispersive Standard-Model prediction for $\pi^0\to e^+e^-$ achieves a ten-fold improvement in precision over the experimental measurement, while the prediction for $K_L\to\mu^+\mu^-$ approaches experimental precision; however, the lattice QCD determinations of the $\pi^0\to\gamma\gamma$ decay width systematically fall below the experimental value, indicating a tension that requires further scrutiny.

## Method
The authors constructed dispersive representations of the transition form factors (TFFs) for $\pi^0\to\gamma^*\gamma^*$ and $K_L\to\gamma^*\gamma^*$, incorporating constraints from analyticity, unitarity, and asymptotic behavior. For $K_L$, the TFF was separated into a dispersive part reconstructed from low-lying singularities (using $\pi\pi$ scattering phase shifts, $K_L\to\pi^+\pi^-\gamma$ data, and leptonic modes) and an asymptotic part from partonic contributions with resummation of leading and subleading logarithms, matched via a continuum threshold. The loop integrals for the dilepton amplitudes were then evaluated using these TFF representations.

## Key numbers
- $\pi^0\to e^+e^-$ branching fraction: $\text{Br}[\pi^0\to e^+e^-]|_\text{SM} = 6.25(3)\times 10^{-8}$, compared to experimental value $6.85(35)\times 10^{-8}$ (KTeV, with radiative corrections)
- $K_L\to\mu^+\mu^-$ branching fraction: $\text{Br}[K_L\to\mu^+\mu^-]|_\text{SM} = 7.44^{+0.41}_{-0.34}\times 10^{-9}$, compared to experimental value $6.84(11)\times 10^{-9}$
- $K_L\to e^+e^-$ branching fraction: $\text{Br}[K_L\to e^+e^-]|_\text{SM} = 8.46(37)\times 10^{-12}$, compared to experimental value $8.7^{+5.7}_{-4.1}\times 10^{-12}$
- Lattice QCD result for $\pi^0\to\gamma\gamma$ decay width: $\Gamma[\pi^0\to\gamma\gamma] = 6.60(91)\,\text{eV}$ (Christ 2022), compared to experimental value $0.2754(21)\,\text{GeV}^{-1}$ for the TFF normalization (PrimEx-II)
- Normalization of $K_L\gamma^*\gamma^*$ TFF: $F_{K_L\gamma^*\gamma^*}(0,0) = -3.389(14)\times 10^{-9}\,\text{GeV}^{-1}$

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous dispersive approach to hadronic light-by-light scattering and hadronic vacuum polarization for the muon anomalous magnetic moment (Hoferichter 2014, 2018, 2020; Hoid 2020). It provides improved Standard-Model predictions that supersede earlier phenomenological estimates (Isidori 2003; Masjuan 2015; Husek 2015; Weil 2017) and are compared with the first lattice QCD determination of $\pi^0\to e^+e^-$ (Christ 2022). The results enable improved constraints on beyond-Standard-Model physics, particularly for $K_L\to\mu^+\mu^-$, and motivate future experimental efforts at NA62, HIKE, and KOTO II, as well as extensions to $\eta^{(\prime)}$ and $K_S$ dilepton decays.
