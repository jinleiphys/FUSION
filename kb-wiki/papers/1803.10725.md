# Quantum Monte Carlo formalism for dynamical pions and nucleons

> In most simulations of nonrelativistic nuclear systems, the wave functions found solving the many-body Schrödinger equations describe the quantum-mechanical amplitudes of the nucleonic degrees of freedom. In those simulations the pionic contributions are encoded in nuclear potentials and electroweak currents, and they determine the low-momentum behavior. In this work we present an alternative quantum Monte Carlo formalism in which both relativistic pions and nonrelativistic nucleons are explicitly included in the quantum-mechanical states of the system. We report the renormalization of the nucleon mass as a function of the momentum cutoff, an Euclidean time density correlation function that deals with the short-time nucleon diffusion, and the pion cloud density and momentum distributions. In the two-nucleon sector we show that the interaction of two static nucleons at large distances reduces to the one-pion exchange potential, and we fit the low-energy constants of the contact interactions to reproduce the binding energy of the deuteron and two neutrons in finite volumes. We show that the method can be readily applied to light-nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors present a quantum Monte Carlo formalism that explicitly includes both relativistic pions and nonrelativistic nucleons as dynamical degrees of freedom, rather than integrating pions out into instantaneous nuclear potentials; they demonstrate that the method reproduces the one-pion exchange potential for static nucleons at large distances and can fit low-energy constants to deuteron and two-neutron properties, but note that the Weinberg-Tomozawa term is small enough to be treated perturbatively in their initial calculations and that the approach is currently limited to leading-order chiral-EFT.

## Method

The authors derive a Hamiltonian from the leading-order heavy-baryon chiral Lagrangian, expressing pion fields in the Schrödinger representation as a collection of harmonic oscillator modes with momentum cutoff \(k_c\) in a periodic box of side \(L\). They employ variational Monte Carlo and Green's function Monte Carlo techniques to solve the many-body Schrödinger equation for systems of one and two nucleons coupled to the pion field, using trial wave functions constructed from pion-mode amplitudes and nucleon spin-isospin states with Born-Oppenheimer-inspired correlations. The low-energy constants \(C_S\) and \(C_T\) of the contact interactions are fitted to reproduce the deuteron binding energy and two-neutron properties in finite volumes.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92\) MeV and nucleon axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A = 1.26\) are used as input parameters.
- The pion mode frequencies are \(\omega_{\mathbf{k}} = \sqrt{k^2 + m_\pi^2}\), where \(m_\pi\) is the physical pion mass.
- The ultraviolet cutoff \(k_c\) is introduced such that \(k \equiv |\mathbf{k}| \leq k_c\), and the infrared cutoff is proportional to \(1/L\) for box size \(L\).
- The smeared-out delta function for contact interactions is \(\delta_{k_c}(\mathbf{r}) = \frac{1}{L^3}\left(1 + 2{\sum_{\mathbf{k}}}' \cos(\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{r})\right)\).
- The nucleon mass renormalization is reported as a function of the momentum cutoff, with two distinct counter terms: kinetic mass counter term \(\beta_K\) and rest mass counter term \(\delta M\).

## Context

This work extends the quantum Monte Carlo approach to nuclear systems by treating pions as explicit dynamical degrees of freedom, contrasting with standard GFMC and AFDMC methods (Carlson 2014) where pions are integrated out into instantaneous potentials and currents. It differs from nuclear lattice methods (Lee 2009) that use static pion auxiliary fields and neglect time derivatives, thereby eliminating self-energy diagrams. The formalism enables direct measurement of pion cloud densities and momentum distributions, and provides a framework to test the validity of the instantaneous pion-exchange approximation in many-body calculations, with potential applications to electron- and neutrino-nucleus scattering in the pion-production region.
