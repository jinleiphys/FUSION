# Spacetime curvature corrections for the Yukawa potential and its application for the Reissner-Nordström Metric

> In this paper, we investigate the influence of the spacetime curvature on the Yukawa potential, focusing on boson-boson interactions derived from the {Φ^3} theory. Using the Bunch-Parker propagator expansion within Born's first approximation, we derive a Yukawa-like potential in a curved spacetime. We analyze the impact of the curvature on the propagator in momentum space, revealing modifications to the potential and showing that the corrections are determined by geometric quantities from Einstein's equations, like the Ricci scalar and tensor. We illustrate this using the Reissner-Nordström metric, highlighting the corrections' magnitude for specific parameters. Our results underscore the nuanced interplay between spacetime curvature and quantum interactions, providing insights into nucleon-nucleon systems in curved spacetimes or near strong gravitational fields.

## Key claim

The paper derives first-order curvature corrections to the Yukawa potential from a \(\phi^3\) theory using the Bunch-Parker propagator expansion, but the corrections are only significant for extremely low-mass black holes (around \(10^{16}\) kg) or for charges far exceeding the cosmic censorship limit, meaning the effect is negligible for typical astrophysical black holes.

## Method

The authors start from the \(\phi^3\) Lagrangian for boson-boson interactions and compute the tree-level scattering amplitude. Using Born's first approximation, they relate the amplitude to the potential in momentum space, then perform the inverse Fourier transform. For curved spacetime, they replace the flat-space Feynman propagator with the Bunch-Parker expansion in Riemann normal coordinates, keeping terms up to first order in the Ricci tensor and Ricci scalar. The resulting integrals are evaluated analytically, yielding a Yukawa-like potential with correction terms proportional to geometric quantities. The formalism is then applied to the Reissner-Nordström metric by computing the Ricci tensor components and substituting them into the general expression.

## Key numbers

* For a black hole of mass \(M = 10^{-15} M_{\odot} \sim 10^{16}\) kg with charge \(Q = 0.99999 Q_{\mathrm{lim}}\) at \(r' = r_+ + 10\) fm, the correction terms \(R_{(1)}\) and \(R_{(2)}\) reach values of order \(1\) MeV\(^2\).
* For a \(3 M_{\odot}\) black hole with \(Q = 0.999 Q_{\mathrm{lim}}\), the relative correction \(|\Delta V / V_0|\) at the potential minimum is \(5.55 \times 10^{-22}\).
* For a \(3 M_{\odot}\) black hole with charge \(Q = 1e\) (one elementary charge), the relative correction is \(3.04 \times 10^{-101}\).
* The extreme charge limit for a Reissner-Nordström black hole is \(Q_{\mathrm{lim}} = \sqrt{4\pi G M^2}\), which for a mass of \(1 m_P\) corresponds to approximately \(11.7 e\) elementary charges.
* The elementary charge in natural units is \(e = \sqrt{4\pi\alpha} \approx 0.30282\).

## Context

This work extends the Bunch-Parker propagator formalism (Bunch and Parker, 1979) to derive curvature corrections to the Yukawa potential, which had not been addressed in the literature. It builds on prior studies of quantum systems in curved spacetimes, including the Klein-Gordon and Dirac equations in Schwarzschild (Elizalde, 1987) and Kerr metrics (Chandrasekhar), and the Born approximation in de Sitter space (Ferrero, 2021). The results enable estimates of how strong gravitational fields near black holes or neutron stars modify one-pion exchange interactions in nucleon-nucleon systems, though the corrections are found to be negligible for astrophysical black holes and only potentially relevant for primordial or very low-mass black holes.
