# Inclusive Two-Gluon and Valence Quark-Gluon Production in DIS and pA

> We calculate production cross sections of a forward quark-gluon pair and of two gluons at mid-rapidity in Deep Inelastic Scattering and in high energy proton-nucleus collisions. The calculation is performed in the framework of the Color Glass Condensate formalism. We first calculate the cross sections in the quasi-classical approximation, which includes multiple rescatterings in the target. We then proceed to include the effects of non-linear small-x evolution in the production cross sections. It is interesting to note that our result for the two-gluon production cross section appears to be in direct violation of AGK cutting rules, which is the first example of such violation in QCD. The calculated quark-gluon and gluon-gluon production cross sections can be used to construct theoretical predictions for two-particle azimuthal correlations at RHIC and LHC (I^{p(d)A}) as well as for Deep Inelastic Scattering experiments at HERA and eRHIC.

## Key claim

The authors derive inclusive two-gluon and valence quark-gluon production cross sections in DIS and pA collisions using the Color Glass Condensate formalism, finding that the two-gluon production cross section violates AGK cutting rules, the first such violation observed in QCD, though this result is obtained under the assumption that the two gluons are separated by a large rapidity interval (y₂ ≫ y₁) and using the large-Nc approximation.

## Method

The calculation proceeds in two stages: first, the cross sections are derived in the quasi-classical approximation (McLerran-Venugopalan model) including all multiple rescatterings on the target nucleus, using light cone perturbation theory in A⁺ = 0 gauge with the large-Nc limit. Second, the effects of non-linear small-x evolution (Balitsky-Kovchegov equation) are incorporated into the production cross sections. For the two-gluon case, the authors assume ordered rapidities (y₂ ≫ y₁) and analyze time-ordering of gluon emissions relative to the target interaction to determine which diagrams dominate. For the valence quark-gluon case, both particles are assumed to be produced at comparable forward rapidities.

## Key numbers

- Quark saturation scale in the McLerran-Venugopalan model: Q_{s0}²(b) = 2 π α_s² ρ T(b), where ρ is the atomic number density and T(b) the nuclear profile function
- The forward scattering amplitude N₀ in the Glauber-Mueller approximation: N₀(x₂, x₂') = 1 - exp[-x₂₂'² Q_{s0}² ln(1/(x₂₂'Λ))/4]
- The evolution equation parameter: ᾱ_s ≡ α_s N_c / π
- The adjoint dipole forward scattering amplitude in large N_c: N_G(x₀, x₁, y) = 2 N(x₀, x₁, y) - N²(x₀, x₁, y)

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of single inclusive gluon production in DIS and pA collisions by Kovchegov and collaborators (2001) and by Kharzeev, Levin, and McLerran (2003), which predicted high-pT suppression in the nuclear modification factor R^{dA} at forward rapidity, a prediction later confirmed by BRAHMS, PHENIX, PHOBOS, and STAR at RHIC. The derived two-particle cross sections provide the theoretical basis for analyzing two-particle azimuthal correlations at RHIC and LHC (p(d)A collisions) and at HERA and eRHIC, going beyond single particle spectra to probe the region where Color Glass Condensate physics is expected to be most pronounced (pT ≲ Q_s). The results also enable quantitative comparison with the correlation analysis framework of Kharzeev, Levin, and McLerran (2004).
