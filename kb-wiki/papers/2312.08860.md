# Baryon electric charge correlation as a magnetometer of QCD

> The correlation between net baryon number and electric charge, $χ_{11}^{\rm BQ}$, can serve as a magnetometer of QCD. This is demonstrated by lattice QCD computations using the highly improved staggered quarks with physical pion mass of $M_π=135~$MeV on $N_τ=8$ and 12 lattices. We find that $χ_{11}^{\rm BQ}$ along the transition line starts to increase rapidly with magnetic field strength $eB\gtrsim 2M_π^2$ and by a factor 2 at $eB\simeq 8M_π^2$. Furthermore, the ratio of electric charge chemical potential to baryon chemical potential, $μ_{\rm Q}/μ_{\rm B}$, shows significant dependence on the magnetic field strength and varies from the ratio of electric charge to baryon number in the colliding nuclei in heavy ion collisions. These results can provide baselines for effective theory and model studies, and both $χ_{11}^{\rm BQ}$ and $μ_{\rm Q}/μ_{\rm B}$ could be useful probes for the detection of magnetic fields in relativistic heavy ion collision experiments as compared with corresponding results from the hadron resonance gas model.

## Key claim

The correlation between net baryon number and electric charge, χ₁₁ᴮᵠ, can serve as a magnetometer of QCD, but the effect becomes significant only for magnetic field strengths eB ≳ 2Mπ², with the correlation increasing by a factor of about 2 at eB ≃ 8Mπ² along the transition line.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action with physical pion mass Mπ = 135 MeV on Nτ = 8 and Nτ = 12 lattices, with spatial volumes 32³ and 48³ respectively. They computed quadratic fluctuations and correlations of net baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness in the presence of constant external magnetic fields ranging from eB ≃ Mπ² to eB ≃ 8Mπ², using the random noise vector method. Continuum estimates were obtained by combining results from both lattice spacings, and comparisons were made with the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model using the QMHRG2020 resonance list.

## Key numbers

- χ₁₁ᴮᵠ increases by a factor of approximately 2.4 at eB ≃ 8Mπ² at T = 145 MeV, based on continuum-estimated lattice QCD results
- Along the transition line, R(χ₁₁ᴮᵠ) reaches about 2.1 at eB ≃ 8Mπ²
- χ₂ᴮ increases by about 45% at eB ≃ 8Mπ² at T = 145 MeV
- χ₂ᵠ remains almost unchanged with magnetic field at T = 145 MeV
- The leading-order ratio (μQ/μB)LO normalized to its zero-field value reaches approximately 2.4 at eB ≃ 8Mπ² for Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions (r = 0.4)
- For Ru+Ru isobar collisions (r = 0.458), R((μQ/μB)LO) reaches about 4 at eB ≃ 8Mπ², about 1.5 times larger than for Au+Au/Pb+Pb
- The HRG model underestimates the continuum lattice QCD result for R(χ₁₁ᴮᵠ) by about 15% at eB ≃ 5.5Mπ² and by about 9% at eB ≃ 8Mπ²
- The proxy R(σ_{Q^PID,p}^{1,1}) underestimates the continuum lattice QCD result by about 22% at eB ≃ 5.5Mπ² and by about 16% at eB ≃ 8Mπ²

## Context

This work extends the only previous lattice QCD study on fluctuations of conserved charges at nonzero magnetic fields by Ding (2021), which used larger-than-physical pion mass at a single lattice cutoff, to physical pion mass with continuum estimates. The results provide QCD baselines for effective theory and model studies such as the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model by Fu (2013) and the Polyakov loop extended chiral SU(3) quark mean field model by Chahal (2023). The proposed observables χ₁₁ᴮᵠ and μQ/μB could enable experimental detection of magnetic fields in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, using already existing data, by comparing results across centrality classes and different collision systems.
