# Quarkonium states in an anisotropic QCD plasma

> We consider quarkonium in a hot QCD plasma which, due to expansion and non-zero viscosity, exhibits a local anisotropy in momentum space. At short distances the heavy-quark potential is known at tree level from the hard-thermal loop resummed gluon propagator in anisotropic perturbative QCD. The potential at long distances is modeled as a QCD string which is screened at the same scale as the Coulomb field. At asymptotic separation the potential energy is non-zero and inversely proportional to the temperature. We obtain numerical solutions of the three-dimensional Schroedinger equation for this potential. We find that quarkonium binding is stronger at non-vanishing viscosity and expansion rate, and that the anisotropy leads to polarization of the P-wave states.

## Key claim

Quarkonium binding is stronger in a QCD plasma with non-zero viscosity and expansion rate (which generates momentum-space anisotropy) than in an isotropic plasma, with the effect that the dissociation temperature for the J/ψ increases from about 1.2 Tc in the isotropic case to about 1.4 Tc for anisotropy parameter ξ = 1.

## Method

The authors constructed a finite-temperature heavy-quark potential by adding the full entropy contribution to the Karsch-Mehr-Satz (KMS) free-energy ansatz, then generalized it to anisotropic media by replacing the isotropic Debye mass mD with an angle-dependent screening scale μ(θ; ξ, T) derived from the hard-thermal-loop resummed gluon propagator in anisotropic perturbative QCD. They solved the three-dimensional time-independent Schrödinger equation numerically on a coordinate-space lattice using the finite-difference time-domain method, extracting ground and excited state energies by evolving an initial random wavefunction in imaginary time.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass set to mc = 1.3 GeV and bottom quark mass set to mb = 4.7 GeV, chosen to reproduce M_J/ψ = 3.1 GeV and M_Υ = 9.4 GeV at low temperature.
- Fixed gauge coupling g = 1.72 with nf = 2 massless quark flavors, giving mD(T)/T ≈ 2.8.
- For ξ = 1 near Tc, the binding energy of the 1S vector cc̅ ground state increases by about 50% compared to the isotropic case, and that of the 1S bb̅ ground state increases by roughly 30%.
- The condition |Ebind| ~ T is met for the J/ψ at 1.2 Tc for ξ = 0 and at 1.4 Tc for ξ = 1.
- The asymptotic potential V∞(T) = 2σ/mD ≈ 0.16 GeV² / T, with string tension σ = 0.223 GeV² and effective Coulomb coupling α ≈ 0.385.

## Context

This work extends the Karsch-Mehr-Satz (KMS) potential model (Karsch, Mehr, Satz, 1988) and the "most confining" isotropic potential studies of Mocsy and Petreczky (2007) to include momentum-space anisotropy arising from hydrodynamic expansion with non-zero shear viscosity. The anisotropy parameter ξ is related to the shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio η/s via ξ = (10/Tτ)(η/s) for one-dimensional boost-invariant expansion. The results enable predictions for quarkonium suppression patterns in heavy-ion collisions that account for viscous effects, and suggest that quarkonium synthesis occurs at higher temperature in a viscous plasma than in a perfectly equilibrated medium.
