# Rotations in isospace: a doorway to the understanding of neutron-proton superfluidity in N=Z nuclei

> The $T$=2 excitations in even-even $N$=$Z$ nuclei are calculated within the isospin cranked mean-field approach. The response of pairing correlations to rotation in isospace is investigated. It is shown that whereas the isovector pairing rather modestly modifies the single-particle moment of inertia in isospace, the isoscalar pairing strongly reduces its value. This reduction of the moments of inertia in isospace with respect to its rigid body value is a strong indicator of collective isoscalar pairing correlations. Beautiful analogies between the role of isovector pairing for the case of spatial rotations and the role of isoscalar pairing for the case of iso-rotations are underlined.

# Rotations in isospace: a doorway to the understanding of neutron-proton superfluidity in N=Z nuclei

## Key claim

The excitation energies of T=2 states in even-even N=Z nuclei can be explained only when isoscalar (T=0) pairing correlations are included in the mean-field model; isovector (T=1) pairing alone accounts for roughly half of the empirical excitation energy. The reduction of the moment of inertia in isospace relative to its rigid-body value serves as a strong indicator of collective isoscalar pairing correlations, but this reduction occurs only for low values of isospin T.

## Method

The authors employ a deformed Woods-Saxon mean-field potential with both isovector and isoscalar seniority-type pairing interactions, solved using the Lipkin-Nogami method with the most general Bogoliubov transformation. The isoscalar pairing strength is fitted to reproduce the empirical Wigner energy strength of approximately 47/A MeV. The response of pairing correlations to rotation in isospace is investigated by cranking the system with an isocranking frequency ω, and the excitation energies of T=2 states are computed at the cranking constraint T_x = √[T(T+1)].

## Key numbers

- The ratio of isoscalar to isovector pairing strength, x^(T=0) = G_(T=0)/G_(T=1), decreases smoothly from approximately 1.65 at the beginning of the sd-shell to approximately 1.40 in the f_(7/2) subshell.
- The Wigner energy strength used to fit the isoscalar pairing is W(A) ≈ 47/A MeV.
- The phase transition where isoscalar pairing correlations drop to zero occurs at or just before the iso-alignment reaches T_x = √6, corresponding to the T=2 state.
- Calculations including only isovector pairing account for roughly half of the empirical T=2 excitation energy for nuclei in the mass range 20 ≤ A ≤ 56.
- The ground state band excitation energy in the schematic single-particle model follows E_T = (1/2) δe T_x², with crossing frequencies ω_c^(n) = δe, 3δe, 5δe, ..., (2n-1)δe.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of neutron-proton pairing in N=Z nuclei by applying the isospin cranking approximation, which was tested within an exactly solvable model by Chen et al. The authors build on their previous work (Satula 1997, 2000) showing that isoscalar pairing can recover the Wigner energy, and now demonstrate that the same model simultaneously explains T=2 excitation energies. The approach draws formal analogy between spatial rotations (Bohr 1969) and isospin rotations, similar to how rotational spectra established evidence for superfluidity in atomic nuclei (Bohr 1958, Bel 1959). The results enable future self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with realistic two-body interactions in both particle-hole and pairing channels to quantitatively investigate the interplay of isoscalar pairing and rotations in isospace.
