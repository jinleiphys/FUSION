# Two-body weak currents in heavy nuclei

> In light and medium-mass nuclei, two-body weak currents from chiral effective field theory account for a significant portion of the phenomenological quenching of Gamow-Teller transition matrix elements. Here we examine the systematic effects of two-body axial currents on Gamow-Teller strength and $β$-decay rates in heavy nuclei within energy-density functional theory. Using a Skyrme functional and the charge-changing finite amplitude method, we add the contributions of two-body currents to the usual one-body linear response in the Gamow-Teller channel, both exactly and though a density-matrix expansion. The two-body currents, as expected, usually quench both summed Gamow-Teller strength and decay rates, but by an amount that decreases as the neutron excess grows. In addition, they can enhance individual low-lying transitions, leading to decay rates that are quite different from those that an energy-independent quenching would produce, particularly in neutron-rich nuclei. We show that both these unexpected effects are related to changes in the total nucleon density as the number of neutrons increases.

# Two-body weak currents in heavy nuclei

## Key claim
Two-body axial currents from chiral effective field theory systematically quench Gamow-Teller strength and beta-decay rates in heavy nuclei, but the quenching decreases as neutron excess grows; contrary to expectations, the two-body currents can enhance individual low-lying transitions in neutron-rich nuclei, producing decay rates that differ significantly from those predicted by an energy-independent quenching factor.

## Method
The authors used energy-density functional theory with a Skyrme functional (SKO') and the charge-changing finite amplitude method (FAM) to compute the linear response in the Gamow-Teller channel. They added two-body axial current contributions, both a short-range contact term and a finite-range pion-exchange term, to the usual one-body operator, implementing the full two-body current exactly and also through a density-matrix expansion (DME) approximation. Calculations were performed in an axially-deformed harmonic oscillator basis using the PyNFAM program, with the finite-range Yukawa function approximated by a sum of Gaussians for computational tractability.

## Key numbers
- Total quenching factor q ranges from 0.86–0.91 in 20O to 0.73–0.80 in 388Og, depending on the low-energy constant (LEC) set used (EGM, RTD, or EM).
- Differences in q between the lightest and heaviest isotopes of Sn, Gd, and Og average 0.013–0.017.
- The DME exchange current overestimates quenching by approximately 5% relative to the full calculation for all nuclei studied.
- The contact term contribution varies q by 5%–10% of its value at cD=0 when cD is varied between ±2.

## Context
This work extends the study of two-body weak currents from chiral effective field theory, previously shown by Gysbers (2019) to account for significant quenching in light and medium-mass nuclei, into the heavy-nucleus regime where ab initio methods are computationally prohibitive. Prior treatments of two-body currents in heavy nuclei for double-beta decay and dark-matter scattering used a simple nuclear-matter approximation (Menendez 2011, Menendez 2012, Klos 2013, Engel 2014). The present results enable future fitting of chiral current low-energy constants in conjunction with energy-density functionals, and demonstrate that the density-matrix expansion provides a computationally efficient approximation accurate to within about 5% for total quenching.
