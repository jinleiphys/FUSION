# Pion in the Medium with a Light-Front Model

> The pion properties in symmetric nuclear matter are investigated with the Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) Model plus the light-front constituent quark model~(LFCQM). The LFCQM has been quite successful in describing the properties of pseudoscalar mesons in vacuum, such as the electromagnetic elastic form factors, electromagnetic radii, and decay constants. We study the pion properties in symmetric nuclear matter with the in-medium input recalculated through the QMC model, which provides the in-medium modification of the LFCQM.

## Key claim

The paper claims that pion properties in symmetric nuclear matter are modified by the medium, with the electromagnetic form factor decreasing and the electromagnetic radius increasing as nuclear density increases, but these results depend on the specific combination of the Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) model and the light-front constituent quark model (LFCQM), and the vector potential effects cancel out in the loop integral, leaving only the quark mass shift as relevant.

## Method

The authors combined the Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) model, which describes nuclear matter through quark degrees of freedom with scalar and vector mean fields, with the light-front constituent quark model (LFCQM) to compute in-medium pion properties. They solved the Dirac equations for up and down quarks self-consistently in symmetric nuclear matter within the Hartree approximation, then used the resulting in-medium quark masses and the light-front valence wave function to calculate the pion electromagnetic form factor, decay constant, and valence quark probability via the plus component of the electromagnetic current in the Breit frame with the Drell-Yan condition.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: \(\rho_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\), with binding energy per nucleon of \(-15.7\) MeV.
* Vacuum constituent quark mass: \(m_q = 220\) MeV; regulator mass: \(m_R = 600\) MeV.
* Vacuum pion decay constant: \(f_\pi = 93.1\) MeV (semi-fitted value); vacuum electromagnetic radius: \(r_\pi = 0.73\) fm (calculated) compared to experimental value \(0.67 \pm 0.02\) fm.
* At nuclear saturation density (\(\rho/\rho_0 = 1.00\)): effective quark mass \(m^*_q = 79.5\) MeV; in-medium decay constant \(f^*_\pi = 40.2\) MeV; electromagnetic radius \(\langle r^{*2}_\pi \rangle^{1/2} = 1.96\) fm; valence quark probability \(\eta^* = 0.930\).
* At \(\rho/\rho_0 = 0.50\): \(m^*_q = 143.2\) MeV; \(f^*_\pi = 68.0\) MeV; radius = 1.00 fm; \(\eta^* = 0.843\).

## Context

This work extends the light-front constituent quark model of de Melo (2002) from vacuum to nuclear matter by incorporating the QMC model of Guichon (1988) and Saito (2007), which has been successfully applied to finite nuclei and hadronic properties in dense matter. The decrease of the in-medium pion decay constant with density agrees with conclusions from pionic-atom experiments, and the results enable future studies of kaons, D-mesons, and vector mesons (\(\rho\), \(\omega\)) in nuclear matter, which the authors state are in progress.
