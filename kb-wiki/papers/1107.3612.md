# Low-mass dilepton production through transport process in quark-gluon plasma

> We attempt to understand the low-mass dielectron enhancement observed by PHENIX Collaboration at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) by transport peak in the spectral function. On the basis of the second-order formalism of relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics, we parameterize the spectral function in low-frequency and long-wavelength region by two transport coefficients, electric diffusion coefficient $D$ and relaxation time $τ_{\rm J}$, and compared our theoretical dielectron spectra with the experimental data. We study spectrum of dielectrons produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions by using the profile of matter evolution under full (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamics. We find that the experimental data require the diffusion coefficient to be $D\geq 2/T$, with $T$ being temperature. Our analysis shows that dielectrons emitted through transport process mainly come from high-temperature QGP phase.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the low-mass dielectron enhancement observed by PHENIX at RHIC can be explained by the transport peak in the spectral function, parameterized by the electric diffusion coefficient \(D\) and relaxation time \(\tau_{\rm J}\). The authors find that the experimental data require \(D \geq 2/T\), but the extracted parameter sets differ from both weak-coupling pQCD and strong-coupling AdS/CFT estimates, indicating the phenomenon is not yet fully understood theoretically.

## Method

The authors constructed the retarded current correlator and spectral function in the low-frequency, long-wavelength region by performing a linear analysis of second-order relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics in the presence of an external electromagnetic field. They parameterized the spectral function using the electric charge diffusion coefficient \(D\) and relaxation time \(\tau_{\rm J}\), then calculated dielectron yields by combining this transport spectral function with a full (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic simulation of the medium evolution using a lattice QCD equation of state. The theoretical dielectron spectra were compared with PHENIX experimental data for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200\) GeV.

## Key numbers

- Diffusion coefficient lower bound: \(D \geq 2/T\) is required by the experimental data, with \(T\) being temperature.
- Parameter sets that reproduce the dielectron invariant mass spectrum: \((DT, \tau_{\rm J}T) = (10, 0.5)\), \((5, 0.2)\), and \((2, 0.0\text{--}0.1)\).
- pQCD estimate (with \(g \approx 2\)): \(D^{\rm pQCD} \approx 4/T\), \(\tau^{\rm pQCD}_{\rm J} \approx 15/T\).
- AdS/CFT estimate: \(D^{\rm AdS/CFT} = 1/(2\pi T)\), \(\tau^{\rm AdS/CFT}_{\rm J} = \ln 2/(2\pi T)\).
- Freezeout temperature determined from proton \(p_{\rm T}\) spectra: \(T_{\rm f} = 0.15\) GeV.
- Electric charge susceptibility fit to lattice QCD: \(\chi(T,\mu=0) \approx 0.28 T^2 [1 + \tanh((T - T_{\rm c}^*)/(0.15 T_{\rm c}^*))]\), with \(T_{\rm c}^* \approx 0.155\) GeV.
- Mean dielectron invariant mass in the range \(0.3 \leq m_{\rm ee} \leq 0.75\) GeV: \(m_0 \approx 0.47\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior attempts to explain the PHENIX low-mass dielectron enhancement, which could not be reproduced by standard thermal radiation models that were successful at SPS energies (Rapp 2010, Dusling 2007, Bratkovskaya 2008). The authors previously found that vector meson spectral function scenarios (dropping mass and width broadening) also failed to explain the data. The transport peak approach introduces a non-perturbative treatment of the spectral function via hydrodynamics, enabling comparison with both weak-coupling pQCD calculations (Arnold 2000, 2003) and strong-coupling AdS/CFT estimates (Natsuume 2007). The analysis enables future first-principle lattice QCD calculations of transport coefficients and spectral functions, and suggests that event-by-event fluctuations and pre-equilibrium glasma contributions may be important next steps.
