# Gaussian matrix elements in a cylindrical harmonic oscillator basis

> We derive a formalism, the separation method, for the efficient and accurate calculation of two-body matrix elements for a Gaussian potential in the cylindrical harmonic-oscillator basis. This formalism is of critical importance for Hartree-Fock and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations in deformed nuclei using realistic, finite-range effective interactions between nucleons. The results given here are also relevant for microscopic many-body calculations in atomic and molecular physics, as the formalism can be applied to other types of interactions beyond the Gaussian form. The derivation is presented in great detail to emphasize the methodology, which relies on generating functions. The resulting analytical expressions for the Gaussian matrix elements are checked for speed and accuracy as a function of the number of oscillator shells and against direct numerical integration.

## Key claim

The paper derives a "separation method" for calculating two-body matrix elements of a Gaussian potential in a cylindrical (deformed) harmonic-oscillator basis, which is claimed to be both efficient and accurate; the abstract glosses over the fact that the method is derived specifically for axially symmetric systems and that the formalism relies on generating functions to produce finite sums of products of one-body matrix elements, with the accuracy checked only against direct numerical integration for a limited set of cases.

## Method

The two-body Gaussian potential is expressed as a sum of products of one-body potential functions using generating functions, separating the radial (cylindrical) and axial (Cartesian) components. The radial component is evaluated using associated Laguerre polynomials and the axial component using Hermite polynomials, with the product of harmonic-oscillator functions reduced via derived T-coefficients. The resulting analytical expressions for the matrix elements are then compared to direct numerical integration for speed and accuracy as a function of the number of oscillator shells.

## Context

This work extends the separation method previously introduced by Gogny (1975) for spherical harmonic-oscillator bases to the cylindrical (axially symmetric) case, which is crucial for Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations of deformed nuclei, particularly in nuclear fission studies. The formalism enables efficient computation of two-body matrix elements for the Gogny effective interaction (Decharge and Gogny, 1980) in fission calculations, where many sets of matrix elements must be computed for different nuclear shapes; the method is also noted as applicable to other interactions beyond the Gaussian form, including Coulomb potentials, and to atomic and molecular physics.
