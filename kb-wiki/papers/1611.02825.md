# Tetra-Quark Interpretation of X(3872) and Z_c(3900) Revisited

> In relation to the newly observed bottom-strange X^{+,-}(5568) mesons, we revisit our tetra-quark interpretation of X(3872) and Z_c(3900). It is discussed that our assignment of X^{+,-}(5568) to charged components of iso-triplet bottom partners of D_{s0}^+(2317) is compatible with the revised version of our tetra-quark interpretation of X(3872) and Z_c(3900).

## Key claim

The paper revises the earlier tetra-quark assignment of X(3872) and Z_c(3900) by reassigning X(3872) from a state with color configuration \(\bar{\bm 3}_c\times {\bm 3}_c\) to one with \({\bm 6}_c\times \bar{\bm 6}_c\), and similarly reassigning Z_c(3900) to the \({\bm 6}_c\times \bar{\bm 6}_c\) iso-triplet partner with opposite C-parity, based on mass differences inferred from the newly observed X(5568) mesons.

## Method

The author classifies tetra-quark states into four groups by flavor symmetry and color configurations (\(\bar{\bm 3}_c\times {\bm 3}_c\) vs. \({\bm 6}_c\times \bar{\bm 6}_c\)), assigning lower-lying states to \(\bar{\bm 3}_c\times {\bm 3}_c\) and higher-lying states to \({\bm 6}_c\times \bar{\bm 6}_c\). Masses of scalar and axial-vector tetra-quark mesons are estimated using a simple quark counting method that takes the mass of D_{s0}^+(2317) as input and uses mass differences such as \(\Delta_{cs}=m_c-m_s\) and \(\Delta_{\rm color}\) between the two color configurations. Decay properties are analyzed by decomposing tetra-quark states into products of \(\{q\bar{q}\}\) pairs and replacing them with pseudoscalar or vector mesons.

## Key numbers

- Mass of X(5568): \(5567.8 \pm 2.9^{+0.9}_{-1.9}\) MeV (from D0 experiment)
- Mass difference \(m_{X(5568)} - m_{D_{s0}^+(2317)} \simeq 3250\) MeV, compared to \((m_{\eta_b} - m_{\eta_c})/2 \simeq 3210\) MeV
- Mass difference \(m_{X(5568)} - m_{X(3872)} \simeq 1.7\) GeV, compared to \((m_{B_s^0} - m_{\eta_c}) \simeq 2.4\) GeV
- Color mass difference \(\Delta_{\rm color} \simeq m_{X(3872)} - m_{\hat{\delta}^c} \simeq 0.6\) GeV
- Predicted mass of hidden-charm scalar \(\hat{\delta}^c \simeq 3.3\) GeV (candidate \(\hat{\delta}^{c0}(3200)\) observed around 3.2 GeV)
- Predicted mass of non-strange scalar \(\hat{D} \simeq 2.2\) GeV
- Predicted mass of charm-strange scalar \(\hat{D}^s \simeq 2.4\) GeV
- Predicted mass of hidden-charm strange scalar \(\hat{\sigma}^{sc} \simeq 3.5\) GeV
- Predicted masses of axial-vector states with \({\bm 6}_c\times \bar{\bm 6}_c\): \(m_{X_I^*(\pm)} \simeq m_{X^*(-)} \simeq m_{X^*(+)} \simeq 3.9\) GeV, \(m_{X^{s*}(\pm)} \simeq 4.1\) GeV
- Form factor \(f_+^{(\pi K)}(0) = 0.961 \pm 0.008\) (flavor SU_f(3) symmetry test)
- Form factor \(f_+^{(\bar{K}D)}(0) = 0.74 \pm 0.03\) (SU_f(4) symmetry test)

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier tetra-quark model for X(3872) and Z_c(3900) (Terasaki, earlier works) in light of the newly observed X(5568) mesons, which are assigned as bottom partners of D_{s0}^+(2317). The revised assignment places X(3872) in the \({\bm 6}_c\times \bar{\bm 6}_c\) color configuration rather than \(\bar{\bm 3}_c\times {\bm 3}_c\), resolving a mass discrepancy with the scalar \(\hat{\delta}^c\) state. The predicted masses differ from those in the diquark model (Maiani, earlier work) and the unitarized chiral model (Oset, earlier work), suggesting that confirmation of the \(\hat{\delta}^{c0}(3200)\) candidate could discriminate between models. The work enables searches for predicted iso-triplet partners of X(3872) and Z_c(3900) and for narrow scalar tetra-quark states co-existing with conventional \(^3P_0\) mesons.
