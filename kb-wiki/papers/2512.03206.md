# Astrophysical Reaction Rates for Charged-Particle Induced Reactions on Proton-Rich Nuclides

> Astrophysical reaction rates for reactions with proton-rich isotopes of Ne to Bi from stability to the proton dripline were calculated with an updated version of the SMARAGD statistical model (Hauser-Feshbach) code. Here, the focus was on reactions with protons or $α$ particles as required for nucleosynthesis in proton-rich matter. For completeness, also neutron-induced reactions are provided for the same set of targets. Some comments on dependencies of rates on various nuclear properties and on the appropriate way to compare to experiments are given. The new rate set for charged-particle induced reactions provides a better description of experimental data than previously widely used rates, especially for reactions involving $α$ particles.

## Key claim

The new SMARAGD-based reaction rate set provides a better description of experimental data than previously widely used rates, especially for reactions involving alpha particles, but the improvement is conditional on the use of an updated optical potential (ATOMKI-V2) and improved treatment of charged-particle transmission coefficients below the Coulomb barrier.

## Method

Astrophysical reaction rates were calculated using version v0.42.0s of the SMARAGD statistical model (Hauser-Feshbach) code for proton-rich isotopes of Ne to Bi from stability to the proton dripline. The calculations employed masses from AME2020, experimentally known spins and parities from the 2021 ENSDF+Nubase snapshot, a modified Lejeune (1980) optical potential for protons, the ATOMKI-V2 folding potential for alpha particles, and an energy-dependent parity distribution in the nuclear level density. Reaction reactivities were computed for temperatures from 0.1 to 10 GK and cross sections for ground-state targets were provided for center-of-mass energies up to 14 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Target nuclides range from Ne to Bi (Z=10 to Z=83) for isotopes from stability to the proton dripline
- Reaction cross sections for ground-state targets are provided for center-of-mass energies up to 14 MeV
- Astrophysical reaction reactivities are provided for temperatures from 0.1 to 10 GK
- Fit parameters use the standard REACLIB parameterisation with seven parameters (a0 through a6) to compute reactivity in cm^3 mole^-1 s^-1 when temperature T9 is given in GK
- Up to 40 discrete levels were included per nucleus, but only up to the last level with unambiguous spin and parity assignment

## Context

This work extends the earlier SMOKER and NON-SMOKER rate compilations by Rauscher and Thielemann (2000, 2001) and supersedes the preliminary SMARAGD evaluation given in the 2010 ApJS paper by Cyburt et al. The new rate set improves upon the widely used TALYS-based rates by Goriely et al. (2008) and the earlier NON-SMOKER rates, particularly for alpha-induced reactions where the ATOMKI-V2 potential (Mohr, 2020, 2021) provides better reproduction of low-energy experimental data. The results enable more accurate nucleosynthesis simulations for proton-rich environments such as explosive hydrogen burning and the rp-process.
