# Novel applications of Lattice QCD: Parton distribution functions, proton charge radius and neutron electric dipole moment

> We briefly discuss the current status of lattice QCD simulations and review selective results on nucleon observables focusing on recent developments in the lattice QCD evaluation of the nucleon form factors and radii, parton distribution functions and their moments, and the neutron electric dipole moment. Nucleon charges and moments of parton distribution functions are presented using simulations generated at physical values of the quark masses, while exploratory studies are performed for the parton distribution functions and the neutron electric dipole moment at heavier than physical value of the pion mass.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD simulations at physical quark masses now produce nucleon observables (charges, form factors, moments) that approach experimental values, but systematic uncertainties from excited-state contamination, finite volume, and lattice spacing still prevent definitive discrimination between competing experimental determinations (e.g., the proton charge radius from muonic hydrogen versus electron scattering).

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD evaluations of nucleon matrix elements using the plateau method, two-state fits, and the summation method to extract ground-state contributions from three-point correlation functions. Connected and disconnected contributions are computed separately, with disconnected diagrams evaluated using stochastic all-to-all propagators on GPUs, for ensembles including \(N_f=2\) twisted-mass clover-improved fermions at the physical point (\(m_\pi = 131\) MeV) and \(N_f=2+1+1\) twisted-mass ensembles at heavier pion masses.

## Key numbers

- Axial charge \(g_A = 1.22(3)(2)\) from the physical TM ensemble (plateau method, \(t_s-t_0 = 1.3\) fm), still slightly below the experimental value.
- Isovector scalar charge \(g_S^{u-d} = 0.93(25)(33)\) and isovector tensor charge \(g_T^{u-d} = 1.00(2)(1)\) at the physical point.
- Disconnected contributions: \(g_A^{u+d} = -0.15(2)\) (854,400 statistics), \(g_A^s = -0.042(10)\) (861,200 statistics); individual quark axial charges \(g_A^u = 0.826(26)\), \(g_A^d = -0.386(14)\).
- Sigma terms: \(\sigma_{\pi N} = 36(2)\) MeV, \(\sigma_s = 37(8)\) MeV, \(\sigma_c = 83(17)\) MeV.
- Isovector momentum fraction \(\langle x \rangle_{u-d} = 0.194(9)(10)\) and helicity moment \(\langle x \rangle_{\Delta u-\Delta d} = 0.259(9)(10)\) in \(\overline{\rm MS}\) at 2 GeV.
- Gluon momentum fraction \(\langle x \rangle_g = 0.273(23)(24)\) at the physical point; momentum sum \(\sum_q \langle x \rangle_q + \langle x \rangle_g = 1.01(10)(2)\).
- Total quark contribution to nucleon spin \(J^{u+d+s} = 0.374(51)(42)\); nucleon spin sum \(J_N = 0.510(50)(42)\) when including gluon contribution.

## Context

This review extends the long-standing lattice QCD effort to compute nucleon structure from first principles, addressing the historical discrepancy in \(g_A\) that was attributed to simulations at heavier-than-physical pion masses. Results are compared with experimental determinations from neutron beta decay, HERMES, COMPASS, and Belle data, as well as with other lattice collaborations including PACS, LHPC, PNDME, and CLS/Mainz. The work enables future precision tests of the Standard Model through improved determinations of scalar and tensor charges for beyond-Standard-Model searches, and provides lattice input for dark matter direct detection via the sigma terms.
