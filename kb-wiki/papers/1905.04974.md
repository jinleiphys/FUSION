# Parity Doubling and the Dense Matter Phase Diagram under Constraints from Multi-Messenger Astronomy

> We extend the recently developed hybrid quark-meson-nucleon model by augmenting a six-point scalar interaction and investigate the consequences for neutron-star sequences in the mass-radius diagram. The model has the characteristic feature that, at increasing baryon density, the chiral symmetry is restored within the hadronic phase by lifting the mass splitting between chiral partner states (parity doubling), before quark deconfinement takes place. At low temperature and finite baryon density, the model predicts a first-, second-order chiral phase transition, or a crossover, depending on the expectation value of the scalar field, and a first-order deconfinement phase transition. We discuss two sets of free parameters, which result in compact-star mass-radius relations that are at tension with the combined constraints for maximum-mass ($2~M_\odot$) and the compactness (GW170817). We find that the most preferable mass-radius relations result in isospin-symmetric phase diagram with rather low temperature for the critical point of the chiral phase transition.

## Key claim

The hybrid quark-meson-nucleon model, augmented with a six-point scalar interaction, predicts that chiral symmetry is restored within the hadronic phase via parity doubling before quark deconfinement occurs; however, the resulting compact-star mass-radius relations are at tension with combined constraints from the maximum-mass (2 solar masses) and the compactness inferred from GW170817, and the most preferable relations yield an isospin-symmetric phase diagram with a rather low temperature for the chiral critical point.

## Method

The authors extended a previously developed hybrid quark-meson-nucleon model by adding a six-point scalar interaction and solved the resulting equations of state for dense matter. They then computed neutron-star mass-radius sequences from these equations of state and compared them against the astrophysical constraints from the 2-solar-mass maximum-mass requirement and the tidal deformability constraint from the GW170817 neutron-star merger event. Two sets of free parameters were explored to assess the model's consistency with these multi-messenger constraints.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron-star mass of 2 solar masses (2 M_sun) is used as a constraint from pulsar observations.
- The GW170817 event provides a compactness constraint that the model's mass-radius relations must satisfy.
- The chiral phase transition in the isospin-symmetric phase diagram has a critical point at a "rather low temperature" (no specific numerical value given in the abstract or truncated text).

## Context

This work extends the hybrid quark-meson-nucleon model previously developed by the same group (Marczenko et al., earlier work) by incorporating a six-point scalar interaction to better describe the transition from hadronic to quark matter. The model's predictions for neutron-star structure are tested against the combined multi-messenger constraints from the 2-solar-mass maximum-mass requirement (from pulsar timing observations) and the tidal deformability measurement from GW170817. The results enable future refinement of dense-matter equations of state that simultaneously satisfy both nuclear physics constraints and astrophysical observations, particularly by identifying which parameter sets are disfavored by the data.
