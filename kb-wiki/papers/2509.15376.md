# Signatures of $K^-$ condensation on neutron star structure and $f-$mode frequencies

> Antikaon ($K^-$) condensation within neutron star matter (NS) depends on the antikaon-nucleon interaction potential ($U_K$). Appearance of $K^-$ generally softens the equation of state (EOS). The impact of this softening on the structure of the NS can be leveraged to find a telltale sign of the phase transition from nucleonic matter to $K^-$ condensation. To investigate the impact of $K^-$ condensation on NS properties using a Bayesian inference framework, we choose two sets of RMF model parameters to obtain a stiff (DD2) and relatively soft (FSU) nucleonic EOS, and explore a wide range of optical potential depths. Multimessenger observations from NICER and LIGO/Virgo constrain the optical potential values to $U_K = -104.72^{+13.82}_{-12.48}$ MeV and $U_K = -66.46^{+2.47}_{-3.42}$ MeV for the stiff and soft cases, respectively. Deeper $K^-$ potentials trigger condensation at a lower density, softening the EOS and lowering the corresponding maximum masses. While slopes of mass-radius and tidal deformability curves overlap between nucleonic and exotic EOSs, their curvature and $f-$mode oscillation properties (frequency and damping time) reveal features attributable to EOS softening. However, distinguishing the specific exotic degrees of freedom responsible for the softening remains an open challenge.

## Key claim

Multimessenger observations from NICER and LIGO/Virgo constrain the antikaon optical potential to \(U_K = -104.72^{+13.82}_{-12.48}\) MeV for a stiff nucleonic equation of state (DD2) and \(U_K = -66.46^{+2.47}_{-3.42}\) MeV for a soft nucleonic equation of state (FSU), but distinguishing antikaon condensation from other exotic degrees of freedom (such as hyperons or quark matter) using curvature and second derivatives of stellar observables remains an open challenge.

## Method

The authors constructed neutron star equations of state using the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model with two parameter sets: the stiff DD2 model and the softer FSU model. They introduced antikaon (\(K^-\)) condensation across a wide range of optical potential depths \(U_K\) from \(-60\) MeV to \(-180\) MeV, then applied a Bayesian inference framework with PyMultiNest to constrain the EOSs using NICER mass-radius data for PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, tidal deformability from GW170817, and maximum mass constraints from Shapiro delay measurements. They computed static structure via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations, tidal deformability, and \(f\)-mode oscillation frequencies and damping times within linearised general relativity, then analysed first and second derivatives and curvature of the mass-radius, mass-tidal deformability, and mass-frequency relations.

## Key numbers

- Optical potential posterior for DD2+\(K^-\): \(U_K = -104.72^{+13.82}_{-12.48}\) MeV (68% credible interval)
- Optical potential posterior for FSU+\(K^-\): \(U_K = -66.46^{+2.47}_{-3.42}\) MeV (68% credible interval)
- Threshold density for \(K^-\) onset: \(2.32\ \rho_0\) (DD2, minimum) to \(4.11\ \rho_0\) (both models, maximum), where \(\rho_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\)
- Maximum mass range with \(K^-\) condensates: \(2.00\)–\(2.37\ M_\odot\) (DD2) and \(2.00\)–\(2.17\ M_\odot\) (FSU)
- Radius at maximum mass range: \(11.08\)–\(12.35\) km (DD2) and \(11.68\)–\(11.77\) km (FSU)
- Central density range for maximum mass stars: \(5.05\)–\(7.03\ \rho_0\) (DD2+\(K^-\)) and \(5.03\)–\(5.64\ \rho_0\) (FSU+\(K^-\))
- Nucleonic maximum masses (no \(K^-\)): \(2.42\ M_\odot\) (DD2, radius \(11.94\) km) and \(2.30\ M_\odot\) (FSU, radius \(11.08\) km)
- Curvature \(\kappa_R\) for nucleonic models does not reach below approximately \(-1.5\); FSU+\(K^-\) can reach as low as \(-7\)

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian analysis of \(K^-\) condensation by Parmar (2024) and the study of radial oscillations with antikaon condensates by Kheto (2023), while applying the curvature diagnostic method introduced by Bauswein (2025) for hyperonic EOSs to the case of antikaon condensation. The authors find that second derivatives of mass-radius, mass-tidal deformability, and mass-frequency relations reveal EOS softening similarly to the hyperon case, meaning these diagnostics alone cannot distinguish antikaons from hyperons or hadron-quark phase transitions. Future gravitational wave detectors such as the Einstein Telescope and Cosmic Explorer, combined with NICER measurements, could exploit these higher-order diagnostics to probe exotic matter in neutron star cores.
