# Observables in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions from two different transport approaches for the same initial conditions

> For nucleus-nucleus collisions at Relativistic-Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) energies we calculate observables in two conceptually transport theories, i.e. the n-body molecular dynamical model RSP and in the Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics (PHSD), starting out from the same distribution of the initial energy density at the quark gluon plasma (QGP) formation time. The RSP is based on the Nambu Jona-Lasinio (NJL) Lagrangian whereas in PHSD the partons are described by the dynamical quasi-particle model (DQPM). Despite of the very different description of the parton properties, their interactions and of the hadronization in both approaches the final transverse momentum distributions of pions and kaons are rather similar. Thus it will be difficult to conclude from the final experimenal data on the details of the initial plasma properties.

## Key claim

Despite fundamentally different descriptions of parton properties, interactions, and hadronization in the RSP (Nambu-Jona-Lasinio based) and PHSD (dynamical quasi-particle model based) transport approaches, the final transverse momentum distributions of pions and kaons from ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energies are rather similar when starting from the same initial energy-density distribution, making it difficult to infer details of the initial plasma properties from experimental data.

## Method

The authors extracted the initial spatial distribution of energy density from the PHSD approach for Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV and converted it into the degrees of freedom of the RSP approach using both an equilibrium conversion (assuming local thermal equilibrium) and an out-of-equilibrium conversion (preserving the actual particle density and momentum distribution from PHSD). They then propagated the system using either the PHSD or RSP equations of motion and compared the resulting parton spectra, final hadron spectra, and elliptic flow.

## Key numbers

- For Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV and b = 2 fm (0%-5% centrality), the initial conditions were extracted at t = t0 + 0.5 fm/c, where t0 is the time of the first hard NN collision.
- The critical energy density for the QGP formation threshold was epsilon_c approximately 0.5 GeV/fm^3 in PHSD and epsilon_c approximately 1 GeV/fm^3 in the NJL model (corresponding to T_c approximately 200 MeV).
- For smooth Gaussian initial conditions with spatial eccentricity epsilon = 0.75, the final elliptic flow v2 from PHSD and RSP differed by only 10%, and the ratio v2/epsilon was almost independent of eccentricity.
- The NJL model gave a shear viscosity over entropy density eta/s approximately 0.1 close to the critical temperature.

## Context

This work extends comparisons between transport approaches for heavy-ion collisions, specifically contrasting the n-body molecular dynamics RSP (based on the NJL Lagrangian) with the parton-hadron-string dynamics PHSD (based on the DQPM). It builds on prior studies of the equation of state and transport coefficients in both models (Marty 2013) and on the ability of PHSD to describe experimental observables (Toneev 2012, Konchakovski 2012, Linnyk 2012-2014). The results suggest that bulk observables like transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flow are insensitive to the detailed parton properties in the QGP, motivating searches for more differential observables that could discriminate between different microscopic descriptions.
