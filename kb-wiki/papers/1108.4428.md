# Optimized perturbation theory for charged scalar fields at finite temperature and in an external magnetic field

> Symmetry restoration in a theory of a self-interacting charged scalar field at finite temperature and in the presence of an external magnetic field is examined. The effective potential is evaluated nonperturbatively in the context of the optimized perturbation theory method. It is explicitly shown that in all ranges of the magnetic field, from weak to large fields, the phase transition is second order and that the critical temperature increases with the magnetic field. In addition, we present an efficient way to deal with the sum over the Landau levels, which is of interest especially in the case of working with weak magnetic fields.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for a self-interacting charged scalar field with a global U(1) symmetry, the phase transition remains second order for all ranges of the external magnetic field (from weak to large fields), and that the critical temperature increases with the magnetic field. This directly contradicts a prior ring-diagram resummation study by Ayala et al. (2011) which had found a first-order transition and a decreasing critical temperature with magnetic field.

## Method

The authors apply the optimized perturbation theory (OPT) to the effective potential of a complex scalar field with a quartic self-interaction, at first order in the OPT expansion. The OPT interpolation introduces a variational mass parameter η, determined by the principle of minimal sensitivity (PMS), which resums daisy and superdaisy diagrams self-consistently. The calculation is performed at finite temperature using Matsubara frequencies and in a constant external magnetic field by summing over Landau levels, with the Euler-Maclaurin formula used to efficiently handle the sum over many Landau levels in the weak-field regime.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for thermal-only case: \( T_c^2 = 18 m^2 / \lambda \), derived exactly at first order in OPT (Eq. 3.13).
- For the example parameters \( m/M = 20 \) and \( \lambda = 0.00375 \), the critical temperature is \( T_c/M \simeq 1385.64 \) (from Fig. 3 caption).
- For the same parameters with magnetic field, the critical temperature at zero field is \( T_c/M \simeq 13.42 \) (from text near Fig. 5).
- The critical magnetic field at fixed temperature \( T > T_c \) is \( eB_c/M^2 \simeq 53.93 \) (from text near Fig. 5).

## Context

This work extends the study of symmetry restoration in scalar field theories to include both thermal effects and an external magnetic field, using the OPT method which automatically avoids the overcounting problems that plagued earlier ring-diagram resummations. The results contradict the findings of Ayala et al. (2011), who used ring-diagram resummation and reported a first-order phase transition with a critical temperature that decreases with magnetic field. The paper also provides an efficient method for summing over Landau levels in weak magnetic fields via the Euler-Maclaurin formula, enabling precise numerical work across all field strengths.
