# Inversion Potential Analysis of the Nuclear Dynamics in the Triton

> We report 3H binding energy calculations using inversion potentials generated from phase shifts corresponding to contemporary nucleon-nucleon potentials as well as modern phase shift analyses. We place limits upon local potential triton binding energy calculations due to the underlying uncertainties in their fit to the nucleon-nucleon phase shifts: 7.7 +/- 0.2 MeV. We explore the role of the nonlocality of momentum dependent potentials in the triton binding energy. In particular, we find the additional binding energy in the case of the Bonn-B potential is due to a long range nonlocality, which may correspond to a three-nucleon force when the Hamiltonian is restricted to local interactions.

## Key claim

The authors estimate that local potential models of the nucleon-nucleon interaction yield a triton binding energy of 7.7 ± 0.2 MeV, and they argue that the additional binding energy seen in the Bonn-B potential (8.14 MeV) arises from a long range nonlocality that may correspond to a three-nucleon force when the Hamiltonian is restricted to local interactions.

## Method

The authors constructed inversion potentials using the Gelfand-Levitan-Marchenko integral equations, which generate partial-wave local potentials that exactly reproduce input phase shifts and deuteron spectroscopic data. They compared triton binding energies from these inversion potentials against those from the original Nijm-II, Paris, and Bonn-B potential models, and also against inversion potentials derived from the Arndt phase shift analysis. The study systematically varied high-energy phase shift extrapolations to quantify uncertainties in the triton binding energy.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy from the Nijm-II model: 7.62 MeV; from its inversion potential: 7.60 MeV
- Triton binding energy from the Paris model: 7.467 MeV; from its inversion potential: 7.472 MeV
- Triton binding energy from the Bonn-B model: 8.14 MeV; from its inversion potential: 7.84 MeV
- Triton binding energy from the Arndt FA91 inversion potential: 7.36 MeV
- Estimated local potential triton binding energy: 7.7 ± 0.2 MeV
- Experimental triton binding energy: 8.48 MeV
- D-state probability P_D for Bonn-B model: 4.99%; for its inversion potential: 5.81%
- D-state probability P_D for Paris model: 5.77%; for its inversion potential: 5.69%
- D-state probability P_D for Nijm-II model: 5.66%; for its inversion potential: 5.53%

## Context

This work extends the recent calculations by Friar, Payne, Stoks, and de Swart (1993) who reported a triton binding energy of 7.62 ± 0.01 MeV using new Nijmegen local potentials. The authors quantify the uncertainty in local potential triton binding energies due to phase shift uncertainties, and they demonstrate that the Bonn-B potential's additional binding arises from long range nonlocality rather than phase shift differences. This enables future work to distinguish between adding three-body forces to local Hamiltonians versus incorporating nonlocal interactions, building on the unitary transform relationship between these approaches established by Polyzou and Gloeckle (1990).
