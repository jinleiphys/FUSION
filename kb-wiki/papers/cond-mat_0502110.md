# Density and spin response functions in ultracold fermionic atom gases

> We propose a new method of detecting the onset of superfluidity in a two-component ultracold fermionic gas of atoms governed by an attractive short-range interaction. By studying the two-body correlation functions we find that a measurement of the momentum distribution of the density and spin response functions allows one to access separately the normal and anomalous densities. The change in sign at low momentum transfer of the density response function signals the transition between a BEC and a BCS regimes, characterized by small and large pairs, respectively. This change in sign of the density response function represents an unambiguous signature of the BEC to BCS crossover. Also, we predict spin rotational symmetry-breaking in this system.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a measurement of the density response function's sign at low momentum transfer provides an unambiguous signature of the BEC-to-BCS crossover in a two-component ultracold fermionic gas, but this claim is based on a mean-field (Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov) approximation at zero temperature, and the authors note that the spin rotational symmetry-breaking prediction is characteristic to their specific model and provides an experimental check for its applicability to real systems.

## Method

The authors use a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) mean-field approach with a BCS-type variational ground state to study a two-component fermionic gas with an attractive short-range interaction. They calculate the zero-temperature instantaneous density and spin response functions as functions of the scattering length, deriving them from the normal and anomalous densities. The model is applied to a one-channel (two-level) system that smoothly interpolates between the BEC and BCS regimes, and the hyperfine structure of atomic levels for 6Li and 40K is incorporated to examine modifications to the spin response.

## Key numbers

* The density response function S_rho(q) changes sign at low momentum transfer across the BEC-BCS crossover, with S_rho(0) varying between positive and negative values as the scattering length parameter eta = (a_0 k_F)^{-1} is varied.
* The spin response function at zero momentum transfer satisfies a sum rule: S_sigma(0) = -1/4, independent of eta = (a_0 k_F)^{-1}.
* The normal and anomalous density contributions at q=0 satisfy I_rho(0) + I_kappa(0) = 1, independent of the scattering length.
* The calculations are performed at fixed system density with k_F <r> approx 0.37, where <r> is the interaction range.

## Context

This work extends the mean-field treatment of the BEC-BCS crossover previously studied by the authors (Mihaila et al., 2005) and builds on earlier theoretical work by Leggett (1980) on the zero-range interaction Hamiltonian. The proposed diagnostic method addresses the need for definitive experimental signatures of superfluidity in ultracold fermionic gases, following experimental claims by Jin (2003) and Ketterle (2003) of reaching the superfluid state on the negative scattering length side of the Feshbach resonance. The approach complements suggestions by Viverit et al. (2001) to use momentum distribution measurements and by Bruun and Baym (2004) to use scattered light for detecting the superfluid transition, while providing a clearer signature near the crossover region where actual experiments are performed.
