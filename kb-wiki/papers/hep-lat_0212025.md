# Lattice QCD study for the Quark-Gluon Mixed Condensate (anti-Q sigma G Q)

> We study the quark-gluon mixed condensate g<\bar{q} sigma G q>, which is another chiral order parameter, using the SU(3)c lattice QCD with the Kogut-Susskind(KS) fermion at the quenched level. We generate 100 gauge configurations on the 16^4 lattice with β= 6.0, and measure the mixed condensate at 16 points in each gauge configuration for each current-quark mass of m_q=21, 36, 52 MeV. From the 1600 data for each m_q, we find the ratio between the mixed condensate and the quark condensate, m_0^2 = g<\bar{q} sigma G q> / <\bar{q}q> \simeq 2.5 GeV^2 at the lattice scale of 1/a \simeq 2 GeV in the chiral limit. The large value of the mixed condensate suggests its importance in the operator product expansions in QCD.

## Key claim

The ratio of the quark-gluon mixed condensate to the quark condensate, \(m_0^2 = g\langle\bar{q}\sigma G q\rangle / \langle\bar{q}q\rangle\), is found to be approximately 2.5 GeV\(^2\) at the lattice scale of \(a^{-1} \simeq 2\) GeV in the chiral limit, which is significantly larger than the phenomenological estimate of \(0.8 \pm 0.2\) GeV\(^2\) proposed in earlier QCD sum rule studies.

## Method

The authors performed SU(3)\(_c\) lattice QCD simulations at the quenched level using the Kogut-Susskind (KS) fermion, which preserves explicit chiral symmetry for zero quark mass. They generated 100 gauge configurations on a \(16^4\) lattice with \(\beta = 6.0\) (lattice spacing \(a \simeq 0.10\) fm) and measured the quark condensate \(\langle\bar{q}q\rangle\) and the mixed condensate \(g\langle\bar{q}\sigma G q\rangle\) at 16 space-time points per configuration for three current-quark masses \(m_q = 21, 36, 52\) MeV, yielding 1600 data points per mass. The condensates were chirally extrapolated to the massless limit using a linear fit.

## Key numbers

- \(m_0^2 \equiv g\langle\bar{q}\sigma G q\rangle / \langle\bar{q}q\rangle \simeq 2.5\) GeV\(^2\) at the lattice scale \(a^{-1} \simeq 2\) GeV in the chiral limit.
- Bare condensate values in the chiral limit: \(a^3 \langle\bar{q}q\rangle = -0.008721(17)\) and \(a^5 g\langle\bar{q}\sigma G q\rangle = -0.005652(14)\) on the \(16^4\) lattice with \(\beta = 6.0\).
- Quark masses used: \(m_q = 21, 36, 52\) MeV, corresponding to \(ma = 0.0105, 0.0184, 0.0263\) respectively.

## Context

This work extends the only prior lattice QCD study of the mixed condensate by K&S (preliminary, with only 5 data points on an \(8^4\) lattice at \(\beta = 5.7\)) to a larger and finer lattice with high statistics (1600 data points per mass). The result \(m_0^2 \simeq 2.5\) GeV\(^2\) is much larger than the phenomenological value \(0.8 \pm 0.2\) GeV\(^2\) used in QCD sum rules by Bel (Bel) and others, suggesting the mixed condensate plays a more important role in operator product expansions than previously assumed. The authors note they are extending this work to finite temperature for relevance to RHIC physics.
