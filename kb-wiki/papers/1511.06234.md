# Algebraic vacuum limits of QCD condensates from in-medium projections of Lorentz tensors

> Utilizing the in-medium Lorentz decomposition of operators generating QCD condensates we derive general constraints among the latter ones by the requirement of a smooth transition from medium to vacuum. In this way we relate the vacuum limits of heretofore unrelated condensates and provide consistency checks for the vacuum saturation hypothesis and the heavy quark mass expansion. The results are general and depend only on the rank and symmetry of the Lorentz tensors to be decomposed. The derived prescription enables to uniquely and directly identify operator product expansion contributions which are algebraically specific for in-medium situations and in particular useful for operators with a higher rank, i.e. larger than three. Four-quark condensates in mass dimension six are exemplified.

## Key claim

The paper derives general algebraic constraints among QCD condensates by requiring a smooth transition from in-medium to vacuum, showing that the vacuum limits of previously unrelated condensates are related through a decomposition scheme that depends only on the rank and symmetry of the Lorentz tensors involved, not on the specific operator.

## Method

The authors develop a formalized framework for decomposing Lorentz tensors into vacuum-specific and medium-specific parts using a projection method based on the metric tensor, Levi-Civita tensor, and medium four-velocity. They define an orthogonal decomposition where the medium-specific projection vector is constructed to be orthogonal to the vacuum projection vector, ensuring that medium-specific condensates vanish identically in vacuum. The method is exemplified for four-quark condensates of mass dimension six, and the resulting algebraic vacuum limits are checked for consistency with the vacuum saturation hypothesis and the heavy quark mass expansion.

## Context

This work extends previous in-medium QCD sum rule studies by Hilger (2009, 2010, 2011) and Buchheim (2014) that were restricted to mass-dimension 4 operator product expansions or mass-shift-only scenarios. The formalism enables systematic inclusion of higher mass-dimension condensates (particularly dimension 6 four-quark condensates) in in-medium evaluations, which is necessary for precision predictions at the densities and temperatures accessible at NICA, FAIR, and J-PARC. The results provide consistency checks for the vacuum saturation hypothesis employed by earlier authors and allow identification of operator product expansion contributions that are algebraically specific to in-medium situations.
