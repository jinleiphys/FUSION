# Kinetic energy spectra for fragments and break-up density in multifragmentation

> We investigate the possibility, in nuclear fragmentation, to extract information on nuclear density at break-up from fragment kinetic energy spectra using a simultaneous scenario for fragment emission. The conclusions we derive are different from the recently published results of Viola et al. [Phys. Rev. Lett. 93, (2004), 132701] assuming a sequential fragment emission and claiming that the experimentally observed decrease of peak centroids for kinetic energy spectra of fragments with increasing bombarding energy is due to a monotonic decrease of the break-up density.

## Key claim
Using a simultaneous (rather than sequential) emission scenario for nuclear multifragmentation, the observed decrease of fragment kinetic energy peak centroids with increasing excitation energy can be reproduced at constant low density, contradicting the claim of Viola et al. (2004) that this decrease implies a monotonic decrease of break-up density.

## Method
The authors employed a microcanonical multifragmentation model (MMM) to simulate the decay of a \(^{197}\)Au source at fixed break-up density \(\rho_0/5\) while varying excitation energy from 3.4 to 7.9 MeV/nucleon. They calculated the average Coulomb energy per fragment, the average kinetic energy per fragment, and the full kinetic energy spectra of intermediate mass fragments (IMF, \(3 \leq Z \leq 15\)) at both break-up and asymptotic stages, including secondary evaporation effects.

## Key numbers
- For \(^{197}\)Au at \(\rho_0/5\) and excitation energies from 3.4 to 7.9 MeV/nucleon, the total Coulomb energy increases by about 1.2 MeV/nucleon, while the average Coulomb energy experienced by a fragment with \(Z=10\) increases by only about 0.18 MeV/nucleon.
- The fragment average kinetic energies decrease with increasing excitation energy despite both thermal and Coulomb energies increasing, due to the strong increase in fragment multiplicity.
- The study considered the freeze-out density range \(\rho_0/7\) to \(\rho_0/3\) and found qualitatively the same results as those presented for \(\rho_0/5\).

## Context
This work extends the debate on how to extract break-up density from fragment kinetic energy spectra, directly contradicting the sequential-emission interpretation of Viola et al. (2004) who claimed a monotonic density decrease from \(\rho_0/3\) with excitation energy. The results are consistent with earlier simultaneous multifragmentation models (Durand, Beaulieu) and with freeze-out densities from statistical models (SMM, MMM) and dynamical models (Bob, Frankland) that range from \(\rho_0/9\) to \(\rho_0/2.5\). The paper enables a reinterpretation of existing experimental data on \(^4\)He+\(^{197}\)Au at 50 MeV/nucleon (Zhang), \(^{14}\)N+\(^{197}\)Au at 20-100 MeV/nucleon (Wile), and \(^3\)He+\(^{197}\)Au at 4.8 GeV (Bracken), but concludes that determining the actual excitation energy dependence of break-up density requires further study.
