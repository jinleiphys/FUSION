# Variation after full projection with triaxially deformed nuclear mean field

> We implemented a variation after projection (VAP) algorithm based on a triaxially deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov vacuum state. This is the first projected mean field study that includes all the quantum numbers (except parity), i.e., spin ($J$), isospin ($T$) and mass number ($A$). Systematic VAP calculations with $JTA$-projection have been performed for the even-even $sd$-shell nuclei with the USDB Hamiltonian. All the VAP ground state energies are within 500 keV above the exact shell model values. Our VAP calculations show that the spin projection has two important effects: (1) the spin projection is crucial in achieving good approximation of the full shell model calculation. (2) the intrinsic shapes of the VAP wavefunctions with spin projection are always triaxial, while the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov methods likely provide axial intrinsic shapes. Finally, our analysis suggests that one may not be possible to associate an intrinsic shape to an exact shell model wave function.

# Variation after full projection with triaxially deformed nuclear mean field

## Key claim

This work presents the first projected mean field study that simultaneously restores spin (J), isospin (T), and mass number (A) quantum numbers from a triaxially deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov vacuum state. The authors find that while VAP-JTA ground state energies for even-even sd-shell nuclei lie within 500 keV of exact shell model values, the intrinsic shapes of the VAP wavefunctions that include spin projection are always triaxial, and for nuclei where the VAP energy exactly matches the shell model result, no unique intrinsic shape can be associated with the wavefunction.

## Method

The authors implement a variation after projection (VAP) algorithm starting from a triaxially deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) vacuum state, using the Thouless theorem to parameterize the variational space with 42 real parameters for sd-shell calculations. They perform systematic VAP calculations with JTA-projection (spin, isospin, and mass number), TA-projection, and A-projection for even-even sd-shell nuclei using the USDB Hamiltonian, and extract intrinsic quadrupole moments Q and triaxial deformation parameters gamma from the projected states.

## Key numbers

- All VAP-JTA ground state energies for even-even sd-shell nuclei are within 500 keV above the exact shell model values; the largest deviation is 0.446 MeV for 26Mg, which has the largest shell model dimension N_JT = 1132
- For 24Mg, the VAP-JTA energy is -86.919 MeV compared to the shell model value of -87.105 MeV, with intrinsic shape parameters Q*_JTA = 19.057 and gamma*_JTA = 16.96 degrees
- The HFB calculation for 24Mg gives E*_HFB = -80.965 MeV with Q*_HFB = 18.659 and gamma*_HFB = 11.96 degrees, the only sd-shell nucleus with a non-axial HFB minimum
- For 20Ne, 28Ne, and 36Ar, the VAP-JTA energies exactly equal the shell model results (e.g., -40.472 MeV for 20Ne), and these nuclei have shell model dimensions N_JT less than or close to N_VAP = 42
- The VAP-TA energy for 24Mg is -82.831 MeV with Q*_TA = 17.295 and gamma*_TA = 0.09 degrees (nearly axial)

## Context

This work extends the variation after projection approach of Ring and Schuck (1980) and earlier projected HFB calculations by Schmid (2004) and others, which typically assumed axial symmetry or projected only a subset of quantum numbers. The authors note that HFB calculations with Gogny force by Delaroche et al. (2010) found almost all 1712 calculated nuclei have axially symmetric HFB minima, and that previous work on 24Mg by Bonche et al. (1987), Koepf and Ring (1988), and Sheline et al. (1988) debated the possibility of triaxial deformation. The results enable systematic comparison of projected mean-field approaches with exact shell model diagonalization in the sd-shell, and suggest that triaxiality in VAP-JTA is a universal beyond-mean-field effect caused by spin projection rather than isospin or particle number projection.
