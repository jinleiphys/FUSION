# Implications of PREX-II on the equation of state of neutron-rich matter

> Laboratory experiments sensitive to the equation of state of neutron rich matter in the vicinity of nuclear saturation density provide the first rung in a "density ladder" that connects terrestrial experiments to astronomical observations. In this context, the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb (Rskin) provides a stringent laboratory constraint on the density dependence of the symmetry energy. In turn, an improved value of Rskin has been reported recently by the PREX collaboration. Exploiting the strong correlation between Rskin and the slope of the symmetry energy L within a specific class of relativistic energy density functionals, we report a value of L=(106 +/- 37)MeV -- that systematically overestimates current limits based on both theoretical approaches and experimental measurements. The impact of such a stiff symmetry energy on some critical neutron-star observables is also examined.

## Key claim

Using a specific class of relativistic energy density functionals, the authors report a value of the slope of the symmetry energy L = (106 ± 37) MeV derived from the PREX-II neutron skin measurement, which systematically overestimates current limits from both theoretical approaches and experimental measurements.

## Method

The authors used a set of 16 covariant energy density functionals (including FSUGold2 and its variants, RMF012-032, and TFa-TFc) to exploit the strong correlation between the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb and the slope of the symmetry energy L. They derived Gaussian probability distributions for L, J (symmetry energy at saturation), and the slope at a lower reference density, then used these to predict neutron-star observables including the direct Urca threshold, stellar radius, and tidal deformability.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness of 208Pb: Rskin = (0.283 ± 0.071) fm (1σ error from combined PREX and PREX-II)
- Slope of symmetry energy at saturation density: L = (106 ± 37) MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation: J = (38.1 ± 4.7) MeV
- Slope of symmetry energy at (2/3)ρ0 ≈ 0.1 fm⁻³: L̃ = (71.5 ± 22.6) MeV
- For the 1σ lower limit Rskin = 0.212 fm: threshold mass for direct Urca cooling M⋆ ≈ 1.45 M⊙ and central density ρ⋆ ≈ 0.42 fm⁻³
- For the central value Rskin = 0.283 fm: threshold mass M⋆ ≈ 0.85 M⊙ and central density ρ⋆ ≈ 0.24 fm⁻³
- Combined PREX-II and NICER constraints yield: 0.21 ≲ Rskin (fm) ≲ 0.31, 13.25 ≲ R⋆¹·⁴ (km) ≲ 14.26, and 642 ≲ Λ⋆¹·⁴ ≲ 955

## Context

This work extends the original PREX result (Abrahamyan 2012) by incorporating the improved PREX-II measurement to constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy. The inferred L value is systematically larger than constraints from theoretical approaches such as those by Zhang (2013), Hebeler (2013), Drischler (2020), and experimental analyses by Chen (2010) and Roca-Maza (2015). The results enable predictions for neutron-star observables that can be tested by NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 (Riley 2019, Miller 2019) and future gravitational-wave detections, while revealing tension with the revised tidal deformability limit of Λ₁·₄ ≲ 580 from GW170817 (Abbott 2018).
