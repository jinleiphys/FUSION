# Spin-polarized neutron matter: Critical unpairing and BCS-BEC precursor

> We obtain the critical magnetic field required for complete destruction of $S$-wave pairing in neutron matter, thereby setting limits on the pairing and superfluidity of neutrons in the crust and outer core of magnetars. We find that for fields $B \ge 10^{17}$ G the neutron fluid is non-superfluid -- if weaker spin-1 superfluidity does not intervene -- a result with profound consequences for the thermal, rotational, and oscillatory behavior of magnetars. Because the dineutron is not bound in vacuum, cold dilute neutron matter cannot exhibit a proper BCS-BEC crossover. Nevertheless, owing to the strongly resonant behavior of the $nn$ interaction at low densities, neutron matter shows a precursor of the BEC state, as manifested in Cooper-pair correlation lengths {being} comparable to the interparticle distance. We make a systematic quantitative study of this type of BCS-BEC crossover in the presence of neutron fluid spin-polarization induced by an ultra-strong magnetic field. We evaluate the Cooper pair wave-function, quasiparticle occupation numbers, and quasiparticle spectra for densities and temperatures spanning the BCS-BEC crossover region. The phase diagram of spin-polarized neutron matter is constructed and explored at different polarizations.

## Key claim

The critical magnetic field required to completely destroy S-wave pairing in neutron matter is B ≥ 10^17 G, but this result carries the qualifier that weaker spin-1 superfluidity might still persist at those field strengths. Additionally, while cold dilute neutron matter cannot exhibit a proper BCS-BEC crossover because the dineutron is unbound in vacuum, it does show a precursor of the BEC state through Cooper-pair correlation lengths comparable to the interparticle distance.

## Method

The authors solved the finite-temperature Dyson equation in the Nambu-Gorkov formalism for spin-polarized neutron matter, using a separable Paris potential (PEST 3) in the 1S0 partial-wave channel and an effective mass computed from Skyrme density functionals (SkIII and SLy4 parametrizations). They self-consistently solved the gap equation and density equations to obtain the pairing gap, quasiparticle spectra, Cooper-pair wave functions, and occupation numbers across a range of densities, temperatures, and spin polarizations. The phase diagram was constructed by evaluating the free energy and identifying the minimum between superfluid and normal phases for homogeneous, isotropic solutions only.

## Key numbers

- Critical magnetic field for complete destruction of S-wave pairing: B ≥ 10^17 G
- Bare neutron-neutron scattering length in the 1S0 channel: -18.5 ± 0.4 fm
- At density log10(ρ/ρ0) = -1.0, T = 0.25 MeV, and zero polarization: pairing gap Δ = 2.46 MeV, chemical potential μ_n = 12.94 MeV, interparticle distance d = 2.46 fm, coherence length ξ_a = 4.33 fm
- At density log10(ρ/ρ0) = -1.5, T = 0.25 MeV, and zero polarization: pairing gap Δ = 1.91 MeV, chemical potential μ_n = 5.65 MeV, interparticle distance d = 3.61 fm, coherence length ξ_a = 3.71 fm
- At density log10(ρ/ρ0) = -2.0, T = 0.25 MeV, and zero polarization: pairing gap Δ = 1.07 MeV, chemical potential μ_n = 2.49 MeV, interparticle distance d = 5.30 fm, coherence length ξ_a = 4.48 fm
- Lowest chemical potential found: 0.24 MeV at ln(ρ/ρ0) = -3.57 and T = 0.05 MeV, where Δ vanishes within numerical accuracy
- Nuclear saturation density used as reference: ρ0 = 0.16 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends prior studies of BCS-BEC crossover in dilute neutron matter by Matsuo (2006), Margueron (2007), and others, generalizing them to the case of spin-polarized neutron matter induced by ultra-strong magnetic fields. The results set limits on neutron superfluidity in magnetar crusts and outer cores, with direct implications for the thermal, rotational, and oscillatory behavior of magnetars studied by Thompson and Duncan (1995). The phase diagram structure parallels that found for isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter by Stein (2012, 2014), though with weaker pairing interaction in neutron matter.
