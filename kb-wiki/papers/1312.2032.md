# Kinetic equations for massive Dirac fermions in electromagnetic field with non-Abelian Berry phase

> We derive a semi-classical effective action and the kinetic equation for massive Dirac fermions in electromagnetic fields. The non-Abelian Berry phase structure emerges from two helicity states of massive fermions with positive energy. The classical spin emerges as a vector in SU(2) helicity space. The continuity equations for the fermion number and the classical spin are derived. The fermion number is conserved while the spin charge is not conserved by anomaly. Previous results about the coefficients of the chiral magnetic effect for the fermion and axial currents in the chiral limit can be reproduced after including the anti-fermion contributions. This provides an example for the emerging spin and non-Abelian Berry phase of Dirac fermions arising from the fermion mass.

## Key claim

The paper derives a semi-classical effective action and kinetic equation for massive Dirac fermions in electromagnetic fields, where the non-Abelian Berry phase structure emerges from the two degenerate positive-energy helicity states. The authors show that the fermion number is conserved while the classical spin charge is not conserved due to an anomaly, and that previous results for the chiral magnetic effect in the massless limit can be reproduced after including anti-fermion contributions.

## Method

The authors use a path integral approach to derive an effective action for massive Dirac fermions, expanding the state space by introducing a classical spin degree of freedom as a vector in SU(2) helicity space. They work in the helicity basis for spin states and perform an adiabatic expansion that neglects negative energy eigenstates (anti-fermions), obtaining equations of motion for position, momentum, and spin. The collisionless Boltzmann equation is then used to derive the kinetic equation and continuity equations for fermion number and classical spin.

## Key numbers

* The Berry curvature components for massive fermions are given as: Ω¹ = (m/E_p³) e_θ, Ω² = (m/E_p³) e_φ, Ω³ = (1/E_p²) p̂, where E_p = √(|p|² + m²).
* The phase space measure is √γ = (1 + e Ω · B), with the condition e|Ω · B| ≪ 1 required for the Hamiltonian dynamics to remain valid.
* The condition m² ≫ eB is stated as necessary to ensure no antifermions can be involved when considering only positive energy solutions.
* For massless fermions, ρ₃ = ∇_p · Ω³ = 4π δ⁽³⁾(p), showing a singularity at zero momentum in the Berry curvature.

## Context

This work generalizes the previous chiral kinetic theory for massless fermions by Chen et al. (2012) to include fermion mass effects, and extends the single-particle semi-classical equations of motion from Chang et al. (2008) and Chuu et al. (2010) by incorporating the axial anomaly and developing a full kinetic theory. The results connect to the Chiral Magnetic Effect and Chiral Vortical Effect studied by Kharzeev et al. (2008), Fukushima et al. (2008), and others, and provide an example of how non-Abelian Berry phase and classical spin emerge from fermion mass, enabling further study of spin transport and anomaly-related phenomena in massive systems.
