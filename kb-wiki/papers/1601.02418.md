# Hartree-Fock and Random Phase Approximation theories in a many-fermion solvable model

> We present an ideal system of interacting fermions where the solutions of the many-body Schroedinger equation can be obtained without making approximations. These exact solutions are used to test the validity of two many-body effective approaches, the Hartree-Fock and the Random Phase Approximation theories. The description of the ground state done by the effective theories improves with increasing number of particles.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) solvable model, the Hartree-Fock (HF) and Random Phase Approximation (RPA) ground-state energies approach the exact solutions as the number of fermions increases, but both effective theories exhibit an unphysical discontinuous transition between two interaction-strength regions that is absent in the exact results.

## Method

The authors solve the many-body Schrodinger equation exactly for the LMG model, which consists of N fermions occupying two N-fold degenerate energy levels with a two-body interaction that scatters pairs between levels. They then compare these exact ground-state energies with those obtained from the variational Hartree-Fock method and from the Random Phase Approximation using the Quasi-Boson Approximation, for various particle numbers (N = 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 20) and interaction strengths (V and W).

## Key numbers

- For N = 2, the exact ground-state energy is \(-\epsilon \sqrt{1 + (V/\epsilon)^2} - W\) (for the lowest eigenvalue of the 3x3 Hamiltonian matrix).
- For N = 4 and W = 0, exact energy eigenvalues include \(0, \pm \sqrt{1+9 (V/\epsilon)^2}, \pm 2 \sqrt{1+3 (V/\epsilon)^2}\).
- The HF ground-state energy in region I (weak interaction) is \(E_{\rm HF} = -\frac{N}{2} (\epsilon + W)\); in region II (strong interaction) it is \(E_{\rm HF} = -\frac{N}{2} \left[ \frac{\epsilon^2 + (N-1)^2(V+W)^2}{2 (N-1)(V+W)} + W \right]\).
- The transition between HF regions occurs at \((N-1)(V+W) = \epsilon\).
- The RPA ground-state energy is given by \(E_{\rm RPA} = E_{\rm HF} + (\omega - A)/2\), where \(\omega = \sqrt{A^2 - |B|^2}\).
- For W = 0 at the transition point, the RPA energy is \(-\frac{N+1}{2} \epsilon\), while the HF energy is \(-\frac{N}{2} \epsilon\).

## Context

This work extends the classic Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model (Lipkin, Meshkov, Glick 1965) by providing a pedagogical derivation of exact solutions and using them to benchmark HF and RPA theories. The authors correct a factor of 4 in the N=6 and a factor of 6 in the N=8 analytical expressions from the original Lipkin et al. (1965) paper. The results enable quantitative assessment of how these many-body approximations improve with particle number and show that the discontinuous phase transition predicted by HF and RPA is an artifact not present in the exact solution.
