# $J/ψ$ Gluonic Dissociation Revisited : II. Hydrodynamic Expansion Effects

> We explicitly take into account the effect of hydrodynamic expansion profile on the gluonic breakup of $J/ψ$'s produced in an equilibrating parton plasma. Attention is paid to the space-time inhomogeneities as well as Lorentz frames while deriving new expressions for the gluon number density $n_g$, average dissociation rate $<\tildeΓ>$, and $ψ$ survival probability $S$. A novel type of partial wave {\em interference} mechanism is found to operate in the formula of $<\tildeΓ>$. Nonrelativistic longitudinal expansion fro small length of the initial cylinder is found to push the $S(p_T)$ graph above the no flow case considered by us earlier \cite{rev1}. However, relativistic flow corresponding to large length of the initial cylinder pushes the curve of $S(p_T)$ downwards at LHC but upwards at RHIC. This mutually different effect on $S(p_T)$ may be attributed to the different initial temperatures generated at LHC and RHIC.

## Key claim

The effect of hydrodynamic expansion on J/psi gluonic dissociation depends sensitively on the initial length of the plasma cylinder and the collision energy: nonrelativistic longitudinal flow (small initial cylinder length) pushes the survival probability curve above the no-flow case, while relativistic flow (large initial cylinder length) pushes the curve downwards at LHC but upwards at RHIC, a difference attributed to the different initial temperatures at the two colliders.

## Method

The authors derived new expressions for the gluon number density, thermally-averaged dissociation rate, and J/psi survival probability that incorporate the hydrodynamic velocity profile of the expanding parton plasma, accounting for space-time inhomogeneities and Lorentz transformations between the fireball, plasma, and J/psi rest frames. They employed Bjorken boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and an empirical transverse expansion ansatz, solving the master rate equations for temperature and fugacity evolution, then computing the survival probability by integrating the modified dissociation rate along the J/psi trajectory and averaging over production configurations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Patra and Menon, 2005) which examined J/psi gluonic dissociation without hydrodynamic flow, and improves upon the treatment of Xu et al. (year not given) who ignored flow velocity and used a factorized Bose-Einstein distribution. The paper introduces a novel partial wave interference mechanism in the thermally-averaged dissociation rate and demonstrates that hydrodynamic expansion profiles must be incorporated into J/psi suppression calculations for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. The results enable more realistic modeling of J/psi survival in expanding quark-gluon plasmas and suggest that transverse flow effects, not addressed here, warrant future investigation.
