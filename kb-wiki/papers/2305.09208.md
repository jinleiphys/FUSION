# Production of nuclei and hypernuclei in pion-induced reactions near threshold energies

> The Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics model is employed to simulate $π^-+\mathrm{C}$ and $π^-+\mathrm{W}$ collisions at p$_\mathrm{lab}=1.7$ GeV motivated by the recent HADES results. By comparing the proton and $Λ$ transverse momentum spectra, it was observed that the data and transport model calculation show a good agreement, if cluster formation is included to obtain the free proton spectra. Predictions of light cluster ($d$, $t$, $^3$He, $^4$He, as well as ${}^{3}_Λ$H and $Ξ$N) multiplicities and spectra are made using a coalescence mechanism. The resulting multiplicities suggest that the pion beam experiment can produce a substantial amount of ${}^{3}_Λ$H, especially in $π^-+\mathrm{W}$ collisions due to the stopping of the $Λ$ inside the large tungsten nucleus. The findings are supplemented by a statistical multi-fragmentation analysis suggesting that even larger hyper-fragments are produced copiously. It is suggested that even double strange hypernuclei are in reach and might be studied in more detail using a slightly higher pion beam momentum.

## Key claim

The UrQMD transport model, combined with coalescence and statistical multi-fragmentation, predicts that pion-induced reactions on tungsten at 1.7 GeV can produce a substantial amount of hypertritons (³ΛH) and even larger hypernuclei, with yields high enough to be accessible in the HADES experiment's data set of approximately 10⁸ recorded events. The claim that "even double strange hypernuclei are in reach" is hedged by the condition that a slightly higher pion beam momentum (reaching at least a center-of-mass energy of √s = mΞ + 2mK) would be required.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD v3.5) transport model to simulate minimum-bias π⁻+C and π⁻+W collisions at a laboratory momentum of 1.7 GeV. Light clusters (d, t, ³He, ⁴He) and hypernuclei (³ΛH, ΞN) were formed via a coalescence mechanism on the kinetic freeze-out hypersurface, and these results were contrasted with calculations from the Statistical Multi-fragmentation Model (SMM) applied to the UrQMD output at a fixed time of 20 fm/c. The simulations used 146 million events for π⁻+C and 41 million events for π⁻+W, with impact parameter ranges of 0<b<2.5 fm and 0<b<6.5 fm respectively.

## Key numbers

- Total cross sections used for normalization: σ(π⁻+C) = 196.35 mb, σ(π⁻+W) = 1327.32 mb
- The UrQMD model reproduces proton and Λ transverse momentum spectra with deviations below 50% across the pT range and all rapidity bins
- Hypertriton (³ΛH) production yield: approximately 10⁻³ per event, implying up to 10⁵ ³ΛH from the HADES data set of ~10⁸ recorded events
- Light nuclear fragments with mass numbers up to A=16 are produced with a yield of 10⁻⁶ per event; hypernuclei with mass number A=10 with a yield of 10⁻⁷ per event
- Ξ production in the tungsten system has a cross section on the order of a few μb
- The SMM coalescence parameter for primary hot cluster recognition was set to vc = 0.22

## Context

This work provides the first baseline predictions for light cluster and hypernuclei production in π⁻+C and π⁻+W collisions at 1.7 GeV, motivated by recent HADES measurements (HADES 2023). It extends previous coalescence studies by Gaebel (2020), Hillmann (2021), and Reichert (2022, 2023) to pion-induced reactions, and contrasts these with the statistical multi-fragmentation approach developed by Botvina (2020) and Buyukcizmeci (2020, 2023). The results suggest that the HADES pion beam program can implement parts of the hypermatter physics program originally proposed for the PANDA detector, and may enable studies of double-strange hypernuclei that are relevant for understanding strange matter in neutron stars (Ozel 2010, Blaschke 2015).
