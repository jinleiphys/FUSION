# Rotational motion of triaxially deformed nuclei studied by microscopic angular-momentum-projection method I: Nuclear wobbling motion

> Rotation of triaxially deformed nucleus has been an interesting subject in the study of nuclear structure. In the present series of work, we investigate wobbling motion and chiral rotation by employing the microscopic framework of angular-momentum projection from cranked triaxially deformed mean-field states. In this first part the wobbling motion is studied in detail. The consequences of the three dimensional cranking are investigated. It is demonstrated that the multiple wobbling rotational bands naturally appear as a result of fully microscopic calculation. They have the characteristic properties, that are expected from the macroscopic triaxial-rotor model or the phenomenological particle-triaxial-rotor model, although quantitative agreement with the existing data is not achieved. It is also found that the excitation spectrum reflects dynamics of the angular-momentum vector in the intrinsic frame of the mean-field (transverse vs. longitudinal wobbling). The results obtained by using the Woods-Saxon potential and the schematic separable interaction are mainly discussed, while some results with the Gogny D1S interaction are also presented.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that multiple wobbling rotational bands naturally emerge from a fully microscopic angular-momentum-projection calculation using cranked triaxially deformed mean-field states, but the authors explicitly state that quantitative agreement with existing experimental data is not achieved.

## Method

The authors employ angular-momentum projection from cranked triaxially deformed mean-field states generated by a Woods-Saxon potential with a schematic separable interaction, solving the Hill-Wheeler equation to obtain rotational band wave functions. The mean-field Hamiltonian includes three-dimensional tilted-axis cranking with rotational frequencies (ω_x, ω_y, ω_z), and the deformation parameters are fixed at β₂=0.42, β₄=0.02, and γ=18° for the study of ¹⁶³Lu. For comparison, some calculations use the selfconsistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method with the Gogny D1S interaction.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameters used for ¹⁶³Lu: β₂=0.42, β₄=0.02, γ=18° (Woods-Saxon potential), corresponding to γ(den) ≈ 11-12° for the density distribution
- Pairing gaps chosen as Δ_n ≈ Δ_p ≈ 0.5 MeV for high-spin wobbling calculations
- Basis states included up to maximum oscillator shells N_osc^max = 12
- Number of mesh points for Euler angle integration: N_α = N_γ = 42, N_β = 80 (sometimes increased to N_α = N_γ = 68, N_β = 126)
- Calculated first moment of inertia for the yrast band of ¹⁶²Yb: 𝒥^(1) ≈ 32 ℏ²/MeV at I ≈ 20 (non-cranked); increased to 𝒥^(1) ≈ 44 ℏ²/MeV at I ≈ 20 with infinitesimal cranking (ω_x = ω_y = ω_z = 0.01 MeV/ℏ)
- Critical angular momentum estimated from Eq. (13) at γ(WS)=18°: I_c ≈ 50.1 for j = 13/2
- Rigid-rotor reference energy subtracted: 0.007 I(I+1) MeV

## Context

This work extends the microscopic angular-momentum-projection approach previously applied to nuclear tetrahedral deformation (Shimada et al., 2013, 2015), γ-vibration (Tagami and Shimizu, 2016), and ground-state rotational bands (Shimada et al., 2015, 2016) in rare earth nuclei. The study contrasts with the projected shell model (PSM) approach (Hara and Sun, 1995) by optimizing mean-field states through cranking rather than including multi-quasiparticle configurations. The results are compared to the phenomenological triaxial-rotor model (Bohr and Mottelson, 1975) and the particle-triaxial-rotor model (Frauendorf and Meng, 1997), and the distinction between longitudinal and transverse wobbling follows the interpretation of Frauendorf and Dönau (2014). The work enables future application of the angular-momentum-projected multi-cranked configuration-mixing method for improved description of high-spin states in triaxially deformed nuclei.
