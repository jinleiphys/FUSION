# Linear Sigma Model at finite baryonic density and symmetry breakings

> The linear sigma model at finite baryonic density with a massive vector field is investigated considering that all the bosonic fields develop non zero expected classical values, eventually associated to condensates and corresponding to dynamical symmetry breakings which might occur in the QCD phase diagram. A modified equation for the classical vector field is proposed with its respective solution. Some in medium properties of the model (mainly masses) are investigated within reasonable prescriptions. In particular the behavior of the {\it in medium} pion and sigma masses and a particular way of calculating {\it in medium} coupling to baryons is investigated. A symmetry radius for finite baryonic densities is proposed and calculated in different ways in terms of the other variables of the model and these different ways of calculating it agree quite well. However, assuming that the pion and sigma masses go to zero close to the restoration of chiral symmetry a too high value for the critical density is obtained $ρ_c \simeq 4.3 ρ_0$.

# Linear Sigma Model at finite baryonic density and symmetry breakings

## Key claim

The linear sigma model with a massive vector field at finite baryonic density yields a critical density for chiral symmetry restoration of approximately 4.3 times nuclear saturation density when one assumes that pion and sigma masses go to zero at the restoration point; however, this value is considered "too high" by the author, suggesting either the model is too simple for high densities or that these meson masses should not be expected to vanish at the phase transition.

## Method

The author investigates the linear sigma model at finite baryonic density by treating all bosonic fields (sigma, pions, and the vector field) as developing classical condensates. The field equations and stability conditions are truncated to obtain analytical solutions, using a variational Gaussian approach for the spin-zero fields and treating the temporal component of the vector field as a variational parameter rather than quantizing it. The stability condition is factorized into three independent prescriptions for the fermionic, vector, and scalar-pseudoscalar sectors, and a "symmetry radius" is defined relating the sum of squared classical fields to the square root of baryonic density.

## Key numbers

- Critical density for chiral symmetry restoration: ρ_c ≈ 4.3 ρ_0, where ρ_0 is nuclear saturation density (0.15 fm⁻³)
- At saturation density, the symmetry radius √(σ̃² + π̃²) takes values: 0.54 v (for u=2), 0.65 v (for u=3), 0.68 v (for u=3.5), and 0.71 v (for u=4), where v = f_π is the vacuum pion decay constant
- The constant C̃ in the symmetry radius expression is estimated as: ±0.41 fm⁻¹/² (u=2), ±0.33 fm⁻¹/² (u=3), ±0.30 fm⁻¹/² (u=3.5), and ±0.28 fm⁻¹/² (u=4)
- For C̃ ≈ −0.15 fm⁻¹/² at ρ_0 = 0.15 fm⁻³, the sum of squared in-medium meson masses (μ_T^*)²(ρ_0) ≈ (1 ± 0.53) μ_T²(ρ_B=0)

## Context

This work extends the linear sigma model approach of Braghin (2005) and Iwara (unreferenced in text) by including a massive classical vector field and considering all mesonic condensates simultaneously. The investigation of pseudoscalar condensation at finite density connects to studies by Toki et al. (unreferenced in text) and to work on CP violation enhancement at finite density. The results are qualitatively consistent with other in-medium hadron property estimations (inmedium, unreferenced in text), but the high critical density obtained suggests that either quark and gluon degrees of freedom must be included for a realistic description, or that the assumption of vanishing meson masses at chiral restoration is incorrect. The symmetry radius formalism provides a framework for connecting different regimes of the QCD phase diagram, with applications to matter-antimatter asymmetry in heavy ion collisions and the Early Universe to be explored in future work.
