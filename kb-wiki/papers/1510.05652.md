# Model-independent determination of the axial mass parameter in quasielastic antineutrino-nucleon scattering

> Understanding the charged current quasielestic (CCQE) neutrino-nucleus interaction is important for precision studies of neutrino oscillations. The theoretical description of the interaction depends on the combination of a nuclear model with the knowledge of form factors. While the former has received considerable attention, the latter, in particular the axial form factor, is implemented using the historical dipole model. Instead, we use a model-independent approach, presented in a previous study, to analyze the muon antineutrino CCQE mineral oil data published by the MiniBooNE collaboration. We combine the cross section for scattering of antineutrinos off protons in carbon and hydrogen, using the same axial form factor for both. The extracted value of the axial mass parameter $m_A = 0.84^{+0.12}_{-0.04} \pm {0.11} \, {\rm GeV}$ is in very good agreement with the model-independent value extracted from MiniBooNE's neutrino data. Going beyond a one-parameter description of the axial form factor, we extract values of the axial form factor in the range of $Q^2=0.1...1.0$ GeV$^2$, finding a very good agreement with the analogous extraction from the neutrino data. We discuss the implications of these results.

## Key claim

Using a model-independent z-expansion approach to analyze MiniBooNE's muon antineutrino CCQE data, the extracted axial mass parameter is consistent with the value from neutrino data, but the extraction relies on the Relativistic Fermi Gas nuclear model and the asymmetry in the error bars may indicate that the nuclear cross section for carbon is too small.

## Method

The authors applied the z-expansion formalism, previously developed in Bhattacharya (2011), to parameterize the axial form factor in a model-independent way, fitting it to MiniBooNE's flux-averaged double differential cross section for muon antineutrino scattering off mineral oil (carbon and hydrogen). They combined the cross sections for antineutrinos scattering off protons in carbon (using the Relativistic Fermi Gas nuclear model with binding energy 0.025 GeV from Moniz (1971)) and off free protons in hydrogen, using the same axial form factor for both, and minimized a chi-squared function that included shape and normalization uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Extracted axial mass from antineutrino data: \( m_A = 0.84^{+0.12}_{-0.04} \pm 0.11 \, \text{GeV} \) (first error experimental, second from form factor shape uncertainty), using \( Q^2_{\text{max}} = 1.0 \, \text{GeV}^2 \)
- Extracted axial mass from neutrino data in Bhattacharya (2011): \( m_A = 0.85^{+0.22}_{-0.07} \pm 0.09 \, \text{GeV} \)
- Dipole model fit to antineutrino data with same \( Q^2_{\text{max}} \): \( m_A^{\text{dipole}} = 1.27^{+0.03}_{-0.04} \, \text{GeV} \)
- Extracted axial radius from antineutrino data: \( r_A = 0.81^{+0.05}_{-0.10} \pm 0.14 \, \text{fm} \)
- Extracted z-expansion coefficient \( a_2 = -9.6^{+4.7}_{-2.6} \) (indication of non-zero curvature, poorly constrained)
- Extracted z-expansion coefficient \( a_1 = 3.4^{+0.9}_{-1.0} \)

## Context

This work extends the model-independent z-expansion method of Bhattacharya (2011), which was applied to MiniBooNE neutrino data, to the corresponding antineutrino data from Aguilar-Arevalo (2013). The results confirm that the discrepancy between dipole-model extractions from neutrino scattering and pion electroproduction is an artifact of the dipole model, and the method enables future extraction of the axial form factor as a function of \( Q^2 \) from multiple experiments, analogous to the world data set for electromagnetic form factors.
