# Collinear limit of the energy-energy correlator in $e^+ e^-$ collisions: transition from perturbative to non-perturbative regimes

> We study the collinear limit of the energy-energy correlator (EEC) in $e^+e^-$ collisions, focusing on the transition from the perturbative QCD regime at relatively large angles to the non-perturbative region at small angles. To describe this transition, we introduce a non-perturbative jet function and perform a global analysis at NNLO+NNLL accuracy using experimental data spanning center-of-mass energies from $Q = 29.0$ to $91.2$ GeV. This marks the first accurate description of the EEC across the entire near-side region ($0^\circ<χ<90^\circ$) within a unified theoretical framework. Our analysis also provides, for the first time, a quantitative extraction of the non-perturbative contribution to the EEC quark jet function, identifying a characteristic transition scale around $2.3$ GeV - distinct from the scale observed in EEC-in-jet measurements in $pp$ collisions at the LHC, which are dominated by gluon jets. These results offer the first evidence for flavor dependence (quark vs. gluon) in the EEC and provide new insights into the interplay between perturbative and non-perturbative QCD dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first accurate description of the energy-energy correlator (EEC) across the entire near-side angular region (0° < χ < 90°) in e+e- collisions within a unified theoretical framework at NNLO+NNLL accuracy, and extracts a non-perturbative quark jet function that reveals a characteristic transition scale around 2.3 GeV, distinct from the gluon-dominated scale observed in pp collisions at the LHC.

## Method

The authors perform a global analysis of the EEC in e+e- annihilation by factorizing the resummed cross section into hard functions and position-space jet functions, where non-perturbative effects are incorporated as a multiplicative exponential function j_np(b) = exp[-(a1*b)^a2] applied to the perturbative jet function at the initial scale. They fit four free parameters (a1, a2, and K-factors A and B) to experimental data from eight datasets spanning center-of-mass energies from Q = 29.0 to 91.2 GeV, using Monte-Carlo replica techniques for uncertainty estimation and scale variation for theory uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- The fit uses 257 data points from eight datasets (OPAL, SLD, TOPAZ, TASSO, MARK II, MAC) spanning center-of-mass energies Q = 29.0 to 91.2 GeV, yielding a total chi-squared of 354 (chi-squared per data point = 1.38)
- The extracted non-perturbative parameters are: a1 = 2.310 (+0.015/-0.010 statistical, +0.248/-0.279 theoretical) GeV and a2 = 1.059 (+0.006/-0.006 statistical, +0.010/-0.036 theoretical)
- The K-factors are A = 0.858 (+0.002/-0.003 statistical, +0.001/-0.002 theoretical) and B = 1.238 (+0.010/-0.010 statistical, +0.009/-0.016 theoretical)
- The characteristic transition scale for quark jets is around 2.3 GeV, compared to 3.8 GeV extracted from gluon-dominated EEC-in-jet measurements in pp collisions at the LHC
- The transition peak occurs around 2.8 GeV when expressed as a function of transverse momentum Q sin(chi/2)

## Context

This work extends the framework of Barata et al. (2024) for EEC inside jets in pp collisions to e+e- annihilation, and improves upon earlier studies by Korchemsky (1999) and Lee et al. (2024) that included only leading power corrections. The analysis provides the first evidence for flavor dependence (quark vs. gluon) in the EEC, as the extracted quark jet transition scale (2.3 GeV) is significantly lower than the gluon jet scale (3.8 GeV) from CMS data fits by Barata et al. (2024). The results enable future combined NNLL fits of e+e- and LHC pp datasets to separately constrain non-perturbative contributions of quark and gluon jets, and can be extended to track-based EEC observables from ALEPH, STAR, and ALICE data, as well as future measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider.
