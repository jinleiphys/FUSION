# Level density of a Fermi gas and integer partitions: a Gumbel-like finite-size correction

> We investigate the many-body level density of gas of non-interacting fermions. We determine its behavior as a function of the temperature and the number of particles. As the temperature increases, and beyond the usual Sommerfeld expansion that describes the degenerate gas behavior, corrections due to a finite number of particles lead to Gumbel-like contributions. We discuss connections with the partition problem in number theory, extreme value statistics as well as differences with respect to the Bose gas.

# Level density of a Fermi gas and integer partitions: a Gumbel-like finite-size correction

## Key claim

Beyond the usual Sommerfeld expansion that describes the degenerate Fermi gas behavior, finite-number-of-particles corrections to the many-body level density follow a modified Gumbel law, but only for single-particle level densities that follow a power law; the correction factor can be larger than one for some values of the exponent, unlike the strictly less-than-one Gumbel correction found for the one-dimensional harmonic oscillator case.

## Method

The authors compute the many-body level density of non-interacting fermions using the saddle-point approximation of the inverse Laplace transform of the grand partition function, with a smooth power-law single-particle level density ρ(ε) = ν ε^(ν-1). They expand the polylogarithm functions that appear in the particle-number and energy equations using the asymptotic inversion formula of Garoni et al. (2005), separating the local Sommerfeld expansion terms from an exponentially small non-local term e^(-βμ) that accounts for the finite particle number and the lower bound of the single-particle spectrum.

## Key numbers

- For ν=1 (one-dimensional harmonic oscillator or two-dimensional billiard), the entropy correction is S = 2√(2η(2)Q) - e^(-t) with t = √(2η(2)/Q) N + ln[√(2η(2)/Q)], where η(2)=π²/12.
- The leading-order entropy for a power-law spectrum is S(Q,N) = 2 √(ν π² Q/6) N^((ν-1)/(2ν)).
- The correction factor F(Q,N) = exp[cos(πν) Γ(ν+1) β₀^(-ν) exp(-β₀ μ₀)] where β₀ = ν √(2η(2)/Q) N^((ν-1)/(2ν)) and μ₀ = N^(1/ν).
- For N=5 fermions with ν=1, the correction term is significant only for excitation energies Q ≲ 15.

## Context

This work extends the Bethe (1936) formula for the many-body level density of a degenerate Fermi gas, which gives a stretched exponential growth ρ_MB ∝ exp(2√(π² ρ̅(μ₀)Q/6)), by adding finite-size corrections. It connects to the partition problem in number theory, where the one-dimensional harmonic oscillator case maps exactly to the integer partition function p_N(n) studied by Erdős and Lehner (1941), who found a Gumbel distribution for the ratio p_N(n)/p(n). The authors generalize this result to power-law spectra using the non-local Sommerfeld expansion of Garoni et al. (2005), and contrast their findings with the bosonic case studied by Comtet et al. (2007), where three different extreme-value distributions appear depending on the exponent ν. The results are relevant for computing level densities of small nuclei where finite-size corrections are most important.
