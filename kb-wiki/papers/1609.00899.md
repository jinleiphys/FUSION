# First Monte Carlo analysis of fragmentation functions from single-inclusive $e^+ e^-$ annihilation

> We perform the first iterative Monte Carlo (IMC) analysis of fragmentation functions constrained by all available data from single-inclusive $e^+ e^-$ annihilation into pions and kaons. The IMC method eliminates potential bias in traditional analyses based on single fits introduced by fixing parameters not well contrained by the data and provides a statistically rigorous determination of uncertainties. Our analysis reveals specific features of fragmentation functions using the new IMC methodology and those obtained from previous analyses, especially for light quarks and for strange quark fragmentation to kaons.

## Key claim

The paper performs the first iterative Monte Carlo (IMC) analysis of fragmentation functions (FFs) from single-inclusive \(e^+ e^-\) annihilation data, claiming that this method eliminates bias from fixing poorly constrained parameters and provides statistically rigorous uncertainties, but the analysis is confined to \(e^+ e^-\) data alone and does not yet constitute a definitive determination of FFs.

## Method

The authors performed a global next-to-leading-order (NLO) QCD analysis of all available single-inclusive \(e^+ e^-\) annihilation cross section data for charged pions and kaons, using an iterative Monte Carlo (IMC) fitting procedure. The IMC method samples parameter space by generating initial priors, performing least-squares fits, and iteratively updating the prior distribution to construct a Monte Carlo ensemble that represents the posterior probability density of the FF parameters. The theoretical cross sections were computed in Mellin moment space to improve computational efficiency, and the data were binned correctly by integrating over each \(z\) bin rather than using bin averages.

## Key numbers

* Total \(\chi^2 / N_{\rm dat} = 1.31\) for pions (459 data points) and \(\chi^2 / N_{\rm dat} = 1.01\) for kaons (391 data points) across all experiments.
* The analysis used data from center-of-mass energies \(Q\) ranging from 9.98 GeV (ARGUS) to 91.28 GeV (SLD), including flavor-tagged data from OPAL at \(Q = 91.20\) GeV and TPC at \(Q = 29.00\) GeV.
* The strong coupling constant was fixed at \(\alpha_s(m_Z) = 0.118\), and heavy quark masses were set to \(m_c = 1.43\) GeV and \(m_b = 4.3\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the iterative Monte Carlo methodology previously introduced by the JAM Collaboration (JAM15) for spin-dependent parton distribution functions to the case of fragmentation functions, addressing ambiguities in strange quark FFs highlighted by Leader et al. (2011) and the DSS (2007) and HKNS (2007) parametrizations. The analysis is a first step toward a definitive determination of FFs that will later include semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering and proton-proton collision data, enabling more reliable extraction of the strange quark contribution to the nucleon spin.
