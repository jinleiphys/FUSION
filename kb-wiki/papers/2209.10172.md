# Pion-mediated Cooper pairing of neutrons: beyond the bare vertex approximation

> In some quantum many particle systems, the fermions could form Cooper pairs by exchanging intermediate bosons. This then drives a superconducting phase transition or a superfluid transition. Such transitions should be theoretically investigated by using proper non-perturbative methods. Here we take the neutron superfluid transition as an example and study the Cooper pairing of neutrons mediated by neutral $π$-mesons in the low density region of a neutron matter. We perform a non-perturbative analysis of the neutron-meson coupling and compute the pairing gap $Δ$, the critical density $ρ_{c}$, and the critical temperature $T_c$ by solving the Dyson-Schwinger equation of the neutron propagator. We first carry out calculations under the widely used bare vertex approximation and then incorporate the contribution of the lowest-order vertex correction. This vertex correction is not negligible even at low densities and its importance is further enhanced as the density increases. The transition critical line on density-temperature plane obtained under the bare vertex approximation is substantially changed after including the vertex correction. These results indicate that the vertex corrections play a significant role and need to be seriously taken into account.

## Key claim

The widely used bare vertex approximation (Migdal-Eliashberg equations) for pion-mediated neutron pairing in low-density neutron matter is unreliable because the one-loop vertex correction is not negligible even at very low densities, and its importance grows with increasing density, substantially altering the predicted critical temperature and critical density of the superfluid transition.

## Method

The authors constructed a non-relativistic model of neutrons coupled to neutral π⁰ mesons via a Yukawa interaction, then solved the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the neutron propagator under the bare vertex approximation to obtain the pairing gap Δ(p), critical density ρ_c, and critical temperature T_c. They subsequently computed the lowest-order (one-loop) vertex correction in the zero-energy and zero-momentum limits and compared the transition critical line on the density-temperature plane with and without this correction.

## Key numbers

- Neutron mass M_N ≈ 940 MeV; pion mass m_π ≈ 135 MeV; coupling constant f_π ≈ 1.0 (f_π²/4π ≈ 0.08)
- Saturation density ρ₀ ≈ 0.17 fm⁻³; the study considers neutron densities ρ < 0.1ρ₀
- The one-loop vertex correction is not negligible even at very low densities and its importance is further enhanced as density increases
- The transition critical line on the density-temperature plane obtained under the bare vertex approximation is substantially changed after including the vertex correction

## Context

This work extends the non-perturbative Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to neutron superfluid transitions, moving beyond the BCS mean-field theory that has dominated previous studies (reviewed by Lombardo, Sedrakian, and Baldo). The authors note that BCS mean-field results appear unreliable even in the ultra-low density limit and that realistic potentials used in BCS calculations are constrained by phase-shift data only below roughly 350 MeV. By treating the pion-mediated interaction non-perturbatively and including vertex corrections, this work provides a framework that can later incorporate other mesons (σ, ω, ρ) to study higher-density regions and unify superfluid transition studies with equation-of-state calculations for neutron stars.
