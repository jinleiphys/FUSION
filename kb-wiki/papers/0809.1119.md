# Determining the Energy Barrier for Decay out of Superdeformed Bands

> An asymptotically exact quantum mechanical calculation of the matrix elements for tunneling through an asymmetric barrier is combined with the two-state statistical model for decay out of superdeformed bands to determine the energy barrier (as a function of spin) separating the superdeformed and normal-deformed wells for several nuclei in the 190 and 150 mass regions. The spin-dependence of the barrier leading to sudden decay out is shown to be consistent with the decrease of a centrifugal barrier with decreasing angular momentum. Values of the barrier frequency in the two mass regions are predicted.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the rapid decrease in barrier height with decreasing nuclear spin explains the superdeformed decay mechanism, and that this spin-dependence is consistent with the decrease of a centrifugal barrier as angular momentum decreases. The barrier height is determined by combining an asymptotically exact quantum tunneling calculation with a two-state statistical model, moving beyond previous semiclassical treatments that could introduce errors of several orders of magnitude in the tunneling width.

## Method

The authors combine a Euclidean path-integral calculation of tunneling matrix elements through an asymmetric cubic potential barrier with the two-state statistical model of superdeformed decay. The two-state model assumes each superdeformed state couples to its nearest-lying normal-deformed level, and the mean-square tunneling matrix element is extracted from experimental branching ratios and electromagnetic decay widths. The barrier height is then determined by equating the path-integral expression for the tunneling matrix element with the statistically-determined value from experiment, and the spin-dependence is analyzed using a two-fluid model of nuclear rotation to account for the centrifugal barrier.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁹²Hg at spin I=12: W/ℏω = 1.8, S/ℏ = 6.5; at I=10: W/ℏω = 1.3, S/ℏ = 4.7
- For ¹⁵²Dy at spin I=28: W/ℏω = 1.6, S/ℏ = 5.8; at I=26: W/ℏω = 1.2, S/ℏ = 4.4
- Self-consistent barrier frequency ℏω = 0.24 MeV for the 190 mass region (from ¹⁹²Hg data)
- Self-consistent barrier frequency ℏω = 0.56 MeV for the 150 mass region (from ¹⁵²Dy data)
- For ¹⁵²Dy: ΔW = W(28)-W(26) = 0.21 MeV, with kinetic moment of inertia ℐ_S^(1) = 85 ℏ²/MeV and aspect ratio η = 2.0
- For ¹⁹²Hg: ΔW = W(12)-W(10) = 0.12 MeV, with kinetic moment of inertia ℐ_S^(1) = 90 ℏ²/MeV and aspect ratio η = 1.65

## Context

This work extends the two-state model of superdeformed decay-out developed by Stafford and Barrett (2005) and Cardamone, Stafford, and Barrett (2008), which extracted the tunneling matrix element V and spreading width Γ^↓ from experiment. It goes beyond the semiclassical treatment of Kruecken (1996) that could not accurately compute the tunneling rate prefactor, and beyond the bare ND-continuum result used by Shimizu (1993) and Khoo (1993) which could introduce errors of several orders of magnitude. The results enable future studies to connect the decay-out phenomenology directly to nuclear structure parameters, though the authors note that better determinations of the normal-deformed level spacing D_N and electromagnetic width Γ_N are needed.
