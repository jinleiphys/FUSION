# Effects of finite nucleon size, vacuum polarization, and electromagnetic spin-orbit interaction on nuclear binding energies and radii in spherical nuclei

> The electromagnetic effects of the finite size of the nucleon are implemented self-consistently on top of the Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculation, where the electric form factors of both protons and neutrons are considered. Furthermore, the vacuum polarization and the electromagnetic spin-orbit interaction are taken into account. The self-consistent finite-size effects give a different Coulomb potential from the conventional one and affect the neutrons as well. The contribution of the finite-size effects to the total energy reaches $ 7 \, \mathrm{MeV} $ in $ {}^{208} \mathrm{Pb} $. The vacuum polarization and the electromagnetic spin-orbit interaction are also non-negligible, especially in the heavy nuclei. These effects provide a comparable contribution of the total energy to that of the isospin symmetry-breaking terms of the nuclear interaction. The mirror nuclei mass difference in $ {}^{48} \mathrm{Ca} $--$ {}^{48} \mathrm{Ni} $ is also studied, and its value is improved by approximately one order of magnitude.

## Key claim

The paper implements the finite size of nucleons self-consistently within Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations, showing that this self-consistent treatment yields a Coulomb potential different from the conventional one and affects neutrons as well as protons, with the total contribution reaching 7 MeV in 208Pb. The authors note that these effects, together with vacuum polarization and electromagnetic spin-orbit interaction, provide a contribution comparable to that of isospin symmetry-breaking terms of the nuclear interaction, but they do not address the relationship between bare and effective isospin symmetry-breaking forces.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations for spherical nuclei, incorporating the electric form factors of both protons and neutrons (using the parameterization of Friedrich and Walcher (2003)) to compute the charge density from the proton and neutron densities via convolution. The Coulomb exchange term was treated using a generalized gradient approximation (GGA) functional rather than the exact Fock term, and the vacuum polarization was included via the Uehling potential. The electromagnetic spin-orbit interaction was added perturbatively using first-order perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- The contribution of finite-size effects to the total energy reaches 7 MeV in 208Pb.
- The GGA Coulomb exchange functional reproduces the Coulomb exact-Fock energy within 100 keV error.
- The simple estimation gives the Coulomb direct energy as approximately 0.60 Z^(5/3) MeV under the point-particle approximation.
- The simple estimation gives the Coulomb exchange energy as approximately -0.46 Z MeV under the point-particle approximation.
- The proton finite-size correction to the total energy is estimated as approximately -0.11 Z MeV.
- The neutron finite-size correction to the total energy is estimated as approximately 0.010 N MeV.
- The reduced Compton wavelength of electrons used in the vacuum polarization calculation is 386.15926796 fm.
- The electromagnetic spin-orbit interaction affects single-particle energies by less than 100 keV.
- The mirror nuclei mass difference in 48Ca-48Ni is improved by approximately one order of magnitude compared to previous calculations.

## Context

This work extends previous studies that considered finite-size effects and vacuum polarization in the context of isobaric analog state energies (Auerbach (1972), Roca-Maza et al. (2018)) by implementing these effects self-consistently within the density functional theory framework. It also builds on the generalized gradient approximation for the Coulomb exchange functional developed by Naito et al. (2018, 2019). The results enable more precise separation of Coulomb contributions from isospin symmetry-breaking nuclear interactions in studies of mirror nuclei mass differences and the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly, and complement the parametrization of isospin symmetry-breaking terms proposed by Baczyk et al. (2018, 2019).
