# Missing-mass spectroscopy of the ${}^{12}{\rm C}(p,d)$ reaction near the $η^\prime$-meson production threshold

> Excitation-energy spectra of $^{11}$C nuclei near the $η^\prime$-meson production threshold have been measured by missing-mass spectroscopy using the $^{12}$C($p$,$d$) reaction. A carbon target has been irradiated with a 2.5 GeV proton beam supplied by the synchrotron SIS-18 at GSI to produce $η^\prime$ meson bound states in $^{11}$C nuclei. Deuterons emitted at $0^\circ$ in the reaction have been momentum-analyzed by the fragment separator (FRS) used as a high-resolution spectrometer. No distinct structure due to the formation of $η^\prime$-mesic states is observed although a high statistical sensitivity is achieved in the experimental spectra. Upper limits on the formation cross sections of $η^\prime$-mesic states are determined, and thereby a constraint imposed on the $η^\prime$-nucleus interaction is discussed.

## Key claim

The experiment searched for eta-prime mesic nuclei via missing-mass spectroscopy of the 12C(p,d) reaction near the eta-prime production threshold, but no distinct structure due to eta-prime-mesic state formation was observed despite high statistical sensitivity; only upper limits on formation cross sections could be determined, constraining the eta-prime-nucleus interaction.

## Method

A 2.5 GeV proton beam from the SIS-18 synchrotron at GSI irradiated a natural carbon target, and deuterons emitted at 0 degrees were momentum-analyzed by the fragment separator (FRS) used as a high-resolution magnetic spectrometer. The experiment employed seven FRS momentum settings to cover a wide excitation-energy region, with the region near the eta-prime production threshold intensively measured. Deuteron identification was performed using time-of-flight measurements between plastic scintillation counters and waveform analysis of scintillator signals, while momentum reconstruction used multi-wire drift chambers at the final focal plane calibrated via elastic D(p,d)p scattering.

## Key numbers

- Proton beam kinetic energy: 2499.1 +/- 2.0 MeV for the 12C(p,d) reaction
- Deuteron momentum at the eta-prime production threshold after energy loss in target: 2814.4 +/- 2.4 MeV/c
- FRS momentum resolving power: about 3.8 x 10^3
- Dispersion at final focal plane (F4): 35.1 mm/%
- Deuteron identification efficiency: 96-97% for all data sets
- Background proton contamination fraction in identified deuteron events: approximately 2 x 10^-4
- Time resolution for the deuteron peak in TOF: sigma = 1.7 x 10^2 ps
- Deuteron rate at F4: approximately 1 kHz; proton rate: approximately 250 kHz
- Carbon target areal density: 4115 +/- 1 mg/cm^2

## Context

This work extends the experimental search for eta-prime-mesic nuclei, following theoretical predictions of eta-prime mass reduction in nuclear matter from models such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Costa 2005, Nagahiro 2006), the linear sigma model (Sakai 2013), and the quark meson coupling model (Bass 2006). The experiment was motivated by earlier CBELSA/TAPS collaboration results (Nanova 2013, 2016) that deduced a real potential depth of V0 = -(39 +/- 7(stat) +/- 15(syst)) MeV and an imaginary part of W0 = -(13 +/- 3(stat) +/- 3(syst)) MeV from eta-prime photo-production on nuclear targets. The results provide upper limits on formation cross sections that constrain the eta-prime-nucleus interaction, and the paper discusses future plans for a follow-up experiment with higher sensitivity.
