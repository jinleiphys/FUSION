# Charmonium in Hot Medium

> We investigate charmonium production in the hot medium created by heavy-ion collisions by setting up a framework in which in-medium charmonium properties are constrained by thermal lattice QCD (lQCD) and subsequently implemented into kinetic approaches. A Boltzmann transport equation is employed to describe the time evolution of the charmonium phase space distribution with the loss and gain term accounting for charmonium dissociation and regeneration (from charm quarks), respectively. The momentum dependence of the charmonium dissociation rate is worked out. The dominant process for in-medium charmonium regeneration is found to be a 3-to-2 process. Its corresponding regeneration rates from different input charm-quark momentum spectra are evaluated. Experimental data on $J/ψ$ production at CERN-SPS and BNL-RHIC are compared with our numerical results in terms of both rapidity-dependent inclusive yields and transverse momentum ($p_t$) spectra. Within current uncertainties from (interpreting) lQCD data and from input charm-quark spectra the centrality dependence of $J/ψ$ production at SPS and RHIC (for both mid- and forward rapidity) is reasonably well reproduced. The $J/ψ$ $p_t$ data are shown to have a discriminating power for in-medium charmonium properties as inferred from different interpretations of lQCD results.

## Key claim

Within current uncertainties from interpreting thermal lattice QCD data and from input charm-quark spectra, both a "weak binding" scenario (J/psi melting temperature approximately 1.25 Tc) and a "strong binding" scenario (melting temperature approximately 2 Tc) can reproduce the centrality dependence of inclusive J/psi yields at SPS and RHIC, but the transverse momentum spectra favor the strong-binding scenario.

## Method

The authors set up a framework where in-medium charmonium properties are constrained by thermal lattice QCD and implemented into a Boltzmann transport equation describing the time evolution of the charmonium phase space distribution. The loss term accounts for charmonium dissociation and the gain term for regeneration from charm quarks, with the dominant regeneration process identified as a 3-to-2 process. The momentum dependence of the dissociation rate is worked out, and regeneration rates from different input charm-quark momentum spectra are evaluated, with results compared to experimental J/psi data from CERN-SPS and BNL-RHIC.

## Key numbers

- J/psi "melting temperatures" of approximately 1.25 Tc (weak binding scenario) and approximately 2 Tc (strong binding scenario) are inferred from identifying either the free or internal energy as the in-medium 2-body potential between charm and anti-charm quarks.
- Experimental data on J/psi production at CERN-SPS and BNL-RHIC are compared with numerical results in terms of rapidity-dependent inclusive yields and transverse momentum (pt) spectra.
- The centrality dependence of J/psi production at SPS and RHIC for both mid- and forward rapidity is reasonably well reproduced within current uncertainties.

## Context

This work extends the traditional picture of charmonium suppression as a signature of Quark-Gluon Plasma formation, as originally proposed by Matsui and Satz (1986), by including both suppression and regeneration mechanisms in a kinetic transport approach. It builds on prior work by Grandchamp and Rapp (2001, 2004) and Zhao and Rapp (2009) on charmonium production in heavy-ion collisions, and uses lattice QCD results from Kaczmarek and Zantow (2005) and Petreczky and Petrov (2004) to constrain in-medium properties. The framework enables discriminating between different interpretations of lattice QCD results through comparison with experimental transverse momentum spectra.
