# Heavy Ion e+ e- Pairs to All Orders in Z alpha

> The heavy ion cross section for continuum e+ e- pair production has been calculated to all orders in Z alpha. Comparison is made with available CERN SPS and RHIC STAR data. Computed cross sections are found to be reduced from perturbation theory with increasing charge of the colliding heavy ions and for all energy and momentum regions investigated. Au or Pb total cross sections are reduced by 28% (SPS), 17% (RHIC),and 11% (LHC). For very high energy (E_e+, E_e- > 3 GeV) forward pairs at LHC the reduction from perturbation theory is a bit larger (17%). Use of zero degree calorimeter triggering (and thus small impact parameter weighting) makes impact parameter representation of exact pair production useful. Preliminary exact calculations in the zero impact parameter limit show a much larger reduction from perturbation theory (about 40%) at both RHIC and LHC.

# Heavy Ion e+ e- Pairs to All Orders in Z alpha

## Key claim
The heavy ion cross section for continuum e+ e- pair production is reduced from perturbation theory when calculated to all orders in Z alpha, with the reduction increasing for larger nuclear charge Z. However, the abstract's claim of a 28% reduction at SPS energies contradicts the experimentally observed Z1^2 Z2^2 scaling, and the author argues this discrepancy needs experimental verification.

## Method
A two-center light cone calculation was performed by exactly solving the semi-classical Dirac equation for colliding delta function potentials in the collider center of mass frame. A physical cutoff of the transverse potential was introduced to regularize the exact Dirac equation amplitude, yielding Coulomb corrections consistent with the Lee-Milstein approximate analytical result. Total cross sections were computed numerically for various heavy ion systems and compared with perturbative Racah formula results.

## Key numbers
- Pb + Au at SPS (gamma = 9.2): exact cross section 2670 b, perturbative 3720 b, reduction of 28% (1050 b)
- Au + Au at RHIC (gamma = 100): exact cross section 28,600 b, perturbative 34,600 b, reduction of 17% (6000 b)
- Pb + Pb at LHC (gamma = 2960): exact cross section 199,000 b, perturbative 224,000 b, reduction of 11% (25,000 b)
- Forward pairs at LHC (E > 3 GeV, 0.00223 rad < theta < 0.00817 rad): perturbative 2.88 b reduced by 18% to 2.36 b
- Zero impact parameter limit for Au + Au at RHIC: perturbative probability P^0(0) = 1.64, exact P(0) = 0.94, reduction of 43%
- Zero impact parameter limit for Pb + Pb at LHC: perturbative probability P^0(0) = 4.07, exact P(0) = 2.39, reduction of 41%

## Context
This work extends the exact Dirac equation approach of Baltz and McLerran (2000) and the scaling observations of Segev and Wells (2000) by incorporating a physical cutoff that yields Coulomb corrections consistent with Lee and Milstein (2000). The calculations contradict the Z1^2 Z2^2 scaling reported by Vane et al. from SPS data, and the STAR collaboration's limit on higher-order corrections (-0.5 sigma_QED < Delta sigma < 0.2 sigma_QED at 90% confidence) does not exclude the 17% reduction predicted for RHIC. The results enable future comparisons of electromagnetic pair production between Au + Au and lighter systems like Ca + Ca at RHIC or LHC, and motivate construction of a computer program to calculate impact-parameter-dependent probabilities P(b) exactly.
