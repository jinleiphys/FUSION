# Hyperon resonances and meson-baryon interactions in isospin 1

> In this work we extend our formalism to study meson-baryon interactions by including $s$- and $u$-channel diagrams for pseudoscalar-baryon systems. We study the coupled systems with strangeness $-1$ and focus on studying the isospin-1 resonance(s), especially in the energy region around 1400 MeV. By constraining the model parameters to fit the cross section data available on several processes involving relevant channels, we find resonances in the isoscalar as well as the isovector sector in the energy region around 1400 MeV.

## Key claim

By extending their coupled-channel formalism to include \(s\)- and \(u\)-channel diagrams for pseudoscalar-baryon interactions, the authors find that isovector (Sigma) resonances with masses around 1400 MeV appear in the complex-energy plane even when the \(u\)-channel contribution is switched off, suggesting these poles are not artifacts of the left-hand cut.

## Method

The authors constructed transition amplitudes for pseudoscalar-baryon (PB) and vector-baryon (VB) systems from chiral Lagrangians, including \(s\)-, \(t\)-, and \(u\)-channel diagrams plus contact interactions, and solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation in on-shell factorization for 14 coupled channels (e.g., \(\bar K N\), \(\pi \Sigma\), \(\bar K^* N\), \(\rho \Sigma\)). They fitted the model parameters, subtraction constants for each channel and couplings between PB and VB channels, to experimental cross-section data for processes such as \(K^- p \to K^- p\), \(\bar K^0 n\), \(\pi^0 \Lambda\), and \(\pi^\pm \Sigma^\mp\), as well as kaonic hydrogen level shift and width data from Bazzi (2011).

## Key numbers

* Two sets of fits (Fit I and Fit II) both achieved \(\chi^2_\text{d.o.f} \sim 1\).
* In isospin 0, the \(\Lambda(1405)\) poles were found at \(1373 - i\,114\) MeV and \(1426 - i\,16\) MeV (Fit I), and at \(1385 - i\,124\) MeV and \(1426 - i\,15\) MeV (Fit II).
* In isospin 1, Fit I yielded two Sigma poles: \(1396 - i\,5\) MeV and \(1367 - i\,57\) MeV; Fit II yielded one Sigma pole at \(1399 - i\,36\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors’ previous coupled-channel study (Khemchandani, 2012) which found two wide Sigma resonances (around 1427 and 1438 MeV) using a simpler Weinberg-Tomozawa term for pseudoscalar-baryon interactions. By now including \(s\)- and \(u\)-channel diagrams for the pseudoscalar sector, inspired by Oller and Meissner (2001) and Guo and Oller (2013), the present results find narrower Sigma states with masses closer to 1400 MeV, consistent with claims from several phenomenological analyses (Wu, 2009; Gao, 2010; Xie, 2014; Xie, 2017). The findings enable further study of the role of these isovector resonances in photoproduction and weak decay processes.
