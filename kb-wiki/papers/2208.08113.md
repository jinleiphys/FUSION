# Semirelativistic Potential Modelling of Bound States: Advocating Due Rigour

> The Poincaré-covariant quantum-field-theoretic description of bound states by the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation usually exhibits an intrinsic complexity that can be attenuated by allowing this formalism to undergo various simplifications. The resulting approximate outcome's reliability can be assessed by applying several rigorous constraints on the nature of the bound-state spectra; most prominent here are existence, number and location of discrete eigenvalues.

# Semirelativistic Potential Modelling of Bound States: Advocating Due Rigour

## Key claim
The spinless Salpeter equation, a simplified approximation to the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation, can yield reliable bound-state spectra only if rigorous mathematical constraints (boundedness from below, number of discrete eigenstates, upper bounds on eigenvalues, and quality of eigenstates via the relativistic virial theorem) are systematically applied; the paper demonstrates these constraints for generalized Hellmann potentials but does not claim that all such potentials produce finite numbers of bound states.

## Method
The author analyzes the spinless Salpeter Hamiltonian \(H = \sqrt{\mathbf{p}^2 + m_1^2} + \sqrt{\mathbf{p}^2 + m_2^2} + V(\mathbf{x})\) for two scalar bound-state constituents, focusing on the equal-mass case \(\widetilde H = 2\sqrt{\mathbf{p}^2 + m^2} + V(\mathbf{x})\). Four rigorous constraints are applied: boundedness from below (using comparison with the relativistic Coulomb problem), an upper bound on the number of discrete eigenstates (via an integral condition on the potential), upper bounds on eigenvalues (via the minimum-maximum theorem with a Laguerre-polynomial basis), and a relativistic generalization of the virial theorem to assess eigenstate quality. These tools are then applied to the class of generalized Hellmann potentials \(V_{\rm H}(r) = -\kappa/r - \upsilon \exp(-b r)/r\).

## Key numbers
- For the Coulomb potential \(V(\mathbf{x}) = -\alpha/|\mathbf{x}|\), the Hamiltonian \(\widetilde H\) is bounded from below if and only if \(\alpha \le 4/\pi = 1.273239\dots\); within this interval, the spectrum satisfies \(\sigma(\widetilde H) \ge 2m\sqrt{1 - (\pi\alpha/4)^2}\).
- For Coulomb couplings \(\alpha \le 1\), an improved lower bound is \(\sigma(\widetilde H) \ge 2m\sqrt{(1 + \sqrt{1 - \alpha^2})/2}\).
- The number of bound states \(N\) for potentials satisfying \(V(r) \in L^{3/2}(\mathbb{R}^3) \cap L^3(\mathbb{R}^3)\) and \(V(\mathbf{x}) \le 0\) is bounded by \(N \le \frac{C}{12\pi} \int_0^\infty dr\, r^2 [|V(r)|(|V(r)| + 4m)]^{3/2}\), with \(C = 14.107590867\) for \(m > 0\) and \(C = 6.074898097\) for \(m = 0\).
- Binding energies \(E_k - 2m\) (in units of \(m\)) for the spinless relativistic Hellmann problem with \(b = \mu = m\) and trial-space dimensions \(d \gtrsim 29\): for \(\kappa = \upsilon = 1/2\), the ground state (\(n_{\rm r}=0, \ell=0\)) has binding energy \(-0.11673\); for \(\kappa=1, \upsilon=-1\), ground state binding energy is \(-0.17951\); for \(\kappa=1, \upsilon=-2\), ground state binding energy is \(-0.14410\).
- Lower bounds on the spectrum for these three cases are \(-0.58578\dots\), \(-1\), and \(-0.37336\dots\), respectively.

## Context
This work extends the rigorous analysis of semirelativistic bound states initiated by the author and collaborators in prior studies of Woods-Saxon (Lucha 2020), Hulthen (Lucha 2020), Yukawa (Lucha 2019), kink-like (Lucha 2018), and generalized-Hellmann potentials (Lucha 2021, Lucha 2022). The paper systematizes the application of four mathematical constraints (boundedness, finiteness of bound-state number, eigenvalue upper bounds, and the relativistic virial theorem) that were previously developed in works by Herbst (1977), Herbst (1979), and Martin and Roy (1989). The results enable reliable numerical computation of bound-state spectra for any spherically symmetric potential in the spinless Salpeter framework, with the generalized Hellmann class serving as a test case; a key open question is that for Coulomb-like long-range potentials the number of bound states is not guaranteed to be finite, requiring case-by-case analysis.
