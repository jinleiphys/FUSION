# A dispersive analysis of the pion vector form factor and $τ^{-}\to K^{-}K_{S}ν_τ$ decay

> We explore the potential of a combined analysis of the decays $τ^{-}\toπ^{-}π^{0}ν_τ$ and $τ^{-}\to K^{-}K_{S}ν_τ$ in the determination of the $ρ(1450)$ and $ρ(1700)$ resonance properties in the frame of resonance Chiral Theory supplemented by dispersion relations. On the one hand, we take advantage of the very precise data on the modulus squared of the pion vector form factor $|F_{V}^π|^{2}$ obtained by Belle to carry out a very dedicated analysis of the region where these resonances come up into play. Our study provides an improved treatment of the systematic theoretical errors and, as a most important result, we conclude that they dominate over the fit uncertainties in the determination of the $ρ(1450)$ and $ρ(1700)$ pole parameters and tend to be larger than in other determinations quoted in the literature where these errors were ignored or underestimated. The results of our analysis are summarized in numerical tables for the form factor modulus and phase, including both statistical and systematic errors, that can be found as ancillary material of this paper. As a byproduct, we also determine the low-energy observables of the pion vector form factor and the $ρ$-pole position. On the other hand, we benefit from the recent experimental data for the transition $τ^{-}\to K^{-}K_{S}ν_τ$ released by BaBar to perform a first analysis of its decay spectrum and discuss the role of these resonances in this decay. We point out that higher-quality data on the $K^{-}K_{S}$ decay channel will allow compete with the $|F_{V}^π|^{2}$ ones and improve the determination of the $ρ(1450)$ and $ρ(1700)$ resonance parameters as a result of a combined analysis. We hope our study to be of interest for present and future experimental analysis of these decays.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that systematic theoretical errors dominate over statistical fit uncertainties in determining the ρ(1450) and ρ(1700) pole parameters from the pion vector form factor, and that these systematic errors tend to be larger than in other determinations where such errors were ignored or underestimated.

## Method

The analysis uses Resonance Chiral Theory (RChT) supplemented by a three-times-subtracted dispersion relation for the pion vector form factor, with the form factor phase obtained from a model including three vector resonances (ρ(770), ρ(1450), ρ(1700)) matched to the ππ scattering phase shift from Roy equations at 1 GeV. The model parameters are fitted to Belle data on |F_V^π|² from τ⁻→π⁻π⁰ν_τ, and separately to BaBar data on the τ⁻→K⁻K_Sν_τ decay spectrum, with systematic uncertainties assessed by varying the dispersive integral cutoff.

## Key numbers

- ρ(770) pole mass: 762.0(3) MeV, pole width: 143.0(2) MeV (from exponential Omnès fit to Belle data)
- ρ(1450) pole mass: 1366(38) MeV, pole width: 488(48) MeV (from exponential Omnès fit)
- ρ(1700) pole mass: 1718(82) MeV, pole width: 397(88) MeV (from exponential Omnès fit)
- Reference dispersive fit (s_cut = 4 GeV²): M_ρ′ = 1376(6) MeV, Γ_ρ′ = 603(22) MeV, M_ρ″ = 1718(4) MeV, Γ_ρ″ = 465(9) MeV
- χ²/d.o.f. = 0.70 for the reference dispersive fit to Belle data
- BaBar τ⁻→K⁻K_Sν_τ data based on 223741 events, compared to only 100 events from CLEO (1996)

## Context

This work extends the authors' series of dedicated analyses of two-meson tau decays using RChT supplemented by dispersion relations, following prior studies of τ⁻→π⁻π⁰ν_τ (Dumm 2013), τ⁻→K_Sπ⁻ν_τ and τ⁻→K⁻η^(′)ν_τ (Escribano 2014, 2013), and τ⁻→π⁻η^(′)ν_τ (Escribano 2016). The analysis uses the ππ scattering phase shift solution of Roy equations from Garcia-Martin (2011) and provides the first analysis of the BaBar τ⁻→K⁻K_Sν_τ decay spectrum. The results enable improved determination of excited ρ resonance parameters, particularly with future higher-quality data from Belle-II.
