# Hierarchy in mid-rapidity fragmentation: mass, isospin, velocity correlations

> We present new features of the transition from nuclear multifragmentation to neck fragmentation in semi-central heavy-ion collisions at Fermi energies as obtained within a microscopic transport model, Stochastic Mean Field (SMF). We show that along this transition specific hierarchy phenomena of some kinematic observables associated with the intermediate mass fragments develop. Their correlations with the dynamics of isospin degree of freedom, predicted by our calculations, open new possibilities to learn about the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy below saturation, as well as about the fragmentation mechanisms. Detailed results are presented for mass symmetric Sn + Sn reactions with different isospin content at 50A MeV .

## Key claim

The transition from nuclear multifragmentation to neck fragmentation in semi-central heavy-ion collisions at Fermi energies exhibits a specific hierarchy in the transverse velocity of intermediate mass fragments (IMFs) when ordered by mass, and this hierarchy correlates with isospin content in a way that is sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy below saturation, but only for the asysuperstiff equation of state (EOS) where the lightest IMFs show a clear decreasing trend of asymmetry with transverse velocity.

## Method

The authors employed the Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) transport model, a semi-classical microscopic approach based on the Boltzmann-Langevin equation, to simulate mass-symmetric Sn+Sn reactions (112Sn+112Sn, 124Sn+124Sn, 132Sn+132Sn) at 50A MeV with impact parameter b=4 fm. They generated 2000 events per entrance channel and per EOS (asysoft and asysuperstiff), classified events by IMF multiplicity, and analyzed the asymptotic velocities (transverse and parallel components relative to the intrinsic axis defined by the heaviest fragments) and isospin asymmetry of IMFs ordered by mass rank within each event.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 50A MeV for all Sn+Sn reactions studied.
- Impact parameter: b=4 fm (transition region between multifragmentation and neck fragmentation).
- IMF multiplicity: events with three IMFs (total fragments N_F=5, ~550 events out of 2000) and four IMFs (N_F=6, ~250 events out of 2000) were selected for analysis.
- Heaviest IMF charge distribution centered around Z=6-8, reaching up to Z=16-18; lightest IMF charge up to Z=8.
- Interaction time in the transition region: 120-140 fm/c.
- Elliptic flow becomes positive above transverse velocity v_T ≈ 1.4 cm/ns.
- Initial asymmetry I=(N-Z)/(N+Z) for the HH system (124Sn+124Sn): I=0.194.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of fragmentation mechanisms at Fermi energies, including spinodal decomposition in two-component nuclear matter (Colonna 2004, Baran 1998, Baran 2001) and neck fragmentation observed experimentally (De Filippo 2005, Di Toro 2006, Milazzo 2002, Russotto 2010) and predicted by transport models (Baran 2004, Papa 2007). The hierarchy in transverse velocity and its correlation with isospin content provides new observables to constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy below saturation, complementing earlier work on central collisions (Colonna 2008) where N/Z of fragments correlated with kinetic energy. The authors note that recent experimental results from the CHIMERA collaboration for Sn+Ni at 35 AMeV (De Filippo 2010) support the existence of this hierarchy and favor an asystiff-like behavior of the symmetry energy.
