# Effect of isospin degree of freedom on the counterbalancing of collective transverse in-plane flow

> Here we aim to understand the effect of isospin dependence of cross section and Coulomb repulsion on the counterbalancing of collective flow

## Key claim

The isospin dependence of the balance energy (E_bal) arises from a complex interplay between nucleon-nucleon collisions, symmetry energy, and Coulomb repulsion; the abstract's claim that the paper aims to understand these effects glosses over the key finding that Coulomb repulsion dominates over symmetry energy in determining the isospin dependence of E_bal, and that the isospin dependence of the cross section becomes increasingly important at high colliding geometry.

## Method

The study uses the Isospin-dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model to calculate the balance energy (E_bal) for isobaric pairs with N/Z = 1 and 1.4 across a mass range (A = 116, 192, 240). Calculations are performed for both full Coulomb and reduced Coulomb (by a factor of 100) as a function of impact parameter, and for selected cases with the collision term switched off to isolate the roles of Coulomb repulsion, mean field, and nucleon-nucleon collisions.

## Key numbers

- For full Coulomb, at all colliding geometries and masses, the system with higher N/Z (1.4) has larger E_bal than the system with N/Z = 1.
- For N/Z = 1.4, E_bal increases with impact parameter; for N/Z = 1, this increase is true only for lighter mass A = 116, while for heavier masses (A = 192, 240) E_bal begins to decrease with impact parameter.
- When Coulomb is reduced by a factor of 100, neutron-rich systems (N/Z = 1.4) have a decreased E_bal compared to neutron-deficient systems (N/Z = 1) at all colliding geometries throughout the mass range.
- With the collision term switched off, E_bal increases by a large magnitude for both A = 116 and A = 240 at both central and peripheral impact parameters, and the increase is of the same order for both masses.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Gautam (2010) and Pak (1998) on the isospin dependence of collective flow and its disappearance, specifically addressing the counterbalancing of attractive and repulsive interactions. The finding that for heavy masses with N/Z = 1, E_bal decreases with impact parameter contradicts earlier results from Gautam (2010) and Pak (1998). The study enables a clearer separation of the roles of Coulomb repulsion, symmetry energy, and nucleon-nucleon collisions in determining the balance energy, which is important for accessing the isospin dependence of the nuclear equation of state.
