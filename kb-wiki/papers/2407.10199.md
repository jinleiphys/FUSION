# Charge radii of $^{11-16}$C, $^{13-17}$N and $^{15-18}$O determined from their charge-changing cross-sections and the mirror-difference charge radii

> Charge-changing cross-sections of $^{11-16}$C, $^{13-17}$N and $^{15-18}$O on a carbon target have been determined at energies around 300 MeV/nucleon. A nucleon separation energy-dependent correction factor has been introduced to the Glauber model calculation for extracting the nuclear charge radii from the experimental CCCSs. The charge radii of $^{11}$C, $^{13,16}$N and $^{15}$O thus were determined for the first time. With the new radii, we studied the experimental mirror-difference charge radii ($ΔR_{\text {ch}}^{\text {mirror}}$) of $^{11}$B-$^{11}$C, $^{13}$C-$^{13}$N, $^{15}$N-$^{15}$O, $^{17}$N-$^{17}$Ne pairs for the first time. We find that the $ΔR_{\text {ch}}^{\text {mirror}}$ values of $^{13}$C-$^{13}$N and $^{15}$N-$^{15}$O pairs follow well the empirical relation to the isospin asymmetry predicted by the $ab$ $initio$ calculations, while $ΔR_{\text {ch}}^{\text {mirror}}$ of $^{11}$B-$^{11}$C and $^{17}$N-$^{17}$Ne pairs deviate from such relation by more than two standard deviations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the correlation between the ratio of experimental charge-changing cross sections to Glauber model calculations and the nucleon separation energy is robust and independent of incident energy for p-shell nuclei, enabling charge radius extraction; however, the mirror-difference charge radii of the $^{11}$B-$^{11}$C and $^{17}$N-$^{17}$Ne pairs deviate from the empirical linear relation to isospin asymmetry predicted by *ab initio* calculations by more than two standard deviations, while the $^{13}$C-$^{13}$N and $^{15}$N-$^{15}$O pairs follow it well.

## Method

Charge-changing cross sections (CCCS) of $^{11-16}$C, $^{13-17}$N, and $^{15-18}$O on a carbon target were measured at energies around 300 MeV/nucleon using the transmission method at the External Target Facility of HIRFL-CSR. A nucleon separation energy-dependent correction factor was introduced to the zero-range optical-limit approximation Glauber model (ZRGM) calculations to extract point-proton radii from the experimental CCCS, and charge radii were then computed using standard corrections for proton and neutron charge radii and the Darwin-Foldy term.

## Key numbers

- CCCS of $^{11}$C at 319(38) MeV/u: 716(20) mb; extracted charge radius $R_{\text{ch}} = 2.32(11)$ fm (first determination).
- CCCS of $^{13}$N at 310(29) MeV/u: 752(35) mb; extracted $R_{\text{ch}} = 2.37(16)$ fm (first determination).
- CCCS of $^{16}$N at 322(18) MeV/u: 813(9) mb; extracted $R_{\text{ch}} = 2.50(7)$ fm (first determination).
- CCCS of $^{15}$O at 301(24) MeV/u: 880(18) mb; extracted $R_{\text{ch}} = 2.69(9)$ fm (first determination).
- The empirical linear fit to the ratio $\sigma_{\text{cc}}^{\text{exp}}/\sigma_{\text{cc}}^{\text{ZRGM}}$ versus separation energy $S_1$ gives slope $-0.005(1)$ and intercept $1.28(3)$, nearly identical to the 900 MeV/nucleon result.
- The linear relation for mirror-difference charge radii is $\Delta R_{\text{ch}}^{\text{mirror}} = 1.574 \cdot I \pm 0.021$, where $I = (N-Z)/A$.

## Context

This work extends the empirical method for extracting charge radii from CCCS, previously developed by Zhang et al. (2023) at 900 MeV/nucleon, to lower incident energies around 300 MeV/nucleon, demonstrating its energy independence for p-shell nuclei. The new charge radii of $^{11}$C, $^{13,16}$N, and $^{15}$O allow the first experimental study of mirror-difference charge radii for four mirror pairs, testing the linear relation to isospin asymmetry predicted by *ab initio* coupled cluster and auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo calculations (Mirror23). The results enable future tests of shell effects, pairing correlations, and isospin symmetry breaking in nuclear theories, particularly for weakly bound proton-rich nuclei such as $^{17}$Ne.
