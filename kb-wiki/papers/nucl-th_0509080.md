# Resolutions of Several Puzzles at Intermediate $p_T$ and Recent Developments in Correlation

> Some of the puzzles on hadron production at intermediate $p_T$ found at RHIC are explained as natural consequences of parton recombination. In that framework for hadronization the correlation among hadrons produced in jets can be calculated. Some new results on both near-side and away-side jet structures are presented.

## Key claim

The paper argues that several puzzles in hadron production at intermediate transverse momentum (3 < pT < 8 GeV/c) at RHIC, which contradict predictions from the fragmentation model, are natural consequences of the parton recombination model of hadronization; the claim is conditional on the dominance of thermal-shower recombination over fragmentation in this momentum range.

## Method

The author applies the parton recombination model, in which hadrons form by combining thermal partons from the medium with shower partons from hard-scattered partons, to calculate hadron spectra and two-particle correlations in heavy-ion collisions. For away-side jet correlations, the author simulates parton rescattering track-by-track using a Gaussian distribution of scattering angles at discrete points in the dense medium, with the distance between successive scattering points depending on local density and parton momentum. For correlation without a trigger, the author computes the correlation function C2(1,2) and the autocorrelation A(η−, φ−) from the angular distributions of shower partons relative to the jet axis.

## Key numbers

- The p/π ratio in Au+Au collisions exceeds 1 at pT ≈ 3 GeV/c, contradicting fragmentation model predictions.
- In d+Au collisions, R^p_CP > R^π_CP, showing species dependence that contradicts the initial-state-only Cronin effect interpretation.
- The forward-backward asymmetry B/F > 1 for 1 < pT < 5 GeV/c in d+Au collisions, contrary to expectations from transverse broadening.
- The associated particle distributions in central AA and pp collisions differ by a factor of 3 at pT ∼ 1 GeV/c on the near side.
- For away-side correlations, the dip-bump structure appears at Δφ ∼ π ± 1 when trigger momentum is less than 4 GeV/c.
- For correlation in pT, C2 is negative for pT around 2 GeV/c in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, becoming positive but very small above 3 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the recombination model previously applied to the p/π ratio (Hwa and collaborators, 2004) to resolve additional puzzles at RHIC, including the Cronin effect species dependence (Fries and collaborators, 2003), forward-backward asymmetry (Jia, 2004), and associated particle distribution differences (Adler and collaborators, 2005). The approach contradicts fragmentation-based explanations and saturation physics approaches (Jalilian-Marian and collaborators, 2004) for the forward-backward asymmetry. The correlation calculations enable future extraction of the jet cone width σ from autocorrelation data, and the track simulation predicts that the away-side dip-bump structure should transition to a peak at higher trigger momentum, with preliminary evidence from RHIC data (d'Enterria and collaborators, 2005).
