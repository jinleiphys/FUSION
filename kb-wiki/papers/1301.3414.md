# Superdeformed oblate superheavy nuclei in the self-consistent approach

> The HFB self-consistent method has been applied to study the properties of several neutron deficient superheavy nuclei with Z=120-124, N=160-168. Their distinctive feature is the existence of minima of the total HFB energy for strongly deformed, oblate shapes. The self-consistent results agree quite remarkably with those currently obtained by using microscopic-macroscopic method.

# Superdeformed oblate superheavy nuclei in the self-consistent approach

## Key claim

The Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) self-consistent method confirms the existence of strongly deformed, oblate (super-oblate, SDO) global energy minima for several neutron-deficient superheavy nuclei with Z=120-124, N=160-168, agreeing quantitatively with prior microscopic-macroscopic predictions. However, the claim is hedged by the fact that only the N=166 isotope of Z=120 has a global SDO minimum among the Z=120 chain, and the fission half-lives remain uncalculated due to the complexity of two-dimensional tunneling.

## Method

The authors performed HFB calculations using the Skyrme SkM* effective interaction with a volume-plus-surface delta pairing force, employing the HFODD solver. They constrained the quadrupole mass tensor components q20 and q22 to map the total HFB energy as a function of the deformation parameters β and γ, without assuming a pre-defined family of nuclear shapes. The calculations covered isotopes with Z=120-124 and N=160-168, and the resulting potential energy surfaces were compared to the microscopic-macroscopic results of Jachimowicz et al. (2011).

## Key numbers

- For nuclei in the region Z=120-126, N=160-168, the SDO minimum occurs at β ~ 0.4, γ = 60°, corresponding to an ellipsoidal axis ratio of ~ 3:2.
- For the Z=120 chain, only the N=166 isotope (286120) has a global super-oblate minimum.
- For Z=122 isotopes, the three lightest (N=162-166) exhibit large oblate deformation; the minimum shifts to spherical starting at N=168.
- For Z=124 isotopes, the transition from SDO to normal oblate occurs at N=170.
- Typical Qα values in the region are around 15 MeV, giving α-decay half-lives T1/2,α around 10^{-9} s.
- For the hindered α-decay from 286120 to 282118, the Qα is 13.6 MeV, yielding T1/2,α = 10^{-5.84} s.
- For 286120, single-particle states near the Fermi level include: neutrons with Ω = 15/2+ at -0.56 MeV and 9/2- at 0.75 MeV; protons with Ω = 13/2+ at -0.66 MeV and 7/2- at 0.42 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent treatment of superheavy nuclei beyond the extensive study by Staszczak et al. (2012), which did not cover this neutron-deficient region, and confirms the SDO minima first hinted at by Cwiok et al. (1996) in the micro-macro model but not previously confirmed by self-consistent calculations. The results enable future work on fission half-lives (requiring two-dimensional tunneling calculations with full mass tensors) and on odd-mass systems, and support the possibility that certain nuclei in this region, particularly 286120, may have α-decay half-lives long enough to be within experimental reach, as proposed by Jachimowicz et al. (2011).
