# N K and Delta K states in the chiral SU(3) quark model

> The isospin I=0 and I=1 kaon-nucleon $S$, $P$, $D$, $F$ wave phase shifts are studied in the chiral SU(3) quark model by solving the resonating group method (RGM) equation. The calculated phase shifts for different partial waves are in agreement with the experimental data. Furthermore, the structures of the $ΔK$ states with L=0, I=1 and I=2 are investigated. We find that the interaction between $Δ$ and $K$ in the case of L=0, I=1 is attractive, which is not like the situation of the $NK$ system, where the $S$-wave interactions between $N$ and $K$ for both I=0 and I=1 are repulsive. Our numerical results also show that when the model parameters are taken to be the same as in our previous $NN$ and $YN$ scattering calculations, the $ΔK$ state with L=0 and I=1 is a weakly bound state with about 2 MeV binding energy, while the one with I=2 is unbound in the present one-channel calculation.

## Key claim

The chiral SU(3) quark model, with parameters taken from previous nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon scattering calculations, predicts a weakly bound Delta-K state with L=0 and I=1 having about 2 MeV binding energy in a one-channel calculation; however, when parameters are fitted to kaon-nucleon phase shifts instead, the same state becomes unbound, indicating the result is parameter-dependent.

## Method

The authors solved the resonating group method (RGM) equation for the kaon-nucleon (KN) system to compute S, P, D, and F wave phase shifts for isospin I=0 and I=1, using the chiral SU(3) quark model with one-gluon-exchange, confinement, and chiral-field-induced potentials. They then applied the same RGM framework to study the Delta-K system with L=0 for I=1 and I=2, analyzing the one-gluon-exchange contributions via generator coordinate method (GCM) matrix elements and solving the bound-state RGM equation. Two sets of model parameters were used: one fitted to KN phase shifts and another fitted to NN phase shifts and YN cross sections from prior work.

## Key numbers

- The up (down) quark mass is taken as 313 MeV, the strange quark mass as 470 MeV, and the harmonic-oscillator width parameter b_u as 0.5 fm.
- The sigma meson mass is set to 675 MeV for the KN case, compared to 595 MeV used in previous NN and YN calculations.
- The Delta-K state with L=0 and I=1 has a binding energy of about 2 MeV when using parameters from NN and YN scattering calculations.
- The cutoff mass Lambda for the chiral field is 1100 MeV.
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant squared over 4 pi is taken as 13.67 from experiment.

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) quark model, previously successful for NN and YN systems (Zhang et al. 1997; Li et al. 2003), to the KN system and to the Delta-K five-quark system containing an antiquark. It improves upon prior RGM calculations by Lemaire et al. (2003) by obtaining correct signs for several partial-wave phase shifts (S01, P11, P03, D13, D05, F15, F07) and better magnitudes for P01, D03, and D15 channels. The Delta-K bound state result contrasts with the resonance state near threshold found by Sarkar et al. (2004) in a baryon-level calculation, and the authors note that future work should include channel coupling between Delta-K and NK* states to determine whether the state becomes a resonance or bound state.
