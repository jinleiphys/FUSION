# Correlations and the ridge in the Color Glass Condensate beyond the glasma graph approximation

> We consider two-gluon production in dilute-dense collisions within the Color Glass Condensate framework, applicable to both proton-nucleus and heavy-light ion collisions. We go beyond the glasma graph approximation which is valid in the dilute-dilute limit and show the correspondence between the glasma graphs and the $k_T$-factorized approach that we use in our calculation. We then identify the classical uncorrelated, and the Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) and Bose enhancement correlated contributions, with the Bose enhancement contribution being suppressed by the number of degrees of freedom with respect to the uncorrelated piece. We show that both the HBT and the Bose enhancement pieces survive the inclusion of higher order contributions in density and that they stem from the quadrupole piece of the two-gluon inclusive cross section. Finally, we illustrate the results using a toy model that allows a simple numerical implementation.

## Key claim

The Bose enhancement and Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) correlations, previously identified in the glasma graph approximation valid for dilute-dilute collisions, survive the inclusion of higher-order density corrections in the dilute-dense limit; somewhat unexpectedly, these correlated contributions stem from the quadrupole piece of the two-gluon inclusive cross section, not from the double-dipole term.

## Method

The authors compute the two-gluon production cross section in dilute-dense collisions within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework using a \(k_T\)-factorized formalism, which goes beyond the glasma graph approximation (valid only in the dilute-dilute limit). They isolate the classical uncorrelated contribution by taking the large-\(N_c\) limit, and then factorize the quadrupole operator into products of dipole operators to identify the HBT and Bose enhancement contributions. The results are illustrated using a toy model with a translationally invariant target that allows a simple numerical implementation.

## Key numbers

* The Bose enhancement contribution is suppressed by a factor of \(1/(N_c^2-1)\) relative to the classical uncorrelated piece (see Eq. (1) and surrounding text).
* The HBT contribution is enhanced relative to the Bose-enhanced term by a factor proportional to the number of sources \(S_{\perp,1} Q_{s,1}^2\), arising from a \(\delta(\vec k_1 - \vec k_2) + \delta(\vec k_1 + \vec k_2)\) structure in the translationally invariant limit (Eq. (HBTtrans)).
* The double-dipole contribution to the Bose enhancement terms is suppressed by a relative factor of \(1/(N_c^2-1)\) compared to the quadrupole contribution (stated in the Introduction and Section 3).

## Context

This work extends the glasma graph approach of Armesto (2006), Dumitru (2008), and Dumitru (2010), which successfully described the ridge in pp collisions via Bose enhancement, to the dilute-dense regime relevant for proton-nucleus and heavy-light ion collisions. It builds on the \(k_T\)-factorized formalism of Kovchegov (2012) and Kovchegov (2013), and on the identification of Bose enhancement and HBT correlations in the dilute-dilute limit by Altinoluk (2015). The results enable a systematic treatment of azimuthal correlations in small collision systems (pp, pPb, dAu, \(^3\)HeAu) beyond the dilute approximation, and clarify which color ensembles in the target dominate the correlated signal.
