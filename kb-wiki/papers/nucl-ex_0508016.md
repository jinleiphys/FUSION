# Experimental study of 113Cd beta decay using CdZnTe detectors

> A search for the 4-fold forbidden beta decay of \iso{Cd}{113} has been performed with CdZnTe semiconductors. With 0.86 kg $\cdot$ days of statistics a half-life for the decay of $T_{1/2} = (8.2 \pm 0.2 (stat.) ^{+0.2}_{-1.0} (sys.)) \cdot 10^{15}$yrs has been obtained. This is in good agreement with published values. A comparison of the spectral shape with the one given on the Table of Isotopes Web-page shows a severe deviation.

# Experimental study of 113Cd beta decay using CdZnTe detectors

## Key claim
The 4-fold forbidden non-unique beta decay of 113Cd has a half-life of (8.2 ± 0.2 (stat.) +0.2/-1.0 (sys.)) × 10^15 years, in good agreement with prior measurements, but the spectral shape from the Table of Isotopes Web-page shows a severe deviation from the observed data, indicating that the correct spectral shape for this decay remains uncertain.

## Method
Four 1 cm^3 CdZnTe semiconductor detectors with coplanar grid technology were operated at the Gran Sasso Underground Laboratory (3500 mwe shielding) for 4781 hours. After data cleaning, three detectors contributed 0.86 kg·days of statistics. A model spectrum using a polynomial correction function S(E) based on the work of Danevich (1996) was folded with detector efficiency from GEANT4 simulations and energy resolution, then normalized to the background-subtracted summed spectrum in the 120–310 keV fit interval.

## Key numbers
- Half-life: T1/2 = (8.2 ± 0.2 (stat.) +0.2/-1.0 (sys.)) × 10^15 years
- Individual detector half-lives (statistical only): Detector 1: (8.1 ± 0.2) × 10^15 years (χ²/ndf = 117.2/169); Detector 3: (7.5 ± 0.3) × 10^15 years (χ²/ndf = 160.4/169); Detector 4: (9.3 ± 0.4) × 10^15 years (χ²/ndf = 318.1/169)
- Total statistics: 0.86 kg·days; fit interval contains about 37000 events, of which 12.6% are background
- Endpoint energy: Q = 320 ± 3 keV from atomic masses (Audi 2003)
- Energy resolution (FWHM): ΔE (keV) = 4.7% × E (keV) + 28 keV, averaged over detectors
- Uncertainty in 113Cd content due to Zn admixture (7–11%): 1.8%

## Context
This work extends prior half-life measurements of 113Cd using CdWO4 as a scintillator by Danevich (1996) (T1/2 = (7.7 ± 0.3) × 10^15 years) and as a cryogenic bolometer by Alessandrello (1994) (T1/2 = (9.0 ± 0.5 (stat.) ± 1 (sys.)) × 10^15 years), using an independent experimental approach with room-temperature semiconductor detectors. The measurement was performed as part of test measurements for the COBRA double beta decay experiment (Zuber 2001). The severe discrepancy between the observed spectral shape and the Table of Isotopes Web-page spectrum motivates future measurements with 64 detectors and lower noise thresholds to resolve the spectral shape uncertainty down to at least 50 keV.
