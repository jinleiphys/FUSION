# Neutrino Oscillations

> This review summarizes recent experimental and theoretical progress in determining neutrino mixing angles and masses through neutrino oscillations. We describe the basic physics of oscillation phenomena in vacuum and matter, as well as the status of solar, reactor, atmospheric, and accelerator neutrino experiments that probe these phenomena. The results from current global analyses of neutrino parameters are given. Future efforts that may improve the precision with which these parameters are known or probe new aspects of the neutrino mixing matrix are described.

## Key claim

This review summarizes the experimental and theoretical status of neutrino oscillations, concluding that the three-flavor mixing framework is well-established but that several key parameters, the mass hierarchy, the Dirac CP-violating phase, and the possible existence of sterile neutrinos, remain undetermined and will require future experiments.

## Method

The authors review the formalism of neutrino mixing in vacuum and matter, including the MSW effect, and present the current status of solar (Homestake, Kamiokande, SAGE, GALLEX/GNO, Super-Kamiokande, SNO, Borexino), reactor (KamLAND), atmospheric, and accelerator neutrino experiments. They describe how global analyses combine data from these experiments to extract the neutrino mixing angles and mass-squared differences, and discuss the prospects for future measurements of the remaining unknown parameters.

## Context

This review extends the work of Bahcall and Davis (1964) on solar neutrino detection and the subsequent discovery of the solar neutrino deficit, which was definitively resolved by SNO (Ahmad 2001, Aharmim 2012) showing that two-thirds of solar neutrinos arrive at Earth as heavy-flavor neutrinos. The review builds on the MSW effect (Wolfenstein 1977, Mikheyev and Smirnov 1986) and the KamLAND reactor experiment (Abe 2008) that eliminated the LOW solution. The results enable future experiments aimed at determining the neutrino mass hierarchy, the Dirac CP phase δ_CP, and searching for sterile neutrinos, as well as non-oscillation probes of absolute neutrino mass and Majorana nature.
