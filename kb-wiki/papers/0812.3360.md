# Eta meson rescattering effects in the p + 6Li --> eta + 7Be reaction near threshold

> The p + 6Li --> eta + 7Be reaction has been investigated with an emphasis on the eta meson and 7Be interaction in the final state. Considering the 6Li and 7Be nuclei to be alpha-d and alpha-3He clusters respectively, the reaction is modelled to proceed via the p + d [alpha] --> 3He [α] + eta reaction with the alpha remaining a spectator. The eta meson interacts with 7Be via multiple scatterings on the 3He and alpha clusters inside 7Be. The individual eta-3He and eta-alpha scatterings are evaluated using few body equations for the eta-3N and eta-4N systems with a coupled channel eta-N interaction as an input. Calculations including four low-lying states of 7Be lead to a double hump structure in the total cross section corresponding to the $L = 1 (J = (1/2)^-, (3/2)^-)$ and $L = 3 (J = (5/2)^-, (7/2)^-)$ angular momentum states. The humps arise due to the off-shell rescattering of the eta meson on the 7Be nucleus in the final state.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the total cross section for the p + 6Li → η + 7Be reaction near threshold exhibits a double hump structure arising specifically from the off-shell rescattering of the eta meson on the 7Be nucleus in the final state, rather than from on-shell rescattering or plane-wave production alone.

## Method

The authors model 6Li as an alpha-deuteron cluster and 7Be as an alpha-3He cluster, so the reaction proceeds via p + d → 3He + η with the alpha as a spectator. The η-7Be final state interaction is treated as a three-body problem of η, 3He, and 4He, where the individual η-3He and η-4He t-matrices are evaluated using few-body equations with a coupled-channel η-N interaction as input. The production amplitude is written in terms of the p + d → 3He + η process, and the total cross sections are calculated for four low-lying states of 7Be (J = 1/2−, 3/2−, 5/2−, 7/2−) using two different models of the elementary η-N t-matrix and two types of nuclear wave functions (Woods-Saxon and Green's function Monte Carlo with Urbana potential).

## Key numbers

- The η-N scattering lengths used in Model A are aηN = (0.75, 0.27) fm and (0.88, 0.41) fm; in Model B they are (0.88, 0.25) fm, (0.77, 0.25) fm, (0.51, 0.26) fm, and (0.4, 0.3) fm.
- The proton beam energies studied range from 658.8 MeV (near threshold for the ground state) to 683 MeV, corresponding to excess energies up to about 20 MeV above threshold.
- At an excess energy of 19 MeV, the theoretical prediction for the ground state angular distribution is around 0.2 nb/sr, compared to the Turin group data point of 4.6 ± 3.8 nb/sr.
- The summed cross section over all four J states at 19 MeV excess energy with FSI is 0.51 nb/sr.
- The double hump structure in the total cross section corresponds to L = 1 (J = 1/2−, 3/2−) and L = 3 (J = 5/2−, 7/2−) angular momentum states of 7Be.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous theoretical studies of η production in p + d → p + d + η and p + d → 3He + η reactions (Upadhyay, Kelkar, and Jain, earlier works) and their η-nucleus interaction studies with deuteron, 3He, and 4He. It was motivated by measurements of the p + 6Li → η + 7Be reaction by the Turin group in 1993 at 683 MeV and a recent proposal to study this reaction at COSY, Julich at 673 MeV. The paper contrasts with an earlier theoretical study by Wilkin (1993) which neglected final state interactions of the meson with the nucleus. The results enable future experimental studies of this reaction to test the predicted double hump structure and the role of off-shell η rescattering in light nuclear systems.
