# Transverse momentum structure of pair correlations as a signature of collective behavior in small collision systems

> We perform 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic calculations of proton-lead and lead-lead collisions at top LHC energy. We show that existing data from high-multiplicity p-Pb events can be well described in hydrodynamics, suggesting that collective flow is plausible as a correct description of these collisions. However, a more stringent test of the presence of hydrodynamic behavior can be made by studying the detailed momentum dependence of two-particle correlations. We define a relevant observable, $r_n$, and make predictions for its value and centrality dependence if hydrodynamics is a valid description. This will provide a non-trivial confirmation of the nature of the correlations seen in small collision systems, and potentially to determine where the hydrodynamic description, if valid anywhere, stops being valid. Lastly, we probe what can be learned from this observable, finding that it is insensitive to viscosity, but sensitive to aspects of the initial state of the system that other observables are insensitive to, such as the transverse length scale of the fluctuations in the initial stages of the collision.

## Key claim

Existing data from high-multiplicity p-Pb collisions can be well described by 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics, making collective flow a plausible explanation; however, a more stringent test requires measuring the full transverse-momentum dependence of two-particle correlations via the observable \(r_n\), whose predicted value and centrality dependence can confirm or rule out hydrodynamic behavior and potentially identify where the hydrodynamic description ceases to be valid.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic calculations using the MUSIC version 2.0 solver for p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV and Pb-Pb collisions at top LHC energy, with thermalization time \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\), freeze-out temperature \(T_{\rm freeze} = 150\) MeV, and equation of state s95p-v1. Initial conditions were generated with a modified Monte Carlo Glauber model that includes negative binomial fluctuations in the entropy per participant to reproduce the observed multiplicity distribution tail, and the transverse granularity parameter \(\sigma\) was varied between 0.4 fm and 0.8 fm. They computed single-particle observables (multiplicity, mean \(p_T\), \(v_2\{2\}\), \(v_3\{2\}\), \(v_2\{4\}\)) and defined the ratio \(r_n = V_{n\Delta}(p_T^a, p_T^b) / \sqrt{V_{n\Delta}(p_T^a, p_T^a) V_{n\Delta}(p_T^b, p_T^b)}\) from the two-particle correlation matrix to test hydrodynamic predictions.

## Key numbers

- Thermalization time \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\) and freeze-out temperature \(T_{\rm freeze} = 150\) MeV used in all calculations.
- Transverse Gaussian width \(\sigma\) varied between 0.4 fm and 0.8 fm to study granularity effects.
- Negative binomial parameters \(\lambda = 5.11\) and \(\kappa = 0.62\) chosen to fit p-p and p-Pb multiplicity distributions.
- Beam rapidity \(y_{\rm beam} = 8.58\) and rapidity shift of 0.465 applied for p-Pb lab frame.
- Most central p-Pb bin (fraction \(6 \times 10^{-5}\) of events) yields \(\langle N_{\rm ch} \rangle = 280\) matched to CMS data.
- For the parameter set \(\sigma = 0.4\) fm and \(\eta/s = 0.08\), \(r_2\) and \(r_3\) are predicted to be close to 1 in highest-multiplicity p-Pb collisions, decreasing monotonically with decreasing multiplicity.
- In 40–50% centrality Pb-Pb collisions, adding NBD fluctuations and reducing \(\sigma\) to 0.4 fm lowers \(r_2\) and \(r_3\) away from measured ALICE data, indicating tension with a simultaneous fit.

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic calculations for small systems by Bozek (2011, 2012, 2013), Bzdak (2013), Qin (2013), and Schenke (2014), and builds on the formalism of Gardim (2012) who introduced the \(r_n\) ratio for heavy-ion collisions. The authors show that while existing p-Pb data (CMS, ATLAS, ALICE) can be described hydrodynamically, the new observable \(r_n\) provides a non-trivial test that can rule out hydrodynamics if violated. The finding that \(r_n\) is insensitive to shear viscosity but sensitive to the transverse length scale of initial-state fluctuations (parameterized by \(\sigma\)) enables future studies to probe the granularity of the initial state in both p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions, potentially resolving tensions between models that fit different data sets.
