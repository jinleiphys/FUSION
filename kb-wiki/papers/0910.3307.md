# On the two-boson exchange corrections to parity-violating elastic electron-proton scattering

> The details of the calculation of the two-boson exchange effects in the parity-violating elastic $ep$ scattering within a simple hadronic model, including both the nucleon and $Δ(1232)$-resonance intermediate states, are presented. We examine the sensitivity of our results with respect to choice of form factors. We emphasize the importance to use correct relations relating N to $Δ$ and $Δ$ to N transition vertex functions. The $NΔ$ Coulomb quadrupole transition is found to play important role at higher $Q^2\geq 3.0$ GeV$^2$. We also elucidate the relation between our results and the well-known result on the $γZE$ effect given by Marciano and Sirlin (MS). The effect of the nucleon contribution $δ_N$ to parity-asymmetry $A_{PV}$, is found to be in general, larger than the corresponding $Δ$ contribution $δ_Δ$ except at extreme forward angles. The corrections to the extracted values of the strange form factors $G^{s}_{E}+βG^{s}_{M}$ from the HAPPEX, A4, and G0 data are also presented. The total TBE corrections to the extracted values of $G^{s}_{E}+βG^{s}_{M}$ in recent experiments of HAPPEX G0, and A4 are, depending on kinematics, found to be small except in a few cases where they range from -20.6% to $48.3 %$.

## Key claim

The two-boson exchange (TBE) corrections to the parity-violating asymmetry \(A_{PV}\) in elastic electron-proton scattering, computed in a hadronic model including both nucleon and \(\Delta(1232)\) intermediate states, are found to be small for the extraction of strange form factors \(G^{s}_{E}+\beta G^{s}_{M}\) from HAPPEX, G0, and A4 data in most kinematics, but can range from \(-20.6\%\) to \(48.3\%\) in a few cases, depending on the choice of form factors and the correct vertex relations for \(N\rightarrow\Delta\) and \(\Delta\rightarrow N\) transitions.

## Method

The authors computed the two-photon exchange (\(2\gamma E\)) and \(\gamma Z\)-exchange (\(\gamma ZE\)) box and cross-box diagrams for parity-violating elastic \(ep\) scattering using a simple hadronic model with nucleon and \(\Delta(1232)\) intermediate states. They employed FeynCalc and LoopTools for analytical and numerical evaluation, regulated infrared divergences by subtracting the soft-photon limit, and used two sets of form factors (set A with dipole forms and set B with more realistic high-\(Q^2\) behavior) to examine sensitivity. The \(N\Delta\) Coulomb quadrupole transition was included, and the correct Hermitian vertex relation \(\Gamma_{\Delta\rightarrow N}(p,q)=-\gamma_{0}[\Gamma_{N\rightarrow\Delta}(p,-q)]^{\dagger}\gamma_{0}\) was enforced.

## Key numbers

- The total TBE corrections to the extracted values of \(G^{s}_{E}+\beta G^{s}_{M}\) from HAPPEX, G0, and A4 data range from \(-20.6\%\) to \(48.3\%\) in a few kinematics, but are otherwise small.
- The \(N\Delta\) Coulomb quadrupole transition plays an important role at higher \(Q^{2}\geq 3.0\) GeV\(^{2}\).
- The nucleon contribution \(\delta_N\) to the parity-asymmetry \(A_{PV}\) is generally larger than the \(\Delta\) contribution \(\delta_\Delta\), except at extreme forward angles.
- The dipole parameters used are \(\Lambda_{1}=0.84\) GeV and \(\Lambda_{2}=1.0\) GeV for the nucleon form factors.
- The \(\gamma N\Delta\) transition coupling constants are \(g_{1}=1.91\), \(g_{2}=2.63\), and \(g_{3}=1.57\), derived from \(G_{M}(0)=3.02\), \(G_{E}(0)=0.0755\), and \(G_{C}(0)=1.1496\).
- The weak \(ZN\Delta\) vector couplings are \(\tilde{g}_{1}=0.47\), \(\tilde{g}_{2}=0.658\), \(\tilde{g}_{3}=0.40\).
- The axial \(ZN\Delta\) couplings are \(h_{1}=-0.263\), \(h_{4}=-0.458\), with \(h_{2}=0\) and \(h_{3}=0\).

## Context

This work extends earlier hadronic calculations of TBE corrections to parity-violating \(ep\) scattering that included only nucleon intermediate states (Zhou 2007, Tjon 2008) by adding the \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance, which is known to dominate low-energy hadron physics (Pascalutsa 2007). It clarifies discrepancies with the calculations of Tjon (2009) by emphasizing the correct Hermitian vertex relation for \(N\leftrightarrow\Delta\) transitions and the role of the Coulomb quadrupole excitation. The results recover the well-known \(\gamma ZE\) effect of Marciano and Sirlin (1984) in the \(Q^{2}=0\) limit, and provide corrections needed for reliable extraction of strange form factors from the HAPPEX, G0, and A4 experiments.
