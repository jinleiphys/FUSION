# Probing the N = 32 shell closure below the magic proton number Z = 20: Mass measurements of the exotic isotopes 52,53K

> The recently confirmed neutron-shell closure at N = 32 has been investigated for the first time below the magic proton number Z = 20 with mass measurements of the exotic isotopes 52,53K, the latter being the shortest-lived nuclide investigated at the online mass spectrometer ISOLTRAP. The resulting two-neutron separation energies reveal a 3 MeV shell gap at N = 32, slightly lower than for 52Ca, highlighting the doubly-magic nature of this nuclide. Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Boguliubov and ab initio Gorkov-Green function calculations are challenged by the new measurements but reproduce qualitatively the observed shell effect.

## Key claim

The N=32 shell closure, previously confirmed in calcium (Z=20), is shown to exist below the magic proton number for the first time via mass measurements of 52,53K, but the empirical shell gap is about 1 MeV weaker than for calcium, indicating the stabilizing effect of the Z=20 proton core.

## Method

Mass measurements of the exotic isotopes 52K and 53K were performed using the multi-reflection time-of-flight mass separator (MR-ToF MS) at ISOLTRAP at ISOLDE/CERN. The nuclides were produced by 1.4-GeV proton impact on a uranium carbide target, surface ionized, and transported to the MR-ToF MS where time-of-flight spectra were collected and calibrated against stable 39K and 52,53Cr reference ions. The resulting mass-excess values were used to compute two-neutron separation energies, which were compared to Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations and ab initio Gorkov-Green function (GGF) calculations.

## Key numbers

- Mass excess of 52K: -17138(33) keV
- Mass excess of 53K: -12298(112) keV
- Two-neutron separation energy drop from 51K to 53K: about 3 MeV, compared to only about 1 MeV from 49K to 51K
- Half-life of 52K: 110(4) ms; half-life of 53K: 30(5) ms, the latter being the shortest-lived nuclide ever investigated with ISOLTRAP
- GGF calculations yield binding energies of 439.52(0.71) MeV for 51K and 443.31(0.85) MeV for 53K, showing overbinding of about 0.7 MeV/A
- The empirical two-neutron shell gap at N=32 for potassium is about 1 MeV lower than for calcium (Z=20)

## Context

This work extends the investigation of the N=32 shell closure, previously established for calcium by Wienholtz (2013) who found a 4 MeV drop in S2n between 52Ca and 54Ca, to the potassium isotopic chain below Z=20. The measurements challenge both Skyrme-HFB calculations (which depend sensitively on pairing strength and tensor terms) and ab initio GGF calculations (which qualitatively reproduce the shell effect but overestimate the N=32 gap). The results enable further tests of chiral interactions and ab initio methods, and motivate new precision mass measurements of scandium isotopes to resolve ambiguity in the S2n values near N=32.
