# Operator Product Expansion and Conservation Laws in Non-Relativistic Conformal Field Theories

> We explore the consequences of conformal symmetry for the operator product expansions in nonrelativistic field theories. Similar to the relativistic case, the OPE coefficients of descendants are related to that of the primary. However, unlike relativistic CFTs the 3-point function of primaries is not completely specified by conformal symmetry. Here, we show that the 3-point function between operators with nonzero particle number, where (at least) one operator has the lowest dimension allowed by unitarity, is determined up to a numerical coefficient. We also look at the structure of the family tree of primaries with zero particle number and discuss the presence of conservation laws in this sector.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in non-relativistic conformal field theories, the operator product expansion (OPE) coefficient for a primary operator with scaling dimension equal to the unitarity bound \(d/2\) is determined up to a numerical coefficient, except when another operator in the OPE has zero particle number, in which case not all coefficients are fixed by symmetry.

## Method

The authors use the Schrodinger algebra to derive constraints on OPE coefficients by commuting the OPE expansion with the generators \(K_i\) (Galilean boost) and \(C\) (special Schrodinger transformation). For the case where one operator has scaling dimension \(d/2\), the resulting equations become a constraint on the primary coefficient \(C_0\) rather than determining descendant coefficients, yielding a partial differential equation that reduces to an ordinary differential equation for the function \(f(x^2/t)\). The authors also analyze the zero-particle-number sector by constructing "alien" operators that are not descendants of the primary but appear in the family tree due to the commutation of ladder operators \(K_i\) and \(P_j\).

## Key numbers

* The unitarity bound on operator dimensions is \(d/2\), where \(d\) is the spatial dimension.
* The scaling dimension of the elementary fermion field \(\psi\) and its Hermitian conjugate \(\psi^\dagger\) is \(d/2\).
* The OPE coefficient \(C_0\) takes the form \(C_0(t,x) = t^{-\frac12 \Delta_{23,1}} f(x^2/t)\), where \(\Delta_{23,1} = \Delta_2 + \Delta_3 - \Delta_1\).
* The solution for \(f\) involves the confluent hypergeometric function \(U(a,b,x)\) and generalized Laguerre polynomial \(L_a^b(x)\) with parameter \(\alpha = \frac12 (\Delta_2 - \Delta_3 - \frac{d}{2})\).
* The null operator at level \(l\) in the zero-particle-number sector is \(O^{(l)} = \partial_t C^{(l)}_{i_1\cdots i_l} + \partial_j C^{(l+1)}_{j i_1\cdots i_l}\), with \(O^{(1)} = O^{(2)} = 0\) corresponding to continuity and energy conservation.

## Context

This work extends the study of operator product expansions in non-relativistic conformal field theories, which were previously explored by Mehen, Stewart, and Wise (1999) for scattering amplitudes of unitary fermions, and by Henkel (1993, 2003) for correlation functions. The paper builds on the Schrodinger algebra first found by Hagen (1972) and Niederer (1972), and addresses the incomplete understanding of operator structure in these theories. The results enable the computation of three-point functions involving operators at the unitarity bound, and provide a framework for identifying conservation laws in the zero-particle-number sector, connecting to the infinite conservation laws found in the free theory by Moroz (2011).
