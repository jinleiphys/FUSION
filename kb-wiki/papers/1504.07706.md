# Temperature dependence of meson screening masses; a comparison of effective model with lattice QCD

> Temperature dependence of pion and sigma-meson screening masses is evaluated by the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with the entanglement vertex (EPNJL model). We propose a practical way of calculating meson screening masses in the NJL-type effective models. The method based on the Pauli-Villars regularization solves the well-known difficulty that the evaluation of screening masses is not easy in the NJL-type effective models. The method is applied to analyze temperature dependence of pion screening masses calculated with state-of-the-art lattice simulations with success in reproducing the lattice QCD results. We predict the temperature dependence of pole mass by using EPNJL model.

# Temperature dependence of meson screening masses; a comparison of effective model with lattice QCD

## Key claim

The Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with the entanglement vertex (EPNJL model) can reproduce lattice QCD results for pion screening masses when the entanglement parameters are set to alpha_1 = alpha_2 = 0.31, but only after rescaling the lattice data by a factor of 154/196 to account for the difference in chiral transition temperature between the 2+1 flavor lattice simulation and the two-flavor effective model.

## Method

The authors compute meson screening masses in the EPNJL model using a new formalism based on Pauli-Villars regularization that avoids the numerical difficulties of previous approaches. Instead of performing the Fourier transform of the momentum-space polarization function directly, they evaluate the one-loop polarization function I_3 by integrating over momentum before taking the Matsubara sum, yielding an infinite series of analytic functions with cuts only on the imaginary axis. The screening mass is then determined from the location of the pole in the complex-q̃ plane, provided the screening mass lies below the threshold mass 2M_{i=1,l=0,alpha=0}.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant f_pi = 93.3 MeV and pion mass M_pi = 138 MeV reproduced at vacuum with parameters m_0 = 6.3 MeV, G_s = 5.0 GeV^{-2}, and Lambda = 0.768 GeV
- After refitting to rescaled lattice data: m_0 = 10.3 MeV and T_0 = 156 MeV, yielding M_pi = 175 MeV at vacuum and T_c = 154 +/- 9 MeV
- Chiral transition temperature T_c = 196 MeV in the original lattice simulations of Cheng (2010), rescaled to T_c = 154 +/- 9 MeV using factor 154/196
- Entanglement parameters alpha_1 = alpha_2 = 0.31 produce the best fit to lattice screening mass data
- Pion and sigma-meson screening masses become equal at T_c = 154 MeV, indicating chiral restoration
- Above T_c, screening masses approach the threshold mass and ultimately 2 pi T

## Context

This work extends the PNJL model framework of Hansen and the earlier NJL screening mass calculations of Florkowski by introducing a practical method to compute screening masses that avoids the numerical difficulties of contour integration in the complex-q̃ plane. The method is applied to analyze the lattice QCD screening mass results of Cheng (2010) for 2+1 flavor QCD, using a two-flavor EPNJL model. The authors show that the entanglement interaction (the dependence of the four-quark coupling on the Polyakov loop) is essential to reproduce lattice data, as the standard PNJL model without entanglement significantly underestimates the screening masses. This enables future predictions of pole masses from lattice screening mass results using the EPNJL model, particularly at temperatures above T_c where the difference between pole and screening masses becomes significant.
