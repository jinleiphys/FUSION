# A qubit model for U(1) lattice gauge theory

> A conceptually simple model for strongly interacting compact U(1) lattice gauge theory is expressed as operators acting on qubits. The number of independent gauge links is reduced to its minimum through the use of Gauss's law. The model can be implemented with any number of qubits per gauge link, and a choice as small as two is shown to be useful. Real-time propagation and real-time collisions are observed on lattices in two spatial dimensions. The extension to three spatial dimensions is also developed, and a first look at 3-dimensional real-time dynamics is presented.

# A qubit model for U(1) lattice gauge theory

## Key claim

The authors present a qubit model for compact U(1) lattice gauge theory in the strong coupling region, where truncation to as few as two qubits per gauge link is shown to be useful; the model is meant to represent the theory in the strong coupling region, not the full theory at all couplings.

## Method

The Hamiltonian for compact U(1) gauge theory is expressed in terms of electric field operators and plaquette operators, then Gauss's law is used to eliminate redundant gauge link degrees of freedom, leaving one independent link per plaquette in planar lattices. The remaining operators are mapped to Pauli operators acting on qubit registers, with truncation of the electric field basis to a finite set of eigenvalues (e.g., -1, 0, 1, 2 for two qubits). Time evolution is computed using the second-order Suzuki-Trotter formula on classical simulators.

## Key numbers

- Two qubits per gauge link (basis states for electric field eigenvalues -1, 0, 1, 2) are shown to be useful; three qubits (eigenvalues -3 to 4) provide a check.
- For a single plaquette at beta^2=1, the ground state eigenvalue from matrix diagonalization is compared to variational quantum eigensolver results with error bars from quantum measurement uncertainties.
- On a 2x8 lattice (row of 7 plaquettes) at beta^2=1, the sum of probabilities from the seven curves deviates from unity by roughly 5 percent.
- On a 2x8 lattice, the transit time for a single excitation is compared to the transit time for two colliding excitations; at beta^2=1 the collision reduces transit time (attractive interaction), while at beta^2=3 the interaction becomes slightly repulsive.
- For a single cube (2^3 lattice) at beta^2=0, the first excited state has eigenvalue 4 and is 12-fold degenerate; the next state has eigenvalue 6.

## Context

This work extends the qubit-based approach to lattice gauge theory pioneered by Byrnes (2005) and others, providing a more qubit-efficient implementation for compact U(1) theory. The model enables real-time dynamics simulations that are inaccessible to standard Euclidean Monte Carlo methods (Preskill 2018). It builds on the known strong-coupling properties of compact U(1) theory (Polyakov 1976, Hasenfratz 1980) and provides a testbed for quantum computation of gauge theories that could eventually be extended to non-Abelian theories and QCD.
