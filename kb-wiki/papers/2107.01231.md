# Gluon matter distribution in the proton and pion from extended holographic light-front QCD

> The holographic light-front QCD framework provides a unified nonperturbative description of the hadron mass spectrum, form factors and quark distributions. In this article we extend holographic QCD in order to describe the gluonic distribution in both the proton and pion from the coupling of the metric fluctuations induced by the spin-two Pomeron with the energy momentum tensor in anti--de Sitter space, together with constraints imposed by the Veneziano model{\color{blue},} without additional free parameters. The gluonic and quark distributions are shown to have significantly different effective QCD scales.

## Key claim

The paper extends holographic light-front QCD to describe gluonic distributions in the proton and pion by coupling metric fluctuations induced by the spin-two Pomeron to the energy-momentum tensor in anti-de Sitter space, constrained by the Veneziano model, without introducing additional free parameters beyond the confining scale and effective quark masses already present in the framework.

## Method

The authors perturb the anti-de Sitter metric with an external spin-two current that couples to the energy-momentum tensor in the bulk, identifying the tensor Pomeron with induced metric fluctuations. They use a negative soft-wall dilaton profile with scale \(\lambda_g = 1/(4\alpha'_P)\) determined from the Pomeron slope, and impose the analytic structure of generalized Veneziano amplitudes for a spin-two current to incorporate the Pomeron intercept. The gluon gravitational form factor \(A^g(Q^2)\) is computed from the overlap of the bulk-to-boundary propagator with twist-\(\tau\) hadron bound-state wave functions, and the gluon parton distribution functions are extracted via the relation between form factors and generalized parton distributions using a reparametrization function \(w(x)\).

## Key numbers

- Gluon mass squared radius for the proton: \(\langle r_g^2 \rangle_p = 2.93 / \lambda_g = (0.34 \, \text{fm})^2\)
- Gluon mass squared radius for the pion: \(\langle r_g^2 \rangle_\pi = 2.41 / \lambda_g = (0.31 \, \text{fm})^2\)
- Momentum fraction carried by gluons at \(\mu = 2\) GeV: \(0.316 \pm 0.025\) for the proton, \(0.463 \pm 0.011\) for the pion
- Pomeron trajectory parameters: intercept \(\alpha_P(0) \simeq 1.08\), slope \(\alpha'_P \simeq 0.25 \, \text{GeV}^{-2}\)
- Quark scale \(\lambda_q \simeq (0.5 \, \text{GeV})^2\), gluon scale \(\lambda_g \simeq 1 \, \text{GeV}^2\)
- Lowest \(2^{++}\) glueball mass from the model: \(M \simeq 1.92\) GeV
- Gluon coefficient for the proton leading Fock state: \(c_{\tau=4} = 0.225 \pm 0.014\) at input scale \(\mu_0 = 1.06 \pm 0.15\) GeV
- Gluon coefficient for the pion: \(g_\pi(x) = (0.429 \pm 0.007) \, g_{\tau=3}(x)\)

## Context

This work extends the holographic light-front QCD framework previously applied to quark distributions in mesons and nucleons by de Teramond et al. (2018) and Liu et al. (2019), as well as strange and charm sea asymmetries by Sufian et al. (2018, 2020). It incorporates gluonic matter with a significantly different effective scale than quark matter, consistent with lattice calculations by Bali (2000) showing that the static potential between gluons scales with the quadratic Casimir operator relative to the quark potential. The predictions for gluon gravitational form factors are compared with lattice QCD computations by Shanahan and Detmold (2018) and Pefkou et al. (2021), while the evolved gluon distributions are compared with global analyses from the NNPDF collaboration (Ball et al., 2017), CTEQ (Hou et al., 2019), JAM (Bailey et al., 2020), and pion-specific analyses by Novikov et al. (2020) and Cao et al. (2021). The framework enables future extension to the other gravitational form factors \(B(t)\) and \(C(t)\) to study internal shear forces and pressure distributions inside hadrons.
