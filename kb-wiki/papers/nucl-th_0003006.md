# Limits on the low energy antinucleon-nucleus annihilations from the Heisenberg principle

> We show that the quantum uncertainty principle puts some limits on the effectiveness of the antinucleon-nucleus annihilation at very low energies. This is caused by the fact that the realization a very effective short-distance reaction process implies information on the relative distance of the reacting particles. Some quantitative predictions are possible on this ground, including the approximate A-independence of antinucleon-nucleus annihilation rates.

# Limits on the low energy antinucleon-nucleus annihilations from the Heisenberg principle

## Key claim

The quantum uncertainty principle imposes a fundamental limit on antinucleon-nucleus annihilation effectiveness at very low momenta (below approximately 200 MeV/c), because the short-distance annihilation process necessarily contains information about the relative distance of the reacting particles, which conflicts with the low momentum of the projectile. This leads to a target-independent annihilation cross section at low energies, provided the annihilation shell thickness Δ is approximately 1 fm for all targets.

## Method

The authors develop a one-dimensional model of antinucleon wavefunction propagation, where an "annihilation shell" of thickness Δ ≈ 1 fm surrounds the nuclear surface, and the wavefunction must go to zero within this shell due to the high annihilation probability. They match the logarithmic derivative of the radial wavefunction χ(r) = rΨ(r) at the matching radius R_m (just outside the shell) using the condition |χ'/χ| ≈ 1/Δ, and derive constraints on the complex scattering length α from the uncertainty principle δk δx > 1. The analysis is extended from one-dimensional examples to the three-dimensional S-wave dominated regime for antinucleon-nucleus scattering.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic momentum scale for deviations from semiclassical behavior is k_b ∼ 1/Δ ∼ 200 MeV/c, where Δ ≈ 1 fm is the annihilation shell thickness.
- For k << 100 MeV/c and for antineutron projectiles, the reaction probability is roughly target-independent with magnitude πΔ/k_cm ≈ 6000 Δ/k_cm mb (Δ in fm, k in MeV/c).
- The imaginary part of the scattering length is estimated as |Im(α)| ≈ Δ ≈ 1 fm or smaller for all nuclear targets.
- The real part of the scattering length Re(α) is positive and estimated as ∼ 1 fm for light nuclei, and ∼ 1.3 A^{1/3} fm for heavy nuclei.
- The zero-energy ρ-parameter (ratio of real to imaginary forward scattering amplitude) is estimated as ρ ≈ -1 for light nuclei and ρ ≈ -A^{1/3} for heavy nuclei.
- For antiproton-proton annihilation, Coulomb forces fix a minimum kinetic energy of ∼ 1 MeV at the proton surface, corresponding to a momentum of ∼ 40 MeV/c.
- The ratio of absorbed to incoming flux scales as (kΔ)^2 for kΔ << 1.

## Context

This work extends the "inversion" phenomenon previously identified by the authors and others (Wyc, 1995; Protasov, 1997; Bianconi, 1999), where stronger attractive potentials produce weaker repulsive effects at low energies. It generalizes the black sphere model analysis from Bianconi (1999) by connecting the effect directly to the Heisenberg uncertainty principle, rather than relying on specific potential models. The paper explains the experimentally observed approximate A-independence of antinucleon-nucleus annihilation rates at momenta below 70 MeV/c (Ober, 1998; Protasov, 1997; Ne, 1999) and the counterintuitive result that antiproton-proton annihilation rates exceed those on deuterium and helium-4 at 30-50 MeV/c. The framework also applies to other strongly absorptive reactions such as kaon-nucleus absorption, and enables predictions for the scaling of annihilation rates into different final-state channels (e.g., two-pion versus two-kaon final states) based on their characteristic annihilation distances.
