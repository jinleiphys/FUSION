# Thermal quenching of electronic shells and channel competition in cluster fission

> Experimental and theoretical studies of fission of doubly-charged Li, Na, and K clusters in the low fissility regime reveal the strong influence of electronic shell effects on the fission products. The electronic entropy controls the quenching of the shell effects and the competition between magic-fragment channels, leading to a transition from favored channels of higher mass symmetry to the asymmetric channel involving the trimer cation at elevated temperatures.

## Key claim

Electronic entropy controls the thermal quenching of shell effects in cluster fission, causing a transition from more mass-symmetric magic-fragment channels at lower temperatures to the asymmetric trimer-cation channel at elevated temperatures, but only when the experimental temperatures are high enough (e.g., 700 K for Li) to suppress the shell stabilization of heavier magic fragments.

## Method

Doubly charged alkali-metal clusters (Li, Na, K) with sizes N=24, 26, 28, and 30 were produced via gas aggregation, photo-excited with a 15 ns Nd-YAG laser pulse at 3.50 eV, and allowed to form an evaporative ensemble. The fission fragments were mass analyzed using a tandem time-of-flight mass spectrometer, and the experimental channel distributions were compared with finite-temperature shell-correction method (FT-SCM) calculations that incorporate electronic entropy, triaxial deformations, and thermal averaging over shape fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- Experimental temperatures of the evaporative ensembles: 700 +/- 100 K for lithium, 400 +/- 100 K for sodium, and 300 +/- 100 K for potassium clusters.
- Fissility parameter X approximately 0.25 for doubly charged alkali clusters with N=30.
- Rayleigh model critical size for barrierless fission N_cr^RM approximately 10 for doubly charged Li, Na, and K clusters.
- Appearance size N_app = 24 for Li_N^2+ and Na_N^2+ clusters, and 19 for K_N^2+ clusters.
- Laser photon energy h nu = 3.50 eV used for photo-excitation.
- Kinetic energy released during fission of order 1 eV (e.g., for N=26).
- Surface energy coefficients used in LDM: 1.30 eV for Li, 0.85 eV for Na, and 0.70 eV for K.
- Major shell spacing formula: hbar omega_0(N) = 49 eV * a_0^2 / (r_s^2 N^{1/3}).

## Context

This work extends the liquid drop model (Rayleigh 1882; Bohr and Mottelson 1975) for cluster fission by demonstrating that quantum shell effects, not classical hydrodynamics, dominate in the low-fissility regime (X << 1). It builds on prior observations of asymmetric fission involving Na_3^+ fragments (Brechignac 1990) and on the finite-temperature shell-correction method developed by Yannouleas and Landman (1997). The results enable temperature-controlled studies of charge instabilities in clusters and show that the electronic entropy, rather than ionic contributions, governs the thermal dependence of shell structure and fission product distributions.
