# Holographic constraints on Bjorken hydrodynamics at finite coupling

> In large-$N_c$ conformal field theories with classical holographic duals, inverse coupling constant corrections are obtained by considering higher-derivative terms in the corresponding gravity theory. In this work, we use type IIB supergravity and bottom-up Gauss-Bonnet gravity to study the dynamics of boost-invariant Bjorken hydrodynamics at finite coupling. We analyze the time-dependent decay properties of non-local observables (scalar two-point functions and Wilson loops) probing the different models of Bjorken flow and show that they can be expressed generically in terms of a few field theory parameters. In addition, our computations provide an analytically quantifiable probe of the coupling-dependent validity of hydrodynamics at early times in a simple model of heavy-ion collisions, which is an observable closely analogous to the hydrodynamization time of a quark-gluon plasma. We find that to third order in the hydrodynamic expansion, the convergence of hydrodynamics is improved and that generically, as expected from field theory considerations and recent holographic results, the applicability of hydrodynamics is delayed as the field theory coupling decreases.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the applicability of hydrodynamics in boost-invariant Bjorken flow is delayed as the field theory coupling decreases, and that to third order in the hydrodynamic expansion the convergence of hydrodynamics is improved, though the authors note that the gradient expansion is believed to be asymptotic with a zero radius of convergence.

## Method

The authors used type IIB supergravity (with leading-order $\alpha'^3$ corrections proportional to $\gamma \sim \lambda^{-3/2}$) and bottom-up Gauss-Bonnet gravity (with coupling $\lambda_{\rm GB}$) to construct holographic dual geometries for boost-invariant Bjorken flow. They computed the time-dependent decay of non-local observables (equal-time scalar two-point functions and space-like Wilson loops) in the hydrodynamic regime, extracting critical proper times $\tau_{\rm crit}$ at which these observables become physically sensible (positive and monotonically decreasing). The analysis included first-order, second-order, and third-order hydrodynamic gradient expansions, with transport coefficients known analytically for both theories.

## Key numbers

* In $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM at infinite coupling, the first-order hydrodynamic bound from the energy density gives $\tau_{\rm hyd}^{\rm 1st} w^{3/2} = 0.19$, while the longitudinal two-point function gives a much stronger constraint $\tau_{\rm crit}^{\rm 1st} w^{3/2} = 2.83$ and Wilson loops give $\tau_{\rm crit}^{\rm 1st} w^{3/2} = 0.65$.
* For the longitudinal two-point function at second order in hydrodynamics, the critical proper time in $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM with coupling corrections is $\tau_{\rm crit}^{\rm 2nd} w^{3/2} = 1.987 + 275.079\, \gamma + \mathcal{O}(\gamma^2) = 1.987 + 41.333\, \lambda^{-3/2} + \mathcal{O}(\lambda^{-3})$.
* For the same observable in Gauss-Bonnet theory at second order, $\tau_{\rm crit}^{\rm 2nd} w^{3/2} = 1.987 - 14.876\, \lambda_{\rm GB} + \mathcal{O}(\lambda_{\rm GB}^2)$.
* At $\gamma = 6.67\times 10^{-3}$ (corresponding to $\lambda = 7.98$) and at $\lambda_{\rm GB} = -0.2$, each increasing $\eta/s$ by $80\%$, $\tau_{\rm crit}^{\rm 2nd} w^{3/2}$ increases by $92.3\%$ in $\mathcal{N}=4$ theory and by $150\%$ in perturbative Gauss-Bonnet theory; the fully non-perturbative Gauss-Bonnet calculation gives a $145\%$ increase.
* The hydrodynamization time from shock wave collisions in Gauss-Bonnet theory is $t_{\rm hyd} T_{\rm hyd} = 0.41 - 0.52 \lambda_{\rm GB} + \mathcal{O}(\lambda_{\rm GB}^2)$ for narrow shocks and $t_{\rm hyd} T_{\rm hyd} = 0.43 - 6.3 \lambda_{\rm GB} + \mathcal{O}(\lambda_{\rm GB}^2)$ for wide shocks.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Pedraza (2014) on non-local observables in Bjorken flow by incorporating higher-order hydrodynamic corrections and finite-coupling effects from holography. It builds on the holographic studies of coupling-dependent quasinormal spectra by Grozdanov (2016) and the shock wave collision simulations by Grozdanov (2016), which found that hydrodynamization time increases as coupling decreases. The results provide analytically tractable estimates for the coupling-dependent validity of hydrodynamics that are consistent with the more complex non-linear shock wave calculations, and enable future comparisons with kinetic theory predictions for the breakdown of hydrodynamics at intermediate coupling.
