# Long-range properties of $1S$ bottomonium states

> In the framework of weakly-coupled pNRQCD, we derive, first, an analytical expression for the chromo-polarizability of $1S$-bottomonium states in agreement with previous determinations. Then we use the QCD trace anomaly to obtain the two-pion production amplitude for the chromo-polarizability operator and match the result to a chiral effective field theory with $1S$-bottomonium states and pions as degrees-of-freedom. In this chiral effective field theory we compute some long-range properties of the $1S$ bottomonium generated by the pion coupling such as the leading chiral logarithm to the $1S$-bottomonium mass and the van der Waals potential between two $1S$-bottomonium states. Both results improve on previously known expressions.

## Key claim

The authors derive an analytical expression for the chromopolarizability of 1S bottomonium states within weakly-coupled pNRQCD, and then use the QCD trace anomaly to match this to a chiral effective field theory with pions, obtaining the leading chiral logarithm to the 1S bottomonium mass and the van der Waals potential between two such states; both results improve on previously known expressions, but the polarizability value carries significant uncertainty from the strong coupling constant and bottom mass.

## Method

The authors start from weakly-coupled potential nonrelativistic QCD (pNRQCD) and integrate out the ultrasoft scale to obtain a gluonic van der Waals effective field theory (gWEFT), where the chromopolarizability beta of 1S bottomonium is computed by matching to pNRQCD using Coulombic octet continuum wave functions. They then use the QCD trace anomaly to hadronize the two-chromoelectric-field polarizability operator into two-pion production, matching the result to a chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) with 1S bottomonium fields and pions as degrees of freedom. From this chiEFT, they compute the leading chiral logarithm contribution to the 1S bottomonium mass and the long-range van der Waals potential between two 1S bottomonium states via a two-pion loop, using a dispersive representation to extract the coordinate-space potential.

## Key numbers

* Chromopolarizability of 1S bottomonium: beta = 0.50^{+0.42}_{-0.38} GeV^{-3}, obtained for bottom mass m = 5 GeV and alpha_s values ranging from 0.3 to 0.5 at scales 2 GeV to 1 GeV respectively.
* The integral I in the polarizability expression evaluates to 0.01143 for N_c = 3.
* The binding energy of charmonium to nuclear matter from lattice QCD is of order 40 MeV or smaller.
* The pion decay constant used is F_pi = 92.419 MeV, and the pion mass in the isospin limit is m_pi^2 approximately (135 MeV)^2.
* The parameter kappa from pionic transitions of quarkonium is 0.186 +/- 0.003 +/- 0.006 from dipion invariant mass fits, and 0.210 +/- 0.027 +/- 0.042 from angular distribution fits.

## Context

This work extends the framework of nonrelativistic effective field theories (NRQCD and pNRQCD) combined with chiral effective field theory to study long-range properties of heavy quarkonium, building on earlier calculations of chromopolarizability by Voloshin (1978), Peskin (1979), Bhanot (1979), and Leutwyler (1980). The results enable first-principles calculations of color van der Waals forces between quarkonium states, relevant for understanding multiquark hadrons such as the pentaquark candidates P_c(4380) and P_c(4450) observed by LHCb, and for studying possible bound states of quarkonium with nuclei as suggested by Brodsky, Schmidt, and de Teramond (1989) and recently explored on the lattice by the NPLQCD Collaboration (2014).
