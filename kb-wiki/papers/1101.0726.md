# Hyperon stars at finite temperature in the Brueckner theory

> We perform Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations of hypernuclear matter at finite temperature and provide convenient analytical parametrizations of the results. We study then the properties of (proto)neutron stars containing hyperons. We find important effects of trapping and finite temperature on the structure of hyperonic stars.

## Key claim

The authors perform Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations of hypernuclear matter at finite temperature and find that trapping and finite temperature have important effects on the structure of hyperonic stars, but the maximum mass of hyperon stars remains quite low (around 1.31–1.37 solar masses), implying that most observed neutron stars would actually be hybrid stars involving a transition to quark matter in the interior.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Goldstone equation at finite temperature within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) framework using the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential plus Urbana three-body forces, and the Nijmegen soft-core NSC89 hyperon-nucleon potentials. They employ the frozen correlations approximation (using T=0 single-particle potentials) to generate finite-temperature results, then provide analytical parametrizations of the free energy density for nucleon densities 0.1–0.8 fm⁻³, hyperon fractions up to 0.9 (Λ) and 0.5 (Σ⁻), and temperatures 0–50 MeV. The equation of state is then used to compute (proto)neutron star configurations via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of cold hyperon stars (N,Y,l): 1.31 M⊙ at central density 1.84 fm⁻³
- Maximum mass of hot trapped hyperon stars (N,Y,l,ν) at S/A=2: 1.58 M⊙ at central density 1.36 fm⁻³
- Maximum mass of cold nucleonic stars (N,l): 1.84 M⊙ at central density 1.36 fm⁻³
- Maximum mass of hot trapped nucleonic stars (N,l,ν) at S/A=2: 1.74 M⊙ at central density 1.36 fm⁻³
- Overall r.m.s. deviation of fit and BHF data points for free energy per baryon: less than 2 MeV
- Minimum mass of protoneutron stars: slightly above 0.5 M⊙
- Temperature range of parametrization: 0–50 MeV
- Nucleon density range of parametrization: 0.1–0.8 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous BHF studies of protoneutron stars (Nicotra et al. 2006; Burgio and Schulze 2009, 2010) by including consistently interacting hyperons at finite temperature, rather than treating hyperons as a non-interacting Fermi gas. It builds on pioneering work by Pons et al. (1999) using relativistic mean field models with hyperons, and Rios et al. (2005) using the BHF approach. The results enable future studies using improved hyperon-nucleon potentials and three-body forces to verify whether the low maximum mass of hyperon stars (necessitating quark matter in heavier objects) is a robust conclusion.
