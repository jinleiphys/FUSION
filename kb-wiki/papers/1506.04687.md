# The Nuclear Symmetry Energy in Heavy Ion Collisions

> In this contribution I discuss the nuclear symmetry energy in the regime of hadronic degrees of freedom. The density dependence of the symmetry energy is important from very low densities in supernova explosions, to the structure of neutron-rich nuclei around saturation density, and to several times saturation density in neutron stars. Heavy ion collisions are the only means to study this density dependence in the laboratory. Numerical simulations of transport theories are used to extract the equation-of-state, and thus also the symmetry energy. I discuss some examples, which relate particularly to the high density symmetry energy, which is of particular interest today. I review the status and point out some open problems in the determination of the symmetry energy in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

Heavy ion collisions interpreted with transport theory can constrain the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, but the results remain inconsistent across different observables (flow versus pion ratios) and different model codes, particularly for densities above saturation.

## Method

The author reviews numerical simulations of transport theory (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck type) applied to heavy ion collisions from Fermi energies (32–150 AMeV) up to intermediate energies (400 AMeV). The simulations model the temporal evolution of one-body phase-space distribution functions under mean-field potentials and two-body collisions, with isospin effects entering via differences in neutron and proton potentials and isospin-dependent cross sections. Observables examined include neutron-to-proton ratios of emitted particles, elliptic flow ratios of neutrons to hydrogen, and pion isospin ratios (\(\pi^-/\pi^+\)).

## Key numbers

- For Sn+Sn collisions at 50 AMeV, a systematic analysis of several observables yielded limits on the symmetry energy exponent \(\gamma\) around \(\gamma \approx 0.6\).
- For Au+Au collisions at 400 AMeV, the ratio of elliptic flow of neutrons to hydrogen gave a best-fit symmetry energy exponent \(\gamma\) of about 0.76 (moderately soft).
- In central \(^{136}\text{Xe}+^{124}\text{Sn}\) collisions at 150 AMeV, the \(n/p\) ratio as a function of transverse energy shows that a softer symmetry energy yields a larger \(n/p\) ratio at lower transverse energies, while the effective mass ordering (smaller neutron effective mass) increases the ratio at higher transverse energies.
- The \(\pi^-/\pi^+\) ratio in Au+Au collisions as a function of incident energy shows results that differ quantitatively and even in trend with stiffness across different model codes, compared to FOPI data.

## Context

This review extends the line of work summarized in Tsang (2012) and Horowitz (2014) on constraining the symmetry energy from nuclear structure, heavy ion collisions, and neutron star observations. Below saturation density, results from Sn+Sn collisions at 50 AMeV (MSU), nuclear mass fits, giant dipole resonance energies, isobaric analog state analyses, and cluster production in the expansion phase converge. Above saturation density, results from flow analyses (ASYEOS experiment, FOPI collaboration) and pion ratio analyses (FOPI data) remain inconsistent, requiring clarification of \(\Delta\) dynamics and threshold effects. The work enables future combined analyses of multiple observables and improved transport theory treatments, particularly for cluster production and in-medium cross sections.
