# Ratio of baryon and electric-charge cumulants at second order with acceptance corrections

> We evaluate the ratio of baryon and electric-charge cumulants at second order from the recent experimental results at $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}}=200$ GeV by the STAR Collaboration. The baryon number cumulant is reconstructed from the proton number distribution, and effects of the finite acceptance on the transverse momentum are corrected assuming the independent particle emission. We show that the obtained ratio has a dependence on the rapidity window. Comparison of the result with the hadron resonance gas model and lattice QCD numerical simulations suggests that if the fluctuations are generated from a thermal medium its temperature is significantly lower than the chemical freezeout temperature.

## Key claim

The ratio of second-order baryon and electric-charge cumulants at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, after correcting for finite transverse-momentum acceptance, shows a dependence on the rapidity window \(\Delta y\) and, when compared to the hadron resonance gas model and lattice QCD, suggests an effective temperature of \(T\simeq134-138\) MeV, significantly lower than the chemical freezeout temperature \(T_{\rm chem}\simeq156\) MeV, provided the fluctuations are assumed to be thermal.

## Method

The authors reconstructed the baryon number cumulant \(\langle N_{\rm B}^2 \rangle_{\rm c}\) from the proton number cumulants measured by STAR (2021) using the procedure of Kitazawa (2011, 2012), and used the electric-charge cumulant \(\langle N_{\rm Q}^2 \rangle_{\rm c}\) from STAR (2019) for the most central (0-5%) Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV. They corrected the effect of the finite transverse-momentum acceptance (\(0.4<p_T<2.0\) GeV for protons, \(0.4<p_T<1.6\) GeV for electric charge) by assuming independent particle emission and applying a binomial efficiency correction with acceptance probabilities \(R_{p_T}\) obtained from a Blast-wave model.

## Key numbers

* The acceptance probability \(R_{p_T}\) for pions in the range \(0.4<p_T<1.6\) GeV is 0.44; for kaons and protons in the same range it is 0.71; for the combined \(\pi\)+K+p sample it is 0.49; for protons in the range \(0.4<p_T<2.0\) GeV it is 0.82.
* The ratio \(\langle N_{\rm B}^2 \rangle_{\rm c}/\langle N_{\rm Q}^2 \rangle_{\rm c}\) after acceptance correction shows a clear increasing trend as a function of the rapidity window \(\Delta y\) from 0.2 to 1.0.
* Comparing the experimental ratio with the hadron resonance gas model yields an effective temperature \(T\simeq134-138\) MeV, depending on \(\Delta y\), which is significantly lower than the chemical freezeout temperature \(T_{\rm chem}\simeq156\) MeV at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of conserved-charge fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, building on the STAR measurements of cumulants (STAR, 2019, 2021) and the reconstruction method for baryon cumulants from protons (Kitazawa, 2011, 2012). The result contradicts the assumption that fluctuations are generated from a thermal medium at chemical freezeout, as suggested by earlier work (Asakawa, 2000; Jeon, 2000), and instead indicates that the fluctuations may originate later in the hadronic phase. The observed rapidity-window dependence motivates further theoretical studies of fluctuation dynamics (Kitazawa, 2013, 2015) and experimental measurements with wider acceptance at future facilities such as FAIR, NICA, and J-PARC-HI.
