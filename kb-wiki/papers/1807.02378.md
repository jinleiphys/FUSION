# Latest predictions from the EbyE NLO EKRT model

> We present the latest results from the NLO pQCD + saturation + viscous hydrodynamics (EbyE NLO EKRT) model. The parameters in the EKRT saturation model are fixed by the charged hadron multiplicity in the 0-5 \% 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions. The $\sqrt{s}$, $A$ and centrality dependence of the initial particle production follows then from the QCD dynamics of the model. This allows us to predict the $\sqrt{s}$ and $A$ dependence of the particle production. We show that our results are in an excellent agreement with the low-$p_T$ data from 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC as well as with the data from the 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC. In particular, we study the centrality dependences of hadronic multiplicities, flow coefficients, and various flow correlations. Furthermore, the nuclear mass number dependence of the initial particle production and hydrodynamic evolution can be tested in the 5.44 TeV Xe+Xe collisions at the LHC. To this end, we show our predictions for charged particle multiplicities, and in particular, show how the deformations of the Xe nuclei reflect into the flow coefficients.

## Key claim

The EbyE NLO EKRT model, with its parameters fixed solely by the charged hadron multiplicity in 0-5% 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions, can predict the center-of-mass energy and nuclear mass number dependence of soft hadronic observables across multiple collision systems, provided the saturation scale remains perturbative.

## Method

The initial conditions for hydrodynamics are computed using NLO perturbative QCD with collinear factorization and a saturation conjecture that controls transverse energy production through a semi-hard scale p_sat, which depends on the product of nuclear thickness functions T_A, sqrt(s), and nuclear mass number A. The local energy density at the formation time tau_s = 1/p_sat is given by e = (K_sat/pi)[p_sat]^4, which is then evolved to a common time tau_0 = 0.2 fm using 0+1D Bjorken hydrodynamics. The subsequent evolution uses a boost-invariant Israel-Stewart type dissipative fluid dynamics with a parametrized temperature-dependent shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s(T), with kinetic freeze-out at T_dec = 100 MeV and the s95p-PCE-v1 equation of state.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale p_sat is defined with a minimum value corresponding to tau_0 = 0.2 fm.
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature is T_dec = 100 MeV; chemical freeze-out temperature is T_chem = 175 MeV.
- For Xe nuclei, the nuclear shape deformation parameters are beta_s = 0.163 and beta_4 = -0.003, taken from Moller (2015).
- The normalized symmetric cumulants are defined as nsc(n, m) = <v_n^2 v_m^2> / <v_n^2><v_m^2> - 1, where angle brackets denote event averaging with proper multiplicity weights.

## Context

This work extends the EbyE NLO EKRT framework previously developed by Niemi (2015) and Paatelainen (2013) by applying it to predict observables in 5.023 TeV Pb+Pb and 5.44 TeV Xe+Xe collisions measured at the LHC, as well as 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC. The framework enables testing the nuclear mass number dependence of initial particle production and hydrodynamic evolution, particularly through the Xe+Xe collision data, where the deformed shape of Xe nuclei (following the deformation parameters of Moller 2015) produces measurable effects on flow coefficients that are necessary to describe the experimental data.
