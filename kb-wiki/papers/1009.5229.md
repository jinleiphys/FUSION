# On the ridge-like structures in the nuclear and hadronic reactions

## Key claim

The ridge-like structure observed in two-particle correlation functions in both nuclear and hadronic reactions can result from the coherent rotation of a transient state of matter, provided that the collisions occur at non-zero impact parameters. The authors argue that the observation of such a structure in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at the LHC indicates that the transient matter in those collisions is strongly interacting, similar to the perfect liquid state found at RHIC in nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Method

The authors present a phenomenological model where the overlap region of colliding hadrons at non-zero impact parameters generates massive virtual quarks with large initial orbital angular momentum. Due to strong interactions, this orbital angular momentum leads to coherent rotation of the quark-pion liquid as a whole in the xz-plane. The particle production mechanism at moderate transverse momenta involves excitation of a part of this rotating transient state, where the excited parts are located near the periphery to avoid absorption (quenching). The geometry of this rotation produces narrow azimuthal angle (Δφ) correlations and broad pseudorapidity (Δη) correlations.

## Key numbers

- At LHC energy √s = 7 TeV, the inelastic overlap function h_inel(s,b) has a peripheral impact parameter dependence due to reflective scattering.
- The mean multiplicity ⟨n⟩(s) at √s = 7 TeV gets its main contribution from collisions with non-zero impact parameters.
- The model predicts a linear relation between average transverse momentum and mean multiplicity: ⟨p_T⟩(s) = a + b⟨n⟩(s), which the authors state is in good agreement with experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the mechanism proposed by the same authors (Troshin and Tyurin, earlier work) for explaining ridge structures in nuclear reactions at RHIC to the new observation of similar structures in proton-proton collisions by the CMS Collaboration at the LHC. The paper argues against the conventional approach of using proton-proton collisions as a reference process for detecting deconfined phase formation by comparing with nucleus-nucleus collisions, since the ridge observation suggests the transient matter in proton-proton collisions is also strongly interacting. The authors note that the ridge structure in nuclear reactions had previously been observed only in peripheral collisions, consistent with their model's requirement of non-zero impact parameters. The work enables predictions for directed flow v₁ and elliptic flow v₂ in hadron reactions, as well as correlations between multiplicity and transverse momentum.
