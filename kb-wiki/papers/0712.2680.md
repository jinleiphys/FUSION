# Tensor and Operator Forms of 3He and 3H Wave Functions for Parity-Violating Nuclear Forces

> Tensor representation (TR) for wave function (WF) of three-nucleon bound state with the total angular momentum I=1/2 is discussed. The WF in TR has 16 complex components depending on vectors of relative momenta. Constraints on the WF imposed by requirements of invariance with respect to space inversion and time reversal are studied. Both parity-even and parity-odd components of the 3N bound state are constructed using 16 scalar functions. The arguments of the functions are magnitudes of relative momenta and scalar product of the momenta. With nuclear forces being time-reversal invariant these functions are real. The WF in TR is converted into an operator form, accounting for parity violating contributions. Properties of operator representations for WFs of 2N and 3N nuclei are compared.

## Key claim

The paper constructs both parity-even and parity-odd components of the 3He and 3H wave functions in tensor and operator forms, under the condition that nuclear forces are time-reversal invariant but may violate parity; the wave function requires 16 real scalar functions (8 for positive parity, 8 for negative parity) rather than the 8 needed when parity is conserved.

## Method

The wave function of the three-nucleon bound state with total angular momentum I=1/2 is expressed in tensor representation (TR) using 16 complex components depending on the Jacobi momenta p and q. Constraints from space inversion and time reversal invariance are applied to reduce the independent components to 16 real scalar functions ψ_λ(p,q,ξ), where ξ is the cosine of the angle between p and q. The tensor representation is then converted into an operator form (OF) using spin-angular operators acting on the spin-zero two-nucleon state, with the scalar functions expressed through polarization operators and cartesian tensors built from the unit vectors p̂, q̂, and the axial vector v = [p̂ × q̂].

## Key numbers

- The wave function in tensor representation has 16 complex components depending on vectors of relative momenta p and q.
- The parity-even component of the WF depends on 8 real scalar functions ψ_λ with λ=1,…,8.
- The parity-odd component of the WF depends on 8 real scalar functions ψ_λ with λ=9,…,16.
- The deuteron WF in tensor representation consists of 9 complex components, compared to 16 for the 3N bound state.
- The 3N bound state has total angular momentum I=1/2.
- The Jacobi momenta are defined as p = 1/2 (k₂ − k₃) and q = 1/3 (2k₁ − k₂ − k₃).

## Context

This work extends the operator representation of three-nucleon wave functions developed by Gerjuoy and Schwinger (1942) and Fachruddin et al. (2004) from the parity-conserving case to include parity-violating components. The tensor representation used here was previously applied by Kotlyar (1987, 2005) to calculate spin-dependent momentum distributions and photodisintegration of 3He. The formalism enables calculations of reaction amplitudes involving parity-violating nuclear forces in three-nucleon systems, and the comparison with the deuteron case (2N bound state) shows that both systems can be described by compact operator forms with similar spin-space structure for collinear momenta.
