# 7Be charge exchange between 7Be3+ ion and exotic long-lived negatively charged massive particle in big bang nucleosynthesis

> The existence of an exotic long-lived negatively charged massive particle, i.e., X-, during big bang nucleosynthesis can affect primordial light element abundances. Especially, the final abundance of 7Li, mainly originating from the electron capture of 7Be, has been suggested to reduce by the 7Be destruction via the radiative X- capture of 7Be followed by the radiative proton capture of the bound state of 7Be and X- (7BeX). We suggest a new route of 7BeX formation, that is the 7Be charge exchange at the reaction of 7Be3+ ion and X-. The formation rate depends on the number fraction of 7Be3+ ion, the charge exchange cross section of 7Be3+, and the probability that produced excited states 7BeX* are converted to the ground state. We estimate respective quantities affecting the 7BeX formation rate, and find that this reaction pathway can be more important than ordinary radiative recombination of 7Be and X-. The effect of the charge exchange reaction is then shown in a latest nuclear reaction network calculation. Quantum physical model calculations for related reactions are needed to precisely estimate the efficiency of this pathway in future.

## Key claim

The authors propose that a nonradiative charge exchange reaction between fully ionized \(^7\)Be\(^{4+}\) and a negatively charged massive particle \(X^-\) via the intermediate hydrogen-like ion \(^7\)Be\(^{3+}\) can form bound states \(^7\)Be\(_X\) more efficiently than the previously assumed radiative recombination, potentially reducing the primordial \(^7\)Li abundance; however, the actual efficiency depends on uncertain cross sections and transition probabilities that require future quantum mechanical calculations.

## Method

The authors estimate the effective rate for \(^7\)Be\(_X\) formation via the charge exchange reaction \(^7\)Be\(^{3+} + X^- \rightarrow ^7\)Be\(_X^* + e^-\) by multiplying three factors: the equilibrium number fraction of \(^7\)Be\(^{3+}\) relative to \(^7\)Be\(^{4+}\) (derived from the Saha equation), the thermal reaction rate for the charge exchange (scaled from protonium formation cross sections), and the probability that excited \(^7\)Be\(_X^*\) states transition to the ground state (dominated by stimulated emission from cosmic background radiation). They then incorporate this pathway into a nuclear reaction network calculation for big bang nucleosynthesis.

## Key numbers

- Equilibrium number ratio of \(^7\)Be\(^{3+}\) to \(^7\)Be\(^{4+}\) at \(T_9 = 0.4\) (temperature \(T \approx 34\) keV): \(n_{\text{Be}^{3+}}/n_{\text{Be}^{4+}} = 5.94 \times 10^{-7}\).
- Binding energy of the ground-state \(^7\)Be\(^{3+}\) ion: 217.7 eV.
- Thermal reaction rate for \(^7\)Be\(^{3+} + X^- \rightarrow ^7\)Be\(_X^* + e^-\): \(\langle \sigma v \rangle = 5.23 \times 10^{-14}~\text{cm}^3~\text{s}^{-1}\) at \(T_9 = 0.4\), assuming a cross section \(\sigma(I(^7\text{Be}^{3+})) = 1.75 \times 10^7\) b.
- Rate for \(^7\)Be\(^{3+}\) to form excited \(^7\)Be\(_X^*\) via charge exchange: \(\Gamma_{\text{Be}^{3+} \rightarrow \text{Be}_X^*} = 3.96 \times 10^4~Y_X~\text{s}^{-1}\) at \(T_9 = 0.4\), where \(Y_X = n_X/n_b\) is the \(X^-\) abundance relative to baryons.
- Spontaneous emission rate of excited \(^7\)Be\(_X^*\) states: \(\Gamma_{u,\text{sp}} \approx 5.83 \times 10^{11}~\text{s}^{-1}\) for \(N_l = 226\) and \(\overline{E_{ul}^2} = (218~\text{eV})^2\).
- Stimulated emission rate of excited \(^7\)Be\(_X^*\) states by cosmic background radiation: \(\Gamma^{\gamma}_{u,\text{st}} \approx 9.21 \times 10^{13}~\text{s}^{-1}\) at \(T_9 = 0.4\) for \(N_l = 226\) and \(\overline{E_{ul}} = 218\) eV.
- Collisional ionization rate of \(^7\)Be\(_X^*\) by \(e^\pm\): \(\Gamma_{\text{Be}_X^*, \text{de}} = 2.79 \times 10^{10}~\text{s}^{-1}\) at \(T_9 = 0.4\) for states with main quantum number \(n \gg 1\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of negatively charged massive particles (\(X^-\)) in big bang nucleosynthesis, which had assumed that bound states \(^7\)Be\(_X\) form only via radiative recombination (Dimopoulos 1989, Rujula 1990). The new charge exchange pathway is analogous to protonium formation (Cohen 1997, Sakimoto 2001) and muonic hydrogen formation (Cohen 2004, Sakimoto 2010). The results enable future quantum mechanical calculations of the relevant cross sections to precisely determine whether this pathway resolves the primordial \(^7\)Li discrepancy between standard BBN predictions and observations of metal-poor stars (Spite 1982, Ryan 1999, Asplund 2005).
