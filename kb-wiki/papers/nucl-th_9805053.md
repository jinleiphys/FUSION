# g-factor of a tightly bound electron

> We study the hyperfine splitting of an electron in hydrogen-like $^{209}Bi ^{82+} $. It is found that the hfs energy splitting can be explained well by considering the g-factor reduction due to the binding effect of a bound electron. We determine for the first time the experimental value of the magnetic moment of a tightly bound electron.

# g-factor of a tightly bound electron

## Key claim

The observed hyperfine splitting in hydrogen-like ²⁰⁹Bi⁸²⁺ can be explained by the g-factor reduction of the bound electron due to the binding effect, rather than by the Bohr-Weisskopf effect or other QED corrections alone. The authors determine the first experimental value of the magnetic moment of a tightly bound electron, finding the extracted g-factor reduction of g_BC^exp = -0.125 g_0, which is close to but slightly smaller in magnitude than the theoretical point-charge value of g_BC = -0.136 g_0.

## Method

The authors calculate the hyperfine splitting of the 1s₁/₂ ground state in hydrogen-like ²⁰⁹Bi⁸²⁺ using Dirac wave functions and the g-factor reduction formula for a point charge, g_BC = -(2/3)(1 - √(1-γ²)) g_0. They compare the resulting wavelength for the M1 transition between F=4 and F=5 hyperfine levels to the experimental measurement of Klaft et al. (1994), and also compute the Bohr-Weisskopf effect and QED corrections to extract the experimental g-factor from the observed wavelength.

## Key numbers

- Experimental wavelength of the M1 transition: λ_exp = 243.87(4) nm (Klaft et al., 1994)
- Point Coulomb wavelength (free electron g-factor): λ_point = 212.7 nm
- Observed difference: δλ = λ_exp - λ_point = 31.17 nm
- Bohr-Weisskopf effect contribution: δλ_BW ≈ 3.5 nm (Schneider et al., 1993); recalculated in this paper as δλ_BW = 1.8 nm
- Vacuum polarization contribution: δλ_vp = -1.6 nm (Schneider et al., 1994); total QED corrections in this paper: δλ_QED = -1.0 nm
- Theoretical g-factor reduction (point charge): g_BC = -0.136 g_0
- Predicted wavelength including g-factor reduction: λ = 246.2 nm
- Extracted experimental g-factor reduction: g_BC^exp = -0.125 g_0
- Bohr-Weisskopf anomaly parameter: ε = -0.0083
- b^(1s) constant: 0.035
- Nuclear magnetic moment of ²⁰⁹Bi: μ = 4.1106 n.m.
- Single-particle magnetic moment: μ_sp = 2.624 n.m.

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing discrepancy between the measured hyperfine splitting in hydrogen-like ²⁰⁹Bi⁸²⁺ and theoretical predictions that included only the Bohr-Weisskopf effect (Bohr and Weisskopf, 1950) and QED corrections. Previous calculations by Finkbeiner et al. (1993) and Schneider et al. (1993, 1994) could not explain the observed 14.9% deviation from the point Coulomb value. The g-factor reduction mechanism was previously observed in muonic atoms by Yamazaki et al. (1974), and this paper extends that concept to electronic atoms with high nuclear charge, enabling future tests of QED corrections under strong magnetic fields.
