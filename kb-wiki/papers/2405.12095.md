# $ {}^{164} \mathrm{Pb} $: A possible heaviest $ N = Z $ doubly magic nucleus

> We confirm by using the Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculation that $ {}^{164} \mathrm{Pb} $ is a possible heaviest $ N = Z $ doubly magic nucleus whose lifetime is long enough to be measured on accelerator experiments. We estimate the proton-emission and alpha-decay half-lives of $ {}^{164} \mathrm{Pb} $. The estimated proton-emission half-life ranges from $ 0.1 \, \mathrm{ps} $ to $ 10 \, \mathrm{ns} $, while the alpha decay can be safely neglected.

## Key claim
Using Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations, the authors confirm that 164Pb is a possible heaviest N=Z doubly magic nucleus, but the claim is conditional: the proton-emission half-life depends strongly on which energy density functional is used, ranging from 0.1 ps to 10 ns, and only some functionals (SLy4, SLy5, SkM*) yield half-lives long enough for experimental measurement.

## Method
The authors performed spherical Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with six energy density functionals (SLy4, SLy5, SkM*, UNEDF0, UNEDF1, UNEDF2) using a volume-type pairing interaction with a 60 MeV cutoff, discretizing the radial coordinate on a 160 x 0.1 fm grid. The one-proton emission half-life was estimated using the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin approximation assuming emission of a 3s1/2 proton, and the alpha-decay half-life was estimated using a phenomenological formula from Koura (2012). Axial deformation was also checked using the HFBTHO code with 24 hbar-omega harmonic-oscillator basis.

## Key numbers
- Proton-emission half-life ranges from 0.18 ps (UNEDF2) to 12000 ps (SLy5), with SLy4 giving 11000 ps
- Alpha-decay half-life is longer than 10^35 s for all functionals (log10 T1/2 ranges from 36 to 170)
- Two-proton separation energy S2p is negative for all functionals, ranging from -4.8937 MeV (SkM*) to -5.8088 MeV (UNEDF1)
- Alpha-decay Q-value Qalpha ranges from 0.3694 MeV (SkM*) to 2.3151 MeV (UNEDF0)
- Proton 3s1/2 single-particle energy ranges from +1.8969 MeV (SLy5) to +2.9433 MeV (UNEDF2)
- Proton skin thickness Rp - Rn is approximately 0.1 fm (SLy4: 5.2099 fm - 5.0911 fm = 0.1188 fm)
- Proton pairing gap of 164Pb is less than 10 keV (0.0003 to 0.0006 MeV across functionals)
- Classical turning points: r1 = 7.5 fm for all functionals except UNEDF2 (7.4 fm); r2 ranges from 40.0 fm (UNEDF2) to 61.5 fm (SLy5)

## Context
This work extends the search for the heaviest N=Z doubly magic nucleus beyond 100Sn, which is the current heaviest known N=Z doubly magic nucleus. The authors benchmark their calculations against experimental data for 100Sn, finding that SLy4, SLy5, and UNEDF0 are most accurate. The prediction that Z=N=82 magicity holds in this extreme proton-rich region supports the recent experimental finding by Yang (2024) that the N=82 magic number remains robust on the neutron-deficient side. The one-to-one correspondence between the proton 3s1/2 single-particle energy and the proton-emission half-life means that future measurement of the half-life would provide a constraint on energy density functionals, particularly regarding isospin symmetry breaking as studied by Naito (2023) and Sagawa (2022).
