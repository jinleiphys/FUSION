# Ground-state properties of even and odd Magnesium isotopes in a symmetry-conserving approach

> We present a self-consistent theory for odd nuclei with exact blocking and particle number and angular momentum projection. The demanding treatment of the pairing correlations in a variation-after-projection approach as well as the explicit consideration of the triaxial deformation parameters in a projection after variation method, together with the use of the finite-range density-dependent Gogny force, provides an excellent tool for the description of odd-even and even-even nuclei. We apply the theory to the Magnesium isotopic chain and obtain an outstanding description of the ground-state properties, in particular binding energies, odd-even mass differences, mass radii and electromagnetic moments among others.

## Key claim

The authors present the first systematic description of both odd and even Magnesium isotopes using a symmetry-conserving beyond-mean-field approach with the Gogny force, achieving excellent agreement with experimental data for ground-state properties. However, the theoretical binding energies show systematic overbinding because the D1S parameterization of the Gogny force was originally fitted to reproduce experimental data with the simpler HFB method, leaving insufficient room for beyond-mean-field effects.

## Method

The authors start from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory with exact blocking for odd nuclei, then perform particle-number variation-after-projection (PN-VAP) constrained to specific deformation parameters (β,γ) across a grid of 116 points. They subsequently apply simultaneous particle-number and angular-momentum projection (PNAMP) to each state in the grid, solving the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation to obtain the projected potential energy surface and identify the ground-state minimum for each isotope.

## Key numbers

- The (β,γ) grid contains 116 points, with the harmonic oscillator basis including N_shells = 8 shells and oscillator length b = 1.01A^(1/6)
- Angular momentum projection uses 32 integration points for each Euler angle (N_α = N_β = N_γ = 32), and particle-number projection uses 11 integration points
- For ²¹Mg, the theoretical spin and parity is I^π = 5/2⁺, with the I = |K| = 1/2 state lying only 200 keV above the ground state
- The theoretical spectroscopic quadrupole moment for ²³Mg is 13.89 efm², compared to the experimental value of 11.4(2) efm²
- The theoretical spectroscopic quadrupole moment for ²⁵Mg is 22.47 efm², compared to the experimental value of 20.1(3) efm²
- The odd-even mass differences show that odd-even nuclei have about 0.5 MeV less pairing than even-even nuclei on average
- The additional overbinding from using 8 harmonic oscillator shells is estimated at 2.3 to 2.7 MeV

## Context

This work extends beyond-mean-field theories (BMFT) from even-even nuclei to odd-even nuclei, following earlier developments with the Skyrme force by Bally (2014) and building on symmetry-conserving configuration mixing (SCCM) approaches for even-even nuclei using the Gogny force by Rodriguez and Egido (2010). The method enables systematic calculation of ground-state properties across an entire isotopic chain including binding energies, odd-even mass differences, mass radii, and electromagnetic moments, with the Magnesium chain chosen for abundant experimental data. The results open the possibility of applying this approach to other isotopic chains and performing generator-coordinate method (GCM) mixing in the (β,γ) plane for softer nuclei like ²⁰Mg.
