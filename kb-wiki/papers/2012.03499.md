# Interpretation of the quasiparticle plus triaxial rotor model

> We discuss in depth the application of the classical concepts for interpreting the quantal results from the triaxial rotor core without and with odd-particle. The corresponding limitations caused by the discreteness and finiteness of the angular momentum Hilbert space and the extraction of the relevant features from the complex wave function and distributions of various angular momentum components are discussed in detail. New methods based on spin coherent states and spin squeezed states are introduced. It is demonstrated that the spin coherent state map is a powerful tool to visualize the angular momentum geometry of rotating nuclei. The topological nature of the concepts of transverse and longitudinal wobbling is clarified and the transitional axis-flipregime is analysed for the first time.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that spin coherent state (SCS) maps provide a powerful tool to visualize the angular momentum geometry of rotating nuclei, and it clarifies the topological nature of transverse and longitudinal wobbling, analyzing the transitional axis-flip regime for the first time. However, the authors caution that the classical vector picture is an oversimplification due to the non-commutativity of angular momentum components and the discreteness of the Hilbert space, meaning the SCS representation is a more accurate "most classical" representation.

## Method
The authors apply the quasiparticle-plus-triaxial rotor (PTR) model to study rotating nuclei, using spin coherent states (SCS) and spin squeezed states (SSS) to map quantal wave functions onto a classical angular momentum geometry. They analyze the angular momentum distributions by constructing reduced density matrices, averaging over degrees of freedom not of interest, and compare the full PTR solutions with approximate treatments like the Holstein-Primakoff boson expansion used by Tanabe and Sugawara-Tanabe (2017). The study focuses on the triaxial rotor model (TRM) without and with an odd particle, using the SCS basis to visualize the structure of the total angular momentum and the high-j orbital angular momentum.

## Key numbers
- The critical angular momentum for the stability of the transverse wobbling (TW) mode is given as $I_c = 10.5$, derived from the condition $c > 0$ in the harmonic approximation.
- The grid steps used for discretizing the differential equations in the harmonic approximation are $\Delta_K = \Delta_k = 0.0625$, with $n_K = I / \Delta_K$ and $n_k = j / \Delta_k$.
- The paper references the triaxial deformation parameter $\gamma = -26^\circ$ (and its rotated value $\gamma = 246^\circ$) for generating K-plots for the m-axis.

## Context
This work extends the classical vector model of rotating nuclei, as reviewed by Frauendorf (2018), by providing a rigorous quantal-to-classical mapping via spin coherent states. It directly addresses the limitations of the precession-cone picture and clarifies the terminology of transverse and longitudinal wobbling originally suggested by Frauendorf and Donau (2014), contrasting it with alternative terminology proposed by Tanabe and Sugawara-Tanabe (2017), Raduta et al. (2020), and Lawrie et al. (2020). The methods introduced enable a more quantitative analysis of angular momentum geometry in complex nuclear systems, applicable to rotational alignment, magnetic rotation, and chiral doubling.
