# Numerical models for stationary superfluid neutron stars in general relativity with realistic equations of state

> We present a numerical model for uniformly rotating superfluid neutron stars, for the first time with realistic microphysics including entrainment, in a fully general relativistic framework. We compute stationary and axisymmetric configurations of neutron stars composed of two fluids, namely superfluid neutrons and charged particles (protons and electrons), rotating with different rates around a common axis. Both fluids are coupled by entrainment, a non-dissipative interaction which in case of a non-vanishing relative velocity between the fluids, causes the fluid momenta being not aligned with the respective fluid velocities. We extend the formalism by Comer and Joynt (2003) in order to calculate the equation of state (EoS) and entrainment parameters for an arbitrary relative velocity. The resulting entrainment matrix fulfills all necessary sum rules and in the limit of small relative velocity our results agree with Fermi liquid theory ones, derived to lowest order in the velocity. This formalism is applied to two new nuclear equations of state which are implemented in the numerical model. We are able to obtain precise equilibrium configurations. Resulting density profiles and moments of inertia are discussed employing both EoSs, showing the impact of entrainment and the dependence on the EoS.

## Key claim

The authors present the first fully general relativistic numerical model for uniformly rotating superfluid neutron stars that incorporates realistic microphysics including entrainment, using two new nuclear equations of state. The model extends the formalism of Comer and Joynt (2003) to calculate the equation of state and entrainment parameters for arbitrary relative velocities between the two fluids, with the condition that superfluidity is maintained.

## Method

The authors model the neutron star as a two-fluid system composed of superfluid neutrons and charged particles (protons and electrons) in a stationary, axisymmetric, and circular spacetime using quasi-isotropic coordinates. They extend the covariant formalism of Carter and collaborators to compute the equation of state and entrainment matrix by choosing the neutron rest frame rather than the neutron zero-momentum frame, allowing calculation to any order in the relative velocity between the fluids. The numerical resolution employs the self-consistent-field method implemented in the LORENE library using multi-domain spectral methods, with four computational domains to handle the transition from two-fluid to one-fluid regions near the stellar surface.

## Key numbers

- The saturation density of infinite symmetric nuclear matter is given as ρ₀ ≈ 2.8 × 10¹⁴ g·cm⁻³
- The outer core composition considered corresponds to densities ranging from approximately ρ₀/2 to approximately 2-3 ρ₀
- Neutron and proton masses used are mₙ = 939.6 MeV and mₚ = 938.8 MeV
- The superfluid critical temperature is estimated to be of the order of ∼10⁹-10¹⁰ K
- The fastest observed pulsar rotation frequency is 716 Hz, corresponding to a surface velocity at the equator of approximately c/6 assuming R ∼ 12 km
- The maximum lag between fluids during a glitch is roughly given by ΔΩ_max ≈ ΔΩ_p/Ω_p × Ω, where ΔΩ_p/Ω_p ∼ 10⁻¹¹ to 10⁻⁵ is the glitch amplitude

## Context

This work extends the numerical models of Prix et al. (2005), who built the first complete numerical solutions of stationary rotating superfluid neutron stars for any rotation rates but only used polytropic equations of state. The authors implement two new realistic density-dependent relativistic mean-field equations of state, following the approach of Typel (1999) and Avancini (2009), adapted to a two-fluid system with entrainment. This enables future dynamical simulations of neutron star oscillations or collapse to black holes using realistic initial states, and provides a foundation for studying pulsar glitches and superfluid neutron star oscillations with proper microphysics.
