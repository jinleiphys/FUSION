# Nonperturbative resummation of de Sitter infrared logarithms in the large-N limit

> We study the O(N) scalar field theory with quartic self-coupling in de Sitter space. When the field is light in units of the expansion rate, perturbative methods break down at very low momenta due to large infrared logarithmic terms. Using the nonperturbative large-N limit, we compute the four-point vertex function in the deep infrared regime. The resummation of an infinite series of perturbative (bubble) diagrams leads to a modified power law which is analogous to the generation of an anomalous dimension in critical phenomena. We discuss in detail the role of high momentum (subhorizon) modes, including the issue of renormalization, and show that they influence the dynamics of infrared (superhorizon) modes only through a constant renormalization factor. This provides an explicit example of effective decoupling between high and low energy physics in an expanding space-time.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the infinite series of nonlocal bubble diagrams contributing to the four-point vertex function in the large-\(N\) limit of \(O(N)\) scalar field theory on de Sitter space can be exactly resummed, producing a modified power law in the deep infrared regime analogous to an anomalous dimension in critical phenomena, but only under the assumption that high-momentum (subhorizon) modes are neglected or contribute only through a constant renormalization factor.

## Method

The authors study the \(O(N)\) scalar field theory with quartic self-coupling in de Sitter space using the nonperturbative large-\(N\) limit, which reduces the self-energy to a local masslike tadpole diagram and expresses the four-point vertex function in terms of a two-point correlator of the composite field \(\chi = \lambda\varphi_a\varphi_a/6N\). They employ the physical momentum (\(p\)-)representation for de Sitter correlators, which exploits the scaling law \(\tilde F_c(\eta,\eta',K) = \hat F(p,p')/K\) with \(p = -K\eta\), to write integral equations for the statistical and spectral components of the bubble-sum function \(\hat I(p,p')\) and solve them analytically in the deep infrared regime \(p,p' \ll 1\) for light fields with \(M^2 \ll 1\).

## Key numbers

* The self-consistent mass for vanishing tree-level mass is \(M^2 = \sqrt{\frac{\lambda F_\nu}{12}\frac{d\Omega_d}{(2\pi)^d}}\), where \(F_\nu = [2^\nu\Gamma(\nu)]^2/4\pi\) and \(\Omega_d = 2\pi^{d/2}/\Gamma(d/2)\), giving a nonanalytic dependence on the coupling \(\lambda\).
* The parameter \(\varepsilon = d/2 - \nu\) satisfies \(\varepsilon = \sigma/d = \alpha(d/2)\sqrt{\lambda}/(12\pi)\) for \(d=3\) with \(\alpha(3/2)=1\), where \(\sigma\) is the self-energy correction.
* The resummed spectral function \(\hat I_\rho^{\rm IR}(p,p')\) has the form \(\frac{\pi_\rho}{\sqrt{pp'}}\mathcal{P}_{\bar\nu}^{\varepsilon}(\ln p/p')\) with \(\bar\nu = \sqrt{\nu^2 - \pi_\rho}\) and \(\pi_\rho = 2\sigma\), changing the infrared exponent from \(\nu\) to \(\bar\nu\).
* The resummed statistical function \(\hat I_F^{\rm IR}(p,p')\) behaves as \(\frac{\pi_F}{(pp')^{\bar\kappa + 1/2}}\) with \(\bar\kappa = \bar\nu - \varepsilon\), where the single-bubble exponent \(\kappa = d/2 - 2\varepsilon\) is modified to \(\bar\kappa\).

## Context

This work extends the large-\(N\) approach to de Sitter space previously applied to two-point functions by Riotto (2008) and Serreau (2011), which described dynamical mass generation screening perturbative infrared divergences, to the four-point vertex function where nonlocal logarithmic divergences appear. The resummation technique parallels the treatment of infrared logarithms in flat-space critical phenomena and high-temperature field theory, and the demonstration that ultraviolet modes only contribute a constant renormalization factor provides an explicit example of effective decoupling between high and low energy physics in expanding space-time, enabling further nonperturbative studies of de Sitter quantum field theory.
