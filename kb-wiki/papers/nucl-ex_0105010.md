# Test of low-energy theorems for p(\vec{gamma},π^{0})p in the threshold region

> The photon asymmetry in the reaction p(\vecγ,π^{0})p close to threshold has been measured for the first time with the photon spectrometer TAPS using linearly polarized photons from the tagged-photon facility at the Mainz Microtron MAMI. The total and differential cross sections were also measured simultaneously with the photon asymmetry. This allowed determination of the S-wave and all three P-wave amplitudes. The low-energy theorems based on the parameter-free third-order calculations of heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory for P1 and P2 agree with the experimental values.

## Key claim

The low-energy theorems (LETs) of heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) at third order for the P-wave combinations \(P_1\) and \(P_2\) agree with the experimental values extracted from the first simultaneous measurement of total cross section, differential cross section, and photon asymmetry for \(p(\vec{\gamma},\pi^{0})p\) near threshold; the agreement holds within systematic and statistical errors, but the experimental value for \(P_3\) is higher than the ChPT prediction, which is attributed to the smaller cross sections used in the ChPT determination of the low-energy constant \(b_P\).

## Method

The experiment was performed at the Mainz Microtron MAMI using a 405 MeV continuous-wave electron beam and the Glasgow/Mainz tagged-photon facility to produce linearly polarized photons via coherent bremsstrahlung in a diamond radiator, with degrees of polarization up to 50% in the energy range from \(\pi^0\) threshold to 166 MeV. Neutral pions produced in a liquid hydrogen target were detected via their two-photon decay in the TAPS spectrometer (six blocks of BaF\(_2\) crystals plus a forward wall of phoswich telescopes), and the total cross section, differential cross section, and photon asymmetry were measured simultaneously. Multipole fits to the observables, assuming only S-wave (\(E_{0+}\)) and P-wave (\(M_{1+}, M_{1-}, E_{1+}\)) contributions, were performed using two different energy-dependence assumptions for the P-wave amplitudes (proportional to \(qk\) or proportional to \(q\)) to extract the threshold values of \(E_{0+}\) and the three P-wave combinations \(P_1\), \(P_2\), and \(P_3\).

## Key numbers

- Threshold value of the S-wave amplitude: \(E_{0+} = (-1.33 \pm 0.08_{\text{stat}} \pm 0.03_{\text{sys}}) \times 10^{-3} / m_{\pi^+}\).
- Threshold value of P-wave combination \(P_1 = (9.47 \pm 0.08_{\text{stat}} \pm 0.29_{\text{sys}}) \times 10^{-3} \, q / m_{\pi^+}^2\).
- Threshold value of P-wave combination \(P_2 = (-9.46 \pm 0.1_{\text{stat}} \pm 0.29_{\text{sys}}) \times 10^{-3} \, q / m_{\pi^+}^2\).
- Threshold value of P-wave combination \(P_3 = (11.48 \pm 0.06_{\text{stat}} \pm 0.35_{\text{sys}}) \times 10^{-3} \, q / m_{\pi^+}^2\).
- Average value of the cusp parameter \(\beta = (3.8 \pm 1.4) \times 10^{-3} / m_{\pi^+}^2\), consistent with the unitary value of \(3.61 \times 10^{-3} / m_{\pi^+}^2\).
- Photon asymmetry measured at a mean energy of 159.5 MeV; the energy dependence of the ChPT prediction for the asymmetry was less than 2% at 90 degrees over the range threshold to 166 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier measurements of total and differential cross sections for \(p(\gamma,\pi^{0})p\) near threshold performed at Mainz (Fuchs et al., 1996) and Saskatoon (Bergstrom et al., 1996), which had confirmed a strong energy dependence of \(E_{0+}\) but could not separate the three P-wave amplitudes. By adding the photon asymmetry measurement, the authors achieved the first full separation of \(P_1\), \(P_2\), and \(P_3\), enabling a direct test of the parameter-free third-order heavy-baryon ChPT predictions for \(P_1\) and \(P_2\) (Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner, 1996). The results also provide a new determination of the low-energy constant \(b_P\) for \(P_3\), which was subsequently used in a fourth-order ChPT calculation (Bernard et al., 2001) that showed cancellation of Delta-isobar contributions by loop corrections, confirming the third-order LETs for \(P_1\) and \(P_2\).
