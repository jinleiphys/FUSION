# Solar, Supernova, and Atmospheric Neutrinos

> In these Canberra summer school lectures we treat a number of topical issues in neutrino astrophysics: the solar neutrino problem, including the physics of the standard solar model, helioseismology, and the possibility that the solution involves new particle physics; atmospheric neutrinos; Dirac and Majorana neutrino masses and their consequences for low-energy weak interactions; red giant evolution as a test of new particle astrophysics; the supernova mechanism; spin-flavor oscillations and oscillations into sterile states, including the effects of density fluctuations; and neutrino-induced and explosive nucleosynthesis.

## Key claim

The combined results from solar neutrino experiments (Homestake, SAGE, GALLEX, Kamiokande, and SuperKamiokande) are very difficult to explain by any plausible change in the standard solar model, leading most researchers to believe the solution involves new particle physics, most likely neutrino oscillations associated with massive neutrinos.

## Method

The authors review the standard solar model (SSM) assumptions and predictions for neutrino fluxes from the pp chain and CNO cycle, then compare these predictions to measurements from radiochemical detectors (37Cl and 71Ga) and water Cerenkov detectors (Kamiokande and SuperKamiokande). They analyze the uncertainties in SSM input parameters, particularly nuclear S-factors for reactions such as 7Be(p,gamma)8B and 3He(alpha,gamma)7Be, and perform Monte Carlo variations of five input parameters to map the allowed flux correlations.

## Key numbers

* Homestake 37Cl capture rate: 2.56 +/- 0.16 +/- 0.16 SNU (1 SNU = 10^-36 captures/atom/s), about 1/3 of the SSM prediction
* SAGE 71Ga capture rate: 66 +/- 13 +/- 6 SNU; GALLEX 71Ga capture rate: 76 +/- 8 SNU; SSM prediction approximately 130 SNU
* Kamiokande II/III 8B neutrino flux: (2.91 +/- 0.24 +/- 0.35) x 10^6 /cm^2/s
* SuperKamiokande 8B neutrino flux after 504 days: (2.44 +/- 0.05 +0.09/-0.07) x 10^6 /cm^2/s, about half the SSM flux
* Recommended S17(0) value: 19 +4/-2 eV b (1 sigma) for the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction
* Recommended S34(0) value: 0.53 +/- 0.05 keV b for the 3He(alpha,gamma)7Be reaction
* SSM central temperature Tc ~ 1.55 x 10^7 K; phi(8B) ~ Tc^18, phi(7Be)/phi(8B) ~ Tc^-10
* Minimum theoretical 71Ga capture rate: 79 SNU, given standard model weak interaction physics

## Context

This paper extends the long-standing solar neutrino problem first identified by Davis (1970s) and reviews the standard solar model calculations of Bahcall, Basu, and Pinsonneault (1998) and Brun, Turck-Chieze, and Morel (1998). The work enables future experiments such as SuperKamiokande and SNO to demonstrate whether neutrino oscillations are occurring, and connects to the contemporaneous SuperKamiokande evidence for atmospheric neutrino oscillations. The nuclear physics uncertainties discussed draw heavily on the Institute for Nuclear Theory workshop results published in Reviews of Modern Physics.
