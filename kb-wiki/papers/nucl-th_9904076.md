# Strange chiral nucleon form factors

> We investigate the strange electric and magnetic form factors of the nucleon in the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to third order in the chiral expansion. All counterterms can be fixed from data. In particular, the two unknown singlet couplings can be deduced from the parity-violating electron scattering experiments performed by the SAMPLE and the HAPPEX collaborations. Within the given uncertainties, our analysis leads to a small and positive electric strangeness radius, $<r_{E,s}^2> = (0.05 \pm 0.09) fm^2$. We also deduce the consequences for the upcoming MAMI A4 experiment.

## Key claim

Within heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to third order, the two unknown singlet low-energy constants can be fixed from the SAMPLE and HAPPEX parity-violating electron scattering data, yielding a small and positive strange electric radius; however, the momentum transfer of the HAPPEX experiment (Q² = 0.48 GeV²) may be too large to trust the third-order chiral treatment.

## Method

The authors calculate the strange electric and magnetic Sachs form factors of the nucleon using SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT) to third order in the chiral expansion (O(p³)). They separate the calculation into singlet and octet current contributions, where the octet components are fixed from the known proton and neutron electromagnetic form factors and the two singlet low-energy constants (b₀ and d₀¹⁰²) are determined by fitting to the SAMPLE and HAPPEX experimental results. The strange form factors are then expressed as combinations of the singlet and octet components.

## Key numbers

- Strange electric radius: ⟨r²_{E,s}⟩ = (0.05 ± 0.09) fm²
- Strange magnetic moment central value: μ_N^{(s, cv)} = 0.18 (in nuclear magnetons)
- Strange magnetic radius: ⟨r²_{M,s}⟩ = -0.14 fm²
- Singlet LEC b₀ = 0.06 ± 0.44
- Singlet LEC d₀¹⁰² = -2.20 ± 0.20
- SAMPLE measurement at Q²_S = 0.1 GeV²: G_SAMPLE^{(s)} = 0.23 ± 0.44
- HAPPEX measurement at Q²_H = 0.48 GeV²: G_HAPPEX^{(s)} = 0.023 ± 0.048
- Prediction for MAMI A4 experiment at Q²_M = 0.23 GeV²: G_MAMI^{(s)} = 0.007 (for central LEC values)

## Context

This work extends the earlier leading-order chiral perturbation theory analysis of Hemmert, Meißner, and Steininger (1998) by including all terms to third order and fixing the singlet low-energy constants from the first SAMPLE and HAPPEX experimental data. The results contradict many model calculations that predicted a negative strange magnetic moment, and the small positive strange electric radius is more compatible with models including π-ρ or K̅K continuum contributions (such as those by Meißner, Müller, and Steininger or Hammer and Meißner) than with dispersive approaches assuming maximal OZI violation (such as those by Jaffe or Hogaasen and Myhrer). The analysis enables predictions for the upcoming MAMI A4 experiment and provides a framework for higher-order calculations that could incorporate decuplet baryons.
