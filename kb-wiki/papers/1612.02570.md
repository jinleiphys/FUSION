# Nuclear excitation by two-photon electron transition

> A new mechanism of nuclear excitation via two-photon electron transitions (NETP) is proposed and studied theoretically. As a generic example, detailed calculations are performed for the $E1E1$ $1s2s\,^1S_0 \rightarrow 1s^2\,^1S_0$ two-photon decay of He-like $^{225}$Ac$^{87+}$ ion with the resonant excitation of the $3/2+$ nuclear state with the energy 40.09(5) keV. The probability for such a two-photon decay via the nuclear excitation is found to be $P_{\rm NETP} = 3.5 \times 10^{-9}$ and, thus, is comparable with other mechanisms, such as nuclear excitation by electron transition and by electron capture. The possibility for the experimental observation of the proposed mechanism is thoroughly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new mechanism for nuclear excitation via two-photon electron transitions (NETP) that, unlike the previously known NEET and NEEC mechanisms, does not require fine-tuning of atomic and nuclear transition energies to each other because the continuous energy spectrum of the two-photon electron decay automatically provides photons matching the nuclear resonance energy, provided the nuclear excitation energy is smaller than the total electron transition energy.

## Method

The authors derived the third-order S-matrix element within relativistic bound-state QED for the NETP process, treating the combined electron-nucleus system with an intermediate cascade state consisting of the excited nucleus and electrons in their ground level. They performed detailed calculations for the E1E1 two-photon decay of the 1s2s ¹S₀ state to the 1s² ¹S₀ ground state in He-like ²²⁵Ac⁸⁷⁺ ion, using the dual-kinetic-balance finite basis set method to represent the Dirac spectrum and employing experimental data for the reduced nuclear transition probability B(EL) of the 3/2+ nuclear state at 40.09(5) keV.

## Key numbers

- Total rate of the 1s2s ¹S₀ two-photon decay in He-like ²²⁵Ac⁸⁷⁺: W = 6.002 × 10¹² s⁻¹, corresponding to a lifetime τ = 0.167 ps.
- Total electron transition energy ΔE = 89.218(2) keV for the 1s2s ¹S₀ → 1s² ¹S₀ transition in ²²⁵Ac⁸⁷⁺.
- Nuclear excitation energy of the 3/2+ state in ²²⁵Ac: ω_ES = 40.09(5) keV.
- Radiative deexcitation rate of the nuclear excited state: W_ES = 0.41 × 10⁹ s⁻¹, with linewidth Γ_ES = 2.7 × 10⁻⁷ eV.
- NETP rate for He-like ²²⁵Ac⁸⁷⁺: W_NETP = 0.21 × 10⁵ s⁻¹.
- Relative NETP probability: P_NETP = 3.5 × 10⁻⁹, defined as W_NETP / W_{1s2s ¹S₀}.
- Signal-to-background ratios for γ₁ photon detection: p_NETP(1 eV) = 1 × 10⁻⁴, p_NETP(10 eV) = 1 × 10⁻⁵, p_NETP(100 eV) = 1 × 10⁻⁶ for detector energy resolutions Δ of 1 eV, 10 eV, and 100 eV, respectively.
- Nuclear excited state half-lifetime in neutral actinium: 0.72(3) ns; for He-like ²²⁵Ac⁸⁷⁺ the nuclear excited state lifetime is 2.4 ns.

## Context

This work extends the family of nuclear excitation mechanisms beyond the previously known NEET process proposed by Morita (1973) and the NEEC process proposed by Goldanskii and Namiot (1976). The NEET process has been experimentally verified only for ¹⁹⁷Au (Kishimoto, 2000), ¹⁸⁹Os (Aoki, 2001), and ¹⁹³Ir (Kishimoto, 2005), all requiring near-degeneracy of atomic and nuclear transition energies. The NETP mechanism removes this constraint by exploiting the continuous spectrum of two-photon electron decay, enabling resonant nuclear excitation for any nuclear level with energy below the total electron transition energy. The predicted probability P_NETP = 3.5 × 10⁻⁹ is comparable to NEET probabilities in previously observed and proposed scenarios (Tkalya, 1992; Harston, 2001). The authors propose experimental verification at the GSI facility using time-delayed detection of nuclear fluorescence photons, which would enable applications including studies of low-lying isomeric states such as ²²⁹ᵐTh (Inamura, 2009; Raeder, 2011; von der Wense, 2016) and ²³⁵ᵐU (Chodash, 2016), isotope separation, and controlled energy release from nuclear isomers.
