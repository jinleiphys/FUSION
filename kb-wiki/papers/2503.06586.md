# Non-hermitian Green's function theory with $N$-body interactions: the coupled-cluster similarity transformation

> We present the diagrammatic theory of the irreducible self-energy and Bethe-Salpeter kernel that naturally arises within the Green's function formalism for a general $N$-body non-hermitian interaction. In this work, we focus specifically on the coupled-cluster self-energy generated by the similarity transformation of the electronic structure Hamiltonian. We develop the biorthogonal quantum theory to construct dynamical correlation functions where the time-dependence of operators is governed by a non-hermitian Hamiltonian. We extend the Gell-Mann and Low theorem to include non-hermitian interactions and to generate perturbative expansions of many-body Green's functions. We introduce the single-particle coupled-cluster Green's function and derive the perturbative diagrammatic expansion for the non-hermitian coupled-cluster self-energy in terms of the `non-interacting' reference Green's function, $\tildeΣ[G_0]$. From the exact equation-of-motion of the single-particle coupled-cluster Green's function, we derive the self-consistent renormalized coupled-cluster self-energy, $\tildeΣ[\tilde{G}]$, and demonstrate its relationship to the perturbative expansion of the self-energy, $\tildeΣ[G_0]$. Subsequently, we show that the usual electronic self-energy can be recovered from the coupled-cluster self-energy by neglecting the effects of the similarity transformation. We show how the coupled-cluster ground state energy can be obtained from the coupled-cluster self-energy and provide an overview of the relationship between approximations for the coupled-cluster self-energy, IP/EA-EOM-CC and the $G_0W_0$ approximation. As a result, we introduce the CC-$G_0W_0$ self-energy by leveraging the connections between Green's function and coupled-cluster theory. Finally, we derive the diagrammatic expansion of the coupled-cluster Bethe-Salpeter kernel.

## Key claim
The paper presents a formally exact Green's function formalism for non-hermitian many-body quantum systems, specifically focusing on the coupled-cluster similarity transformed Hamiltonian. The authors claim that this formalism unifies coupled-cluster and Green's function theories by constructing the irreducible self-energy and Bethe-Salpeter kernel that naturally arise from the non-hermitian N-body interaction, though the practical utility depends on truncation schemes for the coupled-cluster amplitudes.

## Method
The authors develop a biorthogonal quantum theory to construct dynamical correlation functions where time-dependence is governed by a non-hermitian Hamiltonian, extending the Gell-Mann and Low theorem to non-hermitian interactions. They define the single-particle coupled-cluster Green's function using the similarity transformed Hamiltonian and derive both perturbative and self-consistent renormalized expressions for the coupled-cluster self-energy from the exact equation-of-motion. The diagrammatic expansion of the coupled-cluster self-energy is derived in terms of the non-interacting reference Green's function, and the relationship between the perturbative and self-consistent formulations is demonstrated.

## Context
This work extends the conventional Green's function formalism, previously established for hermitian two-body interactions (Hedin 1965) and recently three-body interactions (Carbone 2013, Raimondi 2018), to include general N-body non-hermitian interactions. The formalism builds upon prior work by Coveney and Tew (2023) on the coupled-cluster self-energy within Brueckner coupled-cluster theory, and provides a rigorous foundation for connections between Green's function theory and coupled-cluster methods that were previously explored through the IP/EA-EOM-CC approach (Nooijen 1992, 1993) and approximate G0W0 self-energy connections (Quintero 2022, Monino 2023). This work enables the systematic construction of coupled-cluster self-energy approximations and their relationship to standard many-body perturbation theory approximations such as GF2 and GW.
