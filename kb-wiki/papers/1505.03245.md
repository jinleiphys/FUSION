# Microscopic description of large amplitude collective motion in the nuclear astrophysics context

> In the last 10 years, we have observed an important increase of interest in the application of time-dependent energy density functional theory (TD-EDF). This approach allows to treat nuclear structure and nuclear reaction from small to large amplitude dynamics in a unified framework. The possibility to perform unrestricted three-dimensional simulations using state of the art effective interactions has opened new perspectives. In the present article, an overview of applications where the predictive power of TD-EDF has been benchmarked is given. A special emphasize is made on processes that are of astrophysical interest. Illustrations discussed here include giant resonances, fission, binary and ternary collisions leading to fusion, transfer and deep inelastic processes.

## Key claim

Time-Dependent Energy Density Functional (TD-EDF) theory provides a unified framework for describing both small and large amplitude collective motion in nuclei, and its predictive power has been benchmarked across processes of astrophysical interest including giant resonances, fission, and binary/ternary collisions; however, the approach cannot describe sub-barrier fusion directly due to its semi-classical nature and cannot evolve a nucleus from a compact shape through fission without assuming adiabatic dynamics from an already elongated configuration.

## Method

The authors review applications of TD-EDF, which solves the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (TDHFB) equation \(i \hbar \frac{d}{dt} {\cal R}(t) = [{\cal H}({\cal R}), {\cal R}(t)]\) for the generalized density \({\cal R}\) and self-consistent mean-field \({\cal H}\), using zero-range effective interactions adjusted to infinite nuclear matter and finite nuclei. For fission, the system is assumed to follow adiabatic dynamics from a compact shape to an elongated shape outside the barrier, after which TD-EDF evolution is performed from the deformed configuration. For binary collisions, fusion cross-sections are estimated via the semi-classical approximation \(\sigma_{\rm Fus} (E_{\rm c.m.}) \simeq \frac{\pi\hbar^2}{2 \mu E_{\rm c.m.}} [l_{\rm max}(E_{\rm c.m.}) +1]^2\), and nucleon transfer probabilities are extracted using particle number projection techniques.

## Key numbers

* More than 700 nuclei were considered in a systematic study of the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (GQR) using TD-EDF with pairing, investigating the splitting of collective energy as a function of deformation parameter \(\delta\).
* For binary reactions, TD-EDF is applicable for beam energies lower than 10-15 MeV/A.
* The reaction \(^{48}\)Ca+\(^{40}\)Ca was simulated at beam energy \(E=48.6\) MeV to study transfer reactions below the Coulomb barrier.
* Fission of \(^{258}\)Fm was simulated along three different paths: symmetric compact, symmetric elongated, and asymmetric elongated, with adiabatic surfaces shown for each path.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on TD-EDF that began with early promising applications in the late 1970s and early 1980s (Neg82), but was largely abandoned for over 25 years until recent groups developed unrestricted 3D codes with advanced functionals (Kim97, Sim01, Nak05, Mar05, Uma05, Was08) and pairing correlations (Ave08, Eba10, Ste11, Sca12, Has12, Sca13). The paper enables future work toward a fully microscopic understanding of fission, sub-barrier fusion via extracted nucleus-nucleus potentials (using density-constrained TDHF from Uma06 and dissipative-dynamics TDHF from Lac02), and reactions involving exotic nuclei or ternary processes such as the triple-alpha fusion to form \(^{12}\)C (Uma10).
