# The Reaction $πN \to ππN$ at Threshold

> We consider the chiral expansion for the reaction $πN \to ππN$ in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. To order $M_π$ we derive novel low--energy theorems that compare favorably with recent determinations of the total cross sections for $π^+ p \to π^+ π^+ n$ and $π^- p \to π^0 π^0 n$.

## Key claim

The first two terms in the chiral expansion of the threshold amplitudes for πN → ππN lead to low-energy theorems that compare favorably with recent data for π⁺p → π⁺π⁺n and π⁻p → π⁰π⁰n, but only to order Mπ; the next-to-leading order ππ interaction does not appear at this order, so the results do not yet provide a model-independent link to the ππ scattering amplitude.

## Method

The authors used heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (two-flavor formulation) to compute the chiral expansion of the threshold amplitudes D₁ and D₂ for the reaction πN → ππN. They evaluated tree-level diagrams with insertions from the leading and next-to-leading order πN Lagrangians, keeping terms up to and including order Mπ, and also calculated the imaginary parts of the amplitudes from one-loop diagrams at order Mπ². The resulting amplitudes were used to compute total cross sections for the two channels with available data, using an analytic approximation for three-body phase space and flux factors valid in the threshold region.

## Key numbers

- D₁ = 2.4 fm³, D₂ = −6.8 fm³, using Fπ = 93 MeV, gπN = 13.4, m = 938.27 MeV, Mπ = 139.57 MeV
- 𝒜₃₂ = 2.4 Mπ⁻³, 𝒜₁₀ = 5.5 Mπ⁻³ (from the low-energy theorems), compared to experimental determinations of 𝒜₃₂ = 2.07 ± 0.10 Mπ⁻³ and 𝒜₁₀ = 6.55 ± 0.16 Mπ⁻³ from Bula (1994)
- Parametrized cross section for π⁺p → π⁺π⁺n: σ_tot(Tπ) = 0.225 μb × ((Tπ − 172.4 MeV)/(10 MeV))²
- Parametrized cross section for π⁻p → π⁰π⁰n: σ_tot(Tπ) = 0.442 μb × ((Tπ − 160.5 MeV)/(10 MeV))²
- The order Mπ corrections give approximately 50% of the leading term

## Context

This work extends the relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory tree-level calculation of Beringer (1993) by using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, which provides a strict one-to-one correspondence between loop and small momentum expansions that was problematic in the relativistic approach due to the nonvanishing nucleon mass in the chiral limit (Gasser and Leutwyler, 1988). The results enable future inclusion of loop diagrams and resonance exchange (e.g., Δ(1232)) to ultimately determine how accurately the elastic ππ amplitude can be extracted from single pion production data.
