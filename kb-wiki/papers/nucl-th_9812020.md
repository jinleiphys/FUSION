# Comment on piNN Coupling from High Precision np Charge Exchange at 162 MeV

> In this updated and expanded version of our delayed Comment we show that the np backward cross section, as presented by the Uppsala group, is seriously flawed (more than 25 sd.). The main reason is the incorrect normalization of the data. We show also that their extrapolation method, used to determine the charged piNN coupling constant, is a factor of about 10 less accurate than claimed by Ericson et al. The large extrapolation error makes the determination of the coupling constant by the Uppsala group totally uninteresting.

# Comment on piNN Coupling from High Precision np Charge Exchange at 162 MeV

## Key claim
The Uppsala group's determination of the charged pion-nucleon coupling constant from 31 np backward cross-section data at 162 MeV is invalid because the data are incorrectly normalized (more than 25 standard deviations from the established database) and the extrapolation method used is a factor of about 10 less accurate than claimed, making the result "totally uninteresting."

## Method
The authors compared the Uppsala np backward differential cross-section data against the Nijmegen energy-dependent partial-wave analysis (PWA) database of approximately 5000 np data below 500 MeV, as well as the VPI&SU PWA. They also examined the internal consistency of the Uppsala data by analyzing the three separately normalized angular regions and renormalizing them, and evaluated the claimed extrapolation error of the Difference Method against standard Chew extrapolation techniques and results from Arndt et al. (1990s).

## Key numbers
- The Uppsala data give a chi-squared of 264.0 against the Nijmegen PWA and 236.7 against the VPI&SU PWA for 31 data points.
- The shape of the Uppsala differential cross section is more than 25 standard deviations away from both the Nijmegen and VPI&SU databases.
- The charged coupling constant from energy-dependent PWA's is f_c^2 = 0.0748(3), and the pp pi^0 coupling constant is f_p^2 = 0.0753(5).
- The Uppsala group claimed f_c^2 = 0.0808 with an extrapolation error of 0.0003 and normalization error of 0.0017.
- The Nijmegen group's physical extrapolation analysis of backward np data at a single energy gives a total error of 0.003, which is 10 times larger than the claimed extrapolation error.
- Arndt et al. found values of f_c^2 from single-energy analyses varying from 0.061 to 0.091 with an average of 0.075 and an error of 0.009, which is 30 times the extrapolation error quoted by the Uppsala group.
- The Nijmegen PWA normalizes np differential cross sections with a typical uncertainty of about 0.5%, compared to the 4% normalization error used in the Uppsala Letter.

## Context
This Comment extends the long-standing debate over the charged piNN coupling constant, contradicting the claim by Ericson et al. (1998) that a dedicated single-energy measurement could determine the coupling constant accurately. It supports the modern determinations from energy-dependent PWA's by the Nijmegen group (Rentmeester et al., 1990s) and Arndt et al. (1990s), which use thousands of data points and yield f_c^2 around 0.075. The work enables future experiments to recognize that pole-extrapolation methods from small datasets cannot compete with comprehensive PWA's, and that proper normalization and internal consistency checks are essential.
