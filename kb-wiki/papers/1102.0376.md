# Two-phonon $γ$-vibrational states in rotating triaxial odd-$A$ nuclei

> Distribution of the two phonon $γ$ vibrational collectivity in the rotating triaxial odd-$A$ nucleus, $^{103}$Nb, that is one of the three nuclides for which experimental data were reported recently, is calculated in the framework of the particle vibration coupling model based on the cranked shell model plus random phase approximation. This framework was previously utilized for analyses of the zero and one phonon bands in other mass region and is applied to the two phonon band for the first time. In the present calculation, three sequences of two phonon bands share collectivity almost equally at finite rotation whereas the $K=Ω+4$ state is the purest at zero rotation.

# Two-phonon γ-vibrational states in rotating triaxial odd-A nuclei

## Key claim
The two-phonon γ-vibrational collectivity in the rotating triaxial odd-A nucleus ¹⁰³Nb is distributed almost equally among three sequences of two-phonon bands at finite rotation, whereas the K = Ω + 4 state is the purest at zero rotation. The calculated two-phonon states lie higher than the experimentally observed band, which the author attributes to the truncation of the model space to at most two phonons.

## Method
The author applies the particle vibration coupling (PVC) model based on the cranked Nilsson model plus random phase approximation (RPA) to the odd-A nucleus ¹⁰³Nb. The vacuum is the rotating yrast configuration of an even-even core, and γ-vibrational phonons are constructed in the rotating frame allowing static triaxial deformation. The Hamiltonian is diagonalized in a space comprising one-quasiparticle states, one-quasiparticle states coupled to one γ-phonon, and one-quasiparticle states coupled to two γ-phonons, for each signature sector. The mean-field parameters (pairing gaps Δn = 1.05 MeV, Δp = 0.85 MeV, deformation ε2 = 0.31, triaxiality γ = −7°) are fixed from experimental data and from reproducing the measured signature splitting of the ground band.

## Key numbers
- Pairing gaps: Δn = 1.05 MeV (neutrons), Δp = 0.85 MeV (protons)
- Quadrupole deformation: ε2 = 0.31
- Triaxiality: γ = −7°
- The γ-vibrational energy in the adjacent even-even nucleus ¹⁰⁴Mo used to fix the residual interaction strength: 0.812 MeV
- The three two-phonon sequences each maintain approximately 60% collectivity at finite rotation
- At ωrot = 0, the K = Ω + 4 state is the purest two-phonon state
- The calculated two-phonon states lie higher than the observed 2γ band in ¹⁰³Nb

## Context
This work extends the author's previous PVC calculations, which were applied to signature-dependent properties of one-quasiparticle (zero-phonon) bands (Matsuzaki, 1990) and to E2 intensity relations in one-phonon γ-vibrational bands (Matsuzaki, 1995), to the two-phonon case for the first time. It provides a complementary mean-field-based approach to the triaxial projected shell model calculation of Sheikh et al. (2008) for the same nucleus, ¹⁰³Nb. The results suggest that future experiments may observe more than one sequence of two-phonon γ bands in odd-A nuclei, and that enlarging the model space beyond two phonons could improve agreement with observed excitation energies.
