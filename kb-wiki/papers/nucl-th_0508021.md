# Smallness of the imaginary part of the eta-nucleus scattering length

> In two recent analyses of eta meson production a very small imaginary part was obtained for the eta-He3 scattering length, which is in contradiction with most theoretical predictions. In this report a plausible explanation is given to this unexpectedly weak absorption by suppression of the two main inelasticity channels. It is stressed that the real and imaginary parts of the elementary scattering length do not necessarily always have the same isospin algebraic and spatial properties in a nuclear environment and caution should be exercised in their use in multiple scattering calculations and even constructing simple optical potentials.

## Key claim
The unexpectedly small imaginary part of the eta-He3 scattering length (consistent with zero in recent analyses) can be explained by suppression of the two main inelasticity channels: pionic inelasticity from eta N to pi N is suppressed by nuclear form factors and isospin structure, while nuclear absorption on nucleon pairs is dominated by isosinglet (quasideuteron) pairs and contributes only about 0.05-0.07 fm. The author stresses that the real and imaginary parts of the elementary scattering length do not necessarily share the same isospin and spatial properties in a nuclear environment.

## Method
The author examines two sources of inelasticity separately: the elementary pionic channel eta N to pi N and nuclear break-up channels. For the pionic channel, the isospin structure of the imaginary part (proportional to pi dot tau) is shown to yield no factor-of-three enhancement in isospin-1/2 nuclei like He-3, and the large momentum transfer (about 490 MeV/c) introduces a nuclear form factor suppression factor of about 0.04. For nuclear absorption, the author uses experimental cross section ratios from np to np eta versus pp to pp eta to argue that absorption on isospin-zero pairs dominates, then estimates the quasifree absorption eta d to np using the optical theorem and extrapolations from np to d eta data, with a correction factor of 1.5-2 for the He-3 environment.

## Key numbers
- The global fit to pd to eta He-3 data gave a scattering length a = +/- 4.3 +/- 0.3 + i (0.5 +/- 0.5) fm (Sibirtsev et al., 2004).
- A coupled-channel K-matrix analysis gave a = 4.24 +/- 0.29 + i 0.72 +/- 0.81 fm (Green and Wycech, 2003).
- The elementary eta N scattering length is approximately 0.5 +/- 0.2 + i (0.35 +/- 0.1) fm (Wycech et al., 1995).
- The nuclear form factor suppression factor for pionic inelasticity, using Gaussian distributions with r.m.s. radii 0.6 fm (nucleon) and 1.9 fm (He-3), is 0.04.
- The quasifree absorption eta d to np contributes about 0.01 fm from extrapolation to threshold (Calen et al., 1997) or 0.030-0.035 fm from lowest energy data (Calen et al., 1998).
- With a factor of 1.5-2 for the He-3 environment, the quasideuteron absorption contribution is estimated at 0.05-0.07 fm.
- The cross section ratio sigma(np to np eta) / sigma(pp to pp eta) is about 6.5 (Calen et al., 1998).
- The eta d to NNpi amplitude with final nucleons in a relative triplet P state and pion in p state is suppressed by a factor of 0.54 compared with s-wave emission and by 0.026 compared with elastic zero-energy scattering.

## Context
This work addresses a contradiction between theoretical predictions (which give imaginary parts of the eta-He3 scattering length ranging from 0.5 fm to 6 fm) and the very small imaginary part (consistent with zero) extracted from data by Sibirtsev et al. (2004) and Green and Wycech (2003). The explanation extends earlier work by Haider and Liu (2002) on conditions for eta-nuclear bound states and by Wycech et al. (1995) on the elementary eta N interaction. The result suggests that prospects for bound eta-nuclear states may be improved with a small imaginary part, and the arguments may generalize to other nuclei such as He-4 and to other mesonic interactions.
