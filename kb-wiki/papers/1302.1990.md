# Hot and Dense Hadron Gas (HG): A New Excluded-volume approach

> We formulate a thermodynamically consistent equation of state (EOS), based on excluded-volume approach, for a hot, dense hadron gas (HG). We calculate various thermodynamical quantities of HG and various hadron ratios and compare our model results with the results of other excluded-volume models and experimental data. We also calculate various transport coefficients such as $η/s$ etc. and compare them with other HG model results. Furthermore, we test the validity of our model in getting the rapidity spectra of various hadrons and the effect of flow on them is investigated by matching our predictions with the experimental data.

# Hot and Dense Hadron Gas (HG): A New Excluded-volume Approach

## Key claim
The authors formulate a thermodynamically consistent equation of state for a hot, dense hadron gas using an excluded-volume approach where baryons have a finite hard-core radius of 0.8 fm but mesons are treated as pointlike particles that can overlap and interpenetrate. The model claims to give a better fit to experimental data for hadron ratios and transport coefficients than other excluded-volume models, though the agreement with the URASiMA event generator results is limited to lower temperatures.

## Method
The grand canonical partition function for baryons is derived with an excluded-volume correction that subtracts the total eigenvolume of all baryons from the system volume, while mesons are treated as pointlike. The chemical freeze-out temperature and chemical potential are determined by fitting to experimental data, and then used to compute thermodynamic quantities, hadron ratios, transport coefficients (η/s), and rapidity spectra. Longitudinal flow is incorporated via a free parameter η_max related to the longitudinal flow velocity, and the condition of strangeness neutrality is imposed.

## Key numbers
- Hard-core radius for each baryon: r = 0.8 fm, corresponding to eigenvolume V⁰ = 4πr³/3.
- All baryons, mesons, and their resonances with masses up to 2 GeV/c² are included in the calculation of hadron gas pressure.
- For η/s calculations, a baryonic hard-core size of r = 0.5 fm is used.
- The expected QCD phase transition temperature range is T_c = 170–180 MeV, near which η/s shows a lower value in this model than in the model of Gorenstein et al. (2008).
- Rapidity distributions of π⁺ are shown for central Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier excluded-volume model used to determine the QCD phase boundary and critical end point (Singh 2009, Srivastava 2010). It compares results with the URASiMA event generator (Sasaki 2001), the Cleymans-Suhonen model (Cleymans 1987) for hadron ratios, and the transport coefficient calculations of Gorenstein et al. (2008). The model enables improved description of hadron spectra and collective flow in heavy-ion collisions, and provides a simpler thermodynamically consistent alternative to the approach of Rischke (1991).
