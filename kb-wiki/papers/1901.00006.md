# Hydrodynamics of Fermi arcs: Bulk flow and surface collective modes

> The hydrodynamic description of the Fermi arc surface states is proposed. In view of the strong suppression of scattering on impurities, the hydrodynamic regime for Fermi arc states should be, in principle, plausible. By using the kinetic theory, the Fermi arc hydrodynamics is derived and the corresponding effects on the bulk flow and surface collective modes are studied. For the bulk flow, the key effect of the proposed Fermi arc hydrodynamics is the modification of the corresponding boundary conditions. In a slab geometry, it is shown that, depending on the transfer rates between the surface and bulk, the hydrodynamic flow of the electron fluid inside the slab could be significantly altered and even enhanced near the surfaces. As to the spectrum of the surface collective modes, in agreement with earlier studies, it is found that the Fermi arcs allow for an additional gapless spectrum branch and a strong anisotropy of the surface plasmon dispersion relations in momentum space. The gapped modes are characterized by closed elliptic contours of constant frequency in momentum space.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that Fermi arc surface states in Weyl semimetals can sustain a hydrodynamic regime, and that this surface electron fluid modifies the boundary conditions for the bulk electron fluid, potentially enhancing bulk flow near surfaces depending on the surface-to-bulk transfer rates.

## Method

The authors derive hydrodynamic equations for Fermi arc surface states from kinetic theory, using a relaxation-time approximation for surface-bulk transitions. They solve the steady-state Navier-Stokes equation for bulk flow in a slab geometry with modified boundary conditions that couple to the surface fluid, and separately analyze surface collective modes by solving Poisson's equation with hydrodynamic equations in a semi-infinite geometry.

## Context

This work extends the consistent hydrodynamics framework for Weyl semimetals developed by Gorbar et al. (2017, 2018) by adding a hydrodynamic description of Fermi arc surface states. It builds on earlier studies of Fermi arc plasmons by Song (2017), Andolina (2018), and Losic (2018), which used phenomenological or quantum-mechanical approaches. The results enable experimental verification of electron hydrodynamics in Weyl semimetals via modified flow profiles and anisotropic surface plasmon dispersions measurable by near-field optical spectroscopy or electron energy-loss spectroscopy.
