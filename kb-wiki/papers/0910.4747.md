# Non-spherical proton shape and hydrogen hyperfine splitting

> We show that the non-spherical charge distribution of the proton manifests itself in hydrogen hyperfine splitting as an increase (in absolute value) of the proton Zemach radius and polarization contributions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the proton's non-spherical (prolate) charge distribution, inferred from the $N \to \Delta$ quadrupole transition, increases the absolute value of the Zemach radius and polarization contributions to hydrogen hyperfine splitting, thereby affecting the theoretical prediction at the level of a few parts per million.

## Method

The author decomposes the proton charge form factor into a spherically symmetric isoscalar part and a deformed part proportional to the neutron charge form factor, using SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry relations that connect the $N \to \Delta$ charge quadrupole transition form factor to the elastic neutron charge form factor. Analytical expressions for the Zemach radius are then derived by inserting dipole and Galster parametrizations for the form factors into Zemach's integral formula, separating the spherical and deformed contributions.

## Key numbers

- The Fermi energy is $E_F = 1,418,840.11$ kHz using recent fundamental constants.
- The experimental hydrogen hyperfine splitting is $E^{HFS}_{exp} = 1,420,405,751.7667 \pm 0.0009$ Hz.
- The transition quadrupole moment from experiment is $Q_{p \to \Delta^+}({\rm exp}) = -0.108(9)$ fm$^2$ (Bla01) and $-0.0846(33)$ fm$^2$ (Tia03), compared to the theory prediction $Q_{p \to \Delta^+}({\rm theory}) = -0.0820(20)$ fm$^2$ based on the neutron charge radius $r_n^2 = -0.1161(22)$ fm$^2$.
- The C2/M1 ratio at the real photon point is $C2/M1 = -0.035$, in good agreement with experimental determinations.
- The Zemach radius decomposition yields $r_Z = r_Z(sym) + r_Z(def) = 1.0627$ fm $+ 0.0464$ fm $= 1.1091$ fm.
- The corresponding Zemach correction is $\delta_Z = -(40.14 + 1.75)$ ppm $= -41.89$ ppm; including radiative corrections gives $\delta_Z = -42.52$ ppm.
- After including the Zemach correction, the discrepancy between theory and experiment reduces to $-4.04$ ppm.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies (Buchmann 2004, 2005) relating the $N \to \Delta$ quadrupole transition to nucleon ground state deformation, and applies those results to the precision observable of hydrogen hyperfine splitting. The analysis builds on the classic Zemach formalism (Zemach 1956) for finite-size corrections and uses SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry relations (Gursey and Radicati 1964) to connect elastic and inelastic form factors. The results enable more precise theoretical predictions for hydrogen hyperfine splitting and motivate improved low-momentum-transfer measurements of proton and neutron form factors (Arrington 2007) to further constrain the deformation contribution.
