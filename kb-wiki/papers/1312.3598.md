# Computational challenges in the relativistic few-nucleon problem

> I discuss computational challenges in the relativistic few-nucleon problem and the resolution of some of these challenges. I also discuss the outlook for the future.

## Key claim

Relativistic few-nucleon calculations can be performed using the Bakamjian-Thomas representation, which avoids explicit construction of complicated three-nucleon forces because the S-matrix of the non-cluster-representation dynamics is identical to that of the cluster-representation dynamics; however, this equivalence does not extend to the four-nucleon case unless the generated three-body interactions are included in the four-body mass operator.

## Method

The author constructs a relativistic mass operator for three nucleons by combining pairwise interactions that commute with nine kinematic Poincaré generators, then uses S-matrix-preserving unitary transformations (constructed from Cayley transforms of cluster-separating unitaries) to restore cluster properties. The relativistic Faddeev equations are formulated in terms of the non-cluster mass operator, and the kernel is expressed using non-relativistic transition matrix elements by exploiting the identity of internal wave functions between relativistic and non-relativistic models. The input two-nucleon interactions are obtained by embedding non-relativistic potentials (such as CD Bonn) into the relativistic mass operator via the Coester-Pieper-Serduke prescription.

## Key numbers

- Partial-wave-based three-nucleon calculations in double precision are limited to about 300 MeV; direct integration methods extend to the few-GeV scale.
- The Argonne V18 interaction, Fourier transformed into operator form, requires expansion in terms of 24 spin-isospin operators, reducible to 6 independent spin operators (5 on-shell, 1 additional off-shell).
- Relativistic effects in elastic p-d scattering at 250 MeV show small differences except at back angles, where some enhancement is observed.
- Breakup calculations using the spin-independent Malfliet-Tjon interaction at higher energies show dramatic crossing of relativistic and non-relativistic fivefold differential cross sections as the angle is changed.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic few-nucleon framework (Coester, Pieper, Serduke, 1975) to relativistic kinematics consistent with exact Poincaré symmetry, addressing the cluster property failure in the Bakamjian-Thomas approach (Bakamjian, Thomas, 1953) via S-matrix-preserving unitary transformations (Sokolov, 1977). The approach enables realistic three-nucleon calculations at few-GeV scales using existing non-relativistic potentials, and the discrepancy between calculated and measured large-angle elastic scattering cross sections suggests missing short-distance physics in three-nucleon forces. Future work will apply these methods to the few-GeV scale where chiral perturbation theory and perturbative QCD are not directly applicable.
