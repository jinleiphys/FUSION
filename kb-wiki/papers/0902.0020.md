# Construction of SO(5)>SO(3) spherical harmonics and Clebsch-Gordan coefficients

> The SO(5)>SO(3) spherical harmonics form a natural basis for expansion of nuclear collective model angular wave functions. They underlie the recently-proposed algebraic method for diagonalization of the nuclear collective model Hamiltonian in an SU(1,1)xSO(5) basis. We present a computer code for explicit construction of the SO(5)>SO(3) spherical harmonics and use them to compute the Clebsch-Gordan coefficients needed for collective model calculations in an SO(3)-coupled basis. With these Clebsch-Gordan coefficients it becomes possible to compute the matrix elements of collective model observables by purely algebraic methods.

## Key claim

The paper presents a computer code for explicit construction of SO(5) superset SO(3) spherical harmonics and Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, enabling purely algebraic computation of matrix elements for nuclear collective model observables, but the construction is limited to symmetric irreducible representations (v,0) of SO(5) and the spherical harmonics are obtained by Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization of monomials in four generating functions, with the ordering of degenerate basis monomials at a given seniority being arbitrary and thus yielding one of several possible unitarily-equivalent bases.

## Method

The SO(5) superset SO(3) spherical harmonics are constructed as polynomials in four generating functions defined on the four-sphere S_4, which are products of the unit-length quadrupole moments expressed in Bohr coordinates (gamma, Omega). The basis monomials are organized by degree N and angular momentum L, then orthogonalized via Gram-Schmidt within each L-space to obtain eigenfunctions of the SO(5) Casimir operator Lambda-hat-squared. The Clebsch-Gordan coefficients (isoscalar factors) for coupling symmetric representations are then computed from triple overlap integrals of the spherical harmonics using the Racah factorization lemma.

## Key numbers

* The SO(5) Casimir operator eigenvalue equation: Lambda-hat-squared Psi_{v alpha L M} = v(v+3) Psi_{v alpha L M}, where v is the boson seniority quantum number
* The inner product of the xi functions on the Euler angles: integral xi^{(L')*}_{K'M'} xi^{(L)}_{KM} dOmega = (16 pi^2)/(2L+1) delta_{L'L} delta_{K'K} delta_{M'M}

## Context

This work extends the generating function method of Rowe, Turner, and Repka (2004) for constructing SO(5) superset SO(3) spherical harmonics, providing a practical computational implementation in Mathematica. The code enables routine calculations using the algebraic collective model (ACM) of Rowe (2004-2005), which reduces basis sizes by orders of magnitude compared to conventional diagonalization in a U(5) superset SO(5) oscillator basis as used by Gneuss and Greiner (1969-1970). The Clebsch-Gordan coefficients are also relevant to the U(6) interacting boson model of Iachello and Arima (1987) and can serve as seed coefficients for generating coupling coefficients for more general non-symmetric representations of SO(5) or Sp(4) in an SO(3) basis, as explored by Caprio (2007).
