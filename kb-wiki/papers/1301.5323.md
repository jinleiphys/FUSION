# Incorporating Space-Time Within Medium-Modified Jet Event Generators

> We outline a novel approach to develop an in-medium shower Monte-Carlo event generator based on the higher-twist formalism of jet modification. By undoing one of the light-cone integrals which sets the corresponding light-cone momentum to be equal in the amplitude and the complex conjugate, we introduce an uncertainty in the smaller light-cone momentum component. This allows for the generalization of the standard analytic formalism to a Wigner transform like formalism, where the non-conjugate large light-cone momentum and position are retained for each parton. Jets are generated event-by-event by simulating this Wigner transform kernel. Simple results for longitudinal distributions of partons and hadrons from jets propagating through a hot brick of strongly interacting matter are presented. Values of the transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ are dialed to match phenomenologically relevant cases.

## Key claim

The paper outlines a method to incorporate space-time structure into a medium-modified jet event generator by reintroducing an uncertainty in the smaller light-cone momentum component, converting the standard analytic higher-twist formalism into a Wigner transform-like formalism where both large light-cone momentum and position are retained for each parton.

## Method

The author starts from the higher-twist formalism of jet modification and introduces an uncertainty in the smaller light-cone momentum component \(p^{+}\) by allowing the incoming quark momentum to differ between the amplitude and complex conjugate. This uncertainty is modeled as a Gaussian distribution whose width is set so that the mean light-cone splitting distance equals the formation time \(\tau_f^{-} = 2p^{-}/\mu^{2}\). The resulting distribution of splitting lengths is used to construct a medium-modified Sudakov form factor that includes both vacuum splitting and a medium-induced emission kernel \(K_{p^-,Q^2}(y,\zeta)\) integrated from the starting location \(\zeta_i^{-}\) to \(\zeta_i^{-} + \tau^{-}\). Transverse momentum broadening from multiple scattering is implemented by replacing the delta function in transverse momentum with a Gaussian whose width depends on the transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\) and the light-cone length traversed.

## Key numbers

* The light-cone formation time for a hard parton is given as \(\tau_f^{-} = 2p^{-}/\mu^{2} = 2/p_0^{+}\) (Eq. 13).
* The width of the Gaussian distribution for the offset \(\Delta p_0^{+}\) is set to \(\sigma = 2p^{+}/\pi = 2p_0^{+}/\pi\) (Section III).
* The transverse momentum broadening distribution is \(\phi(L^{-},\vec{l}_\perp) = \frac{1}{4\pi D L^{-}} \exp\left\{-\frac{l_\perp^2}{4 D L^{-}}\right\}\) where the diffusion coefficient \(D = \hat{q}/4\) (Eq. 15).
* The medium-modified single emission kernel is \(K_{p^-,Q^2}(y,\zeta) = \frac{2\hat{q}}{Q^{2}}\left[2 - 2\cos\left\{\frac{Q^2 (\zeta - \zeta_i)}{2 p^- y (1-y)}\right\}\right]\) (Eq. 17).

## Context

This work extends the higher-twist formalism of jet modification (Majumder 2007, 2009) to a Monte-Carlo event generator framework, addressing a fundamental problem not solved by existing in-medium event generators such as Q-PYTHIA (Armesto 2009), MARTINI (Schenke 2009), JEWEL (Zapp 2008, 2012), YaJEM (Renk 2009, 2012), and PYQUEN (Lokhtin 2005): the consistent incorporation of space-time structure and its fluctuations. The approach enables future event-by-event simulations of jet modification in heavy-ion collisions, including the calculation of multi-particle correlations, jet shapes, and energy-momentum flow within jet cones.
