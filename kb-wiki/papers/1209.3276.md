# Bulk Properties of a Fermi Gas in a Magnetic Field

> We calculate the number density, energy density, transverse pressure, longitudinal pressure, and magnetization of an ensemble of spin one-half particles in the presence of a homogenous background magnetic field. The magnetic field direction breaks spherical symmetry causing the pressure transverse to the magnetic field direction to be different than the pressure parallel to it. We present explicit formulae appropriate at zero and finite temperature for both charged and uncharged particles including the effect of the anomalous magnetic moment. We demonstrate that the resulting expressions satisfy the canonical relations, Omega = - P_parallel and P_perp = P_parallel - M B, with M = - d Omega/d B being the magnetization of the system. We numerically calculate the resulting pressure anisotropy for a gas of protons and a gas of neutrons and demonstrate that the inclusion of the anomalous magnetic increases the level of pressure anisotropy in both cases.

## Key claim

The paper derives explicit expressions for the bulk thermodynamic properties of a spin-one-half Fermi gas in a uniform magnetic field, including the anomalous magnetic moment, and demonstrates that the inclusion of the anomalous magnetic moment increases the pressure anisotropy for both charged (proton) and uncharged (neutron) gases, though the effect diminishes at high temperature.

## Method

The authors compute the number density, energy density, longitudinal pressure, transverse pressure, and magnetization from the one-particle distribution function using quantum field theory, with the energy-momentum tensor decomposed into matter and field contributions. For charged particles, transverse momenta are quantized into Landau levels, while for uncharged particles the momentum integration is continuous; both cases are treated at zero and finite temperature with and without the anomalous magnetic moment. The authors verify that the resulting expressions satisfy the canonical thermodynamic relations Omega = -P_parallel and P_perp = P_parallel - M B.

## Key numbers

- Proton mass m_p = 0.939 GeV, anomalous magnetic moment kappa_p = 1.79 * e/(2 m_p) = 0.288633 GeV^{-1} (Heaviside-Lorentz natural units)
- Neutron mass m_n = 0.939 GeV, anomalous magnetic moment kappa_n = -1.91 * e/(2 m_n) = -0.307983 GeV^{-1}
- Magnetic field magnitude used in numerical calculations: 5 x 10^{18} Gauss
- At T = 500 MeV (typical of early LHC heavy ion collisions), the pressure anisotropy for charged protons is less than or approximately 1 percent
- For a zero-temperature gas of protons, the transverse pressure becomes negative at low densities when the anomalous magnetic moment is included, while the longitudinal pressure remains positive at all densities
- For uncharged neutrons without anomalous magnetic moment, the pressures are completely isotropic; with the anomalous magnetic moment, the pressure anisotropy can be quite sizable

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of bulk properties of Fermi gases in magnetic fields by Canuto (1969) and Chiu (1968) to include the transverse pressure with anomalous magnetic moment, which had not appeared previously. The results are relevant for understanding pressure anisotropy in neutron stars (magnetars with surface fields 10^{14}-10^{15} Gauss and interior fields up to 10^{19} Gauss) and in the early-time dynamics of quark-gluon plasma created in relativistic heavy ion collisions. The paper provides a reference for future studies that aim to simultaneously include general relativity, magnetic field effects on the equation of state, and pressure anisotropy in high-magnetic-field neutron stars.
