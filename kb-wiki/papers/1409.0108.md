# Halo nuclei with the Coulomb-Sturmian basis

> The rapid falloff of the oscillator functions at large radius (Gaussian asymptotics) makes them poorly suited for the description of the asymptotic properties of the nuclear wave function, a problem which becomes particularly acute for halo nuclei. We consider an alternative basis for ab initio no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations, built from Coulomb-Sturmian radial functions, allowing for realistic (exponential) radial falloff. NCCI calculations are carried out for the neutron-rich He isotopes, and estimates are made for the RMS radii of the proton and neutron distributions.

## Key claim

The Coulomb-Sturmian basis, with its exponential radial falloff, provides a viable alternative to the harmonic oscillator basis for no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations of halo nuclei, but the energy convergence for tightly-bound systems like ⁴He lags significantly behind that of the oscillator basis, by about two steps in Nmax.

## Method

The authors performed NCCI calculations for ⁴He, ⁶He, and ⁸He using both the conventional harmonic oscillator basis and a Coulomb-Sturmian basis, with the JISP16 nucleon-nucleon interaction (bare, without renormalization) plus Coulomb interaction. For the Coulomb-Sturmian basis, they implemented a change-of-basis transformation from oscillator two-body matrix elements and explored proton-neutron asymmetric bases with different length scales for protons and neutrons, parameterized by the ratio b_n/b_p. The many-body calculations were carried out using the M-scheme code MFDn, with basis truncations up to Nmax=14 for ⁴He, Nmax=12 for ⁶He, and Nmax=10 for ⁸He.

## Key numbers

- ⁴He ground state energy convergence obtained at the ~10 keV level by Nmax=14 in the harmonic oscillator basis
- Two-neutron separation energies: 0.97 MeV for ⁶He and 2.14 MeV for ⁸He
- Experimental radii from Tanihata (2013): ⁴He r_p = 1.457(10) fm; ⁶He r_p = 1.925(12) fm, r_n = 2.74(7) fm; ⁸He r_p = 1.807(28) fm, r_n = 2.72(4) fm
- Crossover radii for ⁴He estimate converged value to within ~0.05 fm at Nmax=6 for both bases
- Converged ⁴He radius with JISP16 agrees with experiment to within ~0.03 fm
- For ⁶He and ⁸He, Coulomb-Sturmian crossover radii with different b_n/b_p ratios (1, 1.189, 1.414) agree with each other to within ~0.1 fm from Nmax≈8 onward
- Estimated neutron radii for ⁶He and ⁸He: r_n ≈ 2.5–2.6 fm, which is ~0.2–0.3 fm smaller than experimental values

## Context

This work extends the framework for NCCI calculations with general radial bases developed by Caprio (2012) to the specific case of halo nuclei, where the Gaussian asymptotics of the conventional harmonic oscillator basis are particularly problematic. The crossover-point method for estimating radii was previously proposed by Bogner (2008) and Cockrell (2012). The results enable future development of more sophisticated extrapolation schemes for long-range observables in ab initio calculations, and suggest that the JISP16 interaction may systematically underestimate neutron radii in neutron-rich helium isotopes.
