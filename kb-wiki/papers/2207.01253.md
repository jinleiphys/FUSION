# Velocity of Sound beyond the High-Density Relativistic Limit from Lattice Simulation of Dense Two-Color QCD

> We obtain the equation of state (EoS) for two-color QCD at low temperature and high density from the lattice Monte Carlo simulation. We find that the velocity of sound exceeds the relativistic limit ($c_s^2/c^2=1/3$) after BEC-BCS crossover in the superfluid phase. Such an excess of the sound velocity is previously unknown from any lattice calculations for QCD-like theories. This finding might have a possible relevance to the EoS of neutron star matter revealed by recent measurements of neutron star masses and radii.

## Key claim
The velocity of sound squared exceeds the relativistic conformal limit of \(c_s^2/c^2 = 1/3\) after the BEC-BCS crossover in the superfluid phase of two-color QCD, a result previously unknown from any lattice calculations for QCD-like theories. The authors note that this finding might have possible relevance to the equation of state of neutron star matter, but the simulation uses heavy quark masses (\(m_{PS} \approx 750\) MeV) and a fixed temperature of \(T \approx 79\) MeV, so the result is not directly applicable to real three-color QCD at zero temperature.

## Method
The authors performed lattice Monte Carlo simulations of two-color QCD with two flavors of Wilson fermions using the Iwasaki gauge action and the Rational Hybrid Monte Carlo (RHMC) algorithm. They employed a fixed-scale method to compute the trace anomaly and obtained the pressure via numerical integration of the quark number density, normalized by the Stefan-Boltzmann limit to reduce discretization effects. The sound velocity was evaluated from the symmetric finite difference of pressure and energy density as a function of chemical potential \(\mu\) at a fixed temperature \(T \approx 79\) MeV on a \(16^3 \times 16\) lattice.

## Key numbers
- The lattice spacing is \(a \approx 0.17\) fm, corresponding to a temperature \(T \approx 79\) MeV (0.39 \(T_c\) where \(T_c = 200\) MeV is the pseudo-critical temperature of the chiral phase transition at \(\mu=0\)).
- The lightest pseudoscalar meson mass at \(\mu=0\) is \(am_{PS} = 0.6229(34)\), giving \(m_{PS} \approx 750\) MeV.
- Superfluidity emerges at \(\mu_c / m_{PS} \approx 0.5\), consistent with chiral perturbation theory.
- The pressure reaches \(p/p_{SB} \approx 0.84\) at the highest density studied (\(\mu/m_{PS} = 1.28\), \(\mu \lesssim 960\) MeV).
- The sound velocity squared exceeds the relativistic limit \(c_s^2/c^2 = 1/3\) after the BEC-BCS crossover and appears to peak around \(\mu \approx m_{PS}\).

## Context
This work extends previous lattice studies of two-color QCD at finite density (Hands 2006, Cotter 2012, Braguta 2016, Iida 2019, Boz 2019, Bornyakov 2022) by providing the first lattice calculation of the sound velocity in this theory. The observed peak in sound velocity supports predictions from effective models based on quark Fermi sea saturation, such as the quarkyonic matter model of McLerran and Reddy (2019) and the microscopic interpretation by Kojo (2021). The result also parallels findings in condensed matter systems with finite-range interactions (Tajima 2022), suggesting a possible universal property of superfluids across the BEC-BCS crossover regime. Future work requires simulations at smaller lattice spacing, lighter quark masses, and lower temperatures to approach the zero-temperature limit and to determine whether such a peak structure appears in real three-color QCD.
