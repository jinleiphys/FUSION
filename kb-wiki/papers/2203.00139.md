# Transport coefficients of the quark-gluon plasma at the critical point and across the first-order line

> A bottom-up Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton holographic model is used to compute, for the first time, the behavior of several transport coefficients of the hot and baryon-rich strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma at the critical point and also across the first-order phase transition line in the phase diagram. The observables under study are of the shear and bulk viscosities, baryon diffusion, thermal conductivity, the jet quenching parameter $\hat{q}$, as well as the heavy-quark drag force and Langevin diffusion coefficients. These calculations provide a phenomenologically promising estimate for these coefficients, given that our model quantitatively reproduces lattice QCD thermodynamics results, both at zero and finite baryon density, besides naturally incorporating the nearly-perfect fluidity of the quark-gluon plasma. We find that the diffusion of baryon charge, and also the shear and bulk viscosities, are suppressed with increasing baryon density, indicating that the medium becomes even closer to perfect fluidity at large densities. On the other hand, the jet quenching parameter and the heavy-quark momentum diffusion are enhanced with increasing density. The observables display a discontinuity gap when crossing the first-order phase transition line, while developing an infinite slope at the critical point. The transition temperatures associated with different transport coefficients differ in the crossover region but are found to converge at the critical point.

## Key claim

The authors use a bottom-up holographic model to compute, for the first time, the behavior of multiple transport coefficients of the quark-gluon plasma at the critical point and across the first-order phase transition line, finding that baryon diffusion and shear and bulk viscosities are suppressed with increasing baryon density while the jet quenching parameter and heavy-quark momentum diffusion are enhanced, with all observables developing an infinite slope at the critical point and displaying a discontinuity gap across the first-order line.

## Method

The authors employ a 5-dimensional Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton (EMD) holographic model whose free parameters were fixed in Critelli (2017) by matching the holographic equation of state and second-order baryon susceptibility at zero baryon chemical potential to lattice QCD results from Borsanyi (2013) and Bellwied (2015). Transport coefficients are computed using well-known holographic Kubo formulas for the shear and bulk viscosities, baryon conductivity, and thermal conductivity, while the jet quenching parameter, heavy-quark drag force, and Langevin diffusion coefficients are calculated via a probe Nambu-Goto action for a classical string on top of the EMD background solutions.

## Key numbers

- The critical end point (CEP) is located at (T^c, μ_B^c) ~ (89, 724) MeV, as predicted in Critelli (2017)
- The EMD model covers baryon chemical potential values up to μ_B ~ 1100 MeV, extending the phase diagram from Grefa (2021)
- The baryon conductivity σ_B/T remains finite at the CEP, indicating the model falls in the model B dynamical universality class
- The baryon diffusion coefficient D_B vanishes at the CEP due to the divergence of the second-order baryon susceptibility χ_2^B
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s = 1/4π for all T > 0 and μ_B ≥ 0, as expected for isotropic holographic models with two-derivative gravity actions
- The energy scaling factor Λ = 1058.83 MeV converts holographic quantities to field theory units

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic analysis of the EMD model from Grefa (2021) to transport properties, and goes beyond earlier holographic calculations by Rougemont (2015, 2017) that were limited to moderate baryon densities far from the critical point. The results provide phenomenologically promising estimates for transport coefficients that can be used as input in hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions at finite baryon density, particularly for the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC and future experiments at FAIR and NICA. The model's predictions for the CEP location differ from a similar recent EMD model by Cai (2022), which predicts (T^c, μ_B^c) ~ (105, 558) MeV, indicating that current lattice QCD results at finite baryon density from Borsanyi (2021) cannot distinguish between the two predictions.
