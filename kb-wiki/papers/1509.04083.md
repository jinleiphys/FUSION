# Heavy flavor in nucleus-nucleus and proton-nucleus: quenching, flow and correlations

> We present recent results for heavy-flavor observables in nucleus-nucleus collisions at LHC energies, obtained with the POWLANG transport setup. The initial creation of c-cbar and b-bbar pairs is simulated with a perturbative QCD approach (POWHEG+PYTHIA); their propagation in the medium (created in the nucleus-nucleus or in proton-nucleus collision) is studied with the relativistic Langevin equation, here solved using weak-coupling transport coefficients. Successively, the heavy quarks hadronize in the medium. We compute the nuclear modification factor and the elliptic flow parameter of the final D mesons both in nucleus-nucleus and in (for the first time, in the POWLANG setup) proton-nucleus collisions and compare our results to experimental data.

## Key claim
The POWLANG transport setup, using weak-coupling (HTL) transport coefficients and a new in-medium hadronization model where heavy quarks combine with thermal light partons, improves agreement with experimental data for D-meson nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow in nucleus-nucleus collisions, and provides first predictions for proton-nucleus collisions.

## Method
The initial production of c-cbar and b-bbar pairs was simulated with POWHEG+PYTHIA (perturbative QCD). Heavy-quark propagation in the medium created in nucleus-nucleus or proton-nucleus collisions was modeled with the relativistic Langevin equation using weak-coupling transport coefficients (Hard Thermal Loop approximation). Hadronization in nuclear collisions was performed by extracting a light antiquark from a thermal distribution at the decoupling temperature and constructing a string for PYTHIA fragmentation, rather than using in-vacuum fragmentation. For proton-nucleus collisions, event-by-event fluctuations in the initial state were simulated with a Monte Carlo Glauber calculation to obtain realistic eccentricity.

## Key numbers
- D-meson nuclear modification factor R_AA in central (0-10%) Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV: in-medium hadronization produces a bump around p_T ~ 1.5 GeV, in qualitative agreement with STAR data
- D-meson elliptic flow v_2 in semicentral (30-50%) Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV: in-medium hadronization at T_dec=155 MeV brings theory closer to ALICE data, with lower T_dec preferred over higher values
- D-meson v_2 in p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV: results shown for smearing parameter sigma=0.2 and 0.4 fm
- Nuclear modification factor for D mesons in p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV: peak predicted at p_T ~ 3-4 GeV, with systematic uncertainty from initial smearing parameter

## Context
This work extends the POWLANG transport setup developed in a series of papers (Nardi and collaborators, 2012-2015) for heavy-flavor observables in nucleus-nucleus collisions. The new in-medium hadronization model improves upon earlier POWLANG calculations that used in-vacuum fragmentation, and the application to proton-nucleus collisions is presented for the first time in this framework. The results enable future studies of heavy-flavor correlations in heavy-ion collisions and extension to d-Au collisions at RHIC, as well as the use of transport coefficients evaluated on the lattice.
