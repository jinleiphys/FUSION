# Testing the density matrix expansion against ab initio calculations of trapped neutron drops

> Microscopic input to a universal nuclear energy density functional can be provided through the density matrix expansion (DME), which has recently been revived and improved. Several DME implementation strategies are tested for neutron drop systems in harmonic traps by comparing to Hartree-Fock (HF) and ab initio no-core full configuration (NCFC) calculations with a model interaction (Minnesota potential). The new DME with exact treatment of Hartree contributions is found to best reproduce HF results and supplementing the functional with fit Skyrme-like contact terms shows systematic improvement toward the full NCFC results.

## Key claim

The density matrix expansion (DME) with phase-space averaging (PSA) and exact treatment of Hartree contributions best reproduces full Hartree-Fock results for trapped neutron drops, and supplementing the functional with fit Skyrme-like contact terms shows systematic improvement toward ab initio no-core full configuration (NCFC) results, but only for the simplified Minnesota potential rather than realistic nuclear interactions.

## Method

The authors tested several DME implementation strategies (Negele-Vautherin, phase-space averaging, naive Taylor expansion, and exact Hartree treatments) by comparing DME-based Hartree-Fock calculations to full Hartree-Fock and ab initio NCFC calculations for neutron drops confined in harmonic oscillator traps with oscillator parameters from 5 MeV to 20 MeV. The calculations used the two-body Minnesota potential for N=8 and N=20 closed-shell neutron systems, and the DME-based functionals were implemented in the HFBRAD and HFBTHO density functional theory solvers. To incorporate correlations beyond Hartree-Fock, the authors either scaled the DME/HF couplings by a density-dependent factor fitted to Brueckner-Hartree-Fock neutron matter results or added Skyrme-like contact terms with volume parameters fitted to neutron matter and surface parameters optimized to NCFC total energies.

## Key numbers

- For N=8 at hbarOmega=10 MeV, HF/PSA with exact Hartree gave total energy deviation from full HF of 0.9 MeV, compared to 2.0 MeV for HF/NV with exact Hartree
- For N=20 at hbarOmega=20 MeV, HF/PSA with exact Hartree gave total energy deviation from full HF of 12.5 MeV, compared to 34.8 MeV for HF/NV with exact Hartree
- The fit/PSA approach with optimized surface parameter C^{rhoDelta rho} = -1.8 MeV fm^5 gave total energy 290.9 MeV for N=8 at hbarOmega=20 MeV, compared to NCFC result of 290.8(2) MeV
- For N=20 at hbarOmega=20 MeV, fit/PSA gave total energy 924.4 MeV compared to NCFC result of 922(6) MeV
- The optimized volume parameters were C^{rho^2} = -18.25 MeV fm^3 and C^{rho tau} = 4.57 MeV fm^5

## Context

This work extends the DME reformulation by Bogner (2008) and the phase-space averaging approach by Gebremariam (2009, 2010) by testing these methods against ab initio NCFC calculations for neutron drops, which were previously used by Gandolfi (2010) and Carlson (2011) with quantum Monte Carlo methods. The results enable future testing of more realistic interactions (including chiral NN and NNN forces) and extensions to self-bound nuclei with pairing, as part of the UNEDF SciDAC-2 collaboration program to develop microscopically informed nuclear energy density functionals.
