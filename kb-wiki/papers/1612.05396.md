# Modeling Charmonium-$η$ Decays of $J^{PC}=1^{--}$ Higher Charmonia

> We propose a new model to create a light meson in the heavy quarkonium transition, which is inspired by the Nambu$-$Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. Hadronic transitions of $J^{PC}=1^{--}$ higher charmonia with the emission of an $η$ meson are studied in the framework of the proposed model. The model shows its potential to reproduce the observed decay widths and make predictions for the unobserved channels. We present our predictions for the decay width of $Ψ\to J/ψη$ and $Ψ\to h_{c}(1P)η$, where $Ψ$ are higher $S$ and $D$ wave vector charmonia, which provide useful references to search for higher charmonia and determine their properties in forthcoming experiments. The predicted branching fraction $\mathcal B(ψ(4415)\to h_{c}(1P)η)=4.62\times 10^{-4}$ is one order of magnitude smaller than the $J/ψη$ channel. Estimates of partial decay width $Γ(Y \to J/ψη)$ are given for $Y(4360)$, $Y(4390)$ and $Y(4660)$ by assuming them as $c\bar{c}$ bound states with quantum numbers $3 ^3D_1$, $3 ^3D_1$ and $5 ^3S_1$, respectively. Our results are in favor of these assignments for $Y(4360)$ and $Y(4660)$. The corresponding experimental data for these $Y$ states has large statistical errors which do not provide any constraint on the mixing angle if we introduce $S-D$ mixing. To identify $Y(4390)$, precise measurements on its hadronic branching fraction are required which are eagerly awaited from BESIII.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new model for hadronic transitions of higher charmonia, inspired by the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) effective interaction, which reproduces observed decay widths for \(\Psi \to J/\psi \eta\) and predicts unobserved channels, but the model's parameters (coupling strength \(g\), oscillator parameter \(\beta\), and mixing angle \(\theta\)) are fitted to data and the predictions for states like \(Y(4390)\) remain uncertain due to large experimental statistical errors.

## Method

The authors model the creation of a light \(\eta\) meson in heavy quarkonium transitions using an NJL-motivated effective Lagrangian that couples a heavy quark line to scalar and pseudoscalar mesons. They compute decay widths for \(\Psi \to J/\psi \eta\) and \(\Psi \to h_c(1P)\eta\) by evaluating hadronic matrix elements with simple harmonic oscillator (SHO) wave functions and a mock-hadron prescription, incorporating \(S\)-\(D\) mixing between \(n^3S_1\) and \((n-1)^3D_1\) states. The coupling constant \(g\) and oscillator parameter \(\beta\) are fitted to experimental data for \(\psi(3686)\), \(\psi(3770)\), \(\psi(4040)\), \(\psi(4160)\), and \(\psi(4415)\), with \(\beta = 0.40\) GeV and \(|\theta| = 13^\circ\) as the best-fit values.

## Key numbers

*   Predicted branching fraction \(\mathcal{B}(\psi(4415) \to h_c(1P)\eta) = 4.62 \times 10^{-4}\), one order of magnitude smaller than the \(J/\psi\eta\) channel.
*   Fitted decay width \(\Gamma(\psi(3686) \to J/\psi\eta) = 0.010\) MeV, matching the experimental value \(0.010 \pm 0.001\) MeV.
*   Fitted decay width \(\Gamma(\psi(3770) \to J/\psi\eta) = 0.025\) MeV, matching the experimental value \(0.025 \pm 0.011\) MeV.
*   Fitted decay width \(\Gamma(\psi(4040) \to J/\psi\eta) = 0.347\) MeV, compared to the experimental value \(0.416 \pm 0.076\) MeV.
*   Fitted decay width \(\Gamma(\psi(4160) \to J/\psi\eta) = 0.204\) MeV, within the experimental upper limit \(< 0.560 \pm 0.080\) MeV.
*   Fitted decay width \(\Gamma(\psi(4415) \to J/\psi\eta) = 0.425\) MeV, slightly above the experimental central value \(< 0.372 \pm 0.120\) MeV but within the upper limit.
*   Ratio \(\Gamma(\psi(4160) \to h_c(1P)\eta) / \Gamma(\psi(4160) \to J/\psi\eta) = 7.887 \times 10^{-2}\).
*   Ratio \(\Gamma(\psi(4415) \to h_c(1P)\eta) / \Gamma(\psi(4415) \to J/\psi\eta) = 6.736 \times 10^{-2}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of hadronic transitions in heavy quarkonia beyond the multipole expansion (ME) and heavy meson chiral Lagrangians (HMCL) frameworks, which are less suitable for higher charmonia with large decay momenta. The model is contrasted with the conventional \(^3P_0\) quark pair creation model and with nonrelativistic effective field theory (NREFT) treatments of intermediate charmed meson loops, such as those by Cao (2016) and Guo (2009, 2010). The predictions for \(\Psi \to J/\psi\eta\) and \(\Psi \to h_c(1P)\eta\) provide references for identifying higher vector charmonia at BESIII and future \(\bar{\text{P}}\)ANDA experiments, and the results favor the \(c\bar{c}\) assignments for \(Y(4360)\) and \(Y(4660)\) as \(3^3D_1\) and \(5^3S_1\) states, respectively, while calling for more precise measurements of \(Y(4390)\).
