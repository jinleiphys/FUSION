# On the running coupling in the JIMWLK equation

> We propose a new method to implement the running coupling constant in the JIMWLK equation, imposing the scale dependence on the correlation function of the random noise in the Langevin formulation. We interpret this scale choice as the transverse momentum of the emitted gluon in one step of the evolution and show that it is related to the "Balitsky" prescription for the BK equation. This slows down the evolution speed of a practical solution of the JIMWLK equation, bringing it closer to the x-dependence inferred from fits to HERA data. We further study our proposal by a numerical comparison of the BK and JIMWLK equations.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new method for implementing the running coupling constant in the JIMWLK equation by placing the scale dependence into the correlation function of the random noise in the Langevin formulation, interpreting the scale as the transverse momentum of the emitted gluon in one evolution step; this slows the evolution speed compared to the "square root" prescription used in prior JIMWLK solutions, bringing it closer to the x-dependence inferred from fits to HERA data, though the resulting BK equation from this proposal is not exactly the same as the Balitsky prescription but is argued to be very close for practical purposes.

## Method

The authors reformulate the JIMWLK Langevin step into a manifestly left-right symmetric form without a deterministic drift term, where the Wilson line is multiplied from both left and right by exponentials of the noise. They then replace the fixed-coupling noise correlator with a Fourier-transformed running coupling, \(\widetilde{\alpha}_{\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{y}} \equiv \int \frac{d^2 \mathbf{k}}{(2\pi)^2} e^{i \mathbf{k}\cdot(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{y})} \alpha_s(k)\), so that the coupling depends on the transverse momentum of the emitted gluon, and numerically compare the resulting rcJIMWLK equation to the rcBK equation with the Balitsky prescription.

## Key numbers

- The fixed coupling BK equation kernel is \(\frac{\alpha_s N_c}{2 \pi^2} \int_{\mathbf{z}} \big( \mathbf{K}_{\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{z}}^2 + \mathbf{K}_{\mathbf{y}-\mathbf{z}}^2 - 2 \mathbf{K}_{\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{z}} \cdot \mathbf{K}_{\mathbf{y}-\mathbf{z}} \big) \big[ \hat{D}(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{z})\hat{D}(\mathbf{z},\mathbf{y}) - \hat{D}(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}) \big]\).
- The "square root" running coupling BK equation uses \(\alpha_s(|\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{z}|) \mathbf{K}_{\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{z}}^2 + \alpha_s(|\mathbf{y}-\mathbf{z}|) \mathbf{K}_{\mathbf{y}-\mathbf{z}}^2 - 2 \sqrt{\alpha_s(|\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{z}|)\alpha_s(|\mathbf{y}-\mathbf{z}|)} \mathbf{K}_{\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{z}} \cdot \mathbf{K}_{\mathbf{y}-\mathbf{z}}\).
- The proposed noise correlator is \(\langle \eta_\mathbf{x}^{a,i} \eta_\mathbf{y}^{b,j} \rangle = \delta^{ab} \delta^{ij} \int \frac{d^2 \mathbf{k}}{(2\pi)^2} e^{i \mathbf{k}\cdot(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{y})} \alpha_s(k)\), replacing the fixed coupling \(\alpha_s \delta^{ab} \delta^{ij} \int \frac{d^2 \mathbf{k}}{(2\pi)^2} e^{i \mathbf{k}\cdot(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{y})}\).

## Context

This work extends the running coupling implementation in the BK equation, specifically the "Balitsky" prescription (Balitsky, 2006) which is the standard for rcBK phenomenology, to the full JIMWLK equation; prior JIMWLK solutions (Lappi and Mantysaari, 2011; Dumitru et al., 2011) used a "square root" running coupling prescription that could not reproduce the Balitsky prescription. The proposed method enables more consistent phenomenological applications of JIMWLK beyond the BK mean-field approximation, such as calculations of multiparticle correlations and the "ridge" effect, by providing a running coupling formulation that is numerically tractable via FFT and yields an evolution speed closer to HERA data.
