# Dependence of direct neutron capture on nuclear-structure models

> The prediction of cross sections for nuclei far off stability is crucial in the field of nuclear astrophysics. We calculate direct neutron capture on the even-even isotopes $^{124-145}$Sn and $^{208-238}$Pb with energy levels, masses, and nuclear density distributions taken from different nuclear-structure models. The utilized structure models are a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model, a relativistic mean field theory, and a macroscopic-microscopic model based on the finite-range droplet model and a folded-Yukawa single-particle potential. Due to the differences in the resulting neutron separation and level energies, the investigated models yield capture cross sections sometimes differing by orders of magnitude. This may also lead to differences in the predicted astrophysical r-process paths. Astrophysical implications are discussed.

## Key claim

Direct neutron capture cross sections for neutron-rich Sn and Pb isotopes, crucial for r-process nucleosynthesis, can differ by orders of magnitude depending on which nuclear-structure model (Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov, relativistic mean field theory, or macroscopic-microscopic finite-range droplet model) provides the input masses, level energies, and spins, but the abstract does not emphasize that these differences arise specifically because the direct capture mechanism is extremely sensitive to whether low-spin states remain bound as the neutron separation energy decreases.

## Method

The authors calculated direct neutron capture cross sections for even-even isotopes 124-145Sn and 208-238Pb using the folding procedure to construct nucleon-nucleus potentials from nuclear density distributions, then solving for E1, M1, and E2 transitions with the code TEDCA. Input masses, excitation energies, spins, parities, and density distributions were taken consistently from three nuclear-structure models: a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) model with Skyrme SkP force, a relativistic mean field theory (RMFT) with parameter set NLSH, and the macroscopic-microscopic finite-range droplet model (FRDM, 1992) with a folded-Yukawa single-particle potential. For 208Pb, the calculated non-resonant Maxwellian-averaged cross section at 30 keV was compared to the experimental value of 0.14(4) mb deduced from subtracting resonant contributions from total cross section measurements.

## Key numbers

- Experimental non-resonant Maxwellian-averaged cross section for 208Pb(n,gamma)209Pb at 30 keV: 0.14(4) mb, deduced from total cross section 0.36(3) mb minus resonant part 0.221(27) mb
- Calculated non-resonant DC cross section for 208Pb at 30 keV using experimental input: 0.135 mb, in excellent agreement with experiment
- Calculated non-resonant DC cross sections for 208Pb at 30 keV using model input: 0.0289 mb (RMFT), 0.0508 mb (FRDM), 0.0135 mb (HFB)
- For 132Sn(n,gamma)133Sn, the Hauser-Feshbach cross sections used previously were of order 10^-4 to 5x10^-5 barn, while the DC contribution is about the same magnitude as the Hauser-Feshbach value
- The r-process path is predicted at neutron separation energies around 2.5-1.7 MeV in the Sn region and 1.5-0.9 MeV in the Pb region

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of direct versus compound nucleus mechanisms for neutron capture on light and intermediate nuclei (Oberhummer 1996, Beer 1995, Krausmann 1996) to heavy neutron-rich nuclei near shell closures, where the statistical Hauser-Feshbach model becomes inapplicable due to low level densities (Rauscher 1997). The results show that the choice of nuclear-structure model can dramatically affect predicted r-process paths and final abundances, particularly for the production of chronometer nuclei like 232Th and 235,238U, and that improved microscopic models are needed for reliable astrophysical predictions.
