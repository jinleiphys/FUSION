# Holography and the speed of sound at high temperatures

> We show that in a general class of strongly interacting theories at high temperatures the speed of sound approaches the conformal value c_s^2=1/3 universally from_below_. This class includes theories holographically dual to a theory of gravity coupled to a single scalar field, representing the operator of the scale anomaly.

# Holography and the speed of sound at high temperatures

## Key claim
The speed of sound approaches the conformal value \(c_s^2 = 1/3\) from **below** in a general class of strongly interacting theories at high temperatures, specifically those holographically dual to gravity coupled to a single scalar field representing the scale anomaly. The deviation is universally negative at leading order in inverse temperature, but the paper explicitly notes that \(c_s^2 = 1/3\) is **not** a universal upper bound on sound velocity, citing QCD at large isospin chemical potential as a counterexample.

## Method
The authors directly calculate one-point functions \(\langle T^{tt}\rangle\) and \(\langle T^{xx}\rangle\) from the holographic correspondence, using the relation \(c_s^2 = dp/d\epsilon\) rather than the more common approaches based on \(c_s^2 = d\log T / d\log s\) or sound-channel pole analysis. They solve the five-dimensional equations of motion for a metric with a scalar field \(\phi\) and potential \(V(\phi)\), imposing boundary conditions at the UV boundary and regularity at the black hole horizon. In the high-temperature (large enthalpy \(w\)) limit, they obtain an analytic solution for the scalar field using hypergeometric functions and extract the leading correction to the speed of sound.

## Key numbers
- The conformal speed of sound squared is \(c_s^2 = 1/3\).
- The leading correction to the speed of sound squared at high temperature is given by \(c_s^2 = 1/3 - \frac{1}{9} c^2 \Delta_- (\Delta_+ - \Delta_-)^2 w^{-\Delta_-/2} D(\Delta_-) + \ldots\), where \(D(\Delta_-) = \frac{\pi 2^{\Delta_-}}{2 - \Delta_-} \cot(\pi \Delta_- / 4) \frac{\Gamma(\Delta_-/2)^2}{\Gamma(\Delta_-/4)^4}\).
- The correction is negative for all values of \(0 < \Delta_- < 2\) (corresponding to \(2 < \Delta_+ < 4\)).
- The scalar field at the horizon is \(\phi_H = c \, w^{\Delta/4 - 1} \, 2^{-\Delta/2} (2\Delta - 4) \frac{\Gamma(\Delta/4)^2}{\Gamma(\Delta/2)}\), where \(\Delta = \Delta_+\).
- An alternative expression for the speed of sound in terms of \(\phi_H\) is \(c_s^2 = 1/3 - \frac{1}{18\pi} (4 - \Delta)(4 - 2\Delta) \tan(\pi \Delta / 4) \, \phi_H^2 + \ldots\).

## Context
This work extends holographic bottom-up approaches to modeling QCD thermodynamics, such as those by Gubser et al. (2008) and Gursoy et al. (2008), which tuned the scalar potential \(V(\phi)\) to match lattice QCD equations of state. The paper establishes a universal property of such models: the speed of sound approaches the conformal value from below at high temperatures, independent of the specific potential. This result was independently obtained by Cherman et al. (2009) using the relation \(c_s^2 = d\log T / d\log s\). The finding is consistent with lattice QCD calculations by Karsch et al. (2007) showing \(c_s^2 < 1/3\) at high temperatures, and with the asymptotic freedom argument for pure Yang-Mills theory. The result enables further model-independent constraints on holographic descriptions of strongly coupled plasmas and clarifies the relationship between the trace anomaly and the speed of sound.
