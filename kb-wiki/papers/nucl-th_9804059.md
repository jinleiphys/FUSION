# Intrinsic structure of two-phonon states in the interacting boson model

> A general study of excitations up to two-phonon states is carried out using the intrinsic-state formalism of the Interacting Boson Model (IBM). Spectra and transitions for the different dynamical symmetries are analyzed and the correspondence with states in the laboratory frame is established. The influence of multi-phonon states is discussed. The approach is useful in problems where the complexity of the IBM spectrum renders the analysis in the laboratory frame difficult.

## Key claim

The intrinsic-state formalism of the Interacting Boson Model (IBM), extended to include up to two-phonon excitations via the Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (TDA), provides a clear interpretation of the structure of two-phonon states that is otherwise difficult to obtain in the laboratory frame; however, the approach is an approximation of order 1/N to the exact IBM, and anharmonicities can only exist for a finite number of bosons (or in the U(5) limit).

## Method

The authors constructed a deformed boson basis from the IBM intrinsic-state formalism, defining one- and two-phonon excited states as replacements of condensed bosons with excited bosons in the condensate wavefunction. They diagonalized the IBM Hamiltonian in this basis (including the ground state) using TDA up to two-particle-two-hole excitations, removed spurious Goldstone bosons, and compared the resulting intrinsic-frame energies and E2 transition matrix elements with exact laboratory-frame calculations for the U(5), SU(3), and O(6) dynamical symmetry limits as well as for transitional Hamiltonians.

## Key numbers

- For the SU(3) limit, the two-phonon bands correspond to the (2N-8,4) and (2N-6,0) representations of SU(3); the (2N-8,4)K=0 band is the combination sqrt(2/3) beta^2 + sqrt(1/3) gamma^2_{K=0}, while (2N-6,0)K=0 is sqrt(1/3) beta^2 - sqrt(2/3) gamma^2_{K=0}.
- In the O(6) limit, the excitation energy for bandheads is E(sigma=N-2n)-E(sigma=N) = 4A(-nN) + 4A(n^2-2n), where n is the number of excited phonons.
- For a quadrupole-plus-pairing Hamiltonian with N=16, a realistic value of the ratio -kappa_0/kappa_2 is around 4.
- For a triaxial mean field with gamma approximately 6 degrees, the anharmonicity parameter xi (defined as E^{ex}_{p^2}/E^{ex}_p - 2) is around 0.5, comparable to that observed in Er-166 and Er-168.

## Context

This work extends the intrinsic-state formalism of Ginocchio (1980) and Dieperink (1980) to two-phonon excitations in the IBM, addressing the long-standing debate over double-gamma excitations in nuclei such as Os-186-192, Pt-194, Er-166, and Er-168. The approach enables clear identification of band character (beta, gamma, beta^2, gamma^2) in cases where laboratory-frame analysis is complicated by high state density, and it establishes that anharmonicities in the IBM are of order 1/N, linking observed anharmonicity in deformed nuclei (e.g., Er-166, Er-168) to possible triaxiality.
