# Neutron stars and phase diagram in a hard-wall AdS/QCD model

> We study the phase diagram of (large-$N_c$) QCD using a simplistic holographic hard-wall model with a dynamical scalar field and a homogeneous Ansatz representing a smeared instanton/baryon density. The resulting phase diagram is qualitatively consistent with expectations, including a mesonic, baryonic, quarkyonic, and quark-gluon plasma phase. As in other holographic models, we also find a baryonic popcorn transition, which appears at large chemical potential as a crossover. We then evaluate the nuclear matter equation of state, which turns out to be rather stiff with a large peaked sound velocity above the conformal limit, construct corresponding neutron stars using the TOV equations, and finally use a full numerical gravity/hydrodynamics computation to extract the gravitational wave signal of neutron star mergers.

## Key claim

Using a simplistic holographic hard-wall model with a dynamical scalar field and a homogeneous Ansatz for smeared instanton/baryon density, the authors obtain a QCD phase diagram that is qualitatively consistent with expectations, but the nuclear matter equation of state is rather stiff with a large peaked sound velocity above the conformal limit, which is a feature that depends on the specific choice of model parameters and the double hard-wall construction.

## Method

The authors employ a bottom-up holographic hard-wall AdS/QCD model with a double hard-wall construction that allows separate adjustment of the deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration temperatures. They use a homogeneous Ansatz where gauge fields and the scalar field depend only on the holographic coordinate z, representing a smeared distribution of baryons at finite density, and solve the resulting one-dimensional equations of motion numerically. The equation of state obtained from the baryonic phase is then used to construct neutron stars via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations and to simulate neutron star mergers using a full numerical gravity/hydrodynamics code to extract gravitational wave signals.

## Key numbers

- The IR cutoff is set to z_IR = L = 1, with L^{-1} = 323 MeV fixed by the rho meson mass
- The deconfinement temperature is T_d = 2^{1/4}/(pi z_0), which is lower than the chiral symmetry restoration temperature T_c = 1/(pi z_IR) approximately 102.6 MeV when z_0 > 2^{1/4} z_IR
- The baryon number density d is related to the IR boundary condition H(z_IR=1) = (4 pi^2 d)^{1/3}
- The physical baryon chemical potential is mu_B = (N_c/2) mu, where mu is the holographic chemical potential
- Neutron star merger simulations use two neutron stars of 1.4 solar masses each at a separation distance of 45 kilometers

## Context

This work extends the hard-wall AdS/QCD approach of Polchinski and Strassler (2001) and the flavor gauge field construction of Pomarol and collaborators (2007) to finite baryon density and temperature, including a double hard-wall that allows separate deconfinement and chiral restoration transitions as in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model with non-antipodal D-branes. The homogeneous Ansatz for baryonic matter follows the approach used in the top-down Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model by Li and collaborators (2015). The resulting stiff equation of state with a peaked sound velocity addresses the need for neutron star models that can reach observed masses of at least 2 solar masses, as discussed by McLerran and collaborators (2018), and enables predictions for gravitational wave signals from neutron star mergers that can be compared with observations from LIGO and Virgo.
