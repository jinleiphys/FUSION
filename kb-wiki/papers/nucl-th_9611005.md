# S-wave $π^0$ production In pp Collision In A Covariant OBE Model

> The total cross section for the pp to pp $π^0$ reaction at energies close to threshold is calculated in a covariant one-boson-exchange model. The amplitudes for the elementary BN to N$π^0$ processes are taken to be the sum of s, u and t pole terms. The main contributions to the primary productionamplitude is due to a sigma meson exchange. Both the scale and energy dependence of the cross section are perfectly reproduced.

## Key claim

The total cross section for the $pp \to pp \pi^0$ reaction near threshold is perfectly reproduced by a covariant one-boson-exchange model where the dominant contribution comes from a sigma meson exchange, specifically its t-pole term, which was not included in previous studies. The authors note that the off mass-shell pion-nucleon rescattering amplitude, even when enhanced as in chiral perturbation theory, does not resolve the discrepancy with experiment and only varies the calculated cross section by about 15%.

## Method

The authors calculate the total cross section using a covariant one-boson-exchange (OBE) model with a two-step mechanism: a virtual boson (pi, sigma, eta, rho, omega, delta) created on one incoming nucleon converts into a pi0 meson on the second nucleon via a B N to pi0 N process. The amplitudes for the elementary B N to pi0 N processes are taken as the sum of s, u, and t pole terms, with the t pole term contributing only for the sigma meson. The NN and piN final state interactions are included coherently using a factorized form, with parameters determined independently from NN and piN scattering data.

## Key numbers

- The transferred 4-momentum at threshold is space-like: $q^2 = -3.3 \text{ fm}^{-2}$.
- The sigma meson coupling constant is $g_{\sigma NN}^2 / 4\pi = 8.28$ and the pion-nucleon coupling constant is $g_{\pi NN}^2 / 4\pi = 14.6$, yielding $g_{\sigma \pi \pi} = 3.3 \pm 0.1$.
- The S-wave NN scattering length is $a_{pp} = -7.82$ fm and effective range $r_{pp} = 2.7$ fm.
- The S11 and S13 piN scattering lengths are $a_1 = 0.173 \ m_{\pi}^{-1}$ and $a_3 = -0.101 \ m_{\pi}^{-1}$, respectively.
- The relative contributions of meson exchanges are $M_{\sigma} : M_{\omega} : M_{\eta} : M_{\rho} \approx 100:40:8:7$.
- For sigma exchange, the sum of s and u pole terms alone amounts to approximately 6% of the eta amplitude.
- The delta meson exchange contribution is less than 0.7% of the eta exchange contribution.

## Context

This work extends the OBE model approach of Horowitz et al. (1990s) for NN interactions to pion production, finding agreement that sigma meson exchange plays an important role near threshold. It contrasts with earlier single-nucleon and pion-rescattering models by Koltun (1960s), Miller (1970s), and Niskanen (1980s) which underestimate the cross section by a factor of 3-5, and with chiral perturbation theory calculations by Park et al. (1990s) and Cohen et al. (1990s) which found destructive interference between rescattering and Born terms. The model enables direct comparison with other calculations by allowing any form of the B N to pi0 N amplitude to be used.
