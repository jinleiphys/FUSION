# Complete Glauber calculations of reaction and interaction cross sections in light ion collisions

> We calculate the differences between reaction and interaction cross sections in the collisions of relativistic light ions with $\text{A}<40$ in the framework of Glauber theory. Although, in the optical approximation of Glauber theory these differences are approximately 1% of the reaction cross sections or less, they increase up to 3-4% when all scattering diagrams of Glauber theory are included in calculation.

# Complete Glauber Calculations of Reaction and Interaction Cross Sections in Light Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The difference between reaction and interaction cross sections for relativistic light ion collisions (A < 40) is approximately 1% of the reaction cross section in the optical approximation of Glauber theory, but increases to 3-4% when all scattering diagrams of the full Glauber theory are included, contrary to earlier assumptions that the difference is negligible.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo Markov Chain (MCMC) calculations using the Metropolis-Hastings algorithm to evaluate the Glauber theory S-matrix elements for nucleus-nucleus collisions, both in the optical approximation and using the complete Glauber theory expressions. Woods-Saxon density distributions were used for projectile and target nuclei, with the c-parameter varied to match experimental interaction cross sections for stable isotopes from carbon-12 to chlorine-35 at approximately 1 GeV per nucleon.

## Key numbers

- The total nucleon-nucleon cross section used was σ_tot_NN = 43 mb, with σ_el_NN = 24.3 mb and slope parameter β = 0.35 fm²
- Experimental interaction cross sections ranged from 853 ± 6 mb for ¹²C to 1327 ± 14 mb for ³⁵Cl on a carbon target
- The Woods-Saxon diffuseness parameter a was fixed at 0.54 fm for all calculations
- The c-parameter for ¹²C was 0.85 fm in optical approximation and 1.39 fm in complete Glauber theory
- The σ_AB→AB* cross section for ¹²C was 10.4 ± 1.0 mb in optical approximation and 34 ± 5 mb in complete Glauber theory
- The σ_AB→AB* cross section for ³⁵Cl was 5.2 ± 1.0 mb in optical approximation and 42 ± 5 mb in complete Glauber theory
- The σ_AB→A*B* cross section for ¹²C was 169 ± 2 mb in optical approximation and 102 ± 1 mb in complete Glauber theory
- The σ_AB→A*B* cross section for ³⁵Cl was 208 ± 4 mb in optical approximation and 128 ± 2 mb in complete Glauber theory
- The absolute values of σ_AB→AB* calculated using complete Glauber theory are approximately 30-40 mb
- The difference leads to an increase in extracted root-mean-square radii by approximately 2% or 0.05 fm for nuclei with A < 40

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses by Oza (2001) and Oza (2002) that assumed the difference between interaction and reaction cross sections was negligible when extracting nuclear density parameters from experimental data. It contradicts the earlier estimate by KSh (2000) and Oga (1999) that the difference was less than a few percent, and provides more rigorous calculations than the "black-sphere" model used by KIO (2003), which found differences of approximately 60 mb or 4-6%. The results enable more accurate extraction of nuclear matter radii, particularly for nuclei with halo or skin structures, where the peripheral nature of the AB→AB* process may cause the difference to increase further.
