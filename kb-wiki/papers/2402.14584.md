# Estimating angular momenta of fission fragments from isomeric yield ratios

> Purpose: To deduce the angular momenta of fission fragments based on the observed isomeric yield ratios (IYR) in 25-MeV proton-induced fission of 238U and to compare these using Wilson's model. Method: A surrogate model of GEF has been developed to generate properties of primary fission fragments. Based on the excitation energy and angular momentum of fission fragments from GEF, an energy versus angular momentum matrix is reconstructed using a set of parameters. With such matrices as input, TALYS is used to calculate the de-excitation of the fission fragments, from which the IYRs are obtained. By varying one of the parameters, the root-mean-square angular momentum (Jrms), which determines the angular momentum distribution of the matrix, Jrms-dependent IYRs are obtained. Considering all primary fission fragments contributing to the IYR for a given fission product, the average angular momentum of those fragments is estimated. Results: Data of 31 IYRs in proton-induced fission of 238U were analysed. As a result, the average Jrms, equivalent to average angular momentum Jav, with uncertainties of 24 fission products, are presented. Considering the neutron emissions of the primary fission fragments, the Jav as a function of the primary fission fragment is presented. A mass dependency of Jav is observed in the proton-induced fission of 238U. Moreover, the Jav for A larger than 131 could be described by the parameterisation proposed by J. Wilson. In general, higher Jav are observed in the present work compared to those from Wilson et al. This is likely due to the higher excitation energy of the fissioning nuclei in this work compared to Wilson's. Furthermore, systematic measurements of the Jav of fission products in the symmetric mass region are presented for the first time. A decreasing trend with mass numbers is observed, which can not be explained by the proposal in Wilson's paper.

## Key claim

The authors deduce average angular momenta of fission fragments from isomeric yield ratios measured in 25-MeV proton-induced fission of 238U, using a surrogate model that couples GEF and TALYS. The key hedge is that the deduced angular momenta depend on the specific parameterization of the excitation-energy versus angular-momentum matrix, and the results are only valid for the 24 fission products where a conclusive angular momentum could be extracted (seven cases yielded no conclusive result).

## Method

A surrogate model was developed based on the fission code GEF (version 2023/1.2) to generate the excitation energy versus angular momentum (Ex-J) matrices of primary fission fragments for the 25-MeV proton-induced fission of 238U. The Ex-J matrix was parameterized using four parameters: root-mean-square angular momentum (Jrms), a curvature parameter C relating mean excitation energy to J(J+1), an energy offset E_offset, and an average energy spread sigma_E. By varying Jrms while keeping the other parameters fixed, Jrms-dependent isomeric yield ratios were calculated using the reaction code TALYS 1.96 to model the de-excitation of the fission fragments. The average angular momentum of the primary fission fragments contributing to each fission product was then estimated by matching the calculated isomeric yield ratios to the 31 measured values.

## Key numbers

*   Average root-mean-square angular momenta (Jrms), equivalent to average angular momentum Jav, were obtained with uncertainties for 24 fission products from 25-MeV proton-induced fission of 238U.
*   For the fission fragment 133Te, the GEF-derived Jrms values from four different scissioning nuclei were 10.8 hbar, 10.7 hbar, 9.2 hbar, and 7.6 hbar, with corresponding probabilities of 0.10, 0.20, 0.37, and 0.33.
*   The reconstructed Ex-J matrix for 133Te used a single Jrms of 9.0 hbar to approximate the four separate values.
*   The curvature parameter C for 133Te ranged from 0.025 MeV/hbar^2 to 0.037 MeV/hbar^2 across the four scissioning nuclei.
*   The average excitation energy offset E_offset for 133Te ranged from 7.6 MeV to 35.1 MeV across the four scissioning nuclei.
*   The average energy spread sigma_E for 133Te ranged from 4.9 MeV to 11.2 MeV across the four scissioning nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the GEF+TALYS approach previously reported by Gao (2023) by developing a surrogate model that allows systematic variation of the angular momentum distribution without crossing the yrast line into unphysical regions. The results are compared to the parameterization proposed by J. Wilson (2021), which assumes angular momenta are generated from collective motion in the ruptured neck; the present work finds that Wilson's model describes the data for mass numbers larger than 131 but fails for 130Sn and 131Te, and cannot explain the decreasing trend with mass number observed in the symmetric mass region. The study provides the first systematic measurements of average angular momenta of fission products in the symmetric mass region, enabling future tests of alternative models such as those by Randrup (2021, 2022) and Bulgac (2022).
