# Thermal photon anisotropic flow serves as a quark-gluon plasma viscometer

> Photons are a penetrating probe of the hot and dense medium created in heavy-ion collisions. We present state-of-the-art calculations of viscous photon emission from nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC. Thermal photons anisotropic flow coefficients v_n are computed, both with and without accounting for viscous corrections to the standard thermal emission rates. These corrections are found to have a larger effect on the v_n coefficients than the viscous suppression of hydrodynamic flow anisotropies. For thermal photons, the ratio v_2{SP}/v_3{SP} shows stronger sensitivity to the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) shear viscosity than for hadrons, and it can thus serve as a sensitive QGP viscometer.

## Key claim

The ratio of elliptic to triangular flow of thermal photons, \(v_2\{\mathrm{SP}\}/v_3\{\mathrm{SP}\}\), shows stronger sensitivity to the specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s\) of the quark-gluon plasma than the same ratio for hadrons, provided that viscous corrections to the photon emission rates are included; these corrections dominate over the viscous suppression of hydrodynamic flow anisotropies.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the boost-invariant code VISH2+1, with initial conditions from Monte-Carlo Glauber (MCGlb) and Monte-Carlo KLN (MCKLN) models. Thermal photon momentum distributions were computed by folding equilibrium and first-order shear-viscosity-corrected emission rates with the evolving hydrodynamic medium, using a lattice-based equation of state. Anisotropic flow coefficients \(v_n\{\mathrm{SP}\}\) were extracted via the scalar product method, correlating thermal photons with charged hadron flow planes.

## Key numbers

- For MCGlb initial conditions, the specific shear viscosity was set to \(\eta/s = 0.08\); for MCKLN initial conditions, \(\eta/s = 0.20\).
- Hydrodynamic simulations started at \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\) and decoupled at \(T = 120\) MeV; chemical freeze-out was at \(T_\mathrm{chem} = 165\) MeV.
- The \(p_T\)-integrated \(v_n\{\mathrm{SP}\}\) for thermal photons was computed over the range \(p_T = 1\) to 4 GeV in Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 2.76\) \(A\) TeV.
- For MCKLN initial conditions with \(\eta/s = 0.20\), the triangular flow \(v_3\{\mathrm{SP}\}\) of thermal photons was about half as large as for MCGlb initial conditions with \(\eta/s = 0.08\).
- The ratio \(v_2\{\mathrm{SP}\}/v_3\{\mathrm{SP}\}\) for thermal photons increases with \(\eta/s\), reflecting stronger viscous suppression of higher harmonic flows.

## Context

This work extends event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic calculations of thermal photon flow, building on prior studies of hadronic observables by Shen (2011) and Qiu (2011) using the same VISH2+1 code. It directly addresses the unexpectedly large direct photon elliptic flow measured at RHIC by Adare (2012) and at the LHC by Lohner (2012), which challenged earlier theoretical models by Chatterjee (2005). The proposed ratio \(v_2/v_3\) for thermal photons offers a new observable to disentangle thermal emission from pre-equilibrium photon sources, such as those from the QCD magnetic anomaly predicted by Basar (2012), and to extract the specific shear viscosity of the QGP.
