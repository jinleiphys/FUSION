# Intrinsic Transverse Momentum and Evolution in Weighted Spin Asymmetries

> The transverse momentum dependent (TMD) and collinear higher twist theoretical factorization frameworks are the most frequently used approaches to describing spin dependent hard cross sections weighted by and integrated over transverse momentum. Of particular interest is the contribution from small transverse momentum associated with the target bound state. In phenomenological applications, this contribution is often investigated using transverse momentum weighted integrals that sharply regulate the large transverse momentum contribution, for example with Gaussian parametrizations. Since the result is a kind of hybrid of TMD and collinear (inclusive) treatments, it is important to establish if and how the formalisms are related in applications to weighted integral observables. The suppression of a large transverse momentum tail, for example, can potentially affect the type of evolution that is applicable. We find that a naive version of a widely used identity relating the $k_T^2$-weighted and integrated Sivers TMD function to a renormalized twist-3 function has strongly ambiguous ultraviolet contributions, and that corrections to it are not necessarily perturbatively suppressed. We discuss the implications for applications, arguing in particular that the relevant evolution for transverse momentum weighted and integrated cross sections with sharp effective large transverse momentum cutoffs is of the TMD form rather than the standard renormalization group evolution of collinear correlation functions.

## Key claim

The widely used identity relating the $k_T^2$-weighted Sivers transverse momentum dependent (TMD) function to a renormalized twist-3 quark-gluon correlation function has strongly ambiguous ultraviolet contributions, and corrections to it are not necessarily perturbatively suppressed, contrary to common assumptions in the field.

## Method

The authors performed an explicit lowest-order perturbative QCD calculation of both sides of the proposed identity, using a non-zero mass quark as the target to regulate collinear singularities while remaining as close to true QCD as possible. They compared the renormalized twist-3 quark-gluon correlation function (calculated in dimensional regularization with minimal subtraction) with the $k_T^2$-weighted integral of the Sivers TMD function, examining whether the difference could be expressed as a perturbatively suppressed correction analogous to the unpolarized case.

## Key numbers

- The violation of the identity involves double logarithmic terms of the form $\ln^2(\mu/(1-x)m_q)$ at order $\alpha_s(\mu)^2$, where $\mu$ is the renormalization scale and $m_q$ is the quark mass
- The lowest order non-vanishing graphs for both the twist-3 correlation function and the TMD Sivers function contribute at order $\alpha_s$ in the strong coupling constant
- The calculation uses dimensional regularization with $D = 4 - 2\epsilon$ and generalized minimal subtraction for renormalization

## Context

This work extends the discussion of the relation between TMD and twist-3 collinear factorization formalisms for single transverse-spin asymmetries, originally proposed by Boer (2003) and studied further by Kang (2010) regarding sensitivity to large $k_T$ cutoff schemes. The paper argues that for phenomenological applications using sharp transverse momentum cutoffs (such as Gaussian parametrizations), the relevant evolution for weighted and integrated cross sections should be of the TMD Collins-Soper type rather than the DGLAP-type evolution of collinear twist-3 correlation functions. This impacts ongoing community efforts to extract non-perturbative TMD correlation functions from experimental data.
