# Three pion spectrum in the $I=3$ channel from lattice QCD

> Three-body states are critical to the dynamics of many hadronic resonances. We show that lattice QCD calculations have reached a stage where these states can be accurately resolved. We perform a calculation over a wide range of parameters and find all states below inelastic threshold agree with predictions from a state-of-the-art phenomenological formalism. This also illustrates the reliability of the formalism used to connect lattice QCD results to infinite volume physics. Our calculation is performed using three positively charged pions, with different lattice geometries and quark masses.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations of three-pion states in the maximal isospin (I=3) channel produce energy eigenvalues that agree with predictions from a relativistic three-body quantization condition (R3Q) when using low-energy constants (LECs) determined from lattice data, though some tension remains because the three-body contact term was set to zero rather than fitted.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations with two mass-degenerate quark flavors at two pion masses (220 MeV and 315 MeV), using six ensembles with different lattice geometries including cubic boxes and boxes elongated in one spatial direction. They constructed three-pion interpolating operators projected onto irreducible representations of the reduced rotational symmetry groups, computed all-to-all quark propagators using Laplacian-Heaviside smearing, and extracted energy eigenvalues from a variational analysis of correlation matrices. The lattice results were compared to predictions from a relativistic three-body quantization condition that uses chiral perturbation theory inspired two-pion interactions (modified inverse amplitude method) with two different sets of low-energy constants, with the three-body contact term set to zero.

## Key numbers

- Pion masses used: 220 MeV and 315 MeV
- Lattice spacing: 0.1210(2)(24) fm for ensembles E1-E3 (315 MeV) and 0.1215(3)(24) fm for ensembles E4-E6 (220 MeV)
- Total of 30 energy eigenvalues extracted below the inelastic threshold of 5 m_pi: 23 at 315 MeV pion mass (ensembles E1, E2, E3) and 7 at 220 MeV pion mass (ensembles E4, E5, E6)
- Reduced chi-squared per data point (chi^2/n) for GW LECs: 2.68 with correlations, 1.90 without correlations
- Reduced chi-squared per data point (chi^2/n) for GL LECs: 4.86 with correlations, 2.23 without correlations
- For GW LECs at 315 MeV ensembles: chi^2/n = 2.93; at 220 MeV ensembles: chi^2/n = 1.86
- Elongation values: eta = 1.00, 1.25, 2.00 for 315 MeV ensembles; eta = 1.00, 1.17, 1.33 for 220 MeV ensembles
- Number of configurations: 300 for E1-E3, 400 for E4-E6
- Example energy eigenvalue: E/m_pi = 3.074(3) for ensemble E1, A1u irrep

## Context

This work extends pioneering lattice calculations of three-hadron states by Beane (2007), Detmold (2008), and more recent work by Horz (2019) and Woss (2019) to a systematic study across multiple volumes and quark masses. The results validate the relativistic three-body quantization condition formalism developed by Mai (2017, 2018, 2019), Blanton (2019), and others, showing that both lattice QCD and phenomenological approaches have reached sufficient maturity to constrain three-body physics. This enables future applications to more challenging problems such as the Roper resonance N(1440)1/2+, the a1(1260) resonance, and exotic mesons being searched for at GlueX at Jefferson Lab.
