# Integrability and Conformal Symmetry in the BCS model

> Latex with macros included, 12 pages, Proceedings of the NATO Advanced Research Workshop on Statistical Field Theories, Como 18-23 June 2001. Editors: Andrea Cappelli and Giuseppe Mussardo. to be published by Kluwer, Academic Publishers. Corrected some typos and further acknowledgments

## Key claim

The reduced BCS Hamiltonian is exactly solvable and its integrability can be understood through a chain of connections to Gaudin’s spin model, inhomogeneous vertex models, perturbed Wess-Zumino-Witten conformal field theory, and twisted elliptic Chern-Simons theory, with the Richardson equations emerging as saddle-point conditions in a conformal field theory limit where the Kac-Moody level \(k\) approaches \(-2\) (i.e., \(\alpha_0 \to \infty\)).

## Method

The paper reviews the historical development and formal connections between Richardson’s exact solution (1963) for the reduced BCS Hamiltonian, the commuting conserved quantities \(R_i\) found by Cambiaggio, Rivas and Saraceno (1997), and Gaudin’s spin model (1976). It shows that Richardson’s equations for the pair energies \(e_\nu\) are identical to the equilibrium conditions of a two-dimensional electrostatic analogue, and that the wavefunction can be expressed as a conformal block of the \(SU(2)\) WZW model with screening operators. The off-shell algebraic Bethe ansatz applied to an inhomogeneous vertex model with a boundary matrix \(K_0\) yields the Richardson equations in the limit \(\eta \to 0\), while the perturbed Knizhnik-Zamolodchikov equations reduce to the eigenvalue equations for the \(R_i\) operators.

## Key numbers

* The mean level spacing of ultrasmall Aluminium grains is \(d \sim 0.45\) meV, with Debye energy \(\omega_D \sim 34\) meV, charging energy \(E_C \sim 46\) meV, and bulk gap \(\Delta \sim 0.38\) meV, giving \(\Omega = 2\omega_D/d \sim 150\) energy levels.
* The ratio \(\Delta/d \approx 0.38/0.45 \approx 0.84\) satisfies Anderson’s condition \(d \ge \Delta\) for possible disappearance of superconductivity.
* The Lanczos method was applied up to \(\Omega = 23\) energy levels, and the Density Matrix Renormalization Group up to \(\Omega = 400\) levels.

## Context

This review extends the line of work connecting the exactly solvable BCS model to integrable systems, following Richardson (1963) and Gaudin (1976). It unifies the conserved quantities of Cambiaggio, Rivas and Saraceno (1997) with the conformal field theory approach of Sierra (2000) and the off-shell algebraic Bethe ansatz of Babujian (1993) and Babujian and Flume (1993), as well as the vertex-model derivation by Amico, Falci and Fazio (2001). The connection to twisted elliptic Chern-Simons theory (Asorey, Falceto and Sierra, 2001) opens the possibility of generalizing the BCS model to other Lie groups and to geometric Langlands correspondence.
