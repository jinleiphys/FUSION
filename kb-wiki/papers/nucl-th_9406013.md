# Shape Coexistence Around $^{44}_{16}$S$_{28}$: The Deformed N=28 Region

> Masses, deformations, radii, and single-particle properties of the very neutron-rich Sulfur isotopes are investigated in the framework of the selfconsistent mean-field theory. The stability of the N=28 magic gap around $^{44}$S is discussed.

## Key claim

The N=28 spherical shell gap is weakened in neutron-rich nuclei below $^{48}$Ca, but the exact degree of deformation in $^{44}$S depends strongly on the theoretical model: the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model predicts a well-deformed prolate ground state, while the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (HF) model predicts a gamma-soft system with only small quadrupole deformation.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent mean-field calculations using two complementary approaches: the Skyrme-HF model with the SIII interaction on a three-dimensional Cartesian spline collocation lattice without imposed self-consistent symmetry, and the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with the NL-SH parameter set using an axially symmetric harmonic oscillator basis expansion. Pairing correlations were treated in the constant-gap approximation with reduced pairing strengths (75 keV for RMF, 200 keV for HF). Quadrupole deformation parameters $\beta_2$ and $\gamma$ were extracted from the calculated quadrupole moments of the proton, neutron, and mass distributions.

## Key numbers

- $^{44}$S: RMF predicts prolate ground state with $\beta_2=0.31$; oblate minimum at $\beta_2=0.16$ lies 0.8 MeV higher; spherical saddle point at $E^*=2.8$ MeV
- $^{44}$S: HF predicts small quadrupole deformation $\beta_2\sim0.13$; well-deformed prolate minimum ($\beta_2=0.28$) lies at $E^*\sim1.5$ MeV
- $^{40,42}$S: RMF predicts prolate ground states with $\beta_2\sim0.25$; oblate minima with $\beta_2\sim-0.16$ lie about 4 MeV higher
- $^{40,42}$S: HF gives prolate ($\beta_2\sim0.25$) and oblate ($\beta_2\sim-0.24$) configurations as practically degenerate
- $^{48}$S: RMF predicts $\beta_2=0.22$; HF predicts $\beta_2=0.13$, $\gamma=10^\circ$
- $^{42}$Si: oblate ground states with $\beta_2$ from -0.32 (FRDM) to -0.22 (HF)
- $^{46}$Ar: spherical (RMF, FRDM) or oblate with $\beta_2=-0.14$ (HF)
- $^{48}$Cr: prolate-deformed with $\beta_2=0.28$ (from prior work cited)
- $^{44}$S experimental half-life: $T_{1/2}=123\pm10$ ms (from prior work cited)
- $^{32}$Mg: low-lying $2^+$ state at 886 keV (from prior work cited)
- Maximum $|\Delta\beta_2|\sim0.10$ in RMF for $^{54}$S
- Systematic shift of $\sim0.08$ fm between HF and RMF results for rms charge radii

## Context

This work extends the study of shape coexistence from the well-known N=20 region (e.g., $^{32}$Mg, where deformed ground states arise from neutron excitations to the $1f_{7/2}$ shell, as shown by Campi (1975) and Bengtsson (1984)) to the neutron-rich N=28 region around $^{44}$S. The calculations contradict the simple spherical shell-model picture and show that the N=28 magic gap is weakened below $^{48}$Ca, consistent with suggestions from Sorlin (1993) based on beta-decay properties. The results enable experimental tests at GSI using fragmentation reactions of $^{48}$Ca on $^{9}$Be at relativistic energies, as planned by Schwalm (1994), and have implications for the nucleosynthesis of heavy Ca-Ti-Cr isotopes in the astrophysical context.
