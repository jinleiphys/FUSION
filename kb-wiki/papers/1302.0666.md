# Complete relativistic second-order dissipative hydrodynamics from the entropy principle

> We present a new derivation of relativistic dissipative hydrodynamic equations, which invokes the second law of thermodynamics for the entropy four-current expressed in terms of the single-particle phase-space distribution function obtained from Grad's 14-moment approximation. This derivation is complete in the sense that all the second-order transport coefficients are uniquely determined within a single theoretical framework. In particular, this removes the long-standing ambiguity in the relaxation time for bulk viscosity thereby eliminating one of the uncertainties in the extraction of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio from confrontation with the anisotropic flow data in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We find that in the one-dimensional scaling expansion, these transport coefficients prevent the occurrence of cavitation even for rather large values of the bulk viscosity estimated in lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The paper derives complete second-order relativistic dissipative hydrodynamic equations where all second-order transport coefficients are uniquely determined within a single theoretical framework, removing the long-standing ambiguity in the relaxation time for bulk viscosity; however, two parameters ψ and χ in the cross terms between bulk/shear and charge current remain undetermined, though they do not contribute to the scaling expansion considered.

## Method

The authors start from the entropy four-current expressed in terms of the single-particle phase-space distribution function via Boltzmann's H-function, then apply Grad's 14-moment approximation to expand the distribution function for small departures from equilibrium. By imposing the second law of thermodynamics (∂μSμ ≥ 0) and retaining terms up to third order in gradients, they obtain linear relationships between thermodynamic fluxes and extended thermodynamic forces, yielding evolution equations for bulk viscous pressure, particle diffusion current, and shear stress tensor with all transport coefficients expressed in terms of known thermodynamic integrals.

## Key numbers

- Shear relaxation time: τπ = 6/T × (η/s), where η/s is the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio
- Bulk relaxation time: τΠ = (ε+P)/(PT) × (ζ/s), where ζ/s is the bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio
- For a classical Boltzmann gas: β0 = 1/P and β2 = 3/(ε+P) + m²β²P/[2(ε+P)²]
- For massless particles, β2 = 3/(ε+P) and β0 = 1/P (finite, unlike previous derivations where β0 diverges)
- Initial conditions for Bjorken evolution: T0 = 310 MeV, τ0 = 0.5 fm/c, with η/s = 1/4π
- Lattice QCD estimates for ζ/s near Tc: 0.5 < ζ/s < 2.0 (in units of η/s|KSS = 1/4π)
- The derived τΠ prevents cavitation for the entire range of ζ/s values (0.5 < ζ/s < 2.0 near Tc) estimated in lattice QCD

## Context

This work extends the Israel-Stewart (Israel, 1979) second-order theory of relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics by providing explicit expressions for all second-order transport coefficients that were previously undetermined within thermodynamics alone (Muronga, 2003). The derivation resolves the ambiguity in the bulk relaxation time τΠ, which had been treated with ad hoc choices in prior hydrodynamic studies (Fries, 2008; Denicol, 2009; Song, 2009; Rajagopal, 2009), and enables more reliable extraction of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio from anisotropic flow data in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The results also enable consistent inclusion of large bulk viscosity near the QCD phase transition temperature without the cavitation (mechanical instability) that plagued earlier treatments.
