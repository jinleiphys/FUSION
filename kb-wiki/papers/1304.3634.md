# Towards the Little Bang Standard Model

> I review recent progress in developing a complete dynamical model for the evolution of the Little Bang fireballs created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and using the model to extract the transport properties and initial density fluctuations of the liquid quark-gluon plasma state of matter of which makes up these Little Bangs during the first half of their lives.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a "Little Bang Standard Model" is being constructed by combining initial-state models (such as IP-Glasma) with viscous hydrodynamic evolution, but the model is still under construction and the specific shear viscosity extracted depends on the initial-state model used, with different models yielding different estimates (e.g., 0.08 vs 0.2 for the specific shear viscosity).

## Method

The author reviews the use of relativistic viscous fluid dynamics (specifically the VISHNU and VISH2+1 codes) coupled to various initial-state models (IP-Glasma, MC-Glauber, MC-KLN) to simulate the evolution of quark-gluon plasma fireballs created in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC. The simulations are compared to experimental measurements of anisotropic flow coefficients (v_n), their event-by-event fluctuations, and flow-plane correlations to extract the specific shear viscosity (eta/s) of the quark-gluon plasma and to discriminate between different initial-state fluctuation models.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy densities in heavy-ion collisions exceed the energy density of atomic nuclei in their ground states by two to three orders of magnitude
- The Little Bang reaches approximate local thermal equilibrium on a time scale of order 1 fm/c (3 ioctoseconds)
- For Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 A GeV at RHIC, the effective specific shear viscosity is around 0.12 (1.5/(4pi))
- For Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 A TeV at the LHC, the effective specific shear viscosity is around 0.2 (2.5/(4pi))
- The pseudocritical temperature for hadronization T_cr is approximately 160 MeV
- The specific shear viscosity near T_cr can be as low as the KSS bound of 1/(4pi) = 0.08
- The IP-Glasma model reproduces the measured spectrum of anisotropic flow coefficients v_n for n=2 through 7 for ultra-central Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 A TeV

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic modeling approach pioneered by Kolb and Heinz (2003) and builds on the IP-Glasma initial-state model developed by Schenke (2012) based on Color Glass Condensate theory. The paper shows that earlier models (MC-Glauber and MC-KLN) cannot simultaneously describe the full set of flow data from the LHC, while the IP-Glasma model succeeds when combined with a temperature-dependent specific shear viscosity. This enables the extraction of both the transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma and the initial quantum fluctuation spectrum, analogous to how the CMB power spectrum constrained Big Bang cosmology.
