# Phase diagram for rotating compact stars with two high density phases

> For the classification of rotating compact stars with two high density phases a phase diagram in the angular velocity (Omega) - baryon number (N) plane is investigated. The dividing line N_crit(Omega) between configurations with one and two phases is correlated to a local maximum of the moment of inertia and can thus be subject to experimental verification by observation of the rotational behavior of accreting compact stars. Another characteristic line, which also can be measured is the transition line to black holes that of the maximum mass configurations. The positions and the shape of these lines are sensitive to changes in the equation of state (EoS) of stellar matter. A comparison of the regional structure of phase diagrams is performed for polytropic and relativistic mean field type EoS and correlations between the topology of the transition lines and the properties of two-phase EoS are obtained. Different scenarios of compact star evolution are discussed as trajectories in the phase diagram. It is shown that a population gap in the Omega - N plane for accreting compact stars would signal a high density phase transition and could be observed in the distribution of so called Z sources of quasi periodic oscillations in low-mass X-ray binaries.

# Phase diagram for rotating compact stars with two high density phases

## Key claim

The dividing line \(N_{\rm crit}(\Omega)\) between one-phase and two-phase compact star configurations is correlated with a local maximum of the moment of inertia, making it potentially observable through rotational behavior of accreting compact stars; a population gap in the \(\Omega\)-\(N\) plane for accreting stars would signal a high-density phase transition and could be observed in the distribution of Z sources of quasi-periodic oscillations in low-mass X-ray binaries.

## Method

The authors compute stationary, rigidly rotating compact star configurations in general relativity using a perturbation expansion following Hartle (1967). They employ two polytropic equations of state (EoS) with a Maxwell construction for the phase transition and compare these to a relativistic mean field (RMF) EoS consisting of a linear Walecka model for hadronic matter and a dynamical quark model for quark matter, using a Gibbs construction. The resulting configurations are classified in the plane of angular velocity \(\Omega\) and baryon number \(N\) to construct phase diagrams.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density \(n_0 = 0.17\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) used as reference for the polytropic EoS.
- Incompressibility of the RMF EoS in \(\beta\)-equilibrium: \(K^0_{\rm RMF} = 1125\ \text{MeV}\).
- Polytropic indices for the SH-S model: \(\Gamma_L = 2.5\) (low density), \(\Gamma_H = 1.9\) (high density).
- Polytropic indices for the H-SH model: \(\Gamma_L = 2.6\) (low density), \(\Gamma_H = 2.2\) (high density).

## Context

This work extends the concept of a phase diagram for rotating compact stars introduced by Glendenning (1992) and builds on earlier studies of braking index signals for deconfinement transitions by Glendenning et al. (1997). The authors provide a classification tool that can be used to constrain the high-density equation of state from observations of accreting compact stars in low-mass X-ray binaries, particularly from quasi-periodic oscillation data obtained with the Rossi X-ray Timing Explorer. The approach enables future identification of hybrid stars and third-family compact stars (so-called "twins") through population clustering in the \(\Omega\)-\(N\) plane.
