# Determination of the $πNN$ form factor from the threshold pion production of $pp$ scattering

> It is shown that the threshold productions of $π^0 pp$, $π^+ np$ and $π^+ d$ from $pp$ collisions can be consistently described by a model consisting of a pion s-wave rescattering and $N\bar N$ pair-terms of heavy-meson exchanges. The large difference between $σ^{tot} (pp\rightarrowπ^+ d)$ and $σ^{tot} (pp\rightarrowπ^+ np)$ is understood from the orthogonality of the deuteron and the $np$ scattering wave functions. In a calculation using the Paris potential to account for the initial and final $NN$ interactions, it is found that the data can be best reproduced by using a soft $πNN$ form factor with $Λ_π =650 $ MeV for a monopole form. This is consistent with an earlier study of pion production in the $Δ$ excitation region.

# Determination of the πNN Form Factor from the Threshold Pion Production of pp Scattering

## Key claim

The threshold pion production data from pp collisions can be consistently described by a model combining pion s-wave rescattering and N̄N pair-terms from heavy-meson exchanges, but only if a soft πNN form factor with a monopole cutoff Λ_π = 650 MeV is used; the abstract's claim of consistency depends on this specific choice of form factor, and using a larger cutoff (e.g., 1000 MeV) significantly overestimates the charged pion production cross sections.

## Method

The production operator for threshold pion production was constructed from nonrelativistic reductions of Feynman amplitudes derived from interaction Hamiltonians for π, σ, and ω fields, including pion s-wave rescattering and N̄N pair-terms of heavy-meson exchanges. Initial and final NN wave functions were calculated from the Paris potential, and the model was fitted to total cross section data for pp → ppπ⁰, pp → π⁺np, and pp → π⁺d reactions. The σ-exchange model (with g_σ²/4π = 16 and Λ_σ = 2000 MeV/c) was used to determine the πNN form factor by varying its monopole cutoff Λ_π to reproduce the charged pion production data.

## Key numbers

- Λ_π = 650 MeV/c: the monopole cutoff for the πNN form factor that best reproduces all threshold pion production data
- f²/4π = 0.079: the πNN coupling constant used in the primary calculation; using the smaller value f²/4π = 0.075 requires a slightly larger cutoff Λ_π = 680 MeV/c to obtain an equally good fit
- λ₁ ≈ 0.005 and λ₂ ≈ 0.05: the πN s-wave interaction parameters calculated from πN s-wave scattering lengths
- g_σ²/4π = 16 and Λ_σ = 2000 MeV/c: the parameters for the σ-exchange model used in the final calculations
- At energies near the π⁺np production threshold (292.30 MeV), the π⁺d production cross sections are almost two orders of magnitude larger than the π⁺np production cross sections

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Lee and Riska (1993), Niskanen (1994), and Horowitz, Meyer, and Griegel (1994) that identified short-range pion production mechanisms in pp → ppπ⁰, by incorporating new data on pp → π⁺np (Daehnick et al.) and pp → π⁺d (Heimberg et al., 1994). The finding of a soft πNN form factor with Λ_π = 650 MeV is consistent with an earlier study by Lee (1983) of pion production in the Δ excitation region. The analysis enables future work that must consider coupling between the final π⁰pp, π⁺np, and π⁺d channels to make further progress.
