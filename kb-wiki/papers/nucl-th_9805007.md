# Neutrino capture by r-process waiting-point nuclei

> We use the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation to include the effects of low-lying Gamow-Teller and first forbidden strength in neutrino capture by very neutron-rich nuclei with N = 50, 82, or 126. For electron neutrinos in what is currently considered the most likely r-process site the capture cross sections are two or more times previous estimates. We briefly discuss the reliability of our calculations and their implications for nucleosynthesis.

# Neutrino capture by r-process waiting-point nuclei

## Key claim
For electron neutrinos in the most likely r-process site (the hot bubble of a type II supernova), capture cross sections on very neutron-rich waiting-point nuclei with N=50, 82, or 126 are two or more times previous estimates when low-lying Gamow-Teller and first-forbidden strength is included, though the authors note their calculations remain uncertain and may still underestimate the true cross sections.

## Method
The authors used the neutron-proton Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) with single-particle energies from a parameterized Woods-Saxon potential, employing two different residual two-body interactions (a delta-function interaction and a combination of 7 Yukawa potentials fit to a Paris-potential G-matrix). They calculated spectrum-averaged neutrino capture cross sections for nuclei with closed neutron shells (N=50, 82, 126) using a supernova electron neutrino spectrum with average energy about 11 MeV and chemical potential eta_nu=3, comparing results from the full QRPA strength distributions to those obtained from a Gaussian approximation of the Gamow-Teller resonance.

## Key numbers
- For N=50 nuclei (Z=26-30, A=76-80): ratio of total spectrum-averaged cross section to allowed-only cross section is 1.6
- For N=82 nuclei (Z=40-48, A=122-130): ratio of total to allowed-only cross section is 1.7-1.8
- For N=126 nuclei (Z=62-68, A=188-194): ratio of total to allowed-only cross section is 2.1-2.3
- For 78Ni (N=50) with delta-function interaction and E_GT=E_IA: total cross section = 21.4 x 10^{-41} cm^2 (allowed contribution 19.9, forbidden 1.5)
- For 124Mo (N=82) with delta-function interaction and E_GT=E_IA: total cross section = 34.3 x 10^{-41} cm^2 (allowed 29.4, forbidden 4.9)
- For 190Gd (N=126) with delta-function interaction and E_GT=E_IA: total cross section = 46.2 x 10^{-41} cm^2 (allowed 34.3, forbidden 11.9)
- For 190Gd with G-matrix interaction and E_GT=E_IA: total cross section = 60.2 x 10^{-41} cm^2 (allowed 38.9, forbidden 21.3)
- Low-lying forbidden strength provides about 5-10% of total spectrum-averaged cross section for N=50, 10-20% for N=82, and 20-35% for N=126

## Context
This work extends earlier estimates of neutrino capture on r-process nuclei by Fuller and Meyer (1995), McLaughlin and Fuller (1996), and Qian et al. (1997), who had not included low-lying Gamow-Teller strength or first-forbidden transitions. The authors show that these previously neglected contributions systematically increase cross sections, particularly for heavier waiting-point nuclei. The results imply that neutrino capture will play a larger role in r-process nucleosynthesis than previously believed, though the authors caution that more precise calculations are not yet warranted given uncertainties in the neutrino flux from the hot bubble.
