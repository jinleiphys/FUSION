# Neutron electric dipole moment with external electric field method in lattice QCD

> We discuss a possibility that the Neutron Electric Dipole Moment (NEDM) can be calculated in lattice QCD simulations in the presence of the CP violating $θ$ term. In this paper we measure the energy difference between spin-up and spin-down states of the neutron in the presence of an uniform and static external electric field. We first test this method in quenched QCD with the RG improved gauge action on a $16^3\times 32$ lattice at $a^{-1}\simeq$ 2 GeV, employing two different lattice fermion formulations, the domain-wall fermion and the clover fermion for quarks, at relatively heavy quark mass $(m_{PS}/m_V \simeq 0.85)$. We obtain non-zero values of NEDM from calculations with both fermion formulations. We next consider some systematic uncertainties of our method for NEDM, using $24^3\times 32$ lattice at the same lattice spacing only with the clover fermion. We finally investigate the quark mass dependence of NEDM and observe a non-vanishing behavior of NEDM toward the chiral limit. We interpret this behavior as a manifestation of the pathology in the quenched approximation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the neutron electric dipole moment (NEDM) can be extracted directly from lattice QCD simulations using an external electric field method, avoiding the difficult momentum extrapolation required by previous form-factor approaches. However, the non-vanishing behavior of NEDM toward the chiral limit is interpreted as a manifestation of the pathology in the quenched approximation, meaning the results are not reliable at light quark masses.

## Method

The authors introduce a static, uniform electric field in Minkowski space into quenched lattice QCD by modifying spatial link variables, then measure the energy difference between spin-up and spin-down neutron states at zero momentum. They construct ratios of nucleon propagators with different spinor components, using reweighting with the factor e^{iθQ} to incorporate the CP-violating θ term, and extract the EDM from the exponential time dependence of these ratios. The method is tested with both domain-wall fermion and clover fermion formulations on 16³×32 and 24³×32 lattices at a lattice spacing of a⁻¹ ≈ 2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a⁻¹ = 1.902(50) GeV, determined from string tension assuming σ = (440 MeV)²
- On 16³×32 lattice with domain-wall fermion at heavy quark mass (m_PS/m_V ≈ 0.88): d_N = -0.017(8) e·fm for neutron, d_N = 0.020(10) e·fm for proton (fitting range t-t_src ∈ [6,11] with t_src=1)
- On 16³×32 lattice with clover fermion at heavy quark mass (m_PS/m_V ≈ 0.85): d_N = -0.021(11) e·fm for neutron, d_N = 0.026(13) e·fm for proton
- On 24³×32 lattice with clover fermion at heaviest quark mass, t_src=8: d_N = -0.025(8) e·fm for neutron, d_N = 0.024(11) e·fm for proton (fitting range 5 ≤ t-t_src ≤ 9)
- On 24³×32 lattice with clover fermion, average over three electric field directions: d_N = -0.0276(72) e·fm for neutron, d_N = 0.0278(87) e·fm for proton
- Topological charge distribution width on 16³×32 lattice: ⟨Q²⟩ = 9.37(44); on 24³×32 lattice: ⟨Q²⟩ = 33.6(1.1)
- Experimental upper bound for neutron: |d_N| < 6.3×10⁻¹³ e·fm (90% C.L.)

## Context

This work extends the earlier form-factor approach by Shintani et al. (2005) for calculating NEDM in lattice QCD, which required extrapolation to zero momentum transfer and suffered from large statistical errors. The new external electric field method avoids momentum extrapolation entirely. The results are compared with the earlier form-factor calculation on the same 16³×32 lattice with domain-wall fermion, which gave F₃(q²≈0.58 GeV²)/m_N = -0.024(5) e·fm for neutron and 0.021(6) e·fm for proton, showing agreement in sign and magnitude. The non-vanishing behavior toward the chiral limit is identified as a quenched artifact, motivating future calculations with dynamical fermions. The method enables direct EDM extraction without momentum extrapolation, but requires careful treatment of boundary effects from the non-periodic electric field.
