# Quarkonia production in a Langevin approach

> We aim to describe the process of dissociation and recombination of quarkonia in the quark-gluon plasma. Therefore we developed a model which allows to observe the time evolution of a system with various numbers of charm-anticharm-quark pairs at different temperatures. The motion of the heavy quarks is realized within a Langevin approach. We use a simplified version of a formalism developed by Blaizot et al. in which an Abelian plasma is considered where the heavy quarks interact over a Coulomb like potential. We have demonstrated, that the system reaches the expected thermal distribution in the equilibrium limit.

# Quarkonia production in a Langevin approach

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that a Langevin-based model with a screened Coulomb potential and constant drag coefficient reproduces the expected thermal equilibrium distribution for charm-anticharm pairs in a box, but the equilibration times are very long, indicating that chemical effects beyond kinetic scales are needed to explain the relaxation.

## Method
The motion of charm and anticharm quarks in a quark-gluon plasma is simulated using Langevin dynamics with a drag force and random momentum kicks, plus an attractive force from a screened Coulomb potential. The simulation is performed in a cubic box with reflecting boundaries, using a constant friction coefficient derived from perturbative QCD with a Debye screening mass. Bound states are defined by the classical condition of negative relative energy of the pair.

## Key numbers
- Charm quark mass set to M = 1.8 GeV
- Critical temperature T_C = 160 MeV with alpha_s(T_C) = 0.5 and running coupling parameter C = 0.76
- Box side length 8 fm for the main simulations
- Cut-off for the potential and friction set to Lambda = 4 GeV
- Initial bound states created with pair energy of -700 MeV and relative velocity from a Maxwell distribution with v_0^2 ~ 0.3
- At T = 160 MeV in an 8^3 fm^3 box, the grand canonical fraction of J/psi is 0.0066 and the numerical fraction is 0.0059
- At T = 160 MeV in a 10^3 fm^3 box, the grand canonical fraction is 0.0035 and the numerical fraction is 0.0029
- At T = 160 MeV in a 12^3 fm^3 box, the grand canonical fraction is 0.002 and the numerical fraction is 0.0017

## Context
This work extends the formalism of Blaizot et al. for heavy-quark interactions in an Abelian plasma, using a simplified version with a constant drag coefficient rather than a distance-dependent one. The model is designed to eventually describe J/psi production in heavy-ion collisions, where recombination processes are needed to explain measurements at RHIC that did not show the increased suppression predicted by Matsui and Satz (1986). The next steps include implementing an expanding fireball medium, distance-dependent friction, and quantum mechanical treatments using Wigner functions as in Shuryak's approach.
