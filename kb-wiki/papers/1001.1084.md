# Five-quark components in baryons

> Evidence has been accumulating for the existence of significant intrinsic non-perturbative five-quark components in various baryons. The inclusion of the five-quark components gives a natural explanation of the excess of $\bar d$ over $\bar u$, significant quark orbital angular momentum in the proton, the problematic mass and decay pattern of the lowest $1/2^-$ baryon nonet, etc.. A breathing mode of $qqq\leftrightarrow qqqq\bar q$ is suggested for the lowest $1/2^-$ baryon octet. Evidence of a predicted member of the new scheme, $Σ^*(1/2^-)$ around 1380 MeV, is introduced.

## Key claim
The paper argues that significant intrinsic non-perturbative five-quark (qqqqq-bar) components exist in baryons, and that including these components resolves several long-standing puzzles in baryon spectroscopy. The claim is hedged by noting that while evidence is accumulating, the picture remains a proposal that requires further experimental confirmation, particularly for the predicted new Σ*(1/2−) resonance around 1380 MeV.

## Method
The author reviews experimental evidence for five-quark components in the proton (from deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan experiments) and in excited baryons (from decay and electromagnetic transition data). A constituent quark model is extended to treat each baryon as a mixture of three-quark (qqq) and five-quark (qqqqq-bar) components, with the five-quark components described by diquark cluster configurations. The model is applied to fit the mass spectrum and angular distributions of the K−p → Λπ+π− reaction to search for a predicted Σ*(1/2−) resonance.

## Key numbers
- The measured value of d-bar − u-bar ≈ 0.12 in the proton demands five-quark components in the proton to be more than 12%.
- For the N*(1535), about 45% five-quark components are needed, and about 20% in the proton, to fit the measured electromagnetic transition γ*N → N*(1535) amplitude.
- Constituent quark masses used: mu = md = 290 MeV, ms = 430 MeV.
- In the fit to K−p → Λπ+π− data, a single Σ* resonance gives mass 1385.3 ± 0.7 MeV and width 46.9 ± 2.5 MeV; a two-resonance fit gives a narrower Σ*(3/2+) at 1386.1+1.1−0.9 MeV with width 34.9+5.1−4.9 MeV and a broader Σ*(1/2−) at 1381.3+4.9−8.3 MeV with width 118.6+55.2−35.1 MeV.
- The Δ*(1620) 1/2− is about 85 MeV below the Nρ threshold; Σ*(1750) 1/2− is about 70 MeV below the NK* threshold; Ξ*(1950) is about 60 MeV below the ΛK* threshold; a predicted Ω* 1/2− would be around 2160 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the meson-cloud model of Thomas and the diquark cluster picture proposed by Zou (2005) to explain the d-bar/u-bar asymmetry in the proton. It contradicts the classical three-quark constituent quark model predictions for the lowest 1/2− baryon nonet, particularly the mass ordering of N*(1535), N*(1440), and Λ*(1405). The paper enables future experimental searches at JPARC, CEBAF, and BEPCII for the predicted Σ*(1/2−) resonance and other five-quark-dominated states, and calls for high-statistics studies of reactions such as K−p → πΣ*, γN → KΣ*, and ψ → Σ-bar Σ*.
