# Five-quark components in baryons

> Evidence has been accumulating for the existence of significant intrinsic non-perturbative five-quark components in various baryons. The inclusion of the five-quark components gives a natural explanation of the excess of $\bar d$ over $\bar u$, significant quark orbital angular momentum in the proton, the problematic mass and decay pattern of the lowest $1/2^-$ baryon nonet, etc.. A breathing mode of $qqq\leftrightarrow qqqq\bar q$ is suggested for the lowest $1/2^-$ baryon octet. Evidence of a predicted member of the new scheme, $╬Ż^*(1/2^-)$ around 1380 MeV, is introduced.

## Key claim
The paper argues that significant intrinsic non-perturbative five-quark (qqqqq-bar) components exist in baryons, and that including these components resolves several long-standing puzzles in baryon spectroscopy. The claim is hedged by noting that while evidence is accumulating, the picture remains a proposal that requires further experimental confirmation, particularly for the predicted new ╬Ż*(1/2ŌłÆ) resonance around 1380 MeV.

## Method
The author reviews experimental evidence for five-quark components in the proton (from deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan experiments) and in excited baryons (from decay and electromagnetic transition data). A constituent quark model is extended to treat each baryon as a mixture of three-quark (qqq) and five-quark (qqqqq-bar) components, with the five-quark components described by diquark cluster configurations. The model is applied to fit the mass spectrum and angular distributions of the KŌłÆp ŌåÆ ╬øŽĆ+ŽĆŌłÆ reaction to search for a predicted ╬Ż*(1/2ŌłÆ) resonance.

## Context
This work extends the meson-cloud model of Thomas and the diquark cluster picture proposed by Zou (2005) to explain the d-bar/u-bar asymmetry in the proton. It contradicts the classical three-quark constituent quark model predictions for the lowest 1/2ŌłÆ baryon nonet, particularly the mass ordering of N*(1535), N*(1440), and ╬ø*(1405).
