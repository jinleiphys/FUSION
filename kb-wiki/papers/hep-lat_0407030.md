# Decuplet Baryon Masses in Partially Quenched Chiral Perturbation Theory

> The masses of the spin-3/2 baryons are calculated to next-to-next-to-leading order in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory and partially quenched heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. The calculation is performed for three light flavors in the isospin limit. These results are necessary for extrapolating QCD and partially quenched QCD lattice calculations of the decuplet baryon masses.

## Key claim

The masses of the spin-3/2 decuplet baryons are calculated to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in both heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) and partially quenched heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (PQHBχPT), working in the isospin limit of SU(3) flavor with three light flavors; the partially quenched calculation uses three valence, three ghost, and three sea quarks with two of the sea quarks degenerate, and the results are necessary for extrapolating lattice QCD and partially quenched lattice QCD data to physical quark masses, but the low-energy constants (LECs) remain undetermined from this calculation alone and must be fixed by fitting to lattice data or experiment.

## Method

The authors embed octet and decuplet baryons in rank-3 flavor tensors and use heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory with a consistent power counting where the octet-decuplet mass splitting Δ is treated as order q (a typical small pion momentum). They compute the decuplet baryon self-energies to NNLO (order m_q²) by evaluating one-loop diagrams with octet and decuplet intermediate states, tree-level contributions from higher-dimensional operators at order q⁴, and wave-function renormalization corrections, using dimensional regularization with modified minimal subtraction (MS-bar). The calculation is performed first in standard χPT and then generalized to PQχPT, where the graded SU(6|3) flavor symmetry group is used to handle valence, sea, and ghost quarks, and the flavor-singlet η propagator is treated with the large m₀ limit appropriate for PQQCD.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant f = 132 MeV (used throughout as the convention for the chiral Lagrangian)
- Leading-order decuplet mass contribution: M_T^(1) = (2/3) γ_M m_T - 2 σ̄_M tr(m_Q), where coefficients m_T are given in Table 1 (e.g., for Δ: 3 m_u; for Ω⁻: 3 m_s)
- Next-to-leading order contribution: M_T^(3/2) = -(5 H²)/(72 π f²) Σ_φ A_φ^T m_φ³ - (C²)/((4π f)²) Σ_φ B_φ^T F(m_φ, -Δ, μ), with coefficients A_φ^T and B_φ^T tabulated (e.g., for Δ: A_π^Δ = 5/6, B_π^Δ = 2/3)
- Next-to-next-to-leading order contribution includes tree-level terms with LECs t₁^M through t₅^M, loop functions L(m,μ) = m² log(m²/μ²) and L̄(m,μ) = m⁴ log(m²/μ²), and additional 1/M_B suppressed terms with coefficients proportional to H²/M_B and C²/M_B

## Context

This work extends the NNLO calculation of decuplet baryon masses in standard χPT (following earlier work by Jenkins (1992), Lebed (1994), and Borasoy (1997)) to the partially quenched case, which is essential for analyzing lattice QCD data where sea and valence quark masses are varied independently. The results enable chiral extrapolations of partially quenched lattice calculations of decuplet baryon masses (such as those by the CP-PACS and UKQCD collaborations) to physical quark masses, and allow determination of the low-energy constants up to order m_q², complementing similar NNLO calculations for octet baryons by Beane (2002) and Walker-Loud (2004).
