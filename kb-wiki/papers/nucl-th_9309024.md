# Microscopic description of the beta delayed deuteron emission from \bbox{^6}He

> The beta delayed deuteron emission from $^6$He is studied in a dynamical microscopic cluster model. This model gives a reasonably good description for all the subsystems of $^6$He and $^6$Li in a coherent way, without any free parameter. The beta decay transition probability to the $^6$Li ground state is underestimated by a few percents. The theoretical beta delayed deuteron spectrum is close to experiment but it is also underestimated by about a factor 1.7. We argue that, in spite of their different magnitudes, both underestimations might have a common origin. The model confirms that the neutron halo part of the $^6$He wave function plays a crucial role in quenching the beta decay toward the $α$ + d channel.

## Key claim

The microscopic cluster model reproduces the beta delayed deuteron spectrum from ^6He to within a factor of about 1.7 of experiment, but the authors argue that both this underestimation and a smaller few-percent underestimation of the ground-state transition probability may share a common origin, possibly meson-exchange current corrections.

## Method

The authors employed a fully microscopic six-nucleon cluster model using the Minnesota nucleon-nucleon interaction with exchange parameter u=0.92, with no free parameters. The ^6He ground state was described with a three-cluster wave function including alpha+nn, n(alpha n), and t+t configurations, while the ^6Li ground state and alpha+d scattering states were described consistently with the same interaction. The Gamow-Teller matrix elements for beta decay to both the ^6Li ground state and the alpha+d continuum were computed analytically using variational wave functions expanded in tempered Gaussian bases covering distances up to 20-25 fm.

## Key numbers

- Theoretical beta decay half-life to ^6Li ground state: t_{1/2} ≈ 835 ms, compared to experimental value 806.7 ± 1.5 ms (a 3-4% deviation)
- Total theoretical branching ratio for beta delayed deuteron emission: 6.0 × 10^{-6}
- Branching ratio for deuterons above experimental cutoff: 3.1 × 10^{-6}, compared to experimental value (7.6 ± 0.6) × 10^{-6} from Borge (1993)
- ^6He alpha+n+n three-cluster separation energy: 0.961 MeV, practically reproducing the experimental value 0.975 MeV
- ^6Li alpha+d cluster separation energy: 1.47 MeV, matching experiment
- ^6He wave function basis functions cover the alpha(nn) relative coordinate up to approximately 20 fm, with Gaussian cutoff starting at roughly 30 fm
- Neglecting the 8-10 fm to 25 fm region of the ^6He wave function causes an order of magnitude error in the decay spectrum

## Context

This work extends the semimicroscopic model of Baye, Suzuki, and Descouvemont (1993) by providing a fully consistent microscopic description of both initial and final states, avoiding the hybrid treatment where the ^6He wave function was microscopic but the alpha+d final state was treated with a potential model. It improves upon the pure alpha+dineutron model of Descouvemont and Leclercq-Willain (1992), which overestimated the branching ratio by an order of magnitude, and the three-body model of Zhukov et al. (1993), which gave inconsistent treatment of the alpha+d scattering state. The results confirm that the neutron halo of ^6He plays a crucial role in quenching the beta decay to the alpha+d channel, and the authors suggest that meson-exchange current corrections, which are typical of weak processes in few-nucleon systems, may explain the remaining discrepancies.
