# News from Lattice QCD on Heavy Quark Potentials and Spectral Functions of Heavy Quark States

> We discuss recent lattice results on in-medium properties of hadrons and focus on thermal properties of heavy quark bound states. We will clarify the relation between heavy quark free energies and potentials used to analyze the melting of heavy quark bound states. Furthermore, we present calculations of meson spectral functions which indicate that the charmonium ground states, J/psi and eta_c, persist in the quark gluon plasma as well defined resonances with no significant change of their zero temperature masses at least up to T ~ 1.5 T_c. We also briefly comment on the current status of lattice calculations at non-vanishing baryon number density.

## Key claim

Lattice calculations of charmonium spectral functions indicate that the J/ψ and ηc ground states persist as well-defined resonances with no significant change of their zero-temperature masses at least up to T ~ 1.5 Tc, contradicting earlier potential model calculations that predicted earlier dissociation; however, the heavy quark free energies commonly used in those models cannot be directly identified with the potential energy because they contain substantial entropy contributions.

## Method

The authors compute thermal meson correlation functions in quenched QCD on isotropic and anisotropic lattices using standard and improved Wilson fermion actions, then reconstruct spectral functions via the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM). They also calculate color-averaged and color-singlet heavy quark free energies from Polyakov loop correlation functions, and extract the heavy quark energy by differentiating the free energy with respect to temperature to separate entropy contributions.

## Key numbers

- Charmonium S-state correlators (J/ψ and ηc) show no significant modification up to T = 1.5 Tc; modifications in the vector channel (J/ψ) are of order 10% at this temperature.
- P-state charmonia show strong thermal modifications already at temperatures slightly above Tc.
- One analysis finds the J/ψ resonance disappears abruptly at T ~ 1.9 Tc (Asakawa 2003), while another finds a resonance peak with reduced strength still visible at T = 2.25 Tc and complete disappearance at T = 3.0 Tc (Datta 2003).
- The asymptotic value of the color-averaged free energy at large distances decreases almost linearly with temperature: F∞(T) ~ -0.2 T.
- The chiral critical point is estimated at μB^crit ~ 400 MeV, compared to the RHIC freeze-out chemical potential μB^freeze ~ 29 MeV.

## Context

This work extends lattice studies of in-medium hadron properties (Hatsuda 2002) and directly addresses the discrepancy between potential model calculations (Digal 2001) that predicted early charmonium dissociation and spectral function analyses (Asakawa 2003, Datta 2003, Umeda 2003) that find bound states up to higher temperatures. The clarification that heavy quark free energies contain significant entropy contributions reconciles lattice results with potential model approaches and enables more reliable predictions for J/ψ suppression in heavy ion collisions. The paper also reviews progress on finite-density QCD (Fodor 2002, Allton 2003, Philipsen 2003) and the equation of state at non-zero baryon chemical potential.
