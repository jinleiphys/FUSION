# Bremsstrahlung from dense plasmas and the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect

> The suppression of the bremsstrahlung cross section due to multiple scattering of the emitting electrons is an important effect in dense media (Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect). Here, we study the emission from a dense, fully-ionized and non-relativistic hydrogen plasma. Using the dielectric approach, we relate optical properties such as emission and absorption to equilibrium force-force correlation functions, which allow for a systematic perturbative treatment with the help of thermodynamic Green functions. By considering self-energy and vertex corrections, medium modifications such as multiple scattering of the emitting electrons are taken into account. Results are presented for the absorption coefficient as a function of the frequency at various densities. It is shown that the modification of the inverse bremsstrahlung due to medium effects becomes more significant in the low frequency and high density region.

## Key claim

The authors present a fully microscopic calculation of the absorption spectrum for a dense, fully-ionized, non-relativistic hydrogen plasma, showing that the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) suppression of bremsstrahlung can be derived from a systematic perturbative treatment of force-force correlation functions using thermodynamic Green functions, without introducing a macroscopic coherence length as a parameter. The suppression becomes more significant in the low frequency and high density region.

## Method

The absorption coefficient is expressed in terms of the real part of a force-force correlation function via the dielectric approach, using linear response theory and the Kubo formula. The force-force correlation function is evaluated diagrammatically using thermodynamic Green functions, where medium effects such as multiple scattering of electrons are included by replacing free electron propagators with dressed propagators obtained from a self-consistent solution of the Dyson equation for the electron self-energy. The self-energy is calculated iteratively using a statically screened Debye-Hückel potential for the electron-ion interaction, and the resulting spectral function is used to compute the absorption coefficient in the high-frequency limit.

## Key numbers

- The plasma parameters used for numerical calculations: electron density \( n_\mathrm{e} = 10^{-6} \, a_\mathrm{B}^{-3} \) and temperature \( k_\mathrm{B}T = 2 \, \mathrm{Ry} = 27.2 \, \mathrm{eV} \).
- The screening parameter is given by \( \kappa^2 = \sum_c n_c Z_c^2 e^2 / (\epsilon_0 k_\mathrm{B} T) \), with the Debye-Hückel potential \( v_q^{\mathrm{ei}} = -Z_\mathrm{i} e^2 / [\epsilon_0 \Omega_0 (q^2 + \kappa^2)] \).
- The Born approximation absorption coefficient is given by Eq. (4): \( \alpha^{\mathrm{B}}(\omega) = (C / \hbar \omega^3) \, n_\mathrm{e} n_\mathrm{i} \, \sinh(\hbar \omega / 2 k_\mathrm{B} T) \, K_0(\hbar \omega / 2 k_\mathrm{B} T) \), where \( C^{-1} = 3 \sqrt{2} \epsilon_0^3 \pi^{3/2} m_\mathrm{e}^{3/2} c (k_\mathrm{B} T)^{1/2} / e^6 \).

## Context

This work extends the quantum-statistical approach developed by Reinholz et al. (2000) for force-force correlation functions and applies it to the LPM effect in dense plasmas, which was previously treated semi-classically by Landau and Pomeranchuk (1953) and quantum-statistically by Migdal (1956). The authors provide a fully microscopic alternative to the coherence-length-based approach of Knoll and Voskresensky (1996), who treated the LPM effect using medium-modified single particle propagators with a finite lifetime set as a parameter. This enables a self-consistent calculation of the electron spectral function and absorption spectrum without external parameters, and can be extended to include vertex corrections and dynamical screening effects.
