# The N N -> N Delta cross section in nuclear matter

> We present calculations of the N N -> N Delta cross section in nuclear matter within the one-pion exchange model taking into account pion collectivity, vertex renormalization by the contact nuclear interactions and Dirac effective masses of the baryons due to coupling with the scalar sigma field. Introducing the Dirac effective masses leads to an in-medium reduction of the cross section. The experimental data on pion multiplicities from the collisions of Ca+Ca, Ru+Ru and Au+Au at 0.4-1.5 A GeV are well described by BUU calculations with the in-medium cross section.

## Key claim

The in-medium \(NN \to N\Delta\) cross section is significantly reduced at high nuclear densities when Dirac effective masses of nucleons and \(\Delta\) resonances are included in the one-pion exchange model, contrary to earlier calculations that omitted this effect and predicted a density-driven increase. The reduction is strong enough to bring transport model predictions of pion multiplicities into agreement with experimental data from heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies.

## Method

The authors calculated the \(NN \to N\Delta\) cross section in nuclear matter using a nonrelativistic one-pion exchange model (OPEM) with medium modifications including pion collectivity via the pion self-energy, vertex renormalization by contact nuclear interactions parameterized by Landau-Migdal parameters, and Dirac effective masses for nucleons and \(\Delta\) resonances from coupling to the scalar \(\sigma\) field. The in-medium cross sections were then implemented into a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport code to simulate pion production in heavy-ion collisions at beam energies of 0.4–1.5 A GeV.

## Key numbers

- The \(\Delta\) pole mass is \(m_\Delta = 1.232\) GeV, with a vacuum width at the pole mass of \(\Gamma_\Delta^0 = 0.118\) GeV.
- The nuclear saturation density is \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The \(\Delta\)-spreading width constant is \(\Gamma_{sp} = 80\) MeV, derived from the empirical spreading potential \(W_{sp} = (23\pm5) - i(43\pm5)\) MeV at \(\rho = \rho_0\).
- For the combination NL1-Set2, the in-medium \(pp \to n\Delta^{++}\) cross section drops by two orders of magnitude when the baryon density increases from 0 to \(3\rho_0\).
- The coupling constants used are \(f = 1.008\) and \(f_\Delta = 2.202\).
- The cut-off parameter \(\beta_0 = 0.2\) GeV/c is used in the \(\Delta\) width parameterization.
- The Dirac effective masses of nucleons are reduced by 300–400 MeV at normal nuclear density, depending on the relativistic mean field parameter set (NL1, NL2, or RHA).

## Context

This work extends earlier OPEM studies by Bertsch (1988), Wu and Ko (1989), and Herrmann and Ring (1995) that predicted an increase of the \(NN \to N\Delta\) cross section with density, by adding the previously neglected Dirac effective mass corrections. The results agree with the reduction found by ter Haar and Malfliet (1987) using the relativistic Dirac-Brueckner approach. The calculated in-medium cross sections replace the phenomenological density-dependent modification factor proposed by Li, Chen, and Ma (2001) and enable more realistic BUU simulations of pion production in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies, resolving the long-standing overprediction of pion multiplicities.
