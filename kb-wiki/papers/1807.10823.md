# High Baryon Densities Achieveable at RHIC and LHC

> In high energy collisions nuclei are practically transparent to each other but produce very hot, nearly baryon-free, matter in the central rapidity region. Where do the baryons go? We calculate the energy loss of the nuclei using the color glass condensate model. Using a space-time picture of the collision we calculate the baryon and energy densities of the receding baryonic fireballs. For central collisions of large nuclei at RHIC and LHC we find baryon densities more than ten times that of atomic nuclei over a large volume which appear at high rapidities. These results can and are being used as initial conditions for subsequent hydrodynamic evolution and could test the equation of state of matter at very high baryon densities.

## Key claim

Using the color glass condensate model, the authors calculate that in central collisions of large nuclei at RHIC and LHC, baryon densities more than ten times that of normal nuclear matter appear over a large volume at high rapidities, but this result depends on the assumption that the matter in the baryonic fireballs equilibrates on the same time scale as in the central rapidity region.

## Method

The authors employed the McLerran-Venugopalan color glass condensate model to compute the energy loss and internal excitation of baryons in high energy heavy ion collisions, solving differential equations for the projectile nucleus kinetic energy and momentum loss. They then used a space-time picture to calculate the baryon and energy densities of the receding baryonic fireballs, and applied a crossover equation of state (switching from hadron resonance gas with excluded volumes to perturbative QCD plasma) to infer temperatures, chemical potentials, and entropy densities assuming equilibration.

## Key numbers

- For central AuAu collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 62.4 GeV, the rapidity of the central core is y_P = 1.89 with entropy per baryon s/n = 16.3.
- For central AuAu collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, the rapidity of the central core is y_P = 1.89 (value not explicitly given for 200 GeV in the text; the text gives y_P = 1.89 for 62.4 GeV and y_P = 3.76 for 2.76 TeV).
- For central PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, the rapidity of the central core is y_P = 3.76 with entropy per baryon s/n = 49.6.
- For central PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, the rapidity of the central core is y_P = 4.06 with entropy per baryon s/n = 58.2.
- The projectile nucleus loses nearly 3 units of rapidity in the first 0.1 fm/c for AuAu at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.
- Baryon densities in the fireballs reach values an order of magnitude greater than normal nuclear matter density.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Anishetty, Koehler and McLerran (1980), which found nuclei compressed by a factor of 3.5 and excited to an energy density of about 2 GeV/fm^3, but which was pursued only sporadically. The results provide initial conditions for subsequent 3-dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamic evolution and could test the equation of state of matter at very high baryon densities, with entropy per baryon values spanning a range on both sides of a suspected critical point in the QCD phase diagram.
