# Pion re-scattering in pi0 production

> This work discusses the approximations frequently used to calculate the contribution to pion production in proton-proton scattering near threshold from irreducible pion re-scattering. The reference result is obtained from the quantum mechanical reduction of the corresponding Feynman diagram to Time-Ordered Field Theory diagrams. We conclude that the Distorted Wave Born Approximation is quite adequate at threshold energies and even above. We discuss the choices for the energy of the exchanged pion, both for its propagator and for the piN amplitude describing the re-scattering vertex. The calculation employs a physical model for nucleons and pions -- pseudo-vector coupling for the piNN vertex and realistic amplitudes for the piN re-scattering and for the NN transitions in the initial and final states.

## Key claim

The Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) is adequate for calculating irreducible pion re-scattering contributions to pion production in proton-proton collisions near threshold and even above, but the choice of energy for the exchanged pion in the re-scattering operator can affect the total cross section by as much as 30%, with the largest effect close to threshold.

## Method

The authors decomposed the Feynman diagram for irreducible pion re-scattering in the reaction pp → ppπ⁰ into Time-Ordered Field Theory (TOPT) diagrams, then extracted the effective pion propagator appropriate for DWBA calculations. They compared this reference result against three common approximations for the exchanged pion energy: the static approximation (pion energy taken as zero), the on-shell approximation (pion energy as the difference between initial and final on-mass-shell energy of the emitting nucleon), and the fixed kinematics approximation (pion energy taken at threshold kinematics). The calculation employed pseudo-vector coupling for the πNN vertex, χPT πN amplitudes, and the Bonn B potential for the NN interaction.

## Key numbers

- The stretched boxes amplitude is less than 1% of the total amplitude for the final-state interaction case, about 6 times more suppressed than in the toy model of Hanhart (2001)
- The stretched boxes cross section is at most 0.02% of the DWBA cross section for the final-state interaction
- For the initial-state interaction, stretched boxes cross sections are less than 1.2% of DWBA cross sections for laboratory energies up to T_lab ~ 440 MeV
- The total cross section can be affected by as much as 30% by the energy choices for the exchanged pion, with the largest effect close to threshold
- The sigma exchange model used m_σ = 550 MeV

## Context

This work generalizes the toy-model studies of Hanhart (2001, 2002) which considered scalar particles and interactions without negative-energy states, by implementing a physical model with pseudo-vector πNN coupling, χPT πN amplitudes, and realistic NN potentials. The results confirm that DWBA is adequate for pion production calculations, but show that the fixed kinematics and on-shell approximations, when applied to both the propagator and the re-scattering vertex, overestimate the cross section significantly, consistent with the findings of Sato (1997). The work enables more reliable calculations of pion production reactions, particularly for charged pion production where the isovector re-scattering term becomes important away from threshold.
