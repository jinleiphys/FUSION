# Mapping collinear in-medium parton splittings

> We map the spectrum of $1\to 2$ parton splittings inside a medium characterized by a transport coefficient $\hat q$ onto the kinematical Lund plane, taking into account the finite formation time of the process. We discuss the distinct regimes arising in this map for in-medium splittings, pointing out the close correspondence to a semi-classical description in the limit of hard, collinear radiation with short formation times. Although we disregard any modifications of the original parton kinematics in course of the propagation through the medium, subtle modifications to the radiation pattern compared to the vacuum baseline can be traced back to the physics of color decoherence and accumulated interactions in the medium. We provide theoretical support to vacuum-like emissions inside the medium by delimiting the regions of phase space where it is dominant, identifying also the relevant time-scales involved. The observed modifications are shown to be quite general for any dipole created in the medium.

## Key claim

The paper maps the spectrum of collinear $1\to 2$ parton splittings inside a QCD medium onto the kinematical Lund plane, showing that vacuum-like emissions dominate in a well-defined region of phase space where the formation time is shorter than both the decoherence time and the broadening time, but the authors explicitly disregard any modifications of the original parton kinematics during propagation through the medium.

## Method

The authors compute the amplitude for a $\gamma \to q\bar q$ splitting inside a static medium using dressed propagators in the high-energy limit, implementing a semi-classical approximation that fixes parton trajectories to classical straight-line paths and removes non-eikonal broadening effects. The inclusive spectrum is derived from the squared amplitude, which involves two-point (dipole) and four-point (quadrupole) correlators of Wilson lines that resum medium interactions, and is expressed as the vacuum spectrum multiplied by a medium modification factor $F_\text{med}$. The analysis identifies distinct regimes based on the competition between the quantum-mechanical formation time $t_\text{f}$, the decoherence time $t_\text{d}$, and the broadening time $t_\text{broad}$.

## Key numbers

* Formation time: $t_\text{f} = \frac{2z(1-z)E}{p^2}$, where $p$ is the relative transverse momentum of the daughter partons, $z$ is the energy fraction, and $E$ is the parent energy.
* Decoherence time: $t_\text{d} \sim \left( \frac{1}{\hat q \theta^2} \right)^{1/3}$, where $\hat q$ is the transport coefficient and $\theta$ is the dipole opening angle.
* Broadening time: $t_\text{broad} \sim \left( \frac{1}{\hat q \theta^2 L} \right)^{1/2}$, where $L$ is the medium length.
* Critical angle for medium resolution: $\theta_c \sim (\hat q L^3)^{-1/2}$; for $\theta < \theta_c$ the medium does not resolve the dipole and $F_\text{med} = 0$.
* Vacuum spectrum: $\frac{dN^\text{vac}}{dz\,d\theta} = \frac{\alpha_\text{em}}{\pi} \frac{P_{q\gamma}(z)}{\theta}$, with $P_{q\gamma}(z) = n_f N_c [z^2 + (1-z)^2]$.

## Context

This work extends the study of medium-induced modifications to parton splittings beyond the instantaneous-dipole approximation used in earlier antenna radiation pattern calculations by Mehtar-Tani et al. (2011-2012) and Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2011), which assumed the dipole forms quasi-instantaneously near the origin. The mapping onto the Lund plane, originally developed for vacuum parton showers by Andersson et al. (1988), provides a framework for interpreting jet de-clustering measurements in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC. The results enable future studies of jet fluctuations arising from in-medium splittings and their impact on energy-loss observables, as recently explored in Monte Carlo studies by Milhano et al. (2015) and analytical calculations by Mehtar-Tani et al. (2017) and Caucal et al. (2018).
