# Anomaly Matching and Low Energy Theories at High Matter Density

> I provide new arguments supporting the validity of the t'Hooft anomaly conditions at non zero quark chemical potential. These constraints strengthen the quark-hadron continuity scenario. Finally I review the 2SC effective Lagrangian for color superconductivity.

# Anomaly Matching and Low Energy Theories at High Matter Density

## Key claim
The t'Hooft anomaly matching conditions remain valid at non-zero quark chemical potential, strengthening the quark-hadron continuity scenario; the proof relies on the Nielsen-Chadha theorem to guarantee that gapless excitations (Goldstone bosons) persist even when Lorentz invariance is broken by the chemical potential, which was not used in prior work by Hsu (2000).

## Method
The author provides new theoretical arguments for the validity of t'Hooft anomaly matching at non-zero chemical potential by invoking the Nielsen-Chadha theorem, which ensures that gapless excitations with linear dispersion relations (type I Goldstone bosons) always appear when symmetries are spontaneously broken, even without Lorentz invariance. The paper then reviews the 2SC (two-flavor color superconductivity) effective Lagrangian, constructing the non-linear realization for the coset space G/H = [SU_c(3) × U_V(1)] / [SU_c(2) × Ũ_V(1)], including the dressed fermion fields and the SU_c(2) glueball Lagrangian that encodes the trace anomaly.

## Key numbers
- The decay width for an SU_c(2) glueball decaying to two photons is Γ(h → γγ) ≈ 1.2 × 10⁻² [M_h / (1 MeV)]⁵ eV, where α = e²/(4π) ≈ 1/137.
- For a glueball mass of order 1 MeV, the decay time is τ ∼ 5.5 × 10⁻¹⁴ s.
- The SU_c(2) glueball melting temperature satisfies T_c ≤ ∜[90 v³ / (2 e π²)] ˆΛ < T_CSC, where T_CSC is the color superconductive transition temperature.
- The pion decay constant F_π is proportional to μ (the quark chemical potential) in the high chemical potential limit.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of t'Hooft anomaly matching at high density initiated by Sannino (2000) and Hsu (2000), addressing a gap in the proof concerning the infrared behavior of the anomalous three-point function. The validity of anomaly matching supports the quark-hadron continuity scenario proposed by Schafer (1998). The paper also reviews the 2SC effective Lagrangian developed by Casalbuoni and collaborators, and the SU_c(2) glueball Lagrangian from Sannino (2002), enabling further study of the QCD phase diagram including the deconfining/confining transition within the color superconductive phase.
