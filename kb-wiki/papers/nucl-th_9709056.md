# Experimental and Computer Simulation Study of the Radionuclides Produced in Thin Bi-209 Targets by 130 MeV and 1.5 GeV Protons

> The results of experimental and computer simulation studies of the yields of residual product nuclei in Bi-209 thin targets irradiated by 130 MeV and 1.5 GeV protons are presented. The yields were measured by direct high-precision gamma-spectrometry. The gamma-spectrometer resolution was 1.8 keV in the 1332 keV line. The gamma-spectra were processed by the ASPRO code. The gamma-lines were identified, and the cross sections defined, by the SIGMA code using the GDISP radioactive database. The process was monitored by the Al-27(p,x)Na-24 reaction. Results are presented for comparisons between the 209-Bi(p,x) reaction yields obtained experimentally and simulated by the HETC, GNASH, LAHET, INUCL, CEM95, CASCADE, and ALICE codes.

## Key claim

The paper presents experimental cross sections for residual nuclides produced in thin Bi-209 targets by 130 MeV and 1.5 GeV protons, and compares them with simulations from seven different codes; the authors note that the accuracy requirements for predicting yields in accelerator-driven transmutation systems are about 30%, but that only 80 to 100 product nuclei can be detected experimentally while the actual number is an order of magnitude higher, so theoretical predictions must be relied upon.

## Method

Thin metallic Bi-209 targets were irradiated with 130 MeV and 1.5 GeV proton beams at the ITEP U-10 synchrotron and a medical beam line. Independent and cumulative yields of residual product nuclei were measured by high-precision gamma-spectrometry (1.8 keV resolution at 1332 keV) using the ASPRO and SIGMA codes for spectrum processing and nuclide identification against the GDISP database, with the Al-27(p,x)Na-24 reaction as the proton flux monitor. The experimental yields were compared with simulations from the HETC, GNASH, LAHET, INUCL, CEM95, CASCADE, and ALICE codes, using a unified proton-nucleus interaction cross section (1663 mbarn at 130 MeV, 1873 mbarn at 1500 MeV) from Letaw's formulas.

## Key numbers

* Monitor cross sections: 9.9 ± 1.0 mb for Al-27(p,x)Na-24 at 1500 MeV; 9.6 ± 1.0 mb at 130 MeV.
* Proton fluences: ~2.4 × 10^13 protons for 1500 MeV irradiations; ~1.5 × 10^11 protons for 130 MeV irradiations.
* Beam parameters at 1500 MeV: ~2 × 10^11 protons/pulse, ~16 pulses/min, ~0.5 s pulse duration, elliptical beam ~25 × 15 mm.
* Beam parameters at 130 MeV: ~5 × 10^9 protons/pulse, ~16 pulses/min, ~100 ns pulse duration, circular beam ~15 mm diameter.
* Unified proton-nucleus interaction cross sections used for code normalization: 1663 mbarn at 130 MeV; 1873 mbarn at 1500 MeV.
* Measurement errors for most yields fall within ~10% to 35%.
* Gamma-spectrometer resolution: 1.8 keV at the 1332 keV line of Co-60.

## Context

This work extends the experimental database for proton-induced reactions on Bi-209 at energies relevant to accelerator-driven subcritical systems (ADS), where Pb, Bi, W, and Hg are candidate target materials. The study provides benchmark data for testing intranuclear cascade, preequilibrium, and evaporation models implemented in codes such as CEM95, CASCADE, INUCL, HETC, LAHET, GNASH, and ALICE, following earlier reviews by Koning (1998) and Michel (1995, 1997) that noted insufficient data above 20 MeV. The results enable systematic evaluation of code predictive power and identification of energy and mass-number ranges where each code performs best, supporting the stepwise improvement of simulation accuracy for transmutation facility design.
