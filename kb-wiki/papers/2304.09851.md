# Energy loss of heavy quarks in the presence of magnetic field

> We study the heavy quark energy loss in the presence of a background magnetic field. The analysis considers the high magnetic field generated by spectators from initial hard collisions that were incorporated using the medium-modified Debye mass, determined from quark condensates at finite temperature and magnetic field via recent lattice quantum chromodynamics (LQCD) calculations. We analyse the impact of medium polarization on the heavy quark propagation in a quark-gluon plasma formed in relativistic heavy-ion colliders like RHIC and LHC. For simplification, we considered the static medium with constant temperature and magnetic field values. Then, we explore the nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) at different magnitudes of magnetic field strengths at fixed temperatures. The energy loss of heavy quarks significantly increases, leading to $R_{AA}$ suppression at higher magnetic field values

## Key claim

The authors claim that heavy quark energy loss due to medium polarization significantly increases in the presence of a background magnetic field, leading to suppression of the nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) by up to 15% at the highest magnetic field strength studied ($eB = 0.6$ GeV$^2$), but this result is obtained under the simplifying assumption of a static medium with constant temperature and magnetic field values, and the effect of magnetic catalysis/inverse magnetic catalysis from LQCD-based quark condensates is found to be negligible for the heavy quark energy loss itself.

## Method

The authors employed semi-classical transport theory based on Wong equations and linearized Yang-Mills equations to compute the polarization energy loss of charm and bottom quarks in a quark-gluon plasma. The magnetic field was incorporated through a medium-modified Debye mass, which was evaluated using LQCD-based quark condensates at finite temperature and magnetic field from Bali et al. (2012, 2014), along with a temperature- and magnetic-field-dependent running coupling constant. The nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ was then calculated using stochastic Langevin dynamics, evolving the heavy quark momentum spectra from an initial FONLL distribution over a time of 7 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- Energy loss of heavy quarks increases by approximately up to 180% at $T = 480$ MeV for the highest magnetic field value studied ($eB = 0.6$ GeV$^2$)
- Six magnetic field values were considered: $eB = 0$, $0.01$, $0.1$, $0.2$, $0.4$, and $0.6$ GeV$^2$
- Three temperatures were studied: $T = 0.16$ GeV (transition temperature), $T = 0.36$ GeV (relevant to RHIC), and $T = 0.48$ GeV (relevant to LHC)
- For the low magnetic field $eB = 0.01$ GeV$^2$, the suppression of $R_{AA}$ relative to $eB = 0$ is around $2$ to $3\%$ at $T = 0.16$ GeV
- The nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ shows maximum suppression at $eB = 0.6$ GeV$^2$ compared to vanishing magnetic field
- The transition from $R_{AA} > 1$ to $R_{AA} < 1$ occurs near transverse momentum $\text{q}_T = 2$ GeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy quark energy loss via medium polarization by Carrington et al. (2016, 2015) and Prakash et al. (2023) by incorporating the effect of a background magnetic field generated by spectators in heavy-ion collisions. The magnetic field is included through the Debye mass using LQCD-based quark condensates from Bali et al. (2012, 2014), following the approach of Nilima et al. (2022) for heavy quark potential in a magnetized medium. This analysis enables more realistic modeling of heavy quark transport in the quark-gluon plasma created at RHIC and LHC, showing that magnetic field effects are crucial for theoretical consistency in characterizing heavy quark energy loss and $R_{AA}$ suppression.
