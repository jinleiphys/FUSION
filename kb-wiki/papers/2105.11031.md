# Impact of NICER's Radius Measurement of PSR J0740+6620 on Nuclear Symmetry Energy at Suprasaturation Densities

> By directly inverting several neutron star observables in the three-dimensional parameter space for the Equation of State of super-dense neutron-rich nuclear matter, we show that the lower radius limit for PSR J0740+6620 of mass $2.08\pm 0.07~M_{\odot}$ from Neutron Star Interior Composition Explorer (NICER)'s very recent observation sets a much tighter lower boundary than previously known for nuclear symmetry energy in the density range of $(1.0\sim 3.0)$ times the saturation density $ρ_0$ of nuclear matter. The super-soft symmetry energy leading to the formation of proton polarons in this density region of neutron stars is clearly disfavoured by the first radius measurement for the most massive neutron star observed reliably so far.

## Key claim

The lower radius limits from NICER's measurement of PSR J0740+6620 set a much tighter lower boundary than previously known for nuclear symmetry energy in the density range of (1.0 to 3.0) times saturation density, disfavoring super-soft symmetry energy that would lead to proton polaron formation, but this conclusion comes from a direct inversion approach that avoids the statistical averaging over the full uncertainty range used in Bayesian analyses.

## Method

The authors performed a direct inversion of neutron star observables in a three-dimensional parameter space for the equation of state, spanning the skewness of symmetric nuclear matter (J0), the curvature of symmetry energy (Ksym), and the skewness of symmetry energy (Jsym). They looped through this parameter space by brute force, solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation at each point to find constant surfaces corresponding to specific neutron star observables, including the lower radius limits from two independent NICER analyses of PSR J0740+6620. The crust-core transition density and pressure were determined consistently for each core equation of state using the NV equation of state for the inner crust and the BPS equation of state for the outer crust.

## Key numbers

- PSR J0740+6620 mass: 2.08 ± 0.07 solar masses (Fonseca 2021)
- Radius from Miller et al. (2021): 13.7 +2.6/-1.5 km (68% confidence)
- Radius from Riley et al. (2021): 12.39 +1.30/-0.98 km (68% confidence)
- Lower radius limit used for inversion from Riley et al. (2021): 11.41 km for a 2.01 solar mass neutron star
- Lower radius limit used for inversion from Miller et al. (2021): 12.2 km for a 2.01 solar mass neutron star
- Central density of a 2.01 solar mass neutron star ranges between approximately 5.3 and 9.5 times saturation density
- Radius of a 2.01 solar mass neutron star ranges between approximately 8.5 and 12.3 km
- Slope parameter L fixed at 58.7 MeV, varied within ±20 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation density: 31.7 ± 3.2 MeV
- Incompressibility K0: approximately 230 ± 20 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of neutron star observables to extract the high-density symmetry energy, building on earlier demonstrations by Lattimer and Prakash (2001) that canonical neutron star radii constrain symmetry energy only below about 2 times saturation density, while massive neutron stars are needed to probe higher densities. The direct inversion approach contrasts with Bayesian statistical analyses (e.g., Biswas 2021, Li 2021, Raaijmakers 2021, Pang 2021, Essick 2021, Huth 2021) that found generally small improvements from NICER's new radius measurements due to the approximately 10-15% statistical uncertainty. The results disfavor the super-soft symmetry energy scenario that was previously suggested by heavy-ion reaction data (Xiao 2009) but remained controversial (Feng 2012, Xie 2014), and rule out the formation of proton polarons in neutron star cores predicted by Kutschera and Wojcik (1993, 1994).
