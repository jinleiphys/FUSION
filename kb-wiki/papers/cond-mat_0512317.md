# The axial breathing mode in rapidly rotating Bose-Einstein condensates and uncertainty of the rotation velocity

> Experiments on the axial breathing mode in a rapidly rotating Bose-Einstein condensate are examined. Assuming a cold cloud without thermal component, we show that errors due to defocus of an imaging camera in addition to an inclination of the rotational axis can lead to a significant underestimate of the rotation rate in the fast rotation limit; within these uncertainties, our theoretical prediction agrees with the experimental data. We also show that, in the fast rotation regime, the Thomas-Fermi theory, which is inapplicable there, underestimates the rotation rate. Underestimate of the rotation rate due to these effects would also partly explain a discrepancy between theory and experiment for the Tkachenko mode frequency in the fast rotation regime.

## Key claim

The paper shows that errors from camera defocus and rotation-axis tilt can cause a significant underestimate of the rotation rate in rapidly rotating Bose-Einstein condensates, and that within these uncertainties the theoretical prediction for the axial breathing mode frequency agrees with experimental data.

## Method

The author uses a variational Lagrangian formalism with a generalized lowest-Landau-level (LLL) wave function that interpolates between a Thomas-Fermi parabola and a Gaussian axial density profile. The rotation velocity is determined not from the experimental values directly but by fitting theoretical rms radii to reanalyzed experimental data, using the same 50% optical-density threshold as the experiment. The mode frequency is then computed from the resulting equilibrium parameters.

## Key numbers

- Trap frequencies: $\omega_\perp = 2\pi \times 8.3$ Hz ($d_\perp \simeq 3.7$ $\mu$m), $\omega_z = 2\pi \times 5.3$ Hz ($d_z \simeq 4.7$ $\mu$m)
- $s$-wave scattering length $a_s \simeq 5.6$ nm for $^{87}$Rb triplet state
- Camera defocus changes the point spread function from 2.4 $\mu$m to 3.4 $\mu$m (one depth of focus)
- The tilt of the rotation axis towards/away from the camera is estimated at up to $\sim 5^\circ$
- In the fast rotation regime, the tilt effect gives a correction in $\Omega_0$ of $\sim 0.002\omega_\perp$
- The difference between $\Omega_0$ and $\Omega_0^{\rm TF}$ (Thomas-Fermi rotation velocity) is at most $\lesssim 4\%$
- The axial breathing mode shifts from $\sqrt{3}\omega_z$ to $2\omega_z$ with increasing $\Omega_0$

## Context

This work extends the author's previous theoretical framework for breathing modes in the mean-field LLL regime (Watanabe, 2005) and provides a detailed comparison with experimental data from Schweikhard et al. (2004). The results contradict the superfluid hydrodynamic models of Sedrakian (2001) and Cozzini (2003), which cannot describe the observed frequency shift. The analysis also shows that Thomas-Fermi theory underestimates the rotation rate in the fast rotation regime, and these same effects may partly explain a discrepancy between theory and experiment for the Tkachenko mode frequency.
