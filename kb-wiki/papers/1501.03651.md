# Pion Transverse Momentum Spectrum, Elliptic Flow and Interferometry in the Granular Source Model in Ultra-Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We systematically investigate the pion transverse momentum spectrum, elliptic flow, and Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) interferometry in the granular source model of quark-gluon plasma droplets in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. The granular source model can well reproduce the experimental results of the Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 200 GeV and the Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =$ 2.76 TeV with different centralities. We examine the parameters of the granular source models with an uniform and Woods-Saxon initial energy distributions in a droplet. The parameters exhibit certain regularities for collision centrality and energy.

## Key claim
The granular source model of quark-gluon plasma droplets can simultaneously reproduce the experimental pion transverse momentum spectra, elliptic flow, and HBT radii for Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV across multiple centralities, but the model's chaoticity parameter lambda is systematically larger than experimental data because the calculation does not include all experimental effects that reduce lambda.

## Method
The authors model the particle-emitting source as a superposition of hydrodynamically evolving QGP droplets with position-dependent initial velocities, using the S95p-PCE165-v0 equation of state and a freeze-out temperature distribution that includes both direct and resonance-decay pions. They determine eight free parameters (three source geometry parameters and five droplet velocity parameters) by fitting the model simultaneously to experimental data on pion transverse momentum spectra, elliptic flow v2, and HBT radii Rout, Rside, and Rlong for Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV and Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV at various centralities. Two variants of the initial energy distribution within each droplet are tested: a uniform distribution and a Woods-Saxon distribution.

## Key numbers
- For RHIC Au-Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, 0-5% centrality with uniform droplet: source transverse radius R_T=5.8 fm, longitudinal size R_z=3.9 fm, transverse expansion parameter a_T_bar=0.469, transverse asymmetry Delta a_T=0.066
- For RHIC Au-Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, 30-50% centrality with uniform droplet: R_T=2.5 fm, R_z=0.5 fm, a_T_bar=0.437, Delta a_T=0.156
- For LHC Pb-Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, 10-20% centrality with uniform droplet: R_T=6.0 fm, R_z=5.5 fm, a_T_bar=0.431, Delta a_T=0.092
- For LHC Pb-Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, 40-50% centrality with uniform droplet: R_T=2.5 fm, R_z=1.8 fm, a_T_bar=0.407, Delta a_T=0.131
- Initial droplet energy density set to 2.2 GeV/fm^3 for all collisions; droplet radius standard deviation sigma_d=2.5 fm
- Chemical freeze-out temperature T_chem=165 MeV; direct emission fraction f_dir=0.75 with temperature width Delta T_dir=10 MeV; decay emission width Delta T_dec=90 MeV
- The model reproduces experimental data well except for elliptic flow at p_T > 2 GeV/c

## Context
This work extends the granular source model previously developed by Zhang et al. (2004, 2006, 2009) which was initially used to explain HBT data from RHIC experiments, and later applied to pion spectra and HBT for most central collisions at RHIC and LHC by Zhang et al. (2011). The present study systematically examines centrality dependence at both RHIC and LHC energies, testing both uniform and Woods-Saxon initial energy distributions within droplets. The results enable future work to use the extracted parameter regularities to predict observables for other collision systems or energies, and to refine the model by incorporating additional effects that would reduce the lambda parameter to match experimental values.
