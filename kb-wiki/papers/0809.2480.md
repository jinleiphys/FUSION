# Relativistic structure of one-meson and one-gluon exchange forces and the lower excitation spectrum of the Nucleon and the Delta

> The lower excitation spectrum of the nucleon and $Δ$ is calculated in a relativistic chiral quark model. Corrections to the baryon mass spectrum from the second order self-energy and exchange diagrams induced by pion and gluon fields are estimated in the field -theoretical framework. Convergent results for the self-energy terms are obtained when including the intermediate quark and antiquark states with a total momentum up to $j=25/2$. Relativistic one-meson and color-magnetic one-gluon exchange forces are shown to generate spin 0, 1, 2, etc. operators, which couple the lower and the upper components of the two interacting valence quarks and yield reasonable matrix elements for the lower excitation spectrum of the Nucleon and Delta. The only contribution to the ground state nucleon and $Δ$ comes from the spin 1 operators, which correspond to the exchanged pion or gluon in the l=1 orbit, thus indicating, that the both pion exchange and color-magnetic gluon exchange forces can contribute to the spin of baryons. Is is shown also that the contribution of the color-electric component of the gluon fields to the baryon spectrum is enormously large (more than 500 MeV with a value $α_s=0.65$) and one needs to restrict to very small values of the strong coupling constant or to exclude completely the gluon-loop corrections to the baryon spectrum. With this restriction, the calculated spectrum reproduces the main properties of the data, however needs further contribution from the two-pion exchange and instanton induced exchange (for the nucleon sector) forces in consistence with the realistic NN-interaction models.

# Relativistic structure of one-meson and one-gluon exchange forces and the lower excitation spectrum of the Nucleon and the Delta

## Key claim

The relativistic one-pion and one-gluon exchange forces between valence quarks generate spin 0, 1, 2, etc. operators that couple the upper and lower Dirac components of the two interacting quarks, with only spin 1 operators contributing to the ground state nucleon and Delta; however, the color-electric component of the gluon fields produces an enormously large contribution (more than 500 MeV at alpha_s = 0.65) that forces one to either use very small values of the strong coupling constant or exclude gluon-loop corrections entirely, and even with this restriction the calculated spectrum still requires further contributions from two-pion exchange and instanton induced exchange forces for the nucleon sector.

## Method

The authors calculate the lower excitation spectrum of the nucleon and Delta in a relativistic chiral quark model based on an effective Lagrangian with scalar plus vector confining potentials, solving the Dirac equation for single-quark states. Second-order perturbative corrections from self-energy and exchange diagrams induced by pion and gluon fields are evaluated using the Gell-Mann and Low theorem, with intermediate quark and antiquark states included up to total angular momentum j = 25/2 to achieve convergence. The model parameters are chosen to reproduce the axial charge g_A of the proton and the pion-nucleon coupling constant via the Goldberger-Treiman relation, with two parameter sets (Model A and Model B) used to test sensitivity to the proton RMS radius.

## Key numbers

- The contribution of the color-electric component of the gluon fields to the baryon spectrum is more than 500 MeV with a strong coupling constant value alpha_s = 0.65.
- Convergent results for the self-energy terms are obtained when including intermediate quark and antiquark states with total momentum up to j = 25/2.
- The pion decay constant is taken as f_pi = 93 MeV.
- The vector potential parameter is fixed at alpha = 0.26 (approximately pi/12) from flux-tube studies.
- The strong coupling constant used is alpha_s = 0.65, corresponding to g_s^2 = 4 pi alpha_s.
- The nucleon-Delta mass splitting from gluon exchange is only 63 MeV with alpha_s = 0.65.
- For Model A, the contribution of pion fields to the N(939) ground state is about 200 MeV, while gluon fields yield more than 500 MeV shift.
- The three methods for center-of-mass correction agree within 50 MeV.
- The nucleon ground state and Roper resonance N*(1440)(1/2+) are overestimated by more than 200 MeV when gluon loop corrections are ignored.
- The orbital excitations N*(1520)(3/2-) and N*(1535)(1/2-) are underestimated by about 40-60 MeV.
- The Delta(1232)(3/2+) and its radial excitation Delta(1600)(3/2+) are overestimated by 50-90 MeV in Model A.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic chiral quark model approach previously developed by Oset (1984), Gutsche (1987, 1989), and Tursunov (2005) to include both one-pion and one-gluon exchange mechanisms for the lower excitation spectrum of SU(2) baryons. It addresses the long-standing "three-body spin-orbit puzzle" identified by Isgur and Karl (1977, 1978), where non-relativistic constituent quark models produced unacceptably large spin-orbit matrix elements in baryons, and engages with the debate between one-gluon exchange models (Isgur, 1999; Capstick, 2000) and Goldstone-boson exchange models (Glowacki, 1996, 1998) regarding the origin of hyperfine interactions. The results confirm the prediction of Glowacki (1997) that one-gluon exchange does not play an important role in the SU(2) baryon spectrum, and the work enables future inclusion of two-pion exchange and instanton induced exchange forces to improve agreement with experimental data from the Particle Data Group (2006).
