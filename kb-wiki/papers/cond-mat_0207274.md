# Nonextensive Statistics and Multiplicity Distribution in Hadronic Collisions

> The multiplicity distribution of particles in relativistic gases is studied in terms of Tsallis' nonextensive statistics. For an entropic index q>1 the multiplicity distribution is wider than the Poisson distribution with the same average number of particles, being similar to the negative binomial distribution commonly used in phenomenological analysis of hadron production in high-energy collisions.

# Nonextensive Statistics and Multiplicity Distribution in Hadronic Collisions

## Key claim
For entropic index q > 1 close to unity, the multiplicity distribution of particles in a high-energy relativistic gas is approximately negative binomial, provided a van der Waals hard-core interaction is introduced to regularize divergences in the generalized partition function. The authors show that a small deviation of q from unity (q = 1.0097 or 1.0030) produces substantially broader distributions than the Boltzmann-Gibbs case, bringing them closer to experimental data from hadronic collisions.

## Method
The authors derive the multiplicity distribution for a relativistic gas using Tsallis nonextensive statistics with q > 1, employing an integral representation of the q-exponential function to connect the generalized partition function to the Boltzmann-Gibbs partition function. They introduce a van der Waals excluded volume (v₀ = 0.368 fm³) to suppress high multiplicities and regularize divergences, then compute canonical multiplicity distributions for a meson gas containing π, η, ρ, and ω mesons at temperatures of 160 MeV and 190 MeV, comparing to NA22 pp collision data at p_lab = 250 GeV/c.

## Key numbers
- Average charged particle multiplicity in NA22 pp data: \(\overline{N} = 7.88 \pm 0.09\) with width \(D = 4.10 \pm 0.05\)
- For the ideal Boltzmann gas at T = 190 MeV, volume V = 18.8 fm³; at T = 160 MeV, V = 43.0 fm³ (both chosen to match the experimental average multiplicity)
- The ideal gas distributions have k ~ 40, while the NA22 data have k = 7
- For the van der Waals gas with v₀ = 0.368 fm³ at T = 190 MeV, V = 24.5 fm³; at T = 160 MeV, V = 49.2 fm³; these distributions have k ~ 100
- For the Tsallis gas with excluded volume v₀ = 0.368 fm³: at β⁻¹ = 190 MeV, V = 9.13 fm³, q = 1.0097; at β⁻¹ = 160 MeV, V = 31.0 fm³, q = 1.0030
- The k-parameter of the negative binomial approximation is given by \(1/k = (q-1)\lambda^2 - 2v_0/V\), where λ depends on β and μ

## Context
This work extends the application of Tsallis nonextensive statistics to multiplicity distributions in hadronic collisions, following earlier demonstrations that the nonexponential transverse energy distributions in high-energy multiparticle production (including e⁺e⁻ annihilation) are well described by Tsallis statistics (Ignacio, Quarati, Wilk, Beck). The authors show that the Boltzmann-Gibbs ideal gas model (which gives Poisson multiplicity) and the van der Waals gas model both produce distributions too narrow to match experimental data from the NA22 collaboration. The results suggest that nonextensive thermal phenomena play an important role in high-energy collisions and provide a thermostatistical explanation for why hadronic multiplicities follow a negative binomial distribution, which has been used phenomenologically by Giovannini and others. The work enables further studies incorporating a larger resonance gas and additional observables such as particle ratios, transverse momentum spectra, and correlations.
