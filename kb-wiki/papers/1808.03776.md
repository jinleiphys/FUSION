# Explore nuclear multiple chirality in $A\sim60$ mass region within covariant density functional theory

> The nuclear multiple chirality in $A\sim60$ mass region is explored by the adiabatic and configuration-fixed constrained covariant density functional theory for cobalt isotopes. The potential-energy curves and triaxiality parameters $γ$ as functions of the deformation parameter $β$ in $^{54,56,57,58,59,60}$Co are obtained. It is found that there are high-$j$ particle(s) and hole(s) configurations with prominent triaxially deformed shapes in these isotopes. This points towards that the existence of chirality or multiple chirality in $A\sim60$ mass region are highly anticipated.

## Key claim
The authors predict that chiral rotation or multiple chiral doublets (MχD) may exist in the A~60 mass region, specifically in the cobalt isotopes ⁵⁴,⁵⁶,⁵⁷,⁵⁸,⁵⁹,⁶⁰Co, based on the identification of triaxially deformed local minima with high-j particle-hole configurations in covariant density functional theory calculations; the claim is hedged by stating that chirality is "highly anticipated" rather than confirmed, and the prediction relies on the existence of suitable configurations rather than direct observation of chiral bands.

## Method
The authors performed adiabatic and configuration-fixed constrained triaxial covariant density functional theory (CDFT) calculations using the point-coupling density functional PC-PK1, with pairing correlations neglected. The Dirac equation was solved in a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis of 12 major shells, and constrained calculations on β² were carried out to obtain triaxial deformation parameters γ for the cobalt isotopes ⁵⁴,⁵⁶,⁵⁷,⁵⁸,⁵⁹,⁶⁰Co. Wave functions were transformed from a Cartesian basis to a spherical basis with quantum numbers ⟨nljm⟩ to identify the configurations of local minima.

## Key numbers
- For ⁵⁴Co, the triaxially deformed local minima have deformation parameters C(β=0.259, γ=18.2°) and D(β=0.259, γ=17.3°).
- For ⁵⁶Co, state C has β=0.180, γ=41.5°.
- For ⁵⁷Co, state C has β=0.199, γ=24.0° and state D has β=0.250, γ=36.0°.
- For ⁵⁸Co, state D has β=0.251, γ=15.5°.
- For ⁵⁹Co, state C has β=0.249, γ=21.2°.
- For ⁶⁰Co, state C has β=0.279, γ=27.0° and state D has β=0.301, γ=15.1°.
- The ground state deformation parameter β increases gradually from 0.12 at ⁵⁴Co to 0.20 at ⁶⁰Co.
- For ⁵⁷Co, the calculated excitation energy of the π f⁻¹₇/₂ ⊗ ν [g¹₉/₂(fp)¹] configuration is 5.790 MeV, compared to the experimental band-head excitation energy of 5.539 MeV.
- In the particle-rotor model calculation for ⁵⁷Co, the moment of inertia parameter was taken as 𝒥₀=6.0 ℏ²/MeV and the Coriolis attenuation factor as ξ=0.9; the energy difference between the calculated partner bands is about 400 keV.

## Context
This work extends the search for nuclear chirality and multiple chiral doublets (MχD) into the lighter A~60 mass region, where chirality had not previously been predicted; prior chiral candidates were concentrated in the A~80, 100, 130, and 190 mass regions (see reviews by Meng (2014), Bark (2014), and Raduta (2016)). The study follows the same methodology used to predict MχD in ¹⁰⁶Rh (Meng, 2006) and in rhodium, silver, and cesium isotopes (Peng, 2008; Yao, 2009; Li, 2011; Qi, 2013; Li, 2018), and it enables experimental searches for chiral doublet bands and MχD in cobalt isotopes, particularly in ⁵⁴,⁵⁷,⁶⁰Co where two triaxially deformed states with high-j configurations were found.
