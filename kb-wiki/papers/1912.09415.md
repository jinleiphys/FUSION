# Microscopic Optical Potential for Elastic Proton-Nucleus Scattering from Chiral Forces

> A microscopic optical potential (OP) is derived from NN chiral potentials at the first-order term within the spectator expansion of the multiple scattering theory and adopting the impulse approximation. The performances of our OP are compared with those of a phenomenological OP in the description of elastic proton scattering data on different isotopic chains. An analogous scheme is adopted to construct a microscopic OP for elastic antiproton-nucleus scattering. The results of our OPs are in reasonably good agreement with the experimental data, for both elastic proton and antiproton-nucleus scattering.

# Microscopic Optical Potential for Elastic Proton-Nucleus Scattering from Chiral Forces

## Key claim
A microscopic optical potential derived from chiral NN potentials at N3LO and N4LO, using the first-order spectator expansion and impulse approximation, can describe elastic proton-nucleus scattering data with accuracy comparable to phenomenological potentials, particularly above 200 MeV, but the agreement is conditional on using sufficiently high cutoff parameters and at least N3LO chiral order.

## Method
The optical potential was constructed from the first-order term of the Watson multiple scattering theory in the spectator expansion, adopting the impulse approximation and optimum factorization approximation. The free NN t-matrix from chiral potentials at N3LO (EM and EGM) and N4LO (EKM and EMN) was folded with nuclear densities from relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations. For antiproton scattering, the same scheme was applied using a newly derived N3LO antiproton-nucleon interaction and nonlocal densities from the No-Core Shell Model with chiral two- and three-nucleon forces.

## Key numbers
- Elastic proton scattering on 16O at 100 MeV and 200 MeV: all N3LO potentials reproduce experimental amplitudes at 100 MeV; at 200 MeV, potentials with lower cutoffs (Lambda = 450 MeV) fail to describe empirical data
- Elastic proton scattering on 58Ni at 295 MeV: microscopic OP gives better description than phenomenological KD potential up to theta ~ 40 degrees
- Elastic proton scattering on 58Ni at 333 MeV: both EKM and EMN microscopic OPs give much better description than KD potential
- Elastic proton scattering on 16O and 40,42,44,48Ca at 318 MeV: KD describes data only at lowest angles; microscopic OPs perform better
- Elastic antiproton scattering on 12C at 180 MeV: microscopic OP describes data very well, particularly at the first minimum of the diffraction pattern
- Energy range tested for proton scattering: 156 to 333 MeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous development of microscopic optical potentials from chiral forces (Vorabbi 2017, 2018, 2019) by comparing systematically with the phenomenological global optical potential of Koning and Delaroche (2003), which was fitted for nuclei with A between 24 and 249 and energies from 1 keV to 200 MeV. The results demonstrate that chiral-potential-based optical potentials can match or exceed phenomenological fits above 200 MeV, where the Koning-Delaroche potential begins to fail. The antiproton scattering extension enables predictions for upcoming experiments at the ELENA ring at CERN and the FAIR facility in Germany. Future improvements identified include incorporating three-body forces in the scattering matrix and going beyond the impulse approximation to include nuclear medium effects.
