# Photon Multiplicity Measurements : From SPS to RHIC and LHC

> Results from the photon multiplicity measurements using a fine granularity preshower photon multiplicity detector (PMD) at CERN SPS are discussed. These include study of pseudo-rapidity distributions of photons, scaling of photon multiplicity with number of participating nucleons, centrality dependence of mean transverse momentum of photons, event-by-event fluctuations in photon multiplicity and localised charged-neutral fluctuations. Basic features of the PMD to be used in STAR experiment at RHIC and in ALICE experiment at LHC are also discussed.

# Photon Multiplicity Measurements: From SPS to RHIC and LHC

## Key claim
Photon multiplicity measured with a fine granularity preshower detector provides a complementary global observable to charged particle measurements in heavy-ion collisions, but the results from the VENUS event generator systematically under-predict photon multiplicity for central collisions, especially for asymmetric ion collisions such as Pb+Nb and Pb+Ni.

## Method
Photon multiplicity was measured using a preshower photon multiplicity detector (PMD) in the WA93 experiment (S+Au and S+S at 200 AGeV) and the WA98 experiment (Pb+Pb, Pb+Ni, and Pb+Nb at 158 AGeV) at CERN SPS. The PMD consisted of scintillator pads read out by CCD cameras, with photon counting efficiency of 65-75% and purity of 54-70% depending on centrality. The analysis included pseudo-rapidity distributions, scaling with number of participating nucleons, event-by-event mean transverse momentum from combined PMD and calorimeter data, event-by-event multiplicity fluctuations, and localized charged-neutral fluctuations to search for disoriented chiral condensates (DCC).

## Key numbers
- Photon multiplicity scaling exponent alpha = 1.12 ± 0.03 for Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV, obtained from fitting total photon number versus number of participating nucleons
- Charged particle scaling exponent alpha = 1.07 ± 0.05 from the same experiment, indicating deviation from naive Wounded Nucleon Model (alpha = 1)
- Pseudo-rapidity peak for WA98 experiment found to be 2.92
- PMD photon counting efficiency: 65-75% for WA93 (central to peripheral), 68-73% for WA98
- PMD photon sample purity: 70% for WA93, 65-54% for WA98 (central to peripheral)
- Upper limit on DCC-like fluctuations set at 10^-2 for DCC domain size with Δφ between 45-90 degrees and 3×10^-3 for Δφ between 90-135 degrees at 90% confidence level
- STAR-PMD: 82944 channels, η coverage 2.3-3.9, central efficiency 62%, purity 61%
- ALICE-PMD: 270,000 channels, η coverage 2.3-3.5, central efficiency 64%, purity 60%

## Context
This work extends the first-ever measurements of photon multiplicity, rapidity, and azimuthal distributions in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions performed by the WA93 and WA98 collaborations at CERN SPS. The results are compared to the VENUS event generator, which systematically under-predicts central collision data. The study of event-by-event fluctuations in photon multiplicity relates to theoretical work on the QCD phase diagram and the possible tri-critical point (Stephanov, 1998). The DCC search follows theoretical predictions by Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993) and sets upper limits on DCC-like fluctuations. The gas-based PMD designs for STAR at RHIC and ALICE at LHC will enable continuation of these studies at higher energies, with improved DCC sensitivity at STAR due to charged particle momentum information from the forward time projection chamber.
