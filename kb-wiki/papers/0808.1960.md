# Computation of the number of neutrino events which can be registered in Borexino detector from the Sun neutrinos flux with energy $E_ν= 0.862 MeV$

> This paper gives an estimation of the number of neutrinos which can be registered in Borexino detector from the Sun neutrinos generated in reaction $^{7}Be + e^{-} \to ^{7}Li + ν_e$ with energy $E_{ν_e} = 0.862 MeV$ in the absence of neutrino oscillations. This number is supposed be between $ N^{theor} = 86.45 ÷96.52 \frac{counters}{(day\cdot 100\quad ton)}$ in dependence on primary neutrino fluxes. Then ratios between number of neutrinos $N^{exper}$ registered in Borexino detector and counted numbers $N^{theor}$, are $\frac{N^{exper}}{N^{theor}} = 0.49 ÷0.54$. This value is close enough to the same value obtained in $^{71}Ga - ^{71}Ge$ experiments in the close energy regions. The value $\frac{N^{exper}}{N^{theor}}$ obtained at supposition that $θ_{1 3} \approx 0$ and absence of the resonance effect approximately equal to $\simeq 0.67$ and it is noticeably greater than the above value. Probably it means that the supposition that $θ_{1 3} \approx 0$ is not justified and there can be a definite deposit of $τ$ neutrinos.

## Key claim

The ratio of experimentally observed Borexino events to the theoretical expectation for 0.862 MeV solar neutrinos from the \(^{7}\text{Be} + e^{-} \to ^{7}\text{Li} + \nu_e\) reaction is \(0.49 \div 0.54\), which is noticeably smaller than the value of \(\simeq 0.67\) predicted under the assumption that the mixing angle \(\theta_{13} \approx 0\) and that the resonance effect is absent; this discrepancy suggests that the assumption \(\theta_{13} \approx 0\) may not be justified and that there could be a contribution from tau neutrinos.

## Method

The author computed the expected event rate in the Borexino detector for 0.862 MeV solar neutrinos by combining the theoretical solar neutrino flux from the Standard Solar Model (SSM) with the \(\nu_e + e^{-} \to \nu_e + e^{-}\) elastic scattering cross section, the electron density of the trimethylbenzene liquid scintillator, and the detector exposure (100 tons over one day). Two primary neutrino flux values from the literature were used: \(0.455 \times 10^{10} \, \text{cm}^{-2} \text{s}^{-1}\) and \(0.508 \times 10^{10} \, \text{cm}^{-2} \text{s}^{-1}\). The cross section was evaluated at \(E_{\nu_e} = 0.862 \, \text{MeV}\) using \(\sin^2 \theta_W = 0.248\) to match experimental data, and the resulting theoretical event rates were compared to the Borexino experimental result of \(47 \pm 7 (\text{stat.}) \pm 12 (\text{syst.})\) counts per day per 100 tons.

## Key numbers

- Theoretical event rate for 0.862 MeV solar neutrinos in Borexino: \(N^{\text{theor}} = 86.45 \div 96.52 \, \text{counters} / (\text{day} \cdot 100 \, \text{ton})\), depending on the primary neutrino flux.
- Experimental event rate reported by Borexino: \(N^{\text{exper}} = 47 \pm 7 (\text{stat.}) \pm 12 (\text{syst.}) \, \text{counters} / (\text{day} \cdot 100 \, \text{ton})\).
- Ratio of experimental to theoretical event rates: \(N^{\text{exper}} / N^{\text{theor}} = 0.49 \div 0.54\).
- Predicted ratio under the assumption \(\theta_{13} \approx 0\) and no resonance effect: \(N^{\text{osc}} / N \simeq 0.667\).
- Solar neutrino flux from the \(^{7}\text{Be}\) reaction in the Standard Solar Model: \(N^{\text{theor}}_{\nu_e} = 0.47 (1 \pm 0.15) \times 10^{10} \, \text{cm}^{-2} \text{s}^{-1}\), with specific values of \(0.455 \times 10^{10} \, \text{cm}^{-2} \text{s}^{-1}\) and \(0.508 \times 10^{10} \, \text{cm}^{-2} \text{s}^{-1}\) used in the calculation.
- Elastic scattering cross section at \(E_{\nu_e} = 0.862 \, \text{MeV}\): \(\sigma_{\nu_e e} (W, Z) = 0.665 \times 10^{-44} \, \text{cm}^2\).
- Electron density in trimethylbenzene: \(n_e = 2.897 \times 10^{23} \, \text{cm}^{-3}\), giving \(N_e = 3.307 \times 10^{31}\) electrons in 100 tons.

## Context

This work provides an independent estimation of the expected solar neutrino event rate in Borexino for the 0.862 MeV line from the \(^{7}\text{Be}\) reaction, extending the calculations of the Standard Solar Model by Bahcall (1995) and later flux determinations. The resulting ratio of observed to expected events (0.49 to 0.54) is noted to be close to that obtained in the \(^{71}\text{Ga} - ^{71}\text{Ge}\) experiments (e.g., those reported by Hampel et al. and Abdurashitov et al.) in similar energy regions. The discrepancy with the prediction assuming \(\theta_{13} \approx 0\) (0.67) suggests that the third mixing angle may not be negligible, enabling further investigation into the role of tau neutrinos and the resonance mechanism in solar neutrino oscillations.
