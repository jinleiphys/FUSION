# Rapidity dependence of transverse-momentum multiplicity correlations

> Following previous work [1], we propose to analyze the rapidity dependence of transverse momentum and transverse-momentum multiplicity correlations. We demonstrate that the orthogonal polynomial expansion of the latter has the potential to discriminate between models of particle production.

# Rapidity dependence of transverse-momentum multiplicity correlations

## Key claim
The orthogonal polynomial expansion of transverse-momentum multiplicity correlations, particularly the sign of the mixed coefficient ⟨a₁b₁⟩, can discriminate between models of particle production such as hydrodynamics and the color glass condensate (CGC) framework. The sign of this coefficient is not obvious a priori and depends on whether multiplicity and transverse momentum rapidity asymmetries have the same or opposite signs.

## Method
The paper proposes expanding the rapidity-dependent correlation functions C_{[N,N]}(y₁,y₂), C_{[P,P]}(y₁,y₂), and C_{[N,P]}(y₁,y₂) in orthogonal polynomials (e.g., Chebyshev or Legendre polynomials), analogous to the expansion of the single-particle rapidity distribution N(y) and the average transverse momentum P_t(y) in terms of fluctuating coefficients a_i and b_i. The coefficients ⟨a_i²⟩, ⟨b_i²⟩, and ⟨a_i b_k⟩ are then extracted from the correlation functions to characterize rapidity-independent and forward-backward asymmetric fluctuations of multiplicity and transverse momentum.

## Key numbers
- The saturation scale evolution parameter λ ≈ 0.3 in the CGC framework, with Q² ∼ e^{±λy} for the two colliding protons.
- The ATLAS Collaboration observed that ⟨a₁²⟩ scales approximately with 1/N_ch and is numerically very similar for p+p, p+Pb, and Pb+Pb collisions at a given event multiplicity N_ch.
- In the CGC example, multiplicity N(y) ∼ S_t Q₂² [2 + ln(Q₁²/Q₂²)] while P_t(y) ∼ (2Q₁ − (2/3)Q₂) / (1 + ln(Q₁/Q₂)), leading to opposite signs for a₁ and b₁.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous analysis of two-particle rapidity correlations (Bzdak, 2012) and the ATLAS measurement of asymmetric rapidity fluctuations in p+p, p+Pb, and Pb+Pb collisions (ATLAS Collaboration, 2016). It proposes a new observable, the mixed multiplicity-transverse momentum correlation, that could distinguish between hydrodynamical models (Bozek, 2013) where ⟨a₁b₁⟩ > 0 and CGC-based models (Dumitru, 2001; Gelis, 2010) where ⟨a₁b₁⟩ < 0. The approach enables future experimental tests of whether ⟨b₁²⟩ and ⟨a₁b₁⟩ satisfy the same scaling with N_ch as ⟨a₁²⟩, and could reveal detailed information on the longitudinal structure of systems created in p+p, p+A, and A+A collisions.
