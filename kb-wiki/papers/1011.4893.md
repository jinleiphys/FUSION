# Low-temperature thermodynamics of the unitary Fermi gas: superfluid fraction, first sound and second sound

> We investigate the low-temperature thermodynamics of the unitary Fermi gas by introducing a model based on the zero-temperature spectra of both bosonic collective modes and fermonic single-particle excitations. We calculate the Helmholtz free energy and from it we obtain the entropy, the internal energy and the chemical potential as a function of the temperature. By using these quantities and the Landau's expression for the superfluid density we determine analytically the superfluid fraction, the critical temperature, the first sound velocity and the second sound velocity. We compare our analytical results with other theoretical predictions and experimental data of ultracold atoms and dilute neutron matter.

# Low-temperature thermodynamics of the unitary Fermi gas: superfluid fraction, first sound and second sound

## Key claim
The model based on zero-temperature spectra of bosonic collective modes and fermionic single-particle excitations accurately describes the low-temperature thermodynamics of the unitary Fermi gas, but only well below the calculated critical temperature of the superfluid phase transition; the model does not show a phase transition and overestimates the critical temperature at 0.34 T_F compared to other determinations near 0.15–0.25 T_F.

## Method
The author constructs a thermodynamic model using the Helmholtz free energy from zero-temperature elementary excitation spectra: bosonic collective excitations with a phonon-like dispersion including a gradient term and fermionic single-particle excitations with a BCS-like gap. From the free energy, the entropy, internal energy, and chemical potential are derived as functions of temperature, and Landau's expression for superfluid density is used to compute the superfluid fraction, first sound velocity, and second sound velocity analytically.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state energy parameter: ξ = 0.42 (from Monte Carlo prediction of Astrakharchik et al.)
- Gradient parameter: λ = 0.25 (best agreement with Monte Carlo data)
- Zero-temperature gap parameter: γ = Δ₀/ε_F ≃ 0.45 (from Monte Carlo simulations)
- Single-particle excitation parameter: ζ ≃ 0.9 (from recent Monte Carlo results)
- Zero-temperature first sound velocity: c₁ = v_F √(ξ/3)
- Critical temperature from the model: T_c/T_F = 0.34 (overestimates compared to other determinations)
- For dilute neutron matter at density n = 0.003 fm⁻³: ξ ≃ 0.46, ζ ≃ 0.82, γ ≃ 0.29, estimated T_c/T_F ≃ 0.09
- For T → 0, second sound velocity: u₂ → c₁/√3 = v_F √ξ / 3

## Context
This work extends the Landau approach to elementary excitations (Landau 1941, Khalatnikov 1965), previously applied to superfluid ⁴He, to the unitary Fermi gas. It builds on the thermodynamic calculations of Bulgac, Drut and Magierski (2008) and Nishida (2009), and uses zero-temperature excitation spectra from density functional and Monte Carlo methods (ETF approach, Magierski 2009). The results are compared with Monte Carlo simulations and experimental data from Horikoshi et al. (2010) for ⁶Li atoms and Nascimbene et al. (2010) for the critical temperature. The model enables analytical calculation of superfluid fraction and sound velocities, and could be extended to the full BCS-BEC crossover.
