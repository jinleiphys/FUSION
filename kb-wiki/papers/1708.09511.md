# Temperature effects on nuclear pseudospin symmetry in the Dirac-Hartree-Bogoliubov formalism

> We present finite temperature Dirac-Hartree-Bogoliubov (FTDHB) calculations for the tin isotope chain to study the dependence of pseudospin on the nuclear temperature. In the FTDHB calculation, the density dependence of the self-consistent relativistic mean fields, the pairing, and the vapor phase that takes into account the unbound nucleon states are considered self-consistently. The mean field potentials obtained in the FTDHB calculations are fit by Woods-Saxon (WS) potentials to examine how the WS parameters are related to the energy splitting of the pseudospin pairs as the temperature increases. We find that the nuclear potential surface diffuseness is the main driver for the pseudospin splittings and that it increases as the temperature grows. We conclude that pseudospin symmetry is better realized when the nuclear temperature increases. The results confirm the findings of previous works using RMF theory at $T=0$, namely that the correlation between the pseudospin splitting and the parameters of the Woods-Saxon potentials implies that pseudospin symmetry is a dynamical symmetry in nuclei. We show that the dynamical nature of the pseudospin symmetry remains when the temperature is considered in a realistic calculation of the tin isotopes, such as that of the Dirac-Hartree-Bogoliubov formalism.

# Temperature effects on nuclear pseudospin symmetry in the Dirac-Hartree-Bogoliubov formalism

## Key claim

Pseudospin symmetry is better realized when nuclear temperature increases, with the nuclear potential surface diffuseness identified as the main driver for pseudospin splittings; the dynamical nature of pseudospin symmetry previously established at T=0 in relativistic mean field theory persists when temperature is included self-consistently in the Dirac-Hartree-Bogoliubov formalism for tin isotopes.

## Method

The authors performed finite-temperature Dirac-Hartree-Bogoliubov (FTDHB) calculations for spherical tin isotopes from A=100 to A=170, using the NL3 interaction in the nonlinear Walecka model, with temperatures varying from T=0 to T=8 MeV. The self-consistent mean-field potentials (scalar Σ_c, neutron V_n, and proton V_p) obtained from the FTDHB calculations were fitted to Woods-Saxon potentials to extract the depth, radius, and surface diffuseness parameters, and the correlation between these parameters and the pseudospin energy splitting was examined as a function of temperature. A vapor subtraction procedure was employed to account for unbound nucleon states at higher temperatures, and the harmonic oscillator basis was truncated at N_F=14 for fermions and N_B=24 for bosons.

## Key numbers

- Temperatures studied: from T=0 up to T=8 MeV for tin isotopes from A=100 to A=170
- The critical superfluidity and superconducting phase transitions occur at T ~ 1 MeV
- Vapor phase effects from unbound nucleon states become important only at temperatures T ≥ 4 MeV
- The pairing gap and energy vanish above T = 0.5 - 1.2 MeV for open-shell and deformed nuclei
- The central potential Σ_c is approximately -60 MeV at the nuclear center at T=0
- Oscillator frequency used: ℏω₀ = 41A^(-1/3) MeV for all calculations
- The overall constant c_pair = 0.55 was used in the pairing interaction for neutrons and protons
- Closed-shell nuclei studied include ¹⁰⁰Sn, ¹³²Sn, and ¹⁷⁶Sn, which have zero pairing gap and energy over the entire temperature range

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pseudospin symmetry in relativistic mean field theory at T=0 (Alberto 2001, Alberto 2002, Lisboa 2003) to finite temperatures using the self-consistent Dirac-Hartree-Bogoliubov formalism. The FTDHB approach was developed earlier (Lisboa 2007, Lisboa 2016) and improves upon relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) calculations (Meng 2006) by including pairing correlations, nucleon, and meson mean fields all self-consistently. The results confirm that the correlation between pseudospin splitting and Woods-Saxon potential parameters found at T=0 (Alberto 2001, Alberto 2002) persists at finite temperature, and the increasing surface diffuseness with temperature is identified as the dominant mechanism for improved pseudospin symmetry realization. This work enables future studies of pseudospin symmetry in hot exotic nuclei and provides a framework for understanding how temperature affects the dynamical symmetry in nuclear systems.
