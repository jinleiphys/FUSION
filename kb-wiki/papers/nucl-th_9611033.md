# New Type of Collective Motion for N $\sim$ Z Nuclei

> We study a new type of collective motion with alpha-particle type of correlations and show that it may be relevant for N $\sim$ Z nuclei.

# New Type of Collective Motion for N ∼ Z Nuclei

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a new dynamical symmetry of the interacting boson model with isospin (IBM-3), based on the O(18) subgroup, may describe collective motion with alpha-particle-like correlations in nuclei where the number of neutrons N is approximately equal to the number of protons Z. The claim is hedged: the author states this collective motion “may be relevant” for N ∼ Z nuclei and that “only additional measurements … will be able to decide.”

## Method

The author constructs a dynamical symmetry chain U(18) ⊃ O(18) ⊃ O(15) × SU_s(2) within the IBM-3 framework, which includes monopole (s) and quadrupole (d) bosons with isospin. The O(18) symmetry is defined by an invariant operator that creates an alpha-particle-like four-nucleon correlation. The energy eigenvalues are derived from a quadrupole interaction expressed in terms of Casimir operators of O(18) and O(15), and the resulting excitation spectra and B(E2) transition strengths are compared to experimental data for N ∼ Z nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For ⁶⁴Ge (N=Z=32), the measured excitation energies are: E*(2⁺₁) = 0.902 MeV, E*(2⁺₂) = 1.579 MeV, E*(4⁺₁) = 2.052 MeV.
- The average two-phonon excitation energy for ⁶⁴Ge is Ē*₂ = 1.88 MeV, giving a ratio Ē*₂/E*(2⁺₁) = 2.09.
- For the O(18) model, the ratio E*₂,Ts/E*₁,₁ ranges between 1.88 and 2.25 for T_s = 0 or 2, respectively.
- For ⁴⁴Ti (T=0), the O(18) model with no effective charge (Δ=0) gives B(E2) = 0.055 (eb)², compared to the measured value 0.061 ± 0.015 (eb)².
- For ⁴⁸Cr (T=0), the O(18) model with no effective charge gives B(E2) = 0.138 (eb)², compared to the measured value 0.133 ± 0.020 (eb)².

## Context

This work extends the interacting boson model (IBM-2) of Iachello and Arima (1987), which successfully describes heavy nuclei with large neutron excess, to the N ∼ Z regime where isospin must be included (IBM-3, introduced by Elliott and White in 1980). The O(18) dynamical symmetry is a new subgroup chain not previously discussed, and it predicts different behavior from the spherical vibrator and the O(6) limit studied by Iachello and Arima (1987) and by Ginocchio and Leviatan (1994). The results enable future experimental tests using radioactive beam facilities to distinguish between spherical vibrator and O(18) collective motion through the isospin dependence of excitation energies and B(E2) values.
