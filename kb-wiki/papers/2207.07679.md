# Jets in evolving matter within the opacity expansion approach

> In a recent study [1] we have extended the opacity expansion approach to describe jet-medium interactions including medium motion effects in the context of heavy-ion collisions. We have computed color field of the in-medium sources, including the effects of the transverse field components and the energy transfer between the medium and jet. The corresponding contributions are sub-eikonal in nature, and were previously ignored in the literature. Here we discuss how our approach can be applied to describe the medium motion effects in the context of Deep Inelastic Scattering.

## Key claim

The opacity expansion approach, previously extended to include sub-eikonal corrections from medium motion in heavy-ion collisions, can be applied equally to cold nuclear matter as probed in deep inelastic scattering at the future Electron-Ion Collider, provided the nature of the background color field is appropriately reinterpreted.

## Method

The authors compute the single-Born and double-Born amplitudes for jet transverse-momentum broadening at first order in opacity, using scalar partons interacting via t-channel gluon exchange with heavy in-medium sources. They derive the sub-eikonal correction term \(\vec{\Gamma}(\vec{q}_\bot)\) that encodes medium motion effects, and also obtain linearized gradient corrections for transversely inhomogeneous matter by performing a hydrodynamic gradient expansion. The resulting jet momentum distribution is expressed in terms of the source density \(\rho(\vec{x})\), the Gyulassy-Wang potential \(v(q)\), and the initial jet distribution.

## Key numbers

* The sub-eikonal correction to the single-Born amplitude is given by \(\vec{\Gamma} (\vec{q}_\bot) = - 2 \frac{\vec{p}_\bot - \vec{q}_\bot}{(1-u_{z})E} + \frac{\vec{q}_\bot}{(1-u_{z}) E} \frac{(p-q)_\bot^2 - p_\bot^2}{\bar\sigma(q_\bot^2)} \frac{\pa\bar\sigma}{\pa q_\bot^2} - \frac{\vec{q}_\bot}{1-u_z} \frac{1}{|J (E, \vec{p}_\bot - \vec{q}_\bot)|^2} \frac{\partial |J|^2}{\partial E}\), where \(E\) is the jet energy and \(u_z\) the longitudinal source velocity.
* For a simple initial profile \(E \frac{dN^{(0)}}{d^3 p} \propto E^{-4} \, \delta^{(2)} (\tvec{p})\), the odd moments of the jet momentum distribution are \(\langle \vec{p}_\perp (p_\perp^2)^k \rangle = \frac{\tvec{u}}{(1-u_z)} \frac{L}{\lambda} \frac{(\mu^2)^{k+1}}{E} \int_0^\infty d\xi \frac{\xi^{k+1}(2+3\x)}{(1+\xi)^2}\), where \(L\) is the medium length, \(\lambda\) the mean free path, and \(\mu\) the Debye mass.
* For a Gaussian initial profile with width \(w\), the linearized gradient effect on the odd moment is \(\langle \vec{p}_\perp \, p_\perp^2 \rangle^{(linear)} \simeq \frac{L}{\lambda} \frac{L}{E} w^2 \mu^2 \frac{\tvec{\nabla}\rho}{\rho} \ln\frac{E}{\mu}\).
* Even moments of the jet momentum distribution are unmodified by flow velocity and gradients in the considered regimes.

## Context

This work extends the opacity expansion formalism of Sadofyev, Sievert, and Vitev (2021) from hot nuclear matter in heavy-ion collisions to cold nuclear matter as probed at the future Electron-Ion Collider. The approach connects to twist-4 parton distributions studied by Kang et al. (2014) and Qiu and collaborators (2004), and to transverse-momentum-dependent parton distributions of nuclei discussed by Kovchegov and collaborators (2015). The formalism enables jet-based tomographic studies of multi-parton correlations, color currents, and orbital angular momentum in both hot and cold QCD matter.
