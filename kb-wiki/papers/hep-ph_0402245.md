# The dihadron fragmentation function and its evolution

> Dihadron fragmentation functions and their evolution are studied in the process of $e^+e^-$ annihilation. Under the collinear factorization approximation and facilitated by the cut-vertex technique, the two hadron inclusive cross section at leading order (LO) is shown to factorize into a short distance parton cross section and a long distance dihadron fragmentation function. We provide the definition of such a dihadron fragmentation function in terms of parton matrix elements and derive its DGLAP evolution equation at leading log. The evolution equation for the non-singlet quark fragmentation function is solved numerically with a simple ansatz for the initial condition and results are presented for cases of physical interest.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first operator definition of a dihadron fragmentation function and derives its DGLAP evolution equation at leading-log accuracy, but the derivation is restricted to the non-singlet quark fragmentation function and assumes a factorization scale \(\mu\) chosen above a physical cut-off scale \(\mu_\perp\) that separates coherent from independent two-parton fragmentation.

## Method

The authors compute the two-hadron inclusive cross section in \(e^+e^-\) annihilation at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) using collinear factorization and the cut-vertex technique of Mueller. They isolate the collinear divergences into a renormalized dihadron fragmentation function, derive its DGLAP evolution equation at leading-log (LL) accuracy, and solve the evolution equation numerically for the non-singlet quark fragmentation function using a simple ansatz for the initial condition.

## Key numbers

* The total cross section for \(e^+e^- \to q\bar{q}\) at leading order is \(\sigma_0^{q\bar{q}} = e_q^2 N_c \frac{4\pi\alpha^2}{3s}\), where \(N_c=3\) is the number of colors.
* The evolution equation for the non-singlet dihadron fragmentation function is \(\frac{\partial D_{NS}^{h_1 h_2}(Q^2)}{\partial \log Q^2} = \frac{\alpha_s}{2\pi} \left[ P_{q\to qg} * D_{NS}^{h_1 h_2}(Q^2) + \hat{P}_{q\to qg} \bar{*} D_{NS}^{h_1}(Q^2) D_g^{h_2}(Q^2) + 1\leftrightarrow 2 \right]\).
* The factorization is valid in the regime \(\Lambda_{QCD}^2 \ll \mu_\perp^2 \ll Q^2\), where \(\mu_\perp\) is a physical cut-off scale introduced to separate independent fragmentation (\(q_\perp > \mu_\perp\)) from coherent fragmentation (\(q_\perp < \mu_\perp\)).

## Context

This work extends the operator definition of single-hadron fragmentation functions (Mueller, 1978) to the dihadron case, and derives evolution equations analogous to the DGLAP equations (Gribov-Lipatov, 1972; Dokshitzer, 1977; Altarelli-Parisi, 1977) for the double fragmentation function. Previous studies considered dihadron fragmentation with a fixed angle or transverse momentum (Konishi, 1978; Konishi, 1979a; Konishi, 1979b) or general evolution equations for n-hadron fragmentation functions (Sukhatme, 1980), but none provided an operator definition. This work enables future studies of medium modifications to dihadron fragmentation functions in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for understanding same-side correlations observed at RHIC (Adler et al., 2003).
