# Charmonium ground and excited states at finite temperature from complex Borel sum rules

> Charmonium spectral functions in vector and pseudoscalar channels at finite temperature are investigated through the complex Borel sum rules and the maximum entropy method. Our approach enables us to extract the peaks corresponding to the excited charmonia, $ψ^\prime$ and $η_c^\prime$, as well as those of the ground states, $J/ψ$ and $η_c$, which has never been achieved in usual QCD sum rule analyses. We show the spectral functions in vacuum and their thermal modification around the critical temperature, which leads to the almost simultaneous melting (or peak disappearance) of the ground and excited states.

## Key claim

The authors claim that, using complex Borel sum rules combined with the maximum entropy method, they can extract both ground and excited charmonium states (J/ψ, ψ′, ηc, ηc′) from QCD sum rules, and that these states melt almost simultaneously near the critical temperature, contrary to the expectation that excited states melt at lower temperatures.

## Method

The authors generalize the standard Borel sum rule by replacing the real Borel mass M² with a complex variable ℳ², constructing complex Borel sum rules (CBSR). They then apply the maximum entropy method (MEM) to extract spectral functions from these sum rules, using a carefully chosen domain in the complex Borel plane. Finite temperature effects are introduced through the temperature dependence of the dimension-4 scalar and twist-2 gluon condensates, which are obtained from quenched lattice QCD simulations via the energy density and pressure.

## Key numbers

- The extracted vacuum masses for the vector channel are 2.954 GeV (1st peak) and 3.624 GeV (2nd peak), compared to experimental values of 3.096 GeV and 3.686 GeV.
- The extracted vacuum masses for the pseudoscalar channel are 2.900 GeV (1st peak) and 3.512 GeV (2nd peak), compared to experimental values of 2.983 GeV and 3.639 GeV.
- The lower limits of the melting temperature for the vector channel are 1.01 Tc (1st peak) and 0.99 Tc (2nd peak).
- The lower limits of the melting temperature for the pseudoscalar channel are 1.01 Tc (1st peak) and 0.99 Tc (2nd peak).
- The peak positions shift to lower energy by about 50 MeV before losing statistical significance.
- The critical value r̃c for the upper boundary is 1/2.2 GeV⁻² for the vector channel and 1/2.0 GeV⁻² for the pseudoscalar channel.

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule analyses of charmonia at finite temperature, which could only extract ground states (Morita 2007, Morita 2009, Dominguez 2009, Gubler 2011, Suzuki 2012). It also builds on the authors' earlier demonstration that CBSR with MEM can resolve excited states in vacuum for the φ meson (Araki 2014). The results contradict the expectation from potential models (Matsui 1986, Karsch 1987) and experimental observations from heavy-ion collisions (Khachatryan 2014, Sirunyan 2016) that excited states melt at lower temperatures than ground states. The approach enables future studies of charmonium melting without assuming a specific functional form for the spectral function, though it cannot yet resolve bottomonium excited states due to their smaller relative mass splittings.
