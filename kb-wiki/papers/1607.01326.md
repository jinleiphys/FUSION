# Has the QCD critical point been observed at RHIC?

> The experimental search for the location of the QCD critical point in the phase diagram is of primary importance. In a recent publication it is claimed that measurements at RHIC lead not only to the location of the critical point ($μ_{cep}=95$ MeV, $T_{cep}=165$ MeV) but also to the verification of its universality class ($3d$ Ising system) by extracting the values of the critical exponents ($γ=1.2$, $ν=0.66$). We argue that this claim is based on an erroneous treatment of scaling relations near the critical point. As a result, the correct interpretation of the measurements cannot be linked to the QCD critical point.

# Has the QCD critical point been observed at RHIC?

## Key claim
The claimed observation of the QCD critical point at RHIC (μ_cep = 95 MeV, T_cep = 165 MeV) with 3d Ising universality class exponents (γ = 1.2, ν = 0.66) is invalid because the analysis incorrectly uses the isothermal compressibility exponent γ instead of the adiabatic compressibility exponent α, leading to a self-inconsistent solution that violates the Josephson scaling relation.

## Method
The authors re-examine the scaling analysis of two-pion interferometry data (R_out^2 - R_side^2) from Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 7.7–200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, as presented in a recent Letter. They identify that the finite-size scaling relation used in the original work, (R_out^2 - R_side^2)_max ~ R̅^{γ/ν}, incorrectly substitutes the isothermal compressibility exponent γ for the adiabatic compressibility exponent α, and they demonstrate the correct thermodynamic relation k_S = k_T (C_V/C_p) and its associated scaling behavior.

## Key numbers
- The claimed critical point location: μ_b^{cep} ≈ 95 MeV, T^{cep} ≈ 165 MeV
- The claimed critical exponents: ν ≈ 0.66, γ ≈ 1.2
- The correct interpretation yields α ≈ 1.15 ± 0.065 instead of γ = 1.2
- The Josephson scaling relation νd = 2 - α for d=3 is violated: with ν ≈ 0.67 ± 0.05, the relation would require α ≈ -0.01, not the observed α ≈ 1.15

## Context
This work directly critiques the analysis of Lacey (2015), which claimed to locate the QCD critical point and determine its universality class from RHIC data. The authors argue that the original treatment confuses isothermal and adiabatic compressibility, and that the resulting exponent pair (α, ν) does not correspond to any known 3d universality class. They note that the scaling property observed in the data may still indicate a new phenomenon in the crossover regime at small chemical potential, and that the corrected finite-size scaling relation (using α instead of γ) remains a useful tool for future searches at higher chemical potential.
