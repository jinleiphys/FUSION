# Skyrme-Random-Phase-Approximation description of E1 strength in 92-100Mo

> The isovector dipole E1 strength in Mo isotopes with A=92,94,96,98,100 is analyzed within the self-consistent separable random-phase approximation (SRPA) model with Skyrme forces SkT6, SkM*, SLy6, and SkI3. The special attention is paid to the low-energy region near the particle thresholds (4-12 MeV), which is important for understanding of astrophysical processes. We show that, due to a compensation effect, the influence of nuclear deformation on E1 strength below 10-12 MeV is quite modest. At the same time, in agreement with previous predictions, the deformation increases the strength at higher energy. At 4-8 MeV the strength is mainly determined by the tail of E1 giant resonance. The four Skyrme forces differ in description of the whole giant resonance but give rather similar results below 12 MeV.

# Skyrme-Random-Phase-Approximation description of E1 strength in 92-100Mo

## Key claim

The influence of nuclear deformation on E1 strength below 10-12 MeV is quite modest due to a compensation effect between the mu=0 and mu=1 branches of the giant dipole resonance, contradicting earlier predictions that deformation significantly increases low-energy E1 strength. However, at energies above 12 MeV, deformation does increase the strength in agreement with previous work.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent separable random-phase approximation (SRPA) calculations with four Skyrme forces (SkT6, SkM*, SLy6, and SkI3) to compute the isovector dipole E1 strength in the even-mass molybdenum isotopes 92-100Mo. The axial equilibrium quadrupole deformation was determined by minimization of the total energy in quadrupole-constrained mean-field calculations, and the photoabsorption cross section was computed using a Lorentz smoothing with averaging parameter Delta=2 MeV to simulate broadening effects beyond SRPA.

## Key numbers

- The particle emission thresholds for (gamma,n) reactions range from 12.7 MeV in 92Mo down to 8.3 MeV in 100Mo
- The equilibrium deformation beta for 100Mo is 0.27 with SLy6 and 0.24 with SkI3; for 98Mo it is 0.22 with SLy6 and 0.24 with SkI3
- The integral photoabsorption exhausts up to 98% of the energy-weighted sum rule EWSR = 9(hbar e)^2/(8pi m*_1) * NZ/A
- The Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule is Sigma_TRK = 60 NZ/A mb MeV
- The GDR energy follows the empirical rule E_GDR = 81 A^{-1/3} MeV
- The calculations used a basis space of single-particle states from the bottom of the potential well up to approximately +16 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous RPA studies of E1 strength in molybdenum isotopes using phenomenological Nilsson (Doenau, 2007) and Woods-Saxon (Schwen, 2007) single-particle potentials, which predicted that prolate/triaxial deformation significantly increases E1 strength at 10-14 MeV. The present self-consistent SRPA approach amends those conclusions by showing the deformation effect is energy-dependent and nearly vanishes below 12 MeV due to compensation between GDR branches. The results enable improved modeling of stellar photodisintegration rates and suggest further exploration of Nd and Sm isotope chains where triaxiality is absent.
