# Microscopic $NN\to NN^{\ast}(1440)$ transition potential: Determination of $πNN^{\ast}(1440)$ and $σNN^{\ast}(1440) $ coupling constants

> A $NN\to NN^{\ast}(1440)$ transition potential, based on an effective quark-quark interaction and a constituent quark cluster model for baryons, is derived in the Born-Oppenheimer approach. The potential shows significant differences with respect to those obtained by a direct scaling of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. From its asymptotic behavior we extract the values of $πNN^{\ast}(1440)$ and $σNN^{\ast}(1440)$ coupling constants in a particular coupling scheme

# Microscopic $NN\to NN^{\ast}(1440)$ transition potential: Determination of $\pi NN^{\ast}(1440)$ and $\sigma NN^{\ast}(1440)$ coupling constants

## Key claim

The $NN\to NN^{\ast}(1440)$ transition potential derived from a constituent quark model shows significant differences from potentials obtained by direct scaling of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, particularly at intermediate and long ranges; the extracted coupling constants are scheme-dependent and their signs are ambiguous due to the arbitrary relative phase between the $N$ and $N^{\ast}(1440)$ wave functions, but the ratio between the two ratios of coupling constants is a definitive, exportable prediction of the model.

## Method

The authors used a Born-Oppenheimer (adiabatic) approximation with harmonic oscillator baryon wave functions written in terms of quarks, sandwiching a quark-quark potential (containing confinement, one-gluon exchange, one-pion exchange, and one-sigma exchange terms) between $NN$ and $NN^{\ast}(1440)$ states. The $N^{\ast}(1440)$ (Roper) resonance was treated as a stable particle, and the model parameters were fixed from $NN$ scattering phase shifts and deuteron properties with no free parameters for the transition. The asymptotic (long-range) behavior of the resulting potential was then fitted to Yukawa forms at the baryon level to extract the meson-baryon coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- $\left| \frac{g_{\pi NN^{\ast}(1440)}}{g_{\pi NN}} \right| = 0.25$ (ratio of pion coupling constants, scheme-independent)
- $\left| \frac{g_{\sigma NN^{\ast}(1440)}}{g_{\sigma NN}} \right| = 0.47$ (ratio of sigma coupling constants, scheme-independent)
- $\frac{g_{\pi NN^{\ast}(1440)}}{g_{\pi NN}} = 0.53 \; \frac{g_{\sigma NN^{\ast}(1440)}}{g_{\sigma NN}}$ (ratio of the two ratios, sign and magnitude predicted)
- $\frac{g_{\pi NN}^{2}}{4\pi} = 14.83$ (pion-nucleon coupling constant used as input)
- $\frac{g_{\sigma NN}^{2}}{4\pi} = 24.4$ (sigma-nucleon coupling constant extracted, scheme-dependent)
- $\frac{g_{\sigma NN^{\ast}(1440)}^{2}}{4\pi} = 5.5$ (sigma-Roper coupling constant extracted, scheme-dependent)
- Quark model parameters: $m_q = 313$ MeV, $b = 0.518$ fm, $\alpha_s = 0.485$, $\alpha_{ch} = 0.027$, $m_\sigma = 3.42$ fm$^{-1}$, $m_\pi = 0.70$ fm$^{-1}$, $\Lambda = 4.2$ fm$^{-1}$

## Context

This work extends the same quark model approach previously applied to the $\Delta(1232)$ resonance and to the $NN^{\ast}(1440)\to NN^{\ast}(1440)$ interaction (Julia-Diaz et al., 2001) to the $NN\to NN^{\ast}(1440)$ transition. It provides a microscopic alternative to purely hadronic treatments of the Roper resonance in nuclear dynamics, such as those used in neutral pion production in proton-proton reactions (Pena et al., 1999), three-nucleon interactions (Coon et al., 1995), and the $p(\alpha,\alpha^{\prime})$ reaction (Hirenzaki, Oset, and Fernandez de Cordoba, 1996). The extracted coupling constant ratios enable improved calculations of processes involving the $N^{\ast}(1440)$ as an intermediate state, including heavy ion collisions and meson production reactions, and the potential can be used in separable expansions for few-body calculations (Julia-Diaz et al., 2002).
