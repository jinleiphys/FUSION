# Statistical properties of non-flow correlations in pp and heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energies

> In this work, we have studied the two-particle cumulant in pp, d-Au, and Au-Au collisions. The two-particle cumulant was treated as an event-by-event distribution, and its skewness and kurtosis were analyzed. The non-flow correlations, like jets and decays, constantly produced a skewed distribution, regardless of the model used. On the contrary, HYDJET++ produced a smooth Gaussian distribution at higher $η$ windows in fixed impact parameter collisions. The skewness increased consistently for higher $η$ windows in all non-QGP models like PYTHIA, PHOJET, QGSJET, and DPMJET. The kurtosis of the distribution also increased with $η$ windows in non-QGP models. The skewness and kurtosis of the distributions produced by HYDJET++ decreased with $Δη$ and eventually reached zero at higher $Δη$.

# Statistical properties of non-flow correlations in pp and heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energies

## Key claim

The skewness of the event-by-event two-particle cumulant distribution is a robust signature of non-flow correlations (jets and decays) that appears in all non-QGP models, while HYDJET++ with hydrodynamics produces a Gaussian distribution with skewness and kurtosis approaching zero at higher pseudorapidity windows; this behavior could serve as an alternative cross-check for non-flow contamination in small collision systems, though the analysis is limited to simulated datasets due to the unavailability of experimental two-particle cumulant distributions.

## Method

The authors computed the two-particle cumulant c2 = (1/N_pairs) Σ Σ cos(2Δφ) on an event-by-event basis for charged hadrons in pp, d-Au, and Au-Au collisions at RHIC energies, using PYTHIA8/Angantyr, HYDJET++, EPOS-LHC, PHOJET, QGSJET, and DPMJET event generators. They analyzed the skewness and excess kurtosis of the c2 distributions as functions of pseudorapidity window Δη and impact parameter, comparing non-QGP models (which produce only non-flow correlations) against HYDJET++ (which includes parameterized hydrodynamic flow with Gaussian eccentricity fluctuations).

## Key numbers

- pp collisions at 200 GeV: all non-QGP models (PYTHIA, EPOS-LHC, PHOJET, QGSJET, DPMJET) produced positively skewed c2 distributions in the pseudorapidity window -4 < η < 4.
- Au-Au collisions at 39 GeV with fixed impact parameter b = 0.1 R_A: HYDJET++ default configuration produced a Gaussian c2 distribution, while the configuration with hydrodynamics off produced a positively skewed distribution.
- Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV and 39 GeV (b = 0.1 R_A): skewness and kurtosis of HYDJET++ c2 distributions decreased with Δη and reached zero at higher Δη.
- In non-QGP models (PYTHIA, PHOJET, QGSJET, EPOS-LHC): skewness increased consistently for higher Δη windows; kurtosis also increased with Δη.
- d-Au collisions: c2 distribution with η-gap = 1 and N_ch > 120 in the -4 to 0 pseudorapidity range produced a symmetric distribution (sub-events in -4 to 0 and 1 to 5 pseudorapidity windows).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study of event-by-event two-particle cumulant distributions (Nayak 2025) by systematically characterizing skewness and kurtosis across multiple event generators. It builds on the observation by Voloshin (2007) that event-by-event spatial anisotropy fluctuations assume a Gaussian form, which HYDJET++ incorporates. The results contrast with standard non-flow removal techniques using η-gaps and higher-order cumulants (Borghini 2000, Borghini 2001); the authors propose that the skewness and kurtosis of the c2 distribution could provide an independent cross-check for non-flow contamination in small collision systems like p-Pb, d-Au, and p-Au at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies.
