# The nucleon resonances in the $J/ψ\to p\bar{p}η'$ decay

> We are aiming to study the $J/ψ\to p\bar{p}η'$ decay in an isobar model and the effective Lagrangian approach. After a careful exploration of the contributions of the $S_{11}(1535)$, $P_{11}(1710)$, $P_{13}(1900)$, $S_{11}(2090)$ and $P_{11}(2100)$ resonances, we conclude that either a subthreshold resonance or a broad $P$-wave state in the near threshold range seems to be indispensable to describe present data of the $πN \to η'N$. Furthermore, at least one broad resonance above $η'N$ threshold is preferred. With this detailed analysis, we could give the invariant mass spectrum and Dalitz plot of the $J/ψ\to p\bar{p}η'$ decay for the purpose of assisting the future detailed partial wave analysis. It is found that the $J/ψ\to p\bar{p}η'$ data are useful for disentangling the above or below threshold resonant contribution, though it still further needs the differential cross section data of $πN \to η'N$ to realize some of the resonant and the non-resonant contribution. Our results are enlightening for the $η'N$ production mechanism and the properties of the nucleon resonances with the mass around 2.0~GeV.

# The nucleon resonances in the $J/\psi \to p\bar{p}\eta'$ decay

## Key claim
The authors conclude that either a subthreshold resonance (such as $S_{11}(1535)$ or $P_{11}(1710)$) or a broad $P$-wave state near the $\eta'N$ threshold is indispensable to describe the existing $\pi N \to \eta'N$ data, and at least one broad resonance above the $\eta'N$ threshold is preferred. However, the analysis shows that multiple combinations of resonances can fit the data equally well, and the available data cannot uniquely discriminate between these scenarios.

## Method
The authors use an isobar model with effective Lagrangians in a covariant orbital-spin coupling scheme to compute the $J/\psi \to p\bar{p}\eta'$ decay amplitude. The unknown coupling constants of $\eta' NN^*$ vertices are determined by fitting to the total cross section data of the $\pi N \to \eta'N$ reaction from threshold up to center-of-mass energy 2.5 GeV, including $s$- and $u$-channel diagrams for five nucleon resonances: $S_{11}(1535)$, $P_{11}(1710)$, $P_{13}(1900)$, $S_{11}(2090)$, and $P_{11}(2100)$. The coupling constants of $J/\psi NN^*$ are extracted from the experimental branching ratios of $J/\psi \to p\bar{p}\eta$ and $J/\psi \to p\bar{p}\pi^0$ decays measured by the BES Collaboration.

## Key numbers
- The branching ratio of $J/\psi \to p\bar{p}\eta'$ measured by BES is $(2.00 \pm 0.23 \pm 0.28) \times 10^{-4}$, corresponding to a partial width $\Gamma_{J/\psi \to p\bar{p}\eta'} = (1.86 \pm 0.27 \pm 0.32) \times 10^{-2}$ keV (using $\Gamma_{J/\psi} = 92.9 \pm 2.8$ keV).
- The calculated $\Gamma_{J/\psi \to p\bar{p}\eta'}$ using the fitted parameters ranges from $0.9 \times 10^{-2}$ to $8.2 \times 10^{-2}$ keV, roughly compatible with the experimental value.
- The total cross section of $\pi N \to \eta'N$ is around 100 nb from threshold to $\sqrt{s} = 2.50$ GeV.
- The $S_{11}(1535)$ contributes $(56 \pm 15)\%$ of the $J/\psi \to p\bar{p}\eta$ branching ratio of $(1.91 \pm 0.02 \pm 0.17) \times 10^{-3}$.
- The fitted coupling constant $g_{\eta'NN^*(1535)}$ is consistent with the value from Cao et al. (2008), with values ranging from $3.02 \pm 1.48$ to $9.48 \pm 4.17$ depending on the solution.
- The $\chi^2$ values for the fits range from 2.02 to 2.77, with the best fit (4r.1-2-3c4a) giving $\chi^2 = 2.02$.

## Context
This work extends the study of nucleon resonances in charmonium decays, following the partial wave analyses of $J/\psi \to p\bar{p}\pi^0$ by BES (2006, 2013) which found resonances such as $N^*(2040)$ with $J^P = 3/2^+$ and $N^*(2300)$ with $J^P = 1/2^+$. It is motivated by the need to explore decay modes with final mesons other than pions to search for "missing" resonances that couple weakly to pions, as discussed by Torres (2013). The analysis is analogous to the study of $J/\psi \to p\bar{p}\phi$ by Dai et al. (2012), and the results are intended to guide future partial wave analyses by BESIII, which plans to collect around ten billion $J/\psi$ events, estimated to contain about two million $J/\psi \to p\bar{p}\eta'$ events. The work also connects to studies of $\eta'$ photoproduction by Huang et al. (2013) and the $pN \to pN\eta'$ reaction by Nakayama et al. (2006, 2007).
