# Determining initial-state fluctuations from flow measurements in heavy-ion collisions

> We present a number of independent flow observables that can be measured using multiparticle azimuthal correlations in heavy-ion collisions. Some of these observables are already well known, such as v2{2} and v2{4}, but most are new--in particular, joint correlations between v1, v2 and v3. Taken together, these measurements will allow for a more precise determination of the medium properties than is currently possible. In particular, by taking ratios of these observables, we construct quantities which are less sensitive to the hydrodynamic response of the medium, and thus more directly characterize the initial-state fluctuations of the event shape, which may constrain models for early-time, non-equilibrium QCD dynamics. We present predictions for these ratios using two Monte-Carlo models, and compare to available data.

## Key claim

By taking ratios of certain multiparticle azimuthal correlations, one can construct observables that are less sensitive to the hydrodynamic response of the medium and thus more directly characterize initial-state fluctuations of the event shape, potentially constraining models for early-time, non-equilibrium QCD dynamics.

## Method

The authors define a set of multiparticle azimuthal correlation observables, including joint correlations between the first three flow harmonics (v1, v2, v3), and show that by taking ratios of these correlations scaled by appropriate powers of the root-mean-square vn values, the dependence on the hydrodynamic response coefficients Kn cancels. They compute predictions for these ratios using two Monte-Carlo models of the initial state: a PHOBOS Glauber Monte-Carlo and a color-glass-condensate (CGC) inspired MC-KLN model, and compare to existing data from STAR and ALICE.

## Key numbers

- For Gaussian fluctuations, the ratios v{n,n,-n,-n}/vn{2}^4 for n=1 and 3 are equal to 2, and likewise for n=2 in central collisions.
- v3{4} should be smaller than v3{2} only by a factor of approximately 2 in mid-central collisions.
- The Glauber model predicts negligible correlation between Ψ2 and Ψ3 up to 40% centrality, while the CGC model predicts a small anticorrelation.
- Predictions are shown for Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV per nucleon pair and Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV per nucleon pair, using 100 million and 20 million events respectively.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of initial-state fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, building on the realization by Alver and Roland (2010) that fluctuations from the internal structure of colliding nuclei generate specific patterns in azimuthal correlations. The proposed observables enable a more direct probe of early-time dynamics than previous measurements, which were more sensitive to medium properties such as shear viscosity (Luzum and Romatschke, 2008). The ratios constructed here can be used to discriminate between different models of the initial state, such as the Glauber and CGC approaches, and may help constrain models for early-time, non-equilibrium QCD dynamics.
