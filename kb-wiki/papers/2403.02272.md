# Study of forbidden $β$ decays within the realistic shell model

> For the first time, half-lives and energy spectra of forbidden $β$ decays are calculated within the realistic shell model. Namely, we approach this issue starting from a realistic nucleon-nucleon potential and deriving effective Hamiltonians and decay operators. Our goal is to explore the sensitivity of the shape of calculated energy spectra to the renormalization of forbidden $β$ -decay operators, an operation that allows to take into account those configurations that are not explicitly included in the chosen model space. The region that has been considered for this investigation are nuclei outside the $^{78}$Ni core, more precisely we have studied the second-forbidden $β$ decays of $^{94}$Nb and $^{99}$Tc, and fourth-forbidden $β$ decays of $^{113}$Cd and $^{115}$In, that are currently of a renewed experimental interest in terms of novel spectroscopic techniques. Our results evidence that the introduction of a renormalized $β$-decay operator leads to a marked improvement of the reproduction of experimental half-lives. As regards the spectra of both second-forbidden and fourth-forbidden decays, we have found that their calculated shapes are in good agreement with the observed ones, even if scarcely responsive to the renormalization of the decay operator. We carry out also a detailed inspection of the different components of the calculated spectra for a deeper insight about their role in reproducing the experimental shapes.

## Key claim

For the first time, half-lives and energy spectra of forbidden beta decays are calculated within the realistic shell model starting from a realistic nucleon-nucleon potential, without any empirical quenching of the axial coupling constant; the renormalization of the decay operator markedly improves the reproduction of experimental half-lives, but the shapes of the calculated energy spectra are scarcely responsive to this renormalization.

## Method

The authors derive effective shell-model Hamiltonians and decay operators from the high-precision CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, using the Vlow-k renormalization procedure and many-body perturbation theory up to third order, within a model space of four proton and five neutron orbitals outside the 78Ni core. They calculate the second-forbidden beta decays of 94Nb and 99Tc, and the fourth-forbidden beta decays of 113Cd and 115In, comparing results obtained with bare and renormalized decay operators against experimental data, and analyze the different components of the calculated energy spectra.

- For the fourth-forbidden decay of 113Cd, the Coulomb displacement energy used in the CVC relation is 13.27 MeV.
- For the fourth-forbidden decay of 115In, the Coulomb displacement energy used in the CVC relation is 13.48 MeV.
- The constant kappa relating half-life to the integrated shape function is 6144 +/- 2 s.
- The perturbative expansion of the Q-box and Theta-box vertex functions includes intermediate states up to a maximum excitation energy of Nmax=16 oscillator quanta.

## Context

This work extends the realistic shell model approach previously applied to double-beta decay of 48Ca, 76Ge, 82Se, 100Mo, 130Te, and 136Xe by Coraggio et al. (2017-2024) to forbidden single-beta decays, validating the renormalization procedure without empirical quenching of g_A. It builds on studies by Mustonen (2006), Haaranen (2016, 2017), and Kostensalo (2021, 2023) that tuned a quenching factor of g_A to reproduce spectra, and is motivated by new experimental techniques such as the COBRA demonstrator (Zuber, 2001), the ACCESS project (Pagnanini, 2023), and the ASPECT-BET detector (Biassoni, 2023). The results enable future application of the same consistent renormalization framework to neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements.
