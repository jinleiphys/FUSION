# Chemical equilibration in viscous quark-gluon plasma and electromagnetic signals

> We investigate the chemical equilibration of the parton distributions in collisions of two heavy nuclei, assuming the partonic fluid to be ideal as well as viscous. The initial conditions are taken from HIJING calculations for Au+Au collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. It was seen that when the viscous drag is taken into account in the fluid flow, the life time of the plasma is increased by nearly a factor of 2. The temperature as well as fugacities evolve slowly than their ideal counterpart. The photon and lepton pair production was also investigated. There is a two fold increase in the photon and lepton pair numbers with viscosity on. The increase in the large $p_T$ photons and the large invariant mass lepton pairs are due to slower rate of temperature evolution.

## Key claim

Including viscous drag in the hydrodynamic expansion of the quark-gluon plasma slows both the cooling rate and the chemical equilibration rate, contrary to the expectation from ideal fluid flow where slower cooling would imply faster equilibration; this leads to a nearly two-fold increase in the lifetime of the plasma and a two-fold increase in photon and lepton pair yields, with the increase at high transverse momentum and high invariant mass attributed to the slower temperature evolution rather than the longer lifetime.

## Method

The author assumed boost-invariant longitudinal expansion of a partonic fluid that achieves kinetic equilibrium at a proper time tau_iso (0.31 fm/c for RHIC, 0.23 fm/c for LHC). The hydrodynamic equation for energy-momentum conservation was solved including shear viscosity, using two different prescriptions for the shear viscosity coefficient (one from Thoma (1991) and one from a kinetic approximation). The chemical equilibration of gluons and quarks was described by master equations driven by the reactions gg ↔ ggg and gg ↔ qqbar, with reaction rates that include color Debye screening and the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect. Initial conditions for temperature and fugacities were taken from HIJING calculations for Au+Au collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, and the evolution was terminated when the temperature fell below the critical temperature T_c = 160 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Plasma lifetime increased by nearly a factor of 2 when viscosity is included, for both RHIC and LHC energies.
- At RHIC, initial conditions: tau_iso = 0.31 fm/c, T_0 = 0.57 GeV, gluon fugacity lambda_g^0 = 0.09, quark fugacity lambda_q^0 = 0.02.
- At LHC, initial conditions: tau_iso = 0.23 fm/c, T_0 = 0.83 GeV, gluon fugacity lambda_g^0 = 0.14, quark fugacity lambda_q^0 = 0.03.
- At LHC energies, even with viscosity, the plasma is not fully equilibrated at T_c: lambda_g ≈ 0.7 and lambda_q ≈ 0.5.
- Photon and lepton pair yields increased by a factor of 2 with viscosity turned on, at both RHIC and LHC energies.
- The lifetime change between the two viscosity prescriptions is less than 2%.

## Context

This work extends the chemical equilibration studies of Biro et al. (1993), who assumed ideal fluid flow, by incorporating shear viscosity into the hydrodynamic evolution. It also builds on the parton cascade model results of Geiger and Muller (1992) and the self-screened parton cascade model of Eskola, Muller, and Wang (1996). The finding that viscosity slows chemical equilibration and increases electromagnetic signals has implications for interpreting photon and dilepton yields as probes of the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC, suggesting that dissipative effects must be accounted for when extracting plasma properties from such signals.
