# On the unlocking of color and flavor in color-superconducting quark matter

> The role of the strange quark mass for the phase structure of QCD at non-vanishing densities is studied by employing a recently developed self-consistent truncation scheme for the Dyson-Schwinger equations of the quark propagators in Landau gauge. Hereby the medium modification of the effective quark interaction by the polarization of gluons is implemented. Taking into account this effect results in significantly smaller dynamical quark masses at the Fermi surface. Due to this reduction the color-flavor locked phase is always the preferred color-superconducting phase at zero temperature and for a realistic strange quark mass.

## Key claim

The paper claims that when medium modifications of the effective quark interaction (gluon polarization) are included in a self-consistent Dyson-Schwinger equation approach, the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase is always energetically preferred over the 2-flavor color superconductor (2SC) phase at zero temperature for realistic strange quark masses, contrary to earlier Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model studies which found a transition to 2SC at moderate densities.

## Method

The authors solved truncated Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) for quark propagators in Landau gauge at non-zero chemical potential, using a Nambu-Gor'kov basis to treat both normal and anomalous (gap) self-energies self-consistently. They included medium modifications of the gluon propagator via the polarization tensor (Debye screening and Landau damping) and employed two different running couplings: one from DSE studies of the Yang-Mills sector (alpha_I) and one extracted from lattice QCD data (alpha_II), treating these as lower and upper bounds. The strange quark current mass was varied while up and down quarks were taken in the chiral isospin limit, and the pressure difference between CFL and 2SC phases was evaluated from the effective action to determine the preferred phase.

## Key numbers

* At chemical potential mu = 400 MeV, the critical renormalized strange quark current mass m_s(nu=2 GeV) above which the 2SC phase becomes preferred is determined; the CFL phase is always preferred for realistic m_s values.
* Mass functions at the Fermi surface in the unbroken phase and CFL doublet channel at mu = 400 MeV are significantly smaller than vacuum constituent masses, e.g., M_s,unbroken(p_F) is much less than M_s,broken_vacuum(0) for both couplings.
* Gap functions at the Fermi surface for the triplet, doublet, and anti-doublet channels at mu = 400 MeV are presented as functions of m_s; for coupling alpha_I, phi_A values range from approximately 10-50 MeV depending on channel and m_s.
* For a renormalized strange quark mass of m_s = 200 MeV at nu = 2 GeV, the transition from 2SC to CFL occurs at a chemical potential around 400-500 MeV depending on the coupling.
* Mass functions at the renormalization scale nu = 2 GeV are comparable to vacuum values and show only slight dependence on chemical potential, confirming the scale is above dynamical chiral symmetry breaking.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to color superconductivity previously developed for the chiral limit (Nickel 2006) by including finite strange quark masses. It contradicts results from NJL-type models (Buballa 2001, Steiner 2002) which found a transition from CFL to 2SC at moderate densities, because those models neglected medium modifications of the gluon propagator. The self-consistent treatment including Debye screening and Landau damping reduces dynamical quark masses at the Fermi surface, keeping Fermi surfaces closer together and stabilizing the CFL phase. This enables more reliable predictions for the QCD phase diagram at non-zero density based directly on QCD degrees of freedom rather than model parameters.
