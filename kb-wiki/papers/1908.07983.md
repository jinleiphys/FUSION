# A way forward towards improvement of tensor force in \textit{pf}-shell

> In many shell model interactions, the tensor force monopole matrix elements often retain systematic trends originating in the bare tensor force. In this work, however, we note for GX-interactions of \textit{pf}-shell that the seven out of ten T = 1 tensor force monopole matrix elements do not share these systematic. We ameliorate this disparity making use of Yukawa-type tensor force and spin-tensor decomposition. Furthermore, we modify the single-particle energy of $1p_{3/2}$ orbit and two TBMEs of $0f$-orbit,and test the revised interaction from Ca to Ge isotopes with various physics viewpoints. The results are found to be satisfactory with respect to the experimental data.

# A way forward towards improvement of tensor force in *pf*-shell

## Key claim

The authors identify that seven out of ten T = 1 tensor force monopole matrix elements in the GX-family of *pf*-shell interactions (GXPF1, GXPF1A, GX1B1) do not follow the systematic attractive/repulsive trends expected from the bare tensor force, and they correct this disparity by replacing all ninety-four T = 1 tensor force two-body matrix elements with semi-empirically calculated values using a Yukawa-type tensor force and spin-tensor decomposition, while noting that the resulting total matrix elements remain similar to the original GX1B1 interaction.

## Method

The authors performed spin-tensor decomposition on the GX1B1 interaction to extract the central, spin-orbit, and tensor force components. They calculated new T = 1 tensor force two-body matrix elements using a Yukawa-type tensor potential with a strength parameter fitted to the tensor force monopole matrix elements of the USDB interaction in the *sd*-shell. They replaced all ninety-four T = 1 tensor force matrix elements of GX1B1 with these calculated values, then additionally modified the single-particle energy of the 1p3/2 orbit by -0.221 MeV and two 0f-orbit two-body matrix elements (V(7777:61) by -0.280 MeV and V(7575:61) by 0.399 MeV). The resulting interaction (GX1R) was tested in shell-model calculations for nuclei from Ca to Ge isotopes using the NUSHELLX@MSU code.

## Key numbers

- Seven out of ten T = 1 tensor force monopole matrix elements in GX-interactions do not possess the expected systematic properties (attractive for j> j'< configurations, repulsive for j> j'> configurations)
- The 1p3/2 single-particle energy was modified by -0.221 MeV
- The V(7777:61) matrix element was modified by -0.280 MeV
- The V(7575:61) matrix element was modified by 0.399 MeV
- The Yukawa potential used a Compton scattering length of 1.41 fm for pion mass m_pi = 139.4 MeV
- The energy gap between 1p3/2 and 0f7/2 orbits in Ca isotopes increases from 2.72 MeV at N = 20 to 4.73 MeV at N = 28
- The root mean square deviation of GX1R matrix elements with respect to GX1B1 is 0.14 MeV
- The 56Ni core component in the ground state of 56Ni is 67%, compared to 93% for the 48Ca core
- The 55Co ground state has 60% one-hole proton-configuration contribution; the first 3/2- and 5/2- states have about 27% one-particle two-hole configuration
- The 57Ni ground state (3/2-) has 53% single-particle strength; the first 5/2- state has 51%; the first 1/2- state has 36%
- The 2+1 and 4+1 states of 56Ni have 41-44% broken-core component
- The difference between theory and experiment for Ca isotopes was in the range of 0.2-0.8 MeV before the additional modifications
- For 60-64Cr, 64Fe, and 64,66Ni nuclei, the difference between GX1R and experiment is 0.2-0.4 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of tensor force systematic properties previously applied to the USDB interaction by Wang and collaborators, and addresses irregularities noted in the GXPF1A interaction by the same group. The GX-family interactions (GXPF1 from Honma (2004), GXPF1A from Honma (2005), and GXPF1B from Honma (2008)) are widely used for *pf*-shell nuclei but were found to have improper tensor force behavior. The corrected interaction GX1R has been applied to calculate nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double beta decay of 48Ca by Sarkar, showing only 1-3% increase relative to GXPF1A. This work enables future exploration of spin-orbit force characteristics and provides a tensor-corrected interaction for *pf*-shell nuclear structure studies.
