# Running Coupling in Non-Perturbative QCD - I. Bare Vertices and y-max Approximation

> A recent claim that in quantum chromodynamics the gluon propagator vanishes in the infrared limit, while the ghost propagator is more singular than a simple pole, is investigated analytically and numerically. This picture is shown to be supported even at the level in which the vertices in the Dyson-Schwinger equations are taken to be bare. The running coupling is shown to be uniquely determined by the equations and to have a large finite infrared limit.

## Key claim

The authors show that the infrared behavior where the gluon propagator vanishes and the ghost propagator is more singular than a simple pole, previously claimed by von Smekal, Hauck and Alkofer (1997) using dressed vertices, is already supported when the vertices in the Dyson-Schwinger equations are taken to be bare, though the critical exponent changes from approximately 0.92 to approximately 0.77.

## Method

The authors write the coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations for the gluon and ghost form factors in Landau gauge, replacing the full three-gluon and ghost-gluon vertices by their bare values and discarding four-gluon and quark-gluon vertices. They then introduce the y-max approximation, which replaces form factors evaluated at the loop momentum by the larger of the two external momenta, converting the angular integrals into one-dimensional Volterra equations. The resulting equations are analyzed analytically for infrared asymptotic power-law solutions and numerically for the full momentum dependence.

## Key numbers

- The infrared exponent for the ghost-loop-only truncation is κ ≈ 0.769479, giving gluon form factor F(x) ~ x^(2κ) and ghost form factor G(x) ~ x^(-κ)
- The running coupling has a finite infrared fixed point: lim_(x→0) α(x) ≈ 11.4702
- The product ν = λ Z̃₁ A₀ B₀² ≈ 0.912771 is fixed by the infrared consistency conditions
- The next-to-leading exponent in the infrared asymptotic expansion is ρ ≈ 1.96964, with the ratio b₁/a₁ = 0.829602
- The quadratic equation for κ is 19κ² + 77κ + 48 = 0, with roots κ₁ ≈ 0.769479 and κ₂ ≈ 3.28315 (the latter rejected as spurious)

## Context

This work extends the approach of von Smekal, Hauck and Alkofer (1997), who claimed that the ghost propagator is more singular than a simple pole in the infrared while the gluon propagator vanishes, using dressed vertex Ansätze. The present paper shows this picture survives even with bare vertices, though the exponent changes. The authors note that Mandelstam (1979) initiated the gluon Dyson-Schwinger study in Landau gauge, and subsequent work by Atkinson (1989) and others neglected ghost-gluon coupling. The results enable further investigation of the running coupling's infrared behavior and its relation to chiral symmetry breaking in the quark equation.
