# Strange baryons in a hot and dense medium within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> Using an extended version of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model we build a simple description of the baryons as diquark-quark bound states. First, a description of the diquarks in a dense and hot medium is presented. Then, we introduce the formalism for the baryons based on the Faddeev equation associated with the so-called `` static approximation'' which finally gives a Bethe-Salpeter equation in the diquarks-quarks channel. By identifying the baryons with the bound states, we can obtain a description of their properties. In particular, we obtain the right mass spectrum for the proton, $Λ$, $Ξ$, and $Σ$ at T=0 and $μ=0$. We extend the formalism to finite temperature and density to obtain a description of the mass change of these baryons in the medium.

# Strange baryons in a hot and dense medium within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

## Key claim

Using an extended SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a static approximation to the Faddeev equation, baryons described as diquark-quark bound states show a mass decrease of almost 30% near the chiral phase transition, but the model is limited by treating the diquark-quark interaction vertex empirically and by the static approximation which assumes infinite mass for the exchanged quark.

## Method

The authors employ an SU(3) NJL Lagrangian with scalar, vector, and 't Hooft interaction terms for the quark-antiquark channel plus a diquark interaction term, regularized with a 3-momentum cutoff. Diquark masses are obtained from the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the scalar channel, and baryons are constructed as diquark-quark bound states using a Faddeev equation reduced to a Bethe-Salpeter equation via the static approximation, where the exchanged quark mass is taken as infinite. Finite temperature and density are incorporated using the imaginary time formalism.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses at T=0, μ=0: m_q = 422 MeV, m_s = 628 MeV
- Diquark masses at T=0, μ=0: m_qq = 557 MeV, m_qs = 760 MeV
- Baryon masses at T=0, μ=0: nucleon = 938 MeV, Λ = 1080 MeV, Σ = 1181 MeV, Ξ = 1278 MeV
- The ratio m_q(T)/m_q(T=0) = 1/2 occurs at T = 220 MeV along the temperature axis
- The ratio m_q(μ)/m_q(μ=0) = 1/2 occurs at ρ/ρ_0 = 2.5 along the density axis
- Nucleon mass at ρ/ρ_0 = 1 is about 700 MeV
- Nucleon and Λ become unstable at T ≈ 220 MeV
- Nucleon and Λ become unstable at ρ/ρ_0 ≈ 2.6 (nucleon mass ≈ 425 MeV, Λ mass ≈ 850 MeV)
- Σ becomes unstable at ρ/ρ_0 ≈ 1.7 (Σ mass ≈ 950 MeV)

## Context

This work extends earlier NJL model calculations for baryons at zero temperature and density by Ishii (1995), Buck (1992), and Tjon (1994) to finite temperature and density, using the same diquark-quark bound state formalism. The nucleon mass at nuclear density (≈ 700 MeV) agrees with results from Walecka-type models by Serot (1992) and other approaches by Bentz (1999) and Zch (2000). The formalism enables future study of the phase transition from a quark-diquark plasma into a hadron gas at finite baryon density, extending similar work already done for mesons by Rehberg (1998).
