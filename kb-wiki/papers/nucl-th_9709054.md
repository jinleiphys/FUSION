# The interaction between H-dibaryons

> We investigate the interaction between H-dibaryons employing a quark cluster model with a one-gluon-exchange potential and an effective meson exchange potential (EMEP). A deeply-bound state of two H-dibaryons due to the medium range attraction of the EMEP is obtained. The bound H--H system has a size of about 0.8 $\sim$ 0.9 fm because of the short-range repulsion generated by the color-magnetic interaction and the Pauli principle.

## Key claim
The paper claims that two H-dibaryons form a deeply-bound state due to medium-range attraction from an effective meson exchange potential, but this conclusion is conditional on using a flavor-singlet scalar meson exchange model; including flavor-octet scalar mesons would likely make the binding even stronger, while the qualitative features of short-range repulsion and medium-range attraction remain unchanged.

## Method
The authors employed a quark cluster model with a one-gluon-exchange potential (OGEP) and a quadratic confinement potential between constituent quarks, treating the H-dibaryon as a (0s)⁶ configuration. They solved the Resonating Group Method (RGM) equation for the relative S-wave motion of two H-dibaryons, including quark exchange up to three-quark exchanges, and added a phenomenological effective meson exchange potential (EMEP) of gaussian form between the H-dibaryons. The EMEP strength was constrained by the binding energy of two Λ hyperons in a double hypernucleus event from KEK.

## Key numbers
- The bound H–H system has a size of about 0.8–0.9 fm.
- The EMEP range parameter α_HH = 1.00 fm, corresponding to an effective scalar meson mass μ_eff ≈ 400 MeV.
- The EMEP strength V_0HH lies in the range −1227 MeV to −1096 MeV, constrained by the double hypernucleus event with ΛΛ binding energy 8.5 ± 0.7 MeV (for ¹⁰_ΛΛBe) or 27.6 ± 0.7 MeV (for ¹³_ΛΛB).
- The binding energy of the H–H ground state is 100–170 MeV for V_0HH in the range −1096 to −1227 MeV.
- A second bound S-wave state appears for V_0HH ≲ −1200 MeV.
- The equivalent hard-core radius a_h is of order 1 fm for k ≳ 2 fm⁻¹.
- The peak of the bound-state wave function is around 0.7 fm for V_0HH = −1227 MeV.
- The limiting strength for zero scattering length (as used in Tamagaki's H-matter work) is V_0HH = −638 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the quark cluster model approach previously applied to the N–H interaction by Sakai et al. (1992, 1995) to the H–H system. It contradicts the earlier phenomenological model of Tamagaki (1991), which assumed a much weaker attraction (V_0HH = −638 MeV) and found H-matter formation at 6–9 times normal nuclear density; the present stronger attraction suggests a lower critical density. The results enable future calculations of H-matter properties and neutron star conversion scenarios, as explored by Faessler et al. (1997). A bound H–H state was also obtained in the Skyrme model by Issinskii (1988) but with a smaller binding energy of 15–20 MeV.
