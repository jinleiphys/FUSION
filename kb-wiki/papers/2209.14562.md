# Statistical hadronization of b-quarks in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energy: a case for partial equilibration of b-quarks?

> Predictions are presented within the framework of the statistical hadronization model for integrated yields of bottomonia in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC. We investigate the centrality dependence of $Υ$ production and provide predictions for a large set of still-unmeasured open-beauty hadrons.

## Key claim

The statistical hadronization model for beauty quarks (SHMb) can describe bottomonium production in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energy only if beauty quarks are assumed to be only partially thermalized, with a thermalization fraction of about 30%, rather than fully equilibrated.

## Method

The authors apply the statistical hadronization model to beauty quarks, using a canonical balance equation with a beauty fugacity determined from the initial direct bbbar production yield. The model employs thermodynamic parameters (T=156.6 MeV, mu_B=0.7 MeV, V=4997 fm^3 at 5.02 TeV) from fits to central Pb-Pb collisions, and includes a corona component for peripheral collisions. The predicted yields for open-beauty hadrons and bottomonia are compared to CMS and ALICE data on Upsilon production at midrapidity and forward rapidity, with the thermalization fraction treated as a free parameter.

## Key numbers

- Temperature from fit to central Pb-Pb collisions: T = 156.6 +/- 1.7 MeV
- Volume at midrapidity for 5.02 TeV: V = 4997 fm^3
- bbbar production cross section in pp at midrapidity: 34.5 +/- 2.4 (stat) +4.7/-2.9 (tot. syst) microbarn
- bbbar rapidity density for 0-10% central Pb-Pb at midrapidity: 0.57
- Beauty fugacity for 0-10% central Pb-Pb at midrapidity: 1.05 x 10^9
- The model overestimates Upsilon(1S) data significantly but describes Upsilon(2S) data quite well
- Data are well described for the case of 30% beauty thermalization

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model previously applied to charm quarks (Andronic 2021) to the beauty sector, revisiting earlier predictions (Andronic 2006) in light of new LHC data from CMS (2018) and ALICE (2020). The results contradict full thermalization assumptions used in transport models (Du 2017, Yao 2020) and hydrodynamical approaches (Krouppa 2016), and suggest that the strongly coupled QGP decorrelates b and bbar quarks but does not fully thermalize them. The findings enable future refinements as new open-beauty states are discovered, which would increase the inferred thermalization fraction.
