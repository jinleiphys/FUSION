# Internal One-Particle Density Matrix for Bose-Einstein Condensates with Finite Number of Particles in a Harmonic Potential

> Investigations on the internal one-particle density matrix in the case of Bose-Einstein condensates with a finite number ($N$) of particles in a harmonic potential are performed. We solve the eigenvalue problem of the Pethick-Pitaevskii-type internal density matrix and find a fragmented condensate. On the contrary the condensate Jacobi-type internal density matrix gives complete condensation into a single state. The internal one-particle density matrix is, therefore, shown to be different in general for different choices of the internal coordinate system. We propose two physically motivated criteria for the choice of the adequate coordinate systems which give us a unique answer for the internal one-particle density matrix. One criterion is that in the infinite particle number limit ($N=\infty$) the internal one-particle density matrix should have the same eigenvalues and eigenfunctions as those of the corresponding ideal Bose-Einstein condensate in the laboratory frame. The other criterion is that the coordinate of the internal one-particle density matrix should be orthogonal to the remaining $(N - 2)$ internal coordinates. This second criterion is shown to imply the first criterion. It is shown that the internal Jacobi coordinate system satisfies these two criteria while the internal coordinate system adopted by Pethick and Pitaevskii for the construction of the internal one-particle density matrix does not. It is demonstrated that these two criteria uniquely determine the internal one-particle density matrix which is identical to that calculated with the Jacobi coordinates. The relevance of this work concerning $α$-particle condensates in nuclei, as well as bosonic atoms in traps, is pointed out.

## Key claim

The internal one-particle density matrix for a Bose-Einstein condensate with finite particle number in a harmonic potential gives different results depending on the choice of internal coordinate system: the Pethick-Pitaevskii-type internal density matrix yields a fragmented condensate, while the Jacobi-type internal density matrix yields complete condensation into a single state. The authors propose two criteria that uniquely determine the adequate internal coordinate system, showing that the Jacobi coordinates satisfy these criteria while the Pethick-Pitaevskii coordinates do not.

## Method

The authors constructed the internal one-particle density matrix for an ideal Bose-Einstein condensate of N spinless bosons in a harmonic trap using two different internal coordinate systems: coordinates relative to the center of mass (Pethick-Pitaevskii-type) and Jacobi coordinates. They solved the eigenvalue problem analytically for both density matrices, obtaining explicit expressions for the eigenvalues (occupation probabilities) and eigenfunctions. They then proposed two physically motivated criteria for selecting the adequate coordinate system: (1) in the infinite particle number limit the internal density matrix should have the same eigenvalues and eigenfunctions as the laboratory-frame density matrix, and (2) the coordinate used in the internal density matrix should be orthogonal to the remaining (N-2) internal coordinates.

## Key numbers

- For the PP-type internal density matrix with N=3 particles, the S-wave occupation probability is 0.804, the P-wave is 0.173, the D-wave is 0.021, and the F-wave is 0.002.
- For the Jacobi-type internal density matrix, the only non-zero eigenvalue is lambda = 1 with 100% occupancy in the 0S single-particle state, independent of particle number N.
- In the macroscopic limit (N to infinity), the PP-type internal density matrix has eigenvalues lambda^(LM)_(n,N=infinity) = 2^3 (3-2*sqrt(2))^(2n+L+3/2), with summed angular-momentum-dependent probabilities Lambda^(L)_(N=infinity) = (2L+1)(2-sqrt(2))(3-2*sqrt(2))^L.
- In the macroscopic limit, the Jacobi-type internal density matrix becomes identical to the laboratory-frame density matrix, with lambda^(LM)_(N=infinity) = delta_(L0) delta_(M0).

## Context

This work extends the discussion by Pethick and Pitaevskii (2000) who proposed using an internal one-particle density matrix to analyze the condensate fraction of a system where only the center-of-mass degree of freedom is excited, but never demonstrated explicit diagonalization results. The paper contradicts the claim of Wilkin et al. (1998) that a rotating condensate with attractive interactions should be characterized as a fragmented condensate, by showing that the fragmentation seen in the laboratory frame arises from the choice of coordinate system rather than the physics. The results justify the use of Jacobi coordinates in earlier work by Matsumura and Yamada (2004) and Yamada et al. (2004) on alpha-particle condensates in nuclei, corroborating the almost ideal alpha-particle condensation nature of the Hoyle state in carbon-12. This work enables a unique definition of the internal one-particle density matrix for self-bound systems such as alpha-particle condensates in nuclei and bosonic atoms in traps.
