# Quantum local-equilibrium state with fixed multiplicity constraint and Bose-Einstein momentum correlations

> The one- and two-boson momentum spectra are derived in the quantum local-equilibrium canonical ensemble of noninteracting bosons with a fixed particle number constraint. We define the canonical ensemble as a subensemble of events associated with the grand-canonical ensemble. Applying simple hydro-inspired parametrization with parameter values that correspond roughly to the values at the system's breakup in $p+p$ collisions at the LHC energies, we compare our findings with the treatment which is based on the grand-canonical ensembles where mean particle numbers coincide with fixed particle numbers in the canonical ensembles. We observe a significantly greater sensitivity of the two-particle momentum correlation functions to fixed multiplicity constraint compared to one-particle momentum spectra. The results of our analysis may be useful for interpretation of multiplicity-dependent measurements of $p+p$ collision events.

## Key claim

The paper derives one- and two-boson momentum spectra in a quantum local-equilibrium canonical ensemble with a fixed particle number constraint, showing that two-particle Bose-Einstein correlation functions are significantly more sensitive to the fixed multiplicity constraint than one-particle spectra, but only under the assumption that the system can be treated as a subensemble of the grand-canonical ensemble and that the characteristic cell size is much larger than the inverse particle mass.

## Method

The authors start from a quasiequilibrium statistical operator for a noninteracting relativistic scalar field and apply a local thermal equilibrium approximation by dividing the freeze-out hypersurface into cells where the temperature and flow velocity are approximately constant. They then impose a fixed particle number constraint using a projection operator to define the canonical ensemble as a subensemble of the grand-canonical ensemble, and derive iterative relations for the one- and two-boson momentum spectra using Gaudin's method and the cyclic invariance of the trace. The calculations are performed for a hydro-inspired parametrization with parameter values corresponding roughly to the system's breakup in proton-proton collisions at LHC energies.

## Key numbers

* The one-particle momentum spectrum in the grand-canonical ensemble is given by the local-equilibrium distribution function f^{leq}(x,p) = 1/(2π)^3 * 1/(exp(β_ν(x)p^ν)-1), where β = 1/T is the inverse temperature.
* The two-particle momentum spectrum in the canonical ensemble with fixed particle number N involves double sums over n=1 to N-1 and s=1 to N-n of terms containing products of G_n^* and G_s^* functions, which do not factorize into a product of single-particle spectra.
* The characteristic cell size L̃ is assumed to satisfy L̃ ≫ 1/m, where m is the particle mass, to justify neglecting aa and a†a† terms in the local-equilibrium approximation.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Akkelin-2) on fixed multiplicity constraints in quantum statistical systems and builds on the Zubarev formalism for nonequilibrium statistical operators. The paper addresses the saturation effect in interferometry correlation radius parameters observed in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at LHC by ATLAS and CMS, where radii do not increase with multiplicity at high charged-particle multiplicities, in contrast to the nearly linear scaling seen in heavy ion collisions (Alice). The results may enable interpretation of multiplicity-dependent measurements in proton-proton collisions and could be extended to include ground-state Bose-Einstein condensation effects, which were treated for nonrelativistic models in a prior work (Akkelin-1).
