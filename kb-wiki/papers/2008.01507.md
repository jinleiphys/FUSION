# Curved Yang-Mills-Higgs gauge theories in the case of massless gauge bosons

> Alexei Kotov and Thomas Strobl have introduced a covariantized formulation of Yang-Mills-Higgs gauge theories whose main motivation was to replace the Lie algebra with Lie algebroids. This allows the introduction of a possibly non-flat connection $\nabla$ on this bundle, after also introducing an additional 2-form $ζ$ in the field strength. We will study this theory in the simplified situation of Lie algebra bundles, hence, only massless gauge bosons, and we will provide a physical motivation of $ζ$. Moreover, we classify $\nabla$ using the gauge invariance, resulting into that $\nabla$ needs to be a Lie derivation law covering a pairing $Ξ$, as introduced by Mackenzie. There is also a field redefinition, keeping the physics invariant, but possibly changing $ζ$ and the curvature of $\nabla$. We are going to study whether this can lead to a classical theory, and we will realize that this has a strong correspondence to Mackenzie's study about extending Lie algebroids with Lie algebra bundles. We show that Mackenzie's obstruction class is also an obstruction for having non-flat connections which are not related to a flat connection using the field redefinitions. This class is related to $\mathrm{d}^\nabla ζ$, a tensor which also measures the failure of the Bianchi identity of the field strength and which is invariant under the field redefinition. This tensor will also provide hints about whether $ζ$ can vanish after a field redefinition.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for curved Yang-Mills-Higgs gauge theories restricted to Lie algebra bundles (massless gauge bosons only), the existence of a field redefinition that transforms the theory into a pre-classical one (flat connection) is obstructed by Mackenzie's cohomology class, which is related to the tensor d^∇ ζ; when d^∇ ζ is non-zero, no field redefinition can make ζ vanish, and the theory cannot be transformed to a classical gauge theory.

## Method

The author restricts the general curved Yang-Mills-Higgs gauge theory framework of Kotov and Strobl to the case of Lie algebra bundles K → N, which correspond to massless gauge bosons. The compatibility conditions for gauge invariance are analyzed, showing that the connection ∇ must be a Lie derivation law covering a pairing Ξ of the tangent bundle TN with the Lie algebra bundle K, as introduced by Mackenzie. The paper then studies field redefinitions that preserve the Lagrangian but can change ∇ and ζ, and relates the existence of such redefinitions leading to a flat connection or vanishing ζ to Mackenzie's theory of extensions of Lie algebroids by Lie algebra bundles.

## Key numbers

- The obstruction class for having a non-flat connection not related to a flat connection by field redefinitions is identified with Mackenzie's obstruction class Obs(Ξ), which is related to the tensor d^∇ ζ.
- The tensor d^∇ ζ is invariant under field redefinitions and measures the failure of the Bianchi identity of the field strength: d^∇ ζ = 0 if and only if the Bianchi identity is satisfied.
- Locally, over a contractible base manifold N, there is always a field redefinition transforming the initial gauge theory to a pre-classical one, but globally there exist examples with Obs(Ξ) ≠ 0.

## Context

This work extends the curved Yang-Mills-Higgs gauge theory introduced by Kotov and Strobl, which replaces the structural Lie algebra with a Lie algebroid, by specializing to Lie algebra bundles and providing a physical motivation for the 2-form ζ. The paper establishes a strong correspondence with Mackenzie's study of extending Lie algebroids with Lie algebra bundles, showing that Mackenzie's obstruction class for such extensions also serves as an obstruction for having non-flat connections that cannot be made flat via field redefinitions. This enables a classification of when a curved Yang-Mills-Higgs gauge theory can be reduced to a classical or pre-classical gauge theory, and provides a physical interpretation of d^∇ ζ as a measure of the failure of the Bianchi identity.
