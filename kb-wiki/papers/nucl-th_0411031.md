# Quantum Opacity, the RHIC HBT Puzzle, and the Chiral Phase Transition

> We present a relativistic quantum mechanical treatment of opacity and refractive effects that allows reproduction of observables measured in two-pion (HBT) interferometry and pion spectra at RHIC. The inferred emission duration is substantial. The results are consistent with the emission of pions from a system that has a restored chiral symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a relativistic quantum-mechanical treatment of opacity and refractive effects, including an optical potential derived from chiral symmetry restoration, can simultaneously reproduce the measured HBT radii and pion spectrum at RHIC, with the inferred emission duration being substantial. The results are consistent with the interpretation that the dense medium has undergone a chiral phase transition, but the authors note that the optical potential parameters are determined by fitting data rather than derived from first principles.

## Method

The authors model the pion emission function using a blast-wave parameterization with boost-invariant longitudinal dynamics, then incorporate a complex optical potential U(b) = -(w0 + w2 p^2)ρ(b) into a two-dimensional Klein-Gordon equation to account for pion interactions with the dense medium. They solve the wave equation exactly using a partial wave expansion, avoiding the eikonal approximation, and compute the two-pion correlation function and single-pion spectrum by evaluating eight-dimensional integrals reduced through symmetry considerations. The parameters T, ηf, Δτ, RWS, aWS, w0, w2, τ0, Δη, and μπ are varied to reproduce STAR 200 GeV Au+Au data for Ro, Rs, Rl and the pion spectrum.

## Key numbers

- Temperature T = 173.2 ± 1.6 MeV, comparable to the expected chiral phase transition temperature (≈160 MeV)
- Emission duration Δτ = 2.852 ± 0.067 fm/c, comfortably smaller than the expansion time τ0 = 8.23 ± 0.10 fm/c
- Source size RWS = 11.728 ± 0.056 fm, which is 4.4 fm larger than a gold nucleus (R = 7.3 fm)
- Flow rapidity ηf = 1.314 ± 0.025, corresponding to a maximum flow velocity of 0.85 c
- Optical potential at p ≈ 1 fm⁻¹: real part -0.58 fm⁻² (momentum-dependent term w2 = 0.582 ± 0.014) and imaginary part -0.121 fm⁻² (w2 imaginary component ±0.002)
- Imaginary potential gives classical mean free path p/|Im[U]| ≈ 8 fm, much less than 2RWS
- Real part of U exceeds mπ² = 0.49 fm⁻² in magnitude, indicating the pion acts as if massless inside the medium
- χ² ≈ 3.7 per data point and 5.6 per degree of freedom for the fit to STAR data
- Pion chemical potential μπ = 123.2 ± 1.1 MeV

## Context

This work addresses the "RHIC HBT puzzle" (Heinz 2002), where hydrodynamic calculations predicted a large Ro/Rs ratio from long emission durations (Rischke 1996, Kolb 2003), but experiments found Ro/Rs ≈ 1 (Adler 2001). The authors extend earlier semi-classical treatments of opacity (Heiselberg 1997) by providing a fully quantum-mechanical treatment that includes refractive effects. The results are compared with the Buda-Lund model (Buda), which uses position-dependent temperature and fugacity in a Boltzmann distribution. The extracted parameters are consistent with predictions from Son and Stephanov (2002) regarding chiral symmetry restoration near the critical temperature, though the authors note that some model calculations (boy, sas) challenge those results. The work predicts low-momentum structure (Ramsauer effect peaks at p ≈ 15-65 MeV/c) that could be tested by the PHOBOS or BRAHMS detectors at RHIC.
