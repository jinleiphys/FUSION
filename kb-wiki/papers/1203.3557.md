# Dilepton production in proton-induced reactions at SIS energies with the GiBUU transport model

> We present dilepton spectra from p+p, d+p and p+Nb reactions at SIS energies, which were simulated with the GiBUU transport model in a resonance model approach. These spectra are compared to the data published by the HADES and DLS collaborations. It is shown that the $ρ$ spectral function includes non-trivial effects already in elementary reactions, due to production via baryon resonances, which can yield large contributions to the dilepton spectrum. Dilepton spectra from nuclear reactions in the energy range of the HADES experiment are thus found to be sensitive also to properties of nucleon resonances in the nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the ρ meson spectral function already contains non-trivial, resonance-induced effects in elementary proton-proton reactions, not only in nuclear matter, and that dilepton spectra from nuclear reactions at HADES energies are sensitive to nucleon resonance properties in the nuclear medium, but this conclusion depends on extending the resonance model beyond the Teis analysis to include double-resonance excitation channels.

## Method

The authors used the GiBUU hadronic transport model to simulate dilepton production in p+p, d+p, and p+Nb reactions at SIS energies (1.25, 2.20, and 3.50 GeV beam kinetic energies). They extended the standard Teis resonance model by adding double-resonance excitation channels (NN → ΔS₁₁(1535), NN → ΔN*, NN → ΔΔ*) to describe multi-meson final states, and compared the simulated spectra to HADES and DLS data after applying the HADES acceptance filter (HAFT v2.0) and experimental cuts.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: 1.25 GeV (√s = 2.42 GeV), 2.20 GeV (√s = 2.76 GeV), and 3.50 GeV (√s = 3.17 GeV) for p+p reactions
- Transition between resonance model and Pythia string model set at √s = 2.6 ± 0.2 GeV for baryon-baryon collisions
- Matrix elements for new double-resonance channels: |M₁|²/16π = 60 mb·GeV² for ΔS₁₁(1535), |M₂|²/16π = 12 mb·GeV² for ΔN*, |M₃|²/16π = 21 mb·GeV² for ΔΔ*
- ρ meson dilepton decay constant C_ρ = 9.078 × 10⁻⁶, ω meson C_ω = 7.666 × 10⁻⁷, φ meson C_φ = 1.246 × 10⁻⁶
- η → e⁺e⁻ branching ratio upper limit: 2.7 × 10⁻⁵ (experimental), pushed to 4.9 × 10⁻⁶ by HADES data
- π⁰ Dalitz form factor parameter b_π⁰ = 5.5 GeV⁻²; η Dalitz form factor Λ_η = 0.676 GeV
- ω Dalitz form factor parameters: Λ_ω = 0.65 GeV, Γ_ω = 75 MeV
- Δ → Nγ decay width: Γ ≈ 0.66 MeV, with form factor |F_Δ(0)| = 3.03
- Dilepton opening angle cut: θ_ee > 9° applied to all spectra

## Context

This work extends the Teis resonance model (Teis, 1996) for nucleon-nucleon collisions to higher energies by adding double-resonance excitation channels, addressing the "DLS puzzle" where in-medium effects failed to explain DLS collaboration data. The paper enables systematic comparison with HADES collaboration measurements (Agakishiev, 2009; Agakishiev, 2012) and provides a framework for disentangling vacuum resonance effects from genuine in-medium modifications of vector meson spectral functions, building on prior transport approaches by Bratkovskaya (1997, 1998) and Effenberger (1999).
