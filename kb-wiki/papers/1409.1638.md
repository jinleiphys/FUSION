# Turbulent thermalization process in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> We discuss the onset of the thermalization process in high-energy heavy-ion collisions from a weak coupling perspective, using classical-statistical real-time lattice simulations as a first principles tool to study the pre-equilibrium dynamics. Most remarkably, we find that the thermalization process is governed by a universal attractor, where the space-time evolution of the plasma becomes independent of the initial conditions and exhibits the self-similar dynamics characteristic of wave turbulence. We discuss the consequences of our weak coupling results for the thermalization process in heavy-ion experiments and briefly comment on the use of weak coupling techniques at larger values of the coupling.

## Key claim

The thermalization process in high-energy heavy-ion collisions is governed by a universal attractor, where the space-time evolution becomes independent of initial conditions and exhibits self-similar dynamics characteristic of wave turbulence, but only within the weak coupling regime (alpha_s much less than 1) and high occupancy (f(t,p) much greater than 1) where classical-statistical simulations are valid.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1 dimensional classical-statistical real-time lattice simulations of Yang-Mills dynamics using color-glass condensate (CGC) initial conditions at very weak coupling (alpha_s ~ 10^-6). They studied the early-time dynamics including plasma instabilities that break longitudinal boost invariance, then employed a general parametrization of the over-occupied state at Q*tau_0 = log^2(alpha_s^-1) to investigate the subsequent onset of thermalization with variable occupancy and momentum space anisotropy.

## Key numbers

- Plasma instabilities lead to an over-occupied plasma on a time scale parametrically given by Q*tau ~ log^2(alpha_s^-1), where Q denotes the characteristic momentum scale
- The scaling exponents observed are alpha ~ -2/3, beta ~ 0, and gamma ~ 1/3 for the self-similar gluon distribution f(tau,p_T,p_z) = (Q*tau)^alpha / alpha_S * f_S((Q*tau)^beta * p_T, (Q*tau)^gamma * p_z)
- Single particle occupancy decreases as f(tau,p_T~Q) ~ (Q*tau)^(-2/3)/alpha_S and becomes of order unity on a time scale Q*tau_quant ~ alpha_S^(-3/2)
- When extrapolating to realistic coupling (alpha_S ~ 0.3, Q ~ 2 GeV), the classical regime ends at tau_quant ~ 0.6 fm/c and the thermalization time is tau_therm ~ 2 fm/c
- The thermalization time scale in the quantum regime is parametrically Q*tau_therm ~ alpha_S^(-13/5)

## Context

This work extends the bottom-up thermalization scenario of Baier (2000) by providing first-principles classical-statistical lattice simulations that confirm the first stage of that scenario, showing consistency with small-angle elastic scattering as the dominant process. The results enable quantitative extrapolation of weak-coupling thermalization predictions to realistic coupling values at RHIC and LHC energies, and they contradict the expectation that plasma instabilities play a significant role in the dynamics of this regime. The paper also identifies serious ultraviolet problems in attempts to extend classical-statistical methods to larger couplings (g >= 0.1), following the work of Moore (2001) and Gelis (2013).
