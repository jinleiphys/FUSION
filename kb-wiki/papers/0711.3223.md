# QE Neutrino CC Cross Sections off 16O

> The charged-current quasi-elastic scattering of muon neutrino on oxygen target is computed for neutrino energy between 200 MeV and 2.5 GeV using different approximations: the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA), the Relativistic Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation with relativistic optical potential (RDWIA), and the Relativistic Fermi Gas Model (RFGM). The comparison with RFGM, which is widely used in data analyses of neutrino experiments, shows that the RFGM fails completely when applied to exclusive cross section data and leads to overestimated values of inclusive and total cross sections.

## Key claim

The Relativistic Fermi Gas Model (RFGM), widely used in neutrino data analyses, overestimates inclusive and total cross sections for charged-current quasi-elastic neutrino scattering off oxygen, and fails completely for exclusive cross sections, whereas the Relativistic Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation (RDWIA) agrees with electron-scattering data to within 10% in the peak region.

## Method

The authors computed charged-current quasi-elastic muon-neutrino cross sections on oxygen-16 using three approximations: Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA), Relativistic Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation with a relativistic optical potential (RDWIA), and the Relativistic Fermi Gas Model (RFGM). In RDWIA, distorted wave functions for the outgoing nucleon were obtained from a relativized Schrodinger equation using the LEA code, originally designed for proton-nucleus and electron-nucleus scattering. For inclusive and total cross sections, only the real part of the optical potential was used, and the effect of short-range nucleon-nucleon (NN) correlations in the ground state was included via a phenomenological spectral function.

## Key numbers

*   The FSI effect reduces the total cross section by about 30% for neutrino energy E_nu = 200 MeV compared to PWIA, decreasing to 10% at 1 GeV.
*   The effect of NN-correlations further reduces the total cross section by about 15% for E_nu = 200 MeV, decreasing to 8% at 1 GeV.
*   The RFGM overestimates the measured inclusive cross section at low momentum transfer (|q| <= 500 MeV/c); the difference with data is about 20% at |q| = 300 MeV/c.
*   For total neutrino cross sections, the RFGM result is about 15% higher than the RDWIA predictions at E_nu ~ 1 GeV.
*   The difference between RDWIA and electron-scattering data is less than 10% in the peak region.
*   The RFGM calculation used Fermi momentum p_F = 225 MeV/c and binding energy epsilon = 27 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of the RDWIA, previously tested against electron-nucleus scattering data (A(e,e'p) data), to neutrino-nucleus scattering on oxygen-16. It directly contradicts the widely used RFGM, which the authors show overestimates cross sections and fails for exclusive channels. The results enable improved Monte Carlo simulations for neutrino oscillation experiments, such as those using water targets, by providing a more accurate nuclear model that could reduce systematic uncertainties.
