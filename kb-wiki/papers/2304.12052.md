# Coexisting single-particle excitations and octupole correlations in transitional nucleus $\mathbf{^{217}Ra}$

> The level structure of the transitional nucleus $\mathrm{^{217}Ra}$ has been extended with the addition of around 20 new transitions. The discrepancies between the placements of several transitions reported in the earlier studies are resolved. The newly-established negative-parity sequence at low excitation energies hints at the expected parity-doublet structures in this nucleus. The properties of the observed simplex bands are compared with that of similar bands in neighboring nuclei. Since the presence of parity-doublet structures reflect octupole correlations, theoretical calculations using reflection-asymmetric triaxial particle rotor model (RAT-PRM) have been performed. A comparison of the observed features of the simplex bands with the predictions of the RAT-PRM calculations suggests that $\mathrm{^{217}Ra}$ exhibits an intermediate the behavior between the extremes of spherical and octupole-deformed nuclei. The termination of the simplex bands at intermediate energies and the structures lying above reflect the dominance of the single-particle excitations at higher excitation energies.

## Key claim

The nucleus ²¹⁷Ra exhibits an intermediate behavior between the extremes of spherical and octupole-deformed nuclei, with the newly-established negative-parity sequence at low excitation energies hinting at the expected parity-doublet structures, though the simplex bands terminate at intermediate energies and single-particle excitations dominate at higher excitation energies.

## Method

High-spin states in ²¹⁷Ra were populated using the ²⁰⁸Pb(¹²C, 3n)²¹⁷Ra fusion-evaporation reaction at beam energies of 68, 72, and 80 MeV, with gamma rays detected by the Indian National Gamma Array (INGA) comprising 13 Compton-suppressed clover detectors and one Low-Energy Photon Spectrometer. The level scheme was constructed based on gamma-gamma coincidence relationships, intensity measurements, directional correlation of oriented states (R_DCO) ratios, and linear-polarization measurements to determine transition multipolarities and spin-parity assignments. Theoretical calculations using the reflection-asymmetric triaxial particle rotor model (RAT-PRM) were performed and compared with the observed simplex band features.

## Key numbers

- Around 20 new transitions were added to the level scheme of ²¹⁷Ra
- The previously reported isomer at 2.4 MeV excitation energy has a half-life of T₁/₂ = 4.62(6) ns
- The 331-keV transition was determined to be a mixed M1+E2 transition with δ = 6.1(36), corresponding to approximately 97% E2 component
- The 405-keV transition was determined to be a pure E1 transition (near zero mixing ratio δ), establishing the 2301-keV state as I^π = 29/2⁻
- The 93-keV transition deexciting the 2394-keV state was confirmed as E2 multipolarity
- B(E1)/B(E2) ratios of approximately 10⁻⁶ fm⁻² were observed, comparable to those in octupole-deformed nuclei

## Context

This work extends previous studies of ²¹⁷Ra by Roy et al., Sugawara et al., and Muralithar et al., resolving discrepancies in transition placements and ordering, particularly for the 229- and 405-keV transitions. The results contrast with the neighboring even-even isotope ²¹⁶Ra, which exhibits single-particle νg₉/₂² excitations (Lonnorth; Muralithar), and ²¹⁸Ra, which shows dominance of octupole correlations (Schulz). The observation of a negative-parity sequence constituting a simplex partner band provides evidence for parity-doublet structures expected in N = 129 isotones, similar to those reported in ²¹⁶Fr, ²¹⁸Ac (Debray), and ²¹⁹Th (Reviol). The RAT-PRM calculations enable future comparisons to understand the evolution from spherical to octupole-deformed shapes in the transitional region.
