# Spectral splits of neutrinos as a BCS-BEC crossover type phenomenon

> We show that the spectral split of a neutrino ensemble which initially consists of electron type neutrinos, is analogous to the BCS-BEC crossover already observed in ultra cold atomic gas experiments. Such a neutrino ensemble mimics the deleptonization burst of a core collapse supernova. Although these two phenomena belong to very different domains of physics, the propagation of neutrinos from highly interacting inner regions of the supernova to the vacuum is reminiscent of the evolution of Cooper pairs between weak and strong interaction regimes during the crossover. The Hamiltonians and the corresponding many-body states undergo very similar transformations if one replaces the pair quasispin of the latter with the neutrino isospin of the former.

## Key claim

The spectral split phenomenon in a neutrino ensemble initially composed of electron-type neutrinos (mimicking the deleptonization burst of a core-collapse supernova) is analogous to the BCS-BEC crossover observed in ultracold atomic gas experiments, but this correspondence does not imply any pairing correlations between the neutrinos themselves.

## Method

The authors describe the neutrino ensemble using an effective two-flavor mixing scenario and the neutrino bulb model under the mean-field approximation. They map the neutrino isospin operators to the quasispin operators of the BCS pairing model, showing that the Hamiltonians for self-interacting neutrinos and BCS pairs have the same SU(2) group structure. The evolution of the neutrino state from strong self-interaction near the supernova core (BEC limit) to weak self-interaction in outer layers (BCS limit) is then analyzed using the same variational BCS wave function formalism, with the neutrino mean field components P± and P0 playing roles analogous to the pairing potential Δ± and Hartree potential in the BCS model.

## Key numbers

- The neutrino-neutrino interaction strength G = (2√2 GF / V) D(r), where GF is the Fermi constant and D(r) ∝ 1/r², causing G to drop as 1/r⁴ as neutrinos move away from the supernova center
- The oscillation frequency for a neutrino with energy εk is ωk = (m₁² - m₂²) / 2εk, where m₁ > m₂ (inverted mass hierarchy)
- A core-collapse supernova releases 99% of its energy in the form of neutrinos in the MeV energy scale
- Supernova 1987A generated 19 neutrino events in Kamiokande and IBM detectors

## Context

This work extends the formal analogy between collective neutrino oscillations and the BCS pairing model of superconductivity previously pointed out by Pehlivan et al. (2011, 2014) and further elaborated by Baker et al. (2016). The spectral split phenomenon, previously observed in numerical simulations by Duan et al. (2006, 2010), is here reinterpreted as a BCS-BEC crossover without a phase transition. This analogy enables the use of well-established condensed matter techniques to study neutrino flavor transformations in supernovae, potentially improving predictions for neutrino signals from future galactic supernovae.
