# Pseudovector versus pseudoscalar coupling in kaon photoproduction - revisited

> The question of pseudovector versus pseudoscalar coupling schemes for the kaon-hyperon-nucleon interaction is re-examined for the reaction $γp\to K^+ Λ$ in several isobaric models. These models typically include Born terms, $K^*$- and $K_1$-exchange in the t-channel, and a few different combinations of spin-1/2 baryon resonances in the $s$- and $u$-channels. The coupling constants are obtained by fitting to a large data set. We find that both pseudoscalar and pseudovector couplings can allow for a satisfactory description of the present database. The resulting coupling constants, $g_{KΛN}$ and $g_{KΣN}$, in the pseudovector coupling scheme are smaller than those predicted using flavor SU(3) symmetry, but consistent with the values obtained in a QCD sum rule calculation.

# Pseudovector versus pseudoscalar coupling in kaon photoproduction - revisited

## Key claim
Both pseudoscalar (PS) and pseudovector (PV) coupling schemes for the kaon-hyperon-nucleon vertex can provide a satisfactory description of the present database for the reaction gamma p -> K+ Lambda, meaning the data do not distinguish between the two coupling schemes. However, the resulting coupling constants in the PV scheme are smaller than those predicted by flavor SU(3) symmetry but consistent with QCD sum rule calculations, and the PV scheme yields more stable leading coupling constants when additional resonances are included.

## Method
The authors constructed three isobaric models for gamma p -> K+ Lambda, each including Born terms, t-channel K*(892) and K1(1270) exchange, and different combinations of spin-1/2 baryon resonances in the s- and u-channels. They performed least-squared fits to 242 data points (differential cross sections and polarization) for both PS and PV coupling schemes, extracting coupling constants and chi-squared per degree of freedom for each model.

## Key numbers
- The fitted coupling constant g_{K Lambda N}/sqrt(4 pi) in the PS scheme ranges from -1.55 (Model I) to -2.41 (Model III), compared to the SU(3) value of -3.7 +/- 0.7 and the QCD sum rule value of -1.96
- In the PV scheme, g_{K Lambda N}/sqrt(4 pi) ranges from -1.24 (Model I) to -1.65 (Model II), approximately 20% smaller than the corresponding PS values
- The chi-squared per degree of freedom for PS models ranges from 1.56 (Model I) to 1.38 (Model III); for PV models from 1.57 (Model I) to 1.38 (Model III)
- The fitted data set consists of 242 data points from the gamma p -> K+ Lambda reaction, compared to 131 data points used in the earlier study by Bennhold and Wright (1987)
- The AS model (Adelseck and Saghai, 1990) gave g_{K Lambda N}/sqrt(4 pi) = -4.11 when fitted to 117 differential cross section data points, but refitting to the larger database changed this value to -1.55

## Context
This work re-examines the question of PS versus PV coupling for the kaon-hyperon-nucleon vertex, first investigated by Bennhold and Wright (1987) using only Born terms and a limited pre-1984 database. It extends that analysis using more complete isobaric models similar to those of Adelseck and Saghai (1990) and Williams, Ji, and Cotanch (1992), with a larger modern database. The results enable future studies of kaon photoproduction from nuclear matter, where the contact interaction present only in the PV scheme could help distinguish the two coupling schemes, and motivate the need for precise Lambda polarization data at backward angles to resolve the question.
