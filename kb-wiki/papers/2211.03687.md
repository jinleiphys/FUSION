# Counting States: A Combinatorial Analysis of SQM Fragmentation

> The Strange Quark matter (SQM) hypothesis states that at extreme pressure and density conditions a new ground state of matter would arise, in which half of the \textit{down} quarks become strange quarks. If true, it would mean that at least the core of neutron stars is made of SQM. In this hypothesis, SQM would be released in the inter-stellar medium when two of these objects merge. It is estimated that $10^{-2} M\odot$ of SQM would be released this way. This matter will undergo a sequence of processes that should result in a fraction of the released SQM becoming heavy nuclei through \textit{r-process}. In this work we are interested in characterizing the fragmentation of SQM, with the novelty of keeping track of the \textit{quark configuration} of the fragmented matter. This is accomplished by developing a methodology to estimate the energy of each fragment as the sum of its \textit{constituent quarks}, the Coulomb interaction among the quarks and fragments' momenta. The determination of the fragmentation output is crucial to fully characterize the subsequent nucleosynthesis.

# Counting States: A Combinatorial Analysis of SQM Fragmentation

## Key claim
The fragmentation of strange quark matter (SQM) released in neutron star mergers can be characterized by enumerating all possible quark configurations that conserve equal numbers of up, down, and strange quarks, with the most probable output being "distilled" configurations that concentrate strange quarks into the minimum number of fragments, rather than the previously assumed all-Lambda baryon output.

## Method
The authors develop a combinatorial formalism based on partitions of the baryon number A into fragment sizes, then apply Polya's enumeration theorem to count the number of quark configurations (distributions of u, d, and s quarks among fragments) that satisfy the constraint N_u = N_d = N_s = A. The energy of each fragment is estimated as the sum of constituent quark masses (m_u = 313 MeV, m_d = 313 MeV, m_s = 555 MeV) plus a Coulomb term depending on the strangelet electric charge. The partition function for each configuration is computed as a sum over fragments of a Boltzmann factor with fragment mass and temperature, and the configuration maximizing entropy (phase space volume) is selected as the fragmentation output.

## Key numbers
- For A=3, the three partitions have Omega values: Omega(lambda_1)=10, Omega(lambda_2)=10, Omega(lambda_3)=1
- For A=6, the partition [4,1,0,0,0,0] (one fragment of size 4 and one of size 1) has Omega=418 and is the most probable at all temperatures studied (2 MeV, 5 MeV, 50 MeV)
- The constituent quark masses used are m_u=313 MeV, m_d=313 MeV, and m_s=555 MeV
- The strong interaction time scale for fragmentation is tau_s = 10^{-24} s, and the weak interaction time scale for subsequent decay is tau_w = 10^{-14} s
- An estimated 10^{-2} solar masses of SQM would be released per merger event

## Context
This work extends the SQM hypothesis originally proposed by Witten (1984) and subsequent studies of strange star mergers by Rosinka (2008) and Pietri (2019). It builds on prior quark combinatorics approaches by Valoshin (1982), Guman (1975), and Anisovich (1978), and on earlier fragmentation studies by Bugaev (2000), Paulucci (2014), and Bucciantini (2019). The formalism enables calculation of the electron fraction Y_e that feeds into r-process nucleosynthesis models such as those of Freiburghaus (1999), Paulucci (2017), and Horvath (2019), and can determine whether the fragmentation output favors heavy element production (e.g., Y_e=1/4 from half Sigma-minus and half Sigma-plus configurations) or weak r-process (Y_e=2/3 from all-Lambda configurations).
