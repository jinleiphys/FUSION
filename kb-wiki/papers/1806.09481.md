# Effective-field-theory predictions of the muon-deuteron capture rate

> We quantify the theoretical uncertainties of chiral effective-field-theory predictions of the muon-deuteron capture rate. Theoretical error estimates of this low-energy process is important for a reliable interpretation of forthcoming experimental results by the MuSun collaboration. Specifically, we estimate the three dominant sources of uncertainties that impact theoretical calculations of this rate: those resulting from uncertainties in the pool of fit data used to constrain the coupling constants in the nuclear interaction, those due to the truncation of the effective field theory, and those due to uncertainties in the axial radius of the nucleon. For the capture rate into the ${}^1S_0$ channel, we find an uncertainty of approximately $4.6~s^{-1}$ due to the truncation in the effective field theory and an uncertainty of $3.9~s^{-1}$ due to the uncertainty in the axial radius of the nucleon, both of which are similar in size to the targeted experimental precision.

## Key claim

The paper quantifies the theoretical uncertainties in chiral effective-field-theory predictions of the muon-deuteron capture rate, finding that the truncation error in the effective field theory and the uncertainty in the nucleon axial radius each contribute uncertainties of similar size to the targeted experimental precision of a few percent, while uncertainties from the fit data used to constrain coupling constants are much smaller.

## Method

The authors used two approaches: (i) a family of 42 chiral potentials at order Q^3 fitted at 7 different regulator cutoffs between 450-600 MeV to 6 different ranges of laboratory energy in the nucleon-nucleon scattering database, and (ii) a set of chiral interactions at orders Q^0, Q^2, and Q^3 with pion-nucleon couplings from a Roy-Steiner analysis, with the short-distance low-energy constant c_D determined from triton binding energy and beta-decay half-life. The muon-capture rate into the singlet S-wave channel of the neutron-neutron system was calculated using nuclear wavefunctions and weak currents consistently derived up to order Q^3 in chiral effective field theory.

## Key numbers

- Capture rate into the ^1S_0 channel from the family of 42 potentials: 252.4^{+1.5}_{-2.1} s^{-1}
- Capture rate using Roy-Steiner based interactions with c_D fixed from triton beta-decay: 252.8 ± 4.6 ± 3.9 s^{-1}, where the first uncertainty (4.6 s^{-1}) is due to EFT truncation and the second (3.9 s^{-1}) is due to the axial radius uncertainty
- Statistical uncertainty from low-energy constant uncertainties: 0.5 s^{-1}
- Order-by-order capture rates with c_D reproducing triton beta-decay: 186.3 s^{-1} at Q^0, 247.3 s^{-1} at Q^2, and 252.8 s^{-1} at Q^3
- Axial radius squared used: r_A^2 = 0.46(22) fm^2
- Estimated total capture rate including higher partial waves: 397.8 s^{-1}

## Context

This work extends prior chiral effective-field-theory calculations of muon-deuteron capture by Ando et al. (2001) and Marcucci et al. (2011, 2010) by providing a more rigorous uncertainty quantification. The analysis builds on recent progress in uncertainty quantification for effective field theories by Carlsson et al. (2015), Hoferichter et al. (2015), and Furnstahl et al. (2015). The results are important for interpreting forthcoming experimental results from the MuSun collaboration at the Paul Scherrer Institut, and the correlation between the muon-capture rate and the proton-proton fusion S-factor enables connections between electroweak observables in the two-nucleon sector.
