# $β$-decay half-lives as an indicator of shape-phase transition in neutron-rich Zr isotopes with particle-vibration coupling effect

> [Background] $β$-decay half-life is sensitive to the shell structure near the Fermi levels. Nuclear deformation thus impacts the $β$-decay properties. [Purpose] A first-order shape-phase transition in neutron-rich Zr isotopes is predicted by some models. We investigate the $β$-decay half-lives of neutron-rich nuclei around $^{110}$Zr, where the shape-phase transition is predicted to occur, to see if the $β$-decay half-life can be an indicator of the shape changes. [Method] The proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (RPA) is adopted to calculate the Gamow-Teller transitions. In addition, we apply the quasiparticle phonon-vibrational coupling (PVC) to consider the phonon couplings. [Results] The spherical and oblate configurations give similar half-lives but shorter ones than the prolate configuration at the RPA level. The PVC effect further reduces the half-lives in general, but the effect is smaller for the deformed configuration than that for the spherical one. As a result, it makes the shape change from the oblate configuration to the spherical configuration visible. Therefore, a sudden shortening of $β$-decay half-lives is always found at the nuclear shape changes. [Conclusions] $β$-decay half-life is an indicator of the shape-phase transition. The shape mixing and the roles of the triaxial deformation are subject to study in the future.

## Key claim

The β-decay half-life can serve as an indicator of a first-order shape-phase transition in neutron-rich Zr isotopes around N=74, but only when the particle-vibration coupling (PVC) effect is included, because at the random-phase approximation (RPA) level the spherical and oblate configurations give similar half-lives and the shape change between them is not visible.

## Method

The authors performed Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the SkM* and SLy4 functionals to obtain the ground states of neutron-rich Zr isotopes under axial symmetry. They then computed Gamow-Teller transition strengths using the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) and, for spherical configurations, extended the calculation with quasiparticle phonon-vibrational coupling (QPVC) to include higher-order correlations. The β-decay half-lives were evaluated from the GT strength distributions using Fermi's golden rule with an effective axial-vector coupling ratio (g_A/g_V)_eff = 1.

## Key numbers

- For ¹¹⁴Zr, the spherical RPA calculation gives a half-life of 6.4 ms; including only the lowest quadrupole phonon in PVC gives 3.9 ms for spherical and 2.6 ms for oblate configurations.
- For ¹¹⁴Zr, including only the lowest octupole phonon in PVC gives 1.1 ms for spherical and 5.4 ms for oblate configurations; including both phonons gives 0.9 ms for spherical and 2.4 ms for oblate configurations.
- The first-excited quadrupole state in ¹¹⁴Zr appears at 1.7 MeV with a strength of 6.4 × 10³ fm⁴ for the spherical configuration, while the K=2 state for the prolate configuration lies at 0.6 MeV with a strength of 1.3 × 10³ fm⁴.
- The lowest octupole state in ¹¹⁴Zr appears at 1.1 MeV for both spherical and oblate configurations, but the strengths for oblate and prolate configurations are more than one order of magnitude smaller than for the spherical configuration.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nuclear deformation effects on β-decay half-lives by Sarriguren (1999-2015) and Ha (2014-2017), which were based on RPA without beyond-RPA effects. The authors build on the recognition that PVC effects are essential for reproducing GT resonance widths (Niu 2014, 2016; Robin 2019) and improving half-life predictions (Niu 2015, 2018; Litvinova 2020). The results enable experimental confirmation of the predicted shape-phase transition around N=74 in Zr isotopes using half-life measurements at radioactive-ion beam facilities, which are more accessible than spectroscopic quadrupole moment measurements. The authors note that shape mixing and triaxial deformation effects remain to be studied.
