# $^{42}$Ca and $^{50}$Ca with the (Many- and Few-body) Unified Method

> A new method unifying many and few-body aspects of nuclear structure has recently been introduced \cite{hov18}. This method combines the many-body description of a core and the few-body structure of this core surrounded by two valence nucleons. For this reason this method is expected to work specially well when applied to nuclei close to the driplines, where the few-body halo structure with one or more nucleons outside the core is established. In this work we apply the new method to nuclei close to the valley of stability, with $^{42}$Ca and $^{50}$Ca as illustrations. We compare the results from uncorrelated mean-field calculations with the ones obtained with the unified method allowing arbitrary correlations in the valence space. We find that the unified method provides results rather similar, although distinguishable, to the Hartree-Fock calculations. The correlations are much less pronounced than at the driplines, which initially were targets for the unified method. The halo structure is not artificially maintained, but the correlations are here demonstrated to be applicable to well-bound nuclei. Excited states built on valence degrees of freedom are calculated for the same nuclei.

# 42Ca and 50Ca with the (Many- and Few-body) Unified Method

## Key claim

The unified method, originally designed for dripline nuclei with halo structures, produces results for the well-bound stable isotopes 42Ca and 50Ca that are similar to but distinguishable from Hartree-Fock calculations, with correlations much less pronounced than at the driplines; the method is demonstrated to be applicable to well-bound nuclei, though it systematically underbinds these systems by 10-15% in two-neutron separation energy due to the use of a free-space neutron-neutron interaction and phase-equivalent potentials for Pauli exclusion.

## Method

The authors treat 42Ca and 50Ca as three-body systems (core plus two valence neutrons) using a unified method that couples Skyrme-Hartree-Fock mean-field descriptions of the 40Ca or 48Ca core with Faddeev three-body calculations in coordinate space via the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method. The core and valence structures are iterated to self-consistency, with the core-valence neutron interaction arising entirely from the chosen Skyrme interaction and the Pauli principle enforced through phase-equivalent potentials or exclusion of adiabatic channels. Four different Skyrme interactions (SKM*, SLy4, SKa, SLy7) and two neutron-neutron interactions (simple gaussian and Argonne v18) are compared.

## Key numbers

- For 42Ca with SKM*: two-neutron separation energy from unified method after convergence is -16.74 MeV, compared to Hartree-Fock value of -19.48 MeV and experimental value of -19.84 MeV
- For 50Ca with SKM*: two-neutron separation energy from unified method after convergence is -10.85 MeV, compared to Hartree-Fock value of -12.48 MeV and experimental value of -11.50 MeV
- For 42Ca with SKM*: unified method gives total binding energy 2.74 MeV less bound than pure Hartree-Fock result
- For 50Ca: unified method gives underbinding between 0.7 MeV and 2 MeV depending on Skyrme force, compared to Hartree-Fock
- Differences between using simple gaussian and Argonne v18 neutron-neutron interactions are less than 150 keV in final two-neutron separation energies
- For 42Ca with SKM*: root-mean-square distance between core and valence neutron (rcn) is 4.12 fm from unified method, 4.06 fm from Hartree-Fock; distance between valence neutrons (rnn) is 5.54 fm from unified method
- For 50Ca with SKM*: rcn is 4.50 fm from unified method, 4.47 fm from Hartree-Fock; rnn is 6.02 fm from unified method
- Core rms radii: 3.40 fm for 40Ca and 3.55 fm for 48Ca with SKM*

## Context

This work extends the unified method previously introduced by Hovdebo et al. (2018) and applied to dripline nuclei such as 26O (Hovdebo et al., 2017), 70Kr (Hovdebo et al., 2018b), and 72Ca (Hovdebo et al., 2018c) to stable nuclei near the valley of stability. The results show that while the method successfully describes halo structures at the driplines, for well-bound nuclei like 42Ca and 50Ca the use of a free-space neutron-neutron interaction and phase-equivalent potentials for Pauli exclusion leads to systematic underbinding of 10-15% in two-neutron separation energy, though less than 1% in total binding energy. The work enables future applications of the unified method across the nuclear chart, demonstrating that the approach is not limited to weakly-bound systems, while also identifying the need for improved treatment of pairing correlations in the valence space for well-bound nuclei.
