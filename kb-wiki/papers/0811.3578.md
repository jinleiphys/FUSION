# Three-body Recombination of Lithium-6 Atoms with Large Negative Scattering Lengths

> The 3-body recombination rate at threshold for distinguishable atoms with large negative pair scattering lengths is calculated in the zero-range approximation. The only parameters in this limit are the 3 scattering lengths and the Efimov parameter, which can be complex valued. We provide semi-analytic expressions for the cases of 2 or 3 equal scattering lengths and we obtain numerical results for the general case of 3 different scattering lengths. Our general result is applied to the three lowest hyperfine states of Lithium-6 atoms. Comparisons with recent experiments provide indications of loss features associated with Efimov trimers near the 3-atom threshold.

## Key claim

The three-body recombination rate for distinguishable atoms with large negative scattering lengths is calculated in the zero-range approximation, with the inclusive effect of deeply-bound dimers accounted for by analytically continuing the Efimov parameter to a complex value; the authors show that a narrow loss feature observed in lithium-6 experiments near 130 G may be attributed to an Efimov state near the three-atom threshold, but they cannot fit the broad loss feature near 500 G or the slope of the rate in the middle of the low-field region.

## Method

The authors solved nine coupled Skorniakov–Ter-Martirosov (STM) integral equations for the S-wave amplitudes in the zero-range limit, subtracting singular terms to obtain regular amplitudes that depend log-periodically on the ultraviolet cutoff. The inclusive effect of all deeply-bound diatomic molecules was incorporated by rotating the integration path into the complex plane via a complex Efimov parameter \(\kappa_* \to \kappa_* \exp(i\eta_*/s_0)\). The resulting rate constant \(K_3\) was computed numerically for three different negative scattering lengths and applied to the three lowest hyperfine states of lithium-6 atoms using the magnetic-field dependence of the scattering lengths.

## Key numbers

- For three equal negative scattering lengths \(a\), the recombination rate constant is \(K_3 = \frac{16 \pi^2 C \sinh(2\eta_*)}{\sin^2[s_0 \ln(D |a| \kappa_*)] + \sinh^2 \eta_*} \frac{\hbar a^4}{m}\) with \(s_0 = 1.00624\), \(C = 29.62(1)\), and \(D = 0.6642(2)\).
- For two equal negative scattering lengths \(a\) and a third that vanishes (\(a_{23}=0\)), the discrete scaling factor is \(e^{\pi/s_0} \approx 1986\) with \(s_0 = 0.413698\), \(C = 0.8410(6)\), and \(D = 0.3169(1)\).
- The relation between the Efimov parameter and the ultraviolet cutoff for three equal scattering lengths is \(\kappa_* = 0.17609(5) \Lambda\).
- For lithium-6, the van der Waals length is \(\ell_{\rm vdW} \approx 62.5 a_0\); the low-field universal region is \(122\ {\rm G} < B < 485\ {\rm G}\) where the smallest scattering length \(a_{12}\) reaches \(-290 a_0 = -4.6 \ell_{\rm vdW}\) near 320 G.
- In the high-field region \(B > 834\ {\rm G}\), the smallest scattering length \(a_{13}\) increases from \(-3285 a_0\) at 834 G to \(-2328 a_0 \approx -37 \ell_{\rm vdW}\) at 1200 G.
- A two-parameter fit to the Heidelberg data in the low-field region gave \(\Lambda = 436 a_0^{-1}\) and \(\eta_* = 0.11\).
- A two-parameter fit to the last two Penn State data points in the high-field region gave \(\Lambda = 37.0 a_0^{-1}\) and \(\eta_* = 2.9 \times 10^{-4}\), predicting a resonant enhancement near 1160 G.

## Context

This work extends the zero-range theory of three-body recombination for identical bosons developed by Braaten and Hammer (2003) to the case of three distinguishable atoms with large negative scattering lengths, providing semi-analytic expressions for two or three equal scattering lengths and numerical results for the general case. The calculations are applied to recent experiments on lithium-6 atoms by Jochim et al. (Heidelberg) and O'Hara et al. (Penn State), suggesting that the narrow loss feature near 130 G may arise from an Efimov trimer near the three-atom threshold, while the broad loss feature near 500 G could not be reproduced. The results enable quantitative predictions for three-body loss rates in the high-field universal region above 834 G, where a search for an Efimov resonance near 1160 G could accurately determine the two three-body parameters \(\kappa_*\) and \(\eta_*\).
