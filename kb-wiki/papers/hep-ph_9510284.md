# Calculation of the anomalous $γπ^* \to ππ$ form factor

> The form factor for the anomalous process $γπ^* \to ππ$, $F^{3π}(s,t,u)$, is calculated as a phenomenological application of the QCD Dyson-Schwinger equations. The chiral-limit value dictated by the electromagnetic, anomalous chiral Ward identity, $F^{3π}(0,0,0)= e N_c/(12π^2 f_π^3)$, is reproduced, {\it independent} of the details of the modelling of the gluon and quark 2-point Schwinger functions. Using a parametrisation of the dressed $u$-$d$ quark 2-point Schwinger function that provides a good description of pion observables, such as $π$-$π$ scattering-lengths and the electromagnetic pion form factor, $F^{3π}(s,t,u)$ is calculated on a kinematic range that proposed experiments plan to explore. Our result confirms the general trend of other calculations; i.e., a monotonic increase with $s$ at fixed $t$ and $u$, but is uniformly larger and exhibits a more rapid rise with $s$.

## Key claim

The chiral-limit value of the anomalous form factor \(F^{3\pi}(0,0,0) = e N_c/(12\pi^2 f_\pi^3)\) is reproduced independent of the details of the quark and gluon 2-point Schwinger functions, but the evolution of \(F^{3\pi}(s,t,u)\) with the Mandelstam variable \(s\) does depend on the model parameters even in the chiral limit.

## Method

The authors evaluated the four-dimensional loop integral for the \(\gamma\pi^*\to\pi\pi\) vertex using dressed quark 2-point Schwinger functions, dressed quark-photon vertices satisfying the Ward-Takahashi identity, and pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, all derived from QCD Dyson-Schwinger equations. The quark 2-point Schwinger function was parametrized by six parameters fitted to reproduce pion observables including the pion decay constant, pion mass, quark condensate, pion charge radius, \(\pi\)-\(\pi\) scattering lengths, and the electromagnetic pion form factor on spacelike \(q^2\in[0,4]\) GeV\(^2\). The calculation was performed in Euclidean space using Gaussian quadrature, working in the rest frame of one outgoing pion, for the kinematic range \(\bar s \in [4,16]\), \(\bar t = -1\), and \(\bar u\) determined by a fixed photon energy of 1 GeV in the proton rest frame.

## Key numbers

* Chiral limit value: \(F^{3\pi}(0,0,0) = e N_c/(12\pi^2 f_\pi^3)\), reproduced independent of model details.
* Fitted parameters: \(C_0 = 0.121\), \(\overline{m} = 0.00897\), \(b_0 = 0.131\), \(b_1 = 2.90\), \(b_2 = 0.603\), \(b_3 = 0.185\), with mass scale \(D = 0.160\) GeV\(^2\) set by requiring \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV.
* Calculated pion decay constant: \(f_\pi = 0.0924\) GeV; calculated pion mass: \(m_\pi = 0.1385\) GeV.
* Calculated quark condensate: \(-\langle \bar q q \rangle^{1/3}_{1\,\text{GeV}^2} = 0.221\) GeV.
* Calculated pion charge radius: \(r_\pi = 0.55\) fm (compared to experimental \(0.663 \pm 0.006\) fm).
* Calculated \(\pi^0\to\gamma\gamma\) coupling: \(g_{\pi^0\gamma\gamma} = 0.505\) (dimensionless), compared to experimental \(0.504 \pm 0.019\).
* Normalized form factor fit: \(\tilde F^{3\pi}(s,t,u) = 1.044 + 0.096\,x + 0.006\,x^2\) with \(x = (\bar s/4 - 1)\), valid for \(\bar t = -1\) and \(\bar u\) from Eq. (ufit).
* The calculation is considered unreliable for \(\bar s > 12\) due to poles in the pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude entering the integration region.

## Context

This work extends Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of anomalous processes, following earlier calculations of the \(\pi^0\to\gamma\gamma\) vertex (Roberts 1994) and the \(\gamma^*\pi^0\to\gamma\) transition (Frank, Mitchell, Roberts, and Tandy 1995). The result is compared with three prior approaches: vector meson dominance models (Terent'ev 1972; Rudaz 1974) and a chiral expansion with vector meson saturation (Bijnens, Bramon, and Cornet 1990). The authors' prediction is uniformly larger and rises more rapidly with \(s\) than all three alternatives, and the proposed CEBAF experiment (Miskimen, Wang, and Yegneswaran) should be able to distinguish between the models. The calculation provides a test of the analytic structure of QCD Schwinger functions in the timelike region, which is relevant for studies of vector meson Bethe-Salpeter and baryon Fadde'ev amplitudes.
