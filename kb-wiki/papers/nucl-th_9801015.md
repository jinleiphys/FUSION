# A relativistic study of the nucleon form factors

> We perform a calculation of the relativistic corrections to the electromagnetic elastic form factors of the nucleon obtained with various Constituent Quark Models. With respect to the non relativistic calculations a substantial improvement is obtained up to Q^2 about 2 (GeV/c)^2.

# A relativistic study of the nucleon form factors

## Key claim
Relativistic corrections to the electromagnetic elastic form factors of the nucleon, calculated by expanding current matrix elements in powers of 1/m and using non-relativistic constituent quark model wave functions, produce a substantial improvement over non-relativistic calculations up to Q² ≈ 2 (GeV/c)², but the corrections are still insufficient to fully reproduce experimental data.

## Method
The authors expand the electromagnetic current matrix elements in powers of the inverse quark mass (1/m), treating the momentum transfer Q² exactly while keeping only zero-order terms in the relative quark momenta. Lorentz boosts are applied to quark spinors to transform from the nucleon rest frame to the Breit frame, and the non-relativistic 3-quark wave functions from three different constituent quark models (harmonic oscillator, hypercentral three-body force, and hypercoulomb potential) are used as input. The resulting relativistic corrections introduce a multiplicative factor from the quark spinor expansion and replace the momentum transfer Q² in the non-relativistic form factors with Q² M²/E².

## Key numbers
- Relativistic corrections improve non-relativistic calculations up to Q² ≈ 2 (GeV/c)²
- For the harmonic oscillator model, the proton charge radius is given by <r²> = 1/α² + 6/M², where M is the nucleon mass and m = M/3
- Harmonic oscillator parameter α = 0.229 GeV gives the correct proton rms radius without relativistic corrections; α = 0.410 GeV reproduces photoexcitation of D₁₃ and F₁₅ resonances with a confinement radius of about 0.5 fm; α = 0.285 GeV gives the correct radius after relativistic corrections
- For the hypercentral three-body force potential: τ = 4.59 and b_conf = 1.61 fm⁻²
- For the hypercoulomb potential: τ = 6.39, with the non-relativistic proton charge form factor given by G_Eⁿʳ(Q²) = 1/[1 + (25/24)(Q²/(τ² m²))]^(7/2)

## Context
This work extends the approach of expanding current matrix elements in powers of 1/m (Pfeiffer et al., Dreschsel et al.) to elastic nucleon form factors, following three main lines of relativistic treatment: expansion of relativistic current operators in 1/m, light-cone approaches (Cardarelli et al., Salme et al., Coester et al.), and full relativistic current matrix element expansion. The authors use three different constituent quark models: the harmonic oscillator model (Isgur et al.), the hypercentral three-body force model (Platania et al.), and the hypercoulomb analytical model (Siegert et al.). The results show that while relativistic corrections improve agreement with data, the persisting discrepancies indicate that additional degrees of freedom such as quark-antiquark pairs and gluons need to be explicitly included in constituent quark models, as noted by previous work on transition form factors (Siegert et al., Bilia et al.).
