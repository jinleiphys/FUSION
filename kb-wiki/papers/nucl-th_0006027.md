# Spin observables for pion production from pd collisions

> We have calculated the proton analyzing power A_{y0} of the pion-production reaction from pd collisions for one energy close to threshold and for another in the region of the $Δ$-resonance. A fair reproduction of the experimental data could be obtained in both cases with a model which includes isoscalar and isovector $πN$ rescatterings in s waves, as well as the p-wave rescattering mechanisms mediated by the $πNN$ and $πN Δ$ vertices. For the analyzing power at threshold we found that the initial-state interaction (ISI) is also quite important.

## Key claim

The paper reports that a model including isoscalar and isovector πN s-wave rescatterings, plus p-wave rescattering via πNN and πNΔ vertices, can fairly reproduce the proton analyzing power A_{y0} for pd → π⁰³He at two energies; the reproduction is qualified as "fair" and the model's success depends on including initial-state interaction (ISI) at threshold and both s-wave diagrams at the Δ-resonance.

## Method

The authors calculated the proton analyzing power A_{y0} for the reaction pd → π⁰³He using a model that includes isoscalar and isovector πN s-wave rescatterings (with a ρ-mediated isovector diagram and a subtracted σ-exchange model for the isoscalar channel) and p-wave rescattering mechanisms via nonrelativistic πNN and πNΔ vertices. The three-nucleon bound-state wave functions were taken from prior calculations, and the initial-state three-nucleon dynamics were solved using Faddeev-type Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equations with high-rank separable representations (PEST and BBEST) of the Paris and Bonn B potentials. Calculations were performed at two pion center-of-mass momenta: one near threshold (P_c.m. ≈ 0.25 m_π) and one near the Δ-resonance (P_c.m. ≈ 1.36 m_π).

## Key numbers

- At threshold (P_c.m. ≈ 0.25 m_π), the solid-line calculation including ISI reproduces the experimental A_{y0} data, while the plane-wave (Born) calculation (dashed line) does not; the ISI effects are "quite important" at this energy.
- At the Δ-resonance (P_c.m. ≈ 1.36 m_π), the solid-line calculation including both s-wave diagrams (isoscalar off-shell effects and isovector ρ exchange) plus p-wave mechanisms reproduces the data in the forward hemisphere, while the dotted line (p-wave only) and dashed line (excluding isoscalar off-shell effects) do not.
- Without the off-shell σ-exchange effects, the threshold cross section was underestimated by one order of magnitude (qualitative statement from the text; no explicit number given for the cross section).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous model for pd → π⁰³He cross sections at threshold (Canton et al., 1999) to spin observables, specifically the analyzing power A_{y0}. It also builds on earlier treatments of the Δ-resonance region (Ref. [2] in the paper) and the formalism for spin observables (Ref. [5]). The off-shell σ-exchange model follows Hamilton (1975) and Oset (1988) as an alternative to the heavy-meson exchange explanation of Lee and Riska (1993). The results enable future tests of the model at backward angles in the Δ-resonance region, where the situation remains controversial, and provide a benchmark for three-nucleon dynamics calculations with different NN potentials (Paris via PEST, Bonn B via BBEST).
