# On QCD sum rules for vector mesons in nuclear medium

> Vector mesons show up in the electromagnetic current-current correlator. QCD sum rules provide a constraint on hadronic models for this correlator. This constraint is discussed for the case of finite nuclear density concerning the longitudinal as well as the transverse part of the current-current correlator at finite three-momentum.

## Key claim

QCD sum rules provide no model-independent prediction about a possible mass shift of vector mesons in nuclear medium; only together with additional assumptions about the width of the respective vector meson can a statement about the density dependence of the masses be deduced from the sum rule analysis.

## Method

The authors derive QCD sum rules for the longitudinal and transverse parts of the current-current correlator for rho and omega mesons at arbitrary three-momentum with respect to the nuclear medium, using the operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension-6 condensates. They calculate the OPE side including scalar condensates, twist-2 spin-2 condensates, twist-2 spin-4 condensates, and twist-4 spin-2 condensates, using a Fermi gas approximation for the nuclear medium and parton distribution moments from deep inelastic scattering. The hadronic side is expressed through a twice-subtracted dispersion relation connecting the OPE result to the hadronic spectral function.

## Key numbers

* Quark condensate nucleon expectation value: ⟨N(0)|q̄q|N(0)⟩ = m_N σ_N / m_q, where σ_N is the nucleon sigma term
* Gluon condensate nucleon expectation value: ⟨N(0)|(α_s/π)G²|N(0)⟩ = -(16/9) m_N m_N^(0), where m_N^(0) is the nucleon mass in the chiral limit
* The hard scale √Q² is typically of order 1 GeV (the order of magnitude of the considered vector meson)
* Light current quark masses are of order a few MeV, negligible compared to the 1 GeV hard scale

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule studies of vector mesons in nuclear matter (Hatsuda and Lee, 1992; Hatsuda, Lee, and Shiomi, 1995; Jin and Leinweber, 1995; Lee, 1997) by considering arbitrary three-momentum of the vector meson and both longitudinal and transverse polarizations, rather than only mesons at rest. The authors emphasize that unlike the traditional narrow-width approximation used in earlier sum rule analyses, their derived sum rules are intended as consistency checks for hadronic models of in-medium vector mesons (such as those by Rapp, Wambach, and collaborators; Klingl, Kaiser, and Weise; and Peters, Mosel, and collaborators) rather than as model-independent predictions. This enables future work to test whether specific hadronic models satisfy the QCD sum rule constraints at finite three-momentum.
