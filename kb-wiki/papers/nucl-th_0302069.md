# Where is the charm quark energy loss at RHIC?

> Heavy quark energy loss in a hot QCD plasma is computed taking into account the competing effects due to suppression of zeroth order gluon radiation below the plasma frequency and the enhancement of gluon radiation due to first order medium induced Bremsstrahlung. The results suggest a surprising degree of cancellation between the two medium effects for charm quarks and provides a possible explanation for the null effect observed by PHENIX in the prompt electron spectrum in Au+Au at $\surd s=$ 130 AGeV.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that the apparent null effect of charm quark energy loss observed by PHENIX in Au+Au collisions at 130 AGeV may be explained by a surprising degree of cancellation between two competing medium effects: the suppression of zeroth-order gluon radiation due to the QCD Ter-Mikayelian plasmon effect and the enhancement of gluon radiation due to first-order medium-induced Bremsstrahlung, with the dead cone effect alone being insufficient.

## Method

The authors computed the zeroth-order (vacuum) energy loss for heavy quarks including the QCD Ter-Mikayelian effect by using hard thermal loop (HTL) self-energies to account for both transverse and longitudinal gluon modes with frequency- and wavenumber-dependent plasmon masses. They then extended the GLV opacity expansion method to first order in opacity to compute the medium-induced radiative energy loss for heavy quarks, incorporating both the quark mass and an effective gluon plasmon mass approximated by the asymptotic value \(\mu/\sqrt{2}\). The net energy loss was obtained by summing the medium-modified zeroth-order loss and the first-order induced loss, and compared to the vacuum energy loss for a 10 GeV charm quark as a function of plasma thickness.

## Key numbers

- The transverse plasmon mass effect reduces the zeroth-order energy loss for charm quarks by approximately 30% relative to the vacuum case, for a medium with Debye screening scale \(\mu = 0.5\) GeV.
- For charm quarks with energy \(E \sim 5-15\) GeV, the first-order induced fractional energy loss \(\Delta E^{(1)}/E \approx 0.2\) with both dead cone and plasmon mass reduction, using \(\alpha_s = 0.3\), \(\mu = 0.5\) GeV, \(\lambda = 1\) fm, and \(L/\lambda = 4\).
- For a 10 GeV charm quark in a plasma with \(\mu = 0.5\) GeV and \(\lambda = 1\) fm, the vacuum energy loss \(\Delta E^{(0)}_{vac}/E \approx 1/3\), while the medium-modified zeroth-order loss \(\Delta E^{(0)}_{med}/E \approx 1/4\).
- The net energy loss for a 10 GeV charm quark is remarkably close to the vacuum value for an effective static opacity medium with \(L \approx 3\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the heavy quark energy loss formalism of Dokshitzer (2001), who identified the dead cone effect, by adding the QCD analog of the Ter-Mikayelian effect previously studied in QED. The authors also generalize the GLV opacity expansion method (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev, 2000-2001) to include both heavy quark mass and gluon plasmon mass. The results provide a possible explanation for the null effect observed by PHENIX (Adcox, 2002) in prompt electron spectra from Au+Au collisions, and suggest that varying beam energy, system size, and centrality at RHIC and LHC could test this cancellation mechanism. The paper also notes that thermal gluon absorption (Wang, 2001) and potential charm elliptic flow could provide additional tests.
