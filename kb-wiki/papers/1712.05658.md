# An Alpha Particle Model for Carbon-12

> We introduce a new model for the Carbon-12 nucleus and compute its lowest energy levels. Our model is inspired by previous work on the rigid body approximation in the $B=12$ sector of the Skyrme model. We go beyond this approximation and treat the nucleus as a deformable body, finding several new states. A restricted set of deformations is considered, leading to a configuration space $\mathcal{C}$ which has a graph-like structure. We use ideas from quantum graph theory in order to make sense of quantum mechanics on $\mathcal{C}$ even though it is not a manifold. This is a new approach to Skyrmion quantisation and the method presented in this paper could be applied to a variety of other problems.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new model for Carbon-12 that treats the nucleus as a deformable body composed of three alpha particles, allowing shapes that interpolate between an equilateral triangle and a linear chain, rather than assuming rigid rotation of either configuration. The key hedge is that the model assumes a restricted set of deformations (only isosceles triangles) and a flat potential energy landscape in the shape-deforming direction, which the author argues is more appropriate than a small-vibrations approximation at the energies of interest.

## Method

The author models Carbon-12 as three point particles (alpha particles) and restricts the configuration space to isosceles triangles that interpolate between an equilateral triangle and a linear chain, parameterized by a shape variable s. The configuration space has a graph-like structure consisting of three 4-manifolds (one for each particle lying on the symmetry axis) intersecting at a 3-manifold of equilateral triangles. Quantum mechanics on this non-manifold space is formulated using ideas from quantum graph theory, with the Schrodinger equation solved numerically after imposing continuity and derivative-matching boundary conditions at the equilateral triangle vertex, along with indistinguishability under the S3 permutation group.

## Key numbers

- The shape parameter s ranges from s=0 (linear chain) to s_max = 1/√3 ≈ 0.58 (equilateral triangle)
- The model predicts three new energy levels for Carbon-12 at approximately 19.8 MeV (3+ state), 21.3 MeV (4- state), and 22.2 MeV (4+ state)
- The energy difference between the lowest-lying 0+ and 2+ states of Carbon-12 is used to fix energy units by matching to experiment
- The potential V(s) is a quartic polynomial with two free parameters (energy difference between minima and barrier height), expressed in MeV

## Context

This work extends the rigid body quantization of B=12 Skyrmions by Manton and Lau (2017), which treated the equilateral triangle and linear chain configurations as rigid rotors and produced rotational bands matching some experimental Carbon-12 states. It contrasts with the algebraic cluster model (ACM) of Bijker and Iachello (2000), which treats excited states as small vibrations of an equilateral triangle but predicts too many low-lying states. The method enables a global treatment of shape deformation that resolves the missing low-lying 1- and 2- states in the rigid body picture, and the quantum graph approach could be applied to other problems such as quantisation of the B=7 Skyrmion.
