# Strangeness in Pb-Pb Collisions at 158 A GeV

> We study relative strange particle abundances measured in Pb-Pb 158 A GeV interactions. The thermal and chemical source parameters of these particles are determined under reaction scenario hypothesis invoking confined and deconfined hadronic matter.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed strange particle abundances in Pb-Pb collisions at 158 A GeV can be explained within a factor of 1.4 by a fully thermally and chemically equilibrated hadronic gas model, but a statistically more significant fit is obtained with a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) model; however, the available data cannot distinguish between early emission from a deconfined fireball and a later hadronization scenario.

## Method

The authors performed a least-squares fit of a thermal model to eight strange (anti)baryon ratios measured by the WA97 and NA49 collaborations, plus an estimated Lambda/K0s ratio. The model parameters included freeze-out temperature Tf, light quark fugacity lambda_q, strange quark fugacity lambda_s, strange phase space occupancy gamma_s, and a parameter Rc describing the relative off-equilibrium abundance of strange mesons and baryons. Fits were performed under three scenarios: direct emission from QGP, QGP with strangeness conservation constraint, and a fully equilibrated hadronic gas (HG) with gamma_s=1 and Rc=1.

## Key numbers

*   The WA97 collaboration reports a factor of at least 2 enhancement in the (Omega+anti-Omega)/(Xi+anti-Xi) ratio in Pb-Pb compared to p-Pb collisions, with a value of approximately 3 (greater than 2 at 95% confidence level).
*   Measured ratios from WA97: anti-Lambda/Lambda = 0.14 +/- 0.03; anti-Xi/Xi = 0.27 +/- 0.05; anti-Omega/Omega = 0.42 +/- 0.12; Xi/Lambda = 0.14 +/- 0.02; anti-Xi/anti-Lambda = 0.26 +/- 0.05; Omega/Xi = 0.19 +/- 0.04; anti-Omega/anti-Xi = 0.30 +/- 0.09.
*   The NA49 collaboration reports anti-Lambda/Lambda = 0.17 +/- 0.03, and (Xi+anti-Xi)/(Lambda+anti-Lambda) = 0.13 +/- 0.03.
*   The best QGP fit yields Tf = 272 +/- 74 MeV, lambda_q = 1.50 +/- 0.07, lambda_s = 1.14 +/- 0.04, gamma_s = 0.63 +/- 0.10, with chi^2 = 1.0.
*   The hadronic gas fit yields Tf = 155 +/- 7 MeV, lambda_q = 1.56 +/- 0.09, lambda_s = 1.14 (from strangeness conservation), gamma_s = 1 (fixed), with chi^2 = 7.6.
*   The baryochemical potential in the hadronic gas fit is mu_b = 205 +/- 10 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of strangeness enhancement in Sulphur-induced reactions at 200 A GeV (WA85, WA94, NA35 collaborations) to the higher multiplicity environment of Pb-Pb collisions. The authors compare their results to previous studies of S-S and S-W data (Letessier, Rafelski, Tounsi, 1996) and find that the specific strangeness yield per baryon increases by only about 20% for central Pb reactions compared to S reactions. The paper enables future discrimination between QGP and hadronic gas scenarios by calling for better data including global strangeness enhancement and specific entropy per baryon measurements.
