# Nonequilibrium effects in hadronic fireball expansion

> We consider a spherical volume of hot and dense hadronic matter (fireball) expanding into a vacuum. It is assumed that initially the fireball matter is in local thermal and chemical equilibrium with vanishing collective velocity. The time evolution of the fireball is studied in parallel within the GiBUU transport model and an ideal hydrodynamic model. The equation of state of an ideal hadronic gas is used in the hydrodynamic calculation. The same set of hadronic species is used in transport and fluid-dynamical simulations. Initial coordinates and momenta of hadrons in transport simulations have been randomly generated by using the Fermi and Bose distributions for (anti)baryons and mesons. The model results for radial profiles of densities and collective velocities of different hadronic species are compared at different times. We find that two considered models predict essential differences in time evolution of hadron abundances, which are especially pronounced for hyperonic species. This gives an evidence of a strong deviation from chemical equilibrium in expanding hadronic matter.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a spherical hadronic fireball expanding into vacuum shows strong deviations from chemical equilibrium, especially for hyperonic species, when comparing microscopic GiBUU transport simulations with ideal hydrodynamic calculations, contradicting the hydrodynamic assumption of maintained local chemical equilibrium throughout the expansion.

## Method
The authors simulated the time evolution of a spherical hadronic fireball (initial radius 6 fm, central energy density 1.3 GeV/fm³, central baryon density 0.45 fm⁻³) using both the GiBUU transport model (version 1.2.2, in cascade mode without mean fields) and a (1+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic model with an ideal hadronic gas equation of state. Initial conditions were generated from Fermi and Bose distributions for all hadronic species (including baryons, antibaryons, mesons, and resonances listed in Table 1), and radial profiles of densities, collective velocities, and particle multiplicities were compared at multiple times up to 40 fm/c.

## Key numbers
- Initial fireball parameters: R = 6 fm, a = 0.3 fm, ε₀ = 1.3 GeV/fm³, n₀ = 0.45 fm⁻³ (typical for heavy-ion collisions at E_lab ≈ 10 AGeV)
- Initial central temperature T ≈ 180 MeV, baryon chemical potential μ ≈ 328 MeV, strangeness chemical potential μ_S ≈ 110 MeV
- Total baryon number of fireball: B_tot ≈ 417
- Initial total antibaryon multiplicity: approximately 18.6
- Λ hyperon multiplicity increases from 13 at t=0 to about 50 at t=40 fm/c in GiBUU, while decreasing in HDM
- Relative fraction of bound Λ's (in hyperonic resonances) decreases from about 80% to 30% between t=0 and t=40 fm/c in GiBUU
- Net Λ production rate is of order 1 c/fm at t ≲ 40 fm/c
- Grid cell sizes: Δr = 2.5 × 10⁻² fm, Δt = 5 × 10⁻³ fm/c
- GiBUU simulations averaged over 5000 events

## Context
This work extends earlier hadrochemical studies (Bir83, Bar88, Gor92) that combined rate equations with simplified hydrodynamics for spatially uniform fireballs neglecting surface effects, and a recent comparison of hydrodynamic and transport calculations for cylindrical gluonic fireballs (Bou09). The results show that ideal hydrodynamics fails to capture the slow chemical equilibration of hyperons and kaons seen in transport simulations, implying that hybrid hydro-cascade models (Bas00, Tea01, Non07, Hir08, Pet08) must account for prolonged chemical nonequilibrium when simulating baryon-rich matter at FAIR, NICA, and lowest RHIC energies.
