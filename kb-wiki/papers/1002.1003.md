# Eccentricity fluctuations in an integrated hybrid approach: Influence on elliptic flow

> The effects of initial state fluctuations on elliptic flow are investigated within a (3+1)d Boltzmann + hydrodynamics transport approach. The spatial eccentricity ($ε_{\rm RP}$ and $ε_{\rm part}$) is calculated for initial conditions generated by a hadronic transport approach (UrQMD). Elliptic flow results as a function of impact parameter, beam energy and transverse momentum for two different equations of state and for averaged initial conditions or a full event-by-event setup are presented. These investigations allow the conclusion that in mid-central ($b=5-9$ fm) heavy ion collisions the final elliptic flow is independent of the initial state fluctuations and the equation of state. Furthermore, it is demonstrated that most of the $v_2$ is build up during the hydrodynamic stage of the evolution. Therefore, the use of averaged initial profiles does not contribute to the uncertainties of the extraction of transport properties of hot and dense QCD matter based on viscous hydrodynamic calculations.

## Key claim

In mid-central (b=5-9 fm) heavy ion collisions at SPS energies, the final elliptic flow is independent of both initial state fluctuations and the equation of state, meaning that using averaged initial profiles does not introduce uncertainties into the extraction of transport properties from viscous hydrodynamic calculations.

## Method

The authors used a (3+1)d hybrid approach combining the UrQMD hadronic transport model for initial conditions and final-state rescattering with an ideal one-fluid hydrodynamic evolution for the hot and dense stage. They compared full event-by-event calculations with calculations using averaged initial conditions at the hydrodynamic starting time t_start, for two different equations of state (a hadron gas EoS and a bag model EoS with a first-order phase transition), across beam energies from E_lab=2-160A GeV.

## Key numbers

- Starting times for hydrodynamic evolution range from t_start=12.417 fm at E_lab=2A GeV to t_start=1.415 fm at E_lab=160A GeV for Au+Au/Pb+Pb collisions
- Initial eccentricity (both ε_RP and ε_part) increases with beam energy and is larger for more peripheral collisions; participant eccentricity is always larger than reaction plane eccentricity
- For mid-central collisions (b=5-9 fm), the difference between ε_RP and ε_part is small and fluctuations are very moderate
- Elliptic flow in the initial state at t_start is compatible with zero at all beam energies
- The main part of elliptic flow is generated during the ideal hydrodynamic expansion, with only a small amount generated in the final dilute stage
- For the bag model EoS, averaged initial conditions lead to reduced elliptic flow for pions at higher p_t compared to fluctuating initial conditions, while for the hadron gas EoS the results are similar

## Context

This work extends the systematic investigation of initial condition effects on hydrodynamic calculations, following studies by Kolb (2000), Huovinen (2001), and others that established elliptic flow as a probe of QCD matter properties. The authors use the UrQMD hybrid approach developed in their earlier work (Petersen 2008, 2009) and compare with experimental data from Alt (2003). The finding that averaged initial conditions are sufficient for mid-central collisions at SPS energies enables more straightforward extraction of transport coefficients from viscous hydrodynamic calculations, though the authors note this may not hold at higher RHIC energies and that other ambiguities such as bulk viscosity contributions (Denicol 2009) and relaxation time treatments (Monnai 2010) remain under debate.
