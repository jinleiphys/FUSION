# Anisotropic Strange Stars in the Spotlight: Unveiling Constraints through Observational Data

> Motivated by the recent suggestions that very massive pulsar (PSR J0952-0607) and very light compact object (HESS J1731-347) exist, in this article, we revisit the possibility of such objects being strange stars instead of the standard hadronic neutron stars. We study the possible presence of local anisotropy and how it affects the macroscopic properties of strange stars and compare our results with the recent constraints presented in the literature. We found that the presence of anisotropy increases the maximum mass, the radius of the canonical star, and its tidal deformability for positive values of $λ_{\rm BL}$ and the opposite for negative values. We also show that although we cannot rule out the possibility of very compact objects being standard hadronic neutron stars, strange stars easily fulfill most of the observational constraints.

## Key claim

Strange stars with local anisotropy can simultaneously satisfy most recent observational constraints, including the very massive pulsar PSR J0952-0607 and the very light compact object HESS J1731-347, but the authors cannot rule out the possibility that these objects are standard hadronic neutron stars.

## Method

The authors model strange quark matter using a vector-enhanced MIT bag model with parameters B^(1/4) = 145 MeV, m_u = m_d = 4 MeV, m_s = 95 MeV, and G_V = 0.3 fm^2, ensuring charge neutrality and chemical stability. They incorporate local anisotropy in pressure using the Bowers-Liang (BL) model, where the anisotropy parameter λ_BL measures the degree of anisotropy, and solve the modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for spherically symmetric static configurations. The resulting mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities are compared against observational constraints from NICER, LIGO/Virgo, and individual pulsar measurements.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass ranges from 2.06 M_sun (λ_BL = -2.0) to 3.00 M_sun (λ_BL = +2.0)
- Canonical star radius R_1.4 ranges from 11.23 km (λ_BL = -2.0) to 11.70 km (λ_BL = +2.0)
- Dimensionless tidal deformability Λ_1.4 ranges from 575 (λ_BL = -2.0) to 802 (λ_BL = +2.0)
- The PSR J0952-0607 mass of 2.35 ± 0.17 M_sun can be explained for anisotropy in the range -1.0 < λ_BL < 2.0
- The lower limit of 2.50 M_sun from GW190814 can be reached for 0.5 < λ_BL < 2.0
- All curves satisfy the Capano et al. (2020) constraint of R_1.4 < 11.9 km
- Only λ_BL = -2.0 satisfies the revised GW170817 bound of 70 < Λ_1.4 < 580

## Context

This work extends previous studies of anisotropic strange stars by Silva et al. (2015) and Doneva et al. (2012), who explored anisotropy in the BL and Horvat models respectively, and applies them to the most recent observational constraints. The paper addresses the tension between the very massive pulsar PSR J0952-0607 (Romani, 2022), the very light HESS J1731-347 object (Doroshenko, 2022), and the tight radius constraint from Capano et al. (2020), which are difficult to reconcile with standard hadronic neutron stars. The results enable future observational campaigns to constrain both the degree of anisotropy and the nature of compact objects, particularly the HESS J1731-347 object which the authors suggest may be an anisotropic strange star.
