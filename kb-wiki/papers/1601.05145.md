# Glueball dynamics in the hot plasma

> We discuss the glueball contribution to the equation of state (EoS) of hot gluon matter below and above $T_c$. It is shown that the strong changing of masses of scalar and pseudoscalar glueballs near $T_c$ plays very important role in the thermodynamics of $SU(3)$ gauge theory. In particular, we give the arguments that these glueballs become massless at $T_G\approx 1.1T_c$ and this phenomenon is crucial in understanding of the mystery in the behavior of trace anomaly founded in the lattice calculation.

# Glueball dynamics in the hot plasma

## Key claim
The scalar and pseudoscalar glueballs become massless at approximately 1.1 times the deconfinement temperature, and this massless behavior is crucial for explaining the trace anomaly peak observed in lattice calculations of pure SU(3) gauge theory. The author argues that this mechanism resolves the "mystery" of the trace anomaly behavior, but the model is qualitative and does not include perturbative QCD corrections.

## Method
The author uses a dilaton approach relating the scalar glueball mass to the gluon condensate, assuming the relation holds above the deconfinement temperature. The temperature-dependent glueball mass is modeled as m(T) = m0 * sqrt(1 - (T/T_G)^4) based on the gluon condensate temperature dependence. The pressure and trace anomaly are computed from a gas of two light glueball states (scalar and pseudoscalar) below T_G, and from a mixed glueball-gluon phase above T_G, with the gluon contribution scaled by (T_G/T)^2 to match lattice scaling.

## Key numbers
- T_G ≈ 1.1 T_c: the temperature at which glueballs become massless and the trace anomaly reaches its maximum
- Δ_max = I(T_G)/T_G^4 = 3.07: the model prediction for the trace anomaly at T_G, compared to the lattice result of 2.48 from Borsanyi (2012)
- m0 = 901 MeV: the zero-temperature glueball mass parameter extracted from the model, using T_c ≈ 270 MeV for pure SU(3) gauge theory
- m(T_c) = 507 MeV: the glueball mass at the deconfinement temperature
- R_G ≤ 0.2 fm: the size of the lowest scalar glueball at zero temperature, cited from Schafer (1994) and deForcrand (1991)

## Context
This work extends the "fuzzy bags" interpretation of Pisarski (2006) for the trace anomaly behavior first observed in lattice calculations by Boyd (1996). It builds on the dilaton approach of Lanik (1984) and Ellis (1984) and on earlier studies of glueball properties above T_c by Vento (2006) and Kochelev (2006, 2015). The model enables a three-phase picture of pure SU(3) gauge theory: massive glueball gas below T_c, light glueball phase between T_c and 1.1 T_c, and mixed massless glueball-gluon phase above 1.1 T_c. The author notes that the model predicts a small violation of the 2(N_c^2 - 1) scaling for the trace anomaly observed in large N_c lattice calculations by Panero (2009), which could be tested with more precise lattice data.
