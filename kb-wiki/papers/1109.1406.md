# Anomalous dipion invariant mass distribution of the $Υ(4S)$ decays into $Υ(1S) π^{+} π^{-}$ and $Υ(2S) π^{+} π^{-}$

> To solve the discrepancy between the experimental data on the partial widths and lineshapes of the dipion emission of $Υ(4S)$ and the theoretical predictions, we suggest that there is an additional contribution which was not taken into account in previous calculations. Noticing that the mass of $Υ(4S)$ is above the production threshold of $B\bar B$, the contribution of the sequential process $Υ(4S)\to B\bar B\to Υ(nS)+S\to Υ(nS)+π^+π^-$ ($n=1,2$) may be sizable, and its interference with that from the direct production would be important. The goal of this work is to investigate if a sum of the two contributions with a relative phase indeed reproduces the data. Our numerical results on the partial widths and the lineshapes $dΓ(Υ(4S)\to Υ(2S,1S)π^+π^-)/d(m_{π^+π^-})$ are satisfactorily consistent with the measurements, thus the role of this mechanism is confirmed. Moreover, with the parameters obtained by fitting the data of the Belle and Babar collaborations, we predict the distributions $dΓ(Υ(4S)\to Υ(2S,1S)π^+π^-)/d\cosθ$ which have not been measured yet.

## Key claim

The discrepancy between experimental data and theoretical predictions for the dipion emission of $\Upsilon(4S)$ can be resolved by including a sequential process $\Upsilon(4S)\to B\bar B\to \Upsilon(nS)+S\to \Upsilon(nS)+\pi^+\pi^-$ ($n=1,2$) whose amplitude interferes with the direct QCD multipole expansion contribution, provided a relative phase between the two amplitudes is introduced and fitted to data.

## Method

The authors model the direct $\Upsilon(4S)\to\Upsilon(nS)\pi^+\pi^-$ transition using an effective Lagrangian based on the Novikov-Shifman parametrization with S-wave and D-wave terms. They compute the hadronic loop contribution from the sequential process $\Upsilon(4S)\to B\bar B\to \Upsilon(nS)\mathcal{S}\to\Upsilon(nS)\pi^+\pi^-$, where $\mathcal{S}$ are scalar mesons ($\sigma(600)$ and $f_0(980)$ for $\Upsilon(1S)$; $\sigma(600)$ only for $\Upsilon(2S)$), using the Cutkosky cutting rule to keep only the absorptive part of the loop. The total amplitude is the sum of direct and loop contributions with a fitted relative phase $\phi_\mathcal{S}^{(n)}$, and the model parameters are determined by fitting the dipion invariant mass distributions and partial widths from Belle and BaBar data using the MINUIT package.

## Key numbers

- Partial width $\Gamma(\Upsilon(4S)\to \Upsilon(1S)\pi^+\pi^-) = (3.65\pm0.67(\mathrm{stat})\pm0.65(\mathrm{syst}))$ keV (Belle)
- Partial width $\Gamma(\Upsilon(4S)\to \Upsilon(1S)\pi^+\pi^-) = (1.8\pm0.4)$ keV (BaBar)
- Partial width $\Gamma(\Upsilon(4S)\to \Upsilon(2S)\pi^+\pi^-) = (2.7\pm0.8)$ keV (BaBar)
- Ratio $\mathcal{R} = \Gamma(\Upsilon(4S)\to \Upsilon(2S)\pi^+\pi^-) / \Gamma(\Upsilon(4S)\to \Upsilon(1S)\pi^+\pi^-) \approx 1.16$ (BaBar)
- Fitted partial width for $\Upsilon(4S)\to \Upsilon(1S)\pi^+\pi^-$: 2.255 keV (Belle data), 3.690 keV (Belle new data), 1.799 keV (BaBar data)
- Fitted partial width for $\Upsilon(4S)\to \Upsilon(2S)\pi^+\pi^-$: 2.642 keV
- Coupling constant $g_{\Upsilon(4S) BB} \simeq 24$
- Pion decay constant $f_\pi = 130$ MeV, pion mass $m_\pi = 140$ MeV
- Scalar meson couplings: $g_{\sigma \pi \pi} = 16.2$ GeV$^{-1}$, $g_{f_0 \pi \pi} = 2.40$ GeV$^{-1}$
- $\sigma(600)$ mass 0.526 GeV, width 0.302 GeV; $f_0(980)$ mass 0.98 GeV, width 0.07 GeV

## Context

This work addresses the failure of the Kuang-Yan model (Kuang 1981) to describe the $\Upsilon(4S)$ dipion lineshapes and the anomalous ratio $\mathcal{R}\approx1.16$ (BaBar 2006), despite that model's success for lower $\Upsilon$ states like $\Upsilon(2S)\to\Upsilon(1S)\pi^+\pi^-$ and $\Upsilon(3S)\to\Upsilon(1S)\pi^+\pi^-$. The proposed mechanism extends earlier coupled-channel ideas applied to $\psi(3770)$ and $\Upsilon(5S)$ decays (Chen 2011). The predictions for the angular distribution $d\Gamma/d\cos\theta$ provide a testable signature for Belle-II or a future Super-B factory.
