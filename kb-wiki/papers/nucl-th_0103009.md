# Cluster Emission and Phase Transition Behavior in the Nuclear Disassembly

> The features of the emissions of light particles ($LP$), charged particles ($CP$), intermediate mass fragments ($IMF$) and the largest fragment ($MAX$) are investigated for $^{129}Xe$ as functions of temperature and "freeze-out" density in the frameworks of isospin dependent lattice gas model and classical molecular dynamics model. Definite turning points for the slopes of average multiplicity of $LP$, CP and $IMF$, and of the mean mass of the largest fragment ($A_{max}$) are shown around a liquid gas phase transition temperature and while the largest variances of the distributions of $LP$, $CP$, $IMF$, and $MAX$ appear there. It indicates that the cluster emission rate can be taken as a probe of nuclear liquid gas phase transition and while the largest fluctuation is simultaneously accompanied at the point of the phase transition within lattice gas model and molecular dynamics model as the traditional thermodynamical theory requires when a phase transition occurs.

## Key claim

The slopes (emission rates) of average multiplicities of light particles, charged particles, and intermediate mass fragments, as well as the mean mass of the largest fragment, show definite turning points at the same temperature where the largest fluctuations of these quantities appear; this temperature is consistent with the liquid-gas phase transition temperature extracted from other observables, indicating that cluster emission rate can serve as a probe of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations using an isospin-dependent lattice gas model (I-LGM) and an isospin-dependent classical molecular dynamics model (I-CMD) for the nucleus 129Xe. In the I-LGM, nucleons were placed on a cubic lattice with nearest-neighbor interactions (epsilon_pn = -5.33 MeV, epsilon_nn = epsilon_pp = 0 MeV) and evolved via Metropolis sampling to achieve thermal equilibrium at a given temperature and freeze-out density. In the I-CMD, the equilibrated lattice configuration was used as initial condition for classical evolution under an isospin-dependent potential, with and without Coulomb interaction, and clusters were identified at asymptotic times.

## Key numbers

- The phase transition temperature extracted from slopes and fluctuations depends on freeze-out density: approximately 4 MeV at 0.18 rho_0, 5 MeV at 0.38 rho_0, and 6 MeV at 0.60 rho_0 in the I-LGM.
- For I-CMD at freeze-out density 0.38 rho_0, the phase transition temperature is about 4 MeV with Coulomb interaction and about 5 MeV without Coulomb interaction.
- The effective power law parameter tau shows a minimum, and the second moment S_2 and information entropy H show maxima at temperatures of 4-4.25 MeV (0.18 rho_0), 5-5.5 MeV (0.38 rho_0), and 6-6.5 MeV (0.60 rho_0) in the I-LGM.
- The intermediate mass fragment multiplicity N_imf shows a rise and fall with temperature, with the peak shifting to lower temperature when Coulomb force is included.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice gas model calculations by Pan and Das Gupta (1995, 1996) and Gupta et al. (1997) that identified phase transition signatures through the effective power law parameter tau, the second moment S_2, and specific heat. It also builds on the author's prior work (Ma, 1999) proposing multiplicity information entropy and nuclear Zipf's law as phase transition diagnostics. The finding that cluster emission rates and their fluctuations signal the phase transition provides a new observable that can be compared with experimental excitation functions, particularly measurements of multiplicity slopes and variances, to identify phase transition signals in nuclear disassembly.
