# Second order hydrodynamics based on effective kinetic theory and electromagnetic signals from QGP

> We study the thermal dilepton and photon production from relativistic heavy ion collisions in presence of viscosities by employing the recently developed second order dissipative hydrodynamic formulation estimated within a quasiparticle description of thermal QCD (Quantum Chromo-Dynamics) medium. The sensitivity of shear and bulk viscous pressures to the temperature dependence of relaxation time is analyzed within one dimensional boost invariant expansion of quark gluon plasma (QGP).The dissipative corrections to the phase-space distribution functions upto first order in gradients are obtained from the Chapman-Enskog like iterative solution of effective Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation. Thermal dilepton and photon production rates for QGP are calculated by employing this viscous modified distribution function. Yields of these particles are quantified for the longitudinal expansion of QGP with different temperature dependent relaxation times. Our analysis employing this second order hydrodynamic model indicates that the spectra of dileptons and photons gets enhanced by both bulk and shear viscosities and is well behaved. Also, these particle yields are found to be sensitive to relaxation time. Further, we do a comparison of these particle spectra with a standard hydrodynamic formulation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that thermal dilepton and photon yields from a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) are enhanced by both shear and bulk viscosities when using a second-order hydrodynamic model based on an effective kinetic theory, but this enhancement is sensitive to the temperature dependence of the relaxation time and is only valid for relaxation times that do not cause unphysical reheating or cavitation.

## Method

The authors employed the recently developed second-order dissipative hydrodynamic formulation within the effective fugacity quasiparticle model (EQPM) for hot QCD medium. The non-equilibrium corrections to the phase-space distribution functions up to first order in gradients were obtained from a Chapman-Enskog like iterative solution of the effective Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation. The evolution equations for shear stress tensor and bulk viscous pressure were solved under one-dimensional boost-invariant Bjorken flow with different temperature-dependent relaxation times, using (2+1) flavor lattice QCD equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Initial conditions: proper time τ₀ = 0.5 fm/c and temperature T₀ = 0.31 GeV, with initial viscous contributions π(τ₀) = Π(τ₀) = 0 GeV/fm³
- Shear viscosity to entropy ratio η/s = 1/4π (the Kovtun-Son-Starinets bound)
- Relaxation time forms considered: τ_R = 2(η/s)/T, 1.5(η/s)/T, (η/s)/T, and a constant τ_R = 0.15 fm/c
- For τ_R = 3(η/s)/T, the pressure anisotropy P_L/P_T becomes negative causing cavitation within approximately 1 fm/c
- Reheating (unphysical temperature increase) occurs for τ_R > 8(η/s)/T and for constant relaxation times greater than 0.5 fm/c, with T'[τ] crossing zero around τ = 2 fm/c for τ_R = 8(η/s)/T and 0.5 fm/c

## Context

This work extends previous studies of thermal particle production from heavy ion collisions by implementing a causal second-order hydrodynamic framework within the EQPM description of QGP, whereas earlier EQPM-based studies (Chandra, 2015) used ideal hydrodynamics. The analysis builds on the second-order viscous hydrodynamics developed by Bhadury (2020) within EQPM, and compares results with the standard hydrodynamic formulation of Muronga (2003). The study enables future investigations of electromagnetic signals from QGP using more realistic hydrodynamic evolution that properly accounts for temperature-dependent relaxation times and avoids unphysical cavitation or reheating.
