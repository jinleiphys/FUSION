# Collective Shape-Phases of Interacting Fermion Systems

> A microscopic theory is presented for identifying shape-phase structures and transitions in interacting fermion systems. The method provides a microscopic description for collective shape-phases, and reveals detailed dependence of such shape-phases on microscopic interaction strengths. The theory is generally applicable to fermion systems such as nuclei, quarks, and in particular trapped cold atoms, where shape-phases may be observed and investigated in a controlled manner.

## Key claim
The paper presents a microscopic mean-field theory for identifying shape-phase structures and transitions in interacting fermion systems, claiming that the method recovers the rich structures of dynamical symmetries previously accounted for by the interacting boson model (IBM). The hedge is that the calculations use a specific numerical example (nuclei with A=130) and a Dyson-type boson mapping with SD pair truncation, so the general correspondence between interaction strengths and shape-phases is asserted to hold for characteristic features but may shift with different configuration parameters.

## Method
The authors start from a fermion mean-field Hamiltonian containing monopole-pairing, quadrupole-pairing, and quadrupole-quadrupole (QQ) interaction terms, then apply a Dyson-type boson mapping with SD pair truncation (keeping only J=0 and J=2 pairs) to obtain an IBM-form Hamiltonian. They diagonalize this Hamiltonian in U(5)-symmetric bases for a nuclear system with A=130, using five active single-particle orbits in the 50-82 shell with total nucleon pairs N=10. They compute energy ratios (R42, R62, R02, R22) and B(E2) transition ratios (B42, B64, B02, B22) as functions of the interaction strengths g0, g2, and k, comparing them to the known values for the U(5), SU(3), and O(6) dynamical symmetries of IBM-1.

## Key numbers
- Critical monopole-pairing strength g0 ≈ 0.12 (with g2 = k = 0) separates spherical U(5) phase (g0 > 0.12) from axially deformed phase; this agrees with empirical value g0 ~ 20/A for A=130.
- For QQ interaction alone (g0 = g2 = 0), k ≈ 0.10 yields an O(6) phase with R42 ≈ 2.5, R62 ≈ 4.5, R02 ≈ 4.5, R22 ≈ 2.5, B42 ≈ 1.38, B64 ≈ 1.52, B02 ≈ 0, and B22 ≈ 1.38.
- For QQ interaction alone, k ≥ 0.20 yields SU(3) symmetry (axially prolate rotor), while k ≈ 0.0 yields overline{SU(3)} symmetry (axially oblate rotor).
- For quadrupole-pairing with g0 = 0.15 and k = 0, g2 in [0, 0.04] gives U(5) vibration phase, g2 in [0.07, 0.10] gives SU(3) axially prolate rotation phase, and g2 > 0.10 suppresses collective motion.

## Context
This work extends the mean-field description of nuclear shape-phase transitions beyond earlier studies that used only monopole-pairing and QQ interactions (Rowe (1998), Luo (2006)) or monopole-pairing and quadrupole-pairing (Ginocchio (2005)), by incorporating all three interaction terms in a unified microscopic framework. The method recovers the dynamical symmetries of the interacting boson model (IBM) as developed by Iachello (1987) and provides a microscopic basis for the Casten triangle of nuclear shapes. The theory enables application to trapped cold atomic fermion systems where interaction strengths can be tuned, potentially explaining the pairing-induced deformations observed in experiments by Zwierlein (2006) and Hulet (2006).
