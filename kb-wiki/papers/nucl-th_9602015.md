# Application of a Two-Parameter Quantum Algebra to Rotational Spectroscopy of Nuclei

> A two-parameter quantum algebra $U_{qp}({\rm u}_2)$ is briefly investigated in this paper. The basic ingredients of a model based on the $U_{qp}({\rm u}_2)$ symmetry, the $qp$-rotator model, are presented in detail. Some general tendencies arising from the application of this model to the description of rotational bands of various atomic nuclei are summarized.

## Key claim

The two-parameter quantum algebra \(U_{qp}({\rm u}_2)\) provides a better description of rotational bands in both superdeformed and deformed nuclei than the one-parameter \(U_q({\rm su}_2)\)-based \(q\)-rotator models or the \(\kappa\)-Poincaré rotator model, but the improvement depends on the mass region and the choice of the domain for the deformation parameters \(q\) and \(p\).

## Method

The authors constructed a \(qp\)-rotator model by taking the Hamiltonian as the Casimir operator of \(U_{qp}({\rm u}_2)\) and restricting physical states to the irreducible representation \((I,I)\), where \(I\) is the nuclear angular momentum. They fitted the model's three parameters (effective moment of inertia \({\cal J}\), and two deformation parameters \(a\) and \(b\)) to experimental \(\gamma\)-transition energies for 20 superdeformed bands in the \(A \approx 130\), 150, and 190 mass regions and for 29 deformed bands in the rare earth and actinide regions, minimizing a standard deviation \(\chi\). They then compared the theoretical dynamical moment of inertia \({\cal J}_{\rm th}^{(2)}\) derived from the fit to experimental values, and also compared the results to those from the \(q\)-rotator models of Iwao (1990) and Raychev (1990) and the \(\kappa\)-Poincaré rotator model of Celeghini (1991).

## Key numbers

* For the \(A \approx 190\) superdeformed bands, the fitted parameter \(b\) is of order \(10^{-3}\) and the parameter \(a\) is of order \(10^{-4}\).
* For the rare earth and actinide deformed bands, the fitted parameter \(b\) is of order \(10^{-2}\) and the parameter \(a\) is of order \(10^{-3}\).
* The parameter \(a\) is reported to be about 10 times lower than the parameter \(b\) across the fitted bands.
* The model was tested on 20 superdeformed bands (in the \(A \approx 130\), 150, and 190 mass regions) and 29 deformed bands (in the rare earth and actinide regions).

## Context

This work extends the one-parameter \(q\)-rotator models of Iwao (1990) and Raychev (1990), which were based on \(U_q({\rm su}_2)\), by introducing a second deformation parameter \(p\) via the algebra \(U_{qp}({\rm u}_2)\). It also provides a comparison with the \(\kappa\)-Poincaré rotator model of Celeghini (1991). The results enable a more flexible phenomenological description of nuclear softness, particularly for heavy deformed and superdeformed nuclei, and suggest that the second parameter \(a\) acts as a correction to the softness described by \(b\).
