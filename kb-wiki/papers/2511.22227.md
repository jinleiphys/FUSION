# Study of $0^+$ and $8^-$ states in even-even $^{250-260}$No isotopes

> Low-lying $K^π=0^+$ and isomeric $8^-$ states in even-even isotopes $^{250-260}$No are explored within the Quasiparticle Random-Phase Approximation (QRPA) method with Skyrme parametrization SLy4. The deformations, single-particle (s-p) spectra and pairing in the isotopes are inspected. The calculations predict a pronounced minimum in the neutron pairing at $A$=252, 254, which significantly affects the properties of $0^+$ and $8^-$ states and leads to a correlation of their spectra. It is shown that $8^-$ isomers are basically low-energy two-quasiparticle (2qp) states. The appearance or absence of these isomers in $^{250-260}$No is explained as a combined effect of the s-p spectra and pairing. The collective $0^+$ states are predicted in all the isotopes as the lowest multipole non-rotational excitations. These states are interpreted as a superposition of pairing vibrations and $β$-vibrations. The results are in a reasonable agreement with available experimental data for $^{252,254}$No.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed correlation between the energies of low-lying \(0^+\) and isomeric \(8^-\) states in \(^{250-260}\)No isotopes arises from a pronounced minimum in neutron pairing at \(A=252,254\), and that the recently observed \(0^+\) state in \(^{254}\)No at 0.888 MeV is best explained as a mixture of pairing and \(\beta\)-vibrations rather than as shape coexistence between normal and superdeformed configurations.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Quasiparticle Random-Phase Approximation (QRPA) calculations using the Skyrme force parametrization SLy4, with single-particle spectra and pairing treated via the BCS approach using the code SKYAX on a two-dimensional grid in cylindrical coordinates. Equilibrium deformations were obtained by energy minimization, and the properties of \(0^+\) and \(8^-\) states were computed for the even-even isotopes \(^{250-260}\)No, comparing with available experimental data for \(^{252,254}\)No.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation \(\beta_2 \approx 0.30\) for all isotopes \(^{250-260}\)No, with hexadecapole deformation \(\beta_4\) decreasing from 0.096 (\(^{250}\)No) to 0.014 (\(^{260}\)No).
- Neutron pairing gap \(\Delta_n\) shows a minimum: 0.32 MeV (\(^{252}\)No) and 0.28 MeV (\(^{254}\)No), compared to 0.51 MeV (\(^{250}\)No) and 0.46 MeV (\(^{260}\)No).
- Calculated \(8^-\) isomer energy in \(^{252}\)No: 1.257 MeV (experimental: 1.254 MeV); in \(^{254}\)No: 1.673 MeV (experimental: 1.295 MeV).
- Calculated lowest \(0^+\) state energy in \(^{254}\)No: 0.616 MeV (experimental: 0.888 MeV); in \(^{252}\)No: 0.740 MeV.
- Reduced E0 transition probability \(\rho^2(E0)\) for the lowest \(0^+\) state: 0.26 \(\times 10^{-3}\) in both \(^{252}\)No and \(^{254}\)No, compared to 5.07 \(\times 10^{-3}\) in \(^{250}\)No and 5.92 \(\times 10^{-3}\) in \(^{256}\)No.

## Context

This work extends previous systematic studies of ground-state properties and low-energy spectra in nobelium isotopes by the same group (Mardyban and Nesterenko, 2024) using the SLy4 force, and builds on experimental measurements of \(8^-\) and \(0^+\) excitations in \(^{254}\)No reported by Tezuka (2022), Forge (2023), and Wahid (2023). The results contradict the interpretation of the 0.888 MeV \(0^+\) state in \(^{254}\)No as shape coexistence between normal and superdeformed shapes (Forge, 2023; Delaroche, 2006), instead supporting a pairing-plus-beta-vibration mechanism. The calculations also address the disputed assignment of the \(8^-\) isomer in \(^{254}\)No, favoring the neutron configuration \(nn[734\uparrow, 613\uparrow]\) over the proton configuration proposed by Clark (2010), Wahid (2023), and Jolos (2023), and suggest that measurement of the gyromagnetic factor \(g_K\) could resolve the assignment.
