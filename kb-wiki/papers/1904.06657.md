# $α$-cluster structure above double-shell closures and $α$-decay of $^{104}$Te

> An analysis of the $α$ + core properties of $^{104}$Te along with a global discussion on the $α$-cluster structure above the double-shell closures is presented from the viewpoint of the local potential model. The $α$ + core interaction is described by a nuclear potential of (1 + Gaussian)$\times$(W.S. + W.S.$^3$) shape with two free parameters, which has been successfully tested in nuclei of different mass regions. The model produces $Q_α$ values and $α$-decay half-lives for $^{104}$Te in agreement with the 2018 experimental data of Auranen et al. in the energy range $5.13 \; \mathrm{MeV} < Q_α < 5.3 \; \mathrm{MeV}$ using an $α$ preformation factor $P_α = 1$. The comparison of the calculated reduced $α$-widths for the ground state bands of $^{104}$Te, $^{94}$Mo and $^{212}$Po indicates that $^{104}$Te has an $α$-cluster degree significantly higher than $^{94}$Mo and much higher than $^{212}$Po. These results point to $^{104}$Te as a preferential nucleus for $α$-clustering in the $N,Z = 50$ region and corroborate the statement on the superallowed $α$-decay in $^{104}$Te.

## Key claim

The (1 + Gaussian)x(W.S. + W.S.^3) nuclear potential model produces Q_alpha values and alpha-decay half-lives for 104Te that agree with the 2018 experimental data of Auranen et al. in the energy range 5.13 MeV < Q_alpha < 5.3 MeV, but only when using an alpha preformation factor P_alpha = 1, which is the maximum possible value and indicates superallowed alpha-decay.

## Method

The authors employed a local potential model with a nuclear potential of (1 + Gaussian)x(W.S. + W.S.^3) shape, containing two free parameters (R and sigma) fitted to reproduce the experimental 0+ and 4+ members of the ground state band. The model was applied to the alpha + core systems of 20Ne, 44Ti, 94Mo, 212Po, and 104Te, with the alpha + 100Sn system for 104Te using the Wildermuth condition quantum number G = 16. Reduced alpha-widths were calculated using R-matrix theory, and alpha-decay half-lives were obtained from the semi-classical approximation.

## Key numbers

- For 104Te, the calculated alpha-decay half-life is 8.866 ns for E(0+) = 5.22 MeV, compatible with the experimental measurement of T_1/2^exp < 18 ns from Auranen et al. (2018)
- The experimental Q_alpha for 104Te is 5.1(2) MeV, and the model reproduces the energy range 5.13 MeV < Q_alpha < 5.3 MeV
- The ratio of reduced alpha-widths gamma_alpha^2(104Te)/gamma_alpha^2(212Po) ranges from approximately 17.7 to 20.5 across the ground state band states 0+ through 10+
- The ratio gamma_alpha^2(104Te)/gamma_alpha^2(94Mo) varies around 1.5 across the same states
- For 104Te, the reduced alpha-width for the 0+ state is 1009.6 eV, and the rms intercluster separation is 5.249 fm
- The radial parameter R for 104Te is 5.713 fm, consistent with the linear fit R = 1.224 A_T^(1/3) fm

## Context

This work extends the alpha-cluster model studies of Buck, Merchant, and Perez (1995) on 20Ne, 44Ti, 94Mo, and 212Po by improving the nuclear potential to correctly reproduce the 0+ bandheads. The results corroborate the superallowed alpha-decay interpretation for 104Te reported by Auranen et al. (2018) and show that 104Te has a higher degree of alpha-clustering than 94Mo, which was previously regarded as a preferential nucleus for alpha-clustering in the N,Z = 50 region. The model enables systematic application to other nuclei near double-shell closures and is being extended to nuclei farther from double-shell closures as demonstrated in Souza and Miyake (2017) for 46Cr and 54Cr.
