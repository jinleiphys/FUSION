# Energy Loss of a Heavy Fermion in an Anisotropic QED Plasma

> We compute the leading-order collisional energy loss of a heavy fermion propagating in a QED plasma with an electron distribution function which is anisotropic in momentum space. We show that in the presence of such anisotropies there can be a significant directional dependence of the heavy fermion energy loss with the effect being quite large for highly-relativistic velocities. We also repeat the analysis of the isotropic case more carefully and show that the final result depends on the intermediate scale used to separate hard and soft contributions to the energy loss. We then show that the canonical isotropic result is obtained in the weak-coupling limit. For intermediate-coupling we use the residual scale dependence as a measure of our theoretical uncertainty. We also discuss complications which could arise due to the presence of unstable soft photonic modes and demonstrate that the calculation of the energy loss is safe.

## Key claim

The leading-order collisional energy loss of a heavy fermion in an anisotropic QED plasma acquires a significant directional dependence, with the effect being large for highly-relativistic velocities; however, the final result depends on the intermediate scale \(q^*\) used to separate hard and soft contributions, and the canonical isotropic result is recovered only in the weak-coupling limit.

## Method

The authors compute the soft contribution to the energy loss using classical field theory, expressing the induced electric field in terms of the photon propagator decomposed into a tensor basis that depends on a fixed anisotropy vector \(\mathbf{n}\). The hard contribution is obtained by summing tree-level diagrams with an infrared cutoff \(q^*\) on the momentum transfer. The soft and hard parts are combined, and the arbitrary separation scale \(q^*\) is fixed using the principle of minimal sensitivity, with the residual scale dependence taken as a measure of theoretical uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- The Debye mass is \(m_D^2 = e^2 T^2 / 3\) for an isotropic Fermi-Dirac distribution \(n_F(p) = (\exp(p/T)+1)^{-1}\).
- In the weak-anisotropy limit, the structure function \(\alpha\) at small \(\omega\) behaves as \(\alpha = m_D^2 \left[ -\frac{\xi}{6}(1+\cos 2\theta_n) - \frac{i\pi}{4} \hat{\omega} \left(1 + \frac{3\xi}{4}(1+\cos 2\theta_n)\right) \right]\), where \(\theta_n\) is the angle between the propagation direction and the anisotropy vector \(\mathbf{n}\).
- The coefficient \(D\) (controlling dynamical shielding) is non-vanishing for all tested values of \(\xi\) and \(\theta_n\), including \(\xi = 100\) and \(\theta_n = 1.5\) radians.

## Context

This work extends the isotropic heavy fermion energy loss calculation of Braaten and Thoma (1991) to anisotropic plasmas, motivated by the early-stage momentum-space anisotropy in relativistic heavy-ion collisions described by the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario of Baier et al. (2001). The authors use the self-energy structure functions derived in their previous work (Romatschke and Strickland, 2003) and show that the calculation remains safe despite the presence of unstable soft photonic modes, which are dynamically shielded. This provides a testing ground for techniques that can be applied to QCD to predict directional dependence of collisional energy loss in an anisotropic quark-gluon plasma.
