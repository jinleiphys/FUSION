# Isospin Dynamics in Peripheral Heavy Ion Collisions at Fermi Energies

> We present a detailed study of isospin dynamics in peripheral collisions at Fermi energies. We consider symmetric and mixed collisions of (124,112)Sn isotopes at 35 and 50 AMeV to study the isospin transport between the different reaction components (residues, gas and possibly intermediate mass fragments) and, in particular, the charge equilibration in the mixed system. We evaluate the effects of drift terms due to asymmetry and density gradients, which are directly related to the poorly known value and slope of the symmetry energy below saturation density. We verify the importance of an isoscalar momentum dependence of the mean field, which is found to influence the isospin transport since it changes the reaction times. We finally suggest two observables particularly sensitive to the isovector part of the nuclear equation-of-state: the correlation between isospin equilibration and kinetic energy loss for binary events, and the isospin content of the produced mid-rapidity fragments for neck fragmentation events.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that isospin transport in peripheral heavy-ion collisions at Fermi energies is sensitive to the poorly known density dependence of the symmetry energy below saturation, but the authors hedge that the isoscalar momentum dependence of the mean field also influences the transport by changing reaction times, so that clean extraction of isovector information requires selecting observables that are not too sensitive to this isoscalar effect.

## Method

The authors performed Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) transport simulations of symmetric and mixed collisions of \(^{124,112}\)Sn isotopes at 35 and 50 AMeV, using momentum-dependent (MD) and momentum-independent (MI) effective interactions with two different density dependences of the symmetry energy (asy-soft and asy-stiff). They analyzed the isospin content of the projectile-like and target-like residues, the gas phase, and intermediate mass fragments (IMFs) from the neck region, using the imbalance ratio to quantify isospin equilibration.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies: 35 AMeV and 50 AMeV for \(^{124,112}\)Sn + \(^{124,112}\)Sn collisions.
- Impact parameters studied: 6 fm, 8 fm, and 10 fm for both beam energies.
- Freeze-out times for MD interactions at 50 AMeV and b=6 fm: 140 fm/c; for MI interactions at same conditions: 150 fm/c.
- Freeze-out times for MD interactions at 35 AMeV and b=6 fm: 180 fm/c; for MI interactions at same conditions: 200 fm/c.
- Compressibility modulus of symmetric nuclear matter used: \(K_{NM}(\rho_0) = 215\) MeV.
- Isoscalar nucleon effective mass used: \(m^*/m = 0.67\).
- Density cut for liquid/gas separation: \(\rho > \rho_{cut}\) with \(\rho_{cut}\) between \(\rho_0/6\) and \(\rho_0/10\).
- Cutoff value for nucleon-nucleon cross sections: \(\sigma_{cut} = 50\) mb.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of isospin transport in heavy-ion collisions (Tsang 1992, Shi 2004) by systematically comparing momentum-dependent and momentum-independent interactions and by focusing on peripheral collisions at Fermi energies. The authors build on their previous work (Colonna 1998, Baran 2005) to propose two new observables sensitive to the isovector equation of state: the correlation between isospin equilibration and kinetic energy loss in binary events, and the isospin content of mid-rapidity fragments in neck fragmentation events. This enables future experiments with radioactive beams to constrain the symmetry energy below saturation density.
