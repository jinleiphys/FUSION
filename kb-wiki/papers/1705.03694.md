# Triangle singularities in $B^-\rightarrow K^-ֿ€^-D_{s0}^+$ and $B^-\rightarrow K^-ֿ€^-D_{s1}^+$

> We study the appearance of structures in the decay of the $B^-$ into $K^- ֿ€^- D_{s0}^+(2317)$ and $K^- ֿ€^- D_{s1}^+(2460)$ final states by forming invariant mass distributions of $ֿ€^- D_{s0}^+$ and $ֿ€^- D_{s1}^+$ pairs, respectively. The structure in the distribution is associated to the kinematical triangle singularity that appears when the $B^- \to K^- K^{*\,0} D^0$ ($B^- \to K^- K^{*\,0} D^{*\,0}$) decay process is followed by the decay of the $K^{*\,0}$ into $ֿ€^- K^+$ and the subsequent rescattering of the $K^+ D^0$ ($K^+ D^{*\,0}$) pair forming the $D_{s0}^+(2317)$ ($D_{s1}^+(2460)$) resonance. We find this type of non-resonant peaks at 2850 MeV in the invariant mass of $ֿ€^- D_{s0}$ pairs from $B^- \to K^- ֿ€^- D_{s0}^+(2317)$ decays and around 3000 MeV in the invariant mass of $ֿ€^- D_{s1}^+$ pairs from $B^- \to K^- ֿ€^- D_{s1}^+(2460)$ decays. By employing the measured branching ratios of the $B^- \to K^- K^{*\,0} D^0$ and $B^- \to K^- K^{*\,0} D^{*\,0}$ decays, we predict the branching ratios for the processes $B^-$ into $K^- ֿ€^-D_{s0}^+(2317)$ and $K^- ֿ€^- D_{s1}^+(2460)$, in the vicinity of the triangle singularity peak, to be about $8\times10^{-6}$ and $1\times 10^{-6}$, respectively. The observation of this reaction would also give extra support to the molecular picture of the $D_{s0}^+(2317)$ and $D_{s1}^+(2460)$.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that non-resonant peaks in the invariant mass distributions of $\pi^- D_{s0}^+$ and $\pi^- D_{s1}^+$ pairs from $B^-$ decays arise from kinematical triangle singularities, not from new resonances; the observation of these peaks would support the molecular (DK and D*K) picture of the $D_{s0}^+(2317)$ and $D_{s1}^+(2460)$ states, but the prediction depends on using measured branching ratios of the intermediate $B^- \to K^- K^{*0} D^0$ and $B^- \to K^- K^{*0} D^{*0}$ decays.

## Method

The authors evaluate triangle Feynman diagrams where $B^-$ decays to $K^- K^{*0} D^0$ (or $K^- K^{*0} D^{*0}$), the $K^{*0}$ decays to $\pi^- K^+$, and the $K^+ D^0$ (or $K^+ D^{*0}$) pair rescatters to form the $D_{s0}^+(2317)$ (or $D_{s1}^+(2460)$) resonance. They compute the loop amplitude using vertices from chiral Lagrangians and a chiral unitary approach for the $D_{s0}^+$ and $D_{s1}^+$ couplings, normalizing the overall strength to the experimentally measured branching ratios of the primary $B^-$ decays.

## Key numbers

- The triangle singularity peak in the $\pi^- D_{s0}^+$ invariant mass from $B^- \to K^- \pi^- D_{s0}^+(2317)$ decays is found at 2850 MeV.
- The triangle singularity peak in the $\pi^- D_{s1}^+$ invariant mass from $B^- \to K^- \pi^- D_{s1}^+(2460)$ decays is found around 3000 MeV.
- The predicted branching ratio for $B^- \to K^- \pi^- D_{s0}^+(2317)$ in the vicinity of the triangle singularity peak is about $8 \times 10^{-6}$.
- The predicted branching ratio for $B^- \to K^- \pi^- D_{s1}^+(2460)$ in the vicinity of the triangle singularity peak is about $1 \times 10^{-6}$.
- The input branching ratio for $B^- \to K^- K^{*0} D^0$ is $(7.5 \pm 1.7) \times 10^{-4}$ (from Belle, as listed in PDG).
- The input branching ratio for $B^- \to K^- K^{*0} D^{*0}$ is $(1.5 \pm 0.4) \times 10^{-3}$ (from Belle, as listed in PDG).
- The coupling constant $g_{D_{s0},DK}$ used for the $D_{s0}^+$ to $DK$ channel is 10.21 GeV (from Gamermann (2006)).
- The coupling constant $g_{D_{s1},D^*K}$ used for the $D_{s1}^+$ to $D^*K$ channel is 9.82 GeV (from a chiral unitary approach).

## Context

This work extends the study of triangle singularities in heavy-flavor decays, following similar analyses of the $\eta(1405)$ decays by Wu (2011) and Aceti (2012), the COMPASS $a_1(1420)$ signal by Liu (2015) and Ketzer (2015), and the $B_c \to B_s \pi \pi$ decay by Liu (2017). It predicts observable kinematic structures in $B^-$ decays that can be tested at LHCb or Belle II, and it provides a way to discriminate between a pure triangle-singularity origin and a genuine resonance interpretation, while also offering indirect support for the molecular nature of the $D_{s0}^+(2317)$ and $D_{s1}^+(2460)$ states.
