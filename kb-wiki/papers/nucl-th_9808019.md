# D' Production in Heavy Ion Collisions

> The production of d' dibaryons in heavy ion collisions due to the elementary process NN -> d' + pion is considered. The cross section NN -> d' + pion is estimated using the vacuum d' width = 0.5 MeV extracted from data on the double charge exchange reactions on nuclei. The d' production rate per single collision of heavy ions is estimated at an incident beam energy of 1 A GeV within the framework of the Quantum Molecular Dynamics transport model. We suggest to analyse the invariant mass spectrum of the NN + pion system in order to search for an abundance of events with the invariant mass of the d' dibaryon. The d' peak is found to exceed the statistical fluctuations of the background at a level of 6 standard deviations for 2 10^5 A central collisions of heavy ions with the atomic number A.

## Key claim

The production of d' dibaryons in heavy ion collisions via the elementary process NN → d'π is estimated to be detectable as a 6σ peak in the invariant mass spectrum of the NNπ system, but only if at least 2 × 10⁵ · A central collisions are analyzed, where A is the atomic number of the colliding nuclei.

## Method

The cross section for NN → d'π was calculated using the vacuum d' width Γ_d' ≈ 0.5 MeV extracted from double charge exchange reactions on nuclei, with a form factor introduced to suppress the vertex at high NN energies (cut-off mass m_FF ≈ 240 MeV). The d' production rate in heavy ion collisions was then simulated perturbatively within the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) transport model for central collisions of ²⁰Ne, ⁵⁹Ni, ¹⁰⁸Ag, and ¹⁹⁷Au at a beam energy of 1 A·GeV, treating d' production as a perturbation to the reaction dynamics.

## Key numbers

- d' mass M_d' = 2063 MeV, extracted from double charge exchange reaction data on nuclei
- Vacuum d' width Γ_d' ≈ 0.5 MeV, extracted from double charge exchange reaction data
- Cross section σ_pp→d'π⁺ = 300 (k/p) / (1 + T²/m_FF²) μb, with m_FF ≈ 240 MeV; at T ≈ 730 MeV and k/p ≈ 1/10, σ ≈ 30 μb
- Ratio of d' production cross section to total NN cross section: ξ ≈ 2 × 10⁻⁴ d' per single NN collision
- d' production rate per single AA collision from QMD simulations: n_d' ≈ 10⁻⁴ · A
- Number of background ppπ⁻ events per 1 MeV bin around the d' mass for ¹⁹⁷Au + ¹⁹⁷Au collisions: ΔN_t/N_C = 55 (from QMD simulations)
- Number of collisions needed for 6σ detection: N_C ≥ 2 × 10⁵ · A (general formula); for ¹⁹⁷Au + ¹⁹⁷Au with cuts: N_C ≥ 3.6 × 10⁷

## Context

This work extends the search for the narrow dibaryon d' (T=0, J^P=0⁻), previously established only in double charge exchange reactions on nuclei from ⁷Li to ⁵⁶Fe (W. 1990s) and preliminarily observed in pp → ppπ⁺π⁻ at CELSIUS (Brodowski 1990s), into the domain of heavy ion collisions. The study uses the QMD transport model (Aichelin 1991) to simulate d' production at SIS energies (GSI Darmstadt), enabling experimental searches that could confirm the d' existence and have implications for Bose condensate formation in dense nuclear matter (Faessler, Krivoruchenko, Martemyanov 1990s).
