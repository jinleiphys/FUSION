# Ab initio-driven nuclear energy density functional method. A proposal for safe/correlated/improvable parametrizations of the off-diagonal EDF kernels

> This programmatic paper lays down the possibility to reconcile the necessity to resum many-body correlations into the energy kernel with the fact that safe multi-reference energy density functional (EDF) calculations cannot be achieved whenever the Pauli principle is not strictly enforced, as is for example the case when many-body correlations are parametrized under the form of empirical density dependencies. Our proposal is to exploit a newly developed ab initio many-body formalism to guide the construction of safe, explicitly correlated and systematically improvable parametrizations of the {\it off-diagonal} energy and norm kernels that lie at the heart of the nuclear EDF method. The many-body formalism of interest relies on the concepts of symmetry breaking {\it and} restoration that have made the fortune of the nuclear EDF method and is, as such, amenable to this guidance. After elaborating on our proposal, we briefly outline the project we plan to execute in the years to come.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a newly developed ab initio many-body formalism based on symmetry breaking and restoration can guide the construction of safe, explicitly correlated, and systematically improvable parametrizations of the off-diagonal energy and norm kernels used in multi-reference energy density functional (EDF) calculations, overcoming the pathologies that arise when the Pauli principle is violated through empirical density dependencies.

## Method

The authors outline a program to use many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) based on a symmetry-breaking reference state, with exact and consistent symmetry restoration at any truncation order, to construct fully correlated off-diagonal norm and energy kernels. These ab initio-derived kernels are then used as a mathematical guide to build effective EDF parametrizations that incorporate missing correlations while avoiding the pathologies of current empirical functionals. The proposal focuses on the SU(2) symmetry group associated with rotational invariance, using Slater determinant reference states and second-order MBPT as a concrete example.

## Key numbers

- The volume of the SU(2) group is given as v_SU(2) = 16π², with Euler angle domains D_SU(2) = [0,4π] × [0,π] × [0,2π]
- The orthogonality relation for Wigner D-functions yields the factor 16π²/(2J+1) for the Kronecker delta normalization
- In state-of-the-art multi-reference calculations, the set of reference states may contain up to about 10⁹ different symmetry-breaking product states

## Context

This programmatic paper extends the earlier work of Duguet et al. (2015) on ab initio MBPT with symmetry breaking and restoration, and addresses the critical pathologies identified by Dobaczewski (2007) in multi-reference EDF calculations. The proposal contrasts with two existing approaches: a posteriori regularization of pathologies as pursued by Lacroix (2008), Bender (2008), and Duguet (2008), and the restriction to effective Hamiltonian-based kernels being developed by Sadoudi (2013), Dobaczewski (2012), and Lacroix (2014). The work enables a systematic path to incorporate many-body correlations into EDF kernels while maintaining safe multi-reference calculations, potentially bridging the gap between ab initio methods and the nuclear EDF approach.
