# Pion-pion scattering above resonances

> We model large angle intermediate energy pion-pion scattering by the pQCD two-gluon (2g) exchange contribution and discuss the onset of the dominance of the Glauber-Gribov-Landshoff (GGL) component. The pQCD 2g exchange contribution becomes substantial already at $|t|\sim$ 1 GeV^{2}, but the pQCD exchange dominance is deferred to $|t|\sim$ 3 GeV^{2} because of competing multiple soft rescattering effects. Based on the NN and $πN$ total cross section data and Regge factorization, we evaluate the dominant soft contribution to the $ππ$ total cross section and find the results consistent with the ones deduced earlier from the absorption model analysis of the $πN \to X N, X Δ$ data.

## Key claim

The pQCD two-gluon exchange contribution to large-angle pion-pion scattering becomes substantial at |t| ~ 1 GeV², but its dominance over competing multiple soft rescattering effects is deferred to |t| ~ 3 GeV², not at the lower momentum transfer where it first becomes appreciable.

## Method

The author computed the pQCD two-gluon exchange amplitude for elastic ππ scattering using both nonrelativistic and light-cone descriptions of the pion wave function, separating the impulse approximation and Glauber-Gribov-Landshoff (GGL) contributions. The soft contribution was evaluated via Regge factorization from NN and πN total cross section data, using single pomeron, f, and ρ reggeon exchanges with absorption corrections from double-scattering amplitudes. The total cross sections and angular distributions were then calculated by combining the soft and hard contributions for center-of-mass energies W = 2–5 GeV.

## Key numbers

- pQCD 2g exchange becomes substantial at |t| ~ 1 GeV², but dominance over soft contributions is deferred to |t| ~ 3 GeV²
- Regge residues from factorization: C_Pom = 8.56 mb, C_f = 13.39 mb, C_ρ = 16.38 mb
- Pomeron trajectory: α_Pom(0) = 1, α_Pom' = 0.25 GeV⁻²; subleading trajectories: α_R(0) = 0.5, α_R' = 0.9 GeV⁻²
- Angular distributions presented for W = 3, 4, 5 GeV with exponential vertex form factor B = 4 GeV⁻²
- At |t| ~ 4 GeV² the cross section begins to approximately scale with energy

## Context

This work extends the Regge factorization approach of Donnachie and Landshoff (1992) for NN and πN scattering to ππ scattering, and uses the absorption model analysis of πN → XN, XΔ data from Zieminska and Szczurek (1984) for comparison. The results enable predictions for ππ total cross sections and angular distributions in the intermediate energy region W = 2–5 GeV, where direct experimental data are unavailable, and clarify the transition between soft Regge-dominated and hard pQCD-dominated regimes.
