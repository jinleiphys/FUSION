# Non-perturbative model for the half-off-shell γN N vertex

> Form factors in the nucleon-photon vertex with one off-shell nucleon are calculated by dressing the vertex with pion loops up to infinite order. Cutting rules and dispersion relations are implemented in the model. Using the prescription of minimal substitution we construct a γπN N vertex and show that it has to be included in the model in order that the Ward-Takahashi identity for the γN N vertex be fulfilled. The vertex is to be applied in a coupled-channel K-matrix formalism for Compton scattering, pion photoproduction and pion scattering. The form factors show a pronounced cusp structure at the pion threshold. As an illustration of a consistent application of the model, we calculate the cross section of Compton scattering. To provide gauge invariance in Compton scattering, a four-point γγN N contact term is constructed using minimal substitution.

## Key claim

The half-off-shell electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon are calculated non-perturbatively by dressing the vertex with pion loops to infinite order, but the model only includes contributions from intermediate states with one nucleon and one pion, and the resulting form factors depend on the specific construction of the contact terms needed to satisfy the Ward-Takahashi identity.

## Method

The authors solve an integral equation for the half-off-shell gamma-N-N vertex using an iterative procedure, where at each step the imaginary parts of loop integrals are found via Cutkosky cutting rules and the real parts are reconstructed using dispersion relations. A four-point gamma-pi-N-N contact vertex is constructed via minimal substitution from the dressed pi-N-N vertex to ensure the Ward-Takahashi identity is satisfied, and a four-point gamma-gamma-N-N contact term is similarly built for Compton scattering. The dressed nucleon propagator and half-off-shell pi-N-N form factors from a previous consistent model (Kondratyuk and Scholten, 1998) are used as input.

## Key numbers

- The pion-nucleon coupling constant is taken as g = 13.02, from Korsch et al. (1995).
- The pion mass m_pi and nucleon mass m are used as the hadronic mass scales, with the pion propagator given by (k^2 - m_pi^2)^{-1}.
- The off-shell nucleon invariant mass squared p^2 must satisfy p^2 greater than or equal to (m + m_pi)^2 for the loop integrals to have non-zero imaginary parts.
- The bare anomalous magnetic moment kappa_B is adjusted to reproduce the physical anomalous magnetic moment of the nucleon at the on-shell point.

## Context

This work extends earlier perturbative dressing approaches by Naus and Koch (1987), Tiemeijer and Tjon (1990), and Bos and Koch (1991) by implementing a non-perturbative integral equation with dispersion relations, and it provides the off-shell vertices needed for the coupled-channel K-matrix formalism developed by Goudsmit et al. (1994) and Korsch et al. (1995) for Compton scattering, pion photoproduction, and pion scattering. The model enables consistent treatment of off-shell effects in these reactions while maintaining gauge invariance through the constructed contact terms, and it demonstrates that the cusp structure at the pion threshold appears in the form factors.
