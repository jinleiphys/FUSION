# Hard exclusive pion electroproduction at backward angles with CLAS

> We report on the first measurement of cross sections for exclusive deeply virtual pion electroproduction off the proton, $e p \to e^\prime n π^+$, above the resonance region at backward pion center-of-mass angles. The $φ^*_π$-dependent cross sections were measured, from which we extracted three combinations of structure functions of the proton. Our results are compatible with calculations based on nucleon-to-pion transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs) and shed new light on nucleon structure.

## Key claim

The first measurement of cross sections for exclusive deeply virtual pion electroproduction off the proton at backward pion center-of-mass angles shows reasonable agreement with calculations based on nucleon-to-pion transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs), but this agreement is not incontrovertible evidence for the validity of the factorized description since the TDA-based description and a phenomenological Regge-pole exchange model yield similar results.

## Method

The CLAS detector at Jefferson Lab was used with a 5.754 GeV electron beam to measure the $e p \to e^\prime n \pi^+$ reaction. Events were selected with $Q^2 > 1.7$ GeV$^2$, $W^2 > 4$ GeV$^2$, $\Delta_T^2 < 0.5$ GeV$^2$, and $\cos\theta^*_{\pi} < 0$ to isolate backward-angle pions. The $\varphi^*_{\pi}$-dependent cross sections were measured and fit to extract the structure functions $\sigma_U = \sigma_T + \epsilon \sigma_L$, $\sigma_{LT}$, and $\sigma_{TT}$.

## Key numbers

* Six $Q^2$ values were measured: 1.71, 2.05, 2.44, 2.92, 3.48, and 4.16 GeV$^2$, at average kinematics $\langle W \rangle = 2.2$ GeV and $\langle -u \rangle = 0.5$ GeV$^2$.
* The kinematic range covered $\xi$ from 0.1 to 0.45.
* The total systematic uncertainty was estimated to be 12%.
* The average $\chi^2$ per degree of freedom for the five lowest-$Q^2$ bin fits was approximately 2.6.
* The $\sigma_{TT}$ and $\sigma_{LT}$ structure functions are roughly equal in magnitude and about 50% of $\sigma_U$, implying an important contribution of the transverse amplitude.

## Context

This work provides the first experimental test of the nucleon-to-pion TDA formulation, extending the study of hard exclusive processes beyond the Generalized Parton Distribution (GPD) framework that describes forward-angle meson production. The same data set was previously analyzed by Park et al. (2013) to extract GPDs from forward-angle pions. The results are compared to the TDA model of Pire et al. (2011) and the effective hadronic description of Laget (2009), which reproduces earlier SLAC photoproduction data and HERMES electroproduction data. Future experiments at PANDA@GSI-FAIR, J-PARC, and JLab with polarized targets and separation of $\sigma_T$ and $\sigma_L$ are needed to confirm the validity of the TDA-based description.
