# Study of possible ωbound states in nuclei with the (γ,p) reaction

> We perform calculations for ωproduction in nuclei by means of the (γ,p) reaction for photon energies and proton angles suited to running and future experiments in present Laboratories. For some cases of possible ωoptical potentials we find clear peaks which could be observable provided a good resolution in the ωenergy is available. We also study the inclusive production of π^0 γin nuclei around the ωenergy and find a double hump structure for the energy spectra, with a peak around a π^0 γenergy of m_ω-100 MeV, which could easily be misidentified by a signal of a bound ωstate in nuclei, while it is due to a different scaling of the uncorrelated π^0 γproduction and ωproduction with subsequent π^0 γdecay.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that a double-hump structure in the $\pi^0\gamma$ energy spectrum from nuclei, with a peak around $m_{\omega}-100$ MeV, could be misidentified as a signal of a bound $\omega$ state in nuclei, but this structure actually arises from the different scaling of uncorrelated $\pi^0\gamma$ production and $\omega$ production with subsequent $\pi^0\gamma$ decay. Observable peaks from genuine $\omega$ bound states require an experimental energy resolution better than 20 MeV, and only appear for certain choices of the $\omega$-nucleus optical potential.

## Method

The authors used the Green function method to calculate formation cross sections for $\omega$-mesic states in the $(\gamma,p)$ reaction on $^{12}$C, evaluating spectra at photon energies of 1.2 and 2.0 GeV and proton angles of 0 and 10.5 degrees. They performed Monte Carlo simulations of the inclusive $A(\gamma,\omega\to\pi^0\gamma)X$ reaction on $^{12}$C, $^{40}$Ca, $^{92}$Nb, and $^{208}$Pb targets, incorporating $\omega$ propagation, in-medium absorption with a width of 100 MeV at normal nuclear density, quasielastic scattering, and final-state interactions of pions, with cuts matching the CBELSA/TAPS experiment at ELSA.

## Key numbers

- The recoilless condition for $\omega$ production at threshold and forward proton emission is satisfied at $E_\gamma \sim 2.7$ GeV.
- At $\theta_p^{\rm lab}=10.5$ degrees, the momentum transfer takes its minimum value at $E_\gamma \sim 1.2$ GeV.
- Elementary cross sections used: 5.0 $\mu$b/sr (0 deg.) and 8.0 $\mu$b/sr (10.5 deg.) at $E_\gamma=1.2$ GeV; 0.7 $\mu$b/sr for both angles at $E_\gamma=2.0$ GeV.
- Three $\omega$-nucleus optical potentials considered: $(V_0,W_0)=-(0,50)$ MeV, $-(100,50)$ MeV, and $-(156,29)$ MeV.
- The absorption width of the $\omega$ in the medium is taken as $\Gamma_{abs} \simeq 100$ MeV $\times \rho(r)/\rho_0$.
- The $\omega \to \pi^0\gamma$ decay width used is $\Gamma_{\pi^0\gamma}=0.76$ MeV.
- Experimental cuts applied: incident photon energy $1.5 < E_\gamma^{in} < 2.6$ GeV, proton polar angle $7^\circ < \theta_p < 14^\circ$, $\pi^0\gamma$ three-momentum $|\vec{p}_{\pi^0\gamma}| < 400$ MeV, photon energy $E_\gamma > 200$ MeV, and pion kinetic energy $T_{\pi^0} > 150$ MeV.
- An energy resolution better than 20 MeV is required to obtain experimental evidence of $\omega$ mesic nucleus formation.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of $\omega$ meson properties in nuclei, which have produced conflicting predictions ranging from 100-200 MeV attraction (Tsushima, Klingl) to no mass change (Mosel) to 50 MeV repulsion (Lutz). It directly addresses the CBELSA/TAPS experiment (Trnka, 2005) that claimed evidence for a 100 MeV decrease of the $\omega$ mass in the medium, and provides a cautionary interpretation of that data. The paper enables future experimental searches for $\omega$ bound states by identifying optimal kinematic conditions ($E_\gamma=2.0$ GeV for forward proton detection, $E_\gamma=1.2$ GeV for finite-angle detection) and the required experimental resolution, while warning against misinterpreting background-induced structures as bound-state signals.
