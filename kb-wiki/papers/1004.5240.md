# Isobar model analysis of pi0-eta photoproduction on protons

> Photoproduction of pi0-eta on protons in the energy range from threshold to 1.4 GeV is discussed. The data for representative angular distributions recently obtained at MAMI C are analyzed using an isobar model. The isobars considered are Delta(1232) and S11(1535) for pi0-p and eta-p states, respectively. In accordance with the results of earlier works the main features of the reaction are explained through the dominance of the D33 wave with a relatively small admixture of positive parity resonances. Comparison with recent experimental results for the photon beam asymmetry is carried out.

## Key claim

The reaction $\gamma p \to \pi^0\eta p$ in the energy range from threshold to 1.4 GeV is dominated by the $D_{33}$ partial wave, particularly the $D_{33}(1700)$ resonance, but the analysis requires a phenomenological rather than dynamical treatment of the $\pi S_{11}$ channel because the $\eta N$ scattering cross section does not exhibit a true resonance behavior due to the closeness of the $S_{11}(1535)$ mass to the $\eta N$ threshold.

## Method

The authors used an isobar model where the reaction amplitude is divided into Born and resonance parts, with the resonance part constructed as a sum of two terms corresponding to intermediate $\eta\Delta(1232)$ and $\pi S_{11}(1535)$ states. The angular distributions of pions in the $\pi p$ rest frame (canonical and helicity systems) and of $\eta$ mesons in the overall center-of-mass frame were analyzed, with the $D_{33}$ wave treated as dominant and other partial waves added successively. The adjustable parameters included the helicity amplitudes $A_{1/2}$ and $A_{3/2}$ for each resonance, the ratio of $\pi S_{11}$ to $\eta\Delta$ branching ratios, and the resonance masses (varied around PDG values), while total widths were fixed from the literature.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section reaches approximately $\sigma \approx 3.5\ \mu b$ at $E_\gamma = 1.4$ GeV, about 0.5 GeV above threshold.
- For the dominant $D_{33}(1700)$ resonance, the exponent $n_{1/2}$ in the energy dependence of the helicity amplitude $A_{1/2}^R(W)$ was set equal to 1, while $A_{3/2}^{D_{33}}(W)$ was fitted separately in each energy bin.
- The $\Delta(1232)$ isobar mass was taken as $M_\Delta = 1232$ MeV with $\Gamma_\Delta(M_\Delta) = 120$ MeV; the $S_{11}(1535)$ isobar mass was $M_{N^*} = 1535$ MeV with $\Gamma_{N^*}(M_{N^*}) = 150$ MeV and $\Gamma_{N^*\eta N}(M_{N^*}) = 45\%\ \Gamma_{N^*}(M_{N^*})$.
- The nominal thresholds for $\eta\Delta$ and $\pi N^*$ channels are $E_\gamma = 1.22$ GeV and $E_\gamma = 1.01$ GeV, respectively, making the $\eta\Delta$ channel effectively opened about 200 MeV higher than $\pi N^*$.
- The ratio $r = \Gamma^{(\pi N^*)}_{D_{33}\to\pi\eta N} / \Gamma^{(\eta\Delta)}_{D_{33}\to\pi\eta N}$ was used to quantify the relative contribution of the $\pi N^*$ channel to the $D_{33}(1700)$ decay.

## Context

This work extends the isobar model analysis of $\gamma p \to \pi^0\eta p$ previously developed in Fix, Ostrick, and Tiator (2005) and applied to MAMI C data in Kashevarov et al. (2009). It contradicts the dynamical coupled-channel approach of Doring, Oset, and Strottman (2006) by using a phenomenological parametrization of the $R \to \pi N^*$ vertex rather than generating it through final-state interactions, finding that the $\pi S_{11}$ configuration is not as pronounced as predicted by that earlier model. The analysis enables extraction of the ratio of $\pi S_{11}$ to $\eta\Delta$ decay widths of the $D_{33}(1700)$ resonance from angular distributions rather than invariant mass spectra, and provides a framework for systematic study of the partial wave structure of $\pi^0\eta$ photoproduction.
