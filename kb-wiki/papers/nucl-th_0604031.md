# Low-Energy Thermal Photons from Meson-Meson Bremsstrahlung

> Within an effective hadronic model including electromagnetic interactions via a U$_{\rm em}$(1) gauge, we reinvestigate photon Bremsstrahlung from a hot hadronic gas as expected to be formed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies. We calculate photon emission from the reactions $ππ\toππγ$ and $πK \toπKγ$ by an explicit (numerical) evaluation of the multi-dimensional phase space integral. This, in particular, allows to avoid the commonly employed soft photon approximation (SPA), as well as to incorporate final-state thermal enhancement factors. % during the hadronic stage of the fireball. Both improvements are shown to result in an appreciable increase of the photon production rate over previous hadronic calculations. Upon convolution over a thermal fireball we find an improvement in the description of recent low transverse-momentum WA98 data at SPS. The influence of both Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal and in-medium effects on "$σ$" and $ρ$-meson exchanges are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

By explicitly evaluating the multi-dimensional phase-space integral for photon Bremsstrahlung from ππ → ππγ and πK → πKγ, the authors find that going beyond the soft photon approximation (SPA) and including final-state thermal enhancement factors increases the low-energy photon production rate by 60-100% over previous hadronic calculations, which helps improve agreement with WA98 data at low transverse momentum but still does not fully explain the measured excess.

## Method

The authors constructed a chiral effective Lagrangian with electromagnetic interactions implemented via Uem(1) gauging, then computed photon emission rates from elastic π-π and π-K scattering by numerically evaluating the full 8-dimensional phase-space integral without resorting to the soft photon approximation. They incorporated final-state Bose enhancement factors (1+f₁)(1+f₂) and meson-chemical potentials appropriate for SPS heavy-ion collisions, then convolved the rates over a thermal fireball model for central Pb-Pb collisions at 158 AGeV.

## Key numbers

- Total increase of soft photon emission over previous computations: 60-100% (the main result of the paper)
- At T=150 MeV and μπ≈40 MeV, relaxing the SPA increases the rate by about 25% at q₀=0.1 GeV and close to 40% at q₀≈0.4 GeV
- Implementing final-state Bose enhancement adds another ~30% to the rate
- π-π Bremsstrahlung exceeds previously dominant baryonic contributions by close to a factor of 2 at q₀=0.2 GeV, and is smaller by a factor of 2 at q₀=0.4 GeV
- πK Bremsstrahlung rate is at the ~20% level of the ππ rate
- Reducing temperature from T=0.18 GeV to 0.11 GeV decreases the ππ rate by a factor of ~10 at q₀=0.1 GeV and ~15 at q₀=0.4 GeV
- For τπcoll=6 fm/c at T=150 MeV, the LPM effect reduces the rate by no more than 15% even at q₀=100 MeV
- For τπcoll=2 fm/c at q₀=100 MeV, the LPM quenching factor becomes 0.4-0.25
- Dropping the σ-meson mass from 0.525 GeV to 0.45 GeV barely changes the thermal emission rate
- Reducing the ρ-meson mass from 0.77 GeV to 0.65 GeV increases low-energy photon production by ~25%

## Context

This work extends earlier hadronic photon calculations by Turbide et al. (2004) and Rapp (2005) that had previously shown agreement with WA98 data at higher momenta (qt≥1.5 GeV) but fell short of the low-momentum WA98 data (qt=0.1-0.3 GeV) extracted using HBT interferometry methods. The authors improve upon the soft photon approximation used in earlier Bremsstrahlung calculations by Haglin (2003) and Roy (1996), and extend the calculations to the strangeness sector (π-K scattering). The results enable more accurate modeling of low-energy thermal photon spectra from the hadronic phase of heavy-ion collisions, which is needed to disentangle hadronic from QGP contributions and to test whether the WA98 low-momentum excess can be fully explained by hadronic sources.
