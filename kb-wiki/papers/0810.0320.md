# Elastic and total reaction cross sections of oxygen isotopes in Glauber theory

> We systematically calculate the total reaction cross sections of oxygen isotopes, $^{15-24}$O, on a $^{12}$C target at high energies using the Glauber theory. The oxygen isotopes are described with Slater determinants generated from a phenomenological mean-field potential. The agreement between theory and experiment is generally good, but a sharp increase of the reaction cross sections from ^{21}O to ^{23}O remains unresolved. To examine the sensitivity of the diffraction pattern of elastic scattering to the nuclear surface, we study the differential elastic-scattering cross sections of proton-^{20,21,23}O at the incident energy of 300 MeV by calculating the full Glauber amplitude.

## Key claim

The authors systematically calculate total reaction cross sections of oxygen isotopes (¹⁵–²⁴O) on a ¹²C target using Glauber theory with Slater-determinant densities from a mean-field potential, finding generally good agreement with experiment except that the sharp increase of reaction cross sections from ²¹O to ²³O remains unresolved.

## Method

The authors construct Slater-determinant wave functions for oxygen isotopes using a phenomenological mean-field potential with central and spin-orbit terms, fitting the potential strength to reproduce either the last-nucleon separation energy (Sₙ model) or the average separation energy for nucleons in the last orbit (⟨Sₙ⟩ model). They calculate total reaction cross sections for oxygen isotopes on a ¹²C target at around 1 GeV per nucleon using the NTG (New Toshio-Glauber) approximation, and compute differential elastic-scattering cross sections for proton-²⁰,²¹,²³O at 300 MeV using both the full Glauber amplitude and the optical-limit approximation (OLA).

## Key numbers

- Total reaction cross section of ¹⁶O on ¹²C at 1.0 GeV: calculated 1003 mb, experimental interaction cross section 982±6 mb
- Total reaction cross section of ²⁰O on ¹²C at 1.0 GeV: calculated 1135 mb, experimental 1078±10 mb
- Total reaction cross section of ²¹O on ¹²C at 1.0 GeV: calculated 1173 mb, experimental 1098±11 mb
- Total reaction cross section of ²³O on ¹²C at 1.0 GeV: calculated 1267 mb, experimental 1308±16 mb
- Matter radius of ²³O in ⟨Sₙ⟩ model: 3.05 fm; adjusted to fit interaction cross section: 3.13 fm and 3.25 fm
- First peak angle for p-²³O elastic scattering at 300 MeV: 21.5° (r_m=3.05 fm), 21.1° (r_m=3.13 fm), 20.5° (r_m=3.25 fm)
- First peak cross section for p-²³O at 300 MeV: 4.7 mb/sr (r_m=3.05 fm), 4.2 mb/sr (r_m=3.13 fm), 3.3 mb/sr (r_m=3.25 fm)
- First peak angle for p-²¹O at 300 MeV: 23.5° (r_m=2.64 fm), 22.3° (r_m=2.85 fm)
- Predicted first peak for p-²⁰O at 300 MeV: 24.8° with cross section 2.43 mb/sr

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Glauber-theory analysis of carbon isotopes (Horiuchi et al., 2007; Abu-Ibrahim et al., 2008) to oxygen isotopes, using the same mean-field approach. The failure to reproduce the sharp cross-section rise from ²¹O to ²³O echoes earlier findings by Kanungo et al. (2002) that no theoretical model could explain this anomaly, and the authors note that subsequent experiments (Sauvan et al., 2004; Cortina-Gil et al., 2004; Nociforo et al., 2005; Elekes et al., 2005; Schiller et al., 2005) have confirmed the ½⁺ ground-state spin of ²³O, leaving the cross-section enhancement as an open question. The elastic-scattering predictions for p-²⁰,²¹,²³O at 300 MeV are intended to guide upcoming experiments at RIKEN and NIRS, where the sensitivity of the diffraction pattern to nuclear surface diffuseness may help resolve the radius discrepancy.
