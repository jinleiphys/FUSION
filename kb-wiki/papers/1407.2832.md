# MCNP6 Simulation of Reactions of Interest to FRIB, Medical, and Space Applications

> The latest, production, version of the Los Alamos Monte Carlo N-Particle transport code MCNP6 has been used to simulate a variety of particle-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus reactions of academic and applied interest to the Facility for Rare Isotope Beams (FRIB), medical isotope production, space-radiation shielding, cosmic-ray propagation, and accelerator applications, including several reactions induced by radioactive isotopes, analyzing production of both stable and radioactive residual nuclei. Here, we discuss examples of validation and verification of MCNP6 compared to recent neutron spectra measured at the Heavy Ion Medical Accelerator in Chiba, Japan; to spectra of light fragments from several reactions measured recently at GANIL, France; INFN Laboratori Nazionali del Sud, Catania, Italy; COSY of the Julich Research Center, Germany; and to cross sections of products from several reactions measured lately at GSI, Darmstadt, Germany; ITEP, Moscow, Russia; LANSCE, LANL, Los Alamos, USA. As a rule, MCNP6 provides quite good predictions for most of the reactions we analyzed so far, allowing us to conclude that it can be used as a reliable and useful simulation tool for FRIB, medical, and space applications involving stable and radioactive isotopes.

## Key claim

MCNP6 provides "quite good predictions for most of the reactions we analyzed so far," but the paper hedges by noting that this conclusion applies only to the specific set of validation and verification comparisons performed, and that earlier versions of the code (MCNPX with an old LAQGSM version) had shown poor results in a prior benchmark report (Remec13).

## Method

The production version of MCNP6, which incorporates the cascade-exciton model (CEM03.03) and the Los Alamos version of the quark-gluon string model (LAQGSM03.03) event generators, was used to simulate particle-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus reactions. The simulations were compared against experimental neutron spectra from HIMAC (Chiba, Japan), light fragment spectra from GANIL (France), INFN Catania (Italy), and COSY (Julich, Germany), as well as cross sections from GSI (Darmstadt, Germany), ITEP (Moscow, Russia), and LANSCE (Los Alamos, USA). The study also included reactions induced by radioactive isotopes and analyzed production of both stable and radioactive residual nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Neutron spectra from 400 MeV/A $^{14}$N + $^{12}$C and $^{64}$Cu at HIMAC were compared with MCNP6 calculations (no numerical discrepancy values given in the text).
- Fragmentation of $^{64}$Ni at 140 MeV/nucleon on $^{9}$Be target was measured at MSU and compared with LAQGSM03.03 in MCNP6 (no numerical cross section values given in the text).
- Cross sections for $^{225}$Ac and other medical isotopes from 800 MeV p + $^{232}$Th were measured at LANL and compared with MCNP6 calculations (no numerical cross section values given in the text).
- Yields from interactions of $\pi^+$ with Fe at 120 GeV/c were compared with MCNP6 results (no numerical yields given in the text).

## Context

This work extends prior validation and verification efforts for MCNP6 documented in Mashnik (2011) and Mashnik (2013), and directly responds to a benchmark report by Remec (2013) that showed poor results from older versions of MCNPX and MARS15 using earlier LAQGSM versions. The study enables the use of MCNP6 as a reliable simulation tool for the Facility for Rare Isotope Beams (FRIB), medical isotope production (including $^{225}$Ac for radiotherapy), space-radiation shielding, and cosmic-ray propagation applications.
