# Can Pulsars in the inner parsecs from Galactic Centre probe the existence of Dark Matter?

> We discuss the formation of dark compact objects in a dark matter environment in view of the possible mass dependence of pulsars on the distribution of dark matter in the Galaxy. Our results indicate that the pulsar masses should decrease going towards the center of the Milky Way due to dark matter capture, thus becoming a probe for the existence and nature of dark matter. We thus propose that the evolution of the pulsar mass in a dark matter rich environment can be used to put constraints, when combined with future experiments, on the characteristics of our Galaxy halo dark matter profile, on the dark matter particle mass and on the dark matter self-interaction strength.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that pulsar masses should decrease as they are located closer to the Galactic Centre due to dark matter capture, and that this mass evolution can serve as a probe for dark matter existence and properties. The claim is hedged by the conservative assumptions used: the authors deliberately excluded the most extreme dark matter density profiles (Einasto α=0.06 and the Di Cintio DC14 profile) and did not include accretion during the neutron star progenitor phase or dark matter self-interaction accretion, meaning the predicted mass decrease is likely an underestimate.

## Method

The authors used the Kouvaris (2013) spherically symmetric accretion formula to compute the dark matter mass accreted by a typical neutron star (1.4 M⊙, R=10 km) as a function of distance from the Galactic Centre, adopting Einasto dark matter density profiles with varying slope parameters α=0.09, 0.11, 0.15, 0.17. They then combined this accreted mass with results from their previous work (Deliyergiyev 2019) that gives the maximum neutron star mass as a function of internal dark matter mass for different dark matter particle masses (100, 200, 500 GeV) and self-interaction strengths (y=0.1, 1, 10, 100), to obtain the neutron star mass as a function of distance from the Galactic Centre.

## Key numbers

- For the reference Einasto profile (α=0.11) with dark matter particle mass 500 GeV and interaction strength y=0.1, the neutron star mass changes from 2 M⊙ to 1 M⊙ at a distance of 0.4 pc from the Galactic Centre.
- For the Einasto profile with α=0.09 (same particle mass and interaction strength), the same mass change from 2 M⊙ to 1 M⊙ occurs at 1.35 pc.
- For α=0.15 and α=0.17 (500 GeV, y=0.1), the mass reduces from 2 M⊙ to 1.2 M⊙ and from 2 M⊙ to 1.7 M⊙, respectively, at 10^3 pc.
- The dark matter accretion for a typical neutron star in the solar neighbourhood is approximately 10^{-11} M⊙.
- The total accreted mass formula gives M_acc = 1.3 × 10^43 (ρ_dm / 0.3 GeV/cm^3) (t / Gyr) f GeV, which is an underestimate by a factor of about 10.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Deliyergiyev 2019, Del Popolo 2019) on dark matter effects in neutron stars, and builds on the formalism of Kouvaris (2013) for dark matter accretion onto neutron stars. It contrasts with earlier proposals to detect dark matter through neutron star temperature changes (Kouvaris 2008, Bertone 2008), which are difficult to observe, by instead proposing a mass-based observable. The results enable future telescopes such as ngVLA, SKA, Athena, NICER, and eXTP to potentially constrain the Milky Way dark matter halo profile, dark matter particle mass, and self-interaction strength by measuring pulsar masses at different distances from the Galactic Centre.
