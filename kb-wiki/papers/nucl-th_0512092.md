# Baryonic Resonances from the Interactions of the Baryon Decuplet and Meson Octet

> We study $S$-wave interactions of the baryon decuplet with the octet of pseudoscalar mesons using the lowest order chiral Lagrangian. We find two bound states in the SU(3) limit corresponding to the octet and decuplet representations. These are found to split into eight different trajectories in the complex plane when the SU(3) symmetry is broken gradually. Finally, we are able to provide a reasonable description for a good number of 4-star ${3/2}^-$ resonances listed by the Particle Data Group. In particular, the $Ξ(1820)$, the $Λ(1520)$ and the $Σ(1670)$ states are well reproduced. We predict a few other resonances and also evaluate the couplings of the observed resonances to the various channels from the residues at the poles of the scattering matrix from where partial decay widths into different channels can be evaluated.

# Baryonic Resonances from the Interactions of the Baryon Decuplet and Meson Octet

## Key claim
The authors find that S-wave interactions of the baryon decuplet with the pseudoscalar meson octet, using only the lowest order chiral Lagrangian with a single natural-sized subtraction constant, dynamically generate a good number of 4-star 3/2- resonances. However, the description is qualified as "reasonable" and some predicted resonances are too broad to have been observed, while others like the Delta(1500) and Xi(2160) stand better chances of observation.

## Method
The tree-level scattering amplitude from the lowest order chiral Lagrangian (Weinberg-Tomozawa term) was used as the kernel of the Bethe-Salpeter equation to obtain a unitarized transition matrix in coupled channels. Poles of the scattering matrix were searched in the complex plane across different Riemann sheets, starting from an SU(3) symmetric limit and gradually breaking the symmetry by changing the baryon and meson masses to their physical values. The residues at the poles provided couplings to different channels, from which partial decay widths were evaluated.

## Key numbers
- The Lambda(1520), Sigma(1670), and Xi(1820) resonances are well reproduced by the model
- A predicted Delta(1500) resonance has a width around 300 MeV
- A predicted Xi(2160) resonance has a width of about 40 MeV
- An Omega(2250) resonance is associated with a dynamically generated state in S=-3, I=0 based on quantum numbers, pole position, and compatibility of the calculated partial decay width with the total width
- A resonance in S=1, I=1 (Delta K channel) appears as a broad bump above the K Delta threshold

## Context
This work extends the successful chiral unitary approach previously applied to S-wave interactions of the baryon octet with the pseudoscalar meson octet, which described many 1/2- resonances such as the N*(1535), Lambda(1405), and Lambda(1670) (Kaiser, Ramos, Oller; Inoue; Jido; Oset and collaborators, 2000-2004). The authors explicitly disagree with the speed plot technique used by Lutz (2004) to identify resonances, arguing that speed plots produce artificial peaks at channel thresholds that were misidentified as resonances. The results enable predictions for partial decay widths into decuplet baryon plus meson channels and suggest a possible exotic pentaquark-like state in the Delta K system that could be observed in pp -> Lambda Delta K and pp -> Sigma Delta K reactions.
