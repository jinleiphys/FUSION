# Evolution of Charge Fluctuations and Correlations in the Hydrodynamic Stage of Heavy Ion Collisions

> Charge fluctuations for a baryon-neutral quark-gluon plasma have been calculated in lattice gauge theory. These fluctuations provide a well-posed rigorous representation of the quark chemistry of the vacuum for temperatures above T_c >~ 155 MeV. Due to the finite lifetime and spatial extent of the fireball created in relativistic heavy ion collisions, charge-charge correlations can only equilibrate for small volumes due to the finite time required to transport charge. This constraint leads to charge correlations at finite relative position that evolve with time. The source and evolution of such correlations is determined by the evolution of the charge fluctuation and the diffusion constant for light quarks. Here, calculations are presented for the evolution of such correlations superimposed onto hydrodynamic simulations. Results are similar to preliminary measurements from STAR, but significant discrepancies remain.

## Key claim
The evolution of charge-charge correlations in the quark-gluon plasma can be calculated by superimposing a diffusion model onto hydrodynamic simulations, using lattice QCD susceptibilities as a source term. The results are similar to preliminary STAR measurements, but significant discrepancies remain, indicating that the model captures the dominant physics but is not yet a complete description.

## Method
The authors calculate the evolution of charge-charge correlation functions in relative spatial rapidity by combining a hydrodynamic simulation of the fireball with a stochastic model for the diffusion of light quarks. The source term for the correlations is derived from the rate of change of the charge susceptibility, taken from lattice QCD calculations, as the system evolves through the phase transition. The resulting quark-level correlations are then projected onto hadron correlations (pions, kaons, protons) using a linear response kernel at the hadronization hypersurface.

## Key numbers
- The initial thermalization time for the hydrodynamic evolution is set at $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm/$c$.
- The hydrodynamic mesh used a time step of $d\tau = 0.02$ fm/$c$ and spatial steps of $dx = dy = 0.1$ fm/$c$.
- The transition temperature for hadronization is assumed to be $T_f = 155$ MeV.
- The test of the Cooper-Frye algorithm against a semi-analytic calculation showed discrepancies below one tenth of one percent for the standard mesh resolution.
- The $v_4$ harmonic, a measure of symmetry breaking from the rectangular mesh, was on the order of $10^{-5}$.

## Context
This work extends the program of using charge balance functions to study the chemical evolution of the quark-gluon plasma, as pioneered by Bass, Danielewicz, and Pratt (2000) and formalized by Pratt (2012). It builds on lattice QCD calculations of charge susceptibilities by Borsanyi et al. (2012) and Bellwied et al. (2015) to provide a rigorous source term for the correlations. The method enables a direct comparison to balance function measurements from the STAR Collaboration (Adamczyk et al., 2016), and the remaining discrepancies point to the need for more refined treatments of the hadronization process or pre-equilibrium dynamics.
