# Drag and Diffusion coefficients in extreme scenarios of temperature and chemical potential

> A comparative study of high and zero temperature plasma for the case of damping rate, drag and diffusion coefficients have been presented. In each of these quantities, it is revealed how the magnetic interaction dominates over the electric one at zero temperature unlike what happens at high temperature.

# Drag and Diffusion coefficients in extreme scenarios of temperature and chemical potential

## Key claim
The paper shows that for quasiparticle damping rate, drag coefficient, and momentum diffusion coefficient, magnetic (transverse) interactions dominate over electric (longitudinal) ones at zero temperature and high chemical potential, whereas at high temperature both sectors contribute at the same order; this qualitative difference arises from Pauli blocking in the degenerate case, which also changes the infrared behavior from logarithmic divergences (hot plasma) to algebraic divergences (ultradegenerate plasma).

## Method
The authors compute the fermion self-energy and the quasiparticle damping rate using the Braaten-Yuan prescription, which introduces an intermediate momentum scale to separate soft and hard momentum exchange, with hard-thermal-loop (HTL) resummed propagators for high temperature and hard-dense-loop (HDL) resummed propagators for zero temperature. The drag coefficient is obtained from the energy loss per unit length by weighting the interaction rate with the energy transfer, and the momentum diffusion coefficient is obtained by weighting with the squared momentum transfer, both evaluated in the two extreme regimes of (T ≠ 0, μ = 0) and (T = 0, μ ≠ 0).

## Key numbers
- High temperature damping rate (transverse sector): γ_t = (g²T)/(2π²) ∫₀^{q*} (dq/q) tan⁻¹(3π ω_p² / (4 q²)), which remains logarithmically divergent.
- High temperature damping rate (longitudinal sector): γ_l = g⁴ T³ / m_D².
- Zero temperature damping rate (longitudinal): γ_l = e² ν² m_D / 64, where ν = |E − μ|/m_D ≪ 1.
- Zero temperature damping rate (transverse): γ_t = e² ν m_D / (24π).
- High temperature drag coefficient: η ≃ (e⁴ T²)/(36π E) [−1/2 + (3/2) log|q_max / m_D| + (1/2) log|2/√π|].
- Zero temperature drag coefficient: η ≃ (e² m_D² ν²)/(48π E) + (e² m_D² ν³)/(96 E) + O(ν⁴), where the first term is magnetic and the second is electric.
- High temperature diffusion coefficient: B ≃ (e⁴ T³)/(18π) [−1/2 + (3/2) log|q_max / m_D| + (1/2) log|2/√π|].
- Zero temperature diffusion coefficient: B ≃ (e² m_D³ ν³)/(72π) + (e² m_D³ ν⁴)/(128) + O(ν⁵).

## Context
This work extends prior calculations of quasiparticle damping rates by Iancu (1997) and Thoma (1991) for hot plasmas, and by Manuel (1997) and Ollitrault (1997) for degenerate plasmas, where finite damping rates were first obtained using HDL propagators with Pauli blocking. It also builds on drag and diffusion coefficient studies by Moore (2005) for high temperature and by Sarkar (2010) for the degenerate case. The results enable a systematic comparison of transport coefficients across the QCD phase diagram, relevant for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC (high temperature) and for compressed baryonic matter at FAIR/GSI as well as neutron star cores (high density).
