# Three-body recombination at finite energy within an optical model

> We investigate three-boson recombination of equal mass systems as function of (negative) scattering length, mass, finite energy, and finite temperature. An optical model with an imaginary potential at short distance reproduces experimental recombination data and allows us to provide a simple parametrization of the recombination rate as function of scattering length and energy. Using the two-body van der Waals length as unit we find that the imaginary potential range and also the potential depth agree to within thirty percent for Lithium and Cesium atoms. As opposed to recent studies suggesting universality of the threshold for bound state formation, our results suggest that the recombination process itself could have universal features.

## Key claim

The authors propose that three-body recombination itself may have universal features, contrary to recent studies suggesting universality only of the threshold for bound state formation. This claim is hedged by the fact that their optical model uses an imaginary potential whose range and depth agree to within thirty percent for Lithium and Cesium atoms when expressed in units of the two-body van der Waals length, but the model parameters still require fitting to experimental data.

## Method

The authors construct an optical model for three-boson recombination of equal-mass systems using hyperspherical coordinates and the adiabatic approximation, where a constant imaginary square-well potential at short hyperradii replaces the usual repulsive three-body cut-off. They solve the hyperradial Schrodinger equation with zero-range two-body potentials and extract the recombination rate from the absorption probability, then parametrize the rate using a Breit-Wigner form with energy-dependent width and resonance position that obey Efimov scaling. The finite-energy calculations are thermally averaged using a Boltzmann distribution to compare with experimental data at finite temperature.

## Key numbers

- The barrier maximum in the effective three-body potential occurs at hyperradius rho = 1.46a with peak height E_B(a) = 0.143 hbar^2/(m a^2), where a is the two-body scattering length
- The Efimov scaling factor for bound or resonance states at zero energy is 22.7, corresponding to exp(pi/s_0) with s_0 = 1.00624
- For Lithium-7, the model fits the measured recombination peak at a approximately -280 a_0 (Bohr radius) using imaginary potential range rho_imag = 0.41 a_0 and depth V_imag = -68 hbar^2/(m a_0^2)
- For Cesium-133, the model uses rho_imag = 1.58 a_0 and V_imag = -10 hbar^2/(m a_0^2) to reproduce data at three different Feshbach resonances
- The zero-energy formula for Lithium-7 uses eta^- = 0.12 and a^(-) = -241 a_0

## Context

This work extends the optical model approach to three-body recombination, previously applied in nuclear physics contexts, to ultracold atomic gases where Efimov physics has been observed experimentally since the landmark Cesium experiment by Kraemer et al. (2006). The model contrasts with earlier theoretical approaches by Nielsen et al. (1999), Esry et al. (1999), Bedaque et al. (2000), and Braaten and Hammer (2001) that assumed or found the decay parameter to be independent of energy and scattering length. The results enable a simple parametrization of recombination rates across different atomic species and temperatures, and suggest that the recombination process may be universal when expressed in van der Waals units, opening the possibility of predicting recombination features in new atomic systems.
