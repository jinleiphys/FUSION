# Right-Left Asymmetry of Radiation from Fission

## Key claim

The right-left asymmetry parameter for electric dipole gamma radiation from fission fragments, arising from the Strutinski-Denisov induced polarisation mechanism, is estimated to be on the order of 10⁻³, which agrees with experimental data on the radiative ROT effect in ²³⁵U fission induced by cold polarised neutrons, though the calculated value is about twice as small as the experimental values.

## Method

The author derives the asymmetry parameter R(θ) by modeling the angular distribution of electric dipole radiation from fission fragments that rotate with axial symmetry before dissipating their collective energy. The fragment wavefunction is expressed with K as a good quantum number, and the angular distribution in the laboratory frame is obtained by integrating over time with an exponential relaxation factor characterized by the dissipation time τ_dis. Two limiting cases are considered: slow rotation (ω ≪ λ) and fast rotation (ω ≫ λ), with the fast-rotation case (δ → π/4) used for numerical estimation based on the average fragment spin I ≈ 7.

## Key numbers

- Total probability of electric dipole gamma emission: N_γ^(E1) = 0.008 fission⁻¹
- Average number of gamma quanta emitted per fission: N_γ^f ≈ 8 fission⁻¹
- Angular distribution parameter a ≈ 1.5 × 10⁻³
- Calculated asymmetry parameter R(θ) ≈ −0.7 × 10⁻³ at angles 35° and 57°
- Experimental asymmetry at 35°: R_exp(35°) = (1.2 ± 0.3) × 10⁻³
- Experimental asymmetry at 57°: R_exp(57°) = (1.8 ± 0.3) × 10⁻³
- Dissipation time: τ_dis ≲ 10⁻¹⁹ s
- Average fragment spin: I = 7–8

## Context

This work extends the Strutinski-Denisov induced polarisation mechanism for electric dipole radiation from fission fragments, originally proposed in Dub93, to explain the ROT effect previously observed for light charged particles in ternary fission (ROT). The results provide a direct confirmation of the snapping-back nuclear surface phenomenon, which had earlier been evidenced only through shaking muons emitted from prompt fission fragments (Halp; Book; D1; D2). The study enables further investigation of the dynamics of nuclear surface relaxation during fission.
