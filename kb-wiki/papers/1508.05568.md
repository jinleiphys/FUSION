# Extended Hartree-Fock study of the single-particle potential: the nuclear symmetry energy, nucleon effective mass, and folding model of the nucleon optical potential

> The nucleon mean-field potential has been thoroughly investigated in an extended Hartree-Fock (HF) calculation of nuclear matter (NM) using the CDM3Y3 and CDM3Y6 density dependent versions of the M3Y interaction. The single-particle (s/p) energies of nucleons in NM are determined according to the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem, which gives rise naturally to a rearrangement term (RT) of the s/p potential at the Fermi momentum. Using the RT obtained exactly at the different NM densities and neutron-proton asymmetries, a consistent method is suggested to take into account effectively the momentum dependence of the RT of the s/p potential within the standard HF scheme. To obtain a realistic momentum dependence of the nucleon optical potential (OP), the high-momentum part of the s/p potential was accurately readjusted to reproduce the observed energy dependence of the nucleon OP over a wide range of energies. The impact of the RT and momentum dependence of the s/p potential on the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy and nucleon effective mass has been studied in details. The high-momentum tail of the s/p potential was found to have a sizable effect on the slope of the symmetry energy and the neutron-proton effective mass splitting at supranuclear densities of the NM. Based on a local density approximation, the folding model of the nucleon OP of finite nuclei has been extended to take into account consistently the RT and momentum dependence of the nucleon OP in the same mean-field manner, and successfully applied to study the elastic neutron scattering on the lead target at the energies around the Fermi energy.

# Extended Hartree-Fock study of the single-particle potential: the nuclear symmetry energy, nucleon effective mass, and folding model of the nucleon optical potential

## Key claim
The rearrangement term (RT) of the single-particle potential, derived exactly from the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem at each nuclear matter density and neutron-proton asymmetry, can be incorporated into the standard Hartree-Fock scheme with an effective momentum dependence that reproduces the observed energy dependence of the nucleon optical potential over a wide energy range, provided the high-momentum part of the s/p potential is readjusted. The high-momentum tail of the s/p potential has a sizable effect on the slope of the symmetry energy and the neutron-proton effective mass splitting at supranuclear densities, but this result is conditional on the specific density-dependent M3Y interaction used (CDM3Y3 or CDM3Y6) and the iterative adjustment of the momentum-dependent scaling factor g(k).

## Method
The authors performed extended Hartree-Fock calculations of homogeneous, spin-saturated nuclear matter using the CDM3Y3 and CDM3Y6 density-dependent versions of the M3Y-Paris interaction, with the single-particle energies determined according to the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem. The rearrangement term of the single-particle potential was computed exactly at the Fermi momentum for each density and asymmetry, then extended to all momenta by factorizing the density dependence and using the same functional form as the HF potential. The high-momentum part of the s/p potential was scaled by a momentum-dependent function g(k), iteratively adjusted to reproduce the empirical energy dependence of the nucleon optical potential in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density, and the isovector density dependence F1(ρ) was refitted to the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock results of Jeukenne, Lejeune, and Mahaux (JLM). The resulting s/p potential was then used to compute the nuclear symmetry energy, nucleon effective masses, and, via local density approximation, the folding model optical potential for elastic neutron scattering on 208Pb.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear incompressibility K = 218 MeV (CDM3Y3) and 252 MeV (CDM3Y6) for symmetric nuclear matter.
- Nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density S0 = 30.1 MeV for both CDM3Y3 and CDM3Y6 interactions.
- Slope of the symmetry energy L = 49.6 MeV (CDM3Y3) and 49.7 MeV (CDM3Y6).
- Saturation density ρ0 ≈ 0.17 fm⁻³.
- The s/p potential reaches zero and changes sign at nucleon momentum k ≈ 3.3 fm⁻¹, corresponding to incident energy around 220 MeV where the empirical Schrodinger-equivalent optical potential becomes repulsive.
- The RT contributes about 20-30% of the total strength of the s/p potential at ρ = ρ0 over a wide range of nucleon momentum.
- At high density ρ = 2ρ0, the repulsive contribution of the RT reaches up to 70% of the HF term.
- The isovector density dependence F1(ρ) was scaled by a factor of 1.3 relative to the JLM results, as found necessary in prior folding model analyses of (p,n) scattering to isobar analog states (Khoa, 2007).

## Context
This work extends earlier Hartree-Fock studies of nuclear matter using density-dependent M3Y interactions (Khoa, 1993, 1995, 1997) by consistently including the rearrangement term from the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem, which was previously neglected. It contrasts with the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations of nuclear matter (Bombaci, 1991; Zuo, 1999, 2014) and the JLM microscopic optical potential (Jeukenne, 1977; Lejeune, 1980) by providing a simpler mean-field method to account for the momentum dependence of the RT. The results for the asymmetry dependence of the s/p potential agree with the recent BHF calculation by Vidana (2013) using the Argonne V18 interaction with Urbana three-body force. The extended folding model enables consistent inclusion of RT and momentum dependence in nucleon-nucleus optical potential calculations for finite nuclei, as demonstrated for elastic neutron scattering on 208Pb at 30.4 and 40 MeV measured by Devlin (2012).
