# Bloom-Gilman duality of inelastic structure functions in nucleon and nuclei

> The Bloom-Gilman local duality of the inelastic structure function of the proton, the deuteron and light complex nuclei is investigated using available experimental data in the squared four-momentum transfer range from 0.3 to 5 (GeV/c)**2. The results of our analysis suggest that the onset of the Bloom-Gilman local duality is anticipated in complex nuclei with respect to the case of the protonand the deuteron. A possible interpretation of this result in terms of a rescaling effect is discussed with particular emphasis to the possibility of reproducing the damping of the nucleon-resonance transitions observed in recent electroproduction data off nuclei.

## Key claim

The onset of Bloom-Gilman local duality for the inelastic part of the structure function is anticipated in complex nuclei (A ≃ 12) compared to the proton and deuteron, with the data suggesting a lower Q² threshold for the dominance of twist-2 operators; this is interpreted as a Q²-rescaling effect consistent with a decrease of the mass scale of about 8% in nuclei.

## Method

The authors analyzed all available experimental data for the inelastic structure functions νW₂ of the proton, deuteron, and light complex nuclei (⁹Be, ¹²C, ¹⁶O) in the Q² range 0.3 to 5 (GeV/c)². They compared the measured structure functions and their Nachtmann moments M_n(Q²) to a "dual" structure function computed from the GRV parton densities evolved at next-to-leading order, including target mass corrections and folded with nucleon momentum distributions (Paris potential for deuteron, spectral function from Cenni et al. (1996) for nuclei). Quasi-elastic contributions were subtracted using the approach of Cenni et al. (1996).

## Key numbers

- The onset of local duality occurs at Q₀² = 1.5 ± 0.1 (GeV/c)² for the nucleon and deuteron, and at Q₀² = 1.0 ± 0.2 (GeV/c)² for nuclei with A ≃ 12.
- The mass scale ratio between deuteron and nuclei derived from the onset of duality is μ_D/μ_A = 1.2 ± 0.1.
- The rescaling factor δ = μ_D²/μ_A² = 1.17 ± 0.09 brings the moments in deuteron and nuclei into best simultaneous agreement around the mass scale region.
- From the rescaling of moments at the static point: μ_D/μ_A = 1.08 ± 0.05.
- From the EMC effect analysis: μ_D/μ_A ≲ 1.10.
- The empirical σ_L/σ_T ratio is parameterized as a·Q²[e^{-bQ²} + c·e^{-dQ²}] with a = 0.014, b = 0.07, c = 40.8, d = 0.78 (Q² in (GeV/c)²).

## Context

This work extends the Bloom-Gilman duality (Bloom and Gilman, 1970) from the proton to complex nuclei, building on the QCD-based justification by De Rujula, Georgi and Politzer (1977) using operator product expansion and Nachtmann moments. The analysis addresses the faster disappearance of nucleon-resonance bumps in nuclei observed in electroproduction data (Geros et al., 1996) and interprets it via a Q²-rescaling mechanism (Jaffe, 1983; Date and Takagi, 1986) where the quark confinement scale increases in the nuclear medium. The results enable future systematic measurements at CEBAF (now Jefferson Lab) to test the rescaling interpretation and investigate the relation between resonance and deep inelastic scattering regions in nuclei.
