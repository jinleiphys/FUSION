# Vacuum fluctuation effects on asymmetric nuclear matter

> The vacuum fluctuation (VF) effects on asymmetric nuclear matter are investigated. Masses of nucleons and mesons are modified in the nuclear medium by calculating the loop-diagram corrections and the density dependence of hadron masses is obtained. The relativistic Lagrangian density with the isovector scalar $δ$ meson is used to calculate the nuclear equation of state (EOS) in the framework of the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach, the effects of the in-medium hadron masses on the properties of neutron stars are finally studied. With the inclusion of the VF corrections, the nuclear EOS becomes softer and the neutron star masses are reduced.

## Key claim

Vacuum fluctuation (VF) corrections, introduced via loop-diagram self-energy calculations, soften the nuclear equation of state (EOS) and reduce neutron star masses compared to the nonlinear relativistic mean-field (NL-RMF) model without such corrections, with the effect depending on the inclusion of the isovector scalar delta meson.

## Method

The authors extended the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach by including loop-diagram corrections to the self-energies of nucleons and mesons (sigma, omega, rho, delta) in asymmetric nuclear matter, using a Lagrangian density that includes the isovector scalar delta meson. They calculated the in-medium effective masses of nucleons and mesons self-consistently from the pole positions of the propagators in the random phase approximation, then derived the nuclear equation of state and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations for neutron star properties.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: 0.16 fm^{-3}, with binding energy per nucleon -16.0 MeV, incompressibility 240.0 MeV, and symmetry energy 31.3 MeV (Table I).
- In the symmetric VF-rho case, in-medium meson effective mass decreases at normal density are approximately 25% for sigma, 20% for omega, and 5% for rho mesons.
- In the VF-rho-delta case, decreases at normal density are 11-13% for rho and 25-27% for delta mesons.
- Maximum neutron star mass for pure neutron matter in VF-rho model: 1.82 solar masses (radius 11.57 km, central density 6.41 times saturation density).
- Maximum neutron star mass for pure neutron matter in VF-rho-delta model: 2.07 solar masses (radius 12.49 km, central density 5.48 times saturation density).
- Maximum neutron star mass for beta-equilibrium (npe) matter in VF-rho model: 1.45 solar masses (radius 10.22 km, central density 8.98 times saturation density).
- Maximum neutron star mass for beta-equilibrium (npe) matter in VF-rho-delta model: 1.51 solar masses (radius 10.77 km, central density 8.20 times saturation density).

## Context

This work extends the earlier NL-RMF calculations of Liu et al. (2005) and Liu et al. (2007) that studied asymmetric nuclear matter and neutron stars with the delta meson, by incorporating vacuum fluctuation effects following the approach of Abhijit et al. for symmetric matter. The VF-RMF model produces a softer symmetry energy and softer EOS than the NL-RMF model, leading to lower neutron star masses, which contrasts with the heavier neutron stars found in the constant-coupling NL-RMF models with the delta meson. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data on in-medium meson mass shifts, particularly for the delta meson where no experimental measurements currently exist.
