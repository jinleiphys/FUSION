# Breathing-mode measurements in Sn isotopes and isospin dependence of nuclear incompressibility

> T. Li {\it et al.}[Phys. Rev. C {\bf 81}, 034309 (2010)] have analyzed their measured breathing-mode energies of some tin isotopes in terms of a first-order leptodermous expansion, and find for the symmetry-incompressibility coefficient $K_τ$ the value of -550 $\pm$ 100 MeV. Removing an approximation that they made, we find that the first-order estimate of $K_τ$ shifts to -661 $\pm$ 144 MeV. However, taking into account higher-order terms in the leptodermous expansion shows that the data are compatible with the significantly lower magnitudes indicated by both another experiment and some theoretical estimates.

# Breathing-mode measurements in Sn isotopes and isospin dependence of nuclear incompressibility

## Key claim

The data from Li et al. (2010) on breathing-mode energies in tin isotopes are compatible with a symmetry-incompressibility coefficient Kτ of about -350 MeV, rather than the -550 ± 100 MeV originally claimed, once higher-order terms in the leptodermous expansion (particularly the surface-symmetry term) are taken into account. However, the required values of the surface and curvature coefficients must be regarded as "effective" because they absorb the effect of all still-higher-order terms that were neglected.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed the measured giant isoscalar monopole resonance (breathing-mode) energies of the seven even-even isotopes 112-124Sn from Li et al. (2010), expressed as finite-nucleus incompressibilities K(Z,A). They first removed the approximation that the A-1/3 term is constant across the isotope chain, instead fixing Kv at 240 ± 10 MeV and fitting Ksf and Kτ to the data. They then extended the leptodermous expansion to include higher-order terms: a surface-symmetry term Kss I2 A-1/3 and a curvature term Kcv A-2/3, setting Kτ to -350 MeV and leaving Ksf, Kss, and Kcv as fitting parameters.

## Key numbers

- Li et al. (2010) original first-order fit gave Kτ = -550 ± 100 MeV
- Removing the constant-A-1/3 approximation shifts the first-order estimate to Kτ = -661 ± 144 MeV
- The well-established range for the incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter Kv is 240 ± 10 MeV
- Most Skyrme and Gogny forces predict Kτ in the range -325 ± 55 MeV
- The Skyrme force BSk18 gives Kτ = -344 MeV
- Three different Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations yield Kτ values in the range -344 to -335 MeV
- The heavy-ion collision measurement of Chen et al. (2009) gives Kτ = -370 ± 120 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Li et al. (2010), who measured breathing-mode energies in Sn isotopes and concluded their data "rule out a vast majority of Skyrme forces." The present authors show that this conclusion was premature because it neglected higher-order leptodermous terms. The value Kτ ≈ -350 MeV is consistent with the heavy-ion experiment of Chen et al. (2009), with Skyrme and Gogny force predictions, and with Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations by Vidana et al. (2009). The authors note that the only fully reliable approach would be self-consistent QRPA or constrained HFB calculations, which is beyond the scope of their note.
