# Simulation of Z(3) walls and string production via bubble nucleation in a quark-hadron transition

> We study the dynamics of confinement-deconfinement (C-D) phase transition in the context of relativistic heavy-ion collisions within the framework of effective models for the Polyakov loop order parameter. We study the formation of $Z(3)$ walls and associated strings in the initial transition from the confining (hadronic) phase to the deconfining (QGP) phase via the so called Kibble mechanism. Essential physics of the Kibble mechanism is contained in a sort of domain structure arising after any phase transition which represents random variation of the order parameter at distances beyond the typical correlation length. We implement this domain structure by using the Polyakov loop effective model with a first order phase transition and confine ourselves with temperature/time ranges so that the first order C-D transition proceeds via bubble nucleation, leading to a well defined domain structure. The formation of $Z(3)$ walls and associated strings results from the coalescence of QGP bubbles expanding in the confining background. We investigate the evolution of the $Z(3)$ wall and string network. We also calculate the energy density fluctuations associated with $Z(3)$ wall network and strings which decay away after the temperature drops below the quark-hadron transition temperature during the expansion of QGP. We discuss evolution of these quantities with changing temperature via Bjorken's hydrodynamical model and discuss possible experimental signatures resulting from the presence of $Z(3)$ wall network and associate strings.

## Key claim

The paper simulates the formation of Z(3) domain walls and associated QGP strings during the confinement-deconfinement phase transition via bubble nucleation, using the Kibble mechanism in a first-order transition model, but the authors acknowledge that lattice results show the quark-hadron transition is most likely a smooth cross-over at zero chemical potential, and their use of a first-order model is justified by the non-zero baryon chemical potential expected in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors use the Pisarski effective potential for the Polyakov loop order parameter with a weakly first-order phase transition, restricting to temperature ranges (182–250 MeV) where bubble nucleation occurs. They numerically solve the 3-dimensional Euclidean bounce equation to obtain critical bubble profiles and nucleation rates, then simulate random nucleation of QGP bubbles in a confining background on a 2+1 dimensional lattice, allowing bubbles to expand and coalesce via field evolution equations. The evolution of the resulting Z(3) wall and string network is tracked using Bjorken's longitudinal scaling hydrodynamical model.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for confinement-deconfinement transition: \(T_c \simeq 182\) MeV
- First-order bubble nucleation possible only up to \(T \simeq 250\) MeV, above which spinodal decomposition occurs
- Bubble sizes at nucleation: approximately 1–1.5 fm radius
- Domain wall velocities estimated to range from 0.5 to 0.8 (in natural units)
- Nucleation temperature chosen for simulations: \(T = 200\) MeV
- Correlation length in hadronic phase taken as \(\xi_{had} = 0.7\) fm
- Parameters for effective potential: \(b_3 = 2.0\), \(b_4 = 0.6016\) (pure gauge), with \(b_4\) rescaled by factor 47.5/16 for QCD with three massless quark flavors

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by some of the same authors (Gupta, Mohapatra, Srivastava, Tiwari) on Z(3) interfaces and QGP strings (z3str, 2004) and on baryon inhomogeneities from collapsing Z(3) walls (znb, 2000), providing the first numerical simulation of their formation via the Kibble mechanism in a heavy-ion collision context. The approach contrasts with spinodal decomposition simulations in the Polyakov loop model by Dumitru (2007), focusing instead on topological defect formation and energy density fluctuations. The results enable future studies of experimental signatures such as event-by-event energy density fluctuations and \(P_T\) enhancement of heavy-flavor hadrons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
