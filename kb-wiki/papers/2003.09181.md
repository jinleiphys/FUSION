# Kaonic Hydrogen and Deuterium in Hamiltonian Effective Field Theory

> The anti-kaon nucleon scattering lengths resulting from a Hamiltonian effective field theory analysis of experimental data and lattice QCD studies are presented. The same Hamiltonian is then used to compute the scattering length for the $K^- d$ system, taking careful account of the effects of recoil on the energy at which the $\bar{K}N$ T-matrices are evaluated. These results are then used to estimate the shift and width of the $1S$ levels of anti-kaonic hydrogen and deuterium. The $K^- p$ result is in excellent agreement with the SIDDHARTA measurement. In the $K^- d$ case the imaginary part of the scattering length and consequently the width of the $1S$ state are considerably larger than found in earlier work. This is a consequence of the effect of recoil on the energy of the $\bar{K}N$ energy, which enhances the role of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance.

# Kaonic Hydrogen and Deuterium in Hamiltonian Effective Field Theory

## Key claim
The recoil of nucleons in the deuteron, neglected in most prior studies, significantly enhances the imaginary part of the $K^-d$ scattering length and consequently the width of the $1S$ state of kaonic deuterium, because the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance lies close to the $\bar{K}N$ threshold and the recoil shifts the center-of-mass energy at which the $\bar{K}N$ T-matrix is evaluated.

## Method
The authors use Hamiltonian Effective Field Theory (HEFT) with Weinberg-Tomozawa potentials and a dipole form factor to solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for coupled $\bar{K}N$, $\pi\Sigma$, $\pi\Lambda$, and $\eta\Lambda$ channels. The $\bar{K}N$ scattering lengths are obtained from a previous fit to experimental $K^-p$ cross sections and lattice QCD data. For kaonic deuterium, the Faddeev equations are solved using the static nucleon approximation and then with a recoil correction where the $\bar{K}N$ T-matrix is evaluated at the center-of-mass energy $E_{\bar{K}N}=m_d+m_K-m_N-q^2/2\mu_3$, with $q$ the spectator nucleon momentum in the deuteron.

## Key numbers
- $a_{I=0} = -1.77 + 1.08i$ fm, $a_{I=1} = 0.27 + 0.52i$ fm (isospin scattering lengths from HEFT)
- $a_{K^-p} = -0.75 + 0.80i$ fm (predicted scattering length)
- $\epsilon_{1S}^p = 307$ eV, $\Gamma_{1S}^p = 533$ eV (kaonic hydrogen, consistent with SIDDHARTA experiment: $\epsilon_{1S}^p = 283\pm36\pm6$ eV, $\Gamma_{1S}^p = 541\pm89\pm22$ eV)
- $a_{K^-d}|_{\rm StaticApprox} = -1.55 + 1.57i$ fm (static nucleon approximation)
- $a_{K^-d} = -0.59 + 2.70i$ fm (with recoil correction)
- $\epsilon_{1S}^d|_{\rm StaticApprox} = 855$ eV, $\Gamma_{1S}^d|_{\rm StaticApprox} = 1127$ eV (static approximation)
- $\epsilon_{1S}^d = 803$ eV, $\Gamma_{1S}^d = 2280$ eV (with recoil correction)
- $\langle a_{I=0}^d \rangle = -1.07 + 3.01i$ fm (average $I=0$ scattering length including recoil, compared to threshold $a_{I=0} = -1.77 + 1.08i$ fm)

## Context
This work extends the HEFT framework of Liu (2016) for $\bar{K}N$ scattering to the $K^-d$ system, finding that the recoil effect produces a much larger width than earlier calculations by Kamalov (2000), Doring (2011), Mizutani (2012), and Hoshino (2017), which used the static nucleon approximation or fixed-center approximation. The result predicts a broad $1S$ state for kaonic deuterium that will be tested by the upcoming SIDDHARTA-2 and J-PARC E57 experiments. The recoil effect is significant because the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance lies within 30 MeV of the $\bar{K}N$ threshold, unlike the $\Delta(1232)$ in the pionic case which is 150 MeV away.
