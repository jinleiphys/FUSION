# Quark-Hadron Phase Transitions in Young and Old Neutron Stars

> The mixed phase of quarks and hadrons which might exist in the dense matter encountered in the varying conditions of temperature and trapped neutrino fraction in proto-neutron stars is studied. The extent that the mixed phase depends upon the thermodynamical parameters as well as on the stiffness of matter in the hadronic and quark phases is discussed. We show that hadronic equations of state that maximize the quark content of matter at a given {\it density} generally minimize the extent of the mixed phase region in a neutron star of a given mass, and that only in extreme cases could a pure quark star result. For both the Nambu Jona-Lasinio and MIT bag quark models, neutrino trapping inhibits the appearance of a mixed phase which leads to possible proto-neutron star metastability. The main difference between the two quark models is the small abundance of strange quarks in the former. We also demonstrate that $\partial T/\partial n<0$ along adiabats in the quark-hadron mixed phase, opposite to what is found for the kaon condensates-hadron mixed phase. This could lead to core temperatures which are significantly lower in stars containing quarks than in those not containing quarks.

## Key claim

The mixed phase of quarks and hadrons can exist in neutron stars of virtually any mass above 1.4 solar masses, but only in extreme cases could a pure quark star result; the extent of the mixed phase region in a star of a given mass is maximized for softer hadronic equations of state, even though stiffer hadronic equations of state maximize the quark content at a given density.

## Method

The authors modeled the hadronic phase using a field-theoretical description with baryons interacting via sigma-, omega-, and rho-meson exchange (the Muller-Serot model), including hyperons and exploring a range of vector meson coupling parameters (zeta and xi) to vary the stiffness of the supernuclear equation of state. The quark phase was modeled using both the MIT bag model (with bag constant B between 150 and 250 MeV fm^-3) and the Nambu Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with parameters fit to experimental meson properties. The mixed phase was constructed using Gibbs phase equilibrium conditions with global charge neutrality, and the authors considered three thermodynamic conditions representing proto-neutron star evolution: initial state (entropy per baryon s=1, electron lepton fraction Y_Le=0.4), maximally heated state (s=2, no neutrinos), and cold catalyzed state (s=0, no neutrinos).

## Key numbers

- Nuclear equilibrium density: n0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- Binding energy at n0: Eb = -16 MeV
- Compressibility at n0: K0 = 250 MeV
- Nucleon effective mass at n0: M*_0 = 0.6 M (where M is the nucleon mass)
- Symmetry energy at n0: e_sym = 35 MeV
- Acceptable ranges for hadronic parameters: 0 <= xi <= 1.5 and 0 <= zeta <= 0.06
- Hyperon coupling constants: x_rho = x_sigma = 0.8, x_omega = 0.895 (from Lambda hyperon binding energy of -28 MeV in nuclei)
- NJL model parameters: Lambda = 602.3 MeV, G*Lambda^2 = 1.835, K*Lambda^5 = 12.36, m_0,s = 140.7 MeV, m_0,u = m_0,d = 5.5 MeV
- MIT bag constant range explored: 150 <= B/(MeV fm^-3) <= 250
- Trapped lepton fraction in initial PNS: Y_Le = 0.4
- Entropy per baryon in initial PNS: s = 1 (in units of Boltzmann constant kB)
- Entropy per baryon in maximally heated star: s = 2
- Temperature range in inner core at peak heating: 30-50 MeV
- Temperatures fall below 1 MeV within minutes after heating
- Mixed phase can exist in neutron stars in the range 1.2-2 solar masses, depending on the model
- The mixed phase region is marked by a large decrease in the incompressibility (partial P/partial n) of matter

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quark-hadron phase transitions in neutron stars by Glen (year not specified), HPS (year not specified), and PCL (year not specified), which had largely ignored finite temperature and were limited to the MIT bag model. The authors include both trapped neutrinos and finite temperature, compare the MIT bag and NJL quark models, explore the effects of hadronic stiffness, include hyperons, and delineate phase diagrams in the lepton number-baryon density plane. The results enable future full proto-neutron star simulations to evaluate the implications for neutrino fluxes and temperatures, since the finding that core temperatures may be significantly lower in stars containing quarks than in those without quarks will affect neutrino opacities.
