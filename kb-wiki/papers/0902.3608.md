# A novel spectral broadening from vector--axial-vector mixing in dense matter

> The presence of baryonic matter leads to the mixing between transverse $ρ$ and $a_1$ mesons through a set of $ωρa_1$-type interactions, which results in the modification to the dispersion relation. We show that a clear enhancement of the vector spectral function appears below $\sqrt{s}=m_ρ$ for small three-momenta of the $ρ$ meson, and thus the vector spectrum exhibits broadening. We also discuss its relevance to dilepton measurements.

# A novel spectral broadening from vector–axial-vector mixing in dense matter

## Key claim
The presence of baryonic matter induces mixing between transverse ρ and a₁ mesons via ωρa₁-type interactions, producing a clear enhancement of the vector spectral function below √s = m_ρ for small three-momenta of the ρ meson. The effect depends on the mixing strength C, which is predicted to be C ≈ 1 GeV·(n_B/n_0) in holographic QCD but may be as small as C ≈ 0.1 GeV in three-color QCD based on empirical estimates.

## Method
The authors introduce a Chern–Simons-type mixing term in a chiral Lagrangian that couples transverse ρ and a₁ mesons at finite baryon density, modifying their dispersion relations. They compute the in-medium vector current correlator using bare propagators with vacuum decay widths, decompose it into longitudinal and transverse parts, and integrate the resulting spectral function over three-momentum to obtain the spin-averaged vector spectrum and dilepton production rate.

## Key numbers
- Normal nuclear matter density: n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Mixing strength from holographic QCD: C ≈ 1 GeV·(n_B/n_0)
- Empirical estimate of mixing strength: C ≈ 0.1 GeV at normal nuclear matter density
- Vacuum masses used: m_π = 0.14 GeV, m_ρ = 0.77 GeV, m_a₁ = 1.26 GeV
- ρ coupling strength: g_ρ = 0.119 GeV²
- Vacuum ρ decay width: Γ_ρ(s = m_ρ²) = 0.15 GeV
- Vacuum a₁ decay width: Γ_a₁(s = m_a₁²) = 0.3 GeV
- Vacuum ω decay width: Γ_ω = 8.49 MeV
- Vacuum φ decay width: Γ_φ = 4.26 MeV
- Vacuum f₁(1285) decay width: Γ_f₁(1285) = 24.3 MeV
- Vacuum f₁(1420) decay width: Γ_f₁(1420) = 54.9 MeV
- Temperature used in dilepton rate calculation: T = 0.1 GeV

## Context
This work extends the study of vector–axial-vector mixing in dense matter, previously derived from a Chern–Simons term in holographic QCD (Holographic QCD, 2009), and contrasts with low-energy theorems for V-A mixing at finite temperature (Low-energy theorem, 1990s) and chiral reduction formulas based on virial expansion (Chiral reduction, 2000s). The results are relevant for interpreting dilepton measurements in heavy-ion collisions at facilities such as J-PARC, GSI/FAIR, and RHIC low-energy running, and may help explain in-medium “mass shifts” of ρ, ω, and φ mesons observed by CBELSA/TAPS and KEK-PS-E325 (CBELSA/TAPS, 2007; KEK-PS-E325, 2005). The paper also suggests that higher Kaluza-Klein modes in holographic QCD may contribute to a stronger mixing than the empirical estimate, and that the mixing does not vanish toward chiral symmetry restoration, unlike the case at finite temperature without baryon density (Harada and Sasaki, 2006).
