# Decay properties of undetected superheavy nuclei with Z>110

> A comprehensive study of favoured and unfavoured $α$-decay, cluster decay, weak-decay along with spontaneous fission in undetected superheavy nuclei within the range for proton number 111$\leq$Z$\leq$118 and neutron number 161$\leq$N$\leq$192 is performed. Half-lives for various mentioned decays are estimated with good accuracy on the basis of NUBASE2020 and are found in excellent match with the known half-lives. $α$-decay mode is found most probable in this wide range and correspondingly potential $α$-decay chains are reckoned. Peculiarly, the chances of cluster emission, as well as weak-decay, are also anticipated in this region of the periodic chart which open new pathways of detection of superheavy nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for undetected superheavy nuclei with Z between 111 and 118, alpha-decay is the most probable decay mode, but cluster emission and weak-decay also have non-negligible probabilities in specific isotopes, opening new detection pathways. This claim is hedged by the fact that the half-life estimates depend on theoretical Q-values from the WS4 mass model when experimental values are unavailable, and the uncertainties in these Q-values are evaluated as only ±0.04 MeV for alpha-decay.

## Method

The authors calculated half-lives for alpha-decay using the new modified Horoi formula (NMHF), for spontaneous fission using the modified Bao formula (MBF), for cluster decay using the universal decay law (UDL), and for weak-decay using the Fiset and Nix formula. They tested these formulas against experimental data from NUBASE2020 for nuclei in the range 111≤Z≤118 and 161≤N≤192, selecting the formula with the lowest root mean square error for each decay mode. For alpha-decay, the NMHF formula was fitted to 424 experimental data points and tested on 40 data points within the superheavy range, achieving an RMSE of 0.56 and uncertainty of ±0.09 seconds.

## Key numbers

- NMHF formula for alpha-decay half-lives achieved RMSE of 0.56 and uncertainty of ±0.09 seconds when tested on 40 experimental data points within 111≤Z≤118 and 161≤N≤192
- MBF formula for spontaneous fission half-lives achieved RMSE of 1.86 when tested on 5 experimental data points for Z>110
- Uncertainties in theoretical Q-alpha values from WS4 mass model were found to be only ±0.04 MeV, evaluated using 69 experimental data points for Z≥106
- For favoured alpha-transitions, predicted half-lives range from log10(T1/2) = -6.43±0.06 seconds for 280Og→276Lv to 8.17±0.16 seconds for 304Cn→300Ds
- For unfavoured alpha-transitions, predicted half-lives range from log10(T1/2) = -5.09±0.07 seconds for 277Mc→273Nh to 9.87±0.17 seconds for 303Rg→299Mt

## Context

This work extends previous studies of superheavy nuclei decay properties by applying recently modified empirical formulas (NMHF, MBF) that were calibrated against the NUBASE2020 dataset, improving upon earlier approaches such as the Royer formula (Royer 2020), the Gamow-like model (Zdeb 2013), and the Coulomb and proximity potential model (Santhosh 2012). The comprehensive treatment of four competing decay modes (alpha, spontaneous fission, cluster, and weak-decay) for all undetected isotopes between Z=111 and Z=118 enables experimental planning for future synthesis attempts at facilities like GSI, RIKEN, and Dubna, particularly for identifying new decay chains and assessing the feasibility of detecting cluster radioactivity in the superheavy region.
