# Hadronic transport coefficients from the linear sigma model at finite temperature

> We investigate general frameworks for calculating transport coefficients for quasiparticle theories at finite temperature. Hadronic transport coefficients are then computed using the linear sigma model (LSM). The bulk viscosity over entropy density ($ζ/s$) is evaluated in the relaxation time approximation (RTA) and the specific shear viscosity ($η/s$) and static electrical conductivity ($σ_{el}/T$) are both obtained in the RTA and using a functional variational approach. Results are shown for different values of the scalar-isoscalar hadron vacuum mass with in-medium masses for the interacting fields. The advantages and limitations of the LSM for studies of strongly interacting matter out of equilibrium are discussed and results are compared with others in the literature.

## Key claim

The paper calculates hadronic transport coefficients (shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and electrical conductivity) using the linear sigma model at finite temperature, but the results depend on the assumed vacuum mass of the sigma meson (400, 600, or 900 MeV) and are limited by the use of tree-level matrix elements with the Mandelstam variables taken in the high-energy limit to bypass singularities from the sigma pole.

## Method

The authors compute transport coefficients by solving the linearized Boltzmann equation for the linear sigma model with classical (Boltzmann) statistics, using thermal effective masses obtained from mean-field equations. Shear viscosity and electrical conductivity are evaluated both in the energy-dependent relaxation time approximation (RTA) and via a functional variational method extended to massive theories with inelastic processes; bulk viscosity is computed only in the RTA due to zero-mode complications in the variational approach for multi-component gases.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum sigma mass values used: m_sigma = {400, 600, 900} MeV, with fixed vacuum pion mass m_pi = 140 MeV and decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV
- Temperature range studied: lower bound T = 150 MeV, extending to higher temperatures through chiral symmetry restoration
- The critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration is not given as a single number; the transition is a crossover (not second-order) due to explicit symmetry breaking
- Shear viscosity eta/s and electrical conductivity sigma_el/T are computed in both RTA and variational method; bulk viscosity zeta/s is computed only in RTA
- The coupling constant lambda is determined by lambda = (m_sigma^2 - m_pi^2)/(2 f_pi^2), yielding different values for each vacuum sigma mass

## Context

This work extends the variational method of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2000, 2003, 2006) to massive theories with inelastic processes, applying it to the linear sigma model originally proposed by Gell-Mann and Levy (1960). The results are compared with other hadronic transport coefficient calculations in the literature, such as those by Prakash (1993), Fernandez-Fraile (2009), and Noronha-Hostler (2015). The paper enables future systematic comparisons between approximation schemes (RTA versus exact variational solutions) and provides a framework for calculating transport coefficients in effective models of strongly interacting matter, though the treatment of zero modes for bulk viscosity in multi-component systems remains an open problem.
