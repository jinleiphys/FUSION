# Finite temperature CFT results for all couplings: O(N) model in 2+1 dimensions

> A famous example of gauge/gravity duality is the result that the entropy density of strongly coupled ${\cal N}=4$ SYM in four dimensions for large N is exactly 3/4 of the Stefan-Boltzmann limit. In this work, I revisit the massless O(N) model in 2+1 dimensions, which is analytically solvable at finite temperature $T$ for all couplings $λ$ in the large N limit. I find that the entropy density monotonically decreases from the Stefan-Boltzmann limit at $λ=0$ to exactly 4/5 of the Stefan-Boltzmann limit at $λ=\infty$. Calculating the retarded energy-momentum tensor correlator in the scalar channel at $λ=\infty$, I find that it has two logarithmic branch cuts originating at $ω=\pm 4 T \ln \frac{1+\sqrt{5}}{2}$, but no singularities in the whole complex frequency plane. I show that the ratio 4/5 and the location of the branch points both are universal within a large class of bosonic CFTs in 2+1 dimensions.

## Key claim

The entropy density of the massless O(N) model in 2+1 dimensions in the large N limit monotonically decreases from the Stefan-Boltzmann limit at zero coupling to exactly 4/5 of that limit at infinite coupling, and this ratio 4/5 is universal within a large class of bosonic CFTs in 2+1 dimensions. The retarded energy-momentum tensor correlator in the scalar channel at infinite coupling has two logarithmic branch cuts originating at specific frequencies but no singularities in the whole complex frequency plane.

## Method

The author analytically solves the O(N) model in 2+1 dimensions at finite temperature in the large N limit by introducing auxiliary fields and performing the path integral via steepest descent. The entropy density is derived from the partition function using the stationarity condition (gap equation) for the thermal mass parameter. The retarded energy-momentum tensor correlator in the scalar channel is obtained by analytic continuation of the Euclidean correlator, with the frequency sum and momentum integrals evaluated in dimensional regularization.

## Key numbers

- Entropy density at zero coupling: \(s_{\rm free} = \frac{3 N T^2 \zeta(3)}{2 \pi}\)
- Entropy density at infinite coupling: \(\lim_{\lambda/T\rightarrow \infty}s = \frac{12 N T^2 \zeta(3)}{10 \pi} = \frac{4}{5} s_{\rm free}\)
- Location of logarithmic branch cuts in the retarded correlator at infinite coupling: \(\omega = \pm 4 T \ln \frac{1+\sqrt{5}}{2} \simeq \pm 1.92 T\)
- Thermal mass parameter at infinite coupling: \(\xi = 2 \ln \frac{1+\sqrt{5}}{2}\)
- Trace of the energy-momentum tensor: \(T^\mu_\mu = \frac{\xi^4 T^4 N}{16 \lambda}\), which vanishes at both zero and infinite coupling

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Sachdev (1993), Florkowski (1994), and Drummond (1997) who had obtained parts of the solution, and assembles them into a complete description of finite-temperature properties for all couplings. The result provides a field-theory analog to the famous 3/4 factor found for \({\cal N}=4\) SYM in 3+1 dimensions via gauge/gravity duality by Gubser (1998), but with a ratio of 4/5 instead of 3/4. The calculation enables future study of 1/N corrections and transport phenomena, and may allow testing the conjectured duality between the O(N) model and higher spin gravity in AdS\(_4\) by comparing the analytic structure of retarded correlators to black brane quasi-normal modes.
