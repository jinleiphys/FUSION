# Dilatons for Dense Hadronic Matter

> The idea that the explicit breaking of scale invariance by the trace anomaly of QCD can be rephrased as a spontaneous breaking has been recently exploited to capture the low-energy strong interaction dynamics of dense (and also hot) matter in terms of two dilaton fields, the "soft" (chi_s) and the "hard" (chi_h) fields, in the frame work of the hidden local gauge symmetry. In the Freund-Nambu model, the spontaneous symmetry breaking of scale symmetry is induced by an explicitly breaking term, while the spontaneous symmetry breaking is possible in the flat potential model which is scale symmetric. We discuss the interplay of the soft and hard dilatons using the spontaneously broken scale symmetry schemes and uncover a novel structure of dense matter hitherto unexplored.

# Dilatons for Dense Hadronic Matter

## Key claim

The explicit breaking of scale invariance by the QCD trace anomaly can be rephrased as spontaneous breaking using two dilaton fields, a "soft" dilaton (χ_s) whose mass goes to zero near chiral restoration, and a "hard" dilaton (χ_h) that remains massive across the chiral transition, but this construction depends on whether the spontaneous symmetry breaking is induced by an explicit breaking term (Freund-Nambu model) or arises from a scale-symmetric flat potential.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian for SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R chiral symmetry in the hidden local gauge symmetry (HLS) framework, incorporating pions, soft and hard dilatons, and vector mesons (ω and ρ) as gauge bosons. They analyze the Freund-Nambu two-scalar model and a scale-invariant flat potential model to study how spontaneous scale symmetry breaking can be realized, and examine the interplay between the two dilaton fields in dense matter. The homogeneous Wess-Zumino term is modified by a factor (χ_s/χ_h)^3 to maintain scale invariance.

## Key numbers

- The soft dilaton mass goes to zero as the chiral restoration point is approached, while the hard dilaton stays massive across the chiral transition.
- In the Freund-Nambu model, the masses take the form m²_ψ = f² φ₀² and m²_χ = τ φ₀², where m_χ goes to zero linearly as τ → 0.
- The "light" dilaton (LD) regime is defined as m_χ < 4π f_π ~ 1 GeV; the "heavy" dilaton (HD) regime is m_χ ≳ 1 GeV.
- With a heavy dilaton, skyrmion matter undergoes a phase transition at relatively low density to a half-skyrmion matter with vanishing quark condensate ⟨q̄q⟩* → 0 but with f_π* ~ ⟨χ⟩* ≠ 0.

## Context

This work extends the Brown-Rho scaling scenario (Brown and Rho, 1991), which originally anchored hadron property scaling on spontaneous breaking of scale invariance in medium, and connects it to the vector manifestation fixed point of HLS studied by Harada and Yamawaki. The two-dilaton structure enables a consistent treatment of the homogeneous Wess-Zumino term and reveals a half-skyrmion phase that the authors identify with the "quarkyonic phase" proposed by McLerran and Pisarski. The paper opens the question of whether the different density/temperature dependence of the two dilaton condensates arises from different manifestations of spontaneous scale symmetry breaking.
