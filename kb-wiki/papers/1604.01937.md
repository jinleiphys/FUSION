# Eta and kaon production in a chiral quark model

> We apply a coupled-channel formalism incorporating quasi-bound quark-model states to calculate pion scattering into eta N, K Lambda and K Sigma channels, as well eta p, eta n, K+Lambda, and K0Sigma+ photo-production processes. The meson-baryon and photon-baryon vertices are determined in a SU(3) version of the Cloudy Bag Model. Our model predicts sizable amplitudes in the P11, P13, P33 and S11 partial waves in agreement with the latest MAID isobar model and the recent partial-wave analyses of the Bonn-Gatchina group. We are able to give a quark-model explanation for the apparent resonance at 1685 MeV in the eta n channel.

## Key claim

The model provides a quark-model explanation for the apparent narrow resonance near 1685 MeV in the eta n channel, attributing it to a combination of the peculiar properties of the relativistic wave functions of the S11 resonances and the presence of the KSigma threshold, though the calculated strength is lower compared to experiment.

## Method

The authors applied a coupled-channel formalism incorporating quasi-bound quark-model states to calculate pion scattering into eta N, K Lambda, and K Sigma channels, as well as photoproduction processes for eta p, eta n, K+ Lambda, and K0 Sigma+. The meson-baryon and photon-baryon vertices were determined in an SU(3) extended version of the Cloudy Bag Model (CBM) including rho and omega mesons, with the K-matrix poles and mixing angles between quark-model states treated as adjustable parameters while keeping other model parameters fixed at ground-state calculation values.

## Key numbers

* Bag radius R_bag = 0.83 fm and pion decay constant f = 76 MeV were used, consistent with ground-state calculations
* The reliability of the calculation is estimated at 20-30% based on experience with the Cloudy Bag Model
* The mixing angle for the S11 partial wave that best reproduces the eta N to pi N branching ratio is close to -30 degrees
* The mixing angle in the P13 partial wave starts from -15 degrees, reaches -30 degrees in the region of the lower resonance, and returns to -15 degrees in the region of the upper resonance
* The eta meson decay constant f_eta is approximately 1.2 times f_pi, and the kaon decay constant f_K is approximately 1.2 times f_pi
* The rho meson coupling strength should be reduced by a factor of 3, consistent with f_rho approximately 200 MeV
* Neglecting recoil effects in electromagnetic calculations is estimated to contribute uncertainty of q^2/4m_N^2, not exceeding 20% below q^2 approximately 1 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous coupled-channel analyses of scattering and electro-production amplitudes in different partial waves (Golli et al., 2005, 2008, 2009, 2011, 2013), which studied the eta N channel in the S11 partial wave and the K Lambda channel. The results agree with the latest MAID isobar model and partial-wave analyses of the Bonn-Gatchina group, and the model offers a quark-model explanation for the narrow structure observed by the GRAAL Collaboration (Kuznetsov et al., 2007) at W approximately 1685 MeV in the gamma n to eta n reaction. The work enables a unified description of elastic and inelastic channels including eta and kaon production, complementing dynamical coupled-channel models from the Bonn-Julich group (Roenchen et al., 2013) and the Osaka-Tokyo-Argonne group (Kamano et al., 2013).
