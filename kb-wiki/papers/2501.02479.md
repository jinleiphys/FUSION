# Non-perturbative calculations of nuclear matter using in-medium similarity renormalization group

> The non-perturbative {\it ab initio} calculations of infinite nuclear matter using In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group (IMSRG) method is developed in this work, which enables calculations with chiral two and three-nucleon forces at N$^2$LO and N$^3$LO. Results from the many-body perturbation theory at different orders and coupled-cluster theory are also presented for comparison. It is shown that different many-body approaches lead to obvious discrepancies with a harder nuclear interaction for both pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter. This work provides a novel alternative infrastructure for future studies of dense nuclear matter and strongly-correlated many-body systems.

## Key claim

The paper develops the first application of the In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group (IMSRG) method to infinite nuclear matter, enabling non-perturbative calculations with chiral two- and three-nucleon forces. The authors show that different many-body approaches (IMSRG, coupled-cluster, many-body perturbation theory) produce obvious discrepancies for harder nuclear interactions in pure neutron matter and for symmetric nuclear matter even with soft interactions, contrary to the abstract's implication that discrepancies appear only for harder interactions in both systems.

## Method

The authors implement the Magnus IMSRG(2) scheme in a plane-wave momentum-space basis with a spherical Fermi sphere, using automatic partial-wave decomposition to handle chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions at N2LO and N3LO. Three-body forces are truncated at the normal-ordered two-body level. For comparison, they also compute the equation of state using many-body perturbation theory up to fourth order (MBPT4) and coupled-cluster theory with double excitations (CCD), all starting from the same Hartree-Fock reference state.

## Key numbers

* Pure neutron matter with NNLOopt (500 MeV cutoff): IMSRG(2) results are very close to CCD results up to 0.28 fm^-3; MBPT3 results are close to configuration-interaction Monte Carlo (CIMC) below 0.2 fm^-3
* Pure neutron matter with N3LO (700 MeV cutoff): significant discrepancies between MBPT3, MBPT4, and IMSRG(2) appear above 0.2 fm^-3, with MBPT4 relatively close to IMSRG(2) below 0.2 fm^-3
* Pure neutron matter with NNLOopt plus local 3N forces (Lambda=500 MeV): IMSRG(2) agrees well with CCD up to 0.30 fm^-3; MBPT3 is slightly above both
* Pure neutron matter with nonlocal 3N regulator (500 MeV): equation of state is considerably harder than with local regulators at higher densities
* Symmetric nuclear matter with NNLOopt plus local 3N forces (Lambda=500 MeV): MBPT3 is significantly different from non-perturbative calculations above saturation density; IMSRG(2) lies above CCD with differences increasing at higher densities

## Context

This work extends the IMSRG method, previously applied to finite nuclei (Heinen and Hergert, 2016), to infinite nuclear matter for the first time. It provides a novel non-perturbative alternative to existing approaches such as coupled-cluster theory (Baardsen et al., 2013) and many-body perturbation theory (MBPT2-MBPT4). The results enable future studies of dense nuclear matter and neutron star equations of state, particularly at densities where perturbative methods fail, and the authors identify IMSRG(3) as the next step to handle harder interactions and short-range correlations at higher densities.
