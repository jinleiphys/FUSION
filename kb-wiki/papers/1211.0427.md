# Implications for compact stars of a soft nuclear equation of state from heavy-ion data

> We study the implications on compact star properties of a soft nuclear equation of state determined from kaon production at subthreshold energies in heavy-ion collisions. On one hand, we apply these results to study radii and moments of inertia of light neutron stars. Heavy-ion data provides constraints on nuclear matter at densities relevant for those stars and, in particular, to the density dependence of the symmetry energy of nuclear matter. On the other hand, we derive a limit for the highest allowed neutron star mass of three solar masses. For that purpose, we use the information on the nucleon potential obtained from the analysis of the heavy-ion data combined with causality on the nuclear equation of state.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a soft nuclear equation of state (EoS) with compression modulus K = 200 MeV, as inferred from subthreshold kaon production in heavy-ion collisions at GSI, is compatible with neutron star observations, but the highest allowed neutron star mass is limited to about three solar masses when causality constraints are applied at densities of 2-3 times nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The authors used a Skyrme-type parametrization of the nuclear EoS fitted to reproduce the soft EoS from the KaoS experiment (K = 200 MeV) and varied the symmetry energy parameters (S0 between 28 and 32 MeV, density dependence exponent gamma between 0.5 and 1.1). They computed non-rotating neutron star configurations for masses of 1.25 solar masses and in the range 1.1-1.6 solar masses, including inner and outer crusts. For the maximum mass limit, they applied the Rhoades-Ruffini method: the known soft EoS from KaoS was used up to a critical density n_crit between 2 and 3 times nuclear saturation density, above which the stiffest possible EoS allowed by causality was attached.

## Key numbers

- Stiffness parameter K = 200 MeV characterizes the soft nuclear EoS from KaoS data (from transport model analysis of kaon multiplicity ratios)
- For a 1.25 solar mass neutron star with K ~ 200 MeV, varying the symmetry energy slope gives radius differences Delta R ~ 1-1.5 km and moment of inertia differences Delta I ~ (2 x 10^43 - 2.5 x 10^43) g cm^2
- Central densities of these 1.25 solar mass stars are less than or equal to 3.4 times nuclear saturation density n0 (n0 ~ 0.17 fm^-3)
- For n_crit = 2 n0, the maximum possible neutron star mass reaches up to 3 solar masses
- For n_crit = 3 n0, the maximum possible mass decreases to about 2.4 solar masses
- The pulsar PSR J1748-2021 with mass 2.7 solar masses is marginally compatible with the soft EoS if the transition to the stiffest EoS occurs at n_crit ~ 2.2-2.5 n0

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the KaoS experiment results (Sturm 2001) to astrophysical applications, connecting heavy-ion data to neutron star properties. It builds on the Rhoades-Ruffini method (Rhoades and Ruffini 1974) for maximum mass limits and prior estimates by Kalogera and Baym (1996) and Hartle (1978) that gave M_high ~ 2.9 solar masses for epsilon_crit = 2 epsilon0. The results are relevant for future missions like LOFT (Feroci 2012) and for interpreting the massive pulsar PSR J1748-2021 (Freire 2008) and the 1.97 solar mass pulsar PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest 2010).
