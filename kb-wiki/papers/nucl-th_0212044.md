# NN Interaction in Chiral Constituent Quark Models

> We review the actual state in the description of the NN interaction by means of chiral constituent quark models. We present a series of relevant features that are nicely explained within the quark model framework.

# NN Interaction in Chiral Constituent Quark Models

## Key claim
The chiral constituent quark model, which combines one-gluon exchange with Goldstone-boson exchanges (pion and sigma), can reproduce NN scattering and bound state data, but the short-range repulsion in S-waves arises primarily from quark antisymmetry effects on the one-pion exchange interaction rather than from configuration mixing driven by one-gluon exchange as in earlier models.

## Method
The authors review the chiral constituent quark model framework, which incorporates one-gluon exchange (OGE), one-pion exchange (OPE), and one-sigma exchange (OSE) interactions at the quark level, together with a linear confining potential. They analyze the energy differences between the [6] and [42] orbital symmetry configurations for six-quark states describing two nucleons, calculating the contributions from each interaction term. They also compute NN phase shifts, NDelta potentials, and baryon spectra using a Faddeev approach with parameters fixed from NN data.

- Constituent quark mass: 313 MeV; harmonic oscillator parameter b = 0.518 fm; OGE coupling alpha_s = 0.485; confinement strength a_c = 67.0 MeV fm^-1; chiral coupling alpha_ch = 0.0269; sigma mass m_sigma = 3.42 fm^-1; pion mass m_pi = 0.7 fm^-1; cutoff Lambda = 4.2 fm^-1
- For the chiral constituent quark model parameters, the OGE energy difference between [42] and [6] configurations is Delta_OGE = -140.99 MeV, the harmonic oscillator energy difference is Delta_ho = 309.10 MeV, the OPE contribution is Delta_OPE = -68.62 MeV, and the OSE contribution is Delta_OSE = 133.05 MeV
- The total interaction energy difference between [42] and [6] configurations is Delta = -76.56 MeV, much smaller than the harmonic oscillator energy difference of 309.10 MeV
- The total interaction energy for the [6] configuration is 455.72 MeV and for the [42] configuration is 379.16 MeV
- Ratio of coupling constants: |g_pi NN*(1440)/g_pi NN| = 0.25; |g_sigma NN*(1440)/g_sigma NN| = 0.47
- Ratio of ratios: g_pi NN*(1440)/g_pi NN = 0.53 * g_sigma NN*(1440)/g_sigma NN

## Context
This review extends the quark-model approach to the NN interaction pioneered by Isgur and Karl (1980s) and the hybrid quark models of Oka and Yazaki (1980s) that used OGE for short-range repulsion and meson exchanges for longer ranges. The chiral constituent quark model, developed by Fernandez, Valcarce, and Gonzalez (1990s), relates the constituent quark mass to chiral symmetry breaking and incorporates Goldstone boson exchanges. The work enables parameter-free extension to other baryon-baryon systems and calculation of meson-baryon-baryon coupling constants, while also connecting to baryon spectroscopy. The model contradicts earlier approaches that required different sigma coupling constants for different partial waves, as the OSE here uses the same parameters fixed from the pi-qq coupling constant.
