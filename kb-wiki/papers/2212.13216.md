# Emulating \emph{ab initio} computations of infinite nucleonic matter

> We construct efficient emulators for the \emph{ab initio} computation of the infinite nuclear matter equation of state. These emulators are based on the subspace-projected coupled-cluster method for which we here develop a new algorithm called small-batch voting to eliminate spurious states that might appear when emulating quantum many-body methods based on a non-Hermitian Hamiltonian. The efficiency and accuracy of these emulators facilitate a rigorous statistical analysis within which we explore nuclear matter predictions for $> 10^6$ different parametrizations of a chiral interaction model with explicit $Δ$-isobars at next-to-next-to leading order. Constrained by nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts and bound-state observables of light nuclei up to \nuc{4}{He}, we use history matching to identify non-implausible domains for the low-energy coupling constants of the chiral interaction. Within these domains we perform a Bayesian analysis using sampling/importance resampling with different likelihood calibrations and study correlations between interaction parameters, calibration observables in light nuclei, and nuclear matter saturation properties.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that subspace-projected coupled-cluster (SPCC) emulators can accurately reproduce full-space coupled-cluster calculations of infinite nuclear matter equations of state, but only after applying a newly developed "small-batch voting" algorithm to eliminate spurious states that arise from the non-Hermitian nature of the coupled-cluster Hamiltonian and the high level density in infinite matter.

## Method

The authors construct SPCC emulators for pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter at five densities (0.12 to 0.20 fm⁻³) using coupled-cluster doubles (CCD) calculations with 66 neutrons and 132 nucleons respectively, on a discrete momentum-space lattice with periodic boundary conditions. They develop a small-batch voting algorithm that randomly removes subsets of the 64 training vectors, solves the reduced generalized eigenvalue problem repeatedly, and identifies the physical ground state as the eigenvector that appears most consistently across batches. The energy per particle at arbitrary densities is obtained via Gaussian process interpolation with a radial basis function kernel and correlation length 0.25 fm⁻³, and saturation properties are extracted from derivatives of the interpolated equation of state.

## Key numbers

- 64 subspace vectors used to construct SPCC emulators for nuclear matter
- 5 densities used for emulation: 0.12, 0.14, 0.16, 0.18, 0.20 fm⁻³
- Standard deviation of relative emulator error: 0.002% for pure neutron matter, 0.8% for symmetric nuclear matter after small-batch voting
- Computational speedup: six orders of magnitude for symmetric nuclear matter with small-batch voting, more than eight orders of magnitude for pure neutron matter without small-batch voting
- 1.7 × 10⁶ different chiral interaction parametrizations explored via history matching
- 17 low-energy coupling constants in the Δ-full chiral effective field theory at next-to-next-to-leading order with cutoff Λ = 394 MeV
- 132 nucleons used for symmetric nuclear matter, 66 neutrons for pure neutron matter
- 50 random parametrizations used for validation at each of 5 densities (250 total validation points)
- Small-batch voting parameters: N_batch = 30, k = 100 batches, relative tolerance ε = 0.02
- Gaussian process correlation length: 0.25 fm⁻³ for both pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter

## Context

This work extends the eigenvector continuation and subspace-projected coupled-cluster methods previously applied to finite nuclei like ¹⁶O (Ekström 2019, Hu 2021) to the more challenging case of infinite nuclear matter, where increased level density and non-Hermiticity produce spurious states. The emulators enable a rigorous statistical analysis using history matching (Vernon 2010, 2014, 2018) and Bayesian sampling/importance resampling, allowing exploration of over 10⁶ chiral interaction parametrizations that would be computationally prohibitive with full coupled-cluster calculations. The approach enables systematic uncertainty quantification for nuclear matter saturation properties and correlations with few-nucleon observables, with detailed posterior predictive distributions presented in a companion paper (Jiang 2022).
