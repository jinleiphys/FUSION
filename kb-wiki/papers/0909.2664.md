# Nuclear modification factor in p+pb collisions at LHC and saturation

> We provide predictions for the nuclear modification factor R_{pA} for pions and direct photon production in p+A collisions at LHC energy at midrapidity within different saturation models fitted to HERA data. In our approach we have no free parameters to adjust and all model parameters are fitted to other reactions. Our approach gives a rather good description of PHENIX data for R_{pA} for pions. We show that, in various saturation models, the pion Cronin enhancement is replaced by a moderate suppression at LHC energy at midrapidity due to gluon shadowing effects. However, Cronin enhancement of direct photons can survive at LHC energy in models with a larger saturation scale. We show that both shadowing and saturation effects are important at LHC in p+A collisions and give rise to a rather sizable effect in the nuclear modification factor R_{pA}. Therefore, a precise measurement of p+A collisions at LHC is crucial in order to understand the underlying dynamics of heavy ion collisions.

# Nuclear modification factor in p+Pb collisions at LHC and saturation

## Key claim
The paper predicts that at LHC midrapidity in p+Pb collisions, the Cronin enhancement observed at RHIC for pions will be replaced by a moderate suppression due to gluon shadowing effects, while direct photon Cronin enhancement may survive in models with a larger saturation scale. Both shadowing and saturation effects are shown to be important and produce a sizable effect in the nuclear modification factor R_{pA}.

## Method
The invariant cross-section for hadron and direct photon production in p+A and p+p collisions was calculated using the light-cone color-dipole factorization scheme. Two different saturation models fitted to HERA deep inelastic scattering data were employed: the phenomenological GBW model and the CGC model based on the non-linear small-x Balitsky-Kovchegov equation. Nuclear shadowing effects were incorporated within the same color-dipole formulation to avoid double counting, with all model parameters fixed from other reactions and no free parameters adjusted.

## Key numbers
- Pion R_{pA} at LHC midrapidity in minimum bias p+Pb collisions shows moderate suppression (replacing Cronin enhancement) in both GBW and CGC models, with the suppression stronger in the CGC model than in the GBW model
- Direct photon R_{pA}^{gamma} at LHC midrapidity shows Cronin enhancement that survives within the GBW color-dipole model (which has a bigger saturation scale) even after inclusion of nuclear shadowing suppression effects
- The saturation scale in the phenomenological GBW model is bigger than in the CGC model (referenced to Fig. 2 in Ref. RS)
- Within the CGC model, both pion and direct photon enhancement at RHIC will be replaced by suppression at LHC

## Context
This work extends the color-dipole approach previously applied to RHIC data (Rezaeian, Kopeliovich, Schafer, 2009) to make predictions for LHC energies. The predictions differ from those of Kharzeev et al. (2009) based on the CGC scenario, who showed a marked suppression for pions at midrapidity at LHC that is stronger than the present prediction. The results indicate that precise measurement of p+A collisions at LHC is crucial for understanding the underlying dynamics of heavy ion collisions, particularly for disentangling initial-state effects from jet-quenching in nuclear collisions.
