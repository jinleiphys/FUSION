# How Bayesian methods can improve $R$-matrix analyses of data: the example of the $dt$ Reaction

> The $^3{\rm H}(d,n)^4{\rm He}$ reaction is of significant interest in nuclear astrophysics and nuclear applications. It is an important, early step in big-bang nucleosynthesis and a key process in nuclear fusion reactors. We use one- and two-level $R$-matrix approximations to analyze data on the cross section for this reaction at center-of-mass energies below 215 keV. We critically examine the data sets using a Bayesian statistical model that allows for both common-mode and additional point-to-point uncertainties. We use Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling to evaluate this $R$-matrix-plus-statistical model and find two-level $R$-matrix results that are stable with respect to variations in the channel radii. The $S$ factor at 40 keV evaluates to $25.36(19)$ MeV b (68% credibility interval). We discuss our Bayesian analysis in detail and provide guidance for future applications of Bayesian methods to $R$-matrix analyses. We also discuss possible paths to further reduction of the $S$-factor uncertainty.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Bayesian methods can improve $R$-matrix analyses of the $^3{\rm H}(d,n)^4{\rm He}$ reaction by explicitly accounting for both common-mode and point-to-point uncertainties, but the authors caution that the channel radius should not be treated as a sampled parameter because it acts as a regulator rather than a physical parameter, and that two-level $R$-matrix results are stable with respect to variations in channel radii only when this distinction is respected.

## Method

The authors used one- and two-level $R$-matrix approximations to analyze cross-section data for the $^3{\rm H}(d,n)^4{\rm He}$ reaction at center-of-mass energies below 215 keV, employing a Bayesian statistical model that includes normalization factors and additional fractional point-to-point uncertainties for each of five experimental data sets. They performed Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling using the \texttt{emcee} package with a mixture of differential evolution and snooker proposals to evaluate the posterior distribution of $R$-matrix parameters (level energies, reduced width amplitudes, electron screening potential) and statistical nuisance parameters, while treating channel radii as fixed rather than sampled parameters.

## Key numbers

* $S$ factor at 40 keV: $25.36(19)$ MeV b (68% credibility interval)
* Energy range of analyzed data: 5 keV to 250 keV center-of-mass
* The broad resonance in the $^3{\rm H}(d,n)^4{\rm He}$ reaction lies at approximately $E \approx 90$ keV
* The $Q$ value of the reaction is 17.6 MeV
* Previous $S$-factor evaluation by Bosch (1993): $25.87 \pm 0.49$ MeV b at 40 keV
* Electron screening potential $U_e$ estimated to be approximately 20-40 eV for positive hydrogen ions incident on diatomic hydrogen gas
* Five experimental data sets used: Conner (1952) with 43 points, Arnold (1953) with 53 points, Kobzev (1966) with 45 points, Jarmie (1984) with 17 points, and Brown (1987) with 8 points

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian $R$-matrix methodology of de Souza (2019) by identifying and eliminating a redundancy in the parameterization (the $E_0$–$E_B$ correlation), demonstrating that channel radii should not be treated as sampled parameters, and investigating the limitations of the single-level approximation by including a $J^\pi = 1/2^+$ background level and a second $3/2^+$ level. The analysis improves upon previous $R$-matrix evaluations by Hale (1987) and Bosch (1992, 1993), and the results enable more reliable extrapolation of the $dt$ reaction cross section to energies relevant for big-bang nucleosynthesis and nuclear fusion reactor design, while the authors identify the Kobzev (1966) data set as having a systematic problem that cannot be remedied without additional knowledge.
