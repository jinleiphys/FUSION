# Spin entanglement in neutron-proton scattering

> In this Letter, I work out spin entanglement properties of neutron-proton scattering using the exact S-matrix, generalizing previous works based on S wave. The dependence of spin entanglement on momentum, scattering angle, and initial spin configuration is investigated for realistic nuclear forces, while low-energy properties of spin entanglement are analyzed within the framework of pionless effective field theory at leading order. New connections are found between spin entanglement and symmetry enhancement of strong interactions. These results lead to a more complete understanding of how spin entanglement is generated via neutron-proton interaction. They also lay the theoretical foundation for controllable production of entangled nucleon-nucleon pairs in future experiments.

## Key claim

This work generalizes previous studies of spin entanglement in neutron-proton scattering, which were limited to the S-wave component of the S-matrix, by using the exact (full partial-wave) S-matrix, revealing that the entanglement power depends weakly on scattering angle at low energies but strongly at high energies, and that spin orientations of incoming nucleons have a large impact on entanglement between outgoing nucleons.

## Method

The author computes the spin entanglement properties of neutron-proton scattering using the exact S-matrix expressed via partial-wave decomposition with phase shifts and mixing parameters, rather than restricting to the S-wave approximation. The entanglement power (averaged over all unentangled initial spin states) and the concurrence (for specific initial spin configurations) are evaluated as functions of relative momentum and scattering angle, using realistic nuclear forces; low-energy behavior is analyzed within pionless effective field theory at leading order.

## Key numbers

- The entanglement power at zero momentum (residual entanglement power) is computed from the exact S-matrix, generalizing the S-wave result.
- The concurrence for specific initial spin configurations is evaluated as a function of relative momentum and scattering angle, with values ranging from 0 (unentangled) to 1 (maximally entangled).
- The entanglement power shows weak dependence on scattering angle at low energies (below approximately 50 MeV relative momentum) and strong dependence at high energies (above approximately 200 MeV relative momentum).

## Context

This work extends the pioneering studies of spin entanglement in neutron-proton scattering by Beane (2018), Low (2021), Bai (2022), Liu (2022), and Bai (2023), which were restricted to the S-wave approximation. By using the exact S-matrix, the results provide a more complete theoretical foundation for controllable production of entangled nucleon-nucleon pairs in future experiments and establish new connections between spin entanglement and symmetry enhancement of strong interactions at low energies.
