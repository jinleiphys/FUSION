# Folding model analysis of proton radioactivity of spherical proton emitters

> Half lives of the decays of spherical nuclei away from proton drip line by proton emissions are estimated theoretically. The quantum mechanical tunneling probability is calculated within the WKB approximation. Microscopic proton-nucleus interaction potentials are obtained by single folding the densities of the daughter nuclei with M3Y effective interaction supplemented by a zero-range pseudo-potential for exchange along with the density dependence. Strengths of the M3Y interaction are extracted by fitting its matrix elements in an oscillator basis to those elements of the G-matrix obtained with the Reid-Elliott soft-core nucleon-nucleon interaction. Parameters of the density dependence are obtained from the nuclear matter calculations. Spherical charge distributions are used for calculating the Coulomb interaction potentials. These calculations provide reasonable estimates for the observed proton radioactivity lifetimes of proton rich nuclei for proton emissions from 26 ground and isomeric states of spherical proton emitters.

# Folding model analysis of proton radioactivity of spherical proton emitters

## Key claim

The DDM3Y effective interaction, when used in a single folding model to generate microscopic proton-nucleus potentials, provides estimates for proton radioactivity half-lives that are in good agreement with experimental data for 26 ground and isomeric states of spherical proton emitters, with accuracy comparable to the unified fission model (UFM) of Balasubramaniam and Arunachalam (2005). The calculations rely on experimentally measured Q-values and the agreement is sensitive to uncertainties in those Q-values.

## Method

The authors calculated proton decay half-lives using the WKB approximation for quantum mechanical tunneling through the barrier formed by the sum of nuclear, Coulomb, and centrifugal potentials. The nuclear potential was obtained by single folding the daughter nucleus density (a spherically symmetric Fermi distribution) with the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, whose density dependence parameters (C = 2.07, β = 1.624 fm²) were determined from nuclear matter saturation properties. The Coulomb potential assumed a spherical charge distribution, and the zero-point vibration energy was taken proportional to the Q-value following the systematics of Poenaru et al. (1986). Calculations were also repeated using the global optical model potential (GOMP) for protons of Koning and Delaroche (2003) and with the inclusion of the Lane isovector potential.

## Key numbers

* Half-lives for 26 spherical proton emitters (ground and isomeric states) were calculated; experimental log₁₀T(s) values range from -5.409 (¹⁴⁵Tm) to 2.049 (¹⁰⁵Sb).
* For ¹⁰⁵Sb (l=2, Q=0.491 MeV): experimental log₁₀T = 2.049 s; present calculation gives 1.97(46) s; UFM gives 2.085 s.
* For ¹⁴⁵Tm (l=5, Q=1.753 MeV): experimental log₁₀T = -5.409 s; present calculation gives -5.14(6) s; UFM gives -5.170 s.
* For ¹⁸⁵Bi (l=0, Q=1.624 MeV): experimental log₁₀T = -4.229 s; present calculation gives -5.44(13) s; UFM gives -5.184 s.
* The density dependence parameter β = 1.624 fm² was used; changing to β = 1.668 fm² caused insignificant changes in the second decimal places of logarithmic half-lives.
* The M3Y interaction strengths are: 7999 MeV for the 4s Yukawa term, -2134 MeV for the 2.5s Yukawa term, and J₀₀(E) = -276(1 - 0.005E/A_p) MeV·fm³ for the zero-range exchange term.
* The density dependence function is g(ρ,E) = C(1 - β(E)ρ^(2/3)) with C = 2.07 and β = 1.624 fm².

## Context

This work extends the application of the DDM3Y effective interaction, previously used successfully for cluster radioactivity (Basu, 2003), α and heavy-ion scattering (Chaudhuri, 1986), nuclear matter calculations (Basu, 2004), and proton elastic scattering (Gupta and Basu, 2005), to the domain of proton radioactivity. It provides an alternative to the Saxon-Woods potential approach of Buck, Merchant and Perez (1992) and the unified fission model of Balasubramaniam and Arunachalam (2005), using a microscopically grounded potential. The results enable further testing of the DDM3Y interaction across diverse nuclear phenomena and suggest that the same interaction framework can describe proton emission, cluster emission, and scattering consistently.
