# Large Nuclear shape transition at finite temperature in a relativistic mean field approach

> The relativistic Hartree-BCS theory is applied to study the temperature dependence of nuclear shape and pairing gap for $^{166}Er$ and $^{170}Er$. For both the nuclei, we find that as temperature increases the pairing gap vanishes leading to phase transition from superfluid to normal phase as is observed in nonrelativistic calculation. The deformation evolves from prolate shapes to spherical shapes at $T\sim 2.7$ MeV. Comparison of our results for heat capacity with the ones obtained in the non-relativistic mean field framework indicates that in the relativistic mean field theory the shape transition occurs at a temperature about 0.9 MeV higher and is relatively weaker. The effect of thermal shape fluctuations on the temperature dependence of deformation is also studied. Relevant results for the level density parameter are further presented. PACS numbers: 21.10.Ma, 21.60.-n, 27.70.+q

## Key claim

The relativistic Hartree-BCS theory predicts that the prolate-to-spherical shape transition in 166Er and 170Er occurs at T ∼ 2.7 MeV in the mean-field approximation, which is about 0.9 MeV higher than in nonrelativistic calculations, and that this transition is relatively weaker (a broad bump in heat capacity rather than a sharp peak). However, when thermal shape fluctuations are included, the transition temperature drops to ∼ 1.6 MeV and becomes somewhat sharper.

## Method

The authors applied the relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory with nonlinear σ-ω-ρ meson couplings, using the NLSH parameter set, and incorporated pairing via the constant-gap BCS approximation. They solved the coupled Dirac and Klein-Gordon equations self-consistently for axially deformed 166Er and 170Er at finite temperatures, minimizing the free energy to obtain the most probable quadrupole deformation β₂ and pairing gaps Δp, Δn. To include thermal fluctuations, they performed constrained calculations of the free energy as a function of β₂ and computed average observables using a Boltzmann-weighted integral over β₂.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state deformation β₂ ∼ 0.3 for both 166Er and 170Er, remaining close to this value for T < 1.5 MeV.
- Neutron pairing gaps vanish at T ∼ 0.4 MeV; proton pairing gaps vanish at T ∼ 0.45 MeV for both nuclei.
- In the mean-field approximation, the prolate-to-spherical shape transition occurs at T ∼ 2.7 MeV.
- The shape transition temperature in the RMF model is about 0.9 MeV higher than in nonrelativistic calculations (which give T ∼ 1.8 MeV).
- With thermal fluctuations included, the shape transition temperature drops to ∼ 1.6 MeV.
- The pairing strength G is taken as 29/A for protons and 21/A for neutrons.
- At T = 2.0 MeV for 170Er, the free energy surface has minima at β₂ = −0.16 and 0.23, with a free energy difference ΔF⁰ = 0.70 MeV (ΔF/T = 0.35).
- At the highest temperature studied (T ∼ 3 MeV), the occupancy of positive-energy single-particle states is n⁺/A = 0.018.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field approach, previously successful for ground-state properties of nuclei across the periodic table, to finite-temperature phenomena. It contrasts with nonrelativistic calculations using the pairing-plus-quadrupole (P+Q) model by Goodman (1981) and the Landau theory of phase transitions by Alhassid et al. (1991), which predict a strong second-order shape transition at T ∼ 1.8 MeV. The results enable comparison with experimental data on giant dipole resonances built on excited states, as studied by Snover (1986) and Gaardhoje (1992), and provide a framework for calculating level density parameters relevant to nuclear reactions.
