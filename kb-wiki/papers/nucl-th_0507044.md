# Analyzing eta' photoproduction data on the proton at energies of 1.5--2.3 GeV

> The recent high-precision data for the reaction $γp\to pη'$ at photon energies in the range 1.5--2.3 GeV obtained by the CLAS collaboration at the Jefferson Laboratory have been analyzed within an extended version of the photoproduction model developed previously by the authors based on a relativistic meson-exchange model of hadronic interactions [Phys. Rev. C \textbf{69}, 065212 (2004)]. The $η'$ photoproduction can be described quite well over the entire energy range of available data by considering $S_{11}$, $P_{11}$, $P_{13}$, and $D_{13}$ resonances, in addition to the $t$-channel mesonic currents. The observed angular distribution is due to the interference between the $t$-channel and the nucleon $s$- and $u$-channel resonance contributions. The $j=3/2$ resonances are required to reproduce some of the details of the measured angular distribution. For the resonances considered, our analysis yields mass values compatible with those advocated by the Particle Data Group. We emphasize, however, that cross-section data alone are unable to pin down the resonance parameters and it is shown that the beam and/or target asymmetries impose more stringent constraints on these parameter values. It is found that the nucleonic current is relatively small and that the $NNη^\prime$ coupling constant is not expected to be much larger than 2.

## Key claim
The reaction $\gamma p \to p\eta'$ can be described well over the entire energy range of 1.5–2.3 GeV by including $S_{11}$, $P_{11}$, $P_{13}$, and $D_{13}$ resonances alongside $t$-channel mesonic currents, but cross-section data alone cannot uniquely determine resonance parameters; beam and/or target asymmetries impose more stringent constraints.

## Method
The authors extended their relativistic meson-exchange model (Nakayama and Haberzettl, 2004) to include energy-dependent resonance widths and spin-3/2 resonances. They fitted the model to high-precision differential cross-section data from the CLAS collaboration for $\gamma p \to p\eta'$, adding resonances one by one until a reasonable fit ($\chi^2/N \leq 1.3$) was achieved. The model incorporates $t$-channel $\rho$ and $\omega$ exchange, nucleon $s$- and $u$-channel contributions, and resonance currents with form factors and a phenomenological contact current to ensure gauge invariance.

## Key numbers
- Photon energy range: 1.5–2.3 GeV (CLAS data).
- Fit quality criterion: $\chi^2/N \leq 1.3$; best fits achieved $\chi^2/N = 1.19$ and $1.04$ for different parameter sets.
- $NN\eta'$ coupling constant $g_{NN\eta'}$ fitted to values between 0.25 and 1.33 across different solution sets, with the conclusion it is not expected to be much larger than 2.
- Resonance masses fitted: e.g., $S_{11}$ at 1958 MeV (Table I), $P_{11}$ at 2104 MeV (Table I), $P_{13}$ at 1885 MeV (Table I), $D_{13}$ at 1823 MeV (Table I); all compatible with Particle Data Group values.
- $t$-channel cutoff parameter $\Lambda_v$ fitted to values of 1275, 1308, and 1515 MeV in different solution sets.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Nakayama and Haberzettl, 2004) of SAPHIR data, which had larger error bars and did not require spin-3/2 resonances. The new high-precision CLAS data (2005) supersede the SAPHIR data and reveal features requiring $j=3/2$ resonances. The analysis enables future extraction of the $NN\eta'$ coupling constant, relevant to the flavor-singlet Goldberger–Treiman relation and the nucleon-spin crisis, but emphasizes that polarization observables (beam and target asymmetries) are needed to constrain resonance parameters beyond what cross sections alone provide.
