# Neutrino propagation in dense hadronic matter

> Neutrino propagation in protoneutron stars requires the knowledge of the composition as well as the dynamical response function of dense hadronic matter. Matter at very high densities is probably composed of other particles than nucleons and little is known on the Fermi liquid properties of hadronic multicomponent systems. We will discuss the effects that the presence of $Λ$ hyperons might have on the response and, in particular, on its influence on the thermodynamical stability of the system and the mean free path of neutrinos in dense matter.

# Neutrino propagation in dense hadronic matter

## Key claim

The presence of $\Lambda$ hyperons in dense hadronic matter can suppress ferromagnetic instabilities and thereby modify the neutrino mean free path, but the effect depends strongly on the choice of hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon Skyrme-type interactions, which are poorly constrained by existing data.

## Method

The authors apply Landau Fermi Liquid Theory to a multicomponent system of neutrons, protons, and $\Lambda$ hyperons, using Skyrme-type effective interactions (SLy10 for nucleon-nucleon, LN1 for hyperon-nucleon, and four variants LL0-LL3 for hyperon-hyperon). They compute the $\beta$-stable composition of protoneutron star matter at finite temperature, then solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the random phase approximation to obtain the dynamical response functions and structure factors, from which the neutrino mean free path is derived via the standard weak-interaction integral.

## Key numbers

- Central densities in protoneutron stars reach $2-3 \rho_0$ (where $\rho_0$ is saturation density) and temperatures up to $50$ MeV.
- For the LL3 hyperon-hyperon interaction, a ferromagnetic transition occurs at $\rho \sim 0.74$ fm$^{-3}$.
- Structure functions are computed at a fixed transferred momentum of $q=30$ MeV.
- Neutrino mean free path calculations use neutrino energy $E_\nu = 3T$ MeV, at temperatures $T=20$ MeV and $T=40$ MeV.
- A spin instability is observed for matter composed of nucleons and electrons at densities above $\rho \sim 0.5$ fm$^{-3}$; the presence of $\Lambda$'s pushes this instability to higher densities.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutrino mean free paths in protoneutron stars, which were mainly performed within relativistic mean field approaches (Reddy, 1997), by applying non-relativistic Fermi Liquid Theory with Skyrme forces. The inclusion of $\Lambda$ hyperons and their interactions (using parameterizations from Lanskoy, 1997 and Lanskoy, 1998) addresses the open question of how multicomponent hadronic matter affects neutrino transport. The results enable more realistic cooling simulations of protoneutron stars and supernova evolution, but also highlight the need for better experimental constraints on hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions.
