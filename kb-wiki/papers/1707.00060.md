# Probing the hot and dense nuclear matter with $K^*,\bar{K}^*$ vector mesons

> We investigate probing the hot and dense nuclear matter with strange vector mesons ($K^*, \bar{K}^*$). Our analysis is based on PHSD which incorporates partonic and hadronic dof and describes the full dynamics of HICs. This allows to study the $K^*$ and $\bar{K}^*$ meson formation from the QGP and the in-medium effects related to the modification of their properties during the propagation in dense and hot matter. We employ relativistic Breit-Wigner spectral functions for the $K^*,\bar{K}^*$ mesons with self-energies obtained from a G-matrix approach to study the role of in-medium effects on the $K^*$ and $\bar{K}^*$ meson dynamics in HIC from FAIR/NICA to LHC energies. According to our analysis most of the final $K^*/\bar{K}^*$s, that can be observed experimentally, are produced during the late hadronic phase and stem dominantly from the $K (\bar{K}) + π\to K^*(\bar{K}^*)$ formation channel. The amount of $K^*/\bar{K}^*$s originating from the QGP channel is comparatively small even at LHC energies and such $K^*/\bar{K}^*$s can hardly be reconstructed experimentally due to the rescattering of final pions and (anti-)kaons. This mirrors the results from our previous study on the strange vector-meson production in HICs at RHIC energies. The influence of the in-medium effects on the dynamics of the $K^*/\bar{K}^*$ is rather small since they are mostly produced at low baryon densities. Additional cuts on the shape of the observed signal and the range of the invariant mass region of the $K^*/\bar{K}^*$ also affect the final spectra. We demonstrate that the $K^*/\bar{K}^*$ in-medium effects are more visible at lower beam energy, e.g. FAIR/NICA and BES RHIC energies, where the production of $K^*/\bar{K}^*$s occurs at larger baryon densities. Finally, we present the experimental procedures to extract information on the in-medium masses and widths by fitting final mass spectra at LHC energies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that most experimentally observable \(K^*/\bar{K}^*\) vector mesons in heavy-ion collisions are produced during the late hadronic phase via \(K(\bar{K}) + \pi \to K^*(\bar{K}^*)\) annihilation, not directly from the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), even at LHC energies; the QGP-originating fraction is small and experimentally unreconstructable due to rescattering of decay products, and in-medium effects on spectral properties are modest at high energies but become more visible at lower beam energies (FAIR/NICA, BES RHIC) where production occurs at higher baryon densities.

## Method

The authors use the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which includes partonic and hadronic degrees of freedom and describes the full time evolution of heavy-ion collisions from initial nucleon-nucleon collisions through QGP formation, dynamical hadronization, and final hadronic interactions. They implement relativistic Breit-Wigner spectral functions for \(K^*\) and \(\bar{K}^*\) mesons with self-energies from a self-consistent coupled-channel G-matrix approach to model in-medium modifications of masses and widths, and simulate collisions from FAIR/NICA to LHC energies, comparing with ALICE data and applying experimental reconstruction procedures.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum pole mass of \(K^*\) and \(\bar{K}^*\): 892 MeV; vacuum decay width: 42 MeV.
- At LHC energy \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV in central Pb+Pb collisions, the \(K(\bar{K}) + \pi\) annihilation channel dominates \(K^*/\bar{K}^*\) production over QGP hadronization and string decay channels.
- For \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.675\) GeV (FAIR/NICA range), \(K^*/\bar{K}^*\) production probes baryon densities above normal nuclear matter density \(\rho_0\), unlike at 200 GeV where densities are much lower.
- The threshold energy for \(K^*\) creation in vacuum is approximately 0.633 GeV (\(M_K + M_\pi\)); at normal matter density \(\rho_0\), the kaon mass shift \(\Delta M(\rho) \approx 0.06 M_K\) raises this threshold.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous PHSD study of \(K^*/\bar{K}^*\) production at RHIC energies (Ilner, 2016) to a wider energy range from FAIR/NICA to LHC, and builds on earlier transport studies with UrQMD (Bleicher, 2002; Vogel, 2010) and EPOS3 (Knospe, 2015) for Pb+Pb collisions at 17.3 GeV and 2.76 TeV, respectively. The in-medium spectral functions are based on a G-matrix approach previously applied to kaon and anti-kaon production (Lutz, 1997; Ramos, 1999; Tolos, 2000, 2006, 2008). The results enable quantitative predictions for future experiments at FAIR and NICA, and provide experimental procedures to extract in-medium masses and widths from final mass spectra at LHC energies.
