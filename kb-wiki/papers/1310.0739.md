# Transverse charge and magnetization densities in the nucleon's chiral periphery

> In the light-front description of nucleon structure the electromagnetic form factors are expressed in terms of frame-independent transverse densities of charge and magnetization. Recent work has studied the transverse densities at peripheral distances b = O(M_pi^{-1}), where they are governed by universal chiral dynamics and can be computed in a model-independent manner. Of particular interest is the comparison of the peripheral charge and magnetization densities. We summarize (a) their interpretation as spin-independent and -dependent current matrix elements; (b) the leading-order chiral effective field theory results; (c) their mechanical interpretation in the light-front formulation; (d) the large-N_c limit of QCD and the role of Delta intermediate states; (e) the connection with generalized parton distributions and peripheral high-energy scattering processes.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the peripheral transverse charge and magnetization densities of the nucleon at distances b = O(M_π⁻¹) can be computed model-independently using chiral effective field theory, and that the leading-order result shows the spin-dependent density is bounded by the spin-independent one (|ρ̃₂^V(b)| < ρ₁^V(b)), though this inequality is specific to the leading-order approximation and can be modified by higher-order corrections or by inclusion of Δ intermediate states as required by the large-N_c limit of QCD.

## Method

The authors compute the isovector transverse charge and magnetization densities using a dispersive representation, where the densities at large distances b = O(M_π⁻¹) are expressed as integrals over the spectral functions on the two-pion cut. The spectral functions are evaluated at leading order in relativistic chiral effective field theory, with the pion-nucleon vertices from the leading-order chiral Lagrangian. The resulting densities are then interpreted through a light-front wave function overlap representation, and their scaling behavior is analyzed in the large-N_c limit of QCD with inclusion of Δ intermediate states.

## Key numbers

- The peripheral densities at b = O(M_π⁻¹) exhibit exponential behavior e^{-2 M_π b} times pre-exponential functions of M_π, M_N, and b.
- In the large-N_c limit, the isovector densities scale as ρ₁^V(b) = O(N_c^0) and ρ̃₂^V(b) = O(N_c) for b = O(N_c^0).
- The Δ contribution to the peripheral densities is ≤ 20% of the nucleon contribution at b > 2 M_π⁻¹ with empirical N-Δ mass splitting and πNΔ coupling.
- The chiral two-pion component becomes numerically dominant only at large distances b ≥ 2 fm.
- The chiral component contributes only ~20% to the b²-weighted moment of the isovector density, but O(1) to the b⁴-weighted and higher moments.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive approach to transverse densities developed by Strikman and Weiss (2010) and the chiral EFT calculations of peripheral nucleon structure by Granados and Weiss (2013). It provides model-independent constraints on the peripheral transverse densities that can be used to construct empirical densities via dispersion integrals with spectral functions including chiral two-pion components, vector meson resonances, and higher-mass continua as in Belushkin et al. (2006) and Lorenz et al. (2012). The results enable connections between low-energy elastic form factors and peripheral high-energy scattering processes that could be studied at a future Electron-Ion Collider.
