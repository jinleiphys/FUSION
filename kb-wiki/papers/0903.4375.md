# Renormalized Polyakov Loop in the Deconfined Phase of SU(N) Gauge Theory and Gauge/String Duality

> We use gauge/string duality to analytically evaluate the renormalized Polyakov loop in pure Yang-Mills theories. For SU(3), the result is in a quite good agreement with lattice simulations for a broad temperature range.

## Key claim

The gauge/string duality model, using a specific five-dimensional background metric with a deformation parameter, analytically reproduces the renormalized Polyakov loop for SU(3) pure Yang-Mills theory with good agreement to lattice data across a broad temperature range, though the agreement is best for temperatures above approximately 1.05 times the critical temperature and degrades at lower temperatures.

## Method

The author uses the Nambu-Goto action for a fundamental string in a five-dimensional background metric with a warp factor w(z) = exp(σ z²)/z² and a blackening factor f(z) = 1 - (z/z_T)^4, where z_T = 1/πT. The Polyakov loop expectation value is computed from the renormalized minimal area of a straight string stretched between the boundary (z=0) and the horizon (z=z_T), with the quark mass divergence subtracted. The deformation parameter σ is fixed from the critical temperature, and the parameter γ = R²/(2α') is either taken from zero-temperature heavy quark potential fits (γ=0.62) or fitted to lattice Polyakov loop data (γ=0.72).

## Key numbers

- For SU(3) with γ=0.62 and normalization constant c=0.10, the maximum discrepancy with lattice data at T = 1.05 T_c is approximately 15%, decreasing to 2% at T = 2.2 T_c, and remaining almost negligible up to T = 20 T_c.
- With a best-fit value γ=0.72, the discrepancy at T = 1.05 T_c reduces to approximately 6%.
- The power-law approximation L(T) ≈ exp(c) (1 - γ (T_c/T)²) agrees with lattice data with accuracy better than 5% over the temperature range 1.05 T_c ≲ T ≲ 20 T_c.
- At the lowest available lattice temperature T = 1.012 T_c, the power-law approximation discrepancy is approximately 7%.
- The exponential-law approximation L(T) ≈ exp[c - γ (T_c/T)²] shows a 27% discrepancy at T = 1.05 T_c.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous gauge/string duality model, which successfully described heavy quark potentials at zero temperature (Andreev and Zakharov, 2006), spatial string tension at finite temperature (Andreev, 2007), and thermodynamics (Andreev, 2008). The results are compared directly to lattice simulations by Gupta (2008) for SU(3) gauge theory. The exponential-law approximation connects to work by Arriola (2005) on dimension-two condensates, while the power-law approximation resembles the behavior observed for the pressure in pure gauge theories by the Pisa group. The model enables analytic calculations of Polyakov loop behavior across a wide temperature range, providing a string-theory-based alternative to lattice simulations for quenched QCD thermodynamics.
