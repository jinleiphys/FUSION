# The Hoyle state in Nuclear Lattice EFT

> We review the calculation of the Hoyle state of $^{12}$C in Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) and its anthropic implications for the nucleosynthesis of $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O in red giant stars. We also review the extension of NLEFT to the regime of medium-mass nuclei, with emphasis on the determination of the ground-state energies of the alpha nuclei $^{16}$O, $^{20}$Ne, $^{24}$Mg and $^{28}$Si by means of Euclidean time projection. Finally, we review recent NLEFT results for the spectrum, electromagnetic properties, and alpha-cluster structure of $^{16}$O.

## Key claim

The paper reviews NLEFT calculations of the Hoyle state and medium-mass nuclei, but the abstract glosses over the fact that the NNLO 3NF overbinds nuclei with A ≥ 4 due to a clustering instability, which is rectified by introducing an effective 4N contact interaction tuned to empirical binding energies, and that the results for A ≥ 16 require this additional interaction to achieve agreement with experiment.

## Method

The authors combine Chiral Effective Field Theory for nucleons with Auxiliary-Field Quantum Monte Carlo lattice calculations, organized in powers of a soft scale Q up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). The LO Hamiltonian is treated non-perturbatively, while NLO and NNLO contributions (including the three-nucleon force and electromagnetic/isospin-breaking terms) are treated as perturbations. For medium-mass nuclei, Euclidean time projection is used with trial states generated from an SU(4)-symmetric Hamiltonian, and the ground-state energies are extracted from the large-time limit of the transient energy, with a spectral decomposition fit using multiple trial states to improve precision.

## Key numbers

- Hoyle state energy above triple-alpha threshold: Δ_h ≈ 379.5 keV (experimental value)
- LO charge radius of ¹²C ground state: r(0₁⁺) = 2.2(2) fm (experimental: 2.47(2) fm)
- LO quadrupole moment of ¹²C 2₁⁺ state: Q(2₁⁺) = 6(2) e fm² (experimental: 6(3) e fm²)
- LO B(E2,2₁⁺→0₁⁺) for ¹²C: 5(2) e²fm⁴ (experimental: 7.6(4) e²fm⁴)
- LO M(E0,0₂⁺→0₁⁺) for ¹²C: 3(1) e fm² (experimental: 5.5(1) e fm²)
- ¹⁶O ground-state energy at NNLO+4N: -131.3(5) MeV (experimental: -127.62 MeV)
- ²⁸Si ground-state energy at NNLO+4N: -233(3) MeV (experimental: -236.54 MeV)
- Maximum error in ground-state energies from ⁴He to ²⁸Si with effective 4N force: no larger than ~3%
- LO charge radius of ¹⁶O ground state: 2.3(1) fm (experimental: 2.710(15) fm)
- LO B(E2,2₁⁺→0₂⁺) for ¹⁶O: 22(4) e²fm⁴ (rescaled: 46(8) e²fm⁴, experimental: 65(7) e²fm⁴)

## Context

This work extends NLEFT calculations from light nuclei (¹²C including the Hoyle state) to medium-mass alpha nuclei up to ²⁸Si, building on earlier NLEFT studies by Epelbaum et al. (2009, 2011, 2012) that described nuclei up to A=12. The results confirm the alpha-cluster structure of ¹⁶O predicted by Wheeler (1937) and later cluster models, and provide the first ab initio evidence for tetrahedral alpha-cluster structure in the ¹⁶O ground state and square arrangement in the first excited 0⁺ state. The work enables future NLEFT calculations with smaller lattice spacing and inclusion of N3LO corrections, and provides constraints on the sensitivity of the triple-alpha process to variations in the light quark mass and fine-structure constant.
