# Effects of nonlocality of nuclear potentials on direct capture reactions

> Calculations of the direct radiative capture reactions are made for the $^{48}$Ca$(n,γ)^{49}$Ca, $^7$Li$(n,γ)^8$Li and $^{12}$C$(p,γ)^{13}$N reactions with the Perey-Buck type nonlocal potentials using a potential model. Our results reproduce the experimental data reasonably well. From comparisons with results obtained by using local potentials, it is found that the cross sections of direct capture reactions may change by around 25\% due to the nonlcality of nuclear potentials.

# Effects of nonlocality of nuclear potentials on direct capture reactions

## Key claim
Using Perey-Buck type nonlocal potentials in a potential model, the authors reproduce experimental data for three direct capture reactions reasonably well, but the claimed 25% change in cross sections due to nonlocality is a maximum value observed only for specific reactions and energy ranges; the effect varies by reaction and is not uniform across all systems studied.

## Method
The authors solve the Schrodinger equation with Perey-Buck type nonlocal potentials (keeping spin-orbit and Coulomb terms local) using an iterative method, starting from an initial local potential and converging to a nonlocal solution. They fit potential parameters to simultaneously reproduce binding energies and s-wave scattering lengths for each nucleon-target system, then calculate direct capture cross sections using a modified version of the RADCAP code. For the 12C(p,gamma)13N reaction, they treat the direct and resonant contributions coherently.

## Key numbers
- For the 48Ca(n,gamma)49Ca reaction, cross sections with nonlocal potentials are about 20% larger than those with local potentials
- For the 7Li(n,gamma)8Li reaction, nonlocal potentials give cross sections about 25% larger than local potentials when incident energy is below around 1 MeV
- For the 12C(p,gamma)13N reaction, the resonance term S_res calculated with local and nonlocal potentials differs by around 25% at about 1 MeV incident energy
- The s-wave scattering length for 48Ca+n is alpha_0 = 0.36 +/- 0.09 fm
- The spin-state scattering lengths for 7Li+n are a_+ = -3.63 +/- 0.05 fm (spin 2) and a_- = 0.87 +/- 0.07 fm (spin 1)
- The range of nonlocality beta = 0.85 fm is used for all nonlocal potentials
- Neutron spectroscopic factors used: for 49Ca, 0.72 (ground state) and 0.86 (first excited state); for 8Li, 0.87 (ground state) and 0.48 (first excited state)
- Proton spectroscopic factor for 13N ground state is 0.81; for the 0.422 MeV resonant state, the fitted SF is 0.36

## Context
This work extends the use of Perey-Buck type nonlocal potentials (Perey 1962) from scattering calculations to direct capture reactions, comparing results with local potential calculations. The study is motivated by astrophysical applications where direct capture dominates at low energies, following earlier potential model work by Christy (1961), Parker (1963), Rolfs (1973), Baye (1985), and Bertulani (2003). The results show that nonlocality affects bound and continuum wave functions differently, with constructive interference in the 7Li(n,gamma)8Li reaction and destructive interference in the other two reactions, suggesting that future calculations should treat nonlocality simultaneously in both bound and scattering states.
