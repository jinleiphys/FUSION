# Giant monopole energies from a constrained relativistic mean-field approach

> Background: Average energies of nuclear collective modes may be efficiently and accurately computed using a non-relativistic constrained approach without reliance on a random phase approximation (RPA). Purpose: To extend the constrained approach to the relativistic domain and to establish its impact on the calibration of energy density functionals. Methods: Relativistic RPA calculations are compared against the predictions of the corresponding constrained approach using two accurately calibrated energy density functionals. Results: We find excellent agreement-at the 2% level or better-between the predictions of the relativistic RPA and the corresponding constrained approach for magic (or semi-magic) nuclei ranging from 16O to 208Pb. Conclusions: An efficient and accurate method is proposed for incorporating nuclear collective excitations into the calibration of future energy density functionals.

## Key claim

The constrained relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach reproduces the full relativistic random phase approximation (RPA) results for giant monopole resonance (GMR) energies to within 2% or better for magic and semi-magic nuclei from 16O to 208Pb, despite the absence of a formal proof of the dielectric theorem for relativistic Hamiltonians.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent relativistic RPA calculations of the isoscalar monopole response for six nuclei (16O, 40Ca, 90Zr, 116Sn, 144Sm, 208Pb) using two accurately calibrated energy density functionals (NL3 and FSUGold). They compared the constrained energy E_con = sqrt(m1/m_{-1}) obtained from the constrained RMF approach, where m1 is computed from the ground-state mean-square radius and m_{-1} from the derivative of the mean-square radius with respect to a harmonic constraint, against the same quantity extracted from the full RPA strength distribution.

## Key numbers

- For NL3, the constrained energy from CRMF versus RPA agreed to within 0.04% for 16O (23.34 vs 23.35 MeV), 0.09% for 40Ca (21.55 vs 21.57 MeV), 0.16% for 90Zr (18.58 vs 18.55 MeV), 0.47% for 116Sn (16.98 vs 17.06 MeV), 0.50% for 144Sm (16.08 vs 16.16 MeV), and 0.21% for 208Pb (14.07 vs 14.10 MeV).
- For FSUGold, the constrained energy from CRMF versus RPA agreed to within 0.87% for 16O (22.89 vs 23.09 MeV), 0.05% for 40Ca (20.67 vs 20.66 MeV), 1.34% for 90Zr (17.70 vs 17.94 MeV), 1.70% for 116Sn (16.20 vs 16.48 MeV), 0.97% for 144Sm (15.37 vs 15.52 MeV), and 1.89% for 208Pb (13.50 vs 13.76 MeV).
- The incompressibility coefficient of symmetric nuclear matter is 271 MeV for NL3 and 230 MeV for FSUGold.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic constrained approach (Thouless 1960, Bohigas 1978, Ring 2004) to the relativistic domain, where only a single prior RMF constrained-vs-RPA comparison existed for the m_{-1} moment in 208Pb (Ma 2001). The method enables efficient incorporation of GMR centroid energies into the calibration of future relativistic energy density functionals, replacing numerically expensive RPA calculations with fast constrained RMF calculations for systematic studies of the incompressibility of neutron-rich matter and the density dependence of the symmetry energy.
