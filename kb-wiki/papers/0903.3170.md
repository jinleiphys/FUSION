# Longitudinal and Transverse Scaling Functions within the Coherent Density Fluctuation Model

> We extend our previous description of the superscaling phenomenon in inclusive electron scattering within the Coherent Density Fluctuation Model (CDFM). This model is a natural extension to finite nuclei of the Relativistic Fermi Gas Model (RFG) within which the scaling variable $ψ^{\prime}$ was introduced. In this work we propose a new modified CDFM approach to calculate the total, longitudinal and transverse scaling functions built up from the hadronic tensor and the longitudinal and transverse response functions in the RFG. We test the superscaling behavior of the new CDFM scaling functions by calculating the cross sections of electron scattering (in QE- and $Δ$-region for nuclei with $12\leq A\leq208$ at different energies and angles) and comparing to available experimental data. The new modified CDFM approach is extended to calculate charge-changing neutrino and antineutrino scattering on $^{12}$C at 1 GeV incident energy.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new version of the Coherent Density Fluctuation Model (CDFM\(_\text{II}\)) that, unlike the earlier CDFM\(_\text{I}\) version, allows the longitudinal and transverse scaling functions to differ from each other (violating "scaling of zero-kind"), and shows that this difference depends on the momentum transfer \(q\) until a sufficiently high \(q\) is reached.

## Method

The authors construct the hadronic tensor and the longitudinal and transverse response functions by weighting the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) model expressions with the CDFM weight function \(|F(x)|^2\), which is derived from either the nuclear density distribution or the nucleon momentum distribution. The resulting response functions are then used to define the total, longitudinal, and transverse scaling functions for inclusive electron scattering. The model is tested by calculating cross sections for electron scattering on nuclei with \(12 \leq A \leq 208\) in the quasielastic and \(\Delta\)-resonance regions, and is extended to charge-changing neutrino and antineutrino scattering on \(^{12}\text{C}\) at 1 GeV incident energy.

## Key numbers

* The maximum of the CDFM\(_\text{II}\) quasielastic scaling function is \(f^{\text{CDFM}_\text{II}}_{\text{max}}(\psi) \approx 0.80\), compared to \(f^{\text{CDFM}_\text{I}}_{\text{max}}(\psi) = 0.75\) for the earlier version.
* The normalization condition for both CDFM\(_\text{I}\) and CDFM\(_\text{II}\) scaling functions is \(\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(\psi) d\psi = 1\).
* Scaling of the first kind is violated for momentum transfers \(q < 0.5\) GeV/c, while for \(q\) of the order of 0.5 GeV/c and above, scaling violation slowly disappears as \(q\) increases.
* The CDFM\(_\text{II}\) scaling functions are calculated for fixed momentum transfer values of \(q = 0.3\), 0.5, 0.8, and 1.0 GeV/c for \(^{12}\text{C}\).
* The model is applied to nuclei with mass numbers \(12 \leq A \leq 208\) (specifically \(^{12}\text{C}\), \(^{27}\text{Al}\), \(^{56}\text{Fe}\), and \(^{197}\text{Au}\)).
* Charge-changing neutrino and antineutrino scattering cross sections are calculated on \(^{12}\text{C}\) at 1 GeV incident energy.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CDFM\(_\text{I}\) approach (Antonov et al., 2004-2008) for describing superscaling in inclusive electron scattering, which assumed equal longitudinal and transverse scaling functions. The new CDFM\(_\text{II}\) approach allows for a violation of this equality, similar to results from the Relativistic Plane-Wave Impulse Approximation (RPWIA) of Caballero et al. (2005). The model enables the study of scaling violations at low momentum transfer and provides a framework for calculating neutrino and antineutrino scattering cross sections, building on the Superscaling (SuSA) approach of Amaro et al. (2005).
