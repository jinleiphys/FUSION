# Exploring the influence of bulk viscosity of QCD on dilepton tomography

> The collective behavior of hadrons and of electromagnetic radiation in heavy-ion collisions has been widely used to study the properties of the the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP). Indeed this collectivity, as measured by anisotropic flow coefficients, can be used to constrain the transport properties of QGP. The goal of this contribution is to investigate the influence of the specific bulk viscosity ($ζ/s$) on dilepton production, both at Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) and Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies. We explore the sensitivity of dileptons to dynamical features that bulk viscosity induces on the evolution of a strongly-interacting medium, and highlight what makes them a valuable probe in the pursuit to also constrain $ζ/s$.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions is sensitive to the specific bulk viscosity (ζ/s) of the Quark-Gluon Plasma, but the authors explicitly state that a better description of data with one value of ζ/s does not necessarily mean that value is preferred, because a similar quality fit could have been obtained with both values by simultaneously changing other model parameters.

## Method

The authors performed hybrid hydrodynamic simulations using IP-Glasma initial conditions, followed by second-order Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics with bulk-to-shear coupling, and finally hadronic transport with UrQMD. They computed thermal dilepton rates from the QGP using Born approximation with viscous corrections, and hadronic dilepton rates using in-medium vector meson spectral functions via the Vector Dominance Model, plus a cocktail of late hadronic decays from the freeze-out hypersurface.

## Key numbers

- At LHC energy (√sNN = 2.76 TeV), the specific shear viscosity used is η/s = 0.095, obtained by fitting to hadronic observables from ALICE and CMS; an alternative simulation uses η/s = 0.16 to better reproduce multiplicity and v2 of hadrons
- At top RHIC energy (√sNN = 200 GeV), η/s = 0.06 is used to reproduce hadronic observables measured by STAR
- The switching temperature for converting fluid to hadrons is TRHIC_sw = 165 MeV at RHIC and TLHC_sw = 145 MeV at LHC
- The hydrodynamical simulation starts at τ0 = 0.4 fm/c with spatial resolution Δx = Δy = 0.17 fm and temporal resolution Δτ = 0.015 fm/c
- The QGP fraction fQGP interpolates linearly between fQGP = 1 at T > 0.22 GeV and fQGP = 0 at T < 0.184 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of bulk viscosity effects on hadronic observables by Ryu et al. (2015, 2017) and on direct photon production by Paquet et al. (2015), now applying the same hydrodynamic framework to dilepton production. The study builds on previous work showing dilepton sensitivity to shear viscous pressure by Vujanovic et al. (2016, 2017), and aims to demonstrate that dileptons can serve as a complementary probe to constrain bulk viscosity alongside hadronic observables.
