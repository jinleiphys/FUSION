# Criteria of Renormalizability in Effective Field Theories

> Any effective field theory relies on power counting rules that allow one to perform a systematic expansion of calculated quantities in terms of some soft scales. However, a naive power counting can be violated due to the presence of various hard scales in a given scheme. A typical example of such a scale is an ultraviolet regulator. This issue is particularly challenging when the interaction is nonperturbative. The power counting is expected to be restored in the course of renormalization, that is by redefining bare low-energy constants in the effective Lagrangian. Whether this procedure eventually leads to a self-consistent framework is not a priory obvious. We discuss various criteria of renormalizability in application to nuclear chiral effective field theory and provide several instructive counterexamples.

# Criteria of Renormalizability in Effective Field Theories

## Key claim
The paper argues that renormalizability of nuclear chiral effective field theory in the nucleon-nucleon sector requires three specific conditions to be simultaneously satisfied: locality of long-range forces, a cutoff of order the hard scale (Λ ∼ Λ_b ≈ 500–700 MeV), and natural size of the finite parts of counter terms. The authors demonstrate that violating any of these conditions leads to failure of renormalizability, even in cases where the naive power counting might appear to be satisfied.

## Method
The authors analyze the renormalization of the nucleon-nucleon amplitude at next-to-leading order within chiral EFT, using the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the LO amplitude and perturbative treatment of NLO corrections. They employ the Bogoliubov-Parasiuk-Hepp-Zimmermann subtraction scheme to handle overlapping divergences in the perturbative expansion, and the Fredholm method to treat nonperturbative channels (¹S₀, ³S₁-³D₁, ³P₀) where the perturbative series does not converge. The renormalizability criteria are then tested against three counterexamples: a nonlocal separable interaction model, the "renormalization-group invariant" scheme with exceptional cutoffs in the ³P₀ channel, and a fine-tuned short-range separable model.

## Key numbers
- The breakdown scale of chiral EFT is Λ_b = 500–700 MeV, defining the hard scale for the expansion parameter Q = q/Λ_b.
- In the perturbative analysis, the one-loop contribution T₂^{[0,1]} scales as m_N Λ³/Λ_b⁶, violating the expected O(Q²) power counting.
- After subtraction, the renormalized amplitude scales as p_on²/Λ_b⁴ ∼ O(Q²), restoring power counting.
- For the "exceptional cutoff" in the ³P₀ channel, the region where renormalization breaks down is extremely narrow, approximately 0.1 MeV wide.
- In the separable model counterexample, the critical parameters leading to ψ(0)=0 are C₀ = -(2π)³/(m_N Λ_b) and a = (5+√205)/6 ≈ 3.22, with C₀ Σ₀(0) ≈ 0.93 (no bound state).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous rigorous proof of renormalizability for chiral EFT in the NN sector at NLO (Gasparyan 2021, Gasparyan 2023) by formulating explicit criteria and testing them against pathological cases. It directly addresses ongoing debates about renormalization in nuclear EFT, particularly the "renormalization-group invariant" approach advocated by Hammer (2019) and others, which attempts to work with cutoffs much larger than Λ_b. The paper provides counterexamples to claims that EFT expansions must work equally well for arbitrarily large cutoffs, and shows that the "exceptional cutoff" problem in the ³P₀ channel (previously noted by Long 2011) cannot be resolved by continuous modifications of renormalization conditions or regulator forms. The results enable a clearer distinction between renormalizable EFT frameworks and phenomenological approaches that lack rigorous power counting.
