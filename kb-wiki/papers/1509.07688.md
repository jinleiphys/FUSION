# Physical observables in the decay $Λ_b \to Λ_c (\to Λ+π)+τ^{-}+\bar ν_τ$

> We analyze the tauonic semileptonic baryon decays $Λ^0_b \to Λ^+_c + τ^{-} +\bar ν_τ$ with particular emphasis on the lepton helicity flip contributions which vanish for zero lepton masses. We calculate the total rate, differential decay distributions, the longitudinal and transverse polarization components of the $Λ^+_c$ and the $τ^-$, and the lepton-side forward-backward asymmetries. We use the covariant confined quark model to provide numerical results on these observables.

## Key claim

The paper provides Standard Model predictions for the tauonic semileptonic baryon decay \(\Lambda_b^0 \to \Lambda_c^+ + \tau^{-} + \bar \nu_\tau\), with particular emphasis on lepton helicity flip contributions that vanish for zero lepton masses; the authors note that the mean values of several integrated observables change considerably when going from the electron mode to the tau mode, including a sign change in the lepton-side forward-backward asymmetry.

## Method

The authors use the covariant confined quark model to compute the transition form factors for the \(\Lambda_b \to \Lambda_c\) current-induced transition via a two-loop Feynman diagram, with the form factors parametrized by a double-pole fit. They then express all physical observables (total rate, differential decay distributions, longitudinal and transverse polarizations of \(\Lambda_c^+\) and \(\tau^-\), and lepton-side forward-backward asymmetries) in terms of helicity amplitudes and structure functions, following the helicity formalism of Körner et al. (1987, 1989). The numerical results are obtained by integrating the \(q^2\)-dependent helicity structure functions over the full phase space.

## Key numbers

* Total nonflip helicity structure functions for the electron mode: \(\Gamma_U = 12.4\), \(\Gamma_L = 19.6\), \(\Gamma_{LT} = -7.73\), \(\Gamma_P = -7.61\), \(\Gamma_{Lp} = -18.5\), \(\Gamma_{LTp} = -3.50\) (all in units of \(10^{-15}\) GeV).
* Total nonflip helicity structure functions for the tau mode: \(\Gamma_U = 3.29\), \(\Gamma_L = 2.90\), \(\Gamma_{LT} = -2.06\), \(\Gamma_P = -1.73\), \(\Gamma_{Lp} = -2.46\), \(\Gamma_{LTp} = -0.66\) (all in units of \(10^{-15}\) GeV).
* Integrated lepton-side forward-backward asymmetry: \(<A_{FB}^{\ell}> = 0.18\) for the electron mode, and \(<A_{FB}^{\ell}> = -0.038\) for the tau mode (sign change).
* Integrated longitudinal polarization of the \(\Lambda_c^+\): \(<P^h_z> = -0.82\) for the electron mode, and \(<P^h_z> = -0.72\) for the tau mode.
* Integrated longitudinal polarization of the \(\tau^-\): \(<P^\ell_z> = -1.00\) for the electron mode, and \(<P^\ell_z> = -0.32\) for the tau mode.
* Integrated transverse polarization of the \(\tau^-\): \(<P^\ell_x> = 0.00\) for the electron mode, and \(<P^\ell_x> = 0.55\) for the tau mode.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis in Gutsche et al. (2015) of the same decay within the Standard Model, correcting an erroneous factor in the definition of the forward-backward asymmetry and providing corrected numerical results. It is motivated by observed tensions between Standard Model predictions and experimental data in tauonic meson decays such as \(B \to \tau \nu\), \(B \to D \tau \bar \nu_\tau\), and \(B \to D^* \tau \bar \nu_\tau\), as summarized by Soffer (2014) and Blake et al. (2015). The results enable future comparisons with experimental data on baryon decays, which can test for new physics beyond the Standard Model in charged current interactions.
