# Quark Hadron Duality at Finite Temperature

> At low temperatures we expect that all QCD observables are defined in terms of hadrons. This includes the partition function as well as the Polyakov loop in all representations. We analyze the physics underlying a microscopic derivation of the hadron resonance gas.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model, while phenomenologically successful in describing lattice QCD results for the trace anomaly below the crossover temperature, is not a theorem or a well-defined approximation from the original QCD Lagrangian; it remains unclear how corrections should be implemented or what the error estimate for the HRG should be.

## Method

The authors compare the trace anomaly computed from the HRG model using the Particle Data Group (PDG) hadron spectrum and the Relativized Quark Model (RQM) spectrum against continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD results from the Wuppertal-Budapest and HotQCD collaborations. They analyze the cumulative number of hadronic states as a function of mass for both the PDG and RQM spectra, and derive relations between hadron masses and sizes using the virial theorem applied to a multiparton Hamiltonian with a linear confining potential.

## Key numbers

- The trace anomaly from the HRG model works accurately for temperatures below about 170 MeV, which is below the maximal scale-violating temperature \(T_s \sim 200\) MeV.
- The lowest lattice data points at \(T = 120\) MeV are first saturated when states with masses above the vector mesons are included.
- Quantum statistical bosonic and baryonic corrections account for less than 1% correction to the classical Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution at about \(T = 200\) MeV.
- The chiral-deconfinement crossover temperature is \(T_c = 155(10)\) MeV.
- For the glueball, a rough estimate using the uncertainty principle gives \(M_0 \approx 4.2\sqrt{\sigma}\).
- The ratio of glueball mass to rho meson mass is approximately \(M_{g,0^{++}} / m_\rho \approx 3/2\).
- The PDG and RQM spectra look very much alike below 1.7-1.8 GeV.
- The RQM spectrum saturates sharply at 2.5 GeV, which is the cutoff mass where the model calculations stopped.

## Context

This work extends the ongoing effort to understand the transition from hadronic to quark-gluon degrees of freedom in QCD at finite temperature, following lattice calculations by the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi 2013) and HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov 2014) that established a smooth crossover rather than a phase transition. The authors build on the HRG model, which has been used to arbitrate discrepancies between lattice collaborations, and compare it with the Relativized Quark Model of Godfrey (1985) and Capstick (1986). The analysis enables future work on quantifying uncertainties in the HRG approach and understanding how finite size and finite width effects of hadrons modify the simple non-interacting resonance gas picture.
