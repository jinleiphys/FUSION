# Theoretical study of the Lambda(1405) resonance in Xi_b^0 -> D^0 pi Sigma decay

> We study the mechanism of the weak decay process of Xi_b^0 into D^0 with a meson-baryon pair. It is shown that the dominant component of the prompt weak decay produces the meson-baryon pair with the spectator strange quark being transferred to the baryon, so that the Kbar N channel is absent. Subsequent final state interaction then reflects the pi Sigma originated Lambda(1405) formation amplitude, which has been difficult to access in previous experimental studies. We predict the line shapes of the pi Sigma invariant mass distribution using a realistic chiral meson-baryon amplitude for the final state interaction. It is shown that the interference between the direct and the rescattering contributions can strongly distort the peak structure of the Lambda(1405) in the mass distribution. This indicates the necessity of a detailed investigation of the reaction mechanism in order to extract the Lambda(1405) property in the pi Sigma mass distribution.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that in the decay Xi_b^0 -> D^0 pi Sigma, the dominant weak decay mechanism produces a meson-baryon pair where the Kbar N channel is absent, but the subsequent interference between the direct production and rescattering contributions can strongly distort the peak structure of the Lambda(1405) in the pi Sigma invariant mass distribution, meaning the observed peak does not directly reflect the resonance properties.

## Method

The authors analyzed the quark-line diagrams for the Cabibbo-favored weak decay of Xi_b^0, identifying the dominant diagram where the b quark decays to c, anti-u, and d, with the spectator strange quark from the initial Xi_b^0 transferred to the baryon. They then expressed the resulting meson-baryon state in terms of hadronic degrees of freedom using a flavor singlet q anti-q pair insertion, and applied a chiral unitary model from Ikeda et al. (2011, 2012) for the final state interaction to calculate the pi Sigma invariant mass distributions.

## Key numbers

- The pole positions of the meson-baryon scattering amplitude used for final state interactions are z1 = 1424 - 26i MeV and z2 = 1381 - 81i MeV, from Ikeda et al. (2011, 2012).
- The emitted D^0 meson has a momentum of approximately 2 GeV in the rest frame of the initial Xi_b^0, which has a mass of approximately 5.8 GeV.
- The pi Sigma spectra show peaks shifted to much lower energy than the pole positions, with the pi^0 Sigma^0 and pi^- Sigma^+ channels exhibiting dips in the Lambda(1405) region around 1400 MeV and enhanced threshold cusps at the Kbar N threshold.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Miyahara (2015) on Lambda_c -> pi^+ MB decay, where the Kbar N -> pi Sigma amplitude dominates, to a process where the pi Sigma -> pi Sigma amplitude is the primary final state interaction. The paper contrasts with previous production studies such as photoproduction gamma p -> K^+ Lambda(1405) by the CLAS Collaboration (Moriya, 2013) and the pp -> K^+ p pi Sigma reaction by the HADES Collaboration (Agakishiev, 2012), showing that the interference effect between direct and rescattering terms is much enhanced here due to the small magnitude of the pi Sigma -> pi Sigma amplitude. This enables a new method to study the Lambda(1405) through weak decays of heavy hadrons, but also demonstrates that naive fits of pi Sigma spectra to |T_ij|^2 are not always valid.
