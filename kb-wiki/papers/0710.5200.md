# Overview of nucleon structure studies

> A brief overview of the recent activity in the measurement of the elastic electromagnetic proton and neutron form factors is presented. It is discussed how the quality of the data has been greatly improved by performing double polarization experiments, and the role of two-photon exchange processes will be highlighted. The spatial information on the quark charge distribibutions in the nucleon resulting from the form factors measurements will be discussed, as well as the steady rate of improvements made in the lattice QCD calculations. It is discussed how generalized parton distributions have emerged as a unifying theme in hadron physics linking the spatial densities extracted from form factors with the quark momentum distribution information residing in quark structure functions. The recent progress in the electromagnetic excitation of the $Δ(1232)$ resonance will also briefly be discussed.

## Key claim

Double polarization experiments have resolved a long-standing discrepancy between Rosenbluth and polarization measurements of the proton form factor ratio, with two-photon exchange corrections now understood to reconcile the two methods, though the reconciliation is only partial at higher momentum transfers above 3 GeV\(^2\).

## Method

The paper reviews elastic electron-nucleon scattering experiments using both Rosenbluth cross-section separation and recoil/target polarization techniques to extract proton and neutron electromagnetic form factors. It also surveys lattice QCD calculations of isovector form factors using hybrid actions (domain wall valence quarks on 2+1 flavor staggered sea quarks) and chiral effective field theory extrapolations, as well as deeply virtual Compton scattering experiments accessing generalized parton distributions.

## Key numbers

- Proton magnetic form factor \(G_{Mp}\) measured up to momentum transfer \(Q^2 \approx 30\) GeV\(^2\)
- Proton electric-to-magnetic form factor ratio \(G_{Ep}/G_{Mp}\) measured via polarization transfer up to \(Q^2 = 5.6\) GeV\(^2\), with a new experiment extending to \(Q^2 \approx 8.5\) GeV\(^2\)
- Neutron electric form factor \(G_{En}\) measured via polarization up to \(Q^2 = 1.5\) GeV\(^2\), with new data under analysis up to 3.4 GeV\(^2\)
- Neutron magnetic form factor \(G_{Mn}\) measured with improved error bars up to \(Q^2 = 4.8\) GeV\(^2\)
- All four nucleon form factors display bump structures around \(Q^2 \approx 0.25\) GeV\(^2\), interpreted as pion cloud signatures
- Lattice QCD calculations performed for pion masses in the range \(m_\pi = 360 - 775\) MeV, with lattice spacing \(a \approx 0.125\) fm and lattice sizes \(L \sim 3\) fm
- Lattice calculations reach pion mass values down to about 350 MeV, with very recent results below 350 MeV
- Chiral effective field theory results for \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance show chiral loops crucial for the \(E2/M1\) ratio at low momentum transfer

## Context

This overview extends the experimental and theoretical work on nucleon electromagnetic form factors reviewed by Hyde-Wright (2004), Arrington (2006), and Perdrisat (2006). The polarization measurements from Jefferson Lab (Jones 2000, Gayou 2002, Punjabi 2005) revealed the discrepancy with Rosenbluth data that two-photon exchange calculations by Guichon and Vanderhaeghen (2003) and others now largely explain. The work enables future programs at the Jefferson Lab 12 GeV upgrade and Compass to map generalized parton distributions, linking form factor spatial densities to parton distribution functions as pioneered by Ji (1998), Goeke (2001), Diehl (2003), and Belitsky (2005).
