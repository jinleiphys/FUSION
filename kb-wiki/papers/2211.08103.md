# The universal scaling of kinetic freeze-out parameters across different collision systems at the LHC energy

> In this paper, we perform the Tsallis Blast-Wave analysis on the transverse momentum spectra of identified hadrons produced in a wide range of collision systems at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) including pp, pPb, XeXe and PbPb collisions. The kinetic freeze-out properties are investigated across these systems varying with the event multiplicity. We find that the extracted kinetic freeze-out temperature, radial flow velocity and the non-extensive parameter exhibit a universal scaling behavior for these systems with very different geometric size, especially when the independent baryon Tsallis non-extensive parameter is considered. This universality may indicate the existence of a unified partonic evolution stage in different collision systems at the LHC energies.

## Key claim

The kinetic freeze-out temperature, radial flow velocity, and non-extensive parameter extracted from Tsallis Blast-Wave fits exhibit a universal scaling behavior across pp, pPb, XeXe, and PbPb collisions at LHC energies, especially when a separate non-extensive parameter for baryons is introduced; this universality may indicate a unified partonic evolution stage in different collision systems.

## Method

The authors performed Tsallis Blast-Wave (TBW) model fits to the transverse momentum spectra of charged pions, kaons, and protons measured by ALICE in pp collisions at 7 and 13 TeV, pPb collisions at 5.02 TeV, XeXe collisions at 5.44 TeV, and PbPb collisions at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV. They used both a default TBW fit with a single non-extensive parameter q for all hadrons and a TBW4 fit with separate non-extensive parameters for mesons (q_M) and baryons (q_B), restricting the fit range to p_T < 3 GeV/c and fixing the flow profile index n to 1 for a linear velocity profile.

## Key numbers

- Average radial flow velocity saturates at about 0.6 when the average charged particle density per unit pseudorapidity approaches 1000.
- The onset of non-zero radial flow starts from average charged particle density around 10 to 20 in both small and large system groups.
- A turning behavior in the multiplicity dependence of freeze-out parameters occurs around average charged particle density of 10 to 15.
- The effective temperature T_eff shows a turning point at average charged particle density around 10, above which it increases monotonously up to average charged particle density around 1000 across all systems.
- In TBW4 fits, the effective temperature grows all the way from low to high multiplicity with different slopes in two regions separated by average charged particle density around 20.

## Context

This work extends the Tsallis Blast-Wave analysis approach previously applied to heavy ion collisions (Shao 2009, Tang 2011, Chen 2020) to a wide range of collision systems at LHC energies, including small systems like pp and pPb collisions. The universal scaling observed supports the idea that a unified mechanism may drive the evolution properties of both small and large systems, similar to the universal strangeness enhancement effect found by ALICE (2017, 2019). The results enable future studies of ultra-central pp collisions and intermediate-size systems like Oxygen-Oxygen collisions to further test this universality.
