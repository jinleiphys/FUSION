# Exploring contributions from incomplete fusion in $^{6,7}$Li+$^{209}$Bi and $^{6,7}$Li+$^{198}$Pt reactions

> We use the breakup absorption model to simultaneously describe the measured cross-sections of the Complete fusion (CF), Incomplete fusion (ICF), and Total fusion (TF) in nuclear reactions induced by weakly bound nuclei $^{6,7}$Li on $^{209}$Bi and $^{198}$Pt targets. The absorption cross-sections are calculated using the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) method with different choices of short range imaginary potentials to get the ICF, CF and TF cross-sections. It is observed that the cross-sections for deuteron-ICF/deuteron-capture are of similar magnitude as the $α$-ICF/$α$-capture, in case of $^{6}$Li projectile, while the cross-sections for triton-ICF/triton-capture is more dominant than $α$-ICF/$α$-capture in case of $^{7}$Li projectile. Both these observations are also corroborated by the experimental data. The ratio of ICF to TF cross-sections, which defines the value of fusion suppression factor is found to be in agreement with the data available from the literature. The cross-section ratio of CF/TF and ICF/TF show opposite behavior, the former decreases while the latter increases as the energy is lowered, which shows the dominance of ICF at below barrier energies. We have also studied the correlation of the ICF cross-sections with the non-capture breakup (NCBU) cross-sections as a function of energy, which shows that the NCBU is more significant than ICF at below barrier energies.

## Key claim

The breakup absorption model within the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) framework can simultaneously describe complete fusion (CF), incomplete fusion (ICF), and total fusion (TF) cross-sections for \({}^{6,7}\)Li on \({}^{209}\)Bi and \({}^{198}\)Pt, but the agreement for individual ICF channels depends on the specific fragment-target system and energy, with d-ICF data for \({}^{6}\)Li+\({}^{198}\)Pt being highly underpredicted by the calculations.

## Method

The authors performed CDCC calculations using the code FRESCO version 2.9, including couplings to resonances and the \(\alpha\)-d or \(\alpha\)-t continuum for \({}^{6}\)Li and \({}^{7}\)Li projectiles, respectively. They used short-range imaginary potentials in the fragment-target interactions to obtain absorption cross-sections, and performed three independent calculations with different combinations of imaginary potentials (both fragments, only \(\alpha\)-target, only d/t-target) to extract \(\sigma_{TF}\), \(\sigma_{\alpha-ICF}\), and \(\sigma_{d/t-ICF}\) separately. The fragment-target real potentials were generated using the cluster folding model, with the Sao Paulo potential for \(\alpha\)-target and global potentials for d-target and t-target.

## Key numbers

* For \({}^{6}\)Li+\({}^{209}\)Bi, the cross-sections for deuteron-ICF and \(\alpha\)-ICF are of similar magnitude.
* For \({}^{7}\)Li+\({}^{209}\)Bi, the triton-ICF cross-section is much higher than the \(\alpha\)-ICF cross-section.
* The ratio \(\sigma_{ICF}/\sigma_{TF}\) at above-barrier energies gives a suppression factor of approximately 30% for CF, in agreement with literature data for \({}^{6,7}\)Li projectiles.
* The short-range imaginary potential parameters used were W=25 MeV, with radius parameters r_w ranging from 0.62 fm (\(\alpha\)-target) to 0.69 fm (t-target) and diffuseness a_w=0.40 fm for all fragment-target systems.
* For \({}^{6}\)Li+\({}^{198}\)Pt, the d-ICF data at the last two measured energies was found to be higher than the calculated total fusion cross-section.

## Context

This work extends the breakup absorption model previously applied to \({}^{9}\)Be projectiles (Parkar et al., 2014) to \({}^{6,7}\)Li projectiles, providing the first simultaneous quantum mechanical description of CF, ICF, and TF data over a wide energy range. It contrasts with earlier semiclassical calculations by Marta et al. (2014) that did not describe the data satisfactorily, and with the CDCC-based approach of Hagino et al. (2000) and Diaz-Torres and Thompson (2002) which did not properly handle fragments of comparable masses. The results enable future studies of the correlation between ICF and non-capture breakup (NCBU), showing that NCBU is more significant than ICF at below-barrier energies while ICF becomes more important above the barrier.
