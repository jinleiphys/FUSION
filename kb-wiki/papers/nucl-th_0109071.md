# Chiral 2$π$-exchange NN-potentials: Relativistic $1/M^2$-Corrections

> We calculate in baryon chiral perturbation theory the relativistic $1/M^2$-corrections to the leading order two-pion exchange diagrams. We give explicit expressions for the corresponding one-loop NN-amplitudes in momentum space. The resulting isovector central and isoscalar spin-spin and tensor NN-amplitudes involve non-static terms proportional to the squared nucleon center-of-mass momentum $p^2$. From the two-pion exchange box diagrams we obtain an isoscalar quadratic spin-orbit NN-amplitude. We give also analytical expressions for the corresponding NN-potentials in coordinate space. The diagrammatic results presented here make the chiral NN-potential complete at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the relativistic \(1/M^2\)-corrections to the leading-order two-pion exchange diagrams in baryon chiral perturbation theory, thereby completing the chiral NN-potential at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N\(^3\)LO). The author notes that these corrections may be sizeable, but a firm conclusion about their relevance can only be drawn from a complete N\(^3\)LO calculation.

## Method

The author expands the relativistic \(\pi\pi NN\)-vertex and the product of pseudovector \(\pi N\)-vertices with a nucleon Dirac-propagator in powers of \(1/M\) up to quadratic order in the nucleon center-of-mass frame. The resulting expressions are multiplied with pion propagators and integrated over the four-dimensional loop momentum for the bubble, triangle, crossed box, and planar box diagrams. The planar box diagram requires special treatment to isolate the iterated \(1\pi\)-exchange before the \(1/M\)-expansion, using residue calculus for the \(dl_0\) integration.

## Key numbers

- The isovector central NN-amplitude from the bubble diagram is \(W_C = (q^2-4p^2) w^2 L(q) / (768\pi^2 M^2 f_\pi^4)\), with \(w = \sqrt{4m_\pi^2+q^2}\) and \(L(q) = (w/q) \ln[(w+q)/(2m_\pi)]\).
- The isovector central NN-amplitude from the triangle diagrams is \(W_C = (g_A^2 L(q))/(96\pi^2 M^2 f_\pi^4) \times \{ (11/4)q^4 + 5m_\pi^2 q^2 + 3m_\pi^4 - 6m_\pi^6 w^{-2} - p^2(8m_\pi^2 + 5q^2) \}\).
- The isoscalar quadratic spin-orbit NN-amplitude from the box diagrams is \(V_Q = -g_A^4 L(q) / (32\pi^2 M^2 f_\pi^4)\).
- At \(r = 0.7\) fm and \(p=0\), the isoscalar central potential \(\widetilde V_C = -185.5\) MeV, the isovector central potential \(\widetilde W_C = -103.7\) MeV, and the isoscalar spin-orbit potential \(\widetilde V_{SO} = 124.8\) MeV.
- At \(r = 1.4\) fm and \(p=0\), the isoscalar central potential \(\widetilde V_C = -1.34\) MeV, the isovector central potential \(\widetilde W_C = -0.72\) MeV, and the isoscalar spin-orbit potential \(\widetilde V_{SO} = 0.85\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral NN-potential calculations of Epelbaum, Gloeckle, and Meissner (2000) and van Kolck and collaborators (1994) by adding the relativistic \(1/M^2\)-corrections that were the only missing pieces at N\(^3\)LO. The results enable complete N\(^3\)LO calculations of the two-nucleon system and empirical analyses of low-energy elastic NN-scattering, which will determine the maximal range of validity of chiral multi-pion exchange in the NN-interaction.
