# Constraints on the dense matter equation of state and neutron star properties from NICER's mass-radius estimate of PSR J0740+6620 and multimessenger observations

> In recent years our understanding of the dense matter equation of state (EOS) of neutron stars has significantly improved by analyzing multimessenger data from radio/X-ray pulsars, gravitational wave events, and from nuclear physics constraints. Here we study the additional impact on the EOS from the jointly estimated mass and radius of PSR J0740+6620, presented in Riley et al. (2021) by analyzing a combined dataset from X-ray telescopes NICER and XMM-Newton. We employ two different high-density EOS parameterizations: a piecewise-polytropic (PP) model and a model based on the speed of sound in a neutron star (CS). At nuclear densities these are connected to microscopic calculations of neutron matter based on chiral effective field theory interactions. In addition to the new NICER data for this heavy neutron star, we separately study constraints from the radio timing mass measurement of PSR J0740+6620, the gravitational wave events of binary neutron stars GW190425 and GW170817, and for the latter the associated kilonova AT2017gfo. By combining all these, and the NICER mass-radius estimate of PSR J0030+0451 we find the radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star to be constrained to the 95% credible ranges 12.33^{+0.76}_{-0.81} km (PP model) and 12.18^{+0.56}_{-0.79} km (CS model). In addition, we explore different chiral effective field theory calculations and show that the new NICER results provide tight constraints for the pressure of neutron star matter at around twice saturation density, which shows the power of these observations to constrain dense matter interactions at intermediate densities.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the new NICER mass-radius estimate for the heavy pulsar PSR J0740+6620, combined with other multimessenger data, tightens constraints on the dense matter equation of state, particularly at around twice saturation density, but the exact radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star depends on the chosen high-density parameterization (piecewise-polytropic vs. speed-of-sound model) and the specific chiral effective field theory calculation used at low densities.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian inference using two high-density equation of state parameterizations: a piecewise-polytropic model and a speed-of-sound model, both matched to chiral effective field theory calculations of neutron matter below 1.1 times nuclear saturation density and to the BPS crust equation of state at lower densities. They combined likelihoods from the NICER/XMM-Newton mass-radius posterior of PSR J0740+6620 from Riley et al. (2021), the NICER mass-radius estimate of PSR J0030+0451 from Riley et al. (2019), gravitational wave posterior distributions from GW170817 and GW190425, and a kilonova light curve analysis of AT2017gfo using a two-component ejecta model connected to binary progenitor properties.

## Key numbers

* Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star constrained to 95% credible ranges: 12.33^{+0.76}_{-0.81} km (piecewise-polytropic model) and 12.18^{+0.56}_{-0.79} km (speed-of-sound model), from the full multimessenger combination including both NICER pulsars, both gravitational wave events, and the kilonova.
* PSR J0740+6620 radius from Riley et al. (2021): 12.39^{+1.30}_{-0.98} km (68% credible region), with mass 2.072^{+0.067}_{-0.066} solar masses.
* Updated radio timing mass of PSR J0740+6620 from Fonseca et al. (2021): 2.08 ± 0.07 solar masses.
* Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star from GW170817 plus AT2017gfo only: 12.12^{+1.10}_{-1.44} km (piecewise-polytropic) and 11.53^{+1.16}_{-1.15} km (speed-of-sound model).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses of NICER data for PSR J0030+0451 (Raaijmakers et al., 2019, 2020) by incorporating the new mass-radius measurement of the heavier pulsar PSR J0740+6620 and a broader set of multimessenger constraints including GW190425 and a kilonova analysis of AT2017gfo. The results are consistent with prior work by Dietrich et al. (2020) and Capano et al. (2019) and show that the new NICER data provide tight constraints on the pressure of neutron star matter at around twice saturation density, demonstrating the power of these observations to constrain dense matter interactions at intermediate densities. The framework enables future combination with upcoming LIGO/Virgo measurements and improved nuclear physics constraints.
