# Neutron correlations and clustering in neutron-rich nuclear systems

> In this paper, we will briefly review the recent progress on neutron correlations and clustering in neutron-rich nuclei from quasi-free scattering experiments. The quasi-free ($p$, $pn$) reaction was measured for Borromean nuclei $^{11}$Li, $^{14}$Be, and $^{17}$B. A surprisingly small $s$-wave component was found for $^{17}$B, revealing a weak neutron halo in $^{17}$B, and the comparative study of the three nuclei shows the surface localization of dineutron correlation and its universality in Borromean nuclei. The two-neutron emission of $^{16}$Be was also studied, finding strong dineutron correlation in $^{16}$Be(g.s.) but weak correlation in $^{16}$Be($2^+$). Two missing-mass measurements using $^{4}$He($^{8}$He, $^{8}$Be) and $^{8}$He($p$, $pα$) reactions provided evidence for the $^{4}n$ resonance, but the $^{3}n$ resonance was not supported by the recent experiment employing the $t(t,^3$He)$^3n$ charge-exchange reaction. The quasi-free $(p,p α)$ reaction has been extended to unstable nuclei, and the recent experiment unravels the $α$-$2n$-$α$ molecule-like cluster structure of $^{10}$Be(g.s.).

## Key claim

The paper reviews recent progress in neutron correlations and clustering in neutron-rich nuclei, emphasizing that quasi-free scattering experiments have revealed a surprisingly small $s$-wave component in $^{17}$B (only 9(2)%), indicating a weak neutron halo, and that dineutron correlation is surface-localized and universal in Borromean nuclei, but the existence of a $^{4}n$ resonance remains debated due to alternative reaction-model explanations for the observed peaks.

## Method

The authors review a series of kinematically complete quasi-free scattering experiments performed at the RIBF facility, using the ($p$, $pn$) reaction on Borromean nuclei ($^{11}$Li, $^{14}$Be, $^{17}$B) and the ($p$, $p\alpha$) reaction on $^{8}$He and $^{10}$Be, with detection systems including the MINOS liquid hydrogen target, SAMURAI spectrometer, DALI2 gamma-ray array, and neutron detector arrays WINDS and NEBULA. Missing-mass measurements using the $^{4}$He($^{8}$He, $^{8}$Be) double-charge-exchange reaction and the $t(t,^{3}$He)$^{3}n$ charge-exchange reaction were employed to search for multi-neutron resonances. The two-neutron decay of $^{16}$Be was studied via the $^{17}$B($p$, $2p$) reaction and analyzed with a three-body model describing both initial structure and time evolution.

## Key numbers

* $^{17}$B: $1s_{1/2}$ spectroscopic factor percentage of 9(2)%, the smallest $s$ or $p$ orbital percentage among known halo nuclei (Yang et al., 2021).
* $^{11}$Li: two-neutron configurations determined as 35(2)% $s^2$ + 59(1)% $p^2$ + 6(4)% $d^2$ from ($p$, $pn$) data (Kubota et al., 2020).
* $^{4}n$ from $^{4}$He($^{8}$He, $^{8}$Be): resonant energy 0.83 $\pm$ 0.65 (stat) $\pm$ 1.25 (syst) MeV, upper limit width 2.6 MeV (FWHM), significance 4.9$\sigma$ (Kisamori et al., 2016).
* $^{4}n$ from $^{8}$He($p$, $p\alpha$): resonant energy 2.37 $\pm$ 0.38 (stat) $\pm$ 0.44 (syst) MeV, width 1.75 $\pm$ 0.22 (stat) $\pm$ 0.30 (syst) MeV, significance beyond 5$\sigma$ (Duer et al., 2022).
* $^{11}$Li: two-neutron separation energy $S_{2n}$ = 369 keV; opening angle of two valence neutrons determined as approximately 48 degrees from Coulomb dissociation (Nakamura et al., 2006).
* $^{16}$Be: $0^+$ ground state and $2^+$ first excited state identified; three-body model predicts dominant dineutron cluster in $0^+$ but weak $n-n$ correlations in $2^+$ (Monteagudo et al., 2024).
* $^{3}n$: no peak structure observed in energy spectrum up to approximately 20 MeV above three-neutron threshold from $t(t,^{3}$He)$^3n$ reaction (Miki et al., 2024).

## Context

This review extends the experimental work on neutron halos initiated by Tanihata et al. (1985) and the dineutron correlation studies in $^{11}$Li by Nakamura et al. (2006), and directly builds on the quasi-free ($p$, $pn$) measurements by Kubota et al. (2020) and Corsi et al. (2023) that established surface localization of dineutron correlation. The $^{4}n$ results from Kisamori et al. (2016) and Duer et al. (2022) are contrasted with alternative reaction-model explanations by Lazauskas et al. (2022) and with ab initio calculations by Pieper (2003), Shirokov et al. (2016), and Fossez et al. (2017). The extension of quasi-free ($p$, $p\alpha$) reactions to unstable nuclei, following the work of Tanaka et al. (2021) on stable tin isotopes, enables the study of $\alpha$ clustering in neutron-rich systems and provides input for modeling nuclear equation of state and neutron star crust properties.
