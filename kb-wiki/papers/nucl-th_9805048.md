# Electromagnetic Structure of the Trinucleons

> The electromagnetic form factors of the trinucleons 3H and 3He are calculated with wave functions obtained with the Argonne AV18 two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions. Full account is taken of the two-body currents required by current conservation with the AV18 interaction as well as those associated with N-Delta transition currents and the currents of Delta resonance components in the wave functions. Explicit three-nucleon current operators associated with the two-pion exchange three-nucleon interaction arising from irreducible S-wave pion-nucleon scattering is constructed and shown to have very little effect on the calculated magnetic form factors. The calculated magnetic form factor of 3H, and charge form factors of both 3H and 3He are in satisfactory agreement with the experimental data. However, the position of the zero in the magnetic form factor of 3He is slightly underpredicted.

# Electromagnetic Structure of the Trinucleons

## Key claim
The calculated magnetic form factor of ³H and charge form factors of both ³H and ³He agree satisfactorily with experimental data, but the position of the zero in the magnetic form factor of ³He is slightly underpredicted (calculated at 3.75 fm⁻¹ versus the experimental range 4.2–4.4 fm⁻¹), independent of whether Δ-isobar configurations are included in the wave function.

## Method
The authors used pair-correlated hyperspherical harmonics (PHH) variational wave functions for ³H and ³He obtained from the Argonne AV18 two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions. They constructed two-body exchange current operators required by current conservation with the AV18 interaction, including model-dependent ρπγ and ωπγ currents, as well as NΔ transition currents and currents from Δ-isobar components in the wave functions. A three-nucleon current operator associated with S-wave pion-nucleon rescattering (Weinberg-Tomozawa term) was also derived and evaluated. Matrix elements were computed using Monte Carlo methods with 400,000 configurations.

## Key numbers
- The calculated zero in the ³He magnetic form factor falls at 3.75 fm⁻¹, below the experimental range of 4.2–4.4 fm⁻¹.
- The one-body predictions for the ³H and ³He magnetic form factors have distinct minima at approximately 3.5 fm⁻¹ and 2.5 fm⁻¹, respectively, in disagreement with experimental data.
- The cutoff parameters used for meson-nucleon form factors were Λ_π = 0.75 GeV and Λ_ρ = Λ_ω = 1.25 GeV, obtained from studies of the deuteron B-structure function.
- The ρπγ transition form factor at the photon point is G_ρπγ(0) = g_ρπγ = 0.56, and the ωπγ value is G_ωπγ(0) = g_ωπγ = 0.68.
- The three-nucleon current associated with S-wave πN rescattering has very little effect on the calculated magnetic form factors for momentum transfer values below 1 GeV/c.

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations by Schiavilla et al. (1989, 1990) that employed the Argonne AV14 interaction, now using the more recent AV18 interaction which includes charge-symmetry-breaking and charge-independence-breaking terms. The calculation addresses the long-standing need for quantitative understanding of trinucleon form factors around and above their first zeros, a problem that persists despite progress on the deuteron tensor polarization at Jefferson Lab. The results enable further testing of three-nucleon interaction models and suggest that the underpredicted zero in the ³He magnetic form factor likely originates from insufficient isovector exchange current strength at high momentum transfer, possibly pointing to limitations in the potential model or missing transverse exchange current mechanisms.
