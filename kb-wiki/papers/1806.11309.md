# A new and finite family of solutions of hydrodynamics: Part II: Advanced estimate of initial energy densities

> We derive a new, exact formula for the estimate of the initial energy densities from a new family of finite and exact solution of relativistic perfect fluid hydrodynamics. The new formula depends non-trivially on the speed of sound and on the shape or width parameter of the measured (pseudo)rapidity distribution.

# A new and finite family of solutions of hydrodynamics: Part II: Advanced estimate of initial energy densities

## Key claim

The authors derive an exact formula for initial energy density from the CKCJ solution that depends on both the acceleration parameter and the speed of sound, but this exact result violates both the Bjorken limit (accelerationless case) and the CNC limit (superhard equation of state) that earlier conjectured formulas were designed to satisfy; the new formula shows that Bjorken's estimate neglects not only acceleration effects but also the work done by pressure during expansion.

## Method

The authors start from the 1+1 dimensional CKCJ exact solution of relativistic perfect fluid hydrodynamics, which generalizes the earlier CNC solution to a realistic equation of state with arbitrary speed of sound. They evaluate the initial energy density at midrapidity by using the proportionality between final state energy density and particle density, expressing the average transverse mass per particle as the ratio of final state energy density to particle density, and then using the CKCJ solution's energy density field in the limit of small space-time rapidity at the final proper time.

## Key numbers

- The exact CKCJ initial energy density formula is: ε₀(κ,λ) = ε₀^Bj × (2λ-1) × (τ_f/τ₀)^{λ(1+1/κ)-1}
- For fits to CMS p+p data at √s = 7 TeV, the difference between the conjectured CNC formula and the new exact result is only 2%
- For fits to PHOBOS Au+Au data at √s_NN = 200 GeV (40-50% centrality), the deviation between the conjectured and exact formulas is almost 12%
- The speed of sound is fixed to c_s² = 1/κ = 0.1 in both the CMS p+p and PHOBOS Au+Au fits
- The effective temperature used for CMS p+p fits at √s = 7 TeV is T_eff = 145 MeV; for PHOBOS Au+Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV it is T_eff = 202 MeV

## Context

This work extends the 1+1 dimensional CNC solution of Csörgő, Nagy and Csanád (2006, 2007), which was valid only for a superhard equation of state (κ=1, c_s²=1), to a new family of solutions (CKCJ) with realistic equation of state. The new exact result supersedes Bjorken's original estimate (Bjorken, 1983) and the conjectured κ-dependent CNC formula (Csörgő, 2008), showing that Bjorken's estimate neglects both acceleration effects and the work done by pressure. The results enable more accurate estimation of initial energy densities in heavy ion and hadron collisions, with further generalizations to three-dimensional expanding fireballs and temperature-dependent speed of sound being explored.
