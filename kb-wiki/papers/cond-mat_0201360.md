# Meron-Cluster Approach to Systems of Strongly Correlated Electrons

> Numerical simulations of strongly correlated electron systems suffer from the notorious fermion sign problem which has prevented progress in understanding if systems like the Hubbard model display high-temperature superconductivity. Here we show how the fermion sign problem can be solved completely with meron-cluster methods in a large class of models of strongly correlated electron systems, some of which are in the extended Hubbard model family and show s-wave superconductivity. In these models we also find that on-site repulsion can even coexist with a weak chemical potential without introducing sign problems. We argue that since these models can be simulated efficiently using cluster algorithms they are ideal for studying many of the interesting phenomena in strongly correlated electron systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the fermion sign problem can be solved completely using meron-cluster methods for a class of models related to the extended Hubbard model family, but the method does not apply to the standard Hubbard model Hamiltonian itself; additional terms must be included, and the modifications do not affect the symmetries of the problem.

## Method

The authors formulate the fermionic path integral in the Fock state basis using occupation numbers and fermion world-lines, then re-write the partition function as a sum over configurations containing both fermions and bond variables. Clusters are defined as sets of connected lattice sites whose flip exchanges occupied and empty sites; clusters whose flip changes the sign are called merons. The method requires three properties: the Boltzmann weight magnitude must be invariant under cluster flips, the effect of a cluster flip on the sign must be independent of other clusters, and a reference configuration with positive weight must be reachable from any configuration.

## Key numbers

* The average sign in the bosonic ensemble is given by ⟨Sign⟩ = exp(−β V Δf), where β is inverse temperature, V is volume, and Δf is the free energy density difference between fermionic and bosonic systems.
* To determine the average sign with sufficient accuracy requires N = exp(2 β V Δf) measurements, making large volumes and low temperatures impossible in practice.
* The transfer matrix element for fermion hopping is cosh(ε t/2) for diagonal configurations and sinh(ε t/2) for off-diagonal configurations, where ε = β/M is the Euclidean time lattice spacing and t is the hopping parameter.
* The Boltzmann weights for plaquette configurations include exp[−ε(U/2 + μ/2d)] for the [0,0,0,0] state and exp[−ε(U/2 − μ/2d)] for the [1,1,1,1] state, where U is the on-site interaction and μ is the chemical potential.

## Context

This work extends the meron-cluster method previously developed for spinless lattice fermions (Chandrasekharan 1999, 2000) to fermions with spin and to systems in the Hubbard model family. The method builds on earlier attempts at constructing fermion cluster algorithms (Wiese 1993, Ammon 1998) and on the loop-cluster algorithm for quantum spin systems (Everz 1993, 1997). The approach enables efficient numerical simulation of strongly correlated electron models that are qualitatively similar to the standard Hubbard model, potentially allowing studies of phenomena such as s-wave superconductivity without the fermion sign problem.
