# Evolution of the moments of multiplicity distributions

> Measured moments of the multiplicity distribution for a given sort of particles are used in the literature for the determination of the phase transition parameters of hot QCD matter in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. We argue that the subsequent cooling in the hadronic phase, however, may drive the multiplicity distribution out of equilibrium. We use a master equation for the description of the evolution of the multiplicity distribution to demonstrate how the different moments depart away from their equilibrium values. If such moments were measured and interpreted as if they were equilibrated, one would obtain different apparent temperatures from different moments.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the subsequent cooling of fireballs in the hadronic phase after hadronization can drive the multiplicity distribution out of equilibrium, and that if measured moments are interpreted as if they were equilibrated, one would obtain different apparent temperatures from different moments. The authors provide a qualitative demonstration, not a quantitative prediction for a specific experimental system.

## Method

The authors use a master equation, adopted from Ko (2000), that describes the time evolution of the full multiplicity distribution for a species carrying a U(1) conserved charge, undergoing the reaction \(a_1a_2 \leftrightarrow b_1 b_2\). They derive analytical expressions for the equilibrium values of the first four factorial moments in terms of modified Bessel functions. They then numerically evolve the master equation under two scenarios: (1) thermalisation at constant temperature to study relaxation times, and (2) a cooling fireball with Bjorken one-dimensional boost-invariant expansion, using the specific reaction \(\pi^+n \leftrightarrow K^+ \Lambda\) with a parametrised cross-section.

## Key numbers

- The initial temperature for the cooling scenario is set to \(T=165\) MeV, dropping to a final kinetic freeze-out temperature of \(100\) MeV.
- The final time for the cooling evolution is set to \(10\) fm/\(c\), with an initial time \(t_0 = 2.2\) fm/\(c\).
- The initial volume is \(V_0 = 125\) fm\(^3\).
- The parameter \(\varepsilon\) in the constant-temperature relaxation study is set to \(0.1\), with an initial mean multiplicity \(n_0 = 0.005\).
- The threshold energy for the \(\pi^+n \leftrightarrow K^+ \Lambda\) reaction is approximately \(530\) MeV above the masses of the incoming particles.

## Context

This work extends studies that relate moments of multiplicity distributions to susceptibilities from lattice QCD and the statistical model, such as those by Alba (2014) and Bellwied (2018), which showed disagreements between temperatures extracted from first moments (yields) and higher moments. The paper goes beyond the work of Stephanov (2009) by including inelastic collisions after chemical freeze-out. It enables a cautionary framework for interpreting experimental data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, specifically the kaon multiplicity fluctuations measured by the STAR collaboration (Adamczyk 2017).
