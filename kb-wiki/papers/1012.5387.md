# Superfluid phases of triplet pairing and neutrino emission from neutron stars

> Neutrino energy losses through neutral weak currents in the triplet-spin superfluid neutron liquid are studied for the case of condensate involving several magnetic quantum numbers. Low-energy excitations of the multicomponent condensate in the timelike domain of the energy and momentum are analyzed. Along with the well-known excitations in the form of broken Cooper pairs, the theoretical analysis predicts the existence of collective waves of spin density at very low energy. Because of a rather small excitation energy of spin waves, their decay leads to a substantial neutrino emission at the lowest temperatures, when all other mechanisms of neutrino energy loss are killed by a superfluidity. Neutrino energy losses caused by the pair recombination and spin-wave decays are examined in all of the multicomponent phases that might represent the ground state of the condensate, according to modern theories, and for the case when a phase transition occurs in the condensate at some temperature. Our estimate predicts a sharp increase in the neutrino energy losses followed by a decrease, along with a decrease in the temperature, that takes place more rapidly than it would without the phase transition. We demonstrate the important role of the neutrino radiation caused by the decay of spin waves in the cooling of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that collective spin-density waves (spin waves) exist in multicomponent triplet-spin superfluid neutron condensates, and that their decay into neutrino pairs produces substantial neutrino emission at the lowest temperatures, when all other neutrino-loss mechanisms are suppressed by superfluidity. The claim is conditional on the existence of a phase transition in the condensate at some temperature, which produces a sharp increase followed by a rapid decrease in neutrino energy losses as temperature drops.

## Method

The author derived the linear response of a multicomponent triplet-spin superfluid neutron liquid to an external axial-vector field using the Matsubara formalism and Dyson equations for the effective three-point vertices, incorporating Fermi-liquid interactions via Landau parameters. The dispersion equation for spin-density oscillations was obtained from the poles of the anomalous vertices, and the neutrino energy-loss rates from both broken Cooper pair recombination and spin-wave decay were evaluated for all unitary phases of the \(^{3}P_{2}\) condensate (with magnetic quantum numbers \(m_j = 0, \pm1, \pm2\)) in the limit of zero momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- The spin-wave excitation energy is smaller than the superfluid energy gap, enabling decay into neutrino pairs at the lowest temperatures.
- The angle-averaged energy gap is approximated as \(\Delta^2 \bar{b}^2 \rightarrow \langle \Delta^2 \bar{b}^2 \rangle = \Delta^2\) in the quasiparticle energy.
- The gap amplitude \(\Delta(T)\) is temperature-dependent and real in the absence of external fields.
- The neutrino energy losses exhibit a sharp increase followed by a decrease as temperature decreases, occurring more rapidly than without a phase transition.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous prediction of spin waves in the one-component \(^{3}P_{2}\) condensate with \(m_j = 0\) (Leinson 2010a, 2010b) to the multicomponent phases that may represent the true ground state of neutron superfluid condensates, as identified by Khodel (2001) and Clark (2006). It contrasts with studies of spin modes in \(p\)-wave superfluid \(^{3}\text{He}\) (Maki 1974, Wolfle 1976), where spin-orbit symmetry allows separate spin and orbital rotations, a symmetry absent in neutron matter due to spin-orbit-dominated pairing. The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star cooling, particularly at late stages when standard pair-recombination neutrino emission is suppressed by superfluidity.
