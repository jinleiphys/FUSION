# Orbital Angular Momentum Small-$x$ Evolution: Exact Results in the Large-$N_c$ Limit

> We construct an exact solution to the revised small-$x$ orbital angular momentum (OAM) evolution equations derived recently, based on an earlier work. These equations are derived in the double logarithmic approximation (summing powers of $α_s \ln^2(1/x)$ with $α_s$ the strong coupling constant and $x$ the Bjorken $x$ variable) and the large-$N_c$ limit, with $N_c$ the number of quark colors. From our solution, we extract the small-$x$, large-$N_c$ expressions of the quark and gluon OAM distributions. Additionally, we determine the large-$N_c$ small-$x$ asymptotics of the OAM distributions to be \begin{align} \notag L_{q+\bar{q}}(x,Q^2) \sim L_G(x,Q^2) \sim ΔΣ(x,Q^2) \sim ΔG(x,Q^2) \sim \left(\frac{1}{x} \right)^{α_h}, \end{align} with the intercept $α_h$ the same as obtained in the small-$x$ helicity evolution, which can be approximated as $α_h \approx 3.66074 \sqrt{\frac{α_s N_c}{2π}}$. This result is in complete agreement with the literature. Additionally, we calculate the ratio of the quark and gluon OAM distributions to the flavor-singlet quark and gluon helicity parton distribution functions respectively in the small-$x$ region.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an exact analytic solution to the revised small-x orbital angular momentum (OAM) evolution equations in the large-Nc limit and double logarithmic approximation, showing that the quark and gluon OAM distributions have the same small-x asymptotic intercept as the helicity parton distribution functions, which resolves a previous discrepancy where the KPS-based analysis had found more than a 50% difference between the quark and gluon OAM intercepts.

## Method

The author solves the coupled evolution equations for the first impact-parameter moments of polarized dipole amplitudes (the "moment amplitudes" I3, I4, I5, I6) using the double Laplace transform method previously applied to the helicity evolution equations by Borden (2023). The moment amplitude evolution equations mix with the helicity dipole amplitudes G and G2 and their neighbor amplitudes, so the solution uses the known analytic solution for the helicity sector from Borden (2023) as input. The resulting moment amplitudes are then related to the quark and gluon OAM distributions through relations derived in Kovchegov (2023) and Kovchegov (2019).

## Key numbers

- The small-x asymptotic intercept for all OAM distributions and helicity PDFs is alpha_h approximately equal to 3.66074 times the square root of (alpha_s N_c / (2 pi)), where alpha_s is the strong coupling constant and N_c is the number of quark colors
- The quark OAM distribution L_{q+qbar}(x,Q^2) and gluon OAM distribution L_G(x,Q^2) both scale as (1/x)^{alpha_h} at small x, matching the behavior of the flavor-singlet quark helicity PDF Delta Sigma(x,Q^2) and gluon helicity PDF Delta G(x,Q^2)
- The evolution equations are derived in the double logarithmic approximation summing powers of alpha_s ln^2(1/x), with x the Bjorken x variable

## Context

This work extends the revised small-x evolution equations for OAM distributions derived in Kovchegov (2023), which corrected the earlier KPS-based analysis in Kovchegov (2019) that had found significantly different intercepts for quark and gluon OAM. The analytic solution confirms the numerical findings of Kovchegov (2023) and agrees with the OAM asymptotics obtained by Boussarie (2019) using the infrared evolution equation formalism. The results enable analytic calculation of the ratios of OAM distributions to helicity PDFs in the small-x region, providing theoretical input needed to constrain the proton spin contributions from unmeasured low-x values at future experiments such as the Electron-Ion Collider.
