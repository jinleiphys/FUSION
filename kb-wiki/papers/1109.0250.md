# Perturbative and Non-Perturbative Origins of the Proton Sea

> Deep Inelastic Scattering and Drell-Yan experiments have measured a light flavor asymmetry in the proton sea. The excess of dbar over ubar quarks can be understood in many models, but the ratio dbar(x)/ubar(x) measured by Fermilab E866 has not been successfully described. Fermilab E-906 will probe the kinematic dependence of this ratio with better resolution and extend it to higher x. We have developed a hybrid model that includes both perturbative and non-perturbative contributions to the proton sea. A meson cloud formalism is used to represent the non-perturbative fluctuation of the proton into meson-baryon states. We include perturbative processes by using a statistical model that uses Fock states of quarks, antiquarks and gluons to represent the parton distributions of the 'bare' hadrons in the meson cloud. We compare our results to the E866 data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that both perturbative and non-perturbative processes contribute to the proton sea asymmetry, but their hybrid model, like the individual meson cloud and statistical models, fails to describe the experimental trend of the ratio dbar(x)/ubar(x) returning to symmetry or showing ubar dominance at larger x values.

## Method

The authors construct a hybrid model combining a meson cloud formalism for non-perturbative contributions with a statistical model using Fock states of quarks, antiquarks, and gluons for perturbative contributions. The statistical model provides the parton distributions for the 'bare' hadrons in the meson cloud expansion, and the resulting quark distributions are compared to Fermilab E866 data.

## Key numbers

- The integrated asymmetry measured by E866 is 0.118 ± 0.012.
- The statistical model alone gives an integrated asymmetry of 0.123.
- In the meson cloud calculation with cutoffs Λ_NM = 0.92 and Λ_ΔM = 0.98, the bare proton probability Z = 0.58, with ⟨Nπ⟩ = 0.20, ⟨Δπ⟩ = 0.11, and higher order terms representing 11% of the proton.
- In the hybrid model with cutoffs Λ_NM = 0.83 and Λ_ΔM = 1.05, the bare proton probability Z = 0.63, with ⟨Nπ⟩ = 0.15, ⟨Δπ⟩ = 0.15, and higher order terms representing 8% of the proton.

## Context

This work extends earlier meson cloud models by Thomas (1983) and Henley and Miller, and the statistical model of Zhang et al., which individually could not describe the E866 ratio data at larger x. The hybrid model aims to incorporate both mechanisms, but still fails to match the experimental trend at higher x. The upcoming Fermilab E-906/SeaQuest experiment will provide higher statistics data to determine the magnitude of disagreement between current theory and experiment.
