# The Equation of State of Nuclear Matter in Heavy Ion Collisions at CERN-SPS Energies from the Viewpoint of Relativistic Hydrodynamics

> We present refits of single inclusive cross section data of mesons and baryons for central 158 $AGeV$ Pb+Pb collisions measured by the NA44 and NA49 Collaborations. In the theoretical approach we use the relativistic hydrodynamical code HYLANDER-C. We investigate several equations of state in their capability to describe the measured single inclusive momentum distributions. Based on the obtained fits we present results of the calculated Bose-Einstein correlation functions of identical pion pairs and discuss their features in comparison to measurements.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that several different equations of state for nuclear matter can reproduce the measured single inclusive momentum distributions of mesons and baryons from central 158 AGeV Pb+Pb collisions at CERN-SPS, but the resulting Bose-Einstein correlation functions of identical pion pairs show distinct features that may discriminate between these equations of state when compared to experimental data.

## Method
The authors used the relativistic hydrodynamical code HYLANDER-C to refit single inclusive cross section data of mesons and baryons from central 158 AGeV Pb+Pb collisions measured by the NA44 and NA49 Collaborations. They tested multiple equations of state for nuclear matter by fitting the calculated momentum distributions to the experimental data, then computed Bose-Einstein correlation functions for identical pion pairs from the best-fit hydrodynamic solutions.

## Key numbers
- Central collision energy: 158 AGeV for Pb+Pb collisions at CERN-SPS
- System: central Pb+Pb collisions measured by NA44 and NA49 Collaborations

## Context
This work extends the application of relativistic hydrodynamics to heavy ion collisions at CERN-SPS energies, building on previous hydrodynamic modeling approaches. The paper uses the HYLANDER-C code to systematically compare different equations of state against the same experimental dataset from NA44 and NA49. The calculation of Bose-Einstein correlation functions from the fitted hydrodynamic solutions provides a new way to discriminate between equations of state that otherwise fit the single-particle spectra equally well, enabling more stringent tests of nuclear matter properties in future comparisons with experimental correlation data.
