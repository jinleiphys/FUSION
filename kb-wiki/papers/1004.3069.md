# Dileptons from the nonequilibrium Quark-Gluon Plasma

> According to the dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) -- matched to reproduce lattice QCD results in thermodynamic limit, -- the constituents of the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) are massive and off-shell quasi-particles (quarks and gluons) with broad spectral functions. In order to address the electromagnetic radiation of the sQGP, we derive off-shell cross sections of $q\bar q\toγ^*$, $q\bar q\toγ^*+g$ and $qg\toγ^*q$($\bar q g\toγ^* \bar q$) reactions taking into account the effective propagators for quarks and gluons from the DQPM. Dilepton production in In+In collisions at 158 AGeV is studied by implementing these processes into the parton-hadron-string dynamics (PHSD) transport approach. The microscopic PHSD transport approach describes the full evolution of the heavy-ion collision: from the dynamics of quasi-particles in the sQGP phase (when the local energy density is above $\sim 1$ GeV/fm$^3$) through hadronization and to the following hadron interactions and off-shell propagation after the hadronization. A comparison to the data of the NA60 Collaboration shows that the low mass dilepton spectra are well described by including a collisional broadening of vector mesons, while the spectra in the intermediate mass range are dominated by off-shell quark-antiquark annihilation, quark Bremsstrahlung and gluon-Compton scattering in the nonperturbative QGP. In particular, the observed softening of the $m_T$ spectra at intermediate masses (1 GeV $\le M \le$ 3 GeV) is approximately reproduced.

## Key claim

The paper claims that dilepton production in the intermediate mass range (1 GeV ≤ M ≤ 3 GeV) from In+In collisions at 158 AGeV is dominated by off-shell quark-antiquark annihilation, quark Bremsstrahlung, and gluon-Compton scattering in the nonperturbative QGP, and that the observed softening of the m_T spectra in this mass range is approximately reproduced by the PHSD transport approach when these partonic channels are included.

## Method

The authors derived off-shell cross sections for the reactions q\bar q → γ*, q\bar q → γ* + g, and qg → γ* q (and the charge-conjugate) using effective propagators for quarks and gluons from the dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM), which is matched to reproduce lattice QCD results in thermodynamic equilibrium. These processes were implemented into the parton-hadron-string dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which describes the full evolution of heavy-ion collisions from the sQGP phase (local energy density above ~1 GeV/fm^3) through hadronization to hadron interactions. The calculations were compared to NA60 data for In+In collisions at 158 AGeV and to PHENIX data for Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- In+In collisions at 158 AGeV: the intermediate mass range for dileptons is defined as 1 GeV ≤ M ≤ 3 GeV, where partonic sources dominate.
- The threshold local energy density for the sQGP phase in PHSD is ~1 GeV/fm^3.
- For Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV, the HSD transport approach underestimates the PHENIX dilepton spectra in the invariant mass range from 0.2 to 0.6 GeV by an average factor of ~3 in the 'collisional broadening' scenario.
- The running coupling α_S in the DQPM is of order O(1), indicating higher-order diagrams are not subleading.
- Off-shell Drell-Yan cross sections were computed for quark masses m_1 = 0.3 GeV, m_2 = 0.15 GeV; m_1 = 0.3 GeV, m_2 = 0.3 GeV; m_1 = 0.3 GeV, m_2 = 0.6 GeV; and m_1 = 0.6 GeV, m_2 = 0.6 GeV.
- For the gluon Bremsstrahlung channel at √s = 4 GeV, the on-shell cross section used a gluon mass cut μ_cut = 0.206 GeV, while off-shell calculations used gluon mass μ = 0.8 GeV and quark masses m_q = 0.6 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the HSD transport approach (Cassing and Bratkovskaya, 1999; Bratkovskaya, 1997; Ehehalt and Cassing, 1996) by incorporating off-shell partonic dilepton production channels from the sQGP, building on the DQPM framework (Cassing, 2006). The results confirm the 'melting rho' scenario favored by NA60 data (NA60 Collaboration, 2008) and CERES data (CERES Collaboration), where the rho meson spectral function broadens in medium rather than shifting in mass. The paper identifies that the PHENIX low-mass enhancement (0.2 to 0.6 GeV) in Au+Au collisions at RHIC cannot be explained by hadronic sources alone, similar to findings by van Hees and Rapp (2008) and Dusling and Zahed (2008), and proposes that off-shell partonic radiation from the sQGP may account for this excess, enabling future quantitative tests at RHIC energies.
