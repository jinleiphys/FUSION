# Turbulent thermalization of weakly coupled non-abelian plasmas

> We study the dynamics of weakly coupled non-abelian plasmas within the frameworks of classical-statistical lattice gauge-theory and kinetic theory. We focus on a class of systems which are highly occupied, isotropic at all times and initially characterized by a single momentum scale. These represent an idealized version of the situation in relativistic heavy ion-collisions in the color-glass condensate picture, where on a time scale $1/Q_s$ after the collision of heavy nuclei a longitudinally expanding plasma characterized by the saturation scale $Q_s$ is formed. Our results indicate that the system evolves according to a turbulent Kolmogorov cascade in the classical regime. Taking this into account, the kinetic description is able to reproduce characteristic features of the evolution correctly.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the thermalization of weakly coupled non-abelian plasmas proceeds via a turbulent Kolmogorov cascade with exponent κ ≃ 4/3, rather than through a quasi-thermal evolution with κ = 1, but this conclusion is explicitly limited to homogeneous, isotropic systems without longitudinal expansion, which is an idealized version of the situation in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The author combines kinetic theory analysis with classical-statistical lattice gauge-theory simulations of SU(2) Yang-Mills theory in 3+1 dimensions. The kinetic theory extends previous work by Baier et al. (2001) and Blaizot et al. (2011) to account for non-thermal fixed points, while the lattice simulations use temporal axial gauge with a Coulomb-type gauge-fixing constraint to define quasi-particle observables from electric and magnetic two-point correlation functions. The evolution of the system is studied for initial conditions with parametrically large occupancies f(p) ∼ α^{-c} for momenta below a scale Q, with 0 < c < 1, and the scaling exponent κ is extracted from fits to the power-law region of the spectrum.

## Key numbers

* The scaling exponent extracted from the spectrum of the correlation function f(t,p) ∼ ⟨EE⟩/|p| is κ ≃ 4/3 over a large time-scale, with the thermal value κ = 1 clearly ruled out over the entire simulation time.
* The hard scale Λ evolves as Λ ∼ Q (t/t_start)^{1/7}, where t_start ∼ Q^{-1} α^{2c-2} is the time scale on which initially hard excitations change appreciably.
* The soft scale (Λ_s/Q)^κ evolves as (Qt)^{(κ-4)/7}, and fits to this scaling yield κ ≃ 1.3 ± 0.1, consistent with κ = 4/3.
* The thermalization time scale is parametrically t_eq ∼ Q^{-1} α^{-2 + c/4}, independent of the exponent κ.
* For overoccupied systems with initial occupancy n_0 = 0.05, 0.1, 0.2, 0.5, and 1, the evolution of the soft scale (Λ_s/Q)^κ as a function of normalized time t/t_start collapses approximately onto a single curve at late times t ≫ t_start.

## Context

This work extends the kinetic theory analysis of Baier et al. (2001) and Blaizot et al. (2011) by incorporating the possibility of a non-thermal fixed point characterized by a turbulent Kolmogorov cascade, and it provides numerical evidence from classical-statistical lattice simulations that such a cascade with exponent κ = 4/3 is realized. The results are consistent with the turbulent behavior observed in early-universe cosmology and cold atomic gas systems, and they contradict the quasi-thermal evolution scenario (κ = 1) that had been assumed in earlier kinetic theory treatments. The paper explicitly notes that the results cannot be directly transferred to expanding systems relevant for heavy-ion collisions, where longitudinal expansion and plasma instabilities are expected to play an important role.
