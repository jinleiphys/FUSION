# A simple model for rapidity fluctuations in the initial state of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Two-particle pseudorapidity correlations are analyzed in a simple model, where in the initial stage of the reaction multiple sources, extended in rapidity, are created. We show how the fluctuations of the length of the sources in rapidity generate correlations in the initial entropy deposition, which later contribute to the observed longitudinal correlations in hadron production. Our analysis, which is analytic and leads to straightforward formulas, allows us to understand the structure of the correlations, in particular to identify the component related to the fluctuation of the numbers of sources and the component from the length fluctuations. We also present the results in terms of the expansion in the basis of the Legendre polynomials. A number of further effects is discussed, such as smearing of the pseudorapidity distributions or resonance decays. Our results reproduce qualitatively and semiquantitatively the basic features of the recent measurements at the LHC.

# A simple model for rapidity fluctuations in the initial state of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The dominant contribution to two-particle pseudorapidity correlations comes from fluctuations in the length of longitudinally-extended sources (strings or flux tubes), not from fluctuations in the number of sources as previously emphasized by Bzdak and Teaney (2012). The model reproduces qualitatively and semiquantitatively the basic features of ATLAS measurements at the LHC, but the higher-rank coefficients show discrepancies that indicate sensitivity to details not captured by the analytic calculation.

## Method

The authors construct an analytic model where multiple independent sources (strings or flux tubes) extend along rapidity, with one end-point fixed to a nucleon or constituent from nucleus A or B and the other end-point randomly and uniformly distributed in the central rapidity range. They derive closed-form expressions for the two-particle pseudorapidity correlation function C(eta1, eta2) and its expansion coefficients a_nm in Legendre polynomials, then compare these to ATLAS data for Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV, p-Pb at 5.02 TeV, and p-p at 13 TeV using GLISSANDO simulations for the wounded nucleon model statistics.

## Key numbers

- For Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, centrality 30-40%, the half-width of the ridge structure in the eta1-eta2 coordinate from length fluctuations is about 2 units of pseudorapidity
- The a_11 coefficient scales as a_11 = r * (s(omega)+1)/5 * (Y/y_b) * 1/<N> to leading order in Y/y_b, where Y=2.4 is the ATLAS acceptance range
- For the model with length fluctuations, the overlaid strength parameter s(omega) ~ 0.1, compared to s(omega) ~ 1 for the model without length fluctuations
- The proportionality constants between observed charged hadrons and sources are: <N_ch> = 4.7 <N_+> for Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV, <N_ch> = 5.1 <N_A> for p-Pb at 5.02 TeV, and <N_ch> = 8.1 <N_+> for p-p at 13 TeV
- The difference between models with and without length fluctuations for a_11 is about a factor of 2

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Bzdak and Teaney (2012), who showed that fluctuations in the number of sources generate eta1*eta2 correlations, by adding the mechanism of fluctuating source lengths. The model has been applied numerically by Bozek and collaborators (2015) to describe event-plane decorrelation (the torque effect) reported by the CMS Collaboration. The analytic results explain the experimental observation by ATLAS of universal 1/N_ch scaling of a_nm coefficients across Pb-Pb, p-Pb, and p-p systems, and support a wounded quark picture over a wounded nucleon picture. The work enables further study of how initial-state longitudinal fluctuations propagate through hydrodynamic evolution to final-state hadron correlations.
