# Gyromagnetic g_s factors of the spin-1/2 particles in the (1/2+,1/2-,3/2-) triad of the four-vector spinor, ψ_μ, irreducibility, and linearity

> We show that the spin (1/2-) particle from the (1/2,1)+(1,1/2) Lorentz irreducible sector of the four-vector spinor can not be described within a linear formalism but behaves as a genuinely quadratic fermion satisfying the generalized Feynman-Gell-Mann equation with a gyromagnetic factor of (-2/3). In contrast, spin (1/2 +) from the (1/2,0)+(0,1/2) sector is confirmed as a genuine linear Dirac fermion whose gyromagnetic factor takes the value of two units.We calculate Compton scatterings off each one of the two targets and obtain both times well behaved cross sections in the ultra relativistic limit and in accord with unitarity.

## Key claim

The spin-1/2- particle residing in the (1/2,1)⊕(1,1/2) irreducible sector of the four-vector spinor cannot be described within a linear formalism but behaves as a genuinely quadratic fermion satisfying the generalized Feynman-Gell-Mann equation with a gyromagnetic factor of -2/3, while the spin-1/2+ particle from the (1/2,0)⊕(0,1/2) sector is confirmed as a genuine linear Dirac fermion with gyromagnetic factor of 2.

## Method

The authors examine the gauged Klein-Gordon equation extended by a g_s σ_{μν}F^{μν}/4 interaction term, where g_s is the gyromagnetic factor, and apply it to the spin-1/2 degrees of freedom within the four-vector spinor ψ_μ. They use Poincaré covariant projectors based on the squared Pauli-Lubanski vector operator to separate the irreducible (1/2,0)⊕(0,1/2) and (1/2,1)⊕(1,1/2) sectors, then compute Compton scattering cross sections for both spin-1/2+ and spin-1/2- targets to verify the gyromagnetic factors and consistency with unitarity.

## Key numbers

- The spin-1/2+ particle from the (1/2,0)⊕(0,1/2) sector has gyromagnetic factor g_{(1/2+)} = 2
- The spin-1/2- particle from the (1/2,1)⊕(1,1/2) sector has gyromagnetic factor g_{(1/2-)} = -2/3
- The spin-3/2- particle within the four-vector spinor has gyromagnetic factor g_{(3/2-)} = 2, fixed from the requirement of causal propagation in an electromagnetic environment

## Context

This work extends the Poincaré covariant projector method developed by Napsuciale (2006) and Delgado Acosta (2010) for describing higher-spin particles within second-order formalisms. It contradicts the assumption that all spin-1/2 fermions must obey the linear Dirac equation with g_s=2, showing instead that the spin-1/2- companion to the spin-3/2- within the four-vector spinor triad requires a quadratic description. The results enable unified treatment of all spin sectors in the four-vector spinor on equal footing using second-order wave equations, which may be relevant for studying interferences between spin-3/2- and lower-spin components in physical processes.
