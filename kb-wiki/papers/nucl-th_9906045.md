# Effective field theory for nuclear matter

> We apply the relativistic chiral Lagrangian to the nuclear equation of state. An effective chiral power expansion scheme, which is constructed to work around nuclear saturation density, is presented. The leading and subleading terms are evaluated and are shown to provide an equation of state with excellent saturation properties. Our saturation mechanism is found to probe details of the nuclear pion dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper presents a chiral power expansion scheme for nuclear matter that is constructed specifically to work around nuclear saturation density, rather than at low densities, and claims that the leading and subleading terms provide an equation of state with "excellent saturation properties" while probing details of nuclear pion dynamics.

## Method

The author applies the relativistic chiral Lagrangian to the nuclear equation of state, developing an effective chiral power expansion scheme where the Fermi momentum is identified as a small scale \(k_F \sim Q\) alongside the pion mass. The method involves a systematic resummation of the density expansion, evaluating leading and subleading chiral orders (Q^6 and Q^7) using a density-dependent effective Lagrangian with local two-nucleon vertices, where pionic effects are treated perturbatively on top of a parquet resummation for the local interaction.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density corresponds to Fermi momentum \(k_{F,0} \simeq 265\) MeV
- The deuteron binding energy \(\epsilon_D \simeq 2\) MeV defines a typical small scale \(\Lambda_S \sim \sqrt{m_N \epsilon_D}\)
- The typical large scale in the expansion is \(\Lambda_L \simeq 4\pi f_\pi \simeq 1\) GeV
- The leading chiral contribution is of order \(Q^6\), proportional to \(k_F^6\) times dimensionless functions
- The subleading contributions are of chiral order \(Q^7\)

## Context

This work extends the author's previous development of chiral power counting rules for nucleon-nucleon scattering (Lutz 1997, Lutz 1998) to the nuclear many-body problem, building on Weinberg's (1990) proposal to derive chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials. The approach contrasts with the heavy baryon formulation of chiral perturbation theory developed by Manohar (1992) and Bernard (1995), and with the potential approach of Kaplan, Savage and Wise (1998). The scheme enables a systematic effective field theory treatment of nuclear matter around saturation density, though the author notes that a fully microscopic calculation solving the parquet theory for the local interaction remains to be completed.
