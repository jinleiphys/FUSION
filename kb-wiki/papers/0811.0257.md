# Generic stability of dissipative non-relativistic and relativistic fluids

> The linear stability of the homogeneous equilibrium of non-relativistic fluids with mass flux and special relativistic fluids with the absolute value of the energy vector as internal energy is investigated. It is proved that the equilibrium is asymptotically stable in both cases due to purely thermodynamic restrictions; the only requirements are the thermodynamic stability and the nonnegativity of the transport coefficients.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the homogeneous equilibrium of both non-relativistic fluids with mass flux and special relativistic fluids with a novel definition of internal energy (the absolute value of the energy vector) is asymptotically stable under purely thermodynamic restrictions, requiring only thermodynamic stability (concave entropy) and nonnegativity of transport coefficients, without any additional constraints on equations of state or material parameters.

## Method

The author linearizes the balance equations (particle number, energy, momentum) around homogeneous equilibrium for both non-relativistic Fourier-Fick-Navier-Stokes fluids and special relativistic fluids with a generalized internal energy definition. Exponential plane-wave perturbations are introduced, and the resulting dispersion relations are analyzed using the Routh-Hurwitz criteria to determine conditions for asymptotic stability. The relativistic treatment uses a general frame (not fixed to Eckart or Landau-Lifshitz conventions) and incorporates a relaxation-type heat conduction equation.

## Key numbers

- For non-relativistic fluids: the shear viscosity coefficient satisfies η > 0, the bulk viscosity coefficient satisfies η_v > 0, the Fourier heat conduction coefficient satisfies λ > 0, the diffusion coefficient satisfies ξ > 0, and the cross-coupling coefficients satisfy λξ - χ² > 0 (with χ = 0 assumed in the stability analysis)
- For non-relativistic fluids: the thermodynamic stability conditions require ∂(1/T)/∂ε < 0, ∂(μ/T)/∂n > 0, and (∂(1/T)/∂ε)(∂(μ/T)/∂n) + (∂(1/T)/∂n)² ≤ 0
- For non-relativistic fluids: the determinant of submatrix R_nr gives ρΓ + ηk² = 0, resulting in a negative Γ (stable mode)
- For relativistic fluids: the internal energy is defined as ε = √(e² - q_a q^a), with series expansion ε ≈ e - q²/(2e) + ... when energy density exceeds momentum density
- For relativistic fluids: the relaxation time in the heat conduction equation is τ = λ/e

## Context

This work extends the author's previous approach (Ván 2008) based on a novel concept of internal energy derived from compatibility of basic balances and entropy inequality. It directly addresses the instability and acausality problems of Eckart's (1940) relativistic fluid theory and the Israel-Stewart (1976, 1979) theory, which requires additional constraints on equations of state and material parameters beyond thermodynamic requirements. The paper proves that with the proposed internal energy definition, generic stability follows from standard thermodynamic stability and positive transport coefficients alone, enabling a simpler foundation for relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics applicable to heavy-ion collision modeling (Muronga 2004, Heinz et al. 2006, Baier and Romatschke 2007, Denicol et al. 2008).
