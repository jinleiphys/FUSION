# Dual superconductivity, monopole condensation and confining string in low-energy Yang-Mills theory. Part I

> We show that the QCD vacuum (without dynamical quarks) is a dual superconductor at least in the low-energy region in the sense that monopole condensation does really occur. In fact, we derive the dual Ginzburg-Landau theory (i.e., dual Abelian Higgs model) directly from the SU(2) Yang-Mills theory by adopting the maximal Abelian gauge. The dual superconductor can be on the border between type II and type I, excluding the London limit. The masses of the dual Abelian gauge field is expressed by the Yang-Mills gauge coupling constant and the mass of the off-diagonal gluon of the original Yang-Mills theory. Moreover, we can rewrite the Yang-Mills theory into an theory written in terms of the Abelian magnetic monopole alone at least in the low-energy region. Magnetic monopole condensation originates in the non-zero mass of off-diagonal gluons. Finally, we derive the confining string theory describing the low-energy Gluodynamics. Then the area law of the large Wilson loop is an immediate consequence of these constructions. Three low-energy effective theories give the same string tension.

## Key claim

The QCD vacuum (without dynamical quarks) is a dual superconductor in the low-energy region, with monopole condensation occurring due to the non-zero mass of off-diagonal gluons, but the dual superconductor is on the border between type II and type I, excluding the London limit.

## Method

The author derives the dual Ginzburg-Landau (DGL) theory directly from SU(2) Yang-Mills theory by adopting the maximal Abelian (MA) gauge, using the non-Abelian Stokes theorem to rewrite the Wilson loop operator in terms of diagonal components, then applying a cumulant expansion and integrating out off-diagonal components. The modified MA gauge includes a quartic ghost interaction term that causes ghost-anti-ghost condensation, which dynamically generates masses for off-diagonal gluons and ghosts, enabling the derivation of three equivalent low-energy effective theories: the DGL theory, a magnetic monopole theory, and a confining string theory.

## Key numbers

- Off-diagonal gluon mass \(M_A = 1.2\) GeV for SU(2), as calculated on a lattice by Amemiya and Suganuma (1999)
- The string tension \(\sigma_{st}\) from the monopole action agrees with that predicted by the DGL theory and the confining string theory
- Higher-order terms in the large \(N\) expansion are suppressed by \(N^{-2}\) compared to the leading order term

## Context

This work extends the dual superconductor picture proposed by Nambu (1974) and the Abelian projection idea of 't Hooft (1981), building on the infrared Abelian dominance suggested by Ezawa and Iwazaki (1982). It provides an analytical derivation that bridges perturbative QCD in the high-energy region with low-energy effective Abelian gauge theory, enabling the undetermined parameters in the low-energy effective theory to be expressed in terms of the original Yang-Mills gauge coupling constant \(g\) and the renormalization group invariant scale \(\Lambda_{QCD}\). The results are consistent with lattice simulations by Suzuki and Yotsuyanagi (1990) and subsequent work confirming monopole dominance in low-energy QCD.
