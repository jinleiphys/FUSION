# Effective nucleon-nucleon interaction and low-lying nuclear magnetic states

> We present a calculation of low energy magnetic states of doubly-closed-shell nuclei. Our results have been obtained within the random phase approximation using different nucleon-nucleon interactions, having zero- or finite-range and including a possible contribution in the tensor channel.

# Effective nucleon-nucleon interaction and low-lying nuclear magnetic states

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a purely phenomenological random phase approximation (RPA) approach, using Woods-Saxon single-particle bases and residual interactions tuned to selected experimental states, can produce a satisfactory description of low-energy magnetic spectra across multiple doubly-closed-shell nuclei. However, the self-consistent RPA calculations using the Gogny D1 interaction systematically invert the order of isospin doublets and disagree with experimental spectra, indicating a specific inadequacy in the isospin-dependent channels of that interaction.

## Method
The authors performed RPA calculations for the low-energy magnetic states of 12C, 16O, 40Ca, 48Ca, 90Zr, and 208Pb. In the phenomenological approach, single-particle bases were generated from Woods-Saxon wells, and four different residual nucleon-nucleon interactions were constructed: zero-range Landau-Migdal (LM) and Landau-Migdal with tensor-isospin (LMtt), and finite-range (FR) and finite-range with tensor-isospin (FRtt) interactions derived from the Argonne v18 potential. In the self-consistent approach, the Hartree-Fock single-particle basis and the RPA residual interaction both used the Gogny D1 interaction. For each calculated state, electromagnetic transverse response functions were computed and compared with experimental electron scattering data.

## Key numbers
- The first 2+ state in 12C and the first 3- states in other nuclei were used to set density-dependent terms of the phenomenological interactions.
- The low-energy magnetic spectrum of 208Pb below 8 MeV, particularly the 12- and 1+ states, was used to adjust the spin, spin-isospin, and tensor channels of the interactions.
- For 12C, the Gogny D1 interaction gave a 1+ state at 11.17 MeV and another at 7.73 MeV, while the FRtt interaction gave 13.87 MeV and 18.05 MeV respectively; the experimental energies are 12.71 MeV and 15.11 MeV.
- For 12C, the D1 interaction gave a 4- state at 19.16 MeV and another at 15.63 MeV, while FRtt gave 17.75 MeV and 19.49 MeV; experimental energies are 18.27 MeV and 19.15 MeV.

## Context
This work extends systematic RPA studies of magnetic states in doubly-closed-shell nuclei, following earlier phenomenological approaches by Blaizot (1977) and Speth (1977) that used density-dependent interactions. The self-consistent results with the Gogny D1 interaction, originally developed by Gogny (1975) and Decharge and Gogny (1980), reveal a systematic failure in reproducing the ordering of isospin doublets across all nuclei studied. The phenomenological approach enables calculation of neutrino scattering cross sections, with potential applications to supernova neutrino detection as discussed by Nowak (2004), where the tensor components of the residual interaction produce large differences in cross sections.
