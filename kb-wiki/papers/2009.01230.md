# Status of CME Search Before Isobar Collisions and Methods of Blind Analysis From STAR

> The STAR collaboration is currently pursuing the blind analysis of the data for isobar collisions that was performed at RHIC in the year 2018 to make a decisive test of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME). Why is it so difficult to detect signals of CME in the experiment? Do we really understand different sources of background? Why observing similar charge separation between p/d+A and A+A does not stop us from pursuing the search for CME? In this contribution, I attempt to address some of these questions and briefly outline a few recent STAR analyses based on new methods and observables to isolate the possible CME-driven signal and non-CME background contributions at the top RHIC energy. Finally, I describe the procedure for the blind analysis of the isobar data. An outstanding question remains -- what happens if we go down in energy? I address this by discussing how the new event-plane detector (EPD) upgrade provides a new capability at STAR towards CME search using the data from the RHIC BES-II program.

## Key claim

The STAR collaboration's blind analysis of 2018 isobar collisions (Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr) is expected to provide a decisive test of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) by exploiting the 10-18% higher magnetic field in Ru+Ru compared to Zr+Zr, but the outcome depends on controlling systematic uncertainties to within a few percent, a precision never before attempted in STAR correlation measurements.

## Method

The analysis uses the gamma-correlator Δγ = γ_OS - γ_SS, where γ = ⟨cos(φ_a^α + φ_b^β - 2Ψ_RP)⟩, measured with respect to multiple event planes (second-order from TPC, first-order from ZDC, forward planes from BBC and EPD). Differential measurements of Δγ versus invariant mass and pseudorapidity gap, mixed-harmonic correlators Δγ_123 with third-order planes, charge separation along participant versus spectator planes, the R-observable, and signed balance functions are all applied to separate CME signal from non-CME background. The blind analysis proceeds through four steps: mock data challenge, isobar-mixed analysis with frozen codes, isobar-blind analysis with limited single-species data for QA, and final unblinding.

## Key numbers

- Ru+Ru has 10-18% higher magnetic field than Zr+Zr, compared to about 4% difference in flow-driven background (Deng 2018, Schenke 2019)
- STAR collected more than 3 billion events for each isobar species in 2018
- The projection aimed for five-sigma significance or better in a scenario where Δγ has 80% non-CME background
- For the CME case: (Δγ_112/v2)^Ru+Ru / (Δγ_112/v2)^Zr+Zr > 1 and this ratio exceeds (Δγ_123/v3)^Ru+Ru / (Δγ_123/v3)^Zr+Zr
- For the CME case using spectator/participant plane method: f_CME^Ru+Ru > f_CME^Zr+Zr > 0
- The EPD covers pseudorapidity 2.1 < |η| < 5.1 for lower-energy CME searches

## Context

This work extends the long-standing CME search program at STAR, which began with the first non-zero Δγ measurement (Abelev 2009) that also identified non-CME backgrounds from neutral resonances (Voloshin 2004) and local charge conservation (Pratt 2010). The isobar collision approach was proposed by Voloshin (2010) alongside uranium collisions. The blind analysis methodology follows procedures outlined in Adam (2019). The results will enable a decisive test of CME at top RHIC energy, while the EPD upgrade opens new capabilities for CME searches at lower energies in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan phase-II program.
