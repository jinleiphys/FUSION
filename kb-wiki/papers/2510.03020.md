# Principal Components of Nuclear Mass Model Residuals

> Principal Component Analysis (PCA) is applied to the residuals of six widely used nuclear mass models to uncover systematic deviations and identify missing physical effects in theoretical nuclear mass predictions. By analyzing the principal components of nuclear mass model residuals, this study reveals that no single dominant pattern governs the discrepancies across models. Instead, the residual structures are largely uncorrelated, indicating that current nuclear mass models fail to capture underlying nuclear residual effects in distinct and model-specific ways. These findings suggest that improvements to nuclear mass models should be guided by model-specific residual analyses rather than a one-size-fits-all approach.

## Key claim

Principal Component Analysis (PCA) of residuals from six nuclear mass models reveals that no single dominant pattern governs the discrepancies across models; instead, the residual structures are largely uncorrelated and model-specific, contradicting the hope that a common missing physical effect could be identified and corrected uniformly.

## Method

PCA was applied to the residuals (experimental mass minus theoretical prediction) of six nuclear mass models (FRDM2012, HFB17, KTUY05, D1M, RMF, and LDM) evaluated on 2421 nuclei common to all models and the AME2020 experimental database. The covariance matrix of the centered residual vectors was diagonalized to extract up to six principal components (PCs), and each model's residual vector was projected onto these PCs to quantify the contribution of each independent error mode.

## Key numbers

* The overlap of the six mass models with AME2020 experimental data covers 2421 nuclei, forming the basis for the residual analysis.
* The eigenvalue of PC1 is scaled to 1; PC2 through PC6 have eigenvalues of 5.6e-1, 4.7e-1, 3.1e-1, 3.0e-1, and 2.7e-1 respectively, showing no single dominant component.
* PC1 contributes 34.4% of the residual variance across models, while the other PCs contribute between 10% and 20% each.
* The overlap of FRDM2012 residuals with PC1 is 0.7223; for LDM, the dominant overlap is with PC2 at 0.8819; for RMF, the dominant overlap is with PC3 at 0.8212.
* Adding the most important PC reduced the RMSD of the RMF model from 2279 keV to 1219 keV.

## Context

This work extends the application of PCA from nuclear mass models themselves (Wu 2024) to the residuals of those models, revealing that unlike the mass predictions (which share a dominant common PC), the residuals are largely uncorrelated. The findings contradict the assumption that a single missing physical effect could uniformly improve all models, and instead suggest that model-specific residual patterns should guide future refinements, particularly for the six models studied: FRDM2012, HFB17, KTUY05, D1M, RMF, and LDM.
