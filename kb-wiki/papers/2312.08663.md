# Analysis of $T_{cc}$ and $T_{bb}$ based on the hadronic molecular model and their spin multiplets

> ${T_{cc}(cc\bar{u}\bar{d})}^{+}$ has been reported by the LHCb experiment in 2022. The analysis using the Breit-Wigner parametrization found the small binding energy, $0.273$ MeV, which is measured from the threshold of $D^{*+}D^{0}$. In this paper, we consider $T_{cc}$ as a $DD^*$ hadronic molecule as a deuteron-like state. The one boson exchange model is employed as for the heavy meson interactions, where we determine the cut-off parameter $Λ$ to reproduce the reported binding energy of $T_{cc}$ with $I(J^P)=0(1^+)$. We discuss the properties of the bound state, and also search for $T_{cc}$ with the quantum numbers other than $0(1^{+})$. Futhermore, we analyze $T_{bb}$ as a bottom counterpart of $T_{cc}$, which involves two bottom quarks, and obtain several bound states. Finally we consider the light-cloud basis for wave functions of the doubly heavy tetraquarks in the heavy quark limit. Using the basis, we find the spin multiplets of their bound states, indicating the spin structures of diquarks in $T_{cc}$ and $T_{bb}$ with the finite quark masses.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the doubly charmed tetraquark \(T_{cc}\) can be described as a \(DD^*\) hadronic molecule using the one boson exchange model, but only when the sigma meson exchange is included; the one pion exchange potential alone cannot produce a bound state for \(T_{cc}\) with reasonable cutoff parameters, contrary to what might be expected from the small experimental binding energy.

## Method

The authors employed the one boson exchange potential (OBEP) including pion, rho, omega, and sigma meson exchanges to describe interactions between heavy mesons (\(D^{(*)}D^{(*)}\) and \(B^{(*)}B^{(*)}\)) in the hadronic molecular model. They solved the coupled-channel Schrodinger equation for systems with various quantum numbers, determining the cutoff parameter \(\Lambda\) by fitting to the experimental binding energy of \(T_{cc}\) with \(I(J^P)=0(1^+)\). The light-cloud basis was then applied in the heavy quark limit to classify the spin multiplets of the bound states.

## Key numbers

* The experimental Breit-Wigner binding energy of \(T_{cc}\) relative to the \(D^{*+}D^{0}\) threshold is \(\delta m_{\mathrm{BW}} = -273 \pm 61 \pm 5 ^{+11}_{-14}\ \mathrm{keV}/c^2\), while the pole mass gives \(\delta m_{\mathrm{pole}} = -360 \pm 40^{+4}_{-0}\ \mathrm{keV}/c^{2}\).
* The cutoff parameter \(\Lambda = 1069.8\ \mathrm{MeV}\) reproduces the experimental binding energy of \(0.273\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(T_{cc}\) with \(0(1^+)\).
* For \(T_{cc}\) with \(0(1^+)\) at \(\Lambda = 1069.8\ \mathrm{MeV}\), the mixing ratios are: \({[DD^{*}]}_{-}({}^3S_{1})\) at \(99.2\%\), \({[DD^{*}]}_{-}({}^3D_{1})\) at \(0.467\%\), \(D^{*}D^{*}({}^3S_{1})\) at \(0.229\%\), and \(D^{*}D^{*}({}^3D_{1})\) at \(0.0854\%\), with a root-mean-square radius of \(6.43\ \mathrm{fm}\).
* For \(T_{bb}\) with \(0(1^+)\) using the same \(\Lambda = 1069.8\ \mathrm{MeV}\), the binding energy is \(46.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\), with mixing ratios: \({[BB^{*}]}_{-}({}^3S_{1})\) at \(70.7\%\), \({[BB^{*}]}_{-}({}^3D_{1})\) at \(4.71\%\), \(B^{*}B^{*}({}^3S_{1})\) at \(21.6\%\), and \(B^{*}B^{*}({}^3D_{1})\) at \(3.00\%\), with a root-mean-square radius of \(0.620\ \mathrm{fm}\).
* Varying the sigma coupling \(g_s\) by \(\pm 10\%\) around \(g_s = 3.4\) yields bound states for \(T_{cc}\) with \(g_s \geq 3.22\), and the cutoff \(\Lambda\) ranges from \(1001.3\ \mathrm{MeV}\) to \(1147.1\ \mathrm{MeV}\) to reproduce the experimental binding energy.

## Context

This work extends the earlier hadronic molecule study of doubly heavy tetraquarks by Ohkoda (2012), which used OPEP and OBEP with rho and omega exchanges but without sigma meson exchange. The authors add the sigma meson exchange to construct a more realistic interaction and determine the cutoff from the LHCb observation of \(T_{cc}\) reported in 2022. The analysis enables predictions for the bottom counterpart \(T_{bb}\) and identifies spin multiplets via the light-cloud basis, suggesting partner states of the tetraquark bound states that could be searched for experimentally.
