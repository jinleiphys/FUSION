# The EMC effect for few-nucleon bound systems in Light-Front Hamiltonian Dynamics

> The light-front formalism for a covariant description of the European Muon Collaboration (EMC) effect, already applied to $^3$He, is formally extended to any nucleus, and used for actually calculating the $^3$H and $^4$He cases. The realistic and accurate nuclear description of few-nucleon bound systems, obtained with both phenomenological and chiral potentials, has been properly combined with the Poincare' covariance and macroscopic locality, automatically satisfying both number of particles and momentum sum rule. While retaining the on-mass-shell nucleon structure functions, one is then able to predict a sizable EMC effect for $^4$He, as already observed for $^3$He. Moreover, the impact on the EMC effect of both i) the short-range correlations, such as those generated by modern nuclear interactions, and ii) the ratio between the neutron and proton structure functions has been studied. The short-range correlations generated by retaining only the standard nuclear degrees of freedom act on the depth of the minimum in the EMC ratio, while the uncertainties linked to the ratio of neutron to proton structure functions are found to be very small. These light-front results facilitates ascribing deviations from experimental data due to genuine QCD effects, not included in a standard nuclear description, and initiating unbiased investigations.

## Key claim
The authors extend a Poincaré-covariant light-front formalism, previously applied only to ³He, to calculate the EMC effect for ³H and ⁴He using realistic nuclear wave functions, and find that short-range correlations from standard nuclear degrees of freedom produce a sizable EMC effect in ⁴He, but the differences between modern potentials are smaller than the overall EMC effect itself, leaving room for genuine QCD contributions.

## Method
The authors compute the nuclear structure function F₂^A(x) via a convolution of the nucleon structure functions with a light-cone momentum distribution f₁^N(ξ) obtained from the light-front spectral function. The nuclear wave functions in momentum space are taken from nonrelativistic calculations with the Argonne V18 plus Urbana IX three-nucleon potential and two chiral effective field theory interactions (NVIa+3N and NVIb+3N), then embedded in a Poincaré-covariant framework using the Bakamjian-Thomas construction and light-front Hamiltonian dynamics. The EMC ratio R^A_EMC(x) is formed by dividing the nuclear structure function ratio by the deuterium ratio.

## Key numbers
- ⁴He binding energy: −28.298 MeV (NVIa+3N) and −28.247 MeV (NVIb+3N)
- The EMC ratio for ⁴He shows a minimum near x ≈ 0.7, with the AV18+3N potential producing a deeper minimum than the two χEFT interactions
- The light-cone momentum distributions peak at ξ = 1/A (ξ = 1/4 for ⁴He, ξ = 1/3 for ³He, ξ = 1/2 for deuteron)
- The momentum sum rule ⟨ξ⟩ = 1/A is numerically satisfied for all calculated distributions
- Both NVIa and NVIb interactions fit NN phase shifts up to 125 MeV with χ² ∼ 1

## Context
This work extends the light-front approach of Pace (2022) from ³He to ³H and ⁴He, providing a baseline that includes only standard nuclear degrees of freedom while respecting Poincaré covariance and macroscopic locality. The results enable future studies to isolate genuine QCD effects (such as nucleon swelling or exotic quark configurations) by subtracting this conventional nuclear description from experimental EMC data. The calculations use wave functions from Marcucci (2019) and Piarulli (2016, 2017, 2022) and are compared to JLab data from Seely (2009) and the MARATHON Collaboration (2021).
