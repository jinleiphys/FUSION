# Multi-particle eccentricities in collisions dominated by fluctuations

> We compute analytically the multi-particle eccentricities, ε_m {2n}, for systems dominated by fluctuations, such as proton-nucleus collisions at the Large Hadron Collider. In particular, we derive a general relation for <ε_2^{2n}>. We further discuss the relations between various multi-particle eccentricities and demonstrate that ε_2{2} > ε_2{4} \simeq ε_2{6} \simeq ε_2{8}, in agreement with recent numerical calculations in a Glauber model.

# Multi-particle eccentricities in collisions dominated by fluctuations

## Key claim

The authors analytically derive that for systems dominated by fluctuations (such as proton-nucleus collisions), the multi-particle eccentricities satisfy the hierarchy ε₂{2} > ε₂{4} ≃ ε₂{6} ≃ ε₂{8}, under the assumption that the average of a ratio equals the ratio of averages and that the radial distribution is azimuthally symmetric on average.

## Method

The authors compute the ensemble average of ε₂^{2n} for N independent points sampled from a general distribution P(r,φ), using a generating function technique with limits of derivatives. They derive an exact formula involving modified Bessel functions and then specialize to the case of a Gaussian radial distribution to obtain closed-form expressions for the cumulants ε₂{2}, ε₂{4}, ε₂{6}, and ε₂{8}. They also provide general relations expressing these eccentricities in terms of radial moments ⟨r^m⟩ for arbitrary radial distributions.

## Key numbers

- For a Gaussian distribution with N independent points: ε₂²{2} = 2/(1+N)
- For a Gaussian distribution: ε₂⁴{4} = 16/[(1+N)²(3+N)]
- For a Gaussian distribution: ε₂⁶{6} = 192/[(1+N)³(3+N)(5+N)]
- For a Gaussian distribution: ε₂⁸{8} = 6144(17+5N)/[11(1+N)⁴(3+N)²(5+N)(7+N)]
- The hierarchy ε₂{2} > ε₂{4} ≃ ε₂{6} ≃ ε₂{8} holds for all tested radial distributions including P_r(r) ~ e^{-r^k/σ^k} for various k and P_r(r) ~ Θ(r₀-r)

## Context

This work extends the numerical observations of Bzdak (2013) by providing analytical verification of the eccentricity hierarchy in fluctuation-dominated systems such as proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC. The results agree with the numerical Glauber model calculations of Bzdak (2013) and with the power-law distribution analysis of Yan (2013). The derived analytical expressions enable further study of whether the same hierarchy observed in Fourier coefficients of azimuthal correlation functions would favor hydrodynamic interpretations over current Color Glass Condensate treatments, which are independent of initial geometry.
