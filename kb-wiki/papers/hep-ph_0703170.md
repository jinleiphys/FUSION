# Omega mesons in medium - revisited

> Recent experimental observation by the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration indicates a downward mass shift of the $ω$ meson when put into a nuclear medium. Therefore it is worthwhile to take a look at theoretical approaches that can describe this mass shift. One such hadronic model was introduced by Klingl et al. (Nucl.Phys. A650:299-312). We will demonstrate that the heavy baryon limit approximation used there has no justification and returns wrong results. The results obtained with a correct treatment of the relevant $2π$-channel lead to unreasonable results for the $ω$ selfenergy in medium.

## Key claim

The heavy baryon limit (HBL) approximation used by Klingl et al. (1999) to select relevant diagrams for the ωN → 2πN channel is unjustified and leads to results that are wrong by an order of magnitude; a fully relativistic treatment of the same Lagrangian yields an in-medium ω mass shift that is unreasonably large, indicating a fundamental problem with the low-energy hadronic model itself.

## Method

The authors reexamine the effective Lagrangian approach of Klingl et al. (1999), which combines vector meson dominance, pseudovector pion-nucleon interaction, and SU(3) flavor symmetry, but includes no direct ω-nucleon-resonance interaction. They calculate the ωN forward-scattering amplitude at tree level for the inelastic channels ωN → πN and ωN → 2πN, then connect it to the in-medium ω selfenergy via the low density theorem for isospin-symmetric nuclear matter at T=0 with ω momentum q=0. The key difference from the prior work is that the present calculation treats all diagrams fully relativistically instead of applying the heavy baryon limit approximation.

## Key numbers

* The imaginary part of the ωN forward-scattering amplitude for the 2π-channel calculated with the full relativistic model is one order of magnitude larger than the result obtained with the heavy baryon limit approximation.
* The real part of the ωN → 2πN scattering amplitude in the full model has opposite sign to the HBL result, meaning attraction in the former case and repulsion in the latter.
* The in-medium ω spectral function peak from the full relativistic calculation is at 544 MeV, compared to the in-medium peak at about 620 MeV found by Klingl et al. (1999) using HBL.
* The vacuum ω mass is not explicitly given in the text, but the decrease from vacuum mass to the in-medium peak is around 240 MeV.
* The CBELSA/TAPS experiment suggests an in-medium ω mass of 722 MeV at 0.6 ρ₀ nuclear density.

## Context

This work was triggered by the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration's experimental indication of a downward mass shift of the ω meson in nuclear matter (Tr05). It reexamines the hadronic model of Klingl et al. (1999, 1997), which predicted such a downward shift using the heavy baryon limit approximation, and finds that approximation unjustified. The results contradict other theoretical approaches that predict a rising mass (DM01, PM01, SL06, Zs02) or a structure with several peaks (Lu02, Mu06). The authors conclude that the low-energy hadronic model itself is problematic and suggest that improved calculations incorporating coupled-channels and rescattering (e.g., a Bethe-Salpeter or K-matrix approach) are needed.
