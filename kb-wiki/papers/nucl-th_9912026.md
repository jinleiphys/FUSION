# Chiral unitary meson-baryon dynamics in the presence of resonances: Elastic pion-nucleon scattering

> We develop a novel approach to chiral meson-baryon dynamics incorporating unitarity constraints and explicit resonance fields. It is based on the most general structure of any pion-nucleon partial wave amplitude neglecting the unphysical cuts as derived from the N/D method. This amplitude is then matched to the one-loop heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory result at third order and to tree level exchanges of baryon- and meson states in the s,t and u channels. This generates the left-hand cuts. The unitarization procedure does not involve form factors or regulator functions. The resonance parameters are determined from fits to the S- and P-wave pion-nucleon partial wave amplitudes for energies up to 1.3 GeV. In particular, the $Δ(1232)$ is accurately reproduced whereas scalar and vector meson couplings are less precisely pinned down. We also obtain a satisfactory description of the $π$N threshold parameters. Further extensions of this method to coupled channels and the three-flavor case are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The authors develop a novel approach to chiral meson-baryon dynamics that combines unitarity constraints with explicit resonance fields, matching the unitarized amplitude to one-loop heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory at third order and to tree-level resonance exchanges, but the method is limited to energies up to 1.3 GeV and does not include inelastic channels.

## Method

The approach uses the N/D method to derive the most general structure of any pion-nucleon partial wave amplitude neglecting unphysical cuts, then matches this amplitude to the one-loop heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory result at third order and to tree-level exchanges of baryon and meson states in the s, t, and u channels. The unitarization procedure does not involve form factors or regulator functions. Resonance parameters are determined from fits to the S- and P-wave pion-nucleon partial wave amplitudes for energies up to 1.3 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The Delta(1232) resonance is accurately reproduced, with its mass taken as m_Delta = 1232 MeV in the tree graphs
- The pion decay constant is taken as F_pi = 92.4 MeV
- The axial-vector coupling is taken as g_A = 1.26 and the nucleon mass as m = 938.27 MeV
- The Roper N*(1440) resonance is included with mass m_R = 1440 MeV and coupling parameter sqrt(R) = 0.53 +/- 0.04
- The rho(770) meson coupling G_V is taken as either 69 MeV (from rho to 2 pi decay) or 53 MeV (from pion charge radius)
- The scalar meson couplings are fixed as bar_c_d = bar_c_m = F_pi / sqrt(6)

## Context

This work extends the N/D method previously applied to meson-meson scattering in ref. nd to the pion-nucleon sector, building on the observation from ref. aspects that dimension-two low-energy constants of chiral perturbation theory are saturated by s-, u-, and t-channel resonance contributions, most notably the Delta and the rho(770). The approach enables future extensions to coupled channels and three-flavor systems, particularly for kaon-nucleon scattering, and aims to provide a first building block for a relativistic coupled-channel scheme that matches accurate chiral perturbation theory predictions, unlike previous studies of chiral SU(3) dynamics with coupled channels by Norbert and Valencia.
