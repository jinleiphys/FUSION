# Photon decay in strong magnetic field in heavy-ion collisions

> We calculate the photon pair production rate in strong magnetic field created in off-central heavy-ion collisions. Photon decay leads to depletion of the photon yield by a few percent at RHIC and by as much as 20% at the LHC. It also generates a substantial azimuthal asymmetry ("elliptic flow") of the final photon distribution. We estimate v_2~2% at RHIC and v_2~14% at LHC. Photon decay measurements is an important tool for studying the magnetic fields in early stages of heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Photon decay in the strong magnetic field created in off-central heavy-ion collisions can deplete the photon yield by a few percent at RHIC and by as much as 20% at the LHC, but this depletion depends on the magnetic field being approximately stationary during the quark-gluon plasma lifetime, an assumption supported by the author's earlier estimate of a relaxation time of about 2.2 fm.

## Method

The author calculates the photon pair-production rate (γ B → f f̅ B) in a strong external magnetic field using the quasi-classical method from Baier (1964) and the earlier calculation by Nikishov (1964). The decay rate is expressed as a function of the photon's transverse momentum, rapidity, and azimuthal angle relative to the reaction plane, using the invariant parameter ϰ. The survival probability of photons is then estimated by multiplying the decay rate by the time the photon spends in the plasma (Δt ≈ 10 fm), and the azimuthal asymmetry is quantified by expanding the decay rate in a Fourier series to extract the elliptic flow coefficient v₂.

## Key numbers

- Photon survival probability at RHIC: P_RHIC ≈ 97% for Δt = 10 fm.
- Photon survival probability at LHC: P_LHC ≈ 80% for Δt = 10 fm.
- Elliptic flow coefficient v₂ at RHIC: v₂ ≈ 2% at k_T = 1 GeV and Δt ≈ 10 fm/c.
- Elliptic flow coefficient v₂ at LHC: v₂ ≈ 14% at k_T = 1 GeV and Δt ≈ 10 fm/c.
- For γ → μ⁺μ⁻ at RHIC at φ = η = 0 and k_T = 1 GeV, the invariant parameter ϰ_μ = 19, indicating the strong-field regime (ϰ ≫ 1).
- The magnetic field strength at √s = 200 GeV is estimated as eB ≈ m_π²/ħ, while the critical field is eB_c = m_e²/ħ.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier study (Tuchin 2010) on synchrotron radiation of gluons by fast quarks in a magnetic field, which argued that the magnetic field is approximately stationary during the quark-gluon plasma lifetime due to a relaxation time of about 2.2 fm. The calculation of photon decay in a strong magnetic field provides a new tool for studying the magnetic fields in early stages of heavy-ion collisions, and the predicted azimuthal asymmetry (elliptic flow) of the final photon distribution can be compared with other possible contributions to photon asymmetry discussed by Alam (1993), Turbide (2005), Chatterjee (2005), Layek (2006), and Kopeliovich (2007). The work also notes that photons polarized parallel to the field are 3/2 times more likely to decay than those polarized transversely, suggesting that polarization measurements could serve as a signature of the strong magnetic field.
