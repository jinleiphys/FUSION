# Interactions between Octet Baryons in the SU_6 Quark model

> The baryon-baryon interactions for the complete baryon octet (B_8) are investigated in a unified framework of the resonating-group method, in which the spin-flavor SU_6 quark-model wave functions are employed. Model parameters are determined to reproduce properties of the nucleon-nucleon system and the low-energy cross section data for the hyperon-nucleon interaction. We then proceed to explore B_8 B_8 interactions in the strangeness S=-2, -3 and -4 sectors. The S-wave phase-shift behavior and total cross sections are systematically understood by 1) the spin-flavor SU_6 symmetry, 2) the special role of the pion exchange, and 3) the flavor symmetry breaking.

# Interactions between Octet Baryons in the SU_6 Quark Model

## Key claim

The baryon-baryon interactions for the complete baryon octet can be understood in a unified quark-model framework where the Hamiltonian is approximately SU_3 scalar, meaning that SU_3 states with a common (λμ) label should have the same interaction if symmetry were exact; however, the model predicts no bound state in any B_8 B_8 system except the deuteron, and the ΛΛ interaction is less attractive than previously thought, consistent with the Demachi-Yanagi and Nagara events for double Λ-hypernuclei.

## Method

The authors employed the resonating-group method (RGM) with spin-flavor SU_6 quark-model wave functions, incorporating a Hamiltonian that includes a confinement potential, the full Fermi-Breit interaction with explicit quark-mass dependence, and effective meson-exchange potentials (EMEP) from scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector mesons acting between quarks. Model parameters were fixed by fitting to np scattering phase shifts for partial waves J ≤ 2 at laboratory energies up to 350 MeV, the deuteron binding energy, the ¹S_0 NN scattering length, and low-energy hyperon-nucleon total cross section data. The resulting model (fss2) was then applied without additional parameters to predict all B_8 B_8 interactions in the strangeness S = -2, -3, and -4 sectors.

## Key numbers

- The strange-to-up-down quark mass ratio used in fss2 is λ = m_s/m_ud = 1.551.
- The model fss2 achieves a phase-shift fit quality of √χ² = 0.59° for NN phase shifts with J ≤ 2 in the energy range T_lab = 25–300 MeV, compared to 1.10° for OBEP, 1.40° for the Paris potential, and 1.32° for the Bonn potential.
- In the ΛΛ-ΞN-ΣΣ coupled-channel system with I=0, the ¹S_0 ΛΛ phase shift in fss2 rises only to approximately 20°, whereas in the previous model FSS it rose to 40°.
- The ΞN (I=0) ¹S_0 phase shift in fss2 rises only to 30°–40°.
- A G-matrix estimate of ΔB_ΛΛ for ⁶_ΛΛHe using fss2 is about 1 MeV.
- The ΞN (I=1) ¹S_0 and ³S_1 phase shifts show a prominent cusp structure at the ΣΛ threshold around p_Ξ ~ 600 MeV/c.
- The ΞΞ total cross sections are about 1/4 to 1/5 of the NN cross sections.
- The ³S_1 phase shift for ΞN (I=0) rises only to about 5°.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous quark-model studies (Fujiwara, Nakamoto, and Suzuki, 1996) for NN and hyperon-nucleon interactions by incorporating vector mesons and improved effective meson-exchange potentials, producing the fss2 model. It provides predictions for all octet-baryon interactions in strangeness sectors S = -2, -3, and -4, which are relevant for hypernuclear physics and strangeness nuclear matter. The results contradict the very large cross sections predicted by the Nijmegen soft-core potentials (Stoks and Rijken, 1999) for (22) channels, and the finding that the ΛΛ interaction is less attractive than previously thought is consistent with the recently discovered Demachi-Yanagi event for ¹⁰_ΛΛBe and the Nagara event for ⁶_ΛΛHe. The work enables systematic studies of double-strangeness hypernuclei and provides a framework for future experimental comparisons.
