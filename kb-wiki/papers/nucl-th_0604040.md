# Interference effect in elastic parton energy loss in a finite medium

> Similar to the radiative parton energy loss due to gluon bremsstrahlung, elastic energy loss of a parton undergoing multiple scattering in a finite medium is demonstrated to be sensitive to interference effect. The interference between amplitudes of elastic scattering via a gluon exchange and that of gluon radiation reduces the effective elastic energy loss in a finite medium and gives rise to a non-trivial length dependence. The reduction is most significant for a propagation length $L< 4/πT$ in a medium with a temperature $T$. Though the finite size effect is not significant for the average parton propagation in the most central heavy-ion collisions, it will affect the centrality dependence of its effect on jet quenching.

## Key claim

Elastic energy loss of a parton undergoing multiple scattering in a finite medium is sensitive to interference effects, similar to radiative energy loss from gluon bremsstrahlung. The reduction is most significant for propagation length \(L < 4/\pi T\) in a medium with temperature \(T\), though the finite size effect is not significant for average parton propagation in the most central heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The author derives the elastic energy loss for a quark propagating through a finite thermal medium by considering double quark scattering processes in deeply inelastic scattering (DIS) off a nucleus, using the same framework of multiple parton scattering previously employed for radiative energy loss. The calculation includes the interference between amplitudes of elastic scattering via one-gluon exchange and those of gluon radiation, with the four-quark correlation functions factorized into a product of the leading quark distribution and the secondary quark distribution in the medium. The analysis is extended to a hot medium in heavy-ion collisions by using thermal quark and gluon distributions at temperature \(T\) and integrating over the spatial profile of the medium.

## Key numbers

- The reduction is most significant for propagation length \(L < 4/\pi T\) in a medium with temperature \(T\).
- For a finite medium with constant parton density and length \(L\), the quark-quark elastic energy loss per unit length is given by \(\frac{dE_{qq}}{dL} = \frac{C_F}{2N_c}\frac{\pi\alpha_s^2}{24}d_q T^2 \left\{1-\frac{6}{\pi TL}\left[\frac{1}{\pi TL}-{\rm cosec}(\pi TL)\right]\right\} \left[2\ln\frac{1+\chi_T}{1-\chi_T}-\frac{9}{4}\chi_T+\frac{3}{8}\chi_T^2\right]\).
- For a finite medium, the quark-gluon elastic energy loss per unit length is \(\frac{dE_{qg}}{dL} = \frac{C_A}{2N_c}\frac{\pi\alpha_s^2}{12}d_g T^2 \left\{1-\frac{3}{\pi TL}\left[{\rm cth}(\pi TL)-\frac{1}{\pi TL}\right]\right\} \left[3\ln\frac{1+\chi_T}{1-\chi_T}-\chi_T\right]\).
- In the limit \(L \rightarrow \infty\), the asymptotic quark-quark energy loss per unit length is \(\left(\frac{dE_{qq}}{dL}\right)_\infty \approx \frac{N_f}{6} C_F\pi\alpha_s^2 T^2 \ln\frac{3ET}{\mu^2}\).
- In the limit \(L \rightarrow \infty\), the asymptotic quark-gluon energy loss per unit length is \(\left(\frac{dE_{qg}}{dL}\right)_\infty \approx C_F\pi\alpha_s^2 T^2 \frac{3}{2} \ln\frac{3ET}{\mu^2}\).
- When \(\pi TL \ll 1\), \(\frac{dE_{qq}}{dL} \approx \frac{7}{60}(\pi T L)^2 \left(\frac{dE_{qq}}{dL}\right)_\infty\) and \(\frac{dE_{qg}}{dL} \approx \frac{1}{15}(\pi T L)^2 \left(\frac{dE_{qg}}{dL}\right)_\infty\).
- The typical average propagation length in most central \(Au+Au\) collisions is about \(L \sim 6\) fm; for average temperature \(T \sim 200-300\) MeV, \(\pi TL \sim 19-28\).

## Context

This work extends the study of parton energy loss in a finite medium by applying the Landau-Pomeranchuck-Migdal (LPM) interference framework, previously used for radiative energy loss by Wang and collaborators (2002), to elastic (collisional) energy loss. The results differ from those of Peigne et al. (2005), who found significant suppression of collisional energy loss for considerably large medium sizes, and from Djordjevic (2006), whose finite size effect arises from imposed finite interaction time rather than the interference mechanism presented here. The findings enable a consistent treatment of both radiative and elastic energy loss in the same framework, which is necessary for accurate modeling of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for peripheral collisions where the propagation length is on the order of 1-2 fm.
