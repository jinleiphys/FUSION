# Parity doublet model for baryon octets: ground states saturated by good diquarks and the role of bad diquarks for excited states

> Parity doublet model is an effective chiral model that includes the chiral variant and invariant masses of baryons. The chiral invariant mass has large impacts on the density dependence of models which can be constrained by neutron star observations. In the previous work, models of two-flavors have been considered up to a few times nuclear saturation density, but in such dense region it is also necessary to consider hyperons. With the chiral invariant masses baryons can stay massive in extreme environments (e.g., neutron stars) where the chiral symmetry restoration takes place. In this work, we generalize the previous $\mbox{SU(2)}_L \times \mbox{SU(2)}_R$ parity models of nucleons to $\mbox{SU(3)}_L \times \mbox{SU(3)}_R$ models of the baryon octet, within the linear realization of the chiral symmetry. The major problem in constructing such models has been too many candidates for the chiral representations of baryons. Motivated by the concepts of diquarks and the mended symmetry, we choose the $(3_L, \bar{3}_R) + (\bar{3}_L, 3_R)$, $(3_L, 6_R) + (6_L, 3_R)$ and $(1_L, 8_R) + (8_L, 1_R)$ representations and use quark diagrams to constrain the possible types of Yukawa interactions. The masses of the baryon octets for positive and negative baryons up to the first excitations are successfully reproduced. As expected from the diquark considerations, the ground state baryons are well dominated by $(3_L, \bar{3}_R) + (\bar{3}_L, 3_R)$ and $(1_L, 8_R) + (8_L, 1_R)$ representations, while the excited states require $(3_L, 6_R) + (6_L, 3_R)$ representations. Important applications of our model are the chiral restoration for strange quarks at large density and the continuity of diquarks from hadronic to quark matter. We also address the problem of large Yukawa couplings which are enhanced in three-flavor construction.

## Key claim

The authors construct a three-flavor SU(3)_L × SU(3)_R parity doublet model for the full baryon octet, showing that ground-state baryons are dominated by chiral representations built from "good" diquarks (flavor-antisymmetric), while the first excited states require inclusion of "bad" diquark (flavor-symmetric) representations to reproduce the correct mass ordering, a problem that could not be solved in their previous work (Minamikawa 2023) using only good-diquark representations.

## Method

The authors introduce three types of baryon fields corresponding to the chiral representations (3_L, \bar{3}_R) + (\bar{3}_L, 3_R), (8_L, 1_R) + (1_L, 8_R), and (3_L, 6_R) + (6_L, 3_R), with mirror assignments for parity doubling. They construct first-order Yukawa interactions to the chiral field M using quark-diagram rules, then perform a mean-field fit of the resulting 6×6 mass matrices to the known masses of N, Λ, Σ, and Ξ baryons for both positive- and negative-parity states up to the first excitations, using the pion and kaon decay constants to fix the meson vacuum expectation values.

## Key numbers

* Pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV and kaon decay constant f_K = 110 MeV are used to set the meson VEVs: 2α = f_π = 93 MeV, 2γ = 2f_K − f_π = 127 MeV.
* Ground-state masses used as inputs: N(939) = 939 MeV, Λ(1116) = 1116 MeV, Σ(1193) = 1193 MeV, Ξ(1318) = 1318 MeV.
* First positive-parity excited states: N(1440) = 1440 MeV, Λ(1600) = 1600 MeV, Σ(1660) = 1660 MeV.
* First negative-parity states: N(1535) = 1530 MeV, Λ(1670) = 1674 MeV, Σ(1750) = 1750 MeV, N(1650) = 1650 MeV, Λ(1800) = 1800 MeV.
* Chiral invariant masses are set to m_0^ψ = m_0^χ = 700 MeV and m_0^η = 1000 MeV for the representative fit shown, with best-fit quality measure f_min^best = 0.43 for this parameter set.

## Context

This work extends previous two-flavor parity doublet models (Yamazaki 2019, Minamikawa 2020, Gao 2022) to three flavors including hyperons, which is necessary for modeling neutron star matter above 2n_0 where strange quarks appear. It directly addresses the failure of the earlier three-flavor construction by Minamikawa (2023), which used only good-diquark representations and could not reproduce the correct mass ordering for excited octet baryons even with second-order Yukawa interactions. The model enables future studies of chiral symmetry restoration for strange quarks at high density and the continuity of diquark correlations from hadronic to quark matter.
