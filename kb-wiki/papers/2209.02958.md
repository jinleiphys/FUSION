# Tetraquark mixing supported by the partial decay widths of two light-meson nonets

> Recently, the tetraquark mixing framework has been proposed as a possible structure for the two light-meson nonets in the $J^P=0^+$ channel, the light nonet composed of $a_0(980)$, $K_0^*(700)$, $f_0(500)$, $f_0(980)$, and the heavy nonet of $a_0(1450)$, $K_0^*(1430)$, $f_0(1370)$, $f_0(1500)$. Among various signatures, we report in this work that their partial decay widths collected from various experimental data in Particle Data Group (PDG) can support this mixing scheme also. In particular, we demonstrate that the couplings of the light nonet to two pseudoscalar mesons estimated from the partial widths are consistently stronger than those of the heavy nonet. This consistent feature agrees qualitatively well with the predictions from the tetraquark mixing framework and, therefore, provides supporting evidence for the tetraquark mixing.

## Key claim

The partial decay widths of the two light-meson nonets in the \(J^P=0^+\) channel, extracted from Particle Data Group (PDG) data, show that the couplings of the light nonet to two pseudoscalar mesons are consistently stronger than those of the heavy nonet, which qualitatively supports the tetraquark mixing framework but does not constitute a quantitative proof.

## Method

The authors extracted experimental partial decay widths for the light nonet (\(a_0(980)\), \(K_0^*(700)\), \(f_0(500)\), \(f_0(980)\)) and the heavy nonet (\(a_0(1450)\), \(K_0^*(1430)\), \(f_0(1370)\), \(f_0(1500)\)) from PDG data, using reported branching ratios and total widths where available, and estimating uncertainties from poorly known ratios. They then compared these experimental widths to theoretical partial widths calculated from the tetraquark mixing model, which expresses physical states as mixtures of two tetraquark configurations (\(|000\rangle\) and \(|011\rangle\)) with mixing parameters \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) fixed by diagonalizing the hyperfine mass matrix. The theoretical widths were computed using an effective Lagrangian with derivative couplings and averaged over a mass distribution to account for the finite total widths of the resonances.

## Key numbers

- \(\Gamma_{exp}[a_0(980)\rightarrow \pi \eta] \approx 60\) MeV, while \(\Gamma_{exp}[a_0(1450)\rightarrow \pi \eta] \approx 15.4\text{--}20.5\) MeV, giving an experimental ratio of \(0.26\text{--}0.34\) compared to a kinematical ratio of \(1.68\).
- \(\Gamma_{exp}[K_0^*(700)\rightarrow \pi K] = 468\) MeV, while \(\Gamma_{exp}[K_0^*(1430)\rightarrow \pi K] = 251.1\) MeV.
- \(\Gamma_{exp}[f_0(980)\rightarrow \pi \pi] \approx 50\) MeV, while \(\Gamma_{exp}[f_0(1500)\rightarrow \pi \pi] \approx 38.1\) MeV.
- \(\Gamma_{exp}[f_0(980)\rightarrow K \bar{K}] \approx 9.5\text{--}46.2\) MeV, while \(\Gamma_{exp}[f_0(1500)\rightarrow K \bar{K}] \approx 9.5\) MeV.
- The tetraquark mixing model predicts coupling strength ratios \(G/G^\prime \approx 4.2\text{--}4.3\) between light and heavy nonet members for the channels considered.

## Context

This work extends the tetraquark mixing model previously proposed by Kim et al. (2016, 2017, 2018, 2019) for the isovector members \(a_0(980)\) and \(a_0(1450)\) to the full set of resonances in both nonets, including the isoscalar states \(f_0(500)\), \(f_0(980)\), \(f_0(1370)\), and \(f_0(1500)\). The model contrasts with earlier approaches such as the diquark-antidiquark model of Jaffe (1977) and the mixing model of Black et al. (1998, 1999). The results enable future quantitative tests of the tetraquark mixing framework as PDG data improve, particularly for the currently unavailable partial widths of \(f_0(500)\rightarrow \pi\pi\) and \(f_0(1370)\rightarrow \pi\pi\).
