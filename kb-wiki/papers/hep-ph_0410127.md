# Equation of state of deconfined matter within dynamical quasiparticle description

> A simple quasiparticle model, motivated by lowest-order perturbative QCD, is proposed. It is applied to interpret the lattice QCD equation of state. A reasonable reproduction of the lattice data is obtained. In contrast to existing quasiparticle models, the present model is formulated in dynamical rather than thermodynamical terms, and is easily applicable to a system with finite baryon density. In particular, the model simulates the confinement property.

## Key claim

The authors propose a quasiparticle model for deconfined QCD matter that is formulated in dynamical (density-dependent) rather than thermodynamic (temperature/chemical-potential-dependent) terms, which allows it to be extended to nonequilibrium configurations. The model reproduces lattice QCD data only above the critical temperature \(T_C\) and only for the special case \(N_f \leq N_c\) (number of flavors less than or equal to number of colors), with the best fits requiring an additional overall normalization factor of 0.9 or a nontrivial auxiliary function to match the lattice pressure normalization.

## Method

The model starts from an effective Lagrangian for massive transverse gluons and quarks with effective masses \(m_g\) and \(m_q\) that depend on scalar densities \(\eta^2\) (gluon scalar density divided by mass) and \(\xi_q^2\) (quark scalar density divided by mass), plus a mean-field self-interaction potential \(B(\eta,\vec\xi)\). Gap equations derived from the Lagrangian are solved for the special case \(N_f = N_c\) by introducing combined variables \(\zeta_q^2 = \eta^2 + (N_g/4N_c)\xi_q^2\) and \(\chi^2 = (\sum_q \zeta_q^4)^{1/2}\), reducing the problem to a single differential equation for \(B(\chi)\). The coupling constant \(g^2(\chi)\) is parametrized with a logarithmic form containing a QCD scale \(\chi_C\) and a regulator \(\chi_0^2\), with two variants: a "1-loop" version (\(f(\chi)\equiv 1\)) and a "2-loop" version with an auxiliary function \(f_{\text{2-loop}}(\chi)\) that yields an effective enhancement factor of about 2.6 in the temperature range \(T_C\) to \(3T_C\).

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature fitted for (2+1)-flavor case: \(T_C = 195\) MeV (both "1-loop" and "2-loop" versions), slightly above the lattice value of 175 MeV.
- Critical temperature fitted for 2-flavor case: \(T_C = 175\) MeV (both versions), matching the lattice value.
- "1-loop" fit parameters: \(\chi_C = 141.3\) MeV, \(B_C/\chi_C^4 = -97.5\), overall normalization factor 0.9.
- "2-loop" fit parameters: \(\chi_C = 119.6\) MeV, \(B_C/\chi_C^4 = -267.5\) (2+1 flavor) or \(-262.0\) (2 flavor), overall normalization factor 1.0.
- Regulator parameter fixed as \(\chi_0^2 = 0.01\,\chi_C^2\).
- Current quark masses used in lattice comparison: \(m_{u0}=m_{d0}=65\) MeV, \(m_{s0}=135\) MeV.
- The model reproduces lattice data only above \(T_C\); below \(T_C\) hadronic degrees of freedom are disregarded.

## Context

This work extends the line of phenomenological quasiparticle models for the QCD equation of state, such as those by Gorenstein (1995), Peshier (1996), Szabo (2003), Rebhan (2003), and Weise (2001), which were formulated in thermodynamic terms (temperature and chemical potential). The key innovation is a dynamical formulation using scalar densities as variables, enabling extension to nonequilibrium systems relevant for heavy-ion collisions. The model is compared to lattice QCD data from the (2+1)-flavor calculations of the Bielefeld group (latEoS, 2004) and earlier 2-flavor lattice data (latEoS0). The approach enables future applications to nonequilibrium configurations of quark-gluon plasma, though the authors note that a general solution for arbitrary \(N_f\) and \(N_c\) remains an open problem.
