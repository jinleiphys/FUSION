# The $K^\ast Kπ$ and $ρππ$ couplings in QCD

> The light cone QCD sum rules are derived for the $K^\ast Kπ$ coupling $g_{K^\ast Kπ} $ and the $ρππ$ coupling $g_{ρππ}$. The contribution from the excited states and the continuum is subtracted cleanly through the double Borel transform with respect to the two external momenta, $p_1^2$, $p_2^2=(p-q)^2$. Our result $g_{K^\ast Kπ}=(8.7\pm 0.5)$ and $g_{ρππ}=(11.5\pm 0.8)$ is in good agreement with the experimental value.

## Key claim

The light cone QCD sum rules yield \(g_{K^\ast K\pi} = (8.7 \pm 0.5)\) and \(g_{\rho\pi\pi} = (11.5 \pm 0.8)\), in good agreement with the experimental values extracted from decay widths (9.08 and 12.16, respectively), but the result depends on the choice of the pion wave function at the symmetric point \(u_0 = 1/2\) and on the continuum thresholds chosen to suppress contamination from higher resonances such as \(a_1(1260)\) and \(K_1(1270)\).

## Method

The author derives two-point correlation functions for the \(K^\ast K\pi\) and \(\rho\pi\pi\) vertices using light cone QCD sum rules (LCQSR). The correlation functions are expanded in terms of pion wave functions (twist-2, twist-3, twist-4) and the strange quark propagator (including perturbative and condensate contributions). A double Borel transform with respect to the two external momenta \(p_1^2\) and \(p_2^2\) is applied to eliminate single-pole terms and subtract excited-state and continuum contributions, with the continuum modeled by a dispersion integral above thresholds \(s_1\) and \(s_2\). The final sum rules depend only on the pion wave functions at the point \(u_0 = 1/2\), which is taken from prior analyses.

## Key numbers

- \(g_{K^\ast K\pi} = 8.7 \pm 0.5\) (from LCQSR, compared to experimental extraction 9.08 from \(\Gamma(K^\ast \to K\pi) = 50.8\) MeV)
- \(g_{\rho\pi\pi} = 11.5 \pm 0.8\) (from LCQSR, compared to experimental extraction 12.16 from \(\Gamma(\rho \to \pi\pi) = 154\) MeV)
- Pion wave function at \(u_0 = 1/2\): \(\varphi_\pi(1/2) = 1.5 \pm 0.2\) (adopted from Zhu (1998) reanalysis)
- Other pion wave function inputs at \(u_0 = 1/2\) and scale \(\mu = 1\) GeV: \(\varphi_\sigma(1/2) = 1.47\), \(g_1(1/2) = 0.022\) GeV\(^2\), \(g_2(1/2) = 0\), \(G_2(1/2) = 0.02\) GeV\(^2\)
- Decay constants: \(f_\rho = (0.18 \pm 0.02)\) GeV, \(f_{K^\ast} = (0.21 \pm 0.02)\) GeV, \(f_K = (0.15 \pm 0.02)\) GeV
- Continuum thresholds: \(s_1 \le (1.5 \pm 0.1)\) GeV\(^2\) for \(K^\ast K\pi\); \(s_2 \le (1.2 \pm 0.1)\) GeV\(^2\) for \(\rho\pi\pi\)
- Strange quark mass: \(m_s = 150\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the light cone QCD sum rule method, previously applied to pion-nucleon couplings by Belyaev and collaborators (1995) and to heavy meson couplings by Zhu and others (1997-1998), to the light vector-meson couplings \(g_{K^\ast K\pi}\) and \(g_{\rho\pi\pi}\). The approach relies on the universal pion wave function at the symmetric point \(\varphi_\pi(1/2)\), which Zhu (1998) had recently re-determined from the \(\pi NN\) coupling. The results enable more reliable input for phenomenological models of nuclear forces and meson decays, and the method can be extended to other three-meson vertices involving pions.
