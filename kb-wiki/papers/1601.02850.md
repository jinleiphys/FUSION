# Influence of the weakly interacting light U boson on the properties of massive PNS

> Considering the octet baryons in relativistic mean field theory and selecting entropy per baryon S=1, we calculate and discuss the influence of U boson on the equation of state, mass-radius, moment of inertia and gravitational redshift of massive protoneutron star (PNS). The effective coupling constant $g_{U}$ of U bosons and nucleons is selected from 0GeV$^{-2}$ to 70GeV$^{-2}$. The results point that U bosons will stiffen the equation of state (EOS). The influence of U bosons on the pressure is more obvious at low density than high density, while, the influence of U bosons on the energy density is more obvious at high density than low density. The U bosons play a significant role in increasing the maximum mass and radius of PNS. When the value of $g_{U}$ changes from 0GeV$^{-2}$ to 70GeV$^{-2}$, the maximum mass of massive PNS increases from 2.11 $M_{\odot}$ to 2.58 $M_{\odot}$, and the radius of PNS corresponding PSR J0348+0432 increases from 13.71 km to 24.35 km. The U bosons will increase the moment of inertia and decrease the gravitational redshift of PNS. For PNS of the massive PSR J0348+0432, the radius and moment of inertia vary directly with $g_{U}$, the gravitational redshift vary inversely with $g_{U}$ approximately.

## Key claim

The weakly interacting light U boson stiffens the equation of state of protoneutron star matter at entropy per baryon S=1, with the effect on pressure being more pronounced at low density and the effect on energy density being more pronounced at high density, conditional on the effective coupling constant \(g_U\) ranging from 0 to 70 GeV\(^{-2}\).

## Method

The authors employed relativistic mean field theory including the full octet of baryons (n, p, \(\Lambda\), \(\Sigma^-\), \(\Sigma^0\), \(\Sigma^+\), \(\Xi^-\), \(\Xi^0\)) interacting via \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), and \(\rho\) mesons, with an additional vector U boson field added in Yukawa-type coupling to baryons. The equation of state was computed at fixed entropy per baryon S=1, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to obtain mass, radius, moment of inertia, and gravitational redshift for protoneutron stars. The effective coupling constant \(g_U = g_u^2 / m_u^2\) was varied from 0 to 70 GeV\(^{-2}\) to study its influence.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of protoneutron star increases from 2.11 \(M_\odot\) (at \(g_U = 0\) GeV\(^{-2}\)) to 2.58 \(M_\odot\) (at \(g_U = 70\) GeV\(^{-2}\)).
- Radius of protoneutron star corresponding to PSR J0348+0432 (mass 2.01 \(M_\odot\)) increases from 13.71 km (at \(g_U = 0\) GeV\(^{-2}\)) to 24.35 km (at \(g_U = 70\) GeV\(^{-2}\)).
- At saturation density \(\rho = 0.145\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(\log P\) increases from 33.84 dyne/cm\(^2\) (at \(g_U = 0\) GeV\(^{-2}\)) to 34.20 dyne/cm\(^2\) (at \(g_U = 70\) GeV\(^{-2}\)), an increment of 1.06%.
- At \(\rho = 0.5\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(\log P\) increases from 35.29 dyne/cm\(^2\) (at \(g_U = 0\) GeV\(^{-2}\)) to 35.43 dyne/cm\(^2\) (at \(g_U = 70\) GeV\(^{-2}\)), an increment of 0.39%.
- At saturation density, \(\log \epsilon\) increases from 14.40 g/cm\(^3\) (at \(g_U = 0\) GeV\(^{-2}\)) to 14.41 g/cm\(^3\) (at \(g_U = 70\) GeV\(^{-2}\)), an increment of 0.06%.
- At \(\rho = 0.5\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(\log \epsilon\) increases from 14.98 g/cm\(^3\) (at \(g_U = 0\) GeV\(^{-2}\)) to 15.03 g/cm\(^3\) (at \(g_U = 70\) GeV\(^{-2}\)), an increment of 0.33%.
- Central density of PSR J0348+0432 decreases from 0.62 fm\(^{-3}\) (at \(g_U = 0\) GeV\(^{-2}\)) to 0.21 fm\(^{-3}\) (at \(g_U = 70\) GeV\(^{-2}\)).

## Context

This work extends the study of U boson effects on cold neutron stars by Zhang et al. (2015) to the hot protoneutron star regime at finite entropy, and builds on relativistic mean field calculations of massive neutron stars by Miyatsu et al. (2013) and Zhao and Jia (2014). The results enable future modeling of protoneutron star evolution and cooling, particularly for explaining the existence of massive neutron stars like PSR J0348+0432 and PSR J1614-2230, and motivate experimental searches for light U bosons as a beyond-standard-model candidate.
