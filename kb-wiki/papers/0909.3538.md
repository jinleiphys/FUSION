# On the cross section ratio sigma_n/sigma_p in eta photoproduction

> The recently discovered enhancement of eta photoproduction on the quasi-free neutron at energies around sqrt(s)~1.67 GeV is addressed within a SU(3) coupled channel model. The quasi-free cross sections on proton and neutron, sigma_n and sigma_p, can be quantitatively explained. In this study, the main source for the peak in sigma_n/sigma_p is a coupled channel effect in S-wave that explains the dip-bump structure in gamma n --> eta n. In particular, the photon coupling to the intermediate meson-baryon states is important. The stability of the result is extensively tested and consistency with several pion- and photon-induced reactions is ensured.

## Key claim

The peak in the cross section ratio σ_n/σ_p for η photoproduction around √s ≈ 1.67 GeV is explained as a coupled-channel effect in S-wave, specifically arising from the photon coupling to intermediate meson-baryon states (KΛ and KΣ loops), without requiring a narrow nuclear resonance; the result is stable under removal of genuine resonance contributions and is already present at next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion.

## Method

The authors employ a SU(3) coupled-channel model based on the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction, unitarized via a Bethe-Salpeter equation, which dynamically generates the N*(1535). The model includes explicit N*(1650) resonance states and a phenomenological background, and the photon coupling to meson and baryon components is implemented respecting gauge invariance. The free parameters are refitted to data from γN → πN, πN → πN, γp → ηp, γn → ηn, πN → ηN, γN → KY, and πN → KY, and the quasi-free cross sections on deuteron are obtained via impulse approximation with Fermi motion folding using the Bonn potential.

## Key numbers

- The peak in σ_n/σ_p occurs at photon energies around E_γ ≈ 1.02 GeV, corresponding to √s ≈ 1.67 GeV.
- The free-neutron cross section γn → ηn exhibits a minimum around E_γ = 930 MeV (near the KΛ threshold) and a maximum at the KΣ threshold.
- The N*(1535) pole position in the present solution is 1537 − 90i MeV (changed from 1608 − 175i MeV in the original model).
- About 80% of the γn → ηn cross section in the √s ≈ 1.6 to 1.7 GeV region arises from S-wave, according to partial-wave analyses from Anisovich (2008).
- The ratio σ_n/σ_p equals 1 over the entire energy range when only the πN intermediate state photon coupling is retained, up to tiny isospin-breaking effects from physical masses.

## Context

This work extends the S-wave coupled-channel model of Döring (2009) to address the enhancement in η photoproduction on the neutron first reported by Kuznetsov (2007) and confirmed by Miyahara (2007) and Jaegle (2008). It contradicts interpretations involving a narrow pentaquark state (Diakonov 1997, Polyakov 2003) or the D15(1675) resonance as in η-MAID (Chiang 2001), and instead supports the S11 interference explanation of Anisovich (2008). The result enables a quantitative, parameter-free understanding of the spike structure without invoking exotic resonances, and can be tested by extending the model to higher partial waves and including the ππN channel consistently.
