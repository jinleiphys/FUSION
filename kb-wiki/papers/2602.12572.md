# NNLL$^\prime$ resummation of azimuthal decorrelation for boosted top quark pair production at the LHC

> The precision program of the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) increasingly relies on the boosted regime, where top quark properties are probed at the TeV scale. However, the simultaneous presence of heavy quark mass effects and large logarithmic corrections from soft radiation poses a significant challenge for theoretical predictions. In this work, we develop a transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization and resummation framework for boosted top quark pair production in the back-to-back limit at the LHC. By employing a two step matching procedure, matching QCD through $\mathrm{SCET}\,+\,\mathrm{HQET}$ onto $\mathrm{SCET}\,+\,\mathrm{bHQET}$, we systematically resum large logarithms associated with both the top quark mass and the azimuthal decorrelation. A key component of our formalism is the first extraction of the two-loop ultra-collinear function, obtained via the refactorization of the fully differential massive soft function. This result completes the set of perturbative ingredients required to achieve $\mathrm{NNLL}^\prime$ accuracy for the azimuthal decorrelation distribution. Our framework establishes a new benchmark for heavy-quark TMD resummation in the boosted limit at hadron colliders.

## Key claim
The paper develops a transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization and resummation framework for boosted top quark pair production at the LHC, achieving NNLL$^\prime$ accuracy for the azimuthal decorrelation distribution. The key condition is that this framework applies specifically in the kinematic hierarchy $p_T \gg m_t \gg p_T \delta\phi$, where $p_T$ is the top quark transverse momentum and $\delta\phi$ is the azimuthal decorrelation angle.

## Method
The authors employ a two-step effective field theory matching procedure: first matching QCD onto a hybrid of soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) and heavy-quark effective theory (HQET), then matching the HQET sector onto boosted heavy-quark effective theory (bHQET) in the boosted limit $p_T \gg m_t$. The two-loop ultra-collinear function, which describes soft radiation collinear to the boosted heavy quarks, is extracted via refactorization of the fully differential massive soft function, completing the perturbative ingredients needed for NNLL$^\prime$ resummation. The cross section is factorized in impact-parameter space, with rapidity logarithms resummed using the Collins-Soper-Sterman formalism and the rapidity renormalization group framework.

## Key numbers
- The kinematic hierarchy is defined by $p_T \gg m_t \gg p_T \delta\phi$, where $m_t$ is the top quark mass and $\delta\phi \equiv \pi - \Delta\phi$ is the azimuthal decorrelation angle
- In the back-to-back limit $\delta\phi \to 0$, the out-of-plane recoil momentum satisfies $|q_x| = p_T \,\delta\phi$
- The power-counting parameter for the boosted limit is $\lambda \equiv m_t/p_T \ll 1$
- The ultra-collinear modes have virtuality $p^2 \sim p_T^2\delta\phi^2 \sim q_x^2$, while the collinear and massive soft modes have virtuality $p^2 \sim p_T^2\lambda^2 \sim m_t^2$

## Context
This work extends the bHQET framework previously developed for $e^+e^-$ event shapes with massive quarks (Fleming 2007, 2008) and threshold resummation for top pair production at hadron colliders (Ferroglia 2012, Pecjak 2016, Czakon 2018) to the TMD regime for boosted top quark pairs. It enables precision studies of top quark properties at TeV scales, including top mass extraction using theoretically clean observables, spin correlation measurements for quantum entanglement studies, and new physics searches at the LHC. The framework establishes a new benchmark for heavy-quark TMD resummation in the boosted limit at hadron colliders.
