# Isospin-symmetry breaking in kaon production: the role of charge-symmetry

> An excess of charged versus neutral kaons has been recently reported by the NA61/SHINE collaboration. Similar excesses were also present in previous experiments, even if with larger errors. Models for hadron productions in heavy ion collisions systematically underestimate the measured charge-to-neutral kaon ratio. In this report, we comment on the role of charge-symmetry as a specific isospin transformation. In particular, we show that the ensemble of the initial colliding nuclei being charge-symmetric is sufficient for producing an equal number of charged and neutral kaons in the isospin-symmetry exact limit.

# Isospin-symmetry breaking in kaon production: the role of charge-symmetry

## Key claim
If the ensemble of initial colliding nuclei is charge-symmetric (i.e., contains both a given nuclear state and its charge-symmetry rotated counterpart with equal probability), then in the isospin-symmetry exact limit the total charged and neutral kaon multiplicities must be equal, predicting a ratio R_K = 1. The experimentally observed excess of charged over neutral kaons therefore represents a direct breaking of charge symmetry that cannot be explained by standard models, which only include small known isospin-breaking effects.

## Method
The author uses charge symmetry (a specific isospin transformation that interchanges members of an isospin multiplet with opposite I_z, e.g., p ↔ n, K⁺ ↔ K⁰) to analyze kaon production amplitudes in heavy-ion collisions. By considering initial nuclear states with definite total isospin I=0 and I=1, and then generalizing to superposed initial states, the author shows that individual collision events can produce unequal numbers of charged and neutral kaons, but averaging over a charge-symmetric ensemble restores equality of charged and neutral kaon multiplicities in the isospin-symmetry limit.

## Key numbers
- The mass splitting between charged and neutral kaons is (m_K⁺ - m_K⁰)/(m_K⁺ + m_K⁰) ≈ -0.004
- The mass splitting between charged and neutral pions is (m_π⁺ - m_π⁰)/(m_π⁺ + m_π⁰) ≈ 0.017
- The decay φ(1024) → K⁺K⁻ is 1.4 times larger than φ(1024) → K⁰K̄⁰
- At high scattering energies (√s_NN ≳ 15 GeV), theoretical models predict R_K ≈ 1.05
- The central experimental value from NA61/SHINE is R_K ≈ 1.18
- The theory-to-experiment mismatch is quantified as approximately 5σ
- The actual experimental ratio Q/B ≈ 0.4, which is less than 1/2

## Context
This work addresses the "kaon puzzle" reported by the NA61/SHINE collaboration (2024) and discussed in the companion theoretical paper by Brylinski et al., where both the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model and the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) approach of Bleicher and Vovchenko systematically underestimate the measured charged-to-neutral kaon ratio. The author shows that the expected ratio R_K = 1 follows from charge symmetry of the initial ensemble, and that known isospin-breaking effects (such as the φ meson decay branching ratio difference) can only produce small deviations (R_K ≈ 1.05), far below the observed value. The work calls for future theoretical efforts exploring models with strong isospin breaking, possibly involving electromagnetic interactions, and experimental studies with nuclei having Q/B = 1/2.
