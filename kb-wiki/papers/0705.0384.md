# Effects of mesonic correlations in the QCD phase transition

> The finite temperature phase transition of strongly interacting matter is studied within a nonlocal chiral quark model of the NJL type coupled to a Polyakov loop. In contrast to previous investigations which were restricted to the mean-field approximation, mesonic correlations are included by evaluating the quark-antiquark ring sum. For physical pion masses, we find that the pions dominate the pressure below the phase transition, whereas above T_c the pressure is well described by the mean-field approximation result. For large pion masses, as realized in lattice simulations, the meson effects are suppressed.

## Key claim

For physical pion masses, mesonic correlations (especially pions) dominate the pressure below the QCD phase transition temperature, while above T_c the mean-field approximation alone describes the pressure well; for large pion masses as used in lattice simulations, meson effects are suppressed, suggesting that previous good agreement between PNJL mean-field results and lattice data may have been partially accidental.

## Method

The authors extended the Polyakov-loop Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model, previously treated only in mean-field approximation, by including mesonic correlations via a quark-antiquark ring sum (1/N_c expansion). They employed a nonlocal four-fermion interaction with a Gaussian form factor motivated by the instanton liquid model, and evaluated the thermodynamic potential as a sum of mean-field and correlation parts for zero chemical potential. The pion and sigma contributions to the pressure were computed numerically from the ring sum of polarization loops.

## Key numbers

* Critical temperature for chiral restoration in the pure quark model: T_c = 116 MeV.
* Critical temperature for deconfinement in the pure gauge sector: T_d = 270 MeV.
* When quark and gluon sectors are coupled in the PNJL model, T_c ≈ T_d ≈ 200 MeV.
* For physical pion mass (m_c = 5.8 MeV, vacuum pion mass 140 MeV), T_c = 202 MeV.
* For large quark mass scaling as m_c = 0.4 T (mimicking lattice conditions), T_c = 231 MeV.
* Model parameters: m_c = 5.8 MeV, Λ = 902.4 MeV, GΛ² = 15.82, with chiral condensate −⟨q̄q⟩^{1/3} = 240 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous PNJL model studies (Ratti et al., 2005; Roessner et al., 2006) that were restricted to mean-field approximation and did not contain any hadronic degrees of freedom in the confined phase. The inclusion of mesonic correlations via the ring sum enables a consistent description of the hadronic phase below T_c, where pions dominate the pressure, and clarifies the role of unphysical quark masses in lattice QCD thermodynamics (Karsch et al., 2000; Ali Khan et al., 2001). The approach enables future studies of nonzero chemical potential and inclusion of baryonic degrees of freedom, as well as investigation of the back-reaction of mesonic correlations on the mean fields.
