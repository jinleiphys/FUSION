# Study for the s-wave in ^{10}Li with analysis of cross-sections

> We study the effect of s-wave cross-sections in $^{4}$He+$n$ and $^{9}$Li+$n$ elastic scattering reactions by using the Jost function method (JFM). In $^{5}$He ($^{4}$He+$n$), the s-wave pole of the $S$-matrix does not contribute so much to the total cross-section. On the other hand, in $^{10}$Li ($^{9}$Li+$n$), the s-wave component can not be neglected due to a relatively strong attraction for the s-waves of the core+$n$ potential. It is shown that the $^{9}$Li-$n$ potential, which is microscopically derived by taking into account the pairing-blocking effect for a p-wave neutron, reproduces the s-wave pole close to the $^{9}$Li+$n$ threshold and strongly enhances the s-wave cross-section near the threshold.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the s-wave component in the 9Li+n system (10Li) cannot be neglected in the total elastic scattering cross-section, because a microscopically derived potential that includes the pairing-blocking effect for a p-wave neutron produces an s-wave virtual state pole close to the threshold, strongly enhancing the s-wave cross-section near that threshold. The authors hedge that this conclusion applies "at this stage" and that there is "no low-lying s-wave resonant state but is a 'virtual' state" in their potential model.

## Method

The authors used the Jost function method (JFM) to calculate total elastic scattering cross-sections for 4He+n and 9Li+n systems. For 4He+n, they employed the KKNN potential. For 9Li+n, they used a folding potential derived microscopically with a pairing-blocking effect for p-wave neutrons, varying the strength of the attractive part for even-parity states (s- and d-waves) to study the effect of the s-wave pole position on the cross-section. They then compared the smeared calculated cross-sections to experimental energy distributions of projectile fragments from 11Li+C → 9Li+n+X reactions.

## Key numbers

*   For the 4He+n system using the KKNN potential, the s-wave resonance pole is at E = 7.50 - i44.7 MeV, and the p3/2 resonance is calculated at Er = 0.74 MeV with Γ = 0.59 MeV (compared to experimental values Er = 0.89 ± 0.05 MeV, Γ = 0.60 ± 0.02 MeV).
*   For the 9Li+n system, the s-wave virtual state pole for (s1/2 ; 2-) is at k = -i0.077 fm-1 (Ev = -0.135 MeV) with a scattering length a0 = -9.97 fm.
*   Varying the potential strength for even-parity states produced s-wave virtual state poles at k = -i0.023 fm-1 (E = -0.012 MeV, a0 = -40.8 fm) for a stronger attraction and at k = -i0.36 fm-1 (E = -2.89 MeV) for a weaker attraction.
*   The p-wave resonance poles for (p1/2 ; 1+) and (p1/2 ; 2+) are at E = 0.422 - i0.161 MeV and 0.542 - i0.242 MeV, respectively.
*   The experimental scattering length from Thompson and Zhukov (1999) is estimated as a0 = -16+4-7 fm.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of the 10Li subsystem within the core+n+n model for the halo nucleus 11Li, following the proposal by Thompson and Zhukov (1994) of a strong s-wave attraction in the 9Li-n potential to reproduce the 11Li binding energy. It builds on the potential model of Kato, Yamada, and Ikeda (1999), which introduced a pairing-blocking effect to explain the strong state dependence. The paper enables a direct comparison between calculated elastic cross-sections and experimental energy distributions from projectile fragmentation (Kobayashi 1993), providing evidence that the s-wave state in 10Li is a virtual state rather than a low-lying resonance, which is a key open question in the field.
