# Model of the electro-weak, gravitational and strong interactions in the O-theory

> Based on the matrix representation of octonion algebra, supplied with specific multiplication rule, the model of electroweak and gravitational interactions is built up. While electroweak interaction in this model is induced by charged W-bosons, other two forces appear to have slightly more complicated nature. Gravitational interaction coincides in the model with dipole interaction of a pair of charged bosons. The dipole consists of a charged vector bosons pair from the major octonion algebra fields. When the charged dipole pair interacts with the neutral bosons pair from the major octonion algebra fields, the charged bosons pair misses its mass. The drop in mass leads to appearance of far-ranging forces of gravitational interaction. Finally, strong interaction appears in the model as internal gravitational solution of 'black whole' type with the peculiar 'gravitational' constant. The solution is a product of interaction of major vector fields pair with charged W-bosons pair. It is inferred from the model that the state space is ten-dimensional. The space is built as a module of the matrix representation of octonion algebra over the particles field (O-module). Similarly to the Standard Weinberg-Salam theory, the particle mass here appears as the product of interaction of massless spinor fields and Higgs field from O-module representation.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a unified field theory based on octonion algebra with a specific multiplication rule, claiming that gravitational interaction emerges as a dipole interaction of charged vector bosons and that strong interaction appears as an internal gravitational solution of "black hole" type, but these results depend on specific choices for relieving non-associativity and on the existence of bound states of the proposed C and E bosons.

## Method

The author constructs a Lagrangian on a ten-dimensional O-module (extended octonion space K) by generalizing the Weinberg-Salam Lagrangian to non-associative algebra, introducing seven vector fields A^A_mu (A=0,...,7) plus two additional fields f^8 and f^9. The non-associativity is handled through probabilistic, minimal, or minimax models that assign weights to different bracket permutations in products of four elements. The geometrization procedure maps the flat Minkowski space action to a curved spacetime action by identifying the vector fields A^5_mu and A^6_mu with the tetrad connection, leading to Einstein equations with the gravitational constant set by the D-boson mass.

## Key numbers

- The mass of the C-boson is given by m_C^2 = (q^(4))^2 m^2 / f, where m is the Higgs vacuum expectation value parameter and f is the quartic coupling
- The mass of the E-boson is m_E^2 = (q^(7))^2 m^2 / f
- The D-boson mass is m_D^2 = 2 m^2 q_D^2 / f
- The gravitational constant kappa is set equal to m_D^2 (kappa = m_D^2)
- The coefficients kappa_1 and kappa_2 in the current terms are approximately 10 (kappa_1, kappa_2 approx 10)
- The non-associativity relieving probabilities are p_1 = 3/4 and p_2 = 1/4 for the quadratic term

## Context

This work extends the author's previous generalizations of the Weinberg-Salam Lagrangian to non-associative algebras (Dorofeev, earlier papers) and proposes a scheme for introducing gravitational and strong interactions within the same octonion framework. The approach contrasts with group-theoretic M-theory constructions, which the author notes lose non-associativity in their Lagrangians. The model enables a geometric interpretation where gravitational interaction arises from the octonion field structure, and it suggests that at high energies the non-associative nature of the Lagrangian could provide new quantization and renormalization methods.
