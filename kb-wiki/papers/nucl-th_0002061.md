# Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov for deformed neutron-rich nuclei

> The two-basis method to solve the HFB for deformed nuclei in coordinate space is examined concerning the precision of the density tail. Small cutoff energies are shown to give rise to ripples in the tail, whose wave length corresponds to the cutoff momentum. More precise solutions require higher cutoff energies, which result in HFB matrices of larger dimensions. To circumvent the difficulty of the large dimension, we employ another method to solve the HFB ---the natural-orbital method introduced originally for spherical nuclei--- to apply it to deformed nuclei. The method can be successfully implemented with a three-dimensional Cartesian mesh representation.

## Key claim
The two-basis method for solving Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations for deformed nuclei in coordinate space produces ripples in the low-density tail when cutoff energies are too small, and the natural-orbital method can circumvent the large matrix dimensions required for high-precision solutions, but its convergence is about ten times slower than the Hartree-Fock case.

## Method
The two-basis method was tested for spherical nuclei by diagonalizing the HFB matrix in a single-particle basis of mean-field Hamiltonian eigenstates up to a cutoff energy, and density profiles were examined as a function of cutoff energy. The natural-orbital method, originally developed for spherical nuclei, was implemented for deformed nuclei using a three-dimensional Cartesian mesh representation with 39 × 39 × 39 mesh points at 0.8 fm spacing, employing a gradient method with imaginary-time evolution and Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization every 50 steps.

## Key numbers
- For the spherical test case (Z,N)=(38,68) with the Braghin-Vautherin force and v_p = -400 MeV, density localization accuracy was approximately 10^{-6} fm^{-3}
- The ripple wavelength in the density tail equals 2πℏ(2m ε_cut)^{-1/2}, half the de Broglie wavelength for the cutoff energy
- For the 3D-mesh natural-orbital test on ^{40}Ca, K=20 natural orbitals were used, which can contain 80 nucleons (2 × A)
- The time step Δτ was set to 10^{-24} sec in the imaginary-time evolution
- For the deformed case, K grows as ε_cut^{3/2}, compared to ε_cut^{1/2} for spherical nuclei

## Context
This work extends the two-basis method of Heenen et al. (1994) and the natural-orbital method of Reinhard et al. (1997) to deformed neutron-rich nuclei. It addresses the challenge that continuum states play an important role in pairing correlation for nuclei near the neutron drip line, where the Fermi level and pairing gap are both approximately ±1 MeV, and that the HF+BCS approximation produces unphysical dilute neutron gas when positive-energy states are included. The natural-orbital HFB method on a 3D mesh enables treatment of deformed nuclei where the two-basis method becomes impractical due to the rapid growth of basis size with cutoff energy.
