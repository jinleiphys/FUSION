# Effects of nuclear structure and quantum interference on diffractive vector meson production in ultra-peripheral nuclear collisions

> We study diffractive vector meson production in ultra-peripheral collisions (UPCs) of heavy nuclei, utilizing a theoretical framework based on the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism. We focus on Au+Au, U+U, Ru+Ru, Zr+Zr, and Pb+Pb collisions, examining the transverse momentum dependence of vector meson production cross-sections and ${\rm cos(2ΔΦ)}$ asymmetries in the decay product distributions to explore the role of nuclear geometry. The angular modulation is due to the linear polarization of the incoming photons and quantum interference effects. We extract nuclear radii and find them to be consistent with experimental data from the STAR collaboration. The amplitudes of the ${\rm cos(2ΔΦ)}$ modulation in the cross-section and the extracted radii depend on the nuclear geometry. This dependence is dominated by the geometry-dependent variation of the minimum impact parameter required for ultra-peripheral collisions.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the transverse momentum dependence of diffractive vector meson production cross-sections and the cos(2ΔΦ) modulation in ultra-peripheral nuclear collisions are sensitive to nuclear geometry and deformation, but this sensitivity is dominated by the geometry-dependent variation of the minimum impact parameter required for ultra-peripheral collisions rather than by the intrinsic nuclear shape alone.

## Method
The authors employ the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism with the dipole picture to compute exclusive vector meson production in ultra-peripheral collisions of heavy nuclei. They model nuclear geometries using Woods-Saxon distributions with deformation parameters, sample nucleon positions event-by-event, and implement quantum interference between the two indistinguishable photon-source/target configurations. The calculation includes the linear polarization of incoming photons and uses a Monte Carlo Glauber model to determine the minimum impact parameter B_min on an event-by-event basis.

## Key numbers
- For Au+Au collisions, the default setup yields an average minimum impact parameter ⟨B_min⟩ = 15 fm, determined by requiring N_coll = 0 from the Monte Carlo Glauber model
- The uranium radius parameter R_WS = 7.2 fm is fixed by fitting STAR data under the assumption that β_2 = 0.28, close to the mass radius determined from effective density functional methods in Ryssens (2023)
- The ρ meson decay width used is Γ_ρ = 0.156 GeV, and the φ meson decay width is Γ_φ = 0.00443 GeV
- The value of the modulus squared of the J/ψ radial wave function at the origin is |φ_J/ψ(0)| = 0.0447 GeV^3
- A minimal distance of d_min = 0.9 fm is imposed between nucleons when sampling in three dimensions
- The Woods-Saxon radius parameter for gold is R_WS = 6.38 fm with diffuseness a_WS = 0.54 fm and β_2 = 0.0
- For lead, R_WS = 6.62 fm with a_WS = 0.546 fm and β_2 = 0.0
- For uranium, R_WS = 7.20 fm with a_WS = 0.55 fm, β_2 = 0.28, and β_4 = 0.093
- For ruthenium (case 1), R_WS = 5.09 fm with a_WS = 0.46 fm and β_2 = 0.16
- For zirconium, R_WS = 5.02 fm with a_WS = 0.52 fm, β_2 = 0.06, and β_3 = 0.20

## Context
This work extends the framework of Xing (2020) and Hagiwara (2020) for describing vector meson production in ultra-peripheral collisions, incorporating nuclear deformation effects within the CGC formalism. It directly compares to STAR collaboration measurements of ρ meson production in Au+Au and U+U collisions (STAR 2022) and provides predictions for isobar Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions, where STAR (2023) has reported differences attributed to different radii and deformations. The results enable future comparisons with ALICE data for Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC and inform the interpretation of nuclear structure measurements at the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider, as discussed in Mantysaari (2023).
