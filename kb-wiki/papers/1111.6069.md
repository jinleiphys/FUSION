# Pretzelosity TMD and Quark Orbital Angular Momentum

> We study the connection between the quark orbital angular momentum and the pretzelosity transverse-momentum dependent parton distribution function. We discuss the origin of this relation in quark models, identifying as key ingredient for its validity the assumption of spherical symmetry for the nucleon in its rest frame. Finally we show that the individual quark contributions to the orbital angular momentum obtained from this relation can not be interpreted as the intrinsic contributions, but include the contribution from the transverse centre of momentum which cancels out only in the total orbital angular momentum.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the relation between quark orbital angular momentum (OAM) and the pretzelosity TMD \(h_{1T}^\perp\) holds only under the assumption of spherical symmetry for the nucleon in its rest frame, and that the individual quark contributions obtained from this relation include a spurious term from the transverse centre of momentum which cancels only in the total OAM.

## Method

The authors compare the overlap representations of the quark OAM and the pretzelosity TMD in terms of light-cone wave functions (LCWFs) for a generic class of \(SU(6)\) symmetric quark models. They derive the condition under which the two representations become identical, identifying spherical symmetry in the nucleon rest frame as the key ingredient. They then separate the intrinsic OAM contribution from the total OAM by defining the transverse centre-of-momentum operator, showing that the pretzelosity-derived OAM for individual flavours includes a non-intrinsic component.

## Key numbers

- The total quark OAM in the three-quark model is given by \(\mathcal L_z = \int [\mathrm{d}x]_3 [\mathrm{d}^2\mathbf{k}]_3 |\phi(r)|^2 \sin^2\frac{\theta(r)}{2}\), implying \(0 \leq \mathcal L_z < 1\).
- The isosinglet axial charge is \(\Delta\Sigma = \int [\mathrm{d}x]_3 [\mathrm{d}^2\mathbf{k}]_3 |\phi(r)|^2 \left(1 - 2\sin^2\frac{\theta(r)}{2}\right)\).
- The total angular momentum is conserved as \(J_z = \frac{1}{2}\Delta\Sigma + \mathcal L_z = \frac{1}{2}\).
- In the nonrelativistic limit \(\theta(r) \to 0\), the quark OAM vanishes \(\mathcal L_z^\text{NR} = 0\) and the nucleon spin arises only from quark spins \(J_z = \frac{1}{2}\Delta\Sigma^\text{NR}\).

## Context

This work extends the model calculations of She et al. (2009), Avakian et al. (2010), and Efremov et al. (2010) by clarifying the physical origin and limitations of the pretzelosity-OAM relation. It contradicts the naive interpretation that the relation gives intrinsic OAM for individual quark flavours, showing instead that the intrinsic OAM is obtained from Wigner distributions as shown by Lorce' (2011). The results enable a proper separation of intrinsic and centre-of-momentum contributions in future model calculations and experimental extractions of quark OAM from TMDs.
