# Mutual assistance between the Schwinger mechanism and the dynamical Casimir effect

> We study massive charged particle production from the vacuum confined between two vibrating plates in the presence of a strong electric field. We analytically derive a formula for the production number based on the perturbation theory in the Furry picture, and show that the Schwinger mechanism by the strong electric field and the dynamical Casimir effect by the vibration assist with each other to dramatically enhance the production number by orders of the magnitude.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that when a strong electric field (Schwinger mechanism) and vibrating plates (dynamical Casimir effect) act simultaneously on a massive charged scalar field, the two production mechanisms assist each other to dramatically enhance particle production by orders of magnitude, effectively reducing the mass gap that each mechanism individually must overcome.

## Method

The author considers a massive charged scalar field confined between two vibrating plates with a strong electric field applied in the transverse direction, using perturbation theory in the Furry picture where the electric field interaction is treated non-perturbatively while the plate vibration is treated perturbatively to lowest order in the small displacement amplitude. The field equation is solved by expanding in the small vibration parameter, using a retarded Green function fully dressed by the strong electric field, and the production number is computed from the in-vacuum expectation value of the out-state number operator via Bogoliubov transformation. For quantitative analysis, the electric field is approximated as constant and homogeneous, allowing exact evaluation of the production number formula using parabolic cylinder functions.

## Key numbers

- The critical electric field for the Schwinger mechanism is \(eE_{\rm cr} \equiv m_{\rm e}^2 \sim \sqrt{10^{29}\;{\rm W/cm^2}}\) for electron-positron pair production
- The present world record for electric field strength is \(eE \sim \sqrt{10^{22}\;{\rm W/cm^2}}\) established at HERCULES
- Forthcoming laser experiments at ELI and XCELS may reach \(eE \sim \sqrt{10^{24{\rm-}25}\;{\rm W/cm^2}}\), still below the critical field by several orders of magnitude
- For the dynamical Casimir effect to produce cavity photons, vibration frequency \(\omega/2\pi \sim 10\;{\rm GHz}\) is required, while for massive particles like electrons \(\omega/2\pi \sim 2m_e/2\pi \sim 100\;{\rm EHz}\) is needed
- An indirect observation of the dynamical Casimir effect achieved \(\omega/2\pi \sim 11\;{\rm GHz}\) using a SQUID-modulated transmission line

## Context

This work extends the dynamically assisted Schwinger mechanism proposed by Schutzhold et al. (2008) and further developed by Taya (2019), Huang et al. (2019), and others, which showed that a fast weak electromagnetic field can enhance the Schwinger mechanism by orders of magnitude. The paper generalizes this idea by replacing the fast weak electromagnetic field with vibrating plates (the dynamical Casimir effect), showing that parametric particle production from mechanical vibration can similarly assist non-perturbative pair production. The formalism based on perturbation theory in the Furry picture, previously successful for describing the dynamically assisted Schwinger mechanism, is shown to smoothly describe the interplay between the two mechanisms, which is not feasible within adiabatic approaches such as the worldline instanton method. This work enables future experimental proposals for observing either the Schwinger mechanism or the dynamical Casimir effect for massive particles by combining both effects to lower the required field strength or frequency thresholds.
