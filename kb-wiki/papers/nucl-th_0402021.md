# Survey of charge symmetry breaking operators for dd -> alpha pi0

> The charge-symmetry-breaking amplitudes for the recently observed d d -> alpha pi0 reaction are investigated. Chiral perturbation theory is used to classify and identify the leading-order terms. Specific forms of the related one- and two-body tree level diagrams are derived. As a first step toward a full calculation, a few tree-level two-body diagrams are evaluated at each considered order, using a simplified set of d and alpha wave functions and a plane-wave approximation for the initial dd state. The leading-order pion-exchange term is shown to be suppressed in this model because of poor overlap of the initial and final states. The higher-order one-body and short-range (heavy-meson-exchange) amplitudes provide better matching between the initial and final states and therefore contribute significantly and coherently to the cross section. The consequences this might have for a full calculation, with realistic wave functions and a more complete set of amplitudes, are discussed.

# Survey of charge symmetry breaking operators for dd → απ⁰

## Key claim

The leading-order pion-exchange charge-symmetry-breaking amplitude for dd → απ⁰ is suppressed in a simplified model due to poor overlap between initial and final states, while higher-order one-body and short-range (heavy-meson-exchange) amplitudes provide better momentum matching and therefore contribute significantly and coherently to the cross section, but this conclusion depends on the use of simplified wave functions and a plane-wave approximation for the initial dd state, and a full calculation with realistic wave functions may alter the relative importance of these contributions.

## Method

The authors used chiral perturbation theory to classify charge-symmetry-breaking operators for the dd → απ⁰ reaction up to next-to-next-to-leading order, deriving explicit forms of one- and two-body tree-level diagrams. They evaluated a subset of tree-level two-body diagrams at each considered order using simplified deuteron and alpha-particle wave functions (Gaussian forms) and a plane-wave approximation for the initial deuteron-deuteron state, without including initial-state interactions. The short-range contributions were modeled via heavy-meson exchange (π-η mixing, ρ, ω, σ exchange) under the resonance saturation hypothesis.

## Key numbers

- The IUCF Cooler ring experiment reported a dd → απ⁰ cross section of σ = 12.7 ± 2.2 pb at T_d = 228.5 MeV and 15.1 ± 3.1 pb at 231.8 MeV, near threshold (T_d = 225.6 MeV).
- The forward-backward asymmetry in np → dπ⁰ was measured as A_fb = [17.2 ± 8 (stat) ± 5.5 (sys)] × 10⁻⁴ at TRIUMF.
- The nucleon mass difference is ΔM = M_n − M_p = 1.29 MeV, with the electromagnetic contribution estimated as δ̄M = −(0.76 ± 0.30) MeV from the Cottingham sum rule.
- The pion-nucleon CSB parameter β₁ + β̄₃ is constrained by Nijmegen phase-shift analysis to (0 ± 9) × 10⁻³.
- The pion decay constant is f_π = 92.4 MeV; the axial couplings are g_A = 1.26 and h_A = 2.8.
- Heavy-meson coupling constants used: g²_σNN/4π = 7.1 (m_σ = 550 MeV/c²), g²_ωNN/4π = 10.6 (m_ω = 783 MeV/c²), g²_ρNN/4π = 0.43 (m_ρ = 770 MeV/c²), with C_ρ = 6.1.
- The initial deuteron center-of-momentum momentum is p ∼ 460 MeV/c at threshold.

## Context

This work provides the first study of charge symmetry breaking in near-threshold dd → απ⁰ using chiral effective field theory techniques, extending earlier work on π-η-η′ mixing at higher energies (cp86) and complementing studies of CSB in np → dπ⁰ (chm79, nis99, vKNM). The analysis is motivated by the recent IUCF observation of dd → απ⁰ (IUCFCSB) and aims to establish the framework for a full calculation with realistic wave functions, initial-state interactions, and Δ-isobar admixtures, which the authors state will be reported in forthcoming papers. The results enable future determination of the low-energy constants δM and δ̄M (related to the quark mass difference and electromagnetic contributions to the nucleon mass splitting) by comparing calculations of both np → dπ⁰ and dd → απ⁰ within the same framework.
