# Stellar matter with a strong magnetic field within density-dependent relativistic models

> The effect of strong magnetic fields on the equation of state (EoS) for compact stars described with density-dependent relativistic hadronic models is studied. A comparison with other mean-field relativistic models is done. It is shown that the largest differences between models occur for low densities, and that the magnetic field affects the crust properties of a star, namely its extension.

## Key claim

The paper shows that while density-dependent relativistic hadronic (DDRH) models and non-linear Walecka model (NLWM) parametrizations (GM3, TM1) behave similarly overall for stellar matter under strong magnetic fields, the largest differences between models occur at low densities (below about 3 times nuclear saturation density), and that the magnetic field significantly affects the crust properties of compact stars by extending the region of mechanical instability, particularly when the nucleon anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) is not included.

## Method

The authors solve the mean-field equations for charge-neutral, beta-equilibrated hadronic matter (protons, neutrons, electrons, muons) in a uniform external magnetic field along the z-axis, using density-dependent relativistic hadronic (DDRH) models both with and without the isovector-scalar delta meson (TW and DDRHdelta models). They compare results with two non-linear Walecka model parametrizations (GM3 and TM1) previously studied in the literature, computing the equation of state (EoS), particle fractions, effective masses, and the upper density limit of mechanical instability for magnetic field strengths up to B* = 10^6 (where B* = B / B^e_c with B^e_c = 4.414 x 10^13 G).

## Key numbers

- The critical magnetic field for electron cyclotron energy equaling electron mass is B^e_c = 4.414 x 10^13 G, defining B* = B / B^e_c.
- For B* = 10^5, the second Landau level for protons starts being occupied only above densities of about 2 times nuclear saturation density (rho_0); for B* = 5 x 10^5, above about 8 rho_0.
- At zero magnetic field, the predicted density at the inner edge of the crust (from thermodynamic instability crossing) ranges from 0.068762 fm^-3 (GM3) to 0.085038 fm^-3 (DDRHdelta), with corresponding pressures from 0.12855 MeV fm^-3 (DDRHdelta) to 0.52246 MeV fm^-3 (TW).
- For B* = 10^6 without AMM, the EoS becomes softer than the B=0 EoS for energy densities below 70 MeV/fm^3.
- The GM3 model has an effective nucleon mass of 0.78 M at saturation without magnetic field, while all other models have effective masses of 0.6 M or smaller.
- The strongest estimated surface magnetic field detected in a magnetar (SGR 1806-20) is of order B* = 10^2.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of strong magnetic fields in compact stars using non-linear Walecka model parametrizations (Broderick et al., Shen et al.) by applying density-dependent relativistic hadronic models (TW, DDRHdelta) that were originally parametrized to reproduce Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations. The authors compare with the GM3 and TM1 parametrizations studied in Broderick et al. and Shen et al., and note that DDRH models at subsaturation densities behave similarly to non-relativistic models with Skyrme forces (e.g., SLy230a from Chabanat et al., NRAPR from Steiner et al.) and variational microscopic calculations (Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall). The results enable more realistic modeling of magnetar crust properties, which are needed for simulations of magnetized star cooling (Pons et al., 2008) that require the EoS of the crust including magnetic field effects.
