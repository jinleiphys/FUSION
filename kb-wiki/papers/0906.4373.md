# Resonant enhancement of nuclear reactions as a possible solution to the cosmological lithium problem

> There is a significant discrepancy between the current theoretical prediction of the cosmological lithium abundance, mostly produced as Be7 during the Big Bang, and its observationally inferred value. We investigate whether the resonant enhancement of Be7 burning reactions may alleviate this discrepancy. We identify one narrow nuclear level in B9, E_{5/2^+} \simeq 16.7 MeV that is not sufficiently studied experimentally, and being just \sim 200 keV above the Be7+d threshold, may lead to the resonant enhancement of Be7(d,γ)B9 and Be7(d,p)ααreactions. We determine the relationship between the domain of resonant energies E_r and the deuterium separation width Γ_d that results in the significant depletion of the cosmological lithium abundance and find that (E_r, ~Γ_{d}) \simeq (170-220,~10-40) keV can eliminate current discrepancy. Such a large width at this resonant energy can be only achieved if the interaction radius for the deterium entrance channel is very large, a_{27} \ge 9 fm. Our results also imply that before dedicated nuclear experimental and theoretical work is done to clarify the role played by this resonance, the current conservative BBN prediction of lithium abundance should carry significantly larger error bars, [Li7/H]_{\rm BBN} = (2.5-6)\times 10^{-10}.

## Key claim
The authors propose that a narrow, poorly-studied nuclear resonance in $^9$B at approximately 16.7 MeV, located about 200 keV above the $^7$Be+d threshold, could resonantly enhance $^7$Be destruction by deuterons and thereby resolve the cosmological lithium problem, but only if the deuteron separation width and interaction radius are at the extreme upper limits allowed by quantum mechanics.

## Method
The authors identified the $5/2^+$ resonance in $^9$B at 16.7 MeV as a candidate for enhancing $^7$Be(d,$\gamma$)$^9$B and $^7$Be(d,p)$\alpha\alpha$ reactions. They parameterized the resonance by its energy $E_r$, deuteron separation width $\Gamma_d$, and total width, then inserted the resonant reaction rate into a standard Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) code to compute the resulting $^7$Li+$^7$Be abundance as a function of these parameters. They compared the required resonance strength to the Wigner limit for the deuteron separation width, using the channel radius $a_{27}$ as a free parameter.

## Key numbers
- The Spite plateau observed lithium abundance is $1.23^{+0.34}_{-0.16}\times 10^{-10}$ (68% confidence), while the standard BBN prediction is $5.24^{+0.71}_{-0.67}\times 10^{-10}$.
- The resonance energy and deuterium separation width required to eliminate the discrepancy are $(E_r, \Gamma_d) \simeq (170-220, 10-40)$ keV.
- The required width can only be achieved if the interaction radius for the deuteron entrance channel is very large, $a_{27} \ge 9$ fm.
- Allowing the resonance parameters to vary within quantum-mechanical constraints, the BBN prediction for lithium abundance becomes $[^7$Li/H$]_{\rm BBN} = (2.5-6)\times 10^{-10}$.
- The mirror resonance in $^9$Be at $16.671 \pm 8$ MeV has a measured total width of $41 \pm 4$ keV.

## Context
This work extends the search for a nuclear physics solution to the cosmological lithium problem, following earlier suggestions that arbitrary rescaling of the $^7$Be(d,p)$\alpha\alpha$ rate by a factor of O(100) could deplete $^7$Be (French, 2004), a hypothesis that was experimentally tested and not supported by Angulo (2005). The authors specifically investigate a known but poorly-studied resonance rather than hypothesizing new resonances, analogous to Hoyle's prediction of the 7.65 MeV resonance in $^{12}$C. If confirmed, this would close the last nuclear "loophole" in BBN lithium predictions; if refuted, it would strengthen the case for astrophysical or new-physics solutions such as decays of meta-stable particles (Jedamzik, 2006; Pospelov, 2007).
