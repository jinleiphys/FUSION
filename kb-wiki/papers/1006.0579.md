# Electric Dipole Moments and Polarizability in the Quark-Diquark Model of the Neutron

> For a bound state internal wave function respecting parity symmetry, it can be rigorously argued that the mean electric dipole moment must be strictly zero. Thus, both the neutron, viewed as a bound state of three quarks, and the water molecule, viewed as a bound state of ten electrons two protons and an oxygen nucleus, both have zero mean electric dipole moments. Yet, the water molecule is said to have a nonzero dipole moment strength $d=eΛ$ with $Λ_{H_2O} \approx 0.385\ \dot{A}$. The neutron may also be said to have an electric dipole moment strength with $Λ_{neutron} \approx 0.612\ fm$. The neutron analysis can be made experimentally consistent, if one employs a quark-diquark model of neutron structure.

# Electric Dipole Moments and Polarizability in the Quark-Diquark Model of the Neutron

## Key claim
While the mean electric dipole moment of the neutron must be strictly zero due to parity symmetry, the neutron can still possess a nonzero electric dipole moment strength defined as the root-mean-square of the dipole operator, and this strength can be consistently described using a quark-diquark model of neutron structure.

## Method
The authors model the neutron as a bound state of a ud diquark and a single d quark connected by a string of length L and tension τ. They compute the neutron electric dipole moment strength from the relativistic Regge trajectory relating the neutron and delta baryon masses, and then derive the neutron polarizability from the dipole strength using the quantum mechanical sum rule connecting the neutron (j=1/2) ground state to the delta (j=3/2) excited state.

## Key numbers
- Neutron electric dipole moment strength: Λ_neutron ≈ 0.6120 fm, corresponding to d = eΛ with Λ = L/3
- Neutron polarizability theoretical prediction: α(theory) ≈ 12.3 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Neutron polarizability experimental value: α(experiment) = (12.0 ± 1.5 ± 2.0) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Ratio of delta mass to neutron mass: M_Δ/M = 1.311
- Reduced Compton wavelength of the neutron: ħ/(Mc) = 0.21009416 fm
- Fine structure constant: e²/(ħc) ≈ 7.29735257 × 10⁻³

## Context
This work extends the analogy between the water molecule and the neutron as composite systems with zero mean dipole moment but nonzero dipole strength, building on the Debye (1929) classical fluctuation-response relation for polarizability. The quark-diquark string model follows earlier work by Srivastava (2000) and Anselmino (1993) on diquark models of hadrons. The predicted neutron polarizability agrees with experimental measurements by Schmiedmayer (1991), and the authors note a similar quark-diquark model successfully describes the proton polarizability as reported by Leon (2001). This enables a consistent framework for understanding hadron electric dipole strengths and polarizabilities without adjustable parameters.
