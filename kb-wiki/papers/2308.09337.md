# Proton number cumulants in a modified van der Waals hadron resonance gas

> An estimate of the proton number cumulants in the hadronic matter is presented considering a van der Waals-type interaction between the constituent particles. We argue that the attractive and repulsive parameters in the VDW hadron resonance gas (VDWHRG) model change as functions of baryochemical potential ($μ_{B}$) and temperature ($T$). This, in turn, affects the estimation of thermodynamic properties and, consequently, the conserved charge fluctuations. We employ a simple parametrization to bring in the center-of-mass energy ($\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$) dependence on temperature and baryochemical potential and then estimate the proton number cumulants with the modified approach. The modified van der Waals hadron resonance gas model (MVDWHRG) explains the existing experimental data very well.

# Proton number cumulants in a modified van der Waals hadron resonance gas

## Key claim

The attractive and repulsive van der Waals parameters in the VDWHRG model are not constant but decrease exponentially as functions of baryochemical potential over temperature (μB/T), and using this modified parameterization the model explains the STAR BES-I net-proton fluctuation data well, whereas the standard VDWHRG with constant parameters fails.

## Method

The authors performed χ² minimization fits of the VDWHRG model to lattice QCD data for scaled energy density and pressure at fixed values of μB/T (0.0, 1.0, 2.0, 2.5) to extract the attractive parameter a and repulsive parameter b at each μB/T. They then fitted these extracted values with negative exponential functions of μB/T to obtain continuous parameterizations a(μB/T) and b(μB/T). Using these modified parameters, they computed net-proton cumulant ratios (Sσ, Sσ³/M, κσ²) and correlation functions as functions of center-of-mass energy √sNN via standard freeze-out parameterizations of T(μB) and μB(√sNN).

## Key numbers

* At μB/T = 0.0: a = 1.650 ± 0.05 GeV fm³, baryon radius rB = 0.635 ± 0.05 fm, χ² = 1.06/20
* At μB/T = 1.0: a = 0.786 ± 0.064 GeV fm³, rB = 0.515 ± 0.05 fm, χ² = 0.91/20
* At μB/T = 2.0: a = 0.275 ± 0.025 GeV fm³, rB = 0.425 ± 0.05 fm, χ² = 1.88/20
* At μB/T = 2.5: a = 0.150 ± 0.05 GeV fm³, rB = 0.385 ± 0.15 fm, χ² = 3.5/20
* Exponential fit parameters: c₁ = 1.66 ± 0.05 GeV fm³, c₂ = -0.88 ± 0.04 for a; d₁ = 541.93 ± 15.98 GeV⁻³, d₂ = -0.61 ± 0.03 for b
* Standard VDWHRG constant parameters used for comparison: a = 329 MeV fm³, b = 3.42 fm³ (from Vovchenko 2015)
* Freeze-out parameterization constants: q₁ = 0.166 GeV, q₂ = 0.139 GeV⁻¹, q₃ = 0.053 GeV⁻³, q₄ = 1.308 GeV, q₅ = 0.273 GeV⁻¹ (from Cleymans 2005)

## Context

This work extends the VDWHRG model of Vovchenko (2015, 2016) by making the attractive and repulsive parameters functions of μB/T rather than constants fitted to nuclear matter ground-state properties. It addresses the failure of constant-parameter VDWHRG to explain STAR BES-I net-proton fluctuation data (STAR 2021), which had been partially attributed to excluded volume effects and global baryon conservation in hybrid models (Vovchenko 2021). The MVDWHRG model shows that at high μB (low √sNN) the VDW parameters weaken toward zero, causing the system to approach ideal HRG behavior, which matches the data trend. The authors note that the liquid-gas phase transition expected from VDW interactions is absent in this model due to the exponential decrease of a and b, but suggest that future lattice QCD data at higher μB/T may reveal a minimum followed by an increase, potentially restoring critical behavior as seen in χEFT studies (Fujimoto 2021).
