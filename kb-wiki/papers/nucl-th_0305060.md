# Low-momentum Pion Enhancement Induced by Chiral Symmetry Restoration

> The thermal and nonthermal pion production by sigma decay and its relation with chiral symmetry restoration in a hot and dense matter are investigated. The nonthermal decay into pions of sigma mesons which are popularly produced in chiral symmetric phase leads to a low-momentum pion enhancement as a possible signature of chiral phase transition at finite temperature and density.

# Low-momentum Pion Enhancement Induced by Chiral Symmetry Restoration

## Key claim
Nonthermal decay of sigma mesons produced in the chiral symmetric phase can lead to a low-momentum pion enhancement that serves as a possible signature of chiral phase transition at finite temperature and density, but the effect is strongest in baryon-free central collisions where temperature effects dominate; the sharp peak predicted from temperature-only scenarios is smoothed when density effects are significant.

## Method
The author uses a simple statistical model to calculate the total final-state pion yield as the sum of direct pions emitted at freeze-out and pions from sigma decay, where the number of sigmas is evaluated at the chiral phase transition temperature. The model incorporates Bjorken scaling hydrodynamics to relate freeze-out and chiral transition volumes and temperatures, and computes the pseudorapidity density of charged pions per participant pair in the mid-rapidity region, distinguishing thermal from nonthermal sigma decay contributions.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature of chiral phase transition: \( T_c = 170\ \mathrm{MeV} \) (from lattice QCD simulations).
- Thermal freeze-out temperature: \( T_f = 120\ \mathrm{MeV} \).
- Pion mass at freeze-out: \( m_\pi(T_f) = 140\ \mathrm{MeV} \) (temperature independent).
- Sigma mass at chiral phase transition: \( m_\sigma(T_c) = 2m_\pi(0) = 280\ \mathrm{MeV} \).
- Vacuum sigma mass: \( m_\sigma(0,0) = 600\ \mathrm{MeV} \).
- Nonthermal pion momentum in vacuum: \( p_0 = 265\ \mathrm{MeV} \).
- Ratio of pions from sigma decay to direct pions: \( r = 52\% \).
- Enhancement factor drops rapidly with window width and saturates above about \( 0.4\ \mathrm{GeV} \); even for \( \beta = 0.9 \) (90% thermal fraction), enhancement in a narrow window remains significant.

## Context
This work extends earlier proposals for chiral transition signatures, such as pion excess from rapid equilibration (Song, 1993), low-energy photon pairs from pion annihilation (Volkov, 1996), dilepton enhancement from sigma leptonic decay (Weldon, 1990), and continuum threshold enhancement in the scalar channel (Chiku, 1998). It specifically builds on the idea of nonthermal sigma decay after freeze-out (Stephanov, 1999; Zhuang, 2002) and provides a quantitative estimate of the enhancement factor. The results enable experimental searches for a low-momentum pion peak in central relativistic heavy-ion collisions as a direct signature of chiral symmetry restoration, though the author notes that sigma width and momentum effects will smooth the sharp peak predicted in the simplest model.
