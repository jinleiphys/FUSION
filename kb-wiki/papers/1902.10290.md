# Constraints on Disconnected Contributions in $ПЂПЂ$ Scattering

> The accuracy of the lattice QCD computation of hadron-hadron scattering at low isospin depends critically on the ability to compute correlation functions with fermionic disconnected Wick contractions. This happens, for instance, in isospin $I=0$ $ПЂПЂ$ scattering, which receives contributions from rectangular and vacuum types of contractions among other easier calculable ones. Combining LГјscher's formula and partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory, we provide precise theory predictions of the discrete energy levels extracted from specific linear combinations of lattice correlation functions corresponding to various types of contractions. Expressions are provided for extracting the unphysical low-energy constants in the partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory from the energy levels for these contractions. The predictions for the rectangular and vacuum contractions may serve as solid tests of the accuracy for existing and future lattice studies of $ПЂПЂ$ scattering.

## Key claim

By combining LГјscher's formula with partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory (PQChPT) at next-to-leading order, the authors provide theory predictions for discrete energy levels from specific linear combinations of lattice correlation functions that isolate rectangular and vacuum-type disconnected contractions; these predictions serve as model-independent tests of lattice QCD calculations, but only up to corrections from higher orders in the chiral expansion and from finite lattice spacing.

## Method

The authors construct four linear combinations of the direct (D), crossed (C), rectangular (R), and vacuum (V) contraction amplitudes in SU(4|2) partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory that behave as effective single-channel S-wave scattering amplitudes, each satisfying the single-channel LГјscher formula. They compute the scattering lengths and effective ranges for these four combinations up to next-to-leading order in PQChPT, then use existing lattice data from the ETM Collaboration on the connected (D and C) correlation functions to fit the poorly-constrained unphysical low-energy constants Lв‚Ђ^{PQ,r} and Lв‚ѓ^{PQ,r}. Finally, they solve LГјscher's formula numerically to predict the energy shifts for the Оі-type (involving rectangular contractions) and Оґ-type (the I=0 channel involving all contractions) correlation functions as functions of lattice size.

## Context

This work extends the earlier SU(4) chiral perturbation theory approach of Guo (2013) and the SU(4|2) partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory calculations of Acharya (2017) by connecting the infinite-volume scattering amplitudes for individual contraction diagrams to finite-volume energy levels accessible in lattice QCD via LГјscher's formula. The predictions for the rectangular and vacuum contraction contributions provide concrete benchmarks for lattice studies such as those by Liu (2016) and Briceno (2016), which struggled with statistical noise in disconnected diagrams. The formalism enables lattice practitioners to use the easily-computed connected correlation functions to constrain unphysical low-energy constants, then predict the more difficult disconnected contributions as a consistency check.
