# Effect of resonance decays on hadron elliptic flows

> The influence of resonance decays on the elliptic flows of stable hadrons is studied in the quark coalescence model. Although difference between the elliptic flow of pions from resonance decays, except the rho meson, and that of directly produced pions is appreciable, those for other stable hadrons are small. Since there are more pions from the decays of rho mesons than from other resonances, including resonance decays can only account partially the deviation of final pion elliptic flow from the observed scaling of hadron elliptic flows, i.e., the hadron elliptic flow per quark is the same at same transverse momentum per quark. The remaining deviation can be explained by including the effect due to the quark momentum distribution inside hadrons.

# Effect of resonance decays on hadron elliptic flows

## Key claim

Resonance decays can account for only about half of the observed deviation of the final pion elliptic flow from the quark number scaling (the rule that hadron elliptic flow per quark is the same at the same transverse momentum per quark); the remaining deviation is explained by including the effect of the quark momentum distribution inside hadrons.

## Method

The authors used a quark coalescence model (Monte Carlo method of Greco and Ko, 2003) to generate transverse momentum distributions of hadrons from soft partons (thermal spectra at T=170 MeV with radial flow up to 0.5c) and hard partons (quenched minijets above 2 GeV/c). They then computed the elliptic flows of stable hadrons from resonance decays (rho, omega, K*, Delta) assuming all resonances follow the same scaled elliptic flow as in the naive quark coalescence model, and compared these to experimental data from PHENIX and STAR at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Parton density at hadronization: about 1.15 fm^{-3} (volume V=900 fm^3)
- Temperature of soft parton thermal spectra: T=170 MeV
- Maximum collective radial flow velocity: 0.5c
- Momentum width of hadron Wigner distribution functions: Delta_p=0.24 GeV/c for mesons, 0.35 GeV/c for baryons
- Quark elliptic flow fit parameters (naive model): v_0=0.078, alpha=1.59, phi=-0.27
- Quark elliptic flow fit parameters (realistic model with momentum distribution): v_0=0.089, alpha=1.89, phi=-0.22
- Rho meson width: 150 MeV (tested up to 300 MeV for collision broadening)
- Rho meson mass in free space: 770 MeV/c^2; a reduction to 500 MeV/c^2 could explain remaining pion flow deviation
- Observed rho meson mass shift at RHIC: about 70 MeV/c^2 less than free-space value

## Context

This work extends the naive quark coalescence model of Voloshin (1999) and the more realistic coalescence models of Greco, Ko, and Levai (2003) and Fries, Muller, Nonaka, and Bass (2003) by adding resonance decay effects. It addresses the observed violation of quark number scaling for pions seen in PHENIX and STAR data at RHIC. The finding that resonance decays account for half the deviation complements the thermal model results of Sollfrank (1997) and Broniowski (2003). The remaining deviation from quark momentum distributions was also studied by Lin and Molnar (2003) for collinear quarks. The paper enables future tests: measuring phi meson elliptic flow at RHIC can distinguish between the coalescence model prediction (phi follows quark number scaling) and the hydrodynamical model prediction (phi flow similar to proton, violating scaling).
