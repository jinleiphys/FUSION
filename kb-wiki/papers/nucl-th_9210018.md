# Electromagnetic Decays of Hyperons (II)

> Excited negative parity hyperon masses are calculated in a chiral bag model in which the pion and the kaon fields are treated as perturbations. We also calculate the hadronic widths of $\lama$ and $\lamb$ as well as the coupling constants of the lightest $I=0$ excited hyperon to the meson-baryon channels, and discuss how the dispersive effects of the hadronic meson-baryon decay channels affect the excited hyperon masses. Meson cloud corrections to the electromagnetic decay widths of the two lightest excited hyperons into ground states $\lamz$ and $\sigz$ are calculated within the same model and are found to be small. Our results strengthen the argument that predictions of these hyperon radiative decay widths provide an excellent test for various quark models of hadrons.

## Key claim

The chiral bag model predicts that meson cloud corrections to the electromagnetic decay widths of the two lightest excited hyperons (Λ(1520) and Λ(1405)) into ground states Λ⁰ and Σ⁰ are small, strengthening the argument that these radiative decay widths provide an excellent test for distinguishing between different quark models of hadrons.

## Method

The authors used a perturbative chiral bag model where pion and kaon fields are treated as perturbations, with the meson cloud surrounding the quark core coupled through a pseudoscalar coupling at the bag surface. They calculated excited negative parity hyperon masses including one-gluon exchange and effective one-pion and one-kaon exchange interactions, then evaluated hadronic widths and coupling constants for Λ(1520) and Λ(1405) using the imaginary parts of baryon self-energy diagrams. Meson cloud corrections to radiative decay widths were computed by constructing meson electromagnetic transition currents where the photon couples only to mesons in flight through the kinetic energy term.

## Key numbers

* Λ(1520) total decay width calculated as 23.6 MeV compared to the measured width of 15.6 MeV
* Λ(1405) πΣ decay width calculated as approximately 6 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 50±2 MeV from Dalitz and Deloff
* For Λ(1405) coupling constants: |G_{ΛπΣ}| = 0.698 and |G_{ΛKN}| = 0.46 with R = 1.2 fm and m_S = 250 MeV
* For a pure flavor singlet Λ(1405): |G_{ΛπΣ}|₁ = 1.211 and |G_{ΛKN}|₁ = 0.327
* Ground state coupling constants: |G_{Λ⁰KN}| = 9.68 and |G_{Σ⁰KN}| = 3.23 with R = 1.125 fm and m_S = 250 MeV
* Bag parameters used: B^{1/4} = 145 MeV, Z₀ = 0.25, α_s = 1.5, m_S = 250 MeV, with bag radius R = 1.2 fm
* The kaon cloud lowers masses of all states by about 30 to 60 MeV relative to results with only pion cloud

## Context

This work extends previous chiral bag model calculations by Umino and Myhrer (1991-1992) to include kaon cloud contributions and mass-difference corrections to baryon self-energy diagrams, addressing the long-standing problem of the Λ(1405) mass and structure. The results contrast with non-relativistic quark model calculations by Isgur and Karl, which predict Λ(1405) as a pure flavor singlet state, while the chiral bag model finds it as an approximately equal mixture of flavor singlet and octet states. The paper enables experimental tests at CEBAF (now Jefferson Lab) to distinguish between quark models through measurements of hyperon radiative decay widths, and calls for reanalysis of K⁻N scattering data to determine whether the Λ(1405) coupling constant ratio differs from the SU(3) value of 2/3.
