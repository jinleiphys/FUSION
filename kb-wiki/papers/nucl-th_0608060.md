# An inversion-asymmetric source function for HBT analysis in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The inversion-asymmetry of the emission source in relativistic heavy ion collision under the Bertsch-Pratt convention is discussed and explicitly exhibited by a Monte Carlo model. The Gaussian source function popularly used in the HBT analysis of relativistic heavy ion collisions is invalid in this case. An inversion-asymmetric source function is suggested. A method for extracting the inversion-asymmetry degree of the source together with the source size from experimental data is proposed.

## Key claim

The Gaussian source function commonly used in HBT analysis is invalid along the outward direction in the Bertsch-Pratt frame because the existence of a fixed transverse momentum vector \(\bs K\) breaks inversion symmetry about \(x_o=0\); the authors propose a three-parameter "NBa" distribution that can describe the resulting inversion asymmetry, and a method to extract both the asymmetry degree and source size from experimental data.

## Method

The authors use the AMPT Monte Carlo model (based on HIJING initial conditions, ZPC parton cascade, coalescence/fragmentation hadronization, and ART hadronic transport) to simulate Au-Au central collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200\) GeV and examine the \(x_o\)-\(x_s\) distribution of hadron pairs at three times (5, 15, 25 fm). They define an inversion-asymmetry degree \(\mathcal{A}\) and propose a three-parameter source function \(P_{NBa}(x)\) that can vary from nearly Gaussian (symmetric) to highly asymmetric. They fit the simulated \(x_o\) distributions to both the NBa and Gaussian functions, then derive the correlation function for a source that is Gaussian in \(x_l\) and \(x_s\) but NBa in \(x_o\).

## Key numbers

- Asymmetry degree \(\mathcal{A}\) for the NBa distribution varies from \(\sim 0\) at large \(N\) to \(\sim 0.5\) at small \(N\) (Fig. 4).
- For a typical Au-Au central collision event at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200\) GeV from AMPT: at \(t=5\) fm, \(R_o = 2.28\) fm and \(\mathcal{A} = 0.0065\); at \(t=15\) fm, \(R_o = 3.30\) fm and \(\mathcal{A} = 0.0565\); at \(t=25\) fm, \(R_o = 4.90\) fm and \(\mathcal{A} = 0.1137\) (Table I).
- The NBa source size is given by \(R_{NBa} = |B| \cdot \sqrt{N+1}\) (Eq. 9).

## Context

This work extends the standard HBT formalism (Hanbury-Brown and Twiss, 1956; Bertsch, 1989; Pratt, 1986) by identifying a previously overlooked symmetry violation: the inversion asymmetry along the outward direction in the Bertsch-Pratt frame. The Gaussian source function, used in prior analyses (e.g., Gyulassy lectures), cannot capture this asymmetry. The proposed NBa distribution and fitting method enable extraction of the asymmetry degree \(\mathcal{A}\) alongside the usual source size parameters \(R_l\), \(R_s\), and \(R_o\) from experimental correlation data, which was not possible with Gaussian fits. The authors recommend applying this method to real experimental data from RHIC.
