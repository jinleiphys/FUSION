# Cooper-Frye sampling with short-range repulsion

> This work incorporates the effect of short-range repulsion between particles into the Cooper-Frye hadron sampling procedure. This is achieved by means of a rejection sampling step, which prohibits any pair of particles from overlapping in the coordinate space, effectively modeling the effect of hard-core repulsion. The new procedure -- called the FIST sampler -- is based on the package Thermal-FIST. It is used here to study the effect of excluded volume on cumulants of the (net-)proton number distribution in central collisions of heavy ions in a broad collision energy range in conjunction with exact global conservation of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness. The results are compared with earlier calculations based on analytical approximations, quantifying the accuracy of the latter at different collision energies. An additional advantage of the new method over the analytic approaches is that it offers the flexibility provided by event generators, making it straightforwardly extendable to other observables.

## Key claim

This work incorporates short-range repulsion between particles into the Cooper-Frye hadron sampling procedure via a rejection sampling step that prohibits overlapping particles in coordinate space, but the method relies on several approximations including the use of an auxiliary excluded volume model for sampling total particle numbers and the single-particle rejection (SPR) approximation for computational feasibility.

## Method

The method samples hadron coordinates uniformly from the Cooper-Frye hypersurface and rejects configurations where any pair of particles with hard-core repulsion overlap in coordinate space, after boosting particles into their center-of-mass frame and propagating the earlier-emitted particle to the later emission time. The total numbers of each hadron species are first sampled from an excluded volume hadron resonance gas model using average effective densities from the Cooper-Frye formula, with exact global conservation of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness enforced through rejection sampling. The single-particle rejection (SPR) approximation is employed for large systems, where newly sampled particles are checked for overlap only against already accepted particles rather than rejecting the entire configuration.

## Key numbers

- Baryon excluded volume parameter set to b = 1 fm^3, corresponding to a classical hard-core radius of r_c = 0.39 fm
- At LHC energies (sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb), the scaled density b n_B = b n_antiB ≈ 0.029 at particlization temperature T = 160 MeV
- The method requires about 5-15 GB of RAM for sampling central Au-Au collisions at RHIC-BES using Cooper-Frye hypersurfaces from the MUSIC code
- The hard-sphere and excluded volume model equations of state are very similar at b n ≲ 0.1, where n ≡ N/V
- The SPR approximation produces accurate results for variance of particle number distributions at densities up to b n = 0.5

## Context

This work extends the subensemble sampler method of Vovchenko (2020) for incorporating excluded volume effects into Cooper-Frye sampling, which was restricted to longitudinally boost-invariant systems at LHC energies, to a broader collision energy range including RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies (sqrt(s_NN) = 2.4-2760 GeV). The new FIST sampler enables study of proton cumulants at lower collision energies where boost invariance does not hold, and provides flexibility for extension to other observables beyond the (net-)proton number cumulants that were previously analyzed using analytic approaches by Vovchenko (2021).
