# Analysis of the Q^2-dependence of charged-current quasielastic processes in neutrino-nucleus interactions

> We discuss the observed disagreement between the Q^2 distributions of neutrino-nucleus quasielastic events, measured by a number of recent experiments, and the predictions of Monte Carlo simulations based on the relativistic Fermi gas model. The results of our analysis suggest that these discrepancies are likely to be ascribable to both the breakdown of the impulse approximation and the limitations of the Fermi gas description. Several issues related to the extraction of the Q^2 distributions from the experimental data are also discussed, and new kinematical variables, which would allow for an improved analysis, are proposed.

# Analysis of the Q²-dependence of charged-current quasielastic processes in neutrino-nucleus interactions

## Key claim

The observed discrepancies between measured Q² distributions of neutrino-nucleus quasielastic events and Monte Carlo simulations based on the relativistic Fermi gas model are likely attributable to both the breakdown of the impulse approximation at low momentum transfer and the limitations of the Fermi gas description, rather than solely to Pauli blocking modifications as previously assumed.

## Method

The authors compared event distributions calculated using the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model and a spectral function (SF) approach for carbon targets, analyzing the relationship between true Q² and reconstructed Q² (Q²_rec). They also performed nonrelativistic nuclear matter response calculations using the correlated Hartree-Fock formalism and compared these to plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) results to test the validity regime of the impulse approximation as a function of momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- The maximum of the Q² distribution occurs at Q² = 0.146 GeV², independent of neutrino energy for sufficiently high energies, corresponding to momentum transfer |q|_* = 2p_F with p_F = 220 MeV
- At neutrino energy 0.8 GeV, the phase space for dσ/dQ² begins to shrink above Q² = 0.607 GeV², producing a knee in the cross section
- The knee positions shift when using Q²_rec: at E_ν = 0.8 GeV, the knee appears at Q²_rec = 0.306 GeV² instead of Q² = 0.607 GeV²
- At |q| ~ 600 MeV, the PWIA and correlated Hartree-Fock responses agree within 10% in the region of the maximum
- MiniBooNE reported 193709 events surviving cuts, with about 180000 events in the range 0 ≤ Q²_rec < 1 GeV²; after cuts to extract axial mass, the sample reduced to ~112000 events (62% of total)
- The ratio of true to observed CCQE events in Monte Carlo generators ranges from 82% to 89% for a 1-GeV ν_μ beam on oxygen
- Events misidentified as inelastic due to pion production in final state interactions are typically 0.5% to 3%
- Scattering off correlated nucleon pairs does not exceed 3% of CCQE events in the MiniBooNE kinematical range

## Context

This work extends the analysis of discrepancies first reported by the K2K, MiniBooNE, and SciBooNE collaborations between measured Q² distributions and RFG-based Monte Carlo predictions. The MiniBooNE Collaboration (2008) introduced an ad hoc parameter κ to modify Pauli blocking in the RFG model, which the authors argue lacks physical justification. The study builds on electron scattering studies (Benhar et al., 2008) showing that the impulse approximation breaks down at low momentum transfer, and on nuclear matter response calculations (Benhar and Farina, 2009) using correlated basis functions. The results enable improved data analysis through proposed new kinematical variables (β and φ) that are model-independent and allow cleaner rejection of regions where the impulse approximation fails.
