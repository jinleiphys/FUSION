# Non-local kinetic simulation of heavy ion collisions

> The numerical solutions of nonlocal and local Boltzmann kinetic equations for the simulation of peripheral and central heavy ion reactions are compared. The experimental finding of enhancement of mid-rapidity matter shows the necessity to include nonlocal corrections. While the in-medium cross section changes the number of collisions and not the transferred energy, the nonlocal scenario changes the energy transferred during collisions. The renormalisation of quasiparticle energies by using the Pauli-rejected collisions leads to a further enhancement of mid-rapidity matter distribution and is accompanied by a dynamical softening of the equation of state. The simulation results are parameterised in terms of time dependent thermodynamical variables in the Fermi liquid sense. This allows one to discuss dynamical trajectories in phase space. A combination of thermodynamical observables is constructed which locates instabilities and points of possible phase transition under iso-nothing conditions. Two different mechanisms of instability, a short time surface-dominated instability and later a spinodal-dominated volume instability is found. The latter one occurs only if the incident energies do not exceed significantly the Fermi energy and might be attributed to spinodal decomposition.

# Non-local kinetic simulation of heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

Nonlocal corrections to the Boltzmann (BUU) kinetic equation, combined with quasiparticle renormalization implemented via Pauli-rejected collisions, are necessary to reproduce the experimentally observed enhancement of mid-rapidity matter in heavy ion collisions; the nonlocal scenario changes the energy transferred during collisions (rather than just the number of collisions), and the quasiparticle renormalization produces a dynamical softening of the equation of state.

## Method

The author numerically solves the nonlocal kinetic equation with Enskog-type shifts for the collision integral, derived from the scattering phase shift of the T-matrix, and compares results to standard local BUU simulations. The quasiparticle renormalization is implemented by applying nonlocal shifts to scattering events that are normally rejected by Pauli blocking, effectively treating them as zero-angle collisions. Simulations are performed for peripheral and central heavy ion reactions, and the results are parameterized in terms of time-dependent thermodynamic variables in the Fermi liquid sense.

## Key numbers

- For Ta + Au at Elab/A = 33 MeV and 8 fm impact parameter: the nonlocal scenario increases transverse energy by about 2 MeV and longitudinal energy by about 1 MeV compared to local BUU, while doubling the cross section in local BUU does not change these energies appreciably.
- For Xe + Sn at 25 MeV lab energy: the point of highest compression is reached around 60 fm/c with a temperature of 9 MeV.
- For Xe + Sn at 25 MeV: the nonlocal kinetic equation yields a temperature about 2 MeV higher than the local BUU result during the expansion phase.
- For Ta + Au at Elab/A = 33 MeV and 8 fm impact parameter: the neck formation persists between 200-240 fm/c in the nonlocal scenario, while BUU breaks apart already at 200 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the nonlocal kinetic theory developed by the author and collaborators (Morawetz et al., 1996-2001) and applies it to heavy ion collisions, building on earlier BUU and Landau equation approaches (Prakash and Ruderman, 1987; Danielewicz et al., 1990; Dorso et al., 1991; Kiderlen and Hofmann, 1994; Ayik et al., 1995). The results are compared to experimental data from the INDRA collaboration (Bocquet, 2000; Plagnol, 2000; Gagnon, 1998; Lukasik, 2000) for Ta + Au collisions at 33 MeV/nucleon. The work enables identification of two distinct instability mechanisms in heavy ion reactions: a short-time surface-dominated instability and a later spinodal-dominated volume instability, with the latter occurring only when incident energies do not significantly exceed the Fermi energy.
