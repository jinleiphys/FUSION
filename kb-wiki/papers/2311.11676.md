# Extracting neutron skin from elastic proton-nucleus scattering with deep neural network

> Based on the relativistic impulse approximation of proton-nucleus elastic scattering theory, the nucleon density distribution and neutron skin thickness of $^{48}$Ca are estimated via the deep learning method. The neural-network-generated densities are mainly compressed to be lower inside the nucleus compared with the results from the relativistic PC-PK1 density functional, resulting in a significant improvement on the large-angle scattering observables, both for the differential cross section and analyzing power. The neutron skin thickness of $^{48}$Ca is captured to be 0.211(11) fm. The relatively thicker neutron skin is deemed reasonable from the perspective of density functional analysis.

## Key claim

The neutron skin thickness of $^{48}$Ca is extracted as 0.211(11) fm using a deep neural network trained on pseudodensities from 16 energy density functionals, with the result deemed reasonable from density functional analysis but falling above most other theoretical and experimental values except the dispersion optical model.

## Method

The authors constructed a five-cell feedforward neural network (observable-to-density network, OTDN) that maps experimental observables (differential cross section and analyzing power) to nuclear density distributions. They generated 600 pseudodensities for $^{48}$Ca by randomly combining densities from 16 energy density functionals (relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov with DD-ME2, PC-PK1, DD-PC1; relativistic mean field with PK1; and 12 Skyrme parameterizations under Skyrme-Hartree-Fock), then computed corresponding observables using the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) with an optimized $NN$ $t$-matrix at 800 MeV. After training, experimental data were fed into OTDN, and Bayesian model averaging with the experimentally known proton radius as prior was used to obtain final weighted densities and neutron skin thickness.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness of $^{48}$Ca: 0.211(11) fm
- Neutron radius of $^{48}$Ca: 3.604(6) fm
- Proton radius of $^{48}$Ca: 3.393(5) fm
- 600 pseudodensities generated from 16 energy density functionals for training
- Incident proton energy: 800 MeV
- Radial grid: 160 points with 0.1 fm spacing, up to 16 fm
- Linear coefficients for scalar-vector density relation: $a_p = 0.961$, $a_n = 0.959$

## Context

This work extends the relativistic impulse approximation approach previously applied to $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb by Zenihiro (2018) and others, and improves upon the two-parameter Fermi model and single-particle approximations used in earlier RIA studies. The result (0.211 fm) exceeds the CREX collaboration measurement (2022) and the high-resolution E1 polarizability experiment (2017), but falls within the range of 48 energy density functionals. The method enables model-independent density extraction for spherical nuclei and can be applied to $^{208}$Pb, though the RIA approximation neglects multiple collisions which may affect heavy nuclei.
