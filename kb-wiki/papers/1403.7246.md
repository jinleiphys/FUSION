# Observation of Efimov Resonances in a Mixture with Extreme Mass Imbalance

> We observe two consecutive heteronuclear Efimov resonances in an ultracold Li-Cs mixture by measuring three-body loss coefficients as a function of magnetic field near a Feshbach resonance. The first resonance is detected at a scattering length of $a_-^{(1)}=-320(10)~a_0$ corresponding to $\sim 7 $ ($\sim 3$) times the Li-Cs (Cs-Cs) van der Waals range. The second resonance appears at $5.8(1.0) a_-^{(1)}$ close to the unitarity-limited regime at the sample temperature of 450 nK. Indication of a third resonance is found in the atom loss spectra. The scaling of the resonance positions is close to the universal scaling value of 4.9 predicted for zero temperature. Deviations from universality might be caused by finite-range and temperature effects, as well as magnetic field dependent Cs-Cs interactions.

# Observation of Efimov Resonances in a Mixture with Extreme Mass Imbalance

## Key claim

Two consecutive heteronuclear Efimov resonances are observed in a ultracold Li-Cs mixture via three-body loss measurements, with a scaling ratio of 5.8(1.0) that is close to but deviates from the universal zero-temperature prediction of 4.9; deviations may arise from finite-range effects, temperature effects, and magnetic-field-dependent Cs-Cs interactions, and a third resonance is indicated but cannot be unambiguously confirmed due to the unitarity-limited regime.

## Method

The three-body loss coefficient L3 for Li+Cs+Cs collisions was extracted from time-dependent atom loss measurements of Cs atoms in a crossed optical dipole trap at approximately 450 nK, as a function of magnetic field near the broad Li-Cs Feshbach resonance at 843 G. The loss curves were fitted to a rate equation including Li+Cs+Cs and Cs+Cs+Cs three-body losses, with the Li+Cs+Cs coefficient as the sole free parameter, while background and Cs-only losses were independently determined from single-species measurements. The magnetic-field-dependent scattering length was calibrated via radio-frequency spectroscopy of universal dimers, yielding the Feshbach resonance pole at 842.9(2) G with width 61.4(7) G.

## Key numbers

- First Efimov resonance at scattering length a_-^(0) = -320(10) a0, corresponding to approximately 7 times the Li-Cs van der Waals range (45 a0) and approximately 3 times the Cs-Cs van der Waals range (101 a0)
- Second Efimov resonance at a_-^(1) = -1871(388) a0, with ratio a_-^(1)/a_-^(0) = 5.8(1.0)
- Resonance positions in magnetic field: B0 = 849.12(6)_stat(3)_sys G and B1 = 843.89(1)_stat(3)_sys G from atom loss data; B0 = 848.90(6)_stat(3)_sys G and B1 = 843.85(1)_stat(3)_sys G from three-body loss coefficient fits
- Feshbach resonance pole at B_FR = 842.9(2) G with width Delta B = 61.4(7) G
- Sample temperature approximately 450 nK; trap frequencies for Cs: 114 Hz x 123 Hz x 11 Hz; for Li: 275 Hz x 308 Hz x 33 Hz
- Third loss feature indicated at B2 = 843.03(5)_stat(3)_sys G, with assigned scattering length a_-^(2) = -13.5(5.2)_stat(3.1)_sys x 10^3 a0, but with systematic error on the order of a_-^(2) itself

## Context

This work extends the study of Efimov resonances from homonuclear systems (Kraemer 2006, Gross 2009, Ottenstein 2008, Pollack 2009, Wild 2012, Huckans 2009, Zaccanti 2009, Berninger 2011, Roy 2013) and the only previously studied heteronuclear system (K-Rb mixtures, Barontini 2009, Bloom 2013) to the Li-Cs mixture with extreme mass imbalance (mass ratio 133/6), where the predicted universal scaling factor of 4.9 (Braaten 2006, DIncao 2006) is much smaller than the homonuclear factor of 22.7 or the K-Rb factor of approximately 131, enabling the observation of multiple consecutive resonances within the experimentally accessible scattering length range. The results enable future studies of finite-size effects on Efimov trimers (Portegies 2011), as the doubly excited trimer size of approximately 0.2 micrometers is comparable to the oscillator length of the trapping potential, and motivate lower-temperature experiments (approximately 30 nK) to push the unitarity limit to larger scattering lengths and resolve the third resonance unambiguously.
