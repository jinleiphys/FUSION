# Cheshire Cat Hadrons

> This article is based on a series of lectures given at ELAF 93 on the description of low-energy hadronic systems {\it in and out} of hadronic medium. The focus is put on identifying, with the help of a Cheshire Cat philosophy, the effective degrees of freedom relevant for the strong interactions from a certain number of generic symmetry properties of QCD. The matters treated are the ground-state and excited-state properties of light- and heavy-quark baryons and applications to nuclei and nuclear matter under normal as well as extreme conditions.

## Key claim

The Cheshire Cat Principle (CCP), which holds exactly in (1+1) dimensions, can be applied approximately in (3+1) dimensions to show that low-energy hadronic properties depend only on generic symmetry features of QCD, not on the detailed dynamics of the chiral bag model used to derive them.

## Method

The author constructs a chiral bag model in (1+1) dimensions with a massless fermion confined inside a bag coupled to a massless boson outside, then demonstrates that quantum anomalies cause the vector current to fail conservation, leading to fermion number leakage that must be carried by a soliton in the pion field. This exact bosonization in two dimensions is extended to (3+1) dimensions by constructing a partially bosonized chiral bag model with SU(3)×SU(3)×U_A(1) flavor symmetry, using boundary conditions and anomaly arguments to motivate effective chiral Lagrangians for hadrons.

## Key numbers

- The boson decay constant in (1+1) dimensions is fixed as f = 1/√(4π)
- The scalar mass in the Schwinger model is derived as m_φ² = e²/π
- The Wess-Zumino-Witten term coefficient is quantized as 2πn/(24π²) with integer n corresponding to the number of colors

## Context

This work extends the "little bag" model proposed by Brown and Rho (1979), in which quarks and gluons are confined in a small bag surrounded by a large pion cloud, to a more modern Cheshire Cat formulation. The approach builds on the effective action framework of Polchinski and Weinberg (1993) and the bosonization results of Coleman and Mandelstam, showing that topological quantities like baryon charge satisfy an exact CCP in (3+1) dimensions while nontopological observables satisfy it approximately. The results enable the description of both light and heavy baryons, as well as nuclear matter under normal and extreme conditions, using effective chiral Lagrangians motivated by QCD symmetries rather than detailed model dynamics.
