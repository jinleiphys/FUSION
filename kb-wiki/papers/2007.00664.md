# Precision tests of fundamental physics with $η$ and $η^\prime$ mesons

> Decays of the neutral and long-lived $η$ and $η'$ mesons provide a unique, flavor-conserving laboratory to test low-energy Quantum Chromodynamics and search for new physics beyond the Standard Model. They have drawn world-wide attention in recent years and have inspired broad experimental programs in different high-intensity-frontier centers. New experimental data will offer critical inputs to precisely determine the light quark mass ratios, $η$-$η'$ mixing parameters, and hadronic contributions to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon. At the same time, it will provide a sensitive probe to test potential new physics. This includes searches for hidden photons, light Higgs scalars, and axion-like particles that are complementary to worldwide efforts to detect new light particles below the GeV mass scale, as well as tests of discrete symmetry violation. In this review, we give an update on theoretical developments, discuss the experimental opportunities, and identify future research needed in this field.

## Key claim

Decays of $\eta$ and $\eta^\prime$ mesons provide a unique, flavor-conserving laboratory for testing low-energy Quantum Chromodynamics and searching for new physics beyond the Standard Model, but the precision of these tests depends critically on the interplay between theoretical developments (chiral perturbation theory, dispersion relations) and upcoming experimental data from high-intensity facilities.

## Method

The paper presents a comprehensive review of theoretical frameworks, including chiral perturbation theory, dispersion relations, and large-$N_c$ expansions, applied to $\eta$ and $\eta^\prime$ decays, alongside experimental opportunities at facilities such as KLOE, BESIII, and the proposed JLab Eta Factory. It systematically surveys decay channels (two-photon, three-pion, radiative, dilepton, and multi-body modes) and their sensitivity to Standard Model parameters (light quark mass ratios, $\eta$–$\eta^\prime$ mixing, transition form factors) and beyond-Standard-Model signals (dark photons, axion-like particles, light Higgs scalars, discrete symmetry violation).

## Key numbers

- $\eta$ total width: $\Gamma_{\eta} = 1.31(5)\ \text{keV}$ (PDG)
- $\eta^\prime$ total width: $\Gamma_{\eta^\prime} = 196(9)\ \text{MeV}$ (PDG fit value)
- $\eta \to 2\gamma$ branching ratio: $39.41(20)\%$
- $\eta^\prime \to 2\gamma$ branching ratio: $2.331(37)\%$ (BESIII)
- $\eta \to 3\pi^0$ branching ratio: $32.68(23)\%$
- $\eta \to \pi^+\pi^-\pi^0$ branching ratio: $22.92(28)\%$
- $\eta^\prime \to \eta\pi^+\pi^-$ branching ratio: $42.6(7)\%$
- $\eta^\prime \to \pi^+\pi^-\gamma$ branching ratio: $28.9(5)\%$
- $\eta \to \pi^+\pi^-\gamma$ branching ratio: $4.22(8)\%$
- $\eta \to \pi^0\gamma\gamma$ branching ratio: $2.56(22)\times 10^{-4}$
- $\eta \to \mu^+\mu^-$ branching ratio: $5.8(8)\times 10^{-6}$
- $\eta \to e^+e^-$ branching ratio: $< 7\times 10^{-7}$ (upper limit)
- $\eta \to \pi^+\pi^-$ branching ratio: $< 4.4\times 10^{-6}$ (upper limit, Babusci 2020)
- $\eta^\prime \to \omega\gamma$ branching ratio: $2.489(76)\%$ (BESIII)
- $\eta^\prime \to \pi^+\pi^-\pi^0$ branching ratio: $3.61(17)\times 10^{-3}$
- Projected JLab Eta Factory sensitivity to baryonic fine structure constant: $10^{-7}$ in $B$ boson mass region $0.14$–$0.55\ \text{GeV}$

## Context

This review updates and extends earlier theoretical and experimental work on $\eta$ and $\eta^\prime$ physics, including the chiral perturbation theory framework developed by Gell-Mann, Oakes, and Renner (1968), the Wess–Zumino–Witten anomaly (1971, 1983), and the discovery of the $\eta$ meson by Pevsner et al. (1961) and $\eta^\prime$ by Kalbfleisch et al. (1964) and Goldberg et al. (1964). It connects to ongoing efforts to resolve the $4.2$ standard deviation discrepancy in the muon anomalous magnetic moment $(g-2)_\mu$ between the Standard Model calculation (Aoyama 2020) and measurements at Brookhaven (Bennett 2006) and Fermilab (Abi 2021, Albahri 2021), and enables future searches for light dark matter candidates and discrete symmetry violation complementary to electric dipole moment experiments.
