# On Mesoatoms of the Deuterium and Possible Existence of Exotic Dibaryons

> As a desirable supplement to the reaction $pp \to pp2γ$, proposed earlier (nucl-th/9712064 and references therein) to probe for the NN--decoupled dibaryon resonances we suggest to use for the same goal the radiative capture processes in mesoatoms of the deuterium which we consider to be especially feasible for further test and invesigation of possible low-lying exotic resonance states.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that radiative pion (or muon) capture processes in deuterium mesoatoms can serve as a feasible experimental probe for low-lying exotic dibaryon resonances that are decoupled from the nucleon-nucleon (NN) decay channel, supplementing earlier proposals using the $pp \to pp2\gamma$ reaction. The author notes that the estimated branching ratio for exciting the $d_1(1920)$ resonance via this process is close to the current experimental upper bound for undetected channels, making it potentially observable.

## Method

The author estimates the branching ratio for the radiative capture process $(\pi^{-}d)_{atom} \to \gamma d_1$ using a phenomenological two-step model where the pion is radiatively captured by a nucleon to form a virtual $\Delta$ resonance, which then associates with a spectator nucleon to form the $d_1(1920)$ dibaryon. The calculation uses standard formulas for capture from atomic 1S states, a Hulthen-type radial wave function for the $d_1\Delta N$ vertex with parameters analogous to the deuteron wave function, and the measured total width of the 1S level of pionic deuterium. The author also estimates the non-resonant two-gamma background using known branching ratios from pionic hydrogen.

## Key numbers

- Branching ratio for the resonance channel: $BR((\pi^{-}p)_{atom} \rightarrow \gamma d_1 \to \gamma \gamma X)_{1S-state} = 0.6\%$
- Estimated resonance excitation probability: $BR((\pi^{-}d)_{atom} \to \gamma d_1) \sim \alpha_{em} BR((\pi^{-}d)_{atom} \to nn) \simeq 0.74/137 \simeq 0.5\%$
- Non-resonant two-gamma background: $BR((\pi^{-}d)_{atom} \to \gamma \gamma X)_{nonres} \simeq 3.4 \times 10^{-5}$
- Ratio for pionic hydrogen: $BR(\pi^{-}p \to 2\gamma/1\gamma) \simeq 1.3 \times 10^{-4}$
- Measured branching ratios for $\pi^{-}d$ capture: $\pi^{-}d \to nn$ at $0.7375 \pm 0.0027$, $\pi^{-}d \to nn\gamma$ at $0.2606 \pm 0.0027$, $\pi^{-}d \to nne^{+}e^{-}$ at $(1.81 \pm 0.02)\times 10^{-3}$, $\pi^{-}d \to nn\pi^{0}$ at $(1.45 \pm 0.09)\times 10^{-4}$
- Upper bound of undetected channels: $\simeq 0.38\%$
- Radiated photon energy for $d_1(1920)$ excitation: $\omega = 92.9$ MeV
- Estimated free-space total width of $d_1$: $\Gamma^{free}_{tot}(d_1)=\Gamma_{rad}(d_1) \simeq 0.5$ KeV
- Estimated in-medium total width enhancement: $\Gamma^{matter}_{tot}(d_1)$ enhanced by 3 orders of magnitude compared to $\Gamma^{free}_{tot}$, leading to $BR(\pi^{-}A \to 2\gamma X)$ of order $\le 10^{-5}$

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier proposal for using the $pp \to pp2\gamma$ reaction (nucl-th/9712064) to search for NN-decoupled dibaryons, now suggesting radiative capture in pionic deuterium as an alternative probe. The paper references prior experimental indications of a narrow $d^{\prime}$ dibaryon slightly above the $\pi NN$ threshold from pion double charge exchange data, and a $d_1$ enhancement below the $\pi NN$ threshold seen in preliminary proton-proton double bremsstrahlung data at 200 MeV. The proposal builds on a previous TRIUMF experiment searching for an exotic isotensor dibaryon with isospin 2, and the author notes that the transition operator in the proposed model transforms as an isovector, meaning the narrow dibaryon would not be excited in radiative pion capture on the isosinglet deuteron if its isospin equals 2, providing a way to discriminate between isospin assignments.
