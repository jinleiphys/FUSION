# Infinite matter properties and zero-range limit of nonrelativistic finite-range interactions

> We discuss some infinite matter properties of two finite-range interactions widely used for nuclear structure calculations, namely Gogny and M3Y interactions. We show that some useful informations can be deduced for the central, tensor and spin-orbit terms from the spin-isospin channels and the partial wave decomposition of the symmetric nuclear matter equation of state. We show in particular that the central part of the Gogny interaction should benefit from the introduction of a third Gaussian and the tensor parameters of both interactions can be deduced from special combinations of partial waves. We also discuss the fact that the spin-orbit of the M3Y interaction is not compatible with local gauge invariance. Finally, we show that the zero-range limit of both families of interactions coincides with the specific form of the zero-range N3LO Skyrme interaction and we emphasize from this analogy the benefits of N3LO.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the central part of the Gogny interaction, which currently uses two Gaussian functions, would benefit from the introduction of a third Gaussian to better reproduce the spin-isospin channel decomposition of the symmetric nuclear matter equation of state obtained from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations; this improvement is demonstrated by fitting the three-Gaussian version to infinite matter properties, but the authors note that a global fit incorporating finite nuclei is needed before a definitive conclusion can be drawn.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations of symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) and pure neutron matter (PNM) using several parametrizations of the finite-range Gogny and M3Y interactions, decomposing the energy per particle into spin-isospin (S,T) channels and partial waves up to L=3 (S, P, D, F waves). They compared these results with BHF calculations from Baldo (1997) and used specific combinations of partial waves (e.g., delta_P, delta_D, delta_F) to isolate the tensor and spin-orbit contributions. They also derived the zero-range limit of both interaction families by expanding their momentum-space matrix elements and showed that this limit coincides with the N3LO Skyrme pseudo-potential.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density for all parametrizations is around rho_0, with sizeable differences in the EoS appearing only at about two times saturation density and beyond.
- The BHF results are considered reliable up to rho approximately 0.4 fm^{-3} based on constraints from heavy ion collision experiments (Danielewicz 2002, Lynch 2009).
- For the three-Gaussian Gogny test, the three fixed ranges were mu^C_1 = 0.25 fm, mu^C_2 = 0.8 fm, mu^C_3 = 1.2 fm.
- Including only the first three partial waves (S, P, D) reproduces the exact EoS within a few percent up to about four times saturation density for the D1S Gogny interaction.
- The M3Y-P7 parametrization follows BHF results for PNM closely because those data enter explicitly in its fitting protocol.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of effective nuclear interactions by comparing finite-range Gogny and M3Y families with microscopic BHF calculations from Baldo (1997) and with chiral effective field theory and many-body perturbation theory results. It builds on prior studies of tensor terms in effective interactions by Otsuka (2006), Lesinski (2007), and Anguiano (2011), and on the N3LO Skyrme pseudo-potential developed by Carlsson (2008) and Raimondi (2011). The results enable future development of improved Gogny parametrizations with a third Gaussian for astrophysical calculations, and establish a formal link between finite-range interactions and the N3LO zero-range Skyrme interaction.
