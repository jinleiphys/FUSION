# Hypernuclear cluster states of $_Λ^{12}\rm{B}$ Unveiled through Neural Network-Driven Microscopic Calculation

> We investigate the hypernuclear cluster states of $_Λ^{12}\mathrm{B}$ using a neural-network-driven microscopic model. We extend the Control Neural Networks (Ctrl.NN) method and systematically calculate the positive-parity spectrum of $_Λ^{12}\mathrm{B}$. By incorporating $sd$-shell excitations and parity-coupling effects into the $_Λ^{12}\mathrm{B}$ hypernuclear system, we reveal structural changes, including clustering effects and new configurations such as isosceles-triangle and $α$-$t$-$α$ linear-chain structures. Furthermore, by comparing with experimental data, we identify that many peaks ($\#$6 and $\#$8) can be interpreted as $p_Λ$ dominant states, which is consistent with shell-model predictions. Notably, based on our analysis of the excited states of $_Λ^{12}\mathrm{B}$, we propose possible candidates for previously unexplained or controversial experimental peaks.

## Key claim

By incorporating $sd$-shell excitations and parity-coupling effects into a neural-network-driven microscopic cluster model, the authors identify that many experimental peaks (##6 and ##8) in $_\Lambda^{12}\rm{B}$ can be interpreted as $p_\Lambda$ dominant states, consistent with shell-model predictions, while also proposing candidates for previously unexplained peaks (##4, ##7) and the controversial peak ##5 based on $s_\Lambda$ dominant states and clustering structures.

## Method

The authors employ a microscopic $2\alpha+t+\Lambda$ four-body cluster model using Hyper-Brink wave functions optimized through the Control Neural Network (Ctrl.NN) method with $K$-projection and parity projection. They adopt Volkov No.2 nucleon-nucleon central force with Majorana parameter $M=0.598$, G3RS spin-orbit interaction at 1600 MeV, and the ESC14 $\Lambda N$ interaction with many-body effect tuned to reproduce the $^{7}_\Lambda\rm{Li}$ spin splitting, using a Fermi momentum parameter $k_F=1.06$ fm$^{-1}$.

## Key numbers

- The $\Lambda$ binding energy $B_\Lambda$ of the ground state is reproduced as 11.53 MeV using $k_F=1.06$ fm$^{-1}$.
- The $\Lambda$ negative-parity component of the ground state is 3%; $s_\Lambda$ dominant states show around 10% negative-parity component, while $p_\Lambda$ dominant states show around 85%.
- The excitation energies of core nucleus $^{11}\rm{B}$ states are reproduced: e.g., $5/2^+_1$ at 6.44 MeV (experiment 7.28 MeV), $1/2^+_1$ at 6.24 MeV (experiment 6.79 MeV), $3/2^+_1$ at 7.61 MeV (experiment 7.98 MeV).
- The $\Lambda$ hyperon is excited to the $p$ orbit around an excitation energy region of 12 MeV, with further structural evolution around $E_x = 15$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic cluster model approach previously applied to $^{11}\rm{B}$ by Zhou (2018) and the HyperAMD framework of Isaka (2020) to the $_\Lambda^{12}\rm{B}$ hypernucleus. It addresses unexplained experimental peaks ##4 and ##7 from the JLab $(e, e'K)$ measurements by Iodice (2007) and Tang (2014), which were not accounted for by the 0$\hbar\omega$ shell-model calculation. The results enable future theoretical cross-section predictions to clarify the excitation mode of peak ##5 and guide experimental exploration of hypernuclear cluster states with linear-chain and isosceles-triangle configurations.
