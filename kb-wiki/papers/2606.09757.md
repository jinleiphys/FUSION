# Partial Pressure Contributions of Hadron Families to the QCD Equation of State

> Lattice simulations provide the thermodynamics of quantum chromodynamics (QCD) as a function of the temperature, at zero-to-moderate values of the baryonic chemical potential. However, the contribution of single hadronic species cannot be directly isolated from lattice calculations. In this work, we find linear combinations of up to fourth order susceptibilities which isolate the contribution of hadrons to the QCD pressure according to their baryon number $B$, electric charge $Q$ and strangeness $S$ content. These combinations are valid, provided that the thermodynamics of a strongly-interacting gas in the low-temperature regime can be modeled as a gas of non-interacting hadrons and their resonances. Finally, we test the validity of these linear combinations in the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model and compare them to available lattice QCD results, using continuum-estimated susceptibilities.

## Key claim

The paper derives linear combinations of up to fourth-order conserved charge susceptibilities that isolate the contribution of hadrons to the QCD pressure grouped by baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge, but these combinations are valid only under the assumption that the low-temperature regime can be modeled as a non-interacting gas of hadrons and resonances (the Boltzmann approximation of the Hadron Resonance Gas model).

## Method

The authors start from the HRG model partition function in the Boltzmann approximation, where the pressure decomposes into 13 partial pressures corresponding to hadron families with specific (B, S, Q) quantum numbers. They then apply the definition of second- and fourth-order susceptibilities to this pressure representation, obtaining an overdetermined linear system of 21 equations relating susceptibilities to the 13 partial pressures. For each partial pressure, they select a specific linear combination of susceptibilities that minimizes the uncertainty from lattice QCD results, using continuum-estimated susceptibilities from state-of-the-art lattice calculations merged with HRG data at low temperature.

## Key numbers

- 13 partial pressures are isolated, corresponding to hadron families grouped by baryon number B, strangeness S, and electric charge Q
- 21 susceptibilities (second- and fourth-order BSQ combinations) are used to construct the linear system
- The Boltzmann approximation fails for two susceptibilities: χ²_Q and χ⁴_Q, due to the small pion mass m_π limiting the approximation criterion (m_i - μ) ≥ T
- The partial pressure P_{131} (Ω hyperon family) clearly overestimates the HRG result, attributed to incomplete resonance spectroscopy for this species
- The partial pressure P_{121} (Ξ hyperon family) shows a similar but less pronounced discrepancy

## Context

This work extends the approach of Bazavov (2013) and Alba (2017) by enabling separation of partial pressures by electric charge in addition to baryon number and strangeness, allowing distinction between proton and neutron families and their resonances. The results use continuum-estimated susceptibilities from Abuali (2025) and Jahan (2025), providing a quantitative benchmark for hadronic model builders to constrain their interaction parameters. The code has been integrated into the MUSES calculation engine (Jahan 2026), enabling future extensions to include charm quantum number when full BSQC susceptibilities become available.
