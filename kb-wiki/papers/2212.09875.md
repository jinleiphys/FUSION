# g-mode Oscillations in Neutron Stars with Hyperons

> A common alternative to the standard assumption of nucleonic composition of matter in the interior of a neutron star is to include strange baryons, particularly hyperons. Any change in composition of the neutron star core has an effect on g-mode oscillations of neutron stars, through the compositional dependence of the equilibrium and adiabatic sound speeds. We study the core g-modes of a neutron star contaning hyperons, using a variety of relativistic mean field models of dense matter that satisfy observational constraints on global properties of neutron stars. Our selected models predict a sharp rise in the g-mode frequencies upon the onset of strange baryons. Should g-modes be observed in the near future, their frequency could be used to test the presence of hyperonic matter in the core of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the onset of hyperons in neutron star cores causes a sharp rise in g-mode oscillation frequencies, and that if g-modes are observed in the future, their frequency could serve as a test for the presence of hyperonic matter, though the authors qualify that this conclusion depends on the specific relativistic mean field model used and that the Cowling approximation (which neglects metric perturbations) affects frequencies at the 5-10% level.

## Method

The authors calculated core g-mode oscillations (n=1, l=2) for neutron stars containing hyperons using six different relativistic mean field models (GM1-Y5, Big Apple, Hornick 65, Hornick 70, DD-MEX, and DD-ME2) that satisfy current observational constraints on neutron star mass and radius. They computed the Brunt-Vaisala frequency from the difference between adiabatic and equilibrium sound speeds, using a generalized expression for the sound speed difference that accounts for multiple baryon species, and solved the linear perturbation equations in the Cowling approximation to obtain the g-mode eigenfrequencies.

## Key numbers

- The heaviest neutron star to date (PSR J0952-0607) has a mass of 2.35_{-0.17}^{+0.17} solar masses
- NICER observations of PSR J0740+6620 yield a mass of 2.08_{-0.07}^{+0.08} solar masses with equatorial radius of 13.7_{-1.5}^{+2.6} km (Miller 2021) and 2.072_{-0.066}^{+0.067} solar masses with radius 12.39_{-0.98}^{+1.30} km (Riley 2021)
- The tidal deformability of a canonical 1.4 solar mass neutron star from GW190814 analysis is 616_{-158}^{+273}
- The Cowling approximation affects g-mode frequencies at the 5-10% level compared to full general relativistic calculations

## Context

This work extends previous studies of g-modes in nucleonic matter (npe and npe-mu matter) by Wei (2018) and in hybrid stars with quark-hadron phase transitions by Jaikumar (2021) to include hyperonic compositions (npe-mu-Y). The authors note that similar to quark matter, the onset of hyperons leads to a dramatic increase in g-mode oscillation frequency, attributed to the softening of the equation of state when strange degrees of freedom appear. The results enable future observational tests of hyperonic matter in neutron star cores if g-modes are detected by current-generation gravitational wave detectors.
