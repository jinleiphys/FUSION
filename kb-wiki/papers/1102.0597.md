# Results from the N* program at JLab

> We discuss the results on the fundamental degrees of freedom underlying the nucleon excitation spectrum and how they evolve as the resonance transitions are investigated with increasingly better space-time resolution of the electromagnetic probe. The search for undiscovered but predicted states continues to be pursued with a vigorous experimental program. While recent data from JLab and elsewhere provide intriguing hints of new states, final conclusions will have to wait for the results of the broad experimental effort currently underway with CLAS, and subsequent analyses involving the EBAC at JLab.

## Key claim

The nucleon excitation spectrum reveals the effective degrees of freedom of low-energy QCD, but final conclusions on undiscovered predicted states require the results of the broad experimental effort currently underway with CLAS and subsequent analyses involving the EBAC at JLab, as recent data provide only intriguing hints rather than definitive evidence.

## Method

The CLAS detector at Jefferson Lab measured photoproduction and electroproduction of single and double pions, eta mesons, and kaons on proton and neutron targets across a wide kinematic range. The data were analyzed using two complementary theoretical approaches: fixed-t dispersion relations (DR) and the Unitary Isobar Model (UIM), with model uncertainties evaluated and added in quadrature. For two-pion electroproduction, a reaction model was developed to isolate resonant contributions from the non-resonant background.

## Key numbers

- Photocoupling of the N(1720)P$_{13}$ state changed significantly with new precise CLAS data, entered into the 2008 edition of the Review of Particle Physics
- The $\gamma^* p \rightarrow \Delta$(1232)P$_{33}$ transition shows no sign of approaching the pQCD regime for $Q^2 < 7$ GeV$^2$
- The N(1520)D$_{13}$ shows helicity-3/2 dominance at the real photon point changing to helicity-1/2 dominance at $Q^2 > 0.5$ GeV$^2$
- The N(1535)S$_{11}$ branching ratios determined from combined $\pi$ and $\eta$ data: $\beta_{\pi N} = 0.485 \pm 0.008 \pm 0.023$ and $\beta_{\eta N} = 0.460 \pm 0.008 \pm 0.022$, to enter the 2010 edition of RPP
- The ratio of branching fractions $\beta_{\eta N} / \beta_{\pi N} = 0.95 \pm 0.03$ for N(1535)S$_{11}$ from fit to amplitudes at $0 \leq Q^2 < 4.5$ GeV$^2$
- CLAS data on single pion electroproduction include approximately 119,000 data points of differential cross sections, longitudinally polarized beam asymmetries, and longitudinal target and beam-target asymmetries
- The $\gamma^* p \rightarrow \Delta$(1232)P$_{33}$ magnetic-dipole form factor shows pion-cloud contribution makes up more than 30% of $G_M^*(Q^2)$ at the photon point
- Stringent upper limits on $\Theta^+_5(1540)$ production cross sections placed in several channels including $\gamma p \rightarrow \bar{K^0} K^0$, $\bar{K^0} K^+ n$, $\gamma n \rightarrow K^- K^+ n$, $\gamma D \rightarrow \Lambda n K^+$, $\gamma p \rightarrow K^+ K^- p$
- The $\Lambda$ hyperon from $\vec{\gamma}+p\rightarrow K^+ +\vec{\Lambda}$ is 100% polarized, with $R^2 \equiv P^2 + C_x^2 + C_z^2$ consistent with 1 throughout the measured region
- CLAS data revealed structure in fully integrated $\pi^+\pi^-p$ electroproduction cross sections at $W \approx 1.7$ GeV, offering evidence for candidate state $3/2^+(1720)$

## Context

This paper reviews results from the JLab N* program using the CLAS detector, extending prior work on nucleon resonance electroexcitation by providing comprehensive data on transition form factors for the four lowest excited states: $\Delta$(1232)P$_{33}$, N(1440)P$_{11}$, N(1520)D$_{13}$, and N(1535)S$_{11}$. The results contradict the quark-diquark model predictions regarding the N(1900)P$_{13}$ state (Santo, 2005) and rule out the interpretation of the Roper resonance as a gluonic baryon excitation (Li, 1992; Li, 1997), instead supporting its description as the first radial excitation of the 3-quark ground state in light-front relativistic quark models (Capstick, 1992; Aznauryan, 2007). The dynamical coupled-channels analysis by the Excited Baryon Analysis Center (EBAC) at JLab, combined with lattice QCD calculations (Alexandrou, 2008), enables future extraction of $\gamma^*NN^*$ electrocouplings from combined single and double pion electroproduction data.
