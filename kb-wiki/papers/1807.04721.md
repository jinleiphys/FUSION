# Hybrid model with dynamical sources for heavy-ion collisions at BES energies

> We develop a (3+1)-dimensional hybrid evolution model for heavy-ion collisions with dynamical sources for the energy-momentum tensor and baryon current. During an initial pre-equilibrium stage based on UrQMD, the four-momenta and baryon numbers carried by secondary particles created within UrQMD are fed continuously, after a short thermalization time, into a (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic evolution module including baryon transport. The sensitivity of the initial conditions to model parameters and the effect of baryon diffusion on the hydrodynamic evolution are studied.

## Key claim

The paper develops a (3+1)-dimensional hybrid model for heavy-ion collisions where the pre-equilibrium stage from UrQMD feeds energy, momentum, and baryon number continuously into a viscous hydrodynamic module, rather than initializing hydrodynamics on a single constant-time surface. The authors emphasize that the resulting initial conditions are sensitive to the formation time parameter and that the space-time rapidity distribution of net baryons differs significantly from the rapidity distribution, unlike in prior approaches.

## Method

The pre-equilibrium stage uses UrQMD to simulate primary nucleon collisions, producing secondary particles via string and resonance decays. These particles free-stream for a formation time τ_form (set to 0.2 fm/c) in their rest frame, after which they are converted into fluid via source terms in the conservation equations for energy-momentum and net baryon current. The hydrodynamic module solves (3+1)-dimensional dissipative hydrodynamics with relaxation equations for bulk viscous pressure, shear stress, and baryon diffusion current, using the gradient of μ_B/T as the driving force for baryon diffusion.

## Key numbers

- Formation time τ_form = 0.2 fm/c used for the temporal switching function that converts particles to fluid
- Temporal smearing parameter Δτ_th = 0.5 fm/c used to avoid large dissipative effects from temporal gradients of source terms
- Two models for the baryon diffusion coefficient κ_B are compared: kinetic theory with C_B = 0.4 (lower half of typically explored range 0.4-1.0) and a holographic model
- At √s = 19.6 A GeV, the net baryon rapidity and space-time rapidity distributions both show a single peak around midrapidity, with the η_s distribution width depending strongly on τ_form
- At t = 10.5 fm/c, the evolved net baryon density distributions from kinetic theory (C_B = 0.4) and holographic models for κ_B agree almost perfectly

## Context

This work extends the dynamical initialization approach of Shen (2017) and Denicol (2018) by using UrQMD as the pre-equilibrium generator instead of a string-fragmentation model, and by including dissipative hydrodynamics with baryon diffusion (unlike Akamatsu (2018) which used JAM without dissipation). The model enables studies of how different initial net-baryon space-time rapidity distributions affect the final baryon momentum distributions through baryon diffusion, with results for Beam Energy Scan energies to be reported in future work.
