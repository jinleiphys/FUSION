# Light scalar mesons in $J / psi ->$NN$meson meson$ decays in a chiral unitary approach

> We study the four-body decays $J/ψ\to N {\bar N}$ {\it meson meson} using a chiral unitary approach to account for the meson meson final state interaction (FSI). The calculation of the $J/ψ\to N \bar N π^+ π^-$ process properly reproduces the experimental data after taking the FSI of mesons and the contribution of intermediate $ρ$ meson into account. The isoscalar resonances $σ$, $f_0(980)$ and the isovector resonance $a_0(980)$ are generated through the FSI of the mesons in the channels $J/ψ\to N \bar N π^0 π^0$ and $J/ψ\to N \bar N π^0 η$, respectively. We also calculate the two mesons invariant mass distribution and the partial decay width of $J/ψ\to N \bar N K^+ K^-$ and $J/ψ\to N \bar N K^0 {\bar K}^0$, on which there is still no experimental data available.

## Key claim

The chiral unitary approach, combined with a simple SU(3) Lagrangian without derivatives for the $J/\psi \to N\bar N MM$ vertex, can reproduce the experimental $\pi^+\pi^-$ invariant mass distribution in $J/\psi \to p\bar p \pi^+\pi^-$ only when the parameter $r_4$ is restricted to the range $-0.3 < r_4 < 0.3$; outside this range a large $f_0(980)$ signal incompatible with experiment appears.

## Method

The authors construct a general SU(3) scalar Lagrangian without derivatives for the $J/\psi N\bar N MM$ vertex, with four unknown coupling parameters $x_1$ through $x_4$. The meson-meson final state interaction is treated using the chiral unitary coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter approach of Oller and Oset (1997), which generates the scalar resonances $\sigma$, $f_0(980)$, and $a_0(980)$ as poles of the S-wave amplitudes. For the $\pi^+\pi^-$ channel, a separate P-wave contribution from an intermediate $\rho$ meson is added, with the $\rho$ width taken as 149.2 MeV. The parameters are fit to the experimental $\pi^+\pi^-$ invariant mass distribution from $J/\psi \to p\bar p \pi^+\pi^-$, and predictions are then made for the $\pi^0\pi^0$, $\pi^0\eta$, $K^+K^-$, and $K^0\bar K^0$ channels.

## Key numbers

- The best fit to the $J/\psi \to p\bar p \pi^+\pi^-$ data yields $r_4 = 0.2$ (no $f_0(980)$ peak) or $r_4 = -0.27$ (a small $f_0(980)$ peak compatible with experiment).
- The coupling constants for $r_4 = 0.2$ are $g_\alpha = 1.1 \times 10^{-6} \text{ MeV}^{-2}$ and $g_{J\rho N\bar N} = 7.1 \times 10^{-5} \text{ MeV}^{-1}$.
- The total width for $J/\psi \to p\bar p \pi^+\pi^-$ is $5.3 \times 10^{-4}$ MeV, corresponding to a branching ratio of $6.0 \times 10^{-3}$, consistent with the experimental value $(6.0 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-3}$.
- The $\rho$ meson contribution alone gives a branching ratio of $1.05 \times 10^{-3}$ for $J/\psi \to p\bar p \pi^+\pi^-$.
- The predicted width for $J/\psi \to p\bar p \pi^0\pi^0$ is $2.2 \times 10^{-4}$ MeV, corresponding to a branching ratio of $2.5 \times 10^{-3}$.
- The predicted branching ratio for $J/\psi \to n\bar n \pi^+\pi^-$ is $6.0 \times 10^{-3}$, consistent with the PDG value $(4 \pm 4) \times 10^{-3}$.
- The $\pi^0\eta$ branching ratio varies from $10^{-4}$ to $10^{-3}$ for $r_2$ between -1 and 1.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Oller and Oset (1997), which successfully described meson-meson scattering up to 1.2 GeV, to the four-body decays of $J/\psi$ into nucleon-antinucleon plus two mesons. It follows earlier studies of final state interaction in radiative $\phi$ decays by the same group, where distinct peaks for $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ were found. The method enables predictions for the $J/\psi \to N\bar N \pi^0\pi^0$, $\pi^0\eta$, $K^+K^-$, and $K^0\bar K^0$ channels, for which experimental data were scarce or absent at the time, and provides a framework to study the nature of light scalar mesons through their manifestation in $J/\psi$ decays.
