# Baryon-Baryon Interactions and Spin-Flavor Symmetry from Lattice Quantum Chromodynamics

> Lattice quantum chromodynamics is used to constrain the interactions of two octet baryons at the SU(3) flavor-symmetric point, with quark masses that are heavier than those in nature (equal to that of the physical strange quark mass and corresponding to a pion mass of $\approx 806~\tt{MeV}$). Specifically, the S-wave scattering phase shifts of two-baryon systems at low energies are obtained with the application of Lüscher's formalism, mapping the energy eigenvalues of two interacting baryons in a finite volume to the two-particle scattering amplitudes below the relevant inelastic thresholds. The values of the leading-order low-energy scattering parameters in the irreducible representations of SU(3) are consistent with an approximate SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry in the nuclear and hypernuclear forces that is predicted in the large-$N_c$ limit of QCD. The two distinct SU(6)-invariant interactions between two baryons are constrained at this value of the quark masses, and their values indicate an approximate accidental SU(16) symmetry. The SU(3) irreducible representations containing the $NN~({^1}S_0)$, $NN~({^3}S_1)$ and $\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(Ξ^0n+Ξ^-p)~({^3}S_1)$ channels unambiguously exhibit a single bound state, while the irreducible representation containing the $Σ^+ p~({^3}S_1)$ channel exhibits a state that is consistent with either a bound state or a scattering state close to threshold. These results are in agreement with the previous conclusions of the NPLQCD collaboration regarding the existence of two-nucleon bound states at this value of the quark masses.

## Key claim

At the SU(3) flavor-symmetric point with quark masses equal to the physical strange quark mass (pion mass ≈ 806 MeV), the leading-order low-energy scattering parameters of two octet baryons are consistent with an approximate SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry predicted in the large-Nc limit of QCD, and further indicate an approximate accidental SU(16) symmetry, though these results are obtained at unphysical quark masses and do not directly connect to phenomenology.

## Method

Lattice QCD correlation functions were computed on multiple ensembles of gauge-field configurations with spatial extents up to ≈ 6.7 fm, and the energy eigenvalues of two interacting octet baryons in a finite cubic volume with periodic boundary conditions were extracted. Lüscher's finite-volume formalism was applied to map these energy eigenvalues to S-wave scattering phase shifts below relevant inelastic thresholds, using the effective range expansion to parametrize the k*cotδ function. For spin-triplet channels, the Blatt-Biedenharn parametrization was adopted, and bound states were identified through both direct volume-dependence analysis of negative-energy states and indirect analytic continuation of scattering amplitudes to the bound-state pole.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass of ≈ 806 MeV at the SU(3) flavor-symmetric point, corresponding to quark masses equal to the physical strange quark mass
- Spatial extent of the largest lattice volume used: ≈ 6.7 fm
- Bound states unambiguously identified in the SU(3) irreducible representations containing the NN (¹S₀), NN (³S₁), and 1/√2(Ξ⁰n+Ξ⁻p) (³S₁) channels
- The irreducible representation containing the Σ⁺p (³S₁) channel exhibits a state consistent with either a bound state or a scattering state close to threshold

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the NPLQCD collaboration (Beane 2012, Beane 2013) on the same gauge-field ensembles, providing the first demonstration of SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry in hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions from lattice QCD. The results contradict claims made in Iritani (2017) regarding the absence of bound states in two-nucleon channels at these quark masses. These calculations establish formal and algorithmic technologies needed for future explorations of multi-baryon systems at physical quark masses, and enable extensions of effective field theory and nuclear many-body techniques (Barnea 2013, Kirscher 2015, Kirscher 2017) to hypernuclear systems at heavy quark masses.
