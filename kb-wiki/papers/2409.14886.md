# Heavy Flavour Energy Loss in Small and Large Systems

> We present suppression results for high-$p_T$ $D$ and $π$ mesons produced in $p / d + A$ and $A+A$ collisions at RHIC and LHC. These results are computed using a convolved elastic and radiative energy loss model, which receives small system size corrections to both the elastic and radiative energy loss. We observe that suppression in small systems is almost entirely due to elastic energy loss; furthermore, we find that our model is acutely sensitive to the transition between hard thermal loop and vacuum propagators in the elastic energy loss. Finally, we consider the central limit theorem approximation, which is commonly used to model the elastic energy loss distribution as Gaussian.

# Heavy Flavour Energy Loss in Small and Large Systems

## Key claim

The paper presents suppression results for high-$p_T$ $D$ and $\pi$ mesons using a convolved elastic and radiative energy loss model that includes small system size corrections; the authors find that suppression in small systems is almost entirely due to elastic energy loss, and that the model is acutely sensitive to the transition between hard thermal loop (HTL) and vacuum propagators in the elastic energy loss, with the central limit theorem approximation for elastic energy loss having negligible effect on the nuclear modification factor.

## Method

The authors compute the nuclear modification factor $R_{AB}$ for pions and $D$ mesons in p/d+A and A+A collisions at RHIC and LHC using a perturbative QCD energy loss model that combines radiative energy loss (DGLV framework, with and without short pathlength corrections) and elastic energy loss (comparing Braaten-Thoma, Gaussian HTL, and Poisson HTL models). Thermodynamic and geometrical quantities are calculated from IP-Glasma initial conditions coupled with subsequent Bjorken expansion. The model results are compared across six variations by independently varying the radiative and elastic energy loss treatments.

## Key numbers

- The uncertainty from the transition between HTL and vacuum propagators in elastic energy loss leads to an $\mathcal{O}(30\text{--}80)\%$ effect in large systems and an $\mathcal{O}(20\text{--}40)\%$ effect in small systems.
- The difference between Poisson HTL and Gaussian HTL results (central limit theorem approximation) is $\mathcal{O}(5\text{--}10)\%$ in large systems and less than $2\%$ in small systems.
- LHC data from central p+Pb collisions finds $R_{AB} \simeq 1\text{--}1.2$ for pions and $D$ mesons at low- to moderate-$p_T$.
- PHENIX observes $R_{AB} \simeq 0.75$ in central d+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV.
- A measured $R_{AB} \sim 0.2$ for leading pions in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV is cited.

## Context

This work extends prior energy loss models by incorporating small system size corrections that are typically neglected in large-system approximations. The DGLV radiative energy loss framework (Djordjevic, Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev, 2003) assumes large pathlength $L$ and neglects terms proportional to $e^{-\mu L}$; a short pathlength correction derived by Kolbe and Horowitz (2015) restores these terms. The elastic energy loss comparison between the Braaten-Thoma model (Braaten and Thoma, 1991) and HTL models (Wicks, 2008) interrogates the uncertainty in the propagator transition. The results enable future work on fitting the strong coupling $\alpha_s$ to data to determine whether small and large system suppression can be simultaneously described by a single model, and motivate further analysis of uncertainties in elastic and radiative energy loss including the large formation time assumption.
