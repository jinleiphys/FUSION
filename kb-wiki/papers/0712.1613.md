# Effective Kbar N interaction based on chiral SU(3) dynamics

> The effective Kbar N interaction based on chiral SU(3) coupled-channel dynamics is derived and its extrapolation below the Kbar N threshold is studied in detail. Starting from the coupled-channel scattering equations, we eliminate the channels other than Kbar N and obtain an effective interaction in the single Kbar N channel. An equivalent local potential in coordinate space is constructed such as to reproduce the full scattering amplitude of the chiral SU(3) coupled-channel framework. We discuss several realistic chiral SU(3)-based models in comparison to reach conclusions about the uncertainties involved. It turns out that, in the region relevant to the discussion of deeply bound Kbar-nuclear few-body systems, the resulting energy-dependent, equivalent local potential is substantially less attractive than the one suggested in previous purely phenomenological treatments.

## Key claim

The effective $\bar{K}N$ interaction derived from chiral SU(3) coupled-channel dynamics, when extrapolated below the $\bar{K}N$ threshold to the region relevant for deeply bound kaonic nuclei, is substantially less attractive than suggested by previous purely phenomenological treatments; the $\bar{K}N$ quasibound state commonly associated with the $\Lambda(1405)$ has a binding energy of only about 12 MeV, not the 27 MeV often used to tune phenomenological potentials.

## Method

Starting from the Bethe-Salpeter equation for coupled-channel meson-baryon scattering in the strangeness S=-1 sector, the authors eliminated channels other than $\bar{K}N$ (specifically $\pi\Sigma$, $\eta\Lambda$, and $K\Xi$) to obtain an effective single-channel interaction that exactly reproduces the full coupled-channel scattering amplitude. They then constructed an equivalent local potential in coordinate space using the leading-order Weinberg-Tomozawa terms from the chiral SU(3) effective Lagrangian, with unitarization via the N/D method, and compared results across several chiral models (Hyodo-Nam-Jido-Hosaka, Oset-Ramos-Bennhold, and Borasoy-Niessler-Weise) to estimate theoretical uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- The $\bar{K}N$ quasibound state pole in the two-channel model is located at $z_1^{(2)} = 1432 - 17i$ MeV, and in the full four-channel model at $z_1^{(4)} = 1428 - 17i$ MeV.
- The second pole (strongly coupled to $\pi\Sigma$) is at $z_2^{(2)} = 1398 - 73i$ MeV in the two-channel model and $z_2^{(4)} = 1400 - 76i$ MeV in the full model.
- The single-channel $\pi\Sigma$ resonance pole (without $\bar{K}N$ coupling) lies at $z_2(\pi\Sigma \text{ only}) = 1388 - 96i$ MeV.
- The $\bar{K}N$ binding energy relative to the isospin-averaged $\bar{K}N$ threshold (1435 MeV) is about 12 MeV, not 27 MeV.
- The meson decay constant used in the baseline model is $f = 106.95$ MeV, with renormalization scale $\mu = 630$ MeV and subtraction constants $a_i = -1.96$ for all channels.

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) coupled-channel approach developed by Kaiser (1995), Oset (1998), Oller (2000), and Lutz (2001) for low-energy $\bar{K}N$ interactions, and directly addresses the controversy over deeply bound kaonic nuclei prompted by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002). The derived effective potential is intended for use in variational and Faddeev calculations of few-body systems such as $K^-pp$, replacing the phenomenological potentials used by Shevchenko (2006, 2007) and Yamazaki (2007) that predicted much stronger binding. The results enable more realistic assessments of whether narrow $\bar{K}$-nuclear bound states can exist, given that the chiral dynamics produces significantly less subthreshold attraction than earlier phenomenological models.
