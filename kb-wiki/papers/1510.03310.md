# Evidence for the virtual beta-gamma transition in 59Ni

> A novel theory of radiative electron capture in second forbidden non-unique transition, is applied to reanalyze experimental data obtained previously for the decay of 59Ni. The measured gamma spectrum is shown to be distorted at the high-energy end, presenting the first direct evidence for the virtual beta-gamma transition through the excited state in 59Co. The complete theoretical predictions, including both the direct and the virtual decay channels, as well as the interference between them, reproduce very well the experimental spectrum. The virtual nuclear component amounts to about 4% of the total gamma intensity.

## Key claim

The measured gamma spectrum of 59Ni shows a distortion at the high-energy end that cannot be explained by the direct radiative electron capture model alone, providing the first direct and unambiguous evidence for the virtual beta-gamma transition through the excited 3/2- state in 59Co, with the virtual nuclear component amounting to about 4% of the total gamma intensity.

## Method

The authors applied a novel length-gauge theory of radiative electron capture (REC) for second forbidden non-unique transitions to reanalyze experimental data from Janas et al. (2005) for the decay of 59Ni. They compared the REC prediction to the measured shape factor, fitting the parameter Λ to the low-energy region (k < 600 keV), then added terms for the virtual "detour" transition (allowed electron capture to the excited 3/2- state followed by E2 gamma emission) and the interference between direct and virtual channels, treating Λ and χ as free parameters in a full-energy-range fit.

## Key numbers

- The half-life of 59Ni is (1.08 ± 0.13) × 10^5 years
- The maximum photon energy for K-capture in 59Ni is Q_K = 1064.8 keV
- The excited 3/2- state in 59Co is located at 1099.3 keV, about 26 keV above the ground state of 59Ni
- The best-fit parameter for the direct transition is Λ = 1.52 ± 0.18
- The best-fit parameter for the virtual transition combination is χ = 0.31 (+0.17, -0.34)
- The total gamma emission probability (for k > 195 keV) is 7.92 × 10^-4 per non-radiative capture
- The virtual component represents 2% and the interference component 2.3% of the total gamma intensity
- The extracted nuclear matrix element |M_(2nu)^(2)| = 0.0349 fm
- The E2 transition matrix element |M_E2| = 21.3 fm^2
- The estimated Gamow-Teller matrix element |M_GT| = 0.157, giving |χ| = 0.25

## Context

This work extends the length-gauge REC theory developed by Pachucki et al. (2015) for first-forbidden unique transitions to second forbidden non-unique transitions, resolving discrepancies that plagued earlier models by Zon and Rapoport (1970) and Kalinowski et al. (1995). Previous experimental searches for virtual beta-gamma transitions in 59Ni by Schmorak (1970), Berenyi (1972), and Janas (2005) were inconclusive because they used either the wrong REC model or its inaccurate Coulomb-free approximation. The results enable future measurements of the combinations of nuclear matrix elements Λ and χ to constrain nuclear structure models, particularly shell-model calculations.
