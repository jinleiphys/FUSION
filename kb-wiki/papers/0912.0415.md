# Strangeness Electromagnetic Production on Nucleons and Nuclei

> Isobar models for the electromagnetic production of kaons are discussed with emphasis on the K^+ photoproduction at very small kaon angles and K^0 photoproduction on deuteron. Distorted-wave impuls approximation calculations of the cross sections for the electroproduction of hypernuclei are presented on the case of the ^{12}B_Λproduction.

# Strangeness Electromagnetic Production on Nucleons and Nuclei

## Key claim
Isobar models for kaon photoproduction, particularly Saclay-Lyon A (SLA) and Kaon-MAID (KM), show large discrepancies at forward kaon angles (up to several hundred percent at 2.2 GeV), and the Saclay-Lyon A model with a fitted K1 resonance decay-width ratio gives good description of deuteron K0 photoproduction data while the Kaon-MAID model cannot reproduce the shape of the momentum spectra even after refitting.

## Method
The authors compare predictions of the Saclay-Lyon A (SLA) and Kaon-MAID (KM) isobar models for K+ photoproduction on protons at forward angles, using effective Lagrangians with Born terms and resonance exchanges. For K0 photoproduction on deuteron, they perform impulse-approximation calculations with the Bonn OBEPQ deuteron wave function, treating the target nucleon off-shell and neglecting final-state interactions. For hypernucleus electroproduction, they use distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) with shell-model nuclear and hypernuclear wave functions and eikonal kaon distortions.

## Key numbers
- At photon energy 2.2 GeV, model predictions for K+ photoproduction at forward kaon angles differ by "several hundreds of per-cent"
- At lower energy, SLA and KM models differ by approximately 30% at theta_K^c.m. approximately 0 degrees
- For d(gamma,K0)Lambda p reaction, SLA model with r_K1 = -2.09 gives chi^2_n.d.f. = 0.88 fitted to low-energy data (0.9 to 1.0 GeV)
- KM model with refitted r_K1 = -2.34 gives chi^2_n.d.f. = 3.64 for the same low-energy data
- Electroproduction of 12_Lambda B at 1.3 GeV with Q^2 = -3 x 10^-6 (GeV/c)^2 shows approximately 30% difference between SLA and KM model predictions
- DWIA calculations for 12_Lambda B at E_gamma^lab = 2.2 GeV show good agreement for Lambda in s-state (first three peaks), but poor description in the p_Lambda region

## Context
This work extends the comparison of isobar models (Saclay-Lyon A from SLA (1995), Kaon-MAID from KM (2000)) for kaon photoproduction, using new data from Tohoku University (Watabe 2007, Tsukada 2008) on deuteron K0 production and JLab Hall A data (Mauro) on hypernucleus electroproduction. The results show that forward-angle K+ data are needed to constrain models for reliable hypernuclear calculations, and that the SLA model's backward-peaked elementary cross section is preferred by the Tohoku deuteron data. The DWIA calculations for p-shell hypernuclei (12_Lambda B, 16_Lambda N) require more complex shell-model treatments including 1h-omega excitations to describe the p_Lambda region, as also noted by Cuss for 16O(e,e'K+)16_Lambda N.
