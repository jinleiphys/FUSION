# QCD modeling of hadron physics

> We review recent developments in the understanding of meson properties as solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation in rainbow-ladder truncation. Included are recent results for the pseudoscalar and vector meson masses and leptonic decay constants, ranging from pions up to c\bar{c} bound states; extrapolation to b\bar{b} states is explored. We also present a new and improved calculation of F_π(Q^2) and an analysis of the πγγtransition form factor for both π(140) and π(1330). Lattice-QCD results for propagators and the quark-gluon vertex are analyzed, and the effects of quark-gluon vertex dressing and the three-gluon coupling upon meson masses are considered.

## Key claim

The rainbow-ladder truncation of Dyson-Schwinger equations, using a two-parameter effective interaction model fitted to the chiral condensate and pion decay constant, successfully describes a wide range of meson properties from pions up to charmonium, but the radially excited pion is quite sensitive to details of the interaction and its decay constant vanishes in the chiral limit as dictated by the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation.

## Method

The authors solve the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation in rainbow truncation and the meson Bethe-Salpeter equation in ladder truncation, using a model effective running coupling that reproduces perturbative QCD above 25 GeV² and is finite in the infrared. For electromagnetic processes, they employ the generalized impulse approximation with the dressed quark-photon vertex obtained from the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation, ensuring current conservation through the vector Ward-Takahashi identity. The calculations are performed in a manifestly Poincaré-covariant framework, solving the bound state equations in moving frames to verify frame independence.

## Key numbers

- Chiral condensate: (0.241 GeV)³ (fitted to reproduce (0.24 GeV)³)
- Pion mass: 0.138 GeV (fitted to experimental value)
- Pion decay constant: 0.131 GeV (fitted to experimental value)
- Kaon mass: 0.497 GeV (fitted to experimental value)
- Kaon decay constant: 0.155 GeV (experimental: 0.160 GeV)
- Rho meson mass: 0.742 GeV (experimental: 0.770 GeV)
- Rho meson decay constant: 0.207 GeV (experimental: 0.216 GeV)
- Phi meson mass: 1.072 GeV (experimental: 1.020 GeV)
- Phi meson decay constant: 0.259 GeV (experimental: 0.236 GeV)
- Ground state pion two-photon coupling G(0,0): approximately 5.4 GeV⁻¹ (from axial anomaly prediction)
- Radially excited pion two-photon coupling G(0,0): approximately 0.7 GeV⁻¹ (not constrained by anomaly)
- Radially excited pion decay constant: approximately -0.0016 GeV (in the convention where f_π = 0.131 GeV)
- Pion form factor calculation limited to Q² < 5 GeV² due to complex conjugate singularities in the quark propagator

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to meson physics developed by Maris and Tandy (1999) and Maris, Roberts, and Tandy (1997-2000), providing a comprehensive review of rainbow-ladder truncation results. The calculations agree with quenched lattice QCD results for the quark mass function from Bowman et al. (2002, 2004) and Skullerud et al. (2001), and with experimental data for the pion form factor from Amendolia et al. (1986) and Volmer et al. (2000). The approach enables systematic calculation of electromagnetic form factors and transition form factors for both ground state and excited mesons, with the radially excited pion results from Holl et al. (2005) demonstrating that the chiral limit behavior is governed by the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation. Future work requires incorporating dressed quark-gluon vertex effects and unquenching effects, as explored by Bhagwat et al. (2003, 2004) and Fischer et al. (2003).
