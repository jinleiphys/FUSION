# Dissipation in the very early stage of the hydrodynamic evolution in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We propose a modification of the hydrodynamic model of the dynamics in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions. A modification of the energy-momentum tensor at the initial stage describes the lack of isotropization of the pressure. Subsequently, the pressure is relaxing towards the equilibrium isotropic form in the local comoving frame. Within the Bjorken scaling solution a bound is found on the decay time of the initial anisotropy of the energy-momentum tensor. For the strongest dissipative effect allowed, we find a relative entropy increase of about 30%, a significant hardening of the transverse momentum spectra, and no effect on the HBT radii.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that dissipative effects in the very early stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, before full local thermalization is achieved, can be modeled as a relaxation of the pressure tensor from a two-dimensional (transverse-only) initial state to an isotropic three-dimensional form. The strongest allowed dissipative effect, corresponding to a relaxation time equal to the initial hydrodynamic starting time, yields a relative entropy increase of about 30%, a significant hardening of transverse momentum spectra, and no effect on HBT radii.

## Method

The author modifies the energy-momentum tensor at the initial stage to describe a lack of isotropization of the pressure, then allows the pressure to relax toward equilibrium isotropic form in the local comoving frame using a simple exponential relaxation equation. For the Bjorken scaling solution with longitudinal expansion only, the hydrodynamic equations reduce to an evolution equation for the energy density that includes a dissipative term. For the full boost-invariant flow with azimuthally symmetric transverse expansion, the equations are solved numerically using an equation of state interpolating between hadron gas and quark-gluon plasma, with initial entropy profiles from the Glauber model for central collisions.

## Key numbers

* Relative entropy increase of 28.4% for the Bjorken scaling solution with relaxation time equal to the initial time (tau_pi/tau_0 = 1), independent of the starting time (tau_0 = 0.2, 0.5, or 1 fm/c).
* Bound on the relaxation time: tau_pi <= tau_0, derived from the requirement that the dissipative correction Pi(tau) does not exceed the pressure p(tau).
* Initial central temperatures of 300 MeV for tau_0 = 1 fm/c and 365 MeV for tau_0 = 0.5 fm/c in ideal hydrodynamics; initial entropy scaled by factor 1/1.3 in dissipative evolution to accommodate entropy production.
* Freeze-out temperature T_f = 165 MeV used for calculating particle spectra and HBT radii.
* Nucleon-nucleon cross section of 42 mb used in the Glauber model for initial entropy profiles.

## Context

This work extends the extreme scenario of two-dimensional transverse-only expansion (Heinz 2002, Bialas 2007, Chojnacki 2007) by introducing a more realistic relaxation mechanism toward isotropic pressure. It contrasts with standard shear viscosity treatments (Teaney 2003, Romatschke 2007, Song 2007) where dissipative effects persist throughout the evolution; here dissipation is confined to the very early stage and vanishes at freeze-out. The approach enables future studies combining early dissipation with nonzero shear or bulk viscosities in later stages, and could be incorporated into more realistic calculations including resonance decays and hadronic rescattering to improve agreement with experimental HBT radii and elliptic flow data.
