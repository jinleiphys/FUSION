# E-by-e jet suppression, anisotropy, medium response and hard-soft tomography

> The Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model for jet propagation and interaction in quark-gluon plasma (QGP) has been used to study jet quenching in high-energy heavy-lion collisions. The suppression of single inclusive jet production, medium modification of $γ$-jet correlation, jet profiles and fragmentation functions as observed in experiments at Large Hadron Collider (LHC) can be described well by LBT in which jet-induced medium response is shown to play an essential role. In event-by-event simulations of jet quenching within LBT, jet azimuthal anisotropies are found to correlate linearly with the anisotropic flows of bulk hadrons from the underlying hydrodynamic events.

## Key claim

The Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model, which includes jet-induced medium response, can describe single inclusive jet suppression, gamma-jet correlations, jet profiles, and fragmentation functions at LHC energies; event-by-event simulations reveal that jet azimuthal anisotropies correlate linearly with the anisotropic flows of bulk hadrons from the underlying hydrodynamic events, enabling a hard-soft tomography of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors used the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model, which tracks jet shower partons, recoil partons from each jet-medium interaction, and "negative" partons representing jet-induced medium excitation, all propagating through a (3+1)D relativistic viscous hydrodynamic medium (CLVisc model). Elastic and inelastic scattering matrix elements are given by pQCD, with inelastic processes including only induced gluon radiation simulated via the high-twist approach; the anti-k_t algorithm is used for jet reconstruction. For the CoLBT-hydro model, jet transport in LBT is coupled to the hydrodynamic evolution through a source term determined by the energy and momentum deposited by the propagating jet shower partons.

## Key numbers

- Strong coupling constant α_s is 0.3 at RHIC and 0.15 at LHC, an effective coupling regulated by Debye screening mass.
- Inclusion of medium response reduces final jet energy loss by as much as 30% for jets with initial jet energy about 400 GeV in Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV.
- The onset of enhancement of soft hadrons from jet-induced medium excitation in gamma-hadron correlations occurs at a fixed value of p_T^h ≈ 2 GeV.
- Event-by-event simulations used 200 fluctuating hydro profiles for each centrality in Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the LBT model (Li 2010, Wang 2013, He 2015, Cao 2017) to event-by-event simulations of jet quenching, building on prior studies of light and heavy flavor hadron suppression and gamma-jet modification. The hard-soft correlation between jet azimuthal anisotropy and bulk hadron elliptic flow enables a new tomographic probe of initial energy density fluctuations and transport properties of the QGP, complementing traditional measurements of jet suppression and fragmentation functions.
