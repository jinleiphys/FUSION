# Two-proton radioactivity within a generalized liquid drop model

> The generalized liquid drop model (GLDM) is firstly extended to study the two-proton ($2p$) radioactivity half-lives of the ground-state of nuclei. According to the comparison between the calculated half-lives and the experimental data, it is shown that the GLDM describes the $2p$ radioactivity half-lives well. In addition, by comparing its accuracy with other models, it is found that the GLDM has a comparable accuracy with them. Finally, the $2p$ radioactivity half-lives of some most probable candidates are predicted with the GLDM by inputting the \textit{Q}$_{2p}$ values (the released energy of the $2p$ radioactivity) from the updated AME 2016 Mass Table, which may be useful for future experiments.

# Two-proton radioactivity within a generalized liquid drop model

## Key claim
The generalized liquid drop model (GLDM) can describe two-proton (2p) radioactivity half-lives of ground-state nuclei with accuracy comparable to the effective liquid drop model (ELDM) and an empirical formula, but only when the orbital angular momentum carried by the 2p pair is zero and the nuclear deformation is neglected; the model fails for the 21+ isomer state of 94Ag and for 16Ne, where deviations reach up to about 3 orders of magnitude.

## Method
The authors treat 2p radioactivity as a two-body problem in which a preformed 2p pair (treated as a 2He cluster) penetrates the Coulomb barrier of the parent nucleus via the WKB approximation. The macroscopic energy includes volume, surface, Coulomb, proximity, and centrifugal potential terms, with the spectroscopic factor S2p determined by fitting to experimental half-lives of 19Mg, 45Fe, 48Ni, and 54Zn. Half-lives are calculated for ground-state to ground-state transitions using experimental Q2p values, and predictions are made for candidate nuclei using Q2p values from the AME 2016 Mass Table.

## Key numbers
- For 6Be (Q2p = 1.371 MeV): experimental log10 T1/2 = -20.30 s, GLDM gives -19.37 s, with log10 HF = -0.94
- For 45Fe (Q2p = 1.154 MeV from Dossat 2005): experimental log10 T1/2 = -2.55 s, GLDM gives -2.87 s, with log10 HF = 0.32
- For 16Ne (Q2p = 1.400 MeV from Woodward 1983): experimental log10 T1/2 = -20.38 s, GLDM gives -16.63 s, with log10 HF = -3.75 (deviation of about 4 orders of magnitude)
- For the 21+ isomer of 94Ag: experimental half-life is 80 s, GLDM predicts 5.0 x 10^4 s
- Predicted true 2p radioactivity candidates: 22Si half-life = 5.06 x 10^-14 s, 34Ca = 1.95 x 10^-11 s, 39Ti = 4.56 x 10^-2 s, 42Cr = 1.33 x 10^-3 s
- Predicted not-true 2p radioactivity candidates: 26S half-life = 2.55 x 10^-15 s, 38Ti = 5.36 x 10^-15 s, 58Ge = 8.02 x 10^-14 s, 59Ge = 1.06 x 10^-7 s

## Context
This work extends the GLDM, previously successful for alpha decay, cluster radioactivity, spontaneous fission, and one-proton emission (Cui 2016, Wang 2010, Wang 2014, Dong 2009), to the study of 2p radioactivity. It follows the approach of Goncalves (2017) who applied the ELDM to 2p decay, and compares results with an empirical formula from Sreeja (2019). The model reproduces experimental data for known 2p emitters (6Be, 12O, 19Mg, 45Fe, 48Ni, 54Zn, 67Kr) but fails for 16Ne and the deformed 21+ isomer of 94Ag, highlighting the need to incorporate nuclear deformation, three-body asymptotic behavior, and configuration mixing as emphasized by Grigorenko (2009), Goigoux (2016), and Rotureau (2006). The predictions for 22Si, 34Ca, 39Ti, and 42Cr provide targets for future experimental searches.
