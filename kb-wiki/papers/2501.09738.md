# Partonic distribution functions and amplitudes using tensor network methods

> Calculations of the parton distribution function (PDF) and distribution amplitude (DA) are highly relevant to core experimental programs as they provide non-perturbative inputs to inclusive and exclusive processes, respectively. Direct computation of the PDFs and DAs remains challenging because they are non-perturbative quantities defined as light-cone correlators of quark and gluon fields, and are therefore inherently time-dependent. In this work, we use a uniform quantum strategy based on tensor network simulation techniques to directly extract these hadronic quantities from first principles using the matrix product state of the hadrons in the same setup. We present exemplary numerical calculations with the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in 1+1 dimensions and compare with available exact diagonalization and quantum circuit simulation results. Using tensor networks, we evaluate the PDF and DA at various strong couplings in the large-qubit limit, which is consistent with expectations at perturbative and non-relativistic limits.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that tensor network methods can directly extract parton distribution functions (PDFs) and distribution amplitudes (DAs) from first principles in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in 1+1 dimensions, achieving results consistent with perturbative and non-relativistic limits in the large-qubit limit, though the method is currently limited to low-dimensional models without dynamical gluon contributions.

## Method

The authors discretize the 1+1 dimensional Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model Hamiltonian using staggered fermions on a lattice, then map it to a spin Hamiltonian via the Jordan-Wigner transformation. They prepare the vacuum and first excited hadron states as matrix product states (MPS) using a variational ground-state search algorithm with charge-preserving constraints, then compute the real-time matrix elements for the PDF and DA using the time-dependent variational principle (TDVP) algorithm. The matrix elements are Fourier transformed to obtain the final PDF and DA distributions, with vacuum subtraction applied for the PDF.

## Key numbers

* Hadron mass for N=102 qubits: M_h a = 1.64187901 at m=0.7a^{-1} and g=0.25
* Tensor network method achieves average error of approximately 10^{-8} compared to exact diagonalization for N=10 qubits at m=0.7a^{-1} and g=0.25
* Simulations use up to N=102 qubits with maximum bond dimension of 800 for state preparation and 810 for time evolution
* Time evolution simulated in the region between z=-10 and z=10 where matrix elements have substantial non-zero contributions
* Variational ground-state search used 100 sweeps and 200 maximum bond dimensions for vacuum state, up to 500 sweeps and 800 maximum bond dimension for hadron state at cutoff 10^{-12}

## Context

This work extends tensor network methods recently demonstrated for PDFs in 1+1 QED by Banuls (2024, 2025) and for hadronic vacuum polarization by Barata (2024), now computing both PDFs and DAs in the same NJL model framework. The approach provides a classical alternative to quantum computing methods for low-dimensional hadronic structure calculations, enabling access to system sizes (up to 102 qubits) beyond current exact diagonalization limits, and can be extended to include dynamical gluon contributions and hadronic tensors in future work.
