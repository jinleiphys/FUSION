# Threshold Pion Electroproduction in Chiral Perturbation Theory

> Electroproduction of pions on the nucleon near the threshold is analyzed within the framework of baryon chiral perturbation theory. We give a thorough discussion of the low--energy theorems related to charged and neutral electropionproduction. It is shown how the axial radius of the nucleon can be related to the S--wave multipoles $E_{0+}^{(-)}$ and $L_{0+}^{(-)}$. The chiral perturbation theory calculations of the $γ^\star p \to π^0 p$ reaction are found to be in good agreement with the recent near threshold data. We also discuss the influence of some isospin--breaking effects in this channel. For future experimental tests of the underlying chiral dynamics, extensive predictions of differential cross sections and multipole amplitudes are presented.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that baryon chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) at one-loop order can describe threshold pion electroproduction, but the low-energy theorems for charged pion electroproduction require a modification due to an additional pion loop effect that alters the relation between the slope of the electric dipole amplitude and the nucleon axial radius, improving agreement with neutrino scattering data.

## Method

The authors performed a one-loop calculation of the invariant amplitudes for the reactions γ*p → π⁺n, γ*n → π⁻p, and γ*p → π⁰p using baryon chiral perturbation theory in flavor SU(2) with equal quark masses, working to first order in the electromagnetic coupling constant. They classified the 66 topologically distinct one-loop Feynman diagrams into three separately gauge-invariant subsets (classes I, II, and III) and fixed the low-energy constants from the nucleon electromagnetic radii, the pion charge radius, and the nucleon axial radius. Isospin-breaking effects were minimally included by using physical pion and nucleon masses in the loop functions.

## Key numbers

- The pion mean square charge radius used to fix the low-energy constant l₆: ⟨r²⟩_π = 0.439 fm²
- The proton and neutron electric mean square radii used to fix b₉ and b̃₉: ⟨r²_E⟩_p = 0.74 fm², ⟨r²_E⟩_n = -0.12 fm²
- The nucleon axial mean square radius from neutrino scattering: ⟨r²_A⟩ = (0.416 ± 0.02) fm², giving b₁₃ = (10.05 ± 0.62) GeV⁻²
- The proton anomalous magnetic moment adjusted to: κ_p = 1.793, with δκ_p = 1.288
- For γ*p → π⁰p at the photon point, the theoretical prediction for |L₀₊|² = 0.2 μb, compared to the experimental result |L₀₊|² = 0.13 ± 0.05 μb; with isospin breaking included, the prediction becomes |L₀₊|² = 0.17 μb
- The data from NIKHEF were obtained in the energy range 0 to 2.5 MeV above threshold at photon four-momenta squared k² = -0.04 to -0.1 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous one-loop CHPT analysis of threshold pion photoproduction (Bernard et al., 1993, referred to as I) to the electroproduction case with virtual photons. It provides a systematic alternative to earlier low-energy theorem approaches by Nambu et al. (1962) and the monograph by De Alfaro et al., and it corrects the relation between the axial radius and electroproduction amplitudes previously used in experimental analyses. The calculations enable future high-precision charged pion electroproduction experiments at NIKHEF, MAMI at Mainz, and MIT Bates to test chiral dynamics, particularly through transverse/longitudinal separation of the data.
