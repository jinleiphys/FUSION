# Chemical Equilibration and Transport Properties of Hadronic Matter near $T_c$

> We discuss how the inclusion of Hagedorn states near $T_c$ leads to short chemical equilibration times of proton anti-proton pairs, $K\bar{K}$ pairs, and $Λ\barΛ$ pairs, which indicates that hadrons do not need to be "born" into chemical equilibrium in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. We show that the hadron ratios computed within our model match the experimental results at RHIC very well. Furthermore, estimates for $η/s$ near $T_c$ computed within our resonance gas model are comparable to the string theory viscosity bound $η/s=1/4π$. Our model provides a good description of the recent lattice results for the trace anomaly close to $T_c=196$ MeV.

## Key claim

The inclusion of Hagedorn states near the critical temperature \(T_c\) allows hadrons to be "born" out of chemical equilibrium and then dynamically reach chemical equilibrium within the hadronic fireball, contradicting the earlier assumption that hadrons must be "born" into chemical equilibrium because time scales were too long.

## Method

The authors constructed an extended Hagedorn gas model with an exponentially rising mass spectrum of hadrons, fitting the model parameters (maximum Hagedorn state mass \(M\), degeneracy \(A\), and effective bag constant \(B\)) to lattice QCD results for the trace anomaly and energy density at two different critical temperatures (\(T_c = 196\) MeV and \(T_c = 176\) MeV). They used rate equations to describe the chemical equilibration of \(p\bar{p}\), \(K\bar{K}\), and \(\Lambda\bar{\Lambda}\) pairs via Hagedorn state decays, and computed an upper bound on the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s\) using the model.

## Key numbers

- For \(T_c = 196\) MeV, the fit parameters are \(A = 0.5\ \text{GeV}^{3/2}\), \(M = 12\) GeV, and \(B = (340\ \text{MeV})^4\).
- For \(T_c = 176\) MeV, the fit parameters are \(A = 0.1\ \text{GeV}^{3/2}\), \(M = 12\) GeV, and \(B = (300\ \text{MeV})^4\).
- Thermal fit without Hagedorn states: \(T_{ch} = 160.4\) MeV, \(\mu_b = 22.9\) MeV, \(\chi^2 = 21.2\).
- Thermal fit with Hagedorn states for \(T_c = 196\) MeV: \(T_{ch} = 165.9\) MeV, \(\mu_b = 25.3\) MeV, \(\chi^2 = 20.9\).
- Thermal fit with Hagedorn states for \(T_c = 176\) MeV: \(T_{ch} = 170.4\) MeV, \(\mu_b = 27.8\) MeV, \(\chi^2 = 18.8\).
- The \(\Lambda\bar{\Lambda}\) pairs reach experimental values already at about \(T = 180\) MeV.
- The \(\eta/s\) estimate near \(T_c\) is comparable to the string theory viscosity bound \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\).

## Context

This work extends the Hagedorn state approach originally introduced by Hagedorn (1968) to explain the exponentially increasing mass spectrum in hadronic scatterings, and updates it with modern lattice QCD results from Cheng (2007) and Zodor (2007). The model provides a mechanism for chemical equilibration that resolves the earlier puzzle raised by "born equilibrium" arguments, and it enables future studies of hadronic transport properties near \(T_c\), including bulk viscosity as discussed in Noronha-Hostler (2008).
