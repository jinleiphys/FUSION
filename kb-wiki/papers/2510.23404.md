# Pentaquarks on the light front, and their mixture with baryons

> In previous papers we developed the light front formulation for Hamiltonians and wave functions (WFs) for mesons and baryons, with both confinement and chiral symmetry breaking. For baryons limited to the lowest Fock component with three quarks, the longitudinal WF is valued in an equilateral triangle with momentum fractions $x_i,i=1,2,3$. The WF was developed both numerically and using a basis function that diagonalizes the Laplacian with Dirichlet boundary conditions. In this paper we extend this analysis to $n$ quark states, and specialize to pentaquarks ($n=5$). We determine their masses and WFs, and address the mixing between baryons and pentaquarks, the issue central to understanding the observed antiquark sea of baryons.

## Key claim

The paper extends the authors' light-front formulation of Hamiltonians and wave functions from mesons and baryons to pentaquarks (five-quark states), determining their masses and wave functions and addressing the mixing between baryons and pentaquarks as a mechanism for understanding the antiquark sea of baryons, though the absolute eigenvalues of the Hamiltonian are not compared to data because attractive Coulomb forces and negative constants normally included in Cornell-type potentials are omitted.

## Method

The authors solve the light-front Hamiltonian for five confined quarks, which includes kinetic energy with constituent quark masses and a linear confining term proportional to the string tension, using the einbein trick to convert the confining square roots into a Laplacian operator. The longitudinal wave functions are defined on a 4-dimensional simplex (pentachoron or A4) in momentum-fraction space, and the authors use variational trial functions (product of sinusoidal functions or product of linear momentum fractions) to approximate the ground and first excited states, while the color-spin-flavor-orbital structure is constructed using representations of the permutation group S4 and diagonalized numerically in Mathematica.

## Key numbers

* The ground state transverse oscillator energy is approximately 0.67 GeV² for constituent quark mass M_q = 0.35 GeV, string tension σ_T = 0.42² GeV², and variational parameter a ≈ 6.
* The first excited transverse oscillator level has energy E_1 = 3E_0, where E_0 is the ground state transverse energy.
* The mass-squared difference between L=0 and L=1 (type 1) pentaquark states is M²(L=1, type 1) - M²(L=0) = 2 √(5 σ_T M² / a), with a ≈ 6 at the variational minimum.
* The antiquark PDF in pentaquarks shows a hard edge behavior ∼ (1-x)^8 at large x.
* For S-shell (L=0) pentaquarks with I=1/2 and S_z=1/2, the color-spin Hamiltonian eigenvalues are: 4.6667, 3.0000, 1.4415, -2.7749, -0.33333, -3.3333, -3.3333.
* For P-shell (L=1) pentaquarks with I=1/2, S_z=1/2, the color-spin Hamiltonian eigenvalues include values such as 9.05, -7.33, 4.67, -3.33, 3.00, -2.77, 1.67, 1.62, 1.44, -0.333 (with multiple degeneracies).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous light-front formulation for mesons (Shuryak 2022) and baryons (Shuryak 2022, Miesch 2023, Miesch 2025) to pentaquarks, following their recent study of pentaquark S- and P-shell states in the center-of-mass frame (Miesch 2025). The mixing between baryons and pentaquarks is central to understanding the flavor asymmetry of the antiquark sea in nucleons and the strong orbital motion in nucleons, long-standing puzzles in hadronic physics. The approach differs from other light-front formulations (Vary 2009, Jia 2018) by using Jacobi coordinates to eliminate center-of-mass motion from the outset and by employing a Hamiltonian suggested by QCD scalar confinement.
