# Lepton-Pair Production in Virtual Compton Scattering off the Proton

> We show that lepton-pair production in Virtual Compton Scattering offers, through interference with the well-known Bethe-Heitler process, a sensitive probe to learn the longitudinal response of resonances and the electromagnetic nucleon form factors. This interference can be measured directly in terms of an asymmetry. The role of off-shell effects in the N-N-$γ$ vertices is investigated as well. An additional N-N-$γ-γ$ contact term in the amplitude, included to ensure gauge invariance of the model, cancels a substantial part of the off-shell effects.

## Key claim

Lepton-pair production in Virtual Compton Scattering (VCS) can serve as a sensitive probe of the longitudinal response of nucleon resonances and electromagnetic nucleon form factors through the interference with the Bethe-Heitler process, but only under specific kinematic conditions (near the kinematical limit and at backward virtual-photon angles) where the asymmetry is measurable and the longitudinal response, though small in the nuclear matrix element, strongly affects the asymmetry.

## Method

The authors calculate the asymmetry A_BH-N between cross sections for e+e- and e-e+ production in VCS off the proton at E_gamma=500 MeV in the lab frame (sqrt(s)=1.349 GeV) and sqrt(k^2)=406 MeV, including the Delta and Roper (P11) resonances in a unitarized K-matrix approach. They model off-shell effects in the N-N-gamma vertex using a Taylor-series expansion with parameters A_i and B_i, and include an N-N-gamma-gamma contact term to restore gauge invariance. The asymmetry is computed as a function of the lepton-pair opening angle theta_d and the virtual-photon angle theta_k, with the lepton pair in the reaction plane (phi_d=0).

## Key numbers

- The asymmetry A_BH-N is calculated at E_gamma=500 MeV in the lab, corresponding to sqrt(s)=1.349 GeV, and at sqrt(k^2)=406 MeV (photon invariant mass for the nuclear contribution).
- For the longitudinal coupling G_3 to the Delta resonance, a value of G_3=20 is used, compared to the default G_3=0.
- Off-shell effects from the Dirac term (parameters A_1=B_1=1 for the "partial F_1" case, and A_1=-1, B_1=1 for the "F_1^-" case) produce changes in the asymmetry of about 5% in A_BH-N.
- The off-shell parameters A_1 and B_1 from a one-loop model are estimated to be of order 0.1, while A_2 and B_2 for the Pauli form factor are of order 1/4.

## Context

This work extends the study of VCS in the time-like region of the photon momentum, following earlier proposals by Alvaredo (1973) and Lvov (1996) to exploit the Bethe-Heitler interference, and complements the approach of Schafer (1995) who studied form factors in dilepton mass spectra under symmetric conditions. The results enable future experiments to distinguish the longitudinal response of resonances from off-shell effects and to test the Vector Meson Dominance model in the region k^2 < M^2, though higher photon energies and inclusion of higher resonances (such as D13) would be needed to access the region near the rho-meson mass.
