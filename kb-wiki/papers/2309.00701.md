# Entropies, level-density parameters, and fission probabilities along the triaxially- and axially-symmetric fission paths in $^{296}$Lv

> We employ a statistical approach to investigate the influence of axial asymmetry on the nuclear level density and entropy along the fission pathways of a superheavy nucleus, explicitly focusing on the $^{296}$Lv isotope. These pathways are determined within multidimensional deformation spaces. Our analysis reveals a significant impact of triaxiality on entropy. Additionally, suppressing shell effects can alter the fission scenario depending on the available excitation energy. We derive the deformation-dependent level density parameter, which plays a crucial role in estimating the survival probability of a superheavy nucleus. Furthermore, we utilize a set of master equations to obtain the time-dependent fission probabilities and calculate the ratio of decay probabilities for both axial and triaxial paths.

## Key claim

Triaxial (nonaxial) deformation significantly modifies the fission barrier of the superheavy nucleus ²⁹⁶Lv, reducing the axial barrier by approximately 1 MeV, and the choice of fission path (axial versus triaxial) strongly affects the behavior of the level-density parameter and entropy along the path, especially when the damping of microscopic (shell and pairing) corrections with excitation energy is taken into account.

## Method

The authors constructed a five-dimensional potential energy surface (PES) for ²⁹⁶Lv using the macroscopic-microscopic method with a Woods-Saxon potential and the Yukawa-plus-exponential macroscopic model, including quadrupole nonaxial deformation β₂₂. They identified axial and triaxial fission paths by following the steepest gradient descent on the PES. Using a superfluid formalism and the back-shifted Fermi-gas model, they calculated the deformation-dependent level-density parameter, entropy, and intrinsic level density at each point along both fission paths as functions of excitation energy, with and without damping of microscopic corrections. Finally, they solved a set of master equations to obtain time-dependent fission probabilities for the two competing paths.

## Key numbers

* The axial fission barrier height for the cold nucleus is 7.31 MeV; the triaxial barrier height is 6.25 MeV.
* At an excitation energy U₀ = 25 MeV (relative to the cold ground state), the damping of microscopic corrections reduces the axial barrier to 3.93 MeV and the triaxial barrier to 3.05 MeV.
* The critical temperature for pairing is about 0.42 MeV at the ground state and about 0.52 MeV at the saddle point; the corresponding total excitation energies are approximately U_cr ≈ 5.14 MeV at the ground state and U_cr ≈ 11.27 MeV at the saddle point.
* The damping effect on the level-density parameter never exceeds 5% at U₀ = 30 MeV.
* The triaxial saddle point shifts from β₂ = 0.37 to β₂ = 0.33 with damping at U₀ = 25 MeV; the axial saddle point shifts from β₂ = 0.25 to β₂ = 0.22.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Rahmatinejad 2021) on the energy dependence of the level-density parameter in superheavy nuclei by explicitly incorporating deformation dependence along both axial and triaxial fission paths. It contrasts with earlier approaches that often assumed axial symmetry or neglected the damping of shell effects with excitation energy, such as the phenomenological formula of Ignatyuk (1975). The results enable more accurate calculations of the survival probability of superheavy compound nuclei formed in fusion reactions, particularly for the isotope ²⁹⁶Lv relevant to experiments at the JINR "superheavy factory," by providing deformation-dependent level-density parameters and fission probabilities for competing fission pathways.
