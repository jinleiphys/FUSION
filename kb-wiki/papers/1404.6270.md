# Double Spin Asymmetries through QCD Instantons

> We revisit the large instanton contribution to the gluon Pauli form factor of the constituent quark noted by Kochelev. We check that it contributes sizably to the single spin asymmetry in polarized $p_\uparrow p \rightarrow πX$. We use it to predict a large double spin asymmetry in doubly polarized $p_\uparrow p_\uparrow\rightarrow ππX$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the anomalously large Pauli form factor for a constituent quark arising from QCD instantons, previously noted by Kochelev, produces a sizable contribution to the single spin asymmetry (SSA) in polarized proton-proton scattering and predicts a large double spin asymmetry (DSA) in doubly polarized proton-proton scattering, with the caveat that the DSA vanishes at infinite center-of-mass energy.

## Method

The authors revisit the effective quark-gluon vertex derived from the instanton vacuum in the dilute instanton approximation, which contains a Pauli form factor term proportional to the instanton density and average size. They compute the SSA for semi-inclusive \(p_\uparrow p \rightarrow \pi X\) by evaluating the first-order differential cross section from the chirality-flip vertex, regulating collinear divergences with a constituent gluon mass. For the DSA in \(p_\uparrow p_\uparrow \rightarrow \pi\pi X\), they calculate the second-order differential cross section from the same vertex, averaging over the transverse azimuthal angle, and express the asymmetries in terms of spin-polarized parton distribution functions.

## Key numbers

* Instanton density: \(n_I \approx 1/\text{fm}^4\); typical instanton size: \(\rho \approx 1/3\ \text{fm}\); constituent quark mass: \(m_q^* \approx 300\ \text{MeV}\); effective gluon mass: \(m_g \approx 420\ \text{MeV}\) (used for regularization).
* SSA results are compared to experimental data at \(\sqrt{s} = 19.4\ \text{GeV}\) (Skeens 1991), with outgoing pion transverse momentum \(\langle K_\perp \rangle = 2\ \text{GeV}\) and parameter \(c = 2\).
* DSA predictions for dijet production are presented at \(\sqrt{s} = 20\ \text{GeV}\), \(50\ \text{GeV}\), and \(100\ \text{GeV}\), assuming each parton carries one third of the proton momentum (\(\langle x_1 \rangle = \langle x_2 \rangle = 1/3\)).
* The DSA for \(\pi^+\pi^+\), \(\pi^-\pi^-\), and \(\pi^+\pi^-\) productions is given by Eqs. (doublepiplus), (doublepiminus), and (doublepiplusminus) respectively, and vanishes as \(\sqrt{s} \rightarrow \infty\).

## Context

This work extends the instanton-based mechanism for spin asymmetries originally proposed by Kochelev (1999), who noted a large Pauli form factor for constituent quarks. The SSA calculation follows the approach of Kochelev (2013) and is comparable in magnitude to earlier work by Ostrovsky (2005) and Qian (2011) using the standard Dirac form factor with changes in the instanton distribution. The paper predicts a new observable, the DSA in doubly polarized dijet production, which could be tested at collider facilities such as RHIC (STAR and PHENIX collaborations).
