# Energy dependence of pion interferometry scales in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions

> A study of energy behavior of the pion spectra and interferometry scales is carried out for the top SPS, RHIC and LHC energies within the hydrokinetic approach. The latter allows one to describe evolution of quark-gluon and hadron matter as well as continuous particle emission from the fluid in agreement with the underlying kinetic equations. The main mechanisms that lead to the paradoxical, at first sight, behavior of the interferometry scales, are exposed. In particular, a slow decrease and apparent saturation of $R_{out}/R_{side}$ ratio with an energy growth happens due to a strengthening of positive correlations between space and time positions of pions emitted at the radial periphery of the system. Such en effect is a consequence of a developing of the pre-thermal collective transverse flows and an increase of the initial energy density in the fireball.

## Key claim

The slow decrease and apparent saturation of the $R_{out}/R_{side}$ ratio around unity at high collision energies is caused by a strengthening of positive correlations between the space and time positions of pions emitted at the radial periphery, which results from the development of pre-thermal collective transverse flows and an increase of the initial energy density in the fireball.

## Method

The authors used the HydroKinetic Model (HKM), which couples hydrodynamic expansion of quark-gluon and hadron matter with continuous particle emission described by escape probabilities derived from the Boltzmann equations. They applied boost-invariant Bjorken-like initial conditions at proper time $\tau_0 = 1$ fm/c with a Glauber-like transverse energy density profile and a linear initial transverse rapidity profile, fitting the maximal initial energy density $\epsilon_0$ and the transverse flow parameter $\alpha$ to reproduce pion transverse spectra at top SPS and RHIC energies. The equation of state was matched to lattice QCD data at high temperature and a non-chemically equilibrated hadron-resonance gas with 359 hadron states below $T_{ch}=165$ MeV, including resonance decays and a 30% recombination factor.

## Key numbers

- Maximal initial energy density for top SPS energy: $\epsilon_0 = 9$ GeV/fm$^3$ (mean $\langle\epsilon\rangle_0 = 6.4$ GeV/fm$^3$)
- Maximal initial energy density for top RHIC energy: $\epsilon_0 = 16.5$ GeV/fm$^3$ (mean $\langle\epsilon\rangle_0 = 11.6$ GeV/fm$^3$)
- LHC calculations performed at $\epsilon_0 = 40$ GeV/fm$^3$ and $\epsilon_0 = 60$ GeV/fm$^3$, corresponding to approximately 4 and 5.6 ATeV
- Transverse flow parameter $\alpha = 0.194$ (mean transverse velocity $\langle v_T \rangle = 0.178$) for SPS; $\alpha = 0.28$ ($\langle v_T \rangle = 0.25$) for RHIC and LHC
- Temperatures at the region of maximal emission for $p_T = 0.3$ GeV/c: 0.105 GeV (SPS), 0.103 GeV (RHIC), and 0.095 GeV (LHC)
- Chemical freeze-out temperature: $T_{ch} = 165$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the hydrokinetic approach previously developed by the authors (Karpenko and Sinyukov, 2009) to address the "RHIC HBT puzzle," where interferometry volumes rose more slowly with energy than expected and the $R_{out}/R_{side}$ ratio remained near unity rather than increasing as predicted by Bertsch (1989) as a signal of QGP formation. The calculations reproduce experimental data from the CERES and NA-49 Collaborations at SPS and the STAR and PHENIX Collaborations at RHIC, and provide predictions for LHC energies. The results enable future studies of how pre-thermal flows and initial energy density shape femtoscopic observables across collision energies.
