# Lower bound on the proton charge radius from electron scattering data

> The proton charge-radius determinations from the electromagnetic form-factor measurements in electron-proton scattering require an extrapolation to zero momentum transfer ($Q^2=0$) which is prone to model-dependent assumptions. We show that the data at finite momentum transfer can be used to establish a rigorous lower bound on the proton charge radius. Using the available $ep$ data at low $Q^2$, we obtain $R_E > 0.850(1)$ fm as the lower bound on the proton radius. This reaffirms the discrepancy between the $ep$ and muonic-hydrogen values, while bypassing the model-dependent assumptions that go into the fitting and extrapolation of the $ep$ data.

## Key claim

The paper shows that finite-$Q^2$ electron-proton scattering data can establish a rigorous lower bound on the proton charge radius without requiring model-dependent extrapolation to $Q^2=0$, yielding $R_E > 0.848$ fm at 95% confidence level, though this bound assumes systematic errors contribute only to the overall normalization of the cross section.

## Method

The authors define a quantity $R_E^2(Q^2) \equiv -6 \ln G_E(Q^2)/Q^2$ which is monotonically increasing toward $Q^2=0$ due to the analytic properties of the form factor, so its value at any finite $Q^2$ provides a lower bound on the true radius squared $R_E^2$. They apply this to two experimental datasets (Bernauer et al. 2014 and Mihovilovic et al. 2016) for $Q^2 < 0.02$ GeV$^2$, and also construct a normalization-free bound $R_{ij}^2$ using ratios of form factors at different $Q^2$ values to cancel overall normalization uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Lower bound from Bernauer dataset (raw average): $R_E > 0.857 \pm 0.003$ fm (statistical error only, 95% confidence)
- Lower bound from Bernauer dataset (normalization-free): $R_E > 0.850 \pm 0.001$ fm (statistical error only, 95% confidence)
- Lower bound from Bernauer subset "1:3" (normalization-free): $R_E > 0.851 \pm 0.003$ fm (statistical error only, 95% confidence)
- Lower bound from Mihovilovic ISR dataset (normalization-free): $R_E > 0.854 \pm 0.014$ fm (statistical error only, 95% confidence)
- The analysis uses data below $Q^2 = 0.02$ GeV$^2$, with the most precise bound $R_E > 0.848$ fm at 95% CL quoted as the main result

## Context

This work addresses the proton-radius puzzle by providing a model-independent lower bound that bypasses the extrapolation issues in standard form-factor fits, which have been debated by Sick (2012), Bernauer (2016), and others. The bound reaffirms the discrepancy with muonic-hydrogen values from Pohl (2010) and Antognini (2013), while the authors note that upcoming PRad experiments reaching $2\times10^{-4}$ GeV$^2$ should set a more stringent bound.
