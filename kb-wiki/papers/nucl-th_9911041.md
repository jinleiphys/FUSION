# One- and two-proton transfer reactions with vibrational Nuclei

> We extend a semiclassical model of transfer reactions to the case in which one of the collision partners is a vibrational nucleus. The model is applied to one- and two-proton stripping reactions in the 37Cl + 98Mo system, for which a rapid transition from normal to anomalous slope in the two proton transfer reaction at energies around the Coulomb barrier is experimentally observed. This behavior is satisfactorily reproduced by the present extension of the model.

## Key claim

The paper extends a semiclassical model of transfer reactions to include the case where one collision partner is a vibrational nucleus, and shows that this extension reproduces the experimentally observed rapid transition from normal to anomalous slope in two-proton transfer for the 37Cl + 98Mo system at energies around the Coulomb barrier, whereas a calculation without vibrational effects predicts the transition at a higher energy than observed.

## Method

The authors extend a semiclassical model that considers two contributing trajectories (a Rutherford-like trajectory and one more strongly affected by the nuclear optical potential) for a given scattering angle. For the vibrational target 98Mo, they parametrize the target radius with a quadrupole vibrational mode, solve coupled equations of motion for the relative motion and the internal vibration, and calculate the transfer probability via WKB tunneling or the Hill-Wheeler formula, averaging over the initial vibrational phase. The model is applied to one- and two-proton stripping reactions in the 37Cl + 98Mo system.

## Key numbers

- The abrupt change in slope for two-proton stripping occurs at approximately Elab ≈ 117 MeV, corresponding to the Coulomb barrier energy.
- For 98Mo, the vibrational parameters used are E2+ = 0.786 MeV and C2 = 70 MeV.
- The optical potential uses diffuseness a = 0.7 fm and imaginary strength W0 = 70 MeV.
- The nuclear potential depth for the transferred particle is given by V0_j(tau_z) = [51 - 33 tau_z (N_j - Z_j)/A_j] MeV, with tau_z = +1 for neutrons and tau_z = -1 for protons.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous semiclassical model (Marta et al., earlier papers) which explained slope anomalies in proton and neutron transfer for structureless spherical nuclei, by now incorporating quadrupole vibrations of the target. The model successfully reproduces data from the SUNY group (Liang, Mahon) on the 37Cl + 98Mo system, where a calculation without vibrational effects predicted the slope transition at too high an energy. The approach can be extended to other degrees of freedom such as octupole vibrations or nuclear deformation, though the latter would require averaging over two Euler angles and many more trajectories.
