# Many-body correlations of quasiparticle random-phase approximation in nuclear matrix element of neutrinoless double-beta decay

> We show that the correlations of the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) significantly reduce the nuclear matrix element (NME) of neutrinoless double-beta decay by a new mechanism in the calculation for $^{150}$Nd $\rightarrow$ $^{150}$Sm. This effect is due mainly to the normalization factors of the QRPA ground states included in the overlap of intermediate states, to which the QRPA states based on the initial and final ground states are applied. These normalization factors arise according to the definition of the QRPA ground state as the vacuum of quasibosons. Our NME is close to those of other groups in spite of this new reduction effect because we do not use the proton-neutron pairing interaction usually used for reproducing the experimental NME of the two-neutrino double-beta ($2νββ$) decay. Our method can repeoduce the experimental $2νββ$ NME for $^{150}$Nd $\rightarrow$ $^{150}$Sm with the quenching axial-vector current coupling without approacing the breaking point of the QRPA. The consistency of QRPA approaches taking different virtual paths under the closure approximation is also discussed, and an extension of the QRPA ground state is proposed.

## Key claim

The quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) ground-state normalization factors, which arise from the definition of the QRPA ground state as the vacuum of quasibosons, significantly reduce the neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix element (NME) for \(^{150}\)Nd \(\rightarrow\) \(^{150}\)Sm via a new mechanism involving the overlap of intermediate states; however, the author's final NME remains close to those of other groups because the calculation does not use the proton-neutron pairing interaction typically employed to reproduce the experimental two-neutrino double-beta decay NME.

## Method

The author calculates the \(0\nu\beta\beta\) NME for \(^{150}\)Nd \(\rightarrow\) \(^{150}\)Sm using the like-particle QRPA with two sets of intermediate states under the closure approximation, specifically employing the two-neutron removal/two-proton addition virtual path [Eq. (11) in the text]. The overlap of QRPA states based on the initial and final ground states is computed by expanding the QRPA ground-state wave functions (defined as the vacuum of quasibosons) in terms of correlation coefficients proportional to the backward amplitudes, with the normalization factors of the QRPA ground states calculated up to fourth order (and partially beyond) in the correlation coefficients. The two-body matrix elements of the \(0\nu\beta\beta\) transition operator (Gamow-Teller and Fermi terms) are evaluated numerically using single-particle wave functions expressed on a B-spline mesh in a cylindrical box, with the neutrino potential singularity handled by a coordinate rotation to \((P,\rho)\) and \((Z,z)\) variables.

## Key numbers

* The NME for \(^{150}\)Nd \(\rightarrow\) \(^{150}\)Sm is reduced significantly by the new mechanism involving QRPA ground-state normalization factors; the author's NME is close to those of other groups despite this reduction.
* The experimental two-neutrino double-beta (\(2\nu\beta\beta\)) NME for \(^{150}\)Nd \(\rightarrow\) \(^{150}\)Sm is reproduced with the quenching axial-vector current coupling without approaching the breaking point of the QRPA.
* The neutrino potential uses \(R = 1.1 A^{1/3}\) fm for the mean nuclear radius.
* The normalization factors \({\cal N}_I\) and \({\cal N}_F\) are expanded up to \(n=5\) for terms proportional to \(\{\text{Tr}(C C^\dagger)\}^n\), though terms with \(n>2\) are very small in the numerical calculation.

## Context

This work extends the QRPA approach to \(0\nu\beta\beta\) decay NME calculations by incorporating a new many-body correlation effect from the overlap of QRPA states based on different ground states, which was not included in prior QRPA calculations. The method is applied to \(^{150}\)Nd \(\rightarrow\) \(^{150}\)Sm, a decay with a large phase-space factor (Kotila and Iachello, 2012; Stoica and Mirea, 2013), making its reliable NME valuable experimentally. The use of like-particle QRPA for the two-particle transfer path follows the suggestion in Vogel (2010) and the approach in Barabash (2009), and the consistency of different virtual paths under the closure approximation is discussed, with an extension of the QRPA ground state proposed for future work.
