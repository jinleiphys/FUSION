# Investigation of pairing correlations on computed Gamow-Teller strength distributions and associated $β$-decay half-lives

> We investigate the effect of pairing correlations on the computed Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions and corresponding $β$-decay half-lives. The calculations are performed for a total of 47 sd-shell nuclei, for $20 < A < 30$, employing the pn-QRPA model. Our calculations use three different values of pairing gaps computed using three different empirical formulae. The GT strength distribution and centroid values change considerably with a change in the pairing gap values. This, in turn, leads to differences in computed half-lives. The pairing gaps computed using the mass-dependent formula result in the calculated half-lives being in better agreement with the measured data.

# Investigation of pairing correlations on computed Gamow-Teller strength distributions and associated β-decay half-lives

## Key claim
The choice of pairing gap formula significantly alters computed Gamow-Teller strength distributions and centroid values, which in turn changes calculated β-decay half-lives; the mass-dependent pairing gap formula (Δ = 12/√A MeV) yields half-lives in better overall agreement with experimental data from Audi et al. (2017) than the three-term or five-term empirical formulae, though many individual nuclei still show deviations exceeding 100%.

## Method
The authors employed the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model with a Nilsson deformed basis and BCS pairing to compute Gamow-Teller strength distributions and β-decay half-lives for 47 sd-shell nuclei with mass numbers 20 < A < 30. Three different empirical formulae for pairing gaps were tested: a mass-dependent formula (TF, Δ = 12/√A MeV), a three-term formula (3TF) using neutron and proton separation energies, and a five-term formula (5TF) using binding energies from the AME2020 mass compilation. The particle-hole and particle-particle interaction strengths were chosen following the 1/A^0.7 relation of Homma et al. (1996), and the Ikeda sum rule was fulfilled.

## Key numbers
- 47 sd-shell nuclei calculated, of which 32 decay by β- emission and 15 by β+ emission
- TF scheme: 29 cases within 100% deviation from experimental half-lives (Audi et al. 2017), 13 within 500%, remaining within 1000%
- 3TF scheme: 24 cases within 100% deviation, 8 within 500%, 1 within 1000%
- 5TF scheme: 24 cases within 100% deviation, 7 within 500%, 3 within 1000%
- For 27Ne, total GT strength using 5TF scheme was 0.04 (arb. units) versus 2.38 (TF) and 2.09 (3TF), leading to a half-life of 4.25 s (5TF) compared to 0.0841 s (TF) and 0.0315 s (experimental)
- For 23F, TF half-life = 0.866 s, 3TF half-life = 3600 s, 5TF half-life = 4330 s, experimental = 2.23 s
- For 25Na, 3TF scheme placed centroid at 3.70 MeV versus 1.02 MeV (TF) and 1.19 MeV (5TF), yielding half-life 5.53 × 10^6 s (3TF) versus 12.2 s (TF) and 59.1 s (experimental)
- For 27P β+ decay, TF centroid = 8.13 MeV, 3TF centroid = 8.82 MeV, 5TF centroid = 7.66 MeV, with half-lives 0.681 s (TF), 10.6 s (3TF), 0.951 s (5TF), experimental = 0.260 s

## Context
This work extends previous pn-QRPA calculations of β-decay properties by Staudt et al. (1990), Hirsch et al. (1993), and Nabi et al. (1999) by systematically testing the sensitivity to pairing gap parametrization. The study is motivated by the need for reliable Gamow-Teller strength distributions for modeling core-collapse supernovae, where electron capture and β-decay rates control the lepton fraction and entropy. The results enable improved selection of pairing prescriptions for future large-scale calculations of stellar weak interaction rates, particularly for nuclei where experimental half-lives are unknown.
