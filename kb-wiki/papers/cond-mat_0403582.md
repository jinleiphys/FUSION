# The microcanonical entropy is multiply differentiable. No dinosaurs in microcanonical gravitation: No special "microcanonical phase transitions"

> The microcanonical entropy $S=\ln[W(E)]$ is the {\em geometrical measure} of the microscopic redundancy or ignorance about the N-body system. {\em Even for astronomical large systems} is the microcanonical entropy everywhere {\em single valued and multiply differentiable}. Also the microcanonical temperature is at all energies single valued and differentiable. It is further shown that the recently introduced singularities of the {\em microcanonical} entropy like "microcanonical phase transitions", and exotic patterns of the {\em microcanonical} caloric curve $T(E)$ like multi-valuednes or the appearance of "dinosaur's necks" are inconsistent with Boltzmann's fundamental definition of entropy.

# The microcanonical entropy is multiply differentiable. No dinosaurs in microcanonical gravitation: No special "microcanonical phase transitions"

## Key claim

The microcanonical entropy S(E) = ln[W(E)] is everywhere single-valued and multiply differentiable (at least (3N-7)/2 times) for any finite N-body system, including astronomically large self-gravitating systems, which means that recently claimed "microcanonical phase transitions" and exotic caloric curve features such as "dinosaur necks" or multi-valued temperature are inconsistent with Boltzmann's fundamental definition of entropy and must be artifacts of mean-field approximations.

## Method

The author derives the microcanonical partition sum W(E) for N particles interacting via a two-body potential bounded from below and confined within volume V, using the standard phase-space integral with delta-function energy constraint and momentum conservation. By explicit differentiation of the entropy S(E) = ln[W(E)], the author shows that the inverse temperature β(E) = dS/dE and its derivative β'(E) = d²S/dE² are both finite and non-vanishing for any finite excitation energy above the potential minimum when 3N > 7, because the theta-function in the configuration-space integral makes the integration boundaries independent of E and cancels singularities from the delta-function.

## Key numbers

- The entropy S(E) is multiply differentiable at least (3N-7)/2 times for any finite N-body system with 3N > 7.
- The inverse temperature β(E) = dS/dE is positive and finite for any finite non-vanishing excitation energy E > min(Φ) when 3N > 7.
- For a star with large N, dT(E)/dE = -β'/β² = 1/(cN), where c(e) is the microcanonical heat capacity per atom, which can be positive or negative but never zero.
- The kinetic energy per degree of freedom is given by ⟨Θ(E-Φ) × 2(E-Φ)/(3N-5)⟩, which differs from the thermodynamic temperature T_thd = 1/β.

## Context

This paper directly contradicts the claims of Barre (2003) and Chavanis (2002), who introduced "microcanonical phase transitions" defined by singularities of the microcanonical entropy or temperature, and who proposed exotic caloric curve features such as multi-valued temperature, hysteretic cyclings, and "dinosaur necks" for self-gravitating systems. The author argues these features are artifacts of mean-field approximations for the entropy, specifically the approximation used in equation 5 of Chavanis (2002). The work reaffirms Boltzmann's original definition of entropy as the geometric measure of ignorance about microscopic degrees of freedom, and establishes that microcanonical thermodynamics based on this definition yields smooth, differentiable thermodynamic functions for all finite N-body systems.
