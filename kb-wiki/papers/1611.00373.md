# Neutrino Interactions and Long-Baseline Experiments

> The extraction of neutrino mixing parameters and the CP-violating phase requires knowledge of the neutrino energy. This energy must be reconstructed from the final state of a neutrino-nucleus reaction since all long-baseline experiments use nuclear targets. This reconstruction requires detailed knowledge of the neutrino reactions with bound nucleons and of the final state interactions of hadrons with the nuclear environment. Quantum-kinetic transport theory can be used to build an event generator for this reconstruction that takes basic nuclear properties, such as binding, into account. Some examples are discussed that show the effects of nuclear interactions on observables in long-baseline experiments

## Key claim
The extraction of neutrino mixing parameters and the CP-violating phase requires knowledge of the neutrino energy, which must be reconstructed from the final state of a neutrino-nucleus reaction because all long-baseline experiments use nuclear targets. The paper argues that quantum-kinetic transport theory can provide a more reliable description of these reactions than standard event generators, which neglect nuclear binding and nucleon potentials.

## Method
The author uses the GiBUU implementation of quantum-kinetic transport theory, which is derived from quantum-kinetic theory and is relativistically covariant, to describe neutrino-nucleus interactions. This approach takes nuclear binding and in-medium transport of hadrons into account, and has been tested on a broad class of nuclear reactions including A+A, p+A, pi+A and gamma+A. The model provides not just inclusive cross sections but also the full final state, including four-vectors of all final-state particles.

## Key numbers
- At the T2K near detector with an average neutrino energy of approximately 0.7 GeV, quasielastic scattering accounts for only about one half of the total inclusive cross section at the maximum of the momentum distribution.
- For the DUNE experiment, the required energy uncertainty for oscillation signal reconstruction is about 100 MeV.
- The inclusive electron scattering cross section on carbon at 560 MeV and 60 degrees has Q^2 = 0.24 GeV^2 at the quasielastic peak.

## Context
This work extends the application of nuclear transport theory, widely used for other nuclear reactions, to neutrino-nucleus interactions for long-baseline experiments. It contrasts with standard neutrino event generators that describe the nuclear ground state as an ensemble of unbound nucleons with a relativistic Fermi gas momentum distribution and neglect nuclear binding. The results enable improved energy reconstruction methods for experiments like T2K and DUNE, particularly through the selection of events with zero pions and exactly one proton, as shown in Mosel (2013).
