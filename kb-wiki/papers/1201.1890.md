# Radiative energy loss reduction in an absorptive plasma

> The influence of the damping of radiation on the radiative energy loss spectrum of a relativistic charge in an infinite, absorptive plasma is studied. We find increasing reduction of the spectrum with increasing damping. Our studies, which represent an Abelian approximation for the colour charge dynamics in the quark-gluon plasma, may influence the analysis of jet quenching phenomena observed in high-energy nuclear collisions. Here, we focus on a formal discussion of the limiting behaviour with increasing radiation frequency. In an absorptive (and polarizable) medium, this is determined by the behaviour of the exponential damping factor entering the spectrum and the formation time of radiation.

## Key claim
The radiative energy loss spectrum of a relativistic charge in an absorptive plasma is increasingly reduced as the damping rate increases, but the formal vacuum limit at high radiation frequency can only be recovered if the charge energy satisfies \(E \ll \min(M^4/\hat{q}, M^2/\Gamma)\), a condition the abstract does not state.

## Method
The authors model the absorptive plasma via a complex index of refraction squared \(n^2(\omega)=1-m^2/\omega^2 + 2i\Gamma/\omega\), where \(m\) is an in-medium gluon mass and \(\Gamma\) a damping rate. Using linear response theory and incorporating multiple deflections of the charge (following Landau (1953)), they compute the radiative energy loss spectrum per unit length from Eq. (1). They then analyze the limiting behavior at high frequency \(\omega\) by examining the exponential damping factor \(\mathcal{F}(t)\) and the formation time \(t_f\) of radiation.

## Key numbers
- For a charge with energy \(E = 50\) GeV, mass \(M = 4.5\) GeV, and \(\hat{q} = 1\) GeV\(^2\)/fm, the vacuum spectrum approaches the \(\omega\)-independent limit \(-\frac{d^2W}{dzd\omega} \simeq \frac{2\alpha\hat{q}}{3\pi M^2}\) for large \(\omega\).
- In an absorptive medium with \(m = 0\) and \(\Gamma = 10, 20, 30, 50, 100\) MeV (from top to bottom in Fig. 1 left panel), the full spectrum is significantly reduced compared to the vacuum result at small and intermediate \(\omega\).
- For \(\omega \gg \Gamma\) and \(m = 0\), the exponential damping factor becomes \(\mathcal{F}(t) \to \exp[-\Gamma\beta t(1-\hat{q} t/(6E^2))]\), which is \(\omega\)-independent in form but its influence varies with \(\omega\) via the formation time.
- The asymptotic vacuum limit can be recovered for large \(\omega\) only if \(E \ll \min(M^4/\hat{q}, M^2/\Gamma)\); for example, with \(M = 4.5\) GeV and \(\hat{q} = 1\) GeV\(^2\)/fm, this requires \(E \ll M^2/\Gamma\) for the damping condition.

## Context
This work extends the authors’ own earlier study (Bluhm (2011)) on polarization and absorption effects in a medium, using an Abelian approximation for colour charge dynamics in the quark-gluon plasma. It contrasts with prior treatments of medium-induced gluon radiation (Gyulassy (1994), Baier (1997), Baier (2000)) that neglected damping, and with studies of dielectric polarization (Kampfer (2000), Djordjevic (2003)). The results enable refined analysis of jet quenching in high-energy nuclear collisions, but the authors note that non-abelian contributions from gluon rescatterings (Baier (1995)) are still missing.
