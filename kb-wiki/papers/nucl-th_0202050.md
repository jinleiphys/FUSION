# Total reaction cross sections for neutron-nucleus scattering

> Neutron total reaction cross sections at 45, 50, 55, 60, 65, and 75 MeV from nuclei 12C, 28Si, 56Fe, 90Zr, and 208Pb have been measured and are compared with (microscopic) optical model predictions. The optical potentials were obtained in coordinate space by full folding effective nucleon-nucleon interactions with realistic nuclear ground state density matrices. Good to excellent agreement is found.

# Total reaction cross sections for neutron-nucleus scattering

## Key claim
Neutron total reaction cross sections predicted by a fully microscopic, nonlocal optical potential formed by folding effective nucleon-nucleon interactions with realistic nuclear ground state density matrices show good to excellent agreement with new experimental data, except that the predictions tend to be higher than the data for all five nuclei at 75 MeV and there is a notable discrepancy for 28Si where the results overpredict the data.

## Method
The authors measured neutron total reaction cross sections at six energies from 45 to 75 MeV for five target nuclei (12C, 28Si, 56Fe, 90Zr, 208Pb). They then constructed complex, nonlocal, coordinate-space optical potentials by full folding effective nucleon-nucleon interactions (derived from Bonn-B potentials via Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone equations) with nuclear ground state density matrices from credible structure models, and solved the associated nonlocal Schrodinger equations using the DWBA98 programs to obtain S-matrix elements and reaction cross sections.

## Key numbers
- Experimental neutron total reaction cross sections for 12C at 45 MeV: 0.435 barn (with uncertainty 0.039 barn)
- Experimental neutron total reaction cross sections for 208Pb at 45 MeV: 2.425 barn (with uncertainty 0.181 barn)
- Experimental neutron total reaction cross sections for 28Si at 55 MeV: 0.493 barn (with uncertainty 0.080 barn), where the largest discrepancy between prediction (0.713 barn) and experiment occurs
- Experimental neutron total reaction cross sections for 208Pb at 75 MeV: 1.922 barn (with uncertainty 0.131 barn), where predictions (2.075 barn for SHF, 2.108 barn for SKX) overestimate the data
- For 208Pb, two Skyrme-Hartree-Fock wave functions were used with a neutron skin of 0.17 fm

## Context
This work extends the microscopic nonlocal optical model approach previously applied to proton scattering by Amos (2000) and De Vismes (2001) to neutron scattering, using new experimental data from Ib (2002) and comparing with the LA-150 evaluated data library from Chadwick (1999). The authors note that earlier neutron reaction cross section predictions consistently overpredicted data by about 10 percent, but the present data show much better agreement, giving confidence that this fully microscopic approach can predict both neutron and proton reaction cross sections for applications ranging from transmutation of waste to nuclear astrophysics.
