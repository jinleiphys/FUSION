# Analysis of the low-energy $η$NN-dynamics within a three-body formalism

> The interaction of an $η$-meson with two nucleons is studied within a three-body approach. The major features of the $ηNN$-system in the low-energy region are accounted for by using a s-wave separable ansatz for the two-body $ηN$- and $NN$-amplitudes. The calculation is confined to the $(J^π;T)=(0^-;1)$ and $(1^-;0)$ configurations which are assumed to be the most promising candidates for virtual or resonant $ηNN$-states. The eigenvalue three-body equation is continued analytically into the nonphysical sheets by contour deformation. The position of the poles of the three-body scattering matrix as a function of the $ηN$-interaction strength is investigated. The corresponding trajectory, starting on the physical sheet, moves around the $ηNN$ three-body threshold and continues away from the physical area giving rise to virtual $ηNN$-states. The search for poles on the nonphysical sheets adjacent directly to the upper rim of the real energy axis gives a negative result. Thus no low-lying s-wave $ηNN$-resonances were found. The possible influence of virtual poles on the low-energy $ηNN$-scattering is discussed.

# Analysis of the low-energy $\eta$NN-dynamics within a three-body formalism

## Key claim

The authors find no low-lying s-wave $\eta NN$-resonances in either the $(J^\pi;T)=(0^-;1)$ or $(1^-;0)$ configurations, despite searching for poles on the nonphysical sheets adjacent to the upper rim of the real energy axis; instead, only virtual $\eta NN$-states are found, with the pole trajectories moving around the $\eta NN$ three-body threshold and continuing away from the physical area.

## Method

The authors use a three-body Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas formalism with s-wave separable rank-one potentials for both the $\eta N$ and $NN$ two-body amplitudes, where the $\eta N$ interaction is modeled via the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance with coupling to both $\eta N$ and $\pi N$ channels. The eigenvalue three-body equation is continued analytically into nonphysical Riemann sheets by contour deformation of the integration path, and the positions of poles of the three-body scattering matrix are investigated as a function of the $\eta N$-interaction strength. The calculation is confined to the $(J^\pi;T)=(0^-;1)$ and $(1^-;0)$ configurations with total orbital angular momentum $L=0$.

## Key numbers

- For the $(1^-;0)$ channel with the $\pi NN$ channel switched off ($g_{N^*}^{(\pi)}=0$) and $g_{N^*}^{(\eta)}=4$, a bound state pole is found at $W_{pol}=W_{\eta NN}-8.08$ MeV.
- For the physical value $g_{N^*}^{(\pi)}=1.5$, the trajectory crosses the real axis at $W \approx W_{\eta NN}+1.51$ MeV.
- The $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance parameters used are: mass $M^*=1535$ MeV, total width $\Gamma=150$ MeV, with $\Gamma_{\eta N}/\Gamma = \Gamma_{\pi N}/\Gamma = 0.5$.
- The $NN$ interaction parameters are taken from Yamaguchi: $\beta_d=1.4488$ fm$^{-1}$, with scattering lengths $a=5.378$ fm for the $^3S_1$-state and $a=-23.690$ fm for the $^1S_0$-state.
- The $\eta N$ vertex parameters are: $g_{N^*}^{(\eta)}=2.0$, $g_{N^*}^{(\pi)}=1.5$, $\beta_{N^*}^{(\eta)}=6.5$ fm$^{-1}$, $\beta_{N^*}^{(\pi)}=4.5$ fm$^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends prior three-body calculations of the $\eta d$-system by Ueda (who found a quasi-bound state at 2430 MeV with width 10-20 MeV), Shevchenko et al. (who confirmed qualitatively Ueda's results and fixed $\eta N$-scattering lengths for which the bound state becomes a low-lying s-wave resonance), and Garcilazo and Peña (who studied $\eta d$ bound states with different interaction models). The present paper uses a fundamentally different method based on searching for poles of the scattering matrix in the complex energy plane, which provides additional insights into the source of pole appearance near the physical domain. The authors also investigate the $(J^\pi;T)=(0^-;1)$ state not previously studied, which plays a dominant role in the final state of the $\gamma d\to\eta X$ reaction near threshold as established in Fix and Arenhoevel (2000). The results suggest that the anomalous enhancement of the $\gamma d\to\eta X$ cross section reported by Metag et al. cannot be explained by a near-lying $S$-matrix pole in the $\eta d$ final state, as this would require an enhancement factor of about 30.
