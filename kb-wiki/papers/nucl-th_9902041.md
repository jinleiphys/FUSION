# Decay of Hypernuclei

> We present a nonrelativistic transition potential for the weak strangeness-changing reaction $ΛN \to NN$. The potential is based on a one meson exchange model (OME), where, in addition to the long-ranged pion, the exchange of the pseudoscalar $K, η$, as well as the vector $ρ, ω, K^*$ mesons is considered. Results obtained for different hypernuclear decay observables are compared to the available experimental data.

# Decay of Hypernuclei

## Key claim

The nonrelativistic one-meson exchange (OME) transition potential for the weak strangeness-changing reaction ΛN → NN, including pseudoscalar (π, K, η) and vector (ρ, ω, K*) mesons, reproduces total nonmesonic decay rates within 15% of the pion-only value, but the neutron-to-proton ratio (Γn/Γp) and the intrinsic asymmetry parameter aΛ are highly sensitive to the inclusion of heavier mesons and to the choice of strong coupling constants, with the Γn/Γp ratio remaining significantly below newer central experimental values.

## Method

The authors constructed a nonrelativistic OME transition potential in momentum space from the free-space Feynman amplitude, incorporating weak parity-violating and parity-conserving couplings derived from soft-meson reduction, SU(3) symmetry, SU(6)w relations, and the pole model. They solved a T-matrix scattering equation in momentum space for the final NN state using the Nijmegen NN potential, and used a correlated ΛN wave function obtained by multiplying harmonic oscillator solutions with a spin-independent correlation function fitted to microscopic G-matrix calculations. Calculations were performed for the ¹²ΛC hypernucleus and other species within a shell-model framework using spectroscopic factors.

## Key numbers

- The Fermi weak coupling constant is G_F m_π² = 2.21 × 10⁻⁷.
- The free Λ decay rate is Γ_Λ = 3.8 × 10⁹ s⁻¹.
- The total nonmesonic decay rate for ¹²ΛC changes by at most 15% relative to the pion-only value when all mesons are included, depending on the choice of strong couplings (Nijmegen vs. Jülich).
- Including K-exchange reduces the Γn/Γp ratio by more than a factor of three relative to the pion-only value; using Nijmegen strong couplings gives a final ratio 34% smaller than the pion-only ratio.
- The intrinsic asymmetry parameter aΛ for ¹²ΛC is reduced by more than a factor of two from the pion-only value when ρ-exchange is included, and increases by about 30% to 50% when all other mesons are added, depending on the strong couplings.
- The measured aΛ for ⁵ΛHe is 0.22 ± 0.20, in disagreement with the theoretical predictions.
- The cross section for the pn → pΛ reaction near threshold is predicted to be of order 10⁻¹² mb.

## Context

This work extends the OME approach to hypernuclear weak decay beyond the long-standing pion-exchange-only models (McKellar, 1970s) by including all ground-state pseudoscalar and vector meson octet members, and addresses the long-standing "Γn/Γp puzzle" where OPE alone gives too small a ratio. The results contradict earlier expectations that heavier mesons would dramatically increase Γn/Γp, instead finding destructive interference that reduces it. The paper enables future experimental tests at KEK, BNL, and DAΦNE (FINUDA) to constrain weak couplings, and motivates improved YN potentials (Nijmegen, Jülich) and chiral perturbation theory calculations of weak NNK couplings (Savage and Springer, 1998). The work also connects to direct quark model approaches (Oka et al., 1998) and three-nucleon emission studies (Ramos et al., 1997) as alternative routes to resolving the Γn/Γp puzzle.
