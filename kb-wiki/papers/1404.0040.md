# An Overview of the Anomalous Soft Photons in Hadron Production

> Productions of soft photon with low transverse momenta in high-energy hadron-hadron collisions and $e^+$-$e^-$ annihilations indicate that they are consistently produced in excess of what are predicted by electromagnetic bremsstrahlung when hadrons (mostly mesons) are produced, but they agree with electromagnetic bremsstrahlung predictions in the absence of hadron production. These excess soft photons are called anomalous soft photons. The occurrence of anomalous soft photons in association with hadron production reveals the presence of additional QED soft photon sources in QCD hadron production. Many different models have been proposed to explain the anomalous soft production. We shall examine specifically a quantum field theory of simultaneous meson and soft photon production in QCDxQED in which the meson production arises from the oscillation of color charge densities of the quarks of the underlying vacuum in a flux tube. As a quark carries both a color charge and an electric charge, the oscillation of the color charge densities will be accompanied by the oscillation of electric charge densities, which will in turn lead to the simultaneous production of anomalous soft photons during the meson production process.

# An Overview of the Anomalous Soft Photons in Hadron Production

## Key claim
Soft photons with low transverse momenta are consistently produced in excess of electromagnetic bremsstrahlung predictions when hadrons (mostly mesons) are produced in high-energy hadron-hadron collisions and e+e- annihilations, but they agree with bremsstrahlung predictions in the absence of hadron production. The paper examines a specific quantum field theory of simultaneous meson and soft photon production in QCDxQED where meson production arises from oscillation of color charge densities of quarks in a flux tube, which is accompanied by oscillation of electric charge densities leading to simultaneous anomalous soft photon production.

## Method
The author reviews experimental data on anomalous soft photon production from multiple experiments (K+p, pi+p, pi-p, pp collisions, and e+e- annihilations) and examines a quantum field theory approach using bosonization of QCD2xQED2 for quarks with two flavors in a flux tube. The theory compactifies the four-dimensional QCDxQED system to two dimensions under longitudinal dominance and transverse confinement, then uses bosonization to describe color and electric charge density oscillations that produce both mesons and photons simultaneously.

## Context
This work reviews experimental data from multiple collaborations including CERN experiments WA27 (1984), NA22 (1993, 1997), WA83 (1997), WA91 (2002), WA102 (2002), and DELPHI (2006-2010) that consistently show anomalous soft photon excess. The paper extends earlier theoretical proposals including the cold quark-gluon plasma model of Van Hove and Lichard (1989), the pion condensate model of Barshay (1989), the string fragmentation model of Czyz and Florkowski (1994), and the synchrotron radiation model of Nachtmann et al. (1994). The specific quantum field theory approach builds on the work of Casher, Kogut, and Susskind (1974) on meson production from color charge density oscillations, and aims to explain the DELPHI observation that anomalous soft photon yield increases much faster with neutral particle multiplicity than with charged particle multiplicity, which the author notes cannot be explained by almost all existing models.
