# A Comment on the Radiative Decays of the $Λ(1405)$

> We examine the radiative decays of the Lambda (1405) to octet baryons, Lambda + gamma and Sigma + gamma. In the limit of SU(3) symmetry the decay rates for these two modes are related, and we compute the leading correction to the relation in chiral perturbation theory. Such measurements will allow the sign of the strong coupling $g_{Λ^*}$ to be determined. The SU(3) violating radiative decay to the baryon decuplet Sigma* + gamma dominated by one-loop graphs is also discussed. Experiments planned for the Jefferson Laboratory should be able to measure some, if not all, of the processes considered.

## Key claim

In the limit of exact SU(3) flavor symmetry, the radiative decay rates of the Lambda(1405) to Lambda gamma and Sigma^0 gamma are related, and the leading corrections to this relation can be computed using chiral perturbation theory; however, the individual decay rates themselves cannot be computed directly from QCD, and the chiral perturbation theory calculation assumes the Lambda(1405) is a fundamental SU(3) singlet field.

## Method

The authors treat the Lambda(1405) as an SU(3) singlet field with spin-parity J^pi = 1/2^- and incorporate it into a chiral Lagrangian. They compute the one-loop contributions from octet baryons and pseudo-Goldstone bosons to the E1 radiative decay amplitudes for Lambda(1405) -> Lambda gamma and Lambda(1405) -> Sigma^0 gamma, using the axial coupling constants F=0.4 and D=0.6 from semileptonic octet baryon decays and the strong coupling |g_{Lambda*}| = 0.40 +/- 0.04 determined from the Lambda(1405) width. They also compute the one-loop contribution to the SU(3) violating decay Lambda(1405) -> Sigma^{*0} gamma.

## Key numbers

- The strong coupling of the Lambda(1405) to octet baryons is |g_{Lambda*}| = 0.40 +/- 0.04, determined from the Lambda(1405) width at loop level (Savage 1994).
- The chiral perturbation theory result for the SU(3) breaking quantity R (defined as (|kappa_Sigma|^2 - 3|kappa_Lambda|^2) / (sqrt(3) sqrt(|kappa_Sigma|^2 + |kappa_Lambda|^2))) is R = -0.075, obtained using F=0.4, D=0.6, g_{Lambda*} = +0.4, and Lambda_chi = 1 GeV.
- The one-loop contribution to the Lambda(1405) -> Sigma^{*0} gamma amplitude is kappa_{10} ~ 0.28 - 0.25 i, giving a crude radiative decay width estimate of Gamma(Sigma^{*0} gamma) ~ 2.5 x 10^{-2} keV and branching fraction Br ~ 5 x 10^{-7} (using E_gamma = 19.9 MeV).
- The meson decay constant used is f = 132 MeV, with f_K ~ 1.22 f_pi.

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of kaon photo-production and radiative capture by Williams, Ji, and Cotanch (1991), which extracted a model-dependent measure of SU(3) breaking in these decays. The paper provides a chiral perturbation theory framework for computing SU(3) breaking corrections to the SU(3) symmetry relation between the two radiative decay modes, enabling comparison with planned high-precision measurements at CEBAF (experiment E89-024) that will detect ~10^3 events for the Lambda(1405) -> Lambda gamma decay. The results can help determine whether the Lambda(1405) can be treated as a fundamental SU(3) singlet field in chiral perturbation theory or whether it has substantial extent consistent with an NK bound state picture, as suggested by Kaiser, Waas, and Weise (1996).
