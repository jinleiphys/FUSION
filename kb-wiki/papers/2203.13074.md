# Theory of Halo Nuclei

> Halo nuclei are characterized by a few weakly bound halo nucleons and a more tightly bound core. This separation of scales can be exploited in a few-body description of halo nuclei, since the detailed structure of the core is not resolved by the halo nucleons. We present an introduction to the effective (field) theory for low-energy properties of halo nuclei. The focus is on halos with S-wave interactions for which universal properties are most pronounced. The special role of the unitary limit is illustrated using the example of multineutron systems and the Efimov effect as a universal binding mechanism for halo nuclei. Connections to ultracold atoms and hadron physics are highlighted and extensions to higher partial waves, Coulomb forces and nuclear reactions are briefly touched upon.

# Theory of Halo Nuclei

## Key claim

Halo nuclei can be described by an effective field theory (Halo EFT) that exploits the separation of scales between weakly bound halo nucleons and a tightly bound core, organizing the description in a controlled expansion around the unitary limit. The approach is not meant to replace *ab initio* methods but rather to provide universal relations between different halo observables, with the caveat that the halo nucleons are treated as distinguishable from core nucleons, introducing an error governed by the Halo EFT expansion parameter.

## Method

The author constructs a non-relativistic effective field theory for halo nuclei using short-range contact interactions between the core and valence nucleons, analogous to pionless EFT for light nuclei. For S-wave interactions, the two-body scattering amplitude is obtained by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a momentum cutoff regularization, and the coupling constants are fixed by matching to the effective range expansion. The three-body system requires a three-body parameter for proper renormalization, leading to the Efimov effect and discrete scaling symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The two-neutron separation energy of $^6$He is about 1 MeV, small compared to the $^4$He core binding and excitation energies of about 28 and 20 MeV, respectively
- The deuteron binding energy is described by the universal formula $B_2 = 1/(m a^2)$ to within 35% accuracy, improving to 12% accuracy when the leading range correction is included
- The Efimov discrete scaling factor is $\lambda_0 = e^{\pi/s_0}$ with $s_0 = 1.00624$
- The Halo EFT breakdown scale $M_{\rm core}$ varies between 50 and 150 MeV depending on the system
- For the deuteron, the expansion parameter $M_{\rm halo}/M_{\rm core} \approx 0.33$, with $M_{\rm halo} \sim \gamma_{nc} = 45.7$ MeV
- The deuteron spin-triplet scattering parameters are $a_{\trip} = 5.4112(15)$ fm and $r_{\trip} = 1.7436(19)$ fm
- The deuteron ANC is $A_{\trip} = 1.295$, and the structure radius in the point-nucleon limit is 1.977 fm
- For $^{11}$Be: $S_{1n} = 0.50164(25)$ MeV, $\langle r^2 \rangle_{nc}^{1/2} = 6.05(23)$ fm (experimental), $r_{nc}/a_{nc} = 0.38$, $A_{nc}/A_{nc,LO} = 1.44$
- For $^{19}$C: $S_{1n} = 0.58(9)$ MeV, $\langle r^2 \rangle_{nc}^{1/2} = 6.6(5)$ fm (experimental), $r_{nc}/a_{nc} = 0.33$, $A_{nc}/A_{nc,LO} = 1.3$

## Context

This work provides a pedagogical introduction to Halo EFT, extending the framework of pionless EFT for light nuclei (Bedaque 2002, Epelbaum 2008, Hammer 2019) to halo systems with resonant S-wave interactions. It builds on the cluster model approach (Ikeda 1968, Horiuchi 1986, Freer 2007) but organizes effects into a controlled expansion with quantified uncertainties. The review enables systematic calculations of halo properties and universal relations that can be combined with *ab initio* inputs or experimental data, with connections to ultracold atoms near Feshbach resonances and hadronic molecules in particle physics (Braaten 2006, Naidon 2016, Guo 2017).
