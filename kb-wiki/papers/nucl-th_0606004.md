# Elliptic flow and system size dependence of transition energies at intermediate energies

> The elliptic flow for $Z\le2$ particles in heavy ion collisions at energies from several tens to several hundreds MeV per nucleon is investigated by means of transport model,i.e. a new version of the Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics model (ImQMD05). In this model, a complete Skyrme potential energy density functional is employed. The influence of different effective interactions and medium corrections of nucleon-nucleon cross sections on the elliptic flow are studied. Our results show that a soft nuclear equation of state and incident energy dependent in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections are required for describing the excitation function of the elliptic flow at intermediate energies. The size dependence of transition energies for the elliptic flow at intermediate energies is also studied. The system size dependence of transition energies fits a power of system size with a exponent of 0.223.

## Key claim

A soft nuclear equation of state and incident-energy-dependent in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections are required to describe the excitation function of elliptic flow at intermediate energies, but the specific parameter \(\eta\) in the phenomenological density-dependent cross-section formula must itself depend on the relative momentum of the colliding pair and the medium density and temperature, rather than being a constant.

## Method

The authors used the Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics model (ImQMD05), which incorporates a complete Skyrme potential energy density functional (excluding the spin-orbit term) and an explicit momentum-dependent term, to simulate heavy-ion collisions for systems from \(^{58}\text{Ni}+^{58}\text{Ni}\) to \(^{197}\text{Au}+^{197}\text{Au}\) at beam energies from several tens to several hundreds of MeV per nucleon. They varied the Skyrme interaction (SkP, SkM*, SLy7, SIII) and the parameter \(\eta\) in the phenomenological in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section \(\sigma_{nn}^* = (1 - \eta \rho / \rho_0) \sigma_{nn}^{\text{free}}\) to compare calculated elliptic flow \(v_2\) for \(Z \le 2\) particles at mid-rapidity with experimental data from FOPI, INDRA, and ALADIN collaborations.

## Key numbers

- The transition energy from positive to negative elliptic flow for \(^{197}\text{Au}+^{197}\text{Au}\) at reduced impact parameter \(b/b_{\text{max}} = 0.38\) is around 100 MeV per nucleon, as confirmed by experiment.
- The difference in calculated transition energies among the four Skyrme interactions (SkP, SkM*, SLy7, SIII) is more than 30 MeV per nucleon.
- For SkP and SkM*, the maximal negative elliptic flow occurs around 400 AMeV; for SLy7 and SIII, it occurs around 250 AMeV.
- At 400 AMeV, a value of \(\eta \approx -0.4\) (i.e., in-medium cross section larger than free) is needed to match experimental elliptic flow.
- The system size dependence of transition energies for symmetric reactions (from \(^{58}\text{Ni}+^{58}\text{Ni}\) to \(^{197}\text{Au}+^{197}\text{Au}\)) fits a power law \(E_{\text{tran}} = x (A_P + A_T)^{-\tau}\) with exponent \(\tau = 0.223\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of elliptic flow as a probe of the nuclear equation of state (EoS) and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections, notably the experimental measurements by the FOPI, INDRA, and ALADIN collaborations (Lukasik 2005, Andronic 2005) and earlier transport-model studies such as Danielewicz (2000) and Chen (2005). The finding that the in-medium cross section may be enhanced at higher incident energies (around 400 AMeV) is qualitatively consistent with predictions by Li (2000, 2004) for elastic nucleon-nucleon cross sections at super-normal densities. The exponent 0.223 for the system-size dependence of transition energies is substantially smaller than the exponent for balance energies of directed flow, indicating that more complex effects (expansion of the compressed zone, spectator shadowing) influence the sign change of elliptic flow.
