# The Relativistic Dirac-Brueckner Approach to Asymmetric Nuclear Matter

> The properties of asymmetric nuclear matter have been investigated in a relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock framework using the Bonn A potential. The components of the self-energies are extracted by projecting on Lorentz invariant amplitudes. Furthermore, the optimal representation scheme for the $T$ matrix, the subtracted $T$ matrix representation, is applied and the results are compared to those of other representation schemes. Of course, in the limit of symmetric nuclear matter our results agree with those found in literature. The binding energy $E_b$ fulfills the quadratic dependence on the asymmetry parameter and the symmetry energy is 34 MeV at saturation density. Furthermore, a neutron-proton effective mass splitting of $m_n^* < m_p^*$ is found. In addition, results are given for the mean-field effective coupling constants.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the subtracted T matrix representation is the optimal scheme for extracting Lorentz components of the nucleon self-energy in asymmetric nuclear matter within the relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock framework, as it avoids the spurious contributions from pseudoscalar admixtures that plague other representations, though the results are contingent on using the Bonn A potential and the reference spectrum approximation.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Bethe-Salpeter equation in the Thompson three-dimensional reduction for asymmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature, using the Bonn A potential for the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction and applying an angle-averaged Pauli operator for the neutron-proton channel with different Fermi spheres. They extracted the Lorentz components of the neutron and proton self-energies by projecting the on-shell T matrix onto Lorentz invariant amplitudes in four different representation schemes: pseudoscalar, conventional pseudovector, complete pseudovector, and subtracted T matrix representation. The self-consistency iteration employed the reference spectrum approximation, assuming the self-energy depends only on density (evaluated at the Fermi surface) rather than momentum.

## Key numbers

* Symmetry energy at saturation density: 34 MeV, extracted from the quadratic dependence of binding energy on the asymmetry parameter.
* The binding energy per nucleon satisfies a quadratic dependence on the asymmetry parameter (the difference between neutron and proton fractions), with the symmetry energy value of 34 MeV at saturation density.
* A neutron-proton effective mass splitting of m_n* < m_p* is found in asymmetric nuclear matter.

## Context

This work extends earlier relativistic Dirac-Brueckner calculations for symmetric nuclear matter by Anastasio (1983), Horowitz (1987), and Brockmann (1990) to the asymmetric case, where protons and neutrons occupy different Fermi spheres. It addresses the ambiguity in decomposing the on-shell T matrix into Lorentz invariant amplitudes, which was previously identified by Nuppenau (1989) and Sehn (1997), and builds on the subtracted T matrix representation developed by Gross (1999) for symmetric matter. The results enable more reliable predictions for neutron-rich systems such as neutron stars, supernova explosions, and neutron-rich nuclei studied at radioactive beam facilities, and provide a benchmark for comparing with nonrelativistic Brueckner calculations by Bombaci (1991) and Zuo (1999) as well as earlier relativistic calculations by Ter Haar (1987) and Engvik (1994, 1996).
