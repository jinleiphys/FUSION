# Unconventional superconductivity as a synchronization problem in nuclear oscillator networks

> We formulate the problem of unconventional $d-$wave superconductivity, with phase fluctuations, pseudogap phenomenon, and local Cooper pairs, in terms of a synchronization problem in random, quantum dissipative, elasto-nuclear oscillator networks. The nodes of the network correspond to {\it localized, collective quadrupolar vibrations} of nuclei-like, elastic inhomogeneities embedded in a dissipative medium. Electrons interacting with such vibrations form local Cooper pairs, with a superfluid $d-$wave pseudogap $Δ_{PG}$, due to an effective, short range attractive interaction of $d_{x^2-y^2}$ character. Phase coherent, bulk superconductivity, with a $d-$wave gap $Δ$, is stabilized when the oscillator network is asymptotically entangled in a nearly decoherence-free environment. Phase coherence will in turn be destroyed, at $T_c$, when the thermal noise becomes comparable to the coupling between oscillators, the superfluid density $K$. The $2Δ/k_B T_c$ ratio is a function of Kuramoto's order parameter, $r=\sqrt{1-K_c/K}$, for the loss of synchronization at $K_c$, and is much larger than the nonuniversal $2Δ_{PG}/k_B T^*$ ratio, where $T^*$ is the temperature at which $Δ_{PG}$ is completely destroyed by thermal fluctuations. We discuss our findings in connection to the available data for various unconventionally high-temperature superconductors.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that unconventional d-wave superconductivity, including the pseudogap phenomenon and phase fluctuations, can be understood as a synchronization problem in a network of localized quadrupolar oscillators representing elastic inhomogeneities in the material, with the crucial condition that bulk superconductivity emerges only when the oscillator network becomes asymptotically entangled in a nearly decoherence-free environment.

## Method

The authors model elastic inhomogeneities in the crystal as spherical insertions whose lowest-energy vibrational mode is the quadrupolar d_{x^2-y^2} deformation, derived from solving Navier's equation for elastic displacements. They quantize these quadrupolar vibrations and derive a particle-vibration coupling Hamiltonian, then use second-order perturbation theory to obtain an effective attractive two-particle interaction that produces local Cooper pairs with a d-wave pseudogap. The network of coupled oscillators is treated using the Kuramoto model, where the superfluid density K acts as the coupling strength between oscillators, and phase coherence (bulk superconductivity) emerges when K exceeds a critical value K_c determined by quenched disorder and thermal noise.

## Key numbers

- The pseudogap-to-T* ratio is given as 2Δ_PG/k_B T* = 3.53/(⟨η²⟩_FS)^{1/2} w(k_F), which is nonuniversal and can be as large as 8 (citing Yazdani 2007)
- The local pseudogap at T=0 is Δ_PG = (2ħΩ)/(⟨η²⟩_FS)^{1/2} w(k_F) × exp{-1/(λ⟨η²⟩_FS w²(k_F))}
- The temperature T* at which the pseudogap is destroyed is k_B T* = 1.13 ħΩ × exp{-1/(λ⟨η²⟩_FS w²(k_F))}
- Kuramoto's order parameter is r = √(1 - K_c/K), where K_c depends on the quenched spread δ and thermal noise k_B T
- The bulk gap at T=0 is Δ = (2ħΩ_σ)/(r(δ,0) ⟨η²⟩_FS^{1/2}) × exp{-1/(r²(δ,0) λ̄ ⟨η²⟩_FS)}

## Context

This work extends the concept of nuclear superfluidity from finite nuclei (Barranco 1999) to condensed matter systems by modeling elastic inhomogeneities as quadrupolar oscillators analogous to nuclear surface vibrations. The synchronization approach using the Kuramoto model (Kuramoto 1975, 1987) provides a framework connecting the superfluid density K to phase coherence, enabling a description of the pseudogap phase as a regime of unsynchronized local Cooper pairs and bulk superconductivity as a synchronized state. The results are discussed in connection to experimental data from scanning tunneling microscopy on Bi₂Sr₂CaCu₂O_{8+δ} (Yazdani 2007) and other high-temperature superconductors.
