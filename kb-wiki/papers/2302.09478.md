# Bayesian quantification of strongly-interacting matter with color glass condensate initial conditions

> A global Bayesian analysis of relativistic Pb + Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}_{\rm NN}$ = 2.76 TeV is performed, using a multistage model consisting of an IP-Glasma initial state, a viscous fluid dynamical evolution, and a hadronic transport final state. The observables considered are from the soft sector hadronic final state. Posterior and Maximum a Posteriori parameter distributions that pertain to the IP-Glasma and hydrodynamic phases are obtained, including the shear and bulk specific viscosity of strong interacting matter. The first use of inference with transfer learning in heavy-ion analyses is presented, together with Bayes Model Averaging.

## Key claim

This Bayesian analysis extracts the temperature-dependent shear and bulk viscosity of strongly-interacting matter from Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV using IP-Glasma initial conditions rather than the more commonly used T_RENTo plus freestreaming model, and finds that the extracted transport coefficients depend on the choice of viscous correction scheme at particlization, with the first application of transfer learning to heavy-ion Bayesian inference.

## Method

A multistage model was used: IP-Glasma for the pre-equilibrium initial state, MUSIC for second-order relativistic viscous hydrodynamics with parametrized shear and bulk viscosity, iS3D for particlization via the Cooper-Frye prescription, and SMASH for the hadronic cascade final state. Bayesian inference was performed using Gaussian process emulators as surrogate models for the expensive simulations, with transfer learning applied to switch between Grad's 14-moment and Chapman-Enskog relaxation time approximation viscous corrections at particlization. The analysis used a Generalized Normal prior distribution with shape parameter beta to smoothly interpolate between uniform and Gaussian priors.

## Key numbers

- Collision system: Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- The IP-Glasma to hydrodynamics switching time tau_0 was allowed up to approximately 1.2 fm, informed by longer hydrodynamic onset times from freestreaming studies
- The strong coupling was fixed to g = 2, compatible with LHC heavy-ion phenomenology
- The particlization temperature T_sw and four parameters for shear viscosity (eta/s)_kink, a_eta_low, a_eta_high, T_eta_kink were varied in the Bayesian analysis
- The equation of state smoothly connects HotQCD lattice calculations at high temperature to a list of stable resonances at low temperature, with a crossover transition at approximately 150 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian extractions of QCD transport coefficients (Bernhard 2019, SIMPSON collaboration 2021, Nijs 2021) by replacing the T_RENTo plus freestreaming initial state model with IP-Glasma, which has microscopic support from the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory. The use of transfer learning between viscous correction schemes (Grad's 14-moment and Chapman-Enskog RTA) is novel for heavy-ion analyses and reduces the required training points by an order of magnitude compared to training a new surrogate model. This enables future systematic studies of how initial state modeling choices affect extracted transport coefficients, and the framework can be extended to other collision systems and energies.
