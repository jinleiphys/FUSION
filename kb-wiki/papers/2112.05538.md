# First Lattice QCD Study of Proton Twist-3 GPDs

> We present first results on selected twist-3 quark GPDs using the quasi-distribution method. This approach relates lattice QCD data and light-cone distribution functions using Large Momentum Effective Theory (LaMET). We calculate quark-antiquark correlators of boosted nucleons coupled to non-local operators with vector and axial Dirac structure, which is transverse to the momentum boost. We use three values of the momentum boost, namely 0.83, 1.25, 1.67 GeV. The GPDs are defined in the symmetric (Breit) frame, which we implement here with 4-vector momentum transfer squared of 0, 0.69 and 1.39 GeV 2 , all at zero skewness. The calculation is performed using one ensemble of two degenerate light, a strange and a charm quark ($N_f$ = 2 + 1 + 1) of maximally twisted mass fermions with a clover term, corresponding to a pion mass of 260 MeV.

# First Lattice QCD Study of Proton Twist-3 GPDs

## Key claim

This work presents the first lattice QCD calculation of selected twist-3 generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of the proton using the quasi-distribution method, but only for the axial twist-3 GPDs at zero skewness; the vector twist-3 GPDs cannot yet be matched to light-cone distributions because the matching kernel is not known, and the calculation neglects mixing with quark-gluon-quark correlators.

## Method

The authors compute quark-antiquark correlators of boosted nucleons coupled to non-local operators with transverse vector and axial Dirac structures, using the quasi-distribution method within Large Momentum Effective Theory (LaMET). Matrix elements are extracted from plateau fits to ratios of three-point and two-point functions on a single $N_f = 2+1+1$ ensemble of maximally twisted mass fermions with a clover term, at a pion mass of 260 MeV and lattice spacing $a \simeq 0.093$ fm. The GPDs are defined in the symmetric (Breit) frame at zero skewness, and the $x$-dependence is reconstructed using the model-independent Backus-Gilbert method; for the axial GPDs, a one-loop matching kernel from twist-3 PDFs is applied to obtain light-cone distributions.

## Key numbers

- Three values of the nucleon momentum boost: 0.83, 1.25, and 1.67 GeV.
- Momentum transfer squared values: $t = 0$, $-0.69$, and $-1.39$ GeV$^2$, all at zero skewness.
- Pion mass of the ensemble: $M_\pi = 260$ MeV; lattice spacing $a \simeq 0.093$ fm; volume $32^3 \times 64$.
- Source-sink time separation: $t_s = 10a$.
- Statistics: up to 219 configurations with 32 source positions per configuration, yielding 56064 total measurements (including factor of 8 from momentum averaging) for the highest boost $P_3 = 1.67$ GeV at $t = -0.69$ GeV$^2$.
- For the axial GPDs, the matrix element $\Pi(\gamma^1 \gamma^5, \Gamma_3)$ (proportional to $\widetilde{G}_3$) is compatible with zero; $\widetilde{G}_4$ has zero real part for $z \le 6a$.

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD program for $x$-dependent parton distributions from leading-twist (twist-2) GPDs, previously studied by the same collaboration (Alexandrou et al., 2020), to the higher-twist (twist-3) sector, which is necessary for proton tomography and spin-orbit correlations (Lorce). The calculation uses the same quasi-distribution/LaMET framework as prior work on unpolarized, helicity, and transversity GPDs (Alexandrou et al., 2020, 2021). The results enable future comparisons with experimental extractions from hard exclusive processes like DVCS, where twist-3 contributions provide power corrections, and motivate development of the missing matching kernel for vector twist-3 GPDs and extension to nonzero skewness.
