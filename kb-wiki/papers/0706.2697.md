# Fluctuations of the chiral condensate and quasi-particle spectra near phase transition

> We investigate the quark spectrum near but above the critical temperature of the chiral transition, taking into account the precursory soft modes. It is found that there appear novel excitation spectra of quasi-quarks and quasi-antiquarks with a three-peak structure. By a detailed analysis on the formation of the three-peak structure using Yukawa models, it is shown that the new quark spectra originate from the mixing between a quark (anti-quark) and an antiquark hole (quark hole) caused by a resonant scattering of the quasi-quark with the soft modes which have a small but finite excitation energy with a small width near the critical temperature.

## Key claim

The quark spectral function near but above the critical temperature of the chiral transition exhibits a three-peak structure at low momentum and low frequency, but this structure only appears when the precursory soft modes of the chiral transition have a nonzero effective mass \(m_\sigma^*(T)\); the three peaks vanish in the high-temperature limit where the boson mass becomes negligible relative to temperature.

## Method

The authors first computed the quark spectral function using the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in the chiral limit, with the quark-antiquark polarization function evaluated in the random phase approximation and the quark self-energy constructed in a non-selfconsistent one-loop approximation. They then analyzed the mechanism of the three-peak structure using Yukawa models of a massless quark coupled to a massive scalar (pseudoscalar) or vector (axial-vector) boson at finite temperature, computing the retarded self-energy and spectral function at one-loop order in the imaginary time formalism.

## Key numbers

- The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with coupling constant \(G_S = 5.5\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\) and three-dimensional cutoff \(\Lambda = 631\ \mathrm{MeV}\) gives a second-order chiral phase transition at \(T_c = 193.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for vanishing quark chemical potential.
- The three-peak structure in the quark spectral function is clearly present at \(\varepsilon \equiv (T - T_c)/T_c = 0.1\) and persists up to \(\varepsilon = 0.2\).
- In the Yukawa model with a scalar boson, the imaginary part of the self-energy \(\mathrm{Im}\Sigma_+(\bm{p}=0,\omega)\) vanishes at \(\omega = 0,\ \pm m\) irrespective of temperature, and the Landau damping peaks appear in the regions \(-1 < \omega/m < 0\) and \(0 < \omega/m < 1\).
- The three-peak structure in the Yukawa model with a scalar boson is shown for \(T/m = 1.4\); for the vector boson case it appears at the lower temperature \(T/m = 1.2\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study Kitazawa (2005) which first reported the three-peak structure in the quark spectrum near the chiral transition, and builds on the identification of precursory soft modes above \(T_c\) by Hatsuda (1985). The analysis using Yukawa models clarifies that the mechanism is a resonant scattering of quasi-quarks off massive bosons, analogous to the resonant scattering mechanism in color superconductivity described by Kitazawa (2005) and Janko (1997), but with the crucial difference that the finite boson mass causes level repulsion at two distinct points in the energy-momentum plane rather than only at the origin. This contrasts with the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation for massless gauge bosons, which yields only two peaks in the quark spectrum as shown by Weldon (1989). The results enable future work incorporating finite quark mass effects, which the authors note may suppress the three-peak structure and is reported as under study.
