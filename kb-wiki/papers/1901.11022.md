# Modeling neutrino-nucleus interactions for neutrino oscillation experiments

> We present our recent progress on the relativistic modeling of neutrino-nucleus reactions for their implementation in MonteCarlo event generators (GENIE, NEUT) employed in neutrino oscillation experiments. We compare charged-current neutrino and antineutrino cross sections obtained within the SuSAv2 model, which is based on the Relativistic Mean Field theory and on the analysis of the superscaling behavior exhibited by ($e,e'$) data. We also evaluate and discuss the impact of multi-nucleon excitations arising from 2p-2h states excited by the action of weak forces in a fully relativistic framework, showing for the first time their implementation in GENIE and their comparison with recent T2K data.

## Key claim
The SuSAv2-MEC model, which combines relativistic mean-field theory with multi-nucleon (2p-2h) excitations, can be implemented in Monte Carlo event generators like GENIE and NEUT to reduce experimental systematic uncertainties in neutrino oscillation experiments from the current 5-10% level by 2-3%, potentially halving required detector volume or exposure time.

## Method
The authors implemented the SuSAv2 model (based on Relativistic Mean Field theory and superscaling analysis of electron scattering data) and fully relativistic 2p-2h meson-exchange current (MEC) calculations into the GENIE event generator. They compared charged-current neutrino and antineutrino cross sections on carbon-12 against T2K CC0π and CC0πNp data, including pion absorption effects modeled by GENIE.

## Key numbers
- Current experimental systematic uncertainties in long-baseline neutrino experiments are in the region of 5-10%, with 3-4% related to flux and cross section predictions
- A reduction of 2-3% on these uncertainties would allow halving either experimental exposure or detector volume
- T2K CC0πNp data on 12C for events with 0 protons above 500 MeV/c were compared with the SuSAv2-MEC model
- T2K CC0π data on 12C were compared with the full SuSAv2-MEC model (QE+2p2h)

## Context
This work extends the SuSAv2-MEC approach previously developed by Megias (2016) and Ivanov (2014) for neutrino-nucleus interactions, implementing it for the first time in the GENIE event generator used by T2K and other oscillation experiments. The model enables reduction of nuclear-related systematic uncertainties in current experiments (T2K) and future long-baseline facilities, while also aiding supernova neutrino analysis and searches for sterile neutrinos and proton decay.
