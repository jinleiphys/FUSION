# Chiral Symmetry, Landau Fermi Liquid And Dense Hadronic Matter

> I describe how effective chiral Lagrangian field theories work for nuclei and nuclear matter by going from the deuteron to dense hadronic matter via BR scaling and Landau Fermi-liquid fixed point parameters, with possible applications to relativistic heavy-ion processes and compact ``nuclear stars.''

## Key claim

Effective chiral Lagrangian field theories, when combined with Brown-Rho (BR) scaling and Landau Fermi-liquid fixed point theory, can describe nuclear phenomena from the deuteron to dense hadronic matter, but the BR scaling relation should be considered as a parameterization within a specific theory rather than as directly physical masses and constants.

## Method

The author develops a theoretical framework that starts with an effective chiral Lagrangian for nucleons and pions in matter-free space, applies chiral perturbation theory to compute two-nucleon processes like radiative np capture, then extends the approach to dense matter by introducing BR scaling where hadron masses and couplings scale with density via the in-medium quark condensate. The BR-scaled chiral Lagrangian is treated at mean-field level and mapped onto Landau Fermi-liquid theory, using renormalization-group decimation around the Fermi surface to relate the scaling parameter Φ to the nucleon effective mass and Landau parameters.

## Key numbers

- Thermal np capture cross section: experimental value σ_exp = 334.2 ± 0.5 mb; single-particle matrix element alone gives σ_imp = 305.6 mb (about 9% too low); chiral Lagrangian calculation gives σ_theory = 334 ± 3 mb
- BR scaling parameter at nuclear matter density ρ₀: Φ(ρ₀) = m_V^*/m_V = 0.78 ± 0.08 from QCD sum-rule calculation
- Predicted nucleon effective mass at ρ₀: m_N^*(ρ₀)/m_N ≈ 0.69, consistent with QCD sum-rule result 0.69^{+0.14}_{-0.07}
- Orbital gyromagnetic ratio isovector correction at ρ₀: δg_l = 0.23 τ₃, compared with δg_l^{proton} = 0.23 ± 0.03 from dipole sum rule
- Axial charge enhancement factor ε_MEC in heavy nuclei (lead region): 1.8 to 2.0 from Warburton's analysis; BR scaling calculation gives ε_MEC = 1.9 to 2.0
- Pionic contribution to Landau parameter F̃₁ at normal matter density: F̃₁^π ≈ -0.153

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to nuclear physics pioneered by Weinberg (1997) and applies it from the deuteron to dense matter, building on the chiral filter mechanism of Kubodera, Delorme, and Rho (1978) and the BR scaling framework of Brown and Rho (1991). The deuteron calculation uses the Argonne v₁₈ potential of Wiringa, Stoks, and Schiavilla (1995) for wave functions while computing exchange currents via chiral perturbation theory. The Landau Fermi-liquid mapping connects to Migdal's (1967) theory of nuclear matter and to Walecka's mean-field model as shown by Matsui (1981). The results enable predictions for kaon production in heavy-ion collisions (tested at GSI by the FOPI and KaoS collaborations) and for kaon condensation in compact stars at densities around 3ρ₀, with implications for neutron star maximum mass and black hole formation as discussed by Brown and Bethe (1994).
