# Dynamical view of pair creation in uniform electric and magnetic fields

> Pair creation in a uniform classical electromagnetic field (Schwinger mechanism) is studied focusing on the time evolution of the distribution of created particles. The time evolution of the distribution in time-dependent fields is also presented as well as effects of back reaction. Motivated by the Glasma flux tube, which may be formed at the initial stage of heavy-ion collisions, we investigate effects of a magnetic field parallel to an electric field, and find that the magnetic field makes the evolution of a fermion system faster.

## Key claim

The paper claims that by defining instantaneous positive and negative frequency solutions to decompose the field operator, one can reveal the longitudinal kinetic momentum distribution of particles created by a uniform electric field and its time evolution, resolving a long-standing paradox about boost invariance. The magnetic field parallel to the electric field is found to make the evolution of a fermion system faster, while suppressing boson pair creation.

## Method

The author performs canonical quantization of charged scalar (boson) and spinor (fermion) fields in a uniform classical electromagnetic field, using the gauge \(A^\mu = (0,0,0,-Et)\). Mode functions are expressed in terms of parabolic cylinder functions, and instantaneous positive and negative frequency solutions are introduced to define time-dependent creation and annihilation operators, enabling the computation of the time-evolving momentum distribution \(\langle 0,\mathrm{in} |a_\mathbf{p}^\dagger(t) a_\mathbf{p}(t) |0,\mathrm{in} \rangle\). The analysis is extended to non-steady electric fields and includes back reaction effects from the created particles.

## Key numbers

- The pair creation probability per unit volume and time for bosons in a pure electric field is given by \(n_\mathbf{p} = \exp(-\pi m_\mathrm{T}^2 / eE)\), where \(m_\mathrm{T}^2 = m^2 + p_x^2 + p_y^2\).
- The critical electric field for \(e^+ e^-\) pair creation is \(E_c = m^2/e \sim 10^{16}\) V/cm.
- In the Color Glass Condensate framework, the initial color field strength is \(gE \sim Q_s^2 \sim 1\) GeV\(^2\).
- The Bogoliubov coefficient for bosons is \(\beta_\mathbf{p} = i e^{-\pi a}\) with \(a = m_\mathrm{T}^2/(2eE)\), giving \(|\beta_\mathbf{p}|^2 = e^{-2\pi a}\).

## Context

This work extends the canonical quantization approach to the Schwinger mechanism developed by Nikishov (1970) and others, which previously only defined asymptotic in and out states and could not resolve the longitudinal momentum distribution or its time evolution. The study is motivated by the Glasma flux tube model from Color Glass Condensate (CGC) for heavy-ion collisions, and it enables future investigations of thermalization scenarios based on the analogy between the Schwinger mechanism and the Hawking-Unruh effect proposed by Kharzeev (2005, 2007). The paper also addresses the need to study effects of a longitudinal magnetic field, which had not been treated in systems with back reaction.
