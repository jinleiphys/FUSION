# Total reaction cross sections from $^{141}$141Pr($α$,$α$)$^{141}$Pr elastic scattering and $α$-induced reaction cross sections at low energies

> Elastic scattering data for $^{141}$Pr($α$,$α$)$^{141}$Pr have been analyzed to derive a new energy-dependent local potential for the $^{141}$Pr-$α$ system. This potential is used successfully to predict the cross section of the $^{141}$Pr($α$,n)$^{144}$Pm reaction at low energies where new experimental data have become available very recently. Contrary to various global potentials, this new potential is able to reproduce simultaneously elastic scattering data around and above the Coulomb barrier and reaction data below the Coulomb barrier for the $^{141}$Pr-$α$ system. Reasons for the partial failure of the global potentials are explained by intrinsic properties of the scattering matrix and their variation with energy. The new local potential may become the basis for the construction of a new global $α$-nucleus potential.

# Total reaction cross sections from 141Pr(α,α)141Pr elastic scattering and α-induced reaction cross sections at low energies

## Key claim

A new energy-dependent local potential for the 141Pr-α system, derived solely from elastic scattering data, can simultaneously reproduce elastic scattering angular distributions around and above the Coulomb barrier and reaction cross sections below the Coulomb barrier, contrary to various global potentials which fail to describe the low-energy reaction data.

## Method

Elastic scattering angular distributions for 141Pr(α,α)141Pr at laboratory energies of 19.0, 23.97, 32.0, 37.7, and 45.0 MeV were analyzed using a folding model for the real part of the nuclear potential and Woods-Saxon parametrizations for the imaginary part, with a surface-type imaginary potential at lower energies and a combination of volume and surface terms at higher energies. A model-independent phase shift analysis was also performed to extract total reaction cross sections with estimated uncertainties. The energy dependence of the imaginary volume integral was parametrized using a Fermi-type function, and the resulting potential was used to calculate total reaction cross sections and compare with experimental data for the 141Pr(α,n)144Pm and 141Pr(α,2n)143Pm reactions.

## Key numbers

- Total reaction cross sections from elastic scattering fits: 465 ± 31 mb at Elab = 19.0 MeV (Ec.m. = 18.48 MeV); 1018 ± 31 mb at 23.97 MeV; 1306 ± 39 mb at 32.0 MeV; 1654 ± 50 mb at 37.7 MeV; 1874 ± 56 mb at 45.0 MeV
- Fermi-type parametrization of imaginary volume integral: JI,0 = 74.16 MeV fm3, E0 = 17.41 MeV, aE = 2.42 MeV
- Alpha-decay preformation factor for 145Pm: P = 12.5 ± 2.7%, derived from experimental partial half-life of (2.0 ± 0.4) × 1017 s and Qα = 2322.2 ± 2.6 keV
- Astrophysical S-factor for 141Pr(α,n)144Pm decreases from approximately 3 × 1026 MeV b below 11 MeV to approximately 0.6 × 1026 MeV b at 14.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Sauerwein et al. (2011), who studied the 141Pr(α,n)144Pm reaction in a narrow energy window and found that global potentials by McFadden and Satchler (1966), Avrigeanu (2010), and Frohlich and Rauscher (2003) could not reproduce the new experimental data. The present study provides a consistent description of all available elastic scattering and reaction data over a broad energy range from 10 to 45 MeV, and the derived energy-dependent potential may become the basis for constructing a new global α-nucleus potential. The reduced cross sections for 141Pr-α follow the same smooth systematic behavior as other tightly bound projectiles on various targets, enabling future extension to a wider target range.
