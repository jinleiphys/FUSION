# Nuclear halo and the coherent nuclear interaction

> The unusual structure of Li11, the first halo nucleus found, is analyzed by the Preparata model of nuclear structure. By applying Coherent Nucleus Theory, we obtain an interaction potential for the halo-neutrons that rightly reproduces the fundamental state of the system.

# Nuclear halo and the coherent nuclear interaction

## Key claim
The authors claim that the two-neutron halo of ¹¹Li can be explained by a virtual interaction between the extra neutrons and the evanescent tail of a coherent pion condensate predicted by the Preparata Coherent Nucleus Theory, rather than by conventional potential models. The claim is conditional on the existence of this specific type of pion condensation, which the authors distinguish from the static incoherent pion condensate proposed by Migdal (1973).

## Method
The authors apply second-order perturbation theory to calculate a dispersive interaction potential between halo neutrons and the evanescent tail of the coherent pion field outside the ⁹Li core, using the non-relativistic reduction of the pseudoscalar πNN coupling with coupling constant g²/4π ≈ 14.3. For the two-neutron case in ¹¹Li, they consider the n-n system in a singlet spin state (S=0) and triplet isospin state (I=1), solve the effective Schrödinger equation for l=0 using an approximate exponential potential fitted to the exact form, and impose a boundary condition at the nuclear surface to account for the Pauli principle.

## Key numbers
- Two-neutron separation energy: E₂ₙ ≈ 300 keV, compared with the experimental value of 294 ± 30 keV from Bertsch and Esbensen (1991).
- Root mean square radius of the halo: rₕ ≈ 7.0 fm.
- Root mean square radius of the total ¹¹Li system: r_RMS ≈ 3.6 fm, compared with the experimental value of 3.55 ± 0.10 fm from Al Kahili, Tostevin, and Thompson (1996).
- Depth of the two-neutron interaction potential: V₀ ≈ 61.3 MeV.
- Minimum potential depth required for binding: (V₀)_min ≈ 48.5 MeV.
- One-neutron potential depth: (V₀)_n ≈ 46.5 MeV, which is below the minimum required for binding, consistent with ¹⁰Li being unbound.
- Renormalized pion frequency inside the nuclear medium: ωᵣ ≈ 100 MeV.
- Coherence Domain radius: R_CD ≈ 4.2 fm.
- Critical radius for exponentially decaying pion field: R_c ≈ 2.42 fm.
- ⁹Li core radius: R_A ≈ 2.5 fm.
- Decay constant of the evanescent pion field: λ ≈ 62 MeV.
- Nuclear matter density: ρ₀ ≈ 0.166 fm⁻³.

## Context
This work extends the Coherent Nucleus Theory of Preparata (1990, 1995), previously applied to the EMC nuclear effect, Coulomb sum rule, deep-inelastic scattering, hypernuclear interactions, low-energy photoabsorption, and neutron stars, to the problem of halo nuclei. The authors contrast their approach with standard potential models where neutrons occupy vacant states of an inert core potential and where pairing between neutrons is crucial for stability. The calculation reproduces the experimental two-neutron separation energy and root mean square radius of ¹¹Li from Bertsch and Esbensen (1991) and Al Kahili, Tostevin, and Thompson (1996), and explains why ¹⁰Li is unbound. The authors state this is the first step in a research program to analyze correlations in two-neutron halos within this framework.
