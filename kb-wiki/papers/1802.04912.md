# Chemical freezeout parameters within generic nonextensive statistics

> The particle production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions seems to be created in a dynamically disordered system which can be best described by an extended exponential entropy. In distinguishing between the applicability of this and Boltzmann-Gibbs (BG) in generating various particle-ratios, generic (non)extensive statistics (GNS) is introduced to the hadron resonance gas model. Accordingly, the degree of (non)extensivity is determined by the possible modifications in the phase space. Both BG extensivity and Tsallis nonextensivity are included as very special cases defined by specific values of the equivalence classes $(c, d)$. We found that the particle ratios at energies ranging between $3.8$ and $2760~$GeV are best reproduced by nonextensive statistics, where $c$ and $d$ range between $\sim0.9$ and $\sim1$. The present work aims at illustrating that the proposed approach is well capable to manifest the statistical nature of the system on interest. We don't aim at highlighting deeper physical insights. In other words, while the resulting nonextensivity is neither BG nor Tsallis, the freezeout parameters are found very compatible with BG and accordingly with the well-known freezeout phase-diagram, which is in an excellent agreement with recent lattice calculations. We conclude that the particle production is nonextensive but should not necessarily be accompanied by a radical change in the intensive or extensive thermodynamic quantities, such as internal energy and temperature. Only, the two critical exponents defining the equivalence classes $(c, d)$ are the physical parameters characterizing the (non)extensivity.

## Key claim

The paper claims that particle production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is nonextensive but does not require a radical change in thermodynamic quantities like temperature or internal energy; instead, the nonextensivity is captured by two critical exponents (c, d) defining equivalence classes, which range between approximately 0.9 and 1 across beam energies from 3.8 to 2760 GeV, and the resulting freezeout parameters remain very compatible with those from extensive Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics.

## Method

The authors implemented generic nonextensive statistics (GNS) into the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model, using a generalized exponential function based on the Lambert W-function that depends on two scaling exponents (c, d). They performed statistical fits of the GNS grand-canonical partition function to various particle ratios measured by E866, NA44, NA49, NA57, STAR, and ALICE experiments at beam energies from 3.8 to 2760 GeV, simultaneously imposing the chemical freezeout condition s/T^3 = 7 and minimizing chi-squared to extract the freezeout temperature T_ch, baryon chemical potential mu_b, and the exponents (c, d).

## Key numbers

- Beam energies analyzed: 3.8, 4.2, 5, 6.4, 7.7, 8, 9, 11.5, 12, 17, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, 130, 200, and 2760 GeV
- The equivalence classes (c, d) range between approximately 0.9 and 1 across all beam energies
- The maximum error in the exponent (c or d) is only 10 percent
- At sqrt(s) approximately 20 GeV, d reaches minimum while c reaches nearly maximum values
- The freezeout temperature from GNS fits is compatible with the extensive BG value of 192 plus or minus 15 MeV (from prior work cited in the paper)

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior application of GNS to lattice QCD thermodynamics and particle ratios at 200 and 2760 GeV (Tawfik 2016, 2017) to a wider range of beam energies. It contrasts with Tsallis nonextensive statistics, which requires a radical reduction in freezeout temperature (the "physical temperature" being 2-3 times smaller than T_ch as reported by Deppmann 2015) and fails to reproduce lattice QCD thermodynamics. The results confirm that HRG with Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics (Karsch 2003, Redlich 2004, Tawfik 2004-2005) successfully describes both lattice thermodynamics and particle ratios, while the GNS approach shows that the nonextensivity is characterized by (c,d) values near unity rather than Tsallis (q,0) with q>1.
