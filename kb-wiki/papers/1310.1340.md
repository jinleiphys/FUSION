# Momentum imbalance of D mesons in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at LHC

> As a new observable for heavy flavor correlations the momentum imbalance $A_D$ of D mesons is proposed. It is defined analogously to the jet momentum imbalance $A_J$ of fully reconstructed jets. However, since D mesons are flavor tagged particles, no jet reconstruction is necessary. $A_D$ quantifies the influence of the medium created in heavy-ion collisions on correlated charm pairs. We present results with the partonic transport model Boltzmann Approach to MultiParton Scatterings (BAMPS), which describes well the nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow of all heavy flavor particles at RHIC and LHC. The $A_D$ distribution in heavy-ion collisions at LHC is shifted to larger values of $A_D$ compared to proton-proton collisions. We argue that this shift is due to medium effects and can be explained partially by a path length imbalance of charm pairs and partially by momentum fluctuations in the initial charm pair distribution.

# Momentum imbalance of D mesons in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at LHC

## Key claim

The momentum imbalance $A_D$ of D mesons is shifted to larger values in heavy-ion collisions compared to proton-proton collisions due to medium effects, but this shift can be explained only partially by path length imbalance of charm pairs; a significant contribution comes from momentum fluctuations in the initial charm pair distribution, especially when using PYTHIA initial conditions.

## Method

The authors used the partonic transport model Boltzmann Approach to MultiParton Scatterings (BAMPS) to simulate charm quarks in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV. Initial heavy quark distributions were generated either with PYTHIA 6.4 or a leading order mini-jet model that produces back-to-back charm pairs. Charm quarks underwent only elastic scatterings with a cross section scaled by a factor $K=3.5$ to mimic radiative processes, and were hadronized to D mesons using Peterson fragmentation with parameter $\epsilon_c = 0.05$.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ of D mesons for central Pb+Pb collisions (impact parameter $b=3.6$ fm) at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV agrees well with experimental data for intermediate and large transverse momenta.
- With low trigger conditions ($p_{T;1}^D > 10$ GeV, $p_{T;2}^D > 4$ GeV, $\Delta\phi > 2\pi/3$), only 15% of the D meson pairs that pass the trigger stem from charm quarks produced in the same initial hard scattering.
- With high trigger conditions ($p_{T;1}^D > 25$ GeV, $p_{T;2}^D > 15$ GeV, $\Delta\phi > 2\pi/3$), more than 75% of D meson pairs are from charm quarks produced in the same hard process.
- The screening mass used for regularizing the t-channel divergence is $\mu^2 = \kappa_t m_D^2$ with $\kappa_t = 1/(2e) \approx 0.2$.

## Context

This work proposes a new observable $A_D$ for heavy flavor correlations, defined analogously to the jet momentum imbalance $A_J$ used by ATLAS (Aad 2010) and CMS (Chatrchyan 2011, 2012). Unlike jet imbalance studies, D mesons are flavor-tagged particles requiring no jet reconstruction. The study extends prior work on heavy flavor correlations by Zhu (2006, 2007), Gossiaux (2009), Younus (2013), and Nahrgang (2013). The results enable future experimental measurements at LHC and comparisons with heavy flavor tagged jet imbalance studies, as well as future inclusion of radiative processes for charm quarks in BAMPS.
