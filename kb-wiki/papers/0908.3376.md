# Ground-state and single-particle energies of nuclei around ^{16}O, ^{40}Ca, and ^{56}Ni from realistic nucleon-nucleon forces

> We perform ab initio calculations for nuclei around ^{16}O, ^{40}Ca, and ^{56}Ni using realistic nucleon-nucleon forces. In particular, ^{56}Ni is computed as the heaviest nucleus in this kind of ab initio calculation. Ground-state and single-particle energies including three-body-cluster effects are obtained within the framework of the unitary-model-operator approach. It is shown that the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential gives quite good results close to the experimental values for all nuclei in the present work.

## Key claim

Using only the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential within the unitary-model-operator approach (UMOA), the authors achieve ground-state energies that reach 93.6% of the experimental value for 16O, 99.5% for 40Ca, and 97.8% (extrapolated) for 56Ni, but all results remain less bound than experiment, suggesting that three-nucleon forces are needed to fully reproduce the data.

## Method

The authors apply the unitary-model-operator approach (UMOA) to compute ground-state and single-particle energies for closed-shell nuclei 16O, 40Ca, and 56Ni, and for single-hole states in 15O and 15N. They use the Nijm-I, CD-Bonn, and chiral N3LO nucleon-nucleon potentials, include the Coulomb force for protons, and evaluate three-body-cluster (3BC) terms in the cluster expansion of the unitarily transformed Hamiltonian. The model space is defined by harmonic-oscillator basis states with boundary parameter ρ1, and convergence is checked by varying ρ1 and the oscillator energy ħΩ.

## Key numbers

- 16O ground-state energy with CD-Bonn: -119.39 MeV (experiment: -127.62 MeV), giving BE/A = 7.46 MeV (experiment: 7.98 MeV)
- 40Ca ground-state energy with CD-Bonn: -340.27 MeV (experiment: -342.05 MeV), giving BE/A = 8.51 MeV (experiment: 8.55 MeV)
- 56Ni ground-state energy with CD-Bonn at ρ1=18: -469.35 MeV; extrapolated to infinite model space: -473.17 MeV (experiment: -483.99 MeV), giving BE/A = 8.45 MeV (experiment: 8.64 MeV)
- Spin-orbit splitting in 15O with CD-Bonn: 5.84 MeV (experiment: 6.18 MeV); in 15N with CD-Bonn: 5.84 MeV (experiment: 6.32 MeV)
- Energy difference between 15O and 15N ground states with CD-Bonn: 3.60 MeV (experiment: 3.54 MeV)
- 3BC contribution to 16O ground state: -3.82 MeV (CD-Bonn), -4.02 MeV (Nijm-I), -13.57 MeV (N3LO)
- 3BC contribution to 56Ni ground state: -16.62 MeV at ρ1=18 (CD-Bonn)

## Context

This work extends ab initio calculations to 56Ni, the heaviest nucleus treated with such methods at the time, using the UMOA framework previously applied to lighter nuclei by Fujii (2004). The results are compared with coupled-cluster calculations by Hagen (2008, 2009) for nuclei in the pf-shell region, and with no-core shell model and Green's function Monte Carlo studies of light nuclei by Pieper (2001) and Navratil (2000). The systematic underbinding and the large 3BC contribution from the N3LO potential point to the need for explicit three-nucleon forces, which the authors identify as the next step for quantitative description of heavier nuclei.
