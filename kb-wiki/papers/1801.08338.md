# Fierz-complete NJL model study II: towards the fixed-point and phase structure of hot and dense two-flavor QCD

> Nambu-Jona-Lasinio-type models are often employed as low-energy models for the theory of the strong interaction to analyze its phase structure at finite temperature and quark chemical potential. In particular at low temperature and large chemical potential, where the application of fully first-principles approaches is currently difficult at best, this class of models still plays a prominent role to guide our understanding of the dynamics of dense strong-interaction matter. In this work, we consider a Fierz-complete version of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with two massless quark flavors and study its renormalization group flow and fixed-point structure at leading order of the derivative expansion of the effective action. Sum rules for the various four-quark couplings then allow us to monitor the strength of the breaking of the axial $U_{\rm A}(1)$ symmetry close to and above the phase boundary. We find that the dynamics in the ten-dimensional Fierz-complete space of four-quark couplings can only be reduced to a one-dimensional space associated with the scalar-pseudoscalar coupling in the strict large-$N_{\rm c}$ limit. Still, the interacting fixed point associated with this one-dimensional subspace appears to govern the dynamics at small quark chemical potential even beyond the large-$N_{\rm c}$ limit. At large chemical potential, corrections beyond the large-$N_{\rm c}$ limit become important and the dynamics is dominated by diquarks, favoring the formation of a chirally symmetric diquark condensate. In this regime, our study suggests that the phase boundary is shifted to higher temperatures when a Fierz-complete set of four-quark interactions is considered.

## Key claim

The dynamics in the ten-dimensional Fierz-complete space of four-quark couplings can only be reduced to a one-dimensional space associated with the scalar-pseudoscalar coupling in the strict large-\(N_c\) limit, but at large chemical potential corrections beyond this limit become important and the phase boundary is shifted to higher temperatures when a Fierz-complete set of four-quark interactions is considered.

## Method

The authors studied the renormalization group (RG) flow and fixed-point structure of a Fierz-complete Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with two massless quark flavors at leading order of the derivative expansion of the effective action. They employed a four-dimensional Fermi-surface adapted regularization scheme and analyzed the ten-dimensional space of pointlike four-quark couplings using the Wetterich equation, monitoring the strength of axial \(U_{\rm A}(1)\) symmetry breaking via sum rules for the four-quark couplings. The phase structure at finite temperature and quark chemical potential was determined by identifying divergences in the four-quark couplings as indicators for the onset of spontaneous symmetry breaking.

## Key numbers

- The Fierz-complete basis of pointlike four-quark interactions is composed of **ten** four-quark channels.
- In the strict large-\(N_c\) limit, the dynamics reduces to a **one-dimensional** subspace associated with the scalar-pseudoscalar coupling.
- For a theory invariant under \(SU(N_c)\otimes SU_{\rm L}(2)\otimes SU_{\rm R}(2)\otimes U_{\rm V}(1)\otimes U_{\rm A}(1)\) transformations, the Fierz-complete basis is composed of **eight** channels.
- The study exclusively considered \(N_c = 3\) for all numerical calculations.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Braun (2017) from a single fermion species to two-flavor QCD, demonstrating that Fierz-completeness affects the dynamics not only at large chemical potential but also at small chemical potential, as illustrated by a significant dependence of the curvature of the finite-temperature phase boundary. The results contradict the common practice in conventional NJL model studies that include only a scalar-pseudoscalar channel, showing that such Fierz-incomplete approximations can lead to qualitatively different predictions for the phase structure. This enables future studies to incorporate Fierz-complete four-quark interactions in low-energy QCD models for more reliable predictions of dense strong-interaction matter, particularly relevant for neutron star physics.
