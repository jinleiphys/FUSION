# Hyperons in neutron star matter within relativistic mean-field models

> Since the discovery of neutron stars with masses around 2 solar masses the composition of matter in the central part of these massive stars has been intensively discussed. Within this paper we will (re)investigate the question of the appearance of hyperons. To that end we will perform an extensive parameter study within relativistic mean field models. We will show that it is possible to obtain high mass neutron stars (i) with a substantial amount of hyperons, (ii) radii of 12-13 km for the canonical mass of 1.4 solar masses, and (iii) a spinodal instability at the onset of hyperons. The results depend strongly on the interaction in the hyperon-hyperon channels, on which only very little information is available from terrestrial experiments up to now.

## Key claim

It is possible to obtain high-mass neutron stars (around 2 solar masses) with a substantial amount of hyperons within relativistic mean-field models, but this possibility depends strongly on the poorly constrained hyperon-hyperon interaction parameters, and a first-order phase transition at the onset of hyperons requires a hyperon-hyperon attraction that is stronger than current experimental data support.

## Method

The authors performed an extensive parameter study within three relativistic mean-field models (GM1, TM1-2, and DDHδ) at zero temperature, varying the coupling constants for hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for non-rotating spherical neutron stars and performed a convexity analysis of the energy density curvature matrix in the three-dimensional space of baryon number, strangeness, and lepton number densities to detect thermodynamic instabilities.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star masses up to 2.32 solar masses were obtained with the GM1 model and no σ* coupling (model Y3, z=0)
- For the DDHδ model with symmetry-inspired couplings and Λ hyperons only, maximum masses ranged from 1.58 to 2.06 solar masses depending on parameters
- Radii for canonical 1.4 solar mass neutron stars were 12.7 km for DDHδ and 13.8 km for GM1 across most parameter sets
- The critical value of the Λ potential in Λ matter at one-fifth saturation density for onset of instability was approximately -30 MeV for GM1 and -40 MeV for DDHδ
- The Λ potential in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation was fixed at -28 MeV, the Ξ potential at -18 MeV, and the Σ potential at 30 MeV
- The pressure of β-equilibrated cold neutron star matter at saturation density ranged from 2.43 to 4.38 MeV/fm³ across models

## Context

This work extends the discussion of the "hyperon puzzle" following the discovery of 2-solar-mass neutron stars by Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013). It contrasts with non-relativistic studies by Raduta (2014) that found a much larger parameter space for hyperonic instabilities consistent with hypernuclear data. The results show that within relativistic mean-field models, obtaining high-mass neutron stars with hyperons requires more repulsive hyperon-hyperon interactions than previously assumed in works such as Weissenborn (2012) and Banik (2014), and that the instability found in non-relativistic models may arise from different functional forms of the energy density. The study enables future work to resolve ambiguities by confronting different functional forms with new experimental hypernuclear data.
