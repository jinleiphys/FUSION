# Modern status of heavy quark sum rules in QCD

> We briefly report the modern status of heavy quark sum rules (HQSR) based on stability criteria by emphasizing the recent progresses for determining the QCD parameters (alpha_s, m_{c,b} and gluon condensates)where their correlations have been taken into account. The results: alpha_s(M_Z)=0.1181(16)(3), m_c(m_c)=1286(16) MeV, m_b(m_b)=4202(7) MeV,<alpha_s G^2> = (6.49+-0.35)10^-2 GeV^4, < g^3 G^3 >= (8.2+-1.0) GeV^2 <alpha_s G^2> and the ones from recent light quark sum rules are summarized in Table 2. One can notice that the SVZ value of <alpha_s G^2> has been underestimated by a factor 1.6, <g^3 G^3> is much bigger than the instanton model estimate, while the four-quark condensate which mixes under renormalization is incompatible with the vacuum saturation which is phenomenologically violated by a factor (2~4). The uses of HQSR for molecules and tetraquarks states are commented.

## Key claim

Heavy quark sum rules (HQSR) based on stability criteria provide improved determinations of QCD parameters (alpha_s, m_c, m_b, and gluon condensates) when their mutual correlations are taken into account, resolving apparent discrepancies between earlier estimates; the SVZ value of the gluon condensate <alpha_s G^2> has been underestimated by a factor of about 1.6, and the dimension-six gluon condensate <g^3 G^3> is much larger than the instanton model estimate.

## Method

The analysis uses exponential/Borel/Laplace sum rules (LSR) and their ratios, as well as Q^2-moment sum rules (MSR) and their ratios, applied to two-point correlators of heavy quark currents (charmonium and bottomium channels). The spectral function is parametrized by a minimal duality ansatz: one resonance plus a QCD continuum above a threshold t_c. Optimal results are extracted using stability criteria with respect to the sum rule variable (tau for LSR, moment degree n for MSR), the continuum threshold t_c, and the perturbative subtraction scale mu, following the Bell-Bertlmann minimum/inflexion point criterion.

## Key numbers

- alpha_s(M_Z) = 0.1181(16)(3)
- m_c(m_c) = 1286(16) MeV
- m_b(m_b) = 4202(7) MeV
- <alpha_s G^2> = (6.49 +/- 0.35) x 10^{-2} GeV^4
- <g^3 G^3> = (8.2 +/- 1.0) GeV^2 <alpha_s G^2> (ratio rho_G = 8.2 +/- 1.0 GeV^2)
- The SVZ value of <alpha_s G^2> (0.04 GeV^4) has been underestimated by a factor of about 1.6
- Four-quark condensate violates vacuum saturation by a factor rho = 2 to 4, with rho * alpha_s <bar-psi psi>^2 = 5.8(9) x 10^{-4} GeV^6
- Tachyonic gluon mass squared: (alpha_s/pi) lambda^2 = -(7 +/- 3) x 10^{-2} GeV^2
- Mixed quark-gluon condensate parameter M_0^2 = 0.8(2) GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the SVZ sum rule approach (Shifman, Vainshtein, Zakharov, 1979) and the author's own previous reviews and books (Narison, 1989, 2004) by incorporating correlations among QCD parameters that were previously treated independently. The analysis uses the Bell-Bertlmann stability criterion (Bell and Bertlmann, 1981) rather than the original SVZ window method. The results enable improved extractions of hadron masses and couplings for molecules and tetraquark states using heavy quark sum rules, and provide constraints that challenge the instanton liquid model (Nikolaev and Radyushkin, 1983; Schafer and Shuryak, 1998; Ioffe and Samsonov, 2000) and the vacuum saturation assumption for four-quark condensates.
