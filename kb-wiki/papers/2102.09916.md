# Screening masses towards chiral limit

> A possible effective restoration of the anomalous $U_A(1)$ symmetry would have a non-trivial effect on the global phase diagram of QCD. In this work we investigate the effective restoration of the $U_A(1)$ through the calculation of scalar and pseudo-scalar screening masses and corresponding susceptibilities, for physical and lower than physical pion masses. Calculations have been performed in (2+1)-flavor HISQ discretization scheme with a physical value of the strange quark mass. Preliminary calculations of the continuum extrapolated scalar and pseudo-scalar masses are presented, based on lattices with three different temporal extent. Non-trivial structure of the difference between scalar and pseudo-scalar susceptibilites are discussed for $N_τ=8$ lattices.

## Key claim
The paper presents preliminary evidence that the effective restoration of the anomalous $U_A(1)$ symmetry, signaled by degeneracy of scalar and pseudo-scalar screening masses, may occur at a lower temperature relative to the pseudo-critical temperature when the pion mass is reduced, but the authors hedge that a better controlled continuum extrapolation and results at higher temperatures are needed to make a definitive statement.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD calculations of scalar and pseudo-scalar screening masses and susceptibilities using the (2+1)-flavor highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action with a physical strange quark mass and light quark masses corresponding to mass ratios $m_s/m_l = 27$ (physical) and $m_s/m_l = 80$ (lighter than physical). They carried out thermodynamic limit extrapolations using a combined fit for scalar and pseudo-scalar channels, followed by continuum limit extrapolations using lattices with three different temporal extents ($N_\tau = 6, 8, 12$) via a linear ansatz in $1/N_\tau^2$ and cubic spline temperature interpolation.

## Key numbers
- For $m_s/m_l = 80$ at $T \approx 148$ MeV, the continuum extrapolated screening masses using infinite volume extrapolated data are $m_S = 191(19)$ MeV and $m_{PS} = 120(5)$ MeV; using largest-volume data they are $m_S = 173(5)$ MeV and $m_{PS} = 129(1)$ MeV.
- The pseudo-critical temperature for $m_s/m_l = 80$ is estimated as $146.5 \pm 1.0$ MeV based on prior work.
- For $m_s/m_l = 80$, scalar and pseudo-scalar masses become degenerate around $T \approx 160-175$ MeV, corresponding to $T/T_{pc} \sim 1.1-1.2$.
- For $m_s/m_l = 27$ (physical pion mass), degeneracy occurs at about 200 MeV, corresponding to $T/T_{pc} \sim 1.3$.
- The finite volume extrapolation parameter $c$ for $m_s/m_l = 80$ at $T \approx 148$ MeV is $1.33(66)$, $1.89(97)$, and $1.83(92)$ for $N_\tau = 6, 8, 12$ respectively, and $2.59(76)$ for $T \approx 166$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous screening mass studies at physical quark masses (Screening Mass Paper, 2018) to lighter-than-physical pions, addressing the inconclusive status of $U_A(1)$ restoration across staggered, overlap, domain wall, and Wilson fermion calculations (Buchoff 2013, Bazavov 2012, Tomiya 2016, Brandt 2016). The results enable future chiral extrapolations to determine whether $U_A(1)$ is effectively restored at the chiral phase transition, which would distinguish between $O(4)$ second-order and first-order scenarios for the QCD phase diagram.
