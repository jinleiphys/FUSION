# The $K^- d \to πΣn$ reaction revisited

> The appearance of some papers dealing with the $K^- d \to πΣn$ reaction, with some discrepancies in the results and a proposal to measure the reaction at forward $n$ angles at J-PARC justifies to retake the theoretical study with high precision to make accurate predictions for the experiment and extract from there the relevant physical information. We do this in the present paper showing results using the Watson approach and the truncated Faddeev approach. We argue that the Watson approach is more suitable to study the reaction because it takes into account the potential energy of the nucleons forming the deuteron, which is neglected in the truncated Faddeev approach. Predictions for the experiment are done as well as spectra with the integrated neutron angle.

## Key claim

The Watson multiple-scattering approach is more suitable than the truncated Faddeev approach for the \(K^- d \to \pi \Sigma n\) reaction because it accounts for the potential energy of the nucleons in the deuteron, which the truncated Faddeev approach neglects; the cusp structures seen in the Faddeev approach are moderated when the binding is properly included, and the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance peak remains visible.

## Method

The authors calculated the \(K^- d \to \pi \Sigma n\) reaction using both the Watson expansion and the truncated Faddeev approach, keeping terms up to double scattering. The two-body meson-baryon amplitudes were taken from the chiral unitary approach. The key difference between the two methods is the treatment of the intermediate kaon energy \(q^0\): in the Watson approach (case A) it is \(q^0 = M_N + k^0 - p_n^0\), while in the Faddeev approach (case B) it includes an additional kinetic-energy term \(|\vec{q} + \vec{p}_n - \vec{k}|^2 / (2 M_N)\). The authors computed differential cross sections at fixed neutron angles and angle-integrated invariant mass spectra for initial kaon momenta of 600 and 800 MeV/\(c\).

## Key numbers

* For \(k_{\text{lab}} = 600\) MeV/\(c\) with a constant \(\bar{K}N \to \pi\Sigma\) amplitude, the cusp in the differential cross section at \(\theta_{\text{cm}}^n = 0^\circ\) appears near \(M_{\pi\Sigma} \sim 1430\) MeV in case B (Faddeev), while it is more moderate in case A (Watson).
* For \(k_{\text{lab}} = 800\) MeV/\(c\) with the same constant amplitude, the cusp shifts to lower energies and is further moderated in both cases.
* With the full chiral unitary amplitudes at \(k_{\text{lab}} = 600\) MeV/\(c\) and \(\theta_{\text{cm}}^n = 0^\circ\), the \(\Lambda(1405)\) peak is visible in case A but is spoiled by a rapid rise near \(1430\) MeV in case B.
* For the angle-integrated spectrum at \(k_{\text{lab}} = 600\) MeV/\(c\) and \(0^\circ < \theta_{\text{cm}}^n < 10^\circ\), the cusps in case A are smeared; for \(10^\circ < \theta_{\text{cm}}^n < 20^\circ\) they are negligible, corresponding to \(\theta_{\text{lab}}^n \sim 5^\circ\).
* In the full-angle-integrated invariant mass spectrum, the cusp structures for case A disappear completely, and a clear \(\Lambda(1405)\) peak is observed in both cases A and B.

## Context

This work revisits the \(K^- d \to \pi \Sigma n\) reaction previously studied by Jido et al. (2009, 2010), which predicted a clear \(\Lambda(1405)\) signal from double scattering. It addresses discrepancies with more recent calculations by Miyagawa et al. (2012), who used a truncated Faddeev approach and attributed the observed peak to a threshold effect, and by Revai (2012), who used full Faddeev equations with an energy-independent separable potential. The present paper argues that the Watson approach, which includes the nucleon-nucleon potential energy, is more accurate and that the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance remains the dominant feature, especially when the neutron angle is integrated. The results provide refined predictions for the proposed J-PARC experiment measuring forward neutrons.
