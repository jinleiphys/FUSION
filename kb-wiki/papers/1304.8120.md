# Event mixing does not reproduce single particle acceptance convolutions for nonuniform pseudorapidity distributions

> We point out that the mixed-event method for two-particle acceptance correction, widely used in particle correlation measurements at RHIC and LHC, is wrong in cases where the single particle pseudorapidity distribution is significantly nonuniform. The correct acceptance should be the convolution of two single-particle efficiency$\times$acceptance functions. The error of the mixed-event method, which guarantees a uniform $Δη$ two-particle combinatorial density, is, however, small in correlation analyses where the two particles are integrated over an extended pseudorapidity $η$ range. With one particle fixed in $η$ and the right acceptance correction, the background-subtracted correlated pair density may reveal not only a short-range but also a long-range $Δη$ dependence. This has important physics implication, and may provide crucial information to disentangle physics mechanisms for the recently observed long-range ridge correlation in asymmetric proton-lead collisions at the LHC.

# Event mixing does not reproduce single particle acceptance convolutions for nonuniform pseudorapidity distributions

## Key claim

The mixed-event method for two-particle acceptance correction, widely used in RHIC and LHC correlation analyses, is formally incorrect when the single-particle pseudorapidity distribution is significantly nonuniform; however, the resulting error is small in analyses where both particles are integrated over an extended pseudorapidity range, but becomes important when one particle is fixed in a narrow pseudorapidity bin.

## Method

The authors derive the correct two-particle acceptance as the convolution of two single-particle efficiency×acceptance functions, and compare it to the mixed-event two-particle density. They analytically compute the combinatorial two-particle density for a linear single-particle pseudorapidity distribution of the form dN/dη ∝ 1 + kη/η_m, and derive the relative error introduced by the mixed-event method. They then discuss the implications for ridge correlation measurements in asymmetric proton-lead collisions at the LHC.

## Key numbers

- For a linear single-particle density with slope k = 1/4 (as measured in central 20% d+Au collisions at RHIC by PHOBOS) over an acceptance range of -2 < η < 2, the mixed-event method gives approximately 3% error at Δη = η_m and approximately 8% error at Δη = 2η_m.
- The relative error formula is (C_triangle - C_mix)/C_triangle = (k²/(6+2k²)) × (|Δη|/η_m) × (2 + |Δη|/η_m).
- The effect of nonuniformity on the two-particle density integrated over trigger and associated particle η is quadratic in k, so when k << 1 the two-particle density is approximately triangular in Δη.

## Context

This work corrects a widespread misconception in the mixed-event acceptance correction technique used by PHOBOS, STAR, PHENIX, CMS, ALICE, and ATLAS for two-particle correlation measurements. The authors show that while the error is small for symmetric collisions where dN/dη is nearly uniform (as noted by PHOBOS (2005) for mid-rapidity), it becomes significant for asymmetric systems such as proton-lead collisions at the LHC where the single-particle pseudorapidity distribution is nonuniform. The correct approach enables measurements of the Δη dependence of the ridge correlation, which could discriminate between hydrodynamic explanations (where ridge strength is proportional to background pair density) and color glass condensate models (as calculated by Dusling (2012) for Au+Au collisions).
