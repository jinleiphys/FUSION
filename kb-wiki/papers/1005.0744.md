# Renormalization of chiral two pion exchange NN interactions with delta excitations: correlations in the partial wave expansion

> In this work we consider the renormalization of the chiral two-pion exchange potential with explicit delta-excitations for nucleon-nucleon scattering at next-to-leading (NLO) and next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO). Due to the singular nature of the chiral potentials, correlations between different partial waves are generated. In particular we show that two-body scattering by a short distance power like singular attractive interaction can be renormalized in all partial waves with a single counterterm, provided the singularities are identical. A parallel statement holds in the presence of tensor interactions when the eigenpotentials in the coupled channel problem also coincide. While this construction reduces the total number of counterterms to eleven in the case of nucleon-nucleon scattering with chiral two-pion exchange interactions with delta degrees of freedom, the differences in the scattering phases as compared to the case with the uncorrelated partial wave renormalization become smaller as the angular momentum is increased in the elastic scattering region.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for chiral two-pion exchange potentials with explicit delta excitations, the short-distance behavior of the reduced wave function becomes independent of angular momentum, allowing all partial waves to be renormalized with a single common counterterm rather than requiring one counterterm per partial wave, provided the singularities are identical; however, the differences in scattering phases compared to uncorrelated partial wave renormalization become smaller as angular momentum increases in the elastic scattering region.

## Method

The authors analyze the renormalization of chiral two-pion exchange potentials with delta excitations at NLO and N2LO by solving the reduced Schrödinger equation in coordinate space with a delta-shell contact interaction. They derive the short-distance behavior of the wave function using the WKB approximation for attractive singular power-law potentials and show that the logarithmic derivative at the cut-off radius becomes independent of angular momentum. For coupled channels with tensor forces, they diagonalize the tensor operator and analyze the eigenpotentials to determine when the same correlation between partial waves holds.

## Key numbers

* The total number of counterterms required for nucleon-nucleon scattering with chiral two-pion exchange interactions including delta degrees of freedom is reduced to eleven when using the correlated partial wave renormalization scheme.
* The finite cut-off corrections to renormalized phase shifts scale as δ_∞(k) − δ_Λ(k) = O(Λ^(−5/2−ν/2)) for large cut-offs, where ν is the order in the chiral expansion.
* The shortest de Broglie wavelength probed in elastic NN scattering below pion production threshold is λ_min ∼ 0.5 fm, providing a natural scale for cut-off radii.
* The WKB approximation for the reduced wave function near the origin yields a short-distance behavior u_{k,l}(r) ≈ A_l (r/R_F)^(n/4) sin[(2/(n−2))(R_F/r)^(n/2−1) + φ_l(k)] for distances where the potential behaves as 2μ V_F(r) → −R_F^(n−2)/r^n with n > 2.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses of renormalization of chiral potentials (Valderrama and Arriola, 2005-2009) by addressing non-central partial waves and explicit delta excitations. It contradicts the naive expectation from earlier studies (Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck, 2005) that singular attractive potentials require an infinite number of counterterms, one per partial wave. The results enable a consistent renormalization scheme with a finite number of counterterms for chiral potentials with delta degrees of freedom, which may provide a better starting point for nuclear matter calculations due to its small D-state probability and improved convergence properties for few-body correlations.
