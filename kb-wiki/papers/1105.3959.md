# Quark-mass dependence of the three-flavor QCD phase diagram at zero and imaginary chemical potential: Model prediction

> We draw the three-flavor phase diagram as a function of light- and strange-quark masses for both zero and imaginary quark-number chemical potential, using the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with an effective four-quark vertex depending on the Polyakov loop. The model prediction is qualitatively consistent with 2+1 flavor lattice QCD prediction at zero chemical potential and with degenerate three-flavor lattice QCD prediction at imaginary chemical potential.

## Key claim

The three-flavor entanglement Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (EPNJL) model predicts a phase diagram for the chiral transition at zero chemical potential and for the Roberge-Weiss (RW) transition at imaginary chemical potential that is qualitatively consistent with lattice QCD, but only for a specific parameter set; the order of the RW transition at the physical point can be either first or second order depending on the chosen values of the entanglement parameters \(\alpha_1\) and \(\alpha_2\) within a triangular region.

## Method

The authors extended the two-flavor EPNJL model to three flavors by introducing a Polyakov-loop-dependent effective four-quark vertex \(G_{\rm S}(\Phi) = G_{\rm S}[1 - \alpha_1 \Phi\Phi^* - \alpha_2(\Phi^3 + \Phi^{*3})]\) into the mean-field thermodynamic potential. Parameters \(\alpha_1 = 0.25\) and \(\alpha_2 = 0.1\) were chosen to simultaneously reproduce 2+1 flavor lattice QCD results for a crossover chiral transition at zero chemical potential and degenerate three-flavor lattice QCD results for a second-order RW endpoint at intermediate quark masses. The model was then used to map the order of the chiral transition and the C-symmetry breaking at the RW endpoint in the light- and strange-quark mass plane.

## Key numbers

* The chiral pseudocritical temperature in the EPNJL model is approximately 160 MeV, coinciding with the deconfinement crossover temperature, compared to the PNJL model where the chiral crossover occurs at about 180 MeV and deconfinement at about 130 MeV.
* The light-quark condensate at zero temperature and chemical potential is \(\sigma_l = -0.0142\) GeV\(^3\).
* The NJL sector parameters are: \(m_l = 5.5\) MeV, \(m_s = 140.7\) MeV, \(\Lambda = 602.3\) MeV, \(G_{\rm S} \Lambda^2 = 1.835\), and \(G_{\rm D}(0) \Lambda^5 = 12.36\).
* The Polyakov-loop potential parameter \(T_0\) is rescaled to 150 MeV to reproduce the lattice QCD deconfinement temperature of about 160 MeV.
* The allowed parameter region for \((\alpha_1, \alpha_2)\) is defined by the inequalities \(-1.5\alpha_1 + 0.3 < \alpha_2 < -0.86\alpha_1 + 0.32\) and \(\alpha_2 > 0\).

## Context

This work extends the two-flavor EPNJL model of Sakai et al. (2010) to three flavors, aiming to reproduce the Columbia plot from lattice QCD by de Forcrand and Philipsen (2007) at zero chemical potential and the RW endpoint results from de Forcrand and Philipsen (2010) for degenerate three flavors. The model enables predictions for the three-flavor phase diagram in the quark-mass plane at imaginary chemical potential, a region where lattice QCD is feasible but where the full Columbia plot for the RW transition was previously unknown. The results are complementary to first-principle lattice QCD and are intended to guide future lattice studies, though the authors caution that predictions for quark masses above about 100 MeV are only qualitative due to limitations of the NJL-type model.
