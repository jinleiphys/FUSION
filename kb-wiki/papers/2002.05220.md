# Importance of non-flow background on the chiral magnetic wave search

> An observable sensitive to the chiral magnetic wave (CMW) is the charge asymmetry dependence of the $π^{-}$ and $π^{+}$ anisotropic flow difference, $Δv_{n}(A_{\rm ch})$. We show that, due to non-flow correlations, the flow measurements by the Q-cumulant method using all charged particles as reference introduce a trivial linear term to $Δv_{n}(A_{\rm ch})$. The trivial slope contribution to the triangle flow difference $Δv_{3}(A_{\rm ch})$ can be negative if the non-flow is dominated by back-to-back pairs. This can explain the observed negative $Δv_{3}(A_{\rm ch})$ slope in the preliminary STAR data. We further find that the non-flow correlations give rise to additional backgrounds to the slope of $Δv_{2}(A_{\rm ch})$ from the competition among different pion sources and from the larger multiplicity dilution to $π^{+}$ ($π^{-}$) at positive (negative) $A_{\rm ch}$.

## Key claim

Non-flow correlations in heavy-ion collisions introduce a trivial linear term to the charge-asymmetry-dependent pion flow difference \(\Delta v_n(A_{\rm ch})\) when the Q-cumulant method uses all charged particles as reference, and this trivial contribution can explain the observed negative slope of \(\Delta v_3(A_{\rm ch})\) in preliminary STAR data without invoking the chiral magnetic wave.

## Method

The authors used analytic derivations of the two-particle cumulant method to show that using all charged hadrons as reference particles automatically generates a term proportional to the charge asymmetry \(A_{\rm ch}\) when non-flow differs between like-sign and unlike-sign pairs. They then constructed a Monte Carlo toy model with Poisson multiplicity fluctuations, measured \(p_T\) and \(\eta\) spectra from 30-40% centrality Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, and forced 20% of pions into back-to-back \(\pi^+\pi^-\) pairs to simulate non-flow correlations. They also developed a two-component model distinguishing primordial pions from resonance-decay pions to study competition effects between different pion sources.

## Key numbers

- Mean multiplicity of charged hadrons set to 380 in \(|\eta|<1\) with \(p_T>0.15\) GeV/c in the toy model.
- Constant elliptic flow \(v_2=4\%\) and triangle flow \(v_3=4\%\) used to generate azimuthal angles in the toy model.
- 20% of multiplicity forced into back-to-back \(\pi^+\pi^-\) pairs in the toy model to simulate non-flow correlations.
- Like-sign non-flow toy model gave a positive slope \(r=1.63\%\) for \(\Delta v_2(A_{\rm ch})\).
- After eliminating the trivial term, normalized \(\Delta v_3(A_{\rm ch})\) slopes in STAR data were 1.5\(\sigma\) above zero for 20-60% centrality.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of backgrounds to the chiral magnetic wave (CMW) search beyond the previously considered Local Charge Conservation (Bzdak, 2013) and isospin chemical potential effects (Hatta, 2015). It directly addresses the preliminary STAR data (Shou, 2018) that showed negative \(\Delta v_3(A_{\rm ch})\) slopes, which had been interpreted as CMW evidence. The results enable a cleaner extraction of CMW signals by providing a method to remove trivial non-flow contributions using single-charge-sign reference particles, and they motivate further study of non-trivial non-flow backgrounds from multiple pion sources and like-sign correlations.
