# Observation of Airy minimum in elastic and inelastic scattering of $^3$He from $^{12}$C at 50.5 and 60 MeV and alpha particle condensation in $^{12}$C

> Angular distributions for elastic and inelastic scattering of $^3$He from $^{12}$C were measured at energies 50.5 and 60 MeV. The Airy minimum of the prerainbow scattering was clearly observed in the angular distributions for the 0$_2^+$ (7.65 MeV) state of $^{12}$C (Hoyle state). The experimental results were analyzed with a coupled channels method with double folding potentials derived from the microscopic wave functions for the ground 0$_1^+$, 2$^+$ (4.44 MeV), 3$^-$ (9.64 MeV) and 0$_2^+$ states. The analysis supports the view that the Hoyle state is a three alpha particle condensate with a large radius of dilute matter distribution.

## Key claim
The paper reports the first clear observation of the Airy minimum in inelastic scattering to the Hoyle state (0₂⁺ at 7.65 MeV) of ¹²C, and the shift of this minimum to larger angles compared to the ground state supports the interpretation of the Hoyle state as a dilute three-alpha-particle condensate with an enlarged radius.

## Method
Angular distributions for elastic and inelastic scattering of ³He from ¹²C were measured at 50.5 and 60 MeV using the U-150M cyclotron in Almaty, Kazakhstan, with silicon detector telescopes covering 2–70°. The data were analyzed using a coupled channels method with double folding potentials derived from microscopic wave functions for the 0₁⁺, 2⁺ (4.44 MeV), 3⁻ (9.64 MeV), and 0₂⁺ states of ¹²C, using the density-dependent M3Y effective interaction (DDM3Y) and a normalization factor N_R = 1.28.

## Key numbers
- Incident energies: 50.5 MeV and 60 MeV for ³He beams on a ¹²C target of thickness 30 μg/cm²
- Volume integral per nucleon pair J_V for the 0₂⁺ state: 565 MeV fm³ at 50.5 MeV and 548 MeV fm³ at 60 MeV
- Root mean square radius of the real folding potential for the 0₂⁺ state: 4.38 fm, compared to 3.58 fm for the ground state at both energies
- First Airy minimum A1 position at 50.5 MeV: 37° for elastic scattering and ~60° for the 0₂⁺ state
- First Airy minimum A1 position at 60 MeV: 35° for elastic scattering and 55° for the 0₂⁺ state
- Vacuum pressure during experiment: 2.3×10⁻⁶ Pa

## Context
This work extends previous studies of refractive ³He scattering from ¹²C at 34.7 and 72 MeV by Ohkubo (2007) and is consistent with results from α-particle scattering on ¹²C by Ohkubo (2004, 2007, 2008). The observation of a shifted Airy minimum for the Hoyle state provides experimental evidence supporting the alpha-particle condensate model proposed by Tohsaki (2001) and the earlier 3α cluster model calculations by Uegaki (1977) and Kamimura (1978). This enables future studies of dilute cluster states in other light nuclei using refractive scattering as a probe of nuclear radii.
