# Kaon HBT radii from perfect fluid dynamics using the Buda-Lund model

> In this paper we summarize the ellipsoidally symmetric Buda-Lund model's results on HBT radii. We calculate the Bose-Einstein correlation function from the model and derive formulas for the transverse momentum dependence of the correlation radii in the Bertsch-Pratt system of out, side and longitudinal directions. We show a comparison to $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200 GeV$ RHIC PHENIX two-pion correlation data and make prediction on the same observable for different particles.

# Kaon HBT radii from perfect fluid dynamics using the Buda-Lund model

## Key claim
The Buda-Lund hydro model predicts that two-kaon HBT radii, when plotted against transverse mass $m_t$, fall on the same universal curve as two-pion HBT radii, based on an ellipsoidally symmetric perfect fluid description of Au+Au collisions at RHIC.

## Method
The authors derived the two-particle Bose-Einstein correlation function from the Buda-Lund emission function, which describes an expanding fireball with ellipsoidal symmetry and includes a core-halo model for long-lived resonances. They obtained analytic formulas for the Bertsch-Pratt HBT radii (out, side, longitudinal) in the LCMS frame after azimuthal averaging, and compared these to existing PHENIX two-pion correlation data at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV. The same formulas were then used to generate predictions for kaon HBT radii.

## Key numbers
- The comparison to data uses $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC from PHENIX (Adler 2004).
- The correlation function intercept parameter $\lambda$ is defined as the square of the ratio of particles emitted from the core versus from the halo (Csorgo 1994).
- The model parameters include central freeze-out temperature $T_0$, mean emission duration $\Delta \tau$, freeze-out time $\tau_0$, and spatial and time-like temperature gradients $a^2$ and $d^2$.

## Context
This work extends the Buda-Lund hydro model, which had previously been successfully applied to single particle spectra and HBT radii for pions at both $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=130$ GeV and $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV (Csanad 2003, Csanad 2004), as well as to data from CERN SPS Pb+Pb collisions (Ster 1999) and p+p collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV (Csorgo 2004). The prediction that kaon HBT radii scale with $m_t$ in the same way as pions enables experimental tests of the universal scaling behavior predicted by perfect fluid hydrodynamics, and can be checked against future PHENIX or other RHIC kaon correlation measurements.
