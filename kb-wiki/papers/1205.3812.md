# Light-Front Dynamic Analysis of Bound States in Scalar Field Model

> The light-front dynamics (LFD) of the scalar field model theory is analyzed to solve the two-body bound-state problem. The light-front two-body bound-state equation is extended to the full LFD kernel including the ladder, cross-ladder, stretched-box, and particle-antiparticle creation/annihilation effects to study the contributions of higher Fock-states. The light-front two-body equation is also modified by the term corresponding to the self-energy corrections and counter-terms. Using the variational principle, we obtain the numerical result of the binding energy B versus the coupling constant α for various mass ratios of the constituent particles including the cases of non-zero exchange particle mass. We also discuss the correlation between the mass spectrum and the corresponding bound-state wavefunction.

## Key claim
The paper extends light-front bound-state analysis of scalar field models beyond the Wick-Cutkosky model (equal constituent masses, zero exchange mass) to include unequal masses and non-zero exchange particle mass, incorporating for the first time the particle-antiparticle creation/annihilation higher-Fock contributions that were omitted in prior cross-ladder analyses by Carbonell and Karmanov (2006).

## Method
The authors formulate the two-body bound-state equation in light-front dynamics (LFD) with a kernel including ladder, stretched-box, cross-ladder, and higher-Fock (particle-antiparticle creation/annihilation) terms up to order g^4, plus self-energy corrections and counter-terms. They solve the equation variationally using a trial wavefunction based on the Hulthen potential solution, minimizing the coupling constant α for fixed binding energy B across various mass ratios β = m1/m2 and exchange masses λ.

## Key numbers
- For β=1, λ=0: the ladder result agrees with previous numerical data from Mangin-Brinet and Carbonell (1999); the analytic ladder-plus-self-energy formula yields the non-relativistic Balmer formula 2m-M = mα²/4 in the zero-binding limit.
- For β=1, λ=2.5: the coupling constant α reaches 57.4 when the binding energy B equals 2m (in units where m2=1).
- For β=5.446×10⁻⁴, λ=2.5: the coupling constant α reaches 25.45 as the binding energy B approaches m.
- The particle-antiparticle creation/annihilation contribution (V^HF) is most significant for β≠1 and λ≠0, and non-negligible for β=1, λ≠0 and β≠1, λ=0 cases.

## Context
This work extends the light-front bound-state analyses of the Wick-Cutkosky model by Ji (1985, 1994) and the cross-ladder and stretched-box calculations by Karmanov and Carbonell (2005, 2006), which did not include particle-antiparticle creation/annihilation effects. The results enable systematic inclusion of higher Fock-state contributions in scalar field models, providing a foundation for future application to QCD bound-state problems. The variational wavefunction based on the Hulthen potential (Hulthen, 1941, 1942) proves suitable for non-zero exchange particle mass cases.
