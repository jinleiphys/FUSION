# The effect of electric and chiral magnetic conductivities on azimuthally fluctuating electromagnetic fields and observables in isobar collisions

> We study the space-time evolution of electromagnetic fields along with the azimuthal fluctuations of these fields and their correlation with the initial matter geometry specified by the participant plane in the presence of finite electric $\left(σ\right)$ and chiral magnetic $\left(σ_χ\right)$ conductivities in Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. We observe the partially asymmetric behavior of the spatial distributions of the electric and magnetic fields in a conducting medium when compared to the Lienard-Wiechert (L-W) solutions, and deceleration of the decay of the fields is observed in both isobar collisions. While studying the correlation between the magnetic field direction and the participant plane, we see the sizeable suppression of the correlation in the presence of finite conductivities when compared to the L-W case, reflecting the importance of taking into account the medium properties such as conductivities while calculating the magnetic field induced observable quantities.

# The effect of electric and chiral magnetic conductivities on azimuthally fluctuating electromagnetic fields and observables in isobar collisions

## Key claim

The correlation between the magnetic field direction and the participant plane is sizeably suppressed in the presence of finite electric and chiral magnetic conductivities compared to the Lienard-Wiechert vacuum case, indicating that medium properties must be included when calculating magnetic-field-induced observables such as the chiral magnetic effect signal in isobar collisions.

## Method

The authors used a Monte-Carlo Glauber model with Woods-Saxon nuclear density profiles (including deformed and halo-type parameter sets) to generate event-by-event spatial distributions of source protons in Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at 200 GeV. They solved Maxwell equations with finite electric conductivity (sigma = 5.8 MeV) and chiral magnetic conductivity (sigma_chi = 1.5 MeV) using Green's function methods in cylindrical coordinates, and compared the resulting electromagnetic fields and their azimuthal correlations with the participant plane to the Lienard-Wiechert vacuum solutions over 100,000 events per impact parameter.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strength ratio eB_y(Ru)/eB_y(Zr) is approximately 1.1, consistent with the 10% difference in proton numbers between Ru (Z=44) and Zr (Z=40)
- Electric conductivity value used: sigma = 5.8 MeV; chiral magnetic conductivity value used: sigma_chi = 1.5 MeV
- For the finite conductivity case, the magnetic field reaches its maximum strength at t_Q approximately 0.05 fm/c for impact parameter b=8 fm and approximately 0.03 fm/c for b=4 fm
- In Ru+Ru collisions at b=9 fm, protons in spectators alone produce about 4% less B_y than protons in the entire nucleus

## Context

This work extends prior studies of electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions by Bzdak (2011), Deng (2012), and Zhao (2019) by incorporating medium feedback effects through electric and chiral magnetic conductivities, following the analytic framework of Li (2016) and Siddique (2021, 2022). The study is motivated by the STAR isobar run comparing Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions to search for the chiral magnetic effect, as proposed by Voloshin (2010). The results enable more realistic predictions for CME observables such as the Delta-gamma correlator, which depends on the squared magnetic field and its azimuthal correlation with the participant plane.
