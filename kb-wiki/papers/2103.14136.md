# Variation of delta baryon mass and hybrid star properties in static and rotating conditions

> The possible conditions for hadron-quark phase transition in hybrid star cores are investigated in the present work. For the hadronic matter part the effective chiral model is adopted. Exotic baryonic degrees like hyperons and the delta baryons are also taken into account. As $Δ$s posses Breit-Wigner mass distribution ($1232 \pm 120$ MeV), the hadronic equation of state is obtained by varying the mass of the delta baryons in this range. For the quark phase the MIT bag model is chosen with repulsive effects of the unpaired quarks. Phase transition is achieved using Gibbs construction and the gross properties of the resultant hybrid star are calculated in both static and rotating conditions and compared with the various constraints on them from different observational and empirical perspectives. The work presents a thorough study of the phase transition properties like the critical density of appearance of quarks, the density range for the persistence of the mixed phase and the population of different hadrons and quarks in hybrid star matter. The hybrid star properties, calculated in both static and rotating conditions, are found to be consistent with the bounds on them from different perspectives.

## Key claim
The paper investigates whether hybrid stars (with a hadron-quark phase transition) can satisfy observational constraints, finding that the mass of the delta baryon (varied within its Breit-Wigner distribution of 1232 ± 120 MeV) critically determines whether the resulting hybrid star meets the maximum mass limits from pulsars like PSR J0348+0432 and PSR J0740+6620, with only the higher delta masses (1232 and 1352 MeV) yielding consistent results.

## Method
The hadronic phase was described using an effective chiral model including the baryon octet (nucleons and hyperons) and the delta quartet, with delta-meson couplings fixed at (x_σΔ, x_ωΔ, x_ρΔ) = (1.35, 1.0, 1.0). The quark phase was modeled with the MIT bag model (bag constant B^{1/4} = 150 MeV, repulsive parameter α_4 = 0.5), and the hadron-quark phase transition was computed using Gibbs construction (assuming low surface tension) for three delta masses: 1112, 1232, and 1352 MeV. Static and rotating (up to Kepler frequency) hybrid star properties were then obtained by solving the TOV equations and using the RNS code, respectively.

## Key numbers
- Delta baryon Breit-Wigner mass distribution: 1232 ± 120 MeV (range 1112 to 1352 MeV).
- Mixed phase density ranges: for m_Δ = 1112 MeV, (2.3–4.1)ρ_0; for m_Δ = 1232 MeV, (3.5–5.8)ρ_0; for m_Δ = 1352 MeV, (4.0–7.1)ρ_0, where ρ_0 ≈ 0.153 fm^{-3}.
- Maximum gravitational masses for hybrid stars: 1.91 M_⊙ (m_Δ = 1112 MeV), 1.98 M_⊙ (m_Δ = 1232 MeV), 2.06 M_⊙ (m_Δ = 1352 MeV); the latter two satisfy the PSR J0740+6620 constraint (2.14^{+0.10}_{-0.09} M_⊙ at 68.3%).
- Radii at 1.4 M_⊙ (R_{1.4}): 11.8 km (m_Δ = 1112 MeV), 12.4 km (m_Δ = 1232 MeV), 12.6 km (m_Δ = 1352 MeV), consistent with GW170817 bounds.
- Maximum surface redshift: 0.57 (m_Δ = 1232 MeV), 0.55 (m_Δ = 1352 MeV), 0.54 (m_Δ = 1112 MeV), all satisfying observational limits from 1E 1207.4-5209 (Z_s = 0.12–0.23) and RX J0720.4-3125 (Z_s = 0.205^{+0.006}_{-0.003}).
- Speed of sound squared peaks in the mixed phase: 0.40 at 3.3ρ_0 (m_Δ = 1112 MeV), 0.57 at 4.5ρ_0 (m_Δ = 1232 MeV), 0.80 at 5.7ρ_0 (m_Δ = 1352 MeV), all within the causal limit (C_s^2 < 1).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of hybrid stars with hyperons and delta baryons (e.g., Drago (2014), Cai, Sun, Sen (2020)) by systematically varying the delta baryon mass within its experimental width and applying Gibbs construction for the phase transition. It contrasts with earlier predictions by Glendenning (1985) that delta formation is disfavored, showing instead that deltas can appear and significantly affect the equation of state. The results enable future comparisons with multi-messenger constraints from GW170817 and NICER (e.g., Miller (2019)), and provide a framework for testing delta mass effects in rotating hybrid star models against rapidly rotating pulsar data like PSR J1748-2446ad.
