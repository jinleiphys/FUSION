# Fusion at near-barrier energies within quantum diffusion approach

> The nuclear deformation and neutron-transfer process have been identified as playing a major role in the magnitude of the sub-barrier fusion (capture) cross sections. There are a several experimental evidences which confirm the importance of nuclear deformation on the fusion. The influence of nuclear deformation is straightforward. If the target nucleus is prolate in the ground state, the Coulomb field on its tips is lower than on its sides, that then increases the capture or fusion probability at energies below the barrier corresponding to the spherical nuclei. The role of neutron transfer reactions is less clear. The importance of neutron transfer with positive Q-values on nuclear fusion (capture) originates from the fact that neutrons are insensitive to the Coulomb barrier and therefore they can start being transferred at larger separations before the projectile is captured by target-nucleus. Therefore, it is generally thought that the sub-barrier fusion cross section will increase because of the neutron transfer. The fusion (capture) dynamics induced by loosely bound radioactive ion beams is currently being extensively studied. However, the long-standing question whether fusion (capture) is enhanced or suppressed with these beams has not yet been answered unambiguously. The study of the fusion reactions involving nuclei at the drip-lines has led to contradictory results.

## Key claim

The quantum diffusion approach can describe sub-barrier capture cross sections by modeling the coupling of relative motion to neutron transfer channels, but the effect of neutron transfer on capture is not universally enhancing: it can enhance, suppress, or leave unchanged the sub-barrier cross section depending on whether the deformations of the interacting nuclei increase, decrease, or stay the same after the transfer.

## Method

The authors model capture using a single collective variable (the relative distance between colliding nuclei) within a quantum diffusion formalism that includes fluctuation and dissipation effects. The nucleus-nucleus potential is calculated with a double-folding Skyrme-type interaction, and the capture probability is obtained by approximating the barrier as an inverted oscillator and integrating a propagator from an initial state to the final state. The effect of one- or two-neutron transfer with positive Q-values is included by assuming transfer occurs just before barrier crossing, changing the mass numbers, isotopic composition, and deformation parameters of the interacting nuclei, which in turn alters the Coulomb barrier height and shape.

## Context

This work extends the quantum diffusion approach previously developed by the same group (Sargsyan et al., various years) to systematically study the role of neutron transfer in sub-barrier fusion across many reaction systems. It contradicts the simple expectation that positive-Q-value neutron transfer always enhances sub-barrier fusion, showing instead that the effect depends on whether the deformations of the colliding nuclei increase or decrease after transfer. The paper enables future studies of breakup effects in reactions with weakly bound nuclei, particularly for systems like \(^{15}\)C+\(^{208}\)Pb and \(^{15}\)C+\(^{232}\)Th where the model cannot explain experimental enhancements at the lowest energies, suggesting the need for additional experimental and theoretical investigations.
