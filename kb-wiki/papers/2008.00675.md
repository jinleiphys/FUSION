# Doubly charmed multibaryon systems

> We study two- and three-baryon systems with two units of charm looking for possible bound states or resonances. All two-baryon interactions are consistently derived from a constituent quark model tuned in the light-flavor hadron phenomenology: spectra and interactions. The presence of the heavy quarks makes the two-body interactions simpler than in the light-flavor sector. Our results show a narrow two-body resonance with quantum numbers $(I,J^P)=(0,0^+)$. It is located 6.2 MeV below the $Σ_cΣ_c$ threshold and has a width of 4.7 MeV. The foregoing two-body state contributes to generate a $N Σ_cΣ_c$ resonance with quantum numbers $(I,J^P)=(1/2,1/2^+)$ and a separation energy of 0.2 MeV.

# Doubly charmed multibaryon systems

## Key claim

The authors predict a narrow two-body resonance in the $(I,J^P)=(0,0^+)$ channel, located 6.2 MeV below the $\Sigma_c\Sigma_c$ threshold with a width of 4.7 MeV, and a three-body $N\Sigma_c\Sigma_c$ resonance with quantum numbers $(I,J^P)=(1/2,1/2^+)$ and a separation energy of 0.2 MeV. However, the three-body result is conditional on using the best suited value of the charm quark Gaussian size parameter ($b_c=0.2$ fm) and on excluding the repulsive triplet isospin $3/2$ $N\Sigma_c$ amplitude; with the default parameter value the three-body resonance signal is lost.

## Method

The authors derived all two-baryon interactions ($N\Lambda_c$, $N\Sigma_c$, $\Lambda_c\Lambda_c$, $\Sigma_c\Sigma_c$) from a constituent quark model (CQM) previously tuned to light-flavor hadron spectra and $NN$ phase shifts. They solved coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations for the two-body $(I,J^P)=(0,0^+)$ system coupling $\Lambda_c\Lambda_c$ and $\Sigma_c\Sigma_c$ channels, and solved Faddeev equations for the three-body $N\Lambda_c\Lambda_c - N\Sigma_c\Sigma_c$ system. The width of the two-body resonance was extracted using the Breit-Wigner formula and verified by analytic continuation of the $S$ matrix on the second Riemann sheet.

## Key numbers

- Two-body $(I,J^P)=(0,0^+)$ resonance: located 6.2 MeV below the $\Sigma_c\Sigma_c$ threshold, with a width of 4.7 MeV; the pole position is $(-6.2 - i\,4.7/2)$ MeV relative to the $\Sigma_c\Sigma_c$ threshold.
- The uncoupled $\Sigma_c\Sigma_c$ system (without $\Lambda_c\Lambda_c$ coupling) shows a bound state 16.2 MeV below the $\Sigma_c\Sigma_c$ threshold, with scattering length 1.79 fm and effective range 0.44 fm.
- $\Lambda_c\Lambda_c$ scattering length: $-6.45$ fm (singlet); $\Lambda_c N$ scattering lengths: $-0.86$ fm (singlet), $-2.31$ fm (triplet).
- $\Sigma_c N$ (isospin $1/2$) scattering lengths: $0.74 - i\,0.18$ fm (singlet), $-5.21 - i\,1.96$ fm (triplet); $\Sigma_c N$ (isospin $3/2$): $-1.25$ fm (singlet), $0.95$ fm (triplet).
- Three-body $N\Sigma_c\Sigma_c$ resonance with $(I,J^P)=(1/2,1/2^+)$: separation energy of 0.2 MeV (using $b_c=0.2$ fm).
- Mass difference between $\Lambda_c\Lambda_c$ and $\Sigma_c\Sigma_c$ thresholds: 338 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model approach previously applied to light-flavor baryon-baryon interactions (Valcarce et al., 2005; Garcilazo et al., 2007) and to charmed baryon-nucleon systems (Garcilazo and Valcarce, 2019) into the doubly charmed multibaryon sector. The two-body results are compared with lattice QCD estimations (Miyamoto et al., 2018) and with other phenomenological approaches (Lee et al., 2011; Meguro et al., 2011; Huang et al., 2014). The mechanism generating the narrow resonance, a bound state in the upper $\Sigma_c\Sigma_c$ channel decaying through coupling to the lower $\Lambda_c\Lambda_c$ channel, is related to the "synchronization of resonances" proposed by Bugg (2008) and to the $d^*(2380)$ resonance in the $\Delta\Delta$ system. The results enable experimental searches for doubly charmed baryonic molecules at LHC, J-PARC, and RHIC.
