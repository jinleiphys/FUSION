# Statistical repulsion on hyperons in two-color dense QCD

> We investigate the onset of hyperons in baryonic (diquark) matter in two-color QCD (QC$_2$D) by introducing heavy quark doublets that emulate strange quarks. An even number of flavors is required to avoid the sign problem in lattice Monte Carlo simulations. To explore QC$_2$D matter containing both light and heavy quarks, we construct a model in which quarks interact with light-light, light-heavy (hyperonic), and heavy-heavy diquarks via Yukawa couplings. As the quark chemical potential increases, the light diquarks condense first and form baryonic matter, and this onset density can be understood in hadronic terms. In contrast, the onset density of hyperons is substantially higher than that estimated from the hadronic sector of the model. This shift reflects an effective repulsion among baryons induced by the pre-occupied light quarks. The Pauli blocking of light quarks suppresses the attractive diquark correlations responsible, in vacuum, for making hyperons lighter than the sum of the constituent light and heavy quark masses. Implications for three-color QCD are also briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The onset density of hyperons in two-color dense QCD is substantially higher than the naive hadronic estimate (μ_B = m_Y) because Pauli blocking from pre-occupied light quarks suppresses the attractive diquark correlations that make hyperons lighter than the sum of their constituent quark masses in vacuum.

## Method

The authors construct a Yukawa model for two-color QCD with light (u,d) and heavy (Q_u,Q_d) quark doublets, where heavy quarks emulate strange quarks while preserving an even number of flavors to avoid the sign problem in lattice simulations. They compute the mean-field effective potential up to one fermion loop, treating the hyperon fields (light-heavy diquarks) perturbatively to second order, and determine the critical chemical potential for hyperon onset by examining the sign of the quadratic term in the hyperon fields.

## Key numbers

- The light diquark condensation onset occurs at μ = m_π/2, where m_π is the pion mass
- The hyperon mass m_Y is expected to be of order the kaon mass or roughly the sum of constituent light and heavy quark masses (heavy quark constituent mass ~500 MeV)
- The heavy quark mass M_Q is taken to be larger than the chemical potential μ (M_Q > μ) in the density regime studied
- The nuclear saturation density n_0 is approximately 0.16 fm^{-3}, with purely nucleonic computations valid up to roughly 1.5-2 n_0 (from cited prior work)

## Context

This work extends the concept of "quark saturation" (Kojo 2021) and statistical repulsion among baryons (Fujimoto 2024) to the two-color QCD framework, where lattice simulations are feasible without the sign problem. The authors build on earlier two-color QCD studies (Takahashi 2009, Iida 2020, Itou 2025) and the IdylliQ model for quarkyonic matter (Fujimoto 2023). The results suggest a mechanism to address the hyperon puzzle in neutron stars that is more fundamental than many-body force descriptions (Lonardoni 2014, Tong 2025), and the framework enables direct comparison with future lattice QC₂D simulations to test the statistical repulsion mechanism.
