# Relaxation time for the alignment between the spin of a finite-mass quark/antiquark and the thermal vorticity in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study the relaxation time required for the alignment between the spin of a finite-mass quark/antiquark and the thermal vorticity, at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential, in the context of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The relaxation time is computed as the inverse of the total reaction rate that in turn is obtained from the imaginary part of the quark/antiquark self-energy. We model the interaction between spin and thermal vorticity within the medium by means of a vertex coupling quarks and thermal gluons that, for a uniform temperature, is proportional to the global angular velocity and inversely proportional to the temperature. We use realistic estimates for the angular velocities for different collision energies and show that the effect of the quark mass is to reduce the relaxation times as compared to the massless quark case. Using these relaxation times we estimate the intrinsic quark and antiquark polarizations produced by the thermal vorticity. We conclude by pointing out that, in spite of the former being larger than the latter, it is still possible to understand recent results from the STAR Beam Energy Scan when accounting for the fact that only a portion of quarks/antiquarks come from the denser and thus thermalized region in the collision.

## Key claim

The relaxation time for spin-vorticity alignment is *reduced* for massive quarks (strange quark mass ~100 MeV) compared to massless quarks, contrary to the naive expectation that reduced phase space would increase it; this reduction arises from new terms in the polarization coefficients that compensate the phase-space restriction. The intrinsic quark polarization is larger than the antiquark polarization, yet the experimentally observed larger \(\overline{\Lambda}\) polarization can still be understood when accounting for the core-corona structure of the collision region.

## Method

The relaxation time is computed as the inverse of the total interaction rate, obtained from the imaginary part of the quark self-energy at one-loop order in a thermal field theory framework. The interaction between spin and thermal vorticity is modeled by an effective vertex coupling quarks to thermal gluons, proportional to the global angular velocity \(\omega\) and inversely proportional to temperature \(T\). The gluon propagator is treated in the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) approximation, and the calculation is performed for massive quarks with arbitrary momentum, removing the earlier massless and small-momentum approximations. The initial angular velocities for Au+Au collisions at various impact parameters and collision energies are obtained from UrQMD simulations.

## Key numbers

- Relaxation time \(\tau \lesssim 5\) fm for quarks in the temperature range \(150\ \mathrm{MeV} < T < 200\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for semicentral collisions at \(b=10\) fm and \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=10,200\) GeV.
- Relaxation time \(\bar{\tau} \lesssim 5\) fm for antiquarks in the same temperature range and collision parameters.
- For quarks, \(\tau\) shows monotonic growth with collision energy across the range studied; for antiquarks, \(\bar{\tau}\) has a minimum for collision energies in the range \(40\ \mathrm{GeV} \lesssim \sqrt{s_{NN}} \lesssim 70\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
- Intrinsic global polarization for both quarks and antiquarks tends to 1 for \(t \simeq 10\) fm at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=19.6\) GeV and \(b=8\) fm, with antiquark polarization \(\bar{z}\) smaller than quark polarization \(z\).
- Angular velocities used: \(\omega \simeq 0.072, 0.051\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=10,200\) GeV at \(b=10\) fm; \(\omega \simeq 0.12, 0.10\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) for the massless comparison case.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation for massless quarks (Ayala 2020) by including finite quark mass and arbitrary momentum, showing that the relaxation time is reduced relative to the massless case. It provides the relaxation times needed to compute intrinsic quark/antiquark polarizations, which can then be translated into \(\Lambda\) and \(\overline{\Lambda}\) polarizations via a core-corona model (Ayala CoreCorona). The results help interpret the STAR Beam Energy Scan data (STAR 2018), where \(\overline{\Lambda}\) polarization rises more steeply than \(\Lambda\) polarization at lower collision energies. Other approaches to compute the relaxation time have been reported by Kapusta (2020), Liu (2020), and Shi (2020).
