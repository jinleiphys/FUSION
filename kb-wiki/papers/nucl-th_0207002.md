# Scaling Behavior of $(N_{ch})^{-1}dN_{ch}/dη$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130$ GeV by the PHOBOS Collaboration and Its Implication --A Possible Explanation Employing the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck Process--

> Recently, interesting data concerning $dN_{ch}/dη$ in Au-Au collisions [$η=-\ln \tan (θ/2)$] with centrality cuts have been reported from the PHOBOS Collaboration. In most treatment these data are divided by the number of participants (nucleons) in collisions. Instead of this method, we use the total multiplicity $N_{ch} = \int (dN_{ch}/dη)dη$ and find that there is scaling phenomenon among $(N_{ch})^{-1}dN_{ch}/dη= dn/dη$ with different centrality cuts at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130$ GeV. To explain this scaling behavior of $dn/dη$, we employ a stochastic approach using the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process with two sources. A Langevin equation is adopted for this explanation. Moreover, comparisons of $dn/dη$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130$ GeV with that at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV are made, and no significant difference is found. A possible method fot the detection of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) through $dN_{ch}/dη$ is presented.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the PHOBOS data for $(N_{ch})^{-1}dN_{ch}/d\eta$ in Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130$ GeV exhibit a scaling behavior across different centrality cuts, which can be explained by an Ornstein-Uhlenbeck stochastic process with two sources, but they hedge that this scaling suggests no singular QGP signature is visible in the averaged quantity, though QGP could still exist with its signature washed out by strong interactions.

## Method

The authors renormalize PHOBOS Collaboration data for $dN_{ch}/d\eta$ by dividing by the total multiplicity $N_{ch} = \int (dN_{ch}/d\eta)d\eta$ to obtain $dn/d\eta$, which they treat as a probability density. They then model $dn/d\eta$ using a Langevin equation for the rapidity variable $y$, assuming two initial sources at $\pm y_{max}$ (representing beam and target leading particles), and solve for the Gaussian distribution given by the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process. They fit this two-source Gaussian to the data for six centrality cuts at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130$ GeV and compare with data at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV.

## Key numbers

- Intercept of $dn/d\eta$ at $\eta = 0$: $c \approx 0.129 \pm 0.005$ (dimensionless, from PHOBOS data at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130$ GeV)
- For centrality 0-6% at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130$ GeV: $N_{ch} = 4070 \pm 63$, $\langle N_{part}\rangle = 340$, $V^2(t) = 2.47 \pm 0.08$, $\chi^2/{\rm n.d.f.} = 3.82/51$
- For centrality 45-55% at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130$ GeV: $N_{ch} = 662 \pm 10$, $V^2(t) = 3.83 \pm 0.27$, $\chi^2/{\rm n.d.f.} = 8.61/51$
- Parameter $p = 1 - e^{-2\gamma t}$ ranges from $0.872 \pm 0.006$ (45-55%) to $0.888 \pm 0.004$ (0-6%) at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130$ GeV
- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV for centrality 0-6%: $p = 0.879 \pm 0.007$, $V^2(t) = 2.67 \pm 0.24$, $c = 0.126$, $\chi^2/{\rm n.d.f.} = 0.63/22$
- $\eta_{rms} = \sqrt{\langle \eta^2 \rangle}$ ranges from 2.52 (45-55%) to 2.33 (0-6%) at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130$ GeV
- Estimated energy density from Bjorken picture: $\varepsilon \sim 1$ GeV/fm$^3$ at 130 GeV, $\varepsilon \sim 1.2$ GeV/fm$^3$ at 200 GeV

## Context

This work extends the stochastic approach to multiparticle production previously applied to KNO scaling (Biyajima 1984) and QCD calculations (Dokshitzer 1993), and contrasts with the standard PHOBOS analysis (Back 2001) which divided by $\langle N_{part}\rangle/2$ rather than $N_{ch}$. The authors note that the NA50 Collaboration (Abreu 2002) found similar $\eta$-scaling in Pb+Pb collisions at SPS described by a single Gaussian, supporting their stochastic approach. The scaling result enables event-by-event analyses using intermittency (Bialas 1986) and wavelets (Suzuki 1995) to search for QGP signatures, and the authors suggest that smaller centrality cuts (0-3% to 0-5%) are needed to probe the fragmentation region more carefully.
