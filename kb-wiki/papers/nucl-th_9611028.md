# Density dependence of the MIT bag parameters from the field theory of hadrons

> A self-consistent description of the MIT bag parameters as functions of the nuclear matter density is presented. The subnuclear degrees of freedom are treated in the Quark-Meson Coupling Model, considering the equilibrium conditions for the bag in the nuclear medium. The hadronic interaction is described in the framework of the quantum field theory of hadrons through several models. We have obtained the behavior of the bag radius and the bag parameters $B$ and $z_0$, taking their derivatives with respect to the mean field value of the scalar meson as free parameters. A discussion on the variation range of these derivatives is given.

## Key claim
The MIT bag parameters (radius, bag constant B, and zero-point parameter z0) can be described as functions of nuclear matter density by imposing self-consistency between the Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) model and Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD), but only if the derivatives dB/dσ̄ and dz0/dσ̄ are treated as free parameters whose allowed range determines whether the bag picture remains stable at high density.

## Method
The authors couple the QMC model, where nucleons are MIT bags containing quarks interacting via scalar (σ) and vector (ω) mesons, to four different QHD hadronic interaction models (differing in the nucleon-scalar meson coupling term V_Nσ). They impose two equilibrium conditions: equality of the bag mass and the QHD effective nucleon mass, and equality of the internal bag pressure and the external hadronic pressure. To close the system, they treat the derivatives dB/dσ̄ and dz0/dσ̄ as constant free parameters and solve self-consistently for the bag radius R, bag constant B, and zero-point parameter z0 as functions of baryon density.

## Key numbers
- At zero baryon density, the bag radius is fixed to R0 = 0.6 fm and the quark mass to m_q = 10 MeV.
- Two parameter sets are used: set I (λ = dB/dσ̄ = -5.28 fm⁻³, μ = dz0/dσ̄ = -0.50 fm) and set II (λ = 0, μ = 1.6 fm).
- For model 4 with set II, the bag radius increases by 12% at saturation density (ρ = 0.15 fm⁻³) compared to its vacuum value, while B decreases by 19%.
- The isothermal compressibility κ at saturation density is constrained to the range 100-300 MeV, used to evaluate the hadronic models.
- The binding energy per nucleon is fixed to Eb = 16 MeV at saturation density ρ = 0.15 fm⁻³.

## Context
This work extends the QMC model of Guichon (1988) and the later work of Saito and Thomas (1994), who established a relationship between QMC and the Walecka model of QHD. It contrasts with the phenomenological approach of Jin and Jennings (1995), who introduced density-dependent bag parameters with no direct dynamical interpretation; in that work, B/B0 decreased by 80% and the bag radius increased by 60% at saturation density, whereas the present approach yields more moderate changes (12% radius increase, 19% B decrease). The results enable future studies of thermal effects and the EMC effect within this self-consistent framework.
