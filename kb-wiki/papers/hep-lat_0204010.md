# The QCD thermal phase transition in the presence of a small chemical potential

> We propose a new method to investigate the thermal properties of QCD with a small quark chemical potential $μ$. Derivatives of the phase transition point with respect to $μ$ are computed at $μ=0$ for 2 flavors of p-4 improved staggered fermions with $ma=0.1,0.2$ on a $16^3\times4$ lattice. The resulting Taylor expansion is well behaved for the small values of $μ_{\rm q}/T_c\sim0.1$ relevant for RHIC phenomenology, and predicts a critical curve $T_c(μ)$ in reasonable agreement with estimates obtained using exact reweighting. In addition, we contrast the case of isoscalar and isovector chemical potentials, quantify the effect of $μ\not=0$ on the equation of state, and comment on the complex phase of the fermion determinant in QCD with $μ\not=0$.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a Taylor expansion method to study QCD at small chemical potential, computing derivatives of observables with respect to $\mu$ at $\mu=0$ rather than performing direct simulations at non-zero $\mu$. The method is claimed to be well-behaved for $\mu_{\rm q}/T_c \sim 0.1$, the regime relevant for RHIC phenomenology, but the authors note that higher-order terms or non-analytic behavior may become relevant for larger $\mu/T$ values.

## Method

The authors compute derivatives of the fermion determinant and chiral condensate with respect to chemical potential $\mu$ at $\mu=0$ using a random noise method on a $16^3\times4$ lattice with 2 flavors of p-4 improved staggered fermions at quark masses $ma=0.1$ and $0.2$. They use these derivatives to construct a Taylor expansion of the reweighting factor and observables up to $O(\mu^2)$, then determine the pseudocritical coupling $\beta_c(\mu)$ from the peak positions of the Polyakov loop susceptibility and chiral condensate susceptibility. The beta-function from string tension data is used to convert lattice results into physical units for the transition temperature $T_c(\mu)$.

## Key numbers

- Second derivative of the critical coupling: ${\rm d}^2 \beta_c/{\rm d} \mu^2 = -1.20(44)$ at $m=0.1$ from chiral susceptibility
- Second derivative of the critical coupling: ${\rm d}^2 \beta_c/{\rm d} \mu^2 = -1.02(56)$ at $m=0.2$ from chiral susceptibility
- Second derivative of the critical coupling: ${\rm d}^2 \beta_c/{\rm d} \mu^2 = -1.01(23)$ at $m=0.2$ from Polyakov loop susceptibility
- Beta-function at $(\beta,m)=(3.65,0.1)$: $a^{-1}({\rm d}a/{\rm d}\beta) = -2.08(43)$
- Curvature of transition line: $T_c({\rm d}^2 T_c/{\rm d}\mu^2_{\rm q}) \approx -0.14$ at $m=0.1$
- Lattice size: $16^3 \times 4$
- Quark masses: $ma = 0.1$ and $0.2$, corresponding to $m_{PS}/m_{V} \approx 0.70$ and $0.85$ respectively
- For isovector chemical potential at $m=0.2$: ${\rm d}^2 \beta_c/{\rm d} \mu_I^2 = -0.96(19)$ from $\chi_L$ and $-0.93(52)$ from $\chi_{\bar\psi\psi}$

## Context

This work extends the reweighting method of Fodor and Katz (2002) for studying QCD at non-zero chemical potential by introducing a Taylor expansion approach that avoids direct calculation of the fermion determinant at non-zero $\mu$. The method is complementary to the exact reweighting approach and is not restricted to small lattice volumes, as it requires only expectation values of local fermion bilinears. The results enable future studies of higher-order contributions in the expansion and provide a framework for investigating the QCD phase diagram at small chemical potential relevant for heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC, where $\mu_{\rm q} \sim 15$ MeV.
