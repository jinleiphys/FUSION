# Exploring Excited Hadrons in Lattice QCD

> Progress in extracting the spectrum of excited hadron resonances in lattice QCD Monte Carlo calculations is reviewed and the key issues and challenges in such computations are outlined. The importance of multi-hadron states as simulations with lighter pion masses are done is discussed, and the need for all-to-all quark propagators is emphasized.

# Exploring Excited Hadrons in Lattice QCD

## Key claim

Progress in extracting the excited hadron resonance spectrum from lattice QCD Monte Carlo calculations is reviewed, but the author emphasizes that most excited hadrons are unstable resonances, so finite-box stationary-state energies obtained from the lattice must be interpreted carefully, and that as simulations move to lighter pion masses, multi-hadron states lying below the excited resonances will require multi-hadron operators and all-to-all quark propagators.

## Method

The approach uses Markov-chain Monte Carlo calculations of temporal correlation functions on a space-time lattice, combining gauge-field smearing via the stout-link procedure, a new quark-field smearing scheme called Laplacian Heaviside (LAPH), covariantly-displaced smeared quark fields as building blocks, and group-theoretical projections onto irreducible representations of the octahedral point group O_h to construct hadron operators. Correlation matrices C_ij(t) are computed for a large set of N operators, and principal effective masses W_alpha(t) from the eigenvalues of C(t_0)^{-1/2} C(t) C(t_0)^{-1/2} are used to extract the eigenenergies of the lowest N states. Stochastic estimation of all-to-all quark propagators with diluted noise vectors is employed to enable multi-hadron operator evaluations.

## Key numbers

- Lattice size: 24^3 × 64, with N_f = 2 configurations using a stout-smeared clover fermion action and Symanzik-improved anisotropic gauge action.
- Lattice spacing: a_s ~ 0.1 fm, with anisotropy a_s / a_t ~ 3.
- Two pion masses used: m_pi = 416 MeV (430 gauge configurations) and m_pi = 578 MeV (363 gauge configurations).
- Displacement length for extended baryon operators: 3a_s (~0.3 fm).
- For the single-site nucleon operator at temporal separation t = 5a_t, time+spin+color+even/odd-space dilution yields an error comparable with the gauge-noise limit using only a single noise vector.
- First excited state in the G_1g channel at these heavy pion masses is significantly higher than the experimentally measured Roper resonance.
- Clear evidence found for a 5/2^- state in the pattern of negative-parity excited states, realized for the first time on the lattice.

## Context

This work extends the variational techniques for extracting excited hadron spectra originally developed by Wolff (1990) and builds on the operator construction methods described in the baryon spectroscopy literature (Morningstar et al., baryons1). The specific nucleon resonance results presented here were obtained on N_f = 2 configurations and reported in Morningstar et al. (nucleons2009). The stochastic all-to-all propagator techniques with dilution schemes follow the approach of the dilution method (dilute1) and the distillation method using low-lying eigenvectors of the covariant Laplacian. The paper enables future lattice QCD calculations at lighter pion masses by demonstrating that multi-hadron operators can be included using stochastic all-to-all quark propagators with diluted-source variance reduction, which will be essential for reliably extracting excited resonances as multi-hadron thresholds become more numerous.
