# Symmetry-Unrestricted Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Calculations for Exotic Shapes in N=Z Nuclei from 64Ge to 84Mo

> By performing fully 3D symmetry-unrestricted Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations, we discuss shape coexistence and possibility of exotic deformations simultaneously breaking the reflection and axial symmetries in proton-rich N=Z nuclei: 64Ge, 68Se, 72Kr, 76Sr, 80Zr and 84Mo. Results of calculation indicate that the oblate ground state of 68Se is extremely soft against the Y33 triangular deformation, and that the low-lying spherical minimum coexisting with the prolate ground state in 80Zr may be unstable against the Y32 tetrahedral deformation.

## Key claim
Fully three-dimensional, symmetry-unrestricted Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations confirm that the oblate ground state of 68Se is extremely soft against the Y33 triangular deformation and that the low-lying spherical minimum coexisting with the prolate ground state in 80Zr is extremely soft against the Y32 tetrahedral deformation, but the potential is so shallow that no definite physical significance can be associated with the equilibrium values of the deformation parameters.

## Method
The authors performed fully three-dimensional coordinate-space Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations on a Cartesian mesh with 1.0 fm spacing inside a spherical container of radius 10.0 fm, using the SIII Skyrme interaction for the mean-field channel and a density-dependent zero-range pairing interaction with standard parameters (V0 = -1000.0 MeV·fm³, ρc = 0.16 fm⁻³). No spatial symmetries were imposed, and constrained calculations with quadratic constraints on mass-quadrupole and octupole moments were used to map potential energy surfaces as functions of deformation parameters.

## Key numbers
- The calculated energy difference between the prolate and oblate HFB solutions in 68Se is 0.52 MeV, in good agreement with the experimental estimate of about 0.6 MeV for the excitation energy of the prolate band-head.
- The barrier between the prolate and oblate minima in 68Se is about 3 MeV with respect to β₂ but only about 0.3 MeV with respect to the triaxial deformation parameter γ.
- The spherical container radius was set to Rmesh = 10.0 fm and mesh spacing to h = 1.0 fm.
- The pairing interaction strength V0 = -1000.0 MeV·fm³ and density cutoff ρc = 0.16 fm⁻³ were used.
- The second minimum in 84Mo appears at β₂ ≈ 0.66, with an excitation energy of about 1.5 MeV.
- The spacing between the [404]9/2 and [301]3/2 levels at the oblate equilibrium in 68Se is about 2.8 MeV for SIII, 3.4 MeV for SkM*, and 3.6 MeV for SLy4 interactions.
- The spacing between the [413]7/2 and [310]1/2 levels is about 3.8 MeV for SIII, 4.1 MeV for SkM*, and 4.2 MeV for SLy4 interactions.
- The local minimum at β33 ≈ 0.10 in 68Se disappears with a 10% increase in the absolute value of V0.
- The calculated pairing gaps mostly lie in the range 1.0–1.6 MeV, consistent with the global trend Δ̄ = 12/√A MeV (1.3–1.5 MeV for A=64–84).

## Context
This work extends the symmetry-unrestricted Skyrme-Hartree-Fock plus BCS calculations of Takami, Yabana and Matsuo (1998, 1999) by implementing a fully selfconsistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov treatment with a density-dependent zero-range pairing interaction, confirming the extreme softness toward non-axial octupole deformations previously suggested. The results are consistent with experimental data on shape coexistence in 68Se from Fischer et al. (2000) and with earlier theoretical calculations by Bonche et al. (1985) and Tajima et al. (1996). The symmetry-unrestricted code provides a mean-field basis for future studies of collective excitation modes using quasiparticle RPA and the Selfconsistent Collective Coordinate method.
