# Accessing Quark Helicity through Dihadron Studies

> We present a new proposal to study the helicity-dependent dihadron fragmentation functions (DiFF), which describe the correlations of the longitudinal polarization of a fragmenting quark with the transverse momenta of the produced hadron pair. Recent experimental searches for this DiFF via azimuthal asymmetries in back-to-back hadron pair production in $e^+e^-$ annihilation by the ${\tt BELLE}$ Collaboration did not yield a signal. Here we propose a new way to access this DiFF in $e^+e^-$ annihilation, motivated by the recently recalculated cross section of this reaction, which explains why there was in fact no signal for the ${\tt BELLE}$ Collaboration to see. In this new approach the azimuthal asymmetry is weighted by the virtual photon's transverse momentum square multiplying sine and cosine functions of difference of azimuthal angles of relative and total momentum for each pair. The integration over the virtual photon's transverse momentum has the effect of separating the convolution between the helicity-dependent DiFFs in the quark and antiquark jets and results in a nonzero collinear expression containing Fourier moments of helicity-dependent DiFFs. A second new measurement is also proposed for two-hadron production in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering, where the asymmetry is weighted in a similar way for a single pair. This results in a collinear factorized form of the asymmetry, which includes the quark helicity parton distribution function and the same helicity-dependent DiFF as in $e^+e^-$ production and will allow us to check the universality of this DiFF.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the previously predicted azimuthal asymmetry for measuring the helicity-dependent dihadron fragmentation function (DiFF) \(G_1^\perp\) in back-to-back hadron pair production in \(e^+e^-\) annihilation should vanish, contrary to earlier expectations, and presents two new weighted asymmetries that can access this function in both \(e^+e^-\) annihilation and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS).

## Method

The authors rederived the leading-twist cross section for back-to-back dihadron pair production in \(e^+e^-\) annihilation, correcting errors in the earlier derivation by Boer (2003). They then constructed weighted azimuthal asymmetries that include a factor of the virtual photon's transverse momentum squared (\(q_T^2\)) to break up the convolution between the helicity-dependent DiFFs in the quark and antiquark jets, yielding a collinear expression. A similar weighted asymmetry was derived for two-hadron production in SIDIS, using a weight of \(P_{h\perp} \sin(\phi_h - \phi_R)\) to factorize the convolution and obtain a product of the collinear helicity parton distribution function (PDF) and the same integrated helicity-dependent DiFF.

## Key numbers

* The previously proposed asymmetry \(\langle \cos(2(\phi_R - \phi_{\bar{R}})) \rangle = 0\) in \(e^+e^-\) annihilation, explaining the null signal from the BELLE Collaboration.
* The new \(e^+e^-\) asymmetry is defined as \(A_{e^+e^-}^{\Rightarrow}(z, \bar{z}, M_h^2, \bar{M}_h^2) = 4 \frac{\sum_{a,\bar{a}} G_1^{\perp a}(z, M_h^2) G_1^{\perp \bar{a}}(\bar{z}, \bar{M}_h^2)}{\sum_{a,\bar{a}} D_1^a(z, M_h^2) D_1^{\bar{a}}(\bar{z}, \bar{M}_h^2)}\), where \(G_1^{\perp a}\) is the integrated combination of zeroth and second Fourier cosine moments of the helicity-dependent DiFF.
* The new SIDIS asymmetry is \(A^{\Rightarrow}_{SIDIS}(x, z, M_h^2) = S_L \frac{\sum_a g_1^a(x) z G_1^{\perp a}(z, M_h^2)}{\sum_a f_1^a(x) D_1^a(z, M_h^2)}\), where \(g_1^a(x)\) is the collinear helicity PDF and \(f_1^a(x)\) is the unpolarized PDF.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for dihadron fragmentation functions by correcting the cross section derivation of Boer (2003) and explaining the null result from the BELLE Collaboration (Abdesselam 2015, Vossen 2015). It enables experimental tests of the universality of the helicity-dependent DiFF \(G_1^\perp\) between \(e^+e^-\) annihilation and SIDIS, analogous to how the interference DiFF (IFF) has been used to extract transversity PDFs via combined analyses of SIDIS and \(e^+e^-\) data (Bacchetta 2011, Radici 2015). The proposed measurements can be performed by reanalyzing existing BELLE data and through future experiments at Jefferson Lab 12 and the Electron-Ion Collider.
