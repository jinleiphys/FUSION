# How accurately can one test CPT conservation with reactor and solar neutrino experiments?

> We show that the combined data from solar neutrino experiments and from the KamLAND reactor neutrino experiment can establish an upper limit on, or detect, potential CPT violation in the neutrino sector of order 10^{-20} GeV to 10^{-21} GeV.

# How accurately can one test CPT conservation with reactor and solar neutrino experiments?

## Key claim
The combined data from solar neutrino experiments and the KamLAND reactor neutrino experiment can establish an upper limit on CPT violation in the neutrino sector of order 10⁻²⁰ GeV to 10⁻²¹ GeV, but only if the Large Mixing Angle (LMA) solution is the correct description of solar neutrino oscillations and if the allowed anti-neutrino oscillation parameter region from KamLAND is a fraction of the allowed solar neutrino parameter space.

## Method
The authors assume CPT is conserved and compute the maximum allowed difference between neutrino and anti-neutrino survival probabilities consistent with expected experimental uncertainties. They adopt the currently allowed 3σ region in (δm², sin²2θ) space from solar neutrino experiments and assume the KamLAND experiment will find anti-neutrino oscillation parameters within a fraction ε ≤ 1 of that allowed region. They then numerically evaluate the quantity ⟨ΔCPT⟩, defined as twice the absolute difference between average neutrino and anti-neutrino survival probabilities divided by their sum, averaged over reactor distances and neutrino energies.

## Key numbers
- Upper limit on K–anti-K mass difference: |m_K – m_anti-K| < 4.4 × 10⁻¹⁹ GeV (90% C.L.), used as a benchmark for comparison
- Dimensional estimate for CPT sensitivity from reactor-detector distance: δCPT ∼ ℏc/L ∼ 10⁻²¹ (200 km/L) GeV, with L ∼ 200 km for KamLAND
- Dimensional estimate for CPT sensitivity from neutrino oscillation frequency: δCPT ∼ δm²/E ≲ 10⁻¹⁹ GeV, using δm² < 4 × 10⁻⁴ eV² for solar neutrino oscillations
- Upper limit on ⟨ΔCPT⟩ from numerical evaluation: ⟨ΔCPT⟩ ≤ 1.1 (3σ limit), for 10⁶ randomly sampled pairs with ε = 1
- Maximum ⟨ΔCPT⟩ occurs for neutrino parameters δm²_ν = 3.3 × 10⁻⁵ eV², sin²2θ_ν = 0.98; anti-neutrino parameters |δm²_anti-ν| = 6.4 × 10⁻⁵ eV², sin²2θ_anti-ν = 0.6
- Earth matter effects simulating CPT violation contribute approximately 0.1 to ⟨ΔCPT⟩
- For the Coleman-Glashow CPT-violating interaction with relative phase η = 0: δb < 1.6 × 10⁻²¹ GeV (3σ limit)
- For η = π/2: δb < 3.1 × 10⁻²⁰ GeV (3σ limit)
- Earth matter effect floor for intrinsic CPT violation: δb ∼ √2 G_F N_e ≈ 1.5 × 10⁻²² GeV

## Context
This work extends earlier discussions of CPT violation in neutrino oscillations by Coleman and Glashow (1997, 1999), Barger, Pakvasa, Weiler and Whisnant (2000), and Murayama and Yanagida (2001). The authors use the then-current solar neutrino oscillation parameter constraints from Bahcall, Gonzalez-Garcia and Pena-Garay (2001) and anticipate data from the upcoming KamLAND reactor experiment described by Piepke (2001). The results enable future experimental tests of CPT conservation in the neutrino sector at sensitivities exceeding the established K–anti-K mass difference limit by one to two orders of magnitude, with the ultimate sensitivity floor set by earth matter effects that mimic CPT violation at the 10⁻²² GeV level.
