# Lattice QCD Thermodynamics with Physical Quark Masses

> Over the past few years new physics methods and algorithms as well as the latest supercomputers have enabled the study of the QCD thermodynamic phase transition using lattice gauge theory numerical simulations with unprecedented control over systematic errors. This is largely a consequence of the ability to perform continuum extrapolations with physical quark masses. Here we review recent progress in lattice QCD thermodynamics, focussing mainly on results that benefit from the use of physical quark masses: the crossover temperature, the equation of state, and fluctuations of the quark number susceptibilities. In addition, we place a special emphasis on calculations that are directly relevant to the study of relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper reviews recent lattice QCD thermodynamics results that benefit from the ability to perform continuum extrapolations with physical quark masses, establishing that the QCD transition at zero baryon density is a crossover rather than a true phase transition, with a pseudo-critical temperature that depends on the observable used for its definition.

## Method

The authors review lattice QCD calculations using improved staggered fermion actions (asqtad, HISQ/tree, stout) and domain wall fermions (DWF) to compute thermodynamic observables including the chiral condensate, chiral susceptibility, equation of state, and quark number susceptibilities. Continuum extrapolations are performed by combining results at multiple lattice spacings (varying N_tau from 6 to 16), and the pseudo-critical temperature is extracted both from direct fits to inflection points in the chiral condensate and from O(N) scaling analysis that relates the crossover behavior to the critical properties of the chiral limit.

## Key numbers

- Crossover temperature from renormalized chiral condensate inflection point (stout action, f_K scale): T_pc = 155 ± 2 ± 3 MeV
- Crossover temperature from subtracted chiral condensate (stout action): T_pc = 157 ± 3 ± 3 MeV
- Crossover temperature from chiral susceptibility peak (stout action): T_pc = 147 ± 2 ± 3 MeV
- Crossover temperature from O(4) scaling analysis (HotQCD, asqtad and HISQ/tree actions): T_pc = 154 ± 8 ± 1 MeV
- For the trace anomaly from which the equation of state is derived, the gluonic term contributes 80% near the crossover, increasing to 90% by 400 MeV
- Systematic errors associated with scale setting are now only 1-2%

## Context

This review summarizes the progress made by multiple lattice collaborations (Budapest-Wuppertal group using stout actions, HotQCD collaboration using asqtad and HISQ/tree actions, and the DWF collaboration) in achieving continuum extrapolations with physical quark masses, extending earlier work with the p4 action by Ejiri (2009) and the scaling analysis framework developed by Engels (2001, 2011). The results enable direct comparisons between lattice QCD calculations and experimental data from RHIC and the LHC, particularly for the equation of state used in hydrodynamic modeling of heavy ion collisions, and open the door to future calculations of heavy quark color transport and screening.
