# Instabilities in asymmetric nuclear matter

> The existence of phase transitions from liquid to gas phases in asymmetric nuclear matter (ANM) is related with the instability regions which are limited by the spinodals. In this work we investigate the instabilities in ANM described within relativistic mean field hadron models, both with constant and density dependent couplings at zero and finite temperatures. In calculating the proton and neutron chemical potentials we have used an expansion in terms of Bessel functions that is convenient at low densities. The role of the isovector scalar $δ$-meson is also investigated in the framework of relativistic mean field models and density dependent hadronic models. It is shown that the main differences occur at finite temperature and large isospin asymmetry close to the boundary of the instability regions.

# Instabilities in asymmetric nuclear matter

## Key claim

The spinodal instability boundaries of asymmetric nuclear matter show significant differences between relativistic mean field models only at finite temperature and large isospin asymmetry, while at zero temperature the differences are not significant. The density-dependent hadronic models (TW and DDHρδ) and the NLδ model (which includes the isovector scalar δ-meson) exhibit distinct behaviors in the high-asymmetry region near the spinodal boundary, particularly at temperatures above 7 MeV.

## Method

The authors calculated the spinodal instability regions for asymmetric nuclear matter using several relativistic mean field models: the nonlinear Walecka model parametrizations NL3 and TM1, the NLδ model including the isovector scalar δ-meson, the density-dependent hadronic model TW (Typel-Wolter), and a density-dependent hadronic model with ρ and δ couplings (DDHρδ) extracted from Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations. The chemical potentials were computed using an expansion in terms of Bessel functions (following Jaqaman et al., 1984) that is accurate at low densities and temperatures above 7 MeV, and the spinodal boundaries were determined from the stability conditions requiring the free energy density to be a convex function of proton and neutron densities.

## Key numbers

- The NL3 parametrization has a critical temperature T_c ~ 15 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter.
- The DDHρδ model gives a symmetry energy of 25 MeV at saturation density, in contrast to the value of 33.4 MeV mentioned in Gaitanos et al. (2004).
- The NLδ model describes saturation at 0.16 fm^{-3}.
- The spinodal boundaries were calculated for temperatures T = 0, 10, and 14 MeV, and for isospin asymmetry parameters δ ranging from 0 to approximately 0.8.
- The expansion for the effective chemical potential works well for symmetric matter at temperatures T ≥ 7 MeV and subsaturation densities ρ < ρ_0, and for asymmetric matter with proton fraction y_p = 0.1 it is adequate for ρ < 0.8 ρ_0.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of liquid-gas phase transitions in asymmetric nuclear matter using relativistic mean field models (Müller and Serot, 1995; Bao et al., 2002; Brito et al., 2001) and Skyrme-type phenomenological equations of state. It specifically investigates whether recent improvements to relativistic models, including density-dependent couplings (Typel and Wolter, 1999) and the isovector scalar δ-meson (Liu et al., 2002), produce different features at subnuclear densities that could affect predictions for neutron star crust properties, multifragmentation, and isospin fractionation reactions. The results enable identification of which physical observables, such as neutron-proton differential flow (Li, 2000) or protoneutron star properties, could discriminate between different models of the equation of state for asymmetric nuclear matter.
