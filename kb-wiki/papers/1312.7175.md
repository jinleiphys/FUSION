# Extracting electric dipole breakup cross section of one-neutron halo nuclei from breakup observables

> How to extract an electric dipole (E1) breakup cross section σ(E1) from one- neutron removal cross sections measured by using 12C and 208Pb targets, σ_(-1n)^C and σ_(-1n)^Pb, respectively, is discussed. It is shown that within about 5% error, σ(E1) can be obtained by subtracting Γσ_(-1n)^C from σ_(- 1n)^Pb, as assumed in preceding studies. However, for the reaction of weakly-bound projectiles, the scaling factor Γis found to be two times as large as that usually adopted. As a result, we obtain 13-20% smaller σ(E1) of 31Ne at 250 MeV/nucleon than extracted in a previous analysis of experimental data. By compiling the values of Γobtained for several projectiles, Γ=(2.30 +/- 0.41)\exp(- S_n)+(2.43 +/- 0.21) is obtained, where S_n is the neutron separation energy. The target mass number dependence of the nuclear parts of the one-neutron removal cross section and the elastic breakup cross section is also investigated.

## Key claim

The paper validates the standard formula for extracting the electric dipole (E1) breakup cross section from inclusive one-neutron removal measurements, but finds that the scaling factor Gamma for weakly-bound projectiles is about two times larger than previously assumed, leading to 13-20% smaller E1 cross sections for 31Ne at 250 MeV/nucleon than reported in earlier analysis.

## Method

The authors used the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method with eikonal approximation (E-CDCC) for elastic breakup and the eikonal reaction theory (ERT) for neutron stripping, applied to a c+n+A three-body system. They examined four conditions underlying the E1 extraction formula for one-neutron halo projectiles (11Be, 15C, 19C, 31Ne) and additional neutron-rich nuclei, using microscopic folding model potentials with the Melbourne nucleon-nucleon g matrix and Hartree-Fock wave functions based on the Gogny D1S force.

## Key numbers

* For 31Ne at 250 MeV/nucleon, the scaling factor Gamma is about 3.86, roughly two times larger than the mean value (1.7-2.6) assumed in previous work (Nakamura 2009)
* The total error of the E1 extraction formula is 1.9% for 31Ne, 4.1% for 11Be, 3.8% for 15C, and 6.7% for 19C at 250 MeV/nucleon
* The compiled Gamma values follow Gamma = (2.30 +/- 0.41)exp(-S_n) + (2.43 +/- 0.21), where S_n is the neutron separation energy in MeV
* For 31Ne, the extracted sigma(E1) is 763 mb, which is 13-20% smaller than values obtained with the previous Gamma range of 1.7-2.6
* The nuclear one-neutron removal cross section for 11Be on 12C is 110 mb, and on 208Pb is 455 mb (sum of elastic breakup 108 mb and stripping 347 mb)
* The E2 contribution for 8B breakup by 208Pb is about 20% of the E1 contribution, with sigma(E2) = 46 mb versus sigma(E1) = 228 mb

## Context

This work extends the method of Nakamura (2009) for extracting E1 breakup cross sections from inclusive measurements, which was used to identify 31Ne as a one-neutron halo nucleus. The paper provides quantitative validation of the extraction formula using sophisticated three-body reaction models (E-CDCC and ERT) that were developed by Ogata (2003, 2006) and Yahiro (2011). The results enable more reliable extraction of E1 cross sections for future studies of neutron halo nuclei, particularly for weakly-bound projectiles where the standard Gamma values underestimate the nuclear contribution.
