# Non-thermal p/pi ratio at LHC as a consequence of hadronic final state interactions

> Recent LHC data on Pb+Pb reactions at sqrt(s_{NN})=2.7 TeV suggests that the p/pi is incompatible with thermal models. We explore several hadron ratios (K/pi, p/pi, Lambda/pi, Xi/pi) within a hydrodynamic model with hadronic after burner, namely UrQMD 3.3, and show that the deviations can be understood as a final state effect. We propose the p/pi as an observable sensitive on whether final state interactions take place or not. The measured values of the hadron ratios do then allow to gauge the transition energy density from hydrodynamics to the Boltzmann description. We find that the data can be explained with transition energy densities of 840 +- 150 MeV/fm^3.

# Non-thermal p/pi ratio at LHC as a consequence of hadronic final state interactions

## Key claim

The apparent deviation of the p/pi ratio from thermal model predictions at LHC energies can be explained by out-of-equilibrium hadronic final state interactions, specifically baryon-antibaryon annihilation in the hadronic afterburner stage. The measured p/pi ratio can therefore be used to experimentally determine the transition energy density from hydrodynamic to Boltzmann description, which the authors find to be 840 ± 150 MeV/fm³.

## Method

The authors employed the UrQMD 3.3 hybrid model, which combines ideal hydrodynamics with a hadronic cascade afterburner, to simulate central Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 2.75 TeV. Initial conditions were generated from PYTHIA and evolved hydrodynamically until a local energy density threshold was reached, at which point hadrons were sampled via the Cooper-Frye formalism and subsequently evolved in the hadronic cascade. The transition energy density was varied in units of ε₀ = 145 MeV/fm³ to study its effect on final hadron ratios including K/π, p/π, Λ/π, and Ξ/π.

## Key numbers

- Transition energy density extracted from comparison to LHC data: 840 ± 150 MeV/fm³
- Ground state energy density used as unit: ε₀ = 145 MeV/fm³
- Meson yields modified at the 10% level for transition energy densities between 3-9 ε₀
- Upper bound on systematic error from lack of detailed balance in proton yield: 8% of starting value (for highest transition temperature)
- Average number of pions created per baryon-antibaryon annihilation: n ≈ 5
- The inverse reaction 5π → p + p̅ is proportional to the fifth power of the pion density

## Context

This work extends previous hybrid model approaches combining hydrodynamics and transport (Paiva 1996, Aguiar 2001, Teaney 2001, Hirano 2005, Nonaka 2006) and addresses the discrepancy between thermal model predictions (Andronic 2008, Becattini 2000, Cleymans 1996) and LHC data for p/π ratios. The authors contrast their findings with earlier work by Rapp and Shuryak (2000, 2001, 2002) which emphasized multi-pion fusion reactions for antibaryon production at SPS energies; the authors argue that those studies overestimated annihilation rates and that the back-reaction is less significant at LHC energies due to rapid expansion. The results enable experimental calibration of the freeze-out energy density in transport models and suggest that a systematic investigation of final state annihilations across all beam energies (SPS, RHIC, LHC) is needed.
