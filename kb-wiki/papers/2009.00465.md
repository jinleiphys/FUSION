# Heavy Quark Radiation in the Quark-Gluon Plasma in the Moliere Theory: Angular Distribution of the Radiation

> We study the effects of adding the Coulomb interactions to the harmonic oscillator (HO) approximation of the heavy parton propagating through the quark-gluon plasma (the extension to QCD of the Molliere theory). We explicitly find the expression for the transverse momentum distribution of the gluon radiation of the heavy quark propagating in the quark gluon plasma in the framework of the Moliere theory, taking into account the BDMPSZ radiation in the harmonic oscillator (HO) approximation, and the Coulomb logarithms described by the additional logarithmic terms in the effective potential. We show that these Coulomb logarithms significantly influence the HO distribution, derived in the BDMPSZ works, especially for the small transverse momenta, filling the dead cone, and reducing the dead cone suppression of the heavy quark radiation (dead cone effect). In addition we study the effect of the phase space constraints on the heavy quark energy loss, and argue that taking into account of both the phase space constraints and of the Coulomb gluons reduces the dependence of the heavy quark energy loss on its mas in the HO approximation.

## Key claim

The Coulomb logarithms, treated as a perturbation around the harmonic oscillator (HO) approximation of the BDMPSZ formalism, significantly modify the transverse momentum distribution of gluon radiation from heavy quarks in the quark-gluon plasma, especially at small transverse momenta where they fill the dead cone and reduce the dead cone suppression of heavy quark radiation; additionally, including both phase space constraints and Coulomb gluons reduces the dependence of heavy quark energy loss on its mass in the HO approximation.

## Method

The author extends the BDMPSZ harmonic oscillator approximation for parton propagation in the quark-gluon plasma by adding Coulomb interactions as a perturbation, following the Moliere theory approach previously applied to light quarks. The effective potential is split into a harmonic oscillator part and a perturbative Coulomb part, and the full propagator is expanded to first order in the perturbative potential. The resulting expressions for the angular distribution of radiated gluons are evaluated numerically using Mathematica for a static Gyulassy-Wang model medium with temperature T = 0.4 GeV and strong coupling alpha_s = 0.3.

## Key numbers

* The Debye mass is m_D = 0.9 GeV, derived from T = 0.4 GeV and alpha_s = 0.3.
* The bare quenching coefficient is hat q = 0.3 GeV^3, determined by media properties and independent of quark mass.
* The BDMPS maximum angle is theta_BDMPS = 0.22 for omega = 5 GeV, 0.13 for omega = 10 GeV, and 0.08 for omega = 20 GeV, for hat q = 0.3 GeV^3.
* For a bottom quark with m_b = 5 GeV at E = 25 GeV, the quenching coefficient S(E) is 0.33 in the HO approximation and 1.1 when Coulomb logarithms are included, with the constraint k_t <= omega and omega <= E.
* For a light quark at E = 25 GeV, the quenching coefficient S(E) is 0.66 in the HO approximation and 1.8 when Coulomb logarithms are included.

## Context

This work extends the BDMPSZ harmonic oscillator approximation for heavy quark energy loss, originally developed by Dokshitzer and Kharzeev (2001) who predicted significant dead cone suppression, to include Coulomb logarithms following the Moliere theory approach recently applied to light quarks by Mehtar-Tani et al. (2019-2020). The results address the discrepancy between the predicted dead cone suppression and experimental data from Wang (2005) and Kurt (2019) showing that heavy and light quark quenching weights are very close down to jet energies of 25-35 GeV. The calculations enable more realistic comparisons to experimental data on R_AA and v_2 for heavy quarks, though the author notes that detailed comparison requires additional model-dependent inputs including medium expansion and proper phase space constraints.
