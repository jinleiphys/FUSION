# The $ρ$ Spectral Function in a Relativistic Resonance Model

> We calculate the spectral function $A_ρ$ of the $ρ$ meson in nuclear matter. The calculation is performed in the {\it low density} approximation, where the in-medium self energy $Σ_{med}$ is completely determined by the vacuum $ρN$ forward scattering amplitude. This amplitude is derived from a relativistic resonance model. In comparison to previous non-relativistic calculations we find a much weaker momentum dependence of $Σ_{med}$, especially in the transverse channel. Special attention is paid to uncertainties in the model. Thus, we compare the impact of different coupling schemes for the $RNρ$ interaction on the results and discuss various resonance parameter sets.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the in-medium ρ meson spectral function using a relativistic resonance model within the low-density approximation, finding a much weaker momentum dependence of the in-medium self energy compared to previous non-relativistic calculations, especially in the transverse channel, but the authors emphasize that the results carry substantial uncertainties from the choice of coupling schemes and resonance parameter sets.

## Method

The in-medium self energy Σ_med of the ρ meson is computed in the low-density (ρ_N T) approximation, where it is determined by the vacuum ρN forward scattering amplitude summed over baryon resonances. The forward scattering amplitude is derived from relativistic, gauge-invariant Lagrangians for spin-1/2, spin-3/2, and spin-5/2 resonances, with coupling constants fixed by fitting to the measured partial decay widths R → Nρ from the Manley et al. (1992) analysis of πN → ππN reactions. The calculation uses a modified resonance propagator that replaces the mass m_R with √k² in the numerator to maintain unitarity, and includes Fermi motion integration and short-range correlations via a Landau-Migdal parameter g' = 0.5 for the P33(1232) resonance.

## Key numbers

- The Brown-Rho model predicts a downward shift for the ρ meson mass of about 15% – 20% at normal nuclear matter density.
- For p-wave resonances, the ambiguity in the non-relativistic reduction (mixing lab-frame and cm-frame kinematics) leads to uncertainties in the forward scattering amplitude that can be as large as a factor of four, since T_mix = T × (m_R²/m_N²) with resonance masses varying between 1.5 and 2 GeV.
- The cutoff parameter for the phenomenological formfactor is taken as Λ = 1 GeV for all resonances.
- The Landau-Migdal parameter used for renormalizing the P33(1232) contribution is g' = 0.5.

## Context

This work extends previous non-relativistic resonance model calculations by Friman et al. (1995) and Post et al. (1998), which included only p-wave resonances or neglected the D13(1520) resonance, respectively. The relativistic treatment resolves an ambiguity in the non-relativistic reduction that produced uncertainties up to a factor of four for p-wave resonances. The results are relevant for interpreting dilepton spectra from the CERES and HELIOS collaborations, and the authors note that further studies of the momentum dependence of the spectral function are needed for quantitative understanding of ρ meson properties in nuclear matter.
