# Generalized Landau level representation: Effect of static screening in the quantum Hall effect in graphene

> By making use of the generalized Landau level representation (GLLR) for the quasiparticle propagator, we study the effect of screening on the properties of the quantum Hall states with integer filling factors in graphene. The analysis is performed in the low-energy Dirac model in the mean-field approximation, in which the long-range Coulomb interaction is modified by the one-loop static screening effects in the presence of a background magnetic field. By utilizing a rather general ansatz for the propagator, in which all dynamical parameters are running functions of the Landau level index $n$, we derive a self-consistent set of the Schwinger-Dyson (gap) equations and solve them numerically. The explicit solutions demonstrate that static screening leads to a substantial suppression of the gap parameters in the quantum Hall states with a broken $U(4)$ flavor symmetry. The temperature dependence of the energy gaps is also studied. The corresponding results mimic well the temperature dependence of the activation energies measured in experiment. It is also argued that, in principle, the Landau level running of the quasiparticle dynamical parameters could be measured via optical studies of the integer quantum Hall states.

## Key claim

Static screening in the low-energy Dirac model of graphene leads to a substantial suppression of the gap parameters in quantum Hall states with broken U(4) flavor symmetry, and the temperature dependence of the calculated energy gaps mimics well the activation energies measured in experiment, though the analysis is performed in a mean-field approximation that neglects fluctuations of order parameters.

## Method

The authors used the generalized Landau level representation (GLLR) for the quasiparticle propagator within the low-energy Dirac model of graphene, employing a mean-field approximation where the long-range Coulomb interaction is modified by one-loop static screening effects in the presence of a background magnetic field. They derived a self-consistent set of Schwinger-Dyson (gap) equations using a general ansatz for the propagator in which all dynamical parameters are running functions of the Landau level index n, and solved these equations numerically using a Newtonian iteration algorithm with a truncated set of 50 Landau levels.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless coupling constant for suspended graphene is given as α ≡ e²/ε₀v_F ≈ 2.2
- The kernel coefficients κ(0)m,n with static screening range from 0.0079 to 0.0744 for the first 11 Landau levels (m,n = 0 to 10)
- The kernel coefficients κ(1)m,n with static screening range from 0.0039 to 0.3271 for the first 11 Landau levels (m,n = 0 to 10)
- The screening effects decrease the values of κ(ρ)m,n by about a factor of 2 compared to the unscreened case in Gorbar (2012)
- The Fermi velocity is v_F ≈ 10⁶ m/s

## Context

This work extends the study of abnormal integer quantum Hall effects in graphene by Gorbar (2012), which analyzed the long-range Coulomb interaction without screening, by including static screening effects and nonzero temperature. The results enable predictions for transport calculations and emission/absorption rates in high-quality graphene devices fabricated on hexagonal boron nitride substrates as used by Young (2012) and Chiappini (2015), and the approach could potentially allow measurement of Landau level running of quasiparticle dynamical parameters via optical studies of integer quantum Hall states.
