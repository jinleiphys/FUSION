# Constraining the Energy Momentum Tensor through DVCS Dispersion Relation beyond Leading Power

> In this letter, we analyse and interpret the kinematic power corrections to deeply virtual Compton scattering dispersion relation. We show that the kinematic corrections at twist-4 can be connected to other form factors of the Energy-Momentum Tensor beyond the pressure distribution involved at leading-power, namely the ones related to Momentum and total Angular Momentum distributions. In the nucleon case, these corrections are not negligible at presently accessible virtualities. The DVCS subtraction constant becomes an experimental constraint on momentum distributions, pressure forces distributions, and total angular momentum distributions. Finally, we use continuum and lattice-QCD results to predict the expected size of the DVCS subtraction constant, and conclude that momentum distributions are responsible of roughly one-third of the experimental signal at $Q^2 = 2\textrm{GeV}^2$.

## Key claim

Kinematic power corrections to the DVCS dispersion relation at twist-4 connect the subtraction constant not only to the pressure distribution (via the Polyakov-Weiss D-term) but also to momentum and total angular momentum distributions; at currently accessible virtualities like Q² = 2 GeV², these corrections are not negligible, with momentum distributions responsible for roughly one-third of the experimental signal.

## Method

The authors derive the twist-4 kinematic corrections to the DVCS dispersion relation for both scalar and spin-1/2 targets, expressing the subtraction constant as a series of generalized form factors A_{n+1,n} and B_{n+1,n} from the double distribution formalism. They truncate the series at n=2, validate the truncation using phenomenological models (the xFitter pion PDF for scalar targets and the Goloskokov-Kroll model for nucleons), and then evaluate the subtraction constant using lattice QCD results from Hackett (2023) and continuum Schwinger method results from Yao (2024) at Q² = 2 GeV².

## Key numbers

- At Q² = 2 GeV² and t = -0.4 GeV², the total power correction accounts for 1/3 (lattice) and 1/4 (CSM) of the experimental signal
- For scalar targets, the first term (n=0) of the A series contributes 90.7% of the total sum at t = -0.0001 GeV², rising to 94.9% at t = -1.2 GeV²
- For nucleon targets, the first term of the A series contributes 84.4% and the B series 81.5% at t = -0.0001 GeV², rising to 88.5% and 86.6% respectively at t = -1.2 GeV²
- The expansion coefficients are c₀ ≈ 0.864 and c₂ ≈ 0.980
- At Q² = 4 GeV², the impact of power corrections is reduced to roughly 15%

## Context

This work extends the leading-twist DVCS dispersion relation framework of Anikin (2007), Diehl (2007), and Dutrieux (2024) by incorporating kinematic power corrections from Braun (2014) and Martinez-Fernández (2025). It contradicts the assumption that the subtraction constant only constrains the pressure distribution (C form factor), showing instead that it relates A, B, and C form factors of the energy-momentum tensor. The results enable experimental extraction of momentum and angular momentum distributions from DVCS data at JLab 6 GeV, JLab 12, JLab 20, and the future Electron-Ion Collider, while also providing benchmarks for lattice QCD and continuum Schwinger method computations.
