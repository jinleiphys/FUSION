# A statistical model analysis of yields and fluctuations in 200 GeV Au-Au collisions

> We show that the simultaneous measurement of yields and fluctuations is capable of falsifying and constraining the statistical hadronization model. We show how such a measurement can test for chemical non-equilibrium, and distinguish between a high temperature chemically equilibrated freeze-out from a supercooled freeze-out with an over-saturated phase space. We perform a fit, and show that both yields and fluctuations measured at RHIC 200 GeV can be accounted for within the second scenario, with both the light and strange quark phase space saturated significantly above detailed balance. We point to the simultaneous fit of the $K/π$ fluctuation and the $K^*/K^-$ ratio as evidence that the effect of hadronic re-interactions after freeze-out is small.

## Key claim

The simultaneous measurement of yields and fluctuations can falsify and constrain the statistical hadronization model, specifically distinguishing between a high-temperature chemically equilibrated freeze-out and a supercooled freeze-out with over-saturated phase space; the authors show that at RHIC 200 GeV, the equilibrium model fails to describe both yields and fluctuations with the same parameters, while the non-equilibrium model succeeds.

## Method

The authors used the Grand Canonical ensemble implemented in the publicly-available SHARE software package to calculate both average yields and event-by-event fluctuations of hadrons produced in 200 GeV Au-Au collisions at RHIC. They performed a fit incorporating particle ratios from STAR, the preliminary value for the K/pi event-by-event fluctuation, and published yields for phi and pi-, with fit parameters including normalization (volume), temperature, charge fugacities (lambda_q, lambda_s, lambda_I3), and phase space occupancies (gamma_q, gamma_s), while enforcing strangeness, charge, and baryon number conservation.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit freeze-out temperature approximately 140 MeV, in agreement with the supercooled hadronization scenario
- Light quark phase space occupancy gamma_q ~ 1.5, strange quark phase space occupancy gamma_s ~ 2, both significantly above the equilibrium value of 1
- Equilibrium freeze-out temperature ranges from 155 to 177 MeV in prior fits, compared to the non-equilibrium best fit of ~140 MeV
- The fit describes both the K/pi fluctuation and the K*/K- ratio with the same statistical parameters, consistent with negligible hadronic reinteraction after freeze-out
- An isospin fugacity lambda_I3 ~ 0.96 would be required to describe the difference between K+/pi+ and K-/pi- fluctuations, but this is excluded by ratios such as pi+/pi- and Q/B

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model (SHM) approach of Hagedorn (1965) and earlier authors by incorporating event-by-event fluctuations as a new observable, building on theoretical work by Jeon and Koch (2000) and others. The authors show that fluctuations can resolve the degeneracy between equilibrium models (with freeze-out temperatures around 155-177 MeV from fits by Barannikova (2003) and Braun-Munzinger et al. (2004)) and non-equilibrium supercooled freeze-out scenarios (with gamma_q ~ 1.5, gamma_s ~ 2 from Rafelski (2003)), which previously had similar statistical significance (~20% difference). The results enable future tests of whether hadronic reinteraction after freeze-out is negligible, and provide a method to probe the nature of the QCD phase transition.
