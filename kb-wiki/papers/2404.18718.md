# Diffusion coefficient matrix for multiple conserved charges: a Kubo approach

> The strongly interacting matter created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions possesses several conserved quantum numbers, such as baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge. The diffusion process of these charges can be characterized by a diffusion matrix that describes the mutual influence of the diffusion of various charges. We derive the Kubo relations for evaluating diffusion coefficients as elements of a diffusion matrix. We further demonstrate that in the weak coupling limit, the diffusion matrix elements obtained through Kubo relations reduce to those obtained from kinetic theory with an appropriate identification of the relaxation times. We illustrate this evaluation in a toy model of two interacting scalar fields with two conserved charges.

## Key claim

The authors derive Kubo (Green-Kubo) relations for the full diffusion coefficient matrix in a system with multiple conserved charges, showing that in the weak coupling limit these relations reduce to kinetic theory results only with an appropriate identification of relaxation times; the derivation is illustrated in a toy model of two interacting scalar fields with two conserved charges, but the general formalism applies to any number of conserved charges such as baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors employ Zubarev's non-equilibrium statistical operator (NESO) method to derive linear response relations connecting the diffusion matrix elements to retarded equilibrium correlation functions of the dissipative charge currents. They express the diffusion coefficients in terms of the zero-momentum, low-frequency limit of the spectral function of the current-current correlators, and then evaluate these spectral functions perturbatively using the imaginary time formalism (Matsubara technique) for a system of two complex scalar fields with quartic self-interactions and mutual coupling, at finite temperature and chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- The diffusion matrix elements in the particle basis are given by \(\kappa_{ab} = \left.\frac{T}{3}\frac{\partial}{\partial \omega}\text{Im}\,G^{R}_{\hat{\mathcal{J}}^{\mu}_{a},\hat{\mathcal{J}}_{\mu\,b}}(\mathbf{0}, \omega)\right|_{\omega \rightarrow 0}\) (Eq. 4.18 in the text), relating them to the retarded Green's function at zero momentum.
- The diffusion matrix in the conserved charge basis is \(\kappa_{AB} = \sum_{a,b} q_{aA} q_{bB} \kappa_{ab}\) (Eq. 4.20), where \(q_{aA}\) is the charge matrix connecting particle species \(a\) to conserved charges \(A\).
- The toy model Lagrangian contains masses \(m_{\phi}\) and \(m_{\xi}\), self-couplings \(\lambda_1\) and \(\lambda_2\), and mutual coupling \(g\) (Eq. 5.2), with no numerical values assigned to these parameters in the paper.

## Context

This work extends the Green-Kubo formalism for transport coefficients, previously applied to shear and bulk viscosity (Harutyunyan 2017, Huang 2011) and electrical conductivity (Aarts 2014), to the case of a diffusion matrix coupling multiple conserved charges. It provides a first-principles field-theoretic alternative to kinetic theory approaches such as the Chapman-Enskog expansion (Greif 2017, Fotakis 2019) and relaxation time approximation (Das 2021), and enables future nonperturbative calculations of cross-diffusion coefficients using lattice QCD or other strong-coupling methods.
