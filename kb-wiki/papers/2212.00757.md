# Basics of factorization in a scalar Yukawa field theory

> The factorization theorems of quantum chromodynamics (QCD) apply equally well to most simple quantum field theories that require renormalization but where direct calculations are much more straightforward. Working with these simpler theories is convenient for stress-testing the limits of the factorization program and for examining general properties of the parton density functions (pdfs) or other correlation functions that might be necessary for a factorized description of a process. With this view in mind, we review the steps of factorization in a real scalar Yukawa field theory for both deep inelastic scattering (DIS) and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) cross sections. In the case of SIDIS, we illustrate how to separate the small transverse momentum region, where transverse momentum dependent (TMD) pdfs are needed, from a purely collinear large transverse momentum region, and we examine the influence of subleading power corrections. We also review the steps for formulating TMD factorization in transverse coordinate space, and we study the effect of transforming to the well-known $b_*$-scheme. Within the Yukawa theory, we investigate the consequences of switching to a generalized parton model (GPM) approach, and compare with a fully factorized approach. Our results highlight the need to address similar or analogous issues in QCD.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the factorization theorems of QCD can be tested in a simpler scalar Yukawa field theory where all calculations are perturbative, and it shows that the differences between factorized and unfactorized cross sections at moderate hard scales can provide guidance on the size of subleading power corrections and the impact of different definitions for parton density functions, though the authors caution that results from this toy theory cannot be directly compared with real QCD.

## Method

The authors calculate the exact cross sections for deep inelastic scattering (DIS) and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) in a real scalar Yukawa field theory with nonzero masses for all fields, using low-order Feynman diagrams at small coupling. They then apply the standard factorization approximations appropriate to the deeply inelastic regime, expanding in small m²/Q², and compare the factorized results with the exact unfactorized calculations for various values of Q and the mass parameters. The procedure includes formulating transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization in transverse coordinate space and studying the effect of the b*-scheme for separating large and small transverse coordinate contributions.

## Key numbers

* The intrinsic mass scales used in the numerical examples are m_N = m_s = 1.0 GeV and m_q = 0.3 GeV, chosen to mimic typical small mass scales in QCD.
* The coupling strength is defined as a_λ ≡ λ²/(16π²), and the authors assume a_λ is small enough that running can be entirely neglected.
* The renormalized quark-in-hadron collinear pdf at order a_λ is given by f^{(1)}_{q/p}(ξ;μ) = a_λ(μ)(1-ξ)[χ(ξ)²/Δ(ξ)² + ln(μ²/Δ(ξ)²) - 1], where Δ(ξ)² ≡ ξ m_s² + (1-ξ)m_q² - ξ(1-ξ)m_N² and χ(ξ)² ≡ (m_q + ξ m_N)².
* The TMD pdf at order a_λ is f^{(1)}_{q/p}(ξ,k_T;μ) = (a_λ(μ)/π)(1-ξ)[k_T² + χ(ξ)²]/[k_T² + Δ(ξ)²]².
* The cutoff definition for the collinear pdf integrated up to k_c is f^c_{q/p}(ξ;μ;k_c) = a_λ(μ)(1-ξ)[ln((Δ(ξ)²+k_c²)/Δ(ξ)²) - k_c²/(k_c²+Δ(ξ)²) + k_c²χ(ξ)²/(Δ(ξ)²(Δ(ξ)²+k_c²))].

## Context

This work extends the approach of Collins (2021) who used scalar Yukawa theory to illustrate the possibility of positivity violations in pdfs defined with the MSbar renormalization scheme. The paper enables systematic stress-testing of factorization assumptions and provides a controlled environment to study the size of subleading power corrections, the consequences of switching between different pdf definitions (such as cutoff versus renormalized definitions), and the impact of large-b modeling in TMD factorization, all of which are relevant to ongoing debates about intrinsic charm in the proton pdf (Ball 2022, Courtoy 2022, Guzzi 2022) and to implementations of TMD factorization at moderate hard scales.
