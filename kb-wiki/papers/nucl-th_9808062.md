# Search for excited states in H-3 and He-3

> The d+N systems are studied in a three-body model, using phenomenological N-N interactions. The scattering matrices are calculated by using the Kohn-Hulthen variational method. Then, they are analytically continued to complex energies and their singularities are localized. We find a virtual state at E=-1.66 MeV in H-3 and a pair of states at E=(-0.42+-i0.52) MeV in He-3 relative to the d+N thresholds, respectively. In addition, we discuss some general aspects and problems of virtual states which may be useful also in the study of other systems, like Li-10.

# Search for excited states in H-3 and He-3

## Key claim

The authors find a virtual state in H-3 and a pair of conjugate poles (quasi-resonances) in He-3 using a three-body model with phenomenological N-N interactions, but note that the insufficient attraction in their model likely moves the poles too far from the negative energy axis compared to what would be obtained with more realistic interactions including three-body forces.

## Method

The d+N systems were studied in a nonrelativistic three-body potential model using the Eikemeier-Hackenbroich (EH) phenomenological N-N interaction, with only J^pi = 1/2+ states considered. The scattering matrices were calculated using the Kohn-Hulthén variational method for a 9-channel model space, then analytically continued to complex energies on the multisheeted Riemann surface to localize singularities.

## Key numbers

- Virtual state in H-3 at E = -1.66 MeV relative to the d+n threshold
- Pair of conjugate poles in He-3 at E = (-0.42 ± i0.52) MeV relative to the d+p threshold
- Calculated binding energies: H-3 at -7.65 MeV and He-3 at -6.99 MeV (compared to experimental values of -8.482 MeV and -7.718 MeV, respectively)
- Preliminary R-matrix analysis results indicate H-3 virtual state at -1.07 MeV and He-3 poles at (-0.72 ± i0.23) MeV
- For the N+N system alone: n+n virtual pole at E = -0.134 MeV and p+p conjugate poles at E = (-0.101 ± i0.515) MeV from the EH interaction

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical efforts to identify excited states in A=3 nuclei, which had been controversial experimentally. The authors note that earlier calculations by Friar and collaborators (1989) found negative-energy poles in the d+n effective-range function at approximately -160 keV using the S-wave MT I-III interaction, and similar poles were found in the d+p phase-shift analysis by Black and collaborators (1998). The results are consistent with preliminary R-matrix analyses of A=3 data being performed by the same authors. The discussion of virtual states and their experimental signatures is intended to be useful for other systems such as Li-10, where experimental confusion about the ground state nature may arise from virtual state effects.
