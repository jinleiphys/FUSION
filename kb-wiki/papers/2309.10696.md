# Nuclear descent from the fission barrier in the presence of long--range memory effects

> We have investigated the peculiarities of nuclear descent from a parabolic fission barrier within a generalized Langevin equation with power--law $f(t-t')=(|t-t'|/τ)^{-α}$ memory function. We have observed much stronger slowing down of the nuclear descent in the presence of long--range memory effects, caused by the power--law memory function at $0<α<1$, than in the presence of short--range memory effects, generated by exponential $f(t-t')={\rm exp}(-|t-t'|/τ)$ memory function. At a specific value of the exponent $α=1/2$ of the power--law memory function, it turned out possible to find analytically the trajectory of the descent and demonstrate that the long--range memory effects give rise to complex time oscillations of nuclear shape, becoming more frequent and damped with the correlation time $τ$. We have found fairly long ($>10^{-20}~{\rm s}$) times of the descent of $^{\rm 236}{\rm U}$ at the values of the correlation time $τ\sim [10^{-24}÷10^{-23}]~{\rm s}$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that long-range memory effects, modeled by a power-law memory function, cause a much stronger slowing down of nuclear descent from the fission barrier than short-range memory effects from an exponential memory function, with the effect being so pronounced that it appears even at extremely small correlation times where exponential-memory dynamics would already be Markovian.

## Method

The authors linearize a generalized Langevin equation for a nuclear shape variable near the top of a parabolic fission barrier and solve it analytically for the case of a power-law memory function with exponent α = 1/2. They compute the trajectory of descent and the time to reach the scission point for 236U by solving the homogeneous part of the equation via Laplace transformation and factorization of the resulting quartic secular equation.

## Key numbers

- For 236U, the fission barrier height is Vb = 8 MeV, the saddle point is at qb = 1.6, and ℏωb = 1.16 MeV.
- The descent time tdes for 236U exceeds 10−20 s at correlation times τ in the range [10−24 ÷ 10−23] s.
- Varying τ from 10−24 s up to 10−22 s gives descent times in the range tdes ∼ [10−20 ÷ 10−17] s.
- The transition between weak and pronounced long-range memory effects occurs at ωbτ ≈ 2 × 10−3.
- The friction strength parameter is fixed at κ0/(Mbωb2) = 42, taken from Fermi-liquid model calculations.
- The scission condition is Epot(qb) − Epot(qsc) = −20 MeV.
- The initial kinetic energy is (πT)/4 at temperature T = 2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier non-Markovian studies of fission dynamics that used only exponential memory functions, such as those by Kora (2001) and Kora (2009). The paper shows that power-law memory functions produce qualitatively similar oscillatory behavior but with quantitatively much longer descent times, and it provides an analytical solution for the α = 1/2 case that includes a slowly decaying algebraic term ∼ 1/√t not present in exponential-memory models. This enables future microscopic calculations of the memory function to be tested against the much stronger slowing-down predictions.
