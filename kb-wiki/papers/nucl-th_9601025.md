# Does The 3N-Force Have A Hard Core?

> The meson-nucleon dynamics that generates the hard core of the RuhrPot two-nucleon interaction is shown to vanish in the irreducible 3N force. This result indicates a small 3N force dominated by conventional light meson-exchange dynamics and holds for an arbitrary meson-theoretic Lagrangian. The resulting RuhrPot 3N force is defined in the appendix. A completely different result is expected when the Tamm-Dancoff/Bloch-Horowitz procedure is used to define the NN and 3N potentials. In that approach, (e.g. full Bonn potential) both the NN {\it and} 3N potentials contain non-vanishing contributions from the coherent sum of meson-recoil dynamics and the possibility of a large hard core requiring explicit calculation cannot be ruled out.

## Key claim

Within the unitary transformation formalism used to define the RuhrPot nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction, the class of meson-exchange processes that generate the hard core in the NN force vanish exactly in the irreducible three-nucleon (3N) force, indicating a small 3N force dominated by light meson-exchange dynamics; however, if the Tamm-Dancoff/Bloch-Horowitz procedure (as used for the full Bonn potential) is adopted instead, both the NN and 3N potentials contain non-vanishing contributions from meson-recoil dynamics and a large hard core in the 3N force cannot be ruled out.

## Method

The authors derived the one-boson-exchange potential (OBEP) and two-boson-exchange potential (TBEP) using both the Tamm-Dancoff/Bloch-Horowitz projection formalism and the unitary transformation (Okubo) method, comparing the resulting effective interactions for the A-body system. They then isolated the contributions from the coherent sum of all (J^pi;T)-exchange processes in the 3N system, specifically the meson-recoil and wave-function re-orthonormalization terms, and evaluated their sum in the non-relativistic limit for the RuhrPot model.

## Key numbers

- The RuhrPot NN interaction fits scattering phases with chi^2/datum = 1.6.
- The RuhrPot NN model predicts deuteron properties: binding energy E_D = -2.224 MeV (experimental -2.224575(9) MeV), quadrupole moment Q_D = 0.276 fm^2 (experimental 0.2860(15) fm^2), asymptotic S-state amplitude A_S = 0.882 fm^(1/2) (experimental 0.8846(8) fm^(1/2)), D/S ratio = 0.025 (experimental 0.0272(4)), and matter radius r_D = 1.932 fm (experimental 1.9560(68) fm).
- The SU(3)_F prediction g^2_NNomega / g^2_NNrho = 9 is retained in the RuhrPot model, compared to values near 27 typical of conventional boson-exchange potentials.
- Triton binding energy calculated with the RuhrPot NN interaction alone gives E_B = -7.64 MeV; including the consistently defined 3N interaction gives E_B = -8.34 MeV, compared to the experimental result of -8.48 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the RuhrPot approach to NN interactions (Eden and Gari, 1991; 1994), which introduced contact terms via a closure approximation to incorporate the summed (J^pi;T)-exchange dynamics that generate the NN hard core, into the 3N system. The result contradicts what would be expected from the Tamm-Dancoff/Bloch-Horowitz procedure used for the full Bonn potential, where no wave-function re-orthonormalization terms appear to cancel the recoil dynamics. The finding supports the validity of existing 3N force models such as the Tucson-Melbourne force (based on pi-pi, pi-rho, and rho-rho exchange fixed by piN scattering data) and the Brazilian force, and enables consistent calculations of three-nucleon observables using the RuhrPot NN and 3N interactions together.
